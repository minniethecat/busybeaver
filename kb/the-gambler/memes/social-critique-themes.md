# Social Critique Themes

Beneath its surface as a gambling novel, *The Gambler* is a sustained satire of European high society. Dostoyevsky uses the international cast of Roulettenberg — Russian gentry, French marquises, German barons, English tourists — to expose the vanity, venality, and performative honour codes of the aristocratic class.
