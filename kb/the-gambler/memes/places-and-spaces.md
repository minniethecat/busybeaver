# Places & Spaces

The geography of *The Gambler* is a triangle: Moscow (the source of inheritance), St. Petersburg (the capital of rank), and Roulettenberg (the zone of exception, where the rules of both are suspended). Within Roulettenberg, smaller spaces — the casino floor, the hotel salon, the garden — each carry their own social logic. This page indexes wiki entries for the novel's places and spaces.

## About This Index

Roulettenberg is a fictional town, but Dostoyevsky based it closely on the German spa towns where he himself gambled — Wiesbaden, Bad Homburg, Baden-Baden. The specificity of place in the novel is never accidental: the casino is international space, the hotel salon is aristocratic space, and the garden is where confidences are exchanged away from the wheel.
