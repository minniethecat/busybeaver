# The Gambler · Section 30

[030-001]“No, [[mr-astley|Mr. Astley]]. After all that has been said I—”

[030-002]“_Take care_ of them!” repeated my friend. “I am certain you are still

[030-003]a gentleman, and therefore I give you the money as one gentleman may

[030-004]give money to another. Also, if I could be certain that you would leave

[030-005]both [[homburg|Homburg]] and the [[gambling|gaming]]-tables, and return to your own country, I

[030-006]would give you a thousand pounds down to start life afresh; but, I give

[030-007]you ten louis d’or instead of a thousand pounds for the reason that at

[030-008]the present time a thousand pounds and ten louis d’or will be all the

[030-009]same to you—you will lose the one as readily as you will the other.

[030-010]Take the money, therefore, and good-bye.”

[030-011]“Yes, I _will_ take it if at the same time you will embrace me.”

[030-012]“With pleasure.”

[030-013]So we parted—on terms of sincere affection.

[030-014]But he was wrong. If _I_ was hard and undiscerning as regards [[polina-alexandrovna|Polina]]

[030-015]and De Griers, _he_ was hard and undiscerning as regards Russian people

[030-016]generally. Of myself I say nothing. Yet—yet words are only words. I

[030-017]need to _act_. Above all things I need to think of [[switzerland|Switzerland]].

[030-018]Tomorrow, tomorrow—Ah, but if only I could set things right tomorrow,

[030-019]and be born again, and rise again from the dead! But no—I cannot. Yet I

[030-020]must show her what I can do. Even if she should do no more than learn

[030-021]that I can still play the man, it would be worth it. Today it is too

[030-022]late, but _tomorrow_. Yet I have a presentiment that things can never

[030-023]be otherwise. I have got fifteen louis d’or in my possession, although

[030-024]I began with fifteen gülden. If I were to play carefully at the

[030-025]start—But no, no! Surely I am not such a fool as that? Yet _why_ should

[030-026]I not rise from the dead? I should require at first but to go

[030-027]cautiously and patiently and the rest would follow. I should require

[030-028]but to put a check upon my nature for one hour, and my fortunes would

[030-029]be changed entirely. Yes, my nature is my weak point. I have only to

[030-030]remember what happened to me some months ago at [[roulettenberg|Roulettenberg]], before

[030-031]my final ruin. What a notable instance that was of my capacity for

[030-032]resolution! On the occasion in question I had lost

[030-033]everything—everything; yet, just as I was leaving the Casino, I heard

[030-034]another gülden give a rattle in my pocket! “Perhaps I shall need it for

[030-035]a meal,” I thought to myself; but a hundred paces further on, I changed

[030-036]my mind, and returned. That gülden I staked upon manque—and there _is_

[030-037]something in the feeling that, though one is alone, and in a foreign

[030-038]land, and far from one’s own home and friends, and ignorant of whence

[030-039]one’s next meal is to come, one is nevertheless staking one’s very last

[030-040]coin! Well, I won the [[bet|stake]], and in twenty minutes had left the Casino

[030-041]with a hundred and seventy gülden in my pocket! That is a fact, and it

[030-042]shows what a last remaining gülden can do.... But what if my heart had

[030-043]failed me, or I had shrunk from making up my mind? ...

[030-044]No; tomorrow all shall be ended!
