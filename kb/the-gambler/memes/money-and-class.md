# Money & Class

Money in *The Gambler* is never just currency — it is status, power, and ultimately a solvent that dissolves social hierarchy. At the roulette table, the General and the tutor stand equal before the wheel. This page indexes wiki entries dealing with money, class, and the social-economic themes that structure Dostoyevsky's novel.

## About This Index

Dostoyevsky wrote *The Gambler* under financial duress, and the novel's treatment of money is unsentimental and precise. These entries trace the economic logic of Roulettenberg — from the Grandmother's profligacy to the tutor's dependence on the General's table. See also [Social Critique Themes](social-critique-themes) for broader societal analysis.
