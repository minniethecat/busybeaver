# Winning

Winning at [[roulette]] is the novel's central fantasy — the dream that draws [[Alexei Ivanovitch]] to [[casino|the tables]], that transforms the [[grandmother]] into a reckless gambler, and that pro[[bet|mise]]s liberation from the hierarchies of class and dependency. Yet Dostoyevsky treats winning not as redemption but as intoxication: the winner is no more free than the loser, merely drunk on a different poison. "Perhaps because one cannot help winning if one is fanatically certain of doing so" (006-094): the novel's psychology of winning is a psychology of delusion, not skill.

## The Fantasy of Winning

For Alexei, winning is never about the money alone. It is about what money represents: escape from being a tutor, a "utshitel," a subordinate. Early in the novel he frames winning as a philosophical question — "As to the question whether [[bet|stake]]s and winnings are, in themselves, immoral is another question altogether" (003-036) — but this detachment is a performance. What he actually wants is to become someone else, and [[roulette]] appears to offer a mechanism for that transformation.

The fantasy is not unique to Alexei. The [[grandmother]] arrives at [[roulettenberg|Roulettenberg]] convinced she will win — not because she understands [[roulette|the game]], but because she feels entitled to victory. "An absolute assurance of winning was shining on her face" (016-014): winning, in her mind, is simply what should happen to someone of her station.

## The Experience of Winning

Dostoyevsky's descriptions of winning emphasise its physicality — it courses through the body, alters perception, dissolves self-control:

> Hot shivers went coursing over my body. I was paid out my winnings in bank-notes — amounting, of course, to a total of four thousand florins. (018-029)

The winner's body betrays him. Alexei's shivers, the grandmother's distribution of alms "when, overjoyed at winning, the [[grandmother|Grandmother]] was distributing alms right and left" (016-199), the unnamed gambler who "kept winning and winning, and raking and raking in his gains" (015-049) — all exhibit the same [[losing|loss]] of composure. Winning is not control; it is abandon.

## Winning and Social Hierarchy

| Winner | Reaction | Social Meaning |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| Alexei | Withdraws winnings, feels transformed | Escape from dependency |
| [[grandmother|Grandmother]] | Distributes winnings as alms | Reasserts aristocratic patronage |
| Unknown gambler | Stakes recklessly while crowd watches | Becomes a spectacle, not a person |

Each winner's behaviour reveals what they believe winning should do: Alexei wants to exit the hierarchy, the grandmother wants to confirm her place atop it, and the anonymous gambler — "the [[gambling|play]]er in question was dead to all besides" (015-060) — has abandoned hierarchy entirely for pure absorption in [[roulette|the game]].

The social dimension of winning is clearest in the scene where [[polina-alexandrovna|Polina]] receives her winnings through Alexei. When he hands her the money (003-164), the transaction is charged with everything unspoken between them — his [[slavery|servitude]], her dependence, the impossibility of money resolving what is fundamentally a question of relationship.

## Dostoyevsky's Critique

The novel's treatment of winning is fundamentally ironic. Every character who wins behaves worse, not better: Alexei becomes more arrogant, the grandmother more reckless, the anonymous gambler more isolated. Winning does not solve problems — it intensifies the traits that created them.

This distinguishes *The Gambler* from moralistic gambling fiction. The problem is not that winning is rare (though it is); the problem is that winning, when it happens, is spi[[superstition|ritual]]ly empty. "[[About]] winning may seem to you ridiculous; yet, pray leave me in peace" (004-007): Alexei's plea to [[polina-alexandrovna|Polina]] acknowledges that his certainty is absurd — but he clings to it anyway, because the alternative is to accept his place in an order he despises.
