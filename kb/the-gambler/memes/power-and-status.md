# Power & Status

The hierarchy in *The Gambler* is rigid and fragile at once. The General commands his household by rank and debt; the servants observe and judge; the aristocrats posture; and inheritance — the promise of future wealth — drives the plot from its first page. This page indexes wiki entries across the vertical axis of social power.

## About This Index

Dostoyevsky understood that power is never simply held — it is performed. The General's dignity rests on an expectation of inheritance; Alexei's position as tutor places him simultaneously inside and outside the household; and the Grandmother's arrival collapses the entire structure in a single afternoon. See also [Money & Class](money-and-class) for the economic dimension and [Character Relations](character-relations) for the interpersonal network.
