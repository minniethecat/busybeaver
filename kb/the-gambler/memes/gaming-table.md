# Gaming Table

The **gaming table** is the central physical object of the Roulettenberg casino — the stage upon which every act of gambling in *The Gambler* is performed. More than a piece of furniture, it is a sacred and cursed space: a point of convergence for hope, desperation, calculation, and ruin.

## Physical Description and Function

The gaming table hosts the roulette wheel and the betting layout where players place their stakes. In the Roulettenberg casino, multiple tables operate simultaneously, each with its own [[croupier]] and its own micro-climate of fortune. Players circle the tables, observing patterns, waiting for the right moment to place a [[bet]].

The table functions as both an altar and an abyss. Alexei repeatedly describes the physical act of approaching it — the crowding, the pushing, the breathless watching of the wheel — as a ritual that transforms ordinary men into gamblers. "Ah, the evening when I took those seventy gülden to the gaming table" [[alexei-ivanovitch|Alexei]] recalls (028-096), his memory centred not on the sum but on the physical act of carrying money to the table.

## Psychological Significance

The gaming table exerts a magnetic pull that Dostoyevsky treats with almost supernatural intensity. Characters who have sworn off [[gambling]] find themselves drawn back; those who have won resolve to leave but remain seated. The table represents the boundary between the ordinary world and the world of chance — crossing it means submitting to forces beyond [[reason]].

Physically, the table is democratic: anyone with a stake may play. Social distinctions briefly dissolve as [[general|the General]]'s stepdaughter rubs shoulders with unknown gamblers. But this democracy is illusory — the table ultimately strips every player of social identity and reduces them to a single type: the gambler.

## In Dostoyevsky's Novel

The gaming table appears throughout the novel as the primary arena of action. Key scenes at the table include:

- The [[grandmother|Grandmother]]'s marathon gambling spree, where she commandeers a table with aristocratic imperiousness
- Alexei's extraordinary winning streak, during which the table becomes the site of his transformation from tutor to wealthy man
- The final, desperate sessions where [[marquis-de-griers|de Griers]] watches fortunes dissolve

The table also serves as a social theatre. The [[grandmother|Grandmother]]'s behaviour at the table — loud, foreign, female, old — scandalises the other players, revealing the unwritten codes of conduct that govern even this ostensibly rule-free space.
