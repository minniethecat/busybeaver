# Roulette Wheel

The **roulette wheel** is the physical heart of the casino in *The Gambler* — a revolving mechanism that determines fortune or ruin with each spin. Dostoyevsky repeatedly returns to the image of the spinning wheel, transforming it from a mere gaming device into a symbol of fate, compulsion, and the uncontrollable forces that govern human life.

## The Mechanism

The wheel operates on simple principles: a croupier sets it spinning, a ball is released in the opposite direction, and the numbered slot in which the ball lands determines the outcome. Players place their [[stake|stakes]] on numbers, colours (red or black), or categories (even or odd) before each spin. When the wheel stops at zero, the [[gambling-bank|bank]] takes everything (015-110).

> The wheel spun and stopped at 13. I had lost! (003-137)

## "The Ever-Revolving Wheel"

Dostoyevsky's most potent image is the wheel in perpetual motion — the "ever-revolving wheel" (015-023) that mesmerises players and draws them into its rhythm. The wheel's ceaseless turning mirrors the psychological cycle of gambling: anticipation, revelation, elation or despair, and then — inevitably — the next spin. This mechanical repetition erases past outcomes and promises fresh possibility with every revolution.

> ...roulette, with its ever-revolving wheel. (015-023)

## The Wheel as Fate

The roulette wheel functions as a secular symbol of fate throughout the novel. Unlike Christian providence or rational calculation, the wheel offers no moral order — it is pure chance, indifferent to virtue or vice. Alexei's pivotal reflection at the novel's close invokes the wheel as the agent of transformation: a single turn changed everything (028-048). The wheel is simultaneously liberating (offering escape from social hierarchy) and destructive (enslaving the player to chance).

> ...a single turn of a roulette wheel, everything for me, has become changed. (028-048)

## The Croupier and the Wheel

The [[croupier]] serves as the wheel's human attendant — spinning it, calling the results, and raking in lost stakes. The relationship between croupier and wheel is almost liturgical: the croupier's calls ("Le jeu est fait!" — 016-005) mark the ritual moments of the gambling ceremony, with the wheel as its altar.
