# Emotion & Morality

Dostoyevsky charts his characters' inner lives with relentless precision — shame, pride, despair, and fleeting pleasure are rendered with the force of lived experience. *The Gambler* is also a moral novel: honour is tested, reproach is a weapon, and prayer appears as a reflex rather than a conviction. This page indexes wiki entries across the emotional and moral spectrum.

## About This Index

Alexei Ivanovitch insists he is a rational gambler — he has a system, he calculates odds, he knows when to walk away. The novel's entire plot is the systematic demolition of that claim. These entries trace the arc from calculation to compulsion. See also [Gambling & Psychology](gambling-psychology) for the psychological dimension of the gambling experience itself.
