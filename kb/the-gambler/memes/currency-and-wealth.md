# Currency & Wealth

Dostoyevsky was precise about money. In *The Gambler*, specific currencies — the gulden, the florin, the franc, the rouble — circulate through the roulette tables of Roulettenberg with a specificity that anchors the novel's psychological drama in material fact. This page indexes wiki entries covering currency, wealth accumulation, debt, and credit.

## About This Index

The values in *The Gambler* are stated with a journalist's precision: Alexei wins 100,000 florins, the General owes the Marquis a ruinous sum, and the Grandmother arrives with pockets full of roubles. This precision is not ornamental — it is the novel's way of showing that money, once counted, cannot be romanticised away. See also [Money & Class](money-and-class) for the social dimension.
