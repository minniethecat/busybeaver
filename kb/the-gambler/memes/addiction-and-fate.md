# Addiction & Fate

Two opposing forces drive the characters of *The Gambler*: the **addictive compulsion** to keep playing, and the **fatalistic belief** that the wheel has already decided. Alexei Ivanovitch swings between these poles — calculating his system one moment, surrendering to "fate" the next. This page indexes wiki entries tagged with `addiction` or `fate`.

## About This Index

Dostoyevsky understood addiction from the inside — he wrote *The Gambler* while battling his own roulette compulsion. The novel's psychological realism on this point is clinical: the fever, the magical thinking, the post-loss clarity followed by relapse. These entries map that territory. See also [Gambling & Psychology](gambling-psychology) for the gambling-psychology intersection.
