# The Hotel

The hotel at [[roulettenberg|Roulettenberg]] is the novel's domestic stage—the place where characters eat, scheme, argue, and recover from their [[loss|gambling loss]]es. Located near the casino, "I was assigned a small room on the fourth floor of the hotel" (001-039) establishes Alexei's subordinate position, while "our party occupied two suites in the hotel" (008-015) maps the social geography. The hotel houses the General's household, [[marquis-de-griers|de Griers]], [[mlle-blanche|Mlle. Blanche]] and her mother, and [[mr-astley|Mr. Astley]]—a concentrated social world where no private life is possible because everyone is always watching.

## Definition

The hotel's architecture is suggested rather than described: a verandah where conversations are overheard ("At the moment, we were approaching my hotel" — 012-013), a staircase where encounters occur, drawing-rooms where the General holds court, and private rooms where [[polina-alexandrovna|Polina]] retreats and Alexei is summoned. "At spas—and, probably, all over Europe—hotel landlords and managers" (012-103) notes the hotel's reputation as "the best, the most expensive." It is a space of forced intimacy—characters who would avoid each other in a larger city must share meals, corridors, and the [[shame|humiliation]] of public financial collapse.

## Role in The Gambler

The hotel and the casino form the novel's binary geography: the hotel is where character is performed, the casino where it is exposed (008-015). [[bet|Bet]]ween them, characters shuttle back and forth, carrying money won at [[casino|the tables]] to the hotel and returning to [[casino|the tables]] with money borrowed at the hotel. This rhythm—hotel to casino, casino to hotel—is the novel's pulse.
