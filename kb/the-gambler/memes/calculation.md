# Calculation

**Calculation** in Dostoyevsky's *The Gambler* represents the gambler's illusory attempt to impose rational order upon the chaos of [[chance]]. The novel repeatedly juxtaposes methodical calculation against impulsive passion, revealing the futility of mathematical systems in the face of gambling's psychological power.

## The Illusion of Control

Alexei initially approaches [[roulette]] with the conviction that "calculation was of little avail" [005-031], yet he remains fascinated by the elaborate rituals of system-players: gamblers who "set down the coups, calculated the chances" [005-034] with pencil and paper. The casino itself is described as a place of intense computation — "an immense amount of calculation" [003-118] accompanies the watching and the staking.

## Calculation vs Impulse

The novel's psychological drama pivots on the tension between rational [[calculation]] and irrational [[desire]]. Alexei observes fellow players who stake "without any sort of calculation — just what came to his head" (015-048), even as others play "quietly, coldly, and after much calculation" (015-086). During his own winning streak, he abandons calculation entirely: "I could not remember my calculations or the order of my stakings" (023-100). This surrender to impulse marks both his greatest financial success and his deepest psychological descent.

## Calculation and Character

The motif of calculation extends beyond the casino to characterize social relationships. Mr. Astley, the English industrialist who embodies "character, perseverance, and calculation" (005-180), serves as a foil to Alexei's passionate impulsiveness. The novel suggests that the calculating temperament — while prudent — lacks the intensity and vulnerability that define the gambler's humanity.

## The Social World of Calculated Play

Dostoyevsky presents calculation not merely as an individual mental activity but as a social performance within the casino. The serious players form a silent community bound by their shared devotion to reckoning: they mark down coups in notebooks, exchange knowing glances, and observe an unwritten code of conduct. Even speaking too loudly is forbidden, "since to do so disturbed the calculations of the players" (015-072). This enforced quietude transforms the gambling hall into something resembling a counting-house — a space where the disciplined orchestration of numbers replaces the noisy chaos one might expect. The calculating gambler does not merely think; he performs his rationality for an audience of fellow calculators, each pretending that the wheel can be subdued by arithmetic.

## Calculation and Currency

The practical act of calculation extends to the mundane mechanics of gambling — converting currencies, counting chips, and reckoning winnings. After the Grandmother's arrival transforms the casino's financial landscape, the staff is described engaged in "complicated calculations which soon reduced them to a state of bewilderment" (018-128). Even the professionals — those whose job is to calculate — find themselves overwhelmed when the sums grow large enough. This detail suggests that calculation has a breaking point: beyond a certain threshold of value and risk, the rational faculty collapses under its own weight. The calculating mind, which seemed so formidable when managing modest stakes, reveals itself as fragile when confronted with the Grandmother's reckless millions.
