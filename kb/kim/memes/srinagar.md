## A Date Marker in Kim's Life

Srinagar enters the narrative not as a visited place but as a temporal landmark. Kim, asked about his birth date, replies: "upon the hour that I cried first fell the great earthquake in Srinagar which is in Kashmir." (002-181) This earthquake, felt across India, became "a leading date in the Punjab" — a fixed point by which the family priest later casts Kim's horoscope. Kim learned this connection from the woman who raised him, who in turn had it from Kimball O'Hara.

## Strategic Gateway to the Hills

In Chapter 13, the Russian spies' route is noted with concern: "They should have come in by Srinagar or Abbottabad. That is their natural road." (013-024) This positions Srinagar as a key entry point into the hill country — a gateway that, when bypassed, signals suspicious behaviour. The lama's own journey from Tibet would have passed through these northern approaches.
