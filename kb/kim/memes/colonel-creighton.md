# Colonel Creighton

**Colonel Creighton** is the British officer who directs the [[the-chain-men|intelligence network]] of the [[the-great-game|Great Game]] in Rudyard Kipling's *[[kimball-ohara|Kim]]*. Operating behind the respectable cover of ethnology and colonial administration, Creighton is the strategic mind who identifies [[kimball-ohara|Kim]]'s potential and orchestrates his development from bazaar boy to trained spy.

## Background

Creighton holds a position in the British Indian administration that allows him to move between the worlds of scholarship and espionage. He is an ethnologist by public profession, studying the peoples and cultures of India — a role that provides both cover and genuine interest. When [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] first meets him, he learns that "the Colonel, [[mahbub-ali|Mahbub Ali]], and I" are "all on one lead-rope" (007-048), revealing Creighton's central position in the intelligence hierarchy.

## Role in the Narrative

Creighton first appears when [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] is captured by [[the-mavericks|the Mavericks regiment]]. Recognizing the boy's unique capabilities, Creighton arranges for [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] to be sent to [[st-xaviers-school|St. Xavier's School]] and then trained for [[the-survey-of-india|the Survey]] of India — which serves as cover for intelligence work. He tells [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] he will "enter [[the-survey-of-india|the Survey]] of India as a chain-man" (007-033), a position that combines legitimate employment with covert operations.

Creighton operates primarily in the background, his influence felt through the agents he directs rather than through direct action. He coordinates [[mahbub-ali|Mahbub Ali]]'s courier work, [[lurgan-sahib|Lurgan Sahib]]'s training program, and [[hurree-babu|Hurree Babu]]'s undercover operations. His genius lies in identifying talent and deploying it effectively — he sees in [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] what [[mahbub-ali|Mahbub Ali]] first glimpsed: a boy who could go where European agents could not.

The novel portrays Creighton as a competent, unsentimental professional. He is neither villain nor hero but a man doing necessary work within the constraints of empire. His treatment of [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] is paternalistic but not cruel; he genuinely believes he is giving the boy a better life while also serving the interests of the British Raj.

## Relationships

- **[[Kimball O'Hara]]** — Creighton's protege, whose education and deployment he orchestrates
- **[[Mahbub Ali]]** — Creighton's key field agent and courier
- **[[Lurgan Sahib]]** — Works with Creighton in training new intelligence operatives
- **[[Hurree Babu]]** — One of Creighton's most valuable undercover agents
