## Geographic Position

The Siwaliks (also known as the Shivalik Hills) are the outermost and lowest range of the Himalayan system, forming the first barrier between the Indo-Gangetic plains and the higher mountain ranges beyond. In the narrative, the Siwaliks mark the threshold between the known world of the plains and the mysterious high country. (013-007)

## Narrative Significance

The crossing of the Siwaliks represents a major transition in the journey of the lama and Kim. Having left Mussoorie behind, they cross the Siwaliks and the half-tropical Doon valley, heading north along the narrow hill roads into the "huddled mountains" of the interior. This passage marks their entry into the true Himalayan wilderness—a world governed by different laws, different peoples, and the raw forces of nature. (013-007) (012-050)

## Climate and Environment

In Chapter 15, the "modified heat of the lower Siwaliks" is contrasted with the extreme cold of the higher elevations. The foothills collect travelers and priests seeking respite from the harsh mountain conditions, serving as a transitional zone between the severe highlands and the warmer plains. (015-008)
