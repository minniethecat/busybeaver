# The Red Bull Prophecy

**The Red Bull Prophecy** is the prediction left by [[kimball-ohara|Kimball O'Hara]] Sr. that his son would encounter "a Red Bull on a green field" (001-035), a prophecy fulfilled when [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] discovers the regimental flag of [[the-mavericks|the Mavericks]] — a red bull on a green background — and is recognized as a white boy by the British authorities.

## In the Novel

The prophecy is established in the novel's opening chapters as part of [[kimball-ohara|Kim]]'s inheritance from his father, an Irish soldier who died in poverty in [[lahore|Lahore]]. His father left him three documents — his birth certificate, his discharge papers, and his Masonic certificate — and a prophecy. The prophecy was [[kimball-ohara|Kim]]'s only spiritual inheritance, a promise that his [[identity-and-disguise|mixed identity]] would one day be resolved by a sign.

The fulfillment of the prophecy occurs when [[kimball-ohara|Kim]], traveling with [[teshoo-lama|the lama]], stumbles upon the camp of [[the-mavericks|the Mavericks regiment]]. [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] sees the red bull flag and recognizes it as his destined sign (005-020). This recognition leads to his capture by the British, his enrollment at [[st-xaviers-school|St. Xavier's]], and his formal entry into the colonial apparatus — the beginning of his transformation from bazaar boy to Sahib-spy.

The prophecy serves multiple functions in the novel's architecture. It provides a mythic framework for [[kimball-ohara|Kim]]'s mundane discovery by the regiment, connecting his individual story to larger patterns of fate. It also introduces the theme of predestination, which runs counter to [[teshoo-lama|the lama]]'s Buddhist teaching about karma and free will.

## Key Passages

> "A Red Bull on a green field" (001-035)
> — The prophecy that shapes Kim's expectations of his own destiny

> The moment of recognition when Kim sees the Mavericks flag represents the collision of his father's Irish-Catholic folk prophecy with the machinery of British imperial administration.

The prophecy operates at the boundary between superstition and destiny, never fully resolving into either category. Its fulfillment is both coincidental and meaningful — a pattern that repeats throughout the novel.

## Symbolism

The Red Bull prophecy connects to several symbolic patterns in the novel:
- **Color symbolism**: Red (blood, passion, violence) against green (life, growth, the land)
- **Animal symbolism**: The bull as strength, fertility, and sacrifice — echoing Hindu symbolism
- **Prophecy and [[identity-and-disguise|identity]]**: The idea that [[kimball-ohara|Kim]]'s destiny is written before he is born
- **Syncretism**: The prophecy blends Irish folk belief with Indian religious symbolism
