## Overview

The Ooryas (Oriyas) were soldiers from the down-country region of Odisha (Orissa) in eastern India, serving in the British Indian Army. They appear in Chapter 4 of *Kim* as part of the diverse soldiery gathered at the Mavericks' regimental camp near Umballa. Described as "thin-legged, grey-bearded Ooryas from down country," they represented one of the many regional and ethnic groups that composed the colonial military. (004-053)

> Half of them were thin-legged, grey-bearded Ooryas from down country. The other half were duffle-clad, felt-hatted hillmen of the North; and that mixture told its own tale, even if he had not overheard the incessant sparring between the two divisions.
>
> (004-053)

## Role in the Narrative

The Ooryas feature in the regimental camp scene where Kim's identity is being investigated. They are depicted as seasoned, older soldiers who observe the unfolding drama with a mixture of amusement and cynicism. When a hillman among the soldiers grows agitated during the interrogation, the Ooryas laugh — a response that nearly provokes violence from the hillman. Throughout the scene, they exchange whispered comments, noting that trouble is brewing and making dry observations about the proceedings. (004-063)(004-066)(004-076)(004-129)

One Oorya, when told the hillman meant to kill him, remarked with grim fatalism that "bullies go to carrion," and another, tying up his shapeless bundle, cursed the hillman's "ill-omened mouth." Their portrayal reflects the ethnic and regional diversity of the British Indian Army, where soldiers from distant provinces served side by side. (004-132)(004-134)(004-140)
