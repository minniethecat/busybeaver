## Location and Institution

Sanawar is a hill station located near Kasauli in the Punjab Hills. It is home to the Lawrence Military Asylum — also referred to as the Military Orphanage at Sanawar — an institution established for the education of children of British soldiers serving in India.

## In the Novel

### The Initial Plan

In Chapter 5, after [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] is captured by the Mavericks and his identity as the son of Kimball O'Hara, Sr., is established, [[father-victor|Father Victor]] and the Regiment arrange for Kim to be sent to the Military Orphanage at Sanawar. The plan is financed partly by the Masonic Lodge and partly by subscription:

> "We'll make a man of you at Sanawar — even at the price o' making you a Protestant."
>
> (005-198)

### Kim's Resistance

Kim, however, resists this arrangement. Having already formed a bond with [[the-teshoo-lama|the Teshoo Lama]] and being accustomed to the freedom of the open Road, he declares his own alternative destination:

> "You will not go to Sanawar. You will go to thee War."
>
> (005-202)

Kim's cryptic statement foreshadows his eventual involvement in [[the-great-game|the Great Game]].

### The Final Arrangement

By Chapter 6, the plan shifts with the arrival of [[the-teshoo-lama|the lama]]'s letter offering three hundred rupees per annum for Kim's education. This, combined with [[colonel-creighton|Colonel Creighton]]'s interest in Kim's potential as an intelligence asset, results in Kim being sent to [[st-xaviers-school|St. Xavier's School]] in [[lucknow|Lucknow]] instead of Sanawar (006-081). Sanawar thus represents the conventional path not taken — the ordinary destiny of an Anglo-Indian orphan, from which Kim's unique talents and connections divert him.

## Significance

Sanawar functions in the novel as the road not taken in Kim's life. The shift from Sanawar to St. Xavier's marks the beginning of Kim's dual education: formal schooling combined with informal training in the arts of espionage.
