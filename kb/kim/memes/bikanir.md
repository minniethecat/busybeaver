Bikanir is a walled city in the Rajasthan desert, known for its extraordinarily deep wells "four hundred feet deep, and lined throughout with camel-bone." (010-009)

[[kimball-ohara|Kim]] and [[mahbub-ali|Mahbub Ali]] visit Bikanir during the monsoon holidays, where Kim maps the city's defenses. He uses his Survey paint-box and a bead rosary to pace distances. (010-009) Kim later tests his revolver with "practice shots in the Bikanir desert." (013-091)

## Geography

Bikanir lies deep in the Thar Desert of Rajasthan, a remote and forbidding landscape. Its wells, sunk to extraordinary depths and lined with camel-bone, testify to the engineering required to sustain life in one of India's most arid regions. (010-009) The walled city historically guarded trade routes between Central Asia and the Indian interior.

## In the Great Game

During the monsoon holidays, Mahbub Ali takes Kim on a camel expedition to Bikanir. Kim applies his Survey training to map the city's fortifications, using his paint-box and a bead rosary to pace distances — techniques learned at Lurgan Sahib's shop. (010-009)

> …to the mysterious city of Bikanir, where the wells are four hundred feet deep, and lined throughout with camel-bone.
>
> (010-009)

The mission represents Kim's transition from schoolboy to field operative, applying Colonel Creighton's training to real intelligence-gathering for the Great Game. (010-009)(013-091)
