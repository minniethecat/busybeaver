## Nature of the Document

The *murasla* is a royal letter—a formal diplomatic document exchanged between rulers. In the context of the Great Game, it serves as a crucial piece of intelligence: a sealed, gilded, and illuminated document that one King sends to another, bound in an embroidered bag. Such letters carry the authority of their sender and contain political communications of the highest order. (013-088) (014-030)

> "The letters I do not understand, but Colonel Creighton will. They must all be kept. The maps — they draw better maps than me — of course. All the native letters — oho! — and particularly the *murasla*." He sniffed the embroidered bag. "That must be from Hilás or Bunár, and Hurree Babu spoke truth. By Jove! It is a fine haul."

## In the Narrative

The *murasla* is the most prized item among the Russian spies' captured documents. Hurree Babu, having observed the spies' baggage, specifically instructs Kim to find "the papers, and specially the *murasla* (King's letter)." When Kim searches the captured *kiltas*, he finds it at the very bottom of a cascade of survey instruments, books, diaries, letters, and maps—an embroidered bag covering a sealed, gilded, and illuminated document. (013-088) (014-030)

## Intelligence Value

Kim immediately recognizes the *murasla's* significance, sniffing the embroidered bag and deducing that it must be "from Hilás or Bunár"—two princely states implicated in the spies' machinations. The document represents eight months of diplomatic work by the Russian agents and is the most damning piece of evidence in the intelligence haul. Kim carries the *murasla* inside his coat and under his belt for safekeeping, understanding its value to Colonel Creighton and the British intelligence apparatus. (014-030) (014-031) (015-085)
