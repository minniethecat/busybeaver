# The Jewel Game at Lurgan's

In the shadowy shop at Simla, Lurgan Sahib laid out a tray of jewels — "a tray of turquoises and rubies and opals" (009-064) — and gave Kim a single minute to study them. It was a test of memory and observation, the foundation skills of a field agent. Kim's performance in the jewel game confirmed what Mahbub Ali had suspected and what Colonel Creighton hoped: that the boy possessed the raw talent for intelligence work.

## Context

Kim had been sent to Lurgan Sahib's shop for training. Lurgan, a dealer in jewels, antiques, and rarities, was also a master of the arts of observation and disguise — a key figure in the Great Game's training apparatus. His shop was "like a Wonder House" (009-020), filled with curiosities that served as props for the training of young agents.

Kim had already demonstrated his street skills — languages, mimicry, quick thinking. But field intelligence required more: the ability to observe a scene, commit every detail to memory, and reconstruct it accurately hours later. This was the discipline of the jewel game.

## The Event

### The tray of jewels

Lurgan Sahib "laid out a tray of jewels before Kim" (009-064) — turquoises, rubies, opals, and other precious stones arranged in careful disorder. Kim was given one minute to study the tray, then the cloth was drawn over it. His task was to recite every stone, its position, and any flaw he had observed.

> "Sometimes very fine jewels will show a flaw if you look long enough" (009-064)

Kim's street-trained eye — sharpened by years of recognizing faces in the Lahore bazaar, reading men's intentions from the smallest gesture — proved formidable. He named the stones, their positions, and identified the flaws Lurgan had deliberately seeded.

### Lurgan's judgment

Lurgan Sahib, "thin as an icicle where tally of jewels was concerned" (009-114), was impressed. The boy's memory was not merely good — it was exceptional. The jewel game had separated those who could serve as agents from those who could only serve as messengers, and Kim had passed decisively.

The training did not end with jewels. Lurgan moved on to other exercises: the identification of disguised men, the reading of coded messages, the arts of patience and observation. But the jewel game remained the signature test — the moment when Kim's talent was formally recognized.

## Consequences

- **Kim is certified as agent material.** The jewel game is the formal assessment that moves Kim from messenger to agent-in-training.
- **Lurgan's curriculum unfolds.** Having established Kim's baseline ability, Lurgan proceeds to advanced training in disguise, memory, and tradecraft.
- **The Great Game gains an asset.** Kim's performance reaches the ears of Creighton, confirming the investment in the boy's education and training.
- **Kim's confidence grows.** The jewel game gives Kim his first taste of formal success in the intelligence world, validating his natural abilities.
