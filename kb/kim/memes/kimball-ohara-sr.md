## Background and Marriage

Kimball O'Hara Sr. was a young Irish colour-sergeant serving in the Mavericks regiment of the British Indian Army. He married the daughter of a colonel's family — a match that defied social expectations of the time (001-003). The marriage produced one son: Kimball O'Hara Jr., known simply as Kim.

## The Prophecy of the Red Bull

Before his death, Kimball O'Hara Sr. left his son with a prophecy: that a Red Bull on a green field would one day come for Kim and change his fortune (001-003). This prophecy — referring to the Mavericks regimental flag — becomes fulfilled when Father Victor and the Mavericks regiment discover Kim in Chapter 5 (005-077).

Father Victor, examining the late O'Hara's documents, exclaims: "Powers of Darkness! Kimball O'Hara! And his son!" (005-084), confirming Kim's identity through his father's military records. The "ne varietur" parchment — Kimball Sr.'s marriage certificate and military discharge papers — had been carefully preserved by the woman who raised Kim (005-077).

> "Not quite so fast. We'll look first," said Father Victor, leisurely rolling out poor Kimball O'Hara's "ne varietur" parchment, his clearance-certificate, and Kim's baptismal certificate. On this last O'Hara---with some confused idea that he was doing wonders for his son---had scrawled scores of times: "Look after the boy. Please look after the boy"---signing his name and regimental number in full.
>
> (005-077)

## Legacy and Character

Though deceased before the novel begins, Kimball O'Hara Sr.'s influence permeates the narrative. His Irish heritage gives Kim his fair skin and English identity — the "sahib" status that enables Kim to move between worlds. His military background connects Kim to the Mavericks and, through them, to the Great Game.

The earthquake mentioned in connection with Kimball O'Hara (002-181) suggests a larger story of hardship and resilience that shaped the O'Hara family's fate in India. Kim's mother, who died of cholera, had received care from the same woman who preserved O'Hara Sr.'s papers.

## Kim's Inheritance

Kim inherits from his father not material wealth but three crucial things: (1) the prophecy of the Red Bull, (2) his British identity papers (the "ne varietur" parchment), and (3) an Irish charm and adaptability that serves Kim throughout his adventures. The description of Kimball O'Hara Sr. as "so filled with virtue" jiggeting down to Umballa (009-142) hints at the spirited nature Kim inherited.
