## Location and Route

Bushahr is a Himalayan hill state located along the eastern route that Hurree Babu traces for the Russian spies' movements. In his explanation to Kim, Hurree sketches on the ground how the spies should have entered via Srinagar or Abbottabad but, having "made mischief in the West," are now marching eastward—down through Leh, along the Indus to Hanlé, and then into Bushahr and the Chini valley. (013-024)

## Narrative Context

Bushahr serves as a geographic marker in the Great Game's mountain theater. Its valleys provide the setting for the final stages of the pursuit, where Hurree Babu expects to intercept the Russian agents. The region is part of the network of hill states through which spies, pilgrims, and traders move, each navigating the difficult terrain for their own purposes. (013-024)

## Poetic Reference

Chapter 15 opens with a reference to "the valleys of Bushahr," from whose heights eagles survey the landscape below—an image that captures the vast scale and strategic vantage points of this Himalayan territory. (015-004)

The opening of Chapter 15 evokes Bushahr's dramatic landscape:

> Up the valleys of Bushahr---the far-beholding eagles of the Himalayas swerve at his new blue-and-white gored umbrella---hurries a Bengali, once fat and well-looking, now lean and weather-worn.
>
> (015-004)
