## Identity

Fostum Sahib is a British sportsman and hunter known among the hill communities of the Himalayas. He is regularly mentioned alongside Yankling Sahib as one of the established British sahibs who employ local *shikarris* (hunting guides and trackers) and travel with proper retinues of servants—cooks, bearers, and orderlies. The hillmen speak of him with familiarity born of long service. (013-127) (013-145)

## Reputation Among Hillmen

The hillmen use Fostum Sahib and Yankling Sahib as benchmarks for what a "true Sahib" should be:

> "Oh, but these are not Angrezi Sahibs — not merry-minded men like Fostum Sahib or Yankling Sahib. They are foreigners — they cannot speak Angrezi as do Sahibs."
>
> (013-119)

This contrasts sharply with the Russian spies, who travel without servants and therefore are judged "Sahibs of no account" by the hillfolk. (013-119) (013-145)

The man from Ao-chung, who serves as both Fostum Sahib's and Yankling Sahib's *shikarri*, invokes their reputations to reassure his fellow coolies that no serious consequences will follow from their flight—true Sahibs like Fostum Sahib would laugh at the affair rather than send police into the hills. (013-142)
