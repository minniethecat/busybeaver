## Overview

Fook Shing was a Chinese bootmaker who kept a shop in the bazar of Lahore. He was one of the diverse cast of characters who populated Kim's childhood world. (001-015)

## Role in Kim's Story

When Kim first saw the Teshoo Lama shuffling round the corner from the Motee Bazar, the lama's yellow, wrinkled face reminded Kim of Fook Shing, the Chinese bootmaker. This comparison helped Kim place the stranger — though the lama was clearly no Chinaman, as Kim's playmate Abdullah noted. Fook Shing had once chased Abdullah out of his shop for spitting at the joss (a Chinese religious figure) above the boots, suggesting he maintained his cultural and religious identity amid the multicultural bazar. (001-015)(001-032)

The lama's appearance immediately recalls Fook Shing to Kim:

> His face was yellow and wrinkled, like that of Fook Shing, the Chinese bootmaker in the bazar. His eyes turned up at the corners and looked like little slits of onyx.
>
> (001-015)
