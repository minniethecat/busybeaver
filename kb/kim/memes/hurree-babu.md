# Hurree Babu

**Hurree Chunder Mookerjee**, known as **Hurree Babu**, is the Bengali intelligence agent whose comic persona — a fussy, verbose, slightly cowardly clerk — conceals his [[identity-and-disguise|identity]] as one of the most effective operatives in the [[the-great-game|Great Game]]. His character embodies the novel's central insight that in the world of espionage, nothing and no one is what they appear to be.

## Background

Hurree Babu is a high-caste Bengali Brahmin who has received a western education, making him a product of the colonial encounter. He works for the British [[the-chain-men|intelligence network]] while maintaining the outward appearance of a harmless, slightly ridiculous [[the-babu|Bengali clerk]] — a stereotype he deliberately performs to disarm suspicion.

## Role in the Narrative

Hurree Babu appears in the novel's final section, where he plays a crucial role in the operation against Russian agents in the Himalayas. His cover as a guide and translator for the Russian spies allows him to gather intelligence while appearing to serve them. His performance of obsequious incompetence is so convincing that the Russians never suspect him until it is too late.

The character subverts colonial stereotypes. While Europeans in the novel dismiss him as a typical Bengali babu — weak, effeminate, un[[loyalty-and-betrayal|trust]]worthy — he is in fact brave, resourceful, and utterly loyal to the [[the-chain-men|intelligence network]]. His success demonstrates that the [[the-great-game|Great Game]] depends on talents that colonial ideology refuses to recognize (012-143).

## Identity and Disguise

Hurree Babu is the novel's supreme example of [[identity-and-disguise|disguise]] as a way of life. Unlike [[kimball-ohara|Kim]], who adopts [[identity-and-disguise|disguise]]s temporarily, Hurree Babu's entire public [[identity-and-disguise|identity]] is a performance. His "real" self — competent, brave, and intelligent — exists only in the hidden world of the [[the-great-game|Great Game]].
