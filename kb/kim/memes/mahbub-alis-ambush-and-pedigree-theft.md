## Background: The Great Game in Lahore

The pedigree theft occurs against the backdrop of the Great Game — the intelligence rivalry between Britain and Russia across Central Asia. Mahbub Ali, a horse-trader and British intelligence operative, operates from his base near the Lahore railway station (001-163). Kim, the street boy who has known Mahbub for years (001-164), is present when the ambush unfolds.

## The Ambush and Theft

Enemy agents drug Mahbub Ali and steal the secret pedigree of the white stallion — a coded intelligence document disguised as a horse genealogy (001-197). The pedigree, which Mahbub had been translating and which was urgently demanded from Umballa (001-203), contains sensitive military intelligence about troop movements and brigade warnings.

Kim witnesses the aftermath and, though only a boy, understands the gravity of the situation. Mahbub Ali, trusting Kim's street-smart instincts, entrusts him with a critical task: to carry a secret message to a British officer at Umballa, bypassing official channels.

> The pedigree of the white stallion was not yet fully translated...
>
> (001-197)

## Kim's First Covert Mission

Mahbub sends Kim to deliver the message, launching the boy's first covert operation (001-184). The message warns Colonel Creighton of a security breach and carries coded intelligence. The incident at Umballa (002-133, 002-143) reveals that the pedigree contained full military details — a disaster for British intelligence.

Creighton later remarks on the significance: a horse's pedigree had been worth a dangerous journey across India (002-169).

## Significance in the Narrative

This event serves as the inciting incident of the spy plot in *Kim*. It: (a) demonstrates Mahbub Ali's dual identity as horse-trader and intelligence agent, (b) introduces Kim to the world of espionage, and (c) establishes the coded-pedigree device as a hallmark of Great Game tradecraft. The event also foreshadows Kim's later recruitment by Colonel Creighton (Chapter 7-8).
