## A Destination for Pilgrims

Prayag — known to the British as Allahabad — is mentioned as a pilgrimage destination of great power. The village priest tells of a *faquir* to whom "Krishna Himself appeared in a vision promising him Paradise without the burning-pyre if he journeyed to Prayag." (003-045) This faquir had "right and reason" (003-044) for his journey, unlike the lama whose destination — a river no one knows — seems mad by comparison.

## The Fifth Pilgrimage

In Chapter 11, a character declares "I go to Prayag [Allahabad] for the fifth time" (011-012), indicating the city's enduring importance as a pilgrimage centre. The repeated journeys suggest a cycle of devotion that contrasts with the lama's single-minded quest for the River of the Arrow.

## Key Passages

> "Ay, but that man had right and reason, for Krishna Himself appeared in a vision promising him Paradise without the burning-pyre if he journeyed to Prayag. This man seeks no God who is within my knowledge."
