## A Junction on the Grand Trunk

Mian Mir is the first station beyond Lahore on the railway line to Umballa. When Kim buys the lama's ticket, a sleepy clerk tries to issue one only "to the next station, just six miles distant" — Mian Mir. (002-011) Kim, savvy to railway tricks, rejects it: "This may serve for farmers, but I live in the city of Lahore. It was cleverly done, Babu. Now give the ticket to Umballa." (002-012)

## "Flung Thee Out at Mian Mir"

Later, when the half-caste guard discovers Kim's ticket is only for Amritzar and tries to put him off the train, Kim reflects: "Never did yogi need chela as thou dost. They would have flung thee out at Mian Mir but for me." (002-014) The station thus marks the first obstacle in the lama's journey — a place where, without Kim's street-smart intervention, the pilgrimage might have ended before it truly began.

Kim saves the lama from being ejected at the first station:

> "Never did yogi need chela as thou dost," he went on merrily to the bewildered lama. "They would have flung thee out at Mian Mir but for me. This way! Come!"
>
> (002-014)
