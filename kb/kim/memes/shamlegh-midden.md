## Location

Shamlegh-midden lies two thousand feet below the village of Shamlegh-under-the-Snow, at the base of a sheer cliff. The terrain is so precipitous that "never yet man has set foot" there, making it the perfect disposal site for items the hillfolk wish to conceal permanently. (013-136) (014-004)

## Narrative Function

The midden plays a crucial role in the disposal of the Russian spies' equipment in Chapter 13. After the confrontation in which the lama is struck, the hillfolk plan to divide the spies' baggage at Shamlegh and throw whatever they do not need "on Shamlegh-midden, where no man has yet set foot." The Ao-chung man uses the midden's remoteness to argue that no evidence will remain of their encounter with the foreigners. (013-136)

In Chapter 14, Shamlegh-midden receives the surveying instruments, theodolites, compasses, and other incriminating equipment that Kim hurls from the window of the hut. The items vanish into the abyss without a sound reaching Kim's ears, symbolizing the permanent disappearance of the Russian agents' material legacy. (014-004)

## In the Text

The hillmen's plan to dispose of the Russian spies' equipment in the inaccessible gorge is explained by the man from Ao-chung:

> "Who is to tell him? Those Sahibs, who cannot speak our talk, or the Babu, who for his own ends gave us money? Will he lead an army against us? What evidence will remain? That we do not need we shall throw on Shamlegh-midden, where no man has yet set foot."
>
> (013-136)
