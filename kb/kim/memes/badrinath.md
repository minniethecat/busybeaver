## Geographic Significance

Badrinath is one of the great Himalayan peaks that crown the skyline on the northern mountain route. Together with its companion peak Kedarnath, it is described as one of the "kings of that wilderness"—a colossal, snow-covered presence that dominates the horizon. The two peaks are among the first features to catch the morning sunlight and the last to relinquish it at dusk. (013-009)

## In the Narrative

Throughout the mountain journey in Chapter 13, Kedarnath and Badrinath serve as fixed reference points against which the travelers measure their progress. Despite days of arduous marching, the two peaks remain stubbornly unchanged in outline, their massive scale dwarfing human effort. Kim observes only the slightest alteration in their profile after extensive travel, underlining the immensity of the Himalayan landscape. (013-027)
