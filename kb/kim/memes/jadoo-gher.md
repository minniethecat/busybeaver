## Overview

The Jadoo-Gher, or "Magic House" as it was known to the native population, was the Masonic Lodge in Lahore — a big blue-and-white building behind the Lahore Museum. To Kim and his father, it was a place of great magical significance, the source of papers that held Kim's entire future. (001-003)

## Kim's Father's Connection

Kimball O'Hara Sr., the former colour-sergeant of the Mavericks, possessed three papers: one he called his "ne varietur" for those words were written below his signature, another his clearance-certificate, and the third was Kim's birth-certificate. In his opium-dreams, O'Hara believed these papers — obtained from the Jadoo-Gher — would make little Kimball a man. He prophesied that Kim's horn would one day be exalted between "monstrous pillars of beauty and strength" — a Masonic image. (001-003)

## The Papers' Legacy

After O'Hara's death, the woman who looked after Kim sewed the parchment, paper, and birth-certificate into a leather amulet-case which she strung round Kim's neck. These papers, years later, were recognised by Father Victor of the Mavericks regiment, confirming Kim's identity as the son of a former soldier and setting in motion his transition from street boy to Sahib-in-training. (001-003)(001-007)(005-103)

Kim's father spoke of the Magic House in his opium-dreams:

> Those things, he was used to say, in his glorious opium-hours, would yet make little Kimball a man. On no account was Kim to part with them, for they belonged to a great piece of magic---such magic as men practised over yonder behind the Museum, in the big blue-and-white Jadoo-Gher---the Magic House, as we name the Masonic Lodge.
>
> (001-003)
