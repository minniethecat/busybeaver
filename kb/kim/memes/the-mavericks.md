# The Mavericks

**The Mavericks** is the fictional Irish regiment of the British Army that serves as [[kimball-ohara|Kim]]'s hereditary military [[identity-and-disguise|identity]]. His father, [[kimball-ohara|Kimball O'Hara]] Sr., was a colour-sergeant in the regiment, and the regiment's symbol — a [[the-red-bull-prophecy|Red Bull on a green field]] — becomes the central prophetic image of [[kimball-ohara|Kim]]'s childhood and the key to his eventual recognition by the British establishment.

The regiment is described as "the finest Regiment in the world" (001-004) by [[kimball-ohara|Kim]]'s father in his opium dreams. Its [[illusion-and-reality|reality]], when [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] finally encounters it, is both more ordinary and more consequential than the myth.

## In *Kim*

The Mavericks first appear as a legend in [[kimball-ohara|Kim]]'s life. His father's dying prophecy — that "nine hundred first-class devils, whose God was a [[the-red-bull-prophecy|Red Bull on a green field]], would attend to [[kimball-ohara|Kim]]" (001-004) — encodes the regiment's symbol into the boy's consciousness.

The prophecy is fulfilled when [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] encounters the regiment's camp near [[Umballa]]. He recognizes the Red Bull on the regimental flag, and [[the-red-bull-prophecy|the prophecy]] that has guided his childhood snaps into focus. A [[the-old-soldier|retired Maverick]] identifies him by his father's features and his birth certificate, confirming his [[identity-and-disguise|identity]].

The regiment's officers take responsibility for [[kimball-ohara|Kim]], arranging his education at [[St Xavier's School]] and providing the financial support that makes his transformation possible. The regiment is both [[kimball-ohara|Kim]]'s past — his father's unit — and his future, transformed: the military discipline and institutional backing it provides become the foundation for his intelligence career.

The Mavericks' camp near [[umballa|Umballa]] is described as a bustling military installation preparing for deployment to [[mahbub-ali|the Afghan]] frontier. Its atmosphere — the pipes, the drill, the regimental pride — is the first sustained portrait of British military life that [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] encounters, and it both attracts and repels him.

## The Red Bull Prophecy

The regiment's insignia — a [[the-red-bull-prophecy|Red Bull on a green field]] — is the novel's central prophetic image. [[kimball-ohara|Kim]]'s father spoke of it on his deathbed. The woman who raised [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] repeated it. And [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] himself, raised in the bazaars of [[lahore|Lahore]], carried [[the-red-bull-prophecy|the prophecy]] like an amulet, never quite believing it but never quite forgetting it.

The prophecy's fulfillment is one of the novel's most satisfying moments. [[kimball-ohara|Kim]], wandering near the Mavericks' camp, sees the flag and understands. The supernatural frame that has surrounded [[the-red-bull-prophecy|the prophecy]] dissolves; what remains is simple institutional [[identity-and-disguise|identity]] — a regiment recognizing one of its own.

The regimental flag reveals the prophecy fulfilled:

> It was no more than an ordinary camp marking-flag; but the regiment, always punctilious in matters of millinery, had charged it with the regimental device, the Red Bull, which is the crest of the Mavericks---the great Red Bull on a background of Irish green.
>
> (005-031)
