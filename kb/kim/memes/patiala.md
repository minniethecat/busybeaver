## Location

Patiala is a princely state and city in the Punjab region of northern India. In the novel, it serves as the setting for one of Kim's unsupervised adventures during his holiday from [[st-xaviers-school|St. Xavier's School]].

## The Fireworks Feast

During his three-month wandering holiday after leaving St. Xavier's, Kim travels to Patiala in the company of a firework-maker to attend a great feast. The event turns disastrous:

> I heard of a great feast forward in Patiala, and thither went I in the company of a firework-maker. It was a great feast. I saw Rajahs, and elephants with gold and silver trappings; and they lit all the fireworks at once, whereby eleven men were killed, my fire-work-maker among them, and I was blown across a tent but took no harm.
>
> (007-133)

Kim recounts this tale to [[mahbub-ali|Mahbub Ali]], who responds with approving "*Shabash!*" (well done). The incident is a vivid example of the casual violence and resilience that characterise Kim's life on the Road.

## Later Reflection

In Chapter 9, Kim reflects on his adventures with a mixture of pride and secrecy. Among his memories is the fact of having been "blown half across the forecourt of a Patiala palace by an explosion of fireworks" (009-141) — an experience that sets him apart from the sheltered boys of St. Xavier's, but which he cannot share openly, bound as he is by the discretion required of a budding intelligence agent.
