# Bandakui

**Bandakui** (modern Bandikui) is a small railway town in Rajasthan, located at a junction on the line between Delhi and Agra. In Kipling's *[[kimball-ohara|Kim]]*, it appears briefly in the Great Game narrative as a safe house for the spy network.

## In *Kim*

E23, the fleeing spy, recounts passing through Bandakui during his desperate journey north. He explains that he "broke through at Bandakui, where I heard there was a charge against me of murder in the city I had left — of the murder of a boy" (011-148). The town is also home to one of the Great Game operatives: "At Bandakui, where lives one of Us, I thought to slip the scent by changing my face, and so made me a Mahratta" (011-150). This safe house allows E23 to alter his appearance and continue his flight, though the respite is temporary.

## Historical Context

Bandikui, in the late 19th century, was a growing railway junction town in the princely state of Jaipur (part of Rajputana). The railway line connecting Delhi to Agra via Bandikui was a vital transport artery, and small junction towns like Bandakui served as convenient meeting points and safe houses for intelligence operatives during the Great Game period.
