## A Village God

When the lama asks his fellow travellers on the train about sacred rivers, the Jat cultivator deflects: "For me, a stream that leaves good silt on my land suffices, and I thank Bhumia, the God of the Homestead." (002-097) Unlike Gunga or the River of the Arrow, Bhumia represents the intimate, local sacred — a deity concerned not with cosmic liberation but with crops, home, and daily welfare.
