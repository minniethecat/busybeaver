## Identity and Role

E23 is a field agent in [[the-great-game|the Great Game]], identified only by his code designation. He is described as a Mahratta from North-West India, trained by [[lurgan-sahib|Lurgan Sahib]] in the arts of disguise and covert communication. (011-196) Unlike Kim, who is still learning, E23 is an experienced operative—"by virtue of his business, was no hollow man." (011-196)

## Kim's Rescue on the Train

E23 first appears in Chapter 11, feverish and exhausted, boarding a train while disguised as a Saddhu (Hindu holy man). He is being hunted by British police who have his description and are searching carriage by carriage. (012-009) Kim recognizes him as a fellow operative and helps conceal his identity.

When the police approach, E23 adopts the mannerisms of a religious mendicant, counting his beads "with a steady jerk of the wrist" while Kim creates a diversion by jeering at him. (012-012) The ruse succeeds—the English officer dismisses them as "a parcel of holy-bolies" and moves on. (012-013)

> "I have found my heart again," said E23, under cover of the platform's tumult. "Hunger and fear make men dazed, or I might have thought of this escape before. I was right. They come to hunt for me. Thou hast saved my head."
>
> (012-008)

## The Hidden Document

After evading capture, E23 reveals his urgent mission: he must send a wire regarding a document he hid before fleeing. (012-014) The document is concealed "under the Queen's Stone, at Chitor, in the place known to us." (011-145) This secret communication point is part of the Great Game's infrastructure—spies leave and retrieve messages at pre-arranged dead drops.

E23 had buried the document while under pursuit, and now that Kim has bought him time, he can report its location to his superiors. He makes contact with [[strickland-sahib|Strickland Sahib]], another operative who helps him reach safety. (012-032)

## Disguise and Tradecraft

E23 demonstrates the sophisticated disguise techniques taught by Lurgan Sahib. He transforms himself from a feverish fugitive into a convincing Saddhu, complete with religious paraphernalia. When the District Superintendent of Police confronts him directly, E23 "not moving a muscle of his countenance, answered with a stream of the filthiest abuse" in character—a performance so convincing that Kim is delighted by its audacity. (012-021)

His choice of disguise is strategic: a Saddhu commands a measure of religious respect and can travel freely without suspicion. When the police officer orders him back to his carriage, E23 withdraws "deferentially and dropping his voice," maintaining his cover while cursing the officer with elaborate oaths. (012-023)

## Significance in the Great Game

E23 represents the network of Indian agents who form the backbone of British intelligence in the novel. His code-name follows the system that also produced Kim's own eventual designation. The fact that Kim—still a novice—can successfully aid a more experienced operative marks an important step in Kim's development as a player in the Game. "I am only a beginner at the Game, that is sure," Kim reflects afterward. "I could not have leaped into safety as did the Saddhu." (012-034)

E23's gratitude to Kim is genuine: "Thou hast saved my head," he tells him, and promises that they "may work together at the Game yet." (012-008) (012-032) His escape is confirmed by a wire reporting "the arrival of E23 at Delhi." (012-043)
