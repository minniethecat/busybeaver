## Location

Akrola of the Ford is one of the villages along the Grand Trunk Road route through the Indian plains. It lies between Aminabad and Sahaigunge, near the smaller settlement of Phulesa. The "Ford" in its name suggests a river crossing, making it a natural waypoint for travelers on the road. (012-050) (012-065)

## In the Narrative

Akrola appears in Chapter 12 as part of the journey route along the Grand Trunk Road, where Kim and the lama travel through the blooming plains:

> So they travelled very easily across and among the broad bloomful fruit-gardens — by way of Aminabad, Sahaigunge, Akrola of the Ford, and little Phulesa — the line of the Siwaliks always to the north, and behind them again the snows.
>
> (012-050)

The village also serves as a point in Hurree Babu's intelligence network. Disguised as a doctor, he visits Akrola of the Ford to gather news, discovering that "all the common people know what you do" — the lama and Kim's journey having become common knowledge for fifty miles around. (012-133) Kim himself later recalls "that girl at Akrola of the Ford" (014-066) when reflecting on the complications of growing up.
