# Huneefa

**Huneefa** is a blind woman of low caste who appears briefly but memorably in *[[kimball-ohara|Kim]]*, applying dyes and charms to transform [[kimball-ohara|Kim]]'s appearance for his mission into the northern hills. She represents the hidden world of folk knowledge and marginal figures whose services are essential to the [[the-great-game|Great Game]].

## Background

Huneefa is a practitioner of folk magic and herbal knowledge, operating at the margins of respectable Indian society. Her blindness and low caste would normally exclude her from any role in official affairs, but the [[the-great-game|Great Game]] operates precisely by exploiting those whom official society ignores.

## Role in the Narrative

When [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] must be transformed for his dangerous mission into the hills, [[mahbub-ali|Mahbub Ali]] brings him to Huneefa. She applies dyes that darken his skin, charms that protect him from harm, and the finishing touches to his [[identity-and-disguise|disguise]] as a native boy. The scene is strange and disorienting — [[kimball-ohara|Kim]], drugged and half-conscious, undergoes a ritual transformation that blurs the line between [[identity-and-disguise|disguise]] and actual [[identity-and-disguise|identity]] change.

Huneefa's role, though small in page count, is symbolically important. She represents the uncanny, liminal dimension of the [[the-great-game|Great Game]] — the point at which espionage touches the occult, and rational intelligence work intersects with folk belief and magic.
