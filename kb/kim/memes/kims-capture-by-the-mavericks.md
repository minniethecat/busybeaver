# Kim's Capture by the Mavericks

While travelling with the lama toward Umballa, Kim stumbles into the camp of the Mavericks — his father's old Irish regiment. The chaplains Father Victor and Bennett find the boy's amulet containing his birth certificate and his father's military papers. In a single moment, the prophecy of the Red Bull is fulfilled, and Kim's life of freedom on the Lahore streets comes to an abrupt end. He is claimed by the regiment, identified as the son of Kimball O'Hara, and set on a path toward formal education and the Great Game.

## Context

Years before, Kim's Irish father — Kimball O'Hara, a colour-sergeant of the Mavericks — had died in poverty, leaving his infant son three papers: a birth certificate, a discharge certificate, and a Masonic "ne varietur." He had prophesied that the Red Bull on a green field — the regimental crest of the Mavericks — would one day come for Kim (001-004). The half-caste woman who raised Kim sewed the papers into a leather amulet and hung it round the boy's neck.

Kim grew up a street boy in Lahore, speaking the vernacular, burned "black as any native" (001-003). He knew nothing of white society. But he wore the amulet, half-believing in its magic. He had told the lama of the prophecy as they walked the Grand Trunk Road: "A Red Bull on a green field, was it?" the lama had asked, listening with tolerant curiosity (002-121). Neither knew how soon the words would become truth.

## The Event

### Into the camp

As Kim and the lama followed the road near Umballa, the band of the Mavericks marched past playing the regiment to camp (005-047). Kim, drawn by the spectacle, crept among the tents. He saw the regimental flag — a Red Bull on a green field — and understood. The prophecy was before him.

The Reverend Arthur Bennett, the Church of England chaplain, discovered the boy lurking in the dark near the Mess tent. Suspicious of a native thief, he seized him by the neck (005-066). Kim's hand went instinctively to the amulet round his throat.

### The amulet opened

Bennett brought Kim to Father Victor, the Catholic chaplain of the Irish soldiers. Together they examined the boy. Father Victor, a gentler man, unstrung Kim's amulet and unfolded the papers within (005-078):

> "Do you know what these things are? They are Kimball O'Hara's clearance-certificate, his _ne varietur_, and — most important of all — Kimball O'Hara's son's birth-certificate. And so, Bennett, this boy is not a beggar's brat but the son of a Maverick" (005-080).

Bennett, who had been ready to have Kim flogged, was stunned. Father Victor, with Irish resourcefulness, saw Providence at work:

> "Ye believe in Providence, Bennett? Here is Kimball O'Hara's son — and the Mavericks are his father's regiment. This is not chance" (005-082).

### Identity revealed

Kim, who had avoided white men all his life, found himself stripped of his native disguise. They scrubbed him, dressed him in European clothes, and questioned him. He told them of Lahore, of Mahbub Ali, of the lama — and Bennett recoiled at every revelation. But Kim kept one secret: the coded message he had carried to Umballa. He understood, even then, that some loyalties were deeper than blood.

Father Victor sent word to the regimental Lodge. The Masons — Kimball O'Hara's brothers — confirmed the papers. Kim was a Maverick's son, and the regiment would claim him.

## Consequences

- **Kim's identity is formalized.** For the first time, he is recognized as a white boy, and his mixed life — street boy, spy-runner, chela — collides with the British military world.

- **The prophecy is fulfilled.** The Red Bull on a green field has come for Kim, exactly as his father foretold. Yet the fulfillment brings Kim no joy; it separates him from the lama and the freedom of the Road (006-002).

- **The Great Game advances.** Mahbub Ali learns of Kim's capture and, recognizing the value of a boy with Kim's skills inside the military system, arranges for him to be sent to St. Xavier's at Lucknow — a formal education that will make him an even more valuable agent (005-187).

- **The lama is bereft.** The holy man loses his chela to the strange white men. He withdraws to a temple, waiting, trusting that the Wheel of Things will return Kim to him.
