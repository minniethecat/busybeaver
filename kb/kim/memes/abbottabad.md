Abbottabad is a British garrison town in the Hazara region of the North-West Frontier (present-day Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan). In *Kim*, it appears as a waypoint in the Himalayan intelligence network, mentioned by Hurree Babu as one of the routes by which foreign agents might enter the hills: "They would have come in by Srinagar or Abbottabad. That is their short road." (013-024)

## Strategic Position

Founded in 1853 by Major James Abbott, the town served as a British military cantonment and administrative center. Its position at the foot of the Himalayas made it a natural gateway between the Punjab plains and the mountain passes — a geography that the Great Game's operatives, both British and Russian, understood well.

## In the Great Game

Hurree Babu's mention of Abbottabad alongside Srinagar places it within the surveillance network that British intelligence maintained along the Himalayan frontier. The "short road" through Abbottabad represents one of several routes by which Russian agents could penetrate the mountain kingdoms, making the town a point of strategic interest in the contest for Central Asian influence.
