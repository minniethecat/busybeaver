# Father Victor

**Father Victor** is the Catholic chaplain of [[the-mavericks|the Mavericks regiment]] who, together with the Anglican chaplain [[mr-bennett|Mr. Bennett]], identifies [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] as a white boy and initiates the process that sends him to [[st-xaviers-school|St. Xavier's School]]. He represents one face of British colonial presence: the religious establishment that accompanied the military.

## Background

Father Victor serves as chaplain to [[the-mavericks|the Mavericks]], the regiment to which [[kimball-ohara|Kim]]'s father had belonged. His role places him at the intersection of military, religious, and educational institutions — all pillars of the colonial establishment.

## Role in the Narrative

When [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] is discovered in [[the-mavericks|the Mavericks]]' camp, having followed the [[the-red-bull-prophecy|Red Bull prophecy]] to its fulfillment (005-020), Father Victor and [[mr-bennett|Mr. Bennett]] interrogate the boy and uncover his [[identity-and-disguise|identity]] as the son of [[kimball-ohara|Kimball O'Hara]] Sr. It is Father Victor who recognizes [[kimball-ohara|Kim]]'s Masonic certificate and who advocates for sending the boy to school rather than to the orphanage.

Father Victor's treatment of [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] is well-meaning but culturally blind. He sees [[kimball-ohara|Kim]] as a white child who must be "saved" from going native, unable to recognize that [[kimball-ohara|Kim]]'s Indian upbringing is not a misfortune but the source of his strength. His paternalism reflects the broader colonial attitude toward those who crossed racial boundaries.
