## A Frontier City of the Great Game

Peshawur is described as an "insalubrious city" on India's North-West frontier that serves as a critical waypoint in the Great Game. (001-202) Kim notes that "the news goes to Pindi and Peshawur" when relaying Mahbub Ali's intelligence about a coming military expedition. (002-169) From the frontier garrisons, "from Pindi and Peshawur they will be drawn" — thousands of soldiers for the punitive expedition. (003-071)

## Strategic Importance

The city lies at the crossroads of espionage, arms dealing, and military operations. A Hindu banker in Peshawur features in Mahbub Ali's intelligence network. (001-203) The city's reputation extends to gun-running and the tools of the spy trade — Kim remarks that "as if every girl in Peshawur did not use it!" when referring to a disguise technique. (001-222) In the hills, an agent complains that "this is as bad as Peshawur!" (008-107)

## In *Kim*

Peshawur represents the shadowy frontier where espionage and commerce blur. The city's name recurs as a shorthand for the military-logistical heart of the Great Game. Creighton Sahib's intelligence operations draw troops from the Peshawar brigades for the coming punitive expedition. (002-157) A gold-embroidered Peshawur turban-cap appears among Lurgan Sahib's shop treasures, hinting at the city's craft traditions. (010-019)

> Therefore Mahbub had avoided halting at the insalubrious city of Peshawur, and had come through without stop to Lahore, where, knowing his country-people, he anticipated curious developments.
>
> (001-202)
