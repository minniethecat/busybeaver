Dupont Sahib is a minor figure mentioned in the latter chapters of *Kim*, a European hunter in the Ladakh region. The Russian agents, constructing their cover story, reference him: "There was a wild ibex, out Ladakh-way, that Dupont Sahib missed on a shoulder-shot." (013-129)

## Role in the Narrative

Dupont Sahib never appears directly in the novel. He exists only as a name invoked by other characters — a detail in the elaborate fabric of rumor and report that constitutes the intelligence world of the Great Game. His mention serves to authenticate the Russian agents' pose as sportsmen and travelers, part of the texture of European presence in the Himalayan frontier.
