## Overview

The Church of England appears in *Kim* as an institutional presence within the British military establishment in India. Its primary representative is the Reverend Arthur Bennett, the Anglican chaplain of the Mavericks regiment. Kipling portrays the Anglican Church as part of the formal, institutional fabric of British rule — present, dutiful, but somewhat removed from the practical realities of Indian life. (005-052)

## Bennett and the Anglican Presence

The Reverend Arthur Bennett serves as the Church of England Chaplain, living by the regiment's rhythms — he "always left Mess after that particular hour" — and carrying out his pastoral duties with institutional correctness. When Kim is discovered in the camp, it is Bennett who first finds him, and Bennett who summons Father Victor for counsel. Kipling notes a characteristic dynamic:

> Between himself and the Roman Catholic Chaplain of the Irish contingent lay, as Bennett believed, an unbridgeable gulf, but it was noticeable that whenever the Church of England dealt with a human problem she was very likely to call in the Church of Rome. Bennett's official abhorrence of the Scarlet Woman and all her ways was only equalled by his private respect for Father Victor.
>
> (005-074)

Thus "whenever the Church of England dealt with a human problem she was likely to call in the Church of Rome" — a wry observation on the practical division of spiritual labour in the regiment. (005-052)(005-064)(005-074)

## Anglican-Catholic Dynamic

The relationship between the Anglican and Catholic chaplains reflects the religious tensions within the British Army. Between Bennett and the Irish Catholic contingent lay what Bennett believed to be "an unbridgeable gulf." Yet the two chaplains cooperated effectively in practical matters, most notably in identifying Kim's parentage and arranging for his education. Bennett's Anglican propriety — he once cried "gross blasphemy" at something Father Victor found unremarkable — was balanced by Father Victor's pragmatic Catholicism. The Anglican chaplain in Lucknow later appears in the narrative when Kim's schooling arrangements are discussed. (005-074)(005-134)(006-081)
