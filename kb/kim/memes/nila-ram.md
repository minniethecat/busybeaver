## Overview

Nila Ram was a timber merchant in Lahore, the owner of a timber-yard situated beyond the Punjab High Court. His yard held fragrant deodar logs that had been driven down the Ravi River, seasoning in the open air. (001-007)

## Role in Kim's Story

Kim used Nila Ram's timber-yard as a secret storage place. There, under some baulks of wood, he kept a complete suit of Hindu kit — the costume of a low-caste street boy — given to him by one of the young men of fashion in Lahore. Before departing on his journey with the lama, Kim went to the timber-yard to retrieve his hidden disguise, digging it out from under the wood pile. This stash was part of Kim's elaborate system of identity-switching, allowing him to move between European and native worlds at will. (001-007)(001-137)

Kim keeps his disguise hidden in the timber-yard:

> Kim stored it in a secret place under some baulks in Nila Ram's timber-yard, beyond the Punjab High Court, where the fragrant deodar logs lie seasoning after they have driven down the Ravi.
