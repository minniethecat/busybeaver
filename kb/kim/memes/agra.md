# Agra

**Agra** is a historic city on the banks of the Yamuna River in northern India. In Kipling's *[[kimball-ohara|Kim]]*, it features briefly as a point of danger on the escape route of the spy E23 during the [[the-great-game|Great Game]] narrative.

## In *Kim*

Agra appears in the confession of E23, the fugitive spy whom Kim encounters on the train to Delhi. After hiding in Chitor, E23 "came to Agra, and would have turned back to Chitor to recover the letter" that he had buried (011-150). However, at Agra his cover is blown: "walking in the streets, a man cried a debt against me, and approaching with many witnesses, would hale me to the courts then and there" (011-152). The man recognizes E23 as his agent for cotton, but this is a ruse — E23 is the very man the enemy agents are hunting. Forced to flee through the Fleshers' Ward and out by the House of the Jew, E23 barely escapes the city.

## Historical Context

In the late 19th century, Agra was a major city in the United Provinces of British India and a key station on the railway line connecting Delhi to the south. The city was the capital of the Mughal Empire in the 16th and 17th centuries and home to the Taj Mahal. During the period of *Kim*, Agra's railway junction and its position on the Grand Trunk Road made it a natural transit point for travellers and spies alike.
