## Location

Ziglaur is a village situated in the Chini valley region of the Himalayan mountains, approximately twenty miles as the eagle flies and forty miles by the winding hill roads. It features a threshing-floor where travelers gather, and its position in the landscape makes it a natural staging point for journeys through the hills. (013-033)

## Role in the Narrative

Ziglaur serves as an important waypoint in Chapter 13. Hurree Babu uses the threshing-floor at Ziglaur as an observation post, sitting there with his binoculars to track the movements of the Russian spies across the valley. The lama and Kim also stop at Ziglaur during their mountain journey, taking shelter in a leaky hut while waiting for a storm to pass. It is in this hut that the lama first shows Kim the Wheel of Life—a painted chart that later becomes the flashpoint of the confrontation with the Russian agents. (013-033) (013-063)

## Communication Hub

The village functions as a node in the hill-country communication network. In Chapter 14, it is noted that news travels quickly through the mountains, and by midnight after the confrontation at Shamlegh, word had already reached Ziglaur—demonstrating the interconnected nature of the hill communities. (014-061)

The lama reveals the sacred painting to Kim at Ziglaur:

> "This is the Wheel of Life," said the lama, "the same I showed thee in the hut at Ziglaur when the rain fell."
>
> (013-063)
