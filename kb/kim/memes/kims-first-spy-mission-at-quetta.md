# Kim's First Spy Mission at Quetta

During the monsoon holidays, Kim traveled to Quetta with Mahbub Ali. There, he conducted his first independent field operation — copying the commissariat sergeant's ledger — a vital act that proved his readiness for intelligence work in the Great Game.

## Context

Kim had completed his education at St. Xavier's and his training under Lurgan Sahib. He had learned memory, disguise, and observation. Now he needed field experience.

Mahbub Ali, the Pathan horse-trader and secret agent, had "business at Quetta" (010-003). The city was a strategic outpost on the northwest frontier. "The Game is so large that one sees but a little at a time" (010-007).

## The Event

### The commissariat ledger

The commissariat was the military supply office. Its ledger recorded procurement and distribution of provisions, fodder, ammunition, and equipment — a portrait of British military readiness on the frontier.

Kim's task: access the commissariat, memorize the ledger, and report its contents. Nothing physical to steal — only intelligence. Using Lurgan's training in observation and memory, Kim copied the sergeant's ledger.

> "Kim, as Mahbub admitted, had business at Quetta" (010-003)

The work was humble but vital. Kim posed as a low-caste servant, drawing no attention while observing everything in the supply offices.

### Intelligence gathered

The ledger revealed supply routes, troop strengths, and provisioning schedules. For Mahbub and Creighton's network, this data helped calibrate operations and anticipate Russian moves along the frontier.

## Consequences

Kim's first solo operation proved his capability. The commissariat data filled a gap in the network's frontier intelligence. His transition from street boy to messenger to field agent was complete, and Mahbub's trust deepened — the Pathan now saw him as a fellow operative, not merely a child courier.
