## The Kangra Hills

Kangra is a hill district in what is now Himachal Pradesh, located in the western Himalayas. In *Kim*, it appears in close association with Kulu, both regions being identified as the homeland of the hillfolk who serve as the Kulu woman's escort on the Grand Trunk Road. Kim observes the composition of her retinue and concludes: "The hillmen would be of her own people — Kulu or Kangra folk." (004-053)

## A Landscape of Contrast

The Kulu woman, traveling south into the dusty Indian plains, speaks longingly of her mountain home, contrasting "the cool and the pines of the Kangra and Kulu hills with the dust and the mangoes of the South." (004-161) This juxtaposition of mountain freshness with plains heat captures the sensory geography that runs throughout the novel — the Hills versus the Plains as two distinct worlds.
