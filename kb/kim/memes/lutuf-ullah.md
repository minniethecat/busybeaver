## Role in the Narrative

Lutuf Ullah is Mahbub Ali's business partner in the horse-trading trade that serves as cover for British intelligence operations (001-162). He operates between Delhi and the northern frontier regions, including Kurdistan (001-162). Mahbub describes him as "a tall man with a broken nose" (008-117).

> Locked doors showed that the owner was away, and a few rude---sometimes very rude---chalk or paint scratches told where he had gone. Thus: "Lutuf Ullah is gone to Kurdistan." Below, in coarse verse: "O Allah, who sufferest lice to live on the coat of a Kabuli, why hast thou allowed this louse Lutuf to live so long?"
>
> (001-162)

## Appearances in the Novel

Lutuf Ullah is first mentioned when Mahbub Ali notes his partner's departure: "Lutuf Ullah is gone to Kurdistan" (001-162). This absence creates the opening for enemy agents to drug Mahbub and steal the pedigree, as Mahbub is operating without his usual partner's protection.

Later, when Mahbub Ali seeks news of his partner, he tells a contact: "I come to look for Lutuf Ullah. I have a truck-load up the line" (008-110). He describes Lutuf Ullah as "my partner" (008-112) and emphasizes his search: "I am here seeking for my partner, Lutuf Ullah" (008-114).

The coded telegram from the Delhi partner — "Lutuf Ullah. Have wired two thousand" (001-204) — is part of the intelligence communication chain involving the pedigree translation.

## Character Significance

Lutuf Ullah represents the commercial network that underpins the Great Game's intelligence operations. Horse-trading provides the cover for Mahbub Ali's spy work, and Lutuf Ullah's movements across the frontier enable the network to function. Though a minor character, his absence during the pedigree theft (Chapter 1-2) creates the vulnerability that enemy agents exploit.
