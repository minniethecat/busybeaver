# Kim's Great Game Recruitment

In the offices and bungalows of the British India administration, Colonel Creighton makes a decision that will shape Kim's future: the street boy who delivered Mahbub Ali's message and survived the Mavericks' discovery will be formally trained as a spy. Kim's recruitment into the Great Game is not marked by ceremony — it is a quiet, pragmatic transaction among men who recognize raw talent and know how to use it.

## Context

Kim had proven himself three times over: he carried Mahbub Ali's coded message to Umballa without reading it (001-197); he kept his nerve when Father Victor and Bennett opened his amulet (005-078); and he impressed even the chaplains with his quickness and loyalty. Mahbub Ali, reporting to Colonel Creighton, recommended the boy not for charity but for service (006-112).

Creighton — ethnologist, spymaster, and colonel — saw in Kim a rare combination: a white boy who could pass as native, who spoke the vernacular, who knew every alley of Lahore and every trick of the bazaar, and who was still young enough to be trained.

## The Event

The recruitment unfolded in stages. First, Creighton himself interviewed Kim in the Mavericks' camp. The colonel asked no direct questions about the Great Game; instead, he listened as Kim talked of the lama, of the Road, of Mahbub Ali. "I'm Creighton," he said simply, and Kim understood that this was the man who had received the message (006-120).

Shortly after, Mahbub Ali arranged for Kim to leave the regiment and be sent to St. Xavier's School at Lucknow — the finest education available for a boy of Kim's background. St. Xavier's was not merely a school; it was a finishing ground for native agents and the sons of railwaymen who might one day serve the Survey of India — Creighton's cover organization for intelligence work.

After his first term at St. Xavier's, Kim was sent in the holidays to Lurgan Sahib in Simla. There the true training began: the jewel game for memory, hypnosis resistance for psychological fortitude, and the crafts of disguise. Kim was no longer merely running errands — he was learning to be an operative.

## Consequences

- **Kim's path was set.** The recruitment formalized Kim's entry into the Great Game. He would spend the rest of the novel moving between his role as the lama's chela and his role as a spy.

- **A dual life began.** From this point, Kim lived in two worlds: the spiritual quest of the lama and the secret war of the intelligence services. The tension between these two callings would define his character and ultimately lead to his identity crisis.

- **The Great Game gained a unique asset.** As Hurree Chunder Mookerjee would later observe, Kim was "a little friend of all the world" — able to pass anywhere, trusted by all, suspected by none. Creighton's recruitment of Kim was one of the most consequential decisions of his career.

## Key Passages
