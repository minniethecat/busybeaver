## Definition

In Mill's qualitative hedonism, **competent judges** are persons who have experienced both higher and lower pleasures and are therefore qualified to determine which are superior.

Mill introduces this to answer the objection that utilitarianism reduces value to quantity alone [002-006].

## Key Arguments

- **The test of experience**: If those "competently acquainted with both" place one pleasure far above the other, that preference provides evidence for qualitative superiority [002-006].

- **Capacity and its vulnerability**: The capacity for nobler feelings "is in most natures a very tender plant, easily killed" by lack of opportunity or hostile social conditions [002-010].

- **The verdict of mankind**: No competent judge would consent to trade human capacities for the fullest animal pleasures, even at the cost of greater dissatisfaction.
