# The Stick

"He turned away and beat the heather with his stick" (008-038). The stick is an extension of the body — a tool for striking the landscape when speech fails. The gesture — turning away, beating the heather — is an act of displacement: the heather receives the blow meant for something or someone else. The stick is the novel's instrument of redirected violence.

## Physical Description

The stick is a walking stick — ordinary, wooden, unadorned. Its ordinariness conceals its function as an instrument of expression.

## Symbolic Meaning

The stick is the phallic object sublimated into gesture. It strikes not people but plants, not flesh but landscape. The novel's characters, constrained by the codes of the country house, express through objects what they cannot express through speech or action.
