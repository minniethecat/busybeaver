# The Whiskey

"Picus came over with the cup for Clarence to fill with whiskey and soda" (007-015). The whiskey circulates through the novel as the Grail's profane double — a liquid that, like the Grail's contents, transforms those who consume it. But where the Grail's transformation is sacred, whiskey's is social: it lubricates conversation, licenses confession, enables the lowering of defenses that the novel's psychological investigations require.

## Symbolic Meaning

Whiskey is the modern Eucharist — the shared drink that creates temporary community. The Gault House circle's gatherings are structured around its consumption. The drink, like the Grail, promises revelation; unlike the Grail, it delivers only the truth of lowered inhibitions.
