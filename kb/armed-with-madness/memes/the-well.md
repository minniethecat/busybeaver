# The Well

"A cup in a well: in a house" (025-041). The well is the cup's hiding place — or its home. The spatial sequence (cup → well → house) maps the Grail's location in nested containers: the house contains the well, the well contains the cup. The well is the threshold between surface and depth, domestic architecture and subterranean water. To find the cup, one must descend.

## Physical Description

The well is never described physically. It exists as a location — the place where the cup was — rather than as a constructed object. Its architectural features are irrelevant; its depth is its meaning.

## Symbolic Meaning

The well is the Grail castle's axis mundi — the vertical passage connecting the domestic world above to the water below. In Grail romance, the Grail is associated with water, wells, and subterranean sources. Butts's well is the modern iteration: a feature of a Dorset country house, not a magical fountain, but still the place where the sacred manifests.

> "the well was there before the house, before any house" (002-030)
