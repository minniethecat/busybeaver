# The Parsifal Score

"The doctor said: 'Parsifal is like a great doctor'" (006-022). Wagner's final opera (1882) — the most famous modern treatment of the Grail legend — enters the novel not as performance but as reference. The score exists somewhere (Bayreuth, a gramophone record, a memory of a performance), and its existence makes the Grail legend available to the Gault House characters in a specifically modern, mediated form: not the medieval romance but the nineteenth-century opera.

## Symbolic Meaning

Parsifal is the Grail legend's most influential modern iteration — the work that, for better or worse, shaped early twentieth-century understanding of the Grail. The doctor's reference to it, in the context of Picus's illness, suggests that the modern Grail quest must pass through Wagner: the medieval cannot be accessed directly but must be approached through the cultural forms that have transmitted it.
