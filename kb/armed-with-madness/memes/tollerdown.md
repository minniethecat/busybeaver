# Tollerdown

Tollerdown enters the novel as a destination and a question. "We really ought to go over to Tollerdown and see if the others have turned up" (002-006). The "others" — Picus, Clarence, the "men from Tollerdown" (003-033) — arrive from Tollerdown bearing the novel's plot. The place is a way station: characters pass through it on their way to Gault, establish temporary quarters there, and then are absorbed into the main house. Yet Tollerdown's very ordinariness — its name, with its overdetermined Englishness — is part of its function. It anchors the mythic events at Gault in a geography of cottages and turn-ups.

## Description

Butts gives Tollerdown no physical description beyond its name. "Where is Tollerdown?" (003-022): the question, asked by a newcomer, positions Tollerdown as a place you have to be told about — local knowledge, not on any map that matters. The "men from Tollerdown" (003-033) are defined by their origin; the place produces a type.

Clarence's cottage — "a cottage on a place called Tollerdown" (003-038) — is the only specific location mentioned. The cottage, like its occupant, is functional rather than aesthetic: a place of work and sleep, not of the Freud game or Grail questing.

> "The men from Tollerdown, of course." (003-033)

## Narrative Function

### Events at Tollerdown

- **The arrivals**: Picus and Clarence first come to Tollerdown, making it the point of entry into the novel's geography. "They went over to Tollerdown, and found our friends had come" (003-021).
- **Clarence's cottage**: "A cottage on a place called Tollerdown" (003-038). The cottage is Clarence's domain — the working-class satellite to Gault's aristocratic center.
- **The men from Tollerdown**: "The men from Tollerdown, of course" (003-033) — the phrase is spoken with the casualness of the obvious, making Tollerdown a known quantity among the initiated.

### Symbolic Meaning

Tollerdown is Gault's satellite — close enough to be part of the mythic geography, far enough to be ordinary. Its function is to provide a baseline of ordinariness against which Gault's strangeness can be measured. The cottage, the turn-ups, the "men" — these are the novel's reminders that even in a Grail quest, someone has to do the washing up.

## Character Associations

- **Clarence**: His cottage is on Tollerdown. The place is his headquarters, his retreat from Gault House when the mythic intensity becomes unsustainable.
- **Picus**: He arrives via Tollerdown, using it as a staging ground before his entrance into Gault.
- **Ross**: A local who knows Tollerdown as he knows all the Dorset geography.
