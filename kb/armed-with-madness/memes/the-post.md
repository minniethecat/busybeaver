# The Post

"Along a river which carried the London post. Too slowly. It must get there" (005-019). The post is the novel's circulatory system — the network that carries letters, records, news from London to Dorset, from Paris to Gault. Its slowness is its defining quality: "too slowly." The post is the modern world's imperfect penetration of the mythic space. Messages arrive, but late; letters come, but after the crisis they address has already transformed.

## Symbolic Meaning

The post is the anti-Grail communication network — profane, bureaucratic, unreliable where the Grail is sacred and immediate. Yet the novel depends on it: Lydia's letter travels by post, the records come by post, the outside world reaches Gault by post. The sacred quest is sustained by profane infrastructure.
