# Zeus

Zeus enters *Armed with Madness* through a single repeated line: "Here lies the Woodpecker who was Zeus" (006-024, 006-034, 006-044). The refrain, chanted by Picus in an early scene, is the novel's most concentrated mythological statement. It identifies Picus — whose name is that of the Roman woodpecker-god Picus Martius — with Zeus, the king of the Greek pantheon, and declares that identification a matter of the past tense: "who was." The woodpecker lies buried; the god he was is gone.

## The Woodpecker-Zeus Identification

### Picus Martius

In Roman mythology, Picus was a king of Latium, a son of Saturn, who was transformed into a woodpecker by Circe when he rejected her advances. The woodpecker (picus) was sacred to Mars, hence "Picus Martius" — the martial woodpecker, an oracular bird whose drumming was read as augury.

### The Butts Synthesis

Butts fuses the Roman Picus with the Greek Zeus, creating a composite figure — "Woodpecker-Zeus" (011-042) — who is at once king of gods and oracular bird, sovereign and scavenger. "He was Zeus" (011-027): the past tense is definitive. The novel's Picus is what remains when the god has departed, leaving only the bird — the drumming, the augury, the unconsummated desire.

### Scylla's Address

"Woodpecker-Zeus," she said, "leave your skeleton alone." (011-042). Scylla's direct address to Picus as "Woodpecker-Zeus" is the novel's most explicit mythological naming. The epithet is tender and mocking in equal measure — she names the god in him to mark his distance from godhood.

> "Here lies the Woodpecker who was Zeus." (006-024)

## Function in the Novel

Zeus functions in the novel not as a character but as an absent presence — the divine authority whose withdrawal makes the Grail quest necessary. Picus is the degraded Zeus, the god become bird, and his quest for the Grail is the attempt to recover what was lost in the transformation. The novel's theological argument, condensed into a single refrain, is that the divine is not absent but transformed — not dead but woodpecker.
