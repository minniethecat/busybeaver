# The Doctor's Visit

"The doctor having come over from Starn to attend to Picus's health" (006-001). The event is announced with clinical clarity — a doctor, a patient, a house call. But what unfolds is something else entirely. The doctor, faced with Picus's unclassifiable condition, reaches not for his medical bag but for opera: "Parsifal is like a great doctor" (006-022). The substitution of Wagner for diagnosis transforms the visit from a medical event into a mythic annunciation.

## Prelude

Picus has been ill since arriving at Gault — or claiming to be ill. "Picus saying he was ill and Clarence doing all the work" (004-006). The illness is at once real and performative, a somatic expression of whatever spiritual crisis the Grail quest is bringing to the surface. Scylla, or someone acting for her, summons the doctor from Starn — the nearest town, the hub of the district, the source of ordinary medical authority.

## The Event

### The Examination

The doctor examines Picus. What he finds is never stated in clinical terms. Instead, Carston holds the doctor's attention, "improvising" (006-001) to keep the visit from ending too quickly — as if the examination needs to be prolonged for the mythic content to emerge.

### The Diagnosis

"The doctor said: 'Parsifal is like a great doctor'" (006-022). The diagnosis is not a diagnosis. By naming Wagner's Grail opera — the great nineteenth-century treatment of the legend — the doctor transforms himself from physician to herald. He has not identified Picus's illness; he has named the quest that might cure it.

### Carston's Improvisation

Carston's role in the scene is catalytic. By extending the visit through conversation, he creates the space in which the doctor's operatic reference can emerge. The American, the outsider, is the one who enables the English doctor to speak myth.

> "The doctor said: 'Parsival is like a great doctor.'" (006-022)

## Aftermath

- **The medical frame abandoned**: After the doctor's visit, Picus's illness is never again discussed in clinical terms. The Grail quest has absorbed the medical narrative.
- **Carston's role established**: Carston's improvisation during the visit marks him as the novel's investigator — the one who creates conditions for revelation.
- **Wagner enters the novel**: "Parsifal" becomes a touchstone, linking the Dorset country house to the European operatic tradition.

## Symbolic Reading

The doctor's visit is the novel's Annunciation scene. The herald (doctor) arrives from the ordinary world (Starn) to announce the extraordinary (Parsifal/Grail) to the quest's central figure (Picus). The annunciation is oblique — Wagner, not scripture — but the structure is medieval: a messenger, a message, a mission.
