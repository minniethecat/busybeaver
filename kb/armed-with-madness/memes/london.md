# London

London is mentioned in *Armed with Madness* more often than it is visited. It is a point of departure and a source of deliveries — "records he had brought them from London" (007-017), "the London post" (005-019) — rather than a setting for sustained action. This is itself significant: the novel's characters have left London behind, and the city figures in the narrative primarily as what they have escaped.

## Description

London is rendered through absence rather than presence. "Not come to London yet" (007-026): the negation is the city's narrative mode. London is where things have not yet arrived, where fashions have not yet reached, where the cutting edge is always elsewhere (Paris, New York). The city's distance from Gault is measured in postal delivery times — "a river which carried the London post. Too slowly" (005-019) — making London a function of communication lag rather than a place.

> "Straight from London, Paris, and New York, the silence was so positive it was like a noise." (003-009)

## Narrative Function

### Events Associated with London

- **Carston's origin**: Carston arrives at Gault "straight from London, Paris, and New York" (003-009). London is the last urban layer before the Dorset silence.
- **The records**: "Records he had brought them from London" (007-017). London is a source of cultural artifacts — music, objects — that the Gault House circle consumes and transforms.
- **The post**: The London post (005-019) carries letters, including Lydia's consequential missive. London is the relay station for the novel's epistolary plot machinery.

### Symbolic Meaning

London represents the metropolitan norm that Gault deviates from. If Paris is the city of aesthetic surfaces, London is the city of practical infrastructure — post, records, travel connections. Its symbolic function is to be left behind: the novel's values are forged in the movement away from London toward Dorset.

## Character Associations

- **Carston**: The American who passed through London on his way to Gault. London is one station in his trajectory from New World to Old.
- **Picus**: He brings records from London (007-017) — the city is a source of cultural capital he deploys at Gault.
- **The Gault House circle**: Collectively defined by their withdrawal from London. The city is where they no longer are.
