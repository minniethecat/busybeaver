# The Records

"He remembered the new records he had brought them from London, and went upstairs to fetch them" (007-017). The records are Picus's contribution to the Gault House gathering — cultural artifacts from the capital, objects that promise to fill the Dorset silence with music. Their function is at once social (entertainment for the house party) and symbolic (the transmission of urban culture to the rural centre).

## Physical Description

The records are never physically described. They are "new" — recent acquisitions — and they come from London. Their physical form matters less than their content and their origin.

## Symbolic Meaning

The records are objects of modernity — mass-produced, portable, carrying recorded sound across geographic distance. Their presence at Gault marks the penetration of the modern into the mythic space. The Grail castle has a gramophone.
