# The Gault Cliff Mica

"She looked up into the sky at Gault Cliff, where the mica glittered" (011-024). The mica — flakes of silicate mineral embedded in the cliff face — is the cliff's luminous particular. It catches light; it makes the stone shimmer; it transforms geology into vision. The mica is the material condition for Scylla's cliff revelation: without the glitter, she might not have looked; without looking, she might not have seen Picus in the rock.

## Symbolic Meaning

The mica represents the natural world's participation in the mythic. It is not supernatural; it is mineral. Yet its glitter — the play of light on stone — enables perception of the sacred in the ordinary. The Grail, the novel suggests, is like mica: a natural particular whose gleam, under the right conditions, reveals what the rock contains.
