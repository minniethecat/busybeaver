# The Star Inn Sign

"At the inn called the Star at Tambourne, plenty of regret for Nanna's going" (026-003). The inn sign — the Star — is the village's central marker, the object by which the community names its gathering place. The star is at once celestial and terrestrial: a heavenly body rendered in painted wood, hanging over a Dorset pub.

## Symbolic Meaning

The Star inn sign is the novel's most local sacred object — the marker of community identity that, like the Grail, gathers people around it. But where the Grail's meaning is cosmic, the Star's meaning is local: it signifies Tambourne, not the heavens.
