# Tracy's Book

"Tracy has a book up in his room. On somebody's——" (012-033). The sentence breaks off — "On somebody's——" — as if the book's provenance cannot be spoken. The dash is the novel's gesture toward forbidden or dangerous knowledge. The book is never opened, never quoted, never described. It exists as pure potential: the text that might explain everything, if only someone could read it.

## Physical Description

No physical description is given. The book is "old" — "one of those old books" (012-033) — and its age is its primary attribute. It is "up in his room," kept apart from the rest of Tracy's collection, suggesting special status.

## Symbolic Meaning

The book is the novel's unread text — the source that might authorize the Grail quest if it could be consulted. Its inaccessibility mirrors the Grail's ambiguity: the knowledge that would settle the question exists, but the novel cannot or will not access it.
