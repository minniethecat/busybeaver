# Europe

Europe is the largest frame of the novel's geography — the continent that contains England, France, and Russia, the civilization that produced both the Grail legend and the Great War. The characters' movement across Europe (London to Paris to Biarritz, Russia to Paris to Dorset) maps a continent in the aftermath of catastrophe, where the old myths persist but their meaning has become uncertain.

## Description

Europe is rendered through its cultural artifacts rather than its geography. The Grail legend (medieval Europe), psychoanalysis (Vienna, 1890s), the Champs-Élysées (modern Paris), the Russian emigration (post-Revolution) — these are Europe's contributions to the novel's intellectual furniture. The continent is less a place than a heritage, and the novel's question is whether that heritage is an inheritance or a burden.

## Narrative Function

Europe's function is to contain the novel's geographic and cultural diversity within a single frame. The movement from Russia (Boris) to France (Paris, Biarritz, Lydia) to England (Dorset, Gault) is a traversal of Europe's postwar condition — a condition the novel diagnoses through the Grail quest.
