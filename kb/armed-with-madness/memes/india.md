# India

India appears at the edge of *Armed with Madness* — a name, a reference, the imperial elsewhere that England's rural landscape both depends on and disavows. Its presence in the novel is minimal but significant: it marks the boundary of the English world, the place where England's claims to centrality encounter their geographical and moral limit.

## Description

India receives no physical description. It exists as a category — one of the places, like Russia and America, that defines England by contrast. Its colonial relationship to England is the unspoken context: the Dorset country house, with its leisure and its Grail questing, is sustained by an empire the novel rarely mentions.

## Narrative Function

India functions as the novel's periphery — the place that England's center cannot acknowledge without destabilizing its own mythic self-conception. Its minimal presence is the point: the Grail quest, for all its claims to universality, unfolds in a specifically English landscape sustained by imperial wealth.
