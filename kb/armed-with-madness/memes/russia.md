# Russia

Russia appears in *Armed with Madness* as absence and identity. Boris is "the Russian" (multiple references) — his nationality is his primary designation, preceding any personal quality. Russia is the place he has lost, the world whose collapse (the Revolution, the Civil War) has deposited him in Paris and then in the orbit of Gault. His displacement is his defining condition; he navigates other people's social orders because his own has been destroyed.

## Description

Russia is never described. It exists as Boris's un narrated past — the experience that has made him what he is, which the novel registers through effects rather than causes. The "awful, delicious fear that Boris could read his mind" (024-007) is the effect; the cause is somewhere in Russia, unreachable.

## Narrative Function

Russia's function is to provide a counterpoint to English stability. If England is the country of ancient continuity (the Grail quest, the Dorset landscape, the heath), Russia is the country of catastrophic rupture. Boris carries that rupture within him; it is what makes him dangerous.
