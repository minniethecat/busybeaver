# The Great War

The Great War (1914–1918) is never narrated in *Armed with Madness*, but it is everywhere present. Clarence's dreams return to it. Boris's exile is its consequence. The Gault House circle's retreat to Dorset is its aftermath. The novel, published in 1928, is set in the war's long shadow — a decade after the Armistice, in a world still learning what the war has done to it.

## The War's Presence

The war enters the novel through Clarence's dreams (005-017, 005-022) and through Boris's displacement. It is the historical ground against which the Grail quest unfolds — the catastrophe that makes the quest for a sacred object at once more necessary (something must survive the wreckage) and more difficult (how can the sacred survive what we have done?).
