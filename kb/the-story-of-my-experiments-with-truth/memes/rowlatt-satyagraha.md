# Rowlatt Satyagraha

The Rowlatt Satyagraha of 1919 was Gandhi's response to the Rowlatt Acts, which extended wartime emergency powers into peacetime — allowing detention without trial and restricting civil liberties. It was Gandhi's first attempt at nationwide satyagraha and led to the establishment of the Satyagraha Sabha in Bombay (153-014). The campaign was overshadowed by the Jallianwala Bagh massacre at Amritsar.

## Background

The Rowlatt Acts were passed despite unanimous Indian opposition. Gandhi saw them as a betrayal of the wartime loyalty Indians had shown and a fundamental attack on civil liberties. He called for a nationwide hartal (strike) and satyagraha.

## Gandhi's Role

Gandhi organized the Satyagraha Sabha to coordinate the campaign. Thousands signed the satyagraha pledge. However, the movement was suspended after violence broke out, and Gandhi acknowledged it as a "Himalayan miscalculation" — he had called for mass civil disobedience before adequately training participants in nonviolence.

## Key Passages

> A separate body called the Satyagraha Sabha was established at my instance. (153-014)
