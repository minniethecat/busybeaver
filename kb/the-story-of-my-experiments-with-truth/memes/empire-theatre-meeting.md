# Empire Theatre Meeting

The mass meeting at the Empire Theatre in Johannesburg on September 11, 1906, is widely recognized as the birth of organized satyagraha. Thousands of Indians gathered to protest the Transvaal government's proposed ordinance requiring Indians to register and carry passes. At the meeting, Sheth Haji Habib proposed that those present take a solemn oath — in the name of God — to defy the law. Gandhi recognized the power of the moment and the movement was born.

## Background

The Transvaal Asiatic Registration Act threatened the Indian community with criminalization. Gandhi had been organizing opposition but the Empire Theatre meeting transformed political protest into a sacred commitment.

## Gandhi's Role

Gandhi initially saw the proposed resistance as political. Habib's suggestion of an oath transformed his understanding, making him realize that satyagraha was not merely political strategy but moral and spiritual commitment.

## Key Passages

> Sheth Haji Habib then rose and proposed that we should take an oath in the name of God.
