# Swami Shraddhanand

Swami Shraddhanand was a prominent Hindu reformer and Arya Samaj leader who invited Gandhi to establish his ashram in Hardvar, one of India's holiest cities. Though Gandhi ultimately chose Ahmedabad instead, Shraddhanand's invitation reflected the respect Gandhi commanded across the spectrum of Hindu opinion, from orthodox to reformist.

## Relationship to Gandhi

"Shraddhanandji wanted me to settle in Hardvar" (133-002), Gandhi wrote, describing the competing invitations he received upon returning to India in 1915. Shraddhanand, who had renounced a successful legal career to become a sannyasi and educational reformer, saw in Gandhi a kindred spirit — someone who combined spiritual commitment with social action. Though Gandhi chose Ahmedabad for practical reasons, he maintained respect for Shraddhanand's work.

## Role in the Narrative

Shraddhanand appears briefly in the narrative of the ashram's founding, but his presence represents the network of Hindu reformers who formed an important constituency for Gandhi's work in India. His later assassination by a Muslim fanatic in 1926 made him a martyr for Hindu-Muslim unity — a cause Gandhi championed.
