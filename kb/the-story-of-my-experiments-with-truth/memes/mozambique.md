# Mozambique

Mozambique was a Portuguese colony on the east coast of Africa, encountered by Gandhi as a port of call during his first voyage from Bombay to Durban in 1893, en route to South Africa to take up a legal case.

## Gandhi's Connection

Gandhi passed through Mozambique during his first journey to South Africa in 1893. The steamer from Bombay made a stop at Mozambique before proceeding to Natal. It was during this voyage that Gandhi first encountered the racial hierarchies that would define his South African experience — the ship's chief officer informed him that all first class berths were engaged because "the Governor-General of Mozambique is going by this boat" ([1893-P01]).

## In the Narrative

### Key Events at This Location

Gandhi's experience at Mozambique was brief but part of a significant journey. When he arrived in Bombay seeking passage to South Africa on behalf of Dada Abdulla and Company, the agent initially told him no first class berth was available and suggested he travel on deck — an offer Gandhi refused: "Those were the days of my first class travelling, and how could a barrister travel as a deck passenger?" ([1893-P01]).

After personally going aboard the boat and meeting the chief officer, Gandhi learned that the Governor-General of Mozambique was traveling on the same vessel, occupying the remaining first class accommodations. This was Gandhi's first exposure to the colonial order of East Africa — a Portuguese governor taking precedence on a ship bound for British Natal.

### Significance to Gandhi's Journey

The Mozambique port call was a minor waypoint, but the voyage as a whole was formative. After the stop at Mozambique, "thence we reached Natal towards the close of May" ([1893-P05]). Gandhi's arrival in South Africa would soon confront him with far more consequential experiences of racial discrimination — most famously on the train at Pietermaritzburg — that catalyzed his transformation from a timid young barrister into a political activist.

## Historical Context

Mozambique was a Portuguese colony from 1505 until 1975, occupying a strategic position on the southeast coast of Africa along the Indian Ocean trade routes. In the 1890s, when Gandhi passed through, the port of Lourenço Marques (now Maputo) and Mozambique Island were key stops on the maritime route between India and South Africa. The colony was administered separately from British South Africa, though the two colonial systems shared overlapping economic and labor networks.
