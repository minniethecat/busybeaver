# Allahabad

Allahabad (now Prayagraj) is a historic city at the confluence of the Ganges and Yamuna rivers in Uttar Pradesh. It appears twice in Gandhi's autobiography: first in 1893 when a delayed chemist's visit caused him to miss his train to Bombay, and later in 1920 when the Khilafat Committee held an all-night session to deliberate on the noncooperation resolution.

## Gandhi's Connection

Gandhi's first encounter with Allahabad occurred in 1893, when he stopped there en route to Bombay before sailing for South Africa. A forty-five-minute train halt turned into an overnight stay when a drowsy chemist took too long dispensing medicine (050-001). The missed connection proved serendipitous — it gave Gandhi an extra day to lobby the editor of *The Pioneer*, an influential Allahabad newspaper, for support of the Indian cause in South Africa.

## In the Narrative

### Key Events at This Location

**1893 — The Missed Train**: During a scheduled forty-five-minute stop at Allahabad station, Gandhi decided to purchase medicine at a chemist's shop. The chemist "was half asleep, and took an unconscionable time in dispensing the medicine" (050-001). When Gandhi returned to the station, the train had departed. The Station Master had briefly held the train but, not seeing Gandhi, had removed his luggage from the carriage (050-001).

**The Pioneer Interview**: Stranded, Gandhi checked into Kellner's hotel and sought an appointment with the editor of *The Pioneer*, a newspaper he understood to be "an opponent of Indian aspirations" (050-002). The editor, Mr. Chesney Jr., granted a hearing and promised to notice Gandhi's writings — though he warned he could not "promise to endorse all the Indian demands" without giving due weight to the Colonial viewpoint (050-002).

**1920 — Noncooperation Deliberations**: Nearly three decades later, Allahabad was the site of a crucial Khilafat Committee session. Gandhi recalled vividly "how once at Allahabad a committee sat all night deliberating upon the subject" of nonviolent noncooperation (166-002). After prolonged debate, the resolution was carried, with the late Hakim Saheb moving from initial scepticism to wholehearted support.

### Significance to Gandhi's Journey

The two Allahabad episodes illustrate contrasting phases of Gandhi's public life. In 1893, he was an unknown young barrister lobbying newspaper editors one by one. By 1920, he was at the center of a nationwide movement, guiding all-night deliberations that would shape the course of Indian history. The city thus bookends Gandhi's transformation from provincial advocate to national leader.
