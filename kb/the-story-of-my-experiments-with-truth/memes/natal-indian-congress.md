# Natal Indian Congress

The Natal Indian Congress (NIC) was Gandhi's first political organization, founded in 1894 shortly after his arrival in South Africa. It was modeled on the Indian National Congress and aimed to unite the diverse Indian community in Natal — merchants and laborers, Hindus and Muslims — around common grievances and to advocate for Indian rights through petitions, publicity, and legal action.

## Gandhi's Relationship

Gandhi was the NIC's founding secretary and driving force. Through the NIC, he learned the skills of political organizing: drafting petitions, building coalitions, managing publicity, and negotiating with authorities. The NIC was the institutional vehicle for Gandhi's early campaigns against discriminatory legislation and became the foundation on which the later satyagraha movement was built.

## Role in the Narrative

The NIC appears in the autobiography as Gandhi's first attempt at sustained political organization. Its successes and failures taught him lessons that shaped the more ambitious organizations he would later build.
