# Natal

Natal was the South African province where Gandhi spent most of his twenty-one years in Africa. Durban, its principal port, was his home base; Phoenix Settlement, on its outskirts, was his first ashram. Natal's large Indian population — descendants of indentured laborers and a growing merchant class — formed the constituency for Gandhi's political and legal work.

## Gandhi's Connection

Natal was where Gandhi built his legal career, founded *Indian Opinion*, organized the Indian community, and first experienced the power of collective action. The £3 tax on former indentured laborers and their descendants, which Gandhi campaigned against, was a Natal-specific grievance that mobilized thousands. His work in Natal established the patterns — community organizing, journalism, legal advocacy, spiritual community — that would characterize his entire career.

## In the Narrative

Natal appears throughout the autobiography's South African chapters as the home base from which Gandhi ventured into the more confrontational politics of the Transvaal. After each campaign, he returned to Natal — to Durban, to Phoenix — to regroup.
