# Calcutta

Calcutta (now Kolkata) was the capital of British India until 1911 and the center of Indian political life when Gandhi first engaged with the Indian National Congress. He attended Congress sessions here, met Gokhale and other leaders, and navigated the complexities of Indian political culture.

## Gandhi's Connection

Gandhi first came to Calcutta in 1901 to attend the Congress session, where he met Gokhale (046-013). The city was where he presented his resolution on South Africa — his first engagement with Indian national politics. In 1947, during the partition violence, Gandhi undertook one of his most significant fasts for communal harmony in Calcutta.

## In the Narrative

Calcutta appears in the autobiography as the stage for Gandhi's political apprenticeship — the city where he learned the workings of the Congress, observed political leaders, and began to develop his own distinctive approach.
