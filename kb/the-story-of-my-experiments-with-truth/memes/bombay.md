# Bombay

Bombay (now Mumbai) was the city where Gandhi attempted to establish his legal career — first after his return from England in 1891, and again after his return from South Africa in 1915. Though his initial efforts failed, Bombay became an important center of support for his political work, with the headquarters of the Satyagraha Sabha established here during the Rowlatt agitation (153-014).

## Gandhi's Connection

Gandhi's first attempt to practice law in Bombay was a failure — he fled the courtroom in embarrassment during his first case. But Bombay's business community, particularly Gujarati merchants, became important financial supporters of his work in South Africa. Upon his final return to India, Bombay served as his political gateway, and the city's leaders played key roles in the independence movement.

## In the Narrative

Bombay appears in the autobiography at crucial transition points: the failed law practice, the departure for South Africa, the return from South Africa, and the founding of the Satyagraha Sabha.

## Key Passages

> A separate body called the Satyagraha Sabha was established at my instance. Its principal members were drawn from Bombay. (153-014)
