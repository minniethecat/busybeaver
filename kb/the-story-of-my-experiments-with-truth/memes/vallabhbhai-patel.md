## Summary

Vallabhbhai Patel (1875–1950), later known as Sardar Patel, was a barrister and Congress leader from Gujarat who became one of Gandhi's most trusted coworkers. The autobiography documents their growing partnership during the Ahmedabad mill workers' strike (1918) and the Kheda Satyagraha (1918), where Patel emerged as a dedicated grassroots organizer who bridged the gap between educated public workers and the peasantry.

## The Ahmedabad Mill Strike

Gandhi first came to know Vallabhbhai Patel intimately during the Ahmedabad mill workers' strike, alongside Shankarlal Banker and Shrimati Anasuyabai [144-016]. During the strike, Vallabhbhai worked to find employment for the striking workers under the Municipality. When that avenue proved limited, Maganlal Gandhi suggested the strikers could be put to work hauling sand from the Sabarmati riverbed for the Ashram's construction needs [146-006].

## The Kheda Satyagraha

In the Kheda campaign against unjust land revenue assessment, Vallabhbhai Patel was among Gandhi's principal comrades, together with Shankarlal Banker, Shrimati Anasuyabehn, Indulal Yagnik, and Mahadev Desai [147-006]. Gandhi noted that Vallabhbhai "came to be the most popular leader of the peasants" in Kheda, with his legal expertise and local roots making him uniquely effective.

The Kheda campaign represented a breakthrough in connecting educated public workers with rural life: "The Kheda campaign compelled the educated public workers to establish contact with the actual life of the peasants" [149-005]. Vallabhbhai, as a successful barrister who immersed himself in village struggles, embodied this transformation.

## Personal Devotion to Gandhi

The autobiography reveals the depth of personal concern Vallabhbhai had for Gandhi. During Gandhi's serious illness in 1918, it was Vallabhbhai who brought the news that Germany had been defeated and that recruiting was no longer necessary — news that relieved one of Gandhi's major preoccupations during his recovery [152-006].

Earlier, Vallabhbhai had accompanied Gandhi on a difficult journey from Nadiad to the Sabarmati station, a distance Gandhi walked "with very great difficulty" during his illness. Vallabhbhai "saw that I was ill" and recognized the severity of Gandhi's condition [152-003].

## The Rowlatt Act and Beyond

As Gandhi recovered from his illness, the Rowlatt Committee's report was published, whose recommendations "startled" him [153-011]. Vallabhbhai Patel was among the select group of about twenty persons invited to the Ashram for the conference where Gandhi first proposed the idea of satyagraha against the Rowlatt Act [153-013].

## Legacy

The autobiography captures Vallabhbhai Patel at the beginning of his career as a mass leader. The barrister who threw himself into the Kheda peasants' cause would go on to become one of the central figures of India's freedom movement, known as the "Iron Man of India" for his role in integrating the princely states after independence. In Gandhi's pages, he appears as the trusted lieutenant whose legal skill, organizational ability, and personal devotion made him indispensable.
