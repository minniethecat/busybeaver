# Rajkot

Rajkot was the city of Gandhi's childhood and adolescence. His family moved here when he was about seven, after his father Karamchand Gandhi became Diwan of Rajkot State. Gandhi attended primary and secondary school here, was married to Kasturba here at age thirteen, and formed many of his earliest memories and relationships in Rajkot.

## Gandhi's Connection

Rajkot was Gandhi's home from approximately age seven until he left for England at eighteen. His father's position as Diwan gave the family a comfortable position in local society. Gandhi attended Alfred High School in Rajkot, where he was an average student — "mediocre" by his own account. His early experiments with meat-eating, smoking, and petty theft all took place in Rajkot, as did his care for his ailing father.

## In the Narrative

Rajkot appears prominently in the autobiography's early chapters. It forms the backdrop for Gandhi's moral struggles as an adolescent — his brief rebellion against vegetarianism, his experiments with smoking, his theft of a bit of gold from his brother, and his devoted nursing of his dying father. These formative experiences in Rajkot shaped the moral seriousness that would characterize his later life.
