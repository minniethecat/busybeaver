# Johannesburg

Johannesburg was the commercial capital of the Transvaal and the city where Gandhi's legal career reached its height — and where he chose to abandon it for public service. It was here, at the Empire Theatre on September 11, 1906, that the Indian community took the solemn oath that marked the birth of organized satyagraha.

## Gandhi's Connection

Gandhi moved to Johannesburg in 1903, establishing a successful legal practice that made him prosperous. But it was also in Johannesburg that he experienced the full force of racial discrimination and committed himself to the struggle. He was jailed multiple times in Johannesburg's prisons. The city was the center of his political organizing, the place where he edited *Indian Opinion*, and the home of his closest associates — Polak, Kallenbach, and others.

## In the Narrative

Johannesburg occupies the center of the South African sections. Key events include the founding of the satyagraha movement at the Empire Theatre, his multiple imprisonments, his negotiations with Smuts, and his relationship with the multi-ethnic community of supporters who gathered around him.
