# Maganlal Gandhi

Maganlal Gandhi was Gandhi's cousin and one of his most trusted lieutenants. He managed the Phoenix Settlement — Gandhi's first ashram — during Gandhi's long absences, maintaining the community and the press. His death in 1928 was a deep personal loss to Gandhi.

## Relationship to Gandhi

Maganlal was both family and comrade. He joined Gandhi's experiments early and proved his reliability through years of service at Phoenix. Gandhi trusted him to maintain the settlement's disciplines — simplicity, manual labor, spiritual practice — during his own travels. His death at a relatively young age was a blow that Gandhi felt keenly.

## Role in the Narrative

Maganlal appears in the background of the South African narrative — not a public figure but an essential pillar of the community that made Gandhi's public work possible. He represents the family members who sacrificed comfort and career to support Gandhi's experiments.

## Key Passages

> Maganlal was more than a cousin to me. He was a faithful comrade in my experiments.
