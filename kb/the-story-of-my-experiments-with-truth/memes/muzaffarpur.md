# Muzaffarpur

Muzaffarpur is a city in Bihar where Gandhi arrived in 1917 as his first stop en route to Champaran. Acting on the advice of Maulana Mazharul Haq, Gandhi broke his journey at Muzaffarpur, where he was met at the station at midnight by Acharya J.B. Kripalani and hosted by Professor Malkani — an act of hospitality that, for a Government professor to "harbour a man like me," was "an extraordinary thing in those days" (137-002).

## Gandhi's Connection

Gandhi arrived at Muzaffarpur in early 1917, at the start of what would become the Champaran Satyagraha. He had been persuaded by Rajkumar Shukla, a peasant from Champaran, to visit the district and investigate the exploitation of indigo farmers by European planters. En route from Patna, Maulana Mazharul Haq suggested Muzaffarpur as a staging point before proceeding to the troubled district (137-001).

## In the Narrative

### Key Events at This Location

**The Midnight Reception**: Gandhi wired ahead to Principal Kripalani, who had been a professor at the Government College in Muzaffarpur before recently resigning. Despite the train arriving at midnight, "he met me at the station with a crowd of students" (137-002). Kripalani had no accommodation of his own, so he arranged for Gandhi to stay with Professor Malkani, who "virtually became my host" (137-002).

**The Risk of Hospitality**: Gandhi noted the political significance of Malkani's welcome: "It was an extraordinary thing in those days for a Government professor to harbour a man like me" (137-002). The remark underscores how even associating with Gandhi in 1917 — before the Rowlatt Satyagraha and noncooperation — carried professional risk.

**The Champaran Briefing**: During his stay at Muzaffarpur, Gandhi gathered preliminary information about conditions in the Champaran district. Professor Kripalani, who had connections in the region, helped orient Gandhi to the local situation before he proceeded to Motihari.

### Significance to Gandhi's Journey

Muzaffarpur was the prelude to Champaran — Gandhi's first major satyagraha campaign on Indian soil. The warm reception by Kripalani and Malkani demonstrated that Gandhi already had a network of educated Indian supporters willing to take personal and professional risks for him. The Champaran inquiry, launched from this staging point, would establish Gandhi's method of fact-finding followed by nonviolent resistance as a template for future campaigns.

## Key Passages

He used to be a professor in the Government College, Muzaffarpur, and had just resigned the post when I went there.
