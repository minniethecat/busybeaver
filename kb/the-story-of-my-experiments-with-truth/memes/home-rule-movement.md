## Background

The demand for Indian Home Rule — or Swaraj — had been a central theme of Indian political discourse since the early twentieth century. Gandhi himself had articulated his vision of self-rule in his 1909 work *Hind Swaraj* or *Indian Home Rule*, written during his years in South Africa. In that book he discussed the immorality of modern civilization and argued for a return to India's traditional self-governing village communities [058-003].

After Gandhi's return to India, his mentor Gokhale would sometimes laugh at some of his ideas in *Hind Swaraj*, saying: "After you have stayed a year in India, your views will correct themselves" [068-002].

## Dr. Besant's Home Rule Agitation

During World War I, Dr. Annie Besant launched a brilliant Home Rule agitation that captured the imagination of the Indian public. The agitation touched even the peasants of Gujarat, though it was the Kheda Satyagraha campaign that compelled educated public workers to establish contact with the actual life of the peasants [080-014].

The Home Rule agitation ran parallel to the establishment of the Home Rule Leagues by Dr. Besant and Bal Gangadhar Tilak in 1916. The movement demanded self-government for India within the British Empire, arguing that India's loyalty and sacrifices during the War entitled her to a greater measure of political autonomy.

## Gandhi and the War Conference

When the Viceroy, Lord Chelmsford, invited various Indian leaders to a war conference in Delhi, Gandhi was urged to attend. At the conference, he made a passionate case linking Indian support for the war effort with the demand for Home Rule:

> "If I could make my countrymen retrace their steps, I would make them withdraw all the Congress resolutions, and not whisper 'Home Rule' or 'Responsible Government' during the pendency of the War... But practically the whole of educated India has decided to take a less effective course... I wish to assure you that the desire for Home Rule has widely penetrated them." [079-019]

Gandhi argued that India's path to self-rule lay through demonstrating loyalty and sacrifice during the Empire's hour of need.
