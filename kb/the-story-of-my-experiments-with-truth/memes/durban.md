# Durban

Durban was Gandhi's first home in South Africa, the city where he arrived in 1893 as a young lawyer and from which he departed in 1914 as the leader of a mass movement. It was here that he was thrown off a train at Pietermaritzburg station, established the Phoenix Settlement, founded *Indian Opinion*, and began the experiments that became satyagraha.

## Gandhi's Connection

Gandhi arrived in Durban at the invitation of Dada Abdulla to handle a commercial dispute. He intended to stay one year; he remained twenty-one. Durban was where he first experienced racial discrimination, where he built his legal practice, and where he organized the Indian community. Phoenix Settlement, established on the outskirts of Durban in 1904, became his first ashram and the headquarters of his work.

## In the Narrative

Durban provides the setting for many of the autobiography's pivotal South African chapters. The train incident at nearby Pietermaritzburg, the founding of Phoenix, the early satyagraha campaigns, and Gandhi's evolution from lawyer to political leader all unfolded in and around Durban.

## Key Passages

> I was invited to South Africa by Dada Abdulla Sheth... I little knew that I was going there to spend twenty years of my life.
