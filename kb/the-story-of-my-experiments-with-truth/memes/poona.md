# Poona

Poona (present-day Pune) was the intellectual and political home of Gopal Krishna Gokhale, Gandhi's acknowledged political mentor. Upon returning to India from South Africa in 1915, Gandhi repeatedly visited Poona to consult with Gokhale and the members of the Servants of India Society. The city occupies a central place in the autobiography as the site of Gandhi's apprenticeship in Indian public life.

## Gandhi's Connection

Gandhi's first significant visit to Poona came during his campaign against the indentured labor system, when he sought the support of Lokamanya Tilak and other leaders. He found the city divided into two political parties but managed to gain the backing of a selfless band of workers who "held a meeting in an unostentatious little place" and sent Gandhi away "rejoicing and more confident of my mission" (053-007).

Later, after Gokhale summoned him from Bombay, Gandhi experienced what he called "precious time" in Poona. Gokhale and the Servants of India Society "overwhelmed me with affection," and Gandhi had frank talks with all of them "on every sort of subject" (126-008). The city was also where Gandhi confronted the delicate question of whether to join the Society after Gokhale's death — a decision that tested his loyalty to his mentor's vision against his own evolving philosophy.

## In the Narrative

Poona functions in the autobiography as a bridge between Gandhi's South Africa years and his Indian campaigns. It was from Poona that he traveled to Rajkot and Porbandar to meet his brother's widow (127-001). A week after arriving at Shantiniketan, Gandhi received a telegram from Poona announcing Gokhale's death — news that sent him rushing back to the city for the funeral rites and plunged the national movement into grief (128-011).

After the shraddha ceremonies, the surviving members of the Society debated its future and whether Gandhi should join. Gandhi reflected that while Gokhale was alive he had simply to obey his mentor's wishes, "a position I loved to be in." With Gokhale gone, he found himself "launching on the stormy sea of Indian public life" in need of a sure pilot (130-001).

## Key Passages

> After this I went to Poona. It is impossible for me to set down all the reminiscences of this precious time. Gokhale and the members of the Servants of India Society overwhelmed me with affection. (126-008)

> Without any ado this erudite and selfless band of workers in Poona held a meeting in an unostentatious little place, and sent me away rejoicing and more confident of my mission. (053-007)

> On arrival in Poona, we found ourselves discussing the future of the Society, and the question as to whether I should join it or not. Launching on the stormy sea of Indian public life, I was in need of a sure pilot. (130-001)
