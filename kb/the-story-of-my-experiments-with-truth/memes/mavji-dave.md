# Mavji Dave

Mavji Dave was an elderly Brahmin friend of the Gandhi family who played a quiet but significant role in Gandhi's early life. A trusted advisor to Gandhi's father Karamchand, Mavji Dave later counseled the young Mohandas and was one of the voices that encouraged him to go to England for legal studies.

## Relationship to Gandhi

Mavji Dave was a family intimate — the kind of wise elder who had standing to offer advice on personal matters. When the question of Gandhi's further education arose after his father's death, Mavji Dave recommended that he study law in England, arguing that it would take only three years and would qualify him for a respectable career. This advice set Gandhi on the path that led to London, South Africa, and everything that followed.

## Role in the Narrative

Mavji Dave appears briefly in the early chapters, but his advice had momentous consequences. He represents the network of family friends and community elders who shaped the young Gandhi's options and horizons.

## Key Passages

> Mavji Dave, a shrewd and learned Brahmin, an old friend and adviser of the family, happened to visit us. He suggested that I should be sent to England to study law.
