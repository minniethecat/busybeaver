# Transvaal

The Transvaal was the South African province where Gandhi's political transformation reached its fullest expression. It was here, in response to discriminatory legislation targeting Indians, that the satyagraha movement was born in 1906 and fought its most decisive campaigns. Johannesburg and Pretoria, both in the Transvaal, were the key theaters of Gandhi's South African work.

## Gandhi's Connection

The Transvaal's restrictive laws — requiring Indians to register, carry passes, and pay a punitive tax — provided the immediate cause for satyagraha. Gandhi organized mass defiance of these laws, courted imprisonment, and negotiated with Transvaal leaders including General Smuts. The Transvaal campaigns (1906–1914) were the laboratory in which satyagraha was tested and refined.

## In the Narrative

The Transvaal is the backdrop for the autobiography's most dramatic South African chapters — the Empire Theatre meeting, the mass imprisonments, the negotiations, and the final settlement. Gandhi's account of the "condition of Indians in the Transvaal" (038-001) captures the context that made satyagraha necessary.
