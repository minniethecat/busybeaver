# Jamnagar

Jamnagar is a city in the Kathiawad region of Gujarat, India. It was the site of Gandhi's first important legal case — an original civil suit — when he began his law practice in Rajkot after returning from England.

## Gandhi's Connection

Gandhi's association with Jamnagar came through his early legal career. After deciding to start work at Rajkot, his old well-wisher Kevalram Mavji Dave — who had originally encouraged him to go to England to study law — started him with three briefs. Among them was "an original case in Jamnagar" which was "rather important" ([1914-P01]). Gandhi hesitated to take it on, telling Dave he could not trust himself to do it justice, but Dave reassured him that "winning or losing is no concern of yours" and that he would assist ([1914-P01]).

## In the Narrative

### Key Events at This Location

The Jamnagar case, along with a case in Veraval, proved to be a turning point in Gandhi's legal career. After his performance in these cases, Dave told him: "You have proved your worth in Jamnagar and Veraval, and I have therefore not the least anxiety about you" ([1914-P35]). This endorsement marked Gandhi's transition from uncertain young barrister to recognized legal professional.

### Significance to Gandhi's Journey

The Jamnagar experience was part of the validation that launched Gandhi's career. Dave's assessment that Gandhi was "destined to do public work" and that they would "not allow you to be buried in Kathiawad" foreshadowed Gandhi's eventual move to Bombay and his entry into larger public life ([1914-P35]). The case demonstrated that Gandhi could handle serious legal matters despite his initial self-doubt.

## Historical Context

Jamnagar was a princely state in the Kathiawad region during the British colonial period, ruled by the Jadeja dynasty. The city was an important commercial and administrative center in the Saurashtra peninsula. Gandhi's legal practice in the Kathiawad region, including Jamnagar and Rajkot, was brief — he soon moved to Bombay seeking wider opportunities, and from there to South Africa, where his political career truly began.
