# Kheda Satyagraha

The Kheda Satyagraha of 1918 was Gandhi's campaign supporting peasants in Gujarat's Kheda district who, facing crop failure, could not pay the government's land revenue demand. Gandhi led them in a campaign of non-payment — not as tax evasion but as a demand for just assessment. The campaign marked "the beginning of an awakening among the peasants of Gujarat, the beginning of their true political education" (149-004).

## Background

Crop failures in Kheda had left peasants unable to pay land revenue, but the British administration refused to grant remission. Gandhi, fresh from the Champaran success, was drawn into the struggle immediately after the Ahmedabad mill strike (147-001).

## Timeline

The campaign involved organized refusal to pay revenue despite government pressure, confiscation of property, and threats of imprisonment. Gandhi and local leaders like Vallabhbhai Patel mobilized peasant communities. The government eventually granted relief to those genuinely unable to pay.

## Gandhi's Role

Gandhi plunged into Kheda immediately after the Ahmedabad strike, organizing peasants and personally confronting district officials. He maintained strict nonviolent discipline throughout, ensuring the campaign remained a moral protest rather than a tax revolt.

## Historical Significance

Kheda consolidated the lessons of Champaran and established Gandhi's method of agrarian satyagraha. It also brought leaders like Vallabhbhai Patel into the independence movement.

## Key Passages

> The Kheda Satyagraha marks the beginning of an awakening among the peasants of Gujarat. (149-004)
