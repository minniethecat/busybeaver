# London Vegetarian Society

The London Vegetarian Society was the organization Gandhi joined during his student years in London (1888–91). It was here that vegetarianism became for him a conscious ethical choice rather than a matter of family tradition. He served on the society's executive committee, wrote articles for its journal, and met the community of ethical vegetarians — including Henry Salt and Dr. Allinson — who shaped his early intellectual development.

## Gandhi's Relationship

The Society was Gandhi's first experience of an organized ethical movement. Through it, he learned how ideas could be promoted through publications, meetings, and community-building. His reading of vegetarian literature — "examined very minutely, reasoning it out from the ethical, physical, and economic points of view" (017-001) — modeled the multi-dimensional thinking that characterized his later approach to all questions.
