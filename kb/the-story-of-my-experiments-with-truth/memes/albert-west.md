# Albert West

Albert West was a British printer who became one of Gandhi's most reliable colleagues during the South African years. As the manager of *Indian Opinion*, he kept the newspaper running through financial crises and Gandhi's frequent absences. West represents the dedicated but less visible supporters whose practical contributions made Gandhi's work possible.

## Relationship to Gandhi

West joined Gandhi in the work of *Indian Opinion* during its early years and proved himself indispensable. While Gandhi traveled, negotiated, and led satyagraha campaigns, West managed the day-to-day operations of the press. His relationship with Gandhi was professional rather than intimate, but it was marked by deep mutual trust. Gandhi knew he could rely on West to maintain the newspaper — the movement's primary instrument of communication — regardless of the difficulties.

## Biography

### Role in the Narrative

West appears in the autobiography primarily in the context of *Indian Opinion*'s operations. His story illustrates the division of labor within the satyagraha movement: not everyone could be a public leader, and the movement depended on those who worked quietly behind the scenes. West was one of those who made the movement's infrastructure function.
