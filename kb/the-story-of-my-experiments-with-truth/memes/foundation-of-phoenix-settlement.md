# Foundation of Phoenix Settlement

The Phoenix Settlement was founded in 1904 when Gandhi, inspired by reading John Ruskin's *Unto This Last* on a train journey, purchased a hundred-acre farm near Durban. He moved the press for *Indian Opinion* there and established a community where manual labor, simple living, and spiritual practice would be integrated into daily life. Phoenix was the prototype for Tolstoy Farm and Sabarmati Ashram.

## Background

Gandhi had been deeply moved by Ruskin's argument that the good of the individual is contained in the good of all, that all work has equal dignity, and that a life of manual labor is the worthiest life. "I determined to change my life in accordance with the ideals of the book."

## Gandhi's Role

Gandhi reorganized *Indian Opinion* around Phoenix, requiring all workers — including himself — to perform manual labor. The settlement survived fires and financial crises, sustained by residents who shared Gandhi's ideals.

## Key Passages

> The book Unto This Last was impossible to lay aside. I determined to change my life.
