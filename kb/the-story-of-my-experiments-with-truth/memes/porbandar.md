# Porbandar

Porbandar is a coastal city on the Arabian Sea in the Kathiawar peninsula of Gujarat. It was the seat of the princely state where Gandhi's father, Karamchand Gandhi, served as Diwan (Prime Minister). Gandhi was born here on October 2, 1869, in a three-story ancestral home that still stands.

## Gandhi's Connection

Gandhi's family had served as Diwans of Porbandar for generations. His grandfather Uttamchand Gandhi and father Karamchand both held the post. Gandhi's earliest memories were of Porbandar: the family home, the coastal landscape, and the religious atmosphere created by his devout mother Putlibai. He lived here for his first seven years before the family moved to Rajkot.

## In the Narrative

### Key Events

Porbandar represents the world into which Gandhi was born — a princely state in Kathiawar, governed by traditional rulers under British paramountcy, where the Gandhi family had risen to prominence through administrative service. Gandhi's caste (Bania) and class (administrative elite) were both shaped by Porbandar's social structure.

### Significance to Gandhi's Journey

Though Gandhi left Porbandar at a young age, the city remained symbolically significant as the origin point of his life's journey. His autobiography begins with his birth and parentage in Porbandar, grounding his story in a specific place and lineage.

## Historical Context

Porbandar was one of hundreds of princely states in British India, ruled by the Jethwa dynasty. The city had a long history as a trading port and was known for its maritime commerce. Gandhi's birth in a princely state, rather than in directly administered British India, shaped his early understanding of Indian political structures.
