# Kheda

Kheda (also spelled Kaira) is a district in central Gujarat that became the site of one of Gandhi's earliest and most significant satyagraha campaigns in India. The Kheda Satyagraha of 1918, launched in support of peasants unable to pay land revenue after crop failures, demonstrated the application of nonviolent resistance to economic grievances and brought Vallabhbhai Patel into the independence movement.

## Gandhi's Connection

Gandhi's involvement in Kheda began with a letter from Mohanlal Pandya and Shankarlal Parikh, informing him of widespread crop failure and asking him to guide the peasants who were unable to pay the revenue assessment (144-001). Gandhi insisted on an on-the-spot inquiry before offering advice, reflecting his methodical approach to satyagraha.

The struggle came immediately after the Ahmedabad mill-hands' strike, giving Gandhi "no breathing time" (147-001). Vallabhbhai Patel, then a successful barrister, suspended his practice to join the campaign — a sacrifice from which, for all practical purposes, he was never able to return (147-006). Other key comrades included Shankarlal Banker, Anasuyabehn, Indulal Yagnik, and Mahadev Desai.

A condition approaching famine had arisen in the district, and the Patidars of Kheda were considering whether to seek suspension of the revenue assessment for the year (147-002). The campaign attracted press attention from the start, unlike the earlier Champaran struggle which had taken place in a remote corner of India (148-001).

## In the Narrative

During the Kheda recruiting campaign for the First World War, an error in diet brought Gandhi to "death's door," forcing him to confront his long-held belief that milk was unnecessary for health. He tried mung water, mahua oil, and almond-milk as substitutes, but "wore out his body in experimenting" (085-005). This health crisis eventually led him to reluctantly resume drinking goat's milk.

## Key Passages

> A condition approaching famine had arisen in the Kheda district owing to a widespread failure of crops, and the Patidars of Kheda were considering the question of getting the revenue assessment for the year suspended. (147-002)

> My principal comrades in this struggle were Sjts. Vallabhbhai Patel, Shankarlal Banker, Shrimati Anasuyabehn, Sjts. Indulal Yagnik, Mahadev Desai and others. Sjt. Vallabhbhai, in joining the struggle, had to suspend a splendid and growing practice at the bar. (147-006)

> Whilst I was engaged on the recruiting campaign in Kheda, an error in diet laid me low, and I was at death's door. I tried in vain to rebuild a shattered constitution without milk. (085-005)
