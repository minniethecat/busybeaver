# Yeravda

Yeravda (also spelled Yerwada) is a locality in Pune, Maharashtra, known for the Yeravda Central Jail, where [[mahatma-gandhi]] was imprisoned on multiple occasions. It was within the walls of this prison that Gandhi wrote the major portion of his autobiography, making it the birthplace of *The Story of My Experiments with Truth*.

## Gandhi's Connection

Gandhi was imprisoned at Yeravda Jail during the non-cooperation movement of the early 1920s. His time there was marked by disciplined study and writing. He observed that while he had hoped to continue various studies after returning to India, the demands of public life made sustained learning impossible except when in jail: most of his reading since 1893 had been done in confinement [049-007]. At Yeravda, he continued this pattern, turning his cell into a scholar's study and a writer's workshop.

## In the Narrative

### Key Events at This Location

The most significant event at Yeravda was Gandhi's writing of the autobiography itself. He records: "I wrote the major portion of this history in Yeravda jail and finished it after I was released" [103-003]. This makes Yeravda not merely a place of confinement but the physical space where Gandhi's life story took written form — a fitting irony that the man who preached truth as the highest virtue should compose his truthful account of himself while imprisoned for that very principle.

Beyond writing, Gandhi used his imprisonment at Yeravda for linguistic study. He made progress in Tamil and Urdu while in jail, noting that he studied Tamil in South African jails and Urdu in Yeravda jail, though he never learned to speak Tamil fluently and found his reading ability fading for want of practice [049-007].

### Significance to Gandhi's Journey

Yeravda represents the paradox of Gandhi's life: that imprisonment, intended by the British to silence him, became a productive sanctuary. The jail afforded him the uninterrupted time that his public life denied him. That the autobiography — his most personal and enduring work — was largely written in prison testifies to his capacity to transform constraint into creative opportunity.
