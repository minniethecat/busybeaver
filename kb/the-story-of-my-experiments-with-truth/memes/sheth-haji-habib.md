# Sheth Haji Habib

Sheth Haji Habib was a Muslim merchant leader in South Africa who played a decisive role at the birth of organized satyagraha. At the mass meeting in the Empire Theatre, Johannesburg, on September 11, 1906, it was Habib who proposed that the assembled Indians take a solemn oath — in the name of God — to defy the proposed registration law. This suggestion transformed a political resolution into a sacred vow, giving satyagraha its distinctive character as a moral and spiritual commitment.

## Relationship to Gandhi

Habib was one of the leaders of the Indian merchant community in the Transvaal who worked alongside Gandhi during the early satyagraha. His proposal of the oath shifted the meeting from a political gathering to a spiritual event. Gandhi immediately recognized the power of the idea and later reflected that this moment marked the birth of satyagraha as a distinct method.

## Role in the Narrative

Habib's intervention illustrates the collaborative nature of satyagraha's development — it was not Gandhi's invention alone but emerged from the collective wisdom of the Indian community. The idea of the solemn oath, suggested by a Muslim merchant, demonstrates the multi-faith character of the movement from its inception.
