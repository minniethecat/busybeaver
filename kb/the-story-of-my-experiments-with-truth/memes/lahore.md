# Lahore

Lahore was a focal point of the 1919 Rowlatt Satyagraha, where Gandhi witnessed an extraordinary outpouring of public support that he described as a scene he could "never efface" from memory. The city also features in the autobiography as a site of hardship during Gandhi's third-class railway travels while campaigning for the abolition of indentured emigration.

## Gandhi's Connection

Gandhi visited Lahore at multiple critical junctures in the autobiography. During his campaign against indentured emigration, he passed through Lahore en route from Karachi to Calcutta, experiencing the bitter hardships of third-class railway travel (011-016). Years later, during the Rowlatt Satyagraha of 1919, he traveled to Lahore where he was greeted by a seething mass of humanity at the railway station — the entire populace had turned out to meet him (035-009).

## In the Narrative

### Key Events at This Location

The most vivid Lahore episode is Gandhi's arrival during the Rowlatt Satyagraha. The railway station was packed from end to end with people who greeted him "as if to meet a dear relation after a long separation" (035-009). Earlier, during the abolition campaign, his journey from Lahore to Delhi had been his "bitterest experience" of third-class travel — the train was impossibly crowded and those who could not find seats were compelled to travel on footboards (011-016).

### Significance to Gandhi's Journey

Lahore demonstrated the depth of popular feeling that Gandhi's satyagraha campaigns could mobilize. The spontaneous mass turnout at Lahore station confirmed that nonviolent resistance had captured the imagination of ordinary Indians far beyond the educated elite. For Gandhi, these scenes were both exhilarating and sobering, reinforcing his sense of responsibility for channeling such popular energy nonviolently.

## Key Passages

> The scene that I witnessed on my arrival at Lahore can never be effaced from my memory. The railway station was from end to end one seething mass of humanity. The entire populace had turned out of doors in eager expectation, as if to meet a dear relation after a long separation, and was delirious with joy.
>
> (035-009)
