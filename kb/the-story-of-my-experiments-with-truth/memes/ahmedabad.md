# Ahmedabad

Ahmedabad was the city Gandhi chose as his base upon returning to India in 1915. The textile center of Gujarat, it offered proximity to both rural India and the political currents of the independence movement. Gandhi's decision to settle here — rather than in Hardvar, Rajkot, or Calcutta — reflected his practical judgment about where his work could be most effective.

## Gandhi's Connection

Gandhi founded the Satyagraha Ashram on the outskirts of Ahmedabad and lived there for over fifteen years. In 1918, he intervened in a dispute between Ahmedabad's textile mill workers and owners, leading a strike and undertaking his first public fast in India. The Ahmedabad mill-hands' strike demonstrated the application of satyagraha to labor relations and established Gandhi's reputation as a champion of workers.

## In the Narrative

Ahmedabad appears in the autobiography primarily in connection with the ashram's founding and the mill strike. The city also served as the base from which Gandhi launched his campaigns in Kheda, Bardoli, and eventually the Salt March.

## Key Passages
