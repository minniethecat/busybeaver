# Kathiawad

Kathiawad is the peninsular region of western Gujarat where Mohandas Gandhi was born and raised. The region's complex politics — a patchwork of small princely states rife with intrigue — shaped Gandhi's earliest understanding of power, and its social customs, including child marriage and purdah, formed the backdrop against which his moral awakening unfolded.

## Gandhi's Connection

The Gandhi family had served as Prime Ministers (Diwans) in several Kathiawad States for three generations. Gandhi's grandfather Uttamchand Gandhi was Diwan of Porbandar before state intrigues forced him to seek refuge in Junagadh. His father Karamchand Gandhi continued the family tradition of service to the Kathiawad princely courts. Gandhi himself was born in Porbandar, schooled in Rajkot, and began his legal practice in the region after returning from England. (045-001)

## In the Narrative

### The Region's Social Customs

Kathiawad's distinctive social practices feature prominently in Gandhi's account of his early years. The region maintained two distinct rites — betrothal and marriage — and Gandhi recounts being betrothed three times without his knowledge before his final marriage at age thirteen. The region also practiced a form of purdah that Gandhi later described as "peculiar, useless and barbarous," which complicated his efforts to educate his wife Kasturbai. (087-001) (121-001)

### Political Intrigue and Disillusionment

Upon returning from England as a barrister, Gandhi encountered Kathiawad's political culture firsthand. He described it as "a conglomeration of small States" with "its rich crop of politics" — petty intrigues between states and officers vying for power. His attempt to use his acquaintance with a British Political Agent to help his brother ended in humiliation, teaching him that "Kathiawad was different from England; that an officer on leave was not the same as an officer on duty." (949-001) (925-001)

### Significance to Gandhi's Journey

The suffocating political atmosphere of Kathiawad became one of Gandhi's motivations for leaving India. He later reflected: "I had gone to South Africa for travel, for finding an escape from Kathiawad intrigues and for gaining my own livelihood." Yet the region also drew him back — after his South African satyagraha, he returned to Kathiawad where local workers greeted him eagerly, and the Viramgam customs hardships he encountered there became his first satyagraha campaign on Indian soil. (1607-001) (4085-001)

## Key Passages

> The Gandhis belong to the Bania caste and seem to have been originally grocers. But for three generations, from my grandfather, they have been Prime Ministers in several Kathiawad States. (045-001)
