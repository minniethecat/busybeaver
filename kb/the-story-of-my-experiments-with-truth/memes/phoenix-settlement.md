# Phoenix Settlement

Phoenix Settlement was Gandhi's first intentional community, founded in 1904 on a hundred-acre farm near Durban. Inspired by Ruskin's *Unto This Last*, Gandhi moved *Indian Opinion* to Phoenix and organized a community where manual labor, simple living, and spiritual practice were integrated into daily life. Phoenix was the prototype for all of Gandhi's later ashrams.

## Gandhi's Connection

Gandhi purchased the Phoenix land after reading Ruskin's *Unto This Last* on a train journey — a book that "transformed" his life. He moved the press for *Indian Opinion* to the farm, where residents — including Gandhi's family and a multi-ethnic community of supporters — lived according to principles of bread labor, equality, and spiritual discipline. Phoenix remained Gandhi's South African home base until his departure in 1914.

## In the Narrative

Phoenix appears in the autobiography as the physical manifestation of Gandhi's emerging philosophy — a place where ideals could be tested in practice. The settlement survived fires, financial crises, and Gandhi's absences, sustained by the dedication of residents like Maganlal Gandhi and Albert West.

## Key Passages

> The book Unto This Last was impossible to lay aside. I determined to change my life in accordance with the ideals of the book.
