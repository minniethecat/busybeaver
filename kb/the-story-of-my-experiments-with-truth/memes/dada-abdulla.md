# Dada Abdulla

Dada Abdulla was a Gujarati Muslim merchant in Durban whose commercial dispute with a relative led him to hire the young barrister Gandhi in 1893. Gandhi traveled to South Africa to handle the case, intending to stay for a year. He remained for twenty-one years. Abdulla thus played an unwitting but pivotal role in the birth of satyagraha.

## Relationship to Gandhi

Abdulla was Gandhi's first client and his introduction to the Indian community in South Africa. Through Abdulla, Gandhi encountered the conditions of Indian indentured laborers and the discrimination faced by the Indian merchant class. Abdulla also introduced Gandhi to the multi-religious character of the Indian diaspora — Hindu, Muslim, Parsi, and Christian merchants living and working together.

## Role in the Narrative

Abdulla appears at the very beginning of the South African story — the man whose legal case set everything else in motion. His significance lies less in his own actions than in the chain of events his case triggered.
