# Pretoria

Pretoria was Gandhi's first destination in the Transvaal and the city where he spent his initial year in South Africa (1893–94). It was en route to Pretoria that he was thrown off a train at Pietermaritzburg — an experience that became foundational to his political awakening. In Pretoria itself, he studied Christianity with a Quaker family, attended church services, and began the interfaith exploration that characterized his spiritual life.

## Gandhi's Connection

Gandhi came to Pretoria to handle the legal case of Dada Abdulla. During his year there, he called a meeting of the local Indian community — his first public speech — urging them to organize against discrimination. He also formed close relationships with Christian friends who challenged and deepened his religious understanding without converting him.

## In the Narrative

Pretoria marks the beginning of Gandhi's South African transformation. The discrimination he experienced and witnessed there, combined with his religious explorations, planted the seeds of satyagraha.
