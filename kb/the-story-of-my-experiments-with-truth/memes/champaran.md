# Champaran

Champaran is a district in Bihar that became the site of Gandhi's first major satyagraha in India. In 1917, at the invitation of local peasants, Gandhi investigated the exploitation of indigo farmers by British planters under the tinkathia system, which forced them to grow indigo on a portion of their land. The Champaran Satyagraha established Gandhi's method on Indian soil and is widely regarded as the beginning of the Indian independence movement's mass phase.

## Gandhi's Connection

Gandhi arrived in Champaran in 1917 knowing little about indigo farming. He was met with resistance from the British administration, which ordered him to leave the district. His refusal — his first act of civil disobedience in India — led to his arrest and trial at Motihari. The government, faced with Gandhi's determination and popular support, backed down and appointed him to an official inquiry committee. The committee's report vindicated the peasants' grievances, and the tinkathia system was abolished.

## In the Narrative

Champaran represents the proving ground of satyagraha in India. Gandhi described it as "a bold experiment with Truth and Ahimsa" (140-001). The campaign demonstrated that the methods developed in South Africa could work in India, and it established Gandhi's reputation as a leader capable of delivering concrete results.
