# Servants of India Society

The Servants of India Society was founded by Gopal Krishna Gokhale in 1905 to train young Indians for a life of public service. Members took vows of poverty and service, modeled partly on religious orders but directed toward secular national goals. Gandhi greatly admired the Society and saw Gokhale's model of disciplined, selfless national service as a template for his own ashrams.

## Gandhi's Relationship

Gandhi was invited to join the Society by Gokhale but was unable to do so due to opposition from some members who distrusted his "extremist" associations. Nevertheless, the Society's model — trained, disciplined workers dedicated to national service — deeply influenced Gandhi's conception of the satyagrahi as a moral soldier.
