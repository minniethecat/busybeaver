# Motihari

Motihari is a town in [[bihar]]'s [[champaran]] district where [[mahatma-gandhi]] was summoned to court in April 1917, an event that marked the formal beginning of the Champaran Satyagraha — Gandhi's first civil disobedience campaign on Indian soil.

## Arrival in Champaran

After stopping in [[patna]], Gandhi proceeded to Motihari, the headquarters of the Champaran district, accompanied by [[rajkumar-shukla]] and several lawyers:

> "So I started with my coworkers for Motihari the same day. Babu Gorakh Prasad harboured us in his home, which became a caravanserai. It could hardly contain us all." [138-004]

On the very day of arrival, Gandhi learned that "about five miles from Motihari a tenant had been ill-treated" — he immediately set out to investigate, accompanied by Babu Dharanidhar Prasad [138-004].

## The Court Summons

Gandhi's investigations into tenant grievances did not go unnoticed by the district authorities. He was served with a notice ordering him to leave Champaran. When he refused to comply, he was summoned to appear before the magistrate in Motihari:

> "The case against me was that I had disobeyed the order to leave Champaran... I said that I had come to Champaran to render humanitarian and national service and that I could not leave." [138-005]

Gandhi pleaded guilty but explained that he was acting under "a higher law" — the law of conscience. He stated he would not leave Champaran voluntarily and was prepared to face the penalty.

## The Government's Retreat

The magistrate, uncertain how to handle this unusual defendant, postponed judgment. The next day, the case was withdrawn on instructions from the Lieutenant Governor. The government's retreat was Gandhi's first major victory in India:

> "The country thus had its first direct object-lesson in Civil Disobedience. The case against me was withdrawn. The order of my removal was cancelled and the Government even agreed to my remaining in Champarat during the enquiry." [138-008]

## The Investigation

From Motihari, Gandhi organized a systematic investigation of tenant grievances. The local vakils — [[brajkishore-prasad]], [[rajendra-prasad]], and others — gathered thousands of statements from peasants about the oppressive *tinkathia* system. What had begun as a court summons against Gandhi in Motihari had become a full-fledged campaign against the indigo planters.
