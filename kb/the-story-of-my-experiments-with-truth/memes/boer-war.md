# Boer War

The Boer War (1899–1902) was the conflict between the British Empire and the Boer republics of the Transvaal and Orange Free State. Gandhi, then a loyal subject of the British Empire despite the discrimination faced by Indians, organized an Indian ambulance corps to serve the British wounded. His participation in the war created lasting tensions with his commitment to ahimsa.

## Background

When war broke out, Gandhi argued that Indians in South Africa, who claimed the rights of British subjects, had a corresponding duty to support the Empire in its hour of need. He organized over a thousand Indians into an ambulance corps that served at the front lines.

## Gandhi's Role

Gandhi led the ambulance corps personally, carrying wounded soldiers from the battlefield under fire. His service earned him the War Medal and the respect of British authorities. However, his wartime participation later troubled him, as it seemed inconsistent with his growing commitment to nonviolence (116-003).

## Historical Significance

The Boer War ambulance corps was Gandhi's first experience of organizing Indians across religious and caste lines for a common cause — a precursor to his later political organizing.

## Key Passages

> It was quite clear to me that participation in war could never be consistent with ahimsa. But it is not always given to one to be equally clear about one's duty. (116-003)
