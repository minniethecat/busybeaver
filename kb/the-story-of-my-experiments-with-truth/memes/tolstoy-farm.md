# Tolstoy Farm

Tolstoy Farm was Gandhi's second South African ashram, established in 1910 on land purchased by Hermann Kallenbach near Johannesburg. Named in honor of Leo Tolstoy, it housed the families of satyagrahis during the final, most intense phase of the South African campaign. It was here that Gandhi refined his educational methods and experimented with a fully communal lifestyle.

## Gandhi's Connection

Tolstoy Farm was made possible by Kallenbach's generosity and was named after the Russian author whose writings had deeply influenced Gandhi. The community lived without servants — everyone, including Gandhi, performed all manual labor. Children were educated through a system of vocational and moral training that anticipated Gandhi's later educational experiments in India.

## In the Narrative

Tolstoy Farm represents the apex of Gandhi's South African community-building. The discipline of shared labor, the multi-faith composition of the community, and the integration of political struggle with daily spiritual practice all reached their fullest expression here. The farm was disbanded when Gandhi returned to India in 1914.
