# Rangoon

Rangoon (now Yangon) was the capital of British Burma, which was then administered as a province of British India. [[mahatma-gandhi]] visited the city in 1902, encountering Indian labourers working under harsh conditions and witnessing the devastating effects of a plague epidemic.

## The 1902 Visit

Gandhi travelled to Rangoon in 1902, still in his early years as a barrister. The journey came about through his legal practice — he had accepted a brief that required him to travel to Burma. The visit exposed him to the extensive network of Indian migration across the British Empire: Indian labourers, traders, and civil servants were scattered across Burma, many living in conditions of severe deprivation.

## Plague and Public Health

During his time in Rangoon, Gandhi encountered a plague epidemic that was ravaging the city. The epidemic and its aftermath reinforced Gandhi's growing convictions about sanitation and public health — themes that would become central to his work in [[durban]] and later in India.

> "The plague broke out with redoubled force in Rangoon... I had however made up my mind to sit in the plague hospital in the capacity of a nurse." [073-007]

Though the full extent of his service in Rangoon during the plague is not detailed in the autobiography, the experience contributed to his understanding of epidemic response and community health work — lessons he would apply during the plague outbreak in South Africa and later in Indian villages.

## Indian Labourers Abroad

The Rangoon visit was part of a broader pattern in Gandhi's early career: encounters with Indian diaspora communities across the Empire. Just as he had discovered the condition of indentured Indians in Natal, in Rangoon he saw Indian labourers working under British colonial administration in Burma. These experiences reinforced his emerging understanding that the Indian struggle for dignity was not limited to India's borders but extended across the Empire.
