# Round Table Conference

The Round Table Conferences (1930–32) were a series of meetings in London to discuss constitutional reform in India. Gandhi attended the Second Round Table Conference in 1931 as the sole representative of the Indian National Congress. The conferences ultimately failed to produce agreement, but they marked Gandhi's return to the international stage after the Salt March.

## Background

The British government convened the conferences following the Simon Commission's failure to secure Indian cooperation. The first conference (1930) was boycotted by Congress. By the second, the Gandhi-Irwin Pact had secured his release from prison and Congress's participation.

## Gandhi's Role

Gandhi traveled to London, staying in the East End among working people. He argued for Congress's claim to represent all Indians, but the conference deadlocked on the issue of separate electorates for minorities — leading to the British Communal Award and Gandhi's subsequent fast.
