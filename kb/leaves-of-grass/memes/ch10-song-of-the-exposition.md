# BOOK XIII: Song of the Exposition

[010-001]Song of the Exposition

[010-002]1

[010-003](Ah little recks the laborer,

[010-004]How near his work is holding him to [[god|God]],

[010-005]The loving Laborer through space and [[time]].)

[010-006]After all not to create only, or found only,

[010-007]But to bring perhaps from afar what is already founded,

[010-008]To give it our own identity, average, limitless, [[freedom|free]],

[010-009]To fill the gross the torpid bulk with vital religious fire,

[010-010]Not to repel or destroy so much as accept, fuse, rehabilitate,

[010-011]To obey as well as command, to follow more than to lead,

[010-012]These also are the lessons of our New World;

[010-013]While how little the New after all, how much the Old, Old World!

[010-014]Long and long has [[grass|the grass]] been growing,

[010-015]Long and long has the rain been falling,

[010-016]Long has the [[earth|globe]] been rolling round.

[010-017]2

[010-018]Come Muse migrate from Greece and Ionia,

[010-019]Cross out please those immensely overpaid accounts,

[010-020]That matter of Troy and Achilles’ wrath, and AEneas’, Odysseus’ wanderings,

[010-021]Placard “Removed” and “To Let” on the rocks of your snowy Parnassus,

[010-022]Repeat at Jerusalem, place the notice high on jaffa’s gate and on

[010-023]Mount Moriah,

[010-024]The same on the walls of your German, French and Spanish castles,

[010-025]and Italian collections,

[010-026]For know a better, fresher, busier sphere, a wide, untried domain

[010-027]awaits, demands you.

[010-028]3

[010-029]Responsive to our summons,

[010-030]Or rather to her long-nurs’d inclination,

[010-031]Join’d with an irresistible, natural gravitation,

[010-032]She comes! I hear the rustling of her gown,

[010-033]I scent the odor of her breath’s delicious fragrance,

[010-034]I mark her step divine, her curious eyes a-turning, rolling,

[010-035]Upon this very scene.

[010-036]The dame of dames! can I believe then,

[010-037]Those ancient temples, sculptures classic, could none of them retain her?

[010-038]Nor shades of Virgil and Dante, nor myriad memories, poems, old

[010-039]associations, magnetize and hold on to her?

[010-040]But that she’s left them all--and here?

[010-041]Yes, if you will allow me to say so,

[010-042]I, my friends, if you do not, can plainly see her,

[010-043]The same undying [[soul]] of earth’s, activity’s, beauty’s, heroism’s

[010-044]expression,

[010-045]Out from her evolutions hither come, ended the strata of her former themes,

[010-046]Hidden and cover’d by to-day’s, foundation of to-day’s,

[010-047]Ended, deceas’d through time, her voice by Castaly’s fountain,

[010-048]Silent the broken-lipp’d Sphynx in Egypt, silent all those century-

[010-049]baffling tombs,

[010-050]Ended for aye the epics of Asia’s, Europe’s helmeted warriors, ended

[010-051]the primitive call of the muses,

[010-052]Calliope’s call forever closed, Clio, Melpomene, Thalia dead,

[010-053]Ended the stately rhythmus of Una and Oriana, ended the quest of the

[010-054]holy Graal,

[010-055]Jerusalem a handful of ashes blown by the wind, extinct,

[010-056]The Crusaders’ streams of shadowy midnight troops sped with the sunrise,

[010-057]Amadis, Tancred, utterly gone, Charlemagne, Roland, Oliver gone,

[010-058]Palmerin, ogre, departed, vanish’d the turrets that Usk from its

[010-059][[sea|waters]] reflected,

[010-060]Arthur vanish’d with all his knights, Merlin and Lancelot and

[010-061]Galahad, all gone, dissolv’d utterly like an exhalation;

[010-062]Pass’d! pass’d! for us, forever pass’d, that once so mighty world,

[010-063]now void, inanimate, phantom world,

[010-064]Embroider’d, dazzling, foreign world, with all its gorgeous legends, myths,

[010-065]Its kings and castles proud, its priests and warlike lords and

[010-066]courtly dames,

[010-067]Pass’d to its charnel vault, coffin’d with crown and armor on,

[010-068]Blazon’d with Shakspere’s purple page,

[010-069]And dirged by Tennyson’s sweet sad rhyme.

[010-070]I say I see, my friends, if you do not, the illustrious emigre, (having it

[010-071]is true in her day, although the same, changed, journey’d considerable,)

[010-072]Making directly for this rendezvous, vigorously clearing a path for

[010-073]herself, striding through the confusion,

[010-074]By thud of machinery and shrill steam-whistle undismay’d,

[010-075]Bluff’d not a bit by drain-pipe, gasometers, artificial fertilizers,

[010-076]Smiling and pleas’d with palpable intent to stay,

[010-077]She’s here, install’d amid the kitchen ware!

[010-078]4

[010-079]But hold--don’t I forget my manners?

[010-080]To introduce the stranger, (what else indeed do I live to chant

[010-081]for?) to thee Columbia;

[010-082]In liberty’s name welcome immortal! clasp hands,

[010-083]And ever henceforth sisters dear be both.

[010-084]Fear not O Muse! truly new ways and days receive, surround you,

[010-085]I candidly confess a queer, queer race, of novel fashion,

[010-086]And yet the same old human race, the same within, without,

[010-087]Faces and hearts the same, feelings the same, yearnings the same,

[010-088]The same old [[love]], beauty and use the same.

[010-089]5

[010-090]We do not blame thee elder World, nor really separate ourselves from thee,

[010-091](Would the son separate himself from the father?)

[010-092]Looking back on thee, seeing thee to thy duties, grandeurs, through

[010-093]past ages bending, building,

[010-094]We build to ours to-day.

[010-095]Mightier than Egypt’s tombs,

[010-096]Fairer than Grecia’s, Roma’s temples,

[010-097]Prouder than Milan’s statued, spired cathedral,

[010-098]More picturesque than Rhenish castle-keeps,

[010-099]We plan even now to raise, beyond them all,

[010-100]Thy great cathedral sacred industry, no tomb,

[010-101]A keep for life for practical invention.

[010-102]As in a waking vision,

[010-103]E’en while I chant I see it rise, I scan and prophesy outside and in,

[010-104]Its manifold ensemble.

[010-105]Around a palace, loftier, fairer, ampler than any yet,

[010-106]Earth’s modern wonder, history’s seven outstripping,

[010-107]High rising tier on tier with glass and iron facades,

[010-108]Gladdening the sun and sky, enhued in cheerfulest hues,

[010-109]Bronze, lilac, robin’s-egg, marine and crimson,

[010-110]Over whose golden roof shall flaunt, beneath thy banner Freedom,

[010-111]The banners of [[america|the States]] and flags of every land,

[010-112]A brood of lofty, fair, but lesser palaces shall cluster.

[010-113]Somewhere within their walls shall all that forwards perfect human

[010-114]life be started,

[010-115]Tried, taught, advanced, visibly exhibited.

[010-116]Not only all the world of works, trade, products,

[010-117]But all the workmen of the world here to be represented.

[010-118]Here shall you trace in flowing operation,

[010-119]In every state of practical, busy movement, the rills of civilization,

[010-120]Materials here under your eye shall change their shape as if by magic,

[010-121]The cotton shall be pick’d almost in the very field,

[010-122]Shall be dried, clean’d, ginn’d, baled, spun into thread and cloth

[010-123]before you,

[010-124]You shall see hands at work at all the old processes and all the new ones,

[010-125]You shall see the various grains and how flour is made and then

[010-126]bread baked by the bakers,

[010-127]You shall see the crude ores of [[california|California]] and Nevada passing on and

[010-128]on till they become bullion,

[010-129]You shall watch how the printer sets type, and learn what a

[010-130]composing-stick is,

[010-131]You shall mark in amazement the Hoe press whirling its cylinders,

[010-132]shedding the printed leaves steady and fast,

[010-133]The photograph, model, watch, pin, nail, shall be created before you.

[010-134]In large calm halls, a stately museum shall teach you the infinite

[010-135]lessons of minerals,

[010-136]In another, woods, plants, vegetation shall be illustrated--in

[010-137]another animals, animal life and development.

[010-138]One stately house shall be the music house,

[010-139]Others for other arts--learning, the sciences, shall all be here,

[010-140]None shall be slighted, none but shall here be honor’d, help’d, exampled.

[010-141]6

[010-142](This, this and these, America, shall be your pyramids and obelisks,

[010-143]Your Alexandrian Pharos, gardens of Babylon,

[010-144]Your temple at Olympia.)

[010-145]The male and female many laboring not,

[010-146]Shall ever here confront the laboring many,

[010-147]With precious benefits to both, glory to all,

[010-148]To thee America, and thee eternal Muse.

[010-149]And here shall ye inhabit powerful Matrons!

[010-150]In your vast state vaster than all the old,

[010-151]Echoed through long, long centuries to come,

[010-152]To sound of different, prouder songs, with stronger themes,

[010-153]Practical, peaceful life, the people’s life, the People themselves,

[010-154]Lifted, illumin’d, bathed in peace--elate, secure in peace.

[010-155]7

[010-156]Away with themes of [[war]]! away with war itself!

[010-157]Hence from my shuddering sight to never more return that show of

[010-158]blacken’d, mutilated corpses!

[010-159]That hell unpent and raid of blood, fit for wild tigers or for

[010-160]lop-tongued wolves, not reasoning men,

[010-161]And in its stead speed industry’s campaigns,

[010-162]With thy undaunted armies, engineering,

[010-163]Thy pennants labor, loosen’d to the breeze,

[010-164]Thy bugles sounding loud and clear.

[010-165]Away with old romance!

[010-166]Away with novels, plots and plays of foreign courts,

[010-167]Away with love-verses sugar’d in rhyme, the intrigues, amours of idlers,

[010-168]Fitted for only banquets of [[night|the night]] where dancers to late music slide,

[010-169]The unhealthy pleasures, extravagant dissipations of the few,

[010-170]With perfumes, heat and wine, beneath the dazzling chandeliers.

[010-171]To you ye reverent sane sisters,

[010-172]I raise a voice for far superber themes for poets and for art,

[010-173]To exalt the present and the real,

[010-174]To teach the average man the glory of his daily walk and trade,

[010-175]To sing in songs how exercise and chemical life are never to be baffled,

[010-176]To manual work for each and all, to plough, hoe, dig,

[010-177]To plant and tend the tree, the berry, vegetables, flowers,

[010-178]For every man to see to it that he really do something, for every woman too;

[010-179]To use the hammer and the saw, (rip, or cross-cut,)

[010-180]To cultivate a turn for carpentering, plastering, painting,

[010-181]To work as tailor, tailoress, nurse, hostler, porter,

[010-182]To invent a little, something ingenious, to aid the washing, cooking,

[010-183]cleaning,

[010-184]And hold it no disgrace to take a hand at them themselves.

[010-185]I say I bring thee Muse to-day and here,

[010-186]All occupations, duties broad and close,

[010-187]Toil, healthy toil and sweat, endless, without cessation,

[010-188]The old, old practical burdens, interests, joys,

[010-189]The family, parentage, childhood, husband and wife,

[010-190]The house-comforts, the house itself and all its belongings,

[010-191]Food and its preservation, chemistry applied to it,

[010-192]Whatever forms the average, strong, complete, sweet-blooded man or

[010-193]woman, the perfect longeve personality,

[010-194]And helps its present life to health and [[joy|happiness]], and shapes its soul,

[010-195]For the eternal real life to come.

[010-196]With latest connections, works, the inter-transportation of the world,

[010-197]Steam-power, the great express lines, gas, petroleum,

[010-198]These triumphs of our time, the Atlantic’s delicate cable,

[010-199]The Pacific railroad, the Suez canal, the Mont Cenis and Gothard and

[010-200]Hoosac tunnels, the [[brooklyn|Brooklyn]] bridge,

[010-201]This earth all spann’d with iron rails, with lines of steamships

[010-202]threading in every sea,

[010-203]Our own rondure, the current globe I bring.

[010-204]8

[010-205]And thou America,

[010-206]Thy offspring towering e’er so high, yet higher Thee above all towering,

[010-207]With Victory on thy left, and at thy right hand Law;

[010-208]Thou Union holding all, fusing, absorbing, tolerating all,

[010-209]Thee, ever thee, I sing.

[010-210]Thou, also thou, a World,

[010-211]With all thy wide geographies, manifold, different, distant,

[010-212]Rounded by thee in one--one common orbic language,

[010-213]One common indivisible destiny for All.

[010-214]And by the spells which ye vouchsafe to those your ministers in earnest,

[010-215]I here personify and call my themes, to make them pass before ye.

[010-216]Behold, America! (and thou, ineffable guest and sister!)

[010-217]For thee come trooping up thy waters and thy lands;

[010-218]Behold! thy fields and farms, thy far-off woods and mountains,

[010-219]As in procession coming.

[010-220]Behold, the sea itself,

[010-221]And on its limitless, heaving breast, the ships;

[010-222]See, where their white sails, bellying in the wind, speckle the

[010-223]green and blue,

[010-224]See, the steamers coming and going, steaming in or out of port,

[010-225]See, dusky and undulating, the long pennants of smoke.

[010-226]Behold, in Oregon, far in the north and west,

[010-227]Or in Maine, far in the north and east, thy cheerful axemen,

[010-228]Wielding all day their axes.

[010-229]Behold, on the lakes, thy pilots at their wheels, thy oarsmen,

[010-230]How the ash writhes under those muscular arms!

[010-231]There by the furnace, and there by the anvil,

[010-232]Behold thy sturdy blacksmiths swinging their sledges,

[010-233]Overhand so steady, overhand they turn and fall with joyous clank,

[010-234]Like a tumult of laughter.

[010-235]Mark the spirit of invention everywhere, thy rapid patents,

[010-236]Thy continual workshops, foundries, risen or rising,

[010-237]See, from their chimneys how the tall flame-fires stream.

[010-238]Mark, thy interminable farms, North, South,

[010-239]Thy wealthy daughter-states, Eastern and Western,

[010-240]The varied products of Ohio, Pennsylvania, Missouri, Georgia, Texas,

[010-241]and the rest,

[010-242]Thy limitless crops, grass, wheat, sugar, oil, corn, rice, hemp, hops,

[010-243]Thy barns all fill’d, the endless freight-train and the bulging store-house,

[010-244]The grapes that ripen on thy vines, the apples in thy orchards,

[010-245]Thy incalculable lumber, beef, pork, potatoes, thy coal, thy gold

[010-246]and silver,

[010-247]The inexhaustible iron in thy mines.

[010-248]All thine O sacred Union!

[010-249]Ships, farms, shops, barns, factories, mines,

[010-250]City and State, North, South, item and aggregate,

[010-251]We dedicate, dread Mother, all to thee!

[010-252]Protectress absolute, thou! bulwark of all!

[010-253]For well we know that while thou givest each and all, (generous as God,)

[010-254]Without thee neither all nor each, nor land, home,

[010-255]Nor ship, nor mine, nor any here this day secure,

[010-256]Nor aught, nor any day secure.

[010-257]9

[010-258]And thou, the Emblem waving over all!

[010-259]Delicate beauty, a word to thee, (it may be salutary,)

[010-260]Remember thou hast not always been as here to-day so comfortably

[010-261]ensovereign’d,

[010-262]In other scenes than these have I observ’d thee flag,

[010-263]Not quite so trim and whole and freshly blooming in folds of

[010-264]stainless silk,

[010-265]But I have seen thee bunting, to tatters torn upon thy splinter’d staff,

[010-266]Or clutch’d to some young color-bearer’s breast with desperate hands,

[010-267]Savagely struggled for, for life or [[death]], fought over long,

[010-268]’Mid cannons’ thunder-crash and many a curse and groan and yell, and

[010-269]rifle-volleys cracking sharp,

[010-270]And moving masses as wild demons surging, and lives as nothing risk’d,

[010-271]For thy mere remnant grimed with dirt and smoke and sopp’d in blood,

[010-272]For sake of that, my beauty, and that thou might’st dally as now

[010-273]secure up there,

[010-274]Many a good man have I seen go under.

[010-275]Now here and these and hence in peace, all thine O Flag!

[010-276]And here and hence for thee, O universal Muse! and thou for them!

[010-277]And here and hence O Union, all the work and workmen thine!

[010-278]None separate from thee--henceforth One only, we and thou,

[010-279](For the blood of the children, what is it, only the blood maternal?

[010-280]And lives and works, what are they all at last, except the roads to

[010-281]faith and death?)

[010-282]While we rehearse our measureless wealth, it is for thee, dear Mother,

[010-283]We own it all and several to-day indissoluble in thee;

[010-284]Think not our chant, our show, merely for products gross or lucre--

[010-285]it is for thee, the soul in thee, electric, spiritual!

[010-286]Our farms, inventions, crops, we own in thee! cities and States in thee!

[010-287]Our freedom all in thee! our very lives in thee!
