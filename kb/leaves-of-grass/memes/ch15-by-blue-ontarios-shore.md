# BOOK XXIII: By Blue Ontario's Shore

[015-0001]By Blue Ontario’s Shore

[015-0002]By blue Ontario’s shore,

[015-0003]As I mused of these warlike days and of peace return’d, and the

[015-0004]dead that return no more,

[015-0005]A Phantom gigantic superb, with stern visage accosted me,

[015-0006]Chant me the poem, it said, that comes from [[soul|the soul]] of [[america|America]],

[015-0007]chant me the carol of victory,

[015-0008]And strike up the marches of Libertad, marches more powerful yet,

[015-0009]And sing me before you go the song of the throes of Democracy.

[015-0010](Democracy, the destin’d conqueror, yet treacherous lip-smiles everywhere,

[015-0011]And [[death]] and infidelity at every step.)

[015-0012]2

[015-0013]A Nation announcing itself,

[015-0014]I [[self|myself]] make the only growth by which I can be appreciated,

[015-0015]I reject none, accept all, then reproduce all in my own forms.

[015-0016]A breed whose proof is in [[time]] and deeds,

[015-0017]What we are we are, nativity is answer enough to objections,

[015-0018]We wield ourselves as a weapon is wielded,

[015-0019]We are powerful and tremendous in ourselves,

[015-0020]We are executive in ourselves, we are sufficient in the variety of

[015-0021]ourselves,

[015-0022]We are the most beautiful to ourselves and in ourselves,

[015-0023]We stand self-pois’d in the middle, branching thence over the [[earth|world]],

[015-0024]From Missouri, Nebraska, or Kansas, laughing attacks to scorn.

[015-0025]Nothing is sinful to us outside of ourselves,

[015-0026]Whatever appears, whatever does not appear, we are beautiful or

[015-0027]sinful in ourselves only.

[015-0028](O Mother--O Sisters dear!

[015-0029]If we are lost, no victor else has destroy’d us,

[015-0030]It is by ourselves we go down to eternal [[night]].)

[015-0031]3

[015-0032]Have you thought there could be but a single supreme?

[015-0033]There can be any number of supremes--one does not countervail

[015-0034]another any more than one eyesight countervails another, or

[015-0035]one life countervails another.

[015-0036]All is eligible to all,

[015-0037]All is for individuals, all is for you,

[015-0038]No condition is prohibited, not [[god|God]]’s or any.

[015-0039]All comes by [[body|the body]], only health puts you rapport with the universe.

[015-0040]Produce great Persons, the rest follows.

[015-0041]4

[015-0042]Piety and conformity to them that like,

[015-0043]Peace, obesity, allegiance, to them that like,

[015-0044]I am he who tauntingly compels men, women, nations,

[015-0045]Crying, Leap from your seats and contend for your lives!

[015-0046]I am he who walks the States with a barb’d tongue, questioning every

[015-0047]one I meet,

[015-0048]Who are you that wanted only to be told what you knew before?

[015-0049]Who are you that wanted only a book to join you in your nonsense?

[015-0050](With pangs and cries as thine own O bearer of many children,

[015-0051]These clamors wild to a race of pride I give.)

[015-0052]O lands, would you be freer than all that has ever been before?

[015-0053]If you would be freer than all that has been before, come listen to me.

[015-0054]Fear grace, elegance, civilization, delicatesse,

[015-0055]Fear the mellow sweet, the sucking of honey--juice,

[015-0056]Beware the advancing mortal ripening of Nature,

[015-0057]Beware what precedes the decay of the ruggedness of states and men.

[015-0058]5

[015-0059]Ages, precedents, have long been accumulating undirected materials,

[015-0060]America brings builders, and brings its own styles.

[015-0061]The immortal poets of Asia and Europe have done their work and

[015-0062]pass’d to other spheres,

[015-0063]A work remains, the work of surpassing all they have done.

[015-0064]America, curious toward foreign characters, stands by its own at all

[015-0065]hazards,

[015-0066]Stands removed, spacious, composite, sound, initiates the true use

[015-0067]of precedents,

[015-0068]Does not repel them or the past or what they have produced under

[015-0069]their forms,

[015-0070]Takes the lesson with calmness, perceives the corpse slowly borne

[015-0071]from the house,

[015-0072]Perceives that it waits a little while in the door, that it was

[015-0073]fittest for its days,

[015-0074]That its life has descended to the stalwart and well-shaped heir who

[015-0075]approaches,

[015-0076]And that he shall be fittest for his days.

[015-0077]Any period one nation must lead,

[015-0078]One land must be the promise and reliance of the future.

[015-0079]These States are the amplest poem,

[015-0080]Here is not merely a nation but a teeming Nation of nations,

[015-0081]Here the doings of men correspond with the broadcast doings of the

[015-0082]day and night,

[015-0083]Here is what moves in magnificent masses careless of particulars,

[015-0084]Here are the roughs, beards, friendliness, combativeness, the soul loves,

[015-0085]Here the flowing trains, here the crowds, equality, diversity, the

[015-0086]soul loves.

[015-0087]6

[015-0088]Land of lands and bards to corroborate!

[015-0089]Of them standing among them, one lifts to the light a west-bred face,

[015-0090]To him the hereditary countenance bequeath’d both [[mother]]’s and father’s,

[015-0091]His first parts substances, earth, water, animals, trees,

[015-0092]Built of the common stock, having room for far and near,

[015-0093]Used to dispense with other lands, incarnating this land,

[015-0094]Attracting it body and soul to himself, hanging on its neck with

[015-0095]incomparable [[love]],

[015-0096]Plunging his seminal muscle into its merits and demerits,

[015-0097]Making its cities, beginnings, events, diversities, wars, vocal in him,

[015-0098]Making its rivers, lakes, bays, embouchure in him,

[015-0099]Mississippi with yearly freshets and changing chutes, Columbia,

[015-0100][[niagara|Niagara]], Hudson, spending themselves lovingly in him,

[015-0101]If the Atlantic coast stretch or the Pacific coast stretch, he

[015-0102]stretching with them North or South,

[015-0103]Spanning between them East and West, and touching whatever is between them,

[015-0104]Growths growing from him to offset the growths of pine, cedar, hemlock,

[015-0105]live-oak, locust, chestnut, hickory, cottonwood, orange, magnolia,

[015-0106]Tangles as tangled in him as any canebrake or swamp,

[015-0107]He likening sides and peaks of mountains, forests coated with

[015-0108]northern transparent ice,

[015-0109]Off him pasturage sweet and natural as savanna, upland, [[prairie]],

[015-0110]Through him flights, whirls, screams, answering those of the

[015-0111]fish-hawk, mocking-bird, night-heron, and eagle,

[015-0112]His spirit surrounding his country’s spirit, unclosed to good and evil,

[015-0113]Surrounding the essences of real things, old times and present times,

[015-0114]Surrounding just found shores, islands, tribes of red aborigines,

[015-0115]Weather-beaten vessels, landings, settlements, embryo stature and muscle,

[015-0116]The haughty defiance of the Year One, [[war]], peace, the formation of

[015-0117]the Constitution,

[015-0118]The separate States, the simple elastic scheme, the [[immigrant|immigrants]],

[015-0119]The Union always swarming with blatherers and always sure and impregnable,

[015-0120]The unsurvey’d interior, log-houses, clearings, wild animals,

[015-0121]hunters, trappers,

[015-0122]Surrounding the multiform agriculture, mines, temperature, the

[015-0123]gestation of new States,

[015-0124]Congress convening every Twelfth-month, the members duly coming

[015-0125]up from the uttermost parts,

[015-0126]Surrounding the noble character of [[mechanic|mechanics]] and farmers, especially

[015-0127]the young men,

[015-0128]Responding their manners, speech, dress, friendships, the gait they

[015-0129]have of persons who never knew how it felt to stand in the

[015-0130]presence of superiors,

[015-0131]The freshness and candor of their physiognomy, the copiousness and

[015-0132]decision of their phrenology,

[015-0133]The picturesque looseness of their carriage, their fierceness when wrong’d,

[015-0134]The fluency of their speech, their [[joy|delight]] in music, their curiosity,

[015-0135]good temper and open-handedness, the whole composite make,

[015-0136]The prevailing ardor and enterprise, the large amativeness,

[015-0137]The perfect equality of the female with the male, the fluid movement

[015-0138]of the population,

[015-0139]The superior marine, [[freedom|free]] commerce, fisheries, whaling, gold-digging,

[015-0140]Wharf-hemm’d cities, railroad and steamboat lines intersecting all points,

[015-0141]Factories, mercantile life, labor-saving machinery, the Northeast,

[015-0142]Northwest, Southwest,

[015-0143]Manhattan firemen, the Yankee swap, southern plantation life,

[015-0144]Slavery--the murderous, treacherous conspiracy to raise it upon the

[015-0145]ruins of all the rest,

[015-0146]On and on to the grapple with it--Assassin! then your life or ours

[015-0147]be the stake, and respite no more.

[015-0148]7

[015-0149](Lo, high toward heaven, this day,

[015-0150]Libertad, from the conqueress’ field return’d,

[015-0151]I mark the new aureola around your head,

[015-0152]No more of soft astral, but dazzling and fierce,

[015-0153]With war’s flames and the lambent lightnings playing,

[015-0154]And your port immovable where you stand,

[015-0155]With still the inextinguishable glance and the clinch’d and lifted fist,

[015-0156]And your foot on the neck of the menacing one, the scorner utterly

[015-0157]crush’d beneath you,

[015-0158]The menacing arrogant one that strode and advanced with his

[015-0159]senseless scorn, bearing the murderous knife,

[015-0160]The wide-swelling one, the braggart that would yesterday do so much,

[015-0161]To-day a carrion dead and damn’d, the despised of all the earth,

[015-0162]An offal rank, to the dunghill maggots spurn’d.)

[015-0163]8

[015-0164]Others take finish, but the Republic is ever constructive and ever

[015-0165]keeps vista,

[015-0166]Others adorn the past, but you O days of the present, I adorn you,

[015-0167]O days of the future I believe in you--I isolate myself for your sake,

[015-0168]O America because you build for mankind I build for you,

[015-0169]O well-beloved stone-cutters, I lead them who plan with decision

[015-0170]and science,

[015-0171]Lead the present with friendly hand toward the future.

[015-0172](Bravas to all impulses sending sane children to the next age!

[015-0173]But damn that which spends itself with no thought of the stain,

[015-0174]pains, dismay, feebleness, it is bequeathing.)

[015-0175]9

[015-0176]I listened to the Phantom by Ontario’s shore,

[015-0177]I heard the voice arising demanding bards,

[015-0178]By them all native and grand, by them alone can these States be

[015-0179]fused into the compact organism of a Nation.

[015-0180]To hold men together by paper and seal or by compulsion is no account,

[015-0181]That only holds men together which aggregates all in a living principle,

[015-0182]as the hold of the limbs of the body or the fibres of plants.

[015-0183]Of all races and eras these States with veins full of poetical stuff most

[015-0184]need poets, and are to have the greatest, and use them the greatest,

[015-0185]Their Presidents shall not be their common referee so much as their

[015-0186]poets shall.

[015-0187](Soul of love and tongue of fire!

[015-0188]Eye to pierce the deepest deeps and sweep the world!

[015-0189]Ah Mother, prolific and full in all besides, yet how long barren, barren?)

[015-0190]10

[015-0191]Of these States the poet is the equable man,

[015-0192]Not in him but off from him things are grotesque, eccentric, fail of

[015-0193]their full returns,

[015-0194]Nothing out of its place is good, nothing in its place is bad,

[015-0195]He bestows on every object or quality its fit proportion, neither

[015-0196]more nor less,

[015-0197]He is the arbiter of the diverse, he is the key,

[015-0198]He is the equalizer of his age and land,

[015-0199]He supplies what wants supplying, he checks what wants checking,

[015-0200]In peace out of him speaks the spirit of peace, large, rich,

[015-0201]thrifty, building populous towns, encouraging agriculture, arts,

[015-0202]commerce, lighting the study of man, the soul, health,

[015-0203][[immortality]], government,

[015-0204]In war he is the best backer of the war, he fetches artillery as

[015-0205]good as the engineer’s, he can make every word he speaks draw blood,

[015-0206]The years straying toward infidelity he withholds by his steady faith,

[015-0207]He is no arguer, he is judgment, (Nature accepts him absolutely,)

[015-0208]He judges not as the judge judges but as the sun failing round

[015-0209]helpless thing,

[015-0210]As he sees the farthest he has the most faith,

[015-0211]His thoughts are the hymns of the praise of things,

[015-0212]In the dispute on God and eternity he is silent,

[015-0213]He sees eternity less like a play with a prologue and denouement,

[015-0214]He sees eternity in men and women, he does not see men and women

[015-0215]as dreams or dots.

[015-0216]For the great Idea, the idea of perfect and free individuals,

[015-0217]For that, the bard walks in advance, leader of leaders,

[015-0218]The attitude of him cheers up slaves and horrifies foreign despots.

[015-0219]Without extinction is Liberty, without retrograde is Equality,

[015-0220]They live in the feelings of young men and the best women,

[015-0221](Not for nothing have the indomitable heads of the earth been always

[015-0222]ready to fall for Liberty.)

[015-0223]11

[015-0224]For the great Idea,

[015-0225]That, O my brethren, that is the mission of poets.

[015-0226]Songs of stern defiance ever ready,

[015-0227]Songs of the rapid arming and the march,

[015-0228]The flag of peace quick-folded, and instead the flag we know,

[015-0229]Warlike flag of the great Idea.

[015-0230](Angry cloth I saw there leaping!

[015-0231]I stand again in leaden rain your flapping folds saluting,

[015-0232]I sing you over all, flying beckoning through the fight--O the

[015-0233]hard-contested fight!

[015-0234]The cannons ope their rosy-flashing muzzles--the hurtled balls scream,

[015-0235]The battle-front forms amid the smoke--the volleys pour incessant

[015-0236]from the line,

[015-0237]Hark, the ringing word Charge!--now the tussle and the furious

[015-0238]maddening yells,

[015-0239]Now the corpses tumble curl’d upon the ground,

[015-0240]Cold, cold in death, for precious life of you,

[015-0241]Angry cloth I saw there leaping.)

[015-0242]12

[015-0243]Are you he who would assume a place to teach or be a poet here in

[015-0244]the States?

[015-0245]The place is august, the terms obdurate.

[015-0246]Who would assume to teach here may well prepare himself body and mind,

[015-0247]He may well survey, ponder, arm, fortify, harden, make lithe himself,

[015-0248]He shall surely be question’d beforehand by me with many and stern questions.

[015-0249]Who are you indeed who would talk or sing to America?

[015-0250]Have you studied out the land, its idioms and men?

[015-0251]Have you learn’d the physiology, phrenology, politics, geography,

[015-0252]pride, freedom, friendship of the land? its substratums and objects?

[015-0253]Have you consider’d the organic compact of the first day of the

[015-0254]first year of Independence, sign’d by the Commissioners, ratified

[015-0255]by the States, and read by Washington at the head of the army?

[015-0256]Have you possess’d yourself of the Federal Constitution?

[015-0257]Do you see who have left all feudal processes and poems behind them,

[015-0258]and assumed the poems and processes of Democracy?

[015-0259]Are you faithful to things? do you teach what the land and [[sea]], the

[015-0260]bodies of men, womanhood, amativeness, heroic angers, teach?

[015-0261]Have you sped through fleeting customs, popularities?

[015-0262]Can you hold your hand against all seductions, follies, whirls,

[015-0263]fierce contentions? are you very strong? are you really of the

[015-0264]whole People?

[015-0265]Are you not of some coterie? some school or mere religion?

[015-0266]Are you done with reviews and criticisms of life? animating now to

[015-0267]life itself?

[015-0268]Have you vivified yourself from the maternity of these States?

[015-0269]Have you too the old ever-fresh forbearance and impartiality?

[015-0270]Do you hold the like love for those hardening to maturity? for the

[015-0271]last-born? little and big? and for the errant?

[015-0272]What is this you bring my America?

[015-0273]Is it uniform with my country?

[015-0274]Is it not something that has been better told or done before?

[015-0275]Have you not imported this or the spirit of it in some ship?

[015-0276]Is it not a mere tale? a rhyme? a prettiness?--Is the good old cause in it?

[015-0277]Has it not dangled long at the heels of the poets, politicians,

[015-0278]literats, of enemies’ lands?

[015-0279]Does it not assume that what is notoriously gone is still here?

[015-0280]Does it answer universal needs? will it improve manners?

[015-0281]Does it sound with trumpet-voice the proud victory of the Union in

[015-0282]that secession war?

[015-0283]Can your performance face the open fields and the seaside?

[015-0284]Will it absorb into me as I absorb food, air, to appear again in my

[015-0285]strength, gait, face?

[015-0286]Have real employments contributed to it? original makers, not mere

[015-0287]amanuenses?

[015-0288]Does it meet modern discoveries, calibres, facts, face to face?

[015-0289]What does it mean to American persons, progresses, cities? [[chicago|Chicago]],

[015-0290]Kanada, Arkansas?

[015-0291]Does it see behind the apparent custodians the real custodians

[015-0292]standing, menacing, silent, the mechanics, Manhattanese, Western

[015-0293]men, Southerners, significant alike in their apathy, and in the

[015-0294]promptness of their love?

[015-0295]Does it see what finally befalls, and has always finally befallen,

[015-0296]each temporizer, patcher, outsider, partialist, alarmist,

[015-0297]infidel, who has ever ask’d any thing of America?

[015-0298]What mocking and scornful negligence?

[015-0299]The track strew’d with the dust of skeletons,

[015-0300]By the roadside others disdainfully toss’d.

[015-0301]13

[015-0302]Rhymes and rhymers pass away, poems distill’d from poems pass away,

[015-0303]The swarms of reflectors and the polite pass, and leave ashes,

[015-0304]Admirers, importers, obedient persons, make but the soil of literature,

[015-0305]America justifies itself, give it time, no disguise can deceive it

[015-0306]or conceal from it, it is impassive enough,

[015-0307]Only toward the likes of itself will it advance to meet them,

[015-0308]If its poets appear it will in due time advance to meet them, there

[015-0309]is no fear of mistake,

[015-0310](The proof of a poet shall be sternly deferr’d till his country

[015-0311]absorbs him as affectionately as he has absorb’d it.)

[015-0312]He masters whose spirit masters, he tastes sweetest who results

[015-0313]sweetest in the long run,

[015-0314]The blood of the brawn beloved of time is unconstraint;

[015-0315]In the need of songs, philosophy, an appropriate native grand-opera,

[015-0316]shipcraft, any craft,

[015-0317]He or she is greatest who contributes the greatest original

[015-0318]practical example.

[015-0319]Already a nonchalant breed, silently emerging, appears on the streets,

[015-0320]People’s lips salute only doers, lovers, satisfiers, positive knowers,

[015-0321]There will shortly be no more priests, I say their work is done,

[015-0322]Death is without emergencies here, but life is perpetual emergencies here,

[015-0323]Are your body, days, manners, superb? after death you shall be superb,

[015-0324]Justice, health, self-esteem, clear the way with irresistible power;

[015-0325]How dare you place any thing before a man?

[015-0326]14

[015-0327]Fall behind me States!

[015-0328]A man before all--myself, typical, before all.

[015-0329]Give me the pay I have served for,

[015-0330]Give me to sing the songs of the great Idea, take all the rest,

[015-0331]I have loved the earth, sun, animals, I have despised riches,

[015-0332]I have given aims to every one that ask’d, stood up for the stupid

[015-0333]and crazy, devoted my income and labor to others,

[015-0334]Hated tyrants, argued not concerning God, had patience and indulgence

[015-0335]toward the people, taken off my hat to nothing known or unknown,

[015-0336]Gone freely with powerful uneducated persons and with the young,

[015-0337]and with the mothers of families,

[015-0338]Read these leaves to myself in the open air, tried them by trees,

[015-0339]stars, rivers,

[015-0340]Dismiss’d whatever insulted my own soul or defiled my body,

[015-0341]Claim’d nothing to myself which I have not carefully claim’d for

[015-0342]others on the same terms,

[015-0343]Sped to the camps, and comrades found and accepted from every State,

[015-0344](Upon this breast has many a dying [[soldier]] lean’d to breathe his last,

[015-0345]This arm, this hand, this voice, have nourish’d, rais’d, restored,

[015-0346]To life recalling many a prostrate form;)

[015-0347]I am willing to wait to be understood by the growth of the taste of myself,

[015-0348]Rejecting none, permitting all.

[015-0349](Say O Mother, have I not to your thought been faithful?

[015-0350]Have I not through life kept you and yours before me?)

[015-0351]15

[015-0352]I swear I begin to see the meaning of these things,

[015-0353]It is not the earth, it is not America who is so great,

[015-0354]It is I who am great or to be great, it is You up there, or any one,

[015-0355]It is to walk rapidly through civilizations, governments, theories,

[015-0356]Through poems, pageants, shows, to form individuals.

[015-0357]Underneath all, individuals,

[015-0358]I swear nothing is good to me now that ignores individuals,

[015-0359]The American compact is altogether with individuals,

[015-0360]The only government is that which makes minute of individuals,

[015-0361]The whole theory of the universe is directed unerringly to one

[015-0362]single individual--namely to You.

[015-0363](Mother! with subtle sense severe, with the naked sword in your hand,

[015-0364]I saw you at last refuse to treat but directly with individuals.)

[015-0365]16

[015-0366]Underneath all, Nativity,

[015-0367]I swear I will stand by my own nativity, pious or impious so be it;

[015-0368]I swear I am charm’d with nothing except nativity,

[015-0369]Men, women, cities, nations, are only beautiful from nativity.

[015-0370]Underneath all is the Expression of love for men and women,

[015-0371](I swear I have seen enough of mean and impotent modes of expressing

[015-0372]love for men and women,

[015-0373]After this day I take my own modes of expressing love for men and

[015-0374]women.) in myself,

[015-0375]I swear I will have each quality of my race in myself,

[015-0376](Talk as you like, he only suits these States whose manners favor

[015-0377]the audacity and sublime turbulence of the States.)

[015-0378]Underneath the lessons of things, spirits, Nature, governments,

[015-0379]ownerships, I swear I perceive other lessons,

[015-0380]Underneath all to me is myself, to you yourself, (the same

[015-0381]monotonous old song.)

[015-0382]17

[015-0383]O I see flashing that this America is only you and me,

[015-0384]Its power, weapons, testimony, are you and me,

[015-0385]Its crimes, lies, thefts, defections, are you and me,

[015-0386]Its Congress is you and me, the officers, capitols, armies, ships,

[015-0387]are you and me,

[015-0388]Its endless gestations of new States are you and me,

[015-0389]The war, (that war so bloody and grim, the war I will henceforth

[015-0390]forget), was you and me,

[015-0391]Natural and artificial are you and me,

[015-0392]Freedom, language, poems, employments, are you and me,

[015-0393]Past, present, future, are you and me.

[015-0394]I dare not shirk any part of myself,

[015-0395]Not any part of America good or bad,

[015-0396]Not to build for that which builds for mankind,

[015-0397]Not to balance ranks, complexions, creeds, and the sexes,

[015-0398]Not to justify science nor the march of equality,

[015-0399]Nor to feed the arrogant blood of the brawn belov’d of time.

[015-0400]I am for those that have never been master’d,

[015-0401]For men and women whose tempers have never been master’d,

[015-0402]For those whom laws, theories, conventions, can never master.

[015-0403]I am for those who walk abreast with the whole earth,

[015-0404]Who inaugurate one to inaugurate all.
