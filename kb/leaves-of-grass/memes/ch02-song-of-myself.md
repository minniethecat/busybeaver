# BOOK III: Song of Myself

[002-0001]Song of Myself

[002-0002]1

[002-0003]I celebrate [[self|myself]], and sing myself,

[002-0004]And what I assume you shall assume,

[002-0005]For every atom belonging to me as good belongs to you.

[002-0006]I loafe and invite my [[soul]],

[002-0007]I lean and loafe at my ease observing a spear of summer [[grass]].

[002-0008]My tongue, every atom of my blood, form’d from this [[earth|soil]], this air,

[002-0009]Born here of parents born here from parents the same, and their

[002-0010]parents the same,

[002-0011]I, now thirty-seven years old in perfect health begin,

[002-0012]Hoping to cease not till [[death]].

[002-0013]Creeds and schools in abeyance,

[002-0014]Retiring back a while sufficed at what they are, but never forgotten,

[002-0015]I harbor for good or bad, I permit to speak at every hazard,

[002-0016]Nature without check with original energy.

[002-0017]2

[002-0018]Houses and rooms are full of perfumes, the shelves are crowded with

[002-0019]perfumes,

[002-0020]I breathe the fragrance myself and know it and like it,

[002-0021]The distillation would intoxicate me also, but I shall not let it.

[002-0022]The atmosphere is not a perfume, it has no taste of the

[002-0023]distillation, it is odorless,

[002-0024]It is for my mouth forever, I am in [[love]] with it,

[002-0025]I will go to the bank by the wood and become undisguised and naked,

[002-0026]I am mad for it to be in contact with me.

[002-0027]The smoke of my own breath,

[002-0028]Echoes, ripples, buzz’d whispers, love-root, silk-thread, crotch and vine,

[002-0029]My respiration and inspiration, the beating of my heart, the passing

[002-0030]of blood and air through my lungs,

[002-0031]The sniff of green leaves and dry leaves, and of the shore and

[002-0032]dark-color’d [[sea]]-rocks, and of hay in the barn,

[002-0033]The sound of the belch’d words of my voice loos’d to the eddies of

[002-0034]the wind,

[002-0035]A few light kisses, a few embraces, a reaching around of arms,

[002-0036]The play of shine and shade on the trees as the supple boughs wag,

[002-0037]The [[joy|delight]] alone or in the rush of the streets, or along the fields

[002-0038]and hill-sides,

[002-0039]The feeling of health, the full-noon trill, the song of me rising

[002-0040]from bed and meeting the sun.

[002-0041]Have you reckon’d a thousand acres much? have you reckon’d the earth much?

[002-0042]Have you practis’d so long to learn to read?

[002-0043]Have you felt so proud to get at the meaning of poems?

[002-0044]Stop this day and [[night]] with me and you shall possess the origin of

[002-0045]all poems,

[002-0046]You shall possess the good of the earth and sun, (there are millions

[002-0047]of suns left,)

[002-0048]You shall no longer take things at second or third hand, nor look through

[002-0049]the eyes of the dead, nor feed on the spectres in books,

[002-0050]You shall not look through my eyes either, nor take things from me,

[002-0051]You shall listen to all sides and filter them from your self.

[002-0052]3

[002-0053]I have heard what the talkers were talking, the talk of the

[002-0054]beginning and the end,

[002-0055]But I do not talk of the beginning or the end.

[002-0056]There was never any more inception than there is now,

[002-0057]Nor any more youth or age than there is now,

[002-0058]And will never be any more perfection than there is now,

[002-0059]Nor any more heaven or hell than there is now.

[002-0060]Urge and urge and urge,

[002-0061]Always the procreant urge of the world.

[002-0062]Out of the dimness opposite equals advance, always substance and

[002-0063]increase, always sex,

[002-0064]Always a knit of identity, always distinction, always a breed of life.

[002-0065]To elaborate is no avail, learn’d and unlearn’d feel that it is so.

[002-0066]Sure as the most certain sure, plumb in the uprights, well

[002-0067]entretied, braced in the beams,

[002-0068]Stout as a horse, affectionate, haughty, electrical,

[002-0069]I and this mystery here we stand.

[002-0070]Clear and sweet is my soul, and clear and sweet is all that is not my soul.

[002-0071]Lack one lacks both, and the unseen is proved by the seen,

[002-0072]Till that becomes unseen and receives proof in its turn.

[002-0073]Showing the best and dividing it from the worst age vexes age,

[002-0074]Knowing the perfect fitness and equanimity of things, while they

[002-0075]discuss I am silent, and go bathe and admire myself.

[002-0076]Welcome is every organ and attribute of me, and of any man hearty and clean,

[002-0077]Not an inch nor a particle of an inch is vile, and none shall be

[002-0078]less familiar than the rest.

[002-0079]I am satisfied--I see, dance, laugh, sing;

[002-0080]As the hugging and loving bed-fellow sleeps at my side through the night,

[002-0081]and withdraws at the peep of the day with stealthy tread,

[002-0082]Leaving me baskets cover’d with white towels swelling the house with

[002-0083]their plenty,

[002-0084]Shall I postpone my acceptation and realization and scream at my eyes,

[002-0085]That they turn from gazing after and down the road,

[002-0086]And forthwith cipher and show me to a cent,

[002-0087]Exactly the value of one and exactly the value of two, and which is ahead?

[002-0088]4

[002-0089]Trippers and askers surround me,

[002-0090]People I meet, the effect upon me of my early life or the ward and

[002-0091]city I live in, or the nation,

[002-0092]The latest dates, discoveries, inventions, societies, authors old and new,

[002-0093]My dinner, dress, associates, looks, compliments, dues,

[002-0094]The real or fancied indifference of some man or woman I love,

[002-0095]The sickness of one of my folks or of myself, or ill-doing or loss

[002-0096]or lack of money, or depressions or exaltations,

[002-0097]Battles, the horrors of fratricidal [[war]], the fever of doubtful news,

[002-0098]the fitful events;

[002-0099]These come to me days and nights and go from me again,

[002-0100]But they are not the Me myself.

[002-0101]Apart from the pulling and hauling stands what I am,

[002-0102]Stands amused, complacent, compassionating, idle, unitary,

[002-0103]Looks down, is erect, or bends an arm on an impalpable certain rest,

[002-0104]Looking with side-curved head curious what will come next,

[002-0105]Both in and out of the game and watching and wondering at it.

[002-0106]Backward I see in my own days where I sweated through fog with

[002-0107]linguists and contenders,

[002-0108]I have no mockings or arguments, I witness and wait.

[002-0109]5

[002-0110]I believe in you my soul, the other I am must not abase itself to you,

[002-0111]And you must not be abased to the other.

[002-0112]Loafe with me on the grass, loose the stop from your throat,

[002-0113]Not words, not music or rhyme I want, not custom or lecture, not

[002-0114]even the best,

[002-0115]Only the lull I like, the hum of your valved voice.

[002-0116]I mind how once we lay such a transparent summer morning,

[002-0117]How you settled your head athwart my hips and gently turn’d over upon me,

[002-0118]And parted the shirt from my bosom-bone, and plunged your tongue

[002-0119]to my bare-stript heart,

[002-0120]And reach’d till you felt my beard, and reach’d till you held my feet.

[002-0121]Swiftly arose and spread around me the peace and knowledge that pass

[002-0122]all the argument of the earth,

[002-0123]And I know that the hand of [[god|God]] is the promise of my own,

[002-0124]And I know that the spirit of God is the brother of my own,

[002-0125]And that all the men ever born are also my brothers, and the women

[002-0126]my sisters and lovers,

[002-0127]And that a kelson of the creation is love,

[002-0128]And limitless are leaves stiff or drooping in the fields,

[002-0129]And brown ants in the little wells beneath them,

[002-0130]And mossy scabs of the worm fence, heap’d stones, elder, mullein and

[002-0131]poke-weed.

[002-0132]6

[002-0133]A child said What is the grass? fetching it to me with full hands;

[002-0134]How could I answer the child? I do not know what it is any more than he.

[002-0135]I guess it must be the flag of my disposition, out of hopeful green

[002-0136]stuff woven.

[002-0137]Or I guess it is the handkerchief of the Lord,

[002-0138]A scented gift and remembrancer designedly dropt,

[002-0139]Bearing the owner’s name someway in the corners, that we may see

[002-0140]and remark, and say Whose?

[002-0141]Or I guess the grass is itself a child, the produced babe of the vegetation.

[002-0142]Or I guess it is a uniform hieroglyphic,

[002-0143]And it means, Sprouting alike in broad zones and narrow zones,

[002-0144]Growing among black folks as among white,

[002-0145]Kanuck, Tuckahoe, Congressman, Cuff, I give them the same, I

[002-0146]receive them the same.

[002-0147]And now it seems to me the beautiful uncut hair of graves.

[002-0148]Tenderly will I use you curling grass,

[002-0149]It may be you transpire from the breasts of young men,

[002-0150]It may be if I had known them I would have loved them,

[002-0151]It may be you are from old people, or from offspring taken soon out

[002-0152]of their [[mother|mothers]]’ laps,

[002-0153]And here you are the mothers’ laps.

[002-0154]This grass is very dark to be from the white heads of old mothers,

[002-0155]Darker than the colorless beards of old men,

[002-0156]Dark to come from under the faint red roofs of mouths.

[002-0157]O I perceive after all so many uttering tongues,

[002-0158]And I perceive they do not come from the roofs of mouths for nothing.

[002-0159]I wish I could translate the hints about the dead young men and women,

[002-0160]And the hints about old men and mothers, and the offspring taken

[002-0161]soon out of their laps.

[002-0162]What do you think has become of the young and old men?

[002-0163]And what do you think has become of the women and children?

[002-0164]They are alive and well somewhere,

[002-0165]The smallest sprout shows there is really no death,

[002-0166]And if ever there was it led forward life, and does not wait at the

[002-0167]end to arrest it,

[002-0168]And ceas’d [[time|the moment]] life appear’d.

[002-0169]All goes onward and outward, nothing collapses,

[002-0170]And to die is different from what any one supposed, and luckier.

[002-0171]7

[002-0172]Has any one supposed it lucky to be born?

[002-0173]I hasten to inform him or her it is just as lucky to die, and I know it.

[002-0174]I pass death with the dying and birth with the new-wash’d babe, and

[002-0175]am not contain’d between my hat and boots,

[002-0176]And peruse manifold objects, no two alike and every one good,

[002-0177]The earth good and the stars good, and their adjuncts all good.

[002-0178]I am not an earth nor an adjunct of an earth,

[002-0179]I am the mate and companion of people, all just as immortal and

[002-0180]fathomless as myself,

[002-0181](They do not know how immortal, but I know.)

[002-0182]Every kind for itself and its own, for me mine male and female,

[002-0183]For me those that have been boys and that love women,

[002-0184]For me the man that is proud and feels how it stings to be slighted,

[002-0185]For me the sweet-heart and the old maid, for me mothers and the

[002-0186]mothers of mothers,

[002-0187]For me lips that have smiled, eyes that have shed tears,

[002-0188]For me children and the begetters of children.

[002-0189]Undrape! you are not guilty to me, nor stale nor discarded,

[002-0190]I see through the broadcloth and gingham whether or no,

[002-0191]And am around, tenacious, acquisitive, tireless, and cannot be shaken away.

[002-0192]8

[002-0193]The little one sleeps in its cradle,

[002-0194]I lift the gauze and look a long time, and silently brush away flies

[002-0195]with my hand.

[002-0196]The youngster and the red-faced girl turn aside up the bushy hill,

[002-0197]I peeringly view them from the top.

[002-0198]The suicide sprawls on the bloody floor of the bedroom,

[002-0199]I witness the corpse with its dabbled hair, I note where the pistol

[002-0200]has fallen.

[002-0201]The blab of the pave, tires of carts, sluff of boot-soles, talk of

[002-0202]the promenaders,

[002-0203]The heavy omnibus, the driver with his interrogating thumb, the

[002-0204]clank of the shod horses on the granite floor,

[002-0205]The snow-sleighs, clinking, shouted jokes, pelts of snow-balls,

[002-0206]The hurrahs for popular favorites, the fury of rous’d mobs,

[002-0207]The flap of the curtain’d litter, a sick man inside borne to the hospital,

[002-0208]The meeting of enemies, the sudden oath, the blows and fall,

[002-0209]The excited crowd, the policeman with his star quickly working his

[002-0210]passage to the centre of the crowd,

[002-0211]The impassive stones that receive and return so many echoes,

[002-0212]What groans of over-fed or half-starv’d who fall sunstruck or in fits,

[002-0213]What exclamations of women taken suddenly who hurry home and

[002-0214]give birth to babes,

[002-0215]What living and buried speech is always vibrating here, what howls

[002-0216]restrain’d by decorum,

[002-0217]Arrests of criminals, slights, adulterous offers made, acceptances,

[002-0218]rejections with convex lips,

[002-0219]I mind them or the show or resonance of them--I come and I depart.

[002-0220]9

[002-0221]The big doors of the country barn stand open and ready,

[002-0222]The dried grass of the harvest-time loads the slow-drawn wagon,

[002-0223]The clear light plays on the brown gray and green intertinged,

[002-0224]The armfuls are pack’d to the sagging mow.

[002-0225]I am there, I help, I came stretch’d atop of the load,

[002-0226]I felt its soft jolts, one leg reclined on the other,

[002-0227]I jump from the cross-beams and seize the clover and timothy,

[002-0228]And roll head over heels and tangle my hair full of wisps.

[002-0229]10

[002-0230]Alone far in the wilds and mountains I hunt,

[002-0231]Wandering amazed at my own lightness and glee,

[002-0232]In the late afternoon choosing a safe spot to pass the night,

[002-0233]Kindling a fire and broiling the fresh-kill’d game,

[002-0234]Falling asleep on the gather’d leaves with my dog and gun by my side.

[002-0235]The Yankee clipper is under her sky-sails, she cuts the sparkle and scud,

[002-0236]My eyes settle the land, I bend at her prow or shout joyously from the deck.

[002-0237]The boatmen and clam-diggers arose early and stopt for me,

[002-0238]I tuck’d my trowser-ends in my boots and went and had a good time;

[002-0239]You should have been with us that day round the chowder-kettle.

[002-0240]I saw the marriage of the trapper in the open air in the far west,

[002-0241]the bride was a red girl,

[002-0242]Her father and his friends sat near cross-legged and dumbly smoking,

[002-0243]they had moccasins to their feet and large thick blankets

[002-0244]hanging from their shoulders,

[002-0245]On a bank lounged the trapper, he was drest mostly in skins, his luxuriant

[002-0246]beard and curls protected his neck, he held his bride by the hand,

[002-0247]She had long eyelashes, her head was bare, her coarse straight locks

[002-0248]descended upon her voluptuous limbs and reach’d to her feet.

[002-0249]The runaway slave came to my house and stopt outside,

[002-0250]I heard his motions crackling the twigs of the woodpile,

[002-0251]Through the swung half-door of the kitchen I saw him limpsy and weak,

[002-0252]And went where he sat on a log and led him in and assured him,

[002-0253]And brought water and fill’d a tub for his sweated [[body]] and bruis’d feet,

[002-0254]And gave him a room that enter’d from my own, and gave him some

[002-0255]coarse clean clothes,

[002-0256]And remember perfectly well his revolving eyes and his awkwardness,

[002-0257]And remember putting plasters on the galls of his neck and ankles;

[002-0258]He staid with me a week before he was recuperated and pass’d north,

[002-0259]I had him sit next me at table, my fire-lock lean’d in the corner.

[002-0260]11

[002-0261]Twenty-eight young men bathe by the shore,

[002-0262]Twenty-eight young men and all so friendly;

[002-0263]Twenty-eight years of womanly life and all so lonesome.

[002-0264]She owns the fine house by the rise of the bank,

[002-0265]She hides handsome and richly drest aft the blinds of the window.

[002-0266]Which of the young men does she like the best?

[002-0267]Ah the homeliest of them is beautiful to her.

[002-0268]Where are you off to, lady? for I see you,

[002-0269]You splash in the water there, yet stay stock still in your room.

[002-0270]Dancing and laughing along the beach came the twenty-ninth bather,

[002-0271]The rest did not see her, but she saw them and loved them.

[002-0272]The beards of the young men glisten’d with wet, it ran from their long hair,

[002-0273]Little streams pass’d all over their bodies.

[002-0274]An unseen hand also pass’d over their bodies,

[002-0275]It descended tremblingly from their temples and ribs.

[002-0276]The young men float on their backs, their white bellies bulge to the

[002-0277]sun, they do not ask who seizes fast to them,

[002-0278]They do not know who puffs and declines with pendant and bending arch,

[002-0279]They do not think whom they souse with spray.

[002-0280]12

[002-0281]The butcher-boy puts off his killing-clothes, or sharpens his knife

[002-0282]at the stall in the market,

[002-0283]I loiter enjoying his repartee and his shuffle and break-down.

[002-0284]Blacksmiths with grimed and hairy chests environ the anvil,

[002-0285]Each has his main-sledge, they are all out, there is a great heat in

[002-0286]the fire.

[002-0287]From the cinder-strew’d threshold I follow their movements,

[002-0288]The lithe sheer of their waists plays even with their massive arms,

[002-0289]Overhand the hammers swing, overhand so slow, overhand so sure,

[002-0290]They do not hasten, each man hits in his place.

[002-0291]13

[002-0292]The negro holds firmly the reins of his four horses, the block swags

[002-0293]underneath on its tied-over chain,

[002-0294]The negro that drives the long dray of the stone-yard, steady and

[002-0295]tall he stands pois’d on one leg on the string-piece,

[002-0296]His blue shirt exposes his ample neck and breast and loosens over

[002-0297]his hip-band,

[002-0298]His glance is calm and commanding, he tosses the slouch of his hat

[002-0299]away from his forehead,

[002-0300]The sun falls on his crispy hair and mustache, falls on the black of

[002-0301]his polish’d and perfect limbs.

[002-0302]I behold the picturesque giant and love him, and I do not stop there,

[002-0303]I go with the team also.

[002-0304]In me the caresser of life wherever moving, backward as well as

[002-0305]forward sluing,

[002-0306]To niches aside and junior bending, not a person or object missing,

[002-0307]Absorbing all to myself and for this song.

[002-0308]Oxen that rattle the yoke and chain or halt in the leafy shade, what

[002-0309]is that you express in your eyes?

[002-0310]It seems to me more than all the print I have read in my life.

[002-0311]My tread scares the wood-drake and wood-duck on my distant and

[002-0312]day-long ramble,

[002-0313]They rise together, they slowly circle around.

[002-0314]I believe in those wing’d purposes,

[002-0315]And acknowledge red, yellow, white, playing within me,

[002-0316]And consider green and violet and the tufted crown intentional,

[002-0317]And do not call the tortoise unworthy because she is not something else,

[002-0318]And the jay in the woods never studied the gamut, yet trills pretty well to me,

[002-0319]And the look of the bay mare shames silliness out of me.

[002-0320]14

[002-0321]The wild gander leads his flock through the cool night,

[002-0322]Ya-honk he says, and sounds it down to me like an invitation,

[002-0323]The pert may suppose it meaningless, but I listening close,

[002-0324]Find its purpose and place up there toward the wintry sky.

[002-0325]The sharp-hoof’d moose of the north, the cat on the house-sill, the

[002-0326]chickadee, the [[prairie]]-dog,

[002-0327]The litter of the grunting sow as they tug at her teats,

[002-0328]The brood of the turkey-hen and she with her half-spread wings,

[002-0329]I see in them and myself the same old law.

[002-0330]The press of my foot to the earth springs a hundred affections,

[002-0331]They scorn the best I can do to relate them.

[002-0332]I am enamour’d of growing out-doors,

[002-0333]Of men that live among cattle or taste of the ocean or woods,

[002-0334]Of the builders and steerers of ships and the wielders of axes and

[002-0335]mauls, and the drivers of horses,

[002-0336]I can eat and sleep with them week in and week out.

[002-0337]What is commonest, cheapest, nearest, easiest, is Me,

[002-0338]Me going in for my chances, spending for vast returns,

[002-0339]Adorning myself to bestow myself on the first that will take me,

[002-0340]Not asking the sky to come down to my good will,

[002-0341]Scattering it freely forever.
