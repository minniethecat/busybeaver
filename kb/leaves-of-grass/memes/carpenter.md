## Overview

The Carpenter appears in Walt Whitman's Leaves of Grass as part of the poet's celebration of American working people. In Whitman's democratic vision, no trade is too humble for poetry.

## In Whitman's Poetry

Whitman includes the carpenter among his catalogues of American types, treating each trade with equal dignity and attention.

## Historical / Biographical Context

The 19th-century carpenter represented the skilled artisan tradition Whitman admired — independent and central to community life.

## Themes & Significance

The Carpenter embodies the dignity of skilled labor and the democratic principle that all honest work is sacred.
