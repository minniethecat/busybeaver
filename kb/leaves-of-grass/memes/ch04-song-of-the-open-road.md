# BOOK VII: Song of the Open Road

[004-001]Song of the Open Road

[004-002]1

[004-003]Afoot and light-hearted I take to [[the-open-road|the open road]],

[004-004]Healthy, [[freedom|free]], the [[earth|world]] before me,

[004-005]The long brown path before me leading wherever I choose.

[004-006]Henceforth I ask not good-fortune, I [[self|myself]] am good-fortune,

[004-007]Henceforth I whimper no more, postpone no more, need nothing,

[004-008]Done with indoor complaints, libraries, querulous criticisms,

[004-009]Strong and content I travel the open road.

[004-010]The earth, that is sufficient,

[004-011]I do not want the constellations any nearer,

[004-012]I know they are very well where they are,

[004-013]I know they suffice for those who belong to them.

[004-014](Still here I carry my old delicious burdens,

[004-015]I carry them, men and women, I carry them with me wherever I go,

[004-016]I swear it is impossible for me to get rid of them,

[004-017]I am fill’d with them, and I will fill them in return.)

[004-018]2

[004-019]You road I enter upon and look around, I believe you are not all

[004-020]that is here,

[004-021]I believe that much unseen is also here.

[004-022]Here the profound lesson of reception, nor preference nor denial,

[004-023]The black with his woolly head, the felon, the diseas’d, the

[004-024]illiterate person, are not denied;

[004-025]The birth, the hasting after the physician, the beggar’s tramp, the

[004-026]drunkard’s stagger, the laughing party of [[mechanic|mechanics]],

[004-027]The escaped youth, the rich person’s carriage, the fop, the eloping couple,

[004-028]The early market-man, the hearse, the moving of furniture into the

[004-029]town, the return back from the town,

[004-030]They pass, I also pass, any thing passes, none can be interdicted,

[004-031]None but are accepted, none but shall be dear to me.

[004-032]3

[004-033]You air that serves me with breath to speak!

[004-034]You objects that call from diffusion my meanings and give them shape!

[004-035]You light that wraps me and all things in delicate equable showers!

[004-036]You paths worn in the irregular hollows by the roadsides!

[004-037]I believe you are latent with unseen existences, you are so dear to me.

[004-038]You flagg’d walks of the cities! you strong curbs at the edges!

[004-039]You ferries! you planks and posts of wharves! you timber-lined

[004-040]side! you distant ships!

[004-041]You rows of houses! you window-pierc’d facades! you roofs!

[004-042]You porches and entrances! you copings and iron guards!

[004-043]You windows whose transparent shells might expose so much!

[004-044]You doors and ascending steps! you arches!

[004-045]You gray stones of interminable pavements! you trodden crossings!

[004-046]From all that has touch’d you I believe you have imparted to

[004-047]yourselves, and now would impart the same secretly to me,

[004-048]From the living and the dead you have peopled your impassive surfaces,

[004-049]and the spirits thereof would be evident and amicable with me.

[004-050]4

[004-051]The earth expanding right hand and left hand,

[004-052]The picture alive, every part in its best light,

[004-053]The music falling in where it is wanted, and stopping where it is

[004-054]not wanted,

[004-055]The cheerful voice of the public road, the gay fresh sentiment of the road.

[004-056]O highway I travel, do you say to me Do not leave me?

[004-057]Do you say Venture not--if you leave me you are lost?

[004-058]Do you say I am already prepared, I am well-beaten and undenied,

[004-059]adhere to me?

[004-060]O public road, I say back I am not afraid to leave you, yet I [[love]] you,

[004-061]You express me better than I can express myself,

[004-062]You shall be more to me than my poem.

[004-063]I think heroic deeds were all conceiv’d in the open air, and all

[004-064]free poems also,

[004-065]I think I could stop here myself and do miracles,

[004-066]I think whatever I shall meet on the road I shall like, and whoever

[004-067]beholds me shall like me,

[004-068]I think whoever I see must be happy.

[004-069]5

[004-070]From this hour I ordain myself loos’d of limits and imaginary lines,

[004-071]Going where I list, my own master total and absolute,

[004-072]Listening to others, considering well what they say,

[004-073]Pausing, searching, receiving, contemplating,

[004-074]Gently, but with undeniable will, divesting myself of the holds that

[004-075]would hold me.

[004-076]I inhale great draughts of space,

[004-077]The east and the west are mine, and the north and the south are mine.

[004-078]I am larger, better than I thought,

[004-079]I did not know I held so much goodness.

[004-080]All seems beautiful to me,

[004-081]can repeat over to men and women You have done such good to me

[004-082]I would do the same to you,

[004-083]I will recruit for myself and you as I go,

[004-084]I will scatter myself among men and women as I go,

[004-085]I will toss a new gladness and roughness among them,

[004-086]Whoever denies me it shall not trouble me,

[004-087]Whoever accepts me he or she shall be blessed and shall bless me.

[004-088]6

[004-089]Now if a thousand perfect men were to appear it would not amaze me,

[004-090]Now if a thousand beautiful forms of women appear’d it would not

[004-091]astonish me.

[004-092]Now I see the secret of the making of the best persons,

[004-093]It is to grow in the open air and to eat and sleep with the earth.

[004-094]Here a great personal deed has room,

[004-095](Such a deed seizes upon the hearts of the whole race of men,

[004-096]Its effusion of strength and will overwhelms law and mocks all

[004-097]authority and all argument against it.)

[004-098]Here is the test of wisdom,

[004-099]Wisdom is not finally tested in schools,

[004-100]Wisdom cannot be pass’d from one having it to another not having it,

[004-101]Wisdom is of [[soul|the soul]], is not susceptible of proof, is its own proof,

[004-102]Applies to all stages and objects and qualities and is content,

[004-103]Is the certainty of the reality and [[immortality]] of things, and the

[004-104]excellence of things;

[004-105]Something there is in the float of the sight of things that provokes

[004-106]it out of the soul.

[004-107]Now I re-examine philosophies and religions,

[004-108]They may prove well in lecture-rooms, yet not prove at all under the

[004-109]spacious clouds and along the landscape and flowing currents.

[004-110]Here is realization,

[004-111]Here is a man tallied--he realizes here what he has in him,

[004-112]The past, the future, majesty, love--if they are vacant of you, you

[004-113]are vacant of them.

[004-114]Only the kernel of every object nourishes;

[004-115]Where is he who tears off the husks for you and me?

[004-116]Where is he that undoes stratagems and envelopes for you and me?

[004-117]Here is [[comrade|adhesiveness]], it is not previously fashion’d, it is apropos;

[004-118]Do you know what it is as you pass to be loved by strangers?

[004-119]Do you know the talk of those turning eye-balls?

[004-120]7

[004-121]Here is the efflux of the soul,

[004-122]The efflux of the soul comes from within through embower’d gates,

[004-123]ever provoking questions,

[004-124]These yearnings why are they? these thoughts in the [[night|darkness]] why are they?

[004-125]Why are there men and women that while they are nigh me the sunlight

[004-126]expands my blood?

[004-127]Why when they leave me do my pennants of [[joy]] sink flat and lank?

[004-128]Why are there trees I never walk under but large and melodious

[004-129]thoughts descend upon me?

[004-130](I think they hang there winter and summer on those trees and always

[004-131]drop fruit as I pass;)

[004-132]What is it I interchange so suddenly with strangers?

[004-133]What with some driver as I ride on the seat by his side?

[004-134]What with some fisherman drawing his seine by the shore as I walk by

[004-135]and pause?

[004-136]What gives me to be free to a woman’s and man’s good-will? what

[004-137]gives them to be free to mine?

[004-138]8

[004-139]The efflux of the soul is happiness, here is happiness,

[004-140]I think it pervades the open air, waiting at all times,

[004-141]Now it flows unto us, we are rightly charged.

[004-142]Here rises the fluid and attaching character,

[004-143]The fluid and attaching character is the freshness and sweetness of

[004-144]man and woman,

[004-145](The herbs of the morning sprout no fresher and sweeter every day

[004-146]out of the roots of themselves, than it sprouts fresh and sweet

[004-147]continually out of itself.)

[004-148]Toward the fluid and attaching character exudes the sweat of the

[004-149]love of young and old,

[004-150]From it falls distill’d the charm that mocks beauty and attainments,

[004-151]Toward it heaves the shuddering longing ache of contact.

[004-152]9

[004-153]Allons! whoever you are come travel with me!

[004-154]Traveling with me you find what never tires.

[004-155]The earth never tires,

[004-156]The earth is rude, silent, incomprehensible at first, Nature is rude

[004-157]and incomprehensible at first,

[004-158]Be not discouraged, keep on, there are divine things well envelop’d,

[004-159]I swear to you there are divine things more beautiful than words can tell.

[004-160]Allons! we must not stop here,

[004-161]However sweet these laid-up stores, however convenient this dwelling

[004-162]we cannot remain here,

[004-163]However shelter’d this port and however calm these [[sea|waters]] we must

[004-164]not anchor here,

[004-165]However welcome the hospitality that surrounds us we are permitted

[004-166]to receive it but a little while.

[004-167]10

[004-168]Allons! the inducements shall be greater,

[004-169]We will sail pathless and wild seas,

[004-170]We will go where winds blow, waves dash, and the Yankee clipper

[004-171]speeds by under full sail.

[004-172]Allons! with power, liberty, the earth, the elements,

[004-173]Health, defiance, gayety, self-esteem, curiosity;

[004-174]Allons! from all formules!

[004-175]From your formules, O bat-eyed and materialistic priests.

[004-176]The stale cadaver blocks up the passage--the burial waits no longer.

[004-177]Allons! yet take warning!

[004-178]He traveling with me needs the best blood, thews, endurance,

[004-179]None may come to the trial till he or she bring courage and health,

[004-180]Come not here if you have already spent the best of yourself,

[004-181]Only those may come who come in sweet and determin’d bodies,

[004-182]No diseas’d person, no rum-drinker or venereal taint is permitted here.

[004-183](I and mine do not convince by arguments, similes, rhymes,

[004-184]We convince by our presence.)

[004-185]11

[004-186]Listen! I will be honest with you,

[004-187]I do not offer the old smooth prizes, but offer rough new prizes,

[004-188]These are the days that must happen to you:

[004-189]You shall not heap up what is call’d riches,

[004-190]You shall scatter with lavish hand all that you earn or achieve,

[004-191]You but arrive at the city to which you were destin’d, you hardly

[004-192]settle yourself to satisfaction before you are call’d by an

[004-193]irresistible call to depart,

[004-194]You shall be treated to the ironical smiles and mockings of those

[004-195]who remain behind you,

[004-196]What beckonings of love you receive you shall only answer with

[004-197]passionate kisses of parting,

[004-198]You shall not allow the hold of those who spread their reach’d hands

[004-199]toward you.

[004-200]12

[004-201]Allons! after the great Companions, and to belong to them!

[004-202]They too are on the road--they are the swift and majestic men--they

[004-203]are the greatest women,

[004-204]Enjoyers of calms of seas and storms of seas,

[004-205]Sailors of many a ship, walkers of many a mile of land,

[004-206]Habitues of many distant countries, habitues of far-distant dwellings,

[004-207]Trusters of men and women, observers of cities, solitary toilers,

[004-208]Pausers and contemplators of tufts, blossoms, shells of the shore,

[004-209]Dancers at wedding-dances, kissers of brides, tender helpers of

[004-210]children, bearers of children,

[004-211]Soldiers of revolts, standers by gaping graves, lowerers-down of coffins,

[004-212]Journeyers over consecutive seasons, over the years, the curious

[004-213]years each emerging from that which preceded it,

[004-214]Journeyers as with companions, namely their own diverse phases,

[004-215]Forth-steppers from the latent unrealized baby-days,

[004-216]Journeyers gayly with their own youth, journeyers with their bearded

[004-217]and well-grain’d manhood,

[004-218]Journeyers with their womanhood, ample, unsurpass’d, content,

[004-219]Journeyers with their own sublime old age of manhood or womanhood,

[004-220]Old age, calm, expanded, broad with the haughty breadth of the universe,

[004-221]Old age, flowing free with the delicious near-by freedom of [[death]].

[004-222]13

[004-223]Allons! to that which is endless as it was beginningless,

[004-224]To undergo much, tramps of days, rests of nights,

[004-225]To merge all in the travel they tend to, and the days and nights

[004-226]they tend to,

[004-227]Again to merge them in the start of superior journeys,

[004-228]To see nothing anywhere but what you may reach it and pass it,

[004-229]To conceive no [[time]], however distant, but what you may reach it and pass it,

[004-230]To look up or down no road but it stretches and waits for you,

[004-231]however long but it stretches and waits for you,

[004-232]To see no being, not [[god|God]]’s or any, but you also go thither,

[004-233]To see no possession but you may possess it, enjoying all without

[004-234]labor or purchase, abstracting the feast yet not abstracting one

[004-235]particle of it,

[004-236]To take the best of [[farmer|the farmer]]’s farm and the rich man’s elegant

[004-237]villa, and the chaste blessings of the well-married couple, and

[004-238]the fruits of orchards and flowers of gardens,

[004-239]To take to your use out of the compact cities as you pass through,

[004-240]To carry buildings and streets with you afterward wherever you go,

[004-241]To gather the minds of men out of their brains as you encounter

[004-242]them, to gather the love out of their hearts,

[004-243]To take your lovers on the road with you, for all that you leave

[004-244]them behind you,

[004-245]To know the universe itself as a road, as many roads, as roads for

[004-246]traveling souls.

[004-247]All parts away for the progress of souls,

[004-248]All religion, all solid things, arts, governments--all that was or is

[004-249]apparent upon this globe or any globe, falls into niches and corners

[004-250]before the procession of souls along the grand roads of the universe.

[004-251]Of the progress of the souls of men and women along the grand roads of

[004-252]the universe, all other progress is the needed emblem and sustenance.

[004-253]Forever alive, forever forward,

[004-254]Stately, solemn, sad, withdrawn, baffled, mad, turbulent, feeble,

[004-255]dissatisfied,

[004-256]Desperate, proud, fond, sick, accepted by men, rejected by men,

[004-257]They go! they go! I know that they go, but I know not where they go,

[004-258]But I know that they go toward the best--toward something great.

[004-259]Whoever you are, come forth! or man or woman come forth!

[004-260]You must not stay sleeping and dallying there in the house, though

[004-261]you built it, or though it has been built for you.

[004-262]Out of the dark confinement! out from behind the screen!

[004-263]It is useless to protest, I know all and expose it.

[004-264]Behold through you as bad as the rest,

[004-265]Through the laughter, dancing, dining, supping, of people,

[004-266]Inside of dresses and ornaments, inside of those wash’d and trimm’d faces,

[004-267]Behold a secret silent loathing and despair.

[004-268]No husband, no wife, no friend, trusted to hear the confession,

[004-269]Another self, a duplicate of every one, skulking and hiding it goes,

[004-270]Formless and wordless through the streets of the cities, polite and

[004-271]bland in the parlors,

[004-272]In the cars of railroads, in steamboats, in the public assembly,

[004-273]Home to the houses of men and women, at the table, in the bedroom,

[004-274]everywhere,

[004-275]Smartly attired, countenance smiling, form upright, death under the

[004-276]breast-bones, hell under the skull-bones,

[004-277]Under the broadcloth and gloves, under the ribbons and artificial flowers,

[004-278]Keeping fair with the customs, speaking not a syllable of itself,

[004-279]Speaking of any thing else but never of itself.

[004-280]14

[004-281]Allons! through struggles and wars!

[004-282]The goal that was named cannot be countermanded.

[004-283]Have the past struggles succeeded?

[004-284]What has succeeded? yourself? your nation? Nature?

[004-285]Now understand me well--it is provided in the essence of things that

[004-286]from any fruition of success, no matter what, shall come forth

[004-287]something to make a greater struggle necessary.

[004-288]My call is the call of [[war|battle]], I nourish active rebellion,

[004-289]He going with me must go well arm’d,

[004-290]He going with me goes often with spare diet, poverty, angry enemies,

[004-291]desertions.

[004-292]15

[004-293]Allons! the road is before us!

[004-294]It is safe--I have tried it--my own feet have tried it well--be not

[004-295]detain’d!

[004-296]Let the paper remain on the desk unwritten, and the book on the

[004-297]shelf unopen’d!

[004-298]Let the tools remain in the workshop! let the money remain unearn’d!

[004-299]Let the school stand! mind not the cry of the teacher!

[004-300]Let the preacher preach in his pulpit! let the lawyer plead in the

[004-301]court, and the judge expound the law.

[004-302]Camerado, I give you my hand!

[004-303]I give you my love more precious than money,

[004-304]I give you myself before preaching or law;

[004-305]Will you give me yourself? will you come travel with me?

[004-306]Shall we stick by each other as long as we live?
