# BOOK XI: A Song of Joys

[008-001]A Song of Joys

[008-002]O to make the most jubilant song!

[008-003]Full of music--full of manhood, womanhood, infancy!

[008-004]Full of common employments--full of grain and trees.

[008-005]O for the voices of animals--O for the swiftness and balance of fishes!

[008-006]O for the dropping of raindrops in a song!

[008-007]O for the sunshine and motion of waves in a song!

[008-008]O the [[joy]] of my spirit--it is uncaged--it darts like lightning!

[008-009]It is not enough to have this [[earth|globe]] or a certain [[time]],

[008-010]I will have thousands of globes and all time.

[008-011]O the engineer’s joys! to go with a locomotive!

[008-012]To hear the hiss of steam, the merry shriek, the steam-whistle, the

[008-013]laughing locomotive!

[008-014]To push with resistless way and speed off in the distance.

[008-015]O the gleesome saunter over fields and hillsides!

[008-016]The leaves and flowers of the commonest weeds, the moist fresh

[008-017]stillness of the woods,

[008-018]The exquisite smell of the earth at daybreak, and all through the forenoon.

[008-019]O the horseman’s and horsewoman’s joys!

[008-020]The saddle, the gallop, the pressure upon the seat, the cool

[008-021]gurgling by the ears and hair.

[008-022]O the fireman’s joys!

[008-023]I hear the alarm at dead of [[night]],

[008-024]I hear bells, shouts! I pass the crowd, I run!

[008-025]The sight of the flames maddens me with pleasure.

[008-026]O the joy of the strong-brawn’d fighter, towering in the arena in

[008-027]perfect condition, conscious of power, thirsting to meet his opponent.

[008-028]O the joy of that vast elemental sympathy which only the human [[soul]] is

[008-029]capable of generating and emitting in steady and limitless floods.

[008-030]O [[mother|the mother]]’s joys!

[008-031]The watching, the endurance, the precious [[love]], the anguish, the

[008-032]patiently yielded life.

[008-033]O the of increase, growth, recuperation,

[008-034]The joy of soothing and pacifying, the joy of concord and harmony.

[008-035]O to go back to the place where I was born,

[008-036]To hear the birds sing once more,

[008-037]To ramble about the house and barn and over the fields once more,

[008-038]And through the orchard and along the old lanes once more.

[008-039]O to have been brought up on bays, lagoons, creeks, or along the coast,

[008-040]To continue and be employ’d there all my life,

[008-041]The briny and damp smell, the shore, the salt weeds exposed at low water,

[008-042]The work of fishermen, the work of the eel-fisher and clam-fisher;

[008-043]I come with my clam-rake and spade, I come with my eel-spear,

[008-044]Is the tide out? I Join the group of clam-diggers on the flats,

[008-045]I laugh and work with them, I joke at my work like a mettlesome young man;

[008-046]In winter I take my eel-basket and eel-spear and travel out on foot

[008-047]on the ice--I have a small axe to cut holes in the ice,

[008-048]Behold me well-clothed going gayly or returning in the afternoon,

[008-049]my brood of tough boys accompanying me,

[008-050]My brood of grown and part-grown boys, who love to be with no

[008-051]one else so well as they love to be with me,

[008-052]By day to work with me, and by night to sleep with me.

[008-053]Another time in warm weather out in a boat, to lift the lobster-pots

[008-054]where they are sunk with heavy stones, (I know the buoys,)

[008-055]O the sweetness of the Fifth-month morning upon the water as I row

[008-056]just before sunrise toward the buoys,

[008-057]I pull the wicker pots up slantingly, the dark green lobsters are

[008-058]desperate with their claws as I take them out, I insert

[008-059]wooden pegs in the ’oints of their pincers,

[008-060]I go to all the places one after another, and then row back to the shore,

[008-061]There in a huge kettle of boiling water the lobsters shall be boil’d

[008-062]till their color becomes scarlet.

[008-063]Another time mackerel-taking,

[008-064]Voracious, mad for the hook, near the surface, they seem to fill the

[008-065]water for miles;

[008-066]Another time fishing for rock-fish in Chesapeake bay, I one of the

[008-067]brown-faced crew;

[008-068]Another time trailing for blue-fish off [[paumanok|Paumanok]], I stand with braced [[body]],

[008-069]My left foot is on the gunwale, my right arm throws far out the

[008-070]coils of slender rope,

[008-071]In sight around me the quick veering and darting of fifty skiffs, my

[008-072]companions.

[008-073]O boating on the rivers,

[008-074]The voyage down the St. Lawrence, the superb scenery, the steamers,

[008-075]The ships sailing, the Thousand Islands, the occasional timber-raft

[008-076]and the raftsmen with long-reaching sweep-oars,

[008-077]The little huts on the rafts, and the stream of smoke when they cook

[008-078]supper at evening.

[008-079](O something pernicious and dread!

[008-080]Something far away from a puny and pious life!

[008-081]Something unproved! something in a trance!

[008-082]Something escaped from the anchorage and driving [[freedom|free]].)

[008-083]O to work in mines, or forging iron,

[008-084]Foundry casting, the foundry itself, the rude high roof, the ample

[008-085]and shadow’d space,

[008-086]The furnace, the hot liquid pour’d out and running.

[008-087]O to resume the joys of the [[soldier]]!

[008-088]To feel the presence of a brave commanding officer--to feel his sympathy!

[008-089]To behold his calmness--to be warm’d in the rays of his smile!

[008-090]To go to [[war|battle]]--to hear the bugles play and the drums beat!

[008-091]To hear the crash of artillery--to see the glittering of the bayonets

[008-092]and musket-barrels in the sun!

[008-093]To see men fall and die and not complain!

[008-094]To taste the savage taste of blood--to be so devilish!

[008-095]To gloat so over the wounds and deaths of the enemy.

[008-096]O the whaleman’s joys! O I cruise my old cruise again!

[008-097]I feel the ship’s motion under me, I feel the Atlantic breezes fanning me,

[008-098]I hear the cry again sent down from the mast-head, There--she blows!

[008-099]Again I spring up the rigging to look with the rest--we descend,

[008-100]wild with excitement,

[008-101]I leap in the lower’d boat, we row toward our prey where he lies,

[008-102]We approach stealthy and silent, I see the mountainous mass,

[008-103]lethargic, basking,

[008-104]I see the harpooneer standing up, I see the weapon dart from his

[008-105]vigorous arm;

[008-106]O swift again far out in [[sea|the ocean]] the wounded whale, settling,

[008-107]running to windward, tows me,

[008-108]Again I see him rise to breathe, we row close again,

[008-109]I see a lance driven through his side, press’d deep, turn’d in the wound,

[008-110]Again we back off, I see him settle again, the life is leaving him fast,

[008-111]As he rises he spouts blood, I see him swim in circles narrower and

[008-112]narrower, swiftly cutting the water--I see him die,

[008-113]He gives one convulsive leap in the centre of the circle, and then

[008-114]falls flat and still in the bloody foam.

[008-115]O the old manhood of me, my noblest joy of all!

[008-116]My children and grand-children, my white hair and beard,

[008-117]My largeness, calmness, majesty, out of the long stretch of my life.

[008-118]O ripen’d joy of womanhood! O happiness at last!

[008-119]I am more than eighty years of age, I am the most venerable mother,

[008-120]How clear is my mind--how all people draw nigh to me!

[008-121]What attractions are these beyond any before? what bloom more

[008-122]than the bloom of youth?

[008-123]What beauty is this that descends upon me and rises out of me?

[008-124]O the orator’s joys!

[008-125]To inflate the chest, to roll the thunder of the voice out from the

[008-126]ribs and throat,

[008-127]To make the people rage, weep, hate, desire, with yourself,

[008-128]To lead [[america|America]]--to quell America with a great tongue.

[008-129]O the joy of my soul leaning pois’d on itself, receiving identity through

[008-130]materials and loving them, observing characters and absorbing them,

[008-131]My soul vibrated back to me from them, from sight, hearing, touch,

[008-132]reason, articulation, comparison, memory, and the like,

[008-133]The real life of my senses and flesh transcending my senses and flesh,

[008-134]My body done with materials, my sight done with my material eyes,

[008-135]Proved to me this day beyond cavil that it is not my material eyes

[008-136]which finally see,

[008-137]Nor my material body which finally loves, walks, laughs, shouts,

[008-138]embraces, procreates.

[008-139]O [[farmer|the farmer]]’s joys!

[008-140]Ohioan’s, Illinoisian’s, Wisconsinese’, Kanadian’s, Iowan’s,

[008-141]Kansian’s, Missourian’s, Oregonese’ joys!

[008-142]To rise at peep of day and pass forth nimbly to work,

[008-143]To plough land in the fall for winter-sown crops,

[008-144]To plough land in the spring for maize,

[008-145]To train orchards, to graft the trees, to gather apples in the fall.

[008-146]O to bathe in the swimming-bath, or in a good place along shore,

[008-147]To splash the water! to walk ankle-deep, or race naked along the shore.

[008-148]O to realize space!

[008-149]The plenteousness of all, that there are no bounds,

[008-150]To emerge and be of the sky, of the sun and moon and flying

[008-151]clouds, as one with them.

[008-152]O the joy a manly [[self]]-hood!

[008-153]To be servile to none, to defer to none, not to any tyrant known or unknown,

[008-154]To walk with erect carriage, a step springy and elastic,

[008-155]To look with calm gaze or with a flashing eye,

[008-156]To speak with a full and sonorous voice out of a broad chest,

[008-157]To confront with your personality all the other personalities of the earth.

[008-158]Knowist thou the excellent joys of youth?

[008-159]Joys of the dear companions and of the merry word and laughing face?

[008-160]Joy of the glad light-beaming day, joy of the wide-breath’d games?

[008-161]Joy of sweet music, joy of the lighted ball-room and the dancers?

[008-162]Joy of the plenteous dinner, strong carouse and drinking?

[008-163]Yet O my soul supreme!

[008-164]Knowist thou the joys of pensive thought?

[008-165]Joys of the free and lonesome heart, the tender, gloomy heart?

[008-166]Joys of the solitary walk, the spirit bow’d yet proud, the suffering

[008-167]and the struggle?

[008-168]The agonistic throes, the ecstasies, joys of the solemn musings day

[008-169]or night?

[008-170]Joys of the thought of [[death|Death]], the great spheres Time and Space?

[008-171]Prophetic joys of better, loftier love’s ideals, [[god|the divine]] wife,

[008-172]the sweet, eternal, perfect [[comrade]]?

[008-173]Joys all thine own undying one, joys worthy thee O soul.

[008-174]O while I live to be the ruler of life, not a slave,

[008-175]To meet life as a powerful conqueror,

[008-176]No fumes, no ennui, no more complaints or scornful criticisms,

[008-177]To these proud laws of the air, the water and the ground, proving

[008-178]my interior soul impregnable,

[008-179]And nothing exterior shall ever take command of me.

[008-180]For not life’s joys alone I sing, repeating--the joy of death!

[008-181]The beautiful touch of Death, soothing and benumbing a few moments,

[008-182]for reasons,

[008-183]Myself discharging my excrementitious body to be burn’d, or render’d

[008-184]to powder, or buried,

[008-185]My real body doubtless left to me for other spheres,

[008-186]My voided body nothing more to me, returning to the purifications,

[008-187]further offices, eternal uses of the earth.

[008-188]O to attract by more than attraction!

[008-189]How it is I know not--yet behold! the something which obeys none

[008-190]of the rest,

[008-191]It is offensive, never defensive--yet how magnetic it draws.

[008-192]O to struggle against great odds, to meet enemies undaunted!

[008-193]To be entirely alone with them, to find how much one can stand!

[008-194]To look strife, torture, prison, popular odium, face to face!

[008-195]To mount the scaffold, to advance to the muzzles of guns with

[008-196]perfect nonchalance!

[008-197]To be indeed a God!

[008-198]O to sail to sea in a ship!

[008-199]To leave this steady unendurable land,

[008-200]To leave the tiresome sameness of the streets, the sidewalks and the

[008-201]houses,

[008-202]To leave you O you solid motionless land, and entering a ship,

[008-203]To sail and sail and sail!

[008-204]O to have life henceforth a poem of new joys!

[008-205]To dance, clap hands, exult, shout, skip, leap, roll on, float on!

[008-206]To be a [[sailor]] of the world bound for all ports,

[008-207]A ship itself, (see indeed these sails I spread to the sun and air,)

[008-208]A swift and swelling ship full of rich words, full of joys.
