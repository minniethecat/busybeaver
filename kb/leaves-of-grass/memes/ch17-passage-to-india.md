# BOOK XXVI: Passage to India

[017-001]Passage to India

[017-002]1

[017-003]Singing my days,

[017-004]Singing the great achievements of [[time|the present]],

[017-005]Singing the strong light works of engineers,

[017-006]Our modern wonders, (the antique ponderous Seven outvied,)

[017-007]In the Old World the east the Suez canal,

[017-008]The New by its mighty railroad spann’d,

[017-009]The seas inlaid with eloquent gentle wires;

[017-010]Yet first to sound, and ever sound, the cry with thee O [[soul]],

[017-011]The Past! the Past! the Past!

[017-012]The Past--the dark unfathom’d retrospect!

[017-013]The teeming gulf--the sleepers and the shadows!

[017-014]The past--the infinite greatness of the past!

[017-015]For what is the present after all but a growth out of the past?

[017-016](As a projectile form’d, impell’d, passing a certain line, still keeps on,

[017-017]So the present, utterly form’d, impell’d by the past.)

[017-018]2

[017-019]Passage O soul to India!

[017-020]Eclaircise the myths Asiatic, the primitive fables.

[017-021]Not you alone proud truths of the [[earth|world]],

[017-022]Nor you alone ye facts of modern science,

[017-023]But myths and fables of eld, Asia’s, Africa’s fables,

[017-024]The far-darting beams of the spirit, the unloos’d dreams,

[017-025]The deep diving bibles and legends,

[017-026]The daring plots of the poets, the elder religions;

[017-027]O you temples fairer than lilies pour’d over by the rising sun!

[017-028]O you fables spurning the known, eluding the hold of the known,

[017-029]mounting to heaven!

[017-030]You lofty and dazzling towers, pinnacled, red as roses, burnish’d

[017-031]with gold!

[017-032]Towers of fables immortal fashion’d from mortal dreams!

[017-033]You too I welcome and fully the same as the rest!

[017-034]You too with [[joy]] I sing.

[017-035]Passage to India!

[017-036]Lo, soul, seest thou not [[god|God]]’s purpose from the first?

[017-037]The earth to be spann’d, connected by network,

[017-038]The races, neighbors, to marry and be given in marriage,

[017-039]The oceans to be cross’d, the distant brought near,

[017-040]The lands to be welded together.

[017-041]A worship new I sing,

[017-042]You captains, voyagers, explorers, yours,

[017-043]You engineers, you architects, machinists, yours,

[017-044]You, not for trade or transportation only,

[017-045]But in God’s name, and for thy sake O soul.

[017-046]3

[017-047]Passage to India!

[017-048]Lo soul for thee of tableaus twain,

[017-049]I see in one the Suez canal initiated, open’d,

[017-050]I see the procession of steamships, the Empress Engenie’s leading the van,

[017-051]I mark from on deck the strange landscape, the pure sky, the level

[017-052]sand in the distance,

[017-053]I pass swiftly the picturesque groups, the workmen gather’d,

[017-054]The gigantic dredging machines.

[017-055]In one again, different, (yet thine, all thine, O soul, the same,)

[017-056]I see over my own continent the Pacific railroad surmounting every barrier,

[017-057]I see continual trains of cars winding along the Platte carrying

[017-058]freight and passengers,

[017-059]I hear the locomotives rushing and roaring, and the shrill steam-whistle,

[017-060]I hear the echoes reverberate through the grandest scenery in the world,

[017-061]I cross the Laramie plains, I note the rocks in grotesque shapes,

[017-062]the buttes,

[017-063]I see the plentiful larkspur and wild onions, the barren, colorless,

[017-064]sage-deserts,

[017-065]I see in glimpses afar or towering immediately above me the great

[017-066]mountains, I see the Wind river and the Wahsatch mountains,

[017-067]I see the Monument mountain and the Eagle’s Nest, I pass the

[017-068]Promontory, I ascend the Nevadas,

[017-069]I scan the noble Elk mountain and wind around its base,

[017-070]I see the Humboldt range, I thread the valley and cross the river,

[017-071]I see the clear [[sea|waters]] of lake Tahoe, I see forests of majestic pines,

[017-072]Or crossing the great desert, the alkaline plains, I behold

[017-073]enchanting mirages of waters and meadows,

[017-074]Marking through these and after all, in duplicate slender lines,

[017-075]Bridging the three or four thousand miles of land travel,

[017-076]Tying the Eastern to the Western sea,

[017-077]The road between Europe and Asia.

[017-078](Ah Genoese thy dream! thy dream!

[017-079]Centuries after thou art laid in thy grave,

[017-080]The shore thou foundest verifies thy dream.)

[017-081]4

[017-082]Passage to India!

[017-083]Struggles of many a [[captain]], tales of many a [[sailor]] dead,

[017-084]Over my mood stealing and spreading they come,

[017-085]Like clouds and cloudlets in the unreach’d sky.

[017-086]Along all history, down the slopes,

[017-087]As a rivulet running, sinking now, and now again to the surface rising,

[017-088]A ceaseless thought, a varied train--lo, soul, to thee, thy sight,

[017-089]they rise,

[017-090]The plans, the voyages again, the expeditions;

[017-091]Again Vasco de Gama sails forth,

[017-092]Again the knowledge gain’d, [[mariner|the mariner]]’s compass,

[017-093]Lands found and nations born, thou born [[america|America]],

[017-094]For purpose vast, man’s long probation fill’d,

[017-095]Thou rondure of the world at last accomplish’d.

[017-096]5

[017-097]O vast Rondure, swimming in space,

[017-098]Cover’d all over with visible power and beauty,

[017-099]Alternate light and day and the teeming spiritual [[night|darkness]],

[017-100]Unspeakable high processions of sun and moon and countless stars above,

[017-101]Below, the manifold [[grass]] and waters, animals, mountains, trees,

[017-102]With inscrutable purpose, some hidden prophetic intention,

[017-103]Now first it seems my thought begins to span thee.

[017-104]Down from the gardens of Asia descending radiating,

[017-105]Adam and Eve appear, then their myriad progeny after them,

[017-106]Wandering, yearning, curious, with restless explorations,

[017-107]With questionings, baffled, formless, feverish, with never-happy hearts,

[017-108]With that sad incessant refrain, Wherefore unsatisfied soul? and

[017-109]Whither O mocking life?

[017-110]Ah who shall soothe these feverish children?

[017-111]Who Justify these restless explorations?

[017-112]Who speak the secret of impassive earth?

[017-113]Who bind it to us? what is this separate Nature so unnatural?

[017-114]What is this earth to our affections? (unloving earth, without a

[017-115]throb to answer ours,

[017-116]Cold earth, the place of graves.)

[017-117]Yet soul be sure the first intent remains, and shall be carried out,

[017-118]Perhaps even now the time has arrived.

[017-119]After the seas are all cross’d, (as they seem already cross’d,)

[017-120]After the great captains and engineers have accomplish’d their work,

[017-121]After the noble inventors, after the scientists, the chemist, the

[017-122]geologist, ethnologist,

[017-123]Finally shall come the poet worthy that name,

[017-124]The true son of God shall come singing his songs.

[017-125]Then not your deeds only O voyagers, O scientists and inventors,

[017-126]shall be justified,

[017-127]All these hearts as of fretted children shall be sooth’d,

[017-128]All [[love|affection]] shall be fully responded to, the secret shall be told,

[017-129]All these separations and gaps shall be taken up and hook’d and

[017-130]link’d together,

[017-131]The whole earth, this cold, impassive, voiceless earth, shall be

[017-132]completely Justified,

[017-133]Trinitas divine shall be gloriously accomplish’d and compacted by

[017-134]the true son of God, the poet,

[017-135](He shall indeed pass the straits and conquer the mountains,

[017-136]He shall double the cape of Good Hope to some purpose,)

[017-137]Nature and Man shall be disjoin’d and diffused no more,

[017-138]The true son of God shall absolutely fuse them.

[017-139]6

[017-140]Year at whose wide-flung door I sing!

[017-141]Year of the purpose accomplish’d!

[017-142]Year of the marriage of continents, climates and oceans!

[017-143](No mere doge of Venice now wedding the Adriatic,)

[017-144]I see O year in you the vast terraqueous globe given and giving all,

[017-145]Europe to Asia, Africa join’d, and they to the New World,

[017-146]The lands, geographies, dancing before you, holding a festival garland,

[017-147]As brides and bridegrooms hand in hand.

[017-148]Passage to India!

[017-149]Cooling airs from Caucasus far, soothing cradle of man,

[017-150]The river Euphrates flowing, the past lit up again.

[017-151]Lo soul, the retrospect brought forward,

[017-152]The old, most populous, wealthiest of earth’s lands,

[017-153]The streams of the Indus and the Ganges and their many affluents,

[017-154](I my shores of America walking to-day behold, resuming all,)

[017-155]The tale of Alexander on his warlike marches suddenly [[death|dying]],

[017-156]On one side China and on the other side Persia and Arabia,

[017-157]To the south the great seas and the bay of Bengal,

[017-158]The flowing literatures, tremendous epics, religions, castes,

[017-159]Old occult Brahma interminably far back, the tender and junior Buddha,

[017-160]Central and southern empires and all their belongings, possessors,

[017-161]The wars of Tamerlane,the reign of Aurungzebe,

[017-162]The traders, rulers, explorers, Moslems, Venetians, Byzantium, the

[017-163]Arabs, Portuguese,

[017-164]The first travelers famous yet, Marco Polo, Batouta the Moor,

[017-165]Doubts to be solv’d, the map incognita, blanks to be fill’d,

[017-166]The foot of man unstay’d, the hands never at rest,

[017-167]Thyself O soul that will not brook a challenge.

[017-168]The mediaeval navigators rise before me,

[017-169]The world of 1492, with its awaken’d enterprise,

[017-170]Something swelling in humanity now like the sap of the earth in spring,

[017-171]The sunset splendor of chivalry declining.

[017-172]And who art thou sad shade?

[017-173]Gigantic, visionary, thyself a visionary,

[017-174]With majestic limbs and pious beaming eyes,

[017-175]Spreading around with every look of thine a golden world,

[017-176]Enhuing it with gorgeous hues.

[017-177]As the chief histrion,

[017-178]Down to the footlights walks in some great scena,

[017-179]Dominating the rest I see the Admiral himself,

[017-180](History’s type of courage, action, faith,)

[017-181]Behold him sail from Palos leading his little fleet,

[017-182]His voyage behold, his return, his great fame,

[017-183]His misfortunes, calumniators, behold him a prisoner, chain’d,

[017-184]Behold his dejection, poverty, death.

[017-185](Curious in time I stand, noting the efforts of heroes,

[017-186]Is the deferment long? bitter the slander, poverty, death?

[017-187]Lies the seed unreck’d for centuries in the ground? lo, to God’s due

[017-188]occasion,

[017-189]Uprising in the night, it sprouts, blooms,

[017-190]And fills the earth with use and beauty.)

[017-191]7

[017-192]Passage indeed O soul to primal thought,

[017-193]Not lands and seas alone, thy own clear freshness,

[017-194]The young maturity of brood and bloom,

[017-195]To realms of budding bibles.

[017-196]O soul, repressless, I with thee and thou with me,

[017-197]Thy circumnavigation of the world begin,

[017-198]Of man, the voyage of his mind’s return,

[017-199]To reason’s early paradise,

[017-200]Back, back to wisdom’s birth, to innocent intuitions,

[017-201]Again with fair creation.

[017-202]8

[017-203]O we can wait no longer,

[017-204]We too take ship O soul,

[017-205]Joyous we too launch out on trackless seas,

[017-206]Fearless for unknown shores on waves of ecstasy to sail,

[017-207]Amid the wafting winds, (thou pressing me to thee, I thee to me, O soul,)

[017-208]Caroling [[freedom|free]], singing our song of God,

[017-209]Chanting our chant of pleasant exploration.

[017-210]With laugh and many a kiss,

[017-211](Let others deprecate, let others weep for sin, remorse, humiliation,)

[017-212]O soul thou pleasest me, I thee.

[017-213]Ah more than any priest O soul we too believe in God,

[017-214]But with the mystery of God we dare not dally.

[017-215]O soul thou pleasest me, I thee,

[017-216]Sailing these seas or on the hills, or waking in the night,

[017-217]Thoughts, silent thoughts, of Time and Space and Death, like waters flowing,

[017-218]Bear me indeed as through the regions infinite,

[017-219]Whose air I breathe, whose ripples hear, lave me all over,

[017-220]Bathe me O God in thee, mounting to thee,

[017-221]I and my soul to range in range of thee.

[017-222]O Thou transcendent,

[017-223]Nameless, the fibre and the breath,

[017-224]Light of the light, shedding forth universes, thou centre of them,

[017-225]Thou mightier centre of the true, the good, the loving,

[017-226]Thou moral, spiritual fountain--affection’s source--thou reservoir,

[017-227](O pensive soul of me--O thirst unsatisfied--waitest not there?

[017-228]Waitest not haply for us somewhere there the [[comrade|Comrade]] perfect?)

[017-229]Thou pulse--thou motive of the stars, suns, systems,

[017-230]That, circling, move in order, safe, harmonious,

[017-231]Athwart the shapeless vastnesses of space,

[017-232]How should I think, how breathe a single breath, how speak, if, out

[017-233]of [[self|myself]],

[017-234]I could not launch, to those, superior universes?

[017-235]Swiftly I shrivel at the thought of God,

[017-236]At Nature and its wonders, Time and Space and Death,

[017-237]But that I, turning, call to thee O soul, thou actual Me,

[017-238]And lo, thou gently masterest the orbs,

[017-239]Thou matest Time, smilest content at Death,

[017-240]And fillest, swellest full the vastnesses of Space.

[017-241]Greater than stars or suns,

[017-242]Bounding O soul thou journeyest forth;

[017-243]What love than thine and ours could wider amplify?

[017-244]What aspirations, wishes, outvie thine and ours O soul?

[017-245]What dreams of the ideal? what plans of purity, perfection, strength?

[017-246]What cheerful willingness for others’ sake to give up all?

[017-247]For others’ sake to suffer all?

[017-248]Reckoning ahead O soul, when thou, the time achiev’d,

[017-249]The seas all cross’d, weather’d the capes, the voyage done,

[017-250]Surrounded, copest, frontest God, yieldest, the aim attain’d,

[017-251]As fill’d with friendship, love complete, the Elder Brother found,

[017-252]The Younger melts in fondness in his arms.

[017-253]9

[017-254]Passage to more than India!

[017-255]Are thy wings plumed indeed for such far flights?

[017-256]O soul, voyagest thou indeed on voyages like those?

[017-257]Disportest thou on waters such as those?

[017-258]Soundest below the Sanscrit and the Vedas?

[017-259]Then have thy bent unleash’d.

[017-260]Passage to you, your shores, ye aged fierce enigmas!

[017-261]Passage to you, to mastership of you, ye strangling problems!

[017-262]You, strew’d with the wrecks of skeletons, that, living, never reach’d you.

[017-263]Passage to more than India!

[017-264]O secret of the earth and sky!

[017-265]Of you O waters of the sea! O winding creeks and rivers!

[017-266]Of you O woods and fields! of you strong mountains of my land!

[017-267]Of you O [[prairie|prairies]]! of you gray rocks!

[017-268]O morning red! O clouds! O rain and snows!

[017-269]O day and night, passage to you!

[017-270]O sun and moon and all you stars! Sirius and Jupiter!

[017-271]Passage to you!

[017-272]Passage, immediate passage! the blood burns in my veins!

[017-273]Away O soul! hoist instantly the anchor!

[017-274]Cut the hawsers--haul out--shake out every sail!

[017-275]Have we not stood here like trees in the ground long enough?

[017-276]Have we not grovel’d here long enough, eating and drinking like mere brutes?

[017-277]Have we not darken’d and dazed ourselves with books long enough?

[017-278]Sail forth--steer for the deep waters only,

[017-279]Reckless O soul, exploring, I with thee, and thou with me,

[017-280]For we are bound where mariner has not yet dared to go,

[017-281]And we will risk the ship, ourselves and all.

[017-282]O my brave soul!

[017-283]O farther farther sail!

[017-284]O daring joy, but safe! are they not all the seas of God?

[017-285]O farther, farther, farther sail!
