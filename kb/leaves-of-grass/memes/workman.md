## Overview

The Workman appears in Walt Whitmans Leaves of Grass as the archetypal laborer — the common working person whose daily toil is celebrated as the foundation of American democracy.

## In Whitmans Poetry

Whitman catalogues workmen among his democratic types, treating their labor with the same dignity he accords to poets and statesmen.

## Historical / Biographical Context

The 19th-century workman represented the independent laborer Whitman admired — central to the artisan republican tradition.

## Themes & Significance

The Workman embodies the dignity of labor, democratic equality of all trades, and the conviction that ordinary working people are the true subjects of American poetry.
