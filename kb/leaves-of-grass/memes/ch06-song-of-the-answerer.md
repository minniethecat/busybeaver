# BOOK IX: Song of the Answerer

[006-001]Song of the Answerer

[006-002]1

[006-003]Now list to my morning’s romanza, I tell the signs of the Answerer,

[006-004]To the cities and farms I sing as they spread in the sunshine before me.

[006-005]A young man comes to me bearing a message from his brother,

[006-006]How shall the young man know the whether and when of his brother?

[006-007]Tell him to send me the signs. And I stand before the young man

[006-008]face to face, and take his right hand in my left hand and his

[006-009]left hand in my right hand,

[006-010]And I answer for his brother and for men, and I answer for him that

[006-011]answers for all, and send these signs.

[006-012]Him all wait for, him all yield up to, his word is decisive and final,

[006-013]Him they accept, in him lave, in him perceive themselves as amid light,

[006-014]Him they immerse and he immerses them.

[006-015]Beautiful women, the haughtiest nations, laws, the landscape,

[006-016]people, animals,

[006-017]The profound [[earth]] and its attributes and the unquiet [[sea|ocean]], (so

[006-018]tell I my morning’s romanza,)

[006-019]All enjoyments and properties and money, and whatever money will buy,

[006-020]The best farms, others toiling and planting and he unavoidably reaps,

[006-021]The noblest and costliest cities, others grading and building and he

[006-022]domiciles there,

[006-023]Nothing for any one but what is for him, near and far are for him,

[006-024]the ships in the offing,

[006-025]The perpetual shows and marches on land are for him if they are for anybody.

[006-026]He puts things in their attitudes,

[006-027]He puts to-day out of himself with plasticity and [[love]],

[006-028]He places his own times, reminiscences, parents, brothers and

[006-029]sisters, associations, employment, politics, so that the rest

[006-030]never shame them afterward, nor assume to command them.

[006-031]He is the Answerer,

[006-032]What can be answer’d he answers, and what cannot be answer’d he

[006-033]shows how it cannot be answer’d.

[006-034]A man is a summons and challenge,

[006-035](It is vain to skulk--do you hear that mocking and laughter? do you

[006-036]hear the ironical echoes?)

[006-037]Books, friendships, philosophers, priests, action, pleasure, pride,

[006-038]beat up and down seeking to give satisfaction,

[006-039]He indicates the satisfaction, and indicates them that beat up and

[006-040]down also.

[006-041]Whichever the sex, whatever the season or place, he may go freshly

[006-042]and gently and safely by day or by [[night]],

[006-043]He has the pass-key of hearts, to him the response of the prying of

[006-044]hands on the knobs.

[006-045]His welcome is universal, the flow of beauty is not more welcome or

[006-046]universal than he is,

[006-047]The person he favors by day or sleeps with at night is blessed.

[006-048]Every existence has its idiom, every thing has an idiom and tongue,

[006-049]He resolves all tongues into his own and bestows it upon men, and

[006-050]any man translates, and any man translates himself also,

[006-051]One part does not counteract another part, he is the joiner, he sees

[006-052]how they join.

[006-053]He says indifferently and alike How are you friend? to the President

[006-054]at his levee,

[006-055]And he says Good-day my brother, to Cudge that hoes in the sugar-field,

[006-056]And both understand him and know that his speech is right.

[006-057]He walks with perfect ease in the capitol,

[006-058]He walks among the Congress, and one Representative says to another,

[006-059]Here is our equal appearing and new.

[006-060]Then the [[mechanic|mechanics]] take him for a mechanic,

[006-061]And the [[soldier|soldiers]] suppose him to be a soldier, and the sailors that

[006-062]he has follow’d the sea,

[006-063]And the authors take him for an author, and the artists for an artist,

[006-064]And the laborers perceive he could labor with them and love them,

[006-065]No matter what the work is, that he is the one to follow it or has

[006-066]follow’d it,

[006-067]No matter what the nation, that he might find his brothers and

[006-068]sisters there.

[006-069]The English believe he comes of their English stock,

[006-070]A Jew to the Jew he seems, a Russ to the Russ, usual and near,

[006-071]removed from none.

[006-072]Whoever he looks at in the traveler’s coffee-house claims him,

[006-073]The Italian or Frenchman is sure, the German is sure, the Spaniard

[006-074]is sure, and the island Cuban is sure,

[006-075]The engineer, the deck-hand on the great lakes, or on the Mississippi

[006-076]or St. Lawrence or Sacramento, or Hudson or [[paumanok|Paumanok]] sound, claims him.

[006-077]The gentleman of perfect blood acknowledges his perfect blood,

[006-078]The insulter, the prostitute, the angry person, the beggar, see

[006-079]themselves in the ways of him, he strangely transmutes them,

[006-080]They are not vile any more, they hardly know themselves they are so grown.

[006-081]2

[006-082]The indications and tally of [[time]],

[006-083]Perfect sanity shows the master among philosophs,

[006-084]Time, always without break, indicates itself in parts,

[006-085]What always indicates the poet is the crowd of the pleasant company

[006-086]of singers, and their words,

[006-087]The words of the singers are the hours or minutes of the light or dark,

[006-088]but the words of the maker of poems are the general light and dark,

[006-089]The maker of poems settles justice, reality, [[immortality]],

[006-090]His insight and power encircle things and the human race,

[006-091]He is the glory and extract thus far of things and of the human race.

[006-092]The singers do not beget, only the Poet begets,

[006-093]The singers are welcom’d, understood, appear often enough, but rare

[006-094]has the day been, likewise the spot, of the birth of the maker

[006-095]of poems, the Answerer,

[006-096](Not every century nor every five centuries has contain’d such a

[006-097]day, for all its names.)

[006-098]The singers of successive hours of centuries may have ostensible

[006-099]names, but the name of each of them is one of the singers,

[006-100]The name of each is, eye-singer, ear-singer, head-singer,

[006-101]sweet-singer, night-singer, parlor-singer, love-singer,

[006-102]weird-singer, or something else.

[006-103]All this time and at all times wait the words of true poems,

[006-104]The words of true poems do not merely please,

[006-105]The true poets are not followers of beauty but the august masters of beauty;

[006-106]The greatness of sons is the exuding of the greatness of [[mother|mothers]]

[006-107]and fathers,

[006-108]The words of true poems are the tuft and final applause of science.

[006-109]Divine instinct, breadth of vision, the law of reason, health,

[006-110]rudeness of [[body]], withdrawnness,

[006-111]Gayety, sun-tan, air-sweetness, such are some of the words of poems.

[006-112]The [[sailor]] and traveler underlie the maker of poems, the Answerer,

[006-113]The builder, geometer, chemist, anatomist, phrenologist, artist, all

[006-114]these underlie the maker of poems, the Answerer.

[006-115]The words of the true poems give you more than poems,

[006-116]They give you to form for yourself poems, religions, politics, [[war]],

[006-117]peace, behavior, histories, essays, daily life, and every thing else,

[006-118]They balance ranks, colors, races, creeds, and the sexes,

[006-119]They do not seek beauty, they are sought,

[006-120]Forever touching them or close upon them follows beauty, longing,

[006-121]fain, love-sick.

[006-122]They prepare for [[death]], yet are they not the finish, but rather the outset,

[006-123]They bring none to his or her terminus or to be content and full,

[006-124]Whom they take they take into space to behold the birth of stars, to

[006-125]learn one of the meanings,

[006-126]To launch off with absolute faith, to sweep through the ceaseless

[006-127]rings and never be quiet again.
