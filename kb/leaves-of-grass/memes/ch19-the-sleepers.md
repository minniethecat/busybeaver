# BOOK XXVIII: The Sleepers

[019-001]The Sleepers

[019-002]1

[019-003]I wander all [[night]] in my vision,

[019-004]Stepping with light feet, swiftly and noiselessly stepping and stopping,

[019-005]Bending with open eyes over the shut eyes of sleepers,

[019-006]Wandering and confused, lost to [[self|myself]], ill-assorted, contradictory,

[019-007]Pausing, gazing, bending, and stopping.

[019-008]How solemn they look there, stretch’d and still,

[019-009]How quiet they breathe, the little children in their cradles.

[019-010]The wretched features of ennuyes, the white features of corpses, the

[019-011]livid faces of drunkards, the sick-gray faces of onanists,

[019-012]The gash’d bodies on [[war|battle]]-fields, the insane in their

[019-013]strong-door’d rooms, the sacred idiots, the new-born emerging

[019-014]from gates, and the [[death|dying]] emerging from gates,

[019-015]The night pervades them and infolds them.

[019-016]The married couple sleep calmly in their bed, he with his palm on

[019-017]the hip of the wife, and she with her palm on the hip of the husband,

[019-018]The sisters sleep lovingly side by side in their bed,

[019-019]The men sleep lovingly side by side in theirs,

[019-020]And [[mother|the mother]] sleeps with her little child carefully wrapt.

[019-021]The blind sleep, and the deaf and dumb sleep,

[019-022]The prisoner sleeps well in the prison, the runaway son sleeps,

[019-023]The murderer that is to be hung next day, how does he sleep?

[019-024]And the murder’d person, how does he sleep?

[019-025]The female that loves unrequited sleeps,

[019-026]And the male that loves unrequited sleeps,

[019-027]The head of the money-maker that plotted all day sleeps,

[019-028]And the enraged and treacherous dispositions, all, all sleep.

[019-029]I stand in the dark with drooping eyes by the worst-suffering and

[019-030]the most restless,

[019-031]I pass my hands soothingly to and fro a few inches from them,

[019-032]The restless sink in their beds, they fitfully sleep.

[019-033]Now I pierce the darkness, new beings appear,

[019-034]The [[earth]] recedes from me into the night,

[019-035]I saw that it was beautiful, and I see that what is not the earth is

[019-036]beautiful.

[019-037]I go from bedside to bedside, I sleep close with the other sleepers

[019-038]each in turn,

[019-039]I dream in my dream all the dreams of the other dreamers,

[019-040]And I become the other dreamers.

[019-041]I am a dance--play up there! the fit is whirling me fast!

[019-042]I am the ever-laughing--it is new moon and twilight,

[019-043]I see the hiding of douceurs, I see nimble ghosts whichever way look,

[019-044]Cache and cache again deep in the ground and [[sea]], and where it is

[019-045]neither ground nor sea.

[019-046]Well do they do their jobs those journeymen divine,

[019-047]Only from me can they hide nothing, and would not if they could,

[019-048]I reckon I am their boss and they make me a pet besides,

[019-049]And surround me and lead me and run ahead when I walk,

[019-050]To lift their cunning covers to signify me with stretch’d arms, and

[019-051]resume the way;

[019-052]Onward we move, a gay gang of blackguards! with mirth-shouting

[019-053]music and wild-flapping pennants of [[joy]]!

[019-054]I am the actor, the actress, the voter, the politician,

[019-055]The emigrant and the exile, the criminal that stood in the box,

[019-056]He who has been famous and he who shall be famous after to-day,

[019-057]The stammerer, the well-form’d person, the wasted or feeble person.

[019-058]I am she who adorn’d herself and folded her hair expectantly,

[019-059]My truant lover has come, and it is dark.

[019-060]Double yourself and receive me darkness,

[019-061]Receive me and my lover too, he will not let me go without him.

[019-062]I roll myself upon you as upon a bed, I resign myself to the dusk.

[019-063]He whom I call answers me and takes the place of my lover,

[019-064]He rises with me silently from the bed.

[019-065]Darkness, you are gentler than my lover, his flesh was sweaty and panting,

[019-066]I feel the hot moisture yet that he left me.

[019-067]My hands are spread forth, I pass them in all directions,

[019-068]I would sound up the shadowy shore to which you are journeying.

[019-069]Be careful darkness! already what was it touch’d me?

[019-070]I thought my lover had gone, else darkness and he are one,

[019-071]I hear the heart-beat, I follow, I fade away.

[019-072]2

[019-073]I descend my western course, my sinews are flaccid,

[019-074]Perfume and youth course through me and I am their wake.

[019-075]It is my face yellow and wrinkled instead of the old woman’s,

[019-076]I sit low in a straw-bottom chair and carefully darn my grandson’s

[019-077]stockings.

[019-078]It is I too, the sleepless widow looking out on the winter midnight,

[019-079]I see the sparkles of starshine on the icy and pallid earth.

[019-080]A shroud I see and I am the shroud, I wrap a [[body]] and lie in the coffin,

[019-081]It is dark here under ground, it is not evil or pain here, it is

[019-082]blank here, for reasons.

[019-083](It seems to me that every thing in the light and air ought to be happy,

[019-084]Whoever is not in his coffin and the dark grave let him know he has enough.)

[019-085]3

[019-086]I see a beautiful gigantic swimmer swimming naked through the eddies

[019-087]of the sea,

[019-088]His brown hair lies close and even to his head, he strikes out with

[019-089]courageous arms, he urges himself with his legs,

[019-090]I see his white body, I see his undaunted eyes,

[019-091]I hate the swift-running eddies that would dash him head-foremost on

[019-092]the rocks.

[019-093]What are you doing you ruffianly red-trickled waves?

[019-094]Will you kill the courageous giant? will you kill him in the prime

[019-095]of his middle age?

[019-096]Steady and long he struggles,

[019-097]He is baffled, bang’d, bruis’d, he holds out while his strength

[019-098]holds out,

[019-099]The slapping eddies are spotted with his blood, they bear him away,

[019-100]they roll him, swing him, turn him,

[019-101]His beautiful body is borne in the circling eddies, it is

[019-102]continually bruis’d on rocks,

[019-103]Swiftly and ought of sight is borne the brave corpse.

[019-104]4

[019-105]I turn but do not extricate myself,

[019-106]Confused, a past-reading, another, but with darkness yet.

[019-107]The beach is cut by the razory ice-wind, the wreck-guns sound,

[019-108]The tempest lulls, the moon comes floundering through the drifts.

[019-109]I look where the ship helplessly heads end on, I hear the burst as

[019-110]she strikes, I hear the howls of dismay, they grow fainter and fainter.

[019-111]I cannot aid with my wringing fingers,

[019-112]I can but rush to the surf and let it drench me and freeze upon me.

[019-113]I search with the crowd, not one of the company is wash’d to us alive,

[019-114]In the morning I help pick up the dead and lay them in rows in a barn.

[019-115]5

[019-116]Now of the older war-days, the defeat at [[brooklyn|Brooklyn]],

[019-117]Washington stands inside the lines, he stands on the intrench’d

[019-118]hills amid a crowd of officers.

[019-119]His face is cold and damp, he cannot repress the weeping drops,

[019-120]He lifts the glass perpetually to his eyes, the color is blanch’d

[019-121]from his cheeks,

[019-122]He sees the slaughter of the southern braves confided to him by

[019-123]their parents.

[019-124]The same at last and at last when peace is declared,

[019-125]He stands in the room of the old tavern, the well-belov’d [[soldier|soldiers]]

[019-126]all pass through,

[019-127]The officers speechless and slow draw near in their turns,

[019-128]The chief encircles their necks with his arm and kisses them on the cheek,

[019-129]He kisses lightly the wet cheeks one after another, he shakes hands

[019-130]and bids good-by to the army.

[019-131]6

[019-132]Now what my mother told me one day as we sat at dinner together,

[019-133]Of when she was a nearly grown girl living home with her parents on

[019-134]the old homestead.

[019-135]A red squaw came one breakfast-[[time]] to the old homestead,

[019-136]On her back she carried a bundle of rushes for rush-bottoming chairs,

[019-137]Her hair, straight, shiny, coarse, black, profuse, half-envelop’d

[019-138]her face,

[019-139]Her step was [[freedom|free]] and elastic, and her voice sounded exquisitely as

[019-140]she spoke.

[019-141]My mother look’d in delight and amazement at the stranger,

[019-142]She look’d at the freshness of her tall-borne face and full and

[019-143]pliant limbs,

[019-144]The more she look’d upon her she loved her,

[019-145]Never before had she seen such wonderful beauty and purity,

[019-146]She made her sit on a bench by the jamb of the fireplace, she cook’d

[019-147]food for her,

[019-148]She had no work to give her, but she gave her remembrance and fondness.

[019-149]The red squaw staid all the forenoon, and toward the middle of the

[019-150]afternoon she went away,

[019-151]O my mother was loth to have her go away,

[019-152]All the week she thought of her, she watch’d for her many a month,

[019-153]She remember’d her many a winter and many a summer,

[019-154]But the red squaw never came nor was heard of there again.

[019-155]7

[019-156]A show of the summer softness--a contact of something unseen--an

[019-157]amour of the light and air,

[019-158]I am jealous and overwhelm’d with friendliness,

[019-159]And will go gallivant with the light and air myself.

[019-160]O [[love]] and summer, you are in the dreams and in me,

[019-161]Autumn and winter are in the dreams, [[farmer|the farmer]] goes with his thrift,

[019-162]The droves and crops increase, the barns are well-fill’d.

[019-163]Elements merge in the night, ships make tacks in the dreams,

[019-164]The [[sailor]] sails, the exile returns home,

[019-165]The fugitive returns unharm’d, [[immigrant|the immigrant]] is back beyond months

[019-166]and years,

[019-167]The poor Irishman lives in the simple house of his childhood with

[019-168]the well known neighbors and faces,

[019-169]They warmly welcome him, he is barefoot again, he forgets he is well off,

[019-170]The Dutchman voyages home, and the Scotchman and Welshman voyage

[019-171]home, and the native of the Mediterranean voyages home,

[019-172]To every port of England, France, Spain, enter well-fill’d ships,

[019-173]The Swiss foots it toward his hills, the Prussian goes his way, the

[019-174]Hungarian his way, and the Pole his way,

[019-175]The Swede returns, and the Dane and Norwegian return.

[019-176]The homeward bound and the outward bound,

[019-177]The beautiful lost swimmer, the ennuye, the onanist, the female that

[019-178]loves unrequited, the money-maker,

[019-179]The actor and actress, those through with their parts and those

[019-180]waiting to commence,

[019-181]The affectionate boy, the husband and wife, the voter, the nominee

[019-182]that is chosen and the nominee that has fail’d,

[019-183]The great already known and the great any time after to-day,

[019-184]The stammerer, the sick, the perfect-form’d, the homely,

[019-185]The criminal that stood in the box, the judge that sat and sentenced

[019-186]him, the fluent lawyers, the jury, the audience,

[019-187]The laugher and weeper, the dancer, the midnight widow, the red squaw,

[019-188]The consumptive, the erysipalite, the idiot, he that is wrong’d,

[019-189]The antipodes, and every one between this and them in the dark,

[019-190]I swear they are averaged now--one is no better than the other,

[019-191]The night and sleep have liken’d them and restored them.

[019-192]I swear they are all beautiful,

[019-193]Every one that sleeps is beautiful, every thing in the dim light is

[019-194]beautiful,

[019-195]The wildest and bloodiest is over, and all is peace.

[019-196]Peace is always beautiful,

[019-197]The myth of heaven indicates peace and night.

[019-198]The myth of heaven indicates [[soul|the soul]],

[019-199]The soul is always beautiful, it appears more or it appears less, it

[019-200]comes or it lags behind,

[019-201]It comes from its embower’d garden and looks pleasantly on itself

[019-202]and encloses the world,

[019-203]Perfect and clean the genitals previously jetting,and perfect and

[019-204]clean the womb cohering,

[019-205]The head well-grown proportion’d and plumb, and the bowels and

[019-206]joints proportion’d and plumb.

[019-207]The soul is always beautiful,

[019-208]The universe is duly in order, every thing is in its place,

[019-209]What has arrived is in its place and what waits shall be in its place,

[019-210]The twisted skull waits, the watery or rotten blood waits,

[019-211]The child of the glutton or venerealee waits long, and the child of

[019-212]the drunkard waits long, and the drunkard himself waits long,

[019-213]The sleepers that lived and died wait, the far advanced are to go on

[019-214]in their turns, and the far behind are to come on in their turns,

[019-215]The diverse shall be no less diverse, but they shall flow and unite--

[019-216]they unite now.

[019-217]8

[019-218]The sleepers are very beautiful as they lie unclothed,

[019-219]They flow hand in hand over the whole earth from east to west as

[019-220]they lie unclothed,

[019-221]The Asiatic and African are hand in hand, the European and American

[019-222]are hand in hand,

[019-223]Learn’d and unlearn’d are hand in hand, and male and female are hand

[019-224]in hand,

[019-225]The bare arm of the girl crosses the bare breast of her lover, they

[019-226]press close without lust, his lips press her neck,

[019-227]The father holds his grown or ungrown son in his arms with

[019-228]measureless love, and the son holds the father in his arms with

[019-229]measureless love,

[019-230]The white hair of the mother shines on the white wrist of the daughter,

[019-231]The breath of the boy goes with the breath of the man, friend is

[019-232]inarm’d by friend,

[019-233]The scholar kisses the teacher and the teacher kisses the scholar,

[019-234]the wrong ’d made right,

[019-235]The call of the slave is one with the master’s call, and the master

[019-236]salutes the slave,

[019-237]The felon steps forth from the prison, the insane becomes sane, the

[019-238]suffering of sick persons is reliev’d,

[019-239]The sweatings and fevers stop, the throat that was unsound is sound,

[019-240]the lungs of the consumptive are resumed, the poor distress’d

[019-241]head is free,

[019-242]The joints of the rheumatic move as smoothly as ever, and smoother

[019-243]than ever,

[019-244]Stiflings and passages open, the paralyzed become supple,

[019-245]The swell’d and convuls’d and congested awake to themselves in condition,

[019-246]They pass the invigoration of the night and the chemistry of the

[019-247]night, and awake.

[019-248]I too pass from the night,

[019-249]I stay a while away O night, but I return to you again and love you.

[019-250]Why should I be afraid to trust myself to you?

[019-251]I am not afraid, I have been well brought forward by you,

[019-252]I love the rich running day, but I do not desert her in whom I lay so long,

[019-253]I know not how I came of you and I know not where I go with you, but

[019-254]I know I came well and shall go well.

[019-255]I will stop only a time with the night, and rise betimes,

[019-256]I will duly pass the day O my mother, and duly return to you.

[019-257]Transpositions

[019-258]Let the reformers descend from the stands where they are forever

[019-259]bawling--let an idiot or insane person appear on each of the stands;

[019-260]Let judges and criminals be transposed--let the prison-keepers be

[019-261]put in prison--let those that were prisoners take the keys;

[019-262]Let them that distrust birth and death lead the rest.
