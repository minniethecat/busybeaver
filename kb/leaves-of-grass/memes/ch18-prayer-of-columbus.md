# BOOK XXVII: Prayer of Columbus

[018-001]Prayer of Columbus

[018-002]A batter’d, wreck’d old man,

[018-003]Thrown on this savage shore, far, far from home,

[018-004]Pent by [[sea|the sea]] and dark rebellious brows, twelve dreary months,

[018-005]Sore, stiff with many toils, sicken’d and nigh to [[death]],

[018-006]I take my way along the island’s edge,

[018-007]Venting a heavy heart.

[018-008]I am too full of woe!

[018-009]Haply I may not live another day;

[018-010]I cannot rest O [[god|God]], I cannot eat or drink or sleep,

[018-011]Till I put forth [[self|myself]], my prayer, once more to Thee,

[018-012]Breathe, bathe myself once more in Thee, commune with Thee,

[018-013]Report myself once more to Thee.

[018-014]Thou knowest my years entire, my life,

[018-015]My long and crowded life of active work, not adoration merely;

[018-016]Thou knowest the prayers and vigils of my youth,

[018-017]Thou knowest my manhood’s solemn and visionary meditations,

[018-018]Thou knowest how before I commenced I devoted all to come to Thee,

[018-019]Thou knowest I have in age ratified all those vows and strictly kept them,

[018-020]Thou knowest I have not once lost nor faith nor [[joy|ecstasy]] in Thee,

[018-021]In shackles, prison’d, in disgrace, repining not,

[018-022]Accepting all from Thee, as duly come from Thee.

[018-023]All my emprises have been fill’d with Thee,

[018-024]My speculations, plans, begun and carried on in thoughts of Thee,

[018-025]Sailing the deep or journeying the [[earth|land]] for Thee;

[018-026]Intentions, purports, aspirations mine, leaving results to Thee.

[018-027]O I am sure they really came from Thee,

[018-028]The urge, the ardor, the unconquerable will,

[018-029]The potent, felt, interior command, stronger than words,

[018-030]A message from the Heavens whispering to me even in sleep,

[018-031]These sped me on.

[018-032]By me and these the work so far accomplish’d,

[018-033]By me earth’s elder cloy’d and stifled lands uncloy’d, unloos’d,

[018-034]By me the hemispheres rounded and tied, the unknown to the known.

[018-035]The end I know not, it is all in Thee,

[018-036]Or small or great I know not--haply what broad fields, what lands,

[018-037]Haply the brutish measureless human undergrowth I know,

[018-038]Transplanted there may rise to stature, knowledge worthy Thee,

[018-039]Haply the swords I know may there indeed be turn’d to reaping-tools,

[018-040]Haply the lifeless cross I know, Europe’s dead cross, may bud and

[018-041]blossom there.

[018-042]One effort more, my altar this bleak sand;

[018-043]That Thou O God my life hast lighted,

[018-044]With ray of light, steady, ineffable, vouchsafed of Thee,

[018-045]Light rare untellable, lighting the very light,

[018-046]Beyond all signs, descriptions, languages;

[018-047]For that O God, be it my latest word, here on my knees,

[018-048]Old, poor, and paralyzed, I thank Thee.

[018-049]My terminus near,

[018-050]The clouds already closing in upon me,

[018-051]The voyage balk’d, the course disputed, lost,

[018-052]I yield my ships to Thee.

[018-053]My hands, my limbs grow nerveless,

[018-054]My brain feels rack’d, bewilder’d,

[018-055]Let the old timbers part, I will not part,

[018-056]I will cling fast to Thee, O God, though the waves buffet me,

[018-057]Thee, Thee at least I know.

[018-058]Is it the prophet’s thought I speak, or am I raving?

[018-059]What do I know of life? what of myself?

[018-060]I know not even my own work past or present,

[018-061]Dim ever-shifting guesses of it spread before me,

[018-062]Of newer better worlds, their mighty parturition,

[018-063]Mocking, perplexing me.

[018-064]And these things I see suddenly, what mean they?

[018-065]As if some miracle, some hand divine unseal’d my eyes,

[018-066]Shadowy vast shapes smile through the air and sky,

[018-067]And on the distant waves sail countless ships,

[018-068]And anthems in new tongues I hear saluting me.
