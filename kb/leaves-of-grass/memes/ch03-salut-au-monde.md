# BOOK VI: Salut au Monde!

[003-001]Salut au Monde!

[003-002]1

[003-003]O take my hand Walt Whitman!

[003-004]Such gliding wonders! such sights and sounds!

[003-005]Such join’d unended links, each hook’d to the next,

[003-006]Each answering all, each sharing [[earth|the earth]] with all.

[003-007]What widens within you Walt Whitman?

[003-008]What waves and soils exuding?

[003-009]What climes? what persons and cities are here?

[003-010]Who are the infants, some playing, some slumbering?

[003-011]Who are the girls? who are the married women?

[003-012]Who are the groups of old men going slowly with their arms about

[003-013]each other’s necks?

[003-014]What rivers are these? what forests and fruits are these?

[003-015]What are the mountains call’d that rise so high in the mists?

[003-016]What myriads of dwellings are they fill’d with dwellers?

[003-017]2

[003-018]Within me latitude widens, longitude lengthens,

[003-019]Asia, Africa, Europe, are to the east--[[america|America]] is provided for in the west,

[003-020]Banding the bulge of the earth winds the hot equator,

[003-021]Curiously north and south turn the axis-ends,

[003-022]Within me is the longest day, the sun wheels in slanting rings, it

[003-023]does not set for months,

[003-024]Stretch’d in due [[time]] within me the midnight sun just rises above

[003-025]the horizon and sinks again,

[003-026]Within me zones, seas, cataracts, forests, volcanoes, groups,

[003-027]Malaysia, Polynesia, and the great West Indian islands.

[003-028]3

[003-029]What do you hear Walt Whitman?

[003-030]I hear [[workman|the workman]] singing and [[farmer|the farmer]]’s wife singing,

[003-031]I hear in the distance the sounds of children and of animals early

[003-032]in the day,

[003-033]I hear emulous shouts of Australians pursuing the wild horse,

[003-034]I hear the Spanish dance with castanets in the chestnut shade, to

[003-035]the rebeck and guitar,

[003-036]I hear continual echoes from the Thames,

[003-037]I hear fierce French [[freedom|liberty]] songs,

[003-038]I hear of the Italian boat-sculler the musical recitative of old poems,

[003-039]I hear the locusts in Syria as they strike the grain and [[grass]] with

[003-040]the showers of their terrible clouds,

[003-041]I hear the Coptic refrain toward sundown, pensively falling on the

[003-042]breast of the black venerable vast [[mother]] the Nile,

[003-043]I hear the chirp of the Mexican muleteer, and the bells of the mule,

[003-044]I hear the Arab muezzin calling from the top of the mosque,

[003-045]I hear the Christian priests at the altars of their churches, I hear

[003-046]the responsive base and soprano,

[003-047]I hear the cry of the Cossack, and [[sailor|the sailor]]’s voice putting to [[sea]]

[003-048]at Okotsk,

[003-049]I hear the wheeze of the slave-coffle as the slaves march on, as the

[003-050]husky gangs pass on by twos and threes, fasten’d together

[003-051]with wrist-chains and ankle-chains,

[003-052]I hear the Hebrew reading his records and psalms,

[003-053]I hear the rhythmic myths of the Greeks, and the strong legends of

[003-054]the Romans,

[003-055]I hear the tale of [[god|the divine]] life and bloody [[death]] of the beautiful

[003-056]God the Christ,

[003-057]I hear the Hindoo teaching his favorite pupil the loves, wars,

[003-058]adages, transmitted safely to this day from poets who wrote three

[003-059]thousand years ago.

[003-060]4

[003-061]What do you see Walt Whitman?

[003-062]Who are they you salute, and that one after another salute you?

[003-063]I see a great round wonder rolling through space,

[003-064]I see diminute farms, hamlets, ruins, graveyards, jails, factories,

[003-065]palaces, hovels, huts of barbarians, tents of nomads upon the surface,

[003-066]I see the shaded part on one side where the sleepers are sleeping,

[003-067]and the sunlit part on the other side,

[003-068]I see the curious rapid change of the light and shade,

[003-069]I see distant lands, as real and near to the inhabitants of them as

[003-070]my land is to me.

[003-071]I see plenteous waters,

[003-072]I see mountain peaks, I see the sierras of Andes where they range,

[003-073]I see plainly the Himalayas, Chian Shahs, Altays, Ghauts,

[003-074]I see the giant pinnacles of Elbruz, Kazbek, Bazardjusi,

[003-075]I see the Styrian Alps, and the Karnac Alps,

[003-076]I see the Pyrenees, Balks, Carpathians, and to the north the

[003-077]Dofrafields, and off at sea mount Hecla,

[003-078]I see Vesuvius and Etna, the mountains of the Moon, and the Red

[003-079]mountains of Madagascar,

[003-080]I see the Lybian, Arabian, and Asiatic deserts,

[003-081]I see huge dreadful Arctic and Antarctic icebergs,

[003-082]I see the superior oceans and the inferior ones, the Atlantic and

[003-083]Pacific, the sea of Mexico, the Brazilian sea, and the sea of Peru,

[003-084]The waters of Hindustan, the China sea, and the gulf of Guinea,

[003-085]The Japan waters, the beautiful bay of Nagasaki land-lock’d in its

[003-086]mountains,

[003-087]The spread of the Baltic, Caspian, Bothnia, the British shores, and

[003-088]the bay of Biscay,

[003-089]The clear-sunn’d Mediterranean, and from one to another of its islands,

[003-090]The White sea, and the sea around Greenland.

[003-091]I behold the mariners of the world,

[003-092]Some are in storms, some in [[night|the night]] with the watch on the lookout,

[003-093]Some drifting helplessly, some with contagious diseases.

[003-094]I behold the sail and steamships of the world, some in clusters in

[003-095]port, some on their voyages,

[003-096]Some double the cape of Storms, some cape Verde, others capes

[003-097]Guardafui, Bon, or Bajadore,

[003-098]Others Dondra head, others pass the straits of Sunda, others cape

[003-099]Lopatka, others Behring’s straits,

[003-100]Others cape Horn, others sail the gulf of Mexico or along Cuba or

[003-101]Hayti, others Hudson’s bay or Baffin’s bay,

[003-102]Others pass the straits of Dover, others enter the Wash, others the

[003-103]firth of Solway, others round cape Clear, others the Land’s End,

[003-104]Others traverse the Zuyder Zee or the Scheld,

[003-105]Others as comers and goers at Gibraltar or the Dardanelles,

[003-106]Others sternly push their way through the northern winter-packs,

[003-107]Others descend or ascend the Obi or the Lena,

[003-108]Others the Niger or the Congo, others the Indus, the Burampooter

[003-109]and Cambodia,

[003-110]Others wait steam’d up ready to start in the ports of Australia,

[003-111]Wait at Liverpool, Glasgow, Dublin, Marseilles, Lisbon, Naples,

[003-112]Hamburg, Bremen, Bordeaux, the Hague, Copenhagen,

[003-113]Wait at Valparaiso, Rio Janeiro, Panama.

[003-114]5

[003-115]I see the tracks of the railroads of the earth,

[003-116]I see them in Great Britain, I see them in Europe,

[003-117]I see them in Asia and in Africa.

[003-118]I see the electric telegraphs of the earth,

[003-119]I see the filaments of the news of the wars, deaths, losses, gains,

[003-120]passions, of my race.

[003-121]I see the long river-stripes of the earth,

[003-122]I see the Amazon and the Paraguay,

[003-123]I see the four great rivers of China, the Amour, the Yellow River,

[003-124]the Yiang-tse, and the Pearl,

[003-125]I see where the Seine flows, and where the Danube, the Loire, the

[003-126]Rhone, and the Guadalquiver flow,

[003-127]I see the windings of the Volga, the Dnieper, the Oder,

[003-128]I see the Tuscan going down the Arno, and the Venetian along the Po,

[003-129]I see the Greek seaman sailing out of Egina bay.

[003-130]6

[003-131]I see the site of the old empire of Assyria, and that of Persia, and

[003-132]that of India,

[003-133]I see the falling of the Ganges over the high rim of Saukara.

[003-134]I see the place of the idea of the Deity incarnated by avatars in

[003-135]human forms,

[003-136]I see the spots of the successions of priests on the earth, oracles,

[003-137]sacrificers, brahmins, sabians, llamas, monks, muftis, exhorters,

[003-138]I see where druids walk’d the groves of Mona, I see the mistletoe

[003-139]and vervain,

[003-140]I see the temples of the deaths of the bodies of Gods, I see the old

[003-141]signifiers.

[003-142]I see Christ eating the bread of his last supper in the midst of

[003-143]youths and old persons,

[003-144]I see where the strong divine young man the Hercules toil’d

[003-145]faithfully and long and then died,

[003-146]I see the place of the innocent rich life and hapless fate of the

[003-147]beautiful nocturnal son, the full-limb’d Bacchus,

[003-148]I see Kneph, blooming, drest in blue, with the crown of feathers on

[003-149]his head,

[003-150]I see Hermes, unsuspected, dying, well-belov’d, saying to the people

[003-151]Do not weep for me,

[003-152]This is not my true country, I have lived banish’d from my true

[003-153]country, I now go back there,

[003-154]I return to the celestial sphere where every one goes in his turn.

[003-155]7

[003-156]I see the [[war|battle]]-fields of the earth, grass grows upon them and

[003-157]blossoms and corn,

[003-158]I see the tracks of ancient and modern expeditions.

[003-159]I see the nameless masonries, venerable messages of the unknown

[003-160]events, heroes, records of the earth.

[003-161]I see the places of the sagas,

[003-162]I see pine-trees and fir-trees torn by northern blasts,

[003-163]I see granite bowlders and cliffs, I see green meadows and lakes,

[003-164]I see the burial-cairns of Scandinavian warriors,

[003-165]I see them raised high with stones by the marge of restless oceans,

[003-166]that the dead men’s spirits when they wearied of their quiet

[003-167]graves might rise up through the mounds and gaze on the tossing

[003-168]billows, and be refresh’d by storms, immensity, liberty, action.

[003-169]I see the steppes of Asia,

[003-170]I see the tumuli of Mongolia, I see the tents of Kalmucks and Baskirs,

[003-171]I see the nomadic tribes with herds of oxen and cows,

[003-172]I see the table-lands notch’d with ravines, I see the jungles and deserts,

[003-173]I see the camel, the wild steed, the bustard, the fat-tail’d sheep,

[003-174]the antelope, and the burrowing wolf

[003-175]I see the highlands of Abyssinia,

[003-176]I see flocks of goats feeding, and see the fig-tree, tamarind, date,

[003-177]And see fields of teff-wheat and places of verdure and gold.

[003-178]I see the Brazilian vaquero,

[003-179]I see the Bolivian ascending mount Sorata,

[003-180]I see the Wacho crossing [[prairie|the plains]], I see the incomparable rider of

[003-181]horses with his lasso on his arm,

[003-182]I see over the pampas the pursuit of wild cattle for their hides.

[003-183]8

[003-184]I see the regions of snow and ice,

[003-185]I see the sharp-eyed Samoiede and the Finn,

[003-186]I see the seal-seeker in his boat poising his lance,

[003-187]I see the Siberian on his slight-built sledge drawn by dogs,

[003-188]I see the porpoise-hunters, I see the whale-crews of the south

[003-189]Pacific and the north Atlantic,

[003-190]I see the cliffs, glaciers, torrents, valleys, of Switzerland--I

[003-191]mark the long winters and the isolation.

[003-192]I see the cities of the earth and make [[self|myself]] at random a part of them,

[003-193]I am a real Parisian,

[003-194]I am a habitan of Vienna, St. Petersburg, Berlin, Constantinople,

[003-195]I am of Adelaide, Sidney, Melbourne,

[003-196]I am of London, Manchester, Bristol, Edinburgh, Limerick,

[003-197]I am of Madrid, Cadiz, Barcelona, Oporto, Lyons, Brussels, Berne,

[003-198]Frankfort, Stuttgart, Turin, Florence,

[003-199]I belong in Moscow, Cracow, Warsaw, or northward in Christiania or

[003-200]Stockholm, or in Siberian Irkutsk, or in some street in Iceland,

[003-201]I descend upon all those cities, and rise from them again.

[003-202]10

[003-203]I see vapors exhaling from unexplored countries,

[003-204]I see the savage types, the bow and arrow, the poison’d splint, the

[003-205]fetich, and the obi.

[003-206]I see African and Asiatic towns,

[003-207]I see Algiers, Tripoli, Derne, Mogadore, Timbuctoo, Monrovia,

[003-208]I see the swarms of Pekin, Canton, Benares, Delhi, Calcutta, Tokio,

[003-209]I see the Kruman in his hut, and the Dahoman and Ashantee-man in their huts,

[003-210]I see the Turk smoking opium in Aleppo,

[003-211]I see the picturesque crowds at the fairs of Khiva and those of Herat,

[003-212]I see Teheran, I see Muscat and Medina and the intervening sands,

[003-213]see the caravans toiling onward,

[003-214]I see Egypt and the Egyptians, I see the pyramids and obelisks.

[003-215]I look on chisell’d histories, records of conquering kings,

[003-216]dynasties, cut in slabs of sand-stone, or on granite-blocks,

[003-217]I see at Memphis mummy-pits containing mummies embalm’d,

[003-218]swathed in linen cloth, lying there many centuries,

[003-219]I look on the fall’n Theban, the large-ball’d eyes, the

[003-220]side-drooping neck, the hands folded across the breast.

[003-221]I see all the menials of the earth, laboring,

[003-222]I see all the prisoners in the prisons,

[003-223]I see the defective human bodies of the earth,

[003-224]The blind, the deaf and dumb, idiots, hunchbacks, lunatics,

[003-225]The pirates, thieves, betrayers, murderers, slave-makers of the earth,

[003-226]The helpless infants, and the helpless old men and women.

[003-227]I see male and female everywhere,

[003-228]I see the serene brotherhood of philosophs,

[003-229]I see the constructiveness of my race,

[003-230]I see the results of the perseverance and industry of my race,

[003-231]I see ranks, colors, barbarisms, civilizations, I go among them, I

[003-232]mix indiscriminately,

[003-233]And I salute all the inhabitants of the earth.

[003-234]11

[003-235]You whoever you are!

[003-236]You daughter or son of England!

[003-237]You of the mighty Slavic tribes and empires! you Russ in Russia!

[003-238]You dim-descended, black, divine-[[soul]]’d African, large, fine-headed,

[003-239]nobly-form’d, superbly destin’d, on equal terms with me!

[003-240]You Norwegian! Swede! Dane! Icelander! you Prussian!

[003-241]You Spaniard of Spain! you Portuguese!

[003-242]You Frenchwoman and Frenchman of France!

[003-243]You Belge! you liberty-lover of the Netherlands! (you stock whence I

[003-244]myself have descended;)

[003-245]You sturdy Austrian! you Lombard! Hun! Bohemian! farmer of Styria!

[003-246]You neighbor of the Danube!

[003-247]You working-man of the Rhine, the Elbe, or the Weser! you working-woman too!

[003-248]You Sardinian! you Bavarian! Swabian! Saxon! Wallachian! Bulgarian!

[003-249]You Roman! Neapolitan! you Greek!

[003-250]You lithe matador in the arena at Seville!

[003-251]You mountaineer living lawlessly on the Taurus or Caucasus!

[003-252]You Bokh horse-herd watching your mares and stallions feeding!

[003-253]You beautiful-bodied Persian at full speed in the saddle shooting

[003-254]arrows to the mark!

[003-255]You Chinaman and Chinawoman of China! you Tartar of Tartary!

[003-256]You women of the earth subordinated at your tasks!

[003-257]You Jew journeying in your old age through every risk to stand once

[003-258]on Syrian ground!

[003-259]You other Jews waiting in all lands for your Messiah!

[003-260]You thoughtful Armenian pondering by some stream of the Euphrates!

[003-261]you peering amid the ruins of Nineveh! you ascending mount Ararat!

[003-262]You foot-worn pilgrim welcoming the far-away sparkle of the minarets

[003-263]of Mecca!

[003-264]You sheiks along the stretch from Suez to Bab-el-mandeb ruling your

[003-265]families and tribes!

[003-266]You olive-grower tending your fruit on fields of Nazareth, Damascus,

[003-267]or lake Tiberias!

[003-268]You Thibet trader on the wide inland or bargaining in the shops of Lassa!

[003-269]You Japanese man or woman! you liver in Madagascar, Ceylon, Sumatra, Borneo!

[003-270]All you continentals of Asia, Africa, Europe, Australia, indifferent

[003-271]of place!

[003-272]All you on the numberless islands of the archipelagoes of the sea!

[003-273]And you of centuries hence when you listen to me!

[003-274]And you each and everywhere whom I specify not, but include just the same!

[003-275]Health to you! good will to you all, from me and America sent!

[003-276]Each of us inevitable,

[003-277]Each of us limitless--each of us with his or her right upon the earth,

[003-278]Each of us allow’d the eternal purports of the earth,

[003-279]Each of us here as divinely as any is here.

[003-280]12

[003-281]You Hottentot with clicking palate! you woolly-hair’d hordes!

[003-282]You own’d persons dropping sweat-drops or blood-drops!

[003-283]You human forms with the fathomless ever-impressive countenances of brutes!

[003-284]You poor koboo whom the meanest of the rest look down upon for all

[003-285]your glimmering language and spirituality!

[003-286]You dwarf’d Kamtschatkan, Greenlander, Lapp!

[003-287]You Austral negro, naked, red, sooty, with protrusive lip,

[003-288]groveling, seeking your food!

[003-289]You Caffre, Berber, Soudanese!

[003-290]You haggard, uncouth, untutor’d Bedowee!

[003-291]You plague-swarms in Madras, Nankin, Kaubul, Cairo!

[003-292]You benighted roamer of Amazonia! you Patagonian! you Feejeeman!

[003-293]I do not prefer others so very much before you either,

[003-294]I do not say one word against you, away back there where you stand,

[003-295](You will come forward in due time to my side.)

[003-296]13

[003-297]My spirit has pass’d in compassion and determination around the whole earth,

[003-298]I have look’d for equals and lovers and found them ready for me in

[003-299]all lands,

[003-300]I think some divine rapport has equalized me with them.

[003-301]You vapors, I think I have risen with you, moved away to distant

[003-302]continents, and fallen down there, for reasons,

[003-303]I think I have blown with you you winds;

[003-304]You waters I have finger’d every shore with you,

[003-305]I have run through what any river or strait of the globe has run through,

[003-306]I have taken my stand on the bases of peninsulas and on the high

[003-307]embedded rocks, to cry thence:

[003-308]What cities the light or warmth penetrates I penetrate those cities myself,

[003-309]All islands to which birds wing their way I wing my way myself.

[003-310]Toward you all, in America’s name,

[003-311]I raise high the perpendicular hand, I make the signal,

[003-312]To remain after me in sight forever,

[003-313]For all the haunts and homes of men.
