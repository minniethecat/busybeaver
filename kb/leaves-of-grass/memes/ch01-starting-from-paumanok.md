# BOOK II: Starting from Paumanok

[001-001]Starting from [[paumanok|Paumanok]]

[001-002]1

[001-003]Starting from fish-shape Paumanok where I was born,

[001-004]Well-begotten, and rais’d by a perfect [[mother]],

[001-005]After roaming many lands, lover of populous pavements,

[001-006]Dweller in Mannahatta my city, or on southern savannas,

[001-007]Or a [[soldier]] camp’d or carrying my knapsack and gun, or a [[miner]]

[001-008]in [[california|California]],

[001-009]Or rude in my home in Dakota’s woods, my diet meat, my drink from

[001-010]the spring,

[001-011]Or withdrawn to muse and meditate in some deep recess,

[001-012]Far from the clank of crowds intervals passing rapt and happy,

[001-013]Aware of the fresh [[freedom|free]] giver the flowing Missouri, aware of

[001-014]mighty [[niagara|Niagara]],

[001-015]Aware of the buffalo herds grazing [[prairie|the plains]], the hirsute and

[001-016]strong-breasted bull,

[001-017]Of [[earth]], rocks, Fifth-month flowers experienced, stars, rain, snow,

[001-018]my amaze,

[001-019]Having studied the mocking-bird’s tones and the flight of the

[001-020]mountain-hawk,

[001-021]And heard at dawn the unrivall’d one, the hermit thrush from the

[001-022]swamp-cedars,

[001-023]Solitary, singing in [[the-west|the West]], I strike up for a New World.

[001-024]2

[001-025]Victory, union, faith, identity, [[time]],

[001-026]The indissoluble compacts, riches, mystery,

[001-027]Eternal progress, the kosmos, and the modern reports.

[001-028]This then is life,

[001-029]Here is what has come to the surface after so many throes and convulsions.

[001-030]How curious! how real!

[001-031]Underfoot [[god|the divine]] soil, overhead the sun.

[001-032]See revolving the globe,

[001-033]The ancestor-continents away group’d together,

[001-034]The present and future continents north and south, with the isthmus

[001-035]between.

[001-036]See, vast trackless spaces,

[001-037]As in a dream they change, they swiftly fill,

[001-038]Countless masses debouch upon them,

[001-039]They are now cover’d with the foremost people, arts, institutions, known.

[001-040]See, projected through time,

[001-041]For me an audience interminable.

[001-042]With firm and regular step they wend, they never stop,

[001-043]Successions of men, Americanos, a hundred millions,

[001-044]One generation playing its part and passing on,

[001-045]Another generation playing its part and passing on in its turn,

[001-046]With faces turn’d sideways or backward towards me to listen,

[001-047]With eyes retrospective towards me.

[001-048]3

[001-049]Americanos! conquerors! marches humanitarian!

[001-050]Foremost! century marches! Libertad! masses!

[001-051]For you a programme of chants.

[001-052]Chants of the prairies,

[001-053]Chants of the long-running Mississippi, and down to the Mexican [[sea]],

[001-054]Chants of Ohio, Indiana, Illinois, Iowa, Wisconsin and Minnesota,

[001-055]Chants going forth from the centre from Kansas, and thence equidistant,

[001-056]Shooting in pulses of fire ceaseless to vivify all.

[001-057]4

[001-058]Take my leaves [[america|America]], take them South and take them North,

[001-059]Make welcome for them everywhere, for they are your own off-spring,

[001-060]Surround them East and West, for they would surround you,

[001-061]And you precedents, connect lovingly with them, for they connect

[001-062]lovingly with you.

[001-063]I conn’d old times,

[001-064]I sat studying at the feet of the great masters,

[001-065]Now if eligible O that the great masters might return and study me.

[001-066]In the name of these States shall I scorn the antique?

[001-067]Why these are the children of the antique to justify it.

[001-068]5

[001-069]Dead poets, philosophs, priests,

[001-070]Martyrs, artists, inventors, governments long since,

[001-071]Language-shapers on other shores,

[001-072]Nations once powerful, now reduced, withdrawn, or desolate,

[001-073]I dare not proceed till I respectfully credit what you have left

[001-074]wafted hither,

[001-075]I have perused it, own it is admirable, (moving awhile among it,)

[001-076]Think nothing can ever be greater, nothing can ever deserve more

[001-077]than it deserves,

[001-078]Regarding it all intently a long while, then dismissing it,

[001-079]I stand in my place with my own day here.

[001-080]Here lands female and male,

[001-081]Here the heir-ship and heiress-ship of the world, here the flame of

[001-082]materials,

[001-083]Here spirituality the translatress, the openly-avow’d,

[001-084]The ever-tending, the finale of visible forms,

[001-085]The satisfier, after due long-waiting now advancing,

[001-086]Yes here comes my mistress [[soul|the soul]].

[001-087]6

[001-088]The soul,

[001-089]Forever and forever--longer than soil is brown and solid--longer

[001-090]than water ebbs and flows.

[001-091]I will make the poems of materials, for I think they are to be the

[001-092]most spiritual poems,

[001-093]And I will make the poems of my [[body]] and of [[death|mortality]],

[001-094]For I think I shall then supply [[self|myself]] with the poems of my soul and

[001-095]of [[immortality]].

[001-096]I will make a song for these States that no one State may under any

[001-097]circumstances be subjected to another State,

[001-098]And I will make a song that there shall be comity by day and by

[001-099][[night]] between all the States, and between any two of them,

[001-100]And I will make a song for the ears of the President, full of

[001-101]weapons with menacing points,

[001-102]And behind the weapons countless dissatisfied faces;

[001-103]And a song make I of the One form’d out of all,

[001-104]The fang’d and glittering One whose head is over all,

[001-105]Resolute warlike One including and over all,

[001-106](However high the head of any else that head is over all.)

[001-107]I will acknowledge contemporary lands,

[001-108]I will trail the whole geography of the globe and salute courteously

[001-109]every city large and small,

[001-110]And employments! I will put in my poems that with you is heroism

[001-111]upon land and sea,

[001-112]And I will report all heroism from an American point of view.

[001-113]I will sing the song of companionship,

[001-114]I will show what alone must finally compact these,

[001-115]I believe these are to found their own ideal of [[love|manly love]],

[001-116]indicating it in me,

[001-117]I will therefore let flame from me the burning fires that were

[001-118]threatening to consume me,

[001-119]I will lift what has too long kept down those smouldering fires,

[001-120]I will give them complete abandonment,

[001-121]I will write the evangel-poem of comrades and of love,

[001-122]For who but I should understand love with all its sorrow and [[joy]]?

[001-123]And who but I should be the poet of comrades?

[001-124]7

[001-125]I am the credulous man of qualities, ages, races,

[001-126]I advance from the people in their own spirit,

[001-127]Here is what sings unrestricted faith.

[001-128]Omnes! omnes! let others ignore what they may,

[001-129]I make the poem of evil also, I commemorate that part also,

[001-130]I am myself just as much evil as good, and my nation is--and I say

[001-131]there is in fact no evil,

[001-132](Or if there is I say it is just as important to you, to the land or

[001-133]to me, as any thing else.)

[001-134]I too, following many and follow’d by many, inaugurate a religion, I

[001-135]descend into the arena,

[001-136](It may be I am destin’d to utter the loudest cries there, the

[001-137]winner’s pealing shouts,

[001-138]Who knows? they may rise from me yet, and soar above every thing.)

[001-139]Each is not for its own sake,

[001-140]I say the whole earth and all the stars in the sky are for religion’s sake.

[001-141]I say no man has ever yet been half devout enough,

[001-142]None has ever yet adored or worship’d half enough,

[001-143]None has begun to think how divine he himself is, and how certain

[001-144]the future is.

[001-145]I say that the real and permanent grandeur of these States must be

[001-146]their religion,

[001-147]Otherwise there is just no real and permanent grandeur;

[001-148](Nor character nor life worthy the name without religion,

[001-149]Nor land nor man or woman without religion.)

[001-150]8

[001-151]What are you doing young man?

[001-152]Are you so earnest, so given up to literature, science, art, amours?

[001-153]These ostensible realities, politics, points?

[001-154]Your ambition or business whatever it may be?

[001-155]It is well--against such I say not a word, I am their poet also,

[001-156]But behold! such swiftly subside, burnt up for religion’s sake,

[001-157]For not all matter is fuel to heat, impalpable flame, the essential

[001-158]life of the earth,

[001-159]Any more than such are to religion.

[001-160]9

[001-161]What do you seek so pensive and silent?

[001-162]What do you need camerado?

[001-163]Dear son do you think it is love?

[001-164]Listen dear son--listen America, daughter or son,

[001-165]It is a painful thing to love a man or woman to excess, and yet it

[001-166]satisfies, it is great,

[001-167]But there is something else very great, it makes the whole coincide,

[001-168]It, magnificent, beyond materials, with continuous hands sweeps and

[001-169]provides for all.

[001-170]10

[001-171]Know you, solely to drop in the earth the germs of a greater religion,

[001-172]The following chants each for its kind I sing.

[001-173]My [[comrade]]!

[001-174]For you to share with me two greatnesses, and a third one rising

[001-175]inclusive and more resplendent,

[001-176]The greatness of Love and Democracy, and the greatness of Religion.

[001-177]Melange mine own, the unseen and the seen,

[001-178]Mysterious ocean where the streams empty,

[001-179]Prophetic spirit of materials shifting and flickering around me,

[001-180]Living beings, identities now doubtless near us in the air that we

[001-181]know not of,

[001-182]Contact daily and hourly that will not release me,

[001-183]These selecting, these in hints demanded of me.

[001-184]Not he with a daily kiss onward from childhood kissing me,

[001-185]Has winded and twisted around me that which holds me to him,

[001-186]Any more than I am held to the heavens and all the spiritual world,

[001-187]After what they have done to me, suggesting themes.

[001-188]O such themes--equalities! O divine average!

[001-189]Warblings under the sun, usher’d as now, or at noon, or setting,

[001-190]Strains musical flowing through ages, now reaching hither,

[001-191]I take to your reckless and composite chords, add to them, and

[001-192]cheerfully pass them forward.

[001-193]11

[001-194]As I have walk’d in Alabama my morning walk,

[001-195]I have seen where the she-bird the mocking-bird sat on her nest in

[001-196]the briers hatching her brood.

[001-197]I have seen the he-bird also,

[001-198]I have paus’d to hear him near at hand inflating his throat and

[001-199]joyfully singing.

[001-200]And while I paus’d it came to me that what he really sang for was

[001-201]not there only,

[001-202]Nor for his mate nor himself only, nor all sent back by the echoes,

[001-203]But subtle, clandestine, away beyond,

[001-204]A charge transmitted and gift occult for those being born.

[001-205]12

[001-206]Democracy! near at hand to you a throat is now inflating itself and

[001-207]joyfully singing.

[001-208]Ma femme! for the brood beyond us and of us,

[001-209]For those who belong here and those to come,

[001-210]I exultant to be ready for them will now shake out carols stronger

[001-211]and haughtier than have ever yet been heard upon earth.

[001-212]I will make the songs of passion to give them their way,

[001-213]And your songs outlaw’d offenders, for I scan you with kindred eyes,

[001-214]and carry you with me the same as any.

[001-215]I will make the true poem of riches,

[001-216]To earn for the body and the mind whatever adheres and goes forward

[001-217]and is not dropt by death;

[001-218]I will effuse egotism and show it underlying all, and I will be the

[001-219]bard of personality,

[001-220]And I will show of male and female that either is but the equal of

[001-221]the other,

[001-222]And sexual organs and acts! do you concentrate in me, for I am determin’d

[001-223]to tell you with courageous clear voice to prove you illustrious,

[001-224]And I will show that there is no imperfection in the present, and

[001-225]can be none in the future,

[001-226]And I will show that whatever happens to anybody it may be turn’d to

[001-227]beautiful results,

[001-228]And I will show that nothing can happen more beautiful than death,

[001-229]And I will thread a thread through my poems that time and events are

[001-230]compact,

[001-231]And that all the things of the universe are perfect miracles, each

[001-232]as profound as any.

[001-233]I will not make poems with reference to parts,

[001-234]But I will make poems, songs, thoughts, with reference to ensemble,

[001-235]And I will not sing with reference to a day, but with reference to

[001-236]all days,

[001-237]And I will not make a poem nor the least part of a poem but has

[001-238]reference to the soul,

[001-239]Because having look’d at the objects of the universe, I find there

[001-240]is no one nor any particle of one but has reference to the soul.

[001-241]13

[001-242]Was somebody asking to see the soul?

[001-243]See, your own shape and countenance, persons, substances, beasts,

[001-244]the trees, the running rivers, the rocks and sands.

[001-245]All hold spiritual joys and afterwards loosen them;

[001-246]How can the real body ever die and be buried?

[001-247]Of your real body and any man’s or woman’s real body,

[001-248]Item for item it will elude the hands of the corpse-cleaners and

[001-249]pass to fitting spheres,

[001-250]Carrying what has accrued to it from the moment of birth to the

[001-251]moment of death.

[001-252]Not the types set up by the printer return their impression, the

[001-253]meaning, the main concern,

[001-254]Any more than a man’s substance and life or a woman’s substance and

[001-255]life return in the body and the soul,

[001-256]Indifferently before death and after death.

[001-257]Behold, the body includes and is the meaning, the main concern and

[001-258]includes and is the soul;

[001-259]Whoever you are, how superb and how divine is your body, or any part

[001-260]of it!

[001-261]14

[001-262]Whoever you are, to you endless announcements!

[001-263]Daughter of the lands did you wait for your poet?

[001-264]Did you wait for one with a flowing mouth and indicative hand?

[001-265]Toward the male of the States, and toward the female of the States,

[001-266]Exulting words, words to Democracy’s lands.

[001-267]Interlink’d, food-yielding lands!

[001-268]Land of coal and iron! land of gold! land of cotton, sugar, rice!

[001-269]Land of wheat, beef, pork! land of wool and hemp! land of the apple

[001-270]and the grape!

[001-271]Land of the pastoral plains, [[grass|the grass]]-fields of the world! land of

[001-272]those sweet-air’d interminable plateaus!

[001-273]Land of the herd, the garden, the healthy house of adobie!

[001-274]Lands where the north-west Columbia winds, and where the south-west

[001-275]Colorado winds!

[001-276]Land of the eastern Chesapeake! land of the Delaware!

[001-277]Land of Ontario, Erie, Huron, Michigan!

[001-278]Land of the Old Thirteen! Massachusetts land! land of Vermont and

[001-279]Connecticut!

[001-280]Land of the ocean shores! land of sierras and peaks!

[001-281]Land of boatmen and sailors! fishermen’s land!

[001-282]Inextricable lands! the clutch’d together! the passionate ones!

[001-283]The side by side! the elder and younger brothers! the bony-limb’d!

[001-284]The great women’s land! the feminine! the experienced sisters and

[001-285]the inexperienced sisters!

[001-286]Far breath’d land! Arctic braced! Mexican breez’d! the diverse! the

[001-287]compact!

[001-288]The Pennsylvanian! the Virginian! the double Carolinian!

[001-289]O all and each well-loved by me! my intrepid nations! O I at any

[001-290]rate include you all with perfect love!

[001-291]I cannot be discharged from you! not from one any sooner than another!

[001-292]O death! O for all that, I am yet of you unseen this hour with

[001-293]irrepressible love,

[001-294]Walking [[new-england|New England]], a friend, a traveler,

[001-295]Splashing my bare feet in the edge of the summer ripples on

[001-296]Paumanok’s sands,

[001-297]Crossing the prairies, dwelling again in [[chicago|Chicago]], dwelling in every town,

[001-298]Observing shows, births, improvements, structures, arts,

[001-299]Listening to orators and oratresses in public halls,

[001-300]Of and through the States as during life, each man and woman my neighbor,

[001-301]The Louisianian, the Georgian, as near to me, and I as near to him and her,

[001-302]The Mississippian and Arkansian yet with me, and I yet with any of them,

[001-303]Yet upon the plains west of the spinal river, yet in my house of adobie,

[001-304]Yet returning eastward, yet in the Seaside State or in Maryland,

[001-305]Yet Kanadian cheerily braving the winter, the snow and ice welcome to me,

[001-306]Yet a true son either of Maine or of the Granite State, or the

[001-307]Narragansett Bay State, or the Empire State,

[001-308]Yet sailing to other shores to annex the same, yet welcoming every

[001-309]new brother,

[001-310]Hereby applying these leaves to the new ones from the hour they

[001-311]unite with the old ones,

[001-312]Coming among the new ones myself to be their companion and equal,

[001-313]coming personally to you now,

[001-314]Enjoining you to acts, characters, spectacles, with me.

[001-315]15

[001-316]With me with firm holding, yet haste, haste on.

[001-317]For your life adhere to me,

[001-318](I may have to be persuaded many times before I consent to give

[001-319]myself really to you, but what of that?

[001-320]Must not Nature be persuaded many times?)

[001-321]No dainty dolce affettuoso I,

[001-322]Bearded, sun-burnt, gray-neck’d, forbidding, I have arrived,

[001-323]To be wrestled with as I pass for the solid prizes of the universe,

[001-324]For such I afford whoever can persevere to win them.

[001-325]16

[001-326]On my way a moment I pause,

[001-327]Here for you! and here for America!

[001-328]Still the present I raise aloft, still the future of the States I

[001-329]harbinge glad and sublime,

[001-330]And for the past I pronounce what the air holds of the red aborigines.

[001-331]The red aborigines,

[001-332]Leaving natural breaths, sounds of rain and winds, calls as of birds

[001-333]and animals in the woods, syllabled to us for names,

[001-334]Okonee, Koosa, Ottawa, Monongahela, Sauk, Natchez, Chattahoochee,

[001-335]Kaqueta, Oronoco,

[001-336]Wabash, Miami, Saginaw, Chippewa, Oshkosh, Walla-Walla,

[001-337]Leaving such to the States they melt, they depart, charging the

[001-338]water and the land with names.

[001-339]17

[001-340]Expanding and swift, henceforth,

[001-341]Elements, breeds, adjustments, turbulent, quick and audacious,

[001-342]A world primal again, vistas of glory incessant and branching,

[001-343]A new race dominating previous ones and grander far, with new contests,

[001-344]New politics, new literatures and religions, new inventions and arts.

[001-345]These, my voice announcing--I will sleep no more but arise,

[001-346]You oceans that have been calm within me! how I feel you,

[001-347]fathomless, stirring, preparing unprecedented waves and storms.

[001-348]18

[001-349]See, steamers steaming through my poems,

[001-350]See, in my poems [[immigrant|immigrants]] continually coming and landing,

[001-351]See, in arriere, the wigwam, the trail, [[hunter|the hunter]]’s hut, the flat-boat,

[001-352]the maize-leaf, the claim, the rude fence, and the backwoods village,

[001-353]See, on the one side the Western Sea and on the other the Eastern Sea,

[001-354]how they advance and retreat upon my poems as upon their own shores,

[001-355]See, pastures and forests in my poems--see, animals wild and tame--see,

[001-356]beyond the Kaw, countless herds of buffalo feeding on short curly grass,

[001-357]See, in my poems, cities, solid, vast, inland, with paved streets,

[001-358]with iron and stone edifices, ceaseless vehicles, and commerce,

[001-359]See, the many-cylinder’d steam printing-press--see, the electric

[001-360]telegraph stretching across the continent,

[001-361]See, through Atlantica’s depths pulses American Europe reaching,

[001-362]pulses of Europe duly return’d,

[001-363]See, the strong and quick locomotive as it departs, panting, blowing

[001-364]the steam-whistle,

[001-365]See, ploughmen ploughing farms--see, miners digging mines--see,

[001-366]the numberless factories,

[001-367]See, [[mechanic|mechanics]] busy at their benches with tools--see from among them

[001-368]superior judges, philosophs, Presidents, emerge, drest in

[001-369]working dresses,

[001-370]See, lounging through the shops and fields of the States, me

[001-371]well-belov’d, close-held by day and night,

[001-372]Hear the loud echoes of my songs there--read the hints come at last.

[001-373]19

[001-374]O camerado close! O you and me at last, and us two only.

[001-375]O a word to clear one’s path ahead endlessly!

[001-376]O something ecstatic and undemonstrable! O music wild!

[001-377]O now I triumph--and you shall also;

[001-378]O hand in hand--O wholesome pleasure--O one more desirer and lover!

[001-379]O to haste firm holding--to haste, haste on with me.
