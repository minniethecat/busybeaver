# BOOK VIII: Crossing Brooklyn Ferry

[005-001]Crossing [[brooklyn|Brooklyn]] Ferry

[005-002]1

[005-003]Flood-tide below me! I see you face to face!

[005-004]Clouds of the west--sun there half an hour high--I see you also face

[005-005]to face.

[005-006]Crowds of men and women attired in the usual costumes, how curious

[005-007]you are to me!

[005-008]On the ferry-boats the hundreds and hundreds that cross, returning

[005-009]home, are more curious to me than you suppose,

[005-010]And you that shall cross from shore to shore years hence are more

[005-011]to me, and more in my meditations, than you might suppose.

[005-012]2

[005-013]The impalpable sustenance of me from all things at all hours of the day,

[005-014]The simple, compact, well-join’d scheme, [[self|myself]] disintegrated, every

[005-015]one disintegrated yet part of the scheme,

[005-016]The similitudes of the past and those of the future,

[005-017]The glories strung like beads on my smallest sights and hearings, on

[005-018]the walk in the street and the passage over the river,

[005-019]The current rushing so swiftly and swimming with me far away,

[005-020]The others that are to follow me, the ties between me and them,

[005-021]The certainty of others, the life, [[love]], sight, hearing of others.

[005-022]Others will enter the gates of the ferry and cross from shore to shore,

[005-023]Others will watch the run of the flood-tide,

[005-024]Others will see the shipping of Manhattan north and west, and the

[005-025]heights of Brooklyn to the south and east,

[005-026]Others will see the islands large and small;

[005-027]Fifty years hence, others will see them as they cross, the sun half

[005-028]an hour high,

[005-029]A hundred years hence, or ever so many hundred years hence, others

[005-030]will see them,

[005-031]Will enjoy the sunset, the pouring-in of the flood-tide, the

[005-032]falling-back to [[sea|the sea]] of the ebb-tide.

[005-033]3

[005-034]It avails not, [[time]] nor place--distance avails not,

[005-035]I am with you, you men and women of a generation, or ever so many

[005-036]generations hence,

[005-037]Just as you feel when you look on the river and sky, so I felt,

[005-038]Just as any of you is one of a living crowd, I was one of a crowd,

[005-039]Just as you are refresh’d by the gladness of the river and the

[005-040]bright flow, I was refresh’d,

[005-041]Just as you stand and lean on the rail, yet hurry with the swift

[005-042]current, I stood yet was hurried,

[005-043]Just as you look on the numberless masts of ships and the

[005-044]thick-stemm’d pipes of steamboats, I look’d.

[005-045]I too many and many a time cross’d the river of old,

[005-046]Watched the Twelfth-month sea-gulls, saw them high in the air

[005-047]floating with motionless wings, oscillating their bodies,

[005-048]Saw how the glistening yellow lit up parts of their bodies and left

[005-049]the rest in strong shadow,

[005-050]Saw the slow-wheeling circles and the gradual edging toward the south,

[005-051]Saw the reflection of the summer sky in the water,

[005-052]Had my eyes dazzled by the shimmering track of beams,

[005-053]Look’d at the fine centrifugal spokes of light round the shape of my

[005-054]head in the sunlit water,

[005-055]Look’d on the haze on the hills southward and south-westward,

[005-056]Look’d on the vapor as it flew in fleeces tinged with violet,

[005-057]Look’d toward the lower bay to notice the vessels arriving,

[005-058]Saw their approach, saw aboard those that were near me,

[005-059]Saw the white sails of schooners and sloops, saw the ships at anchor,

[005-060]The sailors at work in the rigging or out astride the spars,

[005-061]The round masts, the swinging motion of the hulls, the slender

[005-062]serpentine pennants,

[005-063]The large and small steamers in motion, the pilots in their pilothouses,

[005-064]The white wake left by the passage, the quick tremulous whirl of the wheels,

[005-065]The flags of all nations, the falling of them at sunset,

[005-066]The scallop-edged waves in the twilight, the ladled cups, the

[005-067]frolic-some crests and glistening,

[005-068]The stretch afar growing dimmer and dimmer, the gray walls of the

[005-069]granite storehouses by the docks,

[005-070]On the river the shadowy group, the big steam-tug closely flank’d on

[005-071]each side by the barges, the hay-boat, the belated lighter,

[005-072]On the neighboring shore the fires from the foundry chimneys burning

[005-073]high and glaringly into [[night|the night]],

[005-074]Casting their flicker of black contrasted with wild red and yellow

[005-075]light over the tops of houses, and down into the clefts of streets.

[005-076]4

[005-077]These and all else were to me the same as they are to you,

[005-078]I loved well those cities, loved well the stately and rapid river,

[005-079]The men and women I saw were all near to me,

[005-080]Others the same--others who look back on me because I look’d forward

[005-081]to them,

[005-082](The time will come, though I stop here to-day and to-night.)

[005-083]5

[005-084]What is it then between us?

[005-085]What is the count of the scores or hundreds of years between us?

[005-086]Whatever it is, it avails not--distance avails not, and place avails not,

[005-087]I too lived, Brooklyn of ample hills was mine,

[005-088]I too walk’d the streets of Manhattan island, and bathed in the

[005-089]waters around it,

[005-090]I too felt the curious abrupt questionings stir within me,

[005-091]In the day among crowds of people sometimes they came upon me,

[005-092]In my walks home late at night or as I lay in my bed they came upon me,

[005-093]I too had been struck from the float forever held in solution,

[005-094]I too had receiv’d identity by my [[body]],

[005-095]That I was I knew was of my body, and what I should be I knew I

[005-096]should be of my body.

[005-097]6

[005-098]It is not upon you alone the dark patches fall,

[005-099]The dark threw its patches down upon me also,

[005-100]The best I had done seem’d to me blank and suspicious,

[005-101]My great thoughts as I supposed them, were they not in reality meagre?

[005-102]Nor is it you alone who know what it is to be evil,

[005-103]I am he who knew what it was to be evil,

[005-104]I too knitted the old knot of contrariety,

[005-105]Blabb’d, blush’d, resented, lied, stole, grudg’d,

[005-106]Had guile, anger, lust, hot wishes I dared not speak,

[005-107]Was wayward, vain, greedy, shallow, sly, cowardly, malignant,

[005-108]The wolf, the snake, the hog, not wanting in me.

[005-109]The cheating look, the frivolous word, the adulterous wish, not wanting,

[005-110]Refusals, hates, postponements, meanness, laziness, none of these wanting,

[005-111]Was one with the rest, the days and haps of the rest,

[005-112]Was call’d by my nighest name by clear loud voices of young men as

[005-113]they saw me approaching or passing,

[005-114]Felt their arms on my neck as I stood, or the negligent leaning of

[005-115]their flesh against me as I sat,

[005-116]Saw many I loved in the street or ferry-boat or public assembly, yet

[005-117]never told them a word,

[005-118]Lived the same life with the rest, the same old laughing, gnawing, sleeping,

[005-119]Play’d the part that still looks back on the actor or actress,

[005-120]The same old role, the role that is what we make it, as great as we like,

[005-121]Or as small as we like, or both great and small.

[005-122]7

[005-123]Closer yet I approach you,

[005-124]What thought you have of me now, I had as much of you--I laid in my

[005-125]stores in advance,

[005-126]I consider’d long and seriously of you before you were born.

[005-127]Who was to know what should come home to me?

[005-128]Who knows but I am enjoying this?

[005-129]Who knows, for all the distance, but I am as good as looking at you

[005-130]now, for all you cannot see me?

[005-131]8

[005-132]Ah, what can ever be more stately and admirable to me than

[005-133]mast-hemm’d Manhattan?

[005-134]River and sunset and scallop-edg’d waves of flood-tide?

[005-135]The sea-gulls oscillating their bodies, the hay-boat in the

[005-136]twilight, and the belated lighter?

[005-137]What [[god|gods]] can exceed these that clasp me by the hand, and with voices I

[005-138]love call me promptly and loudly by my nighest name as approach?

[005-139]What is more subtle than this which ties me to the woman or man that

[005-140]looks in my face?

[005-141]Which fuses me into you now, and pours my meaning into you?

[005-142]We understand then do we not?

[005-143]What I promis’d without mentioning it, have you not accepted?

[005-144]What the study could not teach--what the preaching could not

[005-145]accomplish is accomplish’d, is it not?

[005-146]9

[005-147]Flow on, river! flow with the flood-tide, and ebb with the ebb-tide!

[005-148]Frolic on, crested and scallop-edg’d waves!

[005-149]Gorgeous clouds of the sunset! drench with your splendor me, or the

[005-150]men and women generations after me!

[005-151]Cross from shore to shore, countless crowds of passengers!

[005-152]Stand up, tall masts of Mannahatta! stand up, beautiful hills of Brooklyn!

[005-153]Throb, baffled and curious brain! throw out questions and answers!

[005-154]Suspend here and everywhere, eternal float of solution!

[005-155]Gaze, loving and thirsting eyes, in the house or street or public assembly!

[005-156]Sound out, voices of young men! loudly and musically call me by my

[005-157]nighest name!

[005-158]Live, old life! play the part that looks back on the actor or actress!

[005-159]Play the old role, the role that is great or small according as one

[005-160]makes it!

[005-161]Consider, you who peruse me, whether I may not in unknown ways be

[005-162]looking upon you;

[005-163]Be firm, rail over the river, to support those who lean idly, yet

[005-164]haste with the hasting current;

[005-165]Fly on, sea-birds! fly sideways, or wheel in large circles high in the air;

[005-166]Receive the summer sky, you water, and faithfully hold it till all

[005-167]downcast eyes have time to take it from you!

[005-168]Diverge, fine spokes of light, from the shape of my head, or any

[005-169]one’s head, in the sunlit water!

[005-170]Come on, ships from the lower bay! pass up or down, white-sail’d

[005-171]schooners, sloops, lighters!

[005-172]Flaunt away, flags of all nations! be duly lower’d at sunset!

[005-173]Burn high your fires, foundry chimneys! cast black shadows at

[005-174]nightfall! cast red and yellow light over the tops of the houses!

[005-175]Appearances, now or henceforth, indicate what you are,

[005-176]You necessary film, continue to envelop [[soul|the soul]],

[005-177][[About]] my body for me, and your body for you, be hung our divinest aromas,

[005-178]Thrive, cities--bring your freight, bring your shows, ample and

[005-179]sufficient rivers,

[005-180]Expand, being than which none else is perhaps more spiritual,

[005-181]Keep your places, objects than which none else is more lasting.

[005-182]You have waited, you always wait, you dumb, beautiful ministers,

[005-183]We receive you with [[freedom|free]] sense at last, and are insatiate henceforward,

[005-184]Not you any more shall be able to foil us, or withhold yourselves from us,

[005-185]We use you, and do not cast you aside--we plant you permanently within us,

[005-186]We fathom you not--we love you--there is perfection in you also,

[005-187]You furnish your parts toward [[immortality|eternity]],

[005-188]Great or small, you furnish your parts toward the soul.
