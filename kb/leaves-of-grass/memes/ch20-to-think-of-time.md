# BOOK XXIX: To Think of Time

[020-001]To Think of [[time|Time]]

[020-002]1

[020-003]To think of time--of all that retrospection,

[020-004]To think of to-day, and the ages continued henceforward.

[020-005]Have you guess’d you yourself would not continue?

[020-006]Have you dreaded these [[earth]]-beetles?

[020-007]Have you fear’d the future would be nothing to you?

[020-008]Is to-day nothing? is the beginningless past nothing?

[020-009]If the future is nothing they are just as surely nothing.

[020-010]To think that the sun rose in the east--that men and women were

[020-011]flexible, real, alive--that every thing was alive,

[020-012]To think that you and I did not see, feel, think, nor bear our part,

[020-013]To think that we are now here and bear our part.

[020-014]2

[020-015]Not a day passes, not a minute or second without an accouchement,

[020-016]Not a day passes, not a minute or second without a corpse.

[020-017]The dull nights go over and the dull days also,

[020-018]The soreness of lying so much in bed goes over,

[020-019]The physician after long putting off gives the silent and terrible

[020-020]look for an answer,

[020-021]The children come hurried and weeping, and the brothers and sisters

[020-022]are sent for,

[020-023]Medicines stand unused on the shelf, (the camphor-smell has long

[020-024]pervaded the rooms,)

[020-025]The faithful hand of the living does not desert the hand of the [[death|dying]],

[020-026]The twitching lips press lightly on the forehead of the dying,

[020-027]The breath ceases and the pulse of the heart ceases,

[020-028]The corpse stretches on the bed and the living look upon it,

[020-029]It is palpable as the living are palpable.

[020-030]The living look upon the corpse with their eyesight,

[020-031]But without eyesight lingers a different living and looks curiously

[020-032]on the corpse.

[020-033]3

[020-034]To think the thought of death merged in the thought of materials,

[020-035]To think of all these wonders of city and country, and others taking

[020-036]great interest in them, and we taking no interest in them.

[020-037]To think how eager we are in building our houses,

[020-038]To think others shall be just as eager, and we quite indifferent.

[020-039](I see one building the house that serves him a few years, or

[020-040]seventy or eighty years at most,

[020-041]I see one building the house that serves him longer than that.)

[020-042]Slow-moving and black lines creep over the whole earth--they never

[020-043]cease--they are the burial lines,

[020-044]He that was President was buried, and he that is now President shall

[020-045]surely be buried.

[020-046]4

[020-047]A reminiscence of the vulgar fate,

[020-048]A frequent sample of the life and death of workmen,

[020-049]Each after his kind.

[020-050]Cold dash of waves at the ferry-wharf, posh and ice in the river,

[020-051]half-frozen mud in the streets,

[020-052]A gray discouraged sky overhead, the short last daylight of December,

[020-053]A hearse and stages, the funeral of an old Broadway stage-driver,

[020-054]the cortege mostly drivers.

[020-055]Steady the trot to the cemetery, duly rattles the death-bell,

[020-056]The gate is pass’d, the new-dug grave is halted at, the living

[020-057]alight, the hearse uncloses,

[020-058]The coffin is pass’d out, lower’d and settled, the whip is laid on

[020-059]the coffin, the earth is swiftly shovel’d in,

[020-060]The mound above is flatted with the spades--silence,

[020-061]A minute--no one moves or speaks--it is done,

[020-062]He is decently put away--is there any thing more?

[020-063]He was a good fellow, [[freedom|free]]-mouth’d, quick-temper’d, not bad-looking,

[020-064]Ready with life or death for a friend, fond of women, gambled, ate

[020-065]hearty, drank hearty,

[020-066]Had known what it was to be flush, grew low-spirited toward the

[020-067]last, sicken’d, was help’d by a contribution,

[020-068]Died, aged forty-one years--and that was his funeral.

[020-069]Thumb extended, finger uplifted, apron, cape, gloves, strap,

[020-070]wet-weather clothes, whip carefully chosen,

[020-071]Boss, spotter, starter, hostler, somebody loafing on you, you

[020-072]loafing on somebody, headway, man before and man behind,

[020-073]Good day’s work, bad day’s work, pet stock, mean stock, first out,

[020-074]last out, turning-in at [[night]],

[020-075]To think that these are so much and so nigh to other drivers, and he

[020-076]there takes no interest in them.

[020-077]5

[020-078]The markets, the government, the working-man’s wages, to think what

[020-079]account they are through our nights and days,

[020-080]To think that other working-men will make just as great account of

[020-081]them, yet we make little or no account.

[020-082]The vulgar and the refined, what you call sin and what you call

[020-083]goodness, to think how wide a difference,

[020-084]To think the difference will still continue to others, yet we lie

[020-085]beyond the difference.

[020-086]To think how much pleasure there is,

[020-087]Do you enjoy yourself in the city? or engaged in business? or

[020-088]planning a nomination and election? or with your wife and family?

[020-089]Or with your [[mother]] and sisters? or in womanly housework? or the

[020-090]beautiful maternal cares?

[020-091]These also flow onward to others, you and I flow onward,

[020-092]But in due time you and I shall take less interest in them.

[020-093]Your farm, profits, crops--to think how engross’d you are,

[020-094]To think there will still be farms, profits, crops, yet for you of

[020-095]what avail?

[020-096]6

[020-097]What will be will be well, for what is is well,

[020-098]To take interest is well, and not to take interest shall be well.

[020-099]The domestic joys, the dally housework or business, the building of

[020-100]houses, are not phantasms, they have weight, form, location,

[020-101]Farms, profits, crops, markets, wages, government, are none of them

[020-102]phantasms,

[020-103]The difference between sin and goodness is no delusion,

[020-104]The earth is not an echo, man and his life and all the things of his

[020-105]life are well-consider’d.

[020-106]You are not thrown to the winds, you gather certainly and safely

[020-107]around yourself,

[020-108]Yourself! yourself!. yourself, for ever and ever!

[020-109]7

[020-110]It is not to diffuse you that you were born of your mother and

[020-111]father, it is to identify you,

[020-112]It is not that you should be undecided, but that you should be decided,

[020-113]Something long preparing and formless is arrived and form’d in you,

[020-114]You are henceforth secure, whatever comes or goes.

[020-115]The threads that were spun are gather’d, the wet crosses the warp,

[020-116]the pattern is systematic.

[020-117]The preparations have every one been justified,

[020-118]The orchestra have sufficiently tuned their instruments, the baton

[020-119]has given the signal.

[020-120]The guest that was coming, he waited long, he is now housed,

[020-121]He is one of those who are beautiful and happy, he is one of those

[020-122]that to look upon and be with is enough.

[020-123]The law of the past cannot be eluded,

[020-124]The law of the present and future cannot be eluded,

[020-125]The law of the living cannot be eluded, it is eternal,

[020-126]The law of promotion and transformation cannot be eluded,

[020-127]The law of heroes and good-doers cannot be eluded,

[020-128]The law of drunkards, informers, mean persons, not one iota thereof

[020-129]can be eluded.

[020-130]8

[020-131]Slow moving and black lines go ceaselessly over the earth,

[020-132]Northerner goes carried and Southerner goes carried, and they on the

[020-133]Atlantic side and they on the Pacific,

[020-134]And they between, and all through the Mississippi country, and all

[020-135]over the earth.

[020-136]The great masters and kosmos are well as they go, the heroes and

[020-137]good-doers are well,

[020-138]The known leaders and inventors and the rich owners and pious and

[020-139]distinguish’d may be well,

[020-140]But there is more account than that, there is strict account of all.

[020-141]The interminable hordes of the ignorant and wicked are not nothing,

[020-142]The barbarians of Africa and Asia are not nothing,

[020-143]The perpetual successions of shallow people are not nothing as they go.

[020-144]Of and in all these things,

[020-145]I have dream’d that we are not to be changed so much, nor the law of

[020-146]us changed,

[020-147]I have dream’d that heroes and good-doers shall be under the present

[020-148]and past law,

[020-149]And that murderers, drunkards, liars, shall be under the present and

[020-150]past law,

[020-151]For I have dream’d that the law they are under now is enough.

[020-152]And I have dream’d that the purpose and essence of the known life,

[020-153]the transient,

[020-154]Is to form and decide identity for the unknown life, the permanent.

[020-155]If all came but to ashes of dung,

[020-156]If maggots and rats ended us, then Alarum! for we are betray’d,

[020-157]Then indeed suspicion of death.

[020-158]Do you suspect death? if I were to suspect death I should die now,

[020-159]Do you think I could walk pleasantly and well-suited toward annihilation?

[020-160]Pleasantly and well-suited I walk,

[020-161]Whither I walk I cannot define, but I know it is good,

[020-162]The whole universe indicates that it is good,

[020-163]The past and the present indicate that it is good.

[020-164]How beautiful and perfect are the animals!

[020-165]How perfect the earth, and the minutest thing upon it!

[020-166]What is called good is perfect, and what is called bad is just as perfect,

[020-167]The vegetables and minerals are all perfect, and the imponderable

[020-168]fluids perfect;

[020-169]Slowly and surely they have pass’d on to this, and slowly and surely

[020-170]they yet pass on.

[020-171]9

[020-172]I swear I think now that every thing without exception has an eternal [[soul]]!

[020-173]The trees have, rooted in the ground! the weeds of [[sea|the sea]] have! the

[020-174]animals!

[020-175]I swear I think there is nothing but [[immortality]]!

[020-176]That the exquisite scheme is for it, and the nebulous float is for

[020-177]it, and the cohering is for it!

[020-178]And all preparation is for it--and identity is for it--and life and

[020-179]materials are altogether for it!

[020-180]BOOK XXX. WHISPERS OF HEAVENLY DEATH

[020-181]Darest Thou Now O Soul

[020-182]Darest thou now O soul,

[020-183]Walk out with me toward the unknown region,

[020-184]Where neither ground is for the feet nor any path to follow?

[020-185]No map there, nor guide,

[020-186]Nor voice sounding, nor touch of human hand,

[020-187]Nor face with blooming flesh, nor lips, nor eyes, are in that land.

[020-188]I know it not O soul,

[020-189]Nor dost thou, all is a blank before us,

[020-190]All waits undream’d of in that region, that inaccessible land.

[020-191]Till when the ties loosen,

[020-192]All but the ties eternal, Time and Space,

[020-193]Nor darkness, gravitation, sense, nor any bounds bounding us.

[020-194]Then we burst forth, we float,

[020-195]In Time and Space O soul, prepared for them,

[020-196]Equal, equipt at last, (O [[joy]]! O fruit of all!) them to fulfil O soul.

[020-197]Whispers of Heavenly Death

[020-198]Whispers of heavenly death murmur’d I hear,

[020-199]Labial gossip of night, sibilant chorals,

[020-200]Footsteps gently ascending, mystical breezes wafted soft and low,

[020-201]Ripples of unseen rivers, tides of a current flowing, forever flowing,

[020-202](Or is it the plashing of tears? the measureless waters of human tears?)

[020-203]I see, just see skyward, great cloud-masses,

[020-204]Mournfully slowly they roll, silently swelling and mixing,

[020-205]With at times a half-dimm’d sadden’d far-off star,

[020-206]Appearing and disappearing.

[020-207](Some parturition rather, some solemn immortal birth;

[020-208]On the frontiers to eyes impenetrable,

[020-209]Some soul is passing over.)

[020-210]Chanting the Square Deific

[020-211]1

[020-212]Chanting the square deific, out of the One advancing, out of the sides,

[020-213]Out of the old and new, out of the square entirely divine,

[020-214]Solid, four-sided, (all the sides needed,) from this side Jehovah am I,

[020-215]Old Brahm I, and I Saturnius am;

[020-216]Not Time affects me--I am Time, old, modern as any,

[020-217]Unpersuadable, relentless, executing righteous judgments,

[020-218]As the Earth, the Father, the brown old Kronos, with laws,

[020-219]Aged beyond computation, yet never new, ever with those mighty laws rolling,

[020-220]Relentless I forgive no man--whoever sins dies--I will have that man’s life;

[020-221]Therefore let none expect mercy--have the seasons, gravitation, the

[020-222]appointed days, mercy? no more have I,

[020-223]But as the seasons and gravitation, and as all the appointed days

[020-224]that forgive not,

[020-225]I dispense from this side judgments inexorable without the least remorse.

[020-226]2

[020-227]Consolator most mild, the promis’d one advancing,

[020-228]With gentle hand extended, the mightier [[god|God]] am I,

[020-229]Foretold by prophets and poets in their most rapt prophecies and poems,

[020-230]From this side, lo! the Lord Christ gazes--lo! Hermes I--lo! mine is

[020-231]Hercules’ face,

[020-232]All sorrow, labor, suffering, I, tallying it, absorb in [[self|myself]],

[020-233]Many times have I been rejected, taunted, put in prison, and

[020-234]crucified, and many times shall be again,

[020-235]All the world have I given up for my dear brothers’ and sisters’

[020-236]sake, for the soul’s sake,

[020-237]Wanding my way through the homes of men, rich or poor, with the kiss

[020-238]of [[love|affection]],

[020-239]For I am affection, I am the cheer-bringing God, with hope and

[020-240]all-enclosing charity,

[020-241]With indulgent words as to children, with fresh and sane words, mine only,

[020-242]Young and strong I pass knowing well I am destin’d myself to an

[020-243]early death;

[020-244]But my charity has no death--my wisdom dies not, neither early nor late,

[020-245]And my sweet love bequeath’d here and elsewhere never dies.

[020-246]3

[020-247]Aloof, dissatisfied, plotting revolt,

[020-248][[comrade|Comrade]] of criminals, brother of slaves,

[020-249]Crafty, despised, a drudge, ignorant,

[020-250]With sudra face and worn brow, black, but in the depths of my heart,

[020-251]proud as any,

[020-252]Lifted now and always against whoever scorning assumes to rule me,

[020-253]Morose, full of guile, full of reminiscences, brooding, with many wiles,

[020-254](Though it was thought I was baffled, and dispel’d, and my wiles

[020-255]done, but that will never be,)

[020-256]Defiant, I, Satan, still live, still utter words, in new lands duly

[020-257]appearing, (and old ones also,)

[020-258]Permanent here from my side, warlike, equal with any, real as any,

[020-259]Nor time nor change shall ever change me or my words.

[020-260]4

[020-261]Santa Spirita, breather, life,

[020-262]Beyond the light, lighter than light,

[020-263]Beyond the flames of hell, joyous, leaping easily above hell,

[020-264]Beyond Paradise, perfumed solely with mine own perfume,

[020-265]Including all life on earth, touching, including God, including

[020-266]Saviour and Satan,

[020-267]Ethereal, pervading all, (for without me what were all? what were God?)

[020-268]Essence of forms, life of the real identities, permanent, positive,

[020-269](namely the unseen,)

[020-270]Life of the great round world, the sun and stars, and of man, I, the

[020-271]general soul,

[020-272]Here the square finishing, the solid, I the most solid,

[020-273]Breathe my breath also through these songs.

[020-274]Of Him I Love Day and Night

[020-275]Of him I love day and night I dream’d I heard he was dead,

[020-276]And I dream’d I went where they had buried him I love, but he was

[020-277]not in that place,

[020-278]And I dream’d I wander’d searching among burial-places to find him,

[020-279]And I found that every place was a burial-place;

[020-280]The houses full of life were equally full of death, (this house is now,)

[020-281]The streets, the shipping, the places of amusement, the [[chicago|Chicago]],

[020-282]Boston, Philadelphia, the Mannahatta, were as full of the dead as

[020-283]of the living,

[020-284]And fuller, O vastly fuller of the dead than of the living;

[020-285]And what I dream’d I will henceforth tell to every person and age,

[020-286]And I stand henceforth bound to what I dream’d,

[020-287]And now I am willing to disregard burial-places and dispense with them,

[020-288]And if the memorials of the dead were put up indifferently everywhere,

[020-289]even in the room where I eat or sleep, I should be satisfied,

[020-290]And if the corpse of any one I love, or if my own corpse, be duly

[020-291]render’d to powder and pour’d in the sea, I shall be satisfied,

[020-292]Or if it be distributed to the winds I shall be satisfied.

[020-293]Yet, Yet, Ye Downcast Hours

[020-294]Yet, yet, ye downcast hours, I know ye also,

[020-295]Weights of lead, how ye clog and cling at my ankles,

[020-296]Earth to a chamber of mourning turns--I hear the o’erweening, mocking

[020-297]voice,

[020-298]Matter is conqueror--matter, triumphant only, continues onward.

[020-299]Despairing cries float ceaselessly toward me,

[020-300]The call of my nearest lover, putting forth, alarm’d, uncertain,

[020-301]The sea I am quickly to sail, come tell me,

[020-302]Come tell me where I am speeding, tell me my destination.

[020-303]I understand your anguish, but I cannot help you,

[020-304]I approach, hear, behold, the sad mouth, the look out of the eyes,

[020-305]your mute inquiry,

[020-306]Whither I go from the bed I recline on, come tell me,--

[020-307]Old age, alarm’d, uncertain--a young woman’s voice, appealing to

[020-308]me for comfort;

[020-309]A young man’s voice, Shall I not escape?

[020-310]As If a Phantom Caress’d Me

[020-311]As if a phantom caress’d me,

[020-312]I thought I was not alone walking here by the shore;

[020-313]But the one I thought was with me as now I walk by the shore, the

[020-314]one I loved that caress’d me,

[020-315]As I lean and look through the glimmering light, that one has

[020-316]utterly disappear’d.

[020-317]And those appear that are hateful to me and mock me.

[020-318]Assurances

[020-319]I need no assurances, I am a man who is preoccupied of his own soul;

[020-320]I do not doubt that from under the feet and beside the hands and

[020-321]face I am cognizant of, are now looking faces I am not cognizant

[020-322]of, calm and actual faces,

[020-323]I do not doubt but the majesty and beauty of the world are latent in

[020-324]any iota of the world,

[020-325]I do not doubt I am limitless, and that the universes are limitless,

[020-326]in vain I try to think how limitless,

[020-327]I do not doubt that the orbs and the systems of orbs play their

[020-328]swift sports through the air on purpose, and that I shall one day

[020-329]be eligible to do as much as they, and more than they,

[020-330]I do not doubt that temporary affairs keep on and on millions of years,

[020-331]I do not doubt interiors have their interiors, and exteriors have

[020-332]their exteriors, and that the eyesight has another eyesight, and

[020-333]the hearing another hearing, and the voice another voice,

[020-334]I do not doubt that the passionately-wept deaths of young men are

[020-335]provided for, and that the deaths of young women and the

[020-336]deaths of little children are provided for,

[020-337](Did you think Life was so well provided for, and Death, the purport

[020-338]of all Life, is not well provided for?)

[020-339]I do not doubt that wrecks at sea, no matter what the horrors of

[020-340]them, no matter whose wife, child, husband, father, lover, has

[020-341]gone down, are provided for, to the minutest points,

[020-342]I do not doubt that whatever can possibly happen anywhere at any

[020-343]time, is provided for in the inherences of things,

[020-344]I do not think Life provides for all and for Time and Space, but I

[020-345]believe Heavenly Death provides for all.

[020-346]Quicksand Years

[020-347]Quicksand years that whirl me I know not whither,

[020-348]Your schemes, politics, fail, lines give way, substances mock and elude me,

[020-349]Only the theme I sing, the great and strong-possess’d soul, eludes not,

[020-350]One’s-self must never give way--that is the final substance--that

[020-351]out of all is sure,

[020-352]Out of politics, triumphs, battles, life, what at last finally remains?

[020-353]When shows break up what but One’s-Self is sure?

[020-354]That Music Always Round Me

[020-355]That music always round me, unceasing, unbeginning, yet long

[020-356]untaught I did not hear,

[020-357]But now the chorus I hear and am elated,

[020-358]A tenor, strong, ascending with power and health, with glad notes of

[020-359]daybreak I hear,

[020-360]A soprano at intervals sailing buoyantly over the tops of immense waves,

[020-361]A transparent base shuddering lusciously under and through the universe,

[020-362]The triumphant tutti, the funeral wailings with sweet flutes and

[020-363]violins, all these I fill myself with,

[020-364]I hear not the volumes of sound merely, I am moved by the exquisite

[020-365]meanings,

[020-366]I listen to the different voices winding in and out, striving,

[020-367]contending with fiery vehemence to excel each other in emotion;

[020-368]I do not think the performers know themselves--but now I think

[020-369]begin to know them.

[020-370]What Ship Puzzled at Sea

[020-371]What ship puzzled at sea, cons for the true reckoning?

[020-372]Or coming in, to avoid the bars and follow the channel a perfect

[020-373]pilot needs?

[020-374]Here, [[sailor]]! here, ship! take aboard the most perfect pilot,

[020-375]Whom, in a little boat, putting off and rowing, I hailing you offer.

[020-376]A Noiseless Patient Spider

[020-377]A noiseless patient spider,

[020-378]I mark’d where on a little promontory it stood isolated,

[020-379]Mark’d how to explore the vacant vast surrounding,

[020-380]It launch’d forth filament, filament, filament out of itself,

[020-381]Ever unreeling them, ever tirelessly speeding them.

[020-382]And you O my soul where you stand,

[020-383]Surrounded, detached, in measureless oceans of space,

[020-384]Ceaselessly musing, venturing, throwing, seeking the spheres to

[020-385]connect them,

[020-386]Till the bridge you will need be form’d, till the ductile anchor hold,

[020-387]Till the gossamer thread you fling catch somewhere, O my soul.

[020-388]O Living Always, Always Dying

[020-389]O living always, always dying!

[020-390]O the burials of me past and present,

[020-391]O me while I stride ahead, material, visible, imperious as ever;

[020-392]O me, what I was for years, now dead, (I lament not, I am content;)

[020-393]O to disengage myself from those corpses of me, which I turn and

[020-394]look at where I cast them,

[020-395]To pass on, (O living! always living!) and leave the corpses behind.

[020-396]To One Shortly to Die

[020-397]From all the rest I single out you, having a message for you,

[020-398]You are to die--let others tell you what they please, I cannot prevaricate,

[020-399]I am exact and merciless, but I love you--there is no escape for you.

[020-400]Softly I lay my right hand upon you, you ’ust feel it,

[020-401]I do not argue, I bend my head close and half envelop it,

[020-402]I sit quietly by, I remain faithful,

[020-403]I am more than nurse, more than parent or neighbor,

[020-404]I absolve you from all except yourself spiritual bodily, that is

[020-405]eternal, you yourself will surely escape,

[020-406]The corpse you will leave will be but excrementitious.

[020-407]The sun bursts through in unlooked-for directions,

[020-408]Strong thoughts fill you and confidence, you smile,

[020-409]You forget you are sick, as I forget you are sick,

[020-410]You do not see the medicines, you do not mind the weeping friends,

[020-411]I am with you,

[020-412]I exclude others from you, there is nothing to be commiserated,

[020-413]I do not commiserate, I congratulate you.

[020-414]Night on the Prairies

[020-415]Night on [[prairie|the prairies]],

[020-416]The supper is over, the fire on the ground burns low,

[020-417]The wearied emigrants sleep, wrapt in their blankets;

[020-418]I walk by myself--I stand and look at the stars, which I think now

[020-419]never realized before.

[020-420]Now I absorb immortality and peace,

[020-421]I admire death and test propositions.

[020-422]How plenteous! how spiritual! how resume!

[020-423]The same old man and soul--the same old aspirations, and the same content.

[020-437]As I sit with others at a great feast, suddenly while the music is playing,

[020-438]To my mind, (whence it comes I know not,) spectral in mist of a

[020-439]wreck at sea,

[020-440]Of certain ships, how they sail from port with flying streamers and

[020-441]wafted kisses, and that is the last of them,

[020-442]Of the solemn and murky mystery about the fate of the President,

[020-443]Of the flower of the marine science of fifty generations founder’d

[020-444]off the Northeast coast and going down--of the steamship Arctic

[020-445]going down,

[020-446]Of the veil’d tableau-women gather’d together on deck, pale, heroic,

[020-447]waiting the moment that draws so close--O the moment!

[020-448]A huge sob--a few bubbles--the white foam spirting up--and then the

[020-449]women gone,

[020-450]Sinking there while the passionless wet flows on--and I now

[020-451]pondering, Are those women indeed gone?

[020-452]Are souls drown’d and destroy’d so?
