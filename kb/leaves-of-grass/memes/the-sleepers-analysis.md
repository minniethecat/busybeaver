## Overview

The Sleepers is one of the major poems in Walt Whitman's *Leaves of Grass*. This page provides a literary analysis of its themes, structure, imagery, and significance within the larger collection.

## Themes & Imagery

The poem explores Whitman's characteristic themes — democracy, the self, the body, nature, and the American experiment — through his innovative free verse form and catalogic technique.

## Critical Significance

This poem has been widely discussed in Whitman scholarship and represents a key aspect of his contribution to American poetry and the development of free verse as a literary form.
