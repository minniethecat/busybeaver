# BOOK X: Our Old Feuillage

[007-001]Our Old Feuillage

[007-002]Always our old feuillage!

[007-003]Always Florida’s green peninsula--always the priceless delta of

[007-004]Louisiana--always the cotton-fields of Alabama and Texas,

[007-005]Always [[california|California]]’s golden hills and hollows, and the silver

[007-006]mountains of New Mexico--always soft-breath’d Cuba,

[007-007]Always the vast slope drain’d by the Southern [[sea]], inseparable with

[007-008]the slopes drain’d by the Eastern and Western seas,

[007-009]The area the eighty-third year of these States, the three and a half

[007-010]millions of square miles,

[007-011]The eighteen thousand miles of sea-coast and bay-coast on the main,

[007-012]the thirty thousand miles of river navigation,

[007-013]The seven millions of distinct families and the same number of dwellings--

[007-014]always these, and more, branching forth into numberless branches,

[007-015]Always the [[freedom|free]] range and diversity--always the continent of Democracy;

[007-016]Always [[prairie|the prairies]], pastures, forests, vast cities, travelers,

[007-017]Kanada, the snows;

[007-018]Always these compact lands tied at the hips with the belt stringing

[007-019]the huge oval lakes;

[007-020]Always [[the-west|the West]] with strong native persons, the increasing density there,

[007-021]the habitans, friendly, threatening, ironical, scorning invaders;

[007-022]All sights, South, North, East--all deeds, promiscuously done at all times,

[007-023]All characters, movements, growths, a few noticed, myriads unnoticed,

[007-024]Through Mannahatta’s streets I walking, these things gathering,

[007-025]On interior rivers by [[night]] in the glare of pine knots, steamboats

[007-026]wooding up,

[007-027]Sunlight by day on the valley of the Susquehanna, and on the valleys

[007-028]of the Potomac and Rappahannock, and the valleys of the Roanoke

[007-029]and Delaware,

[007-030]In their northerly wilds beasts of prey haunting the Adirondacks the

[007-031]hills, or lapping the Saginaw waters to drink,

[007-032]In a lonesome inlet a sheldrake lost from the flock, sitting on the

[007-033]water rocking silently,

[007-034]In farmers’ barns oxen in the stable, their harvest labor done, they

[007-035]rest standing, they are too tired,

[007-036]Afar on arctic ice the she-walrus lying drowsily while her cubs play around,

[007-037]The hawk sailing where men have not yet sail’d, the farthest polar

[007-038]sea, ripply, crystalline, open, beyond the floes,

[007-039]White drift spooning ahead where the ship in the tempest dashes,

[007-040]On solid [[earth|land]] what is done in cities as the bells strike midnight together,

[007-041]In primitive woods the sounds there also sounding, the howl of the

[007-042]wolf, the scream of the panther, and the hoarse bellow of the elk,

[007-043]In winter beneath the hard blue ice of Moosehead lake, in summer

[007-044]visible through the clear waters, the great trout swimming,

[007-045]In lower latitudes in warmer air in the Carolinas the large black

[007-046]buzzard floating slowly high beyond the tree tops,

[007-047]Below, the red cedar festoon’d with tylandria, the pines and

[007-048]cypresses growing out of the white sand that spreads far and flat,

[007-049]Rude boats descending the big Pedee, climbing plants, parasites with

[007-050]color’d flowers and berries enveloping huge trees,

[007-051]The waving drapery on the live-oak trailing long and low,

[007-052]noiselessly waved by the wind,

[007-053]The camp of Georgia wagoners just after dark, the supper-fires and

[007-054]the cooking and eating by whites and negroes,

[007-055]Thirty or forty great wagons, the mules, cattle, horses, feeding

[007-056]from troughs,

[007-057]The shadows, gleams, up under the leaves of the old sycamore-trees,

[007-058]the flames with the black smoke from the pitch-pine curling and rising;

[007-059]Southern fishermen fishing, the sounds and inlets of North

[007-060]Carolina’s coast, the shad-fishery and the herring-fishery, the

[007-061]large sweep-seines, the windlasses on shore work’d by horses, the

[007-062]clearing, curing, and packing-houses;

[007-063]Deep in the forest in piney woods turpentine dropping from the

[007-064]incisions in the trees, there are the turpentine works,

[007-065]There are the negroes at work in good health, the ground in all

[007-066]directions is cover’d with pine straw;

[007-067]In Tennessee and Kentucky slaves busy in the coalings, at the forge,

[007-068]by the furnace-blaze, or at the corn-shucking,

[007-069]In Virginia, the planter’s son returning after a long absence,

[007-070]joyfully welcom’d and kiss’d by the aged mulatto nurse,

[007-071]On rivers boatmen safely moor’d at nightfall in their boats under

[007-072]shelter of high banks,

[007-073]Some of the younger men dance to the sound of the banjo or fiddle,

[007-074]others sit on the gunwale smoking and talking;

[007-075]Late in the afternoon the mocking-bird, the American mimic, singing

[007-076]in the Great Dismal Swamp,

[007-077]There are the greenish waters, the resinous odor, the plenteous

[007-078]moss, the cypress-tree, and the juniper-tree;

[007-079]Northward, young men of Mannahatta, the target company from an

[007-080]excursion returning home at evening, the musket-muzzles all

[007-081]bear bunches of flowers presented by women;

[007-082]Children at play, or on his father’s lap a young boy fallen asleep,

[007-083](how his lips move! how he smiles in his sleep!)

[007-084]The scout riding on horseback over the plains west of the

[007-085]Mississippi, he ascends a knoll and sweeps his eyes around;

[007-086]California life, [[miner|the miner]], bearded, dress’d in his rude costume,

[007-087]the stanch California friendship, the sweet air, the graves one

[007-088]in passing meets solitary just aside the horse-path;

[007-089]Down in Texas the cotton-field, the negro-cabins, drivers driving

[007-090]mules or oxen before rude carts, cotton bales piled on banks

[007-091]and wharves;

[007-092]Encircling all, vast-darting up and wide, the American Soul, with

[007-093]equal hemispheres, one [[love|Love]], one Dilation or Pride;

[007-094]In arriere the peace-talk with the Iroquois the aborigines, the

[007-095]calumet, the pipe of good-will, arbitration, and indorsement,

[007-096]The sachem blowing the smoke first toward the sun and then toward

[007-097]the earth,

[007-098]The drama of the scalp-dance enacted with painted faces and guttural

[007-099]exclamations,

[007-100]The setting out of the [[war]]-party, the long and stealthy march,

[007-101]The single file, the swinging hatchets, the surprise and slaughter

[007-102]of enemies;

[007-103]All the acts, scenes, ways, persons, attitudes of these States,

[007-104]reminiscences, institutions,

[007-105]All these States compact, every square mile of these States without

[007-106]excepting a particle;

[007-107]Me pleas’d, rambling in lanes and country fields, [[paumanok|Paumanok]]’s fields,

[007-108]Observing the spiral flight of two little yellow butterflies

[007-109]shuffling between each other, ascending high in the air,

[007-110]The darting swallow, the destroyer of insects, the fall traveler

[007-111]southward but returning northward early in the spring,

[007-112]The country boy at the close of the day driving the herd of cows and

[007-113]shouting to them as they loiter to browse by the roadside,

[007-114]The city wharf, Boston, Philadelphia, Baltimore, Charleston, New

[007-115]Orleans, San Francisco,

[007-116]The departing ships when the sailors heave at the capstan;

[007-117]Evening--me in my room--the setting sun,

[007-118]The setting summer sun shining in my open window, showing the

[007-119]swarm of flies, suspended, balancing in the air in the centre

[007-120]of the room, darting athwart, up and down, casting swift

[007-121]shadows in specks on the opposite wall where the shine is;

[007-122]The athletic American matron speaking in public to crowds of listeners,

[007-123]Males, females, [[immigrant|immigrants]], combinations, the copiousness, the

[007-124]individuality of [[america|the States]], each for itself--the moneymakers,

[007-125]Factories, machinery, the mechanical forces, the windlass, lever,

[007-126]pulley, all certainties,

[007-127]The certainty of space, increase, freedom, futurity,

[007-128]In space the sporades, the scatter’d islands, the stars--on the firm

[007-129]earth, the lands, my lands,

[007-130]O lands! all so dear to me--what you are, (whatever it is,) I putting it

[007-131]at random in these songs, become a part of that, whatever it is,

[007-132]Southward there, I screaming, with wings slow flapping, with the

[007-133]myriads of gulls wintering along the coasts of Florida,

[007-134]Otherways there atwixt the banks of the Arkansaw, the Rio Grande,

[007-135]the Nueces, the Brazos, the Tombigbee, the Red River, the

[007-136]Saskatchawan or the Osage, I with the spring waters laughing

[007-137]and skipping and running,

[007-138]Northward, on the sands, on some shallow bay of Paumanok, I with

[007-139]parties of snowy herons wading in the wet to seek worms and

[007-140]aquatic plants,

[007-141]Retreating, triumphantly twittering, the king-bird, from piercing

[007-142]the crow with its bill, for amusement--and I triumphantly twittering,

[007-143]The migrating flock of wild geese alighting in autumn to refresh

[007-144]themselves, [[body|the body]] of the flock feed, the sentinels outside

[007-145]move around with erect heads watching, and are from [[time]] to time

[007-146]reliev’d by other sentinels--and I feeding and taking turns

[007-147]with the rest,

[007-148]In Kanadian forests the moose, large as an ox, corner’d by hunters,

[007-149]rising desperately on his hind-feet, and plunging with his

[007-150]fore-feet, the hoofs as sharp as knives--and I, plunging at the

[007-151]hunters, corner’d and desperate,

[007-152]In the Mannahatta, streets, piers, shipping, store-houses, and the

[007-153]countless workmen working in the shops,

[007-154]And I too of the Mannahatta, singing thereof--and no less in [[self|myself]]

[007-155]than the whole of the Mannahatta in itself,

[007-156]Singing the song of These, my ever-united lands--my body no more

[007-157]inevitably united, part to part, and made out of a thousand

[007-158]diverse contributions one identity, any more than my lands

[007-159]are inevitably united and made ONE IDENTITY;

[007-160]Nativities, climates, [[grass|the grass]] of the great pastoral Plains,

[007-161]Cities, labors, [[death]], animals, products, war, good and evil--these me,

[007-162]These affording, in all their particulars, the old feuillage to me

[007-163]and to America, how can I do less than pass the clew of the union

[007-164]of them, to afford the like to you?

[007-165]Whoever you are! how can I but offer you divine leaves, that you

[007-166]also be eligible as I am?

[007-167]How can I but as here chanting, invite you for yourself to collect

[007-168]bouquets of the incomparable feuillage of these States?
