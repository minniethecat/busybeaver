# BOOK XVIII: A Broadway Pageant

[014-0001]A Broadway Pageant

[014-0002]1

[014-0003]Over the Western [[sea]] hither from Niphon come,

[014-0004]Courteous, the swart-cheek’d two-sworded envoys,

[014-0005]Leaning back in their open barouches, bare-headed, impassive,

[014-0006]Ride to-day through Manhattan.

[014-0007]Libertad! I do not know whether others behold what I behold,

[014-0008]In the procession along with the nobles of Niphon, the errand-bearers,

[014-0009]Bringing up the rear, hovering above, around, or in the ranks marching,

[014-0011]When million-footed Manhattan unpent descends to her pavements,

[014-0012]When the thunder-cracking guns arouse me with the proud roar [[love]],

[014-0013]When the round-mouth’d guns out of the smoke and smell I love

[014-0014]spit their salutes,

[014-0015]When the fire-flashing guns have fully alerted me, and

[014-0016]heaven-clouds canopy my city with a delicate thin haze,

[014-0017]When gorgeous the countless straight stems, the forests at the

[014-0018]wharves, thicken with colors,

[014-0019]When every ship richly drest carries her flag at the peak,

[014-0020]When pennants trail and street-festoons hang from the windows,

[014-0021]When Broadway is entirely given up to foot-passengers and

[014-0022]foot-standers, when the mass is densest,

[014-0023]When the facades of the houses are alive with people, when eyes

[014-0024]gaze riveted tens of thousands at a [[time]],

[014-0025]When the guests from the islands advance, when the pageant moves

[014-0026]forward visible,

[014-0027]When the summons is made, when the answer that waited thousands

[014-0028]of years answers,

[014-0029]I too arising, answering, descend to the pavements, merge with the

[014-0030]crowd, and gaze with them.
