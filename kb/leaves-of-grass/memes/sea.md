## Overview

The Sea in Walt Whitman's *Leaves of Grass* is a primal presence — vast, maternal, democratic, and cosmic. It appears throughout the collection as boundary and threshold, origin and destination, the element in which the self dissolves and expands. The sea is Whitman's most potent natural symbol: it levels all distinctions (every wave is equal), connects all shores (every continent touches the same ocean), and suggests infinite depths that mirror the soul's own unplumbed reaches.

## In Whitman's Poetry

The sea is present from the opening lines of the collection: Whitman traces the American geography "down to the Mexican sea" (001-053), establishing the ocean as the nation's physical and symbolic boundary. The sea appears as the setting for voyages ("Passage to India"), as the site of labor and danger (the drowning sailor in "Song of Myself"), and as the maternal origin of all life. Whitman's sea is always in motion — heaving, swelling, crashing — mirroring the restless energy of his own poetic line.

The sea also functions as a democratic symbol: its waves do not discriminate, its tides lift all boats equally, and its vastness dwarfs all human hierarchies. In the sea, Whitman finds an image of the cosmic self — "My course runs below the soundings of plummets" (002-1027) — unfathomable and unbounded.

## Philosophical & Literary Context

The sea has been a central poetic symbol since Homer, and Whitman draws on this deep tradition. The Romantic sea — sublime, terrifying, infinitely suggestive (Coleridge's "Rime of the Ancient Mariner," Turner's seascapes) — is a clear influence. But Whitman's sea is distinctively American and democratic: it is not the sea of imperial conquest or commercial trade but the sea of cosmic connection and infinite possibility. Emerson's "transparent eyeball" merging with the landscape finds its oceanic equivalent in Whitman's self dissolving into the sea.

## Related Concepts

- **Nature**: The sea is nature's most powerful and mysterious form
- **Democracy**: The sea's leveling power — all waves equal, all shores connected
- **The Soul**: The soul is oceanic — "below the soundings of plummets"
- **Death**: The sea is the site of death by drowning and the symbol of dissolution
- **Journey/Voyage**: The sea is the medium of spiritual and physical voyage
