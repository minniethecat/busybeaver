# BOOK XII: Song of the Broad-Axe

[009-001]Song of the Broad-Axe

[009-002]1

[009-003]Weapon shapely, naked, wan,

[009-004]Head from [[mother|the mother]]’s bowels drawn,

[009-005]Wooded flesh and metal bone, limb only one and lip only one,

[009-006]Gray-blue leaf by red-heat grown, helve produced from a little seed sown,

[009-007]Resting [[grass|the grass]] amid and upon,

[009-008]To be lean’d and to lean on.

[009-009]Strong shapes and attributes of strong shapes, masculine trades,

[009-010]sights and sounds.

[009-011]Long varied train of an emblem, dabs of music,

[009-012]Fingers of the organist skipping staccato over the keys of the great organ.

[009-013]2

[009-014]Welcome are all [[earth]]’s lands, each for its kind,

[009-015]Welcome are lands of pine and oak,

[009-016]Welcome are lands of the lemon and fig,

[009-017]Welcome are lands of gold,

[009-018]Welcome are lands of wheat and maize, welcome those of the grape,

[009-019]Welcome are lands of sugar and rice,

[009-020]Welcome the cotton-lands, welcome those of the white potato and

[009-021]sweet potato,

[009-022]Welcome are mountains, flats, sands, forests, [[prairie|prairies]],

[009-023]Welcome the rich borders of rivers, table-lands, openings,

[009-024]Welcome the measureless grazing-lands, welcome the teeming soil of

[009-025]orchards, flax, honey, hemp;

[009-026]Welcome just as much the other more hard-faced lands,

[009-027]Lands rich as lands of gold or wheat and fruit lands,

[009-028]Lands of mines, lands of the manly and rugged ores,

[009-029]Lands of coal, copper, lead, tin, zinc,

[009-030]Lands of iron--lands of the make of the axe.

[009-031]3

[009-032]The log at the wood-pile, the axe supported by it,

[009-033]The sylvan hut, the vine over the doorway, the space clear’d for garden,

[009-034]The irregular tapping of rain down on the leaves after the storm is lull’d,

[009-035]The walling and moaning at intervals, the thought of [[sea|the sea]],

[009-036]The thought of ships struck in the storm and put on their beam ends,

[009-037]and the cutting away of masts,

[009-038]The sentiment of the huge timbers of old-fashion’d houses and barns,

[009-039]The remember’d print or narrative, the voyage at a venture of men,

[009-040]families, goods,

[009-041]The disembarkation, the founding of a new city,

[009-042]The voyage of those who sought a [[new-england|New England]] and found it, the outset

[009-043]anywhere,

[009-044]The settlements of the Arkansas, Colorado, Ottawa, Willamette,

[009-045]The slow progress, the scant fare, the axe, rifle, saddle-bags;

[009-046]The beauty of all adventurous and daring persons,

[009-047]The beauty of wood-boys and wood-men with their clear untrimm’d faces,

[009-048]The beauty of independence, departure, actions that rely on themselves,

[009-049]The American contempt for statutes and ceremonies, the boundless

[009-050]impatience of restraint,

[009-051]The loose drift of character, the inkling through random types, the

[009-052]solidification;

[009-053]The butcher in the slaughter-house, the hands aboard schooners and

[009-054]sloops, the raftsman, [[pioneer|the pioneer]],

[009-055]Lumbermen in their winter camp, daybreak in the woods, stripes of

[009-056]snow on the limbs of trees, the occasional snapping,

[009-057]The glad clear sound of one’s own voice, the merry song, the natural

[009-058]life of the woods, the strong day’s work,

[009-059]The blazing fire at [[night]], the sweet taste of supper, the talk, the

[009-060]bed of hemlock-boughs and the bear-skin;

[009-061]The house-builder at work in cities or anywhere,

[009-062]The preparatory jointing, squaring, sawing, mortising,

[009-063]The hoist-up of beams, the push of them in their places, laying them

[009-064]regular,

[009-065]Setting the studs by their tenons in the mortises according as they

[009-066]were prepared,

[009-067]The blows of mallets and hammers, the attitudes of the men, their

[009-068]curv’d limbs,

[009-069]Bending, standing, astride the beams, driving in pins, holding on by

[009-070]posts and braces,

[009-071]The hook’d arm over the plate, the other arm wielding the axe,

[009-072]The floor-men forcing the planks close to be nail’d,

[009-073]Their postures bringing their weapons downward on the bearers,

[009-074]The echoes resounding through the vacant building:

[009-075]The huge storehouse carried up in the city well under way,

[009-076]The six framing-men, two in the middle and two at each end, carefully

[009-077]bearing on their shoulders a heavy stick for a cross-beam,

[009-078]The crowded line of masons with trowels in their right hands rapidly

[009-079]laying the long side-wall, two hundred feet from front to rear,

[009-080]The flexible rise and fall of backs, the continual click of the

[009-081]trowels striking the bricks,

[009-082]The bricks one after another each laid so workmanlike in its place,

[009-083]and set with a knock of the trowel-handle,

[009-084]The piles of materials, the mortar on the mortar-boards, and the

[009-085]steady replenishing by the hod-men;

[009-086]Spar-makers in the spar-yard, the swarming row of well-grown apprentices,

[009-087]The swing of their axes on the square-hew’d log shaping it toward

[009-088]the shape of a mast,

[009-089]The brisk short crackle of the steel driven slantingly into the pine,

[009-090]The butter-color’d chips flying off in great flakes and slivers,

[009-091]The limber motion of brawny young arms and hips in easy costumes,

[009-092]The constructor of wharves, bridges, piers, bulk-heads, floats,

[009-093]stays against the sea;

[009-094]The city fireman, the fire that suddenly bursts forth in the

[009-095]close-pack’d square,

[009-096]The arriving engines, the hoarse shouts, the nimble stepping and daring,

[009-097]The strong command through the fire-trumpets, the falling in line,

[009-098]the rise and fall of the arms forcing the water,

[009-099]The slender, spasmic, blue-white jets, the bringing to bear of the

[009-100]hooks and ladders and their execution,

[009-101]The crash and cut away of connecting wood-work, or through floors

[009-102]if the fire smoulders under them,

[009-103]The crowd with their lit faces watching, the glare and dense shadows;

[009-104]The forger at his forge-furnace and the user of iron after him,

[009-105]The maker of the axe large and small, and the welder and temperer,

[009-106]The chooser breathing his breath on the cold steel and trying the

[009-107]edge with his thumb,

[009-108]The one who clean-shapes the handle and sets it firmly in the socket;

[009-109]The shadowy processions of the portraits of the past users also,

[009-110]The primal patient [[mechanic|mechanics]], the architects and engineers,

[009-111]The far-off Assyrian edifice and Mizra edifice,

[009-112]The Roman lictors preceding the consuls,

[009-113]The antique European warrior with his axe in combat,

[009-114]The uplifted arm, the clatter of blows on the helmeted head,

[009-115]The [[death]]-howl, the limpsy tumbling [[body]], the rush of friend and foe

[009-116]thither,

[009-117]The siege of revolted lieges determin’d for [[freedom|liberty]],

[009-118]The summons to surrender, the battering at castle gates, the truce

[009-119]and parley,

[009-120]The sack of an old city in its [[time]],

[009-121]The bursting in of mercenaries and bigots tumultuously and disorderly,

[009-122]Roar, flames, blood, drunkenness, madness,

[009-123]Goods freely rifled from houses and temples, screams of women in the

[009-124]gripe of brigands,

[009-125]Craft and thievery of camp-followers, men running, old persons despairing,

[009-126]The hell of [[war]], the cruelties of creeds,

[009-127]The list of all executive deeds and words just or unjust,

[009-128]The power of personality just or unjust.

[009-129]4

[009-130]Muscle and pluck forever!

[009-131]What invigorates life invigorates death,

[009-132]And the dead advance as much as the living advance,

[009-133]And the future is no more uncertain than the present,

[009-134]For the roughness of the earth and of man encloses as much as the

[009-135]delicatesse of the earth and of man,

[009-136]And nothing endures but personal qualities.

[009-137]What do you think endures?

[009-138]Do you think a great city endures?

[009-139]Or a teeming manufacturing state? or a prepared constitution? or the

[009-140]best built steamships?

[009-141]Or hotels of granite and iron? or any chef-d’œuvres of engineering,

[009-142]forts, armaments?

[009-143]Away! these are not to be cherish’d for themselves,

[009-144]They fill their hour, the dancers dance, the musicians play for them,

[009-145]The show passes, all does well enough of course,

[009-146]All does very well till one flash of defiance.

[009-147]A great city is that which has the greatest men and women,

[009-148]If it be a few ragged huts it is still the greatest city in the

[009-149]whole world.

[009-150]5

[009-151]The place where a great city stands is not the place of stretch’d

[009-152]wharves, docks, manufactures, deposits of produce merely,

[009-153]Nor the place of ceaseless salutes of new-comers or the

[009-154]anchor-lifters of the departing,

[009-155]Nor the place of the tallest and costliest buildings or shops

[009-156]selling goods from the rest of the earth,

[009-157]Nor the place of the best libraries and schools, nor the place where

[009-158]money is plentiest,

[009-159]Nor the place of the most numerous population.

[009-160]Where the city stands with the brawniest breed of orators and bards,

[009-161]Where the city stands that is belov’d by these, and loves them in

[009-162]return and understands them,

[009-163]Where no monuments exist to heroes but in the common words and deeds,

[009-164]Where thrift is in its place, and prudence is in its place,

[009-165]Where the men and women think lightly of the laws,

[009-166]Where the slave ceases, and the master of slaves ceases,

[009-167]Where the populace rise at once against the never-ending audacity of

[009-168]elected persons,

[009-169]Where fierce men and women pour forth as the sea to the whistle of

[009-170]death pours its sweeping and unript waves,

[009-171]Where outside authority enters always after the precedence of inside

[009-172]authority,

[009-173]Where the citizen is always the head and ideal, and President,

[009-174]Mayor, Governor and what not, are agents for pay,

[009-175]Where children are taught to be laws to themselves, and to depend on

[009-176]themselves,

[009-177]Where equanimity is illustrated in affairs,

[009-178]Where speculations on [[soul|the soul]] are encouraged,

[009-179]Where women walk in public processions in the streets the same as the men,

[009-180]Where they enter the public assembly and take places the same as the men;

[009-181]Where the city of the faithfulest friends stands,

[009-182]Where the city of the cleanliness of the sexes stands,

[009-183]Where the city of the healthiest fathers stands,

[009-184]Where the city of the best-bodied mothers stands,

[009-185]There the great city stands.

[009-186]6

[009-187]How beggarly appear arguments before a defiant deed!

[009-188]How the floridness of the materials of cities shrivels before a

[009-189]man’s or woman’s look!

[009-190]All waits or goes by default till a strong being appears;

[009-191]A strong being is the proof of the race and of the ability of the universe,

[009-192]When he or she appears materials are overaw’d,

[009-193]The dispute on the soul stops,

[009-194]The old customs and phrases are confronted, turn’d back, or laid away.

[009-195]What is your money-making now? what can it do now?

[009-196]What is your respectability now?

[009-197]What are your theology, tuition, society, traditions, statute-books, now?

[009-198]Where are your jibes of being now?

[009-199]Where are your cavils about the soul now?

[009-200]7

[009-201]A sterile landscape covers the ore, there is as good as the best for

[009-202]all the forbidding appearance,

[009-203]There is the mine, there are the miners,

[009-204]The forge-furnace is there, the melt is accomplish’d, the hammersmen

[009-205]are at hand with their tongs and hammers,

[009-206]What always served and always serves is at hand.

[009-207]Than this nothing has better served, it has served all,

[009-208]Served the fluent-tongued and subtle-sensed Greek, and long ere the Greek,

[009-209]Served in building the buildings that last longer than any,

[009-210]Served the Hebrew, the Persian, the most ancient Hindustanee,

[009-211]Served the mound-raiser on the Mississippi, served those whose

[009-212]relics remain in Central [[america|America]],

[009-213]Served Albic temples in woods or on plains, with unhewn pillars and

[009-214]the druids,

[009-215]Served the artificial clefts, vast, high, silent, on the

[009-216]snow-cover’d hills of Scandinavia,

[009-217]Served those who time out of mind made on the granite walls rough

[009-218]sketches of the sun, moon, stars, ships, ocean waves,

[009-219]Served the paths of the irruptions of the Goths, served the pastoral

[009-220]tribes and nomads,

[009-221]Served the long distant Kelt, served the hardy pirates of the Baltic,

[009-222]Served before any of those the venerable and harmless men of Ethiopia,

[009-223]Served the making of helms for the galleys of pleasure and the

[009-224]making of those for war,

[009-225]Served all great works on land and all great works on the sea,

[009-226]For the mediaeval ages and before the mediaeval ages,

[009-227]Served not the living only then as now, but served the dead.

[009-228]8

[009-229]I see the European headsman,

[009-230]He stands mask’d, clothed in red, with huge legs and strong naked arms,

[009-231]And leans on a ponderous axe.

[009-232](Whom have you slaughter’d lately European headsman?

[009-233]Whose is that blood upon you so wet and sticky?)

[009-234]I see the clear sunsets of the martyrs,

[009-235]I see from the scaffolds the descending ghosts,

[009-236]Ghosts of dead lords, uncrown’d ladies, impeach’d ministers, rejected kings,

[009-237]Rivals, traitors, poisoners, disgraced chieftains and the rest.

[009-238]I see those who in any land have died for the good cause,

[009-239]The seed is spare, nevertheless the crop shall never run out,

[009-240](Mind you O foreign kings, O priests, the crop shall never run out.)

[009-241]I see the blood wash’d entirely away from the axe,

[009-242]Both blade and helve are clean,

[009-243]They spirt no more the blood of European nobles, they clasp no more

[009-244]the necks of queens.

[009-245]I see the headsman withdraw and become useless,

[009-246]I see the scaffold untrodden and mouldy, I see no longer any axe upon it,

[009-247]I see the mighty and friendly emblem of the power of my own race,

[009-248]the newest, largest race.

[009-249]9

[009-250](America! I do not vaunt my [[love]] for you,

[009-251]I have what I have.)

[009-252]The axe leaps!

[009-253]The solid forest gives fluid utterances,

[009-254]They tumble forth, they rise and form,

[009-255]Hut, tent, landing, survey,

[009-256]Flail, plough, pick, crowbar, spade,

[009-257]Shingle, rail, prop, wainscot, lamb, lath, panel, gable,

[009-258]Citadel, ceiling, saloon, academy, organ, exhibition-house, library,

[009-259]Cornice, trellis, pilaster, balcony, window, turret, porch,

[009-260]Hoe, rake, pitchfork, pencil, wagon, staff, saw, jack-plane, mallet,

[009-261]wedge, rounce,

[009-262]Chair, tub, hoop, table, wicket, vane, sash, floor,

[009-263]Work-box, chest, string’d instrument, boat, frame, and what not,

[009-264]Capitols of States, and capitol of the nation of States,

[009-265]Long stately rows in avenues, hospitals for orphans or for the poor or sick,

[009-266]Manhattan steamboats and clippers taking the measure of all seas.

[009-267]The shapes arise!

[009-268]Shapes of the using of axes anyhow, and the users and all that

[009-269]neighbors them,

[009-270]Cutters down of wood and haulers of it to the Penobscot or Kenebec,

[009-271]Dwellers in cabins among the Californian mountains or by the little

[009-272]lakes, or on the Columbia,

[009-273]Dwellers south on the banks of the Gila or Rio Grande, friendly

[009-274]gatherings, the characters and fun,

[009-275]Dwellers along the St. Lawrence, or north in Kanada, or down by the

[009-276]Yellowstone, dwellers on coasts and off coasts,

[009-277]Seal-fishers, whalers, arctic seamen breaking passages through the ice.

[009-278]The shapes arise!

[009-279]Shapes of factories, arsenals, foundries, markets,

[009-280]Shapes of the two-threaded tracks of railroads,

[009-281]Shapes of the sleepers of bridges, vast frameworks, girders, arches,

[009-282]Shapes of the fleets of barges, tows, lake and canal craft, river craft,

[009-283]Ship-yards and dry-docks along the Eastern and Western seas, and in

[009-284]many a bay and by-place,

[009-285]The live-oak kelsons, the pine planks, the spars, the

[009-286]hackmatack-roots for knees,

[009-287]The ships themselves on their ways, the tiers of scaffolds, the

[009-288]workmen busy outside and inside,

[009-289]The tools lying around, the great auger and little auger, the adze,

[009-290]bolt, line, square, gouge, and bead-plane.

[009-291]10

[009-292]The shapes arise!

[009-293]The shape measur’d, saw’d, jack’d, join’d, stain’d,

[009-294]The coffin-shape for the dead to lie within in his shroud,

[009-295]The shape got out in posts, in the bedstead posts, in the posts of

[009-296]the bride’s bed,

[009-297]The shape of the little trough, the shape of the rockers beneath,

[009-298]the shape of the babe’s cradle,

[009-299]The shape of the floor-planks, the floor-planks for dancers’ feet,

[009-300]The shape of the planks of the family home, the home of the friendly

[009-301]parents and children,

[009-302]The shape of the roof of the home of the happy young man and

[009-303]woman, the roof over the well-married young man and woman,

[009-304]The roof over the supper joyously cook’d by the chaste wife, and joyously

[009-305]eaten by the chaste husband, content after his day’s work.

[009-306]The shapes arise!

[009-307]The shape of the prisoner’s place in the court-room, and of him or

[009-308]her seated in the place,

[009-309]The shape of the liquor-bar lean’d against by the young rum-drinker

[009-310]and the old rum-drinker,

[009-311]The shape of the shamed and angry stairs trod by sneaking foot- steps,

[009-312]The shape of the sly settee, and the adulterous unwholesome couple,

[009-313]The shape of the gambling-board with its devilish winnings and losings,

[009-314]The shape of the step-ladder for the convicted and sentenced

[009-315]murderer, the murderer with haggard face and pinion’d arms,

[009-316]The sheriff at hand with his deputies, the silent and white-lipp’d

[009-317]crowd, the dangling of the rope.

[009-318]The shapes arise!

[009-319]Shapes of doors giving many exits and entrances,

[009-320]The door passing the dissever’d friend flush’d and in haste,

[009-321]The door that admits good news and bad news,

[009-322]The door whence the son left home confident and puff’d up,

[009-323]The door he enter’d again from a long and scandalous absence,

[009-324]diseas’d, broken down, without innocence, without means.

[009-325]11

[009-326]Her shape arises,

[009-327]She less guarded than ever, yet more guarded than ever,

[009-328]The gross and soil’d she moves among do not make her gross and soil’d,

[009-329]She knows the thoughts as she passes, nothing is conceal’d from her,

[009-330]She is none the less considerate or friendly therefor,

[009-331]She is the best belov’d, it is without exception, she has no reason

[009-332]to fear and she does not fear,

[009-333]Oaths, quarrels, hiccupp’d songs, smutty expressions, are idle to

[009-334]her as she passes,

[009-335]She is silent, she is possess’d of herself, they do not offend her,

[009-336]She receives them as the laws of Nature receive them, she is strong,

[009-337]She too is a law of Nature--there is no law stronger than she is.

[009-338]12

[009-339]The main shapes arise!

[009-340]Shapes of Democracy total, result of centuries,

[009-341]Shapes ever projecting other shapes,

[009-342]Shapes of turbulent manly cities,

[009-343]Shapes of the friends and home-givers of the whole earth,

[009-344]Shapes bracing the earth and braced with the whole earth.
