# BOOK XXXI: Thou Mother with Thy Equal Brood

[021-0001]Thou Mother with Thy Equal Brood

[021-0002]1

[021-0003]Thou Mother with thy equal brood,

[021-0004]Thou varied chain of different States, yet one identity only,

[021-0005]A special song before I go I’d sing o’er all the rest,

[021-0006]For thee, the future.

[021-0007]I’d sow a seed for thee of endless Nationality,

[021-0008]I’d fashion thy ensemble including [[body]] and [[soul]],

[021-0009]I’d show away ahead thy real Union, and how it may be accomplish’d.

[021-0010]The paths to the house I seek to make,

[021-0011]But leave to those to come the house itself.

[021-0012]Belief I sing, and preparation;

[021-0013]As Life and Nature are not great with reference to [[time|the present]] only,

[021-0014]But greater still from what is yet to come,

[021-0015]Out of that formula for thee I sing.

[021-0016]2

[021-0017]As a strong bird on pinions [[freedom|free]],

[021-0018]Joyous, the amplest spaces heavenward cleaving,

[021-0019]Such be the thought I’d think of thee [[america|America]],

[021-0020]Such be the recitative I’d bring for thee.

[021-0021]The conceits of the poets of other lands I’d bring thee not,

[021-0022]Nor the compliments that have served their turn so long,

[021-0023]Nor rhyme, nor the classics, nor perfume of foreign court or indoor

[021-0024]library;

[021-0025]But an odor I’d bring as from forests of pine in Maine, or breath of

[021-0026]an Illinois [[prairie]],

[021-0027]With open airs of Virginia or Georgia or Tennessee, or from Texas

[021-0028]uplands, or Florida’s glades,

[021-0029]Or the Saguenay’s black stream, or the wide blue spread of Huron,

[021-0030]With presentment of Yellowstone’s scenes, or Yosemite,

[021-0031]And murmuring under, pervading all, I’d bring the rustling [[sea]]-sound,

[021-0032]That endlessly sounds from the two Great Seas of the [[earth|world]].

[021-0033]And for thy subtler sense subtler refrains dread Mother,

[021-0034]Preludes of intellect tallying these and thee, mind-formulas fitted

[021-0035]for thee, real and sane and large as these and thee,

[021-0036]Thou! mounting higher, diving deeper than we knew, thou

[021-0037]transcendental Union!

[021-0038]By thee fact to be justified, blended with thought,

[021-0039]Thought of man justified, blended with [[god|God]],

[021-0040]Through thy idea, lo, [[immortality|the immortal]] reality!

[021-0041]Through thy reality, lo, the immortal idea!

[021-0042]3

[021-0043]Brain of the New World, what a task is thine,

[021-0044]To formulate the Modern--out of the peerless grandeur of the modern,

[021-0045]Out of thyself, comprising science, to recast poems, churches, art,

[021-0046](Recast, may-be discard them, end them--maybe their work is done,

[021-0047]who knows?)

[021-0048]By vision, hand, conception, on the background of the mighty past, the dead,

[021-0049]To limn with absolute faith the mighty living present.

[021-0050]And yet thou living present brain, heir of the dead, the Old World brain,

[021-0051]Thou that lay folded like an unborn babe within its folds so long,

[021-0052]Thou carefully prepared by it so long--haply thou but unfoldest it,

[021-0053]only maturest it,

[021-0054]It to eventuate in thee--the essence of the by-gone time contain’d in thee,

[021-0055]Its poems, churches, arts, unwitting to themselves, destined with

[021-0056]reference to thee;

[021-0057]Thou but the apples, long, long, long a-growing,

[021-0058]The fruit of all the Old ripening to-day in thee.

[021-0059]4

[021-0060]Sail, sail thy best, ship of Democracy,

[021-0061]Of value is thy freight, ’tis not the Present only,

[021-0062]The Past is also stored in thee,

[021-0063]Thou holdest not the venture of thyself alone, not of the Western

[021-0064]continent alone,

[021-0065]Earth’s resume entire floats on thy keel O ship, is steadied by thy spars,

[021-0066]With thee Time voyages in trust, the antecedent nations sink or

[021-0067]swim with thee,

[021-0068]With all their ancient struggles, martyrs, heroes, epics, wars, thou

[021-0069]bear’st the other continents,

[021-0070]Theirs, theirs as much as thine, the destination-port triumphant;

[021-0071]Steer then with good strong hand and wary eye O helmsman, thou

[021-0072]carriest great companions,

[021-0073]Venerable priestly Asia sails this day with thee,

[021-0074]And royal feudal Europe sails with thee.

[021-0075]5

[021-0076]Beautiful world of new superber birth that rises to my eyes,

[021-0077]Like a limitless golden cloud filling the westernr sky,

[021-0078]Emblem of general maternity lifted above all,

[021-0079]Sacred shape of the bearer of daughters and sons,

[021-0080]Out of thy teeming womb thy giant babes in ceaseless procession issuing,

[021-0081]Acceding from such gestation, taking and giving continual strength

[021-0082]and life,

[021-0083]World of the real--world of the twain in one,

[021-0084]World of the soul, born by the world of the real alone, led to

[021-0085]identity, body, by it alone,

[021-0086]Yet in beginning only, incalculable masses of composite precious materials,

[021-0087]By history’s cycles forwarded, by every nation, language, hither sent,

[021-0088]Ready, collected here, a freer, vast, electric world, to be

[021-0089]constructed here,

[021-0090](The true New World, the world of orbic science, morals, literatures

[021-0091]to come,)

[021-0092]Thou wonder world yet undefined, unform’d, neither do I define thee,

[021-0093]How can I pierce the impenetrable blank of the future?

[021-0094]I feel thy ominous greatness evil as well as good,

[021-0095]I watch thee advancing, absorbing the present, transcending the past,

[021-0096]I see thy light lighting, and thy shadow shadowing, as if the entire globe,

[021-0097]But I do not undertake to define thee, hardly to comprehend thee,

[021-0098]I but thee name, thee prophesy, as now,

[021-0099]I merely thee ejaculate!

[021-0100]Thee in thy future,

[021-0101]Thee in thy only permanent life, career, thy own unloosen’d mind,

[021-0102]thy soaring spirit,

[021-0103]Thee as another equally needed sun, radiant, ablaze, swift-moving,

[021-0104]fructifying all,

[021-0105]Thee risen in potent cheerfulness and [[joy]], in endless great hilarity,

[021-0106]Scattering for good the cloud that hung so long, that weigh’d so

[021-0107]long upon the mind of man,

[021-0108]The doubt, suspicion, dread, of gradual, certain decadence of man;

[021-0109]Thee in thy larger, saner brood of female, male--thee in thy

[021-0110]athletes, moral, spiritual, South, North, West, East,

[021-0111](To thy immortal breasts, Mother of All, thy every daughter, son,

[021-0112]endear’d alike, forever equal,)

[021-0113]Thee in thy own musicians, singers, artists, unborn yet, but certain,

[021-0114]Thee in thy moral wealth and civilization, (until which thy proudest

[021-0115]material civilization must remain in vain,)

[021-0116]Thee in thy all-supplying, all-enclosing worship--thee in no single

[021-0117]bible, saviour, merely,

[021-0118]Thy saviours countless, latent within thyself, thy bibles incessant

[021-0119]within thyself, equal to any, divine as any,

[021-0120](Thy soaring course thee formulating, not in thy two great wars, nor

[021-0121]in thy century’s visible growth,

[021-0122]But far more in these leaves and chants, thy chants, great Mother!)

[021-0123]Thee in an education grown of thee, in teachers, studies, students,

[021-0124]born of thee,

[021-0125]Thee in thy democratic fetes en-masse, thy high original festivals,

[021-0126]operas, lecturers, preachers,

[021-0127]Thee in thy ultimate, (the preparations only now completed, the

[021-0128]edifice on sure foundations tied,)

[021-0129]Thee in thy pinnacles, intellect, thought, thy topmost rational

[021-0130]joys, thy [[love]] and godlike aspiration,

[021-0131]In thy resplendent coming literati, thy full-lung’d orators, thy

[021-0132]sacerdotal bards, kosmic savans,

[021-0133]These! these in thee, (certain to come,) to-day I prophesy.

[021-0134]6

[021-0135]Land tolerating all, accepting all, not for the good alone, all good

[021-0136]for thee,

[021-0137]Land in the realms of God to be a realm unto thyself,

[021-0138]Under the rule of God to be a rule unto thyself.

[021-0139](Lo, where arise three peerless stars,

[021-0140]To be thy natal stars my country, Ensemble, Evolution, Freedom,

[021-0141]Set in the sky of Law.)

[021-0142]Land of unprecedented faith, God’s faith,

[021-0143]Thy soil, thy very subsoil, all upheav’d,

[021-0144]The general inner earth so long so sedulously draped over, now hence

[021-0145]for what it is boldly laid bare,

[021-0146]Open’d by thee to heaven’s light for benefit or bale.

[021-0147]Not for success alone,

[021-0148]Not to fair-sail unintermitted always,

[021-0149]The storm shall dash thy face, the murk of [[war]] and worse than war

[021-0150]shall cover thee all over,

[021-0151](Wert capable of war, its tug and trials? be capable of peace, its trials,

[021-0152]For the tug and mortal strain of nations come at last in prosperous

[021-0153]peace, not war;)

[021-0154]In many a smiling mask [[death]] shall approach beguiling thee, thou in

[021-0155]disease shalt swelter,

[021-0156]The livid cancer spread its hideous claws, clinging upon thy

[021-0157]breasts, seeking to strike thee deep within,

[021-0158]Consumption of the worst, moral consumption, shall rouge thy face

[021-0159]with hectic,

[021-0160]But thou shalt face thy fortunes, thy diseases, and surmount them all,

[021-0161]Whatever they are to-day and whatever through time they may be,

[021-0162]They each and all shall lift and pass away and cease from thee,

[021-0163]While thou, Time’s spirals rounding, out of thyself, thyself still

[021-0164]extricating, fusing,

[021-0165]Equable, natural, mystical Union thou, (the mortal with immortal blent,)

[021-0166]Shalt soar toward the fulfilment of the future, the spirit of the

[021-0167]body and the mind,

[021-0168]The soul, its destinies.

[021-0169]The soul, its destinies, the real real,

[021-0170](Purport of all these apparitions of the real;)

[021-0171]In thee America, the soul, its destinies,

[021-0172]Thou globe of globes! thou wonder nebulous!

[021-0173]By many a throe of heat and cold convuls’d, (by these thyself solidifying,)

[021-0174]Thou mental, moral orb--thou New, indeed new, Spiritual World!

[021-0175]The Present holds thee not--for such vast growth as thine,

[021-0176]For such unparallel’d flight as thine, such brood as thine,

[021-0177]The FUTURE only holds thee and can hold thee.

[021-0190]BOOK XXXII. FROM NOON TO STARRY NIGHT

[021-0191]Thou Orb Aloft Full-Dazzling

[021-0192]Thou orb aloft full-dazzling! thou hot October noon!

[021-0193]Flooding with sheeny light the gray beach sand,

[021-0194]The sibilant near sea with vistas far and foam,

[021-0195]And tawny streaks and shades and spreading blue;

[021-0196]O sun of noon refulgent! my special word to thee.

[021-0197]Hear me illustrious!

[021-0198]Thy lover me, for always I have loved thee,

[021-0199]Even as basking babe, then happy boy alone by some wood edge, thy

[021-0200]touching-distant beams enough,

[021-0201]Or man matured, or young or old, as now to thee I launch my invocation.

[021-0202](Thou canst not with thy dumbness me deceive,

[021-0203]I know before the fitting man all Nature yields,

[021-0204]Though answering not in words, the skies, trees, hear his voice--and

[021-0205]thou O sun,

[021-0206]As for thy throes, thy perturbations, sudden breaks and shafts of

[021-0207]flame gigantic,

[021-0208]I understand them, I know those flames, those perturbations well.)

[021-0209]Thou that with fructifying heat and light,

[021-0210]O’er myriad farms, o’er lands and waters North and South,

[021-0211]O’er Mississippi’s endless course, o’er Texas’ grassy plains,

[021-0212]Kanada’s woods,

[021-0213]O’er all the globe that turns its face to thee shining in space,

[021-0214]Thou that impartially enfoldest all, not only continents, seas,

[021-0215]Thou that to grapes and weeds and little wild flowers givest so liberally,

[021-0216]Shed, shed thyself on mine and me, with but a fleeting ray out of

[021-0217]thy million millions,

[021-0218]Strike through these chants.

[021-0219]Nor only launch thy subtle dazzle and thy strength for these,

[021-0220]Prepare the later afternoon of me [[self|myself]]--prepare my lengthening shadows,

[021-0221]Prepare my starry nights.

[021-0227]The face of the singing of music, the grand faces of natural lawyers

[021-0228]and judges broad at the back-top,

[021-0229]The faces of hunters and fishers bulged at the brows, the shaved

[021-0230]blanch’d faces of orthodox citizens,

[021-0231]The pure, extravagant, yearning, questioning artist’s face,

[021-0232]The ugly face of some beautiful soul, the handsome detested or

[021-0233]despised face,

[021-0234]The sacred faces of infants, the illuminated face of [[mother|the mother]] of

[021-0235]many children,

[021-0236]The face of an amour, the face of veneration,

[021-0237]The face as of a dream, the face of an immobile rock,

[021-0244]2

[021-0245]Do you suppose I could be content with all if I thought them their

[021-0246]own finale?

[021-0247]This now is too lamentable a face for a man,

[021-0248]Some abject louse asking leave to be, cringing for it,

[021-0249]Some milk-nosed maggot blessing what lets it wrig to its hole.

[021-0250]This face is a dog’s snout sniffing for garbage,

[021-0251]Snakes nest in that mouth, I hear the sibilant threat.

[021-0252]This face is a haze more chill than the arctic sea,

[021-0253]Its sleepy and wobbling icebergs crunch as they go.

[021-0254]This is a face of bitter herbs, this an emetic, they need no label,

[021-0256]This face is an epilepsy, its wordless tongue gives out the unearthly cry,

[021-0257]Its veins down the neck distend, its eyes roll till they show

[021-0258]nothing but their whites,

[021-0259]Its teeth grit, the palms of the hands are cut by the turn’d-in nails,

[021-0260]The man falls struggling and foaming to the ground, while he

[021-0261]speculates well.

[021-0262]This face is bitten by vermin and worms,

[021-0263]And this is some murderer’s knife with a half-pull’d scabbard.

[021-0264]This face owes to the sexton his dismalest fee,

[021-0265]An unceasing death-bell tolls there.

[021-0266]3

[021-0267]Features of my equals would you trick me with your creas’d and

[021-0268]cadaverous march?

[021-0269]Well, you cannot trick me.

[021-0270]I see your rounded never-erased flow,

[021-0271]I see ’neath the rims of your haggard and mean disguises.

[021-0278]The same wait to clear the rubbish from the fallen tenement,

[021-0279]And I shall look again in a score or two of ages,

[021-0281]as good as myself.

[021-0327]The Mystic Trumpeter

[021-0328]1

[021-0329]Hark, some wild trumpeter, some strange musician,

[021-0330]Hovering unseen in air, vibrates capricious tunes to-[[night]].

[021-0331]I hear thee trumpeter, listening alert I catch thy notes,

[021-0332]Now pouring, whirling like a tempest round me,

[021-0333]Now low, subdued, now in the distance lost.

[021-0334]2

[021-0335]Come nearer bodiless one, haply in thee resounds

[021-0336]Some dead composer, haply thy pensive life

[021-0337]Was fill’d with aspirations high, unform’d ideals,

[021-0338]Waves, oceans musical, chaotically surging,

[021-0339]That now ecstatic ghost, close to me bending, thy cornet echoing, pealing,

[021-0340]Gives out to no one’s ears but mine, but freely gives to mine,

[021-0341]That I may thee translate.

[021-0342]3

[021-0343]Blow trumpeter free and clear, I follow thee,

[021-0344]While at thy liquid prelude, glad, serene,

[021-0345]The fretting world, the streets, the noisy hours of day withdraw,

[021-0346]A holy calm descends like dew upon me,

[021-0347]I walk in cool refreshing night the walks of Paradise,

[021-0348]I scent [[grass|the grass]], the moist air and the roses;

[021-0349]Thy song expands my numb’d imbonded spirit, thou freest, launchest me,

[021-0350]Floating and basking upon heaven’s lake.

[021-0351]4

[021-0352]Blow again trumpeter! and for my sensuous eyes,

[021-0353]Bring the old pageants, show the feudal world.

[021-0354]What charm thy music works! thou makest pass before me,

[021-0355]Ladies and cavaliers long dead, barons are in their castle halls,

[021-0356]the troubadours are singing,

[021-0357]Arm’d knights go forth to redress wrongs, some in quest of the holy Graal;

[021-0358]I see the tournament, I see the contestants incased in heavy armor

[021-0359]seated on stately champing horses,

[021-0360]I hear the shouts, the sounds of blows and smiting steel;

[021-0361]I see the Crusaders’ tumultuous armies--hark, how the cymbals clang,

[021-0362]Lo, where the monks walk in advance, bearing the cross on high.

[021-0363]5

[021-0364]Blow again trumpeter! and for thy theme,

[021-0365]Take now the enclosing theme of all, the solvent and the setting,

[021-0366]Love, that is pulse of all, the sustenance and the pang,

[021-0367]The heart of man and woman all for love,

[021-0368]No other theme but love--knitting, enclosing, all-diffusing love.

[021-0369]O how the immortal phantoms crowd around me!

[021-0370]I see the vast alembic ever working, I see and know the flames that

[021-0371]heat the world,

[021-0372]The glow, the blush, the beating hearts of lovers,

[021-0373]So blissful happy some, and some so silent, dark, and nigh to death;

[021-0374]Love, that is all the earth to lovers--love, that mocks time and space,

[021-0375]Love, that is day and night--love, that is sun and moon and stars,

[021-0376]Love, that is crimson, sumptuous, sick with perfume,

[021-0377]No other words but words of love, no other thought but love.

[021-0378]6

[021-0379]Blow again trumpeter--conjure war’s alarums.

[021-0380]Swift to thy spell a shuddering hum like distant thunder rolls,

[021-0381]Lo, where the arm’d men hasten--lo, mid the clouds of dust the glint

[021-0383]I see the grime-faced cannoneers, I mark the rosy flash amid the

[021-0390]7

[021-0392]Thou melt’st my heart, my brain--thou movest, drawest, changest

[021-0394]And now thy sullen notes send darkness through me,

[021-0395]Thou takest away all cheering light, all hope,

[021-0396]I see the enslaved, the overthrown, the hurt, the opprest of the
