# BOOK XVI: A Song of the Rolling Earth

[013-001]A Song of the Rolling Earth

[013-002]1

[013-003]A song of the rolling [[earth]], and of words according,

[013-004]Were you thinking that those were the words, those upright lines?

[013-005]those curves, angles, dots?

[013-006]No, those are not the words, the substantial words are in the ground

[013-007]and [[sea]],

[013-008]They are in the air, they are in you.

[013-009]Were you thinking that those were the words, those delicious sounds

[013-010]out of your friends’ mouths?

[013-011]No, the real words are more delicious than they.

[013-012]Human bodies are words, myriads of words,

[013-013](In the best poems re-appears [[body|the body]], man’s or woman’s,

[013-014]well-shaped, natural, gay,

[013-015]Every part able, active, receptive, without shame or the need of shame.)

[013-016]Air, soil, water, fire--those are words,

[013-017]I [[self|myself]] am a word with them--my qualities interpenetrate with

[013-018]theirs--my name is nothing to them,

[013-019]Though it were told in the three thousand languages, what would

[013-020]air, soil, water, fire, know of my name?

[013-021]A healthy presence, a friendly or commanding gesture, are words,

[013-022]sayings, meanings,

[013-023]The charms that go with the mere looks of some men and women,

[013-024]are sayings and meanings also.

[013-025]The workmanship of souls is by those inaudible words of the earth,

[013-026]The masters know the earth’s words and use them more than audible words.

[013-027]Amelioration is one of the earth’s words,

[013-028]The earth neither lags nor hastens,

[013-029]It has all attributes, growths, effects, latent in itself from the jump,

[013-030]It is not half beautiful only, defects and excrescences show just as

[013-031]much as perfections show.

[013-032]The earth does not withhold, it is generous enough,

[013-033]The truths of the earth continually wait, they are not so conceal’d either,

[013-034]They are calm, subtle, untransmissible by print,

[013-035]They are imbued through all things conveying themselves willingly,

[013-036]Conveying a sentiment and invitation, I utter and utter,

[013-037]I speak not, yet if you hear me not of what avail am I to you?

[013-038]To bear, to better, lacking these of what avail am I?

[013-039](Accouche! accouchez!

[013-040]Will you rot your own fruit in yourself there?

[013-041]Will you squat and stifle there?)

[013-042]The earth does not argue,

[013-043]Is not pathetic, has no arrangements,

[013-044]Does not scream, haste, persuade, threaten, promise,

[013-045]Makes no discriminations, has no conceivable failures,

[013-046]Closes nothing, refuses nothing, shuts none out,

[013-047]Of all the powers, objects, states, it notifies, shuts none out.

[013-048]The earth does not exhibit itself nor refuse to exhibit itself,

[013-049]possesses still underneath,

[013-050]Underneath the ostensible sounds, the august chorus of heroes, the

[013-051]wail of slaves,

[013-052]Persuasions of lovers, curses, gasps of the [[death|dying]], laughter of young

[013-053]people, accents of bargainers,

[013-054]Underneath these possessing words that never fall.

[013-055]To her children the words of the eloquent dumb great [[mother]] never fail,

[013-056]The true words do not fail, for motion does not fail and reflection

[013-057]does not fall,

[013-058]Also the day and [[night]] do not fall, and the voyage we pursue does not fall.

[013-059]Of the interminable sisters,

[013-060]Of the ceaseless cotillons of sisters,

[013-061]Of the centripetal and centrifugal sisters, the elder and younger sisters,

[013-062]The beautiful sister we know dances on with the rest.

[013-063]With her ample back towards every beholder,

[013-064]With the fascinations of youth and the equal fascinations of age,

[013-065]Sits she whom I too [[love]] like the rest, sits undisturb’d,

[013-066]Holding up in her hand what has the character of a mirror, while her

[013-067]eyes glance back from it,

[013-068]Glance as she sits, inviting none, denying none,

[013-069]Holding a mirror day and night tirelessly before her own face.

[013-070]Seen at hand or seen at a distance,

[013-071]Duly the twenty-four appear in public every day,

[013-072]Duly approach and pass with their companions or a companion,

[013-073]Looking from no countenances of their own, but from the countenances

[013-074]of those who are with them,

[013-075]From the countenances of children or women or the manly countenance,

[013-076]From the open countenances of animals or from inanimate things,

[013-077]From the landscape or waters or from the exquisite apparition of the sky,

[013-078]From our countenances, mine and yours, faithfully returning them,

[013-079]Every day in public appearing without fall, but never twice with the

[013-080]same companions.

[013-081]Embracing man, embracing all, proceed the three hundred and

[013-082]sixty-five resistlessly round the sun;

[013-083]Embracing all, soothing, supporting, follow close three hundred and

[013-084]sixty-five offsets of the first, sure and necessary as they.

[013-085]Tumbling on steadily, nothing dreading,

[013-086]Sunshine, storm, cold, heat, forever withstanding, passing, carrying,

[013-087]The [[soul]]’s realization and determination still inheriting,

[013-088]The fluid vacuum around and ahead still entering and dividing,

[013-089]No balk retarding, no anchor anchoring, on no rock striking,

[013-090]Swift, glad, content, unbereav’d, nothing losing,

[013-091]Of all able and ready at any [[time]] to give strict account,

[013-092]The divine ship sails [[god|the divine]] sea.

[013-093]2

[013-094]Whoever you are! motion and reflection are especially for you,

[013-095]The divine ship sails the divine sea for you.

[013-096]Whoever you are! you are he or she for whom the earth is solid and liquid,

[013-097]You are he or she for whom the sun and moon hang in the sky,

[013-098]For none more than you are the present and the past,

[013-099]For none more than you is [[immortality]].

[013-100]Each man to himself and each woman to herself, is the word of the

[013-101]past and present, and the true word of immortality;

[013-102]No one can acquire for another--not one,

[013-103]Not one can grow for another--not one.

[013-104]The song is to the singer, and comes back most to him,

[013-105]The teaching is to the teacher, and comes back most to him,

[013-106]The murder is to the murderer, and comes back most to him,

[013-107]The theft is to the thief, and comes back most to him,

[013-108]The love is to the lover, and comes back most to him,

[013-109]The gift is to the giver, and comes back most to him--it cannot fail,

[013-110]The oration is to the orator, the acting is to the actor and actress

[013-111]not to the audience,

[013-112]And no man understands any greatness or goodness but his own, or

[013-113]the indication of his own.

[013-114]3

[013-115]I swear the earth shall surely be complete to him or her who shall

[013-116]be complete,

[013-117]The earth remains jagged and broken only to him or her who remains

[013-118]jagged and broken.

[013-119]I swear there is no greatness or power that does not emulate those

[013-120]of the earth,

[013-121]There can be no theory of any account unless it corroborate the

[013-122]theory of the earth,

[013-123]No politics, song, religion, behavior, or what not, is of account,

[013-124]unless it compare with the amplitude of the earth,

[013-125]Unless it face the exactness, vitality, impartiality, rectitude of

[013-126]the earth.

[013-127]I swear I begin to see love with sweeter spasms than that which

[013-128]responds love,

[013-129]It is that which contains itself, which never invites and never refuses.

[013-130]I swear I begin to see little or nothing in audible words,

[013-131]All merges toward the presentation of the unspoken meanings of the earth,

[013-132]Toward him who sings the songs of the body and of the truths of the earth,

[013-133]Toward him who makes the dictionaries of words that print cannot touch.

[013-134]I swear I see what is better than to tell the best,

[013-135]It is always to leave the best untold.

[013-136]When I undertake to tell the best I find I cannot,

[013-137]My tongue is ineffectual on its pivots,

[013-138]My breath will not be obedient to its organs,

[013-139]I become a dumb man.

[013-140]The best of the earth cannot be told anyhow, all or any is best,

[013-141]It is not what you anticipated, it is cheaper, easier, nearer,

[013-142]Things are not dismiss’d from the places they held before,

[013-143]The earth is just as positive and direct as it was before,

[013-144]Facts, religions, improvements, politics, trades, are as real as before,

[013-145]But the soul is also real, it too is positive and direct,

[013-146]No reasoning, no proof has establish’d it,

[013-147]Undeniable growth has establish’d it.

[013-148]4

[013-149]These to echo the tones of souls and the phrases of souls,

[013-150](If they did not echo the phrases of souls what were they then?

[013-151]If they had not reference to you in especial what were they then?)

[013-152]I swear I will never henceforth have to do with the faith that tells

[013-153]the best,

[013-154]I will have to do only with that faith that leaves the best untold.

[013-155]Say on, sayers! sing on, singers!

[013-156]Delve! mould! pile the words of the earth!

[013-157]Work on, age after age, nothing is to be lost,

[013-158]It may have to wait long, but it will certainly come in use,

[013-159]When the materials are all prepared and ready, the architects shall appear.

[013-160]I swear to you the architects shall appear without fall,

[013-161]I swear to you they will understand you and justify you,

[013-162]The greatest among them shall be he who best knows you, and encloses

[013-163]all and is faithful to all,

[013-164]He and the rest shall not forget you, they shall perceive that you

[013-165]are not an iota less than they,

[013-166]You shall be fully glorified in them.

[013-167]Youth, Day, Old Age and Night

[013-168]Youth, large, lusty, loving--youth full of grace, force, fascination,

[013-169]Do you know that Old Age may come after you with equal grace,

[013-170]force, fascination?

[013-171]Day full-blown and splendid-day of the immense sun, action,

[013-172]ambition, laughter,

[013-173]The Night follows close with millions of suns, and sleep and

[013-174]restoring darkness.

[013-175]BOOK XVII. BIRDS OF PASSAGE

[013-176]Song of the Universal

[013-177]1

[013-178]Come said the Muse,

[013-179]Sing me a song no poet yet has chanted,

[013-180]Sing me the universal.

[013-181]In this broad earth of ours,

[013-182]Amid the measureless grossness and the slag,

[013-183]Enclosed and safe within its central heart,

[013-184]Nestles the seed perfection.

[013-185]By every life a share or more or less,

[013-186]None born but it is born, conceal’d or unconceal’d the seed is waiting.

[013-187]2

[013-188]Lo! keen-eyed towering science,

[013-189]As from tall peaks the modern overlooking,

[013-190]Successive absolute fiats issuing.

[013-191]Yet again, lo! the soul, above all science,

[013-192]For it has history gather’d like husks around the globe,

[013-193]For it the entire star-myriads roll through the sky.

[013-194]In spiral routes by long detours,

[013-195](As a much-tacking ship upon the sea,)

[013-196]For it the partial to the permanent flowing,

[013-197]For it the real to the ideal tends.

[013-198]For it the mystic evolution,

[013-199]Not the right only justified, what we call evil also justified.

[013-200]Forth from their masks, no matter what,

[013-201]From the huge festering trunk, from craft and guile and tears,

[013-202]Health to emerge and [[joy]], joy universal.

[013-203]Out of the bulk, the morbid and the shallow,

[013-204]Out of the bad majority, the varied countless frauds of men and states,

[013-205]Electric, antiseptic yet, cleaving, suffusing all,

[013-206]Only the good is universal.

[013-207]3

[013-208]Over the mountain-growths disease and sorrow,

[013-209]An uncaught bird is ever hovering, hovering,

[013-210]High in the purer, happier air.

[013-211]From imperfection’s murkiest cloud,

[013-212]Darts always forth one ray of perfect light,

[013-213]One flash of heaven’s glory.

[013-214]To fashion’s, custom’s discord,

[013-215]To the mad Babel-din, the deafening orgies,

[013-216]Soothing each lull a strain is heard, just heard,

[013-217]From some far shore the final chorus sounding.

[013-218]O the blest eyes, the happy hearts,

[013-219]That see, that know the guiding thread so fine,

[013-220]Along the mighty labyrinth.

[013-221]4

[013-222]And thou [[america|America]],

[013-223]For the scheme’s culmination, its thought and its reality,

[013-224]For these (not for thyself) thou hast arrived.

[013-225]Thou too surroundest all,

[013-226]Embracing carrying welcoming all, thou too by pathways broad and new,

[013-227]To the ideal tendest.

[013-228]The measure’d faiths of other lands, the grandeurs of the past,

[013-229]Are not for thee, but grandeurs of thine own,

[013-230]Deific faiths and amplitudes, absorbing, comprehending all,

[013-231]All eligible to all.

[013-232]All, all for immortality,

[013-233]Love like the light silently wrapping all,

[013-234]Nature’s amelioration blessing all,

[013-235]The blossoms, fruits of ages, orchards divine and certain,

[013-236]Forms, objects, growths, humanities, to spiritual images ripening.

[013-237]Give me O God to sing that thought,

[013-238]Give me, give him or her I love this quenchless faith,

[013-239]In Thy ensemble, whatever else withheld withhold not from us,

[013-240]Belief in plan of Thee enclosed in Time and Space,

[013-241]Health, peace, salvation universal.

[013-242]Is it a dream?

[013-243]Nay but the lack of it the dream,

[013-244]And failing it life’s lore and wealth a dream,

[013-245]And all the world a dream.

[013-354]To You

[013-355]Whoever you are, I fear you are walking the walks of dreams,

[013-356]I fear these supposed realities are to melt from under your feet and hands,

[013-357]Even now your features, joys, speech, house, trade, manners,

[013-358]troubles, follies, costume, crimes, dissipate away from you,

[013-359]Your true soul and body appear before me.

[013-360]They stand forth out of affairs, out of commerce, shops, work,

[013-361]farms, clothes, the house, buying, selling, eating, drinking,

[013-362]suffering, dying.

[013-363]Whoever you are, now I place my hand upon you, that you be my poem,

[013-364]I whisper with my lips close to your ear.

[013-365]I have loved many women and men, but I love none better than you.

[013-366]O I have been dilatory and dumb,

[013-367]I should have made my way straight to you long ago,

[013-368]I should have blabb’d nothing but you, I should have chanted nothing

[013-369]but you.

[013-370]I will leave all and come and make the hymns of you,

[013-371]None has understood you, but I understand you,

[013-372]None has done justice to you, you have not done justice to yourself,

[013-373]None but has found you imperfect, I only find no imperfection in you,

[013-374]None but would subordinate you, I only am he who will never consent

[013-375]to subordinate you,

[013-376]I only am he who places over you no master, owner, better, God,

[013-377]beyond what waits intrinsically in yourself.

[013-378]Painters have painted their swarming groups and the centre-figure of all,

[013-379]From the head of the centre-figure spreading a nimbus of gold-color’d light,

[013-380]But I paint myriads of heads, but paint no head without its nimbus

[013-381]of gold-color’d light,

[013-382]From my hand from the brain of every man and woman it streams,

[013-383]effulgently flowing forever.

[013-384]O I could sing such grandeurs and glories about you!

[013-385]You have not known what you are, you have slumber’d upon yourself

[013-386]all your life,

[013-387]Your eyelids have been the same as closed most of the time,

[013-388]What you have done returns already in mockeries,

[013-389](Your thrift, knowledge, prayers, if they do not return in

[013-390]mockeries, what is their return?)

[013-392]Underneath them and within them I see you lurk,

[013-393]I pursue you where none else has pursued you,

[013-394]Silence, the desk, the flippant expression, the night, the

[013-395]accustom’d routine, if these conceal you from others or from

[013-396]yourself, they do not conceal you from me,

[013-397]The shaved face, the unsteady eye, the impure complexion, if these

[013-398]balk others they do not balk me,

[013-399]The pert apparel, the deform’d attitude, drunkenness, greed,

[013-400]premature death, all these I part aside.

[013-401]There is no endowment in man or woman that is not tallied in you,

[013-403]No pluck, no endurance in others, but as good is in you,

[013-404]No pleasure waiting for others, but an equal pleasure waits for you.

[013-406]to you,

[013-413]These furies, elements, storms, motions of Nature, throes of apparent
