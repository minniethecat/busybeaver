# BOOK XIV: Song of the Redwood-Tree

[011-001]Song of the Redwood-Tree

[011-002]1

[011-003]A [[california|California]] song,

[011-004]A prophecy and indirection, a thought impalpable to breathe as air,

[011-005]A chorus of dryads, fading, departing, or hamadryads departing,

[011-006]A murmuring, fateful, giant voice, out of [[earth|the earth]] and sky,

[011-007]Voice of a mighty [[death|dying]] tree in the redwood forest dense.

[011-008]Farewell my brethren,

[011-009]Farewell O earth and sky, farewell ye neighboring [[sea|waters]],

[011-010]My [[time]] has ended, my term has come.

[011-011]Along the northern coast,

[011-012]Just back from the rock-bound shore and the caves,

[011-013]In the saline air from the sea in the Mendocino country,

[011-014]With the surge for base and accompaniment low and hoarse,

[011-015]With crackling blows of axes sounding musically driven by strong arms,

[011-016]Riven deep by the sharp tongues of the axes, there in the redwood

[011-017]forest dense,

[011-018]I heard the might tree its death-chant chanting.

[011-019]The choppers heard not, the camp shanties echoed not,

[011-020]The quick-ear’d teamsters and chain and jack-screw men heard not,

[011-021]As the wood-spirits came from their haunts of a thousand years to

[011-022]join the refrain,

[011-023]But in my [[soul]] I plainly heard.

[011-024]Murmuring out of its myriad leaves,

[011-025]Down from its lofty top rising two hundred feet high,

[011-026]Out of its stalwart trunk and limbs, out of its foot-thick bark,

[011-027]That chant of the seasons and time, chant not of the past only but

[011-028]the future.

[011-029]You untold life of me,

[011-030]And all you venerable and innocent joys,

[011-031]Perennial hardy life of me with joys ’mid rain and many a summer sun,

[011-032]And the white snows and [[night]] and the wild winds;

[011-033]O the great patient rugged joys, my soul’s strong joys unreck’d by man,

[011-034](For know I bear the soul befitting me, I too have consciousness, identity,

[011-035]And all the rocks and mountains have, and all the earth,)

[011-036]Joys of the life befitting me and brothers mine,

[011-037]Our time, our term has come.

[011-038]Nor yield we mournfully majestic brothers,

[011-039]We who have grandly fill’d our time,

[011-040]With Nature’s calm content, with tacit huge [[joy|delight]],

[011-041]We welcome what we wrought for through the past,

[011-042]And leave the field for them.

[011-043]For them predicted long,

[011-044]For a superber race, they too to grandly fill their time,

[011-045]For them we abdicate, in them ourselves ye forest kings.’

[011-046]In them these skies and airs, these mountain peaks, Shasta, Nevadas,

[011-047]These huge precipitous cliffs, this amplitude, these valleys, far Yosemite,

[011-048]To be in them absorb’d, assimilated.

[011-049]Then to a loftier strain,

[011-050]Still prouder, more ecstatic rose the chant,

[011-051]As if the heirs, the deities of [[the-west|the West]],

[011-052]Joining with master-tongue bore part.

[011-053]Not wan from Asia’s fetiches,

[011-054]Nor red from Europe’s old dynastic slaughter-house,

[011-055](Area of murder-plots of thrones, with scent left yet of wars and

[011-056]scaffolds everywhere,

[011-057]But come from Nature’s long and harmless throes, peacefully builded thence,

[011-058]These virgin lands, lands of the Western shore,

[011-059]To the new culminating man, to you, the empire new,

[011-060]You promis’d long, we pledge, we dedicate.

[011-061]You occult deep volitions,

[011-062]You average spiritual manhood, purpose of all, pois’d on yourself,

[011-063]giving not taking law,

[011-064]You womanhood divine, mistress and source of all, whence life and

[011-065][[love]] and aught that comes from life and love,

[011-066]You unseen moral essence of all the vast materials of [[america|America]], age

[011-067]upon age working in death the same as life,)

[011-068]You that, sometimes known, oftener unknown, really shape and mould

[011-069]the New World, adjusting it to Time and Space,

[011-070]You hidden national will lying in your abysms, conceal’d but ever alert,

[011-071]You past and present purposes tenaciously pursued, may-be

[011-072]unconscious of yourselves,

[011-073]Unswerv’d by all the passing errors, perturbations of the surface;

[011-074]You vital, universal, deathless germs, beneath all creeds, arts,

[011-075]statutes, literatures,

[011-076]Here build your homes for good, establish here, these areas entire,

[011-077]lands of the Western shore,

[011-078]We pledge, we dedicate to you.

[011-079]For man of you, your characteristic race,

[011-080]Here may he hardy, sweet, gigantic grow, here tower proportionate to Nature,

[011-081]Here climb the vast pure spaces unconfined, uncheck’d by wall or roof,

[011-082]Here laugh with storm or sun, here joy, here patiently inure,

[011-083]Here heed himself, unfold himself, (not others’ formulas heed,)

[011-084]here fill his time,

[011-085]To duly fall, to aid, unreck’d at last,

[011-086]To disappear, to serve.

[011-087]Thus on the northern coast,

[011-088]In the echo of teamsters’ calls and the clinking chains, and the

[011-089]music of choppers’ axes,

[011-090]The falling trunk and limbs, the crash, the muffled shriek, the groan,

[011-091]Such words combined from the redwood-tree, as of voices ecstatic,

[011-092]ancient and rustling,

[011-093]The century-lasting, unseen dryads, singing, withdrawing,

[011-094]All their recesses of forests and mountains leaving,

[011-095]From the Cascade range to the Wahsatch, or Idaho far, or Utah,

[011-096]To the deities of the modern henceforth yielding,

[011-097]The chorus and indications, the vistas of coming humanity, the

[011-098]settlements, features all,

[011-099]In the Mendocino woods I caught.

[011-100]2

[011-101]The flashing and golden pageant of California,

[011-102]The sudden and gorgeous drama, the sunny and ample lands,

[011-103]The long and varied stretch from Puget sound to Colorado south,

[011-104]Lands bathed in sweeter, rarer, healthier air, valleys and mountain cliffs,

[011-105]The fields of Nature long prepared and fallow, the silent, cyclic chemistry,

[011-106]The slow and steady ages plodding, the unoccupied surface ripening,

[011-107]the rich ores forming beneath;

[011-108]At last the New arriving, assuming, taking possession,

[011-109]A swarming and busy race settling and organizing everywhere,

[011-110]Ships coming in from the whole round world, and going out to the

[011-111]whole world,

[011-112]To India and China and Australia and the thousand island paradises

[011-113]of the Pacific,

[011-114]Populous cities, the latest inventions, the steamers on the rivers,

[011-115]the railroads, with many a thrifty farm, with machinery,

[011-116]And wool and wheat and the grape, and diggings of yellow gold.

[011-117]3

[011-118]But more in you than these, lands of the Western shore,

[011-119](These but the means, the implements, the standing-ground,)

[011-120]I see in you, certain to come, the promise of thousands of years,

[011-121]till now deferr’d,

[011-122]Promis’d to be fulfill’d, our common kind, the race.

[011-123]The new society at last, proportionate to Nature,

[011-124]In man of you, more than your mountain peaks or stalwart trees imperial,

[011-125]In woman more, far more, than all your gold or vines, or even vital air.

[011-126]Fresh come, to a new world indeed, yet long prepared,

[011-127]I see the genius of the modern, child of the real and ideal,

[011-128]Clearing the ground for broad humanity, the true America, heir of

[011-129]the past so grand,

[011-130]To build a grander future.
