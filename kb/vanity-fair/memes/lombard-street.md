# Lombard Street

Lombard Street is the historic banking center of the City of London. In *Vanity Fair*, it serves as the financial backdrop for the commercial world inhabited by the Osborne and Bullock families.

## Hulker, Bullock & Co.

The banking firm of Hulker, Bullock & Co. is the street's principal presence in the novel. Frederick Bullock, a partner in the firm, courts and marries Maria Osborne:

> When young Bullock (of the firm of Hulker, Bullock & Co., Bankers, Lombard Street), who had been making up to Miss Maria the last two seasons, actually asked Amelia to dance the cotillon, could you expect that the former young lady should be pleased? [012-006]

## George Osborne's Financial Transactions

When George Osborne needs to cash a draft after his rupture with his father, he goes directly to Lombard Street:

> The draft was upon our friends Hulker and Bullock of Lombard Street, to whose house, still thinking he was doing business, George bent his way, and from whom he received his money. [026-027]

This moment is poignant — George still instinctively turns to his father's business connections even as he has been disinherited.

## The Bullock Marriage

Maria Osborne's marriage to Frederick Bullock ties the Osborne family's commercial wealth to the Lombard Street banking establishment. The Bullocks' financial acumen is emphasized throughout:

> My darling Frederick must positively be an eldest son; and—and do ask Papa to bring us back his account in Lombard Street, will you, dear? [046-006]

## Social Aspiration

Despite their banking wealth, the Bullocks aspire to aristocratic society. The distance between Lombard Street and Mayfair is both geographical and social:

> Mrs. Frederick Bullock, for instance, would have gone on her knees from May Fair to Lombard Street, if Lady Steyne and Lady Gaunt had been waiting in the City to raise her up [049-016]

## Significance

Lombard Street represents the commercial foundation of Regency society — the world of banking, credit, and trade that underpins the aristocratic surface Thackeray satirizes. The Osborne family's wealth originates in this world, and their social aspirations are enabled by Lombard Street's financial machinery.
