# Military Figures and Institutions

Thackeray's *Vanity Fair* is saturated with military life. The British Army is not merely a background institution but a vehicle for social ambition (George Osborne's commission), a refuge for the disgraced (Rawdon Crawley's colonial posting), and a measure of genuine worth (Dobbin's steady, unglamorous service).
