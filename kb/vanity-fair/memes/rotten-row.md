# Rotten Row

**Rotten Row** is a broad riding track along the south side of [[hyde-park|Hyde Park]] in London, established in the late seventeenth century and famous during the Regency period as the place for fashionable London society to see and be seen on horseback.

## Rawdon Crawley's admiration

In *Vanity Fair*, Rotten Row appears as the backdrop for Rawdon Crawley's burgeoning infatuation with Becky Sharp. The narrator describes how Rawdon would turn over Becky's jokes in his mind long after she had spoken them:

> If she was jocular, he used to revolve her jokes in his mind, and explode over them half an hour afterwards in the street, to the surprise of the groom in the tilbury by his side, or the comrade riding with him in Rotten Row [016-003].

The passage captures both Rawdon's simple, good-natured devotion and the social world of Regency London, where Rotten Row served as a daily parade ground for the gentry and military men like Rawdon.

## In the novel

Rotten Row appears only in this single reference, but it is a telling detail: Rawdon's position as a cavalry officer and a man about town is reinforced by his presence on this most fashionable of riding tracks. His public explosion of laughter at Becky's wit — remembered from an earlier conversation — humanises the "big warrior" and foreshadows his eventual marriage to her.
