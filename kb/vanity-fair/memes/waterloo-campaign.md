# The Waterloo Campaign

The Battle of Waterloo (1815) is the historical fulcrum of *Vanity Fair*'s middle chapters. George Osborne marches to Belgium with the British Army, Rawdon Crawley and William Dobbin serve in the campaign, and the battle's aftermath — George's death, Amelia's destitution, Dobbin's prolonged devotion — drives the novel's second act.
