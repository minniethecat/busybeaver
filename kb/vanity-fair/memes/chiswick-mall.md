# Chiswick Mall

> (001-001) ...there drove up to the great iron gate of Miss Pinkerton's academy for young ladies, on Chiswick Mall, a large family coach, with two fat horses in blazing harness...

Chiswick Mall is the location of Miss Pinkerton's Academy for Young Ladies, the genteel seminary where the novel opens and where [[amelia-sedley|Amelia Sedley]] and [[rebecca-sharp|Rebecca Sharp]] are first introduced to the reader. The academy, also known as Minerva House (019-011), stands as the portal from which both young women depart into Vanity Fair — Amelia as the beloved and tearful darling, Rebecca as the defiant and ambitious outsider.

## In the Novel

The opening chapter of *Vanity Fair* is set entirely at the academy on Chiswick Mall. On the day of Amelia's departure, the Sedley family coach arrives at the "great iron gate," and inside the establishment Miss Pinkerton presides over the ceremonial leave-taking with pompous dignity (001-003). The scene establishes the novel's satirical tone through Miss Pinkerton's absurd veneration of Dr. Johnson's Dictionary and her dismissive treatment of the charity pupil Rebecca (001-009).

Rebecca's exit from Chiswick Mall is one of the novel's iconic moments: the young woman who had been "a governess for some years" flings Johnson's *Dictionary* out of the carriage window in an act of symbolic rebellion (001-038). Later in the novel, when Miss Pinkerton writes to the Misses Dobbin to recommend a governess, she refers to the academy's continued operations on Chiswick Mall (019-011). The academy and its location represent the respectable, stifling world of female education in the Regency era — a world both Amelia and Becky must navigate, in their very different ways, as they enter adult society.

## Historical Note

Chiswick Mall is a real riverside street in Chiswick, west London, lined with fine Georgian houses facing the Thames. In the Regency period it was a semi-rural retreat for the well-to-do, making it a plausible setting for a respectable ladies' academy. The real Chiswick Mall remains an attractive and desirable address today.
