# Satire and Social Critique

Thackeray's narrative voice is the defining feature of *Vanity Fair*: worldly, ironic, and unsparing. His satire targets pretension in all its forms — social, moral, and literary — and no character, however sympathetic, escapes his knowing glance. This page indexes every wiki entry tagged "satire", gathering the targets, techniques, and thematic vehicles of the novel's comic critique.
