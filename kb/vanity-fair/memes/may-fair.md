# May Fair

> (036-003) ...the respectable house in Curzon Street, May Fair, there was scarcely one of the tradesmen...

May Fair (modern Mayfair) is the heart of fashionable London in the Regency period, an aristocratic district of elegant townhouses, squares, and streets where the novel's social ambitions are most intensely played out. It is in May Fair that [[rebecca-sharp|Becky]] and [[rawdon-crawley|Rawdon Crawley]] establish their precarious household, living "on nothing a year" through a combination of charm, credit, and audacity.

## In the Novel

The Crawleys' establishment at Curzon Street, May Fair, is the centerpiece of their social strategy. Their "respectable house in Curzon Street, May Fair" (036-003) is acquired from the faithful Raggles, and it is here that they "really showed the company" (036-022) — maintaining the appearances of fashionable life without the income to sustain them. The house at No. 201, Curzon Street, "lately the residence of the Honourable Frederick Deuceace" (037-003), provides a front for their social ambitions.

May Fair is also where Becky attends chapel and cultivates her connections with powerful figures, including the [[lord-steyne|Marquess of Steyne]] (041-026). The district's proximity to power — to Gaunt House and the drawing rooms of the great — makes it essential terrain for her campaign. The social geography of May Fair, with its hierarchy of addresses and its relentless scrutiny of appearances, provides the perfect setting for Thackeray's satire of social climbing. As one character observes, "some folks in May Fair will owe him a grudge" (050-020), capturing the district's mixture of wealth, influence, and malice.

## Associated Characters

- [[rebecca-sharp|Rebecca Sharp]] — Lives in Curzon Street, May Fair, at the height of her social career
- [[rawdon-crawley|Rawdon Crawley]] — Maintains the May Fair household on credit and gambling
- [[lord-steyne|Lord Steyne]] — The aristocratic patron whose patronage makes May Fair society accessible to Becky

## Historical Note

Mayfair is one of the most prestigious districts of London, developed in the 18th century and remaining to this day synonymous with wealth and luxury. In the Regency period, it was the epicenter of aristocratic London life. Curzon Street, where the Crawleys live, was and remains a particularly desirable address.
