## Background

Mrs. Bute Crawley — the wife of the rector of Crawley-cum-Snailby — is the novel's study in clerical ambition. Masking ruthless scheming behind the language of Christian duty, she manages her husband's career and her children's prospects with a determination that rivals Rebecca Sharp's.

## Role in the Novel

Martha Bute Crawley is married to the Reverend Bute Crawley, Sir Pitt's brother and the incumbent of the family living. Her entire life is devoted to the advancement of her family's interests — her husband's career, her children's fortunes, and above all the capture of Miss Crawley's inheritance. She is Mrs. Bennet with a prayer book
