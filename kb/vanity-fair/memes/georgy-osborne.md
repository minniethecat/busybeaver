# Georgy Osborne

> Blockquote from corpus

Georgy Osborne — the young George — is the novel's representative of the next generation, the innocent heir around whom the novel's themes of inheritance and continuity crystallise.

## Background

The son of George Osborne and Amelia Sedley, young Georgy is born after his father's death at Waterloo. He is raised by his mother in poverty until his wealthy grandfather — old Mr. Osborne, who had disowned George — claims him. The transfer of Georgy from his mother to his grandfather is one of the novel's most powerful scenes

## Role in the Novel

 Amelia surrenders her son for his material benefit, an act of genuine sacrifice that the novel treats with unusual seriousness.

## Character Analysis

Georgy functions as a symbolic figure

## Historical Context

The character type represented by Georgy Osborne was a recognisable figure in Regency society. Thackeray's treatment draws on contemporary social observation and the satirical tradition.
