## Background

Rosa Dawson — the second Lady Crawley — is one of the novel's most haunting minor characters

## Role in the Novel

 a woman whose voice has been so thoroughly silenced by marriage that she barely exists as a character at all. She is the object of a transaction and a life, but never a subject.

## Character Analysis

Born the daughter of a Mudbury tradesman, Rosa Dawson brought a fortune to her marriage with Sir Pitt Crawley, who promptly absorbed it. As Lady Crawley, she exists in a state of near-complete passivity

## Historical Context

The character type represented by Lady Crawley was a recognisable figure in Regency society. Thackeray's treatment draws on contemporary social observation and the satirical tradition.
