## Overview

Soho in the early nineteenth century was a district of shifting character — part artistic quarter, part immigrant neighbourhood, part shabby-genteel refuge. In *Vanity Fair*, it appears briefly but memorably as the residence of an impecunious character who "owed money for a mile round Soho" [002-012], and as the location of "the old studio in Soho" [002-017].

## Description

Thackeray's Soho is a place of genteel poverty and artistic pretension. The character who "owed money for a mile round Soho, where he lived" [002-012] embodies the district's association with the financially embarrassed. The "old studio in Soho" [002-017] — remembered with regret — suggests the bohemian world of painters and illustrators that had long characterised the area.
