# Regent's Park

**Regent's Park** is one of London's Royal Parks, located in the northwest of central London. In [[vanity-fair]], it serves as a fashionable setting for carriage drives and winter recreation.

## Location

Regent's Park lies north of [[hyde-park]] and west of the City, connected by the New Road (now Marylebone Road). Designed by John Nash in 1811, it was still relatively new during the period of the novel.

## In the Novel

Regent's Park appears four times, always in connection with the Crawley family:

> ...driving round and round the Regent's Park in Mrs. Crawley's carriage... [052-019]

Becky Sharp, now Mrs. Rawdon Crawley, uses carriage drives in the park as a social display, part of her campaign to establish herself in London society.

> ...conducted through the New Road to Regent's Park, and then to Hyde Park... [056-006]

A sequence of fashionable drives through London parks is described, with Regent's Park as a key destination.

> ...sliding and skating in the Regent's Park and the Serpentine, when the frost set in... [056-014]

During a cold winter, the frozen waters of Regent's Park become a venue for ice skating.

> ...he drove the old man round the Regent's Park. The lady's maid and the char... [060-012]

The park continues to serve as a backdrop for domestic and social scenes.
