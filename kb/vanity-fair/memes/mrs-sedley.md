# Mrs. Sedley

Mrs. Sedley, the wife of [[mr-sedley|John Sedley]] and mother of [[jos-sedley|Jos]] and [[amelia-sedley|Amelia]], is the novel's portrait of comfortable, conventional maternity. She presides over the household at [[russell-square|Russell Square]] with a mixture of pride in her children and anxiety about appearances. Her world — the world of merchant-class domesticity — is defined by dinners, morning calls, and the conviction that her Amelia is the most perfect creature ever born.

## Role in the Novel

Mrs. Sedley's role is to embody the unthinking assumptions of her class. She measures worth by income, judges character by manners, and cannot conceive that her world might be vulnerable to catastrophe. When her husband's fortune collapses, her social identity collapses with it — she becomes querulous, nostalgic, unable to comprehend that the rules she lived by no longer apply (001-006).

Her relationship with [[rebecca-sharp|Rebecca Sharp]] is instructive: she is charmed by Rebecca's wit and repelled by her ambition, sensing (without fully articulating) that Rebecca represents a threat to the settled order of things. Her encouragement of a match between Jos and Rebecca is both maternal meddling and social blindness — she never sees what Rebecca really is.

## Relationships

- **[[amelia-sedley|Amelia Sedley]]**: Her beloved daughter, whose perfection she never doubts
- **[[jos-sedley|Jos Sedley]]**: Her son, whose vanity she indulges
- **[[mr-sedley|John Sedley]]**: Her husband, whose ruin destroys her world
- **[[rebecca-sharp|Rebecca Sharp]]**: A guest she welcomes and never understands
