# Smithfield

**Smithfield** is a historic area in the City of London, famous for its meat market and its medieval history as a site of executions and religious houses. It appears once in [[vanity-fair]].

## Location and History

Smithfield lies in the northwest corner of the City of London. Since the 12th century, it served as London's principal livestock market (the "smooth field" of its name). By Thackeray's time, the area was known for the bustling Smithfield Meat Market and its proximity to St. Bartholomew's Hospital and the Charterhouse.

## In the Novel

Smithfield is mentioned once, in a historical comparison:

> ...Convent in old days, when the Smithfield, which is contiguous to it, was a place of execution... [052-001]

This passing reference evokes Smithfield's medieval past as a place of execution — contrasting with the novel's contemporary London setting. The mention comes in a description of the Charterhouse, the school attended by Rawdon Crawley, now established on the site of a former Carthusian monastery.

Thackeray uses the dark historical association to underscore the layered history of London, where sites of medieval violence have become respectable institutions by the 19th century.
