# Park Lane

> (010-019) ...she had a snug little house in Park Lane, and, as she ate and drank a great deal too much...

Park Lane is the London address of [[miss-crawley|Miss Crawley]], the wealthy spinster whose fortune is the object of intense competition among her Crawley relatives. The "snug little house in Park Lane" (010-019) functions as the London headquarters of the Crawley family's inheritance intrigues throughout the first half of the novel.

## In the Novel

Miss Crawley's Park Lane establishment is depicted as a comfortable, well-appointed residence befitting a wealthy woman of fashion. It is described as a "large and well-appointed house in Park Lane" with a travelling chariot (014-001). The house becomes the center of gravity for the Crawley family's ambitions: members come and go, curry favor, and scheme to be remembered in the old lady's will. [[rebecca-sharp|Rebecca Sharp]] is "regularly installed in office in Park Lane" as Miss Crawley's companion and nurse (014-056), and it is in this house that Becky proves her value to the old lady through her skilled flattery and attentive care during Miss Crawley's illnesses.

The Park Lane residence is also the scene of Becky's triumph over the "shoulder-knot aristocracy" who visit Miss Crawley (014-061). When Miss Crawley declares "Send for her to Park Lane, do you hear?" (014-062), she signals Becky's acceptance into her inner circle. However, Park Lane also becomes the place of Becky's first major fall: when news of her secret marriage to [[rawdon-crawley|Rawdon Crawley]] reaches Miss Crawley, the old lady's fury shuts the Park Lane doors against the couple forever. The house thus embodies both the promise and the precariousness of social advancement by patronage.

## Historical Note

Park Lane is one of London's most prestigious addresses, running along the eastern edge of Hyde Park. In the Regency era it was lined with the townhouses of the wealthy and aristocratic. Miss Crawley's residence there signals her social standing and wealth, and Thackeray's depiction accurately reflects the street's fashionable status.
