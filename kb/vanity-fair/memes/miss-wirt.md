# Miss Wirt

## Overview

Miss Wirt is the governess at Queen's Crawley, described by Thackeray as "that raw-boned" young woman [012-005]. She serves as companion and chaperone to Miss Maria and Miss Jane Crawley, the daughters of Sir Pitt Crawley. Throughout the early chapters, Miss Wirt is a sharp and often sardonic observer of the household's romantic and financial dramas.

## Role in the Novel

Miss Wirt first appears when Becky Sharp arrives at Queen's Crawley, traveling in the family coach with the Misses Crawley [012-005]. She is immediately established as a fixture of the household, sharing in the young ladies' amusements and secrets. She and the "black-eyed" Miss Crawley share confidences from which Amelia Sedley is excluded [012-020], hinting at Miss Wirt's insider status in the Crawley social circle.

When Sir Pitt Crawley proposes to Becky Sharp, Miss Wirt is among the first to notice his agitation — she and the Crawley sisters "saw at once by his face" that something was amiss [013-032]. She also shares in the household's financial anxieties, whispering about falling funds [013-039].

Miss Wirt's observations often carry a satirical edge. She notes when George Osborne is "rather too openly" attentive [023-009] and laughs with Maria at the other side of the door during flirtatious encounters [023-013]. After the Hulker ball, she and the Crawley sisters agree on how much better Maria could have done than young Osborne [021-004], revealing her loyalty to the Crawley family perspective.

In her professional capacity, Miss Wirt enters Mr. Osborne's study four times a year to collect the young ladies' quarterly allowance [024-030], a reminder of the financial arrangements that underpin the governess's position in the household.
