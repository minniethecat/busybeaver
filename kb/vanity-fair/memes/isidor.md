# Isidor

## Overview

Isidor is Jos Sedley's Belgian valet, who appears during the Brussels chapters of *Vanity Fair* surrounding the Waterloo campaign. Thackeray portrays him as a comic figure whose alarm during the French advance mirrors — and sometimes exceeds — his master Jos's own cowardice. His repeated cries of alarm and his practical devotion to Jos's comforts make him one of the more memorable minor servants in the novel.

## Role in the Novel

Isidor first appears in Brussels, where Jos Sedley, having fled England after his humiliation at Vauxhall, has set himself up in considerable style. Isidor is summoned to fetch beer for his master [028-006] and is pressed into service as a companion in Jos's anxious moments. When Jos asks whether there is cause for fear, Isidor's responses are not entirely reassuring [028-007].

During the crisis of the Waterloo campaign, Isidor becomes increasingly agitated. He bursts in with the terrified cry "C'est le feu!" [031-067] and reports that the French will be in Brussels within half an hour [032-001]. Yet even amid the panic, Isidor performs his duties — holding Jos's cash-box with "bodily fingers" [031-008] and sternly managing travel documents [031-018]. His loyalty to Jos is pragmatic and serves Thackeray's satirical portrait of Jos Sedley's self-indulgent timidity.

Isidor also has his own moments of indignation — he is "furious" when Jos puts on his shako to flee [031-019], perhaps affronted that his master would play the soldier while abandoning his post.
