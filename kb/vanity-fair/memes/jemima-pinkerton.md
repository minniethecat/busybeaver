## Background

Miss Jemima Pinkerton — the timid younger sister of the formidable Miss Barbara Pinkerton — appears only in the novel's opening chapter, but her brief presence establishes the novel's moral register

## Role in the Novel

 she is the one person in the academy who genuinely cares for Amelia, and she is punished for it.

## Character Analysis

Jemima is the younger sister and assistant of Miss Pinkerton, the headmistress of the academy on Chiswick Mall. Her function is to manage the domestic arrangements — 'all the bills, and the washing, and the mending, and the puddings' (001-025) — while her sister presides over the ceremonial.

## Historical Context

The character type represented by Jemima Pinkerton was a recognisable figure in Regency society. Thackeray's treatment draws on contemporary social observation and the satirical tradition.
