# Ishmael

## Overview

Ishmael is the narrator and protagonist of Herman Melville's *Moby-Dick*, introduced in one of literature's most famous opening lines: "Call me Ishmael" (001-001). A former schoolmaster from Manhattan, he goes to sea whenever he feels a "damp, drizzly November in my soul" — to cure what he calls the "hypos," a melancholic depression that he treats not with suicide but with the discipline and adventure of whaling. As the sole survivor of the Pequod's destruction, he lives to tell the tale, rescued by the [[Rachel]] after floating on Queequeg's coffin for a day and a night.

## Role in the Narrative

Ishmael serves a triple function in the novel. First, he is the **narrator** — the retrospective storyteller whose voice ranges from colloquial humor to philosophical meditation. Second, he is a **participant** in the voyage, forming a deep friendship with [[Queequeg]], observing the crew, and surviving through chance and the buoyancy of a coffin. Third, he is the novel's **philosophical consciousness**, constantly interpreting and reflecting on what he sees — whales, men, the sea, fate, and the "whiteness of the whale."

His narrative arc moves from greenhorn (arriving in [[New Bedford]] knowing nothing of whaling) to experienced sailor, and from a man tempted by nihilism to one who finds meaning in human connection — epitomized by his bond with Queequeg and the famous "squeeze of the hand" episode (094-001).

## Key Appearances

- [[Ch. 1: Loomings]](001-001) — "Call me Ishmael": the narrator explains his motives for going to sea; the "hypos" cured by salt water and shipboard discipline
- [[Ch. 2–3: The Spouter-Inn]](002-003) — Ishmael arrives in New Bedford, confronts the ominous painting and the landlord's warnings, and meets [[Queequeg]]
- [[Ch. 7: The Chapel]](007-010) — In the Whaleman's Chapel, Ishmael contemplates the memorial tablets and accepts that the same fate may be his
- [[Ch. 10: A Bosom Friend]](010-009) — Ishmael and Queequeg become "bosom friends" in bed at the Spouter-Inn; a pagan and a Christian finding common humanity
- [[Ch. 16: The Ship]](016-001) — Ishmael signs aboard the Pequod, meeting the Quaker owners [[Peleg]] and [[Bildad]]
- [[Ch. 41: Moby Dick]](041-001) — "I, Ishmael, was one of that crew; my shouts had gone up with the rest": Ishmael reflects on the madness of the chase and the power of Ahab's will
- [[Ch. 42: The Whiteness of the Whale]](042-025) — Ishmael's meditation on whiteness and its terror; the chapter that defines the novel's metaphysics
- [[Ch. 72: The Monkey-Rope]](072-001) — Ishmael tied to Queequeg by the monkey-rope: a metaphor for human interdependence
- [[Ch. 87: The Grand Armada]](087-001) — Ishmael witnesses the great whale herd, including the sublime vision of nursing mothers in the interior lake
- [[Ch. 94: A Squeeze of the Hand]](094-001) — Ishmael squeezes spermaceti with his shipmates and experiences a mystical ecstasy of human brotherhood
- [[Ch. 102: A Bower in the Arsacides]](102-002) — Ishmael explains how he came to know whale anatomy, revealing his post-voyage researches
- [[Epilogue]](EPI-001) — "And I only am escaped alone to tell thee": Ishmael, buoyed by Queequeg's coffin, is rescued by the [[Rachel]]

## Character Traits

Ishmael's most striking trait is his **narrative consciousness** — an all-encompassing curiosity that turns every experience, whether a whale-sighting or a shipmate's death, into material for philosophical reflection. He is at once inside the story and outside it, a survivor looking back with the wisdom of the saved.

His **melancholic disposition** — the "hypos" — is not weakness but the engine of his quest. "Whenever I find myself growing grim about the mouth; whenever it is a damp, drizzly November in my soul," he goes to sea (001-001). The ocean and the discipline of the ship cure him more effectively than any philosophy.

His **capacity for friendship** is his salvation. His bond with [[Queequeg]] — a pagan "cannibal" whom Ishmael comes to love as a "bosom friend" — embodies the novel's radical democratic vision. It is Queequeg's coffin that saves Ishmael's life in the end, a symbol of how human connection transcends death.

Ishmael is also a **comic narrator**, capable of parody, self-deprecation, and absurdist humor. His description of the Spouter-Inn, the Try Pots, and his own early awkwardness with Queequeg are among the novel's funniest passages.

## Relationships

- **[[Queequeg]]**: Ishmael's "bosom friend," harpooneer, and ultimately savior. Their friendship, which begins in terror and ends in love, is the emotional heart of the novel. Queequeg's coffin becomes Ishmael's life-buoy.
- **[[Ahab]]**: Ishmael observes Ahab with a mixture of fascination and horror. He is drawn into Ahab's quest — "my shouts had gone up with the rest" (041-001) — but his narrative distance allows him to judge what he participated in.
- **[[Pip]]**: Like Ishmael, Pip is a figure of isolation and madness. Ishmael's tenderness toward Pip ("this way comes Pip — poor boy!") reflects his recognition of a kindred soul.
- **[[Starbuck]]**: Ishmael respects Starbuck's prudence and moral courage, even as he records Starbuck's ultimate failure to stop Ahab.
- **The Crew**: Ishmael's democratic sensibility embraces the entire crew — from [[Tashtego]] and [[Daggoo]] to [[Fleece]] the cook — as fellow humans in the "universal thump."

## Notable Quotes

> "Call me Ishmael. Some years ago — never mind how long precisely — having little or no money in my purse, and nothing particular to interest me on shore, I thought I would sail about a little and see the watery part of the world." (001-001)

> "Yes, as every one knows, meditation and water are wedded for ever." (001-001)

> "Heaven have mercy on us all — Presbyterians and Pagans alike — for we are all somehow dreadfully cracked about the head, and sadly need mending." (017-003)

> "I, Ishmael, was one of that crew; my shouts had gone up with the rest; my oath had been welded with theirs; and stronger I shouted, and more did I hammer and clinch my oath, because of the dread in my soul." (041-001)

> "I squeezed that sperm till a strange sort of insanity came over me; and I found myself unwittingly squeezing my co-laborers' hands in it, mistaking their hands for the gentle globules. Such an abounding, affectionate, friendly, loving feeling did this avocation beget." (094-001)

> "And I only am escaped alone to tell thee." (EPI-001)
