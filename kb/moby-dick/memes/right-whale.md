# Right Whale

## Overview

The Right Whale (*Balaena mysticetus*) is one of the two principal whales of *Moby-Dick*, standing in systematic contrast to the [[sperm-whale|Sperm Whale]].

Ishmael devotes Book I, Chapter II of his cetological system to the Right Whale, describing it as "the venerable Leviathan" (032-022).

Where the Sperm Whale embodies power and mystery, the Right Whale represents commercial utility and placid bulk. Together they form the two poles of Melville's whale universe.

The Right Whale is so named because it was the "right" whale to hunt: slow-moving, rich in baleen and oil, and — crucially — it floats when killed, unlike the Sperm Whale which sinks.

## In Cetology (Chapter 32)

Ishmael's system places the Right Whale as the second of the great whales, after the Sperm Whale.

He describes it as "the True Whale" in English usage — "the Greenland or Right Whale" (032-021).

The Right Whale is distinguished by its baleen (whalebone) rather than teeth, its massive head, and its preference for colder waters.

> "In one respect this is the most venerable of the Leviathans, being the one first regularly hunted by man." (032-022)

Ishmael notes that the Right Whale was the foundation of the Greenland fishery — the older, more established branch of the whaling industry.

## Physical Description

The Right Whale is a baleen whale of immense bulk. Ishmael acknowledges the difficulty of describing it:

> "Of the Right Whale, the best outline pictures are in Scoresby." (056-003)

Unlike the Sperm Whale's "perpendicular and fluted" brow, the Right Whale's head is arched and massive — "a large running Right Whale, that rolls his black weedy bulk in an agony of fright" (056-005).

Its mouth contains hundreds of plates of baleen — "whalebone" — that hang from the upper jaw like a curtain, used to strain tiny crustaceans from the water.

The Right Whale lacks the Sperm Whale's formidable jaw and teeth. It is a filter-feeder, a passive consumer of the sea's smallest creatures.

## Feeding and Habitat

The Right Whale feeds on "a certain yellow substance" — brit, the tiny crustaceans that form its diet:

> "Upon which the Right Whale largely feeds. For leagues and leagues it undulated round us, so that we seemed to be sailing through boundless fields of ripe and golden wheat." (058-001)

This image of pastoral abundance — the whale grazing on submarine fields — contrasts sharply with the Sperm Whale's violent predation on giant squid.

Right Whales favor polar and temperate waters. Ishmael notes they are found "in those latitudes, where the Right Whale is often chased" (058-004).

## Commercial Value

The Right Whale was the economic bedrock of the older whale fishery.

Its baleen — "whalebone" — was the plastic of the age: used for corset stays, umbrella ribs, and "other like skrimshander articles" (057-002).

Its oil was of a different quality from sperm oil — thicker, less refined, but abundant. Melville notes the whale's historical importance:

> "Centuries ago the tongue of the Right Whale was esteemed a great delicacy." (065-002)

The Right Whale's commercial qualities made it the "right" whale to hunt: it was valuable, slow, and — critically — it floated after death, allowing whalemen to tow it to the ship.

## Contrast with the Sperm Whale

Melville systematically contrasts the Right Whale with the Sperm Whale:

| Attribute | Sperm Whale | Right Whale |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Head | Toothed, formidable jaw | Baleen, passive filter |
| Oil | Spermaceti (superior) | Train oil (common) |
| Buoyancy | Sinks when killed | Floats when killed |
| Temperament | Aggressive, fights back | Docile, flees |
| Habitat | Tropical, equatorial | Polar, temperate |
| Symbolism | Mystery, power, terror | Utility, antiquity, passivity |

The contrast is made explicit in Chapter 74, where Ishmael compares the two heads side by side:

> "In the Sperm Whale's head there is a certain mathematical symmetry which the Right Whale's sadly lacks." (074-003)

## In the Narrative

Right Whales appear at several key moments in the Pequod's voyage:

On the second day out from the Cape of Good Hope, "numbers of Right Whales were seen" (058-002). They pass unmolested, "secure from attack" because the Pequod seeks Sperm Whales.

In Chapter 73, the Pequod kills a Right Whale — an unusual event for a Sperm Whale ship. Stubb and Flask debate the relative merits of the two species.

Chapter 75 gives Ishmael a chance to "have a good long look at the Right Whale's head" (075-001), completing his comparative anatomy of the two great whales.

## Symbolic Significance

The Right Whale represents a different mode of engagement with the natural world.

Where the Sperm Whale is Ahab's adversary — aggressive, dangerous, symbolic of cosmic resistance — the Right Whale is passive, commercial, almost agricultural.

It is the whale of the old fishery, before the Sperm Whale drew whalemen into the remote Pacific. It represents a less glamorous but more sustainable relationship with the sea.

In the novel's symbolic economy, the Right Whale is earth to the Sperm Whale's fire — the grounding reality against which Ahab's metaphysical quest is measured.
