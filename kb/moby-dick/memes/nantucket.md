# Nantucket

## Overview

Nantucket is the small island off the coast of Massachusetts that serves as the spiritual and economic center of American whaling.

It is from Nantucket that the [[pequod|Pequod]] departs on its fateful voyage. It is the home of [[ahab|Captain Ahab]], [[starbuck|Starbuck]], and much of the crew.

Ishmael describes it as "a mere hillock, and elbow of sand; all beach, without a background" (014-002).

Despite its small size, Melville presents Nantucket as a place of mythic significance. Its whalemen claim dominion over the entire [[pacific|Pacific Ocean]].

## Geography

Nantucket sits about thirty miles south of Cape Cod.

The island is described as barren and sandy, lashed by Atlantic storms. Melville emphasizes its precariousness as a human settlement.

Its location made it ideal for whaling. From Nantucket, ships could reach the Atlantic whaling grounds quickly.

The island's harbor, though not deep, sheltered the whaling fleet for decades.

## The Whaling Capital

At the time of the novel (c. 1840s), Nantucket was the world center of sperm-whale fishing.

Melville devotes Chapter 14 to celebrating Nantucket's whaling history. He describes the island's global reach: "two thirds of this terraqueous globe are the Nantucketer's" (014-005).

Nantucket men dominated the global whaling industry. Boys grew up with "the odor of whale-oil" in their blood.

The island's women managed affairs during the men's long absences at sea.

## Key Locations

The [[pequod|Pequod]] docks at Nantucket's wharves before departure.

The Spouter-Inn, where [[ishmael|Ishmael]] meets [[queequeg|Queequeg]], is in [[new-bedford|New Bedford]].

The Try Pots inn on Nantucket provides their last lodging before sailing.

The Seamen's Bethel, with Father Mapple's pulpit shaped like a ship's prow, overlooks the harbor.

## Association with the Pequod

The Pequod is owned by Nantucket Quakers: Captain Peleg and Captain Bildad.

Both are retired whalemen who now manage ships from shore. Their parsimony and piety embody certain Nantucket types.

Ahab is himself a Nantucket man, though he has been "forty years on the pitiless sea" and is barely known ashore.

The ship departs Nantucket on Christmas Day, sailing into the Atlantic to begin the voyage.

## Cultural Significance

Nantucket in Melville's rendering is more than a place. It is a symbol of human ambition projected onto the vast ocean.

Its whalemen are portrayed as a kind of chosen people. They have "dived the deepest" and "sailed the furthest."

At the same time, the island's prosperity is built on the bloody labor of whaling. Melville does not let the reader forget this.
