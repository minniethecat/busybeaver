# Pequod

## Overview

The Pequod is the [[nantucket|Nantucket]] whaleship at the center of *Moby-Dick*.

She is commanded by [[ahab|Captain Ahab]], with [[starbuck|Starbuck]] as first mate. Her crew includes [[queequeg|Queequeg]], [[ishmael|Ishmael]], and thirty other souls.

Melville describes her as "a ship of the old school, rather small if anything; with an old-fashioned claw-footed look about her" (016-004).

Named after a Native American tribe exterminated by the Puritans, the Pequod carries her doom in her name.

## The Ship

The Pequod is no sleek clipper. She is a working whaleship, weathered by years of the fishery.

Her fittings show "the finished work of a certain ancient" craft. Her whale-bone ornaments and dark wood give her a ceremonial, almost funereal aspect.

Ishmael first sees her at the Nantucket wharf. He notes that "she was apparelled like any barbaric Ethiopian emperor, his neck heavy with pendants of polished ivory" (016-004).

Her three masts rise above the whale-boats hung at her sides. Her try-works, where blubber is rendered into oil, sit amidships.

## The Crew

The Pequod's crew is a microcosm of the world.

The officers are American: [[ahab|Ahab]], [[starbuck|Starbuck]], Stubb, and Flask.

The harpooneers are from the margins of empire: [[queequeg|Queequeg]] (Polynesian), Tashtego (Native American), [[daggoo|Daggoo]] (African).

[[fedallah|Fedallah]] and his phantom crew, smuggled aboard by Ahab, add an occult, non-Christian presence.

The crew speaks a dozen languages. They are united only by Ahab's will.

## The Journey

The Pequod departs Nantucket on Christmas Day.

Her voyage takes her across the Atlantic, around the Cape of Good Hope, through the Indian Ocean, and into the [[pacific|Pacific]].

Along the way, she meets other whalers: the Goney, [[town-ho|Town-Ho]], [[jeroboam|Jeroboam]], [[samuel-enderby|Samuel Enderby]], [[bachelor|Bachelor]], [[rachel|Rachel]], and [[delight|Delight]].

Each gam encounter adds to the ominous atmosphere. The Pequod sails toward an appointment that everyone on board seems to know will not end well.

## Fate

On the third day of the chase, Moby Dick rams the Pequod.

The whale strikes the ship. Moby Dick rams the Pequod's starboard bow.

"The ship sank," Melville writes. Concentric circles seize the lone boat. The Pequod disappears beneath the Pacific.

The Pequod sinks. The whirlpool drags everything down with her.

Only [[ishmael|Ishmael]] survives, buoyed by [[queequeg|Queequeg]]'s coffin. The sky-hawk, nailed to the mast by Tashtego, goes down with the ship.

## Symbolic Significance

The Pequod is more than a ship. She is a world in miniature.

Her name links her to genocide. Her crew spans all races and nations. Her captain wages war on the cosmos.

Critics have read the Pequod as an image of America itself: diverse, driven, beautiful, and doomed by its own monomania.

Her destruction is the novel's final judgment on the quest for mastery over nature.
