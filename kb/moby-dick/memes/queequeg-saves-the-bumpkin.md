# Queequeg Saves the Bumpkin

## Overview

During the passage from New Bedford to Nantucket aboard the packet schooner Moss, [[queequeg|Queequeg]] performs an act of heroism that transforms [[ishmael|Ishmael]]'s wary fascination into absolute loyalty. A greenhorn bumpkin, one of several lubberly passengers who gawk at the companionship between a white man and a tattooed cannibal, is caught mimicking Queequeg behind his back.

Queequeg's response — hurling the young man high into the air "by an almost miraculous dexterity and strength" (013-007) — looks at first like violence. But when the main-sail sheet parts, the boom sweeps the deck, and the bumpkin is flung overboard into freezing water, Queequeg is the only man aboard capable of saving him. The harpooneer secures the loose boom, dives into the sea, and after a tense underwater search, drags the drowning man back to the surface.

The crew's verdict is unanimous: "All hands voted Queequeg a noble trump" (013-014). Ishmael records: "From that hour I clove to Queequeg like a barnacle" (013-014).

## Chronology

- (013-007) — Aboard the Moss, passengers stare at Ishmael and Queequeg with "jeering glances." Among them are "boobies and bumpkins" of "intense greenness." One young bumpkin mimics Queequeg behind his back.
- (013-007) — Queequeg catches the mimicry. Ishmael thinks "the bumpkin's hour of doom was come." Dropping his harpoon, Queequeg catches the young man, and "by an almost miraculous dexterity and strength, sent him high up bodily into the air; then slightly tapping his stern in mid-somerset, the fellow landed with bursting lungs upon his feet."
- (013-008) — The bumpkin runs to the captain: "Capting! Capting! here's the devil."
- (013-009) — The captain confronts Queequeg: "Don't you know you might have killed that chap?"
- (013-010) — Queequeg, puzzled, asks Ishmael what the captain said. Ishmael translates: "that you came near kill-e that man there."
- (013-010) — Queequeg's immortal reply: "ah! him bevy small-e fish-e; Queequeg no kill-e so small-e fish-e; Queequeg kill-e big whale!"
- (013-011) — The captain threatens Queequeg: "I'll kill-e *you*, you cannibal, if you try any more of your tricks aboard here."
- (013-012) — The main-sail sheet parts. The boom flies from side to side, "completely sweeping the entire after part of the deck." The bumpkin Queequeg had handled is swept overboard. Panic ensues; no one dares approach the swinging boom.
- (013-013) — Queequeg drops to his knees, crawls under the boom's path, secures a rope to the bulwarks, and lassos the boom, trapping it. The schooner is brought into the wind.
- (013-014) — Stripped to the waist, Queequeg darts from the side "with a long living arc of a leap." He swims for three minutes searching. The bumpkin has already gone under. Queequeg shoots perpendicularly from the water to scan, then dives deep and disappears. He resurfaces "dragging a lifeless form." The boat picks them up. "The poor bumpkin was restored."
- (013-014) — "All hands voted Queequeg a noble trump; the captain begged his pardon." Ishmael declares: "From that hour I clove to Queequeg like a barnacle; yea, till poor Queequeg took his last long dive."
- (013-015) — Queequeg, indifferent to praise, asks only for fresh water to wash off the brine, lights his pipe, and leans against the bulwarks. His closing reflection: "It's a mutual, joint-stock world, in all meridians. We cannibals must help these Christians."

## Key Participants

- **[[queequeg|Queequeg]]** — the harpooneer whose physical prowess and moral instinct transform a petty confrontation into an act of Christ-like rescue
- **[[ishmael|Ishmael]]** — the narrator, whose bond with Queequeg is sealed by witnessing this event
- **the bumpkin** — unnamed greenhorn, a land-lubber whose mockery precipitates the crisis and whose rescue demonstrates the novel's reversals of civilized and savage
- **the captain** — unnamed master of the Moss, initially hostile, ultimately contrite

## Narrative Significance

The bumpkin rescue is the novel's first major reversal of the civilized/savage binary. The bumpkin, a product of New England farmland, mocks Queequeg as a racialized other; Queequeg's airborne toss confirms the savage stereotype. But when the boom accident creates real danger, it is the "cannibal" — not the captain, not any Christian sailor — who risks his life in freezing water to save a man who had just insulted him.

Queequeg's speeches in this scene — "Queequeg no kill-e so small-e fish-e; Queequeg kill-e big whale!" and "We cannibals must help these Christians" — are among the novel's most quoted lines. The first establishes Queequeg's scale of violence as heroic rather than petty; the second inverts the entire missionary project of the nineteenth century, positioning the cannibal as the Good Samaritan.

Ishmael's response — "From that hour I clove to Queequeg like a barnacle" — marks the moment when intellectual tolerance becomes personal devotion. This allegiance will sustain Ishmael through every subsequent terror aboard the Pequod.

## Aftermath / Consequences

The rescue secures Queequeg's reputation among the sailors and deepens Ishmael's attachment. The captain's apology signals a practical respect that transcends racial prejudice. Queequeg's philosophical indifference to praise — washing off the brine, lighting his pipe — establishes a pattern of quiet capability that will recur throughout the novel: he is always the most competent man aboard, and the least concerned with recognition.

The "mutual, joint-stock world" speech becomes a touchstone for the novel's ethic of radical interdependence, anticipating the multi-ethnic crew of the Pequod and the "Anacharsis Clootz deputation" of Chapter 27.
