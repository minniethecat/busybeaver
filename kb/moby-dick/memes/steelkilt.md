# Steelkilt

## Overview

Steelkilt is a seaman aboard the Town-Ho whose mutiny against the brutal mate Radney forms the central drama of [[Ch. 54: The Town-Ho's Story]]. A "Lakeman and desperado from Buffalo" (054-007), he is described as "a tall and noble animal with a head like a Roman, and a flowing golden beard" — a man of such natural grandeur that, Melville remarks, he "had made Steelkilt Charlemagne, had he been born son to Charlemagne's father" (054-012). His story is told by Ishmael as a narrative within the narrative, relayed at the Golden Inn in Lima to a captivated audience of Spanish gentlemen.

## Role in the Narrative

Steelkilt's story functions as a miniature tragedy of pride within the larger epic of [[Moby Dick]]. The Town-Ho, like the [[Pequod]], is a Nantucket whaler, and the conflict between Steelkilt and Radney mirrors the power dynamics that would destroy [[Ahab]]'s ship — the tension between individual dignity and tyrannical authority.

The chain of events begins when the Town-Ho develops a leak. While the crew works the pumps, the mate Radney — "ugly as a mule; yet as hardy, as stubborn, as malicious" (054-012) — takes a personal dislike to Steelkilt. In an exhausted state after pumping, Steelkilt is ordered by Radney to perform the degrading task of sweeping the deck — work normally assigned to ship's boys. When Steelkilt refuses, Radney threatens him with a cooper's club hammer, and after repeated provocations, touches Steelkilt's cheek with it. In an instant, Steelkilt strikes Radney so hard that "the lower jaw of the mate was stove in his head" (054-023).

What follows is a standoff: Steelkilt and his allies barricade themselves in the forecastle, threatening to sink the ship rather than submit to flogging. After days of siege, most of the mutineers surrender, but Steelkilt holds out until the Town-Ho reaches port. In a final, fateful twist, Radney recovers from his injury and later, during a whale hunt, is killed by [[Moby Dick]] himself — dragged from his boat by the white whale's line. Steelkilt, watching from below decks, sees in Radney's death a "judgment of God" upon his persecutor.

## Character Traits

**Noble pride** is Steelkilt's defining trait. Melville describes him as possessing "a brain, and a heart, and a soul in him, gentlemen, which had made Steelkilt Charlemagne, had he been born son to Charlemagne's father" (054-012). His refusal to submit to Radney's degradation is not mere stubbornness but a principled assertion of dignity — he will not be treated as a boy or a slave.

His **forbearance** before the provocation is emphasized. When Radney first orders him to sweep, Steelkilt "instinctively saw... the stacks of powder-casks heaped up in him and the slow-match silently burning along towards them" (054-019) and chooses restraint. He warns Radney repeatedly before striking: "Take that hammer away, or look to yourself" (054-023). The violence, when it comes, is not impulsive but the predictable result of pushed forbearance.

His **strategic mind** is evident in the mutiny that follows. He organizes the barricade, calculates the captain's fear of outright mutiny if he is killed, and negotiates for the terms of surrender — demanding only that his men not be flogged. He is, in his own way, a natural leader.

## Relationships

- **Radney**: The Vineyarder mate of the Town-Ho. Their antagonism is not personal in origin but systemic — the natural hostility between a man of inferior character in a position of authority and a man of superior character forced to submit.
- **The Canallers**: Steelkilt's two comrades who rush to his defense when the officers try to seize him after he strikes Radney (054-024).
- **[[Moby Dick]]**: The white whale becomes, in the Town-Ho's story, an instrument of cosmic justice — killing Radney in a manner that vindicates Steelkilt's sense that his enemy was "branded for the slaughter by the gods" (054-023).

> "Steelkilt was a tall and noble animal with a head like a Roman, and a flowing golden beard like the tasseled housings of your last viceroy's snorting charger; and a brain, and a heart, and a soul in him, gentlemen, which had made Steelkilt Charlemagne, had he been born son to Charlemagne's father." (054-012)

> "But Radney, the mate, was ugly as a mule; yet as hardy, as stubborn, as malicious. He did not love Steelkilt, and Steelkilt knew it." (054-012)

> "'Mr. Radney, I will not obey you. Take that hammer away, or look to yourself.'" (054-023)

> "Stabbing him in the eye with the unflinching poniard of his glance, Steelkilt, clenching his right hand behind him and creepingly drawing it back, told his persecutor that if the hammer but grazed his cheek he (Steelkilt) would murder him." (054-023)

> "Immediately the hammer touched the cheek; the next instant the lower jaw of the mate was stove in his head; he fell on the hatch spouting blood like a whale." (054-023)

> "'Sink the ship?' cried Steelkilt. 'Aye, let her sink. Not a man of us turns to, unless you swear not to raise a rope-yarn against us.'" (054-040)
