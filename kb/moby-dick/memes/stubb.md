# Stubb

## Overview

Stubb is the second mate of the [[Pequod]], a native of Cape Cod whose defining characteristic is an almost impious good humor that persists through the most mortal perils. Introduced in [[Ch. 27: Knights and Squires]](027-001), he is described as "a happy-go-lucky; neither craven nor valiant; taking perils as they came with an indifferent air." He is inseparable from his short black pipe, which Melville suggests may be the source of his peculiar disposition — a "disinfecting agent" against the world's miseries (027-003). Together with [[Starbuck]] and [[Flask]], he commands one of the Pequod's three whale-boats, and his harpooneer is [[Tashtego]], the Gay Head Indian.

## Role in the Narrative

Stubb functions as the comic relief of the Pequod, but his humor is philosophically significant. Where [[Starbuck]] represents moral gravity and prudence, and [[Flask]] embodies thoughtless pugnacity, Stubb occupies a middle ground of cheerful fatalism. He takes the world as it comes, exemplified by his calm pipe-smoking even in the death-lock of a whale hunt (027-001). His most extended narrative moments are his killing of a sperm whale in [[Ch. 61: Stubb Kills a Whale]] and his subsequent "supper" scene in [[Ch. 64: Stubb's Supper]], where he enlists the old black cook [[Fleece]] to preach a comically futile sermon to the sharks devouring the whale carcass.

During [[Ch. 36: The Quarter-Deck]], Stubb is among the three mates who witness [[Ahab]]'s revelation of his hunt for [[Moby Dick]]. His whispered aside — "the chick that's in him pecks the shell" (036-005) — shows his shrewd perception beneath the jovial surface. He is the first to laugh when Ahab demands the crew's allegiance, and Ahab's magnetic intensity ultimately overwhelms his resistance as it does the others (036-047). He perishes with the Pequod in [[Ch. 135: The Chase — Third Day]].

## Character Traits

**Cheerful fatalism** is Stubb's defining trait. Melville describes him presiding "over his whale-boat as if the most deadly encounter were but a dinner, and his crew all invited guests" (027-001). He is neither a coward nor a hero in the conventional sense; he simply refuses to take any situation, however dire, with full seriousness.

His **inveterate pipe-smoking** is inseparable from his character — "like his nose, his short, black little pipe was one of the regular features of his face" (027-002). Melville speculates that the constant tobacco smoke acts as a "disinfecting agent" against the "nameless miseries" of mortality (027-003), making the pipe not merely a habit but a philosophical instrument.

His **shrewd perception** lurks beneath the jovial exterior. During the Quarter-Deck scene, he is the first to detect the dangerous intensity in Ahab, whispering to Flask that "the chick that's in him pecks the shell" (036-005). Yet this perception does not lead to action; Stubb ultimately yields to Ahab's will, his humor proving no match for monomania.

His **epicurean appetite** for whale flesh — "a high liver" who is "somewhat intemperately fond of the whale as a flavorish thing to his palate" (064-005) — connects him to the elemental, carnivorous realities of the whaling industry. His supper scene with Fleece is at once comic and grotesque, a meditation on predation and death conducted through slapstick.

## Relationships

- **[[Ahab]]**: Stubb respects Ahab's authority but perceives his madness early. Their relationship is one of wary subordination; Stubb laughs when Ahab demands it but never challenges him as [[Starbuck]] does.
- **[[Starbuck]]**: Stubb's superior in rank and gravity. Where Starbuck agonizes over moral choices, Stubb floats past them with a shrug.
- **[[Flask]]**: The third mate; Stubb is "but a peg higher than Flask" (034-006) and the two are often paired in the narrative, particularly during the Quarter-Deck scene.
- **[[Tashtego]]**: Stubb's harpooneer, the Gay Head Indian whom Stubb selected as his "squire" (027-008). They work together in the whale hunt of Chapter 61.
- **[[Fleece]]**: The Pequod's old black cook, whom Stubb comically commands to deliver a sermon to the sharks in Chapter 64.
