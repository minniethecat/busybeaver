# Tashtego

## Overview

Tashtego is the harpooneer for [[Stubb]]'s whale-boat aboard the [[Pequod]], introduced in [[Ch. 27: Knights and Squires]](027-008) as "an unmixed Indian from Gay Head, the most westerly promontory of Martha's Vineyard." A member of the dwindling community of Gay Head Indians who had long supplied Nantucket with its most daring harpooneers, Tashtego carries the legacy of his people's hunting traditions — once directed at the "great New England moose" of the aboriginal forests, now turned to the great whales of the sea. Melville frames this transition in epic terms: "the unerring harpoon of the son fitly replacing the infallible arrow of the sires" (027-008).

Tashtego is most famous for the bizarre accident in [[Ch. 78: Cistern and Buckets]], where he falls headfirst into the spermaceti-filled "Tun" of a whale's head — a near-fatal mishap that requires [[Queequeg]]'s daring underwater rescue. This scene, at once comic and grotesque, is one of the novel's most vivid set-pieces.

## Role in the Narrative

Tashtego represents the global, multi-racial composition of the Pequod's crew and embodies the theme of indigenous hunting traditions adapted to the industrial-scale whaling of the nineteenth century. Alongside [[Queequeg]] (the Polynesian) and [[Daggoo]] (the African), he forms the trio of "pagan" harpooneers whose skill is indispensable to the ship's mission, yet whose personhood is subordinated to the command structure of white officers.

In the whale hunt of [[Ch. 61: Stubb Kills a Whale]](061-015), Tashtego is the one who hurls the harpoon — "Stand up, Tashtego!—give it to him!" — the pivotal moment in the kill. His physical agility is emphasized throughout: in Chapter 78 he is "nimble as a cat," mounting the rigging and descending onto a whale's severed head to bale spermaceti. The accident that follows — his plunge into the "great Tun of Heidelburgh" (078-002) — nearly kills him, but [[Queequeg]] dives to his rescue, delivering him as if by "midwifery" from the whale's head (078-012).

Tashtego also plays a key role in the transmission of the [[Town-Ho]]'s secret story. In [[Ch. 54: The Town-Ho's Story]](054-003), it is revealed that Tashtego learned the secret part of the tragedy from confederate seamen under "Romish injunctions of secrecy," but then "rambled in his sleep, and revealed so much of it in that way, that when he was wakened he could not well withhold the rest" — a comic moment that underscores the porousness of secrets aboard the Pequod.

## Character Traits

**Ancestral hunting prowess** defines Tashtego. Melville describes him as "an inheritor of the unvitiated blood of those proud warrior hunters" who once pursued moose with bows (027-008). His harpooning skill is presented as a direct inheritance — the tool has changed but the predatory instinct is unchanged.

His **physical agility** is remarkable. In Chapter 78 he moves "nimble as a cat" across the rigging, dropping "hand-over-hand" onto the whale's head with the ease of long practice (078-001). This bodily mastery is set against the vulnerability revealed when he plunges into the spermaceti — a reminder that even the most skilled hunter is at the mercy of the sea's grotesque accidents.

His **ethnic "purity"** is emphasized in Melville's racial taxonomy. He is "an unmixed Indian," "an inheritor of the unvitiated blood" (027-008) — language that both romanticizes and exoticizes him. Melville's portrait participates in the nineteenth-century trope of the "noble savage" while also granting Tashtego genuine dignity and skill.

## Relationships

- **[[Stubb]]**: Tashtego's direct superior; Stubb selected him as his harpooneer. The two work closely together in the whale hunts, most notably in Chapter 61 where Stubb calls out "Start her, Tash, my boy."
- **[[Queequeg]]**: The Polynesian harpooneer who saves Tashtego's life in Chapter 78, diving underwater and "clutching the long hair of the Indian" to pull him to safety (078-010). Their bond is one of mutual respect between the Pequod's "savage" harpooneers.
- **[[Daggoo]]**: The African harpooneer for Flask's boat; Daggoo is the first to cry "Man overboard!" when Tashtego falls into the whale's head (078-003).
- **[[Ahab]]**: Tashtego, like the other harpooneers, serves Ahab's obsessive quest without question.
