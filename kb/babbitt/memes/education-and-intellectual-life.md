# Education and Intellectual Life

Education in Zenith is respectable, practical, and safely ornamental. The State University produces solid engineers and booster-loyal graduates; the public schools teach Americanism alongside arithmetic. True intellectual life — the kind that asks uncomfortable questions — is as foreign to Zenith as a Hindu temple. This page aggregates the institutions, people, and ideas that constitute Zenith's carefully domesticated world of learning.

## About This Index

Lewis's treatment of education is among the novel's sharpest satirical knives. The businessman's "education" consists of a college degree that certified his conformity, a Chum Frink poem that passed for culture, and a steady diet of *Advocate-Times* editorials that confirmed what he already believed. The entities indexed here — universities, schools, intellectuals real and self-appointed — map the boundaries of what Zenith considers "thinking," and what it carefully excludes from the category.
