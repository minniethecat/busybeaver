# Hotel Thornleigh

The "largest and most dynamically modern hotel in Zenith" (024-018). Its basement housed the Pompeian Barber Shop (003-037), where Babbitt got his hair cut regularly. The hotel served as a hub for social functions — the smaller ballroom was described as "a refined apartment with pale gray walls and twisted candelabra" (030-014) — and it was the setting for luncheons, including Babbitt's lunch with Tanis Judique (029-059). The hotel barber shop employed manicure girls who became part of Babbitt's romantic fantasies (023-023).

## Description

A modern hotel in downtown Zenith, notable for its size and dynamic modernity. The Pompeian Barber Shop operated in its basement (003-037, 024-018). Its refined smaller ballroom featured "pale gray walls and twisted candelabra" (030-014). The hotel's gold and oak restaurant was a prominent public dining room where businessmen and their guests were visible to the entire Zenith commercial class — "Every one in the gold and oak restaurant of the Thornleigh was staring at her as Babbitt followed her to a table" (029-060).

## Social Function

The Hotel Thornleigh functioned as a venue for business and social display. Babbitt took Tanis Judique there for lunch, noting that "she who had seemed well content" with simpler pleasures was impressed by the hotel's grandeur (029-059). When choosing where to take Tanis, Babbitt "compromised on the Thornleigh" after considering and rejecting the "ladies' annex" of the Athletic Club as too dangerous — the Thornleigh offered respectability with a degree of public visibility that was both desirable and perilous (029-059). During the meal, Vergil Gunch watched them throughout, and Babbitt "watched himself being watched and lugubriously tried to keep from spoiling Tanis's pleasure" (029-060). Tanis herself was impressed: "It's so live and yet so--so refined" (029-060).

The hotel also hosted the Zenith branch of the League of the Higher Illumination, which met in the smaller ballroom (030-014). This connection between New Thought spirituality and a luxury hotel venue illustrates Lewis's satire of how even "higher" pursuits in Zenith were inseparable from commercial settings.

## Satirical Significance

Lewis uses the Hotel Thornleigh as a symbol of Zenith's self-conscious modernity and the role of physical settings in social stratification. The term "dynamically modern" (024-018) encapsulates the boosterish pride that Zenith takes in its commercial institutions, while the juxtaposition of the barber shop — a site of masculine grooming and gossip — in the basement of the city's finest hotel underscores the inseparability of commerce, appearance, and social status. The gold and oak restaurant functions as a theater of social surveillance, where every luncheon is a public performance and where the presence of an unconventional companion — like Tanis — becomes material for gossip (029-060). Tanis's verdict, "so live and yet so--so refined" (029-060), perfectly captures the Thornleigh's — and Zenith's — self-contradictory aspiration to be both dynamically commercial and culturally sophisticated.
