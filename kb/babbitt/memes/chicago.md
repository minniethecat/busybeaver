# Chicago

The real Midwestern metropolis that serves as Zenith's rival and measuring stick. While Zenith boasts of its growth and modernity, Chicago represents the genuine urban greatness that Zenith can only aspire to. The comparison between the two cities is implicit throughout the novel's booster rhetoric.

Babbitt visited Chicago for business and stayed at the Rippleton Hotel near the station, taking a taxi for late-night arrivals as was his custom on the road (010-038). At a dinner party, Tanis Judique asked Babbitt whether he traveled to Chicago and New York regularly, assuming such travel was part of a successful businessman's routine (015-055).
