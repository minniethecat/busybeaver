# Blodgett College

Blodgett College is an American institution of higher education mentioned in *Babbitt* as the undergraduate alma mater of Howard Littlefield.

## Description

Blodgett College awarded Howard Littlefield a Bachelor of Arts degree prior to his doctoral studies at Yale (003-006). The college is mentioned solely in connection with Littlefield's academic credentials, with no further details given about its location, size, or character.

## Social Function

Within the novel, Blodgett College serves as a credential in the academic pedigree of Howard Littlefield, the neighborhood's self-appointed intellectual authority. The combination of the obscure Blodgett College with the prestigious Yale Ph.D. subtly underscores Lewis's satire of middle-class credentialism—Littlefield's authority rests on institutional names that his neighbors cannot evaluate critically (003-006).

## Key Members

- **[[Howard-Littlefield]]** — Bachelor of Arts graduate, later Ph.D. from Yale, serving as employment-manager and publicity-counsel of the Zenith Street Traction Company (003-006)

## Littlefield's Role in Zenith

Howard Littlefield's academic credentials from Blodgett College and Yale authorized his position as the intellectual of Babbitt's social circle. As Babbitt's neighbor on Floral Heights, Littlefield "proved" matters of culture and manners to his fellow businessmen "out of his" store of knowledge (003-007). His expertise was not purely academic: he "furnished publicity and comforting economics to the Street Traction Company" (008-031), demonstrating how the college credential translated into practical business authority in Zenith.

> He was a Bachelor of Arts of Blodgett College, and a Doctor of Philosophy in economics from Yale. He was the employment-manager and publicity-counsel of the Zenith Street Traction Company. (003-006)

Babbitt took "honest pride in being the neighbor of such a savant" (003-008), and the two families maintained a close social relationship, with Babbitt frequently seeking Littlefield's opinion on matters ranging from business statistics to child-rearing (003-012, 003-013, 006-071). At dinner parties, Littlefield held forth on topics such as Prohibition enforcement, insisting that "if I'd been running the thing, I'd have arranged it so that the drinker himself was licensed" (008-035).

## Satirical Significance

Lewis uses Blodgett College as part of the broader satire of American boosterism and credential-worship. The college's obscurity—mentioned only once and never elaborated—contrasts with the weight its name carries among Littlefield's neighbors. The very fact that Littlefield is defined by degrees from institutions his audience has never heard of highlights the hollow reverence for formal education in Zenith's business class.

The irony deepens when considering that Littlefield's intellectual authority, built on the names "Blodgett" and "Yale," is deployed chiefly to validate the prejudices of the Zenith business establishment. His Ph.D. serves not to challenge but to "prove" what his neighbors already believe (003-007), making the college credential a tool of conformity rather than inquiry.
