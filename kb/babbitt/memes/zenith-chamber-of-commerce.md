# Zenith Chamber of Commerce

The Zenith Chamber of Commerce is the coordinating body for the city's commercial interests and the institutional voice of boosterism. Its luncheons, bulletins, and committees provide the organizational infrastructure for the ideology that the [[boosters-club]] celebrates in its ritual life. Where the Boosters' button is the sacrament, the Chamber is the administrative apparatus (004-026, 006-031, 007-025).

## Role in the Novel

The Chamber of Commerce appears throughout the novel as the background hum of Zenith's civic life — the organization that sponsors political candidates, publishes economic propaganda, and coordinates the city's response to any challenge to business orthodoxy. Lewis uses the Chamber to show how boosterism is not merely a mood or a rhetoric but an organized system with its own institutions, publications, and enforcement mechanisms.

## Organizational Structure

| Element | Description | Satirical Significance |

| Affiliations | Employers' Association, American Legion, political parties (004-026) | Interlocking directorate of power |

## Key Scenes

- **The Open Shop campaign** — The Chamber of Commerce, working with "all the decent newspapers," coordinates support for candidates who oppose organized labor. Lewis's irony is perfect: the "decent" newspapers are those that serve business interests (014-002).
- **The victory dinner** — After the Open Shop candidates win, the Chamber celebrates with a dinner that Babbitt attends. The event is a ritual of power congratulating itself (014-010).
- **Babbitt's oratory** — At a Chamber of Commerce luncheon, Babbitt is "oratorical regarding the sin of striking." The scene collapses the distance between business self-interest and moral judgment (016-002).
- **The strike response** — When Babbitt defends the strikers during his rebellion, he invokes his Chamber of Commerce committee work as proof of his loyalty — a defense that fails precisely because the Chamber has no tolerance for dissent (028-021).
- **Post-rebellion coordination** — After Babbitt's return to conformity, the Chamber of Commerce, along with the American Legion, functions as the organizational framework for enforcing orthodoxy (029-062).

## Satirical Significance

The Chamber of Commerce is Lewis's representation of how business interests organize themselves into a de facto government. It does not merely advocate for business; it is the structural mechanism through which the business class rules. Its power is exercised not through force but through the management of consensus — the luncheon oratory, the bulletin editorials, the committee resolutions that define the boundaries of acceptable opinion in Zenith.
