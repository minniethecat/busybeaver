# O'Hearn House

A Zenith hotel operated by Gil O'Hearn, known for its Venetian Ball Room — the city's premier venue for civic banquets and business functions, where Zenith's commercial elite gather to perform the rituals of boosterism over elaborate meals.

## Description

The O'Hearn House is Zenith's principal banquet venue, hosting the city's most important civic-commercial gatherings. Its Venetian Ball Room serves as the setting for the annual Get-Together Fest of the Zenith Real Estate Board, an event the *Advocate-Times* reports with "unusual fullness": "One of the livest banquets that has recently been pulled off occurred last night in the annual Get-Together Fest of the Zenith Real Estate Board, held in the Venetian Ball Room of the O'Hearn House" (014-011).

The hotel's proprietor, Gil O'Hearn, is a figure of local renown: "Mine host Gil O'Hearn had as usual done himself proud and those assembled feasted on such an assemblage of plates as could be rivaled nowhere west of New York, if there" (014-011). The hyperbolic booster rhetoric — claiming the meal rivals anything "west of New York" — captures the provincial anxiety masked as civic pride that defines Zenith's self-image. The drinks served are cider "from the farm of Chandler Mott" (014-011), a Prohibition-era detail that both acknowledges the law and winks at its circumvention: the cider "inspired but did not inebriate."

The O'Hearn House also hosts the Boosters' Club luncheon at which the annual election is held: "The lunch was held in the ballroom of the O'Hearn House" (021-004).

## Social Function

The O'Hearn House functions as the sacred space of Zenith boosterism — a secular cathedral where business transactions are sanctified by the rituals of the banquet: the elaborate meal, the self-congratulatory speeches, the collective performance of civic pride. The hotel's "Venetian Ball Room" name — invoking European grandeur in the American Midwest — is itself a satire of Zenith's pretensions to cosmopolitan sophistication.

## Key Scenes

- **The Real Estate Board Get-Together** — The annual banquet features an "assemblage of plates" rivaling anything "west of New York" (014-011) and Babbitt's election as vice-president of the board
- **The Boosters' Club election lunch** — "The lunch was held in the ballroom of the O'Hearn House. As each of the four hundred Boosters entered" (021-004), the hotel hosts the ritual of leadership succession

## Satirical Significance

The O'Hearn House embodies the novel's satire of provincial grandiosity. The hotel's name and its "Venetian Ball Room" invoke Old World elegance, but the events held there — real-estate banquets with cider from a local farm — are thoroughly, comically American. The booster rhetoric applied to the hotel's cuisine (unrivaled "west of New York") reveals the city's desperate need to measure itself against Eastern standards while simultaneously asserting its own superiority. Gil O'Hearn, "as usual" doing himself proud, is the archetypal Zenith host — competence as performance, hospitality as civic boosterism.
