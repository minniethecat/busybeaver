# Social Class and Conformity

Social class in Zenith is simultaneously the most important and the most unmentionable fact of life. Everyone knows that Floral Heights outranks Linton, that the McKelveys outrank the Babbitts, and that "the right sort of people" is a category policed by a thousand tiny signals — the car you drive, the church you attend, the club that admits you. This page aggregates entities that illuminate the class structure and the relentless pressure to conform that defines middle-class Zenith.

## About This Index

Lewis's genius is to make us feel the weight of conformity without ever letting his characters name it. Babbitt's rebellion and capitulation, Paul Riesling's rupture, Seneca Doane's marginalization — each is a case study in what happens when the individual collides with the social machinery. The entities indexed here trace that machinery's components: the neighborhoods that encode status, the institutions that enforce it, and the rare individuals who challenge it.
