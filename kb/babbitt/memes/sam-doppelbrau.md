# Sam Doppelbrau

Sam Doppelbrau is [[george-babbitt|George Babbitt's]] next-door neighbor in [[floral-heights|Floral Heights]], residing in one of the three Dutch Colonial houses on the block. Secretary of an excellent firm of bathroom-fixture jobbers, Doppelbrau is a respectable, industrious, prosperous citizen whose "ideal of happiness was an eternal cabaret."

## Role in the Novel

Doppelbrau and his wife represent the culture of respectable hedonism that defines Floral Heights after hours. Their life, like that of their social set, is "dominated by suburban bacchanalia of alcohol, nicotine, gasoline, and kisses." They work capably all week and look forward to Saturday night, when they "throw a party" that grows noisier and noisier up to Sunday dawn. Doppelbrau serves as both a foil and a gateway for Babbitt — his example of carefree living helps draw Babbitt into the world of [[tanis-judique|Tanis Judique]] and [[bunch|the Bunch]].

## Key Scenes

- **The morning greeting** — Babbitt, backing out of his garage in a dark mood, shouts "Morning!" to Sam Doppelbrau "with more cordiality than he had intended." (003-004)
- **The closed-car argument** — Ted Babbitt uses the Doppelbraus' ownership of a closed car as ammunition in his campaign for the family to upgrade: "Aw, gee whiz, if the Doppelbraus can afford a closed car, I guess we can!" (006-026)
- **The evening view** — On an April night, Babbitt stands in his sun-parlor looking out at "only the lights of Doppelbrau's house and the dim presence of Babbitt's favorite elm." (006-073)
- **The party at Doppelbrau's** — During Babbitt's rebellion, he joins the Doppelbraus for an evening. By ten o'clock he is calling Mr. Doppelbrau "Sam, old hoss." By eleven they all drive out to the [[old-farm-inn|Old Farm Inn]], where Babbitt sits in the back of Doppelbrau's car with Louetta Swanson and for the first time makes love without self-consciousness. [029-042 through 029-049]
- **Mrs. Doppelbrau's cigarette** — Babbitt is shocked but titillated to discover that Mrs. Sam Doppelbrau is the only woman he knows who smokes. (028-021)

## Character Traits

Doppelbrau is a man of surfaces: prosperous, convivial, and entirely unreflective. Lewis presents him without overt satire because he embodies what Floral Heights admires — a man who has mastered the art of converting money into enjoyment. His significance lies less in his individuality than in his typicality: he is what every Floral Heights husband secretly wishes he could be on Saturday night.
