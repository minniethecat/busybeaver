# Conventions and Public Events

Zenith's civic calendar is a liturgy of boosterism. Real-estate conventions, Boosters' Club elections, annual banquets, and mayoral campaigns — each event is a ritual of self-congratulation through which the city's commercial class renews its faith in itself. These public performances, from the garden party to the convention floor, are where Zenith's ideology becomes spectacle. ## About This Index

Public events in *Babbitt* are the novel's most potent satirical instrument. Lewis stages banquet after banquet, convention after convention, each a set-piece of collective self-deception. The booster rituals — the singing, the back-slapping, the oratory that says nothing — are the mechanism by which Zenith's business class persuades itself that commerce is culture and conformity is freedom. These pages document the machinery behind that persuasion.
