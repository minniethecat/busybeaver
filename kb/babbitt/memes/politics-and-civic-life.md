# Politics and Civic Life

Political and civic life in Zenith is a theater of propriety rather than principle. Elections are won by the candidate who offends the fewest businessmen, and "reform" means whatever the Good Citizens' League says it means. This page aggregates all entities that participate in — or are shaped by — the political and civic machinery of Sinclair Lewis's Zenith.

## About This Index

Politics in *Babbitt* is never about ideology — it is about belonging. The novel's political episodes (the mayoral campaign, the Good Citizens' League crackdown, Babbitt's brief flirtation with liberalism) all serve the same satirical point: in a city where business is the only real religion, politics is merely the公关 department. The entities indexed here range from party machines to reform leagues, each one a cog in the machinery of respectable conformity.
