# International Organization of Boosters' Clubs

## Description

The International Organization of Boosters' Clubs is the global coordinating body of the booster movement, an organization that "has become a world-force for optimism, manly pleasantry, and good business" (021-001). Headquartered (implicitly) in the United States, the International claims chapters "in thirty countries" — though Lewis immediately undercuts this global pretension by noting that "nine hundred and twenty of the thousand chapters, however, are in the United States" (021-001). The International is boosterism writ large: an American export that mistakes its own provincialism for universal truth.

## Social Function

The International's primary function is to confer legitimacy on the local booster clubs by situating them within a global framework. A Zenith Booster who attends the International convention in, say, St. Louis or Chicago returns to Zenith with the prestige of having participated in something larger than local business promotion. The International provides the same satisfactions as the local club — handshakes, speeches, committee work — but on a grander scale, complete with badges that say "Delegate" and hotel lobbies filled with men who look and talk exactly like oneself.

The International also publishes materials that standardize booster ideology across chapters, ensuring that a Booster in Zenith and a Booster in Peoria recite the same slogans about optimism and good citizenship. This standardization is presented as professionalization but functions as indoctrination, spreading a uniform worldview that equates business success with moral worth and treats dissent as a form of illness.

## Key Scenes

The International Organization of Boosters' Clubs is primarily referenced in Chapter XXI, where the visiting secretary of the Zenith Rotary Club appears at a Boosters' meeting and the International's pretensions are lightly mocked. The fact that the International's global reach is almost entirely American — eighty percent of its chapters are in the United States — is the unstated joke of the entire institution.

## Satirical Significance

The International Organization of Boosters' Clubs is a perfect Lewisian joke: an institution whose name promises cosmopolitan reach but whose reality is parochial self-congratulation. The International claims "world-force" status while being essentially an American export — a form of cultural imperialism that mistakes the spread of Rotary-style boosterism for the advance of civilization. The nine-hundred-and-twenty American chapters out of one thousand are themselves a kind of statistical punchline, the math exposing the gap between rhetoric and reality.

The International also represents the booster movement's aspiration to transcend mere commerce and become a philosophy of life. The phrase "a world-force for optimism, manly pleasantry, and good business" (021-001) encapsulates the booster worldview in three terms: optimism (the required emotional state), manly pleasantry (the required social mode), and good business (the required outcome). The International has no spiritual or intellectual content beyond these three values — and it does not need any, because its members have never thought to ask for more.
