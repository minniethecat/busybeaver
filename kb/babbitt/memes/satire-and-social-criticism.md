# Satire and Social Criticism

Sinclair Lewis does not merely describe Zenith — he anatomizes it. The novel's satirical targets range from the individual hypocrisies of its characters to the structural absurdities of American commercial culture. Church as salesmanship, friendship as networking, art as advertising — every institution the novel touches is revealed to have a double life. ## About This Index

Lewis's satire operates through accumulation — not a single devastating blow but a thousand small cuts. The novel's satirical method is embedded in its catalog technique: the relentless listing of brand names, club titles, and commercial slogans that simultaneously establishes Zenith's material density and empties it of meaning. The pages indexed here trace that method from the Boosters' Club anthem to the real-estate board's banquet oratory.
