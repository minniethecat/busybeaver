# Dodsworth Theater

A theater in Zenith that briefly becomes a point of contention in the novel's satire of civic boosterism — its programming of a film titled *The Mayor of Zenith* provokes the Boosters' Club to mobilize an organized campaign of public disapproval.

## Description

The Dodsworth Theater appears in the narrative when Vergil Gunch, a prominent Booster, reports on the club's response to a film that the membership finds objectionable. The film, playing "this week at the Dodsworth Theater," is described as one in which "the mayor of Zenith, the board of education, the minister of the Chatham Road Presbyterian Church, our worthy city attorney, and many others were getting theirs good and plenty" (021-014). The theater thus becomes a site of conflict between artistic representation (however crude) and the city's established power structure.

The theater's name connects it to the Dodsworth family, described elsewhere as the "best-known pioneer family of Zenith" (015-012) — a lineage associated with the Dodsworth-Smoot Transportation Company. The name carries the weight of pioneer prestige, ironic given that the theater's programming has become a target of the civic establishment. The treasurer of the Dodsworth, significantly, "is a Booster and will appreciate the hard work and good fellowship" (021-015) — the theater's financial officer belongs to the very club that is organizing the boycott.

## Social Function

The Dodsworth Theater serves as an instrument of the novel's satire of cultural censorship operating under the guise of civic virtue. The conflict is not about art but about image: the film shows Zenith's leadership in an unflattering light, and the Boosters' response — a coordinated pressure campaign — demonstrates how the city's business elite polices representation. The fact that the theater's own treasurer is a Booster (021-015) collapses the distinction between cultural venue and booster apparatus: the theater is expected to serve the city's reputation, not challenge it.

## Key Scenes

- **The Boosters' film controversy** — Vergil Gunch reports that the Boosters' Club has taken action against *The Mayor of Zenith*, a film playing at the Dodsworth Theater that lampoons the city's leadership (021-014). The "committee on public safety" has been "after the manager of the Dodsworth" and the film will likely be "withdrawn tomorrow" (021-014)

## Satirical Significance

The Dodsworth Theater episode condenses the novel's critique of boosterism into a single transaction: an institution bearing the name of Zenith's most distinguished pioneer family is expected to suppress a film because it embarrasses the city's current leadership. The irony is doubled by the fact that the Dodsworth family itself — "pioneers" of the city — might be expected to value independence; instead, the theater's treasurer is a Booster whose loyalties are to the club, not to any principle of free expression. The film *The Mayor of Zenith* is never described in aesthetic terms — it exists purely as a provocation, and the theater exists purely as a venue that can be pressured into compliance.
