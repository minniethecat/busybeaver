# Lucas Prout

Lucas Prout is the mayor of Zenith — a mattress-manufacturer by trade and the embodiment of "Sound Business" in municipal politics. Propped up by the banks, the Chamber of Commerce, and "all the decent newspapers," Prout is the safe candidate the establishment unites behind to defeat the progressive threat of Seneca Doane.

## Role in the Novel

Prout appears primarily as a political symbol rather than a developed character. He is the Republican-Democratic fusion candidate whose campaign against the labor-backed [[seneca-doane|Seneca Doane]] serves as a vehicle for Lewis's satire of American municipal politics — a system in which "Sound Business" is the only ideology that matters and the mayor is expected to be a figurehead, not a reformer. Babbitt's enthusiastic campaigning for Prout marks his full assimilation into the booster establishment, while later Babbitt's visit to Prout's office to secure leniency for [[paul-riesling|Paul Riesling]] reveals the transactional nature of political loyalty in Zenith.

## Key Scenes

- **The mayoral campaign** — When [[seneca-doane|Seneca Doane]], a lawyer and State University graduate, runs for mayor on a labor ticket, Democrats and Republicans unite behind Prout as the fusion candidate. Prout, "a mattress-manufacturer with a perfect record for sanity," is supported by "the banks, the Chamber of Commerce, all the decent newspapers, and George F. Babbitt" (014-002). Babbitt serves as a precinct-leader and campaign speaker, earning a photograph in the *Advocate-Times* captioned "Leaders of Zenith Finance and Commerce Who Back Prout" (014-003).
- **The election result** — "Mr. Lucas Prout and Sound Business defeated Mr. Seneca Doane and Class Rule, and Zenith was again saved" (014-010). Babbitt receives advance information about highway extensions as his political reward.
- **The Boosters' Club banquet** — Mayor Prout and the leading man of the "Bird of Paradise" company are the two most distinguished guests at the Boosters' annual banquet. Vergil Gunch introduces Prout with a toast: "Hizzonor" will "say a few words about the problems and duties" (021-014).
- **Babbitt's office visit** — When [[paul-riesling]] is arrested for shooting [[zilla-riesling|his wife]], Babbitt rushes to the mayor's office, bribes the doorman with a dollar, and demands a favor: "You remember me, Mr. Prout? Babbitt — vice-president of the Boosters — campaigned for you?" (022-006). The scene exposes the patronage system beneath the booster rhetoric.

## Character Traits

Prout is defined almost entirely by what he represents rather than who he is. He is the "sane" candidate — a word Lewis uses with satirical precision to denote ideological conformity rather than mental health. His mattress-manufacturing background is neither distinguished nor disreputable; it is simply *safe*. The novel never gives Prout a speaking voice of any substance, reducing him to a placeholder for the interests he serves: the banks, the Chamber of Commerce, the newspapers. This absence of personality is precisely the point — the ideal mayor of Zenith is a man whose individual character has been completely absorbed into the institutional consensus.
