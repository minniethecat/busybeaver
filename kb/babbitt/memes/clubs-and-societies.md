# Clubs and Societies

Zenith's club life is the city's circulatory system — the place where business deals are sealed, reputations are made, and the rituals of middle-class masculinity are performed. From the Athletic Club to the Elks, the Boosters to the Moose, each organization is a rung on the status ladder that George Babbitt climbs with anxious determination. ## About Club Life in Zenith

Sinclair Lewis's catalog of Zenith's clubs and societies is one of the novel's great satirical achievements. Each organization — the Boosters' Club with its ritualized "pep," the Athletic Club with its locker-room camaraderie, the Elks and Moose and Red Men with their elaborate hierarchies — serves the same function: to assure the middle-class Zenith male that he belongs. The clubs are a status grid, a business network, and a substitute for genuine community, all wrapped in bunting and banquet speeches.
