# Moorish Grill

A grillroom within the Hotel Sedgwick in Monarch, where George Babbitt has dinner during the annual convention of the state real-estate association (013-062).

## Description

The Moorish Grill is located within the Hotel Sedgwick, Monarch's premier hotel and the lodging choice of Babbitt and his Zenith colleagues during the convention. The restaurant is named with an exoticism — "Moorish" — that reflects the early twentieth-century American fashion for themed dining rooms that borrowed decorative motifs from vaguely "Oriental" sources, a commercial orientalism that promised cosmopolitan sophistication without requiring actual knowledge of foreign cultures.

The grillroom's name is the only detail the novel provides: "They had dinner in the Moorish Grillroom of the Hotel Sedgwick" (013-062). The sentence connects the restaurant to the hotel, and the hotel to the convention, placing the grillroom within the network of commercial hospitality that structures the delegates' experience of Monarch.

## Social Function

The Moorish Grill serves a function common to convention hotels: it provides a setting for the informal business that takes place between the formal sessions. The "they" who dine there (013-062) — Babbitt and his colleagues — are participating in the rituals of convention sociability, where meals are not merely sustenance but occasions for professional bonding. The restaurant's exotic branding is incidental to its real function: it is a room where real-estate men eat together, their conversation presumably continuing the blend of business and boosterism that defines the convention.

The fact that the grillroom is within the Hotel Sedgwick — rather than a freestanding restaurant — reinforces the convention's self-contained character. The delegates move from hotel bedroom to hotel ballroom to hotel grillroom, their experience of Monarch mediated entirely through the hospitality industry.

## Key Scenes

- **Dinner during the convention** — Babbitt dines with colleagues at the Moorish Grillroom of the Hotel Sedgwick (013-062), one of the social meals that constitute the informal program of the real-estate convention

## Satirical Significance

The Moorish Grill is a minor example of the novel's attention to the naming conventions of American commercial culture. "Moorish" promises exoticism and refinement, but the grillroom serves the same food to the same businessmen who could dine at any hotel restaurant in Zenith. The gap between the name's cosmopolitan aspiration and the provincial reality it houses — real-estate men talking shop — is the novel's quiet joke. Like the California Building's name, the Moorish Grill's branding gestures toward a wider world that the novel's characters never truly inhabit.
