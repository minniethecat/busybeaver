# Sidney Finkelstein

Sidney Finkelstein is a ladies'-ready-to-wear buyer and a fixture in [[george-babbitt|Babbitt]]'s social and business circle. Lean, red-headed, and relentlessly "brisk," he represents a p[[art]]icular type of [[zenith|Zenith]] male: commercially sharp, socially aggressive, and perpetually performing masculinity through banter, jokes, and competitive display (005-016, 023-031).

## Role in the Novel

Finkelstein appears throughout the novel as one of the chorus of businessmen whose interactions define the texture of [[zenith|Zenith]]'s masculine [[art|culture]]. His function is primarily atmospheric — he is one of the voices in [[boosters-club|the Boosters' Club]] banter, the dinner-p[[art]]y rep[[art]]ee, the poker-table needling. But Lewis uses him to demonstrate how thoroughly social relations among [[zenith|Zenith]] men are structured by competition: every interaction is a contest, and Finkelstein's briskness is the tempo of that contest (023-027, 023-030).

## Key Scenes

- **The [[boosters-club|Boosters' Club]] dynamic** — Finkelstein p[[art]]icipates in the ritualized teasing that constitutes male bonding in [[george-babbitt|Babbitt]]'s circle. When [[george-babbitt|Babbitt]] is singled out for a joke, Finkelstein's snicker and grin help establish the group's power to enforce conformity through mockery (023-030, 023-031).
- **The poker game** — At the weekly poker gathering, Finkelstein's suggestion of "two bucks apiece" reveals the casual gambling that oils [[zenith|Zenith]]'s social machinery (029-006).
- **"Real he-stuff"** — Finkelstein praises a congressman's speech as "real he-stuff," a phrase that captures the gender ideology underlying [[zenith|Zenith]] [[community|boosterism]]: [[gossip|talk]] must be masculine, vigorous, untainted by the feminine or intellectual (032-007).
- **[[good-citizens-league|Good Citizens' League]]** — Finkelstein joins the reactionary [[good-citizens-league|Good Citizens' League]] alongside [[george-babbitt|Babbitt]], [[vergil-gunch|Gunch]], and McKelvey, demonstrating how his social conformity translates into political conformity (034-007).

## Character Traits

Finkelstein is brisk, lean, and red-headed — his physical description matches his personality. He is a teaser, a needler, a man whose wit is entirely deployed in the service of group cohesion. He is "annoying" to [[george-babbitt|Babbitt]] precisely because he cannot be [[freedom|escape]]d: Finkelstein is everywhere, one of the interloc[[family|kin]]g members of the social machine from which [[george-babbitt|Babbitt]]'s rebellion will briefly liberate him. His wife is described as "merry, brown-eyed, and flapping" — a matching set of briskness (033-032).
