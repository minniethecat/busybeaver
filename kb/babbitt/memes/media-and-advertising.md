# Media and Advertising

The *Advocate-Times* does not report the news; it confirms the mood of the business class. Advertising does not describe products; it describes the life you ought to want. In Zenith, media and advertising form a closed loop: the newspaper prints what advertisers pay for, and advertisers pay for access to readers who have been trained to want what advertising sells. This page aggregates the publications, professionals, and concepts that constitute Zenith's information ecosystem.

## About This Index

Lewis worked in advertising and publishing before he wrote *Babbitt*, and his portrait of the media landscape is mercilessly informed. The *Advocate-Times* is not a newspaper but a mirror held up to its readers' prejudices; the advertising copy Babbitt writes is not salesmanship but a kind of pastoral poetry for the consumer age. The entities indexed here map the system that manufactures what Zenith calls "public opinion" — a product as standardized as the automobiles it celebrates.
