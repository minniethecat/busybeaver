# Union Station

Zenith's central railway terminal, through which travelers arrive from and depart for Chicago, New York, and Maine. The station represents Zenith's connection to the wider world — a liminal space between the city's certainties and the possibilities of elsewhere.

The station served as a gathering point for civic events, including the departure of delegates for the annual convention of the State Association of Real Estate Boards. Martin Lumsen's son Willy carried a tasseled banner reading "Zenith the Zip City — Zeal, Zest and Zowie — 1,000,000 in 1935" as delegates assembled at the station for the midnight train to Monarch (013-019).
