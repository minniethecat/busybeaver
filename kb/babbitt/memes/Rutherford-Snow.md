# Colonel Rutherford Snow

Colonel Rutherford Snow is the owner of the *[[advocate-times|Advocate-Times]]*, Zenith's morning newspaper, and one of the city's most imposing establishment figures. A white-bearded, ferocious old man, Snow embodies the intersection of media power, old-money prestige, and civic authority that defines Zenith's ruling elite.

## Role in the Novel

Snow represents the upper tier of Zenith's power structure — the level above even [[charles-mckelvey|Charles McKelvey]]'s Smart Set. As owner of the *Advocate-Times*, he controls the city's dominant media voice, making him a figure whose approval is sought by civic organizations and whose presence confers legitimacy. When Babbitt encounters Snow among the dinner guests at the Devon Woods dinner party, the colonel's presence is "most dismaying of all" to him — more intimidating even than the surgeon Dilling and the millionaire contractor McKelvey. Snow's ferocity, combined with his strategic support for institutions like the Y.M.C.A., illustrates the novel's theme that Zenith's power elite manages public opinion through a blend of intimidation and calculated benevolence.

## Key Scenes

- **The Devon Woods dinner party** — Snow is among the distinguished guests at a dinner at the McKelveys' Devon Woods estate. In the "whelming presence" of figures like Snow, Babbitt "felt small and insignificant." The colonel's combination of military title, media ownership, and social prestige exemplifies the concentrated power that makes Babbitt feel simultaneously awed and excluded. (032-013)
- **The Y.M.C.A. fundraising campaign** — Despite his ferocity, Snow lends his prestige to the Y.M.C.A. fundraising drive. He is photographed clasping the hand of [[sheldon-smeeth|Sheldon Smeeth]], an image used in movie theaters to promote "how great an influence for manly Christianity the 'good old Y.' had been." When Smeeth subsequently lisps an invitation to a prayer meeting, Snow famously bellows, "What the hell would I do that for?" — a moment that captures both the transactional nature of elite civic patronage and Snow's utter indifference to the pieties he publicly endorses. (034-007)

## Character Traits

Snow is defined by the word "ferocious" — Lewis uses it to describe both his public bellowing and his underlying nature. His white beard and colonel's title confer a patriarchal authority that masks the raw power he wields. His willingness to be photographed for Y.M.C.A. publicity while privately rejecting its religious content exemplifies the hypocrisy that Lewis identifies as structural to Zenith's civic life: elites lend their names to causes without investing their convictions.

## Relationships

- **[[charles-mckelvey|Charles McKelvey]]** — Fellow member of Zenith's ruling elite; Snow moves in the same social orbit as the McKelvey set
- **[[sheldon-smeeth|Sheldon Smeeth]]** — Snow tolerates Smeeth for the Y.M.C.A. photo opportunity but rebuffs his religious overtures with contempt
- **[[george-babbitt|George Babbitt]]** — Babbitt is awed and intimidated by Snow's presence at the Devon Woods dinner
