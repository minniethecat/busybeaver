# Labor and Industry

Zenith presents itself through the language of commerce and civic pride, but beneath the banquet speeches lies an industrial city of factories, foundries, and workingmen. The tension between labor and capital runs through *Babbitt* as an undercurrent — from the Street Traction Company monopoly to the shadowy figures of organized labor. ## About This Index

Industry in *Babbitt* is both substance and symbol. The novel's working-class characters — from the mysterious Jake Offut to the streetcar union men — exist at the margins of the narrative, glimpsed but rarely heard. Lewis ensures the reader senses the productive engine behind Zenith's prosperity even as Babbitt himself remains insulated from it. The tension between booster slogans and industrial reality is one of the novel's sharpest satirical edges.
