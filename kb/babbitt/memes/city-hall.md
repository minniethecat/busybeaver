# City Hall

The center of Zenith's municipal administration — a building that appears in the narrative primarily as a waypoint in Babbitt's movements through the city and as a symbol of the governmental apparatus that business culture treats with genial contempt.

## Description

City Hall is referenced only in passing, its physical appearance unremarked. It is a destination on Babbitt's daily circuit: driving through Zenith's streets, "past the truckmen's curses, to the City Hall; he stopped with a grind of wheels before the office of the city clerk" (022-006). The terse description — curses, destination, stop — reduces civic architecture to a series of vehicular transactions.

The building's interior is gestured at through the figure of the mayor, whose "multifarious duties at City Hall" (021-015) are invoked by Vergil Gunch in the context of the Dodsworth Theater film controversy. The adjective "multifarious" — a Gunchian flourish — suggests a busyness that the novel never documents, reducing municipal government to a rhetorical prop in booster speeches.

## Social Function

City Hall's narrative function is to exist as a counter that the Boosters can invoke when the city's reputation needs defending. Gunch's reference to the mayor's "multifarious duties at City Hall" (021-015) comes in the context of organizing suppression of a film that lampoons city leadership — the invocation of civic dignity is inseparable from the exercise of civic intimidation. City Hall is less a governing institution than a brand asset, to be protected from artistic representation that might damage the city's commercial appeal.

The building itself, glimpsed only through Babbitt's windshield (022-006), embodies the novel's treatment of civic institutions as background architecture — present, functional, but never examined. Government in Zenith exists to facilitate commerce, not to challenge it.

## Key Scenes

- **Babbitt's drive past City Hall** — En route to a meeting with the city clerk, Babbitt drives "past the truckmen's curses, to the City Hall" (022-006), the building serving as a navigation point in his daily rounds
- **Gunch's invocation of the mayor** — In reporting the Boosters' campaign against the Dodsworth Theater film, Gunch references the mayor's "multifarious duties at City Hall" (021-015) as part of the justification for suppressing the film

## Satirical Significance

Lewis reduces City Hall to texture: a building that Babbitt drives past, an office the mayor occupies, a name Gunch brandishes. It is the seat of a government that governs nothing in particular — the real power in Zenith resides at the Athletic Club, the Boosters' Club, and the real-estate board. City Hall's conspicuous absence from the novel's major scenes is itself the satire: in a city run by businessmen for business, municipal government is ceremonial architecture.
