# Boosterism and Civic Pride

Boosterism is the state religion of Zenith. It is a creed with its own liturgy (the weekly Boosters' Club luncheon), its own hymns (Chum Frink's "Zenith Anthem"), and its own eschatology (the city that outgrows Chicago). To "boost" is to belong; to criticize is to betray. This page aggregates the people, organizations, and concepts that power Zenith's endless self-congratulation — and the satirical machinery Lewis deploys to expose its hollowness.

## About This Index

Lewis understood boosterism as something far deeper than civic cheerleading. It is, in *Babbitt*, the ideological glue that holds the business class together — a substitute for religion, philosophy, and genuine community. "To be a booster," as one character puts it, "is to be a real he-American." The entities indexed here collectively portray a city drunk on its own publicity, and the few characters who glimpse the hangover.
