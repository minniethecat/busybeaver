The concepts extracted from *Babbitt* range from broad social themes — conformity, boosterism, class hierarchy — to the specific material obsessions that define Zenith's citizens: the automobile, the standardized house, the business deal. ## About This Index

Concepts are the intellectual architecture of this wiki — they connect individual persons, places, and events into coherent thematic patterns. Where a person page tells you who a character is, a concept page explains what their behavior means within the novel's satirical framework. Each concept page includes a definition, manifestations across chapters, PN-anchored citations, and an analysis of the concept's satirical function in the novel.

Quality levels reflect documentation depth: **featured** pages offer full cross-chapter analysis of the concept's role in Lewis's critique, **standard** pages provide comprehensive coverage with PN-anchored evidence, and **basic** pages offer essential definition awaiting further enrichment.
