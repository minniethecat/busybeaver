# The Roughnecks' Table

The informal daily lunch gathering at the Athletic Club where Babbitt and his peers — Vergil Gunch, Chum Frink, Howard Littlefield, and others — perform masculine friendship and enforce political orthodoxy. The table is where Babbitt feels most at home and most constrained: the warmth of belonging is inseparable from the pressure to conform.

At the Roughnecks' table, the group included Gunch, Finkelstein, Professor Pumphrey, Howard Littlefield, Chum Frink the poet and advertising-agent, and Orville Jones, whose laundry was among the best in Zenith (005-023). They composed "a club within the club" and merrily called themselves "The Roughnecks" (005-023). The table was where Babbitt received the nickname "Old Georgie" and where the rituals of masculine belonging — the back-slapping, the first-name intimacy, the shared political certainties — were performed daily (014-007).
