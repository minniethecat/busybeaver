# Zenith Real Estate Board

## Description

The Zenith Real Estate Board is the professional trade association to which George F. Babbitt belongs. It represents the institutional dimension of Babbitt's professional identity — the collective organization of the men who, like Babbitt, are "nimble in the calling of selling houses for more than people could afford to pay" (001-008). The Board is part of the State Association of Real Estate Boards, the larger organizational hierarchy that structures the profession at the state level (013-004).

## Social Function

The Board serves the standard functions of a trade association — networking, professional standards, advocacy — but in Zenith it also functions as a stage for the performance of booster masculinity. Babbitt's presidency ambitions ("Maybe the Real Estate Board will elect me president," 011-028) reveal the Board as another rung in the ladder of civic honors that the Zenith businessman climbs. The position of Board president is not primarily about advancing real-estate interests but about status: it would place Babbitt "instead of just sitting here talking" at the center of his professional world (011-028).

The Board is also the sponsor of the annual real-estate dinner, where Babbitt delivers his major booster speech — the event that makes him briefly a public figure in Zenith. The dinner functions as a secular liturgy: the businessmen gather, eat, and hear their own values reflected back at them in oratorical form.

## Key Scenes

- **Babbitt's Annual Address (Chapter XIV)**: At the dinner of the Zenith Real Estate Board, Babbitt "made the Annual Address" (014-011), a speech reported "with unabridged and understanding reports" by the *Advocate-Times*. This is Babbitt's finest hour as a public figure — the moment when he embodies the booster ideal so completely that the press treats his platitudes as news.
- **State Association Ambitions (Chapter XXIV)**: Babbitt's involvement in the State Association of Real Estate Boards "means a lot of work and responsibility" (024-012), but brings him into contact with dealers from across the state. The state-level association gives Babbitt a taste of a wider world, foreshadowing his eventual rebellion against Zenith's insularity.

## Satirical Significance

The Real Estate Board is a miniature of Zenith's civic structure: a formally democratic organization whose real function is the mutual ratification of its members' importance. The Board's dinners and elections are rituals of self-congratulation that confer status without requiring achievement. Babbitt's speech to the Board is treated as a significant intellectual event despite consisting entirely of booster slogans — the joke being that everyone in the room already agrees with everything he says. The Board makes real estate, the most material of professions, into a quasi-spiritual calling.
