# Chatham Road Presbyterian Church

Where Babbitt serves as a deacon and where the respectable families of Floral Heights worship. It represents the complete accommodation of American Protestantism to the values of the business class — a church that blesses prosperity, encourages conformity, and transforms prayer into a tool for "attaining financial success" (017-032).

## Role in the Novel

The church functions as an extension of the business world — a place where social connections are cemented and the existing order is sanctified. The church's Sunday school employs a press-agent, whose job is to place "insinuating items about neighborliness and the Bible" (017-032) in the local papers. Young Kenneth Escott gives "six hours a week" to "procure a trickle of insinuating items about neighborliness and the Bible, about class-suppers, jolly but educational, and the value of the Prayer-life in attaining financial success" (017-032).
