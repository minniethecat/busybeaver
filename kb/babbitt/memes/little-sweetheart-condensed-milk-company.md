# Little Sweetheart Condensed Milk Company

A Zenith-based manufacturer of condensed milk and evaporated cream products, part of the city's industrial landscape that its boosters celebrate as proof of Zenith's commercial superiority.

## Description

The Little Sweetheart Condensed Milk Company operates as part of Zenith's manufacturing sector, which includes "factories with high-perched water-tanks and tall stacks — factories producing condensed milk, paper boxes, lighting-fixtures, motor cars" (003-029). The company's condensed milk and evaporated cream products are cited by Zenith boosters as evidence of the city's global commercial reach.

In a speech at the State Association of Real Estate Boards convention, a Zenith delegate proclaims that "the little old Zip City celebrated in every land and clime, wherever condensed milk and pasteboard cartons are known" (014-026). Chum Frink's oft-recited poem similarly declares that "Zenith manufactures more condensed milk and evaporated cream, more paper boxes, and more lighting-fixtures, than any other city in the United States, if not in the world" (014-028). The condensed milk industry, of which Little Sweetheart is a representative firm, thus serves as a cornerstone of Zenith's booster mythology.

## Social Function

The company exemplifies the industrial-commercial nexus of Zenith's booster culture. Its products — condensed milk and evaporated cream — are cited not for their quality or nutritional value but as evidence of the city's manufacturing preeminence. The booster rhetoric surrounding condensed milk production [014-026, 014-028] transforms an unglamorous food-processing industry into a symbol of civic greatness, a pattern Lewis deploys throughout the novel to satirize the booster impulse.

At the Boosters' Club lunch, Hector Seybolt sits among "four hundred Boosters" wearing "a huge celluloid button announcing his name, his nickname, and his business" (021-004), illustrating how personal identity collapses into corporate affiliation in Zenith's business culture.

## Satirical Significance

Lewis uses the condensed milk industry — and the Little Sweetheart brand in particular — to satirize the booster equation of industrial output with civilization itself. The booster speeches that invoke "condensed milk and pasteboard cartons" (014-026) as the measure of Zenith's global significance reduce civic pride to a census of factory products. The brand name "Little Sweetheart" itself, applied to an industrial food product, captures the sentimental veneer over mass production that Lewis exposes throughout the novel.
