# Zenith Real-Estate Convention

The annual convention of the Zenith Real-Estate Board, held at the Monarch Hotel — "Zenith's chief rival to the Thornleigh" (013-004). The convention serves as the stage for [[george-babbitt|George Babbitt's]] most significant public performance: a speech celebrating the booster spirit that earns him the presidency of the State Association of Real-Estate Boards. It represents the apotheosis of the [[business]] religion's liturgical dimension — booster rhetoric as civic sacrament.

## Background

Babbitt is selected as one of the official delegates to represent Zenith: "Babbitt, Rountree, W. A. Rogers, Alvin Thayer, and Elbert Wing" (013-018). The convention gathers real-estate men from across the region, united by their shared faith in growth, commerce, and the moral superiority of the business life.

## Key Events

- **The journey to Monarch** — Babbitt and his fellow delegates travel together, their conversation a running performance of business orthodoxy and male camaraderie.
- **The convention proceedings** — Speeches, banquets, committee meetings — the secular liturgy of the business class.
- **Babbitt's speech** — His address, proclaiming that if Zenith fails to grow to "at least tenth" in population he will count himself a failure, is the novel's most concentrated expression of the booster ideology.

## Babbitt's Speech

The speech is a masterpiece of booster rhetoric: confident, platitudinous, and sincerely felt. Babbitt exhorts his fellow real-estate men to embrace growth as both business strategy and moral imperative. The speech's success — Babbitt is elected president — confirms his standing as a high priest of the business religion, even as the reader recognizes the emptiness of its content.

## Consequences

Babbitt's success at the convention marks the peak of his public career. He returns to Zenith with enhanced professional status and the "desire for rebellion was partly satisfied" (013-080) — a temporary reprieve from the discontent that will later erupt. The convention speech represents everything Babbitt will briefly renounce during his [[babbitts-rebellion|rebellion]].
