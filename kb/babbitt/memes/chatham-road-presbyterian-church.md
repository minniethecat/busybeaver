# Chatham Road Presbyterian Church

The Chatham Road Presbyterian Church is the congregation attended by George Babbitt and his family. It is described as "one of the largest and richest" churches in Zenith, and its pastor, the Reverend Dr. [[john-jennison-drew]], is a figure of considerable civic standing. The church serves as the spiritual arm of Zenith's business civilization — a place where the values of the Rotary Club are sanctified rather than challenged (016-011).

## Role in the Novel

The Chatham Road Presbyterian Church is the institutional setting for Lewis's satire of American Protestantism. It is not a place of spiritual challenge but of social confirmation. Belonging to the church — like belonging to the [[boosters-club]] — signals that one is a "Regular Guy" in good standing with the community. The church's Sunday School is run with corporate efficiency, its press-agent ([[kenneth-escott]]) promotes the pastor as one would promote a product, and its Men's Club excludes those who do not fit the demographic template of Zenith's ruling class (009-033, 014-036, 017-032).

## Key Scenes

- **Dr. Drew's sermon** — On a Sunday when Babbitt is unusually attentive, Drew preaches with "unusual eloquence" about the business of the church being to "increase the world's sum of happiness." The sermon merges Christian doctrine with Rotary Club values (016-014).
- **The Men's Club exclusion** — The club's speaker makes jokes about "Irish, Jewish, and Italian" citizens, revealing the ethnic boundaries of Christian fellowship in Zenith (014-036).
- **The Sunday School campaign** — Babbitt and Escott visit [[william-washington-eathorne]] to enlist his support for the Sunday School. The scene treats religious education as a corporate merger negotiation (017-007, 017-012).
- **Babbitt's return** — After his rebellion, Babbitt is seen at the Chatham Road Presbyterian Church, though "it is recorded that he did not listen" (034-016).

## Satirical Significance

The Chatham Road Presbyterian Church is Lewis's most sustained treatment of organized religion in Zenith. Like every other institution in the novel — the [[boosters-club]], the [[athletic-club]], the [[zenith-real-estate-board]] — the church exists to confirm rather than disturb the existing social order. Its theology is Presbyterian but its real religion is respectability.
