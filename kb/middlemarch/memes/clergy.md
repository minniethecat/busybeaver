# Clergy

The Church is a central institution in Middlemarch: a source of income, social standing, and moral authority. This page indexes every clergyman documented in the wiki, from the scholarly Edward Casaubon to the warm-hearted Camden Farebrother, the ambitious Mr. Tyke, and the evangelical Mr. Bulstrode whose piety masks a guilty past.

## Theological Figures

Eliot uses her clergymen to explore the relationship between institutional religion and genuine moral feeling. Casaubon's arid scholarship stands in contrast to Farebrother's humane wisdom; Bulstrode's self-serving evangelicalism is exposed as hypocrisy; and Dorothea Brooke, though not ordained, embodies the novel's deepest religious impulse — the desire to do good in the world.
