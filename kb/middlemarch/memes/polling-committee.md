# Polling Committee

The temporary political organization that manages [[mr-brooke|Mr. Brooke]]'s [[reform|Reform]] candidacy — a committee whose brief, inglorious existence is the novel's most concentrated satire of electoral politics in the unreformed era.

## Organization Overview

The Polling Committee is assembled hastily, from [[mr-brooke|Mr. Brooke]]'s political allies and [[will-ladislaw|Will Ladislaw]]'s reform-minded associates, to manage the mechanics of a Parliamentary campaign: canvassing, pamphleteering, organizing the public meeting. It is a makeshift organization — improvised, underfunded, uncertain of its own purpose — and its operations reveal, in comic miniature, the structural inadequacy of gentlemanly [[reform|Reform]] as a political force.

## Role in the Narrative

The Committee's work culminates in [[mr-brooke|Mr. Brooke]]'s disastrous speech from the White Hart balcony — after which there is, effectively, nothing more to manage. The campaign collapses; the Committee dissolves; the [[reform|Reform]] cause in [[middlemarch-town|Middlemarch]] reverts to its previous condition of vague sympathy without institutional expression. The episode is a study in the gap between political intention and political effect — a gap that the novel suggests is structural, not personal.

## Key Members

- **[[mr-brooke|Mr. Brooke]]**: Candidate — the Committee's reason for being and its fatal weakness
- **[[will-ladislaw|Will Ladislaw]]**: Political secretary — the Committee's only member with genuine conviction
