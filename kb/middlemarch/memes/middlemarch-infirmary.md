# Middlemarch Infirmary

The town's existing medical institution — distinct from the New Hospital — where Lydgate's appointment as superintendent sets off the professional conflicts that will define his [[middlemarch-town|Middlemarch]] career.

## Organization Overview

The Middlemarch Infirmary is the established charitable medical institution, governed by a General Board drawn from the town's leading citizens. "When the General Board of the Infirmary had met" (018-008) — the Board's deliberations are the arena in which the town's medical and clerical politics play out. The Infirmary is not Lydgate's creation; it predates him, and its existing structures — professional, political, and financial — are precisely what his reforming ambition must contend with.

## Role in the Narrative

Lydgate's election as superintendent of the Infirmary is an early triumph that proves hollow. His refusal to dispense drugs — a principled rejection of the apothecary model — alienates his colleagues, who depend on drug sales for their income. The chaplaincy dispute — whether the Infirmary chaplain should be Mr. Tyke (the evangelical candidate, backed by Bulstrode) or Mr. Farebrother (the broad-church incumbent) — draws Lydgate into [[middlemarch-town|Middlemarch]]'s religious factions. Lydgate "continued to work" (018-045) at the Infirmary, but the institution that should have been the platform for his career becomes instead one more theater of his frustration.

## Key Members

- **[[tertius-lydgate|Tertius Lydgate]]**: Superintendent — his reforming ambition brings him into conflict with the established practitioners
- **[[nicholas-bulstrode|Nicholas Bulstrode]]**: Board member and benefactor — his influence shapes Infirmary politics
- **Mr. Tyke**: Evangelical candidate for chaplain — Bulstrode's preferred choice
- **[[camden-farebrother|Camden Farebrother]]**: Broad-church clergyman — Lydgate's preferred candidate, ultimately displaced
