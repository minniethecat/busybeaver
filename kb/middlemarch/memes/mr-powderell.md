# Mr. Powderell

**Mr. Powderell** is a retired iron-monger in Middlemarch, among the town's older generation of tradesmen. He appears in two distinct contexts: first in a medical consultation with [[tertius-lydgate|Dr. Lydgate]], and later at the auction of [[peter-featherstone|Peter Featherstone]]'s possessions.

## Medical Consultation

Mr. Powderell first appears during the clerical dinner at [[mr-bulstrode|Bulstrode]]'s, where the conversation turns to Lydgate's medical capabilities. The "old Mr. Powderell" — described as "a retired iron-monger of some standing" — expresses scepticism about the young doctor's methods:

> "Ho, ho! Doctor," said old Mr. Powderell [...], "I hope you are not one of those who would set up a new wisdom against the established." [018-016]

He is "sad and silent" when the conversation turns toward the contentious question of medical reform [018-025].

## At the Auction

Mr. Powderell's most vivid moment comes during the auction of Featherstone's goods conducted by Mr. Trumbull. When a picture is put up for sale, Powderell is "much impressed" and asks who painted it [060-019]. Upon learning the painter is unknown, he rises to the occasion:

> "I'll bid a pound!" said Mr. Powderell, in a tone of resolved emotion, as of a man ready to put himself in the breach. [060-021]

The narrator adds, with characteristic irony: "Whether from awe or pity, nobody raised the price on him" [060-021]. The episode captures Powderell's blend of civic impulse and comedic self-importance.

## Character

Though a minor figure, Mr. Powderell represents the established Middlemarch tradesman of the older generation — conservative in medical matters, "constant[ly] charitable" [045-033], and given to small but self-conscious acts of public participation. He is part of the rich tapestry of minor characters that gives Middlemarch its texture.
