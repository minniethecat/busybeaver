# Pinkerton

Pinkerton is the sitting Tory Member of Parliament for Middlemarch, an established political figure whose anticipated resignation opens the seat that Mr. Brooke contests on the Whig-Reform side. Though he never appears directly in the novel, his presence shapes the political storyline of the later chapters (006-016).

## Character Overview

Pinkerton represents the old Tory interest in Middlemarch politics — the established order against which Reform candidates must contend. By the time the election plot unfolds in earnest, Pinkerton is ready to step down, creating a vacancy that draws multiple candidates. His political identity is defined by his Tory affiliation and his long tenure as the borough's representative. The early mention of Pinkerton's impending resignation comes through Mrs. Cadwallader, who wryly notes that Casaubon is spoken of as a possible candidate "on the Whig side when old Pinkerton resigns" (006-016).

## Role in the Narrative

Pinkerton's primary function is to establish the political landscape of Middlemarch. The election contest is framed as a fight "between Pinkerton the old Tory member, Bagster the new Whig member, and Brooke the independent Reform member" (051-015). His committee and supporters remain active in the borough, with "many of Pinkerton's committee whose opinions had a free bias" being canvassed by the various candidates (051-019). The Green Dragon inn serves as the headquarters for Pinkerton's supporters, where "his associates who sat for Pinkerton" gather (051-028).

Through Pinkerton, Eliot sketches the machinery of pre-Reform parliamentary politics: the local interests, the party loyalties, and the practical difficulties facing a newcomer like Mr. Brooke, whose bumbling campaign speech from the White Hart balcony proves disastrous despite Ladislaw's coaching.

## Relationships

- **Mr. Brooke** — political opponent; Brooke stands as an independent Reform candidate for Pinkerton's seat
- **Bagster** — the new Whig candidate who also contests the seat left vacant by Pinkerton (051-015)
- **Will Ladislaw** — Brooke's political aide, who coaches Brooke for the election contest triggered by Pinkerton's resignation (051-028)
