# Medicine and Science

Medicine in Middlemarch is a profession poised between two worlds: the old empiricism of bleeding and blistering, and the new scientific medicine that [[tertius-lydgate|Tertius Lydgate]] brings from Paris and Edinburgh. This page indexes every physician, medical institution, and scientific concept documented in the wiki, tracing the professional rivalries and intellectual conflicts that form one of the novel's major plotlines.

The tragedy of Lydgate is not that his scientific beliefs are wrong — they are right — but that he cannot make them prevail against the social forces arrayed against him. The established physicians of Middlemarch do not need to refute his arguments; they need only to withhold their cooperation, spread their gossip, and wait. Eliot's anatomy of professional failure is also a meditation on the conditions under which truth can gain a hearing in a world governed by interest and custom.
