# Places

The geography of Middlemarch maps a provincial world: the town itself with its shops, inns, and institutions; the surrounding country estates of the landed gentry; the parishes and villages beyond. Each place carries its own social meaning — who lives there, who visits, who aspires to belong — and Eliot uses setting with the precision of a social anatomist.
