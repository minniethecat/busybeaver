# Gentry

The landed gentry form the traditional ruling class of Middlemarch — owners of estates, patrons of parishes, and arbiters of social opinion. This page indexes the gentry families and individuals documented in the wiki, from the conscientious Sir James Chettam to the scatterbrained Mr. Brooke, whose half-hearted reformism marks the twilight of an old order.

The novel traces the slow transformation of the gentry class under pressure from commerce, reform, and the rising professional classes. Sir James, for all his good intentions, cannot halt the shift of power toward manufacturers like Vincy, financiers like Bulstrode, and experts like Lydgate — a quiet revolution that Middlemarch chronicles with precision and sympathy.
