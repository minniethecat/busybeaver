# Families

The world of Middlemarch is woven from family ties — marriages, inheritances, secret kinships, and the quiet obligations of blood. This page indexes the principal family networks documented in the wiki, each entry linking to a page that explores a particular family, its members, and its place in provincial society.

## Family Groups

The major clusters include the Vincys (manufacturers and mayors), the Brookes (landed gentry with a taste for reform), the Garths (honest working folk), the Casaubons (scholarship and property), the Bulstrodes (banking and piety), and the Featherstones (old money and contested wills). Many of these families intersect through marriage — Fred Vincy weds Mary Garth, Will Ladislaw's hidden parentage connects Bulstrode to the Casaubon circle, and Dorothea Brooke's choices reshape two family lines.
