# Middlemarch Corporation

The old, unreformed municipal corporation that governs [[middlemarch-town|Middlemarch]] — a closed circle of property and influence, resistant to change, whose operations represent everything the [[reform|Reform]] movement sought to dismantle.

## Organization Overview

The Middlemarch Corporation is the town's governing body under the unreformed municipal system that predates the 1835 Municipal Corporations Act. "Mr. Vincy was mayor of the old corporation in [[middlemarch-town|Middlemarch]]" (019-004) — the corporation is "old" in the technical sense (unreformed) and in the moral sense (resistant to change). Its membership is drawn from the town's propertied class: manufacturers, merchants, and gentlemen whose interests are served by the status quo. It is, in political terms, a closed shop.

## Role in the Narrative

The corporation functions largely in the background, but its presence shapes the novel's political landscape. Mr. Vincy's mayoralty gives his family social standing but no moral authority; his son Fred's financial recklessness and his daughter Rosamond's social climbing are, in part, products of a world in which status is inherited rather than earned. The corporation represents the old order that Lydgate's medical reform and [[mr-brooke|Mr. Brooke]]'s [[reform|political reform]] both — in their different ways — challenge.

## Key Members

- **Mr. Vincy**: Mayor — a manufacturer whose civic status masks his family's financial and moral precariousness
