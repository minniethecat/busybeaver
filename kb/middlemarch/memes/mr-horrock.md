# Mr. Horrock

Mr. Horrock is a veterinarian ("the vet") in Middlemarch who appears in scenes of horse trading and tavern sociability. Though a minor character, he is rendered memorable by Eliot's description of his peculiar talent for "terribly effective" silent communication (PN=023-016–018).

## Character Overview

Mr. Horrock moves in the same circles as the horse-dealer Bambridge, with whom he is frequently seen at the Green Dragon and other Middlemarch establishments. Eliot describes him as possessing "an apparent unfathomableness" that made his rare utterances carry disproportionate weight among his companions (PN=023-016). His most distinctive trait is his silence — a silence that, combined with an occasional knowing look, proves remarkably persuasive.

## Role in the Narrative

Mr. Horrock appears most prominently in Chapter 23, during Fred Vincy's ill-fated horse purchase. When Fred asks him about a horse, Mr. Horrock's silent response is described as "terribly effective" — "a mixture of laconicism and silence was the basis of Mr. Horrock's professional success" (PN=023-016–018). His wordless communication convinces onlookers of his expertise more effectively than any verbal endorsement could.

He reappears in Chapter 60 amid the gossip surrounding the Raffles scandal. The narrator notes that "the current carried even Mr. Horrock with it," suggesting that even this habitually silent man was drawn into the general speculation about Bulstrode's past (PN=060-016). He is also present when Lydgate plays billiards, observing the stranger who has become the subject of town talk (PN=060-025).

## Relationships

Mr. Horrock associates primarily with **[[mr-bambridge]]**, the horse-dealer, with whom he shares both professional interests and tavern hours. He crosses paths with **[[fred-vincy]]** during the horse-trading episode and encounters **[[tertius-lydgate]]** at social gatherings. His silence stands in pointed contrast to the voluble gossip of Middlemarch society.
