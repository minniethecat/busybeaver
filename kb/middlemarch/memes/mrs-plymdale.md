# Mrs. Plymdale

**Mrs. Plymdale** is a Middlemarch matron and the mother of [[ned-plymdale|Ned Plymdale]], one of [[rosamond-vincy|Rosamond Vincy]]'s suitors. She is a recurring figure in the novel's social landscape, known for her sharp tongue, round-eyed curiosity, and active participation in the town's gossip networks.

## Character and Appearance

Mrs. Plymdale is described as "a round-eyed sharp little woman" who prided herself on speaking her mind [031-018]. She maintained a "long-standing intimacy" with Mrs. Bulstrode, with whom she shared "nearly the same preferences in silks, patterns for underclothing, china-ware, and clergymen" [031-016].

Her social vigilance is evident in her early speculation about Rosamond's marriage prospects. Visiting Mrs. Bulstrode, she remarked pointedly about Rosamond's connection to [[tertius-lydgate|Lydgate]]:

> "If I am to speak my mind," said Mrs. Plymdale, with emphasis, "I must say, Mr. Lydgate ought never to have been allowed to resort to the Vincys." [031-020]

## Ned's Disappointment

When Rosamond chose Lydgate over her son Ned, Mrs. Plymdale was wounded but pragmatic. She consoled herself that Ned "could certainly better afford to marry than people who have their way to make" [031-022]. Her protective maternal instinct coexisted with a tendency toward social scorekeeping.

## Later Appearances

When Rosamond later visited Mrs. Plymdale during her marriage difficulties, Mrs. Plymdale received her with a mixture of sympathy and vindicated superiority. She noted that Rosamond looked "so pale and thin," though her "native sharpness [was] softened by a genuine sense of relief that her Ned had escaped" [064-039][064-042].

Mrs. Plymdale also appears in the novel's wider gossip scenes, including at [[mr-bulstrode|Bulstrode]]'s downfall, where — "falcon-faced" — she pronounced judgment on matters of religion and reputation [074-012].
