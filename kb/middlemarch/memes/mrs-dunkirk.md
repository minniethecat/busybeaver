# Mrs. Dunkirk

Mrs. Dunkirk, born Sarah Dunkirk, was the first wife of Nicholas Bulstrode. She was the daughter of a wealthy Dissenting family and the mother of Will Ladislaw's mother (also named Sarah Dunkirk), making her Will Ladislaw's maternal grandmother (PN=060-030, 061-044).

## Character Overview

The Dunkirk family was prominent among the Dissenting community. Mrs. Dunkirk's father, Mr. Dunkirk, was described as "the richest man in the congregation" and the family's wealth was built on a business that — as Raffles later reveals — involved receiving stolen goods (PN=061-022–025). Mrs. Dunkirk herself is described in Bulstrode's account as "a simple, religious woman" who was unaware of the family business's true nature.

## Role in the Narrative

Although Mrs. Dunkirk never appears directly in the novel, she is central to Bulstrode's backstory, which unravels dramatically in Chapters 60–61. After her father died and her husband Bulstrode took control of the family business, Mrs. Dunkirk remained unaware of its criminal connections. She died before the truth came to light.

The key revelation comes when Raffles confronts Bulstrode: "Your mother's name was Sarah Dunkirk, and that she ran away from her home" (PN=061-044). This concerns Mrs. Dunkirk's daughter — also Sarah — who had a child (Will Ladislaw) out of wedlock and fled the family. Bulstrode's concealment of Will's existence and his appropriation of the Dunkirk fortune form the moral core of the novel's subplot.

## Relationships

Mrs. Dunkirk was the wife of **[[nicholas-bulstrode]]**, marrying him when he was a young man in the Dissenting community. She was the mother of Sarah Dunkirk the younger, who became the mother of **[[will-ladislaw]]**. The Dunkirk wealth, which she inherited from her father, became the foundation of Bulstrode's banking fortune — a legacy that haunts him when Raffles threatens to expose its origins.
