# Joshua Rigg

The illegitimate son of [[peter-featherstone|Peter Featherstone]] whose unexpected inheritance of [[stone-court|Stone Court]] displaces [[fred-vincy|Fred Vincy]] and introduces the Raffles connection into the novel's plot.

## Character Overview

Joshua Rigg is the secret son of [[peter-featherstone|Peter Featherstone]], the product of a liaison that the old landowner had concealed from his respectable relatives. When Featherstone's second will is revealed after his death, Rigg emerges as the principal heir, inheriting [[stone-court|Stone Court]] and its lands to the astonishment and indignation of the Vincy family, who had expected Fred to inherit.

Rigg is described in terms that emphasize his foreignness to [[middlemarch-town|Middlemarch]] society. He is associated with the coastal town of Dogshole, and his appearance and manners mark him as an outsider. Eliot does not develop Rigg's interiority in any depth; he functions primarily as a plot device — the instrument by which Featherstone's fortune is diverted from its expected course and the link through which the sinister figure of [[john-raffles|John Raffles]] enters the narrative.

## Role in the Narrative

Rigg's most important narrative function is to introduce Raffles, his stepfather, into the story. Raffles's recognition of Rigg at [[stone-court|Stone Court]] sets in motion the chain of events that will ultimately expose Bulstrode's past and destroy both Bulstrode's reputation and Lydgate's career. Rigg himself is a transitional figure: he appears, disrupts the Featherstone inheritance plot, and then recedes, leaving behind the Raffles connection that will prove so consequential.

The contrast between Rigg and [[fred-vincy|Fred Vincy]] is thematically significant. Fred, the legitimate heir of provincial respectability, is displaced by Rigg, the illegitimate son from the margins of society. This reversal of expectations is one of the novel's many ironic commentaries on the relationship between moral worth and social position.

## Relationships

- **[[peter-featherstone|Peter Featherstone]]** — His father, whose secret acknowledgment of this illegitimate son overturns the expectations of the Vincy family.
- **[[john-raffles|John Raffles]]** — His stepfather, the disreputable figure whose appearance at [[stone-court|Stone Court]] initiates [[raffles-scandal|the Bulstrode scandal]].
- **[[fred-vincy|Fred Vincy]]** — The displaced heir whose disappointment at losing [[stone-court|Stone Court]] forces him toward a more honest life.
- **[[nicholas-bulstrode|Nicholas Bulstrode]]** — The connection through Raffles that links Rigg to the novel's central scandal.
