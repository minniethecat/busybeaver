# Beyond Good and Evil

## Overview

Friedrich Nietzsche's authored *Beyond Good and Evil: Prelude to a Philosophy of the Future* (1886), the primary text of this wiki. The work represents Nietzsche's mature philosophical position: a critique of the metaphysical tradition from Plato through Schopenhauer, a genealogical analysis of morality, and a call for new philosophers strong enough to create values beyond the opposition of good and evil.

## In *Beyond Good and Evil*

Beyond Good and Evil is the subject of this wiki, not merely a topic within it. The 9 chapters, Preface, and poetic epilogue (From the Heights) constitute the corpus from which all PN-cited passages are drawn. Self-reference is inevitable: every concept page, every person page, every school page bears on the interpretation of this work.

## Summary

The work consists of a Preface (Truth as woman — the problem of dogmatism), followed by 9 chapters: I. On the Prejudices of Philosophers, II. The Free Spirit, III. The Religious Mood, IV. Apophthegms and Interludes, V. The Natural History of Morals, VI. We Scholars, VII. Our Virtues, VIII. Peoples and Fatherlands, IX. What is Noble? The book closes with the poem From the Heights. Written in Nietzsche's characteristic aphoristic style, it ranges across epistemology, psychology, ethics, politics, and aesthetics.

## Connection to Nietzsche's Thought

Beyond Good and Evil is Nietzsche's most comprehensive single work — more systematic than the aphoristic collections, less polemical than the late works. It inaugurates the mature phase of Nietzsche's thinking and introduces the concepts — will to truth, free spirit, master and slave morality, the order of rank, the philosophers of the future — that define his philosophical legacy.
