##### By F W Nietzsche

### Translated by L. A. Magnus

[010-003]1.

[010-004]MIDDAY of Life! Oh, season of delight!

[010-005]My summer's park!

[010-006]Uneaseful joy to look, to lurk, to hark—

[010-007]I peer for friends, am ready day and night,—

[010-008]Where linger ye, my friends? The time is right!

[010-009]2.

[010-010]Is not the glacier's grey today for you

[010-011]Rose-garlanded?

[010-012]The brooklet seeks you, wind, cloud, with longing thread

[010-013]And thrust themselves yet higher to the blue,

[010-014]To spy for you from farthest eagle's view.

[010-015]3.

[010-016]My table was spread out for you on high—

[010-017]Who dwelleth so

[010-018]Star-near, so near the grisly pit below?—

[010-019]My realm—what realm hath wider boundary?

[010-020]My honey—who hath sipped its fragrancy?

[010-021]4.

[010-022]Friends, ye are there! Woe me,—yet I am not

[010-023]He whom ye seek?

[010-024]Ye stare and stop—better your wrath could speak!

[010-025]I am not I? Hand, gait, face, changed? And what

[010-026]I am, to you my friends, now am I not?

[010-027]5.

[010-028]Am I an other? Strange am I to Me?

[010-029]Yet from Me sprung?

[010-030]A wrestler, by himself too oft [[ego|self]]-wrung?

[010-031]Hindering too oft my own self's potency,

[010-032]Wounded and hampered by self-victory?

[010-033]6.

[010-034]I sought where-so the wind blows keenest. There

[010-035]I learned to dwell

[010-036]Where no man dwells, on lonesome ice-lorn fell,

[010-037]And unlearned Man and God and curse and prayer?

[010-038]Became a ghost haunting the glaciers bare?

[010-039]7.

[010-040]Ye, my old friends! Look! Ye turn pale, filled o'er

[010-041]With love and fear!

[010-042]Go! Yet not in wrath. Ye could ne'er live here.

[010-043]Here in the farthest realm of ice and scaur,

[010-044]A huntsman must one be, like chamois soar.

[010-045]8.

[010-046]An evil huntsman was I? See how taut

[010-047]My bow was bent!

[010-048]Strongest was he by whom such bolt were sent—

[010-049]Woe now! That arrow is with peril fraught,

[010-050]Perilous as none.—Have yon safe home ye sought!

[010-051]9.

[010-052]Ye go! Thou didst endure enough, oh, heart;—

[010-053]Strong was thy hope;

[010-054]Unto new friends thy portals widely ope,

[010-055]Let old ones be. Bid memory depart!

[010-056]Wast thou young then, now—better young thou art!

[010-057]10.

[010-058]What linked us once together, one hope's tie—

[010-059](Who now doth con

[010-060]Those lines, now fading, Love once wrote thereon?)—

[010-061]Is like a parchment, which the hand is shy

[010-062]To touch—like crackling leaves, all seared, all dry.

[010-063]11.

[010-064]Oh! Friends no more! They are—what name for those?—

[010-065]Friends' phantom-flight

[010-066]Knocking at my heart's window-pane at night,

[010-067]Gazing on me, that speaks "We were" and goes,—

[010-068]Oh, withered words, once fragrant as the rose!

[010-069]12.

[010-070]Pinings of youth that might not understand!

[010-071]For which I pined,

[010-072]Which I deemed changed with me, kin of my kind:

[010-073]But they grew old, and thus were doomed and banned:

[010-074]None but new kith are native of my land!

[010-075]13.

[010-076]Midday of life! My second youth's delight!

[010-077]My summer's park!

[010-078]Unrestful joy to long, to lurk, to hark!

[010-079]I peer for friends!—am ready day and night,

[010-080]For my new friends. Come! Come! The time is right!

[010-081]14.

[010-082]This song is done,—the sweet sad cry of rue

[010-083]Sang out its end;

[010-084]A wizard wrought it, he the timely friend,

[010-085]The midday-friend,—no, do not ask me who;

[010-086]At midday 'twas, when one became as two.

[010-087]15.

[010-088]We keep our Feast of Feasts, sure of our bourne,

[010-089]Our aims self-same:

[010-090]The Guest of Guests, friend Zarathustra, came!

[010-091]The world now laughs, the grisly veil was torn,

[010-092]And Light and Dark were one that wedding-morn.
