## Overview

Late on the night of [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s collapse, [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] telephoned [[hollander|Thomas Hollander]] to summon him to the Endicott house. Acting on [[roberts|Roberts]]'s information that Hollander was the only person [[herbert-endicott|Endicott]] spoke of in terms implying genuine friendship [011-045], Valcour asked Hollander to sit with the unconscious man until he regained consciousness. The call — and Hollander's subsequent arrival — brought him directly into the investigation, setting the stage for his later exposure as the man who would attempt to kill Endicott with a [[the-damascus-knife|Damascus steel knife]].

## The Telephone Call

[[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] refreshed his memory from his leather reference book and dialled Hollander's number at approximately 12:15 a.m. [011-044]. A man with a smooth, soft voice — later revealed to be a servant — answered and fetched Hollander to the telephone.

Valcour identified himself as a police lieutenant calling from the Endicott house [011-045]. When Hollander heard this, his first response was telling: "He's dead?" [011-046] — betraying an immediate assumption that Endicott had been killed. Valcour noted this reaction and asked pointedly, "Were you expecting him to be?" [011-047], but Hollander deflected.

Valcour explained that Endicott had suffered a heart attack brought on by sudden shock, that his condition was serious, and that [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]] insisted a friend be present when he regained consciousness [011-048]. When Hollander asked about [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]], Valcour explained she had been given a narcotic and was sleeping [011-049].

## Roberts's Role

[[roberts|Roberts]], [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]'s personal maid, was the source of the recommendation to contact Hollander. She told [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] that Hollander's name was "the only one she has ever heard spoken by Mr. Endicott in terms that would imply intimacy" [011-045]. This seemingly innocent piece of information drew Hollander into the heart of the investigation.

## Hollander's Agreement

Despite the late hour, Hollander agreed to come to the Endicott house within about an hour [011-050][011-051][011-052]. Valcour thanked him and ended the call [011-053][011-054]. The delay between the call and Hollander's arrival later became significant — it provided a window during which events at the house continued to unfold.

## Significance

The summoning of Hollander was a pivotal moment in the investigation. By bringing Hollander to Endicott's bedside, Valcour inadvertently placed the would-be murderer in the same room as his intended victim. [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]] later revealed that Hollander carried a [[the-damascus-knife|Damascus steel knife]], and Valcour came to suspect that Hollander was "probably just waiting for a chance to use it" [016-026]. The telephone call transformed Hollander from a name in a reference book to a central figure in the case — a man whose friendship with Endicott masked a deadly intent.
