## Overview

[[nurse-murrow|Nurse Murrow]] was assigned by [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Sanford Worth]] to maintain an overnight vigil at the bedside of the unconscious [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]. Her watch, lasting from late evening through the early morning hours, placed her at the center of the investigation's most critical moments — including the [[hollander-knife-attack-on-endicott|knife attack by Hollander]] and its violent aftermath.

## Assignment and Instructions

[[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]] gave Nurse Murrow her instructions after completing his medical arrangements. He emphasized the seriousness of Endicott's condition: "We cannot overestimate the importance of getting him past it safely" [008-032]. Murrow was to remain in Endicott's room, monitor his condition, and summon Worth immediately if Endicott showed any signs of regaining consciousness. Worth himself planned to sleep in the house to be available [008-032].

## The Atmosphere of the Watch

The assignment unsettled Nurse Murrow. Her professional experience had not prepared her for the peculiar circumstances — a possibly murdered patient, police detectives moving through the house, and an atmosphere of suspicion and fear. After the detectives left her alone, she locked the door and found herself "not a little atwitter," reflecting that her career had featured none of the romantic patients of popular fiction [010-042].

The formality of the police officers added to her discomfort. Their impersonal, official manner — addressing her stiffly as "ma'am" — created what she felt as a "talk-chilling quality," and she eventually gave up attempting conversation, settling "starchily in an armchair" with a lamp adjusted to shade her eyes [010-052].

## The Knife Attack

Nurse Murrow was present in Endicott's room when [[hollander|Thomas Hollander]] produced his [[the-damascus-knife|Damascus steel knife]] and moved toward the bed. The attack was prevented by the police guard — [[cassidy|Cassidy]] and Hansen shot Hollander before he could reach Endicott. In the chaotic aftermath, Nurse Murrow helped arrange an improvised cot for the wounded Hollander and provided medical attention [017-002].

The room, she observed, had taken on "an air about it of a shambles" [017-060] — a testament to how quickly the quiet night watch had erupted into violence.

## Professional Conduct Under Pressure

Despite her earlier discomfort, Nurse Murrow maintained professional composure throughout the crisis. She provided [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]] with accurate observations of Endicott's condition, assisted with the wounded Hollander, and continued her vigil even after the shooting. Her presence ensured that Endicott received continuous medical attention during the hours when the investigation was at its most volatile.

## Significance

Nurse Murrow's night watch represents the intersection of medical care and criminal investigation in the Endicott case. As the only person continuously present in Endicott's room, she was a potential witness to any further attack and a critical link in the chain of care. Her discomfort also highlights the psychological toll on those caught up in a murder investigation — ordinary professionals thrust into extraordinary circumstances.
