## Overview

Mrs. Herbert Endicott is the central figure through whom the murder mystery unfolds. A young, beautiful, and socially prominent New York woman, she lives with her husband [[herbert-endicott|Herbert]] in a well-appointed [[endicott-house|house]]. Her discovery of a [[the-threatening-note|threatening note]] and subsequent [[mrs-endicott-calls-police|call to the police]] sets the entire investigation in motion.

## Role in the Story

Mrs. Endicott is both the discoverer of the crime and a key suspect. At 8:37 p.m. on a Thursday evening, she calls [[new-york-police-department|police headquarters]] (Spring 3100) to report her husband missing (001-002). She directs [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] to her husband's room, where a crumpled body is [[discovery-of-endicotts-body|discovered in the cupboard]] (001-077). She admits during questioning that she had previously told her husband she wished to kill him (004-011), immediately placing herself under suspicion. She reveals Herbert was having an affair with [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]] (001-031), providing a potential motive. Her emotional state oscillates between controlled composure and nervous collapse throughout the investigation.

## Relationships

- **[[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]** (husband): A strained marriage marked by infidelity. Herbert was having an affair with [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]]. Mrs. Endicott describes him as "half animal" (005-039) and admits to threatening his life.
- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]** (investigator): A complex dynamic of suspect and interrogator. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] finds her "a curious, curious woman, with youth and beauty that almost passed belief" (004-002). She is alternately frank and evasive with him.
- **[[marge-myles|Marge Myles]]** (rival): Herbert's mistress, described by Mrs. Endicott as "foreign-looking — the Latin type" with "eyes like black opals" (001-036). She lives at [[marge-myles-apartment|her apartment]] on Fifty-eighth Street.
- **[[roberts|Roberts]]** (maid): Personal maid who assists Mrs. Endicott with dressing. [[roberts|Roberts]] appears visibly shaken after the body's discovery (004-003).
- **Jane** (maid): The maid who announces [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]'s arrival (001-016).

## Analysis

Mrs. Endicott embodies the classic murder mystery archetype of the beautiful wife who may or may not be a murderer. Her admission of wishing Herbert dead, combined with her knowledge of his affair and her controlled-yet-fragile demeanor, makes her the most obvious suspect. However, her forthrightness in revealing incriminating information also suggests either innocence or remarkable cunning. The broken nail on her left little finger, noticed by [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] as a "discrepancy" against her otherwise perfect appearance (001-037), hints at recent physical strain. Her sensitivity to voices and cultured aesthetic sensibilities provide a contrast to the brutality of the crime.
