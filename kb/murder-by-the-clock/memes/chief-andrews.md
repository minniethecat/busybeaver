## Summary

Chief Andrews is the head of the Homicide Bureau who responds to the initial report of [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s death. He arrives at the Endicott house with the medical examiner and a squad of detectives, but after the preliminary investigation yields no signs of forced entry and Endicott is revived, he delegates the case to [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]'s precinct and withdraws [[new-york-police-department|Central Office]] resources pending further developments.

## Role in the Investigation

Andrews first appears at the Endicott house shortly after the body is discovered. A well-built, alert-looking man of about fifty [003-045], he is acquainted with [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] and respects his reputation for extracting useful information from people. He tells [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] to "squeeze every drop that you can" from the witnesses [003-054].

After the medical examination, Andrews is skeptical that a crime has occurred at all. He stands by Endicott's bed looking at the waxlike body in shocked bewilderment [006-017]. He relays the medical examiner's theory that adrenaline might revive Endicott, citing a recent case in Queens where a man pronounced dead for six hours was revived [006-026].

## Departure from the Case

Finding no evidence of forced entry and no definitive proof of foul play, Andrews decides that the situation does not warrant a full [[new-york-police-department|Central Office]] investigation. He tells [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] that unless the adrenaline fails and an autopsy proves poison, it is [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s precinct case to handle [006-038]. He leaves [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] with instructions to investigate the household and departs [006-038], later receiving a promised report on Endicott's successful revival [008-017].

## Significance

Andrews's decision to withdraw [[new-york-police-department|Central Office]] resources leaves [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] with limited support and forces him to conduct the investigation with only the precinct officers at his disposal. This organizational dynamic shapes the entire investigation, as [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] must rely on his own methods and the assistance of officers like [[cassidy|Cassidy]], Hansen, and O'Brian rather than the full machinery of the Homicide Bureau.
