## Overview

The Endicott House is the primary setting for the murder of [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]. A well-appointed multi-story residence in New York City, the house reflects the wealth and social standing of its owners while serving as the enclosed stage upon which the crime and subsequent investigation unfold.

## Significance

The house is the exclusive crime scene — both the murder and the [[discovery-of-endicotts-body|discovery of the body]] occur within its walls. The layout of the second floor proves crucial to the investigation: the corridor connecting [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]'s bedroom, her dressing room, and Herbert's bedroom establishes the spatial relationship between the key actors on the night of the murder (001-042). The fact that the body was found inside the house, despite Herbert being fully dressed for outdoors, is one of the central puzzles of the case (002-055).

## Layout Analysis

The second floor configuration is central to the investigation. [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]'s dressing room is toward the rear of the corridor, her bedroom is beyond it, and [[herbert-endicotts-bedroom|Herbert's room]] is directly across the corridor (001-042). A bathroom connects to [[herbert-endicotts-bedroom|Herbert's room]] (002-011). The [[endicott-house-balcony|rear balcony]] is accessible only by straddling the window sill (002-041). The enclosed nature of the crime scene — no signs of forced entry — suggests the murderer was either a resident or someone admitted to the house.
