## Overview

The discovery of [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s body is the inciting event of the investigation. While searching Herbert's bedroom for additional evidence of [[the-threatening-note|the threatening note]], [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] opens a cupboard door and finds Endicott's body "crumpled into one corner" (001-077). The discovery transforms what began as a worried wife's inquiry into a full-scale homicide investigation.

## Chronology

1. **~8:37 p.m.**: [[mrs-endicott-calls-police|Mrs. Endicott calls police headquarters]], dialling Spring 3100 (001-007)
2. **~9:00 p.m.**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] arrives at the [[endicott-house|Endicott house]] (001-022)
3. **~9:10 p.m.**: [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] shows [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] the threatening note on Herbert's desk (001-048)
4. **~9:15 p.m.**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] asks about checking Endicott's pockets for additional evidence (001-075)
5. **~9:16 p.m.**: [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] indicates the cupboard where Herbert's clothes are kept (001-076)
6. **~9:17 p.m.**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] opens the cupboard door. "The large hulk of a man crumpled into one corner of the cupboard arrested [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]'s attention" (001-077)

## Participants

- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]** (discoverer): Opens the cupboard while searching for evidence, immediately recognizes the body. He touches the body's cheek and notes "the flesh was clammy under the touch of his tentative fingers" (001-077).
- **[[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]** (witness): Present in the room when the body is discovered. Her initial reaction is strikingly detached: she comments on her husband's missing hat (001-077).

## Significance

The discovery reshapes the entire case. Before this moment, [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] was merely a worried wife reporting a missing husband. Afterward, she becomes a potential suspect in a murder investigation. The location of the body — inside the [[endicott-house|house]], in a cupboard, yet Herbert is fully dressed for outdoors — creates the central paradox of the crime. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s immediate reaction is to secure the scene, call for backup, and begin the formal [[police-homicide-procedure|homicide procedure]] (001-078). The fact that [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] was present when the body was found, and her curiously detached reaction, becomes a significant point of interest for the investigation.

## Evidence

- **The body**: [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]], fully dressed in overcoat, crumpled in the cupboard
- **[[the-threatening-note|The threatening note]]**: Found earlier on Herbert's desk (001-048)
- **The missing hat**: [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]'s first observation upon seeing the body (001-077)
- **Physical condition**: "Waxy pallor of the face's skin," no visible marks of violence (002-051)
