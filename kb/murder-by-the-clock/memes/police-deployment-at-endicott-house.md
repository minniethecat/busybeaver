## Overview

Following the discovery of [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s body, [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] systematically deploys police resources to secure the [[endicott-house|Endicott residence]]. Starting from a single telephone call from [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] to police headquarters (002-017), [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] summons the Homicide Bureau chief, the medical examiner, and a detail of uniformed officers to establish a perimeter and begin the investigation.

## Sequence of Events

- **Initial notification**: [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] dials police headquarters (Spring 3100) and her call is transferred to the precinct station (001-007)(001-009).
- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] arrives**: The maid announces "A lieutenant from the precinct station, madam" (001-016). [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] examines the body and determines Endicott is dead (002-002).
- **Summoning medical help**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] telephones [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]]'s office and requests his attendance alongside the medical examiner (002-014).
- **Calling [[new-york-police-department|Central Office]]**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] "dialled the [[new-york-police-department|Central Office]] and, in a suddenly most efficient voice, gave the requisite information" (002-017).
- **Deploying uniformed officers**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] calls his precinct station and tells "the sergeant at the desk to send over a detail of five men in uniform" (002-017).
- **Arrival of personnel**: "The homicide chief and the medical examiner came in, followed by a squad of men" (003-043). Five men from the station house "flooded into the room" — described as "well built, alert, and their uniforms were immaculate" (003-002)(003-003).
- **Stationing guards**: Officer [[obrian|O'Brian]] is posted on guard at the door (007-003). [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] later keeps "a couple of men posted all night in the bathroom" to secure the crime scene (008-037).

## Personnel Deployed

- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]** — Homicide investigator, takes command of the scene.
- **Chief of the Homicide Bureau** — Summoned by [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] to oversee the investigation (002-018).
- **Medical Examiner** — Called to examine Endicott's body and determine cause of death (002-018).
- **[[cassidy|Cassidy]]** — One of the uniformed officers from the station house detail.
- **[[obrian|O'Brian]]** — Officer assigned to guard duty at the door (007-003).
- **Five uniformed officers** — The detail dispatched from the precinct station (002-017)(003-002).

## Significance

The police deployment at [[endicott-house|Endicott House]] establishes the investigative framework for the entire novel. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s swift and methodical response — sealing the house, summoning the medical examiner, and posting guards — prevents contamination of the crime scene and sets the stage for the systematic unraveling of the mystery. The event also introduces the police procedural elements that distinguish *Murder by the Clock* as a methodical detective story rather than a sensational crime thriller.
