## Overview

Cassidy is a young detective from the [[new-york-police-department|New York Police Department]] assigned by [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] to guard the [[endicott-house|Endicott house]] during the investigation. The "starchiest and youngest looking" of the officers present (003-004), Cassidy is an Irish Catholic whose deep superstition about death repeatedly clashes with his professional duties. Despite his terror, he remains at his post through the long night and ultimately fires the shot that wounds [[hollander|Thomas Hollander]] during the climactic confrontation.

## Role in the Story

Cassidy is assigned to stay inside [[herbert-endicotts-bedroom|Endicott's bedroom]] while O'Brian guards the front door and Hansen runs messages (003-004). Alone with [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]], he provides the detective with an informal sounding board — [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] asks him how the servants are reacting (003-010) and how [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]'s voice sounded to him (003-014).

Cassidy's defining trait is his superstitious terror. When he learns Endicott's body shows no marks, he asks if it is "one of them Western hammer murders" and edges away from the cupboard (003-021). During the [[adrenaline-injection|adrenaline revival]], he calls the procedure "sacrilege" (006-006) and positions himself as close to the exit as possible (006-025). Left alone with the revived Endicott — "that live corpse" — he crosses his fingers for protection (006-069).

Despite his fears, Cassidy is competent. He correctly absorbs [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s instructions about the medical chain of command (010-028), and during the crisis he and Hansen fire the shots that bring down Hollander: a bullet to the right wrist, while Hansen hits the right shoulder (016-081). When the pounding on the door becomes "hysterical," Cassidy opens it to find [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]] and the household servants (017-002).

## Relationships

- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]** (superior): [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] treats Cassidy with a mix of amusement and reliance. He uses him as a sounding board and trusts him with critical guard duty, while gently mocking his superstitions: "Forget the fact that you're a superstitious Irishman, Cassidy, and remember that you're a cop" (006-011).
- **Hansen** (partner): Fellow officer sharing the night watch. They coordinate during the shooting of Hollander.
- **[[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]]** (medical authority): Cassidy finds Worth's medical procedures deeply unsettling.
- **[[hollander|Thomas Hollander]]** (suspect): Exchanges a brief nod with Hollander during the vigil (013-059). Later fires the shot that wounds him.

## Analysis

Cassidy functions as both comic relief and a humanising presence in the investigation. His superstitious terror at the "live corpse" provides moments of levity amid the novel's grim atmosphere. Yet his character also serves a structural purpose: his fear underscores the genuine eeriness of Endicott's condition — a man neither fully dead nor fully alive. Cassidy's superstition is not irrational in context; the situation is genuinely uncanny.

His preference for tea over coffee (010-020) and his habit of crossing his fingers (006-069) are the small, specific details that ground him as a recognisable human being rather than a stock police extra. When the crisis arrives, he performs his duty without hesitation, proving that his earlier fears did not compromise his competence.
