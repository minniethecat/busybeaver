## Overview

Officer Hansen is one of the uniformed police officers assigned to guard the Endicott house during [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]'s investigation. He is described as one of the "starchiest and youngest looking" of the officers on the scene [003-004]. Hansen has worked under [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] on a previous case, and his colleague O'Brian describes [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] as "cuckoo," to which Hansen replies, "Cuckoo as a fox" — indicating his respect for the detective's methods [003-005][003-006].

## Role in the Story

Hansen serves as a reliable background presence throughout the investigation, stationed at various posts around the Endicott house. He is summoned, stationed, and given orders by [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] across multiple chapters [010-006][010-009][010-011]. His most significant action comes in Chapter 16, when he fires the shot that wounds [[hollander|Thomas Hollander]] in the right shoulder as Hollander attempts to stab the unconscious [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]] with a knife [016-081]. Later, Hansen is involved in a minor dispute with [[cassidy|Cassidy]] over which of them fired the shot that hit Hollander first [027-011][027-012].

## Key Appearances

**Chapter 3 — Initial Deployment**: Hansen is stationed at the front door alongside O'Brian, assigned to carry messages [003-004].

**Chapter 10 — Summoned by [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] calls Hansen upstairs. Hansen reports formally: "Lieutenant, sir, Officer Hansen reporting" [010-006][010-009]. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] questions him about what has transpired in the house [010-013].

**Chapter 16 — The Shooting of Hollander**: When Hollander attempts to stab the unconscious Endicott, [[cassidy|Cassidy]] shatters Hollander's right wrist with a bullet, and "Hansen's shot caught him in the right shoulder" [016-081]. This is Hansen's most consequential action in the novel.

**Chapter 22 — [[roberts|Roberts]]'s Gun**: Hansen brings [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] a revolver recovered from [[roberts|Roberts]]'s room, which becomes a key piece of evidence [022-021][022-022].

**Chapter 27 — The Dispute**: Hansen and [[cassidy|Cassidy]] argue over who fired first during the Hollander shooting, a minor comic beat in the investigation's closing stages [027-011][027-012].

## Relationships

- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]** — Commanding officer; Hansen follows orders loyally and has worked with him before [003-006][010-009].
- **[[cassidy|Cassidy]]** — Fellow officer; they are frequently paired together on guard duty and share a friendly rivalry [003-005][027-012].
- **[[obrian|O'Brian]]** — Fellow officer stationed at the front door [003-004][003-005].
- **[[hollander|Thomas Hollander]]** — Hansen's shot wounds Hollander during the knife attack on Endicott [016-081].

## Analysis

Hansen is a functional character who represents the competent, unobtrusive presence of the police force throughout the investigation. His presence allows [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] to delegate tasks and maintain control of the crime scene. Hansen's role in the shooting of Hollander marks one of the novel's few moments of direct physical action, contrasting with the otherwise cerebral, interview-based detective work that defines [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s method.
