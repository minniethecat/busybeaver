## Overview

In Chapter 9, titled "Queer Deeps," [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] summons [[roberts|Roberts]], the Endicott household secretary, for questioning. With [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] under sedation and unavailable, [[roberts|Roberts]] becomes a critical source of information about the household's internal dynamics and the events leading up to Endicott's collapse.

## The Interview

- **Summoning [[roberts|Roberts]]**: After [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] is sedated, [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] turns to [[roberts|Roberts]] as the next most knowledgeable person in the household. [[roberts|Roberts]] enters "indifferently" and sits down (009-002).
- **Opening approach**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] begins "with the usual distant skirmishing before launching the main body of his attack" — a tactical interviewing style that probes for inconsistencies before pressing key questions (009-002).
- **Key topic — Endicott's condition**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] explains he wants to discuss "[[herbert-endicott|Mr. Endicott]] — concerning his condition" and relays [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]]'s medical statement (009-003).
- **[[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]'s unavailability**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] notes that "[[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] is under the influence of a narcotic and will not be available before tomorrow morning at the earliest," making [[roberts|Roberts]] the primary source of information for the night (009-003).

## Significance

The [[roberts|Roberts]] interview represents [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s methodical approach to witness examination. Rather than confronting suspects directly, he gathers background information from peripheral household members, building a picture of relationships and motives before narrowing his focus. [[roberts|Roberts]], as secretary, occupies a unique position — close enough to the family to observe intimate details but professionally detached enough to provide relatively objective testimony.
