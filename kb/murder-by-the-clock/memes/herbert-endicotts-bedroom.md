## Overview

[[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s bedroom is the central crime scene of the murder investigation. A second-floor room in the [[endicott-house|Endicott House]], it is where [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]] was confined as an invalid under the care of [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Sanford Worth]] and where his body was discovered crumpled inside the room's cupboard (001-077). The room is notable for its heavy mahogany furnishings — a deliberate contrast to the lighter, more feminine decor of the rest of the house (001-043).

## Location and Layout

The bedroom is on the second floor, directly across the corridor from [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]'s bedroom (001-042). A cupboard is built into one wall, adjacent to a bathroom with "coral-coloured" fittings (002-011). A large, flat-topped desk sits before a rear window overlooking the garden (001-044). A long mahogany chest is placed before the window (008-005, 013-016).

The room's relationship to the [[endicott-house-balcony|rear balcony]] is crucial: the fatal shot was fired from the balcony through this very window (021-031). The window sash was noted to be "up about" a certain height during the investigation (013-016), and [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] observed [[cassidy|Cassidy]] seated on the mahogany chest beside the window (018-019).

## Furnishings and Atmosphere

The bedroom is furnished in "heavy mahoganies" that are described as standing at "sharp variance" with the rest of the house (001-043). This deliberate heaviness — dark wood, masculine, severe — creates a visual separation between Herbert's world and that of his wife. The room feels institutional: it is referred to repeatedly as a "sickroom" (010-023, 010-042), and the presence of [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]]'s medical equipment reinforces its clinical atmosphere.

Key furnishings:
- **The flat-topped desk** (001-044): Where [[the-threatening-note|the threatening note]] was found, written on paper "such as they use at grocers' or small stationers'" (001-050).
- **The mahogany chest** (008-005, 013-016): A long chest beneath the rear window, used as a seat by police officers and, in the novel's final scene, by the sleeping [[cassidy|Cassidy]] (030-003).
- **The cupboard** (001-076–081): The small closet where Endicott's body was discovered, crumpled into one corner.

## The Cupboard Crime Scene

The cupboard is the site of the body's discovery. On the night of the murder, [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] directs [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] to the cupboard, saying "His clothes are in that cupboard" (001-076). When [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] opens it, he finds not clothes but "something" crumpled in one corner — [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s body, giving him "a severe shock" (001-077).

The position of the body within the cupboard is significant: Endicott, who was fully dressed as if prepared to go out (002-055), had been dragged and stuffed into the small space. There were no signs of a struggle "in order to drag him into the cupboard" (002-054), which puzzles [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]. The body's position "as its position in the cupboard would permit" (003-044) is photographed and documented.

A fingerprint man examines the cupboard (003-044), and later [[obrian|O'Brian]] is stationed to guard the room.

## The Investigation Focus

[[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s investigation of the bedroom proceeds methodically:

- **001-042–044**: Initial room survey — corridor access, desk, rear window orientation
- **001-076–081**: Discovery of the body in the cupboard
- **002-051–055**: Night examination by flashlight — [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] finds no wound on Endicott's head "in the cupboard, nor in the bedroom" (002-055), which strikes him as strange since the body had been dragged
- **003-020–044**: Detailed examination of the cupboard and body position
- **006-064**: Herbert's "sudden appearance in the bedroom" — a moment that "strengthened" [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s suspicions
- **007-031**: The doctor had left something "in Endicott's bedroom" — the cupboard [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] had mentioned
- **010-023**: Referred to as "a sickroom over which [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]] has" authority

## The Sickroom Context

The bedroom doubles as a sickroom throughout the novel. [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]], suffering from a heart condition, is bedridden and under constant medical supervision (010-023). The room thus serves a dual function: private bedroom and quasi-hospital ward. This ambiguity — a sick man in a locked house, killed while supposedly incapacitated — is one of the narrative's central tensions.
