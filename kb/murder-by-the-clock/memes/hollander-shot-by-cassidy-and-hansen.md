## Overview

During [[hollander-knife-attack-on-endicott|Hollander's knife attack]] on the unconscious [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]], police officers [[cassidy|Cassidy]] and [[hansen|Hansen]] open fire to stop the assault. This is the novel's most violent moment, transforming the investigation from a contained domestic mystery into a scene of open bloodshed.

## The Shooting

- **[[cassidy|Cassidy]] fires first**: His bullet "shattered Hollander's right wrist," disarming him and preventing the knife from reaching Endicott (016-081).
- **Hansen follows**: Hansen's shot "caught him in the right shoulder," further incapacitating the attacker (016-081).
- **Both officers wounded**: In spite of [[nurse-murrow|Nurse Murrow]]'s protests, both officers sustain leg wounds in the chaotic exchange (016-081).
- **The weapon**: Hollander's knife is later described as "Damascus steel" that he "uses for a paper knife" (014-075).

## Aftermath

- Hollander is carried to an improvised cot arranged by [[nurse-murrow|Nurse Murrow]] for medical treatment (017-002).
- The room takes on "an air about it of a shambles" — a scene of disarray reflecting the violence that has erupted (017-002)(017-060).
- A minor dispute arises between [[cassidy|Cassidy]] and Hansen over which of them fired first, becoming a recurring motif in later chapters (027-011)(027-012).
- [[obrian|O'Brian]] telephones the precinct station and reports the occurrence (016-019).

## Significance

The shooting of Hollander represents the moment when the Endicott case crosses the line from investigation to active violence. It confirms [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s earlier warning to [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]] that Hollander was dangerous (016-026), and it eliminates Hollander as an immediate threat while creating new complications — a wounded suspect requiring medical care and two injured officers.
