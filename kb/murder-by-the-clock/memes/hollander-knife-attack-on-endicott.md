## Overview

In Chapter 16, [[hollander|Thomas Hollander]] — who has been permitted to sit with the unconscious [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]] as a trusted friend — reveals his true intentions by producing a knife and attempting to stab Endicott. The attack is foiled by the police officers guarding the room, and Hollander is shot by [[cassidy|Cassidy]] and [[hansen|Hansen]]. The knife is later described as "Damascus steel" that Hollander "uses for a paper knife" [014-075].

## Chronology

- **Pre-attack suspicion**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]], realizing the danger, tells [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]] that Hollander "is armed with a knife and that he is probably just waiting for a chance to use it" [016-026].
- **The attack begins**: Hollander produces the knife and moves toward the unconscious Endicott.
- **[[cassidy|Cassidy]] fires**: A bullet from [[cassidy|Cassidy]]'s gun "shattered Hollander's right wrist" [016-081].
- **Hansen fires**: Hansen's shot "caught him in the right shoulder" [016-081].
- **Aftermath**: Hollander is carried to an improvised cot arranged by [[nurse-murrow|Nurse Murrow]], and the room takes on "an air about it of a shambles" [017-002][017-060].

## Participants

- **[[hollander|Thomas Hollander]]** — The attacker, posing as Endicott's friend while planning to murder him [016-026].
- **[[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]** — The unconscious victim, defenseless on the bed.
- **[[cassidy|Cassidy]]** — Police officer whose shot shatters Hollander's wrist [016-081].
- **[[hansen|Hansen]]** — Police officer whose shot wounds Hollander's shoulder [016-081].
- **[[nurse-murrow|Nurse Murrow]]** — The attending nurse who helps arrange medical care for Hollander afterward [017-002].
- **[[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Sanford Worth]]** — Alerted by [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] before the attack [016-026].

## Significance

The knife attack is the novel's most dramatic action sequence, transforming Hollander from a sympathetic figure into an antagonist. The event also demonstrates [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s foresight — he anticipated the attack and positioned officers to prevent it. The dispute between [[cassidy|Cassidy]] and Hansen over which of them fired first becomes a minor running joke through the later chapters [027-011][027-012].

## Evidence
