## Overview

Jerry Smith is a friend and secretary of [[hollander|Thomas Hollander]], introduced in Chapter 14 when [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] visits his apartment. Smith is a Southerner — he repeatedly invokes "We of the South, sir" as a moral framing [014-023]. He introduces himself simply: "Smith, Lieutenant — Jerry Smith" [014-014]. Smith is described as having "exceedingly attractive dark eyes" and speaks of Hollander with intense loyalty [014-068].

## Role in the Story

Jerry Smith serves as a character witness for [[hollander|Thomas Hollander]], providing [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] with background on the relationship between Hollander and [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]. When [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] questions whether Hollander might be involved in the Endicott business, Smith becomes defensive: "The straightest, squarest gentleman who ever lived, sir" [014-025][014-029]. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s interview reveals Smith's own criminal record — he was seen "one morning last month in the lineup down at headquarters" [014-018] — and hints at a past incident described as "that gilt-knuckles job" [014-038]. Smith's Southern code of honor and his fierce defense of Hollander complicate [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s attempt to gather impartial information.

## Key Appearances

**Chapter 14 — The Interview**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] visits Smith's apartment and interviews him about Hollander. Smith launches into an elaborate defense of his friend [014-025]. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] questions him about an incident involving a woman's necklace, and Smith's Southern gallantry emerges: "We of the South, sir, do not bring charges against a lady" [014-023]. The conversation becomes tense when [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] mentions Hollander's connection to Endicott [014-029]. Smith admits that Hollander believes [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]] is "a saint" [014-068]. By the interview's end, Smith grows suspicious of [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s methods: "I'll be damned if you talk like a cop" [014-072].

## Relationships

- **[[hollander|Thomas Hollander]]** — Friend and employer; Smith serves as his secretary and defender [014-025][014-029].
- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]** — The detective who interviews him, probing Smith's own criminal past while seeking information about Hollander [014-018][014-056].

## Analysis

Jerry Smith is a minor but colorful character whose Southern identity is played for both comic effect and thematic resonance. His elaborate rhetoric and code of honor contrast with [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s Northern, matter-of-fact detective style [014-023][014-072]. Smith's criminal record — hinted at through [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s references to the lineup and the "gilt-knuckles job" — suggests that Hollander's circle includes characters with their own legal vulnerabilities [014-018][014-038]. His interview is one of the novel's lighter passages, balancing the darker revelations unfolding at the Endicott house.
