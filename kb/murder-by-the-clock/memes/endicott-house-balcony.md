## Overview

The [[endicott-house|Endicott House]] balcony is a shallow stone ledge running the full width of the rear of the [[endicott-house|Endicott House]] — "five windows wide" (002-041). It is the critical crime scene within the murder investigation: the fatal shot that killed [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]] was fired from this balcony (018-003, 021-031). Throughout [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]'s investigation, the balcony becomes the focal point of his reconstruction of the crime — who accessed it, when, and from which window.

## Physical Description

The balcony is a stone construction described as "shallow" (002-041, 017-069), running the width of the rear facade. It is accessible only by straddling the window sills of the rooms that open onto it — there is no door or staircase leading to it (002-041). The stone floor, examined by [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] during his initial inspection, is described as "smugly lacking in clues" (002-044), offering no footprints or physical traces of the shooter.

Access to the balcony requires climbing through a window. The room windows that open onto it include [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s bedroom, [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]'s bedroom, and a third room — the bedroom of [[mrs-siddons|Mrs. Siddons]], the housekeeper (019-008).

## Role in the Murder

The balcony is the murder weapon's firing position. At the moment of the shooting, someone crept along the balcony and fired through [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s window (018-003, 021-031). The sound of the shot, [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] notes, "would surely have been heard" by anyone in the house (019-031), which becomes a key point in his interrogation of witnesses.

The shooter's method of accessing the balcony becomes the central puzzle of the investigation. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] considers several possibilities: the shooter could have come through a window from inside the house, or — more dramatically — climbed up from the ground using a rope or other means (019-004, 027-039). The absence of a rope or climbing apparatus leads [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] to conclude the shooter came from inside.

## Investigation

[[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s balcony investigation proceeds through several stages:

- **Initial inspection (002-041–044)**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] examines the balcony floor but finds no footprints or physical evidence. The "smugly lacking in clues" description underscores the shooter's care.

- **Window-by-window analysis (019-004–008)**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] maps which rooms have windows opening onto the balcony, identifying the rooms of Herbert, [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]], and [[mrs-siddons|Mrs. Siddons]] as the three access points.

- **Night-time reconstruction (021-053–059)**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] goes out onto the balcony himself at dawn, using his flashlight to inspect the sills and stone surface, trying to reconstruct the shooter's movements.

- **[[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]'s denial (023-016–072)**: In a sustained exchange, [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] repeatedly denies having been on the balcony. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] presses her on the glow cast from Herbert's window onto the balcony (023-069), which she could have seen if she had been there.

- **The slipper evidence (022-060)**: A bedroom slipper is found on the balcony. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] suspects it was dropped there as a deliberate "plant" to misdirect the investigation.

## Eyewitness Testimony

[[mrs-siddons|Mrs. Siddons]] testifies that she saw no one on the balcony at the time of the shots (025-005). [[nurse-murrow|Nurse Murrow]] is questioned about her knowledge of the balcony and whether she could have accessed it (029-004, 029-027).

## The Shots

The fatal shots were fired from the balcony into [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s room through his rear window. The trajectory required the shooter to be positioned on the balcony, level with the window (021-031). [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s theory that the killer "shot [[herbert-endicott|Mr. Endicott]]" and then "returned" inside through a window becomes the backbone of the prosecution's case.
