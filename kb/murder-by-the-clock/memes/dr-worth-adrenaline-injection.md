## Overview

After [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]] is found unconscious — presumed dead — in a cupboard, [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Sanford Worth]] arrives at the [[endicott-house|Endicott residence]] and performs an emergency medical procedure: injecting adrenaline directly into Endicott's cardiac muscles. The procedure is successful, and Endicott is revived, transforming the case from a murder investigation into an assault inquiry.

## Procedure

- **Diagnosis**: [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]] determines that Endicott is not dead but in a state of suspended animation. The medical examiner confirms the finding (006-021).
- **The injection**: Worth prepares to "inject adrenaline into the cardiac muscles" — a direct [[adrenaline-injection|cardiac injection]], a dramatic and rarely-performed emergency procedure (006-022).
- **Skepticism**: [[chief-andrews|Chief Andrews]] notes that "adrenaline's been used off and on for years" but rarely with success (006-026). He warns that if "the adrenaline doesn't work and an autopsy proves poison," the case will take a different turn (006-028).
- **Success**: The operation is completed successfully. [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]] hands the stethoscope to the medical examiner and announces: "Endicott lives" (008-008).
- **Aftermath**: Endicott is moved to his bed. [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]] insists that [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] "can't be questioned or disturbed in any way at all until I say so" (008-012).

## Participants

- **[[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Sanford Worth]]** — Endicott's family physician, performs the emergency injection.
- **[[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]** — The patient, revived from suspended animation.
- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]** — Observes the procedure and coordinates with the medical team.
- **[[nurse-murrow|Nurse Murrow]]** and **[[nurse-vickers|Nurse Vickers]]** — Trained nurses present for the operation (008-003).
- **Medical Examiner** — Assists [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]] and later reports to the Homicide Bureau (008-017).

## Significance

The adrenaline injection is the pivotal medical event of the novel. Endicott's survival changes the legal status of the case from homicide to assault, alters the investigative strategy, and creates the central dramatic situation: a man who everyone believes should be dead is instead alive, and his would-be killer must act again. The procedure also establishes [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]]'s medical competence and his protective attitude toward the Endicott family.
