## Overview

Madame Velasquez is the stepmother of [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]] and the widow of Alvarez Velasquez, a Spaniard. She is introduced in Chapter 12 when [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] visits [[marge-myles|Marge]]'s apartment searching for her stepdaughter. Madame Velasquez is a striking, theatrical figure — the narrator describes her wig, her low-cut dress, and the "bunched strands of artificial jewellery" clasped about her thin neck [012-012][012-018]. She tells [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]], "I'm Madame Velasquez — [[marge-myles|Marge]]'s ma. I ain't Spanish myself, but if there ever was a Spaniard, my late husband Alvarez was" [012-011].

## Role in the Story

Madame Velasquez serves as a source of information about [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]] and as an antagonist who has been blackmailing her own stepdaughter. She returns in Chapter 25 with crucial testimony about discovering [[marge-myles|Marge]]'s body [025-013][025-018]. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] later reveals to [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] that [[marge-myles|Marge]] "has been paying blackmail to her stepmother, Madame Velasquez" [029-008]. Her innuendos and accusations complicate the investigation, and [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] reflects on how her "innuendoes against the true identity of anybody came back to him with annoying force" [026-046].

## Key Appearances

**Chapter 12 — Night Visit**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] arrives at [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]]'s apartment around midnight. Madame Velasquez, wearing her distinctive wig and jewelry, claims not to know where [[marge-myles|Marge]] is [012-015][012-028]. She is uncooperative and theatrical. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] identifies himself as the police, and she eventually reveals that she expected [[marge-myles|Marge]] to be home [012-031].

**Chapter 25 — Discovery of the Body**: In the early morning hours, Madame Velasquez returns to the Endicott house — "beyond words" from the intervening hours [025-013]. She informs [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] that she has found [[marge-myles|Marge]]'s body, providing a key turning point in the investigation [025-018].

**Chapters 26–31 — Resolution**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] pieces together the blackmail motive involving Madame Velasquez [026-046][029-008]. By the novel's end, Madame Velasquez is among the dead — [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] thinks of her alongside [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]] and [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]] as casualties of the case [031-009].

## Relationships

- **[[marge-myles|Marge Myles]]** — Stepdaughter, whom she blackmailed [029-008].
- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]** — The investigating detective who interviews her twice and ultimately uncovers the blackmail relationship [012-028][029-008].
- **Alvarez Velasquez** — Late husband, a Spaniard, referenced when explaining her name [012-011].

## Analysis

Madame Velasquez functions as a minor antagonist whose greed complicates the investigation. Her flamboyant appearance — the wig, low-cut dress, and artificial jewelry — marks her as a figure of artifice and deception in a novel centrally concerned with hidden motives and false appearances [012-012][012-018]. Her blackmail of [[marge-myles|Marge]] creates one of the financial entanglements that [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] must untangle. In a story where many characters conceal their true natures, Madame Velasquez's physical artifice mirrors her moral opportunism.
