## Overview

Nurse Vickers (referred to as "Miss Vickers") is the second nurse brought in by [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Sanford Worth]] on the night of [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s collapse. While [[nurse-murrow|Nurse Murrow]] watches over Endicott, Nurse Vickers is assigned to stay with [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] in her room [008-028]. She is a composed professional who displays no nervousness even when viewing Endicott's dead body [019-022].

## Role in the Story

Nurse Vickers serves as [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]'s attendant and later as a witness interviewed by [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] during the investigation. Her professional detachment contrasts with the emotional turmoil of the household. When [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] interviews her in Chapter 19, she answers his questions calmly and directly — her "glance toward the bed was professional and not coloured by any sign of nervousness" [019-022]. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] questions her about what she heard and saw on the night of the shooting, including whether she heard the fatal shot and what she observed of [[roberts|Roberts]]'s behavior [019-029][019-031][019-034].

## Key Appearances

**Chapter 8 — Assignment**: [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]] informs [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] that Nurse Vickers will stay with [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] all night, with two day nurses arriving in the morning [008-028].

**Chapter 8 — The Maid's Message**: Nurse Vickers answers the door to [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]'s room when a maid brings a message. She blocks the entrance, keeping the room sealed [008-081].

**Chapter 19 — [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s Interview**: After Endicott's death, [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] interviews Nurse Vickers. She enters "without the formality of knocking" [019-022]. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] asks her about the night's events, whether she heard the shot, and what she observed of [[roberts|Roberts]]'s state. She maintains professional composure throughout [019-023][019-031][019-053].

## Relationships

- **[[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Sanford Worth]]** — The physician who assigns her to [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]'s care [008-028].
- **[[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]** — Her patient for the night, whom she attends in her room [008-028][008-081].
- **[[nurse-murrow|Nurse Murrow]]** — Colleague; they are mentioned together as contacts at the Nurses' Home [024-008 in corpus].
- **[[roberts|Roberts]]** — The maid whose distressed state Nurse Vickers describes to [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] [019-034].

## Analysis

Nurse Vickers is a functional character who, like [[nurse-murrow|Nurse Murrow]], represents professional competence within the chaos of the Endicott household. Where Murrow provides an interior perspective on the tedium of nursing, Vickers is presented more externally — seen through [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s observation of her calm professionalism [019-022]. Her interview in Chapter 19 contributes to [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s reconstruction of the night's timeline and the movements of the household staff. Together, the two nurses form a professional counterweight to the emotional volatility of the Endicott family and their entanglements.
