## Overview

The final chapters of the novel bring [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]'s investigation to its dramatic conclusion. In Chapter 28 ("Mist Drifting Through Mist"), [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] searches [[endicott-house-attic|the attic]] and makes a crucial discovery [028-001][028-108]. Chapter 29 ("As Is Mirage") features his confrontation with [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]], who initially believes he has "gone mad" [029-002][029-003]. Chapter 30 reveals "The Criminal and Weapon of the Crime" [030-001]. The epilogue (Chapter 31, "Five Years Later") shows the aftermath — [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] is now an Inspector, and the full truth of the murders is laid bare [031-001][031-017].

## Chronology

- **Chapter 28 — The Attic Search**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] races upstairs and searches [[endicott-house-attic|the attic]], finding papers in an upended trunk. He demands to know where [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]] is hidden in the house [028-002][028-108][028-109].
- **Chapter 29 — The Accusation**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] confronts [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] with his reconstruction of the crimes. She calls him mad, but he persists [029-002][029-003]. He reveals that [[marge-myles|Marge]] murdered [[harry-myles|Harry Myles]] in the canoe episode and was blackmailing her stepmother [029-008]. [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] is formally charged: "as soon as my men have thoroughly searched this house you will be formally charged" [029-033].
- **Chapter 30 — The Suicide**: The criminal confronts [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s men — "blue serge — brass buttons — a glinting shield." She pulls the trigger, but "the muzzle of the gun was in her mouth" [030-058][030-059]. The murderer takes her own life rather than face justice.
- **Chapter 31 — Epilogue**: Five years later, "Mrs. Hollander" (the former [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]], now married to [[hollander|Thomas Hollander]]) once again dials the police [031-001][031-002]. The narrator reveals the truth: "She was the true murderer of her husband, and not [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]]" [031-017]. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]], now an Inspector, reflects on whether [[hollander|Thomas Hollander]] too has "become a puppet" [031-018].

## Participants

- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]** — The detective who pieces together the truth and becomes Inspector by the epilogue [031-018].
- **[[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]** — The central figure of the denouement; revealed as her husband's true murderer [031-017].
- **[[marge-myles|Marge Myles]]** — Posthumously exonerated of Endicott's murder, though she killed [[harry-myles|Harry Myles]] [029-008].
- **[[hollander|Thomas Hollander]]** — By the epilogue, married to the former [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] [031-002].
- **[[roberts|Roberts]]** — The maid whose room, testimony, and gun feature in the resolution [028-047 in corpus].

## Significance

The denouement reveals the novel's central twist: [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] — who summoned [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] in Chapter 1 under the pretense of fearing for her husband's safety — was herself the murderer. The epilogue's circular structure (Mrs. Hollander dialing the police exactly as [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] did in Chapter 1) suggests that the patterns of manipulation and violence continue. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s promotion to Inspector and his final reflection on whether [[hollander|Thomas Hollander]] has "become a puppet, too" [031-018] underscore the novel's cynical view of human relationships.
