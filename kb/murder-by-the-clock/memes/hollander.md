## Overview

Thomas Hollander is a central figure in the overnight vigil following [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s collapse. An intimate friend and former war comrade of Endicott, he is summoned by [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] on the recommendation of [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]] to be the first person Endicott sees upon regaining consciousness (009-027). His presence at the [[endicott-house|Endicott house]] through the critical night hours places him in a position of both trust and suspicion.

## Role in the Story

Hollander enters the investigation when [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]] advises that an intimate friend of Endicott should be near him when he recovers consciousness (009-023). [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] locates Hollander through the telephone directory — he lives on East Fifty-second Street (010-039) — and calls him with a carefully worded message from the Endicott home (011-005).

Hollander's telephone conversation with [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] is marked by what [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] later analyses as "negative defense" — every word deflects without committing (012-004). His voice is described as "smooth, soft" with a suspected Southern accent (011-003). When [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] tells him Endicott is dead, Hollander responds with surprise: "Dead? Why no, Hollander. Were you expecting him to be?" (011-022).

Upon arriving at the house, Hollander takes the overnight vigil beside Endicott's bed, placing an upholstered armchair with its back to the bathroom door (013-060). He converses with [[nurse-murrow|Nurse Murrow]], expressing concern that the police suspect [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] (013-052). By morning, he has departed before [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s return (014-006).

## Relationships

- **[[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]** (friend): Fellow war veteran whose life Hollander saved during their military service together (014-035). Endicott and Hollander "were in the same outfit" (014-035). Their friendship is close enough that [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]] immediately names Hollander as the person Endicott should see first upon waking.
- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]** (investigator): A wary, professional dynamic. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] notes Hollander's evasiveness on the phone and later visits his apartment on East Fifty-second Street (012-090).
- **[[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]]** (referrer): Worth recommended Hollander as the trusted friend to sit with Endicott (009-023).
- **[[nurse-murrow|Nurse Murrow]]** (companion during vigil): Shares the night watch with Hollander. Their conversation reveals his concern about the investigation's direction and his defensiveness about the household.

## Analysis

Hollander occupies an ambiguous position in the narrative. As Endicott's closest friend and life-saver, he has the strongest alibi of loyalty. Yet [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s instinct flags him: the telephone conversation is evasive, his midnight presence at the house places him in proximity to the victim, and his early-morning departure before [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s return is unexplained.

The detail of his pupils contracting "until they glittered like the heads of two bright pins" (013-061) suggests heightened alertness or concealed tension. His muttered "Cripes!" at [[nurse-murrow|Nurse Murrow]]'s war reminiscence (013-034) hints at a man uncomfortable with the heroic narrative attached to him. The peculiar description of his walk — suggesting a sailor, "a stolid field, motionless, beneath drifting clouds" (013-014) — paints him as a man whose surface calm conceals inner movement, a fitting portrait for a character whose loyalties remain unproven.
