## Overview

Officer O'Brian is one of the uniformed patrolmen assigned to guard the Endicott house during [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]'s investigation. He is stationed at the front door alongside Hansen, tasked with controlling access to the crime scene [003-004]. O'Brian serves as a point of contact at the house's entrance, and his exchanges with [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] reveal the detective's dry wit and command style [007-005][007-008].

## Role in the Story

O'Brian is a sentinel figure — he guards the front door throughout the night, controlling who enters and leaves the Endicott house. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] frequently checks in with him when moving between floors, using him as a source of information about who has come and gone [007-018][007-030]. O'Brian's presence at the door makes him a fixed point in the geography of the investigation, and his brief interactions with [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] show the chain of command in action.

## Key Appearances

**Chapter 3 — Initial Deployment**: O'Brian is stationed at the front door alongside Hansen. He remarks to Hansen that [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] is "cuckoo," and Hansen agrees — "Cuckoo as a fox" [003-004][003-005].

**Chapter 7 — [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s Interrogations**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] finds O'Brian pressing his nose against the glass of the front door and teases him about it [007-005]. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] questions him about who has entered and left the house, establishing O'Brian as a reliable sentry [007-008][007-014][007-018].

**Chapter 10 — Summoned**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] calls down to O'Brian from the upstairs landing, and O'Brian looks up from below [010-003][010-004].

**Chapter 11 — Hollander's Arrival**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] instructs O'Brian to identify and admit [[hollander|Thomas Hollander]] when he arrives [011-055][011-056].

**Chapter 16 — The Long Night**: O'Brian stirs restlessly in his chair by the hall door, yawns, and checks his watch — a small moment that captures the tedium of guard duty [016-002]. He answers the hall telephone [016-005].

## Relationships

- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]** — Commanding officer; O'Brian follows orders and endures [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s teasing with professional patience [007-005][007-030].
- **[[hansen|Hansen]]** — Fellow officer; they are paired at the front door and share a camaraderie [003-004][003-005].
- **[[cassidy|Cassidy]]** — Fellow officer; one of the team guarding the house [017-002].

## Analysis

O'Brian embodies the uniformed officer as background infrastructure — essential to the operation of the crime scene but never a focus of narrative attention. His presence maintains the sealed environment that [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] needs for his investigation. The novel uses O'Brian and his colleagues to establish the police hierarchy: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] as the detective who thinks, the uniformed officers as the force that executes. O'Brian's longest moment in the spotlight — Chapter 16's restless stirring and watch-checking — humanizes the tedium of police work within the heightened drama of a murder investigation [016-002].
