## Overview

In the early morning hours, [[madame-velasquez|Madame Velasquez]] returns to the Endicott house in a state of shock. The intervening hours since [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] left her at [[marge-myles|Marge]]'s apartment have transformed her — she is "beyond words" as she delivers the news that she has found [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]] dead [025-013]. This discovery reveals that the case involves not one but two murders, fundamentally altering the investigation's scope.

## Chronology

- **[[madame-velasquez|Madame Velasquez]] returns**: She arrives at the Endicott house "in the penetrating light of early morning," visibly shattered [025-013].
- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] questions her**: He tells her to sit down and asks her to describe how she found the body [025-018].
- **The second murder confirmed**: [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]] is dead, adding a second victim to what began as the investigation of [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s collapse.
- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s reflection**: The discovery forces [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] to reconsider his assumptions — he thinks of "[[marge-myles|Marge Myles]] and of [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]], who were dead; and of [[madame-velasquez|Madame Velasquez]] who, too, had died" [031-009].

## Participants

- **[[madame-velasquez|Madame Velasquez]]** — The discoverer of the body, whose earlier evasiveness now takes on new significance.
- **[[marge-myles|Marge Myles]]** — The victim, found dead after being missing throughout the night.
- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]** — The detective who must now solve two murders.

## Significance

[[marge-myles|Marge Myles]]'s death transforms the case from a single suspicious death into a double murder investigation. It eliminates [[marge-myles|Marge]] as a suspect in Endicott's murder and points toward a more complex conspiracy. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] later reveals the blackmail relationship between [[marge-myles|Marge]] and [[madame-velasquez|Madame Velasquez]], and the connection to the forged letter [029-008]. By the novel's end, [[madame-velasquez|Madame Velasquez]] herself is also dead, marking the complete collapse of that branch of the story [031-009].
