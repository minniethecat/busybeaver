## Overview

The Damascus knife was a distinctive blade carried by [[hollander|Thomas Hollander]], described by [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]] as "some sort of a sticker that Tom picked up on the other side" [014-075]. Despite being characterized as a paper knife, the weapon was clearly designed as a stabbing implement — "Damascus steel," Endicott called it — and became the instrument of Hollander's attempted murder of the unconscious Endicott.

## Description and Origin

The knife was of Damascus steel, a material renowned for its distinctive patterned surface and exceptional sharpness. According to [[herbert-endicott|Endicott]], Hollander had acquired it "on the other side" — presumably during travels in Europe or the Middle East, where Damascus steel blades were traditionally crafted [014-075]. Hollander kept it ostensibly as a paper knife, a domestic guise that masked its lethal potential.

## Role in the Murder Attempt

During the [[hollander-knife-attack-on-endicott|knife attack on Endicott]], Hollander produced the Damascus blade and moved toward the unconscious man's bed. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] had anticipated this, warning [[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Worth]] beforehand that Hollander "is armed with a knife and that he is probably just waiting for a chance to use it" [016-026]. The attack was foiled when [[cassidy|Cassidy]] and Hansen shot Hollander before he could reach Endicott.

## Valcour's Analysis

After the attack, [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] reflected on the certainty of Hollander's intent: "One had to believe that Hollander had certainly intended to stab Endicott with that knife; the point was irrefutable" [022-005]. Yet Valcour also recognized that Hollander's motives remained "clear enough and beautifully simple: he wanted to protect Mrs. Endicott" [022-005]. The knife thus represented not simple malice but a twisted form of loyalty — Hollander believed killing Endicott would somehow shield [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] from danger or suspicion.

## As Evidence

The Damascus knife served as physical proof of Hollander's homicidal intent. Its exotic origin and lethal design contradicted the innocent "paper knife" description, suggesting premeditation. The knife's presence in the Endicott house — carried there by Hollander after being summoned by [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s [[valcour-summons-hollander|telephone call]] — demonstrated that Hollander had come prepared for violence even before knowing the full circumstances of Endicott's collapse.

## Symbolic Significance

The Damascus knife embodies the novel's theme of concealed violence within domestic settings. Like the [[dust-disguise|dust disguise]] that transformed a cupboard into a death chamber, the "paper knife" that was really a "sticker" represents the duality at the heart of the crime — ordinary objects and familiar faces masking deadly intentions.
