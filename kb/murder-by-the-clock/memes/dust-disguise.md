## Summary

The dust disguise was a key deduction made by [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] during his investigation of [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s apparent death. Examining the cupboard where Endicott's body was found, [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] noticed streaks in the dust on the top shelf — evidence that someone had deliberately wiped their hands in it. He theorized that the intruder, caught by surprise when Endicott opened the cupboard door, had smeared dust on their face as an impromptu disguise to avoid recognition. This deduction later proved central to identifying [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]] as the intruder.

## Discovery of the Dust Streaks

[[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] made the discovery while examining the cupboard after the [[new-york-police-department|Central Office]] detectives had finished their work. Standing on a chair, he played his flashlight along the top shelf. He noticed streaks in the accumulated dust where it had recently been rubbed away, as if somebody had deliberately wiped both hands in it. The finding linked with a dirty cake of soap he had found earlier [006-042]. He was particularly satisfied because the [[new-york-police-department|Central Office]] men, including [[chief-andrews|Chief Andrews]], had overlooked the streaks entirely [006-044].

## The Disguise Theory

Staring at the dust, [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] reconstructed the scene: a person hiding in the cupboard, tormented by hate or fear, who knows Endicott is about to open the door. With no other means of disguise available, the intruder smeared dust on their hands and face to prevent recognition. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] considered — and quickly dismissed — the idea of an exotic Oriental villain with a penchant for dust, settling instead on the more practical deduction that the intruder had made use of whatever was at hand [006-046].

## Confronting Hollander

[[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] later incorporated the dust disguise theory into his interrogation of [[hollander|Thomas Hollander]]. He presented the theory that Hollander, having obtained [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]'s keys, entered the house intending to kill Endicott. While searching through Endicott's clothes in the cupboard, he heard Endicott approaching. Afraid of being recognized, he disguised himself with dust smeared on his face. When Endicott opened the cupboard door, the shock of the disguised figure stopped his heart [020-085].

## Significance in the Case

The dust disguise deduction was a turning point in [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s investigation. It confirmed that the intruder had been physically present in the cupboard — ruling out theories that Endicott's [[herbert-endicotts-heart-condition|heart attack]] had been triggered by something seen from a distance. The dust streaks, combined with the rifled pockets and the missing hat, established a clear picture of someone searching for something specific when they were surprised by Endicott. This chain of reasoning ultimately led [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] to identify [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]] as the person searching for the [[the-postscript-forgery|forged postscript]] letter [006-048].
