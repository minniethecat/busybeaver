## Overview

[[marge-myles|Marge Myles]]' apartment on Riverside Drive is the destination [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]] claimed he was visiting on the night of his murder. It represents the extramarital relationship at the heart of the case and serves as a potential alternative crime scene or alibi location.

## Significance

[[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]] told his wife [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] he was going to [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]]' apartment at 7 p.m. on the night of his death (001-031). However, his body was found in his own bedroom cupboard at the [[endicott-house|Endicott house]], fully dressed for outdoors. This raises the critical question: did he ever leave the house? The apartment serves as both a potential motive for [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] (jealousy) and a potential lead for investigators seeking to trace Herbert's actual movements.

## Appearances

- **001-031**: [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] tells [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] that Herbert left "to go to the apartment of a woman with whom he thinks he is in love. Her name is [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]], and her apartment is on the Drive" (001-031)
- **001-034**: [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] clarifies she is not seeking divorce evidence — "This woman is nothing of any permanence" (001-034)
- **001-036**: [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] describes seeing [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]] at the St. Regis, noting her "foreign-looking — the Latin type" appearance (001-036)
- **004-039**: [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] reveals Herbert was lying about his destination — he claimed to be going to "an impromptu reunion of some men in his class at the Yale Club" (004-040)

## Investigative Significance

The apartment is a critical investigative lead that remains unexplored in the early chapters. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] suggests calling [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]] directly (001-063), but [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]'s objection — that Herbert would see it as spying — prevents this line of inquiry initially. Whether Herbert ever actually arrived at the apartment on the night of his death is a key unanswered question. If he never arrived, it suggests he was intercepted or killed before leaving his own home.
