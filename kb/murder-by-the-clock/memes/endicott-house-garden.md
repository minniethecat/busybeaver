## Overview

The garden at the rear of the [[endicott-house|Endicott House]] is a formal outdoor space that plays a secondary but significant role in the investigation. Overlooked by the [[endicott-house-balcony|rear balcony]] (021-054) and visible from the [[endicott-house-attic|attic]] window (028-003), the garden is a potential escape route and evidence disposal site. [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] considers the possibility that the murder weapon — a gun — was thrown from the balcony into the garden (023-039), and the notion of accessing the balcony from the garden is entertained as a theory (027-039, 030-042).

## Physical Description

The garden lies at the rear of the house (003-004). It is described as a space that "would bloom into a formal garden" (002-041), suggesting deliberate landscaping rather than a wild yard. The garden is accessible via the servants' entrance and a "garden door" (030-011), which [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] orders to be guarded. The "outline of the garden down below" is visible from the balcony at dawn (021-054), indicating it is directly beneath and extends outward from the rear facade.

The garden is separated from neighboring properties by "intervening gardens" — the houses across the way have their own outdoor spaces, described in "mauve and dark gray" tones at dawn (024-022).

## Role in the Investigation

### Access Route

A key question during the investigation is whether the shooter could have climbed up to the [[endicott-house-balcony|balcony]] from the garden. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] notes that "it would be possible for a sailor to climb up onto the balcony outside this window from the garden" (027-039). Later, the idea is revisited: "What a handy thing to help a man climb up onto the balcony from the garden down below" (030-042). However, no physical evidence (rope, ladder, footprints) supports this theory, and [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] ultimately rejects it in favor of an inside job.

### Evidence Disposal

[[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] directly asks [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]: "Did you throw the gun into the garden, [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]? We're bound to find it" (023-039). This questioning reveals that the garden was identified early as a logical disposal site — a dark outdoor space where a small object could be hidden until morning.

### Police Deployment

When the police arrive, [[cassidy|Cassidy]] and other officers are positioned to cover the rear of the house. [[cassidy|Cassidy]] is ordered to watch "the back of the house into the garden" (003-004), and later "the servants' entrance and garden door" are to be kept guarded (030-011). The garden thus becomes part of the perimeter that seals the crime scene.

## The Garden in the Narrative

The garden serves as a boundary between the enclosed crime scene and the outside world. Its formal, cultivated nature mirrors the ordered appearance of the Endicott household, while the possibility that it conceals the murder weapon introduces the idea of hidden disorder beneath the surface. The view of the garden from the balcony at dawn — "its outline quite distinct" (021-054) — is one of the novel's few exterior descriptions, emphasizing the claustrophobic interiority of the narrative.
