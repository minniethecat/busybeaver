## Overview

After midnight, [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] leaves the Endicott house to visit the apartment of [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]] on the Drive. The chapter heading announces "[[madame-velasquez|Madame Velasquez]] Stirs Up Muck" [012-001]. Instead of [[marge-myles|Marge]], [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] finds her stepmother, [[madame-velasquez|Madame Velasquez]], a theatrical figure in a wig and low-cut dress adorned with artificial jewelry [012-012][012-018].

## Chronology

- **Arrival**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] arrives at [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]]'s apartment around 12:30 a.m. [012-001].
- **Meeting [[madame-velasquez|Madame Velasquez]]**: She introduces herself: "I'm [[madame-velasquez|Madame Velasquez]] — [[marge-myles|Marge]]'s ma. I ain't Spanish myself, but if there ever was a Spaniard, my late husband Alvarez was" [012-011].
- **Questioning about [[marge-myles|Marge]]**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] asks about [[marge-myles|Marge]]'s whereabouts. [[madame-velasquez|Madame Velasquez]] claims not to know where she is [012-015][012-026].
- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] reveals his identity**: He tells her he is the police. She responds, "Yes, [[madame-velasquez|Madame Velasquez]] — [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]" [012-028][012-031].
- **The interview ends**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] leaves without finding [[marge-myles|Marge]], but with new information about her stepmother's role [025-013].

## Participants

- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]** — The detective, extending his investigation beyond the Endicott house.
- **[[madame-velasquez|Madame Velasquez]]** — [[marge-myles|Marge]]'s stepmother, evasive and theatrical [012-011][012-012].

## Significance

This visit expands the investigation's scope beyond the Endicott house. [[madame-velasquez|Madame Velasquez]]'s evasiveness raises [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s suspicions, and her later return to the Endicott house with the news of [[marge-myles|Marge]]'s death makes this initial interview retroactively significant. The blackmail relationship between [[madame-velasquez|Madame Velasquez]] and [[marge-myles|Marge]], which [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] later uncovers, adds a financial motive to the case [029-008].
