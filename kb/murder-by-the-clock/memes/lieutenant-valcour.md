## Overview

Lieutenant Valcour is the police detective assigned to investigate [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s death. A French-Canadian who became naturalized twenty years prior (001-026), he brings a cultured, methodical approach to homicide investigation within the [[new-york-police-department|New York Police Department]]. His "deceptively indifferent eyes" miss nothing, from a broken fingernail to a soiled cake of soap.

## Role in the Story

Lieutenant Valcour arrives at the [[endicott-house|Endicott house]] after [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] calls police headquarters. He personally responds to the call rather than sending a detective (001-023). He discovers [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s body in the cupboard (001-077), then methodically investigates the crime scene — examining the bathroom window, the soiled soap, the missing hat, and [[the-threatening-note|the threatening note]]. He calls in the Homicide Bureau, the [[medical-examiners-office|medical examiner]], and his own men (002-017). Throughout the investigation, he balances official procedure with psychological insight, gently probing [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]'s story while noting every discrepancy.

## Relationships

- **[[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]** (suspect/witness): A nuanced interrogator-subject relationship. Valcour is both charmed and suspicious, noting her beauty and her contradictions. He employs gentle questioning that masks sharp observation.
- **[[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]** (victim): Never met alive. Valcour reconstructs his life and death through physical evidence and witness testimony.
- **[[dr-sanford-worth|Dr. Sanford Worth]]** (collaborator): Valcour works with the family physician to establish cause of death. Their first exchange reveals mutual professional respect with underlying tension (003-030).
- **[[cassidy|Cassidy]]** (subordinate): Young police officer stationed in Endicott's room. Their banter about "Chinese blow guns" and magazine detective stories (003-027) reveals Valcour's patience with his men.
- **[[chief-andrews|Chief Andrews]]** (superior): Homicide bureau chief who arrives with the investigative team (003-045).

## Analysis

Lieutenant Valcour represents the rational, scientific detective emerging in early 20th-century crime fiction. His French-Canadian background adds cultural complexity — an outsider's perspective on American society. His methodical approach to physical evidence (examining soap, towels, window shades, and balcony ledges) is balanced by psychological acuity in reading witnesses. He classifies knocks philosophically (003-002) and reflects on the limits of a detective's knowledge (004-010). His cultured speech and educated manner contrast with the brutality of the crime, making him an effective intermediary between the Endicotts' refined world and the harsh reality of murder investigation.
