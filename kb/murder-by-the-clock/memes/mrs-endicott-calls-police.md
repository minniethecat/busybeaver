## Overview

At 8:37 p.m. on a Thursday evening, [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Herbert Endicott]] calls the [[new-york-police-department|New York police headquarters]], dialling the number Spring 3100 (001-007). This telephone call is the initiating event of the entire investigation. Without it, [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s body might not have been discovered until much later, and the crime scene would have been compromised.

## Chronology

1. **8:37 p.m.**: [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] looks up the police telephone number and dials Spring 3100 (001-007)
2. **Moments later**: An "undeniably masculine voice" answers. [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] asks to be connected to the "proper department" (001-008)
3. **Immediately after**: A second voice answers. [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] explains she is "worried about [[herbert-endicott|Mr. Endicott]]" and requests someone be sent (001-009)
4. **Shortly after 9 p.m.**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] arrives at the [[endicott-house|Endicott house]] (001-022)

## Participants

- **[[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]** (caller): Makes the call from the living room on the second floor (001-011). She debates whether to call the police or a private investigator, ultimately choosing the police because "the police could do something" (001-006). She is impressed by the efficiency with which her call is transferred (001-010).
- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]** (responder): Happens to be leaving for home when the call comes in and decides to respond personally "instead of sending a detective" (001-023).

## Significance

The telephone call establishes several key elements of the case. [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]'s decision to call the police rather than a private investigator suggests genuine concern rather than an attempt to manage appearances. However, her initial uncertainty — wondering whether "police characters she had seen in various plays" were true to life (001-005) — hints at her inexperience with law enforcement and the insulated world she inhabits. [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s personal response, rather than delegating to a subordinate, suggests either the call's unusual nature or his own professional curiosity. The call also establishes the first timeline anchor: 8:37 p.m., approximately ninety minutes after [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]] left the house at 7 p.m.

## Evidence

- **The telephone call**: Placed from the living room on the second floor (001-011)
- **Spring 3100**: The police headquarters number (001-007)
- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s voluntary response**: He states he was "leaving for home when your call was put in" (001-023)
