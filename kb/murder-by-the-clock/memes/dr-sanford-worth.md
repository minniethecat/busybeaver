## Overview

Dr. Sanford Worth is the family physician whose medical knowledge becomes central to the investigation of [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s death and revival. A polished, distinguished bachelor with a Vandyke beard and "sparklingly manicured" hands (003-033), he had treated Endicott's cardiac condition for two years and had been anticipating a crisis. His emergency [[adrenaline-injection|adrenaline injection]] brings Endicott back from clinical death, transforming what appeared to be a closed murder case into an ongoing medical vigil.

## Role in the Story

Dr. Worth is summoned to the [[endicott-house|Endicott house]] after Endicott's body is discovered. [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] reaches him by telephone at his office (002-014). Upon arrival, Worth immediately confirms his prior concern: "I have been afraid of something like this for quite a while, Lieutenant" (003-033). He reveals that Endicott suffered a nervous breakdown two years earlier that left his heart's action impaired (003-041).

Worth's most dramatic contribution is the emergency operation in which he injects [[adrenaline-injection|adrenaline]] directly into Endicott's cardiac muscles (006-022). The procedure succeeds spectacularly: "Endicott lives," he announces (008-008). This revival transforms the investigation — Endicott is no longer a corpse but a potential witness who might identify his attacker.

Throughout the night, Worth maintains medical authority over the sickroom. He arranges for two trained nurses, briefs the police guards on medical protocol (010-023), and insists that [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] not be disturbed (008-026). He recommends [[hollander|Thomas Hollander]] as the friend who should sit with Endicott (009-023), describing him as the person Endicott should see first upon regaining consciousness. Worth stays at the house overnight (008-079), positioning himself as both physician and gatekeeper.

## Relationships

- **[[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]** (patient): Treated for two years following a nervous breakdown. Worth's intimate knowledge of Endicott's cardiac vulnerability makes him one of the few people who understood how a fatal [[herbert-endicotts-heart-condition|heart attack]] could be induced.
- **[[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]]** (patient's wife): Worth is protective of her, insisting she not be disturbed and positioning himself as her advocate with the police.
- **[[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]]** (investigator): A professional but wary relationship. Worth serves as both medical expert and occasional obstacle — his authority over the sickroom limits police access.
- **[[hollander|Thomas Hollander]]** (recommendee): Worth names Hollander as the intimate friend best suited to sit with Endicott through the night.

## Analysis

Dr. Worth's role transcends that of a mere medical witness. His knowledge of Endicott's heart condition, his control over the sickroom, and his protective attitude toward [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] place him at the intersection of medical truth and investigative need. The adrenaline revival he performs is both life-saving and problem-creating: it keeps the case open but also introduces the possibility that Endicott might never speak (008-024).

Worth's characterisation as a polished, slightly vain professional — stroking his Vandyke, raising and lowering his eyebrows (003-036), gazing at his "low patent-leather shoes" (008-024) — provides a note of social comedy amid the grim proceedings. [[nurse-murrow|Nurse Murrow]]'s private assessment captures his dual reputation: "a little bigoted, perhaps, in some things, but so distinguished — admirable — a bachelor, too" (010-054).
