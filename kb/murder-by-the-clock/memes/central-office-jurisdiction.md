## Overview

The investigation of [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s death was shaped from the outset by a critical jurisdictional decision: whether the case warranted the resources of the [[new-york-police-department|Central Office]] Homicide Bureau or should remain a precinct matter. [[chief-andrews|Chief Andrews]], the head of the Homicide Bureau, initially responded to the scene but ultimately withdrew, leaving the case in the hands of [[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] and his precinct officers. This decision fundamentally constrained the investigation's resources and methodology.

## Initial Mobilization

When [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] first called the police, her call was routed from [[new-york-police-department|Centre Street headquarters]] to the local precinct station [001-009]. A patrolman was dispatched, followed shortly by [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]. After discovering Endicott's apparently lifeless body, Valcour immediately called the Central Office to report a possible homicide [002-017], triggering the arrival of [[chief-andrews|Chief Andrews]], the medical examiner, and a squad of detectives.

## Andrews's Assessment

[[chief-andrews|Chief Andrews]], a well-built, alert-looking man of about fifty [003-045], arrived with full homicide resources. But his preliminary assessment quickly undermined the case for Central Office involvement. After the medical examiner found no signs of forced entry and no definitive evidence of foul play, Andrews grew skeptical that a crime had occurred [006-034].

He articulated the prosecutorial challenge bluntly: "If it's a straight heart attack, as Dr. Worth claims, we might just as well drop it. Can you imagine getting up before a jury that's been shown a picture by the defense of a big husky like Endicott and saying, 'This man was scared to his death?'" [003-050]. The difficulty of proving [[fear-as-murder-method|death by fear]] to a jury weighed heavily in Andrews's calculus.

## Withdrawal and Delegation

Finding no compelling evidence of homicide, Andrews made the decision to withdraw Central Office resources: "No use in my sticking around, Valcour. We haven't a case yet, really, that calls for any Central Office work" [006-034]. He explicitly reassigned the matter to Valcour's precinct, saying robbery was a possibility and it was Valcour's precinct to handle [006-034].

Before leaving, Andrews acknowledged Valcour's reputation "in the department for the painless extraction of useful information from people" and encouraged him: "Go to it. And squeeze every drop that you can" [003-054]. This was both a compliment and an abdication — Valcour would have to work the case with precinct resources alone.

## Consequences for the Investigation

The withdrawal of the Central Office had several consequences:

- **Limited manpower**: [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]] relied primarily on precinct officers [[cassidy|Cassidy]], Hansen, and O'Brian rather than the full Homicide Bureau apparatus.
- **Reduced forensic support**: The Central Office's technical resources were no longer available.
- **Investigative autonomy**: Without Central Office oversight, Valcour was free to pursue his own theories and methods — including his unorthodox interrogation techniques.
- **Delayed escalation**: Even after Endicott was revived via [[adrenaline-injection|adrenaline]] and subsequently killed by a [[fatal-shooting-of-herbert-endicott|gunshot through the window]], the re-engagement of Central Office resources was not automatic.

## The Precinct-Central Office Dynamic

The jurisdictional tension reflected a broader reality of police work in the novel's era: the Central Office handled high-profile cases with clear evidence of major crime, while precinct detectives managed the vast majority of investigations. Andrews's decision to withdraw was procedurally defensible — without a corpse or clear evidence of foul play, a homicide investigation could not be formally justified. Yet this procedural correctness came at the cost of leaving a complex murder case in the hands of a single detective with limited support.
