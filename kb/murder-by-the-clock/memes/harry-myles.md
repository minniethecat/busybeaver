## Summary

Harry Myles was the husband of [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]], who died two years before the main events of the novel in a canoe accident on a lake in Maine. The circumstances of his death — he could not swim while [[marge-myles|Marge]] was a famous swimmer — fueled persistent rumors that [[marge-myles|Marge]] had murdered him. His death and a [[the-postscript-forgery|forged postscript]] added to one of his letters become critical elements in [[lieutenant-valcour|Valcour]]'s investigation of [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]]'s murder.

## The Canoe Accident

Harry and [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]] had a camp near an obscure lake in Maine. One evening, their canoe upset, and Harry drowned because he could not swim [005-055]. [[marge-myles|Marge]], in contrast, was famous for her swimming ability. This discrepancy gave rise to gossip that she had deliberately killed him, though no charges were ever brought.

## The Forged Postscript

[[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] later revealed that she had added a [[the-postscript-forgery|forged postscript]] to a letter Harry Myles had sent her before his marriage to [[marge-myles|Marge]] [028-034]. Harry never dated his letters, and the original was harmless. The postscript [[mrs-endicott|Mrs. Endicott]] added made it appear that Harry feared [[marge-myles|Marge]] was planning to murder him. She gave this doctored letter to [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]] when his interest in [[marge-myles|Marge]] seemed to be becoming dangerously serious [028-039].

## Valcour's Theory

[[lieutenant-valcour|Lieutenant Valcour]] used Harry Myles's death as the foundation of his theory against [[marge-myles|Marge Myles]]. He believed that [[marge-myles|Marge]] had indeed murdered Harry, and that [[madame-velasquez|Madame Velasquez]] had been blackmailing her with this knowledge ever since [029-008]. [[herbert-endicott|Herbert Endicott]] then used the [[the-postscript-forgery|forged postscript]] as leverage over [[marge-myles|Marge]], driving her to break into the Endicott house to retrieve the incriminating letter — setting in motion the chain of events that led to Endicott's death.
