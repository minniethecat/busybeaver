## Role in the Story

Susan Dangle is a key member of the criminal conspiracy led by her brother, [[dangle|James Dangle]]. Unlike Sime, who provides physical enforcement, Susan's role involves deception, impersonation, and intelligence-gathering. She infiltrates the household of [[maxwell-cheyne|Maxwell Cheyne]] by posing as another woman, using the access to gather information about the [[the-tracing|linen tracing]] and the [[arnold-price-cipher|cipher]] [008-021], [008-055].

## Background

Susan Dangle is introduced as "Miss Dangle" — the sister of James Dangle [006-022]. Her exact background before the events of the novel is not detailed, but her skills in impersonation suggest experience in deception. She is one of the few female characters in the criminal conspiracy, and her methods contrast with the direct physical tactics employed by her male counterparts.

> But Miss Dangle was not in a mood for pleasantries. [006-022]

## Key Actions

### Infiltration of Cheyne's Household

Susan's most significant operation is infiltrating the Blessington/Cheyne household. She gains access by claiming to be someone else — a deception that holds for a considerable period before being discovered [008-021]. Her presence inside the household gives the conspirators direct access to information about the investigation's progress.

> "But Susan Dangle was at our house for several days." [008-021]

### The Impersonation Exposed

Susan's deception is eventually uncovered when physical discrepancies are noticed. The woman who had been accepted as a guest was short, stout, and clearly not the person she claimed to be [008-055]. The exposure of her impersonation is a significant breakthrough in Cheyne's understanding of the conspiracy's reach.

> The woman was short, stout and the interior showed she was not Susan Dangle — or rather, not the person she had claimed to be. [008-055]

### Later Operations

Susan Dangle reappears in the later stages of the narrative. She is identified as one of the conspirators alongside her brother [013-047]. In the final confrontation aboard the [[the-lescaut|L'Escaut]], Susan is present and involved in the events that bring about the resolution [020-027], [020-030].

> Evidently they recognized Cheyne — among them were Joan Merrill and Susan Dangle. [020-027]

### The Final Confession

After the mutiny aboard the L'Escaut, Susan is one of the survivors who tells Inspector French the tragic outcome of the expedition.

> "Oh!" she cried, with a gesture as if to shut out the sight of some hideous dream. "Oh, it's been awful! I can't speak of it. They're dead! My brother James, Charles Sime, Mr. Merkel, most of the crew, dead — all dead!" [020-034]
