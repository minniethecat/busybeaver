# Mr. Pringle

**Mr. Pringle** is the city analyst in Plymouth, mentioned in [[plymouth-drugging|Chapter 1]]. He conducted the chemical analysis of the coffee served to [[maxwell-cheyne|Maxwell Cheyne]] at the [[edgecombe-hotel|Edgecombe Hotel]], providing key forensic evidence that a drug had been administered.

## Role in the Story

Mr. Pringle is introduced in Chapter 1 when the manager of the Edgecombe Hotel arranges for him to analyze the coffee dregs after Cheyne's sudden illness. The hotel doctor, a close friend of Pringle's, vouches for his prompt service. Pringle's analysis confirmed that the coffee cups contained brandy but no trace of a drug in those particular cups, though the plates used for the meal were also submitted for analysis [001-089].

> "I have already asked the doctor to have it done. He said he would get Mr. Pringle to do it at once: that's the city analyst. They're close friends, and Mr. Pringle would do it to oblige him." [001-089]

## Significance

Pringle's analysis results are relayed to Cheyne by the hotel manager shortly afterward. The finding that no drug was present in the cups themselves—despite Cheyne's certainty that he had been drugged—added to the mystery of the Plymouth incident. The negative result from the cup analysis shifted suspicion toward the plates and food rather than the drink [001-092].

Pringle does not appear in person in the narrative; his role is entirely off-stage, communicated through the hotel manager's reports.
