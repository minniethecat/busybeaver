Mrs. Sproule is the wife of Mr. Sproule and a resident of 17 Colton Street. She is described as an invalid, and her observations proved important to Inspector French's investigation.

## Role in the Story

When Inspector French was investigating the disappearance of Joan Merrill, he traced activity to the Colton Street area. Mrs. Sproule served as a key witness at this location. Mr. Sproule of 17 Colton Street provided testimony [013-071], and his wife's illness (as an invalid) meant she was frequently at home and able to observe neighborhood activity [015-047].

Mrs. Sproule had seen Joan Merrill wearing a distinctive garment: "Sproule saw her wearing it today" [014-071]. She also "had heard the motor stop in the street, and to French it seemed clear that Miss Merrill had been taken direct to Sime's, and kept there until 1:45 p.m. on the following day" [015-016].

On the day in question, Mrs. Sproule observed that "the yellow car had left Sime's house at about 1:45" [015-047], providing French with a precise timeline for the movements of the Blessington gang.
