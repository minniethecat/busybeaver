# Mr. Lowenthal

Mr. Lowenthal was the junior partner of Messrs. Merkel & Lowenthal, a shipping firm with offices in the Rue des Tanneurs, Antwerp [017-011]. Inspector French interviewed him while tracing James Dangle's movements after he sailed aboard the firm's freighter, the *L'Escaut* [017-013].

## Appearance and Demeanor

French's first impression of Lowenthal was deeply unfavorable. He noted "a mean, sly look, close-set, shifty eyes which would not meet French's, and a large mouth with loose, fleshy lips" [017-014]. His manner alternated between blustering and unctuously ingratiating, and French "took an instant dislike to him" [017-014].

## Interview with Inspector French

Lowenthal claimed ignorance of any criminal activity. He stated that Dangle had approached the firm as a writer seeking passage to describe a voyage to Casablanca for a book, and that Merkel agreed to take him as a paying passenger [017-013]. Lowenthal attributed the secrecy of the departure to the legal prohibition against taking passengers without a certificate [017-013].

## French's Suspicions

> French did not believe a single word of Lowenthal's statement, but recognized he lacked sufficient evidence for an arrest [017-022]. He later confronted Lowenthal directly, revealing his identity as a Scotland Yard detective inspector, but the junior partner held firm to his story [017-014].

## Role in the Conspiracy

French was satisfied of Lowenthal's complicity but doubted he could prove it in court [017-022]. Lowenthal had moved into Merkel's office during the senior partner's absence, suggesting the *L'Escaut* voyage was expected to be lengthy [017-013]. When directly accused, Lowenthal's "swarthy face turned a greenish hue and little drops of sweat showed on his forehead" [017-014], confirming French's assessment of him as a coward.
