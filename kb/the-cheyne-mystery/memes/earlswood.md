## Description

Earlswood is a secluded house situated at a crossroads on Dalton Road, Wembley. The house occupies the fourth corner of a crossroads intersection, diagonally opposite a property undergoing building operations. Its hall door is almost hidden from both road and lane by hedges and shrubs, making it an ideal covert location for the gang's activities.

> The two adjoining corners were occupied by houses which presented no interesting features, and in the fourth corner, diagonally opposite that of the building operations, stood Earlswood. Earlswood was particularly secluded, the hall door being almost hidden from both road and lane by hedges and shrubs. [010-006]

## Role in the Story

Dangle's address is given as "Earlswood, Dalton Avenue, Wembley" — this is the gang's second base after they evacuated [[hopefield-avenue|Hopefield Avenue]]. [009-094]

[[maxwell-cheyne|Cheyne]] and [[joan-merrill|Joan Merrill]] conduct a burglary of Earlswood in an attempt to recover the stolen cipher tracing. Joan rings the front door bell while Cheyne enters through the back garden. Cheyne successfully finds the tracing inside, but Dangle and Susan return unexpectedly, forcing a desperate escape. [010-034] [010-035]

Later, both Cheyne (with Dangle) and Inspector French (with his men) visit Earlswood for separate investigations. French conducts a detailed search of the premises, though the gang had by then evacuated. [012-019] [013-006] [015-003]

Cheyne and Joan note that Earlswood bears a strong resemblance to the house in Hopefield Avenue — "Queer thing that these two houses should be so much alike" [010-029].
