# Hopefield Avenue

## Description

Hopefield Avenue is a residential street situated in the outer suburbs of London. It is described as a relatively new thoroughfare penetrating into a recently developed settlement:

> [005-054] bore the imposing title of "Hopefield Avenue." This penetrated into what seemed to be a recently erected settlement

The avenue forms part of a suburban layout that includes a crossroads with [[alwyn-road]], and two adjoining lots were situated along both roads:

> [005-056] two adjoining lots, both along Hopefield Avenue and down the crossroad—Alwyn Road

The street is quiet and lightly trafficked, particularly in contrast to busier parts of London. At night it was notably deserted:

> [008-047] there was plenty of traffic, but Hopefield Avenue was deserted and their footsteps echoed loudly

The property known as [[laurel-lodge]] is located on this street, situated on a lot bounded in part by a paling fence that separates it from the avenue itself [007-008].

## Role in the Story

Hopefield Avenue serves as a key setting in the early chapters of *The Cheyne Mystery*. The house on this street—[[laurel-lodge]]—becomes the focal point of [[maxwell-cheyne]]'s investigation after he traces a criminal gang to this location:

> [010-011] the night he traced the gang to Hopefield Avenue

The events at Hopefield Avenue include a breaking-and-entering incident at Laurel Lodge [009-021], and Cheyne's own covert exploration of the property, during which he finds himself exhausted and hiding behind the paling fence [007-008]. His failure at the Hopefield Avenue house leads him to realise he is up against a formidable adversary [008-001].

The gang eventually abandons the Hopefield Avenue house, relocating their operations to another suburban crossroads property on [[dalton-road]]. The parallels between the two locations are noted explicitly:

> [010-029] this Earlswood and the one in Hopefield Avenue. Both at cross roads, both with similar features

This pattern of using suburban crossroads properties becomes a hallmark of the gang's methods, as confirmed when they later evacuate Dalton Road in the same manner:

> [011-071] been evacuated. Just as we left Hopefield Avenue so we have left Dalton Road

Hopefield Avenue thus represents the first major battleground between Cheyne and the criminals, and establishes the geographical motif—secluded suburban crossroads houses—that recurs throughout the novel. The gang's ability to turn the tables on their pursuers here foreshadows their resourcefulness:

> [011-065] they turned the tables on us in Hopefield Avenue. How you traced us I can't imagine
