# Antwerp

**Antwerp** (Dutch: *Antwerpen*, French: *Anvers*) is a major Belgian port city on the River Scheldt. In *The Cheyne Mystery*, it is the destination of [[inspector-french]]'s continental investigation, where he traces the conspirators' shipping connections through the firm of [[mr-merkel|Merkel]] & [[mr-lowenthal|Lowenthal]].

## In the Story

Antwerp enters the narrative in [[016-022]] when Inspector French puzzles over the place-name variations in [[arnold-price-cipher|Arnold Price's cipher]]: "Antwerpen and Anvers!"

The realization that these are the Dutch and French names for the same city provides a crucial clue for deciphering the cipher message, which uses the French form "Anvers" as part of its code.

In [[016-023]], French tries to recall a shipping firm in Antwerp whose name ends in "...lon" — leading him eventually to Merkel & Lowenthal.

The detail in [[016-025]] mentions "the Cathedral" and asks "Does monsieur know Antwerp?" as French conducts his inquiries.

French arrives in Antwerp in [[016-027]], taking an express train from [[brussels]] after his Channel crossing.

In [[016-028]], he purchases a map of Antwerp at a Brussels bookstall, preparing for his investigation in the city.

The Antwerp investigation is covered in detail in [[the-antwerp-investigation]], during which French traces the conspirators' movements through the port and the shipping offices.

## The Name Duality

The Dutch/French naming duality of Antwerp (Antwerpen/Anvers) serves as a key plot device.

When French notices that the cipher contains city names in their local variants, he recognizes a pattern that helps break [[arnold-price-cipher|the code]].

This linguistic clue mirrors the broader theme of identity and disguise that runs through the novel — just as Antwerp has two names, many characters in the story have dual identities.
