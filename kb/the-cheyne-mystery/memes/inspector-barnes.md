Inspector Barnes is a detective at Scotland Yard who served as the Yard's specialist on nautical and maritime matters. His expertise made him a valuable resource when Inspector French's investigation led to questions involving the sea.

## Role in the Story

When Inspector French needed to trace the movements of the gang across the English Channel, he turned to Barnes. French telephoned him directly: "Put me through to Inspector Barnes… That you, Barnes? I'm on" [019-095]. According to the narrative, "Barnes is our authority on things nautical" [019-096].

## Consultation with French

French introduced him as "Inspector Barnes" and invited him to sit, saying: "Seat yourself, Barnes, and listen to my tale" [019-099] [019-100]. Barnes listened to French's account of the case and responded with confidence: "Well, I should think so," as he lit a cigarette [019-101].

During their review of the geographical evidence, Barnes contributed frank commentary on the suspects: "Dirty savages. But people in open boats don't carry portmanteaus" [019-105]. He worked with a large atlas alongside French, pointing out locations: "About there," Barnes indicated a point north of a specific area [019-108]. After French telephoned his instructions, "Barnes went on" with the analysis [019-109], continuing to work through the geographical puzzle [019-111] [019-113].
