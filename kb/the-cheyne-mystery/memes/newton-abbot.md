# Newton Abbot

**Newton Abbot** is a market town in Devonshire, England, located on the main line of the Great Western Railway between Plymouth and Exeter. In *The Cheyne Mystery*, it serves as an important waypoint during the conspirators' surveillance of [[maxwell-cheyne|Maxwell Cheyne]].

## In the Story

Newton Abbot first appears when Cheyne receives a telegram summoning him there: "please come without fail to Newton Abbot by 5:15 train to meet self" [002-068]. This telegram, later revealed to be part of the conspirators' scheme, is designed to draw Cheyne away from [[warren-lodge|Warren Lodge]].

The town lies on the main line between Plymouth and Paddington [005-021]. During the train pursuit sequence, a local train from Plymouth to [[exeter|Exeter]] passes through Newton Abbot. Cheyne, attempting to evade his pursuers, pushes his bicycle into Newton Abbot station and entrusts it to a porter [005-022].

The train from Plymouth undergoes a change in its passenger composition when it reaches Newton Abbot, and here again Cheyne's movements become difficult for the conspirators to track [005-030]. This momentary confusion gives Cheyne a brief tactical advantage in the cat-and-mouse pursuit.

## Significance

Newton Abbot represents the mid-point in the railway pursuit sequence that dominates Chapter 5. As a junction station on the Great Western main line, it is a natural place for passengers to change trains or alter their routes — making it strategically important for both pursuer and pursued.
