## Role in the Story

Mr. Speedwell is a professional private investigator who enters the case at a critical moment, transforming [[maxwell-cheyne|Maxwell Cheyne]]'s amateur investigation into a professionally supported operation. Working for Horton and Lavender's Private Inquiry Agency, Speedwell provides the methodological expertise and institutional resources that Cheyne lacks [009-001]. His involvement is a turning point — what had been a solitary struggle becomes a coordinated campaign against [[dangle|James Dangle]]'s criminal enterprise.

> Cheyne called at the offices of Horton and Lavender's Private Inquiry Agency and asked if their Mr. Speedwell was within. By good fortune Mr. Speedwell was within. [009-001]

## Background

Speedwell is employed by Horton and Lavender's, a private detective agency in [[london|London]]. His professional background gives him access to investigative resources and a network of contacts that complement Cheyne's personal knowledge of the case. Speedwell's demeanor is described as melancholy and business-like — he is not given to emotional displays but approaches problems with calm, methodical precision [009-047].

## Key Actions

### Initial Consultation

Speedwell's first involvement comes when Cheyne consults him about the [[plymouth-drugging|Plymouth drugging]] incident. Speedwell questions Cheyne closely, laying stress on the absence of abnormal symptoms from the drug — a detail that later proves significant [003-004]. Speedwell remains initially pessimistic about the case [003-015], but his professional interest is engaged.

> Mr. Speedwell laid stress on this point, that there were not the slightest abnormal symptoms. [003-004]

### Strategic Direction

At the crucial meeting in his office, Speedwell takes charge of the investigation's direction. He endorses an aggressive approach, including what he euphemistically calls "the breaking and entering" — a calculated illegal act to recover the [[the-tracing|linen tracing]] from Dangle's possession [009-021]. This decision demonstrates Speedwell's pragmatic philosophy: conventional methods are insufficient against adversaries like Dangle, and calculated risks are necessary.

> "The breaking and entering." Speedwell spoke now in a decisive, business-like tone. [009-021]

### The Price of Information

Speedwell operates on a commercial basis, and his willingness to sell his knowledge to the highest bidder reflects the moral ambiguity of his position. When Cheyne balks at his fee, Speedwell's response is candid.

> "I sell my wares in the best market, Mr. Cheyne," he declared. "You couldn't blame me for that; it's only business. But I don't want to drive a hard bargain with you. I would rather have an amicable settlement." [009-052]

### The Melancholy Counselor

Speedwell's role extends beyond operational planning to strategic counsel. His "melancholy voice" [009-047] dispenses sober assessments that counter Cheyne's impulsiveness. He evaluates risks, particularly where [[joan-merrill|Joan Merrill]]'s safety is concerned, and advises on the timing and sequence of actions.

Speedwell was not above providing candor about the gang's capabilities:

> "This crowd is a pretty shrewd crowd, and they'll not, so to speak, be beating the air in this job of yours. They're going for something, and you may take it from me that something will be worth their going for. At least, if not, I'll eat my hat." [009-126]
