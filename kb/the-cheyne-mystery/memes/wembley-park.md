# Wembley Park

**Wembley Park** is an area in northwest London, served by a railway station on the Metropolitan line.

## Role in the Story

Maxwell Cheyne and Joan Merrill took a Wembley Park train from Baker Street shortly before nine one evening during their investigation [010-001]. They were traveling to the area to follow up on leads related to the criminal gang.

Their return journey proved eventful. After dining, they tubed back to Baker Street and took a second train to Wembley Park [010-031]. It was darker than during their previous visit, and Cheyne became concerned about being followed. Rather than risk being stopped at Wembley Park station, he decided to hang about at the end of the platform [010-056].

Cheyne ultimately chose to pay a driver to take them away from the station, offering a generous price to drive him from Wembley Park rather than taking the train back [010-058].

## Significance

The Wembley Park excursions demonstrated the increasing danger Cheyne and Joan faced as their investigation progressed. The need to avoid the station and arrange alternative transportation highlighted the real threat posed by the criminals who were monitoring their movements.
