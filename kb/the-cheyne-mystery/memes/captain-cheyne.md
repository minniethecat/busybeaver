Captain Cheyne was the father of Maxwell Cheyne, the protagonist of *The Cheyne Mystery*. A commander of one of His Majesty's smaller cruisers in the Royal Navy, he instilled in his son an intense love of the sea and of adventure.

## Background

Maxwell Cheyne was born in 1891, and his father "was a navy man, commander of one of His Majesty's smaller cruisers, and from him the boy presumably inherited his intense love of the sea and of adventure" [001-006]. Captain Cheyne had Irish blood in his veins, which contributed to his character [001-006].

During his naval career, Captain Cheyne's ship was stationed at Malta (Valetta), where he became acquainted with Hubert Parkes, a man who ran the Maltese end of a produce company. Parkes later described knowing Captain Cheyne "fairly intimately" during this period [001-020].

## Legacy

Captain Cheyne was remembered fondly by those who knew him. Parkes recalled that "a great favorite, Captain Cheyne was! The dull old club used to wake up when he came in" [001-020]. His widow (Mrs. Cheyne) kept a photograph of Valetta showing his ship lying in the Grand Harbor [001-021].

The family home, Warren Lodge on the estuary of the Dart, was originally owned by Captain Cheyne. After his death, the family's fortunes declined and they had to sell the property. However, when Maxwell's mother received a legacy from an uncle in Australia, she was able to buy back Warren Lodge, restoring the family to their ancestral home [001-008].
