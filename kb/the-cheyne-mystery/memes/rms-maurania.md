# RMS Maurania

The **RMS *Maurania*** (also styled *S.S. Maurania*) is an ocean liner central to the backstory of *The Cheyne Mystery*. It is the vessel on which [[arnold-price]] served as third officer and where the fabricated story of [[st-john-price]]'s death takes place. Though the *Maurania* never appears directly in the novel's present-day narrative, it is referenced throughout as the setting for several key events in the conspiracy's elaborate backstory.

## In the Story

### Arnold Price's Service

The *Maurania* is introduced in [003-084] as "an eight-thousand-ton liner trading to South Africa," on which the real Arnold Price was appointed third officer. A letter from Price to [[maxwell-cheyne]], dated from the *Maurania*, establishes the ship's role in the backstory and authenticates the connection between the two men.

In [003-085], the letter's heading confirms: "Arnold Price, third officer, S.S. *Maurania*" — a detail that later becomes crucial to verifying the truth of the St. John Price fabrication.

### The St. John Price Incident

According to the fabricated story told in [004-010], St. John Price took his return passage from South Africa on the *Maurania* after diplomatic business during the war. The ship was struck by another vessel while running without lights in the English Channel, creating the circumstances for Arnold Price's dramatic (and ultimately fatal) rescue attempt of his uncle.

As described in [004-012], the collision occurred at night near the end of the voyage. Arnold Price, though not on duty at the time, was on deck and attempted to pull a passenger clear just before the impact. A falling spar broke the passenger's back — and only then did Arnold discover the man was his own uncle, St. John Price.

### Verification and Exposure

In [008-004], [[inspector-french]]'s Admiralty contact confirms that the real Arnold Price had indeed been third officer of the *Maurania* at the time in question — a detail that lent credibility to the otherwise fabricated story. However, the same contact reveals that the real Arnold Price had recently disappeared from his ship in [[bombay|Bombay]].

In [011-044], [[dangle]] reveals his personal connection to the *Maurania*: "I was on the same ship in the engine-room." This explains how the gang obtained intimate knowledge of the ship's operations, enabling them to construct a convincing fabrication.

The full account in [011-045] describes how Arnold Price was injured aboard the *Maurania*: "when they got him aboard the *Maurania* and fixed up in bed they found that he was badly hurt — his back was injured."

## Significance

The *Maurania* serves as the **anchor of authenticity** in the gang's elaborate deception. Because the real Arnold Price genuinely served on the ship, and the real [[dangle]] crewed in its engine room, the conspirators were able to construct a story rich in verifiable detail. The ship thus represents the intersection of truth and fiction that makes the novel's central mystery so compelling.

The *Maurania*'s role also highlights the novel's engagement with maritime life — a recurring motif in Freeman Wills Crofts's work, reflecting the author's background in railway and marine engineering.
