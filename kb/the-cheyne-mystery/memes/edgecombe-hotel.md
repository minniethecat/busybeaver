# Edgecombe Hotel

## Description

The Edgecombe Hotel is located in Plymouth, a port city in Devonshire on the south coast of England. It features a lounge where guests take cocktails, a private room where the central crime of the novel takes place, and an upstairs area where guests stay overnight.

The hotel is a respectable establishment. It is here that Cheyne first encounters the man calling himself Hubert Parkes — a card bearing the legend "Mr. Hubert Parkes, Oakleigh, Cleeve Hill, Cheltenham" is brought to him by a page [001-015]. The hotel's manager takes the drugging incident seriously enough to hire a private investigator from the firm of Speedwell and Co.

> "In the Edgecombe: the Edgecombe Hotel, you know, in Plymouth. I am the manager." [001-056]

## Role in the Story

The Edgecombe Hotel is the setting for the inciting incident of *The Cheyne Mystery*. On a damp March day, [[maxwell-cheyne]] visits Plymouth on business and gravitates to the hotel for lunch. While resting in the lounge, he is approached by a stranger calling himself Hubert Parkes (later revealed as [[blessington]]). The man drugs Cheyne, searches his pocketbook for a vital document, and vanishes — setting in motion the entire chain of burglaries, kidnappings, and pursuit that follows. [001-005] [001-012] [001-018]

> "The card which the boy held out bore in fine script the legend: 'Mr. Hubert Parkes, Oakleigh, Cleeve Hill, Cheltenham.' Cheyne pondered, but he could not recall anyone of the name, and it passed through his mind that the page had probably made a mistake." [001-015]

The drugging at the Edgecombe Hotel becomes a known incident, investigated later by an inquiry agent named Speedwell, who was employed by the hotel manager. [009-010] This fact becomes important when Cheyne confronts Blessington directly:

> "Did you drug me in the Edgecombe Hotel in Plymouth a week ago and then go through my pockets, and did you the same evening burgle my house, break open my safe, and mishandle my servants?" [004-022]

The private detective Speedwell eventually identifies the perpetrator, providing the crucial link between the hotel crime and the wider conspiracy:

> "Simply, sir, that the man who drugged you in the Edgecombe Hotel in Plymouth was named Stewart Blessington, that he lived at Wembley, and that he drugged you in order to ascertain if you carried on your person a certain paper of which he was in search." [009-113]

Even late in the story, Blessington's charm as first displayed at the Edgecombe Hotel remains a notable characteristic — when Cheyne later encounters him at Earlswood, Blessington greets him "apologetically, but with the same charm of manner that he had displayed in the Edgecombe Hotel at Plymouth" [012-019].

## Events

- **The Approach**: Cheyne is approached by a stranger presenting a visiting card in the lounge of the Edgecombe Hotel. [001-015]
- **The Drugging**: Cheyne is drugged by [[blessington|Blessington]] (as Hubert Parkes). After drinks, he falls unconscious and his pockets are searched. [001-018] [001-056]
- **Overnight Stay**: Cheyne, having planned to return to Warren Lodge, is persuaded to remain overnight at the hotel by a woman purporting to be his sister. [001-079]
- **The Investigation**: The hotel manager later employs Speedwell's firm to investigate the alleged drugging.

> "I was employed — or rather my firm was employed — by the manager of the Edgecombe Hotel to investigate a case of alleged drugging which had taken place in the hotel." [009-010]
