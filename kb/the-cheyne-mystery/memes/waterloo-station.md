# Waterloo Station

**Waterloo Station** is a major railway terminus in central London.

## Role in the Story

Waterloo Station played a key role in the criminals' escape strategy. After abducting Maxwell Cheyne, the gang drove their yellow Armstrong Siddeley car near the station, where they executed a vehicle switch. Inspector French deduced that Blessington dropped the passengers at the departure side of Waterloo Station, then picked them up at the arrival side in a different vehicle — a black Napier [015-049]. This allowed the gang to change cars while the passengers mingled with the crowds inside the station.

Sergeant Burnett was assigned to investigate the station and confirmed the theory. A taximan on a nearby stand reported seeing an invalid lady in a sable-colored fur coat being helped into an old black Napier at the arrival side around 2:00 p.m. The other passengers included a second lady and a tall man, matching the descriptions of Joan Merrill, Susan, and Sime [016-005]. Dangle was not observed with them at the station [016-006].

## Significance

The Waterloo Station vehicle switch was a critical moment in the investigation. French considered it a validation of his deductive reasoning: "He had considered it likely that these people would use Waterloo Station to effect the change of cars, and now it seemed that they had done so" [016-006].

The location also figured in earlier sightings. A constable on point duty at the intersection of South and Mitchem Streets, near Waterloo Station, had noticed a yellow motorcar pass close beside him and turn into Hackworth's garage around 2 p.m. on the day of the gang's disappearance [015-018].
