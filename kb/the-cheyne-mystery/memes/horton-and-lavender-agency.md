## Origin

Horton and Lavender's Private Detective Agency was a London-based private investigation firm, located in Locke Street off Southampton Row. The firm employed Mr. Speedwell, a quiet, despondent-looking former police officer who served as their principal operative [415]. The agency was named after its two founders, Horton and Lavender, though neither appeared directly in the narrative — Speedwell was the firm's public face for the Cheyne case [191480].

## Role in the Story

Cheyne first engaged the agency on the recommendation of the manager of the Edgecombe Hotel in Plymouth, following the drugging incident and the Warren Lodge burglary [415]. Speedwell was assigned to investigate and subsequently made "mysterious inquiries on his own account" around Dartmouth.

After Joan Merrill's disappearance, Cheyne visited the agency's offices in London and confronted Speedwell about his lack of progress, demanding to see either Horton or Lavender directly [191480-191647]. The office girl's amused reaction suggested that Speedwell effectively *was* the agency — the partners Horton and Lavender were not readily available, and Speedwell had significant operational autonomy.

Later, Inspector French revealed to Cheyne that Speedwell had been dismissed from the police force on suspicion of taking bribes — though the matter could not be proved. French described Speedwell as "a dangerous man for an amateur to deal with," noting that Speedwell was eager to curry favor with Scotland Yard and "would do anything" to maintain his position [2392].

## Resolution

Despite Speedwell's questionable ethics, the agency's investigation did produce useful intelligence. Speedwell identified Dangle's address at Earlswood and provided information about the conspirators' movements. French acknowledged that Speedwell had "been quite useful" in the investigation, and later sent Speedwell a monetary reward — though this was primarily Joan Merrill's doing rather than French's initiative [2392][3452].
