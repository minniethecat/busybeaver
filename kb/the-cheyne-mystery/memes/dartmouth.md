# Dartmouth

**Dartmouth** is a historic port town in Devonshire, England, situated on the River Dart estuary. In *The Cheyne Mystery*, it is the town nearest to [[warren-lodge|Warren Lodge]], Maxwell Cheyne's home, and serves as the setting for several early incidents.

## In the Story

Dartmouth is closely associated with [[maxwell-cheyne|Maxwell Cheyne]] from the very beginning. The sealed envelope from [[arnold-price|Arnold Price]] is addressed to "Maxwell Cheyne, of Warren Lodge, Dartmouth, Devonshire" [003-085]. Cheyne's bank is located in Dartmouth [004-039], and he conducts his local affairs there.

The town also figures in the conspiracy's operations. Johnson's boat slip at Dartmouth is where the conspirators keep a dinghy, and it is from here that Lamson (Dangle) proposes a yachting trip to Cheyne [003-035]. The launch *Enid* carries the name "S. Johnson, Dartmouth" on its stern [004-062], providing one of the clues French later traces.

During the train pursuit sequence, Cheyne's movements through the Dartmouth area are tracked by the conspirators. The land east of Dartmouth forms a peninsula, and a ferry connection serves the opposite side of the river [005-007] [005-021].

## Significance

Dartmouth serves as Cheyne's home base and the geographic anchor for the early chapters. Its status as a port town is thematically relevant — the novel's plot revolves around sea voyages, ships, and maritime connections, and Dartmouth's harbor setting foreshadows the maritime adventures to come.
