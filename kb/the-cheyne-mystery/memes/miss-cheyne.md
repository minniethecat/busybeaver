Miss Cheyne, called Agatha, is a member of the Cheyne household at Warren Lodge. She appears alongside her mother, Mrs. Cheyne, in several key scenes of the investigation.

## Role in the Story

Agatha is first mentioned when the Hazeltons' servant James found her and Mrs. Cheyne on the doorstep of Warren Lodge, unable to gain entry after the burglary [002-009]. They had been ringing the bell "for some time, but could not attract attention," and James eventually used a ladder to enter through an upper window and open the door for them [002-009].

## Later Appearances

During the course of the investigation, Miss Cheyne telegraphed to know if she could help when Maxwell Cheyne was ill and staying at a nursing home, but the nurse, without troubling her patient, had replied "Not at present" [007-025]. She also provided information to persons inquiring about Cheyne's whereabouts, as when an acquaintance mentioned: "I knew you were ill because I was down at Warren Lodge a month ago looking for you and Miss Cheyne told me" [009-005].

Miss Cheyne played a significant but indirect role in the investigation when Inspector French discovered an important lead. French confirmed that Cheyne had not mentioned his visit to Plymouth to anyone outside his own household, and he found out from Mrs. and Miss Cheyne that they had not mentioned it either [014-002]. However, Miss Cheyne provided a crucial detail: she said the Plymouth visit had been discussed at lunch, which gave French the investigative lead he needed [014-002]. This lunchtime conversation, overheard or mentioned by someone at the table, proved to be a pivotal clue in the case.
