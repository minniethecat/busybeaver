Mrs. Bryce (referred to in the text as "Mrs. Bryce-Harris") is a fabricated identity used by Joan Merrill as a cover story during the investigation of Blessington's activities at Earlswood.

## Role in the Story

When Joan Merrill and Maxwell Cheyne planned to search Blessington's house at Earlswood (the former home of the late Mr. Redstone), Joan's plan required a plausible pretext in case anyone answered the door. She would "simply ask if Mrs. Bryce-Harris was at home and then apologize for having mistaken the address" [010-034].

The name served as a decoy — if a resident or passerby questioned Joan's presence at the front door, she would use inquiries about "the whereabouts of the mythical Mrs. Bryce-Harris, thus insuring time for her companion to beat a retreat" [010-036]. This allowed Cheyne, who was inside the house searching for evidence, adequate warning to escape.

## Significance

The use of the name Mrs. Bryce-Harris demonstrates the careful planning that Joan and Cheyne employed in their amateur investigation. The name was chosen to sound sufficiently conventional that it would not arouse suspicion, and the plan allowed Joan to remain at the door as a lookout while Cheyne searched the premises.
