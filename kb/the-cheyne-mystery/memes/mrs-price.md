Mrs. Price is the wife of Arnold Price, the cryptographer who created the cipher that plays a central role in the mystery. Though she does not appear directly in the narrative, her interests are represented in the negotiations about the equitable distribution of proceeds from the recovered documents.

## Role in the Story

Mrs. Price is mentioned during the negotiations about sharing the recovered assets. Cheyne proposed that "both Frau Schulz and Mrs. Price" should receive "equal shares with ourselves" from any recovery [011-063]. The suggestion was that all involved parties — "including Frau Schulz and Mrs. Price" — should "share equally" in the proceeds [011-065].

The arrangement recognized the contributions of both the Schulz and Price families to the cipher's creation and recovery. The plan would mean that "Frau Schulz would get her eighth and Mrs. Price would get a quite unexpected" windfall from the successful recovery of the cipher-related materials [011-068]. This unexpected benefit reflected Cheyne's sense of fairness towards the families whose lives had been entangled with the cipher's history.

## Relationship to Arnold Price

As the wife of Arnold Price, the cipher's original creator, Mrs. Price represents one of the two surviving families with legitimate claims to the cipher's value. Her husband's cryptographic work, which had lain dormant for years, became the key to unlocking a fortune — a legacy that would have passed to her as his surviving spouse.
