# Brixham

Brixham is a small town and fishing port at the northeast corner of the peninsula east of Dartmouth in South Devon. In *The Cheyne Mystery*, it was the first of three coastal towns searched by Maxwell Cheyne during his pursuit of the conspirators' motor launch [015-012].

## Geography

The peninsula east of Dartmouth is "shaped roughly like an inverted cone, truncated, and connected to the mainland by a broad isthmus" [015-012]. Brixham lies at the northeast corner, with Paignton further north beyond the isthmus and Torquay across Tor Bay. The road from Kingswear in the southwest to Brixham crosses a range of hills from which Tor Bay and the sea to the north and east are visible [015-012].

## Cheyne's Search

Cheyne bicycled to Brixham after watching the *Enid* depart from Dartmouth. From Inner Froward Point to Brixham was about seven miles by road [015-012]. At the little harbor, "a glance showed him that the *Enid* was not there," and he immediately continued to Paignton [015-012].

## Landing Site

> In retrospect, Cheyne understood that the conspirators "must have landed at Brixham, Paignton, or Torquay," as these were the only ports they could reach before he could give the alarm [019-022]. Brixham, being the smallest of the three, was the least suitable for a discreet landing, making Torquay the conspirators' natural choice.
