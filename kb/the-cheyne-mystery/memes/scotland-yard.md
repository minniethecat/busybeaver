# Scotland Yard

## Description

Scotland Yard is the headquarters of London's Metropolitan Police, located in a large building in central London. The novel depicts its interior — a small, plainly furnished room with a table-desk — where [[inspector-french|Inspector French]] conducts his business. The atmosphere is efficient and professional, characterized by French's "cheerful, good humored face" and the quiet authority that "induced confidence" in visitors. [013-001] [013-005]

> "Cheyne was ushered into a small, plainly furnished room, in which at a table-desk was seated a rather stout, clean-shaven man with a cheerful, good humored face and the suggestion of a twinkle about his eye." [013-001]

French's professional demeanor immediately puts visitors at ease, establishing Scotland Yard as a place where serious work gets done efficiently.

> The man was all attention, and his quiet decisive manner induced confidence. [013-005]

## Role in the Story

Scotland Yard serves as the institutional counterpoint to the criminals' operations. When [[maxwell-cheyne]] exhausts his own investigative resources, his thoughts turn to the Yard: "He was loath to confess his incompetence, but if the morning brought no news he would take the letter and the message he would go to Scotland Yard, no matter what the consequences." [011-003] [011-004]

> First, in his hot-blooded way he thought of buying a real pistol, returning to Earlswood, and shooting Blessington and Dangle unless they revealed her whereabouts. Then reason told him that they really might not know ... and his thoughts turned to Scotland Yard. He was loath to confess his own essays in illegality in such an unsympathetic milieu, but of course no hesitation was possible if Joan's safety was at stake. [011-003]

The institution represents the full power of the law — something the conspirators fear. When Cheyne threatens to go to the police, one of his captors retorts: "by all means. Just do go to Scotland Yard and make your complaint. And see how far you'll get." [012-005] But once [[inspector-french|Inspector French]] takes charge, the Yard's resources prove formidable: "On reaching the Yard next morning Inspector French began his day by compiling a list of the various points on which obvious investigations still remained to be made." [015-001]

> On reaching the Yard next morning Inspector French began his day by compiling a list of the various points on which obvious investigations still remained to be made. He had already determined that these should be carried through with the greatest possible dispatch. [015-001]

## Events

- **Cheyne's Decision**: After [[joan-merrill]] disappears, Cheyne resolves to seek help from Scotland Yard despite the risks. [011-003] [012-003]
- **The Consultation**: Cheyne meets Inspector French, who listens to the full story and agrees to take the case. [013-001] [013-002] [013-007]
- **The Investigation**: French coordinates the investigation from Scotland Yard, compiling evidence and directing inquiries. [015-001]
- **Final Resolution**: The case concludes with the arrest of the conspirators by Inspector French acting under Scotland Yard's authority. Inspector French makes the arrest personally, declaring his identity as the basis of his authority.

> "I am Inspector French of Scotland Yard," he announced, while Price and the first officer stood round expectantly. "You are Miss Susan Dangle. Where are the other members of this expedition?" [020-032]
