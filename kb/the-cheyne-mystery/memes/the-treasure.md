# The Treasure

**The Treasure** is the central object of pursuit in the Cheyne mystery — a cache of valuables hidden by [[otto-schulz|Otto Schulz]], a German U-boat captain, during World War I. Its location was encoded in the [[arnold-price-cipher|Arnold Price cipher]], and the race to recover it drives the novel's plot.

## Nature of the Treasure

The treasure was described in romantic terms typical of adventure fiction. Its exact nature — whether gold, jewels, or other valuables — was deliberately kept mysterious to heighten the stakes:

> …maps of desert islands on which treasure was buried, wills of which the testators had died intestate… [002-002]

The conspirators believed the cipher would lead to immense wealth:

> …telling where there was buried treasure! Isn't that all that is wanted? [007-087]

## Discovery and Concealment

[[otto-schulz|Otto Schulz]], while commanding a U-boat, came across something of extraordinary value. Rather than claim it openly during wartime, he concealed its location in a cipher, intending to recover it after the war. His death before he could act on it set the [[arnold-price-cipher|Arnold Price cipher]] adrift, eventually falling into the hands of [[dangle|James Dangle]]'s criminal gang.

[[inspector-french|Inspector French]] later speculated on its nature:

> "Sounds like treasure or salvage or something of that kind." [019-009]

> "I thought of treasure all right. This Schulz might have located some wreck with bullion aboard." [019-010]

## The Recovery

The treasure's location was ultimately traced to the wreck of [[the-lescaut|the L'Escaut]], where Schulz had hidden it. The recovery operation was described as salvage:

> …to a shallow place where its treasure could be salved. [019-117]

## Distribution

After the successful recovery, the proceeds were divided among the rightful claimants. [[joan-merrill|Joan Merrill]]'s generosity in distributing the shares was noted:

> …the girls and go off with the treasure and those who feared murder might be done. [020-041]

The distribution included shares for [[frau-schulz|Frau Schulz]] (the captain's widow), Mrs. Price ([[arnold-price|Arnold Price]]'s widow), [[maxwell-cheyne]], Joan Merrill, and [[inspector-french]].
