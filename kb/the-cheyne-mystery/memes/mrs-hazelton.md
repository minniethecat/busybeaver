Mrs. Hazelton is a neighbor of the Cheyne family who lived near Warren Lodge. The Hazeltons were the nearest neighbors with a telephone, and they generously allowed the Cheynes to receive messages through their line [001-079].

## Role in the Story

When Maxwell Cheyne was delayed in Plymouth, he telephoned Mrs. Hazelton and explained that "unforeseen circumstances had necessitated his remaining overnight" [001-079]. Mrs. Hazelton promised to have the message conveyed to Mrs. Cheyne at Warren Lodge [001-079].

Mrs. Hazelton's grown-up daughter, Ethel (Miss Hazelton), assisted with relaying communications. When the burglary at Warren Lodge was discovered, the Hazeltons' servant James was sent to investigate and found Mrs. Cheyne and Agatha locked out of the house [002-008][002-009].

## Later Involvement

Mrs. Hazelton's household continued to play a role throughout the investigation. Her housemaid was interviewed by Inspector French and provided information about the Cheyne household's movements [014-009][014-018]. Telephone calls were arranged from Mrs. Hazelton's house when needed [002-071].
