Mr. Horton was the co-founder of Horton and Lavender's Private Detective Agency, the firm that employed [[speedwell|Mr. Speedwell]]. By the time of the story's events, Horton had been dead for over ten years, but his name lived on in the agency's title.

## The Agency

Horton and Lavender's Private Detective Agency was located in London. The firm was well-regarded enough that [[maxwell-cheyne|Maxwell Cheyne]] sought their services when he needed investigative help [003-002]. Mr. Speedwell, the operative assigned to Cheyne's case, worked for this agency [003-002]. The agency was referenced externally as well: during a later phase of the investigation, an individual named Simmons was instructed to go to Locke Street, off Southampton Row, and find Speedwell of Horton and Lavender's Detective Agency [013-017].

## The Saturday Morning Confrontation

Nearly two months after his initial contact with the agency, Cheyne called at the offices of Horton and Lavender's Private Detective Agency and asked if their Mr. Speedwell was within [009-001]. By good fortune Speedwell was available, and Cheyne was ushered into his room [009-001]. However, the interview proved unsatisfactory, and Cheyne subsequently demanded to see a principal of the firm.

Cheyne shouted that it was important and "Either Mr. Horton or Mr. Lavender will do" [009-036]. When the office girl asked who he wanted to see, Cheyne repeated firmly, "Mr. Horton or Mr. Lavender," fixing her with a wrathful stare [009-038].

## Legacy

The office attendant, struggling between apprehension and amusement at Cheyne's demand, finally revealed the truth: "Mr. Horton has been dead over ten years and Mr. Lavender at least five" [009-042]. Despite both founders being deceased, the agency continued to operate under their names, a testament to the reputation the firm had established during Horton's lifetime. The agency's operative Speedwell continued to handle investigations under its longstanding name.
