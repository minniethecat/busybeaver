Miss Ethel Hazelton is the grown-up daughter of Mrs. Hazelton, the Cheyne family's telephone-owning neighbors. She played an active role in communicating the news of the Warren Lodge burglary to Maxwell Cheyne.

## Role in the Story

When Maxwell Cheyne called the Hazelton residence, it was Ethel Hazelton who answered. Her voice was one "he recognized," and she spoke "hurriedly," with "perturbation in her tones" [002-008]. She informed Cheyne of the disturbing events at Warren Lodge — that James had been sent over and found Mrs. Cheyne and Agatha locked out, and that the safe had been opened [002-008][002-009].

Cheyne expressed his gratitude, telling her it was "more than good of you both" [002-017]. Ethel also mentioned that she had been "ringing up hotels in Plymouth for the last half hour" trying to locate Cheyne after receiving the message from Mrs. Cheyne [002-013].

## Information Shared

Later in the investigation, Inspector French noted that he had heard "no details other than what Miss Hazelton mentioned on the telephone" regarding certain aspects of the case [002-051].
