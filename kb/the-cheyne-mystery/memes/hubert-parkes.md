Hubert Parkes is a person of interest in the Cheyne Mystery, first mentioned when his visiting card was presented to Maxwell Cheyne at his club.

## First Appearance

Mr. Hubert Parkes first appears when a page at Cheyne's club presents his visiting card: "Hubert Parkes, Oakleigh, Cleeve Hill, Cheltenham" [001-015]. Cheyne "pondered, but he could not recall anyone of the name, and it passed through his mind that the page had probably made a mistake" [001-015]. This visit proved to be the beginning of the chain of events that drew Cheyne into the mystery.

## Edgecombe Hotel Connection

Parkes is later encountered at the Edgecombe Hotel, where he is described as "Hubert Parkes of Edgecombe Hotel notoriety" [006-002]. He is observed "looking on with his back to the fire" [006-002] during a confrontation at the hotel, suggesting he was present at key moments in the unfolding events surrounding Cheyne's drugging.

## Significance

Parkes serves as one of the early clues that Cheyne was being drawn into a larger conspiracy. His unexpected visit to Cheyne's club, followed by his appearance at the Edgecombe Hotel during the critical incident, suggests he may have been connected to the Blessington gang's operations.
