# Sergeant Burnett

**Sergeant Burnett** is a police sergeant at Scotland Yard, working under [[inspector-french|Inspector French]]. He was assigned to surveillance duty at Waterloo Station during the investigation into the disappearance of [[joan-merrill|Joan Merrill]].

## Role in the Story

Sergeant Burnett appears in Chapter 16, reporting to Inspector French after a full day's surveillance at Waterloo Station. French had deduced that the suspects would likely use Waterloo Station to change cars, and Burnett's assignment was to confirm this theory.

Burnett spent the entire day from early morning at the station, and eventually obtained the crucial information French needed. A taximan on a nearby stand had been called to the arrival side of the station at approximately 2:00 p.m., drawing up behind an old black car (thought to be a Napier). The taxi driver's testimony provided French with the lead he was looking for [016-005].

> "Sergeant Burnett, the man he had put on the Waterloo Station job, was waiting for him, and reported success in his mission." [016-005]

## Significance

Burnett's surveillance work at Waterloo Station confirmed French's deduction about the suspects' movements. The evidence he gathered—the taxi driver's account of the old black car—helped French trace the route taken by [[blessington|Blessington]] and his accomplices after they changed vehicles. This intelligence contributed to French's decision to travel to Belgium to continue the pursuit [016-006].

Burnett does not appear again after delivering his report. His role is that of a competent subordinate executing a specific surveillance task.
