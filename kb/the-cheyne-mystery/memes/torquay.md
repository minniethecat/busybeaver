# Torquay

Torquay is a coastal resort town on the English Channel in South Devon, situated on the north shore of Tor Bay. In *The Cheyne Mystery*, it serves as the landing place of the motor launch *Enid* during the conspirators' flight, and the scene of Maxwell Cheyne's desperate pursuit [019-022].

## Geography

Torquay lies north of the peninsula east of Dartmouth, across Tor Bay from Paignton and Brixham. The town is connected by a branch of the Great Western Railway running from Newton Abbot through Torquay and Paignton to Kingswear [019-022]. Torquay is approximately eleven to twelve miles from Inner Froward Point by sea, a distance the *Enid* could cover in about twenty-five minutes [015-012].

## Role in the Story

After the *Enid* left Dartmouth, Cheyne bicycled frantically along the coast road trying to determine her destination. At Paignton, a coastguard reported seeing a launch crossing the bay towards Torquay about three-quarters of an hour earlier [015-012]. Cheyne raced the three miles to Torquay and found the *Enid* lying at moorings in the inner harbor [015-013].

## The Escape

> The conspirators had landed at Torquay before Cheyne could alert the authorities. He realized that "they must have landed at Brixham, Paignton, or Torquay, and of these Torquay was obviously most suitable to their purpose, being larger than the others and their arrival therefore attracting correspondingly less attention" [019-022].

From Torquay, Price and Lewisham took a taxi to Totnes, approximately ten miles by road, where they boarded a train to Exeter [019-022]. This route allowed them to escape before any police alert could be raised.
