# Baker Street

**Baker Street** is a well-known street in central London, also served by an underground station on the Metropolitan line.

## Role in the Story

Baker Street served as the departure point for [[maxwell-cheyne|Maxwell Cheyne]] and [[joan-merrill|Joan Merrill]]'s journey to Wembley Park. They took a Wembley Park train from Baker Street shortly before nine one evening, and a few minutes later alighted at the station whose name was afterwards to become a household word throughout the British Empire [010-001].

On another occasion, after a leisurely dinner, they tubed to Baker Street and again took the train to Wembley Park [010-031]. As they reached Baker Street on the return trip, Joan turned to Cheyne with concern, suggesting that she had had an idea: "Look here, I believe I've got an idea" [010-016]. This moment marked a turning point in their awareness of the danger they faced, as Joan had noticed they were being followed.

## Significance

Baker Street represented a transit hub in the story, connecting central London to the northwestern suburbs where Cheyne's investigation took him. The repeated use of this station as a departure point — first for reconnaissance and later for the burglary — underscored the methodical nature of Cheyne and Joan's investigative journeys. The return via Baker Street also served as the setting for Joan's pivotal realization that they were being tracked by the gang.
