Arthur Lamson is a central antagonist in *The Cheyne Mystery*. He initially presented himself as an inventor seeking Cheyne's help, but was later revealed to be part of a conspiracy involving the Price family inheritance and the cipher created by Arnold Price.

## First Encounter

Lamson first contacted Cheyne at 17 Acacia Terrace, Bland Road, Devonport, where he was described as "a youngish man of middle height and build, with the ruggedly chiselled features usually termed hard-bitten, a thick black toothbrush mustache, and glasses" [003-023].

## The Yacht Abduction

Lamson lured Cheyne onto a motor launch near Plymouth under the pretense of testing a submarine detection device. Once aboard the yacht *The Enid*, Lamson locked Cheyne in a cabin and informed him that he would be held captive until he complied with demands. "I must tell you that you will stay there until you do what we want," Lamson said, with a "whimsical smile curling his lips" [003-074].

During Cheyne's imprisonment, Lamson remained unflappable in the face of his prisoner's rage. When Cheyne "stormed and fumed" and attempted to break through the cabin door, Lamson "shrugged his shoulders at the outburst" and simply closed the viewing panel [003-076].

## True Identity

Lamson later revealed that his name was an alias: "I must admit to you that Lamson is not my name — it is Price, and the Arnold Price whom you knew during the war is my second cousin" [004-009]. Further investigation revealed that even this was a deception — "The man who had introduced himself as Lamson and afterwards said his name was Price bore neither of these appellations: his name was Dangle" [006-024].

## Motivations

Lamson's actions were driven by the Price family's claim to the Hull barony. He and his associates needed Cheyne's cooperation — or at least his silence — while they pursued the inheritance. The cipher created by Arnold Price was a key element in this scheme.
