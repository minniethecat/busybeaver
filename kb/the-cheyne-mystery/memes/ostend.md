# Ostend

**Ostend** (Dutch: *Oostende*, French: *Ostende*) is a Belgian port city on the North Sea coast. In *The Cheyne Mystery*, it serves as a transit point for [[inspector-french]] during his continental investigation to trace the conspirators who have fled England.

## In the Story

Ostend first appears in [016-007] when Inspector French prepares for his journey to the Continent. He books a berth on the 2:00 p.m. boat-train from Victoria Station, traveling via the Harwich–Zeebrugge route (Ostend being a major reference point on this service). The [[harwich]]-Zeebrugge boat departs at 8:40 p.m.

In [016-010], French's train journey is described: after disembarking at Zeebrugge, travelers join the main railway line from Ostend, entering Brussels a few minutes later. This places Ostend as a major node in the European rail network connecting England to the Continent.

The linguistic detail emerges in [016-022] when French puzzles over place-name variations: "Oostende and Ostende" appear alongside "Antwerpen and Anvers" as examples of the Dutch/French naming duality in Belgium — a clue that helps French crack [[arnold-price-cipher|Arnold Price's cipher]].

Ostend is also mentioned in [016-027] as the origin point of "a great through express from Ostend to Brussels, Aix-la-Chapelle, and beyond" — the kind of international train service that was a hallmark of early 20th-century European travel.

## Travel Context

In the novel's period (1920s), Ostend was one of the primary gateways between Britain and Continental Europe, alongside Calais and Boulogne. The city's railway station connected directly to the European express network, making it a natural transit point for [[inspector-french]]'s investigation in Belgium and beyond.
