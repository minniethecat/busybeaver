## Description

Liverpool Street is a major London railway terminus. In the novel, it serves as the departure point for continental travel via the Harwich-Zeebrugge boat route.

> Having too much to see to at the Yard to catch the 2:00 p.m. from Victoria for Ostend, he rang up and engaged a berth on the Harwich-Zeebrugge boat, and that night at 8:40 p.m. he left Liverpool Street for Belgium. [016-007]

## Role in the Story

Liverpool Street station features briefly as Inspector French's departure point when he travels to Belgium in pursuit of the investigation. It is the London terminal for the Great Eastern Railway's boat train service to Harwich, connecting to the overnight ferry to Zeebrugge.
