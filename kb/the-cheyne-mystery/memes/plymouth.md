## Role in the Story

Plymouth is the setting for the opening act of *The Cheyne Mystery*. It is here that [[maxwell-cheyne|Maxwell Cheyne]] travels on business, checks into the [[edgecombe-hotel|Edgecombe Hotel]], and falls victim to the drugging that sets the entire mystery in motion [001-005]. The city reappears throughout the narrative as a key node in the geography of the crime — a place where conspirators meet, telegrams are dispatched, and the threads of the investigation converge.

> The episode took place in the Edgecombe Hotel in Plymouth, and from which started that strange and terrible series of events. [001-005]

## Location and Geography

Plymouth is a historic port city on the south coast of Devon, England. In the novel, it serves as a key transportation hub. Cheyne travels to Plymouth from his home at [[warren-lodge|Warren Lodge]] on a March day, leaving his car at a garage in the city [001-012]. The city's position on the rail network makes it a natural meeting point — characters arrive and depart by train, and telegrams are sent from the General Post Office in Plymouth [002-067].

> In due course he reached Plymouth, left his machine at a garage, and proceeded to the Edgecombe Hotel. [001-012]

## Key Events

### The Drugging at the Edgecombe Hotel

The most significant event to take place in Plymouth is the drugging of Maxwell Cheyne at the [[edgecombe-hotel|Edgecombe Hotel]] [001-056]. The hotel manager later confirms details of the incident, and the drugging becomes the subject of investigation by both Cheyne and eventually [[inspector-french|Inspector French]]. The [[plymouth-drugging|Plymouth drugging]] is the inciting incident of the entire novel.

> "The Edgecombe Hotel, you know, in Plymouth. I am the manager." [001-056]

### Cheyne's Return Visit

Following the drugging, Cheyne returns to Plymouth the next morning to investigate [003-002]. His return underscores the city's importance as a site of evidence-gathering. It is also from Plymouth that the conspirators coordinate their movements, using the city's communication infrastructure.

### The Telegram Trail

The General Post Office in Plymouth plays a crucial role in the investigation. A telegram sent from Plymouth at 3:17 p.m. becomes a key piece of evidence [002-067]. The message, sent to Mr. Ackfield of Plymouth — who is revealed to be the conspirators' lawyer — provides a critical link in tracing the criminal network [002-069].

> A telegram was dispatched from the General Post Office in Plymouth at 3:17 that afternoon. [002-067]

### The Mystery of Foreknowledge

A persistent puzzle throughout the investigation is how the conspirators knew Cheyne would be in Plymouth on that particular day. Cheyne had mentioned his visit only to his mother and sister, yet Blessington (posing as Parkes) was clearly waiting for him at the hotel.

> But even more puzzling than this was the question: How did Parkes, if that was really his name, know that he, Cheyne, was coming to Plymouth that day? It was true that he had mentioned it to his mother and sister a couple of days previously, but he had told no one else and he felt sure that neither had they. But the man had almost certainly been expecting him. [001-096]
