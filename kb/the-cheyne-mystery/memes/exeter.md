# Exeter

**Exeter** is a city in Devonshire, England, situated on the River Exe. In *The Cheyne Mystery*, it serves as a key railway junction during the conspirators' train pursuit of [[maxwell-cheyne|Maxwell Cheyne]].

## In the Story

Exeter lies on the Great Western Railway line between Plymouth and London Paddington, approximately "twenty miles to the northeast" of [[dartmouth|Dartmouth]] [005-021]. When Cheyne attempts to travel to London to consult with his solicitor, the conspirators mount an elaborate train pursuit to prevent him from reaching his destination.

As the slow local train from Plymouth approaches Exeter, Cheyne becomes aware that he is being followed [005-023]. He observes [[lewisham|Tom Lewisham]] and [[arnold-price|Price]] among the passengers, and at the Exeter end of the platform he tries to assess the situation [005-024].

The train reaches Exeter at 5:02 p.m. [005-037], and Cheyne, still attempting to evade his pursuers, considers his options. Two expresses leave Exeter for London, and the conspirators' positioning at the station is designed to intercept him regardless of which train he boards.

## Significance

Exeter represents a crucial point in the cat-and-mouse pursuit that characterizes the novel's middle chapters. As a major railway junction, it is the last place where Cheyne might plausibly shake off his pursuers before reaching London — but the conspirators' careful planning ensures they maintain their surveillance through the station.
