Sergeant Carter is a police officer at Scotland Yard who served under Inspector French during the investigation into the disappearance of Joan Merrill and the activities of the Blessington gang.

## Role in the Story

Sergeant Carter was one of Inspector French's trusted men, called upon for field operations. When French assembled a team to search for Joan Merrill, Carter was among the plain-clothes officers deployed [013-020].

## Key Operations

During the raid on Sime's house at 12 Colton Street, Putney, French directed Carter to cover the back of the property: "Carter and Jones and Marshall go down there and watch the back" [013-020]. Later, Carter was tasked with neighborhood inquiries: "You, Carter, make inquiries in these houses to the right" [013-026].

When French began the detailed search of Blessington's house at Earlswood, he "called Sergeant Carter and a couple more men" to assist [015-003]. After completing the Earlswood search, French sent two men back to the Yard, but kept Carter with him: "while with Sergeant Carter he drove to Horne Terrace" to examine Joan Merrill's rooms [015-012]. Following that, "he and Carter began another job, quite as detailed and a good deal more wearisome than the others" — questioning every resident in the house [015-013].
