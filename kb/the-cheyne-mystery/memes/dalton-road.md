# Dalton Road

## Description

Dalton Road is a road on the fringe of north-west London, near Wembley, about ten minutes' walk from the railway station in a north-easterly direction. It is described as little more than a lane, with street lamps but otherwise largely unlit, and bordered by detached houses built in the style of some forty years prior, each standing in its own grounds with trees, shrubs, and high partition hedges. [010-003] [010-004]

> Dalton Road proved, save for its street lamps and footpath, to be little more than a lane. It led somewhat windingly in an easterly direction off the main road. The country at this point was more thickly populated and there was quite a number of houses in view. All were built in the style of forty years ago, and were nearly all detached, standing in small grounds or lots. [010-004]

A narrow lane running north and south intersects Dalton Road, and at the four corners of this intersection stand houses, each surrounded by unusually large lots with plenty of trees. It is at this crossroads that the house called Earlswood is located, diagonally opposite a property undergoing building extension. [010-006]

Dalton Road debouches at its far end onto Watling Street, the main road between London and St. Albans. [010-008]

## Role in the Story

Dalton Road is the location of the gang's second hideout after they evacuated [[hopefield-avenue|Hopefield Avenue]]. The house called Earlswood, situated at a crossroads along Dalton Road, becomes the focus of [[maxwell-cheyne|Maxwell Cheyne]]'s second attempt to recover the stolen cipher tracing. [011-071]

Cheyne and [[joan-merrill|Joan Merrill]] first reconnoitre Dalton Road at night, noting the geography and the seclusion of [[earlswood|Earlswood]] — its hall door is almost hidden from both road and lane by hedges and shrubs. Cheyne observes that the trees around the property would shield them from being overlooked by neighbours should they need to break in. [010-006] [010-007]

> "Lucky it's got all those trees about it," Cheyne whispered as they passed on down Dalton Road. "If we have to burgle it we can do it without being overlooked by the neighbors." [010-007]

On their second visit, Cheyne and Joan execute their plan: while Joan rings the front door bell to confirm the house is empty, Cheyne crosses Dalton Road, slips into Earlswood through the back garden, and searches the house for the tracing. He succeeds in recovering it, but Dangle and Susan return unexpectedly, forcing a desperate flight. Cheyne escapes out the back, down the garden lane, and turns away from Dalton Road into the night, throwing the tracing over a wall en route for Joan to retrieve later. [010-035] [010-052]

The gang soon evacuates Dalton Road as a precaution. Dangle later tells Cheyne: "Just as we left Hopefield Avenue so we have left Dalton Road. Our party — and Miss Merrill — have taken up their quarters at another house some distance from town." [011-071]

Dalton Road appears again when Cheyne, after being released by the gang, walks along it towards the station a free man with the tracing in his pocket. [012-068] Later, Inspector French leads a police raid on Earlswood, with cars swinging into Dalton Road and surrounding the house — but the gang has already fled. [013-014]
