## Description

Harwich is a port town in Essex, on the east coast of England. In the novel, it is the departure point for the Great Eastern Railway's boat train service to the continent, with overnight ferries sailing from Parkeston Quay to Zeebrugge, Belgium.

> Having too much to see to at the Yard to catch the 2:00 p.m. from Victoria for Ostend, he rang up and engaged a berth on the Harwich-Zeebrugge boat, and that night at 8:40 p.m. he left Liverpool Street for Belgium. [016-007]

## Role in the Story

Inspector French, unable to catch the afternoon train from Victoria to Ostend due to work at Scotland Yard, books passage on the Harwich-Zeebrugge overnight ferry instead. He departs from [[liverpool-street|Liverpool Street]] station at 8:40 p.m. and boards the ferry at Parkeston Quay at 10:30 p.m., arriving in Belgium the following morning. [016-007] [016-009]

This journey marks French's first trip to Belgium and the beginning of the continental phase of his investigation into Joan Merrill's disappearance.
