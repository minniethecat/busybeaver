## Summary

The Dart Chase was Maxwell Cheyne's solo pursuit of the conspirators — James Dangle, Arnold Price, and their accomplice Lewisham — across South Devon and ultimately to London, following their escape by boat after Cheyne's captivity aboard the *L'Escaut*. Cheyne tracked the fugitives from Dartmouth to Torquay, then by train through Newton Abbot to Exeter and London, demonstrating determination and resourcefulness while operating alone without police assistance .

## Context

The pursuit began immediately after Cheyne returned to Dartmouth from his forced voyage aboard the *L'Escaut*. Dangle and his associates had landed at Brixham, Paignton, or Torquay and were attempting to flee. Rather than contacting the police, Cheyne made the impulsive decision to follow them himself, reasoning that he might learn more about their organization and the secret they were pursuing .

## Details

Cheyne started by rowing across the Dart estuary to Warren Lodge, where he collected money, his motor bicycle, and waterproof gear. He crossed the river by ferry to Kingswear and cycled toward Brixham, searching for the *Enid* — the launch Dangle had used . At Brixham harbour the *Enid* was not there; proceeding to Paignton, he again found nothing [741]. At Torquay, however, he located the launch at moorings and learned from local boatmen that two men had disembarked and taken a taxi toward the railway station [741].

Racing to Torquay station, Cheyne discovered the men had taken a train toward Newton Abbot. He cycled the six miles to Newton Abbot at top speed, arriving just as the connecting train from Plymouth was due . Boarding the train, he spotted Lewisham in a third-class compartment and later observed Price — who had dramatically altered his appearance by removing his moustache, glasses, and changing his hat .

At Exeter, both Price and Lewisham changed trains, boarding the London express. Cheyne followed, buying a first-class ticket to Paddington . He tracked them through Paddington station, hailed a taxi, and instructed the driver to follow the fugitives' cab. The pursuit through London was initially successful, passing through Praed Street, Edgware Road, and Maida Vale, but Cheyne lost them when roadworks caused a traffic jam . After hours of searching and questioning taxi drivers at Paddington, he eventually located the driver who had carried Price and Lewisham, who directed him to the Hopefield Avenue area — leading to his later discovery of the conspirators' hideout [789].

## Consequences

Although Cheyne lost the immediate chase in London traffic, the pursuit was not in vain. His tenacity led him to the general vicinity of Hopefield Avenue, and the information from the taxi driver eventually helped him locate Laurel Lodge — the conspirators' headquarters. This discovery set the stage for the Hopefield Avenue break-in and the subsequent violent confrontation. The chase also demonstrated Cheyne's growing skill as an amateur detective, foreshadowing his later collaboration with Joan Merrill.
