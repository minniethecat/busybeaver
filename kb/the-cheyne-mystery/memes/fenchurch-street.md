# Fenchurch Street

**Fenchurch Street** is a street in the City of London, located in the financial district.

## Role in the Story

After Maxwell Cheyne's father died suddenly when Maxwell was nine years old, the family's financial circumstances changed dramatically. The dreams of education at Harrow and Cambridge were abandoned, and instead, young Maxwell was educated at the local grammar school before entering the office of a Fenchurch Street shipping firm as a junior clerk [001-007].

Cheyne spent five years working as a clerk at this Fenchurch Street firm. During this time, he came into contact with Arnold Price, a junior officer on one of the firm's ships. The two young men became friendly during the visits of Price's ship to port [003-084].

## Significance

Fenchurch Street represented the pre-war, pre-investigation phase of Cheyne's life — his modest beginnings as a clerk before his military service, his wounding at the front, and his eventual involvement in the mystery that would define his later years. It was also the place where he formed the connection with Arnold Price, a relationship that would later prove significant to the unfolding mystery.
