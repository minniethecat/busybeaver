# Frau Schulz

**Frau Schulz** is the widow of [[otto-schulz|Otto Schulz]], a German U-boat captain who discovered a hidden treasure during World War I. She appears in the story as a beneficiary of her late husband's secret.

## Role in the Story

Frau Schulz is mentioned in connection with the distribution of the treasure discovered by her husband. The recovery of Otto Schulz's secret leads to negotiations about how the proceeds should be divided among the interested parties.

> It was greed unanimously to send both Frau Schulz and Mrs. Price equal shares… [011-063]

## The Secret Inheritance

After Otto Schulz's death, his widow became the rightful heir to the knowledge of the treasure he had concealed. The [[arnold-price-cipher|Arnold Price cipher]] held the key to locating the treasure, and Frau Schulz's claim was recognized alongside that of [[arnold-price|Arnold Price]]'s widow, Mrs. Price.

> …all, including Frau Schulz and Mrs. Price, to share equally… [011-065]

The distribution was eventually accepted without dispute:

> …he would not have died. Frau Schulz would get her eighth and Mrs. Price… [011-068]

## Schulz's Dying Wish

Otto Schulz's concern for his wife "Magda" was evident in his dying words to Arnold Price. Even as he faced death, his thoughts turned to ensuring she would benefit from his discovery.

> "But," he said, "there is one condition. You must first swear to me on your sacred honor that if you make anything out of it you will, after the war, try to find my wife and hand her one-eighth of what you get. I say one-eighth, because if you get any profits at all they will be so enormous that one-eighth will be riches to Magda." [011-055]

This provision, overheard by James Dangle in the hospital ward, became the moral foundation for including Frau Schulz in the eventual distribution of the treasure. The conspirators, despite their criminal methods, acknowledged this obligation.

> We thought that as far as you were concerned, you were probably expecting nothing and would therefore suffer no disappointment, and we agreed unanimously to send both Frau Schulz and Mrs. Price equal shares with ourselves. [011-063]
