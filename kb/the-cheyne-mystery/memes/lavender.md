Mr. Lavender was the co-founder of Horton and Lavender's Private Detective Agency, alongside [[horton|Mr. Horton]]. By the time of the story, Lavender had been dead for at least five years, but the agency continued to bear his name.

## The Agency

The firm of Horton and Lavender operated as a private detective agency in London. The agency employed operatives including [[speedwell|Mr. Speedwell]], who was assigned to [[maxwell-cheyne|Maxwell Cheyne]]'s case after the events at Plymouth and Warren Lodge [003-002]. Speedwell, of Horton and Lavender's Private Detective Agency, listened with attention to the tales of the drugging and the burglary, thenceforward appearing at intervals and making mysterious inquiries on his own account [003-002].

## The Saturday Morning Visit

Nearly two months after the initial investigation began, Cheyne called at the offices of Horton and Lavender's Private Detective Agency [009-001]. He asked if Mr. Speedwell was within, and by good fortune he was available [009-001]. However, after an unsatisfactory interview with Speedwell, Cheyne demanded to see a principal of the firm, shouting that it was important and "either Mr. Horton or Mr. Lavender will do" [009-036].

The office attendant seemed half startled and half amused by Cheyne's demand and asked who he wanted to see [009-037]. Cheyne repeated firmly, "Mr. Horton or Mr. Lavender," fixing her with a wrathful stare [009-038]. The girl stammered that she did not know where they were, and the corners of her mouth twitched with suppressed amusement [009-039].

## Legacy

When Cheyne continued to demand to know when the principals would be in, the girl finally revealed the truth: "Mr. Horton has been dead over ten years and Mr. Lavender at least five" [009-042]. Cheyne could only glare at her as the reality of the situation sank in [009-043]. The agency nevertheless maintained its original name, a common practice for established firms. The operative Speedwell handled cases under the agency's longstanding reputation, and the firm's name continued to appear in references to the investigation [013-017].
