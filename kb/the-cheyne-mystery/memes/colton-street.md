# Colton Street

## Description

Colton Street is a residential street in the Putney district of London. It is the known address of William [[sime|Sime]], who resided at No. 12, and of Mr. [[sproule|Sproule]], who lived at No. 17 with his invalid wife [[mrs-sproule|Mrs. Sproule]]. [009-094] [013-071]

> "Sime's present address: 12 Colton Street, Putney." [009-094]

The street is situated such that [[inspector-french|Inspector French]] was able to place "men on point duty round about Colton Street" to establish surveillance on Sime's house. [013-073] A constable posted at the eastern end of Colton Street provided crucial observations about vehicle traffic in the area. [013-074]

## Role in the Story

Colton Street becomes a focal point of the police investigation led by [[inspector-french|Inspector French]]. After obtaining Sime's address as No. 12 Colton Street, Putney, French initiates an immediate surveillance operation. [013-017]

> "I have it here—to 12 Colton Street, Putney. We shall carry out the surveillance at once" [013-017]

French and three officers approach No. 12 directly, conducting an on-site investigation of Sime's residence. [013-021] During the operation, French interviews neighbors including Mr. [[sproule|Sproule]] at No. 17, seeking any information about Sime's activities and the yellow motor-car seen in the vicinity. [013-071]

The distance from [[horne-terrace|Horne Terrace]] to Sime's house in Colton Street was estimated at approximately a ten-minute run by car, which proved significant in reconstructing the timeline of the suspects' movements. [015-016] Mrs. [[mrs-sproule|Sproule]], the invalid of Colton Street, provided key witness testimony that a yellow car had left Sime's house during the critical timeframe of the investigation. [015-047]

> "At the eastern end of Colton Street, had seen a car of the size and description" [013-074]
