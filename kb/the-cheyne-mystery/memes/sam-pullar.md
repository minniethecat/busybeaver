# Sam Pullar

**Sam Pullar** is an employee in the Record Department at Lloyd's of London, and a personal contact of [[inspector-french|Inspector French]]. French called upon him for urgent shipping intelligence that proved critical to solving the case.

## Role in the Story

Pullar appears in Chapter 19 when French, having decrypted the [[arnold-price-cipher|Arnold Price cipher]], needed to verify the coordinates hidden within it. The cipher contained the latitude and longitude of a location at sea—specifically "one degree west"—and French immediately thought of Lloyd's unparalleled shipping records.

French telephoned Lloyd's and asked to be put through to the Record Department, specifically requesting Sam Pullar. The conversation reveals a familiar, informal relationship between the two men: French addresses him as "Sam" and mentions they have not seen each other for some time [019-092].

> "Hallo! Is that Lloyd's? Put me through to the Record Department, please... Is Mr. Sam Pullar there? Tell him Inspector French of Scotland Yard wants to speak to him... Hallo, Sam!... Yes... Haven't seen you for ages... Look here, Sam, I want you to do me a favor." [019-092]

Pullar provided French with the precise coordinates (latitude 41°36'N, longitude 28°53'W) and details about the sinking of the *Silurian*, a vessel that had been carrying two and a half million pounds in gold bullion. French relayed the information to [[inspector-barnes|Inspector Barnes]], the Yard's nautical expert, for interpretation [019-100].

## Significance

Pullar's information from Lloyd's records was the breakthrough that allowed French to connect the cipher's coordinates to a real maritime location—the position where the *Silurian* sank. This confirmed that [[otto-schulz|Otto Schulz]]'s encoded message referred to a sunken treasure, and that the conspirators' scheme involved recovering gold bullion from the wreck. The shipping data provided by Pullar was one of the final pieces that unlocked the entire conspiracy [019-104].

Notably, the *Silurian* had not gone directly west after leaving its last port—it had gone south to avoid ice, which explained why the coordinates in the cipher did not match a straightforward great-circle route. This detail, also provided through Pullar, was essential to correctly interpreting the cipher [019-104].
