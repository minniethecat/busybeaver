# Bombay

**Bombay** (present-day Mumbai) is a major Indian port city referenced in *The Cheyne Mystery* as the location where [[arnold-price]] mysteriously disappeared, and as the setting for several key backstory elements of the conspiracy.

## In the Story

Bombay first appears in [[004-002]] as part of the merchant marine routes mentioned in the novel: ships "plying between Basrah and Bombay in connection with the Mesopotamian campaign" during the First World War.

The city becomes significant in [[004-015]] when the false "Lamson" explains that the missing birth certificate needed for the [[lord-hull|Hull barony]] claim was believed to have been recorded in India, "probably at Bombay." This prompted the real Arnold Price to apply for a transfer to India-based service.

The most critical reference appears in [[008-004]]: [[inspector-french]] learns through his Admiralty contact that Arnold Price "had left his ship at Bombay on a few days' leave and had not returned." This disappearance is a pivotal plot point — the real Arnold Price vanished in Bombay, allowing [[dangle]]'s gang to impersonate him.

In [[011-061]], Dangle confirms that Arnold Price was serving on "one of the Bombay-Basrah troopships," adding further detail to the backstory. The admission in [[011-063]] clarifies: "He left his ship at Bombay on a few days' leave to pay a visit somewhere inland, and the man simply vanished." The novel never resolves exactly what happened to the real Arnold Price in Bombay.

## Significance

Bombay represents the **geographical anchor** of the novel's central mystery. The real Arnold Price's disappearance there creates the opportunity for the impersonation that drives the entire plot. The city thus functions as a narrative pivot — the point where the true story ends and the false one begins.

The connection to [[arnold-price-cipher|Price's cipher]] is indirect but important: had Price not vanished in Bombay, [[maxwell-cheyne]] would never have received the cipher, and the events of the novel would not have unfolded.
