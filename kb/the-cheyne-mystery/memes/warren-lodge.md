# Warren Lodge

## Description

Warren Lodge is a small Georgian house standing in pleasant grounds on the estuary of the River Dart, near Dartmouth in Devonshire. It is the Cheyne family's ancestral home. After the death of [[maxwell-cheyne]]'s father, Commander Cheyne, the family faced financial hardship and lost the property. Years later, following an inheritance from an Australian uncle, Maxwell's mother bought it back. [001-008]

The house features a safe where Cheyne keeps valuable documents, a ground floor accessible from the garden, and upper windows that prove vulnerable to burglars. The grounds are bounded by the Dart estuary, and Cheyne's yacht is moored nearby.

> "Mr. Cheyne bought back Warren Lodge, their old home, a small Georgian house standing in pleasant grounds on the estuary of the Dart." [001-008]

## Role in the Story

Warren Lodge serves as Cheyne's refuge and, paradoxically, as one of the primary crime scenes. After the [[edgecombe-hotel|Edgecombe Hotel]] drugging fails to yield the sought-after document, [[blessington|Blessington]]'s conspiracy turns to Cheyne's home. Members of the gang break into the house and force open the safe, searching for Arnold Price's sealed envelope. [004-001]

The burglary is discovered while Cheyne is away: "When you rang up we sent James over to Warren Lodge. He found Mrs. Cheyne and Agatha on the doorstep trying to get in." [002-009] The intruders had entered through an upper window using a ladder, opening the front door for the others.

> "When you rang up we sent James over to Warren Lodge. He found Mrs. Cheyne and Agatha on the doorstep trying to get in. They had been ringing for some time, but could not attract attention. He rang also, and then eventually found a ladder and got in through one of the upper windows. He opened the door for Mrs. Cheyne and Agatha." [002-009]

The sealed envelope from Arnold Price was addressed directly to Warren Lodge, making the house the intended destination of the vital document around which the entire conspiracy revolves.

> It was inscribed: "To Maxwell Cheyne, of Warren Lodge, Dartmouth, Devonshire, from Arnold Price, third officer, S.S. *Maurania*," and on the top was written: "Please retain this envelope unopened until I claim it or until you have received authentic news of my death. Arnold Price." [003-085]

Cheyne later realized that all the crimes — the drugging, the burglary, and the kidnapping — formed a single connected plot centered on the document intended for Warren Lodge.

> When Maxwell Cheyne's paroxysm of fury diminished and he began once more to think collectedly about the unpleasant situation in which he found himself, a startling idea occurred to him. Here at last, surely, was the explanation of his previous adventures! The drugging in the hotel in Plymouth, the burglary at Warren Lodge, and now his kidnapping on the *Enid* were all part and parcel of the same scheme. [004-001]

## Events

- **The Family's Return**: Mrs. Cheyne buys back Warren Lodge after inheriting a legacy. Maxwell leaves his shipping clerk position and settles into country life. [001-008]
- **The Burglary**: Conspirators break into Warren Lodge, searching for Price's letter. Mrs. Cheyne and Agatha return to find the house locked from inside. [002-009] [004-001]
- **The Safe**: Cheyne's wall safe is forced open by the intruders. Though papers are tossed about, nothing appears to have been taken — confirming the burglars were searching for a specific document. [002-013]

> "Your safe had been opened, but they hadn't had time to make an examination when James left. The silver seems to be all there, so that's something." [002-013]

- **The Discovery**: Returning with an electric torch, Cheyne's mother and sister search the rooms and find the safe broken open, confirming a burglary has taken place.

> "I found your electric torch and we began to search the rooms. Then we saw that your safe had been broken open and we knew it was burglary." [002-077]

- **Cheyne's Departure**: Before his yachting trip that leads to further adventures, Cheyne leaves from Warren Lodge. [001-011]
