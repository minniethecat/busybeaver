# Laurel Lodge

**Laurel Lodge** is a house on [[hopefield-avenue]] in Hendon, London, occupied by [[dangle]]. It serves as a key setting in the novel, being the locus of the conspiracy orchestrated by Dangle and his associates, and the target of the burglary that drives much of the early plot.

## Description

Laurel Lodge is situated on Hopefield Avenue in Hendon, a residential area of northwest London. The house is identified by its name displayed in white lettering, visible from the street even in poor light conditions.

> So far as he could see, the house was in complete darkness [005-058]

The property is flanked by a narrow lane that provides access to the rear of the premises. This lane runs between Laurel Lodge and an adjacent vacant lot, allowing observation and covert approach to the back of the house.

> Between the first vacant lot and Laurel Lodge ran a narrow lane, evidently giving access to the back [005-059]

The rear of Laurel Lodge is separated from the lane by a wooden paling fence, which becomes significant during the events of the burglary investigation, as it provides a means of covert entry and exit [005-063]. The front of the house features a hall door with a gate leading to it from the street, and there is a street lamp directly opposite that illuminates passersby [008-053][008-078].

The house is home to James Dangle, who lives there alone except for the assistance of a daily domestic help [014-011].

## Role in the Story

Laurel Lodge is central to the conspiracy and the investigation that unfolds after [[maxwell-cheyne]] becomes entangled in the affair. It is the residence of James Dangle, who is revealed to be a key figure in the criminal plot.

The house is first identified when Cheyne visits Hopefield Avenue to investigate the address he acquired in connection with the coded message at [[alwyn-road]]. He observes the house from outside, noting its darkened state and the adjacent lane [005-058].

Laurel Lodge becomes the site of the [[warren-lodge-burglary]], a pivotal event in the narrative. The burglars gain access via the rear lane and the wooden paling fence, using the lane to transport items in and out of the property [005-063][005-067].

> ...the back premises of Laurel Lodge were separated from the lane by a wooden paling [005-063]

Cheyne later returns to surveil the house at night, using a plank as a makeshift seat across the street to watch for activity [008-051]. His observations confirm suspicious comings and goings, and he eventually enters the property through the gate and rings the bell, encountering an elderly woman who answers the door [008-053][008-069].

The house is also the address written on a critical piece of evidence: a label bearing the name "Mr. J. Dangle, Laurel Lodge, Hopefield Avenue, Hendon," which confirms Dangle's connection to the events at Alwyn Road [014-007].

> Several times: 'Mr. J. Dangle, Laurel Lodge, Hopefield Avenue, Hendon.' So that was the addressee. [014-007]
