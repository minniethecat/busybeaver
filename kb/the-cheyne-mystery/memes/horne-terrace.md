# Horne Terrace

## Description

Horne Terrace is a block of workmen's flats located at Burton Street, Chelsea, London. The building is described as somewhat decayed and past its prime:

> Horne Terrace proved to be a block of workmen's flats, somewhat decayed and grimy. [007-035]

The address first comes to [[maxwell-cheyne]]'s attention when he receives a note headed "17 Horne Terrace, Burton Street, Chelsea" — an address completely unfamiliar to him [007-034]. The building has at least ten flights of stairs, though an ascensor (lift) is also available for those who prefer not to climb [008-005].

Two flats in the building are significant to the story: No. 17 and No. 12. [[joan-merrill]] originally resides in No. 17, but later her flat is identified as No. 12 [010-056].

## Role in the Story

Horne Terrace serves as a pivotal location throughout the investigation. It is the residence of [[joan-merrill]], and [[maxwell-cheyne]] makes repeated visits here — sometimes eagerly racing up the steps [010-060], other times arriving with carefully calculated timing [009-128].

The flat becomes a hub of the investigation when Cheyne brings evidence to spread out on Miss Merrill's table [010-017]. Later, [[inspector-french]] and [[sergeant-carter]] examine Joan Merrill's flat as part of the official inquiry [015-012].

The location also proves significant in relation to other key places in the investigation — Sime's house in [[colton-street]] is approximately a ten-minute drive from Horne Terrace [015-016].

At a critical point in the story, Joan Merrill goes missing, and Horne Terrace yields no clues:

> At Horne Terrace there was no trace or tidings of the missing girl. [013-013]

Cheyne's persistent returns to the location underscore both his personal concern for Miss Merrill and the flat's importance as the last known point of contact with her. Even after finding the door of No. 12 shut [014-033], he continues to return, once arriving in a taxi "bowling towards Horne Terrace" [018-043].
