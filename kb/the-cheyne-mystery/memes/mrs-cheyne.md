Mrs. Cheyne is Maxwell Cheyne's mother, the matriarch of the Cheyne household at Warren Lodge. She purchased the estate and maintained the home while her son was away on his yachting and professional pursuits.

## Role in the Story

Mrs. Cheyne is first mentioned when Maxwell Cheyne, delayed in Plymouth, arranges through the Hazelton neighbors to have a message conveyed to her about his change of plans [001-079]. The Cheynes did not have a telephone of their own, so they relied on the nearby Hazeltons to relay messages [001-079].

When Cheyne's plans changed unexpectedly and he remained in Plymouth overnight, he asked Mrs. Hazelton to inform Mrs. Cheyne of the situation [001-079]. Later, when the burglary at Warren Lodge was discovered, James (the Hazeltons' servant) found Mrs. Cheyne and her daughter Agatha locked out of the house, unable to gain entry [002-009].

## Warren Lodge

Mrs. Cheyne had purchased Warren Lodge, described as "the little estate," and the household was established there under her care [002-036]. The study — which her son used as his private sanctum but which she persisted in calling "the study" — contained the wall safe that became central to the mystery [002-036].

During Cheyne's captivity, his captors sent a wire to Mrs. Cheyne to prevent her from becoming uneasy about his absence [003-074][004-034].
