# Bruges

**Bruges** (Dutch: *Brugge*) is a historic city in West Flanders, Belgium, known for its medieval architecture and canals. In *The Cheyne Mystery*, it plays a key role in [[inspector-french|Inspector French]]'s continental investigation.

## In the Story

Bruges enters the investigation when French examines a torn scrap of paper found among [[arnold-price-cipher|the cipher documents]] — part of a hotel bill from the Grand Hôtel du Sablon in Bruges [015-069]. Tracing this bill becomes one of his primary objectives on the Continent.

The torn bill suggests one of the conspirators stayed at the hotel, and French travels to Bruges to follow this lead [016-001]. He had always heard Bruges mentioned as "the paradise of architects" [016-008], but on this visit, professional duty overrides any touristic interest.

French's investigation in Bruges yields important information about the conspirators' movements. The hotel register and staff interviews help him piece together the timeline of the gang's activities in Belgium [016-013].

## Significance

Bruges serves as a waypoint in the trail that French follows across Belgium. The hotel bill clue demonstrates French's methodical approach — a tiny scrap of paper, carefully preserved and analyzed, leads him to a specific location where he can gather concrete evidence.
