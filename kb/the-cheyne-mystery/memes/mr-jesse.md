Mr. Jesse is the manager of the Edgecombe Hotel in Plymouth, where Maxwell Cheyne was drugged at the beginning of the story. He played a key role in the initial response to the incident and facilitated the early investigation.

## Role in the Story

When Cheyne awoke from being drugged, he found himself in a bedroom of the Edgecombe Hotel with Mr. Jesse attending to him. Jesse introduced himself: "In the Edgecombe: the Edgecombe Hotel, you know, in Plymouth. I am the manager" [001-056].

## Response to the Drugging

Cheyne, disoriented, asked "What's happened, Mr. Jesse? I never did anything like this before!" [001-058]. Jesse delivered the diagnosis directly: "I'll tell you what has happened to you, Mr. Cheyne, though I'm sorry to have to admit it could have taken place in my hotel. You've been drugged. That's what has happened" [001-059].

Jesse investigated the circumstances. When Cheyne mentioned the flask of brandy shared with Parkes, Jesse seized on the detail: "During his fit of coughing he substituted a different flask" [001-075]. He arranged for the coffee cups to be analyzed by a doctor, who confirmed there was brandy but no drug in either cup [001-080].

## Protecting the Hotel

Concerned about the hotel's reputation, Jesse strongly urged Cheyne not to involve the police: "It would not do the hotel any good if it became known that a visitor had been drugged. I sincerely trust, sir, that you can see your way to keep the matter quiet" [001-086]. He proposed an alternative: "If you will agree to the affair being hushed up, I will employ the detective on behalf of the hotel. He will work under your direction and keep you advised of everything he learns" [001-089]. This led to the hiring of Speedwell, the private detective.

Jesse had previously worked with Speedwell, who explained: "I came into contact with Mr. Jesse, the manager, over some diamonds which were lost by a visitor to the hotel and which I had the good fortune to recover" [014-001].
