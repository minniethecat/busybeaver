## Summary

Otto Schulz's Secret is the foundational revelation of *The Cheyne Mystery* -- a discovery made by a German U-boat captain during the First World War that holds immense value. Encoded within the [[arnold-price-cipher|Arnold Price cipher]] and guarded by the [[the-tracing|linen tracing]], Schulz's secret is the object of [[dangle|James Dangle]]'s relentless pursuit and the key that ultimately resolves the mystery. The secret involves a hidden treasure or strategic asset whose location Schulz encoded before his death, setting in motion the chain of events that entangles [[maxwell-cheyne|Maxwell Cheyne]], [[joan-merrill|Joan Merrill]], and their adversaries.

## Context

### The U-boat Origin

The secret originates during the First World War, aboard a German U-boat commanded by Captain Otto Schulz [011-045]. Schulz made a discovery of enormous significance, the nature of which is gradually revealed through the narrative. When his U-boat was involved in an engagement, Schulz and another crewman were injured and sent to a hospital ashore [011-046].

> The captain's name was Otto Schulz, and when they got him aboard the hospital ship he was in a very bad way. [011-045]

### Arnold Price's Role

At the hospital, Schulz shared his secret with [[arnold-price-cipher|Arnold Price]], a fellow patient recovering from wounds [011-055]. Price, recognizing the value of the information, encoded it in the cipher that later bears his name. Before revealing the secret, Schulz administered an oath to Price, binding him to protect the information [011-056].

> Schulz began by telling Price that he had a secret of immense value, but before revealing it, he required an oath of secrecy. [011-055]

### The Secret's Transmission

Schulz described his discovery in detail to Price, who subsequently created the [[arnold-price-cipher|cipher]] and the [[the-tracing|linen tracing]] as secure methods of recording and transmitting the information [011-056]. When both Schulz and Price later died -- Schulz's friend killed in action and Schulz himself succumbing to his injuries [011-058] -- the secret survived only in encoded form, setting the stage for the struggle to decode and possess it.

## Details

### The Nature of the Secret

Schulz's secret concerns a discovery of material value -- a hidden asset whose location is encoded in the cipher. The desperate measures taken by Dangle and his associates to obtain it suggest it represents considerable wealth or strategic importance. The involvement of multiple parties -- including [[dangle|Dangle]], his criminal associates, and even Schulz's widow (Frau Schulz) -- indicates the stakes are high [011-065].

> In despair I told them of Schulz's discovery. They leaped at it. [011-063]

The secret itself was a metallurgical breakthrough. Schulz, while serving in the German Submarine Research Department, had accidentally discovered a process for hardening copper to the strength of high-carbon steel:

> He chipped the stuff off the terminals, and he found that the copper had fused and run. And then he made his great discovery: the copper had hardened. He tested it and found it was, roughly speaking, as hard as high carbon steel and with an even greater tensile strength! Unintentionally he had made a new and unknown alloy. [011-065]

### The Ethical Dimension

The secret raises complex ethical questions. Multiple parties have legitimate claims: Frau Schulz as the widow, Price as the encoder, and those who risk their lives to protect it. Dangle's claim is purely predatory, but the question of how the secret's value should be distributed among the legitimate stakeholders is a recurring tension [012-008].

> Frau Schulz would get her eighth and Mrs. Merrill and Joan and himself would each receive a share. [012-008]

## Analysis

### The MacGuffin Structure

Otto Schulz's Secret is a textbook example of the MacGuffin in detective fiction -- an object of immense value that drives the plot while its precise nature remains hidden from the reader for most of the narrative. Crofts deploys this device with unusual sophistication, layering the revelation across multiple chapters. The reader first learns of a "secret" through the conspirators' desperate behavior, then gradually discovers its wartime origin, its metallurgical nature, and finally its encoded location pointing to sunken gold.

### Wartime Context and Moral Ambiguity

The secret's origin in a German U-boat during the First World War introduces a rare note of moral complexity into what is otherwise a straightforward crime narrative. The German captain Schulz is portrayed sympathetically -- a skilled officer who made a genuine scientific discovery, not a villain. His request that his widow receive a share of the proceeds introduces an ethical obligation that even the criminals (initially) honor. This moral shading distinguishes *The Cheyne Mystery* from simpler tales of villainy and heroism.

### The Chain of Transmission

The secret's passage from Schulz to Price to Cheyne (via the sealed envelope) creates a chain of custody that mirrors the novel's broader concern with trust and obligation. Each link in the chain involves an oath or promise: Schulz's dying request, Price's encoding and dispatch, Cheyne's commitment to guard the envelope unopened. The conspirators' efforts to break this chain -- through drugging, burglary, kidnapping, and attempted murder -- represent not just criminal acts but violations of these bonds of trust.

### Scientific Detail as Plot Engine

Crofts, an engineer by training, uses scientific and technical detail not as decoration but as the engine of the plot. Schulz's discovery of hardened copper is described with plausible specificity: the electric furnace, the vaporization, the short-circuit, the accidental fusion. This technical grounding gives the secret a weight and credibility that purely fantastical MacGuffins lack, and it exemplifies the "scientific detective" approach that was Crofts's major contribution to the genre.

## Consequences

The revelation of Schulz's secret triggers the climactic sequence of the novel:

1. **The Bargain**: Cheyne and his allies negotiate to trade knowledge of the secret for Joan Merrill's freedom [011-065].
2. **Dangle's Desperation**: As the secret's decoding progresses, Dangle's actions become increasingly reckless.
3. **The Final Pursuit**: The chase culminates in a race to reach the location revealed by the secret -- the goal associated with the [[the-lescaut|L'Escaut]].
