# Blessington

## Role in the Story

Blessington is the primary antagonist of *The Cheyne Mystery*, operating under multiple aliases to conceal his criminal activities. He first appears as "Hubert Parkes," the man who approaches [[maxwell-cheyne]] at the Edgecombe Hotel in Plymouth, drugs him, and searches his pockets for a vital document.

His real name is revealed gradually. When Cheyne recounts his story to Price at Hopefield Avenue, Price casually mentions: "Parkes's real name was Blessington." [006-019] [006-020] This revelation marks the beginning of Cheyne's understanding of the organized conspiracy against him. The full unmasking of the gang's identities comes when Cheyne observes them at Hopefield Avenue:

> "At last Cheyne thought he had got the four fixed in his mind. The man on the rug — the man who had drugged him in the Plymouth hotel — was Blessington. The man who had introduced himself as Lamson and afterwards said his name was Price bore neither of these appellations: his name was Dangle. Susan was 'Miss Dangle' and almost certainly sister to James. Lewisham, the motorman of the Enid, was Sime." [006-024]

Blessington is the leader of a criminal trio including Dangle and Sime. He displays both intelligence and ruthlessness — capable of careful planning and quick violence when the situation demands it.

## Background

Blessington's background and motivations are revealed through his own dissertation to Cheyne aboard the *Enid*. He sought a secret — a cipher or coded document — that would lead to a fortune buried by a German submarine commander during World War I. His quest brought him into conflict with Cheyne, who unwittingly held the key in the form of Arnold Price's sealed envelope. [006-026] [003-085]

The private detective Speedwell provided a damning assessment of Blessington's character and methods:

> "Blessington is, in my opinion, the worst of the three. He has ten times the brains of the other two put together and is an out and out scoundrel. He's well enough off in a small way and is supposed to have made his money by systematic blackmail. He's supplying the cash for this little do of yours, whatever it may be. He is believed at Wembley to be something in the city, but I don't think he has any job. Lives on the interest of his money, I should think." [009-108]

> "Parkes — or Blessington — took Price's remark easily." [006-020]

The blackmail allegation finds corroboration when Cheyne searches Blessington's desk at Earlswood:

> "Cheyne did not read anything, but from the fragments of sentences which he could not help seeing there seemed ample corroboration of Speedwell's statements that Blessington lived by professional blackmail. He felt a wave of disgust sweep over him as he went through drawer after drawer of the obscene collection." [010-046]

## Key Actions

- **The Plymouth Drugging**: Under the alias Hubert Parkes, Blessington approaches Cheyne at the Edgecombe Hotel, drugs him, and searches his pocketbook for Arnold Price's letter. [001-018] [001-056]
- **The Warren Lodge Burglary**: When the drugging fails to yield the document, Blessington's gang breaks into Cheyne's home and safe. [004-001]
- **The Kidnapping**: Blessington abducts Cheyne and holds him prisoner aboard the launch *Enid*, later confessing to the drugging:

> "I succeeded in drugging you," Blessington answered, "but I did not succeed in getting what I wanted. Now, Mr. Cheyne, you would like to see the tracing. Show it to him, Dangle, while I go back to the other house for Miss Merrill." [012-045]

- **The Pursuit**: As Inspector French closes in, Blessington flees aboard the *L'Escaut*, leading to the climactic sea chase. [006-044] [006-047]
- **Final Confrontation**: Blessington is ultimately captured by [[inspector-french|Inspector French]] and his accomplices, bringing the conspiracy to an end. [020-032]

## Relationships

- **Maxwell Cheyne** — Blessington's target, whom he drugs, burglarizes, and kidnaps. [001-018] [004-001]
- **Dangle and Sime** — Blessington's co-conspirators. The trio operates as a coordinated criminal enterprise. [006-025]
- **Susan Dangle** — Part of the Dangle family involved in the conspiracy. [006-022]
- **Arnold Price** — The man whose sealed document Blessington most desperately seeks. [006-019]
