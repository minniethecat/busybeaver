## Summary

The *L'Escaut* Voyage was the conspirators' maritime expedition from Antwerp to the Atlantic Ocean to recover Otto Schulz's sunken gold. Aboard the chartered vessel were twenty-eight people including the gang leaders (Blessington, Dangle, Sime, Merkel), three divers, crew, and two captives — Joan Merrill and Susan Dangle. The voyage ended in a confrontation at sea when an Admiralty salvage ship, alerted by Inspector French's investigation, intercepted the *L'Escaut* at the wreck site [3338-3452].

## Context

After obtaining the linen tracing and decoding the cipher that revealed the location of Schulz's sunken treasure, the conspirators planned a maritime salvage operation. James Dangle made multiple trips to Antwerp to arrange the charter of a suitable vessel and hire divers and equipment. The plan was accelerated when Cheyne's break-in at Earlswood recovered the tracing; the gang decided to depart immediately, taking Joan Merrill as a captive to prevent her from alerting the authorities [2749-2761][3452].

## Details

On 24 May, the *L'Escaut* departed Antwerp with twenty-eight souls aboard. The complement included the gang leaders (Blessington, Dangle, Sime, and Merkel), three professional divers with six assistants, the ship's officers and engineer, and a full crew of seamen and engine-room staff [3436]. Joan Merrill was held captive, initially drugged but later treated with a surface of politeness — though she correctly suspected that Blessington had developed an obsession with her.

The voyage proceeded to the wreck site in the Atlantic, where the divers began work recovering gold bars from the sunken vessel. The location had been identified from Arnold Price's cipher — a position approximately one degree west of where the ship was believed to have sunk, in a patch of shallower water where the mountain peaks rose to within 18 fathoms of the surface [3338].

Meanwhile, the Admiralty, acting on intelligence from Inspector French, dispatched a salvage vessel to intercept. A confrontation ensued at the wreck site. Shots were exchanged, and several men were killed or wounded. Of the twenty-eight who had sailed from Antwerp, only nine survived: Joan Merrill, Susan Dangle, four slightly wounded men, and three seriously wounded [3444].

## Consequences

The interception of the *L'Escaut* brought the conspiracy to its conclusion. The remaining gold bars were recovered by the Admiralty vessel, and both ships returned to Chatham. Under a special arrangement, 25% of the bullion was returned to the finders, with Cheyne, Joan, and Price each receiving approximately £200,000. Joan insisted that Susan Dangle receive £10,000 for her help during the trouble aboard the *L'Escaut*, and Inspector French received £1,000 as a gift [3452].
