Chief Inspector Mitchell is a senior officer at Scotland Yard who oversaw the investigation into the Blessington gang and the disappearance of Joan Merrill. As Chief Inspector, he held authority over Inspector French and was consulted on key operational decisions.

## Authorization of the Belgian Investigation

When Inspector French reported his findings and proposed a course of action, the matter came before Chief Inspector Mitchell. French attended an interview with his chief at the Yard at which he produced the torn hotel bill, and having demonstrated the methods by which he had come to identify it with the Grand Hôtel du Sablon in Bruges, suggested that a visit there might be desirable [016-001]. To French's secret relief, Chief Inspector Mitchell "took the same view" regarding the investigation's direction, and it was arranged that French should cross to Belgium as soon as he could get away [016-001]. This alignment meant French received official backing for his planned approach and the resources necessary for international travel.

## Assessment of the Case

After hearing the details assembled by French, Chief Inspector Mitchell "was impressed by the story" [018-024]. His approval as a senior officer validated the investigative work that French had carried out and ensured continued resources for the pursuit of the gang. Mitchell's position in the Yard hierarchy made his endorsement a significant factor in the investigation's momentum.

## Position and Authority

As French's chief at the Yard, Mitchell occupied a supervisory role that required him to evaluate the evidence presented by his subordinates and authorize operational decisions. His willingness to approve the Belgian trip on the strength of the torn hotel bill evidence demonstrated both confidence in French's methods and judgment in allocating Scotland Yard's resources.
