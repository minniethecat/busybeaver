Mr. John Hackworth is a stout gentleman who operated a garage in the Putney area. He was interviewed by Inspector French during the latter's methodical investigation of the neighborhood, and his testimony proved valuable in tracing the movements of a suspicious vehicle.

## Hackworth's Garage

Hackworth's garage was a small establishment located in the Putney neighborhood [015-018]. On the day of French's visit, the inspector and his constable reached "Hackworth's establishment" and pushed open the door to find the proprietor within [015-020]. The garage served as both Hackworth's business and a source of local intelligence about comings and goings in the area.

## Interview with Inspector French

When French arrived, Hackworth identified himself directly: "I'm John Hackworth. Yes, sir?" [015-021]. French approached him pleasantly, saying "I want a word with you, Mr. Hackworth" [015-022]. Hackworth nodded and indicated a farther corner of the premises, where French propounded his questions [015-023][015-025].

Hackworth appeared impressed by French's credentials during the interview [015-025]. At one point he pushed a card up to the electric light to examine it carefully, demonstrating his deliberate and thorough nature [015-029].

## The Yellow Car Evidence

Hackworth's most significant contribution to the case was his testimony about the yellow car. He stated that it had reached the garage at approximately 1:45 [015-047]. French later reflected that what was wrong with the vehicle was "just what Hackworth had said was wrong with it" [015-046], confirming the accuracy of the garage proprietor's observations. This evidence helped establish the timeline of the suspects' movements.

French ultimately thanked "the stout Mr. Hackworth for his help" before departing with his constable [015-043].
