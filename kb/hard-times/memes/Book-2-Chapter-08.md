[024-001] EXPLOSION

[024-002] THE next morning was too bright a morning for sleep, and [[james-harthouse|James Harthouse]]

[024-003] rose early, and sat in the pleasant bay window of his dressing-room,

[024-004] smoking the rare tobacco that had had so wholesome an influence on his

[024-005] young friend. Reposing in the sunlight, with the fragrance of his

[024-006] eastern pipe about him, and the dreamy smoke vanishing into the air, so

[024-007] rich and soft with summer odours, he reckoned up his advantages as an

[024-008] idle winner might count his gains. He was not at all bored for the time,

[024-009] and could give his mind to it.

[024-010] He had established a confidence with her, from which her husband was

[024-011] excluded. He had established a confidence with her, that absolutely

[024-012] turned upon her indifference towards her husband, and the absence, now

[024-013] and at all times, of any congeniality between them. He had artfully, but

[024-014] plainly, assured her that he knew her [[heart]] in its last most delicate

[024-015] recesses; he had come so near to her through its tenderest sentiment; he

[024-016] had associated himself with that feeling; and the barrier behind which

[024-017] she lived, had melted away. All very odd, and very satisfactory!

[024-018] And yet he had not, even now, any earnest wickedness of purpose in him.

[024-019] Publicly and privately, it were much better for the age in which he

[024-020] lived, that he and the legion of whom he was one were designedly bad,

[024-021] than indifferent and purposeless. It is the drifting icebergs setting

[024-022] with any current anywhere, that wreck the ships.

[024-023] When the Devil goeth about like a roaring lion, he goeth about in a shape

[024-024] by which few but savages and hunters are attracted. But, when he is

[024-025] trimmed, smoothed, and varnished, according to the mode; when he is

[024-026] aweary of vice, and aweary of virtue, used up as to brimstone, and used

[024-027] up as to bliss; then, whether he take to the serving out of red tape, or

[024-028] to the kindling of red fire, he is the very Devil.

[024-029] So James Harthouse reclined in the window, indolently smoking, and

[024-030] reckoning up the steps he had taken on the road by which he happened to

[024-031] be travelling. The end to which it led was before him, pretty plainly;

[024-032] but he troubled himself with no calculations about it. What will be,

[024-033] will be.

[024-034] As he had rather a long ride to take that day—for there was a public

[024-035] occasion ‘to do’ at some distance, which afforded a tolerable opportunity

[024-036] of going in for the [[mrs-gradgrind|Gradgrind]] men—he dressed early and went down to

[024-037] breakfast. He was anxious to see if she had relapsed since the previous

[024-038] evening. No. He resumed where he had left off. There was a look of

[024-039] interest for him again.

[024-040] He got through the day as much (or as little) to his own satisfaction, as

[024-041] was to be expected under the fatiguing circumstances; and came riding

[024-042] back at six o’clock. There was a sweep of some half-mile between the

[024-043] lodge and the house, and he was riding along at a foot pace over the

[024-044] smooth gravel, once Nickits’s, when [[josiah-bounderby|Mr. Bounderby]] burst out of the

[024-045] shrubbery, with such violence as to make his horse shy across the road.

[024-046] ‘Harthouse!’ cried Mr. Bounderby. ‘Have you heard?’

[024-047] ‘Heard what?’ said Harthouse, soothing his horse, and inwardly favouring

[024-048] Mr. Bounderby with no good wishes.

[024-049] ‘Then you _haven’t_ heard!’

[024-050] ‘I have heard you, and so has this brute. I have heard nothing else.’

[024-051] Mr. Bounderby, red and hot, planted himself in the centre of the path

[024-052] before the horse’s [[reason|head]], to explode his bombshell with more effect.

[024-053] ‘The Bank’s robbed!’

[024-054] ‘You don’t mean it!’

[024-055] ‘Robbed last night, sir. Robbed in an extraordinary manner. Robbed with

[024-056] a false key.’

[024-057] ‘Of much?’

[024-058] Mr. Bounderby, in his desire to make the most of it, really seemed

[024-059] mortified by being obliged to reply, ‘Why, no; not of very much. But it

[024-060] might have been.’

[024-061] ‘Of how much?’

[024-062] ‘Oh! as a sum—if you stick to a sum—of not more than a hundred and fifty

[024-063] pound,’ said Bounderby, with impatience. ‘But it’s not the sum; it’s the

[024-064] [[facts|fact]]. It’s the fact of the Bank being robbed, that’s the important

[024-065] circumstance. I am surprised you don’t see it.’

[024-066] ‘My [[love|dear]] Bounderby,’ said James, dismounting, and giving his bridle to

[024-067] his servant, ‘I _do_ see it; and am as overcome as you can possibly

[024-068] desire me to be, by the [[performance|spectacle]] afforded to my mental view.

[024-069] Nevertheless, I may be allowed, I hope, to congratulate you—which I do

[024-070] with all my soul, I assure you—on your not having sustained a greater

[024-071] loss.’

[024-072] ‘Thank’ee,’ replied Bounderby, in a short, ungracious manner. ‘But I

[024-073] tell you what. It might have been twenty thousand pound.’

[024-074] ‘I suppose it might.’

[024-075] ‘Suppose it might! By the Lord, you _may_ suppose so. By George!’ said

[024-076] Mr. Bounderby, with sundry menacing nods and shakes of his head. ‘It

[024-077] might have been twice twenty. There’s no knowing what it would have

[024-078] been, or wouldn’t have been, as it was, but for the fellows’ being

[024-079] disturbed.’

[024-080] [[louisa-gradgrind|Louisa]] had come up now, and [[mrs-sparsit|Mrs. Sparsit]], and [[bitzer|Bitzer]].

[024-081] ‘Here’s [[tom-gradgrind|Tom Gradgrind]]’s daughter knows pretty well what it might have

[024-082] been, if you don’t,’ blustered Bounderby. ‘Dropped, sir, as if she was

[024-083] shot when I told her! Never knew her do such a thing before. Does her

[024-084] credit, under the circumstances, in my opinion!’

[024-085] She still looked faint and pale. James Harthouse begged her to take his

[024-086] arm; and as they moved on very slowly, asked her how the robbery had been

[024-087] committed.

[024-088] ‘Why, I am going to tell you,’ said Bounderby, irritably giving his arm

[024-089] to Mrs. Sparsit. ‘If you hadn’t been so mighty particular about the sum,

[024-090] I should have begun to tell you before. You know this lady (for she _is_

[024-091] a lady), Mrs. Sparsit?’

[024-092] ‘I have already had the honour—’

[024-093] ‘Very well. And this young man, Bitzer, you saw him too on the same

[024-094] occasion?’ Mr. Harthouse inclined his head in assent, and Bitzer

[024-095] knuckled his forehead.

[024-096] ‘Very well. They live at the Bank. You know they live at the Bank,

[024-097] perhaps? Very well. Yesterday afternoon, at the close of business

[024-098] hours, everything was put away as usual. In the iron room that this

[024-099] young fellow sleeps outside of, there was never mind how much. In the

[024-100] little safe in young Tom’s closet, the safe used for petty purposes,

[024-101] there was a hundred and fifty odd pound.’

[024-102] ‘A hundred and fifty-four, seven, one,’ said Bitzer.

[024-107] me tell you. I hadn’t victuals enough to snore. And I didn’t four seven

[024-108] one. Not if I knew it.’

[024-109] Bitzer knuckled his forehead again, in a sneaking manner, and seemed at

[024-110] once particularly impressed and depressed by the instance last given of

[024-111] Mr. Bounderby’s moral abstinence.

[024-119] winter evenings, when he has fallen asleep at his table, I have heard

[024-126] testimony.’

[024-127] ‘Well!’ said the exasperated Bounderby, ‘while he was snoring, _or_

[024-138] money gone.’

[024-139] ‘Where is Tom, by the by?’ asked Harthouse, glancing round.

[024-140] ‘He has been helping the police,’ said Bounderby, ‘and stays behind at

[024-141] the Bank. I wish these fellows had tried to rob me when I was at his

[024-144] ‘Is anybody suspected?’

[024-145] ‘Suspected? I should think there was somebody suspected. Egod!’ said

[024-146] Bounderby, relinquishing Mrs. Sparsit’s arm to wipe his heated head.

[024-147] ‘Josiah Bounderby of [[coketown|Coketown]] is not to be plundered and nobody

[024-148] suspected. No, thank you!’

[024-149] Might Mr. Harthouse inquire Who was suspected?

[024-150] ‘Well,’ said Bounderby, stopping and facing about to confront them all,

[024-155] say to;’ here he violently exploded: ‘to a Hand being in it?’

[024-156] ‘I hope,’ said Harthouse, lazily, ‘not our friend Blackpot?’

[024-157] ‘Say Pool instead of Pot, sir,’ returned Bounderby, ‘and that’s the man.’

[024-158] Louisa faintly uttered some word of incredulity and surprise.
