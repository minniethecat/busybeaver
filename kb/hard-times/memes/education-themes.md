# Education Themes

Charles Dickens' *Hard Times* is, at its core, a critique of utilitarian education. These entries explore the novel's portrayal of the Gradgrind system — its philosophy of pure Fact, its human costs, and the countervailing force of Fancy and imagination.

## The Education Debate

Thomas Gradgrind's schoolroom motto — "Now, what I want is, Facts. Teach these boys and girls nothing but Facts" — sets up the central conflict of the novel. The entries below trace how this philosophy plays out across the novel's characters, institutions, and outcomes.
