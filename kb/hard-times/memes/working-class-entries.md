# Working Class in Hard Times

Dickens' portrayal of the working class in *Hard Times* — through characters like Stephen Blackpool and Rachael, through the trade union organizer Slackbridge, and through the industrial landscape itself — offers one of Victorian literature's most nuanced depictions of class struggle.

## Between Two Worlds

The working-class characters in *Hard Times* are caught between the rigid utilitarianism of the Gradgrind system and the allure of fancy and imagination represented by Sleary's circus. Their stories form the moral center of the novel.
