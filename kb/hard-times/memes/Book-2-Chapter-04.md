[020-001] MEN AND BROTHERS

[020-002] ‘OH, my friends, the down-trodden operatives of [[coketown|Coketown]]! Oh, my friends

[020-003] and fellow-countrymen, the slaves of an iron-handed and a grinding

[020-004] despotism! Oh, my friends and fellow-sufferers, and fellow-workmen, and

[020-005] fellow-men! I tell you that the hour is come, when we must rally round

[020-006] one another as One united power, and crumble into dust the oppressors

[020-007] that too long have battened upon the plunder of our [[family|families]], upon the

[020-008] sweat of our brows, upon the labour of our hands, upon the strength of

[020-009] our sinews, upon the God-created glorious rights of [[humanity|Humanity]], and upon

[020-010] the holy and eternal privileges of Brotherhood!’

[020-011] ‘Good!’ ‘Hear, hear, hear!’ ‘Hurrah!’ and other cries, arose in many

[020-012] voices from various parts of the densely crowded and suffocatingly close

[020-013] Hall, in which [[slackbridge|the orator]], perched on a stage, delivered himself of this

[020-014] and what other froth and fume he had in him. He had declaimed himself

[020-015] into a violent heat, and was as hoarse as he was hot. By dint of roaring

[020-016] at the top of his voice under a flaring gaslight, clenching his fists,

[020-017] knitting his brows, setting his teeth, and pounding with his arms, he had

[020-018] taken so much out of himself by this time, that he was brought to a stop,

[020-019] and called for a glass of water.

[020-020] As he stood there, trying to quench his fiery face with his drink of

[020-021] water, the comparison between the orator and the crowd of attentive faces

[020-022] turned towards him, was extremely to his disadvantage. Judging him by

[020-023] [[nature|Nature]]’s evidence, he was above the mass in very little but the stage on

[020-024] which he stood. In many great respects he was essentially below them.

[020-025] He was not so honest, he was not so manly, he was not so good-humoured;

[020-026] he substituted cunning for their simplicity, and passion for their safe

[020-027] solid sense. An ill-made, high-shouldered man, with lowering brows, and

[020-028] his features crushed into an habitually sour expression, he contrasted

[020-029] most unfavourably, even in his mongrel dress, with the great body of his

[020-030] hearers in their plain working clothes. Strange as it always is to

[020-031] consider any assembly in the act of submissively resigning itself to the

[020-032] dreariness of some complacent person, lord or commoner, whom

[020-033] three-fourths of it could, by no human means, raise out of the slough of

[020-034] inanity to their own intellectual level, it was particularly strange, and

[020-035] it was even particularly affecting, to see this crowd of earnest faces,

[020-036] whose honesty in the main no competent observer free from bias could

[020-037] doubt, so agitated by such a leader.

[020-038] Good! Hear, hear! Hurrah! The eagerness both of attention and

[020-039] intention, exhibited in all the countenances, made them a most impressive

[020-040] sight. There was no carelessness, no languor, no idle curiosity; none of

[020-041] the many shades of indifference to be seen in all other assemblies,

[020-042] visible for one moment there. That every man felt his condition to be,

[020-043] somehow or other, worse than it might be; that every man considered it

[020-044] incumbent on him to join the rest, towards the making of it better; that

[020-045] every man felt his only hope to be in his allying himself to the comrades

[020-046] by whom he was surrounded; and that in this belief, right or wrong

[020-047] (unhappily wrong then), the whole of that crowd were gravely, deeply,

[020-048] faithfully in earnest; must have been as plain to any one who chose to

[020-049] see what was there, as the bare beams of the roof and the whitened brick

[020-050] walls. Nor could any such spectator fail to know in his own breast, that

[020-051] these men, through their very delusions, showed great qualities,

[020-052] susceptible of being turned to the happiest and best account; and that to

[020-053] pretend (on the strength of sweeping axioms, howsoever cut and dried)

[020-054] that they went astray wholly without cause, and of their own irrational

[020-055] wills, was to pretend that there could be smoke without fire, death

[020-056] without birth, harvest without seed, anything or everything produced from

[020-057] nothing.

[020-058] The orator having refreshed himself, wiped his corrugated forehead from

[020-059] left to right several times with his handkerchief folded into a pad, and

[020-060] concentrated all his revived forces, in a sneer of great disdain and

[020-061] bitterness.

[020-062] ‘But oh, my friends and brothers! Oh, men and Englishmen, the

[020-063] down-trodden operatives of Coketown! What shall we say of that man—that

[020-064] working-man, that I should find it necessary so to libel the glorious

[020-065] name—who, being practically and well acquainted with the grievances and

[020-066] wrongs of you, the injured pith and marrow of this land, and having heard

[020-067] you, with a noble and majestic unanimity that will make Tyrants tremble,

[020-068] resolve for to subscribe to the funds of the United Aggregate Tribunal,

[020-069] and to abide by the injunctions issued by that body for your benefit,

[020-070] whatever they may be—what, I ask you, will you say of that working-man,

[020-071] since such I must acknowledge him to be, who, at such a time, deserts his

[020-072] post, and sells his flag; who, at such a time, turns a traitor and a

[020-073] craven and a recreant, who, at such a time, is not [[shame|ashamed]] to make to you

[020-074] the dastardly and humiliating avowal that he will hold himself aloof, and

[020-075] will _not_ be one of those associated in the gallant stand for Freedom

[020-076] and for Right?’

[020-077] The assembly was divided at this point. There were some groans and

[020-078] hisses, but the general sense of honour was much too strong for the

[020-079] condemnation of a man unheard. ‘Be sure you’re right, Slackbridge!’

[020-080] ‘Put him up!’ ‘Let’s hear him!’ Such things were said on many sides.

[020-081] Finally, one strong voice called out, ‘Is the man heer? If the man’s

[020-082] heer, Slackbridge, let’s hear the man himseln, ’stead o’ yo.’ Which was

[020-083] received with a round of applause.

[020-084] Slackbridge, the orator, looked about him with a withering smile; and,

[020-085] holding out his right hand at arm’s length (as the manner of all

[020-086] Slackbridges is), to still the thundering sea, waited until there was a

[020-087] profound silence.

[020-088] ‘Oh, my friends and fellow-men!’ said Slackbridge then, shaking his [[reason|head]]

[020-089] with violent scorn, ‘I do not wonder that you, the prostrate sons of

[020-090] labour, are incredulous of the existence of such a man. But he who sold

[020-091] his birthright for a mess of pottage existed, and Judas Iscariot existed,

[020-092] and Castlereagh existed, and this man exists!’

[020-093] Here, a brief press and confusion near the stage, ended in the man

[020-094] himself standing at the orator’s side before the concourse. He was pale

[020-095] and a little moved in the face—his lips especially showed it; but he

[020-096] stood quiet, with his left hand at his chin, waiting to be heard. There

[020-097] was a chairman to regulate the proceedings, and this functionary now took

[020-098] the case into his own hands.

[020-099] ‘My friends,’ said he, ‘by virtue o’ my office as your president, I askes

[020-100] o’ our friend Slackbridge, who may be a little over hetter in this

[020-101] business, to take his seat, whiles this man [[stephen-blackpool|Stephen Blackpool]] is heern.

[020-102] You all know this man Stephen Blackpool. You know him awlung o’ his

[020-103] misfort’ns, and his good name.’

[020-104] With that, the chairman shook him frankly by the hand, and sat down

[020-105] again. Slackbridge likewise sat down, wiping his hot forehead—always

[020-106] from left to right, and never the reverse way.

[020-107] ‘My friends,’ Stephen began, in the midst of a dead calm; ‘I ha’ hed

[020-108] what’s been spok’n o’ me, and ’tis lickly that I shan’t mend it. But I’d

[020-109] liefer you’d hearn the [[truth]] concernin myseln, fro my lips than fro onny

[020-110] other man’s, though I never cud’n speak afore so monny, wi’out bein

[020-111] moydert and muddled.’

[020-112] Slackbridge shook his head as if he would shake it off, in his

[020-113] bitterness.

[020-114] ‘I’m th’ one single Hand in [[josiah-bounderby|Bounderby]]’s mill, o’ a’ the men theer, as

[020-115] don’t coom in wi’ th’ proposed reg’lations. I canna coom in wi’ ’em. My

[020-116] friends, I doubt their doin’ yo onny good. Licker they’ll do yo hurt.’

[020-117] Slackbridge laughed, folded his arms, and frowned sarcastically.

[020-118] ‘But ’t an’t sommuch for that as I stands out. If that were aw, I’d coom

[020-119] in wi’ th’ rest. But I ha’ my reasons—mine, yo see—for being hindered;

[020-120] not on’y now, but awlus—awlus—life long!’

[020-121] Slackbridge jumped up and stood beside him, gnashing and tearing. ‘Oh,

[020-122] my friends, what but this did I tell you? Oh, my fellow-countrymen, what

[020-123] warning but this did I give you? And how shows this recreant conduct in

[020-124] a man on whom unequal laws are known to have fallen heavy? Oh, you

[020-125] Englishmen, I ask you how does this subornation [[performance|show]] in one of

[020-126] yourselves, who is thus consenting to his own undoing and to yours, and

[020-127] to your [[childhood|children]]’s and your children’s children’s?’

[020-128] There was some applause, and some crying of Shame upon the man; but the

[020-129] greater part of the audience were quiet. They looked at Stephen’s worn

[020-130] face, rendered more pathetic by the homely emotions it evinced; and, in

[020-131] the kindness of their nature, they were more sorry than indignant.

[020-132] ‘’Tis this Delegate’s trade for t’ speak,’ said Stephen, ‘an’ he’s paid

[020-133] for ’t, an’ he knows his work. Let him keep to ’t. Let him give no heed

[020-134] to what I ha had’n to bear. That’s not for him. That’s not for nobbody

[020-135] but me.’

[020-136] There was a propriety, not to say a [[pride|dignity]] in these words, that made the

[020-137] hearers yet more quiet and attentive. The same strong voice called out,

[020-138] ‘Slackbridge, let the man be heern, and howd thee tongue!’ Then the

[020-139] place was wonderfully still.

[020-140] ‘My brothers,’ said Stephen, whose low voice was distinctly heard, ‘and

[020-141] my fellow-workmen—for that yo are to me, though not, as I knows on, to

[020-142] this delegate here—I ha but a word to sen, and I could sen nommore if I

[020-143] was to speak till Strike o’ day. I know weel, aw what’s afore me. I

[020-144] know weel that yo aw resolve to ha nommore ado wi’ a man who is not wi’

[020-145] yo in this matther. I know weel that if I was a lyin parisht i’ th’

[020-146] road, yo’d feel it right to pass me by, as a forrenner and stranger.

[020-147] What I ha getn, I mun mak th’ best on.’

[020-148] ‘Stephen Blackpool,’ said the chairman, rising, ‘think on ’t agen. Think

[020-149] on ’t once agen, lad, afore thou’rt shunned by aw owd friends.’

[020-150] There was an universal murmur to the same effect, though no man

[020-151] articulated a word. Every eye was fixed on Stephen’s face. To repent of

[020-152] his determination, would be to take a load from all their minds. He

[020-153] looked around him, and knew that it was so. Not a grain of anger with

[020-154] them was in his [[heart]]; he knew them, far below their surface weaknesses

[020-155] and misconceptions, as no one but their fellow-labourer could.

[020-156] ‘I ha thowt on ’t, above a bit, sir. I simply canna coom in. I mun go

[020-157] th’ way as lays afore me. I mun tak my leave o’ aw heer.’

[020-158] He made a sort of reverence to them by holding up his arms, and stood for

[020-159] the moment in that attitude; not speaking until they slowly dropped at

[020-160] his sides.

[020-161] ‘Monny’s the pleasant word as soom heer has spok’n wi’ me; monny’s the

[020-162] face I see heer, as I first seen when I were yoong and lighter heart’n

[020-163] than now. I ha’ never had no fratch afore, sin ever I were born, wi’ any

[020-164] o’ my like; Gonnows I ha’ none now that’s o’ my makin’. Yo’ll ca’ me

[020-165] traitor and that—yo I mean t’ say,’ addressing Slackbridge, ‘but ’tis

[020-166] easier to ca’ than mak’ out. So let be.’

[020-167] He had moved away a pace or two to come down from the platform, when he

[020-168] remembered something he had not said, and returned again.

[020-169] ‘Haply,’ he said, turning his furrowed face slowly about, that he might

[020-170] as it were individually address the whole audience, those both near and

[020-171] distant; ‘haply, when this question has been tak’n up and discoosed,

[020-172] there’ll be a threat to turn out if I’m let to work among yo. I hope I

[020-173] shall die ere ever such a time cooms, and I shall work solitary among yo

[020-174] unless it cooms—truly, I mun do ’t, my friends; not to brave yo, but to

[020-175] live. I ha nobbut work to live by; and wheerever can I go, I who ha

[020-176] worked sin I were no heighth at aw, in Coketown heer? I mak’ no

[020-177] complaints o’ bein turned to the wa’, o’ bein outcasten and overlooken

[020-178] fro this time forrard, but hope I shall be let to work. If there is any

[020-179] right for me at aw, my friends, I think ’tis that.’

[020-180] Not a word was spoken. Not a sound was audible in the building, but the

[020-181] slight rustle of men moving a little apart, all along the centre of the

[020-182] room, to open a means of passing out, to the man with whom they had all

[020-183] bound themselves to renounce companionship. Looking at no one, and going

[020-184] his way with a lowly steadiness upon him that asserted nothing and sought

[020-185] nothing, Old Stephen, with all his troubles on his head, left the scene.

[020-186] Then Slackbridge, who had kept his oratorical arm extended during the

[020-187] going out, as if he were repressing with infinite solicitude and by a

[020-188] wonderful moral power the vehement passions of the multitude, applied

[020-189] himself to raising their spirits. Had not the Roman Brutus, oh, my

[020-190] British countrymen, condemned his son to death; and had not the Spartan

[020-191] mothers, oh my soon to be victorious friends, driven their flying

[020-192] children on the points of their enemies’ swords? Then was it not the

[020-193] sacred [[duty]] of the men of Coketown, with forefathers before them, an

[020-194] admiring world in company with them, and a posterity to come after them,

[020-195] to hurl out traitors from the tents they had pitched in a sacred and a

[020-196] God-like cause? The winds of heaven answered Yes; and bore Yes, east,

[020-197] west, north, and south. And consequently three cheers for the United

[020-198] Aggregate Tribunal!

[020-199] Slackbridge acted as fugleman, and gave the time. The multitude of

[020-200] doubtful faces (a little conscience-stricken) brightened at the sound,

[020-201] and took it up. Private feeling must yield to the common cause. Hurrah!

[020-202] The roof yet vibrated with the cheering, when the assembly dispersed.

[020-203] Thus easily did Stephen Blackpool fall into the loneliest of lives, the

[020-204] life of solitude among a familiar crowd. The stranger in the land who

[020-205] looks into ten thousand faces for some answering look and never finds it,

[020-206] is in cheering society as compared with him who passes ten averted faces

[020-207] daily, that were once the countenances of friends. Such [[experience]] was

[020-208] to be Stephen’s now, in every waking moment of his life; at his work, on

[020-209] his way to it and from it, at his door, at his window, everywhere. By

[020-210] general consent, they even avoided that side of the street on which he

[020-211] habitually walked; and left it, of all the working men, to him only.

[020-212] He had been for many years, a quiet silent man, associating but little

[020-213] with other men, and used to companionship with his own thoughts. He had

[020-214] never known before the strength of the want in his heart for the frequent

[020-215] recognition of a nod, a look, a word; or the immense amount of relief

[020-216] that had been poured into it by drops through such small means. It was

[020-217] even harder than he could have believed possible, to separate in his own

[020-218] conscience his abandonment by all his fellows from a baseless sense of

[020-219] shame and disgrace.

[020-220] The first four days of his endurance were days so long and heavy, that he

[020-221] began to be appalled by the prospect before him. Not only did he see no

[020-222] [[rachael|Rachael]] all the time, but he avoided every chance of seeing her; for,

[020-223] although he knew that the prohibition did not yet formally extend to the

[020-224] women working in the factories, he found that some of them with whom he

[020-225] was acquainted were changed to him, and he feared to try others, and

[020-226] dreaded that Rachael might be even singled out from the rest if she were

[020-227] seen in his company. So, he had been quite alone during the four days,

[020-228] and had spoken to no one, when, as he was leaving his work at night, a

[020-229] young man of a very light complexion accosted him in the street.

[020-230] ‘Your name’s Blackpool, ain’t it?’ said the young man.

[020-231] Stephen coloured to find himself with his hat in his hand, in his

[020-232] gratitude for being spoken to, or in the suddenness of it, or both. He

[020-233] made a feint of adjusting the lining, and said, ‘Yes.’

[020-234] ‘You are the Hand they have sent to Coventry, I mean?’ said [[bitzer|Bitzer]], the

[020-235] very light young man in question.

[020-236] Stephen answered ‘Yes,’ again.

[020-237] ‘I supposed so, from their all appearing to keep away from you. Mr.

[020-238] Bounderby wants to speak to you. You know his house, don’t you?’

[020-239] Stephen said ‘Yes,’ again.

[020-240] ‘Then go straight up there, will you?’ said Bitzer. ‘You’re expected,

[020-241] and have only to tell the servant it’s you. I belong to the Bank; so, if

[020-242] you go straight up without me (I was sent to fetch you), you’ll save me a

[020-243] walk.’

[020-244] Stephen, whose way had been in the contrary direction, turned about, and

[020-245] betook himself as in duty bound, to the red brick castle of the giant

[020-246] Bounderby.
