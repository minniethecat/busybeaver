# United Aggregate Tribunal

The United Aggregate Tribunal is the trade union organization in [[Charles Dickens]]' *[[hard-times|Hard Times]]*, representing the mill workers of [[Coketown]]. It is led by [[Slackbridge]], a charismatic labor organizer whose fiery rhetoric dominates the union meetings.

## Role in the Novel

The Tribunal appears primarily in Book II, Chapters 4–5, dealing with [[Stephen-Blackpool]]'s refusal to join the union. Stephen, ostracized by his fellow workers for his conscientious objection, becomes an outcast — a position that Dickens uses to explore the moral complexities of [[solidarity|collective action]] and individual conscience.

[[Slackbridge]], the union's leader, addresses the assembled workers with a style that blends genuine grievance with manipulative rhetoric:

> "Oh my friends, the down-trodden operatives of Coketown! Oh my friends and fellow-countrymen, the slaves of an iron-handed and a grinding despotism!" (PN: 002-020-068)

## Significance

The Tribunal represents Dickens' ambivalent portrayal of organized labor. While sympathetic to workers' suffering, Dickens is wary of demagoguery and mob mentality — a tension embodied in the contrast between the principled [[Stephen-Blackpool]] and the manipulative [[Slackbridge]].

> "United Aggregate Tribunal, ever watchful for your welfare, ever zealous for your benefit, the United Aggregate Tribunal!" (PN: 003-032-079)

The Tribunal episodes also serve as a counterpoint to [[Josiah-Bounderby]]'s individualist rhetoric. Where Bounderby insists that every man rises or falls by his own merits, the Tribunal articulates a collective analysis of industrial exploitation — one that the novel treats with qualified sympathy even as it critiques Slackbridge's methods.
