[028-001] DOWN

[028-002] THE national dustmen, after entertaining one another with a great many

[028-003] noisy little fights among themselves, had dispersed for the present, and

[028-004] [[thomas-gradgrind|Mr. Gradgrind]] was at home for the vacation.

[028-005] He sat writing in the room with the deadly statistical clock, proving

[028-006] something no doubt—probably, in the main, that the Good Samaritan was a

[028-007] Bad Economist. The noise of the rain did not disturb him much; but it

[028-008] attracted his attention sufficiently to make him raise his [[reason|head]]

[028-009] sometimes, as if he were rather remonstrating with the elements. When it

[028-010] thundered very loudly, he glanced towards [[coketown|Coketown]], having it in his mind

[028-011] that some of the tall chimneys might be struck by lightning.

[028-012] The thunder was rolling into distance, and the rain was pouring down like

[028-013] a deluge, when the door of his room opened. He looked round the lamp

[028-014] upon his table, and saw, with amazement, his eldest daughter.

[028-015] ‘[[louisa-gradgrind|Louisa]]!’

[028-016] ‘Father, I want to speak to you.’

[028-017] ‘What is the matter? How strange you look! And good Heaven,’ said Mr.

[028-018] [[mrs-gradgrind|Gradgrind]], wondering more and more, ‘have you come here exposed to this

[028-019] storm?’

[028-020] She put her hands to her dress, as if she hardly knew. ‘Yes.’ Then she

[028-021] uncovered her head, and letting her cloak and hood fall where they might,

[028-022] stood looking at him: so colourless, so dishevelled, so defiant and

[028-023] despairing, that he was afraid of her.

[028-024] ‘What is it? I conjure you, Louisa, tell me what is the matter.’

[028-025] She dropped into a chair before him, and put her cold hand on his arm.

[028-026] ‘Father, you have trained me from my cradle?’

[028-027] ‘Yes, Louisa.’

[028-028] ‘I curse the hour in which I was born to such a destiny.’

[028-029] He looked at her in doubt and dread, vacantly repeating: ‘Curse the hour?

[028-030] Curse the hour?’

[028-031] ‘How could you give me life, and take from me all the inappreciable

[028-032] things that raise it from the state of conscious death? Where are the

[028-033] graces of my soul? Where are the sentiments of my [[heart]]? What have you

[028-034] done, O father, what have you done, with the garden that should have

[028-035] bloomed once, in this great wilderness here!’

[028-036] She struck herself with both her hands upon her bosom.

[028-037] ‘If it had ever been here, its ashes alone would save me from the void in

[028-038] which my whole life sinks. I did not mean to say this; but, father, you

[028-039] remember the last time we conversed in this room?’

[028-040] He had been so wholly unprepared for what he heard now, that it was with

[028-041] difficulty he answered, ‘Yes, Louisa.’

[028-042] ‘What has risen to my lips now, would have risen to my lips then, if you

[028-043] had given me a moment’s help. I don’t reproach you, father. What you

[028-044] have never nurtured in me, you have never nurtured in yourself; but O! if

[028-045] you had only done so long ago, or if you had only neglected me, what a

[028-046] much better and much happier creature I should have been this day!’

[028-047] On hearing this, after all his care, he bowed his head upon his hand and

[028-048] groaned aloud.

[028-049] ‘Father, if you had known, when we were last together here, what even I

[028-050] feared while I strove against it—as it has been my task from [[childhood|infancy]] to

[028-051] strive against every [[nature|natural]] prompting that has arisen in my heart; if

[028-052] you had known that there lingered in my breast, sensibilities,

[028-053] affections, weaknesses capable of being cherished into strength, defying

[028-054] all the calculations ever made by man, and no more known to his

[028-055] arithmetic than his Creator is,—would you have given me to the husband

[028-056] whom I am now sure that I hate?’

[028-057] He said, ‘No. No, my poor child.’

[028-058] ‘Would you have doomed me, at any time, to the frost and blight that have

[028-059] hardened and spoiled me? Would you have robbed me—for no one’s

[028-060] enrichment—only for the greater desolation of this world—of the

[028-061] immaterial part of my life, the spring and summer of my belief, my refuge

[028-062] from what is sordid and bad in the real things around me, my school in

[028-063] which I should have learned to be more humble and more trusting with

[028-064] them, and to hope in my little sphere to make them better?’

[028-065] ‘O no, no. No, Louisa.’

[028-066] ‘Yet, father, if I had been stone blind; if I had groped my way by my

[028-067] sense of touch, and had been free, while I knew the shapes and surfaces

[028-068] of things, to exercise my [[fancy]] somewhat, in regard to them; I should

[028-069] have been a million times wiser, happier, more [[love|loving]], more contented,

[028-070] more innocent and human in all good respects, than I am with the eyes I

[028-071] have. Now, hear what I have come to say.’

[028-072] He moved, to support her with his arm. She rising as he did so, they

[028-073] stood close together: she, with a hand upon his shoulder, looking fixedly

[028-074] in his face.

[028-075] ‘With a hunger and thirst upon me, father, which have never been for a

[028-076] moment appeased; with an ardent impulse towards some region where rules,

[028-077] and figures, and definitions were not quite absolute; I have grown up,

[028-078] battling every inch of my way.’

[028-079] ‘I never knew you were unhappy, my child.’

[028-080] ‘Father, I always knew it. In this strife I have almost repulsed and

[028-081] crushed my better angel into a demon. What I have learned has left me

[028-082] doubting, misbelieving, despising, regretting, what I have not learned;

[028-083] and my dismal resource has been to think that life would soon go by, and

[028-084] that nothing in it could be worth the pain and trouble of a contest.’

[028-085] ‘And you so young, Louisa!’ he said with pity.

[028-086] ‘And I so young. In this condition, father—for I [[performance|show]] you now, without

[028-087] fear or favour, the ordinary deadened state of my mind as I know it—you

[028-088] proposed my husband to me. I took him. I never made a pretence to him

[028-089] or you that I loved him. I knew, and, father, you knew, and he knew,

[028-090] that I never did. I was not wholly indifferent, for I had a hope of

[028-091] being pleasant and useful to [[tom-gradgrind|Tom]]. I made that wild escape into something

[028-092] visionary, and have slowly found out how wild it was. But Tom had been

[028-093] the subject of all the little tenderness of my life; perhaps he became so

[028-094] because I knew so well how to pity him. It matters little now, except as

[028-095] it may dispose you to think more leniently of his errors.’

[028-096] As her father held her in his arms, she put her other hand upon his other

[028-097] shoulder, and still looking fixedly in his face, went on.

[028-098] ‘When I was irrevocably married, there rose up into rebellion against the

[028-099] tie, the old strife, made fiercer by all those causes of disparity which

[028-100] arise out of our two individual natures, and which no general laws shall

[028-101] ever rule or state for me, father, until they shall be able to direct the

[028-102] anatomist where to strike his knife into the secrets of my soul.’

[028-103] ‘Louisa!’ he said, and said imploringly; for he well remembered what had

[028-104] passed between them in their former interview.

[028-105] ‘I do not reproach you, father, I make no complaint. I am here with

[028-106] another object.’

[028-107] ‘What can I do, child? Ask me what you will.’

[028-108] ‘I am coming to it. Father, chance then threw into my way a new

[028-109] acquaintance; a man such as I had had no [[experience]] of; used to the

[028-110] world; light, polished, easy; making no pretences; avowing the low

[028-111] estimate of everything, that I was half afraid to form in secret;

[028-112] conveying to me almost immediately, though I don’t know how or by what

[028-113] degrees, that he understood me, and read my thoughts. I could not find

[028-114] that he was worse than I. There seemed to be a near affinity between us.

[028-115] I only wondered it should be worth his while, who cared for nothing else,

[028-116] to care so much for me.’

[028-117] ‘For you, Louisa!’

[028-118] Her father might instinctively have loosened his hold, but that he felt

[028-119] her strength departing from her, and saw a wild dilating fire in the eyes

[028-120] steadfastly regarding him.

[028-121] ‘I say nothing of his plea for claiming my confidence. It matters very

[028-122] little how he gained it. Father, he did gain it. What you know of the

[028-123] story of my [[marriage]], he soon knew, just as well.’

[028-124] Her father’s face was ashy white, and he held her in both his arms.

[028-125] ‘I have done no worse, I have not disgraced you. But if you ask me

[028-126] whether I have loved him, or do love him, I tell you plainly, father,

[028-127] that it may be so. I don’t know.’

[028-128] She took her hands suddenly from his shoulders, and pressed them both

[028-129] upon her side; while in her face, not like itself—and in her figure,

[028-130] drawn up, resolute to finish by a last effort what she had to say—the

[028-131] feelings long suppressed broke loose.

[028-132] ‘This night, my husband being away, he has been with me, declaring

[028-133] himself my lover. This minute he expects me, for I could release myself

[028-134] of his presence by no other means. I do not know that I am sorry, I do

[028-135] not know that I am [[shame|ashamed]], I do not know that I am degraded in my own

[028-136] esteem. All that I know is, your philosophy and your teaching will not

[028-137] save me. Now, father, you have brought me to this. Save me by some

[028-138] other means!’

[028-139] He tightened his hold in time to prevent her sinking on the floor, but

[028-140] she cried out in a terrible voice, ‘I shall die if you hold me! Let me

[028-141] fall upon the ground!’ And he laid her down there, and saw the [[pride]] of

[028-142] his heart and the triumph of his system, lying, an insensible heap, at

[028-143] his feet.
