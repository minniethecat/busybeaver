# Coffin Room Metaphor

Throughout *Crime and Punishment*, Raskolnikov's cramped garret room is described in terms that evoke a coffin or tomb — the physical space mirrors his psychological state, transforming his living quarters into a symbol of spiritual death and suffocating isolation.

## Definition

The coffin-room metaphor refers to Dostoevsky's deliberate spatial symbolism in which Raskolnikov's lodgings — a tiny room under the roof of a five-story building — are repeatedly described using the language of burial, enclosure, and entrapment. The garret "was more like a cupboard than a room" (001-006), a space so small it feels like a "coffin" or "box" (003-438). This is not merely descriptive; the room's claustrophobic character externalizes Raskolnikov's inner state — his intellectual isolation, his moral suffocation after the murder, and his inability to escape the consequences of his crime.

## Expression in the Novel

The metaphor is established in the novel's opening lines: Raskolnikov emerges from "the garret in which he lodged in S. Place" (001-002), immediately linking his living conditions to his state of mind. Dostoevsky reinforces the imagery throughout: "It was a tiny cupboard of a room about six paces in length" (003-003), and Raskolnikov eventually feels "cramped and stifled in the little yellow room that was like a cupboard or a box" (003-437, 003-438). The yellow wallpaper of the room — a color Dostoevsky associates with sickness and decay — deepens the tomb-like atmosphere. Even Sonia's lodgings in the Kapernaumov apartment follow the same pattern: "rooms, or rather cupboards, into which Amalia Lippevechsel's flat was divided" (002-502), suggesting that spatial suffocation is not Raskolnikov's alone but a condition of Petersburg poverty more broadly.

## Key Passages

> "The garret was under the roof of a high, five-storied house and was more like a cupboard than a room." (001-005, 001-006)

> "It was a tiny cupboard of a room about six paces in length." (003-003)

> "At last he felt cramped and stifled in the little yellow room that was like a cupboard or a box." (003-437, 003-438)
