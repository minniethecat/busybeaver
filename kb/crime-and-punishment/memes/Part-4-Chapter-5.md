[025-001] When next morning at eleven o’clock punctually [[rodion-raskolnikov|Raskolnikov]] went into the

[025-002] department of the investigation of criminal causes and sent his name in

[025-003] to [[porfiry-petrovich|Porfiry]] Petrovitch, he was surprised at being kept waiting so long:

[025-004] it was at least ten minutes before he was summoned. He had expected

[025-005] that they would pounce upon him. But he stood in the waiting-room, and

[025-006] people, who apparently had nothing to do with him, were continually

[025-007] passing to and fro before him. In the next room which looked like an

[025-008] office, several clerks were sitting writing and obviously they had

[025-009] no notion who or what Raskolnikov might be. He looked uneasily and

[025-010] suspiciously about him to see whether there was not some guard, some

[025-011] mysterious watch being kept on him to prevent his escape. But there was

[025-012] nothing of the sort: he saw only the faces of clerks absorbed in petty

[025-013] details, then other people, no one seemed to have any concern with him.

[025-014] He might go where he liked for them. The conviction grew stronger in him

[025-015] that if that enigmatic man of yesterday, that phantom sprung out of the

[025-016] earth, had seen everything, they would not have let him stand and wait

[025-017] like that. And would they have waited till he elected to appear at

[025-018] eleven? Either the man had not yet given information, or... or simply

[025-019] he knew nothing, had seen nothing (and how could he have seen anything?)

[025-020] and so all that had happened to him the day before was again a phantom

[025-021] exaggerated by his sick and overstrained imagination. This conjecture

[025-022] had begun to grow strong the day before, in the midst of all his

[025-023] alarm and despair. Thinking it all over now and preparing for a fresh

[025-024] conflict, he was suddenly aware that he was trembling--and he felt a

[025-025] rush of indignation at the thought that he was trembling with [[fear-and-dread|fear]] at

[025-026] facing that hateful Porfiry Petrovitch. What he dreaded above all was

[025-027] meeting that man again; he hated him with an intense, unmitigated hatred

[025-028] and was afraid his hatred might betray him. His indignation was such

[025-029] that he ceased trembling at once; he made ready to go in with a cold and

[025-030] arrogant bearing and vowed to himself to keep as silent as possible,

[025-031] to watch and listen and for once at least to control his overstrained

[025-032] nerves. At that moment he was summoned to Porfiry Petrovitch.

[025-034] neither large nor small, furnished with a large writing-table, that

[025-035] stood before a sofa, upholstered in checked material, a bureau, a

[025-036] bookcase in the corner and several chairs--all government furniture,

[025-037] of polished yellow wood. In the further wall there was a closed door,

[025-038] beyond it there were no doubt other rooms. On Raskolnikov’s entrance

[025-039] Porfiry Petrovitch had at once closed the door by which he had come in

[025-040] and they remained alone. He met his visitor with an apparently genial

[025-041] and good-tempered air, and it was only after a few minutes that

[025-042] Raskolnikov saw signs of a certain awkwardness in him, as though he had

[025-043] been thrown out of his reckoning or caught in something very secret.

[025-045] holding out both hands to him. “Come, sit down, old man... or perhaps

[025-046] you don’t like to be called ‘my dear fellow’ and ‘old man!’--_tout

[025-047] court_? Please don’t think it too familiar.... Here, on the sofa.”

[025-048] Raskolnikov sat down, keeping his eyes fixed on him. “In our domain,”

[025-049] the apologies for familiarity, the French phrase _tout court_, were all

[025-050] characteristic signs.

[025-051] “He held out both hands to me, but he did not give me one--he drew it

[025-052] back in time,” struck him suspiciously. Both were watching each other,

[025-054] “I brought you this paper... about the watch. Here it is. Is it all

[025-055] right or shall I copy it again?”

[025-056] “What? A paper? Yes, yes, don’t be uneasy, it’s all right,” Porfiry

[025-057] Petrovitch said as though in haste, and after he had said it he took the

[025-061] table and put it on his bureau.
