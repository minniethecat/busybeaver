# Afanasy Ivanovich Vakhrushin

Afanasy Ivanovich Vakhrushin is a merchant and distant relative of the Raskolnikov family, mentioned in [[pulcheria-alexandrovna|Pulcheria Alexandrovna]]'s letter to her son. He handles financial transactions on her behalf, sending money to [[rodion-raskolnikov|Raskolnikov]] in [[st-petersburg|St. Petersburg]] and vouching for the family's credit. Like [[praskovya-pavlovna|Praskovya Pavlovna]] and [[marfa-petrovna|Marfa Petrovna]], he is an unseen figure whose economic function shapes the plot — the money he forwards enables Raskolnikov's survival and, indirectly, his crime.

## Overview

Vakhrushin appears only in references by other characters. Pulcheria mentions "Afanasy Ivanovitch Vahrushin, of whom you have probably heard" (010-067) in connection with financial support for Raskolnikov. Dmitri Razumihin mentions him as the source of money transferred to Raskolnikov through his mother's arrangement. He is the novel's most peripheral named character — a name on a letter, a sum of money in a transfer — but his presence illustrates the economic networks that sustain (or fail to sustain) the novel's characters.

## Key Quotes

> "Through Afanasy Ivanovitch Vahrushin, of whom you have probably heard." — Pulcheria's letter, explaining the source of Raskolnikov's money (010-067)
