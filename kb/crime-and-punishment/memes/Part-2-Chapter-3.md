[010-001] He was not completely unconscious, however, all the time he was ill; he

[010-002] was in a feverish state, sometimes delirious, sometimes half conscious.

[010-003] He remembered a great deal afterwards. Sometimes it seemed as though

[010-004] there were a number of people round him; they wanted to take him away

[010-005] somewhere, there was a great deal of squabbling and discussing about

[010-006] him. Then he would be alone in the room; they had all gone away afraid

[010-007] of him, and only now and then opened the door a crack to look at him;

[010-008] they threatened him, plotted something together, laughed, and mocked

[010-010] another person, too, whom he seemed to know very well, though he could

[010-011] not remember who he was, and this fretted him, even made him cry.

[010-013] it all seemed part of the same day. But of _that_--of _that_ he had

[010-014] no recollection, and yet every minute he felt that he had forgotten

[010-015] something he ought to remember. He worried and tormented himself trying

[010-016] to remember, moaned, flew into a rage, or sank into awful, intolerable

[010-017] terror. Then he struggled to get up, would have run away, but someone

[010-018] always prevented him by force, and he sank back into impotence and

[010-019] forgetfulness. At last he returned to complete consciousness.
