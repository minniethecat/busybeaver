# Free Will

Dostoevsky's novel is fundamentally an argument for free will against the deterministic doctrines of his time. The "environment" theory — that crime is caused by social conditions and criminals therefore bear no moral responsibility — is explicitly debated and rejected. Raskolnikov chose to kill, and the novel's entire psychological project is to establish that choice as real, consequential, and answerable.

## Definition

Free will in the novel is the capacity for moral choice that makes conscience meaningful. If human actions are determined by "environment" (019-268), then "conscience" is an illusion and punishment is unjust. The novel insists, against this view, that Raskolnikov acted "of his own free will" (010-435) and that this freedom is precisely what makes his suffering meaningful rather than merely pathological.

## Expression in the Novel

The novel embeds the free-will-versus-determinism debate in its dialogue. Lebeziatnikov and the "socialists" argue that "crime is a protest against the abnormality of the social organisation — nothing more" (019-259–019-268) — a position that eliminates moral agency. Razumihin rebuts: "Environment accounts for a great deal in crime, I can assure you — but not everything" (019-298). Porfiry, the novel's most acute psychologist, asks: "Was it environment drove him to it?" (019-301), implying that the answer is no. Raskolnikov's own insistence that he acted freely — that he "wanted to become a Napoleon" (030-312), not that circumstances forced him — is his refusal of the determinist escape route. Marmeladov, by contrast, is the novel's portrait of someone who has surrendered free will to drink and self-pity. Dounia's decision to accept Luzhin is described as "of her own free will" (016-256), marking the distinction between freely chosen action and coercion.

## Key Passages

> "Crime is a protest against the abnormality of the social organisation — nothing more, and nothing else. No other causes are admitted." (019-259–019-268)

> "Environment accounts for a great deal in crime, I can assure you — but not everything." (019-298)

> "Of his own free will." (010-435)
