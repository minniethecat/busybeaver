[009-001] “And what if there has been a search already? What if I find them in my

[009-002] room?”

[009-003] But here was his room. Nothing and no one in it. No one had peeped in.

[009-004] Even [[nastasya|Nastasya]] had not touched it. But heavens! how could he have left

[009-005] all those things in the hole?

[009-006] He rushed to the corner, slipped his hand under the paper, pulled the

[009-007] things out and lined his pockets with them. There were eight articles in

[009-008] all: two little boxes with ear-rings or something of the sort, he hardly

[009-009] looked to see; then four small leather cases. There was a chain, too,

[009-010] merely wrapped in newspaper and something else in newspaper, that looked

[009-011] like a decoration.... He put them all in the different pockets of his

[009-012] overcoat, and the remaining pocket of his trousers, trying to conceal

[009-013] them as much as possible. He took the purse, too. Then he went out of

[009-014] his room, leaving the door open. He walked quickly and resolutely, and

[009-015] though he felt shattered, he had his senses about him. He was afraid of

[009-016] pursuit, he was afraid that in another half-hour, another quarter of an

[009-017] hour perhaps, instructions would be issued for his pursuit, and so at

[009-018] all costs, he must hide all traces before then. He must clear everything

[009-019] up while he still had some strength, some reasoning power left him....

[009-020] Where was he to go?

[009-021] That had long been settled: “Fling them into [[catherine-canal|the canal]], and all traces

[009-022] hidden in the water, the thing would be at an end.” So he had decided in

[009-023] the night of his delirium when several times he had had the impulse to

[009-024] get up and go away, to make haste, and get rid of it all. But to get

[009-025] rid of it, turned out to be a very difficult task. He wandered along

[009-026] the bank of the Ekaterininsky Canal for half an hour or more and looked

[009-027] several times at the steps running down to the water, but he could not

[009-028] think of carrying out his plan; either rafts stood at the steps’ edge,

[009-029] and women were washing clothes on them, or boats were moored there, and

[009-030] people were swarming everywhere. Moreover he could be seen and noticed

[009-031] from the banks on all sides; it would look suspicious for a man to go

[009-032] down on purpose, stop, and throw something into the water. And what if

[009-033] the boxes were to float instead of sinking? And of course they would.

[009-034] Even as it was, everyone he met seemed to stare and look round, as if

[009-035] they had nothing to do but to watch him. “Why is it, or can it be my

[009-036] fancy?” he thought.

[009-038] Neva. There were not so many people there, he would be less observed,

[009-040] off. He wondered how he could have been wandering for a good half-hour,

[009-041] worried and anxious in this dangerous past without thinking of it

[009-076] hollow in the ground, and he immediately emptied his pocket into it.

[009-079] in the same position again, though it stood a very little higher. But

[009-081] Nothing could be noticed.
