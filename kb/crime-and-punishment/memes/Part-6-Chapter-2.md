[033-002] lighted one. “They are pernicious, positively pernicious, and yet I

[033-003] can’t give them up! I cough, I begin to have tickling in my throat and

[033-004] a difficulty in breathing. You know I am a coward, I went lately to

[033-005] Dr. B----n; he always gives at least half an hour to each patient. He

[033-006] positively laughed looking at me; he sounded me: ‘Tobacco’s bad for

[033-007] you,’ he said, ‘your lungs are affected.’ But how am I to give it up?

[033-008] What is there to take its place? I don’t drink, that’s the mischief,

[033-009] he-he-he, that I don’t. Everything is relative, Rodion Romanovitch,

[033-010] everything is relative!”

[033-012] with disgust. All the circumstances of their last interview suddenly

[033-013] came back to him, and he felt a rush of the feeling that had come upon

[033-014] him then.

[033-016] know?” Porfiry Petrovitch went on, looking round the room. “I came into

[033-017] this very room. I was passing by, just as I did to-day, and I thought

[033-018] I’d return your call. I walked in as your door was wide open, I looked

[033-019] round, waited and went out without leaving my name with your servant.

[033-022] his state of mind.

[033-024] I owe you an explanation and must give it to you,” he continued with a

[033-025] slight smile, just patting Raskolnikov’s knee.

[033-027] face; to his surprise Raskolnikov saw a touch of sadness in it. He had
