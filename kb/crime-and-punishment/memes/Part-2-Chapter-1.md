[008-001] So he lay a very long while. Now and then he seemed to wake up, and at

[008-002] such moments he noticed that it was far into the night, but it did not

[008-003] occur to him to get up. At last he noticed that it was beginning to get

[008-004] light. He was lying on his back, still dazed from his recent oblivion.

[008-005] Fearful, despairing cries rose shrilly from the street, sounds which he

[008-006] heard every night, indeed, under his window after two o’clock. They woke

[008-007] him up now.

[008-008] “Ah! the drunken men are coming out of the taverns,” he thought, “it’s

[008-009] past two o’clock,” and at once he leaped up, as though someone had

[008-010] pulled him from the sofa.

[008-011] “What! Past two o’clock!”

[008-012] He sat down on the sofa--and instantly recollected everything! All at

[008-013] once, in one flash, he recollected everything.

[008-014] For the first moment he thought he was going mad. A dreadful chill came

[008-015] over him; but the chill was from the [[illness-and-delirium|fever]] that had begun long before in

[008-016] his sleep. Now he was suddenly taken with violent shivering, so that his

[008-021] flung himself on the sofa without undressing, without even taking his

[008-022] hat off. It had fallen off and was lying on the floor near his pillow.

[008-024] but...”
