[017-001] “He is well, quite well!” [[zossimov|Zossimov]] cried cheerfully as they entered.

[017-002] He had come in ten minutes earlier and was sitting in the same place

[017-003] as before, on the sofa. [[rodion-raskolnikov|Raskolnikov]] was sitting in the opposite corner,

[017-004] fully dressed and carefully washed and combed, as he had not been for

[017-005] some time past. The room was immediately crowded, yet [[nastasya|Nastasya]] managed

[017-006] to follow the visitors in and stayed to listen.

[017-007] Raskolnikov really was almost well, as compared with his condition the

[017-008] day before, but he was still pale, listless, and sombre. He looked like

[017-009] a wounded man or one who has undergone some terrible physical suffering.

[017-010] His brows were knitted, his lips compressed, his eyes feverish. He spoke

[017-011] little and reluctantly, as though performing a duty, and there was a

[017-012] restlessness in his movements.

[017-014] the impression of a man with a painful abscess or a broken arm. The

[017-015] pale, sombre face lighted up for a moment when his mother and sister

[017-016] entered, but this only gave it a look of more intense suffering, in

[017-017] place of its listless dejection. The light soon died away, but the look

[017-018] of suffering remained, and Zossimov, watching and studying his patient

[017-019] with all the zest of a young doctor beginning to practise, noticed

[017-020] in him no joy at the arrival of his mother and sister, but a sort of

[017-021] bitter, hidden determination to bear another hour or two of inevitable

[017-022] torture. He saw later that almost every word of the following

[017-023] conversation seemed to touch on some sore place and irritate it. But

[017-024] at the same time he marvelled at the power of controlling himself

[017-025] and hiding his feelings in a patient who the previous day had, like a

[017-026] monomaniac, fallen into a frenzy at the slightest word.

[017-028] giving his mother and sister a kiss of welcome which made Pulcheria

[017-029] Alexandrovna radiant at once. “And I don’t say this _as I did

[017-031] his hand.
