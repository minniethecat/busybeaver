# Marmeladov's Death

After leaving [[dmitri-razumihin|Razumihin]], [[rodion-raskolnikov|Raskolnikov]] encounters [[marmeladov|Marmeladov]] crushed under a carriage in the [[hay-market|Hay Market]]. He takes charge, carrying the dying man home, where [[marmeladov|Marmeladov]] expires in [[sonia-marmeladov|Sonia]]'s arms as [[katerina-ivanovna|Katerina Ivanovna]] rages and the children weep. [[rodion-raskolnikov|Raskolnikov]] gives his last money to the family — an act of spontaneous charity that complicates any simple judgment of his character.

## The Scene

"A crowd had gathered. The crushed man was lying in the roadway" (014-250). [[rodion-raskolnikov|Raskolnikov]] recognizes [[marmeladov|Marmeladov]] and insists on carrying him home at his own expense. The deathbed scene is Dostoevsky at his most operatic: the priest administers last rites, [[katerina-ivanovna|Katerina Ivanovna]] alternately curses and weeps, and [[sonia-marmeladov|Sonia]] appears "in her gaudy dress" (014-340) — the prostitute's garments a silent accusation against the world that made her.

## Significance

The scene establishes [[rodion-raskolnikov|Raskolnikov]]'s capacity for compassion — he is not the Nietzschean superman his theory imagines. It also introduces [[sonia-marmeladov|Sonia]] in person and seals [[rodion-raskolnikov|Raskolnikov]]'s bond with the family.
