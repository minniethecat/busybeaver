[016-001] [[dmitri-razumihin|Razumihin]] waked up next morning at eight o’clock, troubled and serious.

[016-002] He found himself confronted with many new and unlooked-for perplexities.

[016-003] He had never expected that he would ever wake up feeling like that. He

[016-004] remembered every detail of the previous day and he knew that a perfectly

[016-005] novel experience had befallen him, that he had received an impression

[016-006] unlike anything he had known before. At the same time he recognised

[016-007] clearly that the dream which had fired his imagination was hopelessly

[016-008] unattainable--so unattainable that he felt positively ashamed of it, and

[016-009] he hastened to pass to the other more practical cares and difficulties

[016-010] bequeathed him by that “thrice accursed yesterday.”

[016-011] The most awful recollection of the previous day was the way he had shown

[016-012] himself “base and mean,” not only because he had been drunk, but

[016-013] because he had taken advantage of the young girl’s position to abuse

[016-014] her _fiancé_ in his stupid jealousy, knowing nothing of their mutual

[016-015] relations and obligations and next to nothing of the man himself. And

[016-016] what right had he to criticise him in that hasty and unguarded manner?

[016-017] Who had asked for his opinion? Was it thinkable that such a creature as

[016-018] Avdotya Romanovna would be marrying an unworthy man for money? So there

[016-019] must be something in him. The lodgings? But after all how could he know

[016-020] the character of the lodgings? He was furnishing a flat... Foo! how

[016-021] despicable it all was! And what justification was it that he was drunk?

[016-022] Such a stupid excuse was even more degrading! In wine is truth, and the

[016-023] truth had all come out, “that is, all the uncleanness of his coarse

[016-024] and envious heart”! And would such a dream ever be permissible to

[016-025] him, Razumihin? What was he beside such a girl--he, the drunken noisy

[016-026] braggart of last night? Was it possible to imagine so absurd and cynical

[016-027] a juxtaposition? Razumihin blushed desperately at the very idea and

[016-028] suddenly the recollection forced itself vividly upon him of how he had

[016-029] said last night on the [[staircases|stairs]] that [[praskovya-pavlovna|the landlady]] would be jealous of

[016-030] Avdotya Romanovna... that was simply intolerable. He brought his fist

[016-031] down heavily on the kitchen stove, hurt his hand and sent one of the

[016-032] bricks flying.

[016-033] “Of course,” he muttered to himself a minute later with a feeling of

[016-034] self-abasement, “of course, all these infamies can never be wiped out or

[016-035] smoothed over... and so it’s useless even to think of it, and I must

[016-036] go to them in silence and do my duty... in silence, too... and not ask

[016-037] forgiveness, and say nothing... for all is lost now!”

[016-038] And yet as he dressed he examined his attire more carefully than usual.

[016-039] He hadn’t another suit--if he had had, perhaps he wouldn’t have put it

[016-040] on. “I would have made a point of not putting it on.” But in any case he

[016-041] could not remain a cynic and a dirty sloven; he had no right to offend

[016-042] the feelings of others, especially when they were in need of his

[016-043] assistance and asking him to see them. He brushed his clothes carefully.

[016-044] His linen was always decent; in that respect he was especially clean.

[016-045] He washed that morning scrupulously--he got some soap from [[nastasya|Nastasya]]--he

[016-046] washed his hair, his neck and especially his hands. When it came to the

[016-047] question whether to shave his stubbly chin or not (Praskovya Pavlovna

[016-048] had capital razors that had been left by her late husband), the question

[016-049] was angrily answered in the negative. “Let it stay as it is! What if

[016-050] they think that I shaved on purpose to...? They certainly would think

[016-051] so! Not on any account!”

[016-052] “And... the worst of it was he was so coarse, so dirty, he had the

[016-053] manners of a pothouse; and... and even admitting that he knew he had

[016-054] some of the essentials of a gentleman... what was there in that to be

[016-055] proud of? Everyone ought to be a gentleman and more than that... and all

[016-056] the same (he remembered) he, too, had done little things... not exactly

[016-057] dishonest, and yet.... And what thoughts he sometimes had; hm... and to

[016-058] set all that beside Avdotya Romanovna! Confound it! So be it! Well, he’d

[016-059] make a point then of being dirty, greasy, pothouse in his manners and he

[016-060] wouldn’t care! He’d be worse!”

[016-061] He was engaged in such monologues when [[zossimov|Zossimov]], who had spent the night

[016-062] in Praskovya Pavlovna’s parlour, came in.

[016-063] He was going home and was in a hurry to look at the invalid first.

[016-064] Razumihin informed him that [[rodion-raskolnikov|Raskolnikov]] was sleeping like a dormouse.

[016-065] Zossimov gave orders that they shouldn’t wake him and promised to see

[016-066] him again about eleven.

[016-067] “If he is still at home,” he added. “Damn it all! If one can’t control

[016-068] one’s patients, how is one to cure them? Do you know whether _he_ will

[016-069] go to them, or whether _they_ are coming here?”

[016-070] “They are coming, I think,” said Razumihin, understanding the object

[016-071] of the question, “and they will discuss their family affairs, no doubt.

[016-072] I’ll be off. You, as the doctor, have more right to be here than I.”

[016-073] “But I am not a father confessor; I shall come and go away; I’ve plenty

[016-074] to do besides looking after them.”

[016-075] “One thing worries me,” interposed Razumihin, frowning. “On the way home

[016-076] I talked a lot of drunken nonsense to him... all sorts of things... and

[016-077] amongst them that you were afraid that he... might become insane.”

[016-078] “You told the ladies so, too.”

[016-079] “I know it was stupid! You may beat me if you like! Did you think so

[016-080] seriously?”

[016-081] “That’s nonsense, I tell you, how could I think it seriously? You,

[016-082] yourself, described him as a monomaniac when you fetched me to

[016-083] him... and we added fuel to the fire yesterday, you did, that is, with

[016-084] your story about the painter; it was a nice conversation, when he was,

[016-085] perhaps, mad on that very point! If only I’d known what happened then

[016-086] at the police station and that some wretch... had insulted him with this

[016-087] suspicion! Hm... I would not have allowed that conversation yesterday.

[016-088] These monomaniacs will make a mountain out of a mole-hill... and

[016-089] see their fancies as solid realities.... As far as I remember, it was

[016-090] [[zametov|Zametov]]’s story that cleared up half the mystery, to my mind. Why, I

[016-091] know one case in which a hypochondriac, a man of forty, cut the throat

[016-095] frantic with hypochondria, and with his morbid exceptional vanity! That

[016-096] may well have been the starting-point of illness. Well, bother it

[016-097] all!... And, by the way, that Zametov certainly is a nice fellow, but

[016-098] hm... he shouldn’t have told all that last night. He is an awful

[016-099] chatterbox!”

[016-100] “But whom did he tell it to? You and me?”

[016-101] “And [[porfiry-petrovich|Porfiry]].”

[016-102] “What does that matter?”

[016-103] “And, by the way, have you any influence on them, his mother and sister?

[016-104] Tell them to be more careful with him to-day....”

[016-105] “They’ll get on all right!” Razumihin answered reluctantly.

[016-106] “Why is he so set against this Luzhin? A man with money and she doesn’t

[016-107] seem to dislike him... and they haven’t a farthing, I suppose? eh?”

[016-108] “But what business is it of yours?” Razumihin cried with annoyance. “How

[016-109] can I tell whether they’ve a farthing? Ask them yourself and perhaps

[016-110] you’ll find out....”

[016-111] “Foo! what an ass you are sometimes! Last night’s wine has not gone off

[016-112] yet.... Good-bye; thank your Praskovya Pavlovna from me for my night’s

[016-113] lodging. She locked herself in, made no reply to my _bonjour_ through

[016-114] the door; she was up at seven o’clock, the samovar was taken into her

[016-115] from the kitchen. I was not vouchsafed a personal interview....”

[016-117] house. Both ladies were waiting for him with nervous impatience. They

[016-118] had risen at seven o’clock or earlier. He entered looking as black as

[016-124] complete and unlooked-for respect (in place of the sneering looks and

[016-126] confusion than if he had been met with abuse. Fortunately there was a

[016-127] subject for conversation, and he made haste to snatch at it.

[016-128] Hearing that everything was going well and that Rodya had not yet waked,

[016-130] “she had something which it was very, very necessary to talk over

[016-135] a dirty and disorderly way that the ladies were ashamed. Razumihin

[016-147] “Tell me, tell me! What do you think...? Excuse me, I still don’t know

[016-148] your name!” Pulcheria Alexandrovna put in hastily.

[016-149] “Dmitri Prokofitch.”

[016-150] “I should like very, very much to know, Dmitri Prokofitch... how he
