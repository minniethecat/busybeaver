## About this index

The symbolic register in *Crime and Punishment* operates through material objects and spatial configurations that acquire moral meaning through repetition and variation. Blood is both physical evidence and metaphysical stain. Thresholds mark boundaries between states of being — between intention and action, confession and concealment, life and death. The city itself becomes a symbolic map: cramped rooms, narrow staircases, and the open expanse of the Neva each signify distinct psychological conditions. This index gathers every page tagged `symbolism`, covering individual motifs, spatial symbolism, and the broader literary function of imagery in Dostoevsky's technique.
