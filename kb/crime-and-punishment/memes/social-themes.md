## About this index

The social dimension of *Crime and Punishment* encompasses the material conditions that shape character and constrain choice. Marmeladov's alcoholism is inseparable from the degradation of the lower civil servant; Sonia's prostitution is a direct consequence of economic desperation; Luzhin's rational egoism is the ideology of an ascendant middle class. The novel's social critique runs through every stratum of St. Petersburg society, from the Haymarket tenements to the comfortable apartments of the professional class. Pages in this index explore poverty, class, gender, family, and the social institutions — police, law, medicine — that structure the novel's world.
