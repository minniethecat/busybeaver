# Sonia's Room

Sonia lives in a rented room at the home of the tailor Kapernaumov — "a strange, irregular room, almost a barn" (024-330). Despite its poverty, this room becomes the novel's most sacred space: here the Gospel of John is read aloud, and here Raskolnikov speaks the words that set his redemption in motion.

## The Lazarus Reading

Raskolnikov visits Sonia and demands she read him "the raising of Lazarus" (024-354). She reads through the entire passage, her voice trembling: "Jesus said unto her, I am the resurrection and the life" (024-425). The room, with its "one candle-end in a broken candlestick" (024-330), becomes a church.

## The Confession

In the novel's Epilogue, Raskolnikov recalls that "he had gone to her for human understanding" (039-008) — it was in this room that he first spoke his crime and began the journey toward redemption.
