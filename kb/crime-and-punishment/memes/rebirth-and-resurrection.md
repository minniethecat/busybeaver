# Rebirth and Resurrection

The novel's title encodes its arc: crime leads to punishment, and punishment — when accepted as suffering rather than merely endured — opens the possibility of rebirth. The Epilogue completes this movement with the promise of "a full resurrection into a new life" (039-828), translating the Lazarus narrative from literary symbol into lived reality.

## Definition

Resurrection in the novel is not merely the afterlife but the renewal of the living: the possibility that a person who has committed the gravest sin can, through suffering accepted and love received, be made new. The Lazarus narrative provides the theological template: "Jesus said unto her, I am the resurrection and the life" (024-425). Raskolnikov, like Lazarus, must be called forth from the tomb of his own making.

## Expression in the Novel

The Lazarus reading (Part 4, Chapter 4) is the novel's central religious scene. Raskolnikov asks Sonia: "Do you believe in Lazarus' rising from the dead?" (019-446). Sonia reads the entire Gospel passage, her voice breaking: "He cried with a loud voice, Lazarus, come forth. And he that was dead came forth" (024-474–024-485). "It is all about the raising of Lazarus," she whispers (024-485). In the Epilogue, the raising of Lazarus is invoked again (039-854), and the novel closes with one of literature's most compressed images of resurrection: "the dawn of a new future, of a full resurrection into a new life. They were renewed by love" (039-828).

## Key Passages

> "Do you believe in Lazarus' rising from the dead?" (019-446)

> "I am the resurrection and the life: he that believeth in me, though he were dead, yet shall he live." (024-425)

> "The dawn of a new future, of a full resurrection into a new life. They were renewed by love." (039-828)

## Character Perspectives

- **Rodion Raskolnikov**: His spiritual death (murder, isolation, fever) and rebirth (confession, suffering, love) form the novel's Lazarus arc. The Epilogue insists that his conversion is genuine — not intellectual assent but a transformation effected through suffering and Sonia's love.

- **Sonia Marmeladov**: The agent of resurrection — her faith, love, and constancy call Raskolnikov from spiritual death. She follows him to Siberia and becomes the instrument of his renewal.
