## About this index

The philosophical dimension of the novel is inseparable from its psychological and narrative structure. Raskolnikov does not merely hold theories — he enacts them, and the novel's plot is the dramatic testing of those theories against reality. The utilitarian calculus that justifies Alyona Ivanovna's murder, the nihilist rejection of moral absolutes, the rational egoism that Luzhin embodies in caricature, and the Christian existentialism that Sonia represents — all of these are philosophical positions given flesh in character and event. This index gathers pages tagged `philosophy` or `existential`, covering the intellectual frameworks, the characters who embody them, and the dramatic confrontations in which they are contested.
