[005-001] “Of course, I’ve been meaning lately to go to [[dmitri-razumihin|Razumihin]]’s to ask for

[005-004] he shares his last farthing with me, if he has any farthings, so that

[005-005] I could get some boots and make myself tidy enough to give lessons...

[005-006] hm... Well and what then? What shall I do with the few coppers I

[005-007] earn? That’s not what I want now. It’s really absurd for me to go to

[005-008] Razumihin....”

[005-009] The question why he was now going to Razumihin agitated him even more

[005-010] than he was himself aware; he kept uneasily seeking for some sinister

[005-011] significance in this apparently ordinary action.

[005-012] “Could I have expected to set it all straight and to find a way out by

[005-013] means of Razumihin alone?” he asked himself in perplexity.

[005-015] musing, suddenly, as if it were spontaneously and by [[fate-and-coincidence|chance]], a fantastic

[005-016] thought came into his head.

[005-018] reached a final determination. “I shall go to Razumihin’s of course,

[005-019] but... not now. I shall go to him... on the next day after It, when It

[005-020] will be over and everything will begin afresh....”

[005-068] powerful impression on the overwrought and deranged nervous system.
