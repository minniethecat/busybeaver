[004-001] His mother’s letter had been a torture to him, but as regards the chief

[004-002] fact in it, he had felt not one moment’s hesitation, even whilst he was

[004-003] reading the letter. The essential question was settled, and irrevocably

[004-004] settled, in his mind: “Never such a marriage while I am alive and

[004-005] Mr. Luzhin be damned!” “The thing is perfectly clear,” he muttered

[004-006] to himself, with a malignant smile anticipating the triumph of his

[004-007] decision. “No, mother, no, [[dounia-raskolnikov|Dounia]], you won’t deceive me! and then they

[004-008] apologise for not asking my advice and for taking the decision without

[004-009] me! I dare say! They imagine it is arranged now and can’t be broken

[004-010] off; but we will see whether it can or not! A magnificent excuse:

[004-011] ‘Pyotr Petrovitch is such a busy man that even his wedding has to be in

[004-012] post-haste, almost by express.’ No, Dounia, I see it all and I know what

[004-013] you want to say to me; and I know too what you were thinking about, when

[004-014] you walked up and down all night, and what your prayers were like before

[004-015] the Holy Mother of Kazan who stands in mother’s bedroom. Bitter is

[004-016] the ascent to Golgotha.... Hm... so it is finally settled; you have

[004-017] determined to marry a sensible business man, Avdotya Romanovna, one

[004-018] who has a fortune (has _already_ made his fortune, that is so much

[004-019] more solid and impressive), a man who holds two government posts and who

[004-020] shares the ideas of our most rising generation, as mother writes, and

[004-021] who _seems_ to be kind, as Dounia herself observes. That _seems_ beats

[004-022] everything! And that very Dounia for that very ‘_seems_’ is marrying

[004-023] him! Splendid! splendid!

[004-024] “... But I should like to know why mother has written to me about ‘our

[004-025] most rising generation’? Simply as a descriptive touch, or with the idea

[004-026] of prepossessing me in favour of Mr. Luzhin? Oh, the cunning of them!

[004-027] I should like to know one thing more: how far they were open with one

[004-028] another that day and night and all this time since? Was it all put into

[004-029] _words_, or did both understand that they had the same thing at heart

[004-031] better not to speak of it. Most likely it was partly like that, from

[004-032] mother’s letter it’s evident: he struck her as rude _a little_, and

[004-033] mother in her simplicity took her observations to Dounia. And she was

[004-034] sure to be vexed and ‘answered her angrily.’ I should think so! Who

[004-035] would not be angered when it was quite clear without any naïve questions

[004-036] and when it was understood that it was useless to discuss it. And why

[004-037] does she write to me, ‘love Dounia, [[rodion-raskolnikov|Rodya]], and she loves you more than

[004-038] herself’? Has she a secret conscience-prick at sacrificing her daughter

[004-039] to her son? ‘You are our one comfort, you are everything to us.’ Oh,

[004-040] mother!”

[004-042] meet Mr. Luzhin at the moment, he might have murdered him.
