## Overview

Perov is a small settlement or village near the [[shipilovka|Shipilovka estate]], mentioned in "The Agent." It functions as a local destination where the estate agent [[sofron|Sofron]] goes to drink.

## Description

Perov is referenced twice in "The Agent" in connection with [[sofron|Sofron]], the estate agent. When [[arkady-pyenotchkin|Arkady Pavlitch Pyenotchkin]] arrives at [[shipilovka|Shipilovka]] and inquires after [[sofron|Sofron]], the bailiff reports that [[sofron|Sofron]] has "gone to Perov" (010-001). Later, when [[sofron|Sofron]] appears, the narrator observes that "the agent must have been making merry at Perov: his face was unmistakably flushed, and there was a smell of spirits about him" (010-002). From these two mentions, Perov appears to be a nearby settlement—possibly a village with a tavern or drinking-house—within riding distance of the [[shipilovka|Shipilovka estate]].

## Role in the Stories

Perov appears only as a background detail in the portrait of [[sofron|Sofron]]'s management style. That the agent can leave the estate to drink at Perov, and that his absence is casually reported by the bailiff, suggests a relaxed oversight structure at [[shipilovka|Shipilovka]]. The detail reinforces [[sofron|Sofron]]'s characterization as a petty tyrant who enjoys the perquisites of his position, including the freedom to indulge himself at the local tavern while the estate runs itself.
