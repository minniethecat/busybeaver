## Overview

Kostomarovo is a town in [[orel-province|Orel Province]] mentioned in Ivan Turgenev's "[[yermolai|Yermolaï]] and the Miller's Wife" (Chapter 2 of *A Sportsman's Sketches*). Its sole appearance in the sketches is in a short exchange of dialogue.

When the [[the-miller|miller]]'s wife [[arina-timofeyevna|Arina]] asks [[yermolai|Yermolaï]] who his companion is, [[yermolai|Yermolaï]] answers:

> A gentleman from Kostomarovo. (002-217)

The town is invoked as a cover identity for the narrator, and nothing further is said of it.

## Description

Kostomarovo is not described in the sketches. The name appears to be derived from *kost'* (bone) and the common Russian place-name suffix *-ovo*, though its exact etymology is uncertain.

It is presumably a small provincial town or possibly a gentleman's estate, sufficiently plausible as an identity for the narrator to pass unchallenged.

## Role in the Stories

The mention of Kostomarovo occurs during a tense nocturnal scene. [[yermolai|Yermolaï]] and the narrator have taken shelter near a mill and are burning a fire. The [[the-miller|miller]]'s wife [[arina-timofeyevna|Arina]], whom [[yermolai|Yermolaï]] apparently knows, approaches and asks him, "Who is it you're with?" (002-216). [[yermolai|Yermolaï]]'s reply is a minor deception: the narrator is not from Kostomarovo at all.

The exchange is significant for what it conceals rather than what it reveals. [[arina-timofeyevna|Arina]]'s story — she was a serving-girl in a noble household, fell in love, was married off to the [[the-miller|miller]] as punishment — is one of the most poignant in the sketches.

This fleeting reference exemplifies how Turgenev uses place names in the sketches. Some are lavishly described, others appear as passing fragments, but each contributes to the dense geographic texture of the Orel-Kaluga countryside.
