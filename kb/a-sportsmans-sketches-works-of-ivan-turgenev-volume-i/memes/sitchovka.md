## Overview

Sitchovka is a settlement near [[fair-springs|Fair Springs]] in [[orel-province|Orel Province]], mentioned in Ivan Turgenev's "[[kassyan|Kassyan of Fair Springs]]" (Chapter 9 of *A Sportsman's Sketches*). It is the place where the [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] [[kassyan|Kassyan]] says he once lived as the narrator's neighbor, establishing a prior connection between the two men that deepens the chapter's exploration of belonging and displacement.

## Description

Sitchovka is not physically described in the sketches; it is evoked only through [[kassyan|Kassyan]]'s brief remark. When the narrator asks [[kassyan|Kassyan]] how long he has known him, [[kassyan|Kassyan]] replies: "A long while. I was his neighbour at Sitchovka up at [[fair-springs|Fair Springs]]" (009-636). The phrase "up at [[fair-springs|Fair Springs]]" suggests Sitchovka lies at some elevation or distance from the spring itself, perhaps higher in the surrounding countryside.

The name Sitchovka follows the common Russian village pattern of surnames or personal names converted to place-names with the *-ovka* suffix. Like many of the minor settlements in the sketches, it exists in the narrative primarily as a point of human connection rather than a described location.

## Role in the Stories

Sitchovka's significance in "[[kassyan|Kassyan of Fair Springs]]" is relational rather than descriptive. [[kassyan|Kassyan]]'s mention of having been the narrator's neighbor at Sitchovka establishes that the two men share a history — however thin — before their encounter in the forest. This prior connection adds a layer of familiarity to their subsequent journey together through the countryside.

The reference also hints at the mobility, or lack thereof, of [[russian-peasantry|peasant]]s like [[kassyan|Kassyan]]. He no longer lives at Sitchovka but at [[fair-springs|Fair Springs]], suggesting a life of displacement. [[kassyan|Kassyan]] is a restless, otherworldly figure — a self-taught healer, a reader of scripture, a man who "does not sit still." His move from Sitchovka to [[fair-springs|Fair Springs]] is one more marker of his unsettled, wandering existence.
