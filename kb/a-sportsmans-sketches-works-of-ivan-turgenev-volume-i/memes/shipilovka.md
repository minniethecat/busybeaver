# Shipilovka

## Overview

Shipilovka is the country estate of [[Arkady Pavlitch Pyenotchkin]] in Turgenev's sketch "[[The Agent]]." Outwardly a model of enlightened estate management — with its French-designed house, English-dressed servants, and capital dinners — Shipilovka conceals a regime of systematic exploitation beneath the benevolent supervision of the agent [[Sofron]] Yakovlitch. It stands as one of the collection's most pointed illustrations of the gap between the liberal [[landowning-gentry|gentry]]'s self-image and the reality of serfdom.

## Description

Shipilovka is introduced through [[arkady-pyenotchkin|Pyenotchkin]]'s own words when the narrator announces his intention to shoot at [[Ryabovo]]. [[arkady-pyenotchkin|Pyenotchkin]] reveals that [[ryabovo|Ryabovo]] is "only four miles from my village Shipilovka, and it's a long while since I've been over to Shipilovka; I've never been able to get the time" (010-101). He extends an invitation tinged with self-deprecation: "You have never been in Shipilovka? I should be ashamed to suggest your putting up for the night in my agent's cottage" (010-108). This seemingly casual remark is the first hint that something about the estate's management is amiss.

The party arrives at Shipilovka instead of [[ryabovo|Ryabovo]] — "it happened so somehow" (010-143) — and the estate's facade quickly comes into focus. [[arkady-pyenotchkin|Pyenotchkin]] boasts that "since [[sofron|Sofron]] had had the management of the place, the Shipilovka [[russian-peasantry|peasant]]s had never been one farthing in arrears" (010-294), presenting the agent's iron discipline as administrative excellence.

## Role in the Stories

Shipilovka is the setting where the chapter's critique of the liberal [[landowning-gentry|landowner]] takes shape. The narrator spends the night there, observing [[sofron|Sofron]]'s obsequious efficiency and the atmosphere of fear it creates. The full truth emerges later at [[ryabovo|Ryabovo]], when [[Anpadist]] tells the narrator: "Why, Shipilovka's hardly reckoned as — what's his name? — Mr. [[arkady-pyenotchkin|Pyenotchkin]]'s at all; he's not the master there; [[sofron|Sofron]]'s the master" (010-453). This verdict transforms Shipilovka from a model estate into a cautionary tale: the absentee liberal [[landowning-gentry|landowner]] who delegates authority to an unscrupulous agent becomes complicit in the very oppression he claims to deplore.
