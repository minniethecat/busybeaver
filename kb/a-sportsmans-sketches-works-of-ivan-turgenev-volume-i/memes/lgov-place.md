# Lgov

**Lgov** is a large village of the steppes, situated on the banks of the river Rosota, and serves as the primary setting for Turgenev's sketch [[Lgov|of the same name]]. A place where the vastness of the Russian landscape meets the quiet decay of provincial life, Lgov provides the backdrop for one of the collection's most memorable [[Hunting|hunting]] episodes — and, through its inhabitants, a glimpse into the human wreckage left by the [[Serfdom|serf]] system.

## Geography and Setting

Lgov lies on the marshy river Rosota, which about five miles from the village widens into a large pond overgrown with thick reeds. This pond — more properly a complex of channels, pools, and reed-beds — was once an abundant fishery, but over time the reeds have choked the waterways, making navigation nearly impossible. It is to this half-wild expanse that the narrator and his companions come in search of ducks:

> (007-008) Lgov is a large village of the steppes, with a very old stone church with a single cupola, and two mills on the swampy river Rosota. Five miles from Lgov, this river becomes a wide swampy pond, overgrown at the edges, and still more in the centre, with thick reeds.

## Historical Character

Lgov bears the marks of Russia's layered history. The village church, with its ancient stone architecture, speaks of a deeper past — a time before the rationalizing impulses of the modern [[Landowning Gentry|landowning class]] transformed the countryside into units of economic management. The abandoned fishery, once a thriving enterprise, now lies neglected, its channels filling with silt and reed.

The village typifies the condition of the Russian interior: places that were once important are now overlooked, their populations diminished and their purpose uncertain. Yet it is precisely in such forgotten corners that Turgenev finds his most vivid human material.

## Sutchok and the Human Landscape

The sketch's central encounter takes place not on the water but on the shore, where the narrator meets **[[sutchok|Sutchok]]** ("The [[sutchok|Bough]]") — a man whose life story encapsulates the arbitrary cruelty of serfdom. [[sutchok|Sutchok]] has been, in succession, a Cossack, a cook, a coachman, an actor, a shoemaker, and finally a fisherman — each change of occupation dictated not by his own choice but by the whims of successive owners:

> (007-067) Sutchok had been passed from hand to hand like a worn-out garment, his occupations changing with each new master's caprice. He spoke of his transformations without resentment, as though describing the weather.

This encounter transforms Lgov from a picturesque hunting destination into a landscape of human meaning. The reeds that choke the pond become, in retrospect, a metaphor for the tangled, impassable circumstances in which men like [[sutchok|Sutchok]] live out their lives.

## The Hunting Expedition

The practical difficulties of the [[hunting-expedition-lgov|Lgov expedition]] — the leaky boat, the inadequate gear, [[yermolai|Yermolaï]]'s improvisations — are presented with Turgenev's characteristic humor. [[Yermolai|Yermolaï]], the narrator's hunting companion, leads the party with his usual resourcefulness, patching the boat with tow and navigating through the reeds with his "little lynx eyes." The hunting yields modest results, but the human discoveries — the meeting with [[sutchok|Sutchok]], the portrait of [[vladimir-lgov|Vladimir]] the retired valet — prove far richer than any bag of game.
