## Overview

Agrafena is the poultry-woman on the estate in "[[The Counting-House]]."

When the narrator seeks a samovar, a servant directs him to "Agrafena, the poultry-woman." (011-081)

Her inclusion alongside Father Timofey and [[Nazar Tarasitch]] indicates she was a recognized contact in the estate's domestic network.

## Appearance and Character

No physical description of Agrafena is given in the text. Her title — "the poultry-woman" — places her among the specialized domestic servants, responsible for chickens, ducks, and other fowl.

Such positions, while low in the formal hierarchy, often carried practical authority, with access to food stores and kitchen supplies.

## Role in the Story

Agrafena appears only as a name in a list. The narrator, arriving at the estate office of [[Nikolai Eremyev]], finds a young man dozing and asks for a samovar.

The young man replies: "Samovars, of course. Go to Father Timofey's, or to the servants' cottage, or else to [[nazar-tarasitch|Nazar Tarasitch]], or to Agrafena, the poultry-woman" (011-079–011-081).

Before the narrator can follow up, a voice shouts from the next room: "Who are you talking to, you blockhead? Can't you let me sleep, dummy!" (011-082). Agrafena remains one node in the web of servants populating the Russian country estate.
