## Overview

Gavrila Antonitch is a grain merchant who appears in "[[the-counting-house|The Counting-House]]," conducting business with [[nikolai-eremyev|Nikolai Eremyev]], head-clerk of [[elena-nikolaevna-losnyakov|Madame Losnyakov]]'s estate at [[ananyevo|Ananyevo]]. The narrator, pretending to sleep, overhears their negotiation through the office partition. (011-232)

## Appearance and Character

Gavrila Antonitch is described as a man of about forty with distinctive physical traits:

> Facing him sat a merchant, a man about forty, lean and pale, who looked as if he had been rubbed with oil. He was incessantly fingering his beard, and very rapidly blinking and twitching his lips. (011-254)

His nervous mannerisms — the blinking, the twitching lips — suggest a man accustomed to the tense, ingratiating rhythms of bargaining with men who hold power over him. (011-255)

## Role in the Story

### The Grain Negotiation

Gavrila Antonitch comes to negotiate grain prices with [[nikolai-eremyev|Nikolai Eremyev]]. The scene is a compact drama of economic corruption under serfdom.

[[nikolai-eremyev|Nikolai Eremyev]] opens at "eight roubles a measure." (011-247) [[gavrila-the-carpenter|Gavrila]] protests: "[[nikolai-eremyev|Nikolai Eremyitch]], sir, you ask a heavy price." (011-249)

The bargaining unfolds through several rounds. [[gavrila-the-carpenter|Gavrila]] tries various tactics — praising the young crops (011-257), invoking God's will (011-263), eventually offering a split arrangement: part payment to Nikolai personally (the "favour") and part as the official transaction price. (011-273)

The deal settles at "three and a half" grey notes for Nikolai's share, with six and a half as the recorded price. (011-279) When [[gavrila-the-carpenter|Gavrila]] briefly threatens to deal with the lady directly, Nikolai calls his bluff and the merchant backs down: "I lost my temper for a minute! That was nothing but talk." (011-286)

[[gavrila-the-carpenter|Gavrila]] instructs Nikolai on the cover story: tell the lady that "[[nikolai-eremyev|Nikolai Eremyitch]] has made a bargain with me for six and a half." (011-300) Nikolai confirms: "That's what you must say, Gavrila Antonitch." (011-302) The merchant hands over a roll of notes — the bribe — and departs. (011-304)

### Significance

The episode is one of Turgenev's most economical depictions of corruption in the serf economy. The overheard dialogue reveals estate officials extracting personal payment from those who must do business with the estate. [[gavrila-the-carpenter|Gavrila]] is both victim and participant: he pays because he must, but his practised manner suggests a routine transaction.
