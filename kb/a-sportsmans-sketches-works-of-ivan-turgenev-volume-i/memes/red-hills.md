## Overview

Red Hills is a village in [[orel-province|Orel Province]] mentioned in Ivan Turgenev's "[[byezhin-prairie-place|Byezhin Prairie]]" (Chapter 8 of *A Sportsman's Sketches*). It appears in the ghost stories told by the [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] boys around the night fire, as the home of one of the several local boys named Ivashka.

## Description

Red Hills is not physically described in the sketches. It is known only as the place of origin of "the other Ivashka who comes from the Red Hills" (008-247).

The village belongs to the constellation of small settlements — [[mihyevska|Mihyevska]], Suhorukov, Red Hills — that make up the local geography of the [[byezhin-prairie-place|Byezhin Prairie]] region.

## Role in the Stories

Red Hills appears in [[ilyusha|Ilyusha]]'s story about the boys who spent a night at the paper-mill. He lists the companions:

> I and my brother Avdushka, with Fyodor of Mihyevska, and Ivashka the Squint-eyed, and the other Ivashka who comes from the Red Hills, and Ivashka of Suhorukov too — and there were some other boys there as well — there were ten of us boys altogether — the whole shift, that is. (008-245–248)

The reference to Red Hills functions as a local identifier, distinguishing one Ivashka from another. The casual way the boys name their villages reflects the deep familiarity of [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] children with the web of settlements surrounding their own.
