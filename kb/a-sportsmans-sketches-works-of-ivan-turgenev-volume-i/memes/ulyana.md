## Overview

Ulyana is an old woman from the village near [[Byezhin Prairie]], mentioned in the boys' fireside conversation about local superstitions. Her story — how she saw her own apparition on the church porch on All Hallows' Eve and died that same year — is told by [[ilyusha|Ilyusha]] as evidence that one can see not only the dead but also the living fated to die (008-442, 008-450, 008-453).

## Role in the Story

Ulyana does not appear directly in "[[byezhin-prairie-place|Byezhin Prairie]]"; she is a figure in the boys' supernatural lore. [[Ilyusha]] recounts that on All Hallows' Day, you can see the living whose turn it is to die that year by sitting in the church porch and watching the road (008-439, 008-441). Last year, old Ulyana did this: at first she sat a long while and saw nothing, only hearing what seemed like a dog whining somewhere (008-444, 008-446). Then she saw a boy in only a shirt — Ivashka Fedosyev, who died that spring (008-447, 008-450). Looking again, she saw a woman approaching — and realized with horror that it was herself (008-452, 008-453). Ulyana died that year, confirming the omen.

Her brief role in the narrative exemplifies the dense folk-belief world of the [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] children, where omens, apparitions, and fatalism interweave with everyday village life.
