## Overview

Parahin is a geographic landmark in the [[byezhin-prairie-place|Byezhin Prairie]] region, mentioned when the lost narrator recognises its distinctive bushes and uses them to orient himself.

## Description

Parahin appears at a critical moment in "[[byezhin-prairie-place|Byezhin Prairie]]," when the narrator, hopelessly lost in the dark, suddenly recognises his surroundings. "Ah! but these are the bushes at Parahin," he cries; "of course! then this must be Sindyev wood" (008-002). The recognition of the bushes at Parahin allows him to reorient himself and eventually find his way to the boys' campfire. Parahin thus functions as a named waypoint in the rural landscape—sufficiently familiar to the hunter-narrator that its vegetation alone serves as a landmark.

## Role in the Stories

Parahin serves a dual narrative function. On the level of plot, it provides the geographic clue that rescues the narrator from being lost overnight. On the level of atmosphere, the name—briefly mentioned and never explained—contributes to the dense texture of real-seeming place names that give the *Sportsman's Sketches* their documentary feel. Like [[sindyev-wood|Sindyev Wood]], Parahin is a place the narrator knows from his hunting excursions, part of the intimate geography of the [[hunting-in-the-sketches|sport]]sman's world.
