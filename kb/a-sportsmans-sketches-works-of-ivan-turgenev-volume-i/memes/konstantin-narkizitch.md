## Overview

Konstantin Narkizitch (also referred to as Konstantin Narkizer) is one of six clerks in the counting-house office of Madame [[Elena Nikolaevna Losnyakov]]'s estate at [[ananyevo|Ananyevo]]. He is distinguished by his skill in composing formal orders and by his brief but revealing dialogue with [[Kuprya Afanasyitch]]. (011-155)

## Role in the Office

The young clerk Fedyushka lists Konstantin among the office staff and credits him as "the great one" for composing official orders. (011-192) The elaborate bureaucratic style of the office's directives — with formal salutations, legalistic phrasing, and the mistress's endorsement "To be executed exactly, Elena Losnyakov" — reflects the kind of document Konstantin composes. (011-173)

## In the Narrative

### Dialogue with Kuprya

Konstantin's only direct appearance occurs in dialogue with [[kuprya|Kuprya Afanasyitch]], the disgraced former chief cashier. [[kuprya|Kuprya]], gaunt and broken, encounters Konstantin and attempts to justify himself: "Ah, well, what could one do! Konstantin Narkizitch." (011-419)

[[kuprya|Kuprya]] appeals: "I have, Konstantin Narkizitch, before you blame me!" (011-421) But Konstantin is curt: "No, you must not say that, Konstantin Narkizitch." (011-423)

This exchange reveals the office hierarchy in action. [[kuprya|Kuprya]]'s use of the respectful patronymic "Konstantin Narkizitch" while addressing a subordinate underscores his fallen status. Konstantin's rebuff — mirroring the form of address back at him — suggests the coldness of office politics.
