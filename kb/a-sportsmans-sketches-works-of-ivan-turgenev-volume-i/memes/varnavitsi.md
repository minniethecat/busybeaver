# Varnavitsi

## Overview

Varnavitsi is a location near the dam in Turgenev's *[[Byezhin Prairie]]*, introduced through the fireside storytelling of the [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] boy [[ilyusha|Ilyusha]]. Unlike the other places in the *[[A Sportsman's Sketches, Volume I|Sketches]]*, which are defined by geography and social life, Varnavitsi belongs to the realm of folklore and the supernatural — a place whose significance lies not in its physical features but in the stories told about it. It is the most vividly imagined of the story's haunted sites, the scene of repeated ghostly visitations that the boys recount with a mixture of fear and conviction.

## Description

Varnavitsi enters the narrative abruptly, in the middle of the boys' exchange of supernatural tales. "Have you heard, boys," begins [[ilyusha|Ilyusha]], "what happened with us at Varnavitsi?" (008-353). The question immediately establishes the place as part of the boys' shared world — "with us" signals that Varnavitsi is local, familiar, within the geography of their daily lives. When [[fedya|Fedya]] asks, "Near the dam?" (008-355), he confirms his own knowledge of the place and the story about to be told. The dam is the landmark that anchors Varnavitsi in physical space, a recognizable feature of the rural landscape.

The story that follows is one of supernatural dread. [[ilyusha|Ilyusha]] explains that Varnavitsi is haunted: "I should think it was haunted! More than once, they say, they have seen the old master there — the late master. He wears a long-skirted coat, and is always groaning, and looking for something on the ground" (008-419). The ghost of the old master is restless, searching the earth for a lost object — a detail that suggests unfinished business, a sin or debt that ties the dead [[landowning-gentry|landowner]] to the soil of Varnavitsi. The repetition of "more than once" underscores the persistence of the haunting: this is not a single, possibly mistaken sighting but a recurring phenomenon attested by multiple witnesses.

## Role in the Stories

Varnavitsi functions in [[Byezhin Prairie]] as a node in the geography of [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] superstition. The dam near Varnavitsi is where the old master's ghost walks, just as other sites in the story are associated with drowned men, rusalkas, and the voices of the dead. The haunting of Varnavitsi serves a thematic purpose: the dead master, a figure of authority in life, remains bound to the land even in death, groaning and searching — a ghostly echo of the serf-owning class that cannot quite let go. The boys' matter-of-fact acceptance of the haunting ("I should think it was haunted!") reveals a [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] cosmology in which the natural and supernatural are continuous, and places carry the moral weight of the events that occurred there.
