# Akim Nahimov

## Overview

Akim Nahimov (1782–1814) is a Russian poet and satirist mentioned in "[[Khor and Kalinich]]," the opening sketch of [[A Sportsman's Sketches]]. He is invoked by the narrator to characterise the literary taste of [[Polutikin]], a minor [[landowning-gentry|landowner]].

[[polutikin|Polutikin]]'s conversation "had certainly never amused anyone" despite his own appreciation of its merits (001-040). He "admired the works of Akim Nahimov and the novel 'Pinna'" (001-041). The reference identifies Nahimov as a real historical figure whose reputation had faded by the 1850s. Nahimov never appears onstage.

## Appearance and Character

Akim Nahimov belonged to the generation of Russian writers who flourished in the early years of Alexander I's reign. He was associated with the [[harkoff|Kharkov]] literary circle and wrote satirical verse, fables, and epigrams in the eighteenth-century Enlightenment tradition.

His works attacked bribery, ignorance, and aristocratic vanity with moral earnestness. They lacked the literary distinction of his contemporaries Derzhavin and Krylov, and his reputation did not survive his generation. This obscurity is precisely what makes him useful to Turgenev: by 1847, a man who still read Nahimov was a man whose literary judgment had fossilised decades earlier.

Turgenev follows the Nahimov reference immediately with other markers of [[polutikin|Polutikin]]'s absurdity: "he stammered; he called his dog Astronomer; instead of 'however' said 'howsomever'" (001-042–001-043). The poet and the dog are listed side by side, items in the same inventory of provincial eccentricity.

## Role in the Story

Nahimov functions in "[[Khor and Kalinich]]" to define [[Polutikin]] by what he admires. The sketch devotes only two sentences to [[polutikin|Polutikin]]'s literary tastes before moving on to [[Khor]] and [[Kalinitch]].

The method is characteristic of Turgenev: a single detail, precisely chosen, can sketch an entire cultural position. [[polutikin|Polutikin]] admires Nahimov and "Pinna"; therefore he is a man of limited intelligence, derivative opinions, and a cultural horizon that does not extend beyond his provincial club.

In the broader context of the Sketches, Nahimov belongs to a group of real historical figures — alongside [[Alexey Grigoryevitch Orlov-Tchesmensky]] — whose names anchor the narrative in actual literary and social history. The gesture is modest: Turgenev refuses the boundary between reportage and invention.
