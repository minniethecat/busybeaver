# Goloplek

## Overview

Goloplek is a village named a single time in *A Sportsman's Sketches*, in the eleventh sketch, [[the-counting-house|THE COUNTING-HOUSE]]. It appears only as the home of a [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] who arrives at the manor office while the narrator is resting there.

## Description

The name is dropped in the course of the counting-house's daily business. As the head steward attends to his affairs, "the clerk on duty came in" to announce a caller: "Sidor is here from Goloplek" (011-322). The steward tells him to "ask him in," but first checks "whether the strange gentleman's still asleep" — the strange gentleman being the [[The Narrator|narrator]], who is feigning sleep to overhear the office at work.

Goloplek is identified only as the place this [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] Sidor comes from, one of the villages under the estate whose people must present themselves before the steward.

## Role in the Stories

The village has no part beyond fixing the origin of a minor caller. Yet the passing mention does quiet work: it shows the reach of the counting-house's authority, extending out to villages like Goloplek whose [[russian-peasantry|peasant]]s come in to settle their business.

Together with [[Tyutyurov]] and [[Mihyevska]], Goloplek helps populate the ordinary map of estate life that surrounds the sketch's central figure, the steward [[Vassily Nikolaevitch]].
