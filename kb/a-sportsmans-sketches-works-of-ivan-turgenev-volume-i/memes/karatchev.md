## Overview

Karatchev (Karachev) is a town mentioned in passing in "[[lgov-place|Lgov]]." It is referenced as the place where a certain [[tatyana-vassilyevna|Tatyana Vassilyevna]], an old maid, died—a correction offered mid-conversation during the hunting expedition at [[lgov-place|Lgov]].

## Description

Karatchev appears in a fleeting exchange during the [[hunting-expedition-lgov|Lgov duck-hunt]]ing episode. When the subject of [[tatyana-vassilyevna|Tatyana Vassilyevna]] arises, one character says she died "in Bolhov," then immediately corrects himself: "that is, at Karatchev." The parenthetical correction suggests Karatchev as a smaller or less familiar locality near [[bolhovo|Bolhovo]], requiring clarification for the listener.

The town is a real settlement in what is now Bryansk Oblast, lending geographic authenticity to Turgenev's fictionalized Orel-Kaluga landscape.

## Role in the Stories

Karatchev functions solely as a geographic reference point in casual dialogue. Its mention alongside [[bolhovo|Bolhovo]] places it within the constellation of provincial towns that form the backdrop of the *Sportsman's Sketches*. Though the town itself is never described or visited, its name contributes to the dense web of real and fictional toponyms that ground the stories in the Russian countryside.
