## Overview

Sitoia is an estate or settlement in [[orel-province|Orel Province]], mentioned in "[[raspberry-spring-place|Raspberry Spring]]." It is referenced as the place where a watchman served, whose daughter Akulina became entangled with the local count.

## Description

Sitoia appears in the recollections of [[mihailo-savelyev-tuman|Tuman]], the old house-serf, as he tells the story of the count's household. He describes Akulina as "the daughter of the watchman at Sitoia; and such a vixen! She would slap the count's face sometimes. She simply bewitched him" (003-002). Sitoia, therefore, was one of the count's properties, with its own watchman—suggesting it was a substantial estate or manor house requiring dedicated staff.

## Role in the Stories

Sitoia forms part of the backdrop to [[mihailo-savelyev-tuman|Tuman]]'s reminiscences about the old count's eccentric household. The watchman's daughter Akulina represents one of the many lives caught up in the orbit of the landowning [[landowning-gentry|gentry]]. Her boldness—slapping the count's face—is presented as both scandalous and emblematic of the disordered relations between master and serf under the old regime.
