# Gniloterka

## Overview

The Gniloterka is a meandering river in the [[orel-province|Orel Province]] landscape of Turgenev's *A Sportsman's Sketches*. It appears in "[[The Peasant Proprietor Ovsyanikov]]" in a vivid historical digression: during Napoleon's retreat from [[moscow|Moscow]] in 1812, a party of [[smolensk|Smolensk]] [[russian-peasantry|peasant]]s attempted to drown the captured French drummer [[Frantz Ivanitch Lejeune]] "under water along the course of the meandering river Gniloterka" (006-575). The river thus serves as the backdrop for one of the *Sketches*' most dramatic anecdotes — a story of near-death, rescue, and the improbable path to assimilation in provincial Russia.

## Description

The Gniloterka enters the narrative through the rambling reminiscences of [[Tuman]], the former steward of a deceased count, who tells the story of Lejeune to the narrator at [[Raspberry Spring]]. According to [[mihailo-savelyev-tuman|Tuman]], Lejeune — a drummer in Napoleon's Grande Armée — was "captured by some [[smolensk|Smolensk]] [[russian-peasantry|peasant]]s" and brought to an ice-hole with the intent of drowning him (006-575). The [[russian-peasantry|peasant]]s, "doubtless through their ignorance of the geographical position of [[orleans|Orleans]], continued to offer him a journey under water along the course of the meandering river Gniloterka" (006-575). The river's epithet — "meandering" — is significant: it suggests a slow, winding watercourse, characteristic of the Orel lowlands, whose frozen surface in winter became an instrument of rough [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] justice.

The rescue comes when a sledge carrying "a stout and red-faced [[landowning-gentry|landowner]] in a wolfskin pelisse" arrives and intervenes, asking the [[russian-peasantry|peasant]]s "'What is it you are doing there?'" (006-575). This [[landowning-gentry|landowner]], learning of Lejeune's musical ability — "'Do you know music?'" — takes the Frenchman into his household, launching Lejeune's improbable Russian career as a music teacher and eventual nobleman.

## Role in the Stories

The Gniloterka episode serves multiple narrative functions. It provides a moment of grim comedy — French eloquence about a *tendre mère* in [[orleans|Orleans]] colliding with the practical drowning plans of [[smolensk|Smolensk]] [[russian-peasantry|peasant]]s. It also dramatizes the precariousness of life during the Napoleonic invasion and the arbitrary nature of survival: Lejeune is saved not by justice but by a passing [[landowning-gentry|landowner]]'s curiosity. Finally, the river itself — unnamed elsewhere in the *Sketches*, remembered only in [[mihailo-savelyev-tuman|Tuman]]'s tale — exemplifies how the landscape of [[orel-province|Orel Province]] is saturated with buried stories. The "meandering" Gniloterka, winding through the countryside, carries the memory of a moment when a French drummer nearly vanished beneath its ice.
