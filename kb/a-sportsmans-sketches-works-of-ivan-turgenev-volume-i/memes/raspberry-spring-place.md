## Overview

Raspberry Spring (Malinovaya Voda) is a natural spring on [[ista-river|the Ista]] River in [[orel-province|Orel Province]], famous enough in its neighbourhood to give its name to the third chapter of the *Sportsman's Sketches*. It is the setting for the narrator's encounter with the old house-serf [[mihailo-savelyev-tuman|Tuman]] and the mysterious [[styopushka|Styopushka]].

## Description

Turgenev provides a precise, painterly description of Raspberry Spring. Reaching [[ista-river|the Ista]] River on a stifling summer day, the narrator descends the steep bank and follows the damp yellow sand toward "the spring, known to the whole neighbourhood as Raspberry Spring" (003-001). The spring gushes from a cleft in the bank, widening gradually into a small but deep creek that falls, twenty paces on, with a "merry babbling sound" into the river. The scene is rendered with sensory richness: short velvety grass grows green around the source, while the sun's rays "scarcely ever reach its cold, silvery water." A birch-wood cup, left by a passing [[russian-peasantry|peasant]], lies on the grass beside the spring—a small, human detail that completes the pastoral picture.

## Role in the Stories

Raspberry Spring serves as the narrative frame for the entire third chapter. The narrator comes to the spring to escape the oppressive heat, and it is there that he encounters two figures from the old world of serfdom: [[styopushka|Styopushka]], a rootless man of uncertain origin who lives in the gardens nearby, and [[mihailo-savelyev-tuman|Tuman]] (Mikhailo Savelyev), a freed house-serf who once served a wealthy count. The babbling of the spring and the cool shade of the bank form the backdrop for [[mihailo-savelyev-tuman|Tuman]]'s extended reminiscences about the count's lavish household, the capricious mistress, the watchman's daughter Akulina, and the vanished world of the eighteenth-century Russian [[landowning-gentry|gentry]]. The spring's tranquil permanence contrasts with the transience of the human lives discussed beside it.
