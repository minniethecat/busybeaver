# Fair Springs

## Overview

Fair Springs (in Russian, Krasivaya Mech — literally "Beautiful Springs") is the rural settlement that gives Turgenev's sketch "[[Kassyan of Fair Springs]]" its title. Located in the countryside of central Russia, it is the home of [[Kassyan]], the enigmatic [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] visionary who guides the narrator through the woods and articulates a mystical reverence for the natural world. The name itself — "Fair Springs" — embodies the chapter's central theme: the beauty and sanctity of uncorrupted nature.

## Description

Fair Springs is introduced when the narrator asks [[kassyan|Kassyan]] where he lives. [[kassyan|Kassyan]] answers simply: "'At Fair Springs'" (009-467), and when pressed whether it is far, confirms the short distance (009-468). Later, [[kassyan|Kassyan]] speaks of the place with pride and longing: "There at home, at Fair Springs, you could get up on to a hill — and ah, my God, what a sight! The river, the meadows, the forest — and there is a church, and beyond, more meadows stretching far away" (009-473). This description reveals Fair Springs not merely as a place-name but as a vantage point — a hill from which one sees the sweep of the Russian landscape, river and forest and meadow in a single view.

[[Annushka]], the young girl who appears in the woods gathering mushrooms, is also connected to Fair Springs. The narrator later learns she is [[kassyan|Kassyan]]'s relative, and when asking about her, [[kassyan|Kassyan]] confirms: "'I was his neighbour at [[sitchovka|Sitchovka]] up at Fair Springs'" (009-636), referring to [[annushka|Annushka]]'s father.

## Role in the Stories

Fair Springs is more than a geographical marker — it is the spiritual center of the chapter that bears its name. [[kassyan|Kassyan]]'s connection to the place defines his character: he is a man rooted in the land, not in the serf-owning estate system. The springs themselves, though never directly described as water features, function as a symbolic source — of beauty, of freedom, of the natural order that [[kassyan|Kassyan]] reveres and the narrator, a hunter from the [[landowning-gentry|gentry]], can only glimpse.
