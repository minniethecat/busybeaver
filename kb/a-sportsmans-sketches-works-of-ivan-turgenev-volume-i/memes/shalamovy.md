## Overview

Shalamovy is a settlement in the [[byezhin-prairie-place|Byezhin Prairie]] area, referenced in the boys' nighttime conversation. It is mentioned as the place where the "heavenly portent"—a solar eclipse—was seen.

## Description

In the course of the campfire conversation among the [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] boys in "[[byezhin-prairie-place|Byezhin Prairie]]," [[fedya|Fedya]] turns to [[pavlusha|Pavlusha]] and asks: "what was seen in your parts at Shalamovy at the heavenly portent?" (008-004). The question refers to a solar eclipse noted in an authorial footnote as "what the [[russian-peasantry|peasant]]s call an eclipse." Shalamovy appears to be a village or settlement in [[pavlusha|Pavlusha]]'s home region, distinct enough to be named as the specific location where the astronomical event was observed.

## Role in the Stories

Shalamovy serves as a geographic anchor in the boys' supernatural storytelling. The mention of a real village name within the context of folk beliefs about the eclipse lends authenticity to the rustic dialogue. It is one of several place names that the boys drop into their conversation, collectively mapping the rural world they inhabit.
