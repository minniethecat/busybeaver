## Overview

Vassily Semenitch was a minor [[landowning-gentry|landowner]] in the Orel province, mentioned in "[[Lgov]]" as the original owner of the serfs who later passed to his unmarried daughter [[Tatyana Vassilyevna]]. His existence is recorded only through the rambling autobiography of [[Sutchok]], the aged serf fisherman who traces his ownership through a succession of masters. Vassily Semenitch represents the countless obscure provincial [[landowning-gentry|landowner]]s whose names survive in the *Sketches* only because their former serfs remember to whom they once belonged.

## Appearance and Character

No physical description of Vassily Semenitch survives in the text. He appears only as a name in a genealogical chain of ownership, reconstructed by [[sutchok|Sutchok]] under the narrator's questioning. The brevity of his mention — "her father, Vassily Semenitch" (007-180) — itself suggests the anonymity of the minor provincial [[landowning-gentry|gentry]], whose existence is attested solely by the legal fact of serf ownership. [[sutchok|Sutchok]]'s matter-of-fact recitation of his masters' names conveys the peculiar intimacy and impersonality of the institution: the [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] knows exactly who owned him, even when that owner is otherwise forgotten.

## Role in the Story

Vassily Semenitch appears in "[[lgov-place|Lgov]]" during the narrator's conversation with [[sutchok|Sutchok]], the fisherman. When the narrator asks from whom [[sutchok|Sutchok]]'s previous owner bought the village, [[sutchok|Sutchok]] replies: "From [[tatyana-vassilyevna|Tatyana Vassilyevna]]" (007-176). Pressed to identify her, [[sutchok|Sutchok]] explains she was an old maid who died in Bolhov — or rather at [[karatchev|Karatchev]] — and adds: "We came to her from her father, Vassily Semenitch. She owned us a goodish while ... twenty years" (007-179).

This brief exchange reveals the pattern of serf inheritance through the female line. Vassily Semenitch's daughter never married, and the serfs passed with her as property. After her death, the village of [[lgov-place|Lgov]] passed through several more owners — a master, a steward, a manager, and finally a "master again" — before [[sutchok|Sutchok]], now "old and good for nothing" (007-188), was dismissed from household service and sent to the remote lake as a fisherman.

Vassily Semenitch's sole narrative function is as an origin point in this chain of ownership, yet the very mention of his name illustrates the world of the *Sketches*: a world in which human beings are transferred between owners like chattel, and the only record of a man's existence is the memory of the serf he once possessed.

> 'From Tatyana Vassilyevna.' 'What Tatyana Vassilyevna was that?' 'Why, that died last year in Bolhov ... that is, at Karatchev, an old maid.... She had never married. Don't you know her? We came to her from her father, Vassily Semenitch.' (007-176)
