# Sitovka

## Overview

Sitovka is a village mentioned in Turgenev's "[[The Counting-House]]" as a waypoint on the rural roads of [[orel-province|Orel Province]]. The narrator, lost while searching for [[Ananyevo]], encounters an old man who gives him directions: "There will be [[ananyevo|Ananyevo]]. Or else you'd go to Sitovka" (011-030). Though the narrator ultimately reaches [[ananyevo|Ananyevo]], Sitovka represents an alternative destination on the same path — one of the many small settlements that dotted the province and formed the backdrop of the *Sketches*.

## Description

Sitovka enters the narrative solely through the directions of an old [[russian-peasantry|peasant]], one of the "[[russian-peasantry|peasant]] greybeards" the narrator meets while trying to locate [[ananyevo|Ananyevo]] (011-028). The old man's instructions are characteristically vague: "you pass it by, and keep right on to the right; keep right on, keep right on, keep right on.... Well, there will be [[ananyevo|Ananyevo]]. Or else you'd go to Sitovka" (011-030). The repetition — "keep right on" — and the casual "or else" convey the informality of rural wayfinding in the pre-modern Russian countryside, where villages were navigated by word-of-mouth rather than by map. Sitovka is presented as equivalent to [[ananyevo|Ananyevo]] in the old man's mind: both are simply places one might go, distinct but interchangeable as destinations along the same road (011-030).

## Role in the Stories

Sitovka's role in the *Sketches* is minimal — a single mention in a brief exchange — yet it contributes to the texture of Turgenev's provincial world. The narrator's search for [[ananyevo|Ananyevo]] leads him through a landscape of unnamed roads, copses, and villages, and Sitovka is one of the names that surfaces in this geography. The fact that the old man names it as an alternative to [[ananyevo|Ananyevo]] suggests that Sitovka was a settlement of comparable standing — known locally, though never visited by the narrator. Like many of the thin places in the *Sketches*, Sitovka exists at the threshold between presence and absence: it is named, located, and then promptly bypassed, part of the dense but incomplete map of rural [[orel-province|Orel Province]] that Turgenev's stories sketch.
