# Simbirsk

## Overview

Simbirsk, a provincial city on the middle [[Volga]], is named a single time in *A Sportsman's Sketches* — in the litany of places recited by the wandering [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] [[Kassyan]] in [[kassyan-of-fair-springs|Kassyan of Fair Springs]]. Its epithet in [[kassyan|Kassyan]]'s mouth, "the fair city," marks it as one of the "noble cities" of his sacred imagined Russia.

## Description

Simbirsk opens [[kassyan|Kassyan]]'s catalogue of the places he has journeyed to. Having dreamed aloud of a blessed land where "apples grow of gold, on silver branches" (009-493), and asked whether he has "journeyed so little already" (009-494), [[kassyan|Kassyan]] answers himself: "I have been to [[romyon|Romyon]] and to Simbirsk the fair city, and even to [[moscow|Moscow of the golden domes]]" (009-495 to 009-496), before going on to name the rivers [[Oka]] "the good nurse" and "our mother [[Volga]]" (009-496 to 009-497). He closes the litany by declaring that "many folks, good Christians have I seen, and noble cities I have visited" (009-497 to 009-498) — Simbirsk standing among those noble cities.

## Role in the Stories

Simbirsk plays no part in the action of the *Sketches*; it survives only as a name in [[kassyan|Kassyan]]'s recital. Like the rivers named alongside it, it serves to extend the horizon of [[kassyan-of-fair-springs|Kassyan of Fair Springs]] far beyond its immediate woodland setting, and to reveal the inner life of [[kassyan|Kassyan]] — a wanderer who measures his own existence by the distant "fair" and "noble" cities of Russia he has managed to reach.

## Historical Context

Simbirsk is a real city on the right bank of the [[volga|Volga]], founded in 1648 and later renamed Ulyanovsk; in Turgenev's day it was the seat of Simbirsk Province. [[kassyan|Kassyan]]'s calling it "the fair city" (009-495) reflects its standing as one of the notable [[volga|Volga]] towns of the imperial era.
