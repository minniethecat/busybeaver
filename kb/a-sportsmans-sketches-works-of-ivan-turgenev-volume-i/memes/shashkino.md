## Overview

Shashkino is a location in [[orel-province|Orel Province]] mentioned in Ivan Turgenev's "[[byezhin-prairie-place|Byezhin Prairie]]" (Chapter 8 of *A Sportsman's Sketches*). It appears in one of the [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] boys' stories told around the night fire, as a destination on a route that passes through a walnut wood and past a little pool.

## Description

Shashkino is not directly described in the sketches; it is referenced only as a destination in [[ilyusha|Ilyusha]]'s ghost story. The boy recounts: "I was going from [[stony-ridge|Stony Ridge]] to Shashkino; I went first through our walnut wood, and then passed by a little pool — you know where there's a sharp turn down to the ravine — there is a water-pit there, you know; it is quite overgrown" (008-539).

The route [[ilyusha|Ilyusha]] describes — through the walnut wood, past the pool, by the ravine with the overgrown water-pit — evokes the dense, layered landscape of the [[byezhin-prairie-place|Byezhin Prairie]] region. Shashkino itself remains offstage, a destination never reached in the narrative, but the journey toward it frames one of the chapter's supernatural tales.

## Role in the Stories

Shashkino appears in the context of [[ilyusha|Ilyusha]]'s tale about a supernatural encounter. He was traveling to Shashkino when he saw, or thought he saw, something uncanny near the water-pit. The location thus belongs to the folkloric geography of the [[byezhin-prairie-place|Byezhin Prairie]] — a landscape where natural features (pools, ravines, woods) are invested with supernatural significance by the [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] imagination.

The mention of Shashkino also anchors [[ilyusha|Ilyusha]]'s story in real, local geography. The boys around the fire know these places: the walnut wood, the little pool, the sharp turn. By naming actual locations, [[ilyusha|Ilyusha]] lends credibility to his tale and draws his listeners deeper into the shared world of village belief.
