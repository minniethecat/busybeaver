# Lebedyan

## Overview

Lebedyan is the provincial town — in the old province of [[Tamboff|Tambov]] — that gives its name to the fourteenth sketch, [[lebedyan|Lebedyan]], and provides its single setting.

The [[The Narrator|narrator]] recalls "how I happened five years ago to be at Lebedyan just in the very thick of the horse-fair" (014-025), and the whole story unfolds inside the town: its hotel, its café, and above all the crowded market square where horses are bought and sold.

Unlike the woods and villages of most of the *Sketches*, Lebedyan is a place of commerce and society rather than of hunting, drawing the narrator into a world of horse-dealers, swaggering officers, and cheating gentleman-traders.

## Description

The narrator reaches the town in the offhand way of a [[hunting-in-the-sketches|sport]]sman whose wanderings carry him far from home: "And so I turned up at Lebedyan, stopped at the hotel, changed my clothes, and went out to the fair" (014-032).

The fair itself is the heart of the place — a noisy market square where stud-farms advertise their stock on hand-lettered signboards.

Over the gate of one little grey house hangs a pen-and-ink sketch of a horse and a notice offering "for sale horses of various colours, brought to the Lebedyan fair from the celebrated steppes stud of [[Anastasei Ivanitch Tchornobai]], [[landowning-gentry|landowner]] of Tambov" (014-324).

Would-be buyers are told to ask for Anastasei Ivanitch himself, or, in his absence, for "[[Nazar Kubishkin]], the coachman" (014-329). The town is rendered entirely through the bustle and small frauds of its fair.

## Role in the Stories

Lebedyan frames the sketch as a self-contained episode of provincial life. In its café the narrator falls in with a fashionable crowd and comes upon the billiard-playing [[prince-n|Prince N----]]; in its market he is drawn into the buying of horses and comes off slightly the worse for the lesson.

When the affair is over he leaves and then briefly returns: "Two days later I left, and in a week I was again at Lebedyan on my way home again" (014-416), finding "almost the same persons" in the café. The town is thus both the stage and the subject of the story.

## Historical Context

Lebedyan was a real district town of Tambov province (today in Lipetsk region), long celebrated for one of the largest annual horse-fairs in central Russia.

Turgenev sets his sketch against this well-known institution, using the fair as a natural gathering-point for the [[landowning-gentry|landowner]]s, officers, and dealers whose manners the story observes.
