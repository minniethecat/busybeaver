# Kostya

## Overview

Kostya is one of the five [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] boys whom the narrator encounters around a campfire in the chapter [[BYEZHIN PRAIRIE]]. [[About]] ten years old, he is distinguished by his pensive and downcast manner.

Of all the boys, Kostya asks the most questions about the supernatural stories being told, and he reacts with the greatest visible fear — shuddering, trembling, and crying out at unexplained sounds in the night. His mournful, wide-eyed curiosity embodies the rural child's immersion in a world thick with folk belief.


## Appearance and Character

Kostya is described as a boy of about ten, with a "pensive and mournful" look (008-212). Turgenev notes that his head "drooped despondently, and he looked away into the distance" (008-229). His gaze was especially striking: his large, dark, liquid eyes seemed to want to express something for which words were inadequate. He was poorly dressed, like most of the [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] boys.

His voice was "shrill" and "thin" (008-284). Kostya's defining trait is his fearful, wondering nature — he is repeatedly described as trembling, shuddering, and opening his eyes wide at the supernatural tales.


## Role in the Story

Kostya contributes several key elements to the fireside storytelling. He tells the story of [[gavrila-the-carpenter|Gavrila]] the carpenter, who encountered a russalka (water-nymph) in the forest and has been melancholy ever since (008-284--008-334). He also raises the topic of Akulina, the mad woman who threw herself into the river, and [[vasya|Vasya]], the boy who drowned (008-607--008-625).

Throughout the night, Kostya serves as the audience for the other boys' tales, asking frequent, trembling questions that drive the conversation forward: "Have you seen any wolves, then?" (008-406), "What is Trishka?" (008-487), and many others. His repeated cries of alarm — "What was that?" (008-532), "Ugh, there it is!" (008-555) — register the atmosphere of gathering dread.

When [[pavlusha|Pavlusha]] goes to the river, Kostya is the one who hears the supernatural voice calling [[pavlusha|Pavlusha]]'s name, and at the end of the chapter, he sighs and shuts his eyes (008-671).
