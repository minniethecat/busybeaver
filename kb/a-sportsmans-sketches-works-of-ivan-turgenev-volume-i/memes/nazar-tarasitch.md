## Overview

Nazar Tarasitch is a figure in the estate hierarchy of "[[The Counting-House]]."

When the narrator seeks a samovar, he is directed to "Nazar Tarasitch, or to [[Agrafena]], the poultry-woman." (011-081)

This places Tarasitch among the domestic staff involved in distributing household goods.

## Appearance and Character

No physical description of Nazar Tarasitch is provided. His patronymic "Tarasitch" is a standard Russian form of address, suggesting familiarity within the estate community.

He is listed alongside [[Agrafena]] and Father Timofey as a resource for samovars, suggesting a position in the estate's domestic economy.

## Role in the Story

Nazar Tarasitch appears in a single line of dialogue. The narrator, encountering a young man dozing in the estate office, asks for a samovar.

The young man replies: "Samovars, of course. Go to Father Timofey's, or to the servants' cottage, or else to Nazar Tarasitch, or to [[agrafena|Agrafena]], the poultry-woman" (011-079–011-081).

Before more can be said, a voice shouts from the next room: "Who are you talking to, you blockhead? Can't you let me sleep, dummy!" (011-082), ending the exchange.
