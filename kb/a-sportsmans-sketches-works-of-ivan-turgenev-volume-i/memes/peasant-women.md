# Peasant Women and Their Lot

Where [[Love and Marriage]] looks at courtship, this theme looks at what surrounded it for a serf woman: a life owned by others, in which even the right to marry or to keep a child was a favour the gentry could withhold.

The women of [[A Sportsman's Sketches]] — lady's maids, washerwomen, nurses and field hands — seldom stand at the centre of a sketch, yet their fates carry some of Turgenev's sharpest charges against [[Serfdom|serfdom]].

## Arina, the Lady's Maid Undone

In [[yermolai-and-the-millers-wife|Yermolaï and the Miller's Wife]], the narrator recognises in the miller's wife a woman once maid to Mr. [[Zvyerkoff]]'s wife in Petersburg:

> (002-262) 'Weren't you his wife's lady's maid?'

[[Arina Timofeyevna|Arina]] had asked only for leave to marry. For that, and for the child that followed, her master's verdict was swift and absolute:

> (002-346) ...her hair should be cut off, she should be dressed in sackcloth, and sent into the country.

## The Rule Against Marriage

Zvyerkoff states the household principle without a flicker of shame, as though it were mere good management:

> (002-296) But my wife has made it a rule never to keep married lady's maids.

A serving woman's usefulness belonged to her mistress; a husband or a child was an inconvenience to be prevented.

## Tatyana in the Counting-House

The same disposability shapes [[Tatyana]] in [[the-counting-house|The Counting-House]], loved by the clerk [[Pavel Andreitch|Pavel]] but simply removed once she becomes awkward for the estate:

> (011-607) ...had sent away the girl Tatyana; it appeared she was not wanted.

## Social and Historical Context

Under serfdom a woman was doubly bound: to her master as property, and to the men of her own class as dependant. Marriage, motherhood, even her hair, could be granted or taken at another's word.

Turgenev rarely moralises outright. He lets a phrase like "not wanted", or a punishment "sent into the country", carry the weight, and leaves the reader to reckon what such casual power cost the women who lived beneath it.
