## Overview

Vassily Nikolaevitch is the head cashier at the chief office of the manor of [[ananyevo|Ananyevo]], the estate of Madame [[Elena Nikolaevna Losnyakov]] in "[[the-counting-house|The Counting-House]]." He is the highest-ranking clerk among the six serf-officials who staff the office. (011-153)

## Role in the Office

The young clerk on duty, Fedyushka, lists the office staff in rank order:

> Well, first there's Vassily Nikolaevitch, the head cashier; then Piotr, one clerk; Piotr's brother, Ivan, another clerk; the other Ivan, a clerk; Konstantin Narkizer, another clerk; and me here — there's a lot of us, you can't count all of them. (011-153)

As head cashier, Vassily Nikolaevitch holds the senior financial position in the estate's administrative apparatus. The office operates under [[nikolai-eremyev|Nikolai Eremyev]] (the head-clerk) and a German manager, [[karlo-karlitch|Lindamandol]], but actual estate management rests with [[elena-nikolaevna-losnyakov|Madame Losnyakov]] herself. (011-145)

## In the Narrative

Vassily Nikolaevitch does not appear directly in the narrative. His presence is conveyed entirely through the references of other characters.

A "red head, adorned with thick whiskers" briefly appears at the office door after the grain negotiation to inquire about the transaction. (011-311) Later, [[nikolai-eremyev|Nikolai Eremyev]] mentions "Vassily Nikolaevitch" in connection with some unspecified business. (011-491) The narrator, overhearing conversations through the partition, never meets him.

The character remains an off-stage presence — a name in the bureaucratic hierarchy, representative of the layers of serf-officialdom that mediate between [[landowning-gentry|landowner]] and [[russian-peasantry|peasant]]s.
