## Overview

Reshetilovo is a village or small estate in [[orel-province|Orel Province]], mentioned in "The Peasant Proprietor [[ovsyanikov|Ovsyanikov]]." Its proprietors appeal to [[ovsyanikov|Luka Petrovitch Ovsyanikov]] for assistance in a local dispute.

## Description

The proprietors of Reshetilovo are small landholders who come to [[ovsyanikov|Ovsyanikov]] seeking his intervention. Their appeal—"Defend us, brother"—reflects [[ovsyanikov|Ovsyanikov]]'s reputation as a just arbiter among the provincial [[landowning-gentry|gentry]] and [[russian-peasantry|peasantry]] alike.

The nature of their grievance is not elaborated, but their approach to [[ovsyanikov|Ovsyanikov]] places Reshetilovo within the network of minor estates whose owners navigated the complex land relations of pre-Reform Russia.

## Role in the Stories

Reshetilovo appears in a single vignette within [[ovsyanikov|Ovsyanikov]]'s extended recollections. The proprietors' petition illustrates the informal justice system of the countryside, where respected elders like [[ovsyanikov|Ovsyanikov]] served as mediators in disputes that the formal legal system failed to address. The episode, though brief, contributes to the portrait of provincial life that [[ovsyanikov|Ovsyanikov]]'s narrative paints.
