# Ryabovo

## Overview

Ryabovo is a village that serves as the narrator's declared hunting destination in Turgenev's sketch "[[The Agent]]." Located thirty-five miles from [[Arkady Pavlitch Pyenotchkin]]'s estate, it is the place where the narrator finally witnesses the true condition of the [[russian-peasantry|peasant]]s living under [[Sofron]] Yakovlitch's administration. Through the testimony of [[Anpadist]], a [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] from Ryabovo, the village becomes the site where the chapter's central critique of estate mismanagement crystallizes.

## Description

Ryabovo is first mentioned when the narrator announces his shooting plans: "Thirty-five miles from here, at Ryabovo" (010-099). [[arkady-pyenotchkin|Pyenotchkin]] seizes on the opportunity to visit his own estate, noting that "Ryabovo's only four miles from my village [[shipilovka|Shipilovka]]" (010-100), and proposes an arrangement: "you can spend the day shooting in Ryabovo and come on in the evening to me" (010-104). He adds candidly that "at Ryabovo you'd have slept in some hayloft" (010-110), implying the village's lack of [[landowning-gentry|gentry]] accommodations.

The narrator's journey to Ryabovo is repeatedly deferred. [[arkady-pyenotchkin|Pyenotchkin]] diverts the party to [[Shipilovka]] instead, and "two hours later" on the following day, the narrator finally reaches Ryabovo, "making ready to begin shooting, accompanied by [[Anpadist]], a [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] I knew well" (010-443).

## Role in the Stories

Ryabovo is the site of the chapter's decisive moral revelation. After the shooting, the narrator speaks with [[anpadist|Anpadist]] about the [[shipilovka|Shipilovka]] [[russian-peasantry|peasant]]s and asks "whether he knew the agent there" (010-447). [[anpadist|Anpadist]] delivers the chapter's damning verdict on [[sofron|Sofron]]: "He's not a man; he's a dog; you couldn't find another brute like him between here and [[kursk|Kursk]]" (010-450). When the narrator expresses surprise, [[anpadist|Anpadist]] adds: "Why, [[shipilovka|Shipilovka]]'s hardly reckoned as — what's his name? — Mr. [[arkady-pyenotchkin|Pyenotchkin]]'s at all; he's not the master there; [[sofron|Sofron]]'s the master" (010-453). This testimony, given in Ryabovo's fields, exposes the inverted power structure of the estate — the steward has usurped the [[landowning-gentry|landowner]], and the [[russian-peasantry|peasant]]s pay the price.
