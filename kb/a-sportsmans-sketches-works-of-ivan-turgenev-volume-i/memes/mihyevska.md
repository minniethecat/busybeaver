# Mihyevska

## Overview

Mihyevska is a village named a single time in *A Sportsman's Sketches*, in the eighth sketch, [[byezhin-prairie|BYEZHIN PRAIRIE]]. It appears only as the home of one of the boys in a story the [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] children tell around their night fire.

## Description

The name is spoken by [[Ilyusha]] as he recounts the eerie night he and his workmates were shut up overnight in the paper-factory. Listing the boys of his shift, he says: "It happened that I and my brother Avdushka, with Fyodor of Mihyevska, and Ivashka the Squint-eyed, and the other Ivashka who comes from the [[Red Hills]], and Ivashka of Suhorukov too — and there were some other boys there as well — there were ten of us boys there altogether" (008-246).

Mihyevska is given only as the place Fyodor comes from — one of the cluster of small villages that supply hands to the mill, named in the same breath as the [[Red Hills]] and Suhorukov.

## Role in the Stories

The village exists in the sketch purely to locate a name. Yet that small specificity is characteristic of the boys' storytelling in [[byezhin-prairie|BYEZHIN PRAIRIE]]: by tagging each companion with his home village, [[Ilyusha]] gives his ghost story the texture of remembered fact, grounding the supernatural in a real, worked landscape of mills and hamlets.

Mihyevska thus belongs to the same modest company of place-names — [[Red Hills]], [[Stony Ridge]], [[Shashkino]] — through which the children map the country around their night pasture.
