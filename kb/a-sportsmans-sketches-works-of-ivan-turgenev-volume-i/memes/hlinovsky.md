# Hlinovsky

## Overview

Hlinovsky is a village or [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] commune that appears in [[The Agent]], adjoining the [[shipilovka|Shipilovka estate]] managed by the bailiff [[Sofron]]. It is named twice, in two very different registers: first as a party to a contentious division of land, and later as the source of acreage that [[sofron|Sofron]] rents into his private empire.

Hlinovsky is one of the small local place-names by which Turgenev builds up the dense, quarrelsome geography of estate life in the *[[A Sportsman's Sketches, Volume I|Sketches]]*.

## Description

The place is never described as a landscape; it is defined entirely by its people and their dealings over land. When [[Arkady Pavlitch Pyenotchkin]] asks whether the division of land shares has been settled, the answer folds Hlinovsky into the story of a recent dispute.

"The Hlinovsky folks made themselves disagreeable about it at first ... they were disagreeable about it, certainly. They wanted this ... and they wanted that ... and God knows what they didn't want!" (010-249). The tone is dismissive, the "Hlinovsky folks" cast as obstinate neighbours who had to be managed into agreement.

## Role in the Stories

Hlinovsky's second appearance exposes the machinery of [[sofron|Sofron]]'s power. Describing how the bailiff has enriched himself at the [[russian-peasantry|peasant]]s' expense, an informant explains that he "rents two hundred acres from the Hlinovsky [[russian-peasantry|peasant]]s alone, and two hundred and eighty from our folks" (010-460).

Here Hlinovsky is a unit of [[sofron|Sofron]]'s accumulation: their land, leased into his control, is one component of the holdings by which he "harries them out of their lives" (010-456). The village thus does double narrative duty — as a neighbour in a land quarrel, and as a source of the acreage that feeds the agent's quiet tyranny.

Through Hlinovsky, Turgenev shows how the abstract language of "land shares" and rented acres masks the concrete transfer of [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] livelihood into a single man's hands.
