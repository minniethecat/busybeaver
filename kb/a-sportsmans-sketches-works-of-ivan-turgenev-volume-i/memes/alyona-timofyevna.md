## Overview

Alyona Timofyevna is a provincial [[landowning-gentry|landowner]] who purchased the village of [[Lgov]] and its serfs, including the elderly house-serf [[Sutchok]]. Her sole appearance in the narrative is through [[Sutchok]]'s recollection of the day she assembled the household, inspected each serf in turn, and capriciously demoted him from coachman to fisherman—a vignette that distills the arbitrary power of the Russian landowning class over their human property (007-150).

## Appearance and Character

[[Sutchok]] describes Alyona Timofyevna with the unguarded frankness of a man who has served too many masters to care about discretion: "she is so fat ... not young" (007-151). She came to [[lgov-place|Lgov]] from her estate in [[tamboff|Tamboff]] and immediately asserted her authority by summoning the entire household for inspection. Her manner during this encounter is revealing:

> "We first kissed her hand, and she said nothing; she was not angry.... Then she began to question us in order; 'How are you employed? what duties have you?'" (007-155)

The ritual of hand-kissing, performed in silence, establishes the feudal distance between mistress and serf. Her questioning proceeds with bureaucratic methodicalness, each serf interrogated about their duties as if they were entries in an inventory.

## Role in the Story

Alyona Timofyevna's decisive moment comes when she reaches [[Sutchok]] in her inspection of the household. When he answers that he is a coachman, she delivers her verdict:

> "'Coachman? Well, a fine coachman you are; only look at you! You're not fit for a coachman, but be my fisherman, and shave your beard. On the occasions of my visits provide fish for the table; do you hear?'" (007-158)

The reclassification is arbitrary, absolute, and delivered with casual contempt. [[Sutchok]]'s identity—coachman, cook, fisherman—has been reshuffled by each successive owner, and Alyona Timofyevna continues the pattern with the insouciance of someone rearranging furniture. Her command that he shave his beard adds a note of petty humiliation: the beard was a mark of masculine dignity for a Russian [[russian-peasantry|peasant]], and its removal signalled his reduction to a menial domestic role.

[[Sutchok]] adds that she also ordered him to "keep my pond in order," a responsibility he finds absurd: "But how is one to keep it in order?" (007-162). The mistress issues commands without understanding the practicalities of their execution—a hallmark of the absentee [[landowning-gentry|landowner]] accustomed to giving orders rather than managing an estate.

Alyona Timofyevna's brief cameo is one of several portraits of female [[landowning-gentry|landowner]]s in [[A Sportsman's Sketches]]. Unlike the shadowy authority of [[Elena Nikolaevna Losnyakov]] in [[The Counting-House]] or the capricious [[Tatyana Vassilyevna]], she is rendered with an almost comic brutality. Her fatness, her [[tamboff|Tamboff]] origins, her peremptory inspection—all cohere into a vignette of provincial despotism in miniature.
