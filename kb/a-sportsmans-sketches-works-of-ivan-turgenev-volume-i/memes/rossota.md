## Overview

The Rossota is a small river in [[orel-province|Orel Province]], mentioned in Ivan Turgenev's "[[lgov-place|Lgov]]" (Chapter 7 of *A Sportsman's Sketches*). It flows past the large steppe village of [[lgov-place|Lgov]], powering two mills, and five miles downstream widens into a swampy pond overgrown with thick reeds — the setting for the chapter's memorable duck-shooting expedition.

## Description

Turgenev describes the Rossota in a single compact passage that conveys both the geography and atmosphere of the [[lgov-place|Lgov]] region. The village of [[lgov-place|Lgov]], with its "very old stone church with a single cupola," stands beside this "swampy little river" (007-009). Two mills harness its modest current. Five miles from [[lgov-place|Lgov]], the Rossota "becomes a wide swampy pond, overgrown at the edges, and in places also in the centre, with thick reeds" (007-010).

The river's transformation from a narrow channel at [[lgov-place|Lgov]] to a broad marshy pond downstream creates the distinctive wetland habitat that draws waterfowl — and thus hunters. The Rossota's reedy, overgrown character makes it a challenging but productive hunting ground.

## Role in the Stories

The Rossota serves as the geographical and dramatic anchor of "[[lgov-place|Lgov]]." The entire chapter unfolds on and around its waters. The narrator and [[yermolai|Yermolaï]] come to [[lgov-place|Lgov]] to shoot ducks on the Rossota's downstream pond. There they meet the former house-serf [[sutchok|Sutchok]] (also called Kuzma), who now serves as the fisherman on the pond and provides them with a leaky punt for their expedition.

The comic disaster that follows — the sinking punt, the narrator and [[yermolai|Yermolaï]] floundering in the reedy water while [[sutchok|Sutchok]] drifts calmly on the surface — is one of the most memorable set-pieces in *A Sportsman's Sketches*. The Rossota, in its quiet, swampy way, stages a scene that mixes slapstick with a subtle portrait of the serf's passive endurance.
