## Overview

Kintlyan Semenitch is mentioned by the [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] [[Vlass]] in "[[Raspberry Spring]]."

When [[vlass|Vlass]] explains that he is destitute and in arrears, he laughs and says that even Kintlyan Semenitch will have to be clever to get anything from him. (003-304)

The identity of Kintlyan Semenitch is not elaborated upon, but the context suggests he may be a local official or bailiff.

## Appearance and Character

No physical description of Kintlyan Semenitch appears in the text. His name — a distinctly Russian patronymic — marks him as a person of some standing in the local community.

The humorous tone in which [[vlass|Vlass]] invokes his name suggests a figure known to the [[russian-peasantry|peasant]]s, someone whose reputation for extracting payments precedes him.

## Role in the Story

Kintlyan Semenitch appears only in [[vlass|Vlass]]'s bitter joke. The [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] declares: "it can't hurt me; they can get nothing from me.... Yes, my friend, you can be as cunning as you please — I'm cleaned out!" (003-302)

Then he laughs and adds, "Kintlyan Semenitch'll have to be clever if —" (003-304). [[vlass|Vlass]] laughs again (003-305), and [[Mihailo Savelyev Tuman]] remarks, "Oh! things are in a sad way, brother [[vlass|Vlass]]" (003-306).

This exchange encapsulates the desperate humor of the Russian [[russian-peasantry|peasant]] — a man with nothing left to lose, mocking those who would take from him.
