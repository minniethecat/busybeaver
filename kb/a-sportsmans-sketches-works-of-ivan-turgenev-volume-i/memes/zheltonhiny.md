## Overview

Zheltonhiny is a settlement mentioned in "[[yermolai|Yermolaï]] and the Miller's Wife" as a place suffering from an outbreak of cattle plague.

## Description

Zheltonhiny appears in the evening fireside conversation at the mill. The [[the-miller|miller]]'s wife, [[arina-timofeyevna|Arina]], reports to [[yermolai|Yermolaï]]: "They've the cattle-plague again at Zheltonhiny; father Ivan's two cows are dead—Lord have mercy on them!" (002-002). The mention of "father Ivan" suggests Zheltonhiny has its own priest and is therefore likely a village with a church, rather than a mere hamlet. The name itself bears a Russian place-name suffix (-iny), consistent with rural settlements of the Orel-Kaluga region.

## Role in the Stories

Zheltonhiny serves a brief but vivid function in establishing the rural backdrop of "[[yermolai|Yermolaï]] and the Miller's Wife." The casual mention of cattle plague anchors the story in the precarious agricultural world of the Russian countryside, where livestock disease was a recurrent threat. The detail also humanizes [[arina-timofeyevna|Arina]], whose concern for the neighbor's livestock reveals the communal bonds of village life.
