## Overview

Apollonius of Chalcedon was a Stoic philosopher who served as one of Marcus Aurelius's direct teachers during his philosophical education. Marcus studied under him before becoming emperor, and Apollonius's instruction left a lasting impression on Marcus's character and habits of mind.

## Teachings and Influence

From Apollonius, Marcus records learning "true liberty, and unvariable steadiness, and not to regard anything at all, though never so little, but right and reason" [003-157]. Apollonius taught the young Marcus to maintain composure and independence of judgment — qualities essential to the Stoic ideal of inner freedom.

Marcus also reflects with gratitude that he "ever knew Apollonius and Rusticus" [004-169], pairing him with his most influential Stoic teacher, Junius Rusticus. This pairing suggests Apollonius held a position of similar importance in Marcus's early intellectual formation.

### Legacy

Though less well-documented than Rusticus or Fronto among Marcus's teachers, Apollonius appears in the Meditations as a formative influence on Marcus's commitment to rational self-governance and indifference to external circumstances.
