## Overview

Antoninus Pius (Titus Aelius Hadrianus Antoninus, 86–161 CE) was Roman emperor from 138 to 161 CE and the adoptive father of Marcus Aurelius. Throughout the Meditations, Marcus holds Antoninus up as the model of how a ruler should live — just, temperate, dutiful, and indifferent to flattery. (001-035) (001-036)

## Character and Governance

Marcus describes Antoninus in the Meditations with sustained admiration. He was a man who governed without vanity, administered justice with care, and remained unaffected by either praise or criticism. His reign was notable for its peace and prosperity — he was "the good emperor" whose personal character set the tone for the empire. (001-118)

Under Antoninus, Marcus received the practical education of a future ruler while continuing his philosophical training. The example of Antoninus — a man who embodied Stoic principles without being a professional philosopher — profoundly shaped Marcuss own understanding of how philosophy and power could coexist.
