## Overview

Evil (Greek κακόν / _kakon_, Latin _malum_) in Stoic philosophy is the privation of the good through vice. Just as nothing is good but virtue, nothing is evil but vice. External misfortunes — pain, loss, death — are not genuinely evil; they are "indifferents" that cannot harm the soul unless it assents to being harmed. (002-044)

## The Nature of Evil

The Stoics located evil exclusively in the will — in the soul's failure to govern itself according to reason. Evil is not a positive force opposed to good but an absence or corruption: the irrational choice, the assent to false impressions, the indulgence of passion. Marcus Aurelius prays for deliverance from "all passion and evil affection, from all rashness and vanity, from all slothfulness and cowardice." Evil is what damages the soul. (002-152)

The Stoic attitude toward external evil — the suffering and injustice of the world — is grounded in the conviction that all things work together for the good of the whole. What appears evil from a limited perspective serves the cosmic purpose. Marcus, reflecting on the hardships of life, quotes a saying with Stoic resonance: "few and evil are the days of man's life." (002-024) (002-131)

## Overcoming Evil

The proper response to evil is not retaliation but transformation. "Evil must be overcome with good." For every fault one encounters in others, nature has provided a counteracting disposition. The Stoic does not fight evil with evil but with the specific virtue that opposes it. (002-167)

Marcus disciplined himself "not only to do, but to intend any evil" — to root out not merely evil actions but evil intentions. His standard was the purity of the inner disposition, not merely outward compliance. He refused even to believe in demons or evil spirits, preferring to find the source of wrongdoing in human ignorance rather than supernatural malice. (003-116) (003-132)

A nature "which thought no evil" — this was the ideal Marcus held before himself. The cultivated soul, like the cultivated mind, simply does not generate evil thoughts. (002-180)
