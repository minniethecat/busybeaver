## Overview

Sextus of Chaeronea was a Stoic philosopher who taught Marcus Aurelius, likely in the 140s CE during Marcus's intensive philosophical education. He was the nephew of the famous biographer and Platonist Plutarch, and his birthplace of Chaeronea in Boeotia carried the prestige of that intellectual lineage.

## Character and Teachings

Marcus records Sextus's most distinctive quality as "mildness and the pattern of a family governed with paternal affection" [004-001]. Unlike some Stoic teachers who emphasized austerity and emotional detachment, Sextus modeled a gentler Stoicism — one compatible with warmth, family life, and natural affection.

Marcus also notes Sextus's "accommodation to all true friendships, and his tolerance of the unlearned and the unthinking" [004-001], qualities that distinguished him from the more severe Stoic teachers of the era.

## Legacy

Sextus is described in the glossary as "a Stoic philosopher, nephew of Plutarch" [034-208], acknowledged as a direct influence on Marcus's moral formation. Together with Rusticus and Apollonius, Sextus formed the core of Marcus's philosophical education — each teacher contributing a different dimension to the future emperor's Stoic practice.
