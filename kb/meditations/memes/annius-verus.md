## Overview

Marcus Annius Verus was a Roman senator who served three times as consul and was the paternal grandfather of Marcus Aurelius. After the early death of Marcus's father, Annius Verus helped raise the future emperor in his household on the Caelian Hill in Rome. He was "sprung of a noble family that had originally come from Spain" [001-022] and had "held high office in Rome" three times [001-025].

## Role in Marcus's Upbringing

When Marcus's father died young, the boy was taken into the household of his grandfather Annius Verus, "and there was brought up" [001-028]. This arrangement was formative: Marcus's early character was shaped by his grandfather's household, where he absorbed the values of Roman aristocratic virtue — restraint, public service, and family honor.

Marcus later recorded his gratitude "that I had a grandfather, both an excellent man, and one that could command his own anger" [003-113], attributing to Annius Verus, alongside his mother, the foundation of his moral education.

### Legacy

Annius Verus represents the familial anchor in Marcus's formation. Where his philosophical teachers shaped his intellect, his grandfather shaped his character — providing the stable household in which the future philosopher-emperor's virtues could take root.
