# Section 32

(032-001) [26] Ad M. Caes., ii. 3.

(032-002) [27] The Emperor [[antoninus-pius|Antoninus Pius]] is spoken of as _dominus meus_.

(032-003) [28] This sentence is written in Greek.

(032-004) [29] Several of these words are Greek, and the meaning is not quite

(032-005) clear.

(032-006) 'After this you will take care not to tell so many fibs of me,

(032-007) especially in the Senate. A monstrous fine speech this is! O if I could

(032-008) kiss your head at every heading of it! You have looked down on all with

(032-009) a vengeance. This oration once read, in vain shall we study, in vain

(032-010) shall we toil, in vain strain every nerve. Farewell always, most sweet

(032-011) master.'

(032-012) Sometimes [[fronto|Fronto]] descends from the heights of eloquence to offer

(032-013) practical advice; as when he suggests how Marcus should deal with his

(032-014) suite. It is more difficult, he admits, to keep courtiers in harmony

(032-015) than to tame lions with a lute; but if it is to be done, it must be by

(032-016) eradicating jealousy. 'Do not let your friends,' says Fronto,'[30] 'envy

(032-017) each other, or think that what you give to another is filched from them.

(032-018) [30] Ad M Caes., iv. 1.

(032-019) Keep away envy from your suite, and you will find your friends kindly

(032-020) and harmonious.'

(032-021) Here and there we meet with allusions to his daily life, which we could

(032-022) wish to be more frequent. He goes to the theatre or the law-courts,[31]

(032-023) or takes part in court ceremony, but his heart is always with his

(032-024) books. The vintage season, with its religious rites, was always spent by

(032-025) Antoninus Pius in the country. The following letters give sonic notion

(032-026) of a day's occupation at that time:(3)

(032-027) [31] ii. 14

(032-028) [32] iv. 5,6.

(032-029) 'MY DEAREST MASTER,--I am well. To-day I studied from the ninth hour of

(032-030) the night to the second hour of day, after taking food. I then put on

(032-031) my slippers, and from time second to the third hour had a most

(032-032) enjoyable walk up and down before my chamber. Then booted and

(032-033) cloaked-for so we were commanded to appear-I went to wait upon my lord

(032-034) the emperor. We went a-hunting, did doughty deeds, heard a rumour that

(032-035) boars had been caught, but there was nothing to see. However, we

(032-036) climbed a pretty steep hill, and in the afternoon returned home. I went

(032-037) straight to my books. Off with the boots, down with the cloak; I spent

(032-038) a couple of hours in bed. I read Cato's speech on the Property of

(032-039) Pulchra, and another in which he impeaches a tribune. Ho, ho! I hear

(032-040) you cry to your man, Off with you as fast as you can, and bring me

(032-041) these speeches from the library of Apollo. No use to send: I have those

(032-042) books with me too. You must get round the Tiberian librarian; you will

(032-043) have to spend something on the matter; and when I return to town, I

(032-044) shall expect to go shares with him. Well, after reading these speeches

(032-045) I wrote a wretched trifle, destined for drowning or burning. No, indeed

(032-046) my attempt at writing did not come off at all to-day; the composition

(032-047) of a hunter or a vintager, whose shouts are echoing through my chamber,

(032-048) hateful and wearisome as the law-courts. What have I said? Yes, it was

(032-049) rightly said, for my master is an orator. I think I have caught cold,

(032-050) whether from walking in slippers or from writing badly, I do not know.

(032-051) I am always annoyed with phlegm, but to-day I seem to snivel more than

(032-052) usual. Well, I will pour oil on my head and go off to sleep. I don't

(032-053) mean to put one drop in my lamp to-day, so weary am I from riding and

(032-054) sneezing. Farewell, dearest and most beloved master, whom I miss, I may

(032-055) say, more than [[rome|Rome]] itself.'

(032-056) 'MY BELOVED MASTER,-I am well. I slept a little more than usual for my

(032-057) slight cold, which seems to be well again. So I spent the time from the

(032-058) eleventh hour of the night to the third of the day partly in reading in

(032-059) Cato's Agriculture, partly in writing, not quite so badly as yesterday

(032-060) indeed. Then, after waiting upon my father, I soothed my throat with

(032-061) honey-water, ejecting it without swallowing: I might say _gargle_, but I

(032-062) won't, though I think the word is found in Novius and elsewhere. After

(032-063) attending to my throat I went to my father, and stood by his side as he

(032-064) sacrificed. Then to luncheon. What do you think I had to eat? A bit of

(032-065) bread so big, while I watched others gobbling boiled beans, onions,

(032-066) and fish full of roe. Then we set to work at gathering the grapes,

(032-067) with plenty of sweat and shouting, and, as the quotation runs, "A few

(032-068) high-hanging clusters did we leave survivors of the vintage." After the

(032-069) sixth hour we returned home. I did a little work, and poor work at that.

(032-070) Then I had a long gossip with my dear mother sitting on the bed. My

(032-071) conversation was: What do you think my friend Fronto is doing just now?

(032-072) She said: And what do you think of my friend Gratia?'[33] My turn now:

(032-073) And what of our little Gratia,[34] the sparrowkin? After this kind of

(032-074) talk, and an argument as to which of you loved the other most, the gong

(032-075) sounded, the signal that my father had gone to the bath. We supped,

(032-076) after ablutions in the oil-cellar-I mean we supped after ablutions, not

(032-077) after ablutions in the oil-cellar; and listened with enjoyment to the

(032-078) rustics gibing. After returning, before turning on my side to snore, I

(032-079) do my task and give an account of the day to my delightful master, whom

(032-080) if I could long for a little more, I should not mind growing a trifle

(032-081) thinner. Farewell, Fronto, wherever you are, honey-sweet, my darling, my

(032-082) [[pleasure|delight]]. Why do I want you? I can love you while far away.'

(032-085) One anecdote puts Marcus before us in a new light:[35]

(032-086) [35] Ad M. Caes ii. 12.

(032-087) 'When my father returned home from the vineyards, I mounted my horse as

(032-088) usual, and rode on ahead some little way. Well, there on the road was a

(032-089) herd of sheep, standing all crowded together as though the place were

(032-090) a desert, with four dogs and two shepherds, but nothing else. Then one

(032-091) shepherd said to another shepherd, on seeing a number of horsemen: 'I

(032-092) say,' says he, 'look you at those horsemen; they do a deal of robbery.'

(032-093) When I heard this, I clap spurs to my horse, and ride straight for the

(032-094) sheep. In consternation the sheep scatter; hither and thither they are

(032-095) fleeting and bleating. A shepherd throws his fork, and the fork falls

(032-096) on the horseman who came next to me. We make our escape.' We like Marcus

(032-097) none the worse for this spice of mischief.

(032-098) Another letter[36] describes a visit to a country town, and shows the

(032-099) antiquarian spirit of the writer:—

(032-100) 'M. CÆSAR to his MASTER M. FRONTO, greeting.

(032-101) 'After I entered the carriage, after I took leave of you, we made a

(032-102) journey comfortable enough, but we had a few drops of rain to wet us.

(032-103) But before coming to the country-house, we broke our journey at Anagnia,

(032-104) a mile or so from the highroad. Then we inspected that ancient town, a

(032-105) miniature it is, but has in it many antiquities, temples, and religious

(032-106) ceremonies quite out of the way. There is not a corner without its

(032-107) shrine, or fane, or temple; besides, many books written on linen, which

(032-108) belongs to things sacred. Then on the gate as we came out was written

(032-109) twice, as follows: "Priest don the fell."[37] I asked one of the

(032-110) inhabitants what that word was. He said it was the word in the Hernican

(032-111) dialect for the victim's skin, which the priest puts over his conical

(032-112) cap when he enters the city. I found out many other things which I

(032-113) desired to know, but the only thing I do not desire is that you should

(032-114) be absent from me; that is my chief anxiety. Now for yourself, when you

(032-115) left that place, did you go to Aurelia or to Campania? Be sure to write

(032-116) to me, and say whether you have opened the vintage, or carried a host of

(032-117) books to the country-house; this also, whether you miss me; I am foolish

(032-118) to ask it, whenas you tell it me of yourself. Now if you miss me and

(032-119) if you love me, send me your letters often, which is a comfort and

(032-120) consolation to me. Indeed I should prefer ten times to read your letters

(032-121) than all the vines of Gaurus or the Marsians; for these Signian vines

(032-122) have grapes too rank and fruit too sharp in the taste, but I prefer wine

(032-123) to must for drinking. Besides, those grapes are nicer to eat dried than

(032-124) fresh-ripe; I vow I would rather tread them under foot than put my teeth

(032-125) in them. But I pray they may be gracious and forgiving, and grant me

(032-126) free pardon for these jests of mine. Farewell, best friend, dearest,

(032-127) most learned, sweetest master. When you see the must ferment in the vat,

(032-128) remember that just so in my heart the longing for you is gushing and

(032-129) flowing and bubbling. Good-bye.'

(032-132) Making all allowances for conventional exaggerations, it is clear from

(032-133) the correspondence that there was deep love between Marcus and his

(032-134) preceptor. The letters cover several years in succession, but soon after

(032-135) the birth of Marcus's daughter, [[faustina|Faustina]], there is a large gap. It does

(032-136) not follow that the letters ceased entirely, because we know part of

(032-137) the collection is lost; but there was probably less intercourse between

(032-138) Marcus and Fronto after Marcus took to the study of philosophy under the

(032-139) guidance of [[rusticus|Rusticus]].

(032-140) When Marcus succeeded to the throne in 161, the letters begin again,

(032-141) with slightly increased formality on Fronto's part, and they go on for

(032-142) some four years, when Fronto, who has been continually complaining of

(032-143) ill-health, appears to have died. One letter of the later period gives

(032-144) some interesting particulars of the emperor's public life, which are

(032-145) worth quoting. Fronto speaks of Marcus's victories and eloquence in the

(032-146) usual strain of high praise, and then continues.[38]

(032-147) 'The army when you took it in hand was sunk in luxury and revelry, and

(032-148) corrupted with long inactivity. At Antiochia the soldiers had been Wont

(032-149) to applaud at the stage plays, knew more of the gardens at the nearest

(032-150) restaurant than of the battlefield. Horses were hairy from lack of

(032-151) grooming, horsemen smooth because their hairs had been pulled out by

(032-152) the roots[39] a rare thing it was to see a soldier with hair on arm or

(032-153) leg. Moreover, they were better drest than armed; so much so, that

(032-154) Laelianus Pontius, a strict man of the old discipline, broke the

(032-155) cuirasses of some of them with his finger-tips, and observed cushions

(032-156) on the horses' backs. At his direction the tufts were cut through, and

(032-157) out of the horsemen's saddles came what appeared to be feathers pluckt

(032-158) from geese. Few of the men could vault on horseback, the rest clambered

(032-159) up with difficulty by aid of heel and knee and leg not many could throw

(032-160) a lance hurtling, most did it without force or power, as though they

(032-161) were things of wool-dicing was common in the camp, sleep lasted all

(032-162) night, or if they kept watch it was over the winecup. By what

(032-163) regulations to restrain such soldiers as these, and to turn them to

(032-164) honesty and industry, did you not learn from Hannibal's sternness, the

(032-165) discipline of Africanus, the acts of Metellus recorded in history.
