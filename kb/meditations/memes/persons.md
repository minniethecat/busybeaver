## Persons

The *Meditations* mentions numerous historical figures, philosophers, and contemporaries of Marcus Aurelius. Many serve as moral exemplars — models of virtue to emulate or cautionary tales to avoid. This page provides a dynamic index of all person pages in the wiki, automatically sorted by label.

## About This Index

This index is dynamically generated from the wiki's frontmatter metadata. The **Quality** column reflects the current assessment of each page (featured → standard → basic). **References** (`total_refs`) counts the number of other wiki pages that link to the person.

Figures range from pre-Socratic philosophers (Heraclitus, Democritus, Empedocles) to classical Greek thinkers (Socrates, Plato) to Hellenistic school founders (Zeno of Citium, Epicurus, Chrysippus), Roman statesmen (Cato, Augustus, Trajan, Hadrian, Antoninus Pius), and Marcus' personal circle (Rusticus, Fronto, Faustina, Lucius Verus).

Pages are listed in alphabetical order by label. To explore individual figures in depth, visit their respective pages and see the **Related** sections for cross-references.
