## Overview

French Baits His Trap is the culminating investigative stratagem devised by Inspector Joseph French to capture John Roper, the principal antagonist of the Starvel Hollow case. French sent a forged telegram to Roper, purportedly from the London stockbroking firm Dashwood & Munce, requesting a meeting with their junior partner at Waverley Station, Edinburgh. The trap exploited Roper's greed and succeeded in drawing him to the station, where French and the Edinburgh police were waiting.

## The Plan

French devised the trap after obtaining Dr. Philpot's confession and understanding the full scope of Roper's crimes. He sent a telegram to Roper through an Edinburgh tobacconist acting as intermediary, posing as Dashwood & Munce, stockbrokers. The message requested Roper to meet their junior partner at Waverley Station on Tuesday at 6:15 p.m., during the waiting period of the 10:00 a.m. train from King's Cross.

French simultaneously coordinated with the Edinburgh police and arranged for a colleague from Scotland Yard to travel by the same train, arriving at Waverley to cooperate as circumstances demanded.

## Execution

On the appointed Tuesday, French and the Edinburgh police positioned themselves at Waverley Station. The train from King's Cross arrived, and Roper appeared to meet the fictitious junior partner. French moved in and arrested Roper at the station. The capture was executed without violence, and Roper was taken into custody.

## Roper's Suspicion

Roper was not entirely deceived. On receiving the telegram, he "half-suspected a trap" and the plan became fully apparent when French's letter to him arrived. However, Roper calculated that he was "either in the trap or out of it" — if he refused to appear, the police would come for him; if he attended, he might still find a way to escape. He chose to attend, walking into French's hands.

## Significance

The trap demonstrated French's methodical investigative approach. Rather than pursuing Roper directly, French created a situation where Roper would voluntarily come to him, minimizing the risk of flight or violent confrontation. The success of the trap allowed French to take Roper into custody with the evidence needed to secure a conviction.
