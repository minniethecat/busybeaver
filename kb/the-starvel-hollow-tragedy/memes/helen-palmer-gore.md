## Profile

Mrs. Helen Palmer-Gore is a kindly York gentlewoman whose invitation to [[ruth-averill|Ruth Averill]] sets crucial plot machinery in motion. Described as "a large, kindly, fussily-mannered woman" [62-62], she is a distant acquaintance of the Averill family whose friendly letter to [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] provides the catalyst for one of the novel's most significant narrative developments.

## Role in the Narrative

Mrs. Palmer-Gore's role, though physically brief, is structurally pivotal. Her letter inviting [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] to visit York arrives at a moment when [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] feels trapped and [[miser]]able at Starvel [62-62]. [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] seizes on the invitation as an escape: "not so much of visiting the Palmer-Gores and York, but of getting away from Starvel" [80-80]. The visit is intended to last a week, but is cut short after only two days when news of [[starvel-hollow-fire|the Starvel tragedy]] reaches them [136-136].

During [[joseph-french|Inspector French]]'s investigation, the Palmer-Gore invitation becomes a central point of inquiry. [[joseph-french|French]] recognizes that if [[john-roper|Roper]] orchestrated [[ruth-averill|Ruth]]'s absence from Starvel on the night of the fire, he must have somehow arranged the invitation. [[joseph-french|French]] travels to York and interviews Mrs. Palmer-Gore at her home, Oakdean, Ashton Drive [1467-1469]. She freely explains that she wrote to [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] after receiving a letter — supposedly from [[simon-averill|Simon Averill]] — suggesting that [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] was lonely and would welcome an invitation [1471-1471].

The authenticity of this exchange becomes a linchpin of [[joseph-french|French]]'s theory. He later questions [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] and learns that she had never expressed any interest in the York flower show — a detail mentioned in Averill's supposed letter — confirming that the letter was forged, almost certainly by [[john-roper|Roper]], as part of his scheme to clear the house before the robbery and fire [1519-1531].

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter I**: Her letter of invitation reaches [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] at Starvel [62-62].
- **Chapter I**: Meets [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] at York station and hosts her for two days [136-136].
- **Later chapters**: Interviewed by [[joseph-french|Inspector French]] at her York home; reveals the circumstances of the invitation [1467-1513].
