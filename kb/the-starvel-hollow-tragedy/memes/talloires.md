## Description

Talloires is a small village on the eastern shore of [[annecy|Lac d'Annecy]] in the Savoy region of France, rather more than halfway down the lake. The hotel manager at [[annecy|Annecy]] describes it to [[joseph-french|French]] as "a picturesque spot" with "no less than three hotels" (013-014). The village is accessible by steamer from [[annecy|Annecy]]; all the steamers call there (013-014).

Opposite the pier at Talloires stands a large hotel where [[joseph-french|French]], on arrival, makes inquiries about [[pierce-whymper|Whymper]]. The setting is one of alpine beauty — the lake surrounded by foothills, small boats at the steps along the shore, and a sense of peaceful tourism that contrasts sharply with the grim investigation [[joseph-french|French]] is pursuing (013-014).

Chapter VI of the novel bears the title "Talloires, Lac d'Annecy," marking the village as the setting for [[joseph-french|French]]'s Continental investigation.

## Significance in the Novel

Talloires is central to the mystery of Pierce [[pierce-whymper|Whymper]]'s trip to France. [[pierce-whymper|Whymper]] told [[joseph-french|French]] that he purchased a return ticket from [[london|London]] to Talloires, near [[annecy|Annecy]], Savoy, ostensibly to carry out a confidential mission for Simon Averill (011-012). This trip was paid for with one of the stolen twenty-pound notes from the Starvel fire, making its funding a critical piece of [[evidence]].

The village is also the supposed residence of two invented figures — M. Prosper Giraud and Mme. Madeleine Blancquart — whom [[pierce-whymper|Whymper]] claimed to be seeking. According to [[john-roper|Roper]]'s fabricated story, the fugitive Theodore Averill had been living at Talloires under the alias Prosper Giraud, supporting himself by writing short stories for magazines, with his old housekeeper Mme. Blancquart blackmailing him after discovering his secret (021-023).

[[joseph-french|French]] travels to Talloires and discovers that [[pierce-whymper|Whymper]] did indeed make inquiries there — offering a reward of 5,000 francs for information about either of the mysterious couple — and that the same inquiries had been received from the police at [[annecy|Annecy]] (015-016). However, no one at Talloires had ever heard of Prosper Giraud or Madeleine Blancquart, and [[joseph-french|French]] ultimately concludes the entire story was "a pure invention of [[john-roper|Roper]]'s" (020-022).

## Real-World Context

Talloires is a real village on [[annecy|Lac d'Annecy]] in the Haute-Savoie department of southeastern France. In the 1920s, when the novel is set, the region was already established as a picturesque tourist destination. The steamer service on the lake was a well-known feature; Crofts accurately depicts the journey from [[annecy|Annecy]] by water. The Hotel Splendide in [[annecy|Annecy]], where both [[pierce-whymper|Whymper]] and [[joseph-french|French]] stayed, was a real grand hotel of the era. The inclusion of Cook's travel agency vouchers for "three days' pension at the Hotel Splendide, [[annecy|Annecy]]" reflects the standard Continental travel arrangements of the period (011-012).
