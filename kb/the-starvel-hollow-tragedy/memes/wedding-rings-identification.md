## Summary

The identification of thirty-nine wedding rings recovered from the Thames was the pivotal breakthrough in Inspector [[joseph-french|French]]'s investigation of the [[starvel|Starvel Hollow]] tragedy. Chief Inspector [[major-mitchell|Mitchell]] deduced that the rings, found by lightermen on the river, had been deliberately discarded to dispose of evidence of a money-laundering scheme. [[joseph-french|French]]'s subsequent investigation traced [[john-roper|John Roper]] — disguised with a moustache and tinted glasses — as the man systematically buying wedding rings across London jewellers, exchanging stolen £20 notes from Averill's safe for clean currency [3107-PN].

## Detailed Account

### The Rings on the Thames

The chain of discovery began when two lightermen, James Gray and William Fuller, found thirty-nine wedding rings on the deck of their vessel as they passed beneath Tower Bridge at about 8:30 p.m. on Monday, 6th December. "Fuller heard a smack on the deck beside him. He looked down and in the light of some of the shore lamps saw some bright objects rolling about on the planks" [2945-PN]. A quarrel broke out between the two men over ownership of the rings, attracting the attention of a constable.

Chief Inspector [[major-mitchell|Mitchell]] recognized the significance of the find immediately. "Such men could never have invented a tale like that. If they had been making it up they would have said they found a bag of rings in the street" [2957-PN]. He concluded the rings had been thrown from Tower Bridge deliberately — not into the river, but at the boat — to dispose of incriminating evidence.

### French's Investigation

[[joseph-french|French]] was assigned to investigate. He visited jewellers across London, circulating a description of a man who had been buying wedding rings and paying with £20 notes. The breakthrough came when he connected the purchases to [[john-roper|Roper]]: "Who was this mysterious individual, this dark-haired man with moustache and glasses, but Roper! Roper it was who had been going about buying wedding rings, and Roper it was who naturally found that he must get rid of such incriminating purchases at the earliest possible moment" [3107-PN].

The scheme was elegant: "For every ring a £20 note, a tainted £20 note, a £20 note from Mr. Averill's wrecked safe up at Starvel. And for every £20 note got rid of over £18 of good, clean, untraceable money brought in. It was a scheme, a great scheme, worthy of the man who had devised the crime as a whole" [3107-PN].

## Significance

The wedding rings identification transformed the investigation. It provided the first hard evidence linking [[john-roper|Roper]] to the stolen notes from Averill's safe, confirming that the fire was not an accident but arson designed to conceal murder and robbery. The discovery also demonstrated the investigative synergy between [[joseph-french|French]] and [[major-mitchell|Mitchell]] — Mitchell spotted the pattern; French traced the man.

The episode revealed [[john-roper|Roper]]'s methodical criminal mind: rather than attempting to pass the stolen notes directly, he constructed an elaborate laundering operation involving jewellery purchases across London, taxi travel, and the dramatic disposal of evidence from Tower Bridge [3157-PN].
