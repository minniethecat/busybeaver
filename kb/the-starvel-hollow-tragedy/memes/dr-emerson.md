## Profile

Dr. Emerson is the elderly physician of [[thirsby|Thirsby]] who, though past his working prime, remains a respected figure in the community. Described as "old and past his work" who "had retired in all but name" [90-90], he had been [[simon-averill|Simon Averill]]'s doctor for eight years before handing over the practice to [[dr-philpot|Dr. Philpot]]. The landlord of the Thirsdale Arms speaks warmly of him: "a good doctor and well respected... he had made plenty and he could afford to retire" [612-612].

## Role in the Narrative

Dr. Emerson appears at several crucial junctures in the investigation. When [[ruth-averill|Ruth Averill]]'s uncle falls ill, the household hesitates to call Emerson — preferring to wait for [[dr-philpot|Dr. Philpot]] — but the doctor's illness forces the issue [88-90].

On the morning of the fire's discovery, Emerson is among the party that hastens to the smouldering ruins of Starvel alongside [[sergeant-kent|Sergeant Kent]] and [[oxley|Mr. Oxley]] [154-154]. Later, he accompanies the vicar to meet [[ruth-averill|Ruth]]'s train and expresses his sympathy for the bereaved young woman [168-168].

At [[the-inquest|the coroner's inquest]], Emerson provides key medical testimony. He confirms that the disinterred remains, though unidentifiable by sight, correspond in build and stature to Simon Averill and the [[john-roper|Roper]]s [284-284]. Crucially, he testifies that Averill suffered from "an incurable complaint which must eventually have killed him" and had been growing "slowly and steadily weaker" for years — [[evidence]] that supports the theory that Averill may have been too feeble to escape the fire [294-294].

[[joseph-french|Inspector French]] interviews Emerson during his systematic canvassing of the "obvious dramatis personae" in the case [734-734]. Though Emerson features less prominently than Philpot in the unfolding mystery, his medical testimony and long history with the Averill household make him an important source of background information.

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter I**: Mentioned as the fallback physician when Dr. Philpot is ill [88-90]; later among the first to reach the fire scene [154-154].
- **Chapter II**: Gives medical testimony at [[the-inquest|the coroner's inquest]] [284-296].
- **Mid-investigation**: Interviewed by [[joseph-french|Inspector French]] [734-734].
