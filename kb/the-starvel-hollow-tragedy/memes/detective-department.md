## Overview

The Detective Department of the Edinburgh police is a Scottish law enforcement division that assists Inspector [[joseph-french|French]] by providing background information on the death of Flora Roper, [[john-roper|John Roper]]'s wife, in Kintilloch, Fife [007-029]. The department compiles and forwards a comprehensive dossier that helps French uncover the Ropers' history of suspicious deaths.

## Role in the Investigation

Early in the investigation, French becomes interested in the death of Flora Roper, which occurred some years before the Starvel fire. To obtain details, he telephones the Detective Department of the Edinburgh police from Scotland Yard, requesting any available information about the accident [007-029].

Two days later, the department sends a voluminous dossier including newspaper cuttings, a detailed report from the local sergeant with a short history of all concerned, Dr. Ferguson's death certificate ("concussion, resulting from a fall"), and a confidential covering letter from the head of the department explaining that the local superintendent's dissatisfaction had prompted a more thorough inquiry than usual [007-031]. Though the case was officially dropped when no suspicious circumstances were found, the dossier provides French with crucial background that, combined with his later discoveries, suggests a pattern of convenient deaths around John Roper.
