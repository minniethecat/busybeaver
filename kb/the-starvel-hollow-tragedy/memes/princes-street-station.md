## Overview

Princes Street Station was a railway station at the west end of Princes Street, Edinburgh. In *The Starvel Hollow Tragedy*, Inspector French arrives at this station on the morning of the day he sets his trap for John Roper at nearby Waverley Station.

## Role in the Investigation

When French devises his plan to capture John Roper at Waverley Station, he must travel to Edinburgh ahead of time to coordinate the operation. He takes the 11:35 p.m. sleeper from Euston on Monday night, and "about eight o'clock the next morning reached Princes Street Station" [019-090].

Emerging from the station, French experiences a moment of reflection. He "had not been to Edinburgh for years, and emerging from the station, he was struck afresh with the beauty of the gardens and the splendour of the Castle Rock" [019-090]. However, his seasoned traveller's eye notes that "Princes Street itself, which he had once thought so magnificent, seemed to have shrunk, and its buildings to have grown smaller and plainer." This prompts a moment of self-awareness: "'Too much foreign travel,' he thought, vaguely regretful of his change of outlook; 'the towns abroad certainly spoil one for ours'" [019-090].

French spends most of the day exploring the historic buildings of Edinburgh's old town. As five o'clock approaches, he enters the Princes Street Gardens and takes up a position near Scott's Monument — the rendezvous point where he will meet Dr. Philpot before proceeding to Waverley Station for the climactic confrontation [019-094].

Princes Street Station thus serves as the entry point for French's Edinburgh operation — his last major journey in the Starvel Hollow investigation.
