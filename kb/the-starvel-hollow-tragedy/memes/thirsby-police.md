## Overview

The Thirsby police force is the local law enforcement authority responsible for the investigation of the [[starvel|Starvel Hollow]] fire. Led by Sergeant Kent, the force is based in the market town of Thirsby and serves the surrounding rural area of Yorkshire [ch01].

As a small-town police force in the 1920s, its resources and forensic capabilities would be limited by the standards of larger cities. The investigation of a potential crime involving fire, multiple deaths, and possible financial [[motive]]s would likely require assistance from county-level or outside authorities if [[the-inquest|the inquest]] findings warrant escalation.

## Role in the Investigation

Sergeant Kent and his men were among the first responders to the Starvel fire, arriving at the scene after the baker raised the alarm in Thirsby [ch01]. The force's initial actions included:

- Securing the fire scene at [[starvel|Starvel Hollow]]
- Organizing the recovery of bodies from the ruins
- Taking statements from witnesses, including the baker and [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] Averill
- Presenting findings at [[the-inquest|the coroner's inquest]] [ch02]

Sergeant Kent personally interviewed [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] at the Oxleys' house on the evening after the tragedy [ch01], and later notified her of [[the-inquest|the inquest]] arrangements [ch02]. His manner is described as "tall, rather brusque" but "polite enough" in the Oxleys' presence.

The Thirsby police represent the official, institutional response to the tragedy — contrasting with the personal, emotional response of [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] and the Oxleys.

## Key Members

- **Sergeant Kent**: Lead investigator. A tall, brusque man who conducts witness interviews and presents the police case at [[the-inquest|the inquest]].
