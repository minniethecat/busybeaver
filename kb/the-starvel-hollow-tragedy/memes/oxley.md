## Profile

Mr. Oxley is the leading solicitor of [[thirsby|Thirsby]] and a central supporting character in *The [[starvel|Starvel Hollow]] Tragedy*. He had charge of all [[simon-averill|Simon Averill]]'s business affairs and, together with his wife Mrs. Oxley, serves as a protector and guide to [[ruth-averill|Ruth Averill]] after the Starvel fire. Described as "kind and clever" [195-195], he is portrayed as a competent, compassionate professional who acts with discretion and good judgment throughout the crisis.

## Role in the Narrative

Mr. Oxley plays a pivotal role in the aftermath of the Starvel fire. He is among the first to reach the scene of the tragedy alongside [[sergeant-kent|Sergeant Kent]] and [[dr-emerson|Dr. Emerson]] [154-154]. Recognizing the need to break the news gently to [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] Averill, he returns to Thirsby and sends a carefully worded telegram: "Terrible accident at Starvel. Your uncle injured. Return Thirsby and stay night with us" [142-142].

As Simon Averill's solicitor, Oxley had drawn up the late man's will and is able to reassure [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] that "she was not to worry as to her future" and that there would be some money coming to her [196-196]. He lends her twenty pounds for mourning clothes, explaining she could repay him when she received her own funds [198-198]. Throughout [[the-inquest|the inquest]], he provides [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] with moral support and practical guidance, using his position as the town's leading solicitor to secure her a seat with counsel [202-202].

Oxley also features in the financial subplot of the novel. He learns from [[tarkington|Mr. Tarkington]], the bank manager, that Simon Averill's fortune was held almost entirely in cash in the safe at Starvel — money that was presumably destroyed in the fire [170-170]. This revelation drives significant plot developments as the investigation turns toward the possibility that the safe may have been fire[[evidence|proof]] and the money stolen.

## Relationships

- **[[ruth-averill|Ruth Averill]]**: Oxley and his wife take [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] into their home after the fire, treating her with genuine care and kindness. He acts as her legal and financial adviser while she navigates the aftermath of her uncle's death.
- **[[simon-averill|Simon Averill]]**: Oxley served as Averill's solicitor for many years, managing his business affairs and drawing up his will.
- **[[tarkington|Mr. Tarkington]]**: Friend and professional colleague. Oxley and Tarkington collaborate closely on the financial aspects of the Averill case, discussing the implications of the lost safe and its contents.
- **[[pierce-whymper|Pierce Whymper]]**: Acquaintance in Thirsby; Oxley is aware of [[pierce-whymper|Whymper]]'s romantic interest in [[ruth-averill|Ruth]].

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter I**: Receives news of the fire, hastens to Starvel, sends the telegram to [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] at York [142-142].
- **Chapter II**: Attends [[the-inquest|the inquest]] with [[ruth-averill|Ruth]], provides context and guidance throughout the proceedings [202-202].
- **Later chapters**: Confers with Tarkington about the financial dimensions of the case and the fate of Averill's fortune [170-170].
