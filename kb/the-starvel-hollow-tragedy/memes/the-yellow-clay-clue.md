## Overview

The Piece of Yellow Clay was a crucial physical clue discovered by Inspector French during his examination of the ruins of Starvel Hollow. A small crescent-shaped scrap of yellow clay, found on the wheel of a handcart in the outhouse, led French to a patch of disturbed ground on the moor behind the house, where the body of Markham Giles had been secretly buried.

## The Discovery

French had completed his examination of the Starvel ruins and was about to leave when "the presence of a small scrap of yellow clay which he had observed on entering, but to which he had given no attention, suddenly struck him as being slightly puzzling." The clay was shaped like a half-moon, with the inner edge showing a definite curvature matching a handcart wheel.

French reasoned that the handcart had been "not only wheeled over a place where there was yellow clay, but had been set down there." Critically, French had not noticed any clay of that colour in the neighbourhood of Starvel, leading him to question where the cart had been used.

## The Search

French deduced that the yellow clay must have been picked up recently — otherwise it would have been knocked off on subsequent journeys — and that the source must be close to Starvel. He began "to walk round the house in circles of ever increasing radius, scrutinising the ground for traces of yellow clay," continuing his search until dusk approached.

Just as darkness threatened to end his search, French found what he wanted: "Out on the open moor at the back of the house and at the bottom of a tiny hollow were unmistakable traces of recent digging. The ground over a few square feet was marked with scraps of disturbed yellow clay and cut sods."

## The Exhumation

French returned with police assistance to excavate the site. He showed the officers "the traces of the yellow clay and the cut sods," explaining that "someone has buried something here." The excavation revealed the body of Markham Giles, the tramp whom Roper had murdered to obtain a substitute corpse for the Starvel Hollow fire. This discovery transformed the case from a suspicious fire into a homicide investigation.

## Significance

The yellow clay clue exemplifies Inspector French's meticulous investigative method. While others might have dismissed a small scrap of clay as irrelevant, French's attention to minute physical details and his systematic search of the surrounding area uncovered the evidence that broke the case open. The discovery of Giles' body proved that the Starvel Hollow fire was not an accident but a calculated murder.
