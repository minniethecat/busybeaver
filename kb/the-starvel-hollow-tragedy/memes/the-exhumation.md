## Summary

The exhumation of [[markham-giles|Markham Giles]]' coffin took place on the night of 13 November 1926 at the Thirsby cemetery. Inspector [[joseph-french|French]], having deduced that [[markham-giles|Giles]]' body had been stolen to serve as a substitute for [[john-roper|John Roper]]'s in the [[starvel|Starvel Hollow]] fire, obtained an exhumation order from the Home Office. When the coffin was raised and opened, it contained no human remains — only dark, peaty earth. This dramatic discovery vindicated [[joseph-french|French]]'s entire line of reasoning and exposed the full extent of [[john-roper|Roper]]'s crimes [2149-PN].

## Detailed Account

### French's Deduction

[[joseph-french|French]] first suspected the [[markham-giles|Giles]] connection during a conversation with [[ruth-averill|Ruth Averill]]. When she mentioned that [[markham-giles|Giles]] had died at the same time as the Starvel fire and described him as "a man of middle height and middle age," [[joseph-french|French]] "almost leaped off his seat" — the description matched the third body found in the Starvel ruins, the body presumed to be [[john-roper|Roper]] [1935-PN] [1953-PN].

[[joseph-french|French]] investigated further, confirming with both [[dr-emerson|Dr. Emerson]] and [[dr-philpot|Dr. Philpot]] that [[markham-giles|Giles]]' death certificate had been issued without an autopsy. Both doctors had expected [[markham-giles|Giles]] to die from his heart condition, and neither had questioned the circumstances. As [[joseph-french|French]] observed: "his preconceived idea made him less critical than he might otherwise have been" [2069-PN].

### Obtaining the Order

[[joseph-french|French]] presented his theory to [[major-valentine|the Chief Constable]], who was initially sceptical: "If you were correct it followed that his coffin would be empty. You opened his coffin and it was empty. A more complete vindication of your line of reasoning it would be hard to imagine" [2419-PN]. Despite doubts, [[joseph-french|French]] persuaded the authorities that only physical examination of the coffin could settle the matter [2087-PN] [2089-PN].

### The Night Operation

Two days after the exhumation order arrived, shortly after midnight, a small party assembled at the Thirsby police station and made their way separately to the cemetery. The group consisted of [[joseph-french|Inspector French]], Dr. Laming (the Home Office representative), Sergeant Kent, Colonel Followes (the local magistrate), a sturdy policeman with tools, the cemetery caretaker, and two gravediggers [2129-PN].

The five officials met within the cemetery gates, which the caretaker locked behind them. Led by the caretaker, they crossed the grounds to the northeastern corner where [[markham-giles|Giles]]' grave lay [2133-PN] [2137-PN].

The two gravediggers stretched a tarpaulin on the grass and began removing the sods. They dug steadily through dark soil and then yellow clay for a full hour before a spade struck something hollow with "a dull thud" [2139-PN] [2143-PN].

The coffin, still perfectly sound after only two months underground, was gradually exposed. The men undermined the walls of their excavation and worked the clay from around the sides. Ropes were passed through the handles, and with "a steady pull, the sinister casket came away from the clay beneath and in a few seconds was lying on the grass beside the hole" [2145-PN].

[[joseph-french|French]] held his electric torch to the brass plate and read: "Markham [[markham-giles|Giles]], died 14th September, 1926. Aged 36" [2145-PN].

### The Revelation

The policeman advanced with a screwdriver and began withdrawing the screws. Everyone except the case-hardened Dr. Laming felt a thrill of excitement. [[joseph-french|French]] was particularly anxious: "So much depended for him on what that raised lid would reveal. If he had put all concerned to the trouble and expense of an unnecessary exhumation, it would count against him" [2147-PN].

When the last screw was removed and the lid was raised, the onlookers experienced not horror but "incredulous amazement." There was "no sign of Markham [[markham-giles|Giles]]' body or any other! Instead, the coffin was half-full of dark, peaty earth; and when this earth was sifted nothing was found embedded in it" [2149-PN].

## Significance

The empty coffin was the conclusive [[evidence|proof]] in [[joseph-french|French]]'s investigation. It established three critical facts:

1. **[[john-roper|Roper]] had faked his death** — The body in the Starvel ruins identified as [[john-roper|Roper]] was actually [[markham-giles|Giles]].

2. **The crime was premeditated murder** — [[john-roper|Roper]] had not merely taken advantage of a natural death; he had killed [[markham-giles|Giles]] to obtain the body he needed for his scheme.

3. **[[joseph-french|French]]'s deductive method was vindicated** — The entire chain of reasoning — from the newspaper ash in the safe to the missing banknotes, to the substituted body — was confirmed by physical [[evidence]].

The exhumation also exposed the inadequacy of the original death certification process. Dr. Emerson had issued [[markham-giles|Giles]]' death certificate "without having really taken any trouble to ascertain the cause of death," relying on expectation rather than examination [2077-PN].
