# Edna Philpot

Edna Philpot (née Menzies) was the wife of Dr. [[dr-philpot]] and the victim of a murder staged to look like an accidental fall at their home, Braeside, in Kintilloch, Fifeshire, on 15 May 1921 (022-001). Her death was witnessed by the servant [[flora-roper|Flora Macfarlane]] and her lover [[john-roper]], whose subsequent [[blackmail]] of Dr. Philpot set in motion the chain of events culminating in the [[starvel-hollow-fire]].

## Background

Edna Menzies was the daughter of the manager of a large factory near Dundee, described as a pretty young woman with a vivacious manner who was a general favourite in Kintilloch, particularly among the athletic and sporting sets of the community (022-001). In May 1914 she married Dr. Herbert Philpot, who was third in command on the medical staff of the Ransome Institute, a large mental hospital near Kintilloch (022-001).

The Philpots had no children and lived at Braeside, a small detached house about half a mile from the town (022-001). Their only servant was [[flora-roper|Flora Macfarlane]], a general domestic who had been with them for over three years (022-001).

## The Murder

On the afternoon of 15 May 1921, Dr. Philpot selected a time when he knew his wife would be alone with him in the house, having granted Flora Macfarlane permission to visit her mother (022-017). He tied a dark-brown silk cord across the top of the lower flight of stairs, then called to his wife from the study, shouting that the house was on fire (022-017).

Mrs. Philpot rushed downstairs, caught her foot in the cord, and fell headlong to the hall below. She was stunned but not killed by the fall. Philpot then seized a cricket bat and struck her savagely on the temple, killing her instantaneously (022-017). The blow produced a bruise consistent with an injury from a fall, and no suspicion was initially aroused (022-017).

Unknown to Philpot, the servant Flora had not gone to visit her mother but had met her lover, John Roper, in a nearby copse. When a heavy shower came on, they retreated to the kitchen, from where they heard Mrs. Philpot's scream and witnessed the murder (022-017).

## Aftermath and Public Account

The official account reported in the press described the death as a tragic accident:

> "The little town of Kintilloch, Fife, has been thrown into mourning by the tragic death on Tuesday evening of Mrs. Edna Philpot, wife of Dr. Herbert Philpot... The deceased lady in some way tripped while descending the stairs at her home, falling down the lower flight." (022-002)

Dr. Philpot's public display of anguish and despair was convincing enough that no suspicion of foul play arose at the time (022-003). He telephoned for help, and Dr. Ferguson, the senior medical officer of the Institute, arrived shortly after, followed by Sergeant MacGregor of the local police (022-003). The death was recorded as accidental.

## Significance to the Starvel Hollow Case

Five years later, when [[joseph-french]] investigated the Starvel Hollow fire, he discovered the connection between the Ropers and the Philpot case. His inquiries led him to Dr. Philpot, and eventually to the signed confession that Philpot had written under Roper's [[blackmail|coercion]] (022-009). The confession revealed the true circumstances of Edna Philpot's death:

> "I, Herbert Philpot, doctor of medicine... under compulsion and in the hope of avoiding exposure, hereby remorsefully confess that I am guilty of attempting the death of my wife, Edna Philpot, by arranging that she should meet with an accident, and when this merely rendered her unconscious, of killing her by striking her on the temple with a cricket bat." (022-009)

This confession became a key piece of evidence in French's reconstruction of John Roper's motives and methods, revealing that the blackmail scheme that financed Roper's lifestyle — and ultimately led to the Starvel Hollow fraud and arson — was founded on the witnessed murder of Edna Philpot (022-010).

## Legacy

Edna Philpot's murder, though initially undetected, proved to be the seed from which the entire Starvel Hollow tragedy grew. Roper's use of the witnessed crime as leverage against Philpot established his pattern of exploitation and his belief that he could manipulate others through knowledge of their secrets — a pattern he later attempted to replicate with [[simon-averill]].
