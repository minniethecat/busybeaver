## Summary

The interrogation of Dr. Herbert [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] by Inspector [[joseph-french|French]] unfolded across two stages. Initially, [[joseph-french|French]] confronted [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] about a confession document found in [[john-roper|Roper]]'s safe deposit box — a document in which [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] appeared to confess to murdering his wife Edna [1121-PN]. [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] admitted the confession existed but claimed it was a forgery produced under [[john-roper|Roper]]'s blackmail [1353-PN]. Later, when the full evidence against him was presented, [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] "broke down completely and made a confession which cleared up the few points which from their nature it was impossible that French could have learnt otherwise" [3478-PN].

## Detailed Account

### The Confession Document

[[joseph-french|French]] first encountered the confession while searching [[john-roper|Roper]]'s safe deposit box at the [[metropolitan-safe-deposit-company|Metropolitan Safe Deposit Company]]. The document, written in [[dr-philpot|Philpot]]'s name, confessed to the murder of his wife Edna by striking her on the temple with a cricket bat [1121-PN]. [[joseph-french|French]] immediately questioned whether this was genuine or a forgery: "Was it really a statement of fact, a genuine confession of murder, written by Philpot? If so, how had it fallen into the hands of Roper, and what had the man been keeping it for? Had he been blackmailing Philpot?" [1127-PN].

### Philpot's Account

When [[joseph-french|French]] confronted [[dr-philpot|Philpot]], the doctor admitted the confession existed but claimed [[john-roper|Roper]] had forced him to write it under threat of exposure. [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] revealed that [[john-roper|Roper]] had witnessed Edna Philpot's death — a fatal fall down the stairs at Braeside — and had threatened to report it as murder unless [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] paid him first ten shillings a week, then later two pounds a week [1383-PN].

[[dr-philpot|Philpot]] further claimed he had outwitted [[john-roper|Roper]] by forging his own handwriting: "He wrote the confession out, and then copied it, letter by letter, *upside down*. The result was a passable imitation of his own handwriting, but one which any expert would recognise as a forgery" [3494-PN].

### The Final Confession

It was only after [[joseph-french|French]] had assembled overwhelming evidence — the wedding rings, the exhumation, the safe discovery — that [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] finally admitted the full truth. He was not merely a victim of blackmail; he was [[john-roper|Roper]]'s willing accomplice in the Starvel Hollow murders, driven by [[greed|greed]] and the desire to free himself permanently from [[john-roper|Roper]]'s hold [3502-PN].

## Significance

The interrogation of [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] revealed the deepest layer of the Starvel Hollow conspiracy. What had appeared to be a simple robbery-murder was in fact a dual conspiracy: [[john-roper|Roper]] and [[dr-philpot|Philpot]], each holding damning knowledge of the other's crimes, bound together by [[blackmail|mutual blackmail]] into committing ever-greater atrocities [3512-PN]. The interrogation demonstrated [[joseph-french|French]]'s method of cumulative pressure — using each piece of evidence to extract progressively deeper admissions until the full truth emerged.
