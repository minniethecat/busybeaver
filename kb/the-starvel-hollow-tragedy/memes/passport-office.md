## Overview

The Passport Office is the British government agency responsible for issuing passports. In *The Starvel Hollow Tragedy*, it plays a supporting but significant role in the manhunt for [[john-roper|John Roper]]: Inspector French requests that Scotland Yard send someone to the Passport Office to obtain a copy of the passport photograph Roper had lodged when applying for a passport to Brazil [016-006]. This photograph is then published in the *Police Gazette* alongside Roper's description, enabling nationwide identification [016-008].

## Role in the Investigation

The Passport Office becomes relevant during the final phase of French's investigation, when he is hunting for the fugitive John Roper. The Kintilloch sergeant had earlier mentioned that Roper applied for a passport to Brazil, suggesting he planned to flee abroad [016-006].

French writes to Scotland Yard, requesting that an officer visit the Passport Office to secure a copy of Roper's passport photograph. This photograph is crucial evidence — it provides a verified likeness of the wanted man, far more reliable than verbal descriptions alone.

Once obtained, French sends the photograph to the Yard with instructions to publish it in the next issue of the *Police Gazette*, along with a detailed physical description he has compiled from interviews with Oxley, Whymper, Ruth, and others [016-008]. The *Police Gazette* circulates to every police force in the country, meaning that within three or four days every officer in Britain would be on the lookout for Roper.

French also requests systematic inquiries at seaports and steamship lines, focusing on routes to Brazil, to determine whether Roper had already left the country [016-009]. The Passport Office photograph thus serves as the linchpin of a coordinated nationwide manhunt.
