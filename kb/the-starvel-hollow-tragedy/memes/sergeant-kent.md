## Profile

Sergeant Kent is a police officer with the Thirsby Constabulary and one of the first officials on the scene after the Starvel fire. He is described as "a tall, rather brusque man" [164-164] who, in the presence of [[oxley|Mr. Oxley]], "was polite enough" [164-164]. As the local investigating officer, Kent shoulders significant responsibility in the early stages of the case, managing the initial response, [[the-inquest|the inquest]] proceedings, and the first witness interviews.

## Role in the Narrative

Sergeant Kent is the first police officer to respond to [[starvel-hollow-fire|the Starvel tragedy]]. After the baker discovers the smoldering ruins, Kent leads his men to the scene alongside [[oxley|Mr. Oxley]], [[dr-emerson|Dr. Emerson]], and other townspeople [154-154]. He quickly takes charge of the official response.

Kent's most prominent role is at [[the-inquest|the inquest]], where he serves as [[dr-lonsdale|the coroner]]'s officer. He calls the jurors by name — opening the proceedings by shouting "Robert Judd!" so suddenly that [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] jumps [204-204] — and administers the oath to each witness [204-204]. He shepherds the jury to view the remains and manages the court's practical operations throughout the hearing [206-206].

Beyond his procedural duties, Kent conducts the initial witness interviews. On the evening after the fire, he calls on [[ruth-averill|Ruth Averill]] at the Oxleys' home to question her about the household and its personnel, though "she had nothing to tell him which could throw any light on the tragedy" [164-164]. He also warns [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] that she will be required to give [[evidence]] at [[the-inquest|the inquest]] [200-200].

When [[major-valentine|Chief Constable Valentine]] later concludes that the case is "rather outside the local men's capacity" [202-202] and applies to [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] for assistance, the investigation passes to [[joseph-french|Inspector French]], and Kent's role recedes.

## Relationships

- **[[joseph-french|Inspector French]]**: Kent's superior once Scotland Yard takes over the investigation. Kent's local knowledge presumably assists [[joseph-french|French]]'s inquiries.
- **[[major-valentine|Major Valentine]]**: Kent's Chief Constable, who ultimately judges the case beyond local resources.
- **[[ruth-averill|Ruth Averill]]**: Kent questions [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] as a key witness, though their interaction is brief and formal.

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter I**: Responds to the Starvel fire with his men [154-154]. Visits [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] to question her about the household [164-164].
- **Chapter II (The Inquest)**: Serves as coroner's officer, managing jurors, witnesses, and court procedure [200-200].
- **Later chapters**: Local police role diminishes as Scotland Yard takes over the investigation.
