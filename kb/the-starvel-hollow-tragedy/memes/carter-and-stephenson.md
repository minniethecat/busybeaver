## Overview

Carter & Stephenson is a firm of safe manufacturers based in Leeds, makers of safe number 12,473 — the safe installed at Starvel Hollow in which Simon Averill kept his cash, including the £1,400 in bank notes that were supposedly destroyed in the fire [ch04]. The firm's expert testimony about the fireproof capabilities of their safes provides the key technical evidence that transforms the Starvel Hollow case from a tragic accident into a criminal investigation.

## Role in the Investigation

**The sergeant's inquiry**: Sergeant Kent, directed by the Chief Constable to investigate the safe, travels to Leeds to interview the makers. Carter & Stephenson confirm that safe 12,473, though twenty years old, was "the best fireproof safe of its day." Critically, they state that bank notes inside it "wouldn't scarcely be browned, not no matter how fierce the fire might be" [ch04]. This expert opinion directly contradicts the fire explanation: if the notes in Averill's safe could not have been destroyed by fire, then their absence means they were stolen.

**French's verification**: [[joseph-french|Inspector French]], after taking over the case, visits Carter & Stephenson's premises on the Briggate in Leeds to verify the sergeant's findings firsthand [ch05]. Introducing himself "in the strictest confidence in his true guise," French asks Mr. Stephenson directly: was the safe absolutely fireproof? The confirmation he receives provides the technical foundation for the entire criminal case.

The firm's testimony is arguably the single most important piece of evidence in the early investigation. Without it, the missing notes could be attributed to the fire, and the possibility of arson, robbery, and murder might never have been seriously considered.
