# Verdict

The **verdict** is a formal legal determination rendered by a coroner's jury in *The Starvel Hollow Tragedy*. The novel features two inquests whose contrasting verdicts — from "accidental death" to "wilful murder" — trace the arc of Inspector French's investigation from initial acceptance to final exposure of the truth.

## The First Inquest: Accidental Death

At the initial inquest into the Starvel Hollow fire, the coroner directs the jury that there is no evidence implicating Roper's drunkenness in the cause of the fire. He suggests that "the three unfortunate people had been burnt in their beds" and "suffocated by the smoke while asleep." [306-306] Following this direction, "without leaving the box the jury found as the coroner directed, the verdict was entered on the records and signed, and the inquest was over." [308-308]

French later reconstructs the calculation behind this outcome: "The coroner's court had brought in a verdict of accidental death. If Tarkington had not kept the numbers of the notes sent out to Averill and advised his headquarters that those notes had been destroyed, no doubts would ever have arisen." [1445-1445] The verdict of accidental death would have closed the case permanently but for the banking system's record-keeping.

## The Second Inquest: Wilful Murder

The adjourned inquest into Dr. Philpot's death yields a fundamentally different outcome. The coroner directs the jury to consider that the fatal syringe "had not been found. Who then had removed it and why?" [2773-2773] He instructs them that if they reject the possibility of error, "they would return a verdict of wilful murder."

After nearly an hour of deliberation, the jury delivers "the expected verdict of wilful murder against some person or persons unknown." [2775-2775] This finding represents the culmination of French's case and the formal legal acknowledgment that the deaths at the center of the novel were not accidental.
