## Overview

The Northern Shires Bank is a fictional financial institution headquartered in Throgmorton Avenue, London. It serves as the clearing bank for the Thirsby branch where Mr. Tarkington manages the accounts of Simon Averill and other local clients. The bank's headquarters plays a critical role in the investigation when it reports that one of the numbered £20 notes from Averill's destroyed safe has been paid in — the discovery that transforms the Starvel Hollow case from an accident into a criminal investigation [ch05].

## Role in the Investigation

The Northern Shires Bank becomes central to the investigation in two stages:

**The initial alert**: Five weeks after the Starvel fire, the bank's headquarters contacts Mr. Tarkington to inform him that a £20 note bearing a number from Averill's safe list has been paid in [ch05]. Since it was believed that all the notes were destroyed in the fire, this discovery proves that at least one note survived — meaning the fire was arson to cover a theft. Mr. Tarkington immediately informs Mr. Oxley, who contacts Scotland Yard, bringing [[joseph-french|Inspector French]] into the case.

**The second discovery**: Later in the investigation, the bank manager telephones Scotland Yard to report that two additional £20 notes from the Starvel list have been deposited that afternoon [ch16]. The cashier discovered the match during end-of-day balancing but could not recall which customer deposited them. [[sergeant-kent|Sergeant Kent]] takes over the inquiry in French's absence, compiling a list of nearly two hundred possible depositors and narrowing it down by eliminating businesses unlikely to handle cash from the public.

The bank's role in tracking the serial numbers of the missing notes provides the crucial evidence that exposes the Starvel Hollow fire as arson, robbery, and ultimately murder. Without the bank's vigilant monitoring, the crime might have gone undetected.
