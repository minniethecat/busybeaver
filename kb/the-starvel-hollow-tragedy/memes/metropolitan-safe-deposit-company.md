## Overview

The Metropolitan Safe Deposit Co., Ltd., located at 25b King William Street in the City of London, is the company where John Roper rented a small safe in their strongrooms. The discovery of this safe deposit box — and its contents — opens a crucial new dimension in [[joseph-french|Inspector French]]'s investigation by revealing Roper's secret activities and his connection to the blackmail scheme [ch09].

## Role in the Investigation

The safe deposit company enters the case when a letter arrives at Starvel addressed to the late John Roper. The letter, from the Metropolitan Safe Deposit Company, reminds the deceased that his safe rental of thirty shillings is now due and requests either payment or instructions for disposal of its contents [ch09].

French considers it "a rather unusual thing that a man in Roper's position should require the services of a safe deposit company" and feels "a certain curiosity regarding the object which required such careful guarding" [ch09]. He visits the King William Street premises and, acting on behalf of Scotland Yard, takes charge of the safe's contents as Roper has no known heirs.

Inside the safe, French discovers a forged confession purportedly written by [[dr-philpot|Dr. Philpot]], along with photographs used for blackmail [ch09]. The confession, though later proven to be a forgery created by Roper himself, reveals the blackmail scheme at the heart of the case: Roper had been extorting money from Philpot over the doctor's affair with a nurse. The photographs found alongside the forged confession provide corroborating evidence of the blackmail operation.

The discovery of the safe deposit box's contents is a pivotal moment in the investigation. It transforms Roper from a victim of the Starvel fire into the central suspect, revealing him as the "evil genius" behind the entire criminal scheme — the theft of Averill's notes, the murder, and the arson [ch16].
