## Overview

The death of Edna Philpot was a staged domestic accident at Braeside, the Philpot family home in Kintilloch, Fife, in May 1921. Dr. Herbert Philpot arranged for his wife to fall down the stairs, resulting in fatal injuries. The death was initially accepted by authorities as accidental, but Inspector French's investigation five years later uncovered it as attempted murder, motivated by Philpot's desire to be with another woman.

## Circumstances of the Death

On the afternoon of 15 May 1921, at approximately 5:30 p.m., Edna Philpot fell down the stairs at Braeside. Dr. Philpot claimed he was writing letters in his study when he heard a sudden scream and the noise of a body falling. He rushed out to find Mrs. Philpot lying unconscious at the bottom of the lower flight of stairs. A large swelling on the back of her head indicated severe trauma.

The maid, Flora Macfarlane, had left the house shortly before the incident to visit her ailing mother who lived nearby. She had obtained permission for this brief absence, which conveniently provided Dr. Philpot with the opportunity to stage the accident without witnesses.

## Investigation

Inspector French, while investigating the Starvel Hollow fire of 1926, uncovered Dr. Philpot's confession admitting to "attempting the death of my wife, Edna Philpot, by arranging that she should fall down the stairs at our home" (Philpot's written confession, 15 May 1921). French subsequently verified the story by consulting the files of *The Scotsman* newspaper for May 1921, which reported on the tragic accident at Kintilloch.

French noted that falling down stairs was an uncommon type of fatal accident, which contributed to his suspicion. He also learned that Flora Macfarlane, the Philpots' maid who had nearly witnessed the death, married John Roper approximately one month after Mrs. Philpot's death — a connection that later proved significant to the blackmail scheme.

## Role in the Blackmail Scheme

John Roper, who knew the truth about Edna Philpot's death through his wife Flora (the former maid), used this knowledge to systematically blackmail Dr. Philpot. The blackmail formed the underlying motivation for the events leading to the Starvel Hollow fire, in which Roper, Philpot, and others perished. During French's interrogation, Philpot was visibly nervous, fearing that if he assisted in Roper's capture, Roper would expose his role in his wife's death.

## Significance

Edna Philpot's death represents the earliest crime in the novel's timeline and serves as the moral fulcrum of the entire plot. It established Roper's leverage over Philpot, initiated the chain of blackmail that eventually consumed multiple lives, and provided Inspector French with the key insight that unraveled the entire conspiracy behind the Starvel Hollow tragedy.
