# Flora Roper

Flora Roper (née Macfarlane) was the wife of [[john-roper]] and, alongside her husband, a servant in the household of [[simon-averill]] at [[starvel]]. Before her marriage, she worked as a general servant for Dr. and Mrs. [[dr-philpot]] at Braeside, Kintilloch, where she became an accidental witness to the murder of [[edna-philpot]].

## Early Life as Flora Macfarlane

Flora Macfarlane lived near Kintilloch in Fifeshire and was employed as the sole domestic servant at Braeside, the home of Dr. Herbert Philpot and his wife Edna (022-001). She had been with the Philpots for over three years at the time of Mrs. Philpot's death and was regarded locally as an efficient servant, though also known as "ay one for the lads" (022-001).

## Witness to Murder

On the afternoon of 15 May 1921, Flora obtained permission to visit her ailing mother, expecting to be absent for about half an hour (022-003). However, her real purpose was to meet her lover, [[john-roper]], in a nearby copse (022-017). When a heavy shower interrupted their meeting, Flora proposed they take shelter in the Philpots' basement kitchen (022-017).

From the kitchen, they heard Dr. Philpot shout to his wife, followed by Mrs. Philpot's scream and the crash of her fall (022-017). Flora ran up the back stairs to the hall, where she was transfixed by the sight of Philpot delivering the fatal blow with a cricket bat (022-017). Roper, who had followed her, restrained her from revealing herself, and both witnessed Philpot's subsequent attempts to stage the death as an accident (022-017).

When Philpot came downstairs and discovered Roper and Flora, they confronted him with what they had seen (022-018). Roper negotiated for a weekly payment of forty shillings — four times his existing wage — in exchange for their silence, along with a signed confession from Philpot (022-019).

## Marriage and Service at Starvel

Flora married John Roper approximately one month after Mrs. Philpot's death (022-010). The couple later entered the service of [[simon-averill]] at Starvel, on the recommendation of Dr. [[dr-philpot]], who described them as capable servants. Both Flora and John were considered good servants by Averill.

## Death in the Starvel Hollow Fire

On the night of 15 September 1926, Flora Roper died alongside her husband John and Simon Averill in the catastrophic fire that destroyed Starvel (001-002). Her remains, along with those of the other victims, were found in the debris of the burned house. The inquest later recorded her death as resulting from "a fire at Starvel, the cause of which there was no evidence to show" (005-007).

## Role in the Investigation

The connection between Flora Roper and the Philpot case became a crucial link in [[joseph-french]]'s investigation of the Starvel Hollow fire (022-010). French's discovery that the Starvel servant John Roper's wife was the same Flora Macfarlane who had served the Philpots connected the two crimes and ultimately led him to uncover the full extent of Roper's [[blackmail]] scheme and his motive for the [[fraud]]-[[arson]] plot at Starvel.
