## Profile

Mr. Tarkington is the manager of the bank in [[thirsby|Thirsby]] where [[simon-averill|Simon Averill]] kept his accounts. He is described as "a tall very thin man with the little moustache and the bald head" [202-202], with a "thin hawk-like" face [162-162] and "thin, measured tones" [202-202]. A meticulous and observant professional, Tarkington plays a crucial role in uncovering the financial mystery at the heart of the plot.

## Role in the Narrative

Tarkington's significance emerges from his intimate knowledge of Simon Averill's financial affairs. As the bank manager who handled Averill's accounts, he is uniquely positioned to reveal the startling truth: that Averill kept his entire fortune — estimated at between thirty and forty thousand pounds — not in securities but in cash, stored in a safe at Starvel [170-170].

This revelation transforms the nature of the investigation. Tarkington confides in his friend [[oxley|Mr. Oxley]] about the arrangement, and later informs him that "practically the whole sum was in [[simon-averill|Mr. Averill]]'s safe" [170-170]. When the safe is found to have been fire[[evidence|proof]], the implication is clear: the money was not destroyed in the fire but stolen — making [[starvel-hollow-fire|the Starvel tragedy]] a case of murder and theft rather than an accident.

Tarkington is present at [[the-inquest|the inquest]], observing the proceedings from the public gallery alongside other Thirsby townspeople [202-202]. His thin, serious expression throughout the proceedings reflects the gravity of what he knows. He later calls on Oxley with "a serious and mystified expression" to discuss the implications of his discovery [202-202], setting in motion the chain of events that leads to [[joseph-french|Inspector French]]'s involvement.

## Relationships

- **[[oxley|Mr. Oxley]]**: Close friend and professional associate. Tarkington shares his confidential knowledge about Averill's finances with Oxley, and the two men collaborate to understand the full picture.
- **[[simon-averill|Simon Averill]]**: Tarkington served as Averill's bank manager, aware of the unusual arrangement of keeping such a large sum in cash.
- **[[ruth-averill|Ruth Averill]]**: Tarkington knows [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] through his connection to Averill's affairs. The loss of the fortune directly impacts her inheritance.
