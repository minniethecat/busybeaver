## Overview

The *Police Gazette* is the official publication of British police forces, circulated to every police officer in the country. In *The Starvel Hollow Tragedy*, Inspector French uses the *Police Gazette* as a key tool in the nationwide manhunt for John Roper, publishing the fugitive's description and photograph to enable identification across all police jurisdictions.

## Role in the Investigation

After compiling a detailed physical description of John Roper through persistent questioning of Oxley, Whymper, Ruth Averill, and other witnesses, French is satisfied with the result. The description, more complete than usually obtainable from unofficial sources, includes Roper's height (about 5 ft. 9 inches), slight build, dark hair, dark eyes with a decided squint, heavy dark eyebrows, clean-shaven sallow complexion, small nose and mouth, pointed chin, small hands and feet, and a slight stoop with a quick step, speaking with "a slight Lowland Scotch accent" [016-021].

French sends this description to Scotland Yard, "asking that it be published in the next issue of the *Police Gazette* along with a copy of the photograph obtained from the Passport Office" [016-021]. The practical implication is significant: "within three or four days every police officer in the land would be applying it to newcomers of less than ten weeks' standing" [016-021].

The *Police Gazette* publication forms part of a coordinated strategy. Alongside the gazette notice, French requests systematic inquiries at seaports and from steamship lines to determine whether Roper has already left the country, concentrating on routes to Brazil [016-022]. The *Police Gazette* thus serves as the domestic arm of a two-pronged manhunt — covering both those still within Britain and those attempting to flee.
