# Opportunity

**Opportunity** functions in the [[starvel-hollow-fire|Starvel Hollow tragedy]] in two distinct senses: as a formal element of criminal investigation alongside means and [[motive]], and as a recurring narrative device through which characters seize critical moments to advance their aims.

## Investigative Significance

As [[joseph-french|Inspector French]] pursues his inquiry, the question of who had the opportunity to commit each element of [[john-roper|Roper]]'s scheme becomes central. After learning of Roper's medical training, French realises that Roper alone possessed the combination of knowledge and proximity to execute the murders:

> Here was food for thought and opportunity for inquiry. Who had disappeared about the time of the tragedy? Was anyone missing in the neighbourhood? Had anyone let it be known that he was leaving the district or going abroad about that date? [1859-PN]

The scope of the investigation opens dramatically as French confronts the scale of inquiry required to trace every individual who might have had opportunity to participate in the crime [1859-PN].

## French's Tactical Use of Opportunity

French himself exploits opportunity as a tactical tool. When he plants a misleading newspaper story designed to draw Roper into the open, he seizes the moment:

> This was French's opportunity. Speaking respectfully and with an air of the utmost candour, he told very nearly the truth. Deliberately he slightly coloured the facts, coloured them with the object and in the hope that somewhere Roper would read what he had said — and be deceived into coming into the open. [2629-PN]

Similarly, French uses a lull in the investigation to conduct solitary fieldwork, recognising that "for his explorations no time more propitious than the present was likely to offer" [1419-PN].

## The Interrogation Opportunity

In his confrontation with [[pierce-whymper|Whymper]], French weaponises the offer of opportunity as an interrogation technique:

> I wish, therefore, to give you an opportunity of explaining the matters which seem to incriminate you. I would like to ask you a number of questions, but before I do so I must warn you that if your answers are unsatisfactory I must arrest you. [982-PN]

## Ruth's Escape

For [[ruth-averill]], the invitation to visit the Palmer-Gores in York represents a personal opportunity to escape [[starvel|Starvel Hollow]]:

> She determined she would use the opportunity of this visit to find out what her chances would be of getting some job by means of which she could support herself, so that she might never be forced to return to Starvel or see any of its inhabitants again. [80-PN]
