## Profile

Dr. Herbert Philpot is a young physician practising in [[thirsby|Thirsby]] who served as [[simon-averill|Simon Averill]]'s usual medical attendant. He is described as "a youngish man who had come to the place some three or four years earlier" and who "had already built up a reputation for care and skill" [90-90]. Despite his professional competence and pleasant manner, Dr. Philpot harbours a destructive secret: he is a compulsive gambler whose habits have brought him to the brink of financial ruin.

## Role in the Narrative

Dr. Philpot is first mentioned early in the novel when [[ruth-averill|Ruth Averill]] wishes to have him examine her ailing uncle. However, Philpot himself is confined to bed with influenza, forcing the household to consider calling [[dr-emerson|Dr. Emerson]] as a substitute [88-90]. His illness conveniently provides him with an [[alibi]] during the night of the Starvel fire.

As [[joseph-french|Inspector French]]'s investigation deepens, Philpot emerges as a figure of considerable interest. The landlord of the Thirsdale Arms reveals that despite Philpot's professional skill and likeable personality, "he had made a muck of things" owing to "systematic gambling" — chiefly on horses [614-614]. This financial vulnerability makes him susceptible to exploitation.

A pivotal discovery occurs when [[joseph-french|French]], searching [[john-roper|John Roper]]'s belongings, uncovers a handwritten confession signed by "Herbert Philpot" in which the doctor admits to murdering his wife, Edna Philpot, by striking her with a cricket bat after a staged accident failed to kill her [1121-1123]. This document raises the possibility that [[john-roper|Roper]] had been blackmailing Philpot — and that Philpot might therefore have a [[motive]] for wanting [[john-roper|Roper]] dead. [[joseph-french|French]] ultimately determines that while the confession is genuine, there is insufficient [[evidence]] linking Philpot to [[starvel-hollow-fire|the Starvel crime]] itself [1401-1401].

## Relationships

- **[[dr-emerson|Dr. Emerson]]**: The senior practitioner in Thirsby who had effectively retired in Philpot's favour. Emerson speaks well of Philpot at [[the-inquest|the inquest]] [286-290].
- **[[simon-averill|Simon Averill]]**: Philpot's patient for approximately four years, having taken over Averill's care from Dr. Emerson.
- **[[john-roper|John Roper]]**: The connection between the two men — represented by the confession document found in [[john-roper|Roper]]'s possession — forms one of the novel's most intriguing subplots.

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter I**: Mentioned as ill with influenza, unable to attend Simon Averill [88-90].
- **Chapter II**: Dr. Emerson testifies at [[the-inquest|the inquest]] on Philpot's behalf [286-290].
- **Mid-investigation**: [[joseph-french|French]] visits Philpot to obtain his impressions of the Starvel household [734-734].
- **Later chapters**: The discovery of Philpot's confession in [[john-roper|Roper]]'s effects creates a major investigative lead [1121-1131].
