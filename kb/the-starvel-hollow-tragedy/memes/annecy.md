## Description

Annecy is a town in the Haute-Savoie department of southeastern France, situated at the northern end of Lac d'Annecy. [[joseph-french|French]] reaches it by a long railway journey: Victoria to Paris (Gare de Lyon), then the day-long express across the great central plain of France, past Dijon, Bourg, Amberieu, through the foothills of the Alps to Culoz and Aix, and finally a little branch line completing the journey (013-014).

The Hotel Splendide, where both [[pierce-whymper|Whymper]] and [[joseph-french|French]] stayed, stands on a wide street looking out across an arm of the lake. [[joseph-french|French]] describes scores of little boats at the steps along the road, and on the opposite side of the water a great building — the theatre — with a park behind it (013-014). The town is connected to [[talloires|Talloires]] by a steamer service; all the steamers call at [[talloires|Talloires]], making the journey a convenient excursion.

## Significance in the Novel

Annecy is the base from which [[joseph-french|French]] conducts his Continental investigation into [[pierce-whymper|Whymper]]'s trip to France. After exhausting leads in Thirsby, [[joseph-french|French]] decides "a visit to Annecy was his only remaining move" — a journey that would either confirm or destroy [[pierce-whymper|Whymper]]'s story about searching for Prosper Giraud and Madeleine Blancquart (013-014).

The town serves as a staging post: [[joseph-french|French]] stays at the Hotel Splendide, just as [[pierce-whymper|Whymper]] had before him, and uses it as his base while taking the steamer to [[talloires|Talloires]]. At Annecy, [[joseph-french|French]] questions the hotel manager about [[pierce-whymper|Whymper]]'s stay, learning that [[talloires|Talloires]] is "a picturesque spot" with three hotels (013-014).

Crucially, the police at Annecy had also received inquiries from [[pierce-whymper|Whymper]] — he had not only searched [[talloires|Talloires]] but had involved the Annecy authorities, offering a 5,000 franc reward for information about the Giraud/Blancquart couple (015-016). This detail becomes central to [[joseph-french|French]]'s eventual conclusion that [[john-roper|Roper]]'s entire story was fabricated: [[pierce-whymper|Whymper]]'s extensive and genuine-seeming inquiries in the Annecy region initially appeared to corroborate his account, but the complete absence of any trace of the supposed residents pointed toward the story being a fiction devised by [[john-roper|Roper]] (020-023).

## Real-World Context

Annecy is a real town in Haute-Savoie, France, and a well-known tourist destination for its medieval old town and alpine lake setting. The Hotel Splendide was a genuine grand hotel of the era. The railway journey that Crofts describes — Victoria–Paris–Dijon–Bourg–Amberieu–Culoz–Aix–Annecy — is geographically accurate, reflecting the PLM (Paris-Lyon-Méditerranée) railway network of the 1920s. The steamer service on Lac d'Annecy remains in operation today. Crofts's attention to railway detail is characteristic of his work; the precise route, changes, and timings reinforce the novel's documentary realism (013-014).
