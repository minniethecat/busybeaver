## Overview

Messrs. Thos. Cook & Son is the real-world travel agency whose London office on Regent Street features prominently in the investigation. Pierce Whymper used their services to book a second-class return journey from London to Talloires, near Annecy in Savoy, France — including train tickets, meal coupons, and travel advice [ch06]. The firm's transaction records become crucial evidence when it is discovered that Whymper paid for his travel arrangements with one of the stolen £20 notes from Averill's safe.

## Role in the Investigation

The Cook's office enters the investigation when [[joseph-french|Inspector French]], tracing the £20 note paid into the Northern Shires Bank, visits their Regent Street premises. The manager, Mr. Bankes, cooperates fully, and within moments "the whole of the transactions between Mr. Pierce Whymper of the one part and Messrs. Thos. Cook & Son of the other part, stood revealed" [ch06].

The records show that Whymper first inquired about the trip on 18th September (the day of [[the-inquest|the inquest]] at Thirsby), received a reply on 21st September, and later purchased the tickets, calling to collect them on 6th October [ch06]. The receipt made out to Whymper for £16 8s. 4d. provides the documentary link between Whymper and the stolen note.

When confronted, Whymper initially denies knowing the note's provenance, claiming he received it as payment from a client. French's extended interrogation — first at Cook's office, then at Whymper's lodging — eventually extracts the truth: Whymper received the note from John Roper, given to him to establish an alibi by being out of the country when the Starvel fire occurred [ch06].
