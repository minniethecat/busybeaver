## Overview

The Criminal Investigation Department (CID) is the detective branch of the Metropolitan Police, operating from New Scotland Yard in London. Inspector [[joseph-french|Joseph French]] is a detective inspector in the CID [003-045]. The department is the organisational home from which French is dispatched to investigate the Starvel Hollow case, and it provides the institutional backing for his inquiries throughout the novel.

## Role in the Investigation

The CID's involvement in the Starvel Hollow case begins when the ripples of Mr. Tarkington's discovery reach "the grim fastness of the Criminal Investigation Department of New Scotland Yard" [003-045]. French's telephone rings, summoning him to his superior's office — the moment that launches the entire investigation.

French identifies himself as "an inspector in the Criminal Investigation Department of New Scotland Yard" when conducting confidential inquiries, notably during his interview with a witness from whom he needs sensitive information [005-072]. The CID affiliation carries the authority of the Metropolitan Police and enables French to operate across jurisdictions.

Throughout the investigation, French relies on CID resources: sending requests through the Yard to the [[passport-office|Passport Office]], arranging for *Police Gazette* publications, coordinating nationwide searches, and drawing on the "amazingly complete and far-reaching organisation of the police" [016-005].

## Relationship to Other Entities

The CID is organisationally part of Scotland Yard, which serves as the headquarters building and broader institutional identity. While [[scotland-yard]] represents the institution as a whole, the CID specifically refers to the detective division in which French serves.
