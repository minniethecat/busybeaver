## Overview

The Peveril Hotel is a hotel in Russell Square, Bloomsbury, London. In *The Starvel Hollow Tragedy*, it is the establishment where John Roper spent his final night in London before fleeing abroad, making it a key location in Inspector French's manhunt.

## Role in the Investigation

The Peveril Hotel marks the critical breakthrough in Inspector French's search for John Roper. After tracing Roper to the [[strand-palace-hotel|Strand Palace Hotel]] on the Tuesday night, French deploys officers to canvass hotels across London. Sergeant Elliott, working the Bloomsbury area, telephones the Yard with the news: "Roper, the man reported, had spent the Thursday night at the Peveril Hotel in Russell Square" [018-018].

Within twenty minutes, French reaches the building. Elliott briefs him: Roper arrived about 7:15 p.m. on Thursday evening, asked for a room, and — claiming a chill — had a fire lit and dined in his room [018-018]. The next morning he paid his bill to the waiter and left about 9:45, walking out with a small suitcase [018-018]. He did not take a taxi from the hotel.

The Peveril Hotel also provides crucial documentary evidence. When French later examines the signatures of "Arthur Lisle Whitman" at [[dashwood-and-munce]], he compares them against the handwriting in the Peveril register. The comparison strengthens his conviction: "At first sight the signatures were not so obviously Roper's as those in the Peveril register, but as French examined them he felt more and more satisfied that the man had indeed written them" [018-072]. The hotel register thus serves as a forensic benchmark — the handwriting sample against which Roper's disguised signatures can be measured.

The Peveril Hotel is the last confirmed London location for Roper before his escape. From Russell Square, the trail leads French north to Edinburgh, and ultimately to the resolution of the Starvel Hollow case.
