## Profile

Simon Averill is the owner of Starvel, an old house situated in a hollow on the Yorkshire moors. He is an elderly invalid, querulous and solitary, whose defining characteristic is his [[miser]]ly obsession with accumulating money. His passion takes the concrete form of collecting coins and banknotes and [[miser|hoarding]] them in his safe [001-010].

He is [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] Averill's uncle and guardian, having taken her in as an orphaned child. Despite providing her with shelter and subsistence, he shows no affection toward her. He requires her to read to him once daily but otherwise makes no attempt to cultivate their relationship [001-010]. His health is in decline at the novel's opening, and he is described as "ailing" and looking "very ill" before [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] departs for York.

## Role in the Narrative

Simon Averill is the central figure around whom the mystery revolves — his death in the Starvel fire is the novel's inciting catastrophe. His character as a [[miser]] who hoards physical currency in his safe establishes both [[motive]] and method for the crime that may have occurred.

In an uncharacteristic act of generosity, Averill gives [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] permission to visit York and provides her with ten pounds for expenses [001-016] [001-026]. This unusual behavior — [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] calls it "an absolute miracle" — may be narratively significant: it ensures [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] is absent when the fire occurs. Whether this was coincidental or arranged is a question the investigation must address.

Averill's [[miser]]ly nature is established through multiple details: he lives in a dilapidated house with minimal staff, his niece wears threadbare clothes three years out of fashion, and his sole passion is the physical accumulation of money in his safe. This characterization makes the destruction of Starvel by fire — and the presumed destruction of his hoard — deeply ironic.

His declining health at the time of the fire raises questions about whether he could have escaped. The attending physician, Dr. Philpot, was himself ill with influenza and unavailable, adding a layer of tragic circumstance to Averill's death [ch01].

## Relationships

- **[[ruth-averill]]**: Niece and ward. Averill provides for her basic needs but offers no emotional connection. His unexpected gift of ten pounds for her York trip is the novel's first mystery-in-miniature.

- **[[john-roper]]**: Attendant and servant, along with his wife Flora. The [[john-roper|Roper]]s replaced the previous elderly servants. Averill communicates with [[ruth-averill|Ruth]] through [[john-roper|Roper]], maintaining his isolation [001-016].

- **[[markham-giles]]**: Son of an old friend of Averill's. Averill offered [[markham-giles|Giles]] a cottage belonging to Starvel at a nominal rent, suggesting some residual loyalty to past friendships despite his otherwise [[miser]]ly nature.

## Key Appearances

- Simon Averill is established through others' descriptions in Chapter 1; his direct appearances are minimal, consistent with his reclusive nature
- His body is among the three recovered from the Starvel ruins
- His will, drawn up by Mr. Oxley, becomes relevant to [[ruth-averill|Ruth]]'s future and potentially to the investigation's [[motive]] analysis
