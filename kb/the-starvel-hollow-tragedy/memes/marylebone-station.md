## Overview

Marylebone Station is a central London railway terminus. In *The Starvel Hollow Tragedy*, it appears in the testimony of a taximan who drove John Roper during his final days in London, marking the point where Roper disappeared after a morning of visiting jewellers.

## Role in the Investigation

During Inspector French's intensive search for John Roper, taxi drivers are identified as key witnesses. Two taximen come forward within ten minutes of each other, having been traced by French's officers.

The first driver provides a detailed account. He had been hailed near Marble Arch about 10:00 a.m. on the Tuesday of the previous week by a man matching Roper's description. The fare had explained that "he was a traveller in precious stones and he wished to be taken to certain jewellers of which he had a list" [018-025]. The taximan spent the entire morning driving from one jeweller to another across London.

Shortly after one o'clock, "the fare had instructed him to drive to Marylebone Station, which was then close by. There the taxi had been paid off, the fare disappearing into the station" [018-025].

French immediately despatches Sergeant Carter to retrace the route with the driver, visiting each jeweller to gather further evidence about Roper's activities: what he was selling, what he was asking, and any other details that might help track his movements.

The Marylebone Station sighting is one of several transport-hub appearances that help French piece together Roper's pattern of movement during his final days in London — using stations to break his trail between different parts of the city.
