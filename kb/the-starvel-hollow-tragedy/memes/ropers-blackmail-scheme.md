## Summary

[[john-roper|John Roper]]'s blackmail of Dr. Herbert [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] was the dark engine driving the events leading to the [[starvel-hollow-fire|Starvel Hollow tragedy]]. Beginning in 1920 at the [[ransome-institute|Ransome Institute]] in [[kintilloch|Kintilloch]], [[john-roper|Roper]] first extorted ten shillings a week from [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] over the doctor's affair with a nurse [1353-PN]. After Edna Philpot's death in 1921, the blackmail escalated to two pounds a week — [[john-roper|Roper]] threatening to reveal evidence that made the accidental fall appear to be murder [1383-PN]. The scheme lasted six years, only ending when [[john-roper|Roper]] disguised his cessation of blackmail as a "reformation" — part of his larger plan to rob Averill at Starvel [1601-PN].

## Detailed Account

### The Nurse Affair

The scheme began in October 1920 at the [[ransome-institute|Ransome Institute]], where both [[john-roper|Roper]] and [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] worked — [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] as a junior assistant doctor, [[john-roper|Roper]] as a male attendant. [[john-roper|Roper]] and his future wife Flora witnessed [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] in a compromising situation with a nurse in the Institute grounds. [[john-roper|Roper]] immediately recognized the opportunity: he forced [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] to write a signed confession of "unseemly conduct" and began demanding ten shillings a week for his silence [1353-PN].

### Escalation After Edna's Death

In May 1921, Edna Philpot died from a fall down the stairs at Braeside, the Philpots' house in [[kintilloch|Kintilloch]]. Flora [[flora-roper|Roper]] — the Philpots' servant before her marriage to [[john-roper|Roper]] — witnessed the aftermath. [[john-roper|Roper]] used her account to construct a murder accusation: he claimed Flora had seen [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] strike his wife with a cricket bat [1121-PN].

[[john-roper|Roper]] immediately escalated his demands: "If Philpot would increase his ten shillings a week to forty, thus enabling Roper and Flora to marry in comfort, the evidence against him would be withheld" [3492-PN]. To secure his hold, [[john-roper|Roper]] demanded a signed murder confession — a document he stored in a safe deposit box at the [[metropolitan-safe-deposit-company|Metropolitan Safe Deposit Company]] [3492-PN].

### The False Reformation

In 1925, [[john-roper|Roper]] abruptly ceased the blackmail, claiming he was "tired of crooked going and wished to live straight" [1383-PN]. He returned the forged confession to [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] and watched him burn it. But [[joseph-french|Inspector French]] later discovered this "reformation" was a ruse: [[john-roper|Roper]] had kept a copy of the confession, and his real motive was to lull [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] into complacency while he planned the Starvel robbery [1383-PN].

[[joseph-french|French]] recognized the tactical logic: "The doctor was the one person in the neighbourhood who knew his real character. If anything untoward happened at Starvel, the doctor would immediately become suspicious. It was, therefore, politic to suggest a reformation of character to Philpot" [1601-PN].

## Significance

[[john-roper|Roper]]'s blackmail scheme reveals his essential character: patient, methodical, utterly unscrupulous. Over six years he systematically extracted money from [[dr-philpot|Philpot]] while constructing an ever-tighter web of compromising documents. The scheme also demonstrates how [[john-roper|Roper]]'s greed operated on multiple scales — from the petty weekly extortion of ten shillings to the grand Starvel Hollow conspiracy involving thousands of pounds.

The blackmail created a fatal interdependence between the two men. Each held knowledge that could destroy the other, binding them together in a conspiracy that escalated from extortion to murder. As [[joseph-french|French]] discovered, the Starvel Hollow tragedy was not a single crime but the explosive endpoint of years of mutual blackmail between two men who had lost all moral restraint [3512-PN].
