## Overview

Waverley Station (also known as Edinburgh Waverley) is the principal railway station in Edinburgh, Scotland. In *The Starvel Hollow Tragedy*, it serves as the location for the climactic trap that Inspector French sets for the fugitive John Roper. French lures Roper to Waverley Station using a forged telegram purporting to be from the stockbrokers Dashwood & Munce, and the station becomes the site of the final confrontation between hunter and hunted.

## Role in the Investigation

Waverley Station plays a pivotal role in the novel's climax. Having deduced that John Roper (in reality Herbert Philpot) is alive and has been transferring money abroad, French devises an elaborate trap.

### The Telegram Bait

French, working with the stockbrokers Dashwood & Munce, sends a telegram to Roper (under his alias "Whitman") in Edinburgh advising of a "serious fall in Brazilian stocks" and requesting a meeting "at Waverley where train waits from 6:15 to 6:33" on the 10:00 a.m. train from King's Cross [019-034]. French believes that Roper, anxious about his investments, will not be able to resist attending.

French reinforces the telegram with a letter, knowing that in ordinary business such a letter would follow and that Roper might notice the omission [019-035].

### The Trap

French writes to Dr. Philpot — whom he suspects of being the real murderer — asking him to come to Edinburgh to identify Roper. In his letter, French states: "We have found that under an alias he has been transferring his money abroad, and in the name of the stockbrokers concerned I have asked him to meet their junior partner at Waverley Station, Edinburgh, on Tuesday next at 6:15 p.m. on the arrival of the 10:00 a.m. from King's Cross" [019-061].

Inspector Tanner is assigned to impersonate Mr. Munce, the junior partner. Tanner travels to Edinburgh by the 10:00 a.m. from King's Cross, and "on reaching Waverley he would cooperate with French as circumstances demanded" [019-088].

### The Confrontation

French himself travels to Edinburgh on the Monday night, arriving at Princes Street Station the next morning. As five o'clock approaches, he waits near Scott's Monument in Princes Street Gardens. Philpot arrives, approaching "from the Waverley Station direction, walking slowly, as if conscious that he was early" [019-094].

The final confrontation at Waverley is described as Philpot's "last move" — "a throw of the dice" [020-071]. Philpot half-suspected a trap on receiving the wire, and the plan became fully apparent when French's letter arrived. He realised he was either "quite safe or irretrievably lost": if French had no inkling of the truth he must keep the appointment; if French knew, nothing could save him [020-071].

The Waverley meeting ends with Philpot's arrest, bringing the Starvel Hollow investigation to its conclusion.
