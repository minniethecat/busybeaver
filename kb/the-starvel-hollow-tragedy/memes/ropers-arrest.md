## Summary

The arrest of [[john-roper|John Roper]] at Waverley Station in [[edinburgh]] was the culmination of Inspector [[joseph-french|French]]'s investigation into the [[starvel|Starvel Hollow]] tragedy. After tracing [[john-roper|Roper]]'s movements following the fire, [[joseph-french|French]] set a trap at the station, knowing [[john-roper|Roper]] would attempt to flee. The arrest brought to justice the perpetrator of one of the most diabolical criminal schemes — a triple murder disguised as an accidental fire, combined with robbery and an elaborate body-substitution plot [2153-PN].

## Detailed Account

### The Manhunt

After the exhumation of [[markham-giles|Markham Giles]]' empty coffin confirmed [[john-roper|Roper]]'s guilt, [[joseph-french|French]] turned his full attention to finding the fugitive. "[[joseph-french|French]] determined that he would immediately begin to trace him and that nothing would induce him to stop until he had succeeded" [2153-PN].

[[joseph-french|French]] circulated a detailed description of [[john-roper|Roper]] through the *Police Gazette*, along with a photograph obtained from the Passport Office. "Within three or four days every police officer in the land would be applying it to newcomers of less than ten weeks' standing" [2813-PN].

The description was compiled from multiple witnesses and was "more complete than was usually obtainable from unofficial sources." [[joseph-french|French]] had obtained it through "persistent questions on each feature in turn" [2813-PN].

### The Sting at Waverley

[[joseph-french|French]] traced [[john-roper|Roper]] to [[edinburgh|Edinburgh]], where the fugitive was hiding while planning his next move. Through careful investigation, [[joseph-french|French]] learned that [[john-roper|Roper]] intended to travel through Waverley Station — [[edinburgh|Edinburgh]]'s main railway terminal and a major junction on the East Coast Main Line.

The station, with its vast glass roof and multiple platforms, presented both an opportunity and a challenge for an arrest. Its busy crowds would provide cover for [[joseph-french|French]]'s men, but could also allow [[john-roper|Roper]] to slip away if the operation was not precisely coordinated.

[[joseph-french|French]] positioned plainclothes officers at key points throughout the station. When [[john-roper|Roper]] appeared on the platform, [[joseph-french|French]] personally moved in to make the arrest. The apprehension was swift and decisive — [[john-roper|Roper]], taken by surprise, offered no resistance.

### Aftermath

[[joseph-french|French]]'s vow was fulfilled: "With set teeth and frowning brow [[joseph-french|French]] vowed to himself that he would not rest until he had the monster who had done this deed safely under lock and key" [2181-PN].

With [[john-roper|Roper]] in custody, the full story of the [[starvel|Starvel Hollow]] tragedy could finally be told. "This, then, was the end of the mystery. The conclusion which [[joseph-french|French]] had reached by analysis and deduction had been tested and had proved true, and that [[evidence|proof]] established at one and the same time the whole of the steps of his line of reasoning" [2153-PN].

## Significance

The arrest of John [[john-roper|Roper]] represented the triumph of [[joseph-french|French]]'s methodical, deductive approach to criminal investigation. The case had begun as a seemingly routine inquiry into insurance company concerns about a country house fire. Through patient observation, logical reasoning, and relentless pursuit of every [[evidence|clue]], [[joseph-french|French]] had:

1. **Uncovered a triple murder** disguised as an accidental fire
2. **Exposed a body-substitution scheme** involving the murder of an innocent invalid
3. **Traced a fugitive** across the country using systematic police work
4. **Brought the perpetrator to justice** through a carefully planned arrest operation

The arrest also vindicated the suspicions of [[tarkington|Mr. Tarkington]] and [[major-valentine|Chief Constable Valentine]], who had been the first to question the official verdict of [[the-inquest|the inquest]] [516-PN].

[[john-roper|Roper]] was revealed to be "guilty of one of the most diabolical plots ever conceived in the mind of a criminal. He had allowed nothing to stand in his way. He had sacrificed the lives of no less than three people in order that he might with the greater security steal his employer's money" [2153-PN].
