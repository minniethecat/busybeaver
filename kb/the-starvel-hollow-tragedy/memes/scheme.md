# Scheme

The **scheme** devised by [[john-roper]] forms the central criminal architecture of the [[starvel-hollow-fire|Starvel Hollow tragedy]]. It is a multi-layered conspiracy combining [[arson]], [[murder]], insurance [[fraud]], body substitution, and currency laundering — described by [[joseph-french|Inspector French]] as "one of the most diabolical plots ever conceived in the mind of a criminal" [2153-PN].

## The Core Plot

Roper's scheme was designed to achieve both wealth and freedom in a single criminal operation. As [[joseph-french|French]] later reconstructed it:

> During this time of waiting the man had doubtless been perfecting his scheme. And then another factor had entered into it. He had come to hate his wife. Why not at one fell stroke achieve both wealth and freedom? The same machinery would accomplish both. [1603-PN]

The scheme required the murder of three people: [[simon-averill]], [[flora-roper]], and the tramp [[james-giles|Markham Giles]], whose body was substituted for Roper's in the fire debris to fake Roper's own death [1953-PN].

## The Wedding Ring Laundering

A critical component of the scheme was Roper's method for converting stolen £20 notes into clean, untraceable currency. He purchased wedding rings with the tainted notes, then sold the rings for clean money:

> For every ring a £20 note, a tainted £20 note, a £20 note from Mr. Averill's wrecked safe up at Starvel. And for every £20 note got rid of over £18 of good, clean, untraceable money brought in. It was a scheme, a great scheme, worthy of the man who had devised the crime as a whole. [3107-PN]

French later discovered that Roper had converted the laundered money into Brazilian securities, planning to escape to South America [3299-PN].

## Ruth Averill's Removal

For the scheme to succeed without detection, [[ruth-averill]] had to be removed from [[starvel|Starvel Hollow]]. This was accomplished through a forged letter purporting to be from Theodore Averill:

> For the safe working of the scheme it was necessary that Ruth Averill should be got rid of. We have seen how this was done, but it unexpectedly involved drugging her uncle to prevent the fraud from becoming known. [3518-PN]

The plan was devised by [[dr-philpot|Dr. Philpot]], who supplied the forged letters and money, while Roper executed the practical details [3518-PN].

## Scheme's Near Success

French reflected on how close the scheme came to succeeding:

> Truly, it was a well-thought-out scheme! And how nearly had it succeeded! But its success would be short-lived. With set teeth and frowning brow French vowed to himself that he would not rest until he had the monster who had done this deed safely under lock and key. [2181-PN]
