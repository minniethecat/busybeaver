## Overview

The Strand Palace Hotel is a hotel on the Strand in central London. In *The Starvel Hollow Tragedy*, it appears as one of the establishments where John Roper stayed during his final days in London, providing Inspector French with a crucial lead in his manhunt.

## Role in the Investigation

During the intensive search for John Roper, Inspector French deploys officers across London to trace the fugitive's movements. One of the key investigative strategies involves checking hotels — if Roper was not living in London, he would need accommodation when visiting.

French's efforts yield results when inquiries reveal that "Roper had spent the Tuesday night at the Strand Palace Hotel" [018-039]. This information is significant for two reasons.

First, it helps establish Roper's movements during the critical period when he was preparing to flee abroad. The Tuesday in question is part of the timeline French is painstakingly reconstructing.

Second, and more strategically, the Strand Palace booking "seemed to negative the suggestion that the man was living in London" [018-039]. If Roper had a London residence, he would not need a hotel room. This finding gives French renewed hope of tracing Roper's whereabouts on the Thursday night — the crucial next link in the chain that would eventually lead him to the fugitive.

The Strand Palace Hotel discovery is one piece of a larger pattern. The following day, a further breakthrough comes when Sergeant Elliott reports that Roper had spent the Thursday night at the Peveril Hotel in Russell Square, bringing French closer to his quarry.
