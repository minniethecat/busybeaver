## Overview

The Beira Bank is a fictional Brazilian financial institution based in Rio de Janeiro. In *The Starvel Hollow Tragedy*, it is mentioned as the bank where [[john-roper|John Roper]] claimed to hold an account, into which the dividends from his supposed investment in the Beira Railway Company were to be paid [018-064].

## Role in the Investigation

The Beira Bank surfaces during French's investigation of Roper's financial affairs. While interviewing a stockbroker, French learns that Roper had purchased shares in the Beira Railway Company and claimed he was sailing to Brazil in a few weeks. Roper stated that he already held an account at the Beira Bank in Rio, to which the railway dividends were to be directed [018-064].

This information helps French confirm Roper's intention to flee the country and his destination. The Beira Bank account — real or fabricated — is part of the paper trail Roper constructed to present himself as a legitimate investor planning a genuine emigration, rather than a murderer escaping justice.
