## Overview

The Home Office is the British government department responsible for immigration, security, and law and order. In *The Starvel Hollow Tragedy*, the Home Office plays a specific procedural role: it must authorise the exhumation of Markham Giles's body — a legal requirement that Inspector French navigates as part of his investigation into the third body found in the Starvel Hollow ruins [012-060]. The Home Office dispatches Dr. Laming as its representative to observe the exhumation [012-084].

## Role in the Investigation

The Home Office enters the investigation at a critical juncture. After French deduces that the third, unidentified body found in the Starvel fire debris was likely Markham Giles — a neighbour who died the night before the fire — he realises that the only way to confirm his theory is to exhume Giles's coffin [012-059]. However, an exhumation requires authorisation from the Home Office [012-060].

In London, obtaining such powers would be straightforward from Scotland Yard, but in Yorkshire the request must go through the local authorities. French presents his findings to Major Valentine, the Chief Constable, who agrees to obtain the necessary order. Valentine confirms that a Home Office representative must be present at the exhumation [012-065].

Two days later, the exhumation order arrives. Dr. Laming, the Home Office representative, joins French, Sergeant Kent, and Colonel Followes (the local magistrate) at the Thirsby cemetery at midnight [012-084]. Dr. Laming proves to be a "case-hardened" official, unmoved by the grim proceedings — unlike the other participants who feel a thrill of horror as the coffin lid is raised [012-093].

The exhumation vindicates French's theory: the coffin contains not Markham Giles's body but dark peaty earth. Dr. Laming congratulates French with "One for you, French. Congratulations" [012-095]. This discovery confirms that someone substituted earth for the body, providing crucial evidence that the Starvel fire was arson and murder.

## Key Members

- **Dr. Laming** — The Home Office representative dispatched to observe the exhumation of Markham Giles's coffin. Described as "case-hardened," he has participated in exhumations before and remains professionally detached throughout the proceedings [012-084] [012-093].
