## Summary

"Philpot's Confession" was a forged document purportedly written by [[dr-philpot|Dr. Herbert Philpot]], confessing to the murder of his wife [[edna-philpot|Edna Philpot]] by pushing her down the stairs at their home in [[kintilloch]]. In reality, the document was forged by [[john-roper|John Roper]] as an instrument of blackmail. [[john-roper|Roper]], who had worked with Philpot at the [[ransome-institute|Ransome Institute]], used his knowledge of an improper relationship between Philpot and a nurse to extort money. The forged confession was discovered by Inspector [[joseph-french|French]] in a safe deposit box [[john-roper|Roper]] maintained in [[london|London]], providing the first direct link between [[john-roper|Roper]] and blackmail [1127-PN] [1131-PN].

## Detailed Account

### The Nurse Incident

While both men worked at the Ransome Institute in Kintilloch, Fife, [[john-roper|Roper]] became aware of an improper relationship between Dr. Philpot and a young nurse. The doctor was a married man with a weak character, and the incident — though "harmless in itself" — would have resulted in his immediate dismissal if reported to [[ransome-institute|the Institute]] authorities: "If [[john-roper|Roper]] had reported me to the authorities my resignation would have followed as a matter of course" [1349-PN].

[[john-roper|Roper]] saw his opportunity. He confronted Philpot and demanded payment in exchange for silence. Philpot, taking "the coward's way," agreed to [[john-roper|Roper]]'s terms [1349-PN].

### The Forged Confession

To secure his hold over the doctor, [[john-roper|Roper]] forged a confession document in Philpot's handwriting. The document purported to be Philpot's admission that he had murdered his wife Edna by pushing her down the stairs at their home. [[john-roper|Roper]] then stored this document in a safe deposit box in [[london|London]] [1127-PN].

The confession was a clever forgery. Philpot himself, when shown the document by [[joseph-french|French]], initially believed it was in his own handwriting. The text described in detail how he had supposedly caught his wife's heel in the stair carpet and pushed her, causing the fatal fall. But the confession contained factual errors: Mrs. Philpot had died by genuine accident, catching her heel in a worn stair carpet with a raised and torn edge [1167-PN].

### Discovery by French

[[joseph-french|French]] discovered the confession while investigating [[john-roper|Roper]]'s affairs after the Starvel fire. Following the trail of [[john-roper|Roper]]'s [[london|London]] connections, he located the safe deposit box and found the document inside [1127-PN].

[[joseph-french|French]] was initially puzzled: "What under the sun did the document mean? Was it really a statement of fact, a genuine confession of murder, written by Philpot? If so, how had it fallen into the hands of [[john-roper|Roper]], and what had the man been keeping it for? Had he been blackmailing Philpot? Or was the whole thing a forgery?" [1127-PN].

### Philpot's Admission

When [[joseph-french|French]] confronted Philpot with the document, the doctor broke down and admitted the truth. He explained that [[john-roper|Roper]] had extorted money from him for years, threatening to expose the nurse incident. Eventually, [[john-roper|Roper]] announced he was "tired of crooked going and wished to live straight," handed Philpot the forged confession, and watched him burn it — or so Philpot believed. [[john-roper|Roper]] had in fact made a copy and kept the original in his safe deposit box [1383-PN].

Philpot told [[joseph-french|French]]: "It is evident he must have made a copy of his forgery and kept one while he let me destroy the other" [1383-PN].

## Significance

Philpot's confession, though forged, was a critical piece of [[evidence]] in [[joseph-french|French]]'s investigation:

1. **Established [[john-roper|Roper]]'s character** — The document proved [[john-roper|Roper]] was "vindictive, unscrupulous, a blackmailer, and as well as that a skilful forger" [2499-PN].

2. **Linked [[john-roper|Roper]] to premeditated crime** — If [[john-roper|Roper]] could plan and execute a years-long blackmail scheme with forged documents, he was certainly capable of the Starvel conspiracy.

3. **Explained [[john-roper|Roper]]'s departure from Scotland** — [[joseph-french|French]] speculated that [[john-roper|Roper]] ceased blackmailing Philpot because he was planning the Starvel robbery: "a reason why he had ceased to blackmail Philpot might be suggested. The doctor was the one person in the neighbourhood who knew his real character. If anything untoward happened at Starvel, the doctor would immediately become suspicious" [1601-PN].

4. **Provided independent corroboration** — The confession story gave [[joseph-french|French]] a witness (Philpot) who could testify to [[john-roper|Roper]]'s methods and character independently of the Starvel [[evidence]].
