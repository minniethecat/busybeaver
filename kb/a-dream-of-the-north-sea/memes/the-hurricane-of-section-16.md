# The Hurricane of Section 16

The **Hurricane of Section 16** is one of the novel's most devastating passages. In a single night of unprecedented violence, the North Sea claims an almost unimaginable toll. Runciman's language rises to a pitch of controlled anguish: 'A hundred smacksmen die to-night. Pitiful! But if this hurricane and all the lesser breezes did not blow, then millions would die who live now in comfort' (016-004). The passage forces the reader to confront the scale of sacrifice that underlay the Victorian fishing industry and the cheap fish on London dinner tables.
