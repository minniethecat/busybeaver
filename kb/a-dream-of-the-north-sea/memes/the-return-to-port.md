# The Return to Port

The **Return to Port** after the great gale is a sequence of quiet power in Runciman's novel. After thirty-six hours of storm, 'the gale had fined off, and the scattered and battered fleet' limped homeward. (005-011) The return is shadowed by loss — 'After a gale like this there are always scores of funerals' — but also marked by the survivor's relief: 'Just to think that of all those splendid, plucky smacksmen, we haven't got one yet!' (004-003)
