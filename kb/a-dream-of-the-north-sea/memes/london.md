# London

**London** is the metropolitan center of England and an important context for James Runciman's *A Dream of the North Sea* (1876). The novel was published in London by James Nisbet and Co. at 21 Berners Street, and the city represented the world of wealth, power, and political influence whose conscience Runciman sought to stir on behalf of the North Sea fishermen.

## The Distant Capital

For the smacksmen of the North Sea, London was a remote abstraction — a place where decisions were made that affected their lives but which most of them would never see. The Mission to Deep Sea Fishermen sought to bridge this distance, bringing the reality of the fishermen's suffering to the attention of metropolitan philanthropists, politicians, and the royal patron to whom the novel is dedicated.

## Publishing Center

As the center of British publishing, London was where Runciman's appeal reached its primary audience. The novel's imprint — "London: James Nisbet and Co., 21, Berners Street, W." — places it squarely in the world of Victorian literary evangelism, of which London was the undisputed capital.
