# James Runciman

**James Runciman** (1852–1891) is the author of *A Dream of the North Sea* (1876). A British journalist and writer, Runciman was deeply knowledgeable about the North Sea fishing industry and the work of the Mission to Deep Sea Fishermen, subjects he treated with intimate authority in both his fiction and journalism.

## Authority

Runciman claimed — credibly — that "of all the tribe of writers, I know most minutely the scope and significance of that Mission" to Deep Sea Fishermen. (P01-006) His other works include *Past and Present*, *Among the North Sea Trawlers*, and *Skippers and Shellbacks* — all drawing on his firsthand knowledge of maritime life.

## The Novel's Occasion

The novel was written as an appeal for the Mission's hospital cruiser program. Runciman dedicated the book to Queen Victoria, crediting her "gracious and practical sympathy" with advancing the cause. The book blends documentary realism with evangelical purpose, using the conventions of Victorian fiction to advocate for the welfare of working fishermen.
