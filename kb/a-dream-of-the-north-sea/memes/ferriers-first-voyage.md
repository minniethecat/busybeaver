# Ferrier's First Voyage

**Ferrier's First Voyage** is the narrative arc that structures much of Runciman's novel. Recruited by Henry Fullerton — 'We decided to kidnap Ferrier. We want to give him a real taste of the work' (002-004) — the young Cambridge doctor leaves behind the security of academic medicine for the brutal reality of the North Sea. His journey from seasickness and shock to genuine respect for the fishermen and a deeper understanding of his vocation is the novel's central coming-of-age story.
