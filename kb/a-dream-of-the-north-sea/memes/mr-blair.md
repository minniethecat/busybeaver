# Mr. Blair

**Mr. Blair** is a character in James Runciman's *A Dream of the North Sea* (1876). Mr. Blair's yacht, a fine vessel put at the Mission's disposal for the North Sea expedition. Blair is described with gentle precision: 'John Blair smiled gently once more, and there was a certain pride visible as his sad eyes twinkled on his young favourite' (002-004). Unlike the boisterous Tom Lennard or the zealous Fullerton, Blair operates in a quieter register — his contribution is material rather than physical, but no less essential.
