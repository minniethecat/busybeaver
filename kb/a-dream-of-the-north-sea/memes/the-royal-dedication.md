# The Royal Dedication

The **Royal Dedication** of *A Dream of the North Sea* to Queen Victoria is the novel's opening gesture — a formal address that frames everything that follows. Runciman writes: 'This book is dedicated to Your Majesty with the respectful admiration of one who is proud to have been associated with an effort to make the world more hopeful and beautiful for men who not long ago knew little hope and felt no beauty.' (P01-005) The dedication acknowledges the Queen's patronage of the Mission and appeals to the highest symbol of national authority on behalf of the fishermen.
