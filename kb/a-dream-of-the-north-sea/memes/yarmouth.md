# Yarmouth

**Yarmouth** (Great Yarmouth) is a fishing port on the Norfolk coast of England and a key location in James Runciman's *A Dream of the North Sea*. As one of the principal bases for the North Sea trawling fleets, Yarmouth was central to the fishing industry Runciman documented and to the Mission to Deep Sea Fishermen's operations.

## The Fishing Port

In the late nineteenth century, Yarmouth was among Britain's most important fishing ports, sending scores of smacks to the distant North Sea grounds. The town's economy and culture were shaped by the sea, with generations of families dependent on the trawling industry. Runciman, writing from direct experience, captures the rhythms of a port where the aftermath of every gale brought "scores of funerals" — and where the surviving vessels put back to sea as soon as the weather permitted.
