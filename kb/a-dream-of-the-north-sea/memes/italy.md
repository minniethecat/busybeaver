# Italy

**Italy** appears in Runciman's novel as a point of religious and cultural contrast. Describing the fishermen's practice of the experience meeting, Runciman writes: "In Italy you may see the sailors creeping into the box while the priest listens to whispers; on the North Sea a sailor builds his own weird faith and his own code of ethics." (010-001) Catholic Italy serves as a foil for the rough, democratic, and distinctly Protestant spirituality of the English fishermen.
