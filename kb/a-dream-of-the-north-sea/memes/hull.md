# Hull

**Hull** (Kingston upon Hull) is a major fishing port on the Yorkshire coast, and one of the ports from which the North Sea trawling fleets sailed in James Runciman's *A Dream of the North Sea*. Along with Yarmouth and Grimsby, Hull formed part of the triangle of east-coast ports that sustained Britain's distant-water fishing industry.

## The Port

Hull's deep-water docks and proximity to the North Sea fishing grounds made it a natural base for the trawling fleets. The port's economy was dominated by fishing, and like Yarmouth, its community lived in intimate relationship with the sea — its bounty, its dangers, and its relentless demand for human sacrifice.
