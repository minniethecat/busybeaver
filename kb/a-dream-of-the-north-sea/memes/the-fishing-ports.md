# The Fishing Ports

The **fishing ports** of England's east coast — Yarmouth, Hull, and Grimsby — formed the landward anchors of the North Sea trawling industry depicted in James Runciman's *A Dream of the North Sea*. These towns were defined by the sea, their economies, cultures, and family structures shaped by the rhythms of the fishing season and the constant toll of maritime mortality.
