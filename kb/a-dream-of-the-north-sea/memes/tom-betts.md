# Tom Betts

**Tom Betts** is a character in James Runciman's *A Dream of the North Sea* (1876). Tom Betts is among the fishermen encountered during the Mission's work in the North Sea. His presence in the narrative represents the ordinary smacksman — not a skipper like Larmor, but one of the crew whose hard labor and constant exposure to danger constitute the human reality behind the Mission's humanitarian rhetoric. Like the other smacksmen Runciman portrays, Betts embodies the 'pick of the North Sea population' — unpolished but courageous, rough-spoken but capable of profound loyalty and self-sacrifice.
