# Henry Fullerton

**Henry Fullerton** is a character in James Runciman's *A Dream of the North Sea* (1876). Fullerton is the catalyst for the novel's action. His idea was to bring a young doctor — Lewis Ferrier — to witness 'the wild work that goes on' among the North Sea trawling fleets (002-005). But his vision extends beyond medical care: he wants to transform how the British public thinks about fishermen. Tom Lennard calls him 'an angel' — and Runciman's portrait confirms that Fullerton's combination of strategic intelligence and moral seriousness makes him the indispensable figure behind the Mission's success.
