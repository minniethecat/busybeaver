# Cambridge

**Cambridge** is the university city where Lewis Ferrier "came south to Cambridge after he had done well at Edinburgh," completing his medical education before being recruited by Henry Fullerton to serve with the Mission to Deep Sea Fishermen. (002-003)

## From Academy to North Sea

Ferrier's journey from the cloistered security of Cambridge to the brutal reality of a North Sea gale is one of the novel's central arcs. Cambridge represents everything the young doctor must unlearn: the assumption that knowledge is acquired in libraries and lecture halls, the comfortable distance between the educated professional and the working poor. The North Sea shatters these assumptions, and Ferrier emerges transformed.
