# Tom Barling

**Tom Barling** is a minor character in James Runciman's *A Dream of the North Sea*, one of the many smacksmen whose presence in the narrative represents the community of North Sea fishermen.

## Role

Like many of the fishermen Runciman names in passing, Barling appears as part of the novel's documentary texture — a real-seeming name that grounds the fiction in the actual world of the North Sea trawling fleets. His brief appearance contributes to the novel's cumulative portrait of a working community, in which individual fishermen are named and recognized rather than treated as an anonymous mass.
