# Lewis Ferrier

**Lewis Ferrier** is the central protagonist of James Runciman's *A Dream of the North Sea* (1876). A young doctor educated at Edinburgh and Cambridge, Ferrier is recruited by Henry Fullerton to serve with the Mission to Deep Sea Fishermen. Through Ferrier's experience — his initial shock, his growing respect for the fishermen, and his eventual spiritual awakening — the reader is guided into the novel's world.

## Background

Ferrier "came south to Cambridge after he had done well at Edinburgh," establishing himself as a capable physician with a promising career ahead. (002-003) He is described as a "resolute" man whose practical skills prove invaluable during the crises at sea. Fullerton selected him deliberately: "We decided to kidnap Ferrier. We want to give him a real taste of the work" — recognizing that the young doctor's combination of medical skill and open-mindedness made him the ideal recruit for the Mission. (002-004)

## At Sea

Ferrier's experience aboard the fishing smack is transformative. Exposed to the brutality of a North Sea gale, he encounters "quite a new sensation" — the realization that his medical expertise, so carefully cultivated in the security of Cambridge, is now needed in conditions of extreme danger and deprivation. (003-006) His response to the storm reveals both his courage and his growing bond with the fishermen.

## Spiritual Awakening

The novel traces Ferrier's movement from conventional religiosity to a more personal faith, shaped by his encounters with the smacksmen and their distinctive Christianity. When he tells Fullerton "I've just had a new experience," Fullerton responds "Thank God to hear you say so" — marking Ferrier's arrival at a deeper understanding of the Mission's work and his own calling. (004-001)
