# Pettingill's Studio

## Overview

Pettingill's studio, belonging to the artist Paul Pettingill, serves as the gathering place for Brewster's circle of friends — the "Little Sons of the Rich." The novel opens and closes in this space: the birthday dinner in Chapter 1 establishes the camaraderie and financial anxieties of Brewster's pre-inheritance life, while the reunion in the final chapters marks the circle's restoration after his experiment in wealth.

## In the Novel

The novel opens around "the long table in Pettingill's studio," where "nine of them" are present for Brewster's birthday celebration (001-002). The studio's atmosphere is bohemian and convivial — it is here that Ellis, the footman, arrives with news of Edwin Brewster's death (001-033), transforming an evening of modest celebration into the catalyst for the entire plot.

The studio reappears at the novel's conclusion: Peggy Gray announces that they are "all going up to Pettingill's studio afterward" for a celebration (032-018). At eleven o'clock, "Pettingill's studio opened its doors to the 'Little Sons of the Rich'" (032-019), and Brewster, his inheritance ordeal concluded, "entered the studio at one o'clock" prepared to face his friends (033-037).

The studio's recurring role — as the stage for the story's beginning and end — gives it a structural significance in the narrative. Between these bookend scenes, the studio is referenced occasionally, as when a character notes that the man "who calcimines your studio could figure on a piece of work" (006-004), grounding Pettingill's artistic identity in the setting.

## Associated Characters

- **Paul Pettingill** — the artist who owns and maintains the studio (001-002)
- **Montgomery Brewster** — guest of honour at the birthday dinner (001-002)
- **Peggy Gray** — organiser of the "Little Sons" reunion (032-018)
- **Ellis** — the footman who arrives at the studio with news of the death (001-033)
