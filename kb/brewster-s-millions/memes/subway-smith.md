# "Subway" Smith

## Character Overview

"Subway" Smith is one of the most distinctive members of Montgomery Brewster's inner circle — a young man whose nickname captures the flavor of turn-of-the-century New York. He appears throughout the novel as a loyal companion, a source of wry commentary, and a steady presence amid the chaos of Brewster's spending campaign. Along with "Rip" Van Winkle, he forms the core of the friend group that remains by Brewster's side through his year of apparent madness.

## Role in the Novel

### The Loyal Friend

Smith is introduced early among Brewster's companions: "An hour later 'Rip' Van Winkle and Subway Smith were there" (001-028). He is part of the fabric of Brewster's social life, present at dinners, parties, and the various escapades that mark the spending year.

When Brewster confides news, it is to his inner circle that he turns: "Brewster confided the news to 'Rip' Van Winkle and 'Subway' Smith" (023-006). Smith's presence in these moments signals his position as a trusted confidant — someone Brewster relies on even when he cannot reveal the full truth.

### Comic Voice

Smith serves as a comic counterpoint to the novel's financial drama. His speeches at social gatherings provide levity: "'Subway' Smith's third address was the hit of the evening" (020-021). He observes Brewster's spending with a mixture of amusement and concern, voicing the reader's own reaction to the absurdity of the challenge.

His nickname itself is a period detail — "Subway" suggesting the recently opened New York City subway system and the rapid modernity it represented. Smith is a creature of his time and place, thoroughly at home in the New York of 1900.

### Steadfastness

Like the other members of Brewster's circle, Smith's loyalty does not depend on understanding. He watches Brewster's "reckless extravagance" with concern but never withdraws his friendship. In a novel where social standing is contingent on wealth and reputation, Smith's constancy represents a form of wealth that cannot be measured in dollars.
