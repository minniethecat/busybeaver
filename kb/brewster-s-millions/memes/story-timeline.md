## Chapter-by-Chapter Summary

| Chapter | Title | Key Events |
|---------|-------|------------|
| 1 | A Toast to Brewster | Introduction of Montgomery Brewster at his birthday dinner; first hints of inheritance (001-001) |
| 2 | The Will | Edwin Brewster's will revealed; Montgomery receives one million dollars (002-002) |
| 3 | Enter Mr. Jones | Swearengen Jones introduces James Sedgwick's conditional bequest; Peggy Gray introduced (003-002) |
| 4 | The Conditions | Terms of Sedgwick's will explained: spend $1M in one year, tell no one, arrive penniless (004-018) |
| 5 | The Lawyer's Advice | Grant & Ripley counsel Brewster; Brewster begins planning his campaign of expenditure (005-014) |
| 6 | New York in a Daze | Brewster takes lavish apartments; begins extravagant dinners; society takes notice (006-002) |
| 7 | The Cruise Begins | Brewster charters the yacht Flora Belle; departure from New York (007-001) |
| 8 | On the Mediterranean | The yacht cruise continues; Brewster's spending reaches new heights (008-001) |
| 9 | Barbara Drew | Brewster's romance with Barbara Drew develops and strains under his secrecy (009-004) |
| 10 | Society's Verdict | New York society judges Brewster's behavior; Mrs. DeMille's ball (010-001) |
| 11 | The Charity Bazaar | Attempts at charitable giving thwarted by will's conditions (011-007) |
| 12 | Wall Street | Brewster's financial speculations; the difficulty of losing money honestly (012-007) |
| 13 | The Panic | Stock market crisis; Brewster's loyalty tested at the bank (013-004) |
| 14 | The Lumber and Fuel Episode | Brewster's ill-fated business venture that threatens to make a profit (014-001) |
| 15 | Florida | Expedition to St. Augustine; the Florida cottage (015-001) |
| 16 | Ponce de Leon | Stay at the Ponce de Leon Hotel; social complications in Florida (016-001) |
| 17 | The Florida Cottage | Life in the Florida retreat; Brewster's spending continues (017-001) |
| 18 | Return to the Yacht | Back aboard the Flora Belle; new opportunities for extravagance (018-003) |
| 19–28 | The Cruise Continues | Extended Mediterranean and European travels; Brewster's fortune dwindles |
| 29 | Homeward Bound | Return voyage to New York; countdown to the deadline (029-012) |
| 30 | The Twenty-Third of September | Brewster's final hours as a millionaire; the accounting (030-001) |
| 31 | The Report | Brewster's detailed report to Swearengen Jones (031-018) |
| 32 | Penniless | The million is gone; Brewster faces the result (032-002) |
| 33 | The Flight of Jones | Swearengen Jones's dramatic exit and return (033-001) |
| 34 | The Verdict | Final resolution of the Sedgwick inheritance (034-001) |
