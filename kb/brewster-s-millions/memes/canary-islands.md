# Canary Islands

## Overview

The Canary Islands, a Spanish archipelago off the northwest coast of Africa, appear in *Brewster's Millions* not as a destination of luxury but as an emergency waypoint. When the yacht *Flora Belle* is damaged by a severe Atlantic storm during Brewster's Mediterranean cruise, the Canaries become the nearest point where repairs can be made — and the site of Brewster's most nerve-wracking trial.

## The Storm and Its Aftermath

The decision to make for the Canaries came after a storm that left the *Flora Belle* crippled (028-041):

> It was decided to make for the Canary Islands, where repairs could be made and the voyage resumed. But where the wind had raged a few days before, it had now disappeared altogether, and for a week the *Flitter* tossed about absolutely unable to make headway. (028-041)

The irony was cruel: having survived a storm that nearly wrecked the yacht, the party found itself trapped by its opposite — a dead calm that left them drifting helplessly.

## The Deadline Crisis

By the time the Canaries decision was made, Brewster was acutely aware of the calendar. The cruise had departed in April, and now — with the first of August arrived — fewer than two months remained before the September 23rd deadline (028-041):

> The first of August had arrived and Monty himself was beginning to be nervous. With the fatal day not quite two months away, things began to look serious. Over one hundred thousand dollars would remain after he had settled the expenses of the cruise, and he was helplessly drifting in mid-ocean. (028-041)

Even if repairs could be made promptly, the voyage from the Canaries to New York would take fourteen days under sail (028-041). The mathematics were brutal: a fortune remained unspent and the most profligate spender in New York was adrift in the Atlantic, unable to find anything to buy.

## Geographical Significance

The Canary Islands' position — roughly 3,300 miles from New York and 700 miles from the nearest European port — made them the logical repair point for a yacht damaged in the eastern Atlantic. Their volcanic origins and Spanish colonial history gave them a distinctive character, but in the novel they function primarily as a geographical plot device: the furthest point from New York where Brewster's spending challenge seems most impossible to complete.

## Associated Characters

- **Montgomery Brewster** — stranded investor, calculating the days remaining (028-041)
- **Captain Abner Perry** — navigated the crippled yacht toward the Canaries (028-043)
- **Mrs. Dan** — alarmed passenger who asked "You don't mean to say that we will drift about until the repairs can be made?" (028-039)
