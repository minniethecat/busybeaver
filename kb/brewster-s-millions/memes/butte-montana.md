# Butte, Montana
## Overview
Butte, Montana, is less a physical setting than a symbolic presence in the novel. It is the home of Swearengen Jones, the executor whose distant oversight determines Brewsters fate. The contrast between Montana plainness and New York sophistication runs through the novel as a moral commentary.
## In the Novel
Joness letters from Butte punctuate the narrative: "Swearengen Jones, Butte, Montana" (008-009). The physical distance mirrors the moral distance between Sedgwicks frontier ethos — "shrewd, careful, and thrifty" (004-008) — and Brewsters metropolitan profligacy.
