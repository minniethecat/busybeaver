## Overview

Dr. Lotless is a physician and member of Montgomery Brewster's extended social circle. He appears primarily in the second half of the novel, most notably as a passenger on the ill-fated yachting expedition aboard the *Flora Belle*, where his medical training proves useful during the shipwreck.

## Role in the Story

Dr. Lotless first appears in connection with Brewster's spending operations, when he arranges for the transformation of a small room as part of the general program of expenditure (015-039). He is "visibly distressed" when informing Brewster about financial complications (015-042), and explains that certain parties "knew you were my friend" in connection with a sum of three thousand dollars (015-044).

Lotless joins the yachting expedition aboard the *Flora Belle* along with his sister Isabel (016-002) (018-016). During the cruise, his emotions run hot: he explodes at another passenger — "you miserable rat" (019-025) — and is "the first to turn fearful" when danger threatens Peggy Gray (024-015).

His medical role becomes critical during the shipwreck, when he attends to a passenger "assisted by all available hands" (025-009). In the aftermath of the disaster, "DeMille and Dr. Lotless talked in the senseless way men will when they have just been snatched from the very maw of death" (027-052), capturing the stunned relief of survivors.

## Relationships

Dr. Lotless appears with his sister Isabel, who is explicitly named as part of the yachting party (018-016). He is connected to Brewster through friendship, as indicated by his remark that "they knew you were my friend" (015-044). He interacts with the broader cast during the cruise, including Joe Bragdon, Reginald Vanderpool, and Dan DeMille (018-016).

## Significance

Dr. Lotless is one of several "Sons" who populate Brewster's social world and participate in his adventures. His medical identity adds functional variety to the cast — unlike the financiers, lawyers, and artists who surround Brewster, Lotless represents a professional class whose skills become narratively useful during the shipwreck sequence. His presence on the *Flora Belle* allows McCutcheon to include medical details in the disaster scenes without introducing an unfamiliar character.

## Notes

Dr. Lotless's first name is never given in the novel. His surname "Lotless" may be intended as a characteristic name suggesting a carefree disposition, consistent with McCutcheon's tendency toward suggestive character names. His sister Isabel Lotless is one of the few named female supporting characters in Brewster's social circle (018-016).
