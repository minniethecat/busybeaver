# Captain Abner Perry

## Overview

Captain Abner Perry is the seasoned master of the yacht *Flora Belle*, commissioned by Montgomery Brewster for a transatlantic pleasure cruise that turns increasingly perilous. A professional mariner of steady temperament, Perry serves as a foil to Brewster's impulsive spending and the chaotic behaviour of his guests, maintaining order amid storms, social drama, and ultimately the foundering of his vessel.

## Role in the Story

Captain Perry enters the narrative as commander of Brewster's chartered yacht, responsible for "fifty men" under his command (017-008). His competence is tested repeatedly during the Atlantic crossing.

When the island chief approaches the yacht with hostile intent, "Captain Perry went forward to greet the mighty chief" and demanded to know the chief's purpose (023-007). His patience frays when dealing with the islanders' demands: "'Dinner?' demanded the now irate Captain Perry, shoving a couple of retainers aside" (023-008). He later deploys men to quell a disturbance, as Brewster calls out: "'Captain Perry, detail half a dozen men to look after those boats'" (023-023).

During the storm that threatens the yacht, Captain Perry is central to damage control — "calling the 'jackies' to the deck pumps" (024-020), and his "detachment of six lusty men" puts down an incipient mutiny (024-032). His professionalism extends to appearing "in his pajamas on deck" when summoned urgently below (024-048).

As the crisis deepens, Perry takes a central role in decision-making. He asks Brewster earnestly about the situation (026-003) and participates in "a general meeting in the main cabin" alongside the first mate and other officers (026-009). Brewster formally addresses him: "'Captain Perry, we are assembled here to discuss a matter of grave importance'" (026-010).

When Brewster orders the yacht to change course for home, "'Captain Perry has his instructions'" (027-011), and Brewster insists: "'Captain Perry takes orders from me and from no one else'" (027-029). There is "anxiety underneath Captain Perry's mask of unconcern" as the situation deteriorates (027-052).

In the aftermath, Perry maintains his composure, his entrance bringing relief to tense conversations: "'Not so bad as that,' said Captain Perry, whose entrance had been most welcome" (028-024). Monty's steadiness ultimately "won Captain Perry's admiration" (028-032). The captain and his officers survive the ordeal, their "touching emotions" noted as they part ways (029-007).

## Relationships

Captain Perry's primary relationship is professional — he is employed by Brewster and answers to him directly (026-003, 027-029). He maintains a respectful but firm boundary with the passengers, coordinating with the first mate and crew in all critical decisions (026-009). His interactions with the islanders reveal a man accustomed to command but capable of exasperation when authority is challenged (023-008).

## Significance

Perry represents the competent, unflappable professional in a narrative otherwise dominated by frivolity and excess. His steady presence aboard the *Flora Belle* grounds the cruise chapters in nautical realism, and his quiet admiration for Brewster's conduct under pressure (028-032) serves as an external validation of the protagonist's growth. As a character, he embodies the novel's implicit respect for earned authority — the kind that comes from skill and experience rather than wealth.
