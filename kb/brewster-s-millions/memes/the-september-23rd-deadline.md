## Overview

The September 23rd deadline is the temporal anchor of the novel's plot — the date of Montgomery Brewster's twenty-sixth birthday, by which he must have disposed of every penny of his one-million-dollar inheritance. As stipulated in James T. Sedgwick's will, Brewster must stand penniless on this date, 「without a penny to his name, without a single article of jewelry, furniture or finance」(004-019), in order to inherit the seven-million-dollar fortune.

## Origin of the Deadline

The deadline originates in the will of James T. Sedgwick, Brewster's maternal uncle, who died in Montana leaving an estate of approximately seven million dollars (004-006). The will specifies that Brewster must first dispose of his grandfather Edwin Peter Brewster's one million dollars before he can claim the larger inheritance (004-018). The date — Brewster's twenty-sixth birthday — was chosen by Sedgwick as a symbolic milestone, coinciding with the anniversary of Brewster's birth (004-018).

## Narrative Function

Throughout the novel, the September 23rd deadline functions as a ticking clock that drives the plot forward. Brewster constantly calculates how much money remains and how many days he has left to spend it. 「Can you dispose of it before September 23rd?」 becomes a recurring question posed by Swearengen Jones (017-004). As the date approaches, Brewster's anxiety intensifies — he is acutely aware that 「September 23rd would find him still drifting」 with money unspent, a prospect that haunts him (028-041).

The deadline structures the novel's action into a compressed timeline: from the reading of the will in early autumn through to the climactic day itself. Brewster's spending campaign must accelerate as the deadline nears, with each chapter marking the passage of time against the remaining balance.

## Resolution

The September 23rd deadline is resolved when Brewster meets Swearengen Jones at Holland House in London on the appointed day. The executor verifies that Brewster has successfully spent the full million dollars, clearing the way for him to receive the seven-million-dollar inheritance from the Sedgwick estate (034-039).

## Significance

The September 23rd deadline transforms what might otherwise be a simple inheritance into a dramatic race against time. As a plot device, it creates urgency, structures the narrative, and provides a clear endpoint toward which all of Brewster's spending schemes are directed. The date itself — his birthday — adds an ironic dimension: the day that should mark his coming of age as a wealthy man instead requires him to be penniless.
