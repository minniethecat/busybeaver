# Brewster Family Tree

The inheritance plot of *Brewster's Millions* turns on two family fortunes passing through
three generations. This page maps the known family relationships as established in the novel.

## The Brewster Line

```
Edwin Peter Brewster (grandfather, d. circa 1900)
├── [unnamed son/daughter] (deceased)
│   └── Montgomery Brewster (b. circa 1875)
│       └── inherits $1M directly from Edwin
└── James T. Sedgwick (uncle by marriage? or Edwin's step-brother?)
    └── bequeaths $7M to Montgomery, conditional on spending the $1M
```

## The Sedgwick Fortune

The [[the-sedgwick-fortune]] originated from Montana mining interests. James T. Sedgwick,
a self-made man who "had left Montana poor and had returned rich" (004-018), structured
his will to test whether his heir possessed the character to handle wealth. The fortune's
conditional bequest creates the novel's central dramatic mechanism:
[[the-million-dollar-challenge]].

## Inheritance Flow

```
Edwin Brewster's $1M ──direct bequest──→ Montgomery Brewster
                                                │
                              must spend $1M in one year
                              without gifts or endowments
                                                │
                              if succeeds ↓      │ if fails ↓
                         James Sedgwick's $7M    $7M goes to charity
```

## Narrative Function

The family structure serves a precise narrative purpose. Both benefactors are dead
before the novel opens, removing any possibility of negotiation or appeal. Brewster
faces the challenge alone, with only the executor [[Swearengen Jones]] to report to.
The absent patriarchs — Edwin's provision, Sedgwick's test — represent two competing
theories of inheritance: one that treats wealth as a gift, another that treats it
as a trial.
