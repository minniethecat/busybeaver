# Mary Valentine

## Overview

Mary Valentine (referred to as "Miss Valentine" in formal address) is a young socialite who joins Montgomery Brewster's chartered cruise aboard the *Flora Belle* alongside her parents, Mr. and Mrs. Valentine, who serve as the official chaperons. Spirited, loyal, and given to minor conspiracies, Mary is part of the inner circle that withstands the voyage's mounting chaos.

## Role in the Story

Mary Valentine first appears among the cruise party alongside her parents, the DeMilles, Peggy Gray, and other guests (018-016). She is quickly established as a sympathetic figure in Brewster's social orbit — someone to whom he turns when tensions rise: "He took refuge with Mrs. Dan or Mary Valentine or Pettingill" (021-022).

Mary is entrusted with confidential knowledge; Brewster later reflects that he "should have shared the secret with Mary Valentine" regarding a critical decision (023-008). Her presence is noted among the core group at the voyage's conclusion: "the DeMilles, Peggy Gray and Mary Valentine" (032-020).

During the storm crisis, "Miss Valentine" responds with concern for Brewster's safety. When he stands revealed on deck, "Mrs. Valentine had seen him," but it is Mary whose reaction is most telling: she "beamed" at Brewster and "nodded her head approvingly" (024-002). She and Peggy later collaborate on "the little farce she and Mary had decided to play for the purpose of turning the tables" (024-025).

Mary also engages in the shipboard debates about the cruise's management, quipping that if she were in charge, "'That would have been a consolation'" — though she is noted for avoiding argument "when Brewster was concerned" (027-003, 027-005).

## Relationships

Mary is the daughter of the Valentines, who serve as the cruise's official chaperons (018-016). She maintains close friendships with Peggy Gray — the two are described as co-conspirators (024-025) — and with Mrs. Dan DeMille (021-022). Her attitude toward Brewster suggests admiration bordering on romantic interest: her approving glance during the crisis (024-002) and her instinct to defend him in arguments (027-005) indicate more than casual friendship.

## Significance

As a supporting member of Brewster's social circle, Mary Valentine represents the loyal friend whose faith in the protagonist remains steadfast even as circumstances deteriorate. Her willingness to keep Brewster's secrets (023-008) and her playful collusion with Peggy (024-025) add texture to the shipboard social dynamics, balancing the more melodramatic elements of the cruise narrative with genuine human warmth.
