## Overview

Oliver "Nopper" Harrison is one of Montgomery Brewster's close friends and a key member of the entourage that helps him spend the Sedgwick inheritance. Self-styled as "superintendent of affairs" for Brewster's spending operations (005-071), Harrison handles practical logistics — leasing apartments, booking wagers, and managing the flow of money — with an enthusiasm that matches Brewster's own desperation to dispose of his fortune.

## Role in the Story

Harrison appears early in the novel, making casual remarks at the birthday dinner in Pettingill's studio (001-015) and rousing Brewster the morning after (002-006). His role crystallizes after the inheritance is announced: Brewster employs him as "superintendent of affairs" for the spending campaign (005-071).

Under Brewster's command, "Nopper" Harrison leases apartments (006-002) and, along with Elon Gardner, has the "receipts for the expenses for the day" (006-012). Brewster "blandly admitted" to Harrison and the two secretaries the details of his spending (006-014). Harrison protests against the pace of expenditure — "money is no object if he wants to spend it like a sailor in port" (006-016) — but carries out his duties faithfully.

Harrison's responsibilities extend to booking "several good wagers" on Brewster's behalf (009-002), and he appears periodically in a state of excitement over the results (009-032). He accompanies Brewster to the bank with Joe Bragdon, where both men "went among the people carelessly" distributing funds (013-006) (013-007). When Brewster's spending strategy shows signs of backfiring, Harrison urges caution: "We'll go to smash as sure as fate" (013-011).

Harrison remains in New York during Brewster's yachting expedition (016-002), sending letters that keep Brewster "busy with the reports" (016-011). His departure from active service is noted with regret (018-002). He returns in the novel's closing chapters as the "only absent 'Son'" at the final gathering (032-020), and his signature on a critical telegram is recognized immediately by his friends (032-025) (034-036).

## Relationships

Harrison is one of Brewster's most trusted operatives, handling money and practical arrangements. He works alongside Elon Gardner as a member of Brewster's informal staff (006-012) and appears with Joe Bragdon at the bank (013-006). His relationship with Brewster has a touch of exasperated loyalty — he protests the wild spending while executing every instruction. The nickname "Nopper" is used by all the characters in Brewster's circle (005-071) (015-008) (032-020).

## Significance

Harrison represents the operational dimension of Brewster's spending — the practical logistics required to move large sums of money quickly. Where other characters represent the moral test (Jones), the legal framework (Grant), or aesthetic excess (Pettingill), Harrison embodies the sheer administrative effort of disposing of a fortune. His presence grounds the novel's more fantastical spending episodes in the recognizable details of leases, receipts, and bank visits. His eventual departure (018-002) signals a turning point in the narrative, as Brewster's entourage begins to thin.

## Notes

The origin of Harrison's nickname "Nopper" is not explained in the novel, though it is used consistently by all characters in Brewster's circle. His full name is Oliver Harrison, as established at his first appearance (001-015). He is distinguished from other "Sons" by being the one who stays behind in New York during the yachting expedition (016-002), serving as Brewster's eyes and ears on land.
