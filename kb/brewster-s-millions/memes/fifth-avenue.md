# Fifth Avenue
## Overview
Brewsters Fifth Avenue apartment is the physical center of his spending campaign. "The most expensive apartments to be found in New York City. The rental was $23,000" (006-002). It is here that he establishes his headquarters, hosts his dinners, and performs the profligacy that New York society watches with fascination and concern.
## In the Novel
The apartment represents Brewsters first major act of extravagance. His friend "Subway" Smith observes that "he spends it like a Chicago millionaire" (006-002), and the apartment becomes a stage for the social performances — dinners, gatherings, confidences — that define his year of spending.
