# The Lumber and Fuel Episode
## Overview
The Lumber and Fuel episode is the novels most dramatic illustration of perverse fortune. Brewster, attempting to lose money through the [[speculation|stock market]], takes a large position in Lumber and Fuel on [[elon-gardner|Elon Gardner]]s advice. Instead of declining, the stock surges: "In ten minutes time Lumber and Fuel was up to 113" (010-031).
## Key Moments
[[elon-gardner|Gardner]] tells Brewster "Take my word for it Lumber cant go any higher" (010-011), but the market defies expectations. Brewster "realized heavily in his investments which caused his friends and his broker to wonder" (011-002). The episode demonstrates that losing money is harder than it appears — wealth, once acquired, seems to possess a momentum of its own.
## Significance
The episode embodies the novels central irony: Brewster cannot lose even when he tries. It also introduces the market as a character-like force — unpredictable, implacable, and seemingly allied against Brewsters goal.
