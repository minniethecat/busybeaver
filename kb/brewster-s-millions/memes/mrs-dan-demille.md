# Mrs. Dan DeMille

## Character Overview

Mrs. Dan DeMille is a significant figure in the New York society world of *Brewster's Millions*. She appears at social gatherings, dinner parties, and the charity bazaar, serving as a barometer of fashionable opinion. Her prominence in the novel's social scenes — she is mentioned frequently by name — reflects the importance of society women as arbiters of reputation in Brewster's world.

## Role in the Novel

Mrs. Dan DeMille is present at key social moments: "Mrs. Dan turned to 'Subway' Smith, who was at her right" (006-027) at one of Brewster's early gatherings. She represents the social audience before whom Brewster must perform his spending — and by whom he will be judged. Her appearances mark the novel's attention to the social infrastructure of wealth: dinners, charity events, and the constant circulation of gossip and reputation.
