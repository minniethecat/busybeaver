The Great White Horse Race is a [[tide|tidal]] race in [[bristol-channel|the Bristol Channel]], somewhere in the waters between [[hartland-point|Hartland Point]] and [[lundy|Lundy]]. [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] was caught in it during his passage toward [[bideford-river|Bideford River]] and described the experience as one of the most harrowing of his cruise.

## Caught in the Race

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s encounter with the White Horse Race was unplanned and unwelcome. He and the [[nona|Nona]] found themselves "in the piece of water called the Great White Horse Race" [011-035] and quickly discovered its reputation was well earned. "We had been caught in the White Horse — for small craft is this same White Horse Race" [011-038], he w[[corruption|rot]]e, the parenthetical observation carrying the weight of hard experience.

The [[nona|Nona]] spent hours "tossing in the White Horse Race all through that morning" [011-040], unable to break free until conditions moderated. The experience was sufficiently memorable that [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] later compared another difficult passage to it: "as had been done on an earlier day in the White Horse Race. The rollers came on and broke aboard" [013-004].

## A Named Peril

Like the other great races [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] catalogued — [[portland-race|Portland]], [[alderney-race|Alderney]], [[wild-goose-race|Wild Goose]] — the White Horse Race had a name that sailors used and respected. The "Great" in its title was not an exaggeration but a warning. [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s willingness to include it in his litany of dangerous waters, alongside races he ranked among the worst in British waters, testifies to the impression it made.

## The Bristol Channel Context

The White Horse Race belongs to the complex of [[tide|tidal]] [[danger|hazard]]s in [[bristol-channel|the Bristol Channel]], where the funnel-shaped coastline and extreme [[tide|tidal]] range — among the highest in the world — create conditions in which races, overfalls, and rips are common. [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s experience in the White Horse was part of a larger education in the peculiar demands of Channel navigation.
