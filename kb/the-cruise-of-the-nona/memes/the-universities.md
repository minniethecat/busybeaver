# The Universities

In British usage, **"the Universities"** typically refers collectively to
the Universities of [[oxford-university|Oxford]] and [[cambridge-university|Cambridge]] — the two ancient English
universities that dominated higher education until the nineteenth century.

## Belloc's Perspective

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] references the universities in *The Cruise of [[nona|the Nona]]*:

> …the Universities of Oxford and Cambridge to boycott his name.
> — [042-006]

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]], an [[oxford-university|Oxford]] man ([[oxford-university|Balliol]] College, 1893–1895), understood the
universities from the inside. His reference to a "boycott" suggests
the power these institutions wielded over intellectual and social
life in [[england|England]] — a power [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]], as a [[religion|Catholic]] and a radical,
often found himself in opposition to.
