# Fishing Boats

## Overview

Fishing boats appear throughout the *[[nona|Nona]]* as a constant presence — in harbour and at [[the-sea|sea]], the working craft that represent the ordinary maritime economy against which [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s cruising is both measured and contrasted. [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] is not a working sailor; he sails for pleasure and for philosophy. But he knows and respects those who sail for a living.

## In the *Nona*

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s encounters with fishing boats are typically matter-of-[[truth|fact]]. They are part of the scenery — like the [[lighthouse|lighthouses]] and headlands — but [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s eye for detail picks out their particularities: the type of rig, the state of the paint, the set of the nets. These are not generic "fishing boats" but specific vessels with specific histories.

The Fishguard fleet appears during [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s windless wait in that harbour — the boats motionless, the men ashore, the whole port suspended in the calm. The contrast between the working boats and [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s pleasure craft is implicit but clear: they are waiting for wind to earn a living; he is waiting for wind to continue a voyage of reflection.

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s respect for working [[the-sea|sea]]farers is genuine. He understands that the [[the-sea|sea]] he philosophises about is the [[the-sea|sea]] they work — and that their knowledge of it is deeper and more practical than his own. The fishing boats represent a form of knowledge that is not written down, not theorised, but lived.

## Types of Fishing Vessels

The British fishing fleet in [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s time included:
- **Smacks**: [[pilot-cutter|cutter]]-rigged fishing vessels, typically 15–25 tons
- **Trawlers**: larger steam or sail vessels dragging nets
- **Luggers**: dipping-lug rigged, fast and weatherly
- **Drifters**: vessels fishing with drift nets, typically for herring

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] would have encountered all of these types along his route from [[holyhead|Holyhead]] to the Wash.
