## Overview

*The Green Elephant* is a play by [[maurice-baring|Maurice Baring]] (1874–1945), the diplomat, journalist, novelist, and [[religion|Catholic]] convert who was one of Hilaire [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s closest friends. The play's exact date and plot are obscure — it was never [[maurice-baring|Baring]]'s most famous work — but its existence is preserved in one of the most charming passages of *The Cruise of [[nona|the Nona]]*.

## Belloc's Reference

In the second dedication of *The Cruise of [[nona|the Nona]]*, [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] recounts his dedications to [[maurice-baring|Baring]]:

> "Another book I dedicated to you was *The Green Overcoat*, and this I dedicated to you because you had written a play called *The Green Elephant*, and we thereby held communion in green things." [P04-004]

The symmetry is deliberate: [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s green-covered novel answers [[maurice-baring|Baring]]'s green-titled play. The phrase "communion in green things" elevates what might seem a trivial coincidence into a symbol of literary friendship — two writers, working in different forms, united by a shared sense of humour and a shared colour.

## Connection to Maurice Baring

[[maurice-baring|Maurice Baring]] was a prolific writer across multiple genres — poetry, fiction, drama, travel writing, and criticism. His plays include *The Grey Stocking* and *The Double Game*, but *The Green Elephant* is among his least documented works. Its survival in [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s dedication is a small monument to the way friendship preserves what bibliography forgets. [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] and [[maurice-baring|Baring]] were part of the circle of [[religion|Catholic]] writers that included G. K. Chesterton and Ronald Knox; their friendship, sustained across decades and wars, was one of the anchoring relationships of [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s life.
