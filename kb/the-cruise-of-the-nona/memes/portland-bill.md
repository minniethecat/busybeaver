# Portland Bill

## Overview

Portland Bill is the southern tip of the Isle of [[isle-of-portland|Portland]] in Dorset, a limestone promontory that juts into the English Channel and marks one of the most significant turning points on any passage along the south coast of [[england|England]]. For sailors, it is known above all for [[portland-race|the Portland Race]] — one of the most dangerous [[tide|tidal]] races in British waters — and for the Shambles, a sandbank that has claimed countless vessels.

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s treatment of Portland Bill is characteristically double: the practical sailor's respect for a dangerous piece of water, and the philosophical observer's de[[lighthouse|light]] in the inexplicable. His most memorable [[isle-of-portland|Portland]] passage is comic rather than harrowing — a strange encounter with a man in a perfect little boat who does not know where he is.

## In the *Nona*

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s most extended [[isle-of-portland|Portland]] passage is the comic set-piece of Section 28. As the *[[nona|Nona]]* makes her way past the Bill, a "fine rich little boat, with canvas so new and so tight" comes foaming past, and her helmsman — "dressed as though for Cowes Week in opéra bouffe" — calls out through a horn: "Are those the Needles?" (028-028).

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] is baffled. The Needles are one of the most recognisable landmarks in [[english-channel|the Channel]] — "as familiar as Piccadilly" — and any sailor who had rounded Portland Bill should know them instantly. "The [[the-sea|sea]] brings all [[adventure|adventures]], but what adventure was this?" (028-028). The encounter becomes a meditation on the limits of knowledge: how can a man be so far from home and so ignorant of where he is?

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] also treats [[isle-of-portland|Portland]] with the respect it deserves as a [[the-sea|sea]]mark. He notes the "clear shores of [[england|England]]" visible abeam — "[[wight|the Wight]], with its brilliant chalk cliffs taking the sun[[lighthouse|light]], and there, very clear, the Hampshire coast, and right at hand, Purbeck: all as neat as a picture" (028-027). This is the [[england|England]] [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] loves: vivid, particular, unchanged.

## Geography & History

Portland Bill has been a major navigational landmark for millennia. The Race — the [[tide|tidal]] turbulence off the Bill — is caused by the meeting of [[tide|tides]] from the English Channel and the North Sea. [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] notes that the Race "is haunted" (028-027), a half-serious nod to the many wrecks in its waters.
