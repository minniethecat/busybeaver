Jack Sound is a narrow [[tide|tidal]] passage between [[skomer|Skomer Island]] and the Pembrokeshire mainland, on the southwestern coast of [[wales|Wales]]. It is one of the two major sounds — alongside [[ramsey-sound|Ramsey Sound]] — that form the challenging maritime gateway around the southern corner of [[wales|Wales]].

## In the *Nona*

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] regarded Jack Sound with genuine apprehension, listing it among the most difficult passages on the [[wales|Welsh coast]]:

> There is one passage more difficult still, called Jack Sound, between Skomer and the mainland, and yet another still narrower one between the mainland and Ramsey Island [011-016].

The sound formed part of the formidable trio of [[wales|Welsh]] passages that dominated [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s thoughts as he sailed south — "at the southern corner of [[wales|Wales]], Jack Sound and [[ramsey-sound|Ramsey]] Sound and the Bishop and his Clerks" [011-018].

The psychological weight of the sound loomed large in [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s narrative. He described having "before me the terrors of Jack Sound" and spoke of the relief he felt as he sailed further from its dangers [011-022, 011-024].

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] made a memorable passage through Jack Sound as evening fell, describing how the [[lighthouse|light]] transformed the experience:

> Jack Sound in that evening light was a revelation of tranquil beauty [011-028].

The [[tide|tidal current]] in the sound was formidable — [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] considered what it would mean for his passage if the tide was running hard [011-026].
