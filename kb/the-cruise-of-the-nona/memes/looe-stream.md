The Looe Stream is a [[tide|tidal]] waterway off the [[cornwall|Cornish coast]], marking an important navigational transition point on the southern coast of [[england|England]]. [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] regarded it as the natural gateway into [[solent|the Solent]] and the eastern Channel waters.

## In the *Nona*

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] and the *[[nona|Nona]]* made for the Looe hour by hour during one passage, and he described its navigational significance in explicit terms:

> Now, with the Looe Stream, which is the gateway into the Solent, the character of the sailing and of the whole sea changes [032-002].

He elaborated further on this transition:

> The Looe Stream is the gate into the Solent, and once you have passed it you are in the sheltered waters of the Isle of Wight, with all the complex tides and harbours of the Solent before you [033-001].

The stream runs like a river in the [[the-sea|sea]], a distinctive [[tide|tidal]] feature that visually and hydrologically marks the boundary between the open Channel and the more enclosed waters further east [033-002].

On one occasion, [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] approached the Looe under the [[danger|threat]] of an ugly thunderstorm looming in the sky [009-006]. Much later in the voyage, the two buoys of the Looe Stream, together with the [[owers|Owers Light]], marked his return toward the eastern waters [039-007].
