## Overview

*On Nothing and Kindred Subjects* (1908) is a collection of essays by Hilaire [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]. The book exemplifies [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s essayistic manner — digressive, witty, apparently casual but underpinned by a coherent [[religion|Catholic]] and European worldview. Its title is characteristically self-deprecating, suggesting both the modesty of the essay form and the paradoxical ambition of writing well about "nothing."

## Belloc's Reference

The reference to *On Nothing* appears not in the main text but in the book's front matter — the second dedication page. [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] dedicates *The Cruise of [[nona|the Nona]]* "To [[maurice-baring|Maurice Baring]]," and the expanded version of this dedication, included in the Standard Ebooks edition, explains the history of [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s dedications to his friend:

> "I dedicated to you two books: one was a book *On Nothing*. This I dedicated to you because you were a great friend of mine, and I loved you." (P04-003)

The companion volume, *[[the-green-overcoat|The Green Overcoat]]*, was dedicated to [[maurice-baring|Baring]] "because you were the only man who could have got out of that scrape." The distinction is revealing: *On Nothing* is the book of affection, the gift of friendship; *[[the-green-overcoat|The Green Overcoat]]* is the book of shared adventure. Together they sketch a portrait of the [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]-[[maurice-baring|Baring]] friendship that spanned decades and included shared [[religion|Catholic]] [[religion|faith]], literary collaboration, and mutual admiration.

[[maurice-baring|Maurice Baring]] (1874–1945) was a diplomat, journalist, novelist, and [[religion|Catholic]] convert — one of the circle of [[religion|Catholic]] writers that included [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]], G. K. Chesterton, and Ronald Knox. He was, by all accounts, a man of extraordinary charm and wit, and [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s dedication suggests the depth of their bond.

## Connection to Belloc

*On Nothing* is meta-[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] — [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] writing about [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s own writing within a book that is itself dedicated to its dedicatee. The self-reference is characteristic: [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] frequently alluded to his own work in passing, not from vanity but from a sense that his books were part of a continuous conversation with his friends and readers. The mention of *On Nothing* in a book about sailing is a small reminder that the author of *The Cruise of [[nona|the Nona]]* was also the author of some forty other volumes — histories, essays, novels, verse, and polemics.
