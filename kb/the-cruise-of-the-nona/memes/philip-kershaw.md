# Philip Kershaw

**Philip Kershaw** was [[hilaire-belloc]]'s close friend and sailing companion. Little is known of Kershaw's life beyond his association with [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]], but the dedication of *The Cruise of [[nona|the Nona]]* makes clear the depth of their friendship:

> To the memory of Philip Kershaw my brave and constant companion… [P03-001]

## The Dedication

The dedication is the sole direct reference to Kershaw in the book, but its placement — on the dedicatory page, before the narrative begins — gives it particular weight. [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] names the book after his boat, the [[nona]], but dedicates it to his companion. The pairing suggests that Kershaw was present on significant voyages, perhaps including the cruise of the [[nona]] itself. The word "memory" indicates that Kershaw had died before the book's publication in 1925.

## In Belloc's Life

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] was an inveterate sailor who spent much of his leisure on the water, often in the company of friends. His sailing companions included figures from his literary and political circles, but Kershaw's absence from the wider historical record suggests he may have been a personal rather than a public friend — someone known to [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] through sailing rather than through letters or politics.

The dedication's language — "brave and constant companion" — evokes the particular bond formed at [[the-sea|sea]], where courage and reliability are the qualities that matter most. For [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]], who w[[corruption|rot]]e extensively about the [[the-sea|sea]] and its demands on character, these were the highest terms of praise.
