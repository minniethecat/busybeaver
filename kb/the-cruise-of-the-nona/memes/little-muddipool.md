Little Muddipool is a place name deployed by [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] as a satirical device in [[the-cruise-of-the-nona|The Cruise of the Nona]]. It appears in a sustained passage mocking the tendency of academic scholars to deny obvious truths in favour of contrarian hypotheses. The name itself — with its s[[lighthouse|light]]ly absurd, diminutive English quality — is part of [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s comic effect.

## In the *Nona*

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] introduces Little Muddipool in Section 12 during a digression on scholarly pedantry, while discussing the [[bideford-river|Bideford]] River region. He presents it as a parable of academic overreach:

> Your pedant says: "All the apparent evidence, all tradition, all that you would call common sense, would make out Little Muddipool to be that same Little Muddipool where the treaty of Little Muddipool was signed. It is called in plain words 'The Treaty of Little Muddipool,' and its last words are 'Made by us at Little Muddipool.' But *I* tell you that it was not Little Muddipool at all, but a place a hundred miles away with a different name" [012-006].

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] uses this invented example to show that the pedant's method — substituting hypothesis for evidence — is identical in spirit to medieval miracle-mongering. Both invert the natural relationship between testimony and assertion. The passage is part of a broader critique running through Section 12 of the way universities breed a culture of contrarian vanity in historical scholarship.

## Satirical Function

Little Muddipool is not a real geographical location but a rhetorical construction. Its name evokes the minor, obscure English coastal places that dot [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s narrative — and the absurdity of denying such a place's identity when the documentary evidence is explicit. The passage sits within [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s larger meditation on the three causes of academic folly: vanity, the degraded love of the marvellous, and the inability to weigh evidence after [[time|years]] of substituting hypothesis for [[truth|fact]].
