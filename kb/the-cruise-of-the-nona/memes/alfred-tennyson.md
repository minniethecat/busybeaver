# Alfred Tennyson

**Alfred Tennyson, 1st Baron Tennyson** (1809–1892) was Poet Laureate of
Great [[england|Britain]] during much of Queen Victoria's reign. He remains one of the
most celebrated poets in the English language, known for works including
"In Memoriam A.H.H.", "The Charge of the Light Brigade", and "Ulysses".

## Belloc's References

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] invokes Tennyson in two contexts within *The Cruise of [[nona|the Nona]]*:

> …works of fiction by Miss Austen! Tennyson wrote admirably of the Revenge…
> — [025-006]

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] praises Tennyson's naval ballad "The Revenge: A Ballad of [[the-fleet|the Fleet]]"
as an example of admirable writing about the [[the-sea|sea]]. Elsewhere, Tennyson is
grouped with other literary figures of the Victorian era:

> You saw in them the names of Tennyson or Browning, of Huxley and the…
> — [039-013]

Tennyson is listed among the intellectual luminaries whose names appeared
in the libraries and minds of [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s generation.
