# The Diver

## Overview

The *Diver* was [[nona|Belloc's boat]] before the *[[nona|Nona]]* — a [[pilot-cutter|cutter]] that he loved and lost. Her name appears in the book's early sections as a touchstone of memory and loss, the vessel against which the *[[nona|Nona]]* is implicitly measured. Every sailor has a first boat, and for [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]], the *Diver* was his.

## In the *Nona*

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s references to the *Diver* are brief but emotionally charged. She was a [[pilot-cutter|cutter]] — a single-masted fore-and-aft rigged vessel, typically smaller and more weatherly than the *[[nona|Nona]]*'s yawl rig. [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s experience sailing the *Diver* shaped his [[the-sea|sea]]manship, and her loss left a wound that the *[[nona|Nona]]*'s acquisition was meant to heal.

The *Diver* represents an earlier phase of [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s sailing life — perhaps a more carefree one, before the responsibilities of politics, journalism, and family weighed so heavily. Her name suits her: a boat that could take her owner into the depths, literally and metaphorically, and from which he emerged a different sailor.

## The Bond Between Sailor and Boat

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s attachment to his boats is one of the *[[nona|Nona]]*'s most endearing qualities. He writes of the *[[nona|Nona]]* with a tenderness that borders on personification — "she lifts splendidly," "she showed herself fit for any weather." The *Diver* is the absent predecessor, the lost love whose memory makes the present attachment more poignant.
