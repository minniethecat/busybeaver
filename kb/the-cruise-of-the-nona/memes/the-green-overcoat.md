## Overview

*The Green Overcoat* (1912) is a novel by Hilaire [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]. It is a comedy of manners and ideas, built around the [[adventure|misadventure]]s that follow from a case of [[fog|mist]]aken identity involving a green overcoat. Like much of [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s fiction, it uses farce as a vehicle for satire — of academics, politicians, journalists, and the comfortable pieties of Edwardian [[england|England]].

## Belloc's Reference

The reference appears in the second dedication of *The Cruise of [[nona|the Nona]]*, addressed to [[maurice-baring|Maurice Baring]]. [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] explains the history of his dedications to [[maurice-baring|Baring]] by listing two earlier books:

> "Another book I dedicated to you was *The Green Overcoat*, and this I dedicated to you because you had written a play called *The Green Elephant*, and we thereby held communion in green things." [P04-004]

The phrase "communion in green things" is vintage [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] — whimsical on the surface, but underneath it suggests the shared imaginative world of two writers who understood each other. [[maurice-baring|Baring]]'s play *[[the-green-elephant|The Green Elephant]]* and [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s novel *The Green Overcoat* became, in this private mythology, paired works, bound by a colour and a friendship.

The earlier dedication in the same passage is *[[on-nothing|On Nothing]]*, dedicated to [[maurice-baring|Baring]] "because you were a great friend of mine, and I loved you." Between the affectionate *[[on-nothing|On Nothing]]* and the playful *The Green Overcoat*, [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] sketches the emotional range of his friendship with [[maurice-baring|Baring]] — one of the most important relationships of his literary life.

## Connection to Belloc

*The Green Overcoat* belongs to the first decade of [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s maturity as a novelist. It appeared between *The Servile State* (1912), his major work of political economy, and *The Cruise of [[nona|the Nona]]* (1925), his great discursive memoir. The novel shows [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] in his [[lighthouse|light]]er vein, but the dedication's reappearance in *The Cruise of [[nona|the Nona]]* suggests that [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] himself regarded it with affection — a record of friendship rather than a bid for literary im[[death|mortality]].
