# Saint Augustine

**Saint Augustine of Hippo** (354–430) was a Roman African theologian and
philosopher whose writings — particularly *Confessions* and *The City of God* —
profoundly influenced Western [[religion|Christianity]] and philosophy. He is recognised as
a Doctor of [[catholic-church|the Church]].

## Belloc's References

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]], a devout [[religion|Catholic]], mentions Saint Augustine twice in
*The Cruise of [[nona|the Nona]]*:

> …with a few kind words to Saint Augustine, and Julius Caesar, and the…
> — [038-001]

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] places Augustine alongside [[julius-caesar|Julius Caesar]] as a foundational
figure of Western civilisation — the spiritual and the temporal powers united in
the author's historical imagination.

> …the rule and the landing of Saint Augustine.
> — [043-007]

The second reference alludes to Saint Augustine of [[canterbury|Canterbury]] (died 604), the
Benedictine monk sent by Pope Gregory the Great to convert the Anglo-Saxons —
a pivotal moment in English [[religion|Catholic]] history that [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]], as a historian of the
Reformation, held in high regard.
