# The Lizard

## Overview

The Lizard is the southernmost point of the British mainland, a peninsula of serpentine rock jutting into the English Channel from the [[cornwall|Cornish coast]]. For [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]], it is both a landmark he has misread from land and a [[lighthouse|light]]house he invokes in one of the book's most extended meditations on the fallibility of human perception at [[the-sea|sea]].

The Lizard appears in the *[[nona|Nona]]* not as a place [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] visits, but as a fixed point in the navigation of [[english-channel|the Channel]] — a [[lighthouse|light]] that every sailor knows, and that every sailor has, at some point, confused with something else.

## In the *Nona*

The Lizard first appears in a passage of self-deprecating confession:

> "Standing upon the shoulder of the Lizard peninsula, and looking eastward upon a clear summer's day, I misread the Dodman" [018-007].

From land, [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] admits, such an error is "unpardonable" — one has fixed points of reference. But from the [[the-sea|sea]], he insists, misreading headlands is far more common than sailing-men admit. The Lizard is the platform for this confession, the solid ground from which even solid ground deceives.

The second and more famous reference comes in [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s catalogue of navigation errors. He tells the story of the *Mohegan*, a ship that struck the Manacles rocks inside Black Head in [[cornwall|Cornwall]]:

> "The Lizard Light is there, staring plain enough; one only has to keep it well on the starboard bow going down-Channel, and one is sure to miss England" [018-013].

The *Mohegan* must have had the Lizard Light on her port bow — an impossible situation for safe navigation — and yet she struck. "No one has ever really explained how it happened" [018-013]. The Lizard Light, for all its clarity, could not save a ship whose crew had made an "absolute and false certitude" [018-013] about their position.

The Lizard appears one final time, in [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s lament for the neglected grandeur of [[portland-bill|Portland Race]]: "Today the straight course of innumerable ships out of [[southampton|Southampton]], making for the Start and the Lizard to [[the-sea|the ocean]], leads them right past it" [028-012]. The Lizard is the waypoint on the route to the open Atlantic — a name in the sailing directions, part of the shared knowledge of every Channel sailor.

## Geography & History

The Lizard Peninsula, in southern [[cornwall|Cornwall]], is the most southerly point of mainland Great [[england|Britain]]. Its name derives from the Cornish *Lys Ardh* ("high court"), not from any reptilian resemblance. The Lizard [[lighthouse|Lighthouse]], established in 1752, was one of the first to use a [[corruption|rot]]ating optic and remains a crucial landfall [[lighthouse|light]] for vessels entering the English Channel from the Atlantic. The serpentine rock of the peninsula gives it a distinctive geology and ecology found nowhere else in [[england|Britain]].
