Poole is a harbour town on the Dorset coast whose large natural harbour features prominently in [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s Channel sailing. The harbour's complex [[tide|tides]] and sandbanks make it both a useful refuge and a navigational challenge.

## In the *Nona*

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] made for Poole as a destination during his coastal passage. The harbour's entry, past the chalk formation known as Old Harry, presented a distinctive approach:

> The harbour of Poole, for which we were making during this long day, deserves a note, for it is here that the strong sweep of the tide past the Wight dies out [029-001].

The approach to Poole involved navigating "up past Old Harry and towards Poole" [029-002]. [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] noted the double tide characteristic of the waters shared by Poole and the [[wight|Isle of Wight]] — "the double tide of [[wight|the Wight]] and Poole, the 'gulder,' as they call it" — a phenomenon of local [[tide|tidal]] hydrology [014-015].

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] observed that while Poole's entry was easy for the ancients, it had traps within for modern mariners [029-005, 029-006]. The bay between Poole and the [[wight|Isle of Wight]] he described as so domesticated that it was almost like a lake [030-001]. Departing from Poole required careful course-setting [030-005].
