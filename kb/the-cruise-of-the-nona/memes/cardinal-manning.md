# Cardinal Manning

## Overview

Cardinal Henry Edward Manning was one of the most prominent English [[religion|Catholic]]s of the nineteenth century, a convert from Anglicanism who became Archbishop of [[parliament|Westminster]] and a leading voice in the [[religion|Catholic]] revival in [[england|England]]. For the [[youth|young]] [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]], a single sentence from Manning was transformative — "one of two experiences that stand out supreme among many hundreds in my own [[youth|early life]]" (010-010).

## In Belloc's Life

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] met Manning when he was twenty [[time|years]] old, and the Cardinal's words left an impression that lasted a lifetime. "One was a sentence which Cardinal Manning said to me when I was but twenty [[time|years]] old" (010-010) — [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] pairs this with a sentence from Dr. Jowett of [[oxford-university|Balliol]] as the two formative utterances of his youth.

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] does not record what Manning said, and the omission is characteristic. The specific words matter less than the [[truth|fact]] of the encounter — the old man speaking with authority to the [[youth|young]], "the dogmatic assertion of truth by the old and the experienced and the revered, to the [[youth|young]]" (010-010). [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] values the form of the encounter as much as its content.
