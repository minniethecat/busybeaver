## Overview

The *West Coast Pilot* is the Admiralty sailing-direction book covering the western coasts of [[england|Britain]] — the waters of North [[devon|Devon]], [[cornwall|Cornwall]], [[bristol-channel|the Bristol Channel]], and beyond. It is the companion volume to the *[[channel-pilot|Channel Pilot]]*, part of the series of British nautical publications that [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] regarded as uniquely excellent among the maritime nations of the world.

## Belloc's Reference

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] invokes the *West Coast Pilot* at the beginning of Section 14, as he prepares to explore unfamiliar harbours along the [[devon|Devon]] and [[cornwall|Cornish coast]]s:

> "I knew nothing of these harbours of North Devon and Cornwall, not even by hearsay. I read up diligently in my *West Coast Pilot* that river which seemed the best for entry along these coasts, and hoped to make it before evening." (014-001)

This is the voice of the conscientious mariner, consulting his book of directions before approaching unknown water. The *West Coast Pilot* represents due diligence — the sailor who reads his [[pilot|Pilot]] is doing what prudence demands.

But [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] also notes the limits of even the best book. Entering a difficult river mouth, he and his companion find themselves caught by unexpected currents. They had counted on the flood tide, but:

> "whether because there was more water than usual in the river, or from some odd effect of the wind, or because locally it is always so (though the *West Coast Pilot* told us nothing about it, nor the chart either), the river was still running out violently" (014-003)

This admission is characteristic of [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s [[the-sea|sea]]-philosophy. The *[[pilot|Pilot]]* is the best guide available, but the [[the-sea|sea]] has mysteries that no book can fully capture. The printed word is authoritative, not omniscient.

In Section 41, during his long discourse on sailing equipment, [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] groups the *West Coast Pilot* with the *[[channel-pilot|Channel Pilot]]* as essential shipboard reading:

> "never fail to have aboard you the book called the *Channel Pilot*, or the *West Coast Pilot*, or, in general, your book of directions for whatever part of the coast you are dealing with." (041-035)

## Connection to Belloc

The twin volumes — *[[channel-pilot|Channel Pilot]]* and *West Coast Pilot* — between them cover the waters [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] sailed throughout *The Cruise of [[nona|the Nona]]*. They symbolise his commitment to navigating by authority rather than by theory. Just as he grounded his [[religion|Catholic]] [[religion|faith]] on the authority of [[the-church|the Church]] rather than on private judgment, so he grounded his [[the-sea|sea]]manship on the authority of the Admiralty rather than on abstract [[tide|tidal]] [[knowledge|science]]. In both domains, [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] trusted accumulated, institutional knowledge over individual speculation.
