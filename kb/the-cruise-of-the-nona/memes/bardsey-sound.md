# Bardsey Sound

## Overview

Bardsey Sound ([[wales|Welsh]]: *Swnt Enlli*) is the narrow strait separating the Lleyn Peninsula from Bardsey Island at the northern entrance to [[cardigan-bay|Cardigan]] Bay. For [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] and the *[[nona|Nona]]*, it was the site of the voyage's most harrowing passage — a gale-driven struggle through tide-race and breaking [[the-sea|seas]] that nearly ended the cruise before it had properly begun.

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]]'s description of the Sound is both precise and metaphysical. He notes the practical details — the triple peaks of the Yreifles, the tide that turns "anywhere between six and seven in the morning" (001-005) — but the experience quickly exceeds mere [[the-sea|sea]]manship. In the Sound, [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] confronts his own [[death|mortality]] with a detachment that is itself the subject of his reflection.

## In the *Nona*

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] approached Bardsey Sound on the morning after departing [[holyhead|Holyhead]]. The wind had freshened, the dawn was bright, and "all was soldier's weather" (001-007) — conditions that seemed ideal for a [[youth|young]] sailor to practice his art. But "what happened was something wholly unexpected; it is always so at [[the-sea|sea]], and that is why it is said that the [[the-sea|sea]] brings all [[adventure|adventures]]" (001-006).

What happened was a gale that transformed the Sound into "a hell of white water and noise" (001-025). The *[[nona|Nona]]* was driven back three times before slipping through on a fourth attempt. [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] records the experience with remarkable candour: "Here I was in Bardsey Sound, with many deaths moving over the howling fury of the [[the-sea|sea]], and not one of them affecting me so much as a shadow passing over a field" (001-023).

The aftermath is equally revealing. Having survived, [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] looks back at [[bardsey-island|Bardsey]] "with sunset on its streaming hills" (001-029) and meditates on its history — the island's monastic community, its pagan past, the long civilizing process that transformed [[wales|Wales]]. [[danger|Danger]], once survived, becomes an occasion for reflection.

## Geography & History

Bardsey Sound is notorious among sailors for its [[tide|tidal]] race, which can run at up to 6 knots. The island itself ([[bardsey-island|Ynys Enlli]]) was an important early Christian site, said to be the burial place of twenty thousand saints. [[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]], a [[religion|Catholic]] writing of a [[religion|Catholic]] land, would have been conscious of this history even as he fought for his life in the Sound's turbulent waters.
