# Battle of Mons

The **Battle of Mons** was fought on 23 August 1914 near Mons, Belgium,
between the British Expeditionary Force (BEF) and the German First Army.
It was the first major engagement of British forces in the [[great-war|First World War]]
and part of the series of Allied defensive battles known as the Battle of the Frontiers.

## Belloc's Reference

[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]] mentions Mons briefly in the context of German military planning:

> …the German surprise on Mons and Charleroi.
> — [028-044]

This single reference places Mons alongside [[battle-of-charleroi|Charleroi]] as an example
of how the German army achieved tactical surprise in the opening weeks of the Great War.
[[hilaire-belloc|Belloc]], who served as a British intelligence officer and war correspondent, was
deeply interested in the military history of the war he lived through.

## Historical Context

The Battle of Mons was part of the larger Battle of the Frontiers (August–September 1914).
Though a tactical defeat for the BEF — which was forced to retreat — the battle became
legendary in [[british-army|British military]] history for the disciplined stand of the outnumbered
British regulars against the advancing German forces.
