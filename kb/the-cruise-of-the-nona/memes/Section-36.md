[036-001]The great light of [[foreland|the Forelands]] on the weather side, the [[goodwin|Goodwin]] Lights on the lee, stood like the lamps of a street in the fall of that darkness as, with the tide beneath us, we ran up for [[thames|London River]]. But, the wind coming from more northerly, we had to choose between beating up and standing out.

[036-002]We did the last, though the wind was falling, because that lane of water is full of the world’s shipping, and it was plainer to keep a course. By midnight the breeze had died down to a light air, and, somewhat later, the sky now very clear, we bore up on the other tack, the starboard tack, and went round till we had the [[death|dying]] air right abeam, and so came under brilliant stars within the point of Thanet land and dropped anchor and slept.

[036-003]But early the next morning we woke to see the sun rising up undimmed over the wide North Sea, cut sharply by the calm horizon. It promised a warm and kindly day. …
