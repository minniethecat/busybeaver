# The Beaux-Arts Tradition

The Beaux-Arts tradition, rooted in the curriculum of the École des Beaux-Arts in Paris, became the dominant architectural language of America's Gilded Age. Mumford traces its influence from Richardson's Paris training through the White City of 1893 to the monumental railroad stations and museums of the early 20th century. This page aggregates all entries connected to the Beaux-Arts tradition and its American academic offshoots.
