# Gothic & Medieval Architecture

Gothic and medieval architecture occupies a central place in Mumford's narrative — first as the communal building tradition of the Middle Ages, then as a powerful source of inspiration for the 19th-century Romantic movement and Gothic Revival. This page aggregates entries covering medieval cathedrals, Gothic theorists (Ruskin, Viollet-le-Duc), and the Gothic Revival architects of America and England.
