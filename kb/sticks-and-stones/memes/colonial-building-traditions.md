# Colonial Building Traditions

The colonial building tradition, as Mumford describes it, was rooted in the medieval craft inheritance brought to America by early settlers. Carpenters and builders, not trained architects, created the first American architecture — a vernacular of wood, brick, and stone adapted to regional conditions. This page aggregates entries covering colonial architecture, the Georgian and Federal styles, and the regional building patterns of early America.
