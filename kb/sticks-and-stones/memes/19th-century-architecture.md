# 19th Century Architecture

The nineteenth century in *Sticks and Stones* is an age of competing architectural ideals — the lingering classical tradition of the early Republic giving way to Romantic medievalism, the professionalization of architecture through the Beaux-Arts system, and the transformative pressures of industrialization and urban growth. Mumford traces this arc from Jefferson's classical vision through the imperial façade of the Gilded Age.
