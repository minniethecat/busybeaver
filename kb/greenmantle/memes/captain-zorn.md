# Captain Zorn

Captain Zorn is a German military officer stationed at the German frontier in John Buchan's 1916 novel *Greenmantle*. He is responsible for interrogating travellers entering Germany and plays a brief but tense role in Richard Hannay's covert journey.

## Role in the Novel

Captain Zorn appears at the frontier checkpoint when Hannay (disguised as the Dutch journalist Cornelis Brandt) and Peter Pienaar attempt to cross into Germany. Zorn is described as having "bright intelligent eyes" and being a veteran of German South West Africa — he "knew every mile of the borders" [^1]. Both Hannay and Pienaar recall having heard his name before, marking him as a figure of some reputation in colonial military circles.

## The Interrogation

Zorn questions the travellers in the _taal_ (the Afrikaans-Dutch dialect of South Africa), putting both men through a careful examination of their cover story. He had been "years in German South West" [^1] and possessed the regional knowledge to detect impostors. Peter Pienaar handles the questioning "to perfection, not pitching it too high" [^1], while Hannay supplies corroborating details when called upon. Zorn ultimately declares himself satisfied but issues a stern warning: "we do not understand slimness in this land. If you are honest you will be rewarded, but if you dare to play a double game you will be shot" [^1].

## Significance

Though a minor character appearing in a single scene, Captain Zorn represents the first serious obstacle in Hannay's journey into Germany. His intimate knowledge of the South African borderlands — precisely the region Hannay and Pienaar are pretending to be from — makes him a uniquely dangerous interrogator. The scene establishes the constant threat of exposure that hangs over Hannay's mission.
