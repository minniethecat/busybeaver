# Lisbon

**Lisbon** in *Greenmantle* is the first foreign port on Richard Hannay's undercover journey. As a neutral port city, it serves as the transition point where Hannay sheds his British identity and begins to inhabit the persona of Cornelis Brandt, a Boer sympathiser from South Africa.

## Arrival

### The Neutral Gateway

Hannay arrives in Lisbon by sea, and the city's neutral status makes it an ideal entry point for a man travelling under a false identity. The arrival is described with vivid sensory detail:

> We dropped anchor off the quays of Lisbon on a shiny blue morning, with the domes of the city rising above the mist. [002-012]

### The First Test

Lisbon is where Hannay's cover is first tested. He must present himself convincingly as a Boer, navigating customs and casual encounters:

> Anyone in Lisbon who knew the Barralonga would have expected to see her captain come ashore. [002-020]

## Role in the Narrative

### Establishing the Disguise

The Lisbon chapter marks the point where Hannay's transformation becomes physical and public. He goes ashore with bravado:

> We went ashore and swaggered into Lisbon as if we owned the place. [002-046]

### The Boer Connection

Lisbon is also where Hannay's backstory as Cornelis Brandt gains its first audience. The port had connections to South Africa, making the Boer cover plausible:

> ...at Lisbon and he had agreed, but all that was changed now. [005-144]

## Geographic Function

| Stage | Location | Narrative Function |
|-------|----------|--------------------|
| **Departure** | London | Mission assignment |
| **Transition** | Lisbon | Disguise activated |
| **Infiltration** | Berlin | Enemy headquarters |
| **Destination** | Constantinople | Mission objective |

Lisbon is the hinge between the familiar world of London and the dangerous world of Germany. It is the last place where Hannay is still fundamentally himself before the deeper layers of his cover identity take hold.
