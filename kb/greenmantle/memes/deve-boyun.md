# Deve Boyun

The **Deve Boyun** ridge is the eastern bastion of the Erzerum defensive system in eastern Anatolia. In *Greenmantle*, Hannay assesses it as a formidable natural fortress — but one that the Russian command intends to bypass, making its strength strategically irrelevant.

## In the Novel

During his reconnaissance of the Erzerum lines, Hannay is impressed by what he sees:

> The Deve Boyun lines seemed to me monstrously strong, and I remembered... [019-040]

The ridge features in captured Russian plans as a reference point. The enemy identifies their axis of advance relative to the "Deve Boyun main position" [019-064], and the Russian commander's strategy is to feint at Deve Boyun while striking elsewhere:

> Otherwise he would go on battering at the Deve Boyun ridge for... [019-072]

Sandy's intelligence reveals the full Russian plan: the northern columns would advance "past the Deve Boyun, and the southern columns up to the passes of the Palantuken" [019-085], enveloping the Erzerum position from both flanks rather than assaulting the ridge directly.

## The Battle

On the day of the Russian attack, the weather transforms the geography:

> To the east was the ridge of Deve Boyun, where the mist was... [019-110]

The mist that shrouds Deve Boyun becomes the aerial screen under which the Russian offensive unfolds — a natural phenomenon that the German-Ottoman defenders had failed to account for.

## Strategic Significance

The Deve Boyun ridge represents a recurring theme in *Greenmantle*: the strongest defences can be rendered useless by a strategy that declines to assault them. The Russians' decision to bypass Deve Boyun rather than batter against it mirrors the novel's larger argument about indirect approach — in war, politics, and intelligence.
