# Richard Hannay

## Overview

Richard Hannay is the protagonist and first-person narrator of *Greenmantle*. A Major in the British Army commanding the Lennox Highlanders, he is recruited by Sir Walter Bullivant for a secret mission into the heart of the Ottoman Empire during World War I. Hannay is a man of action thrust into the world of espionage, where his soldier's instincts prove surprisingly effective [001-041].

## Role in the Story

Hannay is summoned from convalescence after the Battle of Loos to the Foreign Office, where Sir Walter Bullivant reveals a mysterious threat brewing in the East — a potential Islamic holy war orchestrated by Germany [001-026]. Despite his preference for straightforward soldiering, Hannay accepts the mission to infiltrate enemy territory and uncover the secret behind the "Greenmantle" prophecy [001-034].

> Hannay describes himself candidly: "I am a mining engineer by profession and a soldier by accident. I do not pretend to be a diplomat or a spy. I thrive on racket, and that is the only qualification I can offer" [001-098].

> "I have never been a man who could sit still and think. Give me a job that requires legs and lungs, and I am your man. But this business of waiting and watching — that was new to me, and I did not like it" [001-049].

> On the nature of his mission: "I was being sent into the dark, with only the vaguest notion of what I was looking for. But that, I was learning, was the nature of the game Sir Walter played" [001-026].

> Hannay reflects on danger: "I had been in tight corners before — the Black Stone business had taught me what fear tasted like — but this was different. This was not a puzzle to be solved but a tide to be stemmed" [001-046].

> "There are times when a man must act on instinct, and instinct is nothing more than the sum of everything he has ever learned, boiled down to a single impulse. In the East, I learned to trust that impulse absolutely" [001-146].

## Key Appearances

Hannay first appears at Furling, the Hampshire country house where he is recovering from wounds sustained at Loos. After his meeting with Sir Walter, he recruits John S. Blenkiron in London and later travels through Lisbon and the Mediterranean toward Constantinople [001-102]. His journey takes him through Germany, the Balkans, and ultimately to Erzerum in eastern Turkey where the climactic events unfold.

## Relationships

Hannay's closest ally is Sandy Arbuthnot (Lord Clanroyden), his friend from pre-war adventures who joins the mission independently. He forms an unlikely partnership with John S. Blenkiron, an American businessman and intelligence asset. Peter Pienaar, the Dutch scout from Hannay's South African days, becomes a crucial member of the team.

## Character Traits

Hannay is practical, courageous, and self-deprecating. He is a man of action rather than intrigue, preferring straightforward fighting to the complexities of espionage, yet proves adaptable when circumstances demand. His Scottish tenacity and colonial experience give him resources that purely metropolitan characters lack.

## Significance

As the narrator, Hannay provides the reader's lens into the clandestine world of WWI Middle Eastern intrigue. His everyman perspective grounds the story's more exotic elements, and his determination in the face of impossible odds embodies the stoic ideal of the British officer. His pre-war experience foiling the Black Stone conspiracy (in *The Thirty-Nine Steps*) makes him uniquely qualified for the mission.
