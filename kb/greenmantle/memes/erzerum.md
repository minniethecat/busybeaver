# Erzerum

> "The great hills! ... We're going to Erzerum ... Don't you see that the Germans are playing their big card? They're sending Greenmantle to the point of danger in the hope that his coming will rally the Turkish defence." — Sandy Arbuthnot

## Overview

Erzerum (Erzurum) is a fortress city in eastern Anatolia that serves as the climactic setting of *Greenmantle*. It is the stronghold where the German-Ottoman conspiracy reaches its culmination, and where the mysterious prophet Greenmantle is expected to appear [001-034].

## Strategic Significance

Erzerum's military importance stems from its position controlling the historic invasion routes between Anatolia and the Caucasus [001-034]. In the novel, its remoteness from Constantinople makes it an ideal location for the conspirators — far from the prying eyes of neutral observers and the war correspondents at Gallipoli.

> "The Muscovite is sitting down outside the forts of Erzerum. I can tell you they're pretty miserable about the situation in the highest quarters ... Enver is sweating blood to get fresh divisions to Erzerum from Gally-poly, but it's a long road and it looks as if they would be too late for the fair." — Blenkiron reporting the Russian siege

Erzerum's remoteness is underscored by the arduous journey from Angora: "the five hundred miles of road to Erzerum" [016-023], a trek through Anatolian winter that becomes increasingly perilous. As the party nears the city, "things were getting very warm in the neighbourhood of Erzerum. Everybody on that road seemed to be in mad haste either to get there or to get away" [016-240].

## The Fortress and Its Defences

The city's engineered defences were formidable on paper. Posselt, the former governor, "was supposed to be a good engineer and to have made Erzerum impregnable" [017-124]. Yet the novel reveals the fortress's hidden vulnerability: a captured staff map shows "the Erzerum defences, showing the forts and the field trenches" with Stumm's notes marking the weak point at Kara Gubek [018-232]. Hannay recognizes that the map is "a confidential plan of the whole Erzerum *enceinte*, and would be worth untold gold to the enemy" [018-241].

> "On Kara Gubek depended the defence of Erzerum, and it was a broken reed if one knew where the weakness lay." — Hannay, studying Stumm's map

Hannay calculates that if the intelligence could "be conveyed to the Grand Duke he would have Erzerum in his power in a day" [018-275]. The map becomes the story's central tactical object — its delivery to the Russian lines the mission's decisive act.

## In the Narrative

Erzerum becomes the final destination of Hannay's mission after the trail leads away from Constantinople. The city is described as a strategic fortress on the Russian front, where German officers under Colonel von Stumm are preparing a decisive initiative [001-127]. The novel's climax unfolds in and around Erzerum, as Hannay and his companions race to prevent the Greenmantle prophecy from being weaponized.

> "We had to find Greenmantle at Erzerum, and once there we should have Hilda von Einem's protection." — Hannay, on the desperate urgency of the final approach

The city's atmosphere is evoked in a single haunting image: from a rooftop vantage, Hannay hears "thin and faint and beautiful, like the cry of a wild bird, the muezzin from the minarets of Erzerum" [018-324]. The perilous route through the city is undertaken roof-to-roof under cover of darkness: "Hussin took us for a walk over the roofs of Erzerum" [019-198], enabling Hannay's theft of the vital staff map from Stumm's quarters.

## The Russian Assault and Fall

The map's intelligence proves decisive. Hannay grasps the tactical situation: "Erzerum isn't invested by a long chalk. The Russians are round it in a broad half-moon" [019-243], offering routes to slip through the lines. Peter Pienaar undertakes the delivery, and the result is catastrophic for the defenders:

> "In three days the Cossacks will be in the streets of Erzerum." — Hannay, on the map's strategic implications

The siege concludes with the city in flames: "From Erzerum city tall tongues of flame leaped from a dozen quarters" [022-199], as "the defences of Erzerum were crumbling like sand-castles" [022-235]. In the final pages, Cossack horsemen sweep toward the burning city — "Down in the trough lay Erzerum, now burning in many places, and from the east, past the silent forts, horsemen were closing in on it" [022-363].

## Characters Associated

Colonel von Stumm commands the German detachment at Erzerum. Hilda von Einem, the novel's central antagonist, operates from the Erzerum region, preparing "her part in the defence of Erzerum" [021-042]. Sandy Arbuthnot infiltrates the inner circle of the conspiracy at Erzerum. Peter Pienaar undertakes the mission-critical run through the Russian lines to deliver the stolen defence map.
