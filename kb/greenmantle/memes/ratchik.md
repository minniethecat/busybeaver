# Ratchik

The **Ratchik ferry** in *Greenmantle* is a Danube River crossing that serves as a crucial waypoint in Richard Hannay's escape from Germany towards Constantinople. It is one of the few named transit points on the Balkan leg of Hannay's journey, marking the passage from one side of the Danube to the other.

## In the Novel

### Finding the Ferry

Hannay locates the Ratchik crossing through a combination of local knowledge and determination:

> I found the ferry of Ratchik by asking a soldier and a German-speaking peasant. [010-097]

This moment captures the improvisational nature of Hannay's journey — relying on strangers and scraps of German to navigate hostile territory.

### The Crossing

The ferry crossing itself is described as a moment of tense anticipation:

> ...the long lane which led to Ratchik ferry, and found it perfectly quiet. [011-046]

The quietness is both a relief and a source of anxiety — in enemy territory, silence can be as ominous as activity.

## Strategic Importance

The Danube ferry crossings were vital transit points during the war. For Hannay, travelling under a false identity, each crossing represented a point of maximum vulnerability — a place where documents might be checked and cover stories tested.
