# Bosporus

The **Bosporus** in *Greenmantle* is both a literal waterway and a symbol of the threshold between Europe and the mysterious East. It appears repeatedly as the destination toward which Hannay and his companions travel, and later as a lethal instrument of the Ottoman authorities.

## In the Novel

The Bosporus is introduced as a geographic marker delimiting the search for the Greenmantle secret. Sir Walter tells Hannay the quest's eastern boundary:

> ...Bosporus—not yet. It is still in Europe. It may be in Constantinople... [001-086]

Blenkiron adopts the strait as his own destination with characteristic American directness:

> "I'll blow in on the Bosporus by a short tack." [002-016]

Hannay himself commits to the eastern passage:

> "I am going to the Bosporus through Germany, and, not being a neutral, it won't be a very cushioned journey." [002-080]

## Danger and Death

In Constantinople, the Bosporus becomes an instrument of the secret police. Rasta threatens that inconvenient foreigners end up in the water:

> "The current in the Bosporus is pretty strong, and these sad relics of two misguided Dutchmen will be washed..." [013-009]

The threat is repeated: Hannay and Blenkiron could "be in the Bosporus" [014-042] if they fall foul of the authorities. After Hannay's cover is blown, Rasta recognises his identity — a "clue to an identity which was otherwise buried deep in the Bosporus" [015-065] — a grimly literal metaphor for the fate of those who cross German intelligence.

## Departure

When Hannay finally leaves Constantinople for the Erzerum front, the Bosporus marks his passage into the Anatolian interior:

> ...for after we left the Bosporus we ran into scuds of snow... [016-144]

## Strategic Significance

The Bosporus was one of the most contested waterways of the First World War. British and French naval power sought to force the strait in the Gallipoli campaign of 1915; failure ensured continued Ottoman control. In *Greenmantle*, the German-Ottoman grip on the Bosporus symbolises their control over the passage between Europe and the East.
