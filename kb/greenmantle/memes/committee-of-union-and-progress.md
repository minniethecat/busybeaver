# Committee of Union and Progress

## Overview

The [[young-turks|Committee of Union and Progress]] (CUP), referred to throughout *[[greenmantle-concept|Greenmantle]]* simply as "the Committee," was the ruling political party of the Ottoman Empire during World War I. Headed by the triumvirate of [[ennver-pasha|Enver Pasha]], [[talaat-pasha|Talaat Pasha]], and Djemal Pasha, the CUP brought the Ottoman Empire into the war on the side of the Central Powers [012-028]. In Buchan's novel, the Committee appears as an unpopular but German-sustained governing apparatus, loathed by ordinary Turks yet kept in power by [[berlin|Berlin]]'s military and financial support [012-082].

The CUP is the organizational embodiment of the [[young-turks|Young Turk movement]] that had seized power in the 1908 revolution [011-165]. Whereas the Young Turks represent an ideology of Ottoman modernisation and Turkish nationalism, the Committee is the actual governing body — the party machine that appoints ministers, commands the army, and enforces its will through [[ennver-pasha|Enver]]'s secret police, the Khafiyeh [012-024].

## Role in the Story

In *[[greenmantle-concept|Greenmantle]]*, the Committee functions as both antagonist and context. It is the organisation that, under German direction, is orchestrating the Islamic holy war that Hannay's mission must disrupt [001-020]. [[sir-walter-bullivant|Sir Walter]] [[sir-walter-bullivant|Bullivant]]'s briefing establishes the Committee's role: Germany has captured the Ottoman government through its control of [[ennver-pasha|Enver]] and the CUP, and is now using Islam as a weapon against the British Empire [001-031].

The Committee's unpopularity among ordinary Turks is a recurring theme. Buchan portrays the CUP as a clique of adventurers — "a collection of Jews and gipsies" [001-002] — propped up by German money and arms [001-004]. The ordinary Turk "loathes the Committee" but accepts German domination because "Germany's graft is" visible everywhere [012-085].

[[talaat-pasha|Talaat Pasha]] is described as "a sulky dog who wants only to get money" [013-031], while Djemal is dismissed as "the lunatic Djemal" commanding "a rabble" [013-018]. [[ennver-pasha|Enver]], the most prominent of the triumvirate, appears as a Polish adventurer who has somehow gained control of the proud Turkish race [001-001].

## German Patronage

The Committee's dependence on Germany is a central observation in the novel. The "Teuton" carries "the unpopular Committee" on his back [012-085], and Germany has "banked her winnings" on the Ottoman alliance [013-055]. The Kaiser himself appears as a figure the Committee defers to; [[ennver-pasha|Enver]]'s men are "no end proud" of the German connection [008-077].

This patronage relationship is what makes the [[greenmantle-concept|Greenmantle]] conspiracy possible. Because the Committee answers to [[berlin|Berlin]], German intelligence — represented by [[hilda-von-einem|Hilda von Einem]] and Colonel [[stumm|Stumm]] — can operate freely within Ottoman territory, using the Committee's resources to advance the holy war scheme [012-028].

## Significance

The CUP's importance in *[[greenmantle-concept|Greenmantle]]* lies in its function as the institutional bridge between German ambition and Ottoman mobilisation. Without the Committee's control of the Ottoman state apparatus, the German plan to weaponise Islam — the [[greenmantle-concept|Greenmantle]] conspiracy — would lack the infrastructure to reach the Muslim populations of Syria, Persia, and India [001-018].

The novel's portrayal of the Committee reflects the British wartime view of the Ottoman leadership as corrupt, unpopular, and German-puppeted. Buchan's assessment that "Islam in Turkey is becoming a back number, and Krupp guns are the new gods" [001-011] captures the perceived displacement of traditional Ottoman-Islamic legitimacy by the CUP's modernising, German-aligned authoritarianism.
