# Essen

**Essen** in *Greenmantle* appears not as a visited location but as a symbolic source of German military-industrial power. It is invoked through the Krupp arms works — the Essen-based manufacturer whose artillery pieces are described as a quasi-religious force driving German war-making.

## In the Novel

Essen enters the novel indirectly, through the pervasive presence of Krupp guns. In the opening chapter, Sir Walter Bullivant observes the transformation of German power:

> ...number, and that Krupp guns are the new gods. [001-011]

The Krupp works in Essen represent the industrialised warfare that has replaced traditional military values. Later, this same motif is expanded to encompass ideological warfare:

> ...manufacture Holy Wars out of Krupp guns alone and a few staff officers... [001-036]

The most direct reference to Essen comes in the chapter titled "The Essen Barges" [P01-061], and in a brief sighting during Hannay's journey across Germany:

> The Essen Barges [008-071]

## Strategic Significance

In the World War I context, Essen was the heart of German arms production. The Krupp family's steelworks manufactured the heavy artillery that dominated the Western Front. In *Greenmantle*, this industrial power is thematically linked to the novel's central concern — the German ability to manufacture not just weapons but ideological movements, "Holy Wars" produced as systematically as artillery pieces.
