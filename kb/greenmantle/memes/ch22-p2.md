# Ch. 22: The Guns of the North (cont'd, part 2)

[022-001] Then out of the mist there came a voice.

[022-002] It was a woman’s voice, high, penetrating, and sweet, but it spoke in

[022-003] no tongue I knew. Only [[sandy-arbuthnot|Sandy]] understood. He made a sudden movement as

[022-004] if to defend himself against a blow.

[022-005] The speaker came into clear sight on the glacis a yard or two away.

[022-006] Mine was the first face she saw.

[022-007] “I come to offer terms,” she said in English. “Will you permit me to

[022-008] enter?”

[022-009] I could do nothing except take off my cap and say, “Yes, ma’am.”

[022-010] [[john-blenkiron|Blenkiron]], snuggled up against the parapet, was cursing furiously below

[022-011] his breath.

[022-012] She climbed up the _kranz_ and stepped over the edge as lightly as a

[022-013] deer. Her clothes were strange—spurred boots and breeches over which

[022-014] fell a short green kirtle. A little cap skewered with a jewelled pin

[022-015] was on her head, and a cape of some coarse country cloth hung from her

[022-016] shoulders. She had rough gauntlets on her hands, and she carried for

[022-017] weapon a riding-whip. The fog-crystals clung to her hair, I remember,

[022-018] and a silvery film of fog lay on her garments.

[022-019] I had never before thought of her as beautiful. Strange, uncanny,

[022-020] wonderful, if you like, but the word beauty had too kindly and human a

[022-021] sound for such a face. But as she stood with heightened colour, her

[022-022] eyes like stars, her poise like a wild bird’s, I had to confess that

[022-023] she had her own loveliness. She might be a devil, but she was also a

[022-024] queen. I considered that there might be merits in the prospect of

[022-025] riding by her side into Jerusalem.

[022-026] Sandy stood rigid, his face very grave and set. She held out both hands

[022-027] to him, speaking softly in Turkish. I noticed that the six Companions

[022-028] had disappeared from the _castrol_ and were somewhere out of sight on

[022-029] the farther side.

[022-030] I do not know what she said, but from her tone, and above all from her

[022-031] eyes, I judged that she was pleading—pleading for his return, for his

[022-032] partnership in her great adventure; pleading, for all I knew, for his

[022-033] love.

[022-034] His expression was like a death-mask, his brows drawn tight in a little

[022-035] frown and his jaw rigid.

[022-036] “Madam,” he said, “I ask you to tell your business quick and to tell it

[022-037] in English. My friends must hear it as well as me.”

[022-038] “Your friends!” she cried. “What has a prince to do with these

[022-039] hirelings? Your slaves, perhaps, but not your friends.”

[022-040] “My friends,” Sandy repeated grimly. “You must know, Madam, that I am a

[022-041] British officer.”

[022-042] That was beyond doubt a clean staggering stroke. What she had thought

[022-043] of his origin God knows, but she had never dreamed of this. Her eyes

[022-044] grew larger and more lustrous, her lips parted as if to speak, but her

[022-045] voice failed her. Then by an effort she recovered herself, and out of

[022-046] that strange face went all the glow of youth and ardour. It was again

[022-047] the unholy mask I had first known.

[022-048] “And these others?” she asked in a level voice.

[022-049] “One is a brother officer of my regiment. The other is an American

[022-050] friend. But all three of us are on the same errand. We came east to

[022-051] destroy Greenmantle and your devilish ambitions. You have yourself

[022-052] destroyed your prophets, and now it is your turn to fail and disappear.

[022-053] Make no mistake, Madam; that folly is over. I will tear this sacred

[022-054] garment into a thousand pieces and scatter them on the wind. The people

[022-055] wait today for the revelation, but none will come. You may kill us if

[022-056] you can, but we have at least crushed a lie and done service to our

[022-057] country.”

[022-058] I would not have taken my eyes from her face for a king’s ransom. I

[022-059] have written that she was a queen, and of that there is no manner of

[022-060] doubt. She had the soul of a conqueror, for not a flicker of weakness

[022-061] or disappointment marred her air. Only pride and the stateliest

[022-062] resolution looked out of her eyes.

[022-063] “I said I came to offer terms. I will still offer them, though they are

[022-064] other than I thought. For the fat American, I will send him home safely

[022-065] to his own country. I do not make war on such as he. He is Germany’s

[022-066] foe, not mine. You,” she said, turning fiercely on me, “I will hang

[022-067] before dusk.”

[022-068] Never in my life had I been so pleased. I had got my revenge at last.

[022-069] This woman had singled me out above the others as the object of her

[022-070] wrath, and I almost loved her for it.

[022-071] She turned to Sandy, and the fierceness went out of her face.

[022-072] “You seek the truth,” she said. “So also do I, and if we use a lie it

[022-073] is only to break down a greater. You are of my household in spirit, and

[022-074] you alone of all men I have seen are fit to ride with me on my mission.

[022-075] Germany may fail, but I shall not fail. I offer you the greatest career

[022-076] that mortal has known. I offer you a task which will need every atom of

[022-077] brain and sinew and [[courage-and-honour|courage]]. Will you refuse that [[fate-and-providence|destiny]]?”

[022-078] I do not know what effect this vapouring might have had in hot scented

[022-079] rooms, or in the languor of some rich garden; but up on that cold

[022-080] hill-top it was as unsubstantial as the mist around us. It sounded not

[022-081] even impressive, only crazy.

[022-082] “I stay with my friends,” said Sandy.

[022-083] “Then I will offer more. I will save your friends. They, too, shall

[022-084] share in my triumph.”

[022-085] This was too much for Blenkiron. He scrambled to his feet to speak the

[022-086] protest that had been wrung from his soul, forgot his game leg, and

[022-087] rolled back on the ground with a groan.

[022-088] Then she seemed to make a last appeal. She spoke in Turkish now, and I

[022-089] do not know what she said, but I judged it was the plea of a woman to

[022-090] her lover. Once more she was the proud beauty, but there was a tremor

[022-091] in her pride—I had almost written tenderness. To listen to her was like

[022-092] horrid treachery, like eavesdropping on something pitiful. I know my

[022-093] cheeks grew scarlet and Blenkiron turned away his head.

[022-094] Sandy’s face did not move. He spoke in English.

[022-095] “You can offer me nothing that I desire,” he said. “I am the servant of

[022-096] my country, and her enemies are mine. I can have neither part nor lot

[022-097] with you. That is my answer, Madam von Einem.”

[022-098] Then her steely restraint broke. It was like a dam giving before a

[022-099] pent-up mass of icy water. She tore off one of her gauntlets and hurled

[022-100] it in his face. Implacable hate looked out of her eyes.

[022-101] “I have done with you,” she cried. “You have scorned me, but you have

[022-102] dug your own grave.”

[022-103] She leaped on the parapet and the next second was on the glacis. Once

[022-104] more the mist had fled, and across the hollow I saw a field-gun in

[022-105] place and men around it who were not Turkish. She waved her hand to

[022-106] them, and hastened down the hillside.

[022-107] But at that moment I heard the whistle of a long-range Russian shell.

[022-108] Among the boulders there was the dull shock of an explosion and a

[022-109] mushroom of red earth. It all passed in an instant of time: I saw the

[022-110] gunners on the road point their hands and I heard them cry; I heard

[022-111] too, a kind of sob from Blenkiron—all this before I realized myself

[022-112] what had happened. The next thing I saw was Sandy, already beyond the

[022-113] glacis, leaping with great bounds down the hill. They were shooting at

[022-114] him, but he heeded them not. For the space of a minute he was out of

[022-115] sight, and his whereabouts was shown only by the patter of bullets.

[022-116] Then he came back—walking quite slowly up the last slope, and he was

[022-117] carrying something in his arms. The enemy fired no more; they realized

[022-118] what had happened.

[022-119] He laid his burden down gently in a corner of the _castrol_. The cap

[022-120] had fallen off, and the hair was breaking loose. The face was very

[022-121] white but there was no wound or bruise on it.

[022-122] “She was killed at once,” I heard him saying. “Her back was broken by a

[022-123] shell-fragment. Dick, we must bury her here ... You see, she ... she

[022-124] liked me. I can make her no return but this.”

[022-125] We set the Companions to guard, and with infinite slowness, using our

[022-126] hands and our knives, we made a shallow grave below the eastern

[022-127] parapet. When it was done we covered her face with the linen cloak

[022-128] which Sandy had worn that morning. He lifted the body and laid it

[022-129] reverently in its place.

[022-130] “I did not know that anything could be so light,” he said.

[022-131] It wasn’t for me to look on at that kind of scene. I went to the

[022-132] parapet with Blenkiron’s field-glasses and had a stare at our friends

[022-133] on the road. There was no Turk there, and I guessed why, for it would

[022-134] not be easy to use the men of Islam against the wearer of the green

[022-135] ephod. The enemy were German or Austrian, and they had a field-gun.

[022-136] They seemed to have got it laid on our fort; but they were waiting. As

[022-137] I looked I saw behind them a massive figure I seemed to recognize.

[022-138] Stumm had come to see the destruction of his enemies.

[022-139] To the east I saw another gun in the fields just below the main road.

[022-140] They had got us on both sides, and there was no way of escape. [[hilda-von-einem|Hilda]]

[022-141] von Einem was to have a noble pyre and goodly company for the dark

[022-142] journey.

[022-143] Dusk was falling now, a clear bright dusk where the stars pricked

[022-144] through a sheen of amethyst. The artillery were busy all around the

[022-145] horizon, and towards the pass on the other road, where [[palantuken|Fort Palantuken]]

[022-146] stood, there was the dust and smoke of a furious bombardment. It seemed

[022-147] to me, too, that the guns on the other fronts had come nearer. Deve

[022-148] Boyun was hidden by a spur of hill, but up in the north, white clouds,

[022-149] like the streamers of evening, were hanging over the [[euphrates-river|Euphrates]] glen.

[022-150] The whole firmament hummed and twanged like a taut string that has been

[022-151] struck ...

[022-152] As I looked, the gun to the west fired—the gun where Stumm was. The

[022-153] shell dropped ten yards to our right. A second later another fell

[022-154] behind us.

[022-155] Blenkiron had dragged himself to the parapet. I don’t suppose he had

[022-156] ever been shelled before, but his face showed curiosity rather than

[022-157] fear.

[022-158] “Pretty poor shooting, I reckon,” he said.

[022-159] “On the contrary,” I said, “they know their business. They’re

[022-160] bracketing ...”

[022-161] The words were not out of my mouth when one fell right among us. It

[022-162] struck the far rim of the _castrol_, shattering the rock, but bursting

[022-163] mainly outside. We all ducked, and barring some small scratches no one

[022-164] was a penny the worse. I remember that much of the debris fell on Hilda

[022-165] von Einem’s grave.

[022-166] I pulled Blenkiron over the far parapet, and called on the rest to

[022-167] follow, meaning to take cover on the rough side of the hill. But as we

[022-168] showed ourselves shots rang out from our front, shots fired from a

[022-169] range of a few hundred yards. It was easy to see what had happened.

[022-170] Riflemen had been sent to hold us in rear. They would not assault so

[022-171] long as we remained in the _castrol_, but they would block any attempt

[022-172] to find safety outside it. Stumm and his gun had us at their mercy.

[022-173] We crouched below the parapet again. “We may as well toss for it,” I

[022-174] said. “There’s only two ways—to stay here and be shelled or try to

[022-175] break through those fellows behind. Either’s pretty unhealthy.”
