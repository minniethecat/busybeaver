# Rumania

**Rumania** (modern Romania) appears in *Greenmantle* as a neutral kingdom whose geographical position makes it a potential corridor for German influence reaching toward Russia. It is mentioned in the context of the geopolitical chessboard that forms the novel's background.

## In the Novel

Rumania is invoked in Constantinople as one of the pivot points of German strategic planning. In a discussion of the wider war, the alignment of neutral powers is enumerated:

> Rumania and Russia. [013-101]

The context suggests that German intelligence was actively working to bring Rumania into its sphere of influence, part of the broader campaign to encircle Russia from the south.

Hannay's cover identity as a Dutch engineer travelling through Germany to Constantinople places him on a route that skirts the neutral Balkans. Another traveller is noted as "coming through Russia and Rumania" [013-181], highlighting Rumania's role as a transit corridor between the Eastern Front and the Ottoman theatre.

## Strategic Significance

In the First World War, Rumania remained neutral until August 1916, when it entered the war on the Allied side. During the period of *Greenmantle*'s action (late 1915–early 1916), Rumania's neutrality was a critical uncertainty. The kingdom controlled the lower Danube and the Black Sea approaches, and its alignment would determine whether the Central Powers could establish a continuous land bridge from Berlin to Constantinople.
