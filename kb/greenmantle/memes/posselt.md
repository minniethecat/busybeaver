## Overview

General Posselt is the German Governor of Erzerum, the strategic fortress city in eastern Anatolia that serves as the headquarters for the German military mission's eastern operations. As the senior German authority in the region, he controls access to the front lines and plays a pivotal role in the interrogation and clearance of Richard Hannay and his companions.

## Role at Erzerum

When Hannay and his party reach Erzerum, they must report to General Posselt for authorization to proceed further. However, tensions between the German military command and their Turkish allies complicate this meeting. A Turkish staff officer initially refuses access: "You can't see General Posselt," he said; "this is my business" [017-083].

Hannay insists on seeing Posselt directly, leveraging the name of Hilda von Einem to bypass the Turkish obstruction. He tells the German officer that "General Posselt will make things clear. You will see" [017-090]. The German officer's reaction to von Einem's name instantly changes the dynamic, suggesting Posselt was operating under her influence or authority.

## Character

Posselt is presented as a figure caught between competing loyalties — to the German military command, to the Ottoman allies, and to Hilda von Einem's mysterious mission. The German officer describes his predicament: "I'm torn in two between the Turks and my own countrymen. If I please one I offend the other, and the result is a damnable confusion" [017-090].

This dilemma reflects the broader tensions within the German-Ottoman alliance depicted throughout the novel. Posselt ultimately allows Hannay to proceed: "You can go on to Erzerum, but I shall send a man with you to see that you report to headquarters there" [017-090].
