## Overview

Dolly Riddell is a British officer who served as brigade machine-gun officer at Loos. Captured by the Germans when they "blew up a mine at the Quarries," he is encountered by Richard Hannay in a German prison camp where Hannay, under a Dutch cover identity, is being shown around by the camp authorities [004-091].

## The Prison Camp Encounter

When Hannay unexpectedly encounters Dolly Riddell in the prison camp, the situation is precarious. Hannay is operating under a false Dutch identity and any recognition by a fellow British officer would blow his cover. Dolly's "mouth was agape, and I saw he was going to speak" upon recognizing Hannay [004-104].

Hannay acts quickly, stumbling and spilling the prisoners' playing cards to create a moment of proximity. Kneeling to pick up the cards, he whispers urgently to Dolly: "I'm Hannay all right. For God's sake don't wink an eye. I'm here on a secret job" [004-109].

Dolly proves himself a quick study: "Dolly was playing his part well, smiling as if he was amused by the antics of a monkey" [004-104]. When the deputy-commandant reprimands Hannay for speaking to prisoners, Dolly further protects the ruse by complaining dismissively about "High-Dutch" in English, giving Hannay a pretext to exit the scene grumbling angrily [004-111].

## Significance

Though Dolly Riddell appears only briefly, the encounter serves as a tense test of Hannay's cover identity. Dolly's quick-witted cooperation demonstrates the solidarity among British officers and the resourcefulness required of prisoners of war.
