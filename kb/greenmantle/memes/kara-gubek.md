# Kara Gubek

**Kara Gubek** is one of the two great forts guarding the Erzerum defensive position in eastern Anatolia. In *Greenmantle*, it is identified as the critical weak point in the Ottoman defences — a "broken reed" upon which the fate of the entire Russian campaign hangs.

## In the Novel

Hannay first observes Kara Gubek from a distance during his reconnaissance of the Erzerum lines:

> ...of the Euphrates I made out two big forts, Tafta and Kara Gubek... [019-046]

He immediately recognises its importance. Unlike the formidable Deve Boyun ridge, Kara Gubek is the vulnerable point:

> Kara Gubek was the point of interest. It stood on a rib of land between... [019-051]

## The Intelligence Report

Sandy Arbuthnot's reconnaissance produces a damning assessment. A captured document headed "Kara Gubek" states bluntly:

> "_No time to fortify..._" [019-060]

The strategic calculus is stark. The Russian plan recognises that the two forts are the linchpin:

> ...peaks Kara Gubek and Tafta must fall, and enemy will be on left rear of... [019-063]

And the grim conclusion follows:

> Kara Gubek depended the defence of Erzerum, and it was a broken reed if... [019-066]

## Strategic Significance

Kara Gubek's weakness was not fictional. The historical Erzerum offensive of February 1916 succeeded in part because the fortress defences were incomplete. In the novel, this real vulnerability becomes a plot mechanism: the Russian capture of Kara Gubek would unhinge the entire Ottoman position, and the German-Ottoman command's awareness of this weakness drives the desperate political machinations that Hannay must disrupt.
