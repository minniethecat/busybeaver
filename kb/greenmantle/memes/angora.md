# Angora

**Angora** (modern Ankara) is the first significant waypoint in Hannay's overland journey from Constantinople to the Erzerum front. In *Greenmantle*, it appears as a town marked by war — a staging post transformed by the passage of armies and the dislocation of conflict.

## In the Novel

Hannay reaches Angora after two days of travel from Constantinople, travelling by the German-built railway:

> Two days later, in the evening, we came to Angora, the first stage in... [016-134]

The railway journey itself is comfortable — a German *Schlafwagen* (sleeping car) that belies the harshness of the country it traverses:

> We had the railway to Angora, a very comfortable German _Schlafwagen_... [016-142]

But Angora reveals the reality of the Ottoman war effort. Hannay's hopes of securing a motorcar for the onward journey are dashed by the town's condition:

> ...remembering the state of affairs at Angora, I had no hope from that quarter. Cars... [017-028]

The town is described implicitly through comparison — like "Amiens in the retreat" [016-134], evoking the debris and disorganisation of a town caught behind a fighting front.

## Strategic Significance

Angora was a key station on the Baghdad Railway, the German-engineered line connecting Constantinople to the Anatolian interior and onward to Mesopotamia. In 1915–16, the railway was the primary logistical artery for Ottoman forces in the eastern theatre. Hannay's passage through Angora underscores the German technical presence in the Ottoman Empire — a comfortable sleeping car in a landscape of war.
