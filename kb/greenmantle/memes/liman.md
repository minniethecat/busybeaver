# General Liman von Sanders

General Liman von Sanders (referred to in the novel simply as "General Liman" or "old General Liman") is a senior German military officer serving in Constantinople during the events of John Buchan's 1916 novel *Greenmantle*. He represents the official German military presence in the Ottoman capital.

## Historical Background

Liman von Sanders (1855–1929) was a real historical figure — a German general who headed the German military mission to the Ottoman Empire from 1913. He played a significant role in reorganizing the Ottoman Army and later commanded Ottoman forces during the Gallipoli campaign. In *Greenmantle*, Buchan uses him as a background figure representing the formal German-Ottoman military alliance.

## Role in the Novel

General Liman appears only at the margins of the narrative but serves an important structural function: he represents the official, somewhat ineffectual German military hierarchy against which the novel's more dangerous conspirators operate.

Blenkiron, Hannay's American ally, dismisses Liman's intelligence with characteristic bluntness: "I'm the best informed man in Constantinople, for old General Liman only hears one side, and mostly lies at that" [^1]. He later remarks that "our landlady has a bigger brain than Enver and Liman" [^1] — positioning Madame von Einem as the true strategic mind behind the Greenmantle plot.

When Colonel von Stumm needs to move Hannay and his companions to Erzerum, he invokes Liman's authority: "Whoever hinders us will have to answer to General von Liman" [^1]. Liman himself is absent during this episode, having "gone up the line to Nish to meet the Kaiser" [^1] with Ambassador Metternich.

## Significance

Liman functions as a narrative foil — the "official" German power structure that the novel's real antagonists (Hilda von Einem, Stumm, and the Greenmantle conspirators) operate beneath and around. His absence from Constantinople at a critical moment allows the conspiracy to proceed unchecked through official channels, while his name provides cover for the protagonists' forced journey east.
