# Euphrates River

The **Euphrates River** in *Greenmantle* appears near the climax of the narrative, as Richard Hannay and his companions approach the fortress city of Erzerum. Its appearance signals that the party has reached the eastern threshold of Anatolia, on the edge of the Mesopotamian plain that is their ultimate objective.

## In the Novel

### The Recognition

The Euphrates is identified by Hannay himself as the party surveys the landscape from a vantage point near Erzerum. The recognition carries symbolic weight — this is one of the great rivers of history and civilisation:

> "I believe that river is the Euphrates," I said. [017-028]

### The Evening View

Later, in the final stages of the Erzerum episode, the Euphrates glen is described as a place of gathering shadow:

> ...the mists of evening were hanging over the Euphrates glen. [022-149]

## Geographic Context

The Euphrates flows from eastern Anatolia through modern Syria and Iraq, joining the Tigris to form the Shatt al-Arab. In the strategic geography of the First World War, the Euphrates valley was a corridor of military operations in the Mesopotamian campaign.
