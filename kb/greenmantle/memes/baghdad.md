# Baghdad

**Baghdad** in *Greenmantle* is referenced primarily through the Baghdad Railway, the German-engineered infrastructure project that symbolised German imperial ambitions in the Ottoman Empire. The railway itself is a strategic through-route connecting Berlin to the Persian Gulf via Constantinople and Baghdad.

## In the Novel

The Baghdad Railway appears as a cartographic detail that carries great strategic weight. When Hannay examines a map showing the route to the East, the railway is a key feature:

> ...the map showed the Baghdad railway and the through route to Mesopotamia. [005-025]

This single reference encapsulates the geopolitical stakes of the novel — the railway was Germany's path to influence in the Middle East, and British strategy aimed to block it.

## Historical Context

The Baghdad Railway (Bagdadbahn) was a major pre-war German project, running from Konya in Anatolia through Baghdad to Basra on the Persian Gulf. At the outbreak of war in 1914, the line was incomplete, and its strategic potential drove much of the military planning in the Mesopotamian campaign.
