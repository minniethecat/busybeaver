# Holy War

## Overview

The Holy War (jehad) is the German strategic objective in *Greenmantle* — an attempt to ignite a pan-Islamic religious uprising against the Allied powers, particularly Britain. Sir Walter Bullivant believes that the Germans have moved beyond crude propaganda toward something more potent and dangerous [001-031].

## In Greenmantle

The concept is introduced early in Sir Walter's briefing. He notes that "the Kaiser proclaims a Holy War and calls himself Hadji Mohammed Guilliamo" but observes that this "seems to have fallen pretty flat" [001-007]. The failure of the Kaiser's crude appeal is precisely what makes the situation dangerous — the Germans have realized they need something more authentic, which is where the Greenmantle prophecy enters the picture [001-031].

Sir Walter references "old Von der Goltz" who had prophesied a jehad years before — a warning that was dismissed at the time but now appears prescient [001-031]. The "dry wind blowing through the East" metaphor captures the novel's view of religious fervor as a natural force awaiting ignition [001-020].

## Key References

The Kaiser's Holy War proclamation is described as having "fallen pretty flat" despite its theatrical claims — calling himself "Hadji Mohammed Guilliamo" and asserting the Hohenzollerns are "descended from the Prophet" [001-008]. Sir Walter contrasts Constantinople's secularized elite with the provinces where "Islam is strong" and religious feeling remains potent [001-016]. The German strategy shifts from crude propaganda to the cultivation of the Greenmantle figure — a more sophisticated form of religious manipulation [001-031].

## Thematic Significance

The Holy War concept allows Buchan to explore the limits of Western rationalism when confronted with religious motivation. The British characters — products of the Edwardian establishment — must acknowledge that faith moves people in ways that bullets and trade agreements cannot. This recognition carries both strategic insight and Orientalist anxiety about the "irrational" East.

## Related Characters

Sir Walter Bullivant is the first to grasp the Holy War threat. Hilda von Einem is the German operative most closely associated with the plan to weaponize religious belief. Sandy Arbuthnot's understanding of Islamic culture makes him the British agent best equipped to counter the threat at its source.
