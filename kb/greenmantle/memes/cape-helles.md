# Cape Helles

**Cape Helles** is the southern tip of the Gallipoli Peninsula, where British and French forces made their principal landings in April 1915. In *Greenmantle*, it serves as Blenkiron's entry point into the Ottoman theatre — a beachhead that was still actively contested when the novel's characters converged on Constantinople.

## In the Novel

Blenkiron arrives at Cape Helles during the final stages of the Gallipoli campaign. The sight of him there, improbably cheerful amid the wreckage of a failing amphibious operation, is a moment of dark comedy:

> Cape Helles. What rejoiced me was the sight of Blenkiron, as bland as a... [010-119]

Even as the Allied forces were preparing to evacuate the peninsula, the guns continued:

> ...evacuated, but I could hear the guns going hard at Cape Helles. [013-004]

## Historical Context

The landings at Cape Helles on 25 April 1915 were the southern prong of the Gallipoli campaign, intended to capture the high ground and advance toward the Dardanelles forts. Like the ANZAC landings further north, the Helles operation stalled against determined Ottoman defence. The Allied evacuation of Cape Helles was completed on 9 January 1916 — roughly contemporary with the events of *Greenmantle*.
