# Suicidogenic Current

## Overview

The **suicidogenic current** (*courant suicidogène*) is Durkheim's metaphor for the collective force that traverses society and impels individuals toward suicide. It is not a physical force but a social one — a tendency inherent in the collective condition that expresses itself through individual acts. (004-035)

Each society, Durkheim argued, has a collective inclination toward suicide that is as real and measurable as its mortality rate. The suicidogenic current is the mechanism through which social facts exercise their coercive power over individual behavior.

## Mechanism

The current operates not by directly causing any specific individual to die by suicide, but by creating a **collective disposition** that weakens the individual's resistance to self-destruction. When social integration is low (egoistic current) or regulation is absent (anomic current), the collective disposition intensifies and more individuals succumb.
