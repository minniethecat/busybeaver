# Social Regulation

## Overview

**Social regulation** (*régulation sociale*) is, alongside [[Social Integration]], one of the two fundamental axes of Durkheim's suicide typology. Where integration concerns the horizontal bonds connecting individuals to groups, regulation concerns the vertical authority of norms over desires. (005-030)

Human passions, Durkheim argued, are naturally unlimited. Only society — through law, morality, and custom — can set legitimate boundaries on aspiration. When this regulatory force weakens or becomes excessive, suicide results.

## Typology by Regulation

| Regulation Level | Suicide Type | Mechanism |
|-----------------|--------------|-----------|
| Too low | [[Anomie\|Anomic]] | Unbounded desires → chronic frustration |
| Too high | [[Fatalistic Suicide\|Fatalistic]] | Crushed aspirations → hopelessness |

The healthy condition is balanced regulation: norms that constrain without crushing.
