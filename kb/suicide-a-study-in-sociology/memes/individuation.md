# Individuation

## Overview

**Individuation** (*individualisation*) is the historical process through which individuals become increasingly differentiated from the collective consciousness. In Durkheim's analysis, this process is both the defining characteristic of modernity and a primary cause of the rising suicide rate in modern societies. (004-015)

As traditional collective beliefs weaken and individual judgment gains authority, the protective integration that once insulated individuals from suicide erodes. The same process that produces intellectual liberty and scientific progress also increases vulnerability to egoistic suicide.

## Ambivalence

Durkheim's analysis of individuation is notably ambivalent — it is neither condemned as pure pathology nor celebrated as pure progress:

- It enables critical thought, science, and individual rights
- It dissolves the collective beliefs that give existence meaning and purpose
- The suicide rate is thus a "price" paid for modernity
