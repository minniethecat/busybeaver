# Fatalistic Suicide

## Overview

**Fatalistic suicide** (*suicide fataliste*) is Durkheim's fourth and least developed type, mentioned only briefly in a footnote to *Le Suicide*. It results from **excessive regulation** — the opposite of anomic suicide. Where anomie is the absence of norms, fatalism is their oppressive abundance. (005-050)

Durkheim's examples: the suicide of slaves, of married women without divorce rights, of very young husbands — those whose futures are mercilessly blocked and whose passions are violently choked by oppressive discipline.

## Mechanism

Fatalistic suicide occurs when social regulation is so intense and inflexible that the individual sees no possibility of change. The future is "mercilessly blocked" (*impitoyablement fermé*). This is the polar opposite of anomie — not the absence of rules but their crushing excess.
