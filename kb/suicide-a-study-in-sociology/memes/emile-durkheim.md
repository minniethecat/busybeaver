# Emile Durkheim

## Overview

**Emile Durkheim** (1858-1917) was a French sociologist who established sociology as an autonomous academic discipline. His 1897 work *Le Suicide: etude de sociologie* demonstrated that even the most private individual act is socially determined.

## Le Suicide (1897)

In *Le Suicide*, Durkheim identified four types of suicide — [[Egoistic Suicide]], [[Altruistic Suicide]], [[Anomie|anomic]], and [[Fatalistic Suicide|fatalistic]] — organized along axes of [[Social Integration]] and [[Social Regulation]]. The [[Suicide Rate]] was shown to be a [[Social Fact]] determined by collective, not individual, forces.

## Methodological Legacy

Durkheim's concept of the social fact and his empirical demonstration that social structure determines individual behavior remain foundational to sociology. His analysis of individuation and anomie as consequences of modernity continues to inform studies of contemporary social problems.
