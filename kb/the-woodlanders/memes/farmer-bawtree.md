# Farmer Bawtree

Farmer Bawtree is a minor but recurring figure in the Hintock community, a farmer who keeps the cider-house and appears at various social gatherings — the Christmas party at Giles Winterborne's, the timber auction, and Grace Fitzpiers's return. He functions as part of the rustic chorus that provides comic relief and folk wisdom throughout the novel.

## 身份与背景

Farmer Bawtree is described as "who kept the cider-house" [005-018], situating him among the established smallholders of the Hintock district. He appears alongside other local tradesmen — Timothy Tangs the hollow-turner, Robert Creedle, the dairyman, and the master-blacksmith from Great Hintock — marking him as one of the regular members of the rural community whose lives revolve around seasonal work and occasional sociability [005-018] [033-055].

## 性格与特征

Bawtree is characterized by a blunt, emphatic manner of speech and a willingness to offer opinions. When the conversation turns to the new doctor Fitzpiers and his mysterious books, Bawtree dismisses the rumors: "'Tis only an old woman's tale," he says [005-180]. Yet he also shows a pragmatic openness to change, declaring he will switch his medical custom from old Jones to the new doctor: "for my part I shall take my custom from old Jones and go to this one directly I've anything the matter with me" [006-010]. At Grace's Christmas party, he makes a gallant — if rustic — gesture: "Kiss it and make it well," he observes after Grace is sprinkled with water [014-020].

## 关键关系

- **[[timothy-tangs]]**：Bawtree and Timothy Tangs the hollow-turner are repeatedly paired — they arrive together at parties, play cards together, and function as a comic double act in the novel's social scenes [013-147] [014-032].
- **[[suke-damson]]**：Both appear among the villagers watching Grace's arrival [031-110].
- **[[giles-winterborne]]**：Bawtree is a guest at Winterborne's Christmas party, and Winterborne later regrets inviting "such people as Bawtree and the hollow-turner" when he contrasts them with Grace's refinement [014-027].
- **[[old-jones]]**：Bawtree mentions he will leave old Jones's medical practice for the new doctor Fitzpiers [006-010].

## 叙事线索

Bawtree first appears at the opening gathering in Mr. Melbury's pit, standing with Timothy Tangs and Robert Creedle by a fire [005-018]. He participates in the communal discussion about the new doctor, offering both skepticism and pragmatism [005-180] [006-010]. At Winterborne's Christmas party, he and the hollow-turner are among the guests; after supper, the two monopolize the card game [014-032], their "measured jingle" becoming a background rhythm to the evening [014-046]. He reappears among the crowd watching Grace Fitzpiers's return to Hintock [031-110] [033-055]. In the novel's final chapters, Bawtree delivers a piece of marital folk wisdom about a quarrelling couple [066-087].

## 意义与解读

Farmer Bawtree belongs to the novel's chorus of rustic characters — the woodlanders proper — whose presence provides both local color and a grounding counterpoint to the emotional dramas of the central characters. His pairing with Timothy Tangs as an inseparable comic duo (card partners, fellow guests) is a structural device Hardy uses to populate the background of social scenes with recognizable, consistent minor figures. Bawtree's willingness to abandon old Jones for the new doctor also reflects the novel's broader theme of traditional ways yielding to modern influences — even among those most rooted in the old order.
