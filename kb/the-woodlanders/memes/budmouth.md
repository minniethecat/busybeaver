## Overview

Budmouth (based on Weymouth in Dorset) serves as a significant off-stage location in *The Woodlanders*. It is a coastal town that represents both professional advancement and escape — Dr. Fitzpiers contemplates purchasing a practice there, and Mrs. Charmond intends to flee to Budmouth by steamer. Its seaside location marks it as a gateway between the secluded woodlands and the wider world, particularly the continent beyond.

## Fitzpiers's Budmouth Practice

Fitzpiers negotiates the purchase of a medical practice in Budmouth [030-071], presenting it as a step up from his rural position in Hintock. The practice represents his ambition to leave the woodland — "hardly more than a harbour" [030-071] — and return to a more cosmopolitan environment.

Grace Melbury becomes aware of the Budmouth negotiations through intercepted correspondence. A letter to Budmouth [035-160] confirms Fitzpiers's intentions, and Grace confronts him with the knowledge. The Budmouth practice becomes a point of marital tension: Fitzpiers's desire to leave Hintock, and Grace's realization that her husband sees their life in the woodland as temporary and beneath him.

## Escape and Flight

Budmouth functions as a point of departure. Mrs. Charmond plans to cross the Channel to Budmouth by steamer [053-124] as part of her flight from Hintock and the consequences of her liaison with Fitzpiers. The town's position on the coast makes it the natural exit point from Hardy's Wessex — the last English soil before the continent.

Fitzpiers himself sees Budmouth as preferable to Hintock: "Budmouth — it will divert all suspicion" [048-119], suggesting that a move to the coastal town would allow a fresh start away from the scrutiny of the woodland community.

## Geography

Budmouth lies on the Dorset coast, accessible from Hintock by road and connected to the continent by steamer. Its position makes it a natural point of transition — geographically between land and sea, and socially between the insular woodland world and the anonymity of urban or continental life.
