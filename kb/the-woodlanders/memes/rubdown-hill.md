# Rubdown Hill

Rubdown Hill is a prominent geographical feature in the Hintock region of Thomas Hardy's *The Woodlanders*. Located in the upland landscape surrounding the Hintock woodlands, it serves as a landmark for travelers and becomes significant in the narrative as the setting for two pivotal meetings involving Grace Melbury.

## 地理位置

Rubdown Hill rises above the Hintock area, forming part of the elevated terrain that separates Little Hintock from the surrounding parishes. The hill lies along the highway connecting the Hintock settlements to the wider Wessex countryside. Tim Tangs, when describing a circuitous walking route through the local parishes, lists "Rubdown and Millshot" as waypoints along a path that also passes through Delborough [061-090], suggesting the hill's role in the local geography as part of a network of rural landmarks.

## 叙事中的角色

### 与 Giles 的黄昏相遇

The first significant event at Rubdown Hill occurs before Grace Melbury's marriage to Edred Fitzpiers. During a period of emotional distance between Grace and Giles Winterborne, the narrator notes that Giles "had never come into close comradeship with her since her engagement to Fitzpiers, with the brief exception of the evening encounter on Rubdown Hill, when she met him with his cider apparatus" [050-178]. This encounter, though described as "of too cursory a kind for insight" [050-180], represents one of the last moments of direct personal contact between Grace and Giles before her marriage.

### Grace 途经之地

Later in the narrative, after Grace has fled from Fitzpiers following his infidelity, she makes a determined journey back to Hintock. The text describes her progress: "after an hour's progress, she passed over Rubdown Hill, and onward towards that same Hintock, and that same house, out of which she had fled a few days before in irresistible alarm" [057-085]. The hill thus marks a geographical and symbolic threshold — crossing it signals Grace's return to the domestic world she had attempted to escape.

### 约定的重逢地点

In the novel's resolution, when Grace agrees to meet Fitzpiers to discuss their possible reconciliation, she stipulates "that the place of meeting should be the top of Rubdown Hill" [061-045], with the additional condition that Marty South accompany her. The choice of Rubdown Hill as neutral ground — an open, elevated space visible from a distance — reflects Grace's cautious approach to rebuilding trust with her estranged husband.

## 象征意义

Rubdown Hill represents the transitional spaces in Hardy's Wessex — neither fully within the enclosed woodland world of Hintock nor entirely in the outside world. Grace's crossings of the hill correspond to major transitions in her emotional and marital journey: the last informal meeting with Giles, her return to Hintock after fleeing Fitzpiers, and the tentative steps toward reconciliation. The hill's elevated position and open character contrast with the enclosed, intimate spaces of the Hintock woodlands.
