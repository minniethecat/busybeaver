# Shottsford-Forum

Shottsford-Forum is a market town that appears in Thomas Hardy's novel *The Woodlanders*. It serves as a regional centre where characters travel for commerce, lodging, and social calls, and is notable as the location of the Crown Hotel.

## Role in the novel

Shottsford-Forum functions as one of the market towns within the Wessex geography of the novel. Characters frequently travel there by carriage or on foot.[^1] Farmer Bawtree, in conversation with Giles Winterborne, remarks that "Shottsford is Shottsford still—you can't victual your carcass there unless you've got money" [005-168], suggesting both its continuity and the expense of conducting business there.

The town is also associated with neighbouring settlements. Middleton Abbey is described as being "near Shottsford," where a relative of Mrs. Charmond's late husband resides [036-032].

## Key events

Grace Melbury travels to Shottsford-Forum to visit an acquaintance, during which she falls ill. Her father, George Melbury, returns from Shottsford-Forum to learn of her condition [045-022]. He had hired a carriage "all the way from Shottsford," prompting his wife's concern over the expense [045-042].

Fitzpiers, recovering from an injury, is expected by Melbury to ride "along the Shottsford road" to meet his ailing wife, but instead goes elsewhere [045-058]. Later, a letter Grace receives from Fitzpiers is written at "Shottsford or Port-Bredy, or still better, Budmouth," as he attempts to divert suspicion from Hintock House [048-118].

The Crown Hotel at Shottsford-Forum is the establishment where Grace's return fly is paid for after her illness [046-143].

## Wessex geography

Shottsford-Forum is one of several Wessex towns referenced in the novel's geography, alongside Sherton Abbas, Budmouth, and Port-Bredy. The "Shottsford road" [045-058] connects the town to the Hintocks, forming part of the network of rural routes that characters traverse throughout the narrative.

[^1]: Bawtree, the timber-merchant's employee, asks Giles about "the latest news at Shottsford yesterday" [005-166], indicating regular traffic between the Hintocks and the town.
