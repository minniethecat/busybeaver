## Overview

Mrs. Melbury is the second wife of timber merchant George Melbury and stepmother to Grace Melbury in *The Woodlanders*. As "the second Mrs. Melbury" [003-092], she occupies a distinct position in the household — neither Grace's biological mother nor a dominant domestic figure, but a steady, practical presence whose plain observations frequently cut through her husband's grander schemes.

## Role in the Household

Mrs. Melbury manages the domestic sphere with quiet competence. She is present at key family moments: dressing in her best attire for social occasions [013-076], taking off her silk train to help with practical tasks [013-118], and maintaining the household routines while her husband pursues his ambitions for Grace. Her character is defined by directness — "said Mrs. Melbury, dryly" [006-051] — and a pragmatic outlook that contrasts with George Melbury's more anxious temperament.

## Relationship with Grace

As Grace's stepmother, Mrs. Melbury serves as confidante and companion. She accompanies Grace on outings [026-149] and offers support during the complicated courtship with Giles Winterborne and Fitzpiers. When Grace's father fixates on social advancement, Mrs. Melbury provides a counterbalancing presence. She "placidly" attends to domestic duties during tense family dinners [015-089], suggesting a temperament that refuses to be drawn into the melodrama surrounding Grace's romantic prospects.
