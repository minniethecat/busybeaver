# Old Jones

Old Jones is the established physician of the Hintock district whose traditional practice is gradually eclipsed by the arrival of the younger, more fashionable Dr. Fitzpiers. He represents the old order of rural medicine — practical, familiar, but increasingly seen as outdated — and reappears at critical medical moments when Fitzpiers is unavailable.

## 身份与背景

Dr. Jones maintains a medical practice that covers the Hintock district, with its boundaries marked on a map as extending up to a certain point — beyond which Fitzpiers's practice begins [009-075]. When Fitzpiers first arrives, Jones's patients begin to drift away: he is described as "irrepressible old Dr. Jones" who has been "capturing patients" until Fitzpiers's arrival reverses the flow [044-113]. His practice is based about seven miles from Hintock House — a significant distance in a rural district where travel is slow [057-113].

## 性格与特征

Jones is portrayed as a competent but unglamorous practitioner whose medicine reflects an older tradition. Bawtree complains that "that last medicine old Jones gave me had no taste in it at all" [006-012] — a comment that captures the rustic preference for potent-tasting remedies over Jones's more measured prescriptions. Despite losing patients to Fitzpiers, Jones maintains his practice and responds when called in emergencies. When Mrs. Charmond's condition worsens, "old Dr. Jones" attends and "began to look serious" at the gravity of the situation [049-134]. He writes to summon a London specialist when the case exceeds his capabilities [049-151].

## 关键关系

- **[[dr-edred-fitzpiers]]**：Jones's practice is directly threatened by Fitzpiers's arrival. Patients like Bawtree switch their custom from Jones to the newcomer [006-011]. Yet there is no direct animosity — they operate in parallel spheres, with Jones serving as a fallback when Fitzpiers is absent or occupied [057-113].
- **[[felice-charmond]]**：Jones attends Mrs. Charmond during her final illness, recognizing the seriousness of her condition and calling for a London surgeon [049-108] [049-134] [049-151].
- **[[grace-melbury]]**：When Grace falls ill after the events at Hintock House, "Old Jones was called in; he murmured something about a chill" [059-147].

## 叙事线索

Jones is first mentioned when Farmer Bawtree and others discuss the new doctor's arrival and Bawtree declares he will abandon Jones's practice [006-011] [006-012]. The boundary between Jones's and Fitzpiers's territories is marked on a map [009-075]. As Fitzpiers's reputation grows, Jones continues practising — the villagers who had "so long despised" him now find themselves caught between two doctors [033-129]. Jones is called in during Mrs. Charmond's final illness, attending her alongside Fitzpiers and ultimately summoning a London specialist [049-108] [049-134] [049-151]. Later, he treats Grace Fitzpiers [059-147].

## 意义与解读

Old Jones embodies the novel's theme of old ways yielding to new. His eclipse by Fitzpiers is not due to incompetence — Jones recognizes serious illness and knows when to call for help — but to the allure of the new. Fitzpiers represents modernity, scientific ambition, and metropolitan training; Jones represents the familiar, the traditional, the locally rooted. That Jones is the one called when Fitzpiers is unavailable or when a patient needs continuity of care suggests that Hardy is not simply celebrating the new at the expense of the old. The two doctors represent complementary modes of medical practice, and the community needs both.
