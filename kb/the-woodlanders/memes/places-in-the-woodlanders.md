## The Geography of The Woodlanders

Hardy's fictional Wessex maps onto the real Dorset landscape with deliberate precision. Little Hintock corresponds to a hamlet near what is now Melbury Osmond; Sherton Abbas is Sherborne; Budmouth is Weymouth. The novel's geography is itself a narrative device — the isolation of the woodlands from the wider world mirrors the social and emotional isolations that drive the plot.
