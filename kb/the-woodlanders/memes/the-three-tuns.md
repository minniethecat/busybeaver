# The Three Tuns

The Three Tuns is a tavern in [[sherton-abbas|Sherton Abbas]], the market town nearest to [[little-hintock|Little Hintock]]. It serves as a social gathering place for the local community and appears in several key scenes of *The Woodlanders*.

## Location and Character

Situated in Sherton Abbas, The Three Tuns is the kind of public house that has long served the region's inhabitants. [[giles-winterborne|Giles Winterborne]] is particularly associated with the establishment — he tells [[grace-melbury|Grace Melbury]] that he has "felt at home there these twenty years" [051-172], indicating that it is a fixture of his adult life and a place where he feels at ease in his own social world.

## Role in the Novel

The Three Tuns figures in a crucial exchange between Grace and Giles. When Grace admits that she felt "out of place" at The Three Tuns [051-171], the confession becomes a marker of the social distance that her education has created between her and the Hintock community. Giles takes her discomfort as a personal slight, and when she later references "not liking The Three Tuns" [052-154] as part of an explanation for her behavior toward him, the tavern becomes emblematic of the class divide that separates them — Grace has been educated beyond the world of country inns, while Giles remains rooted in it.
