# Nellcombe Bottom

Nellcombe Bottom is a deeply shaded hollow in the Hintock woodlands in Thomas Hardy's *The Woodlanders*. Located in the heavier, damper soil of the oak woods beyond the area where Giles Winterborne works as a woodman, it is notable for its oppressive darkness and its association with local ghost lore. It is the site of a key narrative event: Edred Fitzpiers's mysterious fall from his horse.

## 地理位置

Nellcombe Bottom lies beyond the copse where Giles Winterborne works and past the oak groves, in the direction of Hintock House. The mare carrying Fitzpiers and his companion paces "through the copse where Winterborne had worked, and into the heavier soil where the oaks grew; past Great Willy, the largest oak in the wood, and thence towards Nellcombe Bottom" [046-004]. The hollow occupies the lower-lying ground typical of the Hintock area's varied topography, where "the stiff soil retained moisture for weeks after the uplands were dry" [038-141].

## 黑暗与迷信

Nellcombe Bottom is characterized by an intense, almost impenetrable darkness. The text describes it as "intensely dark now with overgrowth" [046-005], a darkness so profound that it has bred supernatural associations among the local population. The hollow is "popularly supposed to be haunted by the spirits of the fratricides exorcised from Hintock House" [046-006]. This local legend — of brothers who killed one another and whose unquiet spirits were driven out of the manor house only to settle in the nearby hollow — invests Nellcombe Bottom with a Gothic atmosphere that contrasts with the otherwise pastoral character of the Hintock landscape.

## Fitzpiers 的坠马

The most significant event at Nellcombe Bottom occurs during Fitzpiers's mysterious late-night ride. After his unexplained journey — which begins with the clock striking twelve at Owlscombe church and ends with him losing consciousness in the saddle — Fitzpiers is thrown from his horse. Grace is informed that the accident "happened in the wood the other side of Nellcombe Bottom, where 'tis said the ghosts of the brothers walk" [046-164]. The juxtaposition of the physical accident with the supernatural reputation of the place adds a layer of ambiguity characteristic of Hardy's treatment of folk belief: the fall may be purely accidental, or it may carry darker implications.

## 象征意义

Nellcombe Bottom functions as a counterpoint to the sunlit, productive woodland spaces where Giles Winterborne works. Its darkness and association with fratricide — the ultimate violation of kinship bonds — mirror the disintegration of relationships that occurs in the novel's second half: the estrangement of Grace and Fitzpiers, the death of Giles, and the severing of old ties in the Hintock community. It is the darkest point in the novel's geography, both literally and symbolically.
