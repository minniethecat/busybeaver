# Section 67

[067-001]“Now, my own, own [[love-and-desire|love]],” she whispered, “you are mine, and on’y mine;

[067-002]for she has forgot ’ee at last, although for her you died. But

[067-003]I—whenever I get up I’ll think of ’ee, and whenever I lie down I’ll

[067-004]think of ’ee. Whenever I plant the young larches I’ll think that none

[067-005]can plant as you planted; and whenever I split a gad, and whenever I

[067-006]turn the cider-wring, I’ll say none could do it like you. If ever I

[067-007]forget your name, let me forget home and Heaven!—But no, no, my love, I

[067-008]never can forget ’ee; for you was a _good_ man, and did good things!”
