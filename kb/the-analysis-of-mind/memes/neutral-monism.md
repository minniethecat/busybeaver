## Definition

Neutral monism is the metaphysical position that the fundamental constituents of reality are neither mental nor material but a "neutral stuff" out of which both mind and matter are constructed. Russell states this programmatically early in *The Analysis of Mind*: "the stuff of which the world is made is neither mental nor material, but a 'neutral stuff,' out of which both are constructed" (P01-002).

## Russell's Account

Russell develops neutral monism as a solution to the mind–matter problem that avoids the difficulties of both dualism and reductive materialism. He argues that "both mind and matter are composed of a neutral-stuff which, in isolation, is neither mental nor material" (001-037). The same neutral entities appear as mental when arranged in one context (as part of a biography or consciousness-stream) and as material when arranged in another (as part of a physical system).

The concept draws significantly on William James's notion of "pure experience." Russell approvingly notes that "if we call that stuff 'pure experience,' then knowing can easily be explained" (001-032). He aligns his position with the American New Realists, observing that they "speak of 'neutral' entities as the stuff out of which both mind and matter are constructed" (001-036), and that "the American realists call 'neutral' entities, which have neither the hardness and indestructibility of matter, nor the reference to objects which is supposed to characterize mind" (001-050).

In Russell's constructive program, "the" physical object — say, a table — "is to be neutral as between different observers" (005-009), constructed from particular sense-data rather than posited as an unknowable thing-in-itself. The world is ultimately to be "constructed out of a neutral stuff, whose causal laws have no such duality as that of psychology and physics" (015-002).

## Key Distinctions

- **Neutral monism vs. idealism**: Neutral monism denies that reality is fundamentally mental, making it distinct from Berkeleyan or Hegelian idealism
- **Neutral monism vs. dualism**: It rejects Descartes's two-substance ontology; mind and matter are differing arrangements of one underlying stuff
- **Neutral monism vs. materialism**: It denies that the mental can be reduced to the physical; both are constructions from something more basic

## Historical Context

The term "neutral monism" is associated with William James, Ernst Mach, and the American New Realists (particularly Ralph Barton Perry and Edwin Holt). Russell adopted the position after abandoning his earlier dualism, influenced by James's *Essays in Radical Empiricism* (1912) and by discussions with Wittgenstein. *The Analysis of Mind* (1921) represents Russell's most sustained development of the neutral monist framework.
