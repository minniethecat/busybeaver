# William Shakespeare

## Biography

William Shakespeare (1564–1616) was an English playwright, poet, and actor, widely regarded as the greatest writer in the English language. Born in Stratford-upon-Avon, he wrote approximately 39 plays and 154 sonnets. His works span tragedy, comedy, and history, and have been translated into every major living language.

## Philosophical Contributions

Shakespeare's contribution to philosophy lies not in systematic treatises but in his profound exploration of human nature, power, madness, and morality through dramatic form. Characters such as Hamlet, Macbeth, and Lear have served as enduring archetypes for philosophical reflection on agency, guilt, and the human condition.

## Nietzsche's View

Nietzsche's direct engagement with Shakespeare in *Twilight of the [[idol|Idol]]s* is minimal. The single reference appears in a translator's note to *Skirmishes of an Untimely Man*, which adapts Shakespeare's line "Well roared, lion" from *A Midsummer Night's Dream* (Act 5, Scene 1), noting that the lion symbolizes St. Mark in [[jesus|Christ]]ian literature and art [010-2440]. The adaptation serves as a rhetorical flourish within Nietzsche's polemic rather than a substantive engagement with Shakespeare's thought.

> "An adaptation of Shakespeare's 'Well roared, lion' (Mid. N. D., Act 5, Sc. i.), the lion, as is well known, being the symbol for St Mark in Christian literature and Art." [010-2440]
