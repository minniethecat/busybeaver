#### THE HAMMER SPEAKETH

[008-001] “Why so hard!”—said the diamond once unto the charcoal; “are we then

[008-002] not next of kin?”

[008-003] “Why so soft? O my brethren; this is my question to you. For are ye

[008-004] not—my brothers?

[008-005] “Why so soft, so servile and yielding? Why are your hearts so fond of

[008-006] denial and self-denial? How is it that so little fate looketh out from

[008-007] your eyes?

[008-008] “And if ye will not be men of fate and inexorable, how can ye hope one

[008-009] day to conquer with me?

[008-010] “And if your hardness will not sparkle, cut and divide, how can ye hope

[008-011] one day to create with me?

[008-012] “For all creators are hard. And it must seem to you blessed to stamp

[008-013] your hand upon millenniums as upon wax,—

[008-014] —Blessed to write upon the will of millenniums as upon brass,—harder

[008-015] than brass, nobler than brass.—Hard through and through is only the

[008-016] noblest.

[008-017] This new table of values, O my brethren, I set over your heads: Become

[008-018] hard.”

[008-019] —“Thus Spake [[zarathustra|Zarathustra]],”

[008-020] III., 29.
