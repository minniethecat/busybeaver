# Homer

## Biography

Homer (fl. c. 8th century BCE) is the name traditionally given to the composer of the *Iliad* and the *Odyssey*, the two foundational epics of ancient Greek literature. Whether Homer was a single historical individual or a name attached to an oral tradition is the subject of the "Homeric Question." The epics attributed to him shaped Greek education, religion, and self-understanding for over a millennium and established the narrative and formal conventions of the Western epic tradition.

## Philosophical Contributions

Homer's philosophical importance lies in his articulation of a pre-philosophical worldview that later Greek thinkers — from the [[pre-socratics|Pre-Socratics]] to [[plato|Plato]] — would both draw upon and react against. The Homeric epics present a cosmos governed by fate, honor, and the interventions of anthropomorphic gods. This vision of heroic excellence (*aretē*), achieved through deeds of strength and courage on the battlefield, provided the aristocratic value system that Nietzsche saw as the original, healthy expression of Greek culture before its corruption by [[socratic-turn|Socratic rationalism]].

## Nietzsche's View

Nietzsche's reference to Homer in *Twilight of the [[idol|Idol]]s* is brief but situated within a revealing context. In the "My Impossible People" section of *Skirmishes of an Untimely Man*, he describes "the brothers [[goncourt-brothers|Goncourt]], or the two Ajaxes fighting with Homer" [006-009], with music by [[offenbach|Offenbach]]. The image combines the Homeric heroes Ajax (the Greater and the Lesser) with the modern French literary brothers in a comic-operatic tableau. The passage does not critique Homer himself but rather uses Homeric imagery to mock the pretensions of modern literary figures. Nietzsche's deeper admiration for Homeric Greece is evident elsewhere in his corpus, particularly in his elevation of the Homeric noble ideal as the pre-Socratic golden age of Greek culture.

> "The brothers Goncourt, or the two Ajaxes fighting with Homer. Music by Offenbach." [006-009]

## Key Works

- *Iliad* (c. 8th century BCE) — Epic of the Trojan War and the wrath of Achilles
- *Odyssey* (c. 8th century BCE) — Epic of Odysseus's ten-year journey home
