# Will to Power

## Definition

The Will to Power (Wille zur Macht) is Nietzsche's central philosophical principle. It asserts that the fundamental drive of all living beings is not self-preservation (as [[darwin|Darwin]] and Spinoza held) but the active striving to expand one's power, to overcome resistances, and to impose one's own form upon the world. Nietzsche presents this as a psychological, biological, and metaphysical principle.

## In *Twilight of the Idols*

In Chapter 6 (*Skirmishes in a War with the Age*), Nietzsche connects the will to power to human achievement: "triumph over gravitation, man's will to power" (006-211). He identifies the feeling of power, courage, and the will to power as essential to human greatness (006-366).

In *The Antichrist* (Chapter 10), Nietzsche states the inverse relationship between will to power and decadence: "where the will to power is lacking, degeneration sets in" (010-106). He also describes the "nihilistic will to power" (010-203), distinguishing between healthy, life-affirming will to power and its pathological, destructive form.

## Related Concepts

- [[decadence]] — The absence or inversion of will to power
- [[nihilism]] — The extreme form where will to power turns against life itself
- [[instinct]] — The biological foundation from which will to power arises

## Key Passages

> His feeling of power, his will to power, his courage — all fall with the sight of an open door. (006-366)

> Where the will to power is lacking, degeneration sets in. (010-106)

## Critical Context

Nietzsche's concept of will to power draws on Schopenhauer's "will to live" but inverts its valuation. Where Schopenhauer saw the will as a source of suffering to be negated, Nietzsche affirms it as the creative principle of all life and culture.
