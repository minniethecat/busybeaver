The [[fairy|water-fairy]] is a [[the-brook|water]] elemental being who serves as a messenger between [[princess-pyra|Princess Pyra]] and [[prince-fluvius|Prince Fluvius]] in "[[through-the-fire|Through the Fire]]." He is one of three elemental [[fairy]] beings [[little-jack|Jack]] encounters, alongside the [[fire-fairy]] and the [[wind-fairy]], each bound to their respective element (005-125).

## Appearance and Nature

The [[fairy|water-fairy]] appears as "a tiny man dressed in a dull green dress" with "long wavy hair, that looked heavy and wet," his clothes "shiny with [[the-brook|water]]" (005-123). He arrives seated on the windowsill in "a little pool of [[the-brook|water]]," manifesting through the rain outside (005-123). As a [[the-brook|water]] elemental, he is vulnerable to [[fire-element|heat]] — [[princess-pyra|Princess Pyra]]'s fire [[spell]] threatens to dry him up entirely, and it is this vulnerability that compels his obedience (005-126).

## Role in the Story

When [[princess-pyra|Princess Pyra]] [[hope|wish]]es to summon [[prince-fluvius|Prince Fluvius]], she sings a [[spell]] that calls the [[fairy|water-fairy]] to the windowsill. The [[fairy|water-fairy]] regards [[little-jack|Jack]] with immediate suspicion: "Who are you, and what do you want?" (005-123). When [[jack|Jack]] relays the Princess's command — "that he must bring [[prince-fluvius|Prince Fluvius]] here" — the [[fairy|water-fairy]] indignantly resists: "And who are you who dares to ask me to bring the Prince? Do you think our Prince is to be taken about anywhere — and everywhere, just because mortals want him" (005-125).

> And who are you who dares to ask me to bring the Prince? Do you think our Prince is to be taken about anywhere—and everywhere, just because mortals want him.
>
> (005-125)

The Princess responds by intensifying her song, a [[fire-element|heat]] [[spell]] that grows "louder and louder" until the [[fairy|water-fairy]] can no longer endure it. He "sprang up, promising to bring [[prince-fluvius|Prince Fluvius]], or do anything else [[jack|Jack]] [[hope|wish]]ed, if only the song would cease, as he could not endure the [[fire-element|heat]]" — the [[spell]] would have "dried him up altogether" had it continued (005-126). Once the Princess relents, the [[fairy|water-fairy]] "at once disappeared" to fetch the Prince, and moments later [[prince-fluvius|Prince Fluvius]] arrives in a white cloud (005-127).

## Personality

Unlike the teasing, friendly [[fire-fairy]] or the cheerful, [[forget]]ful [[wind-fairy]], the [[fairy|water-fairy]] is cross, proud, and resistant. He speaks sharply to [[little-jack|Jack]] and is indignant at being commanded by a mortal. His loyalty belongs to his own Prince, and he serves [[princess-pyra|Princess Pyra]] only under duress. This contrast highlights the hierarchy and elemental separation that the story's central romance must overcome.
