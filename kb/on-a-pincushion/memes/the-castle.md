# The Castle

The grand royal residence that appears in multiple tales — [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]]'s destination as Queen in "[[the-seeds-of-love|The Seeds of Love]]" and the site of [[princess-ursula|Princess Ursula]]'s emotionally barren court life in "[[a-toy-princess-tale|A Toy Princess]]."

## Overview

The castle functions across [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]]'s tales as a symbol of worldly success that is morally ambiguous. [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] reaches it as her happy ending, but [[princess-ursula|Princess Ursula]] must escape it to find genuine happiness. The castle is beautiful but can be a prison; it represents status but at the cost of authenticity. In "[[a-toy-princess-tale|A Toy Princess]]," the courtiers are so bound by protocol that they cannot tell a [[life|living]] princess from a mechanical puppet (004-058).

## Geography and Description

The castle sits "on the other side of the river" (001-131) from [[the-cottage|Blanchelys's cottage]]. It features golden thrones, a golden cradle for the baby prince (001-161), and grand rooms full of courtiers. In "[[a-toy-princess-tale|A Toy Princess]]," [[princess-ursula|Princess Ursula]]'s room within the castle is notably bare — "there were no toys or dolls about" (004-012) — despite the castle's outward grandeur.

## Narrative Significance

The castle is a test of character. For [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]], it is a reward for virtue. For [[princess-ursula|Ursula]], it is a cage from which she must be freed. That the [[toy-princess|Toy Princess]] performs perfectly there — fooling everyone — is [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]]'s sharpest critique of courtly life: if a puppet can be a princess, what does "princess" actually mean?

## Characters Associated

- [[blanchelys]] — rises from cottage to castle as Queen
- [[princess-ursula]] — [[princess-ursula|the real princess]] who escapes the castle
- [[toy-princess]] — the magical construct that replaces her there

And as the time passed, and she was never naughty, and scarcely ever spoke, all said she was vastly improved, and she grew to be a great favourite.
