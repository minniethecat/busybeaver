# Lamorna Admits Her Fault

The moral turning point of "[[the-story-of-vain-lamorna|The Story of Vain Lamorna]]" — [[lamorna|Lamorna]] sighs to herself, "if it had not been for my [[pride|vanity]], I need not have been hurt at all" (001-094).

## Overview

This moment of self-knowledge is the tale's true climax — more important than the theft or return of the reflection. [[lamorna|Lamorna]] recognizes that her suffering is self-inflicted, the consequence of her own [[pride|vanity]]. This admission is what prompts the [[water-people|water elves]] to release her reflection.

## Context

[[lamorna|Lamorna]] has lost her reflection, her beauty, and her confidence. She has been humbled by absence.

## The Event

[[lamorna|Lamorna]] speaks aloud to herself: "And if it had not been for my [[pride|vanity]], I need not have been hurt at all. It was only that which made me want the bird's wing. Ah, what a little thing beauty is to be so [[vanity|vain]] of!" (001-094)

## Consequences

The [[water-people|water elves]] hear her words and decide she is cured. "She is quite cured of her [[pride|vanity]]. Let us cut the ropes of sand, and let her [[reflection|image]] free." (001-098)

## Participants

- [[lamorna]] — the speaker who achieves self-knowledge
- [[water-people]] — the listeners who act on her [[truth|confession]]
