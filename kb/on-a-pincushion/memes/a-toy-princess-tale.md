# A Toy Princess

A tale of identity and authenticity in which a [[fairy|fairy godmother]] creates a magical mechanical double of a princess, allowing the real child to grow up with love while the puppet rules — and no one notices the difference.

## Synopsis

[[princess-ursula|Princess Ursula]]'s mother dies, leaving her to be raised by a court that provides everything except love and play (004-007). Her [[fairy|fairy godmother]] [[taboret|Taboret]], observing the child's misery — "how pale little [[princess-ursula|Ursula]] was, and how little she ate" (004-016) — creates a [[toy-princess|Toy Princess]], a magical automaton indistinguishable from the real [[princess-ursula|Ursula]] (004-037), and places it at court. The real [[princess-ursula|Ursula]] is raised in a warm, loving home. When she comes of age, [[taboret|Taboret]] reveals the substitution: "Which will you have — my [[princess-ursula|Princess Ursula]], the real one, or your Princess, the Toy one?" (004-106). The courtiers are shocked, but the lesson is clear: a court that cannot tell a puppet from a princess is no place to raise a child.

## Characters

- [[princess-ursula]] — [[princess-ursula|the real princess]], raised in loving obscurity (004-048, 004-050–004-051)
- [[taboret]] — her [[fairy|fairy godmother]], the tale's true hero, who commissions the automaton from a magical toymaker (004-019–004-026)
- [[toy-princess]] — the magical construct that rules in her place, "so like the [[princess-ursula|Princess Ursula]] that no one could have told the difference" (004-037)
- [[fairy-godmother]] — [[taboret|Taboret]]'s archetype

## Themes

- [[beauty]] — the [[toy-princess|Toy Princess]] is physically perfect, but that is not enough
- [[vanity]] — the court's [[pride|vanity]] in prizing appearance over substance (004-016)
- [[spells-and-charms]] — the magic used to create and maintain the automaton (004-019–004-037)

## Structure and Narrative Features

The collection's most sharply satirical tale. [[taboret|Taboret]] commissions the [[toy-princess|Toy Princess]] from a magical toymaker (004-019–004-026), and the substitution plot critiques aristocratic child-rearing and the emptiness of courtly performance. The Toy Princess, a sympathetic automaton, prefigures modern speculative fiction.

## Place in the Collection

Standalone chapter (Chapter 004). The collection's most ambitious narrative concept, blending [[fairy]] tale with social satire.
