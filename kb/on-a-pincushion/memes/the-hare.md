# The Hare

A talking hare who appears in "[[siegfrid-and-handa-tale|Siegfrid and Handa]]" as a [[gratitude|grateful]] guide — rescued by [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] from a trap, it repays his [[kindness]] by directing him toward the [[magic-shoes|magic shoe]]s that will enable his quest.

## Overview

The Hare is the first of several animal helpers [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] encounters in his journey through the forest. Found caught in a trap (002-023), it is freed by [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] and immediately reveals itself as a speaking creature — to [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]]'s astonishment (002-026). The Hare possesses knowledge of the underground world: [[the-underground-cavern|the cavern]] where the little people dwell (002-028), the [[wisdom|wise]] [[the-old-elf|Owl]] who knows about the [[magic-shoes|magic shoe]]s (002-032), and the location of the shoemaker's trove (002-048). It serves as a classic [[fairy]]-tale guide figure — the rescued animal who repays a human's [[kindness]] with supernatural knowledge.

## In the Story

### Role and Significance

The Hare functions as the initiatory guide in [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]]'s quest. Its rescue marks the first test of [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]]'s character — he frees the creature without expectation of reward — and the Hare's gratitude [[transformation|transform]]s a simple act of [[kindness]] into the first link in a chain of magical assistance. Without the Hare's directions, [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] would never find the underground cavern, consult [[the-old-elf|the Owl]], or locate the [[magic-shoes|magic shoe]]s that enable him to rescue [[handa|Handa]]. The Hare thus embodies the [[fairy]]-tale logic of reciprocal [[kindness]]: good done to the natural world returns as supernatural aid.

### Key Actions

- Caught in a trap before [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] finds and frees it (002-023)
- Speaks to [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]], revealing the [[life|existence]] of the underground cavern and the little people (002-028)
- Directs [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] to consult [[the-old-elf|the Owl]], who "flies about at night" and knows hidden things (002-032)
- Gives [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] instructions about the [[magic-shoes|magic shoe]]s before scampering away (002-037, 002-046)
- Waits beside [[the-old-elf|the Owl]] in the underground cavern, confirming its role as intermediary between helpers (002-041)

## Character Interpretation

The Hare represents the [[fairy]]-tale motif of the [[gratitude|grateful]] animal — a stock figure in folklore who rewards a human's unprompted [[kindness]] with magical assistance. What distinguishes this instance is the Hare's role as a *networker*: it does not itself possess the solution to [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]]'s problem, but it knows *who does*. It directs [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] to [[the-old-elf|the Owl]], who in turn knows about the shoemaker and the [[magic-shoes|magic shoe]]s — a chain of referrals that models the quest as a collaborative endeavor across species and realms. The Hare's swift departure after delivering its message ("soon scampered away," 002-037) is typical of such guide figures, who appear briefly, deliver essential information, and vanish — their narrative function fulfilled, their mystery preserved.
