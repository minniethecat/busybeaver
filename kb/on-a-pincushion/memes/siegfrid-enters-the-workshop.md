# Siegfrid Enters the Workshop

The inciting event of "[[siegfrid-and-handa-tale|Siegfrid and Handa]]" — [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] enters the giant's disguised workshop and discovers the truth: the shoemaker is a giant who kidnaps children.

## Overview

[[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] enters what appears to be an ordinary shoemaker's shop. Inside, he discovers captive children who reveal the truth: "He is a giant, who is very cruel and wicked. He has disguised himself as a shoemaker, that he may steal little boys and girls" (002-028). [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]]'s response is eager determination: "That will be capital!" (002-029).

## Context

[[handa|Handa]] has been captured. [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] is searching for her. The workshop is his last lead.

## The Event

[[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] enters. The children explain their captivity. The [[magic-shoes|magic shoe]]s that bind them are revealed. The truth [[transformation|transform]]s the shop from benign workshop to prison.

> "In reality he is a kind of gnome, and very cruel and wicked. He has disguised himself as a shoemaker, that he may steal these little girls. He belongs to a kind which only lives three hundred years, and then they begin to grow small, and gradually become less and less, till at last they vanish quite into air, unless they can steal seven little girls from whose bones they make a dreadful charm, which gives them life for another hundred years."
>
> (002-028)

## Consequences

This discovery launches the rescue: [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] must now destroy the giant's [[magic|enchantment]] and free the children.

## Participants

- [[siegfrid]] — the discoverer
- [[handa]] — one of the captives he finds
