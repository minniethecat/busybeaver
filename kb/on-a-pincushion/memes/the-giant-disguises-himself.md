# The Giant Disguises Himself

The originating event of "[[siegfrid-and-handa-tale|Siegfrid and Handa]]" — a giant "has disguised himself as a shoemaker, that he may steal little boys and girls" (002-028).

## Overview

The giant's disguise is the tale's foundational deception. Unlike other antagonists in the collection who are morally flawed humans ([[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s [[pride|vanity]], [[zaire|Zaire]]'s jealousy), the giant is a magical predator who uses craft to capture children. His disguise as a shoemaker is both clever and sinister — shoes are innocent, necessary, even loving objects, but his are chains.

## Context

Before [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]]'s story begins, the shoemaker has already been active for some time. [[handa|Handa]] and "the other five little girls" have already been captured through his trick of giving them "pretty yellow shoes" that "ran away with her" (002-028). The village's [[memory|prosperity]] has already begun to fail — [[fire-element|heat]], drought, fever — all [[truth|consequences]] of the giant's presence.

## The Event

The giant takes the form of an old shoemaker who sits by the roadside, covering his stall with boots and shoes, singing "queer rhymes." His disguise is effective not because it is elaborate but because it is mundane — no one suspects the shoemaker of being anything worse than a poor salesman. The children, indeed, "grew so used to him that they no longer stopped to stare at him" (002-028).

> "In reality he is a kind of gnome, and very cruel and wicked. He has disguised himself as a shoemaker, that he may steal these little girls. He belongs to a kind which only lives three hundred years, and then they begin to grow small, and gradually become less and less, till at last they vanish quite into air, unless they can steal seven little girls from whose bones they make a dreadful charm, which gives them life for another hundred years."
>
> (002-028)

## Consequences

Without this disguise, there would be no captured children, no [[magic-shoes|magic shoes]], no need for [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]]'s quest. The entire tale unfolds from this single act of magical deception.

## Participants

- The giant (gnome) — the disguised predator
- [[handa]] — the first named victim
- [[siegfrid]] — the eventual rescuer
