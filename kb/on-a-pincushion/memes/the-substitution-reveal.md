# The Substitution Reveal

The climactic [[truth|revelation]] of "[[a-toy-princess-tale|A Toy Princess]]" — [[taboret|Taboret]] reveals to the stunned court that the princess they have served for years is a magical automaton, and the real [[princess-ursula|Princess Ursula]] returns.

## Overview

When [[princess-ursula|Princess Ursula]] comes of age, [[taboret|Taboret]] reveals her long-running substitution. The courtiers learn that they "did not think it was not she, but a toy Princess placed there in her place" (004-058). For years, "as she grew, the toy Princess, in her place at the court, grew too" (004-063) — the mechanical double [[age|aging]] in parallel with [[princess-ursula|the real princess]]. When [[taboret|Taboret]] finally "told her how she had been hidden, and how she had placed a toy Princess in her place" (004-072), the reveal was both a dramatic climax and a satirical judgment on courtly life.

> “My lords,” he faltered, “pray pardon our apparently extraordinary behaviour. We have just received a dreadful blow; we hear on authority, which we cannot doubt, that our dear, dear daughter”—here sobs choked his voice, and he was almost unable to proceed—“is—is—in fact, not our daughter at all, and only a sham.” Here the King sank back in his chair, overpowered with grief, and the fairy Taboret, stepping to the front, told the courtiers the whole story; how she had stolen the real Princess, because she feared they were spoiling her, and how she had placed a toy Princess in her place.
>
> (004-072)

## Context

For years, the [[toy-princess|Toy Princess]] has performed royal duties while the real [[princess-ursula|Ursula]] grew up in loving obscurity. No one noticed the difference.

## The Event

[[taboret|Taboret]] reveals the truth. The court is shocked. The real [[princess-ursula|Ursula]] appears — formed by love rather than protocol.

## Consequences

The court must confront its own blindness. [[a-toy-princess-tale|The Toy Princess]]'s perfect performance is revealed as an indictment: if a puppet can rule unnoticed, what value does royal blood actually hold?
