The [[fairy|fire-fairy]] is a fire elemental being who appears to [[little-jack|Jack]] through the coals of his Christmas Eve fire and becomes his guide to the underground kingdom of [[fireland|Fireland]]. He is one of three elemental [[fairy]] guides in "[[through-the-fire|Through the Fire]]," alongside the [[fairy|water-fairy]] and the [[fairy|wind-fairy]], each tied to and able to travel through their respective element (005-008, 005-153).

## Appearance and Nature

The [[fairy|fire-fairy]] appears as "a little figure, the strangest he had ever beheld," balancing skilfully on a piece of [[fire-element|burning]] coal (005-008). He is "just like a little man, not more than three inches high, dressed from head to foot in orange-scarlet, the colour of [[fire-element|flame]], and wearing on his head a long pointed cap of the same colour" (005-008).

As a fire elemental, the [[fairy|fire-fairy]] lives in and breathes fire — he explains to [[little-jack|Jack]] that "we breathe fire and live in it; we should go out at once if we weren't surrounded by it" (005-009). Without the presence of a [[fairy|fire-fairy]], no fire can burn, for "we light them, and keep them in" (005-009). The concept of "going out" is the [[fairy|fire-fairy]]'s equivalent of death, though he dismisses the subject as unpleasant: "I don't know about [[death|dying]], but of course, without care, one is liable to go out" (005-009).

## Role in "Through the Fire"

On Christmas Eve, when [[little-jack|Jack]] digs the fire with a poker in frustration, the [[fairy|fire-fairy]] protests from within the [[fire-element|flames]]: "For pity's sake, don't do that again; it's enough to break one to bits" (005-008). After introducing himself — though first chiding [[jack|Jack]] that "it's rude to ask questions" — the [[fairy|fire-fairy]] offers to take [[jack|Jack]] to [[fireland|Fireland]], the underground kingdom of the fire-[[fairies]] (005-008, 005-054).

To begin the journey, the [[fairy|fire-fairy]] digs a hole in the coals and drops magic marbles into it; the hole expands into "an immense black gulf" (005-054). He carries [[little-jack|Jack]] on his shoulders, flying through darkness until a faint red light appears below, growing "larger and brighter every moment" (005-058). Upon arriving through a fiery archway, the [[fairy|fire-fairy]] shows [[jack|Jack]] the wonders of [[fireland|Fireland]]: "great hills of every shade of red and orange," lakes of fire, and a sky "one mass of [[fire-element|flame]]" (005-060).

The [[fairy|fire-fairy]] guides [[jack|Jack]] to the King's palace, made of red-hot iron and glistening with precious stones, and to its garden filled with firework-flowers and spinning catherine-wheels (005-068). It is here that he reveals "The Princess!" to [[jack|Jack]] — [[princess-pyra|Princess Pyra]], whom [[jack|Jack]] finds "the most beautiful lady he had ever seen" (005-068).

## Personality

The [[fairy|fire-fairy]] has a teasing, mischievous personality. He winks at [[jack|Jack]], chides him for asking questions, and speaks scornfully of [[jack|Jack]]'s ignorance about [[fairy]]-kind (005-009). Yet beneath the playfulness, he is a loyal and capable guide — he carries [[jack|Jack]] safely through [[darkness|the dark]]ness, warns him not to make noise in [[the-castle|the palace]] garden, and ensures [[jack|Jack]] meets the Princess who will set him on his quest (005-068, 005-136).

When [[little-jack|Jack]]'s time in [[fireland|Fireland]] concludes, it is the [[fairy|wind-fairy]] — not the [[fairy|fire-fairy]] — who carries him onward, the [[fairy|fire-fairy]]'s element unable to travel where [[the-brook|water]] and air must take over (005-153).
