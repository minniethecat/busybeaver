## Identity

The Bracelets are minor but evocative characters in the frame narrative of "On a Pincushion." They are a pair of bracelets hanging over the [[reflection|looking-glass]], above the Pincushion where [[the-brooch|the Brooch]], Shawl-pin, and Pin reside. Though they never speak directly in the story, their presence and behaviour are commented upon by the three protagonists.

## Role in the Frame Story

The Bracelets are first mentioned when [[the-shawl-pin|the Shawl-pin]] complains about them: "'I [[hope|wish]] those Bracelets up there would leave off chattering. There's nothing disturbs my nerves so much as the clatter of talking'" (001-005). [[the-brooch|The Brooch]] agrees, drawing on past experience: "'Bracelets are always great talkers,' said [[the-brooch|the Brooch]]. 'I once passed two months in a jewel-box with a number, and I was truly thankful when I was taken out. Their talking was incessant, and it was impossible to get a wink of sleep'" (001-006).

Notably, the Bracelets are indifferent to the Pincushion dwellers' disdain: they "did not mind them in the least, but went on talking just the same" (001-006). It is their irritating chatter that prompts the Pin to propose storytelling as a distraction: "'Let us do something to drown their noise,' said the Pin. 'Let us tell stories'" (001-006) — a suggestion that directly leads to [[the-brooch|the Brooch]] telling "[[the-story-of-vain-lamorna|The Story of Vain Lamorna]]" and the subsequent chain of embedded tales.

The Bracelets appear once more, indirectly, during the telling of the [[the-sunbeam|Sunbeam]] and Moonbeam story within [[the-brooch|the Brooch]]'s second tale. At the story's emotional climax, the narrative notes that "Even the Bracelets had stopped chattering, and came down to listen" (001-218), suggesting that even the most frivolous creatures can be moved by a tale of tragic love.

## Significance

The Bracelets serve as a framing device within the frame story — their ceaseless chatter represents the background noise of everyday [[pride|vanity]] and gossip against which meaningful storytelling takes place. Their eventual silencing by the power of the [[the-sunbeam|Sunbeam]] and Moonbeam's story underscores the [[transformation|transform]]ative power of narrative.

> "Bracelets are always great talkers," said the Brooch. "I once passed two months in a jewel-box with a number, and I was truly thankful when I was taken out. Their talking was incessant, and it was impossible to get a wink of sleep." And all three scowled up at the Bracelets, who were hanging over the looking-glass, but who did not mind them in the least, but went on talking just the same. "Let us do something to drown their noise," said the Pin. "Let us tell stories."
>
> (001-006)
