# Through the Fire

The final tale in the collection — an ordeal narrative in which [[jack|Jack]] traverses [[the-ice-realm|the frozen realm]] of the [[the-ice-people|ice people]] and witnesses a Princess who endures [[fire-element|flame]] through sustained song.

## Synopsis

[[jack|Jack]], raised on his grandmother's "long, long stories of giants and [[fairy|fairies]]" (005-002), journeys through [[the-ice-realm|the frozen realm]] of the [[the-ice-people|ice people]] where "many people have caught and [[death|frozen to death]]" (005-195). He reaches a Princess who must sing continuously to survive fire — "if she ceased to sing for one moment she would have been burned to death" (005-126). The tale progresses through the elemental extremes of ice and fire toward resolution and rescue. (Chapter 005)

> "All round the palace where our King lives there's flame—flame—nothing but flame for miles, and the Princess's windows look on to burning hills. Ah, what a pity it is people are so discontented! If there is anyone who ought to be happy, it's the Princess Pyra."

(005-030)

## Structure and Narrative Features

An ordeal narrative structured by elemental opposition (ice → fire). The collection's most mythic tale, closest in spirit to the heroic quests of folklore.

## Place in the Collection

Final standalone chapter. Provides the collection's closing movement from domestic magic to elemental ordeal, expanding the scope of what [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]]'s [[fairy]] tales can encompass.
