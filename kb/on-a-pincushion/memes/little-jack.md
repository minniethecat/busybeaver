## Identity

Little Jack is the protagonist of the tale "[[through-the-fire-tale|Through the Fire]]." He is a seven-year-old boy, a cripple who lives with his widowed mother, a music teacher, on the third floor of a small house in a dull old street in London. He is described as "so small and pale that he looked little more than five" (005-001). He spends nearly all day alone in the little sitting-room by the fire while his mother is out teaching.

## Role in "Through the Fire"

On Christmas Eve, feeling particularly sad and lonely, [[jack|Jack]] meets a [[fairy|fire-fairy]] — a tiny man "not more than three inches high, dressed from head to foot in orange-scarlet" who lives in the coals of the fire (005-006). The [[fairy|fire-fairy]] tells [[jack|Jack]] about the underground kingdom of [[fireland|Fireland]] and its [[princess-pyra|Princess Pyra]], who is unhappily in love with [[prince-fluvius|Prince Fluvius]], the [[the-brook|water]] King's son (005-034).

[[jack|Jack]] persuades the [[fairy|fire-fairy]] to take him to [[fireland|Fireland]], where he witnesses the forbidden romance between [[princess-pyra|the fire Princess]] and [[prince-fluvius|the water Prince]] (005-130). The Princess entreats [[jack|Jack]] to travel to the [[north-pole|North Pole]] to ask the old man who lives there how she and [[prince-fluvius|Prince Fluvius]] can be married, as "there is only one person in the world who can help us" (005-136).

## Journey and Reward

[[jack|Jack]] travels on the shoulders of a [[fairy|wind-fairy]] across the sea, past mermaids and ice-people, to the [[north-pole|North Pole]] (005-149). Despite being tempted to ask the old man for his own cure instead, [[jack|Jack]] remains faithful to his promise and delivers the Princess's question (005-176). The old man tells him the solution: the Prince and Princess must wait until they both go out — that is, die — and then they will be free to marry.

One year later, on another Christmas Eve, the Prince and Princess appear to [[jack|Jack]] on a [[the-moonbeam|moonbeam]] and gift him a magic silver belt that will gradually cure his lameness (005-250). "This time we part forever," the Princess says, kissing [[jack|Jack]]'s forehead (005-255). Years afterward, [[jack|Jack]] grows into a tall strong boy and attributes his recovery to "my going to the [[north-pole|North Pole]] for [[princess-pyra|the fire Princess]]" (005-261).
