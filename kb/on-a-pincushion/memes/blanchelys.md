# Blanchelys

A gentle orphan girl in "[[the-seeds-of-love|The Seeds of Love]]," the second embedded tale in *On a Pincushion*, whose quiet virtue and the magic of the rose tree seeds win her a Prince's love and a crown.

## Overview

Blanchelys lives in a small cottage with her [[zaire|cousin Zaire]], opening gates for passersby. When a [[fairy]] gives each girl a [[magic-candles|magic candle]], Blanchelys follows her grandmother's advice to "never light it" (001-130) — but when love stirs in her heart, she lights her candle at midnight and summons [[love|Love]] itself. She is distinguished from her cousin primarily through her gentleness: where [[zaire|Zaire]] rages and envies, Blanchelys endures and loves. Her reward — marriage to the Prince and a thriving rose tree — is presented as the natural consequence of her character.

## In the Story

### Role and Significance

Blanchelys is the virtuous counterpart in what is essentially a paired-character study. She and [[zaire|Zaire]] receive identical magical gifts (the candles), but their outcomes diverge based on character. Blanchelys's unselfish nature — she "put her candle safely away, meaning to take her grandmother's advice, and never to light it" (001-130) — contrasts with [[zaire|Zaire]]'s impatient greed. The tale argues that magic amplifies character rather than replacing it.

> "I will keep it till I know of something I long for very much indeed," she said to herself. So she put the candle safely away; and Blanchelys put hers away also, meaning to take her grandmother's advice, and never to light it.
>
> (001-130)

### Key Actions

- Saves her [[magic-candles|magic candle]] rather than using it impulsively (001-130)
- Watches the Prince ride past with quiet admiration, not [[jealousy|envy]] (001-132)
- Summons her courage to light the candle at midnight, meeting [[love|Love]] under the yew-tree (001-136, 001-137)
- Plants the jewel-like seeds and breathes the Prince's name over them at sunrise (001-144)
- Marries the Prince and bears a son, with the rose tree blooming in proportion to her happiness (001-149, 001-151)

### Relationships

- [[zaire]] — her jealous cousin who ultimately tries to destroy the rose tree
- [[the-rose-tree]] — the magical plant whose health reflects her marriage
- [[magic-candles]] — the [[hope|wish]]-granting item given to both cousins
- [[the-seeds-of-love]] — the jewel-like seeds she plants

## Character Interpretation

Blanchelys represents a Victorian ideal of feminine virtue: patient, self-denying, rewarded through [[patience|waiting]] rather than striving. Her passivity — she never pursues the Prince, only opens her gate — makes her a problematic model by modern standards, but [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]] gives her genuine agency in the midnight candle scene, where she acts alone and at risk.
