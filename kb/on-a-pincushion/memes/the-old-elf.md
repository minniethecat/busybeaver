# The Wise Old Water Elf

The [[age|elder]] statesman of the [[the-brook|water]] people in "[[the-story-of-vain-lamorna|The Story of Vain Lamorna]]," whose brilliant plan to steal [[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s reflection drives the tale's magical intervention and whose wisdom is repeatedly applauded by his community.

## Overview

The [[wisdom|wise]] old elf is the intellectual center of the [[the-brook|water]] people's community. When the other elves debate how to cure [[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s [[pride|vanity]], he proposes the solution: "We must wait till she is leaning over the [[the-brook|water]] looking at herself, and then we must steal her reflection." (001-022) His plan is greeted with universal acclaim — "what a fine thing it is to have a mind like that!" — and lady elves declare that "with such a mind as that, one could rule countries or take cities." His response is to "bow all round and smile pleasantly," as he "prided himself on his good manners."

> “We cannot keep her from looking in glasses or in the brook. There is only one thing, therefore, to be done. It will be difficult, but it is quite possible. We must wait till she is leaning over the water looking at herself, and then we must steal her reflection.”
>
> (001-022)

## In the Story

### Role and Significance

The old elf is [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]]'s miniature philosopher-king — [[wisdom|wise]], courteous, and gently [[vanity|vain]] about his own wisdom. His combination of intellectual brilliance and social grace makes him one of the collection's most [[spells-and-charms|charm]]ing minor characters. He also takes time to educate the young: when a young elf makes an ignorant comment about mirrors, the old elf "was never angry with ignorance in the young, but he [[hope|wish]]ed rather to correct it than blame it."

### Key Actions

- Proposes the plan to steal [[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s reflection (001-022)
- Explains the nature of reflections to the young elf, correcting ignorance with patience
- Directs the [[water-people|water elves]] in capturing [[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s reflection with sand ropes (001-043)
- Orders the release of [[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s reflection when she has repented (001-098)

- [[lamorna]] — the human whose [[pride|vanity]] he cures
