# The Spell Shop

A commercial establishment mentioned in "[[a-toy-princess-tale|A Toy Princess]]" where "every sort of [[spells-and-charms|spell]] or [[spells-and-charms|charm]] was sold" (004-018) — a remarkably modern [[dream|vision]] of magic as commodity.

## Overview

The [[spells-and-charms|spell]] shop is a brief but striking detail in [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]]'s magical world-building. [[magic|Magic]] is not only inherited ([[fairy-godmother|fairy godmothers]]), found ([[magic-candles|magic candle]]s), or innate ([[the-brook|water]] people); it is also purchasable. This commercializes magic in a way that anticipates modern fantasy's [[spells-and-charms|magic shop]]s, and it democratizes magical protection — anyone with money can buy a [[spells-and-charms|counter-spell]].

## Geography and Description

The shop is mentioned in passing — "a shop where every sort of [[spells-and-charms|spell]] or [[spells-and-charms|charm]] was sold" — without detailed description. Its inventory is comprehensive ("every sort"), suggesting a well-stocked commercial establishment. The [[life|existence]] of such a shop implies a magical economy: [[spells-and-charms|spell]]-crafters, suppliers, customers.

## Narrative Significance

The [[spells-and-charms|spell]] shop's brief mention opens a window onto a larger magical world beyond the tales' immediate settings. It suggests that magic in [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]]'s universe is not rare or fading but abundant and commercialized — as ordinary as buying ribbons or candles.
