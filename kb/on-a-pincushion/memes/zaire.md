# Zaire

The jealous cousin of [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] in "[[the-seeds-of-love|The Seeds of Love]]," whose consuming [[jealousy|envy]] drives the tale's central conflict and provides the moral counterpoint to [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]]'s patient virtue.

## Overview

Zaire is introduced as [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]]'s cousin and housemate, but her defining trait is immediate and negative: when the Prince's horses raise dust that "flew into Zaire's face, she fell into a passion" (001-132). Her jealousy of [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] is specific and targeted — it is not general discontent but focused hatred of her cousin's happiness. "Zaire was bitterly jealous of [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]], because she was going to be Queen" (001-150), and she "thought night and day of how she could do her any harm." (001-150)

> Zaire was bitterly jealous of Blanchelys, because she was going to be Queen, though Blanchelys was very kind to her and gave her beautiful things, and took her to live with her at the palace. Still Zaire hated her, and thought night and day of how she could do her any harm.
>
> (001-150)

## In the Story

### Role and Significance

Zaire functions as a foil to [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]]: where [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] is patient, Zaire is impulsive; where [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] loves, Zaire envies; where [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]]'s rose tree blooms, Zaire plots its destruction. Her jealousy escalates from resentment to active [[cruelty|malice]], making her the tale's principal antagonist. Unlike [[lamorna|Lamorna]], who is cured, Zaire's fate is less clearly redemptive — she represents [[jealousy|envy]] as corrosive and self-destructive.

### Key Actions

- Explodes in anger when the Prince's party ignores her (001-132)
- Receives the same [[magic-candles|magic candle]] as [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] but uses it poorly
- Grows "bitterly jealous" and plans harm against her cousin (001-150)
- Learns of the rose tree's importance and questions [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] about it with "glittering" eyes (001-158)

### Relationships

- [[blanchelys]] — her cousin, the object of her consuming jealousy
- [[the-rose-tree]] — the magical plant whose destruction she contemplates

## Character Interpretation

Zaire represents [[jealousy|envy]] as a specific female-coded vice in the Victorian moral imagination — the jealous woman who cannot bear another's happiness. [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]]'s treatment is psychological rather than supernatural: Zaire is not cursed or magically compelled to evil, but simply consumed by ordinary human resentment. This makes her perhaps the collection's most realistic antagonist.
