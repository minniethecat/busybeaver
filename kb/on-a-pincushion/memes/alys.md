## Identity

Alys is a minor character in "[[siegfrid-and-handa-tale|Siegfrid and Handa]]." She is described as the "greatest friend" of [[lisbeth|Lisbeth]], the baker's daughter (002-010). Like the other young women of the village, she is drawn to the cheap, pretty shoes sold by the disguised gnome.

## Role in the Story

After [[lisbeth|Lisbeth]] purchases her red shoes from the gnome's stall, she immediately takes them to Alys: "'Look at these pretty pink shoes,' she said. 'I have just bought them of the old man at the stall, and they only cost five silver pennies. See how pretty they are!'" (002-010).

Alys's response shows a degree of practical scepticism: "'It is true they are very pretty, but I don't think they will wear well; however, as they are so very cheap, that does not much matter. I wonder if he has another pair just like them, that I could buy'" (002-010). Despite her doubts about quality, the appeal of novelty and low price proves irresistible.

Alys then joins [[lisbeth|Lisbeth]] in returning to the stall and buys a matching pair (002-011). This act contributes to the chain reaction of purchases that spreads through the village — first the young girls, then the women, then the men — until [[ralph|Ralph]] the honest shoemaker is abandoned by all his customers.

> So Lisbeth and Alys went back to the stall, and Alys bought a pair of shoes just like Lisbeth’s; and then they both went to show them to the other young girls in the village, and one by one each said she would have a pair for herself, till there was not a girl who was not wearing the old man’s shoes.
>
> (002-011)

Later, when [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] burns the gnome's shoes to break the [[spell]], among the heap are "the pair which Alys had bought like them" (002-040), now worn out and full of holes — confirming her initial suspicion about their poor quality.

## Significance

Alys represents the moment of hesitation before moral compromise. She recognises the shoes are likely inferior, yet still buys them because they are cheap and pretty. Her choice, multiplied across the village, contributes to the community's collective betrayal of [[ralph|Ralph]] and the calamities that follow.
