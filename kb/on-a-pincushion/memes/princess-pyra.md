# Princess Pyra

The fire Princess of [[fire-element|Fire]]land in "[[through-the-fire-tale|Through the Fire]]" — a being of [[fire-element|flame]] who falls in love with [[prince-fluvius|the water Prince]] [[prince-fluvius|Fluvius]] across the elemental divide, risking extinction ("going out") for the chance of union. She is the daughter of the fire King and Queen, described as "the most beautiful woman in [[fire-element|Fire]]land" (005-039).

## Identity and Appearance

Princess Pyra is the only daughter of the fire King and Queen, raised in a palace of red-hot iron that glistens with precious stones of colored [[fire-element|flame]] (005-064). Her long bright golden hair falls almost to her feet, and her face, though very pale, is lit by eyes that shine "just like stars" (005-071, 005-079). She wears a shining [[fire-element|flame]]-coloured dress with a long train, and her characteristic ornament is a pale blue and silver catherine-wheel fixed in her bosom and another in her hair (005-072). When she cries, sparks fall from her eyes instead of tears (005-082).

## The Forbidden Love

Her parents, proud of their only daughter, arrange a match with a neighbouring fire Prince. To complete her education they send her to school in a [[fire-element|burning]] mountain, where one day the [[the-brook|water]] King's son, [[prince-fluvius|Prince Fluvius]], "came and looked over the top of the mountain, and saw our Princess; and they fell in love with each other" (005-034). The match is impossible — the fire King is the [[the-brook|water]] King's "bitterest enemy," and if the lovers approach each other, he would be dried up or she would be put out (005-037).

After her father discovers her speaking with [[prince-fluvius|Prince Fluvius]], he takes her home in a rage and hurries to marry her to the fire Prince. But she "grew so thin that the doctors said they feared if she were much excited she would go out altogether" (005-037). She walks through [[the-castle|the palace]] garden in silence, surrounded by court ladies who cannot comfort her, and "never said a word" (005-073).

## The Quest to the North Pole

Desperate, the Princess discovers that "the old man who sits on the [[north-pole|North Pole]]" — a [[wisdom|wise]] but mischievous being who must answer one question truthfully — can tell her how to marry her Prince (005-136). Unable to travel herself (the sea would quench her), she enlists [[jack|Jack]], a crippled boy who has journeyed to [[fire-element|Fire]]land with a [[fairy|fire-fairy]], to make the journey for her.

She gives [[jack|Jack]] a fireball to keep him warm and warns him not to ask more than one question, or the old man will "seize you and keep you under the ice" (005-142). She instructs him to say: "I come from the fire Princess Pyra, and she is in love with [[prince-fluvius|Prince Fluvius]], [[prince-fluvius|the water Prince]], and wants to know how they are to be married" (005-144).

The Princess possesses the power of [[spell]]-song — a sung [[spell|incantation]] that can compel [[the-brook|water]]-[[fairy|fairies]]. She sings to summon a [[fairy|water-fairy]] to bring [[prince-fluvius|Prince Fluvius]], her song growing "louder and louder, and clearer and clearer" until the [[fairy|water-fairy]] springs up, promising to do anything "if only the song would cease, as he could not endure the [[fire-element|heat]]" (005-126).

## Transformation

When [[jack|Jack]] returns with the old man's answer — "What puts out fire but [[the-brook|water]]? what dries up [[the-brook|water]] but fire? Tell him to give her a kiss!" (005-210) — the Princess understands. She declares: "We must both be changed before we can be happy. Come, then, my Prince; I have no fear, and will willingly risk being quenched altogether, if there is a chance of our union" (005-231).

She steps from the fireplace surrounded by a halo of shining [[fire-element|flame]], and as [[prince-fluvius|Prince Fluvius]] sweeps down from the window "a flood of [[the-brook|water]] splashed on to the floor." They rush into each other's arms, and "a great crash — a sound like a clap of thunder" fills the room (005-234). When the smoke clears, both are [[transformation|transform]]ed — she is "the same, yet not the same" (005-236). Her hair is softer and glitters less; her [[fire-element|burning]] eyes now beam with "a soft, mild light"; the catherine-wheel has become "a bunch of real [[the-brook|water]]-lilies" (005-237–238). For the first time, "real tears fell from her eyes" (005-239).

## The Magic Belt and Farewell

A year later, the Princess and [[prince-fluvius|Prince Fluvius]] return one final time to visit [[jack|Jack]], who is gravely ill. Between them they carry a silver hoop — a magic belt they have spent a year making. She tells [[jack|Jack]]: "You must put it on, and it will make you quite strong, and in a few years you will no longer be a cripple" (005-250). After placing the invisible belt on him, she kisses his forehead and departs forever with the Prince to "live on the other side of the moon" (005-250, 005-255).

## Significance

Princess Pyra is one of [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]]'s most fully realized non-human characters — a being made of [[fire-element|flame]] who experiences human emotions of love, [[grief|sorrow]], and courage. Her [[transformation|transform]]ation through the embrace of her opposite element embodies the tale's theme of unity through complementary opposition. Unlike [[lamorna|Lamorna]] (cured of [[pride|vanity]] through loss) or [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] (rewarded for patient virtue), Pyra's arc is one of active courage — she risks her very [[life|existence]] for love.
