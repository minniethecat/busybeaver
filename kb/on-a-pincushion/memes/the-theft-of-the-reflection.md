# The Theft of the Reflection

The central magical event of "[[the-story-of-vain-lamorna|The Story of Vain Lamorna]]" — on a moonlit night, the [[water-people|water elves]] throw sand ropes over [[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s reflection and pull it beneath the brook, removing her ability to see herself.

## Overview

The theft of [[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s reflection is the tale's turning point. The [[water-people|water elves]], having determined that [[lamorna|Lamorna]] "can never be cured of her [[pride|vanity]] as long as she can see herself" (001-020), execute their plan on a [[the-moonbeam|moonlight]] night when the [[the-brook|water]] is smooth. The reflection is captured with sand ropes — surprisingly physical tools for immaterial magic — and pulled underwater, where it is stored in a special grotto. (001-043, 001-049)

## Context

Before the theft, [[lamorna|Lamorna]] has been scorning [[erick|Erick]]'s love and spending her days admiring her reflection. The [[the-brook|water]] people have debated how to help [[erick|Erick]] and concluded that removing [[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s [[reflection|self-image]] is the only way. The moonlit night provides the conditions for the theft — smooth [[the-brook|water]], clear reflection.

## The Event

"At a given signal, the [[water-people|water elves]] threw up their sand ropes, and caught her reflection, and all pulled it together, and [[lamorna|Lamorna]] started back with a shudder." (001-043) The reflection is thirty times the size of any elf, requiring communal effort to subdue. The elves store it in a special grotto where they can all go to admire it. (001-049)

> At a given signal, the water elves threw up their sand ropes, and caught her reflection, and all pulled it together, and Lamorna started back with a shudder.
>
> (001-043)

## Consequences

[[lamorna|Lamorna]] can no longer see herself in any reflective surface — the brook, her mirror, all show nothing where her face should be. This loss plunges her into despair but ultimately forces her to look outward. She comes to recognize her [[pride|vanity]], and when the [[water-people|water elves]] release her reflection (001-098), she is cured and accepts [[erick|Erick]]'s love.
