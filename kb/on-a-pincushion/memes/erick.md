# Erick

A young fisherman in "[[the-story-of-vain-lamorna|The Story of Vain Lamorna]]" whose patient, steadfast love for [[lamorna|Lamorna]] survives her scorn and is ultimately rewarded when she is cured of [[pride|vanity]].

## Overview

Erick is the moral anchor of "[[the-story-of-vain-lamorna|The Story of Vain Lamorna]]." While [[lamorna|Lamorna]] obsesses over her reflection, Erick tends his fishing lines with care for the creatures he catches. The [[the-brook|water]] people observe that "he was neither cruel nor wicked, but always was careful not to torture the fish he caught, but killed them at once." (001-014) This detail establishes Erick's character through action rather than declaration: he is kind even to fish, making his patient love for [[lamorna|Lamorna]] an extension of his nature rather than a special case.

## In the Story

### Role and Significance

Erick functions as the tale's moral baseline — the person who already possesses the virtues that [[lamorna|Lamorna]] must learn. His love is constant, undemanding, and expressed through action rather than declaration. When [[lamorna|Lamorna]] scorns him, he sighs but does not retaliate. When she loses her beauty, he loves her still. He represents love as steadfastness — not passion but permanence.

> "Ah, Lamorna!" he sighed to himself, "when we were children you said you loved me, and promised me to be my wife, and now you will not speak to me, though you know how I love you."
>
> (001-015)

### Key Actions

- Watches [[lamorna|Lamorna]] unperceived from his boat, loving her in silence (001-014)
- Tells [[lamorna|Lamorna]] that they were promised to each other as children, and he will love no other (001-015)
- Kisses the scar on [[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s forehead, proving his love is for her, not her beauty (001-116)
- Marries [[lamorna|Lamorna]], the happy resolution that the [[the-brook|water]] people celebrate (001-107, 001-117)

### Relationships

- [[lamorna]] — the [[vanity|vain]] girl he loves and eventually marries
- [[water-people]] — the magical beings who help him by curing [[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s [[pride|vanity]]

## Character Interpretation

Erick embodies the Victorian ideal of masculine constancy — patient, hardworking, morally upright without being preachy. He is notably passive: the [[the-brook|water]] people act on his behalf while he simply continues fishing. This passivity may be read as [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]]'s critique of masculine helplessness in emotional matters, or as a celebration of the kind of love that waits rather than demands.
