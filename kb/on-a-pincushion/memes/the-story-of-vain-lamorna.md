# The Story of Vain Lamorna

The first embedded tale in *On a Pincushion*, told by [[the-brooch|the Brooch]] — a story of [[pride|vanity]], magical intervention, and the cure of self-obsession through loss of [[reflection|self-image]].

## Synopsis

[[lamorna|Lamorna]], a beautiful farmer's daughter, spends her days admiring her reflection in the brook (001-008) and scorning the love of the fisherman [[erick|Erick]], who had loved her since childhood (001-014, 001-015). The [[the-water-people|water people]] who live in the brook — tiny benevolent elves shaped like men but only two or three inches high (001-011) — decide to cure her [[pride|vanity]] rather than punish her (001-019). A [[wisdom|wise]] old [[water-people|water elf]] devises a plan to steal her reflection from the [[the-brook|water]] (001-022), which the community greets with acclaim (001-023). The elves wait until [[lamorna|Lamorna]] leans over the brook and capture her reflection with fine ropes of sand (001-027). Without her [[reflection|image]] to admire, [[lamorna|Lamorna]] falls into despair, but the loss forces her to look outward. She comes to recognize her fault and admits it publicly (001-098). When her reflection is restored, she is cured of [[pride|vanity]] and marries [[erick|Erick]]. (001-008 through 001-119)

> "We cannot keep her from looking in glasses or in the brook. There is only one thing, therefore, to be done. It will be difficult, but it is quite possible. We must wait till she is leaning over the water looking at herself, and then we must steal her reflection." (001-022)


> "So it is all for Lamorna," cried one, "worthless Lamorna, who does nothing but look at her own reflection, and loves nothing so much as her own pretty face."
>
> (001-017)


## Characters

- [[lamorna]] — the [[vanity|vain]] farmer's daughter, protagonist
- [[erick]] — the faithful fisherman who loves her
- [[the-wise-old-elf]] — the [[water-people|water elf]] who devises the reflection-theft plan
- [[water-people]] — the magical beings who cure [[lamorna|Lamorna]]

## Themes

- [[vanity]] — the central moral failing, explored as curable illness rather than sin (001-010, 001-012)
- [[beauty]] — the gift that becomes a trap when worshipped (001-008, 001-017)
- [[courage]] — the moral courage to admit fault (001-098)

## Structure and Narrative Features

Embedded within the frame story as a tale told by [[the-brooch|the Brooch]], who claims firsthand knowledge from "before I was cut and polished." The tale uses the brook as both mirror and portal, and the [[the-water-people|water people]]'s miniature society as a parallel moral community. The old elf's courteous intellectual pride (001-026) and his patient correction of the young elf's ignorance about reflections (001-029, 001-030) add [[charm]] and depth to the miniature society.

## Place in the Collection

First of three embedded tales in the "On a Pincushion" chapter. Establishes the collection's signature move: moral correction through gentle magical intervention rather than punishment. The [[the-water-people|water people]] explicitly reject punishment as a goal — "what good will it do to [[erick|Erick]] for us to punish her? Rather let us think how we can cure her of her [[pride|vanity]]" (001-019) — setting the benevolent tone that characterizes [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]]'s [[fairy]] tale ethics.
