# The Giant's Workshop

The enchanted shoemaker's shop in "[[siegfrid-and-handa-tale|Siegfrid and Handa]]," which appears to be an ordinary workshop but is actually a giant's trap — a place of captivity, magical shoe-craft, and ultimately liberation by fire.

## Overview

The giant's workshop is one of [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]]'s most sinister settings: a place that looks innocent — a shoemaker's shop — but is actually designed for kidnapping. The giant "has disguised himself as a shoemaker, that he may steal little boys and girls" (002-028). Children are fitted with [[magic-shoes|magic shoe]]s that bind them, and they are held in an underground cavern. The workshop thus embodies the tale's theme of deceptive appearances and hidden evil.

> "She is in a cavern underground," said the Hare. "She and all the other little girls are sitting there in a row, and they cannot move or speak, because on their feet are magic shoes that the old shoemaker puts on as soon as they come to him." (002-028)

## Geography and Description

The workshop appears to be an ordinary shoemaker's shop from the outside — a deliberate disguise. Inside, it contains rows of enchanted boots and shoes, including the "pretty pair of blue shoes that had first taken [[handa|Handa]]" (002-040). Beneath it, a cavern holds the captive children immobilized by [[magic-shoes|magic shoe]]s. The workshop contains tools for shoemaking that are actually instruments of captivity — the shoes are beautiful but are chains. [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] discovers the truth when the captive children explain the giant's nature (002-028).

## Narrative Significance

The workshop is the tale's dark heart — the place where evil hides in plain sight. Its destruction by fire is the tale's most dramatic act of liberation: [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] burns the entire stock of enchanted footwear, breaking the giant's power (002-045, 002-048). The setting serves as a critique of child labor and child endangerment disguised as honest trade — a recurring concern in Victorian social criticism that [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]] transposes into [[fairy]] tale form.
