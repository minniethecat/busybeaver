# The Pincushion

The object that gives the collection its name — a pincushion that serves as the miniature stage for the frame narrative, where a Brooch, a Shawl-pin, and a Pin tell stories to pass the time. (001-001)

## Overview

The pincushion is both a literal sewing accessory and the collection's conceit: an entire world in miniature, where jewelry and pins have personalities, complaints, and personal histories. [[the-brooch|The Brooch]] is stuck in it "so far that nothing but my head can be seen," [[the-shawl-pin|the Shawl-pin]] has been relegated to it after better days among cashmere, and from this cramped common ground, stories emerge. (001-001) (001-004)

## Geography and Description

The pincushion sits somewhere in a bedroom or dressing room, near a [[reflection|looking-glass]] over which the [[bracelets|Bracelets]] hang chattering. (001-006) It is stuffed firmly enough to hold pins deep, and its surface is the common meeting ground for the three complainants. The [[reflection|looking-glass]] above and the [[bracelets|Bracelets]] nearby create a small society of objects.

## Narrative Significance

The pincushion is a metaphor for the collection itself: a crowded space where diverse voices (tales) are stuck together, complaining about their circumstances, and passing the time through storytelling. It frames the embedded tales as domestic entertainments — stories told not in palaces or around campfires but on a sewing table.

## Characters Associated

- [[the-brooch]] — a pebble brooch stuck in the pincushion
- [[the-shawl-pin]] — a jet shawl-pin stuck nearby
- [[the-pin]] — a common brass pin buried deep

## From the Text

> On a pincushion were a pebble Brooch, a jet Shawl-pin, and a common Pin. They were all complaining because they were so often left behind instead of being taken out like the other brooches and pins.
>
> (001-001)

> “I don’t think,” said the Pin, “that either of you have as much cause to complain as I, for you are neither of you as useful, and might not be wanted, but I am always needed, and so many pins are taken every day that it seems hard I should be left here for nearly a week, and all because I am run so far into the pincushion that nothing but my head can be seen.”
>
> (001-004)
