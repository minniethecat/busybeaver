# The Seeds of Love

The second embedded tale in *On a Pincushion*, told after [[the-brooch|the Brooch]]'s story — a tale of patient virtue rewarded, where a [[magic-candles|magic candle]] summons [[love|Love]] and jewel-like seeds grow into a rose tree whose blooming mirrors the strength of a marriage.

## Synopsis

[[blanchelys|Blanchelys]], a gentle orphan [[life|living]] in a cottage with her jealous [[zaire|cousin Zaire]], receives a [[magic-candles|magic candle]] from a [[fairy]]. (001-124) (001-128) Following her grandmother's advice to save it, she eventually lights it at midnight under the yew-tree and summons [[love|Love]], who gives her jewel-like seeds. (001-134) She plants them at sunrise, and a rose tree grows whose health mirrors the Prince's growing love. [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] marries the Prince and becomes Queen, but [[zaire|Zaire]]'s jealousy threatens the tree — and [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]]'s happiness — until the tale resolves in virtue's triumph. (001-127 through 001-161)

## Characters

- [[blanchelys]] — the gentle protagonist who wins a Prince through patient virtue
- [[zaire]] — her jealous cousin and the tale's antagonist (001-150)
- [[the-rose-tree]] — the magical plant she nurtures
- [[magic-candles]] — the [[hope|wish]]-granting items that set events in motion

## Themes

- [[courage]] — [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] must summon courage to light the candle at midnight
- [[beauty]] — treated as secondary to character
- [[vanity]] — [[zaire|Zaire]]'s jealousy as another form of self-absorption

## Structure and Narrative Features

A Cinderella-variant with magical botany replacing [[taboret|the fairy godmother]]'s direct intervention. The rose tree functions as a continuous magical barometer of love's health.

## Place in the Collection

Second embedded tale in the "On a Pincushion" chapter. Pairs with "[[lamorna|Vain Lamorna]]" as studies in female virtue — [[lamorna|Lamorna]] is cured of [[pride|vanity]], [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] is rewarded for patient goodness.

> Blanchelys had gold-coloured hair, and eyes like blue cornflowers, and she laughed and sang from morning till night. Zaire's hair was black as a raven's wing, and her eyes were like large sloes. She was called the prettiest girl in all the village, but no one loved her as they did the blue-eyed Blanchelys.
>
> (001-124)
