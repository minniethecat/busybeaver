# The Creation of the Toy Princess

The central magical act of "[[a-toy-princess-tale|A Toy Princess]]" — [[taboret|the fairy godmother]] [[taboret|Taboret]] creates a mechanical double of [[princess-ursula|Princess Ursula]], indistinguishable from the real child, and places it at court.

## Overview

Having observed [[princess-ursula|Princess Ursula]]'s emotionally starved life — "never taught any games or given any toys" (004-010) — [[taboret|Taboret]] decides on a radical intervention. She crafts a [[toy-princess|Toy Princess]]: not a [[life|living]] being but a magical automaton, so perfectly made that it passes for [[princess-ursula|the real princess]] in every respect. The creation is an act of both magical skill and maternal love.

## Context

[[princess-ursula|Princess Ursula]]'s mother has died. The court raises her by protocol: no toys, no games, no [[love|affection]]. [[taboret|Taboret]], whom [[princess-ursula|Ursula]] "loved very dearly" (004-005), makes herself invisible to observe the child's life (004-011) before deciding on her course of action.

## The Event

[[taboret|Taboret]] creates the [[toy-princess|Toy Princess]] — a magical construct that looks, moves, and speaks exactly like [[princess-ursula|Ursula]]. The construct is placed at court where "they did not think it was not she" (004-058). The real [[princess-ursula|Ursula]] is secretly taken to a warm, loving home where she can develop naturally. (004-063)

## Consequences

[[a-toy-princess-tale|The Toy Princess]] rules flawlessly for years. The real [[princess-ursula|Ursula]] grows up with love and freedom. When [[princess-ursula|Ursula]] comes of age, [[taboret|Taboret]] reveals the substitution (004-072). The court is shocked to learn they were ruled by a puppet — and could not tell the difference.

## Participants

- [[taboret]] — [[taboret|the fairy godmother]] who creates the double
- [[princess-ursula]] — [[princess-ursula|the real princess]] who is liberated
- [[toy-princess]] — the magical construct created

## From the Text

> She was a pretty little girl, with a round baby face and big merry blue eyes; but as she grew older, her eyes grew less and less merry and bright, and her fat little face grew thin and pale. She was not allowed to play with any other children, lest she might learn bad manners; and she was not taught any games or given any toys. So she passed most of her time, when she was not at her lessons, looking out of the window at the birds flying against the clear blue sky; and sometimes she would give a sad little sigh when her ladies were not listening.
>
> (004-010)

> “I don’t care who I belong to,” said Ursula; “they won’t come and take me from you, and that’s all I care about.” So she grew tall and fair, and as she grew, the toy Princess, in her place at the court, grew too, and always was just like her, only that whereas Ursula’s face was sunburnt and her cheeks red, the face of the toy Princess was pale, with only a very slight tint in her cheeks.
>
> (004-063)
