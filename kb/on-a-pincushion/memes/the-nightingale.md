# The Nightingale

A magical singing bird in "On a Pincushion" who serves as the tale's storyteller, matchmaker, and keeper of [[forget|memory]] — his nightly song ensures that love is never forgotten.

## Overview

The Nightingale is the most artistically self-aware character in the collection. He does not merely sing — he tells stories through song, acting as the tale's internal narrator. His knowledge of the Moonbeam's beauty ("She is more beautiful than the morning," 001-239) and his role as intermediary between the [[the-sunbeam|Sunbeam]] and Moonbeam make him a matchmaker as well as a minstrel. And in the collection's closing line, his promise to "never [[forget]] them, for every night he sings their story" (001-288) elevates him to the role of [[life|living]] [[forget|memory]] — the artist whose work ensures that love outlasts its moment.

> "She is more beautiful than the morning," sang the Nightingale. (001-239)

## In the Story

### Role and Significance

The Nightingale bridges the celestial and the earthly. He talks to the [[the-sunbeam|Sunbeam]], sings to the Moonbeam, and mediates their impossible love across the day-night divide. His role as storyteller within the story creates a mirror of [[de-morgan-author-page|the author]]'s own role: [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]] tells the tale, and within it, the Nightingale sings it. This self-reflexive structure is the collection's most sophisticated narrative device.

### Key Actions

- Tells the [[bullfinch|Bullfinch]] about the Moonbeam's beauty, sparking the [[the-sunbeam|Sunbeam]]'s curiosity (001-225)
- Sings to the [[the-sunbeam|Sunbeam]] of the Moonbeam: "She is more beautiful than the morning" (001-239)
- Promises to sing to the [[the-sunbeam|Sunbeam]] every night, keeping their friendship alive (001-240–001-242)
- Announces the Moonbeam's arrival: "She is coming!" (001-244)
- Makes the eternal vow: "the Nightingale will never [[forget]] them, for every night he sings their story" (001-288)

### Relationships

- [[the-sunbeam]] — his friend, whom he helps find love
- [[the-moonbeam]] — the subject of his most beautiful songs (not yet a page)
- [[on-a-pincushion]] — the tale he inhabits and immortalizes

## Character Interpretation

The Nightingale embodies the Romantic ideal of the artist: he creates beauty that preserves love beyond death. His role also echoes the classical myth of Philomela ([[transformation|transform]]ed into a nightingale), but [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]] strips away the violence of that myth, leaving only the song. His character suggests that the highest purpose of art is to remember — to sing the story so that those who are gone are not forgotten.
