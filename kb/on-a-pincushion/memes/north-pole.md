## Description

The North Pole in "[[through-the-fire-tale|Through the Fire]]" is a realm of solid ice at the far north of the world. It is described as "an immense plain of solid ice" with no open [[the-brook|water]] visible (005-190). The moon shines brightly upon it, and a curious pink light darts up into the sky from the old man's lantern — "a clear pink light that darted up into the sky in bars" emanating from a "curious dark lump in the form of a mushroom, which stood up into the air" (005-196).

## The Old Man of the North Pole

The North Pole is the home of a solitary old man who "knows everything" and sits upon the Pole itself (005-136). He is described as "very mischievous and cunning, and always does his best to deceive anyone who comes to him for help" (005-142). Critically, he is bound to answer the first question asked of him truthfully — but if anyone asks a second question, he will "at once seize you and keep you under the ice" (005-142).

> The old man at the North Pole is very mischievous and cunning, and always does his best to deceive anyone who comes to him for help. You must not, whatever happens, ask him more than one question.
>
> (005-142)

He lives alone, having quarrelled with the old man at the South Pole, with whom he was once on good terms — they "often slid up and down the Pole to see each other" before their falling-out (005-199).

## The Ice-People

The North Pole is inhabited by ice-people — "almost transparent forms of men and women, with deadly white faces and cold glittering eyes" who move noiselessly over the surface (005-190). They never speak but "always glide about" silently. They are dangerous: beneath the clear ice lie the frozen bodies of men and women whom the ice-people have caught and [[death|frozen to death]] (005-195). They flee from fire and warmth.

## Role in the Story

[[little-jack|Little Jack]] travels to the North Pole on the shoulders of a [[fairy|wind-fairy]] to ask the old man how [[princess-pyra|Princess Pyra]] and [[prince-fluvius|Prince Fluvius]] can be married (005-149). The fireball given by the Princess keeps [[jack|Jack]] warm during the journey, and the [[fairy|wind-fairy]] protects him from the ice-people. [[jack|Jack]] successfully asks the single permitted question and receives the answer before returning.
