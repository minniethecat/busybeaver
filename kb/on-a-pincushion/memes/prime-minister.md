# The Prime Minister

A minor courtier in [[a-toy-princess|"A Toy Princess"]] who sits on the King's council. When the King learns that the [[toy-princess]] is not a real person, the Prime Minister defends her as "a truly [[charm]]ing young lady" (004-073). Later, when asked to choose between the artificial toy and the [[life|living]] [[princess-ursula|Princess Ursula]], he says to the [[chancellor|Chancellor]], "I should think there could be no doubt which one would prefer" (004-109) — and votes with the rest of the court for the sham Princess, representing the court's preference for artifice over authenticity.

> "I should think there could be no doubt which one would prefer," said the Prime Minister to the Chancellor.
>
> (004-109)
