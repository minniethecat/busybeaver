# The Seeds of Love

Tiny, jewel-like seeds that fall from roses and must be planted at sunrise — a magical item appearing in the embedded tale "The Seeds of Love," the second story told by [[the-brooch|the Brooch]] in the frame narrative of *On a Pincushion*.

## Overview

The seeds appear when [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] shakes roses with her companion. They are described as being "more like jewels than seeds" (001-144) and come with specific magical instructions: they must be planted in the earth "just as the sun is rising" (001-141) and covered before the sun is fully up. The seeds represent a magical economy of careful, conditional magic — their power depends on correct ritual observance, not merely possession.

> “Take them,” he said, holding them out to Blanchelys, “and plant them in the earth just as the sun is rising; but ere you cover them up breathe over them the name of him whose love you desire. From them will spring a rose-tree, and as it grows so his love for you will grow. While that tree lives he will love you more than all the world, but should it pine and die his love for you would wane and die also, and then only one thing in the world would make it live again. And beware of one thing—never, whatever happens, let any other hand but yours touch the tree, or a terrible misfortune will come of it.”
>
> (001-141)

## In the Narrative

### Acquisition

[[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] and her companion gather the seeds when they shake roses at night. The seeds fall from the roses and are collected in darkness. (001-140) [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] examines them in the morning light and finds them jewel-like. (001-144) The conditional nature of the magic — sunrise planting — suggests a connection to solar magic and the broader theme of light and hidden things that runs through the collection.

### Narrative Function

The seeds serve as a quest object and [[transformation|transform]]ation trigger. They require the hero to act at a specific magical time (dawn), introducing temporal ritual into the magic system. Unlike the [[magic-candles|magic candle]]s (simple [[hope|wish]]-devices) or [[magic-shoes|magic shoe]]s (ongoing [[magic|enchantment]]), the seeds are one-use [[transformation|transform]]ative items whose power is tied to cosmic cycles.

## Literary Context

[[magic|Magic]]al seeds appear in folklore worldwide — from [[jack|Jack]]'s beanstalk beans to the enchanted seeds in Celtic tales. [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]]'s seeds are distinctive in their jewel-like appearance (blurring the line between natural and crafted magic) and their solar timing requirement, which connects botanical magic to celestial mechanics.
