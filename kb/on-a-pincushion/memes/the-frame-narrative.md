# The Frame Narrative

The framing device of *On a Pincushion*, in which a Brooch, a Shawl-pin, and a Pin — stuck together on a pincushion — tell stories to pass the time, producing the embedded tales that form the collection. (001-001)

## Synopsis

A pebble Brooch, a jet Shawl-pin, and a common brass Pin are all stuck in a pincushion, complaining about being overlooked. (001-001) Annoyed by the chattering of [[bracelets|Bracelets]] hanging over the [[reflection|looking-glass]], the Pin proposes they tell stories. (001-006) [[the-brooch|The Brooch]] volunteers first, telling "[[the-story-of-vain-lamorna|The Story of Vain Lamorna]]" (which it claims to have witnessed before being cut and polished). (001-007) The frame establishes an object's-eye view of human folly and a miniature society of pins with distinct personalities and past lives.

## Characters

- [[the-brooch]] — a colorful pebble brooch, first storyteller
- [[the-shawl-pin]] — a jet pin fallen from aristocratic [[servant|service]], moral commentator
- [[the-pin]] — a common brass pin, the practical initiator of storytelling

## Themes

- [[vanity]] — the objects themselves display [[pride|vanity]] about their status
- [[beauty]] — [[the-brooch|the Brooch]]'s colors versus [[the-shawl-pin|the Shawl-pin]]'s plainness

## Structure and Narrative Features

A frame narrative in the tradition of *The Canterbury Tales* or *The Decameron*, miniaturized to a sewing table.

## Place in the Collection

The outermost narrative layer of *On a Pincushion*. All embedded and standalone tales are implicitly framed by this storytelling conceit.

> On a pincushion were a pebble Brooch, a jet Shawl-pin, and a common Pin. They were all complaining because they were so often left behind instead of being taken out like the other brooches and pins.
>
> (001-001)
