# The Sun's Story

A deeply embedded narrative within "[[the-seeds-of-love|The Seeds of Love]]" — the [[the-nightingale|Nightingale]]'s story of the Sun's son, who was [[transformation|transform]]ed and mourned in song.

## Synopsis

Within "[[the-seeds-of-love|The Seeds of Love]]," a [[the-nightingale|Nightingale]] sings a story about the Sun's son. The Sun is "ruled by a mighty [[fairy]], who every morning tells his [[servant|servants]] to open the gates of the East" (001-220). [[the-nightingale|The Nightingale]]'s song tells of love, loss, and [[transformation|transform]]ation, the bird itself being a [[transformation|transform]]ed being — "that is why his song is so sad" (001-288).

## Characters

- The Sun — ruled by a mighty [[fairy]]
- [[the-nightingale|The Nightingale]] — a [[transformation|transform]]ed being who sings the story
- The Sun's son — the subject of the lament

## Themes

- [[beauty]] — the [[the-sunbeam|Sunbeam]]'s hidden nature
- [[fire-element]] — solar fire as both life-giving and dangerous

## Structure and Narrative Features

A triple-embedded narrative (frame → Brooch's tale → [[the-nightingale|Nightingale]]'s song). The deepest narrative layer in the collection.

## Place in the Collection

Embedded within "[[the-seeds-of-love|The Seeds of Love]]" (which is itself embedded in the frame narrative).
