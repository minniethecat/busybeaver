# The Release of the Reflection

The restorative event in "[[the-story-of-vain-lamorna|The Story of Vain Lamorna]]" — the [[water-people|water elves]], convinced of [[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s cure, cut the sand ropes and let her reflection rise back to the surface and return to her.

## Overview

After [[lamorna|Lamorna]] admits her fault, [[the-old-elf|the wise old elf]] declares: "She is quite cured of her [[pride|vanity]]. Let us cut the ropes of sand, and let her [[reflection|image]] free." (001-098) The reflection — which [[lamorna|Lamorna]] once spent her days admiring, "talking to her own reflection" (001-008), and which the [[water-people|water elves]] had plotted to "steal" (001-022) as a cure for her [[vanity]] — is released, rising like a bubble to the surface and reuniting with [[lamorna|Lamorna]].

## Context

The reflection has been held in [[the-brook-grotto|the special grotto]] since the theft. [[lamorna|Lamorna]] has suffered, repented, and grown. The [[water-people|water elves]] have monitored her progress and judged her ready.

## The Event

The sand ropes are cut. The reflection rises. [[lamorna|Lamorna]] sees herself again — but this time, she sees clearly. She is no longer the [[vanity|vain]] girl who admired only her own face.

## Consequences

[[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s reflection is fully restored. She is reunited with her [[reflection|self-image]] — but now with self-knowledge added. The tale moves toward its happy resolution: [[lamorna|Lamorna]] and [[erick|Erick]]'s marriage.

## Participants

- [[water-people]] — the releasers
- [[the-wise-old-elf]] — who gives the order
- [[lamorna]] — the recipient of the restored reflection
