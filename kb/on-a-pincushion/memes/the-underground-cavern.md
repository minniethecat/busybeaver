# The Underground Cavern

The dark prison beneath the giant's workshop in "[[siegfrid-and-handa-tale|Siegfrid and Handa]]," where captured children are held in enchanted captivity — a subterranean space of fear and eventual liberation.

## Overview

The cavern represents the hidden layer beneath deceptive appearances. The workshop above looks innocent; the cavern below holds the truth. It is described as "a cavern underground" (002-028) where [[handa|Handa]] "and all the other little girls and boys" are kept. A dark passage "leads to the underground cavern" (002-046) in which the captive children sit, bound by [[magic-shoes|enchanted shoes]]. The cavern is reached through the workshop, making the journey downward a descent into [[truth|revelation]].

## Geography and Description

The cavern is underground, accessible from the giant's workshop. It is dark and large enough to hold multiple captive children. The [[magic-shoes|magic shoe]]s worn by the captives bind them to this space — they cannot leave while wearing the enchanted footwear.

## Narrative Significance

The cavern is the tale's underworld — a place of captivity that the hero must enter and from which he must rescue the prisoners. [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]]'s descent and return mirrors the mythic hero's journey: katabasis (descent) followed by anabasis (ascent with rescued souls).

> "First listen to me," said the Hare, "for when the shoes begin to burn I shall run off. When they have quite burnt out, you will find a heap of black ashes, and amongst them you must search carefully, and you will find a pair of sandals that seem not the least burnt or singed. These you must put on, and then stamp hard upon the ground, and it will open, and in front of you, you will see a long dark passage which leads to the underground cavern in which sit Handa and her companions."
>
> (002-046)
