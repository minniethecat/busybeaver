# Siegfrid

The heroic protagonist of "[[siegfrid-and-handa-tale|Siegfrid and Handa]]," a tale in *On a Pincushion* — a young man who defeats a giant disguised as a shoemaker, frees captive children, and wins [[handa|Handa]]'s love through courage and quick thinking.

## Overview

Siegfrid's story begins when, as an infant, he is found in a basket by an old couple and raised alongside a foundling girl named [[handa|Handa]]. His defining trait is courage, expressed through action rather than boast:

> Siegfrid summoned all his courage, and sitting on the edge of the hole dropped gently into the passage, and ran down it. (002-049)

Unlike [[fairy]]-tale princes who are born to heroism, Siegfrid is a foundling — his heroism is earned, not inherited.

## In the Story

### Role and Significance

Siegfrid serves as the active hero archetype in the collection. Where other protagonists ([[lamorna|Lamorna]], [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]]) are passive and must be acted upon by magical forces, Siegfrid acts directly: he enters the giant's workshop, discovers the [[magic|enchantment]], and defeats the villain. His heroism combines physical courage with cleverness — he repurposes the giant's own [[magic-shoes|magic shoe]]s as a tool of escape.

## Character Interpretation

Siegfrid's name echoes the Germanic hero Siegfried/Sigurd, but [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]]'s version is a domestic hero — his dragon is a shoemaker-giant, his treasure is [[handa|Handa]], and his weapon is fire and quick thinking rather than a magic sword. This domestication of the epic hero reflects the literary [[fairy]] tale's tendency to shrink epic stakes to personal ones while preserving the heroic structure.

> They soon caught fire, and burnt steadily, but Siegfrid noticed that they made no noise except a low hissing like water boiling. The pile went on burning for some time, and then went out quite suddenly, with a loud bang. (002-045)
