# The Snake Princess

An enchanted princess in "[[the-hair-tree-tale|The Hair Tree]]" who appears in serpent form — a guardian figure whose defeat requires negotiation and cleverness rather than force.

## Overview

The snake-princess is one of the obstacles [[rupert|Rupert]] faces on his quest for the Hair Tree seeds. Unlike the tigress who threatens physically, the snake-princess is a creature of demands: she wants [[rupert|Rupert]] to marry her. "He [[hope|wish]]es me to marry the snake-princess" (003-079, 003-086). Her serpent form is an [[magic|enchantment]] — she is a princess [[transformation|transform]]ed rather than a monster by nature — making her a victim as much as an antagonist.

## In the Story

### The Tortoise-King's Confession

The snake-princess is first mentioned by the [[tortoise-king]] himself, who reveals to [[trevina|Trevina]] that his mother [[hope|wish]]es him to marry the snake-princess instead of a human. "I fear the jealousy of my mother, who [[hope|wish]]es me to marry the snake-princess" (003-079). This positions the snake-princess as an arranged-match figure — desired by [[the-tortoise-king|the tortoise]]-queen as a bride for her son, but not necessarily an active agent herself.

### Encounter with Rupert

When [[rupert|Rupert]] encounters her on the way to the Hair Tree, she makes a demand: marriage. Her condition is a test of his commitment to his quest — and of the [[seagull]]'s cleverness in getting him past her.

## Powers and Nature

The snake-princess is an enchanted being whose serpent form is clearly not her true nature. The [[transformation]] was likely imposed upon her, making her story parallel to [[trevina|Trevina]]'s own [[magic|enchantment]] into a tigress.

> "Be not astonished, lovely Trevina, and be assured that no harm shall come to you. Know that I am no common tortoise, but am the king of the tortoises, and only for your sake have assumed the shape of a common animal, in order that I might dwell near you. I have loved you from the first moment I saw you, and now intend to make you my wife... I should do so at once, were it not that I fear the jealousy of my mother, who wishes me to marry the snake-princess."
>
> (003-079)
