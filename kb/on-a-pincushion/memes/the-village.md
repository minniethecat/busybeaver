# The Village

The unnamed rural village where [[lamorna|Lamorna]] and [[erick|Erick]] live — a pastoral setting bounded by the brook on one side and the sea on the other, whose rhythms of farming and fishing ground the embedded tales.

## Overview

The village is the primary human setting of "[[the-story-of-vain-lamorna|The Story of Vain Lamorna]]." It is a working community — [[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s father is a farmer, [[erick|Erick]] is a fisherman — and its ordinariness contrasts with the magical realm of the [[the-brook|water]] people beneath the brook. The village's proximity to both [[the-brook|water]] (the brook runs through it to the sea) and land (farming fields) makes it a threshold community where the magical and the mundane naturally overlap. (001-010)

## Geography and Description

The village is centered on a brook that "ran into the sea about half a mile farther down" (001-010). [[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s cottage has a fire and a mirror over the chimneypiece (001-033). [[erick|Erick]] keeps his boat nearby and rows out to sea to fish. The village is small enough that everyone knows [[lamorna|Lamorna]] and tells her she is pretty, yet large enough to have a pub where the young people gather.

## Narrative Significance

The village functions as the "normal world" from which the magic of the [[the-brook|water]] people feels extraordinary. [[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s journey is not a physical quest but an internal one — she never leaves the village, but her ability to see it and its people changes when she can no longer see herself. The brook, which runs through the village, is the portal to the magical.

## Characters Associated

- [[lamorna]] — the farmer's daughter who lives in the village
- [[erick]] — the fisherman who rows from the village shore
- [[water-people]] — the magical beings in the brook that runs through the village

## From the Text

> The girl’s name was Lamorna, and she was the daughter of a farmer. Everyone told her she was very pretty, and so indeed she was. She had bright brown hair and big brown eyes, and a mouth like a rosebud. The brook by which she stood ran into the sea about half a mile farther down, and it was full of water people. Water people are a sort of elves, who live beneath the water, and never come to the surface, because if they were to breathe the air they would die.
>
> (001-010)
