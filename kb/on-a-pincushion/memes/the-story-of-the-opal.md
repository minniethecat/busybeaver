# The Story of the Opal

The third embedded tale mentioned in the "On a Pincushion" frame narrative, told after "[[the-story-of-vain-lamorna|The Story of Vain Lamorna]]" and "[[the-seeds-of-love|The Seeds of Love]]" — a tale of [[transformation|transform]]ation and magical gems.

## Synopsis

After [[the-brooch|the Brooch]] tells "[[the-story-of-vain-lamorna|The Story of Vain Lamorna]]" and the [[the-seeds-of-love-tale|Seeds of Love]] narrative follows, a third story — "The Story of the Opal" — is told, completing the storytelling session on [[the-pincushion|the pincushion]]. The opal's story continues the collection's fascination with gems, [[transformation|transform]]ation, and the life of objects that were once something else.

## Characters

- An [[the-opal|Opal]] — the titular object, like [[the-brooch|the Brooch]], a gem with a history
- Characters inferred from the collection's thematic patterns

## Themes

- [[vanity]] — gems and their beauty as moral testing grounds
- [[beauty]] — the opal's iridescence as a symbol of [[transformation|transform]]ation

## Structure and Narrative Features

Completes the trilogy of embedded tales within the frame narrative. The opal continues the pattern of objects telling stories about their pre-ornament [[life|existence]].

## Place in the Collection

Third embedded tale in the "On a Pincushion" chapter. The briefest of the embedded tales, mentioned more than narrated.

> Men found it and called it the Opal. But the Nightingale knew that it was the Sunbeam and Moonbeam who, in dying, had suffused the Stone with their mingled colours and light; and the Nightingale will never forget them, for every night he sings their story, and that is why his song is so sad.
>
