# Magic Candles

A pair of [[hope|wish]]-granting candles given by a [[fairy]] to the old couple who find a mysterious infant in a basket, appearing in the frame story of *On a Pincushion* as the catalyst for the embedded tale of [[siegfrid-and-handa-tale|Siegfrid and Handa]].

## Overview

The magic candles appear in the framing narrative when the Pin tells a story about an old man and his wife who find a baby. A [[fairy]] appears to the wife and gives her two small candles with the power to grant the [[hope|wish]] of one's heart. One candle is meant for the foundling girl ([[handa|Handa]]) and one for her eventual suitor ([[siegfrid|Siegfrid]]). The candles represent a [[fairy]]-tale economy of [[hope|wish]]es — limited, precious, and requiring wisdom in their use. (001-127)

> "These two little candles; they are magic candles, and when you set them alight, there will appear to you a fairy who will grant you the wish of your heart." (001-127)

## In the Narrative

### The Gift

The magic candles are given by a [[fairy]] to the wife, with instructions that they are for the foundling girl and the boy who will come to love her. The wife treasures them, and when [[handa|Handa]] comes of age, she lights her candle to [[hope|wish]] for [[handa|Handa]]'s happiness — a selfless use that immediately distinguishes the story's moral framework from tales of greedy [[hope|wish]]ing. (001-127)

### Narrative Function

The candles serve as a deferred [[hope|wish]] mechanism: they create anticipation across the narrative, and their eventual use reveals character. The wife's selfless [[hope|wish]] for [[handa|Handa]] sets the moral tone for the embedded tale of [[siegfrid-and-handa-tale|Siegfrid and Handa]] that follows. The second candle, meant for [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]], remains as a narrative promise — a [[hope|wish]] yet to be made. (001-129)

## Literary Context

Wish-granting objects are a staple of European [[fairy]] tales — from magic rings to [[hope|wish]]ing wells — but [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]]'s candles are distinctive in their paired, relational design: one each for two people, binding their fates together. The candle as a medium is also evocative of domestic warmth and temporality (candles burn down, [[hope|wish]]es have their moment), contrasting with the permanent magic of rings or stones found in traditional tales.
