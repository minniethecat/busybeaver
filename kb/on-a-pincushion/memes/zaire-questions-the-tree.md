# Zaire Questions the Tree

The moment of psychological danger in "[[the-seeds-of-love|The Seeds of Love]]" — [[zaire|Zaire]] approaches [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] under the rose tree and questions her about what she values most, her "eyes glittering eagerly" (001-158).

## Overview

[[zaire|Zaire]], consumed by jealousy, has learned that [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]]'s happiness is tied to the rose tree. She approaches [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] under the tree's shade and begins a seemingly innocent conversation that is actually an interrogation. "Then if that tree were to die, would you be very unhappy, [[blanchelys|cousin Blanchelys]]?" (001-158)

## Context

[[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] is Queen, happy with her Prince and baby. [[zaire|Zaire]] has been growing more bitter and jealous since [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]]'s rise. She "thought night and day of how she could do her any harm" (001-150).

## The Event

[[zaire|Zaire]] questions [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] about what she loves most. Husband, baby, rose tree. The questioning is gentle in tone but predatory in intent. [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]], innocent, answers truthfully: "if my tree were to die, I think it would break my heart" (001-159).

> "Then if that tree were to die, would you be very unhappy, cousin Blanchelys?" asked Zaire, and her eyes glittered eagerly.
> "Yes, if my tree were to die, I think it would break my heart," said Blanchelys, and she turned quite pale at the thought.
>
> (001-158)(001-159)

## Consequences

[[zaire|Zaire]] now knows exactly how to hurt [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]]. The rose tree is identified as the vulnerability — not [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]]'s life but her happiness.
