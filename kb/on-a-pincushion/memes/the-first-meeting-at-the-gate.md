# The First Meeting at the Gate

The human beginning of [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]]'s love story — the morning after she plants the [[the-seeds-of-love|magic seeds]], the Prince stops at the cottage gate and asks her for a glass of [[the-brook|water]].

## Overview

"That morning, when the King's son rode past to the hunt, he stopped at the cottage door, and asked [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] to give him a glass of [[the-brook|water]]. It was the first time he had ever spoken to her, and her heart beat high with joy." (001-145) This simple, ordinary encounter is the seed from which everything grows — literally, in the rose tree.

## Context

[[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] has just planted the [[magic-candles|magic seeds]] her mother gave her. The seeds are a test: if they grow, her love is true; if not, her suitor is false. The very next morning, the Prince appears — not knowing he is being [[magic|measured]].

## The Event

The encounter is deliberately mundane: a rider, a cottage, a glass of [[the-brook|water]]. There is no grand gesture, no dramatic rescue. The Prince asks; [[blanchelys|Blanchelys]] gives. But for her, this simple exchange is [[transformation|transform]]ative — "her heart beat high with joy."

> That morning, when the King's son rode past to the hunt, he stopped at the cottage door, and asked Blanchelys to give him a glass of water. It was the first time he had ever spoken to her, and her heart beat high with joy. At night, when she went to look at her flowerpot, she found that a tiny shoot was appearing above the earth in the pot.
>
> (001-145)

## Consequences

The seeds sprout. The rose tree grows. The love is true. This simple meeting — a drink of [[the-brook|water]] — sets in motion everything that follows: marriage, a child, and the eventual threat to the rose tree that will test their love again.

## Participants

- [[blanchelys]] — the cottage girl whose heart beats high
- [[the-rose-tree]] — the magical plant that responds to the meeting
