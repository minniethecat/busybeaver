# Handa

The foundling girl raised alongside [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] in the eponymous tale, whose capture by the giant-shoemaker sets the rescue plot in motion and whose [[magic-shoes|magic shoe]]s become the instrument of escape.

## Overview

Handa is introduced as an infant found alongside [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]], the two raised together by an old couple. The [[fairy]]'s [[magic-candles|magic candle]]s are intended for both of them (001-127), binding their fates from the start. Handa's role in the tale is both as the object of rescue — she is captured by the giant and held underground — and as an active participant in escape, since the [[magic-shoes|magic shoe]]s on her feet carry both her and [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] to safety (002-050).

> But little Handa shook her head and said, "You are very cruel, you are letting poor Ralph starve, because you will all buy your boots and shoes of the new old man."
>
> (002-017)

## In the Story

### Role and Significance

Handa is less characterized than [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] — she does not summon courage or wield fire — but she is not merely a damsel in distress. The [[magic-shoes|magic shoe]]s are on her feet, making her the literal vehicle of their escape. Her role as the foundling beloved of the hero echoes a common [[fairy]]-tale pattern (the disguised princess, the worthy orphan), but [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]] gives her the practical function of bearing the magical item that saves them both.

### Key Actions

- Raised as a foundling alongside [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] by an old couple
- Captured by the giant-shoemaker and held in an underground cavern (002-028)
- Wears the [[magic-shoes|magic shoe]]s that [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]] remembers at the critical moment of escape (002-050)
- Marries [[siegfrid|Siegfrid]], fulfilling the [[fairy]]'s long-ago plan

### Relationships

- [[siegfrid]] — her fellow foundling, rescuer, and eventual husband
- [[magic-shoes]] — the enchanted footwear she wears
- [[magic-candles]] — the items that set their shared fate in motion
