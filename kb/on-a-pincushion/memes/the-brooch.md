# The Brooch

A pebble brooch stuck in a pincushion, who serves as the first storyteller in the frame narrative of *On a Pincushion* — an object with [[forget|memory]], personality, and a past life before it became jewelry.

## Overview

The Brooch is one of three complaining objects on [[the-pincushion|the pincushion]] (with [[the-shawl-pin|the Shawl-pin]] and the Pin) who decide to tell stories to drown out the chatter of the [[bracelets|Bracelets]] (001-001). The Brooch volunteers first: "I will tell you one, and I know it to be true, for it was before I was cut and polished, and I was at the place myself where it happened" (001-007). This claim of firsthand knowledge — being at the site of the story before it was [[transformation|transform]]ed into jewelry — is a striking narrative device that grounds the [[fairy]] tales in a material witness. The Brooch tells "[[the-story-of-vain-lamorna|The Story of Vain Lamorna]]."


> On a pincushion were a pebble Brooch, a jet Shawl-pin, and a common Pin. They were all complaining because they were so often left behind instead of being taken out like the other brooches and pins.
>
> (001-001)

## In the Story

### Role and Significance

The Brooch functions as a frame-story narrator, giving the embedded tales a material anchor. As "all sorts of colours" and therefore "might be used any day" (001-001), the Brooch has a cosmopolitan perspective — it has seen more of the world than the plain black Shawl-pin. Its complaint about being left behind on [[the-pincushion|the pincushion]] is also a complaint about being overlooked despite its beauty, a theme that echoes [[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s story.

### Key Actions

- Complains about being left behind despite being colorful and versatile: "you cannot expect to be used every day" (001-003)
- Tells "[[the-story-of-vain-lamorna|The Story of Vain Lamorna]]," claiming firsthand knowledge from before being cut and polished (001-007)
- Comments critically after [[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s story: "Of course some people are [[vanity|vain]] who have nothing whatever to be [[vanity|vain]] of" (001-120)
- Reacts emotionally to the [[the-nightingale|Nightingale]]'s story: "an uncomfortable story," said the Brooch, "in rather a husky voice" (001-217)
- Summons the Pin to tell its story: "now it is your turn" (001-219)
- Delivers the final verdict on the fire story: "it is instructive as well as entertaining" (001-289)

### Relationships

- [[the-shawl-pin]] — fellow complainer and audience for its story (001-001, 001-003)
- [[the-pin]] — fellow object on [[the-pincushion|the pincushion]], whom the Brooch invites to tell the next tale (001-219)
- [[lamorna]] — the subject of the Brooch's embedded tale

## Character Interpretation

The Brooch's claim to have witnessed [[lamorna|Lamorna]]'s story "before I was cut and polished" (001-007) makes it older than its current form — a pebble that became jewelry. This [[transformation|transform]]ation from natural object to crafted ornament mirrors the tales' concern with [[transformation|transform]]ation and identity. The Brooch is the most opinionated of the three storytellers, offering running commentary on the other tales and helping to bind the frame narrative together.
