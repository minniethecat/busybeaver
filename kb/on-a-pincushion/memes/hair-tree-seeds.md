# Hair Tree Seeds

[[magic|Magic]]al seeds from the Hair Tree that restore hair — tiny objects of [[transformation|transform]]ative power that drive the quest narrative of the third tale in the collection.

## Overview

The seeds of the Hair Tree are the most personal of [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]]'s magical items: they address the intimate [[shame|embarrassment]] of hair loss rather than grand [[fairy]]-tale stakes. The hero learns that "the only thing that could ever make my hair grow is some seeds from the Hair Tree" (003-023), setting him on a quest that leads through enchanted forests and past magical guardians. The seeds are carried as "a handful of tiny seeds from his pocket" (003-021), making them simultaneously precious and portable — magic that fits in a palm.

> And as he sang these words he took out a handful of tiny seeds from his pocket, and began to scatter them about. Directly there sprang up a little plant, which grew and grew and grew until it became a big tree, and then it put forth buds, and the buds burst, and there came out not leaves, but tiny locks of hair, which grew and grew until they touched the ground, and covered the tree all over.
>
> (003-021)

## In the Narrative

### The Quest

[[the-hair-tree-tale|The Hair Tree]] seeds function as a classic [[fairy]]-tale quest object. Unlike the [[magic-candles|magic candle]]s (gifted by a [[fairy]]) or [[magic-shoes|magic shoe]]s (taken from an enemy), the seeds must be actively sought in a distant magical country. The hero's journey to obtain them — facing a tigress (003-076) and a [[the-snake-princess|snake-princess]] (003-079) — is the substance of the tale, making the seeds not just a magical item but a narrative structure.

### Powers and Limits

The seeds' magic is specific and limited: they make hair grow. This specificity is characteristic of [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]]'s magic system, where items do one thing well rather than granting unlimited [[hope|wish]]es. The limitation creates narrative discipline: the hero cannot simply [[hope|wish]] his problems away but must use the seeds' specific power at the right moment.

## Literary Context

Quest objects in folklore typically promise wealth, power, or healing from mortal wounds. [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]]'s choice of hair restoration as the ultimate prize is both comic and humane — it treats small vanities as worthy of epic journeys, democratizing the heroic quest. The seeds fit into a broader [[de-morgan-author-page|De Morgan]] pattern of magical botany (cf. the [[the-seeds-of-love-tale|Seeds of Love]], also tiny jewel-like seeds).
