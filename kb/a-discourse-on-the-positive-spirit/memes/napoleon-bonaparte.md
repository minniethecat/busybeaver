**Napoleon Bonaparte** (15 August 1769 – 5 May 1821) was a French military commander and political leader who rose to prominence during the French Revolution and became Emperor of the French from 1804 to 1814.

## Biography

Born in Corsica, Napoleon trained as an artillery officer and achieved rapid advancement during the Revolutionary Wars. He seized power in the Coup of 18 Brumaire (1799) and established the French Empire, conquering much of Europe before his defeat at Waterloo (1815). He died in exile on Saint Helena. His legal reforms, particularly the Napoleonic Code, had lasting influence on civil law systems worldwide.

## Connection to Positivism

Comte treats Napoleon — always under the surname "Bonaparte" — as the embodiment of retrograde tyranny in post-revolutionary France. Across five distinct references in *A Discourse on the Positivist Spirit*, Napoleon is presented as the principal agent of reaction who exploited the metaphysical confusion of the era to impose military despotism.

In Chapter 3, Comte describes the post-revolutionary period as dominated by reaction, with Napoleon as its central figure:

> A long period of reaction ensued. Dating from the official Deism introduced by Robespierre, it reached its height under the aggressive system of Bonaparte. (003-020)

Comte also notes the persistent efforts of the French press "to rehabilitate the name of Bonaparte" (003-032), viewing this as evidence of the intellectual corruption of the metaphysical school.

In the same chapter, Comte explicitly identifies Napoleon as a type of the retrograde leader, pairing him with the Roman emperor Julian:

> The greatest opponents of progress, Julian and Bonaparte; the latter being the more criminal of the two, as having had before him the example of the failure of the former. (003-080)

In Chapter 7, Comte traces a continuous line of counter-revolutionary action:

> The Counter-revolution, begun by Robespierre, carried to its full length by Bonaparte, and continued by the Bourbons. (007-104)

For Comte, Napoleon represents the military-theological phase that [[positive-philosophy|positivism]] must definitively supersede.
