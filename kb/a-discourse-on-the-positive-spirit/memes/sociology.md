# Sociology

**Sociology** is the scientific study of society, patterns of social relationships, social interaction, and culture. [[Auguste Comte]] originally termed this field "Social Physics" (*physique sociale*) and is widely regarded as the father of sociology.

Comte argued that sociology should be the culmination of all sciences — the "queen of the sciences" — because it applies the [[scientific-spirit|scientific method]] to the most complex subject matter: human society itself. He divided sociology into two branches: social statics (the study of [[social-statics|social order]]) and social dynamics (the study of social change and progress).

## Branches of Sociology

Comte divided sociology into two fundamental branches: social statics and social dynamics. Social statics studies the conditions of social order and the laws of social harmony — the consensus among the parts of the social system. Social dynamics investigates the laws of social progress and the successive transformations of human society through history. (002-051)
