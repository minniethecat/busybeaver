**Thomas Malthus** (1766–1834) was an English economist and demographer best known for his theory that population growth tends to outstrip food supply, published in *An Essay on the Principle of Population* (1798).

## Connection to Positivism

Comte condemns Malthus's theory as a pernicious instance of false social doctrine reinforcing selfishness:

> A false social doctrine has often favoured the natural ascendency of Self-love by giving a perverted conception of public well-being. This has been too plainly exemplified in our own time by the deplorable influence which Malthus's sophistical theory of population obtained in England. (004-028)

Comte notes that the theory gained currency primarily in England and was rejected elsewhere in Europe. He regards it as providing a pseudo-scientific justification for ruling-class indifference to social reform — a direct contradiction of [[positivism|Positivism]]'s commitment to the primacy of social feeling over self-interest.
