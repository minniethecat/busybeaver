**Martin Luther** (1483–1546) was a German theologian and the central figure of the Protestant Reformation.

## Connection to Positivism

[[auguste-comte|Auguste Comte]], in his *Discourse on the Positivist Spirit*, references Luther in the context of comparing the state of religious belief across Europe. Comte argues that the decline of Christian belief began well before the Reformation, and that [[catholicism|Catholicism]] persists in Southern Europe because it better satisfies moral and social needs than available alternatives:

> All evidence, especially the evidence of the poets, goes to prove that before Luther's time, there was less belief in the South of Europe, certainly less in Italy, than in the North. (005-138)

Comte uses this observation to support his broader thesis that [[positivism|Positivism]], rather than Protestantism, represents the true heir to the social functions of [[catholicism|Catholicism]] — particularly the separation of spiritual and temporal power.
