**Louis XIV** (1638–1715), known as the Sun King, reigned as King of France from 1643 to 1715. His reign is analyzed by Auguste Comte in the *Discourse on the Positivist Spirit* as the historical anchor of Royalist reactionary tendencies in modern France.

## Biography

Louis XIV ascended the throne as a child and ruled for seventy-two years, the longest recorded reign of any European monarch. He centralized power in the monarchy, built the Palace of Versailles, and pursued an ambitious foreign policy that made France the dominant European power. His reign represented the apogee of absolute monarchy in France.

## Connection to Positivism

In his analysis of the political meaning of Republicanism, Comte identifies the reign of Louis XIV as the historical origin of Royalist reaction in France. He writes that the principle of Republicanism "precludes the possibility of recurrence to Royalism, which, ever since the second half of the reign of Louis XIV, has been the rallying point of all reactionist tendencies." (003-028)

> "It precludes the possibility of recurrence to Royalism, which, ever since the second half of the reign of Louis XIV, has been the rallying point of all reactionist tendencies." (003-028)

Comte views the French Republic as a direct step toward the final regeneration of society, one that breaks definitively with the Royalist tradition anchored in the reign of Louis XIV. By consecrating all human forces to the general service of the community, republicanism recognizes the doctrine of subordinating Politics to Morals, a principle that originated in the Middle Ages under Catholicism but could only be realized under a sounder philosophy.
