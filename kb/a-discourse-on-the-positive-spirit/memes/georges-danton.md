**Georges Danton** (1759–1794) was a leading figure in the early stages of the French Revolution, a founder of the First French Republic, and the first president of the Committee of Public Safety.

## Connection to Positivism

Comte references Danton in his critique of the Ideologists who, despite being successors of the revolutionary tradition, proved incapable of directing what Comte calls the second phase of the Revolution:

> The most advanced of their number, unworthy successors of the school of Voltaire and Danton, have shown themselves thoroughly incapable either morally or intellectually of directing the second phase of the Revolution, which they are hardly able to distinguish from the first phase. (003-032)

Comte positions Danton within the tradition of [[voltaire|Voltaire]] — representing the destructive or critical phase of the Revolution — and distinguishes it from the constructive organic phase that [[positivism|Positivism]] alone is equipped to lead.
