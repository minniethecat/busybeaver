**Jean Racine** (1639–1699) was a French dramatist, one of the great tragedians of the French classical stage alongside [[pierre-corneille|Pierre Corneille]].

## Connection to Positivism

[[auguste-comte|Auguste Comte]], in his *Discourse on the Positivist Spirit*, mentions Racine only once — in the context of discussing how critical fashion can distort literary judgment. He observes that:

> Preference is given for so long a time to Racine over Corneille. (006-029)

Comte's passing reference to Racine serves to illustrate the variability of literary taste, while his own sympathies lie with [[pierre-corneille|Corneille]], whom he praises extensively throughout the work for his historical dramas and moral grandeur.

## Major Works

- *Andromaque* (1667)
- *Phèdre* (1677)
- *Athalie* (1691)
