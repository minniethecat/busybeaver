## Overview

**Religion of Humanity** is Auguste Comte's positivist religion — the culminating synthesis of his philosophical system in which [[humanity|Humanity]] itself ("the Great Being") becomes the object of worship. Comte envisioned it not as a negation of earlier religions but as their fulfillment on a scientific basis, providing the intellectual, moral, and esthetic unity that traditional theology could no longer sustain.

The religion of Humanity coordinates all human faculties — Reason, Imagination, and Feeling — under the supremacy of Affection (007-060). Its motto, "Live for others" (*vivre pour autrui*), expresses the ethical principle of [[altruism]] as the foundation of both individual conduct and social organization.

## Key Principles

Comte's religion of Humanity rests on three interrelated pillars:

**The Great Being** — Humanity conceived not as a mere aggregate of individuals but as a continuous collective existence spanning past, present, and future generations. To "become incorporate with Humanity" is the highest aim of life (007-060).

**The supremacy of Feeling** — In contrast to the intellectual emphasis of traditional philosophy, the religion of Humanity places Affection above Reason and Activity. Science and Art are both instrumental to the service of love (007-071).

**The unity of theory and practice** — The religion synthesizes Science (which provides principles), Poetry (which provides esthetic forms of worship), and Morality (which directs conduct). All three are regenerated by their subordination to the common purpose of serving Humanity (007-099).

## The Role of Science and Poetry

A distinctive feature of Comte's religion is its reconciliation of scientific reason with poetic imagination. Science, when regenerated by its social mission, reveals the laws governing Humanity's existence and provides the "solid basis for consolation" that earlier religions supplied through illusion (007-052). Poetry is called to a higher function than it ever achieved under polytheism: to idealize social life and give beauty to the worship of Humanity (007-038).

The "priesthood of Humanity" would consist of those who, mastering the encyclopedic scale of the sciences, dedicate their intellectual powers to the moral guidance of society — a role in which women and the working classes would actively cooperate (007-071).

## Historical Succession

Comte explicitly positioned the religion of Humanity as the successor to Catholicism. He saw the medieval Church's attempt to create a complete spiritual synthesis as a genuine but premature achievement — one that the Positive religion would now complete on sounder foundations (007-057). This historical consciousness is central to Comte's self-understanding: he did not seek to destroy religion but to fulfill its functions through the only means compatible with the maturity of human reason.

## The Transformation of Politics

The practical dimension of the religion of Humanity is the transformation of politics into morality. Comte envisioned a reorganization in which political action would become "a practical worship of Humanity" — Morality being the worship of the affections, Science and Poetry the worship of the intellect (007-099). This required, however, a prior "reconstruction of opinions and habits of life" extending over at least one generation.
