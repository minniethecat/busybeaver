## Overview

**Scientific law** is a central concept in [[auguste-comte|Auguste Comte]]'s positivist philosophy. For Comte, the goal of all science is the discovery of invariable natural laws—regularities of succession and similitude that allow phenomena to be predicted and, within limits, modified. The positivist conception of law represents a decisive break with theological and metaphysical thinking, which sought absolute causes and essences rather than observable relations.

## Key Principles

Comte distinguished between two types of scientific laws: laws of coexistence (static laws, describing the order of a system at a given moment) and laws of succession (dynamic laws, describing how phenomena change over time). The most fundamental laws are the most general—those of mathematics and astronomy—while the laws of sociology are the most complex and specific.

The discovery of scientific laws proceeds from observation, not from a priori reasoning. Comte insisted that the positive method "rests on scientific laws of human nature or of society" (005-064) rather than on abstract speculation. The authority of scientific laws derives not from any [[absolute-knowledge|absolute truth]] claim but from their verified correspondence with observed phenomena and their practical utility in prediction and action.

## Historical Context

The concept of invariable natural law was central to Comte's argument against both [[dogmatism|theological dogmatism]] and metaphysical absolutism. He argued that "the search for [[absolute-knowledge|absolute truth]]" was futile, and that "the [[order|Order]] of Nature is all that is absolutely indispensable" (002-068) for the establishment of positive knowledge.

In the theological stage, phenomena were explained by the will of supernatural agents; in the [[metaphysics|metaphysical stage]], by abstract entities and forces. Only in the positive stage are phenomena explained by laws—observed regularities that require no further justification. This transformation in the concept of law was, for Comte, the essence of intellectual progress.
