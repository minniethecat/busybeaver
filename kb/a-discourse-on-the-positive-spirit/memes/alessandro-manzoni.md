**Alessandro Manzoni** (1785–1873) was an Italian novelist and poet, best known for his historical novel *The Betrothed* (*I Promessi Sposi*).

## Connection to Positivism

[[auguste-comte|Auguste Comte]], in his *Discourse on the Positivist Spirit*, mentions Manzoni in the context of discussing how the growing historical consciousness of the nineteenth century has influenced literature:

> In our own times where the historical spirit has become stronger, novelists, like Scott and Manzoni, have made similar though less perfect attempts to idealize later periods. (006-064)

Comte groups Manzoni with [[walter-scott|Walter Scott]] as practitioners of the historical novel — a genre that he views as a spontaneous, though imperfect, precursor to the Positivist ideal of art that systematically idealizes all phases of human history for educational purposes.

## Major Works

- *I Promessi Sposi* (The Betrothed, 1827, revised 1840)
- *Inni Sacri* (Sacred Hymns, 1815)
- *Il Cinque Maggio* (1821)
