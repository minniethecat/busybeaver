**Armand Carrel** (1800–1836) was a French journalist, historian, and republican political writer. He was a co-founder of the influential newspaper *Le National* and a prominent voice of the liberal opposition during the July Monarchy.

## Connection to Positivism

Comte singles out Carrel as an exceptional figure among the Ideologists — a man of genuine republican conviction whose death was a significant loss to the cause:

> Formerly I had taken as their type a man of far superior merit, the noble Armand Carrel, whose death was such a grievous loss to the republican cause. But he was a complete exception to the general rule. (003-032)

Comte contrasts Carrel's authentic republicanism with the unworthy successors of the revolutionary tradition. Carrel's death in a duel in 1836 cut short a career that Comte regarded as exemplary.

## Major Works

- *Histoire de la Contre-Révolution en Angleterre* (1827)
- *Résumé de l'histoire de l'Écosse* (1825)
