# Altruism

**Altruism** (from French *altruisme*, coined by [[Auguste Comte]]) is the principle and moral practice of concern for the happiness and well-being of others. In Comte's positivist system, altruism — "living for others" (*vivre pour autrui*) — is the central ethical principle.

Comte placed altruism above egoism in his moral philosophy, arguing that the subordination of self-love to social love is the great ethical problem that [[positivism|Positivism]] solves through its emphasis on the preponderance of the heart over the intellect.

## Etymology and Origin

The term "altruism" (*altruisme*) was coined by [[Auguste Comte]] as a neologism, along with other key terms such as "[[sociology]]" and "dynamics," to express concepts central to his positivist system. (P01-005) Comte derived the word from the Latin *alter* ("other"), embedding his ethical doctrine of "living for others" directly into the language of modern philosophy.
