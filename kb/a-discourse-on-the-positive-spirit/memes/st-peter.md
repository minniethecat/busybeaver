**St Peter** (c. 1–64 CE) was one of the Twelve Apostles of Jesus Christ and is traditionally regarded as the first Bishop of Rome (Pope).

## Connection to Positivism

[[auguste-comte|Auguste Comte]], in his *Discourse on the Positivist Spirit*, uses St Peter as an example in his discussion of the distinction between the concrete order of rank and the abstract order of merit. Comte notes that St [[paul|Paul]], though morally and intellectually superior, nevertheless recognized St Peter's official ecclesiastical authority:

> A still more striking example was furnished by St Paul in recognizing the official superiority of St Peter, of whose moral and mental inferiority to himself he must have been well aware. (007-070)

Comte invokes this example to argue that society can respect the abstract order of personal merit without subverting the concrete order of institutional rank — a principle he extends to the organization of Positivist society.
