**Charles Dunoyer** (1786–1862) was a French economist and political writer associated with the liberal Economic school of early nineteenth-century France.

## Connection to Positivism

[[auguste-comte|Auguste Comte]], in his *Discourse on the Positivist Spirit*, acknowledges the Economic school's critique of communism, singling out Dunoyer as their most advanced representative. Comte writes:

> Wide as the divergence between Positivism and the Economic schools is, Positivists adopt substantially the strictures which they have passed upon Communism; especially those of Dunoyer, their most advanced writer. (004-056)

Comte's reference to Dunoyer occurs within a broader discussion of the dangers of communist theories that suppress individuality. While Comte distances [[positivism|Positivism]] from the Economic school's overall framework, he endorses their critique of utopian schemes that sacrifice liberty to equality.
