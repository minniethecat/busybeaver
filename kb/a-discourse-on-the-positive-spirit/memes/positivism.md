# Positivism

**Positivism** is a philosophical system developed by [[Auguste Comte]] in the early 19th century, holding that all genuine knowledge is derived from sensory experience interpreted through reason and logic, with [[scientific-spirit|scientific method]] as the only valid approach to understanding reality.

Comte argued that human thought progresses through three stages: the theological, the metaphysical, and the positive (scientific). In the positive stage, explanations of phenomena are based on observable facts and their invariable laws, rather than supernatural causes or abstract philosophical principles.

Positivism became the foundation for the modern scientific worldview and gave rise to the discipline of [[sociology]].

## The Law of Three Stages

The cornerstone of Comte's Positivism is the law of the three stages, which holds that each branch of human knowledge passes successively through three theoretical conditions. (002-046)

These stages are the [[theological-philosophy|theological]], the [[metaphysical-philosophy|metaphysical]], and the positive or scientific. This dynamical law governs both individual intellectual development and the historical evolution of civilization.
