**Sir Walter Scott** (1771–1832) was a Scottish historical novelist, poet, and playwright, widely regarded as the founder of the historical novel genre.

## Connection to Positivism

[[auguste-comte|Auguste Comte]], in his *Discourse on the Positivist Spirit*, briefly references Scott in the context of discussing the growing historical consciousness of modern literature. Comte observes that:

> In our own times where the historical spirit has become stronger, novelists, like Scott and Manzoni, have made similar though less perfect attempts to idealize later periods. (006-064)

Comte groups Scott with the Italian novelist [[alessandro-manzoni|Alessandro Manzoni]] as practitioners of the historical novel — a genre he views as a spontaneous precursor to the Positivist ideal of art that idealizes all phases of human history. However, he considers these attempts "less perfect" than what [[positivism|Positivism]] will enable.
