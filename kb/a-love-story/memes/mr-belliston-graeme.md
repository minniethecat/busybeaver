# Mr. Belliston Græme

## Overview

Mr. Belliston Græme is "much talked of in his" day (002-047), a figure whose social prominence makes him part of the novel's extended network. He "possessed a villa" near Bath (013-029) and hosts dinners attended by the novel's principal characters, including Sir Henry, who "dined that day with Mr. Belliston Græme" (039-023). His role is that of the wealthy, well-connected gentleman whose hospitality facilitates social interaction.
