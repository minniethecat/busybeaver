# Section 20

[020-001] "Perfectly," said his brother, "I remember I used to think him mad, because he always looked so melancholy, and used to send us word in the morning when he contemplated a visit; in order that all cats might be kept out of his way."

[020-002] "The very man! I am glad you know so much about him, for it is on this subject I was going to speak. I cannot tell you where he picked up the idea originally--but I believe in a dream--that a cat would occasion his death.

[020-003] "Well! he was at Ascot one year, when a gipsy woman came up to him on the course--told him his fortune--and, to his utter astonishment, warned him to beware of the wild cat.

[020-004] "From that moment, I understand his habits changed. From being a tolerably cheerful companion, he became a wretched hypochondriac; all his energies being directed to the avoiding a contact with any of the feline race.

[020-005] "Featherstone, two or three years ago, embarked in one of the mining speculations--lost great part of his fortune--and found it necessary to try and retrieve his affairs, by a second voyage to [[india|India]].

[020-006] "I heard nothing more of him, till just before leaving [[england|England]], when my old school-fellow, Lockhart, who went as a cadet to the East, called on me--reminded me of our old whimsical friend--and related his tragic death.

[020-007] "Lockhart says that one day he and some mutual friends, persuaded Featherstone to accompany them into the interior of the country, to enjoy the diversion of a boar hunt.

[020-008] "They had had good sport, and were returning homewards, when they suddenly came on a party of natives, headed by the Rajah.

[020-009] "They were mounted on elephants, and surrounding a jungle, in which, as some sepoys had reported, lay a tiger.

[020-010] "You know Lockhart's manner--animated and enthusiastic--making one see the scene he is describing.

[020-011] "I will try and clothe the rest of the story in his own words, although I can hardly hope it will make the same impression on you, that its recital did on me.

[020-012] "'Well, Sir! we all said we would see the sport--all but Featherstone--who said something about coming on.

[020-013] "'We were engaged to dine with Sir John M----, who was in that part of the world, on some six-and-eightpenny mission about indigo.

[020-014] "'The beaters went in, firing and shouting--intending to make him break towards the hunting party.

[020-015] "'We all drew up on one side, to be in view, but out of the way; Featherstone was next me. He suddenly grasped my arm, and pointed to the jungle, his teeth chattering--his face ashy pale. I turned and saw the tiger!--a splendid beast--certainly!

[020-016] "'He seemed not to notice us, and stalked on with an innocent yep! yep!

[020-017] like a sick hound's, more than anything else.

[020-018] "'Suddenly his eye caught us, and flashed fire. At the first view, he crouched to the earth, then came on us, bounding like a tost foot-ball.

[020-019] More magnificent leaps I never beheld! We were struck dumb--but fired--and turned our horses' heads!--all but Featherstone.

[020-020] "'I shall remember the tones of his voice to my dying hour.

[020-021] "'"The cat! Lockhart! the cat!"

[020-022] "'I don't know whether his horse refused the spur--or whether the rider's nerve was gone: but neither appeared to make an effort, till the animal was close on them.

[020-023] "'The horse gave one plunge--and had hardly recovered his feet, when down went horse and rider.

[020-024] "'Featherstone gave a piercing scream! Some of the sepoys were by this time up--and fired.

[020-025] "'The tiger trailed off--the blood spouting down his striped side.

[020-026] "'We came up--it was all over!

[020-027] "'The first stroke of that terrific paw had laid the unfortunate man's scull bare. On his shoulder, were the marks of the animal's teeth.

[020-028] "'The horse was still writhing in agony. One of my pistols relieved him.

[020-029] "'We bore Featherstone to the nearest cantonment, and buried him there.'" "How terrible!" said [[acme|Acmé]], as she gave a slight shudder. "Englishmen are generally more sceptical on these points than we are; and disbelieve supernatural appearances, which we are accustomed to think are not unfrequent. I could tell you many stories, which, in my native island, were believed by our enemies the Turks, as well as by ourselves: but if you would like it, I will tell you a circumstance that occurred to myself, the reality of which I dare not doubt.

[020-030] "You have often, Giorgio! heard me revert with pain, to the horrible scene which took place, on the recapture of our little isle by the infidel Turks; when my family were massacred, and only poor Acmé left to tell their tale."

[020-031] Here the young bride put her handkerchief to her face, and wept bitterly. George put his arm round her and soothed her. She continued her narrative.

[020-032] "You know my escape, and how I was sent to a kinsman, who had promised to have me sent to my kind friends in [[malta|Malta]]. He was a Corfuote, and it was in Corfu I remained for a long--a very long time--and there first met my dear friend, Zöe Scalvo-Forressi. I was then very young. We lived in the Campagna--about four miles from each other.

[020-033] "We had both our Greek ponies, and used often to pass the evenings together; and at length knew our road so well, that often it was night before we parted.

[020-034] "One night, we had been singing together at her house, and it was later than usual when I cantered home.

[020-035] "[[About]] four months had elapsed previous to my landing in Corfu, and I had been eight months there; although at the time, I paid little attention to these circumstances.

[020-036] "My road lay through an olive grove. I had arrived in its centre, where a small knoll stretched away on my right; on whose summit, was a white Greek monastery, backed by some dark cypress trees.

[020-037] "The moon was shining brightly--dancing on the silver side of the olive trees--and illuminating the green sward.

[020-038] "This was smooth and verdant.

[020-039] "My spirits were more than usually buoyant, when suddenly my pony stopped.

[020-040] "I could not conceive the reason.

[020-041] "I looked before me. Immediately in front of me, was the shattered trunk of an old olive tree--it had been blasted by lightning--and sitting quietly at its foot--I saw my own mother, Giorgio! as clearly as I see you now. I could not be mistaken. She wore the same embroidered vest and Albanian shawl, as when I had last seen her.

[020-042] "She conversed with me calmly for many minutes, and--which surprised me much at the time--I felt no dread, and asked her and answered many questions.

[020-043] "She told me I should die early, in a foreign land; and many--many more things, which I dare not repeat; for I cannot contemplate the possibility of their being true.

[020-044] "At the time, I told you I felt composed: without any sense of alarm or surprise. For many days afterwards, however, I never left my bed of sickness.

[020-045] "I told my kinsman all the circumstances, and he discovered beyond a doubt, that it was on that very day, the twelve-month previous, that my poor mother had been murdered."

[020-046] [[sir-henry-delme|Sir Henry]] and George tried to smile at Acmé's story, and account for what she had seen;--but her manner was so impressive, and her ingenious reasonings--delivered in the most earnest tone--seemed to confute so entirely all their speculations, that they were at length content to deem it "wondrous strange."

[020-047] In the best and wisest of us, there is such a tendency to believe in a mysterious link, connecting the living and the departed; that a story of this nature, in exciting our feelings, serves to paralyse our reasoning faculties, and leaves us half converts, to the doctrines that we faintly combat.

[020-048] They looked forth again on the scene. The mountains of Calabria were frowning on them. The village was far behind--and not a straggling light marked its situation.

[020-049] Numberless stars were reflected on the glassy water, whose serenity was no longer ruffled by wing of sea bird, which long ere now had returned to its "wave girded nest."

[020-050] Our party and the watch were the only lingerers on deck.

[020-051] George wrapped Acmé's silk cloak around her, and then carefully assisted her in her descent to the cabin.

[020-055] The land breeze continued to freshen, and the first dawn of morning saw our party on deck, scanning with near view, the opposite coasts of [[sicily|Sicily]] and [[italy|Italy]], as their vessel glided through the Faro of Messina.

[020-056] Some pilot boats,--how unlike those which greet the homeward-bound voyager, as he first hails Britain's chalky cliffs--crowded around the vessel, offering their services to guide it through the strait.

[020-057] Avarice--one incentive to language--had endowed these Sicilian mariners with a competent knowledge of English, which they dealt out vociferously.

[020-058] As the Captain made his selection, the rejected candidates failed not to use that familiar English salâm; half the gusto of which is lost, when used by foreign lip.

[020-059] On the Calabrian coast, the sea-port town of Reggio wore an unusual air of bustle and animation.

[020-060] It was a festa day there; and groups of peasants, in many-coloured costumes, paced up and down the mole; emitting that joyous hum, which is the never-failing concomitant of a happy crowd. Passing through the Faro, the vessel's course lay by the northern coast of Sicily.

[020-061] The current and wind were alike favourable, as it swept on by Melazzo and Lascari.

[020-062] Etna, towering over the lesser mountains, became once more visible; its summit buried in the clouds of heaven.

[020-063] On the right, a luminous crimson ring revealed Stromboli, whose fitful volcano was more than usually active.

[020-064] The following day our party arrived at Palermo. So pleasurable had been their voyage, that it was with a feeling akin to regret, that they heard the rumbling chains of the anchor, rush through the hawse-hole, as their vessel took her station in the bay.

[020-065] After going through those wearisome forms, which a foreign sea-port exacts; and which appear purposely intended, to temper the rapture of the sea-worn voyager, as he congratulates himself on once more treading terra firma; our party found themselves the inmates of the English hotel; and spent the remainder of the day in engaging a cicerone, and in discussing plans for the morrow.

[020-066] The morrow came--sunny and cloudless--and the cicerone bowed to the ground, as he opened the door of the commodious fiacre.

[020-067] "Where shall I drive to, Sir?"

[020-068] "What were our plans, George?" said Sir Henry.

[020-069] "I think," replied George, "that we only formed one plan to change it for another. Let the cicerone decide for us."

[020-070] _He,_ nothing loath, accepted the charge; and taking his station on the box of the carriage, directed the driver.

[020-071] The carriage first stopped before a large stone building. The bell was rung--a veteran porter presented himself--and our party entered the court yard.

[020-072] "What place is this?" said Delmé.

[020-073] "This," rejoined his guide, with the true cicerone fluency, "is the famous lunatic asylum, instituted by the illustrious Baron Pisani. This, gentlemen, is the Baron!"

[020-074] Here a benevolent-looking little man with a large nose, took off his hat.

[020-075] "So much approved of was his beneficent design, that our noble King, and our paternal Government, have not only adopted it; but have graciously permitted the Baron, to continue to preside over that institution, which he so happily commenced, and which he so refulgently adorns."

[020-076] During this announcement, the Baron's face flushed with a simple, but honest pride.

[020-077] These praises did not to him appear exaggerated; for his intentions had been of the purest, and in this institution was his whole soul wrapt up.

[020-078] Acmé became somewhat pale, as she heard where they were, and looked nervously at George; who could not forbear smiling, as he begged they would be under no apprehensions.

[020-079] "Yes! gentlemen," said the Baron, "circumstances in early life made me regard mental disease as the most fearful of all. I observed its victims struggling between reason and insanity; goaded on by the ignorance of empirics, and the harsh treatment of those about them, until light fled the tortured brain, and madness directed its every impulse. You, gentlemen, are English travellers, I perceive! In _your_ happy land, where generosity and wealth go hand in hand, there are, I doubt not, many humane institutions, where those, who--bowed down by misfortunes, or preyed on by disease--have lost the power to take care of themselves, may find a home, where they may be anxiously tended, and carefully provided for.
