# Sacrifice

## Overview

Sacrifice is the moral currency of *A Love Story* — the means by which characters prove the depth of their commitments. The novel treats sacrifice not as tragedy alone but as the highest proof of love, honor, and duty. Sir Henry Delmé embodies this principle: he "never allowed himself to be deterred by any obstacle, when pursuing an object he deemed essential that was worth any sacrifice" (001-028).

## Manifestations in Text

### Sacrifice as Moral Proof

The novel uses sacrifice as an index of character. When Sir Henry declares "there are no sacrifices I would not personally make" (012-010) for those he loves, the statement functions not as hyperbole but as a genuine moral commitment the narrative takes seriously. The willingness to sacrifice — comfort, fortune, even life — distinguishes the novel's heroes from its lesser figures.

### The Cost of Sacrifice

Sacrifice is not glorified without acknowledgment of its cost. The novel notes that one character "had sacrificed all his little fortune" (013-010) for what he believed right, and elsewhere records how "many of his comrades" were compelled "to sacrifice their lives and prospects" (035-033) in military service. These losses are presented as real and significant — sacrifice means genuine deprivation.

### Sacrifice and Love

The novel's most profound sacrifices occur in the context of love. Love that demands nothing and gives everything — this is the standard against which the novel measures its characters. The willingness to sacrifice for the beloved transforms romantic passion into something approaching the sacred.

## Key Passages

> Sir Henry never allowed himself to be deterred by any obstacle, when pursuing an object he deemed essential that was worth any sacrifice. (001-028)

The novel's definition of its protagonist's character: sacrifice as the measure of commitment.

> There are no sacrifices I would not personally make. (012-010)

The unconditional language of sacrificial love — no limit on what can be given.

> He had sacrificed all his little fortune. (013-010)

The material cost of sacrifice acknowledged plainly — sacrifice involves real loss.
