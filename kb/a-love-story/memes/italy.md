# Italy

## Overview

Italy is the primary foreign setting of *A Love Story*, the country through which Sir Henry Delmé travels and where the novel's emotional geography is mapped onto physical landscape. Characters arrive via "the northern parts of Italy" (002-048), travel through its cities and countryside, and are transformed by its beauty and history. The country serves as both romantic backdrop and moral testing ground.

## Geographic Context

Italy is rendered through its cities (Rome, Naples, Florence, Venice), its coasts ("opposite coasts of Sicily and Italy," 020-055), and its classical associations. Sir Henry's "travels in Italy had not extended so far south" (022-007) at certain points, mapping his journey across the peninsula.

## Role in Narrative

Italy is where the novel's emotional intensity peaks. Sir Henry "passed some time in Italy" (014-011), and his experiences there shape his character. The Italian landscape — its moonlit walks, its classical ruins, its musical culture — provides the sensory environment for the novel's romantic and melancholic passages.

## Associated Characters

- **Sir Henry Delmé** — His Italian journey structures much of the narrative
- **George Delmé** — Travels through Italy, his health a constant concern
