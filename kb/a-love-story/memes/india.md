# India

## Overview

India appears in *A Love Story* as a distant imperial destination — the far edge of British colonial reach. Characters have "been in the civil service in India" (019-050), and voyages to India structure lives: a "second voyage to India" (020-005) marks a character's career trajectory. The "East Indian" (026-075) and "Indian friends" (027-030) represent the colonial connections that extend the novel's social world beyond Europe.

## Geographic Context

India is never directly depicted; it exists as a reference point — the place characters have been, are going to, or have returned from. Its distance from England and Malta is part of its meaning: India represents the ultimate remove from home.

## Role in Narrative

India functions as a source of wealth, experience, and exotic reference. The "Indian shell" (026-008) and "Indian idol" (023-002) serve as metonymic markers of the colonial world. Characters associated with India bring the empire's reach into the novel's domestic spaces.
