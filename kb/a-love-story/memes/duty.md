# Duty

## Overview

Duty is one of the organizing moral principles of *A Love Story*. The concept appears across both volumes, functioning as both a practical obligation — military duty, social duty — and a deeper ethical imperative. The narrator consistently frames characters' choices in terms of duty fulfilled or neglected, making it a key lens through which the novel evaluates moral worth.

## Manifestations in Text

### Military and Professional Duty

The novel's military setting gives duty a concrete, institutional form. References to "orderly duty" and "guard" (013-002) establish the disciplinary framework within which characters operate. Military duty "must frequently chase sleep" (005-030), suggesting its relentless, demanding nature — it permits no respite and requires constant vigilance.

### Duty as Moral Imperative

Beyond professional obligation, duty functions as a personal moral compass. The narrator notes that those who are "bed-ridden" would "deem it a duty to witness the solemn" rites (009-021), illustrating how duty extends beyond one's station to encompass moral witness. When Delmé faces temptation, "duty whisper otherwise" (031-053), personifying duty as the voice of conscience that counters romantic impulses.

### Duty After Loss

In the novel's later sections, duty takes on an elegiac quality. After personal tragedy, Delmé recognizes "there was a duty yet to be performed" (039-052), and he returns from "the path of manly duty" (036-045) only with effort. This framing suggests that duty provides structure and purpose even — perhaps especially — in the aftermath of devastating loss.

## Key Passages

> In pursuing the line of duty he had marked out—in acting up to his own strict code of honour—he was not to be deterred. (001-026)

The novel's foundational statement on duty: self-defined, rigorously followed, inseparable from honor.

> It is my duty to avow it; and I frankly confess. (009-004)

Demonstrates duty functioning as an imperative to truth-telling, even when the truth is uncomfortable.

> Romantic country! did not duty whisper otherwise, how would he linger! (031-053)

The tension between romantic impulse and duty rendered as an internal dialogue — duty as the cautionary voice.

> But there was a duty yet to be performed. (039-052)

Even after catastrophic loss, duty persists — providing moral continuity when all else is broken.
