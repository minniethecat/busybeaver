# Emily Delmé

## Overview

Emily Delmé is the youngest of the three Delmé siblings — "the youngest child" whose "mother died in giving her birth" (001-024). The novel presents her as a figure of grace and beauty: "Emily Delmé was not an ordinary being" (001-031). Her character arc moves from protected sister to married woman, and her relationship with Clarendon provides one of the novel's romantic subplots. Her wedding, toward the novel's end, is one of the events that structures the narrative's conclusion.

## Biography

Emily is introduced as the only daughter of the Delmé family, raised by her brothers after their mother's death. The novel describes her as being "more beautiful than striking" (001-033), a distinction that emphasizes grace over mere appearance. She shares her brothers' moral sensibility, "the interest she took in" Sir Henry reflecting the family's emotional closeness (001-034).

Her relationship with Clarendon develops across the novel, with their marriage initially "deferred" (006-024) — a delay that creates narrative tension. Sir Henry "regarded his sister's marriage as an event, at which it might be permitted to feel satisfaction" (038-019), his carefully qualified approval reflecting the novel's concern with propriety and family honor.

Emily's letter to Sir Henry (036-026) reveals her inner life and the depth of her connection to her brother. The novel's later sections return to her, culminating in her wedding — one of the events (alongside deaths and other marriages) that mark the novel's resolution (038-047).

## Relationships

- **Sir Henry Delmé** — Her elder brother and guardian, whose approval of her marriage carries great weight.
- **George Delmé** — Her brother, whose illness and recovery she witnesses.
- **Clarendon** — Her suitor and eventual husband.

## Key Scenes

- **Introduction** (001-031–001-034): The novel's portrait of Emily — her character, beauty, and place in the family.
- **Letter to Sir Henry** (036-026): A moment of intimate communication that reveals her inner life.
- **Marriage** (038-019, 038-047): Her wedding as one of the novel's concluding events.
