# Colonel Vavasour

## Overview

Colonel Vavasour is introduced as a military figure of some distinction — "informed Delmé, that Colonel Vavasour was on the terrace" (011-014). He represents the professional military world that forms the backdrop of the Delmé brothers' lives. The novel presents him as a "strict disciplinarian" on parade (011-025), embodying the values of order and duty that the novel treats with respect.

## Biography

Colonel Vavasour is "employed in reading a German" work when first encountered (011-017), suggesting an intellectual dimension to his military character. The narrative pauses to give a fuller portrait: he "was not only considered in the" highest regard by his superiors (011-021), and his presence commands attention.

His role in the novel is primarily as a figure of military authority — the world of "orderly duty" and "guard" (013-002) that structures the lives of soldiers like Sir Henry and George. He appears alongside other military figures (012-025), forming part of the novel's social tapestry.

## Key Scenes

- **On the terrace** (011-014–011-017): Introduced reading, a moment that reveals character through activity.
- **On parade** (011-025): The strict disciplinarian in his element.
