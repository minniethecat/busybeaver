# Sir Henry Delmé

## Overview

Sir Henry Delmé is the protagonist of *A Love Story*, introduced as the head of an "old Norman" family (001-023) and the central figure around whom all other characters orbit. He is "cast in a finer mould" than ordinary men (001-026), distinguished by his adherence to a "strict code of honour" and his willingness to sacrifice anything for those he loves. The novel follows his journey across Europe and the Mediterranean, tracing how love, grief, and duty shape a man of principle.

## Biography

Sir Henry Delmé inherited the baronetcy and, through prudent management, ensured that "the family estates became wholly unencumbered" (001-026). At the period of the novel, the family "consisted but of three members" (001-024): Sir Henry, his brother George, and his sister Emily. He had "arrived at middle age" (004-007) without having married, his emotional life shaped by the people he protects rather than romantic attachment — until he meets Acmé.

Sir Henry's character is defined by his moral seriousness. He "considered it incumbent on him" to act with integrity in all matters (001-028), and "never allowed himself to be deterred by any obstacle, when pursuing an object he deemed essential that was worth any sacrifice" (001-028). This principle guides his actions throughout the novel, creating both his greatest strengths and his deepest conflicts.

His relationship with Acmé, the young Greek woman, transforms him. The narrative traces his journey from dutiful guardian to passionate lover, and ultimately — through grief — to a man deepened by loss. His travels through Italy and Malta provide the geographical backdrop for his emotional journey.

## Relationships

- **George Delmé** — His younger brother, with whom he shares a bond of "fraternal affection" (007-023). Henry's concern for George's welfare drives significant plot developments.
- **Emily Delmé** — His sister, the youngest of the three Delmé siblings. "The interest she took in" Sir Henry reflects the mutual devotion of the family (001-034).
- **Acmé** — The Greek woman who becomes the object of his deepest love. Their relationship is the novel's emotional core.
- **Clarendon** — A friend whose character contrasts with Sir Henry's — "Clarendon was very dissimilar" (002-007).

## Key Scenes

- **Introduction** (001-024–001-028): The novel establishes Sir Henry's character — his family, his code of honor, his sense of duty.
- **Conversation with Mr. Selby** (003-028–003-029): A moment of social grace that reveals his character through dialogue.
- **In Malta** (004-007): Arriving "at middle age," Sir Henry's life is poised for transformation.
- **With Acmé**: His devotion to Acmé drives the novel's most powerful emotional passages.
