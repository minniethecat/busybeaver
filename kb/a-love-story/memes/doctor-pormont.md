# Doctor Pormont

## Overview

Doctor Pormont is introduced through the mediation of a waiter, who passes word of "a Doctor Pormont, who attended the noble Consul" (023-037). He is "in many respects a singular" character (023-040), one of the novel's more distinctive minor figures. His professional role as physician brings him into contact with the Delmé family, and the anecdotes told about him — including one concerning "the blunted feelings of Doctor Pormont" (023-044) — suggest a man whose medical experience has shaped an unusual personality.

## Biography

Doctor Pormont is "introduced" (023-038) into the narrative as a medical professional whose services are sought by Sir Henry. Sir Henry "briefly explained to Doctor Pormont his purpose in sending for him" (023-047), establishing the doctor's role as a consultant in matters of health. The anecdotes attached to his character paint him as someone whose long medical practice has given him a distinctive, perhaps hardened, perspective on human suffering.

## Key Scenes

- **Introduction via waiter** (023-037): A mediated entrance that establishes his professional standing.
- **Consultation with Sir Henry** (023-047): The medical purpose that brings him into the story.
