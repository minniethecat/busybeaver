# Sir Reginald Delmé

## Overview

Sir Reginald Delmé is the founding patriarch of the Delmé family line — "the chief of his house" (037-056). Though he appears only in memory and monument, his presence looms over the novel's later sections. His chantry and tomb provide one of the novel's most atmospheric settings, where Emily Delmé stands "as fair as the fairest" of her ancestors (038-007).

## Biography

Sir Reginald is introduced through genealogy: the father of the Delmé siblings, "a man of strong feeling" (001-024) whose death preceded the novel's action. His chantry becomes a significant location in the novel's second volume, where "the figure of Sir Reginald Delmé" (038-001) lies in stone effigy. The detail that he "had not died in battle" (038-003) is carefully noted — his hands are "joined as in prayer" rather than clasping a sword, marking him as a man of peace despite his martial lineage.

## Key Scenes

- **The chantry** (037-056–038-008): Emily at the tomb of her ancestor, a meditation on lineage and mortality.
