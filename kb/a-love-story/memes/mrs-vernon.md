# Mrs. Vernon

## Overview

Mrs. Vernon is a widow whose social position in Leamington provides an important setting for the novel's later sections. She is introduced discussing "bygone days" with Mrs. Glenallan (005-020), immediately establishing her as part of the novel's network of genteel society. Her "drawing-room at Leamington" (026-077) becomes a significant location, and her "small mansion" (027-044) hosts key interactions between characters.

## Biography

Mrs. Vernon appears first at a social gathering, part of the fabric of English country society. She is later established as the mother of Julia Vernon, and her drawing-room serves as a site where characters converge. She "first used arguments" (027-048) in matters of character guidance, and is described as being "all complacency" (027-053) — a portrait of the capable, socially adept gentlewoman.

## Key Scenes

- **Discussion with Mrs. Glenallan** (005-020): Among the wider social circle.
- **Drawing-room at Leamington** (026-077): Her domestic space as social stage.
- **Guidance** (027-048): Using argument and persuasion in matters of importance.
