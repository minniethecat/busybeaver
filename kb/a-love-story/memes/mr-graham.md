# Mr. Graham

## Overview

Mr. Graham serves as a trusted friend and confidant within the Delmé circle. When Sir Henry inquires, "Have you seen Mr. Graham?" (012-005), the question marks Graham as someone whose knowledge and presence matter. He is privy to George Delmé's illness: "Mr. Graham has told you of his long illness" (012-008), positioning him as a conduit of crucial information within the novel's social network.

## Biography

Graham appears primarily in the context of George Delmé's illness, where his role as informant and advisor becomes central. He is among those who join the Delmé brothers in the evening (012-025), part of the intimate circle that surrounds the family during crisis. His authority is such that even Sir Henry defers to his judgment: "even if Mr. Graham had not forbidden the interview" (013-022).

## Key Scenes

- **Inquiry about George** (012-005–012-009): Graham as the bearer of medical knowledge.
- **Evening gathering** (012-025): Part of the supportive circle around the Delmé brothers.
