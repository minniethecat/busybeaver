# Mrs. Glenallan

## Overview

Mrs. Glenallan is introduced as "Miss Delmé's worthy aunt" (002-004), a woman whose history with Sir Henry Delmé adds a layer of romantic complexity to the novel. She is "the Mrs. Glenallan of our story" (002-048) — identified as Sir Henry's "love of former days" (003-007). Her presence in the narrative connects the novel's present social world to its emotional past, embodying the theme that love — even past love — continues to shape present relationships.

## Biography

Mrs. Glenallan is one of the beautiful Vignoles sisters who became "the Mrs. Glenallan of our story" (002-048). Her history with Sir Henry is alluded to rather than narrated directly — she is his "love of former days" (003-007), a phrase that suggests both intimacy and distance. She remains part of the Delmé family's social circle, "discussing bygone days" with Mrs. Vernon (005-020), and accompanies Emily in family decisions (006-024).

## Key Scenes

- **Introduction** (002-004): As Miss Delmé's aunt and part of the family circle.
- **Walking with Sir Henry** (003-007): "Stoically" arm in arm with the man who once loved her.
- **Discussion with Mrs. Vernon** (005-020): Among the matrons, discussing the past.
