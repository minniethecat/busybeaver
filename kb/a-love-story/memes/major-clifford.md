# Major Clifford

## Overview

Major Clifford is introduced at a dinner gathering: "Major Clifford, will you sit on the other side?" (013-004). He "merits more especial notice" (013-010) from the narrator, who pauses to give him fuller treatment than most minor characters. Described as "an universal favourite" (013-011), Clifford represents the ideal military companion — sociable, respected, and unassuming.

## Biography

Major Clifford's position owes something to patronage: "The former owed his appointment" to connections (013-008). Yet the narrator insists on his genuine merit, noting that despite being "an universal favourite," there was something distinctive about his character (013-011). His departure from the table occasions a shift in conversation (013-021), marking his social significance by the gap his absence creates.

## Key Scenes

- **Dinner seating** (013-004): Introduced into the social circle.
- **Narrator's notice** (013-010–013-011): The pause for fuller characterization.
- **Departure** (013-021): His exit marks a transition in the evening's dynamic.
