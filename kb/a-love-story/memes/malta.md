# Malta

## Overview

Malta is the primary Mediterranean setting of *A Love Story*, where "a light infantry regiment" is stationed (001-024) and where the Delmé family's emotional dynamics unfold. It is the place where Sir Henry is summoned by his brother's letter — "begged his brother to come to Malta" (004-035) — and where Acmé first appears in the narrative. The island serves as a crossroads of English military life and Mediterranean romance.

## Geographic Context

Malta's position between Sicily and North Africa places it at the center of British Mediterranean power. The novel describes its harbor and barracks, and the "Malta post mark" (004-028) on letters signals the island's role as a communications hub connecting England to its overseas empire.

## Role in Narrative

Malta is where George falls ill, where Acmé presides at the breakfast table (008-020), and where the central relationships of the novel take shape. It is described as both a place of duty — the regiment's station — and a place of transformative emotional experience.

## Associated Characters

- **Sir Henry Delmé** — Summoned to Malta by his brother's need
- **George Delmé** — Stationed in Malta during his illness
- **Acmé** — Lives in Malta, where her relationship with the Delmés develops
