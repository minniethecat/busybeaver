# George Delmé

## Overview

George Delmé is Sir Henry's younger brother, a soldier whose personal history — a tale of passionate love and devastating loss — provides one of the novel's most powerful secondary narratives. He is introduced as one of the three members of the Delmé family (001-024) and serves as both confidant and foil to his elder brother. His illness and the concern it generates in Sir Henry form a major plot thread, while his confession of past passion offers the novel's most sustained meditation on love's aftermath.

## Biography

George is introduced alongside his siblings as part of the compact Delmé family unit. His "bosom companion had been an" intimate friend (008-021) whose deathbed confession shapes George's emotional life. The surgeon's narrative reveals that George "had met Acmé" (008-022) before Sir Henry, creating a complex triangulation of feeling and obligation.

When George falls ill, his condition becomes a central concern. Sir Henry's protective impulse drives him to action, and the "wan features of George Delmé" (012-018) become a visual index of suffering. His recovery, watched over by Acmé and Sir Henry, provides some of the novel's most tender scenes.

George's confession of his own passionate history is a set-piece within the novel. He describes "vainly struggling against a passion" he believed could not be returned (010-001), and his tale becomes a mirror in which Sir Henry sees his own emotional situation reflected.

## Relationships

- **Sir Henry Delmé** — His elder brother, whose concern for George's welfare is a driving force. Their bond exemplifies "fraternal affection" (007-023).
- **Emily Delmé** — His sister, the youngest Delmé.
- **Acmé** — Friend; George met her before Henry, adding complexity to the family dynamics (008-022).

## Key Scenes

- **The surgeon's narrative** (008-021–008-024): George's history revealed through another's testimony.
- **Illness** (012-013, 012-018): George's suffering and the family's response.
- **Confession of passion** (010-001–010-008): His tale of love and loss, told to Sir Henry.
