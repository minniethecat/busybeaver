## Character Overview

The Ricketts are a family who occupy the floor beneath the Durhams in their New York City tenement building. They are never individualized — no first names or personal details are given — and function in the narrative as part of the oppressive crowdedness of city life that drives the Durham family's decision to move to the country. Along with the Daggetts (upstairs), the Ricketts represent the noise, proximity, and lack of privacy endemic to tenement living.

## Role in the Story

The Ricketts appear exclusively in the novel's early chapters, as Robert Durham catalogs the miseries of tenement life that motivate the family's escape to the countryside. Their "tramping overhead" and "children crying downstairs" (013-020) are cited by Robert as specific instances of the sensory assault of urban poverty. The Ricketts, like the Daggetts, serve as foils — they are the life the Durhams are determined to leave behind.

Robert frames the move explicitly in terms of escaping families like the Ricketts: he asks his wife whether the country could be "any worse neighbors than the Daggetts and the Ricketts" (026-003), and in the final chapter, reflects with satisfaction that "the Daggetts and the Ricketts and all their kind are not so very far away" — but far enough that his family now thrives (044-007).

## Key Passages

- **First mention — the tenement hierarchy**: Robert notes that the Durhams live "with the Daggetts above and the Ricketts on the floor beneath" (001-024).
- **The escape plan**: Robert tells his wife they will "leave the Daggetts and the Ricketts, and go to the country" (003-007).
- **Noise complaint**: Robert cites "Ricketts tramping overhead and the Ricketts children crying downstairs" as emblematic of tenement misery (013-020).
- **Rhetorical comparison**: Robert asks whether country neighbors could be worse than "the Daggetts and the Ricketts" (026-003).
- **Final reflection**: In the novel's closing chapter, Robert notes that the Daggetts, Ricketts, "and all their kind" no longer define his family's daily life (044-007).
