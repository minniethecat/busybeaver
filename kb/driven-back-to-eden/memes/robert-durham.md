# Robert Durham

## Character Overview

Robert Durham is the unnamed first-person narrator of *Driven Back to Eden* — though his full name appears only three times in the text, he is known throughout the novel simply as "I." A middle-aged bookkeeper in New York City, he is defined by his sense of paternal responsibility: looking around at "the little group for whom I was responsible," he concludes that city life is failing his family and resolves on the radical solution of moving to the country to "get our living out of the soil" (002-019). He is honest, methodical, self-deprecating ("I am not one of those smart men who can push their way" (002-021)), and deeply committed to his family's welfare.

## Role in the Story

Durham serves as the novel's central consciousness — every event, character, and reflection is filtered through his first-person narration. His arc is one of growing competence: from the anxious city father who "didn't know much besides bookkeeping" (002-021) to the confident farmer who, by year's end, can declare "We Can Make a Living in Eden" (044-001). He approaches country life systematically, studying agricultural manuals through the winter and haunting Washington Market to learn from experienced growers (005-005). He is also the novel's moral voice, framing the family's journey in terms of providence — "we are in God's hands, and He will take care of us" (037-003) — and character formation. His narrative voice is distinctive: warm, occasionally humorous ("our modern Eden will cost a great many backaches" (030-026)), and marked by the conventional pieties of 19th-century domestic fiction.

## Key Passages

- First self-description as "commander in chief of the little band" (001-024) — establishes paternal authority and narrative voice
- "I propose that we go to the country and get our living out of the soil" (002-019) — the novel's thesis, spoken by Durham to his wife
- "I am not one of those smart men who can push their way" (002-021) — self-characterization as honest but unexceptional
- "I haunted Washington Market in the gray dawn" (005-005) — the methodical approach to agricultural knowledge
- "We are in God's hands, and He will take care of us" (037-003) — faith in crisis during the thunderstorm
