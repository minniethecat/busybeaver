## Character Overview

Dr. Lyman is the preacher in the nearest village to Maizeville, mentioned only twice in the novel. He is noted for having "the faculty of keeping young people awake" during his sermons — a practical quality that the Durham family values in their search for a church home.

## Role in the Story

Dr. Lyman appears only as a name in the Durhams' search for community and spiritual life in the country. When the family resolves "to find a church home" on the first mild Sunday of the season, Robert Durham has heard that "Dr. Lyman... had the faculty of keeping young people awake" (026-002) — a recommendation that decides their choice. He reappears at the novel's Thanksgiving, when "in the morning we all heard Dr. Lyman preach, and came home with the feeling that we and the country at large were prosperous" (043-003).

## Key Passages

- **Choosing a church**: Robert Durham decides on Dr. Lyman's church because he has "the faculty of keeping young people awake" — a practical, unpretentious reason for choosing a preacher (026-002).
- **Thanksgiving sermon**: The family hears Dr. Lyman preach on Thanksgiving morning, returning home with a sense of prosperity and gratitude (043-003).

## Relationships

Dr. Lyman is the spiritual leader for the Durham family's church attendance. His congregation includes **[[robert-durham]]**, **[[mrs-durham]]**, and their children, who attend his services on the first mild Sunday and on Thanksgiving Day.
