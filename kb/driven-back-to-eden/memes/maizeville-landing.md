# Maizeville Landing

## Location Overview

Maizeville Landing is the Hudson River steamboat stop where the Durham family first arrives in the country. It is a small rural community consisting of a dock, a few buildings, and access roads leading to the surrounding farms. The landing represents the threshold between urban and rural worlds: the steamboat connects it to New York City, while the roads leading inland connect it to the Durham farm, the Jones property, and the broader agricultural community.

## Role in the Story

The landing appears at key transitional moments. In chapter 10, it is the family's first contact with the country — "Scenes on the Wharf" depicts the bustling activity of produce being loaded and unloaded. John Jones meets the narrator here with his box sleigh. In chapter 13, the family's dramatic arrival — "half-frozen and badly frightened" (013-013) — takes place at the landing, from which they are transported to the farmhouse. Later in the novel, the narrator brings produce to the landing for shipment to Mr. Bogart, making it the family's commercial link to the city market.

## Key Passages

- The wharf scenes in chapter 10 establish the landing as a place of commerce and transition
