## Character Overview

Mrs. Jamison is the widow of old Mr. Jamison, the previous owner of the farm that the Durham family purchases and names "Eden." After her husband's death in January before the novel's events, she has gone to live with a daughter, never appearing directly in the story. Her presence is felt in the old-fashioned domestic traces she left behind in the farmhouse.

## Role in the Story

Mrs. Jamison is introduced through Mr. Jones's narration when he first shows Robert Durham the farm property. Jones explains that the "old Mr. and Mrs. Jamison lived here till lately" but the "old man died" the previous January, and "his wife has gone to live with a daughter" (007-013). The Jamisons had "clung to their old-fashioned ways," cooking over an open fire with a swinging crane (015-024). This detail — which the Durhams preserve for its picturesque quality — is one of the few tangible traces of Mrs. Jamison's decades of domestic life in the farmhouse. Her departure to a daughter's home after her husband's death underscores the generational transition the Durhams represent, as the farm passes from one family to another.

## Key Passages

- **The Jamisons' history**: Mr. Jones tells Robert Durham that "Old Mr. and Mrs. Jamison lived here till lately," that the old man died in January, and his wife "has gone to live with a daughter" (007-013).
- **Old-fashioned domestic life**: "Mr. and Mrs. Jamison had clung to their old-fashioned ways, and had done their cooking over the open fire, using the swinging crane" — a detail the Durhams preserve for the "picture it made" (015-024).

## Relationships

Mrs. Jamison was married to **[[mr-jamison]]**, the deceased previous owner of the Durham farm. She is the mother of at least one daughter, with whom she now lives. Her old-fashioned domestic habits left their mark on the farmhouse that **[[robert-durham]]** and **[[mrs-durham]]** inherit when they move from the city.
