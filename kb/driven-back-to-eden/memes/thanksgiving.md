# Thanksgiving

## Event Overview

Thanksgiving occupies chapter 43, the novel's penultimate chapter. After a year of labor, the Durham family gathers for a feast that celebrates both material abundance and spiritual gratitude. The narrator recalls his mother's teaching: "it was God's plan to give us a chance, and help us make the most of it" (043-005). The holiday brings the family's year full circle — from the frozen March arrival to the abundant November table.

## Narrative Significance

Thanksgiving is the novel's emotional and thematic climax. It brings together all the novel's threads: family unity (all gather at the table), economic success (the food is the product of their own labor), health (the children are "brown as little Indians"), faith (gratitude to God), and community (the Jones family likely joins them). The narrator's reflection on his mother's teaching frames the entire country experiment as a response to providential opportunity — making the most of the "chance" they were given.

## Key Passages

- "My good old mother taught me that it was God's plan to give us a chance, and help us make the most of it" (043-005) — the novel's moral summation
- The Thanksgiving feast as culmination of the agricultural year
