## Overview

The meadow is a substantial tract of grassland on the Durham farm, sloping down from the lane toward the wood lot and the banks of a stream. It serves dual purposes — grazing for the family's cow and ox, and hay production for winter feed. Much of the meadow is low-lying, making spring access difficult until the ground dries, and it contains thickets along its border that must be managed.

## Description in the Text

Robert first glimpses the meadow's potential during his imagination of farm life: John Jones's fat ox "suggested grassy meadows, plowing, juicy steaks" (011-024). The meadow is a landscape of promise rather than immediate utility in the early chapters. When the family arrives, they must cross "the long spongy lane and the meadow" to reach the farm proper (018-005), an experience that underscores the land's raw, unimproved state.

The meadow is described as a "large meadow" on "the southern and eastern slope" (027-008) with grass "running out" — thinning toward the edges. Its lower portion is bordered by a stream and wood lot, accessible by "the lane and the steeply sloping meadow" (026-002). In early summer, parts of the meadow are "full of thickets" that require clearing (028-002). By mid-summer, the grass is tall enough for haying: Mr. Bagley is hired to cut it, and Robert records driving to "a distant part of the upland meadow" to check on the mowing progress (034-042).

## Narrative Significance

The meadow represents the farm's pastoral dimension — a landscape of grass, water, and woodland edge that contrasts with the cultivated order of the garden and orchard. It is the setting for some of the novel's most evocative descriptions of the natural world and serves as the source of winter hay, linking summer labor to winter survival.

The hiring of Mr. Bagley to mow the meadow marks an economic milestone: the Durhams are now employing labor rather than doing everything themselves. The meadow is productive enough to require hired help, signaling the farm's transition from subsistence to small-scale commercial agriculture.
