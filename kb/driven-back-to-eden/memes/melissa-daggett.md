# Melissa Daggett

## Character Overview

Melissa Daggett is a child from the tenement family that lived above the Durhams in New York. Her family, the Daggetts, along with the Ricketts below, represent the anonymous proximity of city life — neighbors who share walls but not community.

## Role in the Story

The Daggett household is part of the narrator's indictment of city living conditions. The children pouring down from the flats above and below are presented as an undifferentiated threat — "a torrent of children pouring down from the flats above and below" (001-009). Melissa appears briefly as a specific figure among this crowd, and the narrator later recognizes "a rural type of Melissa Daggett" (032-002) in a country girl, suggesting the universality of certain character types across urban and rural settings.

## Key Passages

- Appears in the opening scene of children flooding the tenement stairs (001-009)
- "A rural type of Melissa Daggett" recognized in the country (032-002)
