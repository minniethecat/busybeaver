# The Farmhouse

The farmhouse is the novel's symbolic heart — the "citadel of home" (024-009) from which the Durham family's entire country enterprise radiates. It stands on the property Robert Durham purchases from John Jones and undergoes transformation from a neglected old cottage into a warm, productive domestic center.

## Physical Description

The farmhouse is an old country cottage, originally part of the Jones property. Its defining feature is "the wide, old-fashioned fireplace, with its crackling blaze" (007-011) — the image that, on Robert Durham's first scouting visit, melts his city skepticism and convinces him this is the place. The house has a main room centered on the hearth, bedrooms above, and a basement that becomes the site of the family's winter industries. Surrounding the house are the garden, orchard, barn, and outbuildings that constitute the farm's productive infrastructure.

## Symbolic Transformation

The farmhouse undergoes a symbolic transformation paralleling the family's own. When the Durhams first arrive — during a storm, at night — the house is cold, dark, and forbidding. John Jones has lit a fire, but the space is raw and unfamiliar. Within hours, the family has "knelt around our hearth, and made it an altar to God" (013-032), sanctifying the domestic space in a single ritual act. By spring, the narrator can declare: "If all were well in the citadel of home, the chances of the outdoor campaign were greatly improved" (024-009). The farmhouse has become a strategic base — still domestic, but now operational.

## Key Passages

- **"When I entered the main room of the cottage, and saw the wide, old-fashioned fireplace, with its crackling blaze, I thawed so rapidly that John Jones chuckled"** (007-011) — the first glimpse that decides everything
- **"We knelt around our hearth, and made it an altar to God"** (013-032) — the hearth sanctified as the family's spiritual center
- **"If all were well in the citadel of home, the chances of the outdoor campaign were greatly improved"** (024-009) — the farmhouse as military headquarters for the family's agricultural enterprise
