# St. Nicholas Magazine

## Organization Overview

*St. Nicholas Magazine* was an American children's periodical founded in 1873 by Roswell Smith and edited by Mary Mapes Dodge. It was the most prestigious children's magazine of its era, publishing work by Louisa May Alcott, Mark Twain, Rudyard Kipling, Frances Hodgson Burnett, and Edward Payson Roe. The magazine was known for its high literary standards, its avoidance of moralizing in favor of engaging storytelling, and its integration of educational content with entertainment.

## Role in the Book's Context

*Driven Back to Eden* was serialized in *St. Nicholas* before its book publication by Dodd, Mead and Company in 1885. This serialization context explains several features of the novel: its address to "the little people who are following this simple history" (005-005), its integration of agricultural instruction with narrative, and its wholesome, family-centered moral framework. The magazine's educational mission aligns with the novel's project of teaching young readers about country life, commerce, and character through an engaging first-person story.

## Key Facts

- Founded: 1873
- Editor: Mary Mapes Dodge
- Published: Monthly
- Notable contributors: Alcott, Twain, Kipling, Burnett
- Ceased publication: 1943
