# Jones Farm

## Location Overview

The Jones farm is the neighboring property to the Durham farm, owned and operated by John Jones and his family. Unlike the run-down Jamison place, the Jones farm is a working, productive property — a model of what the Durham farm might become with years of skilled labor.

## Role in the Story

The Jones farm appears primarily as a point of reference and comparison. John Jones's competence as a farmer — his knowledge of soil, seasons, and markets — derives from his lifelong work on this land. When he visits the Durham farm to advise on raspberry pruning or onion planting, he is bringing expertise earned on his own property. The farm also serves as a social destination; the families visit back and forth, and Junior Jones is Merton's friend.

## Key Passages

- John Jones's agricultural advice draws on his farm experience (020-003, 022-014)
- The maxim "as the farmer's land grows rich he grows poor" (019-019) — rural wisdom from Jones's experience
