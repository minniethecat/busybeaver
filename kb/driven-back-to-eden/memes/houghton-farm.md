## Overview

Houghton Farm is a model agricultural property located a few miles from the Durham family's farm near Maizeville. It is the subject of an entire chapter ("A Visit to Houghton Farm," Chapter 40) and serves as an aspirational benchmark — an example of what enlightened investment and scientific farming can achieve. Robert Durham takes his family to visit it as a kind of educational pilgrimage before they leave the region.

## Description in the Text

Houghton Farm is depicted as a model of improved agriculture, distinguished from ordinary farms by the capital and expertise of its proprietor. Unlike the neglected Jamison farm the Durhams inherit, Houghton Farm benefits from an owner "who are willing to spend thousands in improving the land" (040-011). The farm represents the application of modern methods and substantial investment to the land — a contrast to both the depleted old-fashioned farming of Jamison and the Durhams' novice efforts.

Robert Durham explicitly frames his visit to Houghton Farm as a learning expedition: "before I do so, I wish to visit Houghton Farm, and shall take you all with me" (039-022). The chapter title itself — "A Visit to Houghton Farm" (040-001) — signals the farm's importance as a destination and a template.

## Narrative Significance

Houghton Farm functions in the novel as a symbol of what farming can become with adequate resources and knowledge. It represents the upper bound of agricultural possibility — the direction the Durhams aspire to move in, even if they cannot match its scale. Robert notes that only one other property in the region — his own — is "after the plan of Houghton Farm" (040-018), positioning the model farm as the standard against which all local agricultural efforts are measured.

The visit occurs late in the narrative (Chapter 40), after the Durhams have gained a season of farming experience. By this point they can appreciate what they see — the visit is a culmination of their agricultural education, not an introduction to it.

## Key Scenes

- **Planning the visit**: Robert announces his intention to visit Houghton Farm before leaving the area, framing it as a family expedition (039-022).
- **The arrival**: The chapter opens with "A Visit to Houghton Farm," devoting the entire chapter to the experience (040-001).
- **The model of investment**: The narrator contrasts Houghton Farm's proprietor — willing to spend thousands on land improvement — with ordinary farmers (040-011).
- **After the plan of Houghton Farm**: Robert notes that his own farm is "the only one possible after the plan of Houghton Farm" in the region (040-018).
