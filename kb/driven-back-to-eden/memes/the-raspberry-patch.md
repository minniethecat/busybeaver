## Overview

The raspberry patch is a sizeable planting of blackcap raspberry bushes located behind the barn on the Durham farm. Like the orchard, it is a legacy from old Mr. Jamison's tenure — "Jamison, you see, grew these raspberries" (020-017) — and it becomes the Durham family's first meaningful cash crop, yielding fruit that Merton and Junior sell before the main harvest season begins.

## Description in the Text

John Jones introduces Robert to the raspberry patch during the initial farm tour, leading him to "the big raspberry patch back of the barn" (020-023). The bushes are described as having "grown into a very snarl" from neglect (020-003), requiring significant pruning and training — a task Robert and the children undertake as one of their first agricultural projects.

The patch is substantial enough to require systematic cultivation: Robert records working "twice through all the raspberry rows, giving us two full days of hard labor" (030-017). The Highland Hardy variety is mentioned specifically when Junior brings in "a red raspberry from the Highland Hardy variety" as the season's first fruit (032-012). The patch's location behind the barn, near the edge of cultivated ground, also makes it a site of minor adventure — a raccoon is spotted "passing through the raspberry field" (032-020), leading to an impromptu chase.

## Narrative Significance

The raspberry patch is the family's introduction to agricultural commerce. Unlike the vegetable garden (which feeds the family) or the orchard (which yields later in the season), the raspberries are the first crop ready for market. Robert notes that they are "gathered and sold before the raspberry season began" elsewhere (027-003), giving the Durhams an early-market advantage and their first taste of farm income.

The patch also serves as a practical school for the children. Winnie and Bobsey help with the picking, and the work of clearing the tangled rows teaches the family the rhythm of farm labor — the satisfaction of transforming neglect into productive order.

## Key Scenes

- **Jones introduces the patch**: "Now come to the big raspberry patch back of the barn" — John Jones shows Robert the overgrown but valuable planting (020-023).
- **Jamison's legacy**: Jones explains that Jamison planted the raspberries, framing the patch as inherited agricultural capital (020-017).
- **Systematic cultivation**: Robert records the hard labor of working "twice through all the raspberry rows" over two full days (030-017).
- **First fruit**: Junior rushes in with the season's first red raspberry from the Highland Hardy variety (032-012).
- **Raccoon chase**: A raccoon spotted crossing the raspberry field leads to an impromptu pursuit through the thick bushes (032-020, 032-027).
