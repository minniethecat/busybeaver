# Alca

Alca is the principal urban setting of *Penguin Island* — the city where Saint Maël first lands, where the Dragon of Alca terrorizes the population, and where the political intrigues of modern Penguinia play out. It is a thinly disguised Paris.

## In the Narrative

Alca is established early as the center of Penguin civilization. Saint Maël "built a wooden church and a monastery" near Alca (013-003), and the city grows into the political, religious, and cultural capital of Penguinia. The Dragon of Alca episode (Books II-III) gives the city its founding myth, and the miracle cult of Saint Orberosia is centralized in the metropolitan church (026-013).

In the modern books, Alca is the setting for the Pyrot Affair trials, the Columban trial, Madame Clarence's drawing-room, and the revolutionary agitation of Books VI-VII. It is the stage on which all of France's political satire is performed.

## Satirical Treatment

Alca-as-Paris allows France to satirize French urban society without naming it directly. The city's institutions — courts, churches, ministries, drawing-rooms — are all recognizable as Third Republic Paris. The name "Alca" (suggesting "alcove" or enclosure) hints at the insular, self-regarding nature of metropolitan society.
