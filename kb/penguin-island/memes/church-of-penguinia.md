# Church of Penguinia

The Church of Penguinia is the institutional embodiment of religion in the novel — founded by Saint Maël, enriched by the miracle cult of Saint Orberosia, and thoroughly politicized by figures like Father Agaric. France portrays it as a corporate entity whose spiritual mission is inseparable from its economic and political interests.

## In the Narrative

The Church originates with Saint Maël's mission and the construction of "a wooden church and a monastery" (013-003). Over the centuries, it grows into a vast institutional apparatus controlling land, collecting offerings, and managing pilgrimage economies. The miracle cult of Orberosia becomes its most lucrative enterprise.

In the modern era, the Church aligns with monarchist forces against the Republic. The Reverend Fathers Agaric and Cornemuse conspire to restore the monarchy, treating the Church as a political instrument. The Church's wealth — including the lead from its own roofs — is used to fund political operations (028-006).

## Satirical Treatment

France's satire emphasizes the gap between the Church's spiritual claims and its material operations. The "record of miracles" kept by the monks (026-014) is presented as bookkeeping, and the transfer of relics to the metropolitan church (026-013) as a corporate relocation. The Church emerges as Penguinia's oldest and most successful corporation.

## Key Passages

> "Old Maël built a wooden church and a monastery" (013-003)

> "The chapter kept a record of the miracles wrought by the blessed Orberosia" (026-014)
