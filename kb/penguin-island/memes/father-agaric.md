# Father Agaric

Father Agaric is the political operative of the Penguin Church — a tall, thin, dark monk who "kept a school and assisted with his services" in a suburb of Alca (038-003). He becomes the architect of a monarchist conspiracy, embodying the anticlerical fear of Church interference in republican politics.

## In the Narrative

Agaric is introduced walking "in deep thought" through the monastery (038-004), and his political instincts are immediately evident. He recruits Prince Crucho to the monarchist cause, arguing that religion and monarchy are natural allies. He warns Crucho "not to compromise religion in political enterprises" (038-030) — even as he does exactly that.

Agaric's conspiracy unravels in Book VI, where his schemes are exposed as both dangerous and farcical. His political maneuvering is ultimately futile, but the damage to the Republic's trust in the Church is lasting.

## Satirical Treatment

France models Agaric on the Jesuit stereotype of French anticlerical literature — the cunning, politically ambitious cleric who uses religion as cover for reactionary politics. His warning not to "compromise religion" while actively compromising it is characteristic irony.

## Historical Context

The Jesuit order was a particular target of French Republican anticlericalism. The order had been expelled from France multiple times, and "Jesuitical" was a common epithet for political cunning disguised as religious piety. Agaric embodies this stereotype.
