[081-001] Of not troubling ourselves about outward things

[081-002] “My Son, in many things it behoveth thee to be ignorant, and to esteem

[081-003] thyself as one dead upon the earth, and as one to whom the whole world

[081-004] is crucified. Many things also thou must pass by with deaf ear, and

[081-005] must rather think upon those things which belong unto thy peace. It is

[081-006] more profitable to turn away thine eyes from those things that

[081-007] displease, and to leave each man to his own opinion, than to give

[081-008] thyself to discourses of strife. If thou stand well with God and hast

[081-009] His judgment in thy mind, thou wilt verily easily bear to be as one

[081-010] conquered.”

[081-011] 2. O [[jesus-christ|Lord]], to what have we come? Behold a temporal loss is mourned

[081-012] over; for a trifling gain we labour and hurry; and spiritual loss

[081-013] passeth away into forgetfulness, and we rarely recover it. That which

[081-014] profiteth little or nothing is looked after, and that which is

[081-015] altogether necessary is negligently passed by; because the whole man

[081-016] slideth away to outward things, and unless he quickly recovereth

[081-017] himself in outward things he willingly lieth down.
