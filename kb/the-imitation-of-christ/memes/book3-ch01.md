[038-001] Of the inward voice of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] to the faithful soul

[038-002] I will hearken what the Lord God shall say within me.(1) Blessed is the

[038-003] soul which heareth the Lord speaking within it, and receiveth the word

[038-004] of [[comfort|consolation]] from His mouth. Blessed are the ears which receive the

[038-005] echoes of the soft whisper of God, and turn not aside to the

[038-006] whisperings of this world. Blessed truly are the ears which listen not

[038-007] to the voice that soundeth without, but to that which teacheth truth

[038-008] inwardly. Blessed are the eyes which are closed to things without, but

[038-009] are fixed upon things within. Blessed are they who search inward things

[038-010] and study to prepare themselves more and more by daily exercises for

[038-011] the receiving of heavenly mysteries. Blessed are they who long to have

[038-012] leisure for God, and free themselves from every hindrance of [[world|the world]].

[038-013] Think on these things, O my soul, and shut the doors of thy carnal

[038-014] desires, so mayest thou hear what the Lord God will say within thee.

[038-015] 2. These things saith thy Beloved, “I am thy salvation, I am thy peace

[038-016] and thy life. Keep thee unto Me, and thou shalt find peace.” Put away

[038-017] thee all transitory things, seek those things that are eternal. For

[038-018] what are all temporal things but deceits, and what shall all created

[038-019] things help thee if thou be forsaken by the Creator? Therefore put all

[038-020] things else away, and give thyself to the Creator, to be well pleasing

[038-021] and faithful to Him, that thou mayest be able to attain true

[038-022] blessedness.

[038-023] (1) Psalm lxxxv. 8.
