[041-001] How we must walk in truth and humility before God

[041-002] “My Son! walk before Me in truth, and in the simplicity of thy heart

[041-003] seek Me continually. He who walketh before Me in the truth shall be

[041-004] safe from evil assaults, and the truth shall deliver him from the wiles

[041-005] and slanders of the wicked. If the truth shall make thee free, thou

[041-006] shalt be free indeed, and shalt not care for the vain words of men.”

[041-007] 2. [[jesus-christ|Lord]], it is true as Thou sayest; let it, I pray Thee, be so with me;

[041-008] let Thy truth teach me, let it keep me and preserve me safe unto the

[041-009] end. Let it free me from all evil and inordinate affection, and I will

[041-010] walk before Thee in great freedom of heart.

[041-011] 3. “I will teach thee,” saith the [[truth|Truth]], “the things which are right

[041-012] and pleasing before Me. Think upon thy [[sin|sins]] with great displeasure and

[041-013] sorrow, and never think thyself anything because of thy good works.

[041-014] Verily thou art a sinner, liable to many passions, yea, tied and bound

[041-015] with them. Of thyself thou always tendest unto nothing, thou wilt

[041-016] quickly fall, quickly be conquered, quickly disturbed, quickly undone.

[041-017] Thou hast nought whereof to glory, but many reasons why thou shouldest

[041-018] reckon thyself vile, for thou art far weaker than thou art able to

[041-019] comprehend.

[041-020] 4. “Let, therefore, nothing which thou doest seem to thee great; let

[041-021] nothing be grand, nothing of value or beauty, nothing worthy of honour,

[041-022] nothing lofty, nothing praiseworthy or desirable, save what is eternal.

[041-023] Let the eternal truth please thee above all things, let thine own great

[041-024] vileness displease thee continually. Fear, denounce, flee nothing so

[041-025] much as thine own faults and sins, which ought to be more displeasing

[041-026] to thee than any loss whatsoever of goods. There are some who walk not

[041-027] sincerely before me, but being led by curiosity and pride, they desire

[041-028] to know my secret things and to understand the deep things of God,

[041-029] whilst they neglect themselves and their salvation. These often fall

[041-030] into great temptations and sins because of their pride and curiosity,

[041-031] for I am against them.

[041-032] 5. “Fear thou the judgments of God, fear greatly the wrath of the

[041-033] Almighty. Shrink from debating upon the works of the Most High, but

[041-034] search narrowly thine own iniquities into what great sins thou hast

[041-035] fallen, and how many good things thou hast neglected. There are some

[041-036] who carry their devotion only in books, some in pictures, some in

[041-037] outward signs and figures; some have Me in their mouths, but little in

[041-038] their hearts. Others there are who, being enlightened in their

[041-039] understanding and purged in their affections, continually long after

[041-040] eternal things, hear of earthly things with unwillingness, obey the

[041-041] necessities of nature with sorrow. And these understand what [[holy-spirit|the Spirit]]

[041-042] of truth speaketh in them; for He teacheth them to despise earthly

[041-043] things and to love heavenly; to neglect [[world|the world]] and to desire heaven

[041-044] all the day and night.”
