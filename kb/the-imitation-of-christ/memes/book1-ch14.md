[014-001] On avoiding [[judgment|rash judgment]]

[014-002] Look well unto thyself, and beware that thou judge not the doings of

[014-003] others. In judging others a man laboureth in vain; he often erreth, and

[014-004] easily falleth into sin; but in judging and examining himself he always

[014-005] laboureth to good purpose. According as a matter toucheth our fancy, so

[014-006] oftentimes do we judge of it; for easily do we fail of true judgment

[014-007] because of our own personal feeling. If God were always the sole object

[014-008] of our desire, we should the less easily be troubled by the erring

[014-009] judgment of our fancy.

[014-010] 2. But often some secret thought lurking within us, or even some

[014-011] outward circumstance, turneth us aside. Many are secretly seeking their

[014-012] own ends in what they do, yet know it not. They seem to live in good

[014-013] [[peace|peace of mind]] so long as things go well with them, and according to

[014-014] their desires, but if their desires be frustrated and broken,

[014-015] immediately they are shaken and displeased. Diversity of feelings and

[014-016] opinions very often brings about dissensions between friends, between

[014-017] countrymen, between religious and godly men.

[014-018] 3. Established custom is not easily relinquished, and no man is very

[014-019] easily led to see with the eyes of another. If thou rest more upon thy

[014-020] own reason or experience than upon the power of [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]], thy light

[014-021] shall come slowly and hardly; for God willeth us to be perfectly

[014-022] subject unto Himself, and all our reason to be exalted by abundant love

[014-023] towards Him.
