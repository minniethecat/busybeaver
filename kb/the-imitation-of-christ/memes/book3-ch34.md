[071-001] That to him who loveth God is sweet above all things and in all things

[071-002] Behold, God is mine, and all things are mine! What will I more, and

[071-003] what more happy thing can I desire? O delightsome and sweet world! that

[071-004] is, to him that loveth the Word, not [[world|the world]], neither the things that

[071-005] are in the world.(1) My God, my all! To him that understandeth, that

[071-006] word sufficeth, and to repeat it often is pleasing to him that loveth

[071-007] it. When Thou art present all things are pleasant; when Thou art

[071-008] absent, all things are wearisome. Thou makest the heart to be at rest,

[071-009] givest it deep peace and festal joy. Thou makest it to think rightly in

[071-010] every matter, and in every matter to give Thee praise; neither can

[071-011] anything please long without Thee but if it would be pleasant and of

[071-012] sweet savour, Thy grace must be there, and it is Thy wisdom which must

[071-013] give unto it a sweet savour.

[071-014] 2. To him who tasteth Thee, what can be distasteful? And to him who

[071-015] tasteth Thee not, what is there which can make him joyous? But the

[071-016] worldly wise, and they who enjoy the flesh, these fail in Thy wisdom;

[071-017] for in the wisdom of the world is found utter vanity, and to be

[071-018] carnally minded is death. But they who follow after Thee through

[071-019] contempt of worldly things, and [[mortification|mortification of the flesh]], are found

[071-020] to be truly wise because they are carried from vanity to verity, from

[071-021] the flesh to the spirit. They taste that the [[jesus-christ|Lord]] is good, and

[071-022] whatsoever good they find in [[creation|creatures]], they count it all unto the

[071-023] praise of the Creator. Unlike, yea, very unlike is the enjoyment of the

[071-024] Creator to enjoyment of the Creature, the enjoyment of eternity and of

[071-025] time, of light uncreated and of light reflected.

[071-026] 3. O Light everlasting, surpassing all created lights, dart down Thy

[071-027] ray from on high which shall pierce the inmost depths of my heart. Give

[071-028] purity, joy, clearness, life to my spirit that with all its powers it

[071-029] may cleave unto Thee with rapture passing man’s understanding. Oh when

[071-030] shall that blessed and longed-for time come when Thou shalt satisfy me

[071-031] with Thy presence, and be unto me All in all? So long as this is

[071-032] delayed, my joy shall not be full. Still, ah me! the old man liveth in

[071-033] me: he is not yet all crucified, not yet quite dead; still he lusteth

[071-034] fiercely against the spirit, wageth inward wars, nor suffereth the

[071-035] soul’s kingdom to be in peace.

[071-036] 4. But Thou who rulest the raging of the sea, and stillest the waves

[071-037] thereof when they arise, rise up and help me. Scatter the people that

[071-038] delight in war.(2) Destroy them by Thy power. Show forth, I beseech

[071-039] Thee, Thy might, and let Thy right hand be glorified, for I have no

[071-040] hope, no refuge, save in Thee, O Lord my God.

[071-041] (1) 1 John ii. 15. (2) Psalm lxviii. 30.
