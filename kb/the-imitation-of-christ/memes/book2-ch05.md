[030-001] Of self-esteem

[030-002] We cannot place too little confidence in ourselves, because grace and

[030-003] understanding are often lacking to us. Little light is there within us,

[030-004] and what we have we quickly lose by negligence. Oftentimes we perceive

[030-005] not how great is our inward blindness. We often do ill and excuse it

[030-006] worse. Sometimes we are moved by passion and count it zeal; we blame

[030-007] little faults in others and pass over great faults in ourselves.

[030-008] Quickly enough we feel and reckon up what we bear at the hands of

[030-009] others, but we reflect not how much others are bearing from us. He who

[030-010] would weigh well and rightly his own doings would not be the man to

[030-011] judge severely of another.

[030-012] 2. The spiritually-minded man putteth care of himself before all cares;

[030-013] and he who diligently attendeth to himself easily keepeth silence

[030-014] concerning others. Thou wilt never be spiritually minded and godly

[030-015] unless thou art silent concerning other men’s matters and take full

[030-016] heed to thyself. If thou think wholly upon thyself and upon God, what

[030-017] thou seest out of doors shall move thee little. Where art thou when

[030-018] thou art not present to thyself? and when thou hast overrun all things,

[030-019] what hath it profited thee, thyself being neglected? If thou wouldst

[030-020] have peace and true unity, thou must put aside all other things, and

[030-021] gaze only upon thyself.

[030-022] 3. Then thou shalt make great progress if thou keep thyself free from

[030-023] all temporal care. Thou shalt lamentably fall away if thou set a value

[030-024] upon any worldly thing. Let nothing be great, nothing high, nothing

[030-025] pleasing, nothing acceptable unto thee, save God Himself or the things

[030-026] of God. Reckon as altogether vain whatsoever [[comfort|consolation]] comes to thee

[030-027] from a creature. The soul that loveth God looketh not to anything that

[030-028] is beneath God. God alone is eternal and incomprehensible, filling all

[030-029] things, the solace of the soul, and the true joy of the heart.
