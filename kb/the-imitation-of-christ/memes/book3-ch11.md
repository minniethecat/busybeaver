[048-001] That the desires of the heart are to be examined and governed

[048-002] “My Son, thou hast still many things to learn, which thou hast not well

[048-003] learned yet.”

[048-004] 2. What are they, [[jesus-christ|Lord]]?

[048-005] 3. “To place thy desire altogether in [[obedience|subjection]] to My good pleasure,

[048-006] and not to be a lover of thyself, but an earnest seeker of My will. Thy

[048-007] desires often excite and urge thee forward; but consider with thyself

[048-008] whether thou art not more moved for thine own objects than for My

[048-009] honour. If it is Myself that thou seekest, thou shalt be well content

[048-010] with whatsoever I shall ordain; but if any pursuit of thine own lieth

[048-011] hidden within thee, behold it is this which hindereth and weigheth thee

[048-012] down.

[048-013] 4. “Beware, therefore, lest thou strive too earnestly after some desire

[048-014] which thou hast conceived, without taking counsel of Me; lest haply it

[048-015] repent thee afterwards, and that displease thee which before pleased,

[048-016] and for which thou didst long as for a great good. For not every

[048-017] affection which seemeth good is to be forthwith followed; neither is

[048-018] every opposite affection to be immediately avoided. Sometimes it is

[048-019] expedient to use restraint even in good desires and wishes, lest

[048-020] through importunity thou fall into distraction of mind, lest through

[048-021] want of discipline thou become a stumbling-block to others, or lest by

[048-022] the resistance of others thou be suddenly disturbed and brought to

[048-023] confusion.

[048-024] 5. “Sometimes, indeed, it is needful to use violence, and manfully to

[048-025] strive against the sensual appetite, and not to consider what the flesh

[048-026] may or not will; but rather to strive after this, that it may become

[048-027] subject, however unwillingly, to the spirit. And for so long it ought

[048-028] to be chastised and compelled to undergo slavery, even until it be

[048-029] ready for all things, and learn to be contented with little, to be

[048-030] delighted with things simple, and never to murmur at any

[048-031] inconvenience.”
