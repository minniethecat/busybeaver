[098-001] That the greatness and [[love|charity]] of God is shown to men in the Sacrament

[098-002] The Voice of the Disciple

[098-003] Trusting in Thy goodness and great [[forgiveness|mercy]], O [[jesus-christ|Lord]], I draw near, the sick

[098-004] to the Healer, the hungering and thirsting to the Fountain of life, the

[098-005] poverty-stricken to the King of heaven, the servant to the Lord, the

[098-006] creature to the Creator, the desolate to my own gentle Comforter. But

[098-007] whence is this unto me, that Thou comest unto me? Who am I that Thou

[098-008] shouldest offer me Thyself? How doth a sinner dare to appear before

[098-009] Thee? And how dost thou vouchsafe to come to the sinner? Thou knowest

[098-010] Thy servant, and Thou knowest that he hath in him no good thing for

[098-011] which Thou shouldest grant him this grace. I confess therefore mine own

[098-012] vileness, I acknowledge Thy goodness, I praise Thy tenderness, and I

[098-013] give Thee thanks for Thine exceeding great love. For Thou doest this

[098-014] for Thine own sake, not for my merits, that Thy goodness may be more

[098-015] manifest unto me, Thy charity more abundantly poured out upon me, and

[098-016] Thy humility more perfectly commended unto me. Therefore because this

[098-017] pleaseth Thee and Thou hast commanded that thus it shall be, Thy

[098-018] condescension pleaseth me also; and oh that mine [[sin|iniquity]] hinder it

[098-019] not.

[098-020] 2. O most sweet and tender Jesus, what reverence, what giving of thanks

[098-021] is due to Thee with perpetual praise for the receiving of Thy sacred

[098-022] Body and Blood, the dignity whereof no man is found able to express.

[098-023] But what shall I think upon in this [[communion|Communion]] in approaching my Lord,

[098-024] whom I am not able worthily to honour, and nevertheless whom I long

[098-025] devoutly to receive? What shall be better and more healthful meditation

[098-026] for me, than utter humiliation of myself before Thee, and exaltation of

[098-027] Thine [[goodness|infinite goodness]] towards me? I praise Thee, O my God, and exalt

[098-028] Thee for evermore. I despise myself, and cast myself down before Thee

[098-029] into the deep of my vileness.

[098-030] 3. Behold, Thou art the Saint of saints and I the refuse of sinners;

[098-031] behold, Thou stoopest unto me who am not worthy to look upon Thee;

[098-032] behold, Thou comest unto me, Thou willest to be with me, Thou invitest

[098-033] me to Thy feast. Thou willest to give me the heavenly food and bread of

[098-034] angels to eat; none other, in truth, than Thyself, The living bread,

[098-035] which didst descend from heaven; and givest life to [[world|the world]].(1)

[098-036] 4. Behold, whence this love proceedeth! what manner of condescension

[098-037] shineth forth herein. What great giving of thanks and praise is due

[098-038] unto Thee for these benefits! Oh how salutary and profitable Thy

[098-039] purpose when Thou didst ordain this! How sweet and pleasant the feast

[098-040] when Thou didst give Thyself for food! Oh how admirable is thy working,

[098-041] O Lord, how mighty Thy power, how unspeakable Thy truth! For Thou didst

[098-042] speak the word, and all things were made; and this is done which Thou

[098-043] hast commanded.

[098-044] 5. A thing wonderful, and worthy of faith, and surpassing all the

[098-045] understanding of man, that Thou, O Lord my God, very God and very man,

[098-046] givest Thyself altogether to us in a little bread and wine, and art so

[098-047] our inexhaustible food. Thou, O Lord of all, who hast need of nothing,

[098-048] hast willed to dwell in us through Thy Sacrament. Preserve my heart and

[098-049] my body undefiled, that with a joyful and [[conscience|pure conscience]] I may be able

[098-050] very often to [celebrate, and](2) receive to my perpetual health. Thy

[098-051] mysteries, which Thou hast consecrated and instituted both for Thine

[098-052] own honour, and for a perpetual memorial.

[098-053] 6. Rejoice, O my soul, and give thanks unto God for so great a gift and

[098-054] precious [[comfort|consolation]], left unto thee in this vale of tears. For so oft

[098-055] as thou callest this mystery to mind and receivest the body of Christ,

[098-056] so often dost thou celebrate the work of thy redemption, and art made

[098-057] partaker of all the merits of Christ. For the charity of Christ never

[098-058] groweth less, and the greatness of His propitiation is never exhausted.

[098-059] Therefore, by continual renewal of thy spirit, thou oughtest to dispose

[098-060] thyself hereunto and to weigh the great mystery of salvation with

[098-061] attentive consideration. So great, new, and joyful ought it to appear

[098-062] to thee when thou comest to communion, as if on this self-same day

[098-063] Christ for the first time were descending into [[mary|the Virgin]]’s womb and

[098-064] becoming man, or hanging on the cross, suffering and dying for the

[098-065] salvation of mankind.

[098-066] (1) John vi. 51. (2) The words in brackets are only suitable for a

[098-067] priest.
