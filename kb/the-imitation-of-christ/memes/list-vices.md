# Index of Vices

A central concern of *The Imitation of Christ* is the recognition and
mortification of vice. Kempis diagnoses the disorders of the soul with
psychological acuity — pride, vainglory, concupiscence, and sloth among
them — and prescribes remedies rooted in grace, self-knowledge, and the
example of Christ. This page indexes all wiki entries tagged as vices.

## Scope

The vice pages in this wiki treat both the capital vices of the classical
tradition and the more subtle spiritual disorders that Kempis identifies —
such as inordinate attachment to creatures, curiosity about the lives of
others, and the desire for human praise. Each page examines the vice's
nature, its manifestations, and the counter-virtues by which it is overcome.
