[108-001] That he who is about to Communicate with [[jesus-christ|Christ]] ought to prepare

[108-002] himself with great diligence

[108-003] The Voice of the Beloved

[108-004] I am the Lover of purity, and Giver of sanctity. I seek a pure heart,

[108-005] and there is the place of My rest. Prepare for Me the larger upper room

[108-006] furnished, and I will keep the Passover at thy house with my

[108-007] disciples.(1) If thou wilt that I come unto thee and abide with thee,

[108-008] purge out the old leaven,(2) and cleanse the habitation of thy heart.

[108-009] Shut out the whole world, and all the throng of [[sin|sins]]; sit as a sparrow

[108-010] alone upon the house-top,(3) and think upon thy transgressions with

[108-011] bitterness of thy soul. For everyone that loveth prepareth the best and

[108-012] fairest place for his beloved, because hereby the affection of him that

[108-013] entertaineth his beloved is known.

[108-014] 2. Yet know thou that thou canst not make sufficient preparation out of

[108-015] the merit of any action of thine, even though thou shouldest prepare

[108-016] thyself for a whole year, and hadst nothing else in thy mind. But out

[108-017] of My tenderness and grace alone art thou permitted to draw nigh unto

[108-018] My table; as though a beggar were called to a rich man’s dinner, and

[108-019] had no other recompense to offer him for the benefits done unto him,

[108-020] but to humble himself and to give him thanks. Do therefore as much as

[108-021] lieth in thee, and do it diligently, not of custom, nor of necessity,

[108-022] but with fear, reverence, and affection, receive the Body of thy

[108-023] beloved Lord God, who vouchsafeth to come unto thee. I am He who hath

[108-024] called thee; I commanded it to be done; I will supply what is lacking

[108-025] to thee; come and receive Me.

[108-026] 3. When I give the grace of devotion, give thanks unto thy God; it is

[108-027] not because thou art worthy, but because I had [[forgiveness|mercy]] on thee. If thou

[108-028] hast not devotion, but rather feelest thyself dry, be instant in

[108-029] prayer, cease not to groan and knock; cease not until thou prevail to

[108-030] obtain some crumb or drop of saving grace. Thou hast need of Me, I have

[108-031] no need of thee. Nor dost thou come to sanctify Me, but I come to

[108-032] sanctify thee and make thee better. Thou comest that thou mayest be

[108-033] sanctified by Me, and be united to Me; that thou mayest receive fresh

[108-034] grace, and be kindled anew to [[amendment|amendment of life]]. See that thou neglect

[108-035] not this grace, but prepare thy heart with all diligence, and receive

[108-036] thy Beloved unto thee.

[108-037] 4. But thou oughtest not only to prepare thyself for devotion before

[108-038] [[communion|Communion]], thou must also keep thyself with all diligence therein after

[108-039] receiving the Sacrament; nor is less watchfulness needed afterwards,

[108-040] than devout preparation beforehand: for good watchfulness afterwards

[108-041] becometh in turn the best preparation for the gaining more grace. For

[108-042] hereby is a man made entirely indisposed to good, if he immediately

[108-043] return from Communion to give himself up to outward consolations.

[108-044] Beware of much speaking; remain in a secret place, and hold communion

[108-045] with thy God; for thou hast Him whom the whole world cannot take away

[108-046] from thee. I am He to whom thou oughtest wholly to give thyself; so

[108-047] that now thou mayest live not wholly in thyself, but in Me, free from

[108-048] all anxiety.

[108-049] (1) Mark xiv. 14, 15. (2) 1 Corinthians v. 7. (3) Psalm cii. 7.
