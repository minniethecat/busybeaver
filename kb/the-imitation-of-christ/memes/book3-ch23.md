[060-001] Of four things which bring great peace

[060-002] “My Son, now will I teach thee the way of peace and of true liberty.”

[060-003] 2. Do, O my [[jesus-christ|Lord]], as Thou sayest, for this is pleasing unto me to hear.

[060-004] 3. “Strive, My Son, to do another’s will rather than thine own. Choose

[060-005] always to have less rather than more. Seek always after the lowest

[060-006] place, and to be subject to all. Wish always and pray that the will of

[060-007] God be fulfilled in thee. Behold, such a man as this entereth into the

[060-008] inheritance of peace and quietness.”

[060-009] 4. O my Lord, this Thy short discourse hath in itself much of

[060-010] perfectness. It is short in words but full of meaning, and abundant in

[060-011] fruit. For if it were possible that I should fully keep it, disturbance

[060-012] would not so easily arise within me. For as often as I feel myself

[060-013] disquieted and weighed down, I find myself to have gone back from this

[060-014] teaching. But Thou, Who art Almighty, and always lovest progress in the

[060-015] soul, vouchsafe more grace, that I may be enabled to fulfil Thy

[060-016] exhortation, and work out my salvation.

[060-017] A PRAYER AGAINST EVIL THOUGHTS

[060-018] 5. O Lord my God, be not Thou far from me, my God, haste Thee to help

[060-019] me,(1) for many thoughts and great fears have risen up against me,

[060-020] afflicting my soul. How shall I pass through them unhurt? how shall I

[060-021] break through them?

[060-022] 6. “I,” saith He, “will go before thee, and make the crooked places

[060-023] straight.”(2) I will open the prison doors, and reveal to thee the

[060-024] secret places.

[060-025] 7. Do, Lord, as Thou sayest; and let all evil thoughts fly away before

[060-026] Thy face. This is my hope and my only comfort, to fly unto Thee in all

[060-027] [[suffering|tribulation]], to hope in Thee, to call upon Thee from my heart and

[060-028] patiently wait for Thy loving kindness.

[060-029] A PRAYER FOR ENLIGHTENMENT OF THE MIND

[060-030] 8. Enlighten me, Blessed Jesus, with the brightness of Thy inner light,

[060-031] and cast forth all darkness from the habitation of my heart. Restrain

[060-032] my many wandering thoughts, and carry away the temptations which strive

[060-033] to do me hurt. Fight Thou mightily for me, and drive forth the evil

[060-034] beasts, so call I alluring lusts, that peace may be within Thy walls

[060-035] and plenteousness of praise within Thy palaces,(3) even in my pure

[060-036] conscience. Command Thou the winds and the storms, say unto the sea,

[060-037] “Be still,” say unto the stormy wind, “Hold thy peace,” so shall there

[060-038] be a great calm.

[060-039] 9. Oh send forth Thy light and Thy truth,(4) that they may shine upon

[060-040] the earth; for I am but earth without form and void until Thou give me

[060-041] light. Pour forth Thy grace from above; water my heart with the dew of

[060-042] heaven; give the waters of devotion to water the face of the earth, and

[060-043] cause it to bring forth good and perfect fruit. Lift up my mind which

[060-044] is oppressed with the weight of [[sin|sins]], and raise my whole desire to

[060-045] heavenly things; that having tasted the sweetness of the [[blessedness|happiness]]

[060-046] which is from above, it may take no pleasure in thinking of things of

[060-047] earth.

[060-048] 10. Draw me and deliver me from every unstable comfort of [[creation|creatures]],

[060-049] for no created thing is able to satisfy my desire and to give me

[060-050] comfort. Join me to Thyself by the inseparable bond of love, for Thou

[060-051] alone art sufficient to him that loveth Thee, and without Thee all

[060-052] things are vain toys.

[060-053] (1) Psalm lxxi. 12. (2) [[isaiah|Isaiah]] xlv. 2. (3) Psalm cxxii. 7. (4) Psalm

[060-054] xliii. 3.
