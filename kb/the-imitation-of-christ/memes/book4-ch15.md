[111-001] That the grace of devotion is acquired by humility and self-denial

[111-002] The Voice of [[jesus-christ|the Beloved]]

[111-003] Thou oughtest to seek earnestly the grace of devotion, to ask it

[111-004] fervently, to wait for it patiently and faithfully, to receive it

[111-005] gratefully, to preserve it humbly, to work with it diligently, and to

[111-006] leave to God the time and manner of heavenly visitation until it come.

[111-007] Chiefly oughtest thou to humble thyself when thou feelest inwardly

[111-008] little or no devotion, yet not to be too much cast down, nor to grieve

[111-009] out of measure. God ofttimes giveth in one short moment what He hath

[111-010] long time denied; He sometimes giveth at the end what at the beginning

[111-011] of prayer He hath deferred to give.

[111-012] 2. If grace were always given immediately, and were at hand at the

[111-013] wish, it would be hardly bearable to weak man. Wherefore the grace of

[111-014] devotion is to be waited for with a [[hope|good hope]] and with humble patience.

[111-015] Yet impute it to thyself and to thy [[sin|sins]] when it is not given, or when

[111-016] it is mysteriously taken away. It is sometimes a small thing which

[111-017] hindereth and hideth grace; (if indeed that ought to be called _small_

[111-018] and not rather _great_, which hindereth so great a good); but if thou

[111-019] remove this, be it small or great, and perfectly overcome it, thou wilt

[111-020] have what thou hast asked.

[111-021] 3. For immediately that thou hast given thyself unto God with all thine

[111-022] heart, and hast sought neither this nor that according to thine own

[111-023] will and pleasure, but hast altogether settled thyself in Him, thou

[111-024] shalt find thyself united and at peace; because nothing shall give thee

[111-025] so sweet relish and delight, as the good pleasure of the Divine will.

[111-026] Whosoever therefore shall have lifted up his will unto God with

[111-027] singleness of heart, and shall have delivered himself from every

[111-028] inordinate love or dislike of any created thing, he will be the most

[111-029] fit for receiving grace, and worthy of the gift of devotion. For where

[111-030] the Lord findeth empty vessels,(1) there giveth He His blessing. And

[111-031] the more perfectly a man forsaketh things which cannot profit, and the

[111-032] more he dieth to himself, the more quickly doth grace come, the more

[111-033] plentifully doth it enter in, and the higher doth it lift up the free

[111-034] heart.

[111-035] 4. Then shall he see, and flow together, and wonder, and his heart

[111-036] shall be enlarged within him,(2) because the hand of the Lord is with

[111-037] him, and he hath put himself wholly in His hand, even for ever. Lo,

[111-038] thus shall the man be blessed, that seeketh God with all his heart, and

[111-039] receiveth not his soul in vain. This man in receiving the Holy

[111-040] [[communion|Eucharist]] obtaineth the great grace of Divine Union; because he hath

[111-041] not regard to his own devotion and comfort, but, above all devotion and

[111-042] comfort, to the glory and honour of God.

[111-043] (1) 2 Kings iv. (2) [[isaiah|Isaiah]] lx. 5.
