[003-001] Of the knowledge of truth

[003-002] Happy is the man whom [[truth|Truth]] by itself doth teach, not by figures and

[003-003] transient words, but as it is in itself.(1) Our own judgment and

[003-004] feelings often deceive us, and we discern but little of the truth. What

[003-005] doth it profit to argue about hidden and dark things, concerning which

[003-006] we shall not be even reproved in the judgment, because we knew them

[003-007] not? Oh, grievous folly, to neglect the things which are profitable and

[003-008] necessary, and to give our minds to things which are curious and

[003-009] hurtful! Having eyes, we see not.

[003-010] 2. And what have we to do with talk about genus and species! He to whom

[003-011] the Eternal Word speaketh is free from multiplied questionings. From

[003-012] this One Word are all things, and all things speak of Him; and this is

[003-013] the Beginning which also speaketh unto us.(2) No man without Him

[003-014] understandeth or rightly judgeth. The man to whom all things are one,

[003-015] who bringeth all things to one, who seeth all things in one, he is able

[003-016] to remain steadfast of spirit, and at rest in God. O God, who art the

[003-017] Truth, make me one with Thee in everlasting love. It wearieth me

[003-018] oftentimes to read and listen to many things; in Thee is all that I

[003-019] wish for and desire. Let all the doctors hold their peace; let all

[003-020] creation keep silence before Thee: speak Thou alone to me.

[003-021] 3. The more a man hath unity and simplicity in himself, the more things

[003-022] and the deeper things he understandeth; and that without labour,

[003-023] because he receiveth the light of understanding from above. The spirit

[003-024] which is pure, sincere, and steadfast, is not distracted though it hath

[003-025] many works to do, because it doth all things to the honour of God, and

[003-026] striveth to be free from all thoughts of self-seeking. Who is so full

[003-027] of hindrance and annoyance to thee as thine own undisciplined heart? A

[003-028] man who is good and devout arrangeth beforehand within his own heart

[003-029] the works which he hath to do abroad; and so is not drawn away by the

[003-030] desires of his evil will, but subjecteth everything to the judgment of

[003-031] right reason. Who hath a harder battle to fight than he who striveth

[003-032] for self-mastery? And this should be our endeavour, even to master

[003-033] self, and thus daily to grow stronger than self, and go on unto

[003-034] perfection.

[003-035] 4. All perfection hath some imperfection joined to it in this life, and

[003-036] all our power of sight is not without some darkness. A lowly knowledge

[003-037] of thyself is a surer way to God than the deep searching of man’s

[003-038] learning. Not that learning is to be blamed, nor the taking account of

[003-039] anything that is good; but a [[conscience|good conscience]] and a holy life is better

[003-040] than all. And because many seek knowledge rather than good living,

[003-041] therefore they go astray, and bear little or no fruit.

[003-042] 5. O if they would give that diligence to the rooting out of [[sin|vice]] and

[003-043] the planting of virtue which they give unto vain questionings: there

[003-044] had not been so many evil doings and stumbling-blocks among the laity,

[003-045] nor such ill living among houses of religion. Of a surety, at the Day

[003-046] of [[judgment|Judgment]] it will be demanded of us, not what we have read, but what

[003-047] we have done; not how well we have spoken, but how holily we have

[003-048] lived. Tell me, where now are all those masters and teachers, whom thou

[003-049] knewest well, whilst they were yet with you, and flourished in

[003-050] learning? Their stalls are now filled by others, who perhaps never have

[003-051] one thought concerning them. Whilst they lived they seemed to be

[003-052] somewhat, but now no one speaks of them.

[003-053] 6. Oh how quickly passeth the [[glory|glory of the world]] away! Would that their

[003-054] life and knowledge had agreed together! For then would they have read

[003-055] and inquired unto good purpose. How many perish through empty learning

[003-056] in this world, who care little for serving God. And because they love

[003-057] to be great more than to be humble, therefore they “have become vain in

[003-058] their imaginations.” He only is truly great, who hath great [[love|charity]]. He

[003-059] is truly great who deemeth himself small, and counteth all height of

[003-060] honour as nothing. He is the truly wise man, who counteth all earthly

[003-061] things as dung that he may win [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. And he is the truly learned man,

[003-062] who doeth the will of God, and forsaketh his own will.

[003-063] (1) Psalm xciv. 12; Numbers xii. 8. (2) John viii. 25 (Vulg.).
