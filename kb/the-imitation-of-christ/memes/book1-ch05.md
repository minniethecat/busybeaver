[005-001] Of the reading of Holy Scriptures

[005-002] It is [[truth|Truth]] which we must look for in Holy Writ, not cunning of words.

[005-003] All Scripture ought to be read in the spirit in which it was written.

[005-004] We must rather seek for what is profitable in Scripture, than for what

[005-005] ministereth to subtlety in discourse. Therefore we ought to read books

[005-006] which are devotional and simple, as well as those which are deep and

[005-007] difficult. And let not the weight of the writer be a stumbling-block to

[005-008] thee, whether he be of little or much learning, but let the love of the

[005-009] pure Truth draw thee to read. Ask not, who hath said this or that, but

[005-010] look to what he says.

[005-011] 2. Men pass away, but the truth of the [[jesus-christ|Lord]] endureth for ever. Without

[005-012] respect of persons God speaketh to us in divers manners. Our own

[005-013] curiosity often hindereth us in the reading of holy writings, when we

[005-014] seek to understand and discuss, where we should pass simply on. If thou

[005-015] wouldst profit by thy reading, read humbly, simply, honestly, and not

[005-016] desiring to win a character for learning. Ask freely, and hear in

[005-017] silence the words of holy men; nor be displeased at the hard sayings of

[005-018] older men than thou, for they are not uttered without cause.
