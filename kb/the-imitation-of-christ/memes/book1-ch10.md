[010-001] Of the danger of superfluity of words

[010-002] Avoid as far as thou canst the tumult of men; for talk concerning

[010-003] [[world|worldly things]], though it be innocently undertaken, is a hindrance, so

[010-004] quickly are we led captive and defiled by vanity. Many a time I wish

[010-005] that I had held my peace, and had not gone amongst men. But why do we

[010-006] talk and gossip so continually, seeing that we so rarely resume our

[010-007] silence without some hurt done to our conscience? We like talking so

[010-008] much because we hope by our conversations to gain some [[comfort|mutual comfort]],

[010-009] and because we seek to refresh our wearied spirits by variety of

[010-010] thoughts. And we very willingly talk and think of those things which we

[010-011] love or desire, or else of those which we most dislike.

[010-012] 2. But alas! it is often to no purpose and in vain. For this outward

[010-013] consolation is no small hindrance to the inner comfort which cometh

[010-014] from God. Therefore must we watch and pray that time pass not idly

[010-015] away. If it be right and desirable for thee to speak, speak things

[010-016] which are to edification. Evil custom and neglect of our real profit

[010-017] tend much to make us heedless of watching over our lips. Nevertheless,

[010-018] devout conversation on spiritual things helpeth not a little to

[010-019] spiritual progress, most of all where those of kindred mind and spirit

[010-020] find their ground of fellowship in God.
