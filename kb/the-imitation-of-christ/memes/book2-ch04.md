[029-001] Of a pure mind and [[simplicity|simple intention]]

[029-002] By two wings is man lifted above earthly things, even by simplicity and

[029-003] purity. Simplicity ought to be in the intention, purity in the

[029-004] affection. Simplicity reacheth towards God, purity apprehendeth Him and

[029-005] tasteth Him. No good action will be distasteful to thee if thou be free

[029-006] within from inordinate affection. If thou reachest after and seekest,

[029-007] nothing but the will of God and the benefit of thy neighbour, thou wilt

[029-008] entirely enjoy inward liberty. If thine heart were right, then should

[029-009] every creature be a mirror of life and a book of holy doctrine. There

[029-010] is no creature so small and vile but that it showeth us the goodness of

[029-011] God.

[029-012] 2. If thou wert good and pure within, then wouldst thou look upon all

[029-013] things without hurt and understand them aright. A pure heart seeth the

[029-014] very depths of heaven and hell. Such as each one is inwardly, so

[029-015] judgeth he outwardly. If there is any joy in [[world|the world]] surely the man

[029-016] of pure heart possesseth it, and if there is anywhere [[suffering|tribulation]] and

[029-017] anguish, the evil conscience knoweth it best. As iron cast into the

[029-018] fire loseth rust and is made altogether glowing, so the man who turneth

[029-019] himself altogether unto God is freed from slothfulness and changed into

[029-020] a new man.

[029-021] 3. When a man beginneth to grow lukewarm, then he feareth a little

[029-022] labour, and willingly accepteth outward [[comfort|consolation]]; but when he

[029-023] beginneth perfectly to conquer himself and to walk manfully in the way

[029-024] of God, then he counteth as nothing those things which aforetime seemed

[029-025] to be so grievous unto him.
