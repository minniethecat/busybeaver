[080-001] Against vain and worldly knowledge

[080-002] “My Son, let not the fair and subtle sayings of men move thee. For the

[080-003] kingdom of God is not in word, but in power.(1) Give ear to My words,

[080-004] for they kindle the heart and enlighten the mind, they bring

[080-005] contrition, and they supply manifold consolations. Never read thou the

[080-006] word that thou mayest appear more learned or wise; but study for the

[080-007] mortification of thy [[sin|sins]], for this will be far more profitable for

[080-008] thee than the knowledge of many difficult questions.

[080-009] 2. “When thou hast read and learned many things, thou must always

[080-010] return to one first principle. I am He that teacheth man knowledge,(2)

[080-011] and I give unto babes clearer knowledge than can be taught by man. He

[080-012] to whom I speak will be quickly wise and shall grow much in the spirit.

[080-013] Woe unto them who inquire into many curious questions from men, and

[080-014] take little heed concerning the way of My service. The time will come

[080-015] when [[jesus-christ|Christ]] will appear, the Master of masters, the Lord of the Angels,

[080-016] to hear the lessons of all, that is to examine the consciences of each

[080-017] one. And then will He search [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]] with candles,(3) and the hidden

[080-018] things of darkness(4) shall be made manifest, and the arguings of

[080-019] tongues shall be silent.

[080-020] 3. “I am He who in an instant lift up the humble spirit, to learn more

[080-021] reasonings of the [[truth|Eternal Truth]], than if a man had studied ten years in

[080-022] the schools. I teach without noise of words, without confusion of

[080-023] opinions, without striving after honour, without clash of arguments. I

[080-024] am He who teach men to despise earthly things, to loathe things

[080-025] present, to seek things heavenly, to enjoy things eternal, to flee

[080-026] honours, to endure offences, to place all hope in Me, to desire nothing

[080-027] apart from Me, and above all things to love Me ardently.

[080-028] 4. “For there was one, who by loving Me from the bottom of his heart,

[080-029] learned divine things, and spake things that were wonderful; he

[080-030] profited more by forsaking all things than by studying subtleties. But

[080-031] to some I speak common things, to others special; to some I appear

[080-032] gently in signs and figures, and again to some I reveal mysteries in

[080-033] much light. The voice of books is one, but it informeth not all alike;

[080-034] because I inwardly am the Teacher of truth, the Searcher of the heart,

[080-035] the Discerner of the thoughts, the Mover of actions, distributing to

[080-036] each man, as I judge meet.”
