[052-001] How we must stand and speak, in everything that we desire

[052-002] “My Son, speak thou thus in every matter, ‘[[jesus-christ|Lord]], if it please Thee, let

[052-003] this come to pass. Lord, if this shall be for Thine honour, let it be

[052-004] done in Thy Name. Lord, if thou see it good for me, and approve it as

[052-005] useful, then grant me to use it for Thy honour. But if thou knowest

[052-006] that it shall be hurtful unto me, and not profitable for the health of

[052-007] my soul, take the desire away from me’! For not every desire is from

[052-008] the [[holy-spirit|Holy Ghost]], although it appear to a man right and good. It is

[052-009] difficult to judge with certainty whether a good or an evil spirit move

[052-010] thee to desire this or that, or whether thou art moved by thine own

[052-011] spirit. Many have been deceived at the last, who seemed at the

[052-012] beginning to be moved by a good spirit.

[052-013] 2. “Therefore, whatsoever seemeth to thee desirable, thou must always

[052-014] desire and seek after it with the [[fear-of-the-lord|fear of God]] and humility of heart,

[052-015] and most of all, must altogether resign thyself, and commit all unto Me

[052-016] and say, ‘Lord, thou knowest what is best; let this or that be,

[052-017] according as Thou wilt. Give what Thou wilt, so much as Thou wilt, when

[052-018] Thou wilt. Do with me as Thou knowest best, and as best shall please

[052-019] Thee, and as shall be most to Thine honour. Place me where Thou wilt,

[052-020] and freely work Thy will with me in all things. I am in Thine hand, and

[052-021] turn me in my course. Behold, I am Thy servant, ready for all things;

[052-022] for I desire to live not to myself but to Thee. Oh, that I might live

[052-023] worthily and perfectly.’”

[052-024] A PRAYER TO BE ENABLED TO DO GOD’S WILL PERFECTLY

[052-025] 3. Grant me Thy grace, most merciful Jesus, that it may be with me, and

[052-026] work in me, and persevere with me, even unto the end. Grant that I may

[052-027] ever desire and wish whatsoever is most pleasing and dear unto Thee.

[052-028] Let Thy will be mine, and let my will alway follow Thine, and entirely

[052-029] accord with it. May I choose and reject whatsoever Thou dost; yea, let

[052-030] it be impossible for me to choose or reject except according to Thy

[052-031] will.

[052-032] 4. Grant that I may die to all [[world|worldly things]], and for Thy sake love to

[052-033] be despised and unknown in this world. Grant unto me, above all things

[052-034] that I can desire, to rest in Thee, and that in Thee my heart may be at

[052-035] peace. Thou art the true peace of the heart, Thou alone its rest; apart

[052-036] from Thee all things are hard and unquiet. In Thee alone, the supreme

[052-037] and eternal God, _I will lay me down in peace and take my rest_.(1)

[052-038] Amen.

[052-039] (1) Psalm iv. 8.
