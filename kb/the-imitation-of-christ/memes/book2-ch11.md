[036-001] Of the fewness of those who love the Cross of [[jesus-christ|Jesus]]

[036-002] Jesus hath many lovers of His heavenly kingdom, but few bearers of His

[036-003] Cross. He hath many seekers of comfort, but few of [[suffering|tribulation]]. He

[036-004] findeth many companions of His table, but few of His fasting. All

[036-005] desire to rejoice with Him, few are willing to undergo anything for His

[036-006] sake. Many follow Jesus that they may eat of His loaves, but few that

[036-007] they may drink of the cup of His passion. Many are astonished at His

[036-008] [[miracle|Miracles]], few follow after the shame of His Cross. Many love Jesus so

[036-009] long as no adversities happen to them. Many praise Him and bless Him,

[036-010] so long as they receive any comforts from Him. But if Jesus hide

[036-011] Himself and withdraw from them a little while, they fall either into

[036-012] complaining or into too great dejection of mind.

[036-013] 2. But they who love Jesus for Jesus’ sake, and not for any [[comfort|consolation]]

[036-014] of their own, bless Him in all tribulation and anguish of heart as in

[036-015] the highest consolation. And if He should never give them consolation,

[036-016] nevertheless they would always praise Him and always give Him thanks.

[036-017] 3. Oh what power hath the pure love of Jesus, unmixed with any gain or

[036-018] love of self! Should not all they be called mercenary who are always

[036-019] seeking consolations? Do they not prove themselves lovers of self more

[036-020] than of Christ who are always seeking their own gain and advantage?

[036-021] Where shall be found one who is willing to serve God altogether for

[036-022] nought?

[036-023] 4. Rarely is any one found so spiritual as to be stripped of all

[036-024] selfish thoughts, for who shall find a man truly [[poverty-of-spirit|poor in spirit]] and

[036-025] free of all [[creation|created things]]? “His value is from afar, yea from the ends

[036-026] of the earth.” A man may give away all his goods, yet that is nothing;

[036-027] and if he do many deeds of [[repentance|penitence]], yet that is a small thing; and

[036-028] though he understand all knowledge, yet that is afar off; and if he

[036-029] have great virtue and zealous devotion, yet much is lacking unto him,

[036-030] yea, one thing which is the most necessary to him of all. What is it

[036-031] then? That having given up all things besides, he give up himself and

[036-032] go forth from himself utterly, and retain nothing of self-love; and

[036-033] having done all things which he knoweth to be his duty to do, that he

[036-034] feel that he hath done nothing. Let him not reckon that much which

[036-035] might be much esteemed, but let him pronounce himself to be in truth an

[036-036] unprofitable servant, as the [[truth|Truth]] Himself saith, When ye have done all

[036-037] things that are commanded you, say, we are unprofitable servants.(1)

[036-038] Then may he be truly poor and naked in spirit, and be able to say with

[036-039] the Prophet, As for me, I am poor and needy.(2) Nevertheless, no man is

[036-040] richer than he, no man stronger, no man freer. For he knoweth both how

[036-041] to give up himself and all things, and how to be lowly in his own eyes.

[036-042] (1) Luke xvii. 10. (2) Psalm xxv. 16.
