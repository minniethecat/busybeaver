[076-001] That man must not be immersed in business

[076-002] “My Son, always commit thy cause to Me; I will dispose it aright in due

[076-003] time. Wait for My arrangement of it, and then thou shalt find it for

[076-004] thy profit.”

[076-005] 2. O [[jesus-christ|Lord]], right freely I commit all things to Thee; for my planning

[076-006] can profit but little. Oh that I did not dwell so much on future

[076-007] events, but could offer myself altogether to Thy pleasures without

[076-008] delay.

[076-009] 3. “My Son, a man often striveth vehemently after somewhat which he

[076-010] desireth; but when he hath obtained it he beginneth to be of another

[076-011] mind, because his affections towards it are not lasting, but rather

[076-012] rush on from one thing to another. Therefore it is not really a small

[076-013] thing, when in small things we resist self.”

[076-014] 4. The true progress of man lieth in self-denial, and a man who denieth

[076-015] himself is free and safe. But the old enemy, opposer of all good

[076-016] things, ceaseth not from temptation; but day and night setteth his

[076-017] wicked snares, if haply he may be able to entrap the unwary. Watch and

[076-018] pray, saith the Lord, lest ye enter into temptation.(1)

[076-019] (1) Matthew xxvi. 41.
