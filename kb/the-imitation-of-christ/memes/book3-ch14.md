[051-001] Of meditation upon the hidden judgments of God, that we may not be

[051-002] lifted up because of our well-doing

[051-003] Thou sendest forth Thy judgments against me, O [[jesus-christ|Lord]], and shakest all my

[051-004] bones with fear and trembling, and my soul trembleth exceedingly. I

[051-005] stand astonished, and remember that the heavens are not clean in thy

[051-006] sight.(1) If Thou chargest Thine angels with folly, and didst spare

[051-007] them not, how shall it be unto me? Stars have fallen from heaven, and

[051-008] what shall I dare who am but dust? They whose works seemed to be

[051-009] praiseworthy, fell into the lowest depths, and they who did eat Angels’

[051-010] food, them have I seen delighted with the husks that the swine do eat.

[051-011] 2. There is therefore no holiness, if Thou O Lord, withdraw Thine hand.

[051-012] No wisdom profiteth, if Thou leave off to guide the helm. No strength

[051-013] availeth, if Thou cease to preserve. No purity is secure, if Thou

[051-014] protect it not. No self-keeping availeth, if Thy holy watching be not

[051-015] there. For when we are left alone we are swallowed up and perish, but

[051-016] when we are visited, we are raised up, and we live. For indeed we are

[051-017] unstable, but are made strong through Thee; we grow cold, but are

[051-018] rekindled by Thee.

[051-019] 3. Oh, how humbly and abjectly must I reckon of myself, how must I

[051-020] weigh it as nothing, if I seem to have nothing good! Oh, how profoundly

[051-021] ought I to submit myself to Thy unfathomable judgments, O Lord, when I

[051-022] find myself nothing else save nothing, and again nothing! Oh weight

[051-023] unmeasurable, oh ocean which cannot be crossed over, where I find

[051-024] nothing of myself save nothing altogether! Where, then, is the

[051-025] hiding-place of glory, where the confidence begotten of virtue? All

[051-026] [[glory|vain-glory]] is swallowed up in the depths of Thy judgments against me.

[051-027] 4. What is all flesh in Thy sight? _For how shall the clay boast

[051-028] against Him that fashioned it?_(2) How can he be lifted up in vain

[051-029] speech whose heart is subjected in truth to God? The whole world shall

[051-030] not lift him up whom [[truth|Truth]] hath subdued; nor shall he be moved by the

[051-031] mouth of all who praise him, who hath placed all his hope in God. For

[051-032] they themselves who speak, behold, they are all nothing; for they shall

[051-033] cease with the sound of their words, but the truth of the Lord endureth

[051-034] for ever.(3)
