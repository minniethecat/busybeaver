[012-001] Of the uses of [[suffering|adversity]]

[012-002] It is good for us that we sometimes have sorrows and adversities, for

[012-003] they often make a man lay to heart that he is only a stranger and

[012-004] sojourner, and may not put his [[trust-in-god|trust]] in any worldly thing. It is good

[012-005] that we sometimes endure contradictions, and are hardly and unfairly

[012-006] judged, when we do and mean what is good. For these things help us to

[012-007] be humble, and shield us from [[glory|vain-glory]]. For then we seek the more

[012-008] earnestly the witness of God, when men speak evil of us falsely, and

[012-009] give us no credit for good.

[012-010] 2. Therefore ought a man to rest wholly upon God, so that he needeth

[012-011] not seek much comfort at the hand of men. When a man who feareth God is

[012-012] afflicted or tried or oppressed with evil thoughts, then he seeth that

[012-013] God is the more necessary unto him, since without God he can do no good

[012-014] thing. Then he is heavy of heart, he groaneth, he crieth out for the

[012-015] very disquietness of his heart. Then he groweth weary of life, and

[012-016] would fain depart and be with [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. By all this he is taught that in

[012-017] [[world|the world]] there can be no perfect security or fulness of peace.
