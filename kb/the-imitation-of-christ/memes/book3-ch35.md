[072-001] That there is no security against temptation in this life

[072-002] “My Son, thou art never secure in this life, but thy spiritual armour

[072-003] will always be needful for thee as long as thou livest. Thou dwellest

[072-004] among foes, and art attacked on the right hand and on the left. If

[072-005] therefore thou use not on all sides the shield of patience, thou wilt

[072-006] not remain long unwounded. Above all, if thou keep not thy heart fixed

[072-007] upon Me with steadfast purpose to bear all things for My sake, thou

[072-008] shalt not be able to bear the fierceness of the attack, nor to attain

[072-009] to the victory of the blessed. Therefore must thou struggle bravely all

[072-010] thy life through, and put forth a strong hand against those things

[072-011] which oppose thee. For to him that overcometh is the hidden manna

[072-012] given,(1) but great misery is reserved for the slothful.

[072-013] 2. “If thou seek rest in this life, how then wilt thou attain unto the

[072-014] rest which is eternal? Set not thyself to attain much rest, but much

[072-015] patience. Seek the true peace, not in earth but in heaven, not in man

[072-016] nor in any created thing, but in God alone. For the love of God thou

[072-017] must willingly undergo all things, whether labours or sorrows,

[072-018] temptations, vexations, anxieties, necessities, infirmities, injuries,

[072-019] gainsayings, rebukes, humiliations, confusions, corrections,

[072-020] despisings; these things help unto virtue, these things prove the

[072-021] scholar of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]; these things fashion the heavenly crown. I will give

[072-022] thee an eternal reward for short labour, and infinite glory for

[072-023] transient shame.

[072-024] 3. “Thinkest thou that thou shalt always have spiritual consolations at

[072-025] thy will? My Saints had never such, but instead thereof manifold

[072-026] griefs, and divers temptations, and heavy desolations. But patiently

[072-027] they bore themselves in all, and trusted in God more than in

[072-028] themselves, knowing that the sufferings of this present time are not

[072-029] worthy to be compared with the glory which shall be revealed in us.(2)

[072-030] Wouldst thou have that immediately which many have hardly attained unto

[072-031] after many tears and hard labours? Wait for the Lord, quit thyself like

[072-032] a man and be strong; be not faint-hearted, nor go aside from Me, but

[072-033] constantly devote thy body and soul to the glory of God. I will reward

[072-034] thee plenteously, I will be with thee in trouble.”(3)

[072-035] (1) Revelation ii. 17. (2) Romans viii. 17. (3) Psalm xci. 15.
