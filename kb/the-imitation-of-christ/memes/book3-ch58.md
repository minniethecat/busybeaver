[095-001] Of deeper matters, and God’s hidden judgments which are not to be

[095-002] inquired into

[095-003] “My Son, beware thou dispute not of high matters and of the hidden

[095-004] judgments of God; why this man is thus left, and that man is taken into

[095-005] so great favour; why also this man is so greatly afflicted, and that so

[095-006] highly exalted. These things pass all man’s power of judging, neither

[095-007] may any reasoning or disputation have power to search out the divine

[095-008] judgments. When therefore the enemy suggesteth these things to thee, or

[095-009] when any curious people ask such questions, answer with that word of

[095-010] the Prophet, _Just art Thou, O [[jesus-christ|Lord]], and true is Thy judgment_,(1) and

[095-011] with this, _The judgments of the Lord are true, and righteous

[095-012] altogether_.(2) My judgments are to be feared, not to be disputed on,

[095-013] because they are incomprehensible to human understanding.

[095-014] 2. “And be not given to inquire or dispute about the merits of the

[095-015] Saints, which is holier than another, or which is the greater in the

[095-016] [[heaven|Kingdom of Heaven]]. Such questions often beget useless strifes and

[095-017] contentions: they also nourish pride and [[pride|vain glory]], whence envyings

[095-018] and dissensions arise, while one man arrogantly endeavoureth to exalt

[095-019] one Saint and another another. But to wish to know and search out such

[095-020] things bringeth no fruit, but it rather displeaseth the Saints; for I

[095-021] am not _the God of confusion but of peace;_(3) which peace consisteth

[095-022] more in true humility than in self-exaltation.

[095-023] 3. “Some are drawn by zeal of love to greater affection to these Saints

[095-024] or those; but this is human affection rather than divine. I am He Who

[095-025] made all the Saints: I gave them grace, I brought them glory; I know

[095-026] the merits of every one; _I prevented them with the blessings of My

[095-027] goodness_.(4) I foreknew my beloved ones from everlasting, _I chose

[095-028] them out of [[world|the world]];_(5) they did not choose Me. I called them by My

[095-029] grace, drew them by My [[forgiveness|mercy]], led them on through sundry temptations. I

[095-030] poured mighty consolations upon them, I gave them perseverance, I

[095-031] crowned their patience.

[095-032] 4. “I acknowledge the first and the last; I embrace all with

[095-033] inestimable love. I am to be praised in all My Saints; I am to be

[095-034] blessed above all things, and to be honoured in every one whom I have

[095-035] so gloriously exalted and predestined, without any preceding merits of

[095-036] their own. He therefore that shall despise one of the least of these My

[095-037] people, honoureth not the great; because I made both small and

[095-038] great.(6) And he who speaketh against any of My Saints speaketh against

[095-039] Me, and against all others in [[paradise|the Kingdom of Heaven]].”

[095-040] They are all one through the bond of [[love|charity]]; they think the same

[095-041] thing, will the same thing, and all are united in love one to another.

[095-042] 5. “But yet (which is far better) they love Me above themselves and

[095-043] their own merits. For being caught up above themselves, and drawn

[095-044] beyond self-love, they go all straightforward to the love of Me, and

[095-045] they rest in Me in perfect enjoyment. There is nothing which can turn

[095-046] them away or press them down; for being full of [[truth|Eternal Truth]], they

[095-047] burn with the fire of inextinguishable charity. Therefore let all

[095-048] carnal and natural men hold their peace concerning the state of the

[095-049] Saints, for they know nothing save to love their own personal

[095-050] enjoyment. They take away and add according to their own inclination,

[095-051] not as it pleaseth the Eternal Truth.

[095-052] 6. “In many men this is ignorance, chiefly is it so in those who, being

[095-053] little enlightened, rarely learn to love any one with perfect spiritual

[095-054] love. They are still much drawn by natural affection and human

[095-055] friendship to these or to those: and as they reckon of themselves in

[095-056] lower matters, so also do they frame imaginations of things heavenly.

[095-057] But there is an immeasurable difference between those things which they

[095-058] imperfectly imagine, and these things which enlightened men behold

[095-059] through supernatural revelation.

[095-060] 7. “Take heed, therefore, My son, that thou treat not curiously those

[095-061] things which surpass thy knowledge, but rather make this thy business

[095-062] and give attention to it, namely, that thou seek to be found, even

[095-063] though it be the least, in the [[kingdom-of-god|Kingdom of God]]. And even if any one

[095-064] should know who were holier than others, or who were held greatest in

[095-065] the Kingdom of Heaven; what should that knowledge profit him, unless

[095-066] through this knowledge he should humble himself before Me, and should

[095-067] rise up to give greater praise unto My name? He who considereth how

[095-068] great are his own [[sin|sins]], how small his virtues, and how far he is

[095-069] removed from the perfection of the Saints, doeth far more acceptably in

[095-070] the sight of God, than he who disputeth about their greatness or

[095-071] littleness.

[095-072] 8. “They are altogether well content, if men would learn to be content,

[095-073] and to refrain from vain babbling. They glory not of their own merits,

[095-074] seeing they ascribe no good unto themselves, but all unto Me, seeing

[095-075] that I of my infinite charity have given them all things. They are

[095-076] filled with so great love of the Divinity, and with such overflowing

[095-077] joy, that no glory is lacking to them, neither can any felicity be

[095-078] lacking. All the Saints, the higher they are exalted in glory, the

[095-079] humbler are they in themselves, and the nearer and dearer are they unto

[095-080] Me. And so thou hast it written that they cast their crowns before God

[095-081] and fell on their faces before the Lamb, and worshipped Him that liveth

[095-082] for ever and ever.(7)

[095-083] 9. “Many ask who is greatest in the Kingdom of Heaven, who know not

[095-084] whether they shall be worthy to be counted among the least. It is a

[095-085] great thing to be even the least in Heaven, where all are great,

[095-086] because all shall be called, and shall be, the sons of God. _A little

[095-087] one shall become a thousand, but the sinner being an hundred years old

[095-088] shall be accursed_. For when the disciples asked _who should be the

[095-089] greatest in the Kingdom of Heaven_, they received no other answer than

[095-090] this, _Except ye be converted and become as little children, ye shall

[095-091] not enter into the Kingdom of Heaven. But whosoever shall humble

[095-092] himself as this little child, the same shall be greatest in the Kingdom

[095-093] of Heaven_.”(8)

[095-094] 10. Woe unto them who disdain to humble themselves willingly with the

[095-095] little children; for the low gate of the kingdom of Heaven will not

[095-096] suffer them to enter in. Woe also to them who are rich, who have their

[095-097] [[comfort|consolation]] here;(9) because whilst the poor enter into the kingdom of

[095-098] God, they shall stand lamenting without. Rejoice ye humble, and exult

[095-099] ye poor, for yours is the kingdom of God if only ye walk in the truth.

[095-101] Psalm xxi. 3. (5) John xv. 19. (6) Wisd. vi. 8. (7) Revelation iv. 10;

[095-102] v. 14. (8) Matthew xviii. 3. (9) Luke vi. 24.
