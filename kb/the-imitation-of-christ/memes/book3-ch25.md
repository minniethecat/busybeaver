[062-001] Wherein firm [[peace|peace of heart]] and true profit consist

[062-002] “My Son, I have said, Peace I leave with you, My peace I give unto you,

[062-003] not as [[world|the world]] giveth give I unto you.(1) All men desire peace, but

[062-004] all do not care for the things which belong unto true peace. My peace

[062-005] is with the humble and lowly in heart. Thy peace shall be in much

[062-006] patience. If thou heardest Me, and didst follow My voice, thou

[062-007] shouldest enjoy much peace.”

[062-008] 2. What then shall I do, [[jesus-christ|Lord]]?

[062-009] 3. “In everything take heed to thyself what thou doest, and what thou

[062-010] sayest; and direct all thy purpose to this, that thou please Me alone,

[062-011] and desire or seek nothing apart from Me. But, moreover, judge nothing

[062-012] rashly concerning the words or deeds of others, nor meddle with matters

[062-013] which are not committed to thee; and it may be that thou shalt be

[062-014] disturbed little or rarely. Yet never to feel any disquiet, nor to

[062-015] suffer any pain of heart or body, this belongeth not to the present

[062-016] life, but is the state of eternal rest. Therefore count not thyself to

[062-017] have found true peace, if thou hast felt no grief; nor that then all is

[062-018] well if thou hast no adversary; nor that this is perfect if all things

[062-019] fall out according to thy desire. Nor then reckon thyself to be

[062-020] anything great, or think that thou art specially beloved, if thou art

[062-021] in a state of great [[devotion|fervour]] and sweetness of spirit; for not by these

[062-022] things is the true lover of virtue known, nor in them doth the profit

[062-023] and perfection of man consist.”

[062-024] 4. In what then, Lord?

[062-025] 5. “In offering thyself with all thy heart to the Divine Will, in not

[062-026] seeking the things which are thine own, whether great or small, whether

[062-027] temporal or eternal; so that thou remain with the same steady

[062-028] countenance in giving of thanks between prosperity and [[suffering|adversity]],

[062-029] weighing all things in an equal balance. If thou be so brave and

[062-030] [[patience|long-suffering]] in hope that when [[comfort|inward comfort]] is taken from thee,

[062-031] thou even prepare thy heart for the more endurance, and justify not

[062-032] thyself, as though thou oughtest not to suffer these heavy things, but

[062-033] dost justify Me in all things that I appoint, and dost bless My Holy

[062-034] Name, then dost thou walk in the true and right way of peace, and shalt

[062-035] have a sure hope that thou shalt again behold My face with joy. For if

[062-036] thou come to an utter contempt of thyself, know that then thou shalt

[062-037] enjoy abundance of peace, as much as is possible where thou art but a

[062-038] wayfaring man.”

[062-039] (1) John xiv. 27.
