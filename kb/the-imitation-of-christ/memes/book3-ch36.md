[073-001] Against vain judgments of men

[073-002] “My Son, anchor thy soul firmly upon God, and fear not man’s judgment,

[073-003] when conscience pronounceth thee pious and innocent. It is good and

[073-004] blessed thus to suffer; nor will it be grievous to the heart which is

[073-005] humble, and which trusteth in God more than in itself. Many men have

[073-006] many opinions, and therefore little [[trust-in-god|trust]] is to be placed in them. But

[073-007] moreover it is impossible to please all. Although Paul studied to

[073-008] please all men in the [[jesus-christ|Lord]], and to become all things to all men,(1) yet

[073-009] nevertheless with him it was a very small thing that he should be

[073-010] judged by man’s judgment.”(2)

[073-011] 2. He laboured abundantly, as much as in him lay, for the building up

[073-012] and the salvation of others; but he could not avoid being sometimes

[073-013] judged and despised by others. Therefore he committed all to God, who

[073-014] knew all, and by patience and humility defended himself against evil

[073-015] speakers, or foolish and false thinkers, and those who accused him

[073-016] according to their pleasure. Nevertheless, from time to time he

[073-017] replied, lest his silence should become a stumbling-block to those who

[073-018] were weak.

[073-019] 3. “Who art thou, that thou shouldst be afraid of a man that shall die?

[073-020] To-day he is, and to-morrow his place is not found. Fear God and thou

[073-021] shalt not quail before the terrors of men. What can any man do against

[073-022] thee by words or deeds? He hurteth himself more than thee, nor shall he

[073-023] escape the judgment of God, whosoever he may be. Have thou God before

[073-024] thine eyes, and do not contend with fretful words. And if for the

[073-025] present thou seem to give way, and to suffer confusion which thou hast

[073-026] not deserved, be not angry at this, nor by impatience diminish thy

[073-027] reward; but rather look up to Me in heaven, for I am able to deliver

[073-028] thee from all confusion and hurt, and to render to every man according

[073-029] to his works.”

[073-030] (1) 1 Corinthians ix. 22. (2) 1 Corinthians iv. 3.
