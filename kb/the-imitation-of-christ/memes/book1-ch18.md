[018-001] Of the example of the holy fathers

[018-002] Consider now the lively examples of the holy fathers, in whom shone

[018-003] forth real perfectness and religion, and thou shalt see how little,

[018-004] even as nothing, is all that we do. Ah! What is our life when compared

[018-005] to theirs? They, saints and friends of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] as they were, served the

[018-006] Lord in hunger and thirst, in cold and nakedness, in labour and

[018-007] weariness, in watchings and fastings, in prayer and holy meditations,

[018-008] in persecutions and much rebuke.

[018-009] 2. O how many and grievous tribulations did the [[apostles|Apostles]], Martyrs,

[018-010] Confessors, Virgins, endure; and all others who would walk in the

[018-011] footsteps of Christ. For they hated their souls in this world that they

[018-012] might keep them unto life eternal. O how strict and retired a life was

[018-013] that of the holy fathers who dwelt in the desert! what long and

[018-014] grievous temptations they did suffer! how often were they assaulted by

[018-015] the enemy! what frequent and fervid prayers did they offer unto God!

[018-016] what strict fasts did they endure! what [[zeal|fervent zeal]] and desire after

[018-017] spiritual profit did they manifest! how bravely did they fight that

[018-018] their vices might not gain the mastery! how entirely and steadfastly

[018-019] did they reach after God! By day they laboured, and at night they gave

[018-020] themselves ofttimes unto prayer; yea, even when they were labouring

[018-021] they ceased not from [[prayer|mental prayer]].

[018-022] 3. They spent their whole time profitably; every hour seemed short for

[018-023] retirement with God; and through the great sweetness of contemplation,

[018-024] even the need of bodily refreshment was forgotten. They renounced all

[018-025] riches, dignities, honours, friends, kinsmen; they desired nothing from

[018-026] [[world|the world]]; they ate the bare necessaries of life; they were unwilling

[018-027] to minister to the body even in necessity. Thus were they poor in

[018-028] earthly things, but rich above measure in grace and virtue. Though poor

[018-029] to the outer eye, within they were filled with grace and heavenly

[018-030] benedictions.

[018-031] 4. They were strangers to the world, but unto God they were as kinsmen

[018-032] and friends. They seemed unto themselves as of no reputation, and in

[018-033] the world’s eyes contemptible; but in the sight of God they were

[018-034] precious and beloved. They stood fast in true humility, they lived in

[018-035] simple obedience, they walked in love and patience; and thus they waxed

[018-036] strong in spirit, and obtained great favour before God. To all

[018-037] religious men they were given as an example, and they ought more to

[018-038] provoke us unto good livings than the number of the lukewarm tempteth

[018-039] to carelessness of life.

[018-040] 5. O how great was the love of all religious persons at the beginning

[018-041] of this sacred institution! O what [[devotion|devoutness]] of prayer! what rivalry

[018-042] in holiness! what strict discipline was observed! what reverence and

[018-043] obedience under the rule of the master showed they in all things! The

[018-044] traces of them that remain until now testify that they were truly holy

[018-045] and perfect men, who fighting so bravely trod the world underfoot. Now

[018-046] a man is counted great if only he be not a transgressor, and if he can

[018-047] only endure with patience what he hath undertaken.

[018-048] 6. O the coldness and negligence of our times, that we so quickly

[018-049] decline from the former love, and it is become a weariness to live,

[018-050] because of sloth and lukewarmness. May progress in holiness not wholly

[018-051] fall asleep in thee, who many times hast seen so many examples of

[018-052] devout men!
