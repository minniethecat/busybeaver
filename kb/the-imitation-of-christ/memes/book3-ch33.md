[070-001] Of instability of the heart, and of directing the aim towards God

[070-002] “My Son, [[trust-in-god|trust]] not thy feeling, for that which is now will be quickly

[070-003] changed into somewhat else. As long as thou livest thou art subject to

[070-004] change, howsoever unwilling; so that thou art found now joyful, now

[070-005] sad; now at peace, now disquieted; now devout, now indevout; now

[070-006] studious, now careless; now sad, now cheerful. But the wise man, and he

[070-007] who is truly learned in spirit, standeth above these changeable things,

[070-008] attentive not to what he may feel in himself, or from what quarter the

[070-009] wind may blow, but that the whole intent of his mind may carry him on

[070-010] to the due and much-desired end. For thus will he be able to remain one

[070-011] and the same and unshaken, the single eye of his desire being

[070-012] steadfastly fixed, through the manifold changes of [[world|the world]], upon Me.

[070-013] 2. “But according as the eye of intention be the more pure, even so

[070-014] will a man make his way steadfastly through the manifold storms. But in

[070-015] many the eye of [[simplicity|pure intention]] waxeth dim; for it quickly resteth

[070-016] itself upon anything pleasant which occurreth, and rarely is any man

[070-017] found altogether free from the blemish of self-seeking. So the Jews of

[070-018] old came to [[bethany|Bethany]], to the house of Martha and [[mary|Mary]], that they might

[070-019] see not [[jesus-christ|Jesus]], but Lazarus, whom he had raised from the dead.(1)

[070-020] Therefore must the eye of the intention be cleansed, that it may be

[070-021] single and right, and above all things which come in its way, may be

[070-022] directed unto Me.”

[070-023] (1) John xii. 9.
