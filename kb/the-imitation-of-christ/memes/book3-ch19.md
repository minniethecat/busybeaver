[056-001] Of bearing injuries, and who shall be approved as truly patient

[056-002] “What sayest thou, My Son? Cease to complain; consider My suffering and

[056-003] that of My saints. Thou hast not yet resisted unto blood.(1) It is

[056-004] little which thou sufferest in comparison with those who have suffered

[056-005] so many things, have been so strongly tempted, so grievously troubled,

[056-006] so manywise proved and tried. Thou oughtest therefore to call to mind

[056-007] the more grievous sufferings of others that thou mightest bear thy

[056-008] lesser ones more easily, and if they seem not to thee little, see that

[056-009] it is not thy impatience which is the cause of this. But whether they

[056-010] be little or whether they be great, study to bear them all with

[056-011] patience.

[056-012] 2. “So far as thou settest thyself to bear patiently, so far thou dost

[056-013] wisely and art deserving of the more merit; thou shalt also bear the

[056-014] more easily if thy mind and habit are carefully trained hereunto. And

[056-015] say not ‘I cannot bear these things from such a man, nor are things of

[056-016] this kind to be borne by me, for he hath done me grievous harm and

[056-017] imputeth to me what I had never thought: but from another I will suffer

[056-018] patiently, such things as I see I ought to suffer.’ Foolish is such a

[056-019] thought as this, for it considereth not the virtue of patience, nor by

[056-020] whom that virtue is to be crowned, but it rather weigheth persons and

[056-021] offences against self.

[056-022] 3. “He is not truly patient who will only suffer as far as seemeth

[056-023] right to himself and from whom he pleaseth. But the truly patient man

[056-024] considereth not by what man he is tried, whether by one above him, or

[056-025] by an equal or inferior, whether by a good and holy man, or a perverse

[056-026] and unworthy; but indifferently from every creature, whatsoever or how

[056-027] often soever [[suffering|adversity]] happeneth to him, he gratefully accepteth all

[056-028] from the hand of God and counteth it great gain: for with God nothing

[056-029] which is borne for His sake, however small, shall lose its reward.

[056-030] 4. “Be thou therefore ready for the fight if thou wilt have the

[056-031] victory. Without striving thou canst not win the crown of patience; if

[056-032] thou wilt not suffer thou refusest to be crowned. But if thou desirest

[056-033] to be crowned, strive manfully, endure patiently. Without labour thou

[056-034] drawest not near to rest, nor without fighting comest thou to victory.”

[056-035] 5. Make possible to me, O [[jesus-christ|Lord]], by grace what seemeth impossible to me

[056-036] by nature. Thou knowest how little I am able to bear, and how quickly I

[056-037] am cast down when a like adversity riseth up against me. Whatsoever

[056-038] [[temptation|trial]] of tribulation may come to me, may it become unto me pleasing and

[056-039] acceptable, for to suffer and be vexed for Thy sake is exceeding

[056-040] healthful to the soul.

[056-041] (1) Hebrews xii. 4.
