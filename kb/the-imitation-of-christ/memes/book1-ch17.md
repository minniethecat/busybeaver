[017-001] Of a Religious life

[017-002] It behoveth thee to learn to mortify thyself in many things, if thou

[017-003] wilt live in amity and concord with other men. It is no small thing to

[017-004] dwell in a religious community or congregation, and to live there

[017-005] without complaint, and therein to remain faithful even unto death.

[017-006] Blessed is he who hath lived a good life in such a body, and brought it

[017-007] to a happy end. If thou wilt stand fast and wilt profit as thou

[017-008] oughtest, hold thyself as an exile and a pilgrim upon the earth. Thou

[017-009] wilt have to be counted as a fool for [[jesus-christ|Christ]], if thou wilt lead a

[017-010] religious life.

[017-011] 2. The clothing and outward appearance are of small account; it is

[017-012] change of character and entire [[mortification|mortification of the affections]] which

[017-013] make a truly religious man. He who seeketh aught save God and the

[017-014] health of his soul, shall find only [[suffering|tribulation]] and sorrow. Nor can he

[017-015] stand long in peace, who striveth not to be least of all and servant of

[017-016] all.

[017-017] 3. Thou art called to endure and to labour, not to a life of ease and

[017-018] trifling talk. Here therefore are men tried as gold in the furnace. No

[017-019] man can stand, unless with all his heart he will humble himself for

[017-020] God’s sake.
