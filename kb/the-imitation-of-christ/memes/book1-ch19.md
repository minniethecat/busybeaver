[019-001] Of the exercises of a religious man

[019-002] The life of a Christian ought to be adorned with all virtues, that he

[019-003] may be inwardly what he outwardly appeareth unto men. And verily it

[019-004] should be yet better within than without, for God is a discerner of our

[019-005] heart, Whom we must reverence with all our hearts wheresoever we are,

[019-006] and walk pure in His presence as do the angels. We ought daily to renew

[019-007] our vows, and to kindle our hearts to zeal, as if each day were the

[019-008] first day of our conversion, and to say, “Help me, O God, in my good

[019-009] resolutions, and in Thy holy service, and grant that this day I may

[019-010] make a good beginning, for hitherto I have done nothing!”

[019-011] 2. According to our resolution so is the rate of our progress, and much

[019-012] diligence is needful for him who would make good progress. For if he

[019-013] who resolveth bravely oftentimes falleth short, how shall it be with

[019-014] him who resolveth rarely or feebly? But manifold causes bring about

[019-015] [[desolation|abandonment]] of our resolution, yet a trivial omission of holy exercises

[019-016] can hardly be made without some loss to us. The resolution of the

[019-017] righteous dependeth more upon the [[grace|grace of God]] than upon their own

[019-018] wisdom; for in Him they always put their [[trust-in-god|trust]], whatsoever they take in

[019-019] hand. For man proposeth, but God disposeth; and the way of a man is not

[019-020] in himself.(1)

[019-021] 3. If a holy exercise be sometimes omitted for the sake of some act of

[019-022] piety, or of some brotherly kindness, it can easily be taken up

[019-023] afterwards; but if it be neglected through distaste or slothfulness,

[019-024] then is it sinful, and the mischief will be felt. Strive as earnestly

[019-025] as we may, we shall still fall short in many things. Always should some

[019-026] distinct resolution be made by us; and, most of all, we must strive

[019-027] against those [[sin|sins]] which most easily beset us. Both our outer and inner

[019-028] life should be straitly examined and ruled by us, because both have to

[019-029] do with our progress.

[019-030] 4. If thou canst not be always examining thyself, thou canst at certain

[019-031] seasons, and at least twice in the day, at evening and at morning. In

[019-032] the morning make thy resolves, and in the evening inquire into thy

[019-033] life, how thou hast sped to-day in word, deed, and thought; for in

[019-034] these ways thou hast often perchance offended God and thy neighbour.

[019-035] Gird up thy lions like a man against the assaults of the devil; bridle

[019-036] thine appetite, and thou wilt soon be able to bridle every inclination

[019-037] of the flesh. Be thou never without something to do; be reading, or

[019-038] writing, or praying, or meditating, or doing something that is useful

[019-039] to the community. Bodily exercises, however, must be undertaken with

[019-040] [[prudence|discretion]], nor are they to be used by all alike.

[019-041] 5. The duties which are not common to all must not be done openly, but

[019-042] are safest carried on in secret. But take heed that thou be not

[019-043] careless in the common duties, and more devout in the secret; but

[019-044] faithfully and honestly discharge the duties and commands which lie

[019-045] upon thee, then afterwards, if thou hast still leisure, give thyself to

[019-046] thyself as thy devotion leadeth thee. All cannot have one exercise, but

[019-047] one suiteth better to this man and another to that. Even for the

[019-048] diversity of season different exercises are needed, some suit better

[019-049] for feasts, some for fasts. We need one kind in time of temptations and

[019-050] others in time of peace and quietness. Some are suitable to our times

[019-051] of sadness, and others when we are joyful in the [[jesus-christ|Lord]].

[019-052] 6. When we draw near the time of the great feasts, good exercises

[019-053] should be renewed, and the prayers of holy men more fervently besought.

[019-054] We ought to make our resolutions from one Feast to another, as if each

[019-055] were the period of our departure from this world, and of entering into

[019-056] the eternal feast. So ought we to prepare ourselves earnestly at solemn

[019-057] seasons, and the more solemnly to live, and to keep straightest watch

[019-058] upon each holy observance, as though we were soon to receive the reward

[019-059] of our labours at the hand of God.

[019-060] 7. And if this be deferred, let us believe ourselves to be as yet

[019-061] ill-prepared, and unworthy as yet of the glory which shall be revealed

[019-062] in us at the appointed season; and let us study to prepare ourselves

[019-063] the better for our end. Blessed is that servant, as the Evangelist Luke

[019-064] hath it, whom, when the Lord cometh He shall find watching. Verily I

[019-065] say unto you He will make him ruler over all that He hath.(2)

[019-066] (1) [[jeremiah|Jeremiah]] x. 23. (2) Luke xii. 43, 44.
