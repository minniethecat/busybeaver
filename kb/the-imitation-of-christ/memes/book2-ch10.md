[035-001] Of gratitude for the [[grace|Grace]] of God

[035-002] Why seekest thou rest when thou art born to labour? Prepare thyself for

[035-003] patience more than for comforts, and for [[cross|bearing the cross]] more than

[035-004] for joy. For who among the men of this world would not gladly receive

[035-005] [[comfort|consolation]] and spiritual joy if he might always have it? For spiritual

[035-006] comforts exceed all the delights of [[world|the world]], and all the pleasures of

[035-007] the flesh. For all worldly delights are either empty or unclean, whilst

[035-008] spiritual delights alone are pleasant and honourable, the offspring of

[035-009] virtue, and poured forth by God into pure minds. But no man can always

[035-010] enjoy these divine comforts at his own will, because the season of

[035-011] temptation ceaseth not for long.

[035-012] 2. Great is the difference between a visitation from above and false

[035-013] liberty of spirit and great confidence in self. God doeth well in

[035-014] giving us the grace of comfort, but man doeth ill in not immediately

[035-015] giving God thanks thereof. And thus the gifts of grace are not able to

[035-016] flow unto us, because we are ungrateful to the Author of them, and

[035-017] return them not wholly to the Fountain whence they flow. For grace ever

[035-018] becometh the portion of him who is grateful and that is taken away from

[035-019] the proud, which is wont to be given to the humble.

[035-020] 3. I desire no consolation which taketh away from me [[contrition|compunction]], I

[035-021] love no contemplation which leadeth to pride. For all that is high is

[035-022] not holy, nor is everything that is sweet good; every desire is not

[035-023] pure; nor is everything that is dear to us pleasing unto God. Willingly

[035-024] do I accept that grace whereby I am made humbler and more wary and more

[035-025] ready to renounce myself. He who is made learned by the gift of grace

[035-026] and taught wisdom by the stroke of the withdrawal thereof, will not

[035-027] dare to claim any good thing for himself, but will rather confess that

[035-028] he is poor and needy. Give unto God the thing which is God’s,(1) and

[035-029] ascribe to thyself that which is thine; that is, give thanks unto God

[035-030] for His grace, but for thyself alone confess thy [[sin|fault]], and that thy

[035-031] punishment is deserved for thy fault.

[035-032] 4. Sit thou down always in the lowest room and thou shalt be given the

[035-033] highest place.(2) For the highest cannot be without the lowest. For the

[035-034] highest saints of God are least in their own sight, and the more

[035-035] glorious they are, so much the lowlier are they in themselves; full of

[035-036] grace and [[glory|heavenly glory]], they are not desirous of vain-glory; resting

[035-037] on God and strong in His might, they cannot be lifted up in any wise.

[035-038] And they who ascribe unto God all the good which they have received,

[035-039] “seek not glory one of another, but the glory which cometh from God

[035-040] only,” and they desire that God shall be praised in Himself and in all

[035-041] His Saints above all things, and they are always striving for this very

[035-042] thing.

[035-043] 5. Be thankful, therefore, for the least benefit and thou shalt be

[035-044] worthy to receive greater. Let the least be unto thee even as the

[035-045] greatest, and let that which is of little account be unto thee as a

[035-046] special gift. If the majesty of the Giver be considered, nothing that

[035-047] is given shall seem small and of no worth, for that is not a small

[035-048] thing which is given by the Most High God. Yea, though He gave

[035-049] punishment and stripes, we ought to be thankful, because He ever doth

[035-050] for our profit whatever He suffereth to come upon us. He who seeketh to

[035-051] retain the favour of God, let him be thankful for the favour which is

[035-052] given, and patient in respect of that which is taken away. Let him pray

[035-053] that it may return; let him be wary and humble that he lose it not.

[035-054] (1) Matthew xxii. 21. (2) Luke xiv. 10.
