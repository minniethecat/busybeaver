# Golgotha

Golgotha, "the place of a skull" (Matthew 27:33), is the site outside the walls of Jerusalem where Jesus Christ was crucified. In Latin tradition it is called Calvary (from *calvaria*, "skull"). It is the geographical centre of the Passion — the place where the Son of God "became obedient unto death, even the death of the cross" (Philippians 2:8).

## Biblical Foundation

All four Gospels identify Golgotha as the place of crucifixion (Matthew 27:33; Mark 15:22; Luke 23:33; John 19:17). Jesus was led there bearing His own cross, though Simon of Cyrene was compelled to carry it part of the way. At Golgotha, Jesus was nailed to the cross between two thieves, mocked by the passers-by, and cried out "My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?" (Matthew 27:46). After three hours of darkness, He "cried with a loud voice, and gave up the ghost" (Mark 15:37). The veil of the Temple was torn, the earth quaked, and the centurion declared, "Truly this man was the Son of God" (Mark 15:39). Golgotha thus becomes the altar of the New Covenant, where Christ the High Priest offers Himself as the perfect [[sacrifice]] for sin.

## In The Imitation of Christ

Thomas a Kempis does not name Golgotha, but the cross that was raised there is the dominant image of his spirituality. The entire Imitation is, in one sense, a meditation on what happened at Golgotha and what it means for the disciple who would follow Christ.

> The whole life of Christ was a cross and martyrdom, and dost thou seek for thyself rest and joy? [037-060]

The cross is not one theme among many; it is the interpretive key to the whole spiritual life. Every trial, every temptation, every act of [[self-denial]] is a return to Golgotha:

> Thus thou shalt ever find thy cross. For thou shalt either feel pain of body, or tribulation of spirit. [037-030]

> If thou bear the cross willingly, it will bear thee and lead thee to the desired end. [037-011]

> If thou also mayest bear thy cross and mayest love to be crucified with Jesus, thou shalt be a worthy disciple of Him who was crucified for thee. [037-019]

Golgotha is not merely a historical location; it is the pattern of the Christian life — the place where self-will is crucified so that the soul may rise with Christ. The royal road of the cross is the path every disciple must walk, and the cross is "the way of blessedness and of true light" (093-047). In the Kempian vision, Golgotha becomes daily reality: every act of [[patience]], every bearing of [[suffering]], every small [[sacrifice]] is a step up that hill, following the One who walked it first.

## Symbolic Meaning

In the Christian spiritual tradition, Golgotha represents the intersection of divine love and human sin — the place where God's redemptive purpose is accomplished through the ultimate act of self-giving. It is also the place of reversal: where the world sees defeat, faith sees victory; where the world sees shame, faith sees glory. For the disciple of The Imitation, Golgotha is not a distant historical site but a daily reality — every act of self-denial, every patient bearing of suffering, is a return to Golgotha in the footsteps of Christ.
