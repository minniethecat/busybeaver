[004-001] Of [[prudence|prudence in action]]

[004-002] We must not [[trust-in-god|trust]] every word of others or feeling within ourselves, but

[004-003] cautiously and patiently try the matter, whether it be of God.

[004-004] Unhappily we are so weak that we find it easier to believe and speak

[004-005] evil of others, rather than good. But they that are perfect, do not

[004-006] give ready heed to every news-bearer, for they know man’s weakness that

[004-007] it is prone to evil and unstable in words.

[004-008] 2. This is great wisdom, not to be hasty in action, or stubborn in our

[004-009] own opinions. A part of this wisdom also is not to believe every word

[004-010] we hear, nor to tell others all that we hear, even though we believe

[004-011] it. Take counsel with a man who is wise and of a [[conscience|good conscience]]; and

[004-012] seek to be instructed by one better than thyself, rather than to follow

[004-013] thine own inventions. A good life maketh a man wise toward God, and

[004-014] giveth him experience in many things. The more humble a man is in

[004-015] himself, and the more obedient towards God, the wiser will he be in all

[004-016] things, and the more shall his soul be at peace.
