[083-001] Of having [[trust-in-god|confidence in God]] when evil words are cast at us

[083-002] “My Son, stand fast and believe in Me. For what are words but words?

[083-003] They fly through the air, but they bruise no stone. If thou are guilty,

[083-004] think how thou wouldst gladly amend thyself; if thou knowest nothing

[083-005] against thyself, consider that thou wilt gladly bear this for God’s

[083-006] sake. It is little enough that thou sometimes hast to bear hard words,

[083-007] for thou art not yet able to bear hard blows. And wherefore do such

[083-008] trivial matters go to thine heart, except that thou art yet carnal, and

[083-009] regardest men more than thou oughtest? For because thou fearest to be

[083-010] despised, thou art unwilling to be reproved for thy faults, and seekest

[083-011] paltry shelters of excuses.

[083-012] 2. “But look better into thyself, and thou shalt know that [[world|the world]] is

[083-013] still alive in thee, and the vain love of pleasing men. For when thou

[083-014] fleest away from being abased and confounded for thy faults, it is

[083-015] plain that thou art neither truly humble nor truly dead to the world,

[083-016] and that the world is not crucified to thee. But hearken to My word,

[083-017] and thou shalt not care for ten thousand words of men. Behold, if all

[083-018] things could be said against thee which the utmost malice could invent,

[083-019] what should it hurt thee if thou wert altogether to let it go, and make

[083-020] no more account of it than of a mote? Could it pluck out a single hair

[083-021] of thy head?

[083-022] 3. “But he that hath no heart within him, and hath not God before his

[083-023] eyes, is easily moved by a word of reproach; but he who trusteth in Me,

[083-024] and seeketh not to abide by his own judgment, shall be free from the

[083-025] fear of men. For I am the Judge and the Discerner of all secrets; I

[083-026] know how the thing hath been done; I know both the injurer and the

[083-027] bearer. From Me went forth that word, by My permission this hath

[083-028] happened, that the thoughts of many hearts may be revealed.(1) I shall

[083-029] judge the guilty and the innocent; but beforehand I have willed to try

[083-030] them both by a secret judgment.

[083-031] 4. “The testimony of men often deceiveth. My judgment is true; it will

[083-032] stand, and it shall not be overturned. It commonly lieth hid, and only

[083-033] to few in certain cases is it made known; yet it never erreth, nor can

[083-034] err, although it seem not right to the eyes of foolish men. To Me,

[083-035] therefore, must men have recourse in all judgment, and must not lean to

[083-036] their opinion. For there shall no evil happen to the just,(2)

[083-037] whatsoever may be sent to him by God. Even though some unjust charge be

[083-038] brought against him, he will care little; nor, again, will he exult

[083-039] above measure, if through others he be clearly vindicated. For he

[083-040] considereth that I am He who try the hearts and reins,(3) who judge not

[083-041] outwardly and according to human appearance; for often in Mine eyes

[083-042] that is found blameworthy which in the judgment of men is held worthy

[083-043] of praise.”

[083-044] 5. O [[jesus-christ|Lord]] God, O Judge, just, strong, and patient, who knowest the

[083-045] frailty and sinfulness of men, be Thou my strength and my whole

[083-046] confidence; for my own conscience sufficeth me not. Thou knowest what I

[083-047] know not; and therefore ought I under all rebuke to humble myself, and

[083-048] to bear it meekly. Therefore mercifully forgive me as often as I have

[083-049] not done this, and grant me the next time the grace of greater

[083-050] [[patience|endurance]]. For better unto me is Thine abundant pity for the attainment

[083-051] of Thy [[forgiveness|pardon]], than the [[justice|righteousness]] which I believe myself to have

[083-052] for defence against my conscience, which lieth wait against me.

[083-053] Although I know nothing against myself, yet I am not hereby

[083-054] justified,(4) because if Thy mercy were removed away, in Thy sight

[083-055] should no man living be justified.(5)

[083-056] (1) Luke ii. 35. (2) Proverbs xii. 21. (3) Psalm vii. 9. (4) 1

[083-057] Corinthians iv. 4. (5) Psalm cxliii. 2.
