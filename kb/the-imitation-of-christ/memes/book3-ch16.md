[053-001] That true solace is to be sought in God alone

[053-002] Whatsoever I am able to desire or to think of for my solace, I look for

[053-003] it not here, but hereafter. For if I alone had all the solaces of this

[053-004] world, and were able to enjoy all its delights, it is certain that they

[053-005] could not endure long. Wherefore, O my soul, thou canst be fully

[053-006] comforted and perfectly refreshed, only in God, [[holy-spirit|the Comforter]] of the

[053-007] poor, and the lifter up of the humble. Wait but a little while, my

[053-008] soul, wait for the Divine promise, and thou shalt have abundance of all

[053-009] good things in heaven. If thou longest too inordinately for the things

[053-010] which are now, thou shalt lose those which are eternal and heavenly.

[053-011] Let temporal things be in the use, eternal things in the desire. Thou

[053-012] canst not be satisfied with any temporal good, for thou wast not

[053-013] created for the enjoyment of these.

[053-014] 2. Although thou hadst all the good things which ever were created, yet

[053-015] couldst not thou be happy and blessed; all thy blessedness and thy

[053-016] felicity lieth in God who created all things; not such felicity as

[053-017] seemeth good to the foolish lover of [[world|the world]], but such as [[jesus-christ|Christ]]’s

[053-018] good and faithful servants wait for, and as the spiritual and pure in

[053-019] heart sometimes taste, whose conversation is in heaven.(1) All human

[053-020] solace is empty and short-lived; blessed and true is that solace which

[053-021] is felt inwardly, springing from the truth. The godly man everywhere

[053-022] beareth about with him his own Comforter, Jesus, and saith unto Him:

[053-023] “Be with me, Lord Jesus, always and everywhere. Let it be my comfort to

[053-024] be able to give up cheerfully all human comfort. And if Thy [[comfort|consolation]]

[053-025] fail me, let Thy will and righteous approval be alway with me for the

[053-026] highest comfort. _For Thou wilt not always be chiding, neither keepest

[053-027] Thou Thine anger for ever_.”(2)

[053-028] (1) Philippians iii. 20. (2) Psalm ciii. 9.
