[101-001] Of the dignity of this Sacrament, and of the office of the priest

[101-002] The Voice of [[jesus-christ|the Beloved]]

[101-003] If thou hadst angelic purity and the holiness of holy John the Baptist,

[101-004] thou wouldest not be worthy to receive or to minister this Sacrament.

[101-005] For this is not deserved by merit of man that a man should consecrate

[101-006] and minister the Sacrament of Christ, and take for food the bread of

[101-007] Angels. Vast is the mystery, and great is the dignity of the priests,

[101-008] to whom is given what is not granted to Angels. For priests only,

[101-009] rightly ordained in the church, have the power of consecrating and

[101-010] celebrating the Body of Christ. The priest indeed is the minister of

[101-011] God, using the Word of God by God’s command and institution;

[101-012] nevertheless God is there the principal Author and invisible Worker,

[101-013] that to whom all that He willeth is subject, and all He commandeth is

[101-014] obedient.

[101-015] 2. Therefore thou must believe God Almighty in this most excellent

[101-016] Sacrament, more than thine own sense or any visible sign at all. And

[101-017] therefore with fear and reverence is this work to be approached. Take

[101-018] heed therefore and see what it is of which the ministry is committed to

[101-019] thee by the laying on of the Bishop’s hand. Behold thou art made a

[101-020] priest and art consecrated to celebrate. See now that thou do it before

[101-021] God faithfully and devoutly at due time, and shew thyself without

[101-022] blame. Thou hast not lightened thy burden, but art now bound with a

[101-023] straiter bond of discipline, and art pledged to a higher degree of

[101-024] holiness. A priest ought to be adorned with all virtues and to afford

[101-025] to others an example of good life. His conversation must not be with

[101-026] the popular and common ways of men, but with Angels in [[heaven|Heaven]] or with

[101-027] perfect men on earth.

[101-028] 3. A priest clad in holy garments taketh Christ’s place that he may

[101-029] pray unto God with all [[prayer|supplication]] and humility for himself and for

[101-030] the whole people. He must always remember the [[crucifixion|Passion of Christ]]. He

[101-031] must diligently look upon Christ’s footsteps and fervently endeavour

[101-032] himself to follow them. He must bear meekly for God whatsoever ills are

[101-033] brought upon him by others. He must mourn for his own [[sin|sins]], and for the

[101-034] sins committed by others, and may not grow careless of prayer and holy

[101-035] oblation, until he prevail to obtain grace and [[forgiveness|mercy]]. When the priest

[101-036] celebrateth, he honoureth God, giveth joy to the Angels, buildeth up

[101-037] the Church, helpeth the living, hath communion with the departed, and

[101-038] maketh himself a partaker of all good things.
