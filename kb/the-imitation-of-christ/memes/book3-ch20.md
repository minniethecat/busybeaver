[057-001] Of confession of our infirmity and of the miseries of this life

[057-002] _I will acknowledge my sin unto Thee;_(1) I will confess to Thee, [[jesus-christ|Lord]],

[057-003] my infirmity. It is often a small thing which casteth me down and

[057-004] maketh me sad. I resolve that I will act bravely, but when a little

[057-005] temptation cometh, immediately I am in a great strait. Wonderfully

[057-006] small sometimes is the matter whence a grievous temptation cometh, and

[057-007] whilst I imagine myself safe for a little space; when I am not

[057-008] considering, I find myself often almost overcome by a little puff of

[057-009] wind.

[057-010] 2. Behold, therefore, O Lord, my humility and my frailty, which is

[057-011] altogether known to Thee. Be merciful unto me, and _draw me out of the

[057-012] mire that I sink not_,(2) lest I ever remain cast down. This is what

[057-013] frequently throweth me backward and confoundeth me before Thee, that I

[057-014] am so liable to fall, so weak to resist my [[sin|passions]]. And though their

[057-015] assault is not altogether according to my will, it is violent and

[057-016] grievous, and it altogether wearieth me to live thus daily in conflict.

[057-017] Herein is my infirmity made known to me, that hateful fancies always

[057-018] rush in far more easily than they depart.

[057-019] 3. Oh that Thou, most mighty God of [[israel|Israel]], Lover of all faithful

[057-020] souls, wouldst look upon the labour and sorrow of Thy servant, and give

[057-021] him help in all things whereunto he striveth. Strengthen me with

[057-022] heavenly fortitude, lest the old man, this miserable flesh, not being

[057-023] yet fully subdued to the spirit, prevail to rule over me; against which

[057-024] I ought to strive so long as I remain in this most miserable life. Oh

[057-025] what a life is this, where tribulations and miseries cease not, where

[057-026] all things are full of snares and of enemies, for when one [[suffering|tribulation]]

[057-027] or temptation goeth, another cometh, yea, while the former conflict is

[057-028] yet raging others come more in number and unexpected.

[057-029] 4. And how can the life of man be loved, seeing that it hath so many

[057-030] bitter things, that it is subjected to so many calamities and miseries.

[057-031] How can it be even called life, when it produces so many deaths and

[057-032] plagues? The world is often reproached because it is deceitful and

[057-033] vain, yet notwithstanding it is not easily given up, because the lusts

[057-034] of the flesh have too much rule over it. Some draw us to love, some to

[057-035] hate. The lust of the flesh, the lust of the eyes, and the pride of

[057-036] life, these draw to love of [[world|the world]]; but the punishments and miseries

[057-037] which righteously follow these things, bring forth hatred of the world

[057-038] and weariness.

[057-039] 5. But, alas! an evil desire conquereth a mind given to the world, and

[057-040] thinketh it [[blessedness|happiness]] to be under the nettles(3) because it savoureth

[057-041] not nor perceiveth the sweetness of God nor the inward gracefulness of

[057-042] virtue. But they who perfectly despise the world and strive to live

[057-043] unto God in holy discipline, these are not ignorant of the divine

[057-044] sweetness promised to all who truly deny themselves and see clearly how

[057-045] grievously the world erreth, and in how many ways it is deceived.

[057-046] (1) Psalm xxxii. 5. (2) Psalm lxix. 14. (3) [[job|Job]] xxx. 7.
