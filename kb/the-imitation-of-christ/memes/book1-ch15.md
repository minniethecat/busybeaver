[015-001] Of works of [[love|charity]]

[015-002] For no worldly good whatsoever, and for the love of no man, must

[015-003] anything be done which is evil, but for the help of the suffering a

[015-004] good work must sometimes be postponed, or be changed for a better; for

[015-005] herein a good work is not destroyed, but improved. Without charity no

[015-006] work profiteth, but whatsoever is done in charity, however small and of

[015-007] no reputation it be, bringeth forth good fruit; for God verily

[015-008] considereth what a man is able to do, more than the greatness of what

[015-009] he doth.

[015-010] 2. He doth much who loveth much. He doth much who doth well. He doth

[015-011] well who ministereth to the public good rather than to his own.

[015-012] Oftentimes that seemeth to be charity which is rather carnality,

[015-013] because it springeth from [[nature|natural inclination]], self-will, hope of

[015-014] repayment, desire of gain.

[015-015] 3. He who hath true and [[charity|perfect charity]], in no wise seeketh his own

[015-016] good, but desireth that God alone be altogether glorified. He envieth

[015-017] none, because he longeth for no selfish joy; nor doth he desire to

[015-018] rejoice in himself, but longeth to be blessed in God as the highest

[015-019] good. He ascribeth good to none save to God only, the Fountain whence

[015-020] all good proceedeth, and the End, the [[peace|Peace]], the joy of all Saints. Oh,

[015-021] he who hath but a spark of true charity, hath verily learned that all

[015-022] [[world|worldly things]] are full of vanity.
