[090-001] That the [[grace|Grace]] of God doth not join itself to those who mind earthly

[090-002] things

[090-003] “My Son, precious is My grace, it suffereth not itself to be joined

[090-004] with outward things, nor with earthly consolations. Therefore thou

[090-005] oughtest to cast away all things which hinder grace, if thou longest to

[090-006] receive the inpouring thereof. Seek a secret place for thyself, love to

[090-007] dwell alone with thyself, desire the conversation of no one; but rather

[090-008] pour out thy devout prayer to God, that thou mayest possess a contrite

[090-009] mind and a [[conscience|pure conscience]]. Count the whole world as nought; seek to be

[090-010] alone with God before all outward things. For thou canst not be alone

[090-011] with Me, and at the same time be delighted with transitory things. Thou

[090-012] oughtest to be separated from thy acquaintances and dear friends, and

[090-013] keep thy mind free from all worldly comfort. So the blessed [[apostles|Apostle]]

[090-014] Peter beseecheth, that [[jesus-christ|Christ]]’s faithful ones bear themselves in this

[090-015] world as strangers and pilgrims.(1)

[090-016] 2. “Oh how great a confidence shall there be to the dying man whom no

[090-017] affection to anything detaineth in [[world|the world]]? But to have a heart so

[090-018] separated from all things, a sickly soul doth not yet comprehend, nor

[090-019] doth the carnal man know the liberty of the spiritual man. But if

[090-020] indeed he desire to be spiritually minded, he must renounce both those

[090-021] who are far off, and those who are near, and to beware of no man more

[090-022] than himself. If thou perfectly conquer thyself, very easily shalt thou

[090-023] subdue all things besides. Perfect victory is the triumph over oneself.

[090-024] For whoso keepeth himself in [[obedience|subjection]], in such manner that the

[090-025] sensual affections obey the reason, and the reason in all things

[090-026] obeyeth Me, he truly is conqueror of himself, and lord of the world.

[090-027] 3. “If thou desire to climb to this height, thou oughtest to start

[090-028] bravely, and to lay the axe to the root, to the end that thou mayest

[090-029] pull up and destroy the hidden inordinate inclination towards thyself,

[090-030] and towards all selfish and earthly good. From this sin, that a man

[090-031] loveth himself too inordinately, almost everything hangeth which

[090-032] needeth to be utterly overcome: when that evil is conquered and put

[090-033] under foot, there shall be great peace and [[peace|tranquillity]] continually.

[090-034] But because few strive earnestly to die perfectly to themselves, and do

[090-035] not heartily go forth from themselves, therefore do they remain

[090-036] entangled in themselves, and cannot be raised in spirit above

[090-037] themselves. But he who desireth to walk at liberty with Me, must of

[090-038] necessity mortify all his evil and inordinate affections, and must

[090-039] cling to no creature with selfish love.”

[090-040] (1) 1 Peter ii. 11.
