[089-001] That a man ought not to reckon himself worthy of [[comfort|consolation]], but more

[089-002] worthy of chastisement

[089-003] O [[jesus-christ|Lord]], I am not worthy of Thy consolation, nor of any spiritual

[089-004] visitation; and therefore Thou dealest justly with me, when Thou

[089-005] leavest me poor and desolate. For if I were able to pour forth tears

[089-006] like the sea, still should I not be worthy of Thy consolation.

[089-007] Therefore am I nothing worthy save to be scourged and punished, because

[089-008] I have grievously and many a time offended Thee, and in many things

[089-009] have greatly sinned. Therefore, true account being taken, I am not

[089-010] worthy even of the least of Thy consolations. But Thou, gracious and

[089-011] merciful God, who willest not that Thy works should perish, to show

[089-012] forth the riches of Thy [[forgiveness|mercy]] upon the vessels of mercy,(1) vouchsafest

[089-013] even beyond all his own deserving, to comfort Thy servant above the

[089-014] measure of mankind. For Thy consolations are not like unto the

[089-015] discoursings of men.

[089-016] 2. What have I done, O Lord, that Thou shouldst bestow any heavenly

[089-017] comfort upon me? I remember not that I have done any good, but have

[089-018] been ever prone to sin and slow to amendment. It is true and I cannot

[089-019] deny it. If I should say otherwise, Thou wouldst rise up against me,

[089-020] and there would be none to defend me. What have I deserved for my [[sin|sins]]

[089-021] but hell and everlasting fire? In very truth I confess that I am worthy

[089-022] of all scorn and contempt, nor is it fit that I should be remembered

[089-023] among Thy faithful servants. And although I be unwilling to hear this,

[089-024] nevertheless I will for the [[truth|Truth]]’s sake, accuse myself of my sins,

[089-025] that the more readily I may prevail to be accounted worthy of Thy

[089-026] mercy.

[089-027] 3. What shall I say, guilty that I am and filled with confusion? I have

[089-028] no mouth to utter, unless it be this word alone, “I have sinned, Lord,

[089-029] I have sinned; have mercy upon me, forgive me.” Let me alone, that I

[089-030] may take comfort a little before I go whence I shall not return even to

[089-031] the land of darkness and the shadow of death.(2) What dost Thou so much

[089-032] require of a guilty and miserable sinner, as that he be contrite, and

[089-033] humble himself for his sins? In true contrition and humiliation of

[089-034] heart is begotten the hope of pardon, the troubled conscience is

[089-035] reconciled, lost grace is recovered, a man is preserved from the wrath

[089-036] to come, and God and the penitent soul hasten to meet each other with a

[089-037] holy kiss.(3)

[089-038] 4. The [[contrition|humble contrition]] of sinners is an acceptable sacrifice unto

[089-039] Thee, O Lord, sending forth a smell sweeter far in Thy sight than the

[089-040] incense. This also is that pleasant ointment which Thou wouldst have

[089-041] poured upon Thy sacred feet, for a broken and contrite heart Thou hast

[089-042] never despised.(4) There is the place of refuge from the wrathful

[089-043] countenance of the enemy. There is amended and washed away whatsoever

[089-044] evil hath elsewhere been contracted.

[089-045] (1) Romans ix. 23. (2) [[job|Job]] x. 20, 21. (3) Luke xv. 20. (4) Psalm li.

[089-046] 17.
