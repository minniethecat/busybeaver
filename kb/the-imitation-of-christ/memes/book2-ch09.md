[034-001] Of the lack of all comfort

[034-002] It is no hard thing to despise human comfort when divine is present. It

[034-003] is a great thing, yea very great, to be able to bear the loss both of

[034-004] human and [[consolation|divine comfort]]; and for the love of God willingly to bear

[034-005] exile of heart, and in nought to seek oneself, nor to look to one’s own

[034-006] merit. What great matter is it, if thou be cheerful of heart and devout

[034-007] when favour cometh to thee? That is an hour wherein all rejoice.

[034-008] Pleasantly enough doth he ride whom the [[grace|grace of God]] carrieth. And what

[034-009] marvel, if he feeleth no burden who is carried by the Almighty, and is

[034-010] led onwards by the Guide from on high?

[034-011] 2. We are willing to accept anything for comfort, and it is difficult

[034-012] for a man to be freed from himself. The holy martyr Laurence overcame

[034-013] the love of [[world|the world]] and even of his priestly master, because he

[034-014] despised everything in the world which seemed to be pleasant; and for

[034-015] the love of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] he calmly suffered even God’s chief priest, Sixtus,

[034-016] whom he dearly loved, to be taken from him. Thus by the love of the

[034-017] Creator he overcame the love of man, and instead of human comfort he

[034-018] chose rather God’s good pleasure. So also learn thou to resign any near

[034-019] and beloved friend for the love of God. Nor take it amiss when thou

[034-020] hast been deserted by a friend, knowing that we must all be parted from

[034-021] one another at last.

[034-022] 3. Mightily and long must a man strive within himself before he learn

[034-023] altogether to overcome himself, and to draw his whole affection towards

[034-024] God. When a man resteth upon himself, he easily slippeth away unto

[034-025] human comforts. But a true lover of Christ, and a diligent seeker after

[034-026] virtue, falleth not back upon those comforts, nor seeketh such

[034-027] sweetness as may be tasted and handled, but desireth rather hard

[034-028] exercises, and to undertake severe labours for Christ.

[034-029] 4. When, therefore, [[consolation|spiritual comfort]] is given by God, receive it with

[034-030] giving of thanks, and know that it is the gift of God, not thy desert.

[034-031] Be not lifted up, rejoice not overmuch nor foolishly presume, but

[034-032] rather be more humble for the gift, more wary and more careful in all

[034-033] thy doings; for that hour will pass away, and temptation will follow.

[034-034] When comfort is taken from thee, do not straightway despair, but wait

[034-035] for the heavenly visitation with humility and patience, for God is able

[034-036] to give thee back greater favour and consolation. This is not new nor

[034-037] strange to those who have made [[temptation|trial]] of the way of God, for with the

[034-038] great saints and the ancient prophets there was often this manner of

[034-039] change.

[034-040] 5. Wherefore one said when the favour of God was present with him, I

[034-041] said in my prosperity I shall never be moved,(1) but he goeth on to say

[034-042] what he felt within himself when the favour departed: Thou didst turn

[034-043] Thy face from me, and I was troubled. In spite whereof he in no wise

[034-044] despaireth, but the more instantly entreateth God, and saith, Unto

[034-045] Thee, O Lord, will I cry, and will pray unto my God; and then he

[034-046] receiveth the fruit of his prayer, and testifieth how he hath been

[034-047] heard, saying, The Lord heard me and had [[forgiveness|mercy]] upon me, the Lord was my

[034-048] helper. But wherein? Thou hast turned my heaviness into joy, Thou hast

[034-049] put off my sackcloth and girded me with gladness. If it was thus with

[034-050] the great saints, we who are poor and needy ought not to despair if we

[034-051] are sometimes in the warmth and sometimes in the cold, for [[holy-spirit|the Spirit]]

[034-052] cometh and goeth according to the good pleasure of His will. Wherefore

[034-053] [[job|holy Job]] saith, Thou dost visit him in the morning, and suddenly Thou

[034-054] dost prove him.(2)

[034-055] 6. Whereupon then can I hope, or wherein may I [[trust-in-god|trust]], save only in the

[034-056] great mercy of God, and the hope of heavenly grace? For whether good

[034-057] men are with me, godly brethren or faithful friends, whether holy books

[034-058] or beautiful discourses, whether sweet hymns and songs, all these help

[034-059] but little, and have but little savour when I am deserted by God’s

[034-060] favour and left to mine own poverty. There is no better remedy, then,

[034-061] than patience and [[self-denial|denial of self]], and an abiding in the will of God.

[034-062] 7. I have never found any man so religious and godly, but that he felt

[034-063] sometimes a withdrawal of the divine favour, and lack of [[devotion|fervour]]. No

[034-064] saint was ever so filled with rapture, so enlightened, but that sooner

[034-065] or later he was tempted. For he is not worthy of the great vision of

[034-066] God, who, for God’s sake, hath not been exercised by some temptation.

[034-067] For temptation is wont to go before as a sign of the comfort which

[034-068] shall follow, and heavenly comfort is promised to those who are proved

[034-069] by temptation. As it is written, To him that overcometh I will give to

[034-070] eat of the tree of life.(3)

[034-071] 8. Divine comfort is given that a man may be stronger to bear

[034-072] adversities. And temptation followeth, lest he be lifted up because of

[034-073] the benefit. The devil sleepeth not; thy flesh is not yet dead;

[034-074] therefore, cease thou not to make thyself ready unto the battle, for

[034-075] enemies stand on thy right hand and on thy left, and they are never at

[034-076] rest.

[034-077] (1) Psalm xxx. 6. (2) Job vii. 18. (3) Revelation ii. 7.
