[058-001] That we must rest in God above all goods and gifts

[058-002] Above all things and in all things thou shalt rest alway in the [[jesus-christ|Lord]], O

[058-003] my soul, for he himself is the eternal rest of the saints. Grant me,

[058-004] most sweet and loving Jesus, to rest in Thee above every creature,

[058-005] above all health and beauty, above all glory and honour, above all

[058-006] power and dignity, above all knowledge and skilfulness, above all

[058-007] riches and arts, above all joy and exultation, above all fame and

[058-008] praise, above all sweetness and [[comfort|consolation]], above all hope and

[058-009] promise, above all merit and desire, above all gifts and rewards which

[058-010] Thou canst give and pour forth, above all joy and jubilation which the

[058-011] mind is able to receive and feel; in a word, above Angels and

[058-012] Archangels and all the army of heaven, above all things visible and

[058-013] invisible, and above everything which Thou, O my God, art not.

[058-014] 2. For Thou, O Lord, my God, art best above all things; Thou only art

[058-015] the Most High, Thou only the Almighty, Thou only the All-sufficient,

[058-016] and the Fulness of all things; Thou only the All-delightsome and the

[058-017] All-comforting; Thou alone the altogether lovely and altogether loving;

[058-018] Thou alone the Most Exalted and Most Glorious above all things; in Whom

[058-019] all things are, and were, and ever shall be, altogether and

[058-020] all-perfect. And thus it falleth short and is insufficient whatsoever

[058-021] Thou givest to me without Thyself or whatsoever Thou revealest or dost

[058-022] promise concerning Thyself, whilst Thou art not seen or fully

[058-023] possessed: since verily my heart cannot truly rest nor be entirely

[058-024] content, except it rest in Thee, and go beyond all gifts and every

[058-025] creature.

[058-026] 3. O my most beloved Spouse, Jesus Christ, most holy lover of my soul,

[058-027] Ruler of this whole [[creation|Creation]], who shall give me the wings of true

[058-028] liberty, that I may flee to Thee and find rest? Oh when shall it be

[058-029] given me to be open to receive Thee to the full, and to see how sweet

[058-030] Thou art, O Lord my God? When shall I collect myself altogether in

[058-031] Thee, that because of Thy love I may not feel myself at all, but may

[058-032] know Thee only above every sense and measure, in measure not known to

[058-033] others. But now I ofttimes groan, and bear my sad estate with sorrow;

[058-034] because many evils befall me in this vale of miseries which continually

[058-035] disturb and fill me with sorrow, and encloud me, continually hinder and

[058-036] fill me with care, allure and entangle me, that I cannot have free

[058-037] access to Thee, nor enjoy that sweet intercourse which is always near

[058-038] at hand to the blessed spirits. Let my deep sighing come before Thee,

[058-039] and my manifold desolation on the earth.

[058-040] 4. O Jesus, Light of Eternal [[glory|Glory]], solace of the wandering soul,

[058-041] before Thee my mouth is without speech, and my silence speaketh to

[058-042] Thee. How long will my Lord delay to come unto me? Let Him come unto

[058-043] me, His poor and humble one, and make me glad. Let Him put forth His

[058-044] hand, and deliver His holy one from every snare. Come, Oh come; for

[058-045] without Thee shall be no joyful day or hour, for Thou art my joy, and

[058-046] without Thee is my table empty. I am miserable, and in a manner

[058-047] imprisoned and loaded with fetters, until Thou refresh me by the light

[058-048] of Thy presence, and give me liberty, and show Thy loving countenance.

[058-049] 5. Let others seek some other thing instead of Thee, whatsoever it

[058-050] shall please them; but for my part nothing else pleaseth or shall

[058-051] please, save Thou, my God, my hope, my [[salvation|eternal salvation]]. I will not

[058-052] hold my peace, nor cease to implore, until Thy grace return, and until

[058-053] Thou speak to me within.

[058-054] 6. “Behold, here I am! Behold, I come to thee, for thou didst call Me.

[058-055] Thy tears and the longing of thy soul, thy [[humility|humbleness]] and contrition of

[058-056] heart have inclined Me, and brought Me to thee.”

[058-057] 7. And I said Lord, I have called upon Thee, and I have longed to enjoy

[058-058] Thee, being ready to reject everything for Thy sake. For Thou didst

[058-059] first move me to seek Thee. Therefore, blessed be Thou, O Lord, who has

[058-060] wrought this good work upon Thy servant, according to the multitude of

[058-061] Thy [[forgiveness|mercy]]. What then hath Thy servant to say in Thy presence, save to

[058-062] humble himself greatly before Thee, being alway mindful of his own

[058-063] [[sin|iniquity]] and vileness. For there is none like unto Thee in all marvels

[058-064] of heaven and earth. Excellent are Thy works, true are Thy judgments,

[058-065] and by Thy [[providence|Providence]] are all things governed. Therefore praise and

[058-066] glory be unto Thee, O [[wisdom|Wisdom]] of the Father, let my mouth and my soul

[058-067] and all created things praise and bless Thee together.
