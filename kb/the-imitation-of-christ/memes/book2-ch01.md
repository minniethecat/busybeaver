[026-001] Of the inward life

[026-002] The kingdom of God is within you,(1) saith the [[jesus-christ|Lord]]. Turn thee with all

[026-003] thine heart to the Lord and forsake this miserable world, and thou

[026-004] shalt find rest unto thy soul. Learn to despise outward things and to

[026-005] give thyself to things inward, and thou shalt see the kingdom of God

[026-006] come within thee. For the kingdom of God is peace and joy in the Holy

[026-007] Ghost, and it is not given to the wicked. Christ will come to thee, and

[026-008] show thee His [[comfort|consolation]], if thou prepare a worthy mansion for Him

[026-009] within thee. All His glory and beauty is from within, and there it

[026-010] pleaseth Him to dwell. He often visiteth the inward man and holdeth

[026-011] with him sweet discourse, giving him soothing consolation, much peace,

[026-012] friendship exceeding wonderful.

[026-013] 2. Go to, faithful soul, prepare thy heart for this bridegroom that he

[026-014] may vouchsafe to come to thee and dwell within thee, for so He saith,

[026-015] if any man loveth me he will keep my words: and my Father will love

[026-016] him, and we will come unto him and make our abode with him.(2) Give,

[026-017] therefore, place to Christ and refuse entrance to all others. When thou

[026-018] hast Christ, thou art rich, and hast sufficient. He shall be thy

[026-019] provider and faithful watchman in all things, so that thou hast no need

[026-020] to [[trust-in-god|trust]] in men, for men soon change and swiftly pass away, but Christ

[026-021] remaineth for ever and standeth by us firmly even to the end.

[026-022] 3. There is no great trust to be placed in a frail and mortal man, even

[026-023] though he be useful and dear to us, neither should much sorrow arise

[026-024] within us if sometimes he oppose and contradict us. They who are on thy

[026-025] side to-day, may to-morrow be against thee, and often are they turned

[026-026] round like the wind. Put thy whole [[faith|trust in God]] and let Him be thy fear

[026-027] and thy love, He will answer for thee Himself, and will do for thee

[026-028] what is best. Here hast thou no continuing city,(3) and wheresoever

[026-029] thou art, thou art a stranger and a pilgrim, and thou shalt never have

[026-030] rest unless thou art closely united to Christ within thee.

[026-031] 4. Why dost thou cast thine eyes hither and thither, since this is not

[026-032] the place of thy rest? In heaven ought thy habitation to be, and all

[026-033] earthly things should be looked upon as it were in the passing by. All

[026-034] things pass away and thou equally with them. Look that thou cleave not

[026-035] to them lest thou be taken with them and perish. Let thy contemplation

[026-036] be on the Most High, and let thy [[prayer|supplication]] be directed unto Christ

[026-037] without ceasing. If thou canst not behold high and heavenly things,

[026-038] rest thou in the passion of Christ and dwell willingly in His sacred

[026-039] wounds. For if thou devoutly fly to the wounds of Jesus, and the

[026-040] precious marks of the nails and the spear, thou shalt find great

[026-041] comfort in [[suffering|tribulation]], nor will the slights of men trouble thee much,

[026-042] and thou wilt easily bear their unkind words.

[026-043] 5. Christ also, when He was in [[world|the world]], was despised and rejected of

[026-044] men, and in His greatest necessity was left by His acquaintance and

[026-045] friends to bear these reproaches. Christ was willing to suffer and be

[026-046] despised, and darest thou complain of any? Christ had adversaries and

[026-047] gainsayers, and dost thou wish to have all men thy friends and

[026-048] benefactors? Whence shall thy patience attain her crown if no adversity

[026-049] befall thee? If thou art unwilling to suffer any adversity, how shalt

[026-050] thou be the friend of Christ? Sustain thyself with Christ and for

[026-051] Christ if thou wilt reign with Christ.

[026-052] 6. If thou hadst once entered into the mind of Jesus, and hadst tasted

[026-053] yea even a little of his tender love, then wouldst thou care nought for

[026-054] thine own convenience or inconvenience, but wouldst rather rejoice at

[026-055] trouble brought upon thee, because the love of Jesus maketh a man to

[026-056] despise himself. He who loveth Jesus, and is inwardly true and free

[026-057] from inordinate affections, is able to turn himself readily unto God,

[026-058] and to rise above himself in spirit, and to enjoy fruitful peace.

[026-059] 7. He who knoweth things as they are and not as they are said or seem

[026-060] to be, he truly is wise, and is taught of God more than of men. He who

[026-061] knoweth how to walk from within, and to set little value upon outward

[026-062] things, requireth not places nor waiteth for seasons, for holding his

[026-063] intercourse with God. The inward man quickly recollecteth himself,

[026-064] because he is never entirely given up to outward things. No outward

[026-065] labour and no necessary occupations stand in his way, but as events

[026-066] fall out, so doth he fit himself to them. He who is rightly disposed

[026-067] and ordered within careth not for the strange and perverse conduct of

[026-068] men. A man is hindered and distracted in so far as he is moved by

[026-069] outward things.

[026-070] 8. If it were well with thee, and thou wert purified from evil, all

[026-071] things would work together for thy good and profiting. For this cause

[026-072] do many things displease thee and often trouble thee, that thou art not

[026-073] yet perfectly dead to thyself nor separated from all earthly things.

[026-074] Nothing so defileth and entangleth the heart of man as impure love

[026-075] towards [[creation|created things]]. If thou rejectest outward comfort thou wilt be

[026-076] able to contemplate heavenly things and frequently to be joyful

[026-077] inwardly.

[026-078] (1) Luke xvii. 21. (2) John xiv. 23. (3) Hebrews xiii. 14.
