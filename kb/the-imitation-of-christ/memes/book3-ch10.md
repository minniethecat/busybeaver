[047-001] That it is sweet to despise [[world|the world]] and to serve God

[047-002] Now will I speak again, O my [[jesus-christ|Lord]], and hold not my peace; I will say in

[047-003] the ears of my God, my Lord, and my King, who is exalted above all, _Oh

[047-004] how plentiful is Thy goodness which Thou hast laid up for them that

[047-005] fear Thee!_(1) But what art Thou to those who love Thee? What to those

[047-006] who serve Thee with their whole heart? Truly unspeakable is the

[047-007] sweetness of the contemplation of Thee, which Thou bestowest upon those

[047-008] who love Thee. In this most of all Thou hast showed me the sweetness of

[047-009] Thy [[love|charity]], that when I was not, Thou madest me, and when I wandered

[047-010] far from Thee, Thou broughtest me back that I might serve Thee, and

[047-011] commandedst me to love Thee.

[047-012] 2. O Fountain of perpetual love, what shall I say concerning Thee? How

[047-013] shall I be unmindful of Thee, who didst vouchsafe to remember me, even

[047-014] after I pined away and perished? Thou hast had [[forgiveness|mercy]] beyond all hope

[047-015] upon Thy servant, and hast showed Thy grace and friendship beyond all

[047-016] deserving. What reward shall I render Thee for this Thy grace? For it

[047-017] is not given unto all to renounce this world and its affairs, and to

[047-018] take up a religious life. For is it a great thing that I should serve

[047-019] Thee, whom every creature ought to serve? It ought not to seem a great

[047-020] thing to me to serve Thee; but rather this appeareth to me a great and

[047-021] wonderful thing, that Thou vouchsafest to receive as Thy servant one so

[047-022] poor and unworthy, and to join him unto Thy chosen servants.

[047-023] 3. Behold all things which I have are Thine, and with them I serve

[047-024] Thee. And yet verily it is Thou who servest me, rather than I Thee.

[047-025] Behold the heaven and the earth which Thou hast created for the service

[047-026] of men; they are at Thy bidding, and perform daily whatsoever Thou dost

[047-027] command. Yea, and this is little; for Thou hast even ordained the

[047-028] Angels for the service of man. But it surpasseth even all these things,

[047-029] that Thou Thyself didst vouchsafe to minister unto man, and didst

[047-030] promise that Thou wouldest give Thyself unto him.

[047-031] 4. What shall I render unto Thee for all these Thy manifold mercies? Oh

[047-032] that I were able to serve Thee all the days of my life! Oh that even

[047-033] for one day I were enabled to do Thee service worthy of Thyself! For

[047-034] verily Thou art worthy of all service, all honour, and praise without

[047-035] end. Verily Thou art my God, and I am Thy poor servant, who am bound to

[047-036] serve Thee with all my strength, nor ought I ever to grow weary of Thy

[047-037] praise. This is my wish, this is my exceeding great desire, and

[047-038] whatsoever is lacking to me, vouchsafe Thou to supply.

[047-039] 5. It is great honour, great glory to serve Thee, and to despise all

[047-040] for Thy sake. For they shall have great grace who of their own will

[047-041] shall submit themselves to Thy most holy service. They who for Thy love

[047-042] have cast away every carnal delight shall find the sweetest [[comfort|consolation]]

[047-043] of the [[holy-spirit|Holy Ghost]]. They who enter the narrow way of life for Thy Name’s

[047-044] sake, and have put away all worldly cares, shall attain great liberty

[047-045] of spirit.

[047-046] 6. Oh grateful and delightsome service of God, whereby man is made

[047-047] truly free and holy! Oh sacred condition of the religious servant,

[047-048] which maketh man equal to the Angels, well-pleasing unto God, terrible

[047-049] to evil spirits, and acceptable to all faithful ones! Oh service to be

[047-050] embraced and ever desired, in which the highest good is promised, and

[047-051] joy is gained which shall remain for evermore!

[047-052] (1) Psalm xxxi. 19.
