[028-001] Of the good, peaceable man

[028-002] First keep thyself in peace, and then shalt thou be able to be a

[028-003] peacemaker towards others. A peaceable man doth more good than a

[028-004] well-learned. A passionate man turneth even good into evil and easily

[028-005] believeth evil; a good, peaceable man converteth all things into good.

[028-006] He who dwelleth in peace is suspicious of none, but he who is

[028-007] discontented and restless is tossed with many suspicions, and is

[028-008] neither [[silence|quiet]] himself nor suffereth others to be quiet. He often saith

[028-009] what he ought not to say, and omitteth what it were more expedient for

[028-010] him to do. He considereth to what duties others are bound, and

[028-011] neglecteth those to which he is bound himself. Therefore be zealous

[028-012] first over thyself, and then mayest thou righteously be zealous

[028-013] concerning thy neighbour.

[028-014] 2. Thou knowest well how to excuse and to colour thine own deeds, but

[028-015] thou wilt not accept the excuses of others. It would be more just to

[028-016] accuse thyself and excuse thy brother. If thou wilt that others bear

[028-017] with thee, bear thou with others. Behold how far thou art as yet from

[028-018] the true [[love|charity]] and humility which knows not how to be angry or

[028-019] indignant against any save self alone. It is no great thing to mingle

[028-020] with the good and the meek, for this is naturally pleasing to all, and

[028-021] every one of us willingly enjoyeth peace and liketh best those who

[028-022] think with us: but to be able to live peaceably with the hard and

[028-023] perverse, or with the disorderly, or those who oppose us, this is a

[028-024] great grace and a thing much to be commended and most worthy of a man.

[028-025] 3. There are who keep themselves in peace and keep peace also with

[028-026] others, and there are who neither have peace nor suffer others to have

[028-027] peace; they are troublesome to others, but always more troublesome to

[028-028] themselves. And there are who hold themselves in peace, and study to

[028-029] bring others unto peace; nevertheless, all our peace in this sad life

[028-030] lieth in humble suffering rather than in not feeling adversities. He

[028-031] who best knoweth how to suffer shall possess the most peace; that man

[028-032] is conqueror of himself and lord of [[world|the world]], the friend of [[jesus-christ|Christ]],

[028-033] and the inheritor of heaven.
