[040-001] How all the words of God are to be heard with humility, and how many

[040-002] consider them not

[040-003] “My Son, hear My words, for My words are most sweet, surpassing all the

[040-004] knowledge of the philosophers and wise men of this world. My words are

[040-005] spirit, and they are life,(1) and are not to be weighed by man’s

[040-006] understanding. They are not to be drawn forth for vain approbation, but

[040-007] to be heard in silence, and to be received with all humility and with

[040-008] deep love.”

[040-009] 2. And I said, “Blessed is the man whom Thou teachest, O [[jesus-christ|Lord]], and

[040-010] instructest him in Thy law, that Thou mayest give him rest in time of

[040-011] [[suffering|adversity]],(2) and that he be not desolate in the earth.”

[040-012] 3. “I,” saith the Lord, “taught the prophets from the beginning, and

[040-013] even now cease I not to speak unto all; but many are deaf and hardened

[040-014] against My voice; many love to listen to [[world|the world]] rather than to God,

[040-015] they follow after the desires of the flesh more readily than after the

[040-016] good pleasure of God. The world promiseth things that are temporal and

[040-017] small, and it is served with great eagerness. I promise things that are

[040-018] great and eternal, and the hearts of mortals are slow to stir. Who

[040-019] serveth and obeyeth Me in all things, with such carefulness as he

[040-020] serveth the world and its rulers?

[040-021] Be thou ashamed, O Sidon, saith the sea;(3)

[040-022] And if thou reason seekest, hear thou me.

[040-023] For a little reward men make a long journey; for [[eternity|eternal life]] many will

[040-024] scarce lift a foot once from the ground. Mean reward is sought after;

[040-025] for a single piece of money sometimes there is shameful striving; for a

[040-026] thing which is vain and for a trifling promise, men shrink not from

[040-027] toiling day and night.”

[040-028] 4. “But, O shame! for an unchangeable good, for an inestimable reward,

[040-029] for the highest honour and for a glory that fadeth not away, it is

[040-030] irksome to them to toil even a little. Be thou ashamed therefore,

[040-031] slothful and discontented servant, for they are found readier unto

[040-032] perdition than thou unto life. They rejoice more heartily in vanity

[040-033] than thou in the truth. Sometimes, indeed, they are disappointed of

[040-034] their hope, but my promise faileth no man, nor sendeth away empty him

[040-035] who trusteth in Me. What I have promised I will give; what I have said

[040-036] I will fulfil; if only a man remain faithful in My love unto the end.

[040-037] Therefore am I the rewarder of all good men, and a strong approver of

[040-038] all who are godly.

[040-039] 5. “Write My words in thy heart and consider them diligently, for they

[040-040] shall be very needful to thee in time of temptation. What thou

[040-041] understandest not when thou readest, thou shalt know in the time of thy

[040-042] visitation. I am wont to visit Mine elect in twofold manner, even by

[040-043] temptation and by comfort, and I teach them two lessons day by day, the

[040-044] one in chiding their faults, the other in exhorting them to grow in

[040-045] grace. He who hath My words and rejecteth them, hath one who shall

[040-046] judge him at the last day.”

[040-047] A PRAYER FOR THE SPIRIT OF DEVOTION

[040-048] 6. O Lord my God, Thou art all my good, and who am I that I should dare

[040-049] to speak unto Thee? I am the very poorest of Thy servants, an abject

[040-050] worm, much poorer and more despicable than I know or dare to say.

[040-051] Nevertheless remember, O Lord, that I am nothing, I have nothing, and

[040-052] can do nothing. Thou only art good, just and holy; Thou canst do all

[040-053] things, art over all things, fillest all things, leaving empty only the

[040-054] sinner. Call to mind Thy tender mercies, and fill my heart with Thy

[040-055] grace, Thou who wilt not that Thy work should return to Thee void.

[040-056] 7. How can I bear this miserable life unless Thy [[forgiveness|mercy]] and grace

[040-057] strengthen me? Turn not away Thy face from me, delay not Thy

[040-058] visitation. Withdraw not Thou Thy comfort from me, lest my soul “gasp

[040-059] after thee as a thirsty land.” Lord, teach me to do Thy will, teach me

[040-060] to walk humbly and uprightly before Thee, for Thou art my wisdom, who

[040-061] knowest me in truth, and knewest me before the world was made and

[040-062] before I was born into the world.

[040-063] (1) John vi. 63. (2) Psalm xciv. 13. (3) [[isaiah|Isaiah]] xxiii. 4.
