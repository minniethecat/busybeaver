[031-001] Of the joy of a [[conscience|good conscience]]

[031-002] The testimony of a good conscience is the glory of a good man. Have a

[031-003] good conscience and thou shalt ever have joy. A good conscience is able

[031-004] to bear exceeding much, and is exceeding joyful in the midst of

[031-005] adversities; an evil conscience is ever fearful and unquiet. Thou shalt

[031-006] rest sweetly if thy heart condemn thee not. Never rejoice unless when

[031-007] thou hast done well. The wicked have never true joy, nor feel internal

[031-008] peace, for there is no peace, saith my God, to the wicked.(1) And if

[031-009] they say “we are in peace, there shall no harm happen unto us, and who

[031-010] shall dare to do us hurt?” believe them not, for suddenly shall the

[031-011] wrath of God rise up against them, and their deeds shall be brought to

[031-012] nought, and their thoughts shall perish.

[031-013] 2. To glory in [[suffering|tribulation]] is not grievous to him who loveth; for such

[031-014] glorying is glorying in the Cross of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. Brief is the glory which

[031-015] is given and received of men. Sadness always goeth hand in hand with

[031-016] the [[glory|glory of the world]]. The glory of the good is in their conscience,

[031-017] and not in the report of men. The joy of the upright is from God and in

[031-018] God, and their joy is in the truth. He who desireth true and eternal

[031-019] glory careth not for that which is temporal; and he who seeketh

[031-020] temporal glory, or who despiseth it from his heart, is proved to bear

[031-021] little love for that which is heavenly. He who careth for neither

[031-022] praises nor reproaches hath great [[peace|tranquillity]] of heart.

[031-023] 3. He will easily be contented and filled with peace, whose conscience

[031-024] is pure. Thou art none the holier if thou art praised, nor the viler if

[031-025] thou art reproached. Thou art what thou art; and thou canst not be

[031-026] better than God pronounceth thee to be. If thou considerest well what

[031-027] thou art inwardly, thou wilt not care what men will say to thee. Man

[031-028] looketh on the outward appearance, but the Lord looketh on the

[031-029] heart:(2) man looketh on the deed, but God considereth the intent. It

[031-030] is the token of a humble spirit always to do well, and to set little by

[031-031] oneself. Not to look for [[comfort|consolation]] from any created thing is a sign

[031-032] of great purity and inward [[faith|faithfulness]].

[031-033] 4. He that seeketh no outward witness on his own behalf, showeth

[031-034] plainly that he hath committed himself wholly to God. For not he that

[031-035] commendeth himself is approved, as [[apostle-paul|St. Paul]] saith, but whom the Lord

[031-036] commendeth.(3) To walk inwardly with God, and not to be held by any

[031-037] outer affections, is the state of a spiritual man.

[031-038] (1) [[isaiah|Isaiah]] lvii. 21. (2) 1 [[samuel|Samuel]] xvi. 7. (3) 2 Corinthians x. 18.
