[087-001] How a desolate man ought to commit himself into the hands of God

[087-002] O [[jesus-christ|Lord]], Holy Father, be Thou blessed now and evermore; because as Thou

[087-003] wilt so it is done, and what Thou doest is good. Let Thy servant

[087-004] rejoice in Thee, not in himself, nor in any other; because Thou alone

[087-005] art the true joy, Thou art my hope and my crown, Thou art my joy and my

[087-006] honour, O Lord. What hath Thy servant, which he received not from Thee,

[087-007] even without merit of his own? Thine are all things which Thou hast

[087-008] given, and which Thou hast made. I am poor and in misery even from my

[087-009] youth up,(1) and my soul is sorrowful unto tears, sometimes also it is

[087-010] disquieted within itself, because of the sufferings which are coming

[087-011] upon it.

[087-012] 2. I long after the joy of peace; for the peace of Thy children do I

[087-013] beseech, for in the light of Thy comfort they are fed by Thee. If Thou

[087-014] give peace, if Thou pour into me holy joy, the soul of Thy servant

[087-015] shall be full of melody, and devout in Thy praise. But if Thou withdraw

[087-016] Thyself as too often Thou art wont, he will not be able to run in the

[087-017] way of Thy [[law|commandments]], but rather he will smite his breast and will

[087-018] bow his knees; because it is not with him as yesterday and the day

[087-019] before, when _Thy candle shined upon his head_,(2) and _he walked under

[087-020] the shadow of Thy wings_,(3) from the temptations which beset him.

[087-021] 3. O Father, righteous and ever to be praised, the hour cometh when Thy

[087-022] servant is to be proved. O beloved Father, it is well that in this hour

[087-023] Thy servant suffer somewhat for Thy sake. O Father, evermore to be

[087-024] adored, as the hour cometh which Thou foreknewest from everlasting,

[087-025] when for a little while Thy servant should outwardly bow down, but

[087-026] always live inwardly with Thee; when for a little while he should be

[087-027] little regarded, humbled, and fail in the eyes of men; should be wasted

[087-028] with sufferings and weaknesses, to rise again with Thee in the dawn of

[087-029] the new light, and be glorified in the heavenly places. O Holy Father,

[087-030] thou hast ordained it so, and so hast willed it; and that is done which

[087-031] Thou Thyself hast commanded.

[087-032] 4. For this is Thy favour to Thy friend, that he should suffer and be

[087-033] troubled in [[world|the world]] for Thy love’s sake, how often soever, and by

[087-034] whomsoever and whosoever Thou hast suffered it to be done. Without Thy

[087-035] counsel and providence, and without cause, nothing cometh to pass on

[087-036] the earth. It is good for me, Lord, that I had been in trouble, that I

[087-037] may learn Thy statutes,(4) and may cast away all pride of heart and

[087-038] [[pride|presumption]]. It is profitable for me that confusion hath covered my

[087-039] face, that I may seek to Thee for [[comfort|consolation]] rather than unto men. By

[087-040] this also I have learned to dread Thine unsearchable judgment, who

[087-041] afflictest the just with the wicked, but not without equity and

[087-042] justice.

[087-043] 5. Thanks be unto Thee, because Thou hast not spared my [[sin|sins]], but hast

[087-044] beaten me with stripes of love, inflicting pains, and sending troubles

[087-045] upon me without and within. There is none who can console me, of all

[087-046] things which are under heaven, but Thou only, O Lord my God, Thou

[087-047] heavenly Physician of souls, who dost scourge and hast [[forgiveness|mercy]], who

[087-048] leadest down to hell and bringest up again.(5) Thy discipline over me,

[087-049] and Thy rod itself shall teach me.

[087-050] 6. Behold, O beloved Father, I am in Thy hands, I bow myself under the

[087-051] rod of Thy correction. Smite my back and my neck that I may bend my

[087-052] crookedness to Thy will. Make me a pious and lowly [[discipleship|disciple]], as Thou

[087-053] wert wont to be kind, that I may walk according to every nod of Thine.

[087-054] To Thee I commend myself and all that I have for correction; better is

[087-055] it to be punished here than hereafter. Thou knowest all things and each

[087-056] of them; and nothing remaineth hid from Thee in man’s conscience.

[087-057] Before they are, thou knowest that they will be, and Thou needest not

[087-058] that any man teach Thee or admonish Thee concerning the things which

[087-059] are done upon the earth. Thou knowest what is expedient for my profit,

[087-060] and how greatly trouble serveth unto the scrubbing off the rust of sin.

[087-061] Do with me according to Thy desired good pleasure, and despise not my

[087-062] life which is full of sin, known to none so entirely and fully as to

[087-063] Thee alone.

[087-064] 7. Grant me, O Lord, to know that which ought to be known; to love that

[087-065] which ought to be loved; to praise that which pleaseth Thee most, to

[087-066] esteem that which is precious in Thy sight, to blame that which is vile

[087-067] in Thine eyes. Suffer me not to judge according to the sight of bodily

[087-068] eyes, nor to give sentence according to the hearing of the ears of

[087-069] ignorant men; but to discern in true judgment between visible and

[087-070] spiritual things, and above all things to be ever seeking after the

[087-071] will of Thy good pleasure.

[087-072] 8. Oftentimes the senses of men are deceived in judging; the lovers of

[087-073] the world also are deceived in that they love only visible things. What

[087-074] is a man better because by man he is reckoned very great? The deceiver

[087-075] deceiveth the deceiver, the vain man the vain, the blind man the blind,

[087-076] the weak man the weak, when they exalt one another; and in truth they

[087-077] rather put to shame, while they foolishly praise. For as humble St.

[087-078] Francis saith, “What each one is in Thine eyes, so much he is, and no

[087-079] more.”

[087-080] (1) Psalm lxxxviii. 15. (2) [[job|Job]] xxix. 3. (3) Psalm xvii. 8. (4) Psalm

[087-081] cxix. 71. (5) Job xiii. 2.
