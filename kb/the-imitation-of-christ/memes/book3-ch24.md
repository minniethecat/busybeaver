[061-001] Of avoiding of curious inquiry into the life of another

[061-002] “My Son, be not curious, nor trouble thyself with vain cares. _What is

[061-003] that to thee? Follow thou Me._(1) For what is it to thee whether a man

[061-004] be this or that, or say or do thus or thus? Thou hast no need to answer

[061-005] for others, but thou must give an answer for thyself. Why therefore

[061-006] dost thou entangle thyself? Behold, I know all men, and I behold all

[061-007] things which are done under the sun; and I know how it standeth with

[061-008] each one, what he thinketh, what he willeth, and to what end his

[061-009] thoughts reach. All things therefore are to be committed to Me; watch

[061-010] thou thyself in godly peace, and leave him who is unquiet to be unquiet

[061-011] as he will. Whatsoever he shall do or say, shall come unto him, for he

[061-012] cannot deceive Me.

[061-013] 2. “Trouble not thyself about the shadow of a great name, nor about the

[061-014] friendship of many, nor about the love of men towards thee. For these

[061-015] things beget distraction and great sorrows of heart. My word should

[061-016] speak freely unto thee, and I would reveal secrets, if only thou didst

[061-017] diligently look for My appearing, and didst open unto Me the gates of

[061-018] thy heart. Be sober and watch unto prayer,(2) and humble thyself in all

[061-019] things.”

[061-020] (1) John xxi. 22. (2) 1 Peter iv. 7.
