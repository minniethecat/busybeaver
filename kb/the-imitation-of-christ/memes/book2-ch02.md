[027-001] Of lowly [[obedience|submission]]

[027-002] Make no great account who is for thee or against thee, but mind only

[027-003] the present duty and take care that God be with thee in whatsoever thou

[027-004] doest. Have a [[conscience|good conscience]] and God will defend thee, for he whom God

[027-005] will help no man’s perverseness shall be able to hurt. If thou knowest

[027-006] how to hold thy peace and to suffer, without doubt thou shalt see the

[027-007] help of the [[jesus-christ|Lord]]. He knoweth the time and the way to deliver thee,

[027-008] therefore must thou resign thyself to Him. To God it belongeth to help

[027-009] and to deliver from all confusion. Oftentimes it is very profitable for

[027-010] keeping us in greater humility, that others know and rebuke our faults.

[027-011] 2. When a man humbleth himself for his defects, he then easily

[027-012] pacifieth others and quickly satisfieth those that are angered against

[027-013] him. God protecteth and delivereth the humble man, He loveth and

[027-014] comforteth the humble man, to the humble man He inclineth Himself, on

[027-015] the humble He bestoweth great grace, and when he is cast down He

[027-016] raiseth him to glory: to the humble He revealeth His secrets, and

[027-017] sweetly draweth and inviteth him to Himself. The humble man having

[027-018] received reproach, is yet in sufficient peace, because he resteth on

[027-019] God and not on [[world|the world]]. Reckon not thyself to have profited in

[027-020] anywise unless thou feel thyself to be inferior to all.
