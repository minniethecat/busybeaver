[009-001] Of [[obedience|Obedience]] and Subjection

[009-002] It is verily a great thing to live in obedience, to be under authority,

[009-003] and not to be at our own disposal. Far safer is it to live in

[009-004] subjection than in a place of authority. Many are in obedience from

[009-005] necessity rather than from love; these take it amiss, and repine for

[009-006] small cause. Nor will they gain freedom of spirit, unless with all

[009-007] their heart they submit themselves for the love of God. Though thou run

[009-008] hither and thither, thou wilt not find peace, save in humble subjection

[009-009] to the authority of him who is set over thee. Fancies about places and

[009-010] change of them have deceived many.

[009-011] 2. True it is that every man willingly followeth his own bent, and is

[009-012] the more inclined to those who agree with him. But if [[jesus-christ|Christ]] is amongst

[009-013] us, then it is necessary that we sometimes yield up our own opinion for

[009-014] the sake of peace. Who is so wise as to have perfect knowledge of all

[009-015] things? Therefore [[trust-in-god|trust]] not too much to thine own opinion, but be ready

[009-016] also to hear the opinions of others. Though thine own opinion be good,

[009-017] yet if for the love of God thou foregoest it, and followest that of

[009-018] another, thou shalt the more profit thereby.

[009-019] 3. Ofttimes I have heard that it is safer to hearken and to receive

[009-020] counsel than to give it. It may also come to pass that each opinion may

[009-021] be good; but to refuse to hearken to others when reason or occasion

[009-022] requireth it, is a mark of pride or wilfulness.
