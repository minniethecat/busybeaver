[092-001] Of the corruption of [[nature|Nature]] and the efficacy of Divine [[grace|Grace]]

[092-002] O [[jesus-christ|Lord]] my God, who hast created me after thine own image and

[092-003] similitude, grant me this grace, which Thou hast shown to be so great

[092-004] and so necessary for salvation, that I may conquer my wicked nature,

[092-005] which draweth me to sin and to perdition. For I feel in my flesh the

[092-006] law of sin, contradicting the law of my mind, and bringing me into

[092-007] captivity to the obedience of sensuality in many things; nor can I

[092-008] resist its [[sin|passions]], unless Thy most holy grace assist me, fervently

[092-009] poured into my heart.

[092-010] 2. There is need of Thy grace, yea, and of a great measure thereof,

[092-011] that my nature may be conquered, which hath alway been prone to evil

[092-012] from my youth. For being fallen through the first man Adam, and

[092-013] corrupted through sin, the punishment of this stain descended upon all

[092-014] men; so that Nature itself, which was framed good and right by Thee, is

[092-015] now used to express the vice and infirmity of corrupted Nature; because

[092-016] its motion left unto itself draweth men away to evil and to lower

[092-017] things. For the little power which remaineth is as it were one spark

[092-018] lying hid in the ashes. This is Natural reason itself, encompassed with

[092-019] thick clouds, having yet a [[discernment|discernment of good and evil]], a distinction

[092-020] of the true and the false, though it be powerless to fulfil all that it

[092-021] approveth, and possess not yet the full light of truth, nor

[092-022] healthfulness of its affections.

[092-023] 3. Hence it is, O my God, that I delight in Thy law after the inward

[092-024] man,(1) knowing that Thy commandment is holy and just and good;

[092-025] reproving also all evil, and the sin that is to be avoided: yet with

[092-026] the flesh I serve the law of sin, whilst I obey sensuality rather than

[092-027] reason. Hence it is that to will to do good is present with me, but how

[092-028] to perform it I find not.(2) Hence I ofttimes purpose many good things;

[092-029] but because grace is lacking to help mine infirmities, I fall back

[092-030] before a little resistance and fail. Hence it cometh to pass that I

[092-031] recognize the way of perfectness, and see very clearly what things I

[092-032] ought to do; but pressed down by the weight of my own corruption, I

[092-033] rise not to the things which are more perfect.

[092-034] 4. Oh how entirely necessary is Thy grace to me, O Lord, for a good

[092-035] beginning, for progress, and for bringing to perfection. For without it

[092-036] I can do nothing, but I can do all things through Thy grace which

[092-037] strengtheneth me.(3) O truly heavenly grace, without which our own

[092-038] merits are nought, and no gifts of Nature at all are to be esteemed.

[092-039] Arts, riches, beauty, strength, wit, eloquence, they all avail nothing

[092-040] before Thee, O Lord, without Thy grace. For the gifts of Nature belong

[092-041] to good and evil alike; but the proper gift of the elect is grace—that

[092-042] is, love—and they who bear the mark thereof are held worthy of

[092-043] everlasting life. So mighty is this grace, that without it neither the

[092-044] gift of prophecy nor the working of miracles, nor any speculation,

[092-045] howsoever lofty, is of any value at all. But neither faith, nor hope,

[092-046] nor any other virtue is accepted with Thee without love and grace.

[092-047] 5. O most blessed grace that makest the [[poverty-of-spirit|poor in spirit]] rich in virtues,

[092-048] and renderest him who is rich in many things humble in spirit, come

[092-049] Thou, descend upon me, fill me early with Thy [[comfort|consolation]], lest my soul

[092-050] fail through weariness and drought of mind. I beseech thee, O Lord,

[092-051] that I may find grace in Thy sight, for Thy grace is sufficient for

[092-052] me,(4) when I obtain not those things which Nature longeth for. If I be

[092-053] tempted and vexed with many tribulations, I will fear no evil, while

[092-054] Thy grace remaineth with me. This alone is my strength, this bringeth

[092-055] me counsel and help. It is more powerful than all enemies, and wiser

[092-056] than all the wise men in [[world|the world]].

[092-057] 6. It is the mistress of truth, the teacher of discipline, the light of

[092-058] the heart, the solace of anxiety, the banisher of sorrow, the deliverer

[092-059] from fear, the nurse of devotion, the drawer forth of tears. What am I

[092-060] without it, save a dry tree, a useless branch, worthy to be cast away!

[092-061] “Let Thy grace, therefore, O Lord, always prevent and follow me, and

[092-062] make me continually given to all good works, through Jesus Christ, Thy

[092-063] Son. Amen.”

[092-064] (1) Romans vii. 12, 22. 25. (2) Romans vii. 18. (3) Philippians iv. 13.

[092-065] (4) 2 Corinthians xii. 9.
