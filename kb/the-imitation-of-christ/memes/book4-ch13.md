[109-001] That the devout soul ought with the whole heart to yearn after union

[109-002] with [[jesus-christ|Christ]] in the Sacrament

[109-003] The Voice of the Disciple

[109-004] Who shall grant unto me, O Lord, that I may find Thee alone, and open

[109-005] all my heart unto Thee, and enjoy Thee as much as my soul desireth; and

[109-006] that no man may henceforth look upon me, nor any creature move me or

[109-007] have respect unto me, but Thou alone speak unto me and I unto Thee,

[109-008] even as beloved is wont to speak unto beloved, and friend to feast with

[109-009] friend? For this do I pray, this do I long for, that I may be wholly

[109-010] united unto Thee, and may withdraw my heart from all [[creation|created things]],

[109-011] and by means of [[communion|Holy Communion]] and frequent celebration may learn more

[109-012] and more to relish heavenly and eternal things. Ah, Lord God, when

[109-013] shall I be entirely united and lost in Thee, and altogether forgetful

[109-014] of myself? Thou in me, and I in Thee;(1) even so grant that we may in

[109-015] like manner continue together in one.

[109-016] 2. Verily Thou art my Beloved, the choicest among ten thousand,(2) in

[109-017] whom my soul delighteth to dwell all the days of her life. Verily Thou

[109-018] art my Peacemaker, in Whom is perfect peace and true rest, apart from

[109-019] Whom is labour and sorrow and infinite misery. Verily Thou art a God

[109-020] that hidest Thyself, and Thy counsel is not with the wicked, but Thy

[109-021] Word is with the humble and the simple. O how sweet, O Lord, is Thy

[109-022] spirit, who that Thou mightest manifest Thy sweetness towards Thy

[109-023] children, dost vouchsafe to refresh them with the bread which is full

[109-024] of sweetness, which cometh down from heaven. Verily there is no other

[109-025] nation so great, which hath its gods drawing nigh to them, as Thou, our

[109-026] God, art present unto all Thy faithful ones,(3) unto whom for their

[109-027] daily solace, and for lifting up their heart unto heaven, Thou givest

[109-028] Thyself for their food and delight.

[109-029] 3. For what other nation is there so renowned as the Christian people?

[109-030] Or what creature is so beloved under heaven as the devout soul to which

[109-031] God entereth in, that he may feed it with His glorious flesh? O

[109-032] unspeakable grace! O wonderful condescension! O immeasurable love

[109-033] specially bestowed upon men! But what reward shall I give unto the Lord

[109-034] for this grace, for [[love|charity]] so mighty? There is nothing which I am able

[109-035] to present more acceptable than to give my heart altogether unto God,

[109-036] and to join it inwardly to Him. Then all my inward parts shall rejoice,

[109-037] when my soul shall be perfectly united unto God. Then shall He say unto

[109-038] me, “If thou wilt be with Me, I will be with thee.” And I will answer

[109-039] Him, “Vouchsafe, O Lord, to abide with me, I will gladly be with Thee;

[109-040] this is my whole desire, even that my heart be united unto Thee.”

[109-041] (1) John xv. 4. (2) Cant. v. 10. (3) Deuteronomy iv. 7.
