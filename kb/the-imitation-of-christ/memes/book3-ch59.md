[096-001] That all hope and [[trust-in-god|trust]] is to be fixed in God alone

[096-002] O [[jesus-christ|Lord]], what is my trust which I have in this life, or what is my

[096-003] greatest comfort of all the things which are seen under [[heaven|Heaven]]? Is it

[096-004] not Thou, O Lord my God, whose mercies are without number? Where hath

[096-005] it been well with me without Thee? Or when could it be evil whilst Thou

[096-006] wert near? I had rather be poor for Thy sake, than rich without Thee. I

[096-007] choose rather to be a pilgrim upon the earth with Thee than without

[096-008] Thee to possess heaven. Where Thou art, there is heaven; and where Thou

[096-009] are not, behold there death and hell. Thou art all my desire, and

[096-010] therefore must I groan and cry and earnestly pray after Thee. In short

[096-011] I can confide fully in none to give me ready help in necessities, save

[096-012] in Thee alone, O my God. Thou art my hope, Thou art my trust, Thou art

[096-013] my Comforter, and most faithful in all things.

[096-014] 2. _All men seek their own;_(1) Thou settest forward only my salvation

[096-015] and my profit, and turnest all things unto my good. Even though Thou

[096-016] dost expose me to divers temptations and adversities, Thou ordainest

[096-017] all this unto my advantage, for Thou are wont to prove Thy beloved ones

[096-018] in a thousand ways. In which proving Thou oughtest no less to be loved

[096-019] and praised, than if Thou wert filling me full of heavenly

[096-020] consolations.

[096-021] 3. In Thee, therefore, O Lord God, I put all my hope and my refuge, on

[096-022] Thee I lay all my [[suffering|tribulation]] and anguish; because I find all to be

[096-023] weak and unstable whatsoever I behold out of Thee. For many friends

[096-024] shall not profit, nor strong helpers be able to succour, nor prudent

[096-025] counsellors to give a useful answer, nor the books of the learned to

[096-026] console, nor any precious substance to deliver, nor any secret and

[096-027] beautiful place to give shelter, if Thou Thyself do not assist, help,

[096-028] strengthen, comfort, instruct, keep in safety.

[096-029] 4. For all things which seem to belong to the attainment of peace and

[096-030] felicity are nothing when Thou art absent, and bring no felicity at all

[096-031] in reality. Therefore art Thou the end of all good, and the fulness of

[096-032] Life, and the soul of eloquence; and to hope in Thee above all things

[096-033] is the strongest solace of Thy servants. _Mine eyes look unto Thee_,(2)

[096-034] in Thee is my trust, O my God, Father of mercies.

[096-035] 5. Bless and sanctify my soul with heavenly blessing that it may become

[096-036] Thy holy habitation, and the seat of Thy eternal glory; and let nothing

[096-037] be found in the Temple of Thy divinity which may offend the eyes of Thy

[096-038] majesty. According to the greatness of Thy goodness and the multitude

[096-039] of Thy mercies look upon me, and hear the prayer of Thy poor servant,

[096-040] far exiled from Thee in the land of the shadow of death. Protect and

[096-041] preserve the soul of Thy least servant amid so many dangers of

[096-042] corruptible life, and by Thy grace accompanying me, direct it by the

[096-043] way of peace unto its home of perpetual light. Amen.

[096-044] (1) Philippians ii. 21 (2) Psalm cxli. 8.

[096-048] The Voice of Christ

[096-049] Come unto Me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will

[096-050] refresh you,(1) saith the Lord. The bread that I will give is My flesh

[096-051] which I give for the life of [[world|the world]].(2) Take, eat: this is My Body,

[096-052] which is given for you; this do in remembrance of Me.(3) He that eateth

[096-053] My flesh and drinketh My blood dwelleth in Me and I in him. The words

[096-054] that I speak unto you, they are spirit, and they are life.(4)

[096-055] (1) Matthew xi. 28 (2) John vi. 51. (3) Matthew xxvi. 26; Luke xxii.

[096-056] 19. (4) John vi. 51, 63.
