[016-001] Of bearing with the faults of others

[016-002] Those things which a man cannot amend in himself or in others, he ought

[016-003] patiently to bear, until God shall otherwise ordain. Bethink thee that

[016-004] perhaps it is better for thy [[temptation|trial]] and patience, without which our

[016-005] merits are but little worth. Nevertheless thou oughtest, when thou

[016-006] findeth such impediments, to beseech God that He would vouchsafe to

[016-007] sustain thee, that thou be able to bear them with a good will.

[016-008] 2. If one who is once or twice admonished refuse to hearken, strive not

[016-009] with him, but commit all to God, that His will may be done and His

[016-010] honour be shown in His servants, for He knoweth well how to convert the

[016-011] evil unto good. Endeavour to be patient in bearing with other men’s

[016-012] faults and infirmities whatsoever they be, for thou thyself also hast

[016-013] many things which have need to be borne with by others. If thou canst

[016-014] not make thine own self what thou desireth, how shalt thou be able to

[016-015] fashion another to thine own liking. We are ready to see others made

[016-016] perfect, and yet we do not amend our own shortcomings.

[016-017] 3. We will that others be straitly corrected, but we will not be

[016-018] corrected ourselves. The freedom of others displeaseth us, but we are

[016-019] dissatisfied that our own wishes shall be denied us. We desire rules to

[016-020] be made restraining others, but by no means will we suffer ourselves to

[016-021] be restrained. Thus therefore doth it plainly appear how seldom we

[016-022] weigh our neighbour in the same balance with ourselves. If all men were

[016-023] perfect, what then should we have to suffer from others for God?

[016-024] 4. But now hath God thus ordained, that we may learn to bear one

[016-025] another’s burdens, because none is without defect, none without a

[016-026] burden, none sufficient of himself, none wise enough of himself; but it

[016-027] behoveth us to bear with one another, to comfort one another, to help,

[016-028] instruct, admonish one another. How much strength each man hath is best

[016-029] proved by occasions of [[suffering|adversity]]: for such occasions do not make a man

[016-030] frail, but show of what temper he is.
