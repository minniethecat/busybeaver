[033-001] Of the intimate love of [[jesus-christ|Jesus]]

[033-002] When Jesus is present all is well and nothing seemeth hard, but when

[033-003] Jesus is not present everything is hard. When Jesus speaketh not

[033-004] within, our comfort is nothing worth, but if Jesus speaketh but a

[033-005] single word great is the comfort we experience. Did not [[mary|Mary]] Magdalene

[033-006] rise up quickly from the place where she wept when Martha said to her,

[033-007] The Master is come and calleth for thee?(1) Happy hour when Jesus

[033-008] calleth thee from tears to the joy of the spirit! How dry and hard art

[033-009] thou without Jesus! How senseless and vain if thou desirest aught

[033-010] beyond Jesus! Is not this greater loss than if thou shouldst lose the

[033-011] whole world?

[033-012] 2. What can [[world|the world]] profit thee without Jesus? To be without Jesus is

[033-013] the [[hell|nethermost hell]], and to be with Jesus is sweet paradise. If Jesus

[033-014] were with thee no enemy could hurt thee. He who findeth Jesus findeth a

[033-015] good treasure, yea, good above all good; and he who loseth Jesus loseth

[033-016] exceeding much, yea, more than the whole world. Most poor is he who

[033-017] liveth without Jesus, and most rich is he who is much with Jesus.

[033-018] 3. It is great skill to know how to live with Jesus, and to know how to

[033-019] hold Jesus is great wisdom. Be thou humble and peaceable and Jesus

[033-020] shall be with thee. Be godly and [[silence|quiet]], and Jesus will remain with

[033-021] thee. Thou canst quickly drive away Jesus and lose His favour if thou

[033-022] wilt turn away to the outer things. And if thou hast put Him to flight

[033-023] and lost Him, to whom wilt thou flee, and whom then wilt thou seek for

[033-024] a friend? Without a friend thou canst not live long, and if Jesus be

[033-025] not thy friend above all thou shalt be very sad and desolate. Madly

[033-026] therefore doest thou if thou trusteth or findest joy in any other. It

[033-027] is preferable to have the whole world against thee, than Jesus offended

[033-028] with thee. Therefore of all that are dear to thee, let Jesus be

[033-029] specially loved.

[033-030] 4. Let all be loved for Jesus’ sake, but Jesus for His own. Jesus

[033-031] Christ alone is to be specially loved, for He alone is found good and

[033-032] faithful above all friends. For His sake and in Him let both enemies

[033-033] and friends be dear to thee, and pray for them all that they may all

[033-034] know and love Him. Never desire to be specially praised or loved,

[033-035] because this belongeth to God alone, who hath none like unto Himself.

[033-036] Nor wish thou that any one set his heart on thee, nor do thou give

[033-037] thyself up to the love of any, but let Jesus be in thee and in every

[033-038] good man.

[033-039] 5. Be pure and free within thyself, and be not entangled by any created

[033-040] thing. Thou oughtest to bring a bare and clean heart to God, if thou

[033-041] desirest to be ready to see how gracious the Lord is. And in truth,

[033-042] unless thou be prevented and drawn on by His grace, thou wilt not

[033-043] attain to this, that having cast out and dismissed all else, thou alone

[033-044] art united to God. For when the [[grace|grace of God]] cometh to a man, then he

[033-045] becometh able to do all things, and when it departeth then he will be

[033-046] poor and weak and given up unto troubles. In these thou art not to be

[033-047] cast down nor to despair, but to rest with calm mind on the will of

[033-048] God, and to bear all things which come upon thee unto the praise of

[033-049] Jesus Christ; for after winter cometh summer, after night returneth

[033-050] day, after the tempest a great calm.

[033-051] (1) John xi. 28.
