[059-001] Of the recollection of God’s manifold benefits

[059-002] Open, O [[jesus-christ|Lord]], my heart in Thy law, and teach me to walk in the way of

[059-003] Thy [[law|commandments]]. Grant me to understand Thy will and to be mindful of

[059-004] Thy benefits, both general and special, with great reverence and

[059-005] diligent meditation, that thus I may be able worthily to give Thee

[059-006] thanks. Yet I know and confess that I cannot render Thee due praises

[059-007] for the least of Thy mercies. I am less than the least of all the good

[059-008] things which Thou gavest me; and when I consider Thy majesty, my spirit

[059-009] faileth because of the greatness thereof.

[059-010] 2. All things which we have in the soul and in the body, and whatsoever

[059-011] things we possess, whether outwardly or inwardly, naturally or

[059-012] supernaturally, are Thy good gifts, and prove Thee, from whom we have

[059-013] received them all, to be good, gentle, and kind. Although one receiveth

[059-014] many things, and another fewer, yet all are Thine, and without Thee not

[059-015] even the least thing can be possessed. He who hath received greater

[059-016] cannot boast that it is of his own merit, nor lift himself up above

[059-017] others, nor contemn those beneath him; for he is the greater and the

[059-018] better who ascribeth least to himself, and in giving thanks is the

[059-019] humbler and more devout; and he who holdeth himself to be viler than

[059-020] all, and judgeth himself to be the more unworthy, is the apter for

[059-021] receiving greater things.

[059-022] 3. But he who hath received fewer gifts, ought not to be cast down, nor

[059-023] to take it amiss, nor to envy him who is richer; but rather ought he to

[059-024] look unto Thee, and to greatly extol Thy goodness, for Thou pourest

[059-025] forth Thy gifts so richly, so freely and largely, without respect of

[059-026] persons. All things come of Thee; therefore in all things shalt thou be

[059-027] praised. Thou knowest what is best to be given to each; and why this

[059-028] man hath less, and that more, is not for us but for Thee to understand,

[059-029] for unto Thee each man’s deservings are fully known.

[059-030] 4. Wherefore, O Lord God, I reckon it even a great benefit, not to have

[059-031] many things, whence praise and glory may appear outwardly, and after

[059-032] the thought of men. For so it is that he who considereth his own

[059-033] poverty and vileness, ought not only to draw therefrom no grief or

[059-034] sorrow, or sadness of spirit, but rather comfort and cheerfulness;

[059-035] because Thou, Lord, hast chosen the poor and humble, and those who are

[059-036] poor in this world, to be Thy friends and acquaintance. So give all

[059-037] Thine apostles witness whom Thou hast made princes in all lands. Yet

[059-038] they had their conversation in this world blameless, so humble and

[059-039] meek, without any malice or deceit, that they even rejoiced to suffer

[059-040] rebukes for Thy Name’s sake,(1) and what things [[world|the world]] hateth, they

[059-041] embraced with great joy.

[059-042] 5. Therefore ought nothing so much to rejoice him who loveth Thee and

[059-043] knoweth Thy benefits, as Thy will in him, and the good pleasure of

[059-044] Thine eternal [[providence|Providence]], wherewith he ought to be so contented and

[059-045] comforted, that he would as willingly be the least as any other would

[059-046] be the greatest, as peaceable and contented in the lowest as in the

[059-047] highest place, and as willingly held of small and low account and of no

[059-048] name or reputation as to be more honourable and greater in the world

[059-049] than others. For Thy will and the love of Thine honour ought to go

[059-050] before all things, and to please and comfort him more, than all

[059-051] benefits that are given or may be given to himself.

[059-052] (1) Acts v. 41.
