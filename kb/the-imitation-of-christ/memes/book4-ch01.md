[097-001] With how great reverence [[jesus-christ|Christ]] must be received

[097-002] The Voice of the Disciple

[097-003] These are Thy words, O Christ, [[truth|Eternal Truth]]; though not uttered at one

[097-004] time nor written together in one place of Scripture. Because therefore

[097-005] they are Thy words and true, I must gratefully and faithfully receive

[097-006] them all. They are Thine, and Thou hast uttered them; and they are mine

[097-007] also, because Thou didst speak them for my salvation. Gladly I receive

[097-008] them from Thy mouth, that they may be more deeply implanted in my

[097-009] heart. Words of such great grace arouse me, for they are full of

[097-010] sweetness and love; but my own [[sin|sins]] terrify me, and my impure

[097-011] conscience driveth me away from receiving so great mysteries. The

[097-012] sweetness of Thy words encourageth me, but the multitude of my faults

[097-013] presseth me down.

[097-014] 2. Thou commandest that I draw near to Thee with firm confidence, if I

[097-015] would have part with Thee, and that I receive the food of immortality,

[097-016] if I desire to obtain [[eternity|eternal life]] and glory. Come unto Me, sayest

[097-017] Thou, all that labour and are heavy laden, and I will refresh you. Oh,

[097-018] sweet and lovely word in the ear of the sinner, that Thou, O Lord my

[097-019] God, dost invite the poor and needy to the [[communion|Communion]] of Thy most holy

[097-020] body and blood. But who am I, O Lord, that I should presume to approach

[097-021] unto Thee? Behold the heaven of heavens cannot contain Thee, and yet

[097-022] Thou sayest, Come ye all unto Me.

[097-023] 3. What meaneth this most gracious condescension, this most lovely

[097-024] invitation? How shall I dare to come, who know no good thing of myself,

[097-025] whence I might be able to presume? How shall I bring Thee within my

[097-026] house, seeing that I so often have sinned in Thy most loving sight?

[097-027] Angels and Archangels stand in awe of Thee, the Saints and just men

[097-028] fear Thee, and Thou sayest, Come unto Me! Except Thou, Lord, hadst said

[097-029] it, who should believe it true? And except Thou hadst commanded, who

[097-030] should attempt to draw near?

[097-031] 4. Behold, Noah, that just man, laboured for a hundred years in

[097-032] building the ark, that he might be saved with the few; and I, how shall

[097-033] I be able in one hour to prepare myself to receive the Builder of the

[097-034] world with reverence? [[moses|Moses]], Thy servant, Thy great and especial

[097-035] friend, made an ark of incorruptible wood, which also he covered with

[097-036] purest gold, that he might lay up in it the tables of the law, and I, a

[097-037] corruptible creature, shall I dare thus easily to receive Thee, the

[097-038] Maker of the [[law|Law]] and the Giver of life? Solomon, the wisest of the

[097-039] kings of [[israel|Israel]], was seven years building his magnificent temple to the

[097-040] praise of Thy Name, and for eight days celebrated the feast of its

[097-041] dedication, offered a thousand peace offerings, and solemnly brought up

[097-042] the Ark of the Covenant to the place prepared for it, with the sound of

[097-043] trumpets and great joy, and I, unhappy and poorest of mankind, how

[097-044] shall I bring Thee into my house, who scarce know how to spend half an

[097-045] hour in devotion? And oh that it were even one half hour worthily

[097-046] spent!

[097-047] 5. O my God, how earnestly these holy men strove to please Thee! And

[097-048] alas! how little and trifling is that which I do! how short a time do I

[097-049] spend, when I am disposing myself to Communion. Rarely altogether

[097-050] collected, most rarely cleansed from all distraction. And surely in the

[097-051] saving presence of Thy Godhead no unmeet thought ought to intrude, nor

[097-052] should any creature take possession of me, because it is not an Angel

[097-053] but the Lord of the Angels, that I am about to receive as my Guest.

[097-054] 6. Yet there is a vast difference between the Ark of the Covenant with

[097-055] its relics, and Thy most pure Body with its ineffable virtues, between

[097-056] those sacrifices of the law, which were figures of things to come, and

[097-057] the true sacrifice of Thy Body, the completion of all the ancient

[097-058] sacrifices.

[097-059] 7. Wherefore then do I not yearn more ardently after Thy adorable

[097-060] presence? Why do I not prepare myself with greater solicitude to

[097-061] receive Thy holy things, when those holy Patriarchs and Prophets of

[097-062] old, kings also and princes, with the whole people, manifested so great

[097-063] affection of devotion towards Thy Divine Service?

[097-064] 8. The most devout king [[david|David]] danced with all his might before the Ark

[097-065] of God, calling to mind the benefits granted to his forefathers in days

[097-066] past; he fashioned musical instruments of various sorts, put forth

[097-067] Psalms, and appointed them to be sung with joy, played also himself

[097-068] ofttimes on the harp, being inspired with the grace of the [[holy-spirit|Holy Ghost]];

[097-069] he taught the people of Israel to praise God with the whole heart, and

[097-070] with unity of voice to bless and praise Him every day. If so great

[097-071] devotion was then exercised, and celebration of [[praise|divine praise]] was

[097-072] carried on before the Ark of the Testimony, how great reverence and

[097-073] devotion ought now to be shown by me and all Christian people at the

[097-074] ministering of the Sacrament, at receiving the most precious Body and

[097-075] Blood of Christ.

[097-076] 9. Many run to diverse places to visit the memorials of departed

[097-077] Saints, and rejoice to hear of their deeds and to look upon the

[097-078] beautiful buildings of their shrines. And behold, Thou art present here

[097-079] with me, O my God, Saint of Saints, Creator of men and Lord of the

[097-080] Angels. Often in looking at those memorials men are moved by curiosity

[097-081] and novelty, and very little fruit of amendment is borne away,

[097-082] especially when there is so much careless trifling and so little true

[097-083] contrition. But here in the [[eucharist|Sacrament of the Altar]], Thou art present

[097-084] altogether, My God, the Man Christ Jesus; where also abundant fruit of

[097-085] eternal life is given to every one soever that receiveth Thee worthily

[097-086] and devoutly. But to this no levity draweth, no curiosity, nor

[097-087] sensuality, only steadfast faith, devout hope, and sincere [[love|charity]].

[097-088] 10. O God, invisible Creator of [[world|the world]], how wondrously dost Thou

[097-089] work with us, how sweetly and graciously Thou dealest with Thine elect,

[097-090] to whom Thou offerest Thyself to be received in this Sacrament! For

[097-091] this surpasseth all understanding, this specially draweth the hearts of

[097-092] the devout and enkindleth their affections. For even thy true faithful

[097-093] ones themselves, who order their whole life to amendment, oftentimes

[097-094] gain from this most excellent Sacrament great grace of devotion and

[097-095] love of virtue.

[097-096] 11. Oh admirable and hidden grace of the Sacrament, which only Christ’s

[097-097] faithful ones know, but the faithless and those who serve sin cannot

[097-098] experience! In this Sacrament is conferred spiritual grace, and lost

[097-099] virtue is regained in the soul, and the beauty which was disfigured by

[097-100] sin returneth again. So great sometimes is this grace that out of the

[097-101] fulness of devotion given, not only the mind but also the weak body

[097-102] feeleth that more strength is supplied unto it.

[097-103] 12. But greatly must we mourn and lament over our lukewarmness and

[097-104] negligence, that we are not drawn by greater affection to become

[097-105] partakers of Christ, in whom all the hope and the merit of those that

[097-106] are to be saved consist. For He Himself is our sanctification and

[097-107] redemption.(1) He is the [[comfort|consolation]] of pilgrims and the eternal

[097-108] fruition of the Saints. Therefore it is grievously to be lamented that

[097-109] many so little consider this health-giving mystery, which maketh heaven

[097-110] glad and preserveth the whole world. Alas for the blindness and

[097-111] hardness of man’s heart, that he considereth not more this unspeakable

[097-112] gift, and even slippeth down through the daily use, into carelessness.

[097-113] 13. For if this most holy Sacrament were celebrated in one place only,

[097-114] and were consecrated only by one priest in the whole world, with what

[097-115] great desire thinkest thou, would men be affected towards that place

[097-116] and towards such a priest of God, that they might behold the divine

[097-117] mysteries celebrated? But now are many men made priests and in many

[097-118] places the Sacrament is celebrated, that the grace and love of God

[097-119] towards men might the more appear, the more widely the Holy Communion

[097-120] is spread abroad over all the world. Thanks be unto Thee, O good Jesus,

[097-121] Eternal Shepherd, who hast vouchsafed to refresh us, poor and exiled

[097-122] ones, with Thy precious Body and Blood, and to invite us to partake

[097-123] these holy mysteries by the invitation from Thine own mouth, saying,

[097-124] Come unto Me, ye who labour and are heavy laden, and I will refresh

[097-125] you.

[097-126] (1) 1 Corinthians i. 30.
