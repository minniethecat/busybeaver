[085-001] Of the [[eternity|day of eternity]] and of the straitnesses of this life

[085-002] Oh most blessed mansion of the City which is above! Oh most clear day

[085-003] of eternity which the night obscureth not, but the Supreme [[truth|Truth]] ever

[085-004] enlighteneth! Day always joyful, always secure and never changing its

[085-005] state into those which are contrary. Oh would that this day might shine

[085-006] forth, and that all these temporal things would come to an end. It

[085-007] shineth indeed upon the Saints, glowing with unending brightness, but

[085-008] only from afar and through a glass, upon those who are pilgrims on the

[085-009] earth.

[085-010] 2. The citizens of heaven know how glorious that day is; the exiled

[085-011] sons of Eve groan, because this is bitter and wearisome. The days of

[085-012] this life are few and evil, full of sorrows and straits, where man is

[085-013] defiled with many [[sin|sins]], ensnared with many passions, bound fast with

[085-014] many fears, wearied with many cares, distracted with many questionings,

[085-015] entangled with many [[vanity|vanities]], compassed about with many errors, worn

[085-016] away with many labours, weighed down with temptations, enervated by

[085-017] pleasures, tormented by poverty.

[085-018] 3. Oh when shall there be an end of these evils? When shall I be

[085-019] delivered from the wretched slavery of my sins? When shall I be

[085-020] mindful, O [[jesus-christ|Lord]], of Thee alone? When shall I rejoice in Thee to the

[085-021] full? When shall I be in true liberty without any impediment, without

[085-022] any burden on mind or body? When shall there be solid peace, peace

[085-023] immovable and secure, peace within and without, peace firm on every

[085-024] side? Blessed Jesus, when shall I stand to behold Thee? When shall I

[085-025] gaze upon the glory of Thy kingdom? When shalt Thou be to me all in

[085-026] all? Oh when shall I be with Thee in Thy Kingdom which Thou hast

[085-027] prepared from the foundation of [[world|the world]] for them that love Thee? I am

[085-028] left destitute, an exile in a hostile land, where are daily wars and

[085-029] grievous misfortunes.

[085-030] 4. Console my exile, mitigate my sorrow, for towards Thee all my desire

[085-031] longeth. For all is to me a burden, whatsoever this world offereth for

[085-032] [[comfort|consolation]]. I yearn to enjoy Thee intimately, but I cannot attain unto

[085-033] it. I long to cleave to heavenly things, but temporal things and

[085-034] unmortified passions press me down. In my mind I would be above all

[085-035] things, but in my flesh I am unwillingly compelled to be beneath them.

[085-036] So, wretched man that I am, I fight with myself, and am made grievous

[085-037] even unto myself, while the spirit seeketh to be above and the flesh to

[085-038] be beneath.

[085-039] 5. Oh how I suffer inwardly, while with the mind I discourse on

[085-040] heavenly things, and presently a crowd of carnal things rusheth upon me

[085-041] whilst I pray. My God, be not Thou far from me, nor depart in wrath

[085-042] from Thy servant. Cast forth Thy lightning and scatter them; send out

[085-043] Thine arrows,(1) and let all delusions of my enemy be confounded.

[085-044] Recall my senses unto Thyself, cause me to forget all worldly things;

[085-045] grant me quickly to cast away and despise the imaginations of sin.

[085-046] Succour me, O Eternal Truth, that no vanity may move me. Come unto me,

[085-047] O Heavenly Sweetness, and let all impurity flee from before Thy face.

[085-048] Pardon me also, and of Thy [[forgiveness|mercy]] deal gently with me, whensoever in

[085-049] prayer I think on anything besides Thee; for truly I confess that I am

[085-050] wont to be continually distracted. For often and often, where in the

[085-051] body I stand or sit, there I myself am not; but rather am I there,

[085-052] whither I am borne by my thoughts. Where my thought is, there am I; and

[085-053] there commonly is my thought where that which I love is. That readily

[085-054] occurreth to me, which naturally delighteth, or pleaseth through

[085-055] custom.

[085-056] 6. Wherefore Thou, who art the Truth, hast plainly said, Where your

[085-057] treasure is, there will your heart be also.(2) If I love heaven, I

[085-058] gladly meditate on heavenly things. If I love the world, I rejoice in

[085-059] the delights of the world, and am made sorry by its adversities. If I

[085-060] love the flesh, I am continually imagining the things which belong to

[085-061] the flesh; if I love the spirit, I am delighted by meditating on

[085-062] spiritual things. For whatsoever things I love, on these I readily

[085-063] converse and listen, and carry home with me the images of them. But

[085-064] blessed is that man who for Thy sake, O Lord, is willing to part from

[085-065] all [[creation|creatures]]; who doth violence to his [[nature|fleshly nature]] and crucifieth

[085-066] the lusts of the flesh by the [[devotion|fervour]] of his spirit, so that with

[085-067] serene conscience he may offer unto Thee a pure prayer, and be made

[085-068] worthy to enter into the angelic choirs, having shut out from himself,

[085-069] both outwardly and inwardly, all worldly things.

[085-070] (1) Psalm lxxi. 12. (2) Matthew vi. 21.
