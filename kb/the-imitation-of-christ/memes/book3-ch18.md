[055-001] That temporal miseries are to be borne patiently after the example of

[055-002] [[jesus-christ|Christ]]

[055-003] “My Son! I came down from heaven for thy salvation; I took upon Me thy

[055-004] miseries not of necessity, but drawn by love that thou mightest learn

[055-005] patience and mightest bear temporal miseries without murmuring. For

[055-006] from the hour of My birth, until My death upon the Cross, I ceased not

[055-007] from bearing of sorrow; I had much lack of temporal things; I

[055-008] oftentimes heard many reproaches against Myself; I gently bore

[055-009] contradictions and hard words; I received ingratitude for benefits,

[055-010] blasphemies for My miracles, rebukes for My doctrine.”

[055-011] 2. Lord, because Thou wast patient in Thy life, herein most of all

[055-012] fulfilling the commandment of Thy Father, it is well that I, miserable

[055-013] sinner, should patiently bear myself according to Thy will, and as long

[055-014] as Thou wilt have it so, should bear about with me for my salvation,

[055-015] the burden of this corruptible life. For although the present life

[055-016] seemeth burdensome, it is nevertheless already made very full of merit

[055-017] through Thy grace, and to those who are weak it becometh easier and

[055-018] brighter through Thy example and the footsteps of Thy saints; but it is

[055-019] also much more full of [[comfort|consolation]] than it was of old, under the old

[055-020] Testament, when the gate of heaven remained shut; and even the way to

[055-021] heaven seemed more obscure when so few cared to seek after the heavenly

[055-022] kingdom. But not even those who were then just and in the way of

[055-023] salvation were able, before Thy Passion and the [[redemption|ransom]] of Thy holy

[055-024] [[death|Death]], to enter the kingdom of heaven.

[055-025] 3. Oh what great thanks am I bound to give Thee, who hast vouchsafed to

[055-026] show me and all faithful people the good and right way to Thine eternal

[055-027] kingdom, for Thy way is our way, and by holy patience we walk to Thee

[055-028] who art our Crown. If Thou hadst not gone before and taught us, who

[055-029] would care to follow? Oh, how far would they have gone backward if they

[055-030] had not beheld Thy glorious example! Behold we are still lukewarm,

[055-031] though we have heard of Thy many signs and discourses; what would

[055-032] become of us if we had not such a light to help us follow Thee?
