[024-001] Of the judgment and punishment of the wicked

[024-002] In all that thou doest, remember the end, and how thou wilt stand

[024-003] before a strict judge, from whom nothing is hid, who is not bribed with

[024-004] gifts, nor accepteth excuses, but will judge righteous judgment. O most

[024-005] miserable and foolish sinner, who art sometimes in fear of the

[024-006] countenance of an angry man, what wilt thou answer to God, who knoweth

[024-007] all thy misdeeds? Why dost thou not provide for thyself against the day

[024-008] of judgment, when no man shall be able to be excused or defended by

[024-009] means of another, but each one shall bear his burden himself alone? Now

[024-010] doth thy labour bring forth fruit, now is thy weeping acceptable, thy

[024-011] groaning heard, thy sorrow well pleasing to God, and cleansing to thy

[024-012] soul.

[024-013] 2. Even here on earth the patient man findeth great occasion of

[024-014] purifying his soul. When suffering injuries he grieveth more for the

[024-015] other’s malice than for his own wrong; when he prayeth heartily for

[024-016] those that despitefully use him, and forgiveth them from his heart;

[024-017] when he is not slow to ask [[forgiveness|pardon]] from others; when he is swifter to

[024-018] pity than to anger; when he frequently denieth himself and striveth

[024-019] altogether to subdue the flesh to the spirit. Better is it now to

[024-020] purify the soul from sin, than to cling to [[sin|sins]] from which we must be

[024-021] purged hereafter. Truly we deceive ourselves by the inordinate love

[024-022] which we bear towards the flesh.

[024-023] 3. What is it which that fire shall devour, save thy sins? The more

[024-024] thou sparest thyself and followest the flesh, the more heavy shall thy

[024-025] punishment be, and the more fuel art thou heaping up for the burning.

[024-026] For wherein a man hath sinned, therein shall he be the more heavily

[024-027] punished. There shall the slothful be pricked forward with burning

[024-028] goads, and the gluttons be tormented with intolerable hunger and

[024-029] thirst. There shall the luxurious and the lovers of pleasure be plunged

[024-030] into burning pitch and stinking brimstone, and the envious shall howl

[024-031] like mad dogs for very grief.

[024-032] 4. No sin will there be which shall not be visited with its own proper

[024-033] punishment. The proud shall be filled with utter confusion, and the

[024-034] covetous shall be pinched with miserable poverty. An hour’s pain there

[024-035] shall be more grievous than a hundred years here of the bitterest

[024-036] [[repentance|penitence]]. No [[silence|quiet]] shall be there, no comfort for the lost, though

[024-037] here sometimes there is respite from pain, and enjoyment of the solace

[024-038] of friends. Be thou anxious now and sorrowful for thy sins, that in the

[024-039] [[judgment|day of judgment]] thou mayest have boldness with the blessed. For then

[024-040] shall the righteous man stand in great boldness before the face of such

[024-041] as have afflicted him and made no account of his labours.(1) Then shall

[024-042] he stand up to judge, he who now submitteth himself in humility to the

[024-043] judgments of men. Then shall the poor and humble man have great

[024-044] confidence, while the proud is taken with fear on every side.

[024-045] 5. Then shall it be seen that he was the wise man in this world who

[024-046] learned to be a fool and despised for [[jesus-christ|Christ]]. Then shall all

[024-047] [[suffering|tribulation]] patiently borne delight us, while the mouth of the ungodly

[024-048] shall be stopped. Then shall every godly man rejoice, and every profane

[024-049] man shall mourn. Then the afflicted flesh shall more rejoice than if it

[024-050] had been alway nourished in delights. Then the humble garment shall put

[024-051] on beauty, and the precious robe shall hide itself as vile. Then the

[024-052] little poor cottage shall be more commended than the gilded palace.

[024-053] Then enduring patience shall have more might than all the power of the

[024-054] world. Then simple obedience shall be more highly exalted than all

[024-055] worldly wisdom.

[024-056] 6. Then a pure and [[conscience|good conscience]] shall more rejoice than learned

[024-057] philosophy. Then contempt of riches shall have more weight than all the

[024-058] treasure of the children of this world. Then shalt thou find more

[024-059] comfort in having prayed devoutly than in having fared sumptuously.

[024-060] Then thou wilt rather rejoice in having kept silence than in having

[024-061] made long speech. Then holy deeds shall be far stronger than many fine

[024-062] words. Then a strict life and sincere penitence shall bring deeper

[024-063] pleasure than all earthly delight. Learn now to suffer a little, that

[024-064] then thou mayest be enabled to escape heavier sufferings. Prove first

[024-065] here, what thou art able to endure hereafter. If now thou art able to

[024-066] bear so little, how wilt thou be able to endure eternal torments? If

[024-067] now a little suffering maketh thee so impatient, what shall hell-fire

[024-068] do then? Behold of a surety thou art not able to have two Paradises, to

[024-069] take thy fill or delight here in this world, and to reign with Christ

[024-070] hereafter.

[024-071] 7. If even unto this day thou hadst ever lived in honours and

[024-072] pleasures, what would the whole profit thee if now death came to thee

[024-073] in an instant? All therefore is vanity, save to love God and to serve

[024-074] Him only. For he who loveth God with all his heart feareth not death,

[024-075] nor punishment, nor judgment, nor hell, because perfect love giveth

[024-076] sure access to God. But he who still delighteth in sin, no marvel if he

[024-077] is afraid of death and judgment. Nevertheless it is a good thing, if

[024-078] love as yet cannot restrain thee from evil, that at least the fear of

[024-079] hell should hold thee back. But he who putteth aside the [[fear-of-the-lord|fear of God]]

[024-080] cannot long continue in good, but shall quickly fall into the snares of

[024-081] the devil.

[024-082] (1) Wisd. v. 1.
