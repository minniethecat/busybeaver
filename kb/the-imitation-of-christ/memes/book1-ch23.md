[023-001] Of meditation upon death

[023-002] Very quickly will there be an end of thee here; take heed therefore how

[023-003] it will be with thee in another world. To-day man is, and to-morrow he

[023-004] will be seen no more. And being removed out of sight, quickly also he

[023-005] is out of mind. O the dulness and hardness of man’s heart, which

[023-006] thinketh only of the present, and looketh not forward to the future.

[023-007] Thou oughtest in every deed and thought so to order thyself, as if thou

[023-008] wert to die this day. If thou hadst a [[conscience|good conscience]] thou wouldst not

[023-009] greatly fear death. It were better for thee to watch against sin, than

[023-010] to fly from death. If to-day thou art not ready, how shalt thou be

[023-011] ready to-morrow? To-morrow is an uncertain day; and how knowest thou

[023-012] that thou shalt have a to-morrow?

[023-013] 2. What doth it profit to live long, when we amend so little? Ah! long

[023-014] life doth not always amend, but often the more increaseth guilt. Oh

[023-015] that we might spend a single day in this world as it ought to be spent!

[023-016] Many there are who reckon the years since they were converted, and yet

[023-017] oftentimes how little is the fruit thereof. If it is a fearful thing to

[023-018] die, it may be perchance a yet more fearful thing to live long. Happy

[023-019] is the man who hath the hour of his death always before his eyes, and

[023-020] daily prepareth himself to die. If thou hast ever seen one die,

[023-021] consider that thou also shalt pass away by the same road.

[023-022] 3. When it is morning reflect that it may be thou shalt not see the

[023-023] evening, and at eventide dare not to boast thyself of the morrow.

[023-024] Always be thou prepared, and so live that death may never find thee

[023-025] unprepared. Many die suddenly and unexpectedly. For at such an hour as

[023-026] ye think not, the Son of Man cometh.(1) When that last hour shall come,

[023-027] thou wilt begin to think very differently of thy whole life past, and

[023-028] wilt mourn bitterly that thou hast been so negligent and slothful.

[023-029] 4. Happy and wise is he who now striveth to be such in life as he would

[023-030] fain be found in death! For a perfect contempt of [[world|the world]], a fervent

[023-031] desire to excel in virtue, the love of discipline, the painfulness of

[023-032] repentance, readiness to obey, [[self-denial|denial of self]], [[obedience|submission]] to any

[023-033] [[suffering|adversity]] for love of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]; these are the things which shall give

[023-034] great confidence of a happy death. Whilst thou art in health thou hast

[023-035] many opportunities of good works; but when thou art in sickness I know

[023-036] not how much thou wilt be able to do. Few are made better by infirmity:

[023-037] even as they who wander much abroad seldom become holy.

[023-038] 5. [[trust|Trust]] not thy friends and kinsfolk, nor put off the work of thy

[023-039] salvation to the future, for men will forget thee sooner than thou

[023-040] thinkest. It is better for thee now to provide in time, and to send

[023-041] some good before thee, than to [[trust-in-god|trust]] to the help of others. If thou art

[023-042] not anxious for thyself now, who, thinkest thou, will be anxious for

[023-043] thee afterwards? Now the time is most precious. Now is the accepted

[023-044] time, now is the [[salvation|day of salvation]]. But alas! that thou spendest not

[023-045] well this time, wherein thou mightest lay up treasure which should

[023-046] profit thee everlastingly. The hour will come when thou shalt desire

[023-047] one day, yea, one hour, for [[amendment|amendment of life]], and I know not whether

[023-048] thou shalt obtain.

[023-049] 6. Oh, dearly beloved, from what danger thou mightest free thyself,

[023-050] from what great fear, if only thou wouldst always live in fear, and in

[023-051] expectation of death! Strive now to live in such wise that in the hour

[023-052] of death thou mayest rather rejoice than fear. Learn now to die to the

[023-053] world, so shalt thou begin to live with Christ. Learn now to contemn

[023-054] all earthly things, and then mayest thou freely go unto Christ. Keep

[023-055] under thy body by [[repentance|penitence]], and then shalt thou be able to have a sure

[023-056] confidence.

[023-057] 7. Ah, foolish one! why thinkest thou that thou shalt live long, when

[023-058] thou art not sure of a single day? How many have been deceived, and

[023-059] suddenly have been snatched away from the body! How many times hast

[023-060] thou heard how one was slain by the sword, another was drowned, another

[023-061] falling from on high broke his neck, another died at the table, another

[023-062] whilst at play! One died by fire, another by the sword, another by the

[023-063] pestilence, another by the robber. Thus cometh death to all, and the

[023-064] life of men swiftly passeth away like a shadow.

[023-065] 8. Who will remember thee after thy death? And who will entreat for

[023-066] thee? Work, work now, oh dearly beloved, work all that thou canst. For

[023-067] thou knowest not when thou shalt die, nor what shall happen unto thee

[023-068] after death. While thou hast time, lay up for thyself undying riches.

[023-069] Think of nought but of thy salvation; care only for the things of God.

[023-070] Make to thyself friends, by venerating the saints of God and walking in

[023-071] their steps, that when thou failest, thou mayest be received into

[023-072] everlasting habitations.(2)

[023-073] 9. Keep thyself as a stranger and a pilgrim upon the earth, to whom the

[023-074] things of the world appertain not. Keep thine heart free, and lifted up

[023-075] towards God, for here have we no continuing city.(3) To Him direct thy

[023-076] daily prayers with crying and tears, that thy spirit may be found

[023-077] worthy to pass happily after death unto its Lord. Amen.

[023-078] (1) Matthew xxiv. 44. (2) Luke xvi. 9. (3) Hebrews xiii. 14.
