[050-001] Of the obedience of one in lowly [[obedience|subjection]] after the example of [[jesus-christ|Jesus]]

[050-002] Christ

[050-003] “My Son, he who striveth to withdraw himself from obedience,

[050-004] withdraweth himself also from grace, and he who seeketh private

[050-005] advantages, loseth those which are common unto all. If a man submit not

[050-006] freely and willingly to one set over him, it is a sign that his flesh

[050-007] is not yet perfectly subject to himself, but often resisteth and

[050-008] murmureth. Learn therefore quickly to submit thyself to him who is over

[050-009] thee, if thou seekest to bring thine own flesh into subjection. For the

[050-010] outward enemy is very quickly overcome if the inner man have not been

[050-011] laid low. There is no more grievous and deadly enemy to the soul than

[050-012] thou art to thyself, if thou art not led by [[holy-spirit|the Spirit]]. Thou must not

[050-013] altogether conceive contempt for thyself, if thou wilt prevail against

[050-014] flesh and blood. Because as yet thou inordinately lovest thyself,

[050-015] therefore thou shrinkest from yielding thyself to the will of others.

[050-016] 2. “But what great thing is it that thou, who art dust and nothingness,

[050-017] yieldest thyself to man for God’s sake, when I, the Almighty and the

[050-018] Most High, who created all things out of nothing, subjected Myself to

[050-019] man for thy sake? I became the most humble and despised of men, that by

[050-020] My humility thou mightest overcome thy pride. Learn to obey, O dust!

[050-021] Learn to humble thyself, O earth and clay, and to bow thyself beneath

[050-022] the feet of all. Learn to crush thy [[sin|passions]], and to yield thyself in

[050-023] all subjection.

[050-024] 3. “Be zealous against thyself, nor suffer pride to live within thee,

[050-025] but so show thyself subject and of no reputation, that all may be able

[050-026] to walk over thee, and tread thee down as the clay in the streets. What

[050-027] hast thou, O foolish man, of which to complain? What, O vile sinner,

[050-028] canst thou answer those who speak against thee, seeing thou hast so

[050-029] often offended God, and many a time hast deserved hell? But Mine eye

[050-030] hath spared thee, because thy soul was precious in My sight; that thou

[050-031] mightest know My love, and mightest be thankful for My benefits; and

[050-032] that thou mightest give thyself altogether to true subjection and

[050-033] humility, and patiently bear the contempt which thou meritest.”
