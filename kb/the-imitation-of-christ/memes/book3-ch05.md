[042-001] Of the wonderful power of the Divine Love

[042-002] I bless Thee, O Heavenly Father, Father of my [[jesus-christ|Lord]] Jesus Christ, for

[042-003] that Thou hast vouchsafed to think of me, poor that I am. O, Father of

[042-004] Mercies and God of all comfort,(1) I give thanks unto Thee, who

[042-005] refreshest me sometimes with thine own comfort, when I am unworthy of

[042-006] any comfort. I bless and glorify Thee continually, with thine only

[042-007] begotten Son and the [[holy-spirit|Holy Ghost]], the Paraclete, for ever and ever. O

[042-008] Lord God, Holy lover of my soul, when Thou shalt come into my heart,

[042-009] all my inward parts shall rejoice. Thou art my glory and the joy of my

[042-010] heart. Thou art my hope and my refuge in the day of my trouble.

[042-011] 2. But because I am still weak in love and imperfect in virtue, I need

[042-012] to be strengthened and comforted by Thee; therefore visit Thou me often

[042-013] and instruct me with Thy holy ways of discipline. Deliver me from evil

[042-014] [[sin|passions]], and cleanse my heart from all inordinate affections, that,

[042-015] being healed and altogether cleansed within, I may be made ready to

[042-016] love, strong to suffer, steadfast to endure.

[042-017] 3. Love is a great thing, a good above all others, which alone maketh

[042-018] every heavy burden light, and equaliseth every inequality. For it

[042-019] beareth the burden and maketh it no burden, it maketh every bitter

[042-020] thing to be sweet and of good taste. The surpassing love of Jesus

[042-021] impelleth to great works, and exciteth to the continual desiring of

[042-022] greater perfection. Love willeth to be raised up, and not to be held

[042-023] down by any mean thing. Love willeth to be free and aloof from all

[042-024] worldly affection, lest its inward power of vision be hindered, lest it

[042-025] be entangled by any worldly prosperity or overcome by [[suffering|adversity]].

[042-026] Nothing is sweeter than love, nothing stronger, nothing loftier,

[042-027] nothing broader, nothing pleasanter, nothing fuller or better in heaven

[042-028] nor on earth, for love was born of God and cannot rest save in God

[042-029] above all [[creation|created things]].

[042-030] 4. He who loveth flyeth, runneth, and is glad; he is free and not

[042-031] hindered. He giveth all things for all things, and hath all things in

[042-032] all things, because he resteth in One who is high above all, from whom

[042-033] every good floweth and proceedeth. He looketh not for gifts, but

[042-034] turneth himself to the Giver above all good things. Love oftentimes

[042-035] knoweth no measure, but breaketh out above all measure; love feeleth no

[042-036] burden, reckoneth not labours, striveth after more than it is able to

[042-037] do, pleadeth not impossibility, because it judgeth all things which are

[042-038] lawful for it to be possible. It is strong therefore for all things,

[042-039] and it fulfilleth many things, and is successful where he who loveth

[042-040] not faileth and lieth down.

[042-041] 5. Love is watchful, and whilst sleeping still keepeth watch; though

[042-042] fatigued it is not weary, though pressed it is not forced, though

[042-043] alarmed it is not terrified, but like the living flame and the burning

[042-044] torch, it breaketh forth on high and securely triumpheth. If a man

[042-045] loveth, he knoweth what this voice crieth. For the ardent affection of

[042-046] the soul is a great clamour in the ears of God, and it saith: My God,

[042-047] my Beloved! Thou art all mine, and I am all Thine.

[042-048] 6. Enlarge Thou me in love, that I may learn to taste with the

[042-049] innermost mouth of my heart how sweet it is to love, to be dissolved,

[042-050] and to swim in love. Let me be holden by love, mounting above myself

[042-051] through exceeding [[devotion|fervour]] and admiration. Let me sing the song of love,

[042-052] let me follow Thee my Beloved on high, let my soul exhaust itself in

[042-053] Thy praise, exulting with love. Let me love Thee more than myself, not

[042-054] loving myself except for Thy sake, and all men in Thee who truly love

[042-055] Thee, as the [[law|law of love]] commandeth which shineth forth from Thee.

[042-056] 7. Love is swift, sincere, pious, pleasant, gentle, strong, patient,

[042-057] faithful, prudent, [[patience|long-suffering]], manly, and never seeking her own;

[042-058] for wheresoever a man seeketh his own, there he falleth from love. Love

[042-059] is circumspect, humble, and upright; not weak, not fickle, nor intent

[042-060] on vain things; sober, chaste, steadfast, [[silence|quiet]], and guarded in all the

[042-061] senses. Love is subject and obedient to all that are in authority, vile

[042-062] and lowly in its own sight, devout and grateful towards God, faithful

[042-063] and always trusting in Him even when God hideth His face, for without

[042-064] sorrow we cannot live in love.

[042-065] 8. He who is not ready to suffer all things, and to conform to the will

[042-066] of the Beloved, is not worthy to be called a lover of God. It behoveth

[042-067] him who loveth to embrace willingly all hard and bitter things for the

[042-068] Beloved’s sake, and not to be drawn away from Him because of any

[042-069] contrary accidents.

[042-070] (1) 2 Corinthians i. 3.
