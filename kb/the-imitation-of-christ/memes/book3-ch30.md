[067-001] Of seeking divine help, and the confidence of obtaining grace

[067-002] “My Son, I the [[jesus-christ|Lord]] am a stronghold in the day of trouble.(1) Come unto

[067-003] Me, when it is not well with thee.

[067-004] “This it is which chiefly hindereth [[consolation|heavenly consolation]], that thou too

[067-005] slowly betakest thyself unto prayer. For before thou earnestly seekest

[067-006] unto Me, thou dost first seek after many means of comfort, and

[067-007] refresheth thyself in outward things: so it cometh to pass that all

[067-008] things profit thee but little until thou learn that it is I who deliver

[067-009] those who [[trust-in-god|trust]] in Me; neither beside Me is there any strong help, nor

[067-010] profitable counsel, nor enduring remedy. But now, recovering courage

[067-011] after the tempest, grow thou strong in the light of My mercies, for I

[067-012] am nigh, saith the Lord, that I may restore all things not only as they

[067-013] were at the first, but also abundantly and one upon another.

[067-014] 2. “For is anything too hard for Me, or shall I be like unto one who

[067-015] saith and doeth not? Where is thy faith? Stand fast and with

[067-016] perseverance. Be [[patience|long-suffering]] and strong. [[consolation|Consolation]] will come unto

[067-017] thee in its due season. Wait for Me; yea, wait; I will come and heal

[067-018] thee. It is temptation which vexeth thee, and a vain fear which

[067-019] terrifieth thee. What doth care about future events bring thee, save

[067-020] sorrow upon sorrow? Sufficient for the day is the evil thereof.(2) It

[067-021] is vain and useless to be disturbed or lifted up about future things

[067-022] which perhaps will never come.

[067-023] 3. “But it is the nature of man to be deceived by fancies of this sort,

[067-024] and it is a sign of a mind which is still weak to be so easily drawn

[067-025] away at the suggestion of the enemy. For he careth not whether he

[067-026] deceive and beguile by true means or false; whether he throw thee down

[067-027] by the love of the present or fear of the future. Therefore let not thy

[067-028] heart be troubled, neither let it be afraid. Believe in Me, and put thy

[067-029] trust in My [[forgiveness|mercy]].(3) When thou thinkest thyself far removed from Me, I

[067-030] am often the nearer. When thou reckonest that almost all is lost, then

[067-031] often is greater opportunity of gain at hand. All is not lost when

[067-032] something goeth contrary to thy wishes. Thou oughtest not to judge

[067-033] according to present feeling, nor so to take or give way to any grief

[067-034] which befalleth thee, as if all hope of escape were taken away.

[067-035] 4. “Think not thyself totally abandoned, although for the time I have

[067-036] sent to thee some [[suffering|tribulation]], or have even withdrawn some cherished

[067-037] consolation; for this is the way to the [[heaven|Kingdom of Heaven]]. And without

[067-038] doubt it is better for thee and for all My other servants, that ye

[067-039] should be proved by adversities, than that ye should have all things as

[067-040] ye would. I know thy hidden thoughts: and that it is very needful for

[067-041] thy soul’s health that sometimes thou be left without relish, lest

[067-042] perchance thou be lifted up by prosperity, and desirous to please

[067-043] thyself in that which thou art not. What I have given I am able to take

[067-044] away, and to restore again at My good pleasure.

[067-045] 5. “When I shall have given, it is Mine; when I shall have taken away,

[067-046] I have not taken what is thine; for every good gift and every perfect

[067-047] gift(4) is from me. If I shall have sent upon thee grief or any

[067-048] vexation, be not angry, nor let thy heart be sad; I am able quickly to

[067-049] lift thee up and to change every burden into joy. But I am just and

[067-050] greatly to be praised, when I do thus unto thee.

[067-051] 6. “If thou rightly consider, and look upon it with truth, thou

[067-052] oughtest never to be so sadly cast down because of adversity, but

[067-053] rather shouldst rejoice and give thanks; yea, verily to count it the

[067-054] highest joy that I afflict thee with sorrows and spare thee not. As My

[067-055] Father hath loved Me, so love I you;(5) thus have I spoken unto My

[067-056] beloved disciples: whom I sent forth not unto worldly joys, but to

[067-057] great strivings; not unto honours, but unto contempt; not unto ease,

[067-058] but to labours; not unto rest, but to bring forth much fruit with

[067-059] patience. My son, remember these words.”

[067-060] (1) Nahum i. 7. (2) Matthew vi. 34. (3) John xiv. 27; Psalm xiii. 5.

[067-061] (4) James i. 17. (5) John xv. 9.
