[079-001] That our peace is not to be placed in men

[079-002] “My Son, if thou set thy peace on any person because thou hast high

[079-003] opinion of him, and art familiar with him, thou shalt be unstable and

[079-004] entangled. But if thou betake thyself to the ever-living and abiding

[079-005] [[truth|Truth]], the desertion or death of a friend shall not make thee sad. In

[079-006] Me ought the love of thy friend to subsist, and for My sake is every

[079-007] one to be loved, whosoever he be, who appeareth to thee good, and is

[079-008] very dear to thee in this life. Without Me friendship hath no strength

[079-009] or [[patience|endurance]], neither is that love true and pure, which I unite not.

[079-010] Thou oughtest to be so dead to such affections of beloved friends, that

[079-011] as far as in thee lieth, thou wouldst rather choose to be without any

[079-012] companionship of men. The nearer a man approacheth to God, the further

[079-013] he recedeth from all earthly solace. The deeper also he descendeth into

[079-014] himself, and the viler he appeareth in his own eyes, the higher he

[079-015] ascendeth towards God.

[079-016] 2. “But he who attributeth anything good to himself, hindereth the

[079-017] [[grace|grace of God]] from coming to him, because the grace of the [[holy-spirit|Holy Ghost]]

[079-018] ever seeketh the humble heart. If thou couldst make thyself utterly

[079-019] nothing, and empty thyself of the love of every creature, then should

[079-020] it be My part to overflow unto thee with great grace. When thou settest

[079-021] thine eyes upon [[creation|creatures]], the face of the Creator is withdrawn from

[079-022] thee. Learn in all things to conquer thyself for thy Creator’s sake,

[079-023] then shalt thou be able to attain unto [[knowledge|divine knowledge]]. How small

[079-024] soever anything be, if it be loved and regarded inordinately, it

[079-025] holdeth us back from the highest good, and corrupteth.”
