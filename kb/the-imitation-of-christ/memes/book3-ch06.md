[043-001] Of the proving of the true lover

[043-002] “My Son, thou art not yet strong and prudent in thy love.”

[043-003] 2. Wherefore, O my [[jesus-christ|Lord]]?

[043-004] 3. “Because for a little opposition thou fallest away from thy

[043-005] undertakings, and too eagerly seekest after [[comfort|consolation]]. The strong

[043-006] lover standeth fast in temptations, and believeth not the evil

[043-007] persuasions of the enemy. As in prosperity I please him, so in

[043-008] [[suffering|adversity]] I do not displease.

[043-009] 4. “The prudent lover considereth not the gift of the lover so much as

[043-010] the love of the giver. He looketh for the affection more than the

[043-011] value, and setteth all gifts lower than the Beloved. The noble lover

[043-012] resteth not in the gift, but in Me above every gift.

[043-013] 5. “All is not lost, though thou sometimes think of Me or of My saints,

[043-014] less than thou shouldest desire. That good and sweet affection which

[043-015] thou sometimes perceivest is the effect of present grace and some

[043-016] foretaste of the [[eternity|heavenly country]]; but hereon thou must not too much

[043-017] depend, for it goeth and cometh. But to strive against the evil motions

[043-018] of the mind which come to us, and to resist the suggestions of the

[043-019] devil, is a token of virtue and great merit.

[043-020] 6. “Therefore let not strange fancies disturb thee, whencesoever they

[043-021] arise. Bravely observe thy purpose and thy upright intentions towards

[043-022] God. It is not an illusion when thou art sometimes suddenly carried

[043-023] away into rapture, and then suddenly art brought back to the wonted

[043-024] [[vanity|vanities]] of thy heart. For thou dost rather unwillingly undergo them

[043-025] than cause them; and so long as they displease thee and thou strivest

[043-026] against them, it is a merit and no loss.

[043-027] 7. “Know thou that thine old enemy altogether striveth to hinder thy

[043-028] pursuit after good, and to deter thee from every godly exercise, to

[043-029] wit, the contemplation of the Saints, the pious remembrance of My

[043-030] passion, the profitable recollection of sin, the keeping of thy own

[043-031] heart, and the steadfast purpose to grow in virtue. He suggesteth to

[043-032] thee many evil thoughts, that he may work in thee weariness and terror,

[043-033] and so draw thee away from prayer and holy reading. Humble confession

[043-034] displeaseth him, and if he were able he would make thee to cease from

[043-035] [[communion|Communion]]. Believe him not, nor heed him, though many a time he hath

[043-036] laid for thee the snares of deceit. Account it to be from him, when he

[043-037] suggesteth evil and unclean thoughts. Say unto him, ‘Depart unclean

[043-038] spirit; put on shame, miserable one; horribly unclean art thou, who

[043-039] bringest such things to mine ears. Depart from me, detestable deceiver;

[043-040] thou shalt have no part in me; but Jesus shall be with me, as a strong

[043-041] warrior, and thou shalt stand confounded. Rather would I die and bear

[043-042] all suffering, than consent unto thee. Hold thy peace and be dumb; I

[043-043] will not hear thee more, though thou plottest more snares against me.

[043-044] The Lord is my light and my salvation: whom then shall I fear? Though a

[043-045] host of men should rise up against me, yet shall not my heart be

[043-046] afraid. The Lord is my strength and my Redeemer.’(1)

[043-047] 8. “Strive thou like a good soldier; and if sometimes thou fail through

[043-048] weakness, put on thy strength more bravely than before, trusting in My

[043-049] more abundant grace, and take thou much heed of vain confidence and

[043-050] pride. Because of it many are led into error, and sometimes fall into

[043-051] blindness well-nigh irremediable. Let this ruin of the proud, who

[043-052] foolishly lift themselves up, be to thee for a warning and a continual

[043-053] exhortation to humility.”

[043-054] (1) Psalms xxvii. 1-3; xix. 14.
