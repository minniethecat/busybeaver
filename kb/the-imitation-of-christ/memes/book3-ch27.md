[064-001] That personal love greatly hindereth from the highest good

[064-002] “My Son, thou must give all for all, and be nothing of thine own. Know

[064-003] thou that the love of thyself is more hurtful to thee than anything in

[064-004] [[world|the world]]. According to the love and inclination which thou hast,

[064-005] everything more or less cleaveth to thee. If thy love be pure, sincere,

[064-006] well-regulated, thou shalt not be in captivity to anything. Do not

[064-007] covet what thou mayest not have; do not have what is able to hinder

[064-008] thee, and to rob thee of inward liberty. It is wonderful that thou

[064-009] committest not thyself to Me from the very bottom of thy heart, with

[064-010] all things which thou canst desire or have.

[064-011] 2. “Why art thou consumed with vain sorrow? Why art thou wearied with

[064-012] superfluous cares? Stand thou by My good pleasure, and thou shalt

[064-013] suffer no loss. If thou seekest after this or that, and wilt be here or

[064-014] there, according to thine own advantage or the fulfilling of thine own

[064-015] pleasure, thou shalt never be in [[silence|quiet]], nor free from care, because in

[064-016] everything somewhat will be found lacking, and everywhere there will be

[064-017] somebody who opposeth thee.

[064-018] 3. “Therefore it is not gaining or multiplying of this thing or that

[064-019] which advantageth thee, but rather the despising it and cutting it by

[064-020] the root out of thy heart; which thou must not only understand of money

[064-021] and riches, but of the desire after honour and vain praise, things

[064-022] which all pass away with the world. The place availeth little if the

[064-023] spirit of devotion is wanting; nor shall that peace stand long which is

[064-024] sought from abroad, if the state of thy heart is without the true

[064-025] foundation, that is, if it abide not in Me. Thou mayest change, but

[064-026] thou canst not better thyself; for when occasion ariseth and is

[064-027] accepted thou shalt find what thou didst fly from, yea more.”

[064-028] A PRAYER FOR CLEANSING OF THE HEART AND FOR HEAVENLY WISDOM

[064-029] 4. Strengthen me, O God, by the grace of Thy [[holy-spirit|Holy Spirit]]. Give me

[064-030] virtue to be strengthened with might in the inner man, and to free my

[064-031] heart from all fruitless care and trouble, and that I be not drawn away

[064-032] by various desires after any things whatsoever, whether of little value

[064-033] or great, but that I may look upon all as passing away, and myself as

[064-034] passing away with them; because there is no profit under the sun, and

[064-035] all is vanity and vexation of spirit.(1) Oh how wise is he that

[064-036] considereth thus!

[064-037] 5. Give me, O [[jesus-christ|Lord]], [[wisdom|heavenly wisdom]], that I may learn to seek Thee

[064-038] above all things and to find Thee; to relish Thee above all things and

[064-039] to love Thee; and to understand all other things, even as they are,

[064-040] according to the order of Thy wisdom. Grant me prudently to avoid the

[064-041] flatterer, and patiently to bear with him that opposeth me; for this is

[064-042] great wisdom, not to be carried by every wind of words, nor to give ear

[064-043] to the wicked flattering Siren; for thus do we go safely on in the way

[064-044] we have begun.

[064-045] (1) Ecclesiastes ii. 11.
