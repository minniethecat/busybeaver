[054-001] That all care is to be cast upon God

[054-002] “My Son, suffer me to do with thee what I will; I know what is

[054-003] expedient for thee. Thou thinkest as a man, in many things thou judgest

[054-004] as human affection persuadeth thee.”

[054-005] 2. [[jesus-christ|Lord]], what Thou sayest is true. Greater is Thy care for me than all

[054-006] the care which I am able to take for myself. For too insecurely doth he

[054-007] stand who casteth not all his care upon Thee. Lord, so long as my will

[054-008] standeth right and firm in Thee, do with me what Thou wilt, for

[054-009] whatsoever Thou shalt do with me cannot be aught but good. Blessed be

[054-010] Thou if Thou wilt leave me in darkness: blessed also be Thou if Thou

[054-011] wilt leave me in light. Blessed be Thou if Thou vouchsafe to comfort

[054-012] me, and always blessed be Thou if Thou cause me to be troubled.

[054-013] 3. “My Son! even thus thou must stand if thou desirest to walk with Me.

[054-014] Thou must be ready alike for suffering or rejoicing. Thou must be poor

[054-015] and needy as willingly as full and rich.”

[054-016] 4. Lord, I will willingly bear for Thee whatsoever Thou wilt have to

[054-017] come upon me. Without choice I will receive from Thy hand good and

[054-018] evil, sweet and bitter, joy and sadness, and will give Thee thanks for

[054-019] all things which shall happen unto me. Keep me from all sin, and I will

[054-020] not fear death nor hell. Only cast me not away for ever, nor blot me

[054-021] out of the book of life. Then no [[suffering|tribulation]] which shall come upon me

[054-022] shall do me hurt.
