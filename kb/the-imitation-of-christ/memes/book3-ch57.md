[094-001] That a man must not be too much cast down when he falleth into some

[094-002] faults

[094-003] “My Son, patience and humility in adversities are more pleasing to Me

[094-004] than much comfort and devotion in prosperity. Why doth a little thing

[094-005] spoken against thee make thee sad? If it had been more, thou still

[094-006] oughtest not to be moved. But now suffer it to go by; it is not the

[094-007] first, it is not new, and it will not be the last, if thou live long.

[094-008] Thou art brave enough, so long as no [[suffering|adversity]] meeteth thee. Thou

[094-009] givest good counsel also, and knowest how to strengthen others with thy

[094-010] words; but when tribulation suddenly knocketh at thine own door, thy

[094-011] counsel and strength fail. Consider thy great frailty, which thou dost

[094-012] so often experience in trifling matters nevertheless, for thy soul’s

[094-013] health these things are done when they and such like happen unto thee.

[094-014] 2. “Put them away from thy heart as well as thou canst, and if

[094-015] tribulation hath touched thee, yet let it not cast thee down nor

[094-016] entangle thee long. At the least, bear patiently, if thou canst not

[094-017] joyfully. And although thou be very unwilling to hear it, and feel

[094-018] indignation, yet check thyself, and suffer no unadvised word to come

[094-019] forth from thy lips, whereby the little ones may be offended. Soon the

[094-020] storm which hath been raised shall be stilled, and inward grief shall

[094-021] be sweetened by returning grace. I yet live, saith the [[jesus-christ|Lord]], ready to

[094-022] help thee, and to give thee more than wonted [[comfort|consolation]] if thou put

[094-023] thy [[trust-in-god|trust]] in Me, and call devoutly upon Me.

[094-024] 3. “Be thou more calm of spirit, and gird thyself for greater

[094-025] [[patience|endurance]]. All is not frustrated, though thou find thyself very often

[094-026] afflicted or grievously tempted. Thou art man, not God; thou art flesh,

[094-027] not an angel. How shouldst thou be able to remain alway in the same

[094-028] state of virtue, when an angel in heaven fell, and the first man in

[094-029] paradise? I am He who lifteth up the mourners to deliverance, and those

[094-030] who know their own infirmity I raise up to my own nature.”

[094-031] 4. O Lord, blessed be Thy word, sweeter to my mouth than honey and the

[094-032] honeycomb. What should I do in my so great tribulations and anxieties,

[094-033] unless Thou didst comfort me with Thy holy words? If only I may attain

[094-034] unto the haven of salvation, what matter is it what things or how many

[094-035] I suffer? Give me a good end, give me a happy passage out of this

[094-036] world. Remember me, O my God, and lead me by the right way unto Thy

[094-037] Kingdom. Amen.
