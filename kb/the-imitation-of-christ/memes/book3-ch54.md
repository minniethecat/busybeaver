[091-001] Of the diverse motions of [[nature|Nature]] and of [[grace|Grace]]

[091-002] “My Son, pay diligent heed to the motions of Nature and of Grace,

[091-003] because they move in a very contrary and subtle manner, and are hardly

[091-004] distinguished save by a spiritual and inwardly enlightened man. All men

[091-005] indeed seek good, and make pretence of something good in all that they

[091-006] say or do; and thus under the appearance of good many are deceived.

[091-007] 2. “Nature is deceitful and draweth away, ensnareth, and deceiveth

[091-008] many, and always hath self for her end; but Grace walketh in simplicity

[091-009] and turneth away from every appearance of evil, maketh no false

[091-010] pretences, and doeth all entirely for the sake of God, in whom also she

[091-011] finally resteth.

[091-012] 3. “Nature is very unwilling to die, and to be pressed down, and to be

[091-013] overcome, and to be in [[obedience|subjection]], and to bear the yoke readily; but

[091-014] Grace studieth [[self-denial|self-mortification]], resisteth sensuality, seeketh to be

[091-015] subdued, longeth to be conquered, and willeth not to use her own

[091-016] liberty. She loveth to be held by discipline, and not to have authority

[091-017] over any, but always to live, to remain, to have her being under God,

[091-018] and for God’s sake is ready to be humbly subject to every ordinance of

[091-019] man.

[091-020] 4. “Nature laboureth for her own advantage, and considereth what profit

[091-021] she may gain from another; but Grace considereth more, not what may be

[091-022] useful and convenient to self, but what may be profitable to the many.

[091-023] 5. “Nature willingly receiveth honour and reverence; but Grace

[091-024] faithfully ascribeth all honour and glory to God.

[091-025] 6. “Nature feareth confusion and contempt, but Grace rejoiceth to

[091-026] suffer shame for the name of [[jesus-christ|Jesus]].

[091-027] 7. “Nature loveth ease and bodily [[silence|quiet]]; Grace cannot be unemployed,

[091-028] but gladly embraceth labour.

[091-029] 8. “Nature seeketh to possess things curious and attractive, and

[091-030] abhorreth those which are rough and cheap; Grace is delighted with

[091-031] things simple and humble, despiseth not those which are rough, nor

[091-032] refuseth to be clothed with old garments.

[091-033] 9. “Nature hath regard to things temporal, rejoiceth in earthly lucre,

[091-034] is made sad by loss, vexed by any little injurious word; but Grace

[091-035] reacheth after things eternal, cleaveth not to those which are

[091-036] temporal, is not perturbed by losses, nor embittered by any hard words,

[091-037] because she hath placed her treasure and joy in heaven where nought

[091-038] perisheth.

[091-039] 10. “Nature is covetous, and receiveth more willingly than she giveth,

[091-040] loveth things that are personal and private to herself; while Grace is

[091-041] kind and generous, avoideth selfishness, is contented with a little,

[091-042] believeth that it is more blessed to give than to receive.

[091-043] 11. “Nature inclineth thee to [[creation|created things]], to thine own flesh, to

[091-044] [[vanity|vanities]] and dissipation; but Grace draweth to God and to virtues,

[091-045] renounceth creatures, fleeth from [[world|the world]], hateth the desires of the

[091-046] flesh, restraineth vagaries, blusheth to be seen in public.

[091-047] 12. “Nature is glad to receive some outward solace in which the senses

[091-048] may have delight; but Grace seeketh to be comforted in God alone, and

[091-049] to have delight in the chief good above all visible things.

[091-050] 13. “Nature doeth everything for her own gain and profit, can do

[091-051] nothing as a free favour, but hopeth to attain something as good or

[091-052] better, or some praise or favour for her benefits; and she loveth that

[091-053] her own deeds and gifts should be highly valued; but Grace seeketh

[091-054] nothing temporal, nor requireth any other gift of reward than God

[091-055] alone; neither longeth she for more of temporal necessities than such

[091-056] as may suffice for the attaining of [[eternity|eternal life]].

[091-057] 14. “Nature rejoiceth in many friends and kinsfolk, she boasteth of

[091-058] noble place and noble birth, she smileth on the powerful, flattereth

[091-059] the rich, applaudeth those who are like herself; but Grace loveth even

[091-060] her enemies, and is not lifted up by the multitude of friends, setteth

[091-061] no store upon high place or high birth, unless there be greater virtue

[091-062] therewith; favoureth the poor man more than the rich, hath more

[091-063] sympathy with the innocent than with the powerful; rejoiceth with the

[091-064] truthful, not with the liar; always exhorteth the good to strive after

[091-065] better gifts of grace, and to become by holiness like unto the Son of

[091-066] God.

[091-067] 15. “Nature quickly complaineth of poverty and of trouble; Grace

[091-068] beareth want with constancy.

[091-069] 16. “Nature looketh upon all things in reference to herself; striveth

[091-070] and argueth for self; but Grace bringeth back all things to God from

[091-071] whom they came at the beginning; ascribeth no good to herself nor

[091-072] arrogantly presumeth; is not contentious, nor preferreth her own

[091-073] opinion to others, but in every sense and understanding submitteth

[091-074] herself to the Eternal wisdom and the Divine judgment.

[091-075] 17. “Nature is eager to know secrets and to hear new things; she loveth

[091-076] to appear abroad, and to make experience of many things through the

[091-077] senses; she desireth to be acknowledged and to do those things which

[091-078] win praise and admiration; but Grace careth not to gather up new or

[091-079] curious things, because all this springeth from the old corruption,

[091-080] whereas there is nothing new or lasting upon earth. So she teacheth to

[091-081] restrain the senses, to shun vain complacency and ostentation, to hide

[091-082] humbly those things which merit praise and real admiration, and from

[091-083] everything and in all knowledge to seek after useful fruit, and the

[091-084] praise and honour of God. She desireth not to receive praise for

[091-085] herself or her own, but longeth that God be blessed in all His gifts,

[091-086] who out of unmingled love bestoweth all things.”

[091-087] 18. This Grace is a supernatural light, and a certain special gift of

[091-088] God, and the proper mark of the elect, and the pledge of eternal

[091-089] salvation; it exalteth a man from earthly things to love those that are

[091-090] heavenly; and it maketh the carnal man spiritual. So far therefore as

[091-091] Nature is utterly pressed down and overcome, so far is greater Grace

[091-092] bestowed and the inner man is daily created anew by fresh visitations,

[091-093] after the image of God.
