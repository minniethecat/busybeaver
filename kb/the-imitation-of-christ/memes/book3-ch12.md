[049-001] Of the inward growth of patience, and of the struggle against evil

[049-002] desires

[049-003] O [[jesus-christ|Lord]] God, I see that patience is very necessary unto me; for many

[049-004] things in this life fall out contrary. For howsoever I may have

[049-005] contrived for my peace, my life cannot go on without strife and

[049-006] trouble.

[049-007] 2. “Thou speakest truly, My Son. For I will not that thou seek such a

[049-008] peace as is without trials, and knoweth no adversities; but rather that

[049-009] thou shouldest judge thyself to have found peace, when thou art tried

[049-010] with manifold tribulations, and proved by many adversities. If thou

[049-011] shalt say that thou art not able to bear much, how then wilt thou

[049-012] sustain the fire hereafter? Of two evils we should always choose the

[049-013] less. Therefore, that thou mayest escape eternal torments hereafter,

[049-014] strive on God’s behalf to endure present evils bravely. Thinkest thou

[049-015] that the children of this world suffer nought, or but little? Thou wilt

[049-016] not find it so, even though thou find out the most prosperous.

[049-017] 3. “‘But,’ thou wilt say, ‘they have many delights, and they follow

[049-018] their own wills, and thus they bear lightly their tribulations.’

[049-019] 4. “Be it so, grant that they have what they list; but how long,

[049-020] thinkest thou, will it last? Behold, like the smoke those who are rich

[049-021] in this world will pass away, and no record shall remain of their past

[049-022] joys. Yea, even while they yet live, they rest not without bitterness

[049-023] and weariness and fear. For from the very same thing wherein they find

[049-024] delight, thence they oftentimes have the punishment of sorrow. Justly

[049-025] it befalleth them, that because out of measure they seek out and pursue

[049-026] pleasures, they enjoy them not without confusion and bitterness. Oh how

[049-027] short, how false, how inordinate and wicked are all these pleasures!

[049-028] Yet because of their sottishness and blindness men do not understand;

[049-029] but like brute beasts, for the sake of a little pleasure of this

[049-030] corruptible life, they incur death of the soul. Thou therefore, my son,

[049-031] go not after thy lusts, but refrain thyself from thine appetites.(1)

[049-032] Delight thou in the Lord, and He shall give thee thy heart’s desire.(2)

[049-033] 5. “For if thou wilt truly find delight, and be abundantly comforted of

[049-034] Me, behold in the contempt of all [[world|worldly things]] and in the avoidance

[049-035] of all worthless pleasures shall be thy blessing, and fulness of

[049-036] [[comfort|consolation]] shall be given thee. And the more thou withdrawest thyself

[049-037] from all solace of [[creation|creatures]], the more sweet and powerful consolations

[049-038] shalt thou find. But at the first thou shalt not attain to them,

[049-039] without some sorrow and hard striving. Long-accustomed habit will

[049-040] oppose, but it shall be overcome by better habit. The flesh will murmur

[049-041] again and again, but will be restrained by [[devotion|fervour]] of spirit. The old

[049-042] serpent will urge and embitter thee, but will be put to flight by

[049-043] prayer; moreover, by useful labour his entrance will be greatly

[049-044] obstructed.”

[049-045] (1) Ecclesiastes xviii. 30. (2) Psalm xxxvii. 4.
