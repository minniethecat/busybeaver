[008-001] Of the danger of too much familiarity

[008-002] Open not thine heart to every man, but deal with one who is wise and

[008-003] feareth God. Be seldom with the young and with strangers. Be not a

[008-004] flatterer of the rich; nor willingly seek the society of the great. Let

[008-005] thy company be the humble and the simple, the devout and the gentle,

[008-006] and let thy discourse be concerning things which edify. Be not familiar

[008-007] with any woman, but commend all good women alike unto God. Choose for

[008-008] thy companions God and His Angels only, and flee from the notice of

[008-009] men.

[008-010] 2. We must love all men, but not make close companions of all. It

[008-011] sometimes falleth out that one who is unknown to us is highly regarded

[008-012] through good report of him, whose actual person is nevertheless

[008-013] unpleasing to those who behold it. We sometimes think to please others

[008-014] by our intimacy, and forthwith displease them the more by the

[008-015] faultiness of character which they perceive in us.
