[100-001] That many good gifts are bestowed upon those who Communicate devoutly

[100-002] The Voice of the Disciple

[100-003] O [[jesus-christ|Lord]] my God, prevent Thou Thy servant with the blessings of Thy

[100-004] sweetness, that I may be enabled to draw near worthily and devoutly to

[100-005] Thy glorious Sacrament. Awaken my heart towards Thee, and deliver me

[100-006] from heavy slumber. Visit me with Thy salvation that I may in spirit

[100-007] taste Thy sweetness, which plentifully lieth hid in this Sacrament as

[100-008] in a fountain. Lighten also mine eyes to behold this so great mystery,

[100-009] and strengthen me that I may believe it with undoubting faith. For it

[100-010] is Thy word, not human power; it is Thy holy institution, not the

[100-011] invention of man. For no man is found fit in himself to receive and to

[100-012] understand these things, which transcend even the wisdom of the Angels.

[100-013] What portion then shall I, unworthy sinner, who am but dust and ashes,

[100-014] be able to search into and comprehend of so deep a Sacrament?

[100-015] 2. O Lord, in the simplicity of my heart, in good and firm faith, and

[100-016] according to Thy will, I draw nigh unto Thee with hope and reverence,

[100-017] and truly believe that Thou art here present in the Sacrament, God and

[100-018] man. Thou willest therefore that I receive Thee and unite myself to

[100-019] Thee in [[love|charity]]. Wherefore I beseech Thy [[forgiveness|mercy]], and implore Thee to

[100-020] give me Thy special grace, to this end, that I may be wholly dissolved

[100-021] and overflow with love towards Thee, and no more suffer any other

[100-022] [[comfort|consolation]] to enter into me. For this most high and most glorious

[100-023] Sacrament is the health of the soul and the body, the medicine of all

[100-024] spiritual sickness, whereby I am healed of my [[sin|sins]], my passions are

[100-025] bridled, temptations are conquered or weakened, more grace is poured

[100-026] into me, virtue begun is increased, faith is made firm, hope is

[100-027] strengthened, and charity is enkindled and enlarged.

[100-028] 3. For in this Sacrament Thou hast bestowed many good things and still

[100-029] bestowest them continually on Thine elect who communicate devoutly, O

[100-030] my God, Lifter up of my soul, Repairer of human infirmity, and Giver of

[100-031] all inward consolation. For Thou pourest into them much consolation

[100-032] against all sorts of [[suffering|tribulation]], and out of the deep of their own

[100-033] misery Thou liftest them up to the hope of Thy protection, and with

[100-034] ever new grace, dost inwardly refresh and enlighten them; so that they

[100-035] who felt themselves to be anxious and without affection before

[100-036] [[communion|Communion]], afterwards being refreshed with heavenly food and drink,

[100-037] find themselves changed for the better. And even in such wise Thou

[100-038] dealest severally with Thine elect, that they may truly acknowledge and

[100-039] clearly make proof that they have nothing whatsoever of their own, and

[100-040] what goodness and grace come to them from Thee; because being in

[100-041] themselves cold, hard of heart, indevout, through Thee they become

[100-042] fervent, zealous, and devout. For who is there coming humbly to the

[100-043] fountain of sweetness, carrieth not away thence at the least some

[100-044] little of that sweetness? Or who standing by a large fire, feeleth not

[100-045] from thence a little of its heat? And Thou art ever a full and

[100-046] overflowing fountain, a fire continually burning, and never going out.

[100-047] 4. Wherefore if it is not suffered to me to draw from the fulness of

[100-048] the fountain, nor to drink unto satisfying, yet will I set my lips to

[100-049] the mouth of the heavenly conduit, that at least I may receive a small

[100-050] drop to quench my thirst, that I dry not up within my heart. And if I

[100-051] am not yet able to be altogether heavenly and so enkindled as the

[100-052] Cherubim and Seraphim, yet will I endeavour to give myself unto

[100-053] devotion, and to prepare my heart, that I may gain if it be but a

[100-054] little flame of the divine fire, through the humble receiving of the

[100-055] life-giving Sacrament. But whatsoever is wanting unto me, O merciful

[100-056] Jesus, Most Holy Saviour, do Thou of Thy kindness and grace supply, who

[100-057] hast vouchsafed to call all unto Thee, saying, Come unto me, all ye

[100-058] that are weary and heavy laden, and I will refresh you.

[100-059] 5. I indeed labour in the sweat of my face, I am tormented with sorrow

[100-060] of heart, I am burdened with sins, I am disquieted with temptations, I

[100-061] am entangled and oppressed with many passions, and there is none to

[100-062] help me, there is none to deliver and ease me, but Thou, O Lord God, my

[100-063] Saviour, to whom I commit myself and all things that are mine, that

[100-064] Thou mayest preserve me and lead me unto life eternal.

[100-065] Receive me unto the praise and glory of Thy name, who hast prepared

[100-066] Thy Body and Blood to be my meat and drink. Grant, O Lord God my

[100-067] Saviour, that with coming often to Thy mysteries the zeal of my

[100-068] devotion may increase.
