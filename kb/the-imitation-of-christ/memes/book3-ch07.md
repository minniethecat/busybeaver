[044-001] Of hiding our grace under the guard of humility

[044-002] “My Son, it is better and safer for thee to hide the grace of devotion,

[044-003] and not to lift thyself up on high, nor to speak much thereof, nor to

[044-004] value it greatly; but rather to despise thyself, and to fear as though

[044-005] this grace were given to one unworthy thereof. Nor must thou depend too

[044-006] much upon this feeling, for it can very quickly be turned into its

[044-007] opposite. Think when thou art in a state of grace how miserable and

[044-008] poor thou art wont to be without grace. Nor is there advance in

[044-009] spiritual life in this alone, that thou hast the grace of [[comfort|consolation]],

[044-010] but that thou humbly and unselfishly and patiently takest the

[044-011] withdrawal thereof; so that thou cease not from the exercise of prayer,

[044-012] nor suffer thy other common duties to be in anywise neglected; rather

[044-013] do thy task more readily, as though thou hadst gained more strength and

[044-014] knowledge; and do not altogether neglect thyself because of the dearth

[044-015] and anxiety of spirit which thou feelest.

[044-016] 2. “For there are many who, when things have not gone prosperous with

[044-017] them, become forthwith impatient or slothful. For the way of a man is

[044-018] not in himself,(1) but it is God’s to give and to console, when He

[044-019] will, and as much as He will, and whom He will, as it shall please Him,

[044-020] and no further. Some who were presumptuous because of the grace of

[044-021] devotion within them, have destroyed themselves, because they would do

[044-022] more than they were able, not considering the measure of their own

[044-023] littleness, but rather following the impulse of the heart than the

[044-024] judgment of the reason. And because they presumed beyond what was

[044-025] well-pleasing unto God, therefore they quickly lost grace. They became

[044-026] poor and were left vile, who had built for themselves their nest in

[044-027] heaven; so that being humbled and stricken with poverty, they might

[044-028] learn not to fly with their own wings, but to put their [[trust-in-god|trust]] under My

[044-029] feathers. They who are as yet new and unskilled in the way of the [[jesus-christ|Lord]],

[044-030] unless they rule themselves after the counsel of the wise, may easily

[044-031] be deceived and led away.

[044-032] 3. “But if they wish to follow their own fancies rather than trust the

[044-033] experience of others, the result will be very dangerous to them if they

[044-034] still refuse to be drawn away from their own notion. Those who are wise

[044-035] in their own conceits, seldom patiently endure to be ruled by others.

[044-036] It is better to have a small portion of wisdom with humility, and a

[044-037] slender understanding, than great treasures of sciences with vain

[044-038] self-esteem. It is better for thee to have less than much of what may

[044-039] make thee proud. He doeth not very discreetly who giveth up himself

[044-040] entirely to joy, forgetting his former helplessness and the [[fear-of-the-lord|chaste fear]]

[044-041] of the Lord, which feareth to lose the grace offered. Nor is he very

[044-042] wise, after a manly sort, who in time of [[suffering|adversity]], or any trouble

[044-043] whatsoever, beareth himself too despairingly, and feeleth concerning Me

[044-044] less trustfully than he ought.

[044-045] 4. “He who in time of peace willeth to be oversecure shall be often

[044-046] found in time of war overdispirited and full of fears. If thou knewest

[044-047] always how to continue humble and moderate in thyself, and to guide and

[044-048] rule thine own spirit well, thou wouldest not so quickly fall into

[044-049] danger and mischief. It is good counsel that when [[devotion|fervour]] of spirit is

[044-050] kindled, thou shouldest meditate how it will be with thee when the

[044-051] light is taken away. Which when it doth happen, remember that still the

[044-052] light may return again, which I have taken away for a time for a

[044-053] warning to thee, and also for mine own glory. Such a [[temptation|trial]] is often

[044-054] more useful than if thou hadst always things prosperous according to

[044-055] thine own will.

[044-056] 5. “For merits are not to be reckoned by this, that a man hath many

[044-057] visions or consolations, or that he is skilled in the Scriptures, or

[044-058] that he is placed in a high situation; but that he is grounded upon

[044-059] true humility and filled with divine [[love|charity]], that he always purely and

[044-060] uprightly seeketh the honour of God, that he setteth not by himself,

[044-061] but unfeignedly despiseth himself, and even rejoiceth to be despised

[044-062] and humbled by others more than to be honoured.”

[044-063] (1) [[jeremiah|Jeremiah]] x. 23.
