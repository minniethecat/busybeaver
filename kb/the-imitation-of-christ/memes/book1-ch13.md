[013-001] Of resisting temptation

[013-002] So long as we live in [[world|the world]], we cannot be without trouble and

[013-003] [[temptation|trial]]. Wherefore it is written in [[job|Job]], The life of man upon the earth

[013-004] is a trial.(1) And therefore ought each of us to give heed concerning

[013-005] trials and temptations, and watch unto prayer, lest the devil find

[013-006] occasion to deceive; for he never sleepeth, but goeth about seeking

[013-007] whom he may devour. No man is so perfect in holiness that he hath never

[013-008] temptations, nor can we ever be wholly free from them.

[013-009] 2. Yet, notwithstanding, temptations turn greatly unto our profit, even

[013-010] though they be great and hard to bear; for through them we are humbled,

[013-011] purified, instructed. All Saints have passed through much [[suffering|tribulation]]

[013-012] and temptation, and have profited thereby. And they who endured not

[013-013] temptation became reprobate and fell away. There is no position so

[013-014] sacred, no place so secret, that it is without temptations and

[013-015] adversities.

[013-016] 3. There is no man wholly free from temptations so long as he liveth,

[013-017] because we have the root of temptation within ourselves, in that we are

[013-018] born in concupiscence. One temptation or sorrow passeth, and another

[013-019] cometh; and always we shall have somewhat to suffer, for we have fallen

[013-020] from perfect [[blessedness|happiness]]. Many who seek to fly from temptations fall yet

[013-021] more deeply into them. By flight alone we cannot overcome, but by

[013-022] [[patience|endurance]] and true humility we are made stronger than all our enemies.

[013-023] 4. He who only resisteth outwardly and pulleth not up by the root,

[013-024] shall profit little; nay, rather temptations will return to him the

[013-025] more quickly, and will be the more terrible. Little by little, through

[013-026] patience and long-suffering, thou shalt conquer by the help of God,

[013-027] rather than by violence and thine own strength of will. In the midst of

[013-028] temptation often seek counsel; and deal not hardly with one who is

[013-029] tempted, but comfort and strengthen him as thou wouldest have done unto

[013-030] thyself.

[013-031] 5. The beginning of all temptations to evil is instability of temper

[013-032] and want of [[faith|trust in God]]; for even as a ship without a helm is tossed

[013-033] about by the waves, so is a man who is careless and infirm of purpose

[013-034] tempted, now on this side, now on that. As fire testeth iron, so doth

[013-035] temptation the upright man. Oftentimes we know not what strength we

[013-036] have; but temptation revealeth to us what we are. Nevertheless, we must

[013-037] watch, especially in the beginnings of temptation; for then is the foe

[013-038] the more easily mastered, when he is not suffered to enter within the

[013-039] mind, but is met outside the door as soon as he hath knocked. Wherefore

[013-040] one saith,

[013-041] Check the beginnings; once thou might’st have cured,

[013-042] But now ’tis past thy skill, too long hath it endured.

[013-043] For first cometh to the mind the simple suggestion, then the strong

[013-044] imagination, afterwards pleasure, evil affection, assent. And so little

[013-045] by little the enemy entereth in altogether, because he was not resisted

[013-046] at the beginning. And the longer a man delayeth his resistance, the

[013-047] weaker he groweth, and the stronger groweth the enemy against him.

[013-048] 6. Some men suffer their most grievous temptations in the beginning of

[013-049] their conversion, some at the end. Some are sorely tried their whole

[013-050] life long. Some there are who are tempted but lightly, according to the

[013-051] wisdom and justice of the ordering of God, who knoweth the character

[013-052] and circumstances of men, and ordereth all things for the welfare of

[013-053] His elect.

[013-054] 7. Therefore we ought not to despair when we are tempted, but the more

[013-055] fervently should cry unto God, that He will vouchsafe to help us in all

[013-056] our tribulation; and that He will, as [[apostle-paul|St. Paul]] saith, with the

[013-057] temptation make a way to escape that we may be able to bear it.(2) Let

[013-058] us therefore humble ourselves under the mighty hand of God in all

[013-059] temptation and trouble, for He will save and exalt such as are of an

[013-060] humble spirit.

[013-061] 8. In temptations and troubles a man is proved, what progress he hath

[013-062] made, and therein is his reward the greater, and his virtue doth the

[013-063] more appear. Nor is it a great thing if a man be devout and zealous so

[013-064] long as he suffereth no affliction; but if he behave himself patiently

[013-065] in the time of adversity, then is there hope of great progress. Some

[013-066] are kept safe from great temptations, but are overtaken in those which

[013-067] are little and common, that the humiliation may teach them not to [[trust-in-god|trust]]

[013-068] to themselves in great things, being weak in small things.

[013-069] (1) Job vii. 1 (Vulg.). (2) 1 Corinthians x. 13.
