[037-001] Of the royal way of the [[cross|Holy Cross]]

[037-002] That seemeth a hard saying to many, If any man will come after Me, let

[037-003] him deny himself and take up his Cross and follow Me.(1) But it will be

[037-004] much harder to hear that last sentence, Depart from me, ye wicked, into

[037-005] eternal fire.(2) For they who now willingly hear the word of the Cross

[037-006] and follow it, shall not then fear the hearing of eternal damnation.

[037-007] This sign of the Cross shall be in heaven when the [[jesus-christ|Lord]] cometh to

[037-008] [[judgment|Judgment]]. Then all servants of the Cross, who in life have conformed

[037-009] themselves to the Crucified, shall draw nigh unto Christ the Judge with

[037-010] great boldness.

[037-011] 2. Why fearest thou then to take up the cross which leadeth to a

[037-012] kingdom? In the Cross is health, in the Cross is life, in the Cross is

[037-013] protection from enemies, in the Cross is heavenly sweetness, in the

[037-014] Cross strength of mind, in the Cross joy of the spirit, in the Cross

[037-015] the height of virtue, in the Cross perfection of holiness. There is no

[037-016] health of the soul, no hope of [[eternity|eternal life]], save in the Cross. Take up

[037-017] therefore, thy cross and follow Jesus and thou shalt go into eternal

[037-018] life. He went before thee bearing His Cross and died for thee upon the

[037-019] Cross, that thou also mayest bear thy cross and mayest love to be

[037-020] crucified upon it. For if thou be dead with Him, thou shalt also live

[037-021] with Him, and if thou be a partaker of His sufferings thou shalt be

[037-022] also of His glory.

[037-023] 3. Behold everything dependeth upon the Cross, and everything lieth in

[037-024] dying; and there is none other way unto life and to true [[peace|inward peace]],

[037-025] except the way of the Holy Cross and of [[mortification|daily mortification]]. Go where

[037-026] thou wilt, seek whatsoever thou wilt, and thou shalt find no higher way

[037-027] above nor safer way below, than the way of the Holy Cross. Dispose and

[037-028] order all things according to thine own will and judgment, and thou

[037-029] shalt ever find something to suffer either willingly or unwillingly,

[037-030] and thus thou shalt ever find thy cross. For thou shalt either feel

[037-031] pain of body, or [[suffering|tribulation]] of spirit within thy soul.

[037-032] 4. Sometimes thou wilt be forsaken of God, sometimes thou wilt be tried

[037-033] by thy neighbour, and which is more, thou wilt often be wearisome to

[037-034] thyself. And still thou canst not be delivered nor eased by any remedy

[037-035] or consolation, but must bear so long as God will. For God will have

[037-036] thee learn to suffer tribulation without consolation, and to submit

[037-037] thyself fully to it, and by tribulation be made more humble. No man

[037-038] understandeth the [[crucifixion|Passion of Christ]] in his heart so well as he who hath

[037-039] had somewhat of the like suffering himself. The Cross therefore is

[037-040] always ready, and every where waiteth for thee. Thou canst not flee

[037-041] from it whithersoever thou hurriest, for whithersoever thou comest,

[037-042] thou bearest thyself with thee, and shalt ever find thyself. Turn thee

[037-043] above, turn thee below, turn thee without, turn thee within, and in

[037-044] them all thou shalt find the Cross; and needful is it that thou

[037-045] everywhere possess patience if thou wilt have internal peace and gain

[037-046] the everlasting crown.

[037-047] 5. If thou willingly bear the Cross, it will bear thee, and will bring

[037-048] thee to the end which thou seekest, even where there shall be the end

[037-049] of suffering; though it shall not be here. If thou bear it unwillingly,

[037-050] thou makest a burden for thyself and greatly increaseth thy load, and

[037-051] yet thou must bear it. If thou cast away one cross, without doubt thou

[037-052] shalt find another and perchance a heavier.

[037-053] 6. Thinketh thou to escape what no mortal hath been able to avoid?

[037-054] Which of the saints in [[world|the world]] hath been without the cross and

[037-055] tribulation? For not even Jesus Christ our Lord was one hour without

[037-056] the anguish of His Passion, so long as He lived. It behooved, He said,

[037-057] Christ to suffer and to rise from the dead, and so enter into his

[037-058] glory.(3) And how dost thou seek another way than this royal way, which

[037-059] is the way of the Holy Cross?

[037-060] 7. The whole life of Christ was a cross and martyrdom, and dost thou

[037-061] seek for thyself rest and joy? Thou art wrong, thou art wrong, if thou

[037-062] seekest aught but to suffer tribulations, for this whole mortal life is

[037-063] full of miseries, and set round with crosses. And the higher a man hath

[037-064] advanced in the spirit, the heavier crosses he will often find, because

[037-065] the sorrow of his banishment increaseth with the strength of his love.

[037-066] 8. But yet the man who is thus in so many wise afflicted, is not

[037-067] without refreshment of consolation, because he feeleth abundant fruit

[037-068] to be growing within him out of the bearing of his cross. For whilst he

[037-069] willingly submitteth himself to it, every burden of tribulation is

[037-070] turned into an assurance of [[consolation|divine comfort]], and the more the flesh is

[037-071] wasted by affliction, the more is the spirit strengthened mightily by

[037-072] inward grace. And ofttimes so greatly is he comforted by the desire for

[037-073] tribulation and adversity, through love of conformity to the Cross of

[037-074] Christ, that he would not be without sorrow and tribulation; for he

[037-075] believeth that he shall be the more acceptable to God, the more and the

[037-076] heavier burdens he is able to bear for His sake. This is not the virtue

[037-077] of man, but the grace of Christ which hath such power and energy in the

[037-078] weak flesh, that what it naturally hateth and fleeth from, this it

[037-079] draweth to and loveth through [[devotion|fervour]] of spirit.

[037-080] 9. It is not in the nature of man to bear the cross, to love the cross,

[037-081] to keep under the body and to bring it into [[obedience|subjection]], to fly from

[037-082] honours, to bear reproaches meekly, to despise self and desire to be

[037-083] despised, to bear all adversities and losses, and to desire no

[037-084] prosperity in this world. If thou lookest to thyself, thou wilt of

[037-085] thyself be able to do none of this; but if thou trustest in the Lord,

[037-086] [[patience|endurance]] shall be given thee from heaven, and the world and the flesh

[037-087] shall be made subject to thy command. Yea, thou shalt not even fear

[037-088] thine adversary the devil, if thou be armed with faith and signed with

[037-089] the Cross of Christ.

[037-090] 10. Set thyself, therefore, like a good and faithful servant of Christ,

[037-091] to the manful bearing of the Cross of thy Lord, who out of love was

[037-092] crucified for thee. Prepare thyself for the bearing many adversities

[037-093] and manifold troubles in this wretched life; because so it shall be

[037-094] with thee wheresoever thou art, and so in very deed thou shalt find it,

[037-095] wherever thou hide thyself. This it must be; and there is no means of

[037-096] escaping from tribulation and sorrow, except to bear them patiently.

[037-097] Drink thou lovingly thy Lord’s cup if thou desirest to be His friend

[037-098] and to have thy lot with Him. Leave consolations to God, let Him do as

[037-099] seemeth best to Him concerning them. But do thou set thyself to endure

[037-100] tribulations, and reckon them the best consolations; for the sufferings

[037-101] of this present time are not worthy to be compared with the glory which

[037-102] shall be revealed in us,(4) nor would they be even if thou wert to

[037-103] endure them all.

[037-104] 11. When thou hast come to this, that tribulation is sweet and pleasant

[037-105] to thee for Christ’s sake, then reckon that it is well with thee,

[037-106] because thou hast found paradise on earth. So long as it is hard to

[037-107] thee to suffer and thou desirest to escape, so long it will not be well

[037-108] with thee, and tribulations will follow thee everywhere.

[037-109] 12. If thou settest thyself to that thou oughtest, namely, to suffer

[037-110] and to die, it shall soon go better with thee, and thou shalt find

[037-111] peace. Though thou shouldest be caught up with Paul unto the third

[037-112] heaven,(5) thou art not on that account secure from suffering evil. I

[037-113] will show him, saith Jesus, what great things he must suffer for My

[037-114] Name’s sake.(6) It remaineth, therefore, to thee to suffer, if thou

[037-115] wilt love Jesus and serve Him continually.

[037-116] 13. Oh that thou wert worthy to suffer something for the name of Jesus,

[037-117] how great glory should await thee, what rejoicing among all the saints

[037-118] of God, what bright example also to thy neighbour! For all men commend

[037-119] patience, although few be willing to practise it. Thou oughtest surely

[037-120] to suffer a little for Christ when many suffer heavier things for the

[037-121] world.

[037-122] 14. Know thou of a surety that thou oughtest to lead the life of a

[037-123] dying man. And the more a man dieth to himself, the more he beginneth

[037-124] to live towards God. None is fit for the understanding of heavenly

[037-125] things, unless he hath submitted himself to bearing adversities for

[037-126] Christ. Nothing more acceptable to God, nothing more healthful for

[037-127] thyself in this world, than to suffer willingly for Christ. And if it

[037-128] were thine to choose, thou oughtest rather to wish to suffer

[037-129] adversities for Christ, than to be refreshed with manifold

[037-130] consolations, for thou wouldest be more like Christ and more conformed

[037-131] to all saints. For our worthiness and growth in grace lieth not in many

[037-132] delights and consolations, but rather in bearing many troubles and

[037-133] adversities.

[037-134] 15. If indeed there had been anything better and more profitable to the

[037-135] health of men than to suffer, Christ would surely have shown it by word

[037-136] and example. For both the disciples who followed Him, and all who

[037-137] desire to follow Him, He plainly exhorteth to bear their cross, and

[037-138] saith, If any man will come after Me, let him deny himself and take up

[037-139] his cross, and follow Me.(7) So now that we have thoroughly read and

[037-140] studied all things, let us hear the conclusion of the whole matter. We

[037-141] must through much tribulation enter into the kingdom of God.(8)

[037-142] (1) Matthew xvi. 24. (2) Matthew xxv. 41. (3) Luke xxiv. 46. (4) Romans

[037-143] viii. 18. (5) 2 Corinthians xii. 2. (6) Acts ix. 16. (7) Luke ix. 23.

[037-144] (8) Acts xiv. 21.
