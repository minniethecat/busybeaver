[084-001] That all troubles are to be endured for the sake of [[eternity|eternal life]]

[084-002] “My Son, let not the labours which thou hast undertaken for Me break

[084-003] thee down, nor let tribulations cast thee down in any wise, but let my

[084-004] promise strengthen and comfort thee in every event. I am sufficient to

[084-005] reward thee above all measure and extent. Not long shalt thou labour

[084-006] here, nor always be weighed down with sorrows. Wait yet a little while,

[084-007] and thou shalt see a speedy end of thine evils. An hour shall come when

[084-008] all labour and confusion shall cease. Little and short is all that

[084-009] passeth away with time.

[084-010] 2. “Do earnestly what thou dost; labour faithfully in My vineyard; I

[084-011] will be thy reward. Write, read, sing, weep, be silent, pray, endure

[084-012] adversities manfully; eternal life is worthy of all these conflicts,

[084-013] yea, and of greater. [[peace|Peace]] shall come in one day which is known to the

[084-014] [[jesus-christ|Lord]]; which shall be neither day nor night,(1) but light eternal,

[084-015] infinite clearness, steadfast peace, and undisturbed rest. Thou shalt

[084-016] not say then, _Who shall deliver me from the body of this death?_(2)

[084-017] nor cry out, _Woe is me, for my sojourning is prolonged_,(3) because

[084-018] death will be utterly destroyed, and there shall be salvation which can

[084-019] never fail, no more anxiety, happy delight, sweet and noble society.

[084-020] 3. “Oh, if thou sawest the unfading crowns of the Saints in heaven, and

[084-021] with what great glory they now rejoice, who aforetime were reckoned by

[084-022] this world contemptibly and as it were unworthy of life, truly thou

[084-023] wouldst immediately humble thyself even to the earth, and wouldst

[084-024] desire rather to be in [[obedience|subjection]] to all, than to have authority over

[084-025] one; nor wouldst thou long for pleasant days of this life, but wouldst

[084-026] more rejoice to be afflicted for God’s sake, and wouldst esteem it gain

[084-027] to be counted for nought amongst men.

[084-028] 4. “Oh, if these things were sweet to thy taste, and moved thee to the

[084-029] bottom of thine heart, how shouldst thou dare even once to complain?

[084-030] Are not all laborious things to be endured for the sake of eternal

[084-031] life? It is no small thing, the losing or gaining the [[kingdom-of-god|Kingdom of God]].

[084-032] Lift up therefore thy face to heaven. Behold, I and all My Saints with

[084-033] Me, who in this world had a hard conflict, now rejoice, are now

[084-034] comforted, are now secure, are now at peace, and shall remain with Me

[084-035] evermore in the Kingdom of My Father.”

[084-036] (1) Zechariah xiv. 7. (2) Romans vii. 24. (3) Psalm cxx.
