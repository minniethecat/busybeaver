[063-001] Of the exaltation of a free spirit, which humble prayer more deserveth

[063-002] than doth frequent reading

[063-003] [[jesus-christ|Lord]], this is the work of a perfect man, never to slacken his mind from

[063-004] attention to heavenly things, and among many cares to pass along as it

[063-005] were without care, not after the manner of one indifferent, but rather

[063-006] with the privilege of a free mind, cleaving to no creature with

[063-007] inordinate affection.

[063-008] 2. I beseech Thee, my most merciful Lord God, preserve me from the

[063-009] cares of this life, lest I become too much entangled; from many

[063-010] necessities of the body, lest I be taken captive by pleasure; from all

[063-011] obstacles of the spirit, lest I be broken and cast down with cares. I

[063-012] say not from those things which the [[vanity|vanity of the world]] goeth about

[063-013] after with all eagerness, but from those miseries, which by the

[063-014] universal curse of mortality weigh down and hold back the soul of thy

[063-015] servant in punishment, that it cannot enter into liberty of spirit, so

[063-016] often as it would.

[063-017] 3. O my God, sweetness unspeakable, turn into bitterness all my fleshly

[063-018] [[comfort|consolation]], which draweth me away from the love of eternal things, and

[063-019] wickedly allureth toward itself by setting before me some present

[063-020] delight. Let not, O my God, let not flesh and blood prevail over me,

[063-021] let not [[world|the world]] and its short glory deceive me, let not the devil and

[063-022] his craftiness supplant me. Give me courage to resist, patience to

[063-023] endure, constancy to persevere. Grant, in place of all consolations of

[063-024] the world, the most sweet unction of Thy Spirit, and in place of carnal

[063-025] love, pour into me the love of Thy Name.

[063-026] 4. Behold, food and drink and clothing, and all the other needs

[063-027] appertaining to the support of the body, are burdensome to the devout

[063-028] spirit. Grant that I may use such things with moderation, and that I be

[063-029] not entangled with inordinate affection for them. To cast away all

[063-030] these things is not lawful, because nature must be sustained, but to

[063-031] require superfluities and things which merely minister delight, the

[063-032] holy law forbiddeth; for otherwise the flesh would wax insolent against

[063-033] the spirit. In all these things, I beseech Thee, let Thy hand guide and

[063-034] teach me, that I in no way exceed.
