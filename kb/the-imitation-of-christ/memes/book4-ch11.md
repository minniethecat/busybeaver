[107-001] That the Body and Blood of [[jesus-christ|Christ]] and the Holy Scriptures are most

[107-002] necessary to a faithful soul

[107-003] The Voice of the Disciple

[107-004] O most sweet Lord Jesus, how great is the blessedness of the devout

[107-005] soul that feedeth with Thee in Thy banquet, where there is set before

[107-006] it no other food than Thyself its only Beloved, more to be desired than

[107-007] all the desires of the heart? And to me it would verily be sweet to

[107-008] pour forth my tears in Thy presence from the very bottom of my heart,

[107-009] and with the pious Magdalene to water Thy feet with my tears. But where

[107-010] is this devotion? Where the abundant flowing of holy tears? Surely in

[107-011] Thy presence and in the presence of the holy Angels my whole heart

[107-012] ought to burn and to weep for joy; for I have Thee in the Sacrament

[107-013] verily present, although hidden under other form.

[107-014] 2. For in Thine own Divine brightness, mine eyes could not endure to

[107-015] behold Thee, neither could the whole world stand before the splendour

[107-016] of the glory of Thy Majesty. In this therefore Thou hast consideration

[107-017] unto my weakness, that Thou hidest Thyself under the Sacrament. I

[107-018] verily possess and adore Him whom the Angels adore in heaven; I yet for

[107-019] a while by faith, but they by sight and without a veil. It is good for

[107-020] me to be content with the light of true faith, and to walk therein

[107-021] until the day of eternal brightness dawn, and the shadows of figures

[107-022] flee away.(1) But when that which is perfect is come, the using of

[107-023] Sacraments shall cease, because the Blessed in [[glory|heavenly glory]] have no

[107-024] need of Sacramental remedy. For they rejoice unceasingly in the

[107-025] presence of God, beholding His glory face to face, and _being changed

[107-026] from glory to glory_(2) of the infinite God, they taste the Word of God

[107-027] made flesh, as He was in the beginning and remaineth for everlasting.

[107-028] 3. When I think on these wondrous things, even [[consolation|spiritual comfort]]

[107-029] whatsoever it be becometh sore weariness to me; for so long as I see

[107-030] not openly my Lord in His own Glory, I count for nothing all which I

[107-031] behold and hear in [[world|the world]]. Thou, O God, art my witness that nothing

[107-032] is able to comfort me, no creature is able to give me rest, save Thou,

[107-033] O my God, whom I desire to contemplate everlastingly. But this is not

[107-034] possible, so long as I remain in this mortal state. Therefore ought I

[107-035] to set myself unto great patience, and submit myself unto Thee in every

[107-036] desire. For even Thy Saints, O Lord, who now rejoice with Thee in the

[107-037] kingdom of heaven, waited for the coming of Thy glory whilst they lived

[107-038] here, in faith and great glory. What they believed, that believe I;

[107-039] what they hoped, I hope; whither they have attained to, thither through

[107-040] Thy grace hope I to come. I will walk meanwhile in faith, strengthened

[107-041] by the examples of the Saints. I will have also holy books for comfort

[107-042] and for a mirror of life, and above them all Thy most holy Body and

[107-043] Blood shall be for me a special remedy and refuge.

[107-044] 4. For two things do I feel to be exceedingly necessary to me in this

[107-045] life, without which this miserable life would be intolerable to me;

[107-046] being detained in the prison of this body, I confess that I need two

[107-047] things, even food and light. Thou hast therefore given to me who am so

[107-048] weak, Thy sacred Body and Blood, for the refreshing of my soul and

[107-049] body, and hast set _Thy Word for a lantern to my feet_.(3) Without

[107-050] these two I could not properly live; for the Word of God is the light

[107-051] of my soul, and Thy Sacrament the bread of life. These may also be

[107-052] called the two tables, placed on this side and on that, in the treasury

[107-053] of Thy holy Church. One table is that of the Sacred [[altar|Altar]], bearing the

[107-054] holy bread, that is the precious Body and Blood of Christ; the other is

[107-055] the table of the Divine [[law|Law]], containing holy doctrine, teaching the

[107-056] true faith, and leading steadfastly onwards even to that which is

[107-057] within the veil, where the Holy of Holies is.

[107-058] 5. Thanks be unto Thee, O Lord Jesus, Light of Light everlasting, for

[107-059] that table of holy doctrine which Thou has furnished unto us by Thy

[107-060] servants the Prophets and [[apostles|Apostles]] and other teachers. Thanks be to

[107-061] Thee, O Creator and Redeemer of men, who to make known Thy love to the

[107-062] whole world has prepared a great supper, in which Thou hast set forth

[107-063] for good not the typical lamb, but Thine own most Holy Body and Blood;

[107-064] making all Thy faithful ones joyful with this holy banquet and giving

[107-065] them to drink the cup of salvation, wherein are all the delights of

[107-066] [[heaven|Paradise]], and the holy Angels do feed with us, and with yet happier

[107-067] sweetness.

[107-068] 6. Oh how great and honourable is the office of the priests, to whom it

[107-069] is given to consecrate the Sacrament of the Lord of majesty with holy

[107-070] words, to bless it with the lips, to hold it in their hands, to receive

[107-071] it with their own mouth, and to administer it to others! Oh how clean

[107-072] ought those hands to be, how pure the mouth, how holy the body, how

[107-073] unspotted the heart of the priest, to whom so often the Author of

[107-074] purity entereth in! From the mouth of the priest ought naught to

[107-075] proceed but what is holy, what is honest and profitable, because he so

[107-076] often receiveth the Sacrament of Christ.

[107-077] 7. His eyes ought to be single and pure, seeing they are wont to look

[107-078] upon the Body of Christ; the hands should be pure and lifted towards

[107-079] heaven, which are wont to hold within them the Creator of heaven and

[107-080] earth. To priests is it specially said in the Law, _Be ye holy, for I

[107-081] the Lord your God am holy._(4)

[107-082] 8. Assist us with Thy grace, O Almighty God, that we who have taken

[107-083] upon us the priestly office, may be able to converse worthily and

[107-084] devoutly with Thee in all purity and [[conscience|good conscience]]. And if we are not

[107-085] able to have our conversation in such innocency of life as we ought,

[107-086] yet grant unto us worthily to lament the [[sin|sins]] which we have committed,

[107-087] and in the spirit of humility and full purpose of a good will, to serve

[107-088] Thee more earnestly for the future.

[107-089] (1) Cant. ii. 17. (2) 2 Corinthians iii. 18. (3) Psalm cxix. 105. (4)

[107-090] Leviticus xix. 2.
