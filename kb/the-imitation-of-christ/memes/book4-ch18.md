[114-001] That a man should not be a curious searcher of the Sacrament, but a

[114-002] humble imitator of [[jesus-christ|Christ]], submitting his sense to holy faith

[114-003] The Voice of the Beloved

[114-004] Thou must take heed of curious and useless searching into this most

[114-005] profound Sacrament, if thou wilt not be plunged into the abyss of

[114-006] doubt. He that is a searcher of Majesty shall be oppressed by the glory

[114-007] thereof.(1) God is able to do more than man can understand. A pious and

[114-008] humble search after truth is to be allowed, when it is always ready to

[114-009] be taught, and striving to walk after the wholesome opinions of the

[114-010] fathers.

[114-011] 2. Blessed is the simplicity which leaveth alone the difficult paths of

[114-012] questionings, and followeth the plain and firm steps of God’s

[114-013] [[law|commandments]]. Many have lost devotion whilst they sought to search into

[114-014] deeper things. [[faith|Faith]] is required of thee, and a sincere life, not

[114-015] loftiness of intellect, nor deepness in the mysteries of God. If thou

[114-016] understandest not nor comprehendest the things which are beneath thee,

[114-017] how shalt thou comprehend those which are above thee? Submit thyself

[114-018] unto God, and humble thy sense to faith, and the light of knowledge

[114-019] shall be given thee, as shall be profitable and necessary unto thee.

[114-020] 3. There are some who are grievously tempted concerning faith and the

[114-021] Sacrament; but this is not to be imputed to themselves but rather to

[114-022] the enemy. Care not then for this, dispute not with thine own thoughts,

[114-023] nor make answer to the doubts which are cast into thee by the devil;

[114-024] but believe the words of God, believe His Saints and Prophets, and the

[114-025] wicked enemy shall flee from thee. Often it profiteth much, that the

[114-026] servant of God endureth such things. For the enemy tempteth not

[114-027] unbelievers and sinners, because he already hath secure possession of

[114-028] them; but he tempteth and harasseth the faithful and devout by various

[114-029] means.

[114-030] 4. Go forward therefore with simple and undoubting faith, and draw nigh

[114-031] unto the Sacrament with supplicating reverence. And whatsoever thou art

[114-032] not enabled to understand, that commit without anxiety to Almighty God.

[114-033] God deceiveth thee not; he is deceived who believeth too much in

[114-034] himself. God walketh with the simple, revealeth Himself to the humble,

[114-035] giveth understanding to babes, openeth the sense to pure minds, and

[114-036] hideth grace from the curious and proud. Human reason is weak and may

[114-037] be deceived; but true faith cannot be deceived.

[114-038] 5. All reason and natural investigation ought to follow faith, not to

[114-039] precede, nor to break it. For faith and love do here especially take

[114-040] the highest place, and work in hidden ways in this most holy and

[114-041] exceeding excellent Sacrament. God who is eternal and incomprehensible,

[114-042] and of infinite power, doth great and inscrutable things in heaven and

[114-043] in earth, and His wonderful works are past finding out. If the works of

[114-044] God were of such sort that they might easily be comprehended by human

[114-045] reason, they should no longer be called wonderful or unspeakable.

[114-046] (1) Proverbs xxv. 27 (Vulg.).
