[088-001] That we must give ourselves to humble works when we are unequal to

[088-002] those that are lofty

[088-003] “My Son, thou art not always able to continue in very fervent desire

[088-004] after virtues, nor to stand fast in the loftier region of

[088-005] contemplation; but thou must of necessity sometimes descend to lower

[088-006] things because of thine original corruption, and bear about the burden

[088-007] of corruptible life, though unwillingly and with weariness. So long as

[088-008] thou wearest a mortal body, thou shalt feel weariness and heaviness of

[088-009] heart. Therefore thou oughtest to groan often in the flesh because of

[088-010] the burden of the flesh, inasmuch as thou canst not give thyself to

[088-011] spiritual studies and [[contemplation|divine contemplation]] unceasingly.

[088-012] 2. “At such a time it is expedient for thee to flee to humble and

[088-013] external works, and to renew thyself with good actions; to wait for My

[088-014] coming and heavenly visitation with sure confidence; to bear thy exile

[088-015] and drought of mind with patience, until thou be visited by Me again,

[088-016] and be freed from all anxieties. For I will cause thee to forget thy

[088-017] labours, and altogether to enjoy eternal peace. I will spread open

[088-018] before thee the pleasant pastures of the Scriptures, that with enlarged

[088-019] heart thou mayest begin to run in the way of My [[law|commandments]]. And thou

[088-020] shalt say, ‘The sufferings of this present time are not worthy to be

[088-021] compared with the glory which shall be revealed in us.’”(1)

[088-022] (1) Romans viii. 18.
