[103-001] Of the [[conscience|examination of conscience]], and purpose of amendment

[103-002] The Voice of [[jesus-christ|the Beloved]]

[103-003] Above all things the priest of God must draw nigh, with all humility of

[103-004] heart and supplicating reverence, with full faith and pious desire for

[103-005] the honour of God, to celebrate, minister, and receive this Sacrament.

[103-006] Diligently examine thy conscience and with all thy might with true

[103-007] contrition and [[confession|humble confession]] cleanse and purify it, so that thou

[103-008] mayest feel no burden, nor know anything which bringeth thee remorse

[103-009] and impedeth thy free approach. Have displeasure against all thy [[sin|sins]]

[103-010] in general, and specially sorrow and mourn because of thy daily

[103-011] transgressions. And if thou have time, confess unto God in the secret

[103-012] of thine heart, all miseries of thine own passion.

[103-013] 2. Lament grievously and be sorry, because thou art still so carnal and

[103-014] worldly, so unmortified from thy passions, so full of the motion of

[103-015] concupiscence, so unguarded in thine outward senses, so often entangled

[103-016] in many vain fancies, so much inclined to outward things, so negligent

[103-017] of internal; so ready to laughter and dissoluteness, so unready to

[103-018] weeping and contrition; so prone to ease and indulgence of the flesh,

[103-019] so dull to zeal and [[devotion|fervour]]; so curious to hear novelties and behold

[103-020] beauties, so loth to embrace things humble and despised; so desirous to

[103-021] have many things, so grudging in giving, so close in keeping; so

[103-022] inconsiderate in speaking, so reluctant to keep silence; so disorderly

[103-023] in manners, so inconsiderate in actions; so eager after food, so deaf

[103-024] towards the Word of God; so eager after rest, so slow to labour; so

[103-025] watchful after tales, so sleepy towards holy watchings; so eager for

[103-026] the end of them, so wandering in attention to them; so negligent in

[103-027] observing the hours of prayer, so lukewarm in celebrating, so

[103-028] unfruitful in communicating; so quickly distracted, so seldom quite

[103-029] collected with thyself; so quickly moved to anger, so ready for

[103-030] displeasure at others; so prone to judging, so severe at reproving; so

[103-031] joyful in prosperity, so weak in [[suffering|adversity]]; so often making many good

[103-032] resolutions and bringing them to so little effect.

[103-033] 3. When thou hast confessed and bewailed these and thy other

[103-034] shortcomings, with sorrow and sore displeasure at thine own infirmity,

[103-035] make then a firm resolution of continual [[amendment|amendment of life]] and of

[103-036] progress in all that is good. Then moreover with full resignation and

[103-037] entire will offer thyself to the honour of My name on the altar of

[103-038] thine heart as a perpetual whole burnt-offering, even by faithfully

[103-039] presenting thy body and soul unto Me, to the end that thou mayest so be

[103-040] accounted worthy to draw near to offer this sacrifice of praise and

[103-041] [[gratitude|thanksgiving]] to God, and to receive the Sacrament of My Body and Blood

[103-042] to thy soul’s health. For there is no oblation worthier, no

[103-043] satisfaction greater for the destroying of sin, than that a man offer

[103-044] himself to God purely and entirely with the oblation of the Body and

[103-045] Blood of Christ in the [[communion|Holy Communion]]. If a man shall have done what in

[103-046] him lieth, and shall repent him truly, then how often soever he shall

[103-047] draw nigh unto Me for [[forgiveness|pardon]] and grace, As I live, saith the Lord, I

[103-048] have no pleasure in the death of a sinner, but rather that he should be

[103-049] converted, and live. All his transgressions that he hath committed,

[103-050] they shall not be mentioned unto him.(1)

[103-051] (1) Ezekiel xviii. 22, 23.
