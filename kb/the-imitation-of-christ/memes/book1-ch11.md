[011-001] Of seeking [[peace|peace of mind]] and of spiritual progress

[011-002] We may enjoy abundance of peace if we refrain from busying ourselves

[011-003] with the sayings and doings of others, and things which concern not

[011-004] ourselves. How can he abide long time in peace who occupieth himself

[011-005] with other men’s matters, and with things without himself, and

[011-006] meanwhile payeth little or rare heed to the self within? Blessed are

[011-007] the single-hearted, for they shall have abundance of peace.

[011-008] 2. How came it to pass that many of the Saints were so perfect, so

[011-009] contemplative of Divine things? Because they steadfastly sought to

[011-010] mortify themselves from all worldly desires, and so were enabled to

[011-011] cling with their whole heart to God, and be free and at leisure for the

[011-012] thought of Him. We are too much occupied with our own affections, and

[011-013] too anxious about transitory things. Seldom, too, do we entirely

[011-014] conquer even a single [[sin|fault]], nor are we zealous for daily growth in

[011-015] grace. And so we remain lukewarm and unspiritual.

[011-016] 3. Were we fully watchful of ourselves, and not bound in spirit to

[011-017] outward things, then might we be wise unto salvation, and make progress

[011-018] in Divine contemplation. Our great and grievous stumbling-block is

[011-019] that, not being freed from our affections and desires, we strive not to

[011-020] enter into the perfect way of the Saints. And when even a little

[011-021] trouble befalleth us, too quickly are we cast down, and fly to the

[011-022] world to give us comfort.

[011-023] 4. If we would quit ourselves like men, and strive to stand firm in the

[011-024] battle, then should we see the [[jesus-christ|Lord]] helping us from [[heaven|Heaven]]. For He

[011-025] Himself is alway ready to help those who strive and who [[trust-in-god|trust]] in Him;

[011-026] yea, He provideth for us occasions of striving, to the end that we may

[011-027] win the victory. If we look upon our progress in religion as a progress

[011-028] only in outward observances and forms, our [[devotion|devoutness]] will soon come to

[011-029] an end. But let us lay the axe to the very root of our life, that,

[011-030] being cleansed from affections, we may possess our souls in peace.

[011-031] 5. If each year should see one fault rooted out from us, we should go

[011-032] quickly on to perfection. But on the contrary, we often feel that we

[011-033] were better and holier in the beginning of our conversion than after

[011-034] many years of profession. [[zeal|Zeal]] and progress ought to increase day by

[011-035] day; yet now it seemeth a great thing if one is able to retain some

[011-036] portion of his first ardour. If we would put some slight stress on

[011-037] ourselves at the beginning, then afterwards we should be able to do all

[011-038] things with ease and joy.

[011-039] 6. It is a hard thing to break through a habit, and a yet harder thing

[011-040] to go contrary to our own will. Yet if thou overcome not slight and

[011-041] easy obstacles, how shalt thou overcome greater ones? Withstand thy

[011-042] will at the beginning, and unlearn an evil habit, lest it lead thee

[011-043] little by little into worse difficulties. Oh, if thou knewest what

[011-044] peace to thyself thy holy life should bring to thyself, and what joy to

[011-045] others, methinketh thou wouldst be more zealous for spiritual profit.
