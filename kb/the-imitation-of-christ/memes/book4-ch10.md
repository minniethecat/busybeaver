[106-001] That [[communion|Holy Communion]] is not lightly to be omitted

[106-002] The Voice of [[jesus-christ|the Beloved]]

[106-003] Thou must frequently betake thee to the Fountain of grace and divine

[106-004] [[forgiveness|mercy]], to the Fountain of goodness and all purity; to the end that thou

[106-005] mayest obtain the healing of thy [[sin|passions]] and vices, and mayest be made

[106-006] stronger and more watchful against all temptations and wiles of the

[106-007] devil. The enemy, knowing what profit and exceeding strong remedy lieth

[106-008] in the [[eucharist|Holy Communion]], striveth by all means and occasions to draw back

[106-009] and hinder the faithful and devout, so far as he can.

[106-010] 2. For when some set about to prepare themselves for Holy Communion,

[106-011] they suffer from the more evil suggestions of Satan. The very evil

[106-012] spirit himself (as is written in [[job|Job]]), cometh among the sons of God

[106-013] that he may trouble them by his accustomed evil dealing, or make them

[106-014] over timid and perplexed; to the intent that he may diminish their

[106-015] affections, or take away their faith by his attacks, if haply he may

[106-016] prevail upon them to give up Holy Communion altogether, or to come

[106-017] thereto with lukewarm hearts. But his wiles and delusions must not be

[106-018] heeded, howsoever wicked and terrible they be; but all his delusion

[106-019] must be cast back upon his own head. The wretch must be despised and

[106-020] laughed to scorn: neither must Holy Communion be omitted because of his

[106-021] insults and the inward troubles which he stirreth up.

[106-022] 3. Often also too much carefulness or some anxiety or other touching

[106-023] confession hindereth from obtaining devotion. Do thou according to the

[106-024] counsel of wise men, and lay aside anxiety and scruple, because it

[106-025] hindereth the [[grace|grace of God]] and destroyeth devotion of mind. Because of

[106-026] some little vexation or trouble do not thou neglect Holy Communion, but

[106-027] rather hasten to confess it, and forgive freely all offences committed

[106-028] against thee. And if thou hast offended any man, humbly beg for pardon,

[106-029] and God shall freely forgive thee.

[106-030] 4. What profiteth it to put off for long time the confession of thy

[106-031] sins, or to defer Holy Communion? Cleanse thyself forthwith, spit out

[106-032] the poison with all speed, hasten to take the remedy, and thou shalt

[106-033] feel thyself better than if thou didst long defer it. If to-day thou

[106-034] defer it on one account, to-morrow perchance some greater obstacle will

[106-035] come, and so thou mayest be long time hindered from Communion and

[106-036] become more unfit. As soon as thou canst, shake thyself from thy

[106-037] present heaviness and sloth, for it profiteth nothing to be long

[106-038] anxious, to go long on thy way with heaviness of heart, and because of

[106-039] daily little obstacles to sever thyself from divine things: nay it is

[106-040] exceeding hurtful to defer thy Communion long, for this commonly

[106-041] bringeth on great torpor. Alas! there are some, lukewarm and

[106-042] undisciplined, who willingly find excuses for delaying repentance, and

[106-043] desire to defer Holy Communion, lest they should be bound to keep

[106-044] stricter watch upon themselves.

[106-045] 5. Alas! how little [[love|charity]], what flagging devotion, have they who so

[106-046] lightly put off Holy Communion. How happy is he, how acceptable to God,

[106-047] who so liveth, and in such [[purity-of-heart|purity of conscience]] keepeth himself, that

[106-048] any day he could be ready and well inclined to communicate, if it were

[106-049] in his power, and might be done without the notice of others. If a man

[106-050] sometimes abstaineth for the sake of humility or some sound cause, he

[106-051] is to be commended for his reverence. But if drowsiness have taken hold

[106-052] of him, he ought to rouse himself and to do what in him lieth; and the

[106-053] Lord will help his desire for the good will which he hath, which God

[106-054] specially approveth.

[106-055] 6. But when he is hindered by sufficient cause, yet will he ever have a

[106-056] good will and pious intention to communicate; and so he shall not be

[106-057] lacking in the fruit of the Sacrament. For any devout man is able every

[106-058] day and every hour to draw near to spiritual communion with Christ to

[106-059] his soul’s health and without hindrance. Nevertheless on certain days

[106-060] and at the appointed time he ought to receive the Body and Blood of his

[106-061] Redeemer with affectionate reverence, and rather to seek after the

[106-062] praise and honour of God, than his own comfort. For so often doth he

[106-063] communicate mystically, and is invisibly refreshed, as he devoutly

[106-064] calleth to mind the mystery of Christ’s incarnation and His Passion,

[106-065] and is inflamed with the love of Him.

[106-066] 7. He who only prepareth himself when a festival is at hand or custom

[106-067] compelleth, will too often be unprepared. Blessed is he who offereth

[106-068] himself to God for a whole burnt-offering, so often as he celebrateth

[106-069] or communicateth! Be not too slow nor too hurried in thy celebrating,

[106-070] but preserve the good received custom of those with whom thou livest.

[106-071] Thou oughtest not to produce weariness and annoyance in others, but to

[106-072] observe the received custom, according to the institution of the

[106-073] elders; and to minister to the profit of others rather than to thine

[106-074] own devotion or feeling.
