[069-001] Of self-denial and the casting away all selfishness

[069-002] “My Son, thou canst not possess perfect liberty unless thou altogether

[069-003] deny thyself. All they are enslaved who are possessors of riches, they

[069-004] who love themselves, the selfish, the curious, the restless; those who

[069-005] ever seek after soft things, and not after the things of [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]];

[069-006] those who continually plan and devise that which will not stand. For

[069-007] whatsoever cometh not of God shall perish. Hold fast the short and

[069-008] complete saying, ‘Renounce all things, and thou shalt find all things;

[069-009] give up thy lust, and thou shalt find rest.’ Dwell upon this in thy

[069-010] mind, and when thou art full of it, thou shalt understand all things.”

[069-011] 2. O Lord, this is not the work of a day, nor children’s play; verily

[069-012] in this short saying is enclosed all the perfection of the religious.

[069-013] 3. “My son, thou oughtest not to be turned aside, nor immediately cast

[069-014] down, because thou hast heard the way of the perfect. Rather oughtest

[069-015] thou to be provoked to higher aims, and at the least to long after the

[069-016] desire thereof. Oh that it were so with thee, and that thou hadst come

[069-017] to this, that thou wert not a lover of thine own self, but wert ready

[069-018] always to My nod, and to his whom I have placed over thee as thy

[069-019] father. Then shouldest thou please Me exceedingly, and all thy life

[069-020] should go on in joy and peace. Thou hast still many things to renounce,

[069-021] which if thou resign not utterly to Me, thou shalt not gain what thou

[069-022] seekest. I counsel thee to buy of Me gold tried in the fire, that thou

[069-023] mayest be rich,(1) that is [[wisdom|heavenly wisdom]], which despiseth all base

[069-024] things. Put away from thee earthly wisdom, and all pleasure, whether

[069-025] common to men, or thine own.

[069-026] 4. “I tell thee that thou must buy vile things with those which are

[069-027] costly and great in the esteem of men. For wonderfully vile and small,

[069-028] and almost given up to forgetfulness, doth true heavenly wisdom appear,

[069-029] which thinketh not high things of itself, nor seeketh to be magnified

[069-030] upon the earth; many honour it with their lips, but in heart are far

[069-031] from it; it is indeed the precious pearl, which is hidden from many.”

[069-032] (1) Revelation iii. 18.
