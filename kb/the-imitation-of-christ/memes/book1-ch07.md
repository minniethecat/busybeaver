[007-001] Of fleeing from vain hope and pride

[007-002] Vain is the life of that man who putteth his [[trust-in-god|trust]] in men or in any

[007-003] created Thing. Be not ashamed to be the servant of others for the love

[007-004] of [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]], and to be reckoned poor in this life. Rest not upon

[007-005] thyself, but build thy hope in God. Do what lieth in thy power, and God

[007-006] will help thy good intent. [[trust|Trust]] not in thy learning, nor in the

[007-007] cleverness of any that lives, but rather trust in the favour of God,

[007-008] who resisteth the proud and giveth grace to the humble.

[007-009] 2. Boast not thyself in thy riches if thou hast them, nor in thy

[007-010] friends if they be powerful, but in God, who giveth all things, and in

[007-011] addition to all things desireth to give even Himself. Be not lifted up

[007-012] because of thy strength or beauty of body, for with only a slight

[007-013] sickness it will fail and wither away. Be not vain of thy skilfulness

[007-014] or ability, lest thou displease God, from whom cometh every good gift

[007-015] which we have.

[007-016] 3. Count not thyself better than others, lest perchance thou appear

[007-017] worse in the sight of God, who knoweth what is in man. Be not proud of

[007-018] thy good works, for God’s judgments are of another sort than the

[007-019] judgments of man, and what pleaseth man is ofttimes displeasing to Him.

[007-020] If thou hast any good, believe that others have more, and so thou

[007-021] mayest preserve thy humility. It is no harm to thee if thou place

[007-022] thyself below all others; but it is great harm if thou place thyself

[007-023] above even one. [[peace|Peace]] is ever with the humble man, but in the heart of

[007-024] the proud there is envy and continual wrath.
