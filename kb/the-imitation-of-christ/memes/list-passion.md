# The Passion of Christ

The Passion of Christ is the moral and mystical centre of *The Imitation of
Christ*. Kempis returns continually to the Cross as the school of all virtue,
the proof of divine love, and the pattern for patient endurance of suffering.
This page gathers all wiki entries tagged with the Passion theme.

## Scope

The Passion-tagged pages in this wiki treat the events of Christ's suffering
and death, their theological significance (redemption, atonement, the victory
of love over sin), and their practical implications for the Christian life —
above all the call to take up one's own cross in imitation of the Master.
