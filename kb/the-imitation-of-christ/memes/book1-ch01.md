[001-001] Of the imitation of [[jesus-christ|Christ]], and of contempt of [[world|the world]] and all its

[001-002] [[vanity|vanities]]

[001-003] _He that followeth me shall not walk in darkness_,(1) saith the Lord.

[001-004] These are the words of Christ; and they teach us how far we must

[001-005] imitate His life and character, if we seek true illumination, and

[001-006] deliverance from all blindness of heart. Let it be our most earnest

[001-007] study, therefore, to dwell upon the life of Jesus Christ.

[001-008] 2. His teaching surpasseth all teaching of holy men, and such as have

[001-009] His Spirit find therein the hidden manna.(2) But there are many who,

[001-010] though they frequently hear the Gospel, yet feel but little longing

[001-011] after it, because they have not the mind of Christ. He, therefore, that

[001-012] will fully and with [[wisdom|true wisdom]] understand the words of Christ, let him

[001-013] strive to conform his whole life to that mind of Christ.

[001-014] 3. What doth it profit thee to enter into deep discussion concerning

[001-015] the Holy Trinity, if thou lack humility, and be thus displeasing to the

[001-016] Trinity? For verily it is not deep words that make a man holy and

[001-017] upright; it is a good life which maketh a man dear to God. I had rather

[001-018] feel contrition than be skilful in the definition thereof. If thou

[001-019] knewest the whole Bible, and the sayings of all the philosophers, what

[001-020] should all this profit thee without the love and [[grace|grace of God]]? Vanity

[001-021] of vanities, all is vanity, save to love God, and Him only to serve.

[001-022] That is the highest wisdom, to cast the world behind us, and to reach

[001-023] forward to the heavenly kingdom.

[001-024] 4. It is vanity then to seek after, and to [[trust-in-god|trust]] in, the riches that

[001-025] shall perish. It is vanity, too, to covet honours, and to lift up

[001-026] ourselves on high. It is vanity to follow the desires of the flesh and

[001-027] be led by them, for this shall bring misery at the last. It is vanity

[001-028] to desire a long life, and to have little care for a good life. It is

[001-029] vanity to take thought only for the life which now is, and not to look

[001-030] forward to the things which shall be hereafter. It is vanity to love

[001-031] that which quickly passeth away, and not to hasten where [[eternity|eternal joy]]

[001-032] abideth.

[001-033] 5. Be ofttimes mindful of the saying,(3) The eye is not satisfied with

[001-034] seeing, nor the ear with hearing. Strive, therefore, to turn away thy

[001-035] heart from the love of the things that are seen, and to set it upon the

[001-036] things that are not seen. For they who follow after their own fleshly

[001-037] lusts, defile the conscience, and destroy the grace of God.

[001-038] (1) John viii. 12. (2) Revelations ii. 17. (3) Ecclesiastes i. 8.
