[075-001] Of a good government in external things, and of having recourse to God

[075-002] in dangers

[075-003] “My Son, for this thou must diligently make thy endeavour, that in

[075-004] every place and outward action or occupation thou mayest be free

[075-005] within, and have power over thyself; and that all things be under thee,

[075-006] not thou under them; that thou be master and ruler of thy actions, not

[075-007] a slave or hireling, but rather a free and true Hebrew, entering into

[075-008] the lot and the liberty of the children of God, who stand above the

[075-009] present and look upon the eternal, who with the left eye behold things

[075-010] transitory, and with the right things heavenly; whom temporal things

[075-011] draw not to cleave unto, but who rather draw temporal things to do them

[075-012] good service, even as they were ordained of God to do, and appointed by

[075-013] the Master Workman, who hath left nought in His creation without aim

[075-014] and end.

[075-015] 2. “And if in any chance of life thou stand not in outward appearances,

[075-016] nor judgest things which are seen and heard by the fleshly sense, but

[075-017] straightway in every cause enterest with [[moses|Moses]] into the tabernacle to

[075-018] ask counsel of God; thou shalt hear a divine response and come forth

[075-019] instructed concerning many things that are and shall be. For always

[075-020] Moses had recourse to the tabernacle for the solving of all doubts and

[075-021] questionings; and fled to the help of prayer to be delivered from the

[075-022] dangers and evil deeds of men. Thus also oughtest thou to fly to the

[075-023] secret chamber of thy heart, and earnestly implore the divine succour.

[075-024] For this cause we read that [[joshua|Joshua]] and the [[israel|children of Israel]] were

[075-025] deceived by the Gibeonites, that they asked not counsel at the mouth of

[075-026] the [[jesus-christ|Lord]],(1) but being too ready to listen to fair speeches, were

[075-027] deceived by pretended piety.”

[075-028] (1) Joshua ix. 14.
