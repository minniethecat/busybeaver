[077-001] That man hath no good in himself, and nothing whereof to glory

[077-002] [[jesus-christ|Lord]], what is man that Thou art mindful of him, or the son of man that

[077-003] Thou visitest him?(1) What hath man deserved, that Thou shouldest

[077-004] bestow thy favour upon him? Lord, what cause can I have of complaint,

[077-005] if Thou forsake me? Or what can I justly allege, if Thou refuse to hear

[077-006] my petition? Of a truth, this I may truly think and say, Lord, I am

[077-007] nothing, I have nothing that is good of myself, but I fall short in all

[077-008] things, and ever tend unto nothing. And unless I am helped by Thee and

[077-009] inwardly supported, I become altogether lukewarm and reckless.

[077-010] 2. But Thou, O Lord, art always the same, and endurest for ever, always

[077-011] good, righteous, and holy; doing all things well, righteously, and

[077-012] holily, and disposing all in Thy wisdom. But I who am more ready to go

[077-013] forward than backward, never continue in one stay, because changes

[077-014] sevenfold pass over me. Yet it quickly becometh better when it so

[077-015] pleaseth Thee, and Thou puttest forth Thy hand to help me; because Thou

[077-016] alone canst aid without help of man, and canst so strengthen me that my

[077-017] countenance shall be no more changed, but my heart shall be turned to

[077-018] Thee, and rest in Thee alone.

[077-019] 3. Wherefore, if I but knew well how to reject all human consolations,

[077-020] whether for the sake of gaining devotion, or because of the necessity

[077-021] by which I was compelled to seek Thee, seeing there is no man who can

[077-022] comfort me; then could I worthily [[trust-in-god|trust]] in Thy grace, and rejoice in

[077-023] the gift of new [[comfort|consolation]].

[077-024] 4. Thanks be to Thee, from whom all cometh, whensoever it goeth well

[077-025] with me! But I am vanity and nothing in Thy sight, a man inconstant and

[077-026] weak. What then have I whereof to glory, or why do I long to be held in

[077-027] honour? Is it not for nought? This also is utterly vain. Verily vain

[077-028] glory is an evil plague, the greatest of [[vanity|vanities]], because it draweth

[077-029] us away from the [[glory|true glory]], and robbeth us of heavenly grace. For

[077-030] whilst a man pleaseth himself he displeaseth Thee; whilst he gapeth

[077-031] after the praises of man, he is deprived of true virtues.

[077-032] 5. But true glory and holy rejoicing lieth in glorying in Thee and not

[077-033] in self; in rejoicing in Thy Name, not in our own virtue; in not taking

[077-034] delight in any creature, save only for Thy sake. Let thy Name, not mine

[077-035] be praised; let Thy work, not mine be magnified; let Thy holy Name be

[077-036] blessed, but to me let nought be given of the praises of men. Thou art

[077-037] my glory, Thou art the joy of my heart. In Thee will I make my boast

[077-038] and be glad all the day long, but for myself let me not glory save only

[077-039] in my infirmities.(2)

[077-040] 6. Let the Jews seek the honour which cometh from one another; but I

[077-041] will ask for that which cometh from God only.(3) Truly all human glory,

[077-042] all temporal honour, all worldly exultation, compared to Thy eternal

[077-043] glory, is but vanity and folly. O God my [[truth|Truth]] and my [[mercy|Mercy]], Blessed

[077-044] Trinity, to Thee alone be all praise, honour, power, and glory for ever

[077-045] and for ever. Amen.

[077-046] (1) Psalm viii. 4. (2) 2 Corinthians xii. 5. (3) John v. 44.
