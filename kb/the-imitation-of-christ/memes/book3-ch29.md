[066-001] How when [[suffering|tribulation]] cometh we must call upon and bless God

[066-002] Blessed be thy name, O [[jesus-christ|Lord]], for evermore, who hast willed this

[066-003] temptation and trouble to come upon me. I cannot escape it, but have

[066-004] need to flee unto Thee, that Thou mayest succour me and turn it unto me

[066-005] for good. Lord, now am I in tribulation, and it is not well within my

[066-006] heart, but I am sore vexed by the suffering which lieth upon me. And

[066-007] now, O dear Father, what shall I say? I am taken among the snares. Save

[066-008] me from this hour, but for this cause came I unto this hour,(1) that

[066-009] Thou mightest be glorified when I am deeply humbled and am delivered

[066-010] through Thee. Let it be Thy pleasure to deliver me;(2) for what can I

[066-011] do who am poor, and without Thee whither shall I go? Give patience this

[066-012] time also. Help me, O Lord my God, and I will not fear how much soever

[066-013] I be weighed down.

[066-014] 2. And now amid these things what shall I say? Lord, Thy will be done.

[066-015] I have well deserved to be troubled and weighed down. Therefore I ought

[066-016] to bear, would that it be with patience, until the tempest be overpast

[066-017] and comfort return. Yet is Thine omnipotent arm able also to take this

[066-018] temptation away from me, and to lessen its power that I fall not

[066-019] utterly under it, even as many a time past thou has helped me, O God,

[066-020] my merciful God. And as much as this deliverance is difficult to me, so

[066-021] much is it easy to Thee, O right hand of the most Highest.

[066-022] (1) John xii. 27. (2) Psalm xl. 16.
