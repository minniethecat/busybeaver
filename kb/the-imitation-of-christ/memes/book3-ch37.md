[074-001] Of pure and [[resignation|entire resignation]] of self, for the obtaining liberty of

[074-002] heart

[074-003] “My Son, lose thyself and thou shalt find Me. Stand still without all

[074-004] choosing and all thought of self, and thou shalt ever be a gainer. For

[074-005] more grace shall be added to thee, as soon as thou resignest thyself,

[074-006] and so long as thou dost not turn back to take thyself again.”

[074-007] 2. O [[jesus-christ|Lord]], how often shall I resign myself, and in what things shall I

[074-008] lose myself?

[074-009] 3. “Always; every hour: in that which is little, and in that which is

[074-010] great. I make no exception, but will that thou be found naked in all

[074-011] things. Otherwise how canst thou be Mine and I thine, unless thou be

[074-012] inwardly and outwardly free from every will of thine own? The sooner

[074-013] thou dost this, the better shall it be with thee; and the more fully

[074-014] and sincerely, the more thou shalt please Me, and the more abundantly

[074-015] shalt thou be rewarded.

[074-016] 4. “Some resign themselves, but with certain reservations, for they do

[074-017] not fully [[faith|trust in God]], therefore they think that they have some

[074-018] provision to make for themselves. Some again at first offer everything;

[074-019] but afterwards being pressed by temptation they return to their own

[074-020] devices, and thus make no progress in virtue. They will not attain to

[074-021] the true liberty of a pure heart, nor to the grace of My sweet

[074-022] companionship, unless they first entirely resign themselves and daily

[074-023] offer themselves up as a sacrifice; without this the union which

[074-024] bringeth forth fruit standeth not nor will stand.

[074-025] 5. “Many a time I have said unto thee, and now say again, Give thyself

[074-026] up, resign thyself, and thou shalt have great [[peace|inward peace]]. Give all

[074-027] for all; demand nothing, ask nothing in return; stand simply and with

[074-028] no hesitation in Me, and thou shalt possess Me. Thou shalt have liberty

[074-029] of heart, and the darkness shall not overwhelm thee. For this strive

[074-030] thou, pray for it, long after it, that thou mayest be delivered from

[074-031] all possession of thyself, and nakedly follow Jesus who was made naked

[074-032] for thee; mayest die unto thyself and live eternally to Me. Then shall

[074-033] all vain fancies disappear, all evil disturbings, and superfluous

[074-034] cares. Then also shall immoderate fear depart from thee, and inordinate

[074-035] love shall die.”
