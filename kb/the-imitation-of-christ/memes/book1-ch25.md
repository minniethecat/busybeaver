[025-001] Of the [[amendment|zealous amendment]] of our whole life

[025-002] Be thou watchful and diligent in God’s service, and bethink thee often

[025-003] why thou hast renounced [[world|the world]]. Was it not that thou mightest live

[025-004] to God and become a spiritual man? Be zealous, therefore, for thy

[025-005] spiritual profit, for thou shalt receive shortly the reward of thy

[025-006] labours, and neither fear nor sorrow shall come any more into thy

[025-007] borders. Now shalt thou labour a little, and thou shalt find great

[025-008] rest, yea everlasting joy. If thou shalt remain faithful and zealous in

[025-009] labour, doubt not that God shall be faithful and bountiful in rewarding

[025-010] thee. It is thy duty to have a [[hope|good hope]] that thou wilt attain the

[025-011] victory, but thou must not fall into security lest thou become slothful

[025-012] or lifted up.

[025-013] 2. A certain man being in anxiety of mind, continually tossed about

[025-014] between hope and fear, and being on a certain day overwhelmed with

[025-015] grief, cast himself down in prayer before the altar in a church, and

[025-016] meditated within himself, saying, “Oh! if I but knew that I should

[025-017] still persevere,” and presently heard within him a voice from God, “And

[025-018] if thou didst know it, what wouldst thou do? Do now what thou wouldst

[025-019] do then, and thou shalt be very secure.” And straightway being

[025-020] comforted and strengthened, he committed himself to the will of God and

[025-021] the perturbation of spirit ceased, neither had he a mind any more to

[025-022] search curiously to know what should befall him hereafter, but studied

[025-023] rather to inquire what was the good and acceptable will of God, for the

[025-024] beginning and perfecting of every good work.

[025-025] 3. Hope in the [[jesus-christ|Lord]] and be doing good, saith the Prophet; dwell in the

[025-026] land and thou shalt be fed(1) with its riches. One thing there is which

[025-027] holdeth back many from progress and fervent amendment, even the dread

[025-028] of difficulty, or the labour of the conflict. Nevertheless they advance

[025-029] above all others in virtue who strive manfully to conquer those things

[025-030] which are most grievous and contrary to them, for there a man profiteth

[025-031] most and meriteth greater grace where he most overcometh himself and

[025-032] mortifieth himself in spirit.

[025-033] 4. But all men have not the same [[sin|passions]] to conquer and to mortify,

[025-034] yet he who is diligent shall attain more profit, although he have

[025-035] stronger passions, than another who is more temperate of disposition,

[025-036] but is withal less fervent in the pursuit of virtue. Two things

[025-037] specially avail unto improvement in holiness, namely firmness to

[025-038] withdraw ourselves from the sin to which by nature we are most

[025-039] inclined, and earnest zeal for that good in which we are most lacking.

[025-040] And strive also very earnestly to guard against and subdue those faults

[025-041] which displease thee most frequently in others.

[025-042] 5. Gather some profit to thy soul wherever thou art, and wherever thou

[025-043] seest or hearest good examples, stir thyself to follow them, but where

[025-044] thou seest anything which is blameworthy, take heed that thou do not

[025-045] the same; or if at any time thou hast done it, strive quickly to amend

[025-046] thyself. As thine eye observeth others, so again are the eyes of others

[025-047] upon thee. How sweet and pleasant is it to see zealous and godly

[025-048] brethren temperate and of good discipline; and how sad is it and

[025-049] grievous to see them walking disorderly, not practising the duties to

[025-050] which they are called. How hurtful a thing it is to neglect the purpose

[025-051] of their calling, and turn their inclinations to things which are none

[025-052] of their business.

[025-053] 6. Be mindful of the duties which thou hast undertaken, and set always

[025-054] before thee the remembrance of the Crucified. Truly oughtest thou to be

[025-055] ashamed as thou lookest upon the life of Jesus Christ, because thou

[025-056] hast not yet endeavoured to conform thyself more unto Him, though thou

[025-057] hast been a long time in the way of God. A religious man who exercises

[025-058] himself seriously and devoutly in the most holy life and passion of our

[025-059] Lord shall find there abundantly all things that are profitable and

[025-060] necessary for him, neither is there need that he shall seek anything

[025-061] better beyond Jesus. Oh! if Jesus crucified would come into our hearts,

[025-062] how quickly, and completely should we have learned all that we need to

[025-063] know!

[025-064] 7. He who is earnest receiveth and beareth well all things that are

[025-065] laid upon him. He who is careless and lukewarm hath trouble upon

[025-066] trouble, and suffereth anguish upon every side, because he is without

[025-067] inward [[comfort|consolation]], and is forbidden to seek that which is outward. He

[025-068] who is living without discipline is exposed to grievous ruin. He who

[025-069] seeketh easier and lighter discipline shall always be in distress,

[025-070] because one thing or another will give him displeasure.

[025-071] 8. O! if no other duty lay upon us but to praise the Lord our God with

[025-072] our whole heart and voice! Oh! if thou never hadst need to eat or

[025-073] drink, or sleep, but wert always able to praise God, and to give

[025-074] thyself to spiritual exercises alone; then shouldst thou be far happier

[025-075] than now, when for so many necessities thou must serve the flesh. O!

[025-076] that these necessities were not, but only the spiritual refreshments of

[025-077] the soul, which alas we taste too seldom.

[025-078] 9. When a man hath come to this, that he seeketh comfort from no

[025-079] created thing, then doth he perfectly begin to enjoy God, then also

[025-080] will he be well contented with whatsoever shall happen unto him. Then

[025-081] will he neither rejoice for much nor be sorrowful for little, but he

[025-082] committeth himself altogether and with full [[trust-in-god|trust]] unto God, who is all

[025-083] in all to him, to whom nothing perisheth nor dieth, but all things live

[025-084] to Him and obey His every word without delay.

[025-085] 10. Remember always thine end, and how the time which is lost returneth

[025-086] not. Without [[diligence|care and diligence]] thou shalt never get virtue. If thou

[025-087] beginnest to grow cold, it shall begin to go ill with thee, but if thou

[025-088] givest thyself unto zeal thou shalt find much peace, and shalt find thy

[025-089] labour the lighter because of the [[grace|grace of God]] and the love of virtue.

[025-090] A zealous and diligent man is ready for all things. It is greater

[025-091] labour to resist sins and passions than to toil in bodily labours. He

[025-092] who shunneth not small faults falleth little by little into greater. At

[025-093] eventide thou shalt always be glad if thou spend the day profitably.

[025-094] Watch over thyself, stir thyself up, admonish thyself, and howsoever it

[025-095] be with others, neglect not thyself. The more violence thou dost unto

[025-096] thyself, the more thou shall profit. Amen.

[025-097] (1) Psalm xxxvii. 3.
