[065-001] Against the tongues of detractors

[065-002] “My Son, take it not sadly to heart, if any think ill of thee, and say

[065-003] of thee what thou art unwilling to hear. Thou oughtest to think worse

[065-004] of thyself, and to believe no man weaker than thyself. If thou walkest

[065-005] inwardly, thou wilt not weigh flying words above their value. It is no

[065-006] small prudence to keep silence in an evil time and to turn inwardly

[065-007] unto Me, and not to be troubled by human judgment.

[065-008] 2. “Let not thy peace depend upon the word of men; for whether they

[065-009] judge well or ill of thee, thou art not therefore any other man than

[065-010] thyself. Where is true peace or [[glory|true glory]]? Is it not in Me? And he who

[065-011] seeketh not to please men, nor feareth to displease, shall enjoy

[065-012] abundant peace. From inordinate love and vain fear ariseth all

[065-013] disquietude of heart, and all distraction of the senses.”
