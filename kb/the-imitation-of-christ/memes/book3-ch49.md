[086-001] Of the desire after [[eternity|eternal life]], and how great blessings are promised

[086-002] to those who strive

[086-003] “My Son, when thou feelest the desire of eternal [[blessedness|happiness]] to be poured

[086-004] into thee from above, and longest to depart from the tabernacle of this

[086-005] body, that thou mayest contemplate My glory without shadow of turning,

[086-006] enlarge thine heart, and take in this holy inspiration with all thy

[086-007] desire. Give most hearty thanks to the Supreme [[goodness|Goodness]], who dealeth

[086-008] with thee so graciously, visiteth thee so lovingly, stirreth thee up so

[086-009] fervently, raiseth thee so powerfully, lest thou sink down through

[086-010] thine own weight, to earthly things. For not by thine own meditating or

[086-011] striving dost thou receive this gift, but by the sole gracious

[086-012] condescension of Supreme [[grace|Grace]] and Divine regard; to the end that thou

[086-013] mayest make progress in virtue and in more humility, and prepare

[086-014] thyself for future conflicts, and cleave unto Me with all the affection

[086-015] of thy heart, and strive to serve Me with fervent will.

[086-016] 2. “My Son, often the fire burneth, but the flame ascendeth not without

[086-017] smoke. So also the desires of some men burn towards heavenly things,

[086-018] and yet they are not free from the temptation of carnal affection. Thus

[086-019] therefore they are not acting with an altogether simple desire for

[086-020] God’s glory when they pray to Him so earnestly. Such, too, is

[086-021] oftentimes thy desire, when thou hast imagined it to be so earnest. For

[086-022] that is not pure and perfect which is tainted with thine own

[086-023] self-seeking.

[086-024] 3. “Seek thou not what is pleasant and advantageous to thyself, but

[086-025] what is acceptable and honourable unto Me; for if thou judgest rightly,

[086-026] thou must choose and follow after My appointment rather than thine own

[086-027] desire; yea, rather than anything that can be desired. I know thy

[086-028] desire, and I have heard thy many groanings. Already thou longest to be

[086-029] in the glorious liberty of the children of God; already the eternal

[086-030] home delighteth thee, and the heavenly country full of joy; but the

[086-031] hour is not yet come; there remaineth still another season, even a

[086-032] season of warfare, a season of labour and probation. Thou desirest to

[086-033] be filled with the Chief Good, but thou canst not attain it

[086-034] immediately. I AM that Good; wait for Me, until the [[kingdom-of-god|Kingdom of God]]

[086-035] shall come.

[086-036] 4. “Thou must still be tried upon earth, and be exercised in many

[086-037] things. [[consolation|Consolation]] shall from time to time be given thee, but abundant

[086-038] satisfying shall not be granted. Be strong therefore, and be thou brave

[086-039] both in working and in suffering things which are against thy nature.

[086-040] Thou must put on the new man, and be changed into another man. Thou

[086-041] must often do what thou wouldst not; and thou must leave undone what

[086-042] thou wouldst do. What pleaseth others shall have good success, what

[086-043] pleaseth thee shall have no prosperity. What others say shall be

[086-044] listened to; what thou sayest shall receive no heed. Others shall ask

[086-045] and receive; thou shalt ask and not obtain. Others shall be great in

[086-046] the report of men, but about thee shall nothing be spoken. To others

[086-047] this or that shall be entrusted; thou shalt be judged useful for

[086-048] nought.

[086-049] 5. “For this cause nature shall sometimes be filled with sadness; and

[086-050] it is a great thing if thou bear it silently. In this and many like

[086-051] things the faithful servant of the [[jesus-christ|Lord]] is wont to be tried, how far he

[086-052] is able to deny himself and bring himself into [[obedience|subjection]] in all

[086-053] things. Scarcely is there anything in which thou hast need to mortify

[086-054] thyself so much as in seeing things which are adverse to thy will;

[086-055] especially when things are commanded thee to be done which seem to thee

[086-056] inexpedient or of little use to thee. And because thou darest not

[086-057] resist a higher power, being under authority, therefore it seemeth hard

[086-058] for thee to shape thy course according to the nod of another, and to

[086-059] forego thine own opinion.

[086-060] 6. “But consider, My Son, the fruit of these labours, the swift end,

[086-061] and the reward exceeding great; and thou shalt find it no pain to bear

[086-062] them then, but rather the strongest solace of thy patience. For even in

[086-063] exchange for this trifling desire which thou hast readily forsaken,

[086-064] thou shalt always have thy will in [[heaven|Heaven]]. There verily thou shalt find

[086-065] all that thou wouldst, all that thou canst long for. There thou shalt

[086-066] have all good within thy power without the fear of losing it. There thy

[086-067] will, ever at one with Mine, shall desire nothing outward, nothing for

[086-068] itself. There no man shall withstand thee, none shall complain of thee,

[086-069] none shall hinder, nothing shall stand in thy path; but all things

[086-070] desired by thee shall be present together, and shall refresh thy whole

[086-071] affection, and fill it up even to the brim. There I will glory for the

[086-072] scorn suffered here, the garment of praise for sorrow, and for the

[086-073] lowest place a throne in the Kingdom, for ever. There shall appear the

[086-074] fruit of obedience, the labour of repentance shall rejoice, and humble

[086-075] subjection shall be crowned gloriously.

[086-076] 7. “Now therefore bow thyself humbly under the hands of all men; nor

[086-077] let it trouble thee who said this or who ordered that; but take special

[086-078] heed that whether thy superior, thy inferior, or thy equal, require

[086-079] anything from thee, or even show a desire for it; take it all in good

[086-080] part, and study with a good will to fulfil the desire. Let one seek

[086-081] this, another that; let this man glory in this, and that man in that,

[086-082] and be praised a thousand thousand times, but rejoice thou only in the

[086-083] contempt of thyself, and in Mine own good pleasure and glory. This is

[086-084] what thou art to long for, even that whether by life or by death God

[086-085] may be ever magnified in thee.”(1)

[086-086] (1) Philippians i. 20.
