## Overview

Herbert George Wells (1866–1946) was an English writer best known for his science fiction novels — *The War of the Worlds*, *The Time Machine*, *The Invisible Man* — as well as his social commentary and popular histories. His most significant connection to Barbellion is that he wrote the Introduction to *The Journal of a Disappointed Man*, published posthumously in 1919. Wells's endorsement gave the Journal an imprimatur of literary seriousness that shaped its initial reception.

## Relationship to Barbellion

The precise nature of Wells's connection to Barbellion is unclear — whether they met personally or Wells was approached by Barbellion's publisher after his death. What is certain is that Wells's Introduction frames the Journal as a document of universal human significance. Barbellion himself mentions Wells only indirectly, describing himself as "just a ragbag of Smollett, H. G. Wells, [[samuel-butler|Samuel Butler]]" (004-496) — a self-deprecating assessment that places Wells among the diverse literary influences that shaped his sensibility.

Wells, like Barbellion, came from a lower-middle-class background and rose through education and [[literature|writing]]. This biographical parallel may explain Wells's sympathy for Barbellion's project — he recognised in the young diarist a kindred spirit of self-improvement and intellectual ambition.

## References in the Journal

The Introduction itself is the most significant Wells reference. Barbellion's single mention:

> "I am just a ragbag of Smollett, H. G. Wells, Samuel Butler, the Daily News, and The Devil." (004-496)

## Biographical Note

Born to a shopkeeper family in Bromley, Kent, Wells won a scholarship to the Normal School of [[science|Science]] (now Imperial College [[london|London]]), where he studied under [[thomas-huxley|T. H. Huxley]]. His early scientific romances established him as a major literary figure, and he later turned to social novels, popular education, and political advocacy. Wells's *The Outline of History* (1920) became one of the best-selling [[literature|books]] of the twentieth century. His Introduction to Barbellion's Journal compared the work to the confessional [[literature|writing]]s of [[marie-bashkirtseff|Marie Bashkirtseff]] and praised its "astonishing sincerity."
