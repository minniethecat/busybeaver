## About

*The American Naturalist* is a monthly peer-reviewed scientific journal published by the American Society of Naturalists since 1867. It covers research in ecology, evolution, behaviour, and systematics, and was already a respected venue in Barbellion's time.

## Barbellion's Engagement

Barbellion's contribution to the *American Naturalist* represents the high-water mark of his [[ambition|scientific ambition]]. In a passage reflecting on his frustrated career at the [[natural-history-museum-london|Natural History Museum]], he reveals that he and his colleague R—— co-authored an anonymous paper for the journal:

> So I had to continue my struggle against odds, and only within the last year or so began to squeeze the authorities with any success. In time I should have revolutionised the study of Systematic Zoology, and the anonymous paper I wrote in conjunction with R—— in the American Naturalist was a rare jewel.

(004-514)

The passage is dense with significance. The paper was **anonymous** — Barbellion could not even claim public credit for his work. Yet he describes it as "a rare jewel," suggesting genuine intellectual pride. The larger claim — "In time I should have revolutionised the study of Systematic Zoology" — is one of the most ambitious statements in the entire journal, revealing the scale of the scientific career he imagined for himself and the magnitude of what his illness cut short.

The *American Naturalist* episode illuminates the gulf between Barbellion's abilities (recognised by his co-author and senior colleague R——) and his institutional position (a low-ranking assistant denied formal [[fame|recognition]]). It belongs to the tragic trajectory of his scientific life: genuine [[genius|talent]] and dedication thwarted by poverty, lack of credentials, and ultimately by multiple sclerosis.
