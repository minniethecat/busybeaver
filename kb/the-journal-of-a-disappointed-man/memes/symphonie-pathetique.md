The **Symphonie Pathétique** (Symphony No. 6 in B minor, Op. 74), [[Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky]]'s final completed symphony, was among the works that most intensely moved Barbellion during his musical awakening in [[london|London]]. He heard it performed at the [[royal-albert-hall|Royal Albert Hall]] and later reflected on its overwhelming emotional power—part of the musical education that displaced his earlier devotion to [[zoology]].

## At the Albert Hall

Barbellion's first documented encounter with the *Pathétique* came at a memorial concert at the [[royal-albert-hall|Royal Albert Hall]], where it was performed alongside other monumental works:

> Went with R—— to the Albert Hall to the *Empress of Ireland* Memorial Concert with massed bands. We heard the *Symphonie Pathétique*, Chopin's Funeral March, "Trauermarsch" from *Götterdammerung*, the "Ride of the Valkyries" and a solemn melody from Bach. This afternoon I regard as a mountain peak in my existence. For two solid hours I sat like an Eagle on a rock gazing into infinity—a very fine sensation for a London Sparrow. (003-491)

## A Frenzy of Exhilaration

On later listening, the symphony's emotional impact had not diminished. Barbellion recorded the physical sensation produced by its third movement—a famously energetic march that builds to a triumphant climax before the devastating finale:

> Just lately I've heard a lot of music including Tchaikovsky's *Pathétique* and Fifth Symphonies, some Debussy, and odd pieces by Dukas, Glinka, Smetana, Mozart. I am chock-full of impressions of all this precious stuff and scarcely know what to write. As usual, the third movement of the *Pathétique* produced a frenzy of exhilaration; I seemed to put on several inches around my chest and wished to shout in a voice of thunder. (003-708)

The symphony's emotional trajectory—from the dark opening through the frenetic march to the unprecedented "[[death|dying]] away" of the finale—resonated with Barbellion's own consciousness of [[death|mortality]] and his hunger for intensity of experience before death.
