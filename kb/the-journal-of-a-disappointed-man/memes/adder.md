## Overview

The **Adder** (*Vipera berus*), also known as the common European adder or viper, is the only venomous snake native to the British Isles. Barbellion encountered the adder during his field expeditions as a young naturalist, both in the wild and through dissection.

## Observations in the Journal

Barbellion's most vivid encounter with an adder occurred during a visit to the Valley of Rocks, where he captured what he identified as a "Red Viper," believing it to be a distinct fourth species of British serpent:

> Went to L—— Sessions. After the Court rose, I transcribed my notes quickly and walked out to the famous Valley of Rocks which Southey described as the ribs of the old Earth poking through. At the bottom of one of the hills saw a snake, a Red Viper. Put my boot on him quickly so that he couldn't get away and then recognised him as a specimen of what I consider to be the fourth species of British Serpent — *Vipera rubra*. (PN=002-298)

The capture turned into an adventure when the seemingly dead snake revived during his railway journey home:

> As he was lifeless I wrapped him in paper and put him in my pocket — though to make assurance doubly sure I left the string on and let its end hang out over my pocket. So home by a two hours' railway journey with the adder in the pocket of my overcoat and the overcoat on the rack over my head. Settled down to the reading of a book on Spinoza's *Ethics*. At home it proved to be quite alive, and, on being pulled out by the string, coiled up on the drawing-room floor and hissed in a fury, to my infinite surprise. (PN=002-300)

He also prepared the skull of a [[grass-snake]], noting with characteristic self-reflective wit:

> I fancy I scooped out the eyes with patent delight — I suppose symbolically, as though, on behalf of the rest of suffering humanity, I were wiping off the old score against the beast for its behaviour in the Garden of Eden. (PN=002-838)

## Natural History

The adder inhabits heathland, moorland, and woodland edges across Britain. Barbellion observed it in the Valley of Rocks, a dramatic coastal landform in Devon. Adders are viviparous and typically emerge from hibernation in early spring. Though venomous, they are generally shy and rarely bite unless provoked — a fact that makes Barbellion's comically reckless handling of the half-dead specimen all the more striking.

## Taxonomic Context

Barbellion's identification of the red variant as a separate species (*Vipera rubra*) reflects the taxonomic understanding of his era, though modern herpetology treats it as a colour morph of *Vipera berus*. The incident exemplifies both his serious naturalist ambitions and the sometimes rough-and-ready methods of the Victorian field collector.
