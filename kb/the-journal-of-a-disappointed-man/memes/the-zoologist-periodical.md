**The Zoologist** was a British monthly periodical of natural history, founded in 1843 and devoted to the publication of field observations, taxonomic notes, and shorter communications in zoology. For the teenage Barbellion, still living in [[devon|Devon]] and dreaming of a scientific career, it represented a portal into the world of professional natural history—and his first publication in its pages was a landmark in his intellectual development.

## A Boyhood Achievement

H. G. Wells noted in his introduction to the journal that Barbellion was already publishing by the age of eighteen:

> By the time he was eighteen he was already publishing quite excellent observations of his own in such periodicals as the *Zoologist*. (P02-003)

## The First Publication

Barbellion's own references to the *Zoologist* are tinged with the nostalgia of a man who had moved beyond his early enthusiasms. Listing the books and periodicals that had once absorbed him—and that now induced "slight mental nausea"—he included:

> My old favourites—Waterton's *Wanderings*, Gilbert White, *The Zoologist*, etc.—have no interest for me. (003-697)

The dismissal is characteristically absolute; yet the *Zoologist* had been the proving ground for the young naturalist, the venue where his earliest contributions to science appeared. That he could now dismiss it testified to the distance he had travelled—from Devonshire field naturalist to London museum man, and from zoology to music.
