## Overview

The *Journal de Marie Bashkirtseff* (published posthumously in 1887) is the diary of [[marie-bashkirtseff|Marie Bashkirtseff]] (1858–1884), a Russian-born artist and diarist who lived in France. The journal, originally written in French, documents her artistic ambitions, intellectual life, and struggle with tuberculosis, which claimed her life at age 25. It became a celebrated work of confessional autobiography in late-nineteenth-century Europe. Mathilde Blind contributed an introductory essay to the English edition, which Barbellion read.

## Barbellion's Engagement

Barbellion discovered [[marie-bashkirtseff|Marie Bashkirtseff]] through a quotation encountered in a book about Strindberg, and was immediately struck by the resemblance to his own sensibility. He went to the Library to read Mathilde Blind's introductory essay to her Journal, and was deeply affected (PN 003-563, PN 003-564). His reaction was one of simultaneous fascination and dismay at finding himself so thoroughly anticipated:

> This evening went to the Library and read about her in Mathilde Blind's introductory essay to her Journal. I am simply astounded. It would be difficult in all the world's history to discover any two persons with temperaments so alike. She is the "very spit of me"! I devoured Mathilde Blind's pages more and more astonished. We are identical! Oh, Marie Bashkirtseff! how we should have hated one another! [PN 003-564]

He later records simply "[[marie-bashkirtseff|Marie Bashkirtseff]]'s *Journal*" in a list of his [[literature|reading]] (PN 003-719), and describes feverishly trying to read it alongside his other activities (PN 003-821). The identification was so profound that he later described his literary parentage as "[[sir-thomas-browne|Sir Thomas Browne]] and my mother [[marie-bashkirtseff|Marie Bashkirtseff]]" (PN 004-152), suggesting the journal shaped his own self-conception as a diarist.
