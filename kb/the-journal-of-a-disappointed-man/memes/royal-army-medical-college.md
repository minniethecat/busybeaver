The **Royal Army Medical College** was a British military medical institution in London, established in 1907 on the Chelsea Embankment (Millbank). It provided postgraduate training for officers of the Royal Army Medical Corps.

## Barbellion's Connection

In a period of active medical consultation—seeking diagnosis for symptoms that would eventually be identified as multiple sclerosis—Barbellion records a visit to the College:

> Spent the afternoon at the Royal Army Medical College in consultation with the Professor. (003-992)

## Historical Context

Founded at the former Millbank Prison site, the Royal Army Medical College served as both a teaching hospital and a research institution during the Edwardian period and the First World War. Its professors were leading military physicians of the era.
