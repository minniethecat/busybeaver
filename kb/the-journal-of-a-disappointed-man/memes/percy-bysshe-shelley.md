## Overview

Percy Bysshe Shelley (1792–1822) was one of the major English Romantic poets, known for lyrics including "Ode to the West Wind" and "To a Skylark" as well as the verse drama *Prometheus Unbound*. Barbellion encountered Shelley as a cultural presence — a postman [[literature|reading]] Shelley on his rounds, a fellow passenger [[literature|reading]] Shelley in an omnibus — and used these sightings to reflect on the relationship between poetry, courage, and everyday life.

## Relationship to Barbellion

Shelley appears in the Journal not through close textual engagement but through emblematic anecdotes. The postman [[literature|reading]] Shelley as he walked his delivery rounds (003-272) became for Barbellion a symbol of intellectual life persisting amid ordinary labour — a vignette that resonated with his own identity as a working-class self-educator. A later reference expresses a [[disappointment|failure]] of nerve: "I hadn't the courage. Shelley in a crowded omnibus suddenly..." (003-931), suggesting Barbellion witnessed someone publicly [[literature|reading]] Shelley and felt, by contrast, his own timidity.

In the final entries, Barbellion compares Shelley unfavourably to Pascal: "If Shelley was 'gold dusty from tumbling in the sunshine,' Pascal had [[honey-bee|bee]]n in the abyss" (004-505), reflecting Barbellion's gravitation toward writers who had confronted suffering directly.

## References in the Journal

The postman anecdote is the most striking Shelley reference:

> "I met a postman once reading Shelley as he walked his rounds." (003-272)

A later moment of self-reproach:

> "But I hadn't the courage. Shelley in a crowded omnibus suddenly..." (003-931)

Shelley's limitations as perceived by the ailing Barbellion:

> "Shelley, too, being an artist could not lose himself in his art." (004-366)

The final, dismissive comparison:

> "If Shelley was 'gold dusty from tumbling in the sunshine,' Pascal had been in the abyss." (004-505)

## Biographical Note

Shelley was expelled from Oxford for publishing a pamphlet on atheism, eloped twice, and lived in exile in Italy, where he drowned at the age of twenty-nine. His poetry is characterised by visionary idealism, radical politics, and a pantheistic reverence for nature. He was married to Mary Shelley, author of *Frankenstein*. Shelley's early death and uncompromising idealism made him the archetype of the Romantic poet for subsequent generations.
