## Overview

Fish, including the Atlantic Cod (*Gadus morhua*) and related species, appear in Barbellion's journal primarily through his observations of fishermen — human interactions with the marine ecosystem that the naturalist records with ethnographic as well as biological interest. His watch of "some fishermen bring in a large catch" (002-061) captures the meeting point of nature, labour, and observation.

## Observations in the Journal

Barbellion's fish observations are filtered through the activity of fishing. He watches fishermen at work near a lighthouse (002-061), noting their methods and their catch. The naturalist here is also an ethnographer — interested not only in the fish but in the human practices that bring fish to shore. This layered observation is characteristic of the journal's method.

## Significance

Fish represent for Barbellion the marine dimension of natural history — less accessible than terrestrial observation, mediated by the skills and labour of fishermen, but no less a part of the natural world he seeks to understand. His observations of fishing are also observations of community, of the working life of coastal people.
