The **Queensland Museum**, founded in 1862 and located in Brisbane, was the principal natural history museum of Queensland, Australia. Its scientific publications—the *Memoirs of the Queensland Museum*—circulated among naturalists worldwide, including Barbellion in his South Kensington office.

## Barbellion's Connection

Barbellion encountered the Queensland Museum through its publications. Reading a massive taxonomic monograph on chalcid wasps, he was struck by the author's extraordinary dedication and quoted it in full:

> The Chalcidoidea are minute winged insects that parasitise other insects, and in the *Memoirs of the Queensland Museum* (Vol. I, 1912) you shall find an enormous catalogue of them by a person named Girault who writes the following dedication… (004-035)

The dedication that follows—a florid, self-dramatising manifesto by the entomologist Alexandre Arsène Girault—fascinated Barbellion because it mirrored his own condition: the isolated naturalist pouring his life into obscure scientific work while raging against institutional indifference.

## In the Journal

Barbellion's attraction to Girault's writing reveals his sympathy with the marginal scientific worker—the taxonomist whose labours are immense, whose audience is tiny, and whose recognition is nil. He saw in Girault's extravagant dedication a version of his own predicament.
