## About

The *Evening Standard* (founded 1827 as the *Standard*) is a [[london|London]] evening newspaper. By Barbellion's time in the early 1910s, it was a well-established daily providing news coverage, political commentary, and cultural reporting to the [[london|London]] commuting public. It competed with the *[[pall-mall-gazette|Pall Mall Gazette]]*, *Westminster Gazette*, and other evening titles for the attention of the city's vast newspaper readership.

## Barbellion's Response

### The Library Evening

Barbellion records a typical evening's [[literature|reading]] at the Library in [[london|London]]:

> Returned to the Library and read the Dublin Review (article on Samuel Butler), North American Review (one on Henry James) and dined at seven. After dinner, read: Evening Standard, Saturday Westminster, and the New Statesman. Smoked six cigarettes and went to bed. Tomorrow Fifth Symphony of Beethoven.

(003-596)

### The Newspaper Regimen

This single passage is the only explicit mention of the *Evening Standard* in the journal, but it encapsulates a larger pattern. Barbellion's newspaper [[illness|consumption]] was not casual browsing but a disciplined programme: two heavyweight quarterlies during the afternoon, followed by a meal, then three newspapers in succession, followed by cigarettes, with [[beethoven|Beethoven]] scheduled for the following day.

The *Evening Standard* appears here as part of a balanced diet of print. After the intellectual labour of the *[[dublin-review|Dublin Review]]* and *[[north-american-review|North American Review]]*, the evening papers offered lighter fare — news, politics, cultural gossip — consumed with the ritual pleasure of cigarettes. This rhythm of intensity followed by relaxation, of high culture alternating with journalism, is characteristic of Barbellion's self-education method. He was, as he described himself, "a ragbag" of heterogeneous [[literature|reading]], and the *Evening Standard* was one thread in that fabric.

### The London Newspaper Ecology

In the 1910s, [[london|London]] supported a dense ecosystem of daily and evening newspapers: the *Times*, *[[daily-news|Daily News]]*, *Daily Chronicle*, *Morning Post*, *Daily Telegraph*, *[[pall-mall-gazette|Pall Mall Gazette]]*, *Westminster Gazette*, and the *Evening Standard* among them. Barbellion read several of these regularly. His access came primarily through the Library and [[literature|reading]] rooms — spaces that democratised access to print for those, like Barbellion, who could not afford subscriptions to multiple papers.
