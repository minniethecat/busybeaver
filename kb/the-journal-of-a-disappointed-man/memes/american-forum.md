## About

*The American Forum* was an American periodical active in the early twentieth century. Its editorial remit appears to have included scientific and general-interest articles, drawing contributions from British as well as American writers.

## Barbellion's Engagement

Barbellion's experience with the *American Forum* is recorded in a single, richly detailed passage that encapsulates the [[disappointment|frustration]]s of the struggling freelance writer in Edwardian Britain:

> A more recent affair I had with the American Forum, which delighted me by publishing my article, but did not pay — though the Editor went out of his way to write that "payment was on publication." I did not venture to remonstrate as I had another article on the stocks which they also printed without payment.

(004-517)

This passage is revealing on several levels. First, the *Forum* published not one but two of his articles — a genuine editorial validation. Second, the Editor's evasive assurance that "payment was on publication" is a classic piece of freelance-world duplicity, one that Barbellion clearly recognises but feels powerless to challenge. His decision not to "venture to remonstrate" while a second article was under consideration reflects the asymmetry of power between a young, unknown writer and an established periodical.

The *American Forum* episode belongs to a broader pattern in which Barbellion achieves modest publication success — enough to sustain hope — but never the financial return or [[fame|recognition]] that would lift him out of precarious journalism. The experience echoes his submissions to [[the-english-review]] (rejected) and [[the-fortnightly-review]] (also rejected).
