The **Royal College of Surgeons of England** (RCS), located at Lincoln's Inn Fields in London, was the principal professional body regulating surgical practice in Britain. Founded in 1800, it conducted examinations and maintained professional standards for surgeons throughout the Empire.

## Barbellion's Connection

Barbellion's contact with the surgical profession was not voluntary but medical—part of the long diagnostic journey that eventually led to his multiple sclerosis diagnosis. The various specialists and examinations he underwent belonged to a professional world regulated by the Royal College:

> Medical specialists—Harley Street men… (003-541)

Though he never names the College directly, the surgical consultations he records (including the afternoon at the [[royal-army-medical-college|Royal Army Medical College]]) took place within the professional framework it governed. The College represented the institutional embodiment of a profession whose verdict on his case—"disseminated sclerosis"—would define the remainder of his life.

## In the Journal

Barbellion's response to the medical profession was characteristically ambivalent. He respected its scientific authority while resenting its power over his fate. The "Harley Street men" appear in his journal as a caste apart—privileged, certain, remote—whose pronouncements he could neither accept nor ignore.
