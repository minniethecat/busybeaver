The **Académie des Sciences** (Paris Academy of Sciences) is the French national scientific academy, founded in 1666 by Louis XIV. By the Edwardian era it stood as one of the most prestigious scientific institutions in the world, a symbol of institutionalised scientific authority.

## Barbellion's Connection

Barbellion invokes the Academy's name in conversation, contrasting the official recognition enjoyed by his more successful contemporaries with his own obscurity:

> "Yes," said I, "and the Paris Academy of Sciences were discussing the question…" (003-757)

His ironic tone—the mock-formal "Yes" and the deliberate inflation of the subject—reveals his characteristic blend of self-deprecation and intellectual ambition.

## In the Journal

Barbellion, who read his own first paper at the [[zoological-society-london|Zoological Society]], was acutely aware of the hierarchy of scientific institutions—from the local natural history society to the great national academies. The Académie represented the pinnacle of that hierarchy, a world of recognition from which he felt himself excluded.
