## Overview

Devon is the landscape of Barbellion's origins — the rural West Country where he was born, where he made his first naturalist observations, and to which he returns in the journal's final Part. In the geography of Barbellion's imagination, Devon is the counterpoint to London: rural where London is urban, restorative where London is exhausting, home where London is exile.

## Barbellion's Devon

Devon appears in the journal primarily through memory and return. Barbellion's early naturalist observations were made in its fields and along its coasts. When, in Part III, he retreats from London to a "country cottage in perfect isolation" (004-155), he is returning — physically and psychologically — to the Devon of his origins. The countryside that formed him becomes the countryside that receives him back, diminished but still observant.

## Significance

Devon represents the possibility of rootedness — a connection to place that London, for all its excitement, could never provide. Barbellion's return to the countryside is not a defeat but a homecoming, and his late journal entries, written in rural seclusion, have a quality of settled attention that the feverish London entries often lack.
