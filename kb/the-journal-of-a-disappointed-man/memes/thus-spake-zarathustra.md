## Overview

*Thus Spake Zarathustra* (*Also sprach Zarathustra: Ein Buch für Alle und Keinen*) is a philosophical novel by Friedrich Nietzsche, composed in four parts between 1883 and 1885. The work presents the teachings of the prophet Zarathustra, who descends from his mountain solitude to proclaim the death of God, the Übermensch, and the doctrine of eternal recurrence. It is Nietzsche's most widely read work and a cornerstone of late-nineteenth-century philosophical literature.

## Barbellion's Engagement

Barbellion encountered Nietzsche through a newspaper quotation that resonated with his own sense of creative suffering (PN 003-555). He recorded the passage in his journal, recognising in Nietzsche's words a description of his own internal struggle — "You must carry a chaos inside you to give birth to a dancing star" (PN 003-555). During a period of acute [[discontent|restlessness]] and mental distress in October 1914, he read *Thus Spake Zarathustra* directly:

> First I read the paper; then I finished the book I was reading — "*Thus Spake Zarathustra*." Not knowing quite what next to do, I took my boots off and poured out another cup of coffee. [PN 003-621]

Later entries show Barbellion [[literature|reading]] Nietzsche more broadly, noting "Reading Nietzsche. What splendid physic he is to Pomeranian puppies like myself!" (PN 003-677, PN 003-678). He sought in Nietzsche a source of courage and philosophical fortitude against his physical decline.
