The **Fabian Society** was a British socialist organisation founded in 1884, dedicated to advancing democratic socialism through gradual reform rather than revolution. Its membership included leading intellectuals of the Edwardian era, notably [[bernard-shaw|George Bernard Shaw]], H. G. Wells, and Sidney and Beatrice Webb.

## Barbellion's Connection

In a moment of indecision characteristic of his restless intellectual life, Barbellion weighed attending a Fabian Society meeting:

> …my wife or should I go to the Fabian Society and hear Bernard Shaw. (003-627)

The juxtaposition—his wife versus a political lecture—captures the tension between domestic life and intellectual ambition that runs through the journal.

## Historical Context

The Fabian Society was instrumental in the founding of the London School of Economics (1895) and the Labour Party (1900). Its emphasis on expert knowledge and scientific administration resonated with the Edwardian faith in rational progress—a faith that Barbellion, as a naturalist, shared, even as his own experience of illness undermined it.
