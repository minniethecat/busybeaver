## Overview

*Punch, or the London Charivari* was a British weekly magazine of humour and satire, founded in 1841 and running until 2002. For Barbellion, *Punch* represents one endpoint of the periodical spectrum — from the highbrow *Hibbert Journal* to the popular *Punch* — to which he submitted manuscripts in his campaign for publication. His reference to receiving "rejected MSS. back from every conceivable kind of periodical, from *Punch* to the *Hibbert Journal*" (004-517) uses these two titles to demarcate the full range of his [[disappointment|failure]].

## Barbellion's Response

The reference to *Punch* is wry and self-deprecating. The image of Barbellion — the earnest [[natural-history|naturalist]], the self-tormenting intellectual — submitting humour pieces to *Punch* is incongruous, and he seems to recognise the incongruity. The catalogue of rejection (004-517) is recited with a mixture of bitterness and pride: bitterness at the rejections, pride at the sheer persistence that the catalogue represents.

## Significance

*Punch* exemplifies the indiscriminate quality of Barbellion's [[ambition|literary ambition]] — he submitted to everyone, was rejected by everyone, and kept submitting. The magazine's name, in the context of the journal, acquires an additional resonance: Barbellion, too, is engaged in a kind of verbal pugilism with the literary world, and he refuses to stay down.
