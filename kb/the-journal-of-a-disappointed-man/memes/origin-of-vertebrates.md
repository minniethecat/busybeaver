## About

*The Origin of Vertebrates* is a 1908 scientific work by British physiologist [[walter-gaskell]] that proposes a theory of vertebrate evolution from invertebrate ancestors, specifically from arthropods.

## Barbellion's Response

Barbellion listed Gaskell's *Origin of Vertebrates* as one of the three most fascinating [[literature|books]] in science he had read, alongside Darwin's *[[expression-of-the-emotions|Expression of the Emotions]]* and [[henri-bergson|Bergson]]'s *[[le-rire-bergson|Le Rire]]* (002-274). The book aligns with his deep interest in evolutionary biology and comparative anatomy.

> The three most fascinating books in Science that I have so far read are (easily):—1. Darwin's *Expression of the Emotions*. 2. Gaskell's *Origin of Vertebrates*. 3. Bergson's *Le Rire*.
>
> (002-274)

## Context

Barbellion's [[literature|reading]] of Gaskell reflects his systematic engagement with contemporary evolutionary science. His journal documents extensive [[literature|reading]] in comparative anatomy, including Wiedersheim's *[[comparative-anatomy-of-vertebrates|Comparative Anatomy of Vertebrates]]*, which he carried with him even to Board of Guardians meetings.
