# Cominius

## Character Overview

Cominius is the Roman [[consulship|consul]] who commands the army at [[corioli|Corioli]] and publicly acclaims [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]'s deeds. As [[consulship|consul]], he represents the political-military establishment that [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]] both serves and ultimately defies.

## Dramatic Function

## Military Role

Cominius serves as one of [[rome|Rome]]'s two generals against the [[volscians|Volscians]], alongside [[titus-lartius|Titus Lartius]] (P01-003). While Lartius commands the siege of [[corioli|Corioli]], Cominius leads the main Roman force against the [[volscians|Volsci]]an field army (003-002). When the bleeding [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Marcius]] arrives from his single-handed assault on [[corioli|Corioli]] to reinforce Cominius, the general greets him with amazement and gratitude; together they rout the [[volscians|Volscians]] (005-002). Cominius later speaks in the [[roman-senate|Senate]] in support of [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]'s bid for the [[consulship|consul]]ship, and after [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]'s [[exile|banishment]], he remains one of the exiled general's steadfast advocates.
