# Aedile

## Character Overview

The Aedile is a plebeian official who assists the tribunes in their political operations — delivering messages, managing logistics, and providing the institutional infrastructure of tribunician power. Though a minor role, the Aedile demonstrates that the tribunes' authority is not merely personal charisma but an institutional apparatus with its own functionaries and procedures.

## Dramatic Appearances

The Aedile appears at key moments in the tribunes' campaign against [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]. In Act III, Scene 1, Brutus summons the aediles to apprehend [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]], invoking their authority as officers of the people (014-002). In Act III, Scene 3, the Aedile reports that [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]] is approaching, accompanied by [[menenius-agrippa|Menenius]] and senators who support him — a moment that tests the tribunes' resolve (016-002). Following [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]'s [[exile|banishment]], Sicinius orders the Aedile to dismiss the assembled citizens, a gesture of the tribunes' attempt to restore civic order after their political victory (018-002).
