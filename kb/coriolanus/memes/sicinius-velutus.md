# Sicinius Velutus

## Character Overview

Sicinius Velutus is the more politically astute of the two tribunes, wielding the office of tribune with strategic precision. He frames [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]'s [[pride|aristocratic pride]] as tyrannical ambition and orchestrates the procedural maneuvers that lead to [[exile|banishment]]. His political intelligence makes him an effective antagonist, though his cynicism raises questions about the legitimacy of his populism.

## Political Role

Sicinius Velutus is one of the two tribunes of the people, elected to represent plebeian interests against patrician power (P01-005). Alongside [[junius-brutus|Junius Brutus]], he orchestrates the political opposition to [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]'s [[consulship|consul]]ship (011-005). When [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]] refuses to perform the ritual humility of soliciting the people's voices, Sicinius seizes the opportunity to turn popular sentiment against him. During the ensuing confrontation, he calls upon the citizens for support — "Help, ye citizens!" (014-002) — demonstrating the tribune's power to mobilise the plebeian body politic against the aristocracy. After [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]'s [[exile|banishment]], Sicinius moves to consolidate the tribunes' victory, ordering the dismissal of the gathered crowds and counseling strategic restraint.
