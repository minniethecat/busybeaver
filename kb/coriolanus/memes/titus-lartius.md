# Titus Lartius

## Character Overview

Titus Lartius is a Roman general serving alongside [[cominius|Cominius]] in the [[volscians|Volsci]]an campaign. His role is primarily military — he witnesses and validates [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]'s heroism at [[corioli|Corioli]], providing institutional recognition of the martial excellence that defines the protagonist.

## Military Command

Titus Lartius is a Roman general, named in the Dramatis Personae as one of the two commanders against the [[volscians|Volscians]] (P01-003). He directs the siege of [[corioli|Corioli]] while [[cominius|Cominius]] engages the [[volscians|Volsci]]an field army (003-002). Lartius witnesses [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Marcius]]'s extraordinary single-handed entry into [[corioli|Corioli]] and later, on the battlefield, he praises [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Marcius]]'s prowess while noting the severity of his wounds: "thy exercise hath been too violent / For a second course of fight" (005-002). Though Lartius's role is limited to the military campaign of Acts I–II, he embodies the Roman martial ethos against which [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]'s extreme valor — and extreme pride — are measured.
