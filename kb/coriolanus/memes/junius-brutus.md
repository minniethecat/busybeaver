# Junius Brutus

## Character Overview

Junius Brutus is the more cautious of the two tribunes, often counselling restraint after Sicinius's more aggressive moves. His advice to 'seem humbler after it is done' (018-002) reflects a sophisticated understanding of plebeian power's fragility — the tribunes must not appear triumphant, for patrician resentment is a permanent political fact.

## Political Strategy

As tribune of the people, Junius Brutus works alongside [[sicinius-velutus|Sicinius Velutus]] to oppose [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]'s bid for the [[consulship|consul]]ship. Brutus orchestrates the political confrontation that leads to [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]'s [[exile|banishment]], recognizing that [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]'s contempt for the plebeians makes him politically vulnerable (011-005). In the [[roman-senate|Senate]], Brutus and Sicinius occupy their tribunician seats as counterweights to patrician power (012-002). After the [[exile|banishment]], Brutus counsels tactical humility — "Let us seem humbler after it is done / Than when it was a-doing" (018-002) — revealing the tribunes' strategic political calculus.
