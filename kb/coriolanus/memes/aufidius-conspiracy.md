## 嫉妒的根源

Coriolanus 投奔 Volscian 阵营后迅速获得 Aufidius 的信任，被委以联军指挥权。他率领 Volscian 军队一路打到罗马城下，声名远播，甚至超过了 Aufidius 本人。Aufidius 的副将（Lieutenant）报告说，士兵们"use him as the grace 'fore meat, / Their talk at table, and their thanks at end"，而 Aufidius 本人则被遮蔽——"you are darken'd in this action, sir, / Even by your own"（023-002）。

Aufidius 在独白中承认这种被取代的屈辱："I seem'd his follower, not partner, and / He waged me with his countenance, as if / I had been mercenary"（029-002）。他痛恨 Coriolanus 的傲慢。这个曾经的敌人"bow'd his nature, never known before / But to be rough, unswayable and free"，用谄媚手段"water'd his new plants with dews of flattery, / Seducing so my friends"（029-002）。

## 阴谋组织

Aufidius 集结了三名共谋者（Conspirators），在 Antium 市政广场密谋除掉 Coriolanus。他告诉共谋者自己的处境如同"a man by his own alms empoison'd, / And with his charity slain"——他对 Coriolanus 的慷慨反而毒害了自己（029-002）。

共谋者指出局势的紧迫性：Coriolanus 回到 Antium 时受到盛大欢迎，"Splitting the air with noise"，而 Aufidius 进城时却"like a post, / And had no welcomes home"（029-002）。Aufidius 揭露了 Coriolanus 的真正罪行：在罗马城下，因为"a few drops of women's rheum"——母亲 Volumnia 的眼泪——他" sold the blood and labour / Of our great action"，与罗马达成和平（029-002）。Aufidius 因此下决心："therefore shall he die, / And I'll renew me in his fall"（029-002）。

第三共谋者提出刺杀策略：趁 Coriolanus 尚未开口向民众辩解之前动手——"at your vantage, / Ere he express himself, or move the people / With what he would say, let him feel your sword"（029-002）。他们承诺会在 Aufidius 先动手后一起跟上——"Which we will second"。

## 公开指控与对峙

在 Lords of Antium 面前，Coriolanus 汇报战绩："prosperously I have attempted and / With bloody passage led your wars even to / The gates of Rome"，并呈上与罗马议定的合约（029-002）。Aufidius 立即打断——"Read it not, noble lords; / But tell the traitor, in the high'st degree / He hath abused your powers"。

当 Coriolanus 愤怒地质问时，Aufidius 公开羞辱他："Ay, Marcius, Caius Marcius: dost thou think / I'll grace thee with that robbery, thy stol'n name / Coriolanus in Corioli?"（029-002）。他指责 Coriolanus 的和平协定是背叛——"Breaking his oath and resolution like / A twist of rotten silk"——并嘲讽他在母亲眼泪面前"whined and roar'd away your victory"。最后以最尖刻的侮辱收尾："Name not the god, thou boy of tears!"（029-002）。

Coriolanus 被彻底激怒，反驳 Aufidius 是他手下败将："Who wears my stripes impress'd upon him; that / Must bear my beating to his grave"，并独自一人在 Corioli"like an eagle in a dove-cote, I / Flutter'd your Volscians"（029-002）。

## 刺杀执行

共谋者高喊"Kill, kill, kill, kill, kill him!"，一拥而上拔剑刺杀了 Coriolanus。Aufidius 站在尸体上（"Aufidius stands on his body"）（029-002）。

## 事后反思

Lords of Antium 对刺杀反应复杂。第二 Lord 评价 Coriolanus 为"the most noble corse that ever herald / Did follow to his urn"（029-002）。第三 Lord 则将部分责任归于 Coriolanus 自身的性格——"His own impatience / Takes from Aufidius a great part of blame"（029-002）。

Aufidius 在 Coriolanus 死后表现出悔意，他命令以军礼安葬："My rage is gone; / And I am struck with sorrow. Take him up. / Help, three o' the chiefest soldiers; I'll be one"（029-002）。他要求鼓手"Beat thou the drum, that it speak mournfully"，并承认 Coriolanus"shall have a noble memory"，尽管他在这座城市中"widow'd and unchilded many a one"（029-002）。

## 戏剧意义

Aufidius 的阴谋展现了嫉妒的政治破坏力——Aufidius 对 Coriolanus 的感情始终在钦佩与仇恨之间摇摆。从 Act I 的单挑对决（008-002），到 Act IV 的热情接纳（"I lov'd the maid I married; never man / Sigh'd truer breath; but that I see thee here, / Thou noble thing! more dances my rapt heart / Than when I first my wedded mistress saw"），再到最终的谋杀，这条关系线是整部悲剧的情感主轴之一。

Aufidius 的阴谋还揭示了荣誉文化的深层悲剧：杀死 Coriolanus 的是他自己拒绝屈膝（lack of stooping）的性格，以及荣誉文化中"the fall of either / Makes the survivor heir of all"的零和逻辑（029-002）。Aufidius 杀死了一个他内心尊敬的人，而 Lords 的评语"valour will weep"点明了这场谋杀的悲剧本质。
