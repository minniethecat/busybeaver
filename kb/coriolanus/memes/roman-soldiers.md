# Roman Soldiers

## Character Overview

The Roman Soldiers are the human material of [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]'s military achievement. Their presence on stage — following him into [[corioli|Corioli]], acclaiming his victory — establishes the martial community in which his identity is formed and his authority is absolute.

## Function in the Drama

The Roman soldiers serve under the command of [[cominius|Cominius]] and [[titus-lartius|Titus Lartius]] during the [[volscians|Volsci]]an campaign, appearing in the battle sequences of Acts I–II (P01-017). They follow [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Marcius]] into [[corioli|Corioli]]'s gates and later fight alongside [[cominius|Cominius]] against the [[volscians|Volsci]]an field army (006-002). Under Lartius, they guard the captured city of [[corioli|Corioli]] (007-002). As a collective, the soldiers represent the martial competence of [[rome|Rome]]'s commoners — a counterpoint to [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]'s insistence that only patrician virtue can sustain the state. Their presence on stage, in drum and colours, provides the sensory texture of Roman military life against which the play's political conflicts unfold.
