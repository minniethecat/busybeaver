# Volscian Territory

## Location Overview

Volscian Territory encompasses the lands of Rome's southern neighbours, including the cities of Corioli and Antium. The movement between Roman and Volscian space structures the play's geography of loyalty and identity. The play opens in Rome but quickly shifts to Volscian ground: the senate-house at Corioli where Aufidius receives the Roman declaration of war (002-002), and the highways that connect the two territories where spies and defectors cross between worlds (019-001).

## Dramatic Function

Volscian Territory is more than a backdrop — it is the alternative political space that defines Coriolanus's trajectory. His capture of Corioli in Act 1 (003-002, 004-002) earns him his name and his highest honor; his journey through Volscian territory to Antium in Act 4 marks his transformation from Roman hero to Roman enemy; and his return to Volscian-held Corioli in Act 5 is the site of his death (029-002).

## Scenes Set Here

- **act-1-scene-2**: The Volscian senate-house at Corioli; Aufidius learns of the Roman approach (002-002).
- **act-1-scene-4**: Before the walls of Corioli; the Roman assault begins (003-002).
- **act-4-scene-3**: A highway between Rome and Antium; a Roman spy meets a Volscian counterpart, revealing Coriolanus's approach (019-002).
