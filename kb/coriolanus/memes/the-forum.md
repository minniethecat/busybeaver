# The Forum

## Location Overview

The Forum is the political space where patrician and plebeian Rome meet. The consular election, the tribunes' speeches, and Coriolanus's political self-destruction all unfold in this public arena (014-002), where speech rather than swords determines fate (012-002).

## Dramatic Function

This location serves as a significant setting in *Coriolanus*, its spatial and symbolic qualities contributing to the play's dramatic effect and thematic resonance.
