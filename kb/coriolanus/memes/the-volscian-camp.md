# The Volscian Camp

## Location Overview

The Volscian Camp is the military community that adopts Coriolanus after his defection. Set at a small distance from Rome (023-001), the camp becomes the base from which Coriolanus wages his campaign of revenge against his native city. The soldiers' adulation of the Roman exile — "they use him as the grace 'fore meat" (023-002) — creates the jealousy in Aufidius that proves fatal. His lieutenant reports that the soldiers "use him as the grace 'fore meat, Their talk at table, and their thanks at end; And you are darken'd in this action, sir, Even by your own" (023-002).

## Dramatic Function

The camp is the institutional space of Coriolanus's second military identity — a Volscian general rather than a Roman one. Aufidius's growing resentment, sparked by the soldiers' preference for Coriolanus (023-002), culminates in the public confrontation and assassination in Act 5, Scene 6, where Coriolanus's martial boasts — "With bloody passage led your wars even to The gates of Rome" (029-002) — provoke the conspirators to strike, bringing the tragedy to its bloody conclusion in the very city that gave Coriolanus his name.

## Scenes Set Here

- **act-4-scene-7**: Aufidius's lieutenant reports the soldiers' adulation of Coriolanus, planting the seeds of jealousy (023-002).
- **act-5-scene-6**: The final confrontation and death of Coriolanus in the public space of Corioli (029-002).
