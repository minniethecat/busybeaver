# Tullus Aufidius

## Character Overview

Tullus Aufidius is the [[volscians|Volsci]]an counterpart to [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]] — a warrior of comparable stature whose hatred of his Roman rival is transformed into alliance, then twisted back into murderous jealousy. His final denunciation of [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]] as 'boy of tears' triggers the mob violence that kills the hero.

## The Rivalry with Coriolanus

Tullus Aufidius is the general of the [[volscians|Volscians]] and [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]'s defining rival. [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]] himself acknowledges Aufidius as a worthy adversary: "I sin in envying his nobility" (001-002). The two warriors meet in single combat during the battle at [[corioli|Corioli]], where they fight fiercely before [[volscians|Volsci]]an soldiers drag Aufidius away (008-002). After [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]'s [[exile|banishment]], Aufidius welcomes his former enemy into the [[volscians|Volsci]]an camp, seeing in the exiled Roman a weapon against [[rome|Rome]]. Yet their partnership carries the seeds of its own destruction: Aufidius grows jealous of [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]'s popularity among the [[volscians|Volscians]], and his conspirators (P01-011) eventually move against the Roman. In the play's final tragedy, Aufidius's resentment erupts in a public confrontation that proves fatal to [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]].
