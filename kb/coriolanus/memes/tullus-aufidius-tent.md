# Aufidius's Tent

## Location Overview

Aufidius's Tent — more precisely, his hall in Antium — is the space where Coriolanus presents himself in exile, offering his martial service to his former enemy (021-002). It is also the space where Aufidius and the conspirators plot his assassination. The tent functions as a space of military counsel that becomes a space of fatal betrayal.

## Dramatic Function

The tent is the threshold of Coriolanus's second life. Entering Aufidius's hall in Act 4, Scene 5, Coriolanus crosses from Roman to Volscian identity, offering the general his "revenge" against Rome (021-002). In Act 5, Scene 6, the same space — now returned to Corioli — witnesses the conspiracy's culmination as the conspirators debate their grievance and act: "let him feel your sword, Which we will second" (029-002), leading to the fatal cry "Kill, kill, kill, kill, kill him!" (029-002). The tent that once received the defector now contains the victim.

## Scenes Set Here

- **act-4-scene-5**: Coriolanus enters Aufidius's hall in Antium and offers his service against Rome (021-002).
- **act-5-scene-6**: The conspirators confront and kill Coriolanus in the public space of Corioli (029-002).
