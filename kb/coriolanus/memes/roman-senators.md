# Roman Senators

## Character Overview

The Roman Senators represent the patrician order in its institutional form. They endorse [[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]] for the [[consulship|consul]]ship, defend him against tribunician attacks, and are helpless to prevent his [[exile|banishment]]. Their collective impotence in the face of plebeian mobilization dramatizes the structural vulnerability of [[patrician-power|aristocratic power]] in the mixed constitution.

## Dramatic Function

The character serves a supporting function in the dramatic structure of *[[caius-marcius-coriolanus|Coriolanus]]*, advancing the play's central conflicts through action and speech.
