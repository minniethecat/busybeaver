# Volscian War Council

## Event Summary

The Volscian War Council of Act 1, Scene 2 establishes the external threat to Rome and introduces Tullus Aufidius as Coriolanus's counterpart. In the senate-house at Corioli (002-001), Aufidius reveals to the Volscian senators that Rome has discovered their military preparations: "They of Rome are entered in our counsels And know how we proceed" (002-002). He reads aloud an intercepted letter naming Cominius, Marcius, and Titus Lartius as the commanders of the Roman expeditionary force (002-002).

Aufidius accepts command against his old enemy with grim determination: "If we and Caius Marcius chance to meet, 'Tis sworn between us we shall ever strike Till one can do no more" (002-002). This oath of mutual destruction with Coriolanus frames the entire play's martial action and foreshadows their fatal convergence in Act V.

## Dramatic Significance

The War Council scene establishes the symmetry between Rome and the Volsci that structures the play. The Volscian senate mirrors the Roman Senate (002-002), Aufidius mirrors Coriolanus, and the military values that unite them — honour, prowess, hatred of the enemy — transcend national allegiance. This symmetry makes Coriolanus's later defection narratively credible: the Volscian world he enters in Act IV is one whose martial code he already understands.

## Participants

- **Tullus Aufidius**: The Volscian general who reveals Rome's military preparations and swears his oath against Coriolanus (002-002).
- **Volscian Senators**: The governing council of Corioli who receive Aufidius's military intelligence and authorize the Volscian response (002-002).

## Aftermath and Consequences

The council's decision to press war against Rome sets in motion the campaign that culminates at the Battle of Corioli. Aufidius's personal oath against Coriolanus — "ever strike Till one can do no more" (002-002) — will be fulfilled only in the final scene, when the conspirators kill Coriolanus at Aufidius's feet.
