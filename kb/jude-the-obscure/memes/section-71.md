[071-001] The cathedral bell close at hand was sounding for five o’clock service.

[071-002] “I should not mind having a spin there with a fellow’s arm round my

[071-003] waist,” she said to one of the men. “But Lord, I must be getting home

[071-004] again—there’s a lot to do. No dancing for me!”

[071-005] When she reached home she was met at the door by Stagg, and one or two

[071-006] other of Jude’s fellow stoneworkers. “We are just going down to the

[071-007] river,” said the former, “to see the boat-bumping. But we’ve called

[071-008] round on our way to ask how your husband is.”

[071-009] “He’s sleeping nicely, thank you,” said Arabella.

[071-010] “That’s right. Well now, can’t you give yourself half an hour’s

[071-011] relaxation, Mrs. Fawley, and come along with us? ’Twould do you good.”

[071-012] “I should like to go,” said she. “I’ve never seen the boat-racing, and

[071-013] I hear it is good fun.”

[071-014] “Come along!”

[071-015] “How I _wish_ I could!” She looked longingly down the street. “Wait a

[071-016] minute, then. I’ll just run up and see how he is now. Father is with

[071-017] him, I believe; so I can most likely come.”

[071-018] They waited, and she entered. Downstairs the inmates were absent as

[071-019] before, having, in fact, gone in a body to the river where the

[071-020] procession of boats was to pass. When she reached the bedroom she found

[071-021] that her father had not even now come.

[071-022] “Why couldn’t he have been here!” she said impatiently. “He wants to

[071-023] see the boats himself—that’s what it is!”

[071-024] However, on looking round to the bed she brightened, for she saw that

[071-025] Jude was apparently sleeping, though he was not in the usual

[071-026] half-elevated posture necessitated by his cough. He had slipped down,

[071-027] and lay flat. A second glance caused her to start, and she went to the

[071-028] bed. His face was quite white, and gradually becoming rigid. She

[071-029] touched his fingers; they were cold, though his body was still warm.

[071-030] She listened at his chest. All was still within. The bumping of near

[071-031] thirty years had ceased.

[071-032] After her first appalled sense of what had happened, the faint notes of

[071-033] a military or other brass band from the river reached her ears; and in

[071-034] a provoked tone she exclaimed, “To think he should die just now! Why

[071-035] did he die just now!” Then meditating another moment or two she went to

[071-036] the door, softly closed it as before, and again descended the stairs.

[071-037] “Here she is!” said one of the workmen. “We wondered if you were coming

[071-038] after all. Come along; we must be quick to get a good place… Well, how

[071-039] is he? Sleeping well still? Of course, we don’t want to drag ’ee away

[071-040] if—”

[071-041] “Oh yes—sleeping quite sound. He won’t wake yet,” she said hurriedly.

[071-042] They went with the crowd down Cardinal Street, where they presently

[071-043] reached the bridge, and the gay barges burst upon their view. Thence

[071-044] they passed by a narrow slit down to the riverside path—now dusty, hot,

[071-045] and thronged. Almost as soon as they had arrived the grand procession

[071-046] of boats began; the oars smacking with a loud kiss on the face of the

[071-047] stream, as they were lowered from the perpendicular.

[071-048] “Oh, I say—how jolly! I’m glad I’ve come,” said Arabella. “And—it can’t

[071-049] hurt my husband—my being away.”

[071-050] On the opposite side of the river, on the crowded barges, were gorgeous

[071-051] nosegays of feminine beauty, fashionably arrayed in green, pink, blue,

[071-052] and white. The blue flag of the boat club denoted the centre of

[071-053] interest, beneath which a band in red uniform gave out the notes she

[071-054] had already heard in the death-chamber. Collegians of all sorts, in

[071-055] canoes with ladies, watching keenly for “our” boat, darted up and down.

[071-056] While she regarded the lively scene somebody touched Arabella in the

[071-057] ribs, and looking round she saw Vilbert.

[071-058] “That philtre is operating, you know!” he said with a leer. “Shame on

[071-059] ’ee to wreck a heart so!”

[071-060] “I shan’t talk of love to-day.”

[071-061] “Why not? It is a general holiday.”

[071-062] She did not reply. Vilbert’s arm stole round her waist, which act could

[071-063] be performed unobserved in the crowd. An arch expression overspread

[071-064] Arabella’s face at the feel of the arm, but she kept her eyes on the

[071-065] river as if she did not know of the embrace.

[071-066] The crowd surged, pushing Arabella and her friends sometimes nearly

[071-067] into the river, and she would have laughed heartily at the horse-play

[071-068] that succeeded, if the imprint on her mind’s eye of a pale, statuesque

[071-069] countenance she had lately gazed upon had not sobered her a little.

[071-070] The fun on the water reached the acme of excitement; there were

[071-071] immersions, there were shouts: the race was lost and won, the pink and

[071-072] blue and yellow ladies retired from the barges, and the people who had

[071-073] watched began to move.

[071-074] “Well—it’s been awfully good,” cried Arabella. “But I think I must get

[071-075] back to my poor man. Father is there, so far as I know; but I had

[071-076] better get back.”

[071-077] “What’s your hurry?”

[071-078] “Well, I must go… Dear, dear, this is awkward!”

[071-079] At the narrow gangway where the people ascended from the riverside path

[071-080] to the bridge the crowd was literally jammed into one hot mass—Arabella

[071-081] and Vilbert with the rest; and here they remained motionless, Arabella

[071-082] exclaiming, “Dear, dear!” more and more impatiently; for it had just

[071-083] occurred to her mind that if Jude were discovered to have died alone an

[071-084] inquest might be deemed necessary.

[071-085] “What a fidget you are, my love,” said the physician, who, being

[071-086] pressed close against her by the throng, had no need of personal effort

[071-087] for contact. “Just as well have patience: there’s no getting away yet!”

[071-088] It was nearly ten minutes before the wedged multitude moved

[071-089] sufficiently to let them pass through. As soon as she got up into the

[071-090] street Arabella hastened on, forbidding the physician to accompany her

[071-091] further that day. She did not go straight to her house; but to the

[071-092] abode of a woman who performed the last necessary offices for the

[071-093] poorer dead; where she knocked.

[071-094] “My husband has just gone, poor soul,” she said. “Can you come and lay

[071-095] him out?”

[071-096] Arabella waited a few minutes; and the two women went along, elbowing

[071-097] their way through the stream of fashionable people pouring out of

[071-098] Cardinal meadow, and being nearly knocked down by the carriages.

[071-099] “I must call at the sexton’s about the bell, too,” said Arabella. “It

[071-100] is just round here, isn’t it? I’ll meet you at my door.”

[071-101] By ten o’clock that night Jude was lying on the bedstead at his lodging

[071-102] covered with a sheet, and straight as an arrow. Through the partly

[071-103] opened window the joyous throb of a waltz entered from the ball-room at

[071-104] Cardinal.

[071-105] Two days later, when the sky was equally cloudless, and the air equally

[071-106] still, two persons stood beside Jude’s open coffin in the same little

[071-107] bedroom. On one side was Arabella, on the other the Widow Edlin. They

[071-108] were both looking at Jude’s face, the worn old eyelids of Mrs. Edlin

[071-109] being red.

[071-110] “How beautiful he is!” said she.

[071-111] “Yes. He’s a ’andsome corpse,” said Arabella.

[071-112] The window was still open to ventilate the room, and it being about

[071-113] noontide the clear air was motionless and quiet without. From a

[071-114] distance came voices; and an apparent noise of persons stamping.

[071-115] “What’s that?” murmured the old woman.

[071-116] “Oh, that’s the Doctors in the theatre, conferring Honorary degrees on

[071-117] the Duke of Hamptonshire and a lot more illustrious gents of that sort.

[071-118] It’s Remembrance Week, you know. The cheers come from the young men.”

[071-119] “Aye; young and strong-lunged! Not like our poor boy here.”

[071-120] An occasional word, as from some one making a speech, floated from the

[071-121] open windows of the theatre across to this quiet corner, at which there

[071-122] seemed to be a smile of some sort upon the marble features of Jude;

[071-123] while the old, superseded, Delphin editions of Virgil and Horace, and

[071-124] the dog-eared Greek Testament on the neighbouring shelf, and the few

[071-125] other volumes of the sort that he had not parted with, roughened with

[071-126] stone-dust where he had been in the habit of catching them up for a few

[071-127] minutes between his labours, seemed to pale to a sickly cast at the

[071-128] sounds. The bells struck out joyously; and their reverberations

[071-129] travelled round the bed-room.

[071-130] Arabella’s eyes removed from Jude to Mrs. Edlin. “D’ye think she will

[071-131] come?” she asked.

[071-132] “I could not say. She swore not to see him again.”

[071-133] “How is she looking?”

[071-134] “Tired and miserable, poor heart. Years and years older than when you

[071-135] saw her last. Quite a staid, worn woman now. ’Tis the man—she can’t

[071-136] stomach un, even now!”

[071-137] “If Jude had been alive to see her, he would hardly have cared for her

[071-138] any more, perhaps.”

[071-139] “That’s what we don’t know… Didn’t he ever ask you to send for her,

[071-140] since he came to see her in that strange way?”

[071-141] “No. Quite the contrary. I offered to send, and he said I was not to

[071-142] let her know how ill he was.”

[071-143] “Did he forgive her?”

[071-144] “Not as I know.”

[071-145] “Well—poor little thing, ’tis to be believed she’s found forgiveness

[071-146] somewhere! She said she had found peace!

[071-147] “She may swear that on her knees to the holy cross upon her necklace

[071-148] till she’s hoarse, but it won’t be true!” said Arabella. “She’s never

[071-149] found peace since she left his arms, and never will again till she’s as

[071-150] he is now!”
