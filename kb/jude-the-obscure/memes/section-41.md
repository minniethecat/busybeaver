[041-001] “Of course I shall do no such thing as examine your luggage! I wish you

[041-002] would take three-quarters of the household furniture. I don’t want to

[041-003] be bothered with it. I have a sort of affection for a little of it that

[041-004] belonged to my poor mother and father. But the rest you are welcome to

[041-005] whenever you like to send for it.”

[041-006] “That I shall never do.”

[041-007] “You go by the six-thirty train, don’t you? It is now a quarter to

[041-008] six.”

[041-009] “You… You don’t seem very sorry I am going, Richard!”

[041-010] “Oh no—perhaps not.”

[041-011] “I like you much for how you have behaved. It is a curious thing that

[041-012] directly I have begun to regard you as not my husband, but as my old

[041-013] teacher, I like you. I won’t be so affected as to say I love you,

[041-014] because you know I don’t, except as a friend. But you do seem that to

[041-015] me!”

[041-016] Sue was for a few moments a little tearful at these reflections, and

[041-017] then the station omnibus came round to take her up. Phillotson saw her

[041-018] things put on the top, handed her in, and was obliged to make an

[041-019] appearance of kissing her as he wished her good-bye, which she quite

[041-020] understood and imitated. From the cheerful manner in which they parted

[041-021] the omnibus-man had no other idea than that she was going for a short

[041-022] visit.

[041-023] When Phillotson got back into the house he went upstairs and opened the

[041-024] window in the direction the omnibus had taken. Soon the noise of its

[041-025] wheels died away. He came down then, his face compressed like that of

[041-026] one bearing pain; he put on his hat and went out, following by the same

[041-027] route for nearly a mile. Suddenly turning round he came home.

[041-028] He had no sooner entered than the voice of his friend Gillingham

[041-029] greeted him from the front room.

[041-030] “I could make nobody hear; so finding your door open I walked in, and

[041-031] made myself comfortable. I said I would call, you remember.”

[041-032] “Yes. I am much obliged to you, Gillingham, particularly for coming

[041-033] to-night.”

[041-034] “How is Mrs.—”

[041-035] “She is quite well. She is gone—just gone. That’s her tea-cup, that she

[041-036] drank out of only an hour ago. And that’s the plate she—” Phillotson’s

[041-037] throat got choked up, and he could not go on. He turned and pushed the

[041-038] tea-things aside.

[041-039] “Have you had any tea, by the by?” he asked presently in a renewed

[041-040] voice.

[041-041] “No—yes—never mind,” said Gillingham, preoccupied. “Gone, you say she

[041-042] is?”

[041-043] “Yes… I would have died for her; but I wouldn’t be cruel to her in the

[041-044] name of the law. She is, as I understand, gone to join her lover. What

[041-045] they are going to do I cannot say. Whatever it may be she has my full

[041-046] consent to.”

[041-047] There was a stability, a ballast, in Phillotson’s pronouncement which

[041-048] restrained his friend’s comment. “Shall I—leave you?” he asked.

[041-049] “No, no. It is a mercy to me that you have come. I have some articles

[041-050] to arrange and clear away. Would you help me?”

[041-051] Gillingham assented; and having gone to the upper rooms the

[041-052] schoolmaster opened drawers, and began taking out all Sue’s things that

[041-053] she had left behind, and laying them in a large box. “She wouldn’t take

[041-054] all I wanted her to,” he continued. “But when I made up my mind to her

[041-055] going to live in her own way I did make up my mind.”

[041-056] “Some men would have stopped at an agreement to separate.”

[041-057] “I’ve gone into all that, and don’t wish to argue it. I was, and am,

[041-058] the most old-fashioned man in the world on the question of marriage—in

[041-059] fact I had never thought critically about its ethics at all. But

[041-060] certain facts stared me in the face, and I couldn’t go against them.”

[041-061] They went on with the packing silently. When it was done Phillotson

[041-062] closed the box and turned the key.

[041-063] “There,” he said. “To adorn her in somebody’s eyes; never again in

[041-064] mine!”

[041-065] V

[041-066] Four-and-twenty hours before this time Sue had written the following

[041-067] note to Jude:

[041-068] It is as I told you; and I am leaving to-morrow evening. Richard and I

[041-069] thought it could be done with less obtrusiveness after dark. I feel

[041-070] rather frightened, and therefore ask you to be sure you are on the

[041-071] Melchester platform to meet me. I arrive at a little to seven. I know

[041-072] you will, of course, dear Jude; but I feel so timid that I can’t help

[041-073] begging you to be punctual. He has been so _very_ kind to me through it

[041-074] all!

[041-075] Now to our meeting!

[041-076] S.

[041-077] As she was carried by the omnibus farther and farther down from the

[041-078] mountain town—the single passenger that evening—she regarded the

[041-079] receding road with a sad face. But no hesitation was apparent therein.

[041-080] The up-train by which she was departing stopped by signal only. To Sue

[041-081] it seemed strange that such a powerful organization as a railway train

[041-082] should be brought to a stand-still on purpose for her—a fugitive from

[041-083] her lawful home.

[041-084] The twenty minutes’ journey drew towards its close, and Sue began

[041-085] gathering her things together to alight. At the moment that the train

[041-086] came to a stand-still by the Melchester platform a hand was laid on the

[041-087] door and she beheld Jude. He entered the compartment promptly. He had a

[041-088] black bag in his hand, and was dressed in the dark suit he wore on

[041-089] Sundays and in the evening after work. Altogether he looked a very

[041-090] handsome young fellow, his ardent affection for her burning in his

[041-091] eyes.

[041-092] “Oh Jude!” She clasped his hand with both hers, and her tense state

[041-093] caused her to simmer over in a little succession of dry sobs. “I—I am

[041-094] so glad! I get out here?”

[041-095] “No. I get in, dear one! I’ve packed. Besides this bag I’ve only a big

[041-096] box which is labelled.”

[041-097] “But don’t I get out? Aren’t we going to stay here?”

[041-098] “We couldn’t possibly, don’t you see. We are known here—I, at any rate,

[041-099] am well known. I’ve booked for Aldbrickham; and here’s your ticket for

[041-100] the same place, as you have only one to here.”

[041-101] “I thought we should have stayed here,” she repeated.

[041-102] “It wouldn’t have done at all.”

[041-103] “Ah! Perhaps not.”

[041-104] “There wasn’t time for me to write and say the place I had decided on.

[041-105] Aldbrickham is a much bigger town—sixty or seventy thousand

[041-106] inhabitants—and nobody knows anything about us there.”

[041-107] “And you have given up your cathedral work here?”

[041-108] “Yes. It was rather sudden—your message coming unexpectedly. Strictly,

[041-109] I might have been made to finish out the week. But I pleaded urgency

[041-110] and I was let off. I would have deserted any day at your command, dear

[041-111] Sue. I have deserted more than that for you!”

[041-112] “I fear I am doing you a lot of harm. Ruining your prospects of the

[041-113] Church; ruining your progress in your trade; everything!”

[041-114] “The Church is no more to me. Let it lie! _I_ am not to be one of

[041-115] The soldier-saints who, row on row,

[041-116] Burn upward each to his point of bliss,

[041-117] if any such there be! My point of bliss is not upward, but here.”

[041-118] “Oh I seem so bad—upsetting men’s courses like this!” said she, taking

[041-119] up in her voice the emotion that had begun in his. But she recovered

[041-120] her equanimity by the time they had travelled a dozen miles.

[041-121] “He has been so good in letting me go,” she resumed. “And here’s a note

[041-122] I found on my dressing-table, addressed to you.”

[041-123] “Yes. He’s not an unworthy fellow,” said Jude, glancing at the note.

[041-124] “And I am ashamed of myself for hating him because he married you.”

[041-125] “According to the rule of women’s whims I suppose I ought to suddenly

[041-126] love him, because he has let me go so generously and unexpectedly,” she

[041-127] answered smiling. “But I am so cold, or devoid of gratitude, or so

[041-128] something, that even this generosity hasn’t made me love him, or

[041-129] repent, or want to stay with him as his wife; although I do feel I like

[041-130] his large-mindedness, and respect him more than ever.”

[041-131] “It may not work so well for us as if he had been less kind, and you

[041-132] had run away against his will,” murmured Jude.

[041-133] “That I _never_ would have done.”

[041-134] Jude’s eyes rested musingly on her face. Then he suddenly kissed her;

[041-135] and was going to kiss her again. “No—only once now—please, Jude!”

[041-136] “That’s rather cruel,” he answered; but acquiesced. “Such a strange

[041-137] thing has happened to me,” Jude continued after a silence. “Arabella

[041-138] has actually written to ask me to get a divorce from her—in kindness to

[041-139] her, she says. She wants to honestly and legally marry that man she has

[041-140] already married virtually; and begs me to enable her to do it.”

[041-141] “What have you done?”

[041-142] “I have agreed. I thought at first I couldn’t do it without getting her

[041-143] into trouble about that second marriage, and I don’t want to injure her

[041-144] in any way. Perhaps she’s no worse than I am, after all! But nobody

[041-145] knows about it over here, and I find it will not be a difficult

[041-146] proceeding at all. If she wants to start afresh I have only too obvious

[041-147] reasons for not hindering her.”

[041-148] “Then you’ll be free?”

[041-149] “Yes, I shall be free.”

[041-150] “Where are we booked for?” she asked, with the discontinuity that

[041-151] marked her to-night.

[041-152] “Aldbrickham, as I said.”

[041-153] “But it will be very late when we get there?”

[041-154] “Yes. I thought of that, and I wired for a room for us at the

[041-155] Temperance Hotel there.”

[041-156] “One?”

[041-157] “Yes—one.”

[041-158] She looked at him. “Oh Jude!” Sue bent her forehead against the corner

[041-159] of the compartment. “I thought you might do it; and that I was

[041-160] deceiving you. But I didn’t mean that!”

[041-161] In the pause which followed, Jude’s eyes fixed themselves with a

[041-162] stultified expression on the opposite seat. “Well!” he said… “Well!”

[041-163] He remained in silence; and seeing how discomfited he was she put her

[041-164] face against his cheek, murmuring, “Don’t be vexed, dear!”

[041-165] “Oh—there’s no harm done,” he said. “But—I understood it like that… Is

[041-166] this a sudden change of mind?”

[041-167] “You have no right to ask me such a question; and I shan’t answer!” she

[041-168] said, smiling.

[041-169] “My dear one, your happiness is more to me than anything—although we

[041-170] seem to verge on quarrelling so often!—and your will is law to me. I am

[041-171] something more than a mere—selfish fellow, I hope. Have it as you

[041-172] wish!” On reflection his brow showed perplexity. “But perhaps it is

[041-173] that you don’t love me—not that you have become conventional! Much as,

[041-174] under your teaching, I hate convention, I hope it _is_ that, not the

[041-175] other terrible alternative!”

[041-176] Even at this obvious moment for candour Sue could not be quite candid

[041-177] as to the state of that mystery, her heart. “Put it down to my

[041-178] timidity,” she said with hurried evasiveness; “to a woman’s natural

[041-179] timidity when the crisis comes. I may feel as well as you that I have a

[041-180] perfect right to live with you as you thought—from this moment. I may

[041-181] hold the opinion that, in a proper state of society, the father of a

[041-182] woman’s child will be as much a private matter of hers as the cut of

[041-183] her underlinen, on whom nobody will have any right to question her. But

[041-184] partly, perhaps, because it is by his generosity that I am now free, I

[041-185] would rather not be other than a little rigid. If there had been a

[041-186] rope-ladder, and he had run after us with pistols, it would have seemed

[041-187] different, and I may have acted otherwise. But don’t press me and

[041-188] criticize me, Jude! Assume that I haven’t the courage of my opinions. I

[041-189] know I am a poor miserable creature. My nature is not so passionate as

[041-190] yours!”
