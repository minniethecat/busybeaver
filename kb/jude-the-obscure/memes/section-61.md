[061-001] “How long has she been going on with this?”

[061-002] “Oh, some few weeks, I think.”

[061-003] Jude went vaguely in the direction of the church, which he had never

[061-004] once approached since he lived out that way years before, when his

[061-005] young opinions were more mystical than they were now. The spot was

[061-006] deserted, but the door was certainly unfastened; he lifted the latch

[061-007] without noise, and pushing to the door behind him, stood absolutely

[061-008] still inside. The prevalent silence seemed to contain a faint sound,

[061-009] explicable as a breathing, or a sobbing, which came from the other end

[061-010] of the building. The floor-cloth deadened his footsteps as he moved in

[061-011] that direction through the obscurity, which was broken only by the

[061-012] faintest reflected night-light from without.

[061-013] High overhead, above the chancel steps, Jude could discern a huge,

[061-014] solidly constructed Latin cross—as large, probably, as the original it

[061-015] was designed to commemorate. It seemed to be suspended in the air by

[061-016] invisible wires; it was set with large jewels, which faintly glimmered

[061-017] in some weak ray caught from outside, as the cross swayed to and fro in

[061-018] a silent and scarcely perceptible motion. Underneath, upon the floor,

[061-019] lay what appeared to be a heap of black clothes, and from this was

[061-020] repeated the sobbing that he had heard before. It was his Sue’s form,

[061-021] prostrate on the paving.

[061-022] “Sue!” he whispered.

[061-023] Something white disclosed itself; she had turned up her face.

[061-024] “What—do you want with me here, Jude?” she said almost sharply. “You

[061-025] shouldn’t come! I wanted to be alone! Why did you intrude here?”

[061-026] “How can you ask!” he retorted in quick reproach, for his full heart

[061-027] was wounded to its centre at this attitude of hers towards him. “Why do

[061-028] I come? Who has a right to come, I should like to know, if I have not!

[061-029] I, who love you better than my own self—better—far better—than you have

[061-030] loved me! What made you leave me to come here alone?”

[061-031] “Don’t criticize me, Jude—I can’t bear it!—I have often told you so.

[061-032] You must take me as I am. I am a wretch—broken by my distractions! I

[061-033] couldn’t _bear_ it when Arabella came—I felt so utterly miserable I had

[061-034] to come away. She seems to be your wife still, and Richard to be my

[061-035] husband!”

[061-036] “But they are nothing to us!”

[061-037] “Yes, dear friend, they are. I see marriage differently now. My babies

[061-038] have been taken from me to show me this! Arabella’s child killing mine

[061-039] was a judgement—the right slaying the wrong. What, __ shall I do! I am

[061-040] such a vile creature—too worthless to mix with ordinary human beings!”

[061-041] “This is terrible!” said Jude, verging on tears. “It is monstrous and

[061-042] unnatural for you to be so remorseful when you have done no wrong!”

[061-043] “Ah—you don’t know my badness!”

[061-044] He returned vehemently: “I do! Every atom and dreg of it! You make me

[061-045] hate Christianity, or mysticism, or Sacerdotalism, or whatever it may

[061-046] be called, if it’s that which has caused this deterioration in you.

[061-047] That a woman-poet, a woman-seer, a woman whose soul shone like a

[061-048] diamond—whom all the wise of the world would have been proud of, if

[061-049] they could have known you—should degrade herself like this! I am glad I

[061-050] had nothing to do with Divinity—damn glad—if it’s going to ruin you in

[061-051] this way!”

[061-052] “You are angry, Jude, and unkind to me, and don’t see how things are.”

[061-053] “Then come along home with me, dearest, and perhaps I shall. I am

[061-054] overburdened—and you, too, are unhinged just now.” He put his arm round

[061-055] her and lifted her; but though she came, she preferred to walk without

[061-056] his support.

[061-057] “I don’t dislike you, Jude,” she said in a sweet and imploring voice.

[061-058] “I love you as much as ever! Only—I ought not to love you—any more. Oh

[061-059] I must not any more!”

[061-060] “I can’t own it.”

[061-061] “But I have made up my mind that I am not your wife! I belong to him—I

[061-062] sacramentally joined myself to him for life. Nothing can alter it!”

[061-063] “But surely we are man and wife, if ever two people were in this world?

[061-064] Nature’s own marriage it is, unquestionably!”

[061-065] “But not Heaven’s. Another was made for me there, and ratified

[061-066] eternally in the church at Melchester.”

[061-067] “Sue, Sue—affliction has brought you to this unreasonable state! After

[061-068] converting me to your views on so many things, to find you suddenly

[061-069] turn to the right-about like this—for no reason whatever, confounding

[061-070] all you have formerly said through sentiment merely! You root out of me

[061-071] what little affection and reverence I had left in me for the Church as

[061-072] an old acquaintance… What I can’t understand in you is your

[061-073] extraordinary blindness now to your old logic. Is it peculiar to you,

[061-074] or is it common to woman? Is a woman a thinking unit at all, or a

[061-075] fraction always wanting its integer? How you argued that marriage was

[061-076] only a clumsy contract—which it is—how you showed all the objections to

[061-077] it—all the absurdities! If two and two made four when we were happy

[061-078] together, surely they make four now? I can’t understand it, I repeat!”

[061-079] “Ah, dear Jude; that’s because you are like a totally deaf man

[061-080] observing people listening to music. You say ‘What are they regarding?

[061-081] Nothing is there.’ But something is.”

[061-082] “That is a hard saying from you; and not a true parallel! You threw off

[061-083] old husks of prejudices, and taught me to do it; and now you go back

[061-084] upon yourself. I confess I am utterly stultified in my estimate of

[061-085] you.”

[061-086] “Dear friend, my only friend, don’t be hard with me! I can’t help being

[061-087] as I am, I am convinced I am right—that I see the light at last. But

[061-088] oh, how to profit by it!”

[061-089] They walked along a few more steps till they were outside the building

[061-090] and she had returned the key. “Can this be the girl,” said Jude when

[061-091] she came back, feeling a slight renewal of elasticity now that he was

[061-092] in the open street; “can this be the girl who brought the pagan deities

[061-093] into this most Christian city?—who mimicked Miss Fontover when she

[061-094] crushed them with her heel?—quoted Gibbon, and Shelley, and Mill? Where

[061-095] are dear Apollo, and dear Venus now!”

[061-096] “Oh don’t, don’t be so cruel to me, Jude, and I so unhappy!” she

[061-097] sobbed. “I can’t bear it! I was in error—I cannot reason with you. I

[061-098] was wrong—proud in my own conceit! Arabella’s coming was the finish.

[061-099] Don’t satirize me: it cuts like a knife!”

[061-100] He flung his arms round her and kissed her passionately there in the

[061-101] silent street, before she could hinder him. They went on till they came

[061-102] to a little coffee-house. “Jude,” she said with suppressed tears,

[061-103] “would you mind getting a lodging here?”

[061-104] “I will—if, if you really wish? But do you? Let me go to our door and

[061-105] understand you.”

[061-106] He went and conducted her in. She said she wanted no supper, and went

[061-107] in the dark upstairs and struck a light. Turning she found that Jude

[061-108] had followed her, and was standing at the chamber door. She went to

[061-109] him, put her hand in his, and said “Good-night.”

[061-110] “But Sue! Don’t we live here?”

[061-111] “You said you would do as I wished!”

[061-112] “Yes. Very well! … Perhaps it was wrong of me to argue distastefully as

[061-113] I have done! Perhaps as we couldn’t conscientiously marry at first in

[061-114] the old-fashioned way, we ought to have parted. Perhaps the world is

[061-115] not illuminated enough for such experiments as ours! Who were we, to

[061-116] think we could act as pioneers!”

[061-117] “I am so glad you see that much, at any rate. I never deliberately

[061-118] meant to do as I did. I slipped into my false position through jealousy

[061-119] and agitation!”

[061-120] “But surely through love—you loved me?”

[061-121] “Yes. But I wanted to let it stop there, and go on always as mere

[061-122] lovers; until—”

[061-123] “But people in love couldn’t live for ever like that!”

[061-124] “Women could: men can’t, because they—won’t. An average woman is in

[061-125] this superior to an average man—that she never instigates, only

[061-126] responds. We ought to have lived in mental communion, and no more.”

[061-127] “I was the unhappy cause of the change, as I have said before! … Well,

[061-128] as you will! … But human nature can’t help being itself.”

[061-129] “Oh yes—that’s just what it has to learn—self-mastery.”

[061-130] “I repeat—if either were to blame it was not you but I.”

[061-131] “No—it was I. Your wickedness was only the natural man’s desire to

[061-132] possess the woman. Mine was not the reciprocal wish till envy

[061-133] stimulated me to oust Arabella. I had thought I ought in charity to let

[061-134] you approach me—that it was damnably selfish to torture you as I did my

[061-135] other friend. But I shouldn’t have given way if you hadn’t broken me

[061-136] down by making me fear you would go back to her… But don’t let us say

[061-137] any more about it! Jude, will you leave me to myself now?”

[061-138] “Yes… But Sue—my wife, as you are!” he burst out; “my old reproach to

[061-139] you was, after all, a true one. You have never loved me as I love

[061-140] you—never—never! Yours is not a passionate heart—your heart does not

[061-141] burn in a flame! You are, upon the whole, a sort of fay, or sprite—not

[061-142] a woman!”

[061-143] “At first I did not love you, Jude; that I own. When I first knew you I

[061-144] merely wanted you to love me. I did not exactly flirt with you; but

[061-145] that inborn craving which undermines some women’s morals almost more

[061-146] than unbridled passion—the craving to attract and captivate, regardless

[061-147] of the injury it may do the man—was in me; and when I found I had

[061-148] caught you, I was frightened. And then—I don’t know how it was—I

[061-149] couldn’t bear to let you go—possibly to Arabella again—and so I got to

[061-150] love you, Jude. But you see, however fondly it ended, it began in the

[061-151] selfish and cruel wish to make your heart ache for me without letting

[061-152] mine ache for you.”

[061-153] “And now you add to your cruelty by leaving me!”

[061-154] “Ah—yes! The further I flounder, the more harm I do!”

[061-155] “O Sue!” said he with a sudden sense of his own danger. “Do not do an

[061-156] immoral thing for moral reasons! You have been my social salvation.

[061-157] Stay with me for humanity’s sake! You know what a weak fellow I am. My

[061-158] two arch-enemies you know—my weakness for womankind and my impulse to

[061-159] strong liquor. Don’t abandon me to them, Sue, to save your own soul

[061-160] only! They have been kept entirely at a distance since you became my

[061-161] guardian-angel! Since I have had you I have been able to go into any

[061-162] temptations of the sort, without risk. Isn’t my safety worth a little

[061-163] sacrifice of dogmatic principle? I am in terror lest, if you leave me,

[061-164] it will be with me another case of the pig that was washed turning back

[061-165] to his wallowing in the mire!”

[061-166] Sue burst out weeping. “Oh, but you must not, Jude! You won’t! I’ll

[061-167] pray for you night and day!”

[061-168] “Well—never mind; don’t grieve,” said Jude generously. “I did suffer,

[061-169] God knows, about you at that time; and now I suffer again. But perhaps

[061-170] not so much as you. The woman mostly gets the worst of it in the long

[061-171] run!”

[061-172] “She does.”

[061-173] “Unless she is absolutely worthless and contemptible. And this one is

[061-174] not that, anyhow!”

[061-175] Sue drew a nervous breath or two. “She is—I fear! … Now

[061-176] Jude—good-night,—please!”

[061-177] “I mustn’t stay?—Not just once more? As it has been so many times—O

[061-178] Sue, my wife, why not?”

[061-179] “No—no—not wife! … I am in your hands, Jude—don’t tempt me back now I

[061-180] have advanced so far!”

[061-181] “Very well. I do your bidding. I owe that to you, darling, in penance

[061-182] for how I overruled it at the first time. My God, how selfish I was!

[061-183] Perhaps—perhaps I spoilt one of the highest and purest loves that ever

[061-184] existed between man and woman! … Then let the veil of our temple be

[061-185] rent in two from this hour!”
