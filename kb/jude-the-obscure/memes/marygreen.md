# Marygreen

Marygreen is the fictional village in [[wessex|Hardy's Wessex]] where the novel begins and ends — the place [[jude-fawley|Jude Fawley]] leaves in pursuit of [[education|learning]] and love, and the place to which he is ultimately pulled back by the inescapable gravity of class and family. It embodies the rural world that the novel's characters both come from and cannot fully transcend.

## Overview

Marygreen is a small, impoverished village in the Berkshire Downs region of [[wessex|Hardy's Wessex]]. It is described as a place of "old-fashioned, simple life" — but the simplicity Hardy depicts is not pastoral nostalgia; it is the grinding limitation of rural poverty. The village contains a school (where [[richard-phillotson|Phillotson]] teaches), a church, a few scattered cottages, and [[jude-fawley|Jude]]'s great-aunt's bakery. Its name — Marygreen — suggests a feminized, pastoral innocence that the novel systematically dismantles.

The village's most significant feature is the well from which [[jude-fawley|Jude]] draws water as a boy — the image that opens the novel: young [[jude-fawley|Jude]] carrying brimming pails past the schoolhouse, already bearing burdens beyond his years.

## In the Novel

### Narrative Significance

Marygreen is the setting of Part First ("At Marygreen") and reappears in Part Sixth when [[jude-fawley|Jude]] returns, dying, to the village of his childhood. The opening chapters establish the village as both nurturing and suffocating — [[jude-fawley|Jude]]'s intellectual curiosity is awakened by [[richard-phillotson|Phillotson]]'s school, but the village offers no path beyond manual labor.

Hardy uses Marygreen to establish the novel's central tension: the gap between rural working-class life and the world of [[education|learning]] represented by [[christminster|Christminster]]. The village is not merely a backdrop but an active force — its traditions, its gossip, its limited horizons shape what [[jude-fawley|Jude]] and his family can become.

## Real-World Basis

| Fictional Name | Real Location | County |
|---------------|---------------|--------|
| Marygreen | Fawley (Berkshire) | Berkshire |

Hardy based Marygreen on the real Berkshire village of Fawley — from which he also took [[jude-fawley|Jude]]'s surname. The conflation of fictional and real geography is characteristic of [[wessex|Hardy's Wessex]]: the emotional truth of place matters more than cartographic accuracy.
