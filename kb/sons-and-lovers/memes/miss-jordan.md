## Introduction

Miss Jordan is a minor character connected with the [[nottingham|Nottingham]] Art School and the winter exhibition at [[Nottingham Castle]]. She represents the institutional world of art [[intellect-and-intellectualism|education]] that offers [[Paul Morel]] a path beyond the mining village.

## In the Novel

Miss Jordan appears in connection with the Art School and the exhibition at [[Nottingham Castle]] (010-003). She visits the [[walter-morel|Morel]] home (006-978), suggesting a personal interest in [[paul-morel|Paul]]'s development beyond mere institutional duty. Her role as an organiser of the Castle exhibition places her at the intersection of [[paul-morel|Paul]]'s artistic aspirations and the social world of [[nottingham|Nottingham]].
