## Introduction

Mr. Leivers is the farmer of [[willey-farm|Willey Farm]] and the father of [[miriam-leivers|Miriam]], Edgar, Geoffrey, Maurice, and [[agatha-leivers|Agatha Leivers]]. He is described as "a good-looking man in the prime of life, with a golden-brown moustache, and blue eyes screwed up against the weather" (006-468). His move to the new farm provides the occasion for [[paul-morel|Paul Morel]]'s first visit, and his warm, vital presence contributes to the atmosphere that draws [[paul-morel|Paul]] repeatedly to [[willey-farm|Willey Farm]].

## Role in the Novel

Mr. Leivers first enters the narrative when he moves his family to a new farm and invites [[gertrude-morel|Mrs. Morel]] to visit (006-294). He appears as a capable, ruddy farmer who swings "the great milk-churns" through the kitchen (007-056), embo[[death|dying]] the physical vitality of rural life. [[paul-morel|Paul]] finds in Mr. Leivers a warmth and energy he admires; he "loved Mr. Leivers, so warm and young and lovable" (009-419).

Unlike the intense, spiritually charged relationships [[paul-morel|Paul]] forms with [[miriam-leivers|Miriam]] and [[mrs-leivers|Mrs. Leivers]], his connection with Mr. Leivers is straightforward and uncomplicated. Mr. Leivers participates in family charades and games, and joins the family in reading *Macbeth* aloud and singing songs "in a circle round the fire" (009-428). He also engages in "bitter debates" (007-581) with the men, revealing an intellectual side beneath the farmer's exterior.

As the novel progresses, Mr. Leivers remains a steady background presence — a symbol of the wholesome, grounded life at [[willey-farm|Willey Farm]] that [[paul-morel|Paul]] finds both alluring and ultimately insufficient for his artistic ambitions.

## Relationships

**[[mrs-leivers|Mrs. Leivers]]** — His wife, with whom he shares a harmonious domestic life. [[paul-morel|Paul]] observes them together in the "warm glow" (008-387) of their home.

**[[miriam-leivers|Miriam Leivers]]** — His daughter. Mr. Leivers is part of the family circle that supports [[miriam-leivers|Miriam]]'s intense interior life, though his relationship with her is less prominent than [[mrs-leivers|Mrs. Leivers]]'s.

**[[paul-morel|Paul Morel]]** — [[paul-morel|Paul]] develops a genuine affection for Mr. Leivers, separate from his complex feelings for [[miriam-leivers|Miriam]]. He enjoys the farmer's vitality and the masculine camaraderie of the farm.

**[[edgar-leivers|Edgar Leivers]]** — His eldest son, who works alongside him on the farm and becomes [[paul-morel|Paul]]'s close friend.
