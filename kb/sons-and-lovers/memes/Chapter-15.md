(015-390) soul could not leave her, wherever she was. Now she was gone abroad

(015-391) into the night, and he was with her still. They were together. But yet

(015-392) there was his body, his chest, that leaned against the stile, his hands

(015-393) on the wooden bar. They seemed something. Where was he?—one tiny

(015-394) upright speck of flesh, less than an ear of wheat lost in the field. He

(015-395) could not bear it. On every side the immense dark silence seemed

(015-396) pressing him, so tiny a spark, into extinction, and yet, almost

(015-397) nothing, he could not be extinct. Night, in which everything was lost,

(015-398) went reaching out, beyond stars and sun. Stars and sun, a few bright

(015-399) grains, went spinning round for terror, and holding each other in

(015-400) embrace, there in a darkness that outpassed them all, and left them

(015-401) tiny and daunted. So much, and himself, infinitesimal, at the core a

(015-402) nothingness, and yet not nothing.

(015-403) “Mother!” he whimpered—“mother!”

(015-404) She was the only thing that held him up, himself, amid all this. And

(015-405) she was gone, intermingled herself. He wanted her to touch him, have

(015-406) him alongside with her.

(015-407) But no, he would not give in. Turning sharply, he walked towards the

(015-408) city’s gold phosphorescence. His fists were shut, his mouth set fast.

(015-409) He would not take that direction, to the darkness, to follow her. He

(015-410) walked towards the faintly humming, glowing town, quickly.
