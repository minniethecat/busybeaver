## Overview

Johann Wolfgang von Goethe (1749–1832) was the preeminent figure of German literature — poet, novelist, playwright, scientist, and statesman. His major works include the two-part drama *Faust*, the novel *The Sorrows of Young Werther*, and the *Wilhelm Meister* novels. In *A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man*, Goethe appears as an emblem of intellectual range: the writer who has written about everything.

## In the Novel

Goethe is mentioned in Chapter V during a conversation among university students. Donovan, defending the breadth of European literary culture, declares: "Goethe and Lessing... have written a great many things about a great many subjects" [005-412]. The remark is part of a wider discussion at the university about literature, nationality, and the scope of the artist's mind.

The name-dropping of Goethe serves a specific function in the scene. Among Stephen's fellow students, continental writers like Goethe, [[guido-cavalcanti-works|Cavalcanti]], and [[gerhart-hauptmann-plays|Hauptmann]] are cultural currency — badges of cosmopolitan sophistication. For Stephen, however, these names are not merely ornaments; they are genuine points of intellectual orientation. Goethe's example — the writer who refuses to be confined to a single genre or a single national tradition — resonates with Stephen's own ambition to forge an artistic identity that transcends the provincial limitations of Dublin.

## Significance

Goethe's place in Stephen's intellectual landscape is bound up with the question of artistic ambition. Goethe was not only a great poet but a scientist (his theory of colour, his work on plant morphology) and a cultural diplomat. For Stephen, who is already feeling the constriction of Irish literary nationalism, Goethe represents the possibility of a European identity — an artist who belongs to the world rather than to a single nation. The casualness with which Donovan invokes Goethe's name only underlines how distant that cosmopolitan ideal remains from the reality of [[university-college-dublin|University College Dublin]].
