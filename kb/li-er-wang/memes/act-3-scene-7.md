[019-001]*Enter Cornwall, Regan, Goneril, Edmund and Servants.*

[019-002]CORNWALL. Post speedily to my lord your husband, show him this letter: the army of France is landed. Seek out the traitor Gloucester.

[019-003]<div style="text-align:right">[Exeunt some of the Servants.]</div>

[019-004]REGAN. Hang him instantly.

[019-005]GONERIL. Pluck out his eyes.

[019-006]CORNWALL. Leave him to my displeasure. Edmund, keep you our sister company: the revenges we are bound to take upon your traitorous father are not fit for your beholding. Advise the Duke where you are going, to a most festinate preparation: we are bound to the like. Our posts shall be swift and intelligent betwixt us. Farewell, dear sister, farewell, my lord of Gloucester.

[019-007]*Enter Oswald.*

[019-008]How now! Where’s the King?

[019-009]OSWALD. My lord of Gloucester hath convey’d him hence: Some five or six and thirty of his knights, Hot questrists after him, met him at gate; Who, with some other of the lord’s dependants, Are gone with him toward Dover: where they boast To have well-armed friends.

[019-010]CORNWALL. Get horses for your mistress.

[019-011]GONERIL. Farewell, sweet lord, and sister.

[019-012]CORNWALL. Edmund, farewell.

[019-013]<div style="text-align:right">[Exeunt Goneril, Edmund and Oswald.]</div>

[019-014]Go seek the traitor Gloucester, Pinion him like a thief, bring him before us.

[019-015]<div style="text-align:right">[Exeunt other Servants.]</div>

[019-016]Though well we may not pass upon his life Without the form of justice, yet our power Shall do a courtesy to our wrath, which men May blame, but not control. Who’s there? The traitor?

[019-017]*Enter Gloucester and Servants.*

[019-018]REGAN. Ingrateful fox! ’tis he.

[019-019]CORNWALL. Bind fast his corky arms.

[019-020]GLOUCESTER. What mean your graces? Good my friends, consider you are my guests. Do me no foul play, friends.

[019-021]CORNWALL. Bind him, I say.

[019-022]<div style="text-align:right">[Servants bind him.]</div>

[019-023]REGAN. Hard, hard. O filthy traitor!

[019-024]GLOUCESTER. Unmerciful lady as you are, I’m none.

[019-025]CORNWALL. To this chair bind him. Villain, thou shalt find—

[019-026]<div style="text-align:right">[Regan plucks his beard.]</div>

[019-027]GLOUCESTER. By the kind gods, ’tis most ignobly done To pluck me by the beard.

[019-028]REGAN. So white, and such a traitor!

[019-029]GLOUCESTER. Naughty lady, These hairs which thou dost ravish from my chin Will quicken, and accuse thee. I am your host: With robber’s hands my hospitable favours You should not ruffle thus. What will you do?

[019-030]CORNWALL. Come, sir, what letters had you late from France?

[019-031]REGAN. Be simple answer’d, for we know the truth.

[019-032]CORNWALL. And what confederacy have you with the traitors, Late footed in the kingdom?

[019-033]REGAN. To whose hands have you sent the lunatic King? Speak.

[019-034]GLOUCESTER. I have a letter guessingly set down, Which came from one that’s of a neutral heart, And not from one oppos’d.

[019-035]CORNWALL. Cunning.

[019-036]REGAN. And false.

[019-037]CORNWALL. Where hast thou sent the King?

[019-038]GLOUCESTER. To Dover.

[019-039]REGAN. Wherefore to Dover? Wast thou not charg’d at peril,—

[019-040]CORNWALL. Wherefore to Dover? Let him first answer that.

[019-041]GLOUCESTER. I am tied to the stake, and I must stand the course.

[019-042]REGAN. Wherefore to Dover, sir?

[019-043]GLOUCESTER. Because I would not see thy cruel nails Pluck out his poor old eyes; nor thy fierce sister In his anointed flesh stick boarish fangs. The sea, with such a storm as his bare head In hell-black night endur’d, would have buoy’d up, And quench’d the stelled fires; Yet, poor old heart, he holp the heavens to rain. If wolves had at thy gate howl’d that stern time, Thou shouldst have said, ‘Good porter, turn the key.’ All cruels else subscrib’d: but I shall see The winged vengeance overtake such children.

[019-044]CORNWALL. See’t shalt thou never. Fellows, hold the chair. Upon these eyes of thine I’ll set my foot.

[019-045]<div style="text-align:right">[Gloucester is held down in his chair, while Cornwall plucks out one of his eyes and sets his foot on it.]</div>

[019-046]GLOUCESTER. He that will think to live till he be old, Give me some help!—O cruel! O you gods!

[019-047]REGAN. One side will mock another; the other too!

[019-048]CORNWALL. If you see vengeance—

[019-049]FIRST SERVANT. Hold your hand, my lord: I have serv’d you ever since I was a child; But better service have I never done you Than now to bid you hold.

[019-050]REGAN. How now, you dog!

[019-051]FIRST SERVANT. If you did wear a beard upon your chin, I’d shake it on this quarrel. What do you mean?

[019-052]CORNWALL. My villain?

[019-053]<div style="text-align:right">[Draws, and runs at him.]</div>

[019-054]FIRST SERVANT. Nay, then, come on, and take the chance of anger.

[019-055]<div style="text-align:right">[Draws. They fight. Cornwall is wounded.]</div>

[019-056]REGAN. [To another servant.] Give me thy sword. A peasant stand up thus?

[019-057]<div style="text-align:right">[Snatches a sword, comes behind, and stabs him.]</div>

[019-058]FIRST SERVANT. O, I am slain! My lord, you have one eye left To see some mischief on him. O!

[019-059]<div style="text-align:right">[Dies.]</div>

[019-060]CORNWALL. Lest it see more, prevent it. Out, vile jelly! Where is thy lustre now?

[019-061]<div style="text-align:right">[Tears out Gloucester’s other eye and throws it on the ground.]</div>

[019-062]GLOUCESTER. All dark and comfortless. Where’s my son Edmund? Edmund, enkindle all the sparks of nature To quit this horrid act.

[019-063]REGAN. Out, treacherous villain! Thou call’st on him that hates thee: it was he That made the overture of thy treasons to us; Who is too good to pity thee.

[019-064]GLOUCESTER. O my follies! Then Edgar was abus’d. Kind gods, forgive me that, and prosper him!

[019-065]REGAN. Go thrust him out at gates, and let him smell His way to Dover. How is’t, my lord? How look you?

[019-066]CORNWALL. I have receiv’d a hurt: follow me, lady. Turn out that eyeless villain. Throw this slave Upon the dunghill. Regan, I bleed apace: Untimely comes this hurt: give me your arm.

[019-067]<div style="text-align:right">[Exit Cornwall, led by Regan; Servants unbind Gloucester and lead him out.]</div>

[019-068]SECOND SERVANT. I’ll never care what wickedness I do, If this man come to good.

[019-069]THIRD SERVANT. If she live long, And in the end meet the old course of death, Women will all turn monsters.

[019-070]SECOND SERVANT. Let’s follow the old Earl, and get the bedlam To lead him where he would: his roguish madness Allows itself to anything.

[019-071]THIRD SERVANT. Go thou: I’ll fetch some flax and whites of eggs To apply to his bleeding face. Now heaven help him!

[019-072]<div style="text-align:right">[Exeunt.]</div>
