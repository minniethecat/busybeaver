[006-001]*Enter Lear, Kent and Fool.*

[006-002]LEAR. Go you before to Gloucester with these letters: acquaint my daughter no further with anything you know than comes from her demand out of the letter. If your diligence be not speedy, I shall be there afore you.

[006-003]KENT. I will not sleep, my lord, till I have delivered your letter.

[006-004]<div style="text-align:right">[Exit.]</div>

[006-005]FOOL. If a man’s brains were in’s heels, were’t not in danger of kibes?

[006-006]LEAR. Ay, boy.

[006-007]FOOL. Then I prythee be merry; thy wit shall not go slipshod.

[006-008]LEAR. Ha, ha, ha!

[006-009]FOOL. Shalt see thy other daughter will use thee kindly, for though she’s as like this as a crab’s like an apple, yet I can tell what I can tell.

[006-010]LEAR. What canst tell, boy?

[006-011]FOOL. She’ll taste as like this as a crab does to a crab. Thou canst tell why one’s nose stands i’the middle on’s face?

[006-012]LEAR. No.

[006-013]FOOL. Why, to keep one’s eyes of either side’s nose, that what a man cannot smell out, he may spy into.

[006-014]LEAR. I did her wrong.

[006-015]FOOL. Canst tell how an oyster makes his shell?

[006-016]LEAR. No.

[006-017]FOOL. Nor I neither; but I can tell why a snail has a house.

[006-018]LEAR. Why?

[006-019]FOOL. Why, to put’s head in; not to give it away to his daughters, and leave his horns without a case.

[006-020]LEAR. I will forget my nature. So kind a father! Be my horses ready?

[006-021]FOOL. Thy asses are gone about ’em. The reason why the seven stars are no more than seven is a pretty reason.

[006-022]LEAR. Because they are not eight?

[006-023]FOOL. Yes indeed: thou wouldst make a good fool.

[006-024]LEAR. To tak’t again perforce!—Monster ingratitude!

[006-025]FOOL. If thou wert my fool, nuncle, I’d have thee beaten for being old before thy time.

[006-026]LEAR. How’s that?

[006-027]FOOL. Thou shouldst not have been old till thou hadst been wise.

[006-028]LEAR. O, let me not be mad, not mad, sweet heaven! Keep me in temper; I would not be mad!

[006-029]*Enter Gentleman.*

[006-030]How now? are the horses ready?

[006-031]GENTLEMAN. Ready, my lord.

[006-032]LEAR. Come, boy.

[006-033]FOOL. She that’s a maid now, and laughs at my departure, Shall not be a maid long, unless things be cut shorter.

[006-034]<div style="text-align:right">[Exeunt.]</div>
