[008-001]*Enter Edmund and Curan, meeting.*

[008-002]EDMUND. Save thee, Curan.

[008-003]CURAN. And you, sir. I have been with your father, and given him notice that the Duke of Cornwall and Regan his Duchess will be here with him this night.

[008-004]EDMUND. How comes that?

[008-005]CURAN. Nay, I know not. You have heard of the news abroad; I mean the whispered ones, for they are yet but ear-kissing arguments?

[008-006]EDMUND. Not I: pray you, what are they?

[008-007]CURAN. Have you heard of no likely wars toward, ’twixt the two dukes of Cornwall and Albany?

[008-008]EDMUND. Not a word.

[008-009]CURAN. You may do, then, in time. Fare you well, sir.

[008-010]<div style="text-align:right">[Exit.]</div>

[008-011]EDMUND. The Duke be here tonight? The better! best! This weaves itself perforce into my business. My father hath set guard to take my brother; And I have one thing, of a queasy question, Which I must act. Briefness and fortune work! Brother, a word, descend, brother, I say!

[008-012]*Enter Edgar.*

[008-013]My father watches: O sir, fly this place; Intelligence is given where you are hid; You have now the good advantage of the night. Have you not spoken ’gainst the Duke of Cornwall? He’s coming hither; now, i’ the night, i’ the haste, And Regan with him: have you nothing said Upon his party ’gainst the Duke of Albany? Advise yourself.

[008-014]EDGAR. I am sure on’t, not a word.

[008-015]EDMUND. I hear my father coming:—pardon me; In cunning I must draw my sword upon you: Draw: seem to defend yourself: now quit you well. Yield: come before my father. Light, ho, here! Fly, brother. Torches, torches!—So farewell.

[008-016]<div style="text-align:right">[Exit Edgar.]</div>

[008-017]Some blood drawn on me would beget opinion Of my more fierce endeavour: [Wounds his arm.] I have seen drunkards Do more than this in sport. Father, father! Stop, stop! No help?

[008-018]*Enter Gloucester and Servants with torches.*

[008-019]GLOUCESTER. Now, Edmund, where’s the villain?

[008-020]EDMUND. Here stood he in the dark, his sharp sword out, Mumbling of wicked charms, conjuring the moon To stand auspicious mistress.

[008-021]GLOUCESTER. But where is he?

[008-022]EDMUND. Look, sir, I bleed.

[008-023]GLOUCESTER. Where is the villain, Edmund?

[008-024]EDMUND. Fled this way, sir. When by no means he could,—

[008-025]GLOUCESTER. Pursue him, ho! Go after.

[008-026]<div style="text-align:right">[Exeunt Servants.]</div>

[008-027]—By no means what?

[008-028]EDMUND. Persuade me to the murder of your lordship; But that I told him the revenging gods ’Gainst parricides did all their thunders bend; Spoke with how manifold and strong a bond The child was bound to the father; sir, in fine, Seeing how loathly opposite I stood To his unnatural purpose, in fell motion With his prepared sword, he charges home My unprovided body, latch’d mine arm; But when he saw my best alarum’d spirits, Bold in the quarrel’s right, rous’d to th’encounter, Or whether gasted by the noise I made, Full suddenly he fled.

[008-029]GLOUCESTER. Let him fly far; Not in this land shall he remain uncaught; And found—dispatch’d. The noble Duke my master, My worthy arch and patron, comes tonight: By his authority I will proclaim it, That he which finds him shall deserve our thanks, Bringing the murderous coward to the stake; He that conceals him, death.

[008-030]EDMUND. When I dissuaded him from his intent, And found him pight to do it, with curst speech I threaten’d to discover him: he replied, ‘Thou unpossessing bastard! dost thou think, If I would stand against thee, would the reposal Of any trust, virtue, or worth in thee Make thy words faith’d? No: what I should deny As this I would; ay, though thou didst produce My very character, I’d turn it all To thy suggestion, plot, and damned practice: And thou must make a dullard of the world, If they not thought the profits of my death Were very pregnant and potential spurs To make thee seek it.

[008-031]GLOUCESTER. O strange and fast’ned villain! Would he deny his letter, said he? I never got him.

[008-032]<div style="text-align:right">[Tucket within.]</div>

[008-033]Hark, the Duke’s trumpets! I know not why he comes. All ports I’ll bar; the villain shall not scape; The Duke must grant me that: besides, his picture I will send far and near, that all the kingdom May have due note of him; and of my land, Loyal and natural boy, I’ll work the means To make thee capable.

[008-034]*Enter Cornwall, Regan and Attendants.*

[008-035]CORNWALL. How now, my noble friend! since I came hither, Which I can call but now, I have heard strange news.

[008-036]REGAN. If it be true, all vengeance comes too short Which can pursue th’offender. How dost, my lord?

[008-037]GLOUCESTER. O madam, my old heart is crack’d, it’s crack’d!

[008-038]REGAN. What, did my father’s godson seek your life? He whom my father nam’d? your Edgar?

[008-039]GLOUCESTER. O lady, lady, shame would have it hid!

[008-040]REGAN. Was he not companion with the riotous knights That tend upon my father?

[008-041]GLOUCESTER. I know not, madam; ’tis too bad, too bad.

[008-042]EDMUND. Yes, madam, he was of that consort.

[008-043]REGAN. No marvel then though he were ill affected: ’Tis they have put him on the old man’s death, To have the expense and waste of his revenues. I have this present evening from my sister Been well inform’d of them; and with such cautions That if they come to sojourn at my house, I’ll not be there.

[008-044]CORNWALL. Nor I, assure thee, Regan. Edmund, I hear that you have shown your father A childlike office.

[008-045]EDMUND. It was my duty, sir.

[008-046]GLOUCESTER. He did bewray his practice; and receiv’d This hurt you see, striving to apprehend him.

[008-047]CORNWALL. Is he pursued?

[008-048]GLOUCESTER. Ay, my good lord.

[008-049]CORNWALL. If he be taken, he shall never more Be fear’d of doing harm: make your own purpose, How in my strength you please. For you, Edmund, Whose virtue and obedience doth this instant So much commend itself, you shall be ours: Natures of such deep trust we shall much need; You we first seize on.

[008-050]EDMUND. I shall serve you, sir, truly, however else.

[008-051]GLOUCESTER. For him I thank your grace.

[008-052]CORNWALL. You know not why we came to visit you?

[008-053]REGAN. Thus out of season, threading dark-ey’d night: Occasions, noble Gloucester, of some poise, Wherein we must have use of your advice. Our father he hath writ, so hath our sister, Of differences, which I best thought it fit To answer from our home; the several messengers From hence attend dispatch. Our good old friend, Lay comforts to your bosom; and bestow Your needful counsel to our business, Which craves the instant use.

[008-054]GLOUCESTER. I serve you, madam: Your graces are right welcome.

[008-055]<div style="text-align:right">[Exeunt. Flourish.]</div>
