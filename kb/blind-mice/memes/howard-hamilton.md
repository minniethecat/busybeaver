# Howard Hamilton

## Overview

Dr. Howard Hamilton is a [[rosedene|Rosedene]] physician whose medical authority is invoked at key moments in the narrative. His role is primarily functional — he represents the official, clinical perspective on events that the novel treats with greater emotional complexity. His prescription of a "rest cure" for [[lucy-winter|Lucy]] (027-038) participates in a long literary tradition of medical intervention in [[gender-roles|women]]'s lives.

## Role in the Novel

Dr. Hamilton appears when the [[the-winter-house|Winter household]]'s emotional turbulence manifests as physical symptoms. His diagnosis and treatment recommendations carry the weight of medical authority, but the novel subtly questions whether that authority is adequate to the domestic and psychological complexities at hand. He is a reminder that [[rosedene|Rosedene]], for all its suburban [[friendship|intimacy]], is connected to the institutional structures — medical, legal, religious — of the wider world.
