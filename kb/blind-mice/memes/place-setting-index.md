# Place & Setting Index

The world of *Blind Mice* moves between two poles: the small-town intimacy of Russellville, where the Winter family home anchors the domestic drama, and the cosmopolitan ambition of New York, where [[james-sprague]] builds his architectural career. Between them, intermediate spaces — the Sheldon house, Chicago, various social venues — mark the characters' social mobility and emotional transitions.

[[rosedene]], the Merwent family estate, serves as both physical setting and symbolic refuge throughout the novel.
