# Katy Lockhart

## Overview

Katy Lockhart (referred to as "[[cousin-minnie|Cousin Minnie]]" by [[nannie-merwent|Nannie]]) represents an alternative model of Lockh[[art-and-aesthetics|art]] womanhood — one defined by practical competence rather than aristocratic pretension. Her presence in the novel, though limited, serves as a pointed contrast to [[nannie-merwent|Nannie]]'s theatrical self-presentation.

## Role in the Novel

The Lockh[[art-and-aesthetics|art]] residence passes into Katy's hands after the family's financial collapse — a fact that [[nannie-merwent|Nannie]] "cordially hated" (005-011). Where [[nannie-merwent|Nannie]] sees the loss of the family home as a catastrophe of status, Katy simply inhabits it, suggesting a [[illness-and-medicine|health]]ier relationship to family herit[[youth-and-aging|age]]. She appears primarily in discussions of family history and property, functioning as a narrative reminder that the Lockh[[art-and-aesthetics|art]] legacy could have taken a different form.
