## Overview

[[lucy-winter|Lucy Winter]] and her mother, [[mrs-lockhart|Mrs. Lockhart]], engage in a tense dispute over Mrs. Lockhart's insistence on throwing a welcome-home party for [[nannie-merwent|Nannie]] and [[dimmie-winter|Dimmie]]. The argument reveals the fundamental clash between Lucy's desire for autonomy and Mrs. Lockhart's compulsion to control her daughter's household and social life.

## Narrative Context

Mrs. Lockhart arrives at Rosedene for a visit that immediately becomes an exercise in assertion of maternal authority. She declares her intention to throw a party:

> "We'll have to give a little party for my grandbaby." [006-053]

Lucy resists, recognizing that the party is less about welcoming Dimmie and Nannie than about Mrs. Lockhart's need to stage-manage her family's social presentation. The dispute escalates through several exchanges, with Mrs. Lockhart repeatedly dismissing Lucy's objections.

## Key Participants

- **[[lucy-winter|Lucy Winter]]** — Stands firm against her mother's domineering plans, asserting control over her own household
- **[[mrs-lockhart|Mrs. Lockhart]]** — Insists on the party, treating Lucy's objections as ingratitude
- **[[nannie-merwent|Nannie Merwent]]** — The nominal guest of honor, caught in the crossfire of Lockhart family dynamics
- **[[dimmie-winter|Dimmie Winter]]** — Mrs. Lockhart's grandson and the stated reason for the party

## Aftermath

> "Thank you, Grandmamma, but I don't want the party," reiterated Lucy. [006-100]
>
> "I don't want to seem ungrateful, Grandmamma, but I'm not coming to any party." [006-102]

Lucy's firm refusal marks a significant assertion of independence. Mrs. Lockhart retreats from the specific demand but the underlying power struggle remains unresolved. The episode foreshadows later confrontations and contributes to the pattern of tension that defines the Lockhart-Winter family dynamic across the novel.
