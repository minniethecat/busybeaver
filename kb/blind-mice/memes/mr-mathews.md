## Overview

Mr. Mathews is a [[food-and-dining|dinner]] guest at the [[the-winter-house|Winter household]] who appears briefly in two chapters. [[john-winter]] [[gender-roles|men]]tions him in party planning with a note of stiffness, and he is later seen in conversation with [[lucy-winter]] during the [[food-and-dining|dinner]] itself. His presence at the Winter table connects the domestic sphere of the novel to the world of [[john-winter]]'s [[business-and-commerce|business]] and professional relationships.

## Biography

Mr. Mathews enters the narrative as one of the guests [[john-winter|John Winter]] plans to invite to a [[food-and-dining|dinner]] party. [[john-winter|John]] [[gender-roles|men]]tions his name with a notable edge: "'Mr. Mathews is coming, too,' stated [[john-winter|John]] stiffly, 'so he won't be able to work Wednesday night'" [034-064]. The stiffness of [[john-winter|John]]'s statement suggests some tension — perhaps Mathews is a [[business-and-commerce|business]] rival, or perhaps [[john-winter|John]] resents the social [[duty-and-obligation|obligation]] of including him.

At the [[food-and-dining|dinner]] itself, Mr. Mathews is seen in conversation with [[lucy-winter|Lucy]]. The narrator notes that "[[lucy-winter|Lucy]], who was [[gossip-and-reputation|talk]]ing with Mr. Mathews quite near, overheard snatches of this conversation, and from time to time her glance sought [[john-winter|John]] and his companion" [035-038]. [[lucy-winter|Lucy]] is distracted during her conversation with Mathews — her attention drifting to her husband — and when [[miss-powell|Miss Powell]] makes a remark, [[lucy-winter|Lucy]] responds "somewhat waspishly" before turning "again to Mr. Mathews" [035-040]. The implication is that Mathews is a polite but unexciting conversational partner, someone [[lucy-winter|Lucy]] [[gossip-and-reputation|talk]]s to while her real attention is elsewhere.

## Character Analysis

Mr. Mathews is one of several background figures who populate the [[the-winter-house|Winter household]]'s social events, serving as markers of the professional network that extends beyond the domestic sphere. [[john-winter|John]]'s stiff [[gender-roles|men]]tion of him — "Mr. Mathews is coming, too" — hints at the social calculus behind [[food-and-dining|dinner]]-party invitations: some guests are wanted, others are [[duty-and-obligation|obligation]]s.

[[lucy-winter|Lucy]]'s interaction with Mathews at the [[food-and-dining|dinner]] is telling. She [[gossip-and-reputation|talk]]s with him, but her attention wanders to [[john-winter|John]] and his companion. When she responds "waspishly" to [[miss-powell|Miss Powell]] before turning back to Mathews [035-040], the impression is of a woman going through the social motions while her mind is elsewhere. Mathews, in this reading, is the decent but unremarkable [[food-and-dining|dinner]] guest — the kind of man one [[gossip-and-reputation|talk]]s to because one must, not because one wants to.

His function in the novel is thus doubly representative: he stands for the network of [[business-and-commerce|business]] acquaintances that [[john-winter|John]] must cultivate, and for the emotional distance between [[lucy-winter|Lucy]] and the social world she inhabits.
