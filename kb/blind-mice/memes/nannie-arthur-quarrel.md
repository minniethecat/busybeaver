## Overview

During the preparations for their wedding, [[nannie-merwent|Nannie Lockhart]] and [[arthur-merwent|Arthur Merwent]] quarrel over the elaborate trousseau being assembled under [[mrs-lockhart|Mrs. Lockhart]]'s direction. The argument is one of several pre-wedding conflicts that reveal the fundamental mismatch between the couple.

## Narrative Context

The wedding preparations at the Lockhart home are dominated by Mrs. Lockhart's grandiose vision for the trousseau. Nannie, caught between her mother's expectations and Arthur's quiet disapproval, finds herself in an impossible position:

> On the work on the extensive trousseau, Nannie and Arthur quarrelled [004-027]

Arthur, "agreeable but uncommunicative" [004-020], responds to Nannie's distress with reserve rather than reassurance. Nannie, for her part, "assumed a light and jesting attitude" [004-022] toward the engagement, masking her anxiety with humor that Arthur does not share or understand.

## Key Participants

- **[[nannie-merwent|Nannie Lockhart Merwent]]** — The bride-to-be, torn between family expectations and her fiancé's disapproval
- **[[arthur-merwent|Arthur Merwent]]** — The groom, quietly resistant to the Lockhart family's social ambitions
- **[[mrs-lockhart|Mrs. Lockhart]]** — Orchestrating the trousseau and wedding as a display of Lockhart prestige

## Aftermath

The quarrel pattern recurred — "on each occasion she returned his ring" [004-027] — establishing a cycle of conflict and reconciliation that characterized their engagement. The trousseau episode foreshadows the deeper fractures that will lead to [[the-divorce-scandal|the divorce scandal]]. Mrs. Lockhart "shed a few impressive tears" [004-045] at the wedding, her emotional display masking the family's knowledge of the couple's incompatibility.

> "It's not too late yet, Nannie," Arthur answered. His voice was grave. [004-032]
