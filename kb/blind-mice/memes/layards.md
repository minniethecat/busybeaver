## Overview

Layard's is a prominent building supply firm in the city, described as "close to all the supply companies" [[001-039]]. The firm functions as a key business entity in the architectural profession storyline, serving as both a supplier for [[winter-sprague-office|Winter & Sprague]]'s construction projects and later as an employer of [[jim-sprague|James Sprague]].

## Role in the Novel

Layard's plays a supporting but structurally significant role in the professional subplot of the novel. It is first introduced when [[jim-sprague|Jim Sprague]] suggests to [[john-winter|John Winter]] and [[lucy-winter|Lucy Winter]] that Winter & Sprague should collaborate with larger firms like Layard's to expand their business scope [[001-039]]. [[lucy-winter|Lucy]] immediately grasps the implication, asking if he means "going into partnership with Layard's" [[001-044]].

The firm appears regularly throughout the narrative as a point of reference for professional activities. [[john-winter|John]] visits Layard's to get prices on furnishing lumber [[013-056]], and later goes there for material specifications for Wilson's housing development [[033-067]]. The firm's offices are located in a tall building visible from the Winter & Sprague office window [[036-022]].

In a pivotal career development, Jim eventually accepts a position as a draughtsman with Layard's, describing it as "a big building supply firm" and noting that "life is serious now" [[013-032]]. Later in the novel, Layard's offers to purchase Jim's share of the Winter & Sprague business, and Mathews, Layard's head bookkeeper, partners with John to buy Jim out [[036-015]], [[036-018]].
