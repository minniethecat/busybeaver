# Theme & Concept Index

*Blind Mice* engages with a range of early twentieth-century social concerns — class distinction, gender expectations, marriage as economic arrangement, and the tension between individual desire and community judgment. This index organizes all concept pages alphabetically, with thematic groupings below for readers interested in specific dimensions of the novel.

The novel's treatment of [[money-and-class]] and [[gender-roles]] runs through nearly every chapter, while motifs such as [[blindness-metaphor]] and [[rosedene-as-refuge]] provide structural coherence to Scott's narrative.
