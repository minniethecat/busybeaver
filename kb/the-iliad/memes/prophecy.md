# Prophecy and Omen

In the Homeric world, the gods constantly send signs — an eagle carrying a fawn, a flash of lightning, a dream-vision in the night. The seer (*mantis*) is the specialist who interprets these signs, translating divine communication into human understanding. Prophecy thus mediates between the cosmic order visible to the gods and the limited perspective of mortals who must act without certainty.

## Definition

Prophecy in Homer encompasses several distinct practices: (1) *bird-augury* — the interpretation of the flight of birds, especially eagles, as omens from Zeus; (2) *oneiromancy* — the interpretation of dreams sent by the gods; (3) *inspired prophecy* — the seer speaks words given by a god, often Apollo; and (4) *sacrificial divination* — reading signs in the entrails of sacrificial [[sacrifice|victim]]s.

## Manifestations in the Epic

Calchas, the Achaean seer, delivers the epic's first prophecy: the plague will not lift until Chryseis is returned to her father. His authority is established by the formulaic decl[[curse|ara]]tion that he "knew things past, present, and to come" — a knowledge granted by Apollo. Yet Calchas also demonstrates the seer's vulnerability: he fears [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]'s [[wrath|anger]] and only speaks when [[achilles|Achilles]] gu[[curse|ara]]ntees his safety.

Bird-omens punctuate the epic's turning points. When the [[trojans|Trojan army]] reaches the Achaean [[achaean-camp|ships]], an eagle carrying a snake appears; the snake bites the eagle, which drops it among the troops. Polydamas interprets this as a warning: the [[trojans|Trojans]] will reach the [[achaean-camp|ships]] but fail to destroy them. [[hector|Hector]], in his overconfidence, rejects the omen — a rejection that proves fatal.

## Greek Terminology

**μάντις (mantis)** — the seer or prophet. **οἰωνός (oiōnos)** — the bird-omen, the most common form of divination. **τέρας (teras)** — a portent or sign, often a natural phenomenon interpreted as divine communication. **ὄνειρος ([[dream|oneiros]])** — the dream, often sent by the gods as a message.
