# Phthia

Phthia is the homeland of Achilles and the Myrmidons, ruled by King Peleus in Thessaly (northern Greece).

## Geography

Phthia is described as "mother of sheep" (009-392), a fertile pastoral region in southern Thessaly. It lies inland from the Gulf of Malis, bordered by the lands of the Hellenes and the Dolopians.

## Role in the Iliad

Phthia is never visited directly in the Iliad but is constantly invoked as the distant home to which Achilles yearns to return. The Myrmidon contingent at Troy — the fiercest of all the Achaean forces — is drawn from Phthia and its surrounding territories. Achilles' repeated threats to abandon Troy and sail back to Phthia are a measure of his alienation from the Achaean cause.

## Notable Passages

> I came to Phthia, mother of sheep, and to King Peleus, who made me welcome. — Phoenix (009-392)
