# Paeonians

The Paeonians (Παίονες) are a contingent of archers and horsemen in the Trojan alliance, led by Pyraechmes from "distant Amydon by the broad-flowing Axius." They appear in the Trojan Catalogue, in several battle books, and are notable for their archery skills (002-693).

## Identity

The Paeonians are introduced as a distinct troop type in the Trojan Catalogue: "Pyraechmes led the Paeonian archers from distant Amydon, by the broad-flowing Axius" (002-693). Their homeland of Paeonia, located in the region of the Axius River (modern Vardar) in the northern Balkans, marks them as one of the more distant allied contingents, reflecting the geographical breadth of the Trojan alliance.

They are enumerated among the "Paeonian bowmen" alongside Carians, Leleges, Cauconians, and the "noble Pelasgi" in Book 10's roll-call of allied forces (010-353), and are later named among the Trojan allies in Book 21 as Achilles pursues the routed enemy forces toward the Scamander (021-136).

## Leadership

Pyraechmes is the named commander of the Paeonian forces. He is killed in Book 16 by Patroclus during his aristeia: "it was Pyraechmes who had led his Paeonian horsemen" — his death marks the turning point of the Paeonian contingent's role in the battle (016-258). After Pyraechmes's fall, the Paeonians are mentioned again in Book 21, where the Trojan Asteropaeus, "captain of the Paeonians," becomes their de facto leader (021-136). The fate of the Paeonians after Asteropaeus's own death at Achilles's hands in Book 21 is not recounted in the *Iliad*.

## Role in the War

- **Book 2**: The Paeonians appear in the Trojan Catalogue under Pyraechmes, described as "archers from distant Amydon" (002-693).
- **Book 10**: Listed among the full roster of Trojan allies as "the Paeonian bowmen" (010-353).
- **Book 16**: Pyraechmes is killed by Patroclus — "it was Pyraechmes who had led his Paeonian horsemen" — a significant combat event that demonstrates Patroclus's battlefield prowess and the cost to the Trojan allies (016-258).
- **Book 21**: Asteropaeus, described as from "far Paeonia, captain of the Paeonians," engages Achilles in single combat and is killed. "Then he went in chase of the Paeonians, who were flying" describes Achilles's subsequent rout of the Paeonian forces (021-136, 021-179).

## Warfare Style

The Paeonians are characterized by two distinct combat roles. In the Catalogue and Book 10, they are described as archers (bowmen), reflecting the northern Balkan tradition of mounted archery. In Book 16, Pyraechmes leads "Paeonian horsemen," suggesting a combined cavalry and archery force. This dual capability — mounted troops who could fight both at range and in close combat — made the Paeonians a versatile component of the Trojan alliance.

## Notable Members

- Pyraechmes — leader of the Paeonians, killed by Patroclus in Book 16
- Asteropaeus — son of Pelagon, "captain of the Paeonians" in Book 21, killed by Achilles
