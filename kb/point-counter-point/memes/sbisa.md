# Sbisa

Sbisa, the Soho restaurant, serves as the novel's most democratic space — a place where characters from different social worlds meet over meals, and where intellectual exchange flourishes outside the constraints of drawing-room etiquette.

## Location and Character

Located in Soho, London's cosmopolitan quarter, Sbisa represents the city's capacity for encounter across class and national lines. Its informality contrasts with the ritualized gatherings at Lady Edward's and the staged intimacy of Burlap's salon.

## Narrative Significance

Restaurant scenes at Sbisa function as the novel's intellectual marketplace — a space where ideas are exchanged, debated, and tested against the grain of different sensibilities.
