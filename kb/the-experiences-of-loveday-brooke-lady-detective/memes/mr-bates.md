## Overview

Jeremiah Bates is a [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] detective assigned to investigate the [[robbery|jewel robbery]] at [[craigen-court|Craigen Court]], the country seat of [[sir-george-cathrow|Sir George Cathrow]]. He operates from the "King's Head" inn in the village, collecting information externally while [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] works [[disguise|undercover]] inside the household.

## Appearance

Bates is described as "a small, wiry man, with red hair and whiskers, and an eager, hungry expression of countenance" [001-052]. He presents himself in the village "got up in the style of a commercial traveler, and carrying the orthodox 'commercial bag' in his hand" [001-052].

## Role in the Investigation

[[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] "sent Bates down to stay in the village and collect all the information he can outside the house" [001-014], recognizing the need for an insider as well: "they want someone within the walls to hobnob with the maids generally" [001-014]. This division of labor — Bates outside, Loveday inside — structures the investigation.

Bates is supremely confident of success: "Quite a simple affair, this, [[loveday-brooke|Miss Brooke]]," he tells Loveday. "A walk over the course, I take it, with you working inside the castle walls and I unearthing without" [001-053]. He adds with characteristic bravado: "if that girl does not find herself in jail before another week is over her head, my name is not Jeremiah Bates" [001-053].

## The Wrong Theory

Bates believes [[stephanie-delcroix|Stephanie Delcroix]], [[lady-cathrow|Lady Cathrow]]'s French maid, is the thief. He is convinced she has a male accomplice — a "pal" — and that by following her silently they will catch both criminals with the stolen jewels.

When [[stephanie-delcroix|Stephanie]] disappears from the house one evening, Bates is "radiant" [001-120], certain she is heading to meet her accomplice: "She thinks she'll find all she wants where she's going — 'the feathered nest' I spoke to you about this morning. Ha! ha! Well, instead of stepping into it, as she fancies she will, she'll walk straight into a detective's arms, and land her pal there into the bargain" [001-120].

Loveday, suspecting a darker outcome, asks grimly: "I can see another possible destination for the girl — the stream that runs through the wood we drove past this morning" [001-124]. Her premonition proves correct — [[stephanie-delcroix|Stephanie]] has drowned herself in despair over being wrongly accused.

## Characteristics

Bates embodies the limitations of conventional police methodology that Loveday consistently transcends. His approach is methodical but unimaginative — setting watches, following suspects, waiting for them to incriminate themselves. His confidence borders on arrogance, and his misreading of [[stephanie-delcroix|Stephanie]]'s character (seeing criminal calculation where there was only despair) nearly results in tragedy.

He speaks in the grandiloquent manner of a self-assured detective: "We want to follow her silently, [[sir-george-cathrow|Sir George]], silently as the shadow follows the man" [001-122]. But his method — watching and waiting — proves ineffective compared to Loveday's psychological insight and direct engagement.
