## Overview

North Cape was a country house in the [[redhill|Redhill]] district of Surrey, owned by a wealthy and popular gentleman named [[mr-jameson|Mr. Jameson]] who possessed "magnificent presentation and other plate." The house was notable for being one of the earliest in the neighbourhood to be fitted with electric lighting.[PN=003-020][PN=003-084]

## Electric Lighting

North Cape's electric lighting system was maintained by an on-site engine housed in a low-built redbrick shed adjoining the stables. A resident engineer operated the system, which [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] described as "one of the greatest safeguards against burglars that a man can give his house" — though she cautioned against relying exclusively upon it.[PN=003-021][PN=003-084]

## In the Stories

North Cape was the target of a dramatic [[robbery|burglary]] attempt by [[the-redhill-sisterhood|the Redhill Sisterhood]] gang. [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] received a cryptic warning — "North Cape will be attacked tonight — a desperate gang — be prepared for a struggle. Above all, guard the electrical engine" — and foiled the [[robbery|burglary]] by extinguishing the electric lights at the critical moment.[PN=003-139][PN=003-140]

The incident was reported in the *Surrey Gazette* as "an exciting scene enacted at North Cape."[PN=003-140]
