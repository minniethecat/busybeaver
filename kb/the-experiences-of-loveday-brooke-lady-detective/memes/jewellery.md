# Lady Cathrow's Jewellery

The [[robbery|theft]] of [[lady-cathrow|Lady Cathrow]]'s jewellery — valued at £30,000 — from [[craigen-court|Craigen Court]] is the crime that opens [[ch01-the-black-bag-left-on-a-doorstep|The Black Bag Left on a Doorstep]], the first [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] story. The robbery is described in the opening paragraph: "[[lady-cathrow|Lady Cathrow]] has lost £30,000 worth of jewellery, if the [[newspaper|newspaper accounts]] are to be trusted" (001-001). The scale of the [[robbery|theft]] — £30,000 in 1890s values, equivalent to several million pounds today — establishes the high stakes of Loveday's professional world from the outset.

## The Safe

[[lady-cathrow|Lady Cathrow]]'s jewellery "was kept in a safe in her dressing-room" (001-006) at [[craigen-court|Craigen Court]]. The security arrangements became a central point of investigation: [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] "lay much stress on the safe being left unlocked that night" (001-013), while Loveday's analysis of the safe-door and the writing left upon it pointed in a different direction. The safe's vulnerability — and the question of who had access to its key — is the crux of the mystery.

## Recovery

Approximately one-third of the stolen jewellery was eventually recovered: "[[About]] a third of the lost jewellery was found there" (001-154), discovered in a location that Loveday's investigation had identified. The partial recovery is typical of the stories' realism — not all stolen property is retrieved, and Loveday's success is measured in incremental gains rather than complete restitution.
