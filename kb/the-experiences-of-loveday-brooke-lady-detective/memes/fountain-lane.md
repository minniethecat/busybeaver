## Overview

Fountain Lane is a small turning in [[brighton|Brighton]], whose inhabitants are plagued by what they believe to be a ghost. The investigation into the haunting — conducted by [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] during her convalescence at the [[metropole-hotel|Métropole Hotel]] — forms the central plot of "[[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|The Ghost of Fountain Lane]]." The lane's modest houses and their terrified tenants provide the human backdrop to what proves to be a calculated hoax.

## Location

Fountain Lane is a small turning in [[brighton|Brighton]], the fashionable seaside resort on England's south coast. It is within reach of the [[metropole-hotel|Métropole Hotel]], where [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] is staying.

> "The inhabitants of Fountain Lane, a small turning leading off —" [006-012]

## Description

Fountain Lane is characterized as a modest residential turning, its houses occupied by tenants whose fears of the supernatural are preyed upon by the orchestrator of the [[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|ghost hoax]]. The lane's narrow, confined geography contributes to the claustrophobic atmosphere of fear.

> "The misery of the landlord of the Fountain Lane houses if all his tenants decided to leave." [006-016]

The ghost is dismissed by [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] as "that ridiculous ghost in Fountain Lane," signalling her characteristic scepticism toward supernatural explanations [006-101]. Her investigation ultimately uncovers the human agency behind the haunting.

> "And the origin of the ghost in Fountain Lane?" [006-124]

## Significance

Fountain Lane represents the "ordinary street with extraordinary events" motif common in detective fiction — a seemingly unremarkable turning whose residents are terrorized by what appears to be a supernatural force. [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]'s rational dismantling of the ghost myth exemplifies her methodology: apply skepticism, observe carefully, and find the human motive behind apparently inexplicable phenomena.
