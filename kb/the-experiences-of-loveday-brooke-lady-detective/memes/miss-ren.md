Miss René Golding, also called [[irene-golding|Irené]], is the only daughter of [[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]] and the central figure in the mystery of "Missing!" (Story 7). Her sudden disappearance from [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]] triggers her father's collapse and the investigation led by [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]].

## Background and Personality

René is the daughter of [[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]] and his Italian wife Antonia. She spent her early childhood in Italy, and her maid Lena — actually Maddalena — was brought from Naples "twelve years ago as nurse" and "naturally enough fell into her duties as René's maid" [007-060]. Her Italian heritage is considered central to her character:

> She became voluble in her description of her dear Miss René, her beauty, her fascinating ways, which she traced entirely to the Italian blood that flowed in her veins; and anecdote after anecdote she related of the happy time when they lived among the lakes and mountains of her native land. [007-104]

René is portrayed as strong-willed and impetuous. [[mrs-greenhow|Mrs. Greenhow]] remarks that "if only she could have been taught to govern her willful temper a happier fate might have befallen her" [007-143]. Her father, however, acknowledges her strong moral compass — when she saw [[mr-gordon-cleeve|Gordon Cleeve]] "cruelly thrashing one of his setters," she "cut him dead — would have nothing whatever to do with him" [007-056].

Her portrait, a pastel hung above her father's writing-table, shows "a large-eyed, handsome girl of eighteen, with a remarkably sweet expression about the mouth" [007-048], though [[mrs-greenhow|Mrs. Greenhow]] is quick to add that "one seldom or never saw her wearing such a sweet expression of countenance" [007-049].

## Disappearance

René was raised believing her mother had died. In reality, Antonia Golding had been cast off by her own father after a marital breakdown and was living in obscurity at the Château Mascagni in Italy. When René learned the truth through a letter from her mother's friend, the effect was immediate:

> The girl told me that when she read that letter all her blood was stirred within her. She was seized with a burning desire there and then to kiss that mother and to right her wrongs. [007-187]

She left [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]] without explanation, traveling to Italy where she was reunited with her mother. The likeness between mother and daughter was striking — René herself told Loveday that "after she had been a few days in the house and the mother had rallied a little in strength, the servants declared it was only by their dress that they could distinguish one from the other" [007-189].

## The Mother's Plan

Antonia Golding, desperate to be reconciled with her husband, devised a plan: she would travel to England in René's place while René remained in Italy as a "force to be held in reserve" [007-191]. If [[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]] refused his wife's plea, René in turn would refuse to return home.

The two simply exchanged clothes — "a matter of convenience merely" since [[mrs-golding|Mrs. Golding]] "had, for some reason or other, returned home with next to no wardrobe; René's dress was suitable for travelling, and not likely to attract attention" [007-193].

Tragically, the mother — wearing René's distinctive dress of "champagne colour, with a large, black hat, trimmed profusely with black plumes" [007-076] — was seen in [[paris|Paris]], walked from the country station to [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]] in drenching rain, and died shortly after arriving. Her body, mistaken for René's, was buried under her daughter's name.

## The Reunion

When Loveday unravels the truth, she brings René back to [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]]. The reunion is dramatic: René — "a sadder, sweeter-faced René than the one who had so impetuously discarded home and father" [007-168] — sweeps into her father's sickroom:

> She swept impetuously past her into the room. "Father, father!" [007-169]

The story closes with the implication that René and [[lord-guilleroy|Lord Guilleroy]], who assisted in the investigation, will marry. [[inspector-ramsay|Inspector Ramsay]] predicts "a couple of weddings in the house before the year comes to an end" [007-196], and Loveday corrects him: "[[lord-guilleroy|Lord Guilleroy]] and the runaway René are the only two who will have to be congratulated as bride and bridegroom" [007-197].

## Significance

[[miss-golding|René Golding]] is one of the most fully drawn young women in [[the-experiences-of-loveday-brooke-lady-detective|the Loveday Brooke stories]]. Her impulsiveness, her fierce loyalty to the mother she never knew, and her ultimate return to her father create a domestic drama that runs parallel to Loveday's professional investigation. The case is solved not through detection of crime but through the patient unraveling of a family tragedy.
