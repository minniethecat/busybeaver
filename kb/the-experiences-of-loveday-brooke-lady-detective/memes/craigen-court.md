## Overview

Craigen Court is the country residence of [[sir-george-cathrow|Sir George Cathrow]] and his wife [[lady-cathrow|Lady Cathrow]]. It is the scene of the [[robbery|jewel robbery]] that opens the [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] series — the [[robbery|theft]] of [[lady-cathrow|Lady Cathrow]]'s £300 necklace from a locked safe, reported to [[ebenezer-dyer|Ebenezer Dyer]] at [[lynch-court|Lynch Court]] on the very first page of "[[the-black-bag-left-on-a-doorstep|The Black Bag Left on a Doorstep]]."

## Location

Craigen Court is situated in the country, accessible by train from [[london|London]]. [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] is instructed by [[ebenezer-dyer|Dyer]] to take the first train the following morning to begin her investigation.

> "You had better start for Craigen Court by the first train tomorrow." [001-042]

The estate has a servants' entrance and a trim groom who can be distinguished from the local country labourers.

## Description

Craigen Court is a country house of sufficient size to have a full staff of servants and a safe in which [[lady-cathrow|Lady Cathrow]] kept her valuable jewellery. The estate is described as having grounds maintained by a gardener.

> "Craigen Court, the residence of Sir George Cathrow." [001-004]

The safe from which the jewels were stolen was found empty, with words written across its interior — a clue that [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] would later connect to the identity of the thief. The household includes footmen and other indoor servants, as well as outdoor staff.

The estate is notable for its Christmas Eve tradition: a young clergyman relative always dines at Craigen Court on Christmas Eve, a detail that becomes pivotal to Loveday's reconstruction of the crime.

> "The young clergyman, it seems, always dines at Craigen Court on Christmas Eve." [001-169]

## Significance

Craigen Court serves as the archetypal country-house crime scene that introduces the [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] series. It establishes the pattern of the stories: a crime occurs in a genteel rural setting, the local police and household are baffled, and [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] arrives from [[london|London]] with her superior observational and deductive skills. The contrast between the ordered appearance of the country house and the hidden criminality within its walls is a theme that recurs throughout the collection.
