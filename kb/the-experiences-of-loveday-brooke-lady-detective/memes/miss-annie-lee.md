Miss Annie Lee is the young woman whose disappearance into the [[redhill|Redhill]] [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]] brings [[harry-emmett|Harry Emmett]] to [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] for help in "[[the-murder-at-troytes-hill|The Murder at Troyte's Hill]]" (Story 3). She is a nursery governess at [[wootton-hall|Wootton Hall]] and [[harry-emmett|Emmett]]'s sweetheart.

## Engagement and Disappearance

For a year, [[harry-emmett|Harry Emmett]] had been engaged to Annie Lee, who worked as a nursery governess "in a large house in the neighbourhood of [[redhill|Redhill]]" [003-057] — [[wootton-hall|Wootton Hall]]. Her mother had died when she was a baby, and both she and [[harry-emmett|Emmett]] believed her father was also dead.

Then, abruptly, everything changed. Annie discovered her father was still alive, broke off her engagement, and vanished. [[harry-emmett|Emmett]] tells Loveday:

> "I suddenly got a letter from her announcing the fact, and at the same time, breaking off her engagement with me." [003-061]

He tore the letter to pieces and wrote back refusing to accept the broken engagement, but received no reply from Annie herself — only a note from [[mrs-copeland|Mrs. Copeland]] of [[wootton-hall|Wootton Hall]] explaining that Annie "had thrown up her engagement and joined some [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]]" [003-061].

## Found in the Sisterhood

Loveday, already investigating the [[redhill|Redhill]] [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]], tells [[harry-emmett|Emmett]]: "I fancy I have already come upon the traces of Miss Annie Lee" [003-064]. She asks [[harry-emmett|Emmett]] to serve as her deputy in watching the [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]]'s movements, adding:

> "I have little doubt but what under the blue serge hood of one of the sisters you will discover the pretty face of Miss Annie Lee." [003-070]

## Vindication

When the criminal conspiracy at the [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]] is exposed, Loveday assures concerned parties that Annie was deceived rather than complicit: "I've no doubt that when the circumstances of her admission to the [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]] are investigated, it will be proved that she has been as much deceived and imposed upon as the man, [[john-murray|John Murray]]" [003-122]. [[mrs-copeland|Mrs. Copeland]]'s "good word" and her own honesty ultimately clear her name.

## Significance

Annie Lee is the emotional impetus for the investigation in Story 3. Her disappearance drives [[harry-emmett|Harry Emmett]] to seek Loveday's help, and her presence inside the false [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]] provides Loveday with a personal stake in exposing the criminal enterprise. As a victim of [[fraud|deception]] rather than a perpetrator, she represents the innocent people caught up in the schemes Loveday unravels.
