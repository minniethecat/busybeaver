Colonel James is a retired army officer and a friend of [[sir-george-cathrow|Sir George Cathrow]] in "[[the-black-bag|The Black Bag]] Left on a Door-Step" (Story 1). He never appears directly in the narrative, but his former employment of [[harry-emmett|Harry Emmett]] is an important detail in the investigation of the Cathrow [[robbery|jewel robbery]].

## Employer of Harry Emmett

Colonel James employed [[harry-emmett|Harry Emmett]] as his footman, a position that brought the young man into frequent contact with the Cathrow household. The housekeeper, [[mrs-williams|Mrs. Williams]], recalls:

> He was footman to Colonel James, who is a great friend of Sir George's, and Harry was constantly backwards and forwards bringing messages from his master. [001-085]

[[harry-emmett|Emmett]]'s father had been a [[london|London]] cabdriver, and [[harry-emmett|Harry]] himself "for a time did so also; then he took it into his head to be a gentleman's servant, and great satisfaction he gave as such" [001-085]. His service with Colonel James was evidently successful — he was known as "a bright, handsome young fellow and so full of fun, that everyone liked him" [001-085].

## Departure of His Servant

[[About]] six months before the events of the story, [[harry-emmett|Emmett]] left Colonel James's employment. He told [[mrs-williams|Mrs. Williams]] "he was going away to look after his dear old grandmother, who had a sweet-stuff shop somewhere or other" [001-087].

This seemingly innocent explanation proves to be a coded reference. As Loveday later explains to [[inspector-ramsay|Inspector Ramsay]], in cabman's vernacular the phrase "the dear old grandmother" refers to a provident association — a mutual aid society — and "the office where they make and receive their payments is styled 'the sweet stuff-shop'" [001-173]. This linguistic clue leads Loveday directly to [[harry-emmett|Emmett]]'s whereabouts at a branch of the association in Wreford.

## Significance

Colonel James is an offstage figure whose role is entirely functional: his employment of [[harry-emmett|Harry Emmett]] establishes the connection between the footman and the Cathrow household, and his status as "a great friend of [[sir-george-cathrow|Sir George]]'s" [001-085] explains why [[harry-emmett|Emmett]] had such easy access to Cathrow Hall. The fact that [[harry-emmett|Emmett]] left his service six months earlier becomes a key timeline detail in Loveday's reconstruction of events.
