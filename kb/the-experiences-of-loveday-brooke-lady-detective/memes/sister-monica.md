Sister Monica is a member of the undenominational [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]] recently founded at [[redhill|Redhill]], in the [[reigate|Reigate]] district. She is the Sister who first approaches [[ebenezer-dyer|Ebenezer Dyer]]'s detective agency to request an investigation into the series of burglaries occurring in the [[redhill|Redhill]] neighborhood, which local police suspect are connected to the [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]].

## Role in the Stories

Sister Monica appears exclusively in the third story, where [[the-redhill-sisterhood|the Redhill Sisterhood]] is under suspicion for a series of local burglaries. She brings the case to [[ebenezer-dyer|Dyer]]'s agency, requesting that a detective be sent to [[redhill|Redhill]] to investigate.

> "This lady gave her name simply as 'Sister Monica,' and stated that she was a member of an undenominational Sisterhood that had recently been founded at Redhill." [003-004]

She later accompanies [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] and [[inspector-gunning|Inspector Gunning]] during their visit to the [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]]'s home at [[paved-court|Paved Court]].

## Characteristics

Sister Monica is portrayed as physically imposing and rather unpleasant in demeanor. The narrator describes her in unflattering terms:

> "I have seen many a man whom I would rather not trust myself alone with, much less hang him, but until I saw Sister Monica's, I never saw a woman's face which so inspired me with a desire to see its owner at least stand her trial for manslaughter." [003-016]

[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] herself acknowledges Sister Monica's difficult personality:

> "Now Sister Monica, I must admit, is not pleasant to look at, nor is she endowed with any too large a share of the suavities and refinements of life." [003-146]
