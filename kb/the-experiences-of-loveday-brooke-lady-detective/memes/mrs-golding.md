## Overview

[[miss-golding|Irené Golding]], born Irené Mascagni, is the Italian wife of [[mr-golding|Richard Golding]] and mother of [[miss-golding|René Golding]]. Her story — believed dead for twelve years, secretly alive as an actress, and tragically drowned while attempting to return to her husband — forms the hidden foundation of "Missing!" (Story 7), the final [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] case.

## Character

[[irene-golding|Irené]] is described by [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] as "a typical Italian woman of the half-educated, passionate, beautiful, animal kind" [007-173]. She was:

- **Motherless**: Spoiled "from babyhood upwards by her old nurse, Antonia" [007-173]
- **Strong-willed**: "could not brook the slightest opposition to her whims and wishes" [007-173]
- **A coquette**: "lovers were an absolute necessity to her" [007-173]
- **Fascinating**: Even in failing health, her daughter [[miss-ren|René]] "described her to me as the most fascinating woman she had ever met" [007-180]

## The "Death" and Secret Life

The pivotal event in [[irene-golding|Irené]]'s history occurred some nineteen or twenty years before the story. After a troubled marriage marked by "remonstrance on [[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]]'s part" being met by appeals to her father for protection, [[irene-golding|Irené]] "left her husband and little daughter and went back to her father's house, vowing that nothing would induce her to leave her beloved Italy" [007-173].

When [[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]] attempted reconciliation from Naples, the old nurse Antonia visited him with false news: that [[irene-golding|Irené]] "had caught a fever during his absence, and had died, and now lay buried in the family vault at [[alguida|Alguida]]" [007-173].

In truth, after a month at her father's country house, she had "joined a company of actors, then passing through [[alguida|Alguida]]" [007-175]. Her "great personal beauty ensured her ready admission to the corps" [007-175], and "in her new life, no doubt, her vanity and innate love of coquetry found ample gratification" [007-175].

## The Return

After eleven years without contact, [[irene-golding|Irené]] returned to her father [[count-mascagni|Count Mascagni]]'s château in failing health. She made contact with her daughter [[miss-ren|René]], who traveled to [[alguida|Alguida]] to meet her mother for the first time [007-180].

The two women, said to be so alike that "the servants declared it was only by their dress that they could distinguish one from the other" [007-180], devised a plan. [[irene-golding|Irené]] would travel to England in [[miss-ren|René]]'s clothes to plead for [[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]]'s forgiveness:

> Instead of attempting negotiations with Mr. Golding through lawyers or by letter, she should herself go to him at his country house, throw herself upon his generosity, plead for forgiveness, and beg to be taken back to his heart once more. [007-180]

## The Tragedy

Arriving at [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]] in the rain, [[irene-golding|Irené]] approached the house but "at the hall door, possibly, her courage suddenly failed her" [007-180]. She looked through a window and saw her husband dining with [[mrs-greenhow|Mrs. Greenhow]] — the woman he intended to marry.

> She sees her husband and the woman he intends to marry seated together at table. She takes in at a glance the refinement of the home, together with the rigid conventionality of English domestic life. A wave of memory, perhaps, brings before her episodes in her past career altogether out of tune with this home picture. She feels the impracticability of the mission on which she is bent. [007-180]

She fled into the darkness, "takes a wrong turning, perhaps" [007-180], and drowned in the stream on the Golding estate. Her body, dressed in [[miss-ren|René]]'s clothes and wearing a wedding ring, was initially identified as [[miss-golding|Miss Golding]].

## The Crucial Clue

The wedding ring proved critical to Loveday's investigation. As Loveday explained to [[inspector-ramsay|Inspector Ramsay]]:

> A keynote of suspicion as to the possibility of Mrs. Golding being still alive had been struck when, as I sat writing at Miss Golding's davenport, I found the words "Mia Madre" scribbled here and there on her blotting-pad. [007-177]

Combined with the wedding ring and [[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]]'s exclamation that his daughter had "aged by a dozen years" [007-177], the pieces fell into place.

## Significance

[[miss-golding|Irené Golding]] never appears alive in the narrative — she is the absent presence whose past decisions drive the entire plot. Her story is the most elaborate family secret in the [[the-experiences-of-loveday-brooke-lady-detective|Loveday Brooke collection]]: a fabricated death, a hidden career as an actress, and a fatal reunion attempt that kills the wrong woman. In a series built around Loveday's ability to see through domestic [[fraud|deception]]s, [[irene-golding|Irené]]'s [[fraud|deception]] — sustained for twelve years — represents the ultimate test of the detective's method.
