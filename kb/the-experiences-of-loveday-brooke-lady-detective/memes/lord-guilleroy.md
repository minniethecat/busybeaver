Lord Guilleroy is a wealthy nobleman and landowner who "owns nearly all the land for miles round [[langford-hall|Langford]]" in [[langford-cross|Leicestershire]]. He is the devoted suitor of [[miss-golding|Irené Golding]], the missing daughter of [[mr-golding|Richard Golding]], and plays an instrumental role in the investigation of her disappearance [007-031].

## Role in the Stories

Lord Guilleroy appears in "Missing!" as both a key figure in the emotional stakes of the case and an active participant in the investigation. He meets [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] at [[langford-cross|Langford Cross Station]] upon her arrival, drives her to [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]], and later travels to [[paris|Paris]] at Loveday's direction to assist with the investigation there. He represents the romantic interest whose life, by his own implication, would be "wrecked" if the search for [[miss-golding|Miss Golding]] proved fruitless [007-031].

## Key Appearances

Lord Guilleroy appears throughout "Missing!":

- He meets [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] at [[langford-cross|Langford Cross Station]] and drives her to [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]], making a favourable impression [007-031]
- He is described as [[miss-golding|Irené Golding]]'s preferred suitor — she gave "unequivocal encouragement to Lord Guilleroy" after rejecting [[mr-gordon-cleeve|Gordon Cleeve]] [007-031]
- He caresses the Golding family dog, Dryad, a magnificent Newfoundland, upon arrival at the Hall [007-042]
- After a hopeful telegram arrives from the [[paris|Paris police]], he departs immediately for [[paris|Paris]] to represent [[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]]'s interests [007-111]
- Loveday dispatches detailed instructions to him in [[paris|Paris]], asking him to follow up leads there [007-130]
- He receives Loveday's communications at the Charing Cross Hotel and carries out her [[paris|Paris]]-based directives [007-150]

## Biography

Lord Guilleroy is the principal landowner in the [[langford-hall|Langford]] district and moves in the highest social circles of the neighbourhood. He is one of only two men who found favour with the imperious [[miss-golding|Irené Golding]] — the other being [[mr-gordon-cleeve|Gordon Cleeve]], the son of a wealthy baronet [007-031]. After [[irene-golding|Irené]] definitively rejected Cleeve (having witnessed him "cruelly thrashing one of his setters"), she gave her encouragement to Lord Guilleroy [007-058].

His wealth and position make him a highly suitable match from a social perspective. Unlike Cleeve, who reacted to rejection with violent threats before departing on a world tour, Lord Guilleroy demonstrates steadfast devotion throughout the crisis.

## Characteristics

[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] is "favourably impressed" with Lord Guilleroy upon their first meeting. He gives her "the idea of being a man of strong commonsense and great energy" [007-031]. His conversation is marked by "a certain reserve" — he declines to "wear his heart upon his sleeve," yet it is "easy to see, from a few words that escaped him, that if the search for [[miss-golding|Miss Golding]] proved fruitless his whole life would be wrecked" [007-031].

Loveday later reflects that he is "one to depend on in an emergency" — "he has, I should say, a good head on his shoulders" [007-111]. This assessment is borne out by his willing and effective cooperation with Loveday's [[paris|Paris]]-based investigation, where she judges him "more useful to me than all the [[paris|Paris police]] put together" if he proves to be the man she takes him for [007-136].

## Relationships

- **[[miss-golding|Irené Golding]]** — the missing young woman to whom he is devotedly attached
- **[[mr-golding|Richard Golding]]** — her father, with whom Lord Guilleroy coordinates
- **[[mr-gordon-cleeve|Gordon Cleeve]]** — his rival for [[irene-golding|Irené]]'s affections, who threatened to shoot him
- **[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]** — the detective who enlists his aid for the [[paris|Paris]] investigation
- **[[mrs-greenhow|Mrs. Greenhow]]** — [[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]]'s fiancée, who speaks of Lord Guilleroy with approval [007-111]

## Significance

Lord Guilleroy represents the ideal of steadfast masculine devotion in Victorian detective fiction. Unlike the volatile [[mr-gordon-cleeve|Gordon Cleeve]], who threatens violence and flees the country, Lord Guilleroy remains constant in his attachment and channels his energy into constructive action. His willingness to place himself at [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]'s disposal — a professional woman detective — demonstrates a practical-mindedness that transcends the class and gender conventions of the period. The tragedy of the story's resolution, with the recovery of [[miss-golding|Miss Golding]]'s body, leaves his ultimate fate poignantly unresolved.
