## Overview

John Murray was a former [[london|South London]] greengrocer who purchased two small houses — numbers 7 and 8 — in [[paved-court|Paved Court]], [[redhill|Redhill]]. His report to [[inspector-gunning|Inspector Gunning]] of the [[reigate|Reigate]] police concerning his tenants, [[the-redhill-sisterhood|the Redhill Sisterhood]], initiated the investigation that brought [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] to [[redhill|Redhill]].[PN=003-004]

## Role in the Stories

Murray's suspicions about the [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]] arose after he noticed a pattern: "in whatever direction those Sisters turn the wheels of their donkey-cart, burglaries, or attempts at burglaries, are sure to follow." He reported these concerns to [[inspector-gunning|Inspector Gunning]], who in turn contacted [[ebenezer-dyer|Ebenezer Dyer]] to secure [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]'s services.[PN=003-006]

It later emerged that Murray was not the innocent landlord he presented himself to be. He was ultimately revealed as one of the captured conspirators: "the names of the captured men are John Murray, Arthur and George Lee (father and sons)."[PN=003-142] Loveday confronted [[inspector-gunning|Inspector Gunning]] about the matter, asking when he "transferred your suspicions to John Murray," revealing that Murray had been part of the criminal operation all along.[PN=003-147]

## Biography

Murray claimed to have sold his greengrocer business in [[london|South London]] and used the proceeds to purchase numbers [[paved-court|7 and 8 Paved Court]], intending to live in one and let the other. He told [[inspector-gunning|Inspector Gunning]] he had difficulty finding a tenant until [[sister-monica|Sister Monica]] made an offer for number 8. In reality, Murray was an accomplice of the [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]] [[robbery|burglary]] ring, using his position as landlord to facilitate their operations.[PN=003-004][PN=003-142]
