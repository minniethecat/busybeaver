## Overview

Three Bridges is a railway junction on the [[london|London]]–[[brighton|Brighton]] line, serving as a transfer point in "[[the-redhill-sisterhood|The Redhill Sisterhood]]." When [[inspector-gunning|Inspector Gunning]] and [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] find themselves recognized at [[redhill|Redhill]] station, they adjust their plan and continue to Three Bridges to await a [[brighton|Brighton]] train back, using the junction's connectivity to their tactical advantage.

## Location

Three Bridges is located on the [[london|London]]–[[brighton|Brighton]] railway line, south of [[redhill|Redhill]] and north of [[brighton|Brighton]]. Its position as a junction makes it a useful transfer point for passengers changing between different services on the line.

> "Instead of landing at Redhill, we'll go on to Three Bridges and wait there for a Brighton train to bring us back." [003-014]

## Description

Three Bridges is a railway junction station, typical of the Victorian railway network's intermediate stops. The station's waiting facilities are mentioned in passing, as [[inspector-gunning|Gunning]] and Loveday are forced to wait there for a connecting train.

> "At Three Bridges they had to wait so long for a Brighton train that it was almost dark when Loveday got back to Redhill." [003-027]

The long wait at Three Bridges causes a delay that affects the timing of Loveday's return to [[redhill|Redhill]], a detail that underscores the logistical realities of Victorian railway travel.

## Significance

Three Bridges exemplifies the role of railway infrastructure in [[the-experiences-of-loveday-brooke-lady-detective|the Loveday Brooke stories]]. The Victorian railway network was not merely a means of transport but a tactical resource — stations could be used to evade notice, change direction, or buy time. [[inspector-gunning|Gunning]]'s use of Three Bridges as an alternative to [[redhill|Redhill]] mirrors his earlier use of [[merstham|Merstham]] as a discreet arrival point, demonstrating the operational awareness that characterizes [[profession|professional detective]] work in the period.
