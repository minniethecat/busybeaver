Martha Watts is a young servant girl living in [[brighton|Brighton]] who works as a help to a shopkeeper. She is the first person to report seeing the "ghost" of [[fountain-lane|Fountain Lane]], making her a key early witness in the sixth story.

## Role in the Stories

Martha Watts appears only in the sixth story, "[[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|The Ghost of Fountain Lane]]." She is the initial witness whose report of a ghostly figure sets the investigation in motion:

> "About a quarter to ten o'clock, a little girl named Martha Watts, who lives as a help to a shopkeeper in East Street, and who had been spending the evening with a friend in Fountain Lane, in passing the archway, saw the ghost." [006-012]

The local police, including Inspector Clampe, dismiss her [[evidence|testimony]] as the imaginings of a child. However, [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] later returns to Martha Watts's account and uses it as a key piece of evidence. When Loveday finally exposes the hoax, she asks Clampe:

> "How do you account for the girl, Martha Watts, seeing the ghost. She was not a member of the Church Defence Association?" [006-147]

The question remains unanswered — Martha Watts's genuine sighting of the human figure behind the "ghost" was the clue that the police overlooked.

## Characteristics

Martha Watts is described simply as a "little girl" — young, working-class, and dismissed by the authorities because of her age and social status. Her [[evidence|testimony]] proves more reliable than the police assumed.
