# Portmanteau

The black leather portmanteau — also referred to as "the black bag" — is the central piece of evidence in "[[the-black-bag-left-on-a-door-step]]." Left mysteriously on a doorstep early one morning, its contents set in motion the first recorded investigation of [[loveday-brooke]].

## Discovery

"A black leather bag, or portmanteau, was found early yesterday morning left on the doorstep of a house in a respectable London street" [001-032]. The bag contained jewellery and valuables that would eventually be traced back to a robbery at [[craigen-court]], the residence of Sir George Cathrow.

## Loveday Brooke's Travels

The portmanteau plays a recurring role in Loveday Brooke's movements. Upon arriving at a new location, she "shouldered her portmanteau" [002-058] and went about her business — the practical luggage serving as both prop for her undercover roles and symbol of her mobile, independent working life.

## As Evidence

The bag's contents provided the crucial link between the doorstep discovery and the robbery at Craigen Court. [[loveday-brooke]]'s analysis of the items within — and more importantly, what was absent — allowed her to reconstruct the thief's movements and motives.
