# Cheque

The stolen cheque is a key piece of evidence in "[[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane]]." Drawn on the [[west-sussex-bank]], it was taken from the household of the Reverend Charles Turner and became the focus of [[loveday-brooke]]'s investigation into what initially appeared to be a ghostly apparition.

## The Crime

"A cheque, the property of the Rev. Charles Turner, drawn on the West Sussex Bank, was stolen" [006-009]. The theft occurred under circumstances that the locals attributed to a ghost haunting Fountain Lane, giving the investigation its distinctive character.

## Loveday Brooke's Analysis

Loveday Brooke immediately recognized the case as something more substantial than a ghost story. Upon being presented with the cheque by a local official who "brought the cheque with me, thinking you might like to examine it" [006-017], she discerned the hallmarks of "a somewhat more commonplace fraud than a stolen cheque" [006-014]. Her investigation would reveal the theft was part of a calculated scheme rather than a supernatural event.

## Significance

The cheque represents one of Loveday Brooke's most notable demonstrations of forensic reasoning—cutting through local superstition to identify a financial crime. The document's connection to the West Sussex Bank provided a paper trail that ultimately exposed the perpetrator.
