Major Druce is a gentleman who appears in Catherine Louisa Pirkis's detective story "A Princess's Vengeance," the fourth story in [[the-experiences-of-loveday-brooke-lady-detective|*The Experiences of Loveday Brooke, Lady Detective*]]. He is the son of [[mrs-craven|Mrs. Druce]] and the fiancé of Princess Dullah-Veira, and his involvement in a puzzling case brings him to the attention of [[loveday-brooke]], the [[loveday-brooke|lady detective]].

## Family and Social Position

Major Druce occupies a respectable position in Victorian society. His engagement to [[princess-dullah-veih|the Princess]] Dullah-Veira marked a significant social alliance. As described in the narrative, the engagement was "arranged," and the subsequent disruption of the wedding plans formed the central mystery that [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] was called upon to investigate. Major Druce's mother, described as a woman of "extraordinary conduct," played a decisive role in the events that unfolded.

## The Investigation

The case involving Major Druce began when his mother advised him to place the matter in the hands of [[ebenezer-dyer|Ebenezer Dyer]]'s detective agency. [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] was assigned to the investigation, and her first significant encounter with Major Druce occurred when he visited the agency:

> "Major Druce, sir, wishes to see you," said the clerk, and Loveday Brooke had to rise and greet Major Druce, who at that moment entered the room. [[004-025]]

Major Druce accompanied [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] through crowded [[london|London]] streets as they pursued the investigation together:

> "A warm afternoon, and no mistake," said Major Druce, as side by side, they entered the packed thoroughfare. [[004-047]]

## Relationship with Loveday Brooke

Major Druce's interactions with [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] reveal him as a man caught between his mother's influence and his own desires. His hansom was described as "like himself, in immaculate condition," suggesting a man of careful appearance and military bearing [[004-033]]. Throughout the investigation, Major Druce alternated between surprise at [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]'s methods and cooperation with her inquiries. At one critical moment:

> Major Druce looked astounded. [[004-077]]

## Role in the Resolution

Following the resolution of the case, [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] "wrote a brief note to Major Druce, requesting him to meet her" as she finalized the investigation [[004-106]]. The case concludes with the implication that neither Mrs. nor Major Druce faced "blame of any sort," suggesting that [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]'s investigation had exonerated them from suspicion [[004-103]].
