Sister Anna is a young member of the undenominational [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]] at [[redhill|Redhill]]. Unlike her older colleague [[sister-monica|Sister Monica]], Sister Anna is described in gentle and sympathetic terms. She is one of the Sisters who care for crippled children at the [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]]'s home in [[paved-court|Paved Court]].

## Role in the Stories

Sister Anna appears only in the third story, "The [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Redhill Sisterhood]]." During [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]'s visit to the [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]]'s home, Sister Anna is seen carrying a crippled child:

> "Presently from the doorway a slight, gentle-looking young woman entered, leading — or rather carrying — a deformed boy of about eight years old. The boy was addressed as 'Sister Anna' by one of the cripples, who held out a skinny hand to detain her." [003-030]

She is immediately commanded by [[sister-monica|Sister Monica]] to take the deformed child away. [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] is struck by Sister Anna's gentle demeanor, which contrasts sharply with [[sister-monica|Sister Monica]]'s harshness:

> "Loveday noted with what a pained, shrinking look the young Sister obeyed, and the tender patience with which she had spoken to the little cripple boy." [003-031]

At the story's conclusion, Loveday expresses her concern for Sister Anna's future:

> "The saddest figure in this affair is poor little Sister Anna," said Loveday pityingly; "and she may not be so badly placed in a Sisterhood where practical Christianity holds sway." [003-154]

## Characteristics

Sister Anna is a "slight, gentle-looking young woman" with a "sweet, sad face" [003-040]. She tends to crippled children with tender patience. Unlike [[sister-monica|Sister Monica]], she is timid and seems cowed by the older woman's authority.
