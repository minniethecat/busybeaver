# Sir George Monroe

Sir George Monroe is a British diplomat and the father of Miss Monroe. He appears in "[[drawn-daggers]]" where his gift of a valuable dagger from Peking sets the story's events in motion.

## Role in "Drawn Daggers"

Sir George Monroe sent his daughter a jewelled dagger as a gift from Peking, "to get her out of the way of his other valuable treasures" [005-009]. This dagger becomes the central object in the theft investigation. Miss Monroe keeps the dagger in a drawer with "five other daggers, all precisely the same in shape and design" [005-009] — a fact that proves crucial to understanding the crime.

## Relationship with Loveday Brooke

When [[loveday-brooke]] investigates the case, she asks Miss Monroe about Sir George Monroe's connection to a certain individual: "I suppose you and Sir George Monroe are old friends?" [005-048]. This line of questioning suggests Sir George Monroe's diplomatic circle may have connections relevant to the case.

## Family

Sir George Monroe is the father of Miss Monroe, who lives in a household that becomes the scene of a jewellery theft. The dagger he sent is described as valuable and distinctive among a set of otherwise identical items.
