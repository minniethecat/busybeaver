## Overview

Miss [[irene-golding|Irené Golding]], commonly called [[miss-ren|René]], is the only daughter of [[mr-golding|Richard Golding]] of [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]], [[langford-cross|Langford Cross]], [[langford-cross|Leicestershire]]. At age 18, she is described as a "lovable and most attractive young woman, with her half-Australian half-Italian parentage" [007-023]. Her sudden disappearance on the morning of September 20th sets in motion the investigation that forms the plot of the seventh and final [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] story, "Missing!"

## Role in the Story

Miss Golding is the subject of the investigation rather than an active participant. The case begins when a reward of five hundred pounds is offered for information leading to her "restoration to her home" [007-002]. She was last seen "about ten o'clock on the morning of the 20th leaving [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]] Park for the high road leading to [[langford-cross|Langford Cross]]" [007-002], dressed in a "dark blue serge walking costume, with white straw sailor hat trimmed with cream ribbon" [007-002].

The case poses several puzzles: whether she took money with her, whether she left voluntarily, and what connection her disappearance might have to the two suitors competing for her affection — [[lord-guilleroy|Lord Guilleroy]] and [[mr-gordon-cleeve|Gordon Cleeve]].

## Key Appearances

Miss Golding does not appear directly in the story until its resolution. Her presence is felt through:

- **The reward notice** [007-002]: A detailed physical description circulated by her father
- **[[witness|Witness]] accounts**: Her temperament and recent behavior are described to [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] by her father, her maid Lena, and others
- **The body found** [007-130]: A woman's body discovered in the stream on the Golding estate, initially identified as Miss Golding
- **The revelation** [007-180]: The true Miss Golding is found alive at her grandfather's château in [[alguida|Alguida]], Italy

## Biography

René Golding lost her mother in infancy — or so she believed. Her father, [[mr-golding|Richard Golding]], was told by the old nurse Antonia that his wife [[irene-golding|Irené]] had died of fever during his six-month business trip to Australia [007-173]. In truth, [[mrs-golding|Irené Mascagni]] had joined a company of actors and fabricated the death story to retain her liberty [007-175].

[[miss-ren|René]] grew up at [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]] under the care of her father and, later, the chaperonage of [[mrs-greenhow|Mrs. Greenhow]], a widow who acted as housekeeper and whom [[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]] intended to marry [007-015].

Her romantic life became a matter of local interest. She "seems to have coquetted pretty equally" with two suitors — [[lord-guilleroy|Lord Guilleroy]] and [[mr-gordon-cleeve|Gordon Cleeve]] — before abruptly cutting Cleeve dead after witnessing him "cruelly thrashing one of his setters" [007-023, 007-050].

## The Disappearance

Shortly before her disappearance, [[miss-ren|René]] received an unsigned letter from Italy [007-058]. The letter, which she "tossed unopened into the fire" [007-058], was actually from her mother [[irene-golding|Irené]], who had returned to her father [[count-mascagni|Count Mascagni]]'s château in failing health and wished to see her daughter.

Despite the burned letter, [[miss-ren|René]] eventually traveled to [[alguida|Alguida]], where she met her mother for the first time. The two women, described as remarkably alike — "the servants declared it was only by their dress that they could distinguish one from the other" [007-180] — devised a plan. [[irene-golding|Irené]] would travel to England in [[miss-ren|René]]'s clothes to plead with [[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]] for forgiveness and reconciliation [007-180].

The plan went tragically wrong. [[irene-golding|Irené]], arriving at [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]] in the rain, saw through a window her husband dining with [[mrs-greenhow|Mrs. Greenhow]] — the woman he planned to marry. Overcome, she fled into the darkness, took a wrong turning, and drowned in the stream on the estate [007-180].

## Rescue and Resolution

[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]], suspecting the truth, dispatched [[lord-guilleroy|Lord Guilleroy]] to follow the maid Lena to Italy. He "tracked Lena home to [[alguida|Alguida]]" and "came upon her talking to Miss Golding, who stood at the gate of her grandfather's château dressed in her mother's Neapolitan dress" [007-187]. Miss Golding was "unfeignedly glad to be taken possession of" by one of her father's English friends [007-187], as her grandfather had been refusing to let her leave.

## Characteristics

Miss Golding is portrayed as spirited and strong-willed — "the imperious yet fascinating young lady" who "ruled her [household] with a rod of iron" [007-138]. Her quick temper is noted by several characters, including [[mrs-greenhow|Mrs. Greenhow]], who remarks after her supposed death that "if only she could have been taught to govern her willful temper a happier fate might have befallen her" [007-161].

Despite her temper, she is capable of decisive moral judgment — her immediate rejection of [[mr-gordon-cleeve|Gordon Cleeve]] after witnessing his cruelty to an animal demonstrates an instinctive sense of justice.

## Significance

Miss Golding's disappearance case serves as the capstone to the [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] series. The resolution — involving a case of mistaken identity with a dead mother, a long-buried family secret, and Loveday's patient, methodical [[investigation|detective work]] — exemplifies the themes of the collection: that surface appearances deceive, that domestic secrets hold keys to crimes, and that the most effective detection often involves understanding human psychology rather than hunting for physical [[clue|clues]].
