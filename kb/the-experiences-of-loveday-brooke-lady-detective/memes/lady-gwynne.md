Lady Gwynne is a society lady and intimate friend of [[mrs-druce|Mrs. Druce]], deeply involved in the fashionable and philanthropic circles that surround [[harem-mission|the Harem Mission]]. She is the person who originally brought [[lucie-cunier|Lucie Cunier]] — the missing Swiss-French amanuensis — from an orphanage in Geneva to England, and later becomes a key conspirator in the scheme to remove Lucie from [[major-druce|Major Druce]]'s attentions [004-013].

## Role in the Stories

Lady Gwynne plays a pivotal role in the conspiracy at the heart of "A Princess's Vengeance." She is one of three ladies — along with [[mrs-druce|Mrs. Druce]] and [[princess-dullah-veih|Princess Dullah-Veih]] — who decide that [[lucie-cunier|Lucie Cunier]] must be removed from the household before [[major-druce|Major Druce]]'s romantic interest in her jeopardizes his engagement to the wealthy Princess [004-149]. Her involvement demonstrates how society women could mobilize their networks — in this case, a fashionable milliner — to accomplish what amounts to a genteel abduction.

## Key Appearances

Lady Gwynne appears in the central chapters of "A Princess's Vengeance":

- She is first described as the woman who brought [[lucie-cunier|Lucie Cunier]] from the orphanage at Echallets, Geneva, intending her to be a governess before noticing her husband's interest in the girl's good looks [004-013]
- She attends [[mrs-druce|Mrs. Druce]]'s reception day, described as a "fair, handsome woman in pale green, with a picture hat" [004-056]
- During the critical moment when [[princess-dullah-veih|the Princess]]'s jewel goes missing, she is observed exchanging a significant glance with [[mrs-druce|Mrs. Druce]] [004-086]
- She starts to her feet and seizes her hat — adjusting it "without so much as a glance at a mirror" — when attention turns to the missing jewel [004-090]
- Loveday later reveals her full role: she was taken into [[mrs-druce|Mrs. Druce]]'s confidence, and the "three ladies in council decided that Lucie, in some fashion, must be got out of the way" [004-149]
- She enlisted her milliner, Madame Céline, to receive and conceal Lucie in a room on the upper floor of the millinery establishment [004-149]

## Biography

Lady Gwynne is a titled society lady with a husband and young children. Her experience with [[lucie-cunier|Lucie Cunier]] is revealing: she originally brought the girl from the Geneva orphanage intending to employ her as a governess, but "when she saw that the girl's good looks had attracted her husband's attention, she thought better of it" [004-013]. This episode — in which she quickly offloaded the attractive young woman onto [[mrs-druce|Mrs. Druce]] — establishes her as a pragmatist who acts decisively to protect her own domestic interests.

Her social connections extend to the world of fashionable commerce: she has sufficient influence with her milliner, Madame Céline, to arrange for Lucie's concealment there [004-149].

## Characteristics

Lady Gwynne is physically described as a "fair, handsome woman in pale green, with a picture hat" [004-056]. Her manner is poised and she moves easily in the highest circles of [[london|London]] society. She is on "very easy and intimate terms" with [[princess-dullah-veih|Princess Dullah-Veih]] and [[mr-cassimi|Hafiz Cassimi]] [004-071].

Her most telling characteristic is her ruthless pragmatism. She removed [[lucie-cunier|Lucie Cunier]] from her own household when the girl's beauty threatened her domestic peace; she then participated in a conspiracy to remove Lucie from [[mrs-druce|Mrs. Druce]]'s household when the girl threatened [[major-druce|Major Druce]]'s advantageous engagement. Her actions, while not criminal in the legal sense, reveal a cold-blooded willingness to dispose of a vulnerable young woman's future for the convenience of her social circle.

## Relationships

- **[[mrs-druce|Mrs. Druce]]** — her intimate friend and [[accomplice|co-conspirator]]
- **[[princess-dullah-veih|Princess Dullah-Veih]]** — the Turkish princess whose financial offer funded the scheme
- **[[lucie-cunier|Lucie Cunier]]** — the Swiss-French girl she brought to England and later helped conceal

- **[[mr-cassimi|Hafiz Cassimi]]** — the Turkish-Egyptian banker's son, with whom she converses at the reception
- **Madame Céline** — her milliner, who provided the hiding place for Lucie
- **Lebrun** — [[mrs-druce|Mrs. Druce]]'s French butler, Lucie's secret fiancé

## Significance

Lady Gwynne exemplifies the dark side of Victorian society's treatment of vulnerable women. Her role in the conspiracy illuminates how a network of privileged women could, with money and social connections, effectively make a person disappear — not through violence, but through the quiet exercise of influence. That she had previously done something similar to protect her own marriage reveals a pattern of behaviour. [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]'s unraveling of the conspiracy exposes a system of genteel coercion that operates entirely outside the notice of the law.
