Mrs. Golightly is the village draper and landlady of Northfield who provides [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] with lodging during her investigation of the [[redhill|Redhill]] [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]] in "[[the-murder-at-troytes-hill|The Murder at Troyte's Hill]]" (Story 3). Her shop and rooms above it serve as Loveday's base of operations for the sur[[disguise|veil]]lance of [[paved-court|Paved Court]].

## The Shop

Mrs. Golightly's establishment is easy to find: "'Golightly' was the singular name that figured above the shopfront, in which were displayed a variety of goods calculated to meet the wants of servants and the poorer classes generally" [003-035]. The shop has a private entrance in addition to the public one, which becomes important later in the story.

## Character

Loveday finds her to be "a civil and obliging person" [003-037]. She shows Loveday to her room above the shop, delivers letters that [[inspector-gunning|Inspector Gunning]] has arranged to have posted, and supplies pen, ink, and strong coffee — which she jokes "would keep a dormouse awake all through the winter without winking" [003-037].

## Knowledge of the Sisterhood

When Loveday questions her about the mysterious [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]] living in [[paved-court|Paved Court]], Mrs. Golightly can provide only limited information — "she could tell her no more than she already knew, beyond the fact that they started every morning on their rounds at eleven o'clock punctually, and that before that hour they were never to be seen outside their door" [003-038]. This detail about their schedule proves useful to Loveday's sur[[disguise|veil]]lance planning.

## Role in the Investigation

Mrs. Golightly later serves as an intermediary when Loveday arranges for [[harry-emmett|Harry Emmett]] (under the alias "Mr. White") to assist in the case. Loveday instructs him to enter the shop as a customer and "explain matters to Mrs. Golightly, who, no doubt, would be in her place behind the counter; tell her he was the brother of the Miss Smith who had hired her room" [003-074]. The key to the side door serves as his credentials.

## Significance

Mrs. Golightly is a minor but useful figure who provides Loveday with a safe base of operations in a village where she is being watched by unknown parties. As a local shopkeeper, she represents the ordinary commerce of village life — a contrast to the criminal conspiracy operating from the [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]] across the road.
