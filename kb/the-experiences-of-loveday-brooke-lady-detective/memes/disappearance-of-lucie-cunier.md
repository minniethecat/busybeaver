# Disappearance of Lucie Cunier

The **disappearance of [[lucie-cunier|Lucie Cunier]]** was a case investigated by [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] in "[[a-princesss-vengeance|A Princess's Vengeance]]." [[lucie-cunier|Lucie Cunier]], a young Frenchwoman serving as [[amanuensis]] to [[mrs-druce|Mrs. Druce]], vanished from her employer's household, leading to revelations involving an Indian princess's long-planned revenge.

## Background

[[lucie-cunier|Lucie Cunier]] was employed as an amanuensis and secretary to [[mrs-druce|Mrs. Druce]], the mother of [[major-druce|Major Druce]]. [[major-druce|Major Druce]] was married to the [[princess-dullah-veih|Princess Dullah-Veih]], an Indian princess of great beauty and wealth.

> "Mrs. Druce, the lady to whom Lucie Cunier acted as amanuensis, took the child with her to France." [004-002]

The Princess had a complex history with [[major-druce|Major Druce]], and her presence in the household created tensions that ultimately led to Lucie's disappearance.

## The Disappearance

[[lucie-cunier|Lucie Cunier]] vanished under mysterious circumstances after [[princess-dullah-veih|the Princess]] Dullah-Veih came to stay with the [[major-druce|Druce]] family. She left her room undisturbed, with her belongings remaining in place — a detail that suggested she had not left voluntarily.

> "You'll find the room exactly as Lucie left it, nothing has been disturbed." [004-098]

The disappearance was reported to [[ebenezer-dyer|Ebenezer Dyer]]'s agency, and [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] took on the investigation. Her inquiries led her to trace [[princess-dullah-veih|the Princess]]'s movements and connections.

## Investigation

[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]'s investigation uncovered that [[princess-dullah-veih|the Princess]] Dullah-Veih had a long-standing grievance. The Princess had "something of a history" [004-020], and Loveday discovered that [[lucie-cunier|Lucie Cunier]]'s disappearance was part of an elaborate revenge scheme.

> "Lucie Cunier, lately your mother's amanuensis." [004-131]

The investigation revealed that Lucie had not been harmed but had been secretly married to a man named Lebrun, with [[princess-dullah-veih|the Princess]] orchestrating the entire affair as an act of vengeance against the [[major-druce|Druce]] family for a perceived wrong done to her years earlier.

## Resolution

[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] traced [[lucie-cunier|Lucie Cunier]] to her new location and confirmed that she had married Lebrun "of her own free will" [004-134]. The Princess's scheme was exposed — she had used her position in the [[major-druce|Druce]] household to arrange Lucie's removal and marriage as retribution.

> "Lucie has married Lebrun of her own free will." [004-136]

The case was resolved without criminal charges, as Lucie's marriage, while orchestrated by [[princess-dullah-veih|the Princess]], was ultimately consensual. The Princess's vengeance was complete, and the [[major-druce|Druce]] family was left to reckon with the consequences of their past actions.
