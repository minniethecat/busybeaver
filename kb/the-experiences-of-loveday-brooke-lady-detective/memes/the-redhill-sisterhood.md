## Overview

The Redhill Sisterhood was an undenominational religious order established at [[redhill|Redhill, Surrey]], operating from 8 [[paved-court|Paved Court]]. Outwardly presented as a charitable institution caring for crippled orphans, the Sisterhood came under police suspicion when a pattern of burglaries was observed following the routes of its members' daily begging tours.

## Function

The Sisterhood's stated purpose was to provide a home for crippled orphans. Members, dressed in dark blue serge nun's habits, made daily circuits through the [[north-cape|Surrey]] countryside with a donkey-cart, collecting broken victuals and cast-off garments. According to the founder's representative, [[sister-monica|Sister Monica]], the order was "undenominational" — unaffiliated with any particular religious sect — and had been recently founded by a wealthy lady who wished to keep her identity secret.[PN=003-004]

In practice, [[inspector-gunning|Inspector Gunning]] of the [[reigate|Reigate]] police came to suspect that the begging tours served as reconnaissance for a [[robbery|burglary]] gang. "In whatever direction those Sisters turn the wheels of their donkey-cart, burglaries, or attempts at burglaries, are sure to follow," he reported.[PN=003-006]

## Appearances

The Sisterhood appears in Chapter 3, "[[the-black-bag|The Black Bag]] Left on a Door-Step." [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] is called to [[redhill|Redhill]] to investigate the organization after [[john-murray|John Murray]], the landlord of [[paved-court|7 and 8 Paved Court]], reports his suspicions to [[inspector-gunning|Inspector Gunning]].[PN=003-001][PN=003-004]

## Key Members

- **[[sister-monica]]** — The leader of the Sisterhood, described by [[inspector-gunning|Inspector Gunning]] as possessing "the lowest and most repulsive" criminal face he had ever seen on a woman.[PN=003-016]
- **[[sister-anna]]** ([[miss-annie-lee|Annie Lee]]) — A younger member of the Sisterhood with "unusual sadness and beauty," formerly a nursery governess at [[wootton-hall|Wootton Hall]]. She joined the Sisterhood after learning her father was a convict at Portland prison.[PN=003-030][PN=003-059][PN=003-061]
- Two additional unnamed Sisters who accompanied the donkey-cart on its daily rounds.[PN=003-029]

## Associated Events

- [[redhill-sisterhood-burglaries]] — A series of country-house burglaries and attempted burglaries in the [[redhill|Redhill]] district, linked by their geographic correlation with the Sisterhood's daily routes.[PN=003-006]

## Historical Context

The Sisterhood reflects Victorian anxieties about disguised criminality operating under the cover of religious or charitable institutions. The late 19th century saw numerous real-world cases of fraudulent charitable organizations, and the figure of the criminal disguised in religious habit appears in several contemporary detective narratives. [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] herself observes that "the popular detective stories, for which there seems so large a demand at the present day, must be, at times, uncommonly useful to the criminal classes," suggesting a self-aware commentary on the genre's relationship with real criminal methodology.[PN=003-026]
