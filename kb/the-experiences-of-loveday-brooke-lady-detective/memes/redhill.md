## Overview

Redhill is a town in [[north-cape|Surrey]], located within the [[reigate|Reigate]] police jurisdiction. It is the principal setting for the third [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] story, "[[the-redhill-sisterhood|The Redhill Sisterhood]]," in which [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] is dispatched by [[ebenezer-dyer|Ebenezer Dyer]] to investigate a series of burglaries attributed to a mysterious sisterhood operating in the district.

## Location

Redhill is situated in [[north-cape|Surrey]], south of [[london|London]], on the [[london|London]] and [[brighton|Brighton coach road]]. It is served by a railway station on the [[london|London]]–[[brighton|Brighton]] line, with [[merstham|Merstham]] being the preceding station. Redhill falls under the jurisdiction of the [[reigate|Reigate]] police, headed by [[inspector-gunning|Inspector Gunning]].

> "Redhill, I must tell you is in the Reigate police district." [003-004]

From Redhill, one can walk to [[merstham|Merstham]] — a distance [[inspector-gunning|Inspector Gunning]] covers on foot to avoid recognition — and the surrounding countryside includes country houses within a ten-mile radius.

## Description

Redhill is characterized as a district where country houses dot the landscape, making it a target for the systematic burglaries that drive the plot. The town has at least one draper's shop on the [[london|London]] Road and is active enough that shops are lit as evening falls.

> "Every country house of any pretension for ten miles round Redhill had been visited." [003-018]

[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] takes a room in Redhill as her base of operations, from which she ventures out on a bicycle to conduct sur[[disguise|veil]]lance and investigation.

> "I want you to go to the room in Redhill that I have hired, take your bicycle with you." [003-070]

The surrounding area is described as having a more rural character, contrasting with [[london|London]]:

> "No doubt in London the streets were walled in and the air charged with unwholesome gases, but out here in the country the air had a sharp bite." [003-044]

## Significance

Redhill serves as the archetypal "country case" setting in the [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] series. Unlike the metropolitan cases centred on [[london|London]], Redhill represents the rural district where local police are out of their depth and must call upon the expertise of [[ebenezer-dyer|Dyer]]'s agency. The town's position on the railway line emphasizes the connectivity between [[london|London]] and the provinces that enables [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]'s mobile [[investigation|detective work]].
