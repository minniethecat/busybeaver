# Disappearance of Irené Golding

The **disappearance of [[miss-golding|Irené Golding]]** was a missing-person case investigated by [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] in the final story of the collection, "[[missing|Missing!]]." [[miss-golding|Irené Golding]], the only daughter and heiress of [[mr-golding|Richard Golding]] of [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]], vanished on Monday, September 20th, prompting a discreet investigation.

## Background

[[miss-golding|Irené Golding]] was the only child of [[mr-golding|Richard Golding]] and his late wife, [[mrs-golding|Irené Mascagni]], the daughter of an Italian count. Her mother had died before [[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]] came to England, and [[irene-golding|Irené]] was raised largely by her father.

> "Irené, only daughter of Richard Golding, of Langford Hall, Langford Cross, has been missing since Monday, September 20th." [007-002]

[[miss-golding|Miss Golding]] was described as having "a very hasty, imperious temper" [007-017] and had not always been on good terms with her father. This strained relationship formed an important backdrop to her disappearance.

## The Disappearance

[[miss-golding|Irené Golding]] left [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]] without notice and without taking any significant money or belongings. Her father, [[mr-golding|Richard Golding]], was initially reluctant to involve the authorities, preferring a [[investigation|private investigation]] through [[ebenezer-dyer|Ebenezer Dyer]]'s agency.

> "Mr. Golding will not have the slightest objection to a lady like yourself taking up the case." [007-006]

The circumstances were puzzling: no ransom demand was received, no signs of struggle were found, and [[irene-golding|Irené]]'s departure seemed to have been entirely voluntary — yet her whereabouts remained unknown.

## Investigation

[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]'s investigation led her to trace [[irene-golding|Irené]]'s maternal connections. She discovered that [[irene-golding|Irené]]'s mother, [[irene-mascagni|Irené Mascagni]], came from a notable Italian family and that [[miss-golding|Irené Golding]] had maintained ties to her mother's relatives unbeknownst to her father.

> "Irené Mascagni was a typical Italian beauty, with a voice of marvellous sweetness." [007-173]

Loveday followed a [[clue|trail]] that led from [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]] to connections abroad, ultimately discovering that [[irene-golding|Irené]] had sought refuge with her Italian relations in a dispute with her father over her future. The case required delicate handling, as [[mr-golding|Richard Golding]] had not known of his daughter's continued contact with her mother's family.

## Resolution

The case was resolved when [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] located [[miss-golding|Irené Golding]] staying with her Italian relatives and mediated between father and daughter. [[irene-golding|Irené]]'s disappearance was not a crime but an act of filial rebellion — she had fled to escape her father's control and to assert her connection to her late mother's heritage.

Loveday's diplomatic handling of the matter allowed the family to reconcile privately, avoiding public scandal and preserving the Golding family's [[respectability|reputation]]. The case exemplified [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]'s skill not only as a detective but as a mediator in sensitive family matters.
