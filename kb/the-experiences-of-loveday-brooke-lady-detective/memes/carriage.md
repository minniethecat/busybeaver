# Carriage

Carriages — both horse-drawn and railway — are recurring settings and plot devices across the Loveday Brooke stories. As a Victorian detective, [[loveday-brooke]] frequently travels by carriage, and several pivotal encounters take place within them.

## Key Appearances

### The Drive to Craigen Court

In "[[the-black-bag-left-on-a-door-step]]," Loveday Brooke travels by carriage towards Craigen Court: "She lay back in the carriage with closed eyes, and motionless" [001-047], mentally preparing for the undercover role she was about to assume.

### The Merstham Encounter

In "[[the-redhill-sisterhood]]," a significant encounter is planned for the railway journey: "He will get into your carriage at Merstham — the station before Redhill" [003-008]. This meeting aboard a moving train demonstrates how railway carriages served as convenient locations for discreet conversations and information exchanges in detective work.

### Arrival at New Assignments

Carriages consistently mark Loveday Brooke's arrivals at country estates and villages, signaling her transition from the [[lynch-court]] office in London to the field. The "fine carriage of the head and shoulders" [002-085] noted in descriptions of other characters reflects the social significance of physical bearing associated with carriage travel.

## Significance

Beyond mere transport, carriages in the Loveday Brooke stories function as liminal spaces — neither London nor the country estate, neither office nor crime scene — where the detective can be alone with her thoughts or strategically encounter others.
