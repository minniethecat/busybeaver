## Overview

Observation is the detective's fundamental faculty of close and systematic scrutiny of persons, places, and details. In [[the-experiences-of-loveday-brooke-lady-detective|the Loveday Brooke stories]], observation is distinguished from mere seeing: it is an active, trained skill that allows the detective to notice what others overlook and to draw inferences from seemingly trivial details.

## In the Stories

[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]'s observational powers are demonstrated throughout the stories. She possesses "so good an eye for an outline" that she can recognize a man she glimpsed briefly on a railway siding even when he has changed his clothes and appearance — identifying him by "the carriage of the head and shoulders."[PN=003-035]

Her method involves patient watching. During the [[redhill|Redhill]] investigation, she sat "with her lamp turned out and safely screened from observation, until close upon midnight, with eyes fixed upon numbers [[paved-court|7 and 8 Paved Court]]," maintaining a vigil to gather intelligence on the suspected [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]].[PN=003-039]

Loveday's approach to observation is also tactical — she knows when to observe directly and when to use a deputy. When she realises she is herself being watched, she recruits a local man to conduct observations on her behalf, instructing him to "watch as closely as possible the movements of these Sisters and report them to me at the hotel."[PN=003-070]
