# Drawn Daggers Incident

The **Drawn Daggers incident** was a series of threatening letters sent to the [[mr-hawke|Rev. Anthony Hawke]], featuring ink drawings of [[the-daggers|daggers]]. The incident was investigated by [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] alongside [[theft-of-miss-monroes-necklace|the theft of Miss Monroe's diamond necklace]] in "[[drawn-daggers|Drawn Daggers]]."

## The Threatening Letters

Two separate envelopes arrived at [[mr-hawke|Mr. Hawke]]'s [[london|London]] address, both bearing Cork and [[london|London]] postmarks and addressed in a "round, school-boyish, copybook sort of hand — the hand so easy to write and so difficult to bring home to any writer on account of its want of individuality" [005-019].

The first envelope contained a single dagger drawing. The second, arriving later, contained two [[the-daggers|daggers]]. Each was roughly six inches in length with remarkably pointed blades.

> "On this sheet of paper were roughly drawn, in ink, two daggers, about six inches in length, with remarkably pointed blades." [005-015]

## Investigation

[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] examined both drawings with a magnifying glass and made a critical observation: the [[the-daggers|daggers]] were drawn by different hands.

> "Dagger number one was, evidently, drawn by a timid, uncertain and inartistic hand — see how the lines wave and how they have been patched here and there. The person who drew the other daggers, I should say, could do better work; the outline, though rugged, is bold and free." [005-022]

This forensic analysis of the drawings was characteristic of Loveday's methodical approach. It suggested that at least two people were involved — one who drew the first dagger (possibly the actual conspirator) and another who drew the subsequent ones (possibly an accomplice).

[[mr-hawke|Mr. Hawke]] initially feared the [[the-daggers|daggers]] came from an Irish secret society, given the Cork postmark. [[ebenezer-dyer|Mr. Dyer]] dismissed this theory: "Members of secret societies generally make pretty sure of their ground before they send out missives of this kind." [005-026]

## Connection to the Necklace Theft

[[ebenezer-dyer|Mr. Dyer]] initially believed the dagger episode and the necklace [[robbery|theft]] were separate: "I am inclined to believe that when we have gone a little further into the matter we shall find that each circumstance belongs to a different group of facts." [005-028]

[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] disagreed. She recognized that the [[the-daggers|daggers]] — "drawn in a double sense" [005-028] — were integrally connected to the [[robbery|theft]]. The drawings were a deliberate distraction, designed to create confusion and redirect suspicion from the actual crime.

## Resolution

The dagger drawings were ultimately traced to individuals within [[mr-hawke|Mr. Hawke]]'s own household, including his nephew Jack. Loveday identified that the [[the-daggers|daggers]] were sent as part of the conspiracy to steal [[miss-monroe|Miss Monroe]]'s necklace — creating fear and confusion while the real crime was carried out.

> "I think I have to thank those 'drawn daggers,' that caused you so much concern." [005-120]

The incident was resolved when the necklace was recovered and the conspirators exposed. Loveday's insight — that the obvious explanation (a secret society) was the wrong one — proved correct: "Sometimes the explanation that is obvious is the one to be rejected, not accepted." [005-002]
