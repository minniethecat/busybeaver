## Overview

Wootton Hall was a country house in the neighbourhood of [[redhill|Redhill, Surrey]]. It had lately changed hands and been rebuilt, resulting in a warren of "complicated passages and corridors." The house was noted for its "splendid family plate in daily use," which was "left entirely to the care of the butler."[PN=003-020]

## In the Stories

Wootton Hall appeared in Chapter 3, "[[the-black-bag|The Black Bag]] Left on a Door-Step." [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] identified it as a potential target for [[the-redhill-sisterhood|the Redhill Sisterhood]] [[robbery|burglary]] ring, marking it as "tempting" in a burglar's assessment. She noted critically that the master of Wootton Hall appeared to trust his complicated passages to preserve his plate, observing that "a dismissed dishonest servant would supply a dozen maps of the place for half-a-sovereign."[PN=003-020]

[[miss-annie-lee|Annie Lee]] served as a nursery governess at Wootton Hall before joining the [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Redhill Sisterhood]]. It was from Wootton Hall that [[mrs-copeland|Mrs. Copeland]] wrote to [[miss-annie-lee|Annie]]'s fiancé, informing him that [[miss-annie-lee|Annie]] had broken off their engagement and joined the [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]].[PN=003-059][PN=003-061]
