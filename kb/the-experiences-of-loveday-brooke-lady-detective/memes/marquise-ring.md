# The Marquise Ring

The marquise ring worn by [[irene-golding|Irené Golding]] is the crucial piece of physical evidence in [[ch07-missing|Missing!]], the final [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] story. Described in the [[reward-handbill|reward handbill]] as "on the third finger of left hand a marquise ring consisting of one large diamond surrounded by small ones" (007-002), the ring's disappearance from [[irene-golding|Irené]]'s body becomes the clue that transforms Loveday's understanding of the case from a missing-person investigation to a [[murder]] [[investigation|inquiry]].

## The Ring's Disappearance

When [[miss-golding|Irené Golding]]'s body is eventually discovered, Loveday notes "this notable exception — the marquise ring has disappeared from the third finger" (007-130). The ring's absence is inconsistent with accidental death or suicide; someone removed it, and the removal points to a motive beyond the mere fact of death. The marquise ring — a distinctive and valuable piece — becomes the silent witness to a crime that might otherwise have passed as misadventure.

## Evidentiary Significance

The ring's disappearance serves multiple narrative functions. It confirms Loveday's suspicion that [[irene-golding|Irené]]'s death was not accidental. It provides a material link between the victim and her killer — someone who had both access to the body and a motive for taking the ring. And it exemplifies Loveday's [[detective-methodology|investigative method]]: her attention to what is absent, not merely what is present. The missing ring is more informative than any piece of evidence found at the scene.
