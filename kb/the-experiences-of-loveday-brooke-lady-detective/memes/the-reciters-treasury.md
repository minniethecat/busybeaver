# The Reciter's Treasury

*The Reciter's Treasury* is a book that appears in [[ch01-the-black-bag-left-on-a-doorstep|The Black Bag Left on a Doorstep]], the first [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] story. Described as a volume "in paper boards, and entitled, *The Reciter's Treasury*. It was published at the low price of a shilling" (001-046), the book serves as a key prop in Loveday's [[disguise|undercover]] investigation at [[craigen-court|Craigen Court]].

## Role in the Investigation

Loveday uses *The Reciter's Treasury* as part of her [[disguise|disguise]] when infiltrating the household at [[craigen-court|Craigen Court]]. Posing as a governess or lady's companion, she carries the cheap volume as a prop of respectability — the kind of improving but affordable literature a young woman of modest means might possess. When the moment comes to reveal her true identity, Loveday takes "from her cloak-pocket The Reciter's Treasury, and handed it to her companion" (001-164), using the book as a signal or container for her investigative findings.

The choice of this particular volume is characteristic of Loveday's [[detective-methodology|methodology]] — she selects objects that are ordinary enough to escape notice but distinctive enough to serve a purpose when required. A shilling book would have been familiar to every class of reader in the 1890s, making it the perfect camouflage for a detective who must move through different social worlds.

## Victorian Cheap Literature

Published at a shilling, *The Reciter's Treasury* belongs to the vast market of cheap print that flourished in late Victorian Britain. The title suggests a collection of pieces suitable for recitation — a popular form of domestic entertainment in an era before radio and recorded sound. Such volumes typically contained poetry, dramatic monologues, and improving prose selections, marketed to the aspirational working and lower-middle classes. Loveday's possession of the book would have signalled to observers that she was a respectable young woman of modest but decent tastes — precisely the impression she wished to create at [[craigen-court|Craigen Court]].
