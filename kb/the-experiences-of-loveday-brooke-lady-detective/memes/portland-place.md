## Overview

Portland Place is a grand thoroughfare in the Marylebone district of [[london|London]], notable in [[the-experiences-of-loveday-brooke-lady-detective|the Loveday Brooke stories]] as the destination of a twenty-minute drive from [[lynch-court|Lynch Court]] during the investigation in "[[a-princesss-vengeance|A Princess's Vengeance]]." [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] walks its length as part of her [[disguise|undercover]] work, using the street's scale to observe and be observed.

## Location

Portland Place is situated in Marylebone, central [[london|London]]. It is described as being a twenty-minute carriage drive from [[lynch-court|Lynch Court, Fleet Street]] — a journey that takes [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] across the breadth of central [[london|London]].

> "It is about twenty minutes' drive from here to Portland Place." [004-032]

> "The twenty minutes' drive from Lynch Court to Portland Place." [004-033]

## Description

Portland Place is one of the widest streets in central [[london|London]], lined with grand Georgian townhouses. [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] walks its length "in leisurely fashion," suggesting a broad, unhurried thoroughfare suitable for a lady to walk unaccompanied.

> "She walked the length of Portland Place in leisurely fashion, and then retraced her steps." [004-103]

A cab driver is instructed to deliver an item at Portland Place on his return journey, indicating the street is a recognized [[london|London]] address on common cab routes.

## Significance

Portland Place is one of several [[london|London]] streets that appear in [[the-experiences-of-loveday-brooke-lady-detective|the Loveday Brooke stories]], each serving as a stage for different types of investigatory activity. Unlike [[lynch-court|Lynch Court]] (the agency's home) or [[fleet-street|Fleet Street]] (its broader context), Portland Place functions as a setting for field work — observation, sur[[disguise|veil]]lance, and the visible presence of the detective in the world she investigates.
