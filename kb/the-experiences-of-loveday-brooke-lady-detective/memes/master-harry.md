# Master Harry

Master Harry Craven is the young son of [[mr-craven]] and the grandson of the murdered lodge-keeper, Sandy. His baffling illness forms a key element of the investigation at Troyte's Hill in "[[the-murder-at-troyte-s-hill]]" [002-004].

## Role in the Story

Master Harry is confined to a sickroom at Troyte's Hill during the events of the second story. As a schoolboy, he had defied the lodge-keeper Sandy and threatened him [002-016], establishing a potential motive that initially draws suspicion. [[loveday-brooke]] is assigned to wait upon "Madam and Master Harry" [002-059, 002-060] as part of her undercover investigation, placing her directly in the household.

## The Illness

The nature of Master Harry's illness is ambiguous — the household treats it as a serious malady requiring constant attendance, but Loveday Brooke's investigation reveals that the sickroom serves as a strategic position from which to observe the household's movements. Loveday Brooke notes her curiosity "to learn how Master Harry will get over the fact" [002-117] that certain events have transpired.

## Family

Master Harry is the son of [[mr-craven]] and grandson of Sandy, the murdered lodge-keeper. The Cravens took possession of Troyte's Hill and Master Harry spent his childhood there [002-016].
