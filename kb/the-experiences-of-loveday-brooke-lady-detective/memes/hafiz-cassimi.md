Hafiz Cassimi, commonly referred to as **[[mr-cassimi|Mr. Cassimi]]**, is the son of a Turkish-Egyptian diplomatic attaché who appears in the fourth story. He is a romantic rival to [[princess-dullah-veih|Princess Dullah-Veih]] and is regarded with suspicion and dislike by several characters.

## Role in the Stories

[[mr-cassimi|Mr. Cassimi]] is introduced early in the fourth story as the focus of [[princess-dullah-veih|Princess Dullah-Veih]]'s attention — and wrath:

> "At the present moment the Princess's displeasure would seem to centre solely upon a certain Hafiz Cassimi, son of the Turkish-Egyptian diplomatic attaché." [004-013]

According to [[ebenezer-dyer|Dyer]], young [[mr-cassimi|Cassimi]] is reputed to be untrustworthy:

> "According to him, young Cassimi is a thoroughgoing Iago, and has completely fooled the Princess." [004-015]

He is under the guardianship of his elder relative, the elder [[mr-cassimi|Cassimi]], arranged by her relatives.

## Characteristics

[[mr-cassimi|Mr. Cassimi]] is described as wearing a turban or fez cap and standing out among the European guests. During a dinner scene, [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] observes him closely and forms a negative impression:

> "All the time we have been dining I have been studying Mr. Cassimi, and I must admit I do not like his expression." [004-061]

He engages in conversation with [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] during the dinner, unaware that he is a suspect in her investigation. [[major-druce|Major Druce]] refers to him contemptuously as "that beast [[mr-cassimi|Cassimi]]" [004-108].
