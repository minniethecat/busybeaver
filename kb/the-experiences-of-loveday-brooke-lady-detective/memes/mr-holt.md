Mr. Holt is a young farmer living near Cathrow Court and one of the suitors of [[stephanie-delcroix|Stephanie Delcroix]] in "[[the-black-bag|The Black Bag]] Left on a Door-Step" (Story 1). Though initially dismissed as irrelevant to the investigation, he proves to be the one who saves [[stephanie-delcroix|Stephanie]]'s life.

## Character

Detective [[mr-bates|Bates]] describes Holt as "one of the girl's lovers, a young farmer of the name of Holt; but as he seems to be an honest, respectable young fellow, and entirely above suspicion, the discovery does not count for much" [001-044]. This assessment — that an honest suitor is not worth investigating — reflects the limitations of conventional police reasoning that Loveday consistently transcends.

[[mr-bates|Bates]] later adds: "He is one of [[stephanie-delcroix|Stephanie]]'s lovers, and I should imagine about the best of the lot. But he does not appear to be first favourite; from what I hear someone else must have made the running on the sly" [001-061]. Since the robbery, [[stephanie-delcroix|Stephanie]] had been giving him "the cold shoulder."

## The Rescue

When [[stephanie-delcroix|Stephanie]] disappears from Cathrow Court, Loveday takes an action that no one else thought to: she "dispatched a messenger in another direction — to young Mr. Holt, at his farm, giving him particulars of the girl's disappearance" [001-121].

Her instinct proves correct. Holt finds [[stephanie-delcroix|Stephanie]] before the night is over, "half dead with cold and fright, on the verge of the stream running through Craigen Wood." [[mrs-williams|Mrs. Williams]] reports that "at the last moment her courage failed her, and instead of throwing herself into the stream, she sank down, half-fainting, beside it" [001-179]. Holt takes her "straight home to his mother, and there, at the farm, she is now, being taken care of and petted generally by everyone" [001-179].

## Significance

Mr. Holt represents the decent, overlooked suitor whose genuine care for [[stephanie-delcroix|Stephanie]] contrasts with the criminal exploitation by [[harry-emmett|Harry Emmett]]. Loveday's decision to alert Holt — rather than relying solely on police channels — demonstrates her understanding that personal knowledge and emotional connections are often more effective than official investigation.
