# Harry Emmett

Harry Emmett is a key figure in the [[craigen-court|Craigen Court]] [[robbery|jewel robbery]] — a handsome young footman whose charm and good looks mask his role as an accomplice to a professional criminal gang. His arrest, orchestrated by [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]], marks the culmination of the first story's investigation and demonstrates Loveday's ability to trace criminal networks through their weakest links.

## Role in the Stories

Emmett appears only in "[[the-black-bag|The Black Bag]] Left on a Doorstep," where he serves as the inside accomplice who enables the [[robbery|jewel robbery]] at [[craigen-court|Craigen Court]]. Formerly a [[london|London]] cab driver, he had "taken it into his head to be a gentleman's servant, and great satisfaction he gave as such" (001-085). His charm made him popular — "he was always such a bright, handsome young fellow and so full of fun, that everyone liked him" (001-085) — but this popularity was precisely what made him useful to the criminal gang that planned the robbery.

## Key Appearances

Emmett's role is revealed gradually. He is initially mentioned as a footman who flirted with all the maids but "was sweetheart to none" (001-085). His father "drove a cab in [[london|London]], and Harry for a time did so also" (001-085) — a detail that connects him to the wider criminal network. Loveday traces the connection between Emmett and the [[the-black-bag|black bag]] left on a doorstep, establishing "the most important link in my [[evidence|chain of evidence]] — how Emmett brought his clerical friend into the business" (001-167).

## Biography

Emmett's biography is one of social aspiration turned to criminal purpose. From a cab-driving background in [[london|London]], he reinvented himself as a gentleman's servant — a role that gave him access to the country houses whose valuables his [[accomplice|criminal associate]]s targeted. His relationship with [[stephanie-delcroix|Stephanie Delcroix]], [[stephanie-delcroix|the French maid]], provided additional inside access and a potential scapegoat when the robbery was discovered.

## Arrest and Aftermath

Loveday's arrest of Emmett is carefully staged. She tells [[ebenezer-dyer|Dyer]]: "I did not, however, see why the [[lynch-court|Lynch Court]] office should not have the credit of the thing, and so telegraphed to you to conduct the arrest" (001-141). When confronted, Emmett finds "himself in the centre of three stalwart individuals, one of whom informed him that he held in his hand a warrant for the arrest of Harry Emmett on the charge of complicity in the [[the-black-bag-robbery|Craigen Court robbery]]" (001-148).
