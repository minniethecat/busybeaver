## Overview

Maria Lisle was a tall, gaunt woman whose spectral appearances in the garden of a [[brighton|Brighton]] cottage played a key role in the [[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|Fountain Lane ghost]] hoax investigated by [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] in Chapter 6, "[[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|The Ghost of Fountain Lane]]."[PN=006-069]

## Role in the Stories

Maria Lisle was the former servant of old [[mrs-turner|Mrs. Turner]]. She was described as "a tall, gaunt, black figure" whose movements through the garden of the cottage where Loveday was staying aroused suspicion.[PN=006-081] Her habit of passing through the garden at dusk, turning "the corner at the farther end of the tall hedge of laurels," gave her an almost ghostly quality.[PN=006-083]

The old summerhouse in the garden, "never entered by anyone save Maria Lisle," was kept in "unswept, undusted condition" save for one chair, suggesting her secret use of the space.[PN=006-086] The explanation for her evening solitude lay in her connection to the larger [[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|ghost hoax]].[PN=006-092]
