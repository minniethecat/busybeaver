# Wesleyan Chapel, Gordon Street

The Wesleyan chapel in Gordon Street, [[brighton|Brighton]], is a Nonconformist place of worship that serves as the institutional anchor for the criminal conspiracy at the heart of [[ch06-the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|The Ghost of Fountain Lane]]. [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]'s investigation traces the threads of the [[fraud|cheque forgery]] back to this chapel, where [[richard-steele|Richard Steele]], a former Wesleyan minister, had gathered a circle of accomplices under the cover of religious fellowship.

## Richard Steele's Ministry

[[richard-steele|Richard Steele]] had been "late Wesleyan minister of a chapel in Gordon Street, [[brighton|Brighton]]" (006-101) before his involvement in the cheque forgery scheme. The chapel provided Steele with a position of trust and moral authority in the community — a position he exploited to recruit confederates and orchestrate the ghostly [[fraud|deception]] at East Downes vicarage. Loveday eventually secures three [[warrant|warrants]]: one for [[maria-lisle|Maria Lisle]], one for Steele, and one for a third accomplice (006-101).

The discovery that Steele had been a preacher adds a layer of moral complexity to the case. As Loveday's attendant observes, "he had at one time been a Wesleyan preacher in the chapel in Gordon Street" (006-133), and Loveday herself later "heard Steele pronounce a fervid eulogy" (006-144) on the very scheme he was perpetrating — a testament to his talent for exploiting religious rhetoric in the service of [[fraud|criminal enterprise]].

## The Chapel in Brighton's Religious Landscape

The Wesleyan chapel's significance in the story is partly demographic. As Loveday notes, "no Dissenting chapel was to be found in the village" (006-064) of East Downes, but [[brighton|Brighton]] — a short distance away — had both "a church at one end and a chapel at the other" (006-065). The chapel served a distinct social class in Victorian [[brighton|Brighton]]: the [[mrs-brown|Mrs. Brown]] who provides Loveday with key information describes herself and her neighbours as belonging "to the Wesleyan" (006-046), indicating the chapel's role as a marker of community identity as much as religious practice.

## The Chapel as Criminal Network

The Gordon Street chapel functions in the story as what modern criminology would recognize as a criminal network embedded within a legitimate institution. Steele's position as minister gave him access to a congregation of trusting individuals, some of whom — like [[maria-lisle|Maria Lisle]] — became his accomplices. The chapel's prayer-meetings and social gatherings provided cover for conspiratorial communication. "A visit to the Wesleyan chapel in Gordon Street" (006-135) is the investigative step that allows Loveday to reconstruct the full extent of the conspiracy.
