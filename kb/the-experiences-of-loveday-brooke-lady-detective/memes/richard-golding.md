Richard Golding, commonly referred to as **[[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]]**, is the owner of [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]] at [[langford-cross|Langford Cross]] and the father of [[irene-golding|Irené Golding]]. He is a central figure in the seventh story, where his daughter's mysterious disappearance brings him to engage [[ebenezer-dyer|Ebenezer Dyer]]'s detective agency.

## Role in the Stories

[[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]] appears solely in the final story. His daughter [[irene-golding|Irené]] vanishes from [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]] on September 20th without a trace. Desperate, he seeks the help of [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] through [[ebenezer-dyer|Dyer]]'s agency. Throughout the investigation, he is portrayed as a man under immense strain.

> "I want to get you to take up the case. Mr. Golding will not have the slightest objection." [007-006]

[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] is struck by his appearance when she first visits [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]]:

> "Mr. Golding greeted her warmly. One glance at his face showed Loveday that the rumours she had heard at the Hall, namely, that Mr. Golding is keeping up with great difficulty a depleted estate, were not without foundation." [007-042]

He reveals to Loveday that his late wife died before he came to England, and that he had kept this fact from [[irene-golding|Irené]] — a secret that proves central to the case's resolution.

## Characteristics

[[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]] is depicted as anxious, agitated, and prone to irritability under stress. His emotional state deteriorates as the investigation proceeds:

> "Mr. Golding's agitation was pitiable." [007-068]

He is deeply attached to his daughter but has concealed significant truths about her origins. Despite his financial difficulties maintaining [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]], he gave [[irene-golding|Irené]] a liberal allowance.
