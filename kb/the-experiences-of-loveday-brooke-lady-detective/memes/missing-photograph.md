# The Missing Photograph

The missing photograph of [[lucie-cunier|Lucie Cunier]] is a pivotal piece of evidence in [[ch04-a-princesss-vengeance|A Princess's Vengeance]], the fourth [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] story. The photograph — described as "a photograph of the missing girl" (004-006) — vanishes under [[suspicion|suspicious circumstances]], and its disappearance becomes central to Loveday's understanding of the case.

## The Theft

The photograph's disappearance is immediately recognized as significant. Loveday concludes that the "photograph must have been stolen by some one in the house" (004-008), narrowing the field of suspects to those with access to the private rooms where it was kept. The [[robbery|theft]] of a photograph — an object of sentimental rather than financial value — signals that the crime is personal rather than mercenary.

## Hafiz Cassimi's Involvement

[[suspicion|Suspicion]] falls on [[hafiz-cassimi|Hafiz Cassimi]], a Turkish-Egyptian visitor to [[london|London]]. "[[mr-cassimi|Cassimi]], he says, has stolen the photograph" (004-015), and the accusation links the photograph's [[robbery|theft]] to the wider plot involving [[princess-dullah-veih|Princess Dullah-Veih]] and the [[harem-mission|Harem Mission]]. Loveday's later reflection — "I wish it had been [[mr-cassimi|Cassimi]]" (004-141) — hints at the photograph's role in a more complex chain of events than the obvious suspect theory would suggest.

## Photographs as Evidence

The missing photograph is one of several instances in [[the-experiences-of-loveday-brooke-lady-detective|the Loveday Brooke stories]] where [[photograph|photographic evidence]] plays a role in investigation. In an era before widespread amateur photography, a portrait photograph was a rare and valuable item — its [[robbery|theft]] was not merely the loss of an image but the destruction of a unique record of a person's appearance.
