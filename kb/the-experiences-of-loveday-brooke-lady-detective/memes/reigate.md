## Overview

Reigate is a town in [[north-cape|Surrey]], notable in [[the-experiences-of-loveday-brooke-lady-detective|the Loveday Brooke stories]] as the seat of the police jurisdiction that covers [[redhill|Redhill]] and the surrounding district. [[inspector-gunning|Inspector Gunning]] of the Reigate police is a recurring contact for [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] and [[ebenezer-dyer|Ebenezer Dyer]], appearing in "[[the-redhill-sisterhood|The Redhill Sisterhood]]" and referenced in "[[the-black-bag-left-on-a-doorstep|The Black Bag Left on a Doorstep]]."

## Location

Reigate is situated in [[north-cape|Surrey]], south of [[london|London]]. Its police jurisdiction extends at least ten miles around, covering [[redhill|Redhill]] and the surrounding country houses. Reigate Hill is a local landmark mentioned in the narrative.

> "Redhill, I must tell you is in the Reigate police district." [003-004]

## Description

Reigate serves primarily as the administrative centre for local policing. [[inspector-gunning|Inspector Gunning]] operates from Reigate, dispatching notes and receiving reports there. The town is also the site of a notable earlier robbery referenced in the first story, where [[ebenezer-dyer|Dyer]]'s agency had assisted.

> "In that robbery at Reigate, the other day, they went to the police and told them." [001-025]

The surrounding countryside includes Reigate Hill:

> "When the moon went down behind Reigate Hill an exciting scene was enacted." [003-140]

## Significance

Reigate represents the local police infrastructure that [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] must navigate in her investigations. Unlike [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] — the metropolitan force — Reigate is a provincial police jurisdiction, headed by a capable but sometimes outmatched inspector. The relationship between [[ebenezer-dyer|Dyer]]'s [[london|London]]-based agency and the Reigate police exemplifies the collaborative (and sometimes competitive) dynamic between private detection and public law enforcement in Victorian England.
