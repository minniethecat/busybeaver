# Redhill Sisterhood Burglaries

The **[[redhill|Redhill]] Sisterhood burglaries** were a series of [[robbery|robberies]] and attempted burglaries in the [[redhill|Redhill]] district of [[north-cape|Surrey]], investigated by [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] in "[[the-redhill-sisterhood|The Redhill Sisterhood]]." The case was notable for the unusual suspicion directed at a recently founded undenominational religious [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]].

## Background

Inspector [[inspector-gunning|Gunning]] of the [[reigate|Reigate]] police brought the case to [[ebenezer-dyer|Ebenezer Dyer]]'s attention. A pattern of country-house burglaries had developed in the district surrounding [[redhill|Redhill]], and [[inspector-gunning|Gunning]] suspected a link to a newly established [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]].

> "The whole neighbourhood has been terrorised and startled by a series of the most daring and successful burglaries committed in the district for ten miles round Redhill, and every country house of any pretension whatsoever has been laid under contribution." [003-018]

## The Sisterhood

The [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]] was an undenominational religious community recently founded at [[redhill|Redhill]] with three members. They traveled the district using a donkey-cart to distribute charitable aid, which gave them knowledge of country houses.

> "They call themselves an undenominational Sisterhood, and say they are connected with no religious body whatsoever." [003-016]

[[inspector-gunning|Inspector Gunning]] noted that "attempts at [[robbery|burglary]] have followed their footsteps wherever they have gone" [003-016]. [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] undertook an [[disguise|undercover]] investigation, taking lodgings near the [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]] and observing their movements.

## Investigation

Loveday pursued the personal histories of the Sisters rather than focusing on direct sur[[disguise|veil]]lance. She discovered that one Sister, [[mrs-copeland|Mrs. Copeland]], had been a domestic servant who left her position to join the [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]] shortly after a [[robbery|burglary]] at her former employer's house.

> "Mrs. Copeland gave up her situation and joined some Sisterhood, and that she, Mrs. Copeland, is now supposed to be working amongst the London poor." [003-061]

Through examination of postal communications intercepted by [[inspector-gunning|Inspector Gunning]], Loveday identified the true perpetrators: a criminal gang using the [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]] as cover.

## Resolution

The case was resolved when [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]], working with [[inspector-gunning|Inspector Gunning]], coordinated a police operation that captured the burglars in the act. The [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]] was largely exonerated, though the episode exposed the vulnerability of loosely organized religious communities to criminal exploitation.
