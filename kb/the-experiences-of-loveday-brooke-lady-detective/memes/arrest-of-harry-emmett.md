# Arrest of Harry Emmett

The **arrest of [[harry-emmett|Harry Emmett]]** was the climactic event of [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]'s investigation into [[the-black-bag-robbery|the Craigen Court robbery]] in "[[the-black-bag-left-on-a-doorstep|The Black Bag Left on a Doorstep]]." [[harry-emmett|Harry Emmett]], a footman employed at [[craigen-court|Craigen Court]], was arrested on a warrant charging him with complicity in the robbery.

## Background

[[harry-emmett|Harry Emmett]] was a footman at [[craigen-court|Craigen Court]], the country seat of [[sir-george-cathrow|Sir George Cathrow]]. He was also known as a "reciter" — a performer of dramatic and comic pieces — and was described as a "general favourite" among the servants.

> "Harry Emmett — footman, reciter, general favourite — had been the prime mover in the whole affair." [001-167]

[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] had been called to [[craigen-court|Craigen Court]] to investigate the [[robbery|theft]] of [[lady-cathrow|Lady Cathrow]]'s jewellery on [[christmas-eve-robbery-at-craigen-court|Christmas Eve]]. Her investigation gradually uncovered [[harry-emmett|Emmett]]'s role in the crime.

## The Arrest

The arrest was carried out with a warrant signed by a magistrate. [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] personally directed the apprehension, confronting [[harry-emmett|Emmett]] at [[craigen-court|Craigen Court]].

> "In his hand a warrant for the arrest of Harry Emmett on the charge of complicity in the Craigen Court robbery." [001-148]

The timing of the arrest — on Christmas Eve, during or immediately after the Cathrow family's Christmas dinner — added to its dramatic impact. The family and guests, who had "sat down to dinner on Christmas Eve" [001-169], were confronted with the revelation that one of their own servants was the perpetrator.

## Evidence

[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]'s case against [[harry-emmett|Harry Emmett]] was built on meticulous investigation. She had traced the [[the-black-bag|black bag]] found on a doorstep to [[harry-emmett|Emmett]]'s possession, and connected his movements on the night of the robbery to the crime.

> "Bearing in mind where the black bag was found, it was not difficult to connect Harry Emmett with its disposal." [001-175]

The evidence included the [[the-black-bag|black bag]] itself, [[harry-emmett|Emmett]]'s clothing which matched descriptions, and his access to the areas of [[craigen-court|Craigen Court]] where the robbery occurred. [[harry-emmett|Emmett]]'s accomplice was also identified.

## Resolution

[[harry-emmett|Harry Emmett]] was [[arrest|taken into custody]] and deposited "in the local jail" [001-154]. The [[jewellery|stolen jewellery]] was recovered, and the case was brought to a successful conclusion. [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]'s first recorded case established her [[respectability|reputation]] as a detective of exceptional skill and discretion.

The arrest was notable not only for the crime solved but for Loveday's method: she had identified the perpetrator through patient observation and logical deduction rather than dramatic confrontation, embodying the professional approach that would characterize her career.
