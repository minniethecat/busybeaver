## Overview

The Princess Dullah-Veih was a Turkish-Egyptian princess, described as "a beauty and a celebrity," who became entangled in the disappearance of [[lucie-cunier|Lucie Cunier]]. Her involvement with [[major-druce|Major Druce]] and the subsequent events form the central mystery of Chapter 4, "A Princess's Vengeance."[PN=004-011]

## Role in the Stories

The Princess appears in Chapter 4 as a figure of suspicion. [[ebenezer-dyer|Mr. Dyer]] notes that she "has something of a history," hinting at a scandalous past.[PN=004-020] She is romantically linked to [[major-druce|Major Druce]], whose engagement to her is described as having the potential "to crown his happiness if the Princess Dullah-Veih had not made it greatly otherwise."[PN=004-136]

## Key Appearances

- Chapter 4, "A Princess's Vengeance" — central figure in the [[disappearance-of-lucie-cunier|Lucie Cunier disappearance]] case[PN=004-011][PN=004-020][PN=004-136]

## Biography

The Princess was the daughter of a Turkish-Egyptian banker. She moved in high European society and was a figure of considerable public interest. Her engagement to [[major-druce|Major Druce]], the son of the widowed [[mrs-druce|Mrs. Druce]], became a matter of significant concern to the Major's mother.[PN=004-013][PN=004-011]
