Mr. Clampe is a [[brighton|Brighton]] innkeeper who brings a [[fraud|financial fraud]] case to the attention of [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] in "[[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|The Ghost of Fountain Lane]]." He also serves as her driver and local guide to the village of East Downes, where the central mystery of the story unfolds [006-003].

## Role in the Stories

Mr. Clampe serves a dual function in "[[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|The Ghost of Fountain Lane]]" — as the initial client who presents the cheque fraud case (a suspicious cheque bearing the penciled notation "144,000") and as Loveday's practical facilitator, driving her to East Downes in his high-wheeled dogcart [006-003]. His local knowledge and connections enable Loveday's initial placement at [[mrs-brown|Mrs. Brown]]'s cottage.

## Key Appearances

Mr. Clampe appears in the early chapters of "[[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|The Ghost of Fountain Lane]]":

- He arrives at Loveday's [[brighton|Brighton]] lodgings with a cheque case, having "had, in fact, to telegraph three times before I could get" her address from [[ebenezer-dyer|Ebenezer Dyer]] [006-003]
- He presents a cheque with the curious penciled notation "144,000" on the back, which catches Loveday's attention [006-019]
- He drives Loveday and an inspector to East Downes in his dogcart, during which journey Loveday asks about local ghost stories [006-030]
- He introduces Loveday to [[mrs-brown|Mrs. Brown]], securing her lodging at the cottage [006-038]
- He departs for the village inn with his dogcart, promising to call on Loveday the following morning [006-055]

## Biography

Mr. Clampe is an innkeeper in [[brighton|Brighton]], evidently of some standing and respectability. He has a professional connection to [[ebenezer-dyer|Ebenezer Dyer]]'s detective agency, though he had to telegraph three times to obtain Loveday's holiday address — suggesting both persistence and a degree of urgency about his business [006-003].

He owns a "first-rate little horse" and a high-wheeled dogcart, indicating a modest prosperity [006-037]. His knowledge of the local area extends beyond [[brighton|Brighton]] to the surrounding villages, including East Downes, where he knows the inhabitants well enough to arrange Loveday's lodging.

## Characteristics

Mr. Clampe is portrayed as a good-humoured, obliging, and somewhat deferential figure. He expresses being "awfully sorry to break into your holiday" when he finds Loveday surrounded by newspapers and reference books on what is supposedly a leisure trip [006-003]. He laughs heartily at Loveday's quips — "Ha, ha! Capital!" — and makes polite conversation about local matters during the drive to East Downes [006-037].

He is observant and practical: when Loveday expresses interest in ghost stories connected to the area, he obligingly shares what he knows. His manner toward [[mrs-brown|Mrs. Brown]] is described as whispering "a hint" as they approach the cottage — suggesting a conspiratorial familiarity with local village dynamics [006-053].

## Relationships

- **[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]** — the detective he engages for the cheque case and drives to East Downes
- **[[ebenezer-dyer|Ebenezer Dyer]]** — the agency head who provided Loveday's address
- **[[mrs-brown|Mrs. Brown]]** — the village woman whose cottage he recommends for Loveday's lodging
- **The [[brighton|Brighton]] inspector** — a police colleague who accompanies them on the drive

## Significance

Mr. Clampe serves as the narrative bridge between [[brighton|Brighton]] and the rural setting of East Downes, where the ghost mystery unfolds. His cheque case, with its cryptic "144,000" notation, provides the initial pretext for Loveday's involvement, while his practical assistance — the dogcart, the introduction to [[mrs-brown|Mrs. Brown]] — enables the investigation to begin. He represents the network of provincial contacts upon which a [[london|London]]-based detective agency relied for cases outside the metropolis.
