# The Ghost of Fountain Lane

**The Ghost of Fountain Lane** was a mysterious apparition that terrorized the inhabitants of a small turning off the main street in [[brighton|Brighton]], investigated by [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] in "[[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|The Ghost of Fountain Lane]]." The case linked a seemingly supernatural phenomenon to a fraudulent cheque scheme.

## Background

[[fountain-lane|Fountain Lane]] was a small turning leading off one of [[brighton|Brighton]]'s main thoroughfares. Its inhabitants were suddenly disturbed by the appearance of a ghostly figure, which caused widespread alarm and disrupted the lives of the residents.

> "The inhabitants of Fountain Lane, a small turning leading off the main street, were startled the other evening by the sudden appearance of a ghost in their midst." [006-012]

The ghost reportedly appeared at night, wearing what witnesses described as a soldierly uniform, and its appearances coincided with a deepening mystery that threatened property values in the lane: "the landlord of the [[fountain-lane|Fountain Lane]] houses would be in a state of misery if all his tenants decided to leave" [006-016].

## Investigation

[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] was brought into the case by [[mr-clampe|Mr. Clampe]], who was deeply interested in the ghost story. However, Loveday quickly recognized that the supernatural elements masked a more mundane crime.

> "I heard all about the Fountain Lane ghost yesterday, before I started for Brighton." [006-043]

Her investigation uncovered that the ghost was not a supernatural phenomenon but a deliberate hoax. The "soldierly character" [006-133] of the apparition was a key clue — it suggested the figure was a real person in costume rather than an ethereal spirit.

Loveday traced the ghost appearances to a scheme involving a stolen cheque. The ghost was being used to frighten residents and draw attention away from the financial crime being perpetrated in the neighborhood.

> "I want to know about the ghost; that's the point that interests me." [006-132]

## The Hoax Revealed

The investigation revealed that the ghost was a living person — an accomplice dressed in a military-style coat — whose appearances were timed to coincide with the movement of the stolen cheque through the banking system. The ghost story served as both a distraction and a means of explaining unusual nighttime activity in the lane.

Loveday noted the contagious nature of ghost sightings: "ghost-seeing is quite as catching as measles" [006-148], observing that once the first sighting was reported, subsequent witnesses were predisposed to interpret any unusual sight or sound as supernatural.

## Resolution

[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] exposed the hoax and recovered the stolen cheque. The "ghost" was identified and the conspirators were brought to justice. The residents of [[fountain-lane|Fountain Lane]] were relieved to learn that their supernatural terror had a thoroughly earthly explanation.

The case demonstrated Loveday's practical approach to seemingly inexplicable phenomena — rather than dismissing the ghost story, she investigated it methodically and uncovered the criminal enterprise it was designed to conceal.
