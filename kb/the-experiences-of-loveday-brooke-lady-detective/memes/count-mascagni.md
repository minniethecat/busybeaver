Count Mascagni is an Italian nobleman from [[alguida|Alguida]], a small hamlet in South Italy. Though he never appears in person in the narrative, his existence and identity are the crucial revelations that resolve the central mystery of the final story.

## Role in the Stories

Count Mascagni is revealed to be the biological father of [[irene-golding|Irené Golding]]. [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] discovers that [[richard-golding|Richard Golding]]'s late wife was, in fact, [[mrs-golding|Irené Mascagni]], daughter of Count Mascagni:

> "It was in connection with the Princess's affairs that I found out that Mr. Golding's wife, Irené, daughter of Count Mascagni, of Alguida, in South Italy, was still alive." [007-171]

The revelation explains [[irene-golding|Irené Golding]]'s disappearance: discovering that her supposed mother was still alive and that she was actually the daughter of an Italian count, [[irene-golding|Irené]] fled to her father.

> "It is most probable that Count Mascagni knew nothing whatever of his daughter's fate until she arrived at the Château Mascagni." [007-183]

The [[princess-dullah-veih|Princess Dullah-Veih]] had known the truth all along: [[mrs-golding|Irené Mascagni]] was "a typical Italian woman of an intensely jealous and passionate temperament" [007-173], who had abandoned her husband and child in Italy before [[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]] met her.
