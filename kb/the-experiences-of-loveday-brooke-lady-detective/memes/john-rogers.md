John Rogers is a former elder of [[wesleyan-chapel-gordon-street|the Wesleyan chapel]] in Gordon Street, [[brighton|Brighton]]. He appears as a suspect in the sixth story, where his sudden disappearance makes him the prime suspect in a cheque [[robbery|theft]] case that is intertwined with the [[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|Fountain Lane ghost]] hoax.

## Role in the Stories

John Rogers appears only in the sixth story, "[[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|The Ghost of Fountain Lane]]." Reverend [[rev-richard-steele|Richard Steele]], the Wesleyan minister, reveals that Rogers has absconded:

> "He was formerly Wesleyan minister of a chapel in Gordon Street, Brighton, and John Rogers, formerly elder of the same chapel, who had seceded from the Methodists and taken a house in Fountain Lane, and set up a sort of rival chapel on his own account, was continually threatening to expose some youthful indiscretion of Mr. Steele's." [006-101]

[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] initially suspects Rogers:

> "I very much fear that John Rogers has bolted," once Loveday whispered to me. [006-103]

However, Loveday later receives news that shifts suspicion away from Rogers:

> "A certain elder of their chapel, John Rogers by name, had seceded from the Wesleyans and set up a chapel of his own in Fountain Lane." [006-135]

It is ultimately revealed that Rogers was not the thief — the stolen cheque was taken by the very person who reported it, as part of an elaborate scheme involving the [[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|ghost hoax]].
