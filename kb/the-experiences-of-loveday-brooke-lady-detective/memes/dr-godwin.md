Dr. Godwin is the physician responsible for the care of [[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]] during his severe illness at [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]]. He appears only in "Missing!" (Story 7), where his daily visits provide the backdrop for [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]'s final interview with her client and the subsequent reunion with [[miss-ren|René Golding]].

## Medical Role

Dr. Godwin attends [[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]] daily during the latter's collapse following his daughter's disappearance. His diagnosis is sobering: while [[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]]'s bodily strength shows improvement, his mental condition is a "serious one," with his brain appearing "to be in a state of semi-stupefaction, which it was possible might be indicative of the softening of its tissues" [007-144].

This guarded prognosis shapes the urgency of Loveday's investigation. The doctor's daily call at a set hour provides the framework for Loveday to time her farewell visit:

> Loveday expressed a wish to see this doctor — to time her farewell visit to Mr. Golding on the following day with Dr. Godwin's daily call. In fact, she would like a little private talk with him before she went in to see his patient. [007-145]

## The Reunion Scene

Dr. Godwin is present at the climactic reunion when Loveday reveals that [[miss-ren|René]] is alive and has returned. When Loveday and the doctor enter the sickroom together, he dismisses the attending nurse so that Loveday may speak privately with the patient:

> "You need not remain," said Dr. Godwin to the nurse, who rose as they entered; and Loveday and the doctor were left alone with the patient. [007-152]

During the emotional scene that follows, Dr. Godwin provides medical support — when [[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]] tries to rise but his limbs fail him, the doctor "poured out a cordial, and put it to his lips" [007-166], then whispers to Loveday to tell her news quickly.

His role as gatekeeper to the patient is made explicit when Loveday announces that [[miss-ren|René]] waits outside:

> "She is only waiting for Dr. Godwin's permission to do so." [007-167]

However, the text notes that "time to grant or refuse that permission was not accorded to Dr. Godwin" [007-168] — [[miss-ren|René]] sweeps into the room before any formal consent can be given.

## Significance

Dr. Godwin functions as a narrative device: his daily call provides the scheduled moment around which Loveday plans her departure, and his medical authority lends gravity to [[mr-golding|Mr. Golding]]'s condition. His presence at the reunion also serves a practical function — ensuring the frail patient is medically supported through the shock of seeing his daughter again.
