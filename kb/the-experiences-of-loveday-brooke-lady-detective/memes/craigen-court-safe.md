# Craigen Court Safe

The safe in [[lady-cathrow|Lady Cathrow]]'s dressing-room at [[craigen-court|Craigen Court]] is the focal point of the [[robbery|theft]] investigation in [[ch01-the-black-bag-left-on-a-doorstep|The Black Bag Left on a Doorstep]]. It is from this safe that jewellery worth £30,000 was stolen, and the circumstances of the safe's access — whether it was locked or unlocked, who held the key — constitute the central forensic puzzle that [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] must unravel.

## The Key Question

The key to the safe was a point of routine vulnerability in the [[craigen-court|Craigen Court]] household. As Loveday learns, "the key of the safe was frequently left in the possession of the housekeeper" (001-009), who was responsible for securing [[lady-cathrow|Lady Cathrow]]'s jewellery each evening. The housekeeper's duty was to "put away any jewellery that might be lying about, lock the safe, and keep the key till her mistress required it again in the morning" (001-010). This domestic routine — a titled lady entrusting her valuables to a servant overnight — created the opportunity for the [[robbery|theft]].

## Scotland Yard's Theory

[[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]]'s investigators placed "much stress on the safe being left unlocked that night" (001-013) — a theory that implied carelessness on the part of the household staff. Loveday, however, questioned this interpretation. Her examination of the safe-door itself, and the message "written across it" (001-024), led her to a different conclusion. Where the police saw negligence, Loveday saw deliberate misdirection — the safe had been "closed" and the writing left upon it was itself a [[clue]].

## Loveday's Analysis

Loveday's attention to the safe-door inscription exemplifies her [[detective-methodology|methodology]]: she treats physical objects as texts to be read rather than mere evidence to be catalogued. The message on the safe — ostensibly left by the thief — contained information that, properly interpreted, pointed away from the obvious suspect and toward the true perpetrator. The safe thus functions in the story not merely as a container for valuables but as a surface for communication, misdirection, and ultimately revelation.
