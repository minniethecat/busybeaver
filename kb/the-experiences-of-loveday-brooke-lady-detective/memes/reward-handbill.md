# Reward Handbill (Irené Golding)

The reward handbill offering "Five Hundred Pounds Reward" for information about the disappearance of [[irene-golding|Irené Golding]] is the document that opens [[ch07-missing|Missing!]], the final [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] story. [[ebenezer-dyer|Ebenezer Dyer]] draws Loveday's attention to the case by "taking up a handbill that lay upon his writing-table" (007-001), and the document's contents set the investigation in motion.

## Contents and Purpose

The handbill announces the disappearance of [[miss-golding|Irené Golding]], a young woman who "was seen leaving the Park" (007-004) on a September afternoon and did not return. The offer of £500 — a substantial sum in the 1890s, equivalent to several years' wages for a working person — signals the seriousness of the case and the resources of the Golding family. [[dick-golding|Richard Golding]] of [[langford-hall|Langford Hall]], [[langford-cross|Leicestershire]], was clearly willing to spend heavily to recover his daughter.

The handbill represents a form of public appeal that was common in Victorian England before the advent of organized missing persons bureaus. Distributed in public places and printed in newspapers, such handbills combined the functions of a modern missing persons report, a wanted poster, and a classified advertisement.

## Dyer's Introduction

[[ebenezer-dyer|Dyer]]'s use of the handbill to introduce the case is characteristic of his relationship with Loveday: he presents her with the public face of a mystery — what the world knows — and trusts her to find what lies beneath. Loveday's immediate response to the handbill is professional skepticism. The document itself provides few [[clue|clues]], but it serves as the formal starting point for an investigation that will ultimately uncover a far more complex story than the simple disappearance it describes.
