# The Stolen Cheque

The **stolen cheque** was a [[fraud|fraudulent]] financial crime connected to the case of [[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|The Ghost of Fountain Lane]], investigated by [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]. A cheque belonging to the [[charles-clampe|Rev. Charles Clampe]] was stolen and presented at a bank, with the [[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|ghost hoax]] in [[brighton|Brighton]] serving as a distraction from the financial crime.

## Background

The Rev. Charles Clampe brought both a stolen cheque and a ghost story to [[ebenezer-dyer|Ebenezer Dyer]]'s agency. While [[mr-clampe|Mr. Clampe]] was primarily interested in the supernatural mystery, [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] recognized the cheque as the more significant element.

> "I brought the cheque with me, thinking you might like to see it." [006-017]

The cheque had been stolen and presented for payment at a bank by an unknown individual. The fraud was described as a "stupid, commonplace fraud" [006-014] — but its connection to the ghost of [[fountain-lane|Fountain Lane]] was anything but ordinary.

## The Fraud

The stolen cheque bore pencilled figures on its surface. When the gentleman "who presented the stolen cheque at [[west-sussex-bank|the Bank]] preferred payment in notes" [006-029], the transaction attracted attention and the fraud was discovered.

[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] noted the elaborateness of the scheme: "Except in its elaborateness it differed in no respect from other cases of stolen cheques" [006-060]. This elaborateness — the ghost, the cheque, the pencilled figures — was precisely what connected the financial crime to the supernatural hoax.

## Connection to the Ghost

[[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] traced the connection between the stolen cheque and the [[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|Fountain Lane ghost]]. The ghost appearances were timed to coincide with the movement of the cheque through the banking system, providing cover for the criminals and explaining unusual nighttime activity.

> "I had arrived so far in my ghost enquiry when you came to me, bringing the stolen cheque with its pencilled figures, 1, 2, 3." [006-135]

The pencilled figures on the cheque — 1, 2, 3 — matched a numbering system used by the conspirators to track their fraudulent transactions. Loveday recognized the pattern and connected it to the ghost scheme.

## Resolution

The stolen cheque was recovered and the fraud exposed as part of [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]]'s resolution of the wider [[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|Fountain Lane ghost]] case. The conspirators who had stolen the cheque were the same individuals operating the [[the-ghost-of-fountain-lane|ghost hoax]], and both crimes were unraveled together.

This case exemplified Loveday's investigative approach: while others were distracted by the sensational ghost story, she focused on the concrete financial evidence that ultimately broke the case.
