## Overview

Inspector Gunning is an officer of the [[reigate|Reigate]] police who appears in "The [[redhill|Redhill]] [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]]" [003] and is mentioned in "Drawn Daggers" [005]. He is described as "a tall, soldier-like individual" [003-010] who works in close coordination with [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] and [[ebenezer-dyer|Ebenezer Dyer]]'s detective agency.

## Role in "The Redhill Sisterhood"

Gunning is the officer who receives an application from a man named [[john-murray|John Murray]], who claims to be suspicious of a group of nuns renting his house in [[paved-court|Paved Court]], [[redhill|Redhill]] [003-004]. [[john-murray|Murray]] reports that "in whatever direction those Sisters turn the wheels of their donkey-cart, burglaries, or attempts at burglaries, are sure to follow" [003-004].

Recognizing the case requires specialized investigative work, Gunning "sent to me [[[ebenezer-dyer|Dyer]]] to secure your services" [003-004], bringing [[loveday-brooke|Loveday Brooke]] into the case. He meets Loveday on the train at [[merstham|Merstham]] — "he will get into your carriage at [[merstham|Merstham]] — the station before [[redhill|Redhill]] — if you will put your hand out of window, with the morning paper in it" [003-006] — a characteristic piece of clandestine police tradecraft.

## Operational Support

Gunning provides extensive logistical support for Loveday's [[disguise|undercover]] work:

- **Accommodation**: He arranges for her to use a room in a draper's shop facing the entrance to [[paved-court|Paved Court]], with a cover story as "a nursery governess on the lookout for a situation" [003-006]
- **Communications**: He "had been careful to have posted to her" letters to maintain her cover identity [003-037]
- **Intelligence**: He provides a specially drawn map covering "the district for ten miles round [[redhill|Redhill]]" with "every country house of any importance marked in red ink" [003-044]
- **Background**: He explains the [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]]'s unusual character — they "call themselves 'undenominational,'" "attend sometimes one place of worship, sometimes another, sometimes none at all" [003-034]

## Character

Gunning is a pragmatic and observant officer. His character assessment of [[sister-monica|Sister Monica]] — "of all the lowest criminal types of faces I have ever seen, I think hers is about the lowest and most repulsive" [003-034] — proves dramatically wrong, as the [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]] is ultimately exonerated.

He is notably annoyed when his operational precautions are compromised — when Loveday spots a suspicious workman near the railway, Gunning is "much annoyed at the circumstance" and reroutes their journey to maintain her cover [003-030].

## Outcome

Despite Gunning's initial suspicions of the [[the-redhill-sisterhood|Sisterhood]], Loveday's investigation reveals the true criminals to be [[john-murray|John Murray]] and the Lee family, who had been using the nuns as a smokescreen for their own [[robbery|burglary]] operations. The final report notes that "great credit is due to Inspector Gunning and his skilled coadjutors for the vigilance and promptitude they have displayed throughout the affair" [003-142].

## Mention in "Drawn Daggers"

In "Drawn Daggers" [005], Gunning is referenced in passing when Loveday threatens [[mr-hawke|Mr. Hawke]] that she will "at once take a note from me to Inspector Gunning at [[reigate|Reigate]]" if he does not cooperate [005-120].
