# The Close

The Close is the cathedral close of Wrychester, a gated precinct surrounding [[Wrychester Cathedral]]. It serves as a key setting in *The Paradise Mystery*, housing the Deanery and several wealthy residents, and providing the backdrop for many of the story's pivotal scenes.

## Description

The Close is entered through a "ruinous gateway" (001-001) that admits visitors from the town into the cathedral precinct. Within its walls are gardens with trees and shrubs (001-002), the Deanery (003-015), and residences of the Close's affluent inhabitants (002-035). The south wall of the Cathedral bounds one side of the Close (003-014), and an archway leads from the Cathedral into the Close near the Deanery (003-015).

> American tourists, sure appreciators of all that is ancient and picturesque in England, invariably come to a halt, holding their breath in a sudden catch of wonder, as they pass through the half-ruinous gateway which admits to the Close of Wrychester. Nowhere else in England is there a fairer prospect of old-world peace.
>
> (001-001)

## Role in the Story

The Close features prominently in the early chapters of the novel. It is here, in a corner of the Close garden, that three people sit at breakfast in the opening scene (001-002). [[Pemberton Bryce]] is seen crossing the Close (002-073) and walking out into it during his investigation (003-029). The Close's gateway serves as a point of entry and exit for several characters (003-052), and its residents—described as having "nice, inquisitive" habits—form part of the social fabric of Wrychester (002-035).

## Associated Characters

- [[Pemberton Bryce]] — frequently crosses and walks through the Close during his investigation
- [[collishaw|Mr. Collishaw]] — a wealthy resident of the Close (002-070)
- The Dean — resides at [[the Deanery]], located within the Close (003-015)

## Location Context

The Close occupies a central position in Wrychester, adjacent to the Cathedral on one side and connected to the town through its gateway. It sits within the broader geography of Wrychester that includes [[the Mitre Hotel]], [[Paradise]], and [[St. Wrytha's Stair]], all within walking distance. As the ecclesiastical heart of the town, the Close and its residents represent the established order against which the novel's mystery unfolds.
