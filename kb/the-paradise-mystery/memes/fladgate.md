# Fladgate

**Fladgate** — whose real name is **Flood** — is a verger at [[wrychester-cathedral|Wrychester Cathedral]] and a former business partner of [[falkiner-wraye|Falkiner Wraye]] in the fraud that ruined [[john-braden|John Brake]] (027-013)(027-037). His office was located on Fladgate Street in London, from which he took his alias (020-010). [[falkiner-wraye|Folliot]] secured him the verger's appointment at the cathedral not long after Folliot himself settled in [[wrychester|Wrychester]] (026-024).

## Role in the Story

Fladgate is one of the novel's late revelations — a man whose presence in [[wrychester|Wrychester]] forms part of the web of concealed identities that [[pemberton-bryce|Pemberton Bryce]] and the police must untangle. As a former associate of [[falkiner-wraye|Wraye]] in the original fraud, Fladgate possesses knowledge of the crime that sent [[john-braden|Brake]] to prison; his position as verger — obtained through [[falkiner-wraye|Wraye]]'s influence — places him within the cathedral community that is central to the mystery (026-024)(026-021).

When confronted by [[mitchington|Mitchington]], Fladgate's black gown lies thrown aside as he faces interrogation about his true identity and his connection to the Paradise affair (026-030). Under pressure, he turns informant, providing evidence against [[falkiner-wraye|Wraye]] to save himself (027-037).

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter XX**: [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] discovers Fladgate's office on the narrow, old-world Fladgate Street in London — the secretary's name displayed outside (020-010)(020-012).
- **Chapter XXVI**: [[mark-ransford|Ransford]] reveals to [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] that [[falkiner-wraye|Folliot]] secured Fladgate's verger appointment (026-024). [[mitchington|Mitchington]] subsequently interrogates Fladgate directly (026-029)(026-030).
- **Chapter XXVII**: The revelation that Fladgate is actually Flood — the man who, along with [[falkiner-wraye|Wraye]], defrauded [[john-braden|Brake]] — comes to light (027-013). Fladgate turns against his former partner, providing the testimony that helps bring [[falkiner-wraye|Wraye]] to justice (027-037).

## Relationships

- **[[falkiner-wraye|Falkiner Wraye (alias Folliot)]]** — Former business partner in the fraud; Wraye secured Fladgate's verger appointment (026-024)(027-037).
- **[[john-braden|John Brake/Braden]]** — Victim of the fraud that Fladgate and Wraye perpetrated (027-037).
- **[[mitchington|Mitchington]]** — The detective who interrogates Fladgate about his true identity (026-029).
- **[[pemberton-bryce|Pemberton Bryce]]** — Investigator who traces connections to Fladgate (020-012).

## Significance to the Mystery

Fladgate represents one of the final pieces in the puzzle of identities that structures *The Paradise Mystery*. His unmasking as Flood — Wraye's co-conspirator — provides the corroborating testimony needed to definitively link [[falkiner-wraye|Wraye]] to the original crime. His willingness to turn informant against his former partner (027-037) echoes the novel's recurring theme of [[secrecy-and-concealment|concealed identity]] giving way to revelation under pressure.

> they’ve found out about those other affairs—the Braden and the Collishaw affairs. Folliot was concerned in them; and who do you think the other was? You’d never guess! That man Fladgate, the verger.
>
> (027-013)
