# The Scrap of Paper

The scrap of paper is a pivotal piece of physical evidence in *The Paradise Mystery*, found in the purse of the dead man, [[John Braden]] (also known as John Brake). Its contents and the secret it holds become a central driver of the plot.

## Discovery

The scrap of paper was discovered in one of the compartments of Braden's purse, "folded curiously" (003-054). [[Pemberton Bryce]] took possession of it from the dead man's belongings before the police could examine it (004-067).

## Description

The scrap of paper was "evidently a quarter of a sheet of common notepaper" (005-002). Its physical characteristics and the manner of its folding suggested deliberate concealment.

> The breast pocket was empty; there was no pocketbook there; there were no papers there. Nor were there any papers elsewhere in the other pockets which he hastily searched: there was not even a card with a name on it. But he found a purse, full of money—banknotes, gold, silver—and in one of its compartments a scrap of paper folded curiously, after the fashion of the cocked-hat missives of another age in which envelopes had not been invented. Bryce hurriedly unfolded this, and after one glance at its contents, made haste to secrete it in his own pocket.
>
> (003-054)

## Significance to the Plot

The scrap of paper becomes the object of intense scrutiny and conflict throughout the novel. Bryce examines it privately and locks it away in "a safely-locked desk" (005-014). Its existence raises the question of what secret it contains (007-022). Later, others demand to know what Bryce has done with it (013-086), and it becomes clear that the paper holds information that certain characters desperately wish to keep hidden (014-014). The secret contained in the scrap of paper is ultimately revealed to be connected to Braden/Brake's past and his relationship with [[Mark Ransford]] (014-064) (014-065).
