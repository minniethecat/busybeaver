# The Paradise Mystery

*The Paradise Mystery* is a mystery novel by J. S. Fletcher, first published in 1920. Set in the English cathedral town of [[wrychester|Wrychester]], it follows Dr. [[mark-ransford|Mark Ransford]], a physician whose carefully guarded past is threatened when a stranger falls to his death from [[st-wrythas-stair|St. Wrytha's Stair]] — and Ransford's ambitious young assistant, [[pemberton-bryce|Pemberton Bryce]], sees the tragedy as a lever to advance his own ends.

## Setting

The novel unfolds in [[wrychester|Wrychester]], an ancient English cathedral city whose heart is the Close: "Nowhere else in England is there a fairer prospect of old-world peace. There before their eyes, set in the centre of a great green sward, fringed by tall elms and giant beeches, rises the vast fabric of the thirteenth-century Cathedral" (001-001).

Within Wrychester, the key locations include:

- **[[paradise|Paradise]]** — an outer court of the Cathedral precincts, "a veritable wilderness" (003-029) where the fatal discovery is made
- **[[st-wrythas-stair|St. Wrytha's Stair]]** — the ancient stone stairway from which [[john-braden|John Braden]] falls to his death
- **[[the-mitre-hotel|The Mitre Hotel]]** — where mysterious strangers lodge and secrets are exchanged
- **[[the-close|The Close]]** — the walled precinct surrounding [[wrychester-cathedral|Wrychester Cathedral]], home to the clergy and gentry
- **[[the-deanery|The Deanery]]** — residence of the Dean and Chapter of Wrychester
- **[[old-well-house|The Old Well House]]** — a secluded spot that holds dark secrets

## Plot Summary

When a stranger named [[john-braden|John Braden]] dies after falling from [[st-wrythas-stair|St. Wrytha's Stair]] in the Cathedral Close, the death is initially ruled a misadventure. But [[pemberton-bryce|Pemberton Bryce]], the ambitious young assistant to Dr. [[mark-ransford|Mark Ransford]], suspects foul play — and more importantly, suspects that his employer knows more than he admits.

Bryce's investigation is driven by self-interest: he hopes to win the hand of [[mary-bewery|Mary Bewery]], Ransford's ward, and believes that unearthing Ransford's secrets will give him leverage. But as he digs deeper — aided by Detective-Sergeant [[mitchington|Mitchington]] and a series of clues including [[scrap-of-paper|a scrap of paper]] found near the body — he uncovers a past stretching back decades, involving falsified identity, a stolen inheritance, and a guarded secret that [[mark-ransford|Ransford]] has spent years protecting.

The mystery thickens with the [[ch12-murder-of-the-masons-labourer|murder of a mason's labourer]] who knew too much, the shadowing of suspects across the ancient streets of Wrychester, and the eventual [[exposure-of-falkiner-wraye|exposure of Falkiner Wraye]] — the man whose true identity lies at the heart of the Paradise mystery.

## Themes

- **[[secrecy-and-concealment|Secrecy and Concealment]]** — The corrosive power of hidden pasts and the lengths to which people go to protect them
- **[[guardianship|Guardianship]]** — The legal and moral bonds that tie Ransford to his wards, and how those bonds shape the plot
- **[[suspicion-and-distrust|Suspicion and Distrust]]** — The atmosphere of mutual suspicion that the murder creates among Wrychester's inhabitants

## Publication

*The Paradise Mystery* was first published in 1920 and is now in the public domain. This wiki is based on the Standard Ebooks edition (2025). The novel belongs to the Golden Age of detective fiction and exemplifies Fletcher's skill in weaving mystery with the atmosphere of an English cathedral town.
