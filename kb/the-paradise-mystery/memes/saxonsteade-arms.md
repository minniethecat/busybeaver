# Saxonsteade Arms

The **Saxonsteade Arms** is a village inn in [[saxonsteade|Saxonsteade]], the estate village of the [[duke-of-saxonsteade|Duke of Saxonsteade]], situated in the wooded countryside outside [[wrychester|Wrychester]]. It serves as a waypoint for characters traveling to and from the ducal estate, appearing in the later chapters of *The Paradise Mystery*.

## Description

The Saxonsteade Arms is a modest country inn typical of an English estate village. It lies outside the little village of Saxonsteade, on the edge of the deep woods that surround the ducal estate (022-001). The inn provides accommodation and stabling for travelers — [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] is noted to have ridden away from it on horseback (022-001).

## Role in the Story

The Saxonsteade Arms appears in two key scenes during the investigation of the Saxonsteade jewels affair:

- In Chapter 22, [[pemberton-bryce|Pemberton Bryce]] rides away from the inn after meeting with the [[duke-of-saxonsteade|Duke of Saxonsteade]], carrying new information about the missing jewels (022-001).
- In Chapter 24, Bryce leaves [[mr-harker|Mr. Harker]] at the inn while he pursues other leads related to the dual investigation of [[death-of-john-braden|Braden's death]] and the jewels (024-001).

The inn thus functions as a staging point in the investigation's transition from Wrychester to the Saxonsteade estate.

## Location Context

The Saxonsteade Arms is situated outside the village of Saxonsteade, on the edge of deep woods through which the road passes from Wrychester. The journey to Saxonsteade from Wrychester takes travelers "through woods and valleys," and the inn sits at the boundary between the cultivated estate lands and the surrounding forest (022-001).
