## 身份与角色

Dr. Everest was a medical practitioner in Wrychester who served alongside [[dr-coates|Dr. Coates]] as a postmortem examiner. His given name is not revealed in the narrative; he appears exclusively in his professional capacity during the investigation into the death of James Collishaw. (012-054)

## The Collishaw Autopsy

When James Collishaw, the Cathedral mason's labourer, was found dead under mysterious circumstances, Dr. Everest was called in to assist Dr. Coates with the postmortem examination. (012-054) Together, the two doctors conducted a thorough autopsy and determined that Collishaw had died of hydrogen cyanide (prussic acid) poisoning. (013-038)

> "Dr. Coates and Dr. Everest are going to do it this afternoon," replied Mitchington. "The Coroner went to them at once, as soon as I told him."
>
> (012-054)

The results were communicated to Inspector [[mitchington|Mitchington]], who then conveyed the findings to [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]]: "Coates, and Everest, who helped him," had confirmed the cause of death. (013-004) Bryce subsequently questioned Dr. Everest and Dr. Coates directly about their findings at the [[wrychester-police-station|police station]]. (013-028)

> "Coates and Everest have carried out an autopsy this afternoon," said Bryce. "Collishaw was poisoned. HCN. No doubt at all about it."
>
> (013-038)

During his interview with the two doctors, Bryce learned the full details of the autopsy report. (013-038) A key question that later arose in the investigation was whether Coates and Everest had discovered a pill or capsule in Collishaw's stomach — a detail that bore directly on the circumstances of the poisoning. (015-064)
