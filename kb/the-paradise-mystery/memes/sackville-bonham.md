# Sackville Bonham

**Sackville Bonham** is the stepson of [[mr-folliot|Mr. Folliot]] (in reality the fugitive swindler [[falkiner-wraye|Falkiner Wraye]]) and son of [[mrs-folliot|Mrs. Folliot]], the domineering matriarch of [[wrychester|Wrychester]] society. A tall, loquacious young man, Sackville serves as [[mary-bewery|Mary Bewery]]'s persistent suitor and [[pemberton-bryce|Pemberton Bryce]]'s presumptive rival for her affections. His role in the narrative is largely that of an unwitting pawn — his conversations with [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] at [[wrychester-club|the Wrychester Club]] and on the golf links provide [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] with both social access and information, while his romantic pursuit of [[mary-bewery|Mary Bewery]] adds a layer of social comedy to the darker mystery plot.

## Role in the Story

Sackville Bonham appears as a secondary figure in [[wrychester|Wrychester]]'s social landscape. He is a member of [[wrychester-club|the Wrychester Club]], an exclusive institution "the members of which were drawn from the leisured, the professional, the clerical, and the military circles of the old city" (005-015). There, he is "noted in [[wrychester|Wrychester]] circles as a loquacious and for[[guardianship|ward]] youth" who eagerly shares his stepfather's opinions on the [[john-braden|Braden]] affair (005-015).

As [[mary-bewery|Mary Bewery]]'s suitor, Sackville is a source of mild tension. [[pemberton-bryce|Pemberton Bryce]] regards him as "his presumptive rival" (005-015), and [[mrs-folliot|Mrs. Folliot]] actively promotes the match. However, [[mary-bewery|Mary]] emphatically rejects the idea, declaring: "I've no more intention of marrying Mr. Sackville Bonham than of eloping with the Bishop! The idea's too absurd to—even be thought of!" (022-063).

Sackville's relationship with [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] is one of unwitting exploitation. At the golf links, [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] "inveigle[s] Sackville Bonham into conversation" and Sackville "fell readily into [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]]'s trap," revealing family information about the [[mr-folliot|Folliot]]s' efforts to clear Ransford's name (022-002). Later, Sackville confides to [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] that "there's wheels within wheels!... We must clear him. That's precisely why [[mr-folliot|Mr. Folliot]] offered his re[[guardianship|ward]]" (022-002) — a statement that reveals more about the [[mr-folliot|Folliot]] household's internal dynamics than Sackville realizes.

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter II**: First appears walking in [[mark-ransford|Dr. Ransford]]'s garden with [[mary-bewery|Mary Bewery]] — "a tall lad, whom he recognized as one Sackville Bonham, stepson of [[mr-folliot|Mr. Folliot]], a wealthy resident of [[cathedral-close|the Close]]" (002-070). The two young people are "laughing and chatting together with evident great friendliness," a sight that prompts [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] to make a cutting remark about [[mrs-folliot|Mrs. Folliot]]'s inquisitive nature (002-070).

- **Chapter III**: Continues his visit, walking and talking with [[mary-bewery|Mary]] in the garden while [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] prepares his departure from Ransford's household (003-021).

- **Chapter V**: At [[wrychester-club|the Wrychester Club]], Sackville holds court among other young men, "busily telling three or four other young men what his stepfather, [[mr-folliot|Mr. Folliot]], had to say about the event" (005-015). He triumphantly presents [[mr-folliot|Mr. Folliot]]'s timeline of the [[john-braden|Braden]] death, arguing it proves the stranger was attacked immediately upon entering the gallery (005-015).

- **Chapter VII**: Accompanies [[mary-bewery|Mary Bewery]] to [[john-braden|John Braden]]'s grave at St. Wigbert's, "one carrying a wreath and the other a large bunch of lilies" (007-009). [[mrs-folliot|Mrs. Folliot]] later recounts that "Sackville, who is a warmhearted boy, was touched" by [[mary-bewery|Mary]]'s gesture and joined her in paying tribute (007-013).

- **Chapter XV**: Attends the [[collishaw|Collishaw]] inquest at the Coroner's Court with his stepfather, [[mr-folliot|Mr. Folliot]]. After[[guardianship|ward]], [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] encounters him in the street (015-037).

- **Chapter XXII**: Encountered by [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] at the golf links. [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] deliberately engages him in conversation to learn about the [[mr-folliot|Folliot]]s' intentions regarding the Ransford case (022-002).

- **Chapter XXVI**: On the day of the final crisis, Sackville is "still where [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] had left him, at the golf-links" — absent while the dramatic confrontation unfolds at the [[mr-folliot|Folliot]] residence and the Old Well House (026-049).

## Relationships

- **[[mary-bewery|Mary Bewery]]** — His romantic interest and the object of his persistent but unsuccessful courtship. [[mary-bewery|Mary]] tolerates his company but firmly rejects any suggestion of marriage.

- **[[mr-folliot|Mr. Folliot]]** — His stepfather, whose opinions Sackville eagerly repeats and whose wealth and status provide Sackville's social position. Unbeknownst to Sackville, [[mr-folliot|Folliot]] is actually the fugitive swindler [[falkiner-wraye|Falkiner Wraye]].

- **[[mrs-folliot|Mrs. Folliot]]** — His mother, a domineering woman who is "the apple of her eye" and who actively promotes the match with [[mary-bewery|Mary Bewery]] (002-070).

- **[[pemberton-bryce|Pemberton Bryce]]** — His "presumptive rival" for [[mary-bewery|Mary Bewery]]'s affections (005-015). [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] regards Sackville with contempt but skillfully manipulates him for information.

## Significance to the Mystery

Sackville Bonham is a peripheral figure in the central mystery of [[john-braden|John Braden]]'s death, but his narrative function is important in several respects. First, his romantic pursuit of [[mary-bewery|Mary Bewery]] establishes a secondary tension that runs parallel to the main plot. Second, his loquaciousness and social prominence make him an unwitting conduit of information — through Sackville, [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] learns about the [[mr-folliot|Folliot]] household's internal discussions and their campaign to "clear" Ransford. Third, his association with the [[mr-folliot|Folliot]] name places him in the orbit of the novel's climactic revelation: that his stepfather, the respected [[mr-folliot|Mr. Folliot]] of [[cathedral-close|the Close]], is in fact the criminal [[falkiner-wraye|Falkiner Wraye]], the man responsible for [[john-braden|John Brake]]'s ([[john-braden|Braden]]'s) original ruin.

Sackville himself remains blissfully ignorant of these darker currents. At the novel's end, he is still at the golf links — a telling detail that underscores his detachment from the serious events that engulf his family.
