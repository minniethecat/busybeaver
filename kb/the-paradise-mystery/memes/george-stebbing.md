# George Stebbing

**George Stebbing** is the secretary of the Second Friendly Society in Wrychester, a young man whose routine office work becomes unexpectedly significant when it intersects with the [[criminal-investigation|Paradise investigation]].

## Role in the Investigation

Jettison, pursuing an independent line of inquiry, calls at the Second Friendly Society's office — a modest establishment on a side street — and finds Stebbing at his desk in an inner office (020-013). Stebbing is described as "a young man, sandy-haired, freckled, and of an open and communicative countenance" (020-013).

Jettison introduces himself as someone looking into the [[criminal-investigation|Paradise affair]], and Stebbing becomes cooperative (020-015). The crucial information Stebbing provides concerns Glassdale, who had recently deposited fifty pounds in gold with the Society:

> "I asked no questions. Legacy, he said — he'd had a bit of luck." (020-024)

Stebbing reveals that Glassdale's deposit was made in gold — an unusual form of payment that Jettison immediately recognizes as significant (020-026). He also provides the name of Glassdale's bank: "Old Bank, in [[monday-market|Monday Market]]" (020-029). This financial trail, traced through Stebbing's routine record-keeping, becomes an important link in the chain of evidence that unravels the mystery.

## Significance

Stebbing is a minor but functional character whose role is purely informational: his position as a society secretary gives him custody of records that reveal a suspect's suspicious financial activity. He has no personal involvement in the crime and no hidden motive — he is simply a clerk doing his job, whose cooperation with Jettison advances the investigation.
