# Detective-Sergeant Jettison

Detective-Sergeant Jettison is an officer from Scotland Yard dispatched to [[wrychester|Wrychester]] to support [[wrychester-police|the local police]] [[criminal-investigation|investigation]] into the suspicious deaths of [[john-braden]] and [[collishaw]]. His arrival brings professional investigative experience from London to the provincial setting, and his methodical theories help reshape the direction of the [[criminal-investigation|inquiry]].

## Role in the Story

Jettison arrives in [[wrychester|Wrychester]] as an outside expert, introduced to [[mitchington]] as "Detective-Sergeant Jettison, from the Yard" (017-028). His presence signals a recognition by the authorities that the [[wrychester|Wrychester]] deaths require resources beyond the capacity of the local constabulary.

Unlike the more impulsive local officers, Jettison brings a measured, experienced approach. He listens carefully, nods with the deliberation of a professional (017-042), and speaks in a "slow, easygoing fashion" that belies his sharp observational skills (020-019). When he proposes his theory of the crime, he commands the absorbed attention of those around him (017-055).

## Key Appearances

### Chapter XVII: The Scotland Yard Theory

Jettison presents his reconstruction of events to [[mitchington|Mitchington]] and [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]]. His theory is direct: [[john-braden|Brake]] came to [[wrychester|Wrychester]] with the [[secrecy-and-concealment|secret]] of the [[saxonsteade|Saxonsteade]] jewels — a [[secrecy-and-concealment|secret]] shared with [[glassdale]] — and either [[john-braden|Brake]] let the information leak or someone else discovered it, leading to his death (017-051). Jettison's theory gains [[mitchington|Mitchington]]'s endorsement: "I'm inclined to Jettison's theory — in fact, I'm certain it's the correct one" (017-049).

However, [[pemberton-bryce]] is not entirely convinced, observing that the theory, while plausible, may not be "entirely correct" (017-061). Jettison accepts this skepticism with professional equanimity, whispering to [[mitchington|Mitchington]] that [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] "knows a great deal more than you think" (017-071) and advising patience: "Let him take his own time" (017-073).

### Chapter XX: The Independent Investigation

Jettison undertakes his own inquiries in [[wrychester|Wrychester]], visiting the police station at noontide (020-001) and then pursuing leads independently. He visits a local establishment where he presents his professional card and secures an audience (020-013). His questioning is methodical and persistent — he wants to know how [[john-braden|the dead man]] obtained certain materials, and he examines ledgers and records with the practiced eye of a career detective (020-023)(020-025)(020-027).

Jettison's reflections on the case reveal his thorough approach. He has been thinking over the timeline, specifically noting that [[john-braden|Brake]] had been in London "two or three days before his death" — a detail that requires explanation (020-035). He has also had to "convince 'em of the" necessity of certain investigative steps, suggesting he must navigate institutional resistance even as he pursues the truth (020-040).

His visit concludes with a request for discretion — "for the present — not a word" (020-030) — and his characteristic professional calm conceals whatever conclusions he has drawn. The narrator notes that the person he questioned "would have liked to sit down and give all his reasons" but Jettison "drew that covert blank," keeping his own counsel (020-031).

## Relationships

- **[[mitchington]]**: Jettison works alongside the local detective, who defers to his Scotland Yard experience (017-028)(017-039).
- **[[pemberton-bryce]]**: Jettison quickly recognizes that [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] possesses information he has not yet shared (017-071) but wisely refrains from pressuring him (017-073).
- **[[glassdale]]**: Jettison's theory places [[glassdale|Glassdale]] as [[john-braden|Brake]]'s fellow ex-convict and co-possessor of the jewel [[secrecy-and-concealment|secret]] (017-046)(017-051).

## Significance to the Mystery

Jettison serves as the professional counterpoint to the amateur [[criminal-investigation|investigation]]s of [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] and the local efforts of [[mitchington|Mitchington]]. His Scotland Yard pedigree lends institutional weight to the [[criminal-investigation|investigation]], and his theory about [[john-braden|Brake]]'s motive provides the official framework within which the case proceeds.

His greatest contribution, however, is his intuitive grasp of [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]]'s role. Alone among the official investigators, Jettison perceives that [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] holds back information — and alone among them, he has the wisdom to let [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] reveal it in his own time rather than forcing a confrontation (017-071)(017-073). This professional patience proves essential to the ultimate resolution of the case, as [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]]'s independent discoveries would eventually dovetail with Jettison's official framework.
