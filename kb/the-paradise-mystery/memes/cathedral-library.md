# Cathedral Library

The **Cathedral Library** (formally the Library of the [[Cathedral Close|Dean and Chapter of Wrychester]]) is a historic library housed in an ancient picturesque building in one corner of [[Cathedral Close|the Close]], renowned among bibliophiles worldwide for its collection of priceless medieval manuscripts, folios, and relics (005-051). It serves as a key setting in the early stages of the Paradise mystery, where librarian [[Ambrose Campany]] first identifies the Barthorpe clue and where [[Pemberton Bryce|Bryce]] conducts his covert research into the tomb of Richard Jenkins.

## Description

> The famous Library of the Dean and Chapter of Wrychester was housed in an ancient picturesque building in one corner of the Close, wherein, day in and day out, amidst priceless volumes and manuscripts, huge folios and weighty quartos, old prints, and relics of the medieval ages, Ambrose Campany, the librarian, was pretty nearly always to be found, ready to show his treasures to the visitors and tourists who came from all parts of the world to see a collection well known to bibliophiles. (005-051)

The Library occupies a building situated at "the other side of the Close" (005-046), within the precincts of [[Wrychester Cathedral]]. Its interior is lined with walls displaying charts and plans of the Cathedral and its surroundings — it was specifically to inspect one of these that [[Pemberton Bryce|Bryce]] came to the Library on the day after [[Death of John Braden|John Braden's death]] (006-008).

The collection includes not only printed books but also original drawings and manuscripts relating to the Cathedral's history. Among the librarian's working materials are drawings of old brasses, coats of arms, and sculptural ornamentation from tombs throughout the Cathedral, the Cloisters, and [[Paradise]] (012-001). These heraldic records prove instrumental in identifying otherwise unmarked ancient tombs.

## Role in the Story

The Cathedral Library plays a pivotal role in the investigation of the [[Paradise]] mystery through three distinct narrative functions:

### The Barthorpe Clue

On the day after [[Death of John Braden|John Braden's death]], [[Pemberton Bryce|Bryce]] visits the Library ostensibly to study Cathedral plans. While there, he overhears [[Ambrose Campany|Campany]] discussing a significant find: among the dead man's possessions at [[The Mitre Hotel]] was an antiquarian book about Barthorpe, a small Leicestershire market-town (005-051). Campany suggests this could be a clue to the dead man's origins, reasoning that a person would be unlikely to purchase an obscure local history unless they had some personal connection to the place.

Bryce immediately recognizes the value of this insight and, after further reflection, decides to travel to Barthorpe himself to investigate (007-025). Unknown to Bryce, the elderly [[mr-harker|Simpson Harker]] — who was also present in the Library during Campany's discussion — independently follows the same Barthorpe trail, revealing himself to be more than the "quiet, meditative old fellow" he appears (008-012).

### The Jenkins Tomb Research

Following his return from Barthorpe, Bryce makes "so many visits to the Cathedral Library that [[Ambrose Campany|Ambrose Campany]] had asked him jestingly if he was going in for archaeology" (012-001). Bryce's true purpose is to identify the tomb of Richard Jenkins in Paradise, which he believes holds the key to the mystery.

His diligence is rewarded when Campany, in the course of showing Bryce illustrations prepared for his history of the Cathedral, displays a drawing of a shield bearing three crows — the coat of arms of Richard Jenkins, armiger (012-001). Campany confirms that this heraldry identifies a specific unmarked tomb "out there in the corner of Paradise, near the east wall of the south porch," whose inscription had long since worn away (012-001). Bryce leaves the Library and immediately walks to Paradise to locate the tomb, though his examination is interrupted by a fresh death (012-005).

### The Stranger's Inquiry

Later in the investigation, it emerges that the mysterious stranger who died at [[St. Wrytha's Stair]] had approached [[Pemberton Bryce|Bryce]] outside the Cathedral with a single question: "Merely to be told where the Cathedral Library was" (011-047). Bryce admits to pointing out the Library to the man but claims never to have seen him again until summoned to his death (011-050). The truth of this account is challenged by [[Mark Ransford|Ransford]] and [[Mary Bewery]], who suspect Bryce of lying about the encounter.

## Location Context

The Cathedral Library sits within the enclosed precinct of [[Cathedral Close|the Close]], at the opposite side from Paradise (005-046). It is a short walk from [[Paradise]] — near enough that Bryce could leave the Library at noon and "plunge in amongst its yews and cypresses" moments later (012-005).

The Library should not be confused with the **Free Library** — the public lending library elsewhere in [[Wrychester]] where Bryce obtains a gazetteer to research Barthorpe (007-025). The Cathedral Library is a scholarly collection attached to the Dean and Chapter, distinct from the municipal institution.
