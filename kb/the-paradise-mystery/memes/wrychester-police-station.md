# Wrychester Police Station

The **Wrychester Police Station** is the headquarters of the local police force in [[Wrychester]] and the operational centre from which [[Inspector Mitchington]] and the Superintendent conduct the investigations into the deaths of [[John Braden]] and [[Collishaw]]. It serves as the gathering point for witnesses, suspects, and evidence throughout the novel's mystery plot, and later as the base of operations for the Scotland Yard detective sent to assist the local force.

## Role in the Story

The police station features prominently as the procedural hub of the investigations:

**The Braden Investigation.** Following the discovery of John Braden's body at the foot of [[St. Wrytha's Stair]], the police station becomes the focal point for the inquiry. After examining the scene at the Cathedral, "Bryce and the inspector crossed over to the police-station" (004-063), where the investigation's direction was discussed. The station doors became the site of public interest: "at the police-station doors a crowd had gathered" and "just inside two or three principal citizens were talking to the Superintendent" (004-064), among them [[Stephen Folliot]].

**The Collishaw Case.** When [[Collishaw]] died suddenly of poisoning, Bryce was the first to alert the police. He sent for Mitchington with urgent instructions: "Run down to the police-station — get hold of Mitchington — quietly, you understand! — bring him here at once" (012-013). Later, after the police took the body away, Bryce "accompanied Mitchington to the police-station and seen the tin bottle and the remains of Collishaw's dinner safely locked up" (012-034), establishing the station as the repository for physical evidence.

**Scotland Yard Intervention.** The police station became the base for the detective sent from New Scotland Yard: "it was drawing near to noon — the great bell of the Cathedral, indeed, was proclaiming noontide to Wrychester as Jettison turned into the police-station and sought Mitchington in his office" (020-001). The two men used Mitchington's office as their command centre, discussing the case and receiving telegrams with crucial information.

**Glassdale's Evidence.** Near the novel's climax, after his encounter with Folliot, [[Glassdale]] "suddenly exchanged his idle sauntering for brisker steps and walked sharply round to the police-station, where he asked to see Mitchington" (026-001) — a decision that would lead to the final confrontation at the [[old-well-house|Old Well House]].

## Location Context

The police station is situated in the heart of Wrychester, within easy walking distance of both the [[Cathedral Close]] and the city's other key locations. Its proximity to the Cathedral meant that news of the Braden tragedy spread quickly, drawing crowds to its doors. The station functions as the interface between the enclosed, secretive world of the Close and the official machinery of law enforcement — characters constantly move between the two, carrying information, suspicion, and evidence.
