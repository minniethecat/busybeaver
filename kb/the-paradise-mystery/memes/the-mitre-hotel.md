# The Mitre Hotel

**The Mitre Hotel** is an inn in [[wrychester|Wrychester]] where [[john-braden|John Braden]] stayed upon his arrival in the city, registering under that name — which was later discovered to be an alias for John Brake (005-006)(005-050). Managed by [[mrs-partingley|Mrs. Partingley]], the Mitre sits near [[wrychester-cathedral|the Cathedral]] and becomes a focal point of the investigation after Braden's death at [[paradise|Paradise]] (004-030)(004-028).

## In the Novel

On the evening of his arrival in [[wrychester|Wrychester]], [[john-braden|Braden]] registered at the Mitre and was given a room (005-006). He went out shortly before nine o'clock and never returned — his body was discovered the next morning at the foot of [[st-wrythas-stair|St. Wrytha's Stair]] (011-070). The police investigation began at the Mitre, where [[mitchington|Detective-Sergeant Mitchington]] examined Braden's room and luggage, finding among his effects an antiquarian book — *The History of Barthorpe* — that [[ambrose-campany|Ambrose Campany]] immediately recognized as a vital clue (005-052)(006-023).

[[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]], as [[mark-ransford|Dr. Ransford]]'s assistant at the time, was present when [[mitchington|Mitchington]] and the police first visited the hotel to question [[mrs-partingley|Mrs. Partingley]] and inspect the dead man's belongings (004-030). It was at the Mitre that Bryce first saw the scrap of paper bearing the name "Ransford" — a discovery that would ultimately lead him to unravel the connection between the dead stranger and the doctor he served (005-006).

The hotel's location placed it at the heart of the [[wrychester|Wrychester]] community, making it a natural gathering point for those drawn into the mystery. [[mr-harker|Harker]], a former prison warder who had known [[john-braden|Brake]] at Portland, also stayed at the Mitre on the night of Braden's arrival, and his recognition of the dead man proved crucial to establishing Braden's true identity (014-015)(014-041).

## The Night of Braden's Disappearance

On the night of his arrival at the Mitre, Braden went out shortly before nine o'clock and was never seen alive again (011-070). The critical two-hour window between his departure from the hotel and his death at [[paradise|Paradise]] became the central puzzle of the investigation (017-051).

[[mr-harker|Harker]], who had known Brake during his imprisonment at Portland, was also staying at the Mitre that night. The two men recognized each other immediately — "I knew him at once — and he knew me" (014-041). This chance encounter, occurring on the very night of Braden's death, placed Harker in a uniquely incriminating position. When Harker later testified at the inquest, his presence at the Mitre on that night became a matter of intense scrutiny (014-015).

The stranger who accompanied Braden to the Mitre — "who came with Brake to the Mitre Hotel at Wrychester — who is he?" (021-049) — was never positively identified by the hotel staff. His identity, and his role in Braden's final hours, remained one of the deepest mysteries of the case.
