Alexander Chilstone is the manager of the London & Colonies Bank in Threadneedle Street, London. His spontaneous appearance at the coroner's inquest, prompted by reading about the case in the newspapers, provided crucial evidence about [[john-braden|John Braden]]'s identity, financial standing, and recent movements.

## Role in the Story

Chilstone serves as an important corroborating witness whose testimony anchors the financial dimension of the mystery. His account of [[john-braden|Braden]]'s substantial deposit — ten thousand pounds — establishes that the dead stranger was a man of means who had prospered in Australia, and his mention of [[john-braden|Braden]]'s intention to make "one or two calls in the country" confirms that [[wrychester|Wrychester]] was a deliberate destination rather than a random stop.

## Key Appearances

- **The bank visit**: On the day before his death, [[john-braden|John Braden]] (using that name) called at the London & Colonies Bank and opened an account, paying in ten thousand pounds via a draft drawn on the bank's Melbourne agents (006-034).
- **[[john-braden|Braden]]'s stated plans**: [[john-braden|Braden]] told Chilstone he had arrived at Charing Cross from Paris that very morning, had been living in Australia for some years speculating in landed property, and intended to settle in England permanently. He remarked that he had "one or two calls, or visits, to make in the country" before returning to London (006-035).
- **Inquest testimony**: After reading of the [[wrychester|Wrychester]] death in the newspapers, Chilstone travelled to [[wrychester|Wrychester]] to offer his evidence. The coroner described his intervention as "unexpected" and his information as "of considerable importance" (006-034).
- **The *Times* advertisement**: Chilstone brought with him a copy of an advertisement from that morning's *Times* which he was certain had been placed by [[john-braden|Braden]] — providing an additional lead on [[john-braden|Braden]]'s activities in London (006-035).
- **Impact on Ransford**: During Chilstone's testimony, [[pemberton-bryce|Pemberton Bryce]] observed that [[mark-ransford|Dr. Ransford]] was visibly agitated — his cheeks paled, his eyes dilated, and his expression suggested more than mere curiosity about the banker's revelations (006-035).

## Relationships

- **[[john-braden|John Braden]]** — Customer. [[john-braden|Braden]] opened his account with Chilstone on the day before his death.
- **Coroner** — Chilstone volunteered his evidence to the coroner upon recognising [[john-braden|the dead man]]'s description in the press.

## Significance to the Mystery

Chilstone's testimony performs several narrative functions. It confirms [[john-braden|Braden]]'s true identity and his recent return to England after years abroad — aligning with the backstory later revealed by [[mr-harker|Harker]] and [[glassdale|Glassdale]]. The ten-thousand-pound deposit demonstrates that [[john-braden|Braden]] (despite his criminal past) was now wealthy, while his casual remark about having sufficient pocket money and his deferral of the chequebook suggest a man who did not anticipate needing immediate access to his funds — perhaps because he did not expect his [[wrychester|Wrychester]] visit to be a long one. The *Times* advertisement Chilstone produced becomes an additional piece of the puzzle in tracing [[john-braden|Braden]]'s movements and motives.
