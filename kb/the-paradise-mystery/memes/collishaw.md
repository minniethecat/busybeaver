# Collishaw

A Wrychester labourer whose sudden death by poison in [[wrychester-cathedral|Wrychester Cathedral]] makes him the second victim in a chain of suspicious deaths that grips [[wrychester|the Cathedral town]].

## Role in the Story

Collishaw is described as "the labourer" (027-037), a working man of [[wrychester|Wrychester]] who became entangled in the [[criminal-investigation|criminal conspiracy]] surrounding [[john-braden|John Braden]]'s death. Unlike Braden — an outsider who arrived with secrets — Collishaw was a local man whose fatal mistake was knowing what he should not. His poisoning deepens the mystery and raises the stakes, proving that the first death at [[paradise|Paradise]] was no isolated incident.

## Death and Discovery

Collishaw's body was found in [[wrychester-cathedral|the Cathedral]], "close up by the nave, just where you found that chap Collishaw" (016-014), near "a very ancient and dilapidated" tomb (016-001). The discovery of his body caused immediate confusion and alarm, confirming the existence of a deliberate killer in [[wrychester|Wrychester]].

The cause of death was poisoning — the same substance later used by [[mr-folliot|Folliot]] to take his own life: "Folliot poisoned himself — took the same stuff, the doctor said, that finished that chap Collishaw" (027-011). This connection between the two deaths established a pattern that [[pemberton-bryce|Pemberton Bryce]] and the [[wrychester-police|police]] would trace back to [[falkiner-wraye|Falkiner Wraye]].

## Connection to the Conspiracy

Collishaw was murdered because he possessed dangerous knowledge about the [[falkiner-wraye|Wraye]]-[[mr-folliot|Folliot]] conspiracy. As the investigation later revealed, Collishaw had been paid for his silence — until silence was no longer sufficient:

> "Wraye — that's Folliot — paid Collishaw fifty pounds, did he?" (026-045)

The payment of fifty pounds (a significant sum for a labourer) suggests Collishaw had learned something crucial about the true identities of the two men, or about their involvement in [[john-braden|Braden]]'s death. When the net began to close, [[falkiner-wraye|Wraye]] eliminated the liability.

[[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]], as a medical man familiar with poisons, was initially suspected: "It would be an easy thing for a medical man to get rid of Collishaw as he undoubtedly was got rid of" (018-009). But the trail eventually led back to [[falkiner-wraye|Wraye]], whose motive — silencing a dangerous witness — became clear once the full scope of the [[guardianship|guardianship]] fraud and identity [[secrecy-and-concealment|concealment]] was exposed.

## Key Appearances

Collishaw appears only indirectly in the novel — his death is reported and investigated, but as a victim he has no living scenes. His significance is forensic: the circumstances of his poisoning, the location of his body in the Cathedral, and the payments he received from [[falkiner-wraye|Wraye]] form a critical evidentiary chain that connects the [[death-of-john-braden|Braden murder]] to the [[death-of-collishaw|Collishaw poisoning]] and ultimately to the [[exposure-of-falkiner-wraye|exposure of Falkiner Wraye]].

## Relationships

- **[[falkiner-wraye]]** — Wraye (alias Folliot) paid Collishaw £50 for silence and later poisoned him when he became a liability.
- **[[mr-folliot]]** — The false identity of Falkiner Wraye; Collishaw's knowledge of Folliot/Wraye's true identity led to his murder.
- **[[john-braden]]** — Collishaw's death was linked to Braden's; the two were twin "mysteries" investigated together.
- **[[pemberton-bryce]]** — Initially suspected of the poisoning due to his medical expertise; later cleared.
- **[[mitchington]]** — The detective-sergeant who investigated the linked Braden-Collishaw deaths.

## Significance to the Mystery

Collishaw's murder serves as the narrative turning point that transforms the Braden affair from a single suspicious death into a pattern of calculated killings. The poisoning — using the same substance that [[mr-folliot|Folliot]] later took — creates a forensic thread that [[mitchington|Mitchington]] and [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] follow to its source. Collishaw is the archetypal "little man who knew too much": a working-class bystander whose proximity to dangerous secrets proved fatal, and whose death exposed the killers' willingness to eliminate anyone who threatened their [[secrecy-and-concealment|secrets]].
