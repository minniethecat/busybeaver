# Monday Market

**Monday Market** is a small square in [[Wrychester]], notable as the location of [[The Mitre Hotel]] — "the quaint old-world inn which filled almost one side of the little square" (004-030). It serves as the setting for several early investigative scenes following [[Death of John Braden|the death of John Braden]].

## Description

The square is described as "the little square known as Monday Market" (004-030), suggesting it was historically the site of a weekly market in Wrychester. Its most prominent feature is [[The Mitre Hotel]], an old coaching inn whose courtyard opens onto the square. The hotel's upper rooms, including the bedroom occupied by [[Death of John Braden|John Braden]], look directly out over Monday Market (004-055).

The square is paved with cobblestones — the Mitre's courtyard is described as "cobble-paved" (004-030) — and retains its old-world character consistent with the medieval atmosphere of [[Cathedral Close|the Close]] and surrounding precincts.

## Role in the Story

Monday Market appears in the narrative exclusively as the setting of [[The Mitre Hotel]]. It is here that [[Pemberton Bryce|Bryce]] and [[Inspector Mitchington]] first arrive to investigate the death of the stranger, crossing to the inn and meeting the landlady [[Mrs. Partingley]] in the courtyard (004-030). The subsequent examination of Braden's belongings takes place in a bedroom overlooking the square.

Later in the investigation, the detective [[Jettison]] learns from the secretary of the Second Friendly Society that one of Wrychester's three banks — the "Old Bank" — is located in Monday Market (020-029).

## Location Context

Monday Market is situated within easy walking distance of [[Cathedral Close|the Close]] and the Cathedral precinct — [[Pemberton Bryce|Bryce]] and Mitchington walk "over the way" to reach it (004-030). The square's proximity to the Cathedral makes it part of the central district of Wrychester, distinct from outlying locations like [[Saxonsteade]] or [[Friary Lane]].

## Key Passages

> …the quaint old-world inn which filled almost one side of the little square known as Monday Market, and in at the courtyard, where, in the bright May sunshine, the landlady, a bustling, comely, middle-aged woman, was superintending the operations of an hostler who was washing down a smart-looking trap.
>
> (004-030)
