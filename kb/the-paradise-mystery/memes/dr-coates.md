# Dr. Coates

**Dr. Coates** is the senior medical practitioner of Wrychester, called upon by the Coroner to perform the postmortem examination following the sudden death of Collishaw, the mason's labourer (012-028).

## Role in the Investigation

When Collishaw is found dead in the Cathedral Close, [[mitchington|Inspector Mitchington]] immediately seeks medical authority. Dr. Ransford being absent — "He went to Highminster — some County Council business" (012-023) — Dick Bewery offers to fetch Dr. Coates instead (012-023).

Bryce advises Mitchington to inform the Coroner, who "will issue a formal order to Dr. Coates to make a postmortem" (012-028). Coates is described as "an elderly man who was the leading practitioner in the town" (012-034). He receives Bryce's careful account of the circumstances and proceeds with the examination.

> Coates says there's no doubt about it! Poisoned! Hydrocyanic acid! (012-058)

## The Collishaw Autopsy

Coates performs the autopsy together with [[dellingham|Dr. Everest]], another Wrychester doctor (012-054). Their findings are definitive: "Both of 'em told me — Coates, and Everest, who helped him. They said it was obvious from the very start" (013-004). The poison was identified as hydrocyanic acid (prussic acid), a rapid-acting compound consistent with the victim having died almost immediately after his dinner.

Coates's formal report becomes the foundation of the official finding that Collishaw was murdered — the second suspicious death in Wrychester in a matter of days, transforming the local investigation into a homicide case.
