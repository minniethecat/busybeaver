Falkiner Wraye is the true identity of the man known in [[wrychester|Wrychester]] as **[[mr-folliot|Mr. Folliot]]** (see [[mr-folliot]]). Originally a small estate agent in [[barthorpe|Barthorpe, Leicestershire]], he and his partner Flood defrauded [[john-braden|John Brake]] of several thousand pounds of bank money, triggering [[john-braden|Brake]]'s imprisonment and lifelong quest for vengeance. Wraye fled England, prospered abroad, and returned under an assumed name to marry a wealthy widow and settle in [[wrychester|the cathedral town]] of [[wrychester|Wrychester]].

## Role in the Story

Wraye is the novel's hidden antagonist — the man whose past crime set the entire mystery in motion. His fraud against [[john-braden|John Brake]] drove [[john-braden|Brake]] to [[wrychester|Wrychester]] in search of him, and [[john-braden|Brake]]'s subsequent death at the cathedral porch set off the chain of events that dominates the narrative. Throughout the story Wraye maintains the persona of "[[mr-folliot|Mr. Folliot]]," a respectable resident of [[wrychester|Wrychester]], while quietly trying to suppress any discovery of his past.

[[pemberton-bryce|Pemberton Bryce]] eventually pieces together the connection between [[john-braden|Brake]]'s search for Wraye and [[mr-folliot|Folliot]]'s physical characteristics — a missing middle finger on his left hand and a scar on his left jaw, both the result of a gun accident in his youth (025-039). When [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] confronts him with this evidence, Wraye's calm facade wavers but does not break (025-043).

## Key Appearances

- **[[barthorpe|Barthorpe]] fraud**: [[john-braden|Brake]] met Wraye through Leicestershire connections after his marriage. Wraye and Flood engaged [[john-braden|Brake]] in [[secrecy-and-concealment|secret]] financial dealings, borrowing bank money for short transactions before disappearing with several thousand pounds, leaving [[john-braden|Brake]] to face the consequences (021-040).
- **Flight and return**: After the fraud, Wraye left England and amassed wealth abroad before returning to marry a rich widow and settle in [[wrychester|Wrychester]] under the name [[mr-folliot|Folliot]] (025-037).
- **[[john-braden|Brake]]'s search**: [[john-braden|John Brake]], after completing his prison term, traveled to Australia and elsewhere, accumulating funds before returning to England with [[wrychester|Wrychester]] as his specific destination — he had learned of Wraye's presence there (014-043).
- **Confrontation with [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]]**: In a tense exchange, [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] reveals he knows Wraye's true identity. Wraye responds with controlled defiance rather than panic (025-039).
- **Connection with [[glassdale|Glassdale]]**: [[glassdale|Glassdale]], who was sent to [[barthorpe|Barthorpe]] by [[mark-ransford|Dr. Ransford]] to investigate, discovers Wraye and Flood's role in [[john-braden|Brake]]'s downfall and shares the information with [[mr-folliot|Folliot]] himself, unaware of [[mr-folliot|Folliot]]'s true identity (024-088).
- **Final exposure**: The truth of Wraye's identity is confirmed by [[glassdale|Glassdale]] and then by Ransford, who reveals to [[mary-bewery|Mary Bewery]] that Wraye and [[mr-folliot|Folliot]] are the same person — the man who caused her father's ruin (026-008) (027-027).

## Relationships

- **[[john-braden|John Brake]] ([[john-braden|John Braden]])** — Victim. Wraye's fraud against [[john-braden|Brake]] led to [[john-braden|Brake]]'s criminal conviction, imprisonment, and lifelong vendetta.
- **Flood** — Partner in crime. The lesser figure in the fraud, who also disappeared with the money.
- **[[mr-harker|Mr. Harker]]** — Indirect connection. [[john-braden|Brake]] sent [[mr-harker|Harker]] to [[barthorpe|Barthorpe]] to investigate Wraye, without revealing that the [[criminal-investigation|inquiry]] was personal (014-058).
- **[[pemberton-bryce|Pemberton Bryce]]** — Antagonist. [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]] deduces Wraye's true identity and attempts to blackmail him.
- **[[glassdale|Glassdale]]** — Unwitting informant. [[glassdale|Glassdale]]'s [[barthorpe|Barthorpe]] research uncovers the Wraye-Flood connection, which he inadvertently shares with Wraye himself (024-088).
- **[[mrs-folliot|Mrs. Folliot]]** — Wife. A wealthy widow whom Wraye married after returning to England (025-037).
- **[[sackville-bonham|Sackville Bonham]]** — Step-son (by marriage to [[mrs-folliot|Mrs. Folliot]]). A rival to [[dick-bewery|Dick Bewery]] for [[mary-bewery|Mary Bewery]]'s affections.
- **[[mark-ransford|Dr. Mark Ransford]]** — Adversary. Ransford, as [[john-braden|Brake]]'s old friend, knows the full history and ultimately brings the truth to light (027-027).

## Significance to the Mystery

Wraye is the axis around which the entire mystery revolves. Every major plot thread — [[john-braden|Brake]]'s arrival in [[wrychester|Wrychester]], his death at the cathedral porch, [[collishaw|Collishaw]]'s poisoning, [[pemberton-bryce|Bryce]]'s [[criminal-investigation|investigation]]s — traces back to Wraye's original crime and his subsequent [[secrecy-and-concealment|concealment]] of his identity. His presence in [[wrychester|Wrychester]] under the [[mr-folliot|Folliot]] alias creates the central dramatic irony of the novel: the respectable gentleman at the heart of cathedral society is the very man whose exposure would unravel everything.

The discovery of Wraye's identity serves as the novel's climactic revelation, connecting all the disparate threads — the [[barthorpe|Barthorpe]] [[criminal-investigation|investigation]]s, the [[john-braden|Braden]]/[[john-braden|Brake]] alias, the [[secrecy-and-concealment|secret]] meetings in [[paradise|Paradise]], and the motive behind the deaths at the cathedral.
