## Biography

Thorkell the High was the third son of Strut-Harald, a famous Earl in [[skaney|Skane]]y (004-002). He was among the lords of [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] present when [[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn Olafson]] arrived with his fleet seeking admission to the warrior brotherhood, and was accounted a "doughty man of his hands and a great champion" (004-002).

During the winter that Styrbiorn spent in Denmark after his flight from Sweden, Thorkell was at his father's hall in [[skaney|Skane]]y together with his brother Sigvaldi. They had been there all the winter and were glad at the coming of their friends and of Styrbiorn (008-003). When spring came, Thorkell sailed with Styrbiorn from [[skaney|Skane]]y south over the sea to [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]], with many ships and a great following (008-008).

His father Strut-Harald, in a candid farewell to Styrbiorn, judged Thorkell bluntly: "Thorkell is a man of war, but he hath little wit" (008-004). This assessment, while harsh, captures Thorkell's role in the saga: a formidable fighting man valued for his strength and loyalty rather than his counsel.

Thorkell's most significant moment comes at the [[battle-of-fyrisfield|Battle of Fyrisfield]]. After his eldest brother Sigvaldi "sneaked away under the cloak of darkness on [[skaney|Skane]]y-side," deserting the host on the eve of battle, Thorkell and his brother [[heming|Heming]] assumed command of the left wing. Remarkably, not even a tenth of Sigvaldi's own men would follow him in desertion; instead they "went all under [[heming|Heming]] and Thorkell," a fact that caused great wonder since Sigvaldi was commonly well followed (015-011). On the left wing, Thorkell and [[heming|Heming]] led the [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]] alongside the Danish contingent under [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald Gormson]]'s captains (015-011).

## Role in the Story

Thorkell the High, together with his brothers Sigvaldi and [[heming|Heming]], forms the Strut-Harald dynasty within the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] fellowship. The three brothers represent three types of warrior: Sigvaldi the cunning, [[heming|Heming]] the dependable follower, and Thorkell the blunt instrument. Thorkell's role in the narrative is that of the steadfast warrior — physically imposing ("the High"), limited in intellect but unwavering in loyalty.

His assumption of command after Sigvaldi's desertion at [[fyrisfield|Fyrisfield]] is his defining moment. Where Sigvaldi's cunning led him to treachery, Thorkell's simpler nature kept him true to his [[oath|oaths]]. The mass defection of Sigvaldi's men to Thorkell and [[heming|Heming]]'s command testifies to the respect that plain courage and fidelity could command even among men accustomed to following the more sophisticated Sigvaldi.

## Historical Basis

Thorkell the High (*Þorkell inn hávi*) is one of the best-attested [[jomsvikings|Jomsvikings]] in historical sources. He appears not only in the *Jómsvíkinga saga* but also in Anglo-Saxon records as a prominent Viking leader who campaigned in England in the early 11th century. The historical Thorkell led a large Viking army that ravaged southern England in 1009–1012, and he later entered the service of King Æthelred the Unready and subsequently King Cnut the Great. Eddison's portrait of Thorkell as a physically imposing warrior of limited cunning aligns with the saga tradition, though the historical Thorkell was evidently a more complex and politically adept figure than the "little wit" assessment suggests.
