# Palnatoki

## Biography

Palnatoki of Fion was the founder and captain of the fortified stronghold of [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]], the legendary home of the [[jomsvikings|Jomsvikings]]. He had built [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] with stone walls "about and about" and installed a sea-gate at the harbour mouth capable of admitting three hundred long-ships (004-001). Under his leadership, the [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]] were governed by a strict code of laws that he alone enforced: no man older than fifty or younger than eighteen might join, all must avenge a comrade's death as a brother's, no women were allowed within [[jomsburg|the burg]], and all plunder must be brought to the pole for common distribution (004-003, 004-004).

Palnatoki's first encounter with Styrbiorn came when the young Swede-prince arrived with sixty ships at [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]]'s seawall. Palnatoki, described as having a "nose like an eagle" (004-014), initially rejected Styrbiorn's request for guesting, remarking "I and my mates be no lovers of guests nor guestings" (004-013). When Styrbiorn refused to leave and the encounter escalated into a sea-fight, Palnatoki observed the young warrior with a careful and appraising eye, studying him "as a skipper studieth a ship or a horseman a horse" (004-019).

The sea-battle between Styrbiorn's ships and the [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]] proved fierce. After the broader fight ended with heavy casualties on both sides, Palnatoki and Styrbiorn faced each other in single combat on the bloodied poop of Styrbiorn's ship (004-051). Both men were grievously wounded. When Styrbiorn, too weary and bloodied to stand, still refused quarter, Palnatoki let fall his sword and declared, "Better side by side shall the swords of us two bite, than one against the other" (004-057). He then proclaimed peace from the ship's poop with a voice "like a trumpet of brass" (004-060).

In the years that followed, Palnatoki grew to hold Styrbiorn in such esteem that "none thought it ill that Styrbiorn should be called captain in [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] whensoever Palnatoki was away out of [[jomsburg|the burg]] about his own affairs whether in Fion or otherwhere" (005-001). When Styrbiorn later needed aid to press his claim to the Swedish throne, Palnatoki convened the council of [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] and declared unconditional support: "we shall give Styrbiorn aid not here nor there, nor for this or that, but freely and for what [[thing-stead|thing]] soever he will take in hand" (012-014). When Styrbiorn turned his fleet toward Denmark, Palnatoki -- though a Dane by kin and fosterer of [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald Gormson]]'s own son -- nonetheless resolved to follow him, saying "I will not be behindhand with thee in friendship" (012-030).

## Role in the Saga

Palnatoki serves as the guardian of the threshold between Styrbiorn's youth and his manhood. His testing of the young Swede in battle and his subsequent admission of him into [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] -- despite Styrbiorn being younger than the Law's minimum of eighteen winters -- marks Styrbiorn's entry into the world of warriors. Palnatoki is the figure who both judges and welcomes: scorner turned sponsor, adversary turned ally.

He is portrayed less as a character with an arc of his own than as a fixed star in the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] firmament: the embodiment of the Jomsviking code, a leader whose authority is absolute within his burg and unquestioned by his men. His support for Styrbiorn is given unconditionally, even when it means sailing against his own countrymen, establishing him as the moral centre of the warrior fellowship.

## Key Relationships

**[[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn the Strong]]** -- Palnatoki tested Styrbiorn's mettle in sea-combat and single combat, found him worthy, and admitted him into the Jomsviking fellowship. He grew to regard Styrbiorn as a peer, yielding him the captaincy in his absence and pledging him unconditional support from all of [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] (004-057, 005-001, 012-014).

**[[harald-bluetooth|King Harald Gormson]] of Denmark** -- A fellow Dane, Palnatoki fostered Harald's son under the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] Law of the [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]]. This tie complicated his loyalties when Styrbiorn resolved to sail against Denmark (012-028).

**Bui the Thick** -- One of the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] lords under Palnatoki's command, a hot-headed warrior whose taunting of Styrbiorn at their first encounter contrasted with Palnatoki's more measured assessment (004-016, 004-028).

**Sigvaldi** -- Son of [[strut-harald|Earl Strut-Harald]], a [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] lord who sat at Palnatoki's left hand in council and was more cautious than his captain in offering unconditional aid to Styrbiorn (012-014, 012-015).

## Historical Basis

Palnatoki (Old Norse: Palnatoki or Palna-Toki) is a semi-legendary figure celebrated in Norse saga tradition, most prominently in the *Jomsvikinga saga*. He is traditionally held to be the founder of the [[jomsvikings|Jomsvikings]], a fabled warrior brotherhood based at [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] on the southern Baltic coast. Some sources identify him as a chieftain from the Danish island of Fyn (Fion), and he is connected through saga tradition to both [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald]] Bluetooth's court and to the fostering of Harald's son Swein. The *Eyrbyggja Saga* and *Heimskringla* both reference the [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]], and Palnatoki appears as a figure of stern, warrior-like authority in the saga accounts. The novel follows these traditions closely while giving him a more rounded personality -- observant, deliberate, and capable of reversing his initial hostility into lasting friendship.
