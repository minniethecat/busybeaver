## Biography

Thorgisl was one of three men — along with [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]] and [[thorir|Thorir]] — held in good esteem by [[king-eric|King Eric]] the Victorious. Most folk believed they were the King's bastard sons, though none knew their fathers or kindred for certain (007-003). Their uncertain parentage was an open secret at the Swedish court, and [[king-eric|King Eric]]'s eye "rested kindly on them" despite their poor reputation among the common [[all-persons|people]].

Thorgisl appears throughout the middle chapters of the saga as a member of the courtly faction opposed to [[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn Olafson]]. When Styrbiorn returned from exile to claim his inheritance, Thorgisl and his companions were "none more forward to take him by the hand" with hollow welcomes (008-005). At the great [[hall-feast|banquet]] in [[upsala|Upsala]], Thorgisl sat with [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]] and [[thorir|Thorir]] on the upper bench near the door, where they drank and made quiet mockery of Styrbiorn's ambition (009-008).

During the [[hall-feast|banquet]], Thorgisl voiced a pragmatic resignation: "Well fares he that sits quiet with his own. Sith Styrbiorn is come into kingdom now in our despite, let us drink and be glad and kick at the pricks no more" (009-010). Yet his companions [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]] and [[thorir|Thorir]] continued to scheme, and Thorgisl joined in their whispered observations of the court — including the dark-haired bondmaid who caught [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]]'s eye (009-012).

Thorgisl was last seen among the court assemblies during the lead-up to the [[battle-of-fyrisfield|Battle of Fyrisfield]], standing with [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]] and [[thorir|Thorir]] "with sneering looks and the swagger of court bravery" (014-008). His ultimate fate is not explicitly described, but it is presumed he fell at [[fyrisfield|Fyrisfield]] alongside the rest of [[king-eric|King Eric]]'s host.

## Role in the Story

Thorgisl serves as part of a triadic antagonist group — the King's three bastards — who represent the petty, scheming court opposition to Styrbiorn. Unlike [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]], who is the most outspoken and aggressive of the three, Thorgisl tends toward a more cautious and observing role. He often tempers [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]]'s rashness with practicality (007-030), and at the [[hall-feast|banquet]] he is the first to suggest accepting Styrbiorn's ascendancy (009-010).

The three bastards' primary narrative function is to illustrate the factional tensions at [[king-eric|King Eric]]'s court. Their uncertain parentage, their closeness to the King, and their hostility toward Styrbiorn create a web of suspicion and political maneuvering that complicates Styrbiorn's claim to the Swedish throne.

## Relationships

- **[[helgi-ericson|Helgi]]** (companion): Eldest and most aggressive of the three bastards; Thorgisl often acts as a moderating voice to [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]]'s schemes (007-030)
- **[[thorir|Thorir]]** (companion): The third of the trio, who shares in the [[hall-feast|banquet]] mockery and court gossip (009-008)
- **[[king-eric|King Eric]] the Victorious** (presumed father): Showed favor to the three bastards, though never formally acknowledged them (007-003)
- **[[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn Olafson]]** (rival): The legitimate claimant whose return threatens the bastards' position at court (008-005)
- **[[thorgnyr-lawman|Thorgnyr the Lawman]]** (acquaintance): The three bastards once sought Thorgnyr's counsel but were rebuffed (007-036)

## Historical Basis

Thorgisl is likely a fictional character created by E.R. Eddison, drawing on the Norse saga convention of illegitimate royal sons as courtly antagonists. The name "Thorgisl" (Þorgísl) is a genuine Old Norse name meaning "Thor's hostage" or "Thor's pledge," common in the Viking Age. However, no historical Thorgisl matching this character's description appears in the known sources for the Styrbiorn legend (*Styrbjarnar þáttr Svíakappa*, *Heimskringla*).
