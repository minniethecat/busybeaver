# Sea-Battle at Jomsburg

## Summary

The sea-battle at [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] was the trial by combat that won [[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn the Strong]] his place among the [[jomsvikings|Jomsvikings]]. Arriving off [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] with sixty ships at summer's end, Styrbiorn hailed [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]] from the seawall and asked admission to the brotherhood. When [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]] rebuffed him as too young — "Thou art a boy. Come back in a year or twain, with a beard grown" (004-027) — Styrbiorn proposed a ship-to-ship fight: even numbers, equal odds, the winner to take all (004-032).

[[palnatoki|Palnatoki]], after considering that Styrbiorn might be either "a young braggart, or else a wolf-cub after mine own heart," accepted the challenge (004-033). Oaths were sworn to Thor, invoking "the southing sun, and the bright lightning-stream, and the rock of Victory-Tyr, and the ring of Ulli" (004-036). [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]] rowed out with his own ship's company against Styrbiorn's, while the fleets of either side waited at two bowshots' distance.

The ship-to-ship fighting was fierce and wound-heavy. [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]] slew several of Styrbiorn's men with his bow before they grappled. When the ships lay aboard, [[eystein-the-fox|Eystein the Fox]], a Norseman of Styrbiorn's company, nearly boarded [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]'s ship but was slain with an axe-hammer by [[bessi-thorlakson|Bessi Thorlakson]], who cried "So knock we the harns out of Foxes" (004-038). The [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]] eventually cleared Styrbiorn's ship; all his men were dead or felled, and he stood alone on the poop facing them.

[[palnatoki|Palnatoki]] offered generous terms: Styrbiorn could depart in peace with all his ships and gear. Styrbiorn refused, demanding single combat instead (004-041). [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]], against his own judgement, agreed: "It is sore against my liking to send to hell untimely so good a man as thou art" (004-046). In their duel, Styrbiorn fought with a bleeding shoulder wound and near exhaustion; [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]], light-footed and catlike, slipped on "a slime of half-clotted gore" and landed flat on his back (004-053). Styrbiorn lowered his sword: "Stand thou up, and let us play out the play" (004-054).

[[palnatoki|Palnatoki]] rose, threw down his sword, and took Styrbiorn's hand: "Better side by side shall the swords of us two bite, than one against the other" (004-057). He suspended the age restriction of the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] Law, and Styrbiorn entered [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] that day with "well an hundred of his men that were of right age and the likeliest among them" (004-061).

## Context

Styrbiorn had come to [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] as an exile, a prince without a kingdom. His two summers of raiding in the East had given him wealth and a fleet of sixty ships, but no home. [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] represented the one power in the North that could give him the strength to reclaim his inheritance — or so he calculated. The sea-battle was therefore not merely a trial of arms but a political gambit of the highest stakes: if he won, he would gain the Jomsviking brotherhood; if he lost, he would lose everything.

[[palnatoki|Palnatoki]], for his part, was a man in the fullness of his power — well past forty, the undisputed captain of the greatest warrior fortress in the North. His decision to accept the challenge was partly pride ("I am not the man I have ever known myself if a boy shall beat me," 004-033) and partly the recognition of something extraordinary in the young Swede: "that this is a young braggart, or else a wolf-cub after mine own heart" (004-033).

## Key Participants

**[[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn the Strong]]** — The challenger, a youth of perhaps sixteen winters who had already slain [[lambi-the-white|Lambi the White]] and raided two summers in the East. His refusal to yield, even when defeated, won him the admiration of the [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]] (004-041, 004-054).

**[[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]** — The captain of [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]], a warrior in his mid-forties, light-footed and iron-sinewed. His decision to take Styrbiorn's hand rather than his life transformed the novel's trajectory (004-057).

**[[eystein-the-fox|Eystein the Fox]]** — Styrbiorn's man from Norway, a famed axe-fighter who was killed by [[bessi-thorlakson|Bessi Thorlakson]] during the boarding fight (004-038).

**[[bui-digri|Bui the Stout]]** — Mocked Styrbiorn from the seawall: "He stuttereth like a pig at the trough" (004-018). His taunts set the tone of the [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]]' initial hostility.

**[[sigvaldi|Sigvaldi Jarl]]** — Opposed admitting Styrbiorn, "crooked of counsel" and "as ready for all deceits and flimflams as an oyster for a fresh tide" (004-033).

**[[earl-wolf|Earl Wolf]]** — Styrbiorn's foster-father, who counselled caution but was overruled. Styrbiorn kept him out of the fight so he could manage the fleet if things went badly (004-025, 004-034).

## Consequences

Styrbiorn's admission to [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] transformed him from an exiled youth with a fleet into a Jomsviking lord with a brotherhood behind him. Within a year, he was called captain in [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]'s absence, and the bonds of loyalty forged in this sea-battle — particularly the friendship with [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]] and Biorn Broadwicker — would carry him through the rest of the saga. The [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]] would follow him to [[fyrisfield|Fyrisfield]] and die with him there.

The battle also established the pattern of Styrbiorn's character: he would rather die than accept less than what he considered his due. [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]] offered him peace with honour; Styrbiorn chose single combat at the edge of death. This same stubbornness would later drive him to refuse Thorgnyr's peace offer on [[fyrisfield|Fyrisfield]] and to fight [[king-eric|King Eric]] to the last.

## Historical Basis

The sea-battle at [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] is the founding myth of the *Jomsvikinga saga*: the admission of a young champion who proves his worth against the established warriors of the fortress. The novel follows the saga tradition in making the combat a trial of both skill and character, with [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]] recognising in Styrbiorn a greatness that overrides the letter of the law. The historical [[jomsvikings|Jomsvikings]], if they existed, would have used such ritual combats (holmgang) as tests of admission and honour, and the [[oath|oaths]] sworn to Thor before the fight reflect authentic Norse practice.
