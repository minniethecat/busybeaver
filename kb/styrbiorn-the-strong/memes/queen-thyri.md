# Thyri Haraldsdottir

## Biography

Thyri was the daughter of [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald Gormson]] of Denmark. At the time of Styrbiorn's first visit to the Danish court during the Yule season, she was a young woman of spirit and discernment. When she perceived that her father harboured ill will toward the [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]] after Biorn's provocative song, she sought out Biorn Broadwicker in secret and warned him plainly: "you were best all get you out of the [[danish-kingdom|Dane-realm]] at your speediest" (006-001, 006-013). Biorn, taken aback, asked for a reason, and she spoke with painful honesty: "The King my father thinketh there is somewhat between me and Styrbiorn. 'Tis foolish, but I have marked him, and I know. There's danger in it. He would not have it so: he hath other plans: [[burisleif|King Burisleif]]" (006-023). Her father wished to marry her to the Wend-king, an old man whose age Biorn frankly remarked upon (006-024). Thyri's riposte, "Not old" (006-025), suggests either loyalty to her father's choice or a determination to appear uncomplaining before a near-stranger.

Thyri's warning was not born of mere apprehension. She knew that [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald]]'s plans for her ran counter to the growing bond between herself and Styrbiorn, and she feared that her father's displeasure might extend to violence. When Biorn suggested they had time on their side, she insisted: "Remember, Biorn, thou hast promised me" (006-033). Biorn saw that there was no turning her and resolved to draw Styrbiorn away to [[skaney|Skane]]y, Fion, or [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] (006-034).

As time passed, Styrbiorn's feelings toward Thyri deepened. At Yule in Denmark, Biorn pressed him to consider the match, noting that "here to thy hand is a maid the fairest that may be, and like-minded with thee, and of as mighty kindred as thou couldest desire" (005-017). Styrbiorn, though reluctant to speak of marriage, admitted "I like her," yet refused to commit (005-018). The troth-plighting was eventually performed with due ceremony at the [[hall-feast|banquet]] in [[upsala|Upsala]], where "the [[betrothal-feast|troth-plighting of Styrbiorn and Thyri]] was performed with due ceremony, and the [[hall-feast|banquet]] was the [[hall-feast|banquet]] of their betrothal, but the wedding should be after the [[thing-stead|Thing]], when Styrbiorn should have taken kingdom" (009-002).

The most dramatic moment of Thyri's story came when Styrbiorn determined to sail to Sweden by force. Defying the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] law that no woman be brought into [[jomsburg|the burg]] — a law enforced by [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]] alone — Styrbiorn brought Thyri to [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] for safety. When [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]] protested that she must go back, Styrbiorn refused, calling her "the Queen" and "the apple of mine eye" (013-019, 013-020). [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]], moved by Styrbiorn's passion, broke his own law: "I will do this for thee, Styrbiorn. And it is a thing I would not do for any other man, nor for myself neither. She shall come in" (013-023). When Styrbiorn's fleet sailed, Thyri came down to the ships with them, "brave and jolly of mien," though Biorn — remembering her dream at [[roiskeld|Roiskeld]] — knew she was hiding her terror at the prospect of her lord faring north to war (013-031).

## Role in the Story

Thyri is the catalyst whose very existence drives much of the political tension in the novel's middle chapters. Through her, the story explores the vulnerability of royal women in the marriage politics of the Viking Age: desired by Styrbiorn, bartered by her father to [[burisleif|King Burisleif]], and caught between the demands of love and duty. Her warning to Biorn in Chapter 6 demonstrates courage and political intelligence, while Styrbiorn's defiance of [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] law on her behalf — bringing a woman into the all-male stronghold — marks the depth of his devotion and his willingness to break even sacred codes for her sake.

## Key Relationships

**[[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn the Strong]]** — Betrothed to Thyri at the [[hall-feast|banquet]] in [[upsala|Upsala]] (009-002), Styrbiorn called her "the apple of mine eye" and defied [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]'s law to keep her safe in [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] (013-020).

**[[harald-bluetooth|King Harald Gormson]]** — Thyri's father, the Dane-king, who loved her but loved not the [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]] (006-027). He planned to marry her to [[burisleif|King Burisleif]] of the Wends rather than let her wed Styrbiorn (006-023).

**Biorn Broadwicker** — Thyri's confidant and ally at the Danish court. She trusted him with her secret fears and extracted his promise to draw Styrbiorn away from danger (006-001, 006-033).
