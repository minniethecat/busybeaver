# Kysingtarn

**Kysingtarn** was a tarn (small lake) in Sweden where a great ball-game gathering was held. It was at this gathering that [[styrbiorn-the-strong|Styrbiorn the Strong]] confronted [[helgi-ericson|Helgi Ericson]], leading to [[all-events|events]] that precipitated Styrbiorn's exile from Sweden.

## Location and Setting

The tarn of Kysingtarn lay within the [[swedish-kingdom|Swedish kingdom]], within riding distance of [[upsala|Upsala]]. In winter, the frozen surface of the tarn provided a natural arena for the ball-game, a popular Norse sport that drew participants from the surrounding countryside. The open space around the tarn allowed for large gatherings of spectators and players alike.

## The Gathering

A great gathering was made at Kysingtarn for playing at the ball. Styrbiorn attended with his chief followers: [[biorn-asbrandson|Biorn Asbrandson]] (the Broadwickers' Champion), [[bessi-thorlakson|Bessi Thorlakson]], [[gunnstein-lowry|Gunnstein Lowry]], and Odd o'Marklands, among other notable [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]] (007-002).

> On a day men made a great gathering at the tarn called Kysingtarn for playing at the ball. Styrbiorn was there and with him they that were chiefest among his following. (007-002)

The gathering at Kysingtarn was not merely a sporting event; it was a social occasion where rivalries and tensions among the Swedish nobility could surface. With Styrbiorn's growing discontent over [[king-eric|King Eric]]'s refusal to grant him his inheritance, and the presence of the King's own men, the scene was set for confrontation.

## The Confrontation with Helgi

When the folk had gathered at the tarn, Styrbiorn sought out [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]] directly. [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]] stood with [[thorir|Thorir]] and [[thorgisl|Thorgisl]], two of [[king-eric|King Eric]]'s trusted men. Styrbiorn approached and challenged them to play, a seemingly innocuous invitation that carried deeper significance given the political tensions of the moment (007-002).

The ball-game at Kysingtarn quickly turned rough. Norse ball-games were known for their physical intensity, and what began as sport escalated into a violent confrontation. The incident at Kysingtarn — where Styrbiorn's frustration with his uncle's court boiled over into open hostility — set in motion the chain of [[all-events|events]] described in the [[ball-game-kysingtarn|ball-game confrontation]] that ultimately led to Styrbiorn's departure from Sweden and his journey to [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]].

## Significance

The gathering at Kysingtarn represents a turning point in Styrbiorn's story. It was the moment when the simmering tensions between Styrbiorn and his uncle's court erupted into the open, making reconciliation impossible and forcing Styrbiorn to seek his fortune beyond the borders of Sweden. The tarn itself — a frozen lake in the Swedish winter — became the symbolic stage on which the break between nephew and uncle, between claimant and king, was made irreparable.
