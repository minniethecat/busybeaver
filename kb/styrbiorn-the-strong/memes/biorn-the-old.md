## Biography

Biorn the Old was a King of Sweden, of the ancient Yngling dynasty whose blood ran "even from that far time when the Gods came first from Asgarth of ancient days, and Yngvi-Frey dwelt among mankind in kingdom in [[upsala|Upsala]]" (001-001). He was the father of [[king-eric|Eric the Victorious]] and [[olaf-biornson|Olaf Biornson]], and the grandfather of [[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn]].

When Biorn the Old came to die, he left his two sons Eric and Olaf as joint Kings in Sweden, and "forty years they reigned together in good brotherly love and friendship" (001-002). This joint kingship arrangement established the precedent that Styrbiorn would later invoke when claiming his father Olaf's half of the kingdom.

## Role in the Story

Biorn the Old is a purely genealogical figure who appears only in the opening paragraphs of Chapter 1, establishing the dynastic lineage that legitimizes both Eric's rule and Styrbiorn's claim. His decision to divide the kingdom between his two sons is the constitutional precedent upon which the entire political conflict of the novel turns.

## Relationships

- **[[king-eric|Eric the Victorious]]**: His elder son, later sole King of Sweden
- **[[olaf-biornson|Olaf Biornson]]**: His younger son, father of Styrbiorn
- **[[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn the Strong]]**: His grandson, whose claim to half the kingdom derives from Biorn's original division

The joint kingship established the precedent for Styrbiorn's later claim:

> Now when King Biorn the Old was come to die, he left his two sons Eric and Olaf joint Kings in Sweden, and forty years they reigned together in good brotherly love and friendship.
>
> (001-002)
