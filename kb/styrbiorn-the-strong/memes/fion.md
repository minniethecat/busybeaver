## Description

Fion (modern Funen) is a large island in Denmark, situated between [[jutland|Jutland]] and Sealand. In Styrbiorn's time it was the homeland of [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]], the renowned [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] leader, who "dwelt in those days in [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]], but had a great place of his own in Fion" (004-001). The island served as [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]'s base when not at [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]], and as a strategic waypoint for Styrbiorn and his company moving between Denmark and the Viking strongholds.

## Role in the Story

### Palnatoki's Domain

[[palnatoki|Palnatoki of Fion]] was one of the most influential figures in the saga. During winter he "sat with [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]] in Fion" (004-061), hosting Styrbiorn and managing his own affairs "whether in Fion or otherwhere" (005-001). Fion represented an alternative center of power to [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]], where [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]] could operate with greater independence from the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] community.

### Strategic Waypoint

When Styrbiorn and his company were deciding their next destination after leaving [[alaburg|Alaburg]], Fion was listed alongside [[skaney|Skane]]y and [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] as one of the possible destinations: "whether to [[skaney|Skane]]y or to Fion or [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] or otherwhere" (006-034). [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]], before returning "home into Fion" (012-002), took Styrbiorn apart for private counsel, declaring "my home is in Fion" (012-028) as he distanced himself from Harald's court.
