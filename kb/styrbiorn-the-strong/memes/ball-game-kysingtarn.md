# Ball-Game at Kysingtarn

The **Ball-Game at Kysingtarn** is a pivotal confrontation in Chapter VII of *[[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn the Strong]]*, in which the simmering hostility between [[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn]] and the three illegitimate sons of [[king-eric|King Eric]] — [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]], [[thorgisl]], and [[thorir]] — erupts into open violence during a winter ball-game on the frozen tarn of [[kysingtarn|Kysingtarn]].

## Summary

On a winter's day during Styrbiorn's early return to Sweden, a gathering assembles at the tarn called [[kysingtarn|Kysingtarn]] for "playing at the ball" — a Norse winter sport similar to knattleikr (007-015). Styrbiorn, accompanied by his sworn brother [[biorn-broadwicker|Biorn the Broadwickers' Champion]], arrives and immediately joins the play.

From the outset, the game is charged with hostility. [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]] and his brothers set themselves against Styrbiorn, and the narrator records that Styrbiorn "played most against [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]], and [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]] gat ever the worst of it" (007-023). What begins as competitive sport rapidly descends into physical violence. The players are described as "keeking and girding" at one another (007-022), the playful insults of a ball-game shading into genuine malice.

The confrontation reaches its peak when, after a particularly aggressive tackle, [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]] appeals to his brothers to join against Styrbiorn. Biorn Broadwicker intervenes on Styrbiorn's behalf, and the incident threatens to become a full brawl. [[earl-wolf|Earl Wolf]] later interrogates the participants about the conduct of [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]] and his faction, asking sharply: "What say [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]] and his? Methinks they have been somewhat busy of late" (007-034).

## Context

### Political Undercurrents

The ball-game occurs against a backdrop of intense political tension. Styrbiorn has returned to Sweden months before his exile was due to end, arriving "with an hundred ships" at the Low (007-001). His early return alarms the court: "men wondered much at this and much guessing there was, what the King was like to do" (007-001).

[[helgi-ericson|Helgi]] and his brothers, who benefit from [[king-eric|King Eric]]'s favor, view Styrbiorn as a direct threat. Earlier, [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]] had suggested that Styrbiorn might send the infant Prince Olaf "to a cold lodging" (007-004), and had spread insinuations about Styrbiorn's relationship with [[queen-sigrid|Queen Sigrid]].

### The Setting: Kysingtarn

[[kysingtarn|Kysingtarn]] is a frozen tarn (small mountain lake) near [[upsala|Upsala]], used by the Swedish court for winter sports. The narrator notes the harsh conditions: "thicker fell the snow, until the ball was hid by it from the players' sight" (007-037). The wild weather mirrors the wild emotions unleashed on the ice, with [[queen-sigrid|Queen Sigrid]] herself coming to watch the play despite the storm (007-036).

## Consequences

### Deepening Feud

The ball-game marks a turning point: what was previously verbal sparring becomes physical combat. Styrbiorn's dominance on the ice humiliates [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]] publicly, intensifying the bastard brothers' hatred. This personal animosity will culminate at the [[battle-of-fyrisfield|Battle of Fyrisfield]], where [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]] leads the King's bodyguard against Styrbiorn's [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]].

### Styrbiorn's Position

The incident demonstrates Styrbiorn's physical prowess — he dominates the game despite being outnumbered — but also reveals the precariousness of his position at the Swedish court. He has powerful enemies operating openly against him, protected by the King's favor.

### Earl Wolf's Intervention

[[earl-wolf|Earl Wolf]]'s investigation of the incident (007-034) shows that Styrbiorn still has powerful protectors. [[earl-wolf|Wolf]], as the King's brother-in-law and Styrbiorn's foster-father, commands sufficient authority to challenge [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]]'s faction. His intervention pr[[all-events|events]] the incident from escalating into immediate bloodshed.

## Historical Basis

The ball-game at [[kysingtarn|Kysingtarn]] is a fictional scene by E.R. Eddison, though it draws on the well-documented Norse tradition of *knattleikr* — a violent ball-game played on ice or field, often used in the sagas as a setting for escalating feuds. Similar ball-game confrontations appear in *Egil's Saga* (Egill's match at Borg) and *Grettir's Saga*, where sporting [[all-events|events]] become vehicles for personal and political conflict. Eddison uses this saga convention to dramatize the growing enmity between Styrbiorn and [[helgi-ericson|Helgi]]'s faction.

Styrbiorn's physical dominance on the ice foreshadows his martial prowess:

> He played most against Helgi, and Helgi gat ever the worst of the market when they two came together. Until on a time when Helgi would have held him to keep him from the ball, Styrbiorn caught him and cast him down so rudely on the hard ice that for a minute's space or more he abode there senseless, and the blood gushed out of his nose, and his knees and knuckles were scraped raw with the rough ice.
>
> (007-023)
