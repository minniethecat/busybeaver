## Biography

King Burisleif was the ruler of the Wends, a Slavic [[all-persons|people]] dwelling on the southern Baltic coast in the region known as [[wendland|Wendland]]. He first enters the saga through the political calculations of [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald Gormson]] of Denmark, who planned to marry his daughter Thyri to the Wendish king rather than see her wed to [[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn Olafson]] (006-023). Thyri herself revealed this plan to Biorn Broadwicker in confidence, speaking plainly that her father "hath other plans: King Burisleif" (006-023).

When Styrbiorn first came to [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]], there was peace between the [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]] and King Burisleif, which may have deterred the Wends from attacking Styrbiorn during his solitary wanderings on the mainland (012-003). This peace, however, did not last. Styrbiorn, consolidating his power at [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]], carried war into [[wendland|Wendland]], determined that the Wends should no longer doubt whether [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] belonged to them or to its own defenders (012-026).

The ensuing campaign was of the hardest. King Burisleif gathered against Styrbiorn "so huge a throng of fighting men as outmanned the [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]] by five or six to one," seizing the river banks on both sides of the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] fleet and casting booms across the river to cut off their retreat (012-026). Despite these overwhelming odds, Styrbiorn won the day after a long day's battle. The peace terms compelled King Burisleif to acknowledge [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]'s dignity as an Earl, to maintain perpetual peace with [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] and treat its men as friends and allies, and to provide ships and men for Styrbiorn's future wars whenever called upon (012-026). Burisleif further gave Styrbiorn "great store of gifts and treasures" as part of the settlement (012-026).

The breaking of the old bridal pact between Harald and Burisleif — by which Thyri had been promised to the Wendish king — was one of the most remarked-upon outcomes of Styrbiorn's diplomatic triumph over Harald. Men "wondered much, thinking that here was a new strong wind set in, to blow so suddenly away that old standing bridal pact made with King Burisleif" (013-001).

## Role in the Story

Burisleif serves as both a political counterweight and a military foil to Styrbiorn. In the novel's marriage politics, he represents the establishment choice — an older king favoured by [[harald-bluetooth|Harald Gormson]] for his daughter — against whom Styrbiorn defines himself as the younger, more forceful suitor. Thyri's lack of enthusiasm for the match [012-038 to 012-039] makes Burisleif's betrothal a symbol of the political constraints placed upon royal women.

Styrbiorn's private ambitions place Burisleif among the three kings whose necks he dreams of putting his foot upon — alongside the King of [[danes|Danes]] and the great King in [[garthrealm|Micklegarth]] (011-019). The defeat of Burisleif in [[wendland|Wendland]] marks Styrbiorn's emergence as a military power in his own right, no longer dependent on Danish hospitality. The peace terms extracted from Burisleif transformed [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] from a defensive outpost into a recognized power with a Wendish alliance, providing the ships and men that would later enable Styrbiorn's invasion of Sweden.

## Relationships

- **[[queen-thyri|Thyri Haraldsdottir]]** (betrothed): Promised to Burisleif by her father, [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald Gormson]], though she did not consent to the match [006-023, 012-038 to 012-039]
- **[[harald-bluetooth|King Harald Gormson]]** (ally): Had arranged the bridal pact with Burisleif, which was later broken by Styrbiorn's demands (013-001)
- **[[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn Olafson]]** (enemy, later forced ally): Defeated Burisleif in [[wendland|Wendland]] and compelled him to a peace treaty that made the Wends allies of [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] (012-026)
- **[[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]** (indirect relationship): Recognized as an Earl under the peace terms Styrbiorn imposed on Burisleif (012-026)

## Historical Basis

The historical King Burisleif is generally identified with Bolesław I the Brave (c. 967–1025), the first King of Poland, though the chronology is uncertain. In Norse sagas, "Burisleif" (or *Búrizleifr*) is the Old Norse rendering of the Slavic name Bolesław. The *Jómsvíkinga saga* and other Scandinavian sources mention a King Burisleif of the Wends who had dealings with the [[jomsvikings|Jomsvikings]]. E.R. Eddison follows this saga tradition in making Burisleif the Wendish king whose marriage alliance with [[harald-bluetooth|Harald Bluetooth]] is undone by Styrbiorn's intervention.
