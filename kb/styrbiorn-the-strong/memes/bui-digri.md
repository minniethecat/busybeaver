# Bui the Stout

## Biography

Bui the Stout (Bui Digri) was a lord of [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] from [[borgundholm|Borgundholm]], brother to [[sigurd-cape|Sigurd Cape]]. He was one of the foremost champions among the [[jomsvikings|Jomsvikings]], present in [[jomsburg|the burg]] when Styrbiorn arrived with his fleet. Through his sister Tofa, who was married to [[sigurd-cape|Sigurd Cape]], Bui was brother-in-law to the three sons of [[strut-harald|Earl Strut-Harald]]: Sigvaldi, [[heming|Heming]], and [[thorkell-the-high|Thorkell the High]] (004-002).

Bui's nature revealed itself at once during Styrbiorn's first approach to [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]]. When [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]] gave Styrbiorn his gruff greeting from the wall, Bui interjected with mocking remarks: of Styrbiorn's defiant reply he cried, "Ay, and 'tis a bird hath claws" (004-016); of Styrbiorn's characteristic stutter he jeered, "He stuttereth like a pig at the trough" (004-018). His taunting rose to a crescendo when he shouted at the young Swede: "Home with thee to thy mammy, crow-chick. If I have not more hair of my tail than thou of thy face, let me end my days in a stinking swine-sty as dry-nurse to the new-weaned yelts" (004-028). When Styrbiorn replied with equal heat, Bui brandished his weapon, crying, "And I have a switch for to smite thy backside withal" (004-030).

Yet for all his bluster, Bui proved a steadfast ally. When Styrbiorn returned from Sweden humiliated and broken, and the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] lords gathered on the seawall to debate his cause, Bui was the first to demand violent retribution. On hearing that Styrbiorn had been cast out for the sake of a woman, he erupted: "Must thou be cast out o' the [[swedish-kingdom|Swede-realm]] and thine own lawful right for a wench sake? And were it possible for any shame-swollen toad to have the spit-proof face to outlive this disgrace, and not make garters of the guts of him that put it on us?" (012-017).

When Styrbiorn refused to wage war on his uncle, Bui disbelieved him, thinking it a test of their loyalty: "Thou dost jest with us. This is but to try us. Come, I'll go with thee. He shall find he hath laid for a pickrell and caught a firedrake. And I'll ask no guerdon but this, that I may lay in the first brand at the burning of [[upsala|Upsala]]" (012-019). Styrbiorn refused him that wish, though the exchange visibly strained his composure (012-020). The majority of the [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]] were of Bui's mind, ready to back Styrbiorn in this or anything else beside (012-022).

## Role in the Story

Bui the Stout is the embodiment of the [[berserk-gang|berserk]]er spirit that the [[jomsvikings|Jomsvikings]] were famed for: violent, reckless, fiercely loyal, and quick to take offence. His function in the narrative is twofold. At the first encounter, he provides comic and threatening friction, testing Styrbiorn's mettle with taunts that echo the bullying of an older boy toward a younger. At the council of Chapter 12, he becomes the storm-petrel of war, the voice demanding blood-revenge when Styrbiorn himself is sunk in melancholy. In both roles, Bui serves as foil and counterpoint — to Sigvaldi's caution, to Styrbiorn's restraint, to [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]'s measured deliberation. He is the id of the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] brotherhood, pure warrior appetite unconstrained by statecraft or prudence.

## Key Relationships

**[[sigvaldi|Sigvaldi Jarl]]** — Where Sigvaldi counselled caution and self-interest, Bui demanded war. The two represent opposite poles of the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] temperament: cunning diplomacy against hot-blooded action (012-021, 012-019).

**[[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn the Strong]]** — Bui began his relationship with Styrbiorn in mockery and ended it in fierce loyalty. Though he called Styrbiorn a "crow-chick" at their first meeting (004-028), he was later the loudest voice demanding war on Styrbiorn's behalf, pledging to lay the first torch to [[upsala|Upsala]] itself (012-019).

**[[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]** — Bui served under [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]'s command and, despite his violent nature, deferred to [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]'s authority as captain of [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] (004-002).
