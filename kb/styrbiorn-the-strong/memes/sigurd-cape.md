## Biography

Sigurd Cape was a lord of [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]], the son of Veseti of [[borgundholm|Borgundholm]] ([[borgundholm|Bornholm]]) and brother of Bui the Thick [004-002, 012-002]. He was among the foremost champions of the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] fellowship, present in [[jomsburg|the burg]] when [[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn Olafson]] arrived with his fleet of sixty ships.

Through his marriage to Tofa, the daughter of [[strut-harald|Earl Strut-Harald]] of [[skaney|Skane]]y, Sigurd Cape was brother-in-law to the three sons of Strut-Harald: Sigvaldi, [[heming|Heming]], and [[thorkell-the-high|Thorkell the High]] (004-002). This marriage web connected the two great families of the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] world — the [[borgundholm|Borgundholm]] lineage of Veseti and the [[skaney|Skane]]y dynasty of Strut-Harald — creating a network of kin obligations that underpinned the fellowship's political cohesion.

After Styrbiorn's first winter in [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]], Sigurd returned to [[borgundholm|Borgundholm]] with his brother Bui to winter at their father Veseti's hall, as was the custom of the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] lords when the harrying season ended (012-002). He was also among the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] lords whom [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald Gormson]] questioned at [[alaburg|Alaburg]] after Styrbiorn's departure, finding them "but a dry well to draw from" — loyal to Styrbiorn and unyielding to royal pressure (013-018).

At the [[battle-of-fyrisfield|Battle of Fyrisfield]], Sigurd Cape fought on the right wing under his brother Bui's command, together with "a great host of the [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]] and the Wendish levies and others from the east" (015-011). This was the wing that faced the brunt of [[king-eric|King Eric]]'s attack, and the saga's description of the battle array [[all-places|places]] Sigurd among the most trusted and senior of the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] commanders.

## Role in the Story

Sigurd Cape appears in the saga primarily as a supporting figure within the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] fellowship — the loyal brother of the more vividly characterized Bui the Thick. His narrative function is to help populate the council scenes and battle arrays with named warriors whose presence conveys the breadth and depth of the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] alliance.

Sigurd's marriage to Tofa is of structural importance to the saga's political [[all-places|geography]]. By marrying Strut-Harald's daughter, Sigurd created a blood tie between [[borgundholm|Borgundholm]] and [[skaney|Skane]]y, binding the two most powerful families in the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] orbit. This network of kinship obligations — reinforced by the shared oath to [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]'s law — was the invisible architecture upon which [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]]'s military power rested.

His presence at [[fyrisfield|Fyrisfield]], commanding alongside Bui on the right wing, [[all-places|places]] him at the heart of the saga's climactic battle. The right wing faced the heaviest fighting against [[king-eric|King Eric]]'s Swedes, and while the saga does not dwell on Sigurd's individual deeds, his inclusion in the battle array signals his status as one of the [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]]' principal fighting men.

## Historical Basis

Sigurd Cape (*Sigurðr kápa*) appears in the *Jómsvíkinga saga* as the brother of Bui the Thick (*Búi digri*) and a prominent Jomsviking. The nickname "Cape" (*kápa*) may refer to a distinctive cloak or garment. His father Veseti (or Véseti) is named in the saga sources as a prominent figure from [[borgundholm|Borgundholm]] ([[borgundholm|Bornholm]]). The marriage connection between Sigurd and Tofa, daughter of Strut-Harald, is attested in the saga tradition and represents the kind of dynastic alliance that was common among the Viking Age aristocracy of the Baltic region. Unlike Bui, who became a legendary figure celebrated in Scandinavian folklore, Sigurd Cape remains a more shadowy historical presence, known primarily through his family connections and his appearance in the battle array at Hjörungavágr (the historical battle that Eddison transforms into [[fyrisfield|Fyrisfield]]).
