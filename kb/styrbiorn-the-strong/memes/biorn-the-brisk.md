## Biography

Biorn the Brisk was one of the named lords dwelling in [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] under [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]'s captaincy at the time of [[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn]]'s first arrival (004-002). He is listed alongside [[bui-digri|Bui the Thick]] of [[borgundholm|Borgundholm]], [[sigurd-cape|Sigurd Cape]], and the three sons of [[strut-harald|Strut-Harald]] — [[sigvaldi|Sigvaldi]], [[heming|Heming]], and [[thorkell-the-high|Thorkell the High]] — as one of the lords "all in their prime age and with power and means to hand with their will" (004-002).

His by-name "the Brisk" suggests a reputation for quickness and energy, fitting the character of a viking warrior in his prime.

## Role in the Story

Biorn the Brisk appears only in the opening roll-call of [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] lords in Chapter 4. Like several of the named [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]], his role is to establish the depth and strength of [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]'s following, demonstrating why [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] was such a formidable power in the Baltic world. He has no speaking lines or further appearances in the narrative.

## Relationships

- **[[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]**: His captain in [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]]
- **[[bui-digri|Bui Digri]], [[sigurd-cape|Sigurd Cape]]**: Fellow [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] lords
- **Sigvaldi, [[heming|Heming]], [[thorkell-the-high|Thorkell the High]]**: Fellow [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] lords, sons of Strut-Harald

Styrbiorn's arrival at Jomsburg brought him into the company of formidable warriors:

> There was as then with Palnatoki in Jomsburg these lords, to wit: Bui the Thick of Borgundholm and Sigurd Cape, his brother; and Biorn the Brisk; and those three sons of Strut-Harald that was in that time a famous Earl in Skaney, Sigvaldi namely and Heming and Thorkell the High.
>
> (004-002)
