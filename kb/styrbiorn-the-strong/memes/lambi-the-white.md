## Biography

Lambi the White was a bonder from [[stocksound|Stocksound]] in [[tenthland|Tenthland]] who became the focus of the bonder-folk's resistance against Styrbiorn. When [[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn Olafson]], then fifteen winters old, slew [[aki|Aki]] of the King's bodyguard at a [[hall-feast|feast]] in [[upsala|Upsala]] and refused to pay blood-money for the killing, the bonders grew deeply discontented (002-001). Though [[king-eric|King Eric]] himself paid the boot and quieted the murmuring for a time, the resentment simmered beneath the surface.

In the King's absence the following spring, a [[thing-stead|Thing]] was summoned in [[upsala|Upsala]] against all law — the King not being present — and fierce disputes broke out between the bonder-folk and Styrbiorn with his friends (002-004). At this [[thing-stead|Thing]], Styrbiorn declared loudly that he was, by his father's right, King of half the realm of Sweden. The bonders, in a tumult of rage, carried matters with so high a hand that they chose and proclaimed Lambi the White as King in Styrbiorn's despite, claiming that half of the kingdom which Styrbiorn claimed for his own.

[[king-eric|King Eric]], learning of these [[all-events|events]], regarded Lambi with contempt — "a fly: a gob of spittle" — but recognized the danger of the precedent (002-061). He bade [[thorgnyr-lawman|Thorgnyr the Lawman]] warn Lambi to keep quiet or depart the land. Styrbiorn himself asked the King's leave to slay Lambi, but Eric forbade him to fight with Lambi within Sweden or its coasts, though he permitted the encounter if they should meet in the outlands or on the main seas (002-061).

Styrbiorn later recounted that during his harrying abroad, he slew "mine uncle's [[outlawry|outlaw]], Lambi the White, that was a sea-king of great note," off [[skaney|Skane]]y-side (004-020).


> They feared not to choose and proclaim one Lambi the White, a man from Stocksound in Tenthland, King in Styrbiorn's despite of that half of the kingdom claimed by him; and therewith the Thing broken up in a tumult, so that it had wanted but a little to have befallen a battle betwixt the King's men and the bonders.
>
> (002-004)

## Role in the Story

Lambi serves as a narrative device demonstrating the fragility of Eric's rule and the bonder-folk's deep-seated resistance to Styrbiorn's fierce and unbridled character. His proclamation as a rival king crystallizes the central political tension of the early chapters: whether Styrbiorn's claim to joint kingship will be recognized or contested. Lambi's subsequent slaying by Styrbiorn also serves as an early demonstration of Styrbiorn's prowess as a warrior, foreshadowing his later career.

## Relationships

- **[[king-eric|King Eric]]**: Regarded Lambi with contempt but political awareness; warned him rather than executing him outright
- **Styrbiorn**: Lambi's usurpation was directly aimed at Styrbiorn's claim; Styrbiorn later slew him in single combat
- **The bonder-folk**: Chose Lambi as their champion against Styrbiorn's lawlessness
- **[[thorgnyr-lawman|Thorgnyr the Lawman]]**: Tasked by Eric with managing Lambi quietly
