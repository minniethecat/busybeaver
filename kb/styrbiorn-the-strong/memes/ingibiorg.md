# Ingibiorg

**Ingibiorg** was a Queen of Sweden, wife of King [[olaf-biornson|Olaf Biornson]], and mother of [[styrbiorn-the-strong|Styrbiorn the Strong]]. She was the daughter of Earl [[thrand-of-sula|Thrand of Sula]]. Her early death, only three years after Styrbiorn's birth, meant that her son never knew his mother, and her brother [[earl-wolf|Earl Wolf]] became Styrbiorn's foster-father and lifelong protector.

## Family and Marriage

Ingibiorg married [[olaf-biornson|King Olaf]] Biornson, who ruled Sweden jointly with his brother King [[king-eric|Eric]]. Through this marriage, she became Queen of Sweden. Her father, Earl [[thrand-of-sula|Thrand of Sula]], was a nobleman of Norway, giving Ingibiorg a lineage that connected the Swedish royal house with the Norwegian aristocracy (001-002).

> Now when King Biorn the Old was come to die, he left his two sons Eric and Olaf joint Kings in Sweden, and forty years they reigned together in good brotherly love and friendship. King Olaf Biornson had to wife Ingibiorg, daughter of Earl Thrand of Sula. (001-002)

Her brother, [[earl-wolf|Earl Wolf]], held an important position in the Swedish court, serving as a trusted advisor to [[king-eric|King Eric]]. This connection ensured that Styrbiorn would be raised by a capable foster-father with strong ties to both the royal household and the Norwegian nobility.

## Children

Queen Ingibiorg bore five children: four daughters — Thora, Thurid, Asdis, and Aud — and finally a son, Biorn, who would later be known as [[styrbiorn-the-strong|Styrbiorn the Strong]] (001-002).

> Their children were Thora and Thurid and Asdis and Aud. Ungainly it seemed to King Olaf that he should get none but girl-children. Howso, in the end it befell that Queen Ingibiorg was brought to bed of a man-child, and he was sprinkled with water and named Biorn after his father's father. (001-002)

The long wait for a male heir — after four daughters — made Styrbiorn's birth especially significant. He was named Biorn after his paternal grandfather, [[biorn-the-old|King Biorn the Old]], establishing his claim to the Swedish royal line.

## Death

Queen Ingibiorg died approximately three years after the birth of her son Styrbiorn. Her brother, [[earl-wolf|Earl Wolf]], later became Styrbiorn's foster-father, stepping into the void left by her early death (001-002).

> But the Queen lived not long, but died the third year after this. (001-002)

## Political Aftermath

Ingibiorg's death had profound consequences for the Swedish succession. When [[olaf-biornson|King Olaf]] died shortly after — under circumstances that many suspected to be poisoning — the young Styrbiorn was left an orphan. [[king-eric|King Eric]] became the sole ruler of Sweden, and the joint kingship arrangement established by [[biorn-the-old|Biorn the Old]] was broken.

According to custom, Styrbiorn should have inherited his father Olaf's half-share of the Swedish kingdom. However, [[king-eric|King Eric]] withheld the inheritance, arguing that Styrbiorn was too young. This refusal became the central conflict of Styrbiorn's youth — each year at the autumn [[blood-offering|sacrifice]], Styrbiorn would demand his father's kingdom, and each year Eric would defer him, saying "when thou shalt be sixteen winters old" (001-022).

[[earl-wolf|Earl Wolf]], as Ingibiorg's brother and Styrbiorn's foster-father, was caught between his loyalty to [[king-eric|King Eric]] and his duty to protect Styrbiorn's interests. He served as mediator between uncle and nephew, urging patience while privately acknowledging the justice of Styrbiorn's claim. The tension between these loyalties defined [[earl-wolf|Wolf]]'s role throughout Styrbiorn's life, culminating in his embassy to [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald Gormson]] to negotiate Styrbiorn's marriage to [[thyri|Thyri]] (008-031).
