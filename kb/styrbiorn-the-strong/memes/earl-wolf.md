# Earl Wolf

## Biography

Earl Wolf was a Swedish nobleman, the brother of Styrbiorn's mother, who was appointed by [[king-eric|King Eric]] to foster the young prince after the death of [[olaf-biornson|King Olaf]] (001-002). He appears first in Chapter 1, mediating between the King and his headstrong nephew on the night of the autumn [[blood-offering|sacrifice]] [[hall-feast|feast]] when Styrbiorn, aged fifteen, refused to attend the King's hall and instead sat brooding on his father's [[howe]] (001-003 to 001-016).

When the King's bodyguards returned empty-handed from their summons, Eric dispatched Earl Wolf personally to fetch the boy. The Earl climbed the steep, windswept howe to find Styrbiorn "unmoved as it, face to the wind," wrapped in a purple mantle and wearing a heavy collar of pure gold (001-010). Earl Wolf spoke to him as a father would, putting an arm about him and reminding him that the King would give him his inheritance when he reached sixteen winters (001-012). Styrbiorn shook him off, calling him "foster-father" but declaring, "Little men's redes, foster-father, shall not serve my turn" (001-013). The Earl chased him back to the King's hall, barely overtaking him at the great door (001-016).

Earl Wolf accompanied Styrbiorn on his viking expedition southward and was present at [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] when the sea-battle erupted between Styrbiorn's ships and [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]'s [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]]. Standing beside Styrbiorn on the poop of the battle-ship, the Earl warned him at a whisper: "This is a rash folly, as I told thee" (004-025). When Styrbiorn insisted on fighting alone, he told the Earl, "I will not have thee with me in this battle, foster-father," trusting him instead to keep the Swede-folk together if Styrbiorn should fall (004-034). The Earl replied, "It is wonderful that I do love thee well enough to humour thee even in this" (004-035).

The Earl's deepest test came when Styrbiorn returned to Sweden with invasion at heart. After the war-arrow was sent out and the [[thing-stead|Thing]] gathered at [[upsala|Upsala]], Earl Wolf rode "dirtied up to the horse's belly" to [[king-eric|King Eric]]'s hall, seeking peace (014-011). He begged the King to relent -- to take back the whole kingdom into his own hand and let Styrbiorn be heir, or at least to undo the crowning of the infant Olaf. "For your old love sake and kinship, Lord, will you not bend, and it were but one finger's breadth?" (014-030). When Eric refused with cold finality, the Earl's face turned "ashen-hued" (014-028). Later, at Styrbiorn's camp on the eve of the [[fyrisfield|Fyrisfield]] battle, he urged his foster-son to pray to the Gods for victory (015-057).

## Role in the Saga

Earl Wolf is the novel's figure of reason and love trapped between two immovable forces. He loves both his kinsman Eric and his foster-son Styrbiorn, and the tragedy of the saga is nowhere more painfully embodied than in his fruitless attempt to reconcile them. He is the voice of moderation in a world of extremes, and his failure to prevent the catastrophe underscores the saga's fatalistic view of human affairs.

Throughout the story, Earl Wolf's love for Styrbiorn is presented as a father's love given freely to a child not his own. His presence at [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] and his willingness to risk his life on the voyage south testify to a devotion that goes beyond duty. The King himself acknowledged this bond when he said, "He is thy dog, I'll not gainsay it" (002-002).

## Key Relationships

**[[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn the Strong]]** -- Earl Wolf's foster-son, the child of his sister. The Earl raised him from the age of five, counselled him through his confrontations with [[king-eric|King Eric]], followed him to [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]], and made a final desperate bid for peace between uncle and nephew (001-002, 004-034, 014-028).

**[[king-eric|King Eric]] the Victorious** -- Eric's man and kinsman, Earl Wolf served the King loyally but never hesitated to speak plainly when the King's stubbornness threatened to destroy both houses. Their tense interview in the hall before the battle reveals the limits of the Earl's loyalty (014-011 to 014-036).

**[[thorgnyr-lawman|Thorgnyr the Lawman]]** -- The Earl and Thorgnyr appear together as onlookers at the ball-game during the tense period of Styrbiorn's return to Sweden, both men sharing the role of elder observers watching [[all-events|events]] they cannot control (008-007).

## Historical Basis

Earl Wolf (Old Norse: Ulfr) is a figure from the saga traditions surrounding Styrbiorn. In the *Styrbjarnar thattr Sviakappa* and related texts, Styrbiorn's fostering by his maternal uncle is a standard element of the story. The figure of the uncle-as-foster-father is a common motif in Norse saga literature, where it often creates divided loyalties that drive the narrative. The novel develops Earl Wolf beyond this conventional role, giving him emotional depth and a tragic awareness that makes him more than a functional plot device.
