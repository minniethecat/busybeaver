# Hlymdale

**Hlymdale** was a legendary location from the Nibelung cycle of Germanic mythology, referenced in verse within the saga. It was the dwelling of Heimir and the place where the valkyrie Brynhild spent her youth.

## In the Saga

Hlymdale appears in a poetic passage recited by [[biorn-asbrandson|Biorn Asbrandson]] (as Hild the Helm'd) during Chapter 12. The verse describes the speaker's time with Heimir in Hlymdale before her fateful involvement with the heirs of Giuki (012-029).

> I was with Heimir in Hlymdale of old:
> Seasons eight I sat there in joy. (012-029)

The passage continues, identifying the speaker as "Hild the Helm'd" — the name by which she was known in Hlymdale (012-031).

> All they hight me in Hlymdale of old
> Hild the Helm'd, whoso knew me. (012-031)

## Mythological Context

In Norse legend, Hlymdale is associated with the story of Brynhild, the shield-maiden and valkyrie who appears in the *Völsunga saga* and the *Poetic Edda*. The verse quoted in the saga draws from this legendary tradition, evoking the tragic tale of broken [[oath|oaths]] and lost love that forms part of the Nibelung cycle.
