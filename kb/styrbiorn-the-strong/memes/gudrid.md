## Biography

Gudrid was the wife of [[skogul-tosti|Skogul-Tosti]], the mightiest and noblest of all men of Sweden who lacked title of dignity, dwelling at Hawkby in [[arland|Arland]] (003-002). She was of the kin of the Earls of [[west-gautland|West Gautland]], bringing noble blood to her marriage. Though described as "fair to look on," folk deemed her "over proud and grasping and somewhat hard of heart" (003-002).

Gudrid took an active role in the political maneuvering surrounding her daughter [[queen-sigrid|Sigrid]]'s marriage prospects. When [[king-eric|King Eric]] came to guest at [[arland|Arland]] and showed marked attention to Sigrid at the [[hall-feast|feast]] — having her bear the cup to him and seating her beside him in the high seat — it was Gudrid who most clearly perceived the significance. While her husband [[skogul-tosti|Tosti]] was confident the match with Styrbiorn was as good as settled ("Beached it is, and laid up"), Gudrid maintained a shrewder wariness (003-009)–(003-010).

She was the first to recognize that Sigrid might refuse even a royal match, noting that her daughter had "said no to a dozen ere now, and not one of them but had been a great match" (003-014), including [[harald-the-grenlander|Harald the Grenlander]], who had since become a king. Gudrid attributed Sigrid's wilfulness to [[skogul-tosti|Tosti]]'s indulgence: " 'Tis thy giving of her her will too much, and cockering of her. She'd ne'er mind me, and now not thee neither" (003-022).

When [[skogul-tosti|Tosti]] observed that Sigrid had inherited her stubborn nature from her mother — "a right daughter of thine, mistress" — Gudrid laughed, acknowledging the truth of it (003-017). Her political instincts proved correct when Sigrid did indeed refuse the match, though not for the reason any had expected: the King had been wooing for himself, not for Styrbiorn.

> His wife was named Gudrid. She was a stirring woman, of the kin of the Earls of West Gautland. She was fair to look on, but folk deemed her over proud and grasping and somewhat hard of heart.
>
> (003-002)
## Role in the Story

Gudrid appears only in Chapter 3, in the private conversation with her husband after the King's first night of [[hall-feast|feast]]ing at Hawkby. Her role is to provide dramatic irony and narrative foreshadowing: while [[skogul-tosti|Tosti]] confidently assumes the King's interest in Sigrid is on Styrbiorn's behalf, Gudrid — watching her daughter's face — senses something amiss. Her caution ("the ship's not beached yet") proves prescient. She also establishes Sigrid's character by proxy, revealing the history of rejected suitors and the stubborn will that will define Sigrid throughout the saga.

## Relationships

- **[[skogul-tosti|Skogul-Tosti]]**: Her husband, whom she ruled through sharp wit and sharper observations
- **Sigrid** (later [[queen-sigrid|Queen Sigrid]]): Her daughter, whose fierce independence she both recognized and failed to control
- **[[king-eric|King Eric]]**: The royal guest whose intentions she correctly suspected before her husband did
