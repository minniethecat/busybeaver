## Description

Kirialaland appears in the saga as one of the remote and semi-mythical regions at the edge of the Norse world. It was described as a place of "unknown [[all-places|places]]" (008-010) and ancient sanctuaries, home to "the temple amid the wolds of Kirialaland" (011-001) where barbarous [[all-persons|people]]s practiced their worship. The land held a dual character — both a real geographic destination for Viking voyagers and a symbol of the mysterious east.


> Styrbiorn and his folk coming to that place found not indeed any troll nor skin-changer, but they sacked Jomala's temple and took the belt of him, that was of a fashion that none yet had seen, wrought with nickers and other worms intertwisted, and took besides so great a spoil of silver and costly treasures as no man could ever remember to have seen taken in one place and in a single day.
>
> (008-010)

## Role in the Story

### The Eastern Voyage

[[bui-digri|Bui Digri]], one of the most formidable [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] lords, "fared north into [[biarmaland|Biarmaland]] and the unknown [[all-places|places]] of Kirialaland, being thither drawn by the mystery that hung over those countries" (008-010). The voyage to Kirialaland represented the outer limit of Norse exploration in the saga — a place beyond the familiar routes of trade and raid.

### The Temple Episode

The temple in Kirialaland became the subject of a notable episode. Bui and his companions "came to the temple amid the wolds of Kirialaland, and the barbarous [[all-persons|people]] had gathered there for their rites" (011-001). The encounter with this alien worship served to emphasize the strangeness of the eastern lands and the boldness of the [[jomsvikings|Jomsvikings]] who dared venture there.

### Symbolic Significance

In the saga's [[all-places|geography]], Kirialaland stands at the opposite extreme from the familiar world of Danish and Swedish courts. When [[all-persons|characters]] speak of fleeing or disappearing, they invoke Kirialaland as an ultimate refuge: "over seas to Kirialaland or the Finn-heaths of Smaland" (012-061) — a place beyond the reach of kings and feuds.
