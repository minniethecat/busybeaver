# Aki of the King's Bodyguard

## Biography

Aki was a man of good kin among the bonders, serving in [[king-eric|King Eric]] the Victorious's bodyguard at [[upsala|Upsala]]. His role in the saga is brief but consequential: his death at Styrbiorn's hands propels the narrative of the young prince's violent and ungovernable nature, and provides the political friction that [[king-eric|King Eric]] must later smooth over with blood-money.

The killing occurred on the night of the autumn blood-offering, when Styrbiorn — then fifteen winters old — had refused to attend the King's [[hall-feast|feast]] and instead sat upon his father's howe in protest. When Styrbiorn was finally persuaded to enter the hall and take his place, he exchanged harsh words with the King's men who had been sent to fetch him, calling them "King's thralls" (001-007). The insult festered.

Later, when the ale had flowed freely and men's discretion had thinned, Aki — "bethinking him not at all that he should as well tickle a [[earl-wolf|wolf]] under the chin as fret Styrbiorn now" — plucked at the young prince's kirtle and demanded amends for the slur cast upon him and his comrades (001-027). Styrbiorn, in a fury, threw his cloak over Aki's head, drenching him in spilled ale. Aki struck back, driving a drinking-horn into Styrbiorn's nose until the blood gushed (001-028). He tried to flee but Styrbiorn caught him in the doorway, wrestled him down, wrenched a sax-knife from Aki's own hand, and drove it into his neck, "clean up to the heft of the knife." Aki died instantly (001-028).

The aftermath was political. Aki was "a man of good kin among the bonders, and many were there who would have done vengeance for him on Styrbiorn without more ado, but that the place and the King's majesty put them in awe" (001-029). The bonder-folk murmured against Styrbiorn's lawless violence and the fact that he paid no weregild for the killing. In the end, [[king-eric|King Eric]] himself paid the blood-money, and the discontent subsided (001-029, 007-008).

> The cup had by then gone many a time about the tables, and men's bellies were well bulked with ale and their wise discretion and judgement something befogged withal. And as in such a season a man will easily let loose his mind to stray into forbidden paths, so Aki of the King's bodyguard —bethinking him, as is most likely, that his fortune was as good as another man's— came up to Sigrid with his ale-horn and made her an offer of his own.
>
> (001-027)
## Role in the Story
The fatal blow fell in the doorway of the hall:

> "Hold thy tongue, King's thrall," said Styrbiorn; and, with the word, cast off the cloak from his shoulders and smothered it over Aki's head. Now Aki had in his hand a great silver-rimmed drinking-horn all full of ale, and it spilt all down the neck of him. He leapt up and cast away the cloak and drave the horn at Styrbiorn's nose so that the blood gushed out of his nose and he staggered back and well nigh into the fire. Aki ran out, but Styrbiorn overtook him in the doorway and caught him by the collar and jerked him backwards. Young as he was, Styrbiorn had yet the strength of an ordinary man full-grown. He was mad wroth, and he drove the man down under him with fist and knee. Aki had by now pulled out a sax-knife and aimed to smite him withal. Styrbiorn rived it out of his hand and drove it into Aki's neck and down into the man's body, clean up to the heft of the knife. Aki needed no other blow but was dead at once.
>
> (001-028)


[[slaying-of-aki|Aki's death]] is the first blood Styrbiorn sheds in the saga. Though Aki is barely a character — he speaks only through the narrator's report of his actions — his killing establishes Styrbiorn's character in terms the saga-world would immediately recognise: hot-tempered, dangerous when provoked, physically formidable beyond his years, and heedless of consequence. The political fallout, with the bonders demanding vengeance and the King paying boot from his own treasury, foreshadows the larger pattern of Styrbiorn's life: a man whose passions create debts that others must settle.

## Key Relationships

**[[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn the Strong]]** — Aki's killer. Styrbiorn was fifteen winters old when he killed Aki in the hall brawl, a slaying for which no weregild was paid by the killer himself (001-027, 001-028).

**[[king-eric|King Eric]] the Victorious** — Aki served in the King's bodyguard. After the killing, [[king-eric|King Eric]] paid the blood-money to the bonder-kin to settle the matter and quiet the discontent (007-008).

**The Bonder-folk** — Aki was of good kin among the bonders, and his death stirred "mighty discontent" among them that required royal intervention to settle (007-008).
