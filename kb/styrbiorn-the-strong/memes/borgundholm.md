# Borgundholm

**Borgundholm** (modern Bornholm) is an island in the Baltic Sea, known as the home of [[bui-digri|Bui the Thick]] and [[sigurd-cape|Sigurd Cape]], two prominent lords of [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]].

## The Lords of Borgundholm

Bui the Thick and his brother [[sigurd-cape|Sigurd Cape]] were among the chief lords in [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] under [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]. [[sigurd-cape|Sigurd]] was married to Tofa, daughter of [[strut-harald|Strut-Harald]], the famous Earl of [[skaney|Skaney]], making the brothers brothers-in-law to Sigvaldi, [[heming|Heming]], and [[thorkell-the-high|Thorkell the High]] (004-003).

> There was as then with Palnatoki in Jomsburg these lords, to wit: Bui the Thick of Borgundholm and Sigurd Cape, his brother... (004-003)

## Role in Styrbiorn's Campaign

When [[styrbiorn-the-strong|Styrbiorn the Strong]] gathered the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] fleet for his campaign against Sweden, [[bui-digri|Bui of Borgundholm]] and [[sigurd-cape|Sigurd]] sailed to [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] to join the host (010-005).

During the winter after Styrbiorn's initial mustering, the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] lords dispersed to their home territories. Bui and [[sigurd-cape|Sigurd]] returned to Borgundholm to stay with their father, the old man Veseti (014-003).

And thither came, some today and some tomorrow till they were all gathered thither, the other lords of Jomsburg, Palnatoki and Bui of Borgundholm and Sigurd his brother and many more, until the burg was filled with men and the harbour thick with long-ships, like a stagnant pool under sallows in the season of the year when the leaves do fall, and the leaves of the sallows lie so many on the face of the pool that hard it is to tell whether it be water there or firm land hid with the leaves.
