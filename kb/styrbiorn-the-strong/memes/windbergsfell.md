# Windbergsfell

## Description

Windbergsfell is the mountain that rises on the eastern side of the valley south of [[upsala|Upsala]], forming a natural boundary to the landscape visible from [[olaf-biornson|King Olaf]]'s howe. From that vantage point, "to the east rose the scree-slopes of Windbergsfell" — a fell whose loose scree lay "on the fell-side like a trailing garment, covering all the slope down from the cliff-wall to the level flats below" (001-031, 015-023). The crest of the fell rose "in cliffs of rock, red and rotten" (015-023).

The mountain stood on the left-hand side of one looking south down the [[fyriswater|Fyriswater]] valley. It flanked the battlefield on [[fyrisfield|Fyrisfield]], its steep slopes and red cliffs forming one wall of the natural amphitheatre where Styrbiorn and [[king-eric|King Eric]] fought their three-day battle. The valley floor between Windbergsfell on the east and the river on the west narrowed as it rose northward toward [[upsala|Upsala]], creating a natural funnel through which the fighting pressed as the battle moved north.


> A mile or more eastward, on their left, rose the scree-slopes of Windbergsfell: southaway from beneath their feet wound Fyriswater amid the flats below Upsala, and there lay spread the far-stretching low country toward Sigtun and the sea, and westward the far mountains of Upland shadowy-gray in the light of morning.
>
> (001-031)

## Role in the Story

Windbergsfell plays its most significant role in the climactic Chapter 15, during the [[battle-of-fyrisfield|Battle of Fyrisfield]]. For the first two days of the battle, "there had been naught to see of the battle from near [[upsala|Upsala]], for their fighting was all in the wide and open lower part of the vale, that was shut off from view by the steep slopes of Windbergsfell" (015-023). The mountain thus served as a screen, hiding the carnage from those waiting in the city — until the third day, when the battle pressed north into the higher valley.

On that third day, the fighting moved into "the higher stretch of the valley betwixt the fell and the river," and the Queen, standing on Olaf's howe, could at last see the battle unfold (015-023). The narrowing valley between Windbergsfell and the [[fyriswater|Fyriswater]] became the corridor through which Styrbiorn's host drove the King's army back step by step toward [[upsala|Upsala]]. The Queen watched the black raven-wings of Styrbiorn's helm where "where those wings flew, there swung back the battle-tide before them, ebbing terribly backward towards [[upsala|Upsala]]" (015-024).

Windbergsfell also appears in the opening chapter as part of the panoramic view from the howe that establishes the [[all-places|geography]] of the saga. The mountain is one of the landmarks — alongside the [[fyriswater|Fyriswater]], the low country toward [[sigtun|Sigtun]], and the far mountains of Upland — that defines the landscape over which the novel's [[all-events|events]] will play out (001-031).

## Associated Characters

**[[queen-sigrid|Queen Sigrid]]** — Watched the battle move into the valley between Windbergsfell and the river, the mountain's cliffs framing her view (015-023).

**[[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn the Strong]]** — Drove the battle north along the valley floor under the shadow of Windbergsfell (015-023, 015-024).
