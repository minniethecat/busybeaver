## Author and Publication

*Styrbiorn the Strong* is a novel by the English author E. R. Eddison, first published in 1926. (018-003)(018-004) Eddison dedicated the book to his brother Colin. (P01-007) The author's prefatory Note is dated from Sidmouth, 2 January 1926. (P02-005)

The novel draws its narrative from the Old Norse *Styrbjarnar þáttr Svíakappa* (the Tale of Styrbiorn the Swedish Champion), preserved in the *Flateyjarbók* manuscript and referenced in the *Eyrbyggja Saga* and Snorri Sturluson's *Heimskringla*. (P02-001)(P02-002)

## Synopsis

The story opens with the young [[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn]], son of King Olaf Biornson, brooding on his father's burial mound at [[upsala|Upsala]]. (001-010) His uncle, [[king-eric|King Eric the Victorious]], rules as sole King of Sweden after the sudden death of Styrbiorn's father. (001-001)(001-002)

Denied his inheritance until he comes of age, the headstrong prince takes service with the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] vikings under Captain [[palnatoki]], winning renown and eventually lordship over the viking stronghold. (P02-001) Styrbiorn returns to Sweden with a fleet to claim his birthright, leading to the climactic [[battle-of-fyrisfield|Battle of Fyrisfield]] where he falls against King Eric's forces. (P02-001)(015-012)

The saga weaves together themes of kinship, honour, vengeance, and fate across the courts and battlefields of tenth-century Scandinavia.

## Main Characters

- **[[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn]]** — the exiled Swedish prince, son of King Olaf Biornson, called "Styrbiorn the Strong" for his prowess. (001-002)(008-036)
- **[[king-eric|King Eric the Victorious]]** — Styrbiorn's uncle, King of the Swedes at [[upsala|Upsala]], who raised Styrbiorn after his father's death. (001-001)(001-002)
- **[[queen-sigrid|Queen Sigrid the Haughty]]** — King Eric's wife, whose counsel shapes the conflict between uncle and nephew. (003-001)(015-015)
- **[[thorgnyr-lawman|Thorgnyr the Lawman]]** — the venerable lawspeaker of the Swedes, advisor to King Eric. (002-007)(002-009)
- **[[palnatoki]]** — captain of the Jomsburg vikings, under whom Styrbiorn served in exile. (P02-001)
- **[[biorn-broadwicker|Biorn Broadwicker]]** — a champion of Jomsburg who fought at Fyrisfield and fled into the woods after Styrbiorn's fall. (P02-001)

## The Saga World

The action unfolds across the Viking world of the late tenth century, from the royal seat at [[upsala|Upsala]] in Sweden to the viking fortress of [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] on the Baltic coast, and to the courts of [[yule-feast-denmark|Denmark]].

The story draws on the historical conflict between paganism and the rising tide of Christianity in the North, the blood-feuds of noble houses, and the Norse worldview in which slain heroes ride up into Valhalla among the Einheriar. (016-003)
