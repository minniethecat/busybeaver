## Biography

Thrand was an Earl of Sula, a region in Norway. His daughter [[ingibiorg|Ingibiorg]] married [[olaf-biornson|King Olaf Biornson]] of Sweden, becoming Queen of the Swedes and the mother of [[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn the Strong]] (001-002). Through this marriage, Thrand became the maternal grandfather of the saga's protagonist.

Thrand is mentioned only in passing in the genealogical passage that opens the novel, but his lineage is significant: through [[ingibiorg|Ingibiorg]], Styrbiorn carried both Swedish royal blood (from his father Olaf) and Norwegian noble blood (from his mother's father Thrand).


> King Olaf Biornson had to wife Ingibiorg, daughter of Earl Thrand of Sula. Their children were Thora and Thurid and Asdis and Aud.
>
> (001-002)

## Role in the Story

Thrand is a purely genealogical figure who appears only to establish [[ingibiorg|Ingibiorg]]'s noble parentage. His existence explains Styrbiorn's connection to Norwegian nobility, which may have facilitated Styrbiorn's later connections in the Norse world, including his recruitment of men like [[eystein-the-fox|Eystein the Fox]] of Halogaland.

## Relationships

- **[[ingibiorg|Ingibiorg]]**: His daughter, who became Queen of Sweden as [[olaf-biornson|Olaf Biornson]]'s wife
- **[[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn the Strong]]**: His grandson, the saga's protagonist
- **[[earl-wolf|Earl Wolf]]**: Styrbiorn's foster-father, described as [[ingibiorg|Ingibiorg]]'s brother (Styrbiorn's maternal uncle) — making him likely Thrand's son
