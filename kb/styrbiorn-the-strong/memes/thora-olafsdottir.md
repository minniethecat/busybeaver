## Biography

Thora was the eldest daughter of [[olaf-biornson|King Olaf Biornson]] and Queen [[ingibiorg|Ingibiorg]] of Sweden. She had three younger sisters: Thurid, Asdis, and Aud (001-002). The four daughters were born before their parents finally had a son — [[styrbiorn|Biorn]], later renamed Styrbiorn — whose birth was long desired by [[olaf-biornson|King Olaf]], who "ungainly it seemed...that he should get none but girl-children" (001-002).

The four sisters lost their mother when [[ingibiorg|Ingibiorg]] died in the third year after Styrbiorn's birth, and their father when Olaf died suddenly five years later — "fallen down dead of a sudden, where he sat at [[hall-feast|ale-drinking]] in his hall," with men whispering of poison (001-002). After these losses, the sisters' younger brother Styrbiorn was taken to be fostered by [[earl-wolf|Earl Wolf]] and raised by their uncle [[king-eric|King Eric]], while the sisters fade from the narrative.


> King Olaf Biornson had to wife Ingibiorg, daughter of Earl Thrand of Sula. Their children were Thora and Thurid and Asdis and Aud. Ungainly it seemed to King Olaf that he should get none but girl-children.
>
> (001-002)

## Role in the Story

Thora and her sisters appear only in a single sentence establishing the family of [[olaf-biornson|King Olaf]]. Their existence serves primarily to emphasize how desperately Olaf wanted a male heir, and how precious Styrbiorn was as the long-awaited son. The four fatherless, motherless sisters represent the collateral human cost of the political violence that shapes the saga's world.

## Relationships

- **[[olaf-biornson|King Olaf]] Biornson**: Her father

- **[[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn the Strong]]**: Her younger brother, the only son and heir
