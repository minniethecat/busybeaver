# Roiskeld

**Roiskeld** (modern Roskilde) was the royal residence of King [[harald-bluetooth|Harald Gormson]] in [[sealand|Sealand]], Denmark. It was here that [[styrbiorn-the-strong|Styrbiorn the Strong]] and his [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] companions spent the winter during their visit to the Danish court, and it was at Roiskeld that the seeds of the later conflict between Styrbiorn and [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald]] were sown.

## Location

Roiskeld was situated in Sealand, at the head of a firth that opened into a great inland sea. [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald]]'s hall stood on a small eminence near the seaside, overlooking the surrounding land and sea (005-003).

> So they sailed to Sealand and up the firth into the land till they were come to the head of the firth where it opens as it were into a great mere or inland sea. As soon as King Harald heard that the Jomsburgers were come thither, he sent messengers to bring them greetings and bid them come to him in his hall which stood up not far from the seaside on a little eminence overlooking the gray land and the gray sea. (005-003)

## Arrival of the Jomsburgers

Styrbiorn and [[biorn-asbrandson|Biorn Asbrandson]] arrived at Roiskeld after sailing west from [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] along the Danish coast and up into the [[danish-kingdom|Dane-realm]]. Upon learning that [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald]] was residing at his great house in Roiskeld that winter, they sailed up the firth to the head of the inlet (005-007). [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald]] received them warmly, bidding them stay with him over the Yuletide season (005-003).

> So those two with their following, when they had bid farewell to Palnatoki and the rest, sailed west along the land and so up into the Dane-realm, and there asked tidings and found that King Harald sat that winter in Sealand in his great house at Roiskeld. (005-007)

## Styrbiorn's Winter at Roiskeld

Styrbiorn and his followers stayed with [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald]] at Roiskeld from winter until Yule. During this time, Styrbiorn demonstrated his superiority in feats of strength and skill, and no man among the King's retinue dared challenge or cross him. [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald]] initially offered Styrbiorn goods, land, and the title of Earl if he would leave [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] and become his man, but Styrbiorn refused (005-009).

Among the younger men at the court, however, Styrbiorn was well-liked. He was blithe and gamesome with the lads and young men who spent that winter at Roiskeld, and they took joy in following him. Whatever Styrbiorn praised, they praised; whatever he disliked, they cried down (005-011).

> But with the lads and young men that were with King Harald that winter in Roiskeld Styrbiorn was so blithe and gamesome that they loved him well, and it was easy to see that they took joy to follow him and back him. (005-011)

The relationship between Styrbiorn and [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald]] soured over the winter. Styrbiorn jested that he might replace Harald as King of the [[danes|Danes]], which Harald outwardly laughed off but inwardly resented (005-009).

> Styrbiorn and his folk were there with King Harald Gormson in Roiskeld all winter till Yule... King Harald, seeing and considering these things, now began to change his mind into another tune, and became exceeding glum when Styrbiorn was by, and short-spoken. (005-009)

## Diplomatic Significance

Roiskeld later figured in diplomatic memory. When [[earl-wolf|Earl Wolf]] was sent to negotiate Styrbiorn's marriage to [[thyri|Thyri]], [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald]]'s resentment of Styrbiorn's behaviour at Roiskeld weighed on his decision. The memory of Styrbiorn lording it over him — and the jest about seizing the [[danish-kingdom|Dane-realm]] — sat in Harald's mind like an undigested meal, and he consented to the match only with great reluctance (008-031).

> There weighed ever on his mind the memory of Styrbiorn's lording it over him last winter in Roiskeld, and of his saying (albeit in jest) that he would do of him and the Dane-realm as he should think fit. (008-031)

## Thyri's Dream

Two years before Styrbiorn's invasion of Sweden, [[thyri|Thyri]] had a prophetic dream at Roiskeld. When Styrbiorn's fleet prepared to sail for Sweden, Biorn recalled this dream, and recognized that Thyri was concealing her terror behind a brave face at the prospect of her lord faring north to war (013-031).

> But Biorn bethought him now of her dream in Roiskeld two years since; and he thought he knew she was finding it hard to put on so blithe a face and press down her fears, seeing her lord now in very earnest about faring North with a great host of war. (013-031)
