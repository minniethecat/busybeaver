## Biography

Thyri was the daughter of [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald Gormson (Harald Bluetooth)]] of Denmark, and the sister of Svein Forkbeard. She first appears in the saga at fourteen winters old, when [[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn]] and his Jomsburg companions spent Yule at her father's court. She was described as "of few words but with a merry eye. Her hair was black and of a dainty curling growth, her mouth sweetly shaped but somewhat large. Folk found it hard to say if she were exceeding fair to look on or not fair at all, for she seemed both the one and the other by turns. She was not tall of stature, yet of a sweet and noble carriage" (005-012).

From the first, Thyri and [[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn]] were drawn to one another. Though both were "wont to be short of speech with other folk," they were "between themselves full of all talk and merriment; and their talk was easy, as if each knew easily the other's mind, as a man knows the countryside he was bred up in and finds all things there of kin with him" (005-013).

But as the Yule season progressed, Thyri's mood became shadowed. She sensed that her father had other plans for her — a marriage alliance with [[burisleif|King Burisleif]] of the Wends — and that King Harald viewed Styrbiorn's attentions toward her with suspicion (006-027). In desperation, she took the extraordinary step of seeking out [[biorn-broadwicker|Biorn Broadwicker]], Styrbiorn's closest companion, to beg him to take Styrbiorn away from Denmark. "Take him away hence," she pleaded. "Keep him out of Sweden, for all sakes." When Biorn told her Styrbiorn planned to sail North to Sweden, she "blanched to the lips" and cried, "Not North. Not North, Biorn" (006-035). Her foreboding would prove prophetic.

Her plan succeeded — Styrbiorn sailed north to Sweden, and the two were parted. But they would not remain apart for long. In Chapter 8, [[king-eric|King Eric]] proposed the match to Styrbiorn: "There is King Harald Gormson hath a daughter, Thyri. That were a match many kings would deem them lucky might they get her. The maid is fair, too, as men tell me, and good-mannered, and of fourteen winters old" (008-023). [[queen-sigrid|Queen Sigrid]], with characteristic venom, remarked that she and Thyri "will be well matched. She hath ugly hair, I am told, but is very meek, and will do all thy bidding and say naught" (008-050).

> Oft would Styrbiorn and Thyri be a-talking together. Biorn marked how those two, that were wont to be short of speech with other folk, were between themselves full of all talk and merriment; and their talk was easy, as if each knew easily the other's mind, as a man knows the countryside he was bred up in and finds all things there of kin with him.
>
> (005-013)

## Betrothal and Wedding

Thyri came to [[upsala|Upsala]] "with a great and honourable company" for the feast and Thing summoned by King Eric. The folk "deemed well of her and praised her beauty. That she loved Styrbiorn was clear as day, and men thought the two of them must have laid their plans for this in Denmark when Styrbiorn was with the King her father last winter" (009-001).

The troth-plighting was performed with due ceremony on the third night of the feast, and Thyri sat beside Styrbiorn at the lower bench, facing King Eric and Queen Sigrid on the upper bench (009-002, 009-003). But the banquet was the same night that [[queen-sigrid|Sigrid]]'s spell-weaving with the [[spae-wifes-lay|Spae-Wife's Lay]] ensnared Styrbiorn's gaze, and amid the tension of that hall, Thyri whispered "some trifle of lovers' talk" in Styrbiorn's ear — only to find him transfixed, staring at the Queen, unable to trust himself with speech (009-093).

The betrothal was broken off in the aftermath of the banquet — Styrbiorn's violent rampage upon discovering he had been cheated of his kingdom led him to flee [[upsala|Upsala]] and return to [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]], leaving Thyri behind.

Years later, when Styrbiorn returned to Denmark with his Jomsburg fleet and cowed King Harald into submission (Chapter 12, "[[cowing-of-the-dane-king|The Cowing of the Dane-King]]"), he met Thyri again at [[alaburg|Alaburg]]. She was bareheaded in the sun, carrying a little rabbit she had saved from a foumart, "the sun shone in blue gleams amid the jet-black curls and folds of her hair" (012-033). Their reunion was awkward and tender — they almost turned away from each other, faces flushed. But by evening she was "easy and open in her talk with him," and "it was as if her mind had been wiped clean now of all knowledge and remembrance of it" — the bad night that had parted them in Upsala (012-035). Styrbiorn demanded her hand as part of his peace terms: "he besides should have to wife Thyri, Harald's daughter" (012-045). They were wed without delay (013-001).

## Queen of the Jomsvikings

After the wedding, Thyri sailed east with Styrbiorn to [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]], despite the [[jomsburg-law|Jomsburg Law]] that forbade bringing any woman into the fortress. [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]] protested: "What is this? She must go back. Thou knowest our law" (013-014). But Styrbiorn overrode him, and "in such wise was Queen Thyri taken into Jomsburg" (013-014). She remained there for twenty-two days while the fleet was fitted out.

When the fleet sailed north for Sweden, Thyri came down to the ships. She was "brave and jolly of mien," but [[biorn-broadwicker|Biorn]] remembered her dream in [[roiskeld|Roiskeld]] from two years before, and "he thought he knew she was finding it hard to put on so blithe a face and press down her fears, seeing her lord now in very earnest about faring North with a great host of war" (013-031).

## Character

Thyri is one of the saga's most perceptive and emotionally intelligent characters. At fourteen, she already reads the political currents around her — she knows her father's suspicions before anyone tells her, and she takes bold, secret initiative to protect Styrbiorn. Unlike the Saga's many warriors and kings who act on pride and impulse, Thyri thinks ahead and acts through trusted intermediaries ([[biorn-broadwicker|Biorn]]). Yet she is not coldly calculating; her love for Styrbiorn is genuine and transparent, "clear as day" to everyone (009-001). Her silence is not emptiness but depth — she speaks little because she weighs her words. Her courage is quiet but real: she faces her father's potential wrath, Sigrid's jealousy, separation from her husband, the violation of Jomsburg law, and the prospect of Styrbiorn's death in battle — and meets each with composure, even if beneath she trembles.

## Relationships

- **[[harald-bluetooth|King Harald Bluetooth]]**: Her father, who loved her but planned to marry her to [[burisleif|King Burisleif]] for political alliance
- **[[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn the Strong]]**: Her betrothed and later husband — the great love of her life
- **[[biorn-broadwicker|Biorn Broadwicker]]**: Styrbiorn's closest companion, whom Thyri trusted with her secret plea to take Styrbiorn from Denmark
- **[[queen-sigrid|Queen Sigrid]]**: Her rival for Styrbiorn's attention — Sigrid viewed her with contemptuous jealousy
- **[[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]**: The Jomsburg chieftain who objected to her presence in the fortress
