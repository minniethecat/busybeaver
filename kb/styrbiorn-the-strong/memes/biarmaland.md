## Description

Biarmaland (Bjarmaland) was a region on the shores of the White Sea in the far north, inhabited by the Bjarmian [[all-persons|people]]. In the Viking Age it was known as a destination for raiding expeditions, rich in furs, walrus ivory, and other northern treasures. The saga presents it as one of the outer reaches of the Norse world — a proving ground for young warriors seeking fame and plunder.

## Role in the Story

### Harrying in Biarmaland

Styrbiorn's early career as a sea-king included extensive raiding in the northern waters. "We harried summer-long in Biarmaland and Balagarth-Side" (004-022), recounting the twelve sea-fights that established Styrbiorn's reputation. The Bjarmian expeditions were formative experiences that transformed Styrbiorn from a discontented Swedish prince into a hardened warrior with a loyal following.

### Bui's Northern Ventures

[[bui-digri|Bui Digri]], the stout [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] lord from [[borgundholm|Borgundholm]], "made war in the coasts of Biarmaland" (004-038) and was "well skilled in" the Bjarmian waters. Together with his companions, Bui "fared north into Biarmaland and the unknown [[all-places|places]] of [[kirialaland|Kirialaland]]" (008-010), drawn by the mystery and wealth of these remote regions. The Biarmaland voyages cemented Bui's reputation as an adventurer willing to go beyond the familiar Viking routes.

### Geographic Extent

In enumerating the scope of [[king-eric|King Eric]]'s dominion, the saga lists "[[garthrealm|Garth realm]] and Biarmaland and [[wendland|Wendland]] and [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]]" (007-041), grouping Biarmaland with other distant but significant territories. This rhetorical device elevated Eric's kingship by associating it with the farthest reaches of the known North.
