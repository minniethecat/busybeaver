## Biography

Gunnstein Lowry was one of the notable warriors in [[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn Olafson]]'s following. Unlike many of the [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]] who were long-established sea-rovers, Gunnstein was among Styrbiorn's "first following out of Sweden before he came to [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]]" (014-055), marking him as one of the original Swedish companions who accompanied the young prince into exile.

Gunnstein is listed alongside [[bessi-thorlakson|Bessi Thorlakson]], Biorn Asbrandson, and Odd o'Marklands as being among "the chiefest among Styrbiorn's following" at the time of the [[ball-game-kysingtarn|ball-game at Kysingtarn]] (007-070). When Styrbiorn sailed for Sweden to reclaim his throne, Gunnstein was among those who fared with him in the fleet of ten ships, alongside [[bessi-thorlakson|Bessi Thorlakson]] and others of lesser note (014-085).

## Role in the Story

Gunnstein Lowry represents the loyal Swedish following that accompanied Styrbiorn from his homeland into exile and back again. His presence underscores that Styrbiorn's cause was not solely a [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] enterprise — he retained a core of Swedish supporters from his earliest days. Though Gunnstein has no individual exploits or dialogue recorded in the novel, his inclusion in the lists of notable companions establishes the breadth and loyalty of Styrbiorn's retinue.

## Historical Basis

Gunnstein Lowry appears to be a fictional character. The name "Gunnstein" (Gunnsteinn) is an Old Norse compound meaning "battle-stone," appropriate for a warrior character. The surname "Lowry" is atypical for Norse naming and may be an Eddison invention or a modernization. No historical Gunnstein matching this description appears in the known Styrbiorn sources.
