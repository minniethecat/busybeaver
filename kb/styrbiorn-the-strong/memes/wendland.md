## Description

Wendland was the realm of the Wendish [[all-persons|people]], situated on the southern Baltic coast. It was ruled by [[burisleif|King Burisleif]], a significant figure in northern politics whose influence extended across the Baltic. The land was known to the Norse as a source of levies and a kingdom with its own martial traditions, its fame spreading "throughout Wendland and Garthrealm and [[estland|Estland]]" (012-025).


> The Queen made Styrbiorn drink and sit down near her beside the fire. There, he looking much in the fire and she on him, they sat a-talking: the Queen saying most, calling to mind old times, asking him of his doings abroad these two years past, of Garth realm and Biarmaland and Wendland and Jomsburg.
>
> (007-041)

## Role in the Story

### Dynastic Connection

Wendland's importance to the saga derives primarily from the marriage of [[burisleif|King Burisleif]]'s daughter [[queen-thyri|Thyri]] to [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald]] Bluetooth of Denmark. This alliance linked the Wendish and Danish royal houses, and created the kinship tie that would later draw Styrbiorn (himself connected to Thyri) into the orbit of both courts.

### Military Significance

During Styrbiorn's campaign for the Swedish throne, Wendland was among the lands from which forces could be drawn. Thyri's kinsmen were "mustering the Wendland levies and others from those parts" (013-025) to support the expedition against [[king-eric|King Eric]]. Earlier, Styrbiorn had "bare war into Wendland, holding it time that the Wends should pay him [[scat-tribute|tribute]]" (012-026), demonstrating the complex relationship between the Norse adventurers and the Wendish kingdom.

### Geographic Landmark

Wendland appears in the saga's geographic imagination alongside other distant realms. [[king-eric|King Eric]]'s dominion was said to encompass "[[garthrealm|Garth realm]] and [[biarmaland|Biarmaland]] and Wendland and [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]]" (007-041), and the moon rose "high over Wendland to the east" (011-011), marking it as a reference point on the eastern horizon.
