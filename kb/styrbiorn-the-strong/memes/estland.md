# Estland

**Estland** (modern Estonia) was an eastern Baltic land that featured both as a source of warriors in [[styrbiorn-the-strong|Styrbiorn the Strong]]'s following and as a target of [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] raiding expeditions. It lay among the eastern territories that Styrbiorn and the [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]] dominated during their years of sea-roving.

## Warriors from Estland

Among Styrbiorn's picked bodyguard of over three-score men was Ere-Skeggi, the young brother of an earl in Estland. Like many of Styrbiorn's followers, he was a conquered foe who became bound to Styrbiorn's service after feeling the might of his conqueror in battle (008-011).

> Ere-Skeggi, that was young brother to an earl in Estland; Olaf the Wend, and many more lords and princes of the east — all these were in Styrbiorn's body-guard and fought before him in battle. (008-011)

The presence of Estland nobles in Styrbiorn's bodyguard demonstrated the reach of his power across the Baltic. Men who had first opposed him in battle were drawn into his service, their loyalty secured by the personal bond between a war-leader and those who had felt his strength and been spared. This pattern of conquest followed by recruitment was central to Styrbiorn's ability to build a multi-national host for his eventual campaign against Sweden.

## Jomsburg Raids

The [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]] under Styrbiorn raided extensively in the eastern lands, and the terror of their name spread throughout [[wendland|Wendland]], [[garthrealm|Garthrealm]], and Estland (012-025).

> The report did fly of him throughout Wendland and Garthrealm and Estland and all those lands of the eastern seas. (012-025)

These raids served a dual purpose: they enriched the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] treasury with plunder and they projected Styrbiorn's power across the Baltic, ensuring that when the time came to gather a fleet for the Swedish campaign, warriors from the eastern lands would answer his call.

## Strategic Position

Estland occupied a strategic position on the eastern shore of the Baltic Sea, facing Sweden across the water. Its proximity to the Swedish coast and to the major sea routes made it a natural target for Viking raiders and a valuable source of fighting men. The relationship between Estland and the Norse powers was one of raiding, trading, and occasional alliance — the typical pattern of Viking-Age Baltic politics.
