## Description

Alaburg was [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald Gormson]]'s ([[harald-bluetooth|Harald Bluetooth]]'s) great house and royal seat, situated on the Limfirth in Denmark. The stronghold comprised substantial buildings befitting [[harald-bluetooth|the Dane-King]]'s status, "a fair great house" (012-032) with halls capable of hosting royal assemblies. It overlooked the strand of the sea (012-045) and commanded the waters of the Limfirth, making it both a defensible position and a center of royal authority.

> That afternoon it so fell out that Styrbiorn, walking in the sun about the home-mead and whiling away the time with looking on the good byres and sheep-pens and on the fire-halls and bowers and other fair buildings that the King had in Alaburg, came on a sudden round a corner of the great hall face to face with Thyri, the King's daughter. She was bareheaded in the sun and was carrying in her arms a little rabbit she had saved from a foumart.
>
> (012-033)
## Role in the Story
At Alaburg, Styrbiorn commanded the Dane-King to call a Thing:

> Styrbiorn came aland and went to see Harald the King straightway. The King said, "Wilt thou bridge the Limfirth with thy ships, Styrbiorn?" Styrbiorn said he would have the King call a Thing now forthright in Alaburg. So the King let call a Thing, and when that was done then Styrbiorn let them know what was in his mind to do. "And now," he said, "I shall look for more than good words from you, father-in-law. There be here three hundred ships of us Jomsburgers ready to fare north with me. And I shall look to you for such furtherance as you may, both in men and ships."
>
> (013-035)


### Styrbiorn's Arrival and Shelter

After Styrbiorn's expedition to the East-way, he came to Alaburg seeking shelter from [[harald-bluetooth|King Harald]], who was his kinsman through marriage to Thyri (013-004). Harald received him "exceeding well" (012-032) and gave Styrbiorn and his men lodging in the houses at Alaburg.

### The Confrontation

The peace at Alaburg proved short-lived. When Harald attempted to expel Styrbiorn's company shamefully, the confrontation escalated within the stronghold itself. The [[thing-stead|Thing]] assembled at Alaburg (013-035) became the stage for Styrbiorn's defiance, where he openly challenged Harald's authority before the assembled [[danes|Danes]].

### Aftermath

Following the confrontation, Styrbiorn departed Alaburg with his ships. Harald "abode ill at ease" (013-018) in the stronghold, having been humiliated. The [[all-events|events]] at Alaburg set in motion the chain of conflicts that would ultimately lead Styrbiorn to seek the Swedish throne and to the battle of [[fyrisfield|Fyrisfield]].
