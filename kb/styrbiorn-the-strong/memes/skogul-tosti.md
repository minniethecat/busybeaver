# Skogul-Tosti

## Biography

Skogul-Tosti, commonly called Tosti, was a mighty nobleman of Sweden whose seat was at [[arland|Arland]], southward from [[upsala|Upsala]]. He was the husband of [[gudrid|Gudrid]] and the father of Sigrid, who would become known to history as [[queen-sigrid|Sigrid the Haughty]], Queen of Sweden (003-001). His hall at [[arland|Arland]] was a place of wealth and hospitality, where [[king-eric|King Eric]] himself came to guest for a nine-night [[hall-feast|feast]] with a great company (003-001 to 003-002).

> Skogul-Tosti was a portly man and a stately, and the most open handed of men in all things which belong to housekeeping, and a showy man in his dress. It liked him better that his worthiness should call him to that reputation, to be the greatest man in all the countryside he might overlook from his home-mead at Hawkby, rather than he should exercise larger dominion over lands and folk and yet be called but the King's man, and the tool of a greatness not his own. He was the greatest of warriors, and spent much time a-warring.
>
> (003-002)

Tosti emerges as a generous host and a shrewd political operator. On the ninth night of the King's [[hall-feast|feast]] at [[arland|Arland]], it was Tosti's daughter Sigrid whom the King singled out to bear him the drinking horn -- a gesture whose significance was not lost on anyone present (003-003 to 003-005). Tosti and his wife [[gudrid|Gudrid]] discussed the King's intentions that night in their bedchamber, with Tosti confident that the King would "bespeak her in marriage for Styrbiorn" (003-011). He was proud of his daughter's high spirit -- "a right daughter of thine, mistress. As skittish as an eel" -- but trusted her wit and ambition: "she'll ne'er say nay to Styrbiorn" (003-017). The following morning, [[king-eric|King Eric]] did indeed take Tosti aside to speak of his daughter, though the match proposed was not for Styrbiorn but for the King himself (003-025).

Tosti's relationship with his wife [[gudrid|Gudrid]] reveals his warmer, private nature. Their bedroom conversation shows a man who is shrewd about politics but fond of his wife, teasing her while he takes off his shoes and kissing her "on the neck and bare shoulder" (003-024). He acknowledged his daughter's stubbornness as an inheritance from her mother yet refused to constrain her will: "she shall have her will, too, whatsome'er she choose" (003-023).

When [[king-eric|King Eric]] summoned his war-gathering against Styrbiorn's invasion, Tosti proved his loyalty decisively. He was "earliest come of all the great men" to [[upsala|Upsala]], having ridden from [[arland|Arland]] "two days and a night without sleep or stay" to answer the King's bidding (014-007). He brought his fighting men to augment the Swedish host that faced Styrbiorn on [[fyrisfield|Fyrisfield]].

## Role in the Saga

Skogul-Tosti's primary role is to provide the connective tissue between Sigrid and the royal court. His hall at [[arland|Arland]] is the stage upon which the King's courtship of Sigrid unfolds, a crucial turning point that redirects the novel's central relationships. Tosti's importance also lies in his representation of the Swedish nobility -- the landed men whose loyalty the King must court and who will, when the crisis comes, put their lives and men at the King's disposal.

His race to [[upsala|Upsala]] with the news of the [[war-arrow]] signals the gravity of the threat and the unifying power of the King's call. Where other bonders wavered in their allegiance, Tosti's loyalty was swift and absolute.

## Key Relationships

**[[queen-sigrid|Queen Sigrid]]** -- Tosti's daughter, of whom he was both proud and somewhat perplexed. He recognised her stubborn will and high pride but refused to coerce her in marriage, leaving the choice in her hands (003-023).

**[[gudrid|Gudrid]]** -- Tosti's wife, a canny woman who was more cautious than her husband in reading the political winds. Their affectionate banter provides one of the few glimpses of a happy marriage in the saga (003-009 to 003-024).

**[[king-eric|King Eric]] the Victorious** -- Tosti hosted the King at [[arland|Arland]] and gave his blessing to Eric's suit for Sigrid's hand. When the war-arrow was sent out, Tosti answered first and fastest of all the great lords (003-001, 014-007).

**[[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn the Strong]]** -- Though Tosti initially expected Styrbiorn to be Sigrid's suitor -- an outcome his wife [[gudrid|Gudrid]] had carefully schemed for -- [[all-events|events]] moved beyond this plan when the King himself claimed Sigrid. Tosti's first association with Styrbiorn was as a prospective son-in-law (003-011).

## Historical Basis

Skogul-Tosti (Old Norse: Skogul-Tosti or Skoglar-Tosti) appears in Norse saga tradition as the father of [[queen-sigrid|Sigrid the Haughty]]. He is mentioned in *Heimskringla* and related texts as a powerful Swedish nobleman whose daughter's marriage to [[king-eric|King Eric]] the Victorious made her Queen of Sweden. The saga tradition records him as a man of influence in the realm, though the novel gives him more depth as a character -- a loving if bemused father, a shrewd householder, and a loyal subject whose response to his King's call was immediate and unwavering.
