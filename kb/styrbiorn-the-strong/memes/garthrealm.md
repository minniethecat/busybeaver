## Description

Garthrealm (Old Norse *Garðaríki*, "realm of towns") was the Norse designation for the lands of the Rus along the great eastern rivers. It encompassed fortified trading settlements and vast territories stretching from the Baltic to the Black Sea. In the saga's political [[all-places|geography]], Garthrealm was counted among the distant dominions acknowledging [[king-eric|King Eric]]'s supremacy, alongside [[biarmaland|Biarmaland]], [[wendland|Wendland]], and [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] (007-041).

> And the report did fly of him throughout Wendland and Garthrealm and Estland and all those lands of the eastern seas, that he was no man, but a troll which irons bite not; and under that fear went his enemies against him into battle.
>
> (012-025)

## Role in the Story

### Eric's Eastern Dominion

The saga enumerates Garthrealm as part of the Swedish king's sphere of influence: "Garth realm and [[biarmaland|Biarmaland]] and [[wendland|Wendland]] and [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]]" (007-041). This rhetorical claim established Eric as an overlord whose authority extended far beyond Sweden proper into the eastern routes that were the source of immense wealth.

### Styrbiorn's Eastern Connections

During his years as a sea-king, Styrbiorn gathered followers from across the Norse world. Among them was a warrior "brought out of Micklegarth or Garthrealm or the Western Isles" (009-022), suggesting Styrbiorn's company drew men from the far eastern trading centers. The eastern connection also provided strategic alternatives — [[sigvaldi|Sigvaldi Jarl]] was said to have "turned away east for Garthrealm with all his ships" (014-008) when political circumstances in Scandinavia became unfavorable.

### Fame and Reputation

Styrbiorn's renown spread "throughout [[wendland|Wendland]] and Garthrealm and [[estland|Estland]] and all those parts" (012-025), marking Garthrealm as a benchmark of the known eastern world. The saga used reference to Garthrealm to establish the geographic scope of its [[all-persons|characters]]' fame and influence.
