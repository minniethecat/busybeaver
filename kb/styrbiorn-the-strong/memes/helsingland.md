## Description

Helsingland was a province in the northern reaches of the Swedish realm, situated many days' journey from the central districts around [[upsala|Upsala]]. It was described as lying this side of the remote Finn-heaths — "many days' journey beyond Helsingland" (001-038) began the territory of the Finns and other [[all-persons|people]]s beyond the settled Swedish lands. Helsingland represented the frontier of the [[swedish-kingdom|Swedish kingdom]], a source of fighting men and a marker of the realm's northern extent.


> When it was spring, the King fared north into the coasted parts of Helsingland and into Jarnberaland a-guesting, and it was full summer when he came home, riding with them of his bodyguard down to the high arm of the firth over against Sigtun.
>
> (002-002)

## Role in the Story

### Styrbiorn's Northern Journey

In his youth, before his break with [[king-eric|King Eric]], Styrbiorn journeyed "north into the coasted parts of Helsingland and into [[jarnberaland|Jarnberaland]] a-guesting" (002-002). This northern progress exposed the young prince to the full breadth of the Swedish realm, from the central royal seat at [[upsala|Upsala]] to the farthest provinces.

### The Fyrisfield Muster

When [[king-eric|King Eric]] summoned forces to oppose Styrbiorn's invasion, the northern provinces answered the call. "Earl Aunund of Fiadrundarland and the Earls of Helsingland and [[tenthland|Tenthland]] and [[east-gautland|East Gautland]]" (014-007) gathered their levies and marched to [[upsala|Upsala]]. Helsingland's contribution to the royal army demonstrated the cohesion of the Swedish kingdom in the face of external threat.

### Reinforcements

As the battle approached, word came that fresh troops "out of Helsingland, was nigh at hand now with a great company" (015-020), reinforcing Eric's already formidable army. The arrival of the Helsingland levies underscored the numerical advantage that would prove decisive at [[fyrisfield|Fyrisfield]].
