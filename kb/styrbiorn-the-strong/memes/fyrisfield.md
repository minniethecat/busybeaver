# Fyrisfield

## Description

Fyrisfield is the stretch of water-meadow lying south of [[upsala|Upsala]], between the city and the [[fyriswater|Fyriswater]], where the climactic battle of the saga takes place. The field is first glimpsed early in the novel, when Sigrid [[skogul-tosti|Skogul-Tosti]]'s daughter gazes south over Fyrisfield from Olaf's howe and sees something there that others cannot: "She was gazing with an altered countenance south over Fyrisfield. Styrbiorn looked up and saw her so stare, wide-eyed, as if in fear: as if in those silent water-meadows she saw some strange matter, hidden until now" (003-058). This moment of second sight prefigures the field's [[norse-fate|destiny]] as the place where the novel's central conflict will be resolved in blood.

The field gains its name from the river [[fyriswater|Fyriswater]] that wound southward from beneath [[upsala|Upsala]] through the flats (001-031). In peacetime it was pasture and water-meadow; in war, it became a killing ground. The battle fought there, spanning the entirety of Chapter 15, would give the field its enduring fame under the name Fyrisvall — the field of the [[fyriswater|Fyris]].

The armies that converged on the water-meadows were of unprecedented scale for the Northlands:

> Styrbiorn brought his army by great journeys all that day and the next, north through the woods and uplands till they came at even of the next day down to the flats of Fyrisfield below Upsala, and there lay the King his uncle with a very great host. And so great were the armies of Styrbiorn and of the King that no man that was there had ever seen so great a war-gathering in all the Northlands.
>
> (015-010)


## Role in the Story

Fyrisfield is the novel's ultimate stage, the arena where all the forces set in motion from the opening chapters converge in a single day of cataclysmic violence. The [[battle-of-fyrisfield|battle of Fyrisfield]] (Chapter 15) pits Styrbiorn's Jomsviking host against the army of [[king-eric|King Eric]], with the fate of the [[swedish-kingdom|Swede-realm]] hanging in the balance.

At dawn before the battle, the Earl came to Styrbiorn with word from Thorgnyr offering peace, and Styrbiorn refused (015-007, 015-008). Biorn Broadwicker then spoke to the host in Styrbiorn's name, galling them into battle with an oration delivered from the shadow of their lord, "on the great stature of Styrbiorn towering above them, and on the great raven-wings shadowing like death" (015-013). Styrbiorn shouted the watchword: "Forth, forth, Styrbiorn's men!" (015-013).

The battle raged from morning until "it began to be dark, so that men might not tell friend from foe," and both sides were so battle-worn that "scarce might they bear weapon aloft" (015-019). A truce was blown and both armies fell back to their camps. But the fighting resumed, and the novel's account of that second day tells of the breaking of Styrbiorn's host and the death of the [[styrbiorn|Swedes' Champion]].

Above the field, on [[olaf-biornson|King Olaf]]'s howe, [[queen-sigrid|Queen Sigrid]] watched the entire battle unfold — the same howe where she had first watched Styrbiorn wrestle with [[moldi|Moldi]] four years before (015-023). She saw what her visions had long foretold. The womenfolk and old men brought meat and drink down to the warriors on Fyrisfield at night, and they "lay out nightlong under their shields" (015-021).

## Associated Characters

**[[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn the Strong]]** — Led the Jomsviking host onto Fyrisfield, refused peace, and gave the watchword that launched the battle (015-008, 015-013).

**[[king-eric|King Eric]] the Victorious** — Commanded the Swedish army on Fyrisfield, defending his kingdom against his nephew's invasion.

**[[queen-sigrid|Queen Sigrid]]** — Watched the battle from Olaf's howe overlooking Fyrisfield, as her visions had foretold (015-023).

**[[earl-wolf|Earl Wolf]]** — Styrbiorn's foster-father, who brought the offer of peace and stood with him at the last (015-005, 015-007).

## Historical Context

The Battle of [[fyriswater|Fyris]]vellir (Fyrisvallarna) is a semi-legendary battle recorded in Norse sources including *Heimskringla* and the *Styrbjarnar thattr Sviakappa*, traditionally dated to the late 10th century (c. 984-986 CE). According to saga tradition, [[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn the Strong]] — nephew of [[king-eric|King Eric]] the Victorious — led a host of [[jomsvikings|Jomsvikings]] against his uncle's forces on the plains south of Old [[upsala|Uppsala]]. The battle ended in Styrbiorn's defeat and death, and Eric earned his by-name "the Victorious" from this triumph. The field's name, Fyrisvall, is preserved in Swedish historical memory and in the modern district name [[fyriswater|Fyris]]lund.
