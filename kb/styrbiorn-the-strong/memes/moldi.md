## Definition

Moldi was [[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn]]'s pet musk-ox, brought from the northlands many days' journey beyond [[helsingland|Helsingland]] (001-038). A yearling when first introduced, Moldi was "littler than common oxen, but heavy enough and strong enough to do with most men as it would" (001-031). He was a dark and shaggy creature, four-legged, sturdy, high-shouldered, tailless, with a marvellous long and hairy coat that trailed well nigh to his big cloven hooves, great curly horns like a ram's, and a hairy face and nose (001-031).

Moldi was more than a mere animal companion. He served as Styrbiorn's wrestling partner — the only creature, save perhaps [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]] himself, who could match Styrbiorn's raw physical strength. Their bouts on [[olaf-biornson|King Olaf]]'s Howe were a daily ritual that demonstrated Styrbiorn's extraordinary might: though Moldi threw him three times out of every dozen encounters, Styrbiorn was victor in all other rounds (001-031).

> It was a yearling musk-ox, littler than common oxen, but heavy enough and strong enough to do with most men as it would; but with Styrbiorn, as was well seen as they tussled and strove together, it was very nicely matched. Round and round they swung for a while, trampling the ground, swaying and staggering back and forth, puffing and snorting both the one and the other, till Styrbiorn broke off the game and leaped backward several paces and there stood panting and laughing, facing his little ox.
>
> (001-031)
## Occurrences in the Novel

Moldi appears at several crucial moments in the narrative, often serving as a silent witness to pivotal human dramas:

**On [[olaf-biornson|King Olaf]]'s Howe (Chapter 1)** : Moldi is first introduced when Styrbiorn seeks him out on the morning after slaying [[aki|Aki]]. Their wrestling match on the howe is watched by [[sigrid|Sigrid Skogul-Tostisdottir]], who observes Styrbiorn's strength with a mixture of fascination and foreboding (001-031)–(001-036). Moldi's presence on the howe — Styrbiorn's place of communion with his dead father — links the animal to Styrbiorn's deepest identity.

**At [[upsala|Upsala]] after Styrbiorn's return (Chapter 8)** : Upon Styrbiorn's return from three years abroad, Moldi — now full-grown — recognizes him instantly and runs to greet him, "which was a wonder after these years" (008-035). Moldi had been left in the care of a thrall named Erland, and the thralls dared not touch the cross-grained beast save when he was in the mood. Yet with Styrbiorn, "he was as gentle as a little calf might be" (008-035).

It is on this occasion that [[queen-sigrid|Queen Sigrid]] watches Styrbiorn wrestle with Moldi, just as she had done years before on Olaf's Howe (008-036). Their conversation during this encounter — with Sigrid stroking Moldi's woolly jowl while delivering veiled words of warning and jealousy — is one of the most charged exchanges in the novel (008-050)–(008-051). Moldi becomes a silent medium through which Sigrid's complex feelings toward Styrbiorn are expressed.

**In Winter's Darkness (Chapter 12)** : During the long winter before the invasion of Sweden, as Styrbiorn grew blacker of mood, he would row out to sea alone with Moldi — the only living creature whose company he could bear (012-003). Moldi's death during this period is cryptically noted: "That was Moldi" (012-006), a laconic epitaph that underscores the bleakness of Styrbiorn's final months.

## Symbolic Significance

Moldi functions as a living symbol of Styrbiorn's nature: wild, untameable by ordinary men, responsive only to Styrbiorn himself. The musk-ox from the far north, beyond the settled lands, mirrors Styrbiorn's own apartness from the civilized order of the Swedish court. When [[queen-sigrid|Queen Sigrid]] says, "It is only to please thee he letteth thee get the upper hand of him. If he would, he could toss thee off like a ball" (008-036), she speaks of Moldi but means the same of all who deal with Styrbiorn.

Moldi is also the only creature with whom Styrbiorn shows unguarded tenderness — rubbing his cheek against Moldi's nose "like a cat" (001-031), whispering private thoughts into his furry ear (008-039). In a story of violent men and political maneuvering, the musk-ox provides the novel's most intimate window into Styrbiorn's unarmoured soul.
