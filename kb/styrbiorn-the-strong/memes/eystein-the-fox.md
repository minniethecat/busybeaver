## Biography

Eystein the Fox was a Norseman by kin out of Halogaland in Norway. He fled abroad from Norway because of [[earl-hakon|Earl Hakon]], having slain one of the Earl's men. His journey took him east across the Keel (the mountain range dividing Norway and Sweden), and thence east overseas to [[holmgarth|Holmgarth]] ([[holmgarth|Novgorod]]) (004-038).

In [[holmgarth|Holmgarth]], [[styrbiorn|Styrbiorn]] took Eystein into his service, and Eystein accompanied him when Styrbiorn made war in the coasts of [[biarmaland|Biarmaland]]. He was "a man well skilled in arms, and most of all with the axe, and was a great manslayer" (004-038) — a fearsome reputation that made him a valuable addition to Styrbiorn's war-band.

## Death at Jomsburg

Eystein met his end during the ship-fight between Styrbiorn and [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]] at [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]]. When Styrbiorn called upon Eystein to help push back the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] vikings who had boarded his ship, Eystein fought with such ferocity that the [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]] were forced to give ground (004-038).

As Eystein attempted to leap aboard [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]'s ship, [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]] thrust at him so hard with a spear on the boss of his shield that Eystein's foot missed the ship's side entirely — he landed in the gap between the two vessels and came pitching forward across [[palnatoki|Palnatoki]]'s gunwale. Before he could right himself, [[bessi-thorlakson|Bessi Thorlakson]] struck him with an axe-hammer so mighty a blow on the head that "all the brains were beaten out of it" (004-038).

The saga notes that there was "a blood-feud of old betwixt those two, Eystein and Bessi," and that Bessi, as he dealt the killing blow, cried out: "So knock we the harns out of Foxes" (004-038) — a grim pun on Eystein's by-name that suggests a long-standing enmity finally resolved in blood.

## Role in the Story

Eystein appears only in the [[jomsburg|Jomsburg]] ship-fight episode, but his dramatic death serves multiple narrative functions. It demonstrates the ferocity of the combat between Styrbiorn's men and the [[jomsvikings|Jomsburgers]], raises the stakes of the duel (Styrbiorn loses one of his best fighters), and introduces [[bessi-thorlakson|Bessi Thorlakson]] as a formidable warrior in his own right. The mention of the old blood-feud also hints at the tangled web of Norse honor-killings and vendettas that form the backdrop of the saga.

- **Styrbiorn**: His lord and captain, who took him into service in [[holmgarth|Holmgarth]]

- **[[bessi-thorlakson|Bessi Thorlakson]]**: His killer, with whom he had an old blood-feud
