## Overview

The village in *The Necromancers* is never named, yet it is the novel's grounding presence — the place of meadows, churchyards, and grocer's shops against which the séances of London and the dramas of the soul are set. It is the home of Laurie Baxter, the site of Amy Nugent's grave, and the landscape of Maggie Deronnais's quiet observation.

## Geography

The village lies within walking distance of woods and meadows. The "meadows beyond the village road" [001-054] stretch in September sweetness, and "the stillness of the woods below the village" [001-067] suggests a landscape of gentle declivities. Laurie's house sits about "a mile from his own house in the village, where stood the little tin church" [001-084] — the Catholic chapel that serves the local faithful.

## Key Locations

The village contains several significant sites. The "village churchyard" [001-025] is where Amy Nugent is buried, "seeing the last of" her at an open grave. The "village schoolroom" [005-009] serves as a venue for entertainments to be patronized. The grocer's shop belonging to Amos Nugent — "the grocer's shop" [005-032] — bears the name "Nugent" in bright capitals and is the novel's most charged commercial space, a reminder of Amy's absent presence.

## Social World

The village is a place of class visibility. The Nugents are "a Baptist grocer of the village" [001-023] — their religious and commercial identity plainly marked. Lady Laura refers to "the village girl" [003-048] when speaking of Amy, a phrase that encodes the social distance between the Catholic gentry and the Baptist tradespeople. The village is small enough that news travels: information "about the village girl, more or less correct" [003-027] circulates among the gentry.

## Symbolic Function

The village represents the ordinary, the diurnal, the Catholic order of work and prayer — everything the séances disrupt. It is where Laurie ought to remain, processing his grief within the rhythms of faith and community. His journeys to London for séances are departures from the village's implicit norm.
