# Automatic Writing

**Automatic writing** is a spiritualist practice in which a person produces written text without conscious volition, the writing hand being guided — according to spiritualist belief — by a discarnate spirit entity. It is one of several physical manifestations associated with mediumship, alongside table-rapping and materialization.

## Role in the Novel

In *The Necromancers*, automatic writing is mentioned as one of the phenomena that the spiritualist circle at the Baxter house explores. Maggie Deronnais, recounting the progression of manifestations she has witnessed, lists it among the escalating phenomena "from the pencil and the rappings and the automatic writing, right up to the appearances" [019-016]. This places automatic writing on a spectrum of spirit communication that culminates in full materialization.

The practice also appears in a more skeptical context when Laurie Baxter questions the medium about the apparatus of spiritualist séances. During a session described in Chapter 7, he asks tentatively about planchette — a related writing device — and receives an explanation from the medium [007-130]–[007-131]. His question "For… for automatic writing?" suggests both curiosity and a degree of incredulity about the practice.
