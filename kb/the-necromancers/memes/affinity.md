# Affinity

In the spiritualist framework depicted in *The Necromancers*, an **affinity** is a pre-existing soul-connection between a living person and a particular disembodied spirit. This concept serves as a metaphysical explanation for why certain spirits communicate through certain mediums, rather than appearing indiscriminately to anyone who calls upon them.

## In the Novel

The concept is introduced by Mr. Vincent, the medium who leads the séances at the Baxter house. During a discussion of spiritualist doctrine in Chapter 7, he explains that Laurie Baxter had "a very strong 'affinity,' as he would have said, with" a particular spirit [007-053]. This affinity is presented as an innate bond that predates the séance and cannot be manufactured through ritual alone.

Mr. Vincent elaborates that the strength of an affinity determines its resilience: even adverse circumstances "could not destroy an 'affinity' if the affinity were robust" [007-062]. This framing transforms spirit communication from a technical procedure into a matter of spiritual compatibility — the medium is not merely operating equipment but participating in a preordained relationship.

The concept of affinity serves a dual narrative purpose. It explains why Laurie, despite his initial skepticism, becomes a focal point for spirit manifestations, and it introduces an element of fatalism into the spiritualist worldview — some souls are simply destined to connect.
