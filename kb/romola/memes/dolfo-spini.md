# Dolfo Spini

Dolfo Spini is the leader of the Compagnacci (the "Evil Companions"),
a gang of dissolute young Florentine men who oppose Savonarola's
puritanical rule. He serves as a political instrument for Tito Melema,
who manipulates Spini and his gang to advance his own interests.

## Character

Spini is described as a "man of appetites" and "gross," a thickheaded but
dangerous figure who leads the Compagnacci with a red feather as his emblem
[045-009]. His brutish nature makes him both useful and unpredictable as
an ally — Tito views him with contempt but relies on him as a tool for
influencing Florentine politics [046-008].

## Role in the Novel

Tito Melema cultivates Spini as a political contact, meeting with him to
discuss the brewing opposition to Savonarola [046-008]. The relationship
is fraught: Tito fears Spini's suspicion could be "as disagreeable in its
consequences as enmity" [046-014], and Spini's volatility creates constant
risk [048-038].

During the Carnival of 1497, Spini and his companions struggle against
Savonarola's attempt to replace traditional festivities with a religious
"Bonfire of the Vanities" [049-003]. His Compagnacci represent the
secular resistance to the Dominican reform movement.
