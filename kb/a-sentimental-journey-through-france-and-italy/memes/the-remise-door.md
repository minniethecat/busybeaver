# The Remise Door

The **[[the-remise|Remise Door]]** at [[calais|Calais]] is one of the most symbolically charged locations in Laurence Sterne's *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]]* (1768). It is at this coach-house doorway that [[yorick|Yorick]] encounters [[the-starling|the caged starling]] — an episode that crystallizes the novel's meditation on liberty, confinement, and the power of a single repeated phrase: "I can't get out."

## 在小说中的角色 (Role in the Novel)

The Remise Door functions as a literal and metaphorical threshold in the narrative:

**[[the-starling|The Starling]]'s Threshold**: Standing at "the [[the-remise|Remise]] door" (Ch. 41, [[the-starling|The Starling]]), [[yorick|Yorick]] hears the starling's plaintive cry — "I can't get out, — I can't get out" — which becomes a refrain echoing through the remainder of the novel. The doorway, neither inside nor outside, becomes a space of suspended action where [[yorick|Yorick]]'s reflections on captivity (his own, the starling's, and by extension, humanity's) take shape.

**A Site of Repetition**: The Remise Door appears across multiple chapters at [[calais|Calais]], marking it as a recurring location in [[yorick|Yorick]]'s [[calais|Calais]] routine. He returns to it, pauses at it, and uses it as a vantage point from which to observe the comings and goings of the coach-yard — making it a fixed point in the otherwise fluid geography of travel.

## 象征意义 (Symbolic Significance)

In Sterne's architectural imagination, doors are never merely doors. The Remise Door is the boundary between the protected interior (the coach-house, the vehicle, the enclosed self) and the open road (travel, freedom, vulnerability). [[yorick|Yorick]]'s habitual pause at this threshold reflects the novel's deeper preoccupation with liminal states — between [[england|England]] and [[france|France]], between sentiment and action, between captivity and liberation.
