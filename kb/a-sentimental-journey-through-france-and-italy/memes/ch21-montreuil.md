## MONTREUIL.

[021-001]The next morning, La Fleur entering

[021-002]upon his employment, I delivered to him the key of my

[021-003]portmanteau, with an inventory of my half a dozen shirts and silk

[021-004]pair of breeches, and bid him fasten all upon the

[021-005]chaise,—get the horses put to,—and desire the

[021-006]landlord to come in with his bill. 

[021-007]C’est un garcon de bonne fortune , said the

[021-008]landlord, pointing through the window to half a dozen wenches who

[021-009]had got round about La Fleur, and were most kindly taking their

[021-010]leave of him, as the postilion was leading out the horses.

[021-011]La Fleur kissed all their hands round and round again, and thrice

[021-012]he wiped his eyes, and thrice he promised he would bring them all

[021-013]pardons from Rome. 

[021-014]—The young fellow, said the landlord, is beloved by all

[021-015]the town, and there is scarce a corner in Montreuil where the

[021-016]want of him will not be felt: he has but one misfortune in the

[021-017]world, continued he, “he is always in love.”—I

[021-018]am heartily glad of it, said I,—’twill save me the

[021-019]trouble every night of putting my breeches under my head.

[021-020]In saying this, I was making not so much La Fleur’s eloge

[021-021]as my own, having been in love with one princess or another

[021-022]almost all my life, and I hope I shall go on so till I die, being

[021-023]firmly persuaded, that if ever I do a mean action, it must be in

[021-024]some interval betwixt one passion and another: whilst this

[021-025]interregnum lasts, I always perceive my heart locked up,—I

[021-026]can scarce find in it to give Misery a sixpence; and therefore I

[021-027]always get out of it as fast as I can—and the moment I am

[021-028]rekindled, I am all generosity and good-will again; and would do

[021-029]anything in the world, either for or with any one, if they will

[021-030]but satisfy me there is no sin in it. 

[021-031]—But in saying this,—sure I am commanding the

[021-032]passion,—not myself.
