## VERSAILLES.

[050-001]And how do you find the French?

[050-002]said the Count de B—, after he had given me the

[050-003]passport. 

[050-004]The reader may suppose, that after so obliging a proof of

[050-005]courtesy, I could not be at a loss to say something handsome to

[050-006]the enquiry. 

[050-007]— Mais passe , pour cela .—Speak

[050-008]frankly, said he: do you find all the urbanity in the French

[050-009]which the world give us the honour of?—I had found every

[050-010]thing, I said, which confirmed it.— Vraiment , said

[050-011]the Count, les François sont polis .—To an

[050-012]excess, replied I. 

[050-013]The Count took notice of the word excès ; and

[050-014]would have it I meant more than I said. I defended myself a

[050-015]long time as well as I could against it.—He insisted I had

[050-016]a reserve, and that I would speak my opinion frankly. 

[050-017]I believe, Monsieur le Count, said I, that man has a certain

[050-018]compass, as well as an instrument; and that the social and other

[050-019]calls have occasion by turns for every key in him; so that if you

[050-020]begin a note too high or too low, there must be a want either in

[050-021]the upper or under part, to fill up the system of

[050-022]harmony.—The Count de B— did not understand music, so

[050-023]desired me to explain it some other way. A polish’d

[050-024]nation, my dear Count, said I, makes every one its debtor: and

[050-025]besides, Urbanity itself, like the fair sex, has so many charms,

[050-026]it goes against the heart to say it can do ill; and yet, I

[050-027]believe, there is but a certain line of perfection, that man,

[050-028]take him altogether, is empower’d to arrive at:—if

[050-029]he gets beyond, he rather exchanges qualities than gets

[050-030]them. I must not presume to say how far this has affected

[050-031]the French in the subject we are speaking of;—but, should

[050-032]it ever be the case of the English, in the progress of their

[050-033]refinements, to arrive at the same polish which distinguishes the

[050-034]French, if we did not lose the politesse du cœur ,

[050-035]which inclines men more to humane actions than courteous

[050-036]ones,—we should at least lose that distinct variety and

[050-037]originality of character, which distinguishes them, not only from

[050-038]each other, but from all the world besides. 

[050-039]I had a few of King William’s shillings, as smooth as

[050-040]glass, in my pocket; and foreseeing they would be of use in the

[050-041]illustration of my hypothesis, I had got them into my hand when I

[050-042]had proceeded so far:— 

[050-043]See, Monsieur le Count, said I, rising up, and laying them

[050-044]before him upon the table,—by jingling and rubbing one

[050-045]against another for seventy years together in one body’s

[050-046]pocket or another’s, they are become so much alike, you can

[050-047]scarce distinguish one shilling from another. 

[050-048]The English, like ancient medals, kept more apart, and passing

[050-049]but few people’s hands, preserve the first sharpnesses

[050-050]which the fine hand of Nature has given them;—they are not

[050-051]so pleasant to feel,—but in return the legend is so

[050-052]visible, that at the first look you see whose image and

[050-053]superscription they bear.—But the French, Monsieur le

[050-054]Count, added I (wishing to soften what I had said), have so many

[050-055]excellences, they can the better spare this;—they are a

[050-056]loyal, a gallant, a generous, an ingenious, and good

[050-057]temper’d people as is under heaven;—if they have a

[050-058]fault—they are too serious . 

[050-059]Mon Dieu ! cried the Count, rising out of his chair. 

[050-060]Mais vous plaisantez , said he, correcting his

[050-061]exclamation.—I laid my hand upon my breast, and with

[050-062]earnest gravity assured him it was my most settled opinion. 

[050-063]The Count said he was mortified he could not stay to hear my

[050-064]reasons, being engaged to go that moment to dine with the Duc de

[050-065]C—. 

[050-066]But if it is not too far to come to Versailles to eat your

[050-067]soup with me, I beg, before you leave France, I may have the

[050-068]pleasure of knowing you retract your opinion,—or, in what manner

[050-069]you support it.—But, if you do support it, Monsieur

[050-070]Anglois, said he, you must do it with all your powers, because

[050-071]you have the whole world against you.—I promised the Count

[050-072]I would do myself the honour of dining with him before I set out

[050-073]for Italy;—so took my leave.
