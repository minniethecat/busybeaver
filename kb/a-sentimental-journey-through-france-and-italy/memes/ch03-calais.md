## CALAIS.

[003-001]—’ Tis very true, said

[003-002]I, replying to a cast upwards with his eyes, with which he had

[003-003]concluded his address;—’tis very true,—and

[003-004]heaven be their resource who have no other but the charity of the

[003-005]world, the stock of which, I fear, is no way sufficient for the

[003-006]many great claims which are hourly made upon it. 

[003-007]As I pronounced the words great claims , he gave a

[003-008]slight glance with his eye downwards upon the sleeve of his

[003-009]tunic:—I felt the full force of the appeal—I

[003-010]acknowledge it, said I:—a coarse habit, and that but once

[003-011]in three years with meagre diet,—are no great matters; and the

[003-012]true point of pity is, as they can be earn’d in the world

[003-013]with so little industry, that your order should wish to procure

[003-014]them by pressing upon a fund which is the property of the lame,

[003-015]the blind, the aged and the infirm;—the captive who lies

[003-016]down counting over and over again the days of his afflictions,

[003-017]languishes also for his share of it; and had you been of the order of mercy , instead of the order of St. Francis, poor

[003-018]as I am, continued I, pointing at my portmanteau, full cheerfully

[003-019]should it have been open’d to you, for the ransom of the

[003-020]unfortunate.—The monk made me a bow.—But of all

[003-021]others, resumed I, the unfortunate of our own country, surely,

[003-022]have the first rights; and I have left thousands in distress upon

[003-023]our own shore.—The monk gave a cordial wave with his

[003-024]head,—as much as to say, No doubt there is misery enough in

[003-025]every corner of the world, as well as within our

[003-026]convent—But we distinguish, said I, laying my hand upon the

[003-027]sleeve of his tunic, in return for his appeal—we

[003-028]distinguish, my good father! betwixt those who wish only to eat

[003-029]the bread of their own labour—and those who eat the bread

[003-030]of other people’s, and have no other plan in life, but to

[003-031]get through it in sloth and ignorance, for the love of

[003-032]God . 

[003-033]The poor Franciscan made no reply: a hectic of a moment

[003-034]pass’d across his cheek, but could not tarry—Nature

[003-035]seemed to have done with her resentments in him;—he showed

[003-036]none:—but letting his staff fall within his arms, he

[003-037]pressed both his hands with resignation upon his breast, and

[003-038]retired.
