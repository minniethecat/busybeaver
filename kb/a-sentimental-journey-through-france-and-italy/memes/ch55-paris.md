## PARIS.

[055-001]When La Fleur came up to wait upon

[055-002]me at supper, he told me how sorry the master of the hotel was

[055-003]for his affront to me in bidding me change my lodgings. 

[055-004]A man who values a good night’s rest will not lie down

[055-005]with enmity in his heart, if he can help it.—So I bid La

[055-006]Fleur tell the master of the hotel, that I was sorry on my side

[055-007]for the occasion I had given him;—and you may tell him, if

[055-008]you will, La Fleur, added I, that if the young woman should call

[055-009]again, I shall not see her. 

[055-010]This was a sacrifice not to him, but myself, having resolved,

[055-011]after so narrow an escape, to run no more risks, but to leave

[055-012]Paris, if it was possible, with all the virtue I enter’d

[055-013]it. 

[055-014]C’est déroger à noblesse , Monsieur , said La Fleur, making me a bow down to the

[055-015]ground as he said it.— Et encore , Monsieur ,

[055-016]said he, may change his sentiments;—and if ( par

[055-017]hazard ) he should like to amuse himself,—I find no

[055-018]amusement in it, said I, interrupting him.— 

[055-019]Mon Dieu ! said La Fleur,—and took away. 

[055-020]In an hour’s time he came to put me to bed, and was more

[055-021]than commonly officious:—something hung upon his lips to

[055-022]say to me, or ask me, which he could not get off: I could not

[055-023]conceive what it was, and indeed gave myself little trouble to

[055-024]find it out, as I had another riddle so much more interesting upon my

[055-025]mind, which was that of the man’s asking charity before the

[055-026]door of the hotel.—I would have given anything to have got

[055-027]to the bottom of it; and that, not out of

[055-028]curiosity,—’tis so low a principle of enquiry, in

[055-029]general, I would not purchase the gratification of it with a

[055-030]two-sous piece;—but a secret, I thought, which so soon and

[055-031]so certainly soften’d the heart of every woman you came

[055-032]near, was a secret at least equal to the philosopher’s

[055-033]stone; had I both the Indies, I would have given up one to have

[055-034]been master of it. 

[055-035]I toss’d and turn’d it almost all night long in my

[055-036]brains to no manner of purpose; and when I awoke in the morning,

[055-037]I found my spirits as much troubled with my dreams, as ever the

[055-038]King of Babylon had been with his; and I will not hesitate to

[055-039]affirm, it would have puzzled all the wise men of Paris as much

[055-040]as those of Chaldea to have given its interpretation.
