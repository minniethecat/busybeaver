## CALAIS.

[014-001]As the little French captain left us, Mons. Dessein came up

[014-002]with the key of the Remise in his hand, and forthwith let us into

[014-003]his magazine of chaises. 

[014-004]The first object which caught my eye, as Mons. Dessein

[014-005]open’d the door of the Remise, was another old

[014-006]tatter’d désobligeant ; and notwithstanding it

[014-007]was the exact picture of that which had hit my fancy so much in

[014-008]the coach-yard but an hour before,—the very sight of it

[014-009]stirr’d up a disagreeable sensation within me now; and I

[014-010]thought ’twas a churlish beast into whose heart the idea

[014-011]could first enter, to construct such a machine; nor had I much more

[014-012]charity for the man who could think of using it. 

[014-013]I observed the lady was as little taken with it as myself: so

[014-014]Mons. Dessein led us on to a couple of chaises which stood

[014-015]abreast, telling us, as he recommended them, that they had been

[014-016]purchased by my lord A. and B. to go the grand tour, but had gone

[014-017]no further than Paris, so were in all respects as good as

[014-018]new.—They were too good;—so I pass’d on to a

[014-019]third, which stood behind, and forthwith begun to chaffer for the

[014-020]price.—But ’twill scarce hold two, said I, opening

[014-021]the door and getting in.—Have the goodness, Madame, said

[014-022]Mons. Dessein, offering his arm, to step in.—The lady

[014-023]hesitated half a second, and stepped in; and the waiter that

[014-024]moment beckoning to speak to Mon. Dessein, he shut the door of

[014-025]the chaise upon us, and left us.
