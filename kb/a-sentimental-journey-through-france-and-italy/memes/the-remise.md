# The Remise

**The Remise** is the coach-house (French *remise* — carriage house) at [[the-landlord|Monsieur Dessein]]'s inn in [[calais|Calais]], where [[yorick|Yorick]]'s sentimental journey first catches fire. Introduced in the early chapters of Laurence Sterne's *A [[sentiment|Sentimental]] [[travel|Journey]] Through [[france|France]] and [[italy|Italy]]* (1768), the Remise and its door serve as the stage for one of the most celebrated sequences in the novel — the prolonged, charged encounter between [[yorick|Yorick]] and a mysterious lady from [[brussels|Brussels]] (Ch. 6–10, [[calais|Calais]]).

## 地理与文化背景

The Remise is part of [[the-landlord|Monsieur Dessein]]'s establishment in [[calais|Calais]], the French port town where English travelers traditionally disembarked. In the 18th century, a *remise* was a covered shed or garage where post-chaises and traveling carriages were kept under cover. [[the-landlord|Monsieur Dessein]], "the most considerable man of his trade in the place" (Ch. 3, [[calais|Calais]]), maintains a "magazine of chaises" there — a collection of vehicles for hire that travelers must inspect before continuing their journey.

The Remise is both a practical commercial space and, in Sterne's hands, an improbably rich theatrical stage. Its door — "THE REMISE DOOR" — becomes a recurring scene heading, as though the building itself is a character in the sentimental drama.

## 在小说中的角色

The Remise serves three overlapping functions in the novel:

**A Stage for [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al Encounter**: The Remise door is where [[yorick|Yorick]] and the lady are repeatedly left alone together — first when [[the-landlord|Monsieur Dessein]] fumbles with the wrong key (Ch. 7, [[calais|Calais]]), and again when a waiter calls him away after locking them both inside a chaise (Ch. 10, [[calais|Calais]]). These accidents of circumstance — "a parcel of nonsensical contingencies" as the lady calls them — create the suspended space in which sentimental feeling can arise unbidden.

**A Threshold of Decision**: At the Remise door, [[yorick|Yorick]] debates whether to offer the lady half his chaise to [[amiens|Amiens]] — an internal drama in which Avarice, Caution, Cowardice, Discretion, Hypocrisy, Meanness, and [[pride|Pride]] all plead against the impulse of generosity (Ch. 9, [[calais|Calais]]). The debate is decided when the lady glides away unperceived, and [[yorick|Yorick]] does not follow. The Remise door thus marks a boundary between impulse and action, sentiment and consequence.

**A Return Point**: Much later in the narrative, [[yorick|Yorick]] gets "into my remise the hour I proposed" to travel to [[versailles|Versailles]] (Ch. 42, Road to [[versailles|Versailles]]), linking the [[calais|Calais]] scenes to the later [[paris|Paris]]-[[versailles|Versailles]] plot. The remise vehicle itself bookends the journey.
