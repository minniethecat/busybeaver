# Naples

**Naples** appears in Laurence Sterne's *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]]* (1768) as a city on [[smelfungus|Smelfungus]]'s satirical itinerary — "from [[rome|Rome]] to Naples, — from Naples to [[venice|Venice]]" (Ch. 2, [[calais|Calais]]). Like [[rome|Rome]] and [[venice|Venice]], Naples is a Grand Tour destination that [[smelfungus|Smelfungus]] reaches — and summarily complains about.

## 在小说中的角色 (Role in the Novel)

Naples is glimpsed only through the sour lens of [[mundungus|Smelfungus]], the novel's caricature of the complaining traveler. For [[smelfungus|Smelfungus]], the city — with its dramatic bay, its volcano, its classical associations — offers nothing but material for grievance. He "wrote an account of 'em, but 'twas nothing but the account of his miserable feelings" (Ch. 2, [[calais|Calais]]).

The Naples that [[smelfungus|Smelfungus]] misses — the city of sunlight and歌剧, of classical ruins and the smoldering Vesuvius — is the Naples that the sentimental traveler *would* have found, had he approached it with openness rather than predetermined discontent. For [[yorick|Yorick]], who never reaches [[italy|Italy]] at all, Naples — like [[venice|Venice]] and [[rome|Rome]] — remains a possibility unspoiled by a traveler's sour disposition.
