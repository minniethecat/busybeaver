## PARIS.

[056-001]It was Sunday; and when La Fleur

[056-002]came in, in the morning, with my coffee and roll and butter, he

[056-003]had got himself so gallantly array’d, I scarce knew

[056-004]him. 

[056-005]I had covenanted at Montreuil to give him a new hat with a

[056-006]silver button and loop, and four louis d’ors, pour

[056-007]s’adoniser , when we got to Paris; and the poor fellow,

[056-008]to do him justice, had done wonders with it. 

[056-009]He had bought a bright, clean, good scarlet coat, and a pair

[056-010]of breeches of the same.—They were not a crown worse, he

[056-011]said, for the wearing.—I wish’d him hang’d for

[056-012]telling me.—They look’d so fresh, that though I knew

[056-013]the thing could not be done, yet I would rather have imposed upon

[056-014]my fancy with thinking I had bought them new for the fellow, than

[056-015]that they had come out of the Rue de Friperie. 

[056-016]This is a nicety which makes not the heart sore at Paris. 

[056-017]He had purchased, moreover, a handsome blue satin waistcoat,

[056-018]fancifully enough embroidered:—this was indeed something

[056-019]the worse for the service it had done, but ’twas clean

[056-020]scour’d;—the gold had been touch’d up, and upon

[056-021]the whole was rather showy than otherwise;—and as the blue

[056-022]was not violent, it suited with the coat and breeches very well:

[056-023]he had squeez’d out of the money, moreover, a new bag and a

[056-024]solitaire; and had insisted with the fripier upon a gold

[056-025]pair of garters to his breeches knees.—He had purchased

[056-026]muslin ruffles, bien brodées , with four livres of

[056-027]his own money;—and a pair of white silk stockings for five

[056-028]more;—and to top all, nature had given him a handsome

[056-029]figure, without costing him a sous. 

[056-030]He entered the room thus set off, with his hair dressed in the

[056-031]first style, and with a handsome bouquet in his breast.—In

[056-032]a word, there was that look of festivity in everything about him,

[056-033]which at once put me in mind it was Sunday;—and, by

[056-034]combining both together, it instantly struck me, that the favour

[056-035]he wish’d to ask of me the night before, was to spend the

[056-036]day as every body in Paris spent it besides. I had scarce

[056-037]made the conjecture, when La Fleur, with infinite humility, but

[056-038]with a look of trust, as if I should not refuse him, begg’d

[056-039]I would grant him the day, pour faire le galant

[056-040]vis-à-vis de sa maîtresse . 

[056-041]Now it was the very thing I intended to do myself

[056-042]vis-à-vis Madame de R—.—I had retained the

[056-043]remise on purpose for it, and it would not have mortified my

[056-044]vanity to have had a servant so well dress’d as La Fleur

[056-045]was, to have got up behind it: I never could have worse spared

[056-046]him. 

[056-047]But we must feel , not argue in these

[056-048]embarrassments.—The sons and daughters of Service part with

[056-049]liberty, but not with nature, in their contracts; they are flesh

[056-050]and blood, and have their little vanities and wishes in the midst

[056-051]of the house of bondage, as well as their task-masters;—no

[056-052]doubt, they have set their self-denials at a price,—and

[056-053]their expectations are so unreasonable, that I would often

[056-054]disappoint them, but that their condition puts it so much in my

[056-055]power to do it. 

[056-056]Behold ,— Behold , I am thy

[056-057]servant —disarms me at once of the powers of a

[056-058]master.— 

[056-059]Thou shalt go, La Fleur! said I. 

[056-060]—And what mistress, La Fleur, said I, canst thou have

[056-061]picked up in so little a time at Paris? La Fleur laid his

[056-062]hand upon his breast, and said ’twas a petite

[056-063]demoiselle , at Monsieur le Count de

[056-064]B—’s.—La Fleur had a heart made for society;

[056-065]and, to speak the truth of him, let as few occasions slip him as

[056-066]his master;—so that somehow or other,—but

[056-067]how,—heaven knows,—he had connected himself with the

[056-068]demoiselle upon the landing of the staircase, during the time I

[056-069]was taken up with my passport; and as there was time enough for

[056-070]me to win the Count to my interest, La Fleur had contrived to

[056-071]make it do to win the maid to his. The family, it seems,

[056-072]was to be at Paris that day, and he had made a party with her,

[056-073]and two or three more of the Count’s household, upon the

[056-074]boulevards. 

[056-075]Happy people! that once a week at least are sure to lay down

[056-076]all your cares together, and dance and sing and sport away the

[056-077]weights of grievance, which bow down the spirit of other nations

[056-078]to the earth.
