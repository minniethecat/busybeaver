## CALAIS.

[011-001]The good old monk was within six

[011-002]paces of us, as the idea of him crossed my mind; and was

[011-003]advancing towards us a little out of the line, as if uncertain

[011-004]whether he should break in upon us or no.—He stopp’d,

[011-005]however, as soon as he came up to us, with a world of frankness:

[011-006]and having a horn snuff box in his hand, he presented it open to

[011-007]me.—You shall taste mine—said I, pulling out my box

[011-008](which was a small tortoise one) and putting it into his

[011-009]hand.—’Tis most excellent, said the monk. Then

[011-010]do me the favour, I replied, to accept of the box and all, and

[011-011]when you take a pinch out of it, sometimes recollect it was the

[011-012]peace offering of a man who once used you unkindly, but not from

[011-013]his heart. 

[011-014]The poor monk blush’d as red as scarlet. Mon

[011-015]Dieu ! said he, pressing his hands together—you never

[011-016]used me unkindly.—I should think, said the lady, he is not

[011-017]likely. I blush’d in my turn; but from what

[011-018]movements, I leave to the few who feel, to analyze.—Excuse

[011-019]me, Madame, replied I,—I treated him most unkindly; and

[011-020]from no provocations.—’Tis impossible, said the

[011-021]lady.—My God! cried the monk, with a warmth of asseveration

[011-022]which seem’d not to belong to him—the fault was in

[011-023]me, and in the indiscretion of my zeal.—The lady opposed

[011-024]it, and I joined with her in maintaining it was impossible, that

[011-025]a spirit so regulated as his, could give offence to any. 

[011-026]I knew not that contention could be rendered so sweet and

[011-027]pleasurable a thing to the nerves as I then felt it.—We

[011-028]remained silent, without any sensation of that foolish pain which

[011-029]takes place, when, in such a circle, you look for ten minutes in

[011-030]one another’s faces without saying a word. Whilst

[011-031]this lasted, the monk rubbed his horn box upon the sleeve of his

[011-032]tunic; and as soon as it had acquired a little air of brightness

[011-033]by the friction—he made me a low bow, and said, ’twas

[011-034]too late to say whether it was the weakness or goodness of our

[011-035]tempers which had involved us in this contest—but be it as

[011-036]it would,—he begg’d we might exchange boxes.—In saying

[011-037]this, he presented his to me with one hand, as he took mine from

[011-038]me in the other, and having kissed it,—with a stream of

[011-039]good nature in his eyes, he put it into his bosom,—and took

[011-040]his leave. 

[011-041]I guard this box, as I would the instrumental parts of my

[011-042]religion, to help my mind on to something better: in truth, I

[011-043]seldom go abroad without it; and oft and many a time have I

[011-044]called up by it the courteous spirit of its owner to regulate my

[011-045]own, in the justlings of the world: they had found full

[011-046]employment for his, as I learnt from his story, till about the

[011-047]forty-fifth year of his age, when upon some military services ill

[011-048]requited, and meeting at the same time with a disappointment in

[011-049]the tenderest of passions, he abandoned the sword and the sex

[011-050]together, and took sanctuary not so much in his convent as in

[011-051]himself. 

[011-052]I feel a damp upon my spirits, as I am going to add, that in

[011-053]my last return through Calais, upon enquiring after Father

[011-054]Lorenzo, I heard he had been dead near three months, and was

[011-055]buried, not in his convent, but, according to his desire, in a

[011-056]little cemetery belonging to it, about two leagues off: I had a

[011-057]strong desire to see where they had laid him,—when, upon

[011-058]pulling out his little horn box, as I sat by his grave, and

[011-059]plucking up a nettle or two at the head of it, which had no

[011-060]business to grow there, they all struck together so forcibly upon

[011-061]my affections, that I burst into a flood of tears:—but I am

[011-062]as weak as a woman; and I beg the world not to smile, but to pity

[011-063]me.
