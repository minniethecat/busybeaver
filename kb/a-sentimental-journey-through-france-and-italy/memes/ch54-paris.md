## PARIS.

[054-001]I was immediately followed up by

[054-002]the master of the hotel, who came into my room to tell me I must

[054-003]provide lodgings elsewhere.—How so, friend? said

[054-004]I.—He answered, I had had a young woman lock’d up

[054-005]with me two hours that evening in my bedchamber, and ’twas

[054-006]against the rules of his house.—Very well, said I,

[054-007]we’ll all part friends then,—for the girl is no

[054-008]worse,—and I am no worse,—and you will be just as I

[054-009]found you.—It was enough, he said, to overthrow the credit

[054-010]of his hotel.— Voyez vous , Monsieur, said he,

[054-011]pointing to the foot of the bed we had been sitting upon.—I

[054-012]own it had something of the appearance of an evidence; but my

[054-013]pride not suffering me to enter into any detail of the case, I exhorted

[054-014]him to let his soul sleep in peace, as I resolved to let mine do

[054-015]that night, and that I would discharge what I owed him at

[054-016]breakfast. 

[054-017]I should not have minded, Monsieur, said he, if you had had

[054-018]twenty girls—’Tis a score more, replied I,

[054-019]interrupting him, than I ever reckon’d upon—Provided,

[054-020]added he, it had been but in a morning.—And does the

[054-021]difference of the time of the day at Paris make a difference in

[054-022]the sin?—It made a difference, he said, in the

[054-023]scandal.—I like a good distinction in my heart; and cannot

[054-024]say I was intolerably out of temper with the man.—I own it

[054-025]is necessary, resumed the master of the hotel, that a stranger at

[054-026]Paris should have the opportunities presented to him of buying

[054-027]lace and silk stockings and ruffles, et tout

[054-028]cela ;—and ’tis nothing if a woman comes with a

[054-029]band-box.—O, my conscience! said I, she had one but I never

[054-030]look’d into it.—Then Monsieur, said he, has bought

[054-031]nothing?—Not one earthly thing, replied I.—Because,

[054-032]said he, I could recommend one to you who would use you en

[054-033]conscience .—But I must see her this night, said

[054-034]I.—He made me a low bow, and walk’d down. 

[054-035]Now shall I triumph over this maître

[054-036]d’hôtel , cried I,—and what then? Then

[054-037]I shall let him see I know he is a dirty fellow.—And what

[054-038]then? What then?—I was too near myself to say it was

[054-039]for the sake of others.—I had no good answer

[054-040]left;—there was more of spleen than principle in my

[054-041]project, and I was sick of it before the execution. 

[054-042]In a few minutes the grisette came in with her box of

[054-043]lace.—I’ll buy nothing, however, said I, within

[054-044]myself. 

[054-045]The grisette would show me everything.—I was hard to

[054-046]please: she would not seem to see it; she opened her little

[054-047]magazine, and laid all her laces one after another before

[054-048]me;—unfolded and folded them up again one by one with the

[054-049]most patient sweetness.—I might buy,—or

[054-050]not;—she would let me have everything at my own

[054-051]price:—the poor creature seem’d anxious to get a

[054-052]penny; and laid herself out to win me, and not so much in a

[054-053]manner which seem’d artful, as in one I felt simple and

[054-054]caressing. 

[054-055]If there is not a fund of honest gullibility in man, so much

[054-056]the worse;—my heart relented, and I gave up my second

[054-057]resolution as quietly as the first.—Why should I chastise

[054-058]one for the trespass of another? If thou art tributary to

[054-059]this tyrant of an host, thought I, looking up in her face, so

[054-060]much harder is thy bread. 

[054-061]If I had not had more than four louis d’ors in my purse,

[054-062]there was no such thing as rising up and showing her the door,

[054-063]till I had first laid three of them out in a pair of ruffles. 

[054-064]—The master of the hotel will share the profit with

[054-065]her;—no matter,—then I have only paid as many a poor

[054-066]soul has paid before me, for an act he could not

[054-067]do, or think of.
