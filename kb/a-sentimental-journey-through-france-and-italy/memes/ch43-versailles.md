## VERSAILLES.

[043-001]I should not like to have my enemy

[043-002]take a view of my mind when I am going to ask protection of any

[043-003]man; for which reason I generally endeavour to protect myself;

[043-004]but this going to Monsieur le Duc de C— was an act of

[043-005]compulsion; had it been an act of choice, I should have done it,

[043-006]I suppose, like other people. 

[043-007]How many mean plans of dirty address, as I went along, did my

[043-008]servile heart form! I deserved the Bastile for every one of

[043-009]them. 

[043-010]Then nothing would serve me when I got within sight of

[043-011]Versailles, but putting words and sentences together, and

[043-012]conceiving attitudes and tones to wreath myself into Monsieur le

[043-013]Duc de C—’s good graces.—This will do, said

[043-014]I.—Just as well, retorted I again, as a coat carried up to

[043-015]him by an adventurous tailor, without taking his measure.

[043-016]Fool! continued I,—see Monsieur le Duc’s face

[043-017]first;—observe what character is written in it;—take

[043-018]notice in what posture he stands to hear you;—mark the

[043-019]turns and expressions of his body and limbs;—and for the

[043-020]tone,—the first sound which comes from his lips will give

[043-021]it you; and from all these together you’ll compound an

[043-022]address at once upon the spot, which cannot disgust the

[043-023]Duke;—the ingredients are his own, and most likely to go

[043-024]down. 

[043-025]Well! said I, I wish it well over.—Coward again! as if man to man

[043-026]was not equal throughout the whole surface of the globe; and if

[043-027]in the field—why not face to face in the cabinet too?

[043-028]And trust me, Yorick, whenever it is not so, man is false to

[043-029]himself and betrays his own succours ten times where nature does

[043-030]it once. Go to the Duc de C— with the Bastile in thy

[043-031]looks;—my life for it, thou wilt be sent back to Paris in

[043-032]half an hour with an escort. 

[043-033]I believe so, said I.—Then I’ll go to the Duke, by

[043-034]heaven! with all the gaiety and debonairness in the

[043-035]world.— 

[043-036]—And there you are wrong again, replied I.—A heart

[043-037]at ease, Yorick, flies into no extremes—’tis ever on

[043-038]its centre.—Well! well! cried I, as the coachman

[043-039]turn’d in at the gates, I find I shall do very well: and by

[043-040]the time he had wheel’d round the court, and brought me up

[043-041]to the door, I found myself so much the better for my own

[043-042]lecture, that I neither ascended the steps like a victim to

[043-043]justice, who was to part with life upon the top most,—nor

[043-044]did I mount them with a skip and a couple of strides, as I do

[043-045]when I fly up, Eliza! to thee to meet it. 

[043-046]As I entered the door of the saloon I was met by a person, who

[043-047]possibly might be the maître d’hôtel ,

[043-048]but had more the air of one of the under secretaries, who told me

[043-049]the Duc de C— was busy.—I am utterly ignorant, said

[043-050]I, of the forms of obtaining an audience, being an absolute

[043-051]stranger, and what is worse in the present conjuncture of

[043-052]affairs, being an Englishman too.—He replied, that did not

[043-053]increase the difficulty.—I made him a slight bow, and told

[043-054]him, I had something of importance to say to Monsieur le

[043-055]Duc. The secretary look’d towards the stairs, as if

[043-056]he was about to leave me to carry up this account to some

[043-057]one.—But I must not mislead you, said I,—for what I

[043-058]have to say is of no manner of importance to Monsieur le Duc de

[043-059]C— —but of great importance to

[043-060]myself.— C’est une autre affaire , replied

[043-061]he.—Not at all, said I, to a man of gallantry.—But

[043-062]pray, good sir, continued I, when can a stranger hope to have

[043-063]access?—In not less than two hours, said he, looking at his

[043-064]watch. The number of equipages in the court-yard seemed to

[043-065]justify the calculation, that I could have no nearer a

[043-066]prospect;—and as walking backwards and forwards in the

[043-067]saloon, without a soul to commune with, was for the time as bad

[043-068]as being in the Bastile itself, I instantly went back to my

[043-069]remise, and bid the coachman drive me to the Cordon Bleu ,

[043-070]which was the nearest hotel. 

[043-071]I think there is a fatality in it;—I seldom go to the

[043-072]place I set out for.
