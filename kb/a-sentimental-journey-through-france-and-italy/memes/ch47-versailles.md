## VERSAILLES.

[047-001]There is not a more perplexing

[047-002]affair in life to me, than to set about telling any one who I

[047-003]am,—for there is scarce any body I cannot give a better

[047-004]account of than myself; and I have often wished I could do it in

[047-005]a single word,—and have an end of it. It was the only

[047-006]time and occasion in my life I could accomplish this to any

[047-007]purpose;—for Shakespeare lying upon the table, and

[047-008]recollecting I was in his books, I took up Hamlet, and turning

[047-009]immediately to the grave-diggers’ scene in the fifth act, I

[047-010]laid my finger upon Yorick, and advancing the book to the Count, with my

[047-011]finger all the way over the name,— Me voici ! said

[047-012]I. 

[047-013]Now, whether the idea of poor Yorick’s skull was put out

[047-014]of the Count’s mind by the reality of my own, or by what

[047-015]magic he could drop a period of seven or eight hundred years,

[047-016]makes nothing in this account;—’tis certain the

[047-017]French conceive better than they combine;—I wonder at

[047-018]nothing in this world, and the less at this; inasmuch as one of

[047-019]the first of our own Church, for whose candour and paternal

[047-020]sentiments I have the highest veneration, fell into the same

[047-021]mistake in the very same case:—“He could not

[047-022]bear,” he said, “to look into the sermons wrote by

[047-023]the King of Denmark’s jester.” Good, my Lord

[047-024]said I; but there are two Yoricks. The Yorick your Lordship

[047-025]thinks of, has been dead and buried eight hundred years ago; he

[047-026]flourished in Horwendillus’s court;—the other Yorick

[047-027]is myself, who have flourished, my Lord, in no court.—He

[047-028]shook his head. Good God! said I, you might as well

[047-029]confound Alexander the Great with Alexander the Coppersmith, my

[047-030]lord!—“’Twas all one,” he

[047-031]replied.— 

[047-032]—If Alexander, King of Macedon, could have translated

[047-033]your Lordship, said I, I’m sure your Lordship would not

[047-034]have said so. 

[047-035]The poor Count de B— fell but into the same error . 

[047-036]— Et , Monsieur , est-il Yorick ? cried

[047-037]the Count.— Je le suis , said

[047-038]I.— Vous ?— Moi ,— moi qui ai

[047-039]l’honneur de vous parler , Monsieur le

[047-040]Comte .— Mon Dieu ! said he, embracing

[047-041]me,— Vous êtes Yorick ! 

[047-042]The Count instantly put the Shakespeare into his pocket, and

[047-043]left me alone in his room.
