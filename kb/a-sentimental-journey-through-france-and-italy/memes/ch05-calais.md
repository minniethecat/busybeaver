## CALAIS.

[005-001]When a man is discontented with

[005-002]himself, it has one advantage however, that it puts him into an

[005-003]excellent frame of mind for making a bargain. Now there

[005-004]being no travelling through France and Italy without a

[005-005]chaise,—and nature generally prompting us to the thing we

[005-006]are fittest for, I walk’d out into the coach-yard to buy or

[005-007]hire something of that kind to my purpose: an old désobligeant [562] in the furthest

[005-008]corner of the court, hit my fancy at first sight, so I instantly

[005-009]got into it, and finding it in tolerable harmony with my

[005-010]feelings, I ordered the waiter to call Monsieur Dessein, the

[005-011]master of the hotel:—but Monsieur Dessein being gone to

[005-012]vespers, and not caring to face the Franciscan, whom I saw on the

[005-013]opposite side of the court, in conference with a lady just

[005-014]arrived at the inn,—I drew the taffeta curtain betwixt us,

[005-015]and being determined to write my journey, I took out my pen and

[005-016]ink and wrote the preface to it in the désobligeant .
