## MARIA.

[064-001]When Maria had come a little to

[064-002]herself, I ask’d her if she remembered a pale thin person

[064-003]of a man, who had sat down betwixt her and her goat about two

[064-004]years before? She said she was unsettled much at

[064-005]that time, but remembered it upon two accounts:—that ill as

[064-006]she was, she saw the person pitied her; and next, that her goat

[064-007]had stolen his handkerchief, and she had beat him for the

[064-008]theft;—she had wash’d it, she said, in the brook, and

[064-009]kept it ever since in her pocket to restore it to him in case she

[064-010]should ever see him again, which, she added, he had half promised

[064-011]her. As she told me this, she took the handkerchief out of

[064-012]her pocket to let me see it; she had folded it up neatly in a

[064-013]couple of vine leaves, tied round with a tendril;—on

[064-014]opening it, I saw an S. marked in one of the corners. 

[064-015]She had since that, she told me, stray’d as far as Rome,

[064-016]and walk’d round St. Peter’s once,—and

[064-017]return’d back;—that she found her way alone across

[064-018]the Apennines;—had travell’d over all Lombardy,

[064-019]without money,—and through the flinty roads of Savoy

[064-020]without shoes:—how she had borne it, and how she had got

[064-021]supported, she could not tell;—but God tempers the

[064-022]wind , said Maria, to the shorn lamb . 

[064-023]Shorn indeed! and to the quick, said I: and wast thou in my

[064-024]own land, where I have a cottage, I would take thee to it, and

[064-025]shelter thee: thou shouldst eat of my own bread and drink of my

[064-026]own cup;—I would be kind to thy Sylvio;—in all thy

[064-027]weaknesses and wanderings I would seek after thee and bring thee

[064-028]back;—when the sun went down I would say my prayers: and

[064-029]when I had done thou shouldst play thy evening song upon thy

[064-030]pipe, nor would the incense of my sacrifice be worse accepted for

[064-031]entering heaven along with that of a broken heart! 

[064-032]Nature melted within me, as I utter’d this; and Maria

[064-033]observing, as I took out my handkerchief, that it was

[064-034]steep’d too much already to be of use, would needs go wash

[064-035]it in the stream.—And where will you dry it, Maria? said

[064-036]I.—I’ll dry it in my bosom, said

[064-037]she:—’twill do me good. 

[064-038]And is your heart still so warm, Maria? said I. 

[064-039]I touch’d upon the string on which hung all her

[064-040]sorrows:—she look’d with wistful disorder for some

[064-041]time in my face; and then, without saying any thing, took her

[064-042]pipe and play’d her service to the Virgin.—The string

[064-043]I had touched ceased to vibrate;—in a moment or two

[064-044]Maria returned to herself,—let her pipe fall,—and

[064-045]rose up. 

[064-046]And where are you going, Maria? said I.—She said, to

[064-047]Moulines.—Let us go, said I, together.—Maria put her

[064-048]arm within mine, and lengthening the string, to let the dog

[064-049]follow,—in that order we enter’d Moulines.
