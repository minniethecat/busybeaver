# Silence

Silence in *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]]* is never empty. It is the space between words where feeling gathers, the pause that amplifies what has been said and anticipates what will follow. Sterne treats silence as a distinct mode of communication — more dangerous than speech because more intimate, more revealing because ungoverned by social form. In a novel built on conversation and gesture, silence is the medium through which the deepest sentimental transactions occur.

## 定义与历史语境

The eighteenth century inherited from classical rhetoric an understanding of silence as a figure of speech in its own right — *aposiopesis*, the meaningful breaking-off. Sterne, steeped in both classical rhetoric and Lockean psychology, understood silence not as the absence of communication but as a different register of it. Locke had written of the "silence of the mind" in reflection; Sterne transposes this inward silence into the social sphere, where it becomes the most delicate instrument of sentimental exchange.

## 在 Sterne 小说中的表现

The novel's first charged silence comes at [[the-remise-door|the Remise Door]] in [[calais|Calais]], when [[yorick|Yorick]] takes the lady's hand. "She looked down — a silence of some moments followed" ([[calais|Calais]], [[the-remise-door|the Remise Door]]). The silence is active, not passive: in it, the pulsations of the arteries carry the conversation forward without words. [[yorick|Yorick]] fears he has made "some slight efforts towards a closer compression of her hand" — the silence is the medium in which this pressure is felt and measured.

Silence can also be fatal. When Mons. Dessein leaves [[yorick|Yorick]] and the lady alone, "a silence of a single moment upon Mons. Dessein's leaving us, had been fatal to the situation — she had infallibly turned about" ([[calais|Calais]], [[the-remise-door|the Remise Door]]). [[yorick|Yorick]] must speak instantly to preserve the encounter; silence here threatens withdrawal. The same silence, differently timed, is the novel's most consequential decision.

In the darker registers, silence becomes torment. [[yorick|Yorick]] notes that a man should withhold the word "till an hour or two, at least, after the time that his silence upon it becomes tormenting" ([[calais|Calais]] chapters) — silence transformed from communication into strategy, a deliberate withholding that heightens desire. The "dead silence" that falls when the Marquis dismisses [[yorick|Yorick]] ([[paris|Paris]] chapters) is silence as social death, the withdrawal of recognition.
