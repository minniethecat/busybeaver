# Turin

**Turin** is the Italian city that serves as [[yorick|Yorick]]'s stated destination in Laurence Sterne's *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]] Through [[france|France]] and [[italy|Italy]]* (1768). The capital of the [[savoy|Duchy of Savoy]] and, in the 1760s, the seat of the Kingdom of Sardinia, Turin represents the [[italy|Italy]] that exists in the novel only as promise — a destination perpetually deferred, never actually reached.

## 地理与文化背景

In the 18th century, Turin was the capital of the House of [[savoy|Savoy]] and a major stop on the Grand Tour. Reaching it from [[france|France]] required crossing the Alps through [[savoy|Savoy]] — a journey "of caution" through "mountains impracticable, and cataracts, which roll down great stones from their summits" (069-002). For English travelers, the passage from [[france|France]] into [[italy|Italy]] via the Mont Cenis pass was a rite of passage, marking the transition from familiar Northern European culture to the exotic South.

[[smelfungus|Smelfungus]], the novel's satirical portrait of Tobias Smollett, is encountered by [[yorick|Yorick]] "at Turin, in his return" (017-004) — already on his way back, his Grand Tour completed in a spirit of complaint and disappointment that [[yorick|Yorick]]'s own approach to travel implicitly rebukes.

## 在小说中的角色

Turin is the novel's structuring absence — the unreached goal that gives the journey its shape. The full title promises "Through [[france|France]] *and [[italy|Italy]]*," and throughout the narrative [[yorick|Yorick]] speaks of his intention to reach Turin. Yet the novel ends before he crosses the Alps. This incompleteness is not a flaw but a structural feature: the sentimental journey is, by its nature, more concerned with the encounters along the way than with the destination.

**The Promise of [[italy|Italy]]**. Turin is invoked at key moments as the journey's end point. [[la-fleur|La Fleur]], when asked about his family, mentions a brother "a drummer in the Piedmontese regiment at Turin" (017-006) — a detail that gives the distant city a human connection. The voiturin contracted "to take his time with a couple of mules, and convoy me in my own chaise safe to Turin, through [[savoy|Savoy]]" (069-001) marks the final commitment to the Italian leg of the journey.

**The Unreached Destination**. The novel famously breaks off before [[yorick|Yorick]] reaches [[italy|Italy]] — and whether this incompleteness was intentional or a result of Sterne's death in 1768 remains a matter of critical debate. In either case, Turin as the never-reached destination has become part of the novel's meaning: the sentimental journey is about the traveling, not the arriving.
