## MONTREUIL.

[023-001]When all is ready, and every

[023-002]article is disputed and paid for in the inn, unless you are a

[023-003]little sour’d by the adventure, there is always a matter to

[023-004]compound at the door, before you can get into your chaise; and

[023-005]that is with the sons and daughters of poverty, who surround

[023-006]you. Let no man say, “Let them go to the

[023-007]devil!”—’tis a cruel journey to send a few

[023-008]miserables, and they have had sufferings enow without it: I

[023-009]always think it better to take a few sous out in my hand; and I

[023-010]would counsel every gentle traveller to do so likewise: he need

[023-011]not be so exact in setting down his motives for giving

[023-012]them;—They will be registered elsewhere. 

[023-013]For my own part, there is no man gives so little as I do; for

[023-014]few, that I know, have so little to give; but as this was the

[023-015]first public act of my charity in France, I took the more notice

[023-016]of it. 

[023-017]A well-a-way! said I,—I have but eight sous in the

[023-018]world, showing them in my hand, and there are eight poor men and

[023-019]eight poor women for ’em. 

[023-020]A poor tatter’d soul, without a shirt on, instantly

[023-021]withdrew his claim, by retiring two steps out of the circle, and

[023-022]making a disqualifying bow on his part. Had the whole parterre cried out, Place aux dames , with one

[023-023]voice, it would not have conveyed the sentiment of a deference

[023-024]for the sex with half the effect. 

[023-025]Just Heaven! for what wise reasons hast thou ordered it, that

[023-026]beggary and urbanity, which are at such variance in other

[023-027]countries, should find a way to be at unity in this? 

[023-028]—I insisted upon presenting him with a single sous,

[023-029]merely for his politesse . 

[023-030]A poor little dwarfish brisk fellow, who stood over against me

[023-031]in the circle, putting something first under his arm, which had

[023-032]once been a hat, took his snuff-box out of his pocket, and

[023-033]generously offer’d a pinch on both sides of him: it was a

[023-034]gift of consequence, and modestly declined.—The poor little

[023-035]fellow pressed it upon them with a nod of

[023-036]welcomeness.— Prenez en—prenez , said he,

[023-037]looking another way; so they each took a pinch.—Pity thy

[023-038]box should ever want one! said I to myself; so I put a couple of

[023-039]sous into it—taking a small pinch out of his box, to

[023-040]enhance their value, as I did it. He felt the weight of the

[023-041]second obligation more than of the first,—’twas doing

[023-042]him an honour,—the other was only doing him a

[023-043]charity;—and he made me a bow down to the ground for

[023-044]it. 

[023-045]—Here! said I to an old soldier with one hand, who had

[023-046]been campaigned and worn out to death in the

[023-047]service—here’s a couple of sous for

[023-048]thee.— Vive le Roi ! said the old soldier. 

[023-049]I had then but three sous left: so I gave one, simply, pour

[023-050]l’amour de Dieu , which was the footing on which it was

[023-051]begg’d.—The poor woman had a dislocated hip; so it

[023-052]could not be well upon any other motive. 

[023-053]Mon cher et très-charitable

[023-054]Monsieur .—There’s no opposing this, said I. 

[023-055]Milord Anglois —the very sound was worth the

[023-056]money;—so I gave my last sous for it . But in

[023-057]the eagerness of giving, I had overlooked a pauvre

[023-058]honteux , who had had no one to ask a sous for him, and who, I

[023-059]believe, would have perished, ere he could have ask’d one

[023-060]for himself: he stood by the chaise a little without the circle,

[023-061]and wiped a tear from a face which I thought had seen better

[023-062]days.—Good God! said I—and I have not one single sous

[023-063]left to give him.—But you have a thousand! cried all the

[023-064]powers of nature, stirring within me;—so I gave

[023-065]him—no matter what—I am ashamed to say how

[023-066]much now,—and was ashamed to think how little, then:

[023-067]so, if the reader can form any conjecture of my disposition, as

[023-068]these two fixed points are given him, he may judge within a livre

[023-069]or two what was the precise sum. 

[023-070]I could afford nothing for the rest, but Dieu vous

[023-071]bénisse ! 

[023-072]— Et le bon Dieu vous bénisse encore , said

[023-073]the old soldier, the dwarf, &c. The pauvre

[023-074]honteux could say nothing;—he pull’d out a little

[023-075]handkerchief, and wiped his face as he turned away—and I

[023-076]thought he thanked me more than them all.
