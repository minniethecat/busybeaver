# Lisle

**Lisle** (modern **Lille**), a fortified city on the Franco-Flemish border, appears in Laurence Sterne's *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]]* (1768) as one of a series of place-names rattled off by the French captain in his rapid-fire courtship of a mysterious lady at [[calais|Calais]].

## 在小说中的角色 (Role in the Novel)

Lisle enters the narrative during the remise-door scene in [[calais|Calais]], where [[yorick|Yorick]] watches a French captain deploy comic efficiency in the art of introduction. Having established that the lady is Flemish — "Apparemment vous êtes Flammande?" — the captain runs through the principal towns of [[flanders|Flanders]] in quick succession: "Peut être de Lisle?" She is not of Lisle. "Nor Arras? — nor Cambray? — nor Ghent? — nor [[brussels|Brussels]]?" At last she answers: she is of [[brussels|Brussels]] (Ch. 2, [[the-remise-door|The Remise Door]]).

The passage is a miniature study in social technique. The captain's geographical guessing game is not idle: each place-name is a conversational gambit, a pretext to demonstrate his own worldliness (he goes on to mention his service at the bombardment of [[brussels|Brussels]] during the last war) while flattering the lady with his attentiveness. Lisle, as the first guess, anchors the sequence in the geography of French [[flanders|Flanders]] — the borderland where French and Flemish identities meet and mingle.

Within the novel's geography, Lisle belongs to the constellation of northern towns — [[calais|Calais]], [[montreuil|Montreuil]], [[boulogne|Boulogne]], [[flanders|Flanders]] — that mark [[yorick|Yorick]]'s passage from [[england|England]] toward [[paris|Paris]]. Unlike [[calais|Calais]] or [[montreuil|Montreuil]], Lisle is not visited; it is merely named, a possible origin for the lady, a signpost in the captain's mental map of the border country. Its thin presence (a single line of dialogue) is characteristic of the novel's outermost layer of place references — locations that exist in the characters' speech but never become settings.
