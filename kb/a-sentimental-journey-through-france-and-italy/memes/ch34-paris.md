## PARIS.

[034-001]The beautiful grisette rose up when

[034-002]I said this, and going behind the counter, reach’d down a

[034-003]parcel and untied it: I advanced to the side over against her:

[034-004]they were all too large. The beautiful grisette measured

[034-005]them one by one across my hand.—It would not alter their

[034-006]dimensions.—She begg’d I would try a single pair,

[034-007]which seemed to be the least.—She held it open;—my

[034-008]hand slipped into it at once.—It will not do, said I,

[034-009]shaking my head a little.—No, said she, doing the same

[034-010]thing. 

[034-011]There are certain combined looks of simple

[034-012]subtlety,—where whim, and sense, and seriousness, and

[034-013]nonsense, are so blended, that all the languages of Babel set

[034-014]loose together, could not express them;—they are

[034-015]communicated and caught so instantaneously, that you can scarce

[034-016]say which party is the infector. I leave it to your men of

[034-017]words to swell pages about it—it is enough in the present

[034-018]to say again, the gloves would not do; so, folding our hands

[034-019]within our arms, we both lolled upon the counter—it was

[034-020]narrow, and there was just room for the parcel to lay between

[034-021]us. 

[034-022]The beautiful grisette looked sometimes at the gloves, then

[034-023]sideways to the window, then at the gloves,—and then at

[034-024]me. I was not disposed to break silence:—I followed

[034-025]her example: so, I looked at the gloves, then to the window, then

[034-026]at the gloves, and then at her,—and so on alternately. 

[034-027]I found I lost considerably in every attack:—she had a

[034-028]quick black eye, and shot through two such long and silken

[034-029]eyelashes with such penetration, that she look’d into my

[034-030]very heart and reins.—It may seem strange, but I could

[034-031]actually feel she did.— 

[034-032]It is no matter, said I, taking up a couple of the pairs next

[034-033]me, and putting them into my pocket. 

[034-034]I was sensible the beautiful grisette had not asked above a

[034-035]single livre above the price.—I wish’d she had asked

[034-036]a livre more, and was puzzling my brains how to bring the

[034-037]matter about.—Do you think, my dear Sir, said she,

[034-038]mistaking my embarrassment, that I could ask a sous too much of a

[034-039]stranger—and of a stranger whose politeness, more than his

[034-040]want of gloves, has done me the honour to lay himself at my

[034-041]mercy?— M’en croyez capable ?—Faith! not

[034-042]I, said I; and if you were, you are welcome. So counting

[034-043]the money into her hand, and with a lower bow than one generally

[034-044]makes to a shopkeeper’s wife, I went out, and her lad with

[034-045]his parcel followed me.
