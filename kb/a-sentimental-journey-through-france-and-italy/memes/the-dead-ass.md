# The Dead Ass

**The Dead Ass** is a celebrated sentimental episode in Laurence Sterne's *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]] Through [[calais|France]] and [[italy|Italy]]* (1768), occurring in chapter 25. [[yorick|Yorick]] encounters a poor peasant mourning his dead donkey on the road near [[montreuil|Montreuil]], and the man's simple, profound grief becomes a powerful illustration of human sympathy.

## Summary

[[yorick|Yorick]] comes upon a peasant sitting by the roadside, lamenting the loss of his donkey (025-050). The man recounts how the donkey had served him faithfully for many years, carrying him and his burdens. Moved by the peasant's unaffected sorrow, [[yorick|Yorick]] reflects on the nature of grief — contrasting the peasant's authentic mourning with the formal, shallow condolences of polite society.

## Themes

- **[[sentiment|Sentiment]]al grief**: The peasant's lament is presented as truer and more moving than aristocratic mourning.
- **The dignity of poverty**: Sterne gives the poor peasant a moral weight typically reserved for high-born characters in 18th-century literature.
- **Animal companionship**: The donkey is not merely property but a companion whose loss is genuinely felt.
