# Venice

**Venice** enters Laurence Sterne's *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]]* (1768) not as an experienced destination but through the satirical figure of [[mundungus|Smelfungus]], who carries his dyspeptic discontent from "[[rome|Rome]] to [[naples|Naples]], — from [[naples|Naples]] to Venice" (Ch. 2, [[calais|Calais]]). Like [[rome|Rome]] and [[naples]], Venice is a name on an itinerary that [[smelfungus|Smelfungus]] reaches — and thoroughly fails to appreciate.

## 在小说中的角色 (Role in the Novel)

Venice appears exclusively through the lens of Sterne's satire of travel writing:

**[[smelfungus|Smelfungus]]'s Venice**: The learned [[smelfungus|Smelfungus]] (a thinly veiled caricature of Tobias Smollett) "wrote an account" of his continental travels — "but 'twas nothing but the account of his miserable feelings." His Venice, like his [[rome|Rome]] and his [[naples|Naples]], is a city seen through the sour lens of a traveler incapable of the openness that defines the sentimental mode. (Ch. 2, [[calais|Calais]])

**The Anti-[[sentiment|Sentiment]]al Itinerary**: [[smelfungus|Smelfungus]]'s Grand Tour — [[boulogne|Boulogne]] → [[paris|Paris]] → [[rome|Rome]] → [[naples|Naples]] → Venice → Vienna → Dresden → Berlin — is a checklist of destinations traveled without feeling. Each city is reduced to an occasion for grievance. For Sterne, this is the antithesis of the sentimental journey, where a single street corner in [[paris|Paris]] or a coach-yard in [[calais|Calais]] can yield more genuine emotion than all of [[smelfungus|Smelfungus]]'s continental capitals combined.

## 象征意义 (Symbolic Significance)

Venice in the novel functions as a negative example — a city that demonstrates, by [[smelfungus|Smelfungus]]'s failure to appreciate it, what the sentimental traveler must avoid. The city of canals and Carnival, of masks and music, becomes for [[smelfungus|Smelfungus]] merely another stop on a tour of disappointments. For [[yorick|Yorick]], who never reaches [[italy|Italy]] at all, the unvisited Venice remains a possibility — unspoiled by the traveler's sour gaze.
