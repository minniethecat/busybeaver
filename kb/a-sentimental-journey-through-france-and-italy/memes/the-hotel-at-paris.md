# The Hotel at Paris

The Hotel at Paris episode from [[sentimentalism|Laurence Sterne]]'s [[a-sentimental-journey|A Sentimental Journey]] recounts [[yorick|Yorick]]'s arrival in [[paris]] and his comic difficulties navigating the city. The scene illustrates the [[sentimentalism|sentimental traveler]]'s reliance on his servant [[la-fleur]] and the theme of social [[embarrassment]].

## Historical Context

The [[hotel-de-modene]] (Hotel de Modene) was a real Parisian establishment on Rue Jacob in the [[france|Faubourg Saint-Germain]] district, popular with English travelers in the mid-18th century. Sterne himself stayed there during his 1762 journey through [[france]].

## Narrative

Yorick arrives at the Hotel de Modene in [[paris]] around nine o'clock at night. He resolves to treat [[la-fleur]] with good humor rather than severity, dropping the subject of his earlier embarrassment entirely. During supper, Yorick talks to [[la-fleur]] with more than usual gaiety about [[paris]] and the [[opera-comique]].

The central comic episode involves Yorick's attempt to ask for directions. Despite consulting a map of [[paris]] spread across the table, he becomes hopelessly lost. After a series of misadventures in the streets, he eventually finds his way back to the hotel through a combination of luck and [[la-fleur]]'s practical assistance.

## Themes

The episode explores the humor of linguistic and cultural confusion, the dynamics between master and servant, and the vulnerability of the foreign traveler. Yorick's decision to treat [[la-fleur]] kindly rather than with severity reflects the sentimental ideal of benevolent social relations.
