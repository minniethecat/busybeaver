## THE BOURBONNNOIS.

[066-001]There was nothing from which I had

[066-002]painted out for my self so joyous a riot of the affections, as in

[066-003]this journey in the vintage, through this part of France; but

[066-004]pressing through this gate, of sorrow to it, my sufferings have

[066-005]totally unfitted me. In every scene of festivity, I saw

[066-006]Maria in the background of the piece, sitting pensive under her

[066-007]poplar; and I had got almost to Lyons before I was able to cast a

[066-008]shade across her. 

[066-009]—Dear Sensibility! source inexhausted of all

[066-010]that’s precious in our joys, or costly in our sorrows! thou

[066-011]chainest thy martyr down upon his bed of straw—and

[066-012]’tis thou who lift’st him up to Heaven!—Eternal

[066-013]Fountain of our feelings!—’tis here I trace

[066-014]thee—and this is thy “ divinity which stirs within

[066-015]me ;”—not that, in some sad and sickening moments,

[066-016]“ my soul shrinks back upon herself , and startles

[066-017]at destruction ;”—mere pomp of words!—but

[066-018]that I feel some generous joys and generous cares beyond

[066-019]myself;—all comes from thee, great—great Sensorium of the world! which vibrates, if a

[066-020]hair of our heads but falls upon the ground, in the remotest

[066-021]desert of thy creation.—Touch’d with thee, Eugenius

[066-022]draws my curtain when I languish—hears my tale of symptoms,

[066-023]and blames the weather for the disorder of his nerves. Thou

[066-024]giv’st a portion of it sometimes to the roughest peasant

[066-025]who traverses the bleakest mountains;—he finds the

[066-026]lacerated lamb of another’s flock.—This moment I

[066-027]behold him leaning with his head against his crook, with piteous

[066-028]inclination looking down upon it!—Oh! had I come one moment

[066-029]sooner! it bleeds to death!—his gentle heart bleeds with

[066-030]it.— 

[066-031]Peace to thee, generous swain!—I see thou walkest off

[066-032]with anguish,—but thy joys shall balance it;—for,

[066-033]happy is thy cottage,—and happy is the sharer of

[066-034]it,—and happy are the lambs which sport about you!
