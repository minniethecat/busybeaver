## CALAIS.

[001-001]When I had fished my dinner, and drank the King of

[001-002]France’s health, to satisfy my mind that I bore him no

[001-003]spleen, but, on the contrary, high honour for the humanity of his

[001-004]temper,—I rose up an inch taller for the accommodation. 

[001-005]—No—said I—the Bourbon is by no means a

[001-006]cruel race: they may be misled, like other people; but there is a

[001-007]mildness in their blood. As I acknowledged this, I felt a

[001-008]suffusion of a finer kind upon my cheek—more warm and

[001-009]friendly to man, than what Burgundy (at least of two livres a

[001-010]bottle, which was such as I had been drinking) could have

[001-011]produced. 

[001-012]—Just God! said I, kicking my portmanteau aside, what is

[001-013]there in this world’s goods which should sharpen our

[001-014]spirits, and make so many kind-hearted brethren of us fall out so

[001-015]cruelly as we do by the way? 

[001-016]When man is at peace with man, how much lighter than a feather

[001-017]is the heaviest of metals in his hand! he pulls out his purse,

[001-018]and holding it airily and uncompressed, looks round him, as if he

[001-019]sought for an object to share it with.—In doing this, I

[001-020]felt every vessel in my frame dilate,—the arteries beat all

[001-021]cheerily together, and every power which sustained life,

[001-022]performed it with so little friction, that ’twould have

[001-023]confounded the most physical précieuse in France;

[001-024]with all her materialism, she could scarce have called me a

[001-025]machine.— 

[001-026]I’m confident, said I to myself, I should have overset

[001-027]her creed. 

[001-028]The accession of that idea carried nature, at that time, as

[001-029]high as she could go;—I was at peace with the world before,

[001-030]and this finish’d the treaty with myself.— 

[001-031]—Now, was I King of France, cried I—what a

[001-032]moment for an orphan to have begg’d his father’s

[001-033]portmanteau of me!
