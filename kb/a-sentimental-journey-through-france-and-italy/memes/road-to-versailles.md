# Road to Versailles

The Road to Versailles episode recounts [[yorick|Yorick]]'s journey from [[paris]] to [[versailles]] and the reflections it prompts. The scene is notable for the embedded story of a caged [[the-starling|starling]] that becomes a powerful symbol of captivity and the longing for [[liberty]].

## Historical Context

[[versailles]] in the 1760s was the seat of the French court under [[louis-xv]]. Foreign visitors to [[paris]] frequently made the journey to [[versailles]] to see the palace and, if possible, gain an audience. The road from [[paris]] to [[versailles]] was approximately 12 miles, a journey of several hours by carriage.

## Narrative

[[yorick]] enters his remise at the appointed hour, with [[la-fleur]] riding behind. The coachman is directed to make his best way to [[versailles]]. Finding little of conventional interest along the road, [[yorick]] fills the blank with a "short history" of a starling.

The starling's story becomes a meditation on [[liberty]] and confinement. [[yorick]] reflects on the universal desire for freedom, drawing connections between the caged bird and the human condition. The episode demonstrates the [[sentimentalism|sentimental]] mode of finding profound meaning in seemingly trivial encounters.

## Characters

| Character | Role |

## Themes

The episode foregrounds themes of [[liberty]], confinement, and the [[sentimentalism|sentimental]] capacity to derive moral reflection from everyday experience. The starling functions as what Sterne scholars call a "sentimental object" — a catalyst for emotional and philosophical response.
