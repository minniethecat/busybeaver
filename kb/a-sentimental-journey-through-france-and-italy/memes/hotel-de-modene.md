# Hôtel de Modène

The **Hôtel de Modène** is the [[paris|Paris]]ian hotel where [[yorick|Yorick]] lodges throughout the [[paris|Paris]] chapters of Laurence Sterne's *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]] Through [[france|France]] and [[italy|Italy]]* (1768). More than a simple setting, it functions as the narrative's private interior — a foil to [[paris|Paris]]'s public spectacles — where the novel's most intimate encounters unfold and where [[yorick|Yorick]]'s domestic relationship with [[la-fleur|La Fleur]] takes shape.

## 在小说中的角色

The Hôtel de Modène serves as [[yorick|Yorick]]'s retreat from the sensory bombardment of [[paris|Paris]] and the stage for several of the novel's key dramatic moments. Unlike the streets, bookshops, and opera houses where [[yorick|Yorick]] performs his public sensibility, the hotel room is where he reflects, worries, and encounters others in unguarded intimacy.

**The Home Base**. [[yorick|Yorick]] records the hotel's name at arrival: "I wrote down the name of the Hôtel de Modene, where I lodged." (036-PPP) It becomes the fixed point around which his [[paris|Paris]]ian wanderings revolve — the place to which he returns after each day's adventures, where [[la-fleur|La Fleur]] waits upon him at supper, and where he composes (or fails to compose) his letters and reflections.

**Directions from the Fille de Chambre**. When [[yorick|Yorick]] asks the fille de chambre for directions at the corner of the [[rue-de-nevers|Rue de Nevers]] — "But is this the way, my dear, said I, to the Hotel de Modene?" — she tells him he may go by the [[rue-de-gueneguault|Rue de Gueneguault]] instead. Her response reveals her own wish: "she wished the Hotel de Modene was in the Rue de St. Pierre," where [[madame-de-r|Madame de R—]] lives. (037-PPP) The exchange transforms a simple direction into a moment of unspoken connection — the girl's wish that [[yorick|Yorick]]'s lodgings were closer to her own world.

**[[la-fleur|La Fleur]]'s Intelligence Network**. [[la-fleur|La Fleur]] uses his knowledge of the hotel to serve [[yorick|Yorick]] in unexpected ways. After following [[yorick|Yorick]] to the bookseller's and witnessing his walk with the fille de chambre along the [[quai-de-conti|Quai de Conti]], [[la-fleur|La Fleur]] "took a shorter cut, — and got to the hotel in time to be inform'd of the affair of the police against my arrival." (041-PPP) The hotel becomes, through [[la-fleur|La Fleur]]'s agency, a node of information as well as shelter — a place where news of [[paris|Paris]]'s administrative machinery reaches [[yorick|Yorick]] before he does.

**The Crimson Curtains**. The hotel's most famous scene occurs when the fille de chambre returns on an errand from [[madame-de-r|Madame de R—]]. The porter tells [[yorick|Yorick]] that "a young woman with a bandbox had been that moment enquiring for me," and when he goes upstairs, he meets her on the landing. She returns with him to his room: "It was a fine still evening in the latter end of the month of May, — the crimson window curtains (which were of the same colour as those of the bed) were drawn close: — the sun was setting, and reflected through them so warm a tint into [[the-fille-de-chambre|the fair fille de chambre]]'s face, — I thought she blush'd; — the idea of it made me blush myself: — we were quite alone; and that superinduced a second blush before the first could get off." (061-PPP) The scene is one of Sterne's most carefully composed interiors — the crimson light, the closed curtains, the solitude, the chain of blushes — transforming the hotel room into a space where the physical and the sentimental merge.

**[[the-passport|The Passport]] Anxiety**. The hotel is also where [[yorick|Yorick]] confronts the administrative peril of being an Englishman in [[paris|Paris]] without papers. It is at the Hôtel de Modène that [[la-fleur|La Fleur]] brings news of the police, and where [[yorick|Yorick]] later strategizes his approach to the [[count-de-b|Count de B—]] at [[versailles|Versailles]] — making the hotel not only a place of intimacy but of practical survival.
