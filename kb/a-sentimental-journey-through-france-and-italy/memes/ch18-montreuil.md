## MONTREUIL.

[018-001]I had once lost my portmanteau from

[018-002]behind my chaise, and twice got out in the rain, and one of the

[018-003]times up to the knees in dirt, to help the postilion to tie it

[018-004]on, without being able to find out what was wanting.—Nor

[018-005]was it till I got to Montreuil, upon the landlord’s asking

[018-006]me if I wanted not a servant, that it occurred to me, that that

[018-007]was the very thing. 

[018-008]A servant! That I do most sadly, quoth I.—Because,

[018-009]Monsieur, said the landlord, there is a clever young fellow, who

[018-010]would be very proud of the honour to serve an

[018-011]Englishman.—But why an English one, more than any

[018-012]other?—They are so generous, said the

[018-013]landlord.—I’ll be shot if this is not a livre out of

[018-014]my pocket, quoth I to myself, this very night.—But they

[018-015]have wherewithal to be so, Monsieur, added he.—Set down one

[018-016]livre more for that, quoth I.—It was but last night, said

[018-017]the landlord, qu’un milord Anglois présentoit un

[018-018]écu à la fille de chambre .— Tant pis

[018-019]pour Mademoiselle Janatone , said I. 

[018-020]Now Janatone, being the landlord’s daughter, and the

[018-021]landlord supposing I was young in French, took the liberty to

[018-022]inform me, I should not have said tant pis —but, tant mieux . Tant mieux , toujours , Monsieur , said he, when there is any thing to be

[018-023]got— tant pis , when there is nothing. It comes

[018-024]to the same thing, said I. Pardonnez-moi , said the

[018-025]landlord. 

[018-026]I cannot take a fitter opportunity to observe, once for all,

[018-027]that tant pis and tant mieux , being two of the

[018-028]great hinges in French conversation, a stranger would do well to

[018-029]set himself right in the use of them, before he gets to

[018-030]Paris. 

[018-031]A prompt French marquis at our ambassador’s table

[018-032]demanded of Mr. H—, if he was H— the poet? No,

[018-033]said Mr. H—, mildly.— Tant pis , replied the

[018-034]marquis. 

[018-035]It is H— the historian, said another,— Tant mieux ,

[018-036]said the marquis. And Mr. H—, who is a man of an

[018-037]excellent heart, return’d thanks for both. 

[018-038]When the landlord had set me right in this matter, he called

[018-039]in La Fleur, which was the name of the young man he had spoke

[018-040]of,—saying only first, That as for his talents he would

[018-041]presume to say nothing,—Monsieur was the best judge what

[018-042]would suit him; but for the fidelity of La Fleur he would stand

[018-043]responsible in all he was worth. 

[018-044]The landlord deliver’d this in a manner which instantly

[018-045]set my mind to the business I was upon;—and La Fleur, who

[018-046]stood waiting without, in that breathless expectation which every

[018-047]son of nature of us have felt in our turns, came in.
