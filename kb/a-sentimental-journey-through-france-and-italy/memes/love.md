# Love

Love permeates *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]]* in multiple forms: romantic infatuation, courtly gallantry, universal benevolence, and the tender affection born of momentary encounters. Sterne treats love not as a single passion but as a spectrum of sympathetic feeling — what the sentimental tradition called "sensibility of heart."

## 定义与历史语境

In the eighteenth-century sentimental novel, love was understood broadly as the capacity for sympathetic attachment. It encompassed both the romantic love of gallant literature and the universal benevolence celebrated by Enlightenment moral philosophy. Sterne, following the tradition of the "man of feeling," presents love as the emotional glue of social life — the impulse that draws strangers toward kindness rather than indifference.

## 在 Sterne 小说中的表现

[[yorick|Yorick]] is described by a friend as "always in love" — a condition that defines his character throughout the journey. His loves are numerous and fleeting: the fille de chambre, [[madame-de-l|Madame de L—]], the grisset in [[paris|Paris]], and [[meeting-maria|Maria at Moulines]]. Each encounter stirs genuine feeling, but Sterne deliberately blurs the line between romantic desire and compassionate sympathy.

The novel critiques those who "hate love for the name's sake," presenting love — in all its forms — as inseparable from the sentimental traveller's openness to experience. When [[yorick|Yorick]] reflects on making love "by sentiments," he underscores the sentimentalist conviction that true love is communicated through feeling, not words.

## 关键段落

> "That grave people hate love for the name's sake." (Paris chapters)

> "To think of making love by sentiments!" (Paris chapters)

> "The world, continued he, 'he is always in love.' — I was sometime before I felt the full force of the compliment." (Paris chapters)

> "having been in love with one princess or another almost all my life." (Paris chapters)
