# Boulogne

**Boulogne** (Boulogne-sur-Mer) is a French port town on the English Channel, referenced in Laurence Sterne's *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]]* (1768) through the figure of [[mundungus|Smelfungus]]. It is the first stop on [[smelfungus|Smelfungus]]'s continental tour — and the first place he finds fault with.

## 在小说中的角色 (Role in the Novel)

Boulogne appears exclusively through the lens of the satirical traveler [[smelfungus|Smelfungus]] (Sterne's caricature of Tobias Smollett), who "travelled from Boulogne to [[paris|Paris]], — from [[paris|Paris]] to [[rome|Rome]], — and so on" (Ch. 2, [[calais|Calais]]), finding grievance at every stop. Boulogne, as the port of entry to [[france|France]], should be a threshold of discovery — but for [[smelfungus|Smelfungus]], it is merely the first item on a checklist of disappointments.

The contrast with [[yorick|Yorick]]'s arrival at [[calais|Calais]] is implicit but pointed. Where [[yorick|Yorick]] finds the sentimental opportunity in every encounter, [[smelfungus|Smelfungus]] finds only material for complaint. Boulogne thus serves as the novel's negative exemplar — demonstrating, at the journey's very first stage, how not to travel.
