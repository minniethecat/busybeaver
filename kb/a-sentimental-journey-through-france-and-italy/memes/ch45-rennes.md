## RENNES.

[045-001]When states and empires have their

[045-002]periods of declension, and feel in their turns what distress and

[045-003]poverty is,—I stop not to tell the causes which gradually

[045-004]brought the house d’E—, in Brittany, into

[045-005]decay. The Marquis d’E— had fought up against

[045-006]his condition with great firmness; wishing to preserve, and still

[045-007]show to the world, some little fragments of what his ancestors

[045-008]had been;—their indiscretions had put it out of his

[045-009]power. There was enough left for the little exigencies of obscurity .—But he had two boys who looked up to him

[045-010]for light ;—he thought they deserved it. He had

[045-011]tried his sword—it could not open the way,—the mounting was too expensive,—and simple economy was

[045-012]not a match for it:—there was no resource but commerce. 

[045-013]In any other province in France, save Brittany, this was smiting the

[045-014]root for ever of the little tree his pride and affection

[045-015]wish’d to see re-blossom.—But in Brittany, there

[045-016]being a provision for this, he avail’d himself of it; and,

[045-017]taking an occasion when the states were assembled at Rennes, the

[045-018]Marquis, attended with his two boys, entered the court; and

[045-019]having pleaded the right of an ancient law of the duchy, which,

[045-020]though seldom claim’d, he said, was no less in force, he

[045-021]took his sword from his side:—Here, said he, take it; and

[045-022]be trusty guardians of it, till better times put me in condition

[045-023]to reclaim it. 

[045-024]The president accepted the Marquis’s sword: he staid a

[045-025]few minutes to see it deposited in the archives of his

[045-026]house—and departed. 

[045-027]The Marquis and his whole family embarked the next day for

[045-028]Martinico, and in about nineteen or twenty years of successful

[045-029]application to business, with some unlook’d for bequests

[045-030]from distant branches of his house, return home to reclaim his

[045-031]nobility, and to support it. 

[045-032]It was an incident of good fortune which will never happen to

[045-033]any traveller but a Sentimental one, that I should be at Rennes

[045-034]at the very time of this solemn requisition: I call it

[045-035]solemn;—it was so to me. 

[045-036]The Marquis entered the court with his whole family: he

[045-037]supported his lady,—his eldest son supported his sister,

[045-038]and his youngest was at the other extreme of the line next his

[045-039]mother;—he put his handkerchief to his face

[045-040]twice.— 

[045-041]—There was a dead silence. When the Marquis had

[045-042]approached within six paces of the tribunal, he gave the

[045-043]Marchioness to his youngest son, and advancing three steps before

[045-044]his family,—he reclaim’d his sword. His sword

[045-045]was given him, and the moment he got it into his hand he drew it

[045-046]almost out of the scabbard:—’twas the shining face of

[045-047]a friend he had once given up—he look’d attentively

[045-048]along it, beginning at the hilt, as if to see whether it was the

[045-049]same,—when, observing a little rust which it had contracted

[045-050]near the point, he brought it near his eye, and bending his head

[045-051]down over it,—I think—I saw a tear fall upon the

[045-052]place. I could not be deceived by what followed. 

[045-053]“I shall find,” said he, “some other

[045-054]way to get it off.” 

[045-055]When the Marquis had said this, he returned his sword into its

[045-056]scabbard, made a bow to the guardians of it,—and, with his

[045-057]wife and daughter, and his two sons following him, walk’d

[045-058]out. 

[045-059]O, how I envied him his feelings!
