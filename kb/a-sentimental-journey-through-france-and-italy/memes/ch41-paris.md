## PARIS.

[041-001]The bird in his cage pursued me

[041-002]into my room; I sat down close to my table, and leaning my head

[041-003]upon my hand, I began to figure to myself the miseries of

[041-004]confinement. I was in a right frame for it, and so I gave

[041-005]full scope to my imagination. 

[041-006]I was going to begin with the millions of my fellow-creatures

[041-007]born to no inheritance but slavery: but finding, however

[041-008]affecting the picture was, that I could not bring it near me, and

[041-009]that the multitude of sad groups in it did but distract

[041-010]me.— 

[041-011]—I took a single captive, and having first shut

[041-012]him up in his dungeon, I then look’d through the twilight

[041-013]of his grated door to take his picture. 

[041-014]I beheld his body half-wasted away with long expectation and

[041-015]confinement, and felt what kind of sickness of the heart it was

[041-016]which arises from hope deferr’d. Upon looking nearer

[041-017]I saw him pale and feverish: in thirty years the western breeze

[041-018]had not once fann’d his blood;—he had seen no sun, no

[041-019]moon, in all that time—nor had the voice of friend or

[041-020]kinsman breathed through his lattice.—His

[041-021]children— 

[041-022]But here my heart began to bleed—and I was forced to go

[041-023]on with another part of the portrait. 

[041-024]He was sitting upon the ground upon a little straw, in the

[041-025]furthest corner of his dungeon, which was alternately his chair

[041-026]and bed: a little calendar of small sticks were laid at the head,

[041-027]notch’d all over with the dismal days and nights he had

[041-028]passed there;—he had one of these little sticks in his

[041-029]hand, and, with a rusty nail he was etching another day of misery

[041-030]to add to the heap. As I darkened the little light he had,

[041-031]he lifted up a hopeless eye towards the door, then cast it

[041-032]down,—shook his head, and went on with his work of

[041-033]affliction. I heard his chains upon his legs, as he turned

[041-034]his body to lay his little stick upon the bundle.—He gave a

[041-035]deep sigh.—I saw the iron enter into his soul!—I

[041-036]burst into tears.—I could not sustain the picture of

[041-037]confinement which my fancy had drawn.—I started up from my

[041-038]chair, and calling La Fleur: I bid him bespeak me a remise, and

[041-039]have it ready at the door of the hotel by nine in the

[041-040]morning. 

[041-041]I’ll go directly, said I, myself to Monsieur le Duc de

[041-042]Choiseul. 

[041-043]La Fleur would have put me to bed; but—not willing he

[041-044]should see anything upon my cheek which would cost the honest

[041-045]fellow a heart-ache,—I told him I would go to bed by

[041-046]myself,—and bid him go do the same.
