## PARIS.

[059-001]When La Fleur came up close to the

[059-002]table, and was made to comprehend what I wanted, he told me there

[059-003]were only two other sheets of it, which he had wrapped round the

[059-004]stalks of a bouquet to keep it together, which he had presented

[059-005]to the demoiselle upon the boulevards.—Then prithee, La

[059-006]Fleur, said I, step back to her to the Count de B—’s

[059-007]hotel, and see if thou canst get it.—There is no doubt of

[059-008]it, said La Fleur;—and away he flew. 

[059-009]In a very little time the poor fellow came back quite out of

[059-010]breath, with deeper marks of disappointment in his looks

[059-011]than could arise from the simple irreparability of the

[059-012]fragment. Juste Ciel ! in less than two minutes that

[059-013]the poor fellow had taken his last tender farewell of

[059-014]her—his faithless mistress had given his gage

[059-015]d’amour to one of the Count’s footmen,—the

[059-016]footman to a young sempstress,—and the sempstress to a

[059-017]fiddler, with my fragment at the end of it.—Our misfortunes

[059-018]were involved together:—I gave a sigh,—and La Fleur

[059-019]echoed it back again to my ear. 

[059-020]—How perfidious! cried La Fleur.—How unlucky! said

[059-021]I. 

[059-022]—I should not have been mortified, Monsieur, quoth La

[059-023]Fleur, if she had lost it.—Nor I, La Fleur, said I, had I

[059-024]found it. 

[059-025]Whether I did or no will be seen hereafter.
