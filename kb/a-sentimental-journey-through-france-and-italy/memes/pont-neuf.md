# Pont Neuf

The **Pont Neuf** (the "New Bridge") is the oldest standing bridge across the Seine in [[paris|Paris]], referenced in Laurence Sterne's *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]]* (1768) as the setting for one of [[yorick|Yorick]]'s characteristic chance encounters. Despite its name, by the 18th century the Pont Neuf was already over a hundred years old — a bustling thoroughfare where all classes of [[paris|Paris]]ian society mingled.

## 在小说中的角色 (Role in the Novel)

The Pont Neuf appears in the [[paris|Paris]] chapters as a space of urban encounter — a place where [[yorick|Yorick]]'s path crosses with strangers in ways that test and reveal his sentimental disposition. The bridge functions in Sterne's narrative geography as:

**A Theater of Chance**: Like the streets and shops of [[paris|Paris]], the Pont Neuf is a stage where [[yorick|Yorick]] is thrown together with unknown figures. The bridge's crowded, public nature makes it the antithesis of the private parlors and hotel rooms where other sentimental exchanges unfold — here, feeling must navigate the press of the crowd.

**A Threshold**: Bridges in Sterne's novel often mark transitions — between [[england|England]] and [[france|France]], between one emotional state and another. The Pont Neuf, spanning the Seine at the heart of [[paris|Paris]], represents a literal and figurative crossing point in [[yorick|Yorick]]'s [[paris|Paris]]ian wanderings.

## 18 世纪背景 (18th-Century Context)

In Sterne's time, the Pont Neuf was famous throughout Europe as the liveliest bridge in the world — lined with shops, thronged with street performers, pickpockets, and vendors. It was said that one could meet anyone on the Pont Neuf: a prince, a beggar, or a philosopher. This democratic chaos made it a natural setting for the kind of spontaneous, unmediated human contact that defines the sentimental journey.
