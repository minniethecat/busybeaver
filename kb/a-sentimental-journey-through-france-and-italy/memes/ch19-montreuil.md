## MONTREUIL.

[019-001]I am apt to be taken with all kinds

[019-002]of people at first sight; but never more so than when a poor

[019-003]devil comes to offer his service to so poor a devil as myself;

[019-004]and as I know this weakness, I always suffer my judgment to draw

[019-005]back something on that very account,—and this more or less,

[019-006]according to the mood I am in, and the case;—and I may add,

[019-007]the gender too, of the person I am to govern. 

[019-008]When La Fleur entered the room, after every discount I could

[019-009]make for my soul, the genuine look and air of the fellow

[019-010]determined the matter at once in his favour; so I hired him

[019-011]first,—and then began to enquire what he could do: But I

[019-012]shall find out his talents, quoth I, as I want

[019-013]them,—besides, a Frenchman can do every thing. 

[019-014]Now poor La Fleur could do nothing in the world but beat a

[019-015]drum, and play a march or two upon the fife. I was

[019-016]determined to make his talents do; and can’t say my

[019-017]weakness was ever so insulted by my wisdom as in the attempt. 

[019-018]La Fleur had set out early in life, as gallantly as most

[019-019]Frenchmen do, with serving for a few years; at the end of

[019-020]which, having satisfied the sentiment, and found, moreover, That

[019-021]the honour of beating a drum was likely to be its own reward, as

[019-022]it open’d no further track of glory to him,—he retired à ses terres , and lived comme il plaisoit à Dieu ;—that is to

[019-023]say, upon nothing. 

[019-024]—And so, quoth Wisdom, you have hired a drummer to

[019-025]attend you in this tour of yours through France and

[019-026]Italy!—Psha! said I, and do not one half of our gentry go

[019-027]with a humdrum compagnon du voyage the same round, and

[019-028]have the piper and the devil and all to pay besides? When

[019-029]man can extricate himself with an équivoque in such

[019-030]an unequal match,—he is not ill off.—But you can do

[019-031]something else, La Fleur? said I.— O qu’oui ! he

[019-032]could make spatterdashes, and play a little upon the

[019-033]fiddle.—Bravo! said Wisdom.—Why, I play a bass

[019-034]myself, said I;—we shall do very well. You can shave,

[019-035]and dress a wig a little, La Fleur?—He had all the

[019-036]dispositions in the world.—It is enough for heaven! said I,

[019-037]interrupting him,—and ought to be enough for me.—So,

[019-038]supper coming in, and having a frisky English spaniel on one side

[019-039]of my chair, and a French valet, with as much hilarity in his

[019-040]countenance as ever Nature painted in one, on the other,—I

[019-041]was satisfied to my heart’s content with my empire; and if

[019-042]monarchs knew what they would be at, they might be as satisfied

[019-043]as I was.
