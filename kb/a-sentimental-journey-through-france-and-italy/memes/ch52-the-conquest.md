## THE CONQUEST.

[052-001]Yes ,—and then—.

[052-002]Ye whose clay-cold heads and luke-warm hearts can argue down or

[052-003]mask your passions, tell me, what trespass is it that man should

[052-004]have them? or how his spirit stands answerable to the Father of

[052-005]spirits but for his conduct under them? 

[052-006]If Nature has so wove her web of kindness, that some threads

[052-007]of love and desire are entangled with the piece,—must the

[052-008]whole web be rent in drawing them out?—Whip me such stoics,

[052-009]great Governor of Nature! said I to myself:—wherever thy

[052-010]providence shall place me for the trials of my

[052-011]virtue;—whatever is my danger,—whatever is my

[052-012]situation,—let me feel the movements which rise out of it,

[052-013]and which belong to me as a man,—and, if I govern them

[052-014]as a good one, I will trust the issues to thy justice; for thou

[052-015]hast made us, and not we ourselves. 

[052-016]As I finished my address, I raised the fair fille de

[052-017]chambre up by the hand, and led her out of the

[052-018]room:—she stood by me till I locked the door and put the

[052-019]key in my pocket,—and then,—the victory being quite

[052-020]decisive—and not till then, I press’d my lips to her

[052-021]cheek, and taking her by the hand again, led her safe to the gate

[052-022]of the hotel.
