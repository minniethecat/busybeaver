## VERSAILLES.

[049-001]As the passport was directed to all

[049-002]lieutenant-governors, governors, and commandants of cities,

[049-003]generals of armies, justiciaries, and all officers of justice, to

[049-004]let Mr. Yorick the king’s jester, and his baggage, travel

[049-005]quietly along, I own the triumph of obtaining the passport was

[049-006]not a little tarnish’d by the figure I cut in it.—But

[049-007]there is nothing unmix’d in this world; and some of the

[049-008]gravest of our divines have carried it so far as to affirm, that

[049-009]enjoyment itself was attended even with a sigh,—and that

[049-010]the greatest they knew of terminated, in a general

[049-011]way , in little better than a convulsion. 

[049-012]I remember the grave and learned Bevoriskius, in his

[049-013]Commentary upon the Generations from Adam, very naturally breaks

[049-014]off in the middle of a note to give an account to the world of a

[049-015]couple of sparrows upon the out-edge of his window, which had

[049-016]incommoded him all the time he wrote, and at last had entirely

[049-017]taken him off from his genealogy. 

[049-018]—’Tis strange! writes Bevoriskius; but the facts

[049-019]are certain, for I have had the curiosity to mark them down one

[049-020]by one with my pen;—but the cock sparrow, during the little

[049-021]time that I could have finished the other half of this note,

[049-022]has actually interrupted me with the reiteration of his caresses

[049-023]three-and-twenty times and a half. 

[049-024]How merciful, adds Bevoriskius, is heaven to his

[049-025]creatures! 

[049-026]Ill fated Yorick! that the gravest of thy brethren should be

[049-027]able to write that to the world, which stains thy face with

[049-028]crimson to copy, even in thy study. 

[049-029]But this is nothing to my travels.—So I

[049-030]twice,—twice beg pardon for it.
