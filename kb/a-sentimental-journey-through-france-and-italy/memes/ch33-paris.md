## PARIS.

[033-001]I had counted twenty pulsations,

[033-002]and was going on fast towards the fortieth, when her husband,

[033-003]coming unexpected from a back parlour into the shop, put me a

[033-004]little out of my reckoning.—’Twas nobody but her

[033-005]husband, she said;—so I began a fresh score.—Monsieur

[033-006]is so good, quoth she, as he pass’d by us, as to give

[033-007]himself the trouble of feeling my pulse.—The husband took

[033-008]off his hat, and making me a bow, said, I did him too much

[033-009]honour—and having said that, he put on his hat

[033-010]and walk’d out. 

[033-011]Good God! said I to myself, as he went out,—and can this

[033-012]man be the husband of this woman! 

[033-013]Let it not torment the few who know what must have been the

[033-014]grounds of this exclamation, if I explain it to those who do

[033-015]not. 

[033-016]In London a shopkeeper and a shopkeeper’s wife seem to

[033-017]be one bone and one flesh: in the several endowments of mind and

[033-018]body, sometimes the one, sometimes the other has it, so as, in

[033-019]general, to be upon a par, and totally with each other as nearly

[033-020]as man and wife need to do. 

[033-021]In Paris, there are scarce two orders of beings more

[033-022]different: for the legislative and executive powers of the shop

[033-023]not resting in the husband, he seldom comes there:—in some

[033-024]dark and dismal room behind, he sits commerce-less, in his thrum

[033-025]nightcap, the same rough son of Nature that Nature left him. 

[033-026]The genius of a people, where nothing but the monarchy is salique , having ceded this department, with sundry others,

[033-027]totally to the women,—by a continual higgling with

[033-028]customers of all ranks and sizes from morning to night, like so

[033-029]many rough pebbles shook long together in a bag, by amicable

[033-030]collisions they have worn down their asperities and sharp angles,

[033-031]and not only become round and smooth, but will receive, some of

[033-032]them, a polish like a brilliant:—Monsieur le Mari is

[033-033]little better than the stone under your foot. 

[033-034]—Surely,—surely, man! it is not good for thee to

[033-035]sit alone:—thou wast made for social intercourse and gentle

[033-036]greetings; and this improvement of our natures from it I appeal

[033-037]to as my evidence. 

[033-038]—And how does it beat, Monsieur? said she.—With

[033-039]all the benignity, said I, looking quietly in her eyes, that I

[033-040]expected.—She was going to say something civil in

[033-041]return—but the lad came into the shop with the

[033-042]gloves.— Apropos , said I, I want a couple of

[033-043]pairs myself.
