## CALAIS.

[015-001]C’est bien

[015-002]comique , ’tis very droll, said the lady, smiling, from

[015-003]the reflection that this was the second time we had been left

[015-004]together by a parcel of nonsensical

[015-005]contingencies,— c’est bien comique , said

[015-006]she.— 

[015-007]—There wants nothing, said I, to make it so but the

[015-008]comic use which the gallantry of a Frenchman would put it

[015-009]to,—to make love the first moment, and an offer of his

[015-010]person the second. 

[015-011]’Tis their fort , replied the lady. 

[015-012]It is supposed so at least;—and how it has come to pass,

[015-013]continued I, I know not; but they have certainly got the credit

[015-014]of understanding more of love, and making it better than any

[015-015]other nation upon earth; but, for my own part, I think them

[015-016]arrant bunglers, and in truth the worst set of marksmen that ever

[015-017]tried Cupid’s patience. 

[015-018]—To think of making love by sentiments ! 

[015-019]I should as soon think of making a genteel suit of clothes out

[015-020]of remnants:—and to do it—pop—at first sight,

[015-021]by declaration—is submitting the offer, and themselves with

[015-022]it, to be sifted with all their pours and contres ,

[015-023]by an unheated mind. 

[015-024]The lady attended as if she expected I should go on. 

[015-025]Consider then, Madame, continued I, laying my hand upon

[015-026]hers:— 

[015-027]That grave people hate love for the name’s

[015-028]sake;— 

[015-029]That selfish people hate it for their own;— 

[015-030]Hypocrites for heaven’s;— 

[015-031]And that all of us, both old and young, being ten times worse

[015-032]frightened than hurt by the very report ,—what a want

[015-033]of knowledge in this branch of commerce a man betrays, whoever

[015-034]lets the word come out of his lips, till an hour or two, at

[015-035]least, after the time that his silence upon it becomes

[015-036]tormenting. A course of small, quiet attentions, not so

[015-037]pointed as to alarm,—nor so vague as to be

[015-038]misunderstood—with now and then a look of kindness, and

[015-039]little or nothing said upon it,—leaves nature for your

[015-040]mistress, and she fashions it to her mind.— 

[015-041]Then I solemnly declare, said the lady, blushing, you have

[015-042]been making love to me all this while.
