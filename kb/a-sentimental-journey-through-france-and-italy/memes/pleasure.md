# Pleasure

Pleasure in *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]]* is rarely simple. Sterne consistently treats pleasure as a threshold — the immediate, sensory delight that opens the door to more complex sentimental states. "The pleasure of the experiment has kept my senses awake," [[yorick|Yorick]] remarks, and the phrase captures the dynamic: pleasure quickens perception, and from quickened perception sentiment flows.

## 定义与历史语境

The eighteenth century inherited from Locke and the empiricists the idea that pleasure, along with pain, is the primary spring of human action. For the sentimental philosophers — Shaftesbury, Hutcheson, Hume — pleasure was not merely sensation but the affective signal of moral good. To take pleasure in benevolence was to feel virtue confirmed. This revaluation of pleasure from Epicurean indulgence to moral barometer gave sentimental writers a language in which enjoyment and ethics could be discussed in the same breath.

## 在 Sterne 小说中的表现

Sterne deploys pleasure across the novel's spectrum of experiences. [[yorick|Yorick]] takes "pleasure" in dinner with a stranger, in the translation of a compliment, in the sight of a pretty face — pleasures that are by turns social, aesthetic, gastronomic, and erotic. Yet Sterne never lets pleasure settle into simple hedonism. A pleasure interrupted becomes a sentimental wound. A pleasure shared becomes the foundation of fellowship.

The [[paris|Paris]] chapters intensify this pattern. The city offers [[yorick|Yorick]] a density of pleasures — theatrical, conversational, culinary — and each becomes a test of whether pleasure can be converted into something finer: sympathy, understanding, connection.

## 关键段落

> "enough;—the pleasure of the experiment has kept my senses awake, and made the observation of what passed in the course of it of use to me." (The Preface chapter)

> "the translation gave me more pleasure than any one I had the honour to make." (The Translation chapter)

> "It would be wicked to withhold a pleasure from the good." (The Supper chapter)

> "gave me half the pleasure." (The Remise Door chapter)
