# Friend

Friendship in *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]]* is the ideal form of human relation — voluntary, affectionate, and free from the compulsions of blood or interest. Sterne presents friendship as the sentimental alternative both to the contractual relations of commerce and the hierarchical bonds of patronage. When [[yorick|Yorick]] calls his servant "friend" and when the Chevalier speaks of "a friend he had once given up," the word carries the full weight of sentimental morality.

## 定义与历史语境

Classical friendship — the Ciceronian ideal of *amicitia* — was a relation of virtue between equals. The eighteenth century inherited this ideal while expanding its scope: Enlightenment thinkers imagined friendship as a bridge across rank and nation, and sentimental literature made friendship's emotional texture its subject. Sterne's treatment belongs to this sentimental reimagining, where friendship is less a formal bond than a quality of feeling that can arise spontaneously between strangers.

## 在 Sterne 小说中的表现

Friendship appears throughout the novel as both a named ideal and a lived practice. The most extended treatment comes in the Chevalier episode, where a former soldier reduced to selling pâtés speaks of a friend who might have helped him — "a friend he had once given up." The pathos of lost friendship becomes a vehicle for sentimental reflection on loyalty, pride, and human interdependence.

[[yorick|Yorick]] himself models friendship across social boundaries. He treats [[la-fleur|La Fleur]] not as a servant but as a companion: "I was unto him as a friend." The supper scene at the peasant's house near the novel's end presents friendship as a domestic virtue, woven into the fabric of daily life — the old man who "made it a rule" to dance with his family after supper embodies friendship as a practice rather than a sentiment alone.

## 关键段落

> "such a cordial situation as Friendship herself could scarce have contrived." (The Snuffbox episode)

> "Friendship and Virtue met together, and kiss'd each other." (The Grace episode)

> "I was unto him as a friend." (Yorick speaking of a traveling companion)

> "Thou hast one comfort, friend, said I, at least, in the loss." (The Dead Ass episode)

> "a friend he had once given up — he look'd attentively at me." (The Chevalier episode)

> "knowing I should meet with a friend in his apartment." (Paris chapters)

> "the voice of friend or kinsman." (The Captive episode)
