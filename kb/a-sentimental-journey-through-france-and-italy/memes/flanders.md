# Flanders

**Flanders** is the historical region spanning parts of modern-day Belgium, [[france|France]], and the Netherlands, referenced in Laurence Sterne's *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]]* (1768) as a transit corridor on the alternative route from [[italy|Italy]] back to [[england|England]].

## 在小说中的角色 (Role in the Novel)

Flanders appears in a single, telling passage: [[yorick|Yorick]], contemplating his return journey, considers "'tis only returning from [[italy|Italy]] through [[germany|Germany]] to Holland, by the route of Flanders, home" (Ch. 66, The [[bourbonnois|Bourbonnois]]). The mention is brief but suggestive — Flanders represents the northern alternative to the French route, a landscape of passage rather than destination.

The detail underscores the novel's geographical consciousness. Every region traversed — [[france|France]], [[savoy|Savoy]], [[italy|Italy]], [[germany|Germany]], Flanders — carries associations and expectations. Flanders, flat and practical, is the antithesis of the dramatic Alpine landscapes that precede it — a landscape of return rather than discovery, of the journey's closing rather than its opening.
