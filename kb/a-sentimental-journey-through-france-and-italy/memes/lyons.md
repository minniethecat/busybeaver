# Lyons

**Lyons** (Lyon) appears in Laurence Sterne's *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]]* (1768) as a geographical waypoint on the road from [[france|France]] to [[italy|Italy]] — the city after which the landscape changes and the traveler's pace must slow.

## 在小说中的角色 (Role in the Novel)

Lyons is mentioned in the [[moulines|Moulines]] chapters as the threshold of the Alpine crossing. [[yorick|Yorick]] reflects that when "you have gained the top of [[mount-taurira|Mount Taurira]], you run presently down to Lyons: — adieu, then, to all rapid movements!" (Ch. 64, [[maria-of-moulines|Maria]]). The city marks a transition in the geography of the journey — and, implicitly, in its emotional tempo.

The descent into Lyons signals the end of the mountainous terrain and the beginning of a more measured landscape. For the sentimental traveler, this slowing down is welcome: "'Tis a journey of caution; and it fares better with sentiments, not to be in a hurry with them" (Ch. 64, [[maria-of-moulines|Maria]]). Lyons thus embodies a principle of sentimental travel — that feeling, unlike the Grand Tourist's checklist, cannot be hurried.
