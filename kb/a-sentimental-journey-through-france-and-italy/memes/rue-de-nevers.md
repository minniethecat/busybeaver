# Rue de Nevers

The **Rue de Nevers** is a small [[paris|Paris]] street near the [[pont-neuf|Pont Neuf]], immortalized in Laurence Sterne's *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]]* (1768) as the setting for a moment of suspended farewell. It is here — "at the corner of the Rue de Nevers" (Ch. 42, [[the-address|The Address]]) — that a parting glance says more than words ever could.

## 在小说中的角色 (Role in the Novel)

The Rue de Nevers is the stage for one of Sterne's characteristic vignettes of unspoken feeling. After an encounter in the streets of [[paris|Paris]], [[yorick|Yorick]] and his companion reach the corner of the Rue de Nevers, "where we were to part" (Ch. 42, [[the-address|The Address]]). The street corner becomes, in Sterne's hands, a theater of hesitation — a pause in which the pressure of an unexpressed sentiment fills the space between two people.

Like the [[rue-de-gueneguault|Rue de Gueneguault]] — another [[paris|Paris]] street where [[yorick|Yorick]] bids farewell — the Rue de Nevers demonstrates Sterne's practice of anchoring emotional climaxes in named, real locations. The specificity of the street name gives the ephemeral moment a permanent address.
