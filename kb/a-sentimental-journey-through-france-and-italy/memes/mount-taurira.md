# Mount Taurira

**Mount Taurira** (*Mont Taurira*), a mountain on the road from [[moulines|Moulines]] to [[lyons|Lyons]], appears in Laurence Sterne's *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]]* (1768) as a geographical waypoint that marks [[yorick|Yorick]]'s transition from the rapid movements of [[france|France]] into the slow, contemplative journey through [[savoy|Savoy]] toward [[turin|Turin]].

## 在小说中的角色 (Role in the Novel)

Mount Taurira makes two appearances, both in the novel's closing chapters as [[yorick|Yorick]] presses toward [[italy|Italy]]. The first is in "[[the-supper|The Supper]]": "A shoe coming loose from the fore foot of the thill-horse, at the beginning of the ascent of mount Taurira, the postilion dismounted, twisted the shoe off, and put it in his pocket" (Ch. 67, [[the-supper|The Supper]]). When a second shoe is lost on the flinty road half a mile higher, [[yorick|Yorick]] is forced to get out and walk — the trivial mishap becoming the occasion for his discovery of a peasant cottage that will shape the evening's events.

The second mention, at the opening of "[[the-case-of-delicacy|The Case of Delicacy]]," frames the mountain as a threshold: "When you have gained the top of Mount Taurira, you run presently down to [[lyons|Lyons]]: — adieu, then, to all rapid movements!" (Ch. 69, [[the-case-of-delicacy|The Case of Delicacy]]). The descent from the mountain marks the end of the swift post-chaise travel that has carried [[yorick|Yorick]] across [[france|France]], and the beginning of the slower voiturin journey — "a journey of caution; and it fares better with sentiments, not to be in a hurry with them."

Mount Taurira thus functions as a geographical pivot in the novel's closing movement. On its ascent, the mundane world of horseshoe nails and postilions still governs; from its summit onward, [[yorick|Yorick]] enters the contemplative space of [[savoy|Savoy]], where the landscape itself seems to conspire with sentiment. The mountain is the last named geographical feature before the novel dissolves into the intimate interiors — the inn at [[lyons|Lyons]], the shared bedchamber — that frame its famously unresolved ending.
