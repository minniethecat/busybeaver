# Spain

**Spain** is referenced in Laurence Sterne's *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]]* (1768) through the story of a grieving mourner encountered at [[nampont|Nampont]] — a man whose journey to and from Spain frames one of the novel's most poignant set-pieces.

## 在小说中的角色 (Role in the Novel)

Spain enters the narrative indirectly, through the voice of the poor mourner whose ass has died at [[nampont|Nampont]]. The man, described as old and travel-worn, tells the assembled crowd that he "had come last from Spain, where he had been from the furthest borders of [[germany|Franconia]]" (Ch. 27, [[the-dead-ass|The Dead Ass]]). The geography is significant: the mourner has crossed half of Europe on foot, from his home in the German borderlands to Santiago de Compostela and back — a pilgrimage of immense personal cost, undertaken in fulfilment of a vow.

That vow, the mourner explains, was made when his three sons — "the finest lads in [[germany|Germany]]" — were stricken by smallpox. Having lost two, he promised heaven that if the youngest were spared, he "would go in gratitude to St. Iago in Spain" (Ch. 27, [[the-dead-ass|The Dead Ass]]). St. Iago — Santiago de Compostela — was one of the three great pilgrimage destinations of medieval Christendom, and the mourner's journey to it represents a devotional act of the humblest kind: the vow of a poor man who has nothing else to offer.

Spain thus functions in the novel as a symbol of completed pilgrimage — a distant land reached and returned from, in contrast to [[italy|Italy]], which [[yorick|Yorick]] never reaches. The mourner has actually been to Spain; his journey is finished, while [[yorick|Yorick]]'s remains perpetually unfinished. The mourner's Spain is the Spain of faith and sacrifice, not of tourism or sentiment — a geography of the soul rather than the itinerary.

It is also worth noting that [[smelfungus|the learned Smelfungus]] (Sterne's caricature of Tobias Smollett) "travelled from [[boulogne|Boulogne]] to [[paris|Paris]], from [[paris|Paris]] to [[rome|Rome]], and so on" — a route that implicitly passes through Spain, among other destinations. [[smelfungus|Smelfungus]]'s jaundiced travels form a satirical counterpoint to the mourner's sincere pilgrimage: both men cross the same European geography, but one moves in bitterness, the other in gratitude.
