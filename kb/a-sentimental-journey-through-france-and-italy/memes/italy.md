# Italy

**Italy** is the geographical destination that gives Laurence Sterne's *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]]* (1768) its full title — *Through [[france|France]] and Italy* — yet it remains perpetually beyond reach. The novel concludes before [[yorick|Yorick]] crosses the Alps, making Italy the great absent presence of the work: a promised land of sentiment, art, and classical association that hovers on the narrative horizon.

## 在小说中的角色 (Role in the Novel)

Italy serves the novel not as a setting but as a structuring absence:

**The Unattained Destination**: From the opening chapters, [[yorick|Yorick]] frames his travels as "a [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]] Through [[france|France]] and Italy" (Ch. 1, [[calais|Calais]]). [[la-fleur|La Fleur]] is engaged to accompany him "the whole tour of [[france|France]] and Italy" (Ch. 24, The Bidet). Yet the narrative never arrives — a structural decision that makes [[france|France]], not Italy, the true theater of sentiment.

**[[travel|The Grand Tour]] Tradition**: Italy in the 18th century was the essential terminus of the Grand Tour — the educational journey undertaken by young English gentlemen. [[yorick|Yorick]]'s failure to reach Italy subverts this tradition. He is not a Grand Tourist collecting antiquities and classical landscapes, but a sentimental traveler for whom the journey's emotional encounters matter more than its geographical completion.

**[[smelfungus|Smelfungus]]'s Italy**: Sterne provides a satirical glimpse of Italy through the character of [[mundungus|Smelfungus]] (a caricature of Tobias Smollett), who "travelled from [[boulogne|Boulogne]] to [[paris|Paris]], — from [[paris|Paris]] to [[rome|Rome]], — and so on" (Ch. 2, [[calais|Calais]]), finding fault at every stop. [[smelfungus|Smelfungus]]'s sour Italy — "from [[rome|Rome]] to [[naples|Naples]], — from [[naples|Naples]] to [[venice|Venice]]" — stands as a warning of how *not* to travel, and by implication, how *not* to reach Italy.

## 象征意义 (Symbolic Significance)

Italy represents, for Sterne's novel, the tension between destination and digression. The sentimental journey values the unexpected encounter, the spontaneous feeling, the roadside interruption — all of which delay the arrival in Italy. The longer [[yorick|Yorick]] lingers in [[france|France]], the more Italy recedes. This is not failure but method: the journey's meaning lies in its digressions, not its destination.
