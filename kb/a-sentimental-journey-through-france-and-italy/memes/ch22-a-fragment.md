## A FRAGMENT.

[022-001]— The town of Abdera,

[022-002]notwithstanding Democritus lived there, trying all the powers of

[022-003]irony and laughter to reclaim it, was the vilest and most

[022-004]profligate town in all Thrace. What for poisons,

[022-005]conspiracies, and assassinations,—libels, pasquinades, and

[022-006]tumults, there was no going there by day—’twas worse

[022-007]by night. 

[022-008]Now, when things were at the worst, it came to pass that the

[022-009]Andromeda of Euripides being represented at Abdera, the whole

[022-010]orchestra was delighted with it: but of all the passages which

[022-011]delighted them, nothing operated more upon their imaginations

[022-012]than the tender strokes of nature which the poet had wrought up

[022-013]in that pathetic speech of Perseus, O Cupid , prince of

[022-014]gods and men ! &c. Every man almost spoke pure

[022-015]iambics the next day, and talked of nothing but Perseus his

[022-016]pathetic address,—“ O Cupid! prince of gods and

[022-017]men !”—in every street of Abdera, in every house,

[022-018]“O Cupid! Cupid!”—in every mouth, like

[022-019]the natural notes of some sweet melody which drop from it,

[022-020]whether it will or no,—nothing but “Cupid! Cupid!

[022-021]prince of gods and men!”—The fire caught—and

[022-022]the whole city, like the heart of one man, open’d itself to

[022-023]Love. 

[022-024]No pharmacopolist could sell one grain of hellebore,—not

[022-025]a single armourer had a heart to forge one instrument of

[022-026]death;—Friendship and Virtue met together, and kiss’d

[022-027]each other in the street; the golden age returned, and hung over

[022-028]the town of Abdera—every Abderite took his eaten pipe, and

[022-029]every Abderitish woman left her purple web, and chastely sat her

[022-030]down and listened to the song. 

[022-031]’Twas only in the power, says the Fragment, of the God

[022-032]whose empire extendeth from heaven to earth, and even to the

[022-033]depths of the sea, to have done this.
