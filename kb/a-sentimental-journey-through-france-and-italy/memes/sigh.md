# Sigh

The sigh in *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]]* is the body's involuntary testimony — a moment when feeling overflows the vessel of polite speech. Sterne uses the sigh as a narrative punctuation mark, a hinge between the spoken and the unspoken that reveals what characters cannot or will not say. Five distinct sighs thread through the novel, each marking a turning point in [[yorick|Yorick]]'s sentimental encounters.

## 定义与历史语境

In eighteenth-century sensibility culture, the sigh occupied a privileged position among the "language of the heart." Unlike words, which could dissemble, the sigh was held to be involuntary — a direct expression of the soul. Locke's empiricism had taught that all knowledge came through the senses; sentimental philosophers like Shaftesbury and Hutcheson extended this to moral feeling, making the sigh a physiological proof of benevolence. The stage melodrama of the period, from Steele's *The Conscious [[love|Love]]rs* onward, made the sigh a dramatic convention — but Sterne characteristically complicates it with irony and self-awareness.

## 在 Sterne 小说中的表现

Sterne deploys the sigh across the full emotional range of the novel: grief, love, embarrassment, and even philosophical reflection. When the old man in the dead ass episode "gave a sigh" after arranging his crust of bread on the bridle, the simplicity of the gesture draws a crowd — the sigh becomes a public performance of private grief, and [[yorick|Yorick]] watching from his post-chaise is the spectator of a sentimental tableau. The girl in the [[rue-de-nevers|Rue de Nevers]] who "gave a low sigh" at [[yorick|Yorick]]'s gallantry marks the limit of what can be said between strangers. The prisoner's "deep sigh" in the [[the-bastile|Bastile]] chapter is the novel's darkest — "I saw the iron enter into his soul," [[yorick|Yorick]] writes, and the sigh becomes the sound of liberty crushed. Even Sterne's philosophical digression on enjoyment remarks that "enjoyment itself was attended even with a sigh," suggesting that all pleasure carries its own dissolution within it. When [[la-fleur|La Fleur]] "echoed" [[yorick|Yorick]]'s sigh at the end of The Fragment, the shared exhalation becomes a moment of comic solidarity in mutual misfortune.

## 关键段落

> "He held it some time in his hand,—then laid it upon the bit of his ass's bridle,—looked wistfully at the little arrangement he had made—and then gave a sigh. The simplicity of his grief drew numbers about him." (The Dead Ass)

> "She gave a low sigh:—I found I was not empowered to enquire at all after it." (The Fille de Chambre, Paris)

> "He gave a deep sigh.—I saw the iron enter into his soul!—I burst into tears." (The Captive, Paris)

> "Enjoyment itself was attended even with a sigh,—and that the greatest they knew of terminated, in a general way, in little better than a convulsion." (The Fragment, Paris)
