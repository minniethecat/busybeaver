## THE POSTILION.

[026-001]The concern which the poor

[026-002]fellow’s story threw me into required some attention; the

[026-003]postilion paid not the least to it, but set off upon the pavé in a full gallop. 

[026-004]The thirstiest soul in the most sandy desert of Arabia could

[026-005]not have wished more for a cup of cold water, than mine did for

[026-006]grave and quiet movements; and I should have had an high opinion

[026-007]of the postilion had he but stolen off with me in something like

[026-008]a pensive pace.—On the contrary, as the mourner finished

[026-009]his lamentation, the fellow gave an unfeeling lash to each of his

[026-010]beasts, and set off clattering like a thousand devils. 

[026-011]I called to him as loud as I could, for heaven’s sake to

[026-012]go slower:—and the louder I called, the more unmercifully

[026-013]he galloped.—The deuce take him and his galloping

[026-014]too—said I,—he’ll go on tearing my nerves to

[026-015]pieces till he has worked me into a foolish passion, and then

[026-016]he’ll go slow that I may enjoy the sweets of it. 

[026-017]The postilion managed the point to a miracle: by the time he

[026-018]had got to the foot of a steep hill, about half a league from

[026-019]Nampont,—he had put me out of temper with him,—and

[026-020]then with myself, for being so. 

[026-021]My case then required a different treatment; and a good

[026-022]rattling gallop would have been of real service to me.— 

[026-023]—Then, prithee, get on—get on, my good lad, said

[026-024]I. 

[026-025]The postilion pointed to the hill.—I then tried to

[026-026]return back to the story of the poor German and his ass—but

[026-027]I had broke the clue,—and could no more get into it again,

[026-028]than the postilion could into a trot. 

[026-029]—The deuce go, said I, with it all! Here am I

[026-030]sitting as candidly disposed to make the best of the worst, as

[026-031]ever wight was, and all runs counter. 

[026-032]There is one sweet lenitive at least for evils, which Nature

[026-033]holds out to us: so I took it kindly at her hands, and fell

[026-034]asleep; and the first word which roused me was Amiens . 

[026-035]—Bless me! said I, rubbing my eyes,—this is the

[026-036]very town where my poor lady is to come.
