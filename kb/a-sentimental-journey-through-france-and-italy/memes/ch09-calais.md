## CALAIS.

[009-001]When I told the reader that I did

[009-002]not care to get out of the désobligeant , because I

[009-003]saw the monk in close conference with a lady just arrived at the

[009-004]inn—I told him the truth,—but I did not tell him the

[009-005]whole truth; for I was as full as much restrained by the

[009-006]appearance and figure of the lady he was talking to.

[009-007]Suspicion crossed my brain and said, he was telling her what had

[009-008]passed: something jarred upon it within me,—I wished him at

[009-009]his convent. 

[009-010]When the heart flies out before the understanding, it saves

[009-011]the judgment a world of pains.—I was certain she was of a

[009-012]better order of beings;—however, I thought no more of her,

[009-013]but went on and wrote my preface. 

[009-014]The impression returned upon my encounter with her in the

[009-015]street; a guarded frankness with which she gave me her hand,

[009-016]showed, I thought, her good education and her good sense; and as

[009-017]I led her on, I felt a pleasurable ductility about her, which

[009-018]spread a calmness over all my spirits— 

[009-019]—Good God! how a man might lead such a creature as this

[009-020]round the world with him!— 

[009-021]I had not yet seen her face—’twas not material:

[009-022]for the drawing was instantly set about, and long before we had

[009-023]got to the door of the Remise, Fancy had finished the

[009-024]whole head, and pleased herself as much with its fitting her

[009-025]goddess, as if she had dived into the Tiber for it;—but

[009-026]thou art a seduced, and a seducing slut; and albeit thou cheatest

[009-027]us seven times a day with thy pictures and images, yet with so

[009-028]many charms dost thou do it, and thou deckest out thy pictures in

[009-029]the shapes of so many angels of light, ’tis a shame to

[009-030]break with thee. 

[009-031]When we had got to the door of the Remise, she withdrew her hand

[009-032]from across her forehead, and let me see the original:—it

[009-033]was a face of about six-and-twenty,—of a clear transparent

[009-034]brown, simply set off without rouge or powder;—it was not

[009-035]critically handsome, but there was that in it, which, in the

[009-036]frame of mind I was in, attached me much more to it,—it was

[009-037]interesting: I fancied it wore the characters of a widow’d

[009-038]look, and in that state of its declension, which had passed the

[009-039]two first paroxysms of sorrow, and was quietly beginning to

[009-040]reconcile itself to its loss;—but a thousand other

[009-041]distresses might have traced the same lines; I wish’d to

[009-042]know what they had been—and was ready to inquire, (had the

[009-043]same bon ton of conversation permitted, as in the days of

[009-044]Esdras)—“ What aileth thee ? and why art thou

[009-045]disquieted ? and why is thy understanding

[009-046]troubled ?”—In a word, I felt benevolence for her;

[009-047]and resolv’d some way or other to throw in my mite of

[009-048]courtesy,—if not of service. 

[009-049]Such were my temptations;—and in this disposition to

[009-050]give way to them, was I left alone with the lady with her hand in

[009-051]mine, and with our faces both turned closer to the door of the

[009-052]Remise than what was absolutely necessary.
