# The Coach-yard

The **Coach-yard** at [[calais|Calais]] is the setting for one of the most philosophically resonant opening episodes of Laurence Sterne's *A [[sentiment|Sentiment]]al [[travel|Journey]]* (1768). It is here that [[yorick|Yorick]], newly arrived in [[france|France]], surveys the available vehicles and selects the *desobligeant* — a choice that is simultaneously practical, whimsical, and emblematic of his entire approach to travel.

## 在小说中的角色 (Role in the Novel)

The coach-yard functions as a theater of choice and character:

**The Choice of the Desobligeant**: Faced with a yard full of vehicles, [[yorick|Yorick]] contemplates each — the chaise, the coach, the desobligeant — and settles on the latter, a carriage designed for a single passenger. "To be fit for a desobligeant," he reflects, "one must be a little unhappy." (Ch. 6, In the Desobligeant) The choice is at once practical (he travels alone) and philosophical (the desobligeant mirrors his solitary, reflective mode of journeying).

**The Stage for [[la-fleur|La Fleur]]**: The coach-yard is also where [[yorick|Yorick]] first encounters [[la-fleur|La Fleur]], the French servant who will accompany him through the remainder of the novel. The yard thus becomes the site of the novel's most enduring relationship — a bond forged in the casual, commercial space of a coaching inn.

## 象征意义 (Symbolic Significance)

The coach-yard at [[calais|Calais]] is the novel's first "stage" — in both the theatrical and the coaching sense. It is the space where [[yorick|Yorick]] transitions from passive arrival to active choice, from being transported to choosing his mode of transport. The vehicles arrayed before him represent different ways of traveling — and by extension, different ways of living and feeling.
