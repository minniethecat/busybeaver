## Overview

[[materialism|Philosophic materialism]] is the doctrine that matter is the fundamental reality, and that consciousness, ideas, and social institutions are products of material conditions. It forms a core component of Marxist theory and, by extension, Bolshevik ideology.

Bertrand Russell devoted a section of *The Practice and Theory of [[bolshevism|Bolshevism]]* (Part II, Chapter I) to examining the relationship between philosophic materialism and Bolshevik political theory. His analysis is notable for its philosophical rigor — Russell was himself a philosopher — and for his conclusion that the connection between the two is weaker than commonly assumed.

## Russell's Analysis

Russell argued that philosophic materialism is logically independent of the economic interpretation of history (historical materialism). He wrote:

> In particular, philosophic materialism does not prove that economic causes are fundamental in politics. [002-003]

He further observed:

> All political events have economic causes, even if philosophic materialism were false. Economic causes operate in the realm of mind, not of matter. [002-004]

Russell's central critique was that the [[bolshevik|Bolsheviks]] had inherited a set of philosophical doctrines from Marx — including materialism — that were "undesirable" as foundations for political theory:

> To base a political theory upon a philosophical doctrine is undesirable. The doctrine may be false, and the political theory may survive its falsification only through obstinacy. [002-007]

## Relationship to Bolshevik Doctrine

Russell identified philosophic materialism as one of several "dogmatic beliefs" that Bolsheviks held with religious fervor:

> ...dogmatic beliefs — such as philosophic materialism, for example — which may be true or false, but which are held with the same kind of unquestioning certainty as that of a Christian believing in the Apostles' Creed. [P01-007]

Despite his philosophical disagreements, Russell acknowledged that "there is no essential connection between Bolshevism and materialism in the philosophic sense." [002-002] The materialist philosophy served more as a legitimizing framework than as a practical guide for Bolshevik policy.
