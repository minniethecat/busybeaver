# French Revolution

The **French Revolution** (1789–1799) served as a frequent point of historical comparison in Bertrand Russell's analysis of [[bolshevism|Bolshevism]]. Russell compared the Bolsheviks variously to the Jacobins, the Directory, and other revolutionary French regimes, using these analogies to illuminate the dynamics of revolutionary power.

## Historical Parallels

Russell noted that the Bolsheviks were conscious of the French revolutionary precedent and sought to learn from it — particularly the lesson that revolution must be defended against both internal and external enemies. Yet he also argued that this consciousness led the Bolsheviks to repeat the French error of substituting terror for consent.
