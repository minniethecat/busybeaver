# Plato's Republic

**Plato's Republic** was invoked by Bertrand Russell in his critique of [[bolshevism|Bolshevism]] as an ironic point of comparison. Russell, a trained philosopher, noted that the Bolshevik claim to represent the "dictatorship of the proletariat" concealed a structure of power — rule by a self-selecting elite claiming superior knowledge — that bore more resemblance to Plato's guardian class than to democratic governance.

## Russell's Analogy

Russell argued that both Platonic and Bolshevik theory rested on the claim that a specially enlightened minority had the right — indeed, the duty — to govern the unenlightened majority, regardless of the majority's consent. This was, for Russell, the fundamental authoritarian temptation that both ancient and modern revolutionary thought had failed to resist.
