# White Army

The **White Army** was the loose coalition of counter-revolutionary military forces that opposed the [[bolshevik|Bolshevik]] Red Army during the [[russian-civil-war|Russian Civil War]] of 1918–1922. Russell observed both the destructive impact of White military operations and the political contradictions that ultimately doomed the White cause.

## Composition and Leadership

The White movement was a heterogeneous coalition united primarily by opposition to Bolshevism. It included monarchists seeking restoration of the Romanov dynasty, liberal constitutionalists who had supported the February Revolution, disaffected Socialists, and Cossack hosts defending traditional autonomies. Its principal commanders included General [[kolchak|Alexander Kolchak]] in Siberia, General [[denikin|Anton Denikin]] in South Russia, and General [[yudenich|Nikolai Yudenich]] in the Baltic region.

## Russell's Observations

Russell witnessed the effects of White Army operations during his journey down the Volga in 1920. The Donets Basin, a crucial coal-mining region, had been devastated by Denikin's forces before their retreat, leaving mines that were "still not in working order." (001-088) The resulting fuel crisis paralysed Russian industry and contributed to the desperate conditions Russell observed in Petrograd and Moscow.

Russell noted the strategic paradox of the White position: their reliance on [[entente|Entente]] support provided military resources but undermined their nationalist credentials. The Bolsheviks successfully portrayed the Whites as puppets of foreign imperialism — a characterisation that resonated with a population weary of foreign intervention.

## Russell's Assessment

Russell did not romanticise the Whites — their association with the old regime and their often-brutal methods were plain. But he regarded the civil war's destructiveness as evidence against the Bolshevik claim that their method of violent revolution was a cost-effective path to socialism. "The evils of war, especially of civil war, are certain and very great; the gains to be achieved by victory are problematical," he wrote. (001-020)
