# Arthur Balfour

Foreign Secretary:A senior British statesman involved in the Allied response to the Russian Revolution — representing, in Russell's context, the conservative establishment's hostility to Bolshevism.

## Role in the Bolshevik Era

Russell's 1920 analysis of Bolshevism was written at a moment when the political landscape of Europe was being transformed by war, revolution, and counter-revolution. The figures who shaped these events — Bolsheviks, their opponents, and the international statesmen who responded to the Russian Revolution — appear in Russell's account as representatives of different political traditions and strategic calculations.

## Russell's Assessment

Russell's treatment of political figures in *The Practice and Theory of Bolshevism* reflected his broader method: recognising the sincerity and intelligence of those with whom he disagreed while insisting on the primacy of actual outcomes over theoretical commitments. His assessments were characteristically nuanced — neither hagiography nor demonisation, but an attempt to understand individuals as products of their circumstances and commitments.
