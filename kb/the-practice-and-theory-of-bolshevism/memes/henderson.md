Arthur Henderson was a British Labour Party leader and a prominent figure of the Second International. He served as Leader of the Labour Party on multiple occasions and held cabinet positions including Home Secretary and Foreign Secretary. In Bertrand Russell's *The Practice and Theory of [[bolshevism|Bolshevism]]*, Henderson appears as a symbol of the parliamentary socialist whom the [[bolshevik|Bolsheviks]] regarded with contempt.

## Lenin's View of Henderson

During Russell's conversation with Lenin in [[moscow|Moscow]] in 1920, Henderson was singled out by name as an example of the uselessness of parliamentary [[socialism|socialism]]. Lenin "thinks that, if Mr. Henderson, for instance, were to become Prime Minister, nothing of importance would be done; organized Labour would then, so he hopes and believes, turn to [[revolution|revolution]]" [001-025]. For Lenin, Henderson represented the reformist Labour leader whose inevitable failure to deliver meaningful change would radicalize the [[proletariat|working class]] and drive them toward revolution.

Russell, however, was sceptical of this analysis: "The reasons which make attempts at violent revolution seem to most of us both improbable and undesirable in this country carry no weight with him, and seem to him mere bourgeois prejudices. When I suggested that whatever is possible in England can be achieved without bloodshed, he waved aside the suggestion as fantastic" [001-025]. Russell added that he "got little impression of knowledge or psychological imagination as regards [[england|Great Britain]]" from Lenin [001-025].

## Russell and the British Labour Delegation

Russell travelled to [[russia|Russia]] in 1920 as part of the British Labour Delegation. The Bolsheviks assumed the delegation had "come to testify to the solidarity of British Labour with Russian [[communism|Communism]]" [001-012], and made extensive propaganda use of the visit. Russell's experience with the delegation informed his nuanced view of Labour figures like Henderson — sympathetic to working-class politics but deeply sceptical of Bolshevik methods.
