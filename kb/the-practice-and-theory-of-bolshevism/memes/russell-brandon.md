# Bertrand Russell

**Bertrand Arthur William Russell** (1872–1970) was a British philosopher, logician, mathematician, and social critic. His 1920 book *The Practice and Theory of Bolshevism* — the source text for this wiki — was based on a visit to Soviet Russia in the summer of 1920, where he met [[lenin|Lenin]], [[trotsky|Trotsky]], and other Bolshevik leaders.

## The 1920 Visit

Russell travelled to Russia as part of a Labour Party delegation. His account is notable for its combination of initial sympathy for socialist ideals with a growing horror at Bolshevik methods. He recorded his observations with the analytical precision of a trained philosopher, producing one of the earliest and most perceptive Western critiques of the Soviet system.

## Russell's Argument

Russell's central thesis was that Bolshevism, while drawing on genuine grievances and legitimate aspirations, had become a system of one-party dictatorship that betrayed the democratic promise of socialism. He distinguished between Bolshevik theory — which he regarded as intellectually serious if flawed — and Bolshevik practice, which he saw as tyrannical.
