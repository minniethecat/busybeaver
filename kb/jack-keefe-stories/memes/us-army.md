The **United States Army** enters Jack Keefe's life with the American entry into World War I in 1917. Drafted into service, Jack trades his White Sox uniform for a military one, his major league salary for $36 per month, and his baseball ambitions for the uncertainties of war.

## In the Stories

Jack's military service produces some of the letters' most revealing passages. The contrast between his baseball salary and Army pay is stark:

> "the salary is $36.00 per mo. instead of $2800.00 per yr." (015-033)

The uniform changes but Jack's character doesn't:

> "it will always be just plain Jack Keefe" (015-034)

> "Corp. Jack Keefe" (015-039)
