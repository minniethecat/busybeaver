**Tris "The Grey Eagle" Speaker** (1888–1958) was one of the greatest players of the dead-ball era — a .345 career hitter, brilliant defensive center fielder, and player-manager for the Cleveland Indians. In Jack Keefe's letters, Speaker appears in game action as part of the constellation of Hall of Fame talent Jack faces (and against whom his results are mixed).

## In the Stories

Speaker appears in baserunning and defensive contexts. In one key sequence:

> "him and Speaker pulled a double steal" (013-052)

The reference, though brief, places Speaker within the aggressive baserunning style that characterized American League baseball — and that gave Jack such trouble.

## Historical Note

Speaker's 792 career doubles remain the all-time record. He was a contemporary and rival of Ty Cobb, with a very different temperament — cerebral where Cobb was volcanic, respected where Cobb was feared. Between them, they defined outfield excellence in the 1910s.
