The **American League** was founded in 1901 by Ban Johnson as a competitor to the established National League. By Jack Keefe's era (1910s), the two leagues had achieved peaceful coexistence under the National Agreement, contesting the World Series annually. Jack's entire professional career — Bedford, Terre Haute, Chicago White Sox — exists within the American League's organizational pyramid.
