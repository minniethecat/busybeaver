The **Chicago White Sox** are a Major League Baseball team in the American League, owned in Jack Keefe's era by Charley Comiskey and playing at Comiskey Park. For Jack, the White Sox represent everything: his break into the major leagues, his professional identity, and the source of his greatest pride (and occasional frustration).

## In the Stories

Jack's relationship with the White Sox is the central professional relationship of the letters. He is sold to the team before the first letter:

> "I been sold to the White Sox" (001-002)

The White Sox uniform transforms his self-image:

> "I ain't a bad looking guy in the White Sox uniform Al" (001-032)

The team's fortunes — pennant races, double-headers, clubhouse politics — provide the narrative structure of his letters. Jack's constant assessment of whether he's being treated fairly by "the club" reveals both his pride and his insecurity.
