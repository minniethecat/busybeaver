**"You Know Me Al"** is Ring Lardner's 1916 collection of six short stories narrated by Jack Keefe, a fictional White Sox pitcher who writes letters to his hometown friend Al Blanchard. The stories first appeared in *The Saturday Evening Post* in 1914 and were collected into book form by George H. Doran Company. They are considered a landmark of American literary humor and a foundational work of baseball fiction.

## The Stories

The collection comprises six epistolary stories tracing Jack's career from rookie signing through brief demotion, return to the majors, military service in World War I, and beyond. Lardner's achievement is Jack's voice — a brilliantly sustained first-person narration that reveals more than the narrator knows.
