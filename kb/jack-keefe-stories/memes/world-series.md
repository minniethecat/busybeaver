The **World Series** is the best-of-seven championship series between the American and National League pennant winners. In Jack Keefe's letters, the World Series is the distant summit — a goal he references when justifying his career choices and sacrifices. Jack never reaches it in the letters, but the possibility shapes his decisions.

## In the Stories

The World Series appears in Jack's imagination as the justification for his contract demands and the reward for his loyalty:

> "a man that give up a big league salary and the world serious dough" (015-025)

Jack's use of "world serious" — his characteristic malapropism — captures both his ambition and his limitations.
