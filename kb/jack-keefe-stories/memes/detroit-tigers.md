The **Detroit Tigers** of the American League are the most vivid opposing team in Jack Keefe's letters. Led by Ty Cobb — the most feared hitter in baseball — the Tigers' lineup, baserunning, and relentless pressure represent the standard against which Jack measures himself. Their encounters with Jack produce the collection's most detailed and dramatic game sequences.

## In the Stories

Jack's outings against Detroit are set pieces. Cobb steals bases with abandon:

> "Cobb stole four and Bush and Crawford and Veach pulled a double steal" (001-125)

Jack simultaneously fears and craves the challenge:

> "if I do we will win the pennant sure because Detroit has got a rotten pitching staff" (004-006)

The Tigers are the American League's measuring stick, and Jack's results against them define his self-assessment.
