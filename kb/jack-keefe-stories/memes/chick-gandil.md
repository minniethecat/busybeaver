**Arnold "Chick" Gandil** (1888–1970) was the Chicago White Sox first baseman during Jack Keefe's tenure, and historically the ringleader of the 1919 Black Sox scandal. In the letters, Gandil appears as a teammate whose own struggles with management and contracts resonate with Jack's financial anxieties.

## In the Stories

Gandil surfaces in Jack's accounts of team politics and finances — the off-field realities that occupy as much of Jack's attention as the games themselves. Jack's awareness of Gandil's contract situation mirrors his own negotiations with Comiskey.

## Historical Note

Gandil is one of the most infamous figures in baseball history as the organizer of the 1919 World Series fix. The letters, set a few years before the scandal, capture a different side — the everyday professional ballplayer navigating contracts, playing time, and clubhouse politics, unaware of the infamy to come.
