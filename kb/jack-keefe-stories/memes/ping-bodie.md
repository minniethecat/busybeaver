Ping Bodie is an outfielder for the [[chicago-white-sox|Chicago White Sox]] who appears in [[jack-keefe|Jack Keefe's]] letters as one of the most vividly drawn members of the team's social circle — a player whose fielding lapses and sharp tongue make him a recurring character in Jack's baseball world.

## Biography

Bodie — born Frank Stephen Bodie, nicknamed "Ping" for the sound his bat supposedly made on contact — was an Italian-American outfielder who played for the White Sox from 1911 to 1914 before moving to the [[philadelphia-athletics|Philadelphia Athletics]] and later the [[new-york-yankees|New York Yankees]]. In Jack's letters, he is a spring training regular, part of the dining-room ensemble that includes [[kid-gleason|Kid Gleason]], [[eddie-collins|Eddie Collins]], and [[jack-fournier|Jack Fournier]].

## In the Stories

### The Dinner Table

Bodie is first introduced at the team hotel, where Jack reports: "Tonight at supper Bodie says How did I look today Kid? Gleason says Just like you always do in the spring. You looked like a cow" [001-044]. The exchange sets the tone for Bodie's role — the player who invites mockery and gives it back, part of the rough camaraderie that defines Jack's early experience of big-league life.

### Spring Training Mishaps

During Jack's first exhibition season, Bodie's fielding becomes a recurring source of frustration:

> "I have pitched four times but have not went over five innings yet. I worked against Oakland two times and against Frisco two times and only three runs have been scored off me. They should only ought to of had one but Bodie misjuged a easy fly ball in Frisco." [001-058]

In another game, "Bodie ought to of threw him out twenty feet" [001-133] on a sacrifice fly, and in yet another contest a reporter's scoring is dismissed by Jack with "They give Barry a hit on a fly ball that Bodie ought to of eat up, only he stumbled or something" [002-036]. Bodie is, in Jack's telling, the teammate whose mistakes make Jack's own performances look better by comparison.

### A Sick Arm

When the team faces [[walter-johnson|Walter Johnson]], Gleason teases the nervous hitters, noting that "Bodie is complaining of a sore arm which he must of strained drawing to two card flushes" [002-085] — a poker joke at the outfielder's expense that captures the clubhouse culture of relentless ribbing.

### Off-Season Appearance

Bodie reappears when Jack returns for a new season, with [[kid-gleason|Gleason]] joking about Jack's physique: "Wait till Bodie sees you and he will want to stick round you all the time because you make him look like a broom straw or something" [004-041], suggesting that Bodie was notably thin — a physical trait that became part of his clubhouse identity.
