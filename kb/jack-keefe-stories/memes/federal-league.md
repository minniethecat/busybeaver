The **Federal League** was an independent professional baseball league that operated as a self-declared "third major league" in 1914–1915. By offering higher salaries and respecting no reserve clause, it briefly disrupted the established baseball order. Jack references it in connection with his own contract negotiations and the Chi-Feds (Chicago Whales).

## Historical Context

The Federal League's challenge forced American and National League owners to raise salaries to retain players. Its collapse after the 1915 season restored the two-league monopoly — and the salary-depressing reserve system Jack labored under. The league's legacy includes Wrigley Field in Chicago, built for the Chi-Feds.
