**World War I** (1914–1918) enters Jack Keefe's letters with the American declaration of war in April 1917. The conflict transforms his life: the baseball diamond is replaced by the training camp, major league salary by Army pay, and the pennant race by the war effort.

## In the Stories

Jack's war experience is filtered through his characteristic voice — pride in his uniform, complaint about the pay, and self-aggrandizement mixed with moments of genuine reflection:

> "a man that give up a big league salary and the world serious dough to help Uncle Sam out" (015-025)

> "the salary is $36.00 per mo. instead of $2800.00 per yr." (015-033)
