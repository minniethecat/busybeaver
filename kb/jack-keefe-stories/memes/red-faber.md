Red Faber — born Urban Charles Faber — was a right-handed pitcher for the Chicago White Sox and a teammate of Jack Keefe's during his major league career. Jack records Faber's performances and personality across numerous letters, revealing a pitcher he both admires and competes with.

## Biography

Urban "Red" Faber pitched for the Chicago White Sox from 1914 through 1933, spending his entire major league career with one team. He was known as a spitball pitcher and would later be elected to the Baseball Hall of Fame. In Jack Keefe's telling, Faber is a consistent presence on the White Sox pitching staff, frequently mentioned alongside other pitchers like [[eddie-cicotte|Eddie Cicotte]].

Faber served in the United States Navy during World War I, stationed at the Great Lakes Naval Training Station [024-005]. This temporarily removed him from the White Sox roster during the war years.

## In the Stories

Faber appears throughout Jack's letters as a fellow pitcher and occasional rival. Jack describes Faber receiving "a good beating" in one game [005-117], and later notes that he himself was lifted from a game, with Faber coming in "to finnish it up" [006-037] — a typical pitching change that reveals their shared role on the staff.

Jack acknowledges Faber's talent, reporting that "everybody is saying what a whale Faber is" [014-087], using the era's slang for an outstanding player. When Jack and Faber both struggle in consecutive games, he notes the shared experience with characteristic candor: "the second game Faber was in the same boat" [018-113].

During the war, Faber's navy service is noted [024-005], and Jack later works out with "Faber and Wolfgang wile the rest of the boys is playing ball" [024-029] — suggesting Faber remained a training partner even while military obligations affected the roster.

Jack also mentions Faber in the context of international baseball talk: "when Cicotte and Faber and Russell begins to talk about the war" [014-005], placing Faber among the more thoughtful members of the team who discussed current events.
