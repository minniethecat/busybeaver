**Ringgold Wilmer "Ring" Lardner** (1885–1933) was an American sports columnist, short story writer, and humorist. His Jack Keefe stories, collected in "You Know Me Al" (1916), are his most famous work — a pioneering use of vernacular first-person narration that influenced Hemingway, Fitzgerald, and generations of American writers.

## Biography

Lardner began his career as a sports reporter in South Bend, Indiana, working his way through Chicago newspapers before landing at the *Chicago Tribune* and *The Saturday Evening Post*. His intimate knowledge of baseball — he covered the White Sox and traveled with the team — gave the Jack Keefe stories their authenticity. He later expanded into broader social satire, but the baseball stories remain his most enduring legacy.
