**Joe Benz** was a real-life pitcher for the Chicago White Sox (1911–1919), known for throwing a no-hitter in 1914. In Jack Keefe's letters, Benz occupies the fraught position of fellow pitcher — the man who warms up when Jack struggles, who replaces Jack when the manager loses patience, and with whom Jack competes for starts.

## In the Stories

Benz appears at the critical moment when Jack is pulled from a game:

> "Callahan called Benz to the bench and sent for me" (001-110)

The dynamic between Jack and Benz illustrates the precariousness of a rookie pitcher's position — every inning that goes to Benz is one that doesn't go to Jack. In the zero-sum economy of a pitching staff, Benz is Jack's most direct competitor for his manager's trust and his owner's money.
