Al Blanchard, often addressed as "Friend Al" or nicknamed "Little Al," is the silent recipient of Jack Keefe's letters throughout Ring Lardner's "You Know Me Al" stories. Though he never appears directly in the narrative, his presence shapes the entire epistolary collection — every letter Jack writes is addressed to him.

## Biography

Al Blanchard is a resident of Bedford, Indiana, Jack Keefe's hometown [001-129]. He appears to be one of Jack's closest friends from his pre-baseball days, maintaining their correspondence throughout Jack's professional career. Jack consistently addresses him with warmth and familiarity, signing off with "Your old pal" or "Your friend" [001-081] [001-096].

Physically, Al is described in less-than-flattering terms by others. Callahan, the White Sox manager, refers to him as a "homely little skate" [004-147], and another character comments on his crossed eyes [005-058]. Jack, however, defends his friend fiercely against such remarks.

## In the Stories

Al serves as the primary audience for Jack's letters, which begin with the salutation "Friend Al" [001-002]. The correspondence spans Jack's entire baseball career — from his first tryout with the White Sox through his honeymoon, military service in World War I, and eventual return to baseball.

Jack tells his future wife Florrie about Al early in their relationship, describing him as someone he has written about extensively [003-013]. This suggests Al is not just a casual acquaintance but a trusted confidant to whom Jack reveals his ambitions, frustrations, and personal life.

The one-sided nature of the letters (we never read Al's replies) creates a distinctive narrative voice — Jack's unfiltered, colloquial, and often self-unaware monologues addressed to his "old pal" [001-002].
