**Sam "Wahoo Sam" Crawford** (1880–1968) was a Hall of Fame outfielder for the Detroit Tigers and one of the greatest hitters of the dead-ball era. In Jack Keefe's letters, Crawford represents the ultimate test — an opposing hitter whose prowess at the plate Jack recounts with a mixture of awe and self-justification.

## In the Stories

Jack's encounters with Crawford showcase the narrator's habit of framing outcomes to his own advantage. When Crawford succeeds, it's luck or defensive failure:

> "Crawford got the luckiest three-base hit I ever see" (001-133)

When Jack prevails, it's skill:

> "I whiffed Crawford" (002-046)

Crawford also appears in baserunning accounts:

> "Cobb stole four and Bush and Crawford and Veach pulled a double steal" (001-125)

## Historical Note

Crawford held the career record for triples (309) and was known for his power in an era when home runs were rare. He and Ty Cobb formed one of baseball's greatest outfield duos in Detroit from 1905–1917.
