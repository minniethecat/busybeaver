Boston is a major American city and the home of the [[boston-red-sox|Boston Red Sox]], one of the most significant opponents in [[jack-keefe|Jack Keefe's]] pitching career. Jack's letters from Boston — dated September 12, September 24, and June 11 across multiple seasons — provide a vivid travelogue of the American League circuit and reveal his competitive relationship with "the Boston bunch."

## Overview

As one of the eight American League cities, Boston was a regular stop on the White Sox road schedule. The [[boston-red-sox|Red Sox]] were a powerhouse during the era of Jack's letters, featuring stars like [[tris-speaker|Tris Speaker]] (who would later join the White Sox) and a formidable pitching staff.

Jack's first mention of Boston comes during spring training contract negotiations, when he lists it among the cities he could play for: "I says St. Louis and Boston and New York" [001-044]. His manager retorts dismissively that "St. Louis and Boston and New York can have you" [001-045] — a rejection that stings precisely because Boston was a desirable destination.

## In the Stories

### The Road Trip Letters

Jack's letters from Boston are among the most geographically specific in the collection. His first dispatch is headed "Boston, Massachusetts, September 12" [002-055], and he immediately registers the city's impression: "Boston is some town, Al, and I wish you was here to see it" [002-060]. He walks "down Broadway the Main Street" [002-066], though his geographic confusion (Broadway is not Boston's main thoroughfare) is characteristic.

### Beating the Boston Club

Jack's competitive pride is most engaged against Boston. He boasts repeatedly about his success against them:

> "The Boston bunch couldn't of hit me with a board" [002-059]

After a particularly satisfying victory, he crows: "I beat Boston and just as good as beat New York" [004-137]. His self-assessment is strategic: "I wisht you could of been there to see me make that Boston Club look like a lot of rummies" [005-024].

### The Speaker Trade

Boston figures prominently in one of the most significant player transactions in the stories: the acquisition of [[tris-speaker|Tris Speaker]] by the White Sox. Jack notes that "We got Speaker from the Boston club" [006-014] and later describes how "the Pres. of the Boston club come out and sined Speaker" [012-095] — a major coup for Chicago that Jack reports with obvious satisfaction.

### Pitching Against the Red Sox

Boston's lineup earns Jack's grudging respect: "Boston and Cleveland" are teams against which he acknowledges a pitcher "would ever last 2 innings" [024-080] without his best stuff. The strategic importance of the Boston series is underscored when "Gleason is saveing me for the Boston serious" [025-056], treating the matchup as a marquee event.

### The Eddie Cicotte Connection

A revealing detail emerges about [[eddie-cicotte|Eddie Cicotte's]] history with Boston: "Eddie hates to beat this Boston club on acct. they canned him" [025-069]. Cicotte, like many players of the era, carried grudges against former teams — and Boston was one of them.
