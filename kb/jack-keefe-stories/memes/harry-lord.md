**Harry Lord** was a real-life third baseman who played for the Chicago White Sox (1910–1914). In Jack Keefe's letters, Lord's name typically appears when something has gone wrong in the field — a bunt not fielded, a ground ball not handled — and Jack is tallying the defensive failures that made his pitching line look worse than it deserved.

## In the Stories

Lord appears most notably when opponents exploit the White Sox infield defense:

> "they started bunting in the fifth and Lord and his buntin was something awful" (001-126)

> "two of which was bunts that Lord ought to of eat up" (002-059)

When Walter Johnson dominates the White Sox lineup, Lord shares in the collective failure:

> "Johnson whiffs Weaver and Chase and makes Lord look like a sucker" (002-094)
