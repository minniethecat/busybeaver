# Trench Life

The trench was not merely a battlefield feature — it was a world.
Sassoon's memoir renders that world in extraordinary sensory detail:
the smell of sandbags after rain, the flicker of a periscope at dawn,
the rattle of a Mills bomb clattering into a dugout.
The pages below, drawn from entries tagged `trench warfare` or `front-line`,
document the physical and psychological texture of life at the forward edge
of the Western Front.

## Notes

The `trench warfare` tag covers both physical features of the trench system
and the broader tactical/material culture of trench fighting. The `front-line`
tag is more narrowly applied to entries specifically concerned with the forward
trench positions (as opposed to support lines, billets, or rear areas).
