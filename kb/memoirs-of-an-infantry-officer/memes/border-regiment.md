# Border Regiment

The British infantry regiment that [[sassoon-siegfried|Sassoon]]'s battalion was ordered to relieve in the front line — a two-sentence appearance that encapsulates the memoir's understanding of trench warfare as a system of inter-unit rotations in which regiments are interchangeable, anonymous, and united only by the shared ordeal of the line.

## Identity & Structure

The Border Regiment was a line infantry regiment of the British Army, its name reflecting the Anglo-Scottish border region from which it historically recruited. During World War I, the regiment raised multiple battalions for service on the Western Front and in other theatres.

The memoir provides no description of the Border Regiment's composition or officers. Its identity — like that of most regiments in the memoir — is established purely by its function: holding a sector of front line until relieved.

## In the Memoir

The Border Regiment enters the narrative in two brief sentences. [[sassoon-siegfried|Sassoon]]'s battalion receives orders to "relieve a battalion of the Border Regiment next day, in the sector in front of [[fricourt|Fricourt]]" [012-003]. The following evening, "by nine o'clock we had relieved the Border Regiment" [012-007].

Between these two sentences lies the entire experience of a trench relief — the night march, the mud, the "dismal circumstance" of taking over unfamiliar positions in darkness. The Border Regiment itself is scarcely more than a name in the narrative, but its presence is essential: it is the unit that held the line before Sassoon's battalion arrived, the anonymous predecessor whose experience will now be repeated by its successors.

The relief is the memoir's most conspicuous structural device, and the Border Regiment is its paradigm case — a unit known only by its name and its departure.

## War Record

The Border Regiment served on the Western Front throughout the war. The memoir's reference locates one battalion in the Fricourt sector on the Somme, a position that placed it in the path of the 1916 offensive. The regiment's broader war record falls outside the memoir's scope.

## Sassoon's Perspective

[[sassoon-siegfried|Sassoon]] has no perspective on the Border Regiment as such — and that absence of perspective is itself the point. The memoir's narrative structure is built on a rhythm of relief and rotation in which regiments are encountered only at the moment of handover, as one anonymous battalion replaces another. The Border Regiment stands for all the units whose names Sassoon records but whose interior life he never enters.

## Historical Counterpart

The Border Regiment was a real British Army regiment. [[sassoon-siegfried|Sassoon]] uses its actual name, as he does with most regiments other than his own fictionalized "Flintshire Fusiliers" ([[royal-welch-fusiliers|Royal Welch Fusiliers]]).
