# Home Front & England

For Sassoon, "England" is never an abstraction. It is Kentish orchards, cricket pitches,
hunting country, and the quiet lanes of the Weald. His memoir contrasts this remembered
pastoral world with the industrial slaughter of the Western Front at every turn.
The pages below gather entries that touch on the civilian sphere — whether the physical
home front (hospitals, training depots, the London of leave) or the idea of England as a
felt presence in the soldier's imagination.
