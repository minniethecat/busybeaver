# Jenny Wren's Cry

## Overview

Jenny Wren's outburst — "Come up and be dead!" — the dolls' dressmaker's characteristic inversion of power relations, in which the disabled child commands the able-bodied adults around her.

This event, though less dramatically central than the novel's major turning points, reveals the texture of Dickens's moral vision. The minor events of *Our Mutual Friend* — the quiet transformations, the small acts of decency or cruelty, the moments when character is revealed through ordinary action rather than dramatic crisis — are the novel's most sustained argument that moral worth is demonstrated not in grand gestures but in daily choices.
