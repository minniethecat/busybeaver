Westminster is the political and legal centre of London, situated on the north bank of the [[river-thames|River Thames]]. In *Our Mutual Friend*, Westminster appears as the seat of law courts, government, and ecclesiastical establishment — the institutional backdrop against which the novel's social dramas unfold.

## Westminster Hall and the Law

Westminster Hall, the ancient great hall of the Palace of Westminster, is referenced as a venue for legal proceedings. A character is "examined as a witness in Westminster Hall" (022-018), underscoring the connection between Westminster and the legal machinery of Victorian England.

> "A man examined as a witness in Westminster Hall. Questions put to him seemed to confuse and bewilder him" (022-018)

## Westminster Abbey

Westminster Abbey, the coronation church and burial place of monarchs, appears in the novel through satirical comparison. When surveying the monumental sculpture in the Abbey, a character wryly notes the vanity of memorials (042-008). The Abbey also connects to the novel's clerical world through abbreviation: "Abbey was only short for Abbey Potterson" (006-006) — though this refers to a character, not the building itself, it nods to Westminster's cultural resonance.

> "He had been much impressed by most of the sculpture in Westminster Abbey" (042-008)

## Westminster Bridge and the Thames

Westminster Bridge spans the Thames, connecting Westminster to the Surrey shore. Characters cross this bridge at pivotal moments — traversing between the north and south banks of London (018-067, 035-028). The bridge serves as a geographical hinge point between different spheres of London life.

## A Seat of Institutions

Westminster embodies the institutional power structures of Victorian England — the law courts where justice is dispensed, the Parliament where laws are made, and the Abbey where the nation's rituals are performed. In a novel deeply concerned with social class and the distribution of wealth, Westminster represents the establishment against which characters like [[nicodemus-boffin|Nicodemus Boffin]] (new money) and [[mr-podsnap|Mr. Podsnap]] (complacent bourgeois respectability) define themselves.
