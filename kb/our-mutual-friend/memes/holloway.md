Holloway is a district in north London, significant in *Our Mutual Friend* as the home of the Wilfer family. It serves as the domestic anchor of [[bella-wilfer|Bella Wilfer]]'s life, representing respectability without wealth — the precarious middle-class existence of the Wilfer household.

## The Wilfer Home

The Wilfer family resides in Holloway, and their house is described as a modest residence in this northern suburb (004-009). [[r-wilfer|Reginald Wilfer]], Bella's father, returns home to Holloway each evening from his clerk's position in the City, tracing the daily commute of the Victorian suburban clerk.

> "His home was in the Holloway region north of London" (004-009)

## Social Significance

Holloway in the 1860s was a developing suburb on the edge of London, neither fashionable nor impoverished. For the Wilfers, it represents their social position — genteel but financially strained. Mrs. Wilfer's pride in their Holloway address reflects the Victorian anxiety about maintaining appearances despite limited means (025-023).

> "I mean your father's house at Holloway" — the house is referenced as a known landmark in the characters' social geography (025-023)

## Journey and Distance

The distance between Holloway and central London features repeatedly in the narrative. Characters walk to Holloway (049-096), travel there by carriage (048-155), and measure the time it takes to return home (054-073). This geographical separation mirrors the social distance between the Wilfers' modest domestic world and the wealthier spheres of London society.

## Proximity to Other Locations

The Holloway area is associated with Maiden Lane, another north London location mentioned in connection with the Wilfers (005-081). The suburb's position on the northern fringe of Victorian London placed it within commuting distance of the City while remaining distinctly separate from the commercial and wealthy districts.
