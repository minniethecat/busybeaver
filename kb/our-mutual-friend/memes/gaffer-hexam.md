Jesse Hexam, universally known as Gaffer Hexam, is a [[the-river-thames|Thames]] waterman who earns his living by the grisly trade of scavenging corpses from the [[the-river-thames|river]] — "a bird of prey" (001-037) who watches the water for bodies to plunder. He is the father of [[lizzie-hexam|Lizzie Hexam]] and [[charley-hexam|Charley Hexam]], and it is his discovery of a body in the [[the-river-thames|Thames]] — the supposed corpse of [[john-harmon|John Harmon]] — that sets the entire plot of the novel into motion. His world is the nocturnal [[the-river-thames|river]], a liminal space where the boundaries between honest labour and [[crime|criminality]] blur beyond recognition.

Gaffer Hexam is introduced in the novel's opening chapter alongside his daughter [[lizzie-hexam|Lizzie]], rowing his boat across the [[the-river-thames|Thames]] between Southwark Bridge and London Bridge. "My father, Jesse Hexam" (003-005): [[charley-hexam|Charley]]'s flat identification of his father in the third chapter establishes the name the [[the-river-thames|river]] community knows, while "Gaffer" — an honorific turned occupational nickname — is the name by which the [[the-river-thames|Thames]]-side world knows him: "Jesse Hexam, commonly called Gaffer on the [[the-river-thames|river]]" (012-111).

## Role in the Novel

Gaffer Hexam functions as the novel's dark catalyst. The body he pulls from the [[the-river-thames|Thames]] — identified as John Harmon — triggers the legal and [[money|inheritance]] machinery that drives the entire first half of the novel. But Hexam himself is more than a plot device: he represents the brutal subsistence economy of the [[the-river-thames|river]], where the dead are a resource and the living are barely distinguishable from them.

His [[death-and-resurrection|death]] by [[death-and-resurrection|drowning]] before the end of Book I removes him from the narrative early, but his shadow falls across the entire novel. His children spend the rest of the story negotiating t[[inheritance|heir]] relationship to his [[inheritance|legacy]]: [[charley-hexam|Charley]] through education and social climbing, [[lizzie-hexam|Lizzie]] through loyalty and self-sacrifice. "That done the Harmon Murder is Gaffer Hexam, the man that found the body" (012-094): the accusation that hangs over him — false, but plausible given his trade — stains his children's lives long after his [[death-and-resurrection|death]].

## Key Relationships

**[[lizzie-hexam|Lizzie Hexam]]**: Gaffer's daughter rows the boat for him in the opening chapter and remains fiercely loyal to him even as his world collapses. T[[inheritance|heir]] bond, forged in the shared labour of the [[the-river-thames|river]], is the emotional anchor of [[lizzie-hexam|Lizzie]]'s character.

**[[charley-hexam|Charley Hexam]]**: Gaffer's son rejects his father's world entirely, pursuing education as an escape from the [[the-river-thames|river]]. "My father, Jesse Hexam" (003-005): the formality of [[charley-hexam|Charley]]'s identification already signals his distance from the man the [[the-river-thames|river]] calls Gaffer.

**[[rogue-riderhood|Rogue Riderhood]]**: Former partner turned betrayer, [[rogue-riderhood|Riderhood]] accuses Hexam of [[crime|murder]]ing John Harmon for his pocket money — an accusation that drives Hexam from the community and ultimately to his [[death-and-resurrection|death]]. "You was no pardner of mine, Gaffer Hexam Esquire" (001-037): [[rogue-riderhood|Riderhood]]'s sarcastic honorific captures the bitter enmity between them.

## Thematic Significance

Gaffer Hexam embodies the novel's preoccupation with the [[the-river-thames|Thames]] as a [[morality|moral]] and economic landscape. The [[the-river-thames|river]] that sustains him also degrades him; his trade — retrieving the dead — places him at the bottom of a social hierarchy that Dickens presents as only marginally less corrupt at its top. His [[death-and-resurrection|death]] by the very water from which he made his living is the novel's first great ironic judgment: the [[the-river-thames|river]] takes back what it gave.
