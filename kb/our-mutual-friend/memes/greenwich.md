Greenwich is a historic town on the south bank of the [[river-thames|River Thames]] in southeast London, featuring as a destination for excursions, dining, and a significant marriage location in *Our Mutual Friend*.

## In the Novel

Greenwich appears at several key moments in the narrative. It is most prominently the location of [[bella-wilfer|Bella Wilfer]]'s marriage to [[john-harmon|John Rokesmith]] at Greenwich Church (055-053). The town also serves as a destination for riverside excursions, particularly by steamboat from London (054-031).

> "And we were married at Greenwich church in the presence of nobody" (055-053)

## Greenwich Dinners

The novel satirically references the "Archbishop of Greenwich" and the famed Greenwich dinners. The Whitebait Dinner at Greenwich was a well-known institution in Dickens's time — a summer ministerial banquet held at Greenwich. Dickens humorously invents the ecclesiastical title "Archbishop of Greenwich" to mock the pomp surrounding these occasions (054-057, 054-062).

> "they are sometimes out of, at Greenwich—were of perfect flavour, and the dinner altogether, including the Archbishop of Greenwich, was worthy of his Grace" (054-056)

## Greenwich Park

Greenwich Park, the royal park adjoining the town, is referenced as a pastoral retreat. In a moment of darker reflection, a character walking "among the harmless trees of Greenwich Park" perceives an ominous vision (054-046).

## Transport Links

Greenwich was reached from central London by river steamboat in the 1860s (054-031). The journey was a popular day-trip, combining river scenery with the attractions of Greenwich — the Park, the Church, and the famous dining establishments. The town could also be reached by road; one character departs from [[london-bridge|London Bridge]] for Greenwich by carriage (062-075).
