## Definition

A **commandment** (Latin: *mandatum*) is a divine precept binding in conscience. In Catholic theology, the commandments of God—especially the Decalogue (Ten Commandments)—constitute the objective moral law. The theological dispute in Pascal's *Provincial Letters* concerns whether fallen humanity, even in a state of grace, possesses the proximate power to fulfill the divine commandments.

## Role in the Controversy

The question of the commandments lies at the very heart of the Jansenist controversy. The first proposition condemned by the Sorbonne concerned precisely this issue: whether the righteous have the power to obey God's commandments.

Pascal reports that the Jansenist position—that the righteous always have the power to obey the commandments through grace, though not always the *proximate* power—was deliberately misrepresented by the Jesuit party. The Sorbonne censure treated the Jansenist distinction between "power" and "proximate power" as heretical, while Pascal maintains it is a standard theological distinction.

The debate intensifies around the question of whether the commandments are "impracticable" to the just. Pascal insists the Jansenists never taught that the commandments are impracticable to the righteous, but rather that grace is required to fulfill them—a position he claims is identical to that of St. Augustine and St. Thomas Aquinas.

## Positions

### Jesuit/Sorbonne Position

The censuring party held that:

- The proposition "the commandments of God are impracticable to the righteous" is heretical
- The righteous always possess the proximate power to obey the divine commandments ([001-017])
- Denial of proximate power is equivalent to denying the power itself

### Jansenist Position (as defended by Pascal)

Pascal maintains:

- The righteous always have the *power* to obey the commandments through grace ([001-024])
- The *proximate power*—the complete and immediate capacity—is not always present without a special grace
- This distinction between power and proximate power is standard in Thomistic theology
- The commandments require grace for their fulfillment; this does not make them "impracticable" ([018-030])
