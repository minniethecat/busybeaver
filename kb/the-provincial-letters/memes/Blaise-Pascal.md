# Blaise Pascal

Blaise Pascal (1623–1662) was a French mathematician, physicist, inventor, philosopher, and Catholic theologian.

## Role in the Provincial Letters

Between 1656 and 1657, Pascal wrote a series of 18 (plus one fragmentary 19th) letters under the pseudonym **Louis de Montalte**, defending the [[Jansenius|Jansen]]ist position against the Jesuits in the theological controversies of 17th-century France. The letters were published clandestinely and became a landmark of French prose and polemical writing.

## Key Contributions

- **Mathematics**: Pascal's theorem, Pascal's triangle, [[probabilism|probability]] theory
- **Physics**: Pascal's law (hydraulics), vacuum experiments
- **Theology**: The *Provincial Letters*, the *Pensées* (unfinished apology for Christianity)
