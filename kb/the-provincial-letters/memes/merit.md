## Definition

**Merit** (Latin: *meritum*) in Catholic theology refers to the quality of a good work performed in a state of grace whereby it deserves a supernatural reward from God. The concept rests on the principle that God has freely ordained that acts performed under the influence of grace and charity are worthy of eternal life—not by their intrinsic value, but by divine promise and the dignity Christ confers upon them.

## Role in the Controversy

The concept of merit enters the *Provincial Letters* indirectly, through the broader Jansenist–Jesuit debate on grace and free will. The Jesuit Molinist position emphasized human free will and the merit of good works performed through it, while the Jansenist position stressed the absolute primacy of grace—to the point where critics accused them of denying merit altogether.

Pascal defends the Jansenist position against the charge of Calvinism. He insists that the Jansenists, like all Catholics, affirm that the righteous truly merit eternal life through good works performed in grace. The dispute concerns not *whether* there is merit but *how* merit relates to grace—specifically, whether merit requires efficacious grace or merely sufficient grace.

Pascal also deploys the language of merit ironically when discussing Jesuit casuistry. He notes that certain Jesuit authors claim "the merit of having cleared up" moral difficulties ([005-022]), and that they attribute to "our modern authors the whole merit" of establishing new moral doctrines ([011-047]). The irony underscores Pascal's critique that the Jesuits treat moral theology as a field for personal distinction rather than faithful transmission.

## Positions

### Jesuit Molinist Position

- Good works performed with sufficient grace are truly meritorious
- Human free will cooperates with grace to produce merit
- The righteous merit eternal life *de condigno* (by a certain congruence of justice)

### Jansenist Position (as defended by Pascal)

- Merit requires efficacious grace, not merely sufficient grace
- The Jansenist position "acknowledges that the righteous have truly and properly merits" ([019-013])
- Merit is entirely dependent on grace and cannot be attributed to unaided free will
- The Jansenist position is identical to that of St. Augustine and the Council of Trent
