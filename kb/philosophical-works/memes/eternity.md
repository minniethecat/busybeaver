## Definition

**Eternity** in Descartes' philosophy is a divine attribute: the mode of existence by which God is not subject to temporal succession. God's knowledge and will are eternal — they do not unfold in time but are complete and unchanging.

## Divine Eternity

Descartes invokes divine eternity to resolve apparent tensions between human freedom and divine foreknowledge. He writes that "our mind is limited, while the power of God, by which he not only knew from all eternity what is or can be, but also willed and preordained it, is infinite" (099-002). Because God's knowledge is eternal rather than temporal, it does not constrain human freedom in the way that temporal foreknowledge might.

## Eternity and Truth

For Descartes, eternal truths — such as those of mathematics — are eternal because they are willed and sustained by God's eternal nature. This connects the concept of eternity to Descartes' doctrine of the creation of the eternal truths, one of his most distinctive metaphysical positions.
