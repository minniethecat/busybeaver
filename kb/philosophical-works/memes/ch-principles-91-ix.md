(144-001) That corporeal substance, when distinguished from its quantity, is confusedly conceived as something incorporeal.

(144-002) Although perhaps some express themselves otherwise on this [[body|matter]], I am nevertheless convinced that they do not think differently from what I have now said: for when they distinguish (corporeal) substance from extension or quantity, they either mean nothing by the word (corporeal) substance, or they form in their minds merely a confused idea of incorporeal substance, which they falsely attribute to corporeal, and leave to extension the true idea of this corporeal substance; which extension they call an accident, but with such impropriety as to make it easy to discover that their words are not in harmony with their thoughts.
