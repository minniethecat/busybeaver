(113-001) How duration, order, and number may be also distinctly conceived.

(113-002) We will also have most distinct conceptions of duration, order, and number, if, in place of mixing up with our notions of them that which properly belongs to the concept of substance, we merely think that the duration of a thing is a mode under which we conceive this thing, in so far as it continues to exist; and, in like manner, that order and number are not in [[perfection|reality]] different from things disposed in order and numbered, but only modes under which we diversely consider these things.
