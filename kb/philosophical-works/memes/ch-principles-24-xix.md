(077-001) That, although we may not comprehend the nature of [[god|God]], there is yet nothing which we know so clearly as his [[perfection|perfections]].

(077-002) This will appear sufficiently certain and manifest to those who have been accustomed to contemplate the idea of God, and to turn their thoughts to his infinite perfections; for, although we may not comprehend them, because it is of the nature of the infinite not to be comprehended by what is finite, we nevertheless conceive them more clearly and distinctly than material objects, for this reason, that, being simple, and unobscured by limits,19 they occupy our mind more fully.
