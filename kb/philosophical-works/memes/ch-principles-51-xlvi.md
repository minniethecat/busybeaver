(104-001) It is shown, from the example of pain, that a perception may be clear without being distinct, but that it cannot be distinct unless it is clear.

(104-002) For example, when anyone feels intense pain, the knowledge which he has of this pain is very clear, but it is not always distinct; for men usually confound it with the obscure judgment they form regarding its nature, and think that there is in the suffering part something similar to the sensation of pain of which they are alone conscious. And thus perception may be clear without being distinct, but it can never be distinct without likewise being clear.
