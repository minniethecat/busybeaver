(137-001) How we likewise know that the human body is closely connected with the mind.

(137-002) We ought also to conclude that a certain body is more closely united to our mind than any other, because we clearly observe that pain and other sensations affect us without our foreseeing them; and these, the mind is conscious, do not arise from itself alone, nor pertain to it, in so far as it is a thing which thinks, but only in so far as it is united to another thing [[extension|extended]] and movable, which is called the human body. But this is not the place to treat in detail of this [[body|matter]].
