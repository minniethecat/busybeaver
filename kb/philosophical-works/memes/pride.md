## Definition

**Pride** (*orgueil* in Descartes' French) is the passion of excessive self-esteem or self-regard. Descartes mentions pride in the context of critiquing the ancient moralists' failure to provide an adequate criterion of virtue.

## Pride in Descartes' Moral Thought

In a rare direct reference to pride, Descartes observes that the ancient moralists "laud the virtues very highly, and exhibit them as estimable far above anything on earth; but they give us no adequate criterion of virtue, and frequently that which they designate with so fine a name is but apathy, or pride, or despair, or parricide" (004-010). This passage reveals Descartes' concern that without a proper method for identifying virtue, what passes for moral excellence may be merely a vice in disguise.

## Pride and the Passions

Though Descartes did not extensively analyze pride in the *Discourse on the Method*, his later work *The Passions of the Soul* treats it within his systematic classification of the passions. Pride there is understood as a species of wonder at one's own excellence, which can be either legitimate (when grounded in genuine merit) or vicious (when excessive or ungrounded).
