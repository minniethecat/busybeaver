(041-001) That we possess a free will, by which we can withhold our [[judgment|assent]] from what is doubtful, and thus avoid error.

(041-002) But meanwhile, whoever in the end may be the author of our being, and however powerful and deceitful he may be, we are nevertheless conscious of a [[will|freedom]], by which we can refrain from admitting to a place in our belief aught that is not manifestly certain and undoubted, and thus guard against ever being deceived.
