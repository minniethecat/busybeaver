# The Besançon Election of 1828

The election for the Chamber of Deputies seat representing Besançon is the political engine of _Albert Savarus_. Albert's candidacy — orchestrated by the Vicar-General, opposed by the Watteville salon, and secretly sabotaged by Rosalie — brings every social force in the novel into collision. This page aggregates all entries connected to the election across characters, events, concepts, and places.

## Notes

The 1828 election is one of the densest narrative threads in _Albert Savarus_, engaging characters across the full social spectrum — from the legitimist aristocracy of the Watteville salon to the liberal-constitutional circles Albert navigates. The tag-based aggregation here captures the novel's treatment of politics as inseparable from personal ambition, romantic intrigue, and provincial power structures.
