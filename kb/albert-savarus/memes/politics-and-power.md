# Politics and Power in _Albert Savarus_

The political dimension is central to Balzac's _Albert Savarus_. The novel unfolds against the backdrop of Restoration France (1814–1830), a period of intense political realignment after the Napoleonic era. Albert Savarus himself is a political aspirant whose career is destroyed by the 1830 July Revolution; his legal and electoral maneuvers in Besançon form the novel's public plot, mirroring the private drama of love and deception. This page aggregates all wiki entries that illuminate the political, legal, and social-power dimensions of the novel.
