# Place Atlas

The geography of _Albert Savarus_ maps a journey from provincial confinement to
romantic liberation and back. This atlas presents every location in the novel
ranked by narrative prominence, with quality scores reflecting the depth of
geographic and symbolic analysis available for each place.

## Reading the Atlas

Places in Balzac function as more than settings: they are narrative actors.
**Besançon** embodies provincial surveillance and social constraint;
**Lake Lucerne** represents the romantic ideal and the elegiac frame narrative;
**Paris** stands for ambition and the wider world Albert yearns to re-enter.
The **References** column reflects how often each location is invoked across
the novel's 21 chapters.
