# Love and Marriage in _Albert Savarus_

Love is the driving force of Balzac's _Albert Savarus_ — a force that shapes every major character's destiny. Albert's hidden love for Francesca Colonna, Rosalie's obsessive infatuation with Albert, and the institution of marriage as a social contract all intersect to create the novel's central drama. This page aggregates all entries that explore love, marriage, romance, and the passions that both elevate and destroy.

## Notes

Love and marriage in _Albert Savarus_ operate on multiple levels — the idealized, spiritual love Albert feels for Francesca; the calculating, possessive "love" Rosalie feels for Albert; and the strategic marriages that characters use to secure social position. The novel suggests that in Restoration society, love and marriage are rarely the same thing. Readers should also consult [[politics-and-power]] for the intersection of marriage with political ambition, and [[deception-and-secrets]] for how secrecy shapes the love plot.
