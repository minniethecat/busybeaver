## Summary

Timber thief [[wessner|Wessner]] attempts to bribe [[freckles|Freckles]] into allowing the theft of [[mclean|McLean]]'s valuable timber. When [[freckles|Freckles]] refuses, a violent confrontation ensues. [[freckles|Freckles]] physically defeats [[wessner|Wessner]], but the arrival of the more dangerous [[black-jack|Black Jack]] raises the stakes, leading to a brutal escalation and [[freckles|Freckles]]' severe beating (004-077–004-143).

## Context

[[freckles|Freckles]] has proven himself a loyal and vigilant guard for [[mclean|McLean]]'s timber. The timber thieves, led by the notorious [[black-jack|Black Jack]], have been stealing valuable lumber from [[limberlost-swamp|the Limberlost]]. [[wessner|Wessner]], a member of the gang, approaches [[freckles|Freckles]] in the clearing to test his loyalty and attempt to turn him (004-077). [[duncan|Duncan]] and [[mclean|McLean]] have grown deeply trusting of [[freckles|Freckles]] and have warned him about the danger posed by the thieves (001-035).

## Account

[[wessner|Wessner]] approaches [[freckles|Freckles]] directly and complains about [[mclean|McLean]], calling him and [[duncan|Duncan]] "slobbering old Scots" in an attempt to find common ground (004-077). Green lights begin to play through the gray of [[freckles|Freckles]]' eyes as he realizes [[wessner|Wessner]]'s intent (004-078). [[wessner|Wessner]], seeing his initial approach failing, switches tactics and offers [[freckles|Freckles]] a bribe — five hundred dollars to allow the theft of timber, five times that if he joins the gang (004-095).

[[freckles|Freckles]] responds with biting sarcasm, calling it a "rare joke on [[mclean|the Boss]]" to steal what he has been trusted to guard while still collecting wages (004-097). His tone shifts to iron command as he demands [[wessner|Wessner]] strip for a fight (004-100). [[duncan|Duncan]], observing from a distance, whispers to [[mclean|McLean]] about [[wessner|Wessner]]'s suspicious behavior (004-105).

The fight is brutal. [[freckles|Freckles]], despite his missing hand, fights with intense fury, declaring he is "itching like death" to get his fingers in [[wessner|Wessner]]'s face (004-110). He eventually defeats [[wessner|Wessner]], catching him by the neck and jerking him to his knees (004-120). [[wessner|Wessner]] breaks down in sobbing surrender, but this marks only a temporary victory.

[[black-jack|Black Jack]] then arrives, and the confrontation escalates dangerously. [[freckles|Freckles]] fights with desperate courage but is ultimately overwhelmed. [[mclean|McLean]] arrives to find [[freckles|Freckles]] badly beaten (004-140, 004-143).

## Participants

- **[[freckles|Freckles]]**: The [[limberlost-swamp|Limberlost]] guard, fiercely loyal to [[mclean|McLean]]. He refuses the bribe and fights courageously despite being outnumbered and physically disadvantaged.
- **[[wessner|Wessner]]**: A timber thief who attempts to corrupt [[freckles|Freckles]]. He is physically defeated and humiliated but later participates in [[freckles|Freckles]]' beating.
- **[[black-jack|Black Jack]]**: The more dangerous timber thief whose arrival turns the confrontation deadly.

## Aftermath

[[freckles|Freckles]]' loyalty under extreme pressure solidifies [[mclean|McLean]]'s absolute trust in him. The brutal beating [[freckles|Freckles]] endures deepens the bond between guard and employer. The confrontation escalates the timber theft conflict from threat to open violence, setting up the climactic events of later chapters. [[mclean|McLean]]'s discovery of [[freckles|Freckles]]' decorated clearing (004-162) after this beating adds poignancy to the revelation of the boy's inner life.
