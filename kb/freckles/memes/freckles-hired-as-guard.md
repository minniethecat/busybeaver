## Summary

[[freckles|Freckles]], a young Irish orphan with only one hand, arrives at the [[grand-rapids|Grand Rapids]] lumber camp and successfully persuades field manager [[mclean|McLean]] to hire him as a guard for [[limberlost-swamp|the Limberlost]] swamp, despite his physical [[physical-difference|disability]] and lack of experience.

## Context

[[freckles|Freckles]] has recently left the Chicago orphanage where he spent his childhood and adolescence. Having been repeatedly passed over for adoption due to his missing hand and what he perceives as his "ugly face," he arrives at [[the-logging-camp|McLean's camp]] determined to find honest work (001-047). The [[limberlost-swamp|Limberlost]] swamp is a dangerous wilderness where [[mclean|McLean]]'s company has just leased two thousand acres of valuable timber (001-030). Previous guards have been threatened by timber thieves, and one employee, [[john-carter|John Carter]], was forced to flee (001-035).

## Account

[[freckles|Freckles]] approaches [[the-logging-camp|the camp]] boss directly as [[mclean|McLean]]'s crew is preparing for supper (001-005). The boss's assistant Sears initially dismisses him as another man wanting work, but [[mclean|McLean]] insists on interviewing him himself (001-015). [[freckles|Freckles]] stands rigid before [[mclean|McLean]], refusing a seat, and presents himself with quiet dignity despite his worn and soiled clothing (001-040).

[[mclean|McLean]] explains the dangers of the job — [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]] is charged with malaria, rattlesnakes, and desperate timber thieves (001-030, 001-035). When asked why he wants the position, [[freckles|Freckles]] responds that he has spent more time thinking about it than anything else in his life (001-045). He recounts his harsh experience at the Chicago orphanage, where he was repeatedly passed over for adoption because of his missing hand (001-047, 001-052). He states plainly that he has no home and no family (001-052).

Despite initial hesitation, [[mclean|McLean]] is moved by [[freckles|Freckles]]' honesty and resolve. Without stealing anything, [[freckles|Freckles]] had already left behind the charity clothes of [[chicago-orphanage|the Home]] and set out on his own (001-055). [[mclean|McLean]] finds himself agreeing to hire the boy, promising him clean clothing and a fresh start (001-060). [[freckles|Freckles]], who has held himself rigid throughout the interview, suddenly breaks down in tears of joy and gratitude (001-065). As he follows [[mclean|McLean]] into [[the-logging-camp|the camp]], his heart and soul are [[music-and-song|singing]] (001-069).

## Participants

- **[[freckles|Freckles]]**: The protagonist, a young Irish orphan with one hand seeking honest work. His courage, honesty, and dignity win over [[mclean|McLean]] despite his physical limitations.
- **[[mclean|McLean]]**: The field manager of the [[timber-industry|lumber company]], a Scottish gentleman who proves himself fair-minded and perceptive. He sees past [[freckles|Freckles]]' missing hand to the boy's essential character.

## Aftermath

This hiring is the foundational event of the novel. It gives [[freckles|Freckles]] his first real position of trust and responsibility, establishing the deep loyalty between him and [[mclean|McLean]] that drives much of the story (001-060). The job as [[limberlost-swamp|Limberlost]] guard sets [[freckles|Freckles]] on a path from anonymous orphan to respected, valued individual — and ultimately leads to his discovery of love, beauty, and his true parentage. His fierce commitment to guarding [[mclean|McLean]]'s timber becomes the moral center of the subsequent conflict with the timber thieves.
