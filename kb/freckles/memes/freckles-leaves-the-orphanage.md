## Summary

[[freckles|Freckles]] leaves the Chicago orphanage where he spent his entire childhood and adolescence. Having reached the full legal age and several years beyond, he sheds his charity clothes and sets out to find honest work, eventually arriving at [[mclean|McLean]]'s lumber camp to seek the guard position (001-047, 001-052, 001-055).

## Context

[[freckles|Freckles]] was taken in by "[[chicago-orphanage|the Home]]" as a young child and remained there for his entire upbringing (001-047). For the most part, the children were a lot of little Irishmen together, and the other children could always find adoptive homes — but nobody would ever take [[freckles|Freckles]] because of his missing hand (001-047). Every few days throughout his life, he was called up, looked over, and refused a home and love "on account of me hand and ugly face" (001-052). Yet [[chicago-orphanage|the orphanage]] was all the home he had ever known, and he did not seem to belong anywhere else (001-052).

## Account

By the time [[freckles|Freckles]] finally leaves [[chicago-orphanage|the orphanage]], he has stayed well past the full legal age and several years beyond (001-047). He has endured a lifetime of rejection — repeatedly examined and refused by families who recoiled at his physical difference (001-052). But rather than succumbing to bitterness, [[freckles|Freckles]] resolves to make his own way in the world.

He leaves [[chicago-orphanage|the Home]] with nothing but his determination. Significantly, he does not even have to steal clothes to avoid starting out in his Home uniform — for [[chicago-orphanage|the orphanage]] staff had already taken all his clean, neat things (001-055). He arrives at [[the-logging-camp|McLean's camp]] in soiled calico, looking at a glance like a tramp (001-002). But beneath the worn clothing is a young man of fierce pride and determination.

When [[mclean|McLean]] interviews him, [[freckles|Freckles]] recounts his orphanage experience with a matter-of-fact dignity that moves the timber boss (001-045). He carries no bitterness about his missing hand, though it has cost him every chance at a family. Instead, he offers [[mclean|McLean]] his honesty, his courage, and his willingness to work. The orphanage taught [[freckles|Freckles]] that he was unwanted, but it failed to crush his spirit. His departure is an act of self-determination — the first choice of his adult life, leading him to [[limberlost-swamp|the Limberlost]] and his destiny.

## Participants

- **[[freckles|Freckles]]**: The young orphan who chooses to leave the only home he has ever known. His departure is an act of courage and self-respect — he will not stay where he is unwanted, and he will not beg for what has been denied him.

## Aftermath

[[freckles|Freckles]]' departure from [[chicago-orphanage|the orphanage]] sets the entire novel in motion. Had he stayed, he would never have met [[mclean|McLean]], never become [[freckles|the Limberlost guard]], and never encountered the Angel. The emotional scars of his orphanage years — the repeated rejections, the internalized belief in his own ugliness and unworthiness — form the psychological backdrop against which every subsequent act of acceptance and love in the novel gains its power. When the Angel trusts him (005-045), when [[mclean|McLean]] discovers his clearing (004-162), and when his true parentage is revealed (017-128), each moment of recognition heals a wound inflicted by a lifetime of being refused.
