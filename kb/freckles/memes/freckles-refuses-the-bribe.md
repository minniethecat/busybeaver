## Summary

[[wessner|Wessner]], a member of [[black-jack|the timber thief]] gang, approaches [[freckles|Freckles]] and attempts to bribe him into allowing the theft of [[mclean|McLean]]'s valuable timber. [[freckles|Freckles]] responds with scathing Irish sarcasm, making it clear that no amount of money could buy his betrayal of the man who trusted him (004-097, 004-100).

## Context

[[freckles|Freckles]] has been guarding [[limberlost-swamp|the Limberlost]] timber faithfully since his hiring by [[mclean|McLean]] (001-060). The timber thieves have been active in [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]], stealing valuable lumber. [[wessner|Wessner]], one of the gang, approaches [[freckles|Freckles]] to test his loyalty (004-077). He begins by complaining about [[mclean|McLean]] and [[duncan|Duncan]], calling them "slobbering old Scots" in an attempt to find common ground with [[freckles|Freckles]] (004-077). But [[freckles|Freckles]]' eyes show green lights of anger at the insult to his employer (004-078).

## Account

[[wessner|Wessner]], realizing his opening gambit has failed, shifts tactics and makes a direct offer (004-080). He proposes that [[freckles|Freckles]] allow the theft of timber — and offers five hundred dollars for the simple act of looking the other way (004-095). He sweetens the deal by suggesting that [[freckles|Freckles]] could make five times that amount in a week if he joins the gang fully (004-095).

[[freckles|Freckles]]' response is masterful in its irony. He calls it a "rare joke on [[mclean|the Boss]]" to be stealing the very thing he was trusted to guard, while still collecting wages (004-097). He mocks [[wessner|Wessner]]'s generosity, saying the thief is treating him "most royal" and that seventeen cents would be a more fitting price for such a betrayal (004-097). His sarcasm makes it unmistakably clear: no bribe, no matter how large, will buy his disloyalty.

Dropping the irony, [[freckles|Freckles]]' [[music-and-song|voice]] hardens with iron authority (004-100). He commands [[wessner|Wessner]] to strip for a fight, transforming from sarcastic boy to outraged general (004-100). The timber thief, who expected to buy cooperation or at least silence, finds himself facing a loyal guard willing to fight for [[mclean|McLean]]'s interests with his fists.

## Participants

- **[[freckles|Freckles]]**: The [[limberlost-swamp|Limberlost]] guard whose fierce loyalty to [[mclean|McLean]] makes him immune to bribery. His sarcastic response to [[wessner|Wessner]]'s offer shows both his Irish wit and his unshakeable [[courage-and-honor|integrity]].
- **[[wessner|Wessner]]**: The timber thief who underestimates [[freckles|Freckles]]' character. His failed bribery attempt leads directly to the violent confrontation that follows.

## Aftermath

[[freckles|Freckles]]' refusal of the bribe (004-097) is the moral pivot of Chapter IV. His loyalty, tested by a significant sum of money, proves absolute. The refusal leads directly to the physical confrontation with [[wessner|Wessner]] (004-100, 004-110) and the later ambush by the gang (004-140). But [[wessner|Wessner]]'s failure to corrupt [[freckles|Freckles]] also cements the boy's reputation with [[mclean|McLean]] and [[duncan|Duncan]] as a person of unimpeachable [[courage-and-honor|integrity]] — a reputation that will prove important in the resolution of the novel.
