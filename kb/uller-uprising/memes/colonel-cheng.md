# Colonel Cheng-Li

Colonel Cheng-Li was the [[terra|Terra]]n Intelligence officer and Constabulary chief stationed at [[skilk|Skilk]], responsible for gathering and analyzing intelligence on [[ulleran|Ulleran]] political and military activity during the period leading up to and throughout the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]].

## Overview

Colonel Cheng-Li served as [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s primary intelligence asset at [[skilk|Skilk]]. His duties spanned counter-intelligence — compiling dossiers on known [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] disciples and anti-[[terra|Terra]]n agitators — and tactical intelligence, providing [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s command with detailed assessments of enemy capabilities. He maintained the Constabulary's vehicle registry and personnel records, and his intelligence briefings were critical to [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] decision-making during both the pre-uprising tension and the siege of [[skilk|Skilk]].

## Background

Cheng-Li held dual roles as [[skilk|Skilk]]'s Intelligence officer and Constabulary chief, giving him both investigative authority and access to law enforcement records. He reported directly to the military command structure under Brigadier-General Barney Mordkovitz and ultimately to General [[carlos-von-schlichten|Carlos von Schlichten]]. His intelligence apparatus included teleprinted communications with other [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] posts and a network of informants that allowed him to track the activities of [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s disciples and other anti-[[terra|Terra]]n elements.

## Role in the Uprising

### Pre-Uprising Intelligence

Cheng-Li played a crucial role in the investigation of [[terra|Terra]]n missionaries suspected of serving as [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s couriers. When [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] identified letters addressed to "[[skilk|Skilk]]ans known to be [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] disciples and rabidly anti-[[terra|Terra]]n," he radioed the list to Cheng-Li, who "teleprinted us back a lot of material on them that looks like the Newgate Calendar" （005-011）. This rapid intelligence response enabled [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] to warn [[paula-quinton|Paula Quinton]] against contact with the implicated individuals.

### Analysis of Rakkeed's Movement

At the pre-uprising staff dinner, Cheng-Li provided critical political intelligence on [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s propaganda. Responding to [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s query about the prophet's previous visit to [[skilk|Skilk]], Cheng-Li outlined [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s core message: "He as much as says that [[niflheim|Niflheim]]'s our home, and that the farms where we raise food here, and those evergreen plantings on Konk Isthmus and between here and Grank are the beginning of an attempt to drive all native life from this planet and make it over for ourselves" （007-027）. This intelligence brief on [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s ecological-territorial narrative helped the command staff understand the depth of [[ulleran|Ulleran]] resentment.

### Night of the Uprising

When the uprising erupted during the banquet, [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] established his command post at the [[skilk|Skilk]] [[telecast]] station. His immediate question — "Where's Colonel Cheng-Li?" （008-029） — underscored Cheng-Li's importance to the command structure. Cheng-Li survived the initial outbreak and was present at the command post. During the chaos, he attempted to manage Jules Keaveney, [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] Resident-Agent, who had fallen into despondency. Cheng-Li's "efforts to drag the Resident out of his despondency had been an excessive success" — Keaveney emerged argumentative rather than cooperative （009-003）.

### Siege Intelligence

Throughout the siege of [[skilk|Skilk]], Cheng-Li remained at the command post providing tactical assessments. He identified the sole 75 mm artillery piece King Firkked possessed, contrasting it with "at least six, possibly ten, 40 mm's" （011-014）. He also maintained detailed intelligence on enemy air assets: "Firkked owns, personally, three ten-passenger aircars, a thing like a troop-carrier that he transports some of his courtiers around in, four airjeeps armed with a pair of 15 mm machine-guns apiece, and two big lorries," with an estimated "two hundred vehicles of all types in [[skilk|Skilk]] and the country around" distributed among both hostile and friendly [[ulleran|Ullerans]] （011-018）.

## Characteristics & Traits

Cheng-Li was a meticulous intelligence professional whose value lay in detailed factual knowledge rather than operational command. He was seen "studying the smoking tip of his cigarette" while delivering precise enemy order-of-battle assessments （011-018）, a mannerism suggesting the calm, analytical temperament of a career intelligence officer. His dual role as Constabulary chief gave him unique access to local law enforcement records that proved invaluable for [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s counter-insurgency effort.
