# Air-Commodore Leslie Hargreaves

Air-Commodore Leslie Hargreaves was the senior [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] air operations commander during the later phase of the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]], responsible for organizing security patrols and aerial defense against [[orgzild|King Orgzild]]'s [[nuclear-weapons|nuclear bomb]] threat.

## Overview

Air-Commodore Hargreaves — addressed as "Les" by [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] — emerged as a key commander in the uprising's nuclear phase. After [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] secured [[konkrook|Konkrook]] and [[skilk|Skilk]], the threat shifted from ground combat to the possibility of atomic attack from [[keegark|Keegark]]. Hargreaves was tasked with organizing the aerial patrol network to intercept any [[keegark|Keegark]]an nuclear delivery vehicle before it could reach its target. Initially skeptical that the [[keegark|Keegark]]ans could develop nuclear capability, he became [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s principal air defense architect, commanding ship-based patrols, fitting civilian craft with radar, and coordinating with the *Sky-Spy* supra-atmosphere observation platform.

## Background

Hargreaves held the rank of Air-Commodore in the [[chartered-uller-company|Chartered Uller Company]]'s aviation service. He commanded [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s contragravity and ship-based air assets, with authority over both military patrol craft and the civilian vessels pressed into defense service. His experience included supra-atmosphere flight operations, and he advocated for "a couple of supra-atmosphere ships in service on this planet" regardless of the war's outcome （014-023）.

## Role in the Uprising

### Nuclear Threat Assessment

When [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] first raised the possibility that [[orgzild|King Orgzild]] had developed a [[nuclear-weapons|nuclear bomb]], Hargreaves expressed professional skepticism: "If that's true, it's a horrible danger to all of us. But I find it hard to believe that the [[keegark|Keegark]]ans could have done so, with their resources and at their technological level" （013-051）. He maintained this doubt even after [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] laid out the chain of evidence: "That still doesn't mean that the Kee-[[ulleran|geeks]] were able to do anything with it" （013-060）.

### Air Defense Command

Despite his doubts, Hargreaves immediately organized the defense. Von Schlichten gave him authority over all security patrols, with "priority on everything second only to Pickering" （013-072）. Hargreaves' patrol plan was comprehensive: "We're going to need the *Elmoran* for patrol work south and east of [[konkrook|Konkrook]], and the *Gaucho* and *Bushranger* to the north and northeast, based on [[king-kankad|Kankad]]'s. We'll keep the *Aldebaran* at [[king-kankad|Kankad]]'s, and use her for emergencies. And we'll have patrols of light contragravity" （014-023）.

### Civilian Craft Conversion

Hargreaves directed the conversion of civilian vessels for defense duty. He requisitioned "a pair of 155 mm rifles from the Channel Battery" to mount on the *Northern Lights* （015-021）. He also fitted "a lot of small craft — combat-cars and civilian aircars — with radar sets, to use for patrolling" （015-027）. Privately owned aircars were similarly converted for radiation-detection reconnaissance over [[keegark|Keegark]].

### The *Sky-Spy* Integration

Hargreaves coordinated with the *Sky-Spy* supra-atmosphere observation craft, positioned "a hundred and fifty miles over [[keegark|Keegark]]," which provided radar, vision, and telemetered radiation detection data （015-021）. This platform enabled continuous monitoring of [[keegark|Keegark]]an contragravity traffic, searching for any vehicle that might be carrying [[orgzild|Orgzild]]'s bomb.

### Nuclear Standoff

As the nuclear standoff developed, Hargreaves maintained round-the-clock patrols while Pickering's team raced to produce a [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] bomb. The patrols identified "normal contragravity traffic between [[keegark|Keegark]] and the farming-villages — aircars, lorries, a few scows — but nothing suspicious" （015-027）. The absence of the two Boer-class freighters [[orgzild|Orgzild]] had captured at [[keegark|Keegark]] — the likely delivery vehicles — remained a concern. Hargreaves received regular intelligence updates via sealed-beam [[telecast]] as [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] waited for the bomb team to succeed.

## Characteristics & Traits

Hargreaves was methodical and professional, combining healthy skepticism with prompt obedience to command. His initial doubt about the [[keegark|Keegark]]an nuclear capability reflected sound technical judgment — even [[terra|Terra]]ns struggled with the engineering — but he did not let that doubt delay defensive preparations. His patrol network, integrating captured and converted civilian vessels with military craft, demonstrated resourcefulness under severe material constraints. His advocacy for permanent supra-atmosphere capability showed strategic foresight.
