# Battle of Konkrook Reservation

The Battle of Konkrook Reservation was the decisive defensive engagement of the early Uller Uprising. Following the assassination of Governor Harrington and the mutiny of native-troops across multiple kingdoms, the Company's main compound at Konkrook came under sustained attack by Ulleran forces seeking to overrun the last Terran stronghold on the planet.

## The Defensive Position

The Company Reservation at Konkrook was a fortified administrative and military compound housing the planetary headquarters of the Chartered Uller Company. It contained barracks, armories, communications centers, command posts, and the residences of senior Company personnel. The Reservation was protected by a wire perimeter, guard towers, and a garrison of Terran and loyal native troops — though the latter category had been severely depleted by the mutiny (010-039).

Von Schlichten, now in command, organized the defense around three principles: containment of the perimeter to prevent infiltration, conservation of ammunition against the prospect of a prolonged siege, and holding the compound's central communications node to maintain contact with off-world Federation forces (010-008).

## The Attacks

The siege involved multiple forms of assault, reflecting the eclectic military capabilities of the rebel coalition. Contragravity air units — captured or turned by mutinous native pilots — conducted strafing runs and attempted to drop troops inside the perimeter. These air attacks were repulsed by the compound's anti-air defenses, including ball-turret guns mounted on command cars and fixed positions along the wire (010-039).

Ground assaults came in waves: the first was the mutinous native-troops from the Company's own barracks, who attacked the labor-camp sector in an attempt to liberate workers and swell their numbers. Later waves consisted of irregular Ulleran infantry armed with a mixture of captured Company weapons and traditional native arms. The wire perimeter held against multiple ground assaults, though at considerable cost in ammunition and casualties (010-012).

## Relief by King Kankad

The siege was broken by the intervention of King Kankad of the Kragan kingdom. Kankad's forces, loyal to the Company and equipped with modern artillery — "the only non-Terran artillery above 75mm on Uller" (005-045) — provided both reinforcement troops and, critically, contragravity airlift capacity. Kankad personally directed the airlift of Kragan reinforcements into the besieged compound, using captured and Company-supplied contragravity vehicles to move troops past the siege lines (010-007)(010-015).

The Kragans' intervention was not simply a military reinforcement but a political statement: the uprising was not a unified native revolt but a civil war among Ulleran kingdoms, with the Kragans choosing the Company over their fellow natives. This fracture in native solidarity was one of the few strategic advantages the Company retained.

## Aftermath

The successful defense of the Konkrook Reservation preserved the Company's command-and-control capability and prevented the complete collapse of Terran authority on Uller. It also bought time for Operation Hildegarde — the desperate nuclear weapons project that would ultimately end the uprising through the destruction of Keegark (014-027).
