# Konk Isthmus

The Konk Isthmus was the geographic landform on which the city of [[konkrook|Konkrook]], the Terran administrative capital of [[uller|Uller]], was built. Formed by the delta of the Konk River where it emptied into Konkrook Channel, the isthmus defined the physical footprint of the largest urban center on Uller and shaped the city's strategic and economic geography.

## Geography & Environment

The isthmus lay at the mouth of the Konk River, which was a seasonal waterway — "the river was dry, now. Except in spring, when it was in flood, there were only a few stagnant, fetid pools in the river bed" (001-019). During flood season, the river deposited silt across the delta, enriching the farmlands that extended inland from the city. The isthmus was bounded by Konkrook Channel to the south and east, separating the mainland city from [[gongonk-island|Gongonk Island]], the Company's island headquarters.

Beyond the isthmus's urban core, the landscape transitioned through forested zones into agricultural territory. "Beyond the forests came the farmlands," with older estates protected by "thick walls of flint and petrified wood" and wide defensive moats — a legacy of centuries of inter-kingdom warfare that predated the Terran presence (001-016). These fortified farm compounds on the isthmus's inland periphery reflected the historically contested nature of the territory, even before the arrival of the Chartered Uller Company.

The isthmus's shoreline along Konkrook Channel served as the city's waterfront, handling both native maritime traffic and Company contragravity operations. The Channel provided deep-water access for the interstellar spaceport on Gongonk Island while separating the Terran administrative zone from the native city proper.

## Strategic Significance

The isthmus's geography made Konkrook both defensible and commercially vital. The Channel provided a natural moat protecting the southern approaches, while the seasonal Konk River defined the city's inland boundary. Control of the isthmus meant control of the river delta, the surrounding farmlands, and access to the interior — which was why the kings of Konkrook had fortified it for millennia, and why the Company chose it as their planetary capital.

## In the Uprising

During the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]], the isthmus's geography shaped the urban combat in Konkrook. The narrow land approaches to the city — constrained by the river on one side and the Channel on the other — channeled the movement of mutinous laborers and native troops. Company forces on Gongonk Island, separated from the mainland by the Channel, could observe and interdict movement on the isthmus while remaining relatively secure from direct ground assault.
