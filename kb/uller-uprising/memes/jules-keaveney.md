# Jules Keaveney

Resident-Agent of the [[chartered-uller-company|Chartered Uller Company]] at [[skilk|Skilk]], a civilian administrator whose contentious relationship with the military and subsequent breakdown during the uprising illustrated the deep divide between [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] commercial interests and the security reality on [[uller|Uller]].

## Overview

Jules Keaveney served as [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s Resident-Agent at [[skilk|Skilk]], one of the major North [[ulleran|Ulleran]] kingdoms. Relatively new to [[uller|Uller]] — he had been on the planet for only two years, compared to [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s fifteen — Keaveney embodied the commercial mindset of [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] administration. His dismissive attitude toward military concerns and patronizing treatment of veteran officers represented a systemic tension that would prove catastrophic when the coordinated uprising began.

## Background

Keaveney arrived on [[uller|Uller]] approximately two years before the events of the uprising. As Resident-Agent at [[skilk|Skilk]], he was responsible for [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] commercial operations in one of the key northern kingdoms. His short tenure and commercial background left him ill-equipped to understand either the military situation or the [[ulleran|Ulleran]] political landscape.

At the banquet in [[skilk|Skilk]], Keaveney sat at the head of the table, a position reflecting his seniority as the ranking [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] civilian at that station.

## Role in the Uprising

### The Skilk Banquet

On the night [[sid-harrington|Governor-General Harrington]] died, Keaveney presided over the banquet at which [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] was the guest of honor. His exchanges with [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] revealed the fundamental disconnect between [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] commercial and military perspectives.

When [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] and other officers expressed concern about native "insolence" and warned of potential insurrection, Keaveney dismissed their warnings: "You soldiers are all alike... If they don't bow and scrape to you and get off the sidewalk to let you pass, you say they're insolent and need a lesson" （006-016）.

He expressed confidence that [[orgzild|King Orgzild of Keegark]] "has things under pretty tight control" and "would not allow a few fanatics to do anything to prejudice these spaceport negotiations" （006-019）.

Keaveney was openly derisive of the military's reliance on [[kragan|Kragan]] mercenaries: "I never could understand the admiration some of you military people have for those cutthroats... You like them because they do your dirty work for you" （006-028）.

He articulated [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s core philosophy explicitly: "We're here to earn dividends for the stockholders of the [[chartered-uller-company|Uller Company]], and we can only do that by gaining the friendship, respect and confidence of the natives" （006-030）. This was immediately challenged by [[paula-quinton|Paula Quinton]], who described her own recent near-death experience at the hands of an [[ulleran|Ulleran]] mob.

### Receiving the News

Keaveney received the radioprint slip announcing [[sid-harrington|Harrington]]'s death and handed it to Mordkovitz （006-036）. When [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] moved to take command, Keaveney attempted to detain him: "Wait a minute, general... How about this?" — gesturing nervously with the radioprint slip （006-038）. Von Schlichten ignored him and left, hearing Keaveney "tapping on his wineglass" as he departed （006-039）.

### Collapse During the Crisis

As the uprising unfolded at [[skilk|Skilk]], Keaveney's confidence evaporated. Von Schlichten found him "blundering" about "in a half-daze" （008-005）. Keaveney wailed that "there won't be one of us left by this time tomorrow... And [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] is finished!" （008-008）.

Colonel Cheng-Li, the Intelligence officer, attempted to calm the panicking Resident, but Keaveney instead became argumentative. His contentiousness — "arguing contentiously" and challenging the military's explanations — represented a shift from dismissive superiority to frightened obstructionism （008-063）.

When told that the uprising had been coordinated without [[telecast]] equipment, Keaveney challenged: "But it's crazy! Not just here; everywhere on [[uller|Uller]]!... How did they do it? They have no [[telecast]] equipment" （008-064）.

## Characteristics & Traits

Keaveney's character arc traces a trajectory from smug complacency through dismissive condescension to panicked collapse. His two years on [[uller|Uller]] had given him enough experience to feel confident but not enough to recognize his own ignorance of the situation.

His core belief — that commercial goodwill could substitute for military preparedness — was the prevailing [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] doctrine and directly contributed to the crisis. When that belief proved catastrophically wrong, Keaveney lacked the psychological resources to adapt, oscillating between despair and unhelpful argumentation.

## Relationships

- **[[carlos-von-schlichten|Carlos von Schlichten]]**: Treated with patronizing condescension before the uprising; dependent on him during it
- **[[paula-quinton|Paula Quinton]]**: Challenged his naive views on native relations with her firsthand experience of [[ulleran|Ulleran]] violence
- **Barney Mordkovitz**: Fellow banquet attendee; Keaveney received the death notice with him
- **Colonel Cheng-Li**: Attempted to manage Keaveney's panic during the crisis
- **Mrs. Jules Keaveney**: His wife, present during the uprising at [[skilk|Skilk]], assisted with tactical mapping
