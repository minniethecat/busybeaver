# Konkrook

Konkrook is the capital city of the Konk Isthmus, the equatorial heartland of Ulleran civilization and the administrative center of the [[chartered-uller-company|Chartered Uller Company]]'s planetary operations. Sprawling along the delta of the Konk River, Konkrook is simultaneously the seat of King Jaikark's puppet monarchy and the site of the Company Reservation on Gongonk Island — a dual power structure that would collapse catastrophically during the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]].

## Geography and Layout

The city extends from the Konk River delta inland, a dense urban landscape of one-story dwellings and shops interspersed with lofty temples, palaces, and fantastically twisted towers. Approaching from the air, the city transitions from the suburbs with their yellow-green parks to the mean, squalid industrial district along the waterfront (001-019).

> "No Terran could possibly remember the names of Konkrook's streets; even native troops recruited from outside found the numbers easier to learn and remember." (001-026)

The streets are numbered rather than named — a practical concession to the city's linguistic complexity. Gongonk Island, separated from the mainland by a channel, houses the Company Reservation — the fortified compound containing Company House, the guard barracks, the military airport, the hospital, and the telecast station. It is both the physical symbol of Terran power and the most vulnerable point in the colonial administration.

## Political Structure

### Power Holders

| Figure | Position | Real Influence |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| King Jaikark XVII | Monarch | Figurehead — narcotic-addled puppet (003-011) |
| Gurgurk | Vizier | True power behind throne; plays both sides (002-007) |
| Rakkeed | Prophet | Anti-Terran preacher, alter ego of Keeluk |
| Company (von Schlichten) | Governor-General | Military and administrative authority |

Konkrook is ruled by King Jaikark the Seventeenth, a Company puppet who spends most of his time in a narcotic stupor under the influence of a native drug combining the effects of hashish, heroin, and yohimbine (003-011). The real power behind the throne is his vizier, Gurgurk, who has been building a political organization with the intention of deposing Jaikark and seizing power himself (002-007). Gurgurk simultaneously supports the anti-Terran preacher Rakkeed, creating a dangerous two-level game in which he fans anti-Company sentiment among the populace while positioning himself as the Company's eventual savior.

King Jaikark's military consists of Household Guards, infantry regiments of spearmen and crossbowmen, and outsized cavalry companies. The Company supplements these forces with [[kragan|Kragan]] Rifles mercenaries — about twenty of whom are loaned to Jaikark's palace as a bodyguard detail.

## The Riot and the Uprising

The opening act of the novel takes place over Konkrook, where General [[carlos-von-schlichten|Carlos von Schlichten]] spots a riot on Fourth Avenue near Seventy-second Street — Ullerans mobbing two Terrans backed against a house (001-026). Von Schlichten's command-car descends into the melee, and the intervention of Company troops and Kragan Rifles suppresses the immediate disturbance. But the riot is a symptom of deeper unrest, fomented by Rakkeed's disciples preaching *znidd suddabit* — death to the Terrans.

When the uprising erupts in full, Konkrook becomes the most critical front. Mutiny breaks out among the native laborers and troops on Gongonk Island, who outnumber the Terrans and their Kragan mercenaries by five thousand to five hundred. The palace revolt engineered by Gurgurk traps Jaikark in his own apartments, defended only by the twenty Kragan Rifles and a handful of loyal courtiers (007-027). The siege of the Company Reservation becomes a grinding battle of attrition, with the terrain board and telecast screen serving as the tactical command center (010-017).

## Population and Society

Konkrook is home to over two hundred thousand inhabitants (008-020). Since [[ulleran|Ullerans]] are bisexual — true functional hermaphrodites — the entire adult population, less the senile, crippled, and very young, represents the military potential in time of war.

The city's social hierarchy includes:
- Ship-owning nobles controlling maritime trade on the Konk River
- The court circle around King Jaikark
- Foreign ambassadors (including the Keegarkan Ambassador who hosted Rakkeed)
- Religious figures and preachers
- A large underclass of laborers and slaves

The Company farms south of the city, across the channel on the mainland, produce food for the Terran population using introduced crops and chemical fertilizers. During the uprising, these farms become a secondary front requiring the commitment of combat [[contragravity]] and Kragan Rifles to defend.
