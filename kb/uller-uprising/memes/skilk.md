# Skilk

Skilk is a city in the northern region of [[uller|Uller]]'s Company-occupied territory, seat of King Firkked and site of one of the Company's major Residencies. During the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]], Skilk became the forward command post of General [[carlos-von-schlichten|Carlos von Schlichten]], who directed planetary military operations from its fortified telecast station.

## Geography and Infrastructure

Skilk is located five thousand miles from [[konkrook|Konkrook]], in the northern reaches of the Company's zone of control (002-020). It is built on a massive flint butte jutting into the swamp at the end of a sharp ridge, with multi-storied buildings piling upward from the top and clinging to the sides (005-027). This natural fortification was a traditional Kragan defensive position, and the high watchtower at the front now carries a telecast-director aimed at an automatic relay station on an unmanned orbiter two thousand miles off-planet (005-027).

The Company Reservation includes Company House, the telecast station, the native-troops barracks, the repair shops, the maintenance yard, the lorry hangars, the cavalry stables, and the power plant. The reservation is surrounded by a perimeter of defensive works, with the nearby mountains to the west providing cover for snipers and guerrilla attacks.

## King Firkked and Local Politics

King Firkked rules Skilk as one of the native monarchs of the northern kingdoms. The city is part of the network of kingdoms stretching from the equatorial Konk Isthmus to the polar regions, each with its own army, court politics, and relationship to the Company. Firkked's position is complicated by the presence of Rakkeed's disciples, who have been preaching *znidd suddabit* among the population, and by the proximity of the large Company military installation.

Company Intelligence tracked Rakkeed's movements across the planet: "Three days after he was reported here at Konkrook, he was reported at Skilk, five thousand miles away, said to be having an audience with King Firkked" (002-020). This pattern of royal audiences across multiple kingdoms demonstrated the reach of Rakkeed's psionic movement and the complicity — willing or coerced — of native rulers.

## The Telecast Station Command Post

When Governor-General [[sid-harrington|Sid Harrington]] was poisoned at a banquet at Company House, von Schlichten was at Skilk. He had been about to depart for Konkrook when the uprising erupted locally. The telecast station became his de facto headquarters. The inner door of the "soundproofed telecast-room" burst open, and three men hurried inside as small-arms fire rattled outside: the Tenth North Uller Native Infantry had mutinied, and they had taken their barracks, supply buildings, the lorry hangars, and the maintenance yard (007-034)(007-035).

From this room, von Schlichten coordinated the defense of the entire planet. He received situation reports from Keegark, Kwurk, Krink, and Kankad's Town, issued orders to Brigadier-General Mordkovitz and Colonel Jarman, and made the decision to declare martial rule at Konkrook (007-028). The telecast screen showed the tactical situation across the Company Reservation, while the terrain board tracked the positions of friendly and enemy forces (010-017).

## The Barracks and Labor-Camp Fighting

The uprising at Skilk began with the mutiny of the Tenth North Uller Native Infantry and elements of the Fifth Zirk Cavalry. The mutineers seized their barracks, supply buildings, the lorry hangars, and the maintenance yard, then began advancing on Company House in a mob (007-035). Colonel Jarman organized the counter-attack: "They blew out all four of the Tenth N.U.N.I.'s barracks; the Tenth and the Zirks are trying to defend the cavalry barracks. Some of our Kragans managed to slip around behind the cavalry stables. They're leading out hipposaurs, and sniping at the rear of the cavalry barracks" (008-011).

The native-troops barracks and labor-camp had to be reduced by Company forces — the labor system, which trained native workers in the operation of contragravity equipment and firearms, had effectively created its own enemy (008-011). The fighting around the repair shops and maintenance yard was particularly critical, as von Schlichten did not want the mutineers "getting hold of that equipment and using it against us" (008-012).

## Defense and Aftermath

The siege of Skilk continued through the night and into the following days. Major Falkenberg commanded the action at the native-troops barracks and labor-camp (010-019). The perimeter was gradually expanded, with a bulge pushed out at the northwest corner towards the explosives magazines. The defenders constructed breastworks of petrified tree trunks, sandbags, mining machinery, and packing cases (010-017). [[paula-quinton|Paula Quinton]], the Extraterrestrials' Rights Association observer, served as von Schlichten's adjutant and was credited with ordering the defense of the explosives domes containing ten thousand tons of thermoconcentrate and five tons of plutonium (010-021).

The successful defense of Skilk preserved the northern Company position and provided the base from which von Schlichten could coordinate the Kragan airlift to Konkrook and the eventual destruction of [[keegark|Keegark]].
