# Command-Car

The command-car is an armored contragravity vehicle used by senior officers of the Chartered Uller Company, most notably General Carlos von Schlichten, who employed it as his mobile command post throughout the Uller Uprising. Larger, more heavily armed, and significantly better protected than the standard airjeep, the command-car combines battlefield mobility with protected command-and-control capability.

## Propulsion System

The command-car's propulsion architecture represents a sophisticated application of contragravity technology. Lift is provided by cold-jet rotors — contragravity units that generate vertical thrust through field manipulation rather than moving air masses, operating silently and without the dust-stirring downdraft that characterizes conventional rotorcraft (001-015). The cold-jet system allows the command-car to hover with minimal acoustic and visual signature — a significant tactical advantage in the low-visibility conditions common on Uller.

Forward motion is produced by hot-jets — directed-thrust units that heat and accelerate atmospheric gases to generate propulsive force. The division of lift and propulsion into separate systems gives the command-car performance characteristics unavailable to any single-system vehicle: it can hover indefinitely on cold-jets, transition rapidly to high-speed forward flight on hot-jets, and shift between modes without the transitional instability that plagues tilt-rotor or vectored-thrust designs (001-015).

This dual-propulsion architecture makes the command-car substantially more complex than an airjeep, requiring dedicated engineering support and specialized pilot training. The trade-off is capability: the command-car can operate from any terrain, hover at any altitude within its performance envelope, and achieve forward speeds comparable to the fastest atmospheric craft in the Company's inventory.

## Armament and Armor

The command-car's primary armament is a ball-turret mounting twin 15mm machine-guns — the same caliber as the airjeep's forward guns but in a fully-articulated turret providing 360-degree traverse and near-vertical elevation. The ball-turret configuration allows the gunner to engage targets in any direction without requiring the vehicle to maneuver, which is essential for a command platform that must maintain a stable position during tactical operations (001-016).

The armor package is substantial by Company standards — capable of defeating the chemical-projectile and low-velocity kinetic weapons employed by most Ulleran forces. Against the 75mm and larger artillery pieces in the Kragan inventory (005-045), the armor provides protection from fragments and near-misses but cannot withstand a direct hit. The command-car relies on mobility and standoff distance rather than passive armor for survivability against heavy weapons.

## Command Role

Von Schlichten's command-car served as his mobile headquarters during the Battle of Konkrook Reservation and subsequent operations. The vehicle's internal configuration includes communications equipment, tactical displays, and workspace sufficient for the general and his immediate staff — typically two to three aides plus the vehicle's pilot and gunner (010-008).

The psychological dimension of the command-car is significant. Von Schlichten's visibility in the vehicle — hovering above the defensive perimeter, directing troop movements by radio while under fire — served as a force multiplier, demonstrating to the defenders that their commander was sharing their danger. Native Ulleran forces, accustomed to kings who commanded from palace thrones rather than battlefields, found the concept of a commander who fought from the front unsettling.

## Limitations

The command-car's complexity is its principal limitation. The dual cold-jet/hot-jet propulsion system requires maintenance beyond the capabilities of field workshops, and the vehicle's pilot must be trained on systems found nowhere else in the Company's inventory. Fuel consumption is high, limiting operational endurance to a few hours of intensive combat maneuvering before refueling is required. The vehicle is also a conspicuous target — its size and the distinctive silhouette of its ball turret make it instantly recognizable to hostile forces.
