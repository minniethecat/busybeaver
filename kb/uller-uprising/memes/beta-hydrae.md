# Beta Hydrae

Beta Hydrae is the primary star of the planetary system that contains Uller (Beta Hydrae II), a Terran-colonized world whose native saurian inhabitants present one of the most challenging colonial situations in the Terran Federation (004-001, 005-035). The star system extends at least to Beta Hydrae VII, whose orbit defines the outer reach of the inner system (005-035).

## The System

The Beta Hydrae system contains at least seven planets. Uller, Beta Hydrae II, is the only habitable world and the focus of the Uller Company's charter. Beta Hydrae V appears in navigation reports, used as a positional reference during aerial combat: an enemy vessel was spotted when it "obscured Beta Hydrae V" against the night sky (014-011). Beta Hydrae VII marks the system's outer boundary, mentioned as the hypothetical dispersal radius for radioactive debris in the event of a nuclear catastrophe on Uller (005-035).

The system was the subject of Willard Stanley-Browne's *Short Sociographic History of Beta Hydrae II*, described by von Schlichten as a "compendium of misinformation" in which "ninety percent of what Stanley-Browne says about them [the Kragans] is completely erroneous" (004-001). This work represents the flawed Terran academic understanding of the system's inhabitants.

## Ulleran Cosmology

Ulleran astronomy holds that Uller is the center of the universe, with Beta Hydrae traveling around it "on a rather complicated double-spiral track" (005-039). This geocentric model, while incorrect, is internally consistent: "As a theory, it explains most of what they're able to observe, and any minor effects that don't conform to it are just ignored" (005-039).

The University of Konkrook houses "a most ingenious affair run by clockwork" — a mechanical orrery that accurately displays the apparent movement and position of Beta Hydrae in the sky according to the Ulleran model (005-039). This instrument, created by native scholars working from flawed premises, parallels the orrery of the full Beta Hydrae system at King Kankad's observatory in Kankad's Town, built by the Kragans under Terran tutelage (005-011).

## Kankad's Observatory

King Kankad's observatory features "a big telescope and an ingenious orrery of the Beta Hydrae system" (005-011), representing the most advanced scientific installation on Uller in non-Terran hands. The observatory is part of Kankad's modernizing program, which also includes the nuclear-power plant — part of the original price for the Kragans giving up brigandage — physics and chemistry labs, a hospital, and an ammunition-loading plant (005-011).

The contrast between the Konkrook University clockwork model (built on false premises) and Kankad's Terran-guided orrery (built on accurate astronomical data) encapsulates the broader tension between indigenous Ulleran knowledge systems and Terran-imposed science.

## Narrative Significance

The Beta Hydrae system provides the novel's cosmic frame of reference. Every mention of the star or its planets reinforces the scale of the Terran colonial project: a species that traveled between stars now governs a world whose inhabitants cannot correctly conceptualize their own sun's motion. Commander Prinsloo's grim observation that nuclear war would scatter "little bits of radioactive geek floating around this system as far out as the orbit of Beta Hydrae VII" (005-035) uses astronomical scale to underscore the existential stakes of the Uller uprising.

The star appears at moments of crisis and navigation — tracking enemy ships against constellations, orienting rescue missions, framing the darkness in which the *Aldebaran* hunts the stolen *Jan Smuts* (014-011). Beta Hydrae is the silent constant above every battlefield, indifferent to the species struggling beneath its light.
