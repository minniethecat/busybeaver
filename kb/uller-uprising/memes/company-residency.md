# Company Residency

Company Residencies were the Uller Company's administrative and military footholds across the planet — fortified compounds in every major Ulleran city that functioned simultaneously as governor's offices, trade stations, barracks, and in times of crisis, as last-stand defensive positions against native uprisings (006-008, 008-035).

## Function and Structure

Each Residency housed the local Resident-Agent, the Company's chief representative in that city or region, along with administrative staff, Company troops, and communication facilities linking the outpost to the planetary government at Konkrook (006-019). The Residency compound typically included office buildings, barracks, docks for Company spacecraft, a telecast station, and defensive fortifications.

The Residency at Skilk, under Resident-Agent Jules Keaveney, exemplifies the typical layout: it housed the Resident's offices, contained a telecast station from which von Schlichten coordinated the Company's response to the uprising, and included dock facilities for ships like the *Procyon* and *Northern Lights* (008-020). The Keegark Residency, under Colonel Rodolfo MacKinnon, stood "in the middle of the city, surrounded by two hundred thousand of King Orgzild's troops and subjects" (007-048), illustrating the fundamental vulnerability of these outposts despite their fortifications.

Many Residencies included farms and agricultural operations. At Konkrook, "most of the combat contragravity and about half the Kragan Rifles had had to be committed to defend the Company farms across the Channel, on the mainland, south of the city" (007-034), indicating that Residencies were not merely administrative centers but nodes in the Company's economic infrastructure.

## The Residency Network

The Company maintained Residencies at Konkrook, Skilk, Keegark, Krink, Grank, Kwurk, and Bwork, among other locations (007-048, 008-020, 008-035, 010-001, 015-089). This network formed the nervous system of Company rule, with each Residency linked by telecast to the Governor-General's headquarters. When Governor-General Harrington was poisoned, the news was "sent out at once, as the geeks knew it would be, to every residency and trading-station on Uller, and that was the signal they'd agreed upon, probably months in advance" (008-035).

The Residency at Krink, under Resident-Agent Francis Xavier Shapiro, reported "rioting in the city and an attempted palace-revolution against King Jonkvank, and that the Residency was under attack" (007-041). Notably, at Krink "the army of King Jonkvank that had mutinied; the Sixth North Uller Native Infantry and the two companies of Zirk cavalry at Krink were still loyal" (007-041), demonstrating that a Residency's survival often depended on the loyalty of native troops rather than Terran military strength alone.

The Grank Residency under Guido Karamessinis had a different experience: King Yoorkerk "seized the Company airport and docks, taken the *Procyon* and the *Northern Lights* and put guards aboard them," but did not attack the Residency itself, "trying to play both sides at once" (008-053). This calculated neutrality — blockading rather than assaulting — illustrates how Residencies could become bargaining chips in the complex politics of the uprising.

## The Residency as Last Stand

When the uprising erupted, many Residencies became death traps. Colonel MacKinnon at Keegark sent a final message "to the effect that the geeks had penetrated his last defenses and that he was about to blow up the Residency; thereafter Keegark went off the air" (010-021). The Keegark Residency was later observed from the air as "another crater since Rodolfo MacKinnon had blown it up under him" (015-089), a self-inflicted nuclear demolition that denied the enemy victory while killing all within.

The Kwurk Residency on the eastern shore of Takkad Sea was successfully evacuated — "The entire garrison of the small Residency at Kwurk, the most northern of the eastern shore Free Cities, had arrived at Kankad's Town in two hundred-foot contragravity scows and five aircars" (010-033). The Kwurk evacuees even detoured to attack Keegark en route: two aircars "turned off at Keegark to pay their respects to King Orgzild," rocketing and machine-gunning Orgzild's nitroglycerine plant and preventing the repair of captured Company ships (010-033).

## Narrative Significance

The Company Residency embodies the paradox of Terran colonial rule on Uller: the Company's presence was simultaneously ubiquitous and precarious. Every Residency was a statement of power and a hostage to local politics. The pattern of their fall during the uprising — Keegark destroyed, Kwurk evacuated, Grank blockaded, Krink attacked — maps the geography of native grievance and the fragility of a rule that depended more on the loyalty of armed natives than on Terran military dominance.

The Residency system also enables the novel's narrative structure. Von Schlichten receives news of the uprising not as a single event but as a cascade of reports from Residency after Residency, each with its own local dynamic. This fragmented, telecast-mediated experience of crisis is central to the novel's depiction of colonial administration as an exercise in managing information from outposts one can neither fully control nor afford to abandon.
