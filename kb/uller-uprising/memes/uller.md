# Uller

Uller is the second planet of the star Beta Hydri, a G0-type solar star approximately the same size as Sol, located 21 light-years from Earth (P01-004). It is the primary colony world of the [[chartered-uller-company|Chartered Uller Company]] and home to an indigenous intelligent species of [[ulleran|Ullerans]] — silicone-based hermaphroditic beings whose biochemistry and society are shaped by the planet's extreme environment.

## Planetary Characteristics

Uller revolves around Beta Hydri in a nearly circular orbit at a distance of 100 million miles, making it somewhat colder than Earth. A year is approximately the same length as a Terran year, but a day lasts 26 hours (P01-005).

The planet's defining feature is its axial tilt: Uller's axis lies in the same plane as its orbit, producing wildly exaggerated seasons. At the poles, temperatures range from 120 degrees Celsius in summer to minus 80 degrees in winter. Only at the equator does the temperature remain relatively stable, hovering near 10 degrees Celsius year-round (P01-006). These equatorial regions are where most Ulleran civilization has concentrated.

The planet's chemistry is dominated by silicon. Calcium and chlorine are rarer than on Earth, while sodium is somewhat more common. The resulting shortage of calcium carbonate means there are few limestone deposits — practically all sedimentary rocks are sandstone-type formations. The water is slightly alkaline, tasting faintly of soap, and leaves a glassy, crackly film when it dries (P01-011). There is no coal on Uller, no petroleum — what would be coal seams on Terra are strata of silicified wood, and fallen timber petrifies within a couple of years (001-015).

## Native Ecology

Uller's native life is based on silicone (organosilicon) compounds rather than carbon. The dominant land animals possess three pairs of legs — six limbs total — to distribute their heavy, silica-dense bodies. Their blood consists of short-chain silicones, and their armor, teeth, and bones are composed of pure amorphous quartz tinted with metallic impurities (P01-017)(P01-018). The [[ulleran|Ullerans]] themselves are the intelligent apex of this evolutionary lineage.

> "The planet's chemistry was dominated by silicon. Calcium and chlorine were rarer than on Terra; sodium was somewhat more common." (P01-011)

Plant life follows a carbon-metabolism model with silicate-shell exteriors. The forests visible from the air are yellow-green with new foliage, and the characteristic dirty gray woodsmoke of Uller marks the progress of charcoal-burning operations — the planet's only natural fuel source. It takes forty years to burn the forests clear back to the flint cliffs of the mountain ranges (001-015). The Company has introduced hemlocks and Norway spruce from Terra, which make better charcoal than the silicate-heavy native wood.

## The Ullerans

The native [[ulleran|Ullerans]] stand erect on stumpy legs with broad, six-toed feet, and possess four arms — one pair from true shoulders, the other connected to a pseudo-pelvis midway down the torso. Their slate-gray rubbery skins are speckled with pinhead-sized quartz crystals formed from perspiration. Their narrow heads are distinctly saurian, with double-lidded red eyes, slit-like nostrils, and wide mouths filled with opalescent teeth (001-003). The total population of the planet is in the hundreds of millions, concentrated in the equatorial kingdoms and city-states of the Konk Isthmus region.

## Human Settlement and Company Rule

The Terran Federation explored Uller and awarded the Chartered Uller Company an exclusive economic franchise. The Company established Residencies in the major native cities, introduced nuclear power plants and [[contragravity]] technology, and began transforming the planetary ecology through introduced Terran flora and the extermination of dangerous native fauna such as the shellosaurs and [[jeel|Jeel]] cannibals. The Company maintains order through a combination of Terran military forces, [[kragan|Kragan]] mercenary regiments, and political relationships with compliant native kings. This colonial order — fragile, cost-constrained, and dependent on native cooperation — would be shattered by the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]].
