# King Orgzild

King of [[keegark|Keegark]] — a calculating [[ulleran|Ulleran]] monarch who maneuvers between diplomacy
and conspiracy, using the spaceport proposal as leverage while secretly aligning
with anti-[[terra|Terra]]n forces across the planet.

## Overview

King Orgzild of Keegark is one of the most strategically dangerous [[ulleran|Ulleran]]
monarchs in the novel. Unlike the factionally constrained kings of [[konkrook|Konkrook]]
and [[skilk|Skilk]], Orgzild rules [[keegark|Keegark]] with tight control over both his feudal
nobility and urban populace. His signature diplomatic initiative — a proposal
to build a spaceport at [[keegark|Keegark]] that would replace [[konkrook|Konkrook]] as [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s
primary base — masks a multi-layered strategy to concentrate [[terra|Terra]]n assets
where he can destroy them [002-032].

Von Schlichten's Intelligence Section identifies Orgzild as the likely source
behind [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s more sophisticated anti-[[terra|Terra]]n rhetoric — "that savage didn't
think an idea like that up for himself; he got it from somebody like Orgzild"
[006-020]. His influence extends from the [[keegark|Keegark]]an court, through Ambassador
Ghroghrank stationed at [[konkrook|Konkrook]], to [[rakkeed-the-prophet|Rakkeed the Prophet]] and [[prince-gurgurk|Gurgurk]]'s
conspiracy.

## Background

Orgzild rules [[keegark|Keegark]], one of the major northern kingdoms of [[uller|Uller]], with an
iron grip. While [[konkrook|Konkrook]] is characterized by factional politics between King
Jaikark and [[prince-gurgurk|Prince Gurgurk]], and [[skilk|Skilk]] by tensions between the feudal nobility
and urban merchants, [[keegark|Keegark]] under Orgzild is unified: "King Orgzild has things
under pretty tight control at [[keegark|Keegark]]" [006-009].

His court projects confidence: he has begun building "two new power-reactors,
to handle the additional power-demand that'll result from the increased business"
he expects from the [[spaceport|spaceport deal]] [002-038] — a calculated display of irreversible
commitment designed to pressure [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] into accepting his offer.

## Role in the Uprising

### The Spaceport Gambit

Orgzild's most visible initiative is his proposal to build a spaceport at [[keegark|Keegark]].
To [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]], it appears to be a straightforward competitive bid — [[keegark|Keegark]]
wants the prestige and economic benefit of hosting the main [[terra|Terra]]n installation,
currently at [[konkrook|Konkrook]] [002-032].

But [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] recognizes the strategic threat: "I wonder if the idea back
of that spaceport proposition isn't to get us concentrated at [[keegark|Keegark]], where
Orgzild could wipe us all out in one surprise blow" [006-010]. The plan would
have a twelve-month window — the time it takes for news to reach [[terra|Terra]] and a
[[terran-federation|Federation]] Space Navy task force to arrive [006-011].

The key question is whether Orgzild believes [[terra|Terra]] even exists: "I doubt if he
even believes there is a [[terra|Terra]]" [006-012] — suggesting Orgzild may interpret
[[terra|Terra]]n claims of distant reinforcements as bluff.

### Plutonium Accumulation

Orgzild's plutonium purchases from [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] — ostensibly for power reactors —
raise alarm when viewed alongside his wider ambitions. If he obtained a nuclear
bomb, even a small one, "he could use it for a model and construct a hundred like
it, with all the plutonium we've been handing out for power reactors" [006-031].
The implication is that [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] has been unwittingly arming its potential
destroyer.

A shipment of power-metal (plutonium) is being delivered to [[keegark|Keegark]] through
[[gorkrink|Prince Gorkrink]], Orgzild's special envoy [003-008], and the [[keegark|Keegark]]an Ambassador
conveys Orgzild's "pleasure at having obtained the plutonium" [003-015].

### The Iron Curtain

By the time the uprising begins, Orgzild has withdrawn into secrecy. Von
Schlichten notes that "Orgzild's pulled down a regular First-Century-model
iron curtain" [003-031], cutting off [[terra|Terra]]n intelligence from [[keegark|Keegark]]'s
internal affairs. When [[eric-blount|Blount]] attempts to reach [[keegark|Keegark]] to discuss the
[[spaceport|spaceport deal]], he finds [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] representatives there "in audience
with King Orgzild, and we can't reach him" [007-024].

### The Keegark Massacre

The conspiracy reaches its climax at [[keegark|Keegark]]. The Residency is surrounded by
"two hundred thousand of King Orgzild's troops and subjects" [008-020]. The
entire [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] party at Orgzild's palace is massacred [008-041], and Orgzild
seizes two [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] spaceships — the *Jan Smuts* and the *Christiaan De Wett* —
both docked at [[keegark|Keegark]] [008-047].

In the counter-offensive, [[terra|Terra]]n forces strike back: aircars rocketing and
machine-gunning [[keegark|Keegark]]ans destroy Orgzild's nitroglycerine plant [010-022],
crippling his war production capability.

## Characteristics & Traits

Orgzild represents the most sophisticated type of [[ulleran|Ulleran]] antagonist —
intelligent, patient, and capable of long-term strategic thinking that
rivals [[terra|Terra]]n planning. Unlike [[prince-gurgurk|Gurgurk]]'s impulsive conspiracy or [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s
religious fanaticism, Orgzild pursues a methodical accumulation of power
through economic leverage, diplomatic manipulation, and military preparation.

His control over [[keegark|Keegark]] contrasts sharply with the fragmented politics of
other [[ulleran|Ulleran]] kingdoms, making him uniquely dangerous. Where other kings
must balance competing factions, Orgzild commands. His "iron curtain"
indicates an understanding of information warfare that other [[ulleran|Ulleran]]
leaders lack.
