# Kingdom of Kankad's Town

The Kingdom of Kankad's Town was a Kragan political entity on Uller, ruled by King Kankad and distinguished from the other native kingdoms by its close alliance with the Chartered Uller Company. The kingdom represented the most successful example of Terran-native collaboration on Uller — a relationship built on mutual military advantage, educational investment, and the Kragans' unique status as the Company's preferred native partner.

## The Kragan People

The Kragans are a distinct Ulleran ethnic group, physically and culturally differentiated from the majority Ulleran population that dominated kingdoms like Konkrook and Keegark. The Company's preference for Kragans as laborers, soldiers, and intermediaries — a preference rooted in the Kragans' reputation for reliability and martial capability — created a structural incentive for Kragan loyalty that operated independently of any ideological alignment with Terran rule.

Kankad's kingdom was the political expression of this privileged relationship. Where other kings chafed under Company suzerainty, Kankad had concluded — correctly, as the uprising would prove — that Kragan interests were better served by cooperation than by rebellion.

## Military Capability

Kankad's kingdom possessed a unique military asset: the only non-Terran artillery above 75mm caliber on the entire planet (005-045). This artillery park was the product of decades of Company favoritism — the Kragans had been permitted to acquire and maintain weapons that no other native power was trusted to possess. The guns were operated by Kragan crews trained to Terran standards, capable of indirect fire support, counter-battery operations, and coordinated bombardment in support of infantry maneuvers.

The artillery's political significance outweighed its military utility. The mere existence of Kragan heavy guns demonstrated to the other kingdoms that the Company's prohibition on native heavy weapons was negotiable — for those who proved their loyalty. This created resentment among the excluded kingdoms while reinforcing Kragan dependence on continued Company goodwill: the guns required ammunition, spare parts, and technical support that only the Company could provide.

## Education and Lingua Terra

Kankad's kingdom was the primary site of Company-sponsored education for native Ullerans. Schools in Kankad's Town taught young Kragans to read Lingua Terra — the Terran common language — using textbooks printed in Johannesburg, Terra (005-045). This educational investment had both practical and ideological dimensions. Practically, Lingua Terra literacy enabled Kragan soldiers and administrators to operate more effectively within the Company's command structure. Ideologically, it exposed successive generations of Kragan elites to Terran cultural values and created a shared linguistic community that bridged the biological communication barrier.

King Kankad himself conversed directly with von Schlichten in Lingua Terra, without need for the "geek-speaker" devices that Terrans required to produce Ulleran speech sounds (005-044). This direct communication — each speaking his own language while understanding the other's — symbolized the partnership's depth and set Kankad apart from rulers like Orgzild and Jaikark, who communicated with Terrans only through interpreters or mechanical aids.

## Role in the Uprising

Kankad's kingdom played the decisive role in the Battle of Konkrook Reservation. When the besieged Company compound faced collapse under sustained native attack, Kankad personally directed the airlift of Kragan reinforcements into the compound using contragravity vehicles (010-007)(010-015). The intervention broke the siege and preserved the Company's command-and-control capability — without which the counter-offensive culminating in the bombing of Keegark would have been impossible.

Kankad's decision to intervene was not merely a military calculation but a political bet on the Company's survival. Had the uprising succeeded, Kankad and his kingdom would have faced retribution from the victorious rebel coalition as collaborators. That Kankad made this bet — and that it paid off — reflected both his assessment of the military balance and his kingdom's deep structural investment in the Company system.

## Legacy

Kankad's kingdom emerged from the uprising as the preeminent native power on Uller — the other major kingdoms had been either destroyed (Keegark), discredited (Konkrook), or neutralized. The post-uprising settlement would necessarily be built around Kragan cooperation, extending the pattern that had defined the Company-Kragan relationship since first contact.
