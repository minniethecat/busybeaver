# Ulleran Modesty Taboos

Ulleran cultural norms around modesty and sexuality differ radically from Terran expectations, creating persistent cross-cultural friction. These taboos are not merely exotic details — they represent the deeper incommensurability of two species whose biology and social evolution have produced fundamentally different moral universes.

## The Nature of Ulleran Modesty

Ulleran modesty taboos center on the exposure of certain body parts and the performance of certain biological functions, but their content is the inverse of Terran expectations. Paula Quinton explains: "it's their version of modesty... Like some of our sex-inhibitions, which they can't even begin to understand" (P02-040). The Terrans find Ulleran taboos arbitrary and incomprehensible; the Ullerans find Terran taboos equally so.

The biological basis is important. All Ullerans are bisexual — "since Ullerans were bisexual, the total population, less the senile, crippled, and very young, could be organized and armed for military operations" (008-020). Each individual can both bear and father young. The concept of sex-based social roles, so central to human (and particularly mid-20th-century human) culture, has no Ulleran equivalent. Ullerans cannot map Terran gender categories onto their own experience any more than Terrans can understand Ulleran modesty norms.

## Cross-Cultural Misunderstanding

These taboos create practical problems for colonial administration. Terran officers and administrators, operating on human cultural assumptions, routinely offend Ulleran sensibilities without knowing they have done so. Conversely, Ulleran behavior that Terrans interpret as rude or bizarre is often the expression of cultural norms they cannot perceive.

The geek-speaker device — a mechanical aid that allows Terrans to produce Ulleran speech sounds — is described as "one of those damn gags" that must be put in the mouth (P02-041). The physical discomfort and indignity of using these devices adds another layer to the communication barrier, reinforcing the separation between the species even when they are ostensibly conversing.

## Narrative Significance

The modesty taboos serve as a concrete illustration of the novel's broader theme: that the gap between Terran and Ulleran cannot be bridged by goodwill or cultural exchange. The two species are biologically and psychologically different in ways that make mutual understanding a matter of approximation rather than achievement. Colonial governance operates in the space between these two incommensurable worlds — making decisions that affect Ulleran lives based on Terran categories that may not apply.
