# Kankad's Town

Kankad's Town was the ancient capital of the [[kragan|Kragan]] kingdom on [[uller|Uller]], ruled by [[king-kankad|King Kankad]]. Situated on the landward edge of a vast swamp between [[konkrook|Konkrook]] and [[skilk|Skilk]], it was one of the oldest continuously inhabited settlements on Uller and served as the primary source of Kragan mercenary forces for the Chartered Uller Company during the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]].

## Geography & Layout

The city occupied a brown-gray rise on the landward edge of a swamp, visible from the air as a compact fortified settlement "that had been there for thousands of years" (005-026). Defensive stone stairways led from the city walls to emplacements of long 90 mm guns, which were among the heaviest artillery pieces on Uller outside Terran control — as [[carlos-von-schlichten|Carlos von Schlichten]] noted, "the guns of King Kankad's Town were the only artillery above 75 mm on Uller in non-Terran hands" (005-045). The city's airport accommodated contragravity vessels, including the armed troopship *Aldebaran* that regularly stopped to embark Kragan infantry battalions.

The city's architecture reflected its antiquity and Kragan character. Unlike the Terran-style buildings of [[gongonk-island|Gongonk Island]] or the flint-and-basalt construction of [[konkrook|Konkrook]], Kankad's Town was built of local materials adapted to the swamp-edge environment, with stone fortifications that dated back millennia.

## Political & Social Structure

Kankad's Town was the seat of King Kankad, the Kragan monarch whose family had ruled the city for generations. The Kragan social structure was clan-based and martial — the city's population provided the bulk of the Kragan Rifles, the mercenary infantry that formed the backbone of Company military forces on Uller. King Kankad maintained a telecast link to Konkrook, allowing direct communication with von Schlichten and the Company command structure (008-018).

The Kragan court operated with a degree of technological sophistication unusual for native Ulleran societies. Kankad himself wore an opal-jeweled grin-plate and wristwatch, signaling both wealth and Terran cultural influence (005-041). His people manufactured contragravity detectors at "our laboratory and reaction-plant," indicating indigenous industrial capability (013-049).

## Strategic & Military Significance

Kankad's Town was the Company's most reliable source of native military manpower. A typical troop movement involved the *Aldebaran* "stopping at King Kankad's Town to pick up a battalion of Kragan Rifles" en route to trouble spots such as the polar mines or Konkrook (004-045). During the uprising, King Kankad organized an improvised airlift that delivered "close to two thousand fresh Kragans on Gongonk Island and the mainland farms" in successive waves, a decisive reinforcement that prevented the fall of the Terran command center (010-039).

The city's artillery — long 90 mm guns — provided the only meaningful native heavy-weapons capability on the planet, and Kankad placed these at von Schlichten's disposal. The Kragan contragravity scows operating from the city could each carry a hundred troops, enabling rapid force projection across hundreds of miles (008-061).

## In the Uprising

Kankad's Town played a critical role throughout the Uprising. After the initial mutiny at Konkrook, von Schlichten ordered the *Aldebaran* to "take her to Kankad's Town, at once" to collect Kragan reinforcements for the beleaguered Gongonk Island garrison (008-061). The garrison of the small Residency at [[kwurk|Kwurk]], forced to abandon their post, successfully evacuated "to Kankad's Town in two hundred-foot contragravity scows," demonstrating the city's role as a safe harbor for allied forces (010-022).

As the conflict escalated to nuclear warfare, Kankad's Town itself became a target. Von Schlichten grimly assessed the strategic calculus: "Kankad's Town will get the second bomb; Orgzild won't dare leave the Kragans after he's wiped us out" (013-064). The city was placed under Sky-Spy surveillance, which reported "normal contragravity traffic between Keegark and the farming-villages around — aircars, lorries, a few scows — but nothing suspicious" in the lead-up to the nuclear phase of the war (014-012). The city survived the conflict, and Kankad was included in von Schlichten's inner circle of sealed-beam telecast communications alongside Air-Commodore Hargreaves and other senior Terran commanders (014-042).
