# King Jaikark XVII

King Jaikark XVII was the [[ulleran|Ulleran]] monarch of [[konkrook|Konkrook]], a [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] puppet whose drug addiction and weak rule enabled his Prime Minister [[prince-gurgurk|Gurgurk]] to build a political organization aimed at usurping the throne.

## Overview

Jaikark XVII ruled [[konkrook|Konkrook]] — the planetary capital and seat of [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] power on [[uller|Uller]] — as a subsidized puppet of the [[chartered-uller-company|Chartered Uller Company]]. His court was notorious for widespread drug addiction, and his political authority was undermined by his Prime Minister [[prince-gurgurk|Gurgurk]], who was "building quite a political organization" with the apparent goal of overthrowing him （003-039）. Jaikark's reign exemplified [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s preferred model of [[ulleran|Ulleran]] governance: a pliable monarch maintained by [[terra|Terra]]n subsidies and protected by [[kragan|Kragan]] mercenaries.

## Background

### Species & Biology

As an [[ulleran|Ulleran]], Jaikark shared the silicon-based physiology common to his species: hermaphroditic, cold-blooded, with [[silicon-based-life|quartz-speckled skin]] and a four-armed anatomy. His physical and mental deterioration from drug addiction was evident: [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] substituted mentally that if Jaikark "weren't doped to the ears," his absence during the riot was more explicable （004-026）. The native drug used by Jaikark and his court circle combined "the effects of hashish, heroin and yohimbine" （004-026）, rendering the monarch politically inert.

### Rise to Power

Jaikark XVII's rule depended entirely on [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] support. The [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] maintained "too much of an investment in King Jaikark" to permit his removal （003-041）, subsidizing his regime with payments that kept the [[konkrook|Konkrook]]an state functioning as a [[terra|Terra]]n proxy. His Spear of State — an ornate weapon with a three-foot blade carried as the proxy-presence of the monarch — was borne before [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] officials at state ceremonies, symbolizing Jaikark's formal authority even as actual power resided with [[prince-gurgurk|Gurgurk]] and [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] （004-013）.

## Role in the Uprising

### The Riot and Assassination Attempt

Jaikark was conspicuously absent during the anti-[[terra|Terra]]n riot that culminated in [[sid-harrington|Governor-General Harrington]]'s assassination. His Household Guards were deployed alongside [[kragan-rifles|Kragan Rifles]] and [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] airjeeps to control the [[konkrook|Konkrook]] streets, but Jaikark himself — incapacitated by drugs — "probably hadn't even heard of the riot" （004-026）. The political vacuum created by his absence allowed [[prince-gurgurk|Gurgurk]] to present himself as the effective [[konkrook|Konkrook]]an authority.

### Gurgurk's Coup

When the uprising spread to [[konkrook|Konkrook]], [[prince-gurgurk|Gurgurk]] launched his long-planned palace coup. The [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] received a report of "a revolt's broken out, presumably headed by [[prince-gurgurk|Gurgurk]]; Household Guards either mutinied or wiped out by the mutineers" （008-005）. Only twenty [[kragan-rifles|Kragan Rifles]] — loaned to Jaikark as a personal guard — held the approaches to the King's apartments alongside "about a dozen of Jaikark's courtiers and their personal retainers" （008-005）.

### Evacuation

Von Schlichten ordered Jaikark's evacuation to Gongonk Island, [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s island stronghold. The order was explicit: "Don't commit more than one company of [[kragan|Kragans]] and ten airjeeps and four combat-cars, and tell them to evacuate Jaikark and his followers and our [[kragan|Kragans]] to Gongonk Island" （008-008）. The limited commitment reflected [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s cold calculation: "If we can pull Jaikark's bacon off the fire, [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] can dictate its own terms to him afterward; if Jaikark's killed, we'll have [[prince-gurgurk|Gurgurk]]'s head off for it, and then take over [[konkrook|Konkrook]]" （008-011）.

## Characteristics & Traits

Jaikark XVII embodied [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s preferred puppet-monarch model: a ruler maintained in power through subsidies, protected by [[terra|Terra]]n-funded [[kragan|Kragan]] mercenaries, and rendered politically passive through drug addiction. His court was defined by its toxic combination of opulence and incapacity — nobles "carried about as much ironmongery as the Prime Minister" （004-016）, their ceremonial weaponry symbolizing a martial tradition that Jaikark himself could not fulfill. His Spear of State, carried by the honor guard, represented royal authority more than the monarch himself ever could.

## Death / Post-novel

Jaikark's ultimate fate after the evacuation to Gongonk Island is not detailed in the novel. The [[chartered-uller-company|Company]]'s willingness to sacrifice him — [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s calculation that Jaikark's death would simply clear the way for "taking over [[konkrook|Konkrook]]" — suggests his continued reign depended on the outcome of the broader military campaign.
