# Kingdom of Konkrook

The Kingdom of Konkrook was the most politically complex of the Ulleran native kingdoms — the site of the Chartered Uller Company's planetary headquarters and the largest Company Reservation, ruled by the puppet king Jaikark but riven by a succession conspiracy that made it the uprising's primary battleground.

## Political Structure

Konkrook's political structure exemplified the Company's preferred model of indirect rule: a native monarchy that exercised traditional authority over its subjects while the Company exercised real power over the monarchy. King Jaikark was, in Company parlance, a "tame" king — installed and maintained by Company support, his legitimacy derived less from traditional Ulleran sources than from the Company's willingness to enforce his decrees (002-012).

This arrangement produced a court culture of intrigue that the Company's political officers — notably Blount and Grinell — consistently underestimated. The Konkrook court was divided between Jaikark's loyalists, who accepted Company domination as the price of their privileges, and a faction centered on Prince Gurgurk, the king's son, who sought to restore genuine native sovereignty through alliance with Rakkeed's religious movement (002-010).

Gurgurk's position was paradoxical: as heir to a puppet throne, he was simultaneously the beneficiary and the prisoner of the Company system. His conspiracy against his father was also, necessarily, a conspiracy against the Company — a fact that gave Gurgurk's ambitions a nationalist dimension that the Company's analysts, who saw him as merely ambitious, failed to appreciate.

## Alliance with Rakkeed

Gurgurk's alliance with Rakkeed the Prophet represented the fusion of political and religious opposition to Company rule (002-013). Rakkeed's psionic abilities — his capacity to project emotionally compelling visions into the minds of his followers — gave the movement a reach that conventional political organizing could not match. Gurgurk provided what Rakkeed lacked: access to the levers of state power, the ability to coordinate military action across kingdom boundaries, and the legitimacy that came from association with an established royal house.

The alliance was consummated through the Keegarkan ambassador's residence in Konkrook, where Rakkeed was hosted during his travels and where the planning for the uprising's opening phase was conducted (002-019). The ambassador's residence — extraterritorial under Ulleran diplomatic conventions — provided a secure venue for the conspiracy that the Company's surveillance apparatus could not penetrate without risking a diplomatic incident.

## The Konkrook Reservation

Konkrook's most significant physical feature was the Company Reservation — a fortified compound housing the planetary administration, military command, communications center, and residences for senior Company personnel. The Reservation was, in effect, a colonial city-state embedded within a native kingdom, its walls marking the boundary between Terran jurisdiction and Ulleran sovereignty.

The Reservation's location in Konkrook rather than in Keegark or Kankad's Town reflected the kingdom's historical role as the Company's preferred political partner — a role that made Konkrook the natural center of Company operations and, when the uprising came, the natural target of rebel military action (010-039).

## The Uprising and Its Aftermath

The uprising struck Konkrook with particular force because the kingdom was both the symbolic center of Company rule and the most internally divided of the native polities. Prince Gurgurk's conspiracy had thoroughly penetrated the Company's native-troops barracks and the domestic staff of the Reservation — the assassination of Governor Harrington was carried out by a Konkrook servant, and the mutiny that followed was organized through Gurgurk's network (007-032).

The siege of the Konkrook Reservation tested the Company's defenses to their limit. The compound held — narrowly, and only with Kragan reinforcement — but the cost in ammunition, casualties, and political capital was immense. After the uprising, Konkrook's political structure was effectively dismantled. Gurgurk was dead or captured; Jaikark's authority, never strong, was irreparably compromised; and the Company's willingness to govern through the Konkrook monarchy had been shattered by the revelation that the king's own son had organized the rebellion.
