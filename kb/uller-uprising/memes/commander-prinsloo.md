# Commander Dirk Prinsloo

Commander Dirk Prinsloo captained [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] spaceship *Aldebaran* during the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]], executing the decisive orbital bombardment of [[keegark|Keegark]] and leading the search for [[orgzild|Orgzild]]'s nuclear-armed freighters.

## Overview

Dirk Prinsloo was the commanding officer of the *Aldebaran*, one of the [[chartered-uller-company|Chartered Uller Company]]'s armed spaceships stationed in the [[uller|Uller]] system. A pragmatic and professionally competent naval officer, Prinsloo participated in the pre-uprising strategic discussions regarding [[orgzild|King Orgzild]]'s intentions, fought in the defense of [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] House during the [[konkrook|Konkrook]] riots, and — most critically — commanded the *Aldebaran* in the orbital bombardment that destroyed the Boer-class freighter *De Wett* at [[keegark|Keegark]] and in the subsequent search for the missing nuclear-armed freighters *Jan Smuts* and *Oom Paul Kruger*.

## Background

Prinsloo held a commander's commission in [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s naval arm and captained the *Aldebaran*, a spaceship capable of orbital operations and contragravity atmospheric flight. Prior to the uprising, he was stationed at [[konkrook|Konkrook]] and participated in high-level strategic discussions among [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s senior military leadership.

During a pre-uprising strategy session, Prinsloo assessed [[orgzild|King Orgzild]]'s potential for nuclear aggression with characteristic directness: "Oh, [[orgzild|Orgzild]] wouldn't be crazy enough to try anything like that. He'd get away with it for just twelve months — the time it would take to get the news to [[terra|Terra]] and for a [[terran-federation|Federation]] Space Navy task-force to get here. And then, there'd be little bits of radioactive [[geek]] floating around this system as far out as the orbit of [[beta-hydrae|Beta Hydrae]] VII" （010-012）. His analysis reflected both awareness of the [[terran-federation|Federation]]'s ultimate military superiority and the practical vulnerability of [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s position.

Prinsloo also demonstrated technical literacy in discussing the [[ulleran|Ullerans]]' understanding of [[interstellar-travel|space travel]]: "Well, it takes six months for a ship to go between here and Nif," he explained, noting that "because of the [[interstellar-travel|hyperdrive]] effects, the experienced time of the voyage, inside the ship, is of the order of three weeks. Taking that as the figure, he'd estimate the distance at about a quarter-million miles, assuming the velocity as being the speed of one of our contragravity-ships here on [[uller|Uller]]. I'm assuming he doesn't even know there is a [[interstellar-travel|hyperdrive]]" （010-020）.

## Role in the Uprising

### Defense of Company House

When the uprising erupted in [[konkrook|Konkrook]], Prinsloo was among the [[terra|Terra]]ns besieged at [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] House. After the [[kragan|Kragans]] under Native-Major Kormork fought their way to the building, [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] observed "Commander Prinsloo of the *Aldebaran*" among the survivors emerging from the beleaguered position alongside Jules Keaveney and his wife, Harry Quong, and Hassan Bogdanoff （014-100）. His survival through the initial assault meant [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] retained one of its most critical naval assets — a qualified spaceship commander.

### Orbital Bombardment of Keegark

Prinsloo's most significant military contribution came when he took the *Aldebaran* to [[keegark|Keegark]], [[orgzild|Orgzild]]'s capital, to execute a bombardment mission. Brigadier-General M'zangwe subsequently reported: "Prinsloo caught the *De Wett* in the docks there and smashed her, but the *Jan Smuts* got away, and we haven't been able to locate the *Oom Paul Kruger*, either. They're probably both on the Eastern Shore, gathering up reinforcements for [[orgzild|Orgzild]]" （015-021）. The destruction of the *De Wett* was a significant blow to [[orgzild|Orgzild]]'s naval capability, though the escape of the other two freighters — both potentially carrying nuclear weapons — created the central military crisis of the late-uprising phase.

### Search for the Missing Freighters

As [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s forces prepared for the final confrontation with [[orgzild|Orgzild]], the *Sky-Spy* reconnaissance platform detected a ship lifting from [[keegark|Keegark]], "presumed one of the Boer-class freighters, either the *Jan Smuts* or the *Oom Paul Kruger*." The ship had been "camouflaged since the time Commander Prinsloo first bombarded [[keegark|Keegark]] with the *Aldebaran*" （015-054）. Von Schlichten immediately ordered Prinsloo to "launch airboats and make a search," cautioning him to "be careful of radiation, but to take no chances on any of the *Gaucho*'s complement being still alive and in need of help" （015-058）. The *Gaucho* — a [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] cutter — had been destroyed intercepting one of the escaping freighters, and Prinsloo was tasked with the grim responsibility of searching for survivors while maintaining the hunt for the nuclear-armed vessels.

## Characteristics & Traits

Prinsloo combined naval professionalism with blunt strategic assessment. His pre-uprising analysis of [[orgzild|King Orgzild]]'s potential nuclear ambitions demonstrated clear-eyed realism — he correctly identified both the existential threat and the twelve-month window before [[terran-federation|Federation]] intervention. In combat, he executed his orders with precision, successfully destroying one enemy freighter and methodically prosecuting the search for the others. His relationship with the broader [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] command structure appears to have been one of mutual professional respect; [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] addressed him directly and trusted him with critical independent operations.
