# Ulleran Hermaphroditism

Ulleran hermaphroditism is the biological condition in which every Ulleran individual is a true, functional hermaphrodite — possessing the reproductive capacity to both produce ova and fertilize the ova of others. This trait is one of the most profound biological differences between the silicon-based Ullerans and carbon-based Terrans, and it shapes every aspect of Ulleran society, from family structure to military mobilization.

## Biological Basis

The Ulleran reproductive system is unified into a single gender. As described in the initial intelligence briefing on Ulleran biology:

> "they had practically no sex-inhibitions; they were all of the same gender, true, functional, hermaphrodites. Any individual among them could bear young, or fertilize the ova of any other individual." (001-003)

This biological arrangement is fundamentally alien to Terran experience. There are no male or female Ullerans — no sexual dimorphism, no gender roles rooted in reproductive specialization. The social implications are vast: concepts like marriage, paternity, and gendered division of labor that structure Terran societies have no Ulleran equivalents.

Unlike sequential hermaphroditism found in some Terran species (where an organism changes sex during its lifetime), Ulleran hermaphroditism is simultaneous and permanent — every individual is reproductively complete at all times.

## Military Implications

The most significant narrative consequence of Ulleran hermaphroditism is its effect on military potential. During the Konkrook uprising, von Schlichten makes a grim calculation:

> "Since Ullerans were bisexual, the total population, less the senile, crippled, and very young, was the military potential." (015-043)

This is a staggering strategic reality. On Terra, military-eligible populations are typically limited to adult males (in societies with gendered militaries) or to a portion of the adult population excluding those needed for child-rearing. On Uller, every healthy adult can both fight and reproduce — there is no "home front" gender kept out of combat, no reproductive bottleneck that limits mobilization. When King Orgzild of Keegark surrounds the Residency with "two hundred thousand" troops, that figure represents nearly the entire adult population of the city.

For Company forces used to fighting on worlds where population and military potential are related but not identical, this biological reality is a constant strategic headache. The Kragans — the Company's mercenary allies — represent one of the few native forces the Terrans can rely on, precisely because their loyalty has been cultivated through education and shared interests rather than biological circumstance.

## Social Organization

The lack of gender distinction reshapes Ulleran social structures in ways that the novel only partially explores:

**Babysitting duty**: Von Schlichten recalls that "it had taken some time before he had become accustomed to the detailing of a noncom and a couple of privates out of each platoon for babysitting duty" (001-003). Since any soldier could become pregnant at any time — there being no gender barrier to conception — military units had to accommodate infant care as a routine part of operations. This is presented not as a weakness but as a logistical reality that Company forces learned to manage.

**Modesty and clothing**: Ullerans wear no clothing "either for warmth or modesty" (001-003). Their cold-blooded physiology eliminates the need for thermal protection across a vast temperature range (from 120°C to -100°C), and the absence of sexual dimorphism eliminates the modesty taboos that drive clothing conventions in most Terran cultures. The Ulleran "uniform" consists merely of the Company emblem stencil-painted on chest and back.

**Sexual psychology**: The novel notes that "on Uller most of the fundamental terms of Terran psychology were meaningless" (001-004) because the sexual drives and anxieties that Freudian psychology placed at the center of human behavior simply have no Ulleran equivalent. At the same time, the Ulleran psyche has its own complex landscape — "the average Ulleran probably had complexes and neuroses that would have had Freud talking to himself" (001-004).

## Narrative Significance

Ulleran hermaphroditism is more than biological worldbuilding — it is a narrative device for estranging the reader from Terran assumptions about sex and gender. Piper uses the Ullerans' unified gender to suggest that many features of human society that we take as "natural" are in fact contingent on our particular reproductive biology.

The military implications of hermaphroditism also serve the plot: the sheer scale of native forces that the Company faces is partially explained by the fact that every Ulleran adult is a potential combatant. This raises the stakes of the uprising and makes the Company's reliance on Kragan mercenaries and technological superiority all the more critical.
