# Bombing of Keegark

The bombing of Keegark was the final military operation of the Uller Uprising — a nuclear strike that destroyed the capital city of the Kingdom of Keegark and killed approximately 150,000 native Ullerans in three detonations (015-034). The strike, personally ordered and led by General Carlos von Schlichten, ended the uprising through the application of overwhelming force but at a moral cost that would define the colonial legacy on Uller.

## The Mission

The bombing mission was conducted by the cutter *Elmoran*, a contragravity military vessel capable of atmospheric flight and precision strike operations. Von Schlichten himself boarded the *Elmoran* for the mission, accompanied by Paula Quinton — a choice that reflected both his determination to personally bear responsibility and his refusal to delegate the moral weight of the attack (015-011).

The primary weapon was the Dire Dawn bomb — one of the nuclear devices assembled under the emergency Operation Hildegarde project. Assembled from reverse-engineered designs extracted from a historical romance novel, the bomb was an improvised weapon built under desperate conditions (014-065). Its yield was sufficient to destroy a city; its design was barely adequate to deliver it.

## The Strike

Three nuclear bombs were detonated over Keegark in rapid succession. The first struck the city center; the second and third targeted military installations and industrial districts on the periphery (015-034). The combined effect was catastrophic. The thermal pulse ignited fires across the city; the blast wave flattened structures for kilometers from each ground zero; the radiation effects, though less immediately visible, would persist long after the fires burned out.

The death toll was approximately 150,000 — a number that represented a significant fraction of Keegark's urban population and an even larger fraction of the kingdom's political and military leadership (015-034). King Orgzild, architect of the nuclear conspiracy that had driven the uprising, was almost certainly among the dead, though the complete destruction of the city center made individual identification impossible.

## Von Schlichten's Decision

The decision to use nuclear weapons against a native population was not made lightly, but it was made quickly. Von Schlichten's strategic calculus was brutal and clear: the Keegarkans had nuclear weapons and had demonstrated the will to use them. If Keegark was not destroyed, Keegark would destroy the Company's forces and potentially escalate to off-world targets. The moral calculus was more troubling. Von Schlichten had, throughout his career, positioned himself as the Company officer who understood and respected native cultures — his decision to incinerate a city of 150,000 Ullerans was a contradiction he could not resolve, only accept.

After the strike, surveying the destruction, von Schlichten made his final judgment: "This must be the last nuclear bombing on Uller" (015-044). It was not a policy statement but a personal vow — a recognition that the weapon he had just used was too terrible for repeated application, even against an enemy that had sought the same weapon for the same purpose.

## Aftermath

The bombing of Keegark ended the organized military phase of the uprising. With Keegark destroyed and Orgzild dead, the rebel coalition lost its technological edge and its coordinating center. The other kingdoms, confronted with the demonstrated willingness of the Company to use nuclear weapons, sued for peace or withdrew to passive resistance.

The bombing also ended von Schlichten's career as a colonial administrator in any meaningful sense. A man who had destroyed a city could not govern the survivors. The political resolution of the uprising — the treaties, the reconstruction, the renegotiation of Company-native relations — fell to others.
