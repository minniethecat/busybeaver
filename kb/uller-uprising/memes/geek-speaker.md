# Geek-Speaker

A prosthetic false palate and tongue-clicker worn in the mouth, officially designated the "enunciator, Ulleran," that enables Terran speakers to produce the phonemes of the Ulleran language — a communication bridge between two species with radically different vocal anatomies (001-013, 012-022).

## Technical Design

The device consists of a false palate and a tongue-clicker mechanism, small enough to be carried in a coat pocket and inserted or removed as needed (001-013). Its colloquial name, "geek-speaker," derives from the Terran slang term "geek" for Ullerans. The device works by mechanically simulating the tongue and palate movements of the saurian Ulleran oral cavity, which Terrans cannot replicate with their own anatomy (012-022). The functional principle is purely mechanical — it is a passive prosthetic requiring no power source, making it reliable in any environment on Uller.

When not in use, the device is removed from the mouth and stored. Users typically carry it in a coat pocket; von Schlichten is described as disgorging and pouching his geek-speaker between conversations (001-033), and removing it to wipe and pouch it after extended use (010-040).

## Ulleran Cultural Context

Use of the geek-speaker without a handkerchief for concealment is considered "the most shocking sort of bad manners" among most Ulleran peoples, who observe a strict eating-and-mouth taboo (005-010). The convention requires that any object entering the mouth be hidden behind a handkerchief — a token-concealment custom that even senior Company officials violate at their social peril.

Kragan culture is a notable exception. King Kankad, the Kragan ruler, regarded von Schlichten openly inserting his geek-speaker "as a matter of course" (005-010), demonstrating the Kragans' pragmatic attitude toward cross-species communication. Paula Quinton, a sociographic researcher, was "as much surprised at Kankad's disregard for the eating taboo as she had been at von Schlichten's open flouting of the convention of concealment" (005-023).

## Practical Usage

The device is employed in virtually every formal interaction between Terrans and Ullerans. Governor-General Harrington, von Schlichten, and Eric Blount all inserted their geek-speakers before receiving the Konkrookan notables in the Audience Hall (001-013). Von Schlichten uses it before entering a telecast booth to ensure his pronunciation is clear (007-011), and even contemplates — but wisely reconsiders — using it to pronounce "Niccolo Machiavelli" to an Ulleran audience, judging that giving them *The Prince* in translation would be inadvisable (011-036).

The geek-speaker was not needed for all Ullerans; Dr. Murillo's Kragan assistant Gorkrink "understood Lingua Terra perfectly" and could be spoken to "without using a geek-speaker" (011-043), indicating that some Ullerans, particularly Kragans, could learn to understand spoken Terran languages.

## Narrative Significance

The geek-speaker functions as more than a translation device — it is a persistent marker of the fundamental biological divide between Terrans and Ullerans. Every diplomatic encounter, interrogation, and telecast address is mediated through this prosthetic, reminding both species of their mutual alienness. The device's colloquial name, incorporating the slur "geek," embodies the casual contempt that characterizes Company-native relations, while its technical designation "enunciator, Ulleran" reflects the bureaucratic pretense of scientific neutrality.

Von Schlichten's easy familiarity with the device — inserting, removing, pouching, and wiping it in mid-crisis — signals his long experience on Uller and pragmatic acceptance of the compromises required to rule a species he does not respect. His moment of restraint in not using it to give the Ullerans Machiavelli (011-036) is a rare instance of the geek-speaker being withheld, underscoring its role as a gatekeeper of Terran knowledge: some things are better left untranslated.
