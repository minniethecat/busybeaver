**Silicone metabolism** is the unique biochemical process by which the dominant animal life of Uller derives energy and builds tissue, based on silicone-chain compounds rather than the carbon-based chemistry of Terran life, and fundamentally shaping the physiology, ecology, and vulnerability of all Ulleran organisms (P01-014, P01-017).

## Overview

The evolutionary biology of Uller diverged from Earth's carbon-based model due to the planet's abundance of silicone (silicon-oxygen compounds) in the primordial environment. As described in the scientific preface to the novel, "because of the abundance of silicone, there was a strong tendency for the microscopic organisms to develop silicate exoskeletons, like diatoms" (P01-014). This early chemical bias set Ulleran evolution on a path fundamentally different from terrestrial biology, ultimately producing the silicone-animal dominance that characterizes the planet's present ecosystem.

Silicone metabolism is distinct from simple silicon-based life. While the latter term describes organisms that incorporate silicon into their structure, silicone metabolism specifically refers to the use of silicone polymer chains — molecules with silicon-oxygen backbones and organic side groups — as the primary structural and energetic material. This distinction is crucial to understanding Ulleran physiology and its vulnerabilities.

## Evolutionary Development

The transition from carbon-based to silicone-dominated life occurred in stages. The earliest organisms were carbon-based with silicate exoskeletons, analogous to Earth's diatoms (P01-014). A transitional phase produced "organisms with silicone tissues and water body fluids, resembling the earthly amphibians" — a type now "practically extinct" save for a few surviving segmented-worm-like species in the backwaters of Uller's central seas (P01-016).

The critical evolutionary breakthrough came when these transitional organisms "began to get short-chain silicones into their circulatory systems, held in solution by OH or NH2 groups on the ends and branches of the chains" (P01-017). The proportion of silicone compounds in their body fluids increased until water was "a minor and then a missing constituent." This produced the first pure silicone animals — creatures whose tissues, circulatory fluids, and structural elements were all silicone-based.

These pure silicone animals proved "practically indestructible" compared to their carbon-based competitors. They "annihilated all but the smaller of the carbon animals, and drove the compromise types into odd corners as relics" (P01-018). From fish-like ancestors with large swim-bladders to compensate for the higher density of silicone tissues, they evolved into the land animals that now dominate Uller — including the intelligent Ulleran species itself.

## Physiological Implications

Silicone metabolism confers several distinctive physiological traits. All Ulleran animal life is cold-blooded, "with a negligible difference between body and ambient temperatures" (P01-019). Because silicones do not freeze or stiffen with cold, silicone animals can remain mobile at temperatures that would incapacitate carbon-based organisms. Conversely, they tolerate extreme heat without protein denaturation, allowing them to range from the temperate equatorial zone to the scorching polar regions.

The metabolic process itself involves consuming carbon-based plants, dehydrating them, and "converting their silicious outer bark and carbonaceous interiors into silicones" (P01-022). The waste products are instructive: carbon and hydrogen are exhaled as CO2 and H2O, while the silicone component is deposited as silicon dioxide (SiO2), which forms "more teeth and armor" — explaining the quartz-speckled appearance of Ulleran mounts and the siliceous character of native animal integuments.

The most critical vulnerability of silicone metabolism is its incompatibility with fluorine. As Paula Quinton notes, employing Ullerans "with silicone body-tissues where they're going to breathe fluorine-tainted air" is ethically questionable (004-023). Fluorine reacts aggressively with silicone compounds, breaking the silicon-oxygen polymer backbone — a chemical vulnerability that has no analog in carbon-based biology and that the Company's industrial processes constantly threaten to exploit.

## Narrative Significance

Silicone metabolism is more than scientific backdrop — it is the biological foundation of the novel's central power asymmetry. The Ullerans are chemically vulnerable in ways that Terrans are not, and this vulnerability is inextricably linked to the Company's industrial presence. Every handling machine, every nuclear mining operation, every orbital shuttle that leaks trace fluorine into the atmosphere represents an existential threat to Ulleran life that Terrans can literally afford to ignore.

This biological dimension deepens the novel's colonial allegory. Terran domination of Uller is not merely political and military — it operates at the molecular level. The Company's processes are chemically toxic to the native population in ways that Terrans, with their carbon-based biology, cannot fully empathize with. The silicone-carbon divide becomes a metaphor for the fundamental incomprehension between colonizer and colonized.
