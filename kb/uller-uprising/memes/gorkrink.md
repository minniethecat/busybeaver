# Prince Gorkrink

A [[keegark|Keegark]]an prince who serves as [[orgzild|King Orgzild]]'s special envoy — and whose
intelligence-gathering on [[niflheim|Niflheim]] about plutonium's military applications
sets the [[ulleran|Ulleran]] nuclear weapons program in motion.

## Overview

Prince Gorkrink operates at the intersection of diplomacy and espionage in
[[orgzild|King Orgzild]]'s network. Dispatched as special envoy to [[konkrook|Konkrook]] ostensibly
to receive a shipment of power-metal (plutonium), his true mission is far
more consequential: during a visit to [[niflheim|Niflheim]], he discovers that plutonium
can be weaponized, and he systematically gathers intelligence on nuclear
bomb construction [003-008][013-014].

Von Schlichten reconstructs the sequence: "When Gorkrink found out that
plutonium can be used for bombs, he began gathering all the information he
could" [013-014]. This intelligence coup enables [[orgzild|Orgzild]]'s nuclear weapons
program and fundamentally shifts the balance of power on [[uller|Uller]].

## Background

Gorkrink is a [[keegark|Keegark]]an prince with the distinctive biology of his kingdom's
elite. Von Schlichten observes "green specks in the hide of that Prince Gorkrink"
[003-029] — pigmentation that distinguishes him from the red-speckled [[konkrook|Konkrook]]an
nobility like [[prince-gurgurk|Gurgurk]] and Ghroghrank.

As an [[ulleran|Ulleran]] of high rank, Gorkrink can navigate the inter-kingdom diplomatic
circuit: [[keegark|Keegark]], [[konkrook|Konkrook]], and [[niflheim|Niflheim]]. His presence in the prologue — one of
"the [[ulleran|Ullerans]]" aboard the ship — establishes his role as a recurring figure in
the interplanetary dimension of the story [P02-009].

## Role in the Uprising

### The Plutonium Shipment

Gorkrink's official mission is diplomatic: he arrives in [[konkrook|Konkrook]] as the guest
of Ambassador Ghroghrank "to receive the shipment of power-metal my royal master
has been honored to be permitted to purchase from [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]" [003-008]. The
four-ton plutonium shipment is delivered during formal diplomatic ceremonies.

### Intelligence Gathering on Niflheim

The critical moment comes during Gorkrink's visit to [[niflheim|Niflheim]]. There, he learns
that plutonium — which [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] has been selling to [[ulleran|Ulleran]] kings for power
reactors — can also be used to construct [[nuclear-weapons|atomic bomb]]s. Grinell's intelligence
branch confirms Gorkrink actively gathered information: he could potentially
read [[lingua-terra|Lingua Terra]] and may have seen "papers or plans" related to nuclear weapons
technology [013-010].

The intelligence haul is devastating. Von Schlichten concludes [[orgzild|Orgzild]] would
not have triggered the uprising until he either had a bomb or knew he couldn't
make one, and since three months elapsed between Gorkrink's return and the
revolt's outbreak, "I think we can assume that he succeeded" [013-021].

### The Poisoning Order

Gorkrink's mission encompasses more than plutonium and intelligence. He
carries instructions from [[orgzild|Orgzild]] to Ambassador Ghroghrank: "to tell Ghroghrank
to pass the word to have [[sid-harrington|Sid Harrington]] poisoned according to plan" [013-021].
The assassination of the [[governor-general|Governor-General]] is thus traced directly to Gorkrink's
diplomatic visit.

### Death

Gorkrink's fate is sealed in the uprising's climactic phase. Von Schlichten
believes "that both [[orgzild|King Orgzild]] and Prince Gorkrink were aboard her when she
blew up" [015-008] — "her" being a ship destroyed during the conflict, though
the specific vessel and circumstances remain ambiguous in the available text.

## Characteristics & Traits

Gorkrink represents a new type of [[ulleran|Ulleran]] threat: the techno-spy. Unlike
traditional native kings who rule through military force and feudal loyalty,
Gorkrink operates in the realm of technical intelligence — acquiring knowledge
that leverage's [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s own technology against it.

His ability to move between worlds ([[keegark|Keegark]], [[konkrook|Konkrook]], [[niflheim|Niflheim]]), speak
[[lingua-terra|Lingua Terra]] well enough to conduct espionage, and systematically gather
technical intelligence makes him uniquely dangerous. He is [[orgzild|Orgzild]]'s most
valuable agent precisely because he understands what the [[terra|Terra]]ns are hiding.

The green-speckled pigmentation and his status as "Prince" suggest he belongs
to [[keegark|Keegark]]'s royal family — not merely a servant but a blood relation trusted
with the kingdom's most sensitive missions.
