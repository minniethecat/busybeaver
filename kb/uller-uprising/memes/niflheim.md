# Niflheim

Niflheim is the fourth planet of Nu Puppis, a blue-white B8-type star 148 light-years from Earth. It is the companion world to [[uller|Uller]] in the Company's franchise territory, a planet so hostile that no human has ever set foot on its surface or breathed its air — "to have done so would have been instant death" (P02-004).

## Environmental Conditions

The atmosphere of Niflheim is a corrosive mixture of free fluorine gas and fluoride compounds. The soil consists of metallic fluorides, damp with acid rains, and the rivers run with pure hydrofluoric acid (P02-004). The atmosphere has a faint yellowish tinge, and under Niflheim's blue-predominant primary, the landscape would appear a vivid green to unshielded human eyes — if human eyes could survive the gamma rays, X-rays, and ultraviolet radiation that bathe the surface (P02-002).

Ordinary spacesuits provide no protection whatsoever. Glass, rubber, and plastic disintegrate in the fluorine atmosphere within minutes. Even the armored vehicles used for surface operations are built to standards exceeding those of normal spacecraft: they are completely insulated and entered only through a triple airlock system — an outer lock evacuated outward, a middle lock maintained in permanent vacuum, and an inner lock evacuated into the vehicle interior before the middle lock can be opened (P02-004).

The gravity is approximately 1 g and the average ambient temperature is about minus 60 degrees Celsius. There are no true mountains on Niflheim — the fluoride-based geology produces only low dunes and hills, never more than a thousand feet high, because fluorides are mechanically weak and cannot support the mass of granite-like formations (P01-032).

## Native Life

Despite its lethal environment, Niflheim hosts indigenous life. The native organisms, called "geeks" by the Terrans, breathe fluorine and metabolize fluorides the way terrestrial life metabolizes oxygen. Their biochemistry is based on carbon-fluorine chains, with teflon-like hard tissues for shells and bones. The blood catalyst is titanium, creating colorless arterial blood and violet venous blood as the titanium cycles between trivalent and tetravalent states (P01-044).

The ecosystem includes small herbivores resembling eight-legged crabs, larger sixteen-legged shell-armored predators, and forests of hundred-foot stalks topped with ragged leaves (P02-003). The native intelligent species, the [[nifl|Nifl]], possess four hands and are particularly adept at operating complex machinery — a trait that made them valuable to the Company as laborers.

## Nuclear Mining Operations

Niflheim is rich in valuable minerals: large natural crystals of fluorides for optical instruments, uranium occurring naturally as uranium hexafluoride ready for the diffusion process, and significant deposits of beryllium, nickel, vanadium, chromium, and indium (P01-048). The Terran presence on Niflheim is entirely industrial — about a thousand Terrans and not more than five hundred Nifl natives work on the planet's surface, all in powered contragravity armor, living on artificial satellites two thousand miles off-planet (004-026).

Mining operations involve nuclear detonations to crack open the planetary crust, releasing gas, steam, and magma in controlled eruptions. These shots produce effects compared to historical volcanic events on Terra (P02-025). The [[contragravity|contragravity]] handling-machines — massive tank-like vehicles fitted with bulldozer blades, derrick booms, and jointed claw-tipped arms — are used to maneuver in the hostile environment (P02-010).

## The Security Leak

It was Niflheim's nuclear mining program that inadvertently armed the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]]. Native Ulleran workers, seconded from the polar mines on Uller, observed the nuclear detonations. One in particular — the Keegarkan prince Gorkrink — spent a year working under Dr. Murillo's seismology team, understood [[lingua-terra|Lingua Terra]], and had access to documents and conversations about nuclear weapons physics (013-010)(013-012). The knowledge he carried back to [[keegark|Keegark]] enabled King Orgzild's nuclear bomb project, which would ultimately force the Company to build its own weapon and destroy Keegark in response.
