# Hassan Bogdanoff

Corporal Hassan Bogdanoff was a [[terra|Terra]]n aircar crewman in the service of the [[chartered-uller-company|Chartered Uller Company]], serving as Harry Quong's frequent partner and ball-turret gunner on [[command-car|von Schlichten's command car]] throughout the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]].

## Overview

Hassan Bogdanoff was one of the "old [[uller|Uller]] hands" — experienced [[terra|Terra]]n personnel who had spent years operating in [[uller|Uller]]'s hostile environment. As the ball-turret gunner on [[command-car|von Schlichten's command car]], he provided critical suppressive fire during the [[konkrook|Konkrook]] rescue, the [[skilk|Skilk]] defense, and the southern reconnaissance campaign. His working partnership with Sergeant Harry Quong formed one of the most effective combat crews in [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s service.

## Background

Bogdanoff held the rank of Corporal in [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] forces on [[uller|Uller]]. He and Harry Quong "were old [[uller|Uller]] hands; they'd done this sort of work before" （001-010）, indicating extensive combat experience in [[ulleran|Ulleran]] operations. On [[uller|Uller]], where "the social difference between a human general and a human aircar-driver was almost infinitesimal" （007-016）, Bogdanoff attended the same banquet as senior officers — a reflection of the egalitarian pressures of life on a hostile frontier world.

## Role in the Uprising

### The Konkrook Rescue

Bogdanoff's combat debut in the novel came during the pivotal rescue at [[konkrook|Konkrook]]. When [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] spotted [[paula-quinton|Paula Quinton]] and Mohammed Ferriera under mob attack, "Bogdanoff rose into the ball-turret and swung the twin 15 mm's around, cutting loose" while Quong brought the car in at shoulder-height. "Between them, they left a swath of mangled, killed, wounded, and stunned natives" （001-010）.

His marksmanship proved decisive at a critical moment. When an elaborately dressed native drew a [[terra|Terra]]n automatic on [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]], "before he could fire, Hassan Bogdanoff had seen and swung his guns around; the double burst caught the native in the chest and fairly tore him apart" （001-014）. Von Schlichten later glanced back to see Bogdanoff already "putting fresh belts into his guns" — the automatic efficiency of a veteran who knew the fight wasn't over （001-034）.

### Skilk Banquet and Night of the Uprising

Bogdanoff attended the banquet at [[skilk|Skilk]] on the night [[sid-harrington|Governor-General Harrington]] died. After the uprising struck, he survived the initial assault and was among the [[terra|Terra]]ns emerging from the besieged [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] buildings during the [[kragan|Kragan]] counterattack, alongside Harry Quong, Jules Keaveney, Commander Prinsloo of the *Aldebaran*, and other [[terra|Terra]]ns （007-033）. He was present for the subsequent defense of [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] compound.

### Southern Reconnaissance

During the flight south toward [[konkrook|Konkrook]], Bogdanoff alternated driving duties with Quong. "Hassan Bogdanoff drove while Harry Quong finished his lunch, then changed places to begin his own" as they bulleted "south along the broad valley and dry bed of the Hoork River" （012-003）. Approaching [[konkrook|Konkrook]] at night, with the city a "lurid smear on the underside of the clouds," Bogdanoff handled radio communication with Gongonk Island while Quong kept the car "high enough to be above the trajectories of incoming shells" （012-010）.

## Characteristics & Traits

Bogdanoff demonstrated exceptional gunnery skills under combat conditions, capable of acquiring and destroying targets in the split second between an enemy drawing a weapon and firing it. His partnership with Harry Quong was seamless — the two alternated between driving and gunner duties without friction. His professionalism extended to the mechanical: after engaging the enemy, he immediately reloaded without being ordered, reflecting the ingrained habits of a veteran combat crewman.
