# Colonel Jarman

Colonel Jarman was the [[terra|Terra]]n officer in command of [[skilk|Skilk]]'s military airfield and its contragravity forces, playing a critical tactical role in [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s defense and counterattack during the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]].

## Overview

Colonel Jarman commanded [[skilk|Skilk]]'s key military aviation infrastructure — the military airport, its contragravity combat vehicles, and its flight crews. During the uprising, Jarman organized vital aircrew transportation, directed combat contragravity against enemy ground forces and improvised airtanks, and maintained aerial patrols that were essential to the defense of the [[skilk|Skilk]] Reservation. His ability to rapidly deploy airjeeps, combat-cars, and armed contragravity — and to improvise crews under fire — made him one of the most tactically significant field commanders in [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s order of battle.

## Background

Jarman held the rank of colonel and was stationed at the military airfield serving the [[skilk|Skilk]] [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] enclave. He commanded the contragravity assets that formed the backbone of [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s tactical air power: airjeeps used for reconnaissance, combat-cars armed with rockets and machine guns, and the all-important [[contragravity|contragravity lift]] capacity that enabled rapid troop movement. He reported to Brigadier-General Barney Mordkovitz as the local commanding officer and ultimately to General [[carlos-von-schlichten|Carlos von Schlichten]].

## Role in the Uprising

### Night of the Uprising

When the uprising erupted, Jarman was one of the first field commanders to respond. As [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] command post at [[skilk|Skilk]] was being established, "Colonel Jarman's coming here with a lorry to get combat-car crews; he's short-handed" （008-028）. The urgency reflected both the scale of the initial assault and Jarman's hands-on approach — he personally went to fetch personnel rather than waiting for them to report.

### Tactical Counterattacks

Jarman's contragravity proved decisive in the early hours of the siege. He organized the defense of the military facilities: "Colonel Jarman's got some contragravity up and working. They blew out all four of the Tenth N.U.N.I.'s barracks" — the mutinous Tenth North [[uller|Uller]] Native Infantry — while simultaneously supporting [[kragan|Kragans]] who were "leading out hipposaurs, and sniping at the rear of the cavalry barracks" （008-053）. His combat-cars helped Kormork's [[kragan|Kragan]] unit "take back the lorry-hangars" （008-058）.

### Preventing Enemy Air Mobilization

One of Jarman's most critical tasks was preventing the rebels from acquiring air capability. Von Schlichten's direct order was to prevent "those [[ulleran|geeks]] get any of that equipment onto contragravity; knock it down as fast as they try to lift out with it" — and to secure "troop-carriers or lorries, to get Falkenberg's Rifles to the equipment-park" （008-066）. Jarman's combat-cars intercepted rioting laborers who had "managed to lift up a number of dump-trucks and powershovels and bulldozers, intending to use them as improvised airtanks," shooting down every one （010-016）.

### Northern Road Interception

When [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] detected a [[geek]] column approaching from [[skilk|Skilk]] along the main highway, he dispatched an urgent order: "My compliments to Colonel Jarman. Tell him there's a [[geek]] mob, or possibly Firkked's regulars, on the main highway from [[skilk|Skilk]], two miles east of the Reservation. Get some combat contragravity over here, at once" （010-042）. Jarman's response was immediate: "eight or ten more flares alight, and the ground-flashes of exploding shells and rockets" as his forces engaged （010-051）.

### Air Superiority

Throughout the siege, Jarman maintained aerial patrols that denied the enemy operational freedom. "Colonel Jarman had his airjeeps and combat-cars working in ever-widening circles over the countryside, shooting up everything in sight that even looked like contragravity equipment" （011-058）. When nuisance sniping began from the mountainside, some patrols were recalled for perimeter defense, but the broader air superiority mission continued. By the final assault, Jarman commanded "fifteen of his remaining twenty airjeeps and five combat-cars" for the climactic battle （012-005）.

## Characteristics & Traits

Jarman was a practical field commander whose value lay in tactical execution rather than strategic planning. His willingness to personally drive a lorry to fetch combat-car crews in the middle of a battle spoke to his hands-on leadership style. He understood contragravity warfare intuitively — the rapid deployment of airjeeps for reconnaissance, combat-cars for ground attack, and troop carriers for mobility — and his forces' ability to shoot down enemy-contested equipment before it could be used as improvised airtanks was critical to maintaining [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s technological advantage.
