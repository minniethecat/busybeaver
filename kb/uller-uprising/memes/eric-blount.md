# Eric Blount

Eric Blount serves as [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] Resident at [[konkrook|Konkrook]], [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s chief civilian representative in the planetary capital. His articulate defense of brutal labor practices, his dismissive attitude toward native political consciousness, and his horrific death — burned alive with thermoconcentrate by a [[keegark|Keegark]]an mob — make him one of the novel's most symbolically charged characters. His killing is explicitly cited as retroactive justification for the nuclear destruction of [[keegark|Keegark]].

## Overview

Blount is the civilian face of [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] rule: a diplomat and administrator who manages relations with the native kingdoms, oversees commercial operations, and represents [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s interests in the complex political landscape of [[konkrook|Konkrook]]. He is intelligent, articulate, and completely convinced of the moral and intellectual superiority of [[terra|Terra]]ns over [[ulleran|Ullerans]] — a conviction he expresses with a candor that makes other [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] men uncomfortable (004-020).

His conversations with [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] and [[sid-harrington|Harrington]] early in the novel establish the ideological framework that the uprising will ultimately destroy. Blount believes that the [[ulleran|Ullerans]] are "a naturally brutal race" whose labor can only be extracted through force, that native political movements are inherently illegitimate, and that any concession to native demands will invite further agitation (004-020). He is not a sadist — he does not enjoy [[ulleran|Ulleran]] suffering — but he is incapable of seeing [[ulleran|Ullerans]] as political subjects with legitimate grievances.

## Background

Blount's background prior to his posting at [[konkrook|Konkrook]] is not detailed in the novel. His position as Resident suggests a career in [[company-rule|Company administration]] — perhaps in commercial operations or lower-level diplomatic postings before his elevation to the [[konkrook|Konkrook]] Residency. His chess games with [[sid-harrington|Harrington]] indicate a level of education and social standing that places him within [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s governing elite, and his comfort with the language of realpolitik suggests experience in the practical realities of colonial governance.

He plays chess regularly with [[sid-harrington|Governor-General Harrington]] at the [[governor-general|Governor-General]]'s residence, using the game as a setting for political discussion (002-010). During these matches, they debate the native question: the rising influence of [[rakkeed-the-prophet|Rakkeed the Prophet]], the wisdom of [[native-labor-system|Company labor]] practices, and the appropriate response to growing [[ulleran|Ulleran]] unrest. Blount consistently advocates for the hardest possible line — no concessions, no reforms, no acknowledgment that native grievances might have any basis in [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] policy.

## Role in the Uprising

### The Pre-Uprising Debates

When [[paula-quinton|Paula Quinton]] arrives to investigate labor conditions for the [[extraterrestrials-rights-association|Extraterrestrials' Rights Association]], Blount becomes her primary antagonist in [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] hierarchy. He defends the existing labor system as economically necessary and morally unproblematic, arguing that the special conditions of [[uller|Uller]] — the "naturally brutal" character of the natives, the harsh polar environment, the strategic importance of fissionable ore extraction — justify practices that would be unacceptable on any [[terra|Terra]]n world.

His most revealing exchange comes during a discussion of [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s growing influence. Blount cannot comprehend why [[ulleran|Ullerans]] would be drawn to a prophet preaching resistance to [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] rule. Von Schlichten, with his deeper understanding of native politics, recognizes the danger. But Blount sees only "[[geek]] superstition" — a native pathology to be managed rather than a political movement to be understood (002-010)(002-013).

### The Death of Eric Blount

Blount is killed during the [[keegark|Keegark]]an assault on the [[konkrook|Konkrook]] Residency. The mob captures him and subjects him to a death of calculated cruelty: he is drenched in thermoconcentrate — a highly flammable compound — and set on fire. The method is significant: thermoconcentrate is a mining chemical associated with [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s polar operations, making Blount's death a symbolic revenge for the labor system he defended.

His death, along with that of Hendrik Lemoyne, is cited by [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] as the moral justification for the nuclear destruction of [[keegark|Keegark]] and its estimated hundred and fifty thousand inhabitants. "He had to remind himself that they were the people who had burned Eric Blount and Hendrik Lemoyne alive," [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] reflects as he watches the mushroom cloud rise over the ruined city (015-043).

## Characteristics

Blount is intelligent and articulate — he is not a fool or a brute. But his intelligence is entirely harnessed to the defense of an unjust system. His chess games with [[sid-harrington|Harrington]] symbolize a certain kind of colonial mentality: the belief that native politics can be managed like pieces on a board, moved and sacrificed according to strategic calculation without reference to native agency or moral considerations.

His racism is distinguished from the general colonial prejudice of the period by its intellectual articulation. Where [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s soldiers use the slur "[[geek]]" as casual profanity, Blount deploys racial categories as a systematic ideology: the [[ulleran|Ullerans]] are naturally brutal, naturally subordinate, naturally incapable of self-government. This ideology is not merely personal prejudice — it is the philosophical foundation of [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] rule, and Blount is its most articulate spokesman.

## Death / Post-novel

Blount's death serves multiple narrative functions. It eliminates the civilian leadership that had advocated for the policies that provoked the uprising. It provides [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] with a moral casus belli for nuclear escalation — the horror of Blount's death balances, in the novel's ethical calculus, the horror of the nuclear response. And it marks the end of an era: the pre-uprising [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] establishment, with its confident racism and its chess-game politics, is literally burned away by the forces it refused to acknowledge.

## Relationships

- **[[sid-harrington|Sid Harrington]]**: His chess partner and fellow member of [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s pre-uprising establishment. They share assumptions about native inferiority and discuss policy over chess (002-010)
- **[[carlos-von-schlichten|Carlos von Schlichten]]**: A professional relationship marked by tension. Blount represents the civilian authority [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] respects but does not admire, and their policy disagreements presage the military takeover that follows [[sid-harrington|Harrington]]'s death
- **[[paula-quinton|Paula Quinton]]**: His ideological antagonist. Her humanitarian investigation threatens the labor system he defends, and their exchanges dramatize the novel's central moral debate
- **[[rakkeed-the-prophet|Rakkeed the Prophet]]**: The native leader whose significance Blount fatally underestimates (002-013)
