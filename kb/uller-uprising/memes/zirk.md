The **Zirk** are a nomadic tribe of [[ulleran|Ullerans]] whose traditional way of life revolved around caravan-driving and caravan-raiding across the surface of [[uller|Uller]]. The arrival of the [[chartered-uller-company|Chartered Uller Company]] and its technological superiority fundamentally disrupted Zirk society, transforming them from independent traders and raiders into a displaced population.

## Traditional Economy

Prior to Company contact, the Zirk formed the circulatory system of Ulleran trade. Their caravans moved goods between Ulleran kingdoms, and their raiding parties extracted tolls and tribute along the trade routes. This dual role — merchant and marauder — placed the Zirk at the center of Ulleran economic life, but also created tensions with settled Ulleran populations who both depended on and feared them.

## Displacement

The Company's introduction of air-freighters rendered the traditional Zirk caravan economy obsolete (004-033). Where caravans had once taken weeks to cross the harsh Ulleran landscape, Company aircraft could transport goods in hours. This technological displacement destroyed the economic foundation of Zirk society and forced many Zirk into Company dependency.

## Notable Individuals

[[rakkeed|Rakkeed]], one of the most prominent Ulleran figures in the narrative, is a Zirk. His leadership of the anti-Company uprising draws on both Zirk martial traditions and the deep grievances of a people whose way of life was destroyed by colonial technology.

## The Fifth Zirk Cavalry

Not all Zirk rejected Company authority. Some Zirk have been incorporated into the colonial military structure as the Fifth Zirk Cavalry, serving as Company auxiliaries (006-007). This mirrors the Company's approach with the [[kragan-rifles|Kragan Rifles]] — selectively recruiting from among the native population to create divided loyalties and reduce unified resistance.

The existence of both anti-Company Zirk insurgents under Rakkeed and pro-Company Zirk cavalrymen illustrates the fractures that colonial rule creates within indigenous societies, splitting tribes and families between resistance and accommodation.
