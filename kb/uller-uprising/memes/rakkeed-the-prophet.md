# Rakkeed the Prophet

A [[zirk|Zirk]] nomad turned religious leader, whose apocalyptic anti-[[terra|Terra]]n crusade galvanizes
[[ulleran|Ulleran]] discontent and threatens [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s hold on the planet.

## Overview

Rakkeed the Prophet is the central antagonist force driving anti-[[terra|Terra]]n sentiment across
[[uller|Uller]]. A charismatic [[zirk|Zirk]] preacher, he travels the northern kingdoms — [[konkrook|Konkrook]], [[skilk|Skilk]],
[[keegark|Keegark]] — delivering sermons against the [[chartered-uller-company|Chartered Uller Company]] and the [[terran-federation|Terran Federation]].
His influence extends from the urban rabble to the courts of native kings, and his
network of disciples penetrates even the [[terra|Terra]]n military garrisons [002-013].

Von Schlichten's Intelligence Section tracks [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] as the prime instigator of
instability: "Everywhere we turn, [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s at the bottom of whatever happens to be wrong"
[002-013]. The name "the Mad Prophet" — used by [[terra|Terra]]ns — reflects both his
otherworldly conviction and [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s dismissive posture toward native spirituality [004-031].

## Background

### Species & Biology

[[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] is a **[[zirk|Zirk]]**, one of the five [[ulleran|Ulleran]] sub-types. The [[zirk|Zirk]] are described
as nomads of the northern regions who traditionally hire out as caravan-drivers
to the northern merchants, and also prey — or used to prey — on the caravans as brigands
[004-033]. As a silicon-based lifeform, [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] shares the standard [[ulleran|Ulleran]]
physiology: hermaphroditic, [[silicon-based-life|quartz-speckled skin]], cold-blooded metabolism adapted
to [[uller|Uller]]'s extreme temperature range.

### Rise to Influence

[[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s origins in the [[zirk|Zirk]] nomad community gave him a natural constituency
among the disaffected. The [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] employs only a few [[zirk|Zirk]] as cavalry or gauchos;
"the rest just sit around and listen to [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s sermons" [004-033]. His
message — that [[niflheim|Niflheim]] is the [[terra|Terra]]ns' true home and that [[terra|Terra]]n farms and
evergreen plantings are the beginning of an attempt to drive all native life from
[[uller|Uller]] [006-019] — resonates with [[ulleran|Ullerans]] displaced by [[chartered-uller-company|Company]]-driven economic change.

## Role in the Uprising

### Preaching the Crusade

[[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s core message is a crusade against the [[terra|Terra]]ns and against Jaikark XVII,
[[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]-controlled King of [[konkrook|Konkrook]] [002-010]. His sermons attract the urban
merchants and artisans "who weren't able or willing to adapt themselves to changing
conditions" brought by [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] economic policies [004-032].

### Political Patronage

Crucially, [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] does not operate alone. [[prince-gurgurk|Prince Gurgurk]], the ambitious cousin
of King Jaikark, has been subsidizing [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s movement — funds traceable through
Intelligence Section records of "sums of money, and dates, and the names of the
intermediaries through whom they were paid" [002-012]. The plan, as [[eric-blount|Blount]]
analyzes it, is that [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] would stir up anti-[[terra|Terra]]n sentiment, direct it
against Jaikark as well as [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]], and when the outbreak comes and Jaikark
is killed, [[prince-gurgurk|Gurgurk]] would "step in, seize the Palace, and use the Royal army to
put down the revolt that he's incited in the first place" [002-013].

[[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] is also welcomed at the [[keegark|Keegark]]an embassy — hosted by the ambassador of
[[orgzild|King Orgzild]] — and at the Royal Palace at [[keegark|Keegark]] itself [002-033]. This layered
patronage across multiple [[ulleran|Ulleran]] kingdoms transforms him from a wandering preacher
into a geopolitical lever.

### Operational Reach

By the time of the novel's events, [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s network spans the continent. His
disciples attempt to infiltrate the Tenth N.U.N.I. and the Fifth [[zirk|Zirk]] Cavalry
to "promote disaffection" among [[terra|Terra]]n-aligned [[terran-military-doctrine|native troops]] [006-007]. Letters
addressed to "[[skilk|Skilk]]ans known to be [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] disciples and rabidly anti-[[terra|Terra]]n"
are intercepted by [[terra|Terra]]n Intelligence [002-044].

His freedom of movement reflects the fractured [[ulleran|Ulleran]] political landscape:
"At [[skilk|Skilk]], [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] comes and goes openly; at Krink he has a price on his head"
[004-035] — the difference determined by whether the local king fears his urban
bourgeoisie (and thus tolerates [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]) or fears his feudal barons (and thus
suppresses him).

### Connection to the Rev. Keeluk

The Reverend Keeluk, who appears prominently in the [[konkrook|Konkrook]] narrative, is
identified as one of [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s followers [002-013], extending his influence
into the religious structures of [[ulleran|Ulleran]] urban society.

## Characteristics & Traits

[[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] possesses the charisma of a prophet and the organizational acumen of
a political operative. His gospel is described as apocalyptic — promising the
overthrow of [[terra|Terra]]n influence and the restoration of native [[ulleran|Ulleran]] primacy.
Even those who dismiss him acknowledge his scale: [[eric-blount|Blount]] judges that "[[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s
bigger than [[prince-gurgurk|Gurgurk]] ever can be" [002-013], suggesting [[prince-gurgurk|Gurgurk]] has underestimated
the force he has been funding.

His rhetoric includes the slogan "Znidd suddabit!" — a phrase with apparent
revolutionary connotations that his followers yell at rallies [004-032].

## Relationships

- **[[prince-gurgurk|Prince Gurgurk]]**: Primary political patron and funder; [[prince-gurgurk|Gurgurk]] intends to
  use [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] as a tool to destabilize Jaikark's throne [002-013]
- **[[orgzild|King Orgzild of Keegark]]**: Hosts [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] at his embassy and palace,
  possibly to pressure [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] into relocating its base to [[keegark|Keegark]] [002-033]
- **The Rev. Keeluk**: A disciple and follower active in [[konkrook|Konkrook]] [002-013]
- **King Firkked of [[skilk|Skilk]]**: Tolerates [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s presence — Firkked fears his
  feudal nobility and courts the pro-[[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] urban merchants [004-035]
- **King Jonkvank of Krink**: Opposes [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] — Jonkvank has his barons' support
  and a [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] subsidy, making him "pro-[[terra|Terra]]n and anti-[[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]" [004-035]
- **[[carlos-von-schlichten|Carlos von Schlichten]]**: Views [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] as the primary threat to [[chartered-uller-company|Company]]
  stability; Intelligence Section actively tracks his movements [002-012]
- **King Jaikark XVII of [[konkrook|Konkrook]]**: Primary target of [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s anti-royal
  rhetoric alongside [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] [002-010]
