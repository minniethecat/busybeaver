**Handling machines** are heavy contragravity-equipped industrial vehicles used by the Terran Company on Uller for construction, excavation, nuclear mining, and combat engineering, characterized by their bulldozer-blade fronts, stubby derrick-booms, and articulated claw-tipped manipulator arms (P02-010).

## Overview

Handling machines represent a fusion of Terran contragravity technology with heavy industrial engineering. Described as "a thing like an oversized contragravity-tank, with a bulldozer-blade, a stubby derrick-boom instead of a gun, and jointed, claw-tipped arms to the sides" (P02-010), they function as mobile industrial platforms capable of operating in Uller's hostile environment. Unlike dedicated military vehicles such as combat-cars and airjeeps, handling machines are dual-purpose equipment, serving both peacetime industrial roles and combat support functions during the uprising.

The machines are large enough to enclose personnel — Gorkrink, an Ulleran noble, is noted as being "inside the handling-machine" during the nuclear mining operation, using one of the personal armor suits (P02-009). Their contragravity lift systems allow them to operate without ground contact, essential for traversing Uller's varied terrain.

## Technical Characteristics and Industrial Role

The handling machine's design prioritizes versatility over speed or combat capability. The bulldozer-blade serves for earth-moving and debris clearance; the derrick-boom provides lifting capacity for heavy loads; and the claw-tipped side arms offer precision manipulation. This configuration made them essential for the Company's infrastructure projects — spaceport construction, fortification building, and the nuclear mining operations that extracted power-metals from dormant volcanoes (P02-013).

The nuclear mining program, in particular, relied heavily on handling machines. They were used to position drilling equipment, manage the nuclear charges, and clear the debris fields after detonation. The machines' enclosed cabins provided some protection against the fluorine-tainted atmosphere released during mining operations, though the seals were imperfect: "it's impossible to keep some trace of fluorine from the air in the handling-machines, or even out on the orbiters, and it plays the devil with their lungs" (P02-043). This atmospheric contamination posed a particular risk to Ulleran operators, whose silicone-based tissues were vulnerable to fluorine exposure (004-023).

## Combat Engineering Applications

During the Uller Uprising, handling machines were pressed into combat engineering roles. Their bulldozer-blades were used to clear barricades and construct defensive positions; their derrick-booms could lift and position heavy armaments or clear wreckage from landing zones. The presence of handling machines alongside personal armor units during the nuclear mining operation (P02-009, P02-010) suggests a coordinated employment of industrial and military assets under combat conditions.

The machines' contragravity mobility gave them tactical advantages that conventional heavy equipment lacked — they could operate in urban combat zones, cross water barriers, and reposition rapidly without the need for road networks. This mobility was particularly valuable during the defense of Company compounds like the Skilk Reservation and Gongonk Island, where rapid fortification was essential to survival.

## Narrative Significance

The handling machine embodies the industrial-technological asymmetry at the heart of the Uller Uprising. While the Ulleran kingdoms possessed medieval-level military technology — crossbows, cavalry, and edged weapons — the Company deployed machines that could reshape terrain itself. The handling machine's presence in both industrial and combat scenes reinforces the novel's theme of corporate colonization: the same equipment that extracts planetary wealth also enforces the political order that makes extraction possible.

The detail about fluorine contamination affecting Ulleran operators (P02-043) introduces a darker subtext: even when Ullerans are employed in skilled technical roles rather than menial labor, the Company's industrial processes are fundamentally incompatible with their biology. The handling machine, designed by and for Terrans, becomes another vector of slow violence against the native population.
