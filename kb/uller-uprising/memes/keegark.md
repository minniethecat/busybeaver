# Keegark

Keegark was one of the major cities of the Konk Isthmus on [[uller|Uller]], ruled by King Orgzild. It was the epicenter of the nuclear weapons conspiracy that defined the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]], and its destruction by the Terran-built *Dire Dawn* bomb represents the novel's darkest moral reckoning — approximately 150,000 Ullerans killed in a single detonation (015-043).

## King Orgzild's Court

King Orgzild of Keegark was one of the most powerful native rulers in the equatorial zone, commanding an army of at least two hundred thousand soldiers and subjects (008-020). Unlike King Jaikark of [[konkrook|Konkrook]] — a drug-addled Company puppet — Orgzild was an active and calculating political operator who cultivated anti-Terran sentiment and positioned himself as the leader of the native resistance.

Orgzild's court included the Keegarkan Ambassador to Konkrook, who hosted the anti-Terran preacher Rakkeed during his visits to the capital. Company Intelligence confirmed through a spy planted among the embassy servants that "the last time Rakkeed was in Konkrook, he was the guest of the Keegarkan Ambassador" (002-019). This diplomatic channel provided direct coordination between the psionic uprising fomented by Rakkeed and the military conspiracy organized by Orgzild.

## The Nuclear Conspiracy

Keegark's role in the uprising was distinguished by a technological ambition unmatched by any other native kingdom: the development of a nuclear weapon. The conspiracy originated with Prince Gorkrink, a Keegarkan royal who was assigned for a year to Dr. Murillo's nuclear mining team on [[niflheim|Niflheim]] (013-010)(013-012). Gorkrink understood [[lingua-terra|Lingua Terra]], observed nuclear detonations directly, and likely had access to documents and conversations about weapons physics. The knowledge he carried back to Keegark enabled Orgzild's bomb project.

Colonel Grinell's Intelligence Section pieced together the threat: intercepted letters, post-uprising interrogation of captured Keegarkan officials, and the discovery that Keegark had concealed a captured Terran freighter for weaponization purposes (012-058)(012-064). The evidence confirmed that Orgzild had not merely aspired to build a nuclear weapon — he had succeeded. The Keegarkans had overcome the hardest challenge of any nuclear program by obtaining fissionable material from the Company's own plutonium-based power reactors, which had been distributed liberally across the planet (013-016).

## The Uprising at Keegark

When the coordinated uprising erupted, Keegark's native troops and townspeople overwhelmed the Company Residency almost immediately. The Resident-Agent, Eric Blount, and his assistant, Hendrik Lemoyne, were at the Palace negotiating with King Orgzild when the attack began — they never returned. They were captured, soaked in thermoconcentrate, and burned alive (008-004)(015-043).

Colonel MacKinnon's last transmission reported that the native forces had penetrated the final defenses and that he was about to blow up the Residency to deny it to the enemy. After that, "Keegark went off the air" (010-015). The *Jan Smuts* and *Christiaan De Wett*, two Company ships in the Keegark docks, were damaged by the Residency's self-destruction.

## Destruction

General [[carlos-von-schlichten|Carlos von Schlichten]] authorized Operation Hildegarde — the construction of a fission bomb using plans recovered from a historical romance novel set in the First Century Atomic Era. The resulting weapon, named *Dire Dawn* by its assembler Hildegarde Hernandez, was delivered by contragravity to Keegark and detonated. The city was "completely obliterated under an outward-rolling cloud of smoke and dust that spread out for five miles at the bottom of the towering column" (015-043).

Von Schlichten witnessed the mushroom cloud and reflected that "he had to remind himself that they were the people who had burned Eric Blount and Hendrik Lemoyne alive," but also resolved that "this must be the last nuclear bomb used on Uller" (015-043)(015-044). The destruction of Keegark broke the back of the uprising and established the principle that any native kingdom attempting to develop weapons of mass destruction would face annihilation.
