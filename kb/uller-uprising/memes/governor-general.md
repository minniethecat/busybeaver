# Governor-General

The Governor-General was the supreme Terran authority on Uller, serving as the Uller Company's chief representative with powers encompassing civil administration, military command, and diplomatic relations with native kingdoms (003-001, 008-041). The title itself reflected the Terran Federation's long-term intention to absorb the planet: "why else give the Company's chief representative the title of Governor-General?" (003-027).

## Authority and Powers

The office combined three normally separate functions: Company executive, military commander-in-chief, and diplomatic plenipotentiary to the Ulleran kingdoms. The Governor-General received native rulers in formal audience, sitting "on the comfortably upholstered bench on the dais of the Audience Hall" (003-001), flanked by senior Company officials. This ceremony mirrored royal courts while maintaining the fiction that the Company was merely a trading enterprise rather than a colonial government.

The Governor-General's authority extended to the use of nuclear weapons — a power that proved decisive during the uprising. Von Schlichten, upon assuming the office, authorized the nuclear bombing of Keegark (015-058) and the Skilk campaign, decisions that no subordinate officer could make independently: "That is a political decision! You have no authority to make promises like that; that is a matter for the Governor-General, at least!" (008-053).

The office also controlled the Company's nuclear energy policy. The Governor-General's staff included specialists managing "the lack of natural fuel on Uller" that had compelled the Company "to set up nuclear power reactors and furnish large quantities of plutonium to the geeks to fuel them" (012-030) — a policy that inadvertently armed King Orgzild with "at least one First-Century Nagasaki-type plutonium bomb" (012-030).

## Sidney Harrington

Governor-General Sidney Harrington was "a ruddy outdoorsman's face and his ragged gray mustache and his old tweed coat spotted with pipe-ashes, he might have been any of the dozen-odd country-gentleman neighbors of von Schlichten's boyhood in the Argentine" (003-001). His unassuming manner concealed the authority he wielded, and his optimism about controlling native princes proved fatal: "Oh, Gurgurk wouldn't dare try anything like that. He knows we wouldn't let him get away with it. We have too much of an investment in King Jaikark" (002-021).

Harrington was poisoned in his room at 22:10 on the night the uprising began (006-008), his death serving as the signal that triggered coordinated attacks on Company facilities across the planet: "The news, of course, was sent out at once, as the geeks knew it would be, to every residency and trading-station on Uller, and that was the signal they'd agreed upon, probably months in advance" (008-035).

His predecessor, "old Jerry Kirke," had been Governor-General during the earlier period when the Company negotiated the treaty with the Kragans, buying their rievers' forts and paying subsidies in exchange for an end to brigandage (004-021).

## Von Schlichten's Succession

Upon learning of Harrington's death and the likely death of Lieutenant-Governor Eric Blount, von Schlichten assumed the office by right of military necessity: "Well, as of now, and until a successor to Sid Harrington can be sent here from Terra, I'm Governor-General" (008-053). He ordered an all-station telecast announcing that "Military Commander-in-Chief Carlos von Schlichten, being informed of the deaths of both Governor-General Harrington and Lieutenant-Governor Blount, assumes the duties of Governor-General, as of 00:01 today" (008-053).

Von Schlichten's tenure as Governor-General was defined by the crisis. He authorized promotions, directed military operations, negotiated with native kings, and made the political decision to use nuclear weapons as a deterrent. His contemplation of what the Kragans might gain from exposure to Terran political philosophy — his hesitation to give them Machiavelli — reveals a Governor-General who understood that information control was as important as military force (011-036).

The thought that "with Eric Blount as Governor-General" things might have gone differently (006-047) — Blount having been poisoned alongside Harrington — suggests the contingency of colonial rule: the entire Company response to the uprising was shaped by which individual happened to survive to assume command.

## Narrative Significance

The Governor-General is the novel's institutional protagonist, an office that concentrates the contradictions of colonial rule into a single chain of command. The Terrans govern Uller through a company charter, not a colonial ministry; the Governor-General is a corporate executive who commands armies and authorizes nuclear strikes. This blurring of public and private power — the Company as state — is the political premise from which all other conflicts derive.

The poisoning of Harrington and von Schlichten's subsequent assumption of the office transforms the Governor-Generalship from a bureaucratic position into a theater command under fire. Von Schlichten's decisions — which kings to support, which cities to bomb — are made without reference to any higher authority. The novel thus poses an uncomfortable question: what does it mean for one man, accountable to a corporate board light-years away, to hold absolute power over an entire planet?
