# Takkad Sea

The Takkad Sea was a large inland body of water in the equatorial zone of [[uller|Uller]], forming the geographic, economic, and cultural center of the region occupied by the Chartered Uller Company. Its shores were ringed by the planet's most significant native kingdoms — [[keegark|Keegark]], [[konkrook|Konkrook]], and the Free Cities — and the sea itself served as a vital transportation corridor and a strategic reference point throughout the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]].

## Geography & Environment

The Takkad Sea lay at the Equatorial Zone of Uller, with its cloud-covered horizon visible from the mountain passes of the northern territories. From the flint keep above the caravan trail, General von Schlichten could see "the horizon was lost in the cloudbank over Takkad Sea" while "the brown and gray and black flint mountains sawed into the sky until they vanished in the distance" (001-034). The sea was bordered to the north by the territories extending toward the polar region — the full area of Company occupation spanned from "the Takkad Sea area at the Equatorial Zone, and the country north of it to the pole" (008-029).

The northwestern corner of the sea was a key navigational reference point for military operations: the cruiser *Procyon* was stationed "over the northwestern corner of Takkad Sea, between Kankad's Town and Keegark" (014-038). The eastern and southern shores of the sea hosted Company trading stations, and the south shore region notably resisted Rakkeed's uprising — "the natives there had refused to rise against them, and had crucified five of Rakkeed's disciples who had come among them preaching *znidd suddabit*" (015-043).

## Cultural & Linguistic Region

The Takkad Sea area formed a distinct cultural and linguistic zone on Uller. The "customary language of the Takkad Sea country" served as a lingua franca among the diverse peoples of the equatorial region, understood by kings, Kragan mercenaries, and Company personnel alike. King Kankad used this language when addressing mixed audiences of Ullerans and Terrans: "Paula knows what she's talking about," he "jabbered in the Takkad Sea language which they all understood" (015-043).

The weaponry of the region reflected its distinct technological traditions. Native fighters from the northern territories used "long northern air-rifles and a few Takkad Sea crossbows," weapons that "would shoot clear through a Terran or halfway through an Ulleran at fifty yards" but were "almost harmless" at ranges exceeding two hundred yards (012-061).

## Strategic & Military Significance

The Takkad Sea was the central axis of Terran military power projection on Uller. The Company's warships — the *Aldebaran*, *Procyon*, *Northern Lights*, and *Northern Star* — operated from stations around and above the sea, using it as a geographic anchor for coordinated fleet movements. During the nuclear crisis, von Schlichten positioned ships along the mountain ranges between river valleys, using the sea as a reference point for radar coverage and inter-ship communication (014-038).

The sea's strategic importance was rooted in the concentration of key native kingdoms along its shores. The chief geopolitical ambition of the Ulleran kings for centuries had been control of the region: "Back as far as geek history goes, the Kings of Keegark have been trying to conquer Konkrook and the Free Cities and make themselves masters of the whole Takkad Sea area" (013-016). This multi-generational rivalry between Keegark and Konkrook made the sea a perennial theater of conflict, and the Company's presence added a new, technologically superior dimension to that ancient struggle.

## Post-Uprising Vision

In the aftermath of the uprising, von Schlichten articulated a vision of Terran consolidation centered on the Takkad Sea as a staging ground for broader planetary governance: "There's going to have to be a Terran seizure of political power in every part of this planet that we occupy, and as soon as we're consolidated around and north of Takkad Sea, we're going to have to move in elsewhere" (012-064). The plan envisioned the sea's littoral as the foundation of a Company-administered zone that would eventually extend across Uller, replacing royal decrees with courts of law and abolishing serfdom.
