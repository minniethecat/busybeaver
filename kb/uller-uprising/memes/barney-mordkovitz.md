# Barney Mordkovitz

Brigadier-General Barney Mordkovitz was the [[terra|Terra]]n military commanding officer stationed at [[skilk|Skilk]], responsible for [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s ground forces in the northern sector when the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]] erupted.

## Overview

Barney Mordkovitz was the senior [[terran-military-doctrine|Company military]] officer at [[skilk|Skilk]], commanding the garrison that included the North [[uller|Uller]] Native Infantry. A black-bearded brigadier with a keen instinct for trouble, he voiced his unease about the [[ulleran|Ullerans]]' pre-uprising behavior at the fateful banquet on the night the rebellion broke out. During the subsequent siege of [[skilk|Skilk]], Mordkovitz fought as part of [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s command team, organizing the defense of [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] compound.

## Background

Mordkovitz held the rank of brigadier-general and served as "the [[skilk|Skilk]] military C.O." （007-020）. He commanded a mixed force of [[terra|Terra]]n officers and native [[ulleran|Ulleran]] troops, including the North [[uller|Uller]] Native Infantry. His experience on [[uller|Uller]] had given him an intimate understanding of native behavior patterns, and he was among the first to recognize that the uncharacteristic calm in [[skilk|Skilk]] presaged violence.

## Role in the Uprising

### Pre-Uprising Intuition

At the banquet on the night of the uprising, Mordkovitz voiced what would prove to be a prescient warning. Speaking to the woman on his right, he described the natives' ominous silence: "They're too confounded meek. Nowadays, nobody yells '*Znidd suddabit!*' at you. Nobody sticks all four thumbs in his mouth and waves his fingers. Nobody commits nuisance on the sidewalk in front of you. They just stand and look at you like a farmer looking at a turkey the week before Christmas, and that I don't like!" （007-020）. His metaphor of the turkey — the week before Christmas — captured the sense of impending doom that more complacent [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] officials missed.

### Night of the Uprising

When the uprising began, a [[terra|Terra]]n sergeant urgently summoned Mordkovitz from the banquet table. "The black-bearded brigadier rose immediately, taking his belt from the back of his chair and putting it on" （007-039）, the swift, practiced motion of a soldier who had been expecting trouble. He conferred briefly with Resident-Agent Jules Keaveney before approaching [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] to coordinate the defense.

### Defense of Company House

During the siege at [[skilk|Skilk]], Mordkovitz was one of the key officers holding the interior of [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] House. When the native infantry broke and the [[kragan|Kragans]] counterattacked, Mordkovitz and Hideyoshi O'Leary "seemed to be in command" of the [[terra|Terra]]ns and loyal [[kragan|Kragans]] defending the inner doors （009-002）. The North [[uller|Uller]] Native Infantry under his command had collapsed catastrophically — "even under their native officers and sergeants, they should not have broken as they did" — but Mordkovitz regrouped and continued the fight alongside the [[kragan|Kragan]] mercenaries.

### Command Post Operations

Throughout the siege, Mordkovitz remained at [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s command post, part of the core command team alongside [[paula-quinton|Paula Quinton]], Colonel Cheng-Li, and Jules Keaveney, "drinking coffee and munching sandwiches" while coordinating the battle on a large terrain board （011-014）. He was present when [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s improvised aerial surveillance system went live via a contragravity-lifted TV pickup, giving the defenders the tactical awareness advantage that would prove decisive.

## Characteristics & Traits

Mordkovitz possessed the gut-level intuition of an experienced frontier officer. His reading of native body language and behavior — the absence of the usual [[ulleran|Ulleran]] provocations — was more accurate than the administrative complacency of [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] civilians. He was decisive under fire, rising immediately at the first sign of trouble and strapping on his weapon without hesitation. His partnership with Hideyoshi O'Leary during the defense of [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] House demonstrated his ability to work across branches in a crisis.
