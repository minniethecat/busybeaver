# Major Falkenberg

Major Falkenberg was a [[terra|Terra]]n infantry officer commanding the Eighteenth Rifles during the [[skilk|Skilk]] siege, who led key counterattacks against rebel-held positions and later assumed adjutant duties in [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s headquarters.

## Overview

Major Falkenberg commanded the Eighteenth Rifles, one of [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s infantry regiments engaged in the defense of the [[skilk|Skilk]] Reservation. His regiment fought in the critical battles to retake the native-troops barracks and the labor camp where rioting workers had been assembled. Falkenberg later rotated into headquarters duty as a temporary adjutant, replacing [[paula-quinton|Paula Quinton]] after Mordkovitz ordered her to rest, demonstrating the fluid personnel management required during extended combat operations.

## Background

Falkenberg held the rank of major and commanded the Eighteenth Rifles, stationed at [[skilk|Skilk]]. His regiment was among the regular [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] forces — distinct from the [[kragan|Kragan]] mercenaries — that formed the backbone of the [[skilk|Skilk]] garrison. His troops were equipped with standard [[terra|Terra]]n infantry weapons.

## Role in the Uprising

### Night of the Uprising

When the uprising erupted, [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] immediately sought Falkenberg's regiment for reinforcement. His first question upon establishing contact with the [[skilk|Skilk]] forces was: "Who's the ranking officer in direct contact with the Eighteenth Rifles? Major Falkenberg?" （008-053）. He ordered Malavez to secure "troop-carriers or lorries, to get Falkenberg's Rifles to the equipment-park" （008-066）, recognizing the need to concentrate infantry at the critical supply depot.

### Recapture of the Barracks

Falkenberg's regiment played a key role in reclaiming territory seized by mutinous [[terran-military-doctrine|native troops]]. He commanded "the action at the native-troops barracks and the labor-camp, the night before" （011-073）. This action was crucial — the native-troops barracks held mutineers from the Tenth North [[uller|Uller]] Native Infantry, while the labor camp contained rioting workers whom Firkked's agents had organized. Falkenberg's success in clearing both positions denied the rebels control of key military infrastructure.

### Direct Communication with Command

During the battle at the equipment park, Falkenberg maintained direct contact with [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]. "Von Schlichten, right overhead. That you, Major Falkenberg? Nice going, major, how are your casualties?" （010-001）. This personal radio contact reflected Falkenberg's status as a reliable field commander whom [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] trusted to hold critical positions. When ordered to coordinate with new arrivals, Falkenberg's response was prompt: "Right. I'll get on it at once, general" （010-010）.

### Headquarters Duty

In the later stages of the siege, Falkenberg rotated through headquarters duty. He informed [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] that [[paula-quinton|Paula Quinton]] — who had been working without rest — had been ordered to bed: "She's taking one. General Mordkovitz chased her off to bed a couple of hours ago, called me in to take her place" （011-073）. Falkenberg served as temporary adjutant with good humor, grinning when recounting [[paula-quinton|Paula]]'s alarm at learning the quantities of thermoconcentrate and plutonium stored under the domes at the Reservation （011-077）.

## Characteristics & Traits

Falkenberg was a solid infantry commander — dependable rather than brilliant, steady under fire rather than spectacular. His regiment performed reliably in the defensive battles, and his willingness to serve in both combat and staff roles demonstrated professional flexibility. His good-humored account of [[paula-quinton|Paula Quinton]]'s near-faint at the ammunition quantities suggested an easy camaraderie with fellow officers.
