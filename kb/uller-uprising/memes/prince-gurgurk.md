# Prince Gurgurk

The [[konkrook|Konkrook]]an equivalent of Prime Minister or Grand Vizier — a master political
operator who subsidizes [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s anti-[[terra|Terra]]n crusade while scheming to usurp
the throne of King Jaikark XVII.

## Overview

Prince Gurgurk is the second most powerful figure in the [[kingdom-of-konkrook|Kingdom of Konkrook]] after
King Jaikark XVII, serving as the "[[konkrook|Konkrook]]an equivalent of Prime Minister or Grand
Vizier" [003-003]. Behind the ceremonial protocol of [[ulleran|Ulleran]] politics, Gurgurk runs
an extensive political machine — building influence, distributing patronage, and
cultivating a network that extends from the Palace to the radical preachers of
the northern territories [002-007].

His defining gambit is a double-game: he funds [[rakkeed-the-prophet|Rakkeed the Prophet]]'s anti-[[terra|Terra]]n
crusade, intending to let [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s followers kill King Jaikark in the ensuing
uprising, after which Gurgurk would step in as [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s rescuer — crushing
the revolt he himself incited and claiming the throne [002-013].

## Background

Gurgurk's political rise reflects the complex dynamics of [[ulleran|Ulleran]] feudal society
under [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] rule. As a prince of the [[konkrook|Konkrook]] royal house, he commands the
allegiance of nobles across the "whole spectrum, from a princeling who was almost
oyster-gray to old Ghroghrank, the [[keegark|Keegark]]an Ambassador, who was even blacker
and more red-speckled than Gurgurk" [003-003].

His appearance marks his status: at formal occasions he wears "a gold helmet and
a thing like a string-vest made of gold wire," and carries "a long sword with a
two-hand grip, a pair of [[terra|Terra]]n automatics built for a hand with six four-knuckled
fingers, and a pair of matched daggers" [003-003] — a display that combines
[[ulleran|Ulleran]] martial tradition with imported [[terra|Terra]]n weaponry.

Von Schlichten observes the deterioration of Gurgurk's deference to [[chartered-uller-company|Company]]
authority: "Six months ago, Gurgurk wouldn't have tried to pull anything like this"
[003-021], indicating a calculated escalation in the Prince's ambition.

## Role in the Uprising

### The Rakkeed Conspiracy

Gurgurk's central scheme is revealed through [[eric-blount|Blount]]'s Intelligence analysis.
The plan has several stages:

1. **Funding [[rakkeed-the-prophet|the Prophet]]**: Gurgurk funnels money to [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] through identifiable
   intermediaries, which Intelligence has tracked — "sums of money, and dates, and
   the names of the intermediaries through whom they were paid" [002-012].

2. **Inciting Revolt**: [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] stirs up anti-[[terra|Terra]]n sentiment against both the
   [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] and King Jaikark, Gurgurk's royal master [002-013].

3. **The Decapitation**: When the uprising breaks out, Jaikark is killed — either
   by [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s followers or in the chaos.

4. **The Takeover**: Gurgurk steps in, seizes the Palace, and uses the Royal Army
   to crush the revolt he incited, positioning himself as "the friend of [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]"
   [002-013]. The duped rebels are rounded up and sold as slaves, with Gurgurk
   pocketing the proceeds [002-013].

### Diplomatic Manipulation

Gurgurk is a master of [[ulleran|Ulleran]] political theater. During formal diplomatic
receptions at the Residency, he delivers lengthy speeches full of protocol
and antiphonal agreement from his retinue — what the [[terra|Terra]]ns cynically term
"[[geek]] chorus" [003-018].

He arrives at the Residency in diplomatic procession to present gifts and
discuss incidents like the Seventy-second Street fracas, carefully maintaining
the appearance of cooperation while advancing his agenda [002-053]. His
entourage includes Ambassador Ghroghrank of [[keegark|Keegark]] and [[gorkrink|Prince Gorkrink]],
connecting [[konkrook|Konkrook]]'s internal politics to the wider inter-kingdom web [003-007].

### The Palace Revolt

Gurgurk's plotting culminates in an armed revolt at Jaikark's Palace, with
the Household Guards either mutinied or wiped out — all except the twenty
[[kragan-rifles|Kragan Rifles]] loaned to Jaikark by [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] [007-027]. Von Schlichten
weighs the implications: if Jaikark survives, [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] can dictate terms;
if Jaikark dies, "we'll have Gurgurk's head off for it, and then take over
[[konkrook|Konkrook]]" [007-030].

Gurgurk also employs poison — a "[[geek]] servant did the job, on orders from
Gurgurk and possibly [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]" [007-032].

## Characteristics & Traits

Gurgurk embodies the [[ulleran|Ulleran]] political archetype of the scheming vizier —
intelligent and ambitious but ultimately short-sighted. His manipulation of
[[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] reveals his method: using proxy forces rather than direct confrontation,
maintaining plausible deniability while his agents do his work.

However, [[eric-blount|Blount]]'s assessment cuts to Gurgurk's fatal flaw: "I think [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s
bigger than Gurgurk ever can be" [002-013]. The implication is that Gurgurk
has unleashed a force — [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s mass religious movement — that he cannot
control, underestimating [[rakkeed-the-prophet|the Prophet]]'s independence and the momentum of the
crusade he has been funding.

His physical description — black skin with heavy red speckling, elaborate
ceremonial armor — reflects the [[konkrook|Konkrook]]an elite's fusion of native warrior
culture with imported [[terra|Terra]]n symbols of power [003-003].
