# Terran Military Doctrine

Terran military doctrine on Uller is shaped by the constraints of colonial warfare: a small number of Terran personnel commanding larger forces of indigenous troops, operating under political restrictions that prohibit the most powerful available weapons, against an enemy who knows the terrain and can blend into the civilian population.

## Native Troops and Mercenaries

The backbone of Company military power on Uller is not Terran regulars but native auxiliaries — primarily the Kragan Rifles. These mercenary regiments are equipped with modified Terran Federation infantry rifles, "stocked and sighted to conform to their physical peculiarities, with long, thorn-like, triangular bayonets" (001-039). Kragans provide the manpower that the small Terran population cannot supply.

The Tenth Native Uller Infantry (N.U.N.I.) and Fifth Zirk Cavalry represent other categories of native troops — some loyal, some infiltrated by Rakkeed's disciples attempting "to promote disaffection" among the enlisted ranks (006-007). The reliability of native troops is the central variable in any military calculation, and the uprising tests these loyalties to destruction.

## The Nuclear Prohibition as Doctrine

The Company's refusal to allow nuclear weapons on Uller is as much military doctrine as political policy. Von Schlichten explains the logic: "It's the same principle as not allowing guards who have to go in among the convicts to carry firearms" (006-031). Nuclear weapons in Terran hands would create a risk of capture; nuclear weapons in native hands would be catastrophic.

This doctrine survives until the Keegarkan bomb project forces its abandonment. The recognition that Orgzild already possesses nuclear capability makes the prohibition irrelevant — the enemy has crossed the threshold, and the only response is to cross it in return (012-064).

## Contragravity as Force Multiplier

Contragravity-enabled maneuver warfare is the Terran tactical advantage. Aircars, airjeeps, combat-cars, and armed freighters provide mobility that native forces cannot match. The Kragan airlift — moving two thousand troops from Kankad's Town to Konkrook using every available contragravity vehicle — demonstrates the operational reach this technology provides (010-007).

When the enemy captures contragravity equipment, the tactical balance shifts. Von Schlichten's urgent order to "knock it down as fast as they try to lift out with it" reflects the doctrine that contragravity superiority must be preserved at all costs (008-029).

## Intelligence and Counterinsurgency

Colonel Grinell's Intelligence Section provides the information that makes targeted action possible. The discovery that Rakkeed was funded by Keegark, the identification of his disciples through intercepted letters, and the post-uprising interrogation of captured Keegarkan officials all depend on an intelligence apparatus that operates in the gaps between native kingdoms (002-012)(002-044)(012-058).

The doctrine that emerges from the uprising emphasizes preemption over reaction: orbital surveillance stations, dispersed Residencies, and the elevation of proven allies (Kragans) to administrative roles that reduce reliance on unreliable native power structures.

## Narrative Significance

Military doctrine in the novel reflects the broader theme of adaptation under constraint. The Company's pre-uprising doctrine assumes native submission; the uprising forces a painful revision. Von Schlichten emerges as the architect of a new doctrine that combines technological superiority (contragravity, orbital surveillance) with political restructuring (Kragan proconsuls) and, when necessary, overwhelming force (nuclear weapons). The doctrine is pragmatic, unsentimental, and effective — a military expression of Piper's hard-nosed view of colonial governance.
