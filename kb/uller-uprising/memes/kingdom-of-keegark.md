# Kingdom of Keegark

The Kingdom of Keegark was one of the major native Ulleran political entities, ruled by King Orgzild and situated in a strategically significant region of the planet. Keegark became the epicenter of the nuclear conspiracy that transformed the Uller Uprising from a conventional colonial rebellion into a crisis of existential proportions. By the uprising's end, the kingdom had been destroyed by nuclear bombing — its capital city annihilated, its king almost certainly killed, and its political structure erased from Uller's map (015-034).

## Political Structure

Like the other Ulleran kingdoms, Keegark was a hereditary monarchy operating under the suzerainty of the Chartered Uller Company. King Orgzild occupied the ambiguous position common to all native rulers under Company rule: he was sovereign within his kingdom's traditional boundaries but subordinate to the Company's political officers in all matters of inter-kingdom relations, trade policy, and military affairs (002-018).

Orgzild's court was more sophisticated than the Company's intelligence services appreciated. The Keegarkan ambassador stationed in Konkrook maintained independent diplomatic contacts — including with Rakkeed the Prophet, whom the ambassador hosted at his residence during the Prophet's travels between kingdoms (002-019). This diplomatic infrastructure provided Keegark with intelligence capabilities that rivaled the Company's own, operating through networks of personal relationships and traditional obligations rather than the technological surveillance systems on which the Company relied.

## The Nuclear Conspiracy

Keegark's defining historical role is as the originator of the native nuclear weapons program. Orgzild's engineers — working from technical information gathered by Prince Gorkrink during his year on Niflheim working alongside Dr. Murillo — succeeded in designing and constructing operational fission weapons (013-006). The Keegarkans exploited the Company's nuclear mining operations on Niflheim as an inadvertent training ground: Ulleran workers who witnessed daily nuclear detonations and handled fissile materials absorbed enough technical understanding to guide a weapons program (013-012).

The Keegarkan nuclear program was not a crude terrorist device but a systematic engineering effort. Orgzild's engineers understood the principles of critical mass, neutron initiation, and high-explosive lensing sufficiently well to construct weapons that the Company assessed as operationally viable — capable of being delivered against targets and producing yields comparable to early Terran fission weapons (014-009).

Orgzild's alliance with Prince Gurgurk of Konkrook and, indirectly, with Rakkeed's religious movement, transformed Keegark's nuclear capability from a regional deterrent into a war-fighting asset. The plan was for Keegark's bombs to support a coordinated multi-kingdom offensive that would drive the Company from Uller entirely (002-019).

## Destruction

The kingdom's destruction came at von Schlichten's hand. Three nuclear bombs, assembled under the Company's emergency Operation Hildegarde program, were dropped on Keegark from the cutter *Elmoran* (015-011). Approximately 150,000 Ullerans were killed in the detonations (015-034). The capital city was destroyed; Orgzild's palace, government, and nuclear facilities were reduced to radioactive rubble.

The destruction was total enough that the Kingdom of Keegark ceased to exist as a political entity. The post-uprising settlement would have to account for a geopolitical vacuum where one of Uller's major kingdoms had been — a problem that the Company, in its desperation to end the military threat, had not considered.

## Legacy

Keegark's nuclear program — and its destruction — left an ambiguous legacy on Uller. To the other native kingdoms, Keegark demonstrated both the possibility of matching Terran military technology and the catastrophic cost of attempting to do so. To the Company, Keegark demonstrated that the technological monopoly on which colonial rule depended was not absolute — that a sufficiently determined native power could, given time and opportunity, close the technology gap in the most dangerous possible way.
