# Assassination of Governor Harrington

The assassination of Governor Sidney Harrington was the opening strike of the Uller Uprising. At precisely 22:10 local time, Harrington was poisoned by a native servant — a "geek" in local parlance — acting on orders from Prince Gurgurk of Konkrook, with the probable complicity of Rakkeed the Prophet (007-032). The assassination was the signal for a coordinated multi-kingdom offensive against the Chartered Uller Company.

## The Attack

Harrington was poisoned in his quarters by a trusted domestic servant — one of the many native workers whom the Company employed in intimate proximity to its senior personnel (007-031). The method — poison rather than a direct assault — reflected the Ulleran approach to high-value targets: deniable, quiet, and capable of being timed with precision. The servant had been positioned in Harrington's household as a sleeper agent, awaiting activation by the conspiracy.

The timing was deliberate. Harrington's death was intended to decapitate the Company's civilian leadership at the exact moment the military phase of the uprising commenced. Without a governor, the Company's political apparatus would be paralyzed; without Harrington's personal authority, no other civilian official could legally assume command of Company forces.

## The Broadcast

Within thirty-three minutes of the assassination, news of Harrington's death was broadcast across Uller via the all-station priority telecast system — the Company's own communications network, now turned against it (006-039). The broadcast served multiple purposes: it confirmed to conspirators across the planet that phase one was complete, it demoralized isolated Terran personnel, and it signaled to native populations that the uprising had begun.

The speed of the broadcast demonstrated that the conspiracy had thoroughly penetrated the Company's communications apparatus. The priority telecast system was designed for emergency use by Company officials; that the assassins could activate it within half an hour meant they had compromised the system in advance.

## Consequences

Harrington's assassination immediately elevated General Carlos von Schlichten to supreme military authority on Uller. As the ranking military officer, von Schlichten invoked emergency powers and assumed command of all Company forces, both Terran and native (006-039). The transition from civilian to military governance — always a latent possibility in the colonial structure — was effected within hours.

The assassination also transformed the political character of the conflict. With Harrington dead, the Company's civilian administration effectively ceased to function. Von Schlichten was now answerable only to the Federation Government via hyperwave — a communications lag that gave him practical autonomy. The war that followed was von Schlichten's war: his strategic decisions, his tactical choices, and ultimately his nuclear strike against Keegark all traced their legal authority to the emergency powers he assumed in the hours after Harrington's death.

The identity of the specific servant who carried out the poisoning is recorded but ultimately incidental. What matters historically is that the Company's most intimate security — the native servants who cooked its officials' food, cleaned their quarters, and raised their children — had been penetrated at the highest level.
