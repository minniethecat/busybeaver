# Paula Quinton

Paula Quinton arrives on [[uller|Uller]] as an investigator for the [[extraterrestrials-rights-association|Extraterrestrials' Rights Association]], tasked with documenting labor abuses in [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s polar mining operations. By the novel's end, she has become [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s most trusted aide, his romantic partner, and an active participant in the military defense of [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] Reservation at [[konkrook|Konkrook]].

## Background

Paula is introduced on the contragravity armor-tender crossing from [[niflheim|Niflheim]] to [[uller|Uller]], where she shares candy with a young [[terra|Terra]]n soldier and watches [[nuclear-weapons|nuclear mining]] detonations on the viewscreen (P02-011)(P02-012). She has never seen nuclear weapons used for mining before — "only by [[telecast]], back Sol-side... Test-shots at the [[terran-federation-space-navy|Federation Navy]] proving-ground on Mars" — marking her as a newcomer to the colonial frontier.

Her assignment is to observe labor conditions at the North Pole mines. The [[extraterrestrials-rights-association|Extraterrestrials' Rights Association]] is a humanitarian watchdog organization that pressures the [[terran-federation|Federation Government]] for native rights protections, making Paula's presence politically sensitive for [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]].

### The Konkrook Attack

Paula's investigation nearly costs her life within hours of arriving on [[uller|Uller]]. Visiting the native clergyman Keeluk (later revealed as the alter ego of [[rakkeed-the-prophet|Rakkeed the Prophet]]), she and her companion Mohammed Ferriera are attacked by a mob. Von Schlichten personally rescues them in a brutal close-quarters engagement, killing multiple [[ulleran|Ullerans]] in the process (001-034)(001-036).

### From Observer to Combatant

As the uprising escalates, Paula transforms from a humanitarian observer into an active combatant and staff officer. She is present at [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s command post during the siege of the [[konkrook|Konkrook]] Reservation, briefing officers on the [[niflheim|Niflheim]] nuclear connection that explains how the [[keegark|Keegark]]ans acquired weapons knowledge (013-006). By the later stages of the siege, she and Barney Mordkovitz "virtually ordered him to get some sleep" — she has become indispensable enough to manage the commander himself (010-016).

## Relationship with von Schlichten

Paula's relationship with [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] develops organically through shared danger. He rescues her; she stays to fight alongside him; by the novel's climactic scenes, they are inseparable. During the bombing mission against [[keegark|Keegark]], "Paula Quinton fell in behind him" as he boards the cutter *Elmoran* — she accompanies him on the nuclear strike itself (015-011).

> "Paula Quinton fell in behind him" — aboard the *Elmoran* for the nuclear strike on Keegark (015-011)

Their partnership represents one of the novel's few moments of genuine human warmth amid the brutal calculus of [[terran-military-doctrine|colonial warfare]].

## Characteristics

Paula is intelligent, resilient, and adaptable. She arrives as a naive idealist — she has never seen nuclear weapons used in mining, let alone combat — and develops rapidly into a hardened colonial operative. Unlike [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] lifers around her, she retains a moral perspective: she is the one who asks the uncomfortable question about why [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] can find such a following, demanding that [[eric-blount|Blount]] and [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] confront the human cost of [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] rule (004-031). But her moral awareness does not prevent her from participating in the defense and, ultimately, the nuclear destruction of [[keegark|Keegark]].
