# Carlos von Schlichten

General Carlos von Schlichten is the [[governor-general|Governor-General]] of the [[chartered-uller-company|Chartered Uller Company]] and the central protagonist of the novel. A career military officer with deep experience on [[uller|Uller]], he commands the [[terra|Terra]]n defense during the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]], makes the decision to use nuclear weapons against [[keegark|Keegark]], and architects the post-war political settlement. He is distinguished by his monocle, his chain-smoking, and his pragmatic ruthlessness.

## Background

Von Schlichten is introduced as General Carlos von Schlichten, commanding officer of [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] forces on [[uller|Uller]]. He wears a monocle, flexes his hands in his gloves as a characteristic gesture, and is never seen without a cigarette — he "threw his cigarette away" only to immediately light another, chain-smoking throughout the novel (001-002).

> "Von Schlichten parried the cut on the steel shaft of his mace. 'Suddabit yourself, you geek bastard!' he shouted back, ramming the spike-end into the opal-filled mouth." (001-034)

His exact origins are never specified, but his name suggests German or Central European ancestry. He has spent years on [[uller|Uller]] and knows the planet, its politics, and its people intimately. He speaks [[lingua-terra|Lingua Terra]] fluently and can communicate directly with native kings like [[king-kankad|Kankad]] without mechanical aids (005-038). His military experience encompasses both conventional [[terran-federation|Terran Federation]] training and the specific demands of colonial [[terran-military-doctrine|counterinsurgency]].

### The Konkrook Riot

Von Schlichten's first major action in the novel is the rescue of [[paula-quinton|Paula Quinton]] and Mohammed Ferriera from a mob in [[konkrook|Konkrook]]. Piloting his command-car personally, he directs his crew — Sergeant Harry Quong and Corporal Hassan Bogdanoff — in a brutal close-quarters engagement. The sequence establishes him as a hands-on commander who leads from the front.

### The Assassination of Harrington

When [[governor-general|Governor-General]] [[sid-harrington|Sid Harrington]] is poisoned — "a [[geek]] servant did the job, on orders from [[prince-gurgurk|Gurgurk]] and possibly [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]" (007-032) — von Schlichten assumes command. His first priority is preventing the fragmentation of [[terra|Terra]]n authority: he immediately flies to [[konkrook|Konkrook]] to stabilize the situation and coordinate the defense of multiple Residencies simultaneously.

### The Nuclear Decision

Von Schlichten's most consequential decision is the authorization of [[operation-hildegarde|Operation Hildegarde]] — the construction and use of nuclear weapons against [[keegark|Keegark]]. He initially opposes nuclear armament: "It's the same principle as not allowing guards who have to go in among the convicts to carry firearms" (006-031). But when intelligence confirms that [[orgzild|King Orgzild]] already possesses nuclear capability, he reverses his position without hesitation:

> "I agree that Keegark should be wiped out, and I'd like to have an immediate estimate on the time it'll take to build a nuclear bomb." (012-061)

The bombing of [[keegark|Keegark]] kills an estimated hundred and fifty thousand [[ulleran|Ullerans]]. Von Schlichten witnesses the mushroom cloud and reflects: "he had to remind himself that they were the people who had burned [[eric-blount|Eric Blount]] and Hendrik Lemoyne alive" (015-043). He resolves that "this must be the last [[nuclear-weapons|nuclear bomb]] used on [[uller|Uller]]" and begins planning the post-war order (015-044).

### The Post-War Settlement

Von Schlichten's closing vision is a militarized [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] rule: orbital surveillance stations, dispersed Residencies, and the elevation of the [[kragan|Kragans]] from mercenaries to proconsular administrators. His famous closing line — "[[uller|Uller]] was not ready for membership in the [[terran-federation|Terran Federation]]; then its people must bow to the [[terra|Terra]]n Pax" (015-046) — defines his philosophy: colonial governance through overwhelming force, applied without sentiment but also without cruelty for its own sake.

## Characteristics

Von Schlichten is pragmatic to the point of ruthlessness, but he is not cruel. He kills efficiently, makes hard decisions without flinching, and treats his native allies (particularly the [[kragan|Kragans]]) with a respect that contrasts with the casual racism of characters like [[eric-blount|Eric Blount]]. His monocle and chain-smoking give him an anachronistic, almost Prussian air — a deliberate character choice by Piper that grounds the space-operatic setting in recognizable military archetypes.

He is also politically astute. His confidence that [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] board will confirm his assumption of the [[governor-general|Governor-General]]ship rests on his understanding that "the [[banking-cartel|Banking Cartel]] and the mercantile interests in [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] are going to realize" the necessity of military control after the uprising (012-006). He navigates the competing interests of [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]], the [[terran-federation|Federation Government]], the native kings, and the [[kragan|Kragan]] allies with the same tactical skill he brings to combat.
