# Konk Valley

The Konk Valley was a river valley on [[uller|Uller]] running southward toward [[konkrook|Konkrook]], parallel to the [[hoork-river|Hoork River]] valley. It was the site of one of the most dramatic military engagements of the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]]: the aerial pursuit and nuclear destruction of the captured Boer-class freighter *Jan Smuts*.

## Geography

The Konk Valley lay east of the Hoork River valley, separated from it by a mountain range whose crest provided radar coverage over both valleys simultaneously. The *Aldebaran* was positioned "north along the crest of the mountain-range between the Hoork and Konk river-valleys, where she could cover both with her own radar and other detection-devices" (014-038). The valley extended from the northern interior southward to Konkrook, a distance of at least five hundred miles — the nuclear incident occurred "five hundred miles north of Konkrook" (015-043).

The valley's terrain provided natural cover for ships attempting to approach Konkrook undetected. Von Schlichten recognized that an enemy vessel "may think they can slip down the Konk Valley either undetected or mistaken for one of our ships from North Uller" (014-039), making it a potential avenue of infiltration that required active monitoring.

## The Konk Valley Nuclear Incident

The valley was the scene of the fleet action that resulted in the nuclear destruction of the *Jan Smuts*, one of the Boer-class freighters captured by King Orgzild's forces at Keegark. The pursuit was tracked in agonizing real time from the command center: "Time crawled slowly around the clock-face on the wall, the sixty-five-second minutes of Uller dragging like hours. The spots that marked the enemy ship and her hunters crawled, too" (014-040).

The *Gaucho*, pursuing the *Jan Smuts* south down the valley, made radar contact when the freighter "had begun to turn in a hairpin-shaped course and was coming south down the Konk Valley" (014-040). When the interception occurred, the result was catastrophic:

> "In the direct-vision screen from the *Sky-Spy*, the whole countryside of the Konk Valley, five hundred miles north of Konkrook, was lighted. The heat and radiation detectors were going insane. And in the shifting confusion on the radar-screen, there was no trace either of the *Jan Smuts* or the *Gaucho*." (015-043)

The mutual destruction of the freighter and its pursuer consumed both vessels and irradiated a significant stretch of the valley. Commander Prinsloo was ordered to "launch airboats and make a search; cautioned him to be careful of radiation, but to take no chances on any of the *Gaucho*'s complement being still alive and in need of help" (015-043).

## Strategic Significance

The Konk Valley incident demonstrated the catastrophic potential of nuclear-armed freighters operating in the planet's geographic corridors, and validated von Schlichten's decision to commit the fleet to intercepting Orgzild's captured ships before they could reach population centers. The destruction in the valley also served as a precursor to the larger nuclear event at Keegark — a demonstration of what happens when nuclear weapons are deployed in Uller's narrow geography.

Shortly after the Konk Valley engagement, the second Boer-class freighter, the *Oom Paul Kruger*, was detected approaching from [[bwork|Bwork]] to the east (015-043), confirming that Orgzild had deployed multiple nuclear-armed vessels along Uller's geographic corridors.
