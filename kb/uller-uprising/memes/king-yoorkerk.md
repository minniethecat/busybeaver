# King Yoorkerk

King Yoorkerk was a silicon-based [[ulleran|Ulleran]] monarch who ruled the northern kingdom of Grank during the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]], known for his opportunistic political maneuvering and attempt to play both the [[terra|Terra]]n [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] and the native rebellion against each other.

## Overview

King Yoorkerk embodied the political pragmatism of the [[ulleran|Ulleran]] northern kingdoms. At the outbreak of the uprising, he seized [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] airport and docks at Grank, captured the starships *Procyon* and *Northern Lights*, and surrounded the Residency — while carefully avoiding any irreversible act of hostility. His strategy of hedging was described by [[carlos-von-schlichten|Carlos von Schlichten]] as "trying to play both sides at once" （009-007）. When the tide turned against the rebellion, Yoorkerk pivoted swiftly to become [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s "great and good friend," contributing troops and handing over the captured prophet [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]].

## Background

Yoorkerk ruled Grank, one of the northern [[ulleran|Ulleran]] kingdoms. His reign was characterized by the same arbitrary royal authority common among [[ulleran|Ulleran]] monarchies, maintained through "the head-payment system, and these arbitrary mass arrests and tax-delinquency imprisonments that are nothing but slave-raids by the [[geek]] princes on their own people" （012-061）. He commanded at least twelve named infantry regiments with the kind of flamboyant [[ulleran|Ulleran]] titles — "Glorious-and-Terrible," "Firm-in-Adversity," "Strength-of-the-Throne," and "Forever-Admirable" among them — that characterized native military organization.

## Role in the Uprising

### Seizure of Company Assets

When the uprising erupted, Yoorkerk moved quickly to secure Grank's [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] facilities. His troops seized [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] airport and docks, took control of the *Procyon* — "the only real warship on [[uller|Uller]], with a main battery of four 200 mm guns" — and its sister ship *Northern Lights*, and surrounded the Residency where Resident-Agent Guido Karamessinis was stationed （010-013）. Von Schlichten assessed the situation as a calculated hedge: "if the rebellion's put down, he'll come forward as your friend and protector; if we're wiped out elsewhere, he'll yell '*Znidd suddabit!*' and swamp you" （009-007）.

### Coerced Realignment

After [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] crushed King Firkked at [[konkrook|Konkrook]], [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] deployed Hideyoshi O'Leary with a psychological weapon: an audiovisual recording of Firkked's severed head displayed on a [[kragan|Kragan]] assegai. Von Schlichten, adapting an idea from "my Nazi ancestors of four hundred and fifty years ago," treated Yoorkerk to this "movie-show" to force his compliance （012-061）. The tactic worked — Yoorkerk released both ships and adopted the narrative that "he is and always was the true and ever-loving friend of [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]" who had merely "acted to prevent certain disloyal elements from harming the people and property of [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]" （015-043）.

### Handover of Rakkeed

As further proof of his realignment, Yoorkerk surrendered [[rakkeed-the-prophet|Rakkeed the Prophet]], whom he had been holding at the Palace as insurance. O'Leary noted that "Yoorkerk was holding our ships and our people in case we lost; he was also holding [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] at the Palace in case we won" （015-044）. Von Schlichten arranged for [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] to be tried by local priests for "blasphemy, heresy, impersonating a prophet, practicing witchcraft without a license" and executed by Yoorkerk's own soldiers, ensuring the King would be irrevocably implicated in the act （015-045）.

### Troop Contributions to Konkrook

Yoorkerk contributed four infantry regiments to the [[konkrook|Konkrook]] campaign — the Seventh, Glorious-and-Terrible; the Fourth, Firm-in-Adversity; the Second, Strength-of-the-Throne; and the Twelfth, Forever-Admirable. These regiments, described by [[paula-quinton|Paula Quinton]] as "the sorriest-looking rabble I ever saw," were armed with Volund-made [[terra|Terra]]n-style rifles. Themistocles M'zangwe integrated them into [[kragan|Kragan]] regiments by battalions, a treatment that made them "just enough insulted… to try to make a good showing" （XII-029）. Von Schlichten recognized the risk that the troops might "develop into reasonably good troops themselves, to the undesired improvement of King Yoorkerk's army" （XII-029）.

## Characteristics & Traits

Yoorkerk's defining characteristic was his calculating opportunism. He was one of two northern kings whom [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] expected would "give us plenty of pretexts" for a [[terra|Terra]]n takeover of power, noting that "if I know that pair as well as I think I do, Jonkvank and Yoorkerk will give us plenty of pretexts, before long" （012-061）. His intelligence was recognized even by his adversaries — [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] remarked that "that [[geek]] is too smart for his own good. Some of these days he's going to play both ends against the middle and both ends'll fold in on him and smash him" （015-044）.
