# Hoork River

The Hoork River was a dry river valley on [[uller|Uller]] that ran southward from the polar regions through [[skilk|Skilk]] to the equatorial city of [[keegark|Keegark]], forming a major north-south geographic corridor. Its broad, arid riverbed served as a highway for military movements and a key strategic axis during the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]].

## Geography

The Hoork River valley extended from the northern polar regions to the delta at Keegark, where it emptied into the bay adjacent to the [[takkad-sea|Takkad Sea]]. The river was normally dry — the text consistently refers to "the dry Hoork River" — indicating either a seasonal watercourse or a fossil riverbed typical of Uller's arid equatorial landscape (009-033)(012-061). The "broad valley and dry bed of the Hoork River" provided a natural route through the flint-mountain terrain that otherwise restricted overland travel (012-061).

The Hoork ran parallel to the [[konk-valley|Konk Valley]] to its east, with a mountain range separating the two watersheds. This parallel geography made both valleys strategically interdependent during the naval campaign: von Schlichten positioned the *Aldebaran* "north along the crest of the mountain-range between the Hoork and Konk river-valleys, where she could cover both with her own radar" (014-038).

## Military Significance

The Hoork River served as a critical defensive line during the uprising. When native forces approached Skilk, Colonel Jarman received orders to "start flying patrols up and down along the Hoork River; this may not be the only gang that's coming out to see us" (009-033). The riverbed provided a natural defensive boundary — fighting in Skilk was described as occurring "entirely on the eastern bank of the dry Hoork River" after reinforcements pushed the native forces back across it (011-048).

The valley was also a route for the movement of Terran naval assets. The *Northern Star* was sent "south down the Hoork Valley" as part of the coordinated fleet response to the Keegarkan nuclear threat (014-038). When Keegark's captured Boer-class freighter *Jan Smuts* was detected lifting off, it was tracked moving "up the Hoork River" valley, using the geographic corridor to approach its target (015-043).

## The Keegark Delta

At its southern terminus, the Hoork River formed the delta upon which the city of Keegark was built. Approaching from the air, von Schlichten saw "the city grew out of the ground-mist, a parti-colored blur at the delta of the dry Hoork River, and then a color-splashed triangle between the river and the bay and the hills on the landward side" (015-046). The river thus defined Keegark's physical geography, channeling the approach to the city and framing its destruction by the *Dire Dawn* nuclear weapon.

## Travel & Transport

The Hoork Valley served as a primary travel corridor between the northern and equatorial regions. Von Schlichten and Paula Quinton traveled south along the valley by aircar as "the sun slid lower and lower toward the horizon behind them" (012-061). The valley's width and relatively flat terrain, carved by ancient water flow, made it the natural route for contragravity vehicles and airboats moving between Skilk, Keegark, and the settlements in between.
