# Spaceport

Interstellar landing and launch facilities on Uller, operated by the Chartered Uller Company, that functioned as critical infrastructure for Terran-Ulleran commerce, military logistics, and political negotiation — most notably in the contentious "spaceport deal" proposed by King Orgzild of Keegark (L435, L437).

## Gongonk Island Spaceport

The primary Company spaceport was located on Gongonk Island, adjacent to the Company's administrative center at Konkrook. The facility featured a ring of mooring-pylons for contragravity ships; these pylons stood empty after the *City of Pretoria* lifted out for Terra two days before the uprising began (L662). The spaceport complex included repair docks where Port-Captain Leavitt confined native laborers during the uprising, keeping them covered with the forward 90 mm gun of the *Northern Star* (L1000).

During the uprising, the spaceport became a critical defensive perimeter. By the second day of fighting, Company forces had contained the rebels "between the spaceport and the native-troops and labor barracks, and down the east side of the island to the farms" (L1089). The spaceport's open terrain and existing defensive infrastructure made it one of the more defensible sectors of the Company's island holdings.

The facility also proved essential for the nuclear phase of Operation Hildegarde. When the Company began constructing atomic weapons, engineers discovered that large empty liquid-oxygen flasks at the spaceport could accommodate fissionables, explosive charges, tampers, and detonators, eliminating the need to cast specialized bomb casings (L1971).

## The Keegark Spaceport Deal

The most politically significant spaceport on Uller was one that did not yet exist. King Orgzild of Keegark proposed building a new spaceport at his city, offering land and forced labor while expecting the Company to furnish structural steel, machinery, and engineering expertise (L437). The proposal was economically advantageous for both parties: the Company would gain a facility it "didn't really need," while Orgzild would capture the commercial traffic that currently flowed through Konkrook.

Company leadership viewed the deal with suspicion. Eric Blount observed that Orgzild's hospitality toward Rakkeed — the anti-Terran religious leader who was "a welcome guest at his embassy here, and at the Royal Palace at Keegark" — suggested a more complex agenda: "I'm beginning to wonder if he isn't fomenting trouble for us here at Konkrook to make us willing to move our main base to his city" (L437).

Colonel Jarman voiced a darker interpretation at a Company dinner: "I wonder if the idea back of that spaceport proposition isn't to get us concentrated at Keegark, where Orgzild could wipe us all out in one surprise blow" (L797). Keaveney, however, dismissed such concerns, arguing that Orgzild "had things under pretty tight control at Keegark" and "would not allow a few fanatics to do anything to prejudice these spaceport negotiations" (L795).

## Strategic Value

Spaceports represented more than transportation infrastructure; they were nodes of military power. The Company's control of all spaceport facilities — both the operational Gongonk Island complex and the proposed Keegark site — meant that the Terrans held a monopoly on interstellar travel to and from Uller. No Ulleran could leave the planet without Company permission; no outside power could land without passing through Company-controlled facilities.

Colonel MacKinnon's fate at Keegark underscored the spaceport's strategic significance. His last message, reporting that "the geeks had penetrated his last defenses and that he was about to blow up the Residency," was followed by Keegark going off the air entirely (L1397). The loss of a potential second spaceport site to rebel forces would have been catastrophic, splitting Company forces between two widely separated locations.

## Narrative Significance

The spaceport deal serves as a political MacGuffin that illuminates the web of competing interests on pre-uprising Uller. Orgzild's offer is simultaneously an economic opportunity, a potential trap, and a reflection of the inter-kingdom rivalries that the Company both exploited and was vulnerable to. The deal's ambiguity — was Orgzild a pragmatic modernizer or a conspirator preparing a death blow? — mirrors the larger uncertainty about native intentions that pervades the novel's first half.

The spaceport's physical presence on Gongonk Island, dominating the skyline with its empty mooring-pylons, functions as a visual symbol of Terran technological superiority and isolation. The pylons' emptiness after the *City of Pretoria* departs (L662) creates a sense of abandonment that foreshadows the uprising; the Terrans' link to their homeworld is physically present but operationally absent.
