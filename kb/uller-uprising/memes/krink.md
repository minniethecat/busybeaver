# Krink

Krink was a northern [[ulleran|Ulleran]] kingdom ruled by King Jonkvank, serving as a crucial pro-[[chartered-uller-company|Company]] ally and military staging point during the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]].

## Overview

Krink was one of the major northern [[ulleran|Ulleran]] political entities, situated approximately five hundred miles northeast of [[skilk|Skilk]] across the mountains. Under King Jonkvank, Krink maintained a pro-[[terra|Terra]]n alignment, subsidized by the [[chartered-uller-company|Chartered Uller Company]], in contrast to the anti-[[terra|Terra]]n stance of neighboring [[skilk|Skilk]] under King Firkked. The city of Krink housed [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] smelting operations that processed polar ore, and during the uprising, Krink's loyal troops — the Sixth North [[uller|Uller]] Native Infantry and [[zirk|Zirk]] cavalry companies — provided critical military reinforcement to [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s defense of [[konkrook|Konkrook]] and the assault on [[skilk|Skilk]].

## Background

Krink's political economy was shaped by the polar mining trade. Ore scooped from the Arctic surface during the brief summer was flown "down to [[skilk|Skilk]] and Krink and Grank, where it's smelted through the winter" （005-026）. The [[ulleran|Ulleran]] practice of combining metallurgy with food preparation — using smelter heat to thaw frozen provisions — was a distinctive cultural feature of the northern kingdoms.

Krink's internal politics reflected the broader [[ulleran|Ulleran]] tension between feudal barons and urban merchant classes. [[eric-blount|Blount]] described the situation: "At Krink, King Jonkvank has the support of his barons, but he's afraid of his urban bourgeoisie, and we pay him a handsome subsidy, so he's pro-[[terra|Terra]]n and anti-[[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]" （005-048）. In contrast to [[skilk|Skilk]], where Firkked courted the merchant class, Jonkvank maintained his authority through the traditional baronial structure, with [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] subsidies reinforcing his position.

## Role in the Uprising

### The Palace Coup Attempt

When the uprising erupted, Krink experienced its own internal convulsion. The Resident-Agent Francis Xavier Shapiro reported "rioting in the city and an attempted palace-revolution against King Jonkvank," with the Residency under attack （008-045）. Crucially, Jonkvank's Sixth North [[uller|Uller]] Native Infantry — unlike the mutinous soldiery — remained loyal, along with "the two companies of [[zirk|Zirk]] cavalry at Krink" （008-045）. The palace coup was orchestrated by Jonkvank's War Minister Hurkkurk, whom Jonkvank later killed, displaying his severed head as proof （010-007）.

### Reinforcement of Konkrook

From his command post at [[skilk|Skilk]], [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] coordinated with Jonkvank to airlift loyal Krinkan troops south. The *Northern Star* made repeated flights, landing "the regiment Murderers, armed with first-quality [[terra|Terra]]n infantry-rifles and a few machine-guns and bazookas, at the Palace at Krink" and returning with additional regiments including the [[jeel|Jeel]]-Feeders （011-060）. By the afternoon of the siege, "the three-lane street connecting the Palace and the Residency had been widened to six, and then to eight" as Krinkan reinforcements poured in （011-060）.

### Assault on Skilk

Krink's contribution extended to the final assault on [[skilk|Skilk]]. "It was five hundred miles to Krink; three hours after lifting out, the *Northern Star* was back again, with two more of King Jonkvank's infantry regiments" （012-031）. The [[zirk|Zirk]] cavalry from Krink fought alongside [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] forces in the streets of [[skilk|Skilk]], "pounding and hacking at a mass of [[skilk|Skilk]]ans whose uniform lack of costume prevented distinguishing between soldiery and townsfolk" （012-036）.

### Post-Victory Settlement

After the fall of [[skilk|Skilk]], [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] engineered a political settlement that made Jonkvank viceroy of [[skilk|Skilk]] while remaining King of Krink. The herald announced: "His Majesty, Jonkvank, King of Krink!" at the victory ceremony, attended by "Hideyoshi O'Leary and Francis N. Shapiro shepherding" the [[ulleran|Ulleran]] nobles （012-088）. Von Schlichten emphasized that Jonkvank would "travel back and forth between Krink and [[skilk|Skilk]]," keeping "the Spear of Krink in Krink, and the Spear of [[skilk|Skilk]] in [[skilk|Skilk]]" （012-100）.

## Characteristics & Traits

Krink as a kingdom was characterized by its strategic pragmatism. Under Jonkvank, it maintained a calculated pro-[[chartered-uller-company|Company]] alignment driven by subsidies and baronial loyalty rather than ideological commitment. Its feudal structure — with "land-owning barons" dealing directly with [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] — provided a more stable political foundation than the urban-merchant-dominated politics of [[skilk|Skilk]] （005-046）. The geographical position five hundred miles northeast of [[skilk|Skilk]], accessible only by contragravity flight, gave Krink a degree of insulation from [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]]'s anti-[[terra|Terra]]n agitation.
