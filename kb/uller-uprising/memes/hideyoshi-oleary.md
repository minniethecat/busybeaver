# Colonel Hideyoshi O'Leary

Colonel Hideyoshi O'Leary served as adjutant and close aide to General [[carlos-von-schlichten|Carlos von Schlichten]] during the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]], acting as the general's most trusted field officer and diplomatic envoy.

## Overview

Hideyoshi O'Leary — known informally as "Hid" — was [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s adjutant and one of the most competent [[terra|Terra]]n officers on [[uller|Uller]]. Present from the novel's opening scene at the mountain fort through the final military operations, O'Leary combined investigative acumen with dry wit and operational reliability. Von Schlichten rated him as his finest adjutant, dispatching him on critical missions to Grank and Krink that required both military competence and political finesse. His promotion to brigadier-general during the campaign testified to his exceptional performance.

## Background

O'Leary held a colonel's commission in the [[chartered-uller-company|Chartered Uller Company]]'s military arm and served a two-year tour on [[niflheim|Niflheim]] early in his career, an experience he recalled with distaste: "Nobody ought to be employed on that planet!" （005-012）. His background included familiarity with [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] intelligence procedures — he conducted [[paula-quinton|Paula Quinton]]'s initial debriefing after the [[konkrook|Konkrook]] assassination attempt with professional restraint, asking "Just what happened, while you and Mr. Ferriera were in Keeluk's house" without sounding "official" （003-008）. Stationed primarily at [[konkrook|Konkrook]] before the uprising, he accompanied [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] on the inspection tour of the northern mountain forts.

## Role in the Uprising

### Reconnaissance at the Mountain Fort

O'Leary appears in the novel's opening military scene, joining [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] and Brigadier-General Themistocles M'zangwe in the command-car during the aerial survey of the [[skilk|Skilk]] pass. While [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] piloted, "Colonel Hideyoshi O'Leary was using the binoculars" （002-027）, his quiet competence marking him as a trusted member of the general's inner circle. During the survey, he anticipated confrontation with the approaching [[skilk|Skilk]]an mob, rapidly identifying the tactical situation.

### Crisis at Konkrook

During the assassination attempt on [[governor-general|Governor-General]] Sidney [[sid-harrington|Harrington]], O'Leary fought alongside [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] in hand-to-hand combat against the [[konkrook|Konkrook]] rioters. He "jumped forward after [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]], stuck his dagger into the neck of a rioter and left it there, then caught the girl around the waist with his free arm" （002-051）, helping rescue [[paula-quinton|Paula Quinton]] from the mob. This action demonstrated both courage under fire and protective instinct.

### The Pre-Uprising Staff Dinner

O'Leary participated in the crucial staff dinner that preceded the uprising. He supplied sardonic commentary on the [[ulleran|Ulleran]] practice of biting off "live chickens' heads, in a sideshow wild-man act" and noting how northern peasants kill "six-legged iguanas that they raise for food" （005-020）. He also showed keen political judgment, characterizing King Jonkvank as "as bloody-minded an old murderer as you'd care not to meet in a dark alley anywhere" （005-049） — an assessment that would later prove ironic when Jonkvank became [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s key northern ally.

### Night of the Uprising

When the uprising erupted, O'Leary was with [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] at the [[skilk|Skilk]] command post. During the early chaos, he was seen "in front of the situation-map" （010-047）, coordinating the tactical response. As the siege unfolded, he remained a steady presence in the command structure.

### Mission to Grank

Von Schlichten dispatched O'Leary on the critical mission to Grank with the *Northern Star* and a contingent of [[kragan-rifles|Kragan Rifles]]. His task was to secure the release of two [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] vessels — the *Northern Lights* and the cruiser *Procyon* — that King Yoorkerk had seized. O'Leary's approach employed psychological warfare: "Hideyoshi's going to treat King Yoorkerk to a movie-show" showing footage of King Firkked's defeat at [[skilk|Skilk]], including "Firkked's head leering at him off the point of that overgrown assegai" （012-068）. The demonstration succeeded brilliantly — Yoorkerk not only released the ships but also handed over [[rakkeed-the-prophet|Rakkeed the Prophet]], whom he had been harboring. O'Leary reported with satisfaction: "Yoorkerk was holding our ships and our people in case we lost; he was also holding [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] at the Palace in case we won" （013-037）.

### Northern Command

Following the Grank success, O'Leary established a northern headquarters coordinating Krink, [[skilk|Skilk]], and Grank. He negotiated Yoorkerk's military contribution — four infantry regiments including the Seventh Glorious-and-Terrible and the Fourth Firm-in-Adversity — which he delivered to [[konkrook|Konkrook]] aboard the *Northern Lights* （015-006）. In recognition of his achievements, [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] promoted him to brigadier-general （013-050）.

## Characteristics & Traits

O'Leary's defining characteristic was his dry, sardonic humor. When M'zangwe bet against [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s plan working, O'Leary responded "Pay me, Them; he didn't drop it" （013-037）. After the Grank mission's success, he joked about giving King Yoorkerk "a nice thirty-piece silver set" as a testimonial gift （013-048）. This wit masked sharp operational intelligence — he was [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s most trusted field officer, able to operate independently on sensitive diplomatic-military assignments far from the main command.

## Relationships

- **[[carlos-von-schlichten|Carlos von Schlichten]]**: Commanding officer and close professional relationship; [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] rated O'Leary as his best adjutant
- **Themistocles M'zangwe**: Fellow officer; the two shared an easy camaraderie and betting relationship
- **[[paula-quinton|Paula Quinton]]**: Initially debriefed her after the assassination attempt; later she temporarily replaced him as adjutant when he was sent north
- **King Yoorkerk**: Negotiated the release of [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] ships and [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] from the Grank monarch
- **King Jonkvank**: Escorted the King of Krink to [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s victory ceremony at [[skilk|Skilk]]
- **Harry Quong & Hassan Bogdanoff**: Fellow passengers in the command-car during the novel's opening reconnaissance
