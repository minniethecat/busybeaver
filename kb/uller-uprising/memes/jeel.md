The **Jeel** are a cannibal tribe within the broader [[ulleran|Ulleran]] species. They are recognized as distinct from the mainstream Ulleran kingdoms, largely due to their practice of cannibalism, which marks them as outsiders within Ulleran civilization.

## Cultural Distinction

Cannibalism among the Jeel is a defining cultural trait that separates them from other Ulleran groups such as the [[kragan|Kragan]] and the [[zirk|Zirk]]. While the specifics of Jeel cannibal practices are not exhaustively documented in Company anthropological records, the practice carries profound significance both within Ulleran society and in the perceptions of Terran observers.

The Jeel's cannibalism creates a complex dynamic in the colonial context. On one level, it reinforces Terran prejudices about Ulleran "savagery" and has been used to justify [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] paternalism. On another level, it places the Jeel at odds with other Ulleran groups who regard the practice with revulsion, fragmenting potential native solidarity against Company rule.

## Place in Ulleran Society

The Jeel occupy a marginalized position within the larger Ulleran social order. Their cannibalism renders them pariahs to mainstream Ulleran kingdoms, limiting their participation in the political and economic networks that connect other Ulleran groups. This marginalization may have been exacerbated by Company policies that selectively engage with accommodating groups like the Kragan while isolating harder-to-control populations.

## Colonial Context

From the perspective of the [[terran-federation-space-navy|Terran Federation]] and the [[banking-cartel|Banking Cartel]] that finances the Company, the Jeel represent one of the more challenging elements of Ulleran society to manage. Their cultural practices resist easy integration into the Company's labor extraction model, and their outsider status within Ulleran society makes them poor candidates for the kind of allied-native strategy that has proven effective with the Kragan Rifles (001-039).

Humanitarian observers, including representatives of the [[extraterrestrials-rights-association|Extraterrestrials' Rights Association]], face difficult questions regarding the Jeel: whether to condemn cannibalism as barbaric or to contextualize it as an indigenous cultural practice deserving of protection against colonial erasure.
