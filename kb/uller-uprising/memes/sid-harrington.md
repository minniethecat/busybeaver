# Sid Harrington

Sid Harrington is the [[governor-general|Governor-General]] of the [[chartered-uller-company|Chartered Uller Company]] on [[uller|Uller]] prior to the events of the main uprising. His assassination by poison — carried out at 22:10 by a native servant on orders from [[prince-gurgurk|Gurgurk]] and possibly [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] — removes [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s civilian leadership at a critical moment and thrusts General [[carlos-von-schlichten|Carlos von Schlichten]] into supreme command (006-039).

## Background

Harrington serves as [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s ranking civilian official on [[uller|Uller]], the representative of the [[chartered-uller-company|Chartered Uller Company]]'s board and the [[terran-federation|Federation Government]]'s delegated authority over the planet. His exact tenure is unspecified, but he has been on [[uller|Uller]] long enough to develop a relaxed, intellectual relationship with his subordinate officials (007-032).

He is a chess player — he and [[eric-blount|Eric Blount]], [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] Resident at [[konkrook|Konkrook]], play together regularly, using the game as a medium for political discussion (002-010). The game serves as a metaphor for the strategic thinking that governs colonial administration: each piece in its place, each move a calculation of force and position.

Harrington cultivates an air of detached confidence about the native question. When the first rumors of [[rakkeed-the-prophet|Rakkeed the Prophet]]'s movement reach [[konkrook|Konkrook]], he dismisses the threat with casual arrogance: "He's just a barbarian caravan-driver," Harrington tells his assembled officers, refusing to take seriously the possibility of a coordinated native uprising (002-014). This dismissal reflects the broader institutional blindness of [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] leadership to the conditions fueling [[ulleran|Ulleran]] resentment.

## Role in the Uprising

### The Pre-Uprising Period

In the weeks before the uprising, Harrington chairs the regular briefings at the [[governor-general|Governor-General]]'s residence where [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] officials and military officers discuss native affairs. These sessions reveal the fault lines within [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] leadership: [[eric-blount|Blount]] defends the harsh labor practices at the polar mines as necessary for dealing with "a naturally brutal race" (004-020), while [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] argues for pragmatic reform. Harrington listens to both sides but takes no decisive action, preferring to maintain the status quo.

Harrington's primary concern appears to be political stability and the avoidance of [[terran-federation|Federation]] intervention. He is aware that [[paula-quinton|Paula Quinton]]'s [[extraterrestrials-rights-association|Extraterrestrials' Rights Association]] investigation could generate pressure on [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] from [[terra|Earth]]-side humanitarian groups (007-032). His response is to allow the investigation while minimizing its practical impact — a characteristically bureaucratic approach to a problem that requires either moral courage or military escalation.

### The Assassination

At 22:10 on an evening during the escalating crisis, Harrington is poisoned. The assassin is a native servant — a "[[geek]]" in [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] parlance — who acts on instructions from [[prince-gurgurk|Gurgurk]], the [[keegark|Keegark]]an king, and possibly directly from [[rakkeed-the-prophet|Rakkeed the Prophet]] himself (006-039). The method — poison rather than direct violence — reflects the asymmetrical nature of native resistance at this stage: the [[ulleran|Ullerans]] cannot yet confront [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] forces directly, but they can strike at the leadership through infiltration and guile.

The assassination is a decapitation strike that should, by all reasonable military calculation, have thrown [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] into disarray. Instead, it has the opposite effect: it removes the cautious, bureaucratic civilian leadership and places command in the hands of a decisive military officer. Harrington's death is thus the turning point that sets the stage for [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s ruthless prosecution of the war, including the eventual nuclear destruction of [[keegark|Keegark]].

## Characteristics

Harrington represents the pre-war [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] establishment: well-meaning but complacent, comfortable with the structures of colonial exploitation as long as they operate smoothly, and fundamentally unable to imagine the natives as a serious military threat. His chess games with [[eric-blount|Blount]] — where he moves pieces on a board while the real game on [[uller|Uller]] moves toward catastrophe — symbolize the detachment of civilian administrators from the violent realities of [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] rule.

He is intelligent and well-educated, and unlike [[eric-blount|Blount]] he does not express explicit racial contempt for the [[ulleran|Ullerans]]. But his moderate sensibility is arguably more dangerous than [[eric-blount|Blount]]'s overt brutality: by refusing to confront the moral and political failures of [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] system, Harrington allows conditions to worsen until rebellion becomes inevitable. His death, tragic as it is for the man himself, is narratively necessary — the novel's logic demands a replacement who can act where Harrington could only deliberate.

## Relationships

- **[[carlos-von-schlichten|Carlos von Schlichten]]**: His military subordinate who succeeds him as [[governor-general|Governor-General]]. Their relationship seems professional but distant; [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] does not mourn Harrington at length, focusing instead on the military crisis
- **[[eric-blount|Eric Blount]]**: His chess partner and [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] Resident at [[konkrook|Konkrook]]. [[eric-blount|Blount]] shares Harrington's dismissive attitude toward native threats, and will later share his fate — killed by the uprising (015-043)
- **[[paula-quinton|Paula Quinton]]**: He is aware of her investigation and allows it to proceed within limits, viewing her as a manageable political nuisance
- **[[rakkeed-the-prophet|Rakkeed the Prophet]]**: The native leader whose threat he fatally underestimated
