# Airjeep

The airjeep is a small contragravity combat vehicle used by the Chartered Uller Company's military forces for reconnaissance, close air support, and light strike missions. Bristling with weapons — 15mm machine-guns and 50mm rocket tubes — the airjeep represents the workhorse of Company air power on Uller (001-004). It is the most numerous and tactically flexible aircraft in the Company's inventory.

## Design and Armament

The airjeep is built around a compact contragravity lift unit that provides vertical takeoff and landing capability without requiring rotor systems or fixed wings. Propulsion is separate from lift: once airborne, the vehicle uses directed thrust for forward motion, enabling speeds and maneuverability comparable to fixed-wing aircraft with the operational flexibility of a hover vehicle (001-015).

The standard armament package includes multiple 15mm machine-guns — rapid-fire projectile weapons firing armor-piercing rounds capable of engaging both infantry and light vehicles — and 50mm rocket tubes mounted on under-wing or under-body hardpoints (001-004). The rocket tubes provide the airjeep with a limited strike capability against hardened targets, though the 50mm rockets have insufficient warhead mass for use against heavily fortified positions.

The airjeep is an open-cockpit or lightly-enclosed design, providing no significant armor protection for the crew. Its defense lies in speed, agility, and the suppressive effect of its forward armament. Against the primitive weapons available to most Ulleran forces — chemical firearms, spears, and low-velocity projectile weapons — the airjeep's combination of altitude and mobility provides effective survivability.

## Crew and Operation

The standard airjeep crew consists of two personnel: a lieutenant-driver who pilots the vehicle and a sergeant-gunner who operates the weapons systems. The lieutenant in command is typically a junior officer — the airjeep is a small-unit asset rather than a strategic platform — and the sergeant is an experienced non-commissioned officer responsible for the vehicle's armament.

The crew configuration reflects the dual role of the airjeep as both a reconnaissance platform and a combat vehicle. The lieutenant concentrates on navigation, threat assessment, and tactical decision-making; the sergeant handles the immediate business of engaging targets. In practice, the division of labor is less formal — both crew members are trained to handle either role if necessary (001-006).

## Tactical Employment

Airjeeps operate in flights of two to four vehicles, providing mutual support and overlapping fields of fire. During the Battle of Konkrook Reservation, airjeeps were employed to suppress contragravity air attacks by rebel forces — engaging enemy air units in a chaotic, low-altitude dogfight environment where the airjeep's agility gave it advantages over larger, less maneuverable platforms (010-039).

Airjeeps also serve as scouts and couriers, exploiting their ability to operate from unprepared landing zones. In the dispersed geography of the Company's Ulleran territories, where road infrastructure is minimal and distances between settlements are measured in hundreds of kilometers, the airjeep's combination of speed, vertical landing capability, and small logistical footprint makes it indispensable.

## Limitations

The airjeep's principal limitation is its vulnerability to ground fire. Without armor, a single well-placed shot can disable the vehicle or kill its crew. Against organized forces equipped with modern weapons — including the heavy artillery that King Kankad's forces possessed (005-045) — airjeeps must operate with extreme caution. The vehicle is also weather-limited: Uller's frequent dust storms and atmospheric disturbances can ground airjeep operations for extended periods.
