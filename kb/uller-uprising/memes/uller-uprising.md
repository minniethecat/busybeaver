# Uller Uprising

The Uller Uprising was a coordinated rebellion by native [[ulleran|Ulleran]] kingdoms against the Terran Federation and the [[chartered-uller-company|Chartered Uller Company]], unfolding across the planet [[uller|Uller]] during a single, catastrophic campaign season. The uprising fused religious mass-movement politics with an unprecedented technological threat — nuclear weapons in the hands of a native power — to produce the most dangerous colonial crisis in Federation history.

## Origins

The uprising grew from two convergent conspiracies that developed largely independently of one another.

### Rakkeed's Religious Movement

The first was the psionic religion of Rakkeed the Prophet, a charismatic Ulleran who preached a synthesis of native mysticism and anti-Terran liberation theology. Rakkeed's movement spread through the native labor camps and urban populations of multiple kingdoms, creating a unified ideological framework for rebellion (002-010).

### Orgzild's Nuclear Program

The second was the nuclear weapons program of King Orgzild of [[keegark|Keegark]], who had his engineers reverse-engineer fission bomb designs and construct working devices in secret (002-019). When Rakkeed allied with Prince Gurgurk of [[konkrook|Konkrook]], the two conspiracies merged into a coordinated military plan (002-013).

### Intelligence Failure

The Company's intelligence failure was near-total. Despite controlling the planetary government through puppet kings and a vast administrative apparatus, the Company's political officers — notably Grinell and Blount — dismissed early warnings about native unrest. By the time [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] recognized the scope of the threat, the uprising had already begun (006-031).

## Key Events

### The Opening Strike

The uprising opened with a simultaneous multi-kingdom offensive. Governor [[sid-harrington|Harrington]] was poisoned by a native servant at 22:10 local time — the assassination was broadcast via all-station priority telecast within thirty-three minutes, paralyzing the Company's civilian command structure (006-039)(007-032). Simultaneously, native troops in the Company's own barracks mutinied, seizing weapons and attacking the Konkrook Reservation compound.

> "A geek servant did the job, on orders from Gurgurk and possibly Rakkeed." — von Schlichten on Harrington's assassination (007-032)

### The Siege of Konkrook

Von Schlichten assumed military command and organized the defense of the Company Reservation at Konkrook. The compound came under sustained attack from contragravity air units, ground assaults by mutinous native-troops, and siege operations from Ulleran forces armed with conventional artillery (010-039). The defense held — barely — thanks to timely reinforcement from [[king-kankad|King Kankad]]'s [[kragan|Kragan]] forces, who provided [[contragravity]] airlift capacity that the Company's own depleted fleet could not (010-007)(010-015).

### The Nuclear Crisis

Intelligence gathered during the siege revealed the true scope of the Keegarkan nuclear program (013-006). Orgzild's engineers, working from technical information covertly gathered by Gorkrink on [[niflheim|Niflheim]], had constructed at least three operational fission weapons. The Company responded with [[operation-hildegarde|Operation Hildegarde]], an emergency reverse-engineering project that produced counter-strike weapons using designs extracted from a historical romance novel (014-027)(014-030).

### The Destruction of Keegark

The uprising ended with the nuclear bombing of Keegark by von Schlichten's forces. Three bombs were dropped from the cutter *Elmoran* in a single mission, inflicting approximately 150,000 immediate casualties (015-034)(015-011). Von Schlichten, who had personally ordered the strike, resolved that this slaughter must be the last of its kind: "This must be the last nuclear bombing on Uller" (015-044).

## Aftermath and Legacy

The uprising fundamentally altered the political calculus of Terran rule on Uller. The Company's monopoly on force — the unspoken foundation of colonial governance — had been broken, if only temporarily. The native kingdoms had demonstrated the capacity to organize across traditional rivalry lines, to absorb and apply Terran military technology, and to threaten the Federation's strategic position on the planet. The destruction of Keegark served as both a military victory and a moral indictment of the colonial system that had made such a war possible.
