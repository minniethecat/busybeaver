# Interstellar Travel

Interstellar travel via hyperdrive is the enabling technology of the Terran Federation. Ships cross the 21 light-years between Sol and Beta Hydri in six months of external time, while the crew experiences only three weeks of subjective duration inside the ship. This relativistic asymmetry between travel time and experienced time shapes every aspect of the novel's political and military situation.

## The Hyperdrive Effect

The novel describes the hyperdrive through its practical effects rather than its physics. "It takes six months for a ship to go between here and Nif," Colonel Prinsloo explains, "because of the hyperdrive effects, the experienced time of the voyage, inside the ship, is of the order of three weeks" (006-015). This six-to-one compression ratio means that a journey of 21 light-years takes only three subjective weeks — fast enough to maintain an interstellar civilization, slow enough to make real-time governance impossible.

The hyperdrive effect also distorts the perception of distance for those unfamiliar with the technology. Prinsloo notes that the native King Orgzild, having observed only local contragravity speeds on Uller, would estimate the distance between Uller and Niflheim as "about a quarter-million miles" rather than light-years, because he extrapolates from the local speeds he knows (006-015).

## The Communication Gap

No faster-than-light communication exists in the novel's universe. Messages travel on ships — the same six-month voyage applies to news, orders, and diplomatic communications. This means that the Governor-General on Uller cannot consult the Federation Government or Company headquarters on Terra for decisions that must be made in hours or days.

This communication gap is the fundamental structural condition of the plot. When Governor-General Harrington is assassinated and the uprising erupts, von Schlichten must make irreversible decisions — including the nuclear destruction of Keegark — without any possibility of higher authorization. He acts on his own authority, knowing that his actions will be reviewed six months later by a government that has no understanding of the situation on the ground.

## Space Navy and Merchant Marine

The Terran Federation Space Navy operates the military vessels, while Company-chartered freighters like the *Northern Star* and passenger ships like the *Aldebaran* handle commercial traffic. The distinction between naval and civilian vessels becomes militarily significant during the uprising: the *Aldebaran* is armed and used as an improvised bomber, while civilian ships are evacuated or pressed into service as troop transports (007-059).

The two missing Boer-class freighters — the *Jan Smuts* and the *Oom Paul Kruger* — represent the strategic threat that captured interstellar vessels pose. If King Orgzild could crew and operate these ships, he could theoretically threaten not just Uller but Niflheim itself (006-016)(014-046).

## Narrative Significance

The six-month communication delay is the novel's most elegant narrative device. It creates autonomous agency for the planetary commanders while maintaining the formal structure of Federation authority. Von Schlichten is simultaneously a loyal servant of the Federation and an absolute sovereign — the six-month gap makes him both. This structural condition mirrors the historical experience of colonial governors in the age of sail, who exercised de facto sovereignty because instructions from the metropole arrived months after the events they addressed.
