# Grank

Grank was a northern [[ulleran|Ulleran]] kingdom ruled by King Yoorkerk, notable for its strategic ambiguity during the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]] and as the site of a key [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] diplomatic mission that secured the release of captured warships and the arrest of [[rakkeed-the-prophet|Rakkeed the Prophet]].

## Overview

Grank occupied a pivotal position in the northern [[ulleran|Ulleran]] political landscape alongside Krink and [[skilk|Skilk]]. Unlike the pro-[[chartered-uller-company|Company]] Krink and the anti-[[chartered-uller-company|Company]] [[skilk|Skilk]], Grank under King Yoorkerk pursued a policy of calculated neutrality — hedging between [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] and the rebels until the balance of power became clear. [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] ore-smelting operations and agricultural installations made Grank economically significant, and the presence of two major [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] vessels — the cruiser *Procyon* and the transport *Northern Lights* — gave it critical military importance.

## Background

Grank was linked to the Arctic mining economy: ore was "flown down to [[skilk|Skilk]] and Krink and Grank, where it's smelted through the winter" （005-026）. The Resident-Agent Guido Karamessinis represented [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] interests in the city. Grank's political structure under Yoorkerk was typical of the northern kingdoms: a feudal monarchy balancing baronial and urban interests, with the king maintaining authority through personal relationships and carefully managed subsidies.

## Role in the Uprising

### Yoorkerk's Ambiguous Stance

When the uprising erupted, Yoorkerk adopted a characteristically ambiguous position. Guido Karamessinis reported that Grank was "quiet, but King Yoorkerk's troops had seized [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] airport and docks, taken the *Procyon* and the *Northern Lights* and put guards aboard them, and were surrounding the Residency" （009-023）. Von Schlichten immediately recognized Yoorkerk's strategy: "It looks as though Yoorkerk's trying to play both sides at once. If the rebellion's put down, he'll come forward as your friend and protector; if we're wiped out elsewhere, he'll yell '*Znidd suddabit!*' and swamp you" （009-026）. He counseled Karamessinis: "Don't antagonize him; we can't afford to fight this war on any more fronts than we are now" （009-026）.

### Grank Under Pressure

As the siege of [[konkrook|Konkrook]] continued, Grank remained in uneasy stasis. Karamessinis was "still at uneasy peace with King Yoorkerk, who was still undecided whether the rebels or [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] were going to be the eventual victors, and afraid to take any irrevocable step in either direction" （011-060）. The [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] cruiser *Procyon* and transport *Northern Lights* sat idle under Yoorkerk's control, their crews and the Grank Residency effectively blockaded.

### O'Leary's Mission

Von Schlichten dispatched Colonel Hideyoshi O'Leary to Grank aboard the *Northern Star* with a contingent of [[kragan-rifles|Kragan Rifles]]. O'Leary's unconventional approach involved screening Yoorkerk an audiovisual recording of [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s destruction of [[skilk|Skilk]] — including "Firkked's head leering at him off the point of that overgrown assegai" （012-068）. The psychological warfare succeeded: Yoorkerk released both *Procyon* and *Northern Lights*, unblockaded the Residency, and handed over [[rakkeed-the-prophet|Rakkeed the Prophet]], whom he had been harboring at the Palace. O'Leary later explained: "Yoorkerk was holding our ships and our people in case we lost; he was also holding [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] at the Palace in case we won" （013-037）.

### Post-Settlement Contribution

With the balance tipped decisively toward [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]], Yoorkerk committed four infantry regiments to the campaign against [[keegark|Keegark]]: the Seventh Glorious-and-Terrible, Fourth Firm-in-Adversity, Second Strength-of-the-Throne, and Twelfth Forever-Admirable. Though described as "the sorriest-looking rabble I ever saw," Hideyoshi O'Leary confirmed they were "the best Yoorkerk has, and they all have [[terra|Terra]]n-style rifles" （015-006）.

## Characteristics & Traits

Grank's defining political characteristic was its opportunistic neutrality — a survival strategy in a region where the balance of power between [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] and rebel forces remained uncertain. King Yoorkerk's "playing both sides at once" （009-023） reflected the precarious position of northern [[ulleran|Ulleran]] monarchs caught between [[terra|Terra]]n military power and [[ulleran|Ulleran]] nationalist sentiment. The city's role as a polar ore smelting center gave it economic importance disproportionate to its military strength.
