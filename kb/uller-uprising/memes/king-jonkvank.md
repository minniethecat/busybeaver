# King Jonkvank

King of Krink, a North [[ulleran|Ulleran]] monarch who maintained a pro-[[terra|Terra]]n alliance during the uprising, providing critical military support to [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s forces in exchange for the promised political control of the rival kingdom of [[skilk|Skilk]].

## Overview

King Jonkvank ruled Krink, a North [[ulleran|Ulleran]] kingdom situated approximately five hundred miles northeast of [[skilk|Skilk]] across the mountains. Unlike his rival King Firkked of [[skilk|Skilk]], Jonkvank maintained a pro-[[terra|Terra]]n and anti-[[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] stance throughout the crisis, receiving a [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] subsidy that helped secure his loyalty. His military support — including the airlift of his elite regiments to [[skilk|Skilk]] — proved decisive in defeating Firkked's forces and securing the northern theater.

## Background

### Political Position

Jonkvank's political situation was the mirror image of Firkked's. While Firkked feared his feudal nobility and courted the anti-[[terra|Terra]]n merchants, Jonkvank "has the support of his barons, but he's afraid of his urban bourgeoisie" （004-026）. The [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] paid him "a handsome subsidy" to maintain his pro-[[terra|Terra]]n orientation.

This alignment made him explicitly anti-[[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] — the prophet "has a price on his head" in Krink, in contrast to [[skilk|Skilk]] where [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] moved openly （004-026）.

### Character

Jonkvank's character was bluntly assessed by Hideyoshi O'Leary: "He's as bloody-minded an old murderer as you'd care not to meet in a dark alley anywhere" （004-027）. This ruthless pragmatism, however, made him a reliable ally — his interests aligned with [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]]'s, and he pursued them with brutal efficiency.

## Role in the Uprising

### The Krink Mutiny

When the coordinated uprising began, Krink experienced simultaneous crises: rioting in the city and an attempted palace-revolution against Jonkvank. Critically, it was the army of King Jonkvank that mutinied — while the Sixth North [[uller|Uller]] Native Infantry, the [[zirk|Zirk]] cavalry companies, and the [[kragan|Kragans]] at Krink remained loyal （008-007）.

Jonkvank's War Minister Hurkkurk was discovered to be in the pay of King Firkked of [[skilk|Skilk]] — a betrayal Jonkvank addressed with characteristic directness: "I have Hurkkurk's head here somewhere, if you want to see it" （008-034）.

### The Alliance with Von Schlichten

Von Schlichten, recognizing Jonkvank's strategic value, negotiated an explicit quid pro quo: Jonkvank would airlift his loyal regiments south to [[skilk|Skilk]], and in return would receive political control of [[skilk|Skilk]] after Firkked's defeat. Von Schlichten authorized Hideyoshi O'Leary to "promise Jonkvank that he can assume political control at [[skilk|Skilk]], after we've stuffed Firkked's head in the dustbin" （008-042）.

When [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] later threatened to reduce [[skilk|Skilk]] to ruins rather than let it fall intact to an enemy, Jonkvank reacted with alarm: "Oh, no, general, don't do all that!... You promised me that I should have [[skilk|Skilk]], on the word of a [[terra|Terra]]n. Are you going to give me a city of ruins and corpses?" He added, with [[ulleran|Ulleran]] practicality: "Ruins are no good to anybody, and I am not a [[jeel|Jeel]], to eat corpses" （010-029）.

### Military Contribution

Jonkvank committed his best regiments to the [[skilk|Skilk]] campaign — units with names reflecting [[ulleran|Ulleran]] military culture: the Murderers, the [[jeel|Jeel]]-Feeders, the Corpse-Reapers, the Devastators, and the Fear-Makers （010-031）.

He was initially cautious about stripping Krink of troops, arguing he "can't take chances that it will break out again as soon as I strip the city of troops" — a position [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] acknowledged as strategically sound （010-032）.

The *Northern Star* made repeated five-hundred-mile flights between Krink and [[skilk|Skilk]], airlifting Jonkvank's regiments. Within hours of the first lift, Jonkvank's [[jeel|Jeel]]-Feeders — armed with [[terra|Terra]]n 9 mm rifles and bazookas — were fighting alongside [[chartered-uller-company|Company]] forces at [[skilk|Skilk]]. By 13:00, after the fourth airlift, the combined [[terra|Terra]]n-Krinkan force had driven the fighting entirely to the eastern bank of the dry Hoork River （011-005）.

## Characteristics & Traits

Jonkvank combined ruthless pragmatism with strategic caution. His willingness to ally with the [[terra|Terra]]ns was not ideological but calculated — [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] paid him, his barons supported him, and the alliance promised territorial expansion at Firkked's expense.

His emotional range, at least in diplomatic exchanges, oscillated between grim determination and elation. When promised [[skilk|Skilk]], "Jonkvank's mouth, until now compressed grimly, parted in a gleaming smile" and he "made an exclamation of pleasure which sounded rather like a boy running along a picket fence with a stick" （008-039）.

## Relationships

- **King Firkked of [[skilk|Skilk]]**: Bitter rival; Firkked's agent Hurkkurk infiltrated Jonkvank's court
- **[[carlos-von-schlichten|Carlos von Schlichten]]**: Key [[terra|Terra]]n ally; the two negotiated the Krink-[[skilk|Skilk]] power transfer
- **Hurkkurk**: Jonkvank's treacherous War Minister, executed for serving Firkked
- **Hideyoshi O'Leary**: Delivered [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s promises and coordinated the Krink airlift
- **Francis Xavier Shapiro**: Resident-Agent at Krink, managed the [[terra|Terra]]n side of the Krink crisis

## Death / Post-novel

Jonkvank survived the uprising and was positioned to assume political control of [[skilk|Skilk]] after Firkked's defeat, as promised by [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]. This would have made him the dominant power among the North [[ulleran|Ulleran]] kingdoms, controlling both Krink and [[skilk|Skilk]].
