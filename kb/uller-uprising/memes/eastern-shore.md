# Eastern Shore

The Eastern Shore was a coastal region of [[uller|Uller]], situated to the east of [[konkrook|Konkrook]] and extending northward along the planetary coast. The region was characterized by a string of independent Free Cities — settlements not directly under the control of the major Ulleran kingdoms — making it a strategically contested zone between Company-allied and hostile native forces during the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]].

## Geography & Layout

The Eastern Shore stretched from the vicinity of Konkrook northward along the Ulleran coast. The most northern of the Free Cities in the region was [[kwurk|Kwurk]], site of a small Company Residency (010-022). The shore's geography included coastal lowlands suitable for contragravity landing operations, which made the Free Cities accessible to both Terran military traffic and hostile native forces.

The region's political geography was defined by its status as a patchwork of independent settlements rather than unified kingdom territory. This fragmentation made the Eastern Shore both a buffer zone and a vulnerability — neither the Company nor the major native kingdoms exercised uncontested sovereignty over the Free Cities.

## Political & Social Structure

The Free Cities of the Eastern Shore maintained their independence from the major Ulleran kingdoms such as Konkrook, Keegark, and the northern realms of [[king-yoorkerk|King Yoorkerk]] and [[king-jonkvank|King Jonkvank]]. The small Company Residency at Kwurk represented the only permanent Terran administrative presence in the region, and its garrison was minimal — during the uprising, the entire Residency garrison was forced to evacuate southward to [[kankads-town|Kankad's Town]] (010-022).

The Free Cities' political independence made them potential allies or liabilities depending on the shifting military situation. King Orgzild, the dominant hostile power on the eastern seaboard, exerted influence over parts of the Eastern Shore, drawing the region into the broader conflict between the Company and native resistance forces.

## Strategic Significance

Control of the Eastern Shore was strategically important for several reasons. First, the coastal route provided a northern approach to Konkrook that bypassed the [[konk-mountains|Konk Mountains]]. Second, the Free Cities' harbors and landing sites could support contragravity operations, making them valuable staging points. Third, the region's political fragmentation meant that whichever power could assert control over the Free Cities gained both territorial depth and denial of those same assets to the enemy.

During the uprising, King Orgzild's forces threatened the Eastern Shore from their strongholds in the eastern territories. The vulnerability of isolated Company outposts in the region was demonstrated by the evacuation of the Kwurk Residency — the garrison "had arrived at Kankad's Town in two hundred-foot contragravity scows," abandoning their post when it became indefensible against the scale of native unrest (010-022).

## In the Uprising

The Eastern Shore served primarily as a flank theater during the uprising. While the main military action concentrated on Konkrook, [[gongonk-island|Gongonk Island]], and the northern polar front, the Eastern Shore remained a concern for von Schlichten's command. The evacuation of Kwurk demonstrated the limits of Company power projection in the region, and the Free Cities' ambiguous loyalties required constant diplomatic and military attention.

The threat of Orgzild consolidating the Eastern Shore Free Cities under his banner was one of the strategic pressures that shaped von Schlichten's decision-making. With Orgzild's forces already controlling much of the eastern territory, the loss of the Free Cities would have given him a coastal corridor threatening Konkrook's eastern flank.
