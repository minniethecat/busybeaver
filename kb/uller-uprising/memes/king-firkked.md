# King Firkked

King of [[skilk|Skilk]], a North [[ulleran|Ulleran]] monarch who aligned with [[rakkeed-the-prophet|Rakkeed the Prophet]]'s anti-[[terra|Terra]]n movement and led military opposition against the [[chartered-uller-company|Chartered Uller Company]] during the uprising.

## Overview

King Firkked ruled [[skilk|Skilk]], one of the major North [[ulleran|Ulleran]] kingdoms, during the period of [[terra|Terra]]n [[company-rule|Company administration]]. Unlike King Jonkvank of Krink, who maintained a pro-[[terra|Terra]]n stance, Firkked aligned himself with the urban merchant class and the prophet [[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]], becoming one of the principal native antagonists of the novel. His kingdom lay in the northern reaches of [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] area, and his army posed the most significant conventional military threat to [[terra|Terra]]n forces during the uprising.

## Background

### Species & Biology

As an [[ulleran|Ulleran]], Firkked was a silicon-based humanoid with the characteristic [[silicon-based-life|quartz-speckled skin]], cold-blooded physiology, and four-armed anatomy of his species. Like other North [[ulleran|Ullerans]], he possessed the distinctive hairless blue-gray skin and opal teeth common to the northern kingdoms.

### Political Position

Firkked's reign at [[skilk|Skilk]] was characterized by political tension between the feudal nobility and the rising urban merchant class （004-025）. Fearful that his barons would attempt to force a "Runnymede" — a charter of rights limiting royal authority — upon him, Firkked chose to curry favor with the urban merchants instead. This political calculus aligned him with the anti-[[terra|Terra]]n sentiment prevalent among the merchant class, who resented [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] for disrupting traditional caravan trade routes and dealing directly with land-owning barons （004-026）.

This alignment made Firkked pro-[[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]] and anti-[[terra|Terra]]n. [[rakkeed-the-prophet|Rakkeed the Prophet]] moved openly in [[skilk|Skilk]], in contrast to Krink where a price had been placed on his head （004-026）.

## Role in the Uprising

### Pre-Uprising Tensions

Before the coordinated uprising, Firkked had already been consolidating power and arming his forces. Von Schlichten's intelligence indicated that Firkked personally owned three ten-passenger aircars, a troop-carrier, four airjeeps armed with 15 mm machine-guns, and two heavy lorries. An estimated two hundred vehicles of all types existed in [[skilk|Skilk]] and the surrounding country （009-013）.

His War Minister Hurkkurk was secretly in the pay of King Firkked, betraying King Jonkvank of Krink — a fact discovered when Hurkkurk was executed for treason （008-034）.

### The Skilk Campaign

When the general uprising began, Firkked's army constituted the primary conventional threat in the northern theater. His regular forces, described as trained and disciplined troops, marched in formation on the [[terra|Terra]]n Reservation south of [[skilk|Skilk]]. However, they made the critical error of halting in place to stare at attacking contragravity craft — unused to aerial warfare, they were devastated by [[terra|Terra]]n air power （009-032）.

Firkked possessed at least one 75 mm gun and six to ten 40 mm guns, though he failed to deploy them effectively against [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] forces （009-031）. His substantial stock of thermoconcentrate fuel — critical for his war effort — was destroyed during the fighting （010-012）.

### The Siege of the Reservation

Firkked's forces, reinforced by [[zirk|Zirk tribe]]smen and armed urban levies, mounted sustained attacks on [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] Reservation. They employed innovative siege tactics, including driving herds of six-legged livestock ahead of their infantry to clear minefields and test electrified wire defenses （010-012）.

His army also commandeered nuclear-powered farm tractors as improvised tanks, using them in the manner of First Century ground warfare to breach the defensive perimeter. This assault penetrated to the middle of the Reservation before being repelled （010-014）.

### Defeat

Firkked's military position collapsed when King Jonkvank, having secured Krink, airlifted loyal regiments — the Murderers, [[jeel|Jeel]]-Feeders, Corpse-Reapers, Devastators, and Fear-Makers — south to [[skilk|Skilk]]. Von Schlichten explicitly promised Jonkvank political control of [[skilk|Skilk]] after "stuffing Firkked's head in the dustbin" （008-042）.

The arrival of the *Northern Star* and Jonkvank's loyal regiments turned the tide decisively. [[zirk|Zirk]] cavalry from Krink fought to within a thousand yards of the Palace, and the combined [[terra|Terra]]n-[[ulleran|Ulleran]] force drove Firkked's remaining troops from the city. His stock of thermoconcentrate fuel was incinerated, leaving six to eight city blocks blackened and lifeless （011-003）.

## Characteristics & Traits

Firkked was described by [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] as a ruler whose "timidity and stupidity" proved fortunate for the [[terra|Terra]]ns （009-021）. He had sufficient military resources to overwhelm [[chartered-uller-company|the Company]] position had he deployed them aggressively — [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]] noted that if Firkked had positioned regiments outside the Reservation to attack simultaneously with the native troop mutinies, "he could have swamped us in twenty minutes" （009-021）.

His political strategy of aligning with the merchant class placed him at odds with his own feudal nobility, creating internal tensions that likely weakened his military effectiveness. The idea that the [[terra|Terra]]n home world was a small artificial planet — propagated by [[orgzild|King Orgzild of Keegark]] — was also reportedly known to Firkked （006-037）.

## Relationships

- **[[rakkeed-the-prophet|Rakkeed the Prophet]]**: Firkked was explicitly pro-[[psionic-phenomenon|Rakkeed]], allowing the prophet to operate openly in [[skilk|Skilk]]
- **King Jonkvank of Krink**: Bitter rival; Firkked's agent Hurkkurk infiltrated Jonkvank's court as War Minister
- **[[orgzild|King Orgzild of Keegark]]**: Fellow anti-[[terra|Terra]]n monarch, source of the artificial-planet theory about [[terra|Terra]]
- **Von Schlichten**: Primary military adversary; Firkked underestimated his tactical capabilities
- **Hurkkurk**: Firkked's paid agent inside Jonkvank's court, later executed for treason

## Death / Post-novel

Firkked's ultimate fate is implied by [[carlos-von-schlichten|von Schlichten]]'s promise to "stuff Firkked's head in the dustbin" （008-042）. The [[zirk|Zirk]] cavalry and Jonkvank's regiments fought to within striking distance of the Palace, and [[skilk|Skilk]] fell to the combined [[terra|Terra]]n-Krinkan forces. The Palace was destroyed, and with it Firkked's reign.
