# Kragan Airlift

A large-scale contragravity troop-transport operation conducted during the first days of the Uller uprising, in which King Kankad's Kragan warriors were airlifted from their home territories to reinforce the besieged Company garrison on Gongonk Island (L1095, L1159, L1397).

## Operational Context

The uprising caught the Company with its forces dispersed across multiple locations on Uller. At Konkrook, the administrative and military headquarters on Gongonk Island was surrounded by hostile native populations. The Company's own native troops — including the Kragan Rifles — were initially of uncertain loyalty, and the loss of Keegark, where Colonel MacKinnon reported that "the geeks had penetrated his last defenses" (L1397), demonstrated the vulnerability of isolated Company positions.

King Kankad of the Kragans, uniquely among Ulleran rulers, maintained his alliance with the Company throughout the crisis. Von Schlichten, recognizing the critical need for reliable infantry, contacted M'zangwe to organize an airlift: "His crowd ought to be coming in in a couple of hours. What can you scrape up to send to Kankad's Town to airlift Kragans in?" (L1095).

## Execution

The airlift was conducted in waves using every available contragravity vehicle — lorries, troop carriers, and the starship *Aldebaran*, which was diverted from its normal duties to serve as a military transport. The first wave, composed of vehicles already at Konkrook augmented by craft sent to Kankad's Town, brought in the initial Kragan contingent (L1095).

A second, larger wave followed: "the second wave of Kankad's improvised airlift, reinforced by contragravity from Konkrook, had come in; there were now close to two thousand fresh Kragans on Gongonk Island and the mainland farms, Kankad himself with them" (L1397). Simultaneously, the *Aldebaran* reached Kankad's Town and began loading an additional thousand Kragans for a third wave.

The airlift also supported the political dimension of the counter-insurgency. Jonkvank, the Company's client-king at Krink, was urged to "airlift troops here" once "things stabilize at Krink" (L1203), extending the airlift model from military reinforcement to political consolidation. The successful defense of Krink, where "we airlifted all our Kragans and half the Sixth N.U.N.I. to the Palace" (L1159), demonstrated the airlift's tactical flexibility.

## Military Significance

The Kragan airlift was the decisive logistical operation of the uprising's early phase. By concentrating loyal native troops at the point of maximum danger — Gongonk Island — while maintaining garrisons at secondary locations, the Company avoided the defeat-in-detail that had claimed isolated outposts like Keegark. The arrival of two thousand Kragan infantry transformed the defense of the island from a desperate perimeter-holding action to a position from which offensive operations could be contemplated.

The airlift also validated the Company's investment in contragravity technology. The ability to move thousands of troops across planetary distances in hours, rather than days or weeks of ground marching, gave the Company an operational tempo that the rebel forces could not match. The Kragan troops were not merely reinforcements; they were a strategically mobile reserve that could be shifted between threatened points faster than the rebels could concentrate their own forces.

## Alliance Dynamics

The airlift's success depended entirely on King Kankad's continued loyalty — a loyalty that was neither automatic nor guaranteed. Other native rulers, including King Yoorkerk of Grank, remained "undecided whether the rebels or the Company were going to be the eventual victors, and afraid to take any irrevocable step in either direction" (L1397). Kankad's willingness to commit his warriors to the Company's defense, and to personally accompany them to the battlefield, was a political decision of enormous consequence.

The Kragan alliance was the Company's single most important political asset during the uprising. Without Kankad's troops, the defense of Gongonk Island would likely have failed; with them, the Company not only survived the initial assault but retained the combat power to transition from defense to pacification. The airlift was thus not merely a logistical achievement but the operational expression of a diplomatic relationship — contragravity engines turning political loyalty into military power.

## Narrative Significance

The Kragan airlift is one of the novel's clearest illustrations of the distinction Piper draws between different Ulleran peoples. The Kragans, consistently portrayed as the most "martial" and "loyal" of the native groups, are contrasted with the treacherous Konkrookans, the fanatical Skilkans, and the vacillating Grankers. This differentiation, while serving the plot's need for native allies, also reflects the colonialist logic of dividing subject peoples into "loyal" and "disloyal" categories.

The airlift chapter's title — "The Geek Luftwaffe and the Kragan Airlift" (L1365) — juxtaposes the rebels' improvised air force with the Company's organized logistics, framing the conflict as a contest between native improvisation and Terran systems. Yet the airlift itself was improvised, assembled from whatever contragravity was available, and depended on the voluntary cooperation of a native king. The neat opposition implied by the chapter title dissolves under scrutiny: both sides were making do with what they had, and the Company's victory owed as much to Kankad's political calculus as to Terran organizational superiority.
