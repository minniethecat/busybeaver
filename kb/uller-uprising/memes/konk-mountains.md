# Konk Mountains

The Konk Mountains, also referred to as the East Konk Mountains, were a mountain range situated to the east of [[konkrook|Konkrook]] on [[uller|Uller]]. The range formed a natural topographic boundary between the coastal Konkrook delta and the interior regions of the planet, and served as a prominent navigational landmark for contragravity air traffic approaching the capital.

## Geography & Environment

The mountains rose east of the Konk River delta, framing the eastern approach to Konkrook. At night, the city lights of Konkrook became visible to approaching aircraft as "a lurid smear on the underside of the clouds" once they cleared the East Konk Mountains, with the lights of [[gongonk-island|Gongonk Island]] and the nuclear reactor providing additional illumination beyond the range (012-026). The mountains thus marked the transition from the dark interior to the heavily urbanized and industrialized Konkrook metropolitan region.

The mountain terrain provided concealment for military operations. During the uprising, contragravity craft used the valleys and ridge lines of the Konk Mountains for low-altitude approach routes, exploiting the topography to avoid detection. A pursuit pilot reported being "down in the valley, altitude about a thousand feet," attempting to visually acquire a target against the sky above the mountain ridgeline (014-054).

## Strategic Significance

The Konk Mountains' position east of Konkrook made them a critical geographic feature in the defense of the capital. Any hostile force approaching Konkrook from the eastern interior would need to cross or circumvent the range. During the uprising, the mountains served as a screening feature — Terran and allied Kragan forces based at Konkrook monitored the eastern approaches for signs of enemy contragravity traffic or ground movement.

## In the Uprising

The mountains figured in the final phase of the conflict. After the nuclear destruction of Keegark, returning Terran aircraft crossed the East Konk Mountains en route to Konkrook, the "twinkle of lights" over the peaks signaling their approach to the beleaguered but still-held capital (013-068). The mountain valleys also provided cover for the low-altitude contragravity pursuit that characterized the final hours of the air campaign, with pilots using the terrain to mask their movements from enemy detection.
