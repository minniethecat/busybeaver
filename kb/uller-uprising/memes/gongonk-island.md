# Gongonk Island

Gongonk Island was the site of the Chartered Uller Company's primary administrative, military, and industrial complex on [[uller|Uller]], situated in Konkrook Channel adjacent to the native city of [[konkrook|Konkrook]]. It housed the Company guardhouse, airport, power plant, and spaceport, and became the central stronghold from which General [[carlos-von-schlichten|Carlos von Schlichten]] directed Terran military operations during the [[uller-uprising|Uller Uprising]].

## Geography & Layout

Gongonk Island lay in Konkrook Channel, separated from the mainland by a body of water that divided it from the Company farms on the southern shore (008-029). The island hosted the blue-gray Company administrative buildings as its most visible landmark, together with a Company airport that "swarming with lorries and airboats" handled traffic from across the planet (006-018). At the southern tip of the island stood a three-thousand-foot globe-shaped spaceport with a ring of mooring-pylons, capable of receiving interstellar vessels such as the *City of Pretoria* from [[niflheim|Niflheim]] (007-017).

The island's green Terran-style farms, irrigated and cultivated by the Company, provided a visible contrast to the native flint-and-basalt architecture of the Konkrook city skyline, and marked the boundary between Company-controlled territory and the surrounding Ulleran kingdoms (004-018)(007-017).

## Military & Strategic Role

The Company guardhouse on Gongonk Island served as the communications nerve center for Company military forces. Von Schlichten "was punching out the combination of the Company guardhouse on Gongonk Island" at the outset of the crisis, coordinating the initial response to the native uprising from the island's command post (004-034).

During the uprising, the island became the primary evacuation destination for Terran personnel and allied Kragan mercenaries. When the Palace coup against King Jaikark erupted, von Schlichten ordered the relief force to "evacuate Jaikark and his followers and our Kragans to Gongonk Island" (006-045). The island's defenses were manned by Kragan mercenaries — approximately five hundred Kragans and fifteen hundred Terran personnel held the island against an estimated five thousand mutinous laborers and native troops (008-029).

The island also functioned as a staging ground for reinforcements. King Kankad's airlift brought "close to two thousand fresh Kragans on Gongonk Island and the mainland farms" in successive waves, providing the manpower needed to turn the tide of the battle for Konkrook (010-039).

## Industrial & Nuclear Infrastructure

The Gongonk Island power plant, under the direction of chief engineer Kent Pickering, provided electrical power for the entire Konkrook area (012-006). More critically, the island housed a technical library and laboratory facilities that became central to the Company's nuclear weapons program during the uprising. When von Schlichten and his staff concluded that they could not rely on Dr. Gouveia Gomes's expertise from Niflheim, they determined that a plutonium bomb "could only be produced on Gongonk Island after lengthy experimental work" (013-016).

Technicians searched the "library here on Gongonk Island" for historical atomic weapons data from the Hiroshima and Nagasaki era, though without optimism about finding usable information — the Company's librarian had apparently been selective about what reference materials to stock (014-032). Nevertheless, the island's industrial infrastructure, including its power plant and machine shops, made it the only feasible location for weapons fabrication on Uller.

## In the Uprising

Throughout the Uprising, Gongonk Island remained the Terran command center and the one location the Company could not afford to lose. Von Schlichten's final military concern was that a nuclear-armed fanatic "may load one into an aircar and make a kamikaze-raid on Gongonk Island" (015-044), reflecting the island's status as the irreplaceable heart of Terran power on the planet. The island survived the uprising intact, and from its command center von Schlichten directed the nuclear destruction of Keegark and the broader military campaign that preserved Company control.
