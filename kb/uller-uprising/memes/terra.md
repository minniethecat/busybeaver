# Terra

The homeworld of the human species and political center of the Terran Federation,
whose distant authority and agonizingly slow communication cycle shape every
strategic calculation on Uller.

## Overview

Terra — Earth — is the distant sun around which all Company politics orbit.
Six months' travel time separates Uller from Terra, creating a one-year
round-trip for any communication or reinforcement [008-104]. This fundamental
fact of interstellar geography gives the novel its central strategic tension:
events on Uller move at the speed of revolution, while Terran response moves
at the speed of starships.

Terra is far more than a location — it is a psychological presence. For Terrans
on Uller, it represents home, civilization, and the ultimate court of appeal.
For Ullerans, it is a semi-mythical place whose very existence some doubt.
The phrase "the fear of Terra" captures the Company's ultimate enforcement
mechanism: the belief that Terran retribution, however delayed, is inevitable.

## Geography & Description

Terra is described in nostalgic, sensory terms by the Terrans stationed on the
hostile Ulleran landscape. Von Schlichten's reverie contrasts Uller's harshness
with Terra's familiar beauty:

> "…Odin, with its two moons, Hugin and Munin, and its wide grasslands and its
> evergreen forests that looked and even smelled like the pinewoods of Terra,
> or Baldur, with snow-capped mountains, and clear, cold lakes, and rocky rivers
> dashing under great vine-hung trees, or Freya, where the people were human to
> the last degree and the women were so breathtakingly beautiful…" [001-017]

Terra had its own technological disruptions: "the horse-breeders and
harness-manufacturers on Terra by the invention of the automobile, or the coal
and hydroelectric interests when direct conversion of nuclear energy to electric
current was developed, or the railroads and steamship lines at the time of the
discovery of the contragravity-field" [004-032] — parallels used by Company
spokesmen to explain the economic dislocations created on Uller.

## Political Significance

### Seat of the Terran Federation

Terra is the capital of the Terran Federation, which governs human-colonized
space. The Federation Space Navy operates from Terra, and it is to Terra that
reports on the Uller situation must be sent: "I'm sending Hid O'Leary to Terra
on the next ship, to make a full report on the situation" [012-006].

The Federation's authority is absolute in theory but attenuated in practice
by distance. As von Schlichten notes, destroying a Company installation would
give a native king "just twelve months — the time it would take to get the
news to Terra and for a Federation Space Navy task-force to get here" [006-011].

### Cultural Center

Terra generates cultural trends that reach even distant Uller. Paula Quinton's
fingernails — "black-lacquered and spotted with little gold stars" — are
identified as "evidently a new feminine fad from Terra" [001-050]. The
Extraterrestrial Sociographic Society, based on Terra, sends researchers
like Paula to study native societies [005-003].

Half the dogs on Terra — and apparently on Uller — are named Stalin [001-076],
a small detail that speaks to Terra's cultural homogeneity and the way Terran
colonists replicate home-world patterns.

## Economic Role

Terra is the destination for Uller's exports. The spaceship *City of Pretoria*,
from Niflheim, loads cargo at Konkrook "for Terra" [001-061], representing the
economic pipeline that connects the colony to the homeworld.

The economic relationship is extractive: the Company's charter is to exploit
Uller's resources for the benefit of Terran shareholders. When the uprising
threatens, one of the first concerns is that "the ships that'll be coming in
until word of our plight can get to Terra won't bring enough to keep us going"
[008-106] — the colony depends on resupply from Terra for survival.

## Ulleran Perceptions of Terra

A recurring theme is Ulleran skepticism about Terra's existence. Von
Schlichten bluntly states of King Orgzild: "I doubt if he even believes
there is a Terra" [006-012]. This disbelief is strategically significant —
if Terra is a Terran fiction, then the threat of Federation retaliation is
empty, and nothing prevents native kings from wiping out the Company.

Conversely, Ullerans who have been educated about Terra express a desire
to visit. King Kankad tells von Schlichten: "When Little Me is old enough
to take over the rule of our people, I would like to go to Terra, myself"
[005-051], and von Schlichten half-promises to take him [005-052].

The cultural gulf is bidirectional. Paula Quinton, the Terran sociologist,
risks being "burned at the stake by the Extraterrestrial Sociographic Society
after I get back to Terra" for her heretical findings about Ulleran society
[005-003], suggesting that Terran academic orthodoxy may be as rigid as any
Ulleran tradition.
