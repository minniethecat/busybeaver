## Overview

Ciudad Juárez is a border city in the state of [[chihuahua|Chihuahua]], across the Rio Grande from [[el-paso|El Paso]], Texas. In the novel it appears as one of the named battles that constitute the legend of [[pancho-villa|Pancho Villa]]. Natera's followers recite it in a litany of Villa's triumphs: "Villa! Villa's battles! Ciudad Juárez ... Tierra Blanca ... Chihuahua ... [[torreon|Torreón]]..." (020-015). The city also marks the site of a political speech — Solís, Natera's chief of staff, recalls a speech he "made at Ciudad Juárez" denouncing Victoriano Huerta (020-007).

## Location & Geography

Ciudad Juárez lies in the Chihuahuan Desert, on the south bank of the Rio Grande, directly opposite El Paso, Texas. Its border position made it strategically vital: control of Juárez meant control of a key crossing point for arms, supplies, and communication with the United States. In the revolutionary geography of the novel, Juárez stands on the northern frontier of Villa's domain.

## Role in the Narrative

Juárez enters the narrative twice in close succession. First, Solís mentions it as the venue for a political speech: he ironically recalls denouncing Huerta in a speech "made at Ciudad Juárez" (020-007), before admitting the gap between revolutionary rhetoric and revolutionary reality. The speech at Juárez represents a moment of political conviction now viewed with irony.

Second, Juárez appears in the catalog of Villa's battles that Natera's men recount to the astonished fighters in Demetrio's camp. The list — "Ciudad Juárez ... Tierra Blanca ... Chihuahua ... Torreón" (020-015) — sketches a map of Villa's military career in a single exhaled breath. Juárez leads the list, chronologically and symbolically: it was one of Villa's first major victories.
