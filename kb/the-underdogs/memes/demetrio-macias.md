# Demetrio Macías

## Overview

**Demetrio Macías** is the protagonist of [[mariano-azuela|Mariano Azuela]]'s *The Underdogs*. A peasant farmer from Limón, [[jalisco|Jalisco]], he becomes the leader of a band of [[mexican-revolution|revolution]]ary fighters after a personal conflict with a local *cacique* (political boss). Throughout the novel, Demetrio embodies the contradiction at the heart of the [[mexican-revolution|Mexican Revolution]]: a man of courage and natural leadership who fights without fully understanding the political cause he serves.

## Biography / Character Profile

### Origins and Rise to Leadership

Demetrio begins the novel as a fugitive, forced to flee his home after a confrontation with a local cacique. He is introduced in Chapter I as a hunted man: "Demetrio buckled his cartridge belt about his waist" `(001-018)`. His reputation as a fierce fighter precedes him — even the Federal soldiers speak of "the famous Demetrio Macias' country" `(001-057)` with a mixture of fear and respect.

When confronted by [[federal-army|Federal troops]], Demetrio stands his ground with characteristic defiance: "Demetrio stood his ground, looking the sergeant straight in the eye" `(001-088)`. This scene establishes his defining traits: courage, pride, and an unwillingness to submit to authority.

### Leadership of the Band

As the novel progresses, Demetrio's band grows from a handful of fugitives to a significant fighting force. His leadership style is instinctive rather than ideological. He fights because he must, not because he has adopted a political program. This distinguishes him from characters like [[luis-cervantes|Luis Cervantes]], who understands [[mexican-revolution|revolution]]ary politics intellectually but lacks Demetrio's authenticity.

### Relationships and Loyalty

Demetrio's relationships reveal his character: his loyalty to his men, his complicated feelings toward [[camilla|Camila]] and [[war-paint|War Paint]] ([[war-paint|La Pintada]]), and his grudging respect for [[luis-cervantes|Luis Cervantes]] despite recognizing the younger man's opportunism. His leadership relies on personal loyalty rather than institutional authority.

## Key Passages

> "Demetrio buckled his cartridge belt about his waist" `(001-018)` — The novel's first image of Demetrio, establishing him as a man of action.

> "Demetrio Macias!" the sergeant cried as he stood up, completely taken aback `(001-083)` — The fear Demetrio's name inspires among Federal soldiers.

> "Well, I'm a revolutionist, too, you know. The Government... " `(005-043)` — Demetrio's simple identification with the revolutionary cause.
