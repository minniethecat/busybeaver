[026-001]Like neighing colts, playful when the rainy season begins, [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]]'s

[026-002]men galloped through the sierra.

[026-003]"To Moyahua, boys. Let's go to Demetrio Macias' country!"

[026-004]"To the country of Monico the cacique!"

[026-005]The landscape grew clearer; the sun margined the diaphanous sky with a

[026-006]fringe of crimson. Like the bony shoulders of immense sleeping

[026-007]monsters, the chains of mountains rose in the distance. Crags there

[026-008]were like heads of colossal native idols; others like giants' faces,

[026-009]their grimaces awe-inspiring or grotesque, calling forth a smile or a

[026-010]shudder at a presentment of mystery.

[026-011]Demetrio Macias rode at the head of his men; behind him the members of

[026-012]his staff: Colonel Anastasio Montanez, Lieutenant-Colonel Pancracio,

[026-013]Majors Luis Cervantes and Blondie. Still further behind came War Paint

[026-014]with Venancio, who paid her many compliments and recited the despairing

[026-015]verses of Antonio Plaza. As the sun's rays began to slip from the

[026-016]housetops, they made their entrance into Moyahua, four abreast, to the

[026-017]sound of the bugle. The roosters' chorus was deafening, dogs barked

[026-018]their alarm, but not a living soul stirred on the streets.

[026-019]War Paint spurred her black horse and with one jump was abreast with

[026-020]Demetrio. They rode forward, elbow to elbow. She wore a silk dress and

[026-021]heavy gold earrings. Proudly her pale blue gown deepened her olive skin

[026-022]and the coppery spots on her face and arms. Riding astride, she had

[026-023]pulled her skirts up to her knees; her stockings showed, filthy and

[026-024]full of runs. She wore a gun at her side, a cartridge belt hung over

[026-025]the pommel of her saddle.

[026-026]Demetrio was also dressed in his best clothes. His broad-brimmed hat

[026-027]was richly embroidered; his leather trousers were tight-fitting and

[026-028]adorned with silver buttons; his coat was embroidered with gold thread.

[026-029]There was a sound of doors being beaten down and forced open. The

[026-030]soldiers had already scattered through the town, to gather together

[026-031]ammunition and saddles from everywhere.

[026-032]"We're going to bid Monico good morning," Demetrio said gravely,

[026-033]dismounting and tossing his bridle to one of his men. "We're going to

[026-034]have breakfast with Don Monico, who's a particular friend of mine ...."

[026-035]The general's staff smiled ... a sinister, malign smile....

[026-036]Making their spurs ring against the pavement, they walked toward a

[026-037]large pretentious house, obviously that of a cacique.

[026-038]"It's closed airtight," Anastasio Montanez said, pushing the door with

[026-039]all his might.

[026-040]"That's all right. I'll open it," Pancracio answered, lowering his

[026-041]rifle and pointing it at the lock.

[026-042]"No, no," Demetrio said, "knock first."

[026-043]Three blows with the butt of the rifle. Three more. No answer.

[026-044]Pancracio disobeys orders. He fires, smashing the lock. The door opens.

[026-045]Behind, a confusion of skirts and children's bare legs rushing to and

[026-046]fro, pell-mell.

[026-047]"I want wine. Hey, there: wine!" Demetrio cries in an imperious voice,

[026-048]pounding heavily on a table.

[026-049]"Sit down, boys."

[026-050]A lady peeps out, another, a third; from among black skirts, the heads

[026-051]of frightened children. One of the women, trembling, walks toward a

[026-052]cupboard and, taking out some glasses and a bottle, serves wine.

[026-053]"What arms have you?" Demetrio demands harshly.

[026-054]"Arms, arms...?" the lady answers, a taste of ashes on her tongue.

[026-055]"What arms do you expect us to have! We are respectable, lonely old

[026-056]ladies!"

[026-057]"Lonely, eh! Where's Senor Monico?"

[026-058]"Oh, he's not here, gentlemen, I assure you! We merely rent the house

[026-059]from him, you see. We only know him by name!"

[026-060]Demetrio orders his men to search the house.

[026-061]"No, please don't. We'll bring you whatever we have ourselves, but

[026-062]please for God's sake, don't do anything cruel. We're spinsters, lone

[026-063]women ... perfectly respectable...."

[026-064]"Spinsters, hell! What about these kids here?" Pancracio interrupts

[026-065]brutally. "Did they spring from the earth?"

[026-066]The women disappear hurriedly, to return with an old shotgun, covered

[026-067]with dust and cobwebs, and a pistol with rusty broken springs.

[026-068]Demetrio smiles.

[026-069]"All right, then, let's see the money."

[026-070]"Money? Money? But what money do you think a couple of spinsters have?

[026-071]Spinsters alone in the world....?"

[026-072]They glance up in supplication at the nearest soldier; but they are

[026-073]seized with horror. For they have just seen the Roman soldier who

[026-074]crucified Our Lord in the Via Crucis of the parish! They have seen

[026-075]Pancracio!

[026-076]Demetrio repeats his order to search.

[026-077]Once again the women disappear to return this time with a moth-eaten

[026-078]wallet containing a few Huerta bills.

[026-079]Demetrio smiles and without further delay calls to his men to come in.

[026-080]Like hungry dogs who have sniffed their meat, the mob bursts in,

[026-081]trampling down the women who sought to bar the entrance with their

[026-082]bodies. Several faint, fall to the ground; others flee in panic. The

[026-083]children scream.

[026-084]Pancracio is about to break the lock of a huge wardrobe when suddenly

[026-085]the doors open and out comes a man with a rifle in his hands.

[026-086]"Senor Don Monico!" they all exclaim in surprise.

[026-087]"Demetrio, please, don't harm me! Please don't harm me! Please don't

[026-088]hurt me! You know, Senor Don Demetrio, I'm your friend!"

[026-089]Demetrio Macias smiles slyly. "Are friends," he asked, "usually

[026-090]welcomed gun in hand?"

[026-091]Don Monico, in consternation, throws himself at Demetrio's feet, clasps

[026-092]his knees, kisses his shoes: "My wife! ... My children! ... Please,

[026-093]Senor Don Demetrio, my friend!"

[026-094]Demetrio with taut hand puts his gun back in the holster.

[026-095]A painful silhouette crosses his mind. He sees a woman with a child in

[026-096]her arms walking over the rocks of the sierra in the moonlight. A house

[026-097]in flames....

[026-098]"Clear out. Everybody outside!" he orders darkly.

[026-099]His staff obeys. Monico and the ladies kiss his hands, weeping with

[026-100]gratitude. The mob in the street, talking and laughing, stands waiting

[026-101]for the general's permission to ransack the cacique's house.

[026-102]"I know where they've buried their money but I won't tell," says a

[026-103]youngster with a basket in his hands.

[026-104]"Hm! I know the right place, mind you," says an old woman carrying a

[026-105]burlap sack to hold whatever the good Lord will provide. "It's on top

[026-106]of something ... there's a lot of trinkets nearby and then there's a

[026-107]small bag with mother-of-pearl around it. That's the thing to look for!"

[026-108]"You ain't talking sense, woman," puts in a man. "They ain't such fools

[026-109]as to leave silver lying loose like that. I'm thinking they've got it

[026-110]buried in the well, in a leather bag."

[026-111]The mob moves slowly; some carry ropes to tie about their bundles,

[026-112]others wooden trays. The women open out their aprons or shawls

[026-113]calculating their capacity. All give thanks to Divine Providence as

[026-114]they wait for their share of the booty.

[026-115]When Demetrio announces that he will not allow looting and orders them

[026-116]to disband, the mob, disconsolate, obeys him, and soon scatters; but

[026-117]there is a dull rumor among the soldiers and no one moves from his

[026-118]place.

[026-119]Annoyed, Demetrio repeats this order.

[026-120]A young man, a recent recruit, his head turned by drink, laughs and

[026-121]walks boldly toward the door. But before he has reached the threshold,

[026-122]a shot lays him low. He falls like a bull pierced in the neck by the

[026-123]matador's sword. Motionless, his smoking gun in his hand, Demetrio

[026-124]waits for the soldiers to withdraw.

[026-125]"Set fire to the house!" he orders Luis Cervantes when they reach their

[026-126]quarters.

[026-127]With a curious eagerness Luis Cervantes does not transmit the order but

[026-128]undertakes the task in person.

[026-129]Two hours later when the city square was black with smoke and enormous

[026-130]tongues of fire rose from Monico's house, no one could account for the

[026-131]strange behavior of the general.
