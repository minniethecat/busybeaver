## Overview

Limón is a small town in the Juchipila canyon region of [[jalisco|Jalisco]], near [[moyahua|Moyahua]], and is the birthplace of [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio Macías]] (013-001).

The first chapter opens with Federal soldiers arriving at Demetrio's hut in Limón, triggering his flight into the sierra. The name recurs across the novel as Demetrio's point of origin — a geographic anchor and a symbol of the home he leaves behind.

## Location & Geography

Limón is situated in the heart of the [[juchipila|Juchipila]] canyon, close to Moyahua, in Jalisco (013-001). The terrain is rugged canyon country — the harsh landscape that provides cover for Demetrio's guerrilla band. It lies within a few days' hard riding from the campaigns further north (031-082).

## Role in the Narrative

Limón serves as the geographic starting point of the novel. The opening scene takes place at Demetrio's hut, as Federal soldiers arrive looking for food (001-055). Their casual menace — they know Demetrio as "Macias the bandit" (001-063) — sets the stakes: the farmer of Limón is already a marked man.

After fleeing into the canyon, Demetrio grounds his identity in Limón. He tells Luis Cervantes: "I was born in Limon, close by Moyahua, right in the heart of the Juchipila canyon" (013-001). This self-definition anchors him in a specific place and class, against which his transformation into a revolutionary general is measured.

As the campaign moves far from home, Limón becomes the place of return Demetrio contemplates but never reaches. He asks how many hours' ride and is told "three days' hard riding" (031-082). The narrative records that "Demetrio did not go to Limon or remember his wife again" (031-095), marking the rupture between his new life and rural origins.

A darkly comic late episode mentions "General Limon," a horse thief sharing the town's name (035-057).

*Demetrio grounds his identity in the specific geography of the canyon country.*

> "How many hours' ride from here to Limon?"
> "It's no matter of hours; it's three days' hard riding, Demetrio." [031-082]

*The distance measures how far the revolution has taken Demetrio from his origins.*

> Since Demetrio did not go to Limon or remember his wife again, Camilla grew very happy. [031-095]

*The homecoming that never happens — Limón becomes the abandoned past.*
