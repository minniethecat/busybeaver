[022-001][[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]], nonplussed, scratched his head: "Look here, don't ask me any

[022-002]more questions.... You gave me the eagle I wear on my hat, didn't you?

[022-003]All right then; you just tell me: 'Demetrio, do this or do that,' and

[022-004]that's all there is to it."

[022-005]To champagne, that sparkles and foams as the beaded bubbles burst at

[022-006]the brim of the glass, Demetrio preferred the native tequila, limpid

[022-007]and fiery.

[022-008]The soldiers sat in groups about the tables in the restaurant, ragged

[022-009]men, filthy with sweat, dirt and smoke, their hair matted, wild,

[022-010]disheveled.

[022-011]"I killed two colonels," one man clamored in a guttural harsh voice. He

[022-012]was a small fat fellow, with embroidered hat and chamois coat, wearing

[022-013]a light purple handkerchief about his neck.

[022-014]"They were so Goddamned fat they couldn't even run. By God, I wish you

[022-015]could have seen them, tripping and stumbling at every step they took,

[022-016]climbing up the hill, red as tomatoes, their tongues hanging out like

[022-017]hounds. 'Don't run so fast, you lousy beggars!' I called after them.

[022-018]'I'm not so fond of frightened geese--stop, You bald-headed bastards: I

[022-019]won't harm you! You needn't worry!' By God, they certainly fell for it.

[022-020]Pop, pop! One shot for each of them, and a well-earned rest for a pair

[022-021]of poor sinners, be damned to them!"

[022-022]"I couldn't get a single one of their generals!" said a swarthy man who

[022-023]sat in one corner between the wall and the bar, holding his rifle

[022-024]between his outstretched legs. "I sighted one: a fellow with a hell of

[022-025]a lot of gold plastered all over him. His gold chevrons shone like a

[022-026]Goddamned sunset. And I let him go by, fool that I was. He took off his

[022-027]handkerchief and waved it. I stood there with my mouth wide open like a

[022-028]fool! Then I ducked and he started shooting, bullet after bullet. I let

[022-029]him kill a poor cargador. Then I said: 'My turn, now! Holy Virgin,

[022-030]Mother of God! Don't let me miss this son of a bitch.' But, by Christ,

[022-031]he disappeared. He was riding a hell of a fine nag; he went by me like

[022-032]lightning! There was another poor fool coming up the road. He got it

[022-033]and turned the prettiest somersault you ever saw!"

[022-034]Talk flew from lip to lip, each soldier vying with his fellow,

[022-035]snatching the words from the other's mouth. As they declaimed

[022-036]passionately, women with olive, swarthy skins, bright eyes, and teeth

[022-037]of ivory, with revolvers at their waists, cartridge-belts across their

[022-038]breasts, and broad Mexican hats on their heads, wove their way like

[022-039]stray street curs in and out among groups. A vulgar wench, with rouged

[022-040]cheeks and dark brown arms and neck, gave a great leap and landed on

[022-041]the bar near Demetrio's table.

[022-042]He turned his head toward her and literally collided with a pair of

[022-043]lubric eyes under a narrow forehead and thick, straight hair, parted in

[022-044]the middle.

[022-045]The door opened wide. Anastasio, Pancracio, Quail, and Meco filed in,

[022-046]dazed.

[022-047]Anastasio uttered a cry of surprise and stepped forward to shake hands

[022-048]with the little fat man wearing a charro suit and a lavender bandanna.

[022-049]A pair of old friends, met again. So warm was their embrace, so tightly

[022-050]they clutched each other that the blood rushed to their heads, they

[022-051]turned purple.

[022-052]"Look here, Demetrio, I want the honor of introducing you to Blondie.

[022-053]He's a real friend, you know. I love him like a brother. You must get

[022-054]to know him, Chief, he's a man! Do you remember that damn jail at

[022-055]Escobedo, where we stayed together for over a year?"

[022-056]Without removing his cigar from his lips, Demetrio, buried in a sullen

[022-057]silence amid the bustle and uproar, offered his hand and said:

[022-058]"I'm delighted to meet you!"

[022-059]"So your name is Demetrio Macias?" the girl asked suddenly. Seated on

[022-060]the bar, she swung her legs; at every swing, the toes of her shoes

[022-061]touched Demetrio's back.

[022-062]"Yes: I'm Demetrio Macias!" he said, scarcely turning toward her.

[022-063]Indifferently, she continued to swing her legs, displaying her blue

[022-064]stockings with ostentation.

[022-065]"Hey, War Paint, what are you doing here? Step down and have a drink!"

[022-066]said the man called Blondie.

[022-067]The girl accepted readily and boldly thrust her way through the crowd

[022-068]to a chair facing Demetrio.

[022-069]"So you're the famous Demetrio Macias, the hero of [[zacatecas|Zacatecas]]?" the girl

[022-070]asked.

[022-071]Demetrio bowed assent, while Blondie, laughing, said:

[022-072]"You're a wise one, War Paint. You want to sport a general!"

[022-073]Without understanding Blondie's words, Demetrio raised his eyes to

[022-074]hers; they gazed at each other like two dogs sniffing one another with

[022-075]distrust. Demetrio could not resist her furiously provocative glances;

[022-076]he was forced to lower his eyes.

[022-077]From their seats, some of Natera's officers began to hurl obscenities

[022-078]at War Paint. Without paying the slightest attention, she said:

[022-079]"General Natera is going to hand you out a little general's eagle. Put

[022-080]it here and shake on it, boy!"

[022-081]She stuck out her hand at Demetrio and shook it with the strength of a

[022-082]man. Demetrio, melting to the congratulations raining down upon him,

[022-083]ordered champagne.

[022-084]"I don't want no more to drink," Blondie said to the waiter, "I'm

[022-085]feeling sick. Just bring me some ice water."

[022-086]"I want something to eat," said Pancracio. "Bring me anything you've

[022-087]got but don't make it chili or beans!"

[022-088]Officers kept coming in; presently the restaurant was crowded. Small

[022-089]stars, bars, eagles and insignia of every sort or description dotted

[022-090]their hats. They wore wide silk bandannas around their necks, large

[022-091]diamond rings on their fingers, large heavy gold watch chains across

[022-092]their breasts.

[022-093]"Here, waiter," Blondie cried, "I ordered ice water. And I'm not

[022-094]begging for it either, see? Look at this bunch of bills. I'll buy you,

[022-095]your wife, and all you possess, see? Don't tell me there's none left--I

[022-096]don't care a damn about that! It's up to you to find some way to get it

[022-097]and Goddamned quick, too. I don't like to play about; I get mad when

[022-098]I'm crossed.... By God, didn't I tell you I wouldn't stand for any

[022-099]backchat? You won't bring it to me, eh? Well, take this...."

[022-100]A heavy blow sent the waiter reeling to the floor.

[022-101]"That's the sort of man I am, General Macias! I'm clean-shaven, eh? Not

[022-102]a hair on my chin? Do you know why? Well, I'll tell you! You see I get

[022-103]mad easy as hell; and when there's nobody to pick on, I pull my hair

[022-104]until my temper passes. If I hadn't pulled my beard hair by hair, I'd

[022-105]have died a long time ago from sheer anger!"

[022-106]"It does you no good to go to pieces when you're angry," a man affirmed

[022-107]earnestly from below a hat that covered his head as a roof does a

[022-108]house. "When I was up at Torreon I killed an old lady who refused to

[022-109]sell me some enchiladas. She was angry, I can tell you; I got no

[022-110]enchiladas but I felt satisfied anyhow!"

[022-111]"I killed a storekeeper at Parral because he gave me some change and

[022-112]there were two Huerta bills in it," said a man with a star on his hat

[022-113]and precious stones on his black, calloused hands.

[022-114]"Down in Chihuahua I killed a man because I always saw him sitting at

[022-115]the table whenever I went to eat. I hated the looks of him so I just

[022-116]killed him! What the hell could I do!"

[022-117]"Hmm! I killed...."

[022-118]The theme is inexhaustible.

[022-119]By dawn, when the restaurant was wild with joy and the floor dotted

[022-120]with spittle, young painted girls from the suburbs had mingled freely

[022-121]among the dark northern women. Demetrio pulled out his jeweled gold

[022-122]watch, asking Anastasio Montanez to tell him the time.

[022-123]Anastasio glanced at the watch, then, poking his head out of a small

[022-124]window, gazed at the starry sky.

[022-125]"The Pleiades are pretty low in the west. I guess it won't be long now

[022-126]before daybreak...."

[022-127]Outside the restaurant, the shouts, laughter and song of the drunkards

[022-128]rang through the air. Men galloped wildly down the streets, the hoofs

[022-129]of their horses hammering on the sidewalks. From every quarter of the

[022-130]town pistols spoke, guns belched. Demetrio and the girl called War

[022-131]Paint staggered tipsily hand in hand down the center of the street,

[022-132]bound for the hotel.
