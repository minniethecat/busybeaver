[011-001]"Look here, Tenderfoot, I want to tell you something," Camilla called

[011-002]to Luis Cervantes, as he made his way to the hut to fetch some boiling

[011-003]water for his foot.

[011-004]For days the girl had been restless. Her coy ways and her reticence had

[011-005]finally annoyed the man; stopping suddenly, he stood up and eyeing her

[011-006]squarely:

[011-007]"All right. What do you want to tell me?"

[011-008]Camilla's tongue clove to her mouth, heavy and damp as a rag; she could

[011-009]not utter a word. A blush suffused her cheeks, turning them red as

[011-010]apples; she shrugged her shoulders and bowed her head, pressing her

[011-011]chin against her naked breast. Then without moving, with the fixity of

[011-012]an idiot, she glanced at the wound, and said in a whisper:

[011-013]"Look, how nicely it's healing now: it's like a red Castille rose."

[011-014]Luis Cervantes frowned and with obvious disgust continued to care for

[011-015]his foot, completely ignoring her as he worked. When he had finished,

[011-016]Camilla had vanished.

[011-017]For three days she was nowhere to be found. It was always her mother,

[011-018]Agapita, who answered Cervantes' call, and boiled the water for him and

[011-019]gave him rags. He was careful to avoid questioning her. Three days

[011-020]later, Camilla reappeared, more coy and eager than ever.

[011-021]The more distrait and indifferent Luis Cervantes grew, the bolder

[011-022]Camilla. At last, she said: "Listen to me, you nice young fellow, I

[011-023]want to tell you something pleasant. Please go over the words of the

[011-024]revolutionary song 'Adelita' with me, will you? You can guess why, eh?

[011-025]I want to sing it and sing it, over again often and often, see? Then

[011-026]when you're off and away and when you've forgotten all about Camilla,

[011-027]it'll remind me of you."

[011-028]To Luis Cervantes her words were like the noise of a sharp steel knife

[011-029]drawn over the side of a glass bottle. Blissfully unaware of the effect

[011-030]they had produced, she proceeded, candid as ever:

[011-031]"Well, I want to tell you something. You don't know that your chief is

[011-032]a wicked man, do you? Shall I tell you what he did to me? You know

[011-033][[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]] won't let a soul but Mamma cook for him and me take him his

[011-034]food. Well, the other day I take some food over to him and what do you

[011-035]think he did to me, the old fool. He grabs hold of my wrist and he

[011-036]presses it tight, tight as can be, and then he starts pinching my legs.

[011-037]"'Come on, let me go,' I said. 'Keep still, lay off, you shameless

[011-038]creature. You've got no manners, that's the trouble with you.' So I

[011-039]wrestled with him, and shook myself free, like this, and ran off as

[011-040]fast as I could. What do you think of that?"

[011-041]Camilla had never seen Luis Cervantes laugh so heartily.

[011-042]"But it is really true, all this you've told me?"

[011-043]Utterly at a loss, Camilla could not answer. Then he burst into

[011-044]laughter again and repeated the question. A sense of confusion came

[011-045]upon her. Disturbed, troubled, she said brokenly:

[011-046]"Yes, it's the truth. And I wanted to tell you about it. But you don't

[011-047]seem to feel at all angry."

[011-048]Once more Camilla glanced adoringly at Luis Cervantes' radiant, clean

[011-049]face; at his glaucous, soft eyes, his cheeks pink and polished as a

[011-050]porcelain doll's; at his tender white skin that showed below the line

[011-051]of his collar and on his shoulders, protruding from under a rough

[011-052]woolen poncho; at his hair, ever so slightly curled.

[011-053]"What the devil are you waiting for, fool? If the chief likes you, what

[011-054]more do you want?"

[011-055]Camilla felt something rise within her breast, an empty ache that

[011-056]became a knot when it reached her throat; she closed her eyes fast to

[011-057]hold back the tears that welled up in them. Then, with the back of her

[011-058]hand, she wiped her wet cheeks, and just as she had done three days

[011-059]ago, fled with all the swiftness of a young deer.
