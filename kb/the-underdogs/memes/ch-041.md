[041-001][[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]] Macias' wife, mad with joy, rushed along the trail to meet

[041-002]him, leading a child by the hand. An absence of almost two years!

[041-003]They embraced each other and stood speechless. She wept, sobbed.

[041-004]Demetrio stared in astonishment at his wife who seemed to have aged ten

[041-005]or twenty years. Then he looked at the child who gazed up at him in

[041-006]surprise. His heart leaped to his mouth as he saw in the child's

[041-007]features his own steel features and fiery eyes exactly reproduced. He

[041-008]wanted to hold him in his arms, but the frightened child took refuge in

[041-009]his mother's skirts.

[041-010]"It's your own father, baby! It's your daddy!"

[041-011]The child hid his face within the folds of his mother's skirt, still

[041-012]hostile.

[041-013]Demetrio handed the reins of his horse to his orderly and walked slowly

[041-014]along the steep trail with his wife and son.

[041-015]"Blessed be the Virgin Mary, Praise be to God! Now you'll never leave

[041-016]us any more, will you? Never ... never.... You'll stay with us always?"

[041-017]Demetrio's face grew dark. Both remained silent, lost in anguish.

[041-018]Demetrio suppressed a sigh. Memories crowded and buzzed through his

[041-019]brain like bees about a hive.

[041-020]A black cloud rose behind the sierra and a deafening roar of thunder

[041-021]resounded. The rain began to fall in heavy drops; they sought refuge in

[041-022]a rocky hut.

[041-023]The rain came pelting down, shattering the white Saint John roses

[041-024]clustered like sheaves of stars clinging to tree, rock, bush, and

[041-025]pitaya over the entire mountainside.

[041-026]Below in the depths of the canyon, through the gauze of the rain they

[041-027]could see the tall, sheer palms shaking in the wind, opening out like

[041-028]fans before the tempest. Everywhere mountains, heaving hills, and

[041-029]beyond more hills, locked amid mountains, more mountains encircled in

[041-030]the wall of the sierra whose loftiest peaks vanished in the sapphire of

[041-031]the sky.

[041-032]"Demetrio, please. For God's sake, don't go away! My heart tells me

[041-033]something will happen to you this time."

[041-034]Again she was wracked with sobs. The child, frightened, cried and

[041-035]screamed. To calm him, she controlled her own great grief.

[041-036]Gradually the rain stopped, a swallow, with silver breast and wings

[041-037]describing luminous charming curves, fluttered obliquely across the

[041-038]silver threads of the rain, gleaming suddenly in the afternoon sunshine.

[041-039]"Why do you keep on fighting, Demetrio?"

[041-040]Demetrio frowned deeply. Picking up a stone absent-mindedly, he threw

[041-041]it to the bottom of the canyon. Then he stared pensively into the

[041-042]abyss, watching the arch of its flight.

[041-043]"Look at that stone; how it keeps on going...."
