## Overview

Moyahua is the home region of [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio Macías]] in the Jalisco highlands, and the geographic anchor of the novel's opening arc. It is from Moyahua that Demetrio flees after the [[federal-army|Federal soldiers]] burn his house, and it is to Moyahua that he returns in triumph at the head of his revolutionary band. The town embodies the novel's cyclical movement between rural origin and revolutionary destiny.

## Location & Geography

Moyahua is situated in the state of [[jalisco|Jalisco]], in the rugged canyon country of west-central Mexico. The narrative situates it in proximity to [[juchipila|Juchipila]] (the canyon where Demetrio's house is burned) and Limón, where Demetrio was born (013-001). The terrain is described as harsh canyon land — suitable for ambush, difficult for Federal patrols, and emblematic of the rural isolation from which the revolution drew its early recruits.

## Role in the Narrative

Moyahua is first mentioned in the context of danger: Demetrio's men fear that Federal soldiers might "get someone from Moyahua to guide them" into the canyon (002-007), threatening their hiding place. This establishes Moyahua as Demetrio's vulnerable home ground.

After the revolutionary campaign sweeps north through [[zacatecas|Zacatecas]] and beyond, Demetrio's band eventually circles back. The return to Moyahua marks a triumphal homecoming: "To Moyahua, boys. Let's go to Demetrio Macías's home town" (026-003). They enter "four abreast," a display of revolutionary swagger that contrasts sharply with the earlier scene of flight (026-016).

But the homecoming is immediately complicated. The house that was once Demetrio's now "belonged to the cacique of Moyahua" (027-002), and Demetrio is acutely aware that the townspeople's reception is ambivalent — they "really want this done" (027-020), suggesting the villagers see Demetrio's return as a settling of scores rather than a liberation. The mare incident (028-007), where Demetrio demands his stolen horse be returned to Moyahua, further illustrates the tangled personal and political economies of the town.

## Key Passages

> "...But if they got someone from Moyahua to guide them..." [002-007]

*The earliest mention of Moyahua establishes it as Demetrio's vulnerable home ground, where local knowledge could betray him to the Federals.*

> I was born in Limon, close by Moyahua, right in the heart of the canyon. [013-001]

*Demetrio grounds his identity in the specific geography of the Moyahua region, linking his personal origin to the canyon landscape that defines his tactical genius.*

> "To Moyahua, boys. Let's go to Demetrio Macías's home town." [026-003]

*The triumphal return — and four abreast they entered (026-016) — marks the revolutionary band's moment of vindication in the place where Demetrio began as a fugitive.*

> ...belonged to the cacique of Moyahua. The previous occupants had abandoned everything. [027-002]

*The house transfer reveals the social turnover of the revolution: old power structures (the cacique) and new (Demetrio's band) overlap uneasily.*
