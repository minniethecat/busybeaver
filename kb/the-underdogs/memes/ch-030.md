[030-001]A whirlwind of dust, scorching down the road, suddenly broke into

[030-002]violent diffuse masses; and [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]]'s army emerged, a chaos of horses,

[030-003]broad chests, tangled manes, dilated nostrils, oval, wide eyes, hoofs

[030-004]flying in the air, legs stiffened from endless galloping; and of men

[030-005]with bronze faces, ivory teeth, and flashing eyes, their rifles in

[030-006]their hands or slung across the saddles.

[030-007]Demetrio and Camilla brought up the rear. She was still nervous,

[030-008]white-lipped and parched; he was angry at their futile maneuver. For

[030-009]there had been battles, no followers of Orozco's to be seen. A handful

[030-010]of Federals, routed. A poor devil of a priest left dangling from a

[030-011]mesquite; a few dead, scattered over the field, who had once been

[030-012]united under the archaic slogan, RIGHTS AND RELIGION, with, on their

[030-013]breasts, the red cloth insignia: Halt! The Sacred Heart of Jesus is

[030-014]with me!

[030-015]"One good thing about it is that I've collected all my back pay," Quail

[030-016]said, exhibiting some gold watches and rings stolen from the priest's

[030-017]house.

[030-018]"It's fun fighting this way," Manteca cried, spicing every other word

[030-019]with an oath. "You know why the hell you're risking your hide."

[030-020]In the same hand with which he held the reins, he clutched a shining

[030-021]ornament that he had torn from one of the holy statues.

[030-022]After Quail, an expert in such matters, had examined Manteca's treasure

[030-023]covetously, he uttered a solemn guffaw.

[030-024]"Hell, Your ornament is nothing but tin!"

[030-025]"Why in hell are you hanging on to that poison?" Pancracio asked

[030-026]Blondie who appeared dragging a prisoner.

[030-027]"Do you want to know why? Because it's a long time since I've had a

[030-028]good look at a man's face when a rope tightens around his neck!"

[030-029]The fat prisoner breathed with difficulty as he followed Blondie on

[030-030]foot; his face was sunburnt, his eyes red; his forehead beaded with

[030-031]sweat, his wrists tightly bound together.

[030-032]"Here, Anastasio, lend me your lasso. Mine's not strong enough; this

[030-033]bird will bust it. No, by God, I've changed my mind, friend Federal:

[030-034]think I'll kill you on the spot, because you are pulling too hard.

[030-035]Look, all the mesquites are still a long way off and there are no

[030-036]telegraph poles to hang you to!"

[030-037]Blondie pulled his gun out, pressed the muzzle against the prisoner's

[030-038]chest and brought his finger against the trigger slowly ... slowly....

[030-039]The prisoner turned pale as a corpse; his face lengthened; his eyelids

[030-040]were fixed in a glassy stare. He breathed in agony, his whole body

[030-041]shook as with ague. Blondie kept his gun in the same position for a

[030-042]moment long as all eternity. His eyes shone queerly. An expression of

[030-043]supreme pleasure lit up his fat puffy face.

[030-044]"No, friend Federal," he drawled, putting back his gun into the

[030-045]holster; "I'm not going to kill you just yet.... I'll make you my

[030-046]orderly. You'll see that I'm not so hardhearted!"

[030-047]Slyly he winked at his companions. The prisoner had turned into an

[030-048]animal; he gulped, panting, dry-mouthed. Camilla, who had witnessed the

[030-049]scene, spurred her horse and caught up with Demetrio.

[030-050]"What a brute that Blondie is: you ought to see what he did to a

[030-051]wretched prisoner," she said. Then she told Demetrio what had occurred.

[030-052]The latter wrinkled his brow but made no answer.

[030-053]War Paint called Camilla aside.

[030-054]"Hey you ... what are you gobbling about? Blondie's my man, understand?

[030-055]From now on, you know how things are: whatever you've got against him

[030-056]you've got against me too! I'm warning you."

[030-057]Camilla, frightened, hurried back to Demetrio's side.
