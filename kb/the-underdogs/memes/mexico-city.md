## Overview

Mexico City appears in the novel as a distant locus of political power whose events reach [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio Macías]] through rumor, orders, and consequences.

The assassination of [[francisco-i-madero|Francisco I. Madero]] in the capital (013-032) sends Federal forces into the countryside to hunt men like Demetrio. The capital remains an abstraction — a place the underdogs never see but whose politics shape their fate.

## Location & Geography

Mexico City is located in the Valley of Mexico, far removed from the novel's Jalisco-Zacatecas highlands. The distance between capital and canyon country mirrors the distance between the elite who make revolutions and the rural poor who fight them (013-032).

## Role in the Narrative

Mexico City first enters through its political violence. Don Monico, the cacique who threatens Demetrio, raises an army after "that story in Mexico City — about the killing of Madero and some other fellow" (013-032). The assassination in the capital directly precipitates Demetrio's persecution.

The capital also appears as the endpoint of the Federal command. A dispatch to "General A. Blanquet, Mexico City" (016-119) reports on rebel activity, showing how the rural war is managed from the capital (016-118).

The underdogs of the title are defined partly by their distance from Mexico City: they fight battles whose political meaning is decided elsewhere (016-121).
