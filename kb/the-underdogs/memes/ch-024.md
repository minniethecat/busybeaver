[024-001]"General [[demetrio-macias|Macias]], I want you to meet my future wife," Luis Cervantes

[024-002]said with great emphasis as he led a beautiful girl into the dining

[024-003]room.

[024-004]They all turned to look at her. Her large blue eyes grew wide in

[024-005]wonder. She was barely fourteen. Her skin was like a rose, soft, pink,

[024-006]fresh; her hair was very fair; the expression in her eyes was partly

[024-007]impish curiosity, partly a vague childish fear. Perceiving that

[024-008]Demetrio eyed her like a beast of prey, Luis Cervantes congratulated

[024-009]himself.

[024-010]They made room for her between Luis Cervantes and Blondie, opposite

[024-011]Demetrio.

[024-012]Bottles of tequila, dishes of cut glass, bowls, porcelains and vases

[024-013]lay scattered over the table indiscriminately. Meco, carrying a box of

[024-014]beer upon his shoulders, came in cursing and sweating.

[024-015]"You don't know this fellow Blondie yet," said War Paint, noticing the

[024-016]persistent glances he was casting at Luis Cervantes' bride. "He's a

[024-017]smart fellow, I can tell you, and he never misses a trick." She gazed

[024-018]at him lecherously, adding:

[024-019]"That's why I don't like to see him close, even on a photograph!"

[024-020]The orchestra struck up a raucous march as though they were playing at

[024-021]a bullfight. The soldiers roared with joy.

[024-022]"What fine tripe, General; I swear I haven't tasted the like of it in

[024-023]all my life," Blondie said, as he began to reminisce about "El Monico"

[024-024]at Chihuahua.

[024-025]"You really like it, Blondie?" responded Demetrio. "Go ahead, call for

[024-026]more, eat your bellyful."

[024-027]"It's just the way I like it," Anastasio chimed in. "Yes, I like good

[024-028]food! But nothing really tastes good to you unless you belch!"

[024-029]The noise of mouths being filled, of ravenous feeding followed. All

[024-030]drank copiously. At the end of the dinner, Luis Cervantes rose, holding

[024-031]a champagne glass in one hand, and said:

[024-032]"General..."

[024-033]"Ho!" War Paint interrupted. "This speech-making business isn't for me;

[024-034]I'm all against it. I'll go out to the corral since there's no more

[024-035]eating here."

[024-036]Presenting Demetrio with a black velvet-covered box containing a small

[024-037]brass eagle, Luis Cervantes made a toast which no one understood but

[024-038]everyone applauded enthusiastically. Demetrio took the insignia in his

[024-039]hands; and with flushed face, and eyes shining, declared with great

[024-040]candor:

[024-041]"What in hell am I going to do with this buzzard!"

[024-042]"Compadre," Anastasio Montanez said in a tremulous voice. "I ain't got

[024-043]much to tell you...."

[024-044]Whole minutes elapsed between his words; the cursed words would not

[024-045]come to Anastasio. His face, coated with filth, unwashed for days,

[024-046]turned crimson, shining with perspiration. Finally he decided to finish

[024-047]his toast at all costs. "Well, I ain't got much to tell you, except

[024-048]that we are pals...."

[024-049]Then, since everyone had applauded at the end of Luis Cervantes'

[024-050]speech, Anastasio having finished, made a sign, and the company clapped

[024-051]their hands in great gravity.

[024-052]But everything turned out for the best, since his awkwardness inspired

[024-053]others. Manteca and Quail stood up and made their toasts, too. When

[024-054]Meco's turn came, War Paint rushed in shouting jubilantly, attempting

[024-055]to drag a splendid black horse into the dining room.

[024-056]"My booty! My booty!" she cried, patting the superb animal on the neck.

[024-057]It resisted every effort she made until a strong jerk of the rope and a

[024-058]sudden lash brought it in prancing smartly. The soldiers, half drunk,

[024-059]stared at the beast with ill-disguised envy.

[024-060]"I don't know what the hell this she-devil's got, but she always beats

[024-061]everybody to it," cried Blondie. "She's been the same ever since she

[024-062]joined us at Tierra Blanca!"

[024-063]"Hey, Pancracio, bring me some alfalfa for my horse," War Paint

[024-064]commanded crisply, throwing the horse's rope to one of the soldiers.

[024-065]Once more they filled their glasses. Many a head hung low with fatigue

[024-066]or drunkenness. Most of the company, however, shouted with glee,

[024-067]including Luis Cervantes' girl. She had spilled all her wine on a

[024-068]handkerchief and looked all about her with blue wondering eyes.

[024-069]"Boys," Blondie suddenly screamed, his shrill, guttural voice

[024-070]dominating the mall, "I'm tired of living; I feel like killing myself

[024-071]right now. I'm sick and tired of War Paint and this other little angel

[024-072]from heaven won't even look at me!"

[024-073]Luis Cervantes saw that the last remark was addressed to his bride;

[024-074]with great surprise he realized that it was not Demetrio's foot he had

[024-075]noticed close to the girl's, but Blondie's. He was boiling with

[024-076]indignation.

[024-077]"Keep your eye on me, boys," Blondie went on, gun in hand. "I'm going

[024-078]to shoot myself right in the forehead!"

[024-079]He aimed at the large mirror on the opposite wall which gave back his

[024-080]whole body in reflection. He took careful aim....

[024-081]"Don't move, War Paint."

[024-082]The bullet whizzed by, grazing War Paint's hair. The mirror broke into

[024-083]large jagged fragments. She did not even so much as blink.
