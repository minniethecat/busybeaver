[002-001]Everything was still swathed in shadows as [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]] Macias began his

[002-002]descent to the bottom of the ravine. Between rocks striped with huge

[002-003]eroded cracks, and a squarely cut wall, with the river flowing below, a

[002-004]narrow ledge along the steep incline served as a mountain trail.

[002-005]"They'll surely find me now and track us down like dogs," he mused.

[002-006]"It's a good thing they know nothing about the trails and paths up

[002-007]here.... But if they got someone from Moyahua to guide them ..." He

[002-008]left the sinister thought unfinished. "All the men from Limon or Santa

[002-009]Rosa or the other nearby ranches are on our side: they wouldn't try to

[002-010]trail us. That cacique who's chased and run me ragged over these hills,

[002-011]is at Mohayua now; he'd give his eyeteeth to see me dangling from a

[002-012]telegraph pole with my tongue hanging out of my mouth, purple and

[002-013]swollen...."

[002-014]At dawn, he approached the pit of the canyon. Here, he lay on the rocks

[002-015]and fell asleep.

[002-016]The river crept along, murmuring as the waters rose and fell in small

[002-017]cascades. Birds sang lyrically from their hiding among the pitaya

[002-018]trees. The monotonous, eternal drone of insects filled the rocky

[002-019]solitude with mystery.

[002-020]Demetrio awoke with a start. He waded the river, following its course

[002-021]which ran counter to the canyon; he climbed the crags laboriously as an

[002-022]ant, gripping root and rock with his hands, clutching every stone in

[002-023]the trail with his bare feet.

[002-024]When he reached the summit, he glanced down to see the sun steeping the

[002-025]valley in a lake of gold. Near the canyon, enormous rocks loomed

[002-026]protrudent, like fantastic Negro skulls. The pitaya trees rose tenuous,

[002-027]tall, like the tapering, gnarled fingers of a giant; other trees of all

[002-028]sorts bowed their crests toward the pit of the abyss. Amid the stark

[002-029]rocks and dry branches, roses bloomed like a white offering to the sun

[002-030]as smoothly, suavely, it unraveled its golden threads, one by one, from

[002-031]rock to rock.

[002-032]Demetrio stopped at the summit. Reaching backward, with his right arm

[002-033]he drew his horn which hung at his back, held it up to his thick lips,

[002-034]and, swelling his cheeks out, blew three loud blasts. From across the

[002-035]hill close by, three sharp whistles answered his signal.

[002-036]In the distance, from a conical heap of reeds and dry straws, man after

[002-037]man emerged, one after the other, their legs and chests naked, lambent

[002-038]and dark as old bronze. They rushed forward to greet Demetrio, and

[002-039]stopped before him, askance.

[002-040]"They've burnt my house," he said.

[002-041]A murmur of oaths, imprecations, and threats rose among them.

[002-042]Demetrio let their anger run its course. Then he drew a bottle from

[002-043]under his shirt and took a deep swig; then he wiped the neck of the

[002-044]bottle with the back of his hand and passed it around. It passed from

[002-045]mouth to mouth; not a drop was left. The men passed their tongues

[002-046]greedily over their lips to recapture the tang of the liquor.

[002-047]"Glory be to God and by His Will," said Demetrio, "tonight or tomorrow

[002-048]at the latest we'll meet the Federals. What do you say, boys, shall we

[002-049]let them find their way about these trails?"

[002-050]The ragged crew jumped to their feet, uttering shrill cries of joy;

[002-051]then their jubilation turned sinister and they gave vent to threats,

[002-052]oaths and imprecations.

[002-053]"Of course, we can't tell how strong they are," said Demetrio as his

[002-054]glance traveled over their faces in scrutiny.

[002-055]"Do you remember Medina? Out there at Hostotipaquillo, he only had a

[002-056]half a dozen men with knives that they sharpened on a grindstone. Well,

[002-057]he held back the soldiers and the police, didn't he? And he beat them,

[002-058]too."

[002-059]"We're every bit as good as Medina's crowd!" said a tall,

[002-060]broad-shouldered man with a black beard and bushy eyebrows.

[002-061]"By God, if I don't own a Mauser and a lot of cartridges, if I can't

[002-062]get a pair of trousers and shoes, then my name's not Anastasio

[002-063]Montanez! Look here, Quail, you don't believe it, do you? You ask my

[002-064]partner Demetrio if I haven't half a dozen bullets in me already.

[002-065]Christ! Bullets are marbles to me! And I dare you to contradict me!"

[002-066]"Viva Anastasio Montanez," shouted Manteca.

[002-067]"All right, all right!" said Montanez. "Viva Demetrio Macias, our

[002-068]chief, and long life to God in His heaven and to the Virgin Mary."

[002-069]"Viva Demetrio Macias," they all shouted.

[002-070]They gathered dry brush and wood, built a fire and placed chunks of

[002-071]fresh meat upon the burning coals. As the blaze rose, they collected

[002-072]about the fire, sat down Indian-fashion and inhaled the odor of the

[002-073]meat as it twisted on the crackling fire. The rays of the sun, falling

[002-074]about them, cast a golden radiance over the bloody hide of a calf,

[002-075]lying on the ground nearby. The meat dangled from a rope fastened to a

[002-076]huizache tree, to dry in the sun and wind.

[002-077]"Well, men," Demetrio said, "you know we've only twenty rifles, besides

[002-078]my thirty-thirty. If there are just a few of them, we'll shoot until

[002-079]there's not a live man left. If there's a lot of 'em, we can give 'em a

[002-080]good scare, anyhow."

[002-081]He undid a rag belt about his waist, loosened a knot in it and offered

[002-082]the contents to his companions. Salt. A murmur of approbation rose

[002-083]among them as each took a few grains between the tips of his fingers.

[002-084]They ate voraciously; then, glutted, lay down on the ground, facing the

[002-085]sky. They sang monotonous, sad songs, uttering a strident shout after

[002-086]each stanza.
