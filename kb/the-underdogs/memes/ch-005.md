[005-001]Suddenly awakening, Quail opened his eyes and stood up.

[005-002]"Montanez, did you hear? A shot, Montanez! Hey, Montanez, get up!"

[005-003]He shook him vigorously until Montanez ceased snoring and in turn woke

[005-004]up.

[005-005]"What in the name of ... Now you're at it again, damn it. I tell you

[005-006]there aren't ghosts any more," Anastasio muttered out of a half-sleep.

[005-007]"I heard a shot, Montanez!"

[005-008]"Go back to sleep, Quail, or I'll bust your nose."

[005-009]"Hell, Anastasio I tell you it's no nightmare. I've forgotten those

[005-010]fellows they hung, honest. It's a shot, I tell you. I heard it all

[005-011]right."

[005-012]"A shot, you say? All right, then, hand me my gun."

[005-013]Anastasio Montanez rubbed his eyes, stretched out his arms and legs,

[005-014]and stood up lazily.

[005-015]They left the hut. The sky was solid with stars; the moon rose like a

[005-016]sharp scythe. The confused rumor of women crying in fright resounded

[005-017]from the various huts; the men who had been sleeping in the open, also

[005-018]woke up and the rattle of arms echoed over the mountain.

[005-019]"You cursed fool, you've maimed me for life."

[005-020]A voice rang clearly through the darkness.

[005-021]"Who goes there?"

[005-022]The shout echoed from rock to rock, through mound and over hollow,

[005-023]until it spent itself at the far, silent reaches of the night.

[005-024]"Who goes there?" Anastasio repeated his challenge louder, pulling back

[005-025]the lock of his Mauser.

[005-026]"One of [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]]'s men," came the answer.

[005-027]"It's Pancracio," Quail cried joyfully. Relieved, he rested the butt of

[005-028]his rifle on the ground.

[005-029]Pancracio appeared, holding a young man by the arms; the newcomer was

[005-030]covered with dust from his felt hat to his coarse shoes. A fresh

[005-031]bloodstain lay on his trousers close to the heel.

[005-032]"Who's this tenderfoot?" Anastasio demanded.

[005-033]"You know I'm on guard around here. Well, I hears a noise in the brush,

[005-034]see, and I shouts, 'Who goes there?' and then this lad answers,

[005-035]'Carranza! Carranza!' I don't know anyone by that name, and so I says,

[005-036]'Carranza, hell!' and I just pumps a bit of lead into his hoof."

[005-037]Smiling, Pancracio turned his beardless head around as if soliciting

[005-038]applause.

[005-039]Then the stranger spoke:

[005-040]"Who's your commander?"

[005-041]Proudly, Anastasio raised his head, went up to him and looked him in

[005-042]the face. The stranger lowered his tone considerably.

[005-043]"Well, I'm a revolutionist, too, you know. The Government drafted me

[005-044]and I served as a private, but I managed to desert during the battle

[005-045]the day before yesterday, and I've been walking about in search of you

[005-046]all."

[005-047]"So he's a Government soldier, eh?" A murmur of incredulity rose from

[005-048]the men, interrupting the stranger.

[005-049]"So that's what you are, eh? One of those damn half-breeds," said

[005-050]Anastasio Montanez. "Why the hell didn't you pump your lead in his

[005-051]brain, Pancracio?"

[005-052]"What's he talking about, anyhow? I can't make head nor tail of it. He

[005-053]says he wants to see Demetrio and that he's got plenty to say to him.

[005-054]But that's all right: we've got plenty of time to do anything we damn

[005-055]well please so long as you're in no hurry, that's all," said Pancracio,

[005-056]loading his gun.

[005-057]"What kind of beasts are you?" the prisoner cried. He could say no

[005-058]more: Anastasio's fist, crashing down upon his face, sent his head

[005-059]turning on his neck, covered with blood.

[005-060]"Shoot the half-breed!"

[005-061]"Hang him!"

[005-062]"Burn him alive; he's a lousy Federal."

[005-063]In great excitement, they yelled and shrieked and were about to fire at

[005-064]the prisoner.

[005-065]"Sssh! Shut up! I think Demetrio's talking now," Anastasio said,

[005-066]striving to quiet them. Indeed, Demetrio, having ascertained the cause

[005-067]of the turmoil, ordered them to bring the prisoner before him.

[005-068]"It's positively infamous, senor; look," Luis Cervantes said, pointing

[005-069]to the bloodstains on his trousers and to his bleeding face.

[005-070]"All right, all right. But who in hell are you? That's what I want to

[005-071]know," Demetrio said.

[005-072]"My name is Luis Cervantes, sir. I'm a medical student and a

[005-073]journalist. I wrote a piece in favor of the [[mexican-revolution|revolution]], you see; as a

[005-074]result, they persecuted me, caught me, and finally landed me in the

[005-075]barracks."

[005-076]His ensuing narrative was couched in terms of such detail and expressed

[005-077]in terms so melodramatic that it drew guffaws of mirth from Pancracio

[005-078]and Manteca.

[005-079]"All I've tried to do is to make myself clear on this point. I want you

[005-080]to be convinced that I am truly one of your coreligionists...."

[005-081]"What's that? What did you say? Car ... what?" Demetrio asked, bringing

[005-082]his ear close to Cervantes.

[005-083]"Coreligionist, sir, that is to say, a person who possesses the same

[005-084]religion, who is inspired by the same ideals, who defends and fights

[005-085]for the same cause you are now fighting for."

[005-086]Demetrio smiled:

[005-087]"What are we fighting for? That's what I'd like to know."

[005-088]In his disconcertment, Luis Cervantes could find no reply.

[005-089]"Look at that mug, look at 'im! Why waste any time, Demetrio? Let's

[005-090]shoot him," Pancracio urged impatiently.

[005-091]Demetrio laid a hand on his hair which covered his ears, and stretching

[005-092]himself out for a long time, seemed to be lost in thought. Having found

[005-093]no solution, he said:

[005-094]"Get out, all of you; it's aching again. Anastasio put out the candle.

[005-095]Lock him up in the corral and let Pancracio and Manteca watch him.

[005-096]Tomorrow, we'll see."
