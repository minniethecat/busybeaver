[035-001]Stale cigarette smoke, the acrid odors of sweaty clothing, the vapors

[035-002]of alcohol, the breathing of a crowded multitude, worse by far than a

[035-003]trainful of pigs.

[035-004]Texas hats, adorned with gold braid, and khaki predominate. "Gentlemen,

[035-005]a well-dressed man stole my suitcase in the station. My life's savings!

[035-006]I haven't enough to feed my little boy now!"

[035-007]The shrill voice, rising to a shriek or trailing off into a sob, is

[035-008]drowned out by the tumult within the train.

[035-009]"What the hell is the old woman talking about?" Blondie asks, entering

[035-010]in search of a seat.

[035-011]"Something about a suitcase ... and a well-dressed man," Pancracio

[035-012]replies. He has already the laps of two civilians to sit on.

[035-013][[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]] and the others elbow their way in. Since those on whom

[035-014]Pancracio had sat preferred to stand up, Demetrio and Luis Cervantes

[035-015]quickly seize the vacant seats.

[035-016]Suddenly a woman who has stood up holding a child all the way from

[035-017]Irapuato, faints. A civilian takes the child in his arms. The others

[035-018]pretend to have seen nothing. Some women, traveling with the soldiers,

[035-019]occupy two or three seats with baggage, dogs, cats, parrots. Some of

[035-020]the men wearing Texan hats laugh at the plump arms and pendulous

[035-021]breasts of the woman who fainted.

[035-022]"Gentlemen, a well-dressed man stole my suitcase at the station in

[035-023]Silao! All my life's savings ... I haven't got enough to feed my little

[035-024]boy now! ..."

[035-025]The old woman speaks rapidly, parrotlike, sighing and sobbing. Her

[035-026]sharp eyes peer about on all sides. Here she gets a bill, and further

[035-027]on, another. They shower money upon her. She finishes the collection,

[035-028]and goes a few seats ahead.

[035-029]"Gentlemen, a well-dressed man stole my suitcase in the station at

[035-030]Silao." Her words produce an immediate and certain effect.

[035-031]A well-dressed man, a dude, a tenderfoot, stealing a suitcase! Amazing,

[035-032]phenomenal! It awakens a feeling of universal indignation. It's a pity:

[035-033]if this well-dressed man were here every one of the generals would

[035-034]shoot him one after the other!

[035-035]"There's nothing as vile as a city dude who steals!" a man says,

[035-036]exploding with indignation.

[035-037]"To rob a poor old lady!"

[035-038]"To steal from a poor defenseless woman!"

[035-039]They prove their compassion by word and deed: a harsh verdict against

[035-040]the culprit; a five-peso bill for the victim.

[035-041]"And I'm telling you the truth," Blondie declares. "Don't think it's

[035-042]wrong to kill, because when you kill, it's always out of anger. But

[035-043]stealing--Bah!"

[035-044]This profound piece of reasoning meets with unanimous assent. After a

[035-045]short silence while he meditates, a colonel ventures his opinion:

[035-046]"Everything is all right according to something, see? That is,

[035-047]everything has its circumstances, see? God's own truth is this: I have

[035-048]stolen, and if I say that everyone here has done the trick, I'm not

[035-049]telling a lie, I reckon!"

[035-050]"Hell, I stole a lot of them sewing machines in Mexico," exclaims a

[035-051]major. "I made more'n five hundred pesos even though I sold them at

[035-052]fifty cents apiece!"

[035-053]A toothless captain, with hair prematurely white, announces:

[035-054]"I stole some horses in [[zacatecas|Zacatecas]], all damn fine horses they was, and

[035-055]then I says to myself, 'This is your own little lottery, Pascual Mata,'

[035-056]I says. 'You won't have a worry in all your life after this.' And the

[035-057]damned thing about it was that General Limon took a fancy to the horses

[035-058]too, and he stole them from me!"

[035-059]"Of course--there's no use denying it, I've stolen too," Blondie

[035-060]confesses. "But ask any one of my partners how much profit I've got.

[035-061]I'm a big spender and my Purse is my friends' to have a good time on! I

[035-062]have a better time if I drink myself senseless than I would have

[035-063]sending money back home to the old woman!"

[035-064]The subject of "I stole," though apparently inexhaustible, ceases to

[035-065]hold the men's attention. Decks of cards gradually appear on the seats,

[035-066]drawing generals and officers as the light draws mosquitoes.

[035-067]The excitement of gambling soon absorbs every interest, the heat grows

[035-068]more and more intense. To breathe is to inhale the air of barracks,

[035-069]prison, brothel, and pigsty all in one.

[035-070]And rising above the babble, from the car ahead ever the shrill voice,

[035-071]"Gentlemen, a well-dressed young man stole ..."

[035-072]The streets in Aguascalientes were so many refuse piles. Men in khaki

[035-073]moved to and fro like bees before their hive, overrunning the

[035-074]restaurants, the crapulous lunch houses, the parlous hotels, and the

[035-075]stands of the street vendors on which rotten pork lay alongside grimy

[035-076]cheese.

[035-077]The smell of these viands whetted the appetites of Demetrio and his

[035-078]men. They forced their way into a small inn, where a disheveled old hag

[035-079]served, on earthenware plates, some pork with bones swimming in a clear

[035-080]chili stew and three tough burnt tortillas. They paid two pesos apiece;

[035-081]as they left Pancracio assured his comrades he was hungrier than when

[035-082]he entered.

[035-083]"Now," said Demetrio, "we'll go and consult with General Natera!"

[035-084]They made for the northern leader's billet.

[035-085]A noisy, excited crowd stopped them at a street crossing. A man, lost

[035-086]in the multitude, was mouthing words in the monotonous, unctuous tones

[035-087]of a prayer. They came up close enough to see him distinctly; he wore a

[035-088]shirt and trousers of cheap white cloth and was repeating:

[035-089]"All good Catholics should read this prayer to Christ Our Lord upon the

[035-090]Cross with due devotion. Thus they will be immune from storms and

[035-091]pestilence, famine, and war."

[035-092]"This man's no fool," said Demetrio smiling.

[035-093]The man waved a sheaf of printed handbills in his hand and cried:

[035-094]"A quarter of a peso is all you have to pay for this prayer to Christ

[035-095]Our Lord upon the Cross. A quarter ..."

[035-096]Then he would duck for a moment, to reappear with a snake's tooth, a

[035-097]sea star, or the skeleton of a fish. In the same predicant tone, he

[035-098]lauded the medical virtues and the mystical powers of every article he

[035-099]sold.

[035-100]Quail, who had no faith in Venancio, requested the man to pull a tooth

[035-101]out. Blondie purchased a black seed from a certain fruit which

[035-102]protected the possessor from lightning or any other catastrophe.

[035-103]Anastasio Montanez purchased a prayer to Christ Our Lord upon the

[035-104]Cross, and, folding it carefully, stuck it into his shirt with a pious

[035-105]gesture.

[035-106]"As sure as there's a God in heaven," Natera said, "this mess hasn't

[035-107]blown over yet. Now it's Villa fighting Carranza."

[035-108]Without answering him, his eyes fixed in a stare, Demetrio demanded a

[035-109]further explanation.

[035-110]"It means," Natera said, "that the Convention won't recognize Carranza

[035-111]as First Chief of the Constitutionalist Army. It's going to elect a

[035-112]Provisional President of the Republic. Do you understand me, General?"

[035-113]Demetrio nodded assent.

[035-114]"What's your opinion, General?" asked Natera.

[035-115]Demetrio shrugged his shoulders:

[035-116]"It seems to me that the meat of the matter is that we've got to go on

[035-117]fighting, eh? All right! Let's go to it! I'm game to the end, you know."

[035-118]"Good, but on what side?"

[035-119]Demetrio, nonplussed, scratched his head:

[035-120]"Look here, don't ask me any more questions. I never went to school,

[035-121]you know.... You gave me the eagle I wear on my hat, didn't you? All

[035-122]right then; you just tell me: 'Demetrio, do this or do that,' and

[035-123]that's all there's to it!"
