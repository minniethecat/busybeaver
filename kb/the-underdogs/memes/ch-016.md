[016-001]At midnight [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]] Macias ordered the march to be resumed. The town

[016-002]was five or six miles away; the best plan was to take the soldiers by

[016-003]surprise, before reveille.

[016-004]The sky was cloudy, with here and there a star shining. From time to

[016-005]time a flash of lightning crossed the sky with a red dart, illumining

[016-006]the far horizon.

[016-007]Luis Cervantes asked Demetrio whether the success of the attack might

[016-008]not be better served by procuring a guide or leastways by ascertaining

[016-009]the topographic conditions of the town and the precise location of the

[016-010]soldiers' quarters.

[016-011]"No," Demetrio answered, accompanying his smile with a disdainful

[016-012]gesture, "we'll simply fall on them when they least expect it; that's

[016-013]all there is to it, see? We've done it before all right, lots of times!

[016-014]Haven't you ever seen the squirrels stick their heads out of their

[016-015]holes when you poured in water? Well, that's how these lousy soldiers

[016-016]are going to feel. Do you see? They'll be frightened out of their wits

[016-017]the moment they hear our first shot. Then they'll slink out and stand

[016-018]as targets for us."

[016-019]"Suppose the old man we met yesterday lied to us. Suppose there are

[016-020]fifty soldiers instead of twenty. Who knows but he's a spy sent out by

[016-021]the Federals!"

[016-022]"Ha, Tenderfoot, frightened already, eh?" Anastasio Montanez mocked.

[016-023]"Sure! Handling a rifle and messing about with bandages are two

[016-024]different things," Pancracio observed.

[016-025]"Well, that's enough talk, I guess," said Meco. "All we have to do is

[016-026]fight a dozen frightened rats."

[016-027]"This fight won't convince our mothers that they gave birth to men or

[016-028]whatever the hell you like...." Manteca added.

[016-029]When they reached the outskirts of the town, Venancio walked ahead and

[016-030]knocked at the door of a hut.

[016-031]"Where's the soldiers' barracks?" he inquired of a man who came out

[016-032]barefoot, a ragged serape covering his body.

[016-033]"Right there, just beyond the Plaza," he answered.

[016-034]Since nobody knew where the city square was, Venancio made him walk

[016-035]ahead to show the way. Trembling with fear, the poor devil told them

[016-036]they were doing him a terrible wrong.

[016-037]"I'm just a poor day laborer, sir; I've got a wife and a lot of kids."

[016-038]"What the hell do you think I have, dogs?" Demetrio scowled. "I've got

[016-039]kids too, see?"

[016-040]Then he commanded:

[016-041]"You men keep quiet. Not a sound out of you! And walk down the middle

[016-042]of the street, single file."

[016-043]The rectangular church cupola rose above the small houses.

[016-044]"Here, gentlemen; there's the Plaza beyond the church. Just walk a bit

[016-045]further and there's the barracks."

[016-046]He knelt down, then, imploring them to let him go, but Pancracio,

[016-047]without pausing to reply, struck him across the chest with his rifle

[016-048]and ordered him to proceed.

[016-049]"How many soldiers are there?" Luis Cervantes asked.

[016-050]"I don't want to lie to you, boss, but to tell you the truth, yes, sir,

[016-051]to tell you God's truth, there's a lot of them, a whole lot of 'em."

[016-052]Luis Cervantes turned around to stare at Demetrio, who feigned

[016-053]momentary deafness.

[016-054]They were soon in the city square.

[016-055]A loud volley of rifle shots rang out, deafening them. Demetrio's horse

[016-056]reared, staggered on its hind legs, bent its forelegs, and fell to the

[016-057]ground, kicking. The Owl uttered a piercing cry and fell from his horse

[016-058]which rushed madly to the center of the square.

[016-059]Another volley: the guide threw up his arms and fell on his back

[016-060]without a sound.

[016-061]With all haste, Anastasio Montanez helped Demetrio up behind him on his

[016-062]horse; the others retreated, seeking shelter along the walls of the

[016-063]houses.

[016-064]"Hey, men," said a workman sticking his head out of a large door, "go

[016-065]for 'em through the back of the chapel. They're all in there. Cut back

[016-066]through this street, then turn to the left; you'll reach an alley. Keep

[016-067]on going ahead until you hit the chapel."

[016-068]As he spoke a fresh volley of pistol shots, directed from the

[016-069]neighboring roofs, fell like a rain about them.

[016-070]"By God," the man said, "those ain't poisonous spiders; they're only

[016-071]townsmen scared of their own shadow. Come in here until they stop."

[016-072]"How many of them are there?" asked Demetrio.

[016-073]"There were only twelve of them. But last night they were scared out of

[016-074]their wits so they wired to the town beyond for help. I don't know how

[016-075]many of them there are now. Even if there are a hell of a lot of them,

[016-076]it doesn't cut any ice! Most of them aren't soldiers, you know, but

[016-077]drafted men; if just one of them starts mutinying, the rest will follow

[016-078]like sheep. My brother was drafted; they've got him there. I'll go

[016-079]along with you and signal to him; all of them will desert and follow

[016-080]you. Then we'll only have the officers to deal with! If you want to

[016-081]give me a gun or something...."

[016-082]"No more rifles left, brother. But I guess you can put these to some

[016-083]use," Anastasio Montanez said, passing him two hand grenades.

[016-084]The officer in command of the Federals was a young coxcomb of a captain

[016-085]with a waxed mustache and blond hair. As long as he felt uncertain

[016-086]about the strength of the assailants, he had remained extremely quiet

[016-087]and prudent; but now that they had driven the rebels back without

[016-088]allowing them a chance to fire a single shot, he waxed bold and brave.

[016-089]While the soldiers did not dare put out their heads beyond the pillars

[016-090]of the building, his own shadow stood against the pale clear dawn,

[016-091]exhibiting his well-built slender body and his officer's cape bellying

[016-092]in the breeze.

[016-093]"Ha, I remember our coup d'etat!"

[016-094]His military career had consisted of the single adventure when,

[016-095]together with other students of the Officers' School, he was involved

[016-096]in the treacherous revolt of Feliz Diaz and Huerta against President

[016-097]Madero. Whenever the slightest insubordination arose, he invariably

[016-098]recalled his feat at the Ciudadela.

[016-099]"Lieutenant Campos," he ordered emphatically, "take a dozen men and

[016-100]wipe out the bandits hiding there! The curs! They're only brave when it

[016-101]comes to guzzling meat and robbing a hencoop!"

[016-102]A workingman appeared at the small door of the spiral staircase,

[016-103]announcing that the assailants were hidden in a corral where they might

[016-104]easily be captured. This message came from the citizens keeping watch

[016-105]on housetops.

[016-106]"I'll go myself and get it over with!" the officer declared impetuously.

[016-107]But he soon changed his mind. Before he had reached the door, he

[016-108]retraced his steps.

[016-109]"Very likely they are waiting for more men and, of course, it would be

[016-110]wrong for me to abandon my post. Lieutenant Campos, go there yourself

[016-111]and capture them dead or alive. We'll shoot them at noon when

[016-112]everybody's coming out of church. Those bandits will see the example

[016-113]I'll set around here. But if you can't capture them, Lieutenant, kill

[016-114]them all. Don't leave a man of them alive, do you understand?"

[016-115]In high good humor, he began pacing up and down the room, formulating

[016-116]the official despatch he would send off no later than today.

[016-117]To His Honor the Minister for War,

[016-118]General A. Blanquet,  

[016-119]Mexico City.

[016-120]Sir:

[016-121]I have the honor to inform your Excellency that on the morning of ... a

[016-122]rebel army, five hundred strong, commanded by ... attacked this town,

[016-123]which I am charged to defend. With such speed as the gravity of the

[016-124]situation called for, I fortified my post in the town. The battle

[016-125]lasted two hours. Despite the superiority of the enemy in men and

[016-126]equipment, I was able to defeat and rout them. Their casualties were

[016-127]twenty killed and a far greater number of wounded, judging from the

[016-128]trails of blood they left behind them as they retreated. I am pleased

[016-129]to state there was no casualty on our side. I have the honor to

[016-130]congratulate Your Excellency upon this new triumph for the Federal

[016-131]arms. Viva Presidente Huerta! Viva Mexico!

[016-132]"Well," the young captain mused, "I'll be promoted to major." He

[016-133]clasped his hands together, jubilant. At this precise moment, a

[016-134]detonation rang out. His ears buzzed, he--
