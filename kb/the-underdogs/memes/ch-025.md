[025-001]Late in the afternoon Luis Cervantes rubbed his eyes and sat up. He had

[025-002]been sleeping on the hard pavement, close to the trunk of a fruit tree.

[025-003]Anastasio, Pancracio and Quail slept nearby, breathing heavily.

[025-004]His lips were swollen, his nose dry and cold. There were bloodstains on

[025-005]his hands and shirt. At once he recalled what had taken place. Soon he

[025-006]rose to his feet and made for one of the bedrooms. He pushed at the

[025-007]door several times without being able to force it open. For a few

[025-008]minutes he stood there, hesitating.

[025-009]No--he had not dreamed it. Everything had really occurred just as he

[025-010]recalled it. He had left the table with his bride and taken her to the

[025-011]bedroom, but just as he was closing the door, [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]] staggered after

[025-012]them and made one leap toward them. Then War Paint dashed in after

[025-013]Demetrio and began to struggle with him. Demetrio, his eyes white-hot,

[025-014]his lips covered with long blond hairs, looked for the bride, in

[025-015]despair. But War Paint pushed him back vigorously.

[025-016]"What the hell is the matter with you? What the hell are you trying to

[025-017]do?" he demanded, furious.

[025-018]War Paint put her leg between his, twisted it suddenly, and Demetrio

[025-019]fell to the ground outside of the bedroom. He rose, raging.

[025-020]"Help! Help! He's going to kill me!" she cried, seizing Demetrio's

[025-021]wrist and turning the gun aside. The bullet hit the floor. War Paint

[025-022]continued to shriek. Anastasio disarmed Demetrio from behind.

[025-023]Demetrio, standing like a furious bull in the middle of the arena, cast

[025-024]fierce glances at all the bystanders, Luis Cervantes, Anastasio,

[025-025]Manteca, and the others.

[025-026]"Goddamn you! You've taken my gun away! Christ! As if I needed any gun

[025-027]to beat the hell out of you."

[025-028]Flinging out his arms, beating and pummeling, he felled everyone within

[025-029]reach. Down they rolled like tenpins. Then, after that, Luis Cervantes

[025-030]could remember nothing more. Perhaps his bride, terrified by all these

[025-031]brutes, had wisely vanished and hidden herself.

[025-032]"Perhaps this bedroom communicates with the living room and I can go in

[025-033]through there," he thought, standing at the threshold. At the sound of

[025-034]his footsteps, War Paint woke up. She lay on the rug close to Demetrio

[025-035]at the foot of a couch filled with alfalfa and corn where the black

[025-036]horse had fed.

[025-037]"What are you looking for? Oh, hell, I know what you want! Shame on

[025-038]you! Why, I had to lock up your sweetheart because I couldn't struggle

[025-039]any more against this damned Demetrio. Take the key, it's lying on that

[025-040]table, there!"

[025-041]Luis Cervantes searched in vain all over the house.

[025-042]"Come on, tell me all about your girl."

[025-043]Nervously, Luis Cervantes continued to look for the key.

[025-044]"Come on, don't be in such a hurry, I'll give it to you. Come along,

[025-045]tell me; I like to hear about these things, you know. That girl is your

[025-046]kind, she's not a country person like us."

[025-047]"I've nothing to say. She's my girl and we're going to get married,

[025-048]that's all."

[025-049]"Ho! Ho! Ho! You're going to marry her, eh? Trying to teach your

[025-050]grandmother to suck eggs, eh? Why, you fool, any place you just manage

[025-051]to get to for the first time in your life, I've left a hundred miles

[025-052]behind me, see. I've cut my wisdom teeth. It was Meco and Manteca who

[025-053]took the girl from her home: I knew that all the time. You just gave

[025-054]them something so as to have her yourself, gave them a pair of cuff

[025-055]links ... or a miraculous picture of some Virgin.... Am I right? Sure,

[025-056]I am! There aren't so many people in the world who know what's what,

[025-057]but I reckon you'll meet up with a few before you die!"

[025-058]War Paint got up to give him the key but she could not find it either.

[025-059]She was much surprised. Quickly, she ran to the bedroom door and peered

[025-060]through the keyhole, standing motionless until her eye grew accustomed

[025-061]to the darkness within. Without drawing away, she said: "You damned

[025-062]Blondie. Son of a bitch! Come here a minute, look!"

[025-063]She went away laughing.

[025-064]"Didn't I tell them all I'd never seen a smarter fellow in all my life!"

[025-065]The following morning, War Paint watched for the moment when Blondie

[025-066]left the bedroom to feed his horses....

[025-067]"Come on, Angel Face. Run home quick!"

[025-068]The blue-eyed girl, with a face like a Madonna, stood naked save for

[025-069]her chemise and stockings. War Paint covered her with Manteca's lousy

[025-070]blanket, took her by the hand and led her to the street.

[025-071]"God, I'm happy," War Paint cried. "I'm crazy ... about Blondie ...

[025-072]now."
