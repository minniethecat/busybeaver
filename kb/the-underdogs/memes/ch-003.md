[003-001]In the brush and foliage of the sierra, [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]] Macias and his

[003-002]threescore men slept until the halloo of the horn, blown by Pancracio

[003-003]from the crest of a peak, awakened them.

[003-004]"Time, boys! Look around and see what's what!" Anastasio Montanez said,

[003-005]examining his rifle springs. Yet he was previous; an hour or more

[003-006]elapsed with no sound or stir save the song of the locust in the brush

[003-007]or the frog stirring in his mudhole. At last, when the ultimate faint

[003-008]rays of the moon were spent in the rosy dimness of the dawn, the

[003-009]silhouette of a soldier loomed at the end of the trail. As they

[003-010]strained their eyes, they could distinguish others behind him, ten,

[003-011]twenty, a hundred. ... Then, suddenly, darkness swallowed them up. Only

[003-012]when the sun rose, Demetrio's band realized that the canyon was alive

[003-013]with men, midgets seated on miniature horses.

[003-014]"Look at 'em, will you?" said Pancracio. "Pretty, ain't they? Come on,

[003-015]boys, let's go and roll marbles with 'em."

[003-016]Now the moving dwarf figures were lost in the dense chaparral, now they

[003-017]reappeared, stark and black against the ocher. The voices of officers,

[003-018]as they gave orders, and soldiers, marching at ease, were clearly

[003-019]audible. Demetrio raised his hand; the locks of rifles clicked. "Fire!"

[003-020]he cried tensely.

[003-021]Twenty-one men shot as one; twenty-one soldiers fell off their horses.

[003-022]Caught by surprise, the column halted, etched like bas-reliefs in stone

[003-023]against the rocks.

[003-024]Another volley and a score of soldiers hurtled down from rock to rock.

[003-025]"Come out, bandits. Come out, you starved dogs!"

[003-026]"To hell with you, you corn rustlers!"

[003-027]"Kill the cattle thieves! Kill 'em!"

[003-028]The soldiers shouted defiance to their enemies; the latter, giving

[003-029]proof of a marksmanship which had already made them famous, were

[003-030]content to keep under cover, quiet, mute.

[003-031]"Look, Pancracio," said Meco, completely black save for his eyes and

[003-032]teeth. "This is for that man who passes that tree. I'll get the son of

[003-033]a ..."

[003-034]"Take that! Right in the head. You saw it, didn't you, mate? Now, this

[003-035]is for the fellow on the roan horse. Down you come, you shave-headed

[003-036]bastard!"

[003-037]"I'll give that lad on the trail's edge a shower of lead. If you don't

[003-038]hit the river, I'm a liar! Now: look at him!"

[003-039]"Oh, come on, Anastasio don't be cruel; lend me your rifle. Come along,

[003-040]one shot, just one!"

[003-041]Manteca and Quail, unarmed, begged for a gun as a boon, imploring

[003-042]permission to fire at least a shot apiece. "Come out of your holes if

[003-043]you've got any guts!"

[003-044]"Show your faces, you lousy cowards!"

[003-045]From peak to peak, the shouts rang as distinctly as though uttered

[003-046]across a street. Suddenly, Quail stood up, naked, holding his trousers

[003-047]to windward as though he were a bullfighter flaunting a red cape, and

[003-048]the soldiers below the bull. A shower of shots peppered upon Demetrio's

[003-049]men.

[003-050]"God! That was like a hornet's nest buzzing overhead," said Anastasio

[003-051]Montanez, lying flat on the ground without daring to wink an eye.

[003-052]"Here, Quail, you son of a bitch, you stay where I told you," roared

[003-053]Demetrio.

[003-054]They crawled to take new positions. The soldiers, congratulating

[003-055]themselves on their successes, ceased firing when another volley roused

[003-056]them.

[003-057]"More coming!" they shouted.

[003-058]Some, panic-stricken, turned their horses back; others, abandoning

[003-059]their mounts, began to climb up the mountain and seek shelter behind

[003-060]the rocks. The officers had to shoot at them to enforce discipline.

[003-061]"Down there, down there!" said Demetrio as he leveled his rifle at the

[003-062]translucent thread of the river.

[003-063]A soldier fell into the water; at each shot, invariably a soldier bit

[003-064]the dust. Only Demetrio was shooting in that direction; for every

[003-065]soldier killed, ten or twenty of them, intact, climbed afresh on the

[003-066]other side.

[003-067]"Get those coming up from under! Los de Abajo! Get the underdogs!" he

[003-068]screamed.

[003-069]Now his fellows were exchanging rifles, laughing and making wagers on

[003-070]their marksmanship.

[003-071]"My leather belt if I miss that head there, on the black horse!"

[003-072]"Lend me your rifle, Meco."

[003-073]"Twenty Mauser cartridges and a half yard of sausage if you let me

[003-074]spill that lad riding the bay mare. All right! Watch me.... There! See

[003-075]him jump! Like a bloody deer."

[003-076]"Don't run, you half-breeds. Come along with you! Come and meet Father

[003-077]Demetrio!"

[003-078]Now it was Demetrio's men who screamed insults. Manteca, his smooth

[003-079]face swollen in exertion, yelled his lungs out. Pancracio roared, the

[003-080]veins and muscles in his neck dilated, his murderous eyes narrowed to

[003-081]two evil slits.

[003-082]Demetrio fired shot after shot, constantly warning his men of impending

[003-083]danger, but they took no heed until they felt the bullets spattering

[003-084]them from one side.

[003-085]"Goddamn their souls, they've branded me!" Demetrio cried, his teeth

[003-086]flashing.

[003-087]Then, very swiftly, he slid down a gully and was lost....
