[034-001]Someone plunged a knife  

[034-002]Deep in my side.  

[034-003]Did he know why?  

[034-004]I don't know why.  

[034-005]Maybe he knew,  

[034-006]I never knew.  

[034-007]The blood flowed out  

[034-008]Of that mortal wound.  

[034-009]Did he know why?  

[034-010]I don't know why.  

[034-011]Maybe he knew,  

[034-012]I never knew.

[034-013]His head lowered, his hands crossed over the pommel of his saddle,

[034-014][[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]] in melancholy accents sang the strains of the intriguing song.

[034-015]Then he fell silent; for quite a while he continued to feel oppressed

[034-016]and sad.

[034-017]"You'll see, as soon as we reach Lagos you'll come out of it, General.

[034-018]There's plenty of pretty girls to give us a good time," Blondie said.

[034-019]"Right now I feel like getting damn drunk," Demetrio answered, spurring

[034-020]his horse forward and leaving them as if he wished to abandon himself

[034-021]entirely to his sadness.

[034-022]After many hours of riding he called Cervantes.

[034-023]"Listen, Tenderfoot, why in hell do we have to go to Aguascalientes?"

[034-024]"You have to vote for the Provisional President of the Republic,

[034-025]General!"

[034-026]"President, what? Who in the devil, then, is this man Carranza? I'll be

[034-027]damned if I know what it's all about."

[034-028]At last they reached Lagos. Blondie bet that he would make Demetrio

[034-029]laugh that evening.

[034-030]Trailing his spurs noisily over the pavement, Demetrio entered "El

[034-031]Cosmopolita" with Luis Cervantes, Blondie, and his assistants.

[034-032]The civilians, surprised in their attempt to escape, remained where

[034-033]they were. Some feigned to return to their tables to continue drinking

[034-034]and talking; others hesitantly stepped up to present their respects to

[034-035]the commander.

[034-036]"General, so pleased! ... Major! Delighted to meet you!"

[034-037]"That's right! I love refined and educated friends," Blondie said.

[034-038]"Come on, boys," he added, jovially drawing his gun, "I'm going to play

[034-039]a tune that'll make you all dance."

[034-040]A bullet ricocheted on the cement floor passing between the legs of the

[034-041]tables, and the smartly dressed young men-about-town began to jump much

[034-042]as a woman jumps when frightened by a mouse under her skirt. Pale as

[034-043]ghosts, they conjured up wan smiles of obsequious approval. Demetrio

[034-044]barely parted his lips, but his followers doubled over with laughter.

[034-045]"Look, Blondie," Quail shouted, "look at that man going out there.

[034-046]Look, he's limping."

[034-047]"I guess the bee stung him all right."

[034-048]Blondie, without turning to look at the wounded man, announced with

[034-049]enthusiasm that he could shoot off the top of a tequila bottle at

[034-050]thirty paces without aiming.

[034-051]"Come on, friend, stand up," he said to the waiter. He dragged him out

[034-052]by the hand to the patio of the hotel and set a tequila bottle on his

[034-053]head. The poor devil refused. Insane with fright, he sought to escape,

[034-054]but Blondie pulled his gun and took aim.

[034-055]"Come on, you son of a sea cook! If you keep on I'll give you a nice

[034-056]warm one!"

[034-057]Blondie went to the opposite wall, raised his gun and fired. The bottle

[034-058]broke into bits, the alcohol poured over the lad's ghastly face.

[034-059]"Now it's a go," cried Blondie, running to the bar to get another

[034-060]bottle, which he placed on the lad's head.

[034-061]He returned to his former position, he whirled about, and shot without

[034-062]aiming. But he hit the waiter's ear instead of the bottle. Holding his

[034-063]sides with laughter, he said to the young waiter:

[034-064]"Here, kid, take these bills. It ain't much. But you'll be all right

[034-065]with some alcohol and arnica."

[034-066]After drinking a great deal of alcohol and beer, Demetrio spoke:

[034-067]"Pay the bill, Blondie, I'm going to leave you."

[034-068]"I ain't got a penny, General, but that's all right. I'll fix it. How

[034-069]much do we owe you, friend?"

[034-070]"One hundred and eighty pesos, Chief," the bartender answered amiably.

[034-071]Quickly, Blondie jumped behind the bar and with a sweep of both arms,

[034-072]knocked down all the glasses and bottles.

[034-073]"Send the bill to General Villa, understand?"

[034-074]He left, laughing loudly at his prank.

[034-075]"Say there, you, where do the girls hang out?" Blondie asked, reeling

[034-076]up drunkenly toward a small well-dressed man, standing at the door of a

[034-077]tailor shop.

[034-078]The man stepped down to the sidewalk politely to let Blondie pass.

[034-079]Blondie stopped and looked at him curiously, impertinently.

[034-080]"Little boy, you're very small and dainty, ain't you? ... No? ... Then

[034-081]I'm a liar! ... That's right! ... You know the puppet dance.... You

[034-082]don't? The hell you don't! ... I met you in a circus! I know you can

[034-083]even dance on a tightrope! ... You watch!"

[034-084]Blondie drew his gun out and began to shoot, aiming at the tailor's

[034-085]feet; the tailor gave a little jump at every pull of the trigger.

[034-086]"See! You do know how to dance on the tightrope, don't you?"

[034-087]Taking his friends by the arm, he ordered them to lead him to the

[034-088]red-light district, punctuating every step by a shot which smashed a

[034-089]street light, or struck some wall, a door, or a distant house.

[034-090]Demetrio left him and returned to the hotel, singing to himself:

[034-091]"Someone plunged a knife  

[034-092]Deep in my side.  

[034-093]Did he know why?  

[034-094]I don't know why.  

[034-095]Maybe he knew,  

[034-096]I never knew."
