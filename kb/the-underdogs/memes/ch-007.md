[007-001]Still drowsy, [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]] ran his hand through his ruffled hair, which

[007-002]hung over his moist forehead, pushed it back over his ears, and opened

[007-003]his eyes.

[007-004]Distinctly he heard the woman's melodious voice which he had already

[007-005]sensed in his dream. He walked toward the door.

[007-006]It was broad daylight; the rays of sunlight filtered through the thatch

[007-007]of the hut.

[007-008]The girl who had offered him water the day before, the girl of whom he

[007-009]had dreamed all night long, now came forward, kindly and eager as ever.

[007-010]This time she carried a pitcher of milk brimming over with foam.

[007-011]"It's goat's milk, but fine just the same. Come on now: taste it."

[007-012]Demetrio smiled gratefully, straightened up, grasped the clay pitcher,

[007-013]and proceeded to drink the milk in little gulps, without removing his

[007-014]eyes from the girl. She grew self-conscious, lowered her eyes.

[007-015]"What's your name?" he asked.

[007-016]"Camilla."

[007-017]"Ah, there's a lovely name! And the girl that bears it, lovelier still!"

[007-018]Camilla blushed. As he sought to seize her wrist, she grew frightened,

[007-019]and Picking up the empty pitcher, flew out the door.

[007-020]"No, Demetrio," Anastasio Montanez commented gravely, "you've got to

[007-021]break them in first. Hmm! It's a hell of a lot of scars the women have

[007-022]left on my body. Yes, my friend, I've a heap of experience along that

[007-023]line."

[007-024]"I feel all right now, Compadre." Demetrio pretended he had not heard

[007-025]him. "I had fever, and I sweated like a horse all night, but I feel

[007-026]quite fresh today. The thing that's irking me hellishly is that Goddamn

[007-027]wound. Can Venancio to look after me."

[007-028]"What are we going to do with the tenderfoot we caught last night?"

[007-029]Pancracio asked.

[007-030]"That's right: I was forgetting all about him."

[007-031]As usual, Demetrio hesitated a while before he reached a decision.

[007-032]"Here, Quail, come here. Listen: you go and find out where's the

[007-033]nearest church around here. I know there's one about six miles away. Go

[007-034]and steal a priest's robe and bring it back."

[007-035]"What's the idea?" asked Pancracio in surprise.

[007-036]"Well, I'll soon find out if this tenderfoot came here to murder me.

[007-037]I'll tell him he's to be shot, see, and Quail will put on the priest's

[007-038]robes, say that he's a priest and hear his confession. If he's got

[007-039]anything up his sleeve, he'll come out with it, and then I'll shoot

[007-040]him. Otherwise I'll let him go."

[007-041]"God, there's a roundabout way to tackle the question. If I were you,

[007-042]I'd just shoot him and let it go at that," said Pancracio

[007-043]contemptuously.

[007-044]That night Quail returned with the priest's robes; Demetrio ordered the

[007-045]prisoner to be led in. Luis Cervantes had not eaten or slept for two

[007-046]days, there were deep black circles under his eyes; his face was

[007-047]deathly pale, his lips dry and colorless. He spoke awkwardly, slowly:

[007-048]"You can do as you please with me.... I am convinced I was wrong to

[007-049]come looking for you."

[007-050]There was a prolonged silence. Then:

[007-051]"I thought that you would welcome a man who comes to offer his help,

[007-052]with open arms, even though his help was quite worthless. After all,

[007-053]you might perhaps have found some use for it. What, in heaven's name,

[007-054]do I stand to gain, whether the [[mexican-revolution|revolution]] wins or loses?"

[007-055]Little by little he grew more animated; at times the languor in his

[007-056]eyes disappeared.

[007-057]"The revolution benefits the poor, the ignorant, all those who have

[007-058]been slaves all their lives, all the unhappy people who do not even

[007-059]suspect they are poor because the rich who stand above them, the rich

[007-060]who rule them, change their sweat and blood and tears into gold..."

[007-061]"Well, what the hell is the gist of all this palaver? I'll be damned if

[007-062]I can stomach a sermon," Pancracio broke in.

[007-063]"I wanted to fight for the sacred cause of the oppressed, but you don't

[007-064]understand ... you cast me aside.... Very well, then, you can do as you

[007-065]please with me!"

[007-066]"All I'm going to do now is to put this rope around your neck. Look

[007-067]what a pretty white neck you've got."

[007-068]"Yes, I know what brought you here," Demetrio interrupted dryly,

[007-069]scratching his head. "I'm going to have you shot!"

[007-070]Then, looking at Anastasio he said:

[007-071]"Take him away. And ... if he wants to confess, bring the priest to

[007-072]him."

[007-073]Impassive as ever, Anastasio took the prisoner gently by the arm.

[007-074]"Come along this way, Tenderfoot."

[007-075]They all laughed uproariously, when a few minutes later, Quail appeared

[007-076]in priestly robes.

[007-077]"By God, this tenderfoot certainly talks his head off," Quail said.

[007-078]"You know, I've a notion he was having a bit of a laugh on me when I

[007-079]started asking him questions."

[007-080]"But didn't he have anything to say?"

[007-081]"Nothing, save what he said last night."

[007-082]"I've a hunch he didn't come here to shoot you at all, Compadre," said

[007-083]Anastasio.

[007-084]"Give him something to eat and guard him."
