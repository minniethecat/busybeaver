## Overview

General Panfilo Natera was a [[mexican-revolution|revolution]]ary commander operating in the region between [[Juchipila]] and Fresnillo during the [[Mexican Revolution]]. He appears in _The Underdogs_ as an allied general whose forces join with [[Demetrio Macías]]'s band for a coordinated advance against the [[Federal Army]]. Natera represents the regional *cacique* (strongman) type — a local warlord whose authority rests on personal loyalty and armed force rather than ideology (015-098).

## Biography

### Assembling Forces at Juchipila

Natera first enters the novel when he is "assembling his men at [[juchipila|Juchipila]]" (012-069). [[Demetrio Macías]], urged by [[Luis Cervantes]], decides to bring his band to join Natera's forces. Cervantes argues the strategic value: "Natera's men are at [[juchipila|Juchipila]], quite a good place" (013-044). The decision to join Natera marks [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]]'s transition from local rebel to participant in the larger [[mexican-revolution|revolution]]ary coalition.

### The Advance Against the Federal Army

The combined forces under Natera's command advance against [[federal-army|Federal Army]] positions. The narrative notes that "on the day General Natera began his advance against the town" (018-001), [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]] and his men played a crucial role. Natera's handshake with [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]] and his toast — "With men like General Natera and Colonel [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]] [[demetrio-macias|Macias]]" (018-006, 018-007) — signals mutual respect between the regional commander and the guerrilla leader.

### The Fresnillo Stronghold

Natera's power base was substantial: "in the Potosí region, Natera ruled over Fresnillo" (015-098). This establishes him as a regional *cacique* whose authority predates and parallels the formal [[mexican-revolution|revolution]]ary command structure. His control over Fresnillo — a significant mining town — gave him economic as well as military power.

### Personality and Leadership

Natera's leadership style is characterized by a "cruel gaze" — when he "fixed his cruel gaze on the officer" (018-017), the narrator reveals the harshness beneath the ceremonial pleasantries. His men are loyal but more subdued than Villa's legendary northerners. When Natera's followers hear the tales of Villa's exploits, they listen with a mixture of admiration and competitive pride (020-033).

## Natera's Men

Natera's soldiers form a distinctive group within the novel. Unlike [[Pancho Villa]]'s legendary northerners, Natera's men are regional fighters, tied to the [[juchipila|Juchipila]]–Fresnillo corridor. They listen with "gaping wonder" at the stories of Villa's exploits, their own identity shaped by comparison with the more famous Division of the North (020-033). The officer corps includes educated men like [[alberto-solis|Alberto Solís]], Natera's chief of staff, who engages in philosophical conversations with [[Luis Cervantes]] (020-007).
