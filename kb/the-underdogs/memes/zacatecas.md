# Zacatecas

## Overview

**Zacatecas** is a city in north-central Mexico, capital of the state of Zacatecas. In *The Underdogs*, it represents a strategic military objective and a symbolic turning point in the [[mexican-revolution|revolution]]ary campaign. The city sits amid hills that made it a natural fortress and the site of one of the decisive battles of the [[mexican-revolution|Mexican Revolution]] in June 1914.

## Location & Geography

Zacatecas lies in a deep valley surrounded by hills in the Sierra Madre Occidental range. The city's topography — "the hills in Zacatecas" `(012-064)` — made it both a mining center (rich in silver) and a formidable military position. Its elevation and surrounding terrain shaped the [[battle-of-zacatecas|Battle of Zacatecas]], in which [[pancho-villa|Pancho Villa]]'s [[villistas|División del Norte]] defeated the [[federal-army|Federal Army]] forces defending the city.

## Role in the Narrative

### Strategic Importance

In the novel, Zacatecas is discussed as a strategic prize. [[luis-cervantes|Luis Cervantes]] explains to [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]]'s men that capturing it will determine the war's outcome: "The [[battle-of-zacatecas|fall of Zacatecas]] will be [[victoriano-huerta|Huerta]]'s requiescat in pace" `(012-071)`. The city's capture by [[villistas|Villa's forces]] in June 1914 effectively ended the [[victoriano-huerta|Huerta]] regime.

### Personal Stakes

The threat posed by the [[mexican-revolution|revolution]]ary forces at Zacatecas has personal consequences for [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]]. A Federal commander "goes in person to Zacatecas to get an army to capture me" `(013-034)`, illustrating how the strategic and personal dimensions of the war are interwoven.
