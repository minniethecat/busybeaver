[017-001]"If we get through the corral, we can make the alley, eh?" [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]]

[017-002]asked.

[017-003]"That's right," the workman answered. "Beyond the corral there's a

[017-004]house, then another corral, then there's a store."

[017-005]Demetrio scratched his head, thoughtfully. This time his decision was

[017-006]immediate.

[017-007]"Can you get hold of a crowbar or something like that to make a hole

[017-008]through the wall?"

[017-009]"Yes, we'll get anything you want, but ..."

[017-010]"But what? Where can we get a crowbar?"

[017-011]"Everything is right there. But it all belongs to the boss."

[017-012]Without further ado, Demetrio strode into the shed which had been

[017-013]pointed out as the toolhouse.

[017-014]It was all a matter of a few minutes. Once in the alley, hugging to the

[017-015]walls, they marched forward in single file until they reached the rear

[017-016]of the church. Now they had but a single fence and the rear wall of the

[017-017]chapel to scale.

[017-018]"God's will be done!" Demetrio said to himself. He was the first to

[017-019]clamber over.

[017-020]Like monkeys the others followed him, reaching the other side with

[017-021]bleeding, grimy hands. The rest was easy. The deep worn steps along the

[017-022]stonework made their ascent of the chapel wall swifter. The church

[017-023]vault hid them from the soldiers.

[017-024]"Wait a moment, will you?" said the workman. "I'll go and see where my

[017-025]brother is; I'll let you know and then you'll get at the officers."

[017-026]But no one paid the slightest attention to him.

[017-027]For a second, Demetrio glanced at the soldiers' black coats hanging on

[017-028]the wall, then at his own men, thick on the church tower behind the

[017-029]iron rail. He smiled with satisfaction and turning to his men said:

[017-030]"Come on, now, boys!"

[017-031]Twenty bombs exploded simultaneously in the midst of the soldiers who,

[017-032]awaking terrified out of their sleep, started up, their eyes wide open.

[017-033]But before they had realized their plight, twenty more bombs burst like

[017-034]thunder upon them leaving a scattering of men killed or maimed.

[017-035]"Don't do that yet, for God's sake! Don't do it till I find my

[017-036]brother," the workman implored in anguish.

[017-037]In vain an old sergeant harangued the soldiers, insulting them in the

[017-038]hope of rallying them. For they were rats, caught in a trap, no more,

[017-039]no less. Some of the soldiers, attempting to reach the small door by

[017-040]the staircase, fell to the ground pierced by Demetrio's shots. Others

[017-041]fell at the feet of these twenty-odd specters, with faces and breasts

[017-042]dark as iron, clad in long torn trousers of white cloth which fell to

[017-043]their leather sandals, scattering death and destruction below them. In

[017-044]the belfry, a few men struggled to emerge from the pile of dead who had

[017-045]fallen upon them.

[017-046]"It's awful, Chief!" Luis Cervantes cried in alarm. "We've no more

[017-047]bombs left and we left our guns in the corral."

[017-048]Smiling, Demetrio drew out a large shining knife. In the twinkling of

[017-049]an eye, steel flashed in every hand. Some knives were large and

[017-050]pointed, others wide as the palm of a hand, others heavy as bayonets.

[017-051]"The spy!" Luis Cervantes cried triumphantly. "Didn't I tell you?"

[017-052]"Don't kill me, Chief, please don't kill me," the old sergeant implored

[017-053]squirming at the feet of Demetrio, who stood over him, knife in hand.

[017-054]The victim raised his wrinkled Indian face; there was not a single gray

[017-055]hair in his head today. Demetrio recognized the spy who had lied to him

[017-056]the day before. Terrified, Luis Cervantes quickly averted his face. The

[017-057]steel blade went crack, crack, on the old man's ribs. He toppled

[017-058]backward, his arms spread, his eyes ghastly.

[017-059]"Don't kill my brother, don't kill him, he's my brother!" the workman

[017-060]shouted in terror to Pancracio who was pursuing a soldier. But it was

[017-061]too late. With one thrust, Pancracio had cut his neck in half, and two

[017-062]streams of scarlet spurted from the wound.

[017-063]"Kill the soldiers, kill them all!"

[017-064]Pancracio and Manteca surpassed the others in the savagery of their

[017-065]slaughter, and finished up with the wounded. Montanez, exhausted, let

[017-066]his arm fall; it hung limp to his side. A gentle expression still

[017-067]filled his glance; his eyes shone; he was naive as a child, unmoral as

[017-068]a hyena.

[017-069]"Here's one who's not dead yet," Quail shouted.

[017-070]Pancracio ran up. The little blond captain with curled mustache turned

[017-071]pale as wax. He stood against the door to the staircase unable to

[017-072]muster enough strength to take another step.

[017-073]Pancracio pushed him brutally to the edge of the corridor. A jab with

[017-074]his knee against the captain's thigh--then a sound not unlike a bag of

[017-075]stones falling from the top of the steeple on the porch of the church.

[017-076]"My God, you've got no brains!" said Quail. "If I'd known what you were

[017-077]doing, I'd have kept him for myself. That was a fine pair of shoes you

[017-078]lost!"

[017-079]Bending over them, the rebels stripped those among the soldiers who

[017-080]were best clad, laughing and joking as they despoiled them. Brushing

[017-081]back his long hair, that had fallen over his sweating forehead and

[017-082]covered his eyes, Demetrio said:

[017-083]"Now let's get those city fellows!"
