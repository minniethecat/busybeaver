[042-001]It was a heavenly morning. It had rained all night, the sky awakened

[042-002]covered with white clouds. Young wild colts trotted on the summit of

[042-003]the sierra, with tense manes and waving hair, proud as the peaks

[042-004]lifting their heads to the clouds.

[042-005]The soldiers stepped among the huge rocks, buoyed up by the happiness

[042-006]of the morning. None for a moment dreamed of the treacherous bullet

[042-007]that might be awaiting him ahead; the unforeseen provides man with his

[042-008]greatest joy. The soldiers sang, laughed, and chattered away. The

[042-009]spirit of nomadic tribes stirred their souls. What matters it whether

[042-010]you go and whence you come? All that matters is to walk, to walk

[042-011]endlessly, without ever stopping; to possess the valley, the heights of

[042-012]the sierra, far as the eye can read.

[042-013]Trees, brush, and cactus shone fresh after rain. Heavy drops of limpid

[042-014]water fell from rocks, ocher in hue as rusty armor.

[042-015][[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]] Macias' men grew silent for a moment. They believed they heard

[042-016]the familiar rumor of firing in the distance. A few minutes elapsed but

[042-017]the sound was not repeated.

[042-018]"In this same sierra," Demetrio said, "with but twenty men I killed

[042-019]five hundred Federals. Remember, Anastasio?"

[042-020]As Demetrio began to tell that famous exploit, the men realized the

[042-021]danger they were facing. What if the enemy, instead of being two days

[042-022]away, was hiding somewhere among the underbrush on the terrible hill

[042-023]through whose gorge they now advanced? None dared show the slightest

[042-024]fear. Not one of Demetrio Macias' men dared say, "I shall not move

[042-025]another inch!"

[042-026]So, when firing began in the distance where the vanguard was marching,

[042-027]no one felt surprised. The recruits turned back hurriedly, retreating

[042-028]in shameful flight, searching for a way out of the canyon.

[042-029]A curse broke from Demetrio's parched lips.

[042-030]"Fire at 'em. Shoot any man who runs away!"

[042-031]"Storm the hill!" he thundered like a wild beast.

[042-032]But the enemy, lying in ambush by the thousand, opened up its

[042-033]machine-gun fire. Demetrio's men fell like wheat under the sickle.

[042-034]Tears of rage and pain rise to Demetrio's eyes as Anastasio slowly

[042-035]slides from his horse without a sound, and lies outstretched,

[042-036]motionless. Venancio falls close beside him, his chest riddled with

[042-037]bullets. Meco hurtles over the precipice, bounding from rock to rock.

[042-038]Suddenly, Demetrio finds himself alone. Bullets whiz past his ears like

[042-039]hail. He dismounts and crawls over the rocks, until he finds a parapet:

[042-040]he lays down a stone to protect his head and, lying flat on the ground,

[042-041]begins to shoot.

[042-042]The enemy scatter in all directions, pursuing the few fugitives hiding

[042-043]in the brush. Demetrio aims; he does not waste a single shot.

[042-044]His famous marksmanship fills him with joy. Where he settles his

[042-045]glance, he settles a bullet. He loads his gun once more ... takes

[042-046]aim....

[042-047]The smoke of the guns hangs thick in the air. Locusts chant their

[042-048]mysterious, imperturbable song. Doves coo lyrically in the crannies of

[042-049]the rocks. The cows graze placidly.

[042-050]The sierra is clad in gala colors. Over its inaccessible peaks the

[042-051]opalescent fog settles like a snowy veil on the forehead of a bride.

[042-052]At the foot of a hollow, sumptuous and huge as the portico of an old

[042-053]cathedral, Demetrio Macias, his eyes leveled in an eternal glance,

[042-054]continues to point the barrel of his gun.
