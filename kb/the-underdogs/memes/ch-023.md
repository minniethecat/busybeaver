[023-001]"What damned fools," said War Paint convulsed with laughter! "Where the

[023-002]hell do you come from?..... Soldiers don't sleep in hotels and inns

[023-003]any more....... Where do you come from? You just go anywhere you like

[023-004]and pick a house that pleases you, see. When you go there, make

[023-005]yourself at home and don't ask anyone for anything. What the hell is

[023-006]the use of the [[mexican-revolution|revolution]]? Who's it for? For the folks who live in

[023-007]towns? We're the city folk now, see? Come on, Pancracio, hand me your

[023-008]bayonet. Damn these rich people, they lock up everything they've got!"

[023-009]She dug the steel point through the crack of a drawer and, pressing on

[023-010]the hilt, broke the lock, opened the splinted cover of a writing desk.

[023-011]Anastasio, Pancracio and War Paint plunged their hands into a mass of

[023-012]post cards, photographs, pictures and papers, scattering them all over

[023-013]the rug. Finding nothing he wanted, Pancracio gave vent to his anger by

[023-014]kicking a framed photograph into the air with the toe of his shoe. It

[023-015]smashed on the candelabra in the center of the room.

[023-016]They pulled their empty hands out of the heap of paper, cursing. But

[023-017]War Paint was of sterner stuff; tirelessly she continued to unlock

[023-018]drawer after drawer without failing to investigate a single spot. In

[023-019]their absorption, they did not notice a small gray velvet-covered box

[023-020]which rolled silently across the floor, coming to a stop at Luis

[023-021]Cervantes' feet.

[023-022][[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]], lying on the rug, seemed to be asleep; Cervantes, who had

[023-023]watched everything with profound indifference, pulled the box closer to

[023-024]him with his foot, and stooping to scratch his ankle, swiftly picked it

[023-025]up. Something gleamed up at him, dazzling. It was two pure-water

[023-026]diamonds mounted in filigreed platinum. Hastily he thrust them inside

[023-027]his coat pocket.

[023-028]When Demetrio awoke, Cervantes said:

[023-029]"General, look at the mess these boys have made here. Don't you think

[023-030]it would be advisable to forbid this sort of thing?"

[023-031]"No. It's about their only pleasure after putting their bellies up as

[023-032]targets for the enemy's bullets."

[023-033]"Yes, of course, General, but they could do it somewhere else. You see,

[023-034]this sort of thing hurts our prestige, and worse, our cause!"

[023-035]Demetrio leveled his eagle eyes at Cervantes. He drummed with his

[023-036]fingernails against his teeth, absent-mindedly. Then:

[023-037]"Come along, now, don't blush," he said. "You can talk like that to

[023-038]someone else. We know what's mine is mine, what's yours is yours. You

[023-039]picked the box, all right; I picked my gold watch; all right too!"

[023-040]His words dispelled any pretense. Both of them, in perfect harmony,

[023-041]displayed their booty.

[023-042]War Paint and her companions were ransacking the rest of the house.

[023-043]Quail entered the room with a twelve-year-old girl upon whose forehead

[023-044]and arms were already marked copper-colored spots. They stopped short,

[023-045]speechless with surprise as they saw the books lying in piles on the

[023-046]floor, chairs and tables, the large mirrors thrown to the ground,

[023-047]smashed, the huge albums and the photographs torn into shreds, the

[023-048]furniture, objets d'art and bric-a-brac broken. Quail held his breath,

[023-049]his avid eyes scouring the room for booty.

[023-050]Outside, in one corner of the patio, lost in dense clouds of

[023-051]suffocating smoke, Manteca was boiling corn on the cob, feeding his

[023-052]fire with books and paper that made the flames leap wildly through the

[023-053]air.

[023-054]"Hey!" Quail shouted. "Look what I found. A fine sweat-cover for my

[023-055]mare."

[023-056]With a swift pull he wrenched down a hanging, which fell over a

[023-057]handsomely carved upright chair.

[023-058]"Look, look at all these naked women!" Quail's little companion cried,

[023-059]enchanted at a de luxe edition of Dante's Divine Comedy. "I like this;

[023-060]I think I'll take it along."

[023-061]She began to tear out the illustrations which pleased her most.

[023-062]Demetrio crossed the room and sat down beside Luis Cervantes. He

[023-063]ordered some beer, handed one bottle up to his secretary, downed his

[023-064]own bottle at one gulp. Then, drowsily, he half closed his eyes, and

[023-065]soon fell sound asleep.

[023-066]"Hey!" a man called to Pancracio from the threshold. "When can I see

[023-067]your general?"

[023-068]"You can't see him. He's got a hangover this morning. What the hell do

[023-069]you want?"

[023-070]"I want to buy some of those books you're burning."

[023-071]"I'll sell them to you myself."

[023-072]"How much do you want for them?"

[023-073]Pancracio frowned in bewilderment.

[023-074]"Give me a nickel for those with pictures, see. I'll give you the rest

[023-075]for nothing if you buy all those with pictures."

[023-076]The man returned with a large basket to carry away the books....

[023-077]"Come on, Demetrio, come on, you pig, get up! Look who's here! It's

[023-078]Blondie. You don't know what a fine man he is!"

[023-079]"I like you very much, General Macias, and I like the way you do

[023-080]things. So if it's all right, I'd like very much to serve under you!"

[023-081]"What's your rank?" Demetrio asked him.

[023-082]"I'm a captain, General."

[023-083]"All right, you can serve with me now. I'll make you major. How's that?"

[023-084]Blondie was a round little fellow, with waxed mustache. When he

[023-085]laughed, his blue eyes disappeared mischievously between his forehead

[023-086]and his fat cheeks. He had been a waiter at "El Monico," in Chihuahua;

[023-087]now he proudly wore three small brass bars, the insignia of his rank in

[023-088]the Northern Division.

[023-089]Blondie showered eulogy after eulogy on Demetrio and his men; this

[023-090]proved sufficient reason for bringing out a fresh case of beer, which

[023-091]was finished in short order.

[023-092]Suddenly War Paint reappeared in the middle of the room, wearing a

[023-093]beautiful silk dress covered with exquisite lace.

[023-094]"You forgot the stockings," Blondie shouted, shaking with laughter.

[023-095]Quail's girl also burst out laughing. But War Paint did not care. She

[023-096]shrugged her shoulders indifferently, sat down on the floor, kicked off

[023-097]her white satin slippers, and wiggled her toes happily, giving their

[023-098]muscles a freedom welcome after their tight confinement in the

[023-099]slippers. She said:

[023-100]"Hey, you, Pancracio, go and get me my blue stockings ... they're with

[023-101]the rest of my plunder."

[023-102]Soldiers and their friends, companions and veterans of other campaigns,

[023-103]began to enter in groups of twos and threes. Demetrio, growing excited,

[023-104]began to narrate in detail his most notable feats of arms.

[023-105]"What the hell is that noise?" he asked in surprise as he heard string

[023-106]and brass instruments tuning up in the patio.

[023-107]"General Demetrio Macias," Luis Cervantes said solemnly, "it's a

[023-108]banquet all of your old friends and followers are giving in your honor

[023-109]to celebrate your victory at [[zacatecas|Zacatecas]] and your well-merited promotion

[023-110]to the rank of general!"
