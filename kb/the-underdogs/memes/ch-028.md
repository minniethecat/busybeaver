[028-001]"I don't know any more about it than you do. The General told me,

[028-002]'Quail, saddle your horse and my black mare and follow Cervantes; he's

[028-003]going on an errand for me.' Well, that's what happened. We left here at

[028-004]noon, and reached the ranch early that evening. One-eyed Maria Antonia

[028-005]took us in.... She asked after you, Pancracio. Next morning Luis

[028-006]Cervantes wakes me up. 'Quail, Quail, saddle the horses. Leave me mine

[028-007]but take the General's mare back to Moyahua. I'll catch up after a

[028-008]bit.' The sun was high when he arrived with Camilla. She got off and we

[028-009]stuck her on the General's mare."

[028-010]"Well, and her? What sort of a face did she make coming back?" one of

[028-011]the men inquired.

[028-012]"Hum! She was so damned happy she was gabbing all the way."

[028-013]"And the tenderfoot?"

[028-014]"Just as quiet as he always is, you know him."

[028-015]"I think," Venancio expressed his opinion with great seriousness, "that

[028-016]if Camilla woke up in the General's bed, it was just a mistake. We

[028-017]drank a lot, remember! That alcohol went to our heads; we must have

[028-018]lost our senses."

[028-019]"What the hell do you mean: alcohol! It was all cooked up between

[028-020]Cervantes and the General."

[028-021]"Certainly! That city dude's nothing but a ..."

[028-022]"I don't like to talk about friends behind their backs," said Blondie,

[028-023]"but I can tell you this: one of the two sweethearts he had, one was

[028-024]mine, and the other was for the General."

[028-025]They burst into guffaws of laughter.

[028-026]When War Paint realized what had happened, she sought out Camilla and

[028-027]spoke with great affection:

[028-028]"Poor little child! Tell me how all this happened."

[028-029]Camilla's eyes were red from weeping.

[028-030]"He lied to me! He lied! He came to the ranch and he told me, 'Camilla,

[028-031]I came just to get you. Do you want to go away with me?' You can be

[028-032]sure I wanted to go with him; when it comes to loving, I adore him.

[028-033]Yes, I adore him. Look how thin I've grown just pining away for him.

[028-034]Mornings I used to loathe to grind corn, Mamma would call me to eat,

[028-035]and anything I put in my mouth had no taste at all."

[028-036]Once more she burst into tears, stuffing the corner of her apron into

[028-037]her mouth to drown her sobs.

[028-038]"Look here, I'll help you out of this mess. Don't be silly, child,

[028-039]don't cry. Don't think about the dude any more! Honest to God, he's not

[028-040]worth it. You surely know his game, dear? ... That's the only reason

[028-041]why the General stands for him. What a goose! ... All right, you want

[028-042]to go back home?"

[028-043]"The Holy Virgin protect me. My mother would beat me to death!"

[028-044]"She'll do nothing of the sort. You and I can fix things. Listen! The

[028-045]soldiers are leaving any moment now. When [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]] tells you to get

[028-046]ready, you tell him you feel pains all over your body as though someone

[028-047]had hit you; then you lie down and start yawning and shivering. Then

[028-048]put your hand on your forehead and say, 'I'm burning up with fever.'

[028-049]I'll tell Demetrio to leave us both here, that I'll stay to take care

[028-050]of you, that as soon as you're feeling all right again, we'll catch up

[028-051]with them. But instead of that, I'll see that you get home safe and

[028-052]sound."
