## Overview

Cuquío is a town in [[jalisco|Jalisco]] that appears as a waypoint on the revolutionary band's route through the highlands and, later, as a site of strategic maneuvering in the conflict's closing phase. It lies between [[tepatitlan|Tepatitlán]] and the sierra (032-006–007), making it a natural stop on the northward push. In Cuquío, Demetrio receives a message that forces the band to reverse course (033-035–036), marking it as a turning point in the campaign's geography.

## Location & Geography

Cuquío is situated in the Jalisco highlands, reached after Tepatitlán when traveling north toward the sierra (032-006). It lies within a day's ride from Tepatitlán — "By noon we'll reach Tepatitlan, Cuquio tomorrow" (032-006) — placing it in the network of small towns that dot the route between [[guadalajara|Guadalajara]] and [[zacatecas|Zacatecas]].

## Role in the Narrative

The band approaches Cuquío as part of their northward advance, with Demetrio declaring the route: Tepatitlán today, Cuquío tomorrow, "and then ... on to the sierra" (032-006–007). The sequence of towns maps the revolutionary band's movement through the Jalisco countryside like stepping stones.

As they near Cuquío, Anastasio Montañés rides up to Demetrio with news of Blondie's latest exploit — a comic interlude typical of the band's camaraderie on the march (033-001–005). But the mood shifts quickly: "In Cuquio Demetrio received a message" (033-035). The message compels them to turn back to Tepatitlán, abruptly reversing the band's trajectory (033-036).

The town resurfaces in the novel's final chapters, when a character is ordered to "stop a band of men coming through Cuquio" in the fight against the Carranzistas (039-060–061). This late mention places Cuquío in the confused geography of the revolutionary civil war, where erstwhile allies have become enemies and every town is a potential ambush point.

## Key Passages

> "By noon we'll reach Tepatitlan, Cuquio tomorrow, and then ... on to the sierra!" [032-006–007]

*Demetrio maps the route northward, with Cuquío as the second day's objective — a waypoint in the revolutionary band's advance through Jalisco.*

> They had almost reached Cuquio, when Anastasio Montanez rode up to Demetrio: "Listen, Compadre, I almost forgot to tell you.... You ought to have seen the wonderful joke that man Blondie played." [033-001–004]

*The approach to Cuquío is punctuated by the band's characteristic dark humor — Anastasio's story of Blondie's "joke" with a farmer's corn complaint is typical of the casual violence that accompanies their passage through rural towns.*

> In Cuquio Demetrio received a message. "We've got to go back to Tepatitlan, General," said Luis Cervantes. [033-035–036]

*A single message received at Cuquío reverses the band's direction — the town marks the pivot point where the northward campaign meets an unforeseen obstacle.*
