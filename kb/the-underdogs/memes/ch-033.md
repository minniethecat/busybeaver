[033-001]They had almost reached Cuquio, when Anastasio Montanez rode up to

[033-002][[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]]: "Listen, Compadre, I almost forgot to tell you.... You ought

[033-003]to have seen the wonderful joke that man Blondie played. You know what

[033-004]he did with the old man who came to complain about the corn we'd taken

[033-005]away for horses? Well, the old man took the paper and went to the

[033-006]barracks. 'Right you are, brother, come in,' said Blondie, 'come in,

[033-007]come in here; to give you back what's yours is only the right thing to

[033-008]do. How many bushels did we steal? Ten? Sure it wasn't more than ten?

[033-009]... That's right, about fifteen, eh? Or was it twenty, perhaps? ... Try

[033-010]and remember, friend.... Of course you're a poor man, aren't you, and

[033-011]you've a lot of kids to raise.... Yes, twenty it was. All right, now!

[033-012]It's not ten or fifteen or twenty I'm going to give you. You're going

[033-013]to count for yourself.... One, two, three ... and when you've had

[033-014]enough you just tell me and I'll stop.' And Blondie pulled out his

[033-015]sword and beat him till he cried for mercy."

[033-016]War Paint rocked in her saddle, convulsed with mirth. Camilla, unable

[033-017]to control herself, blurted out:

[033-018]"The beast! His heart's rotten to the core! No wonder I loathe him!"

[033-019]At once War Paint's expression changed.

[033-020]"What the hell is it to you!" she scowled. Camilla, frightened, spurred

[033-021]her horse forward. War Paint did likewise and, as she trotted past

[033-022]Camilla, suddenly she reached out, seized the other's hair and pulled

[033-023]with all her might. Camilla's horse shied; Camilla, trying to brush her

[033-024]hair back from over her eyes, abandoned the reins. She hesitated, lost

[033-025]her balance and fell in the road, striking her forehead against the

[033-026]stones.

[033-027]War Paint, weeping with laughter, pressed on with utmost skill and

[033-028]caught Camilla's horse.

[033-029]"Come on, Tenderfoot; here's a job for you," Pancracio said as he saw

[033-030]Camilla on Demetrio's saddle, her face covered with blood.

[033-031]Luis Cervantes hurried toward her with some cotton; but Camilla,

[033-032]choking down her sobs and wiping her eyes, said hoarsely:

[033-033]"Not from you! If I was dying, I wouldn't accept anything from you ...

[033-034]not even water."

[033-035]In Cuquio Demetrio received a message.

[033-036]"We've got to go back to Tepatitlan, General," said Luis Cervantes,

[033-037]scanning the dispatch rapidly. "You've got to leave the men there while

[033-038]you go to Lagos and take the train over to Aguascalientes."

[033-039]There was much heated protest, the men muttering to themselves or even

[033-040]groaning out loud. Some of them, mountaineers, swore that they would

[033-041]not continue with the troop.

[033-042]Camilla wept all night. On the morrow at dawn, she begged Demetrio to

[033-043]let her return home.

[033-044]"If you don't like me, all right," he answered sullenly.

[033-045]"That's not the reason. I care for you a lot, really. But you know how

[033-046]it is. That woman ..."

[033-047]"Never mind about her. It's all right! I'll send her off to hell today.

[033-048]I had already decided that."

[033-049]Camilla dried her tears....

[033-050]Every horse was saddled; the men were waiting only for orders from the

[033-051]Chief. Demetrio went up to War Paint and said under his breath:

[033-052]"You're not coming with us."

[033-053]"What!" she gasped.

[033-054]"You're going to stay here or go wherever you damn well please, but

[033-055]you're not coming along with us."

[033-056]"What? What's that you're saying?" Still she could not catch Demetrio's

[033-057]meaning. Then the truth dawned upon her. "You want to send me away? By

[033-058]God, I suppose you believe all the filth that bitch..."

[033-059]And War Paint proceeded to insult Camilla, Luis Cervantes, Demetrio,

[033-060]and anyone she happened to remember at the moment, with such power and

[033-061]originality that the soldiers listened in wonder to vituperation that

[033-062]transcended their wildest dream of profanity and filth. Demetrio waited

[033-063]a long time patiently. Then, as she showed no sign of stopping, he said

[033-064]to a soldier quite calmly:

[033-065]"Throw this drunken woman out."

[033-066]"Blondie, Blondie, love of my life! Help! Come and show them you're a

[033-067]real man! Show them they're nothing but sons of bitches! ..."

[033-068]She gesticulated, kicked, and shouted.

[033-069]Blondie appeared; he had just got up. His blue eyes blinked under heavy

[033-070]lids; his voice rang hoarse. He asked what had occurred; someone

[033-071]explained. Then he went up to War Paint, and with great seriousness,

[033-072]said:

[033-073]"Yes? Really? Well, if you want my opinion, I think this is just what

[033-074]ought to happen. So far as I'm concerned, you can go straight to hell.

[033-075]We're all fed up with you, see?"

[033-076]War Paint's face turned to granite; she tried to speak but her muscles

[033-077]were rigid.

[033-078]The soldiers laughed. Camilla, terrified, held her breath.

[033-079]War Paint stared slowly at everyone about her. It all took no more than

[033-080]a few seconds. In a trice she bent down, drew a sharp, gleaming dagger

[033-081]from her stocking and leapt at Camilla.

[033-082]A shrill cry. A body fell, the blood spurting from it.

[033-083]"Kill her, Goddamn it," cried Demetrio, beyond himself. "Kill her!"

[033-084]Two soldiers fell upon War Paint, but she brandished her dagger,

[033-085]defying them to touch her:

[033-086]"Not the likes of you, Goddamn you! Kill me yourself, Demetrio!"

[033-087]War Paint stepped forward, surrendered her dagger and, thrusting her

[033-088]breast forward, let her arms fall to her side.

[033-089]Demetrio picked up the dagger, red with blood, but his eyes clouded; he

[033-090]hesitated, took a step backward. Then, with a heavy hoarse voice he

[033-091]growled, enraged:

[033-092]"Get out of here! Quick!"

[033-093]No one dared stop her. She moved off slowly, mute, somber.

[033-094]Blondie's shrill, guttural voice broke the silent stupor:

[033-095]"Thank God! At last I'm rid of that damned louse!"
