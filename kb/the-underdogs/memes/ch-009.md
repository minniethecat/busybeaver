[009-001]Ho there, Remigia, lend me some eggs,

[009-002]will you? My chicken has been hatching since morning. There's some

[009-003]gentlemen here, come to eat."

[009-004]Her neighbor's eyes blinked as the bright sunlight poured into the

[009-005]shadowy hut, darker than usual, even, as dense clouds of smoke rose

[009-006]from the stove. After a few minutes, she began to make out the contour

[009-007]of the various objects inside, and recognized the wounded man's

[009-008]stretcher, which lay in one corner, close to the ashy-gray galvanized

[009-009]iron roof.

[009-010]She sat down beside Remigia Indian-fashion, and, glancing furtively

[009-011]toward where [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]] rested, asked in a low voice:

[009-012]"How's the patient, better? That's fine. Oh, how young he is! But he's

[009-013]still pale, don't you think? So the wound's not closed up yet. Well,

[009-014]Remigia, don't you think we'd better try and do something about it?"

[009-015]Remigia, naked from the waist up, stretched her thin muscular arms over

[009-016]the corn grinder, pounding the corn with a stone bar she held in her

[009-017]hands.

[009-018]"Oh, I don't know; they might not like it," she answered, breathing

[009-019]heavily as she continued her rude task. "They've got their own doctor,

[009-020]you know, so--"

[009-021]"Hallo, there, Remigia," another neighbor said as she came in, bowing

[009-022]her bony back to pass through the opening, "haven't you any laurel

[009-023]leaves? We want to make a potion for Maria Antonia who's not so well

[009-024]today, what with her bellyache."

[009-025]In reality, her errand was but a pretext for asking questions and

[009-026]passing the time of day in gossip, so she turned her eyes to the

[009-027]corner where the patient lay and, winking, sought information as to his

[009-028]health.

[009-029]Remigia lowered her eyes to indicate that Demetrio was sleeping.

[009-030]"Oh, I didn't see you when I came in. And you're here too, Panchita?

[009-031]Well, how are you?"

[009-032]"Good morning to you, Fortunata. How are you?"

[009-033]"All right. But Maria Antonia's got the curse today and her belly's

[009-034]aching something fierce."

[009-035]She sat Indian-fashion, with bent knees, huddling hip to hip against

[009-036]Panchita.

[009-037]"I've got no laurel leaves, honey," Remigia answered, pausing a moment

[009-038]in her work to push a mop of hair back from over her sweaty forehead.

[009-039]Then, plunging her two hands into a mass of corn, she removed a handful

[009-040]of it dripping with muddy yellowish water. "I've none at all; you'd

[009-041]better go to Dolores, she's always got herbs, you know."

[009-042]"But Dolores went to Cofradia last night. I don't know, but they say

[009-043]they came to fetch her to help Uncle Matias' girl who's big with child."

[009-044]"You don't say, Panchita?"

[009-045]The three old women came together forming an animated group, and

[009-046]speaking in low tones, began to gossip with great gusto.

[009-047]"Certainly, I swear it, by God up there in heaven."

[009-048]"Well, well, I was the first one to say that Marcelina was big with

[009-049]child, wasn't I? But of course no one would believe me."

[009-050]"Poor girl. It's going to be terrible if the kid is her uncle's, you

[009-051]know!"

[009-052]"God forbid!"

[009-053]"Of course it's not her uncle: Nazario had nothing to do with it, I

[009-054]know. It was them damned soldiers, that's who done it."

[009-055]"God, what a bloody mess! Another unhappy woman!"

[009-056]The cackle of the old hens finally awakened Demetrio. They kept silent

[009-057]for a moment; then Panchita, taking out of the bosom of her blouse a

[009-058]young pigeon which opened its beak in suffocation, said:

[009-059]"To tell you the truth, I brought this medicine for the gentleman here,

[009-060]but they say he's got a doctor, so I suppose--"

[009-061]"That makes no difference, Panchita, that's no medicine anyhow, it's

[009-062]simply something to rub on his body."

[009-063]"Forgive this poor gift from a poor woman, senor," said the wrinkled

[009-064]old woman, drawing close to Demetrio, "but there's nothing like it in

[009-065]the world for hemorrhages and suchlike."

[009-066]Demetrio nodded hasty approval. They had already placed a loaf of bread

[009-067]soaked in alcohol on his stomach; although when this was removed he

[009-068]began to be cooler, he felt that he was still feverish inside.

[009-069]"Come on, Remigia, you do it, you certainly know how," the women said.

[009-070]Out of a reed sheath, Remigia pulled a long and curved knife which

[009-071]served to cut cactus fruit. She took the pigeon in one hand, turned it

[009-072]over, its breast upward, and with the skill of a surgeon, ripped it in

[009-073]two with a single thrust.

[009-074]"In the name of Jesus, Mary, and Joseph," Remigia said, blessing the

[009-075]room and making the sign of the cross; next, with infinite dexterity,

[009-076]she placed the warm bleeding portions of the pigeon upon Demetrio's

[009-077]abdomen.

[009-078]"You'll see: you'll feel much better now."

[009-079]Obeying Remigia's instructions, Demetrio lay motionless, crumpled up on

[009-080]one side.

[009-081]Then Fortunata gave vent to her sorrows. She liked these gentlemen of

[009-082]the [[mexican-revolution|revolution]], all right, that she did--for, three months ago, you

[009-083]know, the Government soldiers had run away with her only daughter. This

[009-084]had broken her heart, Yes, and driven her all but crazy.

[009-085]As she began, Anastasio Montanez and Quail lay on the floor near the

[009-086]stretcher, their mouths gaping, all ears to the story. But Fortunata's

[009-087]wealth of detail by the time she had told half of it bored Quail and he

[009-088]left the hut to scratch himself out in the sun. By the time Fortunata

[009-089]had at last concluded with a solemn "I pray God and the Blessed Virgin

[009-090]Mary that you are not sparing the life of a single one of those

[009-091]Federals from hell," Demetrio, face to wall, felt greatly relieved by

[009-092]the stomach cure, and was busy thinking of the best route by which to

[009-093]proceed to Durango. Anastasio Montanez was snoring like a trombone.
