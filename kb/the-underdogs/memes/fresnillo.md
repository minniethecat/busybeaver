## Overview

Fresnillo is a town in [[zacatecas|Zacatecas]] that serves as the assembly point for General [[panfilo-natera|Pánfilo Natera]]'s forces and the site of Demetrio's first meeting with Natera. It appears at a crucial juncture in the novel — the buildup to the [[battle-of-zacatecas|Battle of Zacatecas]] — and then again when the failed assault sends the rebels reeling back through the same town in disarray. Fresnillo thus frames the arc of revolutionary ambition: assembly and confidence on the way up, defeat and demoralization on the way back.

## Location & Geography

Fresnillo is located in the state of Zacatecas, north of [[jalisco|Jalisco]] and south of the state capital [[zacatecas|Zacatecas]]. Its position made it a natural staging ground — close enough to the capital for a rapid advance, far enough to assemble troops without immediate Federal detection. It lies within Natera's sphere of control in the Potosí region (015-098).

## Role in the Narrative

Fresnillo first enters the narrative as the rallying point for the assault on Zacatecas. Luis Cervantes, unusually excited, announces that "General Panfilo Natera was assembling his men at Fresnillo" and declares that "the fall of Zacatecas will be Huerta's requiescat in pace" (012-070–072). The town hums with revolutionary expectation — it is where the scattered bands coalesce into an army.

The strategic importance of Fresnillo is confirmed by the broader military map: "Natera ruled over Fresnillo" (015-098), alongside Obregón besieging Guadalajara and Torres controlling the Potosí region. Fresnillo is part of a network of revolutionary strongholds that together threaten the Federal position.

Demetrio's meeting with Natera takes place at Fresnillo: "Demetrio with a hundred men went to meet him at Fresnillo" (018-002). Natera receives him cordially, acknowledging Demetrio's reputation. The meeting at Fresnillo marks Demetrio's entry into the larger revolutionary coalition — he is no longer an independent bandit but a colonel in a coordinated campaign.

The arc reverses sharply after the failed assault on Zacatecas. The rebels return through Fresnillo as "a maddened mob, sunburnt, filthy, naked" (019-003–005). The inhabitants of Fresnillo watch with "amazement" (019-002) — the same town that saw them march out in confidence now witnesses their return in defeat.

## Key Passages

*Fresnillo as the gathering point of revolutionary force — the assembly there signals the coming storm against Huerta's Federal army.*

*The meeting at Fresnillo marks Demetrio's incorporation into the larger revolutionary command structure under Natera.*

> It was with amazement that the inhabitants of Fresnillo learned that the rebel attack on Zacatecas had failed completely. [019-002–003]

*Fresnillo witnesses both the confident departure and the shattered return — the town frames the full arc of revolutionary hope and defeat.*
