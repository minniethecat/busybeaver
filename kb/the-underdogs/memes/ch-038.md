[038-001]In [[zacatecas|Zacatecas]] and Aguascalientes, in the little country towns and the

[038-002]neighboring communities, haciendas and ranches were deserted. When one

[038-003]of the officers found a barrel of tequila, the event assumed miraculous

[038-004]proportions. Everything was conducted with secrecy and care; deep

[038-005]mystery was preserved to oblige the soldiers to leave on the morrow

[038-006]before sunrise under Anastasio and Venancio.

[038-007]When [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]] awoke to the strains of music, his general staff, now

[038-008]composed chiefly of young ex-government officers, told him of the

[038-009]discovery, and Quail, interpreting the thoughts of his colleagues, said

[038-010]sententiously:

[038-011]"These are bad times and you've got to take advantage of everythin'. If

[038-012]there are some days when a duck can swim, there's others when he can't

[038-013]take a drink."

[038-014]The string musicians played all day; the most solemn honors were paid

[038-015]to the barrel: but Demetrio was very sad.

[038-016]"Did he know why?  

[038-017]I don't know why."

[038-018]He kept repeating the same refrain.

[038-019]In the afternoon there were cockfights. Demetrio sat down with the

[038-020]chief officers under the roof of the municipal portals in front of a

[038-021]city square covered with weeds, a tumbled kiosk, and some abandoned

[038-022]adobe houses.

[038-023]"Valderrama," Demetrio called, looking away from the ring with tired

[038-024]eyes, "come and sing me a song--sing 'The Undertaker.'"

[038-025]But Valderrama did not hear him; he had no eyes for the fight; he was

[038-026]reciting an impassioned soliloquy as he watched the sunset over the

[038-027]hills.

[038-028]With solemn gestures and emphatic tones, he said:

[038-029]"O Lord, Lord, pleasurable it is this thy land! I shall build me three

[038-030]tents: one for Thee, one for Moses, one for Elijah!"

[038-031]"Valderrama," Demetrio shouted again. "Come and sing 'The Undertaker'

[038-032]song for me."

[038-033]"Hey, crazy, the General is calling you," an officer shouted.

[038-034]Valderrama with his eternally complacent smile went over to Demetrio's

[038-035]seat and asked the musicians for a guitar.

[038-036]"Silence," the gamesters cried. Valderrama finished tuning his

[038-037]instrument.

[038-038]Quail and Meco let loose on the sand a pair of cocks armed with long

[038-039]sharp blades attached to their legs. One was light red; his feathers

[038-040]shone with beautiful obsidian glints. The other was sand-colored with

[038-041]feathers like scales burned slowly to a fiery copper color.

[038-042]The fight was swift and fierce as a duel between men. As though moved

[038-043]by springs, the roosters flew at each other. Their feathers stood up on

[038-044]their arched necks; their combs were erect, their legs taut. For an

[038-045]instant they swung in the air without even touching the ground, their

[038-046]feathers, beaks, and claws lost in a dizzy whirlwind. The red rooster

[038-047]suddenly broke, tossed with his legs to heaven outside the chalk lines.

[038-048]His vermilion eyes closed slowly, revealing eyelids of pink coral; his

[038-049]tangled feathers quivered and shook convulsively amid a pool of blood.

[038-050]Valderrama, who could not repress a gesture of violent indignation,

[038-051]began to play. With the first melancholy strains of the tune, his anger

[038-052]disappeared. His eyes gleamed with the light of madness. His glance

[038-053]strayed over the square, the tumbled kiosk, the old adobe houses, over

[038-054]the mountains in the background, and over the sky, burning like a roof

[038-055]afire. He began to sing. He put such feeling into his voice and such

[038-056]expression into the strings that, as he finished, Demetrio turned his

[038-057]head aside to hide his tears.

[038-058]But Valderrama fell upon him, embraced him warmly, and with a

[038-059]familiarity he showed everyone at the appropriate moment, he whispered:

[038-060]"Drink them! ... Those are beautiful tears."

[038-061]Demetrio asked for the bottle, passed it to Valderrama. Greedily the

[038-062]poet drank half its contents in one gulp; then, showing only the whites

[038-063]of his eyes, he faced the spectators dramatically and, in a highly

[038-064]theatrical voice, cried:

[038-065]"Here you may witness the blessings of the [[mexican-revolution|revolution]] caught in a

[038-066]single tear."

[038-067]Then he continued to talk like a madman, but like a madman whose vast

[038-068]prophetic madness encompassed all about him, the dusty weeds, the

[038-069]tumbled kiosk, the gray houses, the lovely hills, and the immeasurable

[038-070]sky.
