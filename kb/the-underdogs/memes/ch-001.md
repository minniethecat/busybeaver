[001-001]"That's no animal, I tell you! Listen to the dog barking! It must be a

[001-002]human being."

[001-003]The woman stared into the darkness of the sierra.

[001-004]"What if they're soldiers?" said a man, who sat Indian-fashion, eating,

[001-005]a coarse earthenware plate in his right hand, three folded tortillas in

[001-006]the other.

[001-007]The woman made no answer, all her senses directed outside the hut. The

[001-008]beat of horses' hoofs rang in the quarry nearby. The dog barked again,

[001-009]louder and more angrily.

[001-010]"Well, [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]], I think you had better hide, all the same."

[001-011]Stolidly, the man finished eating; next he reached for a cantaro and

[001-012]gulped down the water in it; then he stood up.

[001-013]"Your rifle is under the mat," she whispered.

[001-014]A tallow candle illumined the small room. In one corner stood a plow, a

[001-015]yoke, a goad, and other agricultural implements. Ropes hung from the

[001-016]roof, securing an old adobe mold, used as a bed; on it a child slept,

[001-017]covered with gray rags.

[001-018]Demetrio buckled his cartridge belt about his waist and picked up his

[001-019]rifle. He was tall and well built, with a sanguine face and beardless

[001-020]chin; he wore shirt and trousers of white cloth, a broad Mexican hat

[001-021]and leather sandals.

[001-022]With slow, measured step, he left the room, vanishing into the

[001-023]impenetrable darkness of the night.

[001-024]The dog, excited to the point of madness, had jumped over the corral

[001-025]fence.

[001-026]Suddenly a shot rang out. The dog moaned, then barked no more. Some men

[001-027]on horseback rode up, shouting and sweating; two of them dismounted,

[001-028]while the other hung back to watch the horses.

[001-029]"Hey, there, woman: we want food! Give us eggs, milk, beans, anything

[001-030]you've got! We're starving!"

[001-031]"Curse the sierra! It would take the Devil himself not to lose his way!"

[001-032]"Guess again, Sergeant! Even the Devil would go astray if he were as

[001-033]drunk as you are."

[001-034]The first speaker wore chevrons on his arm, the other red stripes on

[001-035]his shoulders.

[001-036]"Whose place is this, old woman? Or is it an empty house? God's truth,

[001-037]which is it?"

[001-038]"Of course it's not empty. How about the light and that child there?

[001-039]Look here, confound it, we want to eat, and damn quick tool Are you

[001-040]coming out or are we going to make you?"

[001-041]"You swine! Both of you! You've gone and killed my dog, that's what

[001-042]you've done! What harm did he ever do you? What did you have against

[001-043]him?"

[001-044]The woman reentered the house, dragging the dog behind her, very white

[001-045]and fat, with lifeless eyes and flabby body.

[001-046]"Look at those cheeks, Sergeant! Don't get riled, light of my life: I

[001-047]swear I'll turn your home into a dovecot, see?"

[001-048]"By God!" he said, breaking off into song:

[001-049]"Don't look so haughty, dear,  

[001-050]Banish all fears,  

[001-051]Kiss me and melt to me,  

[001-052]I'll drink up your tears!"

[001-053]His alcoholic tenor trailed off into the night.

[001-054]"Tell me what they call this ranch, woman?" the sergeant asked.

[001-055]"Limon," the woman replied curtly, carrying wood to the fire and

[001-056]fanning the coals.

[001-057]"So we're in Limon, eh, the famous Demetrio Macias' country, eh? Do you

[001-058]hear that, Lieutenant? We're in Limon."

[001-059]"Limon? What the hell do I care? If I'm bound for hell, Sergeant, I

[001-060]might as well go there now. I don't mind, now that I've found as good a

[001-061]remount as this! Look at the cheeks on the darling, look at them!

[001-062]There's a pair of ripe red apples for a fellow to bite into!"

[001-063]"I'll wager you know Macias the bandit, lady? I was in the pen with him

[001-064]at Escobedo, once."

[001-065]"Bring me a bottle of tequila, Sergeant: I've decided to spend the

[001-066]night with this charming lady.... What's that? The colonel? ... Why in

[001-067]God's name talk about the colonel now? He can go straight to hell, for

[001-068]all I care. And if he doesn't like it, it's all right with me. Come on,

[001-069]Sergeant, tell the corporal outside to unsaddle the horses and feed

[001-070]them. I'll stay here all night. Here, my girl, you let the sergeant fry

[001-071]the eggs and warm up the tortillas; you come here to me. See this

[001-072]wallet full of nice new bills? They're all for you, darling. Sure, I

[001-073]want you to have them. Figure it out for yourself. I'm drunk, see: I've

[001-074]a bit of a load on and that's why I'm kind of hoarse, you might call

[001-075]it. I left half my gullet down Guadalajara way, and I've been spitting

[001-076]the other half out all the way up here. Oh well, who cares? But I want

[001-077]you to have that money, see, dearie? Hey, Sergeant, where's my bottle?

[001-078]Now, little girl, come here and pour yourself a drink. You won't, eh?

[001-079]Aw, come on! Afraid of your--er--husband ... or whatever he is, huh?

[001-080]Well, if he's skulking in some hole, you tell him to come out. What the

[001-081]hell do I care? I'm not scared of rats, see!" Suddenly a white shadow

[001-082]loomed on the threshold.

[001-083]"Demetrio Macias!" the sergeant cried as he stepped back in terror.

[001-084]The lieutenant stood up, silent, cold and motionless as a statue.

[001-085]"Shoot them!" the woman croaked.

[001-086]"Oh, come, you'll surely spare us! I didn't know you were there. I'll

[001-087]always stand up for a brave man."

[001-088]Demetrio stood his ground, looking them up and down, an insolent and

[001-089]disdainful smile wrinkling his face.

[001-090]"Yes, I not only respect brave men, but I like them. I'm proud and

[001-091]happy to call them friends. Here's my hand on it: friend to friend."

[001-092]Then, after a pause: "All right, Demetrio Macias, if you don't want to

[001-093]shake hands, all right! But it's because you don't know me, that's why,

[001-094]just because the first time you saw me I was doing this dog's job. But

[001-095]look here, I ask you, what in God's name can a man do when he's poor

[001-096]and has a wife to support and kids? ... Right you are, Sergeant, let's

[001-097]go: I've nothing but respect for the home of what I call a brave man, a

[001-098]real, honest, genuine man!"

[001-099]When they had gone, the woman drew close to Demetrio.

[001-100]"Holy Virgin, what agony! I suffered as though it was you they'd shot."

[001-101]"You go to father's house, quick!" Demetrio ordered. She wanted to hold

[001-102]him in her arms; she entreated, she wept. But he pushed away from her

[001-103]gently and, in a sullen voice, said, "I've an idea the whole lot of

[001-104]them are coming."

[001-105]"Why didn't you kill 'em?"

[001-106]"Their hour hasn't struck yet."

[001-107]They went out together; she bore the child in her arms. At the door,

[001-108]they separated, moving off in different directions.

[001-109]The moon peopled the mountain with vague shadows. As he advanced at

[001-110]every turn of his way Demetrio could see the poignant, sharp silhouette

[001-111]of a woman pushing forward painfully, bearing a child in her arms.

[001-112]When, after many hours of climbing, he gazed back, huge flames shot up

[001-113]from the depths of the canyon by the river. It was his house,

[001-114]blazing....
