[037-001]"WHY do you hide?" [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]] asked the prisoners.

[037-002]"We're not hiding, Chief, we're hitting the trail."

[037-003]"Where to?"

[037-004]"To our own homes, in God's name, to Durango."

[037-005]"Is this the road to Durango?"

[037-006]"Peaceful people can't travel over the main road nowadays, you know

[037-007]that, Chief."

[037-008]"You're not peaceful people, you're deserters. Where do you come from?"

[037-009]Demetrio said, eyeing them with keen scrutiny.

[037-010]The prisoners grew confused; they looked at each other hesitatingly,

[037-011]unable to give a prompt answer.

[037-012]"They're Carranzistas," one of the soldiers said.

[037-013]"Carranzistas hell!" one of them said proudly. "I'd rather be a pig."

[037-014]"The truth is we're deserters," another said. "After the defeat we

[037-015]deserted from General Villa's troops this side of Celaya."

[037-016]"General Villa defeated? Ha! Ha! That's a good joke."

[037-017]The soldiers laughed. But Demetrio's brow was wrinkled as though a

[037-018]black shadow had passed over his eyes.

[037-019]"There ain't a son of a bitch on earth who can beat General Villa!"

[037-020]said a bronzed veteran with a scar clear across the face.

[037-021]Without a change of expression, one of the deserters stared

[037-022]persistently at him and said:

[037-023]"I know who you are. When we took Torreon you were with General Urbina.

[037-024]In [[zacatecas|Zacatecas]] you were with General Natera and then you shifted to the

[037-025]Jalisco troops. Am I lying?"

[037-026]These words met with a sudden and definite effect. The prisoners gave a

[037-027]detailed account of the tremendous defeat of Villa at Celaya.

[037-028]Demetrio's men listened in silence, stupefied.

[037-029]Before resuming their march, they built a fire on which to roast some

[037-030]bull meat. Anastasio Montanez, searching for food among the huizache

[037-031]trees, descried the close-cropped neck of Valderrama's horse in the

[037-032]distance among the rocks.

[037-033]"Hey! Come here, you fool, after all there ain't been no gravy!" he

[037-034]shouted.

[037-035]Whenever anything was said about shooting someone, Valderrama, the

[037-036]romantic poet, would disappear for a whole day.

[037-037]Hearing Anastasio's voice, Valderrama was convinced that the prisoners

[037-038]had been set at liberty. A few moments later, he was joined by Venancio

[037-039]and Demetrio.

[037-040]"Heard the news?" Venancio asked gravely.

[037-041]"No."

[037-042]"It's very serious. A terrible mess! Villa was beaten at Celaya by

[037-043]Obregon and Carranza is winning all along the line! We're done for!"

[037-044]Valderrama's gesture was disdainful and solemn as an emperor's. "Villa?

[037-045]Obregon? Carranza? What's the difference? I love the [[mexican-revolution|revolution]] like a

[037-046]volcano in eruption; I love the volcano because it's a volcano, the

[037-047]revolution because it's the revolution! What do I care about the stones

[037-048]left above or below after the cataclysm? What are they to me?"

[037-049]In the glare of the midday sun the reflection of a white tequila bottle

[037-050]glittered on his forehead; and, jubilant, he ran toward the bearer of

[037-051]such a marvelous gift.

[037-052]"I like this crazy fool," Demetrio said with a smile. "He says things

[037-053]sometimes that make you think."

[037-054]They resumed their march; their uncertainty translated into a

[037-055]lugubrious silence. Slowly, inevitably, the catastrophe must come; it

[037-056]was even now being realized. Villa defeated was a fallen god; when gods

[037-057]cease to be omnipotent, they are nothing.

[037-058]Quail spoke. His words faithfully interpreted the general opinion:

[037-059]"What the hell, boys! Every spider's got to spin his own web now!"
