[018-001]On the day General Natera began his advance against the town of

[018-002][[zacatecas|Zacatecas]], [[demetrio-macias|Demetrio]] with a hundred men went to meet him at Fresnillo.

[018-003]The leader received him cordially.

[018-004]"I know who you are and the sort of men you bring. I heard about the

[018-005]beatings you gave the Federals from Tepic to Durango."

[018-006]Natera shook hands with Demetrio effusively while Luis Cervantes said:

[018-007]"With men like General Natera and Colonel Demetrio Macias, we'll cover

[018-008]our country with glory."

[018-009]Demetrio understood the purpose of those words, after Natera had

[018-010]repeatedly addressed him as "Colonel."

[018-011]Wine and beer were served; Demetrio and Natera drank many a toast.

[018-012]Luis Cervantes proposed: "The triumph of our cause, which is the

[018-013]sublime triumph of Justice, because our ideal--to free the noble,

[018-014]long-suffering people of Mexico--is about to be realized and because

[018-015]those men who have watered the earth with their blood and tears will

[018-016]reap the harvest which is rightfully theirs."

[018-017]Natera fixed his cruel gaze on the orator, then turned his back on him

[018-018]to talk to Demetrio. Presently, one of Natera's officers, a young man

[018-019]with a frank open face, drew up to the table and stared insistently at

[018-020]Cervantes.

[018-021]"Are you Luis Cervantes?"

[018-022]"Yes. You're Solis, eh?"

[018-023]"The moment you entered I thought I recognized you. Well, well, even

[018-024]now I can hardly believe my eyes!"

[018-025]"It's true enough!"

[018-026]"Well, but ... look here, let's have a drink, come along." Then:

[018-027]"Hm," Solis went on, offering Cervantes a chair, "since when have you

[018-028]turned rebel?"

[018-029]"I've been a rebel the last two months!"

[018-030]"Oh, I see! That's why you speak with such faith and enthusiasm about

[018-031]things we all felt when we joined the [[mexican-revolution|revolution]]."

[018-032]"Have you lost your faith or enthusiasm?"

[018-033]"Look here, man, don't be surprised if I confide in you right off. I am

[018-034]so anxious to find someone intelligent among this crowd, that as soon

[018-035]as I get hold of a man like you I clutch at him as eagerly as I would

[018-036]at a glass of water, after walking mile after mile through a parched

[018-037]desert. But frankly, I think you should do the explaining first. I

[018-038]can't understand how a man who was correspondent of a Government

[018-039]newspaper during the Madero regime, and later editorial writer on a

[018-040]Conservative journal, who denounced us as bandits in the most fiery

[018-041]articles, is now fighting on our side."

[018-042]"I tell you honestly: I have been converted," Cervantes answered.

[018-043]"Are you absolutely convinced?"

[018-044]Solis sighed, filled the glasses; they drank.

[018-045]"What about you? Are you tired of the revolution?" asked Cervantes

[018-046]sharply.

[018-047]"Tired? My dear fellow, I'm twenty-five years old and I'm fit as a

[018-048]fiddle! But am I disappointed? Perhaps!"

[018-049]"You must have sound reasons for feeling that way."

[018-050]"I hoped to find a meadow at the end of the road. I found a swamp.

[018-051]Facts are bitter; so are men. That bitterness eats your heart out; it

[018-052]is poison, dry rot. Enthusiasm, hope, ideals, happiness-vain dreams,

[018-053]vain dreams.... When that's over, you have a choice. Either you turn

[018-054]bandit, like the rest, or the timeservers will swamp you...."

[018-055]Cervantes writhed at his friend's words; his argument was quite out of

[018-056]place ... painful.... To avoid being forced to take issue, he invited

[018-057]Solis to cite the circumstances that had destroyed his illusions.

[018-058]"Circumstances? No--it's far less important than that. It's a host of

[018-059]silly, insignificant things that no one notices except yourself ... a

[018-060]change of expression, eyes shining-lips curled in a sneer-the deep

[018-061]import of a phrase that is lost! Yet take these things together and

[018-062]they compose the mask of our race ... terrible ... grotesque ... a race

[018-063]that awaits redemption!"

[018-064]He drained another glass. After a long pause, he continued:

[018-065]"You ask me why I am still a rebel? Well, the revolution is like a

[018-066]hurricane: if you're in it, you're not a man ... you're a leaf, a dead

[018-067]leaf, blown by the wind."

[018-068]Demetrio reappeared. Seeing him, Solis relapsed into silence.

[018-069]"Come along," Demetrio said to Cervantes. "Come with me."

[018-070]Unctuously, Solis congratulated Demetrio on the feats that had won him

[018-071]fame and the notice of Pancho Villa's northern division.

[018-072]Demetrio warmed to his praise. Gratefully, he heard his prowess

[018-073]vaunted, though at times he found it difficult to believe he was the

[018-074]hero of the exploits the other narrated. But Solis' story proved so

[018-075]charming, so convincing, that before long he found himself repeating it

[018-076]as gospel truth.

[018-077]"Natera is a genius!" Luis Cervantes said when they had returned to the

[018-078]hotel. "But Captain Solis is a nobody ... a timeserver."

[018-079]Demetrio Macias was too elated to listen to him. "I'm a colonel, my

[018-080]lad! And you're my secretary!"

[018-081]Demetrio's men made many acquaintances that evening; much liquor flowed

[018-082]to celebrate new friendships. Of course men are not necessarily even

[018-083]tempered, nor is alcohol a good counselor; quarrels naturally ensued.

[018-084]Yet many differences that occurred were smoothed out in a friendly

[018-085]spirit, outside the saloons, restaurants, or brothels.

[018-086]On the morrow, casualties were reported. Always a few dead. An old

[018-087]prostitute was found with a bullet through her stomach; two of Colonel

[018-088]Macias' new men lay in the gutter, slit from ear to ear.

[018-089]Anastasio Montanez carried an account of the events to his chief.

[018-090]Demetrio shrugged his shoulders. "Bury them!" he said.
