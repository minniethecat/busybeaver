# Discovery of the Will

The revelation of documentary evidence — [[eustace-peters|Peters]]' will or related papers — that shifts the understanding of motive and inheritance.

> "I am not sure why I am being so precise in recording our movements at that time. Perhaps it is merely from an impulse to try and live over again a period of my life which was one of great and of increasing, not diminishing, agitation. But having begun, I will"
> — （005-005）

## Overview

Discovery of the Will is one of the key events in *Tracks in the Snow*, occurring during the investigation of **[[eustace-peters|Eustace Peters]]**' murder. The event shapes the trajectory of the narrative and the understanding of the characters involved.

## In the Novel

The event unfolds within the 1890s English setting, observed and narrated by **[[long-wilton|Long Wilton]]** in the first person. Its significance to the murder investigation becomes apparent as the narrative develops.
