# The Bedroom

The crime scene — [[eustace-peters|Eustace Peters]]' bedroom at his house, where his body was discovered on the morning of 29 January.. This appears in the narrative of *Tracks in the Snow* during the investigation of **[[eustace-peters|Eustace Peters]]**' murder.

> "After some hours Callaghan entered my room to tell me that dinner would be ready in half an hour, that I might get up for it if I liked, or have it brought to my bedroom. He then turned on me reproachfully. “Why had I not shown him these papers long ago, when he came to stay with me?” I was at a loss for an answer, for in fact when I had told him"
> — （020-004）

## In the Novel

The Bedroom features in the story of the murder investigation. The narrative, told by **[[long-wilton|Long Wilton]]** in the first person, situates key events within the context of the bedroom.

## Significance

The role of the bedroom in the novel contributes to the atmosphere and setting of the 1890s [[england|English countryside]] mystery. Its presence in the narrative helps establish the social and physical world in which [[murder|the crime]] and its investigation unfold.
