# The Footprint Investigation

The examination of footprints in the snow around [[eustace-peters|Peters]]' house — the [[clue|physical evidence]] that initially implicates [[reuben-trethewy|Trethewy]] and later helps exonerate him.

> "As for the actual carriage-drive I was fortunately able to remember (and it was the only useful thing that I did observe for myself) that when I had arrived there had been no footmarks between the gate and the front door except the unmistakable print of the goloshes worn by the housemaid on her way to call the police. But the tracks on the lawns"
> — （002-006）

## Overview

The Footprint Investigation is one of the key events in *Tracks in the Snow*, occurring during the investigation of **[[eustace-peters|Eustace Peters]]**' murder. The event shapes the trajectory of the narrative and the understanding of the characters involved.

## In the Novel

The event unfolds within the 1890s English setting, observed and narrated by **[[long-wilton|Long Wilton]]** in the first person. Its significance to the murder investigation becomes apparent as the narrative develops.
