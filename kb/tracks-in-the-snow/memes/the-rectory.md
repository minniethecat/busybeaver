# The Rectory

[[long-wilton|Long Wilton]]'s home in [[long-wilton-village|the village]] — [[robert-jenkinson|the rector]]y serves as a base for the amateur investigation and a site of key conversations.. This appears in the narrative of *Tracks in the Snow* during the investigation of **[[eustace-peters|Eustace Peters]]**' murder.

> "We left the house for the Rectory soon (my man was to come with a barrow for the luggage), but before we left, one further piece of evidence had accidentally come to my knowledge. I learnt from something which the housekeeper was saying to the maid that the ladder was one which was always kept in the pump-house, that the pump-house was always kept"
> — （004-007）

## In the Novel

The Rectory features in the story of the murder investigation. The narrative, told by **[[long-wilton|Long Wilton]]** in the first person, situates key events within the context of [[robert-jenkinson|the rector]]y.

## Significance

The role of [[robert-jenkinson|the rector]]y in the novel contributes to the atmosphere and setting of the 1890s [[england|English countryside]] mystery. Its presence in the narrative helps establish the social and physical world in which [[murder|the crime]] and its investigation unfold.
