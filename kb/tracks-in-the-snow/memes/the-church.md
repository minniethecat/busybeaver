# The Church

The village church where [[long-wilton|Long Wilton]] serves as rector — a site of funerals, moral reflection, and community gathering.. This appears in the narrative of *Tracks in the Snow* during the investigation of **[[eustace-peters|Eustace Peters]]**' murder.


## In the Novel

The Church features in the story of the murder investigation. The narrative, told by **[[long-wilton|Long Wilton]]** in the first person, situates key events within the context of the church.

## Significance

The role of the church in the novel contributes to the atmosphere and setting of the 1890s [[england|English countryside]] mystery. Its presence in the narrative helps establish the social and physical world in which [[murder|the crime]] and its investigation unfold.
