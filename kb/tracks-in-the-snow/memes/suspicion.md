# Suspicion

The state of doubt and mistrust that pervades the investigation — suspicion falls on multiple characters and drives the narrative of [[investigation|detection]].. The concept is explored throughout *Tracks in the Snow*, appearing in the investigation of **[[eustace-peters|Eustace Peters]]**' murder.

## Historical Context

In the context of 1890s [[england|England]] — the setting of *Tracks in the Snow* — suspicion reflected the social, legal, and intellectual currents of the late Victorian period. The novel's treatment of this subject draws on contemporary understanding and practice.

## Analysis

The portrayal of suspicion in *Tracks in the Snow* contributes to the novel's broader thematic concerns. Through the first-person narrative of **[[long-wilton|Long Wilton]]**, the reader encounters this concept not as abstract theory but as lived experience — shaped by the pressures of a murder investigation and the moral weight of seeking justice.
