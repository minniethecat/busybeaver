# Oxford

The university city connected to key characters — [[long-wilton|Wilton]]'s academic background and connections to educated society.. This appears in the narrative of *Tracks in the Snow* during the investigation of **[[eustace-peters|Eustace Peters]]**' murder.

> "In the course of the summer my wife and I paid our annual visit together to London, and I had a few days in Oxford before the end of the summer"
> — （012-001）

## In the Novel

Oxford features in the story of the murder investigation. The narrative, told by **[[long-wilton|Long Wilton]]** in the first person, situates key events within the context of oxford.

## Significance

The role of oxford in the novel contributes to the atmosphere and setting of the 1890s [[england|English countryside]] mystery. Its presence in the narrative helps establish the social and physical world in which [[murder|the crime]] and its investigation unfold.
