# The Garden

The grounds and gardens surrounding the houses in [[long-wilton-village|the village]] — sites of private conversations, discoveries, and the natural setting that frames the human drama.. This appears in the narrative of *Tracks in the Snow* during the investigation of **[[eustace-peters|Eustace Peters]]**' murder.

> "Some ten minutes after, I was about to go, when he suddenly came from behind and addressed me, asking me to choose a day for dinner or luncheon at his club. > — （012-003）

## In the Novel

The Garden features in the story of the murder investigation. The narrative, told by **[[long-wilton|Long Wilton]]** in the first person, situates key events within the context of the garden.

## Significance

The role of the garden in the novel contributes to the atmosphere and setting of the 1890s [[england|English countryside]] mystery. Its presence in the narrative helps establish the social and physical world in which [[murder|the crime]] and its investigation unfold.
