# England

The national setting of the novel — 1890s England provides the legal, social, and cultural context for the murder investigation.. This appears in the narrative of *Tracks in the Snow* during the investigation of **[[eustace-peters|Eustace Peters]]**' murder.

> "Thus I tried to think, as I left England for a few weeks in the late spring of 1897 to join my wife and our daughter, who was now much stronger, in Italy; but, whatever I tried to think, I had always with me that consciousness of a purpose frustrated or let go, which is perhaps the hardest thing to bear well and the most enervating thing to bear"
> — （016-002）

## In the Novel

England features in the story of the murder investigation. The narrative, told by **[[long-wilton|Long Wilton]]** in the first person, situates key events within the context of england.

## Significance

The role of england in the novel contributes to the atmosphere and setting of the 1890s English countryside mystery. Its presence in the narrative helps establish the social and physical world in which [[murder|the crime]] and its investigation unfold.
