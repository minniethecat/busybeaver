# The Death of Ellen Trethewy

The second death in the novel — [[ellen-trethewy|Ellen Trethewy]]'s drowning, which deepens the mystery and redirects the investigation.

> "So then, as I had half-guessed, Longhurst was dead. It was not that he was alive and Cartwright pretended he was dead, he was dead, and Cartwright had a motive for falsely pretending he was"
> — （009-006）

## Overview

The Death of Ellen Trethewy is one of the key events in *Tracks in the Snow*, occurring during the investigation of **[[eustace-peters|Eustace Peters]]**' murder. The event shapes the trajectory of the narrative and the understanding of the characters involved.

## In the Novel

The event unfolds within the 1890s English setting, observed and narrated by **[[long-wilton|Long Wilton]]** in the first person. Its significance to the murder investigation becomes apparent as the narrative develops.
