## Overview

Mrs. Travers was the cook and housekeeper at [[grenvile-combe]], the home of [[eustace-peters|Eustace Peters]]. She was "sharp-visaged and sharp-tongued" but "made Peters very comfortable" [001-009]. Her barley water was noted as a remedy during illness in the workhouse [008-008].

## Role

As Peters' housekeeper, Mrs. Travers was responsible for the domestic management of Grenvile Combe. She was one of the few people who knew the household intimately and could speak to Peters' habits and routines — though her sharp tongue may have made her a difficult witness to interview [001-009].

The narrator mentioned her in connection with the workhouse, where her barley water was used — suggesting her reputation for practical nursing extended beyond the Peters household [008-008].
