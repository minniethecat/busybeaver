# Sharnall's Diatribe Against Wealth

## Overview

Mr. Sharnall's furious outburst against the Blandamers and the worship of wealth is the novel's most explicit statement of social criticism.

## Narrative Role

In the organ loft, Sharnall's long-simmering resentment erupts: "I hate all Blandamers. I wish they were as dead and buried as dodos" (010-072). His condemnation extends beyond the family to a society that values wealth above all else.
