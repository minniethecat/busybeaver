# Cullerne Grammar School

## Overview

The grammar school is mentioned in Sharnall's account of Martin Joliffe's youth: "he went to Cullerne Grammar School, and did well, and got a scholarship at Oxford" (003-052). It represents the meritocratic possibility that Cullerne offered its talented sons — a possibility that, in Martin's case, was destroyed by the nebuly coat obsession.

## Description

Cullerne Grammar School, where Martin Joliffe was educated and earned his scholarship to Oxford, represents the pathway of merit that offered bright working-class boys a route out of Cullerne — a route that, for Martin, led ultimately to tragedy..
