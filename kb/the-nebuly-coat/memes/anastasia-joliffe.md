# Anastasia Joliffe

## Overview

Anastasia Joliffe is the novel's heroine — the daughter of a Cullerne lodging-house keeper whose engagement to Lord Blandamer transforms her social position and resolves the novel's central romance. She is characterised by quiet dignity, loyalty to her family, and a capacity for love that transcends the class boundaries that constrain her world. Her journey from obscure spinster to future Lady Blandamer is the novel's most dramatic instance of social mobility.

## Biography

Anastasia lives at Bellevue Lodge with her aunt, Miss Euphemia Joliffe, where she assists in running the lodging house that accommodates Westray. Her social position is ambiguous — connected to the Blandamer mystery through her half-brother Martin, whose madness and death have marked her life, yet excluded from polite society by her family's reduced circumstances.

Lord Blandamer's courtship of Anastasia is conducted with discretion. The engagement, when announced, produces an immediate social transformation. "The ladies who had contemned a lodging-house keeper's daughter courted the betrothed of a peer" (019-059). The engagement ring — an emerald surrounded by diamonds in green and white, the colours of the nebuly coat — physically embodies the union of romantic love with heraldic inheritance (019-059).

Her departure for London after the engagement removes her from the novel's final act, but her presence continues to be felt. She represents the possibility of happiness — a resolution achieved through love rather than litigation.

## Relationships

Anastasia's relationship with **Lord Blandamer** is the novel's central romance. Her loyalty to her half-brother **Martin Joliffe** survives his madness and the consumption of her savings. With her aunt **Miss Euphemia Joliffe**, she shares the quiet domestic world of Bellevue Lodge. Her interactions with **Westray** are marked by restraint on both sides — a road not taken.

## Significance

Anastasia represents the redemptive possibilities of love within a social order otherwise characterised by greed, obsession, and decay. Her engagement to Lord Blandamer suggests that the Blandamer legacy can be renewed through genuine affection rather than genealogical obsession — a resolution that the novel offers tentatively but hopefully.

## Key Quotes

> "Green and white being the colours of his lordship's shield, what they call the nebuly coat, you know" (019-059).
