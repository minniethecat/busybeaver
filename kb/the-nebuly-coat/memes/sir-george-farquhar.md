# Sir George Farquhar

## Overview

Sir George Farquhar is the senior architect and consulting authority for the Cullerne Minster restoration. As Westray's superior, he embodies the established professional establishment — knighted, respected, and cautious. His distance from Cullerne (he remains in London) contrasts with Westray's immersion in the town's life.

## Biography

Sir George appears primarily through his instructions to Westray and his occasional visits. He has "conceived with regard to the restoration" (002-012) a comprehensive plan, but his concern for the tower's stability is tempered by professional caution. His warning to Westray about the tower's condition — "he had been specially cautioned" (002-027) — establishes the central architectural problem.

His professional eminence is signalled by his knighthood and his role as consulting architect rather than hands-on supervisor. The day-to-day responsibility falls to Westray, creating the space for the younger architect's independent judgment to develop.

## Relationships

Sir George's relationship with **Westray** is that of mentor to pupil — he sets the professional standards but allows Westray operational autonomy. His warning about the tower becomes a measure of Westray's developing judgment: the pupil must decide when professional caution must yield to urgent action.

## Significance

Sir George represents the professional establishment — competent, established, and remote. His physical absence from Cullerne is crucial to the plot, as it leaves Westray to make decisions that will have life-or-death consequences.

## Key Quotes

> "He had been specially cautioned" about the tower (002-027).

> "It is a great privilege, this restoration to which you are called" (004-038).
