# The Clerk

## Overview

A legal clerk in Cullerne whose handling of documents related to the Blandamer inheritance connects the professional world of law to the personal drama of legitimacy and identity..

## Biography

The clerk occupies a liminal position in Cullerne's professional world — educated enough to handle legal documents, humble enough to know his place. His work brings him into contact with the papers that record and transmit property and identity, the documentary infrastructure of the social order that the novel anatomises. When Westray needs access to records concerning the Blandamer inheritance, it is through such minor functionaries that knowledge must be sought. The clerk embodies the paradox of the minor character: essential to the plot's machinery, invisible in its drama.

## Relationships

As a minor figure, interactions with major characters are limited but significant — each encounter revealing a facet of Cullerne life that the central characters alone cannot access.

## Significance

Minor characters in *The Nebuly Coat* collectively constitute the town's collective memory — a distributed knowledge of church, family, and history that no single authority can monopolise. Their testimony, scattered across brief appearances, provides the evidential foundation upon which the novel's revelations depend.
