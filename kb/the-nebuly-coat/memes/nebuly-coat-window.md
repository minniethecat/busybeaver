# The Nebuly Coat Window

## Overview

The great stained-glass window in the south transept of Cullerne Minster, created by Van Linge to the order of the third Lord Blandamer, is the novel's central artwork — a visual repository of Blandamer heraldry and history.

## In the Novel

The window was "filled with glass to the order of the third Lord Blandamer, and the sea-green and silver of the nebuly coat was many times repeated, beside figuring in chief at the head of the window" (021-002). Westray's study of its heraldry occupies his winter evenings, and his sketches of its shields become a crucial record.

## Significance

This work's role in the narrative and its symbolic resonance.
