# Canon Parkyn

## Overview

Canon Parkyn is the Rector of Cullerne, the clergyman responsible for the restoration of the minster and the spiritual welfare of the parish. He represents the established Church — respectable, proper, and concerned above all with the maintenance of institutional dignity. His enthusiasm for the restoration project provides the official framework within which Westray operates.

## Biography

The Rector has been "very successful in collecting money for the restoration" (001-005), demonstrating his skill at the essential clerical function of fundraising. His relationship with the architectural professionals is courteous but hierarchical — he defers to Sir George Farquhar's expertise while maintaining the authority of his ecclesiastical office.

Canon Parkyn's social position places him at the intersection of church, gentry, and town. He interacts with Lord Blandamer as patron, with Westray as professional, and with his parishioners as pastor. His departure from an event because "he had an engagement which would occupy him for an hour" (012-037) reflects the busy round of clerical obligations that define his life.

## Relationships

The Rector's relationship with **Lord Blandamer** is marked by an anxiety about patronage — his hope that the lord "would have contributed handsomely to the restoration fund" (002-035) is disappointed, leaving him dependent on smaller donors. With **Westray**, he maintains professional cordiality. His dealings with **Mr. Sharnall** reveal the tension between the established clergy and the impoverished musician who serves the church.

## Significance

Canon Parkyn represents the institutional Church as it actually functioned in Edwardian England — less concerned with theology than with fabric, finance, and social order. His presence grounds the novel in the practical realities of ecclesiastical administration.

## Key Quotes
