# The Thirty-Year Silence of the Bells

## Overview

The silencing of Cullerne Minster's great bells — un-rung for thirty years due to fears of tower collapse — is a decades-long event that measures the tower's slow deterioration.

## Narrative Role

The bells have been silent for three decades: "them bells was never rung for thirty year" (002-040). This silence is itself a measure of decay — a wound in the community's acoustic identity.
