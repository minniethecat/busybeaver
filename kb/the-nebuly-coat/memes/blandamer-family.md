# The Blandamer Family

## Overview

The Blandamer family, whose coat of arms gives the novel its title, represents the apex of Cullerne's social hierarchy — an aristocratic lineage whose attenuated legitimacy drives the novel's central mystery.

## Role in the Novel

The Blandamer family's history is inscribed in the fabric of Cullerne Minster — in the stained glass of the transept window, in the Fording pews, in the heraldic devices that adorn the church. Their coat of arms — "barry nebuly of six, argent and vert" (002-050) — is the novel's central symbol. The family's legitimacy, their relationship to the town, and the question of the true heir form the substance of the inheritance plot.
