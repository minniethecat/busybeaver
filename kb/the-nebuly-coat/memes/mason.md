# The Mason

## Overview

The mason working on the Cullerne Minster restoration represents the practical craft knowledge that complements Westray's professional architectural expertise..

## Biography

The mason's craft knowledge provides a counterpoint to Westray's professional training. Where the architect reads the church through drawings and calculations, the mason reads it through stone and mortar — a tactile understanding born of hands-on work. His presence at the restoration site grounds the novel's architectural discourse in material reality. The mason represents a form of knowledge that the novel respects but that the class system undervalues — practical wisdom acquired through labour rather than education.

## Relationships

As a minor figure, interactions with major characters are limited but significant — each encounter revealing a facet of Cullerne life that the central characters alone cannot access.

## Significance

Minor characters in *The Nebuly Coat* collectively constitute the town's collective memory — a distributed knowledge of church, family, and history that no single authority can monopolise. Their testimony, scattered across brief appearances, provides the evidential foundation upon which the novel's revelations depend.
