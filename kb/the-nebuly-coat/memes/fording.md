# Fording

## Overview

Fording is the Blandamer family seat — a great country house whose "stately" presence can be seen "on a clear day from Cullerne" (008-023). It embodies the hereditary wealth and status that the novel's inheritance plot concerns, standing as both destination and symbol for those who, like Martin Joliffe, dreamed of claiming the Blandamer legacy.

## Description

The house is approached through a long avenue, entered through great gates bearing "the nebuly coat which was set over the great gates" (023-023) in elaborate ironwork. Its distance from Cullerne — a seven-mile drive from the station — reinforces the social distance between the Blandamers and the town. The interior, glimpsed during Westray's visits, is furnished with the accumulated possessions of generations.

## In the Novel

Fording serves as Lord Blandamer's seat of power and the site of crucial revelations. It is here that Westray is invited to dine, here that confidences are exchanged, and here that the portrait of the old lord — "the green bars of the nebuly coat" seeming to dance before Westray's eyes — presides over scenes of gathering tension (023-072).

Fording also represents what Martin Joliffe sought and never attained: legitimate possession of the Blandamer inheritance. The house is the material manifestation of the identity he craved, visible from Cullerne but forever out of reach.
