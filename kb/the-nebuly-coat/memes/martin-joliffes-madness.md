# Martin Joliffe's Madness and Death

## Overview

Martin Joliffe's obsessive belief that he was the rightful Blandamer heir — and his subsequent madness and death — is the tragic backstory that drives the novel's inheritance mystery.

## Narrative Role

Martin Joliffe's belief that he was "not a Joliffe at all, but a Blandamer" (003-052) consumed his life. His genealogical obsession — the "nebuly coat craze" — "seized him and crept over his mind, like the paralysis that crept over his body later on" (003-052). He died in poverty and delusion, leaving his papers as the documentary legacy of his obsession.
