# The Sexton

## Overview

The sexton of Cullerne Minster is a repository of local knowledge about the church, its bells, and the Blandamer family — a minor figure whose long memory makes him a crucial witness to the tower's history..

## Biography

The sexton has served at the church for decades, his memory stretching back to events that others have forgotten. His testimony about the bells — that they have been silent for thirty years (002-040) — provides crucial evidence about the tower's condition. He remembers "as if 'twere yesterday" the drowning of Martin Joliffe's parents in Cullerne Bay (002-045), connecting the church's physical history to the town's human tragedies. His warnings about the tower's instability, delivered in the broad Cullerne dialect that Falkner renders phonetically, carry the authority of lifelong observation. The sexton represents institutional memory in its most embodied form — a man whose knowledge of the church is written not in documents but in decades of daily presence within its walls.

## Relationships

As a minor figure, interactions with major characters are limited but significant — each encounter revealing a facet of Cullerne life that the central characters alone cannot access.

## Significance

Minor characters in *The Nebuly Coat* collectively constitute the town's collective memory — a distributed knowledge of church, family, and history that no single authority can monopolise. Their testimony, scattered across brief appearances, provides the evidential foundation upon which the novel's revelations depend.
