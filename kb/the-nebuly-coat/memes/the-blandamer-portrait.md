# The Portrait of the Old Lord Blandamer

## Overview

The portrait of the previous Lord Blandamer at Fording, whose penetrating grey eyes seem to watch the novel's climactic scenes, represents the persistence of the past into the present.

## In the Novel

The portrait hangs at Fording, its subject's eyes seeming to observe the final confrontation (023-072). "The green bars of the nebuly coat danced before Westray's eyes, till they seemed to live, to be again three wriggling caterpillars" (023-072).

## Significance

This work's role in the narrative and its symbolic resonance.
