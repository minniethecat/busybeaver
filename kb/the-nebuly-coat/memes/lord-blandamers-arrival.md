# Lord Blandamer's Arrival in Cullerne

## Overview

Lord Blandamer's dramatic appearance at Cullerne Minster — sitting in the Fording pews and revealing his identity only when challenged — marks the moment when the novel's central mystery acquires a living embodiment.

## Narrative Role

When a stranger sits in the Fording seats — reserved "for Lord Blandamer's family" (007-035) — the sexton protests, only to be told: "I will make room if Lord Blandamer brings his family" (007-036). The revelation of his identity transforms him from rumour to presence.
