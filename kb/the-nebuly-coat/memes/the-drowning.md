# The Drowning at Cullerne Bay

## Overview

The drowning of Martin Joliffe's parents in Cullerne Bay is the foundational tragedy of the Joliffe family — an event whose consequences ripple through the novel.

## Narrative Role

The drowning of Martin Joliffe's father and mother — "drowned together in Cullerne Bay" (002-045) — left the Joliffe children orphaned. The sexton remembers it "as if 'twere yesterday" (002-045), the event preserved in Cullerne's collective memory.
