# Bellevue Lodge

## Overview

Bellevue Lodge is the lodging house in Cullerne where Westray resides during the restoration. Kept by Miss Euphemia Joliffe, it serves as the novel's domestic centre — a space where the public drama of the Blandamer inheritance intersects with the private lives of the Joliffe family.

## Description

The house contains the crucial hidden object of the novel: a painting of the nebuly coat concealed beneath decorative flowers. Westray eventually recognises "the unusual moulding and the size of the picture" (023-058), understanding that what he took for decoration is in fact evidence. The house's modest scale and respectable shabbiness reflect the Joliffes' reduced circumstances.

## In the Novel

Bellevue Lodge becomes "a centre of attraction" (019-059) after Anastasia's engagement is announced, as Cullerne society executes its "volte face" toward the family. Miss Euphemia holds court here, discussing every detail of the engagement with visitors who had previously disdained a lodging-house keeper's daughter.

The house is also the repository of Martin Joliffe's papers — the genealogical research that forms the documentary heart of the Blandamer mystery. Miss Euphemia's guardianship of these papers, and of the hidden painting, gives this modest domestic space a significance that belies its humble appearance.
