# Saint Sepulchre's Church

## Overview

Saint Sepulchre's is the derelict church in the old quarter of Cullerne, distinguished by its heavy buttresses and its bell-turret. Though its congregation has gone, its chimes continue to play their melodies — "Bermondsey" and other tunes — marking the hours with mechanical fidelity.

## Description

The church is first seen by Westray on his walk through the old town with Sharnall. It has "mullioned windows with stone tracery, and a bell-turret at the west end; but its most marked feature was a row of heavy buttresses which shored up the side facing the road" (003-008). The buttresses, "built of brick" and forming triangles with the ground, give the building a wounded appearance — a church held upright by external supports.

## In the Novel

Saint Sepulchre's chimes are the novel's most persistent auditory motif. Their tunes — specified with documentary precision in the Appendix, complete with key signatures and time signatures — sound through the story, marking moments of tension and resolution. "The deep and slow melodious chimes of Saint Sepulchre told the hour" (012-074).

The church's condition — "It was not ruinous, but Ichabod was written on its walls" (003-023) — makes it a mirror of Cullerne Minster. Both churches are in decline, but where the minster has its restoration project, Saint Sepulchre has only its chimes, playing on into emptiness.
