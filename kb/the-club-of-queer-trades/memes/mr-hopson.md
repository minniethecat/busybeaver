# Mr. Hopson

Mr. Hopson is a junior employee in P. G. Northover's Adventure and Romance Agency, appearing briefly but memorably in the third section of "The Tremendous Adventures of Major Brown." He is introduced when Northover summons him to assist with Major Brown's paperwork, and his movements are characterized by an almost supernatural efficiency.

## Role in the Story

Hopson is first mentioned when Northover calls him into the office to greet Major Brown: "Mr Hopson," said Northover, "this is Major Brown. Will you please finish that thing for him I gave you this morning and bring it in?" (003-176). Hopson's response and demeanor mark him as a perfectly drilled subordinate — he acknowledges with a brief "Yes, sir" (003-178) and "vanished like lightning" (003-178), his exit as rapid as his entrance.

The speed and precision of Hopson's movements suggest a man thoroughly trained in the operational demands of Northover's peculiar business. While Northover continues conversing with Basil Grant and Major Brown, he remarks that he must "continue to work until Mr Hopson is ready" (003-180), implying that Hopson is entrusted with completing the administrative preparations for Brown's adventure package.

Hopson's return is equally theatrical: a knock at the door is followed "almost simultaneous with the turning of the handle, and Mr Hopson came in again with the same silent rapidity, placed a paper before his principal, and disappeared again" (003-188). The repeated emphasis on his speed — "vanished," "silent rapidity," "disappeared" — paints Hopson less as an individual personality than as a perfectly functioning cog in Northover's commercial machinery.

## Significance

As a minor character, Hopson functions primarily as atmospheric detail, reinforcing the quasi-industrial efficiency with which Northover manufactures adventures. His silent, rapid service mirrors the broader theme of the Club of Queer Trades: the transformation of eccentricity and romance into a systematized commercial enterprise. Like [[burrows|Burrows]] and [[james|James]] — fellow servants in other households — Hopson represents the anonymous labor that enables the extravagant schemes of his employer.
