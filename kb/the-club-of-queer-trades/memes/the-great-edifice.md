# The Great Edifice

## Location

The unnamed 'great edifice' where the Club of Queer Trades 'reigns' — an architectural metaphor for the institutional weight that legitimizes unconventional professions.

## In the Stories

- (001-020)

## Significance

The Great Edifice represents Chesterton's use of architectural imagery to convey institutional legitimacy — the club occupies the physical vocabulary of power even as it subverts every convention of respectable commerce.
