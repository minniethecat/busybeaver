# Professor Chick

**Professor Chick** is a minor but memorable member of [[the-club-of-queer-trades-org|the Club of Queer Trades]], mentioned in the framing chapters of G.K. Chesterton's 1905 collection. He appears only in the exposition that establishes the Club's governing principles, yet his presence serves as one of the collection's most effective punchlines.

## Role in the Club

Professor Chick's function in the narrative is to clarify the Club's one inviolable rule: that a queer trade must be a genuine commercial source of income, not a mere hobby. The narrator explains that "the Club would not receive a man simply because he chose to pass his days collecting broken sardine tins, unless he could drive a roaring trade in them. Professor Chick made that quite clear" (001-038).

This rule — that eccentricity alone is insufficient, and that a queer trade must be economically self-sustaining — is foundational to the Club's identity. It distinguishes the queer tradesman from the mere crank or hobbyist. The queer trade is not an escape from the ordinary world of work but a transformation of it: a profession so specialized that it appears absurd to outsiders while functioning as a genuine livelihood.

## The Unrevealed Trade

Chesterton deploys Professor Chick as a comic device through deliberate omission. Immediately after establishing Chick as the authority on the Club's commercial requirement, the narrator adds: "And when one remembers what Professor Chick's own new trade was, one doesn't know whether to laugh or cry" (001-039). The trade itself is never disclosed.

This deliberate lacuna is characteristic of Chesterton's narrative technique throughout the collection. By withholding the specific nature of Chick's trade — while simultaneously insisting that it is both commercially viable and emotionally overwhelming — Chesterton achieves a double effect: the reader's curiosity is inflamed, and the Club's defining paradox is reinforced. The queer trades are simultaneously ridiculous and serious, comic and dignified, impossible and practical.

## Symbolic Function

Professor Chick embodies the collection's central philosophical assertion: that the line between the absurd and the meaningful is thinner than conventional society admits. His unnamed trade — which makes one "not know whether to laugh or cry" — is a miniature of the entire collection's method, in which each story reveals a profession that initially appears ludicrous but is ultimately shown to answer a genuine human need.
