# Uxbridge Road

Uxbridge Road is a major arterial road in west London, running from Shepherd's Bush to Uxbridge. It appears in "The Eccentric Seclusion of the Old Lady" ([[ch016]]), where it forms part of a cab journey undertaken by [[basil-grant|Basil Grant]] and the narrator.

## In the Stories

In [[ch016]], Basil Grant and the narrator take a cab ride that traces a route through several London landmarks. The journey goes:

> across [[westminster-bridge|Westminster Bridge]], through [[trafalgar-square|Trafalgar Square]], along Piccadilly, and up the Uxbridge Road.

Basil says "scarcely a word" during this journey, and the sequence of locations — from central London westward along Uxbridge Road — traces a path away from the city's heart toward its quieter periphery. This movement mirrors the story's theme of retreat and seclusion: the farther they travel, the closer they come to the hidden truth Basil is about to reveal.

## Significance

Chesterton names Uxbridge Road without elaboration, trusting his London readers to recognize it. In 1905, the road was already a well-known thoroughfare connecting the western suburbs to central London. Its inclusion in the cab route roots the otherwise improbable adventure in verifiable geography — a characteristic Chesterton technique.
