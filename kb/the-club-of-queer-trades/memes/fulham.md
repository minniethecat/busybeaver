# Fulham

Fulham is a district in west London, situated on the north bank of the River Thames. In G. K. Chesterton's *The Club of Queer Trades*, Fulham appears as the home of a dentist who attends one of the Club's extraordinary dinner gatherings.

## In the Stories

During the dinner party in "The Noticeable Conduct of Professor Chadd," the narrator describes the assembled company, noting a "small dentist from Fulham" among the guests (015-015). The dentist is characterized as being, "in all reasonable certainty," precisely like every other dentist from Fulham (015-016) — an observation that plays on the Chestertonian tension between individuality and type.

This passing reference exemplifies Chesterton's method of using London geography to sketch social character. The mention of Fulham is not elaborated, but it places the dentist within a recognizable social and geographical context, lending verisimilitude to the fantastic events of the Club dinner.

## Significance

Like [[clapham|Clapham]] and other suburban districts mentioned in the collection, Fulham represents the unremarkable, respectable London from which the Club draws its members. The "small dentist from Fulham" embodies the [[ordinary-life|ordinary citizen]] whose hidden eccentricity — the prerequisite for Club membership — lies concealed beneath a surface of suburban conventionality.
