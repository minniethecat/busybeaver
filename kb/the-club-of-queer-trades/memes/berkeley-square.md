# Berkeley Square

**Berkeley Square** is a garden square in the Mayfair district of London's West End, appearing in "The Painful Fall of a Great Reputation" as the destination toward which [[basil-grant|Basil Grant]] leads the narrator on a mysterious nocturnal walk.

## In the Story

Berkeley Square features in a characteristic Basil Grant episode. After the dinner party at the Beaumont household, Basil declares that he must visit a man in Berkeley Square — a "very good man," as he describes him, whose nature remains tantalizingly obscure to the narrator. The walk through London at night, from the Embankment to Mayfair, is presented as a pilgrimage of sorts, with Berkeley Square as its terminus.

The choice of Berkeley Square is significant. Mayfair was, and remains, one of London's most exclusive residential districts. A "very good man" living in Berkeley Square would be a man of substance and standing — precisely the sort of person whose goodness, in Chesterton's paradoxical universe, might take the most unexpected forms.

## The Mayfair Context

Berkeley Square sits at the heart of Mayfair, a district synonymous with aristocratic wealth and social power. The square itself, laid out in the mid-eighteenth century, is surrounded by elegant Georgian townhouses that in Chesterton's time were occupied by some of London's wealthiest families. The plane trees at its centre, which survive to the present day, give the square its distinctive character.

For Basil Grant to lead the narrator "in silence" toward Berkeley Square after the dinner party at the Beaumonts' is to move deeper into the world of aristocratic London. The journey from [[westminster|Westminster]] through [[st-johns-wood|St John's Wood]] to Mayfair traces a social ascent — from the political centre through the comfortable professional classes to the highest echelon of London society. That the resolution of the story takes place here is thematically significant: the "painful fall" of Sir Walter's reputation is, after all, a fall from precisely this social height.
