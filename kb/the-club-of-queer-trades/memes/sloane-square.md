# Sloane Square

Sloane Square is a public square in the [[london-in-the-queer-trades|Chelsea]] district of London, mentioned in two stories of *The Club of Queer Trades*. It is associated with a restaurant that becomes a recurring destination and with the green omnibuses that serve as transport in the tales.

## In the Stories

### The Eccentric Seclusion of the Old Lady (ch019)

In [[ch019]], after a series of events, the characters consider taking a green omnibus to a restaurant in Sloane Square. [[basil-grant|Basil Grant]] offhandedly mentions the destination, and his companions react with a mix of bemusement and irritation at his seemingly casual direction.

### The Strange Crime of John Boulnois (ch022)

In [[ch022]], Sloane Square appears again as the intended destination of the green omnibus. The narrator notes that they are "too late for the theatre in Sloane Square," but not too late for the restaurant — the same establishment referenced in the earlier story.

## Significance

Sloane Square serves a dual function in Chesterton's London geography. It is both an anchor in real, recognizable London and a marker of the characters' social world — the theatre and restaurant of Sloane Square belong to a milieu of modest gentility. The green omnibus that connects it to other parts of the city becomes almost a leitmotif, appearing at crucial narrative junctures.

Chesterton's use of Sloane Square exemplifies his technique of grounding improbable adventures in precisely named London locations, lending the fantastic an air of the everyday.
