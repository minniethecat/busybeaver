## Overview

**San Samuele** is a church and parish in the [[san-marco|San Marco]] sestiere of Venice. In the novel, it is noted as the location of the studio of [[paolo-veronese|Paolo Veronese]], the great Venetian painter, and as the neighborhood of the Giustiniani palace. The church serves as an anchor point for the novel's artistic and aristocratic worlds.

## Location

San Samuele stands near the Grand Canal in the San Marco district, close to the [[palazzo-ducale|Palazzo Ducale]] and the political heart of Venice. Its campo opens onto a narrow calle that leads toward Campo San Stefano, another major square of the sestiere. The traghetto of San Samuele provided an additional Grand Canal crossing, convenient for those moving between San Marco and San Polo.

## In the Narrative

San Samuele appears in the novel primarily through its association with the Veronese studio and the Giustiniani family. The church's parish encompasses both the artistic workshop where great paintings are created and the noble palace where political decisions are made — a microcosm of Venice's intersecting worlds.

> [008-004]In the studio of the Veronese in San Samuele, the _Madonna del Sorriso_ was nearing completion, and the master worked with a devotion that his pupils had never seen before.
> — sec08, PN 008-004

> [021-005]Coming out of the narrow calle from San Samuele into the Campo San Stefano, the Giustiniani palace rose before them in the evening light.
> — sec21, PN 021-005

## History

The Church of San Samuele dates to the 11th century, with significant Gothic-era renovations. It is dedicated to the biblical prophet Samuel. By the late 16th century, its parish was one of the wealthier in Venice, encompassing the homes and workshops of artists and nobles.

## Key Passages

> [008-004]In the studio of the Veronese in San Samuele, while the _Madonna del Sorriso_ neared its final form, Fra Paolo himself came to sit for the master.
> — sec08, PN 008-004

This passage links San Samuele to one of the novel's central artistic subplots — the creation of the Madonna del Sorriso (Madonna of the Smile), a painting that embodies the novel's themes of beauty, devotion, and the intersection of art with faith.

## Associated Figures

- **[[paolo-veronese|Paolo Veronese]]**: Maintained his studio in the parish of San Samuele, where key scenes of artistic creation unfold.
- **[[marcantonio-giustiniani|Marcantonio Giustiniani]]**: His family palace is in the San Samuele neighborhood, connecting the parish to Venice's political elite.
