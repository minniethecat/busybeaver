# Bragadin

Bragadin was a Venetian nobleman and senator, remembered for his audacious promise to manufacture gold for the [[venetian-senate|Signoria]] — a claim publicly denounced by [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo Sarpi]].

## The Summons

Bragadin resided in a palace on the [[giudecca|Giudecca]]. One day he was "summoned from his palace on the [[giudecca|Giudecca]] to make his promised gold for the [[signoria|Signoria]]" [038-019]. He passed through the [[merceria|Merceria]] in procession, attended by "his two familiars, carrying his alembics and the包包 of his mysteries" [038-019] — the apparatus of an alchemist.

The spectacle drew crowds who surged through the streets "struggling with each other lest they should lose the sight as he passed through [[piazza-san-marco|the Piazza]]" [038-020].

## Fra Paolo's Denunciation

As Bragadin passed through [[piazza-san-marco|the Piazza]] [[san-marco|San Marco]], [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo]]'s voice cut through the crowd — "cold, and scornful, and low, but no man lost the words":

> "Thou art wearied in the multitude of thy counsels. Let now the astrologers, the star-gazers, the monthly prognosticators stand up and save thee from these things that shall come upon thee!" [038-020]

This public rebuke, couched in scriptural language, stopped the crowd's excitement. The people "looked aghast" at the condemnation [038-020].

## Exposure

The skepticism proved warranted. [[leonardo-donato|Leonardo Donato]], then of the Council, "walked about the chamber giving sign of much displeasure while Bragadin made his gold" [039-002]. The following day, [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo]] was summoned before the [[signoria|Signoria]] — "being the only man who hath dared speak his mind before the [[signoria|Signoria]] had proved the worthlessness of Bragadin's promise" [039-002].

The "fine gold-maker" was forced to exchange "his palace on the [[giudecca|Giudecca]] for a fine apartment in the Pozzi" [039-002] — the Pozzi being the lower chambers of the prison, a sharp reversal of fortune.
