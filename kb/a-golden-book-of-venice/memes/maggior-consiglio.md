## Overview

The **Maggior Consiglio** (Great Council) was the supreme governing body of the [[republic-of-venice|Venetian Republic]], the sovereign assembly of all patrician males whose families were inscribed in the *[[golden-book|Libro d'Oro]]* ([[golden-book|Golden Book]]). It was the largest of Venice's governing councils and the ultimate source of all political authority — every other council, including the [[venetian-senate|Senate]], the [[collegio|Collegio]], and the [[council-of-ten|Council of Ten]], derived its powers from the Great Council.

Membership in the Maggior Consiglio was hereditary, not elective, a principle formalized by the *Serrata* (the "Closing") of 1297, which fixed the patriciate as a closed caste. By the late sixteenth century, the Council comprised roughly 1,200 to 2,500 members.

## Structure

The Maggior Consiglio met in the Great Council Hall (*Sala del Maggior Consiglio*) of the [[palazzo-ducale|Palazzo Ducale]], one of the largest rooms in Europe. Its membership included every adult male of the patrician families — those whose names appeared in the [[golden-book|Golden Book]] of the [[venetian-nobility|Venetian nobility]].

The title *di Maggior Consiglio* signified membership in this body and was a mark of the highest social and political standing. As one character reflects:

> [027-003] immediate seat of right in the Maggior Consiglio.

The honor of admission was so significant that it could provoke intense emotion when conferred — or denied.

## Role in the Narrative

The Maggior Consiglio appears at critical junctures of the novel's political drama, functioning both as a physical setting for scenes of state ceremony and as the institutional embodiment of Venetian sovereignty.

The protagonist [[marcantonio-giustiniani|Marcantonio Giustiniani]] is explicitly identified as a member:

> [019-002] Marcantonio Giustiniani, di Maggior Consiglio, as Secretary to the Venetian Republic

His diplomatic report is delivered before the assembled Council:

> [019-004] render his report before our Maggior Consiglio — an oration diplomatic and of moment.

At the novel's climactic moments, the Council assembles with all its nobles:

> [048-022] all the nobles of the Maggior Consiglio and many of the high officials.

## Powers and Functions

The Great Council was the ultimate sovereign body. Its powers included:

- **Electing the [[doge|Doge]]** — though the process was indirect and elaborate, the Council was the electoral foundation
- **Electing all magistrates and officials** — every office in the Republic was filled by election from within the Council
- **Passing legislation** — major laws required the Council's approval
- **Constitutional authority** — the Council could amend the fundamental laws of the Republic

In moments of national crisis, the Council was convened alongside other bodies:

> [029-001] the College, the Senate, and the Great Council will presently be called to vote.

## Key Passages

The Council's authority is repeatedly invoked as the legitimizing force behind Venetian governance. Admission was a closely guarded privilege:

> [008-010] claim admission to the Great Council as a noble, born in lawful wedlock.

The formal language of Council authorization runs through the Republic's decrees:

> [017-025] The permission of the Maggior Consiglio, under favor of this imperious necessity, was forthwith granted.
