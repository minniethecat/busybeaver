# The Piombi

The Piombi (*I Piombi*, "the Leads") were the notorious prison cells located directly under the lead roof of the [[ducal-palace|Ducal Palace]] in Venice. Their name derives from the lead (*piombo*) roofing above, which made the cells suffocatingly hot in summer and bitterly cold in winter.

## Function

The Piombi served as a prison for those accused of crimes against the State, under the jurisdiction of the [[council-of-ten|Council of Ten]]. Prisoners were held there pending investigation and trial by the Republic's secretive tribunals.

> The guards bore their burden from the cloister to the gondola of the prisons of the Piombi.[040-005]

## Notable Prisoners

The Piombi housed various figures who fell afoul of the Venetian state, including those accused in assassination plots. Fra Paolo Sarpi's would-be assassins were destined for these cells after their plot was uncovered by the Council of Ten's network of informants.

## In Context

The Piombi were one of several prison facilities in Venice, distinct from the *Pozzi* (the Wells) in the lower levels of the palace. Together with the public executions between the columns of [[san-marco|San Marco]] and [[san-teodoro|San Teodoro]], the Piombi represented the coercive underside of Venetian justice—the dark complement to the gilded splendor of the Republic's public face.
