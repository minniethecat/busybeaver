## Overview

The **Avvogadori di Comun** (also rendered *Avvogadori di Commun* or *Avogadori di Comun*) were the three state attorneys of the [[republic-of-venice|Venetian Republic]], charged with safeguarding the legality of all governmental proceedings. Their office sat within the [[palazzo-ducale|Palazzo Ducale]], adjacent to the chambers of the [[doge|Doge]] and the higher councils, reflecting their role as the legal conscience of the Republic.

Among their most significant duties was the custody of the *[[golden-book|Libro d'Oro]]* — the [[golden-book|Golden Book]] in which the names of all noble families were inscribed — making them the gatekeepers of the [[venetian-nobility|Venetian patriciate]].

## Structure and Functions

The three Avvogadori were elected from the patriciate and served as the Republic's legal watchdogs. Their powers included:

- **Prosecuting cases** before the [[maggior-consiglio|Maggior Consiglio]], [[venetian-senate|Senate]], and [[council-of-ten|Council of Ten]]
- **Overseeing elections** to ensure procedural legality
- **Maintaining the [[golden-book|Golden Book]]** — registering legitimate noble births and safeguarding the integrity of the patriciate

As legal officers, their authority was formidable:

> [013-011] witnessed by the three solemn Avvogadori — those officers of the law who keep the Golden Book.

## Role in the Narrative

The Avvogadori appear at crucial legal and genealogical moments. Their custody of the [[golden-book|Golden Book]] makes them central to questions of noble legitimacy — a recurring theme in a novel concerned with birth, honor, and belonging:

> [008-009] The *Avvogadori* do not keep my 'Libro d'Oro' — as if that were a thing to deny!

Their chambers are the site where critical documents are filed and where the [[doge|Doge]] receives petitions:

> [017-021] the adjoining chambers of the Avvogadori and laid before the Doge.

The legal weight of their office is invoked in moments of formal procedure:

> [017-018] required the summoning of the Avvogadori di Commun, though it was uttered in disdain.

## Key Passages

The solemnity and gravity of the Avvogadori's office is captured in their formal presence:

> [013-009] reached the chambers of the *Avvogadori*, where sat the keepers of the Golden Book.

The recording function of the office extends to personal affairs:

> [043-018] shall be found filed with the Avvogadori del Commun.
