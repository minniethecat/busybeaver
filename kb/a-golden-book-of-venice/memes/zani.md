## Overview

Patriarch Zani was the Patriarch of Venice whose death during a diplomatic dispute with the [[curia|Holy See]] created a vacancy that was filled by [[vendramin|Patriarch Vendramin]]. His case became a flashpoint in the ongoing tension between Venice and Rome over ecclesiastical appointments—a prelude to the [[papal-interdict|Papal Interdict of 1606]]–1607.

## In the Narrative

Zani appears in the narrative only through recollection. [[marcantonio-[[giustinian-giustiniani|giustinian]]i|Marcantonio Giustiniani]], having recently returned from Rome where he served as Secretary to the Venetian Ambassador, recounts to the assembled nobles how Zani was summoned to Rome by Pope Clement VIII to receive his benediction—only to be subjected to an unexpected examination that appeared to undermine Venice's ancient right of appointing its own Patriarch. The Republic viewed this cross-questioning as a deliberate humiliation. Zani's death shortly afterward left the see vacant, and Venice appointed Vendramin as his successor, setting the stage for further conflict with Rome.
