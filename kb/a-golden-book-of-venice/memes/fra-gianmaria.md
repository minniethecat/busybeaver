# Fra Gianmaria

Fra Gianmaria was a [[Servite]] friar in Venice, best known as the childhood mentor of [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo Sarpi]] and later the Superior of the Servite monastery.

## Role as Mentor

Fra Gianmaria was the teacher of the young Pierino (the future [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo Sarpi]]) from the time the boy passed beyond the rudimentary instruction of Don Ambrogio [002-020]. The friar was "known throughout Venice for his learning" [002-015] and held great confidence in his pupil's abilities, "proud of the confidence of the learned friar in her child" [002-021].

## The Prodigy's Teacher

Under Fra Gianmaria's instruction, the boy's intellect developed at an extraordinary pace. The friar often "came upon some new surprise, for he perceived that the boy's mind held some hidden spring of knowledge which was to him unfathomable" [003-003]. On one occasion, Gianmaria admitted to [[fra-giulio|Fra Giulio]] that he had asked the boy a question to which he himself did not know the answer, and the child "hath so lucidly responded that his words have carried with them the conviction that he had made a discovery" [003-004].

Yet Gianmaria was also protective. When other Servite friars proclaimed the boy a "modern wonder of a Child in the Temple," Fra Gianmaria "hushed them, and was afraid" [003-003] — recognizing the dangers of excessive attention.

## As Superior

In later years, Fra Gianmaria became the Superior of the Servite monastery. He is addressed as "Father Gianmaria, who was now the Superior" [012-003] and commanded authority over the friars, including the now-famous [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo]]. When the Chief of the [[council-of-ten|Council of Ten]] visited the monastery, Fra Gianmaria received him "in formal state, with a secretary and attendants and an officer of the guards" [039-012].

## Defense of Fra Paolo

When [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo]]'s safety was threatened and the convent court was in uproar - with cries of "Our [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo]]!" and "Our Consultore!" — it was Fra Gianmaria who ordered a Te Deum to be sung "for the safety of [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo]]" [040-006], a gesture that both celebrated his former pupil and calmed the agitated friars.

## Relationship with Fra Paolo

The mentor-student relationship between Fra Gianmaria and [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo]] evolved over decades. While Gianmaria had occasionally been stern with the younger friar — retorting "with much asperity" to Pierino's ideas [011-003] — their bond endured. Fra Gianmaria's influence shaped the intellectual foundations of the man who would become Venice's most celebrated consultore.
