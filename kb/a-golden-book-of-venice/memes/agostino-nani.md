# Agostino Nani

Agostino Nani was the Venetian ambassador to the [[curia|Holy See]] during the [[papal-interdict-proclamation|Papal Interdict]] crisis, serving as the Republic's principal diplomatic channel to [[pope-paul-v|Pope Paul V]].

## Role During the Interdict

While the [[giustinian-giustiniani|Giustinian]]i family and other Venetians were still in Rome, "Signor Agostino Nani, watchful as an ambassador went to and fro" [022-014], monitoring the escalating tension between Venice and the [[curia|Holy See]].

When the crisis reached its peak, it was Ambassador Nani who delivered the Republic's formal replies to the Pope's demands. Day after day, the Senate sat in session to hear "its delinquencies rehearsed in the words of [[pope-paul-v|Paul V]], by the graphic pen of his Excellency Agostino Nani, Ambassador from the Republic to the [[curia|Holy See]]" [024-012]. His dispatches detailed the "ceaseless repetitions of demand on the part of the Sovereign Pontiff" met with "ceaseless repetitions of refusal, most deferently couched, from the courtly representative of the offending power" [024-012].

## The Senate's Vote

When the Senate resolved to resist the Pope's demands, letters were addressed to "the imperious Paul to make an end of his demands, declaring that to the Republic alone belonged the right to govern her own in matters temporal" [025-003]. The vote passed "without dissent of a single voice," and Nani was charged with delivering this defiant message to the Pontiff.

Nani "presently delivered in an audience with his Holiness" [026-009] the Republic's position, though the Pope "had already superbly made other plans" [025-003].

## Diplomatic Significance

Agostino Nani occupied one of the most sensitive diplomatic posts in Europe at a moment of acute crisis. His role required the delicate balance of representing Venetian defiance while maintaining the courtly forms of deference expected at the papal court. His "graphic pen" [024-012] provided the Senate with detailed intelligence from Rome, shaping Venice's strategic response to the Interdict.
