## Overview

The **Nicolotti** were one of the two great working-class factions (*fazioni*) of Venice, alongside their rivals the [[castellani|Castellani]]. Based in the western sestiere around the church of San Nicolò dei Mendicoli, the Nicolotti were fishermen, gondoliers, and artisans organized under a *[[piero-salin|gastaldo grande]]* — a chief elected from their own ranks who governed their internal affairs, managed their treasury, and led them in the celebrated bridge battles and regattas that punctuated Venetian public life.

## Structure

The Nicolotti were organized hierarchically under their gastaldo, who commanded the loyalty of the faction with near-feudal devotion. Members wore the distinctive black cap and sash:

> [004-001] the black cap and sash of the "Nicolotti" accentuated the lines of the figure.

The gastaldo managed a faction treasury, commanded the *bancali* (bench-leaders), and represented Nicolotti interests before the Republic's authorities. The role carried genuine prestige:

> [004-017] "It is a good thing to be a governor of the Nicolotti!"

The faction's identity was bound to its patron saints — San Nicolò and San Raffaele — and its banner bore sacramental words:

> [036-011] the unfurled banner of the Nicolotti, with the sacramental words.

## Role in the Narrative

The Nicolotti feature prominently through their gastaldo, [[piero-salin|Piero Salin]], who serves as a bridge figure between the common people and the patrician world of the novel. The faction appears in scenes of public spectacle — regattas, bridge battles — and in moments of political tension when the loyalty of the common people becomes decisive.

The Nicolotti's rivalry with the [[castellani|Castellani]] is a constant backdrop:

> [036-005] The Nicolotti knew it well when, a few months later, their rivals had the humiliation of seeing their own brave colors trailing in the dust.

Yet the narrative also shows their capacity to unite against the Republic's enemies:

> [048-014] Nicolotti and Castellani — *allies*, since they never had been conquered.

The faction's treasury and loyalty prove instrumental when the Republic needs popular support:

> [045-002] from the treasury of the Nicolotti, which was in his keeping.

## Key Passages

The deep loyalty of the Nicolotti to their gastaldo is vividly rendered:

> [036-012] who had all their lives been Nicolotti of the Nicolotti — a Venetian of the Venetians.

Their battle-cry echoes through the novel's public scenes:

> [036-017] cries of "the black cap! The Nicolotti! Viva San Nicolò!"

The gastaldo's authority extends even into personal affairs:

> [042-006] "Is not my Marco?" A gondola of the Nicolotti detached itself from a group.
