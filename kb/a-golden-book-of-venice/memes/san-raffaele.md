## Overview

San Raffaele, formally San Raffaele Arcangelo, is a church in the Dorsoduro sestiere. Together with [[san-nicolo|San Nicolò]], it served as the spiritual home of the Nicolotti, the gondolier faction whose fierce rivalry with the Castellani defined working-class life in Renaissance Venice. The traghetto of San Nicolò è San Raffaele was one of the ferry crossings by which Venetian gondoliers earned their livelihood.

## Location

The church of San Raffaele stands in Dorsoduro, not far from [[san-nicolo|San Nicolò dei Mendicoli]], in the western part of the sestiere. The traghetto (ferry crossing) bearing the combined name "San Nicolò è San Raffaele" linked the two parishes and served the working people of this quarter.

## In the Narrative

San Raffaele is always mentioned in connection with [[san-nicolo|San Nicolò]], reflecting the pairing of the two churches as patron shrines of the Nicolotti faction. When [[piero-salin|Piero Salin]] is elected gastaldo grande, the ducal secretary formally consigns to him the standard of San Nicolò as proof that he is "the chief gastaldo and head of the people of San Nicolò and San Raffaele." During the jubilant celebrations that follow his election, the crowd shouts for both San Nicolò and San Raffaele, underscoring the twin loyalties of the Nicolotti.

The traghetto of San Nicolò è San Raffaele also appears in a devotional context, when a votive offering is sent "pel luminar al Madonna"—for the lighting of the Madonna's shrine.

## Legacy

The church of San Raffaele Arcangelo remains an active parish church in Dorsoduro. Though the Nicolotti-Castellani rivalry has long since faded into history, the pairing of San Raffaele with San Nicolò in the novel reflects the authentic parish-based organization of Venetian gondolier society, where religious devotion and civic identity were inseparably intertwined.
