## Overview

The Savii (singular _Savio_), meaning "Wise Men," were senior magistrates of the [[republic-of-venice|Republic of Venice]] who formed part of the [[collegio|Collegio]], the Republic's executive cabinet. They were charged with the preparation of diplomatic dispatches and sat on the dais alongside the [[doge|Doge]] and the six Ducal Councillors during sessions of the [[maggior-consiglio|Maggior Consiglio]] and the Senate. Their commanding presence in the Council Chamber reflected their role as the Republic's voice in foreign affairs.

## Structure

The Savii were organized into several boards with distinct portfolios:

- **Savii Grandi** (Savi Grandi) — the senior board, responsible for general policy and diplomatic correspondence
- **Savii di Terraferma** — overseers of Venice's mainland (Terraferma) dominions
- **Savii agli Ordini** — responsible for maritime and naval affairs

Collectively, the Savii sat with the [[doge|Doge]] and his six Councillors on the _[[collegio|Collegio]]_, the steering committee that set the Senate's agenda. Their deliberations shaped the Republic's response to crises, including the [[papal-interdict|Papal Interdict of 1606]]–1607.

## Powers and Functions

The Savii's primary function was diplomatic: they drafted the Republic's official correspondence to foreign courts, the Papacy, and ambassadors abroad. During the Interdict crisis, it was the Savii who "prepared the reply to these communications from his Holiness," a task requiring legal precision and political tact.

Beyond diplomacy, the Savii wielded significant influence in the Council Chamber. Their seats on the dais placed them "close under the eyes" of the [[doge|Doge]] and the [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Chief Counsellor]], allowing them to monitor the temper of the assembly and signal the government's position. A "terrible glance from more than one of the commanding Savii" was sufficient to silence dissent, as demonstrated when a senator rashly invoked the _Serrata Consiglio_ during a heated debate.

The Savii also had archival authority: they could demand the production of historical decrees from the Republic's archives to settle legal disputes, as when a fourteenth-century marriage decree was summoned to verify a nobleman's claim before the [[maggior-consiglio|Maggior Consiglio]].

## In the Narrative

The Savii appear at critical junctures in the novel's depiction of Venetian governance. During [[giustinian-giustiniani|Giustinian Giustiniani]]'s dramatic appeal to the [[maggior-consiglio|Maggior Consiglio]], the Savii listened with "surprise not unmingled with resentment" as the young nobleman asserted knowledge of state matters. One Savio, seated near the dais, attempted to sway the assembly by whispering "_Remember the Serrata Consiglio_," only to be silenced by the stern gaze of his colleagues.

Later, during the Republic's confrontation with [[pope-paul-v|Pope Paul V]], the Savii drafted the diplomatic reply delivered by [[agostino-nani|Agostino Nani]] in Rome — an epistle that assured the Holy Father of Venice's loyalty while quietly maintaining that "the Republic had transgressed no right in making laws for her own independent civil government." At the climactic Senate vote on the Interdict, "the faces of the [[doge|Doge]] and of his Savii recorded the sense of security with which it was needful to impress the assembly."

Among the Savii sat [[giustinian-giustiniani|Giustinian Giustiniani]], a Chief of the [[council-of-ten|Council of Ten]], who found himself silenced by conflicting emotions when his son outmaneuvered him before the [[maggior-consiglio|Maggior Consiglio]].

## Notable Members

- **[[giustinian-giustiniani|Giustinian Giustiniani]]** — a Chief of the [[council-of-ten|Council of Ten]] who sat among the Savii during the [[maggior-consiglio|Maggior Consiglio]] debate over his son's marriage. His position on the dais placed him directly under the watchful gaze of the [[doge|Doge]] and [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Chief Counsellor]], rendering him powerless to intervene.
