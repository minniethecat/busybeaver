# Giant Stairway (Scala dei Giganti)

The Giant Stairway (*Scala dei Giganti*, or Giants' Staircase) is the monumental staircase in the courtyard of the [[ducal-palace|Ducal Palace]] of Venice. It is named for the colossal statues of Mars (god of war) and Neptune (god of the sea) that flank its ascent—symbols of Venice's dominion over land and sea.

## Ceremonial Role

The Giant Stairway was the setting for one of the most important rituals of the Venetian Republic: the coronation of the Doge. At the head of the landing, the newly elected noble received the *beretta* (the ducal cap) and formally assumed the rank of *Serenissimo* (Most Serene).

Above the landing, the winged Lion of St. Mark presided over this ceremony, symbolizing the divine and civic sanction under which the Doge received his authority.

> Up and down the splendid stairway that opened immediately from the Porta della Carta the Venetians came and went—nobles old and young; the people, bringing wrongs to be adjusted, or favors to be granted, or some secret message for the terrible Bocca di Leone.[013-007]

## Symbolism

The pairing of Mars and Neptune embodied the dual foundation of Venetian power: military might and maritime dominion. Together with the Lion of St. Mark enthroned above, the statuary on the Giant Stairway constituted a program of political theology that every Venetian noble absorbed simply by ascending those steps.

## In the Narrative

When [[marcantonio-giustiniani|Marcantonio Giustiniani]] came to the Ducal Palace to be enrolled in the [[maggior-consiglio|Great Council]], he ascended this staircase under the watchful eyes of the gods of war and sea, acutely conscious of the weight of Venetian tradition pressing upon him.[013-003]
