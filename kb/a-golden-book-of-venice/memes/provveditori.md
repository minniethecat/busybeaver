## Overview

The Provveditori (superintendents or commissioners) were magistrates of the [[la-serenissima|Venetian Republic]] charged with specific administrative and regulatory functions. The novel mentions two types: the **Provveditori al Comun**, who collected government taxes and were "viewed with jealous eyes by the gondoliers," and the **Provveditori alle Pompe**, who enforced sumptuary laws governing luxury, display, and public extravagance.

## Structure

The Provveditori operated as specialized boards rather than a single unified body. Each board had a defined jurisdiction:

- **Provveditori al Comun** — tax collectors responsible for gathering government revenues from the population
- **Provveditori alle Pompe** — sumptuary officials who regulated luxury spending, particularly on clothing, gondola decorations, and public festivities

The Provveditori answered to the [[venetian-senate|Venetian Senate]] and the [[collegio|Collegio]]. Their authority extended across all social classes, though the enforcement of sumptuary laws could be suspended by special dispensation for state occasions — as when the ambassadors' [[gondola|gondolas]] received a dispensation from the Provveditori alle Pompe for the annual Arsenal fête.

## In the Narrative

The Provveditori al Comun appear in the novel through the complaints of a young gondolier ferrying the painter [[paolo-veronese|Paolo Veronese]]. Mistaking the splendidly dressed artist for a government official, the gondolier worries aloud: "It is the first time I have the honor of conducting his Excellency; he is perhaps of the Provveditori al Comun?" These officials, he explains, collected taxes that burdened the working gondoliers already struggling with guild fees, license costs, and mandatory contributions to the Church.

The Provveditori alle Pompe are mentioned during the description of the [[doge|Doge]]'s annual visit to [[arsenal-of-venice|the Arsenal]]. For this grand occasion, a dispensation from the sumptuary laws was granted: "the Signori, 'i provveditori alle pompe' have nothing to say, for there is a dispensation! the velvets and satins and golden fringes — it will be a true glimpse of the _paradiso_!"
