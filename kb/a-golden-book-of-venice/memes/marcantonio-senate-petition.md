## Background

[[marcantonio-giustiniani|Marcantonio Giustiniani]], the only son of the powerful Giustiniani family, had fallen in love with [[marina-[[girolamo-magagnati|magagnati]]|Marina Magagnati]], the daughter of a master glassmaker of [[murano|Murano]]. Though the Giustiniani were "like princes in Venice," Marcantonio was resolved to marry Marina despite the social distance between their houses (016-005).

## The Summons

The summons from the [[council-of-ten|Council of Ten]] was presented with ceremony on the night of the fête at Ca' Giustiniani. Marcantonio was grateful for the support of Paolo Cagliari ([[paolo-veronese|Veronese]]), who accompanied him to the [[collegio|Sala di Collegio]] in the Ducal Palace. The young noble saw this as an opportunity to present his petition for Marina's recognition before the [[signoria|Signoria]] (016-015).

The industries of Murano had long enjoyed the Republic's "fostering care," and the gift-piece of crystal was deemed "of quite sufficient importance to occupy the attention of the senators" (016-020). It was a rare honor for a young noble to be summoned before the Collegio to receive formal commendation (016-021).

## The Petition

Marcantonio resolved to make his maiden speech before the august body a plea for Marina's admission to the favor of the Signoria. "Already fortune had been kind to him beyond his hopes, and, with the daring of youth, he was resolved to claim the possible" (016-015).

His father, Senator [[giustinian-giustiniani|Giustinian Giustiniani]], sat among the senators in the chamber — stern and unaware of his son's intention. Marcantonio chose not to weaken his courage by any previous contest with his father (016-016).

## Outcome

The event was significant for placing Marcantonio's personal petition within a formal state proceeding. His public declaration before the Signoria was a decisive step toward securing recognition for Marina and demonstrated his willingness to risk political standing for love.
