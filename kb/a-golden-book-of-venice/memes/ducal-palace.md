# Ducal Palace (Palazzo Ducale)

The Ducal Palace (*Palazzo Ducale*) is the seat of Venetian government and the residence of the Doge, standing at the edge of [[piazza-san-marco|Piazza San Marco]] facing the Bacino di San Marco. Its rose-tinted Gothic façade and elaborate sculptural program make it one of the most iconic buildings in Venice.

## Architecture and Layout

The palace features a magnificent sea façade of rose-tinted walls and delicate Gothic tracery. The courtyard centers on two superb bronze well-heads, magnificently wrought. The [[giant-stairway|Giant Stairway]] (*Scala dei Giganti*), guarded by statues of Mars and Neptune, ascends from the courtyard to the state apartments. At the head of the landing, newly elected Doges received the *beretta* and swore their coronation oath.[013-007]

The palace gate is framed by elaborate allegorical sculpture: Justice enthroned above between the Lions of St. Mark, attended by Courage, Prudence, Hope, and Charity. The [[porta-della-carta|Porta della Carta]] provides the ceremonial entrance from the Piazzetta side.[013-005]

## Governmental Functions

The Ducal Palace housed the principal organs of Venetian government:

- The chambers of the *Avvogadori di Comun*, keepers of the [[golden-book|Golden Book]]
- The Sala di Collegio, where the [[collegio|Full College]] met with the Doge
- The offices of the [[council-of-ten|Council of Ten]]
- Secretaries' quarters where scribes worked far into the night sifting and preparing papers for the Councils

> Under the deep shadow of the gateway the business of the Ducal Palace was already progressing. Secretaries at their desks were preparing papers for discussion, while their assistants came and went with messages from the various departments of the great body of workers within the palace; they were too absorbed to look up as this Chief of the Ten passed them, so oblivious were they of anything but their duty that the stir about them left them serene and undisturbed.[013-005]

## Symbolism and Ceremony

The palace was a stage for Venetian civic ritual. The Doge viewed processions and fêtes from the splendid balcony overlooking the Piazzetta. The palace's sculptural decorations all lead up to Venice enthroned, holding out the motto: *"Fortis, justa, trono furias, mare sub pede pono"* (Strong, just, I put the furies beneath my throne and the sea beneath my foot).[013-003]

> Between the wonderful, mystic, Eastern glory of San Marco, all shadows and symbolisms and harmonies, and the positive, realistic assertions, aesthetic and spiritual, of the Frari, lay the entire reach of the art and religion of the Most Serene Republic.[001-009]

## Notable Events

During the Interdict crisis, Fra Paolo Sarpi was offered a suite in the Ducal Palace for his protection, though he preferred to remain in his cell at the Servite convent. The Senate pleaded with him to accept quarters connected by a guarded passage.[040-007]

The palace also witnessed the death of Doge [[marino-grimani|Marino Grimani]] during the height of the Interdict tensions, and the subsequent election of [[leonardo-donato|Leonardo Donato]] as his successor.
