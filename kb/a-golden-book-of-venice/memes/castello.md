## Overview

**Castello** is the easternmost of Venice's six sestieri, stretching from the eastern edge of [[san-marco|San Marco]] to the island of Sant'Elena. It is the largest sestiere by area and home to two of Venice's most significant institutions: the [[arsenal-of-venice|Arsenale]] and the ancient cathedral of San Pietro di Castello, which served as the seat of the Patriarch of Venice until the Napoleonic era.

## Location

Castello occupies the eastern end of Venice, bounded by the Bacino di San Marco to the south and the northern lagoon. The sestiere encompasses the [[arsenal-of-venice|Arsenale]] complex, the church of [[san-zanipolo|San Zanipolo]], and the island church of San Pietro di Castello, which was connected to the main body of Venice by bridges.

## The Sestiere

Castello spans a diverse urban landscape — from the workers' quarters near the [[arsenal-of-venice|Arsenale]] to the stately churches at its eastern edge. The area around the Arsenale was the industrial heart of Renaissance Venice, employing thousands of *arsenalotti* who built and maintained the Republic's war fleet.

At the eastern tip, San Pietro di Castello preserves the memory of an earlier, island-based Venice. Before [[san-marco|San Marco]] rose to prominence, San Pietro was the city's religious center — a status it retained as the patriarchal seat:
