## Overview

The **Piazzetta San Marco** is the narrow piazza opening from [[san-marco|Piazza San Marco]] onto the Bacino di San Marco. Framed by the two granite columns — one bearing the winged lion of [[venice|St. Mark]], the other St. Theodore — the Piazzetta is Venice's ceremonial water-gate, where state visitors disembark and processions form. In the novel, it is the threshold between the Republic's public theater and the private world of the lagoon.

## Location

The Piazzetta occupies the space between the [[palazzo-ducale|Doge's Palace]] to the east and the Libreria Marciana to the west, opening southward to the Bacino. The two columns dominate its waterfront end, framing the view from the water and creating an architectural portal unique in Venice. Gondolas and state barges dock at the *Molo*, the broad steps descending to the water.

## The Columns

The two columns of the Piazzetta are among Venice's most iconic monuments. The column of St. Theodore stands beside that of the winged lion, together symbolizing Venice's dual identity: Byzantine heritage and the Republic's self-made mythology. Between these columns, executions were carried out — a grim contrast to the ceremonial welcome they otherwise offered.
