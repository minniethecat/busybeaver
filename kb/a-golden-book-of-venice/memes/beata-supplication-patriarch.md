## Background

During the Papal [[papal-interdict-proclamation|Interdict crisis]], a noble lady of Venice — deeply devout and anguished by the spiritual separation from Rome — had fled the city in desperation. Believing the Madonna of [[san-donato|San Donato]] had commanded her in a vision to seek the Pope and plead for Venice, she had lost all her loved ones on the journey and arrived back in Venice in a state of hallucination and physical collapse.

## The Supplication

Before reaching the dais prepared for the [[signoria|Signoria]] at the end of the great hall of the Ducal Palace, she paused, swaying, nearly falling (050-001). Then, gathering her strength, she glided forward and knelt at the feet of the Patriarch. The entire Venetian court, nobles, and ambassadors looked down from the circling galleries "expectant and awestruck under the spell of so strange a vision" (050-006).

> "With prayers and penance have I striven — and ceased not — since the anguish of thy displeasure came upon Venice. Oh, Holy Father! for all the mothers who understand and grieve, and for our innocent little ones... lift thy wrath from Venice, that our Heavenly Father hide not his face in clouds too heavy for our prayers to reach him!" (050-009)

She feverishly unclasped the lustrous pearls from her throat and girdle, laying them at the feet of the Patriarch as an offering, declaring that she had given "all the dear happiness of my life" to reach him with this prayer (050-011).

## The Revelation

The Patriarch, "moved with immeasurable compassion," raised her tenderly and spoke the truth: "My daughter, Venice is restored to favor. The Interdict is removed!" (050-014–015). The assembly echoed the news from group to group, gallery to gallery, "until it gathered impetus and rolled like a Hallelujah Chorus through the vast, vaulted chamber" (050-015).

## Aftermath

The dying woman stood radiant for a moment — her face "like an angel's for joy and love" — then her faint, fluttering breath died in a _Benedicite_ as the vesper bells of Venice came softly through the twilight (050-018–019). Her supplication, though born of hallucination, became a powerful symbol of the spiritual anguish the Interdict had inflicted on the faithful of Venice.
