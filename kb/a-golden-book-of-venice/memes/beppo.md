## Overview

Beppo is a Venetian schoolboy who attends the school of San Marcuolo alongside the young Pierino, the future [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo Sarpi]]. Bare-legged and unlearned in Latin, he represents the voice of common Venetian childhood — loyal, irreverent, and instinctively protective of his friend.

## Role in the Narrative

Beppo appears during the buildup to the great disputation at the Church of the Servi. He serves as an informal scout for the neighborhood children, reporting that the church "is filled with great ones" and identifying [[don-ambrogio|Don Ambrogio Morelli]] among the arriving dignitaries. In a confidential aside, he confesses his skepticism of formal learning: "Don Ambrogio just wants to cram us boys, but it's no use knowing too much, even for a priest" [001-023].

When his friends speculate that Pierino will one day become a priest like his uncle, Beppo responds earnestly: "I believe thee; and that was he going in behind the banner, with the Servi" [002-002]. His faith in his friend's destiny is simple and complete.

Beppo's finest moment comes during the disputation itself. Pushing his way through the crowd of dignitaries and nobles, the "bare-legged Beppo" plants himself before the pulpit in "wide-eyed astonishment," watching every movement of his former schoolfellow. The Latin arguments mean nothing to his "unlearned ears," but "his heart throbbed with sudden loyalty in seeing his comrade the centre of such a festa." Beppo resolves to stay and "help him to get fair play, if he should need it, since it was well known that Pierino could not fight, for all his Latin!" [002-022].

## Historical Context

Beppo is a fictional character who represents the class of ordinary Venetian children — the _popolani_ — whose lives intersected briefly with the city's great institutions. The school of San Marcuolo, attached to the parish church of San Marcuola on the [[grand-canal|Grand Canal]], was one of many parish schools that provided elementary education to Venetian boys. Beppo's inability to understand the Latin disputation reflects the linguistic divide between the learned clergy and the common people of Venice.

## Relations

- **[[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo Sarpi]]** — Pierino, Beppo's schoolfellow at San Marcuolo. Beppo's protective loyalty to the boy who "could not fight, for all his Latin" reveals the human affection that the young prodigy inspired.
- **[[don-ambrogio|Don Ambrogio Morelli]]** — The Abbé of San Marcuolo, whom Beppo regards with the healthy skepticism of a schoolboy.

## Significance

Beppo is a minor but memorably drawn character who provides a rare glimpse of the world outside the cloister and the palazzo. His bare-legged, unlettered presence at the theological disputation creates a moment of dramatic contrast — the common child witnessing the birth of a prodigy. Through Beppo's eyes, the reader is reminded that [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo Sarpi]], for all his austere genius, was once simply Pierino, a boy who had friends willing to fight for him.
