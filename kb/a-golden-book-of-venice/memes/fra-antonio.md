# Fra Antonio

Fra Antonio was a Servite friar who served as an agent of the Roman Chapter, sent to Venice with a covert mission. His jealousy of [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo Sarpi]] and suspected designs against the consultore's life led to his arrest by the [[council-of-ten|Council of Ten]].

## Jealousy of Fra Paolo

Fra Antonio could never accept the veneration with which the other friars regarded [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo]]. While [[fra-giulio|Fra Giulio]] and others celebrated [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo]]'s triumphs, Fra Antonio "could not be converted to the prevailing tone of admiration for this abnormal being who walked among them not as other men" [038-017]. Another friar mocked him: "thou, in canonized shoes, couldst walk but wearily, Fra Antonio, lest they should lead thee in unwonted ways!" [038-018].

When [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo]] passed through the cloister, his glance took in "every expression, from the affectionate smile of old [[fra-giulio|Fra Giulio]] to the jealous discontent of Fra Antonio, whose gaze drooped before him" [039-004].

## The Roman Mission

[[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo]] confronted Fra Antonio privately, revealing that the Roman Chapter knew nothing of his presence in Venice: "the head of the Roman Chapter hath made inquiry for thee, and knew naught of thy presence here. Thou wilt soon be recalled. That thou doest — do quickly" [039-005]. Fra Antonio turned pale and nearly collapsed at this revelation, confirming the clandestine nature of his mission.

## Arrest by the Council of Ten

During a formal visit by the Chief of the [[council-of-ten|Council of Ten]] to the monastery, Fra Antonio attempted to flee. While the other friars were distracted, he slipped toward the dormitory passage, but his "unsteady foot stumbled in a hollow of the worn brick pavement" [039-017]. The Chief of [[council-of-ten|the Ten]] called him out before the full assembly.

The Chief revealed that "word hath reached Venice, privately, from special friendly sources in Rome, that thou art come hither charged with a message of vital import to a trusted servant of the Republic" [039-026]. Fra Antonio was formally charged: the Superior announced that he was "described by trustworthy advices from our Chapter in Rome as a person who hath designs upon the life of a member of this community" [039-029].

## Mercy and Fate

Despite the gravity of the accusation, [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo]] showed restraint. Rather than demanding immediate punishment, he leaned toward Fra Antonio and spoke privately: "It were better for thee to confess — or depart in silence" [039-029], offering his would-be assassin a path to avoid the full consequences of his crime. Fra Antonio was ultimately led away to the gondola of the Piombi, the prison of the [[council-of-ten|Council of Ten]], where he let out "a faint cry... and put his hands before his face" [040-002].

## Character Significance

Fra Antonio represents the internal threat to [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo]] from within the Church itself — a friar who, rather than celebrating [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo]]'s gifts, resented them, and who was willing to serve Roman interests against a fellow Servite. His arrest demonstrated the reach of the Venetian intelligence network and the [[council-of-ten|Council of Ten]]'s determination to protect [[fra-paolo-sarpi|Fra Paolo]] as a valuable asset to the Republic.
