## Overview

The **Castellani** were one of the two great working-class factions of Venice, alongside their rivals the [[nicolotti|Nicolotti]].

Based in the eastern sestiere of Castello, which included the [[arsenal-of-venice|Arsenal of Venice]] and the shipbuilding community, the Castellani were traditionally associated with [[arsenal-of-venice|the Arsenal]] workers and represented the eastern half of the city in the factional contests that defined Venetian popular culture.

The rivalry between Castellani and [[nicolotti|Nicolotti]] was institutionalized, not suppressed: the Republic channeled factional energy into regulated public spectacles — bridge battles and regattas — that became defining features of Venetian civic life.

## Structure

Like the [[nicolotti|Nicolotti]], the Castellani were organized under their own gastaldo and maintained a distinct identity with its own colors, patron saints, and traditions. The rivalry between the two factions was fierce but governed by elaborate rules of engagement:

> [036-005] So the rival faction of the Castellani bore marvelous testimony to his prowess with the bridge.

The factional divide was deeply ingrained in Venetian identity:

> [005-003] "Ah, the Castellani!" said Piero, with the contempt of a true Nicolotto.

## Role in the Narrative

The Castellani appear primarily through the lens of their rivalry with the [[nicolotti|Nicolotti]] — a rivalry that the [[nicolotti|Nicolotti]] gastaldo [[piero-salin|Piero Salin]] embodies with passionate partisanship. Yet the narrative complicates this simple opposition, showing moments when factional loyalty yields to larger purposes.

When the Republic faces external threat, the old rivals unite:

> [048-014] Nicolotti and Castellani — *allies*, since they never had been conquered.

This alliance culminates in joint action:

> [048-019] the people — as well Nicolotti as Castellani.

The bridge battle remains the signature expression of the rivalry:

> [045-018] a great regatta awaited them — Nicolotti against Castellani — and silently, saving voice and strength for the final onset.

## Key Passages

The feud between the factions colors daily life in Venice:

> [030-031] confound the scoundrel Castellani, who threw a good Nicolotto overboard.

The gastaldo's partisanship is a mark of his character:

> [034-008] comfiture of their rivals the Castellani. For Piero was a faithful gastaldo.
