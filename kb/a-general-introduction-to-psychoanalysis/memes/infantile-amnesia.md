# Infantile Amnesia

**Infantile amnesia** is the normal forgetting that veils the earliest years of childhood. Freud regards it not as a simple failure of memory but as the direct continuation of the infantile amnesias "which hides the beginnings of the sexual life from most people" (018-028). The experiences of the first years of childhood "fall victim to the infantile amnesia, the forgetting we have already discussed as a normal process" (021-019).

> The forgetting we have already discussed as a normal process — most of the experiences and impulses prior to the latent period then fall victim to the infantile amnesia.
>
> (021-019)
