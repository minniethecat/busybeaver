## Biography

Eugen Bleuler (1857–1939) was a Swiss psychiatrist who served as director of the Burghölzli clinic in Zurich. Under his leadership, the Zurich School became a major center for psychoanalytic research in the early twentieth century (006-028).

## Role in Psychoanalysis

Bleuler led the Zurich School alongside Carl Jung, and together they developed the association-experiment method. By asking subjects to explain unusual reactions through further associations, they demonstrated that these reactions were determined by the subject's complexes. Freud credited Bleuler and Jung with building "the first bridge from experimental psychology to psychoanalysis" (006-028).

Freud also acknowledged Bleuler's lasting contribution to descriptive psychopathology. The term "ambivalence" — designating the coexistence of hostile and affectionate feelings toward the same person — was introduced by Bleuler and adopted by Freud in his discussion of melancholia and other narcissistic conditions (026-042).
