## Biography

Ambroise-Auguste Liébeault (1823–1904) was a French physician who, together with Hippolyte Bernheim, founded the Nancy School of hypnotism. He is referred to in the English translation of Freud's lectures under the variant spelling "Siebault" (006-014).

## Role in Psychoanalysis

In 1889, Freud traveled to Nancy to witness the "extraordinarily enlightening demonstrations of Siebault and Bernheim" (006-014). These demonstrations showed that hypnotic experiences seemingly forgotten upon waking could be recovered through persistent urging — a finding that suggested the existence of unconscious mental processes that could be made conscious through appropriate technique. This observation contributed to the foundation of Freud's later development of free association and the psychoanalytic method.

The Nancy School, led by Liébeault and Bernheim, advanced the view that hypnosis operated through suggestion rather than neurological pathology, in direct opposition to the Salpêtrière School of Charcot. This debate over the nature of hypnosis was an important episode in the prehistory of psychoanalysis.
