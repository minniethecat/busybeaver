# Eros

**Eros** is the name Freud gives to the life instincts — the set of drives that serve to preserve, bind together, and create ever larger unities. First fully articulated in *Beyond the Pleasure Principle* (1920), Eros encompasses the sexual instincts and the ego-instincts, opposing the destructive tendencies of the [[Death Instinct]].

In the *Introductory Lectures*, the concept appears in the context of object choice: the heterosexual object choice is presented as "more natural to narcism than the heterosexual" (026-039) — a suggestion that the erotic instinct is shaped by narcissistic as much as by object-directed forces.
