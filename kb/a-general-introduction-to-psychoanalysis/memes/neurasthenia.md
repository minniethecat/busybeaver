# Neurasthenia

**Neurasthenia** is one of the three "actual neuroses" in Freud's diagnostic classification, along with [[Anxiety Neurosis]] and [[Hypochondria]]. The actual neuroses are distinguished from the psychoneuroses ([[Hysteria]], compulsion neurosis) by their presumed somatic rather than psychical etiology.

Freud describes "the pure forms of true neuroses: neurasthenia, anxiety neurosis and hypochondria" (024-025) as a distinct nosological group. He notes that the contrast can be "most easily observed between neurasthenia and the transference neuroses" (024-026) — the former lacking the symbolic meaning characteristic of psychoneurotic symptoms.

> The symptom in the true neuroses is frequently the nucleus and incipient stage of development of the psychoneurotic symptom. Such a connection is most easily observed between neurasthenia and the transference neuroses.
>
> (024-026)
