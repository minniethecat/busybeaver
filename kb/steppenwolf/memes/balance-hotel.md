# Balance Hotel

## Definition

The Balance Hotel in *[[steppenwolf-novel|Steppenwolf]]* is a modest public establishment where [[hermine|Hermine]] arranges to meet [[harry-haller|Harry Haller]] for tea. Like many spaces in the novel, its significance is functional rather than symbolic: it serves as neutral ground for the encounter that sets [[harry-haller|Harry]]'s transformation in motion. The name "Balance" is suggestive — the hotel hosts the meeting that will begin to rebalance [[harry-haller|Harry]]'s lopsided existence — but Hesse does not belabor the symbolism.

## In the Novel

The Balance Hotel appears when [[hermine|Hermine]] — having extracted [[harry-haller|Harry]]'s "oath of obedience" — arranges their next meeting:

> "She reminded me coolly of my oath of obedience and arranged a meeting for tea on the following day at the Balance Hotel."

The scene that follows is one of instruction. [[hermine|Hermine]] orders for [[harry-haller|Harry]], teaches him about food and pleasure, and continues the project of re-educating his senses that will culminate in the [[dance]] lessons and the [[masked-ball|Masked Ball]]. The hotel's function is to provide a space outside [[harry-haller|Harry]]'s familiar territory — neither his attic room nor the [[black-eagle|Black Eagle]] tavern — where new patterns of behavior can be established.

## Significance

The Balance Hotel belongs to a class of transitional spaces in *Steppenwolf* — places that are neither the privacy of [[harry-haller|Harry]]'s room nor the full publicity of the [[dance-halls|dance hall]], but intermediate zones where the passage from isolation to society can be rehearsed. [[hermine|Hermine]]'s choice of a hotel rather than a tavern or restaurant is strategic: it is neutral ground, free of the associations that cling to [[harry-haller|Harry]]'s habitual haunts. The new self requires a new setting.
