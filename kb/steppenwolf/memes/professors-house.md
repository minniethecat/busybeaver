## Location and Setting

The professor's house is the private residence of a young academic—a former acquaintance of [[harry-haller|Harry Haller]]—located in the unnamed town where [[steppenwolf-novel|the novel]] takes place. [[harry-haller|Haller]] encounters the professor by chance near the Library (001-050), and the ensuing dinner invitation becomes the catalyst for one of [[steppenwolf-novel|the novel]]'s most painful scenes. The house, with its domestic comforts and bourgeois assumptions, represents everything [[harry-haller|Haller]] simultaneously longs for and despises.

The physical space is characterized by its conventional middle-class furnishings and cultural pretensions—most notably a framed print of [[goethe|Goethe]] that [[harry-haller|Haller]] finds offensive. The contrast between the professor's comfortable domesticity and [[harry-haller|Haller]]'s rootless, anguished existence defines the visit's emotional dynamics.

## Role in the Narrative

The dinner at the professor's house (spanning approximately PN 001-050 through 001-114) is a critical turning point in [[steppenwolf-novel|the novel]]'s early movement. [[harry-haller|Haller]]'s social discomfort, his alienation from bourgeois norms, and his inability to perform the expected courtesies of dinner-party conversation culminate in a disastrous evening. The episode exposes the unbridgeable gap between [[harry-haller|Haller]]'s inner life and the social world he must navigate.

The visit triggers [[harry-haller|Haller]]'s most severe crisis to that point—a suicidal despair that drives him toward the [[black-eagle|Black Eagle]] and his fateful encounter with [[hermine|Hermine]]. The professor's house thus functions as the last station of [[harry-haller|Haller]]'s old life before he is drawn into the transformative world of [[dance]], [[jazz]], and the [[magic-theater|Magic Theater]].

## Key Passages

> By the Library I met a young professor of whom in earlier years I used to see a good deal. (001-050)

This chance encounter sets in motion the dinner that will precipitate [[harry-haller|Haller]]'s crisis—a reminder that in [[steppenwolf|Steppenwolf]], casual social interactions often carry existential weight.

> When the professor spoke of Haller as a traitor to his country, I felt the whole abyss that separated me from this man and his kind. (001-054)

The moment crystallizes [[harry-haller|Haller]]'s alienation from the bourgeois intellectual world—his values, his identity, his very notion of loyalty are incomprehensible to the professor.

> I took leave of Goethe and of the professor. I seized my hat and coat from the hall and hurried away. (001-060)

[[harry-haller|Haller]]'s abrupt departure, collapsing the distinction between [[goethe|Goethe]] (the immortal) and the professor (the mortal), signals his rejection of the entire [[bourgeois-existence|bourgeois world]] the dinner represents.
