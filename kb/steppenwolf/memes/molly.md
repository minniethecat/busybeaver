# Molly

## Character Overview

Molly is a peripheral figure in the [[masked-ball|Masked Ball]] sequence of *[[steppenwolf-novel|Steppenwolf]]*, appearing alongside [[dora|Dora]] and other unnamed dancers. Like her counterpart [[dora|Dora]], she exists not as a fully realized character but as a presence in the ballroom's eroticized atmosphere — one of the bodies through which [[harry-haller|Harry Haller]] learns the lessons of sensation and self-forgetting that [[hermine|Hermine]] has prescribed.

## In the Novel

Molly's appearance is confined to the [[masked-ball|Masked Ball]]. The novel provides no backstory, no dialogue, and no individuating details. She is a name dropped into a crowd scene, her function purely atmospheric. This minimal characterization is deliberate: the [[masked-ball|Masked Ball]] is the novel's great scene of ego-dissolution, and characters who retain too much individuality would work against its centrifugal logic.

## Significance

Molly, like [[dora|Dora]] and the other ball participants, represents the human warmth of the collective — the embodied sociality that [[harry-haller|Harry]] has fled throughout his life as a solitary intellectual. Her very namelessness within the narrative (she is named once and never described) is significant: the ball does not offer [[harry-haller|Harry]] relationships with individuated persons but immersion in a collective energy. Molly is a wave in that ocean — not important in herself but essential to the whole in which [[harry-haller|Harry]] finally learns to swim.
