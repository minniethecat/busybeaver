## Overview

Detective Method Acting is [[tommy-beresford|Tommy Beresford]]'s signature investigative technique. Rather than developing a single consistent approach to detection, Tommy adopts a different fictional detective's persona for each case, complete with their characteristic mannerisms, speech patterns, and investigative philosophy. This method is made possible by the extensive library of detective fiction housed in the inner office of the [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Agency]], and it is documented across cases including the [[affair-of-the-pink-pearl|Affair of the Pink Pearl]], the [[sunningdale-mystery|Sunningdale Mystery]], and [[finessing-the-king|Finessing the King]].

## Origins

The technique originates from Tommy's collection of detective fiction. As [[tuppence-beresford|Tuppence]] observes, he keeps books on crime and detection in the inner office, and when a new case arrives, he consults these volumes to select the appropriate detective model. The agency's empty but "artistically labeled files" (002-003) are part of this theatrical approach — props in an elaborate performance that blurs the line between the real detective agency and its fictional models.

## Application

When a case arrives, Tommy studies the relevant detective fiction and immerses himself in the chosen persona. He adopts not only the detective's methods but their personality — their manner of speech, their interviewing style, and their approach to evidence. This method allows him to approach each case with fresh eyes, avoiding the cognitive ruts of a fixed investigative style. The technique draws inspiration from the tradition of [[blunts-brilliant-detectives|Blunt's Brilliant Detectives]], the fictional compendium from which Tommy selects his models.

The technique is both practical and performative. It provides genuine methodological variety while also entertaining Tommy, who clearly enjoys the theatrical dimension of detective work. However, the chosen persona influences his actual deductive process — a Holmes-inspired Tommy focuses on physical evidence, while a different persona might emphasize psychological profiling.

## Tuppence's Role

While Tommy performs the detective roles, [[tuppence-beresford|Tuppence Beresford]] serves as the grounding presence. She does not adopt fictional personas, relying instead on her natural intuition and sharp observation. This contrast — Tommy's studied performance versus Tuppence's instinctive detection — creates a productive tension in their investigations. Tuppence's role is not merely supplementary; her skepticism of Tommy's more extravagant performances often proves instrumental in redirecting the investigation toward productive lines of inquiry.

## Relationship to Other Concepts

The method acting technique intersects with several other key concepts in the Partners in Crime universe. It is enabled by the [[number-sixteen-code|Number Sixteen code]] investigation — the intelligence operation that provides the Agency's true purpose — since the theatricality of Tommy's personas helps maintain the cover. It also connects to the broader [[amateur-detective-tradition|amateur detective tradition]], of which the Beresfords are self-aware inheritors. The technique's literary self-consciousness is characteristic of Christie's metafictional play throughout the collection.

## Significance

Detective Method Acting reflects the novel's metafictional relationship with the detective genre. Christie uses the technique to pay homage to the detective fiction tradition while also gently satirizing it. The technique also serves a practical narrative function, allowing each chapter to adopt the flavor of a different sub-genre of detective story. This structural innovation — a series of linked stories each filtered through a different literary lens — is one of the distinctive achievements of *[[partners-in-crime-novel|Partners in Crime]]* as a work of genre fiction.
