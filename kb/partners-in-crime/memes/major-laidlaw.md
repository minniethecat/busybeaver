## Overview

**Major Laidlaw** is a tall, fair military man who figures prominently in Chapter 10, "[[the-crackler|The Crackler]]." He is known in certain circles for financial dealings involving counterfeit currency, and his movements — particularly with an empty suitcase — draw the attention of [[tommy-beresford|Tommy Beresford]].

## Introduction to the Case

Major Laidlaw's name is raised early in the investigation as a person of interest:

> "A Major Laidlaw—perhaps you've heard the name?" (010-039)

The response confirms his reputation:

> "Yes. Major Laidlaw is pretty well known in connection with—" (010-041)

## The Empty Suitcase

A peculiar detail of Major Laidlaw's behaviour attracts Tommy's attention:

> "Quite so," said Tommy. "Major Laidlaw goes out with an empty suitcase." (010-048)

This observation — a man departing with luggage that contains nothing — is the kind of anomaly that, in detective fiction and in the Beresfords' experience, signals illicit activity. The empty suitcase suggests he intends to fill it with something obtained during his outing.

## Physical Description

Major Laidlaw is described in military terms befitting his rank:

> "Major Laidlaw was a tall fair man, typically—" (010-071)

His bearing and appearance align with his military background, presenting a respectable façade that masks his involvement in the counterfeit scheme.

## Role in "The Crackler"

The "Crackler" case involves forged banknotes being circulated through London. Major Laidlaw's role in this scheme — whether as a distributor, facilitator, or principal — is established through Tommy's investigation. His connection to his wife, [[mrs-laidlaw|Mrs. Laidlaw]], and her circle of admirers proves significant to unravelling the operation.
