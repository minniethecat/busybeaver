## Overview

**Miss Sheringham** serves as a confidential secretary at the [[international-detective-agency|detective agency]] operated by [[tommy-beresford|Tommy]] and [[tuppence-beresford|Tuppence Beresford]] under the [[theodore-blunt|Theodore Blunt]] persona. She appears in Chapter 14, "[[clergymans-daughter|The Clergyman's Daughter]]," where she assists Tommy with a case involving a poisoning at a rectory.

## Introduction

Tommy introduces Miss Sheringham to a client as part of the agency's professional apparatus:

> "This is my confidential secretary, Miss Sheringham." (014-013)

This introduction establishes her as a trusted member of the agency's staff, present during client consultations and privy to sensitive case details.

## Assistance in the Investigation

During a discussion of the case, Tommy actively solicits her input:

> "What do you think — ought we to look into this for you — eh, Miss Sheringham?" (014-039)

Her response is affirmative and professional:

> "Certainly, Miss Sheringham," said Tommy. "An excellent proposition." (014-046)

The exchange indicates that Miss Sheringham is not merely a passive note-taker but is consulted for her judgement on whether to accept a case — a mark of the trust Tommy places in her.

## Character and Role

Like [[miss-ganges|Miss Ganges]], another secretary in the agency's orbit, Miss Sheringham is part of the theatrical apparatus that Tommy and Tuppence construct around their detective enterprise. The "confidential secretary" role lends an air of professionalism and legitimacy to what is, at its core, the Beresfords' amateur enthusiasm for detection. Miss Sheringham plays her part in this performance with competence and discretion.
