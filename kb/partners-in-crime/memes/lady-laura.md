## Overview

Lady Laura Barton is a key witness in Chapter 3, "[[finessing-the-king|Finessing the King]]." An "old and dear friend" of the [[kingston-bruce]] family (003-070), she was present at the tea party during which a valuable pearl was stolen, making her a person of interest in [[tommy-beresford]]'s investigation.

## Background

Lady Laura is the daughter of the late Earl of Barchester (003-070). She moves in aristocratic circles and is a longtime friend of Colonel and Mrs. Kingston Bruce. She was staying at the Kingston Bruce residence at the time of the theft, along with her French maid, Elise.

## The Pearl Theft Investigation

When the Kingston Bruces' pearl went missing, Lady Laura was among those present at the gathering. The other attendees included "my wife and daughter — and Lady Laura. Young St. Vincent was dining with us, and [[mr-rennie|Mr. Rennie]]" (003-080). Her social standing made her an unlikely suspect, but Tommy insisted on interviewing everyone present.

Tommy gave specific instructions about Lady Laura: "Don't let Lady Laura leave the house" (003-163), recognizing that all witnesses must remain available until the investigation was complete. Tuppence was introduced to Lady Laura as "[[miss-robinson|Miss Robinson]]" (003-203), maintaining the Beresfords' cover identities.

> Lady Laura bent her head, and then proceeded to give her account of the afternoon's events. (003-204)

After lunch, Lady Laura "entered into conversation" (003-205) with Tuppence, providing details about the household dynamics and the events surrounding the theft. Her testimony helped narrow the field of suspects.

## The Resolution

The investigation ultimately revealed that the thief was not Lady Laura but her French maid, Elise. The dramatic conclusion came when Tommy announced: "They've not arrested Lady Laura. They've arrested Elise" (003-228). Lady Laura had been entirely innocent — her presence at the scene was coincidental, and her maid had acted without her knowledge.

## Key Relationships

- **Colonel [[kingston-bruce]]** — An old family friend in whose home the theft occurred.
- **Elise** — Lady Laura's French maid, who was ultimately revealed as the pearl thief (003-228).

## Appearances

Lady Laura appears in Chapter 3, "[[finessing-the-king|Finessing the King]]," where her presence as a houseguest during the pearl theft makes her an important witness and briefly a person of suspicion.
