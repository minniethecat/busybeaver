## Overview

**Miss Ganges** is a confidential secretary employed at the detective agency run by [[tommy-beresford|Tommy Beresford]] and [[tuppence-beresford|Tuppence Beresford]] under the guise of [[theodore-blunt|Theodore Blunt]]. She appears in Chapter 8, "[[blindmans-buff|Blindman's Buff]]," where she assists Tommy during the investigation of a peculiar case involving a blindfolded man.

## Identity

Miss Ganges is introduced by Tommy with a distinctive origin story:

> "Confidential secretary," he said. "Miss Ganges. Found on the banks of the Indus." (008-063)

This whimsical backstory, suggesting she was discovered near the Indus River in South Asia, is consistent with the theatrical and playful manner in which Tommy and Tuppence operate their detective agency. The name "Ganges" itself evokes the Ganges River of India, reinforcing the exotic persona Tommy has constructed.

## Role in "Blindman's Buff"

### Assistance During the Investigation

Miss Ganges accompanies Tommy during the lunchtime confrontation at the detective agency's office:

> "Miss Ganges and I will come with you," he said. (008-077)

She is present when Tommy invites a visitor to lunch, serving as a witness and assistant:

> "Miss Ganges—I am lunching here tomorrow." (008-079)

### Practical Support

Her role includes practical duties such as handling Tommy's cane:

> "My cane, Miss Ganges? Thank you. Directions, please." (008-086)

She is also directed to arrange transportation:

> "Take Miss Ganges in it, will you." (008-093)

## Character Traits

Miss Ganges appears to be a competent and composed assistant, fulfilling the role of confidential secretary with quiet efficiency. Her exotic "origin story" — being "found on the banks of the Indus" — is part of the theatrical atmosphere cultivated by [[tommy-beresford|Tommy Beresford]] during his impersonation of the famous detective [[theodore-blunt|Theodore Blunt]]. She plays along with the elaborate pretense that characterises the Beresfords' detective enterprise.
