## Overview

The Three Arts Club is a London social venue that serves as the setting for one of the Agency's most dramatic cases. On the night of a masked costume ball, [[lady-merivale|Lady Merivale]] is stabbed with a jewelled dagger — a murder witnessed indirectly by Tommy and Tuppence from the adjoining booth.

## The Masked Ball

Tommy and Tuppence attend the ball in costume — Tommy as the Ace of Spades, Tuppence as the Queen of Hearts — following a cryptic personal advertisement couched in bridge terminology (005-010). The event gathers a fashionable London crowd in elaborate disguises, masks concealing identities and intentions alike.

## The Murder

In the booth next to the Beresfords', a woman dressed as the Queen of Hearts is with a man dressed as the King. Tuppence hears a cry followed by a man's laughter and investigates. She finds the woman stabbed through the heart with a jewelled dagger. The dying woman whispers: "Bingo did it" — a declaration that implicates [[captain-hale|Captain Hale]] (005-022).

## Significance

The Three Arts Club, with its costumes and masks, provides the perfect setting for a crime of mistaken identity. Everyone is pretending to be someone else — and the real killer, [[sir-arthur-merivale|Sir Arthur Merivale]] in his newspaper-clad costume, exploits the anonymity of the masked ball to commit murder. The setting literalizes the theme of deception that runs through the entire collection.
