## Overview

Miss Logan is a central figure in Chapter 12, "[[unbreakable-alibi|The Unbreakable Alibi]]" — she is the companion to an elderly woman known as Aunt Lucy and becomes the victim of a sophisticated poisoning attempt using ricin. [[tommy-beresford]] and [[tuppence-beresford]] investigate her case.

## Background

Miss Logan served as "Aunt Lucy's companion" (012-056) in a well-to-do household. She lived in the house alongside other staff and family members. Her position gave her intimate access to the household but also placed her in proximity to someone who wished her harm.

## The Poisoning

Miss Logan fell seriously ill under mysterious circumstances. [[dr-burton|Dr. Burton]] attended to her and was "very excited over the matter" (012-026), initially attributing the illness to various natural causes. However, the symptoms suggested something more sinister.

When Tommy and Tuppence visited, they found her recovering but still weak. Miss Logan "looked at them with obvious suspicion" (012-165) when questioned, uncertain whether the detectives were truly there to help. Tommy showed her an item and asked directly: "Is this yours, Miss Logan?" (012-168), probing for clues about the poison's origin.

> "Disgraceful," said Miss Logan. "I will not have the servants talked about in this way." (012-174)

Even in her weakened state, Miss Logan maintained a protective attitude toward the household staff, revealing her loyalty and sense of propriety.

## The Ricin Revelation

The investigation uncovered a disturbing truth: Miss Logan had been injected with ricin, a deadly toxin. Tommy noticed "a lot of small red dots on Miss Logan's arm" (012-212), which proved to be injection sites. When confronted, he stated directly: "You injected Ricin for some purpose" (012-248), revealing the method of the attempted murder.

[[dr-burton|Dr. Burton]] confirmed he had "given her a hypodermic injection" (012-215) as part of medical treatment, but the ricin had been administered separately by someone else in the household — someone who had access to both the toxin and Miss Logan's medical care.

## Appearances

Miss Logan appears in Chapter 12, where her poisoning case demonstrates the Beresfords' ability to unravel medically sophisticated crimes.
