## Overview

The British Secret Service is the government intelligence organization that employs [[tommy-beresford|Tommy Beresford]] and orchestrates the detective agency cover operation. Under the direction of [[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]], the Service uses the [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Agency]] to monitor a network of foreign agents operating in London, while Tommy and Tuppence simultaneously run legitimate investigations.

## Tommy's Role

Before the events of the novel, Tommy worked for the Secret Service in a desk capacity — "pure office work" as Tuppence describes it (001-014). During the war, both Tommy and Tuppence had been active field operatives chasing German spies, but Tommy's current role has become administrative. [[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]]'s proposal to take over the detective agency represents a return to active field work, though under a civilian cover.

## The Intelligence Operation

The operation targets a network of international agents communicating through letters with Russian stamps. The number 16 written under the stamp serves as the recognition signal (001-048). Tommy is instructed to copy all such letters and forward the originals to [[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]], and to report any visitor who references the number 16.

The operation proves more dangerous than anticipated. [[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]] later warns that "the hornet's nest we've stirred up is bigger than anyone imagined" (012-001), and the Beresfords become direct targets of the spy ring.

## Relationship with Scotland Yard

The Secret Service operates separately from [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]], and the police are careful not to interfere with intelligence operations. [[inspector-marriot|Inspector Marriot]] explains: "It's not our department, and we don't interfere" (005-005). However, when the Beresfords face criminal threats, the two organizations collaborate, with [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] providing armed backup for arrests.

## The Number 16 Mission

The specific intelligence operation assigned to Tommy centers on a Russian agent designated as Number 16. The operational details are:

- **Method of Communication**: Letters addressed to the agency bearing Russian stamps are the primary channel. The identifying mark — the number 16 written beneath the stamp — indicates a message from the agent.
- **Urgency**: [[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]] emphasizes the importance of the mission: Number 16 is a high-value target whose communications could reveal a significant international conspiracy.
- **Connection to Detective Work**: The mission intersects with the agency's legitimate cases throughout the novel, culminating in *[[ch17-the-man-who-was-no-16|The Man Who Was No. 16]]*, where the detective and intelligence threads converge.

## Significance

The British Secret Service provides the espionage framework that distinguishes *[[partners-in-crime-novel|Partners in Crime]]* from a purely domestic detective novel. The intelligence thread runs beneath the episodic cases, surfacing periodically to raise the stakes and ultimately converging with the detective plot in the novel's climax.
