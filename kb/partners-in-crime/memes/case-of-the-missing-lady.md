## Overview

The Case of the Missing Lady is a pivotal investigation that interweaves the detective agency's legitimate work with the intelligence operation underlying it. A client seeks help finding a missing woman, but the case proves to have deeper connections to the spy ring that [[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]] has tasked the Beresfords with monitoring. The investigation marks a turning point where the danger of the intelligence operation becomes real and immediate.

## Background

The case arrives at the [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Agency]] in the form of a visitor seeking help locating a woman who has disappeared under mysterious circumstances. The initial consultation follows standard missing-person protocols, with Tommy and Tuppence gathering the details of the disappearance, the woman's last known whereabouts, and the client's relationship to her. However, inconsistencies in the client's account alert the Beresfords to hidden dimensions of the case — this is no ordinary missing person inquiry.

## The Case

A visitor arrives at the Agency seeking help locating a woman who has vanished. The client's story contains gaps and contradictions that Tommy's methodical questioning exposes. Meanwhile, Tuppence observes details about the visitor's demeanor and appearance that suggest the client is not who they claim to be. The investigation quickly moves from a simple missing-person case to something far more sinister.

## Intelligence Connections

The investigation intersects with the [[number-sixteen-code|number sixteen]] — the coded signal [[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]] instructed the Beresfords to watch for. Letters with Russian stamps, when moistened, reveal the number 16 written underneath (001-051). The case leads Tommy and Tuppence into a confrontation with international agents who have penetrated the Agency's cover, forcing them to fight not just for the resolution of the case but for their own survival.

## Resolution

The case culminates in a tense showdown at the Agency office, where Tuppence's quick thinking and coordination with [[inspector-marriot|Inspector Marriot]] result in the capture of a ring of international criminals. Marriot arrives with armed backup, taking the conspirators into custody (007-016). The resolution demonstrates the effectiveness of the Agency's dual-purpose design — the detective work and the intelligence operation reinforce each other, with the legitimate cases providing cover for the covert mission.

## Key Characters

- **[[tommy-beresford|Tommy Beresford]]** — Lead investigator, whose methodical questioning exposes the client's inconsistencies
- **[[tuppence-beresford|Tuppence Beresford]]** — Observer whose intuition first suspects the case is not what it seems
- **[[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]]** — Intelligence chief whose warning about the number sixteen proves prescient
- **[[inspector-marriot|Inspector Marriot]]** — Scotland Yard detective who provides crucial backup at the climax

## Significance

This case demonstrates the successful integration of the Agency's dual purpose — solving genuine detective cases while serving as an intelligence cover. It marks a turning point where the danger of the intelligence operation becomes real, and the Beresfords prove their resourcefulness under pressure. The case also illustrates how the Beresfords' experience and instincts — honed by their wartime intelligence work — allow them to recognize when a seemingly straightforward case conceals deeper, more dangerous layers.
