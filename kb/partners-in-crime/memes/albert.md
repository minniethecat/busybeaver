## Overview

Albert is the devoted servant of Tommy and [[tuppence-beresford|Tuppence Beresford]] who transitions from domestic service to becoming the office boy at the [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Agency]]. He is a source of comic relief in the novel, known for his enthusiastic but sometimes over-the-top impersonations of service roles.

## From Servant to Office Boy

When the Beresfords take over the detective agency, Albert relinquishes his domestic role for that of an office boy. His conception of the character includes "a paper bag of sweets, inky hands, and a tousled head" (002-002). Previously, he had been imitating a Long Island butler, and Tuppence expresses relief that she has "cured him of asking for people's cards and bringing them to me on a salver" (001-040).

Albert announces visitors with theatrical formality — introducing [[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]] "much as though it were a Royal title" (001-042).

## Role at the Agency

In the outer office, Albert serves as the first point of contact for visitors. His job is to delay prospective clients with "various artistic devices" while Tommy and Tuppence observe through peepholes (008-001). He also participates in staged telephone conversations and other theatrical effects the Beresfords use to maintain their professional cover.

When the agency is briefly left in Albert's charge, he demonstrates his reliability despite his comic mannerisms (010-001).

## Memorable Moments

Albert provides some of the novel's most memorable comic interludes:

- **The Royal Announcement** (001-042): When [[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]] first visits, Albert announces him "much as though it were a Royal title," setting the tone for his theatrical approach to office work.
- **The Long Island Butler** (001-040): Before becoming office boy, Albert had been imitating a Long Island butler, asking for visitors' cards and presenting them on a salver — a habit Tuppence was relieved to have broken.
- **The Paper Bag of Sweets** (002-002): Albert's conception of the ideal office boy includes "a paper bag of sweets, inky hands, and a tousled head" — a caricature he embraces with enthusiasm.
- **The Peephole Duty** (008-001): Albert's job of delaying clients while Tommy and Tuppence observe through peepholes showcases his theatrical talents, as he performs "various artistic devices" to buy time.
- **Left in Charge** (010-001): When briefly left in charge of the agency, Albert demonstrates unexpected reliability, proving that beneath his comic exterior lies genuine competence.

## Personality

Albert is loyal, eager to please, and takes his role-playing seriously — perhaps too seriously at times. He brings warmth and humor to the narrative. Despite his comic tendencies, he proves himself genuinely useful to the Beresfords' operations.
