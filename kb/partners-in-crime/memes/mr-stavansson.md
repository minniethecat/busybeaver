## Overview

**Mr. Stavansson** is an explorer who visits [[tommy-beresford|Tommy Beresford]] and [[tuppence-beresford|Tuppence Beresford]] at their [[international-detective-agency|detective agency]] in Chapter 7, "[[case-of-the-missing-lady|The Case of the Missing Lady]]." He seeks their help in locating his fiancée, who has disappeared under mysterious circumstances.

## Appearance and Demeanour

Mr. Stavansson is described as a well-dressed man of action. Tommy greets him with dry humour:

> "Fashion agrees with you, Mr. Stavansson," said Tommy drily. "And every other line of life, I should say." (007-025)

Tommy's assessment of the client's character is perceptive:

> "I perceive, a man of action, Mr. Stavansson," said Tommy, smiling. (007-027)

## The Missing Fiancée Case

Mr. Stavansson presents his problem to Tommy, who listens attentively:

> "Now, Mr. Stavansson," said Tommy. "There is nothing you have omitted?" (007-041)

Tommy offers commentary on the nature of criminal investigation to reassure his client:

> "You must often have noticed, Mr. Stavansson, in reading of crime, how one seemingly insignificant detail is the clue to the whole mystery." (007-046)

At the conclusion of the interview, Tommy requests contact details:

> "Leave me your address, Mr. Stavansson, and I will report progress to you." (007-048)

## Connection to the Case

The investigation of Mr. Stavansson's missing fiancée leads Tommy and Tuppence to a nursing home where [[doctor-horriston|Dr. Horriston]] practises a questionable medical treatment. The case involves a missing lady who has seemingly vanished without a trace, and Mr. Stavansson's distress drives him to seek the Beresfords' unconventional assistance.

## Character Traits

Mr. Stavansson is presented as a gentleman explorer — capable, well-dressed, and direct. He is a "man of action" rather than a cerebral detective, placing his trust in the Beresfords' investigative abilities. His emotional investment in finding his missing fiancée lends the case its urgency.
