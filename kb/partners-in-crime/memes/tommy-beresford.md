## Overview

Tommy Beresford is one of the two protagonists of *[[partners-in-crime-novel|Partners in Crime]]*. Married to Tuppence for six years, he works in the [[british-secret-service|Secret Service]] in an office capacity. When [[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]] offers the couple the opportunity to run the [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Agency]] under the alias [[theodore-blunt|Theodore Blunt]], Tommy takes on the role of the agency's manager, while secretly continuing intelligence work for the government.

## Background

Before the events of the novel, Tommy and Tuppence served as wartime intelligence operatives chasing German spies. Six years after their marriage, Tommy has settled into a comfortable but unexciting life, working for the [[british-secret-service|Secret Service]] in a desk role. Tuppence describes him as having become "prosperous and sleek and comfortable" (001-020).

Tommy is practical, cautious, and dryly humorous. When Tuppence yearns for excitement, he responds with skeptical pragmatism: "It is all very well to want things to happen — they might not be pleasant things" (001-022).

## Role at the Agency

Under the alias [[theodore-blunt|Theodore Blunt]], Tommy manages the [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Agency]] from an office at [[118-haleham-street|118 Haleham Street]], Bloomsbury (002-002). The cover serves a dual purpose: the agency takes on genuine detective cases while Tommy monitors letters with Russian stamps for intelligence purposes, looking for the number 16 written under the stamp (001-048).

Tommy enjoys the role-playing aspects of the work, frequently adopting different personas to match the detective styles of famous fictional sleuths. He keeps a collection of detective fiction from which he draws inspiration for his various disguises and methods.

## Notable Cases

Tommy personally leads several of the agency's most significant investigations:

- **[[affair-of-the-pink-pearl|The Affair of the Pink Pearl]]**: Tommy takes the lead in investigating the theft of a valuable pink pearl from the [[kingston-bruce|Kingston Bruce]] family, adopting the persona of a methodical detective.
- **[[finessing-the-king|Finessing the King]]**: Tommy infiltrates a social gathering to solve a coded murder announcement, demonstrating his aptitude for undercover work.
- **[[case-of-the-missing-lady|The Case of the Missing Lady]]**: Tommy investigates the disappearance of a woman, navigating misleading clues with characteristic skepticism.
- **[[ch17-the-man-who-was-no-16|The Man Who Was No. 16]]**: The case that ties directly to Tommy's intelligence mission — tracking the elusive Russian agent known as Number 16.

## Personality

Tommy displays a measured, ironic temperament that contrasts with Tuppence's impulsiveness. He is intelligent and resourceful but more cautious than his wife. His dry wit is evident throughout their adventures. Underneath his comfortable exterior, he retains the courage and quick thinking from his wartime intelligence days.

Tommy's approach to detective work is methodical rather than intuitive. He prefers to gather facts and observe before drawing conclusions, making him an effective counterweight to Tuppence's more instinctive style. His enjoyment of role-playing — adopting the mannerisms of fictional detectives — reveals a playful side beneath his pragmatic surface.

## Key Relationships

- **[[tuppence-beresford|Tuppence Beresford]]** — His wife and detective partner. Tommy is devoted to her and frequently plays the straight man to her flights of fancy. Their partnership balances Tuppence's intuition with Tommy's methodical nature.
- **[[albert|Albert]]** — The Beresfords' loyal office boy (formerly their domestic servant). Tommy relies on Albert for various operational tasks and treats him with patient amusement.
- **[[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]]** — Tommy's superior in the [[british-secret-service|Secret Service]], who assigns him the detective agency cover operation. Tommy respects Carter's judgment and follows his directives with the seriousness of a career intelligence officer.
- **[[inspector-marriot|Inspector Marriot]]** — A [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] detective who provides Tommy with cases and acts as a liaison between the agency and official police channels. Their professional relationship is built on mutual respect.

## Appearances

Tommy appears throughout all seventeen chapters of *Partners in Crime*, either in the office, on investigations, or at home with Tuppence. He is central to every case the agency takes on. Key chapters featuring Tommy in a leading investigative role include *The Affair of the Pink Pearl*, *Finessing the King*, *The Case of the Missing Lady*, *The Crackler*, *The Sunningdale Mystery*, and the climactic *The Man Who Was No. 16*.
