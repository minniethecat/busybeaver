## Overview

[[blunts-brilliant-detectives|Blunt's Brilliant Detectives]] is the public-facing commercial brand of the [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Agency]]. It is the name advertised in the *Daily Leader* and presented to clients — a carefully crafted fiction of institutional scale and professional competence that masks the reality of two enthusiastic amateurs and an office boy.

## The Advertisement

[[international-detective-agency|The Agency]]'s advertisement reads: "The [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Agency]]. [[theodore-blunt|Theodore Blunt]], Manager. Private Inquiries. Large staff of confidential and highly skilled Inquiry Agents. Utmost discretion. Consultations free. 118 Haleham St.WC." (001-084). The phrase "large staff" refers, in actuality, to Tommy, Tuppence, and [[albert|Albert]]. "Utmost discretion" is genuine; the fact that there are no staff to be indiscreet merely reinforces it.

## The Business Model

[[international-detective-agency|The Agency]]'s fee structure is deliberately flexible. For straightforward cases, a fixed rate applies. Tuppence introduces a "special twenty-four hour service" with doubled fees — a tactic that impresses clients even when, as in "[[pot-of-tea|A Pot of Tea]]," the solution was prearranged (002-012). By the final cases, the Agency commands substantial fees, including a percentage of recovered treasure in "[[red-house|The Red House]]" (015-020).

## The Brand Paradox

The most remarkable feature of [[blunts-brilliant-detectives|Blunt's Brilliant Detectives]] is that it becomes what it pretends to be. By the end of the collection, the Agency has solved murders, dismantled smuggling rings, rounded up counterfeiters, and neutralized a foreign spy. The "large staff of confidential and highly skilled Inquiry Agents" is still just two people — but they have earned the description.

## Significance

The firm represents the blurring of commercial enterprise and state service. [[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]]'s intelligence operation subsidizes a legitimate business that, in turn, produces genuine results. The advertisement in the *Daily Leader* is both a cover story and, increasingly, the truth.
