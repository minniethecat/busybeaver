## Overview

**Dr. Horriston** is a medical practitioner referenced in Chapter 7, "[[case-of-the-missing-lady|The Case of the Missing Lady]]." His involvement in the case stems from a new treatment he administers — injections of an unspecified serum — that plays a role in the disappearance of [[mr-stavansson|Mr. Stavansson]]'s fiancée.

## Identification

Dr. Horriston is recognised by an observer near the nursing home where the missing lady was last seen:

> "Through a screen of bushes, he recognised Dr. Horriston." (007-120)

This identification links the doctor directly to the location and events surrounding the disappearance.

## The Treatment

The doctor's practice involves a novel medical procedure:

> "He had gone off to do this new treatment of Dr. Horriston's. It's injections of some sort." (007-175)

The vague description of "injections of some sort" contributes to the air of medical mystery surrounding the case. Whether the treatment is legitimate or a cover for more sinister activities is a question [[tommy-beresford|Tommy Beresford]] must investigate.

## Role in the Case

[[case-of-the-missing-lady|The Case of the Missing Lady]] involves a fiancée who vanishes after entering a nursing home. Dr. Horriston's treatment — and his presence at the facility — places him at the intersection of medical care and potential foul play. The nature of his injections and their effect on the missing woman are central to unravelling the mystery.
