## Overview

**Cicely March** is a young lady who visits [[tommy-beresford|Tommy Beresford]] at the [[international-detective-agency|detective agency]] in Chapter 16, "[[ambassadors-boots|The Ambassador's Boots]]." Her visit sets in motion a chain of events involving a physical struggle and a dramatic confrontation.

## Introduction at the Agency

Miss March arrives at the agency as a client seeking assistance:

> "A young lady to see you, sir. Miss Cicely March. She says she has called in a—" (016-115)

She is shown into Tommy's office, where the meeting commences:

> "Cicely March entered, and Tommy followed." (016-190)

## The Confrontation

### Passage Through the Rooms

After the initial meeting, Cicely March moves through the adjoining rooms, with Tommy in pursuit:

> "Cicely March passed straight on through a—" (016-192)

The situation escalates into physical danger. Tommy witnesses her in distress:

> "Just in front of him was Cicely March wrestling vainly to free herself." (016-194)

### Resolution

Following the struggle, Miss March's reaction is one of astonishment:

> "Cicely March was looking at him with a startled expression." (016-199)

## Role in "The Ambassador's Boots"

The case of "[[ambassadors-boots|The Ambassador's Boots]]" involves a mix-up of luggage aboard a transatlantic liner. Cicely March's involvement connects to this central mystery, though the precise nature of her role — whether she is a genuine client, an unwitting pawn, or connected to the substitution scheme — unfolds through the investigation.

## Character Traits

From the limited but dramatic appearances in the chapter, Cicely March is depicted as a determined young woman who takes initiative by calling upon the detective agency. Her physical struggle suggests she is caught up in circumstances beyond her control, and her startled response to Tommy's intervention indicates the danger was unexpected.
