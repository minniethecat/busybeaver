## Overview

**Mary Chilcott** is a young woman staying as a house guest at Thurnly Grange in Chapter 12, "[[house-of-lurking-death|The House of Lurking Death]]." Described as an old school friend, she is present during the mysterious poisoning that [[tommy-beresford|Tommy Beresford]] is called to investigate.

## Arrival at Thurnly Grange

Mary Chilcott is introduced as one of the residents at the house where the poisoning occurs:

> "There is a girl called Mary Chilcott, an old school friend of mine, staying with us." (012-056)

Her connection to the household is through friendship rather than family, placing her among the circle of young people gathered at the country house — each of whom may have a motive or may be a potential victim.

## Reaction to the Crime

When the poisoning is discovered and the investigation begins, Mary Chilcott expresses her distress directly to [[tommy-beresford|Tommy Beresford]], who is investigating under the [[theodore-blunt|Theodore Blunt]] persona:

> "Thank heaven you have come, Mr. Blunt," said Mary Chilcott. "This affair seems too terrible." (012-122)

Her relief at the detective's arrival and her description of the affair as "too terrible" reflect the atmosphere of fear that grips the household after the poisoning.

## Personal Impression

After the case concludes, Tommy reflects on the people involved:

> "Tommy—did you like Mary Chilcott?" (012-201)

This question, posed to Tommy by his wife [[tuppence-beresford|Tuppence]], suggests that Mary Chilcott made enough of an impression — whether positive or ambiguous — to warrant discussion after the case was resolved.
