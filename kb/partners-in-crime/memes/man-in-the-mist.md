## Overview

"The Man in the Mist" begins with professional embarrassment — a case at [[adlington-hall|Adlington Hall]] solved by the local police while Tommy and Tuppence were still in disguise — and ends with a murder investigation in a fog-shrouded village. Tommy, still dressed as a Roman Catholic priest, encounters the beautiful actress [[gilda-glen|Gilda Glen]] and her volatile former lover, leading to a crime that appears impossible.

## The Adlington Failure

Tommy and Tuppence have been called to [[adlington-hall|Adlington Hall]] to find a stolen pearl necklace. Tommy, disguised as a priest, is tracking a gambling Countess while Tuppence works the golf links. The case collapses when the local Inspector arrests the second footman — a known thief who confesses immediately. The Beresfords retreat to the Grand Adlington Hotel to console themselves with cocktails (009-001).

## The Chance Encounter

At the hotel, they run into an old friend, Mervyn "Bulger" Estcourt, who is escorting the famous actress [[gilda-glen|Gilda Glen]]. [[gilda-glen|Miss Glen]] is struck by Tommy's clerical appearance and asks to visit the local church with him — a meeting that draws the jealousy of [[james-reilly|James Reilly]], her estranged lover (009-007).

## The Crime

[[gilda-glen|Gilda Glen]] makes an appointment to meet Tommy at the vicarage that evening. When Tommy and Tuppence arrive, a thick mist covers the village. A policeman they pass confirms he saw [[gilda-glen|Miss Glen]] enter the gate moments earlier. Minutes later, [[james-reilly|James Reilly]] bursts out of the house in a frenzy — and [[gilda-glen|Gilda Glen]] is found dead upstairs, struck on the head (009-015).

## The Investigation

All evidence points to Reilly: he had motive (jealousy), opportunity, and the policeman saw no one else enter or leave. But Tommy is troubled by the case. He realizes the logical flaw: people assume footsteps going upstairs mean someone entering, but they could equally mean someone leaving. The policeman's sudden appearance from the gate, the fog, the maid's movements — all were misinterpreted (009-025).

## The Resolution

Tommy identifies the real killer: the policeman himself — [[gilda-glen|Gilda Glen]]'s secret husband. He had encountered her at the gate, taken her inside, and killed her with his truncheon in a jealous rage. His official presence gave him perfect cover. The "man in the mist" was never a mysterious stranger — he wore a uniform no one questions (009-028).

## Significance

The case is a masterclass in the fallibility of eyewitness testimony. Every witness — the policeman, [[mrs-honeycott|Mrs. Honeycott]], the maid — reported honestly what they perceived, yet all were wrong. Tommy's insight about the "two ends of the stick" — that the same evidence can point in opposite directions — anticipates modern cognitive psychology's findings about perception and testimony.
