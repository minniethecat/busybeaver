## Overview

Monica Deane is a client of the [[international-detective-agency]] who appears in Chapters 14–15, "[[house-of-lurking-death|The House of Lurking Death]]." The daughter of a clergyman, she seeks [[tommy-beresford]] and [[tuppence-beresford]]'s help after receiving a mysterious box of poisoned chocolates — a threat connected to an inheritance she is due to receive.

## Background

Monica Deane introduces herself to Tuppence: "My name is Monica Deane. My father was the rector of Little Hamsley" (014-020). She is a modest young woman, unaccustomed to wealth, who stands to inherit a considerable fortune upon reaching a certain age. This inheritance, however, has made her the target of foul play.

Tuppence studied Monica carefully during their first meeting, noting her sincerity. Monica blushed when questioned (014-048), revealing a shy and honest disposition. She worried about "terms" (014-061), indicating her modest financial circumstances.

## The Poisoned Chocolates

Monica received a box of chocolates through the post, which she brought to the agency. Testing revealed the chocolates were laced with poison — someone was attempting to murder her before she could claim her inheritance. The Beresfords took the case seriously, recognizing the genuine danger to their client.

> Tuppence had been eyeing Monica keenly, now she spoke with sudden decision. "We will take the case." (014-044)

The investigation led to Monica's family home, where Tommy and Tuppence uncovered a plot involving household members seeking to prevent Monica from receiving her money. The treasure was eventually secured: "The treasure is ours — or rather Monica's" (015-080).

## Happy Resolution

Through the Beresfords' detective work, Monica not only survived the attempt on her life but secured her inheritance. Tommy reflected: "Isn't it lucky for Monica that we're both rich and honest?" (015-131). With her newfound wealth, Monica could now "marry her nice young man" (015-134), a happy conclusion to a dangerous case.

## Personality

Monica is gentle, sincere, and somewhat naive about the world's dangers. She murmurs "something noncommittal" (015-050) when faced with awkward questions, suggesting a reserved nature. Her father's identity as a clergyman shaped her moral character, and her gratitude toward the Beresfords is genuine.

## Appearances

Monica Deane is central to Chapters 14–15, "[[house-of-lurking-death|The House of Lurking Death]]," where the attempt on her life provides one of the most dangerous cases the Beresfords face.
