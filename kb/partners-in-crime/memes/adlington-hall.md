## Overview

Adlington Hall is a country estate where [[blunts-brilliant-detectives|Blunt's Brilliant Detectives]] suffer one of their few professional humiliations. Called in to investigate a stolen pearl necklace, Tommy and Tuppence deploy elaborate disguises and surveillance — only to be beaten to the solution by the local Inspector of Police, who unemotionally arrests the second footman (009-001).

## The Failed Case

Tommy, disguised as a Roman Catholic priest, tracks a gambling Countess, while Tuppence works the golf links cultivating the nephew of the house. Their efforts prove entirely unnecessary: the second footman, a known thief, confesses without resistance. The Beresfords withdraw "with what dignity they could muster" to the Grand Adlington Hotel (009-002).

## Aftermath

The Adlington failure becomes a running joke between Tommy and Tuppence. It is from the Grand Adlington Hotel that they encounter Bulger Estcourt and [[gilda-glen|Gilda Glen]], launching the "Man in the Mist" case that redeems their professional reputation. The failure thus functions narratively as the low point that precedes the Beresfords' most ingenious deduction.

## Significance

Adlington Hall represents the reality that even the most enthusiastic amateurs sometimes lose to routine police work. The case is a humbling reminder that elaborate methods are not always superior to simple ones — a lesson the Beresfords absorb without fully admitting.
