## Overview

Dr. Charles Bower presents himself as a Hampstead physician seeking the services of the [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Agency]] — but this persona is a carefully constructed façade. In reality, "Dr. Bower" is the alias of a notorious criminal known as **Clubfoot**, a mastermind who walks with a pronounced limp and possesses formidable physical strength. His consultation with [[tommy-beresford]] and [[tuppence-beresford]] in "[[finessing-the-king]]" (Chapter 3) is an elaborate trap — and Tommy recognises it as such from the outset (003-037).

## Physical Description

Bower is described as a "big man" with a "massive" physique and a decided limp — the characteristic that earned him the underworld moniker "Clubfoot" (003-037). Tommy assesses him as "an ugly customer to tackle" despite his lameness, noting the evident power in his frame (003-037). His voice is described as harsh, adding to an overall impression of latent menace.

## The Consultation

Bower's cover story is elaborate and carefully constructed. He claims to be a doctor living at The Larches, Hangman's Lane, Hampstead, where he has been researching "certain obscure alkaloids" — deadly and virtually untraceable poisons (003-018). According to his account, someone has twice lured him away from his home with fake medical summonses and ransacked his desk in search of his research notes. He names his two nephews as possible suspects: the "good boy" Henry and the "wild, extravagant, and persistently idle" Bertram (003-018).

He proposes a plan: he will pretend to be deceived by another fake summons to Bournemouth, and Tommy should meet him outside his house at eleven o'clock that night to catch the intruder in the act (003-037).

## Tuppence's Recognition

The moment Bower departs, [[tuppence-beresford]] delivers her verdict in a single word: *"Clubfoot!"* (003-037) She has recognised the limping giant from newspaper descriptions of a wanted criminal. The entire consultation, she realises, is a ruse — yet Tommy, far from being alarmed, decides to "walk into a trap with my eyes open," anticipating that Dr. Bower will "get a little surprise" (003-037).
