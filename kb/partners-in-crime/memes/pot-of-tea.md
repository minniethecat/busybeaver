## Overview

"A Pot of Tea" chronicles the opening of the [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Agency]] and its very first client. [[tommy-beresford|Tommy]], as the pseudo [[theodore-blunt|Mr. Blunt]], and [[tuppence-beresford|Tuppence]], as his efficient secretary, set up their offices in Bloomsbury. Their first case — a young man searching for a vanished sweetheart — proves to be less a mystery and more a matchmaking opportunity.

## The Agency Opens

The Beresfords take possession of the offices on the second floor of a dilapidated building in Bloomsbury. [[albert|Albert]] transforms from Long Island butler to office boy, complete with "a paper bag of sweets, inky hands, and a tousled head" (002-004). Tommy occupies the private office as "[[theodore-blunt|Mr. Blunt]]," while Tuppence mans the adjoining room with a typewriter and gas ring for tea. Everything is in place — except clients. After a week without business, they grow discouraged (002-013).

## The Client

Their first client is [[lawrence-st-vincent|Lawrence St. Vincent]], a nervous young man who has lost track of a woman he met at a hat shop, Madame Violette's. He is clearly smitten — describing her hair as "sort of golden, but very deep, like a jolly old sunset" (002-008). Tuppence takes over the interview from Tommy, and with characteristic confidence guarantees results within twenty-four hours under their "special service" (002-012).

## The Resolution

Tuppence's guaranteed solution is no miracle: she has orchestrated the entire situation. The missing woman, Janet Smith, is in fact her old nursing colleague from hospital days. Tuppence arranged for Janet to encourage St. Vincent's interest and then disappear — all to prompt him to propose. Janet is found in Tuppence's office making tea (002-016). The case ends with the three of them drinking to the success of "[[blunts-brilliant-detectives|Blunt's Brilliant Detectives]]."

## Significance

This case establishes the pattern for the lighter side of the Agency's work. While Tommy approaches detection methodically, Tuppence relies on intuition, social connections, and a flair for the dramatic. Her "twenty-four hour special service" — though fraudulent in this instance — becomes an Agency hallmark.
