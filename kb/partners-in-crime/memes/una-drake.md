## Overview

Una Drake is the central figure in Chapter 13, "[[unbreakable-alibi|The Unbreakable Alibi]]." An Australian woman with a taste for detective fiction and elaborate games, she devises a scheme to test her suitor [[montgomery-jones]]'s devotion — creating an apparently impossible alibi by appearing to be in two locations simultaneously.

## Background

Una Drake lives at 180 Clarges Street (013-065) and moves in fashionable London circles. She is friends with people like Janet St. Vincent and the Le Marchants. Una is described by those who know her as someone who is "just as keen on detective stories" as Montgomery Jones (013-048), suggesting she shares his interest in crime fiction.

## The Unbreakable Alibi

The mystery that Montgomery Jones brings to the [[international-detective-agency]] is entirely of Una's making. She constructed an elaborate alibi puzzle: appearing to have been in London dining at the Savoy on Tuesday evening while simultaneously being seen on a train to Torquay, hundreds of miles away. Jones was baffled, but the Beresfords quickly suspected a game: "Una has got some little game on" (013-090).

> "I know it was Tuesday because Una impressed it on me at the time." (013-092)

The resolution revealed the key: Una had a **twin sister, Vera**. "Una has a twin sister, Vera, who lives in Torquay" (013-230). The sister traveled to Torquay while Una remained in London, creating the illusion of an impossible alibi. The entire scheme was a test of Jones's resourcefulness — and, by extension, his devotion.

## Key Relationships

- **[[montgomery-jones]]** — Her suitor, whose detective abilities (or lack thereof) she tested with her alibi scheme. The Beresfords' successful resolution allowed Jones to prove his dedication.
- **Vera Drake** — Una's twin sister, living in Torquay, whose physical similarity to Una made the alibi scheme possible. She cooperated with Una's plan (013-230).

## Personality

Una is playful, clever, and slightly mischievous. Her elaborate alibi test reveals a woman with a sense of humor and a love of mystery. Far from malicious, her scheme was designed to be solved — she wanted Jones to succeed, perhaps testing whether he cared enough to hire competent help.

## Appearances

Una Drake appears in Chapter 13, "[[unbreakable-alibi|The Unbreakable Alibi]]," where her twin-based alibi puzzle provides the Beresfords with one of their more lighthearted cases.
