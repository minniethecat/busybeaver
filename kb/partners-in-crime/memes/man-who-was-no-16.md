## Overview

"The Man Who Was No. 16" is the climactic finale of the Beresfords' career as [[blunts-brilliant-detectives|Blunt's Brilliant Detectives]]. [[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]] warns that a legendary Russian agent — the creator of the No. 16 code — has been dispatched from Moscow. Tommy must match wits with a master of disguise while racing to rescue Tuppence from a kidnapping at the Blitz Hotel.

## The Warning

[[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]] summons Tommy and Tuppence for their final briefing. The intelligence network they have been tracking has gone silent — Moscow suspects the [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Bureau]] is compromised. A special agent, known only as No. 16, is en route. He is "a past master in the art of disguise," an accomplished linguist who can pass as multiple nationalities, and the architect of the original code (017-001).

A dictaphone is installed in the Agency office, and the passwords are confirmed: mention of the number sixteen, answered with a sentence containing the same number; and the inquiry "Have you ever crossed the Channel?" answered with "I was in Berlin on the 13th of last month" (017-005).

## The Confrontation

The trap is sprung when a nurse and a young boy arrive at the office, claiming Tuppence has been taken to the Blitz Hotel. Tommy follows the trail to Room 318, where [[mrs-van-snyder|Mrs. Van Snyder]], an American lady, reports that she was drugged and her nephew impersonated. But Tommy's "little grey cells" detect the impossibility: too much happened in five minutes for the story to be credible (017-010).

Tommy returns to the Blitz with [[albert|Albert]] and Evans. At gunpoint, he orders [[mrs-van-snyder|Mrs. Van Snyder]] off the bed. Beneath the bolster, lying horizontally across the bed frame, is Tuppence — drugged but alive. Tommy seizes [[mrs-van-snyder|Mrs. Van Snyder]]'s elaborately dressed hair, which comes off in his hand. "As I thought," he declares. "*No. 16!*" (017-015).

## Aftermath

The superspy is taken into custody by [[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]]'s men. Tuppence recovers under a doctor's care and, in the final pages, announces that their detective career is over — not because of the danger, but because of "Our Baby." The Beresfords retire from [[blunts-brilliant-detectives|Blunt's Brilliant Detectives]] to begin a new chapter of their lives (017-020).

## Significance

The finale is both a satisfying resolution to the espionage arc and a farewell to the Agency. Tommy's climactic deduction — that [[mrs-van-snyder|Mrs. Van Snyder]] could not have been both victim and perpetrator in the available time — is his most methodical feat of detection. The revelation of Tuppence's pregnancy provides the emotional coda: the adventure that began with "I wish something would happen" in Chapter 1 ends with the greatest adventure of all.
