## Overview

**Mrs. Sessle** is the wife of [[captain-sessle|Captain Anthony Sessle]], whose murder forms the central mystery of Chapter 11, "[[sunningdale-mystery|The Sunningdale Mystery]]." As the victim's spouse, Mrs. Sessle is inevitably drawn into the investigation conducted by [[tommy-beresford|Tommy Beresford]].

## Marriage to Captain Sessle

Mrs. Sessle was married to [[captain-sessle|Captain Anthony Sessle]], a military man whose death at a golf course — apparently by a sunstroke, but later determined to be murder — brings the Beresfords into the case. The circumstances of the marriage and Mrs. Sessle's relationship with her husband become matters of investigative interest.

## Role in the Investigation

As the widow of the murdered man, Mrs. Sessle occupies a dual position in the narrative: she is both a grieving spouse deserving of sympathy and a potential suspect with a motive. In the tradition of detective fiction, the spouse is always a person of interest, and the [[sunningdale|Sunningdale]] Mystery is no exception. Tommy's investigation must determine whether Mrs. Sessle's grief is genuine or whether she had a hand in her husband's death.

## The Widow's Position

The murder leaves Mrs. Sessle in a precarious social and financial position. As a military widow, her circumstances — inheritance, social standing, and future prospects — all hang in the balance pending the resolution of the case. The outcome of Tommy's investigation will determine not only who killed [[captain-sessle|Captain Sessle]] but also what becomes of the woman he left behind.
