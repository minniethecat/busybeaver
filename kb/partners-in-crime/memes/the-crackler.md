## Overview

"The Crackler" is Tommy's first major undercover assignment with [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]]. [[inspector-marriot|Inspector Marriot]] recruits him to infiltrate a gang distributing counterfeit notes across London. The case introduces Tommy to the seductive [[marguerite-laidlaw|Marguerite Laidlaw]] and culminates in a battle of wits with the American gangster [[mr-ryder|Hank Ryder]], who believes he has outsmarted the celebrated [[theodore-blunt|Mr. Blunt]].

## The Assignment

[[inspector-marriot|Inspector Marriot]] arrives at the Agency with a proposition: would "[[blunts-brilliant-detectives|Blunt's Brilliant Detectives]]" help round up a major counterfeiting ring? Tommy, excited by the prospect of an Edgar Wallace-style case, agrees. He is to pose as a criminal contact, a man who can "move" large quantities of forged notes (010-003).

## The Infiltration

Tommy insinuates himself into the gang, working through a series of intermediaries. At the center of the operation is [[marguerite-laidlaw|Marguerite Laidlaw]], a beautiful and dangerous woman who tests Tommy's loyalties. The gang's leader, [[mr-ryder|Hank Ryder]] — known in underworld circles as "The Crackler" — is an American with a genial smile and complete ruthlessness (010-012).

## The Trap

Ryder, it transpires, has been onto Tommy from the start. He leads Tommy to a house in Soho, confident that he has trapped the detective. "I've been laughing fit to bust all this evening — leading you here like a little child," he gloats (010-020). Ryder has even anticipated Tommy's signal — a chalk cross on the door — having heard the fairy tale as a boy and marked every door in the alley with an identical cross.

## The Counter-Trap

Tommy, however, has outsmarted the outsmarted. While marking the chalk cross to engage Ryder's attention, he also emptied a bottle of valerian on the ground. The pungent smell attracted every cat in the neighborhood, who assembled outside the correct door — a living signal for [[albert|Albert]], who had followed on his motorcycle, and for the police (010-025).

[[inspector-marriot|Inspector Marriot]] bursts in, and Tommy introduces him to "[[mr-ryder|Mr. Hank Ryder]], who knows all the best fairy tales." The counterfeiting ring is dismantled.

## Significance

The chapter's title — Tommy's own coinage for the counterfeiter — never appears in the criminal dictionaries. It is pure Beresford invention. The case demonstrates Tommy's growth as an operative: he anticipates his opponent's counter-moves and layers multiple signals (chalk cross as decoy, valerian as real marker) rather than relying on a single trick.
