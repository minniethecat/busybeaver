## Overview

Mr. Hollaby is a partner at the Porcupine Assurance Company and the friend and business associate of [[captain-sessle|Captain Sessle]]. He is present at the [[sunningdale|Sunningdale]] golf course on the day Sessle is murdered, making him both a key witness and, initially, a potential suspect in the [[sunningdale-mystery]]. Tommy describes him as "old Hollaby" (011-098), suggesting a man of established business reputation and mature years.

## The Day of the Murder

On the day of [[captain-sessle|Captain Sessle]]'s death, Hollaby and Sessle are playing golf together at [[sunningdale|Sunningdale]]. The sequence of events is critical to the investigation: "Sessle and old Hollaby are holing out on the sixth green" (011-098) when Sessle, in apparently good spirits, asks Hollaby to hurry "before it got too dark to see" (011-033).

Hollaby's position on the course provides a timeline reference. He "was replacing the pin in the hole" (011-034) shortly before Sessle's death, and his account of Sessle's final minutes is central to reconstructing the crime. He mentions to two other golfers — Mr. Board and Mr. Lecky — Sessle's "sudden indisposition" (011-037), which later proves to be fatal.

## As Witness

Hollaby's testimony carries weight because he knew the victim well and was in close proximity at the time of death. He can identify individuals connected to the case: "Mr. Hollaby knows her well by sight" (011-052), referring to a woman seen near the golf course. His knowledge of Sessle's personal and professional affairs makes him an invaluable source of information.

The investigation also touches on Hollaby's own business circumstances. The Porcupine Assurance Company faces financial difficulties connected to the case, and Tommy notes "the Hollabys? They say they're ruined" (011-074), suggesting the murder has wider financial repercussions.

## The Alibi

A pivotal element of the case is Hollaby's apparent alibi. [[tommy-beresford|Tommy]] observes that "Hollaby knew all right" (011-114), pointing to the fact that Hollaby possessed information he did not initially disclose. However, the investigation ultimately clears Hollaby, whose "irreproachable alibi" (011-116) withstands scrutiny.

[[inspector-marriot|Inspector Marriot]] acknowledges that [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] "had our suspicions of those Hollabys, but nothing to go upon" (011-121). The suspicion lingers through much of the investigation but is eventually laid to rest when the true culprit is identified.

## Relationship to the Case

Mr. Hollaby serves as the respectable businessman witness — a familiar type in detective fiction. His presence on the golf course, his professional connection to the victim, and his apparent candor all mark him as the kind of witness whose testimony seems reliable but must still be verified. The resolution of the [[sunningdale|Sunningdale]] case depends in part on Tommy's ability to see past the surface plausibility of Hollaby's account and identify the hidden truth, aided by the testimony of [[doris-evans]].
