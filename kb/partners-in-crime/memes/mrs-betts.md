## Overview

Mrs. Betts is a household member at the [[kingston-bruce]] residence who becomes entangled in the investigation of a stolen pearl in Chapter 3, "[[finessing-the-king|Finessing the King]]." Her relationship with [[kingston-bruce|Colonel Kingston Bruce]] and her presence at the scene of the theft make her a person of interest in [[tommy-beresford]]'s inquiry.

## Background

Mrs. Betts lives in the Kingston Bruce household with her husband, Mr. Betts. She was present at the tea party during which the pearl disappeared. The pearl had been left "lying where Mrs. Betts had left it" (003-072) — a detail that initially pointed suspicion in her direction, as she was one of the last people known to have handled the item.

## The Investigation

Tommy immediately recognized Mrs. Betts as a key witness: "I should like to know one thing — what is Mrs. Betts' attitude in all this?" (003-085). He requested a private interview with her, and "Mrs. Betts, followed by her husband, burst into the room" (003-106) — her entrance suggesting both eagerness to cooperate and possible nervousness.

> "Let me ask you a few questions, Mrs. Betts," said Tommy smoothly. (003-108)

Tommy observed "hesitancy on the faces of [[kingston-bruce|Colonel Kingston Bruce]] and Mrs. Betts" (003-115) when certain topics arose, suggesting an unspoken tension between them. The investigation revealed that the hiding place for the stolen pearl was "in the apartments of Mrs. Betts" (003-125) — a fact that seemed damning until Tommy explained his reasoning.

Mrs. Betts reacted to Tommy's deductive skills with genuine admiration: "Wouldn't that be too cute?" (003-126), apparently untroubled by the suspicion that had fallen on her. Tommy requested her permission for "my assistant's looking through your rooms" (003-129), and she cooperated fully. She was eventually "departed" (003-131) from the investigation, cleared of wrongdoing.

## Personality

Mrs. Betts is described as somewhat naive and easily impressed — her "admiring" response to Tommy's deductions suggests a simple, straightforward character. Her willingness to cooperate and lack of defensiveness ultimately helped clear her name.

## Appearances

Mrs. Betts appears in Chapter 3, "[[finessing-the-king|Finessing the King]]," as part of the Kingston Bruce household during the pearl theft investigation.
