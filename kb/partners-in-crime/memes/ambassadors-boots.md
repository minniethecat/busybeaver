## Overview

"The Ambassador's Boots" begins with a trivial mystery — a switched kitbag — and spirals into one of the Agency's most significant cases: the dismantling of a cocaine distribution ring operating through a Bond Street beauty salon. Tommy's investigation demonstrates the Agency's evolution from amateur sleuthing to professional criminal intelligence work.

## The Initial Problem

The United States Ambassador, [[mr-wilmott|Randolph Wilmott]], consults the Agency about a peculiar incident. On arrival by liner, his kitbag was swapped with one belonging to Senator Ralph Westerham — or so the valet was told. When the Ambassador mentioned the mix-up to the real [[senator-westerham|Senator Westerham]], the Senator denied all knowledge. Someone had used Westerham's name to access the Ambassador's luggage for roughly an hour (016-001).

Tommy questions why anyone would want the Ambassador's bag temporarily. The bag contained boots — nothing of apparent value. Reversing the question proves illuminating: perhaps the *other* bag was what mattered, and someone wanted it to pass through Customs as diplomatic luggage, which is exempt from inspection (016-006).

## The Investigation

Tommy advertises for information about the kitbag and receives a visit from [[cicely-march|Cicely March]], a woman with "golden hair" who claims to have been on the same liner. Tommy, schooled by Tuppence, recognizes the hair as peroxide — a deception. When her story is interrupted by a staged attack from a mysterious stranger, Tommy notes that she shows dismay rather than fear. He plays along, feigning credulity (016-010).

The trail leads to Cyclamen Ltd., a Bond Street beauty salon. Tommy, deliberately delaying their arrival to give the police time to set up, enters with [[cicely-march|Cicely March]] — and walks into a trap. A chloroform-soaked cloth is thrown over his head (016-015).

## The Resolution

The trap is itself trapped. [[tuppence-beresford|Tuppence]], following Tommy's written instructions, has alerted [[inspector-marriot|Scotland Yard]]. Inspector Grace and another officer, posing as bored husbands in the salon's waiting area, spring into action. The salon's stock of "bath salts" — pink crystals on top, pure cocaine beneath — is discovered in a wall safe (016-020).

[[cicely-march|Cicely March]] (real name: O'Hara) and her accomplices are arrested. Tommy explains his reasoning to the Ambassador: the kitbag switch was a smuggling operation, using diplomatic immunity to move narcotics through Customs. The case earns [[blunts-brilliant-detectives|Blunt's Brilliant Detectives]] both a substantial fee and [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]]'s enduring gratitude.

## Significance

This case shows Tommy operating at full professional capacity: he anticipates the enemy's moves, layers deception within deception, and coordinates with the police while maintaining his cover. Tuppence's role — following the written plan and leading the backup team — demonstrates their evolution into a genuine operational partnership rather than a detective-and-secretary arrangement.
