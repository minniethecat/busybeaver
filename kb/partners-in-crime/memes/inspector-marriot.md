## Overview

Inspector Marriot is a [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] detective who serves as an ally and liaison to Tommy and Tuppence throughout their investigations. Short and thick-set with shrewd eyes, he is a capable professional who respects the Beresfords' abilities while maintaining the official authority of [[scotland-yard|the Yard]].

## Relationship with the Agency

Marriot provides the Beresfords with cases that fall outside normal police jurisdiction or require unofficial investigation. He knows the true nature of the [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Agency]] — that it is a cover operation — but respects its boundaries: "It's not our department, and we don't interfere" (005-005).

When the Beresfords find themselves in genuine danger, Marriot arrives with armed backup. In one critical instance, he and two men "armed with revolvers stepped into the room, with the sharp command: 'Hands Up!'" (007-016), apprehending a ring of international criminals.

## Detective Philosophy

Tuppence articulates Marriot's philosophy late in the novel: professional detectives "must know all about criminals. They know the real thing when they see it — and they know when it isn't the real thing" (009-010). Marriot's professional instinct tells him when the obvious suspect is not the true criminal, which leads him to involve the Beresfords in cases that require unconventional investigation.

## Key Appearances

- **[[affair-of-the-pink-pearl|The Affair of the Pink Pearl]]**: Marriot first appears investigating the theft at [[the-laurels|The Laurels]], establishing his working relationship with the agency. He recognizes the Beresfords' unofficial investigative value.
- **[[finessing-the-king|Finessing the King]]**: Marriot consults with Tommy about a coded death announcement, demonstrating his willingness to share sensitive police intelligence with the agency.
- **[[case-of-the-missing-lady|The Case of the Missing Lady]]**: Marriot provides background on the missing person case, allowing Tommy and Tuppence to pursue leads outside normal police procedures.
- **[[ch17-the-man-who-was-no-16|The Man Who Was No. 16]]**: In the climactic case, Marriot and his men provide critical armed backup when the Beresfords confront an international criminal ring (004-001).
- He provides critical backup during the takedown of a spy ring operating against the agency (007-016).
- He brings the case of the missing lady, which proves to be one of the most complex investigations (008-001).
- In the [[sunningdale|Sunningdale]] mystery, Marriot repeatedly hints that the case is not as clear-cut as it appears, hoping Tuppence will find the true solution (009-010).

## Personality

Marriot is described as imperturbable — even in the face of life-threatening situations, he remains calm and professional. He is pragmatic, willing to work outside formal channels when the situation demands it, and he values the unique perspective the Beresfords bring to detective work.
