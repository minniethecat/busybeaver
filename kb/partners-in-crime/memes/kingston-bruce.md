## Overview

Colonel Charles Kingston Bruce is a retired military officer and client of the [[international-detective-agency]] in Chapter 3, "[[finessing-the-king|Finessing the King]]." A pearl of considerable value has been stolen during a tea party at his home, and he engages [[tommy-beresford]] and [[tuppence-beresford]] to recover it under circumstances of some delicacy.

## Background

Colonel Kingston Bruce lives with his wife, Mrs. Kingston Bruce — described as "a plaintive lady with a languishing air" (003-098) — and his daughter, Beatrice Kingston Bruce. The household also includes their domestic staff. The Colonel is a man of some means, owning a valuable collection that includes the stolen pearl.

Though initially reluctant to involve the police — he mentions having been "in the police" himself, "when we were in India" (003-086) — he eventually turns to the Beresfords' agency.

> Colonel Kingston Bruce looked very impressive as he stood in the doorway. "You will understand, gentlemen, that this is a matter of the utmost delicacy." (003-079)

## The Stolen Pearl

The theft occurred during a tea party with friends of the Kingston Bruces. The pearl, part of the Colonel's collection, disappeared, and suspicion fell on various members of the household and guests. His daughter Beatrice was particularly affected: "I never touched it," she declared fiercely, "I was watching him all the time" (003-101).

[[mrs-betts|Mrs. Betts]], a member of the household staff, was also present and became entangled in the investigation. Tommy observed "hesitancy on the faces of Colonel Kingston Bruce and [[mrs-betts|Mrs. Betts]]" (003-115), suggesting complications beneath the surface of the case.

The Colonel gave Tommy full authority to investigate: "I give you carte blanche" (003-119), though his wife murmured tearful doubts: "Do you think that is wise, Charles?" (003-120).

## Personality

Colonel Kingston Bruce projects military bearing and authority, looking "very impressive as he stood in the doorway" (003-079). However, his authoritative manner masks uncertainty about the domestic situation unfolding in his household. He is protective of his family's reputation, which is why he sought private investigation over police involvement.

## Key Relationships

- **Beatrice Kingston Bruce** — His daughter, a central figure in the pearl theft investigation. She protested her innocence vigorously.
- **Mrs. Kingston Bruce** — His wife, a voluble and emotional woman who was "voluble" in conversation (003-099) and tearfully concerned about the investigation.
- **[[mrs-betts|Mrs. Betts]]** — A household member whose relationship with the Colonel complicated the investigation (003-115).

## Appearances

Colonel Kingston Bruce appears in Chapter 3, "[[finessing-the-king|Finessing the King]]," where his household becomes the setting for one of the Beresfords' early cases involving a stolen pearl and family secrets.
