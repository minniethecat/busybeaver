## Overview

Scotland Yard, the headquarters of London's Metropolitan Police, plays a supporting role throughout *[[partners-in-crime-novel|Partners in Crime]]*. Its detectives, particularly [[inspector-marriot|Inspector Marriot]], serve as official counterparts to Tommy and Tuppence's amateur agency, providing cases, backup, and the official authority the Beresfords sometimes require.

## Relationship with the Agency

Scotland Yard maintains a cooperative but arms-length relationship with the [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Agency]]. [[inspector-marriot|Inspector Marriot]] is the primary liaison, bringing cases that either fall outside normal police jurisdiction or require unofficial investigation. The Yard knows the true nature of the agency — that it is a [[british-secret-service|Secret Service]] cover — and "don't interfere" with its intelligence work (005-005).

When the Beresfords face genuine danger from international criminals, Scotland Yard provides armed backup. In the takedown of the spy ring at the agency office, [[inspector-marriot|Inspector Marriot]] arrives with "two men armed with revolvers" (007-016).

## Key Personnel

- **[[inspector-marriot|Inspector Marriot]]** — The primary liaison between Scotland Yard and the agency. Short, thick-set, with shrewd eyes, he values the Beresfords' unconventional methods while maintaining proper police procedure.

- **[[inspector-dymchurch|Inspector Dymchurch]]** — Another Yard detective of similar physical type to Marriot, who briefly appears in the investigation of visitors to the agency (005-004).

## Institutional Role

Scotland Yard represents the official, institutional approach to crime-solving that contrasts with the Beresfords' amateur, improvisational methods. The collaboration between the two is productive precisely because they operate differently: the Yard provides resources, authority, and procedural rigor, while Tommy and Tuppence contribute flexibility, intuition, and the freedom to pursue unconventional leads.

## Notable Interactions with the Agency

Scotland Yard's relationship with the [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Agency]] involves several significant collaborations:

- **The Pink Pearl Affair**: [[inspector-marriot|Inspector Marriot]] initially handles the jewel theft at [[the-laurels|The Laurels]] before recognizing the case could benefit from the Beresfords' unofficial approach.
- **The International Criminal Ring**: In the climactic confrontation, Marriot and armed officers from the Yard provide critical backup when Tommy and Tuppence face a dangerous international conspiracy.
- **Information Sharing**: Throughout the novel, Marriot serves as a conduit for police intelligence, sharing case details that allow the agency to investigate from angles unavailable to official police.

## Significance

Scotland Yard's willingness to work with the Beresfords reflects the changing nature of detective work in the early twentieth century, as professional police forces began to recognize the value of specialized private investigation. The collaboration also serves a narrative function, providing the Beresfords with access to cases and resources they could not obtain on their own.
