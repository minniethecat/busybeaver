## Overview

Mrs. Maurice Leigh Gordon — born the Honorable Hermione Crane, second daughter of Lord Lanchester — is a central figure in the case recounted in "[[ambassadors-boots]]" (Chapter 7). A young widow whose first husband was killed in the Great War, she became engaged to the noted explorer [[mr-stavansson|Gabriel Stavansson]] before his departure on a two-year Arctic expedition. Upon Stavansson's return, he found her vanished into a suspicious nursing home — setting in motion an investigation by [[tommy-beresford]] and [[tuppence-beresford]].

## Background

Hermione Crane was born into the aristocracy as the second daughter of Lord Lanchester (007-001). She married Maurice Leigh Gordon, who was killed in the First World War, leaving her a widow at a young age. Following her husband's death, she retained her married name — Mrs. Maurice Leigh Gordon — by which she was known in society (007-001). Approximately two years before the events of the story, she became engaged to Gabriel Stavansson, the celebrated Arctic explorer, shortly before his departure on an extended expedition to the North Pole (007-001).

## Disappearance

When Stavansson returned to England three days before consulting the [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Agency]], he expected to reunite with his fiancée. Instead, he found her at a nursing home where the staff denied her presence — claiming "there is nobody of the name of Mrs. Leigh Gordon here" (007-001). The situation was deeply suspicious: the nursing home's doctor spoke of her in hostile terms, referring to her as "the Leigh Gordon woman" and muttering that he would "have to take steps" (007-001).

The investigation by Tommy and Tuppence revealed that Mrs. Leigh Gordon was being held at the nursing home against her will. Tuppence infiltrated the facility and located her, communicating in whispers to avoid detection. The case culminated in a tense rescue (007-001).
