## Overview

Mr. Carter is the chief of the [[british-secret-service|Secret Service]] and Tommy's superior. A "tall grey-haired man with piercing eyes and a tired smile" (001-043), he is the catalyst for the novel's events: he offers Tommy the [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Agency]] as a cover for intelligence work, while also encouraging Tuppence's desire for adventure.

## The Agency Assignment

Mr. Carter visits the Beresfords at their flat with a proposal. The real [[theodore-blunt|Theodore Blunt]] has been detained by [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] for indiscretions, and Carter sees an opportunity. He suggests Tommy take a six-month leave from his desk job and operate the detective agency under Blunt's name (001-050).

The assignment has a dual purpose: Tommy and Tuppence can run legitimate detective investigations, while also monitoring letters with Russian stamps. When moistened, the stamp reveals the number 16 written underneath — a signal for intelligence communications. Any visitor referencing the number 16 is to be reported immediately (001-051).

## Personality

Carter is authoritative yet avuncular. He understands the Beresfords well — he knows Tuppence craves excitement and frames the assignment as something that "might amuse Mrs. Tommy to try her hand at a little detective work" (001-053). His tired smile suggests the weight of his responsibilities, but he retains a twinkling sense of humor.

## Key Instructions

Carter gives the Beresfords two primary directives when assigning them the agency:

1. **Monitor Russian Communications**: "All letters that come to the office with a Russian stamp on them are to be opened and read" (001-048). The real target is an agent known as Number 16 — the Beresfords must watch for the number 16 written under the stamp.

2. **Maintain the Cover**: The agency must operate convincingly as a legitimate detective business. "You must run the agency as though it were a genuine concern," Carter instructs, emphasizing that the cover's credibility is essential to the intelligence operation.

3. **Report to Carter Directly**: All intelligence findings go through Carter alone. The Beresfords are told not to involve [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] in the covert side of the operation, though they may collaborate with [[inspector-marriot|Inspector Marriot]] on legitimate cases.

## Role in the Plot

Carter appears primarily at the beginning to set events in motion and later when the situation escalates. He warns Tommy when "the hornet's nest we've stirred up is bigger than anyone imagined" (012-001), demonstrating his continuing oversight of the operation.
