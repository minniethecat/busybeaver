## Overview

"Blunt's Brilliant Detectives" is the self-appointed slogan of the [[international-detective-agency]], created by [[tommy-beresford]] and [[tuppence-beresford]] as both a piece of advertising copy and a knowing wink at the theatricality of their enterprise. The phrase combines the alias "[[theodore-blunt|Theodore Blunt]]" with self-promotional hyperbole — functioning simultaneously as genuine marketing, a private joke between the Beresfords, and a metafictional commentary on the conventions of detective fiction (001-047).

## Origin

The slogan emerges directly from the agency's founding documents. The newspaper advertisement that establishes the Blunt persona reads:

> "The International Detective Agency. Theodore Blunt, Manager. Private Inquiries. Large staff of confidential and highly skilled Inquiry Agents. Utmost discretion. Consultations free. 118 Haleham St. WC." (001-047)

The real [[theodore-blunt|Theodore Blunt]] has been detained by [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] "for rather indiscreet foreign business" (001-088), and [[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]] proposes Tommy assume the identity. The slogan crystallizes as the Beresfords settle into their roles at the [[beresford-flat]] before relocating to the agency — Tommy seated "behind the desk" as "the pseudo [[theodore-blunt|Mr. Blunt]] trying to look as though he had run a detective agency all his life" (002-002).

The phrase receives its formal toast after the agency's first successes:

> "Here's to the success of the International Detective Agency. Blunt's Brilliant Detectives! May they never know failure!" (002-152)

## The Theatrical Framework

The slogan is one component of a comprehensive theatrical apparatus that transforms a modest Bloomsbury office into a convincing detective firm. This framework includes:

| Element | Description | PN Source |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Office staging | Three rooms with "artistically labelled files, all empty" | (002-003) |
| Telephone theatre | Rehearsed phone calls: "They want [[theodore-blunt|Mr. Blunt]] to take up the case" | (002-050) |
| Typing sounds | Tuppence provides "a perfect fusillade of typing" to impress clients | (002-002) |
| Peephole surveillance | "Cunningly contrived peepholes" allow client screening | (002-011) |
| Busy persona | [[albert|Albert]] tells callers "[[theodore-blunt|Mr. Blunt]] is so terribly busy. He's engaged with [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]]" | (002-047) |

"Blunt's Brilliant Detectives" is the verbal equivalent of these visual and auditory props — a branding device that projects confidence and competence to clients while amusing its creators.

## Dual Significance

The slogan operates on three levels simultaneously:

1. **Genuine marketing**: It attracts clients. [[kingston-bruce|Colonel Kingston Bruce]] specifically mentions having "noticed your advertisements myself" (003-066), demonstrating the slogan's effectiveness as commercial copy.

2. **Private irony**: For Tommy and Tuppence, the phrase is an inside joke. They are, after all, former intelligence operatives playing detective — "amateurs in one sense we cannot help being" (003-005). The hyperbole of "Brilliant" carries a self-deprecating irony that only they fully appreciate.

3. **Metafictional commentary**: Tommy studies detective fiction to inform his personas, working through "Half hours with the Great Masters" (003-005). The slogan's knowing theatricality mirrors Christie's own awareness of the genre's conventions — it is simultaneously a celebration of and gentle parody of the detective fiction tradition.

## Usage in the Narrative

The slogan serves as a recurring motif throughout the novel. Chapter III opens with Tuppence entering "the inner sanctum of the [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Agency]] — (Slogan — Blunt's Brilliant Detectives)" (003-001). It appears in Tommy's newspaper advertisements and is invoked ironically when cases go wrong — "an infernal machine destined to blow Blunt's Brilliant Detectives to pieces" (004-005).

The phrase's durability across the narrative mirrors the resilience of the Beresfords' enterprise itself. Despite numerous close calls and apparent failures, "Blunt's Brilliant Detectives" endures — a testament to the couple's genuine competence beneath the theatrical facade.

## Relationship to Detective Method

The slogan is the public face of a broader concept explored in the novel: [[detective-method-acting]], Tommy's practice of adopting different fictional detective personas for each case. Where the method-acting approach is internal and procedural, the slogan is external and promotional. Together they form a complete philosophy of detective work as performed identity — the detective is not merely someone who solves crimes but someone who convincingly *plays* the role of detective.
