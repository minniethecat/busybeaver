## Overview

The Blitz Hotel appears in several cases as a neutral meeting ground and, in the climactic finale, as the site of Tuppence's abduction. Its name — suggestive of the German bombing campaigns of the Great War — adds a layer of period-specific resonance to its role as a place of danger.

## Role in Cases

In "[[blindmans-buff|Blindman's Buff]]," Tommy instructs Tuppence to lunch at the Blitz while he remains at the office — a decision that proves fateful when the espionage ring strikes during her absence (008-007). Tuppence's coded warning — "Come to the Blitz and follow the two strangers" — uses the hotel as a rendezvous point for [[albert|Albert]]'s pursuit of the kidnappers.

In "[[man-who-was-no-16|The Man Who Was No. 16]]," the Blitz becomes the setting for the final confrontation. A nurse and a young boy arrive at the Agency claiming Tuppence has been taken to Room 318. Tommy follows, discovering Tuppence drugged and concealed beneath the bolster of [[mrs-van-snyder|Mrs. Van Snyder]]'s bed. The hotel room becomes the stage for Tommy's climactic deduction and the unmasking of the legendary superspy No. 16 (017-015).

## Significance

The Blitz Hotel functions as a liminal space — neither the safety of home nor the controlled environment of the office. It is where the Agency's cases intersect with the wider world, and where the final threat to the Beresfords is neutralized. The hotel's name, evoking wartime bombardment, subtly underscores the danger that has shadowed their detective career from the beginning.
