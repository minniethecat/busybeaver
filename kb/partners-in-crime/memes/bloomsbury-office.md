## Overview

The Bloomsbury office at [[118-haleham-street|118 Haleham Street]] is the physical heart of the [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Agency]]. Located on the second floor of a "somewhat dilapidated building in Bloomsbury," it consists of three rooms: the outer office ([[albert|Albert]]'s domain), the private office of "[[theodore-blunt|Mr. Blunt]]," and Tuppence's adjoining room (002-001).

## Layout

The outer office is where [[albert|Albert]] greets clients, alternating between Long Island butler and tousled office boy as circumstance requires. A button under his desk activates a buzzer in Tuppence's room — the signal that a client has arrived and the typewriter symphony should commence (002-005).

The private office, marked "Private" on the door, is furnished with "an immense businesslike desk, a lot of artistically labelled files, all empty, and some solid leather-seated chairs." Tommy sits behind the desk "trying to look as though he had run a detective agency all his life" (002-003).

Tuppence's room contains a typewriter, tables and chairs "of an inferior type to those in the room of the great Chief, and a gas ring for making tea" (002-004). A communicating door connects the two inner offices, used strategically for concealed witnesses during confrontations.

## Dramatic Events

The office serves as the stage for many of the Agency's most dramatic moments. [[sir-arthur-merivale|Sir Arthur Merivale]] vaults through the window to his death in "[[gentleman-dressed-in-newspaper|The Gentleman Dressed in Newspaper]]" (006-020). Tommy is abducted at gunpoint in "[[blindmans-buff|Blindman's Buff]]" (008-005). A dictaphone is installed by [[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]]'s men in the final case (017-005).

## Significance

The Bloomsbury office is more than a setting — it is a theatrical space where the Beresfords perform their roles. The "artistically labelled files, all empty" epitomize the Agency's nature: a convincing facade that gradually becomes the real thing.
