## Overview

The International Detective Agency is the central setting and structural device of *[[partners-in-crime-novel|Partners in Crime]]*. Located on the second floor of a building at [[118-haleham-street]], Bloomsbury (002-002), the agency is operated by [[tommy-beresford]] under the alias [[theodore-blunt|Theodore Blunt]], with [[tuppence-beresford]] as co-proprietor and [[albert]] as office boy. The firm handles genuine detective cases while secretly serving as an intelligence cover operation for the [[british-secret-service]].

## Origin

The agency comes into being through a confluence of opportunity and necessity. The real [[theodore-blunt|Theodore Blunt]] has been detained by [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]]: "[[theodore-blunt|Mr. Blunt]] has been rather indiscreet, I'm afraid. In consequence, he's — er — where he can't do any harm for the moment" (001-088). [[mr-carter|Mr. Carter]] of the [[british-secret-service|Secret Service]] sees the vacancy as an ideal cover for Tommy, who has been restless in his desk job:

> "Take a six months' leave from your office job. By the end of that time, the excitement will have died down. The lease of 118 Haleham Street expires then, and by that time, I think it is quite possible that Mr. Blunt may — er — be able to resume his activities" (001-050).

Tommy protests that "I don't know anything about detective work," to which Carter replies that it "doesn't matter. You can soon pick it up" (001-050).

## Physical Layout

The agency occupies three rooms on the second floor, each serving a distinct theatrical function:

| Room | Occupant | Function | Theatrical Element |
|------|----------|----------|-------------------|
| Outer Office | [[albert]] | Client reception | "Paper bag of sweets, inky hands, and a tousled head" — the perfect office boy persona (002-002) |
| "Clerks" | [[tuppence-beresford]] | Typing, surveillance | "Frenzied typing" creates an "atmosphere of industry" (002-004); peephole for client observation (002-011) |
| "Private" | [[tommy-beresford]] (as Blunt) | Client interviews | "Immense businesslike desk," "artistically labelled files, all empty," telephone for staged calls (002-003) |

The entire setup is a carefully constructed illusion. The advertised "large staff of confidential and highly skilled Inquiry Agents" (001-047) consists of exactly three people. The "atmosphere of industry" (002-032) is pure theatre — but it works, impressing every client who walks through the door.

## Dual Purpose

The agency serves two functions that occasionally intersect:

### Legitimate Detective Work

The Beresfords handle real cases brought by clients responding to their newspaper advertisements. These range from jewel theft to missing persons to murder investigations. Each case is documented in a separate chapter, forming the novel's episodic structure. The cases include:

| Case | Client | Nature |
|------|--------|--------|
| [[affair-of-the-pink-pearl]] | Col. Kingston Bruce | Jewel theft |
| [[case-of-the-missing-lady]] | Gabriel Stavansson | Missing person |
| [[sunningdale-mystery]] | (various) | Mystery investigation |
| [[house-of-lurking-death]] | [[lois-hargreaves|Lois Hargreaves]] | Poisoning |

### Intelligence Cover

The agency's true purpose — from the [[british-secret-service|Secret Service]]'s perspective — is to monitor communications from a European intelligence network. The key indicator is the number sixteen:

> "Letters are occasionally received with a Russian stamp on them. In the top left hand corner a small code mark — a number sixteen. The letter is opened, the contents noted, and a copy sent to me. If anyone calls at the agency and uses the number sixteen in conversation, you are to report immediately" (001-048).

This dual function creates productive tension: the detective cases provide genuine cover, while the intelligence work provides the underlying stakes that elevate the novel beyond a simple series of detective puzzles.

## The Blunt Persona

The alias "[[theodore-blunt|Theodore Blunt]]" is more than a name — it is a complete performance identity. Tommy's embodiment of Blunt is carefully studied:

> "Behind the desk sat the pseudo Mr. Blunt trying to look as though he had run a detective agency all his life" (002-002).

The persona allows Tommy a freedom he doesn't have as himself. As Blunt, he can be authoritative, mysterious, and theatrical in ways that the real Tommy — "prosperous and sleek and comfortable" (001-020) — cannot. The Blunt identity is the agency's most valuable asset, a character so convincing that clients never question it.

## Notable Cases

The agency handles a diverse range of investigations across the seventeen chapters of *Partners in Crime*:

- **[[affair-of-the-pink-pearl|The Affair of the Pink Pearl]]**: The agency's first major case — a jewel theft at [[the-laurels|The Laurels]] investigated by Tommy as Blunt and Tuppence as Miss Robinson.
- **[[finessing-the-king|Finessing the King]]**: A coded newspaper message leads to a murder investigation with secret society connections.
- **[[case-of-the-missing-lady|The Case of the Missing Lady]]**: A woman's disappearance requiring undercover work by Tuppence.
- **[[blindmans-buff|Blindman's Buff]]**: A case of mistaken identity and international intrigue.
- **[[the-crackler|The Crackler]]**: Counterfeit currency investigation.
- **[[ch11-the-sunningdale-mystery|The Sunningdale Mystery]]**: A murder investigation in a golfing community.
- **[[ch12-the-house-of-lurking-death|The House of Lurking Death]]**: A country house mystery with poison and hidden motives.
- **[[ch13-the-unbreakable-alibi|The Unbreakable Alibi]]**: An apparently impossible alibi testing the agency's deductive skill.
- **[[ch14-the-clergymans-daughter|The Clergyman's Daughter]]**: A case involving a rector's daughter and suspicious circumstances.
- **[[ch15-the-red-house|The Red House]]**: An investigation centered on a mysterious house.
- **[[ch16-the-ambassadors-boots|The Ambassador's Boots]]**: A diplomatic incident with switched luggage.
- **[[ch17-the-man-who-was-no-16|The Man Who Was No. 16]]**: The climactic case connecting detective work with Tommy's intelligence mission.

## Theatrical Advertising

The agency's marketing strategy, summarized in the slogan [[blunts-brilliant-detectives]], includes newspaper advertisements promising "guaranteed twenty-four hours service" — a detail Tuppence invented on impulse that becomes one of the agency's most effective selling points (003-067). The advertisement's blend of genuine service promise and theatrical hyperbole perfectly captures the agency's dual nature.

## Significance

The International Detective Agency is the structural backbone of *[[partners-in-crime-novel|Partners in Crime]]*. It provides the framing device for the episodic case format while anchoring the overarching espionage plot. More than that, the agency represents the intersection of the detective and spy genres — a space where Tommy and Tuppence can be simultaneously amateur sleuths solving village mysteries and intelligence operatives protecting national security.
