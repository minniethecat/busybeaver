## Overview

Dr. Burton is a medical practitioner who appears in Chapter 12 in connection with the poisoning of [[miss-logan]]. He attends to the victim and provides [[tommy-beresford]] with medical context for the investigation, though his own role comes under scrutiny.

## Background

Dr. Burton serves as the attending physician for the household where [[miss-logan|Miss Logan]] works as Aunt Lucy's companion. He was "very excited over the matter" (012-026) when [[miss-logan|Miss Logan]] first fell ill, suggesting both professional curiosity and concern. His initial diagnosis attributed the illness to common causes, but the severity of the symptoms prompted deeper investigation.

## The Investigation

When Tommy arrived to investigate, Dr. Burton was already at the house. Tommy introduced himself authoritatively and began questioning the doctor about the case (012-102). Dr. Burton "performed the necessary introductions" (012-121) and facilitated Tommy's access to the patient.

> "Dr. Burton," he said authoritatively. "I want a word with you." (012-102)

Tommy asked Dr. Burton "whether you are thoroughly convinced that these cases of illness are purely accidental" (012-112), pressing the doctor to reconsider his initial assumptions. The doctor acknowledged that the symptoms were unusual and agreed to have samples analyzed: "I suppose Dr. Burton will have the paste analysed" (012-132).

A critical piece of evidence emerged around Dr. Burton's medical treatments. He confirmed he had "given her a hypodermic injection" (012-215) as part of his care — a detail that became significant when Tommy discovered injection marks on [[miss-logan|Miss Logan]]'s arm that suggested ricin had been administered through the same route. The investigation ultimately determined who had exploited Dr. Burton's legitimate medical procedures to disguise an attempted murder.

## Appearances

Dr. Burton appears in Chapter 12, where his medical authority provides both cover and context for the ricin poisoning investigation.
