## Overview

Miss Robinson is the professional alias adopted by [[tuppence-beresford]] while working at the [[international-detective-agency]]. Introduced by [[tommy-beresford]] (as [[theodore-blunt|Theodore Blunt]]) as his "confidential secretary" (003-019), this identity allows Tuppence to participate in investigations while maintaining the agency's professional cover.

## Role at the Agency

Throughout the novel, Tommy routinely introduces Tuppence as "my confidential secretary, Miss Robinson" (003-019)(007-010)(013-020) to clients and witnesses. This persona gives Tuppence a legitimate reason to be present during interviews and to take notes, while keeping her true identity as Tommy's wife and partner concealed.

Miss Robinson is presented as efficient and competent. Tommy frequently instructs her to "take notes" (004-032) or "make a note of it, please" (003-037), reinforcing the professional secretary image. Clients accept her presence without question, allowing Tuppence to observe and gather intelligence discreetly.

## Investigative Role

Beyond the secretarial cover, Miss Robinson actively participates in investigations. She interviews witnesses — "Miss Robinson was delegated to interview the household staff" (003-115) — and fetches evidence when needed: "Miss Robinson, will you kindly fetch the sponge?" (003-111).

Tuppence enjoys the role: references to "the character of the efficient Miss Robinson" (016-042) suggest she finds satisfaction in the performance. Tommy occasionally teases her through the persona with brusque remarks like "Late again, Miss Robinson" (016-175), adding a layer of private humor to their public roles.

## Notable Cases as Miss Robinson

- **[[affair-of-the-pink-pearl|The Affair of the Pink Pearl]]**: Miss Robinson accompanies Mr. Blunt to [[the-laurels|The Laurels]], where her sharp observations of the household dynamics prove crucial to solving the theft. She is the first to perceive the emotional undercurrents among the Kingston Bruce family.
- **[[case-of-the-missing-lady|The Case of the Missing Lady]]**: Tuppence adopts the Miss Robinson persona for undercover inquiries, using the confidential secretary cover to access information unavailable to official investigators.
- **[[finessing-the-king|Finessing the King]]**: Miss Robinson assists in decoding the social dynamics of the masked ball, where her ability to read people through their dress, manner, and speech patterns contributes to identifying the key figures in the case.

## Appearances

Miss Robinson appears throughout the novel — in Chapters 2, 3, 4, 7, 12, 13, 16, and 17 — whenever Tuppence is working at the agency in her professional capacity. The alias is used with clients, witnesses, and [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] contacts, making it one of the most frequently referenced identities in the book.
