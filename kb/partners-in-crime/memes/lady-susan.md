## Overview

Lady Susan Clonray is a supporting figure in Chapter 7 of *[[partners-in-crime-novel|Partners in Crime]]*, "[[adventure-of-the-sinister-stranger|The Adventure of the Sinister Stranger]]." The aunt of a young woman whose disappearance triggers an investigation by [[tommy-beresford]], Lady Susan resides in Pont Street, London, and serves as both guardian and gatekeeper to information about her niece's whereabouts.

## Background

Lady Susan Clonray lived in Pont Street, a fashionable address in London's Belgravia district (007-024). A woman of social standing — described as "a perfectly charming woman" (007-026) — she maintained a household where her niece was staying at the time of the events in question.

When her niece vanished, Lady Susan became the natural first point of inquiry. The girl's fiancé, Gabriel Stavansson, sought information from her directly. Lady Susan's account was that her niece had gone to meet him — an explanation that initially seemed to settle the matter.

> "Mind you, I'm not saying that Lady Susan isn't a perfectly charming woman." (007-026)

## Role in the Investigation

Lady Susan's involvement took a more complex turn when discrepancies emerged in her account. [[tommy-beresford|Tommy Beresford]], investigating under his "[[theodore-blunt|Mr. Blunt]]" alias, identified a critical inconsistency: "Lady Susan must have made a bloomer over a date" (007-028). Her timeline did not align with other evidence.

Despite this, Lady Susan maintained her position. She appeared "quite satisfied" by the initial explanation and did not press further (007-034). Her niece, meanwhile, was required "to report all her movements to Lady Susan" (007-029) — a condition that made the aunt's apparent lack of concern unusual.

The investigation gradually revealed that Lady Susan was "backing her up" (007-056) — supporting her niece's story about meeting her fiancé, even when that story became increasingly difficult to reconcile with the facts. Whether Lady Susan was an unwitting accomplice or a knowing participant in whatever scheme was afoot remained a matter of interpretation throughout the chapter.

When Tommy attempted to follow up with Lady Susan directly, he found her unavailable — "She was out too" (007-138) — part of a pattern of key witnesses being unreachable at critical moments. Later, it emerged that Lady Susan "has been sworn to secrecy" (007-175) regarding certain matters, suggesting she was privy to information she chose not to disclose.

## Personality

Lady Susan presents the image of a charming, socially adept aristocrat — pleasant, accommodating, and superficially cooperative. Beneath this polished exterior, however, lies a more guarded nature. Her willingness to vouch for her niece's account, despite its evident flaws, suggests either fierce familial loyalty or a deliberate choice to prioritize social appearances over factual accuracy.

## Key Relationships

- **[[tommy-beresford]]** — Tommy, operating as "[[theodore-blunt|Mr. Blunt]]," investigated Lady Susan's claims and identified the date discrepancy that complicated the case. His attempts to question her directly were repeatedly thwarted.
- **Gabriel Stavansson** — The fiancé of Lady Susan's niece and the client who brought the case. Stavansson sought information from Lady Susan about his missing betrothed and initially accepted her account at face value.
- **Her niece** — The missing young woman at the center of the case, required to report her movements to Lady Susan and backed by her aunt's vouching for her story.

## Appearances

Lady Susan Clonray does not appear directly in the narrative. She is referenced throughout Chapter 7, "[[adventure-of-the-sinister-stranger|The Adventure of the Sinister Stranger]]," as a key source of information — and misinformation — in the investigation into her niece's disappearance. Her Pont Street residence serves as a recurring point of reference, and her statements and unavailability drive key turns in the detective work.
