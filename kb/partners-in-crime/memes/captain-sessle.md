## Overview

Captain Anthony Sessle is a central figure in Chapter 11 of *[[partners-in-crime-novel|Partners in Crime]]*, "[[man-who-was-no-16|The Man Who Was No. 16]]." A well-known ex-soldier and golfing figure, Sessle is found stabbed to death at the [[sunningdale|Sunningdale]] golf course. His murder becomes a case that [[tommy-beresford]] and [[tuppence-beresford]] investigate through the [[international-detective-agency]].

## Background

Captain Sessle was a retired military officer who had settled into civilian life as a keen golfer. He was "a well known figure on the links" at [[sunningdale|Sunningdale]] (011-030), where his handicap was notably low — evidence of his skill at the game. He lived with his wife, [[mrs-sessle|Mrs. Sessle]], near the golf course.

Before his death, Sessle had discovered financial irregularities: he was "on the verge of discovery" regarding the embezzlement of funds (011-053). This discovery appears to have been the motive for his murder, as someone with access to those accounts stood to lose everything if Sessle exposed the truth.

> "The case stands like this. Captain Sessle was on the verge of discovery — and the person whose peculations he was about to expose was not the kind of person to stick at murder." (011-053)

## The Murder at Sunningdale

On the day of his death, Sessle played a round of golf at [[sunningdale|Sunningdale]] with his friend [[mr-hollaby|Mr. Hollaby]] and [[mr-hollaby|Hollaby]]'s son. Sessle was "the first to reach the tee" at the sixth hole (011-034). He played through to the eighth hole, then "declared abruptly that the light was too bad and that he was going home" (011-037). The [[mr-hollaby|Hollaby]]s were the last people to see Sessle alive.

Later that day, a girl named [[doris-evans]] was arrested and charged with Sessle's murder. She had been seen asking for directions to Captain Sessle's house (011-041) and was found near the scene. However, the investigation revealed complications — Sessle had been stabbed, and the prosecution's case against Evans was not straightforward.

[[tommy-beresford|Tommy Beresford]], through his investigation, uncovered that Sessle had been impersonated: "It wasn't Sessle who met [[doris-evans|Doris Evans]] at a Cinema" (011-110). The real killer was someone who knew Sessle well enough to imitate him convincingly — someone who "knew Sessle pretty well — knew, for instance, that his handicap was two" (011-114).

## Key Relationships

- **[[mrs-sessle|Mrs. Sessle]]** — His wife, described as a small woman. Investigators initially considered whether she could have been involved, but her stature made the physical requirements of the crime improbable (011-052).
- **[[mr-hollaby|Mr. Hollaby]]** — Sessle's golf partner, who was among the last to see him alive on the links. [[mr-hollaby|Hollaby]] parted from Sessle "in full view of the caddies" (011-077), giving him a verifiable alibi.
- **[[doris-evans]]** — The young woman arrested and charged with Sessle's murder. She claimed to have been summoned by Sessle, but the man she met was an impostor.

## Appearances

Captain Sessle appears only in Chapter 11, "[[man-who-was-no-16|The Man Who Was No. 16]]," where his murder at [[sunningdale|Sunningdale]] provides the central mystery for the Beresfords' investigation. Though he does not appear directly in the narrative (his death having already occurred), his circumstances, relationships, and the details of his final day are extensively discussed through witness testimony and detective work.
