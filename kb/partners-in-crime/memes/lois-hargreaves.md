## Overview

Lois Hargreaves is a young heiress and the central client in "[[house-of-lurking-death]]" (Chapter 14). Having unexpectedly inherited the substantial Radclyffe fortune and the country estate of Thurnly Grange from her aunt, [[lady-radclyffe|Lady Radclyffe]], she becomes the target of a mysterious poisoner operating from within her own household. Her case brings her to the [[international-detective-agency|International Detective Agency]], where [[tommy-beresford]] and [[tuppence-beresford]] undertake an investigation that reveals a deadly conspiracy.

## Background

Lois was brought up by her wealthy aunt, [[lady-radclyffe|Lady Radclyffe]], at Thurnly Grange — a "great rambling old fashioned house" in the heart of the English countryside (014-001). She was [[lady-radclyffe|Lady Radclyffe]]'s only living relation. Also living at the Grange was [[dennis-radclyffe|Dennis Radclyffe]], the nephew of [[lady-radclyffe|Lady Radclyffe]]'s late husband. [[lady-radclyffe|Lady Radclyffe]] had always intended to leave the bulk of her fortune to Dennis — it was, she said, "Radclyffe money, and ought to go to a Radclyffe" (014-001).

However, when Dennis was twenty-two, [[lady-radclyffe|Lady Radclyffe]] quarrelled violently with him over his debts. When she died a year later, Lois was astonished to discover that her aunt had changed her will, leaving everything to her instead. Feeling deeply uneasy about this outcome, Lois made a will of her own upon turning twenty-one — just three weeks before consulting the Agency — leaving the entire inheritance to Dennis (014-001).

## The Poisoned Chocolates

Lois's consultation with the Agency concerned a box of chocolates sent anonymously through the post. The chocolates contained arsenic — not enough to kill, but sufficient to make several members of the household seriously ill (014-001). [[dr-burton|Dr. Burton]], the local physician, noted that this was the third such incident in the neighbourhood and urged Lois to involve the police (014-001).

Lois resisted, however, for a deeply unsettling reason: through her absent-minded habit of doodling three intertwined fish on parcel labels, she had traced the chocolate wrapper to a parcel that had arrived at Thurnly Grange itself. The sender, she realised with dread, was someone living under her own roof (014-001).

## Personality

Lois presents as nervous and high-strung during her consultation — her hands twist together and she fidgets with her handbag clasp (014-001). She is, however, clear-headed and methodical in presenting the facts. She prefers discretion to publicity, and shows a strong sense of fairness in her determination to return the Radclyffe fortune to Dennis despite having no legal obligation to do so. Her decision to consult a private detective rather than the police reflects both her distaste for scandal and her fear of what the investigation might reveal about those closest to her.
