# French House of Peers

The upper chamber of the French parliament under the Restoration and [[july-monarchy|July Monarchy]]. [[la-baudraye|La Baudraye]]'s elevation to the peerage is his culminating social triumph. [[dinah-de-la-baudraye|Dinah]] jokes that 'the entomology of society ought to be represented in the Upper House.' Standard

## Context

This organization appears in Balzac's "[[paris|Parisian]]s in the Country," which brings together two stories — *[[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart the Great]]* and *The [[provincial-muse|Muse of the Department]]* — set in the [[loire|Loire Valley]] and [[paris|Paris]] during the [[july-monarchy|July Monarchy (1830–1848)]].

## In the Novels

The corpus provides detailed references to this organization. Balzac uses it to illuminate the social, political, and economic realities of post-Revolutionary [[tours|France]], where old hierarchies have collapsed and new forms of power — commercial, journalistic, political — are emerging.
