# Le Globe

*Le Globe* was a [[paris|Parisian]] newspaper founded in 1824 by Pierre Leroux and Paul-François Dubois. Initially a liberal literary and philosophical journal, it became the official organ of the [[saint-simonism|Saint-Simonian]] movement after the [[july-revolution-1830]]. In Balzac's *[[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart the Great]]*, it is mentioned as one of the publications whose doctrines briefly swayed [[felix-gaudissart]] from his commercial faith.

## History and Significance

Founded under the Restoration as a forum for liberal [[concept-index|ideas]], *Le Globe* established itself as one of the most intellectually distinguished periodicals of its era. Its contributors included some of the brightest minds of the generation: Sainte-Beuve, Jouffroy, and Charles de Rémusat. After the [[july-revolution-1830|July Revolution of 1830]], the paper was acquired by the Saint-Simonians under the leadership of Michel Chevalier and Prosper Enfantin, becoming the movement's principal platform.

## In Balzac's Fiction

> The Saint-Simonians were so loud in their praises of Gaudissart the Great, showed him so much attention, that he believed in their doctrine. (002-030)

The *Globe*, as the "organ of the doctrines of Saint-Simon" (002-030), represents the intersection of [[newspaper-press|journalism]] and ideology that Balzac found both fascinating and suspect. For [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]], the paper was not merely a publication but a gateway into a world of [[concept-index|ideas]] that momentarily threatened to distract him from commerce. His flirtation with [[saint-simonism|Saint-Simonianism]] — mediated through the *Globe* — is one of the comic highlights of the novella: [[felix-gaudissart|the commercial traveler]] attempting to reconcile the gospel of industrial reorganization with the practical business of selling hats. (002-050)

## The Saint-Simonian Moment

The [[saint-simonism|Saint-Simonian movement]] advocated for a reorganization of society around industrial production, the abolition of inheritance, and the spiritual authority of artists and scientists. Its brief moment of cultural prominence in the early 1830s coincides with the opening of Balzac's fictional world. The *Globe* was its most influential voice until the movement fractured under government [[newspaper-press|press]]ure and internal disputes in 1832.
