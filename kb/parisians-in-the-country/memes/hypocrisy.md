# Hypocrisy

The elaborate theatrical deceptions sustaining [[adultery|illicit love]] and social respectability. [[etienne-lousteau|Lousteau]] stages an entire performance with [[bixiou|Bixiou]]. His entire relationship with [[dinah-de-la-baudraye|Dinah]] is sustained by performed emotion. [[dinah-de-la-baudraye|Dinah]] 'had been acting a farce for two months past.' Standard

## Context in Balzac's Fiction

This theme appears throughout *[[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart the Great]]* and *The [[provincial-muse|Muse of the Department]]*, two stories in Balzac's collection "[[paris|Parisian]]s in the Country" that together form a panoramic study of French society under the [[july-monarchy|July Monarchy]].

## In the Novels

The corpus at `corpus/raw/0255-[[paris|Parisian]]s in the Country-5df198de/doc_final.md` contains detailed treatment of this theme. Balzac uses it to illuminate the social and psychological realities of his [[person-index|characters]] — from the commercial ambition of [[felix-gaudissart]] to the provincial [[ambition|aspiration]]s of [[dinah-de-la-baudraye]].
