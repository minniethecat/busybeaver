# Mademoiselle Chamarolles

Headmistress of a boarding school for young ladies in [[bourges|Bourges]], where [[dinah-de-la-baudraye|Dinah Piédefer]] was educated and recognized as the school's finest student. Her establishment represented the limited educational opportunities available to provincial girls of the post-Revolutionary era.

## Identity and Appearance

Mademoiselle Chamarolles ran a school in [[bourges|Bourges]] that educated the daughters of the provincial bourgeoisie. [[dinah-de-la-baudraye|Dinah]] was "reputed to be the cleverest of Mademoiselle Chamarolles' pupils" [005-026], and maintained a friendship with another student, Anna de Grossetête, who was "her bosom friend at Mademoiselle Chamarolles' school" [005-060].

## Role in the Plot

The school provides an important backstory for [[dinah-de-la-baudraye|Dinah]]'s intellectual development. [[dinah-de-la-baudraye|Dinah]]'s education at Chamarolles' establishment made her "far superior to the phoenix of Mademoiselle Chamarolles' school" — exceeding even the school's highest standards [005-120]. This early recognition of her gifts foreshadows her later transformation into the literary "[[provincial-muse|Muse of the Department]]."

Chamarolles herself is a background figure, but her institution represents the narrow channel through which provincial female [[genius-concept|talent]] could flow in early 19th-century [[tours|France]].
