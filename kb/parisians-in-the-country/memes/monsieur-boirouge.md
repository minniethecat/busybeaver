# Monsieur Boirouge

The Presiding Judge of [[sancerre|Sancerre]], whose official position made him one of the most prominent figures in local society. He attended the famous dinner party where [[etienne-lousteau|Étienne Lousteau]] was entertained, and his reactions to the literary conversation reflected provincial bewilderment at [[paris|Parisian]] sophistication.

## Identity and Appearance

Monsieur Boirouge served as Presiding Judge in [[sancerre|Sancerre]] and "happened to have a house" conveniently located for social occasions [005-084]. His judicial rank placed him at the top of the local professional hierarchy, alongside [[monsieur-de-clagny|the Public Prosecutor]].

## Role in the Plot

At the dinner at [[la-baudraye|La Baudraye]], Boirouge epitomized provincial incomprehension of [[paris|Parisian]] literary discourse. When [[etienne-lousteau|Lousteau]] held forth, Boirouge turned to his wife and exclaimed, "[[la-baudraye|La Baudraye]] is talking!" [005-708] — a remark that revealed both his awe at her learning and his inability to follow the conversation himself.

Later, when others "laughed at the little man" ([[etienne-lousteau|Lousteau]]), only "Monsieur Boirouge, the Presiding Judge, remarked" with some gravity [005-1178], suggesting he retained a measure of judicial dignity even amid social frivolity. His presence at such gatherings was obligatory rather than enthusiastic — part of the machinery of [[society|provincial society]].
