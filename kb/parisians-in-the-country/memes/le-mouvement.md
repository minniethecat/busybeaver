# Le Mouvement

## Background

*Le Mouvement* was a Republican newspaper published in [[paris|Paris]] during the early years of the [[july-monarchy|July Monarchy]] (1830s). It represented the left-wing, republican opposition to Louis-Philippe's constitutional monarchy, distinguishing it from the more moderate *[[le-globe|Le Globe]]*, which had Saint-Simonian leanings.

> The Mouvement, a Republican paper, invited subscriptions.
>
> (002-030)

## Role in *Gaudissart the Great*

In Balzac's *[[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart the Great]]*, *Le Mouvement* appears as one of the publications the [[commercial-traveler|traveling salesman]] [[felix-gaudissart|Félix Gaudissart]] peddles to provincial subscribers. The paper is consistently paired with *[[le-globe|Le Globe]]*, representing the two poles of opposition [[newspaper-press|journalism]] that [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]]'s commercial operation distributes:

> To take the Globe and the Mouvement, and to pay their insurances.
>
> (002-050)

[[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]] bundles [[subscription-publishing|newspaper subscription]]s with insurance policies, treating political [[newspaper-press|journalism]] and financial products as equivalent commodities. This conflation is central to Balzac's satire of [[felix-gaudissart|the commercial traveler]] who reduces all value — political, intellectual, and financial — to the logic of the sale.

## Gaudissart's Sales Pitch

The paper features prominently in [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]]'s self-aggrandizing narrative. He boasts of his success in selling subscriptions to *Le Mouvement* as proof of his commercial prowess:

> I have got no more than sixty-two Mouvements so far.
>
> (002-067)

The number sixty-two — presented as a disappointment — reveals the scale of [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]]'s ambition and the reach of the provincial subscription network. His sales technique involves inflating his achievements: he claims he could claim ten subscriptions if he had only sold one, demonstrating [[felix-gaudissart|the commercial traveler]]'s fluid relationship with truth:

> If I had ten subscriptions to the Mouvement, I should simply say I have got no more than sixty-two Mouvements so far; and that in my whole life I never met with such difficulty in getting subscribers.
>
> (002-042), (002-067)

## Political Significance

As a Republican organ, *Le Mouvement* represented the radical opposition during a period when the [[july-monarchy|July Monarchy]] was consolidating power. [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]]'s casual reference to selling both *[[le-globe|Le Globe]]* and *Le Mouvement* — papers representing different political tendencies — underscores [[felix-gaudissart|the commercial traveler]]'s ideological indifference. Politics, in [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]]'s world, is just another product line:

> I must give my mind to insuring property, to the Globe, to the Mouvement, to the Children's paper, and to the Article de Paris.
>
> (002-056)
