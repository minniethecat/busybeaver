# Monsieur Mitouflet

The landlord of the [[soleil-dor-vouvray|Soleil d'Or]] inn in [[tours|Tours]], a respectable establishment where commercial travelers like [[felix-gaudissart|Félix Gaudissart]] stayed while conducting business in the region.

## Identity and Appearance

Monsieur Mitouflet kept the [[soleil-dor-vouvray|Soleil d'Or]], an inn in [[tours|Tours]] that served as [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]]'s headquarters during his visit to the region. As an innkeeper, he occupied the social position of a respectable tradesman who facilitated the commerce that connected provincial towns to the wider economy.

## Role in the Plot

Mitouflet appears at the opening of [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]]'s adventure. When [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]] announced his intention to fight a duel, he addressed Mitouflet directly: "Monsieur Mitouflet, I am going to fight [[monsieur-margaritis|Monsieur Margaritis]]" [002-293], demonstrating the familiarity between the [[commercial-traveler|traveling salesman]] and his landlord.

Later, "Monsieur Mitouflet," said [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]], as they went downstairs [002-316], the innkeeper served as a witness to [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]]'s preparations and a sounding board for his schemes. His presence helps establish the commercial infrastructure of provincial [[tours|France]] — the network of inns and hotels that made [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]]'s profession possible.
