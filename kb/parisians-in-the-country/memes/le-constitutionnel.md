# Le Constitutionnel

A major liberal [[paris|Paris]] daily newspaper founded during the Hundred Days (1815), known for its oppositional stance and anti-English [[eloquence|rhetoric]].

## Context

[[etienne-lousteau|Lousteau]] uses it as a sarcastic reference: "in Perfidious Albion, as the Constitutionnel has it." The paper is invoked as shorthand for a certain anti-English liberal discourse in [[parisian-literary-life|Parisian journalism]].

## In Balzac's World

The newspaper represents one pole of the [[paris|Parisian]] [[newspaper-press|press]] ecosystem that Balzac maps with sociological precision — from the Saint-Simonian *Globe* to the republican *[[le-mouvement|Mouvement]]* to the conservative *Journal des Débats*.
