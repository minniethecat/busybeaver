# Entail

The legal mechanism of primogeniture preserving landed estates. [[la-baudraye|La Baudraye]] creates an entail covering [[anzy|Anzy]], [[la-baudraye|La Baudraye]], and [[la-hautoy|La Hautoy]] by letters patent of December 1820 (005-055). This prevents fragmentation of his estate and secures dynastic pretensions (005-827).

## Context in Balzac's Fiction

This theme appears throughout *[[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart the Great]]* and *The [[provincial-muse|Muse of the Department]]*, two stories in Balzac's collection "[[paris|Parisian]]s in the Country" that together form a panoramic study of French society under the [[july-monarchy|July Monarchy]].


> Thanks to sordid economy and an indemnity paid him for the estate belonging to his father, nationalized and sold in 1793, by the year 1827 the little man could realize the dream of his whole life.
>
> (005-055)

## In the Novels

M. de la [[la-baudraye|Baudraye]], having created the entail, had "the satisfaction of knowing that an entail had been created, by letters patent of December 1820" (005-055). He later exclaims of his heir: "He is heir to one of the finest entailed estates in [[tours|France]]" (005-1091). Balzac uses this theme to illuminate the social and psychological realities of his [[person-index|characters]] — from the commercial ambition of [[felix-gaudissart]] to the provincial [[ambition|aspiration]]s of [[dinah-de-la-baudraye]].
