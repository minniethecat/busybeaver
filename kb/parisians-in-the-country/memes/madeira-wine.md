# Madeira Wine

The fortified wine from the Portuguese island of Madeira, referenced in [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart the Great]] where the wine of [[clos-margaritis|Clos Margaritis]] is so rich it is passed off as Madeira in [[paris|Paris]].

## Context in Balzac's Fiction

This concept appears in Balzac's [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart the Great]], where the prized wine of [[margaritis|Margaritis]]'s Clos vineyard in [[vouvray|Vouvray]] is described as being of such exceptional quality that it is passed off as genuine Madeira in the [[paris|Paris]] market.

## In the Novels

The substitution of [[vouvray|Vouvray]] for Madeira is a small but telling detail in Balzac's anatomy of commercial deception. Just as [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]] sells intellectual products whose value is uncertain, the [[vouvray|Vouvray]] winegrowers sell their product under a false flag. The Madeira reference connects provincial viticulture to global commerce and the world of luxury goods.
