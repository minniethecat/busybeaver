# Madame Popinot-Chandier

Wife of [[monsieur-boirouge|the Presiding Judge]] [[monsieur-boirouge|Boirouge]] and mother of [[gatien|Gatien]]. Initially an opponent of [[dinah-de-la-baudraye|Dinah]], she becomes her vocal supporter when her son is welcomed into the [[la-baudraye|La Baudraye]] circle. Represents the pragmatic reversals of provincial opinion. Standard

## Context in Balzac's Fiction

This person appears in Balzac's "[[paris|Parisian]]s in the Country" — comprising *[[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart the Great]]* and *The [[provincial-muse|Muse of the Department]]* — two stories set in the [[loire|Loire Valley]] and [[paris|Paris]] during the [[july-monarchy|July Monarchy]] that together form a panoramic study of French provincial and metropolitan life.

## In the Novels

The corpus provides detailed treatment of this person, using it to illuminate the social, psychological, and historical realities of post-Revolutionary and post-Napoleonic [[tours|France]].
