# Duel

The code of honor satisfied through ritualized combat. The duel between [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]] and [[monsieur-vernier|Vernier]] is a comic set-piece — "A duel was a thing unheard of in this benign region."

## Context in Balzac's Fiction

This theme appears in *[[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart the Great]]*, where [[felix-gaudissart|the commercial traveler]]'s honor is challenged after he is tricked by the madman [[margaritis|Margaritis]].

When [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]]'s commercial honor is challenged by [[monsieur-vernier|Monsieur Vernier]], the prospect of armed confrontation astonishes a pacific provincial community:

> The whole borough of Vouvray was in a stir over the meeting between Gaudissart and Monsieur Vernier. A duel was a thing unheard of in this benign region.
>
> (002-292)

## In the Novels

The duel is described with comic precision: [[monsieur-mitouflet|Mitouflet]], an "instructor of grenadiers," hits [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]] sixty-eight times with foils during practice. The actual duel is deliberately farcical — "large horse pistols loaded to the muzzle, they are sure to kick and miss, and each man withdraws with unblemished honor." [[monsieur-vernier|Vernier]] "narrowly missed killing a cow." The duel ends in reconciliation and a shared breakfast.
