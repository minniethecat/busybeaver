# Debt

Chronic [[usury-and-debt|financial obligation]] as the defining condition of the [[bohemian-life|literary bohemia]]n. [[etienne-lousteau|Lousteau]]'s 'indigent months,' the upholsterer's agreement, [[dinah-de-la-baudraye|Dinah]] pawning jewels, bailiff's seizure — [[usury-and-debt|debt]] forces every moral compromise. ## Context

This concept appears throughout Balzac's two stories set in and around [[sancerre|Sancerre]] and [[paris|Paris]]. The corpus at `corpus/raw/0255-[[paris|Parisian]]s in the Country-5df198de/doc_final.md` contains numerous references that inform this analysis.

The theme of [[usury-and-debt|debt]] permeates the de la [[la-baudraye|Baudraye]] family history. M. de la [[la-baudraye|Baudraye]] senior "did not go to [[paris|Paris]] to claim his [[usury-and-debt|debt]]s; but after Napoleon's death he resolved to go to [[paris|Paris]] to claim his due from the [[duc-de-navarreins|Duc de Navarreins]]" (005-022). Armed with a power of attorney "to enable him to recover his [[usury-and-debt|debt]]s from the [[duc-de-navarreins|Duc de Navarreins]]" (005-027), he confronted the principal [[usury-and-debt|debt]]or (005-033), only to recover "scarcely ten percent on [[usury-and-debt|debt]]s from these fine gentlemen" (005-040). This bitter lesson in the power relations of [[usury-and-debt|debt]] shaped the financial cunning of the next generation.

[[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]] himself traces his commercial philosophy to an unexpected source:

> He acknowledged his indebtedness to the hatter's trade, saying that it was by studying the outside of the head that he had learned to understand its inside, that he was accustomed to find caps to fit folks, to throw himself at their head, and so forth. His jests on hats were inexhaustible.
>
> (002-020)

## In the Novel

The theme is woven through both narratives, manifesting differently in the commercial satire of *[[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart the Great]]* and the social drama of *The [[provincial-muse|Muse of the Department]]*.
