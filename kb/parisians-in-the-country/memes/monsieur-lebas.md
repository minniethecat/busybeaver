# Monsieur Lebas

Deputy judge of the [[sancerre|Sancerre]] tribunal and a regular member of the provincial circle that gathered around [[dinah-de-la-baudraye|Madame de la Baudraye]].

## Identity and Appearance

Monsieur Lebas served as deputy to [[monsieur-boirouge|the Presiding Judge]] in the [[sancerre|Sancerre]] tribunal [005-272]. As a member of the local judiciary, he belonged to the small circle of educated professionals who constituted [[dinah-de-la-baudraye|Dinah de la Baudraye]]'s salon society — a group that included [[monsieur-de-clagny|the Public Prosecutor]] [[monsieur-de-clagny|Monsieur de Clagny]], [[monsieur-boirouge|the Presiding Judge]], and the Paymaster-General [[monsieur-gravier|Monsieur Gravier]] [005-721].

## Character and Behavior

Lebas appears as a reliable subordinate within the provincial judiciary. When [[monsieur-de-clagny|Monsieur de Clagny]] wished to free himself from his official duties to attend a social engagement at [[la-baudraye|La Baudraye]], he "begged him as his deputy to sit for him" [005-272] — a favor that suggests collegial relations among the [[sancerre|Sancerre]] legal officials. Lebas participated in the cultural life of the town, attending the celebrated dinner at [[anzy|Anzy]] where [[etienne-lousteau|Lousteau]]'s [[paris|Parisian]] wit collided with provincial expectations [005-721].

## Social Relations

Lebas is professionally subordinate to [[monsieur-boirouge|the Presiding Judge]] ([[monsieur-boirouge|Monsieur Boirouge]]) and socially allied with [[monsieur-de-clagny|Monsieur de Clagny]], [[monsieur-de-clagny|the Public Prosecutor]], who relies on him as a deputy [005-272]. He is one of the inner circle invited to stay overnight at [[anzy|Anzy]] after the dinner party, alongside [[monsieur-de-clagny|Clagny]], [[gatien-boirouge|Gatien Boirouge]], and [[monsieur-gravier|Monsieur Gravier]] [005-721]. His presence in the [[sancerre|Sancerre]] circle that "discovered" romantic meanings in [[etienne-lousteau|Lousteau]]'s conversation [[place-index|places]] him among the local admirers of [[dinah-de-la-baudraye|Madame de la Baudraye]] [005-719].

## Role in the Plot

Lebas is a background figure who helps establish the texture of provincial official life in [[sancerre|Sancerre]]. His role as [[monsieur-de-clagny|Clagny]]'s deputy enables the plot: when [[monsieur-de-clagny|Clagny]] delegates his court duties to Lebas, he is free to attend the gathering at [[la-baudraye|La Baudraye]], where his devotion to [[dinah-de-la-baudraye|Dinah]] [[place-index|places]] him in competition with [[etienne-lousteau|Lousteau]] [005-272]. Lebas appears among the group whose provincial incomprehension of [[etienne-lousteau|Lousteau]]'s [[paris|Parisian]] irony underscores the cultural gulf between [[paris|the capital]] and [[provincial-life|the provinces]] [005-719].

## Narrative Assessment

> Monsieur Lebas will not join you early, for Monsieur de Clagny has begged him as his deputy to sit for him. [005-272]

> The Presiding Judge, and his deputy, Lebas, had discovered there... [005-719]

## Historical and Literary Context

The figure of the provincial magistrate's deputy is a recurring minor type in Balzac's fiction, representing the lower rungs of the Restoration judiciary. Such men populate the small-town settings that Balzac used to explore the relationship between professional ambition and provincial stagnation.
