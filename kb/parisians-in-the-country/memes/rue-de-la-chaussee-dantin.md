# Rue de la Chaussee d'Antin

Fashionable [[paris|Paris]] street on the way to the Boulevards and the Bois where [[etienne-lousteau|Lousteau]] sees [[dinah-de-la-baudraye|Dinah]]'s carriage in the novel's closing pages.

## Context in Balzac's Fiction

This place appears in Balzac's "[[paris|Parisian]]s in the Country" — comprising *[[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart the Great]]* and *The [[provincial-muse|Muse of the Department]]* — two stories set in the [[loire|Loire Valley]] and [[paris|Paris]] during the [[july-monarchy|July Monarchy (1830–1848)]].

## In the Novels

Balzac uses this place to illuminate the social, psychological, and historical realities of post-Revolutionary and post-Napoleonic [[tours|France]], where traditional hierarchies have collapsed and new forms of power — commercial, journalistic, political — are emerging.
