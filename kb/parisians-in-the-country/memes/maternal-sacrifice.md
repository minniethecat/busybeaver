# Maternal Sacrifice

A mother's surrender of her children as the ultimate cost of [[adultery|illicit love]]. [[dinah-de-la-baudraye|Dinah]] gives up her two sons to [[la-baudraye|La Baudraye]] at age six as part of the financial settlement of her separation from [[etienne-lousteau|Lousteau]].

## Context in Balzac's Fiction

This theme appears in *The [[provincial-muse|Muse of the Department]]*, where [[dinah-de-la-baudraye|Dinah]]'s maternal sacrifice becomes the bargaining chip in her financial negotiations with her husband.

## In the Novels

[[dinah-de-la-baudraye|Dinah]] laments "selling — what is most precious to a mother's heart — selling my children! (005-1133)" The children's fate is the leverage [[la-baudraye|La Baudraye]] uses to control [[dinah-de-la-baudraye|Dinah]]. At the novel's end, she lavishes "all her disappointed affection" on them when she has lost everything else (005-1239). The children serve as both bargaining chips and objects of genuine maternal devotion.
