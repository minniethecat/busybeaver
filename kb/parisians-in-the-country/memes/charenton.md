# Charenton

The famous insane asylum near [[paris|Paris]], alluded to as the alternative to [[madame-margaritis|Madame Margaritis]]'s devoted home care of her mad husband — 'a hundred louis, or perhaps a thousand crowns, the price charged at Charenton.' Standard

## Context in Balzac's Fiction

This place appears in Balzac's "[[paris|Parisian]]s in the Country" — comprising *[[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart the Great]]* and *The [[provincial-muse|Muse of the Department]]* — two stories set in the [[loire|Loire Valley]] and [[paris|Paris]] during the [[july-monarchy|July Monarchy]] that together form a panoramic study of French provincial and metropolitan life.

## In the Novels

The corpus provides detailed treatment of this place, using it to illuminate the social, psychological, and historical realities of post-Revolutionary and post-Napoleonic [[tours|France]].
