# French Expedition to Spain 1823

The military intervention restoring Ferdinand VII to the Spanish throne, mentioned by [[monsieur-gravier|Gravier]] who was appointed to a post in Spain but got no further than [[tours|Tours]].

## Context in Balzac's Fiction

This event appears in Balzac's "[[paris|Parisian]]s in the Country" — comprising *[[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart the Great]]* and *The [[provincial-muse|Muse of the Department]]* — two stories set in the [[loire|Loire Valley]] and [[paris|Paris]] during the [[july-monarchy|July Monarchy (1830–1848)]].

## In the Novels

Balzac uses this event to illuminate the social, psychological, and historical realities of post-Revolutionary and post-Napoleonic [[tours|France]], where traditional hierarchies have collapsed and new forms of power — commercial, journalistic, political — are emerging.
