# French Consulate

The government of [[tours|France]] from 1799 to 1804 under Napoleon as First Consul. Under the Consulate 'his prospects were but slender' — [[la-baudraye|La Baudraye]]'s father returned from emigration. [[madame-hadot|Madame Hadot]] 'lived at the time of the Consulate.' Standard

## Context in Balzac's Fiction

This event appears in Balzac's "[[paris|Parisian]]s in the Country" — comprising *[[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart the Great]]* and *The [[provincial-muse|Muse of the Department]]* — two stories set in the [[loire|Loire Valley]] and [[paris|Paris]] during the [[july-monarchy|July Monarchy]] that together form a panoramic study of French provincial and metropolitan life.

## In the Novels

The corpus provides detailed treatment of this event, using it to illuminate the social, psychological, and historical realities of post-Revolutionary and post-Napoleonic [[tours|France]].
