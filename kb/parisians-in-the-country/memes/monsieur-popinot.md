# Monsieur Popinot

A [[paris|Parisian]] merchant whose career exemplified the social mobility available to the bourgeoisie under the [[july-monarchy|July Monarchy]]. His elevation to government minister and peer of [[tours|France]] was cited as a model of success.

## Identity and Appearance

Monsieur Popinot belonged to the [[paris|Parisian]] commercial elite. He "was yesterday made Minister of Commerce" [002-052] and had been "twice returned deputy for an arrondissement in [[paris|Paris]]" [002-056] before being elevated to the peerage. His career trajectory — from trade to politics to the nobility — embodied the [[ambition|aspiration]]s of Balzac's bourgeois [[person-index|characters]].

## Role in the Plot

Popinot does not appear directly in the narrative but is invoked as a shining example of what commercial [[genius-concept|talent]] could achieve. His name is mentioned to inspire or im[[newspaper-press|press]]: he represents the upper limit of bourgeois ambition, the proof that a successful merchant could become a Count and Peer of [[tours|France]]. In a story about commercial travel and social climbing, Popinot serves as the horizon toward which [[person-index|characters]] like [[felix-gaudissart|Gaudissart]] aspire.
