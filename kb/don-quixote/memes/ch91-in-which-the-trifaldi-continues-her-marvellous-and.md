IN WHICH THE TRIFALDI CONTINUES HER MARVELLOUS AND MEMORABLE STORY

p39a.jpg (96K)


[091-001]By every word that Sancho uttered, the duchess was as much delighted as

[091-002]Don Quixote was driven to desperation. He bade him hold his tongue, and

[091-003]the Distressed One went on to say: “At length, after much questioning

[091-004]and answering, as the princess held to her story, without changing or

[091-005]varying her previous declaration, the Vicar gave his decision in favour

[091-006]of Don Clavijo, and she was delivered over to him as his lawful wife;

[091-007]which the Queen Doña Maguncia, the Princess Antonomasia’s mother, so

[091-008]took to heart, that within the space of three days we buried her.”

[091-009]“She died, no doubt,” said Sancho.

[091-010]“Of course,” said Trifaldin; “they don’t bury living people in Kandy,

[091-011]only the dead.”

[091-012]“Señor Squire,” said Sancho, “a man in a swoon has been known to be

[091-013]buried before now, in the belief that he was dead; and it struck me

[091-014]that Queen Maguncia ought to have swooned rather than died; because

[091-015]with life a great many things come right, and the princess’s folly was

[091-016]not so great that she need feel it so keenly. If the lady had married

[091-017]some page of hers, or some other servant of the house, as many another

[091-018]has done, so I have heard say, then the mischief would have been past

[091-019]curing. But to marry such an elegant accomplished gentleman as has been

[091-020]just now described to us—indeed, indeed, though it was a folly, it was

[091-021]not such a great one as you think; for according to the rules of my

[091-022]master here—and he won’t allow me to lie—as of men of letters bishops

[091-023]are made, so of gentlemen knights, specially if they be errant, kings

[091-024]and emperors may be made.”

[091-025]“Thou art right, Sancho,” said Don Quixote, “for with a knight-errant,

[091-026]if he has but two fingers’ breadth of good fortune, it is on the cards

[091-027]to become the mightiest lord on earth. But let señora the Distressed

[091-028]One proceed; for I suspect she has got yet to tell us the bitter part

[091-029]of this so far sweet story.”

[091-030]“The bitter is indeed to come,” said the countess; “and such bitter

[091-031]that colocynth is sweet and oleander toothsome in comparison. The

[091-032]queen, then, being dead, and not in a swoon, we buried her; and hardly

[091-033]had we covered her with earth, hardly had we said our last farewells,

[091-034]when, _quis talia fando temperet a lachrymis?_ over the queen’s grave

[091-035]there appeared, mounted upon a wooden horse, the giant Malambruno,

[091-036]Maguncia’s first cousin, who besides being cruel is an enchanter; and

[091-037]he, to revenge the death of his cousin, punish the audacity of Don

[091-038]Clavijo, and in wrath at the contumacy of Antonomasia, left them both

[091-039]enchanted by his art on the grave itself; she being changed into an ape

[091-040]of brass, and he into a horrible crocodile of some unknown metal; while

[091-041]between the two there stands a pillar, also of metal, with certain

[091-042]characters in the Syriac language inscribed upon it, which, being

[091-043]translated into Kandian, and now into Castilian, contain the following

[091-044]sentence: ‘These two rash lovers shall not recover their former shape

[091-045]until the valiant Manchegan comes to do battle with me in single

[091-046]combat; for the Fates reserve this unexampled adventure for his mighty

[091-047]valour alone.’ This done, he drew from its sheath a huge broad

[091-048]scimitar, and seizing me by the hair he made as though he meant to cut

[091-049]my throat and shear my head clean off. I was terror-stricken, my voice

[091-050]stuck in my throat, and I was in the deepest distress; nevertheless I

[091-051]summoned up my strength as well as I could, and in a trembling and

[091-052]piteous voice I addressed such words to him as induced him to stay the

[091-053]infliction of a punishment so severe. He then caused all the duennas of

[091-054]the palace, those that are here present, to be brought before him; and

[091-055]after having dwelt upon the enormity of our offence, and denounced

[091-056]duennas, their characters, their evil ways and worse intrigues, laying

[091-057]to the charge of all what I alone was guilty of, he said he would not

[091-058]visit us with capital punishment, but with others of a slow nature

[091-059]which would be in effect civil death for ever; and the very instant he

[091-060]ceased speaking we all felt the pores of our faces opening, and

[091-061]pricking us, as if with the points of needles. We at once put our hands

[091-062]up to our faces and found ourselves in the state you now see.”

[091-063]Here the Distressed One and the other duennas raised the veils with

[091-064]which they were covered, and disclosed countenances all bristling with

[091-065]beards, some red, some black, some white, and some grizzled, at which

[091-066]spectacle the duke and duchess made a show of being filled with wonder.

[091-067]Don Quixote and Sancho were overwhelmed with amazement, and the

[091-068]bystanders lost in astonishment, while the Trifaldi went on to say:

[091-069]“Thus did that malevolent villain Malambruno punish us, covering the

[091-070]tenderness and softness of our faces with these rough bristles! Would

[091-071]to heaven that he had swept off our heads with his enormous scimitar

[091-072]instead of obscuring the light of our countenances with these

[091-073]wool-combings that cover us! For if we look into the matter, sirs (and

[091-074]what I am now going to say I would say with eyes flowing like

[091-075]fountains, only that the thought of our misfortune and the oceans they

[091-076]have already wept, keep them as dry as barley spears, and so I say it

[091-077]without tears), where, I ask, can a duenna with a beard go to? What

[091-078]father or mother will feel pity for her? Who will help her? For, if

[091-079]even when she has a smooth skin, and a face tortured by a thousand

[091-080]kinds of washes and cosmetics, she can hardly get anybody to love her,

[091-081]what will she do when she shows a countenance turned into a thicket? Oh

[091-082]duennas, companions mine! it was an unlucky moment when we were born

[091-083]and an ill-starred hour when our fathers begot us!” And as she said

[091-084]this she showed signs of being about to faint.
