[035-001]CERTAIN SKINS OF RED WINE, AND BRINGS THE NOVEL OF “THE ILL-ADVISED

[035-002]CURIOSITY” TO A CLOSE

[035-003]There remained but little more of the novel to be read, when Sancho

[035-004]Panza burst forth in wild excitement from the garret where Don Quixote

[035-005]was lying, shouting, “Run, sirs! quick; and help my master, who is in

[035-006]the thick of the toughest and stiffest battle I ever laid eyes on. By

[035-007]the living God he has given the giant, the enemy of my lady the

[035-008]Princess Micomicona, such a slash that he has sliced his head clean off

[035-009]as if it were a turnip.”

[035-010]“What are you talking about, brother?” said the curate, pausing as he

[035-011]was about to read the remainder of the novel. “Are you in your senses,

[035-012]Sancho? How the devil can it be as you say, when the giant is two

[035-013]thousand leagues away?”

[035-014]Here they heard a loud noise in the chamber, and Don Quixote shouting

[035-015]out, “Stand, thief, brigand, villain; now I have got thee, and thy

[035-016]scimitar shall not avail thee!” And then it seemed as though he were

[035-017]slashing vigorously at the wall.

[035-018]“Don’t stop to listen,” said Sancho, “but go in and part them or help

[035-019]my master: though there is no need of that now, for no doubt the giant

[035-020]is dead by this time and giving account to God of his past wicked life;

[035-021]for I saw the blood flowing on the ground, and the head cut off and

[035-022]fallen on one side, and it is as big as a large wine-skin.”

[035-025]his bed’s head, and the spilt wine must be what this good fellow takes

[035-026]for blood;” and so saying he went into the room and the rest after him,

[035-027]and there they found Don Quixote in the strangest costume in the world.

[035-028]He was in his shirt, which was not long enough in front to cover his

[035-029]thighs completely and was six fingers shorter behind; his legs were

[035-051]between her champion and her opponent. As for Sancho, he went searching

[035-068]The landlord was beside himself at the coolness of the squire and the

[035-081]“Did not I say so?” said Sancho on hearing this. “You see I wasn’t
