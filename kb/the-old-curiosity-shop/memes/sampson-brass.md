## Narrative Details

Sampson Brass, introduced formally as "Sampson Brass of [[bevis-marks|Bevis Marks in the city]] of London, Gentleman, one of her Majesty's attorneys of the Courts of King's Bench and Common Pleas at Westminster" (013-001), is the corrupt lawyer who becomes Quilp's willing instrument. He is described as "a tall, meagre man, with a nose like a wen, a protruding forehead, retreating eyes, and hair of a deep red" and an attorney "of no very good repute" (011-006).

He works from a small office in [[bevis-marks|Bevis Marks]] alongside his sister and clerk [[sally-brass|Sally Brass]] — "a lady of thirty-five or thereabouts, of a gaunt and bony figure, and a resolute bearing" (033-006). Together they form a grotesque partnership, running a legal practice sustained by dubious clients and shady dealings. Miss [[sally-brass|Sally Brass]] serves as her brother's "clerk, assistant, housekeeper, secretary, confidential plotter, adviser, intriguer, and bill of costs increaser" (033-005).

When Quilp requires a legal agent to fabricate a case against [[kit-nubbles|Kit Nubbles]], Brass willingly complies. He falsifies evidence and orchestrates Kit's arrest. The scheme is eventually exposed through the tenacity of [[dick-swiveller|Dick Swiveller]] and the Marchioness, and Brass is driven from his practice in disgrace.
