# Miss Cheggs

**Miss Cheggs** is a minor character in [[Charles Dickens]]'s novel
*[[The Old Curiosity Shop]]*. She is the sister of [[cheggs|Mr. Cheggs]],
the market-gardener, and appears in Chapter 8 as an active partisan in
her brother's romantic rivalry with [[dick-swiveller|Dick Swiveller]]
for the affections of [[miss-sophy-wackles|Miss Sophy Wackles]].

## The Wackles Party

Miss Cheggs arrives at the Wackles party accompanying her brother, "who
making straight to Miss Sophy and taking her by both hands, and kissing
her on both cheeks, hoped in an audible whisper that they had not come
too early" (008-016). She immediately sets about undermining Swiveller's
position, "not confining herself to expressing by scornful smiles a
contempt for Mr. Swiveller's accomplishments," but also whispering
expressions of condolence into Miss Sophy's ear about "her being worried
by such a ridiculous creature" (008-021).

> "You must dance with Miss Cheggs," said Miss Sophy to Dick Swiveller...
> "She's such a nice girl — and her brother's quite delightful." (008-022)

Miss Sophy's directive that Swiveller dance with Miss Cheggs is a
tactical manoeuvre — she has herself "danced twice with Mr. Cheggs and
made great show of encouraging his advances" (008-022). Miss Cheggs
functions as both chaperone and proxy, her presence lending the
respectability of family sanction to her brother's suit.

## Comic Function

Miss Cheggs's role is that of the comic ally — a stock figure in
Dickens's courtship scenarios. She darts between dancers to deliver
bulletins to Mrs. and Miss Wackles, her remarks "gall and wormwood to
Richard Swiveller's soul" (008-041), and she returns to Sophy with
breathless reports: "Alick has been saying such things to Sophy. ...
you can't think how out he has been speaking!" (008-042, 008-044).
Her exaggerated loyalty to her brother's cause contributes to the
chapter's farcical portrayal of romantic rivalry.
