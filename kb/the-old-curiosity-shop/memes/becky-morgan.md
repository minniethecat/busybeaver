# Becky Morgan

**Becky Morgan** is a minor but symbolically significant figure in
[[Charles Dickens]]'s novel *[[The Old Curiosity Shop]]*. She is a
deceased person buried in the village churchyard, mentioned in
Chapter 54 during a conversation between [[nell-trent|Little Nell]]
and [[the-sexton|the sexton]].

## The Sexton's Preoccupation

> "How old was Becky Morgan?" asked the sexton. (054-013)

The sexton, an old man whose work brings him into daily contact with
mortality, becomes fixated on the question of Becky Morgan's age at
death. He pauses in his digging, "cleansing his spade with a piece of
slate he had by him for the purpose — and scraping off, in the process,
the essence of Heaven knows how many Becky Morgans" (054-016), to
ponder the matter.

## Symbolic Significance

Becky Morgan represents the anonymous dead whose lives are reduced to
names on gravestones, known only to the sexton who buries them. Her
age troubles the old man because it serves as a personal benchmark
against which he measures his own remaining years. "It was plain that
Becky Morgan's age still troubled him, though why, the child could
scarcely understand" (054-041).

By the chapter's end, the sexton and Nell's grandfather console
themselves with "the little fiction they had agreed upon, respecting
Becky Morgan; whose decease was no longer a precedent of uncomfortable
application, and would be no business of theirs for half-a-score of
years to come" (054-058). Becky Morgan thus becomes a device through
which Dickens explores the human tendency to deflect the contemplation
of [[death|death]] by transforming it into an abstract puzzle.
