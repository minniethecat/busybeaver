## Narrative Details

Barbara is the maid in the Garland household in Finchley, where [[kit-nubbles|Kit Nubbles]] finds employment after Nell and her grandfather flee London. She is a gentle-hearted young woman described as "that softhearted, gentle, foolish little Barbara" (068-010). Her introduction is marked by a memorable scene in the Garland kitchen — "at the bottom of the stairs there was such a kitchen as was never before seen or heard of out of a toyshop window" (022-023).

Kit's mother is carefully vetted by Mrs. Garland before Barbara is allowed to associate with the family, [[mrs-jiniwin|the old lady]] noting "that it's necessary to be very careful and particular in such a matter as this" (021-009). Barbara, who had "lived a very quiet life, blushed very much and was quite as embarrassed and uncertain what she ought to say or do" (022-024) upon first meeting Kit.

Over time, Barbara becomes Kit's steady romantic interest and source of comfort. When Kit is falsely accused of theft, Barbara remains steadfast in her belief in his innocence, visiting him in his distress. After Kit's vindication, Barbara is finally able to speak her feelings openly, and the two become an established couple. In the novel's closing chapters, she appears with Kit at the final reunions, "the ever [[hope|faith]]ful Barbara's mother, still holding the baby as if she had never put it down since that sad day when they little hoped" (068-009).
