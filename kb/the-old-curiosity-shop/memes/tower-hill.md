# Tower Hill

Tower Hill is a district in London where [[daniel-quilp|Daniel Quilp]] resides with his wife [[mrs-quilp|Mrs. Quilp]]. The location, dominated by the "old grey Tower," serves as a key setting in the novel, reflecting Quilp's grotesque character through its gloomy, oppressive atmosphere [004-001].

## In the Novel

Mr. and Mrs. Quilp "resided on Tower Hill; and in her bower on Tower Hill, Mrs. Quilp was left a hopeless, melancholy widow" after the dwarf's death [004-001]. The dwarf's lodging "comprised, besides the needful accommodation for himself and Mrs. Quilp, all the requisites for carrying on his business" [004-003].

Quilp uses Tower Hill as a symbol of his power over his wife: "You shall come with me to Tower Hill, and see Mrs. Quilp" [006-021], he tells [[kit-nubbles|Kit]], wielding his domestic authority as both threat and boast. His legal identity is formally linked to the location: "Daniel Quilp of Tower Hill, and Sampson Brass of Bevis Marks" [013-001].

In moments of high emotion, Quilp "reached Tower Hill" and "gazed up at the window of his own house" [049-003], the location anchoring his domestic and professional identities.

## Symbolic Significance

The "old grey Tower" and "piles of building on the river side" [005-024] mirror Quilp's own character — ancient, oppressive, and crumbling. The Tower "turned from its own proper colours" as night fell [004-067], suggesting the moral darkness that surrounds Quilp's household.
