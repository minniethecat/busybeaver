# Daniel Quilp

The villainous dwarf and primary antagonist in [[the-old-curiosity-shop|The Old Curiosity Shop]]. Daniel Quilp is one of Dickens's most memorable villains — a malevolent dwarf whose cruelty, greed, and perverse humor drive much of the novel's conflict. He is the embodiment of voracious evil who torments his wife, schemes against the novel's heroes, and meets a fitting end by drowning.

## Narrative Details

Quilp is introduced as "an elderly man of remarkably hard features and forbidding aspect, and so low in stature as to be quite a dwarf" (003-001). He identifies himself bluntly: "Quilp is my name. You might remember. It's not a long one—Daniel Quilp" (003-021). He lives with his wife on Tower Hill, where he keeps a household marked by his volatile temper and perverse humor (004-001).

Quilp's occupations are diverse and his methods unscrupulous (004-002). He holds a mortgage over the old curiosity shop and uses his financial power to torment Nell's grandfather. He takes over the shop itself and sleeps in Nell's old bed as an act of possessive cruelty. He enlists the corrupt lawyer [[sampson-brass|Sampson Brass]] as his agent, and together they scheme against [[kit-nubbles|Kit Nubbles]].

> "Here's Nelly Trent, dear Mrs. Quilp," said her husband. "A glass of wine my dear, and a biscuit, for she has had a long walk."
>
> (006-040)

In his final scene, pursued by the authorities, Quilp falls from a wharf and drowns in the Thames — a death as turbulent and dark as his life.
