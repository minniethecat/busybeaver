# The Single Gentleman

The mysterious lodger in [[the-old-curiosity-shop|The Old Curiosity Shop]]. The Single Gentleman is a figure who enters the novel's middle chapters as an enigma — a lodger at Brass's establishment who proves to be far more than he first appears, ultimately becoming a crucial agent in the exposure of Quilp's conspiracy.

## Role in the Novel

The Single Gentleman operates as a quiet detective within the novel's plot: he investigates the circumstances of Nell's disappearance, gathers intelligence from within Brass's own household, and ultimately helps bring about the collapse of the conspiracy against Kit.

## Character Traits

A gruff, eccentric exterior conceals sharp intelligence and deep feeling — a man who uses his apparent oddness as a shield.

## Significant Appearances

The Single Gentleman appears in the [[bevis-marks|Bevis Marks]] chapters (XXXIII onwards), where his eccentric behaviour masks a serious purpose.

## Narrative Details

The Single Gentleman first appears when he takes lodgings at the Brass establishment in [[bevis-marks|Bevis Marks]] — the very house with the bill, "First floor to let to a single gentleman," tied to the door (033-004). His arrival is announced by the "bumping sounds" of his enormous trunk "being nearly twice as wide as the stairs" (034-032). When at last the trunk is wrestled into his room, he simply "sat down upon it and wiped his bald head and face with his handkerchief" (034-033).

Brass sees only an eccentric tenant, but the [[the-bachelor|Single Gentleman]]'s conduct is studied. He negotiates his rent with gruff precision — "Two weeks! Two years. I shall live here for two years. Here. Ten pounds down. The bargain's made" (034-041). His abruptness masks his true purpose: he is investigating the disappearance of [[nell-trent|Nell Trent]], whose grandfather is connected to his past.

The Single Gentleman's role becomes critical when Kit is falsely accused. The "distant squeak" that alarms him and causes him to burst from his room — running "violently down the stairs, out into the street" without his hat (037-013) — signals the moment when his quiet investigation gives way to urgent action. He becomes a key witness and agent in the exposure of the Brass-Quilp conspiracy, helping to secure Kit's acquittal and bring down the villains.
