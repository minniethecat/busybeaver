## Narrative Details

Nell lives with her grandfather in the old curiosity shop, where she passes her days among "the old and curious things" that fill the premises. Her grandfather speaks of her with intense devotion, asking "who ever loved a child as I love Nell?" (001-035). She has a childlike, innocent manner — "it was childlike and full of hilarity" (001-046) — that touches nearly everyone she meets.

Nell is deeply attached to her grandfather and follows him when they are forced to flee London. Her grandfather's gambling addiction, fed by his desperate desire to secure her future, becomes the very force that destroys their security. Her older brother Fred Trent, who is absent for much of Nell's early life, reappears with mercenary intentions (002-041). Throughout their wanderings, Nell remains the moral compass — patient, prayerful, and protective of the old man.
