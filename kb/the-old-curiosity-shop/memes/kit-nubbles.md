## Narrative Details

Kit is described as "a shock-headed shambling awkward lad with an uncommonly wide mouth, very red cheeks, a turned-up nose, and certainly the most comical expression of face I ever saw" (001-049). Nell's grandfather speaks of him dismissively — "She always laughs at poor Kit" (001-047) — but Kit's devotion to the family never wavers.

When Nell and her grandfather flee London, Kit is left behind and finds work with the Garlands, a kindly family in Finchley. Mr. Garland takes a particular interest in him, and Kit becomes a valued servant. At the Garland household he meets [[barbara|Barbara]], the maid, with whom a gentle romance develops (022-024). Throughout this period Kit does everything he can to locate the missing Nell.

Kit's fortunes turn dark when Quilp and the Brass siblings conspire to frame him for theft. He is arrested and brought to trial (056-022). Eventually the scheme is uncovered, Kit is vindicated, and the Notary who exposed the plot refers to "Honest Kit" in his denunciation of the conspirators (060-023).
