# Jowl

Jowl (also called Mr. Jowl or Joe Jowl) is a professional gambler and the gruffer of the two card-sharpers who prey upon the [[the-grandfather|Grandfather]]'s gambling addiction in [[the-old-curiosity-shop|The Old Curiosity Shop]]. He works in partnership with [[isaac-list|Isaac List]].

## In the Novel

Jowl appears primarily in Chapter 42, where he and List have cornered the desperate Grandfather in a squalid room. He plays the role of the hard-edged partner to List's softer approach. When List intercedes with apparent kindness, Jowl retorts with sarcasm: "Tender-hearted lamb, I am, to be sitting here at my time of life" [042-023].

He presents himself as a man of "honorable dealing" who has been maligned [042-034], yet his predatory nature is unmistakable. Lying down with "a careless air" [042-035], he pressures the old man with false reluctance: "I have passed my word with feigned reluctance" [042-043]. He urges immediate action: "Why not tonight?" [042-045].

Jowl's casual cruelty is evident in his mockery — "Ha, ha, ha! Joe Jowl's half sorry he advised you" [042-040] — and his final pragmatic assessment as he "warmed his hands at the fire" [042-052] while dividing the spoils.

He is last mentioned alongside List as one of "the gamblers" whose scheme ultimately collapses [073-020].

## Character

Jowl represents the openly menacing predator, in contrast to List's oily persuasion. Where List wheedles, Jowl threatens and mocks. His physical description — lying carelessly, warming his hands while discussing his victim's ruin — embodies Dickens's moral grotesque: corruption made visible in gesture and posture.
