## Narrative Details

[[the-marchioness|The Marchioness]] has been a silent witness to the entire conspiracy. From her subterranean position in the Brass household, she has "always remained somewhere in the bowels of the earth under [[bevis-marks|Bevis Marks]], and never came to the surface unless the bell rang" (036-007) — but she heard everything. She knows where the evidence was planted. She knows who gave the orders.

Her bond with [[dick-swiveller|Dick Swiveller]] gives her the courage to act. When Swiveller falls gravely ill, she tends him in secret, and it is from his sickbed that she resolves to tell what she knows. Her testimony is the crucial evidence that breaks the case — the word of a half-starved servant girl against the legal machinery of [[sampson-brass|Sampson Brass]] and the wealth of [[daniel-quilp|Daniel Quilp]].

The rescue is not merely a plot twist but a moral reversal: the most powerless character in the novel becomes the agent of justice. [[the-marchioness|The Marchioness]], who was not even granted a name by her abusers, speaks the truth that frees Kit and condemns the conspirators. In a novel filled with innocents who suffer passively, she acts — and her action changes everything.
