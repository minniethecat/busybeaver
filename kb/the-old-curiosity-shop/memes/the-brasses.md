# The Brasses

**The Brasses** are a sibling duo in [[Charles Dickens]]'s novel
*[[The Old Curiosity Shop]]*: [[sally-brass|Sally Brass]] and
[[sampson-brass|Sampson Brass]]. Together they operate a legal practice
at [[bevis-marks|Bevis Marks]] and serve as accomplices to the
malevolent [[daniel-quilp|Daniel Quilp]].

## The Brass Partnership

Sampson Brass is an attorney "of no very good repute" (011-006) who
acts as Quilp's legal agent, while his sister Sally serves as his
clerk, managing the office with a vigour and sharpness that surpasses
her brother's. The two are intensely loyal to each other — and to Quilp,
whose schemes they facilitate without moral scruple. Their household
includes [[the-marchioness|the Marchioness]], a small servant girl whom
Sally Brass mistreats, keeping her in a state of near-starvation.

The Brasses represent the corruption of the legal profession in
Dickens's fictional world. Unlike the principled notary
[[mr-witherden|Mr. Witherden]], who also practises at Bevis Marks,
the Brasses use the law as a tool of oppression and deceit, their
familial bond reinforcing rather than moderating their ethical failures.

## Individual Portraits

- **[[sally-brass|Sally Brass]]** — The formidable dragoness of

- **[[sampson-brass|Sampson Brass]]** — A weak-willed attorney whose
