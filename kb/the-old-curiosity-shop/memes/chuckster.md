# Mr. Chuckster

Mr. Chuckster is a clerk in the office of [[sampson-brass|Sampson Brass]] and a friend of [[dick-swiveller|Dick Swiveller]] in [[the-old-curiosity-shop|The Old Curiosity Shop]]. He is characterized by his self-importance, comic pretension, and distinctive habit of wearing a pen behind his ear.

## In the Novel

Chuckster first appears in Chapter 14, assisting in the notary's office. Brass calls him to "bring in Mr. Abel's articles" [014-025], and he serves as a witness to the legal document [014-030]. He is described arriving "with a pen behind his ear and his face inflamed with the exercise of composing" [014-031] — a detail that captures his comic self-regard.

His pomposity is a source of humor: when the Grandfather raises an objection, Chuckster "roared outright and appeared to enjoy the joke exceedingly" [014-037]. He stands outside the office "with his hands under his coat-tails" observing the scene [020-041], affecting the manner of a man of importance.

In Chapter 38, he condescends to interact with [[kit-nubbles|Kit]], addressing him as "Snobby" [038-009] and issuing commands with exaggerated authority: "Pull up, Snobby. Go and see" [038-011]. He pointedly ignores Kit's protests: "To this remonstrance Mr. Chuckster deigned no other answer" [038-013].

Chuckster cultivates a reputation as "a gentleman of a cultivated mind" [038-046] and participates in the [[glorious-apollos|Glorious Apollos]] lodge alongside Swiveller. Despite his pretensions, he remains a minor clerk, his grandiosity undercut by his circumstances.

## Character

Chuckster functions as comic relief and as a satirical portrait of petty officialdom. His pen-behind-the-ear, his ceremonial manner, and his condescension to those below him all suggest the clerk who mistakes proximity to power for power itself. He is a warm-up sketch for characters like Guppy in *Bleak House* — the legal functionary whose self-estimation far exceeds his station.
