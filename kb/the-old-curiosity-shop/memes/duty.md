# Duty

The theme of duty permeates [[the-old-curiosity-shop|The Old Curiosity Shop]] as a central element of Dickens's moral vision. [[nell-trent|Nell Trent]] embodies this quality most fully — her journey is an extended meditation on its meaning and cost. The novel distinguishes between authentic and counterfeit expressions, with true duty demonstrated through action rather than rhetoric.

## In the Novel

Nell's experience represents the theme in its most concentrated form. Her response to circumstances reveals duty as both a personal virtue and a social critique — the novel indicts a world that demands such duty from the vulnerable while sparing the powerful.

## Key Examples

- **[[nell-trent|Nell Trent]]**: The primary embodiment, giving the theme its most powerful expression in the novel
- **[[kit-nubbles|Kit Nubbles]]**: Represents duty in its everyday form — consistently demonstrated through reliable action
- **The schoolmaster**: His quiet practice of duty shows the theme operating in ordinary life

## Dickens's Treatment

The novel treats duty as a fundamental moral quality — one that distinguishes the virtuous from the vicious and gives meaning to suffering.
