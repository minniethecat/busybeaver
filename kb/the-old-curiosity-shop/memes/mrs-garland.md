# Mrs. Garland

**Mrs. Garland** is a character in [[Charles Dickens]]'s novel
*[[The Old Curiosity Shop]]*. She is the wife of [[mr-garland|Mr. Garland]],
mother of [[abel-garland|Abel Garland]], and a resident of
[[abel-cottage|Abel Cottage, Finchley]].

## Role in the Novel

Mrs. Garland is introduced in Chapter 21 when she and her husband
interview [[kit-nubbles|Kit Nubbles]] for a position in their household.
She explains to Kit's mother that "we're only three in family, and are
very quiet regular folks, and it would be a sad thing if we made any
kind of mistake" (021-009). With her husband, she formally hires Kit
"at an annual income of Six Pounds, over and above his board and lodging"
(021-011).

## Character

Mrs. Garland is portrayed as a gentle, home-loving woman who takes an
interest in the domestic affairs of those around her. During Kit's
interview, she attends to Kit's younger siblings and, hearing from
Kit's mother about the remarkable circumstances of their births, shares
corresponding stories about the birth of her own son Abel (021-011).
Kit comes to regard her, along with the rest of the Garland household,
as family, and [[abel-cottage|Abel Cottage]] as "his own proper home"
(038-001).

> In exact proportion as he became manageable by Kit he became utterly
> ungovernable by anybody else ... but as Kit always represented that
> this was only his fun, or a way he had of showing his attachment to
> his employers, Mrs. Garland gradually suffered herself to be persuaded
> into the belief. (038-006)

Her trusting nature is shown in her relationship with the Garland
family pony, whose unruly behaviour she learns to accept through Kit's
reassuring explanations (038-006). [[barbara|Barbara]], the Garlands'
servant, completes the household.
