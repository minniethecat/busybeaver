# Isaac List

Isaac List is a professional gambler and associate of [[jowl|Jowl]] in [[the-old-curiosity-shop|The Old Curiosity Shop]]. He preys upon the [[the-grandfather|Grandfather]]'s gambling addiction, participating in the card games that ultimately ruin the old man financially.

## In the Novel

Isaac List first appears in Chapter 29 at the gambling house where he and Jowl play cards. He is described as "of a more slender figure" than his companion [029-032]. During the game, he is addressed familiarly as "old Isaac" [029-020], suggesting long experience in the gambling world.

In Chapter 30, List "rose the only winner" when the play ended [030-001], having taken money from the Grandfather. He is portrayed as calculating and manipulative, using soft words to entrap his victim.

List reappears in Chapter 42, squatting in a dark room with Jowl and the desperate Grandfather [042-014]. He plays the role of the sympathetic accomplice, interceding with apparent kindness: "Don't be hard upon him, Jowl" [042-022] and "He's very sorry for giving offence" [042-024]. This facade of compassion masks his predatory intent as he urges the old man to commit further funds: "But could you? Is your bank strong enough?" [042-030].

He is ultimately mentioned alongside Jowl as one of "the gamblers" who, "with their trusty company," pass from the narrative after their scheme collapses [073-020].

## Character

List embodies the calculating, soft-spoken predator who uses apparent reasonableness to manipulate. Unlike the gruff Jowl, List's approach is one of oily persuasion, making him the more insidious of the two gamblers. His figure — "slender" and squatting — reinforces Dickens's grotesque characterization of moral corruption.
