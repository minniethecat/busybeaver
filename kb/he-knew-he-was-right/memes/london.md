# London

Primary metropolitan setting of the novel, center of Trevelyan's social and professional life.

## Overview

A significant location in *He Knew He Was Right*, London serves as the setting for key events in the novel's domestic and social drama. The location reflects Trollope's characteristic attention to the relationship between place and character, where setting is never merely backdrop but shapes the possibilities and constraints within which characters act.

## Significance in the Novel

London functions as more than setting — it is a narrative space that defines character possibilities and social meanings. The novel's geography maps onto its moral structure, with different locations associated with different values, constraints, and forms of social life.
