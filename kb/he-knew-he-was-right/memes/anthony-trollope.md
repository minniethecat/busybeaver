# Anthony Trollope

**Anthony Trollope** (1815–1882) was a British novelist and Post Office official, one of the most prolific and critically regarded authors of the Victorian era. He wrote 47 novels, including *He Knew He Was Right* (1869), the Chronicles of Barsetshire series, and the Palliser novels. Trollope is celebrated for his acute social observation, his psychologically complex characterizations, and his unsentimental portrayal of Victorian institutions — particularly marriage, politics, and the church.

## Connection to *He Knew He Was Right*

*He Knew He Was Right* was published in 1869, during the peak of Trollope's career. It appeared first in serial form (as was customary for Victorian novels) with illustrations by [[Marcus Stone]]. The novel represents one of Trollope's most ambitious psychological studies — an extended examination of pathological jealousy and its destructive effects on a marriage. Unlike the more genial satire of the Barsetshire novels, *He Knew He Was Right* is notable for its dark trajectory: the protagonist's descent into madness and death is rendered with a clinical precision unusual for its era.

The novel draws on Trollope's deep knowledge of the legal and social constraints governing Victorian marriage, a theme he explored throughout his fiction but never with quite this degree of tragic intensity.
