# Marcus Stone

**Marcus Stone** (1840–1921) was a British illustrator and painter, best known for his illustrations for the novels of [[Anthony Trollope]] and Charles Dickens. For *He Knew He Was Right* (1869), Stone provided the original illustrations that accompanied the novel's serial publication. As noted in the novel's front matter:

> illustrated by Marcus Stone, and those illustrations can [001-006]

> With Illustrations by Marcus Stone [001-014]

## Connection to *He Knew He Was Right*

Stone's illustrations for *He Knew He Was Right* were part of the novel's original serial publication, appearing alongside the text as was customary for Victorian fiction. His visual interpretations of key scenes — the Trevelyan household, the confrontations between [[Louis Trevelyan]] and [[Emily Trevelyan]], the rural settings of [[Nuncombe Putney]] and [[Exeter]] — shaped the novel's initial reception, giving readers a visual vocabulary for Trollope's characters and settings.

Stone was one of the most sought-after illustrators of the 1860s, working with Trollope on several novels. His style — detailed, naturalistic, and emotionally restrained — complemented Trollope's own realist aesthetic.
