# The Daily Record

**The Daily Record** is a fictional London newspaper in [[Anthony Trollope]]'s *He Knew He Was Right* (1869) that employs [[Hugh Stanbury]] as a journalist. It represents the rising profession of journalism in Victorian England — a profession that the novel treats with characteristic Trollopian ambivalence, balancing respect for its growing social power against anxiety about its intrusion into private life.

## Role in the Novel

The Daily Record serves two crucial narrative functions:

1. **Hugh Stanbury's livelihood**: Stanbury's position at the newspaper enables his independence from his autocratic aunt, Miss Stanbury of [[Exeter]]. His salary, though modest, allows him to pursue his courtship of [[Nora Rowley]] without being dependent on family patronage.

2. **The public sphere**: When [[The Trevelyan Separation]] becomes a matter of public controversy, the newspaper becomes a battleground. Stanbury uses his platform to defend [[Emily Trevelyan]]'s reputation against rumours spread by [[Louis Trevelyan]]'s allies, making the Daily Record an instrument of justice in the novel's moral economy.

## Journalism in Victorian Context

Trollope wrote *He Knew He Was Right* at a moment when journalism was transforming from a disreputable trade into a recognized profession. The novel reflects this transition: Hugh Stanbury's career choice is controversial within his family (Miss Stanbury considers it beneath their dignity), yet the novel ultimately validates it as honest, useful work. The Daily Record thus embodies one of Trollope's recurring themes — the dignity of professional labour in a society still organized around inheritance and patronage.
