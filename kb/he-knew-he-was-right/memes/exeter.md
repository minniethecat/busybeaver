# Exeter

**Exeter** is the ancient cathedral city in Devonshire that serves as a significant setting in [[Anthony Trollope]]'s *He Knew He Was Right* (1869). It is the home of the formidable **Miss Jemima Stanbury**, one of the novel's most memorable minor characters, whose wealth, opinions, and social authority connect the rural world of [[Nuncombe Putney]] to the broader currents of Victorian society. Exeter in the novel functions as a middle ground — neither the corrupt sophistication of [[London]] nor the rustic simplicity of the Devonshire village, but a place where provincial dignity and social power intersect.

## Miss Stanbury's Exeter

The city is defined in the novel almost entirely through the figure of **Miss Jemima Stanbury**, an elderly spinster of considerable fortune who inhabits a house in the Cathedral Close. Miss Stanbury is a magnificent creation — generous, tyrannical, opinionated, and deeply invested in the lives of her relatives:

> She was a lady who liked to have her own way, and who generally had it. [002-050, adapted]

Her Exeter household becomes a site of both refuge and contestation. She takes in her niece [[Dorothy Stanbury]], whose gentle nature both soothes and exasperates her aunt. She also exerts powerful influence over [[Hugh Stanbury]]'s prospects, alternately threatening disinheritance and offering patronage depending on whether his journalistic career and choice of wife meet with her approval.

## The Cathedral Close

Exeter's Cathedral Close — the exclusive residential precinct surrounding Exeter Cathedral — is the novel's most evocative representation of provincial English respectability. Miss Stanbury's house in the Close is a symbol of her status: ancient, dignified, and governed by codes of behaviour as rigid as any in London drawing rooms, yet distinctly provincial in character.

## Role in the Novel's Social Geography

| Setting | Moral Character | Key Figures |
|---------|----------------|-------------|
| [[London]] | Sophisticated but corrupting | Louis Trevelyan, Colonel Osborne |
| **Exeter** | Respectable, socially powerful | Miss Stanbury, Dorothy Stanbury |
| [[Nuncombe Putney]] | Simple, honest, sheltering | Mrs. Stanbury, Emily Trevelyan (in exile) |

Exeter's intermediate position in this moral geography is crucial: it is close enough to Nuncombe Putney to connect the village to the wider world, but far enough from London to preserve its moral integrity.
