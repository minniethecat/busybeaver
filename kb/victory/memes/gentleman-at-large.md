# A gentleman at large

## Quotation

> "My name? Oh, plain Mr. Jones — put that down — a gentleman at large. And this is Ricardo."
>
> (011-038)

## Context

Mr. Jones arrives at Schomberg's hotel in Surabaya with his 'secretary' Martin Ricardo and their 'alligator-hunter' servant Pedro. When Schomberg, complying with hotel registration requirements, asks his name, Jones delivers this response with languid irony. The phrase 'gentleman at large' — meaning a gentleman unattached to any fixed establishment — is technically accurate while being comprehensively misleading about the nature of Jones's activities.

## Significance

The self-description 'gentleman at large' is a masterpiece (011-038) of Conrad's ironic method. Jones is indeed a gentleman in manner — well-spoken, fastidious, possessed of a certain code — but he is also a gambler, a thief, and ultimately a killer. The phrase exposes the hollowness of the category 'gentleman' when detached from moral substance. Jones is Heyst's dark double: both are 'gentlemen at large' in the colonial world, unattached to any institution or community, operating by personal codes that place them outside conventional morality. The difference is that Jones has embraced the predatory logic of his detachment, while Heyst has not — and this difference proves fatal.
