## Overview

The siege of the bungalow occupies the final section of *Victory* (Part IV), transforming Heyst's isolated compound on Samburan into a stage for the novel's fatal convergence of characters (026-005) (037-001). Mr. Jones, Ricardo, and Pedro — the criminal trio from Schomberg's Hotel (011-038) — arrive on the island seeking Heyst's rumored treasure (025-100) (025-106). What follows is not a treasure hunt but a psychological siege, a waiting game in which the thin veneer of civilization peels away to reveal the violence beneath.

The siege unfolds over three days of mounting tension (030-065) (031-024). Heyst and Lena are barricaded in the bungalow; Wang, the Chinese servant, has armed himself and retreated to the compound's periphery (033-012) (033-008); Mr. Jones and his men prowl the grounds, uncertain of their quarry and increasingly at odds with one another (026-005) (032-024). The jungle itself becomes a participant — silent, watching, indifferent (033-003).

## Key Events

The siege reaches its crisis when Ricardo, attempting to assault Lena, is killed by Mr. Jones — who mistakes him for Heyst (037-055) (039-018) (039-025). Mr. Jones then confronts Heyst, and in the confrontation that follows, Lena is fatally shot while attempting to protect Heyst (038-001) (038-010) (038-018). Mr. Jones, discovering his error, dies by drowning or at Heyst's hand (039-025). The bungalow, once a refuge, becomes a tomb.

## Aftermath

Davidson arrives on his regular mail-boat run to find the compound in ruins (039-004) (039-009) (039-011). Heyst has perished in the fire that consumes the bungalow (039-020) (039-023). Only Wang survives, carrying Lena's body from the flames (039-023). The "victory" of the novel's title takes on its bitterest irony: there are no survivors, only witnesses to the wreckage.
