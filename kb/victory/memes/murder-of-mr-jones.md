# Murder of Mr. Jones

## Summary

After the fatal shooting of Lena, Mr. Jones vanishes from the bungalow verandah "like the spectre he was" (038-001). In the hours that follow, Jones — separated from his dead secretary and stranded on an island he cannot leave — encounters his own end at the wharf of Samburan. Davidson's investigation reveals two deaths in rapid succession: first, Jones shoots Ricardo through the heart during a confused exchange in the dark; then Jones himself is found dead, "huddled up on the bottom between two piles, like a heap of bones in a blue silk bag" (039-025). Conrad leaves the exact cause ambiguous — accident or perhaps not — but Wang's satisfaction at the discovery suggests the Chinaman may have played a more active role than Davidson's account admits.

## Context

The chain of events leading to Jones's death begins with Lena's plan to disarm Ricardo, which she executes by seizing his knife during their struggle in the bungalow (037-001). Heyst, freed from Jones's custody, returns to the bungalow and charges through the door (038-001). Jones fires a shot over Heyst's shoulder and flees. As Davidson recounts: "Mr. Jones, after firing his shot over Heyst's shoulder, had thought it proper to dodge away. Like the spectre he was, he had noiselessly vanished from the verandah" (038-001).

Ricardo, wounded and disarmed, staggers into the night searching for "the governor" (038-001). Wang, meanwhile, has been acting independently — he casts the boat adrift and shoots the hairy Pedro when the latter awakens (039-023). This act of quiet, decisive sabotage traps Jones on Samburan. With the boat gone, his henchman Pedro dead, and Ricardo later killed by his own hand, Jones's carefully orchestrated criminal expedition collapses entirely.

## Key Participants

- **Mr. Jones** — the "spectral" antagonist, a gentleman outlaw who despises women and has orchestrated the siege of Heyst's bungalow to extract imagined riches. His death reverses the predator-prey relationship: the hunter becomes the trapped.
- **Martin Ricardo** — Jones's "secretary" and faithful follower, whose infatuation with Lena undermines Jones's authority and precipitates the final chaos. Jones kills him, "neatly through the heart" (039-025).
- **Wang** — Heyst's Chinese servant, whose quiet agency proves decisive. He shoots Pedro, casts off the boat, and later "was very pleased when he discovered" Jones's body (039-025).
- **Axel Heyst** — the man whose bungalow becomes the stage for the entire tragedy. He hears Jones's fatal shot from the verandah.
- **Lena** — already dead from Jones's bullet, her sacrifice sets in motion the final chain of events.

## Key Passages

The final confrontation between Jones and Ricardo — the governor and his broken secretary — unfolds in the darkness after Lena's death. Davidson and Heyst overhear it from the verandah:

> "As we stood there, just before I left him, for he said he wanted to be alone with his dead for a time, we heard a snarly sort of voice near the bushes by the shore calling out: 'Is that you, governor?' 'Yes, it's me.' 'Jee-miny! I thought the beggar had done for you. He has started prancing and nearly had me. I have been dodging around, looking for you ever since.' 'Well, here I am,' suddenly screamed the other voice, and then a shot rang out. 'This time he has not missed him,' Heyst said to me bitterly, and went back into the house." (039-014 to 039-019)

Davidson's investigation the next day confirms both deaths:

> "The carcass of that follower, secretary, or whatever the unclean ruffian called himself, I left where it lay, to swell and rot in the sun. His principal had shot him neatly through the heart. Then, apparently, this Jones went down the wharf to look for the boat and for the hairy man. I suppose he tumbled into the water by accident — or perhaps not by accident. The boat and the man were gone, and the scoundrel saw himself all alone, his game clearly up, and fairly trapped. Who knows? The water's very clear there, and I could see him huddled up on the bottom between two piles, like a heap of bones in a blue silk bag, with only the head and the feet sticking out." (039-025)

Wang's reaction to finding the body is telling: "Wang was very pleased when he discovered him. That made everything safe, he said" (039-025).

## Consequences

Jones's death completes the elimination of all three intruders: Pedro shot by Wang (039-023), Ricardo shot by Jones (039-025), and Jones dead at the wharf (039-025). The island is "safe" again — but at the cost of Lena's life and, ultimately, Heyst's. Wang's remarkable agency throughout transforms him from a background servant into the only survivor who acted with consistent, effective purpose.

The ambiguity surrounding Jones's death — accident or something more deliberate — mirrors the novel's broader epistemological uncertainties. Davidson's phrase "or perhaps not by accident" (039-025) leaves the question open, and Wang's self-satisfaction strongly implies his involvement.

## Analysis

Jones's death represents the logical endpoint of his worldview. A man who lived by predation and contempt for humanity finds himself alone, trapped, and stripped of every advantage. His spectral quality — Conrad repeatedly describes him in terms of death, corpses, and ghosts — becomes literal. The "heap of bones in a blue silk bag" (039-025) is the final image: a man who pretended to aristocratic elevation reduced to detritus tangled among wharf pilings.

The contrast with Wang is crucial. Wang — whom Heyst earlier envied for his "obedience to his instincts, the powerful simplicity of purpose" (016-043) — acts decisively at every turn: he shoots Pedro, casts off the boat, and is "very pleased" to find Jones dead (039-025). Where Heyst's philosophy of detachment leads to paralysis and tragedy, Wang's instinctual pragmatism ensures survival. The Chinaman who seemed merely a background figure emerges as the narrative's secret agent of resolution.

Jones's murder of Ricardo also warrants attention. Having lost his secretary's loyalty to Lena — "A woman had intervened!" (036-094) — Jones takes revenge not on the woman (already dead by his own bullet) but on the man who betrayed him. The shooting is cold, immediate, and final: a single shot through the heart (039-025). It is the act of a man who, even in defeat, refuses to surrender his despotic authority.
