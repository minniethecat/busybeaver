# Zangiacomo Ladies' Orchestra

## Description

The Zangiacomo Ladies' Orchestra is a touring musical ensemble performing at Schomberg's Hotel in Surabaya when the novel's main action begins. Run by a German who dyes his hair and beard black and uses the Italian name "Zangiacomo" for professional purposes, the orchestra is a seedy operation that exploits its female members (005-029) — providing them with minimal wages and subjecting them to the predatory attention of hotel guests:

> As we know, Heyst had gone to stay in Schomberg's hotel in complete ignorance that his person was odious to that worthy. When he arrived, Zangiacomo's Ladies' Orchestra had been established there for some time.
>
> (008-001)

The orchestra members, mostly Italian and one English girl (Lena), perform nightly for the hotel's clientele of colonial traders and sea captains: "Beastly life for these women!" Davidson observes (005-045).

## Role in the Novel

The orchestra serves as the mechanism by which Lena enters the novel. She is a member of the group, trapped in a degrading situation from which she sees no escape. Heyst's decision to rescue her — to spirit her away from Schomberg's Hotel to Samburan — is his second major breach of his father's philosophy (after the Morrison rescue) (010-007).

The orchestra also represents the gender dynamics of the colonial world. The women are commodities, imported like trade goods for the entertainment of expatriate men. Schomberg's particular interest in Lena, and his fury when Heyst takes her away, adds sexual predation to the orchestra's already exploitative structure.

## Key Members

- **Zangiacomo** — the operator, a German using an Italian stage name
- **Lena** — the young English member whose rescue drives the plot
- **Mrs. Zangiacomo** — Zangiacomo's wife, who assists in running the orchestra
