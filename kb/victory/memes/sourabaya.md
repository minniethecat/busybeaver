# Sourabaya

## Description

Sourabaya (modern Surabaya) is a port city on the island of Java, serving as a commercial hub in Conrad's Malay Archipelago. It is the seat of Tesman Brothers, a trading firm described as a "tip-top house" (001-013), and functions as the financial and logistical anchor for the region's colonial commerce. The Tesmans maintained their principal countinghouse there, and through it a long-standing connection existed between the firm and Axel Heyst (004-043).

Conrad associates Sourabaya with the physical toll of the tropics:

> …the little shiver of the tropics which in Sourabaya, especially, often means fever and the hospital to the newcomers.
>
> (005-002)

## Role in the Novel

Sourabaya serves as the invisible commercial backdrop to the human drama on Samburan. It is the place where business is transacted — where Morrison's bills are settled, where the Tesmans wash their hands of the failing Tropical Belt Coal Company (003-020), and where Heyst retreats for practical errands. The city never appears directly as a scene; it is invoked through dialogue and memory, always as the counterpoint to Samburan's isolation.

The city also marks the trajectory of secondary characters. Schomberg, the hotel-keeper, dragged his wife "up and down that wilderness" of the archipelago before settling "ultimately in Sourabaya" (002-055). Heyst himself travels there during the narrative, leaving Wang to clear the grounds in his absence (017-001). In his fevered state after the novel's climactic violence, Heyst murmurs the word "Sourabaya" — a name that to Lena, listening, carries "the discouragement at the outlandish sound" (009-012).
