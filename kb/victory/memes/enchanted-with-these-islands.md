# I am enchanted with these islands!

## Quotation

> "I am enchanted with these islands!"
>
> (001-009)

## Context

Heyst speaks these words in the billiard-room of a Malacca club (001-008), early in his wanderings through the Malay Archipelago. The exclamation, overheard by the narrator and other club members, is uncharacteristically effusive for the usually reserved Swede. It is this single moment of unguarded enthusiasm that earns him the enduring nickname "Enchanted Heyst" — a label that will follow him through every subsequent phase of his life in the islands.

## Significance

The epithet 'Enchanted Heyst' is one of Conrad's most effective pieces of ironic characterization. The word 'enchanted' suggests a man under a spell, disconnected from practical reality — and the novel proceeds to demonstrate exactly how dangerous that disconnection can be. Heyst's enchantment with the islands is genuine, but it is also a form of evasion. The islands enchant him precisely because they allow him to remain at a distance from the entanglements of human society. The nickname, bestowed in a moment of casual clubroom banter, becomes a prophecy: Heyst will indeed be a man enchanted — spellbound by his own philosophy of detachment — until the spell is broken by violence.
