# Capricorn

## Description

The *Capricorn* was a trading brig owned and mastered by Morrison, operating in the waters of the Dutch East Indies. It was the vessel around which Morrison's livelihood and identity revolved (002-001).

## Morrison's Attachment

Morrison's emotional connection to the *Capricorn* went beyond mere ownership. He "pined after" his ship with an affection that was compared to pining for the tropics themselves — or for a sweetheart in England. The brig represented his independence and his place in the world (003-002).

> In truth, he pined after the *Capricorn* — I don't mean only the tropic; I mean the ship too.
>
> (003-002)

## Fate

The loss of the *Capricorn* was the beginning of Morrison's downward spiral. The brig was taken from him by the Portuguese authorities in Delli on a pretence of irregular papers, leaving Morrison destitute and desperate — the circumstance that led to his fateful meeting with Heyst (002-009).
