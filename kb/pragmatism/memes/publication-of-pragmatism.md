## Overview

The publication of [[william-james|William James]]'s _[[pragmatism|Pragmatism]]: A New Name for Some Old Ways of Thinking_ in 1907 was the founding event of pragmatism as a self-conscious philosophical movement. The book originated as a series of eight lectures delivered at the Lowell Institute in Boston in November–December 1906, and at Columbia University in January 1907. These lectures — the primary source for this wiki — presented [[william-james|James]]'s mature statement of the pragmatic method and its application to problems of [[truth|truth]], [[reality|real]]ity, [[religion|religion]], and [[philosophy|philosophy]].

## Background

By 1906, [[william-james|James]] had been developing pragmatic themes for nearly three decades. His _[[principles-of-psychology|Principles of Psychology]]_ (1890) had established his reputation, and _[[the-will-to-believe|The Will to Believe]]_ (1897) had introduced his voluntarist epistemology. But pragmatism as a named, self-conscious philosophical method had been launched by [[charles-sanders-peirce|Charles Sanders Peirce]] in the 1870s and revived by [[william-james|James]] in an 1898 lecture at Berkeley. The Lowell Lectures were [[william-james|James]]'s opportunity to present a comprehensive, accessible statement of pragmatism to a broad public audience.

## Significance

The publication of _[[pragmatism|Pragmatism]]_ was immediately recognized as a major philosophical event. The book:

- **Introduced pragmatism to a mass audience**: The lectures were written in [[william-james|James]]'s characteristic vivid, accessible style, avoiding technical jargon while engaging deep philosophical issues.
- **Defined the temperament typology**: The famous distinction between tender-minded and tough-minded temperaments, and pragmatism's role as mediator, was first presented here.
- **Articulated the pragmatic theory of truth**: [[william-james|James]]'s controversial claim that truth "happens to an idea" — that ideas become true through their verification in experience — was developed in these lectures.
- **Internationalized pragmatism**: The book brought pragmatism to a global audience, catalyzing the Italian pragmatist movement ([[giovanni-papini|Papini]]) and the Oxford pragmatist school ([[fcs-schiller|Schiller]]).
- **Provoked ongoing debate**: The pragmatic theory of truth provoked immediate criticism from idealists ([[fh-bradley|Bradley]], [[josiah-royce|Royce]]) and realists ([[bertrand-russell|Russell]], [[ge-moore|Moore]]), launching a debate that continues in contemporary philosophy.

## Legacy

_Pragmatism_ remains the most widely read introduction to pragmatist philosophy. Its ideas — the pragmatic method, the temperament typology, the instrumental theory of truth, the reconciliation of science and religion — continue to influence philosophy, psychology, and the broader intellectual culture.
