## Definition

Materialism is the metaphysical doctrine that [[reality|real]]ity consists entirely of matter and its motions — that everything, including mind, consciousness, and value, can be explained in terms of material processes. In [[william-james|William James]]'s _[[pragmatism|Pragmatism]]_, materialism represents the tough-minded extreme of the philosophical temperament, the mirror image of [[idealism|idealist]] absolutism. Where [[idealism|idealism]] reduces everything to mind, materialism reduces everything to matter. [[william-james|James]] treats both as one-sided abstractions: pragmatism mediates between them by taking experience — which is neither exclusively mental nor exclusively material — as the starting point.

## Origin & Context

Materialism has ancient roots (Democritus, Epicurus, Lucretius) but gained new force in the nineteenth century with the rise of mechanistic science, [[darwinism|Darwinian]] evolution, and physiological psychology. The discovery that mental processes correlate with brain processes seemed to suggest that mind is nothing but brain — a view that [[william-james|James]] spent his career resisting.

[[william-james|James]] was trained as a physiologist and was deeply sympathetic to the scientific, empirical spirit that motivated materialism. His objection was not to empiricism but to the metaphysical leap from "mental processes correlate with brain processes" to "mental processes are nothing but brain processes." This leap, he argued, is not required by the evidence and has pernicious practical consequences.

## Key Arguments

### The Pragmatic Critique of Materialism

[[william-james|James]] applies the [[pragmatic-method|pragmatic method]]: what practical difference does materialism make?

1. **Materialism and meaning**: If mind is merely a byproduct of matter, then human purposes, values, and meanings are epiphenomenal — they make no causal difference. This view undermines the reality of moral effort.

2. **Materialism and consciousness**: Materialism cannot explain the qualitative, subjective character of [[consciousness|conscious]] experience — what it *feels like* to be conscious. This is the "hard problem" of consciousness that remains unresolved.

3. **The pragmatic alternative**: [[pragmatism|Pragmatism]] suspends the metaphysical question of what reality is "made of" and focuses on the practical consequences of ideas. From this standpoint, both material and mental descriptions can be valid in their respective contexts.

### Materialism in the Temperament Typology

In [[william-james|James]]'s classification:

| Temperament | Metaphysical Expression | Limitation |
|-------------|------------------------|-----------|
| Tender-minded | [[idealism|Idealism]] | Neglects the material conditions of experience |
| Tough-minded | Materialism | Neglects the reality of consciousness and value |
| Pragmatist | [[radical-empiricism|Radical empiricism]] | Takes "pure experience" as fundamental, neutral between mind and matter |

## Variants & Interpretations

- **Eliminative materialism**: The strong claim that mental states do not exist at all — only brain states exist. The most extreme form; rejected by James.
- **Reductive materialism**: The claim that mental states exist but are identical to brain states. Also rejected by James.
- **Non-reductive physicalism**: The claim that mental states depend on physical states but are not identical to them. Closer to James's position.
- **Pragmatic neutral monism**: James's [[radical-empiricism|radical empiricism]]: "pure experience" is the fundamental stuff, and both mind and matter are functional classifications within it.

## Criticisms & Debates

The materialism debate continues in contemporary philosophy of mind. Advances in neuroscience have strengthened the case for materialism in some respects, but the "hard problem" of [[consciousness|consciousness]] remains a significant challenge. [[william-james|James]]'s pragmatic approach — focusing on the practical consequences of different metaphysical positions rather than trying to settle them on purely theoretical grounds — offers a distinctive perspective on this ongoing debate.
