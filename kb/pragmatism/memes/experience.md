## Definition

In [[william-james|James]]'s pragmatism, experience is the continuous, fluid stream of conscious life — not a mosaic of discrete sensations but a seamless continuum in which qualities and relations are given together. Against the atomistic empiricism of [[david-hume|Hume]], [[william-james|James]] insisted that experience "was initially a continuum" (002-142), a confused whole requiring analysis rather than synthesis. Experience serves as both the source and the ultimate test of all knowledge: nothing counts as true except through its capacity to guide action within the ongoing course of experience.

## Origin & Context

The conception of experience in pragmatism emerges directly from [[william-james|James]]'s r[[darwinism|evolution]] in psychology. In his *[[principles-of-psychology|Principles of Psychology]]* (1890), [[william-james|James]] overthrew the [[david-hume|Hume]]an model that had dominated philosophy for over a century — the view that experience consisted of "a mosaic, of which the stones were the detached sensations, and their washed-out copies, the ideas" (002-030). [[david-hume|Hume]] had accepted "the practically sufficient analysis of experience which all find ready-made in language" (002-011), starting from sensation-atoms and then puzzling over how they could be connected.

> Experience was a mosaic, of which the stones were the detached sensations, and their washed-out copies, the ideas. (002-030)

[[immanuel-kant|Kant]] attempted to patch up [[david-hume|Hume]]'s analysis by adding synthetic *[[apriorism|a priori]]* machinery to the mind, treating "experience as it came through the channels of sense" as "a manifold, a whirl of separate sensations" (002-075). But the entire framing was a mistake. [[william-james|James]]'s key insight was that "this separation was secondary and artificial" (002-141) — the atomistic analysis was something thought *imposed* on experience, not something it found there.

## Key Arguments

1. **Experience is a continuum, not a mosaic**: [[william-james|James]] overturned the atomistic paradigm by arguing that experience comes as a fluid whole. Relations — temporal, spatial, causal — are perceived just as directly as the qualities they connect.

> The stream of experience no doubt contains what it is afterwards possible to single out as 'sensations,' but it presents them also as connected by 'relations.' Moreover, the 'sensations' or 'qualities' and their 'relations' exhibit the immediate indiscerptible unity of a fluid rather than a succession of flashes. (002-142)

2. **Immediate experience requires analysis, not synthesis**: The classical problem of how to "synthesize" discrete sensations into a connected world dissolves once we recognize the starting point.

> The great crux in Hume is thus seen to be illusory. Immediate experience does not require 'synthesis': it calls for 'analysis.' It is not a jigsaw puzzle, to be pieced together without glue: it is a confused whole which has to be divided and set in order for clear thinking. (002-155)

[[david-hume|Hume]]'s error was starting "from experience *as partly analysed* by common sense, and not from the flux *as given*" (002-159). He mistook the selections he had already made for the whole.

3. **All analysis is a choice driven by purpose**: The mind is not a passive receiver but an active selector, emphasizing what serves its interests and ignoring the rest.

> A mind that analyses cannot but be *active* in handling its experience. It manipulates it to serve its ends. It emphasizes only those portions of the flux which seem to it important. (002-198)

This volitional contribution is "all-pervasive in our thinking" (002-206), and once recognized as indispensable, must be accepted as legitimate. The distinction between "[[reality|real]]ities" and "illusions" depends on "the differential values we attach to the parts of the flux according as they seem important or interesting to us or not" (002-202).

4. **[[truth|Truth]] is validated in the course of experience**: [[pragmatism|Pragmatism]] makes experience the ultimate tribunal for testing all beliefs. [[truth|Truth]] is not a static property but something achieved when ideas prove their worth in guiding us through experience.

> Truth is a *value* and a satisfaction; but 'intellectual satisfaction' is not a morbid delight in dialectical and verbal juggling: it is the satisfaction which rewards the hard labour of rationalizing experience and rendering it more conformable with human desires. (005-185)

Postulates are tested through "verification in the course of fresh experience, and can always be reconstructed or abandoned, should it cease to edify" (003-071).

5. **The [[pragmatism|pragmatist]] [[knowledge|theory of knowledge]] rests on the new account of experience**: By recognizing that experience is a continuum and that the mind actively selects from it, pragmatism charts a third way between empiricism and apriorism — "a compromise, which ignores neither that which we bring to experience (like empiricism), nor that which we gain from experience (like apriorism)" (003-056).

## Variants & Interpretations

- **[[david-hume|Hume]]an atomism**: Experience as a mosaic of discrete, unconnected sensation-atoms — the view [[william-james|James]] overturned.
- **[[immanuel-kant|Kantian]] apriorism**: Experience as raw sensory manifold requiring synthetic *[[apriorism|a priori]]* forms from the mind to achieve coherence — a view [[william-james|James]] regarded as doubly removed from [[reality|real]]ity (002-178).
- **[[william-james|James]]ian [[empiricism|radical empiricism]]**: Experience as a continuous flux whose relations are as [[reality|real]] as its terms — the foundation for pragmatism's epistemology. This later developed into [[william-james|James]]'s doctrine of "[[empiricism|radical empiricism]]," which insists that nothing [[reality|real]] can be admitted except what is given in experience and its connections.

## Criticisms & Debates

Critics, particularly from the [[rationality|rational]]ist and [[absolutism|absolutist]] camps, charged that pragmatism's reliance on experience reduces truth to mere subjective satisfaction, leaving no room for stable, objective knowledge. [[william-james|James]] responded by arguing that experience itself supplies the check on wishful thinking. Experience is not infinitely malleable — "it may be that experience refuses to" confirm our [[postulation|postulates]] (003-155).

> Experience does not warrant such gratuitous demands. It confirms neither an unchanging reality behind appearances nor a cosmic purpose independent of human purposes. (008-121)

The [[absolutism|absolutist]] appeal to "the perfect consistency of the experience of an Absolute" (005-132) offers only a "test [that] is again illusory, and productive of a fictitious 'ideal' completeness of knowledge" (005-137). [[pragmatism|Pragmatism]] prefers to stay within the actual, ongoing course of human experience, where "every day brings fresh experience, and may bring fresh truth" (008-103).
