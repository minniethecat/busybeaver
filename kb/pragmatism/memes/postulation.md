## Definition

Postulation is the doctrine that the fundamental principles of knowledge — such as the laws of causation, the axioms of mathematics, and the rules of logic — are neither empirical generalizations from experience (as empiricism holds), nor innate _[[apriorism|a priori]]_ necessities of thought (as [[rationality|rational]]ism claims), but are instead freely chosen postulates. These postulates are tested and verified by their practical success in organizing the flux of experience into a coherent, predictable order.

> A postulate thus differs essentially from the '_a priori_ truth' by its dependence upon the will, by its being the product of a free choice. (003-077)

## Origin & Context

The doctrine of postulation emerges in [[william-james|James]]'s _[[pragmatism|Pragmatism]]_ as a compromise between two traditional epistemological positions: empiricism (which treats principles as generalizations from experience) and apriorism (which treats them as necessary truths of [[rationality|reason]]). [[william-james|James]] frames postulation as a "third alternative" that resolves the impasse between these two schools.

> Let us, therefore, try a compromise, which ignores neither that which we bring to experience (like empiricism), nor that which we gain from experience (like apriorism). This compromise is effected by the doctrine of postulation. (003-058)

The concept is deeply rooted in the [[darwinism|Darwinian]] intellectual climate of the late nineteenth century. Just as [[charles-darwin|Darwin]] showed that species are not fixed creations but evolving adaptations, [[william-james|James]] argues that postulates are not eternal truths but practical instruments that survive so long as they prove useful. The "fitness of a postulate to survive is being continually tested" (003-100), and the whole process has "a thoroughly _biological_ tinge" (003-099).

## Key Arguments

**Postulates are freely chosen, not given.** We do not discover first principles; we invent them as working [[hypothesis|hypotheses]] and test them against experience:

> We have always to select the assumptions upon which we mean to act in our commerce with reality. We select the rules upon which we go, and we select the 'facts' by which we claim to support our rules. (003-077)

**Postulation is a reciprocal process.** Postulates shape how we interpret facts, but facts in turn test and reshape postulates:

> Postulation and the verifying of postulates is thus a process of reciprocal discrimination and selection. The postulate once formulated, we seek in the flux for confirmations of it, and thus construct a system of 'facts' which are relative to it; if, on the other hand, this process of selection is unfruitful, and the confirmations of our rule turn out infinitesimal, we alter the rule; and thus the 'facts' in the case reject the postulate. (003-085--003-086)

**Successful postulates become axioms; failed ones become prejudices.** A postulate that consistently proves its worth is elevated:

> A long and successful course of service raises a postulate to the dignity of an 'axiom' — i.e., a principle which it is incredible anyone should think worth disputing — whereas repeated failure in application degrades it to the position of a prejudice — i.e., an _a priori_ opinion which is always belied by its consequences. (003-073)

**Causation as a paradigm case.** [[william-james|James]] uses the postulate of causation as the central example:

> We therefore postulate a right to _dissect_ the flux, to fit together selected series without reference to the rest. Thus, a systematic network of natural 'laws' is slowly knit together, and chaos visibly transforms itself into scientific order. The postulation of 'causes' is verified by its success. Moreover, it is to be noted that to this postulate there is no alternative. (003-103--003-104)

## Variants & Interpretations

- **Biological interpretation**: Postulation as intellectual [[darwinism|natural selection]] — ideas compete, and those that best enable prediction and control survive (003-099--003-101).
- **Voluntarist interpretation**: Postulation as an act of will — "thought is everywhere inspired by will" (003-101), emphasizing the active, desire-driven nature of knowledge-seeking.
- **Pragmatic interpretation**: Postulation as a tool — principles are _instruments_ for achieving harmony with our environment (003-101), not mirrors of [[reality|real]]ity.

## Criticisms & Debates

[[william-james|James]] himself acknowledges that postulation can be accused of "faking" the facts — selectively emphasizing aspects of experience that conform to our expectations while discarding the rest (003-085). However, he argues that this selectivity is not a flaw but the essential method of all [[science|scientific thinking]], and that the only alternative — treating the total universe as a single cause — would make prediction impossible and "entangle truth in a totality of things which is unique at every instant, and never can recur" (003-104).

The doctrine also faces the challenge of circularity: if postulates are chosen because they promise satisfaction, and satisfaction is measured by the success of the postulate, how do we avoid a closed loop? [[william-james|James]]'s answer is pragmatic: the process is [[darwinism|evolution]]ary and self-correcting; postulates that fail to deliver are simply abandoned.
