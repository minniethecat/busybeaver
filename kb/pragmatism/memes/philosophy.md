## Definition

Philosophy, in [[william-james|James]]'s Pragmatic understanding, is not a fixed system of abstract truths but a collective name for the evolving "philosophy of action" that seeks to interpret experience and guide conduct (001-155). It is distinguished from science by its concern with the "whole of [[reality|real]]ity" rather than particular aspects of it, yet it must remain accountable to practical consequences (001-008).

## Origin & Context

[[william-james|James]] locates his [[pragmatism|Pragmatism]] within a long tradition of philosophical inquiry, but identifies two major forces that shaped its emergence. The first is **[[darwinism|Darwinism]]**, which [[william-james|James]] calls the "fountain-head of Pragmatic philosophy" (001-099), introducing [[darwinism|evolution]]ary and functional modes of thinking. The second is the **reaction of science upon philosophy**, which demanded that philosophical concepts be tested against concrete experience rather than remaining purely speculative (001-114).

> "It is not the _pétroleuse_ of philosophy. It is not an incendiary, destroying all that has gone before. It is a mediator and reconciler." (001-051)

The publication of [[william-james|James]]'s _[[principles-of-psychology|Principles of Psychology]]_ in 1890 marked a pivotal moment, introducing the "[[psychology|new psychology]]" that would transform philosophy by grounding it in the empirical study of mental life (001-150, 001-153, 001-156).

## Key Arguments

**1. Philosophy as an evolving activity.** [[william-james|James]] argues that "shifting systems of philosophy illustrate only the changing fashions of the human mind" (001-032). Philosophy does not converge on a single truth but adapts to new evidence and circumstances, much like biological [[darwinism|evolution]].

**2. The [[pragmatism|pragmatic method]] as mediator.** Rather than overthrowing previous philosophy, [[pragmatism|Pragmatism]] seeks to reconcile competing traditions. [[william-james|James]] explicitly rejects the image of the r[[darwinism|evolution]]ary incendiary (_pétroleuse_) and presents [[pragmatism|Pragmatism]] as a "mediator and reconciler" between [[empiricism|empiricist]] and [[rationality|rational]]ist approaches (001-051).

**3. Philosophy and action.** The defining feature of Pragmatic philosophy is its orientation toward action. [[william-james|James]] uses the term "philosophy of action" to characterize the movement as a whole (001-155). A philosophical idea's meaning is found not in its logical form alone but in the practical difference it makes in human life.

**4. The [[david-hume|Hume]]an inheritance.** [[william-james|James]] acknowledges that [[david-hume|David Hume]]'s psychology "reigned absolutely in philosophy" for a period, establishing empiricism as a dominant force (002-003). However, [[david-hume|Hume]]'s approach also "entangled philosophy" in difficulties that [[pragmatism|Pragmatism]] seeks to resolve by reframing the relationship between mind and experience (002-008, 002-134).

> "Until the year 1890... the psychology of Hume reigned absolutely in philosophy. All empiricists followed him." (002-003)

## Variants & Interpretations

- **Rationalist philosophy**: Emphasizes _[[apriorism|a priori]]_ principles and logical deduction; [[william-james|James]] treats this as one pole of the philosophical temperament, contrasting it with empiricism (001-013).
- **Empiricist philosophy**: Grounded in sensory experience and [[david-hume|Hume]]'s psychological framework; [[william-james|James]] inherited this tradition but sought to move beyond its limitations (002-003, 002-008).
- **Pragmatic philosophy**: [[william-james|James]]'s own position — philosophy must be judged by its practical consequences, not its logical pedigree. It mediates between [[rationality|rational]]ism and empiricism (001-014, 001-051).

## Criticisms & Debates

One recurring criticism of Pragmatic philosophy is that it reduces philosophical inquiry to mere utility, abandoning the search for timeless truth. [[william-james|James]] addresses this by insisting that [[pragmatism|Pragmatism]] is "too superficial" only if one ignores the depth of its engagement with lived experience (001-008). The charge that [[pragmatism|Pragmatism]] is merely a "philosophy of action" that neglects contemplation is countered by its insistence that action and thought are inseparable (001-155).

The unresolved tension between philosophy's aspiration to universality and its historical contingency — acknowledged in [[william-james|James]]'s observation that systems "illustrate only the changing fashions" (001-032) — remains a central debate in metaphilosophy.
