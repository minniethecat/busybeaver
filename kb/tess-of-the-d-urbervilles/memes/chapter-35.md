[035-001]Her narrative ended; even its re-assertions and secondary explanations were

[035-002]done. Tess’s voice throughout had hardly risen higher than its opening

[035-003]tone; there had been no exculpatory phrase of any kind, and she had not wept.

[035-004]But the complexion even of external things seemed to suffer transmutation as

[035-005]her announcement progressed. The fire in the grate looked

[035-006]impish—demoniacally funny, as if it did not care in the least about her

[035-007]strait. The fender grinned idly, as if it too did not care. The light from the

[035-008]water-bottle was merely engaged in a chromatic problem. All material objects

[035-010]nothing had changed since the moments when he had been kissing her; or rather,

[035-011]nothing in the substance of things. But the essence of things had changed.

[035-012]When she ceased, the auricular impressions from their previous endearments

[035-013]seemed to hustle away into the corner of their brains, repeating themselves as

[035-014]echoes from a time of supremely purblind foolishness.

[035-015]Clare performed the irrelevant act of stirring the fire; the intelligence had

[035-029]senses: “Why didn’t you tell me before? Ah, yes, you would have

[035-030]told me, in a way—but I hindered you, I remember!”

[035-036]“In the name of our love, forgive me!” she whispered with a dry

[035-037]mouth. “I have forgiven you for the same!”

[035-040]Angel.”

[035-042]“But you do not forgive me?”

[035-056]That’s what I have felt, Angel!”

[035-063]“I repeat, the woman I have been loving is not you.”
