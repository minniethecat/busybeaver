## Role in the Novel

Felix Clare is the middle brother of the Clare family, serving as a curate in a town adjoining Emminster. Together with his brother Cuthbert Clare, he represents the rigid Evangelical tradition from which [[Angel Clare]] has broken away. In Angel's estimation, "Felix seemed to him all Church"—a man whose mental horizons had narrowed to fit his ecclesiastical office. (025-131) (025-205)

## Biography

Felix is the son of [[Reverend James Clare]] and Mrs Clare of Emminster. Though "an offshoot from a family of clergymen," Hardy observes that he displayed "growing mental limitations" characteristic of a mind confined too long within a single narrow tradition. (025-212)

When Angel returns home to announce his marriage, Felix's response typifies the family's Evangelical disapproval. Felix voices concern about Angel's future with a farmer's daughter, and when Angel mentions that Tess is knowledgeable in Greek and Latin, both Felix and Cuthbert exclaim in disbelief. Angel ultimately confides nothing of his true situation to either brother. (025-228) (026-128)

## Key Relationships

- [[Angel Clare]] — Younger brother, whose unconventional choices he disapproves of
- [[Reverend James Clare]] — Father, whose ministry he follows
- [[Cuthbert Clare]] — Elder brother, with whom he shares the family's Evangelical outlook
