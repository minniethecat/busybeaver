[014-001]It was a hazy sunrise in August. The denser nocturnal vapours, attacked by the

[014-002]warm beams, were dividing and shrinking into isolated fleeces within hollows

[014-003]and coverts, where they waited till they should be dried away to nothing.

[014-004]The sun, on account of the mist, had a curious sentient, personal look,

[014-005]demanding the masculine pronoun for its adequate expression. His present

[014-006]aspect, coupled with the lack of all human forms in the scene, explained the

[014-007]old-time heliolatries in a moment. One could feel that a saner religion had

[014-008]never prevailed under the sky. The luminary was a golden-haired, beaming,

[014-009]mild-eyed, God-like creature, gazing down in the vigour and intentness of youth

[014-010]upon an earth that was brimming with interest for him.

[014-011]His light, a little later, broke though chinks of cottage shutters, throwing

[014-012]stripes like red-hot pokers upon cupboards, chests of drawers, and other

[014-013]furniture within; and awakening harvesters who were not already astir.

[014-014]But of all ruddy things that morning the brightest were two broad arms of

[014-015]painted wood, which rose from the margin of yellow cornfield hard by Marlott

[014-016]village. They, with two others below, formed the revolving Maltese cross of the

[014-017]reaping-machine, which had been brought to the field on the previous evening to

[014-018]be ready for operations this day. The paint with which they were smeared,

[014-019]intensified in hue by the sunlight, imparted to them a look of having been

[014-020]dipped in liquid fire.

[014-021]The field had already been “opened”; that is to say, a lane a few

[014-022]feet wide had been hand-cut through the wheat along the whole circumference of

[014-023]the field for the first passage of the horses and machine.

[014-024]Two groups, one of men and lads, the other of women, had come down the lane

[014-025]just at the hour when the shadows of the eastern hedge-top struck the west

[014-026]hedge midway, so that the heads of the groups were enjoying sunrise while their

[014-027]feet were still in the dawn. They disappeared from the lane between the two

[014-028]stone posts which flanked the nearest field-gate.

[014-030]grasshopper. The machine had begun, and a moving concatenation of three horses

[014-036]equable pace; the glistening brass star in the forehead of the fore horse first

[014-038]and then the whole machine.
