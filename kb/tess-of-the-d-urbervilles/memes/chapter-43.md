[043-001]There was no exaggeration in Marian’s definition of Flintcomb-Ash farm as

[043-002]a starve-acre place. The single fat thing on the soil was Marian herself; and

[043-003]she was an importation. Of the three classes of village, the village cared for

[043-004]by its lord, the village cared for by itself, and the village uncared for

[043-005]either by itself or by its lord (in other words, the village of a resident

[043-006]squire’s tenantry, the village of free or copy-holders, and the

[043-007]absentee-owner’s village, farmed with the land) this place,

[043-008]Flintcomb-Ash, was the third.

[043-009]But Tess set to work. Patience, that blending of moral courage with physical

[043-010]timidity, was now no longer a minor feature in Mrs Angel Clare; and it

[043-011]sustained her.

[043-012]The swede-field in which she and her companion were set hacking was a stretch

[043-013]of a hundred odd acres in one patch, on the highest ground of the farm, rising

[043-014]above stony lanchets or lynchets—the outcrop of siliceous veins in the

[043-015]chalk formation, composed of myriads of loose white flints in bulbous, cusped,

[043-016]and phallic shapes. The upper half of each turnip had been eaten off by the

[043-017]live-stock, and it was the business of the two women to grub up the lower or

[043-018]earthy half of the root with a hooked fork called a hacker, that it might be

[043-019]eaten also. Every leaf of the vegetable having already been consumed, the whole

[043-020]field was in colour a desolate drab; it was a complexion without features, as

[043-021]if a face, from chin to brow, should be only an expanse of skin. The sky wore,

[043-022]in another colour, the same likeness; a white vacuity of countenance with the

[043-023]lineaments gone. So these two upper and nether visages confronted each other

[043-024]all day long, the white face looking down on the brown face, and the brown face

[043-025]looking up at the white face, without anything standing between them but the

[043-026]two girls crawling over the surface of the former like flies.

[043-027]Nobody came near them, and their movements showed a mechanical regularity;

[043-028]their forms standing enshrouded in Hessian “wroppers”—sleeved

[043-029]brown pinafores, tied behind to the bottom, to keep their gowns from blowing

[043-030]about—scant skirts revealing boots that reached high up the ankles, and

[043-031]yellow sheepskin gloves with gauntlets. The pensive character which the

[043-032]curtained hood lent to their bent heads would have reminded the observer of

[043-033]some early Italian conception of the two Marys.

[043-035]the landscape, not thinking of the justice or injustice of their lot. Even in

[043-036]such a position as theirs it was possible to exist in a dream. In the afternoon

[043-038]if they did not work they would not be paid; so they worked on. It was so high
