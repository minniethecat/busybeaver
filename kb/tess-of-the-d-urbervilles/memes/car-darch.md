## Role in the Novel

Car Darch, known by the nickname "Queen of Spades," is one of the rural working women who populate the margins of Tess's story. She was until lately "a favourite of" [[Alec d'Urberville]] at Trantridge, and her earthy boldness stands in stark contrast to Tess's refined vulnerability. (010-154)

## Key Scenes

Car first appears during the journey from Trantridge to the market town of Chaseborough, where she serves as the "leading pedestrian" of the group, carrying a basket on her head with an air of brazen assurance. During the journey, one of her companions teases her crudely: "What's that a-creeping down thy back, Car Darch?"—a remark about something dripping from her basket. (010-171) (010-176)

Later that night, amid the chaos and dust of the return journey from Chaseborough, the Queen of Spades "forgot the stain on her bodice" as the group descended into drunken disorder. (010-254)

By the time Tess reaches Flintcomb-Ash, Car has reappeared there as a field worker, now joined by her junior counterpart, the Queen of Diamonds—her younger sister Nancy Darch. The two sisters represent the fallen women whom Alec has cast aside, serving as a cautionary mirror to Tess's own situation. (043-188)
