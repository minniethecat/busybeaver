[020-001]The season developed and matured. Another year’s instalment of flowers,

[020-002]leaves, nightingales, thrushes, finches, and such ephemeral creatures, took up

[020-003]their positions where only a year ago others had stood in their place when

[020-004]these were nothing more than germs and inorganic particles. Rays from the

[020-005]sunrise drew forth the buds and stretched them into long stalks, lifted up sap

[020-006]in noiseless streams, opened petals, and sucked out scents in invisible jets

[020-007]and breathings.

[020-008]Dairyman Crick’s household of maids and men lived on comfortably,

[020-009]placidly, even merrily. Their position was perhaps the happiest of all

[020-010]positions in the social scale, being above the line at which neediness ends,

[020-011]and below the line at which the *convenances* begin to cramp natural

[020-012]feelings, and the stress of threadbare modishness makes too little of enough.

[020-013]Thus passed the leafy time when arborescence seems to be the one thing aimed at

[020-014]out of doors. Tess and Clare unconsciously studied each other, ever balanced on

[020-015]the edge of a passion, yet apparently keeping out of it. All the while they

[020-016]were converging, under an irresistible law, as surely as two streams in one

[020-017]vale.

[020-018]Tess had never in her recent life been so happy as she was now, possibly never

[020-019]would be so happy again. She was, for one thing, physically and mentally suited

[020-020]among these new surroundings. The sapling which had rooted down to a poisonous

[020-021]stratum on the spot of its sowing had been transplanted to a deeper soil.

[020-022]Moreover she, and Clare also, stood as yet on the debatable land between

[020-023]predilection and love; where no profundities have been reached; no reflections

[020-024]have set in, awkwardly inquiring, “Whither does this new current tend to

[020-025]carry me? What does it mean to my future? How does it stand towards my

[020-026]past?”

[020-027]Tess was the merest stray phenomenon to Angel Clare as yet—a rosy,

[020-028]warming apparition which had only just acquired the attribute of persistence in

[020-029]his consciousness. So he allowed his mind to be occupied with her, deeming his

[020-030]preoccupation to be no more than a philosopher’s regard of an exceedingly

[020-031]novel, fresh, and interesting specimen of womankind.

[020-032]They met continually; they could not help it. They met daily in that strange

[020-033]and solemn interval, the twilight of the morning, in the violet or pink dawn;

[020-034]for it was necessary to rise early, so very early, here. Milking was done

[020-035]betimes; and before the milking came the skimming, which began at a little past

[020-036]three. It usually fell to the lot of some one or other of them to wake the

[020-037]rest, the first being aroused by an alarm-clock; and, as Tess was the latest

[020-038]arrival, and they soon discovered that she could be depended upon not to sleep

[020-039]through the alarm as others did, this task was thrust most frequently upon her.

[020-040]No sooner had the hour of three struck and whizzed, than she left her room and

[020-041]ran to the dairyman’s door; then up the ladder to Angel’s, calling

[020-042]him in a loud whisper; then woke her fellow-milkmaids. By the time that Tess

[020-043]was dressed Clare was downstairs and out in the humid air. The remaining maids

[020-044]and the dairyman usually gave themselves another turn on the pillow, and did

[020-045]not appear till a quarter of an hour later.

[020-046]The gray half-tones of daybreak are not the gray half-tones of the day’s

[020-047]close, though the degree of their shade may be the same. In the twilight of the

[020-048]morning, light seems active, darkness passive; in the twilight of evening it is

[020-049]the darkness which is active and crescent, and the light which is the drowsy

[020-050]reverse.

[020-051]Being so often—possibly not always by chance—the first two persons

[020-052]to get up at the dairy-house, they seemed to themselves the first persons up of

[020-053]all the world. In these early days of her residence here Tess did not skim, but

[020-054]went out of doors at once after rising, where he was generally awaiting her.

[020-055]The spectral, half-compounded, aqueous light which pervaded the open mead

[020-056]impressed them with a feeling of isolation, as if they were Adam and Eve. At

[020-057]this dim inceptive stage of the day Tess seemed to Clare to exhibit a dignified

[020-058]largeness both of disposition and physique, an almost regnant power, possibly

[020-059]because he knew that at that preternatural time hardly any woman so well

[020-060]endowed in person as she was likely to be walking in the open air within the

[020-061]boundaries of his horizon; very few in all England. Fair women are usually

[020-062]asleep at mid-summer dawns. She was close at hand, and the rest were nowhere.

[020-063]The mixed, singular, luminous gloom in which they walked along together to the

[020-064]spot where the cows lay often made him think of the Resurrection hour. He

[020-065]little thought that the Magdalen might be at his side. Whilst all the landscape

[020-066]was in neutral shade his companion’s face, which was the focus of his

[020-067]eyes, rising above the mist stratum, seemed to have a sort of phosphorescence

[020-068]upon it. She looked ghostly, as if she were merely a soul at large. In reality

[020-069]her face, without appearing to do so, had caught the cold gleam of day from the

[020-070]north-east; his own face, though he did not think of it, wore the same aspect

[020-071]to her.

[020-072]It was then, as has been said, that she impressed him most deeply. She was no

[020-073]longer the milkmaid, but a visionary essence of woman—a whole sex

[020-074]condensed into one typical form. He called her Artemis, Demeter, and other

[020-075]fanciful names half teasingly, which she did not like because she did not

[020-076]understand them.

[020-077]“Call me Tess,” she would say askance; and he did.

[020-078]Then it would grow lighter, and her features would become simply feminine; they

[020-079]had changed from those of a divinity who could confer bliss to those of a being

[020-080]who craved it.

[020-081]At these non-human hours they could get quite close to the waterfowl. Herons

[020-082]came, with a great bold noise as of opening doors and shutters, out of the

[020-083]boughs of a plantation which they frequented at the side of the mead; or, if

[020-084]already on the spot, hardily maintained their standing in the water as the pair

[020-085]walked by, watching them by moving their heads round in a slow, horizontal,

[020-086]passionless wheel, like the turn of puppets by clockwork.

[020-087]They could then see the faint summer fogs in layers, woolly, level, and

[020-088]apparently no thicker than counterpanes, spread about the meadows in detached

[020-089]remnants of small extent. On the gray moisture of the grass were marks where

[020-090]the cows had lain through the night—dark-green islands of dry herbage the

[020-091]size of their carcasses, in the general sea of dew. From each island proceeded

[020-092]a serpentine trail, by which the cow had rambled away to feed after getting up,

[020-093]at the end of which trail they found her; the snoring puff from her nostrils,

[020-094]when she recognized them, making an intenser little fog of her own amid the

[020-095]prevailing one. Then they drove the animals back to the barton, or sat down to

[020-096]milk them on the spot, as the case might require.

[020-097]Or perhaps the summer fog was more general, and the meadows lay like a white

[020-098]sea, out of which the scattered trees rose like dangerous rocks. Birds would

[020-099]soar through it into the upper radiance, and hang on the wing sunning

[020-100]themselves, or alight on the wet rails subdividing the mead, which now shone

[020-101]like glass rods. Minute diamonds of moisture from the mist hung, too, upon

[020-102]Tess’s eyelashes, and drops upon her hair, like seed pearls. When the day

[020-103]grew quite strong and commonplace these dried off her; moreover, Tess then lost

[020-104]her strange and ethereal beauty; her teeth, lips, and eyes scintillated in the

[020-105]sunbeams and she was again the dazzlingly fair dairymaid only, who had to hold

[020-106]her own against the other women of the world.

[020-107]About this time they would hear Dairyman Crick’s voice, lecturing the

[020-108]non-resident milkers for arriving late, and speaking sharply to old Deborah

[020-109]Fyander for not washing her hands.

[020-110]“For Heaven’s sake, pop thy hands under the pump, Deb! Upon my

[020-111]soul, if the London folk only knowed of thee and thy slovenly ways,

[020-112]they’d swaller their milk and butter more mincing than they do

[020-113]a’ready; and that’s saying a good deal.”

[020-114]The milking progressed, till towards the end Tess and Clare, in common with the

[020-115]rest, could hear the heavy breakfast table dragged out from the wall in the

[020-116]kitchen by Mrs Crick, this being the invariable preliminary to each meal; the

[020-117]same horrible scrape accompanying its return journey when the table had been

[020-118]cleared.
