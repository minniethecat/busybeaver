## Role in the Novel

Mrs Brooks is the proprietress of The Herons, a fashionable boarding-house in the seaside resort of Sandbourne. Her role, though confined to a single chapter, is pivotal: she is the sole witness to the events leading to Alec d'Urberville's death, overhearing the confrontation between [[Tess Durbeyfield]] and [[Alec d'Urberville]] from her position below stairs. (056-001) (056-011)

## Key Scenes

Mrs Brooks first notices something amiss when Tess re-enters her upstairs room. From her vantage point she observes Tess's agitation and hears sounds of a quarrel. (056-016) (056-042)

When she sees a red stain spreading on the ceiling, Mrs Brooks is seized by qualms of misgiving. She eventually opens the door to find Alec d'Urberville dead, stabbed through the heart, and raises the alarm. (056-094) (056-103)

## Key Relationships

- [[Alec d'Urberville]] — The paying guest in her finest apartments, whose murder she discovers
- [[Tess Durbeyfield]] — The young woman she observes with growing unease
