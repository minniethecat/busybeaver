## Role in the Novel

Cuthbert Clare is the elder of [[Angel Clare]]'s two brothers and, like his father [[Reverend James Clare]], a clergyman. A classical scholar by training, Cuthbert represents the established Church tradition that Angel—who prefers the open air and practical farming—deliberately distances himself from. In Angel's reckoning, "Cuthbert all Church" contrasted with his own broader outlook. (025-132) (025-205)

## Biography

Cuthbert is the son of Reverend James Clare of Emminster and brother to Angel and Felix Clare. He takes holy orders and settles into a clerical living. Though he partakes of the family's narrow Evangelical worldview, Hardy notes that Cuthbert was "upon the whole, the more liberal-minded" of the two elder brothers, possessing a somewhat broader grasp of ideas than Felix. (025-216)

When Angel returns from Brazil, he learns that Cuthbert has proposed to and been accepted by [[Mercy Chant]], the very woman their parents had once hoped Angel would marry—a neat resolution that underscores the Clare family's tightly ordered world. (057-008)
