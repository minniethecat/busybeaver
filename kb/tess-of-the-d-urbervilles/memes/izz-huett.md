# Izz Huett

## Role in the Novel

Izz Huett is one of the four dairy maids at [[Talbothays Dairy]], described as "the pale girl with dark damp hair." (021-124) She is quieter and more introspective than the jolly [[Marian]] or the fragile [[Retty Priddle]], harboring a deep, unspoken passion for [[Angel Clare]].

Izz plays a significant role late in the novel when [[angel-clare|Angel Clare]], returning from Brazil in a state of remorse, briefly considers taking her with him instead of seeking [[Tess Durbeyfield]]. (053-098) Her honest admission that nobody could love him more than [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]] dissuades him and directs him back toward his wife.

## Biography

Izz Huett works alongside [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]], [[marian|Marian]], and [[retty-priddle|Retty Priddle]] at [[dairyman-crick|Dairyman Crick]]'s [[talbothays-dairy|Talbothays Dairy]]. (021-005) She is depicted as sensitive and sincere — when confronted with [[angel-clare|Angel]]'s hypothetical question about accompanying him, she confesses her love but also her conviction that [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]]'s love is greater. (053-104)

Her emotional blush reveals her feelings more clearly than she would like. (021-134) Despite her feelings for [[angel-clare|Angel]], she remains a loyal friend to [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]], never acting on her own desires. (021-160)

## Key Relationships

- **[[Tess Durbeyfield]]** — fellow milkmaid and friend
- **[[Marian]]** — fellow milkmaid, the eldest and most outspoken
- **[[Retty Priddle]]** — fellow milkmaid, the youngest
- **[[Angel Clare]]** — the object of her unrequited affection

## Key Scenes

- **The Dairy Maid Confessions** (021-121–021-172): Izz joins the other maids in admitting her feelings for [[angel-clare|Angel Clare]].
- **The Nighttime Conversation** (023-205): Izz's voice is heard in the darkness as the maids discuss [[angel-clare|Angel]].
- **[[angel-clare|Angel]]'s Return** (053-098–053-104): [[angel-clare|Angel Clare]] asks Izz if she would go with him; her truthful answer about [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]]'s greater love redirects his path.

## Analysis

Izz Huett functions as a foil to [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]] — she shares the same unrequited devotion to [[angel-clare|Angel Clare]] but lacks [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]]'s exceptional qualities that make [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]]'s tragedy so profound. Her final act of honesty, telling [[angel-clare|Angel]] that [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]]'s love surpasses her own, elevates her from a mere rival to a figure of moral clarity.
