## Role in the Novel

Jonathan Kail is one of the dairymen working under [[Dairyman Crick]] at Talbothays Dairy. He appears in the communal life of the dairy, joining the other workers at meals and in the milking parlour. His presence contributes to the richly textured portrait of rural working life that forms the backdrop to Tess and Angel's courtship. (017-090) (021-006)

## Key Scenes

Among the dairy folk, Jonathan Kail is noted for his blunt speech. When the conversation turns to Angel Clare, Kail remarks pointedly that "he's rotten as touchwood," voicing a rough-hewn scepticism about the gentleman's intentions. (021-020)

Later, during the period of Tess's anguish after her marriage, his manner takes on a more sombre cast. There was "something subdued in Jonathan Kail's tone which had not been there before," and Tess hears his "heavy footsteps up and down" the dairy, a sound that underscores the gravity of the changing season. (034-179) (034-226)

## Key Relationships

- [[Angel Clare]] — The gentleman dairyman of whom he is openly critical
