[033-001]Angel felt that he would like to spend a day with her before the wedding,

[033-002]somewhere away from the dairy, as a last jaunt in her company while there were

[033-003]yet mere lover and mistress; a romantic day, in circumstances that would never

[033-004]be repeated; with that other and greater day beaming close ahead of them.

[033-005]During the preceding week, therefore, he suggested making a few purchases in

[033-006]the nearest town, and they started together.

[033-007]Clare’s life at the dairy had been that of a recluse in respect the world

[033-008]of his own class. For months he had never gone near a town, and, requiring no

[033-009]vehicle, had never kept one, hiring the dairyman’s cob or gig if he rode

[033-010]or drove. They went in the gig that day.

[033-012]concern. It was Christmas Eve, with its loads a holly and mistletoe, and the

[033-013]town was very full of strangers who had come in from all parts of the country

[033-015]superadded to beauty on her countenance by being much stared at as she moved

[033-016]amid them on his arm.
