## Character Overview

Farmer Groby is the proprietor of [[flintcomb-ash|Flintcomb-Ash]], the bleak upland farm where **[[Tess Durbeyfield]]** works during the winter following her abandonment by [[angel-clare|Angel Clare]]. Unlike the genial **[[Dairyman Crick]]** of [[talbothays-dairy|Talbothays]], Groby is a "hard taskmaster" (046-027) who treats his workers as instruments of production. His farm, situated on a barren chalk plateau, represents the industrialized agriculture that Hardy saw as destroying the traditional rhythms of rural life.

## The Flintcomb-Ash Regime

Groby's management style is defined by relentless efficiency and personal cruelty. He "had insisted upon their being on the spot at the word of command" (047-012), treating the field workers — mostly women — with the same impersonal authority he would apply to machinery. The workers call him "he who is called Farmer Groby" (047-050), a circumlocution that suggests both fear and contempt.

His specific cruelty toward [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]] is marked: "Groby gave as his motive in selecting [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]] that she was too proud" (047-083). He assigns her the most physically demanding tasks — turning the threshing machine, feeding the steam-engine — not because she is the strongest worker but specifically to humble her. This personal animus transforms Groby from a merely harsh employer into an agent of [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]]'s degradation.

## The Threshing Scene

The threshing sequence at [[flintcomb-ash|Flintcomb-Ash]] represents the novel's most concentrated depiction of agricultural labor under industrial conditions. Groby oversees the steam-powered threshing machine with "a semi-clerical expression of certitude" (046-027), his face set in the mechanical rhythm of the work. When [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]] and her fellow workers pause from exhaustion, Groby appears on horseback: "To [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]]'s surprise Farmer Groby came up" (048-061), enforcing the unremitting pace.

The scene functions as an indictment of the new agricultural economy: the steam engine, the wage-relationship, and the boss's surveillance combine to strip labor of all dignity, turning human beings into mere components of a productive system.

## Thematic Contrast with Dairyman Crick

Hardy constructs Groby as the structural opposite of [[dairyman-crick|Dairyman Crick]]. The comparison is explicit and systematic:

| [[talbothays-dairy|Talbothays]] (Crick) | [[flintcomb-ash|Flintcomb-Ash]] (Groby) |
|---|---|
| Valley of the Great Dairies | Barren chalk uplands |
| Communal meals, shared stories | Isolated labor, mechanical rhythm |
| Paternal authority | Impersonal taskmaster |
| Natural rhythms of milking | Steam-driven threshing machine |
| [[love-and-desire|Love]] flourishes ([[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]] + [[angel-clare|Angel]]) | Spirit is crushed |

This contrast maps onto Hardy's larger critique of the transition from traditional to industrial agriculture, from Gemeinschaft to Gesellschaft, from organic community to mechanical society.

## Disappearance

Groby vanishes from the narrative after [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]] and [[marian|Marian]] leave [[flintcomb-ash|Flintcomb-Ash]] "almost without notice, leaving Groby to prosecute them if he chose" (052-062). His abrupt narrative disappearance — he is never mentioned again — reflects his function in the novel: he is less a developed character than an embodiment of a social force, the "hard taskmaster" whose cruelty is systemic rather than personal.

## Thematic Significance

Groby represents the economic reality that underlies [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]]'s tragedy. Her "fall" is not merely sexual and moral but economic: the loss of [[angel-clare|Angel]]'s protection forces her into the labor market, where employers like Groby exploit her vulnerability. Hardy's critique extends beyond individual cruelty to the system that makes such cruelty possible.

The [[flintcomb-ash|Flintcomb-Ash]] chapters, with Groby as their presiding spirit, constitute the novel's most sustained piece of social documentation — a damning portrait of women's agricultural labor in late-Victorian England.
