[004-001]Rolliver’s inn, the single alehouse at this end of the long and broken

[004-002]village, could only boast of an off-licence; hence, as nobody could legally

[004-003]drink on the premises, the amount of overt accommodation for consumers was

[004-004]strictly limited to a little board about six inches wide and two yards long,

[004-007]drank, and threw the dregs on the dusty ground to the pattern of Polynesia, and

[004-008]wished they could have a restful seat inside.

[004-009]Thus the strangers. But there were also local customers who felt the same wish;

[004-010]and where there’s a will there’s a way.

[004-017]unavailable for dwellers at this end; but the far more serious question, the
