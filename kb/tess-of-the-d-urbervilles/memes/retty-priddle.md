# Retty Priddle

## Role in the Novel

Retty Priddle is the youngest and most fragile of the four dairy maids at [[Talbothays Dairy]]. (021-005) She is described as delicate and sensitive, contrasting with the robust [[Marian]] and the composed [[Izz Huett]].

Retty's infatuation with [[Angel Clare]] is the most intense and self-destructive among the dairy maids. (021-123) After [[angel-clare|Angel]] marries [[Tess Durbeyfield]], Retty attempts to drown herself — an act that underscores the emotional devastation [[angel-clare|Angel]] unwittingly leaves in his wake. (034-192)

## Biography

Retty works as a milkmaid at [[talbothays-dairy|Talbothays]] alongside [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]], [[marian|Marian]], and [[izz-huett|Izz]]. She is introduced as part of the quartet of young women who are all in love with [[angel-clare|Angel Clare]]. (021-005) Her delicate constitution and emotional vulnerability set her apart from the other maids.

After [[angel-clare|Angel Clare]]'s departure with [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]] following their marriage, Retty's despair drives her to attempt suicide by drowning. (034-192) The event is reported to [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]] and [[angel-clare|Angel]] at [[wellbridge|Wellbridge]], casting a shadow over the early days of their marriage.

## Key Relationships

- **[[Angel Clare]]** — the object of her intense, unrequited love
- **[[Tess Durbeyfield]]** — fellow milkmaid who ultimately wins [[angel-clare|Angel]]'s affection
- **[[Marian]]** — the eldest milkmaid and a protective figure
- **[[Izz Huett]]** — fellow milkmaid, also in love with [[angel-clare|Angel]]

## Key Scenes

- **Introduction at [[talbothays-dairy|Talbothays]]** (021-005): Retty is introduced alongside the other dairy maids.
- **[[the-confession|The Confession]] Scene** (021-121–021-173): Retty's unspoken anguish is evident as the maids discuss their feelings for [[angel-clare|Angel]]. (021-123)
- **The Suicide Attempt** (034-192): [[dairyman-crick|Dairyman Crick]] reports that "poor little Retty Priddle hev tried to drown herself" upon learning of [[angel-clare|Angel]]'s marriage to [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]].

## Analysis

Retty Priddle represents the extreme emotional cost of the romantic ideal that [[angel-clare|Angel Clare]] embodies. Her suicide attempt — the most drastic action taken by any of the dairy maids — highlights the destructive power of unrequited love and the vulnerability of young women in Hardy's rural world. She serves as a darker foil to [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]], demonstrating what [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]] might have become had she lacked [[tess-durbeyfield|Tess]]'s resilience.
