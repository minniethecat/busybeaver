[046-001]Several days had passed since her futile journey, and Tess was afield. The dry

[046-002]winter wind still blew, but a screen of thatched hurdles erected in the eye of

[046-003]the blast kept its force away from her. On the sheltered side was a

[046-004]turnip-slicing machine, whose bright blue hue of new paint seemed almost vocal

[046-006]“grave”, in which the roots had been preserved since early winter.

[046-012]the choppings of the hook in Tess’s leather-gloved hand.
