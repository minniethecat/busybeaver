## Overview

The **Great Spirit** (also called the **Great Mystery** or **_Wakan Tanka_** in the Sioux language) is the supreme spiritual power in Sioux cosmology. All ceremonies, prayers, and offerings are directed to the Great Spirit, who governs life, death, health, and the natural world. Unlike the personal gods of some traditions, the Great Spirit is understood as a pervasive, mysterious force that animates all creation.

## In the Stories

### Blessing Daily Work

Sioux women would invoke the Great Spirit in their daily labor. One woman "asks the Great Spirit to bless her work, that she may have a good yield" (018-014). Going to a hilltop "is asking the Great Spirit to give summer rain and sunshine" (018-017) — showing how directly the people connected agricultural success to spiritual favor.

### Driving Out Evil

The Great Spirit's power was invoked against malevolent forces. When an evil spirit possessed a young man, his grandmother "made a prayer to the Great Spirit to drive out the evil spirit" (025-026) and after applying her medicine, the affliction passed (025-027). This demonstrates the Great Spirit's role as protector against darkness.

### Sacred Places

Certain locations were believed to be dwelling places of spiritual power. One site was described as "the abode of some Great Spirit" (027-033), where travelers showed special reverence. Such places were treated as holy ground, set apart for communion with the divine.

### Cries for Mercy

In moments of deepest suffering, the Sioux cried directly to the Great Spirit. A man in anguish "cried: 'Great Spirit, relieve my suffering brother'" (029-062), showing the personal, immediate relationship believers had with this power. The Great Spirit was not distant — he was called upon in crisis.

## Cultural Significance

- The Great Spirit is the ultimate recipient of all Sioux prayer and ceremony
- Unlike lesser spirits, the Great Spirit governs all aspects of existence
- Sacred places (hills, unusual rock formations) were considered abodes of the Great Spirit
- Prayers to the Great Spirit cover: healing, protection, agricultural blessing, and deliverance from evil
- The concept parallels other Plains tribes' supreme being beliefs (Wakan Tanka / Gitche Manitou)
