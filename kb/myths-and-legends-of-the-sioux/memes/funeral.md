## Overview

**Funeral** customs in Sioux tradition, as depicted in the tales, centered on scaffold burial — the body was wrapped in fine robes and blankets and laid on an elevated platform of forked posts and poles, five to seven feet above the ground (033-009–033-014). After the funeral, the family gave away all the deceased's belongings — horses, robes, blankets — cut their hair close to the head, and dressed in the poorest clothing as signs of mourning (033-015–033-018). Mourning typically lasted a year or more, with relatives urging the bereaved to eventually set aside their grief and rejoin community life (033-019–033-023).
