[005-001]The little rabbit lived with his old grandmother, who needed a new

[005-002]dress. “I will go out and trap a deer or an elk for you,” he said. “Then

[005-003]you shall have a new dress.”

[005-004]When he went out hunting he laid down his bow in the path while he

[005-005]looked at his snares. An elk coming by saw the bow.

[005-006]“I will play a joke on the rabbit,” said the elk to himself. “I will

[005-007]make him think I have been caught in his bow string.” He then put one

[005-008]foot on the string and lay down as if dead.

[005-009]By and by the rabbit returned. When he saw the elk he was filled with

[005-010]joy and ran home crying: “Grandmother, I have trapped a fine elk. You

[005-011]shall have a new dress from his skin. Throw the old one in the fire!”

[005-012]This the old grandmother did.

[005-013]The elk now sprang to his feet laughing. “Ho, friend rabbit,” he called,

[005-014]“You thought to trap me; now I have mocked you.” And he ran away into

[005-015]the thicket.

[005-016]The rabbit who had come back to skin the elk now ran home again.

[005-017]“Grandmother, don’t throw your dress in the fire,” he cried. But it was

[005-018]too late. The old dress was burned.
