## Overview

**Hake** (meaning "last born" in the Sioux language) is the orphan protagonist of "Story of the Two Young Friends." Raised by his destitute grandmother and shunned by well-dressed boys, Hake is chosen by [[chaske]] as his lifelong friend based on character rather than status. Through this friendship, Hake rises from poverty to become a respected warrior, wins the love of Pretty Feather, and proves his bravery on the warpath — demonstrating that worth is measured by deeds, not birth.

## In the Stories

### Story of the Two Young Friends (030-001)

#### From Orphan to Chosen Friend

Hake was the poorest boy in the village — an orphan living with his old grandmother, dressed so poorly that other boys tormented him and refused his company. His fortunes changed when Chaske, the son of the great war chief, studied the dispositions of all the village boys and chose Hake as his Koda (friend):

> "It was a lucky day for Hake when he was chosen for the friend and companion of Chaske. The orphan boy was taken to the lodge of his friend's parents and dressed up in fine clothes and moccasins." (030-019–030-021)

Hake and Chaske became inseparable — playing, hunting, trapping, and growing into fine young warriors together.

#### Love and the Warpath

When the two friends came of age to marry, Hake unexpectedly won the heart of Pretty Feather, the beautiful daughter of the [[medicine-man]] — the very woman his friend Chaske loved. Honoring the warrior tradition, Hake declared he would prove himself before marrying:

> "Hake said, 'I will do as you say, my friend, but before I can marry the medicine man's daughter, I will have to go on the warpath and do some brave deed, and will start in ten days.'" (030-097–030-098)

#### Hospitality to the Spirit

At Ghost Creek, when a wandering spirit appeared in their camp, it was Hake who first showed hospitality to the restless ghost:

> "Hake, being the one who was doing the cooking, poured out some tea into his own cup, and putting a piece of pounded meat and marrow into a small plate, placed it before the stranger, saying: 'Eat, my friend, we are on the warpath and do not carry much of a variety of food with us, but I give you the best we have.'" (030-145–030-148)

This act of generosity toward the unknown — giving the best of what little they had — marked Hake as worthy in the spirit's eyes.

#### Victory and Return

Hake followed the spirit's instructions faithfully. He chased and killed one of the two enemy riders, took his scalp and gun, and presented the old coward's scalp to the spirit, freeing it to join its forefathers:

> "When they arrived at the place, there stood the spirit. Hake presented him with the old man's scalp and then the spirit showed them the large band of horses." (030-238–030-240)

The friends returned with a great herd of horses and two enemy scalps — Hake had earned his feathers. He was reunited with Pretty Feather, whom Chaske had revived from her death-like trance, and the couple was married with great celebration and gifts.
