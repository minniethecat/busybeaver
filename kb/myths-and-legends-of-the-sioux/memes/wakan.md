## Overview

_Wakan_ is a central concept in Lakota (Sioux) spirituality, most commonly translated as "holy" or "sacred." It describes anything imbued with spiritual power or mystery — not in the sense of moral purity, but as a quality of being connected to the unseen spirit world. The term appears throughout Sioux oral traditions applied to ghosts, medicine men, animals, and natural phenomena.

The supreme expression of this concept is _Wakan Tanka_ — the Great Spirit or Great Mystery — the ultimate spiritual power governing the universe. However, _wakan_ as a quality could manifest in lesser forms: a haunted place, a person of spiritual reputation, or a bird regarded as an omen.

## Manifestations in the Stories

### The Wakan Ghost

In the Story of the Two Young Friends (Chapter 30), old warriors warn travelers of a haunted creek:

> There is a ghost who haunts that creek, and any one who camps there is disturbed all through the night, and besides they never return, because the ghost is Wakan (holy), and the enemies conquer the travelers every time.
>
> (030-121–030-124)

The ghost's _wakan_ nature made it both sacred and dangerous. Travelers who camped near the ghost's domain "never return," and the proper response was respectful avoidance rather than confrontation.

### The Wakan Holy Man

A chief in the Story of the Pet Crow (Chapter 31) earned renown as "a Wakan (Holy man)" (031-035). His reputation as a _wakan_ figure — one with special spiritual insight or power — spread across the tribes, and his warriors' success in ambush and battle was attributed to this spiritual authority.

### The Wakan Bird

In the story of White Plume (Chapter 35), a mysterious bird appeared during a ceremonial feast. The great chief and medicine men examined it and "pronounced the bird 'Wakan' (something holy)" (035-067–035-068). The people then filed beneath the bird in single file to view it closely, treating it as a sacred omen worthy of veneration.

## Wakan and Wakan Tanka

While _wakan_ describes a quality of spiritual power that can manifest in many forms, _Wakan Tanka_ (literally "Great Holy" or "Great Mystery") refers specifically to the supreme spiritual entity — the Great Spirit to whom all ceremonies and prayers are directed. Every instance of _wakan_ in the world was understood as an emanation or expression of this greater spiritual reality.
