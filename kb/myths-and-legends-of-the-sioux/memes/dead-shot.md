## Overview

**Dead Shot** (also known as **"He Who Never Misses His Mark"**) is a Sioux warrior renowned for his flawless accuracy with the bow and arrow. His name is a title earned through demonstrated skill rather than a birth name. He is married to **Beautiful Dove**, and together they host a feast that draws visitors from across the region, including the notable character White Plume.

## In the Stories

### The Story of White Plume (Chapter 035)

Dead Shot earned his name through consistent demonstration of his archery skill:

> He was given the title of "Dead Shot," or "He who never misses his mark," for his unerring accuracy with the bow and arrow. (035-003)

He and his wife, Beautiful Dove, are known for their hospitality. They issue an open invitation to all who pass through their territory:

> "Dead Shot and his wife, Beautiful Dove, invite all who are hungry to come and eat at their tepee." (035-051)

When a stranger arrives in need, fortune leads him to Dead Shot's dwelling:

> Luckily the stranger came to Dead Shot's tent, and as was their custom, they offered him food and shelter. (035-081)

That night, Dead Shot and his wife discuss the stranger's identity and the unusual circumstances of his arrival:

> That night Dead Shot and his wife sat up away into the night, speaking in low tones about their mysterious guest. (035-132)

Dead Shot's reputation extends to the chief's circle. When White Plume passes through the region, the chief asks a young man who had visited Dead Shot's camp whether he encountered the famous warrior:

> "Did you see White Plume when you went to Dead Shot's camp?" the chief asked. (035-196)

Dead Shot serves as a model of Sioux hospitality and the recognition that comes from mastery of essential warrior skills. His union with Beautiful Dove and their open-handedness toward strangers reflects the Sioux value of generosity.
