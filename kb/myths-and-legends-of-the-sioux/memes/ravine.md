## Overview

The **ravine** — also called a **coulee** — in Sioux folklore is a cut in the earth that conceals and reveals. "They had just topped a hill and came to a little ravine or coulee" (013-002) and "suddenly they saw coming up from the ravine a beautiful woman" (013-003) — the ravine births the unexpected. A man is seen "coming up through a deep ravine" (023-055), his emergence from depth making him visible inch by inch. Warriors leave a marker "over in the next ravine" (030-183) — the ravine as landmark and message-board. Steeper and narrower than the valley, more abrupt than the creek bed, the ravine is the landscape's crease — a fold where the ordinary world buckles and the extraordinary appears.

## In the Stories

Chapter 013 contains the most vivid ravine scene. "They had just topped a hill and came to a little ravine or coulee" (013-002) — the ravine is discovered at the crest, revealed only when one is upon it. "Suddenly they saw coming up from the ravine a beautiful woman. She was" (013-003) — the ravine is a place of emergence, its depths giving up figures of beauty and power. The suddenness is essential: the ravine conceals until the last moment, then reveals.

In Chapter 023, "a man coming up through a deep ravine" (023-055) is observed — his movement through depth makes him vulnerable, visible to watchers above. The ravine here is both passage and trap, a route that exposes those who use it.

Chapter 030 uses the ravine as a directional marker: "We will leave over in the next ravine. He will then" (030-183) — the ravine is a landmark on the warpath, a place of signals and messages left for those who follow.

## Cultural Significance

- **Ravine as emergence point**: The beautiful woman rising from the ravine (013-002) makes it a birth-cleft, a place where the earth delivers figures of power
- **Ravine as concealment**: Its depth hides those within it (023-055) — the ravine is a route for those who do not wish to be seen
- **Ravine as sudden discovery**: "They had just topped a hill and came to a little ravine" (013-002) — the ravine is not visible from a distance; it surprises
- **Ravine as landmark**: "Over in the next ravine" (030-183) — ravines structure the landscape for travelers and warriors, serving as waypoints and message-sites


Two young men encounter a mysterious woman emerging from a ravine:
