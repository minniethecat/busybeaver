## Overview

The **village** is the center of Sioux social life in the tales — the place from which journeys begin and to which they return. "There was great rejoicing in her father's camp, and all the village women" (004-004) celebrate together — the village acts as a collective body. "The camping party left the village and went on their journey" (004-030) — departure from the village is the narrative's inciting event. The village contains lodges, families, councils, feasts — it is the container of all domestic and political life. Individual villages are distinguished by their inhabitants: "the village of the Bears" (009-010), "the crawfish village" (011-013), "the wolves' village" (010-039) — each village is defined by its people, not its location.

## In the Stories

### The Village as Collective Actor

A young woman runs away and "the whole village turned" (010-003) out to search — the village mobilizes as one. "All the villagers were called to go down to" (011-015) a gathering — collective action is the village's default mode. When a rabbit attacks, "there was a great outcry in the village. Openmouthed, all the bears" (009-041) respond — the village is a single organism of alarm and reaction.

### The Village as Home

Warriors return to their villages for recognition. A traveler "came at one end of the village to a hut where lived an old" (009-014) woman — the village periphery is where the elderly and marginal dwell. The "first lodge on the edge of the village" (009-016) is the entry point, the threshold between the wild and the social.

### Named Villages

Each village takes its character from its people. "The rabbit set out for the village of the Bears" (009-010) — the Bear village is a destination, a place with a known identity. "He led her to a large village" (010-009) — size matters; a large village implies power and prosperity. "I have been in the wolves' village" (010-039) — visiting another village is a significant act, requiring report and explanation.

## Cultural Significance

- **Village as collective body**: "All the village women" (004-004) and "the whole village" (010-003) act in unison — the village has a single will
- **Village as identity**: Named by its inhabitants (Bears, wolves, crawfish) — the people define the place
- **Departure and return**: The village is the fixed point from which journeys radiate and to which they return (004-030)
- **Periphery and center**: The village edge (009-016) houses the old and marginal; the center is for gatherings and decisions
