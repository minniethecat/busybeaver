## Overview

**Big Eagle** is a Sioux brave renowned for his hunting skills and the courageous deeds he performed. His name appears in a tale of wandering and discovery: after the death of his first wife, he remarries and travels with his new wife and her mother. On an expedition, he encounters a mysterious old man who leads him to a great village of his people, where he is honored and given a seat of distinction.

## In the Stories

### The Brave Who Went on the Warpath Alone (Chapter 024)

Big Eagle is introduced as a man celebrated for his brave acts:

> His name was Big Eagle. This man they honored for the brave acts he had performed. (024-010)

He is instructed to survey the land and select the best location for his people:

> Big Eagle was told to look carefully over the ground and select the best site for the new camp. (024-013)

Big Eagle had been married before, but his wife had died five winters earlier. Now remarried, he travels with his wife and her mother. During the journey, he encounters a solitary old man. Making signs to communicate across a language barrier, Big Eagle attempts to understand the stranger:

> Finally, Big Eagle made signs to the man, explaining as well as possible that he was traveling with his family. (024-032)

The old man leads Big Eagle to an enormous camp hidden beyond a stream:

> The old man made signs for Big Eagle to remain where he was while he went ahead to announce the visitor to the village. Soon Big Eagle could see men gathering around the hill. (024-054)

Big Eagle is received with honor:

> The elders motioned Big Eagle to the seat of honor in the tepee, recognizing him as a distinguished visitor. (024-061)

After some time living among this people, Big Eagle grows concerned for the safety of his own village, which is far away. He sends the women back with an escort:

> Fearing for their safety and the long journey back to the village of Big Eagle, he sent them back to their own village under protection. (024-081–024-082)

Eventually the old man who guided him dies, and Big Eagle assumes leadership. His story exemplifies the Sioux virtues of courage, diplomacy across tribal boundaries, and loyalty to one's family.
