## Overview

The **shore** in Sioux folklore is a liminal zone where water meets land — and where the ordinary meets the extraordinary. Characters "swim to shore" (028-061) after underwater journeys, emerging into a changed world. The shore is where beings gather: "on the shore sunning themselves" (025-009) sit turtles, and men sit "on the opposite shore" (032-022) watching the water. An artichoke stands "on the shore of a lake" (008-001), an unlikely sentinel. The shore is both destination and departure point, the edge where stories pivot.

## In the Stories

The shore appears across multiple chapters as a transition point. In Chapter 007, a "green knoll that rose from its shore" (007-024) marks the lakeshore — the knoll itself becomes mobile, a wonder visible from the shore. A friend calls out "on the seashore" (007-050), the shore a place of calling and response.

In Chapter 008, "on the shore of a lake stood an artichoke" (008-001), an opening image that places an ordinary plant in an extraordinary position — rooted on the boundary between elements. The artichoke "would come out upon the shore and wander over" (008-004) the land, the shore as launching point for inland adventure.

Chapter 028 describes a transformative shore arrival: "Swimming to shore he found it was summer" (028-061) — the character emerges from water into a different season, the shore a threshold between temporal worlds.

The shore in Chapter 032 is a place of encounter and conflict. Unktomi, "swimming to the opposite shore" (032-017), crosses the water. On arrival, he finds "a man sitting on the opposite shore" (032-022), and later comes "upon two men sitting on the shore" (032-023). The shore becomes a meeting ground. Further east "on the shore of the" (032-033) water, more encounters await. A tree stands "near to the shore" (032-041), and children "going close to the shore, discovered" (032-049) something in the water — the shore as lookout point.

In Chapter 025, a boy sees beings "on the shore sunning themselves" (025-009) and "ran to the shore, but saw only bubbles coming" (025-014) — the shore is where surface life meets submerged mystery.

## Cultural Significance

- **Shore as arrival**: Characters swim to shore and find the world changed — a new season (028-061), new encounters
- **Shore as gathering place**: Turtles sun themselves (025-009), men sit and watch (032-022), children discover (032-049)
- **Shore as boundary**: The line between water and land is where transformations become visible
