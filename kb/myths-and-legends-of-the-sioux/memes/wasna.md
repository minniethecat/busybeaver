## Overview

**Wasna** (also known as **Pemmican**) is a traditional Sioux food consisting of dried buffalo (or other game) meat pounded into a paste, mixed with melted fat and sometimes dried berries. It was a staple provision for hunting parties, war parties, and long journeys across the plains. Highly nutritious and long-lasting, wasna could sustain warriors and travelers for extended periods without spoiling.

## In the Stories

Wasna appears most notably in the story of "The Wasna (Pemmican Man) and the Unktomi (Spider)" (032-001). In this tale, the Unktomi — a trickster figure in Sioux folklore — encounters the Pemmican Man and attempts to deceive him. The story illustrates the cultural significance of wasna as a valuable food resource.

The Pemmican Man himself is a personification of this essential food, reflecting the deep respect the Sioux held for the sustenance that allowed them to survive on the plains.

## Cultural Significance

- Wasna was more than food — it was survival technology. A small amount could sustain a warrior for a full day of travel or battle
- The preparation was a communal activity, with women traditionally responsible for pounding the dried meat and mixing it with fat
- The name "wasna" itself comes from the Sioux language, while "pemmican" derives from the Cree word *pimîhkân*
