[002-001]Once upon a time a prairie mouse busied herself all fall storing away a

[002-002]cache of beans. Every morning she was out early with her empty cast-off

[002-003]snake skin, which she filled with ground beans and dragged home with her

[002-004]teeth.

[002-005]The little mouse had a cousin who was fond of dancing and talk, but who

[002-006]did not like to work. She was not careful to get her cache of beans and

[002-007]the season was already well gone before she thought to bestir herself.

[002-008]When she came to realize her need, she found she had no packing bag. So

[002-009]she went to her hardworking cousin and said:

[002-010]“Cousin, I have no beans stored for winter and the season is nearly

[002-011]gone. But I have no snake skin to gather the beans in. Will you lend me

[002-012]one?”

[002-013]“But why have you no packing bag? Where were you in the moon when the

[002-014]snakes cast off their skins?”

[002-015]“I was here.”

[002-016]“What were you doing?”

[002-017]“I was busy talking and dancing.”

[002-018]“And now you are punished,” said the other. “It is always so with lazy,

[002-019]careless people. But I will let you have the snake skin. And now go, and

[002-020]by hard work and industry, try to recover your wasted time.”
