[013-001]Two young men were out strolling one night talking of love affairs. They

[013-002]passed around a hill and came to a little ravine or coulee. Suddenly

[013-003]they saw coming up from the ravine a beautiful woman. She was painted

[013-004]and her dress was of the very finest material.

[013-005]“What a beautiful girl!” said one of the young men. “Already I love her.

[013-006]I will steal her and make her my wife.”

[013-007]“No,” said the other. “Don’t harm her. She may be holy.”

[013-008]The young woman approached and held out a pipe which she first offered

[013-009]to the sky, then to the earth and then advanced, holding it out in her

[013-010]extended hands.

[013-011]“I know what you young men have been saying; one of you is good; the

[013-012]other is wicked,” she said.

[013-013]She laid down the pipe on the ground and at once became a buffalo cow.

[013-014]The cow pawed the ground, stuck her tail straight out behind her and

[013-015]then lifted the pipe from the ground again in her hoofs; immediately she

[013-016]became a young woman again.

[013-017]“I am come to give you this gift,” she said. “It is the peace pipe.

[013-018]Hereafter all treaties and ceremonies shall be performed after smoking

[013-019]it. It shall bring peaceful thoughts into your minds. You shall offer it

[013-020]to the Great Mystery and to mother earth.”

[013-021]The two young men ran to the village and told what they had seen and

[013-022]heard. All the village came out where the young woman was.

[013-023]She repeated to them what she had already told the young men and added:

[013-024]“When you set free the ghost (the spirit of deceased persons) you must

[013-025]have a white buffalo cow skin.”

[013-026]She gave the pipe to the medicine men of the village, turned again to a

[013-027]buffalo cow and fled away to the land of buffaloes.
