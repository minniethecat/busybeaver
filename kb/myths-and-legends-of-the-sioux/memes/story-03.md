[003-001]A little girl owned a pet rabbit which she loved dearly. She carried it

[003-002]on her back like a babe, made for it a little pair of moccasins, and at

[003-003]night shared with it her own robe.

[003-004]Now the little girl had a cousin who loved her very dearly and wished to

[003-005]do her honor; so her cousin said to herself:

[003-006]“I love my little cousin well and will ask her to let me carry her pet

[003-007]rabbit around;” (for thus do Indian women when they wish to honor a

[003-008]friend; they ask permission to carry about the friend’s babe).

[003-009]She then went to the little girl and said:

[003-010]“Cousin, let me carry your pet rabbit about on my back. Thus shall I

[003-011]show you how I love you.”

[003-012]Her mother, too, said to her: “Oh no, do not let our little grandchild

[003-013]go away from our tepee.”

[003-014]But the cousin answered: “Oh, do let me carry it. I do so want to show

[003-015]my cousin honor.” At last they let her go away with the pet rabbit on

[003-016]her back.

[003-017]When the little girl’s cousin came home to her tepee, some rough boys

[003-018]who were playing about began to make sport of her. To tease the little

[003-019]girl they threw stones and sticks at the pet rabbit. At last a stick

[003-020]struck the little rabbit upon the head and killed it.

[003-021]When her pet was brought home dead, the little rabbit’s adopted mother

[003-022]wept bitterly. She cut off her hair for mourning and all her little girl

[003-023]friends wailed with her. Her mother, too, mourned with them.

[003-024]“Alas!” they cried, “alas, for the little rabbit. He was always kind and

[003-025]gentle. Now your child is dead and you will be lonesome.”

[003-026]The little girl’s mother called in her little friends and made a great

[003-027]mourning feast for the little rabbit. As he lay in the tepee his adopted

[003-028]mother’s little friends brought many precious things and covered his

[003-029]body. At the feast were given away robes and kettles and blankets and

[003-030]knives and great wealth in honor of the little rabbit. Him they wrapped

[003-031]in a robe with his little moccasins on and buried him in a high place

[003-032]upon a scaffold.
