[006-001]The rabbit once went out on the prairie in winter time. On the side of a

[006-002]hill away from the wind he found a great company of girls all with grey

[006-003]and speckled blankets over their backs. They were the grouse girls and

[006-004]they were coasting down hill on a board. When the rabbit saw them, he

[006-005]called out:

[006-006]“Oh, maidens, that is not a good way to coast down hill. Let me get you

[006-007]a fine skin with bangles on it that tinkle as you slide.” And away he

[006-008]ran to the tepee and brought a skin bag. It had red stripes on it and

[006-009]bangles that tinkled. “Come and get inside,” he said to the grouse

[006-010]girls. “Oh, no, we are afraid,” they answered. “Don’t be afraid, I can’t

[006-011]hurt you. Come, one of you,” said the rabbit. Then as each hung back he

[006-012]added coaxingly: “If each is afraid alone, come all together. I can’t

[006-013]hurt you _all_.” And so he coaxed the whole flock into the bag. This

[006-014]done, the rabbit closed the mouth of the bag, slung it over his back and

[006-015]came home. “Grandmother,” said he, as he came to the tepee, “here is a

[006-016]bag full of game. Watch it while I go for willow sticks to make spits.”

[006-017]But as soon as the rabbit had gone out of the tent, the grouse girls

[006-018]began to cry out:

[006-019]“Grandmother, let us out.”

[006-020]“Who are you?” asked the old woman.

[006-021]“Your dear grandchildren,” they answered.

[006-022]“But how came you in the bag?” asked the old woman.

[006-023]“Oh, our cousin was jesting with us. He coaxed us in the bag for a joke.

[006-024]Please let us out.”

[006-025]“Certainly, dear grandchildren, I will let you out,” said the old

[006-026]woman as she untied the bag: and lo, the grouse flock with

[006-027]achuck-a-chuck-achuck flew up, knocking over the old grandmother and

[006-028]flew out of the square smoke opening of the winter lodge. The old woman

[006-029]caught only one grouse as it flew up and held it, grasping a leg with

[006-030]each hand.

[006-031]When the rabbit came home with the spits she called out to him:

[006-032]“Grandson, come quick. They got out but I have caught two.”

[006-033]When he saw what had happened he was quite angry, yet could not keep

[006-034]from laughing.

[006-035]“Grandmother, you have but one grouse,” he cried, “and it is a very

[006-036]skinny one at that.”
