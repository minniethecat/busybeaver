## Overview

The **Pet Crane** is a young crane chick rescued from its nest by the twelve-year-old son of a hunter (034-007–034-009). Raised among the family's children, the crane grows to understand human speech and joins in their games (034-014–034-016). When game migrates and the family faces starvation, the crane's father — learning from Mr. Fox of their plight — brings fat (034-030–034-037). Later, when a Crow war party surrounds the family's tent, the pet crane is disguised in human clothing to distract the enemy while the family escapes — a sacrifice that costs the crane its life (034-066–034-084). His story is one of the most poignant examples of reciprocal bonds between humans and animals in Sioux folklore.

## In the Stories

### The Story of the Pet Crane (Chapter 034)

A hunter's twelve-year-old son, roaming the forest, discovers a crane's nest with a single surviving chick:

> During his ramblings, he discovered a crane's nest, with only one young crane occupying it. No doubt some fox or traveling weasel had eaten the rest of the crane's brothers and sisters. (034-007–034-009)

The boy takes pity on the orphaned bird:

> "I will take this poor little crane home and will raise him as a pet for our baby. If I leave him here some hungry fox will be sure to eat the poor little fellow." He carried the young crane home and it grew up among the family. (034-010–034-012)

The crane becomes part of the human household, understanding all the family said and joining in the children's games, though unable to speak (034-014–034-016).

When game vanishes from the region and the hunter returns empty-handed, the family is reduced to cherry bark tea. Late that night, the cry of a crane rings out:

> Instantly the pet crane awoke, stepped outside and answered the call. The crane which had given the cry was the father of the pet crane, and learning from Mr. Fox of the starving condition of his son and his friends, he flew to the hunting grounds of the tribe, and as there had been a good kill that day, the crane found no trouble in securing a great quantity of fat. This he carried to the tent of the hunter and, hovering over the tent he suddenly let the fat drop to the earth and at once the pet crane picked it up and carried it to the woman. (034-030–034-037)

The woman renders the fat through the night. While she works, she sees a hand reflected in the pan of grease — a Crow scout counting the sleeping family members (034-048–034-059). Recognizing the danger, she dresses the pet crane in a blanket and buffalo-hide hat and instructs it to run around the tent as a decoy while the family flees in three directions (034-066–034-074). The ruse works. The Crow war party attacks what they believe is a child, and:

> The next morning the family returned to see what had become of their pet crane. There, riddled to pieces, lay the poor bird who had given up his life to save his dear friends. (034-082–034-084)
