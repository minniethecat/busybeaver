[029-001]There once lived a Sioux couple who had two children, a boy and a girl.

[029-002]Every fall this family would move away from the main camp and take

[029-003]up their winter quarters in a grove of timber some distance from the

[029-004]principal village. The reason they did this was that he was a great

[029-005]hunter and where a village was located for the winter the game was

[029-006]usually very scarce. Therefore, he always camped by himself in order to

[029-007]have an abundance of game adjacent to his camp.

[029-008]All summer he had roamed around following the tribe to wherever their

[029-009]fancy might take them. During their travels this particular year there

[029-010]came to the village a strange girl who had no relatives there. No one

[029-011]seemed very anxious to take her into their family, so the great hunter’s

[029-012]daughter, taking a fancy to the poor girl, took her to their home and

[029-013]kept her. She addressed her as sister, and the parents, on account of

[029-014]their daughter, addressed her as daughter.

[029-015]This strange girl became desperately in love with the young man of the

[029-016]family, but being addressed as daughter by the parents, she could not

[029-017]openly show her feelings as the young man was considered her brother.

[029-018]In the fall when the main village moved into a large belt of timber for

[029-019]their winter quarters, the hunter moved on to another place two days’

[029-020]travel from the main winter camp, where he would not be disturbed by any

[029-021]other hunters.

[029-022]The young man had a tent by himself, and it was always kept nice and

[029-023]clean by his sister, who was very much attached to him. After a long

[029-024]day’s hunt in the woods, he would go into his tent and lie down to rest,

[029-025]and when his supper was ready his sister would say, “My brother is so

[029-026]tired. I will carry his supper to him.”

[029-027]Her friend, whom she addressed as sister, would never go into the young

[029-028]man’s tent. Along towards spring there came one night into the young

[029-029]man’s tent a woman. She sat down by the door and kept her face covered

[029-030]so that it was hidden from view. She sat there a long time and finally

[029-031]arose and went away. The young man could not imagine who this could be.

[029-032]He knew that it was a long distance from the village and could not make

[029-033]out where the woman could have come from. The next night the woman came

[029-034]again and this time she came a little nearer to where the young man lay.

[029-035]She sat down and kept her face covered as before. Neither spoke a word.

[029-036]She sat there for a long time and then arose and departed. He was very

[029-037]much puzzled over the actions of this woman and decided to ascertain on

[029-038]her next visit who she was.

[029-039]He kindled a small fire in his tent and had some ash wood laid on it so

[029-040]as to keep fire a long time, as ash burns very slowly and holds fire a

[029-041]long time.

[029-042]The third night the woman came again and sat down still nearer his bed.

[029-043]She held her blanket open just a trifle, and he, catching up one of the

[029-044]embers, flashed it in her face; jumping up she ran hurriedly out of the

[029-045]tent. The next morning he noticed that his adopted sister kept her face

[029-046]hidden with her blanket. She chanced to drop her blanket while in the

[029-047]act of pouring out some soup, and when she did so he noticed a large

[029-048]burned spot on her cheek.

[029-049]He felt so sorry for what he had done that he could eat no breakfast,

[029-050]but went outside and lay down under an oak tree. All day long he lay

[029-051]there gazing up into the tree, and when he was called for supper he

[029-052]refused, saying that he was not hungry, and for them not to bother him,

[029-053]as he would soon get up and go to bed. Far into the night he lay thus,

[029-054]and when he tried to arise he could not, as a small oak tree grew

[029-055]through the center of his body and held him fast to the ground.

[029-056]In the morning when the family awoke they found the girl had

[029-057]disappeared, and on going outside the sister discovered her brother held

[029-058]fast to the earth by an oak tree which grew very rapidly. In vain were

[029-059]the best medicine men of the tribe sent for. Their medicine was of no

[029-060]avail. They said: “If the tree is cut down the young man will die.”

[029-061]The sister was wild with grief, and extending her hands to the sun, she

[029-062]cried: “Great Spirit, relieve my suffering brother. Any one who releases

[029-063]him I will marry, be he young, old, homely or deformed.”

[029-064]Several days after the young man had met with the mishap, there came to

[029-065]the tent a very tall man, who had a bright light encircling his body.

[029-066]“Where is the girl who promised to marry any one who would release

[029-067]her brother?” “I am the one,” said the young man’s sister. “I am the

[029-068]all-powerful lightning and thunder. I see all things and can kill at one

[029-069]stroke a whole tribe. When I make my voice heard the rocks shake loose

[029-070]and go rattling down the hillsides. The brave warriors cower shivering

[029-071]under some shelter at the sound of my voice. The girl whom you had

[029-072]adopted as your sister was a sorceress. She bewitched your brother

[029-073]because he would not let her make love to him. On my way here I met her

[029-074]traveling towards the west, and knowing what she had done, I struck her

[029-075]with one of my blazing swords, and she lies there now a heap of ashes. I

[029-076]will now release your brother.”

[029-077]So saying he placed his hand on the tree and instantly it crumbled to

[029-078]ashes. The young man arose, and thanked his deliverer.

[029-079]Then they saw a great black cloud approaching, and the man said: “Make

[029-080]ready, we shall go home on that cloud.” As the cloud approached near to

[029-081]the man who stood with his bride, it suddenly lowered and enveloped them

[029-082]and with a great roar and amidst flashes of lightning and loud peals of

[029-083]thunder the girl ascended and disappeared into the west with her Thunder

[029-084]and Lightning husband.
