[012-001]A Dakota had married an Arikara woman, and by her had one child. By and

[012-002]by he took another wife. The first wife was jealous and pouted. When

[012-003]time came for the village to break camp she refused to move from her

[012-004]place on the tent floor. The tent was taken down but she sat on the

[012-005]ground with her babe on her back The rest of the camp with her husband

[012-006]went on.

[012-007]At noon her husband halted the line. “Go back to your sister-in-law,”

[012-008]he said to his two brothers. “Tell her to come on and we will await you

[012-009]here. But hasten, for I fear she may grow desperate and kill herself.”

[012-010]The two rode off and arrived at their former camping place in the

[012-011]evening. The woman still sat on the ground. The elder spoke:

[012-012]“Sister-in-law, get up. We have come for you. The camp awaits you.”

[012-013]She did not answer, and he put out his hand and touched her head. She

[012-014]had turned to stone!

[012-015]The two brothers lashed their ponies and came back to camp. They told

[012-016]their story, but were not believed. “The woman has killed herself and my

[012-017]brothers will not tell me,” said the husband. However, the whole village

[012-018]broke camp and came back to the place where they had left the woman.

[012-019]Sure enough, she sat there still, a block of stone.

[012-020]The Indians were greatly excited. They chose out a handsome pony, made

[012-021]a new travois and placed the stone in the carrying net. Pony and travois

[012-022]were both beautifully painted and decorated with streamers and colors.

[012-023]The stone was thought _“wakan”_ (holy), and was given a place of honor

[012-024]in the center of the camp. Whenever the camp moved the stone and travois

[012-025]were taken along. Thus the stone woman was carried for years, and

[012-026]finally brought to Standing Rock Agency, and now rests upon a brick

[012-027]pedestal in front of the Agency office. From this stone Standing Rock

[012-028]Agency derives its name.
