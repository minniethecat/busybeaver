[011-001]Sharp and cunning is the raccoon, say the Indians, by whom he is named

[011-002]Spotted Face.

[011-003]A crawfish one evening wandered along a river bank, looking for

[011-004]something dead to feast upon. A raccoon was also out looking for

[011-005]something to eat. He spied the crawfish and formed a plan to catch him.

[011-006]He lay down on the bank and feigned to be dead. By and by the crawfish

[011-007]came near by. “Ho,” he thought, “here is a feast indeed; but is he

[011-008]really dead. I will go near and pinch him with my claws and find out.”

[011-009]So he went near and pinched the raccoon on the nose and then on his soft

[011-010]paws. The raccoon never moved. The crawfish then pinched him on the ribs

[011-011]and tickled him so that the raccoon could hardly keep from laughing. The

[011-012]crawfish at last left him. “The raccoon is surely dead,” he thought.

[011-013]And he hurried back to the crawfish village and reported his find to the

[011-014]chief.

[011-015]All the villagers were called to go down to the feast. The chief bade

[011-016]the warriors and young men to paint their faces and dress in their

[011-017]gayest for a dance.

[011-018]So they marched in a long line--first the warriors, with their weapons

[011-019]in hand, then the women with their babies and children--to the place

[011-020]where the raccoon lay. They formed a great circle about him and danced,

[011-021]singing:

[011-022]“We shall have a great feast

[011-023]“On the spotted-faced beast, with soft smooth paws:

[011-024]“He is dead!

[011-025]“He is dead!

[011-026]“We shall dance!

[011-027]“We shall have a good time!

[011-028]“We shall feast on his flesh.”

[011-029]But as they danced, the raccoon suddenly sprang to his feet.

[011-030]“Who is that you say you are going to eat? He has a spotted face, has

[011-031]he? He has soft, smooth paws, has he? I’ll break your ugly backs. I’ll

[011-032]break your rough bones. I’ll crunch your ugly, rough paws.” And he

[011-033]rushed among the crawfish, killing them by scores. The crawfish warriors

[011-034]fought bravely and the women ran screaming, all to no purpose. They did

[011-035]not feast on the raccoon; the raccoon feasted _on them!_
