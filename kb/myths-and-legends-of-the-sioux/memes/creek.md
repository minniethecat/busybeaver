## Overview

The **creek** in Sioux folklore is the most intimate of watercourses — smaller than a river, more personal than a lake. A traveler "came to a little creek, and being thirsty took a" (020-154) drink, the creek as refreshment. But creeks also harbor danger: "there is a ghost who haunts that creek, and any one who camps" (030-121) there risks supernatural encounter. A "thickly wooded creek within the enemies' bounds" (030-119) is a place of ambush, and a "creek bend where he could drive the" (023-139) quarry is a site of strategic concealment. The creek threads through the Sioux landscape as both blessing and menace — its water sustains, its banks conceal, and its darkness holds spirits.

## In the Stories

The most detailed creek sequence occurs in Chapter 030, where a party approaches "a thickly wooded creek within the enemies' bounds" (030-119). The creek is described as looking "the ideal place to camp" (030-120) — its appeal is exactly what makes it dangerous. The warning is explicit: "there is a ghost who haunts that creek, and any one who camps" (030-121) there invites calamity. When the party arrives, "it seemed as if they had stepped" (030-136) into another realm entirely.

In Chapter 020, a lone traveler "came to a little creek, and being thirsty took a good" (020-154) drink — the simplest human interaction with water, rendered significant by its place in the journey. He then goes "to a hill which overhung the creek" (020-156), the elevation providing perspective on the water below.

Chapter 037 offers a moment of wonder: a traveler, "on arriving at the creek to" (037-089) his destination, "noticed a door placed into the creek bank on the opposite side of" (037-097) the water — the creek bank concealing a hidden entrance, a secret dwelling, a place not meant to be seen.

## Cultural Significance

- **Creek as rest-stop**: The thirsty traveler drinking (020-154) makes the creek a place of replenishment along the journey
- **Creek as haunted space**: The ghost-haunted creek (030-121) ties water to the spirit world — flowing water does not cleanse this darkness
- **Creek as tactical feature**: The creek bend (023-139) and wooded banks (030-119) provide concealment for hunters and warriors
- **Creek as threshold**: The door in the creek bank (037-097) suggests that creeks are entry points to other worlds, their banks not merely earth but portals


Veteran warriors warn young scouts of a haunted waterway:

> The old men said, "That creek looks the ideal place to camp, but don't
> camp there by any means, because there is a ghost who haunts that creek,
> and any one who camps there is disturbed all through the night, and
> besides they never return, because the ghost is Wakan (holy), and the
> enemies conquer the travelers."
>
> (030-119–030-123)
