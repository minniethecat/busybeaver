## Overview

The **valley** in Sioux folklore is a landscape of depth and passage — a cleft in the earth that travelers must descend into and climb out of. A "beautiful valley which was threaded by a beautiful" (023-046) creek offers one of the most vivid pastoral images in the collection. Warriors "looking down into the valley" (023-062) from above assess the land below. A traveler "looked down into the long valley" (037-083) before descending. The valley is not merely scenery — it is the space within which journeys unfold, the bowl that holds creek and plain and the movement of the story.

## In the Stories

The most concentrated valley sequence occurs in Chapters 023–024, where a party looks down into a "beautiful valley" (023-046), a landscape so appealing it draws the eye and frames the action. The valley is "threaded by a beautiful creek fringed with" (023-046) vegetation — a pastoral interlude in a narrative otherwise dominated by conflict. From "some hill, looking down into the valley on the" (024-048) approach, the valley provides a natural survey point.

In Chapter 037, a traveler "looked steadily down into the long valley with a" (037-083) sense of purpose, beginning a descent that carries the narrative into its final phase. "Across the valley was a long plain" (037-084) — the valley floor gives way to open country, the journey continuing through varied terrain.

A character in Chapter 023 positions himself at "some creek bend where he could drive the" (023-139) quarry — the valley's creek providing both concealment and strategy.

## Cultural Significance

- **Valley as journey-space**: Valleys are places one descends into (037-083), traverses, and climbs out of — they structure the topography of narrative travel
- **Valley as beauty**: The "beautiful valley" (023-046) is an aesthetic landscape, a moment of pastoral description in a story otherwise concerned with action
- **Valley as vantage**: "Looking down into the valley" (023-062, 024-048) — the valley is something observed from above, a landscape understood through elevation
- **Valley as shelter**: The creek-threaded valley (023-046) provides cover and resource — water, vegetation, concealment


A traveler pauses at a ridge to survey the landscape before him:

> he sat down and gazed long and steadily down into the long valley with a
> beautiful creek winding through it. Across the valley was a long plain
> stretching for miles beyond and finally ending at the foot of another
> range of hills, the counterpart of the one upon which he sat.
>
> (037-083–037-086)
