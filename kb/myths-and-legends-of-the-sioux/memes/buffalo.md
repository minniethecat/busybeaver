## Overview

The **Buffalo** (_Tatanka_ in the Sioux language) was the cornerstone of traditional Sioux life. It provided meat for food, hides for clothing and shelter, bones for tools, and occupied a central place in Sioux ceremony and cosmology. The rare white buffalo was considered the most sacred of all animals, and the buffalo cow was the form taken by the divine messenger who brought the Peace Pipe.

## In the Stories

### Source of Sustenance

The buffalo hunt was a regular communal activity. Leaders announced, "Your people are going off on a buffalo hunt. Tomorrow at noon they will" depart (010-030), and "You must go on a buffalo hunt, my father and all the hunters" (010-049). When asked what she had to eat, one character answered simply: "Buffalo meat" (010-014). After successful hunts, the people were "laden with the buffalo meat" (010-054).

> Thus she lived for a year, all the wolves being very kind to her. At the
> end of that time the wolf chief said to her:
>
> "Your people are going off on a buffalo hunt. Tomorrow at noon they will
> be here. You must then go out and meet them or they will fall on us and
> kill us."
>
> (010-030–010-031)

### The Sacred White Buffalo

The most sacred form is the white buffalo cow, which appears in the Peace Pipe origin story. The divine messenger transformed into "a buffalo cow" after delivering the sacred pipe (013-013), and instructed that when releasing spirits, "you must have a white buffalo cow skin" (013-025). After her message was delivered, she "turned again to a buffalo cow and fled away to the land of buffaloes" (013-026).

> She laid down the pipe on the ground and at once became a buffalo cow.
> The cow pawed the ground, stuck her tail straight out behind her and
> then lifted the pipe from the ground again in her hoofs; immediately she
> became a young woman again.
>
> (013-013–013-014)

### Shelter and Clothing

Buffalo hides provided physical shelter. One character's "tent was made of buffalo skins, and his dress was made of deer skin" (026-002). Coarse buffalo skin garments (\_wei-zi\_) were worn by those of humble station (015-016), while finer hides marked higher status.

## Cultural Significance

- Buffalo provided the material foundation of Sioux life: food, shelter, clothing, and tools
- The buffalo hunt was a communal enterprise requiring coordination and spiritual preparation
- The white buffalo cow is the most sacred animal form — messenger of the Great Spirit
- The "land of buffaloes" represents both a physical destination and a spiritual realm
- Buffalo appear in multiple origin stories and ceremonies
