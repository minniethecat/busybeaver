## Overview

The **Peace Pipe** (_Chanunpa_ in the Sioux language, also known as the **Calumet**) is one of the most sacred objects in Sioux tradition. According to legend, it was given to the people by a mysterious woman who appeared from a ravine and transformed between human and buffalo form. The pipe is used to solemnize treaties, open ceremonies, and bring peaceful thoughts to those who smoke it.

## In the Stories

The origin of the Peace Pipe is recounted in "Story of the Peace Pipe" (013-001). Two young men encountered a beautiful woman emerging from a ravine:

> "I am come to give you this gift," she said. "It is the peace pipe. Hereafter all treaties and ceremonies shall be performed after smoking it. It shall bring peaceful thoughts into your minds. You shall offer it to the Great Mystery and to mother earth." (013-017–013-020)

She also instructed that when releasing the spirits of the deceased, a white buffalo cow skin must be used (013-024). After delivering her message, she gave the pipe to the medicine men, transformed back into a buffalo cow, and fled to the land of buffaloes.

## Cultural Significance

- The Peace Pipe is central to Sioux diplomacy and spiritual practice — no treaty or ceremony is complete without it
- The offering sequence (to the sky, then to the earth) reflects the Sioux cosmology of Great Mystery above and Mother Earth below
- The connection to the white buffalo — the most sacred animal in Sioux tradition — underscores the pipe's holiness
