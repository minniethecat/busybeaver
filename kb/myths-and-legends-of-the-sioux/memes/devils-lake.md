## Overview

**Devils Lake** (sometimes called **Spirit Lake**) is a large natural lake in northeastern North Dakota and the site of a historic Indian Agency. In the context of Sioux folklore, Devils Lake holds special significance as one of the places where the author Marie L. McLaughlin — herself of one-fourth Sioux blood — first heard the stories that would become her 1916 collection _Myths and Legends of the Sioux_.

## In the Stories

Devils Lake appears in the Foreword to the collection, where McLaughlin recounts her personal connection to the stories. Her husband served as Indian Agent at Devils Lake before being transferred to Standing Rock Agency on the Missouri River in 1881. Her time at Devils Lake provided the foundation for her collection of 38 Sioux tales — stories she heard directly from Sioux elders who still remembered the old ways.

The lake and surrounding area remain part of the ancestral territory of the Dakota people, and the stories collected at Devils Lake reflect the rich oral tradition of the region's Indigenous inhabitants.
