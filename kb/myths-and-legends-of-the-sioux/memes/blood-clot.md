## Overview

The **Blood Clot** is a magical object in the story "The Bear and the Rabbit Hunt Buffalo" (Chapter 022). After being forced to hunt buffalo for a gluttonous bear who denies him even a taste of blood, Rabbit secretly seizes a small clot that rolls past the bear's feet (022-052–022-054). The clot hardens against Rabbit's body, and in frustration he throws it through his chimney hole — whereupon it transforms into a baby who cries "Ate! Ate!" (father, father). After being thrown out twice more, the clot becomes a handsome youth whom Rabbit adopts as his son (022-060–022-068). The son later kills the tyrannical bear, sparing only the kind-hearted youngest cubs (022-104–022-108).
