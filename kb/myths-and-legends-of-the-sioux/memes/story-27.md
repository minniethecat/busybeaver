[027-001]A young man was once hunting and came to a steep hill. The east side

[027-002]of the hill suddenly dropped off to a very steep bank. He stood on

[027-003]this bank, and at the base he noticed a small opening. On going down to

[027-004]examine it more closely, he found it was large enough to admit a horse

[027-005]or buffalo. On either side of the door were figures of different animals

[027-006]engraved into the wall.

[027-007]He entered the opening and there, scattered about on the floor, lay many

[027-008]bracelets, pipes and many other things of ornament, as though they had

[027-009]been offerings to some great spirit. He passed through this first room

[027-010]and on entering the second it was so dark that he could not see his

[027-011]hands before his face, so becoming scared, he hurriedly left the place,

[027-012]and returning home told what he had seen.

[027-013]Upon hearing this the chief selected four of his most daring warriors to

[027-014]go with this young man and investigate and ascertain whether the young

[027-015]man was telling the truth or not. The five proceeded to the butte, and

[027-016]at the entrance the young man refused to go inside, as the figures on

[027-017]either side of the entrance had been changed.

[027-018]The four entered and seeing that all in the first chamber was as the

[027-019]young man had told, they went on to the next chamber and found it so

[027-020]dark that they could not see anything. They continued on, however,

[027-021]feeling their way along the walls. They finally found an entrance that

[027-022]was so narrow that they had to squeeze into it sideways. They felt their

[027-023]way around the walls and found another entrance, so low down that

[027-024]they had to crawl on their hands and knees to go through into the next

[027-025]chamber.

[027-026]On entering the last chamber they found a very sweet odor coming from

[027-027]the opposite direction. Feeling around and crawling on their hands and

[027-028]knees, they discovered a hole in the floor leading downward. From this

[027-029]hole came up the sweet odor. They hurriedly held a council, and decided

[027-030]to go no further, but return to the camp and report what they had found.

[027-031]On getting to the first chamber one of the young men said: “I am going

[027-032]to take these bracelets to show that we are telling the truth.” “No,”

[027-033]said the other three, “this being the abode of some Great Spirit, you

[027-034]may have some accident befall you for taking what is not yours.” “Ah!

[027-035]You fellows are like old women,” said he, taking a fine bracelet and

[027-036]encircling his left wrist with it.

[027-037]When they reached the village they reported what they had seen. The

[027-038]young man exhibited the bracelet to prove that it was the truth they had

[027-039]told.

[027-040]Shortly after this, these four young men were out fixing up traps for

[027-041]wolves. They would raise one end of a heavy log and place a stick under,

[027-042]bracing up the log. A large piece of meat was placed about five feet

[027-043]away from the log and this space covered with poles and willows. At

[027-044]the place where the upright stick was put, a hole was left open, large

[027-045]enough to admit the body of a wolf. The wolf, scenting the meat and

[027-046]unable to get at it through the poles and willows, would crowd into the

[027-047]hole and working his body forward, in order to get the meat, would push

[027-048]down the brace and the log thus released would hold the wolf fast under

[027-049]its weight.

[027-050]The young man with the bracelet was placing his bait under the log when

[027-051]he released the log by knocking down the brace, and the log caught his

[027-052]wrist on which he wore the bracelet. He could not release himself and

[027-053]called loud and long for assistance. His friends, hearing his call, came

[027-054]to his assistance, and on lifting the log found the young man’s wrist

[027-055]broken. “Now,” said they, “you have been punished for taking the

[027-056]wristlet out of the chamber of the mysterious butte.”

[027-057]Some time after this a young man went to the butte and saw engraved on

[027-058]the wall a woman holding in her hand a pole, with which she was holding

[027-059]up a large amount of beef which had been laid across another pole, which

[027-060]had broken in two from the weight of so much meat.

[027-061]He returned to the camp and reported what he had seen. All around the

[027-062]figure he saw marks of buffalo hoofs, also marked upon the wall.

[027-063]The next day an enormous herd of buffalo came near to the village, and

[027-064]a great many were killed. The women were busy cutting up and drying the

[027-065]meat. At one camp was more meat than at any other. The woman was hanging

[027-066]meat upon a long tent pole, when the pole broke in two and she was

[027-067]obliged to hold the meat up with another pole, just as the young man saw

[027-068]on the mysterious butte.

[027-069]Ever after that the Indians paid weekly visits to this butte, and

[027-070]thereon would read the signs that were to govern their plans.

[027-071]This butte was always considered the prophet of the tribe.
