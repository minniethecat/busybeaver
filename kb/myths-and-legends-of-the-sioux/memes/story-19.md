[019-001]The Rabbit nation were very much depressed in spirits on account of

[019-002]being run over by all other nations. They, being very obedient to their

[019-003]chief, obeyed all his orders to the letter. One of his orders was,

[019-004]that upon the approach of any other nation that they should follow the

[019-005]example of their chief and run up among the rocks and down into their

[019-006]burrows, and not show themselves until the strangers had passed.

[019-007]This they always did. Even the chirp of a little cricket would send them

[019-008]all scampering to their dens.

[019-009]One day they held a great council, and after talking over everything for

[019-010]some time, finally left it to their medicine man to decide. The medicine

[019-011]man arose and said:

[019-012]“My friends, we are of no use on this earth. There isn’t a nation

[019-013]on earth that fears us, and we are so timid that we cannot defend

[019-014]ourselves, so the best thing for us to do is to rid the earth of our

[019-015]nation, by all going over to the big lake and drowning ourselves.”

[019-016]This they decided to do; so going to the lake they were about to jump

[019-017]in, when they heard a splashing in the water. Looking, they saw a lot of

[019-018]frogs jumping into the lake.

[019-019]“We will not drown ourselves,” said the medicine man, “we have found a

[019-020]nation who are afraid of us. It is the frog nation.” Had it not been for

[019-021]the frogs we would have had no rabbits, as the whole nation would have

[019-022]drowned themselves and the rabbit race would have been extinct.
