[010-001]A Dakota girl married a man who promised to treat her kindly, but he did

[010-002]not keep his word. He was unreasonable, fault-finding, and often beat

[010-003]her. Frantic with his cruelty, she ran away. The whole village turned

[010-004]out to search for her, but no trace of the missing wife was to be found.

[010-005]Meanwhile, the fleeing woman had wandered about all that day and the

[010-006]next night. The next day she met a man, who asked her who she was.

[010-007]She did not know it, but he was not really a man, but the chief of the

[010-008]wolves.

[010-009]“Come with me,” he said, and he led her to a large village. She was

[010-010]amazed to see here many wolves--gray and black, timber wolves and

[010-011]coyotes. It seemed as if all the wolves in the world were there.

[010-012]The wolf chief led the young woman to a great tepee and invited her in.

[010-013]He asked her what she ate for food.

[010-014]“Buffalo meat,” she answered.

[010-015]He called two coyotes and bade them bring what the young woman wanted.

[010-016]They bounded away and soon returned with the shoulder of a fresh-killed

[010-017]buffalo calf.

[010-018]“How do you prepare it for eating?” asked the wolf chief.

[010-019]“By boiling,” answered the young woman.

[010-020]Again he called the two coyotes. Away they bounded and soon brought into

[010-021]the tent a small bundle. In it were punk, flint and steel--stolen, it

[010-022]may be, from some camp of men.

[010-023]“How do you make the meat ready?” asked the wolf chief.

[010-024]“I cut it into slices,” answered the young woman.

[010-025]The coyotes were called and in a short time fetched in a knife in its

[010-026]sheath. The young woman cut up the calf’s shoulder into slices and ate

[010-027]it.

[010-028]Thus she lived for a year, all the wolves being very kind to her. At the

[010-029]end of that time the wolf chief said to her:

[010-030]“Your people are going off on a buffalo hunt. Tomorrow at noon they will

[010-031]be here. You must then go out and meet them or they will fall on us and

[010-032]kill us.”

[010-033]The next day at about noon the young woman went to the top of a

[010-034]neighboring knoll. Coming toward her were some young men riding on their

[010-035]ponies. She stood up and held her hands so that they could see her. They

[010-036]wondered who she was, and when they were close by gazed at her closely.

[010-037]“A year ago we lost a young woman; if you are she, where have you been,”

[010-038]they asked.

[010-039]“I have been in the wolves’ village. Do not harm them,” she answered.

[010-040]“We will ride back and tell the people,” they said. “Tomorrow again at

[010-041]noon, we shall meet you.”

[010-042]The young woman went back to the wolf village, and the next day went

[010-043]again to a neighboring knoll, though to a different one. Soon she

[010-044]saw the camp coming in a long line over the prairie. First were the

[010-045]warriors, then the women and tents.

[010-046]The young woman’s father and mother were overjoyed to see her. But when

[010-047]they came near her the young woman fainted, for she could not now bear

[010-048]the smell of human kind. When she came to herself she said:

[010-049]“You must go on a buffalo hunt, my father and all the hunters. Tomorrow

[010-050]you must come again, bringing with you the tongues and choice pieces of

[010-051]the kill.”

[010-052]This he promised to do; and all the men of the camp mounted their ponies

[010-053]and they had a great hunt. The next day they returned with their ponies

[010-054]laden with the buffalo meat. The young woman bade them pile the meat in

[010-055]a great heap between two hills which she pointed out to them. There was

[010-056]so much meat that the tops of the two hills were bridged level between

[010-057]by the meat pile. In the center of the pile the young woman planted a

[010-058]pole with a red flag. She then began to howl like a wolf, loudly.

[010-059]In a moment the earth seemed covered with wolves. They fell greedily on

[010-060]the meat pile and in a short time had eaten the last scrap.

[010-061]The young woman then joined her own people.

[010-062]Her husband wanted her to come and live with him again. For a long time

[010-063]she refused. However, at last they became reconciled.
