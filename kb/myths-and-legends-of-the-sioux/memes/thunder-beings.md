## Overview

The **Thunder Beings** (_Wakinyan_ in the Sioux language) were among the most powerful supernatural entities in Sioux cosmology — sky spirits who manifested as thunder and lightning. They could transform, destroy enemies, and ascend into the heavens amid "flashes of lightning and loud peals of" thunder (029-082). The Thunder Beings embodied raw cosmic power: capable of killing with lightning, yet also woven into the sacred order of the universe.

## In the Stories

### Manifestations of Power

Thunder and lightning were not impersonal forces but the actions of living beings. A powerful entity declared: "I am all-powerful lightning and thunder. I see all things and can kill" (029-068), revealing the Thunder Beings as conscious, willful agents capable of both perception and destruction. In battle, "flash upon flash of lightning burst forth from the" heavens (031-086), and "deafening peals of thunder followed each flash" (031-087) — the sky itself became a weapon.

### Transformation and Ascension

Mortals could be taken up by the Thunder Beings. A girl was transformed: "thunder the girl ascended and disappeared" (029-083), carried into the sky amid thunder and lightning. This ascension was not death but transfiguration — joining the ranks of the sky powers. The ability to "become like" an animal, bird, fish, "cloud, thunder and lightning, or in fact" any form (020-003) was a mark of supreme spiritual power, with thunder and lightning representing the highest tier of transformation.

### Thunder in the Landscape

Thunder marked the boundary between the ordinary and the sacred. The "rumblings of distant thunder could be heard" (031-084) before battle, building "nearer, and" nearer (031-084) as spiritual forces gathered. A warrior's approach was "as the rumble of distant thunder" (035-273), his power described in the language of the storm. Even the sound of thunder "would not alarm the babies" (031-065) of a certain people — suggesting that familiarity with the Thunder Beings' voice was a mark of spiritual fortitude.

> "I am the all-powerful lightning and thunder. I see all things and can
> kill at one stroke a whole tribe. When I make my voice heard the rocks
> shake loose and go rattling down the hillsides. The brave warriors
> cower shivering."
>
> (029-067–029-070)

## Cultural Significance

- Thunder Beings were conscious agents, not natural phenomena — they perceived, decided, and acted
- Lightning was their weapon, capable of killing enemies at will
- Transformation into thunder/lightning represented the apex of spiritual power
- Mortals could be taken up by the Thunder Beings in ascension narratives
- The rumble of thunder signaled the presence of the sacred in battle
- Thunder Beings bridged the sky and earth, representing cosmic order and its terrifying power
