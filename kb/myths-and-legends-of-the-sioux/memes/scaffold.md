## Overview

The **burial scaffold** in Sioux tradition is an elevated platform upon which the dead are placed. Unlike earth burial, the scaffold raises the body toward the sky, creating a liminal space between the world of the living and the spirit realm. In the stories, the scaffold is never merely a funeral structure — it is a site of ongoing transformation. A "clear spring burst forth from the side of a bank" beneath one scaffold (033-032), and the dead themselves speak from atop it: "I expect it is the dead girl's scaffold" (033-042). The scaffold marks where grief and wonder intersect.

## In the Stories

The most detailed scaffold account appears in Chapter 033. A girl dies and her people "erected a scaffold" (033-011) built "from five to seven" feet high with stout poles. Her body is placed "upon a scaffold" (003-032) where it remains. Later, "a hunter and his wife passed by the scaffold which held the dead girl" (033-029), and at that moment "from the scaffold a clear spring burst forth" (033-032) — water from death.

The dead girl herself speaks from the scaffold: the hunter, addressing her, asks about "that scaffold" (033-042), and "it bowed its head" (033-061) in acknowledgment — the scaffold becomes a place where the boundary between living and dead dissolves. Another hunter finds that vegetation grows richly around it (033-034), nourished by the spring. In another tale, the dead is simply placed "upon a scaffold" (015-006), with a knife in hand, set apart from the living world.

The scaffold also appears as a place of ongoing vigil: "the next day the hunter moved" (033-076) near the scaffold, returning to it, and in another scene a body rests on "the top of the newly erected scaffold" (033-120), suggesting the scaffold is freshly built for each death, a temporary architecture of mourning that becomes permanent through the stories told around it.

## Cultural Significance

- **Scaffold as threshold**: The raised dead occupy a space between earth and sky, neither fully departed nor fully present
- **Scaffold as life-giver**: Water springs from beneath the scaffold (033-032), death paradoxically generating new life
- **Scaffold as witness**: The dead on the scaffold observe the living, bow their heads, and remain active presences in the narrative
