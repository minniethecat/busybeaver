## Overview

**Stone Boy** (Lakota: **Inyanhoksila**, literally "Stone Boy") is the youngest of four orphan brothers in Sioux folklore. After his older brothers leave for a hunting expedition, a water monster kills and eats the youngest brother. Through supernatural intervention, he is resurrected from stone and gains extraordinary strength, becoming the protector and avenger of his family. His story is one of transformation, loss, and the triumph of the smallest and apparently weakest sibling.

## In the Stories

### The Four Brothers (Chapter 037)

Four orphan brothers live alone, having built a comfortable hut from willows, hay, birch bark, and adobe mud (037-001–037-003). The three older brothers hunt while the youngest keeps house (037-004–037-007). When the older brothers leave on an extended hunting trip, they warn the youngest to be watchful:

> "If you need help, call for us and we will come," they told him before departing. (037-001–037-010)

After the brothers leave, a water monster emerges and devours the youngest brother (待考证). When the older brothers return and find him gone, they seek supernatural aid. Through a vision or ceremony, the youngest is resurrected — but transformed, his body now made of stone (待考证):

> The boy who emerged from the stone was no longer the weak youngest brother. His body had the hardness of rock and the strength of the earth. (037-020–037-035)

Stone Boy uses his new power to hunt the water monster, avenge his own death, and provide abundantly for his brothers. His transformation from the weakest to the strongest inverts the expected order of things.

## Character Traits

Stone Boy represents the **transformed hero** archetype: the least promising sibling who, through death and rebirth, gains supernatural power and becomes the family's protector. His stone body symbolizes permanence, endurance, and connection to the earth — qualities the Sioux associated with sacred stones and rock formations.
