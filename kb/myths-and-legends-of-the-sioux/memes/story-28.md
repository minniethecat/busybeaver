[028-001]Near to a Chippewa village lay a large lake, and in this lake there

[028-002]lived an enormous turtle. This was no ordinary turtle, as he would often

[028-003]come out of his home in the lake and visit with his Indian neighbors.

[028-004]He paid the most of his visits to the head chief, and on these occasions

[028-005]would stay for hours, smoking and talking with him.

[028-006]The chief, seeing that the turtle was very smart and showed great

[028-007]wisdom in his talk, took a great fancy to him, and whenever any puzzling

[028-008]subject came up before the chief, he generally sent for Mr. Turtle to

[028-009]help him decide.

[028-010]One day there came a great misunderstanding between different parties of

[028-011]the tribe, and so excited became both sides that it threatened to cause

[028-012]bloodshed. The chief was unable to decide for either faction, so he

[028-013]said, “I will call Mr. Turtle. He will judge for you.”

[028-014]Sending for the turtle, the chief vacated his seat for the time being,

[028-015]until the turtle should hear both sides, and decide which was in the

[028-016]right. The turtle came, and taking the chief’s seat, listened very

[028-017]attentively to both sides, and thought long before he gave his decision.

[028-018]After thinking long and studying each side carefully, he came to the

[028-019]conclusion to decide in favor of both. This would not cause any hard

[028-020]feelings. So he gave them a lengthy speech and showed them where they

[028-021]were both in the right, and wound up by saying:

[028-022]“You are both in the right in some ways and wrong in others. Therefore,

[028-023]I will say that you both are equally in the right.”

[028-024]When they heard this decision, they saw that the turtle was right, and

[028-025]gave him a long cheer for the wisdom displayed by him. The whole tribe

[028-026]saw that had it not been for this wise decision there would have been a

[028-027]great shedding of blood in the tribe. So they voted him as their judge,

[028-028]and the chief, being so well pleased with him, gave to him his only

[028-029]daughter in marriage.

[028-030]The daughter of the chief was the most beautiful maiden of the Chippewa

[028-031]nation, and young men from other tribes traveled hundreds of miles for

[028-032]an opportunity to make love to her, and try to win her for a wife. It

[028-033]was all to no purpose. She would accept no one, only him whom her father

[028-034]would select for her. The turtle was very homely, but as he was prudent

[028-035]and wise, the father chose him, and she accepted him.

[028-036]The young men of the tribe were very jealous, but their jealousy was all

[028-037]to no purpose. She married the turtle. The young men would make sport of

[028-038]the chief’s son-in-law. They would say to him: “How did you come to have

[028-039]so flat a stomach?” The turtle answered them, saying:

[028-040]“My friends, had you been in my place, you too would have flat stomachs.

[028-041]I came by my flat stomach in this way: The Chippewas and Sioux had

[028-042]a great battle, and the Sioux, too numerous for the Chippewas, were

[028-043]killing them off so fast that they had to run for their lives. I was on

[028-044]the Chippewa side and some of the Sioux were pressing five of us, and

[028-045]were gaining on us very fast. Coming to some high grass, I threw myself

[028-046]down flat on my face, and pressed my stomach close to the ground, so

[028-047]the pursuers could not see me. They passed me and killed the four I was

[028-048]with. After they had gone back, I arose and lo! my stomach was as you

[028-049]see it now. So hard had I pressed to the ground that it would not assume

[028-050]its original shape again.”

[028-051]After he had explained the cause of his deformity to them, they said:

[028-052]“The Turtle is brave. We will bother him no more.” Shortly after this

[028-053]the Sioux made an attack upon the Chippewas, and every one deserted the

[028-054]village. The Turtle could not travel as fast as the rest and was left

[028-055]behind. It being an unusually hot day in the fall, the Turtle grew very

[028-056]thirsty and sleepy. Finally scenting water, he crawled towards the point

[028-057]from whence the scent came, and coming to a large lake jumped in and

[028-058]had a bath, after which he swam towards the center and dived down, and

[028-059]finding some fine large rocks at the bottom, he crawled in among them

[028-060]and fell asleep. He had his sleep out and arose to the top.

[028-061]Swimming to shore he found it was summer. He had slept all winter. The

[028-062]birds were singing, and the green grass and leaves gave forth a sweet

[028-063]odor.

[028-064]He crawled out and started out looking for the Chippewa camp. He came

[028-065]upon the camp several days after he had left his winter quarters, and

[028-066]going around in search of his wife, found her at the extreme edge of the

[028-067]village. She was nursing her baby, and as he asked to see it, she showed

[028-068]it to him. When he saw that it was a lovely baby and did not resemble

[028-069]him in any respect, he got angry and went off to a large lake, where he

[028-070]contented himself with catching flies and insects and living on seaweed

[028-071]the remainder of his life.
