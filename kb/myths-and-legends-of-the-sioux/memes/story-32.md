[032-001]Once upon a time there appeared from out of a large belt of timber a

[032-002]man attired in the fat of the buffalo. On his head he wore the honeycomb

[032-003]part of the stomach. To this was attached small pieces of fat. The fat

[032-004]which covered the stomach he wore as a cloak. The large intestines he

[032-005]wore as leggings, and the kidney fat as his moccasins.

[032-006]As he appeared he had the misfortune to meet “Unktomi” (spider) with

[032-007]his hundreds of starving children. Upon seeing the fat, Unktomi and his

[032-008]large family at once attacked the man, who, in order to save his life,

[032-009]started to run away, but so closely did Unktomi and his family pursue

[032-010]him that in order to make better time and also get a little better

[032-011]start, he threw off his head covering, which the Unktomi family hastily

[032-012]devoured, and were again closing in upon him. He then threw off his

[032-013]cloak and they devoured that, and were close upon him again, when he

[032-014]threw off his leggings. These were hastily eaten up, and, as they drew

[032-015]near to a lake, the man threw off the kidney fat, and, running to

[032-016]the edge of the lake, dived down into the water and kept beneath the

[032-017]surface, swimming to the opposite shore. After the Unktomi family had

[032-018]eaten the kidney fat they came to the water’s edge, and the grease was

[032-019]floating on the surface of the water which they lapped up, until there

[032-020]was not a grease spot left floating on the surface.

[032-021]The small morsels had only sharpened their appetites, and as they saw

[032-022]the man sitting on the opposite shore, Unktomi and his family proceeded

[032-023]around the lake and came upon two men sitting on the shore. Unktomi saw

[032-024]that the other man was “Wakapapi” (pounded beef). The family surrounded

[032-025]the two and Unktomi ordered them to fight. Fearing Unktomi and his

[032-026]large family, they at once commenced to fight and Pounded Meat was soon

[032-027]killed. The hungry family at once fell to eating him. So busy were they

[032-028]that none noticed the fat man sneak off and disappear.

[032-029]When they had finished the pounded beef man they looked around to fall

[032-030]upon the fat man, but nowhere could he be seen. Unktomi said, “I will

[032-031]track him and when I find him, I will return for you, so stay here and

[032-032]await my return.”

[032-033]He followed the fat man’s tracks until farther east on the shore of the

[032-034]lake he found the fat man in the act of skinning a deer, which he had

[032-035]killed. (He had held on to his bow and arrows when he jumped into the

[032-036]lake). “My,” said Unktomi, “this will make a fine meal for my hungry

[032-037]children. I will go after them, so hurry and cut the meat up into small

[032-038]pieces so they each can have a piece.”

[032-039]“All right, go ahead and get your family,” said Fat Man. During

[032-040]Unktomi’s absence, the fat man hurriedly cut the meat up into small

[032-041]pieces and carried them up into a tree that stood near to the shore.

[032-042]When he had carried it all up he threw sand and dirt upon the blood, and

[032-043]so left no trace of the deer.

[032-044]On the arrival of Unktomi and his family, no signs of the fat man or

[032-045]the deer could be found. They wandered about the spot looking for tracks

[032-046]which might lead them to where the fat man had cached the meat, as

[032-047]Unktomi said he could not have carried it very far. Now the fat man was

[032-048]up in the tree and sat watching them. The reflection of the tree was in

[032-049]the water, and some of the children going close to the shore, discovered

[032-050]it as they looked at the reflection. The fat man cut a piece of meat and

[032-051]extending it towards them, drew back his hand and put the meat into his

[032-052]mouth.

[032-053]“Come quick, father, here he is eating the meat,” said the children.

[032-054]Unktomi came and seeing the reflection, thought the fat man was down in

[032-055]the lake. “Wait, I will bring him up for you.” So saying, he dived down,

[032-056]but soon arose without anything. Again and again he tried, but could not

[032-057]reach the bottom. He told the children to gather rock for him. These

[032-058]he tied around his neck and body, and dived down for the last time. The

[032-059]last the children saw of their father was the bubbles which arose to the

[032-060]surface of the lake. The rocks being too heavy for him, held him fast

[032-061]to the bottom, and some hungry fish soon made a feast out of the body of

[032-062]poor “Unktomi.”
