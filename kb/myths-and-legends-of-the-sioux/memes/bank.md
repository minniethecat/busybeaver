## Overview

The **bank** in Sioux stories is more than a river's edge — it is a vertical landscape feature that shapes narrative action. Characters "threw himself down on the bank" (007-046) in exhaustion, "lay down on the bank and feigned to be dead" (011-006), and "wandered along a river bank" (011-003) in search of adventure. The bank conceals: a "door placed into the creek bank" (037-097) hides a dwelling within the earth. The bank challenges: warriors "jumped the coaster off a bank about four" (037-264) feet high, and travelers come upon a "very steep bank" (027-002) that demands attention. The bank is where the horizontal world of water meets the vertical world of earth.

## In the Stories

The bank appears across eight chapters as a significant narrative feature. In Chapter 007, a weary lover "threw himself down on the bank" (007-046) — the bank as resting place, a slope that receives the exhausted body.

In Chapter 011, a young man wanders "along a river bank, looking for" (011-003) game or discovery. He "lay down on the bank and feigned to be dead" (011-006) — the bank as stage for deception, a place to watch and wait.

Chapter 027 opens with a dramatic descent: a traveler suddenly "dropped off to a very steep bank" (027-002). Standing on "this bank" (027-003), "at the base he noticed a" (027-003) dwelling — the steep bank as threshold to hidden habitation.

Chapter 033 describes a miraculous spring: water "burst forth from the side of a bank" (033-032) beneath the burial scaffold, and "vegetation bordering its banks" (033-034) grows lush from the water's nourishment.

Chapter 037 contains the richest bank imagery. A character finds "high perpendicular banks" (037-093) along a creek and "for a long time he stood" (037-093) regarding them. He discovers "a door placed into the creek bank on the opposite side" (037-097) — a dwelling carved into the earth itself. Later, revelers "jumped the coaster off a bank about four" (037-264) feet, using the bank as a launch point. One prepares to "go over the cut bank and land in a heap at the bottom" (037-278), the "cut bank" a deliberate precipice. Standing "of the bank, and looking down" (037-290), a character sees others below — the bank as vantage and divider.

In Chapter 020, a rabbit travels "along the banks of a" (020-082) stream, and in Chapter 024, a chase stretches "for two miles, along the banks of a small" (024-049) river — the bank as path, a route that follows the water's course.

## Cultural Significance

- **Bank as refuge**: The weary throw themselves down on banks (007-046), the slope offering rest
- **Bank as dwelling**: Doors are cut into banks (037-097), homes hidden within the earth's edge
- **Bank as threshold**: Steep banks (027-002) mark narrative transitions — what lies below changes the story
- **Bank as source**: Springs burst from banks (033-032), water and life emerging from the cut earth
