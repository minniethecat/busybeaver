## Overview

**Adoption** in Sioux folklore is the mechanism by which the community absorbs the unattached. A rabbit orphan is taken in by "the little rabbit's adopted mother" (003-021), a youth is "adopted by the youth and took him into his" (022-067) household, and an outsider is "adopted into the family as their own" (030-023). Adoption crosses species boundaries — rabbits adopt other rabbits, but also humans — and transforms strangers into relatives with full social standing. Unlike temporary hospitality, adoption is permanent: the adopted person becomes kin.

## In the Stories

### The Orphan Rabbit

A mother rabbit is killed while hunting, and the orphaned baby is taken in: "the little rabbit's adopted mother" (003-021) cares for him. "As he lay in the tepee his adopted" (003-027) family surrounds him. The adopted mother is not a biological relative but takes on the full role — feeding, sheltering, and protecting the orphan. The adoption is complete and unquestioned.

### Rabbit Adopts a Youth

In a striking scene, "Rabbit at once adopted the youth and took him into his" (022-067) household. The adoption is immediate — "at once" — suggesting that recognition of need triggers instant incorporation. Rabbit, the trickster, here acts as a legitimate social agent, his adoption carrying full weight.

### The Adopted Sister Revealed

A more complex adoption occurs when "his adopted sister kept her face" (029-045) hidden. The adoption is later revealed to have a supernatural dimension: the "adopted as your sister was a sorceress" (029-072). Adoption, it turns out, can bring danger as well as blessing — the adopted person may not be what they seem. Another tale mentions "who had been taken as their adopted sister" (037-059), showing that sister-adoption was a recurring pattern.

### Full Incorporation

A character is "adopted into the family as their own" (030-023), the phrase "as their own" marking the completeness of the transformation. The adopted person is not a guest, not a dependent — they are family. After adoption, "his adopted mother had a" (030-361) role in his wedding, showing that adopted kin participate fully in major life events.

## Cultural Significance

- **Adoption as social weaving**: Adoption transforms unrelated individuals into kin, expanding the family and strengthening the social fabric
- **Immediacy of adoption**: "Rabbit at once adopted" — adoption decisions are made quickly, the need for kinship overriding deliberation
- **The adopted as potentially dangerous**: The sorceress adopted sister (029-072) reveals that incorporation carries risk — the outsider may bring supernatural danger
- **Full kinship**: The adopted are "as their own" (030-023), participating in weddings and family ceremonies — adoption is not partial membership
- **Cross-species adoption**: Rabbits adopt rabbits, but the pattern implies that adoption was a universal social mechanism, not limited by blood
