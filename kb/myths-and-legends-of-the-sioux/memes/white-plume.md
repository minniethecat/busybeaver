## Overview

**White Plume** is the hero of the eponymous tale (story-35), one of the longest and most richly detailed narratives in the Sioux collection. He is the son of a legendary archer known as "Dead Shot" and a beautiful woman named Beautiful Dove.

The boy was conferred the title of White Plume (035-078). His story spans his miraculous birth, childhood training, adventures in the spirit world, and his eventual triumph as a warrior.

## In the Stories

White Plume's story begins with his parents:

> "There once lived a young couple who were very happy. The young man was noted throughout the whole nation for his accuracy with the bow and arrow, and was given the title of 'Dead Shot,' or 'He who never misses his mark,' and the young woman, noted for her beauty, was named Beautiful Dove." (035-001–035-005)

When White Plume is born, his father crafts him special arrows — one painted red, one blue, and one yellow — and a fine hickory bow. His mother hangs them in a porcupine-quill quiver above his sleeping hammock, speaking to him of the great hunter he will become (035-010–035-015).

White Plume grows into a skilled marksman like his father. His adventures take him far from his village — he encounters supernatural beings, undergoes trials of courage, and ultimately proves himself as one of the great heroes of Sioux oral tradition.
