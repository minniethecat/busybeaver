# Elnora Comstock

## Overview

Elnora Comstock is the protagonist of Gene Stratton-Porter's 1909 novel *A Girl of [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]]*. She is a determined and intelligent young woman who overcomes poverty and her mother's emotional distance through her love of nature, particularly moth collecting in [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] Swamp. (001-001)

## Character

Elnora is characterized by her resilience, intelligence, and deep connection to the natural world. Despite growing up in poverty with a emotionally distant mother, she maintains her dignity and pursues her [[education-as-liberation|education]] with remarkable determination. (001-050)

Her love of music, particularly the violin, represents her artistic sensibility and connection to her deceased father. Her expertise in moth collecting demonstrates both scientific knowledge and practical survival skills. (004-100)

## Significance

Elnora represents the archetype of the self-made individual, achieving success through [[work-and-self-reliance|hard work]] and connection to nature rather than social privilege. Her story is also a meditation on the relationship between humans and the natural world, embodied in her connection to [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] Swamp. (001-010)
