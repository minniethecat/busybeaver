# Comstock Garden

## Description

The Comstock Garden occupies the space behind the Comstock cabin, serving as a transitional zone between the domestic world of [[comstock-cabin|the cabin]] and the wild expanse of [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] Swamp. It is bordered by bushes and low trees that shield it from the deeper swamp, and it features a gate that opens onto the path leading into [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]]. (006-338)

The garden is practical as well as beautiful — it provides vegetables and herbs for the Comstock table — but its primary significance in the novel is as a setting for encounters, conversations, and moments of transition. It is where [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] enters and leaves the domestic sphere, where Philip approaches [[comstock-cabin|the cabin]], and where the boundary between civilization and wilderness is most visible. (008-014)

> She followed the garden path to the gate and walked toward the woods. (012-043)

## Location

The garden lies directly behind the Comstock cabin, at the edge of the cleared land that separates the homestead from [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]]. It is bordered by "bushes and low trees" (008-014) that provide a natural screen. The back garden gate opens onto a path that leads into the deeper swamp, toward [[limberlost-pool|the pool]] and [[freckles-room|Freckles's Room]]. From the garden, [[limberlost-pool|the pool]] cannot be seen, but the awareness of its proximity is constant. (010-058)

The garden is also accessible from the road that runs past [[comstock-cabin|the cabin]], and [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] often enters through the back garden gate when returning from [[onabasha|Onabasha]] or [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]], using it as a private entrance that allows her to transition between her public and private worlds. (010-074)

## Significance

The garden represents a threshold space — neither fully inside the domestic world of [[comstock-cabin|the cabin]] nor fully immersed in the wild swamp. For [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]], crossing the garden marks her passage between her two identities: the schoolgirl who must navigate [[onabasha|Onabasha]] society and [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]] girl who collects moths and knows [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]]'s secrets. (012-043)

The garden is also a place of encounter between [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] and [[philip-ammon|Philip Ammon]]. It is here that Philip first approaches [[comstock-cabin|the cabin]], here that he waits for [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]], and here that many of their early conversations take place. The garden's liminal quality — between public and private, wild and tame — mirrors the developing relationship between [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] and Philip, which exists between the propriety of town society and the freedom of [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]]. (012-104)

For [[katharine-comstock|Katharine Comstock]], the garden is part of the domestic sphere she maintains in Robert's memory. Her care for the garden, like her care for [[comstock-cabin|the cabin]], is an expression of her love for the home they built together — even as [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]], which claimed Robert, presses against the garden's borders. (008-014)

## Key Scenes

- **The garden box**: [[wesley-sinton|Wesley Sinton]] places a box "back of the garden" for [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] to collect moths, a practical kindness that supports her collecting. (006-338)

- **[[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s return from school**: [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] enters through the back garden gate after her long walk from [[onabasha|Onabasha]], using the garden as her private threshold. (010-074)

- **Philip's approach**: Philip follows the garden path to the gate, drawn toward [[comstock-cabin|the cabin]] and toward [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]. (012-043)

- **The silent garden**: [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] is in the garden but does not respond when Philip calls, her silence revealing the emotional distance that still exists between them. (012-110)

- **Following the garden path**: [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] follows the garden path into [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]], transitioning from the domestic world to the wild where she moves most freely. (012-436)
