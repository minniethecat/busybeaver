# Comstock Attic

## Description

Elnora's attic bedroom is a long, narrow room that stretches the full length of the [[comstock-cabin|Comstock Cabin]], with a window at each end. It is reached by climbing the narrow stairs from [[comstock-cabin|the cabin]]'s main floor below. The room is spare but personal — [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] keeps a small table, a chair, and her growing collection of books and school things there. By night she lights candles for illumination, setting them on the little table as she reads and studies. (004-537)

> In her attic Elnora lighted two candles, set them on her little table, stacked the books, and put away the precious clothes. (004-537)

The attic's proportions create practical challenges. A stiff draught sweeps through when both windows are open, and the room is so long that light from a candle at one end does not reach the other. From the east window, [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] can see the stars, the sky, and the dark outline of [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]]'s trees spreading into the forest beyond. The cabin stands well back from the road, making the attic window a private vantage point that cannot be observed by passersby. (004-545)

The attic was too long, the light too near the other end, and [[comstock-cabin|the cabin]] stood much too far back from the road. (004-584)

## Location

The attic occupies the upper floor of the [[comstock-cabin|Comstock Cabin]], which sits on the northwest edge of [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] Swamp. The cabin is the nearest dwelling to [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]] and is isolated from the town of [[onabasha|Onabasha]] by a distance of roughly three miles. [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] has occupied the attic since she was a very small child, climbing the narrow stairs each day to her private world above. (003-005)

Then she climbed the narrow stairs to the attic she had occupied since a very small child. (003-005)

The attic's two windows offer contrasting views — the east window faces toward [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] and the stars, while the west window looks out toward the road and the land beyond [[comstock-cabin|the cabin]]. This dual orientation mirrors [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s own position between the wild world of [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]] and the civilized expectations of the town.

## Significance

The attic is [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s sole private domain in a cabin dominated by her mother's grief and silence. It is where she keeps the precious clothes purchased for her by Wesley and [[margaret-sinton|Margaret Sinton]], where she stores her growing library of books, and where she studies by candlelight into the night. The attic represents aspiration — a place of [[education-as-liberation|learning]] and self-improvement within the humble cabin walls. (004-581)

When [[pete-corson|Pete Corson]] watches from the road, trying to see into the lighted attic window, the scene underscores the attic as a vulnerable yet protected space — visible to the outside world but inaccessible, a sanctuary that holds [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s private hopes and treasures. The gleam of candlelight from the attic window becomes, in the narrative, a symbol of [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s quiet [[work-and-self-reliance|industry]] and the life of the mind flourishing within [[comstock-cabin|the cabin]]. (004-581)

The attic also serves as a spatial metaphor for [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s position within the Comstock household — she is above the ground floor of daily domestic life, in a liminal space between the constraints of home and the freedom represented by the open sky and [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] visible through her windows.

## Key Scenes

- **[[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s morning routine**: On the morning of her first day at [[onabasha-high-school|Onabasha High School]], [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] climbs to the attic to dress in the hated brown calico and heavy shoes. The attic is where she prepares herself to face the world. (003-005)

- **Treasures in the attic**: After returning from her first trip to [[onabasha|Onabasha]] with the Sintons, [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] lights two candles in her attic, carefully puts away her new school clothes, hangs the hat and umbrella, folds the raincoat, and spreads the new dress over a chair. She reverently opens one of her new books — a moment of private joy and hope. (004-537)

- **[[pete-corson|Pete Corson]]'s surveillance**: Having stolen [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s money from the moth case in [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]], [[pete-corson|Pete Corson]] sees the gleam from [[comstock-cabin|the cabin]] window and approaches, wanting to learn why [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s attic was lighted at that hour. He cannot see inside from the road, but the lighted window signals her presence and her studiousness to the outside world. (004-581)

- **The view from the east window**: [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] closes the east window against the stiff draught and pauses to look at the stars, the sky, and the dark outline of [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]]'s trees. From this vantage, she spots a tiny flash of light near her specimen case in [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]] below — a moment of quiet observation that connects her attic refuge to the wild world she loves. (004-545)
