Jimmy is the younger brother of Billy and Belle, one of the children taken in by Wesley and Margaret Sinton. Like his siblings, Jimmy is rescued from a life of poverty and brought into the warmth and stability of the Sinton farm at the edge of the Limberlost.

## Arrival at the Sinton Farm

Jimmy comes to the Sinton household as part of the group of children discovered by Wesley Sinton. Billy first mentions Jimmy when describing his family's situation — his mother has left, his father spent all the money on drink, "and Jimmy and Belle are both crying for breakfast" (005-213). Margaret Sinton, having lost her own children, embraced the opportunity to care for these neglected youngsters. Jimmy, along with Belle and Billy, became part of the Sinton family's daily life.

## Role in the Household

As a younger child in the Sinton household, Jimmy represents the quiet success of the Sintons' charitable intervention. When Billy receives food from Elnora, he saves portions for Jimmy and Belle, showing the sibling loyalty that defines the group (005-240). Later, Jimmy and Belle accompany Billy to visit Elnora and bring her a gift (006-167). While Billy's dramatic story arc — his mischief, his father's death, and his eventual redemption — occupies much of the narrative attention, Jimmy's presence underscores the broader impact of the Sintons' generosity on multiple young lives.

## Relationship with Siblings

Jimmy shares the Sinton household with his older brother Billy and sister Belle. Elnora mentions to her mother that she gave food to "a couple of boys named Jimmy and Billy" (006-360), noting their desperate situation. The sibling group provides a sense of family continuity within the Sinton home, even as each child follows their own path. Jimmy's bond with Belle is particularly close, as the two younger children navigate their new life together.
