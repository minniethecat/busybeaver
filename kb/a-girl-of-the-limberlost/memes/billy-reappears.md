# Billy Reappears After the Tragedy

## Summary

Billy, a ragged and hungry young boy, reappears during Elnora Comstock's school lunch hour in Chapter VI. The chapter subtitle — "Wherein Mrs. Comstock Indulges in 'Frills,' and Billy Reappears" — signals this event as a significant narrative thread. Billy, together with his siblings Jimmy and Belle, lives in extreme poverty. Elnora shares her lunch with the children, and Billy's raw, unfiltered behavior — both endearing and tempestuous — provides a window into the hardships of rural Indiana childhood. (006-207)

## Context

Elnora Comstock is attending high school in Onabasha when she encounters Billy during her lunch break. Billy is not a new character but one who "reappears" — the chapter title implies prior acquaintance, likely from the Limberlost community. He arrives with his siblings, all clearly undernourished and neglected. Their mother has abandoned them, leaving the children to fend for themselves. (006-167) (006-360)

Elnora, who herself grew up in poverty with a distant mother, immediately sympathizes with Billy and his siblings. She shares her lunch — bread, chicken, bologna, cake — and the children devour it with desperate gratitude. "They said it was the very best cake they ever tasted," Elnora tells her friend Ellen Brownlee. (006-360)

## Key Participants

**[[elnora-comstock|Elnora Comstock]]** — A high school girl who, despite her own hardships, generously shares her lunch with Billy and his siblings. She attempts to civilize Billy's manners, telling him, "I would like you much better if you were cleaner." (006-207)

**Billy** — A young boy living in desperate poverty, Billy is emotionally volatile — one moment eager and grateful, the next throwing dust in a fury. He reappears throughout the novel as a figure of both tragedy and resilience. (006-216) (006-455)

**Jimmy and Belle** — Billy's younger siblings, equally neglected. Belle, nearly ten, is described with a hint of Billy's protective pride: "Not even Belle, and she's most ten!" (006-360)

## Key Events

Billy spots Elnora eating her lunch and approaches eagerly. "How did you like the bologna?" he questions, his face lighting up — "Dawn broke beautifully over Billy's streaked face." (006-409) Elnora divides her milk, cake, and sandwiches among the children (006-421), but Billy is disappointed: "Aw, I thought they'd be meat!" (006-425)

When the food runs out, Billy's temper erupts. He screams at the others, throws handfuls of dust, and Elnora must physically restrain him: "Billy! I'll never give you another bite, if you throw dust on anyone!" (006-458) Billy immediately collapses into remorse, burying his fists in his eyes and sobbing into Elnora's skirt. (006-460)

## Aftermath

Elnora's encounter with Billy cements her awareness of the poverty surrounding the Limberlost. Billy reappears in later chapters — he eventually finds shelter with the Sintons and undergoes a moral transformation. His trajectory from feral child to disciplined young man forms one of the novel's subplots, paralleling Elnora's own journey from rural obscurity to accomplishment.
