## Overview

Sadie Reed is a student at [[onabasha-high-school]] who, along with other classmates, mocks [[elnora-comstock]]'s homemade [[social-class-and-appearance|clothing]] and hat on her first day of school. Her behavior represents the social cruelty [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] faces as a poor country girl among town students, making her a minor but emblematic antagonist in the early chapters.

## Character

Sadie Reed is depicted as one of the more forward participants in the group mockery of [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]. When [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s lunch box contents are displayed and ridiculed, Sadie Reed "glanced from the hat to the dress and smiled cynically" (005-324). When a boy's genuine laugh breaks the tension, "Sadie Reed blushed" (005-329), suggesting she had been participating in mean-spiritedness rather than genuine amusement. Her brief responses — "You needn't" and a short laugh — paint her as part of the social clique that judges by appearances (005-342).

> "Sadie Reed glanced from the hat to the dress and smiled cynically." (005-324)

## Relationships

Sadie Reed's role is defined entirely by her interaction with [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] in the schoolyard scene. She is part of the group of town girls who, by their dress and manners, highlight [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s social exclusion. Unlike some classmates who later become [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s friends, Sadie Reed remains in the narrative only as a representative of the initial hostility [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] must overcome.

## Key Events

**The Schoolyard Mockery**: When [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] arrives at school in her homemade [[social-class-and-appearance|clothing]] and the hat her mother made, Sadie Reed joins other students in examining and mocking her appearance. She cynically compares the hat to the dress, participating in the humiliation (005-322 to 005-342).

**The Turning Point**: When a boy laughs genuinely at the absurdity of the situation, the mood shifts — "Every one shouted" (005-329) — and Sadie Reed blushes, momentarily embarrassed by her own cruelty.

## Significance

Sadie Reed is a minor character who embodies the social barrier between [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] and the town students. Her brief appearance serves to establish the depth of [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s isolation and the cruelty she must endure. Unlike characters such as [[edith-carr]] or [[hart-henderson]], Sadie Reed does not have a redemptive arc; she remains a snapshot of the small-town social dynamics that [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] must transcend through her own merit and perseverance.
