# Limberlost Swamp

## Description

The Limberlost Swamp is a vast wetland in northeastern Indiana that serves as the primary setting for Gene Stratton-Porter's novel. It is home to countless species of birds, moths, and other wildlife, and plays a central role in the story as both a physical location and a symbolic space. (001-005)

## Location

The swamp is located in Indiana, near the fictional town of [[onabasha|Onabasha]]. In the early 20th century, [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] was a real wetland area that has since been largely drained for agriculture. The novel captures it in its primeval state. (001-010)

## Significance

The Limberlost represents multiple themes: it is a source of sustenance and income for [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] through moth collecting, a place of mystery and danger, and a symbol of the natural world's beauty and value. The swamp's eventual destruction mirrors themes of loss and change throughout the novel. (004-050)
