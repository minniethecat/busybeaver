# Billy

## Overview

Billy is a hungry, neglected boy associated with the Corson gang who first appears when [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] and the [[bird-woman|Bird Woman]] encounter him in [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]]. His story is one of the novel's most direct illustrations of the redemptive power of kindness: through [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s simple acts of feeding him and the Sintons' willingness to employ him, Billy transforms from a feral, swearing child into a contributing member of the [[community-support|community]]. (006-207)

## Character

### The Hungry Child

Billy's introduction establishes him as a child of neglect. His first words to [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] are practical and desperate, and his reaction to food reveals how rarely he eats:

> "I like the bologna?" questioned Billy eagerly. (006-406)

When [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] offers him a sandwich, dawn "broke beautifully over Billy's streaked face." (006-409) He catches the food with trembling hands, wolfing it down while [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] watches with pity.

When the food runs out, his disappointment is raw:

> "Thought they'd be meat!" lamented Billy. (006-425)

### The Foul-Mouthed Survivor

Billy's language reflects his upbringing among the Corson gang. When [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] corrects him for swearing, his response reveals his world:

> "Then Belle's no lady!" said Billy succinctly. (006-216)

His coarse speech is not malice but survival — the language of [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]]'s rough men who have raised him by neglect rather than care.

### The Reformed Boy

Under the influence of [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s kindness and the Sintons' practical charity — food, work, and shelter — Billy begins to change. His transformation is gradual but unmistakable, proving that even the most neglected child can find a better path when someone cares enough to feed him. (006-434)

## Key Events

1. **First Meeting in the Swamp** (006-207) — [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] encounters Billy in [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]], a ragged boy connected to the Corson gang.

2. **The Bologna Sandwich** (006-406) — [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] shares her lunch with Billy, and his eager, hungry response reveals the depth of his neglect.

3. **The Swearing Lesson** (006-216) — When Billy swears, [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] gently corrects him, and his blunt response — "Then Belle's no lady!" — shows both his rough upbringing and his capacity for honesty.

4. **Jimmy and Billy** (006-360) — Billy appears with another boy named Jimmy, establishing that he is part of a group of neglected swamp children.

5. **Working for the Sintons** (007-741) — [[margaret-sinton|Margaret Sinton]] and Wesley take Billy in, giving him food, work, and the stability he has never known.

## Significance

Billy serves as a powerful emblem of the novel's belief in the redemptive power of simple kindness. He arrives as a feral, swearing, perpetually hungry child of [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]]'s criminal fringe; through food, work, and gentle correction, he becomes proof that no child is beyond hope. (006-409)

His story also illuminates [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s character: she does not judge Billy by his language or his gang connections, but sees the hungry child beneath and feeds him without condition. (006-421)
