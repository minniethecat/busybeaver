## Overview

Duncan is a former resident of the Limberlost Swamp area who served as the intermediary between Freckles and Elnora Comstock. Before moving away, Duncan received Freckles's possessions for safekeeping and later passed them to Elnora, unknowingly setting her on the path to becoming a naturalist.

## Role in the Narrative

Duncan appears only in recollections and references, never as an active character. When he "moved away" (001-350), he left behind Freckles's possessions — books, a collecting case, and specimen boxes — which eventually came into Elnora's possession. Elnora later recalls that "Mr. Duncan moved away" and that before leaving, he gave her the items that once belonged to Freckles (003-159).

The transfer of Freckles's possessions through Duncan represents a pivotal, if indirect, moment in Elnora's development. The books and collecting equipment Duncan gave her (008-047) provided the foundation for her moth-collecting practice and her self-education in natural history.

## Significance

Duncan's role, though minor in page-time, is symbolically significant. He represents the invisible chain of kindness and mentorship that runs through the Limberlost community. Without Duncan's decision to pass Freckles's belongings to Elnora, she would have lacked the tools and knowledge to pursue her passion for moths. His brief appearance in the narrative underscores the novel's theme that even small acts of generosity can have far-reaching consequences.
