# Comstock Cabin

## Description

The Comstock Cabin is a simple log structure at the edge of [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] Swamp, with windows at both the east and west ends. It was built when [[robert-comstock|Robert Comstock]] cleared a space in the forest and erected the home where [[katharine-comstock|Katharine]] would live with him, and where [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] would be born and raised. The cabin is described as having multiple rooms — the kitchen and living area being the main gathering space, with small bedrooms at either end. (004-546)

The cabin's west window overlooks the well, and its positioning on the edge of [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]] means that the sounds of [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] — birdsong, wind through the trees, the violin's notes — are a constant presence within its walls. (004-277)

> It was a beautiful cabin at the northwest end of the swamp. (003-217)

## Location

The cabin sits on the northwest edge of [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] Swamp, approximately three miles from the town of [[onabasha|Onabasha]]. It is the nearest dwelling to [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]] itself, with the [[wesley-sinton|Wesley Sinton]] farm being the closest neighboring homestead. The cabin is accessible by a road that runs along the edge of [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]], and a path leads from the cabin into the deep interior of [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]]. (003-003)

[[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] walks the distance to [[onabasha-high-school|Onabasha High School]] each day, a journey that takes her from the wild world of [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]] to the ordered society of the town. The cabin's isolated location contributes to both [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s [[work-and-self-reliance|self-reliance]] and her social challenges. (001-064)

## Significance

The Comstock Cabin is the emotional center of the novel's domestic drama. Within its walls, [[katharine-comstock|Katharine Comstock]] grieves Robert for nearly two decades, refusing to engage with the world beyond her grief. It is where [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] grows up, where she practices the violin, where she keeps her [[moth-collecting|moth collection]] and Freckles's books, and where the central mother-daughter relationship unfolds. (005-082)

The cabin's isolation mirrors [[katharine-comstock|Katharine]]'s self-imposed isolation from the [[community-support|community]]. When [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] begins to succeed — bringing home money from moth sales, receiving visitors, playing the violin — the cabin transforms from a place of [[grief-and-loss|mourning]] to a place of life and accomplishment. By the novel's end, the cabin hosts concerts and welcomes visitors including [[philip-ammon|Philip Ammon]]. (021-280)

> Down at the Comstock cabin the violin played on until Elnora was too tired to lift the bow. (017-065)

## Key Scenes

- **[[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s first day of school preparation**: The cabin is described as she prepares for her first day at [[onabasha-high-school|Onabasha High School]], highlighting the poverty of her circumstances. (002-040)

- **[[wesley-sinton|Wesley Sinton]]'s visits**: Wesley comes to the cabin to check on the Comstock women, sitting by the fire and offering counsel to [[katharine-comstock|Katharine]] about [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s future. (001-296)

- **The violin at the cabin**: [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] practices her violin at the cabin, the music drifting out over [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]]. The violin becomes a symbol of beauty within the humble cabin's walls. (017-065)

- **The concert at the cabin**: [[philip-ammon|Philip Ammon]] gathers people at the Comstock cabin for a concert where [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] plays, a triumphant moment that signals the cabin's transformation from a place of grief to one of celebration. (021-280)

- **Philip's approach**: [[philip-ammon|Philip Ammon]] sees "the gleam from the window of the Comstock cabin" as he walks toward it through the darkness, drawn by the light and the promise of [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s company. (004-580)
