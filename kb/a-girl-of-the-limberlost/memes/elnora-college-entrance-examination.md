## Summary

Following her [[elnora-graduates-high-school|high school graduation]], [[elnora-comstock|Elnora Comstock]] prepares to take the entrance examinations for the college she has long aspired to attend. (017-240) Having spent four years ranking at the top of her [[education-as-liberation|high school]] classes while simultaneously [[work-and-self-reliance|earning]] money through moth collecting to fund her [[education-as-liberation|education]], [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] faces the exams with the same [[work-and-self-reliance|self-reliance]] and determination that have defined her journey. (010-325) The examinations represent the final gate between [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] and the higher [[education-as-liberation|education]] that she has pursued since her first humiliating day of [[education-as-liberation|high school]]. (017-260)

## Context

[[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s path to the college entrance examination was shaped by years of relentless effort. Throughout [[education-as-liberation|high school]], she maintained top academic standing while working multiple jobs: teaching [[nature-study|natural history]] in the lower grades, collecting and selling moth specimens to the [[bird-woman|Bird Woman]], and practising her violin. (010-177)

The financial strain of graduation ceremonies — photographs, gifts, dresses, and the class entertainment — nearly derailed her plans, as she contemplated breaking into her prized collection to cover the costs. (010-238) She persevered, however, and the graduation itself was a triumph, marked by the unexpected gift of a handsome dress from her mother and the presentation of [[freckles-room|Freckles's Room]] — the legacy of the former Limberlost guardian — at her graduation celebration. (017-200)

With [[education-as-liberation|high school]] behind her, the college entrance examinations loomed as the final academic obstacle. [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] knew she must have a diploma and pass the examinations to enter the college she had chosen, and she approached the challenge with characteristic thoroughness. (010-291)

## Preparation

Elnora's preparation for the examinations reflected the discipline she had cultivated over four years. She rose early each morning to complete her household duties — feeding the chickens, preparing breakfast, sweeping the cabin — before turning to her books. Her study materials included the textbooks she had purchased with her moth money and notes accumulated across years of top-ranking classwork. (017-245)

Unlike students who could afford tutors or preparatory academies, Elnora prepared entirely on her own, reviewing Latin, mathematics, natural sciences, and literature in the intervals between her work obligations. Her knowledge of natural history was reinforced by her daily practice of moth collecting, which gave her an experiential understanding of biology and ecology that textbook study alone could not provide. (017-250)

The financial anxiety that had shadowed her entire high school career — the fear of not having enough money for books, for tuition, for the incidentals of student life — added a layer of pressure to her examination preparation. She knew that passing the examinations was only the first step; she still needed to find the funds to actually attend. This dual burden, academic and financial, defined Elnora's approach to the college entrance examination: she prepared not merely to pass but to excel, knowing that scholarships might be her only path forward. (017-255)

## Key Participants

**[[elnora-comstock|Elnora Comstock]]** — Now a young woman on the verge of adulthood, [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] brings to the examinations the same fierce determination that carried her through [[education-as-liberation|high school]]. Her academic record — first in most of her classes and standing high in all branches — testifies to her intellectual capacity, yet her success was hard-won: she balanced study with the practical demands of [[work-and-self-reliance|earning]] a living. (010-325) The examination is a measure not only of her knowledge but of the self-discipline she developed through years of early mornings, late nights, and ceaseless work. (017-240)

**[[katharine-comstock|Katharine Comstock]]** — [[katharine-comstock|Mrs. Comstock]]'s attitude toward [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s college ambitions had softened considerably by this point. She no longer actively opposed [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s [[education-as-liberation|education]]al aspirations, though the financial realities of college remained a source of tension. (017-250) Her emotional transformation — from the woman who refused to pay for [[education-as-liberation|high school]] books to a mother who could at least accept her daughter's ambitions — formed the backdrop to [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s academic journey. (017-260)

## Aftermath

[[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] passed her entrance examinations successfully, a result that surprised no one who knew her academic record but which represented a profound personal victory nonetheless. (017-280) The way to college was now technically open, though the financial question remained unresolved. The summer that followed — the summer of [[philip-ammon|Philip Ammon]]'s convalescence and the Yellow Emperor's capture — would determine whether [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] could actually afford to attend. (017-300)

## Significance

The college entrance examination represents more than an academic milestone in [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s story — it is the culmination of a four-year struggle for self-determination through [[education-as-liberation|education]]. When [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] first entered [[onabasha-high-school|Onabasha High School]], she was a poorly dressed country girl mocked by her peers. (001-267) By the time she sat for her college examinations, she had transformed herself through work, intellect, and sheer persistence into a scholar worthy of higher [[education-as-liberation|education]]. (017-280)

The examination also serves as a narrative threshold: beyond it lies the wider world [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] has fought to reach — a world of college, travel, and social advancement that her mother could never have imagined for her. That [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] reaches this threshold through her own efforts, funded by moths collected in [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]], embodies the novel's central argument that character and determination can overcome the barriers of class and circumstance. (017-300)
