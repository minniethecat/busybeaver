# CHAPTER XXV

[025-001]WHEREIN PHILIP FINDS ELNORA, AND EDITH CARR OFFERS A YELLOW EMPEROR

[025-002]“Oh, I need my own violin,” cried [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]. “This one may be a thousand

[025-003]times more expensive, and much older than mine; but it wasn’t inspired

[025-004]and taught to sing by a man who knew how. It doesn’t know ‘beans,’ as

[025-005]mother would say, about [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]].”

[025-006]The guests in the O’More music-room laughed appreciatively.

[025-007]“Why don’t you write your mother to come for a visit and bring yours?”

[025-008]suggested [[freckles|Freckles]].

[025-009]“I did that three days ago,” acknowledged Elnora. “I am half expecting

[025-010]her on the noon boat. That is one reason why this violin grows worse

[025-011]every minute. There is nothing at all the matter with me.”

[025-012]“Splendid!” cried the Angel. “I’ve begged and begged her to do it. I

[025-013]know how anxious these mothers become. When did you send? What made

[025-014]you? Why didn’t you tell me?”

[025-015]“‘When?’ Three days ago. ‘What made me?’ You. ‘Why didn’t I tell you?’

[025-016]Because I can’t be sure in the least that she will come. Mother is the

[025-017]most individual person. She never does what every one expects she will.

[025-018]She may not come, and I didn’t want you to be disappointed.”

[025-019]“How did I make you?” asked the Angel.

[025-020]“Loving Alice. It made me realize that if you cared for your girl like

[025-021]that, with Mr. O’More and three other children, possibly my mother,

[025-022]with no one, might like to see me. I know I want to see her, and you

[025-023]had told me to so often, I just sent for her. Oh, I do hope she comes!

[025-024]I want her to see this lovely place.”

[025-025]“I have been wondering what you thought of Mackinac,” said Freckles.

[025-026]“Oh, it is a perfect picture, all of it! I should like to hang it on

[025-027]the wall, so I could see it whenever I wanted to; but it isn’t real, of

[025-028]course; it’s nothing but a picture.”

[025-029]“These people won’t agree with you,” smiled Freckles.

[025-030]“That isn’t necessary,” retorted Elnora. “They know this, and they love

[025-031]it; but you and I are acquainted with something different. The

[025-032]Limberlost is life. Here it is a carefully kept park. You motor, sail,

[025-033]and golf, all so secure and fine. But what I like is the excitement of

[025-034]choosing a path carefully, in the fear that the quagmire may reach out

[025-035]and suck me down; to go into [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]] naked-handed and wrest from it

[025-036]treasures that bring me books and [[social-class-and-appearance|clothing]], and I like enough of a

[025-037]fight for things that I always remember how I got them. I even enjoy

[025-038]seeing a canny old vulture eyeing me as if it were saying: ’Ware the

[025-039]sting of the rattler, lest I pick your bones as I did old Limber’s.’ I

[025-040]like sufficient danger to put an edge on life. This is so tame. I

[025-041]should have loved it when all the homes were cabins, and watchers for

[025-042]the stealthy Indian canoes patrolled the shores. You wait until mother

[025-043]comes, and if my violin isn’t angry with me for leaving it, to-night we

[025-044]shall sing you the Song of the Limberlost. You shall hear the big gold

[025-045]bees over the red, yellow, and purple flowers, bird song, wind talk,

[025-046]and the whispers of [[sleepy-snake-creek|Sleepy Snake Creek]], as it goes past you. You will

[025-047]know!” Elnora turned to Freckles.

[025-048]He nodded. “Who better?” he asked. “This is secure while the children

[025-049]are so small, but when they grow larger, we are going farther north,

[025-050]into real forest, where they can learn [[work-and-self-reliance|self-reliance]] and develop

[025-051]backbone.”

[025-052]Elnora laid away the violin. “Come along, children,” she said. “We must

[025-053]get at that backbone business at once. Let’s race to the playhouse.”

[025-063]hand right straight along, and my mother is running for a hot drink and

[025-064]a cushion. It’s a kind of a sick person, but they are going to make him
