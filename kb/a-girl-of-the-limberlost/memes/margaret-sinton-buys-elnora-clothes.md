# Margaret Sinton Buys Elnora Clothes

## Summary

After [[elnora-first-day-of-school|Elnora's disastrous first day of high school]], [[wesley-sinton|Wesley Sinton]] and his wife [[margaret-sinton|Margaret]] realise that [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s shabby country clothes were a major reason for her humiliation. (002-013) They drive her to [[onabasha|Onabasha]], where [[margaret-sinton|Margaret]] — following the example of the town high school girls — purchases dresses, hats, and other necessities, giving [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] the appearance of a proper student. This act of surrogate parenting marks the beginning of the Sintons' sustained care for [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] and symbolises the community support that enables her transformation. (002-051)

## Context

[[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s first day at [[onabasha-high-school|Onabasha High School]] ended in humiliation. Dressed in a faded calico dress that her mother [[katharine-comstock|Katharine Comstock]] had made her years earlier, she was mocked by the other students. (002-013) [[wesley-sinton|Wesley Sinton]], who has long watched over [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] from a distance, is disturbed by her treatment. "We ought to have known better than to let her go that way," he tells [[margaret-sinton|Margaret]]. "It wasn't only clothes; there were books, and entrance fees for out-of-town people, that she didn't know about." (002-014)

The Sintons, who have no children of their own, have long felt a parental tenderness toward [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]. [[wesley-sinton|Wesley]] tells [[margaret-sinton|Margaret]]: "Between us we must see that the girl is not too hard pressed about money, and that she is dressed so she is not ashamed." (002-023)

## Key Participants

**[[margaret-sinton|Margaret Sinton]]** — [[wesley-sinton|Wesley Sinton]]'s capable and warm-hearted wife. Though she modestly claims she is "not just what you call expert" at dressmaking, she rises to the occasion with resourcefulness and determination. In the [[brownlee-store|Brownlee store]], she boldly approaches the high school girls to ask about their clothing, winning them over with her warmth. (002-026) (002-065)

**[[wesley-sinton|Wesley Sinton]]** — The kind neighbour who drives [[margaret-sinton|Margaret]] and [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] to [[onabasha|Onabasha]]. Though he defers to [[margaret-sinton|Margaret]] on matters of women's clothing, his determination to help [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] drives the entire expedition. (002-045)

**[[elnora-comstock|Elnora Comstock]]** — The girl being outfitted. She has never had proper clothes and is overwhelmed by the Sintons' generosity. Her transformation from shabby to presentable is a turning point in her social acceptance.

**[[ellen-brownlee|Ellen Brownlee]]** — A high school girl whose father owns the dry goods store. She intervenes helpfully, directing [[margaret-sinton|Margaret]] to the best clerk and facilitating the purchases. (002-086) Her kindness establishes a pattern of community support that will recur throughout the novel.

## Key Events

### The Decision

[[wesley-sinton|Wesley Sinton]], troubled by [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s humiliating first day, wakes [[margaret-sinton|Margaret]] early. Over breakfast, he declares their responsibility: "Between us we must see that the girl is not too hard pressed about money, and that she is dressed so she is not ashamed." (002-023) [[margaret-sinton|Margaret]], though uncertain of her own dressmaking skills, agrees: "I could beat Kate Comstock all to pieces." (002-027)

### At the Dry Goods Store

In [[brownlee-store|the Brownlee dry goods store]], [[margaret-sinton|Margaret]] is momentarily daunted — until several high school girls enter. She approaches them directly, explaining her mission: "I know a girl who is coming to the high school, and she has no mother to buy her clothes... I want her to look just like you do." (002-068) The girls warm to her immediately, clustering around to describe their school wardrobes: gingham or linen dresses, bright and pretty, with felt hats for colder weather. (002-081)

### Ellen Brownlee's Help

[[ellen-brownlee|Ellen Brownlee]], one of the high school girls, reveals that her father owns the store. She personally guides [[margaret-sinton|Margaret]] to the best clerk and helps select the appropriate fabrics and styles. (002-086) The purchases transform [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s appearance, giving her for the first time clothing that allows her to walk into [[onabasha-high-school|the high school]] without shame.

## Aftermath

The clothing purchased at [[brownlee-store|the Brownlee store]] enables [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] to return to school with confidence. The Sintons' generosity establishes a pattern of surrogate parenting that will sustain [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] throughout her high school years. [[margaret-sinton|Margaret]], in particular, becomes a second mother to the girl — a role she embraces with warmth and practical good sense. This act of community charity also introduces [[ellen-brownlee|Ellen Brownlee]], who will become one of [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s closest friends.
