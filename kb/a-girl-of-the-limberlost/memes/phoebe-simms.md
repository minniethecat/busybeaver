## Overview

Phoebe Simms is a neighbor of the Comstock family who contributes to [[elnora-comstock]]'s early collecting efforts. Her gift of peacock wing feathers provides [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] with exotic specimens for her collection display and becomes a small but meaningful act of [[community-support|community]] kindness toward the struggling girl.

## Character

Phoebe Simms is portrayed through her actions rather than direct description. She gathers peacock feathers that fall in her orchard and gives them to [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] for her collection (005-335). When [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] uses these feathers to decorate her display, her mother [[katharine-comstock]] remarks that "the Almighty puts the same kind on Phoebe Simms's" peacocks as on the birds of distant lands (005-350), drawing a connection between the humble local woman and the broader natural world [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] studies.

> "They are from a living bird. Phoebe Simms gathers them in her orchard." (005-335)

## Relationships

Phoebe Simms is connected to [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] through the gift of peacock feathers, which [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] uses for both her collecting display and her school hat decoration. Her relationship to the Comstock family appears to be that of a kind neighbor — one of the few in the early chapters whose interaction with [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] is generous rather than judgmental.

## Key Events

**The Feather Gift**: Phoebe Simms gives [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] "beautiful peacock wing feathers" (002-192) that [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] treasures and incorporates into her collection display.

**The Orchard Gathering**: When [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] needs more peacock feathers for her hat decoration before school, she recalls that Phoebe Simms gathers them in her orchard, where peacocks shed their feathers naturally (005-335).

**Her Mother's Observation**: [[katharine-comstock|Katharine Comstock]]'s remark about the Almighty putting the same feathers "on Phoebe Simms's" peacocks as on birds worldwide (005-350) serves as a subtle moment of maternal wisdom — suggesting that beauty and value can be found close to home.

## Significance

Though Phoebe Simms appears only briefly, she represents the quieter strains of [[community-support|community]] support that sustain [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]. Unlike the dramatic interventions of characters like the [[bird-woman]] or [[wesley-sinton]], Phoebe's kindness is small and neighborly — a gift of feathers that costs nothing but gives [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] pride in her collection. Her presence also anchors one of the novel's thematic threads: that the natural wonders of [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] region rival those of exotic lands, and that local knowledge and local generosity have their own dignity.
