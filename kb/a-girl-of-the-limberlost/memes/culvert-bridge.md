## Description

The culvert bridge spans a deep ditch at the outskirts of [[onabasha|Onabasha]], on the road leading from the countryside into the city. "On the bridge crossing a deep culvert at the suburbs" [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] glances back toward [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] on her first day of [[education-as-liberation|high school]], the bridge marking the threshold between her rural home and the urban world of the school. (001-065)

The bridge is a modest structure — a crossing over a drainage culvert — but it becomes a recurring waypoint in [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s daily journey. She kneels on its planks, sets down her lunch box, and later sits on its edge with children she has rescued, sharing her meagre food. (001-231) The ditch below the bridge is deep enough to swallow a thrown lunch box, and the culvert itself offers shelter — though not always secure, as a tramp once discovered her hidden meal. (002-225)

## Location

The bridge lies on the main road between [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] countryside and [[onabasha|Onabasha]], just at the edge of the suburbs. (001-065) [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] crosses it each morning on her walk to [[onabasha-high-school|the high school]] and each afternoon on her way home. The location places it at a physical and symbolic border: behind lies [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]], [[comstock-cabin|the cabin]], and the rural poverty of her home life; ahead lies the city, the school, and the social world she struggles to enter.

The bridge is near enough to the [[bird-woman|Bird Woman]]'s collecting grounds that [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] encounters her there, and close enough to the poorer neighbourhoods of [[onabasha|Onabasha]] that half-starved children from the city's margins find their way to it.

## Significance

The culvert bridge functions as a site of transition and vulnerability in [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s daily life. It is the place where she first feels the pull of two worlds — looking back toward [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] with longing, then forward toward the city with apprehension. (001-065) Her habit of leaving her lunch box at the bridge rather than carrying it to school reveals her shame about her poverty and her desire to hide her rural origins.

The bridge is also a site of generosity and first contact. After rescuing a distressed boy at the bridge, [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] shares her lunch with him and his siblings, kneeling on the planks and dividing her scanty food. (005-175) This act of kindness, performed at the bridge, establishes her character as someone who gives even when she has little. It is at this same bridge that she first encounters the [[bird-woman|Bird Woman]], the collector who will transform her economic prospects by buying her moth specimens.

The bridge thus connects [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] not only to the city but to the key relationships that will shape her future: the [[bird-woman|Bird Woman]] as mentor and patron, and the city children as recipients of her compassion.

## Key Scenes

**First Morning Crossing** (001-065) — On her first day at the [[onabasha-high-school|Onabasha High School]], [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] pauses on the bridge to look back. Her glance toward [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] captures her reluctance to leave the known world, and her forward step into the city marks the beginning of her journey of self-transformation.

**The Stolen Lunch** (001-231–001-236) — Returning from her humiliating first day, [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] kneels on the bridge to retrieve her hidden lunch box, only to find it empty. She throws the box into the ditch and sits on the bridge, too stunned even to cry — a moment of quiet devastation at a place that has become a repository for her hidden pain.

**The Tramp and the Culvert** (002-225) — It is later revealed that [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s practice of hiding her lunch under the culvert was discovered by a passing tramp, who took the food. The episode underlines the vulnerability of her hiding place and her precarious existence at the edge of the city.

**Rescue at the Bridge** (005-175) — [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] reaches the bridge to find a small boy in distress. She rescues him, sits with him on the bridge, and divides her lunch among him and his siblings. The scene transforms the bridge from a site of [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s own suffering into a place where she becomes a protector and provider for others even more vulnerable than herself.
