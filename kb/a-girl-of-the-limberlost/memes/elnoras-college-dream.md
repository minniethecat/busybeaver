# Elnora's College Dream

## Overview

[[elnora-comstock|Elnora Comstock]]'s determination to attend college is one of the driving forces of *A Girl of [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]]*. From the moment she enters [[education-as-liberation|high school]], [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] sets her sights on higher [[education-as-liberation|education]] — a goal that for a poor girl from [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]]'s edge in early 20th-century Indiana represents not just [[education-as-liberation|learning]] but liberation. The college fund she builds, penny by penny from moth specimens, embodies her [[work-and-self-reliance|self-reliance]] and unwavering purpose. (005-122)

## The College Fund

### Origins

The idea of a college fund begins early in [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s [[education-as-liberation|high school]] career. [[wesley-sinton|Wesley Sinton]], the neighbor who watches over her, plants the seed:

> "You are going to start a college fund. You know you are going to college." (005-158)

[[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] embraces this vision with characteristic determination. Every moth she sells to the [[bird-woman|Bird Woman]], every specimen she prepares, contributes to a growing fund. The money is sacred — designated for her future, not to be touched for any present need. (005-160)

### Discipline and Sacrifice

The college fund demands discipline. [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] wears patched clothes, walks miles to school, and forgoes comforts that other girls take for granted. When the temptation to spend arises, she reminds herself of the larger goal:

> If she spent the college money she knew she could not replace it. (010-230)

She calculated that the fund needed to cover her "first year in college," and every decision was measured against that target. (010-177)

### The Race Against Time

A recurring anxiety in the novel is that [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] must start college "when the other girls were going" or risk never going at all. The social pressure to keep pace with her peers adds urgency:

> She wanted to start to college when the other girls were going. (010-238)

> If she did not start to college that fall she never would. (010-325)

## Obstacles

### Financial Hardship

Money is the primary obstacle. The ten dollars [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] needs for her first term's books is a sum that shocks her mother. Each year brings new expenses, and the college fund grows slowly, moth by moth. (001-500)

### Maternal Resistance

[[katharine-comstock|Katharine Comstock]] does not initially support [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s [[education-as-liberation|education]]al ambitions. The tension between [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s aspirations and her mother's fears creates some of the novel's most poignant moments:

> She was afraid to say college at that time. (011-428)

[[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] learns to navigate her mother's emotions, waiting until the right moment to discuss her plans:

> She thought it best not to talk college until she was of age. (011-432)

### Social Class

[[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s classmates at [[onabasha-high-school|Onabasha High School]] come from families that can afford college without sacrifice. The contrast between their path and hers highlights the class barrier she must overcome. When [[philip-ammon|Philip Ammon]]'s acquaintance [[edith-carr|Edith Carr]] is mentioned, the question arises: "Is [[edith-carr|Edith Carr]] a college girl?" — establishing college as a marker of [[social-class-and-appearance|social status]] as well as [[education-as-liberation|education]]. (015-264)

## The Turning Point

As [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s [[moth-collecting|moth collection]] grows in value and her reputation as a naturalist spreads, the college dream becomes tangible. [[philip-ammon|Philip Ammon]] recognizes the significance of her collection:

> "You are completing a collection which means college." (013-139)

The support of allies — the [[bird-woman|Bird Woman]], the Sintons, and eventually her mother — transforms the dream from solitary ambition to shared project:

> "I wish you might go to college this fall with the other girls," said Philip. (015-239)

## Significance

[[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s college dream represents the novel's core belief in self-improvement through [[education-as-liberation|education]] and [[work-and-self-reliance|hard work]]. It connects the various threads of her life — moth collecting, violin playing, academic study — into a unified vision of the future. The dream is never merely about a degree; it is about becoming the person she is capable of being. (005-122)

The college fund also serves as a measure of [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s character. Unlike characters who inherit wealth or social position, [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] earns her future through knowledge of the natural world — transforming [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]]'s wild riches into the currency of human advancement. (013-139)
