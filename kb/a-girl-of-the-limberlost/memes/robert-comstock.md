## Overview

Robert Comstock was the husband of [[katharine-comstock]] and the father of [[elnora-comstock]]. His death in [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] swamp before [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s birth is the defining tragedy of the Comstock family, leaving [[katharine-comstock|Katharine]] embittered and the family struggling financially. The circumstances of his death — an affair with [[elvira-carney]] and his drowning while trying to conceal his movements — cast a long shadow over the entire novel.

## Character

Robert Comstock never appears directly in the narrative, but his character is revealed through the recollections of others, particularly [[margaret-sinton]]'s confrontation with [[katharine-comstock]] in Chapter XII. He is portrayed as a man of weakness — "one of those men who couldn't trust himself" (012-152) — whose infidelity and subsequent death set in motion the events that define [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s childhood and [[katharine-comstock|Katharine]]'s bitterness.

> "He was one of those men who couldn't trust himself, and so no woman was safe with him." (012-152)

## Relationships

Robert Comstock married [[katharine-comstock|Katharine]] and together they purchased and cleared land near [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] swamp (010-434). His betrayal of [[katharine-comstock|Katharine]] with [[elvira-carney]] was the central rupture of their marriage. On the night of his death, he attempted to cross a dangerous section of the quagmire to conceal the direction he was coming from — returning from [[elvira-carney|Elvira Carney]]'s home (012-152). His drowning left [[katharine-comstock|Katharine]] a widow before [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] was born.

## Key Events

**Land Purchase and Clearing**: Robert and [[katharine-comstock|Katharine]] purchased land and started clearing it together, establishing [[comstock-cabin|the Comstock home]]stead (010-434).

**The Affair**: Robert began a relationship with [[elvira-carney|Elvira Carney]], a neighbor. On the night of his death, he had been visiting her and planned to attend a dance (012-152).

**Death in the Swamp**: Returning from [[elvira-carney|Elvira Carney]]'s home, Robert attempted the wrong side of the quagmire to hide his direction of travel. The swamp "sucked him down, slow and deliberate" (012-239), and he drowned in [[limberlost-pool|the pool]] where [[katharine-comstock|Katharine]] would later fear [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] might also be lost (011-226).

**Aftermath**: In a moment of catharsis, [[katharine-comstock|Katharine]] gathered every item that had belonged to Robert — except his gun and revolver — and threw them into [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]], symbolically freeing herself (012-297).

## Significance

Robert Comstock's moral failure and death are the catalyst for the novel's central conflict: [[katharine-comstock|Katharine]]'s emotional withdrawal and [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s struggle for [[education-as-liberation|education]] and identity. The [[secrets-and-truth|revelation]] of his infidelity in Chapter XII is a turning point for [[katharine-comstock|Katharine]], allowing her to redirect her long-held grief and bitterness toward its true source, and to see the injustice she has done to [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]. His memory serves as both wound and eventual release for the Comstock women.
