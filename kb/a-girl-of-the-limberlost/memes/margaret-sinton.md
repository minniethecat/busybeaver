# Margaret Sinton

## Overview

Margaret Sinton is [[wesley-sinton|Wesley Sinton]]'s wife and, alongside her husband, one of [[elnora-comstock|Elnora Comstock]]'s earliest and most steadfast allies. Where Wesley provides practical support and honest confrontation, Margaret offers the warmth, nurture, and maternal tenderness that [[katharine-comstock|Katharine Comstock]] withholds. Together, the Sintons form the supportive family [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] lacks at home. (002-121)

## Character

### The Warm-Hearted Neighbor

Margaret Sinton's character is defined by her instinctive kindness toward [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]. From the earliest chapters, she takes an active interest in the girl's welfare, worrying about her appearance, her [[education-as-liberation|education]], and her emotional well-being:

> "I bet you so too," said Margaret Sinton. "You undress right away and..." (004-231)

When [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] returns from school, it is Margaret who notices the details — her hair, her clothes, her spirit — and acts on what she sees. (002-121)

### The Dressmaker

One of Margaret's most tangible acts of love is helping [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] with her [[social-class-and-appearance|clothing]]. She ties ribbons, arranges peacock quills, and transforms [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s appearance:

> One after another Margaret Sinton tied the ribbons, creasing each with careful fingers until the hat was worthy of any girl in Onabasha. (004-346)

When she finishes, "Margaret Sinton cried out" with joy at the transformation she has helped create. (004-374)

### The Quiet Advocate

Unlike her husband's direct confrontations, Margaret Sinton's influence is gentler but equally persistent. She "compelled" [[katharine-comstock|Katharine Comstock]] to produce [[social-class-and-appearance|clothing]] for [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] (012-182) and consistently advises [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] toward choices that broaden her world. When [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] hesitates about opportunities that would take her beyond [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]], Margaret is the voice urging her forward. (012-651)

## Key Events

1. **The Hair Ribbons** (002-121) — Margaret takes an early interest in [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]], commenting on her hair and appearance, beginning her role as the girl's maternal advocate.

2. **The Dress and Hat** (004-231) — After [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s humiliating [[elnora-first-day-of-school|first day at school]], Margaret helps transform her [[social-class-and-appearance|clothing]], tying ribbons and arranging the peacock quill hat that gives [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] her first taste of confidence.

3. **The Doughnuts** (009-066) — Margaret's kitchen becomes a sanctuary where [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] finds comfort and "spicy doughnut not an hour from Margaret Sinton's frying."

4. **The Advice** (012-651) — When [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] must choose between staying with her mother and pursuing her future, Margaret advises her to go — a difficult counsel that prioritizes [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s growth over [[katharine-comstock|Katharine]]'s need.

5. **Helping with [[billy|Billy]]** (007-741) — Margaret participates in the [[community-support|community]] effort to rehabilitate [[billy|Billy]], demonstrating her kindness extends beyond the Comstock family.

## Significance

Margaret Sinton embodies the novel's theme that nurturing love can come from unexpected sources. She is not [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s mother by blood, but her consistent warmth, practical care, and emotional availability make her the mother [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] deserves. (004-346)

Where Wesley confronts, Margaret comforts; where he challenges [[katharine-comstock|Katharine]] directly, Margaret leads by example. Together, the Sintons demonstrate that [[community-support|community]] — not just biology — creates family, a central message of the novel. (012-182)
