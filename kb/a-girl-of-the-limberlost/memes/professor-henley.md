## Overview

Professor Henley is the superintendent of [[onabasha-high-school]] who plays a crucial role in [[elnora-comstock]]'s [[education-as-liberation|education]]. When [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] arrives at [[onabasha-high-school|the high school]] lacking tuition money, Professor Henley recognizes her unusual knowledge of [[nature-study|natural history]] and helps arrange for her to pay tuition through the sale of moth specimens. His intervention is one of the first instances of an adult outside the Comstock family supporting [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s ambitions.

## Character

Professor Henley is portrayed as observant, patient, and quietly generous. When [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] first enters his office, he "looked at her steadily" (001-207), taking in her unusual [[social-class-and-appearance|clothing]] and evident poverty but also her dignity. He is described as having "waited some time for this" — meaning a student with real knowledge of the natural world (003-107). Rather than dismissing [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] for her inability to pay, he sees an opportunity to acquire valuable specimens for the school's collection while giving a deserving student a chance.

> "That being the case," said Professor Henley, "it just occurred to me that you might be able to help me." (003-087)

## Relationships

Professor Henley serves as [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s academic patron at a critical moment. While not a warm father figure, he provides the institutional access that [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] needs. He communicates with [[wesley-sinton]] about [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s potential and later sends her a butterfly book when she is home ill (011-288), showing continued interest in her development. His recognition of [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s talent also reaches the [[bird-woman]], who becomes [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s most important mentor.

## Key Events

**First Meeting**: When [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] arrives without tuition money, Professor Henley examines her carefully and recognizes she is not an ordinary case (001-207).

**The Tuition Arrangement**: Rather than turning [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] away, Professor Henley proposes that she pay her tuition by contributing moth specimens to the school's [[nature-study|natural history]] collection, solving both her financial problem and the school's need for local specimens (003-087).

**Ongoing Support**: When [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] falls ill, Professor Henley sends her a butterfly book, demonstrating that his interest in her [[education-as-liberation|education]] extends beyond the initial transaction (011-288).

## Significance

Professor Henley represents the possibility of institutional kindness in a world that often fails [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]. His willingness to see past her poverty and recognize her knowledge is the first validation [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] receives outside her immediate circle. The tuition arrangement he proposes — exchanging natural specimens for [[education-as-liberation|education]] — becomes the model for [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s entire strategy of self-support through moth collecting. He is a small but pivotal figure whose decision opens the door to [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s future.
