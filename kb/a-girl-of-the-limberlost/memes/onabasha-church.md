# Onabasha Church

## Description

The Onabasha Church is described as "the big church" in town, a substantial stone or brick building that serves as the primary house of worship for the [[community-support|community]]. It is large enough to accommodate the town's population and has space outside where carriages can be hitched during services. (011-063)

The church is a center of social as well as religious life in [[onabasha|Onabasha]]. It is where the [[community-support|community]] gathers for Sunday services, where significant events are celebrated, and where the town's social hierarchies are on display. For [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]], attending church at [[onabasha|Onabasha]] represents her gradual acceptance into town society. (011-035)

## Location

The church is located in [[onabasha|Onabasha]], within walking distance of the commercial district. It is a prominent building, visible and recognizable — [[wesley-sinton|Wesley Sinton]] waits "before the big church" when he comes to town, using it as a landmark and meeting point. (011-063)

The church is accessible by the roads that connect [[onabasha|Onabasha]] to the surrounding farms, and families from throughout the area travel there for Sunday services. For the Comstock family, attending church requires a walk or carriage ride of several miles from [[comstock-cabin|the cabin]]. (007-428)

## Significance

The church represents the organized [[community-support|community]] life from which [[katharine-comstock|Katharine Comstock]] has withdrawn. [[margaret-sinton|Margaret Sinton]] observes that [[katharine-comstock|Katharine]] has "always done the church-going and Bible reading" for the family — meaning that [[katharine-comstock|Katharine]], despite her isolation, maintains some connection to religious observance, or at least did before her grief consumed her. (007-428)

For [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]], the church becomes a venue for her musical talents. Her violin playing, developed through years of solitary practice at [[comstock-cabin|the cabin]], finds a public audience at church events. The church is where [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s gifts are recognized by the [[community-support|community]], contributing to her growing reputation and acceptance in [[onabasha|Onabasha]]. (011-035)

The church also serves as a meeting place for the [[community-support|community]]. [[wesley-sinton|Wesley Sinton]] waits before it with his team, and the gatherings before and after services are opportunities for the exchange of news and the building of social bonds. (011-063)
