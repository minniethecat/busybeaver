# Sinton Farm

## Description

The Sinton Farm is the homestead of Wesley and [[margaret-sinton|Margaret Sinton]], the nearest neighbors to the Comstock family. The Sintons are prosperous farmers who live a comfortable country life — their home is well-kept, with a large kitchen where Margaret bakes her famous doughnuts and a living area where neighbors gather. (001-296)

The farm is described as being "in the black of the rich loam above [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]]," suggesting fertile farmland that contrasts with the wild, untamed swamp below. It lies within walking distance of the Comstock cabin, close enough that Wesley can check on the Comstock women regularly. (004-003)

> Wesley, the nearest neighbour, seated himself beside the fireplace. (001-296)

## Location

The Sinton Farm sits on high ground above [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] Swamp, a short walk from the Comstock cabin. The Sintons are the Comstocks' closest neighbors in an otherwise sparsely populated area. The farm is accessible by the same road that leads past [[comstock-cabin|the cabin]] and into [[onabasha|Onabasha]]. (004-003)

## Significance

The Sinton Farm represents everything the [[comstock-cabin|Comstock Cabin]] is not — warmth, welcome, [[community-support|community]], and practical prosperity. Where [[katharine-comstock|Katharine Comstock]] has withdrawn into grief, [[margaret-sinton|Margaret Sinton]] keeps an open door and a ready kitchen. The farm serves as a second home for [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] throughout her childhood, and the Sintons' steady kindness provides a counterbalance to [[katharine-comstock|Katharine]]'s emotional withdrawal. (001-296)

[[margaret-sinton|Margaret Sinton]]'s kitchen is particularly significant — her doughnuts, her willingness to help with [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s clothes, and her open-hearted maternal warmth make the Sinton Farm a place of emotional refuge. [[wesley-sinton|Wesley Sinton]]'s steady support for [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s [[education-as-liberation|education]] and moth collecting further cements the farm's role as a base of practical assistance. (004-231)

As the novel progresses, the Sinton Farm also becomes a place where the [[community-support|community]] gathers to discuss and celebrate [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s accomplishments. The Sintons are among the first to recognize [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s worth, and their farm is where many plans for her support are hatched. (021-221)

## Key Scenes

- **[[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s first day**: [[wesley-sinton|Wesley Sinton]] visits the Comstock cabin after [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s disastrous first day of school, then returns to the farm to confer with Margaret about how to help. (001-296)

- **[[margaret-sinton|Margaret Sinton]]'s assistance**: Margaret helps prepare [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s clothes and appearance for school, tying ribbons and arranging her hair at the farm. (004-231)

- **The gathering**: Commotion at the Sinton home as Wesley and Margaret prepare for an event related to [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s rising fortunes. (021-221)

- **Margaret's counsel**: [[margaret-sinton|Margaret Sinton]] offers her perspective on [[philip-ammon|Philip Ammon]]'s [[love-and-courtship|courtship]] of [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]], her kitchen providing the setting for important conversations about love and marriage. (007-719)
