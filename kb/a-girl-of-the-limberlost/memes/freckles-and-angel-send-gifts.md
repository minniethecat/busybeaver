# Freckles and the Angel Send Graduation Gifts

## Summary

On the day of Elnora Comstock's high school graduation, the Bird Woman retrieves a long-stored trunk from the cedar closet — the "Swamp Angel's stuff" — and transforms its contents into Elnora's graduation attire. (011-112) The Angel's clothing, worn at parties when she was sixteen, is altered to fit Elnora: a white silk waist, a soft white dress, delicate accessories. (011-124) (011-231) Beyond the clothing, Freckles and the Angel send a curly-maple dressing table and fine toilet articles, ensuring that "no girl of the class had finer gifts." (011-286) The gifts connect Elnora's academic triumph to the legacy of the Limberlost's former guardians.

## Context

Elnora has completed four years of high school against considerable odds — poverty, her mother's initial indifference, and the social cruelties of Onabasha's wealthier students. Commencement approaches, and the question of what she will wear weighs heavily. Her mother, Katharine Comstock, has finally made her a new dress, but its quality is uncertain. (011-023) (011-048)

The Bird Woman — Elnora's mentor and employer, a naturalist who buys her moth specimens — remembers the trunk sent years ago by the Angel (wife of Freckles, the legendary one-handed Limberlost guardian). "I want that trunk with the Swamp Angel's stuff in it, from the cedar closet," she tells Elnora. (011-112) Together they drag the heavy trunk to Elnora's room and begin unpacking its treasures.

## Key Participants

**[[elnora-comstock|Elnora Comstock]]** — The graduating senior, overwhelmed by the generosity of people she has never met. The gifts affirm that her connection to the Limberlost extends far beyond the swamp's boundaries.

**Freckles (Terence O'More)** — The former Limberlost guardian who lost a hand protecting the swamp's timber. Now married and living in the north, he sends a curly-maple dressing table as a graduation gift. (011-283)

**The Angel (Mrs. Terence O'More)** — Freckles's wife, whose girlhood wardrobe — preserved for years — becomes Elnora's graduation finery. She also sends "wonderful toilet articles." (011-284)

**The [[bird-woman|Bird Woman]]** — Elnora's naturalist mentor, who facilitates the gift-giving and adds her own embroidered linen cover and silver flower vase. (011-285)

## Key Events

The Bird Woman and Elnora unpack the Angel's trunk. Out comes a white silk waist with a low neck and elbow sleeves — "short in the waist, for the Angel had worn it at a party when she was sixteen." (011-127) The Bird Woman loosens the sleeves and arranges them in puffs. A soft white dress follows, requiring only minor alteration to serve for Commencement. (011-231)

The Bird Woman had written to ask the Angel's permission, and "the reply had been to give the girl the contents of the chest." (011-239) When she tells Elnora, tears fill the girl's eyes. Later, an expressed crate arrives with the curly-maple dressing table from Freckles. (011-283) The Angel's toilet articles fill its drawers, and the Bird Woman adds her own touches. (011-285)

## Aftermath

Elnora attends Commencement dressed in the Angel's clothes, carrying the blessing of the Limberlost's most beloved figures. The gifts are more than material — they represent the passing of a torch from one generation of the swamp's children to the next. The event foreshadows Elnora's eventual meeting with Freckles and the Angel on Mackinac Island (Chapter XXIII), where the bond between them becomes deeply personal.
