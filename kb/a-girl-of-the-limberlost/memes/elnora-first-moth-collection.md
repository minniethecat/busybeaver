## Summary

[[elnora-comstock|Elnora Comstock]] presents her first collection of moth specimens to the [[bird-woman|Bird Woman]], who identifies the rarest specimen as a valuable Saturniid moth worth a hundred dollars according to her price list. (003-060) [[bird-woman|The Bird Woman]] agrees to purchase the entire collection, providing [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] with her first substantial income. This transaction launches [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s career as a specimen collector, establishing the economic foundation that would fund her [[education-as-liberation|high school]] [[education-as-liberation|education]] and her nascent [[elnoras-college-dream|college fund]]. (003-138)

## Context

[[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] had been [[moth-collecting|collecting moths]] and butterflies in [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] since childhood, building her knowledge from books and supplies left to her by Freckles through Duncan. (003-047) For years, the collection grew in secret — [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] tending caterpillars, waiting for cocoons to hatch, and carefully mounting the specimens in Freckles's old storage case deep in [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]]. (003-055)

The turn toward selling her collection came out of financial desperation. On [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s first day at [[onabasha-high-school|Onabasha High School]], she was humiliated by her country appearance and the discovery that she would need money for books and tuition. (001-267) Remembering the advice Freckles had left — to stick to [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]] and be brave — [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] recognized that her [[moth-collecting|moth collection]] could be the solution to her financial needs. (003-060)

## Meeting the Bird Woman

The morning after her humiliating first day of school, Elnora rose at four o'clock to shell beans, feed the animals, and prepare breakfast before walking to Onabasha. She went to the bank first, where the cashier recognized "specimens" in her pasteboard box and directed her to the Bird Woman. The maid at the Bird Woman's home had standing orders: anyone with moths was to be brought in immediately. (003-060)

> "Is the Bird Woman at home?" she asked of the maid. "She is at lunch," was the answer. "Please ask her if she will see a girl from the Limberlost about some moths?" inquired Elnora. "I never need ask, if it's moths," laughed the girl. "Orders are to bring any one with specimens right in. Come this way."

The Bird Woman received Elnora warmly, inviting her to share lunch — sandwiches, salad, and hot chocolate unlike anything Elnora had ever tasted. When Elnora hesitantly described her collection — over two hundred eggs, about a hundred perfect specimens with intact wings, legs, and antennae — the Bird Woman's response was electrifying. (003-062)

> "Young woman, that's the rarest moth in America," said the Bird Woman solemnly. "If you have a hundred of them, they are worth a hundred dollars according to my list. I can use all that are not damaged."

Elnora explained that the collection was stored in Freckles's old case in the Limberlost, and that Duncan had given her the key, the books, and Freckles's message: "stick to the swamp and be brave, and my hour would come." The Bird Woman arranged to drive out that afternoon with specimen boxes and a price list to appraise the full collection. When she asked if Elnora was free to sell, Elnora answered: "They are mine. No one but God knows I have them." (003-065)

## Key Participants

**[[elnora-comstock|Elnora Comstock]]** — A determined sixteen-year-old who has spent years cultivating her [[moth-collecting|moth collection]] in secret. Her knowledge of [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]]'s species is self-taught, supported by the reference books Freckles left behind. (003-055) The sale represents her first act of economic agency, transforming her solitary passion into a livelihood. (003-138)

**[[bird-woman|The Bird Woman]]** — A visiting naturalist and author who purchases moth specimens for her scientific publications. She recognizes the quality and rarity of [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s collection immediately, exclaiming that the specimen [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] shows her is "the rarest moth in America." (003-060) [[bird-woman|The Bird Woman]] becomes [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s most important patron and mentor, providing not only income but also validation of [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s scientific competence. (003-138)

**Duncan** — The Scottish caretaker who, following Freckles's instructions, gave [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] the books, equipment, and storage case that made her collection possible. His act of passing on Freckles's legacy connects [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] to the previous generation's history in [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]]. (003-047)

## Aftermath

The sale of the first collection had immediate and lasting effects. With the [[bird-woman|Bird Woman]]'s payment, [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] could afford her schoolbooks and tuition, ending the humiliation of being unable to pay her way at [[onabasha-high-school|Onabasha High School]]. (004-075) [[bird-woman|The Bird Woman]] became a recurring buyer, providing [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] with a steady income stream that funded four years of [[education-as-liberation|high school]] and the beginning of a [[elnoras-college-dream|college fund]]. (005-122)

The transaction also gave [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] social validation. [[bird-woman|The Bird Woman]], a figure of scientific authority, treated [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] as a competent naturalist rather than as a poor country girl, offering [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] her first experience of being valued for her knowledge rather than judged by her appearance. (003-138)

## Significance

[[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s first [[moth-collecting|moth collection]] sale marks the novel's foundational moment of economic self-determination. At sixteen, without family wealth or social connections, [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] converts her knowledge of the natural world into the means to pursue [[education-as-liberation|education]] — the central aspiration of her young life. (005-122)

The event establishes the novel's running motif of transformation: caterpillars become moths, a poor country girl becomes a scholar, and [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] — a site of her father's death — becomes the source of [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s freedom. The exchange between [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] and the [[bird-woman|Bird Woman]] also introduces one of the novel's key relationships, a mentoring bond between women of different generations and social stations founded on shared scientific passion. (004-080)
