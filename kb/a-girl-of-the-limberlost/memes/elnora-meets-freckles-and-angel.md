# Elnora Meets Freckles and the Angel

## Summary

Following [[edith-carr-visits-limberlost|Edith Carr's visit]] to [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]], [[elnora-comstock|Elnora Comstock]] makes the difficult decision to withdraw and give [[philip-ammon|Philip Ammon]] the space to freely choose between herself and [[edith-carr|Edith Carr]]. (023-034) She travels to Mackinac Island, where she meets Terence O'More — known as Freckles, the legendary one-handed former guardian of [[limberlost-swamp|the Limberlost]] — and his wife, the Angel. Their meeting unites two generations of Limberlost figures and connects the novel's world with Gene Stratton-Porter's earlier work, *Freckles*. (023-091)

## Context

After [[edith-carr-visits-limberlost|Edith Carr's dramatic appearance]] at [[comstock-cabin|the Comstock cabin]], [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] concludes that she must remove herself from the situation. Edith has claimed that if she had Philip in her presence for one hour, he would return to her. (023-018) Rather than compete, [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] decides to let Philip discover his own heart. "She must be taught that Phil is absolutely free, and yet he will not go to her," [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] tells her mother. (023-039)

[[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] recalls the connection between [[limberlost-swamp|the Limberlost]] and Freckles — a young man who once guarded the swamp's timber, lost a hand in the line of duty, and later married a woman known simply as "the Angel." Duncan, the old Scotchman who had been Freckles's companion, had given [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] Freckles's books and moth-collecting equipment, forging a link between them before they ever met. (023-086)

## Key Participants

**[[elnora-comstock|Elnora Comstock]]** — The girl of [[limberlost-as-symbol|the Limberlost]] who travels north seeking refuge from the emotional turmoil of the romantic triangle. Her connection to Freckles is established before their meeting: she uses his collecting case and has read his books. (023-086)

**Freckles (Terence O'More)** — The former guardian of [[limberlost-swamp|the Limberlost]], a man whose courage and persistence in the face of hardship made him legendary. Now married to the Angel and a father of four, he resides on Mackinac Island. He immediately embraces [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] upon learning she is from the swamp he loved: "Any boy or girl who ever suffered one pang in the Limberlost has a claim to the best drop of blood in my heart." (023-091)

**The Angel (Mrs. Terence O'More)** — Freckles's wife, a woman of exquisite beauty and warm heart. She welcomes [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] into their home, listens to her troubles, and becomes both confidante and advisor. Her laughter at the situation — at Freckles's insistence that [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s flight will only make [[philip-ammon|Philip]] pursue her more ardently — helps [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] see the humour in her predicament. (023-112) (023-188)

## Key Events

### The Arrival at Mackinac

[[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] and Freckles travel together by train, talking of [[limberlost-swamp|the swamp]], [[bird-woman|the Bird Woman]]'s work, and [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s nature lectures in the schools. In the evening they cross the Straits of Mackinac by boat under sheets of white moonlight. (023-096) On the dock, the Angel sweeps into Freckles's arms, exclaiming at his month-long absence — though Freckles gently corrects her: "Four days, Angel, only four days by the clock." (023-112)

### Confiding in the Angel

That night, [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] tells the Angel the full story of [[philip-ammon|Philip]], [[edith-carr|Edith]], and her decision to leave. The Angel is astonished: "You insane creature! How crazy of you to leave him to her!" (023-133) Yet she also acknowledges the wisdom in [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s choice: "Really it is the only way. I can see that." (023-135)

### Freckles's Verdict

Later the Angel returns to [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s room, having told Freckles the story. Freckles finds it deeply amusing: "He thinks it's the funniest thing that ever happened. He says that to run away from Mr. Ammon, when you had made him no promise at all, when he wasn't sure of you, won't send him home to her; it will set him hunting you!" (023-191) Freckles declares that [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]] could not have chosen a course more certain to bind Philip to her. The Angel, catching the humour, rocks on the bedside laughing. (023-188)

## Aftermath

[[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]'s stay with Freckles and the Angel on Mackinac Island provides her with the sanctuary she needs. Freckles's prediction proves correct — rather than returning to [[edith-carr|Edith]], [[philip-ammon|Philip]] does indeed come searching for [[elnora-comstock|Elnora]]. The meeting bridges the worlds of two Stratton-Porter novels and establishes that the legacy of the Limberlost — its hardships and its beauty — binds together all who have known it.
