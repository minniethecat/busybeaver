# 第一幕 第二场

[001-001] state in the Castle.

[001-002] Enter Claudius King of Denmark, Gertrude the

[001-003] Queen, Hamlet, Polonius, Laertes, Voltemand,

[001-004] Cornelius, Lords and Attendant.

[001-005] KING.

[001-006] Though yet of Hamlet our dear brother’s death

[001-007] The memory be green, and that it us befitted

[001-008] To bear our hearts in grief, and our whole kingdom

[001-009] To be contracted in one brow of woe;

[001-010] Yet so far hath discretion fought with nature

[001-011] That we with wisest sorrow think on him,

[001-012] Together with remembrance of ourselves.

[001-013] Therefore our sometime sister, now our queen,

[001-014] Th’imperial jointress to this warlike state,

[001-015] Have we, as ’twere with a defeated joy,

[001-016] With one auspicious and one dropping eye,

[001-017] With mirth in funeral, and with dirge in marriage,

[001-018] In equal scale weighing delight and dole,

[001-019] Taken to wife; nor have we herein barr’d

[001-020] Your better wisdoms, which have freely gone

[001-021] With this affair along. For all, our thanks.

[001-022] Now follows, that you know young Fortinbras,

[001-023] Holding a weak supposal of our worth,

[001-024] Or thinking by our late dear brother’s death

[001-025] Our state to be disjoint and out of frame,

[001-026] Colleagued with this dream of his advantage,

[001-027] He hath not fail’d to pester us with message,

[001-028] Importing the surrender of those lands

[001-029] Lost by his father, with all bonds of law,

[001-030] To our most valiant brother. So much for him.

[001-031] Now for ourself and for this time of meeting:

[001-032] Thus much the business is: we have here writ

[001-033] To Norway, uncle of young Fortinbras,

[001-034] Who, impotent and bed-rid, scarcely hears

[001-035] Of this his nephew’s purpose, to suppress

[001-036] His further gait herein; in that the levies,

[001-037] The lists, and full proportions are all made

[001-038] Out of his subject: and we here dispatch

[001-039] You, good Cornelius, and you, Voltemand,

[001-040] For bearers of this greeting to old Norway,

[001-041] Giving to you no further personal power

[001-042] To business with the King, more than the scope

[001-043] Of these dilated articles allow.

[001-044] Farewell; and let your haste commend your duty.

[001-045] CORNELIUS and VOLTEMAND.

[001-046] In that, and all things, will we show our duty.

[001-047] KING.

[001-048] We doubt it nothing: heartily farewell.

[001-049] [Exeunt Voltemand and Cornelius.]

[001-050] And now, Laertes, what’s the news with you?

[001-051] You told us of some suit. What is’t, Laertes?

[001-052] You cannot speak of reason to the Dane,

[001-053] And lose your voice. What wouldst thou beg, Laertes,

[001-054] That shall not be my offer, not thy asking?

[001-055] The head is not more native to the heart,

[001-056] The hand more instrumental to the mouth,

[001-057] Than is the throne of Denmark to thy father.

[001-058] What wouldst thou have, Laertes?

[001-059] LAERTES.

[001-060] Dread my lord,

[001-061] Your leave and favour to return to France,

[001-062] From whence though willingly I came to Denmark

[001-063] To show my duty in your coronation;

[001-064] Yet now I must confess, that duty done,

[001-065] My thoughts and wishes bend again toward France,

[001-066] And bow them to your gracious leave and pardon.

[001-067] KING.

[001-068] Have you your father’s leave? What says Polonius?

[001-069] POLONIUS.

[001-070] He hath, my lord, wrung from me my slow leave

[001-071] By laboursome petition; and at last

[001-072] Upon his will I seal’d my hard consent.

[001-073] I do beseech you give him leave to go.

[001-074] KING.

[001-075] Take thy fair hour, Laertes; time be thine,

[001-076] And thy best graces spend it at thy will!

[001-077] But now, my cousin Hamlet, and my son—

[001-078] HAMLET.

[001-079] [Aside.] A little more than kin, and less than kind.

[001-080] KING.

[001-081] How is it that the clouds still hang on you?

[001-082] HAMLET.

[001-083] Not so, my lord, I am too much i’ the sun.

[001-084] QUEEN.

[001-085] Good Hamlet, cast thy nighted colour off,

[001-086] And let thine eye look like a friend on Denmark.

[001-087] Do not for ever with thy vailed lids

[001-088] Seek for thy noble father in the dust.

[001-089] Thou know’st ’tis common, all that lives must die,

[001-090] Passing through nature to eternity.

[001-091] HAMLET.

[001-092] Ay, madam, it is common.

[001-093] QUEEN.

[001-094] If it be,

[001-095] Why seems it so particular with thee?

[001-096] HAMLET.

[001-097] Seems, madam! Nay, it is; I know not seems.

[001-098] ’Tis not alone my inky cloak, good mother,

[001-099] Nor customary suits of solemn black,

[001-100] Nor windy suspiration of forc’d breath,

[001-101] No, nor the fruitful river in the eye,

[001-102] Nor the dejected haviour of the visage,

[001-103] Together with all forms, moods, shows of grief,

[001-104] That can denote me truly. These indeed seem,

[001-105] For they are actions that a man might play;

[001-106] But I have that within which passeth show;

[001-107] These but the trappings and the suits of woe.

[001-108] KING.

[001-109] ’Tis sweet and commendable in your nature, Hamlet,

[001-110] To give these mourning duties to your father;

[001-111] But you must know, your father lost a father,

[001-112] That father lost, lost his, and the survivor bound

[001-113] In filial obligation, for some term

[001-114] To do obsequious sorrow. But to persevere

[001-115] In obstinate condolement is a course

[001-116] Of impious stubbornness. ’Tis unmanly grief,

[001-117] It shows a will most incorrect to heaven,

[001-118] A heart unfortified, a mind impatient,

[001-119] An understanding simple and unschool’d;

[001-120] For what we know must be, and is as common

[001-121] As any the most vulgar thing to sense,

[001-122] Why should we in our peevish opposition

[001-123] Take it to heart? Fie, ’tis a fault to heaven,

[001-124] A fault against the dead, a fault to nature,

[001-125] To reason most absurd, whose common theme

[001-126] Is death of fathers, and who still hath cried,

[001-127] From the first corse till he that died today,

[001-128] ‘This must be so.’ We pray you throw to earth

[001-129] This unprevailing woe, and think of us

[001-130] As of a father; for let the world take note

[001-131] You are the most immediate to our throne,

[001-132] And with no less nobility of love

[001-133] Than that which dearest father bears his son

[001-134] Do I impart toward you. For your intent

[001-135] In going back to school in Wittenberg,

[001-136] It is most retrograde to our desire:

[001-137] And we beseech you bend you to remain

[001-138] Here in the cheer and comfort of our eye,

[001-139] Our chiefest courtier, cousin, and our son.

[001-140] QUEEN.

[001-141] Let not thy mother lose her prayers, Hamlet.

[001-142] I pray thee stay with us; go not to Wittenberg.

[001-143] HAMLET.

[001-144] I shall in all my best obey you, madam.

[001-145] KING.

[001-146] Why, ’tis a loving and a fair reply.

[001-147] Be as ourself in Denmark. Madam, come;

[001-148] This gentle and unforc’d accord of Hamlet

[001-149] Sits smiling to my heart; in grace whereof,

[001-150] No jocund health that Denmark drinks today

[001-151] But the great cannon to the clouds shall tell,

[001-152] And the King’s rouse the heaven shall bruit again,

[001-153] Re-speaking earthly thunder. Come away.

[001-154] [Exeunt all but Hamlet.]

[001-155] HAMLET.

[001-156] O that this too too solid flesh would melt,

[001-157] Thaw, and resolve itself into a dew!

[001-158] Or that the Everlasting had not fix’d

[001-159] His canon ’gainst self-slaughter. O God! O God!

[001-160] How weary, stale, flat, and unprofitable

[001-161] Seem to me all the uses of this world!

[001-162] Fie on’t! Oh fie! ’tis an unweeded garden

[001-163] That grows to seed; things rank and gross in nature

[001-164] Possess it merely. That it should come to this!

[001-165] But two months dead—nay, not so much, not two:

[001-166] So excellent a king; that was to this

[001-167] Hyperion to a satyr; so loving to my mother,

[001-168] That he might not beteem the winds of heaven

[001-169] Visit her face too roughly. Heaven and earth!

[001-170] Must I remember? Why, she would hang on him

[001-171] As if increase of appetite had grown

[001-172] By what it fed on; and yet, within a month—

[001-173] Let me not think on’t—Frailty, thy name is woman!

[001-174] A little month, or ere those shoes were old

[001-175] With which she followed my poor father’s body

[001-176] Like Niobe, all tears.—Why she, even she—

[001-177] O God! A beast that wants discourse of reason

[001-178] Would have mourn’d longer,—married with mine uncle,

[001-179] My father’s brother; but no more like my father

[001-180] Than I to Hercules. Within a month,

[001-181] Ere yet the salt of most unrighteous tears

[001-182] Had left the flushing in her galled eyes,

[001-183] She married. O most wicked speed, to post

[001-184] With such dexterity to incestuous sheets!

[001-185] It is not, nor it cannot come to good.

[001-186] But break, my heart, for I must hold my tongue.

[001-187] Enter Horatio, Marcellus and Barnardo.

[001-188] HORATIO.

[001-189] Hail to your lordship!

[001-190] HAMLET.

[001-191] I am glad to see you well:

[001-192] Horatio, or I do forget myself.

[001-193] HORATIO.

[001-194] The same, my lord,

[001-195] And your poor servant ever.

[001-196] HAMLET.

[001-197] Sir, my good friend;

[001-198] I’ll change that name with you:

[001-199] And what make you from Wittenberg, Horatio?—

[001-200] Marcellus?

[001-201] MARCELLUS.

[001-202] My good lord.

[001-203] HAMLET.

[001-204] I am very glad to see you.—Good even, sir.—

[001-205] But what, in faith, make you from Wittenberg?

[001-206] HORATIO.

[001-207] A truant disposition, good my lord.

[001-208] HAMLET.

[001-209] I would not hear your enemy say so;

[001-210] Nor shall you do my ear that violence,

[001-211] To make it truster of your own report

[001-212] Against yourself. I know you are no truant.

[001-213] But what is your affair in Elsinore?

[001-214] We’ll teach you to drink deep ere you depart.

[001-215] HORATIO.

[001-216] My lord, I came to see your father’s funeral.

[001-217] HAMLET.

[001-218] I prithee do not mock me, fellow-student.

[001-219] I think it was to see my mother’s wedding.

[001-220] HORATIO.

[001-221] Indeed, my lord, it follow’d hard upon.

[001-222] HAMLET.

[001-223] Thrift, thrift, Horatio! The funeral bak’d meats

[001-224] Did coldly furnish forth the marriage tables.

[001-225] Would I had met my dearest foe in heaven

[001-226] Or ever I had seen that day, Horatio.

[001-227] My father,—methinks I see my father.

[001-228] HORATIO.

[001-229] Where, my lord?

[001-230] HAMLET.

[001-231] In my mind’s eye, Horatio.

[001-232] HORATIO.

[001-233] I saw him once; he was a goodly king.

[001-234] HAMLET.

[001-235] He was a man, take him for all in all,

[001-236] I shall not look upon his like again.

[001-237] HORATIO.

[001-238] My lord, I think I saw him yesternight.

[001-239] HAMLET.

[001-240] Saw? Who?

[001-241] HORATIO.

[001-242] My lord, the King your father.

[001-243] HAMLET.

[001-244] The King my father!

[001-245] HORATIO.

[001-246] Season your admiration for a while

[001-247] With an attent ear, till I may deliver

[001-248] Upon the witness of these gentlemen

[001-249] This marvel to you.

[001-250] HAMLET.

[001-251] For God’s love let me hear.

[001-252] HORATIO.

[001-253] Two nights together had these gentlemen,

[001-254] Marcellus and Barnardo, on their watch

[001-255] In the dead waste and middle of the night,

[001-256] Been thus encounter’d. A figure like your father,

[001-257] Armed at point exactly, cap-à-pie,

[001-258] Appears before them, and with solemn march

[001-259] Goes slow and stately by them: thrice he walk’d

[001-260] By their oppress’d and fear-surprised eyes,

[001-261] Within his truncheon’s length; whilst they, distill’d

[001-262] Almost to jelly with the act of fear,

[001-263] Stand dumb, and speak not to him. This to me

[001-264] In dreadful secrecy impart they did,

[001-265] And I with them the third night kept the watch,

[001-266] Where, as they had deliver’d, both in time,

[001-267] Form of the thing, each word made true and good,

[001-268] The apparition comes. I knew your father;

[001-269] These hands are not more like.

[001-270] HAMLET.

[001-271] But where was this?

[001-272] MARCELLUS.

[001-273] My lord, upon the platform where we watch.

[001-274] HAMLET.

[001-275] Did you not speak to it?

[001-276] HORATIO.

[001-277] My lord, I did;

[001-278] But answer made it none: yet once methought

[001-279] It lifted up it head, and did address

[001-280] Itself to motion, like as it would speak.

[001-281] But even then the morning cock crew loud,

[001-282] And at the sound it shrunk in haste away,

[001-283] And vanish’d from our sight.

[001-284] HAMLET.

[001-285] ’Tis very strange.

[001-286] HORATIO.

[001-287] As I do live, my honour’d lord, ’tis true;

[001-288] And we did think it writ down in our duty

[001-289] To let you know of it.

[001-290] HAMLET.

[001-291] Indeed, indeed, sirs, but this troubles me.

[001-292] Hold you the watch tonight?

[001-293] MARCELLUS and BARNARDO.

[001-294] We do, my lord.

[001-295] HAMLET.

[001-296] Arm’d, say you?

[001-297] Both.

[001-298] Arm’d, my lord.

[001-299] HAMLET.

[001-300] From top to toe?

[001-301] BOTH.

[001-302] My lord, from head to foot.

[001-303] HAMLET.

[001-304] Then saw you not his face?

[001-305] HORATIO.

[001-306] O yes, my lord, he wore his beaver up.

[001-307] HAMLET.

[001-308] What, look’d he frowningly?

[001-309] HORATIO.

[001-310] A countenance more in sorrow than in anger.

[001-311] HAMLET.

[001-312] Pale, or red?

[001-313] HORATIO.

[001-314] Nay, very pale.

[001-315] HAMLET.

[001-316] And fix’d his eyes upon you?

[001-317] HORATIO.

[001-318] Most constantly.

[001-319] HAMLET.

[001-320] I would I had been there.

[001-321] HORATIO.

[001-322] It would have much amaz’d you.

[001-323] HAMLET.

[001-324] Very like, very like. Stay’d it long?

[001-325] HORATIO.

[001-326] While one with moderate haste might tell a hundred.

[001-327] MARCELLUS and BARNARDO.

[001-328] Longer, longer.

[001-329] HORATIO.

[001-330] Not when I saw’t.

[001-331] HAMLET.

[001-332] His beard was grizzled, no?

[001-333] HORATIO.

[001-334] It was, as I have seen it in his life,

[001-335] A sable silver’d.

[001-336] HAMLET.

[001-337] I will watch tonight;

[001-338] Perchance ’twill walk again.

[001-339] HORATIO.

[001-340] I warrant you it will.

[001-341] HAMLET.

[001-342] If it assume my noble father’s person,

[001-343] I’ll speak to it, though hell itself should gape

[001-344] And bid me hold my peace. I pray you all,

[001-345] If you have hitherto conceal’d this sight,

[001-346] Let it be tenable in your silence still;

[001-347] And whatsoever else shall hap tonight,

[001-348] Give it an understanding, but no tongue.

[001-349] I will requite your loves. So, fare ye well.

[001-350] Upon the platform ’twixt eleven and twelve,

[001-351] I’ll visit you.

[001-352] ALL.

[001-353] Our duty to your honour.

[001-354] HAMLET.

[001-355] Your loves, as mine to you: farewell.

[001-356] [Exeunt Horatio, Marcellus and Barnardo.]

[001-357] My father’s spirit in arms! All is not well;

[001-358] I doubt some foul play: would the night were come!

[001-359] Till then sit still, my soul: foul deeds will rise,

[001-360] Though all the earth o’erwhelm them, to men’s eyes.

[001-361] [Exit.]
