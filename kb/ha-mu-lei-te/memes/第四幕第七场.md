# 第四幕 第七场

[004-001] the Castle.

[004-002] Enter King and Laertes.

[004-003] KING.

[004-004] Now must your conscience my acquittance seal,

[004-005] And you must put me in your heart for friend,

[004-006] Sith you have heard, and with a knowing ear,

[004-007] That he which hath your noble father slain

[004-008] Pursu’d my life.

[004-009] LAERTES.

[004-010] It well appears. But tell me

[004-011] Why you proceeded not against these feats,

[004-012] So crimeful and so capital in nature,

[004-013] As by your safety, wisdom, all things else,

[004-014] You mainly were stirr’d up.

[004-015] KING.

[004-016] O, for two special reasons,

[004-017] Which may to you, perhaps, seem much unsinew’d,

[004-018] But yet to me they are strong. The Queen his mother

[004-019] Lives almost by his looks; and for myself,—

[004-020] My virtue or my plague, be it either which,—

[004-021] She’s so conjunctive to my life and soul,

[004-022] That, as the star moves not but in his sphere,

[004-023] I could not but by her. The other motive,

[004-024] Why to a public count I might not go,

[004-025] Is the great love the general gender bear him,

[004-026] Who, dipping all his faults in their affection,

[004-027] Would like the spring that turneth wood to stone,

[004-028] Convert his gyves to graces; so that my arrows,

[004-029] Too slightly timber’d for so loud a wind,

[004-030] Would have reverted to my bow again,

[004-031] And not where I had aim’d them.

[004-032] LAERTES.

[004-033] And so have I a noble father lost,

[004-034] A sister driven into desperate terms,

[004-035] Whose worth, if praises may go back again,

[004-036] Stood challenger on mount of all the age

[004-037] For her perfections. But my revenge will come.

[004-038] KING.

[004-039] Break not your sleeps for that. You must not think

[004-040] That we are made of stuff so flat and dull

[004-041] That we can let our beard be shook with danger,

[004-042] And think it pastime. You shortly shall hear more.

[004-043] I lov’d your father, and we love ourself,

[004-044] And that, I hope, will teach you to imagine—

[004-045] Enter a Messenger.

[004-046] How now? What news?

[004-047] MESSENGER.

[004-048] Letters, my lord, from Hamlet.

[004-049] This to your Majesty; this to the Queen.

[004-050] KING.

[004-051] From Hamlet! Who brought them?

[004-052] MESSENGER.

[004-053] Sailors, my lord, they say; I saw them not.

[004-054] They were given me by Claudio. He receiv’d them

[004-055] Of him that brought them.

[004-056] KING.

[004-057] Laertes, you shall hear them.

[004-058] Leave us.

[004-059] [Exit Messenger.]

[004-060] [Reads.] ‘High and mighty, you shall know I am set naked on your

[004-061] kingdom. Tomorrow shall I beg leave to see your kingly eyes. When I shall,

[004-062] first asking your pardon thereunto, recount the occasions of my sudden and more

[004-063] strange return.

[004-064] HAMLET.’

[004-065] What should this mean? Are all the rest come back?

[004-066] Or is it some abuse, and no such thing?

[004-067] LAERTES.

[004-068] Know you the hand?

[004-069] KING.

[004-070] ’Tis Hamlet’s character. ‘Naked!’

[004-071] And in a postscript here he says ‘alone.’

[004-072] Can you advise me?

[004-073] LAERTES.

[004-074] I am lost in it, my lord. But let him come,

[004-075] It warms the very sickness in my heart

[004-076] That I shall live and tell him to his teeth,

[004-077] ‘Thus diest thou.’

[004-078] KING.

[004-079] If it be so, Laertes,—

[004-080] As how should it be so? How otherwise?—

[004-081] Will you be rul’d by me?

[004-082] LAERTES.

[004-083] Ay, my lord;

[004-084] So you will not o’errule me to a peace.

[004-085] KING.

[004-086] To thine own peace. If he be now return’d,

[004-087] As checking at his voyage, and that he means

[004-088] No more to undertake it, I will work him

[004-089] To an exploit, now ripe in my device,

[004-090] Under the which he shall not choose but fall;

[004-091] And for his death no wind shall breathe,

[004-092] But even his mother shall uncharge the practice

[004-093] And call it accident.

[004-094] LAERTES.

[004-095] My lord, I will be rul’d;

[004-096] The rather if you could devise it so

[004-097] That I might be the organ.

[004-098] KING.

[004-099] It falls right.

[004-100] You have been talk’d of since your travel much,

[004-101] And that in Hamlet’s hearing, for a quality

[004-102] Wherein they say you shine. Your sum of parts

[004-103] Did not together pluck such envy from him

[004-104] As did that one, and that, in my regard,

[004-105] Of the unworthiest siege.

[004-106] LAERTES.

[004-107] What part is that, my lord?

[004-108] KING.

[004-109] A very riband in the cap of youth,

[004-110] Yet needful too, for youth no less becomes

[004-111] The light and careless livery that it wears

[004-112] Than settled age his sables and his weeds,

[004-113] Importing health and graveness. Two months since

[004-114] Here was a gentleman of Normandy,—

[004-115] I’ve seen myself, and serv’d against, the French,

[004-116] And they can well on horseback, but this gallant

[004-117] Had witchcraft in’t. He grew unto his seat,

[004-118] And to such wondrous doing brought his horse,

[004-119] As had he been incorps’d and demi-natur’d

[004-120] With the brave beast. So far he topp’d my thought

[004-121] That I in forgery of shapes and tricks,

[004-122] Come short of what he did.

[004-123] LAERTES.

[004-124] A Norman was’t?

[004-125] KING.

[004-126] A Norman.

[004-127] LAERTES.

[004-128] Upon my life, Lamord.

[004-129] KING.

[004-130] The very same.

[004-131] LAERTES.

[004-132] I know him well. He is the brooch indeed

[004-133] And gem of all the nation.

[004-134] KING.

[004-135] He made confession of you,

[004-136] And gave you such a masterly report

[004-137] For art and exercise in your defence,

[004-138] And for your rapier most especially,

[004-139] That he cried out ’twould be a sight indeed

[004-140] If one could match you. The scrimers of their nation

[004-141] He swore had neither motion, guard, nor eye,

[004-142] If you oppos’d them. Sir, this report of his

[004-143] Did Hamlet so envenom with his envy

[004-144] That he could nothing do but wish and beg

[004-145] Your sudden coming o’er to play with him.

[004-146] Now, out of this,—

[004-147] LAERTES.

[004-148] What out of this, my lord?

[004-149] KING.

[004-150] Laertes, was your father dear to you?

[004-151] Or are you like the painting of a sorrow,

[004-152] A face without a heart?

[004-153] LAERTES.

[004-154] Why ask you this?

[004-155] KING.

[004-156] Not that I think you did not love your father,

[004-157] But that I know love is begun by time,

[004-158] And that I see, in passages of proof,

[004-159] Time qualifies the spark and fire of it.

[004-160] There lives within the very flame of love

[004-161] A kind of wick or snuff that will abate it;

[004-162] And nothing is at a like goodness still,

[004-163] For goodness, growing to a pleurisy,

[004-164] Dies in his own too much. That we would do,

[004-165] We should do when we would; for this ‘would’ changes,

[004-166] And hath abatements and delays as many

[004-167] As there are tongues, are hands, are accidents;

[004-168] And then this ‘should’ is like a spendthrift sigh

[004-169] That hurts by easing. But to the quick o’ th’ulcer:

[004-170] Hamlet comes back: what would you undertake

[004-171] To show yourself your father’s son in deed,

[004-172] More than in words?

[004-173] LAERTES.

[004-174] To cut his throat i’ th’ church.

[004-175] KING.

[004-176] No place, indeed, should murder sanctuarize;

[004-177] Revenge should have no bounds. But good Laertes,

[004-178] Will you do this, keep close within your chamber.

[004-179] Hamlet return’d shall know you are come home:

[004-180] We’ll put on those shall praise your excellence,

[004-181] And set a double varnish on the fame

[004-182] The Frenchman gave you, bring you in fine together

[004-183] And wager on your heads. He, being remiss,

[004-184] Most generous, and free from all contriving,

[004-185] Will not peruse the foils; so that with ease,

[004-186] Or with a little shuffling, you may choose

[004-187] A sword unbated, and in a pass of practice,

[004-188] Requite him for your father.

[004-189] LAERTES.

[004-190] I will do’t.

[004-191] And for that purpose I’ll anoint my sword.

[004-192] I bought an unction of a mountebank

[004-193] So mortal that, but dip a knife in it,

[004-194] Where it draws blood no cataplasm so rare,

[004-195] Collected from all simples that have virtue

[004-196] Under the moon, can save the thing from death

[004-197] This is but scratch’d withal. I’ll touch my point

[004-198] With this contagion, that if I gall him slightly,

[004-199] It may be death.

[004-200] KING.

[004-201] Let’s further think of this,

[004-202] Weigh what convenience both of time and means

[004-203] May fit us to our shape. If this should fail,

[004-204] And that our drift look through our bad performance.

[004-205] ’Twere better not assay’d. Therefore this project

[004-206] Should have a back or second, that might hold

[004-207] If this did blast in proof. Soft, let me see.

[004-208] We’ll make a solemn wager on your cunnings,—

[004-209] I ha’t! When in your motion you are hot and dry,

[004-210] As make your bouts more violent to that end,

[004-211] And that he calls for drink, I’ll have prepar’d him

[004-212] A chalice for the nonce; whereon but sipping,

[004-213] If he by chance escape your venom’d stuck,

[004-214] Our purpose may hold there.

[004-215] Enter Queen.

[004-216] How now, sweet Queen?

[004-217] QUEEN.

[004-218] One woe doth tread upon another’s heel,

[004-219] So fast they follow. Your sister’s drown’d, Laertes.

[004-220] LAERTES.

[004-221] Drown’d! O, where?

[004-222] QUEEN.

[004-223] There is a willow grows aslant a brook,

[004-224] That shows his hoary leaves in the glassy stream.

[004-225] There with fantastic garlands did she make

[004-226] Of crow-flowers, nettles, daisies, and long purples,

[004-227] That liberal shepherds give a grosser name,

[004-228] But our cold maids do dead men’s fingers call them.

[004-229] There on the pendant boughs her coronet weeds

[004-230] Clamb’ring to hang, an envious sliver broke,

[004-231] When down her weedy trophies and herself

[004-232] Fell in the weeping brook. Her clothes spread wide,

[004-233] And mermaid-like, awhile they bore her up,

[004-234] Which time she chaunted snatches of old tunes,

[004-235] As one incapable of her own distress,

[004-236] Or like a creature native and indued

[004-237] Unto that element. But long it could not be

[004-238] Till that her garments, heavy with their drink,

[004-239] Pull’d the poor wretch from her melodious lay

[004-240] To muddy death.

[004-241] LAERTES.

[004-242] Alas, then she is drown’d?

[004-243] QUEEN.

[004-244] Drown’d, drown’d.

[004-245] LAERTES.

[004-246] Too much of water hast thou, poor Ophelia,

[004-247] And therefore I forbid my tears. But yet

[004-248] It is our trick; nature her custom holds,

[004-249] Let shame say what it will. When these are gone,

[004-250] The woman will be out. Adieu, my lord,

[004-251] I have a speech of fire, that fain would blaze,

[004-252] But that this folly douts it.

[004-253] [Exit.]

[004-254] KING.

[004-255] Let’s follow, Gertrude;

[004-256] How much I had to do to calm his rage!

[004-257] Now fear I this will give it start again;

[004-258] Therefore let’s follow.

[004-259] [Exeunt.]
