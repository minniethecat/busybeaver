# 第一幕 第一场

[001-001] Enter Francisco and Barnardo, two sentinels.

[001-002] BARNARDO.

[001-003] Who’s there?

[001-004] FRANCISCO.

[001-005] Nay, answer me. Stand and unfold yourself.

[001-006] BARNARDO.

[001-007] Long live the King!

[001-008] FRANCISCO.

[001-009] Barnardo?

[001-010] BARNARDO.

[001-011] He.

[001-012] FRANCISCO.

[001-013] You come most carefully upon your hour.

[001-014] BARNARDO.

[001-015] ’Tis now struck twelve. Get thee to bed, Francisco.

[001-016] FRANCISCO.

[001-017] For this relief much thanks. ’Tis bitter cold,

[001-018] And I am sick at heart.

[001-019] BARNARDO.

[001-020] Have you had quiet guard?

[001-021] FRANCISCO.

[001-022] Not a mouse stirring.

[001-023] BARNARDO.

[001-024] Well, good night.

[001-025] If you do meet Horatio and Marcellus,

[001-026] The rivals of my watch, bid them make haste.

[001-027] Enter Horatio and Marcellus.

[001-028] FRANCISCO.

[001-029] I think I hear them. Stand, ho! Who is there?

[001-030] HORATIO.

[001-031] Friends to this ground.

[001-032] MARCELLUS.

[001-033] And liegemen to the Dane.

[001-034] FRANCISCO.

[001-035] Give you good night.

[001-036] MARCELLUS.

[001-037] O, farewell, honest soldier, who hath reliev’d you?

[001-038] FRANCISCO.

[001-039] Barnardo has my place. Give you good-night.

[001-040] [Exit.]

[001-041] MARCELLUS.

[001-042] Holla, Barnardo!

[001-043] BARNARDO.

[001-044] Say, what, is Horatio there?

[001-045] HORATIO.

[001-046] A piece of him.

[001-047] BARNARDO.

[001-048] Welcome, Horatio. Welcome, good Marcellus.

[001-049] MARCELLUS.

[001-050] What, has this thing appear’d again tonight?

[001-051] BARNARDO.

[001-052] I have seen nothing.

[001-053] MARCELLUS.

[001-054] Horatio says ’tis but our fantasy,

[001-055] And will not let belief take hold of him

[001-056] Touching this dreaded sight, twice seen of us.

[001-057] Therefore I have entreated him along

[001-058] With us to watch the minutes of this night,

[001-059] That if again this apparition come

[001-060] He may approve our eyes and speak to it.

[001-061] HORATIO.

[001-062] Tush, tush, ’twill not appear.

[001-063] BARNARDO.

[001-064] Sit down awhile,

[001-065] And let us once again assail your ears,

[001-066] That are so fortified against our story,

[001-067] What we two nights have seen.

[001-068] HORATIO.

[001-069] Well, sit we down,

[001-070] And let us hear Barnardo speak of this.

[001-071] BARNARDO.

[001-072] Last night of all,

[001-073] When yond same star that’s westward from the pole,

[001-074] Had made his course t’illume that part of heaven

[001-075] Where now it burns, Marcellus and myself,

[001-076] The bell then beating one—

[001-077] MARCELLUS.

[001-078] Peace, break thee off. Look where it comes again.

[001-079] Enter Ghost.

[001-080] BARNARDO.

[001-081] In the same figure, like the King that’s dead.

[001-082] MARCELLUS.

[001-083] Thou art a scholar; speak to it, Horatio.

[001-084] BARNARDO.

[001-085] Looks it not like the King? Mark it, Horatio.

[001-086] HORATIO.

[001-087] Most like. It harrows me with fear and wonder.

[001-088] BARNARDO

[001-089] It would be spoke to.

[001-090] MARCELLUS.

[001-091] Question it, Horatio.

[001-092] HORATIO.

[001-093] What art thou that usurp’st this time of night,

[001-094] Together with that fair and warlike form

[001-095] In which the majesty of buried Denmark

[001-096] Did sometimes march? By heaven I charge thee speak.

[001-097] MARCELLUS.

[001-098] It is offended.

[001-099] BARNARDO.

[001-100] See, it stalks away.

[001-101] HORATIO.

[001-102] Stay! speak, speak! I charge thee speak!

[001-103] [Exit Ghost.]

[001-104] MARCELLUS.

[001-105] ’Tis gone, and will not answer.

[001-106] BARNARDO.

[001-107] How now, Horatio! You tremble and look pale.

[001-108] Is not this something more than fantasy?

[001-109] What think you on’t?

[001-110] HORATIO.

[001-111] Before my God, I might not this believe

[001-112] Without the sensible and true avouch

[001-113] Of mine own eyes.

[001-114] MARCELLUS.

[001-115] Is it not like the King?

[001-116] HORATIO.

[001-117] As thou art to thyself:

[001-118] Such was the very armour he had on

[001-119] When he th’ambitious Norway combated;

[001-120] So frown’d he once, when in an angry parle

[001-121] He smote the sledded Polacks on the ice.

[001-122] ’Tis strange.

[001-123] MARCELLUS.

[001-124] Thus twice before, and jump at this dead hour,

[001-125] With martial stalk hath he gone by our watch.

[001-126] HORATIO.

[001-127] In what particular thought to work I know not;

[001-128] But in the gross and scope of my opinion,

[001-129] This bodes some strange eruption to our state.

[001-130] MARCELLUS.

[001-131] Good now, sit down, and tell me, he that knows,

[001-132] Why this same strict and most observant watch

[001-133] So nightly toils the subject of the land,

[001-134] And why such daily cast of brazen cannon

[001-135] And foreign mart for implements of war;

[001-136] Why such impress of shipwrights, whose sore task

[001-137] Does not divide the Sunday from the week.

[001-138] What might be toward, that this sweaty haste

[001-139] Doth make the night joint-labourer with the day:

[001-140] Who is’t that can inform me?

[001-141] HORATIO.

[001-142] That can I;

[001-143] At least, the whisper goes so. Our last King,

[001-144] Whose image even but now appear’d to us,

[001-145] Was, as you know, by Fortinbras of Norway,

[001-146] Thereto prick’d on by a most emulate pride,

[001-147] Dar’d to the combat; in which our valiant Hamlet,

[001-148] For so this side of our known world esteem’d him,

[001-149] Did slay this Fortinbras; who by a seal’d compact,

[001-150] Well ratified by law and heraldry,

[001-151] Did forfeit, with his life, all those his lands

[001-152] Which he stood seiz’d of, to the conqueror;

[001-153] Against the which, a moiety competent

[001-154] Was gaged by our King; which had return’d

[001-155] To the inheritance of Fortinbras,

[001-156] Had he been vanquisher; as by the same cov’nant

[001-157] And carriage of the article design’d,

[001-158] His fell to Hamlet. Now, sir, young Fortinbras,

[001-159] Of unimproved mettle, hot and full,

[001-160] Hath in the skirts of Norway, here and there,

[001-161] Shark’d up a list of lawless resolutes,

[001-162] For food and diet, to some enterprise

[001-163] That hath a stomach in’t; which is no other,

[001-164] As it doth well appear unto our state,

[001-165] But to recover of us by strong hand

[001-166] And terms compulsatory, those foresaid lands

[001-167] So by his father lost. And this, I take it,

[001-168] Is the main motive of our preparations,

[001-169] The source of this our watch, and the chief head

[001-170] Of this post-haste and rummage in the land.

[001-171] BARNARDO.

[001-172] I think it be no other but e’en so:

[001-173] Well may it sort that this portentous figure

[001-174] Comes armed through our watch so like the King

[001-175] That was and is the question of these wars.

[001-176] HORATIO.

[001-177] A mote it is to trouble the mind’s eye.

[001-178] In the most high and palmy state of Rome,

[001-179] A little ere the mightiest Julius fell,

[001-180] The graves stood tenantless and the sheeted dead

[001-181] Did squeak and gibber in the Roman streets;

[001-182] As stars with trains of fire and dews of blood,

[001-183] Disasters in the sun; and the moist star,

[001-184] Upon whose influence Neptune’s empire stands,

[001-185] Was sick almost to doomsday with eclipse.

[001-186] And even the like precurse of fierce events,

[001-187] As harbingers preceding still the fates

[001-188] And prologue to the omen coming on,

[001-189] Have heaven and earth together demonstrated

[001-190] Unto our climatures and countrymen.

[001-191] Re-enter Ghost.

[001-192] But, soft, behold! Lo, where it comes again!

[001-193] I’ll cross it, though it blast me. Stay, illusion!

[001-194] If thou hast any sound, or use of voice,

[001-195] Speak to me.

[001-196] If there be any good thing to be done,

[001-197] That may to thee do ease, and grace to me,

[001-198] Speak to me.

[001-199] If thou art privy to thy country’s fate,

[001-200] Which, happily, foreknowing may avoid,

[001-201] O speak!

[001-202] Or if thou hast uphoarded in thy life

[001-203] Extorted treasure in the womb of earth,

[001-204] For which, they say, you spirits oft walk in death,

[001-205] Speak of it. Stay, and speak!

[001-206] [The cock crows.]

[001-207] Stop it, Marcellus!

[001-208] MARCELLUS.

[001-209] Shall I strike at it with my partisan?

[001-210] HORATIO.

[001-211] Do, if it will not stand.

[001-212] BARNARDO.

[001-213] ’Tis here!

[001-214] HORATIO.

[001-215] ’Tis here!

[001-216] [Exit Ghost.]

[001-217] MARCELLUS.

[001-218] ’Tis gone!

[001-219] We do it wrong, being so majestical,

[001-220] To offer it the show of violence,

[001-221] For it is as the air, invulnerable,

[001-222] And our vain blows malicious mockery.

[001-223] BARNARDO.

[001-224] It was about to speak, when the cock crew.

[001-225] HORATIO.

[001-226] And then it started, like a guilty thing

[001-227] Upon a fearful summons. I have heard

[001-228] The cock, that is the trumpet to the morn,

[001-229] Doth with his lofty and shrill-sounding throat

[001-230] Awake the god of day; and at his warning,

[001-231] Whether in sea or fire, in earth or air,

[001-232] Th’extravagant and erring spirit hies

[001-233] To his confine. And of the truth herein

[001-234] This present object made probation.

[001-235] MARCELLUS.

[001-236] It faded on the crowing of the cock.

[001-237] Some say that ever ’gainst that season comes

[001-238] Wherein our Saviour’s birth is celebrated,

[001-239] The bird of dawning singeth all night long;

[001-240] And then, they say, no spirit dare stir abroad,

[001-241] The nights are wholesome, then no planets strike,

[001-242] No fairy takes, nor witch hath power to charm;

[001-243] So hallow’d and so gracious is the time.

[001-244] HORATIO.

[001-245] So have I heard, and do in part believe it.

[001-246] But look, the morn in russet mantle clad,

[001-247] Walks o’er the dew of yon high eastward hill.

[001-248] Break we our watch up, and by my advice,

[001-249] Let us impart what we have seen tonight

[001-250] Unto young Hamlet; for upon my life,

[001-251] This spirit, dumb to us, will speak to him.

[001-252] Do you consent we shall acquaint him with it,

[001-253] As needful in our loves, fitting our duty?

[001-254] MARCELLUS.

[001-255] Let’s do’t, I pray, and I this morning know

[001-256] Where we shall find him most conveniently.

[001-257] [Exeunt.]
