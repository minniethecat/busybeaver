# 第五幕 第二场

[005-001] Castle.

[005-002] Enter Hamlet and Horatio.

[005-003] HAMLET.

[005-004] So much for this, sir. Now let me see the other;

[005-005] You do remember all the circumstance?

[005-006] HORATIO.

[005-007] Remember it, my lord!

[005-008] HAMLET.

[005-009] Sir, in my heart there was a kind of fighting

[005-010] That would not let me sleep. Methought I lay

[005-011] Worse than the mutinies in the bilboes. Rashly,

[005-012] And prais’d be rashness for it,—let us know,

[005-013] Our indiscretion sometime serves us well,

[005-014] When our deep plots do pall; and that should teach us

[005-015] There’s a divinity that shapes our ends,

[005-016] Rough-hew them how we will.

[005-017] HORATIO.

[005-018] That is most certain.

[005-019] HAMLET.

[005-020] Up from my cabin,

[005-021] My sea-gown scarf’d about me, in the dark

[005-022] Grop’d I to find out them; had my desire,

[005-023] Finger’d their packet, and in fine, withdrew

[005-024] To mine own room again, making so bold,

[005-025] My fears forgetting manners, to unseal

[005-026] Their grand commission; where I found, Horatio,

[005-027] Oh royal knavery! an exact command,

[005-028] Larded with many several sorts of reasons,

[005-029] Importing Denmark’s health, and England’s too,

[005-030] With ho! such bugs and goblins in my life,

[005-031] That on the supervise, no leisure bated,

[005-032] No, not to stay the grinding of the axe,

[005-033] My head should be struck off.

[005-034] HORATIO.

[005-035] Is’t possible?

[005-036] HAMLET.

[005-037] Here’s the commission, read it at more leisure.

[005-038] But wilt thou hear me how I did proceed?

[005-039] HORATIO.

[005-040] I beseech you.

[005-041] HAMLET.

[005-042] Being thus benetted round with villanies,—

[005-043] Or I could make a prologue to my brains,

[005-044] They had begun the play,—I sat me down,

[005-045] Devis’d a new commission, wrote it fair:

[005-046] I once did hold it, as our statists do,

[005-047] A baseness to write fair, and labour’d much

[005-048] How to forget that learning; but, sir, now

[005-049] It did me yeoman’s service. Wilt thou know

[005-050] The effect of what I wrote?

[005-051] HORATIO.

[005-052] Ay, good my lord.

[005-053] HAMLET.

[005-054] An earnest conjuration from the King,

[005-055] As England was his faithful tributary,

[005-056] As love between them like the palm might flourish,

[005-057] As peace should still her wheaten garland wear

[005-058] And stand a comma ’tween their amities,

[005-059] And many such-like ‘as’es of great charge,

[005-060] That on the view and know of these contents,

[005-061] Without debatement further, more or less,

[005-062] He should the bearers put to sudden death,

[005-063] Not shriving-time allow’d.

[005-064] HORATIO.

[005-065] How was this seal’d?

[005-066] HAMLET.

[005-067] Why, even in that was heaven ordinant.

[005-068] I had my father’s signet in my purse,

[005-069] Which was the model of that Danish seal:

[005-070] Folded the writ up in the form of the other,

[005-071] Subscrib’d it: gave’t th’impression; plac’d it safely,

[005-072] The changeling never known. Now, the next day

[005-073] Was our sea-fight, and what to this was sequent

[005-074] Thou know’st already.

[005-075] HORATIO.

[005-076] So Guildenstern and Rosencrantz go to’t.

[005-077] HAMLET.

[005-078] Why, man, they did make love to this employment.

[005-079] They are not near my conscience; their defeat

[005-080] Does by their own insinuation grow.

[005-081] ’Tis dangerous when the baser nature comes

[005-082] Between the pass and fell incensed points

[005-083] Of mighty opposites.

[005-084] HORATIO.

[005-085] Why, what a king is this!

[005-086] HAMLET.

[005-087] Does it not, thinks’t thee, stand me now upon,—

[005-088] He that hath kill’d my king, and whor’d my mother,

[005-089] Popp’d in between th’election and my hopes,

[005-090] Thrown out his angle for my proper life,

[005-091] And with such cozenage—is’t not perfect conscience

[005-092] To quit him with this arm? And is’t not to be damn’d

[005-093] To let this canker of our nature come

[005-094] In further evil?

[005-095] HORATIO.

[005-096] It must be shortly known to him from England

[005-097] What is the issue of the business there.

[005-098] HAMLET.

[005-099] It will be short. The interim is mine;

[005-100] And a man’s life’s no more than to say ‘One’.

[005-101] But I am very sorry, good Horatio,

[005-102] That to Laertes I forgot myself;

[005-103] For by the image of my cause I see

[005-104] The portraiture of his. I’ll court his favours.

[005-105] But sure the bravery of his grief did put me

[005-106] Into a tow’ring passion.

[005-107] HORATIO.

[005-108] Peace, who comes here?

[005-109] Enter Osric.

[005-110] OSRIC.

[005-111] Your lordship is right welcome back to Denmark.

[005-112] HAMLET.

[005-113] I humbly thank you, sir. Dost know this waterfly?

[005-114] HORATIO.

[005-115] No, my good lord.

[005-116] HAMLET.

[005-117] Thy state is the more gracious; for ’tis a vice to know him. He hath much land,

[005-118] and fertile; let a beast be lord of beasts, and his crib shall stand at the

[005-119] king’s mess; ’tis a chough; but, as I say, spacious in the possession of dirt.

[005-120] OSRIC.

[005-121] Sweet lord, if your lordship were at leisure, I should impart a thing to you

[005-122] from his Majesty.

[005-123] HAMLET.

[005-124] I will receive it with all diligence of spirit. Put your bonnet to his right

[005-125] use; ’tis for the head.

[005-126] OSRIC.

[005-127] I thank your lordship, ’tis very hot.

[005-128] HAMLET.

[005-129] No, believe me, ’tis very cold, the wind is northerly.

[005-130] OSRIC.

[005-131] It is indifferent cold, my lord, indeed.

[005-132] HAMLET.

[005-133] Methinks it is very sultry and hot for my complexion.

[005-134] OSRIC.

[005-135] Exceedingly, my lord; it is very sultry,—as ’twere—I cannot tell how. But, my

[005-136] lord, his Majesty bade me signify to you that he has laid a great wager on your

[005-137] head. Sir, this is the matter,—

[005-138] HAMLET.

[005-139] I beseech you, remember,—

[005-140] [Hamlet moves him to put on his hat.]

[005-141] OSRIC.

[005-142] Nay, in good faith; for mine ease, in good faith. Sir, here is newly come to

[005-143] court Laertes; believe me, an absolute gentleman, full of most excellent

[005-144] differences, of very soft society and great showing. Indeed, to speak feelingly

[005-145] of him, he is the card or calendar of gentry; for you shall find in him the

[005-146] continent of what part a gentleman would see.

[005-147] HAMLET.

[005-148] Sir, his definement suffers no perdition in you, though I know, to divide him

[005-149] inventorially would dizzy th’arithmetic of memory, and yet but yaw neither, in

[005-150] respect of his quick sail. But, in the verity of extolment, I take him to be a

[005-151] soul of great article and his infusion of such dearth and rareness as, to make

[005-152] true diction of him, his semblable is his mirror and who else would trace him

[005-153] his umbrage, nothing more.

[005-154] OSRIC.

[005-155] Your lordship speaks most infallibly of him.

[005-156] HAMLET.

[005-157] The concernancy, sir? Why do we wrap the gentleman in our more rawer breath?

[005-158] OSRIC.

[005-159] Sir?

[005-160] HORATIO.

[005-161] Is’t not possible to understand in another tongue? You will do’t, sir, really.

[005-162] HAMLET.

[005-163] What imports the nomination of this gentleman?

[005-164] OSRIC.

[005-165] Of Laertes?

[005-166] HORATIO.

[005-167] His purse is empty already, all’s golden words are spent.

[005-168] HAMLET.

[005-169] Of him, sir.

[005-170] OSRIC.

[005-171] I know you are not ignorant,—

[005-172] HAMLET.

[005-173] I would you did, sir; yet in faith if you did, it would not much approve me.

[005-174] Well, sir?

[005-175] OSRIC.

[005-176] You are not ignorant of what excellence Laertes is,—

[005-177] HAMLET.

[005-178] I dare not confess that, lest I should compare with him in excellence; but to

[005-179] know a man well were to know himself.

[005-180] OSRIC.

[005-181] I mean, sir, for his weapon; but in the imputation laid on him, by them in his

[005-182] meed he’s unfellowed.

[005-183] HAMLET.

[005-184] What’s his weapon?

[005-185] OSRIC.

[005-186] Rapier and dagger.

[005-187] HAMLET.

[005-188] That’s two of his weapons. But well.

[005-189] OSRIC.

[005-190] The King, sir, hath wager’d with him six Barbary horses, against the which he

[005-191] has imponed, as I take it, six French rapiers and poniards, with their assigns,

[005-192] as girdle, hangers, and so. Three of the carriages, in faith, are very dear to

[005-193] fancy, very responsive to the hilts, most delicate carriages, and of very

[005-194] liberal conceit.

[005-195] HAMLET.

[005-196] What call you the carriages?

[005-197] HORATIO.

[005-198] I knew you must be edified by the margin ere you had done.

[005-199] OSRIC.

[005-200] The carriages, sir, are the hangers.

[005-201] HAMLET.

[005-202] The phrase would be more german to the matter if we could carry cannon by our

[005-203] sides. I would it might be hangers till then. But on. Six Barbary horses

[005-204] against six French swords, their assigns, and three liberal conceited

[005-205] carriages: that’s the French bet against the Danish. Why is this all imponed,

[005-206] as you call it?

[005-207] OSRIC.

[005-208] The King, sir, hath laid that in a dozen passes between you and him, he shall

[005-209] not exceed you three hits. He hath laid on twelve for nine. And it would come

[005-210] to immediate trial if your lordship would vouchsafe the answer.

[005-211] HAMLET.

[005-212] How if I answer no?

[005-213] OSRIC.

[005-214] I mean, my lord, the opposition of your person in trial.

[005-215] HAMLET.

[005-216] Sir, I will walk here in the hall. If it please his Majesty, it is the

[005-217] breathing time of day with me. Let the foils be brought, the gentleman willing,

[005-218] and the King hold his purpose, I will win for him if I can; if not, I will gain

[005-219] nothing but my shame and the odd hits.

[005-220] OSRIC.

[005-221] Shall I re-deliver you e’en so?

[005-222] HAMLET.

[005-223] To this effect, sir; after what flourish your nature will.

[005-224] OSRIC.

[005-225] I commend my duty to your lordship.

[005-226] HAMLET.

[005-227] Yours, yours.

[005-228] [Exit Osric.]

[005-229] He does well to commend it himself, there are no tongues else for’s turn.

[005-230] HORATIO.

[005-231] This lapwing runs away with the shell on his head.

[005-232] HAMLET.

[005-233] He did comply with his dug before he suck’d it. Thus has he,—and many more of

[005-234] the same bevy that I know the drossy age dotes on,— only got the tune of the

[005-235] time and outward habit of encounter; a kind of yeasty collection, which carries

[005-236] them through and through the most fanned and winnowed opinions; and do but blow

[005-237] them to their trial, the bubbles are out.

[005-238] Enter a Lord.

[005-239] LORD.

[005-240] My lord, his Majesty commended him to you by young Osric, who brings back to

[005-241] him that you attend him in the hall. He sends to know if your pleasure hold to

[005-242] play with Laertes or that you will take longer time.

[005-243] HAMLET.

[005-244] I am constant to my purposes, they follow the King’s pleasure. If his fitness

[005-245] speaks, mine is ready. Now or whensoever, provided I be so able as now.

[005-246] LORD.

[005-247] The King and Queen and all are coming down.

[005-248] HAMLET.

[005-249] In happy time.

[005-250] LORD.

[005-251] The Queen desires you to use some gentle entertainment to Laertes before you

[005-252] fall to play.

[005-253] HAMLET.

[005-254] She well instructs me.

[005-255] [Exit Lord.]

[005-256] HORATIO.

[005-257] You will lose this wager, my lord.

[005-258] HAMLET.

[005-259] I do not think so. Since he went into France, I have been in continual

[005-260] practice. I shall win at the odds. But thou wouldst not think how ill all’s

[005-261] here about my heart: but it is no matter.

[005-262] HORATIO.

[005-263] Nay, good my lord.

[005-264] HAMLET.

[005-265] It is but foolery; but it is such a kind of gain-giving as would perhaps

[005-266] trouble a woman.

[005-267] HORATIO.

[005-268] If your mind dislike anything, obey it. I will forestall their repair hither,

[005-269] and say you are not fit.

[005-270] HAMLET.

[005-271] Not a whit, we defy augury. There’s a special providence in the fall of a

[005-272] sparrow. If it be now, ’tis not to come; if it be not to come, it will be now;

[005-273] if it be not now, yet it will come. The readiness is all. Since no man has

[005-274] aught of what he leaves, what is’t to leave betimes?

[005-275] Enter King, Queen, Laertes, Lords, Osric and

[005-276] Attendants with foils &c.

[005-277] KING.

[005-278] Come, Hamlet, come, and take this hand from me.

[005-279] [The King puts Laertes’s hand into Hamlet’s.]

[005-280] HAMLET.

[005-281] Give me your pardon, sir. I have done you wrong;

[005-282] But pardon’t as you are a gentleman.

[005-283] This presence knows, and you must needs have heard,

[005-284] How I am punish’d with sore distraction.

[005-285] What I have done

[005-286] That might your nature, honour, and exception

[005-287] Roughly awake, I here proclaim was madness.

[005-288] Was’t Hamlet wrong’d Laertes? Never Hamlet.

[005-289] If Hamlet from himself be ta’en away,

[005-290] And when he’s not himself does wrong Laertes,

[005-291] Then Hamlet does it not, Hamlet denies it.

[005-292] Who does it, then? His madness. If’t be so,

[005-293] Hamlet is of the faction that is wrong’d;

[005-294] His madness is poor Hamlet’s enemy.

[005-295] Sir, in this audience,

[005-296] Let my disclaiming from a purpos’d evil

[005-297] Free me so far in your most generous thoughts

[005-298] That I have shot my arrow o’er the house

[005-299] And hurt my brother.

[005-300] LAERTES.

[005-301] I am satisfied in nature,

[005-302] Whose motive in this case should stir me most

[005-303] To my revenge. But in my terms of honour

[005-304] I stand aloof, and will no reconcilement

[005-305] Till by some elder masters of known honour

[005-306] I have a voice and precedent of peace

[005-307] To keep my name ungor’d. But till that time

[005-308] I do receive your offer’d love like love,

[005-309] And will not wrong it.

[005-310] HAMLET.

[005-311] I embrace it freely,

[005-312] And will this brother’s wager frankly play.—

[005-313] Give us the foils; come on.

[005-314] LAERTES.

[005-315] Come, one for me.

[005-316] HAMLET.

[005-317] I’ll be your foil, Laertes; in mine ignorance

[005-318] Your skill shall like a star i’ th’ darkest night,

[005-319] Stick fiery off indeed.

[005-320] LAERTES.

[005-321] You mock me, sir.

[005-322] HAMLET.

[005-323] No, by this hand.

[005-324] KING.

[005-325] Give them the foils, young Osric. Cousin Hamlet,

[005-326] You know the wager?

[005-327] HAMLET.

[005-328] Very well, my lord.

[005-329] Your Grace has laid the odds o’ the weaker side.

[005-330] KING.

[005-331] I do not fear it. I have seen you both;

[005-332] But since he is better’d, we have therefore odds.

[005-333] LAERTES.

[005-334] This is too heavy. Let me see another.

[005-335] HAMLET.

[005-336] This likes me well. These foils have all a length?

[005-337] [They prepare to play.]

[005-338] OSRIC.

[005-339] Ay, my good lord.

[005-340] KING.

[005-341] Set me the stoups of wine upon that table.

[005-342] If Hamlet give the first or second hit,

[005-343] Or quit in answer of the third exchange,

[005-344] Let all the battlements their ordnance fire;

[005-345] The King shall drink to Hamlet’s better breath,

[005-346] And in the cup an union shall he throw

[005-347] Richer than that which four successive kings

[005-348] In Denmark’s crown have worn. Give me the cups;

[005-349] And let the kettle to the trumpet speak,

[005-350] The trumpet to the cannoneer without,

[005-351] The cannons to the heavens, the heavens to earth,

[005-352] ‘Now the King drinks to Hamlet.’ Come, begin.

[005-353] And you, the judges, bear a wary eye.

[005-354] HAMLET.

[005-355] Come on, sir.

[005-356] LAERTES.

[005-357] Come, my lord.

[005-358] [They play.]

[005-359] HAMLET.

[005-360] One.

[005-361] LAERTES.

[005-362] No.

[005-363] HAMLET.

[005-364] Judgement.

[005-365] OSRIC.

[005-366] A hit, a very palpable hit.

[005-367] LAERTES.

[005-368] Well; again.

[005-369] KING.

[005-370] Stay, give me drink. Hamlet, this pearl is thine;

[005-371] Here’s to thy health.

[005-372] [Trumpets sound, and cannon shot off within.]

[005-374] HAMLET.

[005-375] I’ll play this bout first; set it by awhile.

[005-376] [They play.]

[005-378] LAERTES.

[005-379] A touch, a touch, I do confess.

[005-380] KING.

[005-381] Our son shall win.

[005-382] QUEEN.

[005-387] Good madam.

[005-388] KING.

[005-396] QUEEN.

[005-400] KING.

[005-401] I do not think’t.

[005-402] LAERTES.

[005-498] Young Fortinbras, with conquest come from Poland,

[005-499] To the ambassadors of England gives

[005-501] HAMLET.

[005-504] I cannot live to hear the news from England,

[005-505] But I do prophesy th’election lights

[005-506] On Fortinbras. He has my dying voice.

[005-507] So tell him, with the occurrents more and less,

[005-508] Which have solicited. The rest is silence.

[005-509] [Dies.]

[005-510] HORATIO.

[005-511] Now cracks a noble heart. Good night, sweet prince,

[005-512] And flights of angels sing thee to thy rest.

[005-513] Why does the drum come hither?

[005-514] [March within.]

[005-515] Enter Fortinbras, the English Ambassadors and

[005-516] others.

[005-517] FORTINBRAS.

[005-518] Where is this sight?

[005-519] HORATIO.

[005-520] What is it you would see?

[005-521] If aught of woe or wonder, cease your search.

[005-522] FORTINBRAS.

[005-523] This quarry cries on havoc. O proud death,

[005-524] What feast is toward in thine eternal cell,

[005-525] That thou so many princes at a shot

[005-526] So bloodily hast struck?

[005-527] FIRST AMBASSADOR.

[005-528] The sight is dismal;

[005-529] And our affairs from England come too late.

[005-530] The ears are senseless that should give us hearing,

[005-531] To tell him his commandment is fulfill’d,

[005-532] That Rosencrantz and Guildenstern are dead.

[005-533] Where should we have our thanks?

[005-534] HORATIO.

[005-535] Not from his mouth,

[005-536] Had it th’ability of life to thank you.

[005-537] He never gave commandment for their death.

[005-538] But since, so jump upon this bloody question,

[005-539] You from the Polack wars, and you from England

[005-540] Are here arriv’d, give order that these bodies

[005-541] High on a stage be placed to the view,

[005-542] And let me speak to th’ yet unknowing world

[005-543] How these things came about. So shall you hear

[005-544] Of carnal, bloody and unnatural acts,

[005-545] Of accidental judgements, casual slaughters,

[005-546] Of deaths put on by cunning and forc’d cause,

[005-547] And, in this upshot, purposes mistook

[005-548] Fall’n on the inventors’ heads. All this can I

[005-549] Truly deliver.

[005-550] FORTINBRAS.

[005-551] Let us haste to hear it,

[005-552] And call the noblest to the audience.

[005-553] For me, with sorrow I embrace my fortune.

[005-554] I have some rights of memory in this kingdom,

[005-555] Which now to claim my vantage doth invite me.

[005-556] HORATIO.

[005-557] Of that I shall have also cause to speak,

[005-558] And from his mouth whose voice will draw on more.

[005-559] But let this same be presently perform’d,

[005-560] Even while men’s minds are wild, lest more mischance

[005-561] On plots and errors happen.

[005-562] FORTINBRAS.

[005-563] Let four captains

[005-564] Bear Hamlet like a soldier to the stage,

[005-565] For he was likely, had he been put on,

[005-566] To have prov’d most royally; and for his passage,

[005-567] The soldiers’ music and the rites of war

[005-568] Speak loudly for him.

[005-569] Take up the bodies. Such a sight as this

[005-570] Becomes the field, but here shows much amiss.

[005-571] Go, bid the soldiers shoot.
