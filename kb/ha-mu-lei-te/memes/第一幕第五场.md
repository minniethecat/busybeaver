# 第一幕 第五场

[001-001] the Castle.

[001-002] Enter Ghost and Hamlet.

[001-003] HAMLET.

[001-004] Whither wilt thou lead me? Speak, I’ll go no further.

[001-005] GHOST.

[001-006] Mark me.

[001-007] HAMLET.

[001-008] I will.

[001-009] GHOST.

[001-010] My hour is almost come,

[001-011] When I to sulph’rous and tormenting flames

[001-012] Must render up myself.

[001-013] HAMLET.

[001-014] Alas, poor ghost!

[001-015] GHOST.

[001-016] Pity me not, but lend thy serious hearing

[001-017] To what I shall unfold.

[001-018] HAMLET.

[001-019] Speak, I am bound to hear.

[001-020] GHOST.

[001-021] So art thou to revenge, when thou shalt hear.

[001-022] HAMLET.

[001-023] What?

[001-024] GHOST.

[001-025] I am thy father’s spirit,

[001-026] Doom’d for a certain term to walk the night,

[001-027] And for the day confin’d to fast in fires,

[001-028] Till the foul crimes done in my days of nature

[001-029] Are burnt and purg’d away. But that I am forbid

[001-030] To tell the secrets of my prison-house,

[001-031] I could a tale unfold whose lightest word

[001-032] Would harrow up thy soul; freeze thy young blood,

[001-033] Make thy two eyes like stars start from their spheres,

[001-034] Thy knotted and combined locks to part,

[001-035] And each particular hair to stand on end

[001-036] Like quills upon the fretful porpentine.

[001-037] But this eternal blazon must not be

[001-038] To ears of flesh and blood. List, list, O, list!

[001-039] If thou didst ever thy dear father love—

[001-040] HAMLET.

[001-041] O God!

[001-042] GHOST.

[001-043] Revenge his foul and most unnatural murder.

[001-044] HAMLET.

[001-045] Murder!

[001-046] GHOST.

[001-047] Murder most foul, as in the best it is;

[001-048] But this most foul, strange, and unnatural.

[001-049] HAMLET.

[001-050] Haste me to know’t, that I, with wings as swift

[001-051] As meditation or the thoughts of love

[001-052] May sweep to my revenge.

[001-053] GHOST.

[001-054] I find thee apt;

[001-055] And duller shouldst thou be than the fat weed

[001-056] That rots itself in ease on Lethe wharf,

[001-057] Wouldst thou not stir in this. Now, Hamlet, hear.

[001-058] ’Tis given out that, sleeping in my orchard,

[001-059] A serpent stung me; so the whole ear of Denmark

[001-060] Is by a forged process of my death

[001-061] Rankly abus’d; but know, thou noble youth,

[001-062] The serpent that did sting thy father’s life

[001-063] Now wears his crown.

[001-064] HAMLET.

[001-065] O my prophetic soul!

[001-066] Mine uncle!

[001-067] GHOST.

[001-068] Ay, that incestuous, that adulterate beast,

[001-069] With witchcraft of his wit, with traitorous gifts,—

[001-070] O wicked wit, and gifts, that have the power

[001-071] So to seduce!—won to his shameful lust

[001-072] The will of my most seeming-virtuous queen.

[001-073] O Hamlet, what a falling off was there,

[001-074] From me, whose love was of that dignity

[001-075] That it went hand in hand even with the vow

[001-076] I made to her in marriage; and to decline

[001-077] Upon a wretch whose natural gifts were poor

[001-078] To those of mine. But virtue, as it never will be mov’d,

[001-079] Though lewdness court it in a shape of heaven;

[001-080] So lust, though to a radiant angel link’d,

[001-081] Will sate itself in a celestial bed

[001-082] And prey on garbage.

[001-083] But soft! methinks I scent the morning air;

[001-084] Brief let me be. Sleeping within my orchard,

[001-085] My custom always of the afternoon,

[001-086] Upon my secure hour thy uncle stole

[001-087] With juice of cursed hebenon in a vial,

[001-088] And in the porches of my ears did pour

[001-089] The leperous distilment, whose effect

[001-090] Holds such an enmity with blood of man

[001-091] That swift as quicksilver it courses through

[001-092] The natural gates and alleys of the body;

[001-093] And with a sudden vigour it doth posset

[001-094] And curd, like eager droppings into milk,

[001-095] The thin and wholesome blood. So did it mine;

[001-096] And a most instant tetter bark’d about,

[001-097] Most lazar-like, with vile and loathsome crust

[001-098] All my smooth body.

[001-099] Thus was I, sleeping, by a brother’s hand,

[001-100] Of life, of crown, of queen at once dispatch’d:

[001-101] Cut off even in the blossoms of my sin,

[001-102] Unhous’led, disappointed, unanel’d;

[001-103] No reckoning made, but sent to my account

[001-104] With all my imperfections on my head.

[001-105] O horrible! O horrible! most horrible!

[001-106] If thou hast nature in thee, bear it not;

[001-107] Let not the royal bed of Denmark be

[001-108] A couch for luxury and damned incest.

[001-109] But howsoever thou pursu’st this act,

[001-110] Taint not thy mind, nor let thy soul contrive

[001-111] Against thy mother aught; leave her to heaven,

[001-112] And to those thorns that in her bosom lodge,

[001-113] To prick and sting her. Fare thee well at once!

[001-114] The glow-worm shows the matin to be near,

[001-115] And ’gins to pale his uneffectual fire.

[001-116] Adieu, adieu, adieu. Remember me.

[001-117] [Exit.]

[001-118] HAMLET.

[001-119] O all you host of heaven! O earth! What else?

[001-120] And shall I couple hell? O, fie! Hold, my heart;

[001-121] And you, my sinews, grow not instant old,

[001-122] But bear me stiffly up. Remember thee?

[001-123] Ay, thou poor ghost, while memory holds a seat

[001-124] In this distracted globe. Remember thee?

[001-125] Yea, from the table of my memory

[001-126] I’ll wipe away all trivial fond records,

[001-127] All saws of books, all forms, all pressures past,

[001-128] That youth and observation copied there;

[001-129] And thy commandment all alone shall live

[001-130] Within the book and volume of my brain,

[001-131] Unmix’d with baser matter. Yes, by heaven!

[001-132] O most pernicious woman!

[001-133] O villain, villain, smiling damned villain!

[001-134] My tables. Meet it is I set it down,

[001-135] That one may smile, and smile, and be a villain!

[001-136] At least I am sure it may be so in Denmark.

[001-137] [Writing.]

[001-138] So, uncle, there you are. Now to my word;

[001-139] It is ‘Adieu, adieu, remember me.’

[001-140] I have sworn’t.

[001-141] HORATIO and MARCELLUS.

[001-142] [Within.] My lord, my lord.

[001-143] MARCELLUS.

[001-144] [Within.] Lord Hamlet.

[001-145] HORATIO.

[001-146] [Within.] Heaven secure him.

[001-147] HAMLET.

[001-148] So be it!

[001-149] MARCELLUS.

[001-150] [Within.] Illo, ho, ho, my lord!

[001-151] HAMLET.

[001-152] Hillo, ho, ho, boy! Come, bird, come.

[001-153] Enter Horatio and Marcellus.

[001-154] MARCELLUS.

[001-155] How is’t, my noble lord?

[001-156] HORATIO.

[001-157] What news, my lord?

[001-158] HAMLET.

[001-159] O, wonderful!

[001-160] HORATIO.

[001-161] Good my lord, tell it.

[001-162] HAMLET.

[001-163] No, you’ll reveal it.

[001-164] HORATIO.

[001-165] Not I, my lord, by heaven.

[001-166] MARCELLUS.

[001-167] Nor I, my lord.

[001-168] HAMLET.

[001-169] How say you then, would heart of man once think it?—

[001-170] But you’ll be secret?

[001-171] HORATIO and MARCELLUS.

[001-172] Ay, by heaven, my lord.

[001-173] HAMLET.

[001-174] There’s ne’er a villain dwelling in all Denmark

[001-175] But he’s an arrant knave.

[001-176] HORATIO.

[001-177] There needs no ghost, my lord, come from the grave

[001-178] To tell us this.

[001-179] HAMLET.

[001-180] Why, right; you are i’ the right;

[001-181] And so, without more circumstance at all,

[001-182] I hold it fit that we shake hands and part:

[001-183] You, as your business and desire shall point you,—

[001-184] For every man hath business and desire,

[001-185] Such as it is;—and for my own poor part,

[001-186] Look you, I’ll go pray.

[001-187] HORATIO.

[001-188] These are but wild and whirling words, my lord.

[001-189] HAMLET.

[001-190] I’m sorry they offend you, heartily;

[001-191] Yes faith, heartily.

[001-192] HORATIO.

[001-193] There’s no offence, my lord.

[001-194] HAMLET.

[001-195] Yes, by Saint Patrick, but there is, Horatio,

[001-196] And much offence too. Touching this vision here,

[001-197] It is an honest ghost, that let me tell you.

[001-198] For your desire to know what is between us,

[001-199] O’ermaster’t as you may. And now, good friends,

[001-200] As you are friends, scholars, and soldiers,

[001-201] Give me one poor request.

[001-202] HORATIO.

[001-203] What is’t, my lord? We will.

[001-204] HAMLET.

[001-205] Never make known what you have seen tonight.

[001-206] HORATIO and MARCELLUS.

[001-207] My lord, we will not.

[001-208] HAMLET.

[001-209] Nay, but swear’t.

[001-210] HORATIO.

[001-211] In faith, my lord, not I.

[001-212] MARCELLUS.

[001-213] Nor I, my lord, in faith.

[001-214] HAMLET.

[001-215] Upon my sword.

[001-216] MARCELLUS.

[001-217] We have sworn, my lord, already.

[001-218] HAMLET.

[001-219] Indeed, upon my sword, indeed.

[001-220] GHOST.

[001-221] [Cries under the stage.] Swear.

[001-222] HAMLET.

[001-223] Ha, ha boy, sayst thou so? Art thou there, truepenny?

[001-224] Come on, you hear this fellow in the cellarage.

[001-225] Consent to swear.

[001-226] HORATIO.

[001-227] Propose the oath, my lord.

[001-228] HAMLET.

[001-229] Never to speak of this that you have seen.

[001-230] Swear by my sword.

[001-231] GHOST.

[001-232] [Beneath.] Swear.

[001-233] HAMLET.

[001-234] Hic et ubique? Then we’ll shift our ground.

[001-235] Come hither, gentlemen,

[001-236] And lay your hands again upon my sword.

[001-237] Never to speak of this that you have heard.

[001-238] Swear by my sword.

[001-239] GHOST.

[001-240] [Beneath.] Swear.

[001-241] HAMLET.

[001-242] Well said, old mole! Canst work i’ th’earth so fast?

[001-243] A worthy pioner! Once more remove, good friends.

[001-244] HORATIO.

[001-245] O day and night, but this is wondrous strange.

[001-246] HAMLET.

[001-247] And therefore as a stranger give it welcome.

[001-248] There are more things in heaven and earth, Horatio,

[001-249] Than are dreamt of in your philosophy. But come,

[001-250] Here, as before, never, so help you mercy,

[001-251] How strange or odd soe’er I bear myself,—

[001-252] As I perchance hereafter shall think meet

[001-253] To put an antic disposition on—

[001-254] That you, at such times seeing me, never shall,

[001-255] With arms encumber’d thus, or this head-shake,

[001-256] Or by pronouncing of some doubtful phrase,

[001-257] As ‘Well, we know’, or ‘We could and if we would’,

[001-258] Or ‘If we list to speak’; or ‘There be and if they might’,

[001-259] Or such ambiguous giving out, to note

[001-260] That you know aught of me:—this not to do.

[001-261] So grace and mercy at your most need help you,

[001-262] Swear.

[001-263] GHOST.

[001-264] [Beneath.] Swear.

[001-265] HAMLET.

[001-266] Rest, rest, perturbed spirit. So, gentlemen,

[001-267] With all my love I do commend me to you;

[001-268] And what so poor a man as Hamlet is

[001-269] May do t’express his love and friending to you,

[001-270] God willing, shall not lack. Let us go in together,

[001-271] And still your fingers on your lips, I pray.

[001-272] The time is out of joint. O cursed spite,

[001-273] That ever I was born to set it right.

[001-274] Nay, come, let’s go together.

[001-275] [Exeunt.]
