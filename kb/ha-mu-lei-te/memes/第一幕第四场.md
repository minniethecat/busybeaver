# 第一幕 第四场

[001-001] platform.

[001-002] Enter Hamlet, Horatio and Marcellus.

[001-003] HAMLET.

[001-004] The air bites shrewdly; it is very cold.

[001-005] HORATIO.

[001-006] It is a nipping and an eager air.

[001-007] HAMLET.

[001-008] What hour now?

[001-009] HORATIO.

[001-010] I think it lacks of twelve.

[001-011] MARCELLUS.

[001-012] No, it is struck.

[001-013] HORATIO.

[001-014] Indeed? I heard it not. It then draws near the season

[001-015] Wherein the spirit held his wont to walk.

[001-016] [A flourish of trumpets, and ordnance shot off within.]

[001-017] What does this mean, my lord?

[001-018] HAMLET.

[001-019] The King doth wake tonight and takes his rouse,

[001-020] Keeps wassail, and the swaggering upspring reels;

[001-021] And as he drains his draughts of Rhenish down,

[001-022] The kettle-drum and trumpet thus bray out

[001-023] The triumph of his pledge.

[001-024] HORATIO.

[001-025] Is it a custom?

[001-026] HAMLET.

[001-027] Ay marry is’t;

[001-028] And to my mind, though I am native here,

[001-029] And to the manner born, it is a custom

[001-030] More honour’d in the breach than the observance.

[001-031] This heavy-headed revel east and west

[001-032] Makes us traduc’d and tax’d of other nations:

[001-033] They clepe us drunkards, and with swinish phrase

[001-034] Soil our addition; and indeed it takes

[001-035] From our achievements, though perform’d at height,

[001-036] The pith and marrow of our attribute.

[001-037] So oft it chances in particular men

[001-038] That for some vicious mole of nature in them,

[001-039] As in their birth, wherein they are not guilty,

[001-040] Since nature cannot choose his origin,

[001-041] By their o’ergrowth of some complexion,

[001-042] Oft breaking down the pales and forts of reason;

[001-043] Or by some habit, that too much o’erleavens

[001-044] The form of plausive manners;—that these men,

[001-045] Carrying, I say, the stamp of one defect,

[001-046] Being Nature’s livery or Fortune’s star,—

[001-047] His virtues else,—be they as pure as grace,

[001-048] As infinite as man may undergo,

[001-049] Shall in the general censure take corruption

[001-050] From that particular fault. The dram of evil

[001-051] Doth all the noble substance of a doubt

[001-052] To his own scandal.

[001-053] HORATIO.

[001-054] Look, my lord, it comes!

[001-055] Enter Ghost.

[001-056] HAMLET.

[001-057] Angels and ministers of grace defend us!

[001-058] Be thou a spirit of health or goblin damn’d,

[001-059] Bring with thee airs from heaven or blasts from hell,

[001-060] Be thy intents wicked or charitable,

[001-061] Thou com’st in such a questionable shape

[001-062] That I will speak to thee. I’ll call thee Hamlet,

[001-063] King, father, royal Dane. O, answer me!

[001-064] Let me not burst in ignorance; but tell

[001-065] Why thy canoniz’d bones, hearsed in death,

[001-066] Have burst their cerements; why the sepulchre,

[001-067] Wherein we saw thee quietly inurn’d,

[001-068] Hath op’d his ponderous and marble jaws

[001-069] To cast thee up again! What may this mean,

[001-070] That thou, dead corse, again in complete steel,

[001-071] Revisit’st thus the glimpses of the moon,

[001-072] Making night hideous, and we fools of nature

[001-073] So horridly to shake our disposition

[001-074] With thoughts beyond the reaches of our souls?

[001-075] Say, why is this? Wherefore? What should we do?

[001-076] [Ghost beckons Hamlet.]

[001-077] HORATIO.

[001-078] It beckons you to go away with it,

[001-079] As if it some impartment did desire

[001-080] To you alone.

[001-081] MARCELLUS.

[001-082] Look with what courteous action

[001-083] It waves you to a more removed ground.

[001-084] But do not go with it.

[001-085] HORATIO.

[001-086] No, by no means.

[001-087] HAMLET.

[001-088] It will not speak; then will I follow it.

[001-089] HORATIO.

[001-090] Do not, my lord.

[001-091] HAMLET.

[001-092] Why, what should be the fear?

[001-093] I do not set my life at a pin’s fee;

[001-094] And for my soul, what can it do to that,

[001-095] Being a thing immortal as itself?

[001-096] It waves me forth again. I’ll follow it.

[001-097] HORATIO.

[001-098] What if it tempt you toward the flood, my lord,

[001-099] Or to the dreadful summit of the cliff

[001-100] That beetles o’er his base into the sea,

[001-101] And there assume some other horrible form

[001-102] Which might deprive your sovereignty of reason,

[001-103] And draw you into madness? Think of it.

[001-104] The very place puts toys of desperation,

[001-105] Without more motive, into every brain

[001-106] That looks so many fathoms to the sea

[001-107] And hears it roar beneath.

[001-108] HAMLET.

[001-109] It waves me still.

[001-110] Go on, I’ll follow thee.

[001-111] MARCELLUS.

[001-112] You shall not go, my lord.

[001-113] HAMLET.

[001-114] Hold off your hands.

[001-115] HORATIO.

[001-116] Be rul’d; you shall not go.

[001-117] HAMLET.

[001-118] My fate cries out,

[001-119] And makes each petty artery in this body

[001-120] As hardy as the Nemean lion’s nerve.

[001-121] [Ghost beckons.]

[001-122] Still am I call’d. Unhand me, gentlemen.

[001-123] [Breaking free from them.]

[001-124] By heaven, I’ll make a ghost of him that lets me.

[001-125] I say, away!—Go on, I’ll follow thee.

[001-126] [Exeunt Ghost and Hamlet.]

[001-127] HORATIO.

[001-128] He waxes desperate with imagination.

[001-129] MARCELLUS.

[001-130] Let’s follow; ’tis not fit thus to obey him.

[001-131] HORATIO.

[001-132] Have after. To what issue will this come?

[001-133] MARCELLUS.

[001-134] Something is rotten in the state of Denmark.

[001-135] HORATIO.

[001-136] Heaven will direct it.

[001-137] MARCELLUS.

[001-138] Nay, let’s follow him.

[001-139] [Exeunt.]
