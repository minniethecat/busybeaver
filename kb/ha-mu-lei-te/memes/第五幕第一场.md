# 第五幕 第一场

[005-001] Enter two Clowns with spades, &c.

[005-002] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-003] Is she to be buried in Christian burial, when she wilfully seeks her own

[005-004] salvation?

[005-005] SECOND CLOWN.

[005-006] I tell thee she is, and therefore make her grave straight. The crowner hath sat

[005-007] on her, and finds it Christian burial.

[005-008] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-009] How can that be, unless she drowned herself in her own defence?

[005-010] SECOND CLOWN.

[005-011] Why, ’tis found so.

[005-012] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-013] It must be se offendendo, it cannot be else. For here lies the point: if

[005-014] I drown myself wittingly, it argues an act: and an act hath three branches. It

[005-015] is to act, to do, and to perform: argal, she drowned herself wittingly.

[005-016] SECOND CLOWN.

[005-017] Nay, but hear you, goodman delver,—

[005-018] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-019] Give me leave. Here lies the water; good. Here stands the man; good. If the man

[005-020] go to this water and drown himself, it is, will he nill he, he goes,—mark you

[005-021] that. But if the water come to him and drown him, he drowns not himself. Argal,

[005-022] he that is not guilty of his own death shortens not his own life.

[005-023] SECOND CLOWN.

[005-024] But is this law?

[005-025] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-026] Ay, marry, is’t, crowner’s quest law.

[005-027] SECOND CLOWN.

[005-028] Will you ha’ the truth on’t? If this had not been a gentlewoman, she should

[005-029] have been buried out o’ Christian burial.

[005-030] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-031] Why, there thou say’st. And the more pity that great folk should have

[005-032] countenance in this world to drown or hang themselves more than their even

[005-033] Christian. Come, my spade. There is no ancient gentlemen but gardeners,

[005-034] ditchers, and grave-makers: they hold up Adam’s profession.

[005-035] SECOND CLOWN.

[005-036] Was he a gentleman?

[005-037] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-038] He was the first that ever bore arms.

[005-039] SECOND CLOWN.

[005-040] Why, he had none.

[005-041] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-042] What, art a heathen? How dost thou understand the Scripture? The Scripture says

[005-043] Adam digg’d. Could he dig without arms? I’ll put another question to thee. If

[005-044] thou answerest me not to the purpose, confess thyself—

[005-045] SECOND CLOWN.

[005-046] Go to.

[005-047] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-048] What is he that builds stronger than either the mason, the shipwright, or the

[005-049] carpenter?

[005-050] SECOND CLOWN.

[005-051] The gallows-maker; for that frame outlives a thousand tenants.

[005-052] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-053] I like thy wit well in good faith, the gallows does well. But how does it well?

[005-054] It does well to those that do ill. Now, thou dost ill to say the gallows is

[005-055] built stronger than the church; argal, the gallows may do well to thee. To’t

[005-056] again, come.

[005-057] SECOND CLOWN.

[005-058] Who builds stronger than a mason, a shipwright, or a carpenter?

[005-059] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-060] Ay, tell me that, and unyoke.

[005-061] SECOND CLOWN.

[005-062] Marry, now I can tell.

[005-063] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-064] To’t.

[005-065] SECOND CLOWN.

[005-066] Mass, I cannot tell.

[005-067] Enter Hamlet and Horatio, at a distance.

[005-068] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-069] Cudgel thy brains no more about it, for your dull ass will not mend his pace

[005-070] with beating; and when you are asked this question next, say ‘a grave-maker’.

[005-071] The houses he makes last till doomsday. Go, get thee to Yaughan; fetch me a

[005-072] stoup of liquor.

[005-073] [Exit Second Clown.]

[005-074] [Digs and sings.]

[005-075] In youth when I did love, did love,

[005-076] Methought it was very sweet;

[005-077] To contract, O, the time for, a, my behove,

[005-078] O methought there was nothing meet.

[005-079] HAMLET.

[005-080] Has this fellow no feeling of his business, that he sings at grave-making?

[005-081] HORATIO.

[005-082] Custom hath made it in him a property of easiness.

[005-083] HAMLET.

[005-084] ’Tis e’en so; the hand of little employment hath the daintier sense.

[005-085] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-086] [Sings.]

[005-087] But age with his stealing steps

[005-088] Hath claw’d me in his clutch,

[005-089] And hath shipp’d me into the land,

[005-090] As if I had never been such.

[005-091] [Throws up a skull.]

[005-092] HAMLET.

[005-093] That skull had a tongue in it, and could sing once. How the knave jowls it to

[005-094] th’ ground, as if ’twere Cain’s jawbone, that did the first murder! This might

[005-095] be the pate of a politician which this ass now o’er-offices, one that would

[005-096] circumvent God, might it not?

[005-097] HORATIO.

[005-098] It might, my lord.

[005-099] HAMLET.

[005-100] Or of a courtier, which could say ‘Good morrow, sweet lord! How dost thou, good

[005-101] lord?’ This might be my lord such-a-one, that praised my lord such-a-one’s

[005-102] horse when he meant to beg it, might it not?

[005-103] HORATIO.

[005-104] Ay, my lord.

[005-105] HAMLET.

[005-106] Why, e’en so: and now my Lady Worm’s; chapless, and knocked about the mazard

[005-107] with a sexton’s spade. Here’s fine revolution, an we had the trick to see’t.

[005-108] Did these bones cost no more the breeding but to play at loggets with ’em? Mine

[005-109] ache to think on’t.

[005-110] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-111] [Sings.]

[005-112] A pickaxe and a spade, a spade,

[005-113] For and a shrouding-sheet;

[005-114] O, a pit of clay for to be made

[005-115] For such a guest is meet.

[005-116] [Throws up another skull.]

[005-117] HAMLET.

[005-118] There’s another. Why may not that be the skull of a lawyer? Where be his

[005-119] quiddits now, his quillets, his cases, his tenures, and his tricks? Why does he

[005-120] suffer this rude knave now to knock him about the sconce with a dirty shovel,

[005-121] and will not tell him of his action of battery? Hum. This fellow might be in’s

[005-122] time a great buyer of land, with his statutes, his recognizances, his fines,

[005-123] his double vouchers, his recoveries. Is this the fine of his fines, and the

[005-124] recovery of his recoveries, to have his fine pate full of fine dirt? Will his

[005-125] vouchers vouch him no more of his purchases, and double ones too, than the

[005-126] length and breadth of a pair of indentures? The very conveyances of his lands

[005-127] will scarcely lie in this box; and must the inheritor himself have no more, ha?

[005-128] HORATIO.

[005-129] Not a jot more, my lord.

[005-130] HAMLET.

[005-131] Is not parchment made of sheep-skins?

[005-132] HORATIO.

[005-133] Ay, my lord, and of calf-skins too.

[005-134] HAMLET.

[005-135] They are sheep and calves which seek out assurance in that. I will speak to

[005-136] this fellow.—Whose grave’s this, sir?

[005-137] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-138] Mine, sir.

[005-139] [Sings.]

[005-140] O, a pit of clay for to be made

[005-141] For such a guest is meet.

[005-142] HAMLET.

[005-143] I think it be thine indeed, for thou liest in’t.

[005-144] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-145] You lie out on’t, sir, and therefore ’tis not yours.

[005-146] For my part, I do not lie in’t, yet it is mine.

[005-147] HAMLET.

[005-148] Thou dost lie in’t, to be in’t and say it is thine. ’Tis for the dead, not for

[005-149] the quick; therefore thou liest.

[005-150] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-151] ’Tis a quick lie, sir; ’t will away again from me to you.

[005-152] HAMLET.

[005-153] What man dost thou dig it for?

[005-154] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-155] For no man, sir.

[005-156] HAMLET.

[005-157] What woman then?

[005-158] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-159] For none neither.

[005-160] HAMLET.

[005-161] Who is to be buried in’t?

[005-162] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-163] One that was a woman, sir; but, rest her soul, she’s dead.

[005-164] HAMLET.

[005-165] How absolute the knave is! We must speak by the card, or equivocation will undo

[005-166] us. By the Lord, Horatio, these three years I have taken note of it, the age is

[005-167] grown so picked that the toe of the peasant comes so near the heel of the

[005-168] courtier he galls his kibe.—How long hast thou been a grave-maker?

[005-169] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-170] Of all the days i’ th’ year, I came to’t that day that our last King Hamlet

[005-171] o’ercame Fortinbras.

[005-172] HAMLET.

[005-173] How long is that since?

[005-174] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-175] Cannot you tell that? Every fool can tell that. It was the very day that young

[005-176] Hamlet was born,—he that is mad, and sent into England.

[005-177] HAMLET.

[005-178] Ay, marry, why was he sent into England?

[005-179] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-180] Why, because he was mad; he shall recover his wits there; or if he do not, it’s

[005-181] no great matter there.

[005-182] HAMLET.

[005-183] Why?

[005-184] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-185] ’Twill not be seen in him there; there the men are as mad as he.

[005-186] HAMLET.

[005-187] How came he mad?

[005-188] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-189] Very strangely, they say.

[005-190] HAMLET.

[005-191] How strangely?

[005-192] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-193] Faith, e’en with losing his wits.

[005-194] HAMLET.

[005-195] Upon what ground?

[005-196] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-197] Why, here in Denmark. I have been sexton here, man and boy, thirty years.

[005-198] HAMLET.

[005-199] How long will a man lie i’ th’earth ere he rot?

[005-200] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-201] Faith, if he be not rotten before he die,—as we have many pocky corses nowadays

[005-202] that will scarce hold the laying in,—he will last you some eight year or nine

[005-203] year. A tanner will last you nine year.

[005-204] HAMLET.

[005-205] Why he more than another?

[005-206] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-207] Why, sir, his hide is so tann’d with his trade that he will keep out water a

[005-208] great while. And your water is a sore decayer of your whoreson dead body.

[005-209] Here’s a skull now; this skull hath lain in the earth three-and-twenty years.

[005-210] HAMLET.

[005-211] Whose was it?

[005-212] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-213] A whoreson, mad fellow’s it was. Whose do you think it was?

[005-214] HAMLET.

[005-215] Nay, I know not.

[005-216] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-217] A pestilence on him for a mad rogue! A pour’d a flagon of Rhenish on my head

[005-218] once. This same skull, sir, was Yorick’s skull, the King’s jester.

[005-219] HAMLET.

[005-220] This?

[005-221] FIRST CLOWN.

[005-222] E’en that.

[005-223] HAMLET.

[005-224] Let me see. [Takes the skull.] Alas, poor Yorick. I knew him, Horatio, a

[005-225] fellow of infinite jest, of most excellent fancy. He hath borne me on his back

[005-226] a thousand times; and now, how abhorred in my imagination it is! My gorge rises

[005-227] at it. Here hung those lips that I have kiss’d I know not how oft. Where be

[005-228] your gibes now? your gambols? your songs? your flashes of merriment, that were

[005-229] wont to set the table on a roar? Not one now, to mock your own grinning? Quite

[005-230] chop-fallen? Now get you to my lady’s chamber, and tell her, let her paint an

[005-231] inch thick, to this favour she must come. Make her laugh at that.—Prithee,

[005-232] Horatio, tell me one thing.

[005-233] HORATIO.

[005-234] What’s that, my lord?

[005-235] HAMLET.

[005-236] Dost thou think Alexander looked o’ this fashion i’ th’earth?

[005-237] HORATIO.

[005-238] E’en so.

[005-239] HAMLET.

[005-240] And smelt so? Pah!

[005-241] [Throws down the skull.]

[005-242] HORATIO.

[005-243] E’en so, my lord.

[005-244] HAMLET.

[005-245] To what base uses we may return, Horatio! Why may not imagination trace the

[005-246] noble dust of Alexander till he find it stopping a bung-hole?

[005-247] HORATIO.

[005-248] ’Twere to consider too curiously to consider so.

[005-249] HAMLET.

[005-250] No, faith, not a jot. But to follow him thither with modesty enough, and

[005-251] likelihood to lead it; as thus. Alexander died, Alexander was buried, Alexander

[005-252] returneth into dust; the dust is earth; of earth we make loam; and why of that

[005-253] loam whereto he was converted might they not stop a beer-barrel?

[005-254] Imperious Caesar, dead and turn’d to clay,

[005-255] Might stop a hole to keep the wind away.

[005-256] O, that that earth which kept the world in awe

[005-257] Should patch a wall t’expel the winter’s flaw.

[005-258] But soft! but soft! aside! Here comes the King.

[005-259] Enter priests, &c, in procession; the corpse

[005-260] of Ophelia, Laertes and Mourners following; King,

[005-261] Queen, their Trains, &c.

[005-262] The Queen, the courtiers. Who is that they follow?

[005-263] And with such maimed rites? This doth betoken

[005-264] The corse they follow did with desperate hand

[005-265] Fordo it own life. ’Twas of some estate.

[005-266] Couch we awhile and mark.

[005-267] [Retiring with Horatio.]

[005-268] LAERTES.

[005-269] What ceremony else?

[005-270] HAMLET.

[005-271] That is Laertes, a very noble youth. Mark.

[005-272] LAERTES.

[005-273] What ceremony else?

[005-274] PRIEST.

[005-275] Her obsequies have been as far enlarg’d

[005-276] As we have warranties. Her death was doubtful;

[005-277] And but that great command o’ersways the order,

[005-278] She should in ground unsanctified have lodg’d

[005-279] Till the last trumpet. For charitable prayers,

[005-280] Shards, flints, and pebbles should be thrown on her.

[005-281] Yet here she is allowed her virgin rites,

[005-282] Her maiden strewments, and the bringing home

[005-283] Of bell and burial.

[005-284] LAERTES.

[005-285] Must there no more be done?

[005-286] PRIEST.

[005-287] No more be done.

[005-288] We should profane the service of the dead

[005-289] To sing sage requiem and such rest to her

[005-290] As to peace-parted souls.

[005-291] LAERTES.

[005-292] Lay her i’ th’earth,

[005-293] And from her fair and unpolluted flesh

[005-294] May violets spring. I tell thee, churlish priest,

[005-295] A minist’ring angel shall my sister be

[005-296] When thou liest howling.

[005-297] HAMLET.

[005-298] What, the fair Ophelia?

[005-299] QUEEN.

[005-300] [Scattering flowers.] Sweets to the sweet. Farewell.

[005-301] I hop’d thou shouldst have been my Hamlet’s wife;

[005-302] I thought thy bride-bed to have deck’d, sweet maid,

[005-303] And not have strew’d thy grave.

[005-304] LAERTES.

[005-305] O, treble woe

[005-306] Fall ten times treble on that cursed head

[005-307] Whose wicked deed thy most ingenious sense

[005-308] Depriv’d thee of. Hold off the earth a while,

[005-309] Till I have caught her once more in mine arms.

[005-310] [Leaps into the grave.]

[005-311] Now pile your dust upon the quick and dead,

[005-312] Till of this flat a mountain you have made,

[005-313] To o’ertop old Pelion or the skyish head

[005-314] Of blue Olympus.

[005-315] HAMLET.

[005-316] [Advancing.]

[005-317] What is he whose grief

[005-318] Bears such an emphasis? whose phrase of sorrow

[005-319] Conjures the wand’ring stars, and makes them stand

[005-320] Like wonder-wounded hearers? This is I,

[005-321] Hamlet the Dane.

[005-322] [Leaps into the grave.]

[005-323] LAERTES.

[005-324] [Grappling with him.] The devil take thy soul!

[005-325] HAMLET.

[005-326] Thou pray’st not well.

[005-327] I prithee take thy fingers from my throat;

[005-328] For though I am not splenative and rash,

[005-329] Yet have I in me something dangerous,

[005-330] Which let thy wiseness fear. Away thy hand!

[005-331] KING.

[005-332] Pluck them asunder.

[005-333] QUEEN.

[005-334] Hamlet! Hamlet!

[005-335] All.

[005-336] Gentlemen!

[005-337] HORATIO.

[005-338] Good my lord, be quiet.

[005-339] [The Attendants part them, and they come out

[005-340] of the grave.]

[005-341] HAMLET.

[005-342] Why, I will fight with him upon this theme

[005-343] Until my eyelids will no longer wag.

[005-344] QUEEN.

[005-345] O my son, what theme?

[005-346] HAMLET.

[005-347] I lov’d Ophelia; forty thousand brothers

[005-348] Could not, with all their quantity of love,

[005-349] Make up my sum. What wilt thou do for her?

[005-350] KING.

[005-351] O, he is mad, Laertes.

[005-352] QUEEN.

[005-353] For love of God forbear him!

[005-354] HAMLET.

[005-355] ’Swounds, show me what thou’lt do:

[005-356] Woul’t weep? woul’t fight? woul’t fast? woul’t tear thyself?

[005-357] Woul’t drink up eisel? eat a crocodile?

[005-358] I’ll do’t. Dost thou come here to whine?

[005-359] To outface me with leaping in her grave?

[005-360] Be buried quick with her, and so will I.

[005-361] And if thou prate of mountains, let them throw

[005-362] Millions of acres on us, till our ground,

[005-363] Singeing his pate against the burning zone,

[005-364] Make Ossa like a wart. Nay, an thou’lt mouth,

[005-365] I’ll rant as well as thou.

[005-366] QUEEN.

[005-367] This is mere madness:

[005-368] And thus awhile the fit will work on him;

[005-369] Anon, as patient as the female dove,

[005-370] When that her golden couplets are disclos’d,

[005-371] His silence will sit drooping.

[005-372] HAMLET.

[005-373] Hear you, sir;

[005-374] What is the reason that you use me thus?

[005-375] I lov’d you ever. But it is no matter.

[005-376] Let Hercules himself do what he may,

[005-377] The cat will mew, and dog will have his day.

[005-378] [Exit.]

[005-379] KING.

[005-380] I pray thee, good Horatio, wait upon him.

[005-381] [Exit Horatio.]

[005-382] [To Laertes]

[005-383] Strengthen your patience in our last night’s speech;

[005-384] We’ll put the matter to the present push.—

[005-385] Good Gertrude, set some watch over your son.

[005-386] This grave shall have a living monument.

[005-387] An hour of quiet shortly shall we see;

[005-388] Till then in patience our proceeding be.

[005-389] [Exeunt.]
