# 第四幕 第二场

[004-001] the Castle.

[004-002] Enter Hamlet.

[004-003] HAMLET.

[004-004] Safely stowed.

[004-005] ROSENCRANTZ and GUILDENSTERN.

[004-006] [Within.] Hamlet! Lord Hamlet!

[004-007] HAMLET.

[004-008] What noise? Who calls on Hamlet? O, here they come.

[004-009] Enter Rosencrantz and Guildenstern.

[004-010] ROSENCRANTZ.

[004-011] What have you done, my lord, with the dead body?

[004-012] HAMLET.

[004-013] Compounded it with dust, whereto ’tis kin.

[004-014] ROSENCRANTZ.

[004-015] Tell us where ’tis, that we may take it thence,

[004-016] And bear it to the chapel.

[004-017] HAMLET.

[004-018] Do not believe it.

[004-019] ROSENCRANTZ.

[004-020] Believe what?

[004-021] HAMLET.

[004-022] That I can keep your counsel, and not mine own. Besides, to be demanded of a

[004-023] sponge—what replication should be made by the son of a king?

[004-024] ROSENCRANTZ.

[004-025] Take you me for a sponge, my lord?

[004-026] HAMLET.

[004-027] Ay, sir; that soaks up the King’s countenance, his rewards, his authorities.

[004-028] But such officers do the King best service in the end: he keeps them, like an

[004-029] ape, in the corner of his jaw; first mouthed, to be last swallowed: when he

[004-030] needs what you have gleaned, it is but squeezing you, and, sponge, you shall be

[004-031] dry again.

[004-032] ROSENCRANTZ.

[004-033] I understand you not, my lord.

[004-034] HAMLET.

[004-035] I am glad of it. A knavish speech sleeps in a foolish ear.

[004-036] ROSENCRANTZ.

[004-037] My lord, you must tell us where the body is and go with us to the King.

[004-038] HAMLET.

[004-039] The body is with the King, but the King is not with the body. The King is a

[004-040] thing—

[004-041] GUILDENSTERN.

[004-042] A thing, my lord!

[004-043] HAMLET.

[004-044] Of nothing. Bring me to him. Hide fox, and all after.

[004-045] [Exeunt.]
