# 第二幕 第二场

[002-001] Castle.

[002-002] Enter King, Queen, Rosencrantz, Guildenstern and

[002-003] Attendants.

[002-004] KING.

[002-005] Welcome, dear Rosencrantz and Guildenstern.

[002-006] Moreover that we much did long to see you,

[002-007] The need we have to use you did provoke

[002-008] Our hasty sending. Something have you heard

[002-009] Of Hamlet’s transformation; so I call it,

[002-010] Since nor th’exterior nor the inward man

[002-011] Resembles that it was. What it should be,

[002-012] More than his father’s death, that thus hath put him

[002-013] So much from th’understanding of himself,

[002-014] I cannot dream of. I entreat you both

[002-015] That, being of so young days brought up with him,

[002-016] And since so neighbour’d to his youth and humour,

[002-017] That you vouchsafe your rest here in our court

[002-018] Some little time, so by your companies

[002-019] To draw him on to pleasures and to gather,

[002-020] So much as from occasion you may glean,

[002-021] Whether aught to us unknown afflicts him thus

[002-022] That, open’d, lies within our remedy.

[002-023] QUEEN.

[002-024] Good gentlemen, he hath much talk’d of you,

[002-025] And sure I am, two men there are not living

[002-026] To whom he more adheres. If it will please you

[002-027] To show us so much gentry and good will

[002-028] As to expend your time with us awhile,

[002-029] For the supply and profit of our hope,

[002-030] Your visitation shall receive such thanks

[002-031] As fits a king’s remembrance.

[002-032] ROSENCRANTZ.

[002-033] Both your majesties

[002-034] Might, by the sovereign power you have of us,

[002-035] Put your dread pleasures more into command

[002-036] Than to entreaty.

[002-037] GUILDENSTERN.

[002-038] We both obey,

[002-039] And here give up ourselves, in the full bent,

[002-040] To lay our service freely at your feet

[002-041] To be commanded.

[002-042] KING.

[002-043] Thanks, Rosencrantz and gentle Guildenstern.

[002-044] QUEEN.

[002-045] Thanks, Guildenstern and gentle Rosencrantz.

[002-046] And I beseech you instantly to visit

[002-047] My too much changed son. Go, some of you,

[002-048] And bring these gentlemen where Hamlet is.

[002-049] GUILDENSTERN.

[002-050] Heavens make our presence and our practices

[002-051] Pleasant and helpful to him.

[002-052] QUEEN.

[002-053] Ay, amen.

[002-054] [Exeunt Rosencrantz, Guildenstern and some

[002-055] Attendants.]

[002-058] Th’ambassadors from Norway, my good lord,

[002-059] Are joyfully return’d.

[002-060] KING.

[002-061] Thou still hast been the father of good news.

[002-062] POLONIUS.

[002-063] Have I, my lord? Assure you, my good liege,

[002-064] I hold my duty, as I hold my soul,

[002-065] Both to my God and to my gracious King:

[002-066] And I do think,—or else this brain of mine

[002-067] Hunts not the trail of policy so sure

[002-068] As it hath us’d to do—that I have found

[002-069] The very cause of Hamlet’s lunacy.

[002-070] KING.

[002-071] O speak of that, that do I long to hear.

[002-072] POLONIUS.

[002-073] Give first admittance to th’ambassadors;

[002-074] My news shall be the fruit to that great feast.

[002-075] KING.

[002-076] Thyself do grace to them, and bring them in.

[002-077] [Exit Polonius.]

[002-078] He tells me, my sweet queen, that he hath found

[002-079] The head and source of all your son’s distemper.

[002-080] QUEEN.

[002-081] I doubt it is no other but the main,

[002-082] His father’s death and our o’erhasty marriage.

[002-083] KING.

[002-084] Well, we shall sift him.

[002-085] Enter Polonius with Voltemand and Cornelius.

[002-086] Welcome, my good friends!

[002-087] Say, Voltemand, what from our brother Norway?

[002-088] VOLTEMAND.

[002-089] Most fair return of greetings and desires.

[002-090] Upon our first, he sent out to suppress

[002-091] His nephew’s levies, which to him appear’d

[002-092] To be a preparation ’gainst the Polack;

[002-093] But better look’d into, he truly found

[002-094] It was against your Highness; whereat griev’d,

[002-095] That so his sickness, age, and impotence

[002-096] Was falsely borne in hand, sends out arrests

[002-097] On Fortinbras; which he, in brief, obeys,

[002-098] Receives rebuke from Norway; and in fine,

[002-099] Makes vow before his uncle never more

[002-100] To give th’assay of arms against your Majesty.

[002-101] Whereon old Norway, overcome with joy,

[002-102] Gives him three thousand crowns in annual fee,

[002-103] And his commission to employ those soldiers

[002-104] So levied as before, against the Polack:

[002-105] With an entreaty, herein further shown,

[002-106] [Gives a paper.]

[002-107] That it might please you to give quiet pass

[002-108] Through your dominions for this enterprise,

[002-109] On such regards of safety and allowance

[002-110] As therein are set down.

[002-111] KING.

[002-112] It likes us well;

[002-113] And at our more consider’d time we’ll read,

[002-114] Answer, and think upon this business.

[002-115] Meantime we thank you for your well-took labour.

[002-116] Go to your rest, at night we’ll feast together:.

[002-117] Most welcome home.

[002-118] [Exeunt Voltemand and Cornelius.]

[002-119] POLONIUS.

[002-120] This business is well ended.

[002-121] My liege and madam, to expostulate

[002-122] What majesty should be, what duty is,

[002-123] Why day is day, night night, and time is time

[002-124] Were nothing but to waste night, day and time.

[002-125] Therefore, since brevity is the soul of wit,

[002-126] And tediousness the limbs and outward flourishes,

[002-127] I will be brief. Your noble son is mad.

[002-128] Mad call I it; for to define true madness,

[002-129] What is’t but to be nothing else but mad?

[002-130] But let that go.

[002-131] QUEEN.

[002-132] More matter, with less art.

[002-133] POLONIUS.

[002-134] Madam, I swear I use no art at all.

[002-135] That he is mad, ’tis true: ’tis true ’tis pity;

[002-136] And pity ’tis ’tis true. A foolish figure,

[002-137] But farewell it, for I will use no art.

[002-138] Mad let us grant him then. And now remains

[002-139] That we find out the cause of this effect,

[002-140] Or rather say, the cause of this defect,

[002-141] For this effect defective comes by cause.

[002-142] Thus it remains, and the remainder thus. Perpend,

[002-143] I have a daughter—have whilst she is mine—

[002-144] Who in her duty and obedience, mark,

[002-145] Hath given me this. Now gather, and surmise.

[002-146] [Reads.]

[002-147] To the celestial, and my soul’s idol, the most beautified Ophelia—

[002-148] That’s an ill phrase, a vile phrase; ‘beautified’ is a vile

[002-149] phrase: but you shall hear.

[002-150] [Reads.]

[002-151] these; in her excellent white bosom, these, &c.

[002-152] QUEEN.

[002-153] Came this from Hamlet to her?

[002-154] POLONIUS.

[002-155] Good madam, stay awhile; I will be faithful.

[002-156] [Reads.]

[002-157] Doubt thou the stars are fire,

[002-158] Doubt that the sun doth move,

[002-159] Doubt truth to be a liar,

[002-160] But never doubt I love.

[002-161] O dear Ophelia, I am ill at these numbers. I have not art to reckon my groans.

[002-162] But that I love thee best, O most best, believe it. Adieu.

[002-163] Thine evermore, most dear lady, whilst this machine is to him,

[002-164] HAMLET.

[002-165] This in obedience hath my daughter show’d me;

[002-166] And more above, hath his solicitings,

[002-167] As they fell out by time, by means, and place,

[002-168] All given to mine ear.

[002-169] KING.

[002-170] But how hath she receiv’d his love?

[002-171] POLONIUS.

[002-172] What do you think of me?

[002-173] KING.

[002-174] As of a man faithful and honourable.

[002-175] POLONIUS.

[002-176] I would fain prove so. But what might you think,

[002-177] When I had seen this hot love on the wing,

[002-178] As I perceiv’d it, I must tell you that,

[002-179] Before my daughter told me, what might you,

[002-180] Or my dear Majesty your queen here, think,

[002-181] If I had play’d the desk or table-book,

[002-182] Or given my heart a winking, mute and dumb,

[002-183] Or look’d upon this love with idle sight,

[002-184] What might you think? No, I went round to work,

[002-185] And my young mistress thus I did bespeak:

[002-186] ‘Lord Hamlet is a prince, out of thy star.

[002-187] This must not be.’ And then I precepts gave her,

[002-188] That she should lock herself from his resort,

[002-189] Admit no messengers, receive no tokens.

[002-190] Which done, she took the fruits of my advice,

[002-191] And he, repulsed,—a short tale to make—

[002-192] Fell into a sadness, then into a fast,

[002-193] Thence to a watch, thence into a weakness,

[002-194] Thence to a lightness, and, by this declension,

[002-195] Into the madness wherein now he raves,

[002-196] And all we wail for.

[002-197] KING.

[002-198] Do you think ’tis this?

[002-199] QUEEN.

[002-200] It may be, very likely.

[002-201] POLONIUS.

[002-202] Hath there been such a time, I’d fain know that,

[002-203] That I have positively said ‘’Tis so,’

[002-204] When it prov’d otherwise?

[002-205] KING.

[002-206] Not that I know.

[002-207] POLONIUS.

[002-208] Take this from this, if this be otherwise.

[002-209] [Points to his head and shoulder.]

[002-210] If circumstances lead me, I will find

[002-211] Where truth is hid, though it were hid indeed

[002-212] Within the centre.

[002-214] How may we try it further?

[002-215] POLONIUS.

[002-216] You know sometimes he walks four hours together

[002-218] QUEEN.

[002-219] So he does indeed.

[002-220] POLONIUS.

[002-221] At such a time I’ll loose my daughter to him.

[002-222] Be you and I behind an arras then,

[002-223] Mark the encounter. If he love her not,

[002-224] And be not from his reason fall’n thereon,

[002-225] Let me be no assistant for a state,

[002-226] But keep a farm and carters.

[002-227] KING.

[002-228] We will try it.

[002-232] POLONIUS.

[002-233] Away, I do beseech you, both away

[002-244] Not I, my lord.

[002-297] Fare you well, my lord.
