# 第三幕 第二场

[003-001] Castle.

[003-002] Enter Hamlet and certain Players.

[003-003] HAMLET.

[003-004] Speak the speech, I pray you, as I pronounced it to you, trippingly on the

[003-005] tongue. But if you mouth it, as many of your players do, I had as lief the

[003-006] town-crier spoke my lines. Nor do not saw the air too much with your hand,

[003-007] thus, but use all gently; for in the very torrent, tempest, and, as I may say,

[003-008] whirlwind of passion, you must acquire and beget a temperance that may give it

[003-009] smoothness. O, it offends me to the soul to hear a robustious periwig-pated

[003-010] fellow tear a passion to tatters, to very rags, to split the ears of the

[003-011] groundlings, who, for the most part, are capable of nothing but inexplicable

[003-012] dumb shows and noise. I would have such a fellow whipped for o’erdoing

[003-013] Termagant. It out-Herods Herod. Pray you avoid it.

[003-014] FIRST PLAYER.

[003-015] I warrant your honour.

[003-016] HAMLET.

[003-017] Be not too tame neither; but let your own discretion be your tutor. Suit the

[003-018] action to the word, the word to the action, with this special observance, that

[003-019] you o’erstep not the modesty of nature; for anything so overdone is from the

[003-020] purpose of playing, whose end, both at the first and now, was and is, to hold

[003-021] as ’twere the mirror up to nature; to show virtue her own feature, scorn her

[003-022] own image, and the very age and body of the time his form and pressure. Now,

[003-023] this overdone, or come tardy off, though it make the unskilful laugh, cannot

[003-024] but make the judicious grieve; the censure of the which one must in your

[003-025] allowance o’erweigh a whole theatre of others. O, there be players that I have

[003-026] seen play—and heard others praise, and that highly—not to speak it profanely,

[003-027] that, neither having the accent of Christians, nor the gait of Christian,

[003-028] pagan, nor man, have so strutted and bellowed that I have thought some of

[003-029] Nature’s journeymen had made men, and not made them well, they imitated

[003-030] humanity so abominably.

[003-031] FIRST PLAYER.

[003-032] I hope we have reform’d that indifferently with us, sir.

[003-033] HAMLET.

[003-034] O reform it altogether. And let those that play your clowns speak no more than

[003-035] is set down for them. For there be of them that will themselves laugh, to set

[003-036] on some quantity of barren spectators to laugh too, though in the meantime some

[003-037] necessary question of the play be then to be considered. That’s villainous, and

[003-038] shows a most pitiful ambition in the fool that uses it. Go make you ready.

[003-039] [Exeunt Players.]

[003-040] Enter Polonius, Rosencrantz and Guildenstern.

[003-041] How now, my lord?

[003-042] Will the King hear this piece of work?

[003-043] POLONIUS.

[003-044] And the Queen too, and that presently.

[003-045] HAMLET.

[003-046] Bid the players make haste.

[003-047] [Exit Polonius.]

[003-048] Will you two help to hasten them?

[003-049] ROSENCRANTZ and GUILDENSTERN.

[003-050] We will, my lord.

[003-051] [Exeunt Rosencrantz and Guildenstern.]

[003-052] HAMLET.

[003-053] What ho, Horatio!

[003-054] Enter Horatio.

[003-055] HORATIO.

[003-056] Here, sweet lord, at your service.

[003-057] HAMLET.

[003-058] Horatio, thou art e’en as just a man

[003-059] As e’er my conversation cop’d withal.

[003-060] HORATIO.

[003-061] O my dear lord.

[003-062] HAMLET.

[003-063] Nay, do not think I flatter;

[003-064] For what advancement may I hope from thee,

[003-065] That no revenue hast, but thy good spirits

[003-066] To feed and clothe thee? Why should the poor be flatter’d?

[003-067] No, let the candied tongue lick absurd pomp,

[003-068] And crook the pregnant hinges of the knee

[003-069] Where thrift may follow fawning. Dost thou hear?

[003-070] Since my dear soul was mistress of her choice,

[003-071] And could of men distinguish, her election

[003-072] Hath seal’d thee for herself. For thou hast been

[003-073] As one, in suffering all, that suffers nothing,

[003-074] A man that Fortune’s buffets and rewards

[003-075] Hast ta’en with equal thanks. And blessed are those

[003-076] Whose blood and judgement are so well co-mingled

[003-077] That they are not a pipe for Fortune’s finger

[003-078] To sound what stop she please. Give me that man

[003-079] That is not passion’s slave, and I will wear him

[003-080] In my heart’s core, ay, in my heart of heart,

[003-081] As I do thee. Something too much of this.

[003-082] There is a play tonight before the King.

[003-083] One scene of it comes near the circumstance

[003-084] Which I have told thee, of my father’s death.

[003-085] I prithee, when thou see’st that act a-foot,

[003-086] Even with the very comment of thy soul

[003-087] Observe mine uncle. If his occulted guilt

[003-088] Do not itself unkennel in one speech,

[003-089] It is a damned ghost that we have seen;

[003-090] And my imaginations are as foul

[003-091] As Vulcan’s stithy. Give him heedful note;

[003-092] For I mine eyes will rivet to his face;

[003-093] And after we will both our judgements join

[003-094] In censure of his seeming.

[003-095] HORATIO.

[003-096] Well, my lord.

[003-097] If he steal aught the whilst this play is playing,

[003-098] And ’scape detecting, I will pay the theft.

[003-099] HAMLET.

[003-100] They are coming to the play. I must be idle.

[003-101] Get you a place.

[003-102] Danish march. A flourish. Enter King, Queen, Polonius,

[003-103] Ophelia, Rosencrantz, Guildenstern and others.

[003-105] How fares our cousin Hamlet?

[003-106] HAMLET.

[003-107] Excellent, i’ faith; of the chameleon’s dish: I eat the air, promise-crammed:

[003-108] you cannot feed capons so.

[003-109] KING.

[003-110] I have nothing with this answer, Hamlet; these words are not mine.

[003-111] HAMLET.

[003-112] No, nor mine now. [To Polonius.] My lord, you play’d once i’

[003-113] th’university, you say?

[003-114] POLONIUS.

[003-115] That did I, my lord, and was accounted a good actor.

[003-116] HAMLET.

[003-117] What did you enact?

[003-120] HAMLET.

[003-121] It was a brute part of him to kill so capital a calf there. Be the players

[003-124] Ay, my lord; they stay upon your patience.

[003-129] POLONIUS.

[003-130] [To the King.] O ho! do you mark that?

[003-131] HAMLET.

[003-132] Lady, shall I lie in your lap?

[003-133] [Lying down at Ophelia’s feet.]

[003-134] OPHELIA.

[003-135] No, my lord.

[003-136] HAMLET.

[003-137] I mean, my head upon your lap?

[003-138] OPHELIA.

[003-139] Ay, my lord.

[003-140] HAMLET.

[003-141] Do you think I meant country matters?

[003-142] OPHELIA.

[003-143] I think nothing, my lord.

[003-144] HAMLET.

[003-145] That’s a fair thought to lie between maids’ legs.

[003-146] OPHELIA.

[003-147] What is, my lord?

[003-148] HAMLET.

[003-149] Nothing.

[003-150] OPHELIA.

[003-151] You are merry, my lord.

[003-152] HAMLET.

[003-153] Who, I?

[003-154] OPHELIA.

[003-155] Ay, my lord.

[003-156] HAMLET.

[003-157] O God, your only jig-maker! What should a man do but be merry? For look you how

[003-158] cheerfully my mother looks, and my father died within’s two hours.

[003-159] OPHELIA.

[003-160] Nay, ’tis twice two months, my lord.

[003-161] HAMLET.

[003-162] So long? Nay then, let the devil wear black, for I’ll have a suit of sables. O

[003-163] heavens! die two months ago, and not forgotten yet? Then there’s hope a great

[003-164] man’s memory may outlive his life half a year. But by’r lady, he must build

[003-165] churches then; or else shall he suffer not thinking on, with the hobby-horse,

[003-166] whose epitaph is ‘For, O, for O, the hobby-horse is forgot!’

[003-167] Trumpets sound. The dumb show enters.

[003-168] Enter a King and a Queen very lovingly; the Queen embracing him and he her.

[003-169] She kneels, and makes show of protestation unto him. He takes her up, and

[003-170] declines his head upon her neck. Lays him down upon a bank of flowers. She,

[003-171] seeing him asleep, leaves him. Anon comes in a fellow, takes off his crown,

[003-172] kisses it, pours poison in the King’s ears, and exits. The Queen returns, finds

[003-173] the King dead, and makes passionate action. The Poisoner with some three or

[003-174] four Mutes, comes in again, seeming to lament with her. The dead body is

[003-175] carried away. The Poisoner woos the Queen with gifts. She seems loth and

[003-176] unwilling awhile, but in the end accepts his love.

[003-177] [Exeunt.]

[003-178] OPHELIA.

[003-179] What means this, my lord?

[003-180] HAMLET.

[003-181] Marry, this is miching mallecho; it means mischief.

[003-182] OPHELIA.

[003-183] Belike this show imports the argument of the play.

[003-184] Enter Prologue.

[003-185] HAMLET.

[003-186] We shall know by this fellow: the players cannot keep counsel; they’ll tell

[003-187] all.

[003-188] OPHELIA.

[003-189] Will they tell us what this show meant?

[003-190] HAMLET.

[003-191] Ay, or any show that you’ll show him. Be not you ashamed to show, he’ll not

[003-192] shame to tell you what it means.

[003-193] OPHELIA.

[003-194] You are naught, you are naught: I’ll mark the play.

[003-195] PROLOGUE.

[003-196] For us, and for our tragedy,

[003-197] Here stooping to your clemency,

[003-198] We beg your hearing patiently.

[003-199] HAMLET.

[003-200] Is this a prologue, or the posy of a ring?

[003-201] OPHELIA.

[003-202] ’Tis brief, my lord.

[003-203] HAMLET.

[003-204] As woman’s love.

[003-205] Enter a King and a Queen.

[003-206] PLAYER KING.

[003-207] Full thirty times hath Phoebus’ cart gone round

[003-208] Neptune’s salt wash and Tellus’ orbed ground,

[003-209] And thirty dozen moons with borrow’d sheen

[003-210] About the world have times twelve thirties been,

[003-211] Since love our hearts, and Hymen did our hands

[003-212] Unite commutual in most sacred bands.

[003-213] PLAYER QUEEN.

[003-214] So many journeys may the sun and moon

[003-215] Make us again count o’er ere love be done.

[003-216] But, woe is me, you are so sick of late,

[003-217] So far from cheer and from your former state,

[003-218] That I distrust you. Yet, though I distrust,

[003-219] Discomfort you, my lord, it nothing must:

[003-220] For women’s fear and love holds quantity,

[003-221] In neither aught, or in extremity.

[003-222] Now what my love is, proof hath made you know,

[003-223] And as my love is siz’d, my fear is so.

[003-224] Where love is great, the littlest doubts are fear;

[003-225] Where little fears grow great, great love grows there.

[003-226] PLAYER KING.

[003-227] Faith, I must leave thee, love, and shortly too:

[003-228] My operant powers their functions leave to do:

[003-229] And thou shalt live in this fair world behind,

[003-230] Honour’d, belov’d, and haply one as kind

[003-231] For husband shalt thou—

[003-232] PLAYER QUEEN.

[003-233] O confound the rest.

[003-234] Such love must needs be treason in my breast.

[003-235] In second husband let me be accurst!

[003-236] None wed the second but who kill’d the first.

[003-237] HAMLET.

[003-238] [Aside.] Wormwood, wormwood.

[003-239] PLAYER QUEEN.

[003-240] The instances that second marriage move

[003-241] Are base respects of thrift, but none of love.

[003-242] A second time I kill my husband dead,

[003-243] When second husband kisses me in bed.

[003-244] PLAYER KING.

[003-245] I do believe you think what now you speak;

[003-246] But what we do determine, oft we break.

[003-247] Purpose is but the slave to memory,

[003-248] Of violent birth, but poor validity:

[003-249] Which now, like fruit unripe, sticks on the tree,

[003-250] But fall unshaken when they mellow be.

[003-251] Most necessary ’tis that we forget

[003-252] To pay ourselves what to ourselves is debt.

[003-253] What to ourselves in passion we propose,

[003-254] The passion ending, doth the purpose lose.

[003-255] The violence of either grief or joy

[003-256] Their own enactures with themselves destroy.

[003-257] Where joy most revels, grief doth most lament;

[003-258] Grief joys, joy grieves, on slender accident.

[003-259] This world is not for aye; nor ’tis not strange

[003-260] That even our loves should with our fortunes change,

[003-261] For ’tis a question left us yet to prove,

[003-262] Whether love lead fortune, or else fortune love.

[003-264] The poor advanc’d makes friends of enemies;

[003-265] And hitherto doth love on fortune tend:

[003-266] For who not needs shall never lack a friend,

[003-267] And who in want a hollow friend doth try,

[003-268] Directly seasons him his enemy.

[003-269] But orderly to end where I begun,

[003-270] Our wills and fates do so contrary run

[003-271] That our devices still are overthrown.

[003-272] Our thoughts are ours, their ends none of our own.

[003-273] So think thou wilt no second husband wed,

[003-274] But die thy thoughts when thy first lord is dead.

[003-277] Sport and repose lock from me day and night,

[003-278] To desperation turn my trust and hope,

[003-286] PLAYER KING.

[003-483] My lord, the Queen would speak with you, and presently.

[003-484] HAMLET.

[003-485] Do you see yonder cloud that’s almost in shape of a camel?

[003-486] POLONIUS.

[003-487] By the mass, and ’tis like a camel indeed.

[003-488] HAMLET.

[003-489] Methinks it is like a weasel.

[003-490] POLONIUS.

[003-491] It is backed like a weasel.

[003-492] HAMLET.

[003-493] Or like a whale.

[003-494] POLONIUS.

[003-495] Very like a whale.

[003-496] HAMLET.

[003-497] Then will I come to my mother by and by.—They fool me to the top of my bent.—I

[003-498] will come by and by.

[003-499] POLONIUS.

[003-500] I will say so.

[003-501] [Exit.]

[003-502] HAMLET.

[003-503] By and by is easily said. Leave me, friends.

[003-504] [Exeunt all but Hamlet.]

[003-505] ’Tis now the very witching time of night,

[003-506] When churchyards yawn, and hell itself breathes out

[003-507] Contagion to this world. Now could I drink hot blood,

[003-508] And do such bitter business as the day

[003-509] Would quake to look on. Soft now, to my mother.

[003-510] O heart, lose not thy nature; let not ever

[003-511] The soul of Nero enter this firm bosom:

[003-512] Let me be cruel, not unnatural.

[003-513] I will speak daggers to her, but use none;

[003-514] My tongue and soul in this be hypocrites.

[003-515] How in my words somever she be shent,

[003-516] To give them seals never, my soul, consent.

[003-517] [Exit.]
