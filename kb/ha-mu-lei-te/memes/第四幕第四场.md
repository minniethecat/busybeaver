# 第四幕 第四场

[004-001] Denmark.

[004-002] Enter Fortinbras and Forces marching.

[004-003] FORTINBRAS.

[004-004] Go, Captain, from me greet the Danish king.

[004-005] Tell him that by his license, Fortinbras

[004-006] Craves the conveyance of a promis’d march

[004-007] Over his kingdom. You know the rendezvous.

[004-008] If that his Majesty would aught with us,

[004-009] We shall express our duty in his eye;

[004-010] And let him know so.

[004-011] CAPTAIN.

[004-012] I will do’t, my lord.

[004-013] FORTINBRAS.

[004-014] Go softly on.

[004-015] [Exeunt all but the Captain.]

[004-016] Enter Hamlet, Rosencrantz, Guildenstern &c.

[004-017] HAMLET.

[004-018] Good sir, whose powers are these?

[004-019] CAPTAIN.

[004-020] They are of Norway, sir.

[004-021] HAMLET.

[004-022] How purpos’d, sir, I pray you?

[004-023] CAPTAIN.

[004-024] Against some part of Poland.

[004-025] HAMLET.

[004-026] Who commands them, sir?

[004-027] CAPTAIN.

[004-028] The nephew to old Norway, Fortinbras.

[004-029] HAMLET.

[004-030] Goes it against the main of Poland, sir,

[004-031] Or for some frontier?

[004-032] CAPTAIN.

[004-033] Truly to speak, and with no addition,

[004-034] We go to gain a little patch of ground

[004-035] That hath in it no profit but the name.

[004-036] To pay five ducats, five, I would not farm it;

[004-037] Nor will it yield to Norway or the Pole

[004-038] A ranker rate, should it be sold in fee.

[004-039] HAMLET.

[004-040] Why, then the Polack never will defend it.

[004-041] CAPTAIN.

[004-042] Yes, it is already garrison’d.

[004-043] HAMLET.

[004-044] Two thousand souls and twenty thousand ducats

[004-045] Will not debate the question of this straw!

[004-046] This is th’imposthume of much wealth and peace,

[004-047] That inward breaks, and shows no cause without

[004-048] Why the man dies. I humbly thank you, sir.

[004-049] CAPTAIN.

[004-050] God b’ wi’ you, sir.

[004-051] [Exit.]

[004-052] ROSENCRANTZ.

[004-053] Will’t please you go, my lord?

[004-054] HAMLET.

[004-055] I’ll be with you straight. Go a little before.

[004-056] [Exeunt all but Hamlet.]

[004-057] How all occasions do inform against me,

[004-058] And spur my dull revenge. What is a man

[004-059] If his chief good and market of his time

[004-060] Be but to sleep and feed? A beast, no more.

[004-061] Sure he that made us with such large discourse,

[004-062] Looking before and after, gave us not

[004-063] That capability and godlike reason

[004-064] To fust in us unus’d. Now whether it be

[004-065] Bestial oblivion, or some craven scruple

[004-066] Of thinking too precisely on th’event,—

[004-067] A thought which, quarter’d, hath but one part wisdom

[004-068] And ever three parts coward,—I do not know

[004-069] Why yet I live to say this thing’s to do,

[004-070] Sith I have cause, and will, and strength, and means

[004-071] To do’t. Examples gross as earth exhort me,

[004-072] Witness this army of such mass and charge,

[004-073] Led by a delicate and tender prince,

[004-074] Whose spirit, with divine ambition puff’d,

[004-075] Makes mouths at the invisible event,

[004-076] Exposing what is mortal and unsure

[004-077] To all that fortune, death, and danger dare,

[004-078] Even for an eggshell. Rightly to be great

[004-079] Is not to stir without great argument,

[004-080] But greatly to find quarrel in a straw

[004-081] When honour’s at the stake. How stand I then,

[004-082] That have a father kill’d, a mother stain’d,

[004-083] Excitements of my reason and my blood,

[004-084] And let all sleep, while to my shame I see

[004-085] The imminent death of twenty thousand men

[004-086] That, for a fantasy and trick of fame,

[004-087] Go to their graves like beds, fight for a plot

[004-088] Whereon the numbers cannot try the cause,

[004-089] Which is not tomb enough and continent

[004-090] To hide the slain? O, from this time forth,

[004-091] My thoughts be bloody or be nothing worth.

[004-092] [Exit.]
