# 第四幕 第五场

[004-001] the Castle.

[004-002] Enter Queen, Horatio and a Gentleman.

[004-003] QUEEN.

[004-004] I will not speak with her.

[004-005] GENTLEMAN.

[004-006] She is importunate, indeed distract.

[004-007] Her mood will needs be pitied.

[004-008] QUEEN.

[004-009] What would she have?

[004-010] GENTLEMAN.

[004-011] She speaks much of her father; says she hears

[004-012] There’s tricks i’ th’ world, and hems, and beats her heart,

[004-013] Spurns enviously at straws, speaks things in doubt,

[004-014] That carry but half sense. Her speech is nothing,

[004-015] Yet the unshaped use of it doth move

[004-016] The hearers to collection; they aim at it,

[004-017] And botch the words up fit to their own thoughts,

[004-018] Which, as her winks, and nods, and gestures yield them,

[004-019] Indeed would make one think there might be thought,

[004-020] Though nothing sure, yet much unhappily.

[004-021] ’Twere good she were spoken with, for she may strew

[004-022] Dangerous conjectures in ill-breeding minds.

[004-023] QUEEN.

[004-024] Let her come in.

[004-025] [Exit Gentleman.]

[004-026] To my sick soul, as sin’s true nature is,

[004-027] Each toy seems prologue to some great amiss.

[004-028] So full of artless jealousy is guilt,

[004-029] It spills itself in fearing to be spilt.

[004-030] Enter Ophelia.

[004-031] OPHELIA.

[004-032] Where is the beauteous Majesty of Denmark?

[004-033] QUEEN.

[004-034] How now, Ophelia?

[004-035] OPHELIA.

[004-036] [Sings.]

[004-037] How should I your true love know

[004-038] From another one?

[004-039] By his cockle hat and staff

[004-040] And his sandal shoon.

[004-041] QUEEN.

[004-042] Alas, sweet lady, what imports this song?

[004-043] OPHELIA.

[004-044] Say you? Nay, pray you mark.

[004-045] [Sings.]

[004-046] He is dead and gone, lady,

[004-047] He is dead and gone,

[004-048] At his head a grass green turf,

[004-049] At his heels a stone.

[004-050] QUEEN.

[004-051] Nay, but Ophelia—

[004-052] OPHELIA.

[004-053] Pray you mark.

[004-054] [Sings.]

[004-055] White his shroud as the mountain snow.

[004-056] Enter King.

[004-057] QUEEN.

[004-058] Alas, look here, my lord!

[004-059] OPHELIA.

[004-060] [Sings.]

[004-061] Larded all with sweet flowers;

[004-062] Which bewept to the grave did not go

[004-063] With true-love showers.

[004-064] KING.

[004-065] How do you, pretty lady?

[004-066] OPHELIA.

[004-067] Well, God dild you! They say the owl was a baker’s daughter. Lord, we know what

[004-068] we are, but know not what we may be. God be at your table!

[004-069] KING.

[004-070] Conceit upon her father.

[004-071] OPHELIA.

[004-072] Pray you, let’s have no words of this; but when they ask you what it means, say

[004-073] you this:

[004-074] [Sings.]

[004-075] Tomorrow is Saint Valentine’s day,

[004-076] All in the morning betime,

[004-077] And I a maid at your window,

[004-078] To be your Valentine.

[004-079] Then up he rose and donn’d his clothes,

[004-080] And dupp’d the chamber door,

[004-081] Let in the maid, that out a maid

[004-082] Never departed more.

[004-083] KING.

[004-084] Pretty Ophelia!

[004-085] OPHELIA.

[004-086] Indeed la, without an oath, I’ll make an end on’t.

[004-087] [Sings.]

[004-088] By Gis and by Saint Charity,

[004-089] Alack, and fie for shame!

[004-090] Young men will do’t if they come to’t;

[004-091] By Cock, they are to blame.

[004-092] Quoth she, before you tumbled me,

[004-093] You promis’d me to wed.

[004-094] So would I ha’ done, by yonder sun,

[004-095] An thou hadst not come to my bed.

[004-096] KING.

[004-097] How long hath she been thus?

[004-098] OPHELIA.

[004-099] I hope all will be well. We must be patient. But I cannot choose but weep, to

[004-100] think they would lay him i’ th’ cold ground. My brother shall know of it. And

[004-101] so I thank you for your good counsel. Come, my coach! Good night, ladies; good

[004-102] night, sweet ladies; good night, good night.

[004-103] [Exit.]

[004-104] KING.

[004-105] Follow her close; give her good watch, I pray you.

[004-106] [Exit Horatio.]

[004-107] O, this is the poison of deep grief; it springs

[004-108] All from her father’s death. O Gertrude, Gertrude,

[004-109] When sorrows come, they come not single spies,

[004-110] But in battalions. First, her father slain;

[004-111] Next, your son gone; and he most violent author

[004-112] Of his own just remove; the people muddied,

[004-113] Thick, and unwholesome in their thoughts and whispers

[004-114] For good Polonius’ death; and we have done but greenly

[004-115] In hugger-mugger to inter him. Poor Ophelia

[004-116] Divided from herself and her fair judgement,

[004-117] Without the which we are pictures or mere beasts.

[004-118] Last, and as much containing as all these,

[004-119] Her brother is in secret come from France,

[004-120] Feeds on his wonder, keeps himself in clouds,

[004-121] And wants not buzzers to infect his ear

[004-122] With pestilent speeches of his father’s death,

[004-123] Wherein necessity, of matter beggar’d,

[004-124] Will nothing stick our person to arraign

[004-125] In ear and ear. O my dear Gertrude, this,

[004-126] Like to a murdering piece, in many places

[004-127] Gives me superfluous death.

[004-128] [A noise within.]

[004-129] QUEEN.

[004-130] Alack, what noise is this?

[004-131] KING.

[004-132] Where are my Switzers? Let them guard the door.

[004-133] Enter a Gentleman.

[004-134] What is the matter?

[004-135] GENTLEMAN.

[004-136] Save yourself, my lord.

[004-137] The ocean, overpeering of his list,

[004-138] Eats not the flats with more impetuous haste

[004-139] Than young Laertes, in a riotous head,

[004-140] O’erbears your offices. The rabble call him lord,

[004-141] And, as the world were now but to begin,

[004-142] Antiquity forgot, custom not known,

[004-143] The ratifiers and props of every word,

[004-144] They cry ‘Choose we! Laertes shall be king!’

[004-145] Caps, hands, and tongues applaud it to the clouds,

[004-146] ‘Laertes shall be king, Laertes king.’

[004-147] QUEEN.

[004-148] How cheerfully on the false trail they cry.

[004-149] O, this is counter, you false Danish dogs.

[004-150] [A noise within.]

[004-151] KING.

[004-152] The doors are broke.

[004-153] Enter Laertes, armed; Danes following.

[004-154] LAERTES.

[004-155] Where is this king?—Sirs, stand you all without.

[004-156] Danes.

[004-157] No, let’s come in.

[004-158] LAERTES.

[004-159] I pray you, give me leave.

[004-160] DANES.

[004-161] We will, we will.

[004-162] [They retire without the door.]

[004-163] LAERTES.

[004-164] I thank you. Keep the door. O thou vile king,

[004-165] Give me my father.

[004-166] QUEEN.

[004-167] Calmly, good Laertes.

[004-168] LAERTES.

[004-169] That drop of blood that’s calm proclaims me bastard;

[004-170] Cries cuckold to my father, brands the harlot

[004-171] Even here between the chaste unsmirched brow

[004-172] Of my true mother.

[004-173] KING.

[004-174] What is the cause, Laertes,

[004-175] That thy rebellion looks so giant-like?—

[004-176] Let him go, Gertrude. Do not fear our person.

[004-177] There’s such divinity doth hedge a king,

[004-178] That treason can but peep to what it would,

[004-179] Acts little of his will.—Tell me, Laertes,

[004-180] Why thou art thus incens’d.—Let him go, Gertrude:—

[004-181] Speak, man.

[004-182] LAERTES.

[004-183] Where is my father?

[004-184] KING.

[004-185] Dead.

[004-186] QUEEN.

[004-187] But not by him.

[004-188] KING.

[004-189] Let him demand his fill.

[004-190] LAERTES.

[004-191] How came he dead? I’ll not be juggled with.

[004-192] To hell, allegiance! Vows, to the blackest devil!

[004-193] Conscience and grace, to the profoundest pit!

[004-194] I dare damnation. To this point I stand,

[004-195] That both the worlds, I give to negligence,

[004-196] Let come what comes; only I’ll be reveng’d

[004-197] Most throughly for my father.

[004-198] KING.

[004-199] Who shall stay you?

[004-200] LAERTES.

[004-201] My will, not all the world.

[004-202] And for my means, I’ll husband them so well,

[004-203] They shall go far with little.

[004-204] KING.

[004-205] Good Laertes,

[004-206] If you desire to know the certainty

[004-207] Of your dear father’s death, is’t writ in your revenge

[004-208] That, sweepstake, you will draw both friend and foe,

[004-209] Winner and loser?

[004-210] LAERTES.

[004-211] None but his enemies.

[004-212] KING.

[004-213] Will you know them then?

[004-214] LAERTES.

[004-215] To his good friends thus wide I’ll ope my arms;

[004-216] And, like the kind life-rendering pelican,

[004-217] Repast them with my blood.

[004-218] KING.

[004-219] Why, now you speak

[004-220] Like a good child and a true gentleman.

[004-221] That I am guiltless of your father’s death,

[004-222] And am most sensibly in grief for it,

[004-223] It shall as level to your judgement ’pear

[004-224] As day does to your eye.

[004-225] DANES.

[004-226] [Within.] Let her come in.

[004-227] LAERTES.

[004-228] How now! What noise is that?

[004-229] Re-enter Ophelia, fantastically dressed with

[004-230] straws and flowers.

[004-231] O heat, dry up my brains. Tears seven times salt,

[004-232] Burn out the sense and virtue of mine eye.

[004-233] By heaven, thy madness shall be paid by weight,

[004-234] Till our scale turn the beam. O rose of May!

[004-235] Dear maid, kind sister, sweet Ophelia!

[004-236] O heavens, is’t possible a young maid’s wits

[004-237] Should be as mortal as an old man’s life?

[004-238] Nature is fine in love, and where ’tis fine,

[004-239] It sends some precious instance of itself

[004-240] After the thing it loves.

[004-241] OPHELIA.

[004-242] [Sings.]

[004-243] They bore him barefac’d on the bier,

[004-244] Hey non nonny, nonny, hey nonny

[004-245] And on his grave rain’d many a tear.—

[004-246] Fare you well, my dove!

[004-247] LAERTES.

[004-248] Hadst thou thy wits, and didst persuade revenge,

[004-249] It could not move thus.

[004-250] OPHELIA.

[004-251] You must sing ‘Down a-down, and you call him a-down-a.’ O, how the wheel

[004-252] becomes it! It is the false steward that stole his master’s daughter.

[004-253] LAERTES.

[004-254] This nothing’s more than matter.

[004-255] OPHELIA.

[004-256] There’s rosemary, that’s for remembrance; pray love, remember. And there is

[004-257] pansies, that’s for thoughts.

[004-258] LAERTES.

[004-259] A document in madness, thoughts and remembrance fitted.

[004-260] OPHELIA.

[004-261] There’s fennel for you, and columbines. There’s rue for you; and here’s some

[004-262] for me. We may call it herb of grace o’ Sundays. O you must wear your rue with

[004-263] a difference. There’s a daisy. I would give you some violets, but they wither’d

[004-264] all when my father died. They say he made a good end.

[004-265] [Sings.]

[004-266] For bonny sweet Robin is all my joy.

[004-267] LAERTES.

[004-268] Thought and affliction, passion, hell itself

[004-269] She turns to favour and to prettiness.

[004-270] OPHELIA.

[004-271] [Sings.]

[004-272] And will he not come again?

[004-273] And will he not come again?

[004-274] No, no, he is dead,

[004-275] Go to thy death-bed,

[004-276] He never will come again.

[004-277] His beard was as white as snow,

[004-278] All flaxen was his poll.

[004-279] He is gone, he is gone,

[004-280] And we cast away moan.

[004-281] God ha’ mercy on his soul.

[004-282] And of all Christian souls, I pray God. God b’ wi’ ye.

[004-283] [Exit.]

[004-284] LAERTES.

[004-285] Do you see this, O God?

[004-286] KING.

[004-287] Laertes, I must commune with your grief,

[004-288] Or you deny me right. Go but apart,

[004-289] Make choice of whom your wisest friends you will,

[004-290] And they shall hear and judge ’twixt you and me.

[004-291] If by direct or by collateral hand

[004-292] They find us touch’d, we will our kingdom give,

[004-293] Our crown, our life, and all that we call ours

[004-294] To you in satisfaction; but if not,

[004-295] Be you content to lend your patience to us,

[004-296] And we shall jointly labour with your soul

[004-297] To give it due content.

[004-298] LAERTES.

[004-299] Let this be so;

[004-300] His means of death, his obscure burial,—

[004-301] No trophy, sword, nor hatchment o’er his bones,

[004-302] No noble rite, nor formal ostentation,—

[004-303] Cry to be heard, as ’twere from heaven to earth,

[004-304] That I must call’t in question.

[004-305] KING.

[004-306] So you shall.

[004-307] And where th’offence is let the great axe fall.

[004-308] I pray you go with me.

[004-309] [Exeunt.]
