## Overview

Fanny Dashwood is the wife of [[john-dashwood]] and the sister of [[edward-ferrars|Edward]] and [[robert-ferrars]]. She is the chief architect of the Dashwood sisters' disinheritance, using her influence over her husband to persuade him that any support for his half-sisters would impoverish their own son. Her cold economic logic in the novel's second chapter is one of Austen's most devastating portraits of selfishness masked as prudence (002-003).
