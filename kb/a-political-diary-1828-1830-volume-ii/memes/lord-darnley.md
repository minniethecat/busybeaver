# Lord Darnley

## Overview

Lord Darnley (John Bligh, 4th Earl of Darnley) was an Irish peer who sat in the House of Lords. In Ellenborough's diary, he appears as a figure willing to take positions on political questions, participating in debates and offering opinions on matters before the house.

## Role in the Diary

Darnley features as an active parliamentary peer. Ellenborough records him alongside other speakers during debates, mentioning him together with Lord Winchilsea and O'Connell: "and _Lord Darnley!_ speaking for it. Both these" (004-006) suggesting his alignment on this issue was noteworthy. Darnley "repeated the same thing to me…" (004-008), indicating a personal conversation with Ellenborough. On institutional matters, Darnley was among the peers who thought certain "statements were hazardous. Lords Darnley and Bute declared" (024-193) their reservations.

## Key Appearances

- **Debate participation**: Darnley spoke on Irish and parliamentary reform questions alongside O'Connell and Lord Winchilsea (004-006).
- **Admiralty role**: At one point, Darnley was mentioned in connection with the office of "first Lord of the Admiral…" (030-104).
- **Political discussions**: Darnley joined other peers — including Lord Londonderry — in debating government positions (031-094).
