# Lord Morpeth

## Overview

George Howard, Viscount Morpeth (1802–1864), later 7th Earl of Carlisle, was a Whig MP who represented Yorkshire during the Wellington administration. He appears in Ellenborough's diary as a rising opposition figure preparing a reform motion, and later served as Chief Secretary for Ireland under Melbourne.

## Role in the Diary

Morpeth's appearances in the diary, though brief, capture him at a significant moment. Ellenborough records that "Lord Morpeth is to make a motion for the r—" (030-075), almost certainly a reference to parliamentary reform, which became the defining issue of the 1830–1832 period. He is also listed alongside Duncombe and Bethel among the Whig MPs active in opposition (038-131).

## Key Appearances

- **(030-075)** Preparing a motion on reform in the House of Commons
- **(038-131)** Listed among active Whig MPs in opposition
