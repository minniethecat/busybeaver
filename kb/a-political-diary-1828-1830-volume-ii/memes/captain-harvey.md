# Captain Harvey

## Overview

Captain Harvey of the 4th Dragoons was a British cavalry officer who served as an interpreter and intermediary in Anglo-Persian relations. In Ellenborough's diary, he conveys a sensitive diplomatic message from the Shah of Persia to King George IV.

## Role in the Diary

Captain Harvey appears in the diary on October 28, in an entry concerning diplomatic protocol with Persia. Ellenborough records: "Captain Harvey of the 4th Dragoons called by the King's desire to say the King of Persia told him when he was at Teheran that he was hurt at not receiving a letter from the King" (050-030, 050-031, 050-032).

The omission was apparently inadvertent. Ellenborough explained to Captain Harvey that "the King had announced his accession to the Shah of Persia as he had to other sovereigns" (050-033, 050-034), suggesting that the failure to send a personal letter was a bureaucratic oversight rather than a deliberate slight.

Captain Harvey's role extended beyond mere interpretation: "Captain Harvey was interpreter to his regiment" (050-034). The diary adds that "it seemed to me that he rather wished to command the Persian troops" (050-034, 050-035), hinting at the captain's ambitions in the Persian military sphere.

## Key Appearances

- **Royal messenger**: Called by the King's desire to relay the Shah of Persia's displeasure at not receiving a formal letter (050-030).
- **Interpreter**: Served as interpreter to his regiment, facilitating communication in Persian affairs (050-034).
- **Military ambition**: Appeared interested in commanding Persian troops (050-034).
