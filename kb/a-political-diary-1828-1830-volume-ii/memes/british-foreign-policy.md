# British Foreign Policy

## Overview

British foreign policy during the Wellington ministry (1828–1830) was conducted by Lord Aberdeen at the Foreign Office. The approach was generally cautious and conservative, aimed at maintaining the European settlement established after the Napoleonic Wars and protecting British commercial and strategic interests abroad.

## European Relations

Relations with France, Spain, and Portugal occupied significant diplomatic attention. The Eastern Question, concerning the fate of Greece and the Ottoman Empire, was another major foreign policy concern of the period. (003-015) (003-016)

## Colonial and Commercial Interests

British trade and colonial interests shaped foreign policy decisions across the globe, from the Mediterranean to the Americas. The diary records numerous discussions of foreign affairs and their intersection with domestic politics. (009-155) (016-108)
