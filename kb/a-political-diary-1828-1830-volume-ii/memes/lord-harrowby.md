**Dudley Ryder, 1st Earl of Harrowby** (1762–1847) was a British Tory politician and peer. He was active in the [[House of Lords]] during the late 1820s, where he spoke on constitutional questions including the Regency Bill, as recorded in the diary of [[Lord Ellenborough]].

== Parliamentary Activity ==

Lord Harrowby delivered a speech in the House of Lords that Ellenborough described as "long and sensible; but heavily delivered." (001-029) This assessment captures the diarist's mixed view of Harrowby as a thoughtful but uninspiring parliamentary speaker. Harrowby was also noted among the social circles of the political elite, alongside figures such as [[Lord Eldon]]. (001-044)

== Regency Bill ==

During the political crisis surrounding the health of [[George IV]], Lord Harrowby favoured a short Regency Bill to be passed, a position that aligned with a pragmatic approach to the constitutional question. (036-030) Ellenborough noted that Harrowby "took the same line" as another peer on the matter, both in voting and in argument. (036-160)
