# Lord Ravensworth

## Overview

Lord Ravensworth (Thomas Henry Liddell, 1st Baron Ravensworth) was a Tory peer raised to the peerage in 1821. In Ellenborough's diary, he appears as someone with access to the King's private opinions on the government and its policies, making him a useful barometer of royal sentiment.

## Role in the Diary

Ravensworth serves in the diary as a conduit for the King's political views. On one occasion, "I met Lord Ravensworth and talked to him upon the su…" (003-156), and the conversation turned to the monarch's attitudes. Ravensworth appeared to Ellenborough to have insight into royal thinking, as he "seemed to think his Royal mas…" (003-161) held certain views on current political questions. Later in the year, returning from Roehampton, Ellenborough again encountered Ravensworth, "who told me the King" (037-101) — confirming his role as a regular bearer of royal intelligence.

## Key Appearances

- **Royal sentiment**: Ravensworth conveyed the King's views on government matters, serving as an informal channel between court and cabinet circles (003-156, 003-161).
- **Social encounters**: Ellenborough met Ravensworth on the road from Roehampton and received political updates from him (037-101).
