# Lord Ellenborough

## Overview

Edward Law, 1st Earl of Ellenborough, served as Lord Privy Seal in the [[Duke of Wellington]]'s administration from 1828 to 1830. He is the author of the diary that constitutes the corpus of this wiki.

The diary provides a daily record of Cabinet meetings, parliamentary debates, and political manoeuvring during a turbulent period of British history, capturing his own role in government while also documenting the personalities around him.

## Role in the Diary

Ellenborough was an active participant in the Wellington Cabinet, not merely its recorder. The diary shows him drafting diplomatic instructions, attending the King at Windsor, and taking positions on key questions of policy.

He opposed "the eventual amalgamation" of certain offices (046-105), intervened in debates to correct misrepresentations from the [[duke-of-richmond|Duke of Richmond]], and maintained close working relationships with figures such as [[Lord Aberdeen]] and [[robert-peel|Robert Peel]].

His family connections appear in the diary as well. His brother, the Honourable H. S. Law, is mentioned in a footnote (020-076), and a reference to a Stewart relation "also sister-in-law of Lord Ellenborough" records a family tie to the political world (029-154).

A contemporary footnote observes: "It may be remembered that Lord Ellenborough strongly" held certain views — though the truncated reference suggests a position well known to readers of the day (015-017).

As Lord Privy Seal, Ellenborough held a senior Cabinet position. The diary reveals him at the centre of government decision-making: reading despatches, drafting responses, and forming judgments on men and measures throughout the administration.

## Key Appearances

- **Cabinet deliberations**: Regular participant in Wellington's Cabinet discussions across 1828–1830.

- **Policy positions**: Opposed the amalgamation of certain government offices (046-105).

- **Family in government**: Brother H. S. Law and sister-in-law connected through the Stewart family (020-076, 029-154).

- **Diary author**: The entire corpus represents his private political record of the Wellington ministry.
