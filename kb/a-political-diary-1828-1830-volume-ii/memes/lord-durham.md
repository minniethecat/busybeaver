# Lord Durham

## Overview

Lord Durham (John George Lambton, 1st Earl of Durham) was a prominent radical [[whig-party|Whig]] politician known as "Radical Jack" for his advocacy of parliamentary reform. A close ally of [[Lord Grey]], Durham was a persistent thorn in the side of the [[Duke of Wellington]]'s Tory administration during the period covered by the diary.

## Role in the Diary

The diary captures Durham as an active and provocative opposition figure. Ellenborough recorded Durham telling him directly that "the King will" pursue a particular course (001-122), suggesting Durham's proximity to court circles. Durham appears frequently in House of Lords debates, where he "opened the fire" on government positions (031-093) and on occasion "spoke temperately and well" against the administration (039-056).

The diary reveals the Wellington government's watchfulness towards Durham. The author records going "to the House for fear Lord Durham should play us a trick" (039-136), indicating a sense of constant vigilance against his parliamentary manoeuvres. Durham's association with the Coal Committee alongside [[Lord Londonderry]] is noted, though both were described as "most wrong-headed upon it" (028-118).

## Key Appearances

- **Reform advocacy**: Durham and Lord Radnor "evidently intended" coordinated action on reform questions (037-144), and Durham moved the printing of related parliamentary papers (037-147).
- **Petition work**: In the House, Durham presented a petition "against" government policy (028-006).
- **Relations with Grey**: The diary notes that Lord Grey "would not stipulate for Lord Durham" in ministerial arrangements, describing the latter as politically challenging (043-009).
- **Removal from office**: Ellenborough recorded condolences "on Lord Durham's being removed" from some position (054-121), marking a political setback.
