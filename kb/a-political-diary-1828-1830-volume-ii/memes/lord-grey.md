# Lord Grey

## Overview

Charles Grey, 2nd Earl Grey, was the leader of the Whig opposition during the period covered by Ellenborough's diary (1828–1830). He became Prime Minister in November 1830 following Wellington's fall, and his government passed the Great Reform Act 1832. The diary traces the government's wary attitude toward Grey and speculation about his political role.

## Opposition Leader

Lord Grey was viewed with deep suspicion by the Wellington government. As Ellenborough recorded, "Lord Grey is very hostile." (012-045) The Duke considered that he "could not offer the Privy Seal to Lord Grey" despite earlier communications. (002-086) There had been previous approaches to Grey: "in reality he was in communication with Lord Grey in 1820-21, after the Queen's trial." (002-090)

## Path to Power

By 1830, there were reports of "some approximation between Lord Grey" and elements of the political establishment. (015-120) Rumours circulated about "a notion of bringing in Lord Grey," though Ellenborough suspected this report. (015-123) When Wellington fell in November 1830, Grey formed a Whig government committed to parliamentary reform.
