# Duke of Cumberland

## Overview

Ernest Augustus, Duke of Cumberland and Teviotdale, was the fifth son of King George III and a prominent ultra-Tory figure in the [[house-of-lords|House of Lords]]. A fierce opponent of Catholic emancipation, Cumberland wielded significant influence at court and became a persistent obstacle to the [[Duke of Wellington]]'s reformist policies. He later succeeded to the throne of Hanover in 1837.

## Role in the Diary

The diary opens with Cumberland in a central role: Ellenborough records that at Windsor, "the Duke of Cumberland spoke very warmly indeed" on a political question (P01-179). When the King's intentions became known, Cumberland's reaction was sharp — on learning of the fixed date, he was visibly angered (P01-198).

Cumberland's influence at court runs throughout the early chapters. The diary records efforts to deliver a petition to Cumberland so that he might present it (001-008), and concerns about informing him of ministerial developments (001-103).

His ultra-Tory stance is particularly clear in a passage where the King threatens to make Cumberland a Minister if Wellington cannot secure Catholic emancipation (046-087), and Cumberland is described as "very violent" on the subject (046-088). This places Cumberland at the heart of the ultra-Tory faction during the Wellington ministry.

The relationship between Cumberland and the government was tense. Cumberland "said he must withdraw his support" at a critical juncture (001-051), and the King himself observed "all would have been quiet without Cumberland" (015-016), placing the Duke at the centre of the ultra-Tory challenge to Wellington.

These appearances span the full period of the diary from the Prologue through the summer of 1828, and they reveal a figure who was both a political actor in his own right and a symbol of the resistance that Wellington faced from within the royal family and the Tory right.

Cumberland's broader conduct drew criticism: [[Robert Peel]] reported that Cumberland "had been abusing every one at" an important gathering (003-163), and at the King's levee, Cumberland stood "at the King's left hand" alongside the Ultra-Tories (003-035).

## Key Appearances

- **Windsor intervention (Prologue)**: Spoke "very warmly" at Windsor, setting early political dynamics in motion (P01-179, P01-198).

- **Court influence**: Served as a channel for petitions and communications to the King (001-008, 002-096).

- **Ultra-Tory opposition**: Aligned with hardline opponents of the Wellington government's policies (001-037, 003-035).

- **Crisis point**: Declared withdrawal of support from the government, triggering a political crisis (001-051, 001-166).
