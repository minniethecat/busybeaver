**Sir Edward Knatchbull** (1781–1849) was a British Tory politician who represented Kent in the [[House of Commons]]. He was a prominent ultra-Tory and led a faction of MPs opposed to Catholic emancipation and parliamentary reform.

== Parliamentary Leadership ==

The diary records Sir E. Knatchbull leading a parliamentary faction of about twenty-three MPs. (023-018) This group, described dismissively in the diary, represented the ultra-Tory wing that resisted concessions on Catholic relief and reform. The diarist, [[Edward Law, 1st Earl of Ellenborough]], noted that Knatchbull's leadership was significant enough to warrant tracking.

== Opposition to Wellington ==

Knatchbull was among those who opposed the Wellington ministry from the right. Along with [[Lord Winchelsea]], Sir Edward Knatchbull was a persistent presence at political gatherings. (048-012) He chaired meetings (049-093) and was part of a network of ultra-Tory figures that included Lord Falmouth and Sir R. Vyvyan. (054-129)

== Election and Local Politics ==

The diary captures a vivid scene of Knatchbull "in a cocked hat, attended by..." his supporters during an election context. (041-154) The "Knatchbull squad" received no cheers, (041-159) illustrating the contested nature of local political support. Knatchbull also expressed doubts about arming the militia. (050-021)

Charles Burrell and Sir E. Knatchbull were noted as opposing the diarist on a matter (025-038), showing the political divisions even among the landed gentry of the time.
