**James Maitland, 8th Earl of Lauderdale** (1759–1839) was a Scottish Whig peer and political economist. He was active in the [[House of Lords]] during the late 1820s, serving as a proxy for fellow Whig peers and participating in parliamentary discussions on trade policy.

== Political Activity ==

The diary records Lord Lauderdale's presence in the House of Lords. A conversation in the House with Lord Lauderdale touched on China trade. (006-087) This reflects his engagement with commercial and imperial policy, consistent with his reputation as a writer on political economy.

== Whig Proxy Network ==

Lauderdale was part of the Whig proxy network that maintained opposition voting strength in the upper house. His proxy was held by the [[Duke of Bedford]], one of the leading Whig magnates. (026-088) The diary notes "Whig proxies Bedford, Lauderdale, Wilton," confirming his place within this coordinated political arrangement. (026-119) This proxy system was consequential for Whig strategy during the Wellington ministry. (026-090)
