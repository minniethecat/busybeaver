# General Muffling

## Overview

General Muffling (Friedrich Karl Ferdinand von Müffling) was a Prussian general and military diplomat who served as an intermediary in the Russo-Turkish War of 1828–1829. In Ellenborough's diary, he appears at Constantinople relaying Russian peace terms and facilitating communication between the Turkish and Russian sides.

## Role in the Diary

Ellenborough first notes Muffling's arrival at Constantinople, reporting that "General Muffling, the Prussian, is arrived at Constantinople. He reports the moderate views of the Emperor Nicholas, and states them" (010-147). Muffling thus served as a channel for Russian intentions to the British, at a time when Ellenborough was closely monitoring the Eastern Question.

Muffling played an active diplomatic role during the peace negotiations. Robert Gordon, the British ambassador, "managed very prudently to get General Muffling to send his secretary to the Russian head-quarters with the Turkish Plenipotentiaries" (011-177). Though Muffling "would have gone with them to the Reis Effendi had he been well enough" (011-178–179), his secretary went in his stead and was "thus enabled to state distinctly what had passed in the conference held with the Effendi" (011-181–182).

Ellenborough assessed that Muffling, "going rather beyond his instructions, has been made to do good" (012-018), suggesting that the Prussian general's independent initiative helped advance the peace process. Ellenborough saw these developments as tending "to the preservation of peace" (012-019).

## Key Appearances

- **Arrival at Constantinople**: Muffling arrived in the Ottoman capital and reported Emperor Nicholas's moderate peace terms (010-147).
- **Diplomatic facilitation**: At Gordon's request, Muffling sent his secretary to accompany the Turkish plenipotentiaries to Russian headquarters (011-177–179).
- **Beyond instructions**: Ellenborough noted that Muffling had exceeded his formal brief, but to beneficial effect (012-018).
