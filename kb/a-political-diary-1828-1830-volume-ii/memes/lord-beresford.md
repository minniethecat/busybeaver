**William Carr Beresford, 1st Viscount Beresford** (1768–1854) was a British Army officer and politician. A distinguished general of the Napoleonic Wars, he served as Master-General of the Ordnance during the [[Duke of Wellington|Wellington]] ministry of 1828–1830.

== Military Career ==

Beresford earned his reputation in the Peninsular War, where he served under Wellington and commanded the Portuguese Army as its marshal. His military standing was such that he was a natural choice for senior office when Wellington formed his government.

== Master-General of the Ordnance ==

In the Wellington cabinet, Beresford held the post of Master-General of the Ordnance. The diary records him alongside other ministerial figures: "Aberdeen, Goulburn, Herries, Murray, Beresford, Lord F." (046-047) He was responsible for the artillery and engineering branches of the army.

[[Lord Ellenborough]] noted Beresford's grasp of military logistics: "Lord Beresford told me there were 3,000 artillerymen" (050-162) under his command.

== Political Role ==

Beresford was not only a military man but also a political actor. The diary records parliamentary manoeuvres involving "Lord Durham and Lord Beresford. We succeeded at last in settling the" matter. (005-125) However, he was not always the most effective speaker: "Lord Beresford, having no good word at his disposal, said he did not second" (005-127) a motion successfully.

There were also discussions of sending him on a diplomatic mission: [[Lord Ellenborough]] noted speculation that "Lord Beresford going to Portugal as Minister" (024-096) was under consideration. Ellenborough later wrote: "I had said I believed Lord Beresford would go to Portugal as Minister, if" circumstances permitted. (035-142)

== Connection to Wellington ==

As a Peninsular War veteran and long-time associate of Wellington, Beresford was among the Duke's most loyal supporters in cabinet. His appointment to the Ordnance reflected Wellington's preference for military men in key positions within his administration.
