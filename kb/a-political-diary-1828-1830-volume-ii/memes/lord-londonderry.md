# Lord Londonderry

## Overview

Lord Londonderry (Charles William Vane, 3rd Marquess of Londonderry), born Charles Stewart, was a military officer and Tory peer. He was the half-brother of the celebrated Foreign Secretary Viscount Castlereagh. By the time of Ellenborough's diary (1828–1830), Londonderry had acquired a reputation for erratic behaviour, particularly in the [[house-of-lords|House of Lords]].

## Role in the Diary

Ellenborough's observations of Londonderry are consistently unflattering. At a Committee on London Bridge, Londonderry was "a little better than before" (005-074) on one occasion, but the improvement was temporary: at the next committee he was "more insane than ever" (006-016). His behaviour in the House drew frequent criticism — he "made a foolish speech" (006-020) and later "made a very foolish speech about" coal duties (030-146).

Despite his eccentricities, Londonderry maintained political connections. He passed notes to Ellenborough during debates — "Lord Londonderry threw a note over to me" (003-128) — and his voting loyalty to the government was questionable: it was feared he "would have voted against us" on at least one occasion (004-180). The diary notes he had been "much disgusted" with the Cumberland faction (006-128), and without Hardinge he would have been unreliable on appointments (006-131).

His Windsor connections were noteworthy — he "went to Windsor yesterday" for an audience (029-148) — and his family connections included his brother-in-law Wood (029-153).

## Key Appearances

- **London Bridge Committee (Chapter 5–6)**: Frequent committee attendee, noted for erratic contributions.
- **House of Lords speeches**: Delivered multiple "foolish" speeches that drew the diarist's criticism.
- **Coal Committee (Chapter 28–30)**: Active in debates alongside [[Lord Durham]].
