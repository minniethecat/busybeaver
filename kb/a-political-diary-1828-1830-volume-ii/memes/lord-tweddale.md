# Lord Tweddale

## Overview

Lord Tweddale (George Hay, 8th Marquess of Tweeddale) was a Scottish peer and military officer who served in the Peninsular War and later as Governor of Madras. In Ellenborough's diary, he is mentioned only in passing as a potential appointee to an Irish office, considered by the Prime Minister.

## Role in the Diary

Ellenborough records a moment of political speculation during a conversation about appointments. Reflecting on the difficulty of selecting a suitable candidate for India, he notes: "I should not be much surprised if he thought of Lord Tweddale, whom he thought of for Ireland. I do not know him at all" (017-096–097). The "he" in question was the Duke of Wellington, who as Prime Minister was considering Tweddale for an Irish post — though the arrangement never materialised.

Ellenborough's admission that he did not know Tweddale personally, combined with the brief mention, suggests the peer was a peripheral figure in the diarist's political circle.

## Key Appearances

- **Irish appointment speculation**: Wellington considered Lord Tweddale for a post in Ireland; Ellenborough noted the possibility without knowing the man (017-096–097).
