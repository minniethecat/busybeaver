# July Revolution 1830

## Overview

The July Revolution of 1830 (27–29 July) overthrew the Bourbon monarchy of Charles X and brought Louis-Philippe, Duke of Orleans, to the French throne. This event occurred during the final months of Wellington's ministry and had profound effects on British foreign policy and domestic politics, influencing the momentum for parliamentary reform.

## France Under the Bourbons

The government of Charles X under the ministry of Prince de Polignac had been moving in an increasingly conservative direction. Ellenborough noted concerns about the state of the French Ministry well before the crisis: "I do not like Lord Stuart's account of the state of the French Ministry." (017-040) The new French Ministry under Polignac was viewed with suspicion. (011-119)

## The Revolution and Its Impact

The revolution established a constitutional monarchy under Louis-Philippe. The Wellington government had to navigate the changed diplomatic landscape carefully. The events in France emboldened reform movements across Europe, including in Britain, where the call for parliamentary reform intensified.
