# Duke of Leinster

## Overview

The Duke of Leinster (Augustus Frederick FitzGerald, 3rd Duke of Leinster) was the premier peer of Ireland and one of the largest landowners in that country. In Ellenborough's diary, he appears as a Whig-aligned magnate whose political stance was a matter of concern to the Tory government.

## Role in the Diary

Leinster features in the diary as a figure whose political alignment mattered to the administration. Ellenborough records with concern that "O'Connell is alarmed. The Duke of Leinster is ready to sign a" (049-066) protest or declaration, suggesting the Duke's willingness to lend his considerable prestige to opposition causes. This was significant: when the premier Irish peer aligned with O'Connell's agitation, it represented a challenge to the government's authority in Ireland.

Leinster also appears in lists of peers attending political functions. He was named among those present at gatherings alongside Lord Winchilsea and Lord Camden (051-014), and on another occasion, someone "was desired by the Duke of Leinster," (051-019) to carry a message or perform an errand, indicating his status as a person whose requests commanded attention.

## Key Appearances

- **Political alignment**: The Duke was ready to sign a protest document that alarmed O'Connell himself, signaling his engagement with Irish political agitation (049-066).
- **Court and political gatherings**: Leinster attended functions alongside senior Tory peers such as Lord Camden and Lord Winchilsea (051-014).
- **Influence**: His requests carried weight, with others acting on his desires (051-019).
