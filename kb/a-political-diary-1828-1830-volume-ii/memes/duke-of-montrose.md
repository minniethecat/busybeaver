**James Graham, 3rd Duke of Montrose** (1755–1836) was a Scottish Tory peer and politician. He held office in the [[Duke of Wellington]]'s administration during the late 1820s before resigning his post, as recorded in the political diary of [[Lord Ellenborough]].

== Political Career ==

The diary records the Duke of Montrose attending a Cabinet dinner at the Lord Chancellor's residence, confirming his place within the inner circle of the Wellington government. (021-175) This places him among the senior office-holders who gathered regularly to discuss government business.

== Resignation ==

The Duke of Montrose resigned from his office during the period covered by the diary. (037-033) The King subsequently placed the office at the disposal of another candidate, a transition that was noted in Ellenborough's political observations. (037-103) The resignation formed part of the broader reshuffling of offices that characterised the later stages of the Wellington ministry.
