# Duke of Gloucester

## Overview

Prince William Frederick, Duke of Gloucester and Edinburgh (1776–1834), was a member of the British royal family and a great-grandson of King George II. He married Princess Mary, a daughter of George III, and served as Chancellor of the University of Cambridge. In the [[house-of-lords|House of Lords]], he generally supported the [[tory-party|Tory]] administration.

## Role in the Diary

The Duke of Gloucester appears in Ellenborough's diary through references to both himself and the Duchess of Gloucester. He is listed among the Dukes of the realm in the diary's enumeration of the peerage: "Dukes: Cumberland, Gloucester, Brandon, Richmond, Newcastle" (P01-162), placing him among the senior ranks of the nobility.

The Duke and Duchess appear together in a notable political anecdote: when "talking to the Duchess of Gloucester, he said, 'If they want a Pri'" (020-130), followed by the revealing remark that "they might have had the Duke of Gloucester" (020-131), suggesting that the Duke was considered as a potential candidate for some office or role.

The Duchess appears independently in several entries. Ellenborough notes that "the Duchess of Gloucester did not before, and will not" (008-159) in what appears to be a political context. Later, the Duchess "arrived an hour later" at a gathering marked by melancholy (032-053).

## Key Appearances

- **Peerage listing**: Named among the senior Dukes of the realm (P01-162).
- **Political consideration**: The Duke was mentioned as a potential candidate for office (020-131).
- **Royal family presence**: Both the Duke and Duchess appear at court and political gatherings throughout the diary.
