**Sir Thomas Munro, 1st Baronet** (1761–1827) was a Scottish soldier and administrator of the [[East India Company]], who served as Governor of Madras. His writings on Indian governance were studied by political figures in Britain, as recorded in the diary of [[Lord Ellenborough]].

== Memorandum on India ==

Ellenborough read Sir Thomas Munro's memorandum on Indian affairs, which contained observations on the governance and administration of British territories in India. (018-039) He noted the memorandum's content with reference to another reader's reaction. (018-043)

== Posthumous Reputation ==

The diarist spent an entire day reading the "Life of Sir Thomas Munro," describing it as containing "a great deal" of valuable material and concluding it was "a most valuable book." (021-070) (021-078) This sustained reading, recorded across two diary entries, reflects the high regard in which Munro's career and writings were held by British political elites concerned with imperial governance.
