# Colonel Willoughby Cotton

## Overview

Colonel Willoughby Cotton was a British Army officer who served in India during the late 1820s. He had commanded _en second_ during the Ava campaign and subsequently served as Adjutant-General of the King's troops in Bengal.

## Role in the Diary

Ellenborough met Colonel Willoughby Cotton on September 4, 1829, recording: "Saw Colonel Willoughby Cotton, who commanded _en second_ in Ava" (011-059). Cotton had lately visited all the stations of the army in Bengal as Adjutant-General and reported that "no army can be in finer order" under Lord Combermere (011-059).

Cotton also provided intelligence on Colonel Skinner. He reported that Skinner was about 55 years old and his son was a merchant who "goes every year into Cashmere for shawls" (011-079). Skinner still commanded about 1,300 men, quartered not far from Delhi.

## Key Appearances

- **Ava campaign veteran**: Commanded _en second_ during the First Anglo-Burmese War (011-059).
- **Army inspection**: As Adjutant-General, visited all Bengal army stations and reported the army in excellent order (011-059).
- **Military intelligence**: Provided details about Colonel Skinner's age, family, command, and location near Delhi (011-079).
