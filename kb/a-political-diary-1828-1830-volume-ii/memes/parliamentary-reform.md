# Parliamentary Reform

## Overview

The question of Parliamentary Reform was a growing political force in Britain during the late 1820s. Reform sentiment had been building since the end of the Napoleonic Wars, and by the Wellington ministry, it was an unavoidable element of political discourse.

## Reform in Parliament

Reform proposals were discussed in various contexts within both Houses of Parliament. The diary records reform-related discussions, including those concerning the corporation and test acts, and broader questions of political representation. (007-120) (007-121)

## Political Context

Reform agitation was linked to other major issues of the day. The interconnected nature of Reform, Catholic Emancipation, and economic distress made the political landscape particularly complex. (043-048) (051-040)
