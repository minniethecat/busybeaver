# Colonel Salmond

## Overview

Colonel Salmond was a British military officer who appears in Ellenborough's diary during the spring of 1829. He is mentioned in the context of official procedures and administrative business, suggesting a role connected to military administration or ordnance.

## Role in the Diary

Ellenborough records an interaction involving Colonel Salmond concerning procedural regularity. The diarist states that "I have expressed a wish to see Colonel Salmond" (008-084) to expedite business. A conversation took place with "Colonel Salmond and Mr. Wilson on Monday, that there was no irregularity in that course" (008-087). Ellenborough further noted that he "immediately determined to desist from it" after the conversation (008-088).

The exchange appears to relate to concerns about proper procedure. Ellenborough defended himself, recording that he "believed I had so expressed myself at the time to Colonel Salmond" (008-089), indicating that the colonel was an official with whom matters of protocol were discussed.

## Key Appearances

- **Administrative consultation**: Ellenborough wished to see Colonel Salmond to expedite official business (008-084).
- **Procedural discussion**: A conversation on Monday with Colonel Salmond and Mr. Wilson addressed the regularity of a particular course of action (008-087).
- **Self-exculpation**: Ellenborough assured others that he had made his position clear to Colonel Salmond at the relevant time (008-089).
