# Mrs. Hastings

## Overview

Mrs. Hastings was the widow of a figure connected to the [[East India Company]], whose pension case appears in Ellenborough's diary. The diarist, as President of the Board of Control, took an active role in advancing her claim through the official channels of government finance.

## Role in the Diary

Mrs. Hastings's case occupies several diary entries, illustrating the personal and administrative dimensions of Ellenborough's work at the India Board. Her husband — referred to as "old Hastings" (017-169) — had served the East India Company, and after his death his widow sought a pension from the government.

Ellenborough records the procedural steps: "Hastings to the King, applying for a pension. The King recommends it to the Treasury" (017-146). As President of the Board of Control, Ellenborough undertook to "bring the question of Mrs. Hastings's pension before the Chairs" (017-168) — the Chairmen of the East India Company. He prepared a memorandum "showing all that had been done for old Hastings" (017-169) to support her case. The Chairs indicated they "will consider favourably Mrs. Hastings's case; but she must provide further documentation" (018-085). Ellenborough later records "the result of my communication to the Chairs respecting Mrs. Hastings" (018-120), confirming his personal intervention.

## Key Appearances

- **(017-146)** The King recommended Mrs. Hastings's pension application to the Treasury
- **(017-168)** Ellenborough raised the case with the Chairs of the East India Company
- **(017-169)** A memorandum was prepared documenting the service of "old Hastings"
- **(018-085)** The Chairs indicated they would consider the case favourably
- **(018-120)** Ellenborough communicated the result to the Chairs
