**Dom Pedro I** (1798–1834) was Emperor of Brazil from 1822 to 1831 and briefly King of Portugal as Pedro IV in 1826. The diary covers him during the Portuguese succession crisis, when his daughter [[Donna Maria]]'s claim to the Portuguese throne was contested by his brother [[Don Miguel]].

== The Portuguese Succession ==

Pedro had abdicated the Portuguese throne in favour of his young daughter Donna Maria, but his brother Miguel exploited the power vacuum. The great powers sought Pedro's direct involvement. The diary records that "Austria, and England" were to "ask Don Pedro distinctly what he" intended to do about the Portuguese situation. (016-107) This tripartite approach reflected the Concert of Europe's concern with Iberian stability.

Pedro's position was constrained. At one point he was described as the "Emperor Don Pedro, father of" the young queen, a formulation that emphasised his familial rather than political role. (017-044) The diary noted with some scepticism: "Emperor Pedro. The intention seems to be to" acknowledge Miguel under certain "conditions, when Pedro admits he can do nothing." (018-017)

== Recognition of Miguel ==

The central diplomatic question was whether Pedro would accept his brother's usurpation. The British government believed there was "little doubt that the Emperor Pedro means to" come to terms with Miguel. (024-086) Pedro had established a regency "in whose name Don Pedro has" continued to govern Brazil while pursuing his daughter's claim. (026-061)

The diary records the delicate diplomacy: the British could not do "anything with Don Pedro, and that we must acknowledge" Miguel as the practical ruler. (042-099) Pedro himself proposed "conditions of what Don Pedro considers a conciliatory arrangement." (042-161) Yet his leverage was limited, "since these terms were" offered, the situation had shifted. (042-166)
