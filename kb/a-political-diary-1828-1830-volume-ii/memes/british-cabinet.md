# British Cabinet

## Overview

The British Cabinet under the Duke of Wellington was the executive decision-making body of the United Kingdom from 1828 to 1830. Ellenborough served in this cabinet and his diary provides an insider's account of its meetings, deliberations, and internal dynamics. The Cabinet met regularly, often at Apsley House, to coordinate government policy on Catholic Emancipation, foreign affairs, and domestic legislation.

## In the Diary

Ellenborough's diary entries frequently reference Cabinet meetings: "Cabinet at 2." (001-006) These meetings were the forum for deciding government strategy, including the handling of petitions to the King and the management of parliamentary business. The Cabinet's composition included Wellington, Peel, Lord Bathurst, and others who appear throughout the diary.

## Decision-Making

The Cabinet made collective decisions on sensitive matters. When the petition crisis arose, Wellington "writes to-night to tell the King he is going, and to repeat his advice of this morning as coming from the Cabinet." (001-014) The gravity of the situation was clear: "If the King will not take Peel's advice we go out." (001-016)
