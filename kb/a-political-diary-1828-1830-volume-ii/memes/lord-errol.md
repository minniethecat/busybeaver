# Lord Errol

## Overview

William Hay, 18th Earl of Erroll (1801–1846), was a Scottish peer and court official. In Ellenborough's diary he appears in his capacity as Master of the Horse to Queen Adelaide, placing him in proximity to the royal household during the final years of George IV's reign and the accession of William IV.

## Role in the Diary

Errol is recorded in connection with Privy Council proceedings. Ellenborough notes him alongside Sir Charles Pole before the diarist "went into the Privy Council" (034-077), suggesting Errol's attendance at official functions. His appointment as Master of the Horse to the Queen (037-040) is noted as a matter of court record, reflecting the diary's attention to official appointments and court arrangements.

## Key Appearances

- **(034-077)** Attended Privy Council proceedings alongside Sir Charles Pole
- **(037-040)** Recorded as Master of the Horse to Queen Adelaide
