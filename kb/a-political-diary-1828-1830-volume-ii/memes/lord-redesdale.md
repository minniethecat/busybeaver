# Lord Redesdale

## Overview

John Freeman-Mitford, 1st Baron Redesdale, was a British barrister and politician. He served as Speaker of the House of Commons (1801–1802) and Lord Chancellor of Ireland (1802–1806). In Ellenborough's diary, he appears in the House of Lords as a conservative voice opposing piecemeal legal reform.

## Role in the Diary

Ellenborough records a debate on the Lord Chancellor's Bill, which proposed creating a new Chancery judge. Lord Redesdale joined the opposition on procedural grounds: "Opposition from Lord Eldon, Lord Redesdale, and Lord Holland, all saying they wished to see the whole plan before they agree to a part" (004-174–175).

This placed Redesdale in unusual company alongside ultra-Tory Lord Eldon and Whig Lord Holland, united in demanding comprehensive rather than fragmentary reform.

Lord Tenterden, by contrast, "approved of the making of the new judge, but wished his functions had been better defined" (004-176–177). The opposition did not press for a division.

## Key Appearances

- **Chancery reform debate**: Redesdale joined Eldon and Holland in opposing the Chancellor's Bill, arguing that the full reform plan should be presented before any part was enacted (004-174–175).
