# Sir Sidney Beckwith

## Overview

Sir Sidney Beckwith was a British military officer who received an important Indian appointment during the period covered by Ellenborough's diary. He appears as a figure of sufficient standing to be included among senior military commanders at official functions.

## Role in the Diary

Ellenborough records Sir Sidney Beckwith's appointment in April 1829: "Informed them of Sir Sidney Beckwith's appointment to the command at Bombay" (001-085). This was a significant colonial military posting, placing Beckwith in charge of British forces in the Bombay Presidency.

Beckwith also appears at a large military gathering later in the diary. He is listed among distinguished company: "Lord Dalhousie, and Sir Sidney Beckwith, and their aides-de-camp; a great many Directors, and in all rather more than 100 people" (006-168). The occasion, attended by senior figures including Sir Herbert Taylor, Sir Byam Martin, and Sir A. Dickson, underscores Beckwith's position within the military establishment.

## Key Appearances

- **Indian command**: Appointed to the command at Bombay, a senior colonial military post (001-085).
- **Official gathering**: Attended a large military function alongside Lord Dalhousie and other senior officers, with over 100 people present (006-168).
