# Lord Stanhope

## Overview

Lord Stanhope (Philip Henry Stanhope, 4th Earl) was a British peer and politician who appears in Ellenborough's diary as a persistent parliamentary operator, introducing motions and demanding returns from the government. Though a Tory by affiliation, his motions often created work for ministers and required careful preparation to answer.

## Role in the Diary

Lord Stanhope appears in Ellenborough's diary almost exclusively as a parliamentary antagonist — a peer whose motions required the government to prepare materials, gather information, and respond in debate. The diary records Ellenborough repeatedly preparing for Stanhope's motions, first on a committee proposal (024-177) and later on shipping returns (030-003, 030-034).

Stanhope's persistence is evident from the diary's entries spanning multiple days on the same subjects. The diary records a "long conversation on Lord Stanhope's motion for to-morrow" (024-177) and notes that the House sat from 4 until 2 on his motion for a Committee (024-184), yet only fifteen members were present for the vote (025-001). His motion on shipping required Ellenborough to prepare materials (030-003), gather "more information," and then finally answer him (030-035).

## Key Appearances

- **Motion for a Committee**: Stanhope moved for a Committee of the House on a significant subject, with Ellenborough recording it as "Lord Stanhope's motion" and noting the lengthy sitting it occasioned (024-184). Despite the extended debate, the attendance was thin: "but fifteen present for Lord Stanhope's" motion (025-001).
- **Shipping returns motion**: Stanhope moved for returns on shipping, requiring Ellenborough to prepare materials (030-003) and seek additional information (030-034). The diary records Ellenborough finally answering the motion after preparation (030-035).
- **Ecclesiastical affairs**: The diary notes Stanhope in connection with church livings — "Stanhope in commendam" — and records discussion of an "exchange of Stanhope for a living near Exeter" (053-078, 053-128), suggesting involvement in church patronage matters.
