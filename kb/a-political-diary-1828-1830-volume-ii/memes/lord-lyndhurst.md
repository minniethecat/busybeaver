**John Singleton Copley, 1st Baron Lyndhurst** (1772–1863) was a British lawyer and Tory politician. He served three terms as Lord Chancellor and was a prominent figure in the legal and political establishment of the 1820s and 1830s.

== Social and Political Circle ==

The diary records the diarist meeting "the Lievens, Lyndhursts, Sir J. Murray, and others" at a social gathering. (001-060) This places the Lyndhursts within the elite social-political network of early 1828. A later entry simply notes "Lyndhurst to-day" (004-084), suggesting regular contact between the diarist and the Lord Chancellor.

== Personal Connections ==

Lady Lyndhurst also appears in the diary. The diarist rode "a white New Forest pony of Lady Lyndhurst's" (035-080), and she was noted as having "carelessly carried off" something in her carriage. (044-171) These domestic details illustrate the close social ties between the Lyndhurst household and the diarist, [[Edward Law, 1st Earl of Ellenborough]].
