# Colonel Tod

## Overview

Colonel James Tod (1782–1835) was a British East India Company officer and the author of "Annals and Antiquities of Rajasthan" (published as "Rajastan" in the diary). Ellenborough read his work and expressed keen interest in the region he described.

## Role in the Diary

Ellenborough records reading Colonel Tod's work during the summer of 1829. He noted: "Read some of Colonel Tod's 'Rajastan.' I had rather see Rajastan or Rajpootana than any part of India. It would really be interesting" (008-171). This reveals Ellenborough's intellectual curiosity about the Indian subcontinent, where he would later serve as Governor-General.

The diary also references a detail from Tod's writings about Indian princely dynasties: "The father was still living when Colonel Tod wrote. The House of Oudeypore was the only native reigning family who disdained to intermarry" (008-177) with other princely houses, noting the unique prestige of the Mewar dynasty.

## Key Appearances

- **Literary interest**: Ellenborough read portions of Tod's "Rajastan" and expressed a desire to visit the region (008-171).
- **Historical record**: The diary preserves a reference to Tod's observations about the House of Oudeypore and its marriage customs (008-177).
