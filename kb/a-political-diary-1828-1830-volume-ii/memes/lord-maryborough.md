# Lord Maryborough

## Overview

Lord Maryborough (William Wellesley-Pole, 3rd Earl of Mornington) was the elder brother of the [[Duke of Wellington]] and a significant political figure in his own right. As Baron Maryborough, he served in Wellington's administration and appears in Ellenborough's diary as a concerned adviser watching the government's position unfold.

## Role in the Diary

Maryborough appears in the diary as a figure anxious about the direction of government. Ellenborough records a meeting where Maryborough came to him with visible concern: "yesterday to Lord Maryborough, and with a face of surprise,…" (029-078), suggesting he had received unsettling political news. His closeness to the Duke of Wellington gave his worries particular weight.

In later passages, Maryborough acted as an intermediary, approaching Sir Henry Hardinge to express his views. "Lord Maryborough had been to Hardinge to expre…" (051-152) his opinion on a political matter. The diary also records that Maryborough was urged "not to tell the Duke his appr…" (051-156), indicating that his brotherly access to Wellington was something others sought to manage carefully.

## Key Appearances

- **Anxious counsel**: Maryborough approached Ellenborough with evident concern about political developments (029-078).
- **Intermediary role**: He conveyed his views to Hardinge, acting as a channel between concerned peers and the government (051-152).
- **Fraternal discretion**: Others cautioned Maryborough about what he shared with his brother the Duke, highlighting the sensitivity of his position (051-156).
