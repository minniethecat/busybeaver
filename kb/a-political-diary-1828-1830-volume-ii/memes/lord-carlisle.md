# Lord Carlisle

## Overview

George Howard, 6th Earl of Carlisle (1773–1848), was a Whig politician active during the late Georgian and early Victorian periods. He appears in Ellenborough's diary during the crisis months of 1830, when Whig criticism of the Wellington administration intensified. Ellenborough records Carlisle's shifting rhetoric as a barometer of opposition sentiment.

## Role in the Diary

Lord Carlisle appears in the diary as a voice of the Whig opposition. Ellenborough notes him presenting a petition (026-147), but the most revealing entries come from the tense political climate of late 1830. Carlisle's public posture toward the Duke of Wellington shifted notably — he "began to abuse the Duke" (053-045), then shortly afterward "was obliged to begin to praise" (053-047), suggesting the rapid oscillation of opposition tactics as the ministry's position weakened. Ellenborough treats Carlisle's rhetorical turns as evidence that "the tide is turning" (053-045) in the political landscape.

- **(053-045)** Attacked the Duke of Wellington as political tides shifted
- **(053-047)** Compelled to moderate his criticism shortly afterward
