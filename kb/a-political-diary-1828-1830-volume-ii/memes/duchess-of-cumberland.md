# Duchess of Cumberland

## Overview

Frederica of Mecklenburg-Strelitz (1778–1841), the Duchess of Cumberland, was the wife of [[Ernest Augustus, Duke of Cumberland]] — the fifth son of [[George III]] and later King of Hanover. In Ellenborough's diary she appears as a figure of political consequence, exercising influence through her husband and directly corresponding with the King on matters of state.

## Role in the Diary

The Duchess of Cumberland features in the diary chiefly in connection with continental diplomacy and court politics. Ellenborough records a diplomatic application "made through the Duke of Cumberland to the King" regarding Prussia, with the King returning an answer through the same channel (020-037). The Duchess herself was directly engaged: she "complains the answer of Aberdeen was very different from that given through the Duke of Cumberland by the King" (020-043), demonstrating her active involvement in diplomatic communications.

The diary also notes her growing political influence. Ellenborough records that the "Duchess of Cumberland gaining influence. Her note to the Duke the other day, to tell him the Duke of Cumberland had been four hours with the King" (020-141) — suggesting she was monitoring and influencing the Duke's access to the sovereign. Earlier references indicate political wariness: "Old Eldon wants a guarantee that no more Whigs will be admitted" in connection with the Duchess (008-160).

## Key Appearances

- **(008-160)** Linked to political guarantees sought by Lord Eldon regarding Whig appointments
- **(020-037)** Diplomatic application involving Prussia made through the Duke and Duchess of Cumberland
- **(020-043)** Complained that Lord Aberdeen's answer differed from the King's response
- **(020-141)** Noted as "gaining influence" — her note informed the Duke about the King's audiences
