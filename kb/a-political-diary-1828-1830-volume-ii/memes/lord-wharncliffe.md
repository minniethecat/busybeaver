# Lord Wharncliffe

## Overview

Lord Wharncliffe (James Archibald Stuart-Wortley-Mackenzie, 1st Baron) was a British peer and politician who appears in Ellenborough's diary as a figure the government engaged with on foreign and domestic policy matters. His independent stance sometimes placed him in opposition to ministers, but Ellenborough considered him someone who could be reasoned with.

## Role in the Diary

Lord Wharncliffe appears in the diary as a peer whose views mattered to the government. The diary records Ellenborough speaking to him directly on political subjects (036-156), noting that Wharncliffe was "well with us" at one point regarding the Terceira affair, in contrast to [[Lord Lansdowne]] who was "wide" on the same question (024-129). This suggests Wharncliffe was seen as persuadable — someone ministers could work with rather than a fixed opponent.

Ellenborough's personal interactions with Wharncliffe included direct conversations about parliamentary business. After Wharncliffe "spoke against us" on one vote, Ellenborough spoke with him about "the same thing," and received a reply that indicated willingness to engage (036-156). The diary also records Wharncliffe offering assessments of other political figures, such as his view of Duncombe (038-131).

## Key Appearances

- **Terceira affair**: Wharncliffe was noted to be "well with us" on the Terceira question, a diplomatic matter involving Portugal and the Azores (024-129). His alignment with the government on this foreign policy issue was contrasted favourably with other peers.
- **Parliamentary motions**: Wharncliffe intended "on Tuesday to propose" something in the House, and Ellenborough sent word to Loch to inform him of "Lord Wharncliffe's intention" (025-005, 025-008). When Wharncliffe was told he would examine Lord Amherst, it suggests a motion involving Indian or military affairs.
- **East Retford question**: Wharncliffe "moved a resolution with the view" on East Retford (039-070), a borough at the centre of parliamentary reform debates concerning the transfer of its seats.
- **Speaking against the government**: On one recorded occasion, Wharncliffe "spoke against us" in debate (036-048). Ellenborough followed up with him privately afterwards, recording Wharncliffe's response (036-156).
