# Sir Brook Taylor

## Overview

Sir Brook Taylor was a British diplomat who served as minister to the Court of Prussia in Berlin. In Ellenborough's diary, he appears as the intermediary through whom the Foreign Office conducted sensitive correspondence with the King of Prussia during the late 1820s.

## Role in the Diary

Ellenborough records Taylor's role in two episodes involving royal diplomacy. In the first, the Foreign Secretary Lord Aberdeen, acting on the Duke of Cumberland's request, "wrote through Sir Brook Taylor to the King of Prussia, and civilly put him off" (020-041). The letter concerned a démarche the King of Prussia had made via the Duke of Cumberland, and Ellenborough notes that the King "saw, and approved" the response (020-042–043).

The second reference concerns a letter to Taylor "respecting Duke Charles of Mecklenburgh" (020-086). The King later claimed he "never saw or sanctioned" this letter, but Ellenborough records that "It bears his initials and approval, which have been traced out in ink over his pencil" (020-087–088), casting doubt on the King's denial.

## Key Appearances

- **Prussian correspondence**: Aberdeen used Taylor as the channel to communicate with the King of Prussia regarding the Duke of Cumberland's intervention (020-041).
- **Mecklenburgh letter**: A disputed letter to Taylor concerning Duke Charles of Mecklenburgh became the subject of royal discrepancy, with the King's pencilled approval traced over in ink (020-086–088).
