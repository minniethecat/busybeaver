# Lord Chandos

## Overview

Lord Chandos (Richard Temple-Nugent-Brydges-Chandos-Grenville, Marquess of Chandos) was the son and heir of the 1st [[duke-of-buckingham|Duke of Buckingham and Chandos]]. During the period of Ellenborough's diary, he was actively considered for government office, with the [[Duke of Wellington]] repeatedly seeking to bring him into the Cabinet or ministry.

## Attempts to Enter Government

Wellington made persistent efforts to secure a government position for Lord Chandos. The Duchess of Cumberland told Ellenborough that "the Duke wanted to bring in Lord Chandos, by way of" strengthening the ministry. (P01-142) This was part of a broader strategy: the administration discussed moves "to bring in Lord Chandos and M. Fitzgerald" as potential recruits. (002-060)

The matter was debated at Cabinet level. When "Chandos was proposed, I should rather say suggested, but rejected" by some members of the government. (005-021) Wellington persisted, instructing that "we might get Courtenay to resign at once and get in Lord Chandos. I am to" arrange the details. (006-114) The Duke communicated the rationale through Loch: Ellenborough saw "Loch the Duke's note and told him why Lord Chandos's being brought in was" necessary for political balance. (006-118)

## Political Alignment

Lord Chandos hosted meetings of political allies at his residence. Bankes noted that "he had attended none of the meetings at Lord Chandos's," suggesting that Chandos served as a gathering point for certain Tory factions. (002-008) Hardinge, visiting "Stowe when Lord Chandos in" residence, reported on Chandos's political stance. (021-072) Chandos could be independently minded: he "said to Hardinge he would never" compromise on certain principles. (021-075)

When the Mint position became available, "Chandos did not take the Mint, he went to the Duke and offered to remain" in his current role. (022-076) This episode illustrated both Wellington's continued interest in placing him and Chandos's own preference regarding which office suited him.
