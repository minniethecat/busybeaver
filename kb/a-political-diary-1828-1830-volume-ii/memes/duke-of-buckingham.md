# Duke of Buckingham

## Overview

Richard Temple-Nugent-Brydges-Chandos-Grenville, 1st Duke of Buckingham and Chandos, was a prominent peer who served as Lord Steward of the Household. In Ellenborough's diary, he appears as a frequent parliamentary adversary of the [[Duke of Wellington]]'s administration, using his position in the [[house-of-lords|House of Lords]] to challenge government measures and hint at his own claims to office.

## Role in the Diary

Buckingham's appointment as Lord Steward drew the diarist's immediate disapproval: "The Duke of Buckingham is Lord Steward. A bad appointment" (038-017). This set the tone for a relationship marked by friction. Buckingham made a speech against the government, recorded tersely by Ellenborough (033-170), and seems to have been angling for further preferment: "It seems the Duke of Buckingham hints that he must have something" (038-112).

Despite their political differences, Buckingham remained a figure the government had to reckon with. He was counted among the supporters on certain divisions alongside Lords Bute and Wicklow (036-049), and after debates he would remain in the chamber: "Afterwards the Duke of Buckingham, when most peers had gone away" (036-003). At the levée, Buckingham reported to the diarist that "the King was much out" of sorts (054-125), and on another occasion told him "they had formed their" opinion in the House (053-107).

## Key Appearances

- **Speech against government**: Delivered a speech against the Wellington ministry in the House of Lords (033-170).
- **Lord Steward appointment**: Appointed to the Household post, which Ellenborough judged "a bad appointment" (038-017).
- **Seeking preferment**: Hinted at his desire for further office, causing Government concern (038-112).
- **Levée reports**: Served as intermediary between court and government (053-107, 054-125).
