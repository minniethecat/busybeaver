# Lord Wellesley

## Overview

Richard Wellesley, 1st Marquess Wellesley, was the elder brother of the [[Duke of Wellington]] and one of the most distinguished — and difficult — political figures of his generation. A former Governor-General of India and Foreign Secretary, Wellesley brought immense prestige but also considerable friction to his brother's administration, as recorded with visible frustration in Ellenborough's diary.

## Role in the Diary

Wellesley's relationship with the government was complicated. He held high office but proved unreliable in its routines. The diary records with exasperation that "Lord Wellesley never would go to Council" (025-135), a pattern of absence that must have grated on a ministry that depended on senior figures for collective decision-making. His extensive official correspondence occupied the Cabinet's attention: Ellenborough records reading "Lord Wellesley's letter" on the Batta question — an Indian military allowance dispute — and noting he had "read every line of it" and proceeded to write to him (016-017, 022-143).

By the later stages of the administration, Wellesley was being positioned as a potential successor. The diary records a report from Sir Henry Hardinge indicating "Lord Wellesley was to succeed me" (055-012), suggesting that Ellenborough himself might be replaced by Wellington's brother. An informant "tells me Lord Wellesley assures him there" is a prospect, though the broken entry leaves details uncertain (043-070).

The Cabinet's relationship with Wellesley was characterised by wariness. He was a figure of independent power and unpredictable loyalties — a [[whig-party|Whig]] by inclination serving in a [[tory-party|Tory]] ministry, with the added complexity of being the Prime Minister's brother.

## Key Appearances

- **Council absence**: Consistently failed to attend Council meetings, frustrating the Cabinet (025-135).
- **Indian correspondence**: His letter on the Batta question was read and responded to by Ellenborough (016-017, 022-143).
- **Succession speculation**: Rumoured as Ellenborough's potential successor in office (043-070, 055-012).
- **Diplomatic channel**: Served as a conduit of information, though his accounts were treated with caution (043-070).
