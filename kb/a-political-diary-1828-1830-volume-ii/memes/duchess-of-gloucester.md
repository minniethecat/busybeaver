# Duchess of Gloucester

## Overview

Princess Mary, Duchess of Gloucester and Edinburgh (1776–1857), was the fourth daughter of George III and Queen Charlotte. In Ellenborough's diary, she appears at Windsor during the final illness of George IV in June 1830, and is noted for her refusal to receive the Duchess of Cumberland at court.

## Role in the Diary

Ellenborough records a social rift at court involving the Duchess of Gloucester. During a discussion about the Duke of Cumberland, Hardinge noted that the Duke "determined not to leave England, but to send for the Duchess and his son" (008-157–158). The diary notes pointedly: "The Duchess of Gloucester did not before, and will not now, receive the Duchess of Cumberland" (008-159–160). This reflects the enduring tension within the royal family following the Cumberland scandals.

The Duchess of Gloucester also appears in conversation with the King. When discussing the prospect of a continental prince, the King remarked to her: "If they want a Prince of my family, they might have had the Duke of Gloucester," at which "the Duchess burst out a-laughing" (020-130–132).

During the King's final illness, Ellenborough noted her arrival at Windsor: "The bulletin to-day is very alarming... The Duchess of Gloucester arrived an hour later. I thought the Duke had stayed to be there at the King's death" (032-051–054).

## Key Appearances

- **Court tensions**: The Duchess pointedly refused to receive the Duchess of Cumberland, reflecting divisions within the royal family (008-159–160).
- **King's conversation**: The King's remark about the Duke of Gloucester drew a burst of laughter from the Duchess (020-130–132).
- **The King's final days**: She arrived at Windsor as the King's condition deteriorated (032-051–054).
