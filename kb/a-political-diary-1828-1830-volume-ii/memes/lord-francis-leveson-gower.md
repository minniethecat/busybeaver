# Lord Francis Leveson Gower

## Overview

Lord Francis Leveson Gower was a younger member of the [[Duke of Wellington]]'s Tory administration during the period of Ellenborough's diary. Referred to throughout the diary as "Lord F. Leveson," he served in government posts and was involved in key debates on Ireland and parliamentary matters. He was a son of the 1st Duke of Sutherland and corresponded privately with senior ministers including [[Robert Peel]].

## Role in the Diary

Lord Francis Leveson Gower appears in Ellenborough's diary as a junior but active figure in the Tory government. The diary records him expressing views on a range of issues, from the Irish question (009-106) to the state of government accounts (009-070). His correspondence with Peel is noted, with the diary observing that his private letters were in "an odd" state (016-090).

Some in government regarded him as inexperienced — one entry dismisses him as "a mere boy" in the context of Irish administration (032-012). Others were more supportive: the diary records discussion of making him a peer (035-129) and notes his inclusion in Cabinet reshuffle lists alongside Goulburn, Herries, and others (043-017). He was evidently a figure whose merits and prospects were actively debated within the ministry.

## Key Appearances

- **Irish affairs**: Leveson Gower appears regularly in discussions on Ireland, at one point being "much alarmed" about the Irish question (009-106), and later holding out "the apprehension" of difficulties (009-174).
- **The Brunswickers**: He expressed views on the Brunswicker question, reportedly thinking "rather favourably" of the situation at one point (008-150).
- **Cabinet reshuffling**: His name appears in lists of potential office-holders during Cabinet reorganization discussions (043-017), and there was suggestion that he "should be made a peer" (035-129).
- **Criticism**: Not all assessments were positive — one entry bluntly notes that someone else was preferred "instead of Lord Francis Leveson, who was always wrong" (048-128).
