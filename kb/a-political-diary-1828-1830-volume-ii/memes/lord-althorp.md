# Lord Althorp

## Overview

Lord Althorp (John Charles Spencer, Viscount Althorp, later 3rd Earl Spencer) was a leading Whig politician who served as Chancellor of the Exchequer and Leader of the [[house-of-commons|House of Commons]] in [[Lord Grey]]'s administration. He was a trusted lieutenant of Grey and played a central role in steering the [[reform-bill|Reform Bill]] through the Commons.

## Role in the Diary

Ellenborough's diary records several mentions of Lord Althorp during the political manoeuvring of 1828–1830. Althorp appears as a figure the Wellington government sought to win over: the diary notes that "he can do no harm, and Lord Althorp a very friendly one" (026-095), suggesting attempts to cultivate him as a moderate ally.

In discussions of cabinet formation, Ellenborough records that Althorp was considered for the Board of Trade: "might be divided, Lord Althorpe having the Board of Trade" (035-145). A meeting at his residence is noted as politically significant: "There was a meeting at Lord Althorpe's yesterday" (037-074).

Althorp's importance to the Whig coalition is underscored by the observation that "Lord Althorpe will not come in without Lord Grey" (043-008), demonstrating his loyalty to Grey. The diary also notes that Grey succeeded "in getting Lord Althorp after much solicitation" (054-147), showing the effort required to secure his commitment to government.

His earlier association with James Graham and the "Althorpe people" (007-044) indicates his role as a rallying point for reform-minded Whigs.

## Key Appearances

- **Cabinet negotiations**: Althorp was sought for the Board of Trade during coalition discussions (035-145).
- **Whig leadership**: His loyalty to Lord Grey was a factor in cabinet formation (043-008).
- **Meeting at Althorp's**: Political discussions were held at his residence (037-074).
