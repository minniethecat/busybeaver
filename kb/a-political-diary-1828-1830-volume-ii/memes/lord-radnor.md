# Lord Radnor

## Overview

Lord Radnor (William Pleydell-Bouverie, 3rd Earl of Radnor) was a Whig peer and reform advocate who sat in the [[house-of-lords|House of Lords]]. Known for his independent political views, he supported parliamentary reform and often aligned with [[Lord Durham]] on progressive causes.

## Role in the Diary

Lord Radnor appears in Ellenborough's diary as a vocal and independent-minded peer. The diarist records that "Lord Radnor made a good vulgar speech" (024-190), noting the popular appeal of his oratory. His interventions were noted as effective opposition: the government struggled to answer his points during debate (033-173).

Radnor's political positioning is noted in connection with the [[Duke of Buckingham]]: "Duke of Buckingham for us, Lord Radnor" (036-049), suggesting that Radnor was considered an ally worth cultivating by the government's supporters.

His alignment with reformist Whigs is clear: "Lord Radnor made some observations upon t" (037-141), and more significantly, "it is clear Lord Durham and Lord Radnor evidently intend to" (037-144) pursue a joint political course. This pairing with [[Lord Durham]] places Radnor firmly in the advanced reform wing of the Whig party.

## Key Appearances

- **Popular oratory**: Delivered a speech noted for its broad appeal (024-190).
- **Reform alliance**: Paired with Lord Durham in pursuing progressive objectives (037-144).
- **Opposition voice**: Part of the Whig bloc with Buckingham that challenged the government (036-049).
