**Prince Charles of Bavaria** (1795–1875) was a member of the House of Wittelsbach and a younger son of King Maximilian I Joseph of Bavaria. He appears in [[Lord Ellenborough]]'s diary in the context of the diplomatic negotiations over the Greek succession — one of several European princes considered for the newly-created Greek throne.

== Candidate for the Greek Throne ==

The Greek War of Independence (1821–1829) and the subsequent establishment of an independent Greek state under the London Protocol of 1830 created the question of who would rule the new kingdom. The diary records the diplomatic maneuvering among the Great Powers over suitable candidates.

[[Lord Ellenborough]] notes that some parties "rather wished for Prince Charles of Bavaria" (013-010), while Russia favored other candidates. A subsequent dispatch "named Prince Charles of Bavaria, and the second son of the Ki" (016-047) as being under consideration — indicating the Bavarian prince was a serious contender in the diplomatic discussions of the period.

== Competing Candidates ==

Prince Charles was not the only candidate. The diary lists multiple princes under consideration: "Orange, Prince Charles of Bavaria, Prince Otho of Bavaria, the" (021-023) alongside "Hesse Darmstadt, and Prince Charles of Mecklenburgh" (021-025). The Duke of Wellington and his diplomatic corps weighed the merits of each.

[[Lord Ellenborough]] records that "with regard to the last, Prince Charles of Mecklenburgh, the Duke" (021-028) had reservations. Prince Charles of Mecklenburgh, the brother of the late Queen Louise of Prussia, was also under consideration but faced objections — the diary notes "Charles of Mecklenburgh, which the King says he never" (020-087) approved.

== Outcome ==

In the end, the Powers settled on Prince Otho of Bavaria, who became the first King of Greece in 1832. [[Lord Ellenborough]] records the fallback position: "if he should refuse, then to Prince Charles of Bavaria" (044-003) — indicating that the Bavarian prince remained the second-choice candidate throughout the negotiations.
