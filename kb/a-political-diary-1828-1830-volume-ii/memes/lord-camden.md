# Lord Camden

## Overview

Lord Camden (John Jeffreys Pratt, 1st Marquess Camden) was a senior Tory peer and a loyal supporter of the [[Duke of Wellington]]'s administration. A former Lord Lieutenant of Ireland, he appears in Ellenborough's diary as a regular attendee at court and political functions, and as someone to whom the King turned for company and counsel.

## Role in the Diary

Lord Camden appears in Ellenborough's diary as a steady, uncontentious figure in the Tory establishment. Unlike some peers who attracted dramatic entries, Camden is recorded attending events, offering quiet opinions, and maintaining his place in government circles. The diary notes him among the guests at a gathering with Lady C. Wood (030-100), and as present at various official functions.

Camden was close enough to the monarch that "the King desired Lord Camden to come and see him frequently" (054-127), suggesting a relationship of trust and regular access. His country seat near Sevenoaks in Kent also features as a point of reference in the diary (039-020).

## Key Appearances

- **Court attendance**: Camden appears at drawing-rooms in formal dress alongside the Duke of Norfolk and other peers (034-019), and is listed among attendees at official gatherings (050-091).
- **Parliamentary opinion**: On one occasion, "Lord Camden thought" — indicating he offered his view — during a discussion about a division in the House of Lords where "a hundred on a division" was anticipated (007-042).
- **Personal matters**: The diary records a moment of sympathy: "Poor Lord Camden, in the meantime, has the lumbago" (050-092), a small detail that humanizes the peer and shows Ellenborough's attention to personal ailments among colleagues.
- **Consultations**: Camden spoke to Ellenborough on at least one political subject actively under discussion (053-116), and his presence is noted alongside figures such as Lord Winchilsea and the Duke of Leinster (051-014).
