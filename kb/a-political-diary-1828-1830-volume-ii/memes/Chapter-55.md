[055-001]I told him Peel would be in Opposition in a fortnight, as soon as he

[055-002]recovered his health and his spirits. There has been a report that the Duke

[055-003]had declared he would not take office again--which is untrue.

[055-004]Office. Saw Jones. Received a letter from the Chairs asking whether I had

[055-005]given Sir J. P. Grant authority to appeal to my sanction for his remaining

[055-006]in India, notwithstanding the Order in Council for his return. My answer is

[055-007]_No_. I add that I imagine the misapprehension arose out of some private

[055-008]communications from Sir J. P. Grant's friends, of the purport of a

[055-009]conversation with me which must have been inaccurately reported to him. I

[055-010]showed my draft reply to Lord Rosslyn, and begged him to show it to Grant's

[055-011]son.

[055-012]The report Hardinge gave me was that Lord Wellesley was to succeed me.
