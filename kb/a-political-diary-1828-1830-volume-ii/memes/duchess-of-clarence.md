# Duchess of Clarence

## Overview

Adelaide of Saxe-Meiningen (1792–1849), the Duchess of Clarence, was the wife of [[William IV|William, Duke of Clarence]], who succeeded [[George IV]] as king in 1830. In Ellenborough's diary she is discussed as a candidate for the Regency, a question that dominated court politics during the late 1820s as the health of George IV declined.

## Role in the Diary

The Duchess of Clarence appears in the diary almost exclusively in the context of the Regency question. Ellenborough records that she "would be Queen Regent" (028-100) — making her one of the competing candidates for the role of Regent during the minority of [[Princess Victoria]]. The political difficulty of the choice is reflected in the diarist's note that Lord B. "seemed to say we *could not* have the Duchess of Clarence as Regent" (030-022), implying constitutional or political objections.

Her relationship with the other royal women is also noted. Ellenborough observes that "the Duchess of Clarence and the Duchess of Kent were and are great friends, and the Duchess of Clarence is very fond of the young Princess" (030-105). This friendship between the two leading female figures at court was politically significant, as the [[Duke of Wellington]] and his cabinet weighed the competing claims of the Queen and the [[Duchess of Kent]] for the Regency.

## Key Appearances

- **(028-100)** Designated as a candidate to serve as Queen Regent
- **(030-022)** Objections raised to her serving as Regent — "we *could not* have the Duchess of Clarence"
- **(030-105)** Noted as great friends with the Duchess of Kent and fond of the young Princess Victoria
