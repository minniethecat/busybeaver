**Donna Maria** (Maria II of Portugal, 1819–1853) was the daughter of Emperor [[Don Pedro]] of Brazil and the ultimately successful claimant to the Portuguese throne. The diary records the diplomatic manoeuvring surrounding her during the Portuguese succession crisis.

== The Portuguese Succession ==

The diary identifies Donna Maria as "the ultimately successful candidate for the Portuguese throne, Donna Maria de Gloria." (017-045) Her father, Don Pedro, had abdicated the Portuguese throne in her favour but her claim was contested by her uncle, [[Don Miguel]], leading to a protracted civil conflict known as the Liberal Wars.

== Regency in Terceira ==

Don Pedro established a government in his daughter's name from abroad. The diary records that "Don Pedro has established a Regency in Terceira" in the name of "Queen Donna Maria." (026-061) Terceira, in the Azores, served as a liberal stronghold and the base from which Donna Maria's supporters would eventually reclaim the mainland.

== Marriage Diplomacy ==

Donna Maria's marriage became a subject of international negotiation. The diary notes that Don Pedro proposed a "conciliatory arrangement" that was "entirely inadmissible. They are founded upon the marriage of Donna Maria da Gloria, and England, France, and Austria are to guarantee her against any" threat. (042-162) The great powers' involvement in the Portuguese succession reflected the broader pattern of 1830s European diplomacy, where dynastic questions routinely drew in Britain, France, and Austria as guarantors.
