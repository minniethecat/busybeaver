# Mrs. Fox

## Overview

Mrs. Fox was a presence in London society during the period of Ellenborough's diary. Her appearances are recorded at prominent social gatherings connected with the opening of Parliament, alongside other notable ladies of the day.

## Role in the Diary

Mrs. Fox appears twice in Ellenborough's diary, both in connection with the social events surrounding Parliament. At the opening of the session in April 1829, the diarist noted: "The House was full of ladies. Mrs. Fox, Lady Jersey, Lady Pitt and her" party were in attendance (001-043). The presence of so many ladies at the House reflected the intense public interest in the proceedings.

On another occasion, Ellenborough recorded a gathering where "Mrs. Fox, and some other ladies were there — Lady Stanhope. The old Duchess" was also present (001-118). Her association with Lady Jersey, Lady Pitt, and Lady Stanhope places her within the higher reaches of Regency and late-Georgian political society.

## Key Appearances

- **Opening of Parliament**: Present in the House among a large gathering of ladies, alongside Lady Jersey and Lady Pitt (001-043).
- **Society gathering**: Attended an event with Lady Stanhope and other figures of London society (001-118).
