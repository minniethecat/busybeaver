# Banking and Currency

## Overview

Questions of banking and currency were significant in British political discourse during the late 1820s. The Bank of England's charter, the circulation of small notes, and monetary policy were subjects of ongoing parliamentary and governmental attention.

## The Bank Charter

The renewal and terms of the Bank of England's charter involved complex negotiations between the Government, the Bank, and various commercial interests. These negotiations had important implications for the broader financial system. (001-169) (001-170)

## Currency and Economy

The Government's currency measures affected trade, credit, and the broader economy. Debates about monetary policy reflected deeper divisions about the direction of British economic policy. (001-174) (004-103)
