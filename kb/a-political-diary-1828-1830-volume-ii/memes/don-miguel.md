**Dom Miguel I** (1802–1866) was King of Portugal from 1828 to 1834. He usurped the throne from his young niece [[Donna Maria]], daughter of his brother [[Don Pedro]], triggering a succession crisis that preoccupied the British government throughout the diary's period.

== The Usurpation and European Reaction ==

Miguel's seizure of power created a diplomatic dilemma for the great powers. The diary records anxiety about other nations moving first: the government "fears France may acknowledge Miguel first, and thus take our place" in Portuguese affairs. (016-109) This competitive diplomacy between Britain and France characterised the early response to Miguel's rule.

There were proposals for a negotiated settlement, with "a stipulation for the recognition of Miguel" in exchange for concessions, including an amnesty for his opponents. (017-046) Britain was deeply reluctant: the diarist recorded that ministers were "very unwilling to recognise Don Miguel." (024-091)

== The Road to Recognition ==

Despite initial reluctance, pragmatic considerations pushed towards accepting Miguel's rule. The diary observed: "Whenever Miguel is recognised, I think Lord" Aberdeen would need to manage the diplomatic fallout carefully. (024-095) Some in the government considered it "imprudent, abusing Miguel and making awkward admissions." (024-131)

The threat of force was contemplated: Britain might threaten to send forces to "Lisbon if Don Miguel will not grant the amnesty." (027-080) Yet there was optimism that "Miguel would be on good terms with us" if recognition were granted. (035-143)

The diplomatic calculus shifted towards recognition. The diary records that "the Brazilians will acknowledge Miguel as the Regent, if he will" accept certain conditions. (042-096) Britain concluded "that we must acknowledge Don Miguel as soon as" practical. (042-099) The hope was that "Miguel will pass that decree we are" demanding, and "recognition of Miguel might lead to the prevention" of further conflict. (042-174) By mid-1830, the government prepared "to announce the recognition of Miguel." (049-042)
