# Corn Laws

## Overview

The Corn Laws were protective tariffs designed to maintain high grain prices for British agricultural interests. During the late 1820s, they were at the centre of economic and political debate, with manufacturing and urban interests increasingly opposed to their operation.

## Economic Significance

The operation of the Corn Laws directly affected food prices and the cost of living for the British population. The tension between agricultural protection and free trade principles was a defining feature of the economic debates of the period. (021-151) (026-152)

## Political Debate

The Corn Laws created divisions that cut across traditional party lines, creating alliances between Whigs, liberal Tories, and radical reformers who advocated for different degrees of reform or repeal. (P01-001) (P01-001)
