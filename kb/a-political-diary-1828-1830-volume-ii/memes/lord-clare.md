# Lord Clare

## Overview

Lord Clare (John FitzGibbon, 2nd Earl of Clare) was an Irish peer who appears in Ellenborough's diary as an ambitious figure seeking a colonial governorship. During the 1828–1830 period, he was actively pursuing appointment to a senior post in India and maintaining close contact with the [[Duke of Wellington]]'s administration.

## Role in the Diary

Clare's primary concern in the diary is his desire for advancement to a major Indian governorship. [[Lord Ellenborough]] recorded that he "mentioned Lord Clare's wish to look forward to the Government of Bombay or Madras" to the Duke, noting that Wellington "did not by any means receive the proposition unfavourably." (006-007) Ellenborough conveyed the encouraging response to Clare the same day. (006-009)

Clare maintained direct correspondence with both Ellenborough and Wellington. He "received a letter from Lord Clare, who saw the Duke yesterday," reporting that the "Duke was very kind and told him he should get all the information he could before the Committee of next year." (008-019) Ellenborough pledged: "I shall most willingly assist him." (008-021)

## Political Connections

Clare kept Ellenborough informed of Irish political developments. He "told me Glengall was to be the new Irish peer" (006-014), demonstrating his awareness of patronage networks in Ireland. His election to some body or committee was noted with the observation that "there was but one black ball in the election of Lord Clare, and the Chairs think that was put in by mistake; no one objected" (025-160), suggesting broad acceptance among his peers.

Ellenborough himself reminded the Duke "of Lord Clare's appointment, not yet approved by the King." (027-008) The pair also discussed Indian policy together: Ellenborough "brought Lord Clare home after church, and showed him my letter to Mr. Elphinstone respecting the chiefs of Kattywar and the Guicowar." (027-096) They "talked over the policy to be pursued with regard to them." (027-097)

Clare would eventually secure his ambition, serving as Governor of Bombay from 1831 to 1835.
