# Robert Peel

## Overview

Robert Peel served as Home Secretary in the Duke of Wellington's government during 1828–1830. He was a central figure in the passage of Catholic Emancipation and appears frequently in Ellenborough's diary as a close advisor to the Prime Minister. Peel was the Leader of the House of Commons while Wellington led the Lords.

## Role in the Government

Peel was the Duke's key ally in the Commons. When the petition crisis arose, "Peel is going down to Windsor himself" to advise the King directly. (001-013) His advice was considered decisive: "If the King will not take Peel's advice we go out." (001-016) The weight placed on Peel's counsel reflects his standing within the government.

## Assessments and Influence

Peel provided candid assessments of the political situation. On the King's health, "Peel said, is very blind. He has lost the sight of one eye." (011-018) Both Peel and the Duke shared the opinion that "the King would be most." (011-020) On Ireland, "Peel seems to think Ireland stands much better since the proclamation." (010-105)
