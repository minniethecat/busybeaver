# Lord Abinger

## Overview

James Scarlett, 1st Baron Abinger, was an English barrister and politician. He served as Attorney-General (1827–1828, 1829–1830) under the Duke of Wellington and was later raised to the peerage as Baron Abinger. In Ellenborough''s diary, he appears during the legal promotions of 1829, when Scarlett was appointed chief law officer of the Crown.

## Role in the Diary

Ellenborough records the legal appointments under Wellington's government in May 1828, noting that "the King will be delighted with Scarlett for the legal promotions" (003-086). The entry identifies Scarlett as the incoming Attorney-General: "[Footnote: Sir James Scarlett, afterwards Lord Abinger.] as Attorney-General, and the Chancellor tells me Bickersteth is to be Solicitor" (003-087–088).

A year later, in June 1829, Ellenborough describes a Council meeting at which the King received the new law officers: "The Attorney-General (Scarlett), [Footnote: Afterwards Lord Abinger.] the Chief Justice of the Common Pleas (Tindal), and the Solicitor-General (Sugden) all kissed hands" (005-181–184). The King was in poor health, "walked in with great difficulty on two crutches" and "had a chair brought for him, and had him wheeled out" (005-178–180).

Ellenborough, who gave up the Privy Seal on this occasion, noted dryly: "The King did not address a word to me, who gave up the seal, or to Lord Rosslyn" (005-186).

## Key Appearances

- **Attorney-General appointment (1828)**: Scarlett was selected as Attorney-General, with the King reportedly delighted with the choice (003-086–088).
- **Kissing hands (1829)**: Scarlett, Tindal, and Sugden formally kissed hands as the new law officers before a frail King at Council (005-181–184).
