# Duke of Northumberland

## Overview

Hugh Percy, 3rd Duke of Northumberland (1785–1847), was a British peer and Tory politician. He served as Lord-Lieutenant of Ireland from 1829 to 1830 under the [[Duke of Wellington]]'s administration, and was known for his extensive correspondence with [[Robert Peel]] on Irish affairs.

## Role in the Diary

The Duke of Northumberland appears in Ellenborough's diary primarily in connection with Irish administration. His appointment as Lord-Lieutenant placed him at the centre of [[british-cabinet|Cabinet]] discussions about Ireland during a period of intense agitation for Catholic emancipation and its aftermath.

Ellenborough records reading "the Duke of Northumberland's letter to Peel on the state" of Ireland (022-062), indicating his role as a key source of intelligence from Dublin. His practical concerns about the office are noted: "The Duke of Northumberland says the salary of the Lord-L" (024-166), likely referring to the financial arrangements of the Lord-Lieutenancy.

Further correspondence is recorded with "a letter from the Duke of Northumberland" (028-075), confirming his regular communication with the government. References to Irish papers and the Duke of Northumberland (008-149) suggest his involvement in press and public opinion matters.

A later entry mentions "Northumberland House" (053-052), the Percy family's London residence at Charing Cross, though this appears in a context unrelated to Irish administration.

## Key Appearances

- **Irish correspondence**: Regular letters to Peel on the state of Ireland informed Cabinet decisions (022-062).
- **Lord-Lieutenancy matters**: Practical concerns about the office were communicated to the government (024-166).
- **Irish press**: His name appears in connection with Irish papers (008-149).
