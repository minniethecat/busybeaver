# Lord Strangford

## Overview

Percy Smythe, 6th Viscount Strangford, was a British diplomat who served as ambassador to Portugal (1806–1808), Sweden (1817–1820), and as ambassador to Russia (1825–1826). In Ellenborough''s diary, he appears at the royal court and later brings news from India regarding colonial judicial appointments.

## Role in the Diary

Ellenborough first mentions Strangford in July 1828, during a court appearance: "Stratford Canning and Lord Strangford were at the Court, to be presented on their return" (008-147). Both diplomats had recently returned from overseas postings — Strangford from his Russian embassy and Canning from Constantinople.

In April 1830, Strangford brings sombre news to the House of Lords: "In the House Lord Strangford told me that Sir W. Seymour... was dead. He died in December — a short time after the birth of his son" (028-022–024). Seymour had recently been appointed a judge at Bombay. Ellenborough reflected on the pattern of mortality: "Really the mortality amongst judges is awful" (028-025).

## Key Appearances

- **Court appearance (July 1828)**: Strangford and Stratford Canning attended court upon returning from their diplomatic missions (008-147).
- **News from India (April 1830)**: Strangford informed Ellenborough of Sir W. Seymour's death in Bombay, shortly after his appointment as judge (028-022–024).
