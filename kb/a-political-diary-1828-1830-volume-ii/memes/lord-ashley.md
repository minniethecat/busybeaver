# Lord Ashley

## Overview

Lord Ashley (Anthony Ashley-Cooper, 1801–1885) was a Tory politician who held junior office during the Duke of Wellington's administration of 1828–1830. He appears in Ellenborough's diary as a young office-holder navigating Treasury appointments and India Board business. He later succeeded to the earldom of Shaftesbury and became one of the most celebrated social reformers of the Victorian era.

## Role in the Diary

Ashley appears in the diary primarily in connection with government patronage and Treasury appointments. Ellenborough records considering him for a Lordship of the Treasury (023-066), and discusses moving him to Horace Twiss's place (019-083). Ashley's potential transfer to the India Board is also noted (023-071), reflecting the fluid reshuffling of junior ministerial posts characteristic of the period.

Ellenborough's tone toward Ashley is somewhat impatient — at one point Ashley "came and bored me about a petition" (001-099) — but elsewhere he acknowledges that Ashley "spoke good stuff apparently" during a parliamentary exchange (025-070). Ashley also prepared a memorandum on judicial matters that Ellenborough read with interest (018-020).
