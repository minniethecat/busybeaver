# Irish Question

## Overview

Ireland was at the centre of British political debate throughout the late 1820s. Its religious divisions, economic distress, and political representation demanded constant attention from the Duke of Wellington's ministry.

## The Primate and the Church

The Primate of Ireland featured prominently in political discussions, making representations on behalf of the Irish Protestant interest. The diary records the Primate's involvement in peerage matters and church affairs. (P01-061) (P01-106)

## Catholic Relief and Ireland

The settlement of the Catholic question was intimately tied to the condition of Ireland. The diary records Lord Ellenborough's observations about Ireland as affected by the laws, and his reflections on what could be done for the happiness of Ireland and for the strength of the Empire through legislative measures. (P01-121) (P01-125) (P01-138)
