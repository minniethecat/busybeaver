# Prince John of Saxony

## Overview

Prince John of Saxony (1801–1873), later King Johann of Saxony, was a prince of the House of Wettin who emerges in Ellenborough's diary as one of the European royal candidates considered for a foreign throne during the diplomatic maneuverings of the late 1820s.

## Role in the Diary

The diary records Prince John of Saxony in the context of international candidate selection — likely for the throne of [[Greece]], which was the subject of great-power diplomacy following Greek independence. Ellenborough notes that "The French have suggested Prince John of Saxony" (016-051), placing him among the various princes proposed by the European powers.

His candidacy was managed alongside that of other princes. The diary records that the government deferred action "till our candidates, Prince John of Saxony and Ferdinand of Orange, were disposed of" (020-116), referring to the practice of advancing multiple candidates through diplomatic channels before committing to one. He is also listed alongside "Prince Philip of Hesse Homburgh, Prince John of Saxony, Prince Frederick of Orange" (021-022) as part of the roster of princely candidates under consideration.

## Key Appearances

- **(016-051)** Suggested by the French as a candidate for the Greek throne
- **(020-116)** Listed alongside Ferdinand of Orange among British-backed candidates awaiting disposition
- **(021-022)** Named among several European princes under consideration
