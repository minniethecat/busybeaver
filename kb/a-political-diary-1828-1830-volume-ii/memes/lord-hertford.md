# Lord Hertford

## Overview

Francis Seymour-Conway, 3rd Marquess of Hertford, was a British Tory politician and courtier who served as Lord Chamberlain and held extensive political influence through his wealth and family connections. In Ellenborough''s diary, he appears as the recipient of a letter from the diarist and as host to the Duke of Wellington at his Suffolk estate.

## Role in the Diary

Ellenborough writes of the Duke of Wellington's visit to Sudborn, Hertford's Suffolk seat, in October 1829. While there, the Duke discussed Russian diplomacy with Fitzgerald: "He was at Sudborn, [Footnote: Seat of Lord Hertford, in Suffolk] where the Duke was. The Duke was not so much inclined to think the Russians would make any considerable concessions as Aberdeen, but he thought, and had made Fitzgerald think, it would be premature to remonstrate" (014-089–093).

In a later entry, Ellenborough records corresponding with Hertford on a matter of patronage: "Wrote to Lord Hertford and enclosed an extract from my letter to Lady Belfast" (017-111–112). The letter related to a cadetship granted to Mr. Verner, a favour Ellenborough had arranged for Lady Belfast: "Begged her to make his family and friends understand thoroughly that this was a private favour I had led her to expect long before the discussion of the Catholic question" (017-108–110).

## Key Appearances

- **Sudborn meeting (October 1829)**: The Duke of Wellington stayed at Hertford's Suffolk seat, where Russian policy was discussed (014-089–093).
- **Correspondence (date uncertain)**: Ellenborough wrote to Hertford enclosing an extract concerning a cadetship favour arranged through Lady Belfast (017-111–112).
