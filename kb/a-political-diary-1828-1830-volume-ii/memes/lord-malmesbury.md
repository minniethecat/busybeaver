# Lord Malmesbury

## Overview

James Harris, 2nd Earl of Malmesbury, was a reliable Tory supporter in the [[house-of-lords|House of Lords]] during the [[Duke of Wellington]]'s administration. The diary records his votes with the government on the [[franchise-bill-1829|Franchise Bill]] and other contentious measures, marking him as a dependable, if not prominent, ally of the ministry.

## Role in the Diary

Malmesbury's stance on the Franchise Bill was noted in the diary's preliminary vote analysis. He was listed among those who shared "the desire of Lord Malmesbury and [[lord-grey|Lord Grey]]" on one aspect of the measure, but the record is nuanced: he "likewise expressed himself against" certain provisions (P01-028). In a parallel entry, the diary notes his alignment with Lord Grey on a specific point (P01-024).

When votes were counted on Franchise, Malmesbury appeared among the opponents of the bill alongside Winchelsea and Clanricarde (P01-156, P01-163). Yet in subsequent divisions, he proved a government ally: "Coplestone. Dudley, Lord Malmesbury, Lord Gower, voted with us" (026-085), showing his political alignment was pragmatic rather than factional. The Bishops also joined him in seeking procedural delay (005-111).

The diary also records a moment of critical scrutiny. Ellenborough notes an effort to "show the inaccuracy of one of Lord Malmesbury's conclusions" (037-095), suggesting his arguments in debate were not always beyond reproach, even from government supporters.

## Key Appearances

- **Franchise Bill debate**: Featured in the vote analysis, opposing certain provisions of the 1829 Franchise Bill alongside Winchelsea (P01-024, P01-028, P01-156, P01-163).
- **Government division support**: Voted with the Wellington ministry on at least one recorded division (026-085).
- **Procedural intervention**: Joined the Bishops in seeking to delay proceedings (005-111).
- **Debate scrutiny**: His conclusions in debate were challenged for inaccuracy (037-095).
