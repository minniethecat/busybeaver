# Lord Rosslyn

## Overview

Lord Rosslyn (James St Clair-Erskine, 2nd Earl of Rosslyn) served as Lord Privy Seal in the [[Duke of Wellington]]'s administration from 1829. A loyal [[tory-party|Tory]], he was a regular attendee of Cabinet meetings and a dependable government supporter during a period of significant political turbulence.

## Role in the Diary

Rosslyn appears as a steady, cordial presence in the Wellington Cabinet. The diary records his appointment as Privy Seal filling a vacancy (005-020), and the Duke "showed me a letter from Lord Rosslyn, accepting most cordially" the position (005-087). His appointment was "in the newspapers" shortly after (005-105) and he was "sworn in as Privy Councillor" at the first opportunity (005-185).

However, Rosslyn's personal circumstances intrude: he was "having just lost his son" when appointed, which affected his availability (005-056). There were also lingering questions about his reliability — Arbuthnot "asked me if I thought Lord Rosslyn would be cordial with us" (007-046), and at one point Rosslyn "had not answered" a summons, with "none following his example" (007-104).

Despite these concerns, Rosslyn became a fixture: he dined at Lord Bathurst's (005-189), attended Cabinet regularly — Ellenborough "saw Lord Rosslyn there, as I used to be last year" (009-041) — and participated in discussions with Fitzgerald, Peel, and Herries (007-133).

## Key Appearances

- **Appointment as Privy Seal (Chapter 5)**: Accepted the position cordially, though delayed by personal bereavement.
- **Cabinet meetings**: Regular attendance throughout 1828–1829, noted as a steady presence.
- **London Bridge Committee**: Alongside [[Lord Londonderry]], he participated in parliamentary committee work.
