# Lord Holland

## Overview

Lord Holland (Henry Richard Vassall-Fox, 3rd Baron Holland) was a leading Whig statesman and the nephew of Charles James Fox. A fixture of Whig political and literary society, Holland House became a celebrated salon. During the Wellington administration he was a frequent and effective opposition speaker in the [[house-of-lords|House of Lords]].

## Role in the Diary

The diary records Lord Holland as a versatile parliamentary operator. Ellenborough notes Holland giving notice of a motion on one day (023-031), raising questions about Admiralty orders on another (024-125), and making procedural motions _pro formâ_ to advance bills (038-024). His speaking ability commanded respect: "A most dull debate, till Lord Holland spoke," Ellenborough writes, adding simply "I answered him" (026-079).

Holland's engagement with foreign affairs appears repeatedly. He was closely involved with Portuguese matters (023-131) and the Libel law (038-124). The diary reveals Holland's personal rapport even across party lines — Ellenborough wrote to Holland "when I came home to call his attention" to a matter and received a reply showing Holland was "fully alive to the consequences" (029-180).

His social network is evident: Holland spoke "in favour of" various peers at gatherings (P01-156), and the diary later notes that "Lord Holland is very much with" those who have called upon Talleyrand (047-161), tying him to diplomatic circles.

## Key Appearances

- **Parliamentary debate**: Gave notice of motions (023-031), intervened in debates (026-079), and made procedural motions (038-024).
- **Portuguese question**: Active in scrutiny of Portuguese papers and policy (023-131).
- **Reform sympathies**: Alongside Grey and others, "seemed to think" reform measures were inadequate (034-085).
- **Foreign diplomacy**: Connected with Talleyrand's circle (047-161), suggesting informal diplomatic channels.
