# Lord Shaftesbury

## Overview

Lord Shaftesbury (Cropley Ashley-Cooper, 6th Earl of Shaftesbury) was a reliable Tory peer who served the [[house-of-lords|House of Lords]] as Deputy Speaker during the absence of the Lord Chancellor. His role in Ellenborough's diary is primarily procedural, managing the passage of legislation and chairing sessions.

## Procedural Role in the Lords

Shaftesbury's most frequent function was presiding over the House in the Chancellor's absence. Ellenborough records: "Lord Shaftesbury, in the absence of the Chancellor, sat as Speaker. I moved the bills _pro formâ_ for him." (P01-033) This was a routine but necessary function of the Upper House's daily business.

## The Royal Assent Error

Shaftesbury was one of the principal figures managing a procedural crisis when a bill received Royal Assent in error. "Lord Shaftesbury thought our House ought to inform the Commons we had discovered the error." (006-094) The corrective steps "were settled between the Speaker, Lord Shaftesbury, and Courtenay." (006-098)

When the Commons proved uncooperative, Ellenborough records: "I suggested that Lord Shaftesbury should acquaint the House with the circumstance, and that we should appoint a Committee to inquire before the message from the Commons came up. This was done." (006-100)

## Political Character

Shaftesbury appears in the diary as a steady, uncontentious figure — consulted on procedure, entrusted with the chair, and relied upon to manage delicate parliamentary situations. His role as a procedural authority in the [[house-of-lords|House of Lords]] placed him at the intersection of the [[tory-party|Tory]] government's legislative agenda and the daily operation of the Upper House.
