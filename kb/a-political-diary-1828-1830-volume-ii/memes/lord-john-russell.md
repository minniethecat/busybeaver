**Lord John Russell** (1792–1878), later 1st Earl Russell, was a leading British Whig politician and a principal architect of parliamentary reform. He was the younger son of the [[Duke of Bedford]] and became one of the most influential figures in the [[Whig Party]], serving twice as Prime Minister.

== Parliamentary Career ==

The diary records Lord J. Russell's motion in the [[House of Commons]]. (024-179) During this period, Russell was emerging as a leading voice for reform within the Whig opposition, challenging the Wellington ministry's resistance to constitutional change.

== Electoral Fortunes ==

The diary notes a significant political setback: Lord J. Russell was not returned for Bedford. (042-014) This electoral defeat was a temporary obstacle in Russell's career, which would soon see him become the principal draftsman of the [[Reform Bill]] and the leading figure of the reform movement in the Commons.
