# Duke of Buccleuch

## Overview

Walter Montagu Douglas Scott, 5th Duke of Buccleuch (1806–1884), was one of the wealthiest landowners in Scotland and a Tory peer. During the period of Ellenborough's diary, the young Duke appears at political dinners and events, where the diarist encountered him alongside other prominent figures of the day.

## Role in the Diary

Ellenborough recorded the Duke of Buccleuch's presence at a dinner that included "Ashley, and Bankes; Duke of Buccleuch, Lord Camden, Lord Montagu" (006-166). The gathering brought together Tory peers and MPs, indicative of Buccleuch's place within the governing political circles of the late 1820s.

Later in the same period, Ellenborough had occasion to converse with the Duke directly. He noted: "We had a good deal of conversation. He seems a fine young man" (006-185). This favourable impression from the diarist, who was not given to casual praise, suggests Buccleuch's early promise within Tory political society.

## Key Appearances

- **Political dinner**: Present at a gathering of Tory peers and MPs, seated alongside Lord Camden and Lord Montagu (006-166).
- **Conversation with Ellenborough**: The diarist conversed with the young Duke and formed a positive impression of his character (006-185).
