**William Amherst, 1st Earl Amherst** (1773–1857) was a British diplomat and colonial administrator. He served as Ambassador to China (1816) and Governor-General of India (1823–1828). By the time of the diary in 1828–1830, he had returned from India and was active in the [[House of Lords]].

== Diplomatic Career ==

Amherst's diplomatic career was most noted for his ill-fated embassy to China in 1816. The diary references this episode: [[Lord Ellenborough]] notes that a correspondent "took out Lord Amherst as Ambassador to China twelve years before." (008-006) The embassy famously failed when Amherst refused to perform the kowtow ceremony before the Jiaqing Emperor.

The Chinese reaction was swift: "The instant the Chinese found the ship was gone and Lord Amherst" had departed, trade arrangements shifted. (008-009) The mission's failure became a cautionary tale in British diplomatic circles.

== In the House of Lords ==

During the period of the diary, Amherst was active in the House of Lords. [[Lord Wharncliffe]] was advised that "he should examine Lord Amherst" (025-012) on certain matters, indicating Amherst's relevance in parliamentary affairs.

Amherst is listed alongside [[Lord Clare]] and Auckland in the diary's record of peers attending to business. (033-084) His presence in these proceedings places him among the active Tory peers during the Wellington administration.

== Later Life ==

Amherst was created Earl Amherst in 1826 following his return from India. Though he did not hold major office during the 1828–1830 period, he remained an influential voice in the Lords on colonial and foreign policy matters.
