# Lord Lansdowne

## Overview

Lord Lansdowne (Henry Petty-Fitzmaurice, 3rd Marquess of Lansdowne) was one of the foremost [[whig-party|Whig]] statesmen of the period. Having served as Chancellor of the Exchequer in the Ministry of All the Talents (1806–1807), he remained a leading voice for moderate reform and Catholic emancipation in the [[house-of-lords|House of Lords]] throughout the late 1820s.

## Role in the Diary

Ellenborough's diary records Lansdowne as a persistent but generally respected opponent. In the Lords, Lansdowne "spoke better than he has done for some time" on one occasion (P01-065), though his speeches could be wearisome — "a long speech from Lord Lansdowne, still less wanted" (001-030). He "put a pompously worded question" to the government (004-066) and regularly challenged government policy.

Lansdowne's political alignment with [[Lord Grey]] was significant: after one debate "others left Lord Lansdowne to coalesce with Lord Grey" (007-105), signalling the consolidation of Whig opposition leadership. He was active on slavery issues, bringing in Zachary Macaulay as a witness on colonial matters (023-123), and participated in cross-examinations of witnesses (026-141).

The diary records meetings at Lansdowne's residence, including one attended by the Duke of Newcastle (032-037), suggesting his role as a Whig gathering point. In debates on religious questions, Lansdowne was noted "throwing out" objections (033-122). The Lord Chancellor "made an admirable speech" with Lansdowne contributing afterwards (036-073).

## Key Appearances

- **House of Lords debates**: A regular speaker challenging government measures across multiple chapters.
- **Slavery and colonial questions**: Brought forward witnesses and evidence on colonial administration.
- **Whig coalition-building**: Emerged alongside Lord Grey as a central figure in Whig opposition strategy (007-105).
- **Reform advocacy**: Though more moderate than some Whigs, he pushed for measured reform — "Lord Lansdowne thought the Whigs should not push us à l'outrance" (037-070).
