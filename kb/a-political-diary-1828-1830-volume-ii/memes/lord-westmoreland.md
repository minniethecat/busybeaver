# Lord Westmoreland

## Overview

Lord Westmoreland (John Fane, 10th Earl of Westmoreland) was a veteran Tory politician who served as Lord Privy Seal in several administrations. In Ellenborough's diary, he appears as a figure who combined political engagement with an entertaining personality, participating in parliamentary debates and court functions.

## Role in the Diary

Westmoreland features in the diary as a peer who mixed political activity with a distinctive personal style. Ellenborough records that "Westmoreland made an odd, entertaining fro…" (P01-100), suggesting a personality that stood out in the formal political circles of the day. As a member of the House of Lords, he participated in parliamentary business and was connected to government patronage, with one entry noting the disposition of an office that "nature, will offer it to Lord Westmoreland" (024-098).

## Key Appearances

- **At court**: At St. James's, Lord Westmoreland reported to Ellenborough on political developments, telling him about events at a gathering the previous day (039-059).
- **Parliamentary activity**: Westmoreland spoke in the House of Lords on the East Retford Bill, contributing to the debate that followed another peer's remarks (039-138).
