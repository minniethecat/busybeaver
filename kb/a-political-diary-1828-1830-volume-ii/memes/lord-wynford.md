# Lord Wynford

## Overview

Lord Wynford (William Draper Best, 1st Baron Wynford) was a British judge and Tory politician who served as Chief Justice of the Common Pleas from 1824 to 1829. Created Baron Wynford in 1829, he was a conservative voice in the [[house-of-lords|House of Lords]], often aligning with [[Lord Eldon]] and [[Lord Tenterden]] on judicial matters.

## Role in the Diary

Wynford appears in Ellenborough's diary chiefly in relation to judicial business and political consultations. Early in the diary, Ellenborough records that "Lord Wynford communicated with the Duke" (005-033), indicating his role as an intermediary with the [[Duke of Wellington]].

On judicial matters, Wynford was a consistent conservative. During debate on the Scotch Judicature Bill, "Lord Wynford made a" contribution (038-004), engaging with proposed reforms to the Scottish legal system. He also "wrote to Peel on the Duke of" (038-159), participating in the correspondence network between the judiciary and the [[british-cabinet|Cabinet]].

Wynford's judicial conservatism placed him alongside Eldon and Tenterden: the diary notes "Lords Wynford, Tenterden, and Eldon all aga" (036-074), suggesting a bloc of senior judges opposed to particular reforms. His earlier judicial standing is noted alongside "Lord Eldon and Lord Wynford" (020-094).

A more personal note records that "we had Wynford with us" at a gathering, adding that "there is no explaini" (039-057).

## Key Appearances

- **Scotch Judicature Bill**: Wynford spoke on the proposed reforms to the Scottish judiciary (038-004).
- **Conservative judicial bloc**: Aligned with Eldon and Tenterden in opposing certain legal changes (036-074).
- **Correspondence with Peel**: Participated in the political-judicial correspondence network (038-159).
