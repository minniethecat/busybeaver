**Mr. Ricketts** appeared as a witness before a parliamentary committee in March 1830. The diary records his examination with a note of mild bemusement at his performance.

== Committee Appearance ==

On March 31, 1830, the committee convened at one o'clock. "Examined Mr. Ricketts, the half-caste, when Lord Carlisle had examined him in chief." (026-164) The diary describes Ricketts as being of mixed race, though no further biographical details are recorded in the text.

Ricketts's testimony was not impressive: "Mr. Ricketts did not seem to know much about the law." (026-165) The diarist, who sat on the committee, observed Ricketts's demeanour with detached amusement: "It was odd enough to observe him looking round to me after every answer." (026-167) This detail suggests Ricketts sought reassurance or approval from the committee members after each response.

== A Contrast ==

Immediately after Ricketts, the committee heard from a Mr. Baker, who provided "a strong contrast indeed with Mr. Ricketts. He gave very curious evidence relative to the trade of the Arabs of Malabar." (026-168) The juxtaposition underscores the diarist's low estimate of Ricketts as a witness.

The entry is a rare glimpse into the mechanics of parliamentary committee work in the period, and one of the diary's few references to a person of mixed race appearing in an official capacity alongside figures such as the [[Duke of Wellington]].
