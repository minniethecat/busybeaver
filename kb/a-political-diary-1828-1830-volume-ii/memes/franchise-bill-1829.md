# Franchise Bill 1829

## Overview

The Franchise Bill of 1829 was a parliamentary measure to revise electoral franchises, specifically concerning East Retford and later Galway. It occupied significant parliamentary time during Wellington's first session and intersected with the Catholic Relief Bill debate. Ellenborough recorded its progress in the House of Lords.

## The East Retford Bill

The House of Lords made progress on the Franchise Bill in April 1829. Ellenborough recorded: "House. Got through the report of the Franchise Bill. Third reading fixed for to-morrow." (001-024) The measure attracted significant support, passing with "a majority of 104. The Franchise Bill was likewise read a third time." (001-034) The timing coincided with the Catholic Relief Bill, and members were advised "to pray, but not to trust that the Franchise Bill and the Relief Bill will" both succeed without complication. (006-073)

## The Galway Extension

The franchise question extended to Irish boroughs, with the "Galway Franchise Bill read second time." (033-150) Counsel were to have been heard, and the bill attracted close scrutiny of electoral practices. The matter carried political weight: one participant was "very certain of his case" regarding the Galway Franchise Bill. (033-158)
