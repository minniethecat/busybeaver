**Charles Long, 1st Baron Farnborough** (1760–1838) was a British Tory politician, art connoisseur, and courtier. He appears in the diary of [[Lord Ellenborough]] as a figure close to [[George IV]], involved in both the social life of the political elite and the practical arrangements of the King's household.

== Court Role ==

Lord Farnborough acted as an intermediary between the King and the political world. The diary records that the King told Farnborough of his intention to live at Windsor, a significant detail in the context of the monarch's retreat from public life in London. (034-071) This confidence suggests Farnborough enjoyed personal access to George IV.

== Political and Social Connections ==

Farnborough was present at Lady Brownlow's ball, a gathering of the political and aristocratic elite where Ellenborough conversed with him. (003-141) He performed ceremonial functions at court, including the stamping of documents when the King was "perfectly" well and engaged in official business. (032-020)
