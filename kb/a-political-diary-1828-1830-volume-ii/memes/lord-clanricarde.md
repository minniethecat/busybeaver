# Lord Clanricarde

## Overview

Ulick de Burgh, 1st Marquess of Clanricarde (1802–1874), was an Anglo-Irish Whig peer who sat in the House of Lords during the Wellington administration. He appears in Ellenborough's diary as an opposition speaker whose motions on Irish and ecclesiastical matters drew the government's attention, though not always its respect.

## Role in the Diary

Clanricarde surfaces in the diary as a persistent voice on Irish and ecclesiastical issues. He moved a motion on the "eternal Terce" — a Scottish church finance question — which Ellenborough recorded as the subject of parliamentary debate (026-117). The diarist's dismissive tone in "So much for Lord Clanricarde's speech" (053-058) reflects the government's confidence in brushing aside opposition motions. Clanricarde was also noted in connection with Lord Holland and Whig peers speaking on Irish affairs (P01-156), and alongside Goderich in a division that ultimately did not materialise (023-178).

## Key Appearances

- **(P01-156)** Spoke in Lords debate alongside Malmesbury and Winchelsea
- **(026-117)** Moved a motion on the Scottish Terce (ecclesiastical finance)
- **(053-058)** Speech noted and dismissed by Ellenborough
