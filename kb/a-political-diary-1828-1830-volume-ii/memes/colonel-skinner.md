**Colonel Skinner** was a British officer serving in India during the late 1820s. The diary provides a vivid character sketch, describing him as a man of considerable landed wealth and local influence.

== Character and Estate ==

The diarist was told that "Lieutenant-Colonel Skinner was a man of large landed property. He had raised his corps very much from his own estate and neighbourhood, and was a sort of feudal chieftain." (008-001) He was noted to have been "educated like a native" (008-003), suggesting a deep integration into Indian society uncommon among British officers of the period.

== Personal Details ==

Colonel Willoughby Cotton provided further details: "Colonel Skinner is about 55. His son is a merchant, and goes every year into Cashmere for shawls." (011-079) This detail places Skinner within the Anglo-Indian military-mercantile class, with family connections to the valuable Kashmir shawl trade.

The portrait that emerges is of an officer who embodied the hybrid character of early British India — a military commander with the habits of a local magnate, raising troops from his own estates and integrated into the regional economy through his son's commercial ventures.
