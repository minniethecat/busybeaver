## Overview

Sir Henry Ashton is mentioned as one of Dorian Gray's former associates whose reputation was destroyed.

Lord Henry cites him as part of a pattern of ruined lives that follow in Dorian's wake.

## Biography

Lord Henry catalogues Sir Henry Ashton among Dorian's ruined friends:

> "There was Sir Henry Ashton, who had to leave England with a tarnished name."
>
> (012-019)

When Dorian protests, Lord Henry presses the question more pointedly:

> "You ask me about Henry Ashton and young Perth. Did I teach the one his vices, and the other his debauchery?"
>
> (012-020)

The scandal echoes to the novel's final chapter.

When Dorian's body is discovered disfigured in the attic, one of the men who sneers at the scene is Sir Henry Ashton's uncle (020-016).

The family's bitterness endures to the very end of the narrative.

## Relationships

Sir Henry Ashton was a close associate of [[dorian-gray]], whose influence appears to have precipitated his downfall.

The connection also concerns [[lord-henry-wotton]], who catalogues the pattern of ruined friends surrounding Dorian.
