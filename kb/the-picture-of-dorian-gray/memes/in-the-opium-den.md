## Summary

Unable to bear the weight of [[basil-hallward|Basil Hallward]]'s murder [[murder-of-basil-hallward]], [[dorian-gray|Dorian Gray]] [[dorian-gray]] flees to the [[opium-dens-where-one-could-buy-oblivion|opium dens]] of the London docks.

In a squalid den, he encounters ruined Adrian [[sin|Sin]]gleton and is nearly killed by [[james-vane|James Vane]] [[james-vane]].

[[dorian-gray|Dorian]] escapes because his supernatural youth makes James doubt his identity.

## Context

[[sin|Sin]]ce murdering [[basil-hallward|Basil]] [[basil-confronts-dorian]] and blackmailing Alan Campbell [[alan-campbell]], [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] finds no peace.

The [[the-portrait|portrait]] grows ever more hideous. Society distractions fail.

> "The hideous hunger for opium began to gnaw at him. His throat burned." (016-005)

## Detailed Account

On a foggy evening, [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] navigates the dark streets of the docklands.

> "There were opium dens where one could buy oblivion, dens of horror where the memory of old sins could be destroyed by the madness of sins that were new." (016-002)

The "heavy odour of opium met him" as he entered the den (016-015).

Among the patrons, he recognizes Adrian [[sin|Sin]]gleton—once a charming friend, now destroyed by [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s influence.

> "The hideous hunger for opium began to gnaw at him. His throat burned, and his delicate hands twitched nervously together." (016-005)

A woman calls [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] "[[dorian-gray|Prince Charming]]." [[james-vane|James Vane]] emerges from the shadows.

> "You are the devil in shape and form! For your sin, for as sure as I am James Vane, you are going to die." (016-052)

James presses a revolver to [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s head. [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] points out his impossible youth.

A man who ruined [[sibyl-vane|Sibyl]] eighteen years ago would be middle-aged. James hesitates. [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] flees into the fog.

## Aftermath

James learns the truth and resumes his hunt.

He appears at [[selby-royal|Selby Royal]] where he dies at a hunting party [[death-of-james-vane]].

[[dorian-gray|Dorian]] briefly resolves to reform—tested in his [[dorians-attempted-reform|attempted reform]].

## Thematic Significance

The opium den marks [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s lowest descent. He once sought beauty; now he seeks numbness.

Adrian [[sin|Sin]]gleton shows what [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] would be without supernatural protection.

The encounter with [[james-vane|James Vane]] externalizes [[conscience|guilt]] that opium cannot silence.

The avenger at the door is the past that cannot be outrun—the [[conscience]] that refuses to die.
