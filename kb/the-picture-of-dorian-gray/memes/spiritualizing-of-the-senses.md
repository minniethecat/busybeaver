## Quotation

> "He sought to elaborate some new scheme of life that would have its reasoned philosophy and its ordered principles, and find in the spiritualizing of the senses its highest realization." (011-008)

## Context

In Chapter 11, the narrator describes how [[dorian-gray|Dorian Gray]] was not content merely to be a fashionable figure — "a mere *arbiter elegantiarum*, to be consulted on the wearing of a jewel, or the knotting of a necktie, or the conduct of a cane." In his "inmost heart," he desired something more: to become a philosophical figure, someone who had not merely a style but a system.

This sentence articulates that system. [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] seeks a "reasoned philosophy" and "ordered principles" — the language of intellectual seriousness — but the content of that philosophy is the transformation of bodily experience into something [[soul|spirit]]ual. The "[[soul|spirit]]ualizing of the senses" is the key phrase: it is not the denial of the senses (asceticism) nor the indulgence of the senses (profligacy) but their *elevation* — making sensation itself a kind of worship.

## Analysis

The phrase "[[soul|spirit]]ualizing of the senses" is an oxymoron that functions as a program. In traditional Christian thought, the senses belong to the body, and the body is the enemy of the [[soul|spirit]]. To "[[soul|spirit]]ualize" the senses is to collapse this opposition — to insist that bodily experience can be a form of [[soul|spirit]]ual practice, that sensuality and [[soul|spirit]]uality are not opposites but possible allies.

The sentence's structure mirrors its content. It begins with intellectual seriousness ("reasoned philosophy," "ordered principles") and ends with sensual transcendence ("its highest realization"). The movement from reason to ecstasy, from order to [[soul|spirit]]ualization, is the movement [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s life attempts — and fails — to make.

The irony, which the novel unfolds across its remaining chapters, is that [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s attempt to spiritualize the senses leads not to transcendence but to degradation. The "new scheme of life" becomes a justification for cruelty, murder, and self-destruction. The senses, far from being [[soul|spirit]]ualized, become tyrannical. The project fails on its own terms: instead of elevating the senses to the status of [[soul|spirit]], it reduces the [[soul|spirit]] to the level of the senses.

## Themes

- [[aestheticism]]: [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s project is aestheticism made systematic — not just a taste for beauty but a philosophy of life grounded in sensation.
- [[new-hedonism]]: The sentence is the seed of the "[[pleasure|new Hedonism]]" that [[lord-henry-wotton|Lord Henry]] prophesied and [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] attempted to practice.
- [[art-and-life]]: The "[[soul|spirit]]ualizing of the senses" is the method by which [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] attempts to turn life into art.
