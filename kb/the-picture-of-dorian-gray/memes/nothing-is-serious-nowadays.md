## Quotation

> "Nothing is serious nowadays. At least nothing should be." (012-009)

## Context

It is the night of November 9th — the eve of [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s thirty-eighth birthday. [[dorian-gray|Dorian Gray]] is walking home from [[lord-henry-wotton|Lord Henry]]'s house when [[basil-hallward|Basil Hallward]] intercepts him on [[mayfair|Grosvenor Square]]. [[basil-hallward|Basil]] has come to confront [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] about the "most [[mind-of-the-well-informed-man|dreadful thing]]s" being said against him in London before departing for Paris (012-017). He asks to come in, explaining that he has "something to say" (012-006).

[[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s response is to deflect from the start. Before [[basil-hallward|Basil]] can even state his purpose, [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] issues a preemptive warning: "mind you don't talk about anything serious. Nothing is serious nowadays. At least nothing should be" (012-009). The irony is acute: [[basil-hallward|Basil]] has come to deliver the most serious conversation of the novel — the confrontation that will lead directly to his own murder.

## Analysis

The sentence is a masterpiece of dramatic irony and psychological evasion. [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s claim that "nothing is serious nowadays" is both a statement of his aesthetic philosophy and a desperate attempt to prevent the conversation that he knows is coming. The self-correction — "at least nothing should be" — betrays the gap between his philosophy and reality: things are serious, even if [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] wishes they were not.

The line functions as a shield. By declaring seriousness obsolete, [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] attempts to disarm [[basil-hallward|Basil]]'s moral authority before [[basil-hallward|Basil]] can wield it. If nothing is serious, then [[basil-hallward|Basil]]'s accusations — of [[sin|corruption]], of ruined reputations, of "[[mind-of-the-well-informed-man|dreadful thing]]s" — can be dismissed as the tedious moralizing of a man who has failed to keep up with the times.

But the sentence also reveals the fragility of [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s position. The fact that he must insist on the death of seriousness — that he must say it aloud, as a preemptive command — suggests that seriousness is very much alive. The [[the-portrait|portrait]] in the locked room upstairs is the proof: the most serious thing in [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s life, and the one thing he cannot speak of.

## Themes

- [[seriousness]]: The epigram attempts to banish seriousness from social discourse, treating it as a category error.
- [[evasion]]: The line is a rhetorical maneuver — a way of refusing the conversation [[basil-hallward|Basil]] has come to have.
- [[hypocrisy]]: [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s claim that nothing is serious is belied by the [[the-portrait|portrait]] he is about to reveal.
- [[aestheticism]]: The dismissal of seriousness is a logical extension of the aestheticist creed: if life is art, then nothing in life can have the weight of moral consequence.
