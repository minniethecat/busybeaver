## Overview

Lady Gwendolen is Lord Henry Wotton's sister and a figure in London society. She is first mentioned when Dorian Gray encounters her at the opera, where he joins her in her box. Dorian describes her as "perfectly charming" (009-003). By the time of Basil Hallward's final confrontation with Dorian in Chapter 12, Lady Gwendolen's social standing has been completely destroyed through her association with Dorian. Basil observes that "not a breath of scandal had ever touched her" before she met Dorian, but now "not a single decent woman in London would drive with her in the park" (012-023).


> "When you met Lady Gwendolen, not a breath of scandal had ever touched her. Is there a single decent woman in London now who would drive with her in the park? Why, even her children are not allowed to live with her. Then there are other stories—stories that you have been seen creeping at dawn out of dreadful houses and slinking in disguise into the foulest dens in London. Are they true? Can they be true?"
>
> (012-023)
