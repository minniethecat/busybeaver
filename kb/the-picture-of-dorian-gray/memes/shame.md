## Definition

Shame is the painful feeling of humiliation caused by the consciousness of wrong or foolish behaviour.

In the novel, shame is externalized in the transforming portrait, which becomes the physical repository for Dorian's moral degradation while his outward appearance remains untouched (008-015).

## The Portrait as Shame

After cruelly rejecting Sibyl Vane, Dorian first notices the change in the portrait and recognizes what it means:

> He looked upon the mask of his shame. Then he drew the screen aside and saw himself face to face.
>
> (008-015)

Dorian resolves to hide the portrait, accepting that it alone would "bear the burden of his shame" (008-074) while he remained free to pursue pleasure.

## Dorian's Torment

Dorian's relationship to shame is defined by concealment. As he contemplates his future, he reflects:

> The future should be so full of shame. Some love might come across his life and purify him... And yet the thing would still live on.
>
> (010-028)

He knows that no one but himself would ever see the evidence of his shame (010-036), yet the portrait's existence haunts him.
