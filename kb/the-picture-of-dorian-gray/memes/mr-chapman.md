## Overview

Mr. Chapman is a minor character who appears at Lady Narborough's dinner party in Chapter 15.

He is mentioned among the guests and participates in the evening's social interactions, including a notable moment when Dorian declines his sherry.

## Biography

Mr. Chapman is first noted among the assembled company at Lady Narborough's dinner, alongside Lady Alice Chapman, his hostess's daughter (015-005).

> The men laughed, and Mr. Chapman got up solemnly from the foot of the table.
>
> (015-053)

When sherry is offered later in the evening:

> "No sherry, thank you, Mr. Chapman. Something has happened to you."
>
> (015-072)

Dorian's refusal of the sherry is significant — it occurs in the context of his growing paranoia and unsettled state of mind.

His remark that "something has happened" to Chapman may reflect Dorian's own projection.

## Relationships

Mr. Chapman is a guest of [[lady-narborough]] and briefly interacts with [[dorian-gray]] during the dinner.

His presence contributes to the social texture of the chapter without advancing any particular plotline.
