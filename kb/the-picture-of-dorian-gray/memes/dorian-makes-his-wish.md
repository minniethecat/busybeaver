## Overview

In Chapter 2, [[dorian-gray|Dorian Gray]] makes the wish that determines the entire novel's course.

Gazing at [[basil-hallward|Basil Hallward]]'s finished [[the-portrait|portrait]], [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] realizes his own beauty for the first time. [[lord-henry-wotton|Lord Henry]]'s words about youth's transience echo in his mind.

He wishes the [[the-portrait|portrait]] would age instead of him. This wish — "I would give my soul for that!" (002-073) — becomes reality through the novel's implicit supernatural mechanism.

## Context

The scene takes place in [[basils-studio|Basil's studio]], immediately after [[lord-henry-wotton|Lord Henry]]'s speech on youth and beauty.

[[basil-hallward|Basil]] has just finished the [[the-portrait|portrait]]. [[lord-henry-wotton|Lord Henry]] persuades [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] to stay rather than go to the theatre.

Alone with [[lord-henry-wotton|Lord Henry]] in the garden, [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] absorbs the philosophy of [[pleasure|hedonism]] (002-022). When they return inside, he sees the [[the-portrait|portrait]] with transformed eyes.

## The Wish

Standing before the [[the-portrait|portrait]], [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] experiences a moment of painful self-awareness:

The sense of his own beauty came on him like a revelation (002-065). He had never felt it before. But with this awareness comes the realization that his beauty will fade.

"How sad it is! I shall grow old, and horrible, and dreadful. But this picture will remain always [[youth|young]]" (002-073). In despair, [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] offers his soul in ex[[transformation|change]] for eternal youth.

[[lord-henry-wotton|Lord Henry]] and [[basil-hallward|Basil]] witness the moment. [[basil-hallward|Basil]] tries to destroy the [[the-portrait|portrait]], but [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] stops him — the bargain is sealed.

## Immediate Aftermath

The [[the-portrait|portrait]] is kept at [[basil-hallward|Basil]]'s studio. But within days, [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] takes it to his own home.

When [[sibyl-vane|Sibyl Vane]] dies, [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] notices the first [[transformation|change]]: "a touch of cruelty in the mouth" (008-001). The supernatural mechanism is real. The [[the-portrait|portrait]] will record every sin.

## Participants

- [[dorian-gray]]: The wisher — a [[beauty|beautiful]] youth who trades his soul for eternal youth.

- [[lord-henry-wotton]]: The catalyst — his philosophy of beauty and youth plants the seed.

- [[basil-hallward]]: The artist — his masterpiece becomes the instrument of a Faustian bargain.

## Consequences

This single wish sets every subsequent event in motion. The [[the-portrait|portrait]] becomes [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s hidden conscience — a visual record of his [[sin|moral decay]].

Without this scene, there is no novel. It is the narrative's hinge point: the moment innocence becomes ambition, and beauty becomes a curse.
