## Overview

Lady Hamilton is invoked in Chapter 11 as a point of comparison in the description of one of Dorian Gray's ancestral portraits. The passage describes an ancestor's mother "with her Lady Hamilton face and her moist, wine-dashed lips" (011-032), evoking the celebrated beauty of Emma, Lady Hamilton (1765–1815), the famous mistress of Admiral Lord Nelson and a renowned subject of George Romney's portraits. The reference places Dorian's lineage within a tradition of scandalous beauty, suggesting that his own dual nature — outward charm concealing inward corruption — may have antecedents in his family history.
