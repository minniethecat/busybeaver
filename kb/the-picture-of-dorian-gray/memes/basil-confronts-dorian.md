## Summary

On the eve of his departure for Paris, [[basil-hallward|Basil Hallward]] [[basil-hallward]] visits [[dorian-gray|Dorian Gray]] [[dorian-gray]] to confront him.

[[basil-hallward|Basil]] insists on seeing [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s soul. [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] reveals the hideously transformed [[the-portrait|portrait]].

[[basil-hallward|Basil]] begs him to pray. [[dorian-gray|Dorian]], seized by uncontrollable hatred, stabs [[basil-hallward|Basil]] to death.

## Context

In the years since [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s wish [[dorian-makes-his-wish]], his beauty remains untouched while the [[the-portrait|portrait]] grows monstrous.

Whispers about [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s [[influence|corrupting influence]] follow him through London.

[[basil-hallward|Basil]], who has loved [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] since the day they met, cannot reconcile the rumours with the [[beauty|beautiful]] [[youth|young]] man.

He visits [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] on the night before leaving for Paris, determined to hear the truth.

## Detailed Account

[[basil-hallward|Basil]] arrives at [[mayfair|Dorian's house]] late at night. He tells [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] of the terrible rumours.

"The most [[mind-of-the-well-informed-man|dreadful thing]]s are being said against you in London" (012-032). He lists ruined reputations and corrupted innocents.

[[dorian-gray|Dorian]] listens with weary amusement. Finally, he offers to show [[basil-hallward|Basil]] the answer.

> "You have chattered enough about corruption. Now you shall look on it face to face." (013-012)

[[dorian-gray|Dorian]] takes [[basil-hallward|Basil]] upstairs to the old schoolroom. The [[the-portrait|portrait]] is hidden behind a purple curtain.

When he draws the curtain, [[basil-hallward|Basil]] recoils in horror.

> "What does this mean?" cried Basil, his own "soul" reflected in the painting. (008-028)

The [[the-portrait|portrait]] shows a hideous face—[[basil-hallward|Basil]]'s own work, transformed beyond recognition.

> "It is never too late, Dorian. Let us kneel down and try if we cannot remember a prayer." (013-046)

[[basil-hallward|Basil]] [[transformation|fall]]s to his knees, begging [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] to repent. For a moment, [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] is moved.

Then a hideous hatred surges through him. He seizes a knife and stabs [[basil-hallward|Basil]] repeatedly.

## Aftermath

[[dorian-gray|Dorian]] locks the schoolroom door and goes calmly downstairs.

He sends for [[alan-campbell]] and blackmails him into destroying the body—the event covered in [[alan-campbell-disposal]].

The [[the-portrait|portrait]] now bears blood on the painted feet and hands (014-007).

## Thematic Significance

[[basil-hallward|Basil]]'s confrontation is the novel's most direct collision between appearance and reality.

The artist created the [[the-portrait|portrait]] as an ideal of beauty; he returns to find it a record of evil.

The scene dramatizes the Wildean inversion: artwork has become the truest representation of the soul.

[[basil-hallward|Basil]]'s death marks the shift from psychological horror to actual crime—from sin to murder.
