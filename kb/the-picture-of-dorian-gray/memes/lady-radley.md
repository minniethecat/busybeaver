## Overview

Lady Radley is the wife of Lord Radley, who serves as one of Dorian Gray's guardians.

She is mentioned in passing across several chapters but never appears directly in a scene, functioning as part of the background texture of Dorian's aristocratic world.

## Biography

Lady Radley is first referenced when Dorian worries about his guardians' expected reaction to his engagement to Sibyl Vane:

> "I don't know what my guardians will say. Lord Radley is sure to be furious."
>
> (006-025)

Later, among Dorian's treasured possessions, a gift from her is noted:

> ...a present from Lady Radley, his guardian's wife, a pretty mother-of-nacre...
>
> (010-037)

A further society reference indicates that a character married Lady Radley's maid and established himself through that connection (012-012).

## Relationships

Lady Radley is married to [[lord-radley]], one of Dorian Gray's guardians.

Her connection to [[dorian-gray]] is established through the guardianship arrangement and her gift to him.
