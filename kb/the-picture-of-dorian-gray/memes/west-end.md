## Description

The West End is the affluent district of central London known for its theatres, exclusive clubs, and fashionable society.

In the novel, it appears as a marker of social ambition and respectability — the realm Dorian Gray aspires to conquer through Sibyl Vane's theatrical success (004-087), and the world that later judges and partially rejects him (011-029).

## Key Appearances

Dorian declares his intention to launch Sibyl Vane's career at a West End theatre after buying out her contract:

> I shall take a West End theatre and bring her out properly. She will make the world as mad as she has made me.
>
> (004-087)

Later, Dorian's reputation suffers when he is nearly blackballed from a West End club, and rumours circulate about his conduct:

> He was very nearly blackballed at a West End club of which his birth and social position fully entitled him to become a member.
>
> (011-029)
