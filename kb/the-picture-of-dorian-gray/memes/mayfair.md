## Description

Mayfair is London's most fashionable residential district, home to aristocrats, politicians, and the wealthy. (004-001) [[dorian-gray|Dorian Gray]]'s house is located here — a setting of tasteful luxury where he entertains society and maintains the impeccable [[reputation|public image]] that conceals his private life. The contrast between the elegant drawing rooms of Mayfair and the locked schoolroom where [[dorian-hides-the-portrait|the portrait is hidden]] is one of the novel's most effective spatial metaphors.

## Significance in the Novel

Mayfair represents the surface of [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s life — the respectable facade behind which his [[sin|corruption]] unfolds. It is where [[lord-henry-wotton|Lord Henry]] visits, where society gossip circulates, and where [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] plays the role of the cultured gentleman. The geography of the novel repeatedly contrasts Mayfair with London's darker districts (the East End, the [[opium-dens-where-one-could-buy-oblivion|opium dens]]), mapping [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s moral duality onto the city's physical space.

- **[[dorian-gray|Dorian Gray]]**: Mayfair is his stage — the setting for his public performance of [[reputation|respectability]]
- **[[lord-henry-wotton|Lord Henry Wotton]]**: A frequent visitor, comfortable in the aristocratic milieu

## Atmosphere / Symbolism

Mayfair symbolizes the Victorian cult of appearances — a world where surface is everything and the hidden room upstairs must never be opened.
