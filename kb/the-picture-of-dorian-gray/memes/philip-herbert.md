## Overview

Philip Herbert is one of the ancestral figures whose portrait hangs in the picture-gallery of Dorian Gray's country house at Selby Royal. In Chapter 11, as Dorian contemplates the lineage that flows in his veins, the narrative mentions "Philip Herbert, described by Francis Osborne, in his Memoires on the Reigns of Queen Elizabeth and King James, as a man who was 'caressed by the Court for his handsome face, which kept him not long company'" (011-032). He is part of the gallery of ancestors that Dorian studies obsessively during the years of his secret corruption, finding in their portraits a dark mirror of his own dual existence.
