## Summary

After murdering [[basil-hallward|Basil Hallward]] (Ch13), [[dorian-gray|Dorian Gray]] summons Alan Campbell — a brilliant [[youth|young]] chemist with whom he was once intimate but is now estranged. [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] blackmails Campbell with an unspoken secret from their shared past, compelling him to destroy [[basil-hallward|Basil]]'s body using chemical means. Campbell, trapped by the threat of exposure, performs the gruesome task in ex[[transformation|change]] for his freedom. Hours later, [[basil-hallward|Basil Hallward]] has vanished without trace — leaving only a "horrible odour of nitric acid" in the locked schoolroom.

## Context

[[dorian-gray|Dorian]] has just murdered [[basil-hallward|Basil Hallward]] in the schoolroom of his house on [[mayfair|Grosvenor Square]]. The body lies on the table, and [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s first thought is of practical disposal. He remembers Alan Campbell — a scientifically brilliant [[youth|young]] man, five years his junior, with whom he was once close. Their friendship ended abruptly, and Campbell now devotes himself entirely to chemistry. [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] knows a secret about Campbell — the nature of which Wilde never explicitly reveals — that gives him irresistible leverage.

## Detailed Account

### The Summons (Ch14)

[[dorian-gray|Dorian]] writes Campbell an urgent note: "Alan, I want you. Come at once." When Campbell arrives, he is reluctant and hostile — their friendship ended badly. [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] leads him upstairs to the schoolroom, locks the door behind them, and reveals a draped form on the table:

> Campbell gazed at him in silence. There was a look of contempt in his face... "You are mad, Dorian. What are you going to do to me?" (014-036)

[[dorian-gray|Dorian]] reveals that a dead body is under the covering and that Campbell must destroy it — chemically, completely, leaving no trace. Campbell refuses.

### The Blackmail

[[dorian-gray|Dorian]] writes a few lines on a piece of paper. Campbell reads it, turns "livid," and [[transformation|fall]]s back in his chair:

> A horrible sense of sickness came over him. He could not utter a word. (014-040)

The nature of the secret is never stated, but its power over Campbell is absolute. [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] says only: "I am sorry for you, Alan. But you leave me no alternative. I have a letter written already." The threat of sending it — to whom, about what, we never learn — compels Campbell to comply.

### The Disposal

Campbell orders his assistant to deliver chemicals, a chest, and equipment from his laboratory. The materials arrive: nitric acid, irons, and other apparatus. Campbell works through the afternoon in the locked schoolroom while [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] waits downstairs:

> He began to wonder if he and Basil Hallward had ever met, and, if so, what they had thought of each other. (014-057)

When [[dorian-gray|Dorian]] finally returns upstairs hours later, [[basil-hallward|Basil Hallward]] is gone. "There was nothing left of the dead man but a handful of salt." The schoolroom reeks of nitric acid.

## Aftermath / Consequences

1. **Perfect Disappearance**: [[basil-hallward|Basil Hallward]] vanishes without trace — no body, no evidence, no murder investigation that can touch [[dorian-gray|Dorian]].
2. **Campbell's Suicide**: Months later, Alan Campbell shoots himself in his laboratory — another death on [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s conscience.
3. **[[dorian-gray|Dorian]]'s Impunity**: The successful disposal emboldens [[dorian-gray|Dorian]]; he feels invincible for the next several years.
