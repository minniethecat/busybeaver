# Article 52: The function of the passions, and how to list them

[052-001] The passions that are aroused in us by the objects that stimulate the senses aren’t different for every difference among the objects, but only corresponding to differences in how the objects can harm or benefit us, or more generally have importance for us. What the passions do for us consists solely in this:

[052-002] • they dispose our soul to want the things that nature decides are useful to us, and to persist in this volition; and

[052-003] •the agitation of the spirits that normally causes the passions also disposes the body to move in ways that help to bring about those ·useful· things.

[052-004] That’s why a list of the passions requires only an orderly examination of all the various ways—ways that are important to us—in which our senses can be stimulated by their objects. Now I shall list all the principal passions according to the order in which they can thus be found.
