# Article 45: The soul’s power with respect to its passions

[045-001] Our passions can’t be aroused or suppressed directly by the action of our will, but only indirectly by our representing to ourselves things that are usually joined with the passion we want to have or opposed to the one we want to fend off. To arouse boldness and suppress fear in ourselves, it’s not enough to have a volition to do so. We have to set ourselves to think about the reasons, objects, or precedents which argue that •the danger isn’t great, that •there’s always more security in defence than in flight, that •we’ll gain glory and joy if we conquer, and nothing but regret and shame if we flee—things like that.
