# Article 125: Why laughter doesn’t accompany the greatest joys

[125-001] Although laughter might seem to be one of the chief signs of joy, joy can’t cause laughter except when it is moderate and mixed with an element of wonder or hatred. We find by experience that when we are unusually joyful, the thing we are joyful about never makes us burst out laughing, and other things don’t draw us into laughter ·at those times of great joy· as easily as they do when we are sad. The reason for this is that in great joys the lungs are always so full of blood that they can’t be swollen any more by renewed surges of it.
