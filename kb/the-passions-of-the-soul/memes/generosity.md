# Generosity (Générosité)

The virtue of **generosity** (*générosité*) is the cornerstone of [[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' ethical philosophy, presented in Part III of *The [[the-passions-of-the-soul|Passions of the Soul]]*. It is both a passion and a virtue, bridging the divide between the body's mechanical responses and the soul's free rational activity.

## Definition

[[rene-descartes|Descartes]] defines true generosity as consisting of two components:

1. **Knowledge**: Knowing that nothing truly belongs to a person except the free control of their volitions, and that the good or bad use of this freedom is the only valid reason for praise or blame. [[153-001]]
2. **Resolution**: Feeling within oneself a firm and constant resolution to use one's freedom well — never lacking the will to undertake and carry out whatever one judges to be best. [[153-001]]

> True generosity, which brings it about that a person's self-esteem is as great as it legitimately can be, consists only in (i) his knowing that nothing truly belongs to him except this free control of his volitions, and (ii) his feeling within himself a firm and constant resolution to use his freedom well. — Article 153

## Key Properties

### Relation to Self and Others

Generous people do not contemn others. Because their self-knowledge does not depend on anything external, they easily believe that others can possess the same knowledge and feeling. When they see others acting wrongly, they are more inclined to excuse than to blame, attributing errors to lack of knowledge rather than lack of virtuous will. [[154-001]]

### Humility as a Virtue

The most generous people are usually also the most humble. Humility as a virtue arises from reflecting on the infirmity of human nature and on one's own potential for wrongdoing — wrongs no less serious than those of others. Since all possess free will equally, no one should rate themselves above others. [[155-001]]

### Remedy for the Passions

Generosity serves as a sovereign remedy for all the disorders of the [[passion|passions]]. Those who possess it:

- Are naturally led to do great things without overreaching [[156-001]]
- Value doing good to others above self-interest [[156-001]]
- Have complete command over their [[passion|passions]], especially des[[anger|ire]]s, jealousy, and envy [[156-001]]
- Are perfectly courteous, gracious, and obliging to everyone [[156-001]]

## How Generosity Can Be Acquired

Generosity can be cultivated through good education and habituation. What are commonly called "virtues" are habits of the soul that dispose it to have certain thoughts. These thoughts can be produced by the soul alone, but they are often strengthened by movements of the [[animal-spirits|animal spirit]]s — in which case they are both *actions of virtue* and *[[passion|passions]] of the soul*. [[161-001]]

[[rene-descartes|Descartes]] emphasizes that everyone is capable of acquiring generosity, because the free will that grounds it is equally present in all human beings.

## Contrast with Vices

| Virtue | Corresponding Vice |
|--------|-------------------|
| Generosity | Vanity / Vainglory |
| Humility (virtuous) | Abjectness / Servility |

Vanity and abjectness are not only vices but also [[passion|passions]], because their commotion is quite apparent in the external demeanour of those suddenly elated or depressed. [[160-001]]
