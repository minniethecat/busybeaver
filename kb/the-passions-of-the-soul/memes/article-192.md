# Article 192: Approval

[192-001] Approval, properly speaking, is a desire to see good come to someone for whom we have good will; but I am here using ‘approval’ ·in a narrower sense·, to mean this good will when it is aroused in us by some good action of the person in question (we’re naturally inclined to love those who do things we judge to be good even if we get no benefit from them). Approval in this sense is a kind of love, not of desire, though it is always accompanied by the desire to see good come to the one of whom we approve. And it is usually combined with pity, because the reverses that we see unfortunate people suffer make us reflect all the more on their merits.
