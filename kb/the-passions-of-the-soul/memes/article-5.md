# Article 5: It is an error to believe that the soul gives movement and heat to the body

[005-001] This will enable us to avoid a very serious error that many have fallen into—I reckon that it’s the primary cause of our failure to give a good account of the passions or of anything else belonging to the soul. The error goes like this:

[005-002] ‘Since dead bodies don’t have any heat or motion, it is the absence of the soul that causes them to be cold and motionless.’

[005-003] Thus it has been wrongly believed that our natural heat and all our bodily movements depend on the soul; whereas we ought to hold that the dependence goes the other way—the soul leaves our body when we die only because this heat ceases and the organs that move the body decay.
