## 定义

**Indignation**（愤慨、义愤）是笛卡尔激情分类体系中的一种**厌恶（[[desire|aversion]]）/ 仇恨（hatred）的特殊形态**——当他人做出**不涉及自身**的恶行时，灵魂产生的道德义愤。笛卡尔严格区分 indignation 与 anger（[[anger|愤怒]]）：indignation 针对他人遭受的不公，anger 针对自身遭受的伤害。

> Similarly, when others do something bad that doesn't relate to us in any way, that brings it about that we feel indignation—nothing else—towards them; and when what they have done is bad for us, that arouses anger as well. [[065-001]]

这一区分是笛卡尔激情伦理学的基石：indignation 是 moral passion（道德激情），而 anger 是 self-regarding passion（自利激情）。

## 笛卡尔的论述

### 作为激情复合体

笛卡尔在 [[127-001]] 中分析了 indignation 的构成要素——它不是单一激情，而是三种激情的复合：

> So that joy, hatred and wonder all contribute to indignation. [[127-001]]

- **Joy（喜悦）**：认识到自己不会受此恶行之害
- **Hatred（仇恨）**：对恶行本身的厌恶
- **Wonder（惊奇）**：恶行的出现违背了"人们理应按善行事"的预设

> We usually assume that everything will be done as we think it ought to be, i.e. done in the way we consider good. So when it happens otherwise we are surprised, and we wonder at it. [[197-001]]

### 与 laughter（笑）的关系

indignation 有时伴随笑声，笛卡尔区分了两种笑：

- **人为的笑**：通常是被造作出来的 [[127-001]]
- **自然的笑**：来自"看到自己不会受害"的喜悦 + "恶行之新奇"的惊奇

> But when it is natural, it seems to result from the joy we get from seeing that we can't be harmed by the evil that we are indignant about, and from finding ourselves surprised by the novelty of the evil or by our unexpected encounter with it. [[127-001]]

### 与 pity 和 derision 的差异化结合

面对恶行时，不同性情的人将 indignation 与不同的激情结合：

> some people combine pity to their indignation, and others derision, depending on whether they bear good-will or ill-will towards those whom they see acting wrongly. [[196-001]]

- **善意者** → indignation + pity（[[pity|怜悯]]）：如赫拉克利特之泪
- **恶意者** → indignation + derision（嘲弄）：如德谟克利特之笑

### 道德心理学：外表 vs 实质

笛卡尔对 indignation 的道德心理学有深刻洞见——它更常见于**想要显得有德**的人，而非**真正有德**的人：

> Indignation shows up much more in people who want to appear virtuous than in ones who really are. Those who love virtue can't see the vices of others without some aversion, but they become impassioned only about the greatest and most extraordinary vices. [[198-001]]

真正的有德者只对最严重、最罕见的恶行产生强烈的 indignation。

### 神学界限

笛卡尔为 indignation 划定了明确的神学边界：

> To direct one's indignation not only to the actions of human beings but also to the works of God or of nature is impertinent and absurd. [[198-004]]

indignation 只适用于人类行为的道德判断，不可僭越至神圣或自然领域。

## 与其他概念的关系

### Indignation vs Anger（愤怒）

| 维度 | Indignation | Anger |
|------|-----------|-------|
| 对象 | 他人受到的恶 | 自身受到的恶 |
| 动机 | 道德义愤 | 自保与复仇欲望 |
| 强度 | 温和 | 极猛烈 |
| 身体反应 | 鼻/唇运动 | 额头皱纹 + 血液沸腾 |

> Anger is also a kind of hatred or aversion that we have towards those who have done (or tried to do) harm not just to someone but to us in particular. Thus it contains everything that indignation contains as well as an extra feature: anger is based on an action that affects us and for which we have a desire to avenge ourselves. [[199-001]]

### Indignation ←→ Approval（赞许）

笛卡尔建立了对称概念对：

- **Indignation** ←→ **Approval**（赞许）：针对他人行为的道德判断
- **Anger** ←→ **Gratitude**（感恩）：针对涉及自身的行为反应

> anger ... is directly opposed to gratitude, as indignation is to approval. [[199-001]]

### Indignation 与 Generosity（慷慨）

在笛卡尔的伦理学体系中，[[generosity]]（慷慨）是管理[[anger|愤怒]]的最佳解药。慷慨之人将 indignation 作为 anger 的上限替代：

> Thus it brings it about that if we are generous we limit ourselves to contempt, or at the most to indignation, for the wrongs that others would ordinarily be offended by. [[203-001]]

这意味着：面对冒犯，慷慨者以 indignation（而非 anger）回应——将个人伤害转化为道德义愤，从而保持灵魂的自主与平静。

## 后世影响与解释

笛卡尔的 indignation 概念对后世有重要影响：

1. **斯宾诺莎《伦理学》**：将 indignation 定义为"对他人之恶的仇恨"（Ethics III, Prop. 22, Scholium），基本沿用笛卡尔框架
2. **18 世纪道德情感主义**：Adam Smith 的"resentment"（愤慨）与笛卡尔的 indignation 有结构相似性，均为 moral spectator 对不义的回应
3. **当代道德心理学**：笛卡尔对"indignation vs anger"的区分预示了现代研究中"moral outrage vs personal anger"的分野
