# Article 178: Derision

[178-001] Ridicule or derision is a kind of joy mixed with hatred [see Glossary], which results from our perceiving some small misfortune in a person who we think deserves it: we hate this misfortune, but enjoy seeing it come to someone who deserves it. When this comes upon us unexpectedly, the surprise of wonder causes us to burst into laughter, in accordance with what I said ·in article 126· about the nature of laughter. But the misfortune must be small; if it is great, we can’t believe that the person who has it deserves it, unless we were born mean or hate him very much.
