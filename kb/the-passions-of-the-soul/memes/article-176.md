# Article 176: The function of fear

[176-001] As for fear or terror, I don’t see that it can ever be praiseworthy or useful. So it’s not a particular passion, but merely an excess of shrinking reluctance, wonder and anxiety—an excess that is always unvirtuous, just as boldness is an excess of courage that is always good (provided the end proposed is good). Because the principal cause of fear is surprise, there’s no better way to avoid it than to think ahead and prepare oneself for any eventuality that one might fear.
