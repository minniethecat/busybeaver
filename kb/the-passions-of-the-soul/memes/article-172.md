# Article 172: Emulation

[172-001] Emulation is also a kind of courage, but in another sense. Courage can be regarded as a genus that divides into

[172-002] (1) as many species as it has different objects, and into

[172-003] (2) as many species as it has causes.

[172-004] Boldness is one of the (1) species, and emulation is one of the (2) species. Emulation is nothing but an energy that disposes the soul to tackle things in which it hopes to be able to succeed because it sees others succeed in them; so it is a species of courage the external cause of which is the example ·or model that the person is emulating·. I specify external cause because there must always also be an internal cause, that consists in the body’s being so disposed that desire and hope have more power to send a lot of blood to the heart than anxiety or despair have to stop it.
