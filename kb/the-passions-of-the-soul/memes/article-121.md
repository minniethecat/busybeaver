# Article 121: Listlessness can also be caused by other passions

[121-001] It is true that hatred, sadness and even joy may also cause some listlessness when it is very violent, because it makes the soul wholly engrossed in thinking about its object; this happens chiefly when these passions are combined with the desire for something that we can’t do anything to acquire at the present time. But listlessness is encountered much more in love than in all the other passions, because •we stop to think about objects to which we join ourselves de volonté [see Glossary] much more than we do to think about objects from which we separate ourselves, or about any other objects, and because •listlessness doesn’t depend on surprise but requires some time to develop.
