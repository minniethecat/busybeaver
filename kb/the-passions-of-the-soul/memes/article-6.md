# Article 6: How a living body differs from a dead one

[006-001] To avoid this error, let us note that death is never due to •the absence of the soul but only to •the decay of some principal part of the body. And let us recognize that

[006-002] the body of a living man

[006-003] differs from

[006-004] the body of a dead man

[006-005] in just the same way that

[006-006] a watch or other automaton (i.e. self-moving machine) when it is wound up and contains within itself the physical source of the movements for which it is designed, together with everything else needed for its operation

[006-007] differs from

[006-008] the same watch or machine when it is broken and the source of its movement has stopped working.
