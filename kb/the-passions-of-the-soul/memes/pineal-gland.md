# Pineal Gland (Glande Pinéale)

The **pineal gland** (*la glande pinéale*, also called the *conarion*) occupies a singular position in [[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' psycho-physiology: it is the **principal seat of the soul** — the one point in the body where the immaterial [[soul]] acts d[[anger|ire]]ctly on the material [[animal-spirits]], and where the [[animal-spirits|spirits]] in turn impress the [[passion|passions]] upon the soul.

## 定义

[[rene-descartes|Descartes]] identifies the gland in Article 31:

> It is ... a certain very small gland deep inside the brain, in a place where all the cavities containing the animal spirits meet. It hangs in the middle of these cavities, suspended in such a way that the slightest movements of the spirits can move it and it can in turn redirect the spirits. [[031-003]]

The gland's unique anatomical position — suspended at the convergence of the brain's ventricles, the only unpa[[anger|ire]]d structure in an otherwise bilaterally symmetrical organ — is the physical basis for its metaphysical role.

> What convinces me that this gland is the only place in the body where the soul can exercise its functions in this way is ... that all the other parts of our brain are double, as are all the organs of our external senses. [[032-001]]

## 笛卡尔的论述

### 灵魂的居所

In Article 34, [[rene-descartes|Descartes]] consolidates his argument that the pineal gland is the "principal seat":

> The little gland that is the principal seat of the soul is suspended among the cavities that contain the animal spirits, so that it can be moved by them in as many different ways as there are sensible differences in the objects they represent. [[034-004]]

The gland's role is not merely passive. The soul, through its volitions, can also move the gland:

> The will ... brings it about that the gland leans in various directions, thereby turning the spirits towards different pores of the brain. [[042-001]]

### 双向交互机制

The pineal gland is the site of a two-way causal interaction:

1. **Body → Soul**: Sensory stimuli agitate the [[animal-spirits|animal spirit]]s, which move the gland in characteristic patterns. Each pattern corresponds to a specific passion — fear, [[love]], wonder, etc.:
   > The image formed on the gland proceeds from there to radiate towards all the other parts. [[036-001]]

2. **Soul → Body**: The soul's volitions red[[anger|ire]]ct the gland, altering the flow of [[animal-spirits|spirits]] into the nerves:
   > The soul ... brings it about that the gland moves in the way needed to drive the spirits towards the pores whose opening can bring about this movement. [[043-001]]

### 个体差异与习惯

The connection between specific gland movements and specific [[passion|passions]] is not ent[[anger|ire]]ly innate. In Article 39, [[rene-descartes|Descartes]] notes:

> A terrifying object makes on the gland a movement that causes fear in some men but not in others. This is because the brains of different men are not all disposed in the same way, and the same movement of the gland that in some men arouses fear may in others arouse courage. [[039-001]]

This plasticity allows for the habituation and training of the [[passion|passions]] — the therapeutic goal of [[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' ent[[anger|ire]] project.

### 与心脏的关系

Even [[passion|passions]] that seem to arise from the heart (like fear's racing pulse) are actually mediated through the gland:

> The heat in the heart suffices to make the gland move in a way that sends fear to the soul. [[038-001]]

## 与其他概念的关系

- **[[soul]]** — The pineal gland is the soul's唯一 point of physical contact with the body; the soul exercises all its bodily functions through this organ (Articles 31–34).

- **[[animal-spirits]]** — The gland is suspended in the cavities containing [[animal-spirits|animal spirit]]s; the [[animal-spirits|spirits]] move the gland, and the gland red[[anger|ire]]cts the [[animal-spirits|spirits]].

- **[[passion]]** — Every passion is ultimately caused by a specific movement of the pineal gland, produced by the agitation of the [[animal-spirits|animal spirit]]s (Article 34).

- **[[free-will]]** — The will acts on the body by moving the pineal gland; this is the mechanism through which the soul exercises its freedom.

## 后世影响与解释

[[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' choice of the pineal gland was empirically motivated — it was the only known unpa[[anger|ire]]d brain structure — but the deeper philosophical claim (that [[soul|mind]]-body interaction requ[[anger|ire]]s a single, unified interface point) has proven remarkably durable.

Modern neuroscience rejects the pineal gland as the seat of consciousness but continues to grapple with [[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' "binding problem": how unified conscious experience arises from distributed neural processing. The Cartesian pineal gland is thus both a superseded anatomical hypothesis and an enduring philosophical metaphor.
