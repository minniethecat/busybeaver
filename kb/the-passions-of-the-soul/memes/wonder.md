# Wonder (L'Admiration)

**Wonder** (*l'admiration*) is the first and most epistemologically significant of the six primitive [[passion|passions]] in [[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' *The [[the-passions-of-the-soul|Passions of the Soul]]*. Unlike the other [[passion|passions]] — which move the soul toward or away from objects judged good or evil — wonder is concerned solely with **novelty** and **knowledge**, making it the passion most d[[anger|ire]]ctly tied to the intellect.

## 定义

[[rene-descartes|Descartes]] defines wonder through its distinctive object — the unusual — and its distinctive bodily effect — the complete focus of attention:

> When we first encounter an object that surprises us, and we judge it to be new, or very different from anything we previously knew or from what we supposed it should be, this brings it about that we wonder and are astonished at it. [[053-001]]

The crucial point is that wonder has no opposite:

> Wonder has no contrary, because if an object has nothing in it that surprises us, we are not moved by it at all and we regard it without any passion. [[053-001]]

## 笛卡尔的论述

### 生理机制

Wonder is unique among the [[passion|passions]] in having no accompanying change in the heart or blood:

> This passion has the particularity that we don't notice it to be accompanied by any change in the heart or in the blood, as occurs with the other passions. The reason for this is that wondering at x doesn't involve any view of x as good or evil — it involves only knowledge of the thing wondered at. [[071-001]]

Because wonder has no cardiac component, the brain can concentrate its cognitive resources ent[[anger|ire]]ly on the object:

> The impression of the object of wonder has so much power that it sometimes occupies the brain so forcefully that the animal spirits are all directed towards it. [[073-001]]

### 认知功能

The special usefulness of wonder is its role in learning:

> The special usefulness of wonder lies in its getting us to learn and retain in our memory things that we had previously been ignorant of. [[075-001]]

However, [[rene-descartes|Descartes]] warns against excessive wonder:

> Excessive wondering — wondering too much — is much commoner than wondering too little. Excessive wonder — which is mere astonishment, not going beyond surprise — can be a great obstacle to knowledge. [[076-001]]

### 矫正与习惯

The cure for excessive wonder is not the suppression of the passion but its habituation through exposure:

> The more we encounter unusual things that we wonder at, the more accustomed we become to refraining from wondering too much at them. [[078-001]]

This aligns with [[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' broader therapeutic program: [[passion|passions]] are not to be eliminated but trained through practice.

### 与其他激情的复合

Wonder readily combines with other [[passion|passions]]. In Article 125, [[rene-descartes|Descartes]] analyses the passion of esteem as a compound:

> Esteem ... is an inclination of the soul to represent to itself the value of the thing esteemed. This inclination is caused by a special movement of the animal spirits, modified by wonder and by love or by joy. [[125-001]]

Joy combined with the "surprise of wonder" produces a particularly intense delight — the pleasure of discovering something new and good simultaneously. [[126-001]]

## 与其他概念的关系

- **[[passion]]** — Wonder is the first of the six primitive [[passion|passions]]; it serves as the template for [[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' method of analysing each passion "separately" (Article 96).

- **[[love]] / [[hatred]] / [[desire]] / [[joy]] / [[sadness]]** — The other five primitive [[passion|passions]]; wonder is unique among them in having no cardiac component and no contrary.

- **[[soul]]** — Wonder is a [[passion|passion of the soul]], caused solely by the movement of [[animal-spirits]] in the brain without any involvement of the heart.

- **[[animal-spirits]]** — In wonder, all the [[animal-spirits|animal spirit]]s are d[[anger|ire]]cted toward the brain region holding the impression of the novel object, intensifying attention.

## 后世影响与解释

[[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' treatment of wonder as the "first passion" — a cognitively oriented affect that precedes and enables all evaluative [[passion|passions]] — has been taken up by contemporary philosophers of emotion (e.g., [[rene-descartes|Descartes]] scholar Deborah Brown) and cognitive scientists studying curiosity and surprise.

The idea that wonder involves heightened attention without approach/avoidance motivation anticipates modern distinctions between epistemic emotions (curiosity, surprise, confusion) and valenced emotions (fear, joy, anger).
