# Joy (La Joie)

**Joy** (*la joie*) is one of the six primitive [[passion|passions]] in [[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' *The [[the-passions-of-the-soul|Passions of the Soul]]*. It is the passion of **enjoyment** — the soul's pleasurable commotion upon representing a present good as its own. Together with [[sadness]], it forms the hedonic axis around which all other [[passion|passions]] are organized.

## 定义

[[rene-descartes|Descartes]] defines joy through its object — a present good — in Article 61:

> The thought of a present good arouses joy in us, and the thought of a present evil arouses sadness, when the good or evil is one that is represented as belonging to us. [[061-001]]

The critical qualifier is "represented as belonging to us": joy is not triggered by any good whatsoever, but specifically by goods we take to be *ours* — our achievements, our possessions, our [[love]]d ones' wellbeing.

## 笛卡尔的论述

### 理智的喜悦与激情的喜悦

[[rene-descartes|Descartes]] makes a crucial distinction between two forms of joy:

> There is also an intellectual joy which isn't itself a passion but which almost always accompanies the passion. This intellectual joy is the soul's own proper pleasure, arising from the mere consideration of a good that the understanding represents to it as its own. [[091-002]][[091-004]]

Intellectual joy arises from the soul's own rational activity — the pleasure of understanding, of reasoning well, of contemplating truth. [[passion|Passion]]al joy arises from the body's movement of the [[animal-spirits|animal spirit]]s. But in practice, the two almost always occur together:

> This intellectual joy can hardly fail to be accompanied by the passion of joy, because as soon as the understanding perceives a good as its own, the animal spirits flow to the brain and there excite the passion. [[091-005]]

### 与爱的区别

Joy is often conflated with [[love]], but [[rene-descartes|Descartes]] carefully distinguishes them:

- **Love** joins us to a good (whether possessed or not).
- **Joy** is the enjoyment of a good we already possess.

We can [[love]] something without joy (if it is absent) and feel joy without [[love]] (if we enjoy a good we do not [[love]]). However, the two most commonly occur together: we [[love]] what brings us joy.

### 生理表现

Joy has the most benign physiological profile of all the [[passion|passions]]:

> In joy the pulse is even and regular, but quicker and fuller than usual; we feel a pleasant warmth that isn't just in the chest but also spreads through all the external parts of the body; and we lose our appetite because the digestion is already working less than usual. [[097-001]]

This pattern — even pulse, diffuse warmth, reduced [[desire|appetite]] — reflects joy's nature as an expansive, non-agitating passion. Unlike [[desire]] (which tenses the body for pursuit) or [[hatred]] (which contracts it for rejection), joy simply enjoys.

### 笑与欢乐

[[rene-descartes|Descartes]] provides a subtle physiological analysis of laughter as an expression of joy mixed with surprise:

> Laughter consists in the fact that the blood coming from the right cavity of the heart through the arterial vein, suddenly and repeatedly inflating the lungs... [[124-001]] (paraphrase of the laughter mechanism)

Joy expressed as laughter is a compound passion: joy at a present good, mixed with [[wonder]] (surprise) at its unexpectedness.

## 与其他概念的关系

- **[[sadness]]** — Joy's d[[anger|ire]]ct contrary; the two are the hedonic poles of [[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' passion system.

- **[[love]]** — Joy is the enjoyment of a possessed good; [[love]] is the joining to a good whether possessed or not.

- **[[desire]]** — Joy is the satisfaction of des[[anger|ire]]; des[[anger|ire]] is the anticipation of joy.

- **[[hatred]]** — Joy can co-occur with hatred when we rejoice at an enemy's misfortune.

- **[[wonder]]** — Joy compounded with the surprise of wonder produces delight — the most intense form of joy.

- **[[generosity]]** — The generous person feels joy primarily from the exercise of free will, a form of intellectual joy.

## 后世影响与解释

[[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' distinction between intellectual and passional joy anticipated Spinoza's three kinds of joy in the *Ethics* (passional joy → adequate ideas → intellectual [[love]] of God) and influenced Malebranche's account of the soul's proper pleasure in God.

The contemporary distinction between "happiness" (a long-term evaluative state) and "joy" (an occurrent emotion) owes something to [[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' careful separation of the intellectual from the passional.
