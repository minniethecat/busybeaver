# Article 70: Wonder: its definition and cause

[070-001] Wonder is a sudden surprise of the soul that brings it to focus on things that strike it as unusual and extraordinary. It is caused

[070-002] (1) by an impression in the brain, which represents the object as unusual and therefore worthy of special consideration; and

[070-003] (2) a movement of the spirits, which the impression disposes •to flow strongly to the impression’s place in the brain so as to strengthen and preserve it there, and also •to flow into the muscles controlling the sense organs so as to keep them focussed on the object of the wonder.
