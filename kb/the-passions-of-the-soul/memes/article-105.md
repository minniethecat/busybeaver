# Article 105: ...in sadness

[105-001] In sadness, on the other hand, the openings into the heart are tightly contracted by the small nerve surrounding them, and the blood in the veins isn’t agitated at all; so very little of that blood goes to the heart. Meanwhile the passages through which alimentary juice flows from the stomach and intestines to the liver remain open; so the appetite doesn’t diminish except when hatred, which is often joined to sadness, closes these passages.
