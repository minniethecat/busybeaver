# Article 29: Explanation of the remainder of the definition

[029-001] I add that they relate particularly to the soul so as to distinguish them from sensations that relate to external objects (e.g. smells, sounds and colours) or to our body (e.g. hunger, thirst and pain). I also add that they are caused, maintained and strengthened by some movement of the spirits so as to •distinguish them from our volitions (which can also be called ‘commotions of the soul that relate to it’, but are caused by the soul itself), and so as to •explain their last and most immediate cause—·the last link in the causal chain leading to them·—-which again distinguishes them from other sensations.
