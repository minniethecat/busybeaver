# Article 181: The function of laughter in teasing

[181-001] It isn’t bad-mannered to laugh when we hear someone else’s teasing; indeed it may be churlish not to laugh. But when someone is teasing, it is more fitting for him to refrain from laughing, so as not to seem surprised by what he is saying or impressed by his wit in thinking it up. His teasing will have more effect on the hearers if he keeps a straight face.
