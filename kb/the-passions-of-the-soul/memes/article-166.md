# Article 166: Confidence and despair

[166-001] And neither of these passions ever accompanies desire without leaving some room for the other. For when hope is so strong that it entirely drives out anxiety, its nature changes and it is called ‘confidence’ or ‘assurance’. And when we are sure that what we desire will come about, then although we still want it to come about we’re no longer agitated by the passion of desire, which made us await the outcome with concern. Similarly on the other side: when anxiety is so extreme that it leaves no room for hope, it changes into despair; and this despair by representing the thing desired as impossible entirely extinguishes desire, which applies only to things that are possible.
