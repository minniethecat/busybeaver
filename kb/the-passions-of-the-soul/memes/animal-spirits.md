# Animal Spirits (Esprits Animaux)

The **animal spirits** (*les esprits animaux*) are the most fundamental physiological concept in [[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' theory of the [[passion|passions]]. They function as the material intermediary between the immaterial [[soul]] and the mechanical [[pineal-gland]] — a fluid medium through which the soul moves the body and the body stirs the soul's [[passion|passions]].

## 定义

[[rene-descartes|Descartes]] introduces the animal spirits in Article 7 by analogy with a "very fine wind":

> What is most noteworthy here is that all the most animated and most subtle parts of the blood that the heat rarefies in the heart enter the brain cavities continuously and in large amounts. The reason they go there rather than elsewhere is that all the blood that leaves the heart through the great artery goes straight up towards the brain, ...

where it forms a certain very fine wind, or rather a very [[soul|anima]]ted and very pure flame, which is called the animal spirits. [[007-001]]

In the Glossary of Article 212, a more precise definition is given:

> This stuff was supposed to be the most rarefied and agitated parts of the blood, which serve to carry movements from the brain to the muscles and from the sense organs back to the brain. [[212-003]]

## 笛卡尔的论述

### 生成机制

The animal spirits are produced through a mechanical — not vitalistic — process. In Article 10, [[rene-descartes|Descartes]] describes their formation:

> The most animated and most subtle parts of the blood constitute the animal spirits. All that is needed for them to be this is that they be separated from the less subtle parts. [[010-001]]

This separation is purely physical: the heat in the heart rarefies the blood, and the finest particles are filtered through the arteries into the brain cavities. No special "soul power" or occult faculty is requ[[anger|ire]]d — the spirits are generated by the same mechanical laws that govern all matter.

### 运动传导

The animal spirits serve as the transmission mechanism between brain and body:

> What needs to be known is that the continuous flow of animal spirits from the brain to any muscle produces a tiny swelling in that muscle, which tightens it and pulls the limb to which it is attached. [[011-001]]

This flow is d[[anger|ire]]cted through tubular nerves — the spirits enter specific nerve channels and, by their mere physical pressure, cause muscles to contract:

> The animal spirits that are carried by these tubes do no more than produce a certain motion in the muscles by their passage. [[012-004]]

### 多样性与运动控制

The variety of bodily movements corresponds d[[anger|ire]]ctly to the variety in the spirits' flow:

> The cause of variety in the flow of the animal spirits to the muscles is the variety of the movements by which the brain brings it about that different spirits enter different muscles. [[014-001]]

[[rene-descartes|Descartes]] compares this system to the grottoes and fountains of the royal gardens at Saint-Germain, where water flowing through hidden pipes [[soul|anima]]tes statues and produces music — a purely mechanical model of what had previously been attributed to "vegetative" or "sensitive" souls.

### 灵魂与身体的交互中介

The animal spirits are the only physical link between the soul (at the [[pineal-gland]]) and the body:

> The last and most immediate cause of the passions of the soul is simply the agitation by which the animal spirits move the little gland in the middle of the brain. [[034-002]]

When the soul wills a movement, it acts on the pineal gland, which red[[anger|ire]]cts the flow of animal spirits into the appropriate nerves. Conversely, when sensory stimuli or bodily states agitate the spirits, their movement of the pineal gland produces a passion in the soul.

> The soul is really joined to the whole body, and ... it exercises its functions immediately by means solely of the animal spirits, the nerves, and even the blood. [[034-001]]

## 与其他概念的关系

- **[[passion]]** — The [[passion|passions]] are caused, maintained, and strengthened by the movement of animal spirits; the spirits are the "last and most immediate cause" of every passion (Article 27).

- **[[soul]]** — The animal spirits are the mechanical bridge between the immaterial soul and the material body; they do not think or feel themselves but transmit motions in both d[[anger|ire]]ctions.

- **[[pineal-gland]]** — The pineal gland is the seat of the soul and the point where animal spirits are d[[anger|ire]]cted; the soul moves the gland, which red[[anger|ire]]cts the spirits' flow.

- **[[free-will]]** — The soul's volitions act through the animal spirits; the will does not d[[anger|ire]]ctly control the spirits but red[[anger|ire]]cts them via the pineal gland.

- **[[love]] / [[hatred]] / [[desire]] / [[joy]] / [[sadness]] / [[wonder]]** — Each of the six primitive [[passion|passions]] has a characteristic pattern of animal spirit agitation.

## 后世影响与解释

The concept of animal spirits dominated neurophysiology well into the 18th century. Although the specific mechanism was superseded by the discovery of electrical nerve impulses (Galvani, late 18th century), [[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' core insight — that mental functions are mediated by physical processes in the nervous system — remains foundational to modern neuroscience.

The transition from "animal spirits flowing through hollow nerves" to "electrochemical signals propagating along neurons" represents one of the most d[[anger|ire]]ct continuities between Cartesian physiology and contemporary science.
