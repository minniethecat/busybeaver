# Sadness (La Tristesse)

**Sadness** (*la tristesse*) is one of the six primitive [[passion|passions]] in [[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' *The [[the-passions-of-the-soul|Passions of the Soul]]*. It is the passion of **suffering** — the soul's painful commotion upon representing a present evil as its own.

Together with [[joy]], it forms the hedonic axis around which all other [[passion|passions]] are organized, but its physiological and psychological effects are far more disruptive than joy's.

## 定义

[[rene-descartes|Descartes]] pairs sadness with joy in Article 61 as the hedonic contrary:

> The thought of a present evil arouses sadness, when the evil is one that is represented as belonging to us. [[061-001]]

The qualification "represented as belonging to us" is crucial: an evil we observe from a distance (a stranger's misfortune, a historical catastrophe) may not trigger sadness unless we take it as in some sense *ours*. The passion depends on the soul's self-involvement in the represented state of affairs.

## 笛卡尔的论述

### 理智的悲伤与激情的悲伤

As with joy, [[rene-descartes|Descartes]] distinguishes intellectual from passional sadness:

> There is also an intellectual sadness which isn't itself a passion but which almost always accompanies the passion. This sadness arises from the belief that one has some evil. [[093-003]]

Intellectual sadness is the soul's own recognition of an evil; passional sadness is the bodily commotion that this recognition triggers. The two are distinct but practically inseparable.

### 生理表现

Sadness has the most distinctive and unpleasant physiological signature of all the [[passion|passions]]:

> In sadness the pulse is weak and slow, and we feel as though our heart is constricted by bonds that freeze it, transmitting their chill to the rest of the body. Nevertheless, we sometimes have a good appetite and feel that our stomach is faithfully doing its duty — provided there is no hatred mixed with the sadness. [[100-001]]

[[rene-descartes|Descartes]] makes an important therapeutic observation: sadness alone (without [[hatred]]) preserves [[desire|appetite]]. The compounding of sadness with hatred is what makes grief so physically destructive:

> Sadness doesn't diminish except when hatred, which is often joined to sadness, is removed. [[105-001]]

This has practical implications for [[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' therapeutic program: treating pathological sadness involves disentangling it from the hatred that intensifies and prolongs it.

### 悲伤的多样性

Unlike joy — which [[rene-descartes|Descartes]] treats as relatively uniform — sadness takes many forms depending on its cause:

> The thought of a past evil arouses sadness when it is represented as belonging to us. This sadness has two species — envy and pity. [[062-001]] (paraphrase)

- **Envy** is sadness at another's good, which we represent as an evil to ourselves.
- **Pity** is sadness at another's evil, which we represent as though it were our own.
- **Repentance** is sadness at our own past evil actions — a form of sadness that [[rene-descartes|Descartes]] considers potentially useful.

### 悲伤的功能与限度

[[rene-descartes|Descartes]] does not condemn sadness outright. Like all [[passion|passions]], it has a natural function:

> The passions all have a good use: they strengthen and preserve thoughts in the soul that it is good for the soul to preserve. Sadness, for example, strengthens the thought of the evil that caused it, which can help us avoid such evils in the future. [[061-001]] (paraphrase of Descartes' general argument)

But sadness becomes pathological when it persists beyond its natural function — when the [[animal-spirits|animal spirit]]s' movement becomes habitual and the soul can no longer detach from the representation of the evil.

## 与其他概念的关系

- **[[joy]]** — Sadness's d[[anger|ire]]ct contrary; the two are the hedonic poles structuring [[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' ent[[anger|ire]] passion system.

- **[[hatred]]** — The most dangerous compound: sadness + hatred produces debilitating grief; removing hatred often cures sadness.

- **[[love]]** — Sadness can co-occur with [[love]] when the [[love]]d object is absent, threatened, or lost (grief).

- **[[desire]]** — Sadness generates the des[[anger|ire]] to escape; des[[anger|ire]] compounded with sadness is anxious and contractive.

- **[[wonder]]** — Unlike wonder (which has no cardiac component), sadness profoundly affects the heart, pulse, and digestion.

- **[[generosity]]** — The generous person experiences sadness only for genuine evils and without the compounding excess of hatred.

## 后世影响与解释

[[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' physiological account of sadness — as a constriction of the heart and a slowing of the [[animal-spirits|animal spirit]]s — was one of the earliest attempts to ground a specific emotion in specific bodily mechanisms.

While the details are obsolete, the approach anticipates contemporary affective neuroscience, which maps distinct emotions to distinct patterns of autonomic and neural activity.

[[rene-descartes|Descartes]]' therapeutic insight — that sadness is worsened by its compounding with hatred — also prefigures cognitive-behavioral approaches that target the judgments and appraisals that sustain depressive states.
