# Article 32: How we know that this gland is the principal seat of the soul

[032-001] What convinces me that this gland is the only place in the body where the soul can directly exercise its functions is my conviction that all the other parts of our brain are double, as are all the organs of our external senses—eyes, hands, ears and so on. ·The fact that sense-organs come in pairs is central to my argument·. We often have one simple thought about one object at one time; so there must be some place where two sense-impressions coming through a matched pair of sense-organs can be brought together in a single impression before reaching the soul, so that they don’t present it with two objects instead of one. It makes sense to think of these impressions as being unified in this gland by means of the spirits that brush by it going into the brain. There’s nowhere else in the body where they could exist in the unified form except as a result of the unifying activities of this gland.
