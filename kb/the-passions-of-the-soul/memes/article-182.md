# Article 182: Envy

[182-001] What we usually call ‘envy’ is a vice—a natural perversity that certain people have, making them annoyed when they see good coming to others. But I’m using ‘envy’ here to signify a passion, and indeed a passion that isn’t always unvirtuous. So: the passion of envy is a kind of sadness mingled with hatred, arising from seeing good coming to people one •thinks are unworthy of it. This •thought can be justified only in regard to goods due to fortune. What about the advantages—of the soul or even of the body—that we possess from birth? God gave us those before we were capable of doing any evil, so we are certainly worthy of them!
