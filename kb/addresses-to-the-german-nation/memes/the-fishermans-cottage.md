## Overview

The Fisherman's Cottage is the secluded home where [[Undine]] is raised after being found as a child. Located on a narrow spit of land extending into a lake, it is the dwelling of [[The Old Fisherman]] and his wife, who became Undine's foster parents. The cottage is the first setting in the [[Undine]] narrative and represents the world of simple, natural domesticity from which Undine's story departs.

## Significance

The Fisherman's Cottage embodies the prelapsarian innocence from which the [[Undine]] narrative unfolds. Like [[The Mill]] in [[Good-for-Nothing]], it is a site of humble domestic labor and natural harmony. The cottage's isolation—set on a remote peninsula—mirrors Undine's own liminal status between the human and elemental worlds.
