## Overview

**Registrator Heerbrand** is a supporting character in E.T.A. Hoffmann's fairy tale "The Golden Pot" (Der goldne Topf).

A jovial civil servant in Dresden, Heerbrand is a close friend of [[conrector-paulmann|Conrector Paulmann]] and a frequent guest at his evening gatherings.

He serves as a suitor to [[veronica|Veronica]] and, through his proposal to her, provides the "happy ending" of the bourgeois subp[[destiny|lot]]—contrasting with the supernatural union of [[anselmus|Anselmus]] and [[serpentina|Serpentina]].

## The Cheerful Philistine

Heerbrand embodies the **contented burgher**—a man entirely at [[heimat|home]] in the material world, untouched by the supernatural forces that torment [[anselmus|Anselmus]]. His good [[romantic-nature|nature]] and conviviality make him a sympathetic figure even as Hoffmann gently satirizes his limitations:

> [021-053] The Conrector, with his two daughters, as well as Registrator Heerbrand, all on the point of stepping into a gondola.

> [021-062] "May one not, even when awake, have foolish fancies?" interrupted Registrator Heerbrand.

## Music and Sociability

Heerbrand is an enthusiastic p[[romantic-art|art]]icipant in the [[romantic-music|music]]al evenings at [[conrector-paulmann|Paulmann]]'s house. His appreciation for [[veronica|Veronica]]'s singing contains the seeds of his romantic interest:

> [021-066] "Mademoiselle," said Registrator Heerbrand, "you have a voice like a crystal bell!"

> [021-070] Registrator Heerbrand laid a music-leaf on the frame of the harpsichord.

## Suitor to Veronica

As the tale progresses, Heerbrand's interest in [[veronica|Veronica]] becomes explicit. His courtship—practical, cheerful, and entirely conventional—offers [[veronica|Veronica]] a path to respectable happiness after her hopes for [[anselmus|Anselmus]] prove futile:

> [021-075] Veronica was filled with joy at Registrator Heerbrand's proposal.

> [021-140] Registrator Heerbrand came, as he had promised, after tea.

His proposal and [[veronica|Veronica]]'s acceptance close the bourgeois p[[destiny|lot]] line on a note of comfortable resolution—the "normal" happy ending that Hoffmann places alongside the mystical union in Atlantis.

## The Drinking Companion

Heerbrand's conviviality extends to enthusiastic drinking, a trait Hoffmann renders with comic affection:

> [021-101] Registrator Heerbrand was much overcome with Liqueur.

> [021-127] In the Linke Bath, Registrator Heerbrand drank so much strong double beer that he began singing student songs.

## Character Significance

Heerbrand represents the **unreflective happiness** of the philistine—a state Hoffmann portrays not with contempt but with a kind of affectionate irony. He is the man who will never see [[serpentina|Serpentina]], never know the terror and glory of the crystal bottle, but who will enjoy his pipe, his beer, and his [[veronica|Veronica]] in comfortable contentment.
