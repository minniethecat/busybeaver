## Overview

The Elder-Tree stands at the wall of Linke's garden in [[Dresden]], near the banks of [[The Elbe River]]. It is the threshold site where [[Anselmus]] experiences visions of the supernatural—golden serpents, crystal bells, and [[Serpentina]] herself. The tree serves as the primary portal between mundane reality and the magical realm in [[The Golden Pot]] narrative.

Overwhelmed, he embraces the tree:

> …had clasped the trunk of the elder-tree, and was calling incessantly… (021-044)

Skeptical onlookers dismiss his behavior:

> …of foolish stuff beneath the elder-tree. This was the more shocking t… (021-051)

Later, the experience returns to his memory:

> …strange things he had seen at the elder-tree again started forth into his… (021-054)

The visionary sounds associated with it are described with musical imagery:

> Crystal bells in elder-trees sound strangely, strangely! (021-067)

## Significance

The Elder-Tree is the central liminal space in [[The Golden Pot]]. It marks the boundary between the ordinary world of Dresden and the mythic realm of [[Archivarius Lindhorst|the Salamander]]. Unlike the built places in the narrative (houses, offices), the elder-tree is a natural portal—its branches, blossoms, and rustling leaves become the medium through which the supernatural manifests. In the Romantic tradition, it embodies the idea that nature is the gateway to the transcendent.
