## Overview

**The Apple-Woman** (also called the Apple-Wife or Old Witch) is a central antagonist in E.T.A. Hoffmann's fairy tale "The Golden Pot" (Der goldne Topf).

She is first encountered as a crone selling apples and cakes at the Black Gate in Dresden (021-028), and later reveals herself as a powerful witch aligned with the dark forces that oppose [[archivarius-lindhorst|Archivarius Lindhorst]] and his salamander lineage (021-078).

## Role in The Golden Pot

### The Collision at the Black Gate

The Apple-Woman makes her first appearance on Ascension Day when the student [[anselmus|Anselmus]] accidentally knocks over her basket of apples and cakes (021-028):

> [021-028] On Ascension-day, at three o'clock in the afternoon, a young man in Dresden came running through the Black Gate, falling right into a basket of apples and cakes, which an old and very ugly woman was there exposing to sale. All that escaped being smashed to pieces was scattered away, and the street-urchins joyfully divided the booty.

She curses [[anselmus|Anselmus]] as he flees, crying after him: "Ay, run, run thy ways, thou Devil's bird!" (021-028). This seemingly trivial accident sets in motion the entire supernatural [[destiny|plot]], as [[anselmus|Anselmus]]'s subsequent despair under the elder-tree leads to his first vision of [[serpentina|the serpent-maiden]]s.

### The Bronze Door-Knocker

The Apple-Woman's witch-[[romantic-nature|nature]] is shockingly revealed when [[anselmus|Anselmus]] arrives at Lindhorst's house (021-078):

> [021-078] As the last stroke tingled through the air with loud clang from the steeple-clock of the Kreuzkirche, he lifted his hand to grasp this same knocker, the metal visage twisted itself, with horrid rolling of its blue-gleaming eyes, into a grinning smile. Alas, it was the Apple-woman of the Black Gate! The pointed teeth gnashed together in the loose jaws, and in their chattering through the skinny lips there was a growl of: "Thou fool, fool, fool!—Wait, wait!—Why didst run!—Fool!"

### The Witch's Final Mockery

When [[anselmus|Anselmus]] is imprisoned in a crystal bottle on Lindhorst's shelf, the Apple-Woman appears as an old coffee-pot to mock his plight (021-186):

> [021-186] Ere long he could observe that it proceeded from an old coffee-pot, with half-broken lid, standing over against him on a little shelf. As he looked at it more narrowly, the ugly features of a wrinkled old woman by degrees unfolded themselves; and in a few moments, the Apple-wife of the Black Gate stood before him. She grinned and laughed at him, and cried with screeching voice: "Ey, Ey, my pretty boy, must thou lie in limbo now? To the crystal thou hast run; did I not tell thee long ago?"

## Character Significance

The Apple-Woman represents the **dark, chthonic feminine** in Hoffmann's symbolic universe—a counterforce to the celestial-feminine principle embodied by [[serpentina|Serpentina]].

She is associated with black magic, base metals (bronze, copper), and the demonic, in contrast to Lindhorst's alchemical gold and [[serpentina|Serpentina]]'s golden-green radiance.

Hoffmann draws on the folkloric tradition of the witch as marketplace crone (021-028)—a figure of plebeian cunning who disrupts the bourgeois order represented by the Black Gate. Her curse on [[anselmus|Anselmus]] functions as a narrative catalyst, transforming an ordinary mishap into a supernatural ordeal (021-028).
