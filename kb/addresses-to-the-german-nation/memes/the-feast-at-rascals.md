## Overview

**The Feast at Rascal's** is a significant social event in Adelbert von Chamisso's "[[peter-schlemihl|Peter Schlemihl]]'s Wundersame Geschichte" (1814). After [[peter-schlemihl|Peter Schlemihl]] acquires inexhaustible wealth through the [[the-shadow-bargain|Shadow Bargain]], his servant [[rascal|Rascal]]—who has taken full advantage of his master's [[destiny|fortune]]—hosts a magnificent feast. The banquet becomes a test of [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]]'s ability to maintain his social standing as a shadowless man.

## Context

[[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]], now rich but shadowless, attempts to live normally by surrounding himself with servants and spending lavishly. [[rascal|Rascal]], his chief servant, has grown prosperous on his master's wealth and hosts the feast in grand style:

> [014-008] I commanded a feast to be prepared for the evening...

The feast is a display of [[rascal|Rascal]]'s pretensions:

> [014-011] ...in a faltering tone, to honor this feast by assuming the office whose duties I was best acquainted with...

The celebration is marked by ostentation and an undercurrent of unease:

> [014-013] ...joyfully to celebrate a joyful feast.

The feast's magnificence cannot fully conceal [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]]'s fundamental problem—without a shadow, he remains a social pariah, and the very wealth that funds the banquet is the cause of his alienation:

> [014-015] The magnificence of my feast, and my behavior at it, held all the guests in astonishment...

## Significance

### Wealth Without Belonging

The feast dramatizes the central irony of [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]]'s situation: he has unlimited money but cannot purchase what he truly [[sehnsucht|desire]]s—normal human community. The richer the banquet, the more acutely his shadowless condition is felt.

### Rascal's Ascendancy

The feast marks [[rascal|Rascal]]'s rise from servant to social aspirant. Having exploited [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]]'s wealth, [[rascal|Rascal]] now performs the role of host—a foreshadowing of his ultimate betrayal, when he abandons [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]] entirely.

### The Social Gaze

Every feast guest is a potential witness to [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]]'s secret. The shadowless man must navigate the scrutiny of a society that judges by appearances, and the feast—with its bright lights and close qu[[romantic-art|art]]ers—is the most dangerous of stages.
