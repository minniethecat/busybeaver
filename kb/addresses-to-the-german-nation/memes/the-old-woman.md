## Overview

The Old Woman is the caretaker of the mysterious castle where the [[Good-for-Nothing]] protagonist stays. Described as very ugly with a toothless mouth, she holds the keys to the castle, moves with silent authority, and serves as a liminal figure between the ordinary world and the castle's hidden life.

## Role in the Narrative

The Old Woman appears at the castle door, an unsettling gatekeeper:

> At the half-open castle door, stood a very ugly old woman in a black camisole and petticoat. (005-009)

She leads the protagonist through the castle with wordless authority:

> She walked before me across the courtyard, while the old woman by all sorts of courteous signs motioned me to follow. (005-010)

Her toothless mouth becomes a memorable image of decayed authority:

> The old woman ground away with her toothless mouth the while. (005-013)

### Keeper of the Keys

The Old Woman's defining attribute is the bunch of keys she carries, symbolizing her control over the castle's spaces:

> The old woman with the bunch of keys, who had unlocked the gate for me, came to meet me. (006-006)

This role gives her an intimate knowledge of all castle affairs. When questioned about the count:

> When I sometimes questioned the old woman as to the count's name and what went on in the castle, she only said, "You will soon enough find out." (006-007)

### The Watchful Presence

The Old Woman observes everything with unnerving vigilance. Her silent scrutiny makes the protagonist self-conscious:

> I was ashamed in presence of the old woman, who began to smirk and wink in the most mysterious manner. (006-017)

> The old woman, the grim old man, the maids — all seemed to know something. (006-020)

She is also the link to the student world — [[The Tall Student]] is her relative:

> He was a student and a relative of the old woman's, and was spending his vacation at the castle. (006-009)

## Relationships

- **[[The Tall Student]]** — Her relative, a pale student who spends his vacation at the castle. (006-009)
- **[[Good-for-Nothing]]** — The protagonist, whom she guides through the castle while watching his every move with knowing amusement. (005-009, 006-006, 006-017)
- **The Count** — The unseen master of the castle about whom she remains deliberately mysterious. (006-007)

## Themes

- The liminal gatekeeper — figures who control access to hidden worlds
- Age and decay as markers of ancient, mysterious authority
- The castle as a space of secrets, mediated by its servants
