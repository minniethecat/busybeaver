## Overview

The Gardener is a minor but recurring character in the [[Good-for-Nothing]] protagonist's early life at the castle. He employs the [[wandering-motif|wandering]] [[childhood-motif|youth]] as a [[garden-motif|garden]]er's boy, providing him with food, lodging, and wages, while his frequent absences grant the protagonist the freedom to daydream and observe the [[The Beautiful Lady|Beautiful Lady]].

## Role in the Narrative

The Gardener first appears indirectly through the castle maid's offer of employment:

> The lady's-maid appeared; she came up to me, declared that I was a charming young fellow, and that her mistress had sent to ask me if I did not want a place as gardener's boy. (001-012)

The protagonist accepts and finds the work pleasant, though the Gardener's absences define his daily rhythm:

> Whenever the gardener was away and I was alone, I took out my short tobacco-pipe, sat down, and thought of all the beautiful, polite things with which I could have entertained that lovely lady. (001-013)

The Gardener's house becomes the protagonist's vantage point for watching the Beautiful Lady:

> In the evening—it was Saturday, and, in joyous anticipation of the coming Sunday, I was standing, fiddle in hand, at the window of the gardener's house, still thinking of the sparkling eyes. (001-016)

### Discipline and Freedom

The Gardener scolds the protagonist when his day[[dream-motif|dreaming]] interferes with honest work:

> Many days passed and I did not see her, either in the garden or at the window. The gardener scolded me for laziness; I was out of humor, tired of myself and of all about me. (001-024)

Yet the protagonist rises early, before the Gardener stirs, to steal glimpses of the Beautiful Lady at her window:

> Contrary to all my habits hitherto, I now rose betimes every day, before the gardener and the other assistants were stirring. (001-019)

### Final Appearance

At the story's joyful conclusion, the Gardener reappears as a celebrant:

> I recognized all the faces. The old gardener beat the drum, the students from Prague formed up, the students carried banners. (010-022)

## Relationships

- **[[Good-for-Nothing]]** — The Gardener employs the protagonist as a [[garden-motif|garden]]er's boy; his scolding and frequent absences shape the [[childhood-motif|youth]]'s early life at the castle. (001-012, 001-024)
- **[[The Beautiful Lady]]** — The Gardener's proximity to the castle allows the protagonist precious glimpses of her at her window. (001-019)
