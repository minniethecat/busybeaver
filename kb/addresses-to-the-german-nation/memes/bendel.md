## Overview

Bendel is [[Peter Schlemihl]]'s loyal servant, friend, and confidant in Chamisso's *[[peter-schlemihl|Peter Schlemihl]]'s Wundersame Geschichte*. Distinguished by his honest physiognomy, active zeal, and able prudence, Bendel becomes [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]]'s most trusted companion—the one person who knows his master's secret and remains steadfast through every calamity.

## Role in the Narrative

Bendel enters [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]]'s service on the recommendation of a consultant who helps arrange the newly wealthy man's household:

> He recommended for the services about my person a certain Bendel, whose honest and intelligent physiognomy immediately captivated me. He it was whose attachment has since accompanied me consolingly through the wretchedness of life, and has helped me to support my gloomy lot. (012-010)

### The Search for the Gray Man

When [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]] regrets his bargain with the man in the gray coat, he entrusts Bendel with the urgent task of finding him:

> I ordered Bendel to me, for he appeared to possess address and skill. I told him the time, the place in which I had seen him; I described to him all who had been present. (012-015)

Loading Bendel with gold and jewels, [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]] commissions him:

> "Bendel," said I, "these level many wants, and reconcile him who is in need with his lot; but these also purchase that which is like to the very dearest." (012-016)

Bendel pursues the task with admirable diligence, though the gray man proves elusive:

> Thus much was clear from the circumstantial relation of Bendel, whose active zeal and able proceeding, although with such fruitless result, received from me their merited commendation. (012-018)

### Keeper of Secrets

Bendel is entrusted with [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]]'s deepest secret—the loss of his shadow—and manages it with extraordinary discretion:

> It is indescribable how providently Bendel continued to conceal my defect. He was everywhere before me, and provided against everything. (013-015)

When the shadow's absence is publicly exposed at a [[garden-motif|garden]] p[[romantic-art|art]]y, Bendel rushes to his master's aid:

> He was terrified as he saw me; one word revealed to him all. Post horses were immediately fetched. (013-021)

### Rivalry with Rascal

Bendel's honesty contrasts sharply with [[Rascal]]'s knavery. Bendel warns [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]]:

> Bendel, the next morning, revealed to me in confidence that the suspicion which he had long entertained of Rascal's honesty was now become certainty. (014-012)

[[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]] reflects on the division of roles between his two servants:

> Rascal remained the first of my servants, but Bendel was my friend and my confidant. (014-014)

## Relationships

- **[[Peter Schlemihl]]** — His master and friend; Bendel is the one person [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]] trusts with every secret. (012-010, 014-014)
- **[[Rascal]]** — His foil and adversary; Bendel's honesty exposes [[rascal|Rascal]]'s treachery. (014-012)
