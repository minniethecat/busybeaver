## Overview

Peter Schlemihl is the eponymous protagonist of Adelbert von Chamisso's *Peter Schlemihl's Wundersame Geschichte* (The Wonderful History of Peter Schlemihl), one of the embedded tales within the larger narrative. A man of means who makes a fateful bargain—selling his shadow to a mysterious stranger in exchange for inexhaustible wealth—Schlemihl discovers that the loss of his shadow renders him an outcast, cut off from human society despite his riches.

## Role in the Narrative

Schlemihl first appears in the story as a wealthy but shadowless man. A messenger reports his initial encounter:

> "I have yet a message to deliver which a person gave me whom I met at the door and whose parting words were these: 'Tell Mr. Peter Schlemihl he will not see me here again.'" (012-019)

His reputation precedes him. When asked about him, others describe him in glowing terms:

> "May not, Count, a certain Peter Schlemihl be not quite unknown to you? A man of superior character and singular attainments—" (015-011)

The mysterious stranger later confronts him with a proposal, which Schlemihl stubbornly refuses:

> "It grieves me, Monsieur Schlemihl, that you obstinately decline the business which I propose to you as a friend. Perhaps another time I may be more fortunate." (015-040)

### The Shadow's Consequences

The man in gray offers Schlemihl a cap of invisibility, explaining its practical advantage:

> "But you must admit that such a cap is much more convenient. It covers not only your person but your shadow at the same time, and as many others as you have a mind to carry with you." (016-016)

Schlemihl's tale becomes known beyond his own circle; it is read aloud as a moral lesson:

> I heard something of which the subject was Peter Schlemihl read aloud, and articulately, but I could not collect the sense. I saw a friendly man, and a very lovely woman in black dress appear at my bedside. (021-008)

## Relationships

- **[[Bendel]]** — Schlemihl's loyal servant and confidant, who stands by him through his ordeals. (012-010)
- **[[Rascal]]** — A knavish servant who exploits and ultimately betrays Schlemihl. (013-021)
- **The Man in the Gray Coat** — The mysterious figure who purchases Schlemihl's shadow and repeatedly offers him sinister bargains. (012-019, 015-040)
