## Overview

Rascal is the treacherous servant of [[Peter Schlemihl]] in Chamisso's *[[peter-schlemihl|Peter Schlemihl]]'s Wundersame Geschichte*. An arrant knave who insinuates himself into [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]]'s household through cleverness, Rascal contrasts starkly with the loyal [[Bendel]]. He embezzles his master's wealth, exploits his secret, and ultimately orchestrates [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]]'s ruin.

## Role in the Narrative

Rascal enters the story suddenly, when [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]] flees a social catastrophe—the public exposure of his missing shadow:

> I took only one of my people with me, an arrant knave, called Rascal, who had contrived to make himself necessary to me by his cleverness and who could suspect nothing of today's occurrence. (013-021)

### The Inventive Servant

Rascal proves resourceful in managing [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]]'s public affairs, helping to maintain the illusion of his master's legitimacy:

> The mysterious power of my purse, the exertions of Bendel, and the inventiveness of Rascal succeeded in triumphing over time itself. It is really astonishing how richly and beautifully everything was arranged in those few hours. (014-008)

When [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]] is mistaken for a traveling monarch, Rascal cleverly reinforces the misapprehension:

> I sent out Rascal to make inquiry. He brought word to this effect: That the people had received reliable intelligence that the good king of Prussia traveled through the country under the name of a count. (014-006)

### Betrayal

[[bendel|Bendel]] soon uncovers Rascal's true [[romantic-nature|nature]]:

> Bendel, the next morning, revealed to me in confidence that the suspicion which he had long entertained of Rascal's honesty was now become certainty—that he had yesterday embezzled large sums. (014-012)

Despite this, Rascal retains his position:

> Rascal remained the first of my servants, but Bendel was my friend and my confidant. (014-014)

Rascal's ultimate betrayal comes when he conspires to marry [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]]'s be[[romantic-love|love]]d [[mina|Mina]], using his knowledge of [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]]'s secret as leverage:

> At this moment Mr. Rascal was announced, and stepped impudent and mocking, Rascal's shadow between her and me. (016-001, 016-032)

The criminal proceedings that follow prove fatal:

> The criminal process had cost Mr. Rascal his life. (021-010)

## Relationships

- **[[Peter Schlemihl]]** — His master whom he exploits and betrays; Rascal knows [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]]'s secret of the missing shadow and uses it against him. (013-021, 016-032)
- **[[Bendel]]** — His moral opposite and adversary; [[bendel|Bendel]]'s honesty continually exposes Rascal's schemes. (014-012)
- **[[mina|Mina]]** — The woman [[peter-schlemihl|Schlemihl]] [[romantic-love|love]]s, whom Rascal schemes to marry instead. (016-030)
