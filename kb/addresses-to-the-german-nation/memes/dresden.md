## Overview

Dresden is the principal setting of [[The Golden Pot]], the Saxon capital on the banks of [[The Elbe River]] where [[Anselmus]] lives and experiences his visionary encounters. The city is described in terms of grandeur—"lordly Dresden, stretching, bold and proud"—providing the mundane urban framework against which Hoffmann's supernatural events unfold. Key locations within Dresden include the Black Gate, Linke's garden, and [[The Elder-Tree]] by the city wall.

## In the Source Text

The narrative opens with Anselmus in motion through the city:

> …the afternoon, a young man in Dresden came running through the Blac… (021-028)

The city is described in a panoramic Romantic view:

> …behind him rose lordly Dresden, stretching, bold and proud… (021-031)

Later, a domestic arrangement is mentioned:

> …to keep house here in Dresden with his three daughters… (021-145)

## Significance

Dresden in [[The Golden Pot]] is more than a backdrop—it is the meticulously rendered real world that Hoffmann juxtaposes with the mythic realm of [[Archivarius Lindhorst]]. The city's solid, "lordly" architecture, its Black Gate, its gardens and bridges—all ground the narrative in the recognizable, only to have the supernatural erupt through everyday spaces. The crossing of [[The Elbe River]] and the visions beneath [[The Elder-Tree]] transform ordinary Dresden locations into thresholds between worlds, embodying the Romantic conviction that the marvelous lies hidden within the mundane.
