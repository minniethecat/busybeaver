## Overview

The Castle Garden is a walled, secluded space adjoining the toll-house where the [[Good-for-Nothing]] lives as Receiver of Toll at the [[Imperial Customs Office]]. It connects to the larger castle grounds through a breach in the wall, making it a liminal zone between the official world and the enchanted world of the castle.

## In the Source Text

The [[garden-motif|garden]] is introduced with detail emphasizing its secluded character:

> A very pretty little toll-house with a red-tiled roof stood near, with a gay little flower-garden inclosed by a picket-fence behind it. A breach in the wall connected this garden with the most secluded and shady part of the castle garden itself. (002-001)

It is while dozing here that the protagonist is discovered and appointed toll-keeper. The [[garden-motif|garden]] then becomes the site of his first glimpse of [[The Beautiful Lady]], the moment that anchors his emotional journey:

> Through the half-opened lattice of the cool, dark summer-house buried amid flowers, I saw the sparkle of a pair of beautiful, youthful eyes. (001-015)

At the narrative's close, the garden reappears: "The tall trees in the castle [[garden-motif|garden]] were still murmuring." (010-002)
