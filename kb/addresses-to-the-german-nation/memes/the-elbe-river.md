## Overview

The Elbe River flows through [[Dresden]] and forms the natural landscape around which much of [[The Golden Pot]] unfolds. It serves as both a geographical marker—the "Elbe road" and "Elbe-bridge" orienting characters in the city—and a symbolic boundary between the ordinary and the supernatural. Crossings of the Elbe in the narrative often coincide with visionary or transformative moments.

## Significance

The Elbe River in [[The Golden Pot]] functions as more than a geographical feature—it is the fluid boundary between Dresden's mundane civic life and the supernatural world. Crossings of the Elbe (by road, by bridge) parallel Anselmus's own crossings between the ordinary and the visionary. The river's "gold-dyed waves" reflect the alchemical and transformative themes central to Hoffmann's narrative, tying the natural landscape to the mythic realm of [[Archivarius Lindhorst]].
