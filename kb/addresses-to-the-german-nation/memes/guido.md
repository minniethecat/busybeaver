## Overview

Guido (*Herr Guido*) is a painter and [[romantic-music|music]]ian who travels with [[Painter Lionardo]] through the narrative. Gentler and more refined than his companion, Guido plays the zither and sings from balconies, embodying the Romantic ideal of the [[romantic-art|art]]ist as both visual creator and [[romantic-music|music]]al soul.

## Role in the Narrative

Guido is introduced alongside Lionardo:

> I am the painter Lionardo, and my friend here is a painter also, called Guido. (003-034)

Throughout the carriage journey, Guido's mannerisms set him ap[[romantic-art|art]]:

> Only now and then Herr Guido put his pretty head out of the carriage window. (004-004)

> My hat dropped off, and Herr Guido screamed aloud. (004-005)

### The Balcony Performance

Guido's most striking moment comes at the castle, where he performs from an elevated platform:

> Suddenly Herr Guido appeared on the balcony above, in his shirt-sleeves, with his zither. (004-009)

> When Herr Guido was singing on the balcony, he noticed me and threw down a rose. (004-015)

His [[romantic-music|music]]al talent is paired with his physical beauty — the zither becomes his sig[[romantic-nature|nature]] instrument:

> Only the zither upon which Herr Guido had played was hanging on the wall. (004-014)

### Recognition and Reunion

Guido's reputation precedes him. When the protagonist mentions his name, recognition is instant:

> "What!" he cried, "the two painters called Guido and Lionardo?" When I assented, he sprang up. (007-015)

The name also evokes the historical painter Guido Reni, as noted in an [[romantic-art|art]] discussion where the protagonist is mocked for ignorance:

> You talk of masters Leonardo da Vinci and Guido Reni; but you know nothing about them. (007-015)

In the final reunion, it is Guido whom the protagonist first recognizes:

> I exclaimed joyfully, "It is Herr Guido!" swinging myself quickly down from the horse. (010-007)

> "It is Herr Guido!" I exclaimed, louder than before. (010-018)

## Relationships

- **[[Painter Lionardo]]** — Fellow painter and constant travelling companion; the two form a complementary pair, with Guido as the gentler, more [[romantic-art|art]]istic soul. (003-034, 004-004)
- **[[Good-for-Nothing]]** — The protagonist, whom Guido and Lionardo take under their wing and bring along on their carriage journey. (003-034, 004-002)
- **[[The Beautiful Lady]]** — The mysterious woman whom the protagonist idealises; Guido's song on the balcony echoes the protagonist's own romantic longing. (004-015)

## Themes

- Music as spiritual expression — the zither and singing as Guido's primary mode of being
- The duality of [[romantic-art|art]]istic temperaments — Guido's gentleness contrasted with Lionardo's fire
- Beauty and refinement in the Romantic [[romantic-art|art]]ist archetype
