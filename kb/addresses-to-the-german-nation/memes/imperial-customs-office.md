## Overview

The Imperial Customs Office is the bureaucratic institution at the castle gate where the [[Good-for-Nothing]] is unexpectedly appointed as Receiver of Toll. It embodies the orderly, institutional world that stands in contrast to the [[wandering-motif|wandering]] life, and serves as the protagonist's first—and temporary—foothold in settled society.

## In the Source Text

### The Appointment

The office appears early in the narrative when the toll-gate keeper dies suddenly. The protagonist, asleep in [[The Castle Garden]], is discovered and brought before the Bailiff:

> When we reached the office, where as yet it was hardly light, the Bailiff, behind a huge inkstand and piles of books and papers, gave me a long lecture about my duties. (002-001)

The elaborate bureaucratic formality—"behind a huge inkstand and piles of books"—gently satirizes the machinery of state while providing the protagonist with a comic initiation into officialdom.

### The Toll-House

The protagonist occupies the toll-house—a pretty cottage with a red-tiled roof and flower [[garden-motif|garden]] adjoining [[The Castle Garden]]—for a time before [[Wanderlust]] calls him away again. The toll-house sits at the boundary between [[the-castle-garden|the garden]] (romance, mystery) and the highroad (journey, freedom).
