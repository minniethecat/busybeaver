## Overview

The Elder Lady is the older of two aristocratic ladies who encounter the [[Good-for-Nothing]] protagonist during his travels. More maternal and encouraging than her [[youth-motif|young]]er companion, she takes a protective interest in the [[youth-motif|young]] [[wandering-motif|wanderer]], recognizing something special in him.

## Role in the Narrative

The Elder Lady first appears on the road, ordering her carriage to stop when she hears the protagonist singing:

> When I stopped singing the elder ordered the coachman to stop the carriage, and the younger leaned far out. (001-008)

She repeatedly seeks him out, showing a persistent and kindly interest:

> I was about to retire, when the elder perceived me. "Aha, you are just the one we want," she said. (001-027)

Her maternal warmth is most evident when she gives the protagonist a farewell kiss:

> I do not remember what, and the elder of my two ladies gave me a kiss on the forehead. (001-035)

### The Mask and Recognition

The Elder Lady is later identified when she removes her mask at a social gathering, a moment of revelation:

> I took off my mask. It was the other — the elder lady! (002-026)

This scene of unmasking connects her to the world of courtly intrigue and hidden identities that permeates the narrative.

### Role as Guide

Throughout the early journey, the Elder Lady serves as a gentle guide, offering the protagonist direction and encouragement. Her questions are probing but kind, treating him not as a servant but as a person of interest. She and [[The Beautiful Lady|her younger companion]] represent the world of refinement and mystery that draws the protagonist forward.

## Relationships

- **[[The Beautiful Lady]]** — The [[youth-motif|young]]er of the two ladies, with whom the Elder Lady travels and whom she seems to chaperone. The protagonist's romantic fixation on the [[youth-motif|young]]er lady is tacitly enabled by the Elder Lady's protective presence. (001-008, 001-027)
- **[[Good-for-Nothing]]** — The protagonist, whom she treats with unexpected warmth and encouragement, seeing past his humble appearance. (001-027, 001-035)

## Themes

- Maternal gui[[dance-motif|dance]] and the protective elder figure
- Recognition of hidden worth — seeing the protagonist's potential beneath his ragged exterior
- The mystery of aristocratic travel and concealed identities
