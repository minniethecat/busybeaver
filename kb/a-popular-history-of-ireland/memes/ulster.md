**Ulster** (Irish: _Ulaidh_) is the northernmost of [[Ireland]]'s four historic provinces, bounded by the Atlantic Ocean to the north and west, [[Leinster]] to the south, and [[Connaught]] to the southwest. It comprises nine counties: Antrim, [[armagh|Armagh]], Cavan, Donegal, Down, Fermanagh, Londonderry, Monaghan, and Tyrone. In ancient Irish tradition, Ulster was the seat of the Red Branch Knights and the hero [[Cú Chulainn]], immortalized in the Ulster Cycle of tales. During the Tudor and Stuart periods, Ulster was the last bastion of Gaelic independence, and the subsequent [[Plantation of Ulster]] fundamentally transformed its ethnic and religious composition.

## Ancient Ulster

Ulster occupies a preeminent place in Irish mythology. The province was home to the Ulaid people, and its ancient capital, Emain Macha (near modern [[Armagh]]), features centrally in the epic _Táin Bó Cúailnge_:

> "Ulster was Cuchullin: so called from _cu_, a hound, or watch-dog, and Ullin." (002-051)

> "EMANIA, near Armagh, for Ulster; LEIGHLIN, for Leinster; CASHEL, for Munster; and CRUCHAIN for Connaught." (002-088)

The province was divided as part of the tribute system of ancient Ireland:

> "One-third to Ulster, one-third to Connaught, and one-third to the two provinces of Leinster and Munster." (004-095)

## The Red Branch Knights

The _Craobh Rua_ (Red Branch) was the premier warrior order of ancient [[Ulster]], comparable to the _Fianna_ of [[Leinster]]. Under King Conchobar mac Nessa, these warriors defended the province from the incursions of Queen Medb of [[Connaught]] in the great cattle raid of Cooley:

> "The Militia of Ulster; the _Fiann_, or Militia of Leinster, and the Clan-Degaidh of Munster." (002-043)

## The Last Gaelic Stronghold

During the Tudor conquest of [[Ireland]], Ulster—under the leadership of [[Hugh O'Neill]], [[hugh-oneill|Earl of Tyrone]], and [[hugh-roe-odonnell|Red Hugh O'Donnell]]—mounted the most formidable resistance to English expansion. The [[Nine Years' War]] (1594–1603) ended with the Treaty of Mellifont, and the subsequent [[Flight of the Earls]] (1607) left the province leaderless, paving the way for the Plantation.

## The Plantation of Ulster

The [[Plantation of Ulster]] (begun 1609) was the most consequential colonial project in Irish history. English and Scottish settlers were introduced on a massive scale, displacing the native Gaelic population and creating a Protestant majority that would define the province's political allegiance for centuries:

> "The ruler of Ulster was now the English Crown, and the Plantation of Ulster became the model for subsequent plantations." (004-065)
