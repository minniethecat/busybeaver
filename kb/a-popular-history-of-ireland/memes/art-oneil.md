Art O'Neil was a member of the powerful [[O'Neil dynasty]] in late sixteenth-century [[Ulster]]. He is best remembered for his role in the dramatic escape of [[Hugh Roe O'Donnell]] from Dublin Castle in the winter of 1592 — an event in which O'Neil perished, becoming a martyr to the Gaelic cause on the eve of the [[Nine Years War]].

## The Escape from Dublin Castle

In 1592, [[Hugh Roe O'Donnell]] — who had been held as a hostage by the English authorities in Dublin Castle since 1587 — made his second attempt at escape. Together with Henry and Art O'Neil, sons of John the Proud, he escaped through a sewer of the Castle during Christmas week (058-234). In the streets of Dublin they found O'Hagan, the confidential agent of [[Tyrone|Hugh O'Neill, Earl of Tyrone]], waiting to guide them to the fastness of Glenmalure, the stronghold of [[Feagh Mac Hugh O'Byrne]] in the [[Wicklow Mountains]].

## The Art O'Neil of Clandeboy

A later Art O'Neil — described as "son of Turlogh" and an early adversary of the great [[Hugh O'Neill]] — appears in the record during the Nine Years War. This Art O'Neil of [[Clandeboy]] was won over by Sir Henry Dowcra, the English commander at [[Derry]], who had been recommended by [[Francis Bacon]] to the [[Earl of Essex]]. Dowcra "succeeded in the summer in winning over Art O'Neil, son of Turlogh, the early adversary of the great Hugh".

## Final Years

As the Nine Years War drew to its bitter conclusion, Art O'Neil of Clandeboy remained with Hugh O'Neill in his last retreat. Along with O'Neill's brother [[Cormac McMahon]], he shared the

wintry hardships of the final asylum near Lough Neagh — the most secure of O'Neill's fastnesses, at Glencancean (062-136).
