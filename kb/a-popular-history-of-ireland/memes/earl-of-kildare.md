The **Earl of Kildare** was a title held by the junior branch of the Geraldine family in [[Leinster]], created in 1316. The Earls of Kildare rose to unprecedented power in the 15th and 16th centuries, serving as Lords Deputy of Ireland and exercising near-royal authority from their seat at [[Maynooth]] Castle. The most celebrated holder was Gerald FitzGerald, the 8th Earl—known in Irish annals as _Gerald Mór_ (Gerald the Great)—who dominated Irish affairs from 1477 to 1513. His son, the 9th Earl (Gerald Óg), continued the family's ascendancy until the rebellion of "Silken Thomas" in 1534 brought the Tudor Crown's wrath upon the house, leading to executions, attainder, and the beginning of direct English rule in [[Ireland]].

## Rise of the Kildare Geraldines

The Kildare branch descended from John FitzThomas FitzGerald, who was created 1st Earl of Kildare in 1316. Their power base in [[Leinster]] placed them closer to [[Dublin]] and the seat of English administration than their Desmond cousins, giving them greater influence over the colonial government.

> "The first Earl of Kildare draws our attention naturally to the elder branch of the same house—the Earls of Desmond." (031-176)

## Gerald Mór: The Great Earl

Gerald FitzGerald, the 8th Earl (r. 1477–1513), was the apex of Kildare power. As Lord Deputy, he commanded the loyalty of both the Anglo-Irish nobility and many Gaelic chieftains. McGee describes him in superlative terms:

> "Gerald, eighth Earl of Kildare (called in the Irish Annals Garrett More, or the Great Earl)." (044-306)

His administration was marked by the convening of Parliament at [[Dublin]]:

> "While his friend, the Earl of Kildare, called a Parliament at Dublin." (045-022)

His liberation from the Tower of London restored Kildare dominance:

> "The liberation of the Earl of Kildare from the Tower, and his restoration to the Deputyship in 1496." (045-318)

## Conflict with the Tudor Crown

The contest between Anglo-Irish autonomy and Tudor centralization defined the later Kildare era:

> "The contest between the Earl of Kildare and Lord Grey for the Deputyship marks the beginning of a new era." (045-085)

The 9th Earl, Gerald Óg, struggled to maintain the family's position against [[henry-viii|Henry VIII]]'s ministers:

> "The ninth and last Catholic Earl of Kildare, in the ninth year of his imprisonment, died in the Tower." (049-003)

## The Silken Thomas Rebellion

In 1534, Thomas FitzGerald ("Silken Thomas"), son of the 9th Earl, launched a dramatic rebellion against [[henry-viii|Henry VIII]], renouncing his allegiance at the Council table in [[Dublin]]. This futile rising led to the execution of Thomas and five of his uncles at Tyburn, the confiscation of Kildare lands, and the eclipse of the Geraldine ascendancy.
