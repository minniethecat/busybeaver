## Overview

Donnell More O'Donnell succeeded to the lordship of Tyrconnell in the year 1208 and ruled for thirty-three years until his death in 1241. His long and distinguished career spanned a critical period in 13th-century Ireland, as the native Irish lords resisted the expansion of the [[Norman Invasion of Ireland|Anglo-Norman colony]].

## Biography

### Succession and Early Campaigns

Donnell More succeeded his father in the lordship of Tyrconnell in 1208:

> [032-104] Donnell More O'Donnell, who, succeeding to the lordship of Tyrconnell in 1208, died in 1241, after an equally long and almost equally distinguished career. (032-104–032-105)

Early in his reign he signalized himself by capturing the castles of Birr, Kinnetty, Ballyroane and other strongholds from the invaders (031-183–031-184).

### Marriage Alliance

Donnell More strengthened his political position through marriage:

> [032-141] O'Donnell had married a daughter of Cathal Crovdearg, so that ties of blood gave him claims upon the powerful house of O'Conor. (032-141)

This alliance with the O'Conors of Connaught was part of a broader strategy of cooperation among the native Irish dynasties against Anglo-Norman encroachment.

### Military Exploits

In 1213, O'Donnell despatched Finn O'Brollaghan to establish a religious foundation, and pursued a fugitive who had insulted him into Clanrickarde territory (034-041–034-050). He led forces alongside other Irish chieftains and is recorded as a patron of Murray O'Daly of Lissadil, the celebrated poet (034-039).

One of his most notable military achievements came in cooperation with Hugh O'Neill:

> [032-133] its defenders slain by Hugh O'Neil and Donald More O'Donnell acting together. After this event we have no trace of a foreign force. (032-133–032-134)

This joint operation between the O'Neills and O'Donnells demonstrated the effectiveness of inter-dynastic cooperation against the Anglo-Norman presence.

### Succession

After Donnell More's death in 1241, he was succeeded by Melaghlin O'Donnell, who ruled from 1241 to 1247. Godfrey O'Donnell succeeded from 1248 to 1257, and Donnell Oge from 1257 to 1281 (032-106–032-107). His son Donnell Oge would later come to the relief of his people during a time of crisis (034-014–034-015).

## Legacy

Donnell More O'Donnell's long reign of thirty-three years provided stability to Tyrconnell during a turbulent century. His military successes against the invaders, his marriage alliance with the O'Conors, and his co-operation with Hugh O'Neill established a pattern of native resistance that would sustain O'Donnell power in the northwest for centuries to come.
