# Presbyterian

**Presbyterian** refers to the Protestant community in Ireland that adhered to the Presbyterian form of church governance, introduced primarily through [[Plantation of Ulster|Scottish settlement in Ulster]] during the 17th century. Unlike the established [[Church of Ireland]], Presbyterians faced varying degrees of legal restriction and toleration over the centuries, forming a distinctive religious and political bloc in Irish society.

## Origins in the Plantation of Ulster

The Presbyterian presence in Ireland originated with the [[Plantation of Ulster]] in the early 17th century, when Scottish settlers brought their Calvinist faith and Presbyterian polity to the northern province. The plantation scheme, begun under [[James I]], encouraged settlement by English and Scottish Protestants on lands confiscated from Gaelic Irish lords.

## Under the Commonwealth and Restoration

During the Commonwealth period, [[Oliver Cromwell]]'s administration regarded Presbyterianism with suspicion, fearing its potential to challenge central authority. The [[restoration|Restoration]] of [[Charles II]] brought renewed pressure on non-conforming Protestant groups.

The [[Restoration]] settlement reinforced the position of the established [[Church of Ireland]] while leaving Presbyterians outside the framework of religious conformity. Nevertheless, Presbyterian communities continued to grow in [[ulster|Ulster]], sustained by ongoing Scottish migration and natural increase.

## The Williamite Period

The [[Williamite War]] proved a turning point for Irish Presbyterians. Presbyterian settlers in [[ulster|Ulster]] played an active role in resisting the forces of [[James II]], with towns like Enniskillen becoming centres of Williamite resistance.
