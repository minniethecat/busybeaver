## Overview

Felim mac Crimthann (Feidhlimid mac Crimthainn) was King of [[Munster]] during the first half of the ninth century. He was one of the most powerful Irish rulers of the Norse period and was accorded the high title of King of Munster by the annalists of the years 840 to 847. (004-108)

Felim was the son of Crimthan, a descendant of the Eóganacht dynasty that had ruled Munster for centuries. (009-306)

## Reign and Military Campaigns

Felim mac Crimthann rose to power during a turbulent period in Irish history. The Norse invasions, which had begun at the close of the eighth century, were in full force during his reign. The annalists gave him the same high title as the greatest of his predecessors, recognising him as one of the leading rulers of [[Ireland]]. (004-108)

Felim contended with the northern Uí Néill dynasties in battle and in political rivalry, as the struggle between north and south continued throughout his reign. (009-115) He continued the tradition of strong Munster kingship during this and the following reigns. (009-286)

> The southern annalists say he did homage to Felim of Munster. (010-017)

This homage acknowledged Munster's supremacy in the southern half of [[Ireland]]. Among the chiefs who acknowledged Felim's authority were Cenfalad and other local rulers. (011-065)

## The Norse Threat

Felim's reign coincided with intensified Norse activity along the Irish coasts. The Vikings had established longphorts at [[Dublin]] and other sites, and their raids penetrated deep into the interior. (004-108)

Felim had to balance resistance to the Norsemen with the traditional inter-provincial rivalries that defined Irish politics. (009-115)

> The warlike Felim or the learned Cormac—we have seen the same policy pursued in every age. (013-019)

## Legacy

Felim mac Crimthann was remembered as "the warlike Felim," a ruler who maintained Munster's position against both Norse incursions and northern rivals. (013-019) He continued the tradition of strong Munster kingship that would later find its greatest expression in the reign of [[Brian Boru]].

Felim was the son of Crimthan and his career illustrated the capacity of provincial kings to exercise wide authority even during the most disruptive period of the Norse age. (009-306) (009-286)
