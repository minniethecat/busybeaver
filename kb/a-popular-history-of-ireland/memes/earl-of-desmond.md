The **Earl of Desmond** was a title held by the senior branch of the Geraldine family in [[Munster]], first created in 1329. The Earls of Desmond ruled over a vast palatinate encompassing much of modern County [[Limerick]], County Kerry, and County [[Cork]], exercising near-sovereign authority that repeatedly brought them into conflict with the English Crown. The most notable holders of the title include [[earl-of-kildare|Gerald FitzGerald]], the 3rd Earl (known as "Gerald the Poet"), James FitzGerald, the 7th Earl, and Thomas FitzGerald, the 8th Earl, whose rebellion against [[Henry II|Henrician]] reforms led to the first Desmond Rebellion. The title was ultimately forfeited after the disastrous second Desmond Rebellion (1579–1583), which resulted in the devastation of Munster and the subsequent [[Plantation of Munster]].

## Origins and Creation

The Desmond Geraldines traced their descent from Maurice FitzGerald, a companion of [[Strongbow]] who participated in the [[Norman Invasion of Ireland]] in 1169. The title "Earl of Desmond" was created in 1329 when Maurice FitzThomas FitzGerald was elevated to the peerage by King Edward III, becoming the 1st Earl. The term "Desmond" derives from the Irish _Deasmhumhain_ (South [[Munster]]), reflecting the family's territorial base in the southern province.

> "The first Earl of Kildare, draws our attention naturally to the elder branch of the same house—the Earls of Desmond." (031-176)

## The Fourth Earl: Gerald the Justiciar

[[earl-of-kildare|Gerald FitzGerald]], the 4th Earl of Desmond (d. 1398), served as Justiciar of Ireland and carried forward the family's expansion:

> "Leaving to Gerald, fourth Earl of Desmond, the task of carrying it into execution." (040-116)

His tenure as Justiciar demonstrated the dual role the Earls often played—both as agents of the Crown and as defenders of their own territorial interests. As McGee notes:

> "The Earl of Desmond, fresh from his Justiceship in Ireland." (040-197)

## The Eighth Earl and the Tudor Crisis

Thomas FitzGerald, the 8th Earl of Desmond, ruled during the transformative reign of [[henry-viii|Henry VIII]]. He and his ally, the [[Earl of Kildare]], were targeted by the Tudor Crown's centralizing policies:

> "Ormond, and Thomas, eighth Earl of Desmond, were invited to stand as sponsors at the baptism of the young Prince Edward." (044-118)

Despite such gestures of inclusion, pressure mounted:

> "Thomas, eighth Earl of Desmond, for his services to the House of Lancaster." (044-268)

## The Final Earls and the Desmond Rebellions

The 10th Earl, Maurice FitzGerald, led the family during the first Desmond Rebellion (1569–1573):

> "Was joined by Maurice, tenth Earl of Desmond, the Lord Barry, and other Munster lords." (045-203)

The second Desmond Rebellion (1579–1583), led by [[earl-of-kildare|Gerald FitzGerald]], the 15th Earl, resulted in the complete destruction of the Desmond patrimony. As described in the chronicles:

> "The Earl of Desmond and his relatives—the Powers, Barrys, Roches." (048-083)
