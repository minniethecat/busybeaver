## Overview

The mesa—from the Spanish word for "table"—is a characteristic landform of the American South[[the-west|west]]: a flat-topped hill with steep sides, standing above the surrounding plain like a natural fortress. In the poetry of the [[the-cattle-trail|cattle trail]], the mesa appears as a landmark of the range, a place of movement and outlook.

Hoofbeats "poundin' down the mesa" (032-001) capture the sound and speed of horses crossing this elevated ground, while the [[cowboy-culture|cowboy]] whose "home, the boundless mesa" (073-001) defines him speaks to the mesa as more than geography—it is identity, the platform from which a man surveys all that he ranges over.
