## Overview

The prairie is the ground beneath every cattle trail poem. Appearing in 10 passages with that specific name, and implicitly present in nearly every poem, the prairie is the anthology's foundational landscape. "Ho! wind of the far, far prairies!" (071-001) captures its defining quality: distance. The prairie is "far, far"—a landscape defined by what it lacks (trees, buildings, boundaries) rather than what it contains.

Unlike the desert (hostile) or mountains (distant), the prairie is the cowboy's intimate acquaintance. He rides across it daily, sleeps on it nightly, and knows its every variation—the way grass moves in wind, the way light changes across its surface, the way sound carries across its unbroken expanse. The prairie is home.

## Examples

### Prairie as Freedom

The prairie is freedom's physical form:

> "Ho! wind of the far, far prairies!
> Free as the waves of the sea!" (071-001)

The prairie-sea metaphor recurs throughout the anthology, collapsing land into ocean and the cowboy into a mariner on a grass sea.

### Prairie as Silence

The prairie's vastness produces its defining acoustic quality:

The silence of the prairie is not absence of sound but a quality of space—sound travels farther and means differently on the open plain.

### Prairie and Sky

[[prairie|Prairie]] and sky form the anthology's master visual composition:

> "But sky and plain, my dreams and me" (067-001)

The prairie provides the horizontal, the sky the vertical, and the cowboy the solitary vertical figure—the only thing standing between earth and heaven.

## Poetic Function

[[prairie|Prairie]] imagery fulfills three roles:

1. **Foundational Setting** — The prairie is where everything happens; it is the stage on which all other imagery performs.

2. **[[freedom|Freedom]]'s [[places-index|Geography]]** — The unbroken prairie is freedom made visible—no fences, no walls, no limits.

3. **Scale Device** — The prairie's vastness establishes the cowboy's relationship to space: he is small but not insignificant, solitary but not lost.
