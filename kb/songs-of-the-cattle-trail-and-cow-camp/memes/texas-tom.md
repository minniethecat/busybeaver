## Overview

Texas Tom appears in 7 passages across the anthology, functioning as both character and archetype. He is the storytelling cowboy, the yarn-spinner whose tales of [[love|romance]] and adventure entertain his companions around the campfire. In one notable passage, "Texas Tom was out / Takin' of a moonlight walk" (022-001), Tom's courtship becomes the occasion for a comic narrative that reveals cowboy attitudes toward [[love]], [[home]], and the tension between trail life and domesticity.

Tom represents the oral tradition that the anthology itself preserves—he is the singer inside the songs, the cowboy who turns experience into story. His [[texas|Texas]] origin marks him as a figure of the southern trail, distinct from the [[montana|Montana]] or Wyoming cowboys.

> Funny how it come about!
> Me and Texas Tom was out
> Takin' of a moonlight walk,
> Fillin' in the time with talk.
> Every star up in the sky
> Seemed to wink as we went by.
>
> (022-001)

## Character Type

Texas Tom embodies the "yarning" cowboy—the one who transforms the day's events into entertainment for the night's campfire. His stories blend truth and exaggeration in the tradition of Western [[cowboy-humor|tall tales]], and his courtship narratives in particular reveal the cowboy's complex relationship with women, domesticity, and the settled life that the trail excludes.
