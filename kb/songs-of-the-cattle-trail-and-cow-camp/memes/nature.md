## Overview

The vast, indifferent, and beautiful natural world of the cattle trail—prairie, mountain, river, and sky as both adversary and companion.

The theme appears across multiple parts of the anthology, surfacing in both the action-driven poems of Part I and the reflective verses of Parts II and III (072-001, P02-001, 003-001).

## Key Passages

> "Where nature's handiwork lies in view" (072-001)
> "every wind careening / Seems here to breathe a song" [P02-001]

## Significance

This theme contributes to the anthology's broader portrait of cowboy life, intersecting with related themes such as [[the-west]], [[night-imagery]], [[stars-imagery]]. The recurrence of nature and the natural world across poems by different authors suggests it is not merely one poet's preoccupation but a shared cultural concern of the cattle trail era.
