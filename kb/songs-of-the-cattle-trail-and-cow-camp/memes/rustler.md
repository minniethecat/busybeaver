## Overview

A **rustler** was a cattle thief—a man who made his living by cutting out unbranded calves (or altering [[branding|brands]] on branded stock) and driving them to his own range or to a crooked buyer. In the vast, unfenced West where herds wandered for months without oversight, rustling was both a constant temptation and, to the large ranchers, a capital offense. The tension between the cowboy's code of honor and the rustler's opportunism runs through much of Western narrative.

Lomax's collection captures the rustler in a single compressed line of [[the-west|frontier]] biography: "He was outlaw, rustler, killer—so the border whispered low" (004-001). The three terms blur together, and the verb "whispered" suggests that the line between fact and reputation, between justice and gossip, was itself a [[the-west|frontier]] territory.
