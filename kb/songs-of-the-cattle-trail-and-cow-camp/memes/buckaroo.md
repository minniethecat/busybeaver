## Overview

A [[buckaroo]] is the cowboy of the American West, the term itself an English rendering of the Spanish *vaquero* (cow-herder). In the poetry of the cattle trail, the [[buckaroo]] appears in two guises: as the envious onlooker watching a skilled rider tear across the range—"Every [[buckaroo]] that sees her / Tearin' cross the range astride / Has some mighty jealous feelin's" (032-001)—and as the rider himself, struggling to land "a-straddle / Like a woolly [[buckaroo]]" as the horse beneath him bucks and twists (042-001). The word carries the full weight of the cowboy's self-image: rough, resilient, and defined by the contest between man and horse.
