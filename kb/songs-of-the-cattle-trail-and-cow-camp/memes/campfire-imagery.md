## Overview

The campfire as the cowboy's hearth on the open [[prairie]]—a center of warmth, story, song, and the temporary community of the trail crew.

## Examples

Campfire Imagery appears across all three parts of the anthology, woven into narratives of [[trail-driving]], night watching, and campfire gatherings. The poets—many of them working cowboys themselves—drew on direct sensory experience, giving their imagery an authenticity that distinguishes these verses from the romanticized Western poetry of Eastern writers.

## Poetic Function

Campfire Imagery serves multiple functions in the anthology: it roots the poems in the cowboy's immediate physical world, it creates atmosphere and mood, and it transforms the mundane details of trail life into vehicles for emotional and philosophical reflection.
