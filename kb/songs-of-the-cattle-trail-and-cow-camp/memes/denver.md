## Overview

Denver appears as the quintessential cowboy town—a place of arrival after long months on the trail, where the cowboy's world briefly intersects with civilization's pleasures. The city figures in three distinct poems across the collection, each showing a different face: the dance-hall town, the [[outlaw]]'s refuge, and the carnival city where range skills find no purchase.

## In the Poems

### A High-Toned Dance (Chapter 47)

In "The Bronc That Wouldn't Bust," the speaker recalls arriving in Denver and being swept into town life: "When I happened into Denver and was asked to take a prance / In the smooth and easy mazes of a high-toned dance" (047-001). The poem juxtaposes the cowboy's rough trail skills with the formal dance floor, where "squinters" (eyes) watch with "astonished sort of play" (047-001) as the trail hand navigates an unfamiliar social terrain.

### Denver Jim, the Outlaw (Chapter 63)

"The [[outlaw|Outlaw]]" centers on [[denver-jim|Denver Jim]], a fugitive whose name ties his identity to the city: "If it ain't [[denver-jim|Denver Jim]] that's corralled here at last" (063-001). The posse's pursuit converges on Denver itself: "Come boys, up and mount and to Denver away" (063-001). When cornered, [[denver-jim|Denver Jim]] faces a [[the-west|frontier]] verdict—"You have got to the end of your [[lasso|rope]], [[denver-jim|Denver Jim]]" (063-001)—a phrase that plays on both the hangman's [[lasso|rope]] and the trail [[lasso|rope]] that defines cowboy life.

### The Carnival Cowboy (Chapter 68)

"The Bandit's Grave" places the cowboy in Denver's urban carnival setting, where range-hardened men are "Rounded up right here in Denver, runnin' on new sort o' feed" (068-001). Displaced from [[prairie]] to Midway, they grow "restless an' oneasy, like they threatened to stampede" (068-001)—the cowboy's instinctual response to confinement, whether in a [[corral]] or a carnival.

> When I happened into Denver and was asked to take a prance
> In the smooth and easy mazes of a high-toned dance.
>
> (047-001)
