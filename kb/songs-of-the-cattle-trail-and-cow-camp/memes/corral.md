## Overview

The corral is the gravitational center of the cowboy's working world—a place where cattle are gathered, sorted, branded, and claimed. In the verse collected by [[john-a-lomax|John A. Lomax]], the corral serves as far more than a practical enclosure: it is a stage for courtship, a proving ground for courage, and a metaphor for the boundaries between the wild and the domestic.

## In the Anthology

Across the poems of *Songs of the Cattle Trail and Cow Camp*, the corral appears as a site of both work and emotional significance.

The cowboy's [[homesickness|longing for home]] is often expressed in terms of the corral—the place where the trail drive ends and belonging begins. In one of the collection's most mythic visions, the cowboy imagines paradise itself as a vast celestial corral:

> With Saturn's rings for our home corral and the Dipper our water hole
>
> (009-001)

The image elevates the humble cattle pen to cosmic scale, suggesting that even in dreams of the afterlife, the cowboy's frame of reference is the working landscape he knows.

The corral is also a place of courtship and domestic ritual. One speaker promises to "open the little home corral" (021-001) as a gesture of welcome and intimacy. Another poem captures a moment of romantic possibility:

> She stands there a-smilin' / At the bars of the corral.
>
> (032-001)

Yet the corral is not always a place of warmth. It can become a theater of confrontation, as when two rivals meet "in the dust of that bare corral" (043-001), knowing that only one may leave with his pride intact. The same enclosure that holds cattle can hold men in their grudges.

## Cultural Significance

The corral bridges the working life of the cowboy and his emotional world. To "corral" cattle—or a man, as when [[denver-jim|Denver Jim]] is "corralled here at last" (063-001)—is to impose order on the wild. The act represents the cowboy's mastery over nature, but the poets of the anthology never let the reader forget that the bars of the corral work both ways: they hold things in, but they also hold things out.
