## Overview

In the language of the cattle trail, a **cinch** is first and foremost the broad strap—usually of horsehair or mohair—passed under a [[horse-imagery|horse]]'s belly and tightened to hold the saddle in place. The action of tightening it gives the [[cowboy-culture|cowboy]] idiom its texture: "he swells to beat the nation / When you're cinchin' up the slack" (042-001), a description of a horse that puffs itself up to resist the tightening girth. From this literal meaning, the word broadened into Western slang for any certainty or easy prospect—"you've a cinch on happiness" (026-001)—a guarantee as firm as a well-cinched saddle. In a single word, the practical and the philosophical meet on the [[the-cattle-trail|trail]].
