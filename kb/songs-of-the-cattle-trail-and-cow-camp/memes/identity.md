## Overview

The cowboy's ongoing project of self-definition—what it means to be a cowboy, to belong to the West, and to live by an unwritten code.

The theme appears across multiple parts of the anthology, surfacing in both the action-driven poems of Part I and the reflective verses of Parts II and III (003-001, 006-001, 067-001).

## Key Passages

> "Where there's more of singing and less of sighing" (003-001)
> "For a man to break his pride" (006-001)

## Significance

This theme contributes to the anthology's broader portrait of cowboy life, intersecting with related themes such as [[the-cowboy-code]], [[cowboy-culture]], [[the-west]]. The recurrence of identity and self-definition across poems by different authors suggests it is not merely one poet's preoccupation but a shared cultural concern of the cattle trail era.
