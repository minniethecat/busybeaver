## Overview

The cowboy's code of courage—facing stampedes, storms, and hostile encounters without flinching, as celebrated throughout the anthology.

The theme appears across multiple parts of the anthology, surfacing in both the action-driven poems of Part I and the reflective verses of Parts II and III (009-001, 070-001, 006-001).

## Key Passages

> "courage that never tires" (009-001)
> "He saw the gleam of a big head-light" (070-001)

## Significance

This theme contributes to the anthology's broader portrait of cowboy life, intersecting with related themes such as [[danger]], [[cowboy-culture]], [[the-cowboy-code]]. The recurrence of courage and bravery across poems by different authors suggests it is not merely one poet's preoccupation but a shared cultural concern of the cattle trail era.
