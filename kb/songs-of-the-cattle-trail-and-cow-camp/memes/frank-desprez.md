# Frank Desprez

Frank Desprez (1853–1916) was an English-born poet and journalist whose ballad "[[lasca|Lasca]]" became one of the most distinctive poems in [[john-a-lomax|John A. Lomax]]'s anthology. Unlike the comic cowboy yarns and anthemic declarations that dominate the collection, "[[lasca|Lasca]]" is a tragic [[love|romance]] set along the [[rio-grande|Rio Grande]] borderlands — a story of love and violent [[death-imagery|death]] that brings an operatic register to the anthology [010].

## Life & Context

Desprez was born in Bristol, England, and worked as a journalist and theatrical writer. He is best known for "[[lasca|Lasca]]," a narrative poem that tells the story of a [[texas|Texas]] cowboy's love for a Mexican woman and her [[death-imagery|death]] in a [[stampede|cattle stampede]]. The poem was originally published in a London periodical in the 1880s and was set to music in various arrangements. Its inclusion in the Lomax anthology reflects the collection's reach beyond purely American sources — Desprez's English perspective on the Texan borderlands gave the poem a romantic distance that distinguished it from the rough-hewn authenticity of the cowboy-composed songs.

| Poem | PN Range | Type |
|------|----------|------|
| "[[lasca|Lasca]]" | (010-001) | narrative/tragic ballad |

"[[lasca|Lasca]]" occupies a distinctive position in the anthology's character gallery. Unlike the comic figures of cowboy yarns such as [[Snagtooth-Sal]] or "[[texas-tom|Texas Tom]]," [[lasca|Lasca]]'s story is tragic and romantic in the high literary sense — closer to the tradition of *Carmen* or other operatic borderland heroines. Her [[death-imagery|death]] in the stampede makes her one of the few characters in the collection whose fate is not humorous but genuinely mournful.

> I want free life, and I want fresh air;
> And I sigh for the canter after the cattle,
> The crack of the whips like shots in battle,
> The medley of hoofs and horns and heads
> That wars and wrangles and scatters and spreads;
> The green beneath and the blue above,
> And dash and danger, and life and love—
>
> (010-001)

## Significance

Desprez represents an unusual figure in the Lomax collection: the foreign-born poet whose vision of the American West was shaped by literary imagination rather than lived experience. His "[[lasca|Lasca]]" adds a register of tragic seriousness to an anthology otherwise dominated by the comic, the sentimental, and the celebratory. The poem's endurance — it was still being recited and set to music decades after its composition — testifies to its hold on the Western imagination.
