## Overview

A **[[latigo]]** (from the Spanish *látigo*, meaning whip or strap) is the broad leather strap that connects the [[cinch]] ring to the saddle's rigging. Passed through the cinch ring and back up through the saddle's rigging dee multiple times, the [[latigo]] is what allows a rider to tighten the cinch and secure the saddle to the horse. Without it, the finest saddle is useless.

In Lomax's collection, the [[latigo]] appears in a moment of sensory precision: "singing like a banjo string / And the latigoes creak and strain" (060-001). The image aligns the leather strap with a musical instrument—the creak of the [[latigo]] as the horse moves becomes part of the soundtrack of the trail, as essential to the cowboy's experience as the creak of saddle leather or the jingle of [[spurs]].
