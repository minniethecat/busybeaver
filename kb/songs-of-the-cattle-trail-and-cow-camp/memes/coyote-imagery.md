## Overview

The coyote appears in 17 passages throughout [[Songs-of-the-Cattle-Trail-and-Cow-Camp|*Songs of the Cattle Trail and Cow Camp*]], making it the most frequently mentioned animal in the anthology after the horse. Unlike the horse—a tool and partner—the coyote is pure wildness: an untamed presence whose howl defines the auditory landscape of the night range. "The coyotes sing their lullaby" (002-001) is among the anthology's most evocative lines, transforming a predator's call into a form of music.

The coyote's symbolic function is complex. It is a competitor (preying on calves), a companion (its howl filling the silence of the night watch), and a mirror (a fellow creature living on the margins of settlement). The cowboy and the coyote share the night, and the coyote's voice gives the darkness a language the cowboy understands.

## Examples

### Coyote as Musician

The coyote's howl is consistently framed as song:

> "And the coyotes sing their lullaby" (002-001)

This metaphor elevates the coyote from nuisance to participant in the range's musical life. The coyote's "song" parallels the cowboy's own singing—both are voices in the wilderness.

### Coyote as Night's Voice

The coyote's howl is the defining sound of night on the range:

> "The coyote's howl is heard at dark from some
> Low-lying hill" (066-001)

The howl emerges from a specific location ("low-lying hill") but fills the entire auditory space, a pinpoint source with vast effect—much like the cowboy himself.

### Coyote and Silence

The coyote's call often punctuates silence rather than breaking it:

> "There hain't a stir. Less it's a night-hawk's sudden whir,
> Or cottonwoods a-whisperin'" (026-001)

Even when the coyote isn't explicitly named, the anticipation of its howl haunts the night scenes, the silence defined by the sound everyone expects.

## Poetic Function

Coyote imagery serves three functions:

1. **Auditory Landscape** — The coyote's howl is the range's soundtrack, establishing place through sound rather than sight.

2. **Wildness Emblem** — Unlike cattle (domesticated) or horses (tamed), the coyote remains genuinely wild, a reminder that the cowboy inhabits nature's domain, not the other way around.

3. **Companion in Solitude** — The howl fills the silence of the night watch, making [[loneliness|solitude]] bearable by populating it with another living presence.
