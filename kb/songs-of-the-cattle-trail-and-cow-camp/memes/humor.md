## Overview

The comic tradition in cowboy poetry—[[cowboy-humor|tall tales]], practical jokes, self-deprecation, and the humor that made trail life bearable.

The theme appears across multiple parts of the anthology, surfacing in both the action-driven poems of Part I and the reflective verses of Parts II and III (046-001, 008-001, 011-001).

## Key Passages

> "Wrangle up your mouth-harps, drag your banjo out" (046-001)
> "broncho versus bicycle race" (008-001)

## Significance

This theme contributes to the anthology's broader portrait of cowboy life, intersecting with related themes such as [[cowboy-humor]], [[singing-and-songmaking]], [[cowboy-culture]]. The recurrence of humor and comedy across poems by different authors suggests it is not merely one poet's preoccupation but a shared cultural concern of the cattle trail era.
