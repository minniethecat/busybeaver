## Overview

In the language of the cattle trail, a maverick is an unbranded calf—free for the taking by whoever can [[lasso|rope]] and mark it first. The term, which originates from the [[texas|Texas]] cattleman Samuel Maverick who refused to brand his stock, came to describe any creature that walks outside the bounds of ownership. In the poems collected by Lomax, [[the-maverick|the maverick]] carries this double meaning: a practical challenge for the working cowboy, and a metaphor for wildness, independence, and the untamed spirit.

## In the Anthology

The maverick appears in the poems both as literal livestock and as a figure of speech for the outsider. [[high-chin-bob|High Chin Bob]], one of the anthology's memorable rogues, is described with a [[lasso|rope]] "of sinful pride and maverick-hungry" (013-001)—the phrase capturing the eagerness with which a cowboy pursues the unclaimed.

The practical work of the [[the-round-up|roundup]] centers on the maverick's transformation from wild creature to property. "He knows all the tricks when he brands mavericks" (053-001) describes the skill required—a maverick is not merely [[lasso|rope]]d but understood, its evasions anticipated. The verb "to maverick" even enters the language of the trail as a term for wandering beyond one's proper bounds: one character is scolded to "quit a-playin' maverick" (028-001), as though wildness were a choice that could be set aside.

The maverick also carries a note of elegy. In a poem reflecting on the closing of the [[the-west|frontier]], the speaker mourns:

> No more the mavericks, winding stream of horns
> To Kansas bound
>
> (066-001)

Here the maverick becomes a symbol not of defiance but of loss—the great herds, like the open range itself, belong to a world that is passing.

## Cultural Significance

The maverick embodies a central tension in [[cowboy-culture|cowboy culture]]: the simultaneous desire to master the wild and to preserve it. [[branding|Branding]] a maverick is an act of possession, yet the poets of this anthology recognize that something essential is lost when every unbranded creature has been claimed. The maverick represents the horizon of freedom that the cowboy both chases and consumes.
