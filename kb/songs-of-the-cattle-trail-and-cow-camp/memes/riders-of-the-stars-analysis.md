## Overview

"[[Riders-of-the-Stars|Riders of the Stars]]," by [[Henry-Herbert-Knibbs]], is one of the anthology's most ambitious poems — a work that elevates the cowboy from trail hand to cosmic rider, his herd not cattle but constellations, his range not the [[texas|Texas]] plains but the night sky itself.

> At a round-up on the Gila One sweet morning (006-001)

Though set at a literal [[round-up]] on the [[gila-river|Gila River]], the poem transforms its physical setting into a spiritual landscape. The cowboy's work becomes a metaphor for a larger journey: the [[round-up]] of the stars, the trail drive across the heavens.

## Cosmic Imagery

The poem's central conceit — the cowboy as rider among the stars — draws on the anthology's recurring cosmic imagery. Stars, constellations, and the night sky appear throughout the collection as markers of both beauty and [[loneliness|solitude]]. In "Riders of the Stars," Knibbs pushes this imagery to its logical extreme: the cowboy does not merely ride beneath the stars; he rides among them.

> My glory sings in the toil they crave (009-001)

The poem's conflation of earthly labor and celestial glory is a key move in cowboy poetics: the assertion that the cowboy's work, however humble, participates in something cosmic. This is the cowboy sublime — the moment when trail dust becomes stardust.

## Knibbs's Vision

[[henry-herbert-knibbs|Henry Herbert Knibbs]], a Canadian-born writer who contributed multiple poems to the anthology, was drawn to the visionary potential of Western imagery. His poems often locate spiritual meaning in physical labor — the cowboy not as brute but as mystic, the trail not as commerce but as pilgrimage. "Riders of the Stars" is his fullest expression of this tendency.

## Thematic Connections

The poem connects to several of the anthology's major thematic threads:

- **The [[west]] as spiritual destination** — the West is not merely a region but a state of being
- **[[stars-imagery]]** — the night sky as the cowboy's ultimate companion
- **[[riding-imagery]]** — [[riding-imagery|riding]] as the fundamental mode of cowboy existence
