## Overview

Austin, the capital of [[texas|Texas]], appears in the front matter of *Songs of the Cattle Trail and Cow Camp* not as a setting for cowboy verse but as the editorial origin point of the collection itself. The Introduction closes with the signature line "[[john-a-lomax|J. A. L.]] / The University of [[texas|Texas]], / Austin, July 9, 1919" (P03-003), placing the anthology's compilation squarely within the academic environment of the University of [[texas|Texas]] at Austin.

[[john-a-lomax|John A. Lomax]], the volume's editor, was a pioneering folklorist and a product of the University of [[texas|Texas]]. His work collecting [[singing-and-songmaking|cowboy songs]] began during his time as a Traveling Fellow from Harvard University, but the preface's Austin dateline roots the finished book in [[texas|Texas]] soil—a fitting origin for a collection devoted to the songs of the cattle trail.

## In the Poems

Austin is referenced in a single, formal context—the editorial colophon of the Introduction. Lomax's closing lines identify his institutional home:

> "J. A. L.
> The University of Texas,
> Austin, July 9, 1919." (P03-003)

Though not a subject of cowboy verse, Austin's presence in the book's paratext connects the scholarly labor of folk song collection to the broader cattle-trail geography that the poems themselves map. The University of [[texas|Texas]], situated in the state capital, was a natural institutional home for the collecting and publishing of [[texas|Texas]]-centered cowboy lore.
