## Overview

The cowboy's solitary night watch over the herd—a ritual of vigilance, song, and communion with the dark [[prairie]].

As depicted throughout *Songs of the Cattle Trail and Cow Camp*, the night guard was an integral part of the cowboy's world—not merely a practical necessity but a source of identity, pride, and poetic inspiration.

## In the Anthology

The poems collected by [[john-a-lomax|John A. Lomax]] capture the night guard as lived experience. The night guard's [[loneliness|solitude]] is vividly rendered: "I'm night guard all alone tonight, Dead homes" (033-001), opening a poem that uses the [[loneliness|isolation]] of the watch as a setting for reflection on home, loss, and the cowboy's wandering life.

> I'm night guard all alone tonight, Dead homes...
>
> (033-001)

## Cultural Significance

The Night Guard represents more than a practical facet of trail life; it embodies the values and self-image of the cowboy. The anthology preserves these details at the moment when the open-range era was fading, making it not only literature but a document of a vanished way of life.
