## Overview

Riding is the fundamental action of [[cowboy-culture|cowboy]] life, and the anthology's poets return to it constantly — riding the sunset rounds (009-001), riding the range, riding herd. The motion of the horse becomes a metaphor for [[freedom]], control, and the cowboy's relationship with the natural world.

## Examples

The image of [[riders-imagery|riders]] in motion appears throughout: "riding the sunset rounds, Hunting each other among the stars" (009-001), and "I'm not a-riding airships Nor an electric flying machine" (044-001) — contrasting the traditional cowboy's saddle with modern mechanization.

## Poetic Function

Riding imagery establishes physical authenticity, conveys the rhythm and freedom of trail life, and serves as a symbol of the cowboy's mastery over his environment and his partnership with the horse.
