## Overview

Sage appears in 13 passages throughout the anthology, making it the most frequently named plant. More than visual scenery, sage provides the range's characteristic scent — a pungent, aromatic presence that defines the Western landscape in olfactory terms. For the cowboy, sage is the smell of home, of the open range, of a world defined by what grows wild rather than what is cultivated.

> I know every foot o' sage brush Clear to the southern line (014-001)

Sage's symbolic role is distinctive: unlike mountains (distant and visual) or wind (invisible and tactile), sage is intimate and immediate. It brushes against the rider's legs, its scent rises when crushed under hoof, and its gray-green color carpets the plains. The plant's hardiness — thriving in arid conditions that defeat other vegetation — mirrors the cowboy's own resilience.

> Out there in the sagebrush A-harkin' to the "Coo-oo" (038-001)

## Examples

### Sage as Landscape Carpet

Sage defines the visual texture of the plains. The gray-green of sagebrush is the range's default color — the background against which other images (cattle, [[riders-imagery|riders]], storms) are composed.

> All day across the sagebrush flat, Beneath the sun of June (067-001)

### Sage as Scent

The plant's olfactory presence is its strongest contribution. Sage scent carries memories and emotions for the cowboy — it is the smell of the trail, of morning, of the range itself. More than any visual image, the scent of sage is what returning cowboys associate with "out there."

> Where the grizzly dwells And sage brush grows (072-001)

### Sage and Solitude

Sage often appears in passages emphasizing the range's emptiness. The plant's ubiquity in sparse landscapes makes it a marker of the cowboy's [[loneliness|isolation]] — endless sage means endless distance from human settlement.

> For a sight of the sage grass gray, For the dazzling sun (071-001)

## Poetic Function

Sage imagery serves three purposes:

1. **Olfactory Signature** — Sage provides the range's scent profile, making the Western landscape sensorily specific rather than generic. The sage thrush's evening song (065-001) combines sound with the plant's ambient presence.

2. **Resilience Metaphor** — The plant's ability to thrive in harsh conditions parallels the cowboy's own hardiness, reflected in the persistent recurrence of sagebrush across the anthology's landscapes (073-001).

3. **Place Marker** — Sage defines "out there" in botanical terms; its presence signals genuine wildness rather than settled land, as in the sagehen hunting scenes (047-001) (049-001) (050-001) that frame the plant within cowboy recreation.
