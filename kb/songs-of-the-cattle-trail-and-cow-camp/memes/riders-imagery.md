## Overview

The riders of the cattle trail poems are rendered in heroic and mythic terms — "the last of all rough riders, and the best" (006-001), "bow-legged boys in leathern breeches" (009-001). The anthology transforms working [[cowboy-culture|cowboys]] into legendary figures through its rider imagery.

## Examples

"Ten thousand riders marched" down the Golden Street (009-001), and the first-person voice declares himself "the last of all rough riders, and the best" (006-001). These images blend cowboy authenticity with epic scale, drawing parallels between the cattle trail and classical heroism.

## Poetic Function

Riders imagery elevates the cowboy from laborer to legend, creates a sense of fraternity among the men of the trail, and connects the American [[the-west|West]] to timeless traditions of mounted warriors and nomadic horsemen.
