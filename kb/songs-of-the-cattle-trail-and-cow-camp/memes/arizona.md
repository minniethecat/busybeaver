## Overview

Arizona Territory occupies the southwestern edge of the cattle trail world depicted in *Songs of the Cattle Trail and Cow Camp*. Though less prominent than [[texas|Texas]] or [[montana|Montana]], Arizona's desert ranges and canyon country form part of the broader Western geography that shapes the anthology's imagination.

## In the Anthology

While Arizona does not host a dedicated poem in the collection, it appears as a reference point in the Western landscape—a territory associated with the southern ranges, Mexican cultural influence, and the arid conditions that defined so much of the cowboy's working world.

## Geographic Context

During the open-range era (1860s–1890s), Arizona was still a territory (statehood came in 1912, just a few years before Lomax's anthology was published in 1916). Its [[prairie-imagery|grasslands]] supported cattle ranching, and its border with Mexico made it a crossroads of Anglo and Mexican cowboy traditions.

## Significance

Arizona represents the southern and [[the-west|west]]ern periphery of the cattle trail world—a reminder that the cowboy's geography extended beyond the central [[texas|Texas]]-to-[[montana|Montana]] axis into the desert South[[the-west|west]].
