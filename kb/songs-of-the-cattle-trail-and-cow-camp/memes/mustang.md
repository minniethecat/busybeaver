## Overview

The **[[mustang]]**—from the Spanish *mestengo* (stray, ownerless)—is the feral horse of the North American West, descended from animals brought by Spanish conquistadors in the 16th century. By the era of the cattle trail, vast herds of mustangs roamed the Great Plains, and the cowboy who could break one to saddle had earned a mount of legendary toughness and sure-footedness.

In Lomax's collection, the [[mustang]] appears in a single, tender image of partnership: "I used to ride / On a mouse-grey [[mustang]] close to my side, / With blue sarcenet eyes" (010-001). The detail—a specific color, a particular closeness—elevates the generic range horse to a named companion, capturing the bond between rider and mount that runs through all cowboy verse.
