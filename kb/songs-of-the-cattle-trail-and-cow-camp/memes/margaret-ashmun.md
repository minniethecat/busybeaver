## Overview

Margaret Ashmun is a poet whose contribution to the [[John-A-Lomax|Lomax]] anthology is "The Vigilantes" (ch064), a poem that celebrates [[the-west|frontier]] vigilante justice in elevated, almost mythic language. Her poem casts the vigilantes as agents of cosmic order — "whirlwinds that winnow the West" — and is notable as one of the few poems in the collection by a female poet.

## Literary Context

"The Vigilantes" stands out in the Lomax anthology for its unapologetic embrace of extralegal [[the-west|frontier]] justice. The poem opens with a powerful declaration of identity: "We are the whirlwinds that winnow the West — / We scatter the wicked like straw!" (064-001). The vigilantes are portrayed not as a mob but as instruments of cosmic justice — "the Nemeses, never at rest" who embody "Justice, and Right, and the Law" (064-001).

Ashmun's use of the first-person plural ("We are") aligns the reader with the vigilantes' perspective, creating an immersive rhetoric of collective moral purpose. The imagery of winnowing — separating wheat from chaff — suggests a purification of the [[the-west|frontier]], with the vigilantes as the instrument of that purification. The phrase "Nemeses, never at rest" elevates the vigilantes from mere [[the-west|frontier]] enforcers to agents of an almost Greek-tragic conception of inexorable justice.

The poem's setting — "Moon on the snow and a blood-chilling blast" — places the vigilantes' work in a harsh, unforgiving landscape that mirrors the harshness of their mission. The [[snow-imagery|winter imagery]] reinforces the poem's moral severity: this is not the sunlit West of cattle drives and campfire songs, but a landscape of judgment.

As one of the few identified female poets in the Lomax anthology, Ashmun's presence is significant. Her choice of subject — vigilante justice, typically coded as masculine — demonstrates that women poets in the cowboy tradition did not confine themselves to domestic or sentimental themes.

> We are the whirlwinds that winnow the West—
> We scatter the wicked like straw!
> We are the Nemeses, never at rest—
> We are Justice, and Right, and the Law!
> Moon on the snow and a blood-chilling blast,
> Sharp-throbbing hoofs like the heart-beat of fear,
> A halt, a swift parley, a pause—then at last
> A stiff, swinging figure cut darkly and sheer
> Against the blue steel of the sky.
>
> (064-001)
