## Overview

A **[[dogie]]** (also spelled *dogey*) is a motherless or orphaned calf on the open range. The word's origin is uncertain—some trace it to the Spanish *dogal* (a [[lasso|rope]] or halter), others to the cowboy's observation that a calf who has lost its mother survives on "dough-guts" (milk-swollen belly giving way to grass-filled leanness). Whatever its etymology, the [[dogie]] became one of the most enduring terms in Western speech, immortalized in the traditional cowboy song "Git Along, Little Dogies."

In Lomax's collection, the term appears in a single, resonant phrase: "Not crowding the [[dogie]] stars too much on their way to Mars" (009-001)—a line that uses "[[dogie]] stars" as a colloquial image for the celestial trail the cowboy's charges seem to follow, blending the mundane work of [[trail-driving|cattle driving]] with the cosmic scale of the [[sky-imagery|Western sky]].
