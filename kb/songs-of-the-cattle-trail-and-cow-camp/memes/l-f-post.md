## Overview

L. F. Post is the co-author, with [[glenn-norton|Glenn Norton]], of "The Gila Monster Route" (ch070), one of the longer narrative poems in the [[John-A-Lomax|Lomax]] anthology. The poem tells the story of a "ding-bat" (tramp or hobo) stranded in the desert after being thrown off a freight train, capturing the darker side of Western travel — not the romantic cattle drive but the desperate journey of the dispossessed.

## Literary Context

"The Gila Monster Route" is distinctive within the Lomax anthology for its subject matter: it is not a cowboy poem but a hobo poem. The protagonist is a "ding-bat" — tramp slang for a vagrant or hobo — sitting "on a rotting tie" after being "ditched by a shock and a cruel fate" (070-001). The poem's railroad setting places it in the early twentieth-century world of transcontinental freight trains rather than the nineteenth-century world of the cattle trail.

Post and Norton employ a rich vocabulary of railroad slang: "The con high-balled, and the manifest freight / Pulled out on the stem behind the mail" (070-001). This specialized language — *con* (conductor), *high-ball* (signal to proceed), *manifest freight* (fast freight), *on the stem* (on the main line) — immerses the reader in a subculture as distinct as the cowboy's, with its own codes and dangers.

The poem's title refers to [[gila-river|the Gila]] Monster Route, a desert railroad line in the American South[[the-west|west]]. The Gila monster, a venomous lizard native to the region, serves as an apt metaphor for the route itself: slow-moving but deadly, a place where the unprepared do not survive.

> The lingering sunset across the plain
> Kissed the rear-end door of an east-bound train,
> And shone on a passing track close by
> Where a ding-bat sat on a rotting tie.
> He was ditched by a shock and a cruel fate.
>
> (070-001)

Post's collaboration with [[glenn-norton|Glenn Norton]] on this poem is notable — most poems in the anthology are single-author works. The joint attribution suggests a shared experience or a deliberate partnership in crafting this narrative of railroad life.
