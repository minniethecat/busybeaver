## Overview

Mountains appear in 14 passages throughout the anthology, serving as the visual anchor of the Western landscape. Unlike the desert (which threatens) or the sky (which dwarfs), mountains provide stability—"the snow is on the mountains" (046-001) as a seasonal fact, a fixed reference point in the cowboy's mobile world.

The mountains' function in the anthology is largely structural: they define directions ("the mountains are covered with snow" (P03-001)), mark the seasons, and establish the visual scale against which human activity is measured. "Mountains blue along the skyline" (077-001) captures their role as the horizon's punctuation—distant, beautiful, and fundamentally indifferent.

## Examples

### Mountains as Seasonal Markers

Mountains track time through their snow line:

> "For the snow is on the mountains and the wind is on the plain" (046-001)

The parallel structure places mountain snow and plains wind in grammatical equality—two facts of nature that define the cowboy's season.

### Mountains as Horizon

Mountains define the visual boundary:

> "Mountains blue along the skyline—
> I don't envy anyone" (077-001)

The blue mountains are the last thing the speaker sees before declaring contentment—the visual world complete, nothing lacking.

### Mountains and Home

Mountains appear in the anthology's opening invocation of the West:

> "Where the mountains are covered with snow" (P03-001)

From the first page, mountains establish the West's defining visual feature—white peaks against [[sky-imagery|blue sky]], a landscape template that the rest of the anthology fills in.

### Mountains as Aspiration

Some passages give mountains a spiritual dimension:

> "the mountains are covered with snow" (P03-001) echoes the Romantic tradition of peaks as places of purity and transcendence, though the anthology handles this register lightly, preferring the practical to the sublime.

## Poetic Function

Mountain imagery serves three purposes:

1. **Geographic Anchor** — Mountains orient the reader (and the cowboy) within the vast Western landscape, providing direction and scale.

2. **Temporal Marker** — Snow on the mountains marks winter; their constancy contrasts with the cowboy's mobility.

3. **Beauty Beyond Utility** — Unlike cattle or trails, mountains have no practical function for the cowboy—they are pure aesthetic presence, evidence that the range contains beauty the cowboy notices even when it doesn't serve his work.
