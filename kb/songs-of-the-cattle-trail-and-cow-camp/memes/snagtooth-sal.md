# Snagtooth Sal

Snagtooth Sal is the title character and lost love of one of the most memorable laments in the [[John-A-Lomax|Lomax]] anthology — a tragicomic ballad of courtship and loss set in [[laramie|Laramie]], in which the narrator's present [[loss|grief]] over Sal's [[death-imagery|death]] is rendered with a rawness that cuts through the poem's surface humor.

## Character Background

Sal is sketched entirely through the narrator's memory. We know she was a [[laramie|Laramie]] girl — the poem begins "Walkin' down through [[laramie|Laramie]] with Snagtooth Sal" (025-001) — and that the narrator loved her with a devotion that endures past her [[death-imagery|death]]: "Sal, Sal, / My heart is broke today — / Broke in two forever when they laid you in the clay" (025-001). Her nickname, with its unromantic specificity ("Snagtooth"), is characteristic of cowboy ballad convention — the affectionate diminutive that humanizes rather than idealizes, making the [[loss|grief]] that follows more, not less, affecting.

The poem provides only fragments of Sal's character, but what emerges is a figure of simple, genuine affection. The narrator's wish — "Bury me tomorrow where the lily blossoms spring / Underneath the willows where the little robins sing" (025-001) — is a cowboy elegy in miniature, the flowers-and-birds pastoral tradition transposed to the Western graveyard.

## Key Appearances

Sal appears exclusively in the poem named for her:

| Poem | PN Range | Type |
|------|----------|------|
| "Snagtooth Sal" | (025-001)–(025-001) | lyric lament |

The poem's four stanzas move from recollection of happy youth to the present pain of loss, with a refrain ("Sal, Sal, / My heart is broke today") that reinforces the narrator's unchanging [[loss|grief]].

## Significance

Sal is one of the few female characters in the anthology who is mourned rather than courted, remembered rather than pursued. Where other poems treat romantic relationships with comic distance or sentimental convention, "Snagtooth Sal" registers genuine loss — the narrator's declaration that he "would give creation to be walkin' with my gal" (025-001) is among the most direct expressions of [[loss|grief]] in the collection. In this, Sal belongs to a small group of figures (alongside [[Lasca]]) whose presence in the anthology adds emotional depth below the comic surface of cowboy yarns and dance-hall songs.

> I was young and happy and my heart was light and gay,
> Singin', always singin' through the sunny summer day;
> Happy as a lizard in the wavin' chaparral,
> Walkin' down through Laramie with Snagtooth Sal.
> Sal, Sal,
> My heart is broke today—
> Broke in two forever when they laid you in the clay;
> I would give creation to be walkin' with my gal—
> Walkin' down through Laramie with Snagtooth Sal.
>
> (025-001)
