## Overview

Dawn appears in 3 passages across *Songs of the Cattle Trail and Cow Camp* as a liminal moment between night and day. For the [[cowboy-culture|cowboy]], dawn is the boundary between the reflective [[loneliness|solitude]] of night and the demands of the working day. It carries associations of hope, anticipation, and the resumption of trail life.

## Examples

### Dawn as Hope and Anticipation

The approaching dawn represents the promise of change:

> "almost wishing that the dawn would never find me" (004-001)

The speaker's wish for night to persist reveals dawn as the moment that ends reverie and brings reality. This tension between wanting to stay in a moment and knowing it must end is central to the cowboy's relationship with time.

### Waiting for Dawn

Dawn marks the [[the-west|frontier]] between rest and action:

> "A-waitin' for the dawn" (038-001)

The vigil through the night hours, waiting for first light, is a recurring motif. Dawn brings both relief (the long night is over) and demand (the work day begins).

### Dawn as Event Horizon

> "like lightning until the early dawn" (048-001)

Here dawn is the endpoint of sustained activity—a celebration that lasts through the night until morning breaks.
