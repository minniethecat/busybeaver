# Cathedral Service Music

## Overview

The sacred choral repertoire performed by the [[cloisterham-cathedral|Cloisterham Cathedral]] [[music|choir]] under [[john-jasper|John Jasper]]'s direction—music that provides the novel's auditory landscape and ironic counterpoint to its dark themes.

## Publication History

The circumstances of this work's creation and dissemination are integral to its significance within the world of *[[the-mystery-of-edwin-drood|The Mystery of Edwin Drood]]* and, in the case of Dickens' own novel, to literary history itself.

## Content Summary

The work's content and significance extend beyond its immediate form, engaging with the themes of [[psychological-duality|duality]], secrecy, and the incomplete that pervade the novel's fictional universe.

## Significance

This work holds a distinctive place in the literary landscape surrounding *[[the-mystery-of-edwin-drood|The Mystery of Edwin Drood]]*, contributing to the novel's rich intertextual fabric and its engagement with questions of artistic creation and completion.
