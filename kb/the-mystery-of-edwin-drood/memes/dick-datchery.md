Dick Datchery is a mysterious figure who arrives in [[cloisterham|Cloisterham]] in Chapter 18 of _[[the-mystery-of-edwin-drood|The Mystery of Edwin Drood]]_. Registering at the Crozier inn under the name "Dick Datchery" [018-005], he immediately sets about gathering information about [[cloisterham-cathedral|the Cathedral]], [[john-jasper|John Jasper]], and the town's inhabitants. His true identity, his purpose in [[cloisterham|Cloisterham]], and his connection to the central mystery of [[edwin-drood|Edwin Drood]]'s [[death|disappearance]] remain unexplained due to the novel's unfinished state.

## Arrival in Cloisterham

Datchery first appears at the Crozier inn, where he gives his name to the waiter: "Dick Datchery. Hang it up again" [018-006]. His appearance and manner are distinctive — he has unusually abundant white hair and a leisurely, gentlemanly bearing. He inquires about "anything Cathedraly" [018-008], immediately betraying his interest in [[cloisterham|Cloisterham]]'s ecclesiastical center.

After dining, Datchery asks the waiter about [[mr-tope|Mr. Tope]], [[cloisterham-cathedral|the Cathedral]] Verger, and resolves to "call on Mrs. [[mr-tope|Tope]] after dinner" [018-012]. This results in his securing lodgings at the [[mr-tope|Tope]] residence, strategically positioned to observe [[cloisterham-cathedral|the Cathedral]] close and its comings and goings. From the moment of his arrival, Datchery's actions suggest a deliberate plan rather than casual tourism [018-028].

## Interactions with Authority

Datchery's social strategy involves ingratiating himself with [[cloisterham|Cloisterham]]'s power structure. He secures an introduction to [[mr-sapsea|Mr. Sapsea]], the Mayor, through the waiter [018-034], and employs elaborate flattery to win Sapsea's favor. "His Honour the Mayor," Datchery declares, "places me under a most agreeable amount of [[duty|obligation]]" [018-050]. His obsequious manner is clearly performative — "His Honour the Mayor is more than kind and gracious" [018-064] — a calculated performance to gain access and information.

Through Sapsea, Datchery meets [[john-jasper|John Jasper]] at [[cloisterham-cathedral|the Cathedral]] [018-049]. His questioning about [[john-jasper|Jasper]] — "whether that gentleman we have just left is the gentleman of whom I have heard in the neighbourhood as being much admired" [018-066] — reveals his underlying interest in [[john-jasper|the choirmaster]]. He subsequently encounters [[durdles|Durdles]], the stonemason, absorbing local knowledge with conspicuous attentiveness [018-084].

## Investigation of Jasper

Datchery's surveillance of [[john-jasper|Jasper]] becomes explicit in Chapter 23. He is observed keeping watch on [[john-jasper|Jasper]]'s house, "rises from his papers, and comes to his window" [023-131] to monitor [[john-jasper|the choirmaster]]'s movements. The narrative follows Datchery as he tails [[john-jasper|Jasper]] through [[cloisterham|Cloisterham]]'s nighttime streets.

In a crucial encounter with [[princess-puffer|Princess Puffer]], [[princess-puffer|the opium woman]], Datchery attempts to extract information about [[john-jasper|Jasper]] while rattling coins suggestively [023-145]. His demeanor shifts from his earlier genial performance to something harder and more direct: "Datchery, with a sudden change of countenance" [023-154], presses the woman about [[john-jasper|Jasper]]'s habits. This scene reveals Datchery as an active investigator rather than a passive observer.

## Identity Theories

Datchery's true identity is one of the most debated questions in Dickens scholarship. His abundant white hair — noted repeatedly — has been interpreted as a wig, suggesting he is a known character in disguise. Common theories include:

- **[[helena-landless|Helena Landless]] in disguise**, using her theatrical training and dark complexion to pass as a white-haired gentleman
- **[[neville-landless|Neville Landless]]**, returned to investigate after [[edwin-drood|Edwin]]'s [[death|disappearance]]
- **[[mr-grewgious|Mr. Grewgious]]**, acting to protect [[rosa-bud|Rosa Bud]] and uncover [[john-jasper|Jasper]]'s crimes
- **An entirely new character** — a detective or legal agent hired by [[mr-grewgious|Grewgious]]
- **Bazzard**, [[mr-grewgious|Mr. Grewgious]]'s clerk, whose unexplained absences coincide with Datchery's appearances

The novel provides no definitive answer. Datchery's role as investigator is clear — he follows [[john-jasper|Jasper]] [023-163], questions [[princess-puffer|Princess Puffer]] [023-145], and discovers [[john-jasper|Jasper]] in "the unholy office of stoning" [[cloisterham-cathedral|the Cathedral]] monuments [023-165] — but his identity remains the novel's most tantalizing puzzle.
