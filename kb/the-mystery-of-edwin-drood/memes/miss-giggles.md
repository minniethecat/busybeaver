Miss Giggles is a minor character in Charles Dickens's unfinished novel *The Mystery of Edwin Drood*. She is a student at Miss Twinkleton's Nuns' House seminary in Cloisterham.

Miss Giggles is first mentioned when Miss Twinkleton, in the midst of a classroom address about "Rumour in Cloisterham," assigns her the task of supplying "the name and date of birth" of the Poet within half an hour [009-014]. Later that day, she becomes embroiled in a classroom fracas when Miss Ferdinand aims a water-bottle at her; Miss Giggles responds by drawing "a tablespoon in defence" [009-015].

Dickens characterises Miss Giggles as "deficient in sentiment" [013-002]. During the end-of-term festivities, while other girls exchange confidences about "golden youth of England expected to call," Miss Giggles "did indeed profess that she, for her part, acknowledged such homage by making faces at the golden youth" — a position for which "this young lady was outvoted by an immense majority" [013-002].
