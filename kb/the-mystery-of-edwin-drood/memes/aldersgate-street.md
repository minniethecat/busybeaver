# Aldersgate Street

## Overview

Aldersgate Street is a location in central London, near the General Post Office, that figures in the novel's final completed chapter. It is here that [[dick-datchery|Dick Datchery]], the mysterious investigator, takes lodgings upon his arrival in London as he pursues his investigation of [[john-jasper|John Jasper]].

## In the Novel

Datchery arrives in London carrying "his travelling baggage easily carried in his hand, and he repairs with it, on foot, to a hybrid hotel in a little square behind Aldersgate Street, near the General Post Office" [023-007]. The street's proximity to the Post Office is significant—Datchery is a man who watches and waits, and Aldersgate Street positions him within the communications hub of the city.

Later, when he returns to interrogate the opium woman about Jasper, "he repairs to the back of Aldersgate Street, where a door immediately opens to his knocking" [023-108]. The door that "immediately opens" suggests that the area behind Aldersgate Street is a place where certain kinds of transactions—information, confidences, secrets—are conducted with practiced efficiency. Datchery's repeated returns to Aldersgate Street mark the street as the geographical center of his investigative operation.

## Significance

Aldersgate Street represents the London of commerce and communication as opposed to the London of degradation represented by the opium den. It is a threshold space—neither the respectable world of [[cloisterham|Cloisterham]] nor the criminal underworld of the East End—where an investigator can operate between worlds, gathering intelligence from both.
