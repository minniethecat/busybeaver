# The Mystery of Edwin Drood

## Overview

The unfinished novel by Charles Dickens, published in six monthly installments from April to September 1870, whose interrupted composition constitutes one of literature's most famous unresolved mysteries.

## Publication History

The circumstances of this work's creation and dissemination are integral to its significance within the world of *The Mystery of Edwin Drood* and, in the case of Dickens' own novel, to literary history itself.

## Content Summary

The work's content and significance extend beyond its immediate form, engaging with the themes of [[psychological-duality|duality]], secrecy, and the incomplete that pervade the novel's fictional universe.

## Significance

This work holds a distinctive place in the literary landscape surrounding *The Mystery of Edwin Drood*, contributing to the novel's rich intertextual fabric and its engagement with questions of artistic creation and completion.
