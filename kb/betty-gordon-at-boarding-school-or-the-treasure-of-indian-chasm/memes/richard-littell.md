# Richard Littell

## Overview

Richard Littell, commonly referred to as **Mr. Littell**, is the father of [[bobby-littell|Bobby Littell]], [[libbie-littell|Libbie Littell]], and [[louise-littell|Louise Littell]]. A wealthy businessman based in [[washington-dc|Washington]], he is affectionately called "[[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]]" by [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]] and [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]], making him a second "[[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]]" alongside [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s own uncle, [[uncle-dick|Richard Gordon]]. (002-017) He plays a key role in arranging [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]]'s admission to a military academy. (003-020) (003-024)

## Role in the Story

### A Second Uncle Dick

[[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]] already had one "[[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]]" — her guardian [[uncle-dick|Richard Gordon]] — when she acquired a second: "[[betty-gordon|Betty]] acquired a second '[[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]]' in the person of Richard Littell, the father of three lively daughters." (002-017) Mr. Littell and his family became close to both [[betty-gordon|Betty]] and [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]], with the [[littell-family|Littell household]] in [[washington-dc|Washington]] serving as a warm, welcoming base for the young people.

### Arranging Bob's Education

Mr. Littell took an active interest in [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]]'s future. He wrote to [[bob-henderson|Bob]] about a military academy, and identified five boys who were [[friendship|friends]] of the school — all of whom he recommended to [[bob-henderson|Bob]]. (003-020) (003-024) [[betty-gordon|Betty]] notes that "Mr. Littell wrote me about a military academy" and that all five of the boys "Mr. Littell told me of are [[friendship|friends]]." (003-020) (003-024) Later, [[betty-gordon|Betty]] mentions the [[tucker-twins|Tucker twins]] as among "the five boys Mr. Littell wrote you about." (006-039)

### Recovery and Hospitality

Mr. Littell suffered an injury from which he "fully recovered," and by the time of the [[fairfields|Fairfields]] house party, he was once again greeting guests behind his wife. (007-020) His warm welcome to [[betty-gordon|Betty]] and [[bob-henderson|Bob]] was characteristic: "'Glad to have you two here again,' Mr. Littell had said." (007-022)

### The Party Incident

During a late-night disturbance at [[fairfields|Fairfields]], a servant reported, "I was just going after Mr. Littell," and another added, "I came down to call Mr. Littell and bumped into you." (008-028) (008-030) Along with [[mrs-littell|Mrs. Littell]], he maintained firm household rules: "Mr. and [[mrs-littell|Mrs. Littell]] rigidly insisted that the last guest be in the house by a certain hour." (007-037)

## Relationships

- [[Bobby Littell]] — eldest daughter; spirited and affectionate
- [[Libbie Littell]] — daughter; short, plump, and warm-hearted
- [[Louise Littell]] — daughter; the quieter, more reserved Littell sister
- [[Mrs. Littell]] — wife; happiest when entertaining, a gracious hostess at [[fairfields|Fairfields]]
- [[Bob Henderson]] — practically a ward; Mr. Littell arranged his military academy placement
- [[Betty Gordon]] — calls him "[[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]]" and considers him family
- [[Uncle Dick]] ([[uncle-dick|Richard Gordon]]) — the original "[[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]]" with whom Mr. Littell shares both a first name and [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s affection
