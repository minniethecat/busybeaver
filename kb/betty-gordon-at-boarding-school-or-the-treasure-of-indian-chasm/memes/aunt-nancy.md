# Aunt Nancy

## Overview

Aunt Nancy is the cook at [[Shadyside School]], affectionately called "good old Aunt Nancy" by the students. She is known for her kindness and her treats, including cake, ice-cream, and nut cake.

## Role in the Story

Aunt Nancy is first mentioned as "the fat cook" who, along with half a dozen helpers, was permitted to enter the scene of an important event (013-052).

She is generous with the students, providing cake and ice-cream during gatherings. [[bobby-littell|Bobby Littell]] noted that Aunt Nancy had treats ready after the students wrote their bylaws (014-024).

Aunt Nancy is particularly fond of the students who are kind. She thinks [[libbie-littell|Libbie]] is "an angel on general principles" and would fix her supper on a tray (020-010).

During the nutting expedition, Aunt Nancy promised the girls nut cake with ice-cream (022-006). [[norma-guerin|Norma]] remarked on Aunt Nancy's thoughtfulness when she heard they were going after nuts and prepared accordingly (022-023). The girls praised her tact, saying "Aunt Nancy has the real thing" (022-024).

At the celebratory feast after the play, Aunt Nancy and her assistants "fairly outdid themselves" in preparing an elaborate spread that added to everyone's enjoyment (023-055).

## Relationships

- [[bobby-littell|Bobby Littell]] — Student who frequently mentions her treats (014-024)
- [[libbie-littell|Libbie Littell]] — Student she is especially fond of (020-010)
- [[norma-guerin|Norma Guerin]] — Student who appreciates her thoughtfulness (022-023)
