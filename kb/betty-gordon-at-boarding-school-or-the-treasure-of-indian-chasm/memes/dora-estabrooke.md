# Dora Estabrooke

## Overview

**Dora Estabrooke** is a student at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside School]], described as "a fat girl who was good-natured." (014-032) Though her appearances in the story are brief, she represents the broader community of [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]] girls — friendly, cheerful, and part of the social fabric of the school. She is a friend of [[constance-howard|Constance Howard]] and participates in the school's boating activities.

## Role in the Story

### A Good-Natured Presence

Dora first appears in the context of the Saturday race preparations, where she is listed alongside [[constance-howard|Constance Howard]] among those bringing treats for the picnic: "[[constance-howard|Constance Howard]] and Dora Estabrooke, a fat girl who was good-natured, had more substantial treats." (014-032) Her contribution of food, and the affectionate description of her character, suggest a girl who was well-liked despite — or perhaps because of — her unpretentious nature.

### The Boating Expedition

Dora also participated in the school's rowing activities. During the preparations for a boating event, the assignment was made: "That's nothing — I'm to row Dora Estabrooke twice around the lake," one of the girls remarked, indicating that Dora was included in the school's outdoor pursuits. (020-025) The comment suggests Dora was a passenger of some substance — a logistical challenge for the rower — but the tone is one of fond exasperation rather than complaint.
