# Uncle Dick

## Overview

Richard Gordon, known to everyone as "Uncle Dick," is [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]]'s guardian and a central adult figure in the series. A representative of an oil company, he is a warm, practical man who has taken responsibility for both [[betty-gordon|Betty]] and [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]], providing them with a stable home after their difficult early years. (002-013)

## Role in the Story

### A Guardian's Decision

Uncle Dick makes the pivotal decision to send [[betty-gordon|Betty]] to boarding school. [[betty-gordon|Betty]] announces happily: "Uncle Dick says so." (001-019) [[bob-henderson|Bob]] confirms that "Uncle Dick is willing to let you go, is he?" — and [[betty-gordon|Betty]] replies that he has been persuaded by the advice of Mr. and [[mrs-littell|Mrs. Littell]]. (001-049) (003-028)

### The Oil Business

Uncle Dick works for an oil company, and his professional life occasionally intersects with the story. He is described as having "solved the problem of what to do with the boy" — referring to [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]] — through his association with the oil company. (002-013) His practical, level-headed approach to problems extends to his guardianship.

### A Kindly Presence

Mr. Gordon is a genial figure. When he is present at meals, "he insisted on a certain amount of form." (002-030) In one memorable moment, he "sat down on the step" — an informal gesture that reveals his approachable nature. (003-006) He is generous with both [[betty-gordon|Betty]] and [[bob-henderson|Bob]], promising that Christmas plans will work out. (003-014)

## Relationships

- [[Betty Gordon]] — ward and beloved guardian
- [[Bob Henderson]] — ward; Uncle Dick became [[bob-henderson|Bob]]'s guardian at [[bob-henderson|Bob]]'s and his aunts' request (002-018)
- [[Mrs. Littell]] — family friend whose advice he respects
