# Carter

## Overview

Carter is the butler at [[fairfields|Fairfields]], the Littell family's country estate. Described as "the personification of propriety," Carter is a dignified and kindly figure who has known [[betty-gordon|Betty]] from her previous visits and welcomes her warmly. (007-016)

## Role in the Story

### A Warm Welcome

When [[betty-gordon|Betty]] arrives at [[fairfields|Fairfields]], [[bobby-littell|Bobby]] directs her with mock formality to "ask Carter, the personification of propriety, for a drink of water." (007-016) Despite his formal bearing, Carter's domain is invaded by the happy chaos of arriving guests, with "telescope grips and tennis rackets piled on the sideboard, and spilling over into Carter's domain." (007-017)

### A Familiar Face

Carter "beamingly greeted" [[betty-gordon|Betty]], showing that their acquaintance predates this visit. (007-021) His warm recognition of [[betty-gordon|Betty]] underscores her place in the Littell family's extended circle. As a member of the [[fairfields-household|Fairfields staff]], Carter represents the gracious, well-ordered world that provides the backdrop for the young characters' adventures.
