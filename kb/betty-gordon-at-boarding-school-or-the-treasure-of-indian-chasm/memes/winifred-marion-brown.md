# Winifred Marion Brown

## Overview

**Winifred Marion Brown**, sometimes referred to as **W. M. Brown**, is a student at [[salsette-academy|Salsette Academy]] and one of the five boys recommended to [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]] by [[richard-littell|Mr. Littell]]. Known for his musical talent — he was a capable pianist — and for asking his [[friendship|friends]] to use his initials rather than his full given name, Winifred was a gentle and slightly formal presence among the more boisterous [[salsette-academy|Salsette]] boys. (007-015)

## Role in the Story

### One of the Five Boys

Winifred was part of the core group of boys that formed the [[salsette-academy|Salsette]]-[[shadyside-school|Shadyside]] social circle. [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]] mentioned him alongside the [[tucker-twins|Tucker twins]], [[timothy-derby|Timothy Derby]], and [[sydney-cooke|Sydney Cooke]]: twelve [[friendship|friends]] in all who would make the school year memorable. (006-039) He was also known as "W. M. Brown, who asks his [[friendship|friends]] to use his initials," a formality that his companions cheerfully ignored. (007-015)

### The Piano Player

Winifred's most notable contribution was his musical ability. The piano at [[salsette-academy|Salsette Academy]] was shared among the boys, and "Winifred Marion Brown, in addition to his ability at the keyboard," was a central figure in the informal concerts and sing-alongs that brought [[salsette-shadyside-alliance|the two schools]] together. (016-007) At the [[nutting-party|Nutting Party]], his playing provided the backdrop for the social mingling between [[shadyside-student-body|the Shadyside girls]] and [[salsette-academy|Salsette]] boys.

### Persuaded to Play

During the journey to school, [[tommy-tucker|Tommy Tucker]] "persuaded Winifred Marion Brown to play a game with him," drawing the more reserved boy out of his shell. (009-019) This was typical of the Tucker twins' influence on Winifred — they pulled him into their schemes and adventures despite his more dignified inclinations.

### The Rescue Party

Winifred joined the rescue effort in Chapter 22: "after them came [[gilbert-lane|Gilbert Lane]] and [[timothy-derby|Timothy Derby]] and Winifred Marion Brown," proving that despite his formal manner, he was ready to act when [[friendship|friends]] needed help. (022-041)
