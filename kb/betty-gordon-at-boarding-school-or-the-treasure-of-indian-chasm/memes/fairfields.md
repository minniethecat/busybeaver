# Fairfields

## Overview

Fairfields is the Littell family's country estate, where [[mrs-littell|Mrs. Littell]] hosts a memorable house party that brings together the students of [[shadyside-school|Shadyside School]] and [[salsette-academy|Salsette Academy]]. [[bobby-littell|Bobby Littell]] organizes the gathering, and it is at Fairfields that many of the characters meet for the first time. (006-038)

## Setting Description

The estate features a large house with a welcoming atmosphere. [[mrs-littell|Mrs. Littell]] stands "on the lowest step" to greet arriving guests. (007-019) [[carter-butler|Carter]], the dignified butler, presides over the household with "the personification of propriety," though the arrival of young guests quickly fills his domain with "telescope grips and tennis rackets piled on the sideboard." (007-016) (007-017) The house is large enough to accommodate many guests, and [[mrs-littell|Mrs. Littell]] "was happiest when entertaining young people." (007-024)

## Significance

Fairfields serves as the novel's key social gathering point outside the school setting. The house party allows for the full ensemble of characters to interact — [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]] girls, [[salsette-academy|Salsette]] cadets, and adult hosts all mix in a festive atmosphere. The events at Fairfields establish relationships and dynamics that carry through the rest of the story.
