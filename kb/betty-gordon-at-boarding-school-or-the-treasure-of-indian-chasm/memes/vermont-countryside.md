# Vermont

## Overview

Vermont is the home state of several [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]] students, including [[libbie-littell|Libbie Littell]] and [[frances-martin|Frances Martin]]. [[libbie-littell|Libbie]] is mentioned as "coming down from Vermont" to attend school. (001-023) The shared Vermont background creates a bond between the girls from the Green Mountain State, even as they adapt to boarding [[boarding-school-life|school life]].

## Setting Description

Vermont is depicted as a rural, wholesome region — the kind of place that produces "girls out after an education." (007-011) The Vermont girls at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]] share a certain sensibility, and their common origin is noted by the other students. [[bobby-littell|Bobby Littell]] lists "another Vermont girl we don't know — [[frances-martin|Frances Martin]]" among the [[fairfields|Fairfields]] guests, showing that Vermont identity matters in the school's social geography. (006-039)

## Significance

Vermont represents the rural American heartland from which many of the characters come. It contrasts with the more sophisticated settings of [[washington-dc|Washington]] and the institutional world of the boarding schools, grounding the characters in a particular regional identity.
