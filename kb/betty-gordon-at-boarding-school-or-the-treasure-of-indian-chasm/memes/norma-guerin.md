# Norma Guerin

## Overview

Norma Guerin is a new student at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside School]] whose letter to [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]] sets the story in motion. She is the daughter of a family who once lived near [[indian-chasm|Indian Chasm]], and she possesses crucial knowledge about the [[the-treasure-of-indian-chasm|lost treasure]] that gives the book its title. (013-003)

## Role in the Story

### The Letter

Norma's letter to [[betty-gordon|Betty]] is the catalyst for the plot. [[betty-gordon|Betty]] receives it in Chapter 1, exclaiming "It's from Norma Guerin!" (001-063) The letter announces that Norma and her sister [[alice-guerin|Alice]] are also going to [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]], which delights [[betty-gordon|Betty]]. (002-040)

### At Shadyside

Norma arrives at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]] and is warmly received. [[betty-gordon|Betty]] meets her at [[shadyside-station|the station]] among the arriving students. (010-023) Norma is observant and notices details others miss, whispering about the "gorgeous furs" worn by a new arrival. (010-030) [[betty-gordon|Betty]] wonders about Norma's family background, noting that Norma's mother "had belonged to one of the old families." (007-030)

### The Treasure Secret

Norma holds the key to the central mystery. She calmly declares that the treasure is "at the bottom of [[indian-chasm|Indian Chasm]]." (013-003) Her family history connects her to the lost diamonds, and her knowledge drives the treasure hunt that occupies the later chapters of the book.
