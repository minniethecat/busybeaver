# Betty Gordon

## Overview

Betty Gordon is the protagonist of the Betty Gordon series by [[alice-guerin|Alice]] B. Emerson. An orphan of New England stock, she travels from the [[oil-industry|Oklahoma oil]] fields to attend [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]], a girls' boarding school near [[washington-dc|Washington]] D.C. Resourceful, warm-hearted, and spirited, Betty forms deep [[friendship|friends]]hips and navigates the challenges of [[boarding-school-life|school life]] with courage and loyalty.

## Role in the Story

### Before Shadyside

Betty Gordon was an orphan who came from the East to the [[oil-industry|Oklahoma oil]] fields to join her only living relative, her beloved [[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]]. (002-012) Before arriving at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]], Betty spent a summer at [[bramble-farm|Bramble Farm]] with the Peabody family, where she met [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]], who became her closest friend. (002-013) She later traveled to [[washington-dc|Washington]] D.C., where she was "innocently kidnapped" by the three Littell daughters, only to have the entire family become her firm [[friendship|friends]]. (002-017)

### At Boarding School

Betty learns she will attend boarding school through a letter from [[bobby-littell|Bobby Littell]]. (001-019) She rooms with [[bobby-littell|Bobby]] at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]] and quickly becomes a central figure in the social life of the school, befriending old acquaintances [[norma-guerin|Norma]] and [[alice-guerin|Alice Guerin]] while navigating tensions with wealthy students like [[ada-nansen|Ada Nansen]]. (010-023)

Betty maintains a close bond with [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]], who attends the nearby [[salsette-academy|Salsette Academy]] across the lake. (010-017) Throughout the school year, Betty demonstrates resourcefulness and moral courage — from helping the [[guerin-family|Guerin sisters]] cope with their financial struggles to braving the dangers of [[indian-chasm|Indian Chasm]] in the climactic treasure hunt.

### Character Traits

Betty is described as having dark eyes and dark hair, with a vivid, expressive face and a distinctive dimple. (001-011) (001-015) She has a practical sensibility, making her own clothes from handed-down fabric, and a healthy interest in fashion tempered by the school's modest dress code. (001-057) (001-059) [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]] notes her tact and ability to handle people with fewer words than the impulsive [[bobby-littell|Bobby Littell]]. (001-047)
