# Washington DC

## Overview

Washington, D.C. is where [[mrs-littell|Mrs. Littell]] lives and where [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]] visits before departing for boarding school. [[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]] arranges for [[betty-gordon|Betty]] to stay there: "That will give me a week in Washington, and [[mrs-littell|Mrs. Littell]] has asked me to stay with them." (001-061) The capital represents the adult world that [[betty-gordon|Betty]] is temporarily stepping away from as she enters boarding [[boarding-school-life|school life]].

## Setting Description

Washington is depicted as a bustling, sophisticated city — the seat of government and society. For [[betty-gordon|Betty]], it is an exciting stop on her journey to [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]], a chance to see the capital and spend time with the Littell family before the school year begins.

## Significance

Washington serves as a transitional space in [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s journey. It is the last stop of her old life before she enters the new world of [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]]. The city's sophistication contrasts with the rural setting of the school, emphasizing the scope of [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s journey.
