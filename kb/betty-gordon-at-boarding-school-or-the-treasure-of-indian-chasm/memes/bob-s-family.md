# Bob Henderson's Extended Family

## Overview

[[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]]'s extended family — his mother's sisters, the Saunders family, and the legacy of the family farm — form the backstory that shapes his character. After his difficult years at the county poor farm and [[bramble-farm|Bramble Farm]], [[bob-henderson|Bob]]'s aunts claimed him, and with [[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]]'s help, the family farm was sold for ninety thousand dollars, securing [[bob-henderson|Bob]]'s future. (002-018)

## Members

- [[Bob Henderson]] — the center of the family's concern
- [[The Saunders Sisters]] — maternal aunts

## Role

[[bob-henderson|Bob]]'s extended family represents the theme of redemption through family ties. After his early hardships, the discovery of his mother's people and the sale of the family farm provide him with both emotional belonging and material security. This backstory makes [[bob-henderson|Bob]]'s loyalty to [[betty-gordon|Betty]] — the first person who was kind to him — all the more meaningful.
