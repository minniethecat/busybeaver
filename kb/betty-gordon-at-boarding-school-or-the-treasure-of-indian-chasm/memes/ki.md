# Ki

## Overview

**Ki** was an Indian farmhand who worked at [[bramble-farm|Bramble Farm]], where [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]] spent several months after his mother's death. Though his appearances in the story are brief, Ki is portrayed with quiet dignity — a hardworking man who shared what little he had, including a fox-skin that became one of [[bob-henderson|Bob]]'s treasured possessions. (002-030) (005-012)

## Role in the Story

### Life at Bramble Farm

Ki was described as "the Indian who helped with [[bramble-farm|the farm]] work" at [[bramble-farm|Bramble Farm]], one of the laborers who kept [[bramble-farm|the farm]] running. (002-030) During [[bob-henderson|Bob]]'s difficult months at [[bramble-farm|the farm]], Ki was a rare source of simple kindness. Unlike the [[mrs-peabody|Peabodys]], who treated [[bob-henderson|Bob]] as cheap labor, Ki offered small gestures of [[friendship|friends]]hip that [[bob-henderson|Bob]] remembered long after leaving.

### The Fox-Skin Gift

Ki gave [[bob-henderson|Bob]] a fox skin — a gift that held both practical and sentimental value. [[bob-henderson|Bob]] planned to share it: "I want Ki to have a piece, too," he said of the fox-skin cap he intended to make, revealing the reciprocal nature of their [[friendship|friends]]hip. (004-039) When [[bob-henderson|Bob]] and [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]] finally reached [[bramble-farm|the farm]], "Ki, it developed, when they reached [[bramble-farm|the farm]], was pleased with his piece of the skin." (004-040)

Later, [[bob-henderson|Bob]] showed off his handiwork: "a piece made from that fox skin Ki gave me." (005-012) The fox skin became a tangible link to [[bob-henderson|Bob]]'s life before he met [[betty-gordon|Betty]] — a reminder of [[bramble-farm|the farm]], of Ki's generosity, and of the resourcefulness [[bob-henderson|Bob]] had developed during those hard months.
