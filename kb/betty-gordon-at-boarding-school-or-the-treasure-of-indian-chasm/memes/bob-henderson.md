# Bob Henderson

## Overview

Bob Henderson is [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]]'s closest friend and fellow ward of [[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]] Gordon. A year older than [[betty-gordon|Betty]], Bob is an orphan who rose from harsh circumstances at [[bramble-farm|Bramble Farm]] and the county poor farm to become the loyal champion of the dark-eyed girl who befriended him. He attends [[salsette-academy|Salsette Academy]], the boys' military school across the lake from [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]].

## Role in the Story

### Background

Bob's early life was bleak — spent at the county poor farm and later at [[bramble-farm|Bramble Farm]], where conditions were "harsh and unlovely." (002-015) His fortunes changed when he met [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]], who smiled at him and suggested they had "a common bond of [[friendship|friends]]hip" as fellow orphans. (002-015) Bob eventually tracked down his mother's people, [[miss-charity-saunders|the Saunders sisters]], and with [[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]]'s help, the family farm was sold for ninety thousand dollars. (002-018)

### At Salsette Academy

Bob travels with [[betty-gordon|Betty]] to the [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]] area, attending [[salsette-academy|Salsette Academy]] across the lake. (010-017) At [[shadyside-station|the station]], [[salsette-cadet-corps|the Salsette cadets]] in their blue and silver uniforms are everywhere, and [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s first thought is "of how nice Bob Henderson would look in uniform." (010-004) Bob stays in close contact with [[betty-gordon|Betty]] throughout the school year, and their bond remains central to the story.

### The Treasure

In the climactic chapter, Bob leads [[betty-gordon|Betty]] through the snow after they become lost, taking responsibility for their predicament when they stumble into [[indian-chasm|Indian Chasm]]. (025-002) (025-005) Together they discover the treasure that gives the book its subtitle.

### Character Traits

Bob is described as a slim, tanned youth with blue eyes. (001-011) He is practical and action-oriented — when a premature oil blast rains down on them, he immediately takes charge. (001-070) Despite his rough upbringing, Bob shows unexpected emotional intelligence, as when he perceptively notes that [[betty-gordon|Betty]] "could probably control [[libbie-littell|Libbie]] better with less words" than the impulsive [[bobby-littell|Bobby Littell]]. (001-047) He rides an awkward but fast white horse named Reuben. (002-004)

## Relationships

- [[Miss Faith Saunders]] — aunt, one of Bob's mother's sisters
- [[Miss Charity Saunders]] — aunt, sister of Faith
