# Mrs. Eustice

## Overview

Mrs. Eustice is the principal of [[shadyside-school|Shadyside School]], the daughter of the school's founder, Mrs. Warde. A white-haired, dignified woman, she presides over the school with a blend of authority and genuine warmth that earns her the affection of her students. (007-029) (011-046) (011-051)

## Role in the Story

### The Principal's Heritage

Mrs. Eustice's connection to [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]] runs deep: her mother, Mrs. Warde, was the original founder of the school, and Mrs. Eustice has carried on the family tradition as its principal. (007-029) The girls regard her as "a direct descendant" of some notable figure, and they eagerly seek "good seats" to hear her speak. (011-044)

### The Welcome Address

Mrs. Eustice's welcome address to the students is a central moment in the opening of the school year. At two o'clock, "white-haired Mrs. Eustice mounted the platform and tapped for order." (011-046) Her opening words — "I welcome you, my girls" — set a tone of maternal authority and inclusion. (011-051) The speech is met with "a thunder of applause," and Mrs. Eustice steps down to personally greet the new arrivals. (011-055)

### Enforcing Discipline with Grace

Mrs. Eustice is firm about school rules. [[ada-nansen|Ada Nansen]]'s mother warned that "Mrs. Eustice never allowed the girls to dress extravagantly." (010-036) When necessary, she cancels privileges: the girls learn that "Mrs. Eustice canceled her [[edentown|Edentown]] permit," demonstrating that rules are enforced equally. (014-029) Yet her authority is never tyrannical — she is warm in her personal interactions, gazing at [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s hand with appreciation and declaring, "I must have a special talk" with her. (012-002) (012-006)

### The Pet Scheme

Mrs. Eustice is associated with the [[the-mysterious-four|Mysterious Four]], which is described as "Mrs. Eustice's pet scheme." (013-044) Her personal interest in the secret society adds an air of legitimacy to an organization that some students and parents regard with suspicion. She schedules individual appointments with new students, and [[betty-gordon|Betty]], after her meeting, feels that she has a clearer sense of purpose: "Ever since I heard Mrs. Eustice talk I feel that I know what I want to do." (012-041) (013-011)

### A Figure of Moral Authority

Mrs. Eustice represents the moral center of [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]]. Her speeches inspire students to reflect on their conduct, and her personal attention to each girl reinforces the school's values. When [[betty-gordon|Betty]] asks permission for [[constance-howard|Constance Howard]] to join an outing, Mrs. Eustice's approval reflects her trust in [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s judgment. (015-037)
