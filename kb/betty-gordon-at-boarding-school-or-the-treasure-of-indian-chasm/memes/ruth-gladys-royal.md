# Ruth Gladys Royal

## Overview

Ruth Gladys Royal is a student at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside School]] and the close friend ("chum") of [[ada-nansen|Ada Nansen]]. Like [[ada-nansen|Ada]], she hails from San Francisco, and the two form the core of a rival clique at the school. (010-045) (011-037) (014-030)

## Role in the Story

### Arrival and First Impressions

Ruth is first spotted at [[shadyside-station|the station]] by [[bobby-littell|Bobby]], who whispers, "Look quick! That's Ruth Gladys Royal!" (010-045) Her baggage is examined and the watchers count "three trunks" consigned to Ruth Gladys Royal. (011-010) She arrives alongside [[ada-nansen|Ada Nansen]] as the two are swept forward together when [[mrs-eustice|Mrs. Eustice]] greets the new students. (011-058)

### Ada's Chum

[[ada-nansen|Ada Nansen]] introduces Ruth as "my chum" from San Francisco, and Ruth reciprocates the [[friendship|friends]]hip effusively. (011-037) [[bobby-littell|Bobby Littell]] had earlier warned about Ruth, recalling a previous encounter: "Like Ruth Gladys Royal," she giggled, suggesting a history of friction. (007-040) Together, Ruth and [[ada-nansen|Ada]] form a small circle — described by others as "absolute snobs" who consider other students "perfect nobodies." (014-030)

### Antagonism and Character

Ruth's disposition is noted as difficult: [[norma-guerin|Norma Guerin]] observes that Ruth and [[ada-nansen|Ada]] "have dispositions like vinegar." (014-046) She is paired with [[norma-guerin|Norma]] in a gymnasium activity, an arrangement that highlights the tension between the rival groups. (014-039) Throughout the story, Ruth remains aligned with [[ada-nansen|Ada Nansen]], and when mischief occurs — such as when someone might "do it to plague [[libbie-littell|Libbie]]" — Ruth is among the suspects. (019-017)

### Later Suspicion

When trouble arises, [[bobby-littell|Bobby]] is quick to suspect "[[ada-nansen|Ada Nansen]] or Ruth Royal!" (022-056) Ruth's close association with [[ada-nansen|Ada]] means she shares in both the antagonism and the eventual softening of relations between the rival groups at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]].
