# Miss Lacey

## Overview

Miss Lacey is a corridor teacher at [[Shadyside School]] responsible for supervising the younger students. She is pleasant and observant, noticing social dynamics among her charges.

## Role in the Story

Miss Lacey first appears when she introduces herself to new students as their corridor teacher (010-056). She explains the school's policies, noting that while [[betty-gordon|Betty]] and [[frances-martin|Frances Martin]] were in different rooms, nominally they would be considered together (011-001).

During school gatherings, [[betty-gordon|Betty]] observes Miss Lacey alongside [[miss-anderson|Miss Anderson]] and other faculty members (011-027). Miss Lacey is attentive to social tensions—she swept her girls forward when [[ada-nansen|Ada Nansen]] attempted to dominate a situation (011-058).

Miss Lacey is perceptive about student relationships. [[norma-guerin|Norma]] reported that Miss Lacey was seen talking to [[ada-nansen|Ada]] and [[ruth-gladys-royal|Ruth]] (012-024), and it was believed that [[mrs-eustice|Mrs. Eustice]] must have spoken to Miss Lacey because she told the girls something important (012-025). She is also pleasant in informal moments, reminding students of time limits with a friendly tone (012-043).

During [[nutting-party-group|the nutting party]], Miss Lacey was observant of the dynamics that had developed among the girls (016-019), though she went on to her seat without interfering (016-023). Later, she appears alongside the school nurse, [[miss-morris|Miss Morris]], during a medical situation (018-018).
