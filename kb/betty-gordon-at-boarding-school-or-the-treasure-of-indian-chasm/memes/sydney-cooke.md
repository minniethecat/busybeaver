# Sydney Cooke

## Overview

**Sydney Cooke** is a student at [[salsette-academy|Salsette Academy]] and one of the five boys recommended to [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]] by [[richard-littell|Mr. Littell]]. Described as shy and quiet, Sydney was the boy who "ought to" — in [[bobby-littell|Bobby Littell]]'s unfinished teasing — suggesting he had a reputation among his [[friendship|friends]] for something left humorously unsaid. (007-015) He appears alongside the [[tucker-twins|Tucker twins]], [[timothy-derby|Timothy Derby]], and [[winifred-marion-brown|Winifred Marion Brown]] throughout the story as part of the [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]]-[[salsette-academy|Salsette]] social circle.

## Role in the Story

### One of the Five Boys

Sydney was among the group of boys [[richard-littell|Mr. Littell]] recommended to [[bob-henderson|Bob]]. [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]] listed him alongside "the Tucker twins, [[timothy-derby|Timothy Derby]], Sydney Cooke and [[winifred-marion-brown|Winifred Marion Brown]]" as the twelve [[friendship|friends]] who would make the school year enjoyable. (006-039)

### The Remarkable Discovery

Sydney had a talent for finding things others overlooked. During the journey to school, the group "might have been left alone after this, if Sydney Cooke had not discovered a remarkable amount of candy in his bag." (009-019) This discovery — and his willingness to share — made him briefly the center of attention and earned him the gratitude of his hungry companions.

### A Shy Nature

At the [[nutting-party|Nutting Party]], [[bobby-littell|Bobby Littell]] "made [[friendship|friends]] with the shy Sydney Cooke, who had been dreading this very experience." (016-006) [[bobby-littell|Bobby]]'s warmth helped Sydney overcome his social anxiety, and [[nutting-party-group|the nutting party]] became a turning point in his [[friendship|friends]]hips with [[shadyside-student-body|the Shadyside girls]].

### Standing Firm in the Storm

When danger threatened during [[caught-in-the-storm|the storm]], Sydney showed unexpected courage. "Sydney and I intend to stay," [[gilbert-lane|Gilbert Lane]] declared, with Sydney standing resolutely beside him. (017-061) The shy boy proved that quietness was not weakness.
