# The Lake

## Overview

The lake lies between [[shadyside-school|Shadyside School]] and [[salsette-academy|Salsette Academy]], serving as both a physical boundary and a connecting element between the two institutions. [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]] describes [[salsette-academy|Salsette]] as being "across the lake" from [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]], a phrase that captures both proximity and separation. (010-017) The lake is a constant feature of the landscape, visible from both schools and central to the geography of the story.

## Setting Description

The lake is large enough to be a defining feature of the landscape but small enough that [[salsette-shadyside-alliance|the two schools]] are clearly within sight of each other. Its shores include access points for both schools, and the surrounding terrain includes wooded hills and the approach to [[indian-chasm|Indian Chasm]].

## Significance

The lake creates the geographic tension that mirrors the social dynamics between [[salsette-shadyside-alliance|the two schools]]. Close enough for easy visits, far enough to maintain institutional separation, the lake shapes how the boys and girls interact. It is a constant, quiet presence in the background of the story — the still water that separates the orderly world of the schools from the wild mystery of [[indian-chasm|Indian Chasm]].
