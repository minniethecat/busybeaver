# Frances Martin

## Overview

Frances Martin is a [[vermont-countryside|Vermont]] girl who attends [[shadyside-school|Shadyside School]] alongside [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]] and the Littell sisters. Quiet and unassuming, Frances surprises her classmates with unexpected depths — she is "the last girl one would have" expected to show particular talents, yet proves herself in surprising ways. (007-047) She shares a [[vermont-countryside|Vermont]] background with [[libbie-littell|Libbie Littell]]. (006-039)

## Role in the Story

### A Quiet Presence

Frances is introduced as "another [[vermont-countryside|Vermont]] girl we don't know" when [[bobby-littell|Bobby Littell]] lists the guests for the [[fairfields|Fairfields]] house party. (006-039) [[betty-gordon|Betty]] meets her there, where [[bobby-littell|Bobby]] introduces her: "[[betty-gordon|Betty]], this is Frances Martin, a [[vermont-countryside|Vermont]] girl who is out after an education." (007-011) Frances smiles "a slow, sweet smile" — a characteristic gesture that hints at her gentle nature. (007-012)

### Unexpected Depth

Despite her quiet demeanor, Frances proves herself capable in surprising situations. When the group plans a hike, "[[bob-henderson|Bob]] was not allowed to beg off. Frances Martin, the last girl one would have expected to insist, backed up [[bob-henderson|Bob]]." (007-047) This moment reveals a determined streak beneath her calm exterior.

### In the Storm

During the dramatic storm in the woods, Frances faces her limitation: "Frances Martin can't see when the rain gets in her eyes." (017-058) When she cries out, [[betty-gordon|Betty]] and [[bob-henderson|Bob]] rush to her aid. (017-065) These moments show her vulnerability while also highlighting the group's loyalty to one another.
