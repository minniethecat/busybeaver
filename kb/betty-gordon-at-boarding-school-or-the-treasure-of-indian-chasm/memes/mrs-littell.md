# Mrs. Littell

## Overview

Mrs. Littell is the mother of [[Bobby Littell]] and [[Libbie Littell]], and one of the most frequently appearing adult characters in the story.

Living in [[washington-dc|Washington]], she is a generous hostess who opens her home to [[Betty Gordon]] and takes a maternal interest in the young people of her daughters' circle. (001-061)

Her warmth, practical wisdom, and capable presence make her a trusted figure among the [[Shadyside School]] girls and [[Salsette Academy]] boys alike. Mr. and Mrs. Littell's advice is sought on important matters affecting [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s future. (003-028)

## Role in the Story

### A Washington Welcome

Mrs. Littell first enters the story by inviting [[betty-gordon|Betty]] to spend a week in [[washington-dc|Washington]] before the school term begins:

> "I am going to let Bob stay with me a week in Washington, and Mrs. Littell has asked me to stay with them." (001-061)

This invitation sets [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s journey to [[washington-dc|the capital]] in motion, deepening her [[friendship|friends]]hip with the Littell family. The [[littell-family|Littell household]] becomes a waypoint between [[Bramble Farm]] and [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s new world at boarding school.

### Hostess of Fairfields

Mrs. Littell is at her most vivid during the [[fairfields|Fairfields]] house party. Her gift for hospitality is unmistakable:

> "How we have missed you!" cried Mrs. Littell, standing on the lowest step of the wide veranda, both hands extended. (007-019)

She is "happiest when entertaining," (007-024) and Mr. and Mrs. Littell together "knew to perfection the secret of making young people have a good time." (007-045)

### A Mother's Steady Hand

When [[Libbie Littell]] suffers a fainting spell during [[fairfields-house-party|the house party]], Mrs. Littell's composure and tenderness shine. She dresses quickly and comes to her daughter's aid:

> "I'll be right out," said Mrs. Littell reassuringly. (008-035)

[[betty-gordon|Betty]] watches as Mrs. Littell takes charge with quiet competence, whispering instructions and working alongside [[betty-gordon|Betty]] to care for the unconscious [[libbie-littell|Libbie]].

Then, in a moment of striking physical capability:

> Mrs. Littell lifted her in strong arms, put her on the bed, and began to bathe her face with cold water. (008-043)

After the crisis passes, she commands the children to bed with characteristic warmth, thanking [[Bob Henderson]] for his assistance. (008-044)

### A Thoughtful Correspondent

Mrs. Littell's care extends beyond in-person hospitality. She writes letters to [[shadyside-student-body|the Shadyside girls]] and sends thoughtful gift packages:

> Bobby each had a box from Mrs. Littell, who sent packages of sensible and welcome gifts. (022-019)

[[Norma Guerin]] is also familiar with Mrs. Littell's letters, (015-035) showing that her maternal concern reaches beyond her own daughters to embrace their [[friendship|friends]].
