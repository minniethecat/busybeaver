# Doctor Guerin

## Overview

Doctor Hal Guerin is a country doctor who practices near [[bramble-farm|Bramble Farm]]. He is the father of [[norma-guerin|Norma Guerin]] and [[alice-guerin|Alice Guerin]], and the husband of [[mrs-guerin|Mrs. Guerin]]. Described as "easy-going" and generous to a fault, Doctor Guerin is beloved by the community — especially by [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]], who declares he "would do anything in the world" for the doctor. (002-051) (002-050)

## Role in the Story

### A Country Doctor's Character

Doctor Guerin's defining trait is his reluctance to pursue payment from his patients. [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]] observes: "You know Dr. Guerin is so easy-going he never collects his bills." (002-048) [[betty-gordon|Betty]] concurs: "I don't think you could make Doctor Guerin take [[class-and-wealth|money]], even as a loan." (002-052) This generosity endears him to the community but also places financial strain on the [[guerin-family|Guerin family]].

### The Guerin Household

Doctor Guerin lives with his wife and their two daughters: "Doctor Hal Guerin and his wife and daughters had always lived near [[bramble-farm|Bramble Farm]]." (002-051) The family's modest circumstances are partly due to the doctor's forgiving nature about medical bills — a fact that becomes relevant when the question of sending [[norma-guerin|Norma]] and [[alice-guerin|Alice]] to [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]] arises. (002-040) (002-041)

### Bob's Gratitude

[[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]] feels a deep personal debt to Doctor Guerin. When the idea of establishing a permanent medical practice for the community is raised — "one like Doctor Guerin's" — [[bob-henderson|Bob]]'s loyalty is immediate: "Didn't he row me that time he thought I had smallpox, and I only had the measles?" (002-050) He declares emphatically that he "would do anything in the world for Doctor Guerin." (002-050)

### Later Years

At the end of the novel, Doctor Guerin and his wife plan to spend the winter in Florida, with the implication that the warmer climate will benefit their health. (025-047) (025-049)

## Relationships

- [[Norma Guerin]] — elder daughter; spirited and determined to attend [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]]
- [[Alice Guerin]] — younger daughter; sweet-natured and loyal to [[norma-guerin|Norma]]
- [[mrs-guerin|Mrs. Guerin]] — wife; partner in the country practice and household
- [[Bob Henderson]] — devoted friend; would do anything for the doctor
- [[Betty Gordon]] — admires the doctor's character and is close [[friendship|friends]] with his daughters
