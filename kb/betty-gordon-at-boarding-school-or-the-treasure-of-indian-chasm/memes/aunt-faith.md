# Aunt Faith

## Overview

Aunt Faith is [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]]'s aunt. She is generous with her possessions, giving fabric and clothing to [[betty-gordon|Betty]] and [[bob-henderson|Bob]].

## Role in the Story

Aunt Faith first appears when [[betty-gordon|Betty]] mentions that a dress material was wash silk that Aunt Faith said she could have if she could use it (001-054). [[bob-henderson|Bob]] comments on the size difference between [[betty-gordon|Betty]] and Aunt Faith, noting it would be impossible for them to wear the same clothes (001-055).

Aunt Faith had fine material stored in her trunk, which [[betty-gordon|Betty]] explained was how she got the fabric for a project (003-009). Later, [[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]] mentions that Aunt Faith has commissioned him to buy two of something, as she and some [[friendship|friends]] want to spend the winter somewhere (003-014).

[[bob-henderson|Bob]] also mentions wanting Aunt Faith to get a runabout (a small car), showing her involvement in family decisions (002-035).

## Relationships

- [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]] — Nephew (001-054)
- [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]] — Recipient of her generosity (001-054)
- [[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]] — Acts on her commissions (003-014)
