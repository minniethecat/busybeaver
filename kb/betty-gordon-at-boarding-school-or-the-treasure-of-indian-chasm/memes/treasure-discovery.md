# The Treasure Discovery

## Overview

The discovery of the [[the-treasure-of-indian-chasm|Macklin treasure]] at the bottom of [[Indian Chasm]] is the climactic event of the novel, fulfilling the promise of the book's subtitle.

Set in motion by [[Norma Guerin]]'s revelation in Chapter 13, the treasure hunt builds across twelve chapters before reaching its thrilling conclusion in the final chapter. [[Betty Gordon]] and [[Bob Henderson]] make the discovery together, braving snow and the dangerous chasm to recover a fortune that has lain hidden for years.

> "The Macklin treasure! We've found it!" cried Betty. (025-018)

## Background

### The Legend

The [[the-treasure-of-indian-chasm|lost treasure]] is first mentioned as a family secret known to the [[The Guerin Family|Guerin family]]. [[norma-guerin|Norma]] reveals its location with calm certainty:

> "At the bottom of Indian Chasm," declared Norma calmly. (013-003)

The treasure belonged to the Macklin family and was stolen by a band with a grudge. (018-004) For years it lay concealed in a narrow crack within [[indian-chasm|the chasm]], (025-022) waiting to be found.

### The Wait

Between [[norma-guerin|Norma]]'s revelation and the actual discovery, the treasure haunts the characters' thoughts. [[betty-gordon|Betty]] thinks to herself in frustration:

> "I wish I could get hold of that treasure, or a part of it." (015-046)

The treasure becomes an obsession. [[Libbie Littell|Libbie]] dreams about [[indian-chasm|the chasm]] "nearly every night." (020-007) Even as Christmas approaches, [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s mind remains fixed on the hidden fortune. (020-005)

## The Discovery

### The Expedition

The climactic discovery occurs in Chapter 25. [[Bob Henderson]] leads [[betty-gordon|Betty]] through the snow, determined to reach [[indian-chasm|the chasm]]:

> "Indian Chasm," repeated Bob firmly. "Come on — we're almost there." (025-003)

The descent is dangerous. Darkness threatens, and the terrain is treacherous. But the thought of the treasure sustains them:

> The thought of the found treasure sustained them and gave them courage. (025-031)

### The Find

At the bottom of [[indian-chasm|the chasm]], they find what they have been seeking — a heap of treasure where the Macklin fortune had been concealed. [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s cry echoes through the ravine:

> "The Macklin treasure! We've found it!" cried Betty. (025-018)

The treasure had been hidden in a narrow crack, placed there "not long ago" by someone who had rediscovered and relocated it. (025-022) The find confirms that the legend was true all along.

## Aftermath

The recovery of the treasure creates immediate excitement:

> The finding and recovery of the missing treasure created a wave of excitement. (025-046)

Authorities take charge of the recovered valuables, which are appraised at a remarkable sum:

> Appraised at nearly two thousand dollars. (025-050)

The discovery brings the novel to its satisfying conclusion, though it is "not the last of [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s adventures." (025-054)
