# Louise Littell

## Overview

Louise Littell is the sister of [[bobby-littell|Bobby]] and [[libbie-littell|Libbie Littell]], and a student at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside School]]. She is described as "peace-loving" and serves as a calming influence among the more excitable members of her circle. At [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]], she rooms with [[constance-howard|Constance Howard]], a girl from a Western army post. (002-042) (010-051)

## Role in the Story

### The Littell Family

Louise belongs to the [[littell-family|Littell family]], who are established residents of the region. [[mrs-littell|Mrs. Littell]] is her mother, and she has two sisters: [[bobby-littell|Bobby]], the eldest, and [[libbie-littell|Libbie]], the youngest. [[mrs-eustice|Mrs. Eustice]] remarks to Louise, "You look like her," referring to an older family resemblance shared by the Littell sisters. (012-003)

### At Shadyside

Louise arrives at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]] with her sisters and is impressed by [[shadyside-station|the station]], exclaiming, "Isn't it a pretty station!" (010-001) At the school, she rooms with [[constance-howard|Constance Howard]], sharing one side of [[shadyside-dormitory|the dormitory]] while [[betty-gordon|Betty]] and [[bobby-littell|Bobby]] occupy the other. (011-001) Louise quickly warms to her roommate: after [[constance-howard|Constance]] smiles at her, "Louise warmed perceptibly." (010-052)

### A Peace-Loving Nature

Louise's temperament is distinctly conciliatory. When tensions arise among the girls, it is "peace-loving Louise" who suggests, "The five minutes are up," steering the group away from conflict. (013-041) She maintains an optimistic outlook even in difficult situations — during a challenging moment, "the optimistic Louise" contributes a hopeful note. (017-010) Her steady temperament balances the more impulsive personalities of [[bobby-littell|Bobby]] and the other girls.

### Adventures with Friends

Louise participates fully in [[boarding-school-life|school life]]. During the [[nutting-party|nutting party]], she and [[frances-martin|Frances Martin]] call out, "Here's a tree!" as they search for nuts. (016-028) She is among the girls caught in [[caught-in-the-storm|the storm]] and lends [[class-and-wealth|money]] to [[libbie-littell|Libbie]] when needed — though [[libbie-littell|Libbie]] later worries, "I've borrowed so much from Louise that I can't ask her again!" (018-029)
