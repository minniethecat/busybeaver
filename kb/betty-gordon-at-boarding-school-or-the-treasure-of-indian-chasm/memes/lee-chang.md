# Lee Chang

## Overview

Lee Chang is the Chinese cook for the bunk house in the [[oil-industry|oil fields]] where [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]] lives with her uncle [[uncle-dick|Richard Gordon]]. He opens the very first paragraph of the novel, and is known for his delicious apple tarts and his protective, affectionate attitude toward [[betty-gordon|Betty]]. (001-001) (001-002) Though a minor character, his warm presence helps establish the homelike atmosphere of the [[gordon-household|Gordon household]] before [[betty-gordon|Betty]] departs for boarding school.

## Role in the Story

### First Appearance

The novel opens with Lee Chang in [[shadyside-kitchen|the kitchen]]: "'Apple tart, [[betty-gordon|Miss Betty]].' The Chinese cook flourished his rolling pin with one hand and a large red apple in the other." (001-001) This immediately establishes him as a familiar, kindly presence in [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s daily life. He is described as "cook for the bunk house in the [[oil-industry|oil fields]]." (001-002)

### Apple Tarts and Affection

Lee Chang's apple tarts are legendary among the characters. [[betty-gordon|Betty]] tells him, "I know your apple tarts, Lee Chang — they are delicious." (001-004) [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]] is equally enthusiastic: "'Apple tart?' shouted [[bob-henderson|Bob]]. 'Show me, Lee Chang! I'd rather have a corner of your apple tart than all the chocolate cake in the world.'" (001-032) (001-034)

### Concern for Bob

Lee Chang shares [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s concern for [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]]. He pokes his head out of [[shadyside-kitchen|the kitchen]] door and inquires, "'That [[bob-henderson|Bob]] no come?'" (001-006) When [[bob-henderson|Bob]] does not arrive as expected, "Lee Chang grunted and returned to his work." (001-009) His worry is noted again later: "Lee Chang was much worried when I went to find you." (003-002)

### A Persuasive Send-off

Before [[betty-gordon|Betty]] leaves for the East, Lee Chang makes sure she is well fed. "'[[betty-gordon|Miss Betty]]!' Lee Chang's voice was persuasive. '[[betty-gordon|Miss Betty]] — you no eat-a my apple tart?'" (001-031) He gives her a plate to take with her, and [[betty-gordon|Betty]] later asks, "Will you tell some one to take the plate back to Lee Chang?" (001-064)

> "Lee Chang's wish was fulfilled when he saw Betty eating her luncheon on the train." (001-035)

## Relationships

- [[Betty Gordon]] — treated with fatherly devotion; he fusses over her meals and worries about her travels
- [[Bob Henderson]] — shares a warm, teasing [[friendship|friends]]hip over Lee Chang's cooking
- [[Uncle Dick]] ([[uncle-dick|Richard Gordon]]) — employer; Lee Chang works in the oil field bunk house

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter 1** — Opens the novel in [[shadyside-kitchen|the kitchen]]; sends [[betty-gordon|Betty]] off with apple tart (001-001)
- **Chapter 3** — Mentioned as having been worried when [[bob-henderson|Bob]] disappeared (003-002)
