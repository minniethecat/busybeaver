# Oil Industry

## Overview

The Oklahoma oil industry serves as the foundational setting for [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]]'s backstory and her relationship with [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]]. Before the ivy-covered walls of [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]], [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s world was defined by oil fields, drilling rigs, and the rough-and-tumble life of petroleum workers. [[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]] Gordon's work as an oil company representative provided [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s livelihood and shaped her pragmatic, unpretentious character. The novel's opening chapters immerse the reader in this industrial landscape before [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s departure for boarding school.

## Manifestations

### The Premature Oil Well Blast

The novel opens with dramatic oil-field action: a premature oil well explosion that drenches [[betty-gordon|Betty]] and those nearby in crude oil. "[[bob-henderson|Bob]]'s first experience with an oil well set off prematurely" (002-001) introduces the danger and unpredictability of petroleum work. [[betty-gordon|Betty]] "made a little face at the taste of oil in her mouth" (002-008) — a sensory detail that grounds the story in physical reality. This explosive opening establishes the oil fields as a place of risk, excitement, and industrial power.

### Life in the Oil Fields

The oil fields are depicted as a self-contained world with its own rhythms and social structures. Workers "worked that section of the oil fields" (001-035) and lived in bunk houses. [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s familiarity with this masculine, labor-intensive environment explains her resilience and adaptability — qualities that serve her well at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]]. The "heavy rain of blackest oil" (001-067) is both a literal description and a symbol of the transformative power of industry.

### Uncle Dick and the Oil Company

[[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]] Gordon represents the professional class within the oil industry. "His time and skill pledged to the oil company he represented" (002-013), [[uncle-dick|Mr. Gordon]]'s work provided the means for [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s education and social advancement. The oil company — an impersonal yet benevolent force — underwrites [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s journey from the industrial frontier to the civilized East.

### Oil Wealth and Social Mobility

The oil industry drives the novel's themes of social mobility and new wealth. [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s transition from "the oil section of Oklahoma" (002-018) to [[shadyside-school|Shadyside School]] is made possible by oil [[class-and-wealth|money]] — a form of wealth earned through industry rather than inherited through generations. This distinguishes [[betty-gordon|Betty]] from both the old-[[class-and-wealth|money]] Littells and the ostentatious Nansens, positioning her as a bridge between worlds.

### Departure from the Oil Fields

[[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s departure from Oklahoma marks a decisive break with her past. The oil fields recede into memory as [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]]'s ordered campus replaces the chaotic industrial landscape. Yet the values forged in that environment — hard work, loyalty, and resilience — remain central to [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s character throughout the novel.
