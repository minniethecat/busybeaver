# The Nutting Party

## Overview

The nutting party is an autumn tradition at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside School]], where students venture into the woods to gather nuts. The outing in Chapter 16 provides a backdrop for [[friendship|camaraderie]], discovery, and the simple pleasures of boarding [[boarding-school-life|school life]]. It is during this excursion that [[bobby-littell|Bobby Littell]] spots [[coasting-hill|the hill]] and exclaims, "What a dandy hill for coasting!" (016-036)

## Narrative

Students from both [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]] and [[salsette-academy|Salsette]] participate in [[nutting-party-group|the nutting party]]. Those who do not go nutting take alternative outings — "some took carriages to [[edentown|Edentown]]." (022-025) The nutting party represents the wholesome outdoor recreation that balances the academic and social dimensions of [[boarding-school-life|school life]].

## Significance

The nutting party is one of the novel's key outdoor set pieces, showcasing the natural beauty surrounding [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]] and the students' easy [[friendship|camaraderie]]. It also plants the seed for [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s later [[betty-goes-coasting|coasting adventure]] by introducing [[coasting-hill|the hill]].
