# Grandma Watterby

## Overview

Grandma Watterby is the elderly matriarch of the [[bramble-farm|Watterby farm]], located near the [[gordon-household|Gordon household]] in the [[oil-industry|oil fields]]. A hospitable, sharp-tongued old woman, she presides over a bustling farm household that includes her son Will Watterby and his wife. The Watterby farm serves as a way station and second home for [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]] and [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]] during their comings and goings. (001-030) (002-023)

## Role in the Story

### The Watterby Farm

The [[bramble-farm|Watterby farm]] sits "on the Saunders' place and the Watterby farm" — a landmark in the rural landscape where [[betty-gordon|Betty]] and [[bob-henderson|Bob]] had spent time together. (001-030) (002-020) [[betty-gordon|Betty]] notes, "I'm supposed to ride to the Watterby farm for lunch." (001-030) The farm becomes a gathering point for the travelers before their departure for the East.

### Hospitality and Comfort

When [[bob-henderson|Bob]] arrives covered in oil and dirt, it is Grandma Watterby who takes charge: "'You'd better come to Grandma Watterby's and get tidied up, [[bob-henderson|Bob]]. It's only a step.'" (002-023) The "sympathetic Watterby family came to the oil-spotted lad's rescue." (002-024) [[bob-henderson|Bob]] borrows "a clean shirt from Will Watterby, in which the boy, being much larger than [[bob-henderson|Bob]], was lost completely." (002-025)

Mrs. Will Watterby offers warm hospitality: "'Of course you may,' said Mrs. Will Watterby warmly. 'Did you fix a place for the little boy?'" (002-028) The waffles are particularly memorable — "Mrs. Watterby's waffles were quite as good as her husband's praise of them." (002-030)

### A Sharp Tongue

Grandma Watterby is not afraid to speak her mind. When someone asks an impertinent question, "old Grandma Watterby" responds "severely": "'No question, Emma,' reproved old Grandma Watterby severely." (002-029) Despite her sharpness, her authority and kindness are respected by all.

### Practical Help

Grandma Watterby provides practical assistance to the travelers. She helps store trunks in the "Watterby attic" (003-034) (003-039) and the "hospitable Watterbys had insisted that the travelers" stay for meals. (003-040) When [[betty-gordon|Betty]] discusses future plans, she notes: "Grandma Watterby thinks maybe she can get the..." (003-005) The family had "promised Grandma Watterby to be there for supper." (004-021)

### Wisdom and Experience

Grandma Watterby speaks from long experience of the region: "'Nobody from there before,' said Grandma Watterby earnestly." (005-001) Her knowledge of local ways and people makes her a valued source of counsel for the young travelers.

> "Mrs. Watterby, when the meal was over... This, for Mrs. Watterby, was a great step forward." (002-032) (002-033)

## Relationships

- [[Betty Gordon]] — regards Grandma Watterby as a grandmother figure; often stays at [[bramble-farm|the farm]]
- [[Bob Henderson]] — recipient of the Watterby family's hospitality and clean shirts
- Will Watterby — son; runs [[bramble-farm|the farm]] with his wife
- Mrs. Will Watterby — daughter-in-law; known for her excellent waffles
- [[Uncle Dick]] ([[uncle-dick|Richard Gordon]]) — neighbor; the Watterby farm is near the Gordon household
