# Miss Anderson

## Overview

Miss Anderson is the physical instructor at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside School]]. She is one of the first faculty members [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]] meets upon arrival, greeting new students at [[shadyside-station|the station]] and shepherding them to the school. Described as "smiling and unhurried," she oversees gymnasium classes, plays piano for after-dinner dancing, and maintains a brisk, good-humored authority over her charges. (010-015) (010-046)

## Role in the Story

### Welcome to Shadyside

Miss Anderson first appears at the railway station, introducing herself to the arriving students: "'I'm Miss Anderson, the physical instructor.'" (010-015) She directs the porters and organizes the new girls, leading them toward [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]]: "Miss Anderson, smiling and unhurried, marshalled the little procession." (010-046) She tells [[betty-gordon|Betty]], "'And you will meet her and the other teachers,'" guiding her into [[boarding-school-life|school life]]. (010-048)

### The Red Sweater Incident

When [[betty-gordon|Betty]] felt left out and homesick, Miss Anderson noticed and intervened: "Miss Anderson came up to her bringing a red sweater." (010-050) This small act of kindness helped [[betty-gordon|Betty]] feel included among her new classmates.

### Gymnasium and Athletics

As physical instructor, Miss Anderson oversaw the gymnasium program. She "beamed approvingly" upon [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s progress in gymnasium classes. (013-012) She also enforced standards: during one session, "Miss Anderson discovered that she was wearing" improper attire and the "physical instructor put the others through a rigorous drill." (014-034) (014-036)

### Music and Social Life

Beyond athletics, Miss Anderson contributed to the social life of the school. "After dinner Miss Anderson played for them to dance in the big hall." (012-016) Her playing created a warm atmosphere for the girls to relax together.

### Wit and Authority

Miss Anderson possessed a sharp wit, as shown when she addressed [[betty-gordon|Betty]] and [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]]: "'You and your "men,"' said Miss Anderson briskly. 'I was just saying that it is next to impossible to keep you girls in order when there is a boy anywhere around.'" (014-031)
