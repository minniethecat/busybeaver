# Constance Howard

## Overview

Constance Howard is a student at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside School]] from a Western army post. She is the roommate of [[louise-littell|Louise Littell]] and a participant in the school's social and athletic life. Described as a girl who "has a way with her," Constance is an excellent swimmer and plays the ukulele. (010-051) (017-004) (023-048)

## Role in the Story

### A Western Girl at Shadyside

[[bobby-littell|Bobby Littell]] first describes Constance to [[betty-gordon|Betty]] at [[shadyside-station|the station]]: "Her father is a Captain in the army stationed at some Western army post. Her name is Constance Howard, and she doesn't know a single soul." (010-051) When Constance smiles, [[louise-littell|Louise]] "warmed perceptibly." (010-052) She is "from the West, too — the Presidio," connecting her geographically to the broader world beyond [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]]. (011-038)

### Rooming with Louise

Constance rooms with [[louise-littell|Louise Littell]] on one side of [[shadyside-dormitory|the dormitory]], while [[betty-gordon|Betty]] and [[bobby-littell|Bobby]] are on the other. (011-001) This arrangement places her firmly within the [[betty-gordon|Betty]]–[[littell-family|Littell]] social circle rather than the rival [[ada-nansen|Ada Nansen]] faction.

### Talents and Personality

"Clever Constance Howard" brings her ukulele to a gathering, providing entertainment and lightening the mood. (013-053) She is also an excellent swimmer, a skill that proves valuable during [[caught-in-the-storm|the storm]]. (017-004) She sees little snow at her Western home and finds New England winter a novel experience, enjoying the coasting with wide-eyed wonder. (024-006) She participates in the [[nutting-party|nutting party]] — "round-eyed" at the sight of supplies stored for the event. (016-044)

### Acceptance and Recognition

Constance eventually earns recognition from the broader school community. To "the astonishment of the school," she and [[ada-nansen|Ada Nansen]] are selected for a notable honor. [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]] remarks, "Constance Howard has a way with her," acknowledging her ability to bridge even the most entrenched social divisions. (023-036) (023-048)

### A Quiet Struggle

Constance, like other students, faces academic challenges at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]]. She is told she "must add up an unusual number of demerits" before being allowed certain privileges, a reminder that life at boarding school involves discipline as well as adventure. (020-027)
