# Shadyside Dormitory

## Overview

The dormitory building at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside School]] is where [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]] and her [[friendship|friends]] live during the school year. The girls occupy interconnected suites, creating an intimate community. [[betty-gordon|Betty]] and [[bobby-littell|Bobby]] share "the center room in a suite" with four other girls, while [[norma-guerin|Norma]] and [[alice-guerin|Alice Guerin]] have "a room that adjoined." (011-001)

## Setting Description

The dormitory is a multi-story building with steps that "the girls ran up" eagerly. (018-011) The room arrangement is described in detail: interconnected suites with a center room and adjoining chambers. This layout creates a honeycomb of [[friendship|friends]]hips, where conversations flow between rooms and the girls form a close-knit community.

## Significance

The dormitory is the setting for the most intimate moments of the story — whispered conversations, shared secrets, and the quiet rhythms of boarding [[boarding-school-life|school life]]. It is here that "[[libbies-secret|Libbie's Secret]]" unfolds in Chapter 18, and where the girls' bonds are forged in the everyday rituals of [[boarding-school-life|school life]]. The dormitory represents the private, feminine world that exists alongside the public adventures of the plot.
