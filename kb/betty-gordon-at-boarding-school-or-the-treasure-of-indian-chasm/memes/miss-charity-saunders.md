# Miss Charity Saunders

## Overview

**Miss Charity Saunders** was one of the two elderly Saunders sisters — along with her sister [[miss-faith-saunders|Miss Faith Saunders]] — who owned the Saunders farm in the countryside near [[bramble-farm|Bramble Farm]]. (002-019) The sisters were [[saunders-sisters|the aunts]] of [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]], and their quiet rural life was transformed when oil was discovered on their property, leading to a sale that secured their financial future. (002-018)

## Role in the Story

### A Windfall from the Land

The Saunders sisters became an example of justice prevailing over greed. "The story of the 'Saunders' place' and of the unscrupulous land sharks who had tried to cheat the two old ladies out of their land" ended in vindication: [[bramble-farm|the farm]] sold for ninety thousand dollars. (002-018) The sisters' steadfast refusal to be cheated — despite the pressure from speculators — became a quiet lesson in integrity that resonated throughout the early chapters.

### A Haven for Bob

During the months when [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]] had no permanent home, the Saunders farm was one of the few places where he was welcomed. [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]] "divided her time between the Saunders' place and the Watterby farm, where she and [[bob-henderson|Bob]] had shared many a simple meal." (002-020) The Saunders sisters provided [[bob-henderson|Bob]] not just with meals and clean shirts (002-025), but with the familial connection he had lost when his mother died.

### The Farmhouse After the Sale

Even after [[bramble-farm|the farm]] was sold, the old Saunders farmhouse retained its meaning for those who had known it. [[betty-gordon|Betty]] visited "with her parents into the old Saunders farmhouse" and was struck by the happiness of those who returned there. (004-027)
