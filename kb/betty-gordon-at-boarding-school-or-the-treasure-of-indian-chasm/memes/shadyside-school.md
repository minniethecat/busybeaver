# Shadyside School

## Overview

Shadyside School is the boarding school for girls that [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]] and her [[friendship|friends]] attend. Located across a lake from [[salsette-academy|Salsette Academy]], the boys' military school, Shadyside provides the primary setting for the novel's events. [[betty-gordon|Betty]] learns that "[[norma-guerin|Norma]] and [[alice-guerin|Alice Guerin]] are going to Shadyside" — and soon she joins them there. (002-040)

## Setting Description

Shadyside is situated in a rural area near [[edentown|the town]] of [[edentown|Edentown]]. The school building is described as having dormitory rooms where the girls live. [[betty-gordon|Betty]], [[bobby-littell|Bobby]], and their [[friendship|friends]] share interconnected suites — [[betty-gordon|Betty]] and [[bobby-littell|Bobby]] occupy "the center room in a suite" of rooms. (011-001) [[norma-guerin|Norma]] and [[alice-guerin|Alice Guerin]] share an adjoining room, creating an intimate community among the students. (011-001)

The [[shadyside-dormitory|dormitory building]] has steps that the girls run up and down, and the school grounds include areas for recreation and socializing. (018-011)

## Significance

Shadyside is more than a backdrop — it is the crucible for the girls' [[friendship|friends]]hips, rivalries, and adventures. The school's proximity to [[indian-chasm|Indian Chasm]] makes the treasure hunt possible, and its social dynamics drive much of the plot. The contrast between Shadyside's orderly [[boarding-school-life|school life]] and the wild mystery of the treasure creates the novel's central tension.
