# Norma's Letter

## Overview

The arrival of [[norma-guerin|Norma Guerin]]'s letter is the catalyst for the novel's plot. [[betty-gordon|Betty]] exclaims, "It's from [[norma-guerin|Norma Guerin]]!" as she opens the letter that reveals both [[guerin-family|Guerin sisters]] will attend [[shadyside-school|Shadyside School]]. (001-063) This news sets in motion the chain of events that will lead [[betty-gordon|Betty]] to boarding school and, ultimately, to the treasure.

## Narrative

[[betty-gordon|Betty]] receives the letter and shares its contents with [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]]. "What do you think! [[norma-guerin|Norma]] and [[alice-guerin|Alice Guerin]] are going to [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]]!" (002-040) The letter transforms [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s vague plans into concrete excitement, and her enthusiasm is contagious.

## Significance

[[norma-guerin|Norma]]'s letter is the inciting incident of the novel. Without it, [[betty-gordon|Betty]] might not have been so eager to attend [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]], and the treasure mystery might never have come to light. The letter connects the characters' past to their future and introduces the Guerin family's connection to the treasure.
