# The Fairfields House Party

## Overview

The [[fairfields|Fairfields]] house party, held at the Littell family's country estate, is the novel's central social event. Organized by [[bobby-littell|Bobby Littell]], the party brings together students from [[shadyside-school|Shadyside School]] and [[salsette-academy|Salsette Academy]] for a weekend of festivities. (006-038) The gathering establishes the relationships and dynamics that carry through the rest of the story.

## Narrative

### The Invitation

[[bobby-littell|Bobby Littell]] organizes the event, writing to invite the other girls to [[fairfields|Fairfields]]. [[betty-gordon|Betty]], who has been staying with [[mrs-littell|Mrs. Littell]] in [[washington-dc|Washington]], is among the first to arrive. (006-038) The guest list includes the Tucker twins, [[frances-martin|Frances Martin]], [[norma-guerin|Norma]] and [[alice-guerin|Alice Guerin]], and the Littell sisters.

### The Arrival

Guests arrive to a warm welcome. [[mrs-littell|Mrs. Littell]] stands "on the lowest step" greeting arrivals, while [[carter-butler|Carter]] the butler presides with dignified propriety. (007-019) (007-016) The formal atmosphere quickly gives way to youthful chaos as "telescope grips and tennis rackets" pile up around the house. (007-017)

### The Festivities

[[mrs-littell|Mrs. Littell]], "happiest when entertaining young people," ensures the party is a success. (007-024) The weekend includes meals, games, and socializing between [[shadyside-student-body|the Shadyside girls]] and [[salsette-academy|Salsette]] cadets. It is at [[fairfields|Fairfields]] that many of the novel's key relationships are cemented.

## Significance

The [[fairfields|Fairfields]] house party serves as the novel's ensemble moment, bringing the full cast together in a festive setting. It establishes the social geography of the story — who is [[friendship|friends]] with whom, the dynamics between [[salsette-shadyside-alliance|the two schools]], and the warm adult presence that supports the young characters.
