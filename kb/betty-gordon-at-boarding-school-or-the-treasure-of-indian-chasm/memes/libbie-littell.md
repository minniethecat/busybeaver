# Libbie Littell

## Overview

Libbie Littell is [[bobby-littell|Bobby Littell]]'s younger sister, a student at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside School]]. Short and plump, Libbie is sensitive and easily frightened, but deeply loyal to her [[friendship|friends]]. Her aunt expects her to require special watching over, a responsibility that falls to [[betty-gordon|Betty]]. (001-043)

## Role in the Story

### A Girl to Watch Over

Libbie is first mentioned as coming down from [[vermont-countryside|Vermont]] to join the school. (001-023) [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s guardian, [[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]], teases her that if Libbie comes, "her aunt expects [[bobby-littell|Bobby]] to have her hands full, and she hopes I will keep an eye on Libbie." (001-043) [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]] perceptively notes that [[betty-gordon|Betty]] "could probably control Libbie better with less words" than the impulsive [[bobby-littell|Bobby]]. (001-047)

### At Shadyside

Libbie is part of the close-knit group of girls at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]]. She is described as "short, plump" and given to enthusiastic hugs — at one point relinquishing her claim on [[betty-gordon|Betty]] "with a sigh" after embracing her. (007-010) She wants to do everything the older girls do. (006-039)

### In the Storm

During the dramatic storm in Chapter 17, Libbie shows her vulnerable side. As the rain intensifies, she is "fixing to have hysterics." (017-056) Despite her timidity, she remains part of the group's adventures.

### A Secret

Libbie becomes central to one of the story's subplots — "[[libbies-secret|Libbie's Secret]]" is the title of Chapter 18, suggesting she harbors a mystery of her own that adds to the book's intrigue.
