# The Oil Company

## Overview

The oil company that employs Richard "[[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]]" Gordon is a background presence in the novel, providing the economic stability that allows the Gordon household to thrive. [[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]]'s position as a company representative brought him into contact with the circumstances that led to [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]]'s rescue. (002-013)

## Role

The oil company represents the working world that exists beyond the novel's school settings. [[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]]'s employment enables him to serve as guardian to both [[betty-gordon|Betty]] and [[bob-henderson|Bob]], and his professional competence mirrors his capable approach to guardianship. The company also connects to [[bob-henderson|Bob]]'s story — it was through the oil business that [[uncle-dick|Uncle Dick]] "found a boy who had been abandoned." (002-013)
