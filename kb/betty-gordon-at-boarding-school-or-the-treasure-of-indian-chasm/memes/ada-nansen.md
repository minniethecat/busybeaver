# Ada Nansen

## Overview

Ada Nansen is a student at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside School]] from San Francisco. She is the leader of a rival clique that includes her close friend [[ruth-gladys-royal|Ruth Gladys Royal]], and serves as an antagonist to [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]] and her [[friendship|friends]]. Ada is associated with the [[the-mysterious-four|Mysterious Four]], a secret society at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]]. (010-027) (011-037) (013-054)

## Role in the Story

### Arrival at Shadyside

Ada first appears at [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]] with an abundance of luggage — so many trunks that it worries [[norma-guerin|Norma Guerin]], who fears [[mrs-eustice|Mrs. Eustice]] "might make a fuss." (010-028) (010-034) A stout girl greets Ada affectionately upon her arrival. (010-044) Her name is quickly noted by [[betty-gordon|Betty]], who examines the baggage tags and reads "'Miss Ada Nansen.'" (011-009)

### The Rival Clique

[[ruth-gladys-royal|Ruth Gladys Royal]] introduces Ada as "my chum" from San Francisco. (011-037) Together they form a faction at the school, and Ada regards [[betty-gordon|Betty]] with "positive dislike." (011-043) [[bobby-littell|Bobby Littell]] remarks of her, "I wish Ada Nansen would take a good look at herself." (011-050) Ada and her [[friendship|friends]] are described as "absolute snobs" who consider other students "perfect nobodies." (014-030)

### The Mysterious Four

Ada is a member of the [[the-mysterious-four|Mysterious Four]], a secret society whose nature is little understood by the other girls. She whispers dismissively to [[ruth-gladys-royal|Ruth Royal]], "I wouldn't give fifty cents to belong." (013-044) Yet when the leader calls "Ada Nansen" during a meeting, she participates. (013-054) Her mother is among those who consider [[mrs-eustice|Mrs. Eustice]] "peculiarly" for her connection to the [[the-mysterious-four|Mysterious Four]]. (013-013)

### Physical Mishaps and Redemption

Ada is captain of a [[salsette-cadet-corps|gymnasium squad]]. (014-028) She has an accident on the stairs that leaves her needing assistance, and [[betty-gordon|Betty]] helps her up — leading [[norma-guerin|Norma]] to declare it was "absolutely the most generous thing" she'd ever seen. (014-048) (015-028) Later, Ada's attitude softens: to the astonishment of the school, she and [[constance-howard|Constance Howard]] are selected for an honor. (023-036) [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]] notes, "Any one that can persuade Ada Nansen to do anything nice is qualified for Congress." (023-048)

### The Theatricals

Ada is eager to participate in the school [[dramatical-performance|dramatics]], keen to be assigned a part. (021-053) (021-058) When the cast list is posted, Ada Nansen's name appears as "the maid." (021-058)

## Relationships

- [[Ruth Gladys Royal]] — close friend ("chum") from San Francisco, ally in the rival clique
- [[Constance Howard]] — fellow member of the rival faction
- [[Betty Gordon]] — rival; their relationship warms after [[betty-gordon|Betty]] helps Ada when she falls
- [[Mrs. Eustice]] — principal of [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]]; Ada's mother is critical of her
