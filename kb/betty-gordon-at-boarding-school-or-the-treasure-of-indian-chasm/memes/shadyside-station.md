# Shadyside Station

## Overview

The railway station near [[shadyside-school|Shadyside School]] and [[salsette-academy|Salsette Academy]] is where [[betty-gordon|Betty Gordon]] first arrives in the world of the novel. It is here that she sees [[salsette-cadet-corps|the Salsette cadets]] in their "blue and silver uniforms" for the first time, and her first thought is "of how nice [[bob-henderson|Bob Henderson]] would look in uniform." (010-004) The station is the threshold between [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s old life and her new one.

## Setting Description

The station is a bustling arrival point where students from both schools converge at the start of term. [[salsette-academy|Salsette]] cadets are "everywhere" when [[betty-gordon|Betty]] arrives, their blue and silver uniforms marking them out among the crowd. (010-004) The station serves both schools and the nearby town of [[edentown|Edentown]], making it a natural meeting place and the gateway to the [[shadyside-school|Shadyside]]-[[salsette-academy|Salsette]] world.

## Significance

The station represents beginnings and transitions. It is where [[betty-gordon|Betty]]'s boarding school adventure starts, where characters arrive and depart, and where the two school communities intersect. In a novel about journeys — both geographic and personal — the station is the literal point of arrival and departure.
