# Legal Themes and Events

The law is both antagonist and stage machinery in Wilkie Collins' *No Name* (1862). The novel turns on the catastrophic legal consequence of the Vanstone parents' secret: because Andrew and [[mrs-vanstone|Mrs. Vanstone]] were not legally married at the time of their daughters' birth, Magdalen and Norah are deemed illegitimate and disinherited. This page aggregates every character, event, concept, and place connected to the novel's legal machinery — from the Inns of Court to the coverture doctrine that strips women of legal personhood.

## Legal Context

Collins, who studied law at Lincoln's Inn, brings a lawyer's precision to the novel's central legal question. The plot exposes the injustice of the Laws of Coverture and the rigid class and gender hierarchies embedded in Victorian inheritance law. For the key legal concept, see [[coverture]]. For the institutional settings, see [[lincolns-inn]] and [[the-legal-discovery]].
