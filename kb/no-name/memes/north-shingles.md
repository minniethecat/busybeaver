North Shingles is [[captain-wragge|Captain Wragge]]'s rented residence in the seaside village of [[aldborough|Aldborough]], Suffolk, and the stage upon which the central action of the middle volume of Wilkie Collins's *No Name* unfolds. It is here that [[magdalen-vanstone|Magdalen Vanstone]], in her first major disguise as the captain's "niece" Miss Bygrave, executes the [[the-inheritance-scheme|inheritance scheme]] — the calculated courtship and entrapment of [[noel-vanstone|Noel Vanstone]] intended to recover the [[combe-raven|Combe-Raven]] fortune. With 76 mentions across the corpus, North Shingles is the novel's most frequently referenced domestic interior and the strategic headquarters of its most sustained conspiracy.

The house is situated directly on the Aldborough parade, commanding a view of the sea and, more importantly for Wragge's purposes, a view of the neighboring lodging where Noel Vanstone and his formidable housekeeper [[mrs-lecount|Mrs. Lecount]] are staying. Wragge selects the property with his characteristic attention to tactical advantage: its proximity enables the constant surveillance and carefully timed "accidental" encounters that drive the courtship forward. The captain describes his methods with characteristic self-regard: "I never do things by halves" (ch80).

Life at North Shingles is organized around a theatrical division of roles. Wragge plays the respectable retired officer escorting his orphaned niece; Magdalen plays the modest, sheltered young gentlewoman; and [[mrs-wragge|Mrs. Wragge]] — the captain's giantess wife — is coached to remain as silent and invisible as possible, her intellectual limitations making her a constant liability to the performance. The domestic comedy of the Wragge household, with the captain shouting instructions at his wife ("Mrs. Wragge is not deaf. She's only a little slow. Constitutionally torpid"), provides the novel's most sustained comic relief while simultaneously darkening the moral atmosphere of the conspiracy.

The scheme reaches its critical phases within these walls. It is at North Shingles that Magdalen first meets Noel Vanstone, that the courtship progresses through carefully orchestrated walks and visits, and that the captain devises his masterstroke — the plan to neutralize Mrs. Lecount by orchestrating her removal to [[zurich|Zurich]] on a false errand. Wragge lays out the strategy with the precision of a military campaign: "To-day you tell Mrs. Lecount I have tried to take you in... To-morrow you cut me on the Parade. The day after you refuse to go out... And the next day to that you go to St. Crux" (ch80). The house thus functions as both domestic space and operational headquarters, its mundane rooms — parlor, dining room, bedrooms — transformed by the conspiracy into the apparatus of fraud.

North Shingles also provides the setting for the moral reckoning that follows the scheme's success. After the marriage to Noel Vanstone is achieved, Magdalen returns to the house no longer as Miss Bygrave but as Mrs. Noel Vanstone — and immediately confronts the horror of what she has done. The domestic interior that served as theater for the conspiracy becomes the prison of her remorse. Collins's architectural imagination — his use of houses as moral indicators — is nowhere more effective than in the contrast between the bustling, scheming North Shingles of the courtship chapters and the tense, guilt-ridden house of the aftermath.
