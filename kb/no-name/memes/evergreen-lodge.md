# Evergreen Lodge, Clifton

The Marrable family residence in Clifton, a suburb of Bristol, serving as a setting for amateur theatricals in the novel's opening chapters and later as [[norah-vanstone|Norah Vanstone]]'s place of employment as governess.

## Overview

Evergreen Lodge is the home of Mr. Marrable, Mrs. Marrable, and their daughter Miss Marrable. Located in Clifton near Bristol, it first appears in the early chapters of the novel as the venue for an ambitious amateur theatrical production — an event that marks [[magdalen-vanstone|Magdalen Vanstone]]'s first experience with performance and disguise, skills she later deploys in her campaign to recover her inheritance.

## Role in the Narrative

Evergreen Lodge serves two distinct narrative functions. In the opening chapters, it is the setting for the private theatricals where Magdalen's talent for performance is first revealed. The Marrables host a production that draws the Vanstone sisters and Frank Clare, establishing key character dynamics before the novel's central catastrophe. As the narrator remarks, the enterprise "stood in need of good wishes to help it" — a humorous understatement given the chaotic rehearsals that follow.

Later in the novel, after the Vanstone family's financial ruin, Evergreen Lodge becomes Norah Vanstone's place of employment as governess to the Marrable family. This represents Norah's path of respectable endurance, in contrast to Magdalen's path of active deception.
