# [[aldborough|Aldborough]] Locations

Aldborough, the small seaside town on the Suffolk coast, is the setting for the novel's second act. Here, [[magdalen-vanstone|Magdalen Vanstone]] — disguised as Miss Bygrave — and Captain Wragge mount their campaign against Noel Vanstone and Mrs. Lecount. The town's geography is Collins' stage machinery: the lodging houses where identities are fabricated, the shoreline walks where plots are hatched, and the social spaces where the comedy of Victorian seaside life plays out against the darker machinery of fraud and revenge. This page aggregates all locations associated with Aldborough.

## Aldborough in No Name

Collins knew Aldborough well — he visited in the 1850s and set portions of the novel there with an almost topographical precision. The key location is [[north-shingles]], the lodging house where Magdalen and Wragge establish their base of operations. Nearby [[sea-view]] and [[dunwich]] extend the coastal geography. For the full index of all places in the novel, see the [[place-index]]. For the novel's other key setting, see the [[london-locations]] index.
