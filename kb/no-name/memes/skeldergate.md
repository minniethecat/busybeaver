# Skeldergate, [[york|York]]

Skeldergate is a historic street in York running along the western bank of the
River Ouse. In Wilkie Collins's *No Name*, it appears as the location of the legal
offices where key proceedings in the Vanstone [[the-will|inheritance]] case unfold, forming the
setting for the novel's Second Scene.

## Role in the Novel

Collins locates the legal machinery of the Vanstone case at Skeldergate, using the
specificity of the York address to ground the novel's legal proceedings in a
concrete geography. The Second Scene — "Skeldergate, York" — establishes the
northern legal circuit as the arena in which the Vanstone sisters' fate will be
determined.

The Skeldergate offices represent the impersonal, geographically distant legal
system that controls the Vanstone inheritance. The journey from [[combe-raven|Combe-Raven]] in
Somerset to Skeldergate in York traces the physical distance between family tragedy
and its legal adjudication.
