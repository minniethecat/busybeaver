## Overview

Mrs. Girdlestone is a minor character in Wilkie Collins's novel *No Name*. She is the married sister of [[george-bartram]] and the niece of [[admiral-bartram]]. Though she never appears directly in the novel, she is significant as the alternate beneficiary named in the Secret Trust — the legal instrument that underpins the novel's central inheritance plot.

## The Secret Trust

Mrs. Girdlestone is named in the Secret Trust created by the late Mr. Vanstone ([[andrew-vanstone]]'s brother). The terms stipulate that if George Bartram fails to marry within six calendar months of the testator's death, the inheritance passes to Mrs. Girdlestone instead.

> "If your nephew fails to comply with these conditions...I request you, in the case here supposed, to pass him over altogether; and to give the fortune left you in my will to his married sister, Mrs. Girdlestone."

The testator specifically wishes to keep Mrs. Girdlestone's potential inheritance secret from "an unscrupulous person" — meaning [[mrs-lecount]] or [[magdalen-vanstone]] — fearing that fraud and conspiracy might be directed at her as they were at George.

## Death and Aftermath

Mrs. Girdlestone dies suddenly of an illness that initially appeared too slight to alarm anyone. Her death is communicated to the household at [[st-crux-in-the-marsh]] by Admiral Bartram in a letter:

> "The illness of my niece, Mrs. Girdlestone — which appeared to be so slight as to alarm none of us, doctors included — has ended fatally. I received this morning the shocking news of her death."

Her husband, Mr. Girdlestone, is described as "quite frantic with grief." [[george-bartram]] goes to his brother-in-law's to superintend the funeral arrangements. The admiral plans to take Mr. Girdlestone away afterward for a change of scene, closing up St. Crux for a month or six weeks.

## Narrative Significance

Mrs. Girdlestone's death has important consequences for the plot. It removes the alternate beneficiary from the Secret Trust, making George's compliance with the marriage condition the sole remaining path for the inheritance. It also causes a temporary upheaval at St. Crux — the house is shut up and a new servant who had been engaged (actually [[magdalen-vanstone]] in disguise) is told to wait six weeks. This delay creates a crucial plot complication.

The servants at St. Crux wear half-mourning for Mrs. Girdlestone, and [[magdalen-vanstone]] in her disguise as Louisa the parlor-maid dons a lavender-colored stuff gown ordered for the occasion.
