# Kingsland

Kingsland is a district in northeast [[london|London]] that appears in Wilkie Collins's
*No Name* as the base of operations for [[mr-de-bleriot|Mr. de Bleriot]], a French
agent employed by [[captain-wragge|Captain Wragge]] for investigative work during
the novel's later stages.

## Role in the Narrative

Kingsland enters the novel in connection with Captain Wragge's counter-espionage
against [[mrs-lecount|Mrs. Lecount]]. Wragge engages de Bleriot, whose offices at
"Dark's Buildings, Kingsland" serve as a discreet base for the intelligence-gathering
activities that are crucial to Wragge's schemes in the post-[[aldborough|Aldborough]]
chapters.

The choice of Kingsland — a location on the northeastern margins of London, away from
the legal centers of Lincoln's Inn and the fashionable squares of Kensington — reflects
the covert, marginal nature of de Bleriot's work. The district's anonymity suits
Wragge's need for an operative who can move unnoticed through the networks of
information that connect the novel's antagonists.

De Bleriot's Kingsland base provides Wragge with a London intelligence hub parallel to
the more formal legal channels of Serle Street and Coney Street, York. This parallel
information network is what allows Wragge to stay one step ahead of Mrs. Lecount's
schemes.
