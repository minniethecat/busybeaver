# Banqueting Hall

The Banqueting Hall is a vast, deteriorating ceremonial hall at [[st-crux-in-the-marsh|St. Crux]],
the estate of [[admiral-bartram|Admiral Bartram]] in Wilkie Collins's *No Name*. Nicknamed
"Freeze-your-Bones" by the old servant Mazey, it forms a desolate passage between the
north and east wings of the house, and becomes the stage for Magdalen Vanstone's
clandestine night searches.

## Description

Collins presents the Banqueting Hall as a monument to decay. A row of lofty windows
extends "over a frontage of more than a hundred feet in length," while on the opposite
wall hangs "a dismal collection of black, begrimed old pictures, rotting from their
frames." The immense space is lit by a single "gaunt ancient tripod of curiously chased
metal, standing lonely in the middle of the hall." The author emphasizes the extreme
cold: "the cold of the marble floor struck through the narrow strip of matting laid
down, parallel with the windows, as a foot-path for passengers across the wilderness of
the room" [[ch057-st-crux]].

## Role in the Narrative

The Banqueting Hall functions as a threshold space — both physical and symbolic. Because
Admiral Bartram lacks the resources to heat the entire house, the hall divides St. Crux
into two separate residences occupied by season: the north wing in winter and spring,
the east wing in summer and autumn [[ch057-st-crux]].

It is through this frozen passage that Magdalen Vanstone, disguised as the parlor-maid
Louisa, crosses repeatedly during her night searches. The hall's emptiness and silence
amplify every sound, making her every crossing a test of nerve. On the night of the key
discovery, the hall becomes the site of her most terrifying experience — an unexplained
sound that "shuddered the stillness of the Banqueting-Hall with the terror of a sound —
a sound faint and momentary, like the distant rushing of the wind" [[ch058-night-search]].

The hall's "Arctic Passage" quality is emphasized by the occasional need to light fires
for days before the admiral inspects unoccupied rooms, after which "Freeze-your-Bones"
is "once more abandoned for weeks and weeks together to damp, desolation, and decay"
[[ch057-st-crux]].
