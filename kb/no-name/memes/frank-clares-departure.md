# [[frank-clare|Frank Clare]]'s Departure

Frank Clare's repeated departures from [[combe-raven|Combe-Raven]] mark the pivotal turning points in
the early romance plot of *No Name*, each one separating [[magdalen-vanstone|Magdalen
Vanstone]] from her beloved and deepening her emotional entanglement even as the
physical distance between them widens. The cumulative effect of these departures —
culminating in the enforced voyage to China after the Vanstone family's financial ruin
— transforms Magdalen from a spirited, mischievous girl into a woman driven by loss.

## Context

Frank Clare was raised under the negligent eye of his father, Mr. Clare, whose
misanthropic philosophy held that Society conspires to reward the undeserving. From an
early age, Frank's characteristic indolence was masked by good manners and a handsome
appearance. His boyhood intimacy with Magdalen at Combe-Raven gradually deepened into
romantic attachment. Mr. Vanstone, out of affection for Frank, took responsibility for
the young man's professional prospects — a task Frank's own father disdainfully
refused:

> "Here is this perfectly hopeless booby, Frank," pursued the philosopher. "He has
> never done anything in his life to help himself, and, as a necessary consequence,
> Society is in a conspiracy to carry him to the top of the tree."
> ([[ch008-CHAPTER-VI]])

Mr. Clare's summary of Frank's prospects was characteristically cynical: "put him into
the army, the Church, politics, what you please, and let him drift: he'll end in being
a general, a bishop, or a minister of State, by dint of the great modern qualification
of doing nothing whatever to deserve his place" ([[ch008-CHAPTER-VI]]).

## The Three Departures

**First Departure — The Civil Engineer**: At seventeen, Frank departed for the north
of England as a pupil to a civil engineer, under terms arranged by Mr. Vanstone. His
progress was slow; reports described him as "a little too ready to despond about
himself." After stints on railway works and in Belgium, the connection was terminated
and Frank returned home — his professional prospects no better than before
([[ch005-CHAPTER-IV]], [[ch005-CHAPTER-V]]).

**Second Departure — The London Office**: A wealthy cousin exerted influence to place
Frank in a great Mercantile Firm in the City. The letter promised that "the first
'good thing' the House had to offer, either at home or abroad, would be placed at his
disposal" ([[ch008-CHAPTER-VI]]). Frank was sent away by the parliamentary train the
next morning. His final evening at Combe-Raven revealed both the depth of his
attachment to Magdalen and the weakness of his character:

> When he rose to go; when he felt the warm, clinging pressure of Magdalen's fingers
> round his hand, and the lock of her hair which she slipped into it at the same
> moment, he became awkward and confused. He might have betrayed Magdalen and
> betrayed himself, but for Mr. Vanstone, who innocently covered his retreat by
> following him out, and patting him on the shoulder all the way. "God bless you,
> Frank!" cried the friendly voice that never had a harsh note in it for anybody.
> "Your fortune's waiting for you. Go in, my boy — go in and win."
> ([[ch009-CHAPTER-IV]])

The effect on Magdalen was profound and visible to all:

> All her movements had grown languid; all her usual occupations were pursued with
> the same weary indifference; she spent hours alone in her own room; she lost her
> interest in being brightly and prettily dressed; her eyes were heavy, her nerves
> were irritable, her complexion was altered visibly for the worse — in one word,
> she had become an oppression and a weariness to herself and to all about her.
> ([[ch011-CHAPTER-III]])

**Third Departure — China**: The City firm, having discovered Frank's limitations,
proposed to send him to their correspondents in China for five years, to learn the
tea and silk trades. Mr. Clare — abandoning his usual philosophy — accompanied Frank
to Combe-Raven and "pointed peremptorily southeastward (from Somersetshire to China)"
with the single word: "Go!" ([[ch013-CHAPTER-I]]).

The China plan was forestalled when Mr. Vanstone, discovering the secret attachment
between Frank and Magdalen, negotiated a compromise: Frank would remain at the London
office for a probationary year, after which — if he proved himself worthy — the
marriage could proceed:

> If, during the year of probation, he failed to justify the confidence placed in
> him — a confidence which had led Mr. Vanstone to take unreservedly upon himself
> the whole responsibility of Frank's future prospects — the marriage scheme was to
> be considered, from that moment, as at an end. ([[ch013-CHAPTER-III]])

This reprieve was shattered by the Vanstone parents' sudden deaths and the legal
revelation that disinherited Magdalen, destroying the financial foundation of the
marriage. Frank's departure for China, once averted by paternal kindness, became an
inescapable necessity.

## Consequences

Frank's departure — and the circumstances that made it final — hardened Magdalen's
resolve. The loss of her fortune, her home, and her beloved within a matter of weeks
transformed her from a spirited girl into a determined woman bent on recovering her
inheritance by any means. The memory of Frank, represented by the lock of his hair
she carried in a white silk bag alongside her father's last words, became both a
source of private anguish and a motive force behind her later schemes.

In a symbolic act of renunciation performed alone on a strange shore, Magdalen
eventually casts the lock of Frank's hair into the sea — severing the last physical
connection to her girlhood love and accepting the solitary path she must now walk
([[ch062-CHAPTER-II]]). His departure also left [[norah-vanstone|Norah]] as
Magdalen's sole remaining familial connection — a relationship tested throughout the
subsequent narrative.
