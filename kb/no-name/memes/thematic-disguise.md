# Disguise and Deception

Disguise is the central theatrical device of Wilkie Collins' *No Name* (1862). [[magdalen-vanstone|Magdalen Vanstone]]'s transformation into the governess Miss Bygrave — and her later impersonations — drives the novel's revenge plot against Noel Vanstone and Mrs. Lecount. This page aggregates every character, event, and place connected to the theme of disguise and deception, from the parlor-maid costume to the legal fictions that enable the plot.

## The Central Disguise Plot

Magdalen Vanstone's use of disguise is not merely theatrical — it reflects the novel's critique of Victorian gender roles and the limited agency available to women. Stripped of her inheritance by the legal system, Magdalen adopts a series of false identities to reclaim what the law denied her. For the full narrative arc, see the events [[magdalens-parlor-maid-disguise]] and the character study of [[magdalen-vanstone]].
