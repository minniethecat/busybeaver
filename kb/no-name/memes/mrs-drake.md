# Mrs. Drake

The formidable housekeeper of [[admiral-bartram|Admiral Bartram]]'s estate at St. Crux-in-the-Marsh, Mrs. Drake is a sharp-eyed professional who runs the household with meticulous authority and becomes an unwitting obstacle in [[magdalen-vanstone|Magdalen Vanstone]]'s scheme.

## Overview

Mrs. Drake serves as housekeeper at St. Crux-in-the-Marsh, the Norfolk estate of Admiral Bartram. She is a veteran domestic manager whose "sharp eyes" miss little, and her correspondence with the Admiral — conducted when he is away from the estate — marks her as a trusted senior servant. When Magdalen infiltrates St. Crux disguised as a new parlor-maid, Mrs. Drake becomes both her supervisor and, inadvertently, her most dangerous observer.

## Role in the Narrative

Mrs. Drake appears in the St. Crux section of the novel, where Magdalen takes employment as a parlor-maid in pursuit of the Secret Trust.

**The Housekeeper's Domain.** Mrs. Drake is introduced through her correspondence with Admiral Bartram ([[ch110-CHAPTER-CX]], [[ch111-CHAPTER-CXI]]), which establishes her as the admiral's reliable agent in household matters. When Magdalen arrives at St. Crux, Mrs. Drake formally installs her in the servant's quarters, leads her through the pantry and linen-room, and shows her the dining-room where she is to lay the cloth for one person — [[george-bartram|Mr. George Bartram]] having not returned with his uncle.

**Testing the New Parlor-Maid.** Mrs. Drake watches Magdalen attentively during her first duties and is forced to acknowledge, reluctantly, "that the new servant thoroughly understood her work." The following day, charged by the admiral with guiding Magdalen through "a voyage of domestic discovery" of the house's rooms and bell-system, Mrs. Drake executes the duty with characteristic formality.

## Relationships

- **[[admiral-bartram|Admiral Bartram]]** — Her employer, with whom she corresponds on household matters and who trusts her to manage St. Crux in his absence.
- **[[magdalen-vanstone|Magdalen Vanstone]]** — Mrs. Drake knows her only as the new parlor-maid; her scrutiny represents a constant threat to Magdalen's disguise.
