# East Library

The East Library is a room in the east wing of [[st-crux-in-the-marsh|St. Crux]],
the estate of [[admiral-bartram|Admiral Bartram]] in Wilkie Collins's *No Name*.
It is the repository of the admiral's private documents and the hiding place of
[[the-secret-trust|the Secret Trust]], making it the focal point of
[[magdalen-vanstone|Magdalen Vanstone]]'s climactic nocturnal search.

## Role in the Narrative

The East Library is where Admiral Bartram keeps his private papers, including the
Secret Trust document that controls the Vanstone inheritance. The admiral's keys to
the library table drawer — where the larger keys to other repositories are kept —
become the object of Magdalen's quest. As Mrs. Lecount's letter recounts, the admiral's
habits regarding these keys were unpredictable: "He had no regular times for leaving
them or for taking them away with him; he had no discoverable reason for now securing
them in the library-table drawer, and now again locking them up in some other place"
[[ch059-east-wing]].

It is in the East Library that the novel's central theft is discovered. Mrs. Lecount
reports that "our new parlor-maid was discovered by Mr. Mazey, at a late hour of the
night (with her master's basket of keys in her possession), prying into the private
documents kept in the east library" [[ch059-discovery]]. This discovery — Magdalen,
as Louisa, caught with the keys — precipitates her flight from St. Crux and the novel's
final sequence of events.

The East Library thus serves as the culminating space of Magdalen's impersonation plot:
the room where her disguise nearly succeeds in retrieving the document that could
restore her fortune, and where it ultimately fails. The room embodies the novel's
thematic tension between legal documents and the human lives they govern.
