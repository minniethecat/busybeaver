# New Street, Spring Gardens, [[london|London]]

New Street, Spring Gardens is the London address where [[norah-vanstone|Norah Vanstone]] takes up her
position as governess after the [[disinheritance|disinheritance]] that destroys her family. It is the
setting for one of the novel's most emotionally charged sequences: Magdalen's
secret observation of her sister's public humiliation.

## Role in the Novel

After being forced from Combe-Raven, Norah Vanstone secures employment as governess
in a household on New Street, Spring Gardens — an address near Charing Cross and
St. James's Park. Her letters to Magdalen describe in detail "her hours for
teaching, her hours of leisure, her hours for walking out with her pupils" through
the nearby parks.

The location becomes the site of a pivotal narrative moment when Magdalen —
disguised beyond recognition — drives to New Street, follows her sister to the
parks, and witnesses Norah being publicly mocked by the children in her charge and
dismissed by their grandmother. The scene crystallises the novel's meditation on
the cost of respectability: Norah's genteel endurance in New Street is, in its own
way, as performative as Magdalen's theatrical deceptions.
