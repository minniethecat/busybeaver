# Sea View Cottage

The [[aldborough|Aldborough]] seaside lodging occupied by [[noel-vanstone|Noel Vanstone]] and his housekeeper Mrs. Lecount during the novel's middle volume, serving as the opposing headquarters to [[captain-wragge|Captain Wragge]]'s North Shingles in [[the-inheritance-scheme|the inheritance scheme]].

## Overview

Sea View Cottage (or simply "Sea View") is one of the principal locations in Aldborough, the Suffolk seaside resort where much of the novel's middle act unfolds. It stands in direct opposition to [[north-shingles]], Captain Wragge's rented residence, with the two dwellings defining the spatial geography of the central conflict between Magdalen's conspiracy and Mrs. Lecount's counter-plot.

## Role in the Narrative

Sea View functions as Mrs. Lecount's operational base. From her bedroom at Sea View, she writes the letter intended to warn Norah Vanstone about Magdalen's scheme, spending "nearly two hours" perfecting her draft. The cottage is where she receives daily bulletins on "Miss Bygrave's" feigned illness, and where Noel Vanstone's growing infatuation with Magdalen increasingly undermines her authority.

The spatial relationship between Sea View and North Shingles drives the novel's social comedy and suspense. Captain Wragge and Mrs. Lecount meet "nearly midway between Sea-view Cottage and North Shingles," each encounter a tactical negotiation. Noel Vanstone's invitation to the Wragge party to visit Sea View marks a turning point — the moment Mrs. Lecount's control over her master begins to slip.
