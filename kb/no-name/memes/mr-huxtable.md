# Mr. Huxtable

A theatrical agent based in [[bow-street|Bow Street]], Covent Garden, Mr. Huxtable is an incidental but pivotal figure in [[magdalen-vanstone|Magdalen Vanstone]]'s flight from home — the professional connection she hopes will launch her stage career, and the trail that leads her pursuers after her.

## Overview

Mr. Huxtable is the actor who supervised the amateur theatrical performance at Clifton in which Magdalen took part. She keeps his professional card, and when she leaves Combe-Raven it is to Mr. Huxtable's address that she first directs the cabman. Though she never actually meets him — he is away in the country when she calls at his London lodgings — his name and profession become the first link in the chain of inquiry that [[captain-wragge|Captain Wragge]] and the private detective follow northward. He is, by all accounts, a "perfectly honest man."

## Role in the Narrative

**The Card.** Magdalen possesses Mr. Huxtable's professional card from the Clifton theatricals — the only tangible connection she has to the world of the stage. When she vanishes, [[miss-garth|Miss Garth]] remembers the card and identifies Huxtable as the actor who supervised the performance ([[ch028-CHAPTER-XXVIII]]). This information sets the pursuit in motion.

**The Trail.** A private inquiry traces Magdalen to Huxtable's Bow Street address, where he was called downstairs early in the morning to speak to a young lady in a cab. He writes his country address on the card for her, and she leaves for the Great Northern terminus. When she reaches his lodgings in York, she finds him away until eight in the evening — and never returns. Huxtable's statement is simple: "he and the young lady have never set eyes on each other." The investigator vouches for his honesty: "To the best of my knowledge, he is a perfectly honest man" ([[ch028-CHAPTER-XXVIII]]).

**The Search in York.** Captain Wragge, tracking Magdalen independently, uses Huxtable's lodgings as a focal point, reasoning that she will reappear there between seven and eight. The streets around Huxtable's neighborhood become the stage for the captain's search ([[ch028-CHAPTER-XXVIII]]).

## Relationships

- **[[magdalen-vanstone|Magdalen Vanstone]]** — He never meets her, but his card and professional identity become the mechanism of her attempted escape into theatrical life.
- **[[captain-wragge|Captain Wragge]]** — Uses Huxtable's address as the pivot of his search for Magdalen in York.
- **[[miss-garth|Miss Garth]]** — Remembers Huxtable's card from the Clifton performance and identifies him as Magdalen's likely contact.
