# Sergeant Bulmer

Sergeant Bulmer is a police detective who appears in the investigation surrounding
the Vanstone [[the-will|inheritance]] case in Wilkie Collins's *No Name*. His involvement
represents the intrusion of official law enforcement into the private catastrophe of
the Vanstone family.

## Role in the Novel

Bulmer's role is that of the professional investigator — a figure who, in the
sensation novel genre, often serves to bridge the gap between private wrongdoing and
public accountability. His investigation touches on Magdalen's flight from home and
the broader legal proceedings that follow from the discovery of the Vanstone
parents' invalid marriage.

Though a minor character, Bulmer embodies one of the novel's recurring tensions:
the relationship between personal morality and legal enforcement. His presence
reminds the reader that the Vanstone catastrophe is not merely a family tragedy but
a matter of public record, subject to the scrutiny of the law's official
representatives.
