# Mr. de Bleriot

A General Agent with an office in [[london|London]], Alfred de Bleriot is engaged by [[mrs-lecount|Mrs. Lecount]] to conduct a confidential investigation into [[noel-vanstone|Noel Vanstone]]'s secret movements and the true identity of his new wife.

## Overview

Mr. de Bleriot is a professional inquiry agent retained by Mrs. Lecount during her stay at St. Crux. Acting on her detailed instructions, he traces Noel Vanstone's movements from Aldborough, verifies the circumstances of his marriage, and investigates the background of the woman calling herself "Mrs. Noel Vanstone." His communications with Mrs. Lecount form the epistolary framework through which the reader learns the progress of her campaign against Magdalen. Though he never appears directly in the narrative, his letters reveal him as a competent, methodical professional who executes his employer's instructions with discretion and efficiency.

## Role in the Narrative

Mr. de Bleriot's involvement is confined to the St. Crux section of the novel (Chapters 80–89), where his correspondence with Mrs. Lecount drives the investigation subplot.

**Initial engagement.** Mrs. Lecount writes to de Bleriot from St. Crux on October 23rd, 1847, invoking "the friendly remembrance of the commercial relations formerly existing between my brother and yourself" ([[ch080-CHAPTER-LXXX]]). She commissions him to discover Noel Vanstone's current place of abode — a matter which Mr. Loscombe, the family lawyer, will not disclose without formal permission.

**Tracing the Vanstones.** De Bleriot's first report ([[ch081-CHAPTER-LXXXI]]) confirms that he has located Noel Vanstone and his wife at a lodging in London, and has begun the surveillance Mrs. Lecount requires. He provides precise addresses, dates of arrival, and observations about the couple's mode of living.

**The investigation expands.** Mrs. Lecount subsequently broadens his brief: she wants information about the woman who married Noel Vanstone — her background, her family, and any evidence that might suggest the marriage was irregular. De Bleriot's letters in chapters 84 and 86 ([[ch084-CHAPTER-LXXXIV]], [[ch086-CHAPTER-LXXXVI]]) report his findings with professional thoroughness, though the ultimate limits of what an inquiry agent can discover — as opposed to what only Mrs. Lecount's personal knowledge can supply — become increasingly apparent.

**Closing the case.** His final reports ([[ch088-CHAPTER-LXXXVIII]], [[ch089-CHAPTER-LXXXIX]]) confirm the essential facts Mrs. Lecount needs to act, bringing the investigation to its conclusion. Throughout the correspondence, de Bleriot signs himself with the formal precision of a man accustomed to legal documents: "ALFRED DE BLERIOT."

## Relationships

- **[[mrs-lecount|Mrs. Lecount]]** — His client, whose late brother had commercial dealings with de Bleriot. She engages him on the strength of this prior connection and provides detailed instructions for each stage of his inquiry.
- **[[noel-vanstone|Noel Vanstone]]** — The subject of de Bleriot's investigation. He traces Vanstone's movements from Aldborough to London after the secret marriage.
- **[[mr-loscombe|Mr. Loscombe]]** — Noel Vanstone's lawyer, whose refusal to disclose his client's whereabouts creates the need for de Bleriot's services.
