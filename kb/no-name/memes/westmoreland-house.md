# Westmoreland House

Westmoreland House, in Kensington, [[london|London]], is the Vanstone family's London residence
in Wilkie Collins's *No Name*. After the family leaves [[combe-raven|Combe-Raven]] in Somerset, it
becomes the primary base for [[norah-vanstone|Norah Vanstone]] and [[miss-garth|Miss Garth]],
and a key location for the novel's correspondence-based revelations.

## Role in the Narrative

Westmoreland House appears initially as the forwarding address for legal communications
regarding the Vanstone inheritance. Mr. Pendril writes to this address, as does Captain
Wragge's correspondent. Letters sent to and from Westmoreland House carry the
intelligence that shapes the novel's final revelations [[ch044-correspondence]].

The house becomes the site of a significant confrontation. Captain Wragge visits
Westmoreland House and leaves a letter explaining his purpose: to obtain Norah's
address and to "acknowledge plainly that I suspected you of being again in
correspondence with Mrs. Lecount" [[ch051-lecounts-return]]. This visit confirms the
web of surveillance and suspicion that characterizes the novel's later chapters.

Later, Westmoreland House serves as the address from which Norah writes to Magdalen,
maintaining the bond between the separated sisters. The house represents the diminished
but stable world of the surviving Vanstone women after the catastrophe at Combe-Raven.
