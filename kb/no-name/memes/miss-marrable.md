# Miss Marrable

A lively and "delightful" young woman from [[evergreen-lodge|Evergreen Lodge, Clifton]], Miss Marrable is the neighbor whose amateur theatrical party provides [[magdalen-vanstone|Magdalen Vanstone]] with her first taste of the stage — a performance that will have far-reaching consequences.

## Overview

Miss Marrable arrives at Combe-Raven with her parents, Mr. and Mrs. Marrable of Evergreen Lodge, Clifton — the father a Bristol merchant and mere acquaintances of the Vanstones. She is, by Magdalen's enthusiastic account, "so impulsive, so sensible, so entirely unaffected," and the two young women immediately strike up a partnership in theatrical enterprise. The private theatricals she organizes at Evergreen Lodge become the occasion of Magdalen's first public performance and the source of the professional card that later sends her to [[mr-huxtable|Mr. Huxtable]]'s door in Bow Street.

## Role in the Narrative

**The Invitation.** The Marrable family's visit to Combe-Raven interrupts an evening of high spirits among the Vanstones and [[frank-clare|Frank Clare]]. Mr. Vanstone groans at the intrusion — "Hang all visitors — they worry one's life out" — but Magdalen is electrified. The Marrables have come with an invitation to a private theatrical performance at Evergreen Lodge ([[ch005-CHAPTER-V]]).

**The Theatrical Conspiracy.** Within five minutes of meeting, Miss Marrable and Magdalen have settled the entire casting. Miss Marrable confesses the company's dilemma: they cannot find a gentleman willing to "grapple with the hideous difficulties of Falkland," the jealous lover. Magdalen immediately volunteers Frank Clare for the role, and when Frank protests, she declares with mock severity: "Miss Marrable and I are both remarkable for the firmness of our characters. When we say Mr. So-and-So is positively to act the part of Falkland, we positively mean it." The performance goes forward — a production marked, by Mr. Vanstone's later account, by "dramatic disasters of which Miss Marrable impersonated the innocent cause" ([[ch005-CHAPTER-V]]).

**The Consequence.** The Clifton theatricals give Magdalen her first experience of performing before an audience. The actor who supervises the production — [[mr-huxtable|Mr. Huxtable]] — gives her his professional card. When the catastrophe of her parents' death and disinheritance leaves her with no conventional path forward, it is this card, and the memory of the Evergreen Lodge stage, that shapes her decision to try her fortune in the theater.

## Relationships

- **[[magdalen-vanstone|Magdalen Vanstone]]** — Fellow theatrical enthusiast; the two young women form an instant bond over the shared enterprise of casting and performing.
- **[[frank-clare|Frank Clare]]** — Press-ganged by Magdalen and Miss Marrable into playing Falkland; his reluctance is no match for their combined determination.
- **[[mr-huxtable|Mr. Huxtable]]** — The professional actor who supervised the Evergreen Lodge performance and whose card becomes Magdalen's ticket to the London stage.
