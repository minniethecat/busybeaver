# Rosemary Lane

Rosemary Lane is a narrow, secluded alley off [[skeldergate|Skeldergate]] in York, used by
[[captain-wragge|Captain Wragge]] as a temporary hiding-place for
[[magdalen-vanstone|Magdalen Vanstone]] during the opening phase of their
conspiracy in Wilkie Collins's *No Name*.

## Description

The lane is described as a passage at the southern end of Skeldergate, connecting
to the paved footway atop the ancient Walls of York:

> A narrow little lane leads up to the paved footway surmounting the ancient Walls
> of York. The one small row of buildings, which is all that the lane possesses, is
> composed of cheap lodging-houses, with an opposite view, at the distance of a few
> feet, of a portion of the massive city wall. This place is called Rosemary Lane.
> Very little light enters it; very few people live in it; the floating population
> of Skeldergate passes it by; and visitors to the Walk on the Walls, who use it as
> the way up or the way down, get out of the dreary little passage as fast as they
> can. [[ch028-CHAPTER-I]]

The lane's obscurity — a "lost corner of York" — makes it a natural refuge for
Wragge's schemes. Its cheap lodging-houses attract no attention, and its position
at the edge of the city wall provides convenient access and escape routes.

## Role in the Narrative

Rosemary Lane is the setting for the earliest phase of the Wragge-Magdalen
conspiracy. Wragge intercepts Magdalen at York and conceals her in one of the
lane's lodging-houses, where the two plan their joint campaign against the
Vanstone inheritance [[ch028-CHAPTER-I]]. It is here that Wragge, presenting
himself as a distant relation, persuades Magdalen to accept his partnership
— a pivotal moment that launches the Aldborough scheme.

The lane also serves as Magdalen's prison. She wakes the following morning to
a "miserable room" whose sordid contrast with her former life shocks her
"sense of bodily self-respect" [[ch030-CHAPTER-III]]. This visceral reaction
reinforces Magdalen's determination to reclaim what has been taken from her.

The strategic significance of Rosemary Lane is underscored by Wragge's escape
plan: he arranges a hired chaise to collect the party from the end of the lane
at dawn, with Mrs. Wragge's disguise accessories and a picnic hamper as cover
[[ch030-CHAPTER-III]]. The lane's connection to the Walk on the Walls provides
both surveillance vantage points and a discreet exit route away from the railway
station, where the lawyer's clerk is waiting.
