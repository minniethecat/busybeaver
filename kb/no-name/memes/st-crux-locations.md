# St. Crux Locations

[[st-crux-in-the-marsh|St. Crux-in-the-Marsh]], Admiral Bartram's decaying Lincolnshire estate, dominates the novel's third act. It is a Gothic space — labyrinthine corridors, a banqueting hall frozen in time, east libraries where secrets are hidden in plain sight — and Collins uses its atmospheric gloom to mirror the moral darkness of the secret trust's final concealment. Here, Magdalen, disguised as a parlor-maid, executes the last and most dangerous phase of her plan. This page aggregates all locations within and around St. Crux.

## St. Crux in the Novel

The estate's key spaces include the [[banqueting-hall]] — a vast, unused ceremonial room that Admiral Bartram preserves exactly as his late wife left it — and the [[east-library]], where the critical document that reveals the secret trust is eventually found. Magdalen's parlor-maid disguise (see [[magdalens-parlor-maid-disguise]]) allows her to navigate this space undetected. For the other major settings, see the [[aldborough-locations]] and [[london-locations]] indices.
