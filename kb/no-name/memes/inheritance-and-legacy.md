# [[the-will|Inheritance]] and Legacy

Inheritance is the engine of Wilkie Collins' *No Name* (1862). The novel opens with the Vanstone parents' sudden death and the catastrophic revelation that their daughters, born before the parents' legal marriage, are illegitimate and thus disinherited. From this wound, every subsequent plot movement follows: Magdalen's campaign of deception, [[the-secret-trust|the secret trust]] that redirects the fortune, and the series of deaths — Noel Vanstone, Admiral Bartram — that remap the inheritance landscape. This page aggregates every character, event, concept, and place connected to the novel's inheritance machinery.

## The Inheritance Arc

The Vanstone fortune — £80,000 — moves through the novel like a character in its own right, passing from Andrew Vanstone to Michael Vanstone, then to Noel Vanstone, into the secret trust administered by Admiral Bartram, and finally, after a chain of deaths and legal discoveries, to Norah and Magdalen. Each transfer is shadowed by the question of legitimacy: whose claim is morally valid, and whose is legally enforceable?
