# Mrs. Marrable

The wife of Mr. Marrable, a Bristol merchant, and the mother of an imperious only daughter whose seventeenth-birthday theatricals at [[evergreen-lodge|Evergreen Lodge, Clifton]], set in motion one of the novel's early set-pieces.

## Overview

Mrs. Marrable belongs to the respectable mercantile society of Clifton, near Bristol. Together with her husband and her daughter — [[miss-marrable|Miss Marrable]] — she visits Combe-Raven to secure the Vanstone sisters' participation in an amateur production of Sheridan's *The Rivals*. As a character she is almost entirely defined by her parental role: the long-suffering mother of a tyrannical only child. Her function in the narrative is to enable the theatrical episode that gives Magdalen her first taste of performance — an experience that foreshadows the disguises and deceptions of the novel's later sections.

## Role in the Narrative

Mrs. Marrable appears only in the early chapters set around Combe-Raven, during the preparations for the play at Evergreen Lodge.

**The visit to Combe-Raven.** Mrs. Marrable, her husband, and her daughter arrive at the Vanstones' home as "mere acquaintances" — Mr. Vanstone has met Mr. Marrable "once or twice, when the girls forced me to take them to parties at Clifton" ([[ch012-CHAPTER-XII]]). Their purpose is to appeal for actors: the play is cast but for the two difficult parts of "Lucy" the waiting-maid and "Falkland," Julia's jealous lover.

**The domestic tyrant.** Collins sketches Mrs. Marrable's situation with economy and humor. Miss Marrable is "that hardest of all born tyrants — an only child" who "had never granted a constitutional privilege to her oppressed father and mother since the time when she cut her first tooth" ([[ch014-CHAPTER-XIV]]). Mrs. Marrable, like her husband, exists in a state of permanent parental submission: she "gave up the drawing-room to be laid waste for a stage and a theater," enduring broken furniture, stained walls, and domestic chaos as the price of her daughter's amusement.

**At the rehearsal.** During the first rehearsal at Evergreen Lodge, Mrs. Marrable hovers anxiously at the edge of the proceedings, asking Miss Garth in a whisper whether Miss Vanstone knows her part ([[ch015-CHAPTER-XV]]). Her maternal anxiety — "anxiously addressing herself to Miss Garth, in a corner of the theater" — is one of several small touches that render her a sympathetic figure despite her near-invisibility in the larger plot.

## Relationships

- **[[miss-marrable|Miss Marrable]]** — Her only daughter, whose determination to celebrate her seventeenth birthday with an amateur theatrical production drives the entire episode. Mrs. Marrable, like her husband, is entirely subject to her daughter's will.
- **Mr. Marrable** — Her husband, a Bristol merchant, who shares her fate of parental subjection to their imperious child.
- **[[magdalen-vanstone|Magdalen Vanstone]]** — Recruited by Miss Marrable to play "Lucy" the waiting-maid. Mrs. Marrable witnesses Magdalen's unexpectedly brilliant first performance at rehearsal.
