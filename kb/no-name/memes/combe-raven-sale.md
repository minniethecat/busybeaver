# The [[combe-raven|Combe-Raven]] Sale

The disposal of the Vanstone family estate at Combe-Raven represents the
definitive material rupture that transforms the novel's opening domestic tragedy
into a campaign for restitution. Following the railway accident that killed both
parents ([[vanstone-parents-death]]), the legal machinery of inheritance
transfers the entire estate to [[michael-vanstone|Michael Vanstone]], the
estranged elder brother, who immediately orders the sale of the property.

## The Event

The sequence begins when [[mr-pendril|Mr. Pendril]], the family solicitor,
reveals to Miss Garth that Andrew Vanstone's marriage to the girls' mother
(post-dating their births) has invalidated his will, leaving Norah and Magdalen
with no legal claim to their father's fortune ([[ch018-CHAPTER-XIII]]). The
entire estate of eighty thousand pounds passes to Michael Vanstone as the next of
kin to the legitimate infant child who survived Mrs. Vanstone by only a few
hours.

Mr. Pendril explains the devastating legal logic:

> The whole fortune of eighty thousand pounds has virtually passed into his
> possession already. ([[ch018-CHAPTER-XIII]])

Michael Vanstone, who had been estranged from his brother for thirty years,
responds to Mr. Pendril's communications through an agent in London. His
instructions ultimately result in the sale of Combe-Raven — the house, its
contents, and the surrounding land. The Vanstone sisters are forced to leave
their childhood home with nothing but personal belongings.

## Consequences

The sale of Combe-Raven is the proximate cause of Magdalen's determination to
recover the inheritance by any means necessary. It transforms the abstract legal
injustice described in [[the-legal-discovery]] into a concrete dispossession:
the physical loss of home, the dispersal of family possessions, and the
reduction of the Vanstone sisters to a state of dependency.

The event also drives Norah and Magdalen into the care of Miss Garth at
Westmoreland House, Kensington, setting the stage for Magdalen's flight
([[magdalen-flees-home]]) and her subsequent recruitment of
[[captain-wragge|Captain Wragge]] as her co-conspirator
([[captain-wragges-recruitment]]).
