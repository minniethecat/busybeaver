# Rye Lane

Rye Lane is the Blackstable street where Rosie Gann grew up, mentioned in the neighbor's gossip that reconstructs her origins: "They lived in the next 'ouse to us in Rye Lane. Me an' Rosie used to go to Sunday school together." [005-075] The street is the geographic marker of Rosie's class position — the humble world she married out of and, in the town's eyes, never fully escaped.

## Overview

Rye Lane functions as social evidence. The neighbor's testimony locates Rosie precisely in Blackstable's class geography — not the vicarage, not Ferne Court, but Rye Lane, where the Gann family lived in proximity to manual laborers and servants. The detail is weaponized by gossip: this is where she *came from*, and where, in the town's judgment, she still belongs.
