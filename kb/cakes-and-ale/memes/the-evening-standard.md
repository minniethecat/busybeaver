# The Evening Standard

The Evening Standard is the London newspaper where "I read … an article by Mr. Evelyn Waugh in the course of which he remarked that to write novels in the first person was a contemptible practice." [016-017] The reference is doubly ironic: Ashenden (a first-person narrator in a first-person novel) is being told by a real writer (Waugh, who would later write first-person novels himself) that his narrative method is disreputable.

## Role in the Novel

The Evening Standard mention is Maugham at his most playful. The nested ironies — a fictional first-person narrator responding to a real writer's criticism of first-person narration, in a novel widely understood to be a roman-à-clef — collapse the distance between fiction and reality. The newspaper is the medium through which the literary world talks to itself, and Maugham uses it to wink at the reader.
