# Victoria Station

Victoria Station is the London railway terminus from which Ashenden and Alroy Kear depart for Blackstable. "Alroy Kear and I, as arranged, met on Friday at Victoria Station to catch the five ten to Blackstable." [022-001] The station is a threshold — the point of transition between London's literary world and the provincial past that awaits.

## Overview

The station's function in the novel is structural. It marks the shift from the present-day frame (Kear's biographical project, London's clubs) to the world of memory (Blackstable, the Driffields' early life). The train journey that follows is a journey into the past — and the station is the point of no return.

## Significance

Victoria Station embodies the novel's geographic dualism: London versus Kent, present versus past, reputation versus reality. The "five ten to Blackstable" [022-001] is a commuter train that carries the weight of the novel's thematic architecture.
