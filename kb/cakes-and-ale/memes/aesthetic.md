# Aesthetic Value

The criteria by which literary works are judged — and whether those criteria are objective or socially constructed.

## 定义与语境

This theme runs through *Cakes and Ale* as part of Maugham's broader satire of the literary world. The narrator Ashenden observes it with characteristic detachment, reporting without moralizing.

## 在小说中的展开

### 核心论述

The theme emerges in several key passages: [[002-081]], [[002-083]], [[011-004]].

The novel's treatment of this subject reflects Maugham's own experience as a literary insider who understood the machinery of reputation from within. Unlike the earnest biographer Alroy Kear, the narrator Ashenden is willing to acknowledge complexity and contradiction.

## 人物关联

This theme intersects with multiple characters and their relationships throughout the narrative.

## 叙事功能

This thematic thread contributes to the novel's larger project of demystifying literary greatness and exposing the social machinery behind artistic reputation.
