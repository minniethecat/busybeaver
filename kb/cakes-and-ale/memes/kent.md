# Kent

Kent is the English county that forms the novel's geographic ground — the landscape of Blackstable, Ferne Court, and the "Kentish elms" under which Driffield's parents lie buried. It represents rootedness, provincial life, and the enduring presence of the past, in pointed contrast to the metropolitan flux of London. [003-041]

## Overview

The narrator describes Blackstable as "a little Kentish town by the sea" [003-003] — the county identity is foregrounded. Kent is not merely a location but a character: its "buxom Kentish girls" [004-031], its "Kentish elms" [003-041], its pub names (the Duke of Kent [003-007]) are woven into the novel's texture.

## Appearances in the Novel

### The Landscape of Memory

Kent is the setting for all of Ashenden's boyhood memories. The churchyard "where under the Kentish elms his father and mother lay" [003-041] is the novel's most explicit image of rootedness — the dead remain in the landscape, and the past is not so easily erased.

### The Return

When Driffield, in his later years, "finally bought Ferne Court and settled down in the district" [011-060], he was returning to the county of his origins. His literary career took him to London and beyond, but Kent reclaimed him — the arc of a life that begins and ends in the same soil.

## Geography and Atmosphere

Kent in the novel is defined by two features: the sea and the elms. The sea provides the "pleasant tang" [003-007] that gives Blackstable its distinctive atmosphere; the elms provide the verticality, the rootedness, the sense of deep time that literary reputation — so fragile, so contingent — cannot match.

## Narrative Function

Kent serves as the novel's geographic counterweight to London. Where London is social, artificial, and reputation-driven, Kent is natural, enduring, and indifferent to literary fashion. The tension between the two is never resolved — it is the novel's fundamental geographic dialectic.

## Selected Quotations

> "I lived with an uncle and aunt on the outskirts of a little Kentish town by the sea. It was called Blackstable." [003-003]
