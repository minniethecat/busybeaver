# The Blue Morocco Manuscript

At Ferne Court, "Mrs. Driffield took from a shelf a manuscript bound in blue morocco, and while the rest of the party reverently examined it I had a look at the books with which the room was lined." [004-055] The manuscript is a fetish object — the physical trace of literary creation, preserved and displayed for the reverence of visitors.

## Role in the Novel

The blue morocco manuscript is the novel's quietest satire of literary culture. It is displayed like a religious relic, handled with ceremony, and treated as evidence of genius. But Ashenden, the narrator, is more interested in the books on the shelves — the actual reading matter, rather than the curated artifact. The manuscript is the biography in embryonic form: the raw material of writing, bound in expensive leather and drained of life.
