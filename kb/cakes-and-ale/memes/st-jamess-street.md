# St. James's Street

St. James's Street is the London club district where Alroy Kear's club is located — and where the novel's central negotiation takes place. "A little before one I strolled round to my own and had the cocktail, which I was pretty sure Roy would not offer me. Then I walked down St. James's Street, looking idly at the shop windows." [002-001]

## Overview

The street is the geographic heart of masculine literary London — the world of clubs that excludes women (Rosie, Amy, Mrs. Barton Trafford's salon) and operates by different rules. Ashenden's walk down St. James's Street [002-001] is a walk through the infrastructure of literary power.

## Significance

St. James's Street represents the institutional side of the literary world — clubs rather than salons, negotiation rather than cultivation. It is Kear's territory, and Ashenden navigates it with the ironic detachment of someone who knows the game too well to be impressed by the setting.
