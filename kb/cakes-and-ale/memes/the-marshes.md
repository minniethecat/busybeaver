# The Marshes

The marshes are the liminal landscape at the edge of Blackstable, mentioned in the narrator's topographic description of the town: "a certain number of short streets, recently built, that ended on one side in the country and on the other in the marshes." [003-006]

## Overview

The marshes represent the wild edge of Blackstable — the point where the town's grid of streets dissolves into an older, less manageable landscape. Like the sea, they are a reminder of a world beyond the narrow social judgments that define Blackstable life.

## Significance

As a geographic feature, the marshes function as a boundary: between town and country, between the made and the natural, between the social world and the indifferent landscape that surrounds it. Their presence at the edge of Blackstable's topography is quietly suggestive — the town's certainties end where the marshes begin.
