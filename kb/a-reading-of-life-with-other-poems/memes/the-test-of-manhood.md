## THE TEST OF MANHOOD

[004-001] LIKE a flood river whirled at rocky banks,

[004-002] An army issues out of wilderness,

[004-003] With battle plucking round its ragged flanks;

[004-004] Obstruction in the van; insane excess

[004-005] Oft at the heart; yet hard the onward stress

[004-006] Unto more spacious, where move ordered ranks,

[004-007] And rise hushed temples built of shapely stone,

[004-008] The work of hands not pledged to grind or slay.

[004-009] They gave our earth a dress of flesh on bone;

[004-010] A tongue to speak with answering heaven gave they.

[004-011] Then was the gracious birth of man's new day;

[004-012] Divided from the haunted night it shone.

[004-013] That quiet dawn was Reverence; whereof sprang

[004-014] Ethereal Beauty in full morningtide.

[004-015] Another sun had risen to clasp his bride:

[004-016] It was another earth unto him sang.

[004-017] Came Reverence from the Huntress on her heights?

[004-018] From the Persuader came it, in those vales

[004-019] Whereunto she melodiously invites,

[004-020] Her troops of eager servitors regales?

[004-021] Not far those two great Powers of Nature speed

[004-022] Disciple steps on earth when sole they lead;

[004-023] Nor either points for us the way of flame.

[004-024] From him predestined mightier it came;

[004-025] His task to hold them both in breast, and yield

[004-026] Their dues to each, and of their war be field.

[004-027] The foes that in repulsion never ceased,

[004-028] Must he, who once has been the goodly beast

[004-029] Of one or other, at whose beck he ran,

[004-030] Constrain to make him serviceable man;

[004-031] Offending neither, nor the natural claim

[004-032] Each pressed, denying, for his true man's name.

[004-033] Ah, what a sweat of anguish in that strife

[004-034] To hold them fast conjoined within him still;

[004-035] Submissive to his will

[004-036] Along the road of life!

[004-037] And marvel not he wavered if at whiles

[004-038] The forward step met frowns, the backward smiles.

[004-039] For Pleasure witched him her sweet cup to drain;

[004-040] Repentance offered ecstasy in pain.

[004-041] Delicious licence called it Nature's cry;

[004-042] Ascetic rigours crushed the fleshly sigh;

[004-043] A tread on shingle timed his lame advance

[004-044] Flung as the die of Bacchanalian Chance,

[004-045] He of the troubled marching army leaned

[004-046] On godhead visible, on godhead screened;

[004-047] The radiant roseate, the curtained white;

[004-048] Yet sharp his battle strained through day, through night.

[004-049] He drank of fictions, till celestial aid

[004-050] Might seem accorded when he fawned and prayed;

[004-051] Sagely the generous Giver circumspect,

[004-052] To choose for grants the egregious, his elect;

[004-053] And ever that imagined succour slew

[004-054] The soul of brotherhood whence Reverence drew.

[004-055] In fellowship religion has its founts:

[004-056] The solitary his own God reveres:

[004-057] Ascend no sacred Mounts

[004-058] Our hungers or our fears.

[004-059] As only for the numbers Nature's care

[004-060] Is shown, and she the personal nothing heeds,

[004-061] So to Divinity the spring of prayer

[004-062] From brotherhood the one way upward leads.

[004-063] Like the sustaining air

[004-064] Are both for flowers and weeds.

[004-065] But he who claims in spirit to be flower,

[004-066] Will find them both an air that doth devour.

[004-067] Whereby he smelt his treason, who implored

[004-068] External gifts bestowed but on the sword;

[004-069] Beheld himself, with less and less disguise,

[004-070] Through those blood-cataracts which dimmed his eyes,

[004-071] His army's foe, condemned to strive and fail;

[004-072] See a black adversary's ghost prevail;

[004-073] Never, though triumphs hailed him, hope to win

[004-074] While still the conflict tore his breast within.

[004-075] Out of that agony, misread for those

[004-076] Imprisoned Powers warring unappeased,

[004-077] The ghost of his black adversary rose,

[004-078] To smother light, shut heaven, show earth diseased.

[004-079] And long with him was wrestling ere emerged

[004-080] A mind to read in him the reflex shade

[004-081] Of its fierce torment; this way, that way urged;

[004-082] By craven compromises hourly swayed.

[004-083] Crouched as a nestling, still its wings untried,

[004-084] The man's mind opened under weight of cloud.

[004-085] To penetrate the dark was it endowed;

[004-086] Stood day before a vision shooting wide.

[004-087] Whereat the spectral enemy lost form;

[004-088] The traversed wilderness exposed its track.

[004-089] He felt the far advance in looking back;

[004-090] Thence trust in his foot forward through the storm.

[004-091] Under the low-browed tempest's eye of ire,

[004-092] That ere it lightened smote a coward heart,

[004-093] Earth nerved her chastened son to hail athwart

[004-094] All ventures perilous his shrouded Sire;

[004-095] A stranger still, religiously divined;

[004-096] Not yet with understanding read aright.

[004-097] But when the mind, the cherishable mind,

[004-098] The multitude's grave shepherd, took full flight,

[004-099] Himself as mirror raised among his kind,

[004-100] He saw, and first of brotherhood had sight:

[004-101] Knew that his force to fly, his will to see,

[004-102] His heart enlarged beyond its ribbed domain,

[004-103] Had come of many a grip in mastery,

[004-104] Which held conjoined the hostile rival twain,

[004-105] And of his bosom made him lord, to keep

[004-106] The starry roof of his unruffled frame

[004-107] Awake to earth, to heaven, and plumb the deep

[004-108] Below, above, aye with a wistful aim.

[004-109] The mastering mind in him, by tempests blown,

[004-110] By traitor inmates baited, upward burned;

[004-111] Perforce of growth, the Master mind discerned,

[004-112] The Great Unseen, nowise the Dark Unknown.

[004-113] To whom unwittingly did he aspire

[004-114] In wilderness, where bitter was his need:

[004-115] To whom in blindness, as an earthy seed

[004-116] For light and air, he struck through crimson mire.

[004-117] But not ere he upheld a forehead lamp,

[004-118] And viewed an army, once the seeming doomed,

[004-119] All choral in its fruitful garden camp,

[004-120] The spiritual the palpable illumed.

[004-121] This gift of penetration and embrace,

[004-122] His prize from tidal battles lost or won,

[004-123] Reveals the scheme to animate his race:

[004-124] How that it is a warfare but begun;

[004-125] Unending; with no Power to interpose;

[004-126] No prayer, save for strength to keep his ground,

[004-127] Heard of the Highest; never battle's close,

[004-128] The victory complete and victor crowned:

[004-129] Nor solace in defeat, save from that sense

[004-130] Of strength well spent, which is the strength renewed.

[004-131] In manhood must he find his competence;

[004-132] In his clear mind the spiritual food:

[004-133] God being there while he his fight maintains;

[004-134] Throughout his mind the Master Mind being there,

[004-135] While he rejects the suicide despair;

[004-136] Accepts the spur of explicable pains;

[004-137] Obedient to Nature, not her slave:

[004-138] Her lord, if to her rigid laws he bows;

[004-139] Her dust, if with his conscience he plays knave,

[004-140] And bids the Passions on the Pleasures browse:--

[004-141] Whence Evil in a world unread before;

[004-142] That mystery to simple springs resolved.

[004-143] His God the Known, diviner to adore,

[004-144] Shows Nature's savage riddles kindly solved.

[004-145] Inconscient, insensitive, she reigns

[004-146] In iron laws, though rapturous fair her face.

[004-147] Back to the primal brute shall he retrace

[004-148] His path, doth he permit to force her chains

[004-149] A soft Persuader coursing through his veins,

[004-150] An icy Huntress stringing to the chase:

[004-151] What one the flash disdains;

[004-152] What one so gives it grace.

## Section 3

[004-153] But is he rightly manful in her eyes,

[004-154] A splendid bloodless knight to gain the skies,

[004-155] A blood-hot son of Earth by all her signs,

[004-156] Desireing and desireable he shines;

[004-157] As peaches, that have caught the sun's uprise

[004-158] And kissed warm gold till noonday, even as vines.

[004-159] Earth fills him with her juices, without fear

[004-160] That she will cast him drunken down the steeps.

[004-161] All woman is she to this man most dear;

[004-162] He sows for bread, and she in spirit reaps:

[004-163] She conscient, she sensitive, in him;

[004-164] With him enwound, his brave ambition hers:

[004-165] By him humaner made; by his keen spurs

[004-166] Pricked to race past the pride in giant limb,

[004-167] Her crazy adoration of big thews,

[004-168] Proud in her primal sons, when crags they hurled,

[004-169] Were thunder spitting lightnings on the world

[004-170] In daily deeds, and she their evening Muse.

[004-171] This man, this hero, works not to destroy;

[004-172] This godlike--as the rock in ocean stands;--

[004-173] He of the myriad eyes, the myriad hands

[004-174] Creative; in his edifice has joy.

[004-175] How strength may serve for purity is shown

[004-176] When he himself can scourge to make it clean.

[004-177] Withal his pitch of pride would not disown

[004-178] A sober world that walks the balanced mean

[004-179] Between its tempters, rarely overthrown:

[004-180] And such at times his army's march has been.

[004-181] Near is he to great Nature in the thought

[004-182] Each changing Season intimately saith,

[004-183] That nought save apparition knows the death;

[004-184] To the God-lighted mind of man 'tis nought.

[004-185] She counts not loss a word of any weight;

[004-186] It may befal his passions and his greeds

[004-187] To lose their treasures, like the vein that bleeds,

[004-188] But life gone breathless will she reinstate.

[004-189] Close on the heart of Earth his bosom beats,

[004-190] When he the mandate lodged in it obeys,

[004-191] Alive to breast a future wrapped in haze,

[004-192] Strike camp, and onward, like the wind's cloud-fleets.

[004-193] Unresting she, unresting he, from change

[004-194] To change, as rain of cloud, as fruit of rain;

[004-195] She feels her blood-tree throbbing in her grain,

[004-196] Yet skyward branched, with loftier mark and range.

[004-197] No miracle the sprout of wheat from clod,

[004-198] She knows, nor growth of man in grisly brute;

[004-199] But he, the flower at head and soil at root,

[004-200] Is miracle, guides he the brute to God.

[004-201] And that way seems he bound; that way the road,

[004-202] With his dark-lantern mind, unled, alone,

[004-203] Wearifully through forest-tracts unsown,

[004-204] He travels, urged by some internal goad.

[004-205] Dares he behold the thing he is, what thing

[004-206] He would become is in his mind its child;

[004-207] Astir, demanding birth to light and wing;

[004-208] For battle prompt, by pleasure unbeguiled.

[004-209] So moves he forth in faith, if he has made

[004-210] His mind God's temple, dedicate to truth.

[004-211] Earth's nourishing delights, no more gainsaid,

[004-212] He tastes, as doth the bridegroom rich in youth.

[004-213] Then knows he Love, that beckons and controls;

[004-214] The star of sky upon his footway cast;

[004-215] Then match in him who holds his tempters fast,

[004-216] The body's love and mind's, whereof the soul's.

[004-217] Then Earth her man for woman finds at last,

[004-218] To speed the pair unto her goal of goals.

[004-219] Or is't the widowed's dream of her new mate?

[004-220] Seen has she virulent days of heat in flood;

[004-221] The sly Persuader snaky in his blood;

[004-222] With her the barren Huntress alternate;

[004-223] His rough refractory off on kicking heels

[004-224] To rear; the man dragged rearward, shamed, amazed;

[004-225] And as a torrent stream where cattle grazed,

[004-226] His tumbled world. What, then, the faith she feels?

[004-227] May not his aspect, like her own so fair

[004-228] Reflexively, the central force belie,

[004-229] And he, the once wild ocean storming sky,

[004-230] Be rebel at the core? What hope is there?

[004-231] 'Tis that in each recovery he preserves,

[004-232] Between his upper and his nether wit,

[004-233] Sense of his march ahead, more brightly lit;

[004-234] He less the shaken thing of lusts and nerves;

[004-235] With such a grasp upon his brute as tells

[004-236] Of wisdom from that vile relapsing spun.

[004-237] A Sun goes down in wasted fire, a Sun

[004-238] Resplendent springs, to faith refreshed compels.
