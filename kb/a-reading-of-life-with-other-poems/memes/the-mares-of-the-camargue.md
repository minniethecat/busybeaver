## THE MARES OF THE CAMARGUE

[018-001] FROM THE _Mireio_ OF MISTRAL

[018-002] A HUNDRED mares, all white! their manes

[018-003] Like mace-reed of the marshy plains

[018-004] Thick-tufted, wavy, free o' the shears:

[018-005] And when the fiery squadron rears

[018-006] Bursting at speed, each mane appears

[018-007] Even as the white scarf of a fay

[018-008] Floating upon their necks along the heavens away.

[018-009] O race of humankind, take shame!

[018-010] For never yet a hand could tame,

[018-011] Nor bitter spur that rips the flanks subdue

[018-012] The mares of the Camargue. I have known,

[018-013] By treason snared, some captives shown;

[018-014] Expatriate from their native Rhone,

[018-015] Led off, their saline pastures far from view:

[018-016] And on a day, with prompt rebound,

[018-017] They have flung their riders to the ground,

[018-018] And at a single gallop, scouring free,

[018-019] Wide-nostril'd to the wind, twice ten

[018-020] Of long marsh-leagues devour'd, and then,

[018-021] Back to the Vacares again,

[018-022] After ten years of slavery just to breathe salt sea

[018-023] For of this savage race unbent,

[018-024] The ocean is the element.

[018-025] Of old escaped from Neptune's car, full sure,

[018-026] Still with the white foam fleck'd are they,

[018-027] And when the sea puffs black from grey,

[018-028] And ships part cables, loudly neigh

[018-029] The stallions of Camargue, all joyful in the roar;

[018-030] And keen as a whip they lash and crack

[018-031] Their tails that drag the dust, and back

[018-032] Scratch up the earth, and feel, entering their flesh, where he,

[018-033] The God, drives deep his trident teeth,

[018-034] Who in one horror, above, beneath,

[018-035] Bids storm and watery deluge seethe,

[018-036] And shatters to their depths the abysses of the sea.

[018-037] _Cant._ iv.
