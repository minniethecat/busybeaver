## WRITTEN FOR THE CHARING CROSS ALBUM

[012-001] I

[012-002] SEEN, too clear and historic within us, our sins of omission

[012-003] Frown when the Autumn days strike us all ruthlessly bare.

[012-004] They of our mortal diseases find never healing physician;

[012-005] Errors they of the soul, past the one hope to repair.

[012-006] II

[012-007] Sunshine might we have been unto seed under soil, or have scattered

[012-008] Seed to ascendant suns brighter than any that shone.

[012-009] Even the limp-legged beggar a sick desperado has flattered

[012-010] Back to a half-sloughed life cheered by the mere human tone.

[012-011] ALTERNATION

[012-012] BETWEEN the fountain and the rill

[012-013] I passed, and saw the mighty will

[012-014] To leap at sky; the careless run,

[012-015] As earth would lead her little son.

[012-016] Beneath them throbs an urgent well,

[012-017] That here is play, and there is war.

[012-018] I know not which had most to tell

[012-019] Of whence we spring and what we are.

[012-020] HAWARDEN

[012-021] WHEN comes the lighted day for men to read

[012-022] Life's meaning, with the work before their hands

[012-023] Till this good gift of breath from debt is freed,

[012-024] Earth will not hear her children's wailful bands

[012-025] Deplore the chieftain fall'n in sob and dirge;

[012-026] Nor they look where is darkness, but on high.

[012-027] The sun that dropped down our horizon's verge,

[012-028] Illumes his labours through the travelled sky,

[012-029] Now seen in sum, most glorious; and 'tis known

[012-030] By what our warrior wrought we hold him fast.

[012-031] A splendid image built of man has flown;

[012-032] His deeds inspired of God outstep a Past.

[012-033] Ours the great privilege to have had one

[012-034] Among us who celestial tasks has done.
