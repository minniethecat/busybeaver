## FORESIGHT AND PATIENCE

[016-001] SPRUNG of the father blood, the mother brain,

[016-002] Are they who point our pathway and sustain.

[016-003] They rarely meet; one soars, one walks retired.

[016-004] When they do meet, it is our earth inspired.

[016-005] To see Life's formless offspring and subdue

[016-006] Desire of times unripe, we have these two,

[016-007] Whose union is right reason: join they hands,

[016-008] The world shall know itself and where it stands;

[016-009] What cowering angel and what upright beast

[016-010] Make man, behold, nor count the low the least,

[016-011] Nor less the stars have round it than its flowers.

[016-012] When these two meet, a point of time is ours.

[016-013] As in a land of waterfalls, that flow

[016-014] Smooth for the leap on their great voice below,

[016-015] Some eddies near the brink borne swift along,

[016-016] Will capture hearing with the liquid song,

[016-017] So, while the headlong world's imperious force

[016-018] Resounded under, heard I these discourse.

[016-019] First words, where down my woodland walk she led,

[016-020] To her blind sister Patience, Foresight said:

[016-021] --Your faith in me appals, to shake my own,

[016-022] When still I find you in this mire alone.

[016-023] --The few steps taken at a funeral pace

[016-024] By men had slain me but for those you trace.

[016-025] --Look I once back, a broken pinion I:

[016-026] Black as the rebel angels rained from sky!

[016-027] --Needs must you drink of me while here you live,

[016-028] And make me rich in feeling I can give.

[016-029] --A brave To-be is dawn upon my brow:

[016-030] Yet must I read my sister for the How.

[016-031] My daisy better knows her God of beams

[016-032] Than doth an eagle that to mount him seems.

[016-033] She hath the secret never fieriest reach

[016-034] Of wing shall master till men hear her teach.

[016-035] --Liker the clod flaked by the driving plough,

[016-036] My semblance when I have you not as now.

[016-037] The quiet creatures who escape mishap

[016-038] Bear likeness to pure growths of the green sap:

[016-039] A picture of the settled peace desired

[016-040] By cowards shunning strife or strivers tired.

[016-041] I listen at their breasts: is there no jar

[016-042] Of wrestlings and of stranglings, dead they are,

[016-043] And such a picture as the piercing mind

[016-044] Ranks beneath vegetation. Not resigned

[016-045] Are my true pupils while the world is brute.

[016-046] What edict of the stronger keeps me mute,

[016-047] Stronger impels the motion of my heart.

[016-048] I am not Resignation's counterpart.

[016-049] If that I teach, 'tis little the dry word,

[016-050] Content, but how to savour hope deferred.

[016-051] We come of earth, and rich of earth may be;

[016-052] Soon carrion if very earth are we!

[016-053] The coursing veins, the constant breath, the use

[016-054] Of sleep, declare that strife allows short truce;

[016-055] Unless we clasp decay, accept defeat,

[016-056] And pass despised; "a-cold for lack of heat,"

[016-057] Like other corpses, but without death's plea.

[016-058] --My sister calls for battle; is it she?

[016-059] --Rather a world of pressing men in arms,

[016-060] Than stagnant, where the sensual piper charms

[016-061] Each drowsy malady and coiling vice

[016-062] With dreams of ease whereof the soul pays price!

[016-063] No home is here for peace while evil breeds,

[016-064] While error governs, none; and must the seeds

[016-065] You sow, you that for long have reaped disdain,

[016-066] Lie barren at the doorway of the brain,

[016-067] Let stout contention drive deep furrows, blood

[016-068] Moisten, and make new channels of its flood!

[016-069] --My sober little maid, when we meet first,

[016-070] Drinks of me ever with an eager thirst.

[016-071] So can I not of her till circumstance

[016-072] Drugs cravings. Here we see how men advance

[016-073] A doubtful foot, but circle if much stirred,

[016-074] Like dead weeds on whipped waters. Shout the word

[016-075] Prompting their hungers, and they grandly march,

[016-076] As to band-music under Victory's arch.

[016-077] Thus was it, and thus is it; save that then

[016-078] The beauty of frank animals had men.

[016-079] --Observe them, and down rearward for a term,

[016-080] Gaze to the primal twistings of the worm.

[016-081] Thence look this way, across the fields that show

[016-082] Men's early form of speech for Yes and No.

[016-083] My sister a bruised infant's utterance had;

[016-084] And issuing stronger, to mankind 'twas mad.

[016-085] I knew my home where I had choice to feel

[016-086] The toad beneath a harrow or a heel.

[016-087] --Speak of this Age.

[016-088] --When you it shall discern

[016-089] Bright as you are, to me the Age will turn.

[016-090] --For neither of us has it any care;

[016-091] Its learning is through Science to despair.

[016-092] --Despair lies down and grovels, grapples not

[016-093] With evil, casts the burden of its lot.

[016-094] This Age climbs earth.

[016-095] --To challenge heaven.

[016-096] --Not less

[016-097] The lower deeps. It laughs at Happiness!

[016-098] That know I, though the echoes of it wail,

[016-099] For one step upward on the crags you scale.

[016-100] Brave is the Age wherein the word will rust,

[016-101] Which means our soul asleep or body's lust,

[016-102] Until from warmth of many breasts, that beat

[016-103] A temperate common music, sunlike heat

[016-104] The happiness not predatory sheds!

[016-105] --But your fierce Yes and No of butting heads,

[016-106] Now rages to outdo a horny Past.

[016-107] Shades of a wild Destroyer on the vast

[016-108] Are thrown by every novel light upraised.

[016-109] The world's whole round smokes ominously, amazed

[016-110] And trembling as its pregnant AEtna swells.

[016-111] Combustibles on hot combustibles

[016-112] Run piling, for one spark to roll in fire

[016-113] The mountain-torrent of infernal ire

[016-114] And leave the track of devils where men built.

[016-115] Perceptive of a doom, the sinner's guilt

[016-116] Confesses in a cry for help shrill loud,

[016-117] If drops the chillness of a passing cloud,

[016-118] To conscience, reason, human love; in vain:

[016-119] None save they but the souls which them contain.

[016-120] No extramural God, the God within

[016-121] Alone gives aid to city charged with sin.

[016-122] A world that for the spur of fool and knave,

[016-123] Sweats in its laboratory, what shall save?

[016-124] But men who ply their wits in such a school,

[016-125] Must pray the mercy of the knave and fool.

[016-126] --Much have I studied hard Necessity!

[016-127] To know her Wisdom's mother, and that we

[016-128] May deem the harshness of her later cries

[016-129] In labour a sure goad to prick the wise,

[016-130] If men among the warnings which convulse,

[016-131] Can gravely dread without the craven's pulse.

[016-132] Long ere the rising of this Age of ours,

[016-133] The knave and fool were stamped as monstrous Powers.

[016-134] Of human lusts and lassitudes they spring,

[016-135] And are as lasting as the parent thing.

[016-136] Yet numbering locust hosts, bent they to drill,

[016-137] They might o'ermatch and have mankind at will.

[016-138] Behold such army gathering: ours the spur,

[016-139] No scattered foe to face, but Lucifer.

[016-140] Not fool or knave is now the enemy

[016-141] O'ershadowing men, 'tis Folly, Knavery!

[016-142] A sea; nor stays that sea the bastioned beach.

[016-143] Now must the brother soul alive in each,

[016-144] His traitorous individual devildom

[016-145] Hold subject lest the grand destruction come.

[016-146] Dimly men see it menacing apace

[016-147] To overthrow, perchance uproot the race.

[016-148] Within, without, they are a field of tares:

[016-149] Fruitfuller for them when the contest squares,

[016-150] And wherefore warrior service they must yield,

[016-151] Shines visible as life on either field.

[016-152] That is my comfort, following shock on shock,

[016-153] Which sets faith quaking on their firmest rock.

[016-154] Since with his weapons, all the arms of Night,

[016-155] Frail men have challenged Lucifer to fight,

[016-156] Have matched in hostile ranks, enrolled, erect,

[016-157] The human and Satanic intellect,

[016-158] Determined for their uses to control

[016-159] What forces on the earth and under roll,

[016-160] Their granite rock runs igneous; now they stand

[016-161] Pledged to the heavens for safety of their land.

[016-162] They cannot learn save grossly, gross that are:

[016-163] Through fear they learn whose aid is good in war.

[016-164] --My sister, as I read them in my glass,

[016-165] Their field of tares they take for pasture grass.

[016-166] How waken them that have not any bent

[016-167] Save browsing--the concrete indifferent!

[016-168] Friend Lucifer supplies them solid stuff:

[016-169] They fear not for the race when full the trough.

[016-170] They have much fear of giving up the ghost;

[016-171] And these are of mankind the unnumbered host.

[016-172] --If I could see with you, and did not faint

[016-173] In beating wing, the future I would paint.

[016-174] Those massed indifferents will learn to quake:

[016-175] Now meanwhile is another mass awake,

[016-176] Once denser than the grunters of the sty.

[016-177] If I could see with you! Could I but fly!

[016-178] --The length of days that you with them have housed,

[016-179] An outcast else, approves their cause espoused.

[016-180] --O true, they have a cause, and woe for us,

[016-181] While still they have a cause too piteous!

[016-182] Yet, happy for us when, their cause defined,

[016-183] They walk no longer with a stumbler blind,

[016-184] And quicken in the virtue of their cause,

[016-185] To think me a poor mouther of old saws!

[016-186] I wait the issue of a battling Age;

[016-187] The toilers with your "troughsters" now engage;

[016-188] Instructing them through their acutest sense,

[016-189] How close the dangers of indifference!

[016-190] Already have my people shown their worth,

[016-191] More love they light, which folds the love of Earth.

[016-192] That love to love of labour leads: thence love

[016-193] Of humankind--earth's incense flung above.

[016-194] --Admit some other features: Faithless, mean;

[016-195] Encased in matter; vowed to Gods obscene;

[016-196] Contemptuous of the impalpable, it swells

[016-197] On Doubt; for pastime swallows miracles;

[016-198] And if I bid it face what _I_ observe,

[016-199] Declares me hoodwinked by my optic nerve!

[016-200] --Oft has your prophet, for reward of toil,

[016-201] Seen nests of seeming cockatrices coil:

[016-202] Disowned them as the unholiest of Time,

[016-203] Which were his offspring, born of flame on slime.

[016-204] Nor him, their sire, have known the filial fry:

[016-205] As little as Time's earliest knew the sky.

[016-206] Perchance among them shoots a lustrous flame

[016-207] At intervals, in proof of whom they came.

[016-208] To strengthen our foundations is the task

[016-209] Of this tough Age; not in your beams to bask,

[016-210] Though, lighted by your beams, down mining caves

[016-211] The rock it blasts, the hoarded foulness braves.

[016-212] My sister sees no round beyond her mood;

[016-213] To hawk this Age has dressed her head in hood.

[016-214] Out of the course of ancient ruts and grooves,

[016-215] It moves: O much for me to say it moves!

[016-216] About his AEthiop Highlands Nile is Nile,

[016-217] Though not the stream of the paternal smile:

[016-218] And where his tide of nourishment he drives,

[016-219] An Abyssinian wantonness revives.

[016-220] Calm as his lotus-leaf to-day he swims;

[016-221] He is the yellow crops, the rounded limbs,

[016-222] The Past yet flowing, the fair time that fills;

[016-223] Breath of all mouths and grist of many mills.

## Section 6

[016-224] To-morrow, warning none with tempest-showers,

[016-225] He is the vast Insensate who devours

[016-226] His golden promise over leagues of seed,

[016-227] Then sits in a smooth lake upon the deed.

[016-228] The races which on barbarous force begin,

[016-229] Inherit onward of their origin,

[016-230] And cancelled blessings will the current length

[016-231] Reveal till they know need of shaping strength.

[016-232] 'Tis not in men to recognize the need

[016-233] Before they clash in hosts, in hosts they bleed.

[016-234] Then may sharp suffering their nature grind;

[016-235] Of rabble passions grow the chieftain Mind.

[016-236] Yet mark where still broad Nile boasts thousands fed,

[016-237] For tens up the safe mountains at his head.

[016-238] Few would be fed, not far his course prolong,

[016-239] Save for the troublous blood which makes him strong.

[016-240] --That rings of truth! More do your people thrive;

[016-241] Your Many are more merrily alive

[016-242] Than erewhile when I gloried in the page

[016-243] Of radiant singer and anointed sage.

[016-244] Greece was my lamp: burnt out for lack of oil;

[016-245] Rome, Python Rome, prey of its robber spoil!

[016-246] All structures built upon a narrow space

[016-247] Must fall, from having not your hosts for base.

[016-248] O thrice must one be you, to see them shift

[016-249] Along their desert flats, here dash, there drift;

[016-250] With faith, that of privations and spilt blood,

[016-251] Comes Reason armed to clear or bank the flood!

[016-252] And thrice must one be you, to wait release

[016-253] From duress in the swamp of their increase.

[016-254] At which oppressive scene, beyond arrest,

[016-255] A darkness not with stars of heaven dressed,

[016-256] Philosophers behold; desponding view.

[016-257] Your Many nourished, starved my brilliant few;

[016-258] Then flinging heels, as charioteers the reins,

[016-259] Dive down the fumy AEtna of their brains.

[016-260] Belated vessels on a rising sea,

[016-261] They seem: they pass!

[016-262] --But not Philosophy!

[016-263] --Ay, be we faithful to ourselves: despise

[016-264] Nought but the coward in us! That way lies

[016-265] The wisdom making passage through our slough.

[016-266] Am I not heard, my head to Earth shall bow;

[016-267] Like her, shall wait to see, and seeing wait.

[016-268] Philosophy is Life's one match for Fate.

[016-269] That photosphere of our high fountain One,

[016-270] Our spirit's Lord and Reason's fostering sun,

[016-271] Philosophy, shall light us in the shade,

[016-272] Warm in the frost, make Good our aim and aid.

[016-273] Companioned by the sweetest, ay renewed,

[016-274] Unconquerable, whose aim for aid is Good!

[016-275] Advantage to the Many: that we name

[016-276] God's voice; have there the surety in our aim.

[016-277] This thought unto my sister do I owe,

[016-278] And irony and satire off me throw.

[016-279] They crack a childish whip, drive puny herds,

[016-280] Where numbers crave their sustenance in words.

[016-281] Now let the perils thicken: clearer seen,

[016-282] Your Chieftain Mind mounts over them serene.

[016-283] Who never yet of scattered lamps was born

[016-284] To speed a world, a marching world to warn,

[016-285] But sunward from the vivid Many springs,

[016-286] Counts conquest but a step, and through disaster sings.
