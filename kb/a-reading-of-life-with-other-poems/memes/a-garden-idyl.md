## A GARDEN IDYL

[015-001] WITH sagest craft Arachne worked

[015-002] Her web, and at a corner lurked,

[015-003] Awaiting what should plump her soon,

[015-004] To case it in the death-cocoon.

[015-005] Sagaciously her home she chose

[015-006] For visits that would never close;

[015-007] Inside my chalet-porch her feast

[015-008] Plucked all the winds but chill North-east.

[015-009] The finished structure, bar on bar,

[015-010] Had snatched from light to form a star,

[015-011] And struck on sight, when quick with dews,

[015-012] Like music of the very Muse.

[015-013] Great artists pass our single sense;

[015-014] We hear in seeing, strung to tense;

[015-015] Then haply marvel, groan mayhap,

[015-016] To think such beauty means a trap.

[015-017] But Nature's genius, even man's

[015-018] At best, is practical in plans;

[015-019] Subservient to the needy thought,

[015-020] However rare the weapon wrought.

[015-021] As long as Nature holds it good

[015-022] To urge her creatures' quest for food

[015-023] Will beauty stamp the just intent

[015-024] Of weapons upon service bent.

[015-025] For beauty is a flower of roots

[015-026] Embedded lower than our boots;

[015-027] Out of the primal strata springs,

[015-028] And shows for crown of useful things

## Section 5

[015-029] Arachne's dream of prey to size

[015-030] Aspired; so she could nigh despise

[015-031] The puny specks the breezes round

[015-032] Supplied, and let them shake unwound;

[015-033] Assured of her fat fly to come;

[015-034] Perhaps a blue, the spider's plum;

[015-035] Who takes the fatal odds in fight,

[015-036] And gives repast an appetite,

[015-037] By plunging, whizzing, till his wings

[015-038] Are webbed, and in the lists he swings,

[015-039] A shrouded lump, for her to see

[015-040] Her banquet in her victory.

[015-041] This matron of the unnumbered threads,

[015-042] One day of dandelions' heads

[015-043] Distributing their gray perruques

[015-044] Up every gust, I watched with looks

[015-045] Discreet beside the chalet-door;

[015-046] And gracefully a light wind bore,

[015-047] Direct upon my webster's wall,

[015-048] A monster in the form of ball;

[015-049] The mildest captive ever snared,

[015-050] That neither struggled nor despaired,

[015-051] On half the net invading hung,

[015-052] And plain as in her mother tongue,

[015-053] While low the weaver cursed her lures,

[015-054] Remarked, "You have me; I am yours."

[015-055] Thrice magnified, in phantom shape,

[015-056] Her dream of size she saw, agape.

[015-057] Midway the vast round-raying beard

[015-058] A desiccated midge appeared;

[015-059] Whose body pricked the name of meal,

[015-060] Whose hair had growth in earth's unreal;

[015-061] Provocative of dread and wrath,

[015-062] Contempt and horror, in one froth,

[015-063] Inextricable, insensible,

[015-064] His poison presence there would dwell,

[015-065] Declaring him her dream fulfilled,

[015-066] A catch to compliment the skilled;

[015-067] And she reduced to beaky skin,

[015-068] Disgraceful among kith and kin

[015-069] Against her corner, humped and aged,

[015-070] Arachne wrinkled, past enraged,

[015-071] Beyond disgust or hope in guile.

[015-072] Ridiculously volatile

[015-073] He seemed to her last spark of mind;

[015-074] And that in pallid ash declined

[015-075] Beneath the blow by knowledge dealt,

[015-076] Wherein throughout her frame she felt

[015-077] That he, the light wind's libertine,

[015-078] Without a scoff, without a grin,

[015-079] And mannered like the courtly few,

[015-080] Who merely danced when light winds blew,

[015-081] Impervious to beak and claws,

[015-082] Tradition's ruinous Whitebeard was;

[015-083] Of whom, as actors in old scenes,

[015-084] Had grannam weavers warned their weans,

[015-085] With word, that less than feather-weight,

[015-086] He smote the web like bolt of Fate.

[015-087] This muted drama, hour by hour,

[015-088] I watched amid a world in flower,

[015-089] Ere yet Autumnal threads had laid

[015-090] Their gray-blue o'er the grass's blade,

[015-091] And still along the garden-run

[015-092] The blindworm stretched him, drunk of sun.

[015-093] Arachne crouched unmoved; perchance

[015-094] Her visitor performed a dance;

[015-095] She puckered thinner; he the same

[015-096] As when on that light wind he came.

[015-097] Next day was told what deeds of night

[015-098] Were done; the web had vanished quite;

[015-099] With it the strange opposing pair;

[015-100] And listless waved on vacant air,

[015-101] For her adieu to heart's content,

[015-102] A solitary filament.
