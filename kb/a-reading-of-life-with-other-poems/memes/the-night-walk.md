## THE NIGHT-WALK

[006-001] AWAKES for me and leaps from shroud

[006-002] All radiantly the moon's own night

[006-003] Of folded showers in streamer cloud;

[006-004] Our shadows down the highway white

[006-005] Or deep in woodland woven-boughed,

[006-006] With yon and yon a stem alight.

[006-007] I see marauder runagates

[006-008] Across us shoot their dusky wink;

[006-009] I hear the parliament of chats

[006-010] In haws beside the river's brink;

[006-011] And drops the vole off alder-banks,

[006-012] To push his arrow through the stream.

[006-013] These busy people had our thanks

[006-014] For tickling sight and sound, but theme

[006-015] They were not more than breath we drew

[006-016] Delighted with our world's embrace:

[006-017] The moss-root smell where beeches grew,

[006-018] And watered grass in breezy space;

[006-019] The silken heights, of ghostly bloom

[006-020] Among their folds, by distance draped.

[006-021] 'Twas Youth, rapacious to consume,

[006-022] That cried to have its chaos shaped:

[006-023] Absorbing, little noting, still

[006-024] Enriched, and thinking it bestowed;

[006-025] With wistful looks on each far hill

[006-026] For something hidden, something owed.

[006-027] Unto his mantled sister, Day

[006-028] Had given the secret things we sought

[006-029] And she was grave and saintly gay;

[006-030] At times she fluttered, spoke her thought;

[006-031] She flew on it, then folded wings,

[006-032] In meditation passing lone,

[006-033] To breathe around the secret things,

[006-034] Which have no word, and yet are known;

[006-035] Of thirst for them are known, as air

[006-036] Is health in blood: we gained enough

[006-037] By this to feel it honest fare;

[006-038] Impalpable, not barren, stuff.

## Section 4

[006-039] A pride of legs in motion kept

[006-040] Our spirits to their task meanwhile,

[006-041] And what was deepest dreaming slept:

[006-042] The posts that named the swallowed mile;

[006-043] Beside the straight canal the hut

[006-044] Abandoned; near the river's source

[006-045] Its infant chirp; the shortest cut;

[006-046] The roadway missed; were our discourse;

[006-047] At times dear poets, whom some view

[006-048] Transcendent or subdued evoked

[006-049] To speak the memorable, the true,

[006-050] The luminous as a moon uncloaked;

[006-051] For proof that there, among earth's dumb,

[006-052] A soul had passed and said our best.

[006-053] Or it might be we chimed on some

[006-054] Historic favourite's astral crest,

[006-055] With part to reverence in its gleam,

[006-056] And part to rivalry the shout:

[006-057] So royal, unuttered, is youth's dream

[006-058] Of power within to strike without.

[006-059] But most the silences were sweet,

[006-060] Like mothers' breasts, to bid it feel

[006-061] It lived in such divine conceit

[006-062] As envies aught we stamp for real.

[006-063] To either then an untold tale

[006-064] Was Life, and author, hero, we.

[006-065] The chapters holding peaks to scale,

[006-066] Or depths to fathom, made our glee;

[006-067] For we were armed of inner fires,

[006-068] Unbled in us the ripe desires;

[006-069] And passion rolled a quiet sea,

[006-070] Whereon was Love the phantom sail.
