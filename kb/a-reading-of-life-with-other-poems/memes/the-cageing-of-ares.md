## THE CAGEING OF ARES

[005-001] ILIAD, v. V. 385

[005-002] [_Dedicated to the Council at The Hague_.]

[005-003] HOW big of breast our Mother Gaea laughed

[005-004] At sight of her boy Giants on the leap

[005-005] Each over other as they neighboured home,

[005-006] Fronting the day's descent across green slopes,

[005-007] And up fired mountain crags their shadows danced.

[005-008] Close with them in their fun, she scarce could guess,

[005-009] Though these two billowy urchins reeked of craft,

[005-010] It signalled some adventurous master-trick

[005-011] To set Olympians buzzing in debate,

[005-012] Lest it might be their godhead undermined,

[005-013] The Tyranny menaced. Ephialtes high

[005-014] On shoulders of his brother Otos waved

[005-015] For the bull-bellowings given to grand good news,

[005-016] Compact, complexioned in his gleeful roar

[005-017] While Otos aped the prisoner's wrists and knees,

[005-018] With doleful sniffs between recurrent howls;

[005-019] Till Gaea's lap receiving them, they stretched,

[005-020] And both upon her bosom shaken to speech,

[005-021] Burst the hot story out of throats of both,

[005-022] Like rocky head-founts, baffling in their glut

[005-023] The hurried spout. And as when drifting storm

[005-024] Disburdened loses clasp of here and yon

[005-025] A peak, a forest mound, a valley's gleam

[005-026] Of grass and the river's crooks and snaky coils,

[005-027] Signification marvellous she caught,

[005-028] Through gurglings of triumphant jollity,

[005-029] Which now engulphed and now gave eye; at last

[005-030] Subsided, and the serious naked deed,

[005-031] With mountain-cloud of laughter banked around,

[005-032] Stood in her sight confirmed: she could believe

[005-033] That these, her sprouts of promise, her most prized,

[005-034] These two made up of lion, bear and fox,

[005-035] Her sportive, suckling mammoths, her young joy,

[005-036] Still by the reckoning infants among men,

[005-037] Had done the deed to strike the Titan host

[005-038] In envy dumb, in envious heart elate:

[005-039] These two combining strength and craft had snared,

[005-040] Enmeshed, bound fast with thongs, discreetly caged

[005-041] The blood-shedder, the terrible Lord of War;

[005-042] Destroyer, ravager, superb in plumes;

[005-043] The barren furrower of anointed fields;

[005-044] The scarlet heel in towns, foul smoke to sky,

[005-045] Her hated enemy, too long her scourge:

[005-046] Great Ares. And they gagged his trumpet mouth

[005-047] When they had seized on his implacable spear,

[005-048] Hugged him to reedy helplessness despite

[005-049] His godlike fury startled from amaze.

[005-050] For he had eyed them nearing him in play,

[005-051] The giant cubs, who gambolled and who snarled,

[005-052] Unheeding his fell presence, by the mount

[005-053] Ossa, beside a brushwood cavern; there

[005-054] On Earth's original fisticuffs they called

[005-055] For ease of sharp dispute: whereat the God,

[005-056] Approving, deemed that sometime trained to arms,

[005-057] Good servitors of Ares they would be,

[005-058] And ply the pointed spear to dominate

[005-059] Their rebel restless fellows, villain brood

[005-060] Vowed to defy Immortals. So it chanced

[005-061] Amusedly he watched them, and as one

[005-062] The lusty twain were on him and they had him.

[005-063] Breath to us, Powers of air, for laughter loud!

[005-064] Cock of Olympus he, superb in plumes!

[005-065] Bound like a wheaten sheaf by those two babes!

[005-066] Because they knew our Mother Gaea loathed him,

[005-067] Knew him the famine, pestilence and waste;

[005-068] A desolating fire to blind the sight

[005-069] With splendour built of fruitful things in ashes;

[005-070] The gory chariot-wheel on cries for justice;

[005-071] Her deepest planted and her liveliest voice,

[005-072] Heard from the babe as from the broken crone.

[005-073] Behold him in his vessel of bronze encased,

[005-074] And tumbled down the cave. But rather look--

[005-075] Ah, that the woman tattler had not sought,

[005-076] Of all the Gods to let her secret fly,

[005-077] Hermes, after the thirteen songful months!

[005-078] Prompting the Dexterous to work his arts,

[005-079] And shatter earth's delirious holiday,

[005-080] Then first, as where the fountain runs a stream,

[005-081] Resolving to composure on its throbs.

[005-082] But see her in the Seasons through that year;

[005-083] That one glad year and the fair opening month.

[005-084] Had never our Great Mother such sweet face!

[005-085] War with her, gentle war with her, each day

[005-086] Her sons and daughters urged; at eve were flung,

[005-087] On the morrow stood to challenge; in their strength

[005-088] Renewed, indomitable; whereof they won,

[005-089] From hourly wrestlings up to shut of lids,

[005-090] Her ready secret: the abounding life

[005-091] Returned for valiant labour: she and they

[005-092] Defeated and victorious turn by turn;

[005-093] By loss enriched, by overthrow restored.

[005-094] Exchange of powers of this conflict came;

[005-095] Defacement none, nor ever squandered force.

[005-096] Is battle nature's mandate, here it reigned,

[005-097] As music unto the hand that smote the strings;

[005-098] And she the rosier from their showery brows,

[005-099] They fruitful from her ploughed and harrowed breast.

[005-100] Back to the primal rational of those

[005-101] Who suck the teats of milky earth, and clasp

[005-102] Stability in hatred of the insane,

[005-103] Man stepped; with wits less fearful to pronounce

[005-104] The mortal mind's concept of earth's divorced

[005-105] Above; those beautiful, those masterful,

[005-106] Those lawless. High they sit, and if descend,

[005-107] Descend to reap, not sowing. Is it just?

[005-108] Earth in her happy children asked that word,

[005-109] Whereto within their breast was her reply.

[005-110] Those beautiful, those masterful, those lawless,

[005-111] Enjoy the life prolonged, outleap the years;

[005-112] Yet they ('twas the Great Mother's voice inspired

[005-113] The audacious thought), they, glorious over dust,

[005-114] Outleap not her; disrooted from her soar,

[005-115] To meet the certain fate of earth's divorced,

[005-116] And clap lame wings across a wintry haze,

[005-117] Up to the farthest bourne: immortal still,

[005-118] Thenceforth innocuous; lovelier than when ruled

[005-119] The Tyranny. This her voice within them told,

[005-120] When softly the Great Mother chid her sons

[005-121] Not of the giant brood, who did create

[005-122] Those lawless Gods, first offspring of our brain

[005-123] Set moving by an abject blood, that waked

[005-124] To wanton under elements more benign,

[005-125] And planted aliens on Olympian heights;--

[005-126] Imagination's cradle poesy

[005-127] Become a monstrous pressure upon men;--

[005-128] Foes of good Gaea; until dispossessed

[005-129] By light from her, born of the love of her,

[005-130] Their lordship the illumined brain rejects

[005-131] For earth's beneficent, the sons of Law,

[005-132] Her other name. So spake she in their heart,

[005-133] Among the wheat-blades proud of stalk; beneath

[005-134] Young vine-leaves pushing timid fingers forth,

[005-135] Confidently to cling. And when brown corn

[005-136] Swayed armied ranks with softened cricket song,

[005-137] With gold necks bent for any zephyr's kiss;

[005-138] When vine-roots daily down a rubble soil

[005-139] Drank fire of heaven athirst to swell the grape;

[005-140] When swelled the grape, and in it held a ray,

[005-141] Rich issue of the embrace of heaven and earth;

[005-142] The very eye of passion drowsed by excess,

[005-143] And yet a burning lion for the spring;

[005-144] Then in that time of general cherishment,

[005-145] Sweet breathing balm and flutes by cool wood-side,

[005-146] He the harsh rouser of ire being absent, caged,

[005-147] Then did good Gaea's children gratefully

[005-148] Lift hymns to Gods they judged, but praised for peace,

[005-149] Delightful Peace, that answers Reason's call

[005-150] Harmoniously and images her Law;

[005-151] Reflects, and though short-lived as then, revives,

[005-152] In memories made present on the brain

[005-153] By natural yearnings, all the happy scenes;

[005-154] The picture of an earth allied to heaven;

[005-155] Between them the known smile behind black masks;

[005-156] Rightly their various moods interpreted;

[005-157] And frolic because toilful children borne

[005-158] With larger comprehension of Earth's aim

[005-159] At loftier, clearer, sweeter, by their aid.
