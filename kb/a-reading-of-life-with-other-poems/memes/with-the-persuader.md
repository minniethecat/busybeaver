## WITH THE PERSUADER

[003-001] WHO murmurs, hither, hither: who

[003-002] Where nought is audible so fills the ear?

[003-003] Where nought is visible can make appear

[003-004] A veil with eyes that waver through,

[003-005] Like twilight's pledge of blessed night to come,

[003-006] Or day most golden? All unseen and dumb,

[003-007] She breathes, she moves, inviting flees,

[003-008] Is lost, and leaves the thrilled desire

[003-009] To clasp and strike a slackened lyre,

[003-010] Till over smiles of hyacinth seas,

[003-011] Flame in a crystal vessel sails

[003-012] Beneath a dome of jewelled spray,

[003-013] For land that drops the rosy day

[003-014] On nights of throbbing nightingales.

[003-015] Landward did the wonder flit,

[003-016] Or heart's desire of her, all earth in it.

[003-017] We saw the heavens fling down their rose;

[003-018] On rapturous waves we saw her glide;

[003-019] The pearly sea-shell half enclose;

[003-020] The shoal of sea-nymphs flush the tide;

[003-021] And we, afire to kiss her feet, no more

[003-022] Behold than tracks along a startled shore,

[003-023] With brightened edges of dark leaves that feign

[003-024] An ambush hoped, as heartless night remain.

[003-025] More closely, warmly: hither, hither! she,

[003-026] The very she called forth by ripened blood

[003-027] For its next breath of being, murmurs; she,

[003-028] Allurement; she, fulfilment; she,

[003-029] The stream within us urged to flood;

[003-030] Man's cry, earth's answer, heaven's consent; O she,

[003-031] Maid, woman and divinity;

[003-032] Our over-earthly, inner-earthly mate

[003-033] Unmated; she, our hunger and our fruit

[003-034] Untasted; she our written fate

[003-035] Unread; Life's flowering, Life's root:

[003-036] Unread, divined; unseen, beheld;

[003-037] The evanescent, ever-present she,

[003-038] Great Nature's stern necessity

[003-039] In radiance clothed, to softness quelled;

[003-040] With a sword's edge of sweetness keen to take

[003-041] Our breath for bliss, our hearts for fulness break.

[003-042] The murmur hushes down, the veil is rent.

[003-043] Man's cry, earth's answer, heaven's consent,

[003-044] Her form is given to pardoned sight,

[003-045] And lets our mortal eyes receive

[003-046] The sovereign loveliness of celestial white;

[003-047] Adored by them who solitarily pace,

[003-048] In dusk of the underworld's perpetual eve,

[003-049] The paths among the meadow asphodel,

[003-050] Remembering. Never there her face

[003-051] Is planetary; reddens to shore sea-shell

[003-052] Around such whiteness the enamoured air

[003-053] Of noon that clothes her, never there.

[003-054] Daughter of light, the joyful light,

[003-055] She stands unveiled to nuptial sight,

[003-056] Sweet in her disregard of aid

[003-057] Divine to conquer or persuade.

[003-058] A fountain jets from moss; a flower

[003-059] Bends gently where her sunset tresses shower.

[003-060] By guerdon of her brilliance may be seen

[003-061] With eyelids unabashed the passion's Queen.

[003-062] Shorn of attendant Graces she can use

[003-063] Her natural snares to make her will supreme.

[003-064] A simple nymph it is, inclined to muse

[003-065] Before the leader foot shall dip in stream:

[003-066] One arm at curve along a rounded thigh;

[003-067] Her firm new breasts each pointing its own way

[003-068] A knee half bent to shade its fellow shy,

[003-069] Where innocence, not nature, signals nay.

[003-070] The bud of fresh virginity awaits

[003-071] The wooer, and all roseate will she burst:

[003-072] She touches on the hour of happy mates;

[003-073] Still is she unaware she wakens thirst.

[003-074] And while commanding blissful sight believe

[003-075] It holds her as a body strained to breast,

[003-076] Down on the underworld's perpetual eve

[003-077] She plunges the possessor dispossessed;

[003-078] And bids believe that image, heaving warm,

[003-079] Is lost to float like torch-smoke after flame;

[003-080] The phantom any breeze blows out of form;

[003-081] A thirst's delusion, a defeated aim.

[003-082] The rapture shed the torture weaves;

[003-083] The direst blow on human heart she deals:

[003-084] The pain to know the seen deceives;

[003-085] Nought true but what insufferably feels.

[003-086] And stabs of her delicious note,

[003-087] That is as heavenly light to hearing, heard

[003-088] Through shelter leaves, the laughter from her throat,

[003-089] We answer as the midnight's morning's bird.

[003-090] She laughs, she wakens gleeful cries;

[003-091] In her delicious laughter part revealed;

[003-092] Yet mother is she more of moans and sighs,

[003-093] For longings unappeased and wounds unhealed.

[003-094] Yet would she bless, it is her task to bless:

[003-095] Yon folded couples, passing under shade,

[003-096] Are her rich harvest; bidden caress, caress,

[003-097] Consume the fruit in bloom; not disobeyed.

[003-098] We dolorous complainers had a dream,

[003-099] Wrought on the vacant air from inner fire,

[003-100] We saw stand bare of her celestial beam

[003-101] The glorious Goddess, and we dared desire.

[003-102] Thereat are shown reproachful eyes, and lips

[003-103] Of upward curl to meanings half obscure;

[003-104] And glancing where a wood-nymph lightly skips

[003-105] She nods: at once that creature wears her lure.

[003-106] Blush of our being between birth and death:

[003-107] Sob of our ripened blood for its next breath:

[003-108] Her wily semblance nought of her denies;

[003-109] Seems it the Goddess runs, the Goddess hies,

[003-110] The generous Goddess yields. And she can arm

[003-111] Her dwarfed and twisted with her secret charm;

[003-112] Benevolent as Earth to feed her own.

[003-113] Fully shall they be fed, if they beseech.

[003-114] But scorn she has for them that walk alone;

[003-115] Blanched men, starved women, whom no arts can pleach.

[003-116] The men as chief of criminals she disdains,

[003-117] And holds the reason in perceptive thought.

[003-118] More pitiable, like rivers lacking rains,

[003-119] Kissing cold stones, the women shrink for drought.

[003-120] Those faceless discords, out of nature strayed,

[003-121] Rank of the putrefaction ere decayed,

[003-122] In impious singles bear the thorny wreaths:

[003-123] Their lives are where harmonious Pleasure breathes

[003-124] For couples crowned with flowers that burn in dew.

[003-125] Comes there a tremor of night's forest horn

[003-126] Across her garden from the insaner crew,

[003-127] She darkens to malignity of scorn.

[003-128] A shiver courses through her garden-grounds:

[003-129] Grunt of the tusky boar, the baying hounds,

[003-130] The hunter's shouts, are heard afar, and bring

[003-131] Dead on her heart her crimsoned flower of Spring.

[003-132] These, the irreverent of Life's design,

[003-133] Division between natural and divine

[003-134] Would cast; these vaunting barrenness for best,

[003-135] In veins of gathered strength Life's tide arrest;

[003-136] And these because the roses flood their cheeks,

[003-137] Vow them in nature wise as when Love speaks.

[003-138] With them is war; and well the Goddess knows

[003-139] What undermines the race who mount the rose;

[003-140] How the ripe moment, lodged in slumberous hours,

[003-141] Enkindled by persuasion overpowers:

[003-142] Why weak as are her frailer trailing weeds,

[003-143] The strong when Beauty gleams o'er Nature's needs,

[003-144] And timely guile unguarded finds them lie.

[003-145] They who her sway withstand a sea defy,

[003-146] At every point of juncture must be proof;

[003-147] Nor look for mercy from the incessant surge

[003-148] Her forces mixed of craft and passion urge

[003-149] For the one whelming wave to spring aloof.

[003-150] She, tenderness, is pitiless to them

[003-151] Resisting in her godhead nature's truth.

[003-152] No flower their face shall be, but writhen stem;

[003-153] Their youth a frost, their age the dirge for youth.

[003-154] These miserably disinclined,

[003-155] The lamentably unembraced,

[003-156] Insult the Pleasures Earth designed

[003-157] To people and beflower the waste.

[003-158] Wherefore the Pleasures pass them by:

[003-159] For death they live, in life they die.

[003-160] Her head the Goddess from them turns,

[003-161] As from grey mounds of ashes in bronze urns.

[003-162] She views her quivering couples unconsoled,

[003-163] And of her beauty mirror they become,

[003-164] Like orchard blossoms, apple, pear and plum,

[003-165] Free of the cloud, beneath the flood of gold.

[003-166] Crowned with wreaths that burn in dew,

[003-167] Her couples whirl, sun-satiated,

[003-168] Athirst for shade, they sigh, they wed,

[003-169] They play the music made of two:

[003-170] Oldest of earth, earth's youngest till earth's end:

[003-171] Cunninger than the numbered strings,

[003-172] For melodies, for harmonies,

[003-173] For mastered discords, and the things

[003-174] Not vocable, whose mysteries

[003-175] Are inmost Love's, Life's reach of Life extend.

[003-176] Is it an anguish overflowing shame

[003-177] And the tongue's pudency confides to her,

[003-178] With eyes of embers, breath of incense myrrh,

[003-179] The woman's marrow in some dear youth's name,

[003-180] Then is the Goddess tenderness

[003-181] Maternal, and she has a sister's tones

[003-182] Benign to soothe intemperate distress,

[003-183] Divide despair from hope, and sighs from moans.

[003-184] Her gentleness imparts exhaling ease

[003-185] To those of her milk-bearer votaries

[003-186] As warm of bosom-earth as she; of the source

[003-187] Direct; erratic but in heart's excess;

[003-188] Being mortal and ill-matched for Love's great force;

[003-189] Like green leaves caught with flames by his impress.

[003-190] And pray they under skies less overcast,

[003-191] That swiftly may her star of eve descend,

[003-192] Her lustrous morning star fly not too fast,

[003-193] To lengthen blissful night will she befriend.

## Section 2

[003-194] Unfailing her reply to woman's voice

[003-195] In supplication instant. Is it man's,

[003-196] She hears, approves his words, her garden scans,

[003-197] And him: the flowers are various, he has choice.

[003-198] Perchance his wound is deep; she listens long;

[003-199] Enjoys what music fills the plaintive song;

[003-200] And marks how he, who would be hawk at poise

[003-201] Above the bird, his plaintive song enjoys.

[003-202] She reads him when his humbled manhood weeps

[003-203] To her invoked: distraction is implored.

[003-204] A smile, and he is up on godlike leaps

[003-205] Above, with his bright Goddess owned the adored.

[003-206] His tales of her declare she condescends;

[003-207] Can share his fires, not always goads and rends:

[003-208] Moreover, quits a throne, and must enclose

[003-209] A queenlier gem than woman's wayside rose.

[003-210] She bends, he quickens; she breathes low, he springs

[003-211] Enraptured; low she laughs, his woes disperse;

[003-212] Aloud she laughs and sweeps his varied strings.

[003-213] 'Tis taught him how for touch of mournful verse

[003-214] Rarely the music made of two ascends,

[003-215] And Beauty's Queen some other way is won.

[003-216] Or it may solve the riddle, that she lends

[003-217] Herself to all, and yields herself to none,

[003-218] Save heavenliest: though claims by men are raised

[003-219] In hot assurance under shade of doubt:

[003-220] And numerous are the images bepraised

[003-221] As Beauty's Queen, should passion head the rout.

[003-222] Be sure the ruddy hue is Love's: to woo

[003-223] Love's Fountain we must mount the ruddy hue.

[003-224] That is her garden's precept, seen where shines

[003-225] Her blood-flower, and its unsought neighbour pines.

[003-226] Daughter of light, the joyful light,

[003-227] She bids her couples face full East,

[003-228] Reflecting radiance, even when from her feast

[003-229] Their outstretched arms brown deserts disunite,

[003-230] The lion-haunted thickets hold apart.

[003-231] In love the ruddy hue declares great heart;

[003-232] High confidence in her whose aid is lent

[003-233] To lovers lifting the tuned instrument,

[003-234] Not one of rippled strings and funeral tone.

[003-235] And doth the man pursue a tightened zone,

[003-236] Then be it as the Laurel God he runs,

[003-237] Confirmed to win, with countenance the Sun's.

[003-238] Should pity bless the tremulous voice of woe

[003-239] He lifts for pity, limp his offspring show.

[003-240] For him requiring woman's arts to please

[003-241] Infantile tastes with babe reluctances,

[003-242] No race of giants! In the woman's veins

[003-243] Persuasion ripely runs, through hers the pains.

[003-244] Her choice of him, should kind occasion nod,

[003-245] Aspiring blends the Titan with the God;

[003-246] Yet unto dwarf and mortal, she, submiss

[003-247] In her high Lady's mandate, yields the kiss;

[003-248] And is it needed that Love's daintier brute

[003-249] Be snared as hunter, she will tempt pursuit.

[003-250] She is great Nature's ever intimate

[003-251] In breast, and doth as ready handmaid wait,

[003-252] Until perverted by her senseless male,

[003-253] She plays the winding snake, the shrinking snail,

[003-254] The flying deer, all tricks of evil fame,

[003-255] Elusive to allure, since he grew tame.

[003-256] Hence has the Goddess, Nature's earliest Power,

[003-257] And greatest and most present, with her dower

[003-258] Of the transcendent beauty, gained repute

[003-259] For meditated guile. She laughs to hear

[003-260] A charge her garden's labyrinths scarce confute,

[003-261] Her garden's histories tell of to all near.

[003-262] Let it be said, But less upon her guile

[003-263] Doth she rely for her immortal smile.

[003-264] Still let the rumour spread, and terror screens

[003-265] To push her conquests by the simplest means.

[003-266] While man abjures not lustihead, nor swerves

[003-267] From earth's good labours, Beauty's Queen he serves.

[003-268] Her spacious garden and her garden's grant

[003-269] She offers in reward for handsome cheer:

[003-270] Choice of the nymphs whose looks will slant

[003-271] The secret down a dewy leer

[003-272] Of corner eyelids into haze:

[003-273] Many a fair Aphrosyne

[003-274] Like flower-bell to honey-bee:

[003-275] And here they flicker round the maze

[003-276] Bewildering him in heart and head:

[003-277] And here they wear the close demure,

[003-278] With subtle peeps to reassure:

[003-279] Others parade where love has bled,

[003-280] And of its crimson weave their mesh:

[003-281] Others to snap of fingers leap,

[003-282] As bearing breast with love asleep.

[003-283] These are her laughters in the flesh.

[003-284] Or would she fit a warrior mood,

[003-285] She lights her seeming unsubdued,

[003-286] And indicates the fortress-key.

[003-287] Or is it heart for heart that craves,

[003-288] She flecks along a run of waves

[003-289] The one to promise deeper sea.

[003-290] Bands of her limpid primitives,

[003-291] Or patterned in the curious braid,

[003-292] Are the blest man's; and whatsoever he gives,

[003-293] For what he gives is he repaid.

[003-294] Good is it if by him 'tis held

[003-295] He wins the fairest ever welled

[003-296] From Nature's founts: she whispers it: Even I

[003-297] Not fairer! and forbids him to deny,

[003-298] Else little is he lover. Those he clasps,

[003-299] Intent as tempest, worshipful as prayer,--

[003-300] And be they doves or be they asps,--

[003-301] Must seem to him the sovereignty fair;

[003-302] Else counts he soon among life's wholly tamed.

[003-303] Him whom from utter savage she reclaimed,

[003-304] Half savage must he stay, would he be crowned

[003-305] The lover. Else, past ripeness, deathward bound,

[003-306] He reasons; and the totterer Earth detests,

[003-307] Love shuns, grim logic screws in grasp, is he.

[003-308] Doth man divide divine Necessity

[003-309] From Joy, between the Queen of Beauty's breasts

[003-310] A sword is driven; for those most glorious twain

[003-311] Present her; armed to bless and to constrain.

[003-312] Of this he perishes; not she, the throned

[003-313] On rocks that spout their springs to the sacred mounts.

[003-314] A loftier Reason out of deeper founts

[003-315] Earth's chosen Goddess bears: by none disowned

[003-316] While red blood runs to swell the pulse, she boasts,

[003-317] And Beauty, like her star, descends the sky;

[003-318] Earth's answer, heaven's consent unto man's cry,

[003-319] Uplifted by the innumerable hosts.

[003-320] Quickened of Nature's eye and ear,

[003-321] When the wild sap at high tide smites

[003-322] Within us; or benignly clear

[003-323] To vision; or as the iris lights

[003-324] On fluctuant waters; she is ours

[003-325] Till set of man: the dreamed, the seen;

[003-326] Flushing the world with odorous flowers:

[003-327] A soft compulsion on terrene

[003-328] By heavenly: and the world is hers

[003-329] While hunger after Beauty spurs.

[003-330] So is it sung in any space

[003-331] She fills, with laugh at shallow laws

[003-332] Forbidding love's devised embrace,

[003-333] The music Beauty from it draws.
