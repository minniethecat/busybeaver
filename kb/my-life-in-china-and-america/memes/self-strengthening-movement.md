# Self-Strengthening Movement

## Overview

The Self-Strengthening Movement (c. 1861–1895) was a period of institutional reform during the late Qing dynasty, initiated by powerful regional officials like Tsang Kwoh Fan, Li Hung Chang, and Tso Tsung Tang. Its slogan was "Chinese learning as the base, Western learning for practical use."

## Yung Wing's Role

Yung Wing's educational scheme found its most receptive audience among the Self-Strengthening reformers. Tsang Kwoh Fan, the movement's leading figure, became Yung Wing's most important patron. It was through Tsang's support that Yung Wing secured imperial approval for the Chinese Educational Mission (013-005).

## Key Achievements

The movement established China's first modern arsenal, shipyard, textile factory, and steel mill. It also funded the Chinese Educational Mission, which Yung Wing considered the movement's most significant contribution to China's long-term development.

## Limitations and Legacy

The movement's narrow focus on military and industrial technology, without corresponding political and educational reform, ultimately limited its effectiveness. The Sino-Japanese War of 1894–1895 exposed its inadequacies and led to more radical reform demands.
