# Treaty of Nanking

## Overview

The Treaty of Nanking (1842) was the first of the "unequal treaties" imposed on China by Western powers. Signed at the conclusion of the First Opium War, it fundamentally altered China's relationship with the outside world and created the conditions under which Yung Wing's unique life path became possible.

## Key Provisions

The treaty required China to cede Hong Kong Island to Britain, open five ports (Shanghai, Canton, Ningpo, Fuchow, and Amoy) to foreign trade and residence, pay a large indemnity, and establish a fixed tariff schedule.

## Impact on Yung Wing's World

The treaty port system enabled foreign missionaries and educators to establish a permanent presence in China. Without the opening of ports like Canton and the proximity of Macao and Hong Kong as foreign enclaves, the missionary schools that educated Yung Wing would not have been possible. Yung Wing was born in 1828, just fourteen years before the treaty was signed (001-002).

## Legacy

The treaty inaugurated the system of extraterritoriality and foreign concessions that would shape Chinese politics for the next century, fueling both the reform movements Yung Wing participated in and the revolutionary nationalism that followed.
