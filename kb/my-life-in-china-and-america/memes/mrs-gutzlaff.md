# Mrs. Karl Gutzlaff

## Overview

Mrs. Karl Gutzlaff (née Mary Wanstall) was an English missionary who arrived in Macao in 1834 and established schools for Chinese children under the auspices of the Ladies Association in London for the promotion of female education in India and the East (001-004).

## Yung Wings First Teacher

When seven-year-old Yung Wing was brought to her school by his father in 1835, Mrs. Gutzlaff became the first English lady he had ever seen. Her imposing appearance initially frightened the boy, but her kindness and sympathy soon won him over. Yung Wing later wrote that he came to look upon her more like a mother (001-007).

## The Runaway Incident

One memorable episode from Yung Wings first year involved an attempted escape. Yung Wing and six older girls tried to flee across the channel to Pedro Island, only to be captured mid-voyage. Mrs. Gutzlaff punished them with public humiliation, placing the children on display before the entire school (001-009).

## Legacy

Mrs. Gutzlaffs school was originally for girls only, but pending the organization of the Morrison Education Society School, a boys department was added. This is how Yung Wing, as one of the first two boys admitted, began his English education (001-011). Before departing for America with three blind Chinese girls, she charged Dr. Benjamin Hobson with finding Yung Wing and placing him in the Morrison School.
