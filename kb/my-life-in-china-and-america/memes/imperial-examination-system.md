# Imperial Examination System

## Overview

The imperial examination system (keju) was the primary method of selecting government officials in imperial China for over a millennium. Candidates spent years memorizing the Confucian classics to pass increasingly competitive examinations (001-005).

## Decline and Abolition

By the late 19th century, the examination systems focus on classical texts was increasingly seen as inadequate for modern governance. Yung Wings advocacy for Western-style education represented an alternative path.
