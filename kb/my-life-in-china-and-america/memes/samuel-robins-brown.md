# Samuel Robins Brown

## Overview

The Reverend Samuel Robins Brown was an American missionary educator who served as the principal teacher of the Morrison Education Society School in Macao and later Hong Kong. A Yale graduate himself, Brown played a pivotal role in bringing Yung Wing and other Chinese students to the United States for higher education (001-018).

## Teacher and Mentor

Brown recognized Yung Wings intellectual promise and provided the rigorous classical education that prepared him for admission to Monson Academy and eventually Yale College. His influence extended beyond academics — he instilled in his Chinese students a vision of how Western learning could serve Chinas development.

## Bringing Students to America

When Brown returned to the United States due to health reasons, he offered to bring Chinese students with him for further study. Yung Wing, along with two classmates (Wong Shing and Wong Foon), accepted this offer, beginning the first organized movement of Chinese students to America.
