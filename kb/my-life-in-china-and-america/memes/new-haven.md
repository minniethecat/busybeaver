# New Haven

## Overview

New Haven, Connecticut, was the site of Yale College, where Yung Wing studied from 1850 to 1854 and became the first Chinese graduate of an American university. The city's academic environment and New England culture deeply influenced his worldview, providing him with the Western education that would later define his career as a diplomat and reformer.

## Yale Years

During his four years at Yale, Yung Wing experienced the full range of American collegiate life. Despite financial hardship — he worked various jobs to support himself — he excelled academically and won prizes in English composition. His classmates included future senators, judges, and industrialists, connections that would serve him throughout his diplomatic career.
