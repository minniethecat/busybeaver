# Confucian Education

## Overview

For centuries, the Confucian education system was the exclusive avenue to political preferment, influence, power, and wealth in China. Young men devoted years to memorizing the Four Books and Five Classics in preparation for the imperial civil service examinations (001-005).

## Contrast with Western Education

Yung Wing's parents made the unusual decision to send him to a foreign school rather than a regular orthodox Confucian school, where his elder brother was placed (001-005). Yung Wing himself reflected that this decision was a mystery, as a "Chinese cult is the only avenue in China that leads to political preferment."

## Limitations

The Confucian system's focus on classical texts and literary composition left little room for the study of science, technology, or international affairs. This intellectual isolation contributed to China's vulnerability in the face of Western military and economic power in the 19th century.

## Reform Efforts

The Self-Strengthening Movement of the 1860s–1890s sought to supplement Confucian education with Western technical learning, following the formula of "Chinese learning as the base, Western learning for practical use."
