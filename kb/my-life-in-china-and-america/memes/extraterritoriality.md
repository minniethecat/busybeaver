# Extraterritoriality

## Overview

Extraterritoriality was one of the most consequential features of the unequal treaty system imposed on China after the Opium Wars. Under this system, foreign nationals residing in the treaty ports were subject to the laws of their own countries, administered by their own consular courts, rather than Chinese law (020-006).

## Impact on Yung Wing

As a naturalized American citizen, Yung Wing benefited from extraterritorial protections during his business and official activities in China. However, his unique position as both an insider and outsider in Chinese society gave him a critical perspective on the system.
