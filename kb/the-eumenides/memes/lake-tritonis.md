# Lake Tritonis

**Lake Tritonis** is a mythical lake in Libya, known in Greek mythology as the birthplace of [[athena|Athena]]. In *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]*, [[orestes|Orestes]] invokes the "Tritonid waters" when he prays for [[athena|Athena]] to come to his aid at the [[areopagus|Areopagus]].

## Mythological Significance

According to Greek legend, the epithet "Tritonia" traditionally applied to [[athena|Athena]] derived from the belief that she was born at Lake Tritonis (002-024). This origin myth places [[athena|Athena]]'s birth not on Mount Olympus but in the distant, liminal geography of North Africa — a detail that Aeschylus uses to emphasize [[athena|Athena]]'s capacity to hear prayers from afar.

In some versions of the myth, Lake Tritonis was the site where [[athena|Athena]] emerged fully armed from the head of [[zeus|Zeus]]. In others, she was raised beside its waters by the river-god Triton.

## Role in *The Eumenides*

[[orestes|Orestes]], having arrived at [[athena|Athena]]'s statue on the [[areopagus|Areopagus]], prays for the goddess to hear him wherever she may be:

> "Therefore, wheresoe'er thou art, In some far wilderness of Libyan earth, By those Tritonid waters of thy birth; ... Oh, hear! A goddess heareth though afar: Bring me deliverance in this mine hour!" (001-035)

The invocation of Lake Tritonis serves a dramatic purpose: by listing multiple possible locations for [[athena|Athena]] (Libya, [[phlegra|Phlegra]], [[troy|Troy]]), [[orestes|Orestes]] acknowledges the goddess's vast reach while expressing the desperation of a suppliant who does not know where his protector currently resides.

The "far wilderness" imagery contrasts with [[athena|Athena]]'s eventual appearance from the civilized Greek world — she arrives not from Libya but from the [[scamander-river|Scamander River]] near [[troy|Troy]], where she had been receiving newly conquered lands (001-048).


## Geography

The historical Lake Tritonis was likely a shallow salt lake or marsh in what is now southern Tunisia or eastern Algeria. In antiquity, the region was associated with the Triton River and its delta. Modern scholars identify several possible locations, including the Chott el Djerid, a large seasonal salt lake.
