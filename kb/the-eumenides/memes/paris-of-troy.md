# Paris of Troy

**Paris** (Greek: Πάρις), also called **Alexander**, was a prince of [[troy|Troy]], son of King Priam and Queen Hecuba. His abduction of [[helen-of-troy|Helen]], wife of [[menelaus|Menelaus]], triggered the [[the-trojan-war|Trojan War]].

## The Judgment of Paris

According to myth, Paris was asked to judge which of three goddesses — [[hera|Hera]], [[athena|Athena]], or Aphrodite — was the fairest. Each offered him a bribe: [[hera|Hera]] offered power, [[athena|Athena]] wisdom, Aphrodite the most beautiful woman in the world. Paris chose Aphrodite, who gave him Helen. This "Judgment of Paris" is the mythological origin of the entire [[the-oresteia|Oresteia]] backdrop.

## Connection to The Oresteia

Paris's abduction of Helen from [[sparta|Sparta]] triggered the chain of events that led to [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]'s expedition to [[troy|Troy]], the [[sacrifice-of-iphigenia|sacrifice of Iphigenia]] at [[aulis|Aulis]], the ten-year war, [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]'s return and [[death-of-agamemnon|murder]], and [[orestes|Orestes]]'s matricide. Paris never appears in *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]*, but his act is the ultimate cause of the crisis the play resolves.
