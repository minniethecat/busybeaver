# The Sacrifice of Iphigenia

The **Sacrifice of [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]]** was the killing of [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]], daughter of [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]], at [[aulis|Aulis]] to appease the wrath of [[artemis|Artemis]] and allow the Greek fleet to sail for [[troy|Troy]]. This event is the originating crime of the [[the-oresteia|Oresteia]] trilogy.

## The Event

The Greek fleet, assembled at [[aulis|Aulis]] for the [[the-trojan-war|Trojan War]], was becalmed by [[artemis|Artemis]]. The prophet [[calchas|Calchas]] declared that the goddess, angered by [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]'s killing of a sacred stag (or, in Aeschylus' version, the eagles' feast on a pregnant hare), demanded the sacrifice of [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]]. [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]], torn between his duty as commander and his love as a father, lured [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]] to [[aulis|Aulis]] under the pretext of marriage to [[achilles|Achilles]] and sacrificed her.

## Aeschylus' Treatment

In the *[[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]*, Aeschylus describes the sacrifice in harrowing detail but with characteristic ambiguity. The chorus recounts how [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]] was "lifted above the altar like a goat, her mouth gagged to prevent a curse upon the house." Yet Aeschylus does not clearly condemn [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] — the king was, after all, obeying a divine command.

Aeschylus, in the *[[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]*, gives his answer to how humanity can escape the endless chain of vengeance: "Only in the thought of [[zeus|Zeus]], whatever [[zeus|Zeus]] may be." (P01-008) The prophet [[calchas|Calchas]] describes the ravenous feast of the two Eagles, the wrath of [[artemis|Artemis]], and the vengeance exacted in the death of [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]] — and beyond that, a yet further vista of vengeances re-avenged.

## The First Link in the Chain

> The prophet Calchas has been describing the ravenous feast of the two Eagles; the wrath of Artemis thereat and the vengeance exacted in the death of Iphigenia; the future vengeance to be exacted for that death; and beyond a yet further vista of venge.
>
> (P01-008)

The sacrifice of [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]] is the first crime in the trilogy's chain of vengeance:
1. **[[aulis|Aulis]]**: [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] sacrifices [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]]
2. **[[argos|Argos]]**: [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] kills [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] in revenge
3. **[[argos|Argos]]**: [[orestes|Orestes]] kills [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] in revenge
4. **[[delphi|Delphi]] → [[athens|Athens]]**: The [[erinyes|Furies]] pursue [[orestes|Orestes]]

Each act of vengeance is "just" by the logic of the blood-feud, yet the chain is endless. The resolution of *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]* — [[athena|Athena]]'s establishment of the [[the-areopagus-court|Areopagus Court]] — is the first intervention that can break this cycle.
