# Prophecy in Greek Religion

**Prophecy** (Greek: μαντεία, *manteia*) was a central institution of ancient Greek religion, and the [[delphic-oracle|Delphic Oracle]] was its supreme seat. [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]] opens with the institution of prophecy and uses it to frame the play's central theological arguments.

## The Delphic Oracle

The [[pythian-prophetess|Pythian Prophetess]]'s opening speech establishes the sacred lineage of the oracle: [[mother-earth|Earth]] → [[themis|Themis]] → [[phoebe|Phoebe]] → [[apollo|Apollo]]. (002-005) This peaceful succession makes [[delphi|Delphi]] the model of orderly divine transition — the theological ideal that the play will apply to the [[erinyes|Furies]].

## Apollo as Prophêtês

[[apollo|Apollo (Loxias)]] is emphatically presented as the *Prophêtês Dios*, the revealer of [[zeus|Zeus]]'s will. He declares: "Never have I spoken on my throne of prophecy any word concerning man, woman or city, which was not commanded by [[zeus|Zeus the Father]]." (P01-011)

Prophecy at [[delphi|Delphi]] is not an independent divine power — it is the medium through which [[zeus|Zeus]]'s will is communicated to mortals.

> Never have I spoken on my throne of prophecy any word concerning man, woman or city, which was not commanded by Zeus the Father.
>
> (P01-011)

## Prophecy and the Trial

The trial of [[orestes|Orestes]] raises the question: what happens when prophecy commands violence? [[orestes|Orestes]] killed his mother at [[apollo|Apollo]]'s command. If [[apollo|Apollo]] speaks for [[zeus|Zeus]], and [[zeus|Zeus]] commands matricide, then how can the [[erinyes|Furies]] — who punish the shedding of [[kindred-blood|kindred blood]] — be right to pursue [[orestes|Orestes]]?

The resolution is that [[zeus|Zeus]] can also command forgiveness. The same prophetic authority that demanded the killing now demands the acquittal. Prophecy, like law, is subject to the higher wisdom of the divine Father who "made a road to Thought."
