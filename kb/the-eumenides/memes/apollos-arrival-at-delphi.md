# Apollo's Arrival at Delphi

**Apollo's Arrival at Delphi** is the foundational event of the [[delphi|Delphi]]c sanctuary, recounted by the [[pythian-prophetess|Pythian Prophetess]] in the opening speech of [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]].

## Aeschylus' Version

The prophetess describes how [[apollo|Apollo]] came to [[delphi|Delphi]] peacefully, receiving the oracle as a gift from [[phoebe|Phoebe]], his grandmother:

> He left his isle, he left his Delian seas, He passed Athena's wave-worn promontories, In haste this great Parnassus to possess And Delphi, thronèd in the wilderness.
>
> (001-002)

[[apollo|Apollo]] was escorted by "a folk born of [[hephaistos|Hephaistos]], pioneer / Of God's way, making sweet a bitter land." (001-002) Their king, [[delphos|Delphos]], led them, and [[zeus|Zeus]] "into his heart put mystic flame / And prophet here enthroned him." (001-002)

## Revision of Traditional Myth

Aeschylus deliberately departs from the traditional myth, in which [[apollo|Apollo]] seized [[delphi|Delphi]] by violence, slaying the dragon [[python-the-dragon|Python]] and driving out the earlier powers. The succession at [[delphi|Delphi]] — from [[mother-earth|Earth]] to [[themis|Themis]] to [[phoebe|Phoebe]] to [[apollo|Apollo]] — was peaceful:

> The ordinary legend told of Apollo's battle with a dragon, born of Earth, and the anger of Earth thereafter; Aeschylus insists that there was never any strife among the gods at Delphi.
>
> (002-005)

As the endnotes emphasize, the peaceful succession was "by whose own / Accepted will, with never strife nor stress." (002-005)

## Theological Significance

The peaceful arrival is central to the play's argument. By presenting [[apollo|Apollo]]'s acquisition of [[delphi|Delphi]] as a gift rather than a conquest, Aeschylus establishes a model of harmonious divine succession. [[apollo|Apollo]] "left his isle, he left his Delian seas" (002-006) and came to take possession of [[delphi|Delphi]] without violence. This model prefigures [[athena|Athena]]'s peaceful incorporation of the [[erinyes|Furies]] into the Athenian civic order — the old powers are not destroyed but honored and transformed.
