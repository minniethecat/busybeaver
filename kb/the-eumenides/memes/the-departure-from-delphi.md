# The Departure from Delphi

The **Departure from [[delphi|Delphi]]** marks the moment in [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]] when [[orestes|Orestes]] leaves the sanctuary of [[apollo|Apollo]] at [[delphi|Delphi]] and begins his journey to [[athens|Athens]]. (001-001)

## Apollo's Instructions

[[apollo|Apollo]] commands [[orestes|Orestes]] to flee while the [[erinyes|Furies]] sleep:

> But none the less, fly thou and falter not. / For these shall hunt thee, ever on through earth / Unwandered, through the vast lands of the North, / The sea-ways and the cities ringed with sea. / But faint not. Clasp thy travail unto thee; / On till thou come to Pallas' Rock, and fold / Thine arms in prayer about her image old.
>
> (001-001)

[[apollo|Apollo]] also appoints [[hermes|Hermes]] as [[orestes|Orestes]]' escort:

> Do thou, O Hermes, brother of my blood, / Watch over him. Thou guide of man, make good / The name thou bearest, shepherding again / My suppliant.
>
> (001-001)

These instructions establish the play's central movement: from the old sacred center to the new civic one. (P01-009)

## Hermes Psychopompos

[[hermes|Hermes]]' role here draws on his traditional function as Psychopompos — the guide of souls to the underworld. But the journey is inverted: instead of guiding a soul down to death, [[hermes|Hermes]] guides a living man toward civic salvation. [[orestes|Orestes]]' journey from [[delphi|Delphi]] to [[athens|Athens]] is a kind of symbolic death and rebirth: he leaves the old world of blood-vengeance behind and enters the new world of civic justice. (002-014)

## Transition

The departure marks the play's geographical transition from [[delphi|Delphi]] (the old sacred center, the world of [[apollo|Apollo]] and prophecy) to [[athens|Athens]] (the new civic center, the world of [[athena|Athena]] and law). (P01-010) The journey itself — through "the vast lands of the North, the sea-ways and the cities ringed with sea" — is the purgatorial wandering that separates [[orestes|Orestes]]' pollution from his eventual acquittal.
