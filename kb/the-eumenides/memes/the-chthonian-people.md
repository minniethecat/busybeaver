# The Chthonian People

The **[[chthonian|Chthonian]] People** (Greek: χθόνιοι, "those beneath the earth") are the dead who dwell in the underworld, whose wrath and power are central to the religious world of [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]].

## Murray's Analysis

Murray's Introduction explains the relationship between the dead, the earth, and justice:

"It is because [[mother-earth|Earth]] is the home of the dead, the [[chthonian|Chthonian]] people, both the great ancestors who know what is [[themis|Themis]] and 'the wronged ones in the darkness' who cry for justice on their oppressors. Their wrath is her wrath." (P01-006)

The dead are not passive. They cry out for justice, and their cries are heard by [[mother-earth|Earth]], who sends up "blight and barrenness and plague" against those who have wronged them — just as "to the innocent in normal times she sends life and fruitfulness."

> It is because Earth is the home of the dead, the Chthonian people, both the great ancestors who know what is Themis and "the wronged ones in the darkness" who cry for justice on their oppressors. Their wrath is her wrath.
>
> (P01-006)

## The Furies as the Dead's Wrath

The [[erinyes|Furies]] are the personal form of the dead's wrath. When [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] is murdered, she becomes one of the [[chthonian|Chthonian]] people, and it is her wrath — her continued demand for justice — that wakes the [[erinyes|Furies]] and drives them to pursue [[orestes|Orestes]]. She is "among all the dead / Dishonoured," wandering "in shame," crying out for vengeance.

## Blessing and Curse

The [[chthonian|Chthonian]] people have a dual power: they can curse or bless. (002-046) The same powers that blight the land can make it fertile; the same [[erinyes|Furies]] who pursue [[orestes|Orestes]] can — once honored — become the [[transformation-of-the-furies|Eumenides]], guardians of Athenian prosperity. This duality is essential to the play's resolution: [[athena|Athena]] does not destroy the chthonian powers but redirects them from [[curse|curse]] to blessing.
