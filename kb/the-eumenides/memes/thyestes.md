# Thyestes

**Thyestes** (Greek: Θυέστης) was a son of Pelops and brother of Atreus. His bitter conflict with Atreus — and the [[curse|curse]] he laid upon the [[argos|House of Atreus]] — forms the ancestral background of [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]] and the entire [[the-oresteia|Oresteia]] trilogy.

## The Curse of the House of Atreus

The conflict between Thyestes and Atreus is one of the most horrifying stories in Greek mythology. Atreus, enraged by Thyestes' adultery with his wife, killed Thyestes' children and served them to their father at a banquet. When Thyestes discovered what he had eaten, he cursed Atreus and all his descendants.

This [[curse|curse]] — the Curse of the House of [[the-house-of-atreus|Atreus]] — is the dark inheritance that hangs over [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] (son of Atreus) and his children [[orestes|Orestes]], [[electra|Electra]], and [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]].

## Father of Aegisthus

Thyestes was the father of [[aegisthus|Aegisthus]], who would later murder [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] and rule [[argos|Argos]] with [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]]. Murray's Introduction observes that "[[aegisthus|Aegisthus]], as son of Thyestes, acted quite correctly in killing [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]" (P01-001) — the blood-feud demanded it. [[aegisthus|Aegisthus]] was avenging his father's wrongs, just as [[orestes|Orestes]] would later avenge [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]].

## Role in The Eumenides

Though Thyestes does not appear in *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]*, his curse is the dark background against which the play's action unfolds. The entire trilogy asks: how can the chain of vengeance — begun by Atreus' crime against Thyestes — be broken? The answer is [[athena|Athena]]'s establishment of the [[the-areopagus-court|Areopagus Court]], which replaces the endless cycle of blood-vengeance with civic justice.
