# Delphi

Delphi, located on the slopes of [[mount-parnassus|Mount Parnassus]], is the oldest and most revered oracle site in the Greek world — "the prophet navel-stone, the earth's deep centre" (002-013). In Aeschylus' *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]*, Delphi is where the action begins: it is the site of [[orestes|Orestes']] purification from the blood-pollution of matricide, administered by [[apollo]] at the omphalos — and it is from Delphi that the [[erinyes|Furies]] pursue him to [[athens]].

## Mythological History

### The Succession of Prophets

[[pythian-prophetess|The Pythia]]'s opening speech (001-033) traces the sacred lineage: [[mother-earth|Mother Earth]] → [[themis|Themis]] → [[phoebe|Phoebe]] → [[apollo]]. Each succeeded the last peacefully, without violence — a contrast with the cosmic power-struggles seen elsewhere in Greek myth. The speech concludes with [[apollo|Apollo]] installed at his seat at "the [[omphalos|Navel-Stone]]" — a stone understood to mark the centre of the earth.

> The priestess first praises the Holders of the Throne of prophecy at Delphi — Earth, Themis, Phoebe, Apollo; then the other divine beings in the neighbourhood, including the River Pleistos, and ending suitably with Zeus the Perfector or Accomplisher (Teleios). The ordinary legend told of Apollo's battle with a dragon, born of Earth, and the anger of Earth thereafter; Aeschylus insists that there was never any strife among the gods at Delphi.
>
> (002-005)

### The Delphic Oracle

The [[delphic-oracle|Delphic Oracle]], delivered by the [[pythian-prophetess|Pythian Prophetess]], was the most authoritative prophetic voice in the Greek world. In the play, the oracle's authority is invoked by [[apollo|Apollo]] himself when he claims to speak for [[zeus|Zeus]]: "I have never spoken from my seat of prophecy of man or woman, city or tribe, except at the bidding of [[zeus|Zeus]], the Father of the Olympians" (002-019).

### The Corycian Cave

The [[corycian-cave|Corycian Cave]], a natural grotto on [[mount-parnassus|Mount Parnassus]] above Delphi, was sacred to the nymphs and to [[pan|Pan]]. In the play, [[athena|Athena]] invokes it as one of the sacred sites of her land.

## Role in The Eumenides

### Orestes' Purification

The play opens at Delphi, where [[orestes|Orestes]] has fled after killing his mother [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]]. He clings to the omphalos — the navel-stone — as a suppliant, his hands still stained with blood. [[apollo|Apollo]] has performed the ritual purification: "the blood upon your hands is sleeping now and waning" (001-044). The purification ritual involved the blood of a piglet, which was poured over the suppliant's hands and then washed away — symbolically transferring the pollution to the animal.

### The Pursuit of the Furies

The [[erinyes|Furies]] — the ancient goddesses who enforce the blood-feud — have pursued [[orestes|Orestes]] to Delphi and surround him even as he clings to the omphalos. They appear as a chorus of sleeping figures, visible only to the [[pythian-prophetess|Pythia]], who is terrified by the sight and flees the temple. The [[erinyes|Furies]]' presence at the holiest site in Greece creates a dramatic tension between the old and new divine orders.

### The Journey to Athens

[[apollo|Apollo]] instructs [[orestes|Orestes]] to flee to [[athens]], where he will find judgement at the court of [[athena]]. This sets in motion the central movement of the play: from Delphi (purification) → to [[athens|Athens]] (judgement) → to the [[areopagus-hill|Areopagus]] (trial). The journey represents the transition from ritual purification to civic justice.

## Sacred Geography

Delphi's sacred topography includes:

- **The Temple of [[apollo|Apollo]]** — The main sanctuary, housing the omphalos and the seat of prophecy
- **The [[corycian-cave|Corycian Cave]]** — A sacred grotto on the mountain above
- **The Castalian Spring** — The sacred spring used for ritual purification

The sanctuary's location on the slopes of [[mount-parnassus|Mount Parnassus]], overlooking the [[pleistos-river|Pleistos River]] valley, added to its dramatic setting.
