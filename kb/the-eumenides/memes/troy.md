# Troy

Troy (also called Ilium or Ilion) is the ancient city in Asia Minor that was the site of the legendary [[the-trojan-war|Trojan War]]. In Aeschylus' *[[erinyes|Eumenides]]*, Troy figures in two important contexts: as the scene of [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]'s military victory, and as the origin point of [[athena|Athena]]'s territorial claim to [[athens|Athens]].

## In the Eumenides

### Orestes' Lineage and Troy

When [[orestes|Orestes]] presents his lineage before [[athena|Athena]], he invokes his father's famous exploit at Troy:

> My father's name was known upon thy lips, Agamemnon, marshal of a thousand ships, With whom thou madest Troy, that city of pride, No more a city. (001-069)

This reference establishes [[orestes|Orestes]]' noble heritage through his father's heroic role in the [[the-trojan-war|Trojan War]], framing his plea within the shared history between himself and [[athena|Athena]].

### Athena's Land Grant

The endnotes explain the political geography behind [[athena|Athena]]'s reference to having come "from [[scamander-river|Scamander]]":

> Athena comes from Sigêum in the neighbourhood of Troy, which in the time of Aeschylus had long been part of the Athenian Empire. Tradition said that it had been given by the Greek army to the "Sons of Theseus" (the Athenians) for their services in the Trojan War. (002-028)

This detail grounds [[athena|Athena]]'s claim in historical Athenian imperial geography—at the time of the play's performance, the region near Troy was Athenian territory, making [[athena|Athena]]'s journey from Troy to [[athens|Athens]] both mythologically resonant and politically contemporary.

### Ideal Geography

The endnotes also discuss Troy in the context of Aeschylus' use of "ideal geography":

> The real Mount Ida was about thirty miles in the wrong direction, but the ideal Ida was simply the mountain of Troy. (002-021)

This observation highlights how Aeschylus treats geography poetically rather than literally, using place names for their symbolic and mythological weight rather than cartographic accuracy.

## Connection to the Play's Geography

Troy is connected to the **[[erinyes|Eumenides]]** through [[scamander-river|the Scamander River]], which flows near Troy. [[athena|Athena]] states she heard [[orestes|Orestes]]' prayer while at the [[scamander-river|Scamander]] (001-068), establishing a chain of geographic reference: Troy → [[scamander-river|Scamander]] → [[athens|Athens]].
