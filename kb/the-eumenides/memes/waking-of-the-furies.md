# The Waking of the Furies

The Waking of the Furies is the dramatic event that launches the central conflict of *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]*: the ghost of [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] appears at [[delphi|Delphi]], where the [[erinyes|Furies]] have fallen asleep after their pursuit of [[orestes|Orestes]], and rouses them with bitter accusations and urgent demands for renewed vengeance.

## Overview

After [[apollo|Apollo]] has purified [[orestes|Orestes]] and sent him to [[athens|Athens]] under [[hermes|Hermes]]' protection, the [[erinyes|Furies]] — exhausted — fall asleep in the [[delphi|Delphi]]c shrine. The ghost of [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] enters and delivers a speech of extraordinary bitterness and urgency: "Ye sleep. O God, and what are sleepers worth? 'Tis you, have left me among all the dead Dishonoured" (001-007).

She contrasts her own dishonored state in the underworld — "Alway, for that blood I shed, Rebuke and hissing cease not, and I go Wandering in shame" — with the absence of any pursuit of her murderer: "for his Who smote me, being of my blood, there is No wrath in all the darkness: there is none Cares for a mother murdered by her son" (001-007).

## Context

The [[erinyes|Furies]] have pursued [[orestes|Orestes]] from [[argos|Argos]] to [[delphi|Delphi]]. [[apollo|Apollo]] has performed the purification and put them to sleep — a temporary reprieve that allows [[orestes|Orestes]] to escape. [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]]'s ghost rises from the underworld to demand that they fulfill their function. Her claim is both personal (she is the victim) and cosmic (the law of the blood-feud must be enforced).

## The Event

[[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] shows them her wound: "Open thine heart to see this gash!" (001-007). She reminds them of the offerings she made to them in life. When they moan but do not wake, her frustration escalates to fury: "Moan on, but he is vanished and forgot. So strong the prayers of them that love me not!" (001-007).

Finally, they stir, speaking in their sleep: "At him! At him! Catch, catch, catch! Ah, beware!" [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]]'s final command — "Awake! Be strong! And faithful keep Thy lust of pain through all the drugs of sleep... Thy blood-mist fold about him, like a doom. Waste him with vapour from thy burning womb. A second chase is death! Pursue! Pursue!" (001-007) — sends them after [[orestes|Orestes]] once more.

## Aftermath

The [[erinyes|Furies]] wake fully, discover [[orestes|Orestes]] has fled, and confront [[apollo|Apollo]]. The sharp exchange between [[apollo|Apollo]] and the Leader of the [[erinyes|Furies]] sets the terms for the theological conflict that the trial will adjudicate.

## In The Eumenides

The ghost scene is unique in Greek tragedy — a dead character appearing not as a divine apparition but as a nightmare that literally wakes the sleeping [[erinyes|Furies]]. It dramatizes the inexorability of the blood-feud: even death does not end the victim's claim. The dead demand justice, and the chthonian powers exist to provide it.
