# Pleistos River

The Pleistos River is a watercourse near [[delphi|Delphi]] in [[phocis|Phocis]], mentioned in the opening prayer of Aeschylus' *[[erinyes|Eumenides]]*. It forms part of the sacred geography of the [[delphi|Delphi]]c sanctuary, which serves as the play's initial setting.

## In the Eumenides

### The Prophetess's Prayer

[[pythian-prophetess|The Pythian Prophetess]] opens the play with an invocation that moves through the succession of prophetic deities at [[delphi|Delphi]], then extends to the surrounding natural and divine features of the landscape:

> And Pleistos' fountains and Poseidon's power I call, and Him who brings the Perfect Hour, Zeus, the Most Highest. (001-001)

The Pleistos is invoked alongside [[poseidon|Poseidon]] and [[zeus|Zeus]], placing it within the sacred topography of [[delphi|Delphi]]. The endnotes explain:

> The priestess first praises the Holders of the Throne of prophecy at Delphi—Earth, Themis, Phoebe, Apollo; then the other divine beings in the neighbourhood, including the River Pleistos, and ending suitably with Zeus the Perfector or Accomplisher (Teleios). (002-005)

### Sacred Geography

The inclusion of the Pleistos in the Prophetess's prayer reflects the ancient Greek practice of honoring local natural features as part of a sanctuary's sacred landscape. The river, along with the [[corycian-cave|Corycian Cave]], [[mount-parnassus|Mount Parnassus]], and the Castalian Spring, defined the physical and spiritual boundaries of [[delphi|Delphi]].
