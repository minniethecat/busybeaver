# The Founding of the Areopagus

The [[founding-of-the-areopagus]] is the culminating event of Aeschylus' *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]*: [[athena|Athena]], having presided over the [[trial-of-orestes|trial of Orestes]] and cast the deciding vote for his acquittal, establishes the [[the-areopagus-court|court]] as a permanent institution of Athenian civic life.

The founding transforms what began as an ad hoc divine intervention into an enduring structure of [[justice-dike|justice]], integrating the [[erinyes|chthonic powers]] into the [[olympian-order|Olympian order]] under the [[civic-order|civic framework]] of the polis.


## Overview

After the trial verdict, [[athena|Athena]] declares the [[areopagus|Areopagus]] a permanent court: "This Council I appoint, this court of law, Untainted by desire of gain, revered, Quick to strike down, yet quick to bless, a guard Of the land's peace, unsleeping, for the sake Of sleeping men" (001-077 ff.). She specifies its composition (the best citizens), its jurisdiction (homicide), and its founding principle: that fear of the law, properly directed, is a civic good.

The [[erinyes|Furies]], initially enraged at their defeat — "Woe on you, woe, ye younger gods!

Ye have trampled the great Laws of old Beneath your chariots!" (001-060) — are persuaded by [[athena|Athena]] to accept a permanent home beneath the [[areopagus-hill|Areopagus hill]], where they will be worshipped as the [[erinyes|Eumenides]] and bless the land rather than [[curse|curse]] it.

The [[transformation-of-the-furies|transformation]] of the [[erinyes|Furies]] into the [[erinyes|Eumenides]] is inseparable from the founding: the old order is not destroyed but incorporated.


## Context

[[the-areopagus-court|The Areopagus]] was, historically, the oldest council and court of [[athens|Athens]]. At the time of the play's production (458 BCE), its political powers had recently been curtailed by [[ephialtes|Ephialtes]]' democratic reforms (462 BCE). Aeschylus' myth — tracing the court's founding to [[athena|Athena]] herself, with the jury of twelve [[athenian-citizens|Athenian citizens]] established at the very moment of the first trial — gives the [[areopagus|Areopagus]] a divine charter that transcends contemporary political controversy.

## The Event

[[athena|Athena]]'s founding act has three components:

1. **Institution of the court**: composition (best citizens), jurisdiction (homicide), and location ([[areopagus-hill|Hill of Ares]]) specified
2. **[[transformation-of-the-furies|Persuasion of the Furies]]**: the [[erinyes|chthonic powers]] must consent to the new order for it to be legitimate; [[athena|Athena]] achieves this through reasoned persuasion and the offer of honor
3. **Establishment of the [[erinyes|Eumenides]]' cult**: ensuring that the ancient powers are honored rather than suppressed, their shrine established beneath the hill

| Component | Agent | Method | Outcome |
|----------|-------|--------|---------|
| Court institution | [[athena|Athena]] | Divine decree | Permanent homicide court |
| Fury persuasion | [[athena|Athena]] | Reason + honor + civic integration | [[erinyes|Furies]] → [[erinyes|Eumenides]] |
| Cult foundation | [[athena|Athena]] | Ritual establishment | Shrine beneath [[areopagus|Areopagus]] |
| Cosmic reconciliation | [[zeus|Zeus]] (via [[athena|Athena]]) | Divine will executed | Olympian + [[chthonian|Chthonic]] united |

## Aftermath

[[the-areopagus-court|The Areopagus]] became, and remained throughout antiquity, the most prestigious homicide court in [[athens|Athens]]. Even after its political powers were reduced, its religious authority — rooted in the foundation myth Aeschylus dramatized — ensured its continued significance. The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]' shrine beneath the hill was a site of [[the-chthonian-people|chthonian]] worship for centuries.

## In The Eumenides

The founding is the play's resolution: the old law ([[the-blood-feud|blood-feud]], [[erinyes|Erinyes]], mechanical [[justice-dike|Dikê]]) is not destroyed but incorporated into the new (civic justice, [[areopagus|Areopagus]], the will of [[zeus|Zeus]]). The reconciliation is cosmic as well as civic — the Olympian and [[the-chthonian-people|chthonian]] powers are united in service of the polis.
