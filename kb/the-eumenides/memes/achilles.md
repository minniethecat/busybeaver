# Achilles

**Achilles** (Greek: Ἀχιλλεύς) was the greatest Greek warrior of the [[the-trojan-war|Trojan War]], son of Peleus and the sea-nymph Thetis. He is connected to the [[the-oresteia|Oresteia]] through the circumstances of [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]]'s death.

## Iphigenia's Lure

[[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] lured his daughter [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]] to [[aulis|Aulis]] by telling her she was to marry Achilles. This deception — using the greatest hero of the age as a false bridegroom — adds a further layer of cruelty to the sacrifice. Achilles himself, in some versions of the myth, was unaware of the ruse and attempted to defend [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]] when he learned the truth.

## The Iliad Connection

Achilles' famous horse, Xanthus, who breaks into speech to warn his master of approaching death in the *Iliad* (XIX.418), is cited by Murray in his Introduction. When the horse speaks, "the [[erinyes|Erinyes]] 'check his voice'" — the horse, "however well-intentioned, was obviously transgressing his [[moira|Moira]]." (P01-005)

> And in the Iliad, at the great moment when Achilles' horse breaks into speech to warn his master of approaching death, the Erinyes "check his voice" (*Il.* XIX 418). The horse, however well-intentioned, was obviously transgressing his Moira.
>
> (P01-005)

This is an early attestation of the [[erinyes|Furies]] as guardians of [[moira|Moira]], the allotted portion.
