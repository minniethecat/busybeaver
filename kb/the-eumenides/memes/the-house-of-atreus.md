# The House of Atreus

The House of Atreus is the royal dynasty of [[argos|Mycenae]] (also associated with [[argos|Argos]]), the most cursed family in Greek mythology. Its multigenerational history of crime and vengeance — from Atreus's cannibal banquet through [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]'s sacrifice of [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]] to [[orestes|Orestes]]' matricide — forms the narrative substrate of Aeschylus' *Oresteia*, of which *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]* is the culminating resolution.

## Role

The House of Atreus exemplifies the logic of the blood-feud. Murray traces the chain (P01-001): "[[aegisthus|Aegisthus]], as son of [[thyestes|Thyestes]], acted quite correctly in killing [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]], and [[orestes|Orestes]] in killing him.

Similarly, when [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] killed [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] the eternal law compelled [[orestes|Orestes]] to avenge him, however reluctantly" (P01-001).

Each act of vengeance is both required (by the blood-feud) and polluting (because it sheds kindred blood). The dynasty is trapped in a cycle that no individual act of justice can break.


## In The Eumenides

By the time of *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]*, the [[argos|House of Atreus]] has been decimated. [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] is dead, killed by [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]]. [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] and [[aegisthus|Aegisthus]] are dead, killed by [[orestes|Orestes]]. [[orestes|Orestes]] — the sole surviving male heir — is a polluted fugitive. The family's future depends on whether [[orestes|Orestes]] can be cleansed and acquitted, breaking the cycle that has destroyed every previous generation.

The resolution — [[orestes|Orestes]]' acquittal at the [[areopagus|Areopagus]] — is thus not only a personal deliverance but a dynastic one. The House of Atreus, freed from the curse, can continue. The old law that trapped the family in an endless cycle of vengeance is superseded by a civic justice that can judge, forgive, and restore.

## Significance

The House of Atreus is the human case study for Aeschylus' theological argument. If even this family — the most cursed in mythology — can be freed from the blood-feud through the institutions of civic justice, then the new order of [[zeus|Zeus]] and [[athena|Athena]] is genuinely redemptive.

> We have seen in the Agamemnon how the law that “The doer must suffer,” or that Sin must bring Punishment, is mainly envisaged by the most primitive Greek tradition as a form of the blood-feud. It is blood calling for blood.
>
> (P01-001)
