# The Athenian Jury

The **Athenian Jury** is the body of twelve citizens selected by [[athena|Athena]] to judge [[orestes|Orestes]] in the first homicide trial at the [[the-areopagus-court|Areopagus Court]]. Their institution is one of the central events of [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]].

## Athena's Selection

[[athena|Athena]] declares that she will select "the noblest of the breed" — twelve Athenian citizens — to serve as jurors. Her criteria are not aristocratic birth but moral quality: "men that have clean hands and are true to the city, equally hating both the anarchic spirit and the spirit of tyranny."

## The Tied Vote

The jury's vote is evenly divided — six for conviction, six for acquittal. This tie is not a dramatic failure but a theological point: the old law (represented by the [[erinyes|Furies]]) and the new law (represented by [[apollo|Apollo]]) are in equipoise. Resolution requires something beyond mechanical counting — it requires the personal judgment of [[athena|Athena]], who casts her vote for acquittal.

## Democratic Justice

The institution of the jury is the democratic heart of the play. In replacing the private vengeance of the [[the-blood-feud|blood-feud]] with a panel of citizens, Aeschylus dramatizes the transition from aristocratic blood-justice to democratic legal procedure. The jury represents the community — the *polis* — exercising the power of judgment that once belonged to the victim's kin alone.
