# Delphos

Delphos is the eponymous king (or hero) of [[delphi|Delphi]], mentioned in a single passage of the [[pythian-prophetess|Pythian Prophetess]]'s opening prayer in *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]*. When [[apollo|Apollo]] came to [[delphi|Delphi]], "a folk born of [[hephaistos|Hephaistos]]" accompanied him as "pioneer of God's way, making sweet a bitter land. And much this people and the King whose hand Then steered them, Delphos, glorified his name" (001-001).

## Role in the Play

Delphos's role is purely genealogical: his name authenticates the [[delphi|Delphi]]c tradition by providing a human founder who welcomed the divine. The sequence — [[mother-earth|Earth]], [[themis|Themis]], [[phoebe|Phoebe]], [[apollo|Apollo]], Delphos — moves from the primordial ([[mother-earth|Earth]]) through the divine ([[themis|Themis]], [[phoebe|Phoebe]], [[apollo|Apollo]]) to the human (Delphos), grounding the oracular institution in both cosmic and civic history.
