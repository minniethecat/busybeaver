# Calchas

**Calchas** (Greek: Κάλχας) was the seer of the Greek army during the [[the-trojan-war|Trojan War]]. Murray's Introduction to [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]] mentions his crucial role in the backstory of the [[the-oresteia|Oresteia]].

## The Prophecy at Aulis

At [[aulis|Aulis]], when the Greek fleet was becalmed, Calchas interpreted the omen of two eagles devouring a pregnant hare as a sign of [[artemis|Artemis]]'s wrath. The goddess, he declared, demanded the sacrifice of [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]], daughter of [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]. This prophecy set the entire tragic chain in motion. (P01-008)

## Murray's Citation

Murray describes the prophet's crucial role and the chain of vengeance his prophecy initiated:

> The prophet Calchas has been describing the ravenous feast of the two Eagles; the wrath of Artemis thereat and the vengeance exacted in the death of Iphigenia; the future vengeance to be exacted for that death; and beyond a yet further vista of vengeances re-avenged.
>
> (P01-008)

Calchas thus functions as the one who articulates the seemingly endless chain of blood-vengeance that the Oresteia must ultimately transcend.
