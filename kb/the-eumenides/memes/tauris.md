# Tauris

**Tauris** (Greek: Ταυρίς) was the ancient name for the Crimean peninsula on the northern shore of the Black Sea. In some versions of the myth of [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]] — most famously Euripides' *[[iphigenia|Iphigenia]] in Tauris* — [[artemis|Artemis]] transported [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]] to Tauris at the moment of her sacrifice at [[aulis|Aulis]], substituting a deer in her place.

## The Tauric Artemis

In Tauris, [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]] served as priestess of [[artemis|Artemis]] Tauropolos, a local form of [[artemis|Artemis]] associated with human sacrifice. Euripides' play dramatizes [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]]'s recognition of her brother [[orestes|Orestes]], who arrives in Tauris after his acquittal in [[athens|Athens]], and their escape together with the sacred image of [[artemis|Artemis]].

## Aeschylus' Version

Aeschylus, in the [[the-oresteia|Oresteia]], does not use the Tauris version of the myth. In the *[[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]*, [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]] is described as being sacrificed — "lifted above the altar like a goat" — with no suggestion of substitution. The Aeschylean [[iphigenia|Iphigenia]] dies, and her death is the first crime in the chain that *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]* must resolve.
