# The Nymphs of Parnassus

The **Nymphs** (Greek: Νύμφαι) are female nature spirits in Greek mythology. In [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]], the Nymphs of [[mount-parnassus|Mount Parnassus]] are invoked by the [[pythian-prophetess|Pythian Prophetess]] in her opening prayer at [[delphi|Delphi]].

## In The Eumenides

After acknowledging the succession of prophetic powers at [[delphi|Delphi]] — [[mother-earth|Earth]], [[themis|Themis]], [[phoebe|Phoebe]], [[apollo|Apollo]] — and honoring [[athena|Athena]], the prophetess turns to the sacred landscape:

"Next, She before the shrine, [[athena|Pallas]], is praisèd, and the Nymphs who keep / Yon old Corycian bird-belovèd steep, / Deep-caverned, where things blessèd come and go."

The "deep-caverned" steep is the [[corycian-cave|Corycian Cave]], a sacred grotto on [[mount-parnassus|Mount Parnassus]] where the Nymphs were worshipped alongside [[pan|Pan]] and [[dionysus|Dionysus]].

## The Sacred Landscape

The Nymphs occupy a specific position in the prophetess's prayer. They come after the great powers of the [[delphi|Delphi]]c throne ([[mother-earth|Earth]] → [[themis|Themis]] → [[phoebe|Phoebe]] → [[apollo|Apollo]]) and [[athena|Athena]], but before the more ecstatic powers of [[dionysus|Bromios]] ([[dionysus|Dionysus]]) and the waters of the [[pleistos-river|Pleistos River]]. This placement reflects the Nymphs' role as intermediaries — beings of the wild natural world who are neither fully Olympian nor fully chthonian.

## Worship

The [[corycian-cave|Corycian Cave]], located about 10 km from [[delphi|Delphi]] on the slopes of [[mount-parnassus|Parnassus]], was a significant cult site. Archaeological excavations have revealed thousands of figurines, pottery, and other offerings dedicated to the Nymphs and [[pan|Pan]]. The cave served as a place of worship for locals and pilgrims who sought the Nymphs' favor for fertility, protection, and connection to the wild.
