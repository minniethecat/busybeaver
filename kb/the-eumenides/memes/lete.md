# Leto

**Leto** (Greek: Λητώ) is a Titaness in Greek mythology, daughter of the Titans Coeus and [[phoebe|Phoebe]], and mother of [[apollo|Apollo]] and [[artemis|Artemis]] by [[zeus|Zeus]].

## The Genealogical Chain

Leto's genealogical position is significant for understanding [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]]. She is the daughter of [[phoebe|Phoebe]], the Titaness who held the [[delphic-oracle|Delphic Oracle]] and passed it peacefully to [[apollo|Apollo]] "as a birthtide gift." (002-005) The gift passes through the maternal line: from [[phoebe|Phoebe]] to her grandson [[apollo|Apollo]] — in whose name ([[apollo|Phoebus]]) [[phoebe|Phoebe]]'s memory is preserved.

## Birth of Apollo

According to myth, [[hera|Hera]] — jealous of Leto's union with [[zeus|Zeus]] — pursued the pregnant Titaness across the earth, forbidding any land to give her shelter. Finally, the floating island of [[delos|Delos]] offered refuge, and there Leto gave birth to [[apollo|Apollo]] and [[artemis|Artemis]]. The endnotes to the Murray translation explain that [[apollo|Apollo]] "left his isle, he left his Delian seas" to come to [[delphi|Delphi]] — his birthplace at [[delos|Delos]] and his prophetic home at [[delphi|Delphi]] being the two poles of his divine geography.

## Role in the Oresteia

Leto does not appear in *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]*, but her genealogical position is part of the theological architecture. [[apollo|Apollo]] inherits the oracle through the maternal line ([[phoebe|Phoebe]] → Leto → [[apollo|Apollo]]), a peaceful succession that Aeschylus emphasizes against the tradition of violent conquest.
