# Electra

Electra, daughter of [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] and [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] and sister of [[orestes|Orestes]], is a central figure in many treatments of the [[orestes|Orestes]] myth — particularly in Sophocles' *Electra* and Euripides' *Electra* (P01-002) — but she does not appear in Aeschylus' *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]*. Her absence from the final play of the *Oresteia* is characteristic of Aeschylus' focus: the *[[erinyes|Eumenides]]* is concerned not with the family drama of the [[argos|House of Atreus]] but with the cosmic and civic resolution of the cycle of vengeance.

Murray's Introduction mentions Electra in connection with [[orestes|Orestes]]' fear of leaving no avenger: "It is one of the fears that torment [[orestes|Orestes]] in Euripides' *Electra*, 'Who will do justice on me, when she is dead?'" (P01-002)

## Role in the Trilogy

Electra appears in the *Choëphoroe*, the second play of Aeschylus' *Oresteia* trilogy. There, she comes to [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]'s tomb with libation-bearers, recognizes her brother [[orestes|Orestes]] by a lock of hair and footprints, and joins him in invoking their father's spirit to aid the vengeance against [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] and [[aegisthus|Aegisthus]].

> But suppose there is no avenger left, what then? It is one of the fears that torment Orestes in Euripides’ Electra, “Who will do justice on me, when she is dead?” In that case, though there is no visible human avenger, the Law nevertheless acts.
>
> (P01-002)
