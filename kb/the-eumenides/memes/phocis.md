# Phocis

**Phocis** (Greek: Φωκίς) is a historical region of central Greece, bounded by Boeotia to the east and Locris to the north. It contains [[delphi|Delphi]] and [[mount-parnassus|Mount Parnassus]], the sacred landscape at the heart of [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]].

## Sacred Geography

Phocis was, in antiquity, dominated by the sanctuary of [[delphi|Delphi]] and the [[delphic-oracle|Delphic Oracle]]. The [[pleistos-river|Pleistos River]] flows through the region, passing below [[delphi|Delphi]] on its way to the Gulf of Corinth. The [[corycian-cave|Corycian Cave]] lies in the mountains above, and the whole region was considered sacred to [[apollo|Apollo]], the [[the-nymphs|Nymphs]], [[pan|Pan]], and [[dionysus|Dionysus]].

## Role in The Eumenides

The play opens in Phocis — at the temple of [[apollo|Apollo]] in [[delphi|Delphi]]. The region is established through the [[pythian-prophetess|Pythia]]'s opening prayer as a place of layered sanctity: the prophetic throne passing from [[mother-earth|Earth]] to [[themis|Themis]] to [[phoebe|Phoebe]] to [[apollo|Apollo]], the sacred cave of the [[the-nymphs|Nymphs]], the ecstatic mountain of [[dionysus|Dionysus]], the fountains of the Pleistus, and the power of [[poseidon|Poseidon]]. (002-005)

## Historical Significance

Phocis was a member of the Amphictyonic League, the association of Greek tribes that administered the sanctuary of [[delphi|Delphi]]. The region's identity was deeply tied to the oracle — a relationship reflected in Aeschylus' careful enumeration of the divine powers associated with the [[delphi|Delphi]]c landscape.
