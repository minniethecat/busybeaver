# Athena's Areopagus Speech

**[[athena|Athena]]'s [[areopagus|Areopagus]] Speech** (001-077) is the constitutional moment of [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]] — the performative utterance by which [[athena|Athena]] establishes the [[the-areopagus-court|Areopagus Court]] as a permanent institution.

## The Speech

"Hear now mine ordinance, ye who have striven / This day to give, what none before hath given, / True judgement o'er spilt blood. O Attic Folk, / Henceforth for ever, under Aigeus' yoke, / This Council and this Judgement Seat by me / Are stablisht."

[[athena|Athena]] locates the court on [[areopagus-hill|Ares' Hill]], associates it with the [[amazons-camp|Amazons']] ancient siege, and charges the citizens to "enfold and love / That spirit that nor anarch is nor thrall."

## The Performative Act

The speech is a *performative utterance*: by speaking it, [[athena|Athena]] creates the institution she describes. This is the divine equivalent of legislative speech — the word that becomes law. The [[areopagus|Areopagus]] exists because [[athena|Athena]] declares that it exists.

## Political Philosophy

[[athena|Athena]]'s speech articulates a political philosophy of balanced governance:
- Neither anarchy ("the anarch spirit") nor tyranny ("the spirit of tyranny")
- Fear as a civic virtue — "that Fear which is both Ruth and Law within"
- The court as "a guard to keep / Vigil above my people while they sleep"

This vision of checks and balances, institutional permanence, and civic reverence was central to Athenian self-understanding.
