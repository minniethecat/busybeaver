# The Suppliant Ritual

The **Suppliant Ritual** (Greek: ἱκεσία, *hikesia*) was a formal ritual in ancient Greek religion by which a person in danger or pollution placed themselves under divine protection. [[orestes|Orestes]] performs this ritual at both [[delphi|Delphi]] and [[athens|Athens]] in [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]].

## The Tokens

The [[pythian-prophetess|Pythian Prophetess]] describes the ritual objects [[orestes|Orestes]] carries:

"a naked new-drawn brand / Wreathed in beseeching wool, a suppliant's weed / Of snow-white fleece"

The key elements are:
- An olive branch (*hiketeria*), stripped of leaves
- White wool wound around the branch
- The suppliant's posture: seated at the altar or clasping a sacred image

The white wool symbolized purity and the suppliant's peaceful intent. The olive branch connected the ritual to [[athena|Athena]], whose sacred tree was the olive.

## The Ritual Gesture

The suppliant approached a sacred place — a temple altar, a statue of a god, or the hearth of a powerful person — and physically grasped it. As long as the suppliant maintained contact with the sacred object, they were under divine protection. To drag a suppliant from the altar was sacrilege; to kill one at the altar was one of the gravest crimes in Greek religion (P01-009).

## In The Eumenides

[[orestes|Orestes]] performs the suppliant ritual twice:
1. At [[delphi|Delphi]], where he clings to the [[omphalos|Omphalos]] (the [[omphalos|Navel-Stone]]), "the inmost Altar of the world"
2. At [[athens|Athens]], where he embraces the ancient image of [[athena|Athena]]

The ritual frames the entire action of the play and establishes [[orestes|Orestes]]' claim to protection. The dilemma of the suppliant — the tension between protecting the helpless and upholding justice — lies at the heart of [[athena|Athena]]'s decision to found the [[areopagus|Areopagus]] court (002-031). [[zeus|Zeus]] himself is the ultimate protector of suppliants — [[zeus|Zeus]] Hikesios — and [[apollo|Apollo]]'s protection of [[orestes|Orestes]] is an extension of [[zeus|Zeus]]'s will (P01-016).
