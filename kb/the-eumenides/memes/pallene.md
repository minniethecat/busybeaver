# Pallene

**Pallene** (Greek: Παλλήνη) was the westernmost peninsula of [[chalcidice|Chalcidice]] in northern Greece. In ancient tradition, it was identified as the location of [[phlegra|Phlegra]], the mythical battlefield of the [[the-gigantomachy|Gigantomachy]].

## Phlegra and the Gigantomachy

Ancient sources located [[phlegra|Phlegra]] — where the [[olympian-order|Olympian Gods]] defeated the Giants — in the Pallene peninsula. The volcanic nature of the region may have contributed to this association: the landscape of Pallene, with its geothermal activity, seemed to bear the scars of cosmic battle.

## Role in The Eumenides

Pallene is not directly mentioned in [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]], but its association with [[phlegra|Phlegra]] provides geographical grounding for [[athena|Athena]]'s reference to the Gigantomachy. When [[athena|Athena]] reminds the [[erinyes|Furies]] of their shared victory over the Giants, she invokes a battle traditionally located in this northern Greek landscape — far from [[athens|Athens]] and [[delphi|Delphi]], but cosmically significant nonetheless.
