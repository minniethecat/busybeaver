# Chalcidice

**Chalcidice** (Greek: Χαλκιδική) is a three-pronged peninsula in northern Greece, in the region of Macedonia. Its westernmost peninsula, [[pallene|Pallene]], was traditionally identified as the location of [[phlegra|Phlegra]], the battlefield of the [[the-gigantomachy|Gigantomachy]].

## Geography and Myth

Chalcidice's three peninsulas — [[pallene|Pallene]], Sithonia, and Acte (Mount Athos) — extend into the northern Aegean Sea like the prongs of [[poseidon|Poseidon]]'s trident. The region's volcanic and geothermal features contributed to its mythological associations with cosmic battle. The landscape of [[pallene|Pallene]], scarred and smoking, seemed to bear witness to the war between gods and giants.

## In The Eumenides

Chalcidice is not directly mentioned in [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]], but its mythological geography provides the setting for the Gigantomachy that [[athena|Athena]] invokes when persuading the [[erinyes|Furies]] to accept incorporation into the Athenian order. The reference to a battle fought in distant Chalcidice reminds the [[erinyes|Furies]] that their allegiance to the [[olympian-order|Olympian order]] predates and transcends any local conflict.
