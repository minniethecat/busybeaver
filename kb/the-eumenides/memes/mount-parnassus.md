# Mount Parnassus

Mount Parnassus (Greek: Παρνασσός) is the sacred mountain that towers above [[delphi|Delphi]] in [[phocis|Phocis]], central Greece. In *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]*, it is mentioned in the [[pythian-prophetess|Pythian Prophetess]]'s opening prayer as the site of the [[corycian-cave|Corycian Cave]] and the haunt of [[dionysus|Dionysus]] and the [[the-nymphs|Nymphs]].

## Location

Parnassus rises to approximately 2,457 meters, its slopes descending to the [[pleistos-river|Pleistos River]] valley where [[delphi|Delphi]] lies. The [[corycian-cave|Corycian Cave]], sacred to the [[the-nymphs|Nymphs]] and [[pan|Pan]], is located on its upper slopes. The mountain was associated with [[apollo|Apollo]], the Muses, and (in winter, when [[apollo|Apollo]] was said to depart for the land of the Hyperboreans) with [[dionysus|Dionysus]].

## In The Eumenides

[[pythian-prophetess|The Pythian Prophetess]], in her opening prayer, invokes the deities of the region: "Next, She before the shrine, [[athena|Pallas]], is praisèd, and the [[the-nymphs|Nymphs]] who keep Yon old Corycian bird-belovèd steep, Deep-caverned, where things blessèd come and go. And [[dionysus|Bromios]] walks the mountain, well I know, Since first he led his Maenad host on high And doomed King [[pentheus|Pentheus]] like a hare to die" (001-001).

This passage establishes the sacred landscape that surrounds [[delphi|Delphi]] — the mountain is not merely a backdrop but a populated divine space, inhabited by nymphs, visited by [[dionysus|Dionysus]], resonant with myth (the death of [[pentheus|Pentheus]], the ecstatic worship of the Maenads). The Olympian order at [[delphi|Delphi]] does not erase these older, wilder powers; it coexists with them.

## Religious Significance

Parnassus was sacred to [[apollo|Apollo]] and the Muses, and the [[corycian-cave|Corycian Cave]] was an important cult site. The mountain's association with [[dionysus|Dionysus]] — who was worshipped there in winter, when [[apollo|Apollo]]'s oracle was silent — represents the complementary relationship between the two gods: [[apollo|Apollo]] of order and clarity, [[dionysus|Dionysus]] of ecstasy and transformation.
