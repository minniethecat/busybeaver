# Leda

**Leda** (Greek: Λήδα) was the queen of [[sparta|Sparta]], wife of [[tyndareus|Tyndareus]], and mother of [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]], Helen, and the Dioscuri (Castor and Pollux). She belongs to the genealogical background of [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]].

## The Swan

Leda is best known in Greek mythology for her encounter with [[zeus|Zeus]], who approached her in the form of a swan. From this union, according to the most common version of the myth, Helen was born — the woman whose beauty would launch the [[troy|Trojan War]]. [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] was born from Leda's marriage to [[tyndareus|Tyndareus]], though some versions made her a daughter of [[zeus|Zeus]] as well.

## Maternal Legacy

The motherhood of Leda connects the Oresteia's central female characters to divine origins. [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]]'s formidable nature — her intelligence, her political authority, her capacity for violence — is consistent with a lineage that touches both [[sparta|Sparta]]n royalty and Olympian divinity. The play's complex treatment of [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] reflects this dual heritage.
