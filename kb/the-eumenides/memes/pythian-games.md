# The Pythian Games

The **[[delphic-oracle|Pythia]]n Games** (Greek: Πύθια) were one of the four great [[pan|Pan]]hellenic festivals of ancient Greece, held every four years at [[delphi|Delphi]] in honor of [[apollo|Apollo]] Pythios. They are part of the broader cultural world that [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]] inhabits.

## The Festival

The Pythian Games were second in prestige only to the Olympic Games. Unlike the Olympics, which were purely athletic, the [[delphic-oracle|Pythia]]n Games included musical and poetic competitions — a reflection of [[apollo|Apollo]]'s association with the lyre and the arts. The games commemorated [[apollo|Apollo]]'s mythical victory over the serpent [[python-the-dragon|Python]] at [[delphi|Delphi]].

## Connection to The Eumenides

While the [[delphic-oracle|Pythia]]n Games are not directly mentioned in *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]*, [[athena|Athena]]'s establishment of the [[the-areopagus-court|Areopagus Court]] and her institution of the [[transformation-of-the-furies|cult of the Eumenides]] parallels the kind of foundational act that established the great festivals. [[athena|Athena]]'s speech founding the court is performative — like the foundation of a festival, it creates a permanent institution through ritual speech.

## Aeschylus and Delphi

Aeschylus' presentation of [[delphi|Delphi]] in *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]* emphasizes peaceful succession ([[mother-earth|Earth]] → [[themis|Themis]] → [[phoebe|Phoebe]] → [[apollo|Apollo]]) over violent conquest ([[apollo|Apollo]] slaying the [[python-the-dragon|Python]]). (002-005) This revision of myth may reflect the poet's respect for the [[delphi|Delphi]]c sanctuary and its institutions, including the games.
