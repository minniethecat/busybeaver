# Tyndareus

**Tyndareus** (Greek: Τυνδάρεως) was the legendary king of [[sparta|Sparta]], husband of [[leda|Leda]], and father of [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] and Helen. His lineage forms part of the genealogical background of [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]].

## Father of Clytemnestra

Tyndareus was the mortal father of [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]], though in some versions of the myth, [[zeus|Zeus]] was her true father. [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]]'s royal [[sparta|Sparta]]n lineage — she was a princess of [[sparta|Sparta]] before her marriage to [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] — contributed to her formidable authority and her characterization as a woman "with a man's heart."

## The Oath of Tyndareus

Tyndareus was most famous in Greek myth for the [[oath|Oath]] that bears his name. When Helen's suitors competed for her hand, Tyndareus — fearing conflict among the rejected suitors — required all of them to swear an oath to defend Helen's chosen husband. This oath bound the Greek kings to join the [[troy|Trojan War]] when Helen was abducted by Paris, making Tyndareus an indirect but crucial figure in the war that forms the Oresteia's background.
