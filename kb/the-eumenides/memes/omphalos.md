# The Omphalos (Navel-Stone)

The **Omphalos** (Greek: ὀμφαλός, "navel") is the sacred stone at [[delphi|Delphi]] believed to mark the exact center of the earth. In [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]], it is before this stone — "the inmost Altar of the world" — that [[orestes|Orestes]] takes refuge as a [[supplication|suppliant]]. As the endnotes on staging explain, the Omphalos Altar was the sacred focal point that, when replaced by a statue of [[athena|Athena]], signaled the shift from [[delphi|Delphi]] to [[athens|Athens]] in performance (002-004).

## In The Eumenides

The [[pythian-prophetess|Pythian Prophetess]] discovers [[orestes|Orestes]] at the Omphalos when she enters the inner shrine:

> I made my way into the Holy Place, / And there, at the inmost Altar of the world, / A man abhorred of God, his body hurled / Earthward in desperate prayer; blood on his hand / Yet reeking, and a naked new-drawn brand / Wreathed in beseeching wool, a suppliant's weed / Of snow-white fleece.
>
> (001-001)

The "inmost Altar of the world" is the Omphalos. [[orestes|Orestes]]' supplication at this most sacred of Greek sanctuaries is a dramatic assertion of his claim to divine protection. He holds the "naked new-drawn brand / Wreathed in beseeching wool" — the ritual tokens of a suppliant.

## Mythological Significance

According to Greek myth, [[zeus|Zeus]] released two eagles from opposite ends of the earth, and they met at [[delphi|Delphi]], marking it as the world's center. The Omphalos stone was placed at this meeting point. It was kept in the inner sanctuary (adyton) of [[apollo|Apollo]]'s temple and was considered the most sacred object at [[delphi|Delphi]].

## Symbolism in the Play

The Omphalos is the geographical center of the play's action — the point from which [[orestes|Orestes]] is driven outward (to [[athens|Athens]]) and toward which the chain of vengeance has driven him. Its position as the earth's "navel" also carries maternal symbolism: the navel is the scar of birth, the mark of connection to the mother. [[orestes|Orestes]], who has killed his mother, clings to the navel of [[mother-earth|Mother Earth]] — a powerful image of his desperate need for purification and rebirth.
