# The Titanomachy

The **Titanomachy** (Greek: Τιτανομαχία, "Battle of the Titans") was the ten-year war in which the [[olympian-order|Olympian Gods]], led by [[zeus|Zeus]], overthrew the Titans, the earlier generation of gods. (P01-009) This cosmic conflict is the mythological background against which [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]] unfolds.

## The Conflict

The Titans, children of Uranus (Sky) and [[mother-earth|Gaia]] ([[mother-earth|Earth]]), were the first ruling generation of gods. Cronus, the youngest Titan, overthrew his father Uranus at [[mother-earth|Gaia]]'s urging. Cronus, in turn, was warned that his own child would overthrow him — and so he swallowed each of his children at birth. But his wife Rhea saved the infant [[zeus|Zeus]], who later forced Cronus to disgorge his siblings and led them in war against the Titans.

## Victory of the Olympians

With the aid of the Cyclopes and the Hecatoncheires (Hundred-Handers), [[zeus|Zeus]] and the Olympians defeated the Titans and imprisoned them in Tartarus. This victory established the Olympian order — the divine regime under which the action of *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]* takes place.

## Parallels in The Eumenides

The Titanomachy is a model of violent divine succession — the old gods overthrown by force. [[athena|Athena]]'s strategy in *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]* represents a conscious alternative: instead of conquering the old gods (the [[erinyes|Furies]]), she persuades and incorporates them. The play thus imagines a new kind of divine transition — peaceful, persuasive, and permanent.
