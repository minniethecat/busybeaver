# The Binding Song of the Furies

The **Binding Song** (Greek: Ὕμνος Δέσμιος, *Hymnos Desmios*) is the ritual chant performed by the [[erinyes|Furies]] in [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]] as they seek to ensnare [[orestes|Orestes]] with their magic. It is one of the most powerful and terrifying passages in Greek tragedy.

## The Ritual

The Binding Song is a ritual of pursuit and capture. The [[the-furies-chorus|Chorus of Furies]] dances around [[orestes|Orestes]], weaving a spell through their words and movements. The song describes the [[erinyes|Furies]]' power to pursue their victims across land and sea, to drain them of life and sanity, and to deliver them to the darkness below.

## Murray's Translation

In Gilbert Murray's translation, the Binding Song is rendered with driving, incantatory rhythms:

"Up, then, and sing the spell that shall make his senses swim, / The downward, draggling spell, / The [[erinyes|Furies]]' hymn, / The chains for the mind, / The song of them that strike, that destroy the soul and dim, / Not tuned to the lyre, not sweet for the day, that wither the human kind!"

## Dramatic Function

The Binding Song represents the climax of the [[erinyes|Furies]]' power in the play. It is their most direct and concerted attack on [[orestes|Orestes]]. The ritual nature of the song — with its repetitions, its incantatory rhythms, and its vivid imagery of pursuit and consumption — creates a moment of intense dramatic tension. As the endnotes observe, "the triumphant cry of the Fury, and the Binding Song to fix his despair" marks the moment when hope seems lost for [[orestes|Orestes]] before [[athena|Athena]]'s entrance (002-025).

The scene also demonstrates the nature of the [[erinyes|Furies]]' power: it is not physical force but a kind of spiritual and psychological binding. They "sing" their victim to destruction, draining his mind and body through the power of their curse.

## The Song's Failure

Despite its power, the Binding Song ultimately fails. [[athena|Athena]]'s intervention — the establishment of the [[trial-of-orestes|trial]] and the [[the-areopagus-court|Areopagus Court]] — replaces the [[erinyes|Furies]]' ritual magic with a new form of binding: the binding of law, civic procedure, and reasoned judgment.

> This song falls into two parts: a solemn and even philosophical statement of the place of the Avengers in the Cosmos, and a magical chorus or “Binding Song,” sinister and terrifyin.
>
> (002-026)
