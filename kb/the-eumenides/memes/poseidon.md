# Poseidon

**Poseidon** (Greek: Ποσειδῶν) is the god of the sea, earthquakes, and horses, and one of the twelve [[the-olympian-gods|Olympian Gods]]. He is mentioned in [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]] in the opening prayer of the [[pythian-prophetess|Pythian Prophetess]].

## In The Eumenides

The Prophetess invokes Poseidon near the end of her prayer (001-001):

"And [[pleistos-river|Pleistos]]' fountains and Poseidon's power I call, and Him who brings the Perfect Hour, [[zeus|Zeus]], the Most Highest."

The invocation follows a geographical pattern: the prophetess calls upon the powers of the [[delphi|Delphi]]c landscape — the [[pleistos-river|Pleistos River]], Poseidon (as god of waters and the earth-shaker associated with the region), and finally [[zeus|Zeus]] the Perfector.

## Role in the Play

Poseidon's invocation is brief but significant. It places him within the sacred topography of [[delphi|Delphi]] and reinforces the prophetess's comprehensive piety — she acknowledges all divine powers connected to the sanctuary before taking her seat. The endnotes to the Murray translation explain that the prophetess "praises the Holders of the Throne of prophecy at [[delphi|Delphi]]... then the other divine beings in the neighbourhood, including the River [[pleistos-river|Pleistos]], and ending suitably with [[zeus|Zeus]]."

## Mythological Context

In Greek mythology, Poseidon is the brother of [[zeus|Zeus]] and Hades, and after the overthrow of the Titans, he received dominion over the sea. Though primarily a marine deity, he was also worshipped as the "[[mother-earth|Earth]]-Shaker" (Ennosigaios), associated with earthquakes — a connection relevant to the seismically active region around [[delphi|Delphi]] and [[mount-parnassus|Parnassus]].
