# Divine Succession

**Divine succession** (Greek: διαδοχή, *diadochê*) is the orderly transfer of divine power from older to younger generations of gods. It is a central theological theme in [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]], where peaceful succession is presented as the alternative to violent cosmic conflict.

## The Delphic Model

> Themis, as Dr. Jane Harrison has shown, is the correct tribal custom, the thing that is always done—always, that is, by the people who really know.
>
> (P01-003)

The [[pythian-prophetess|Pythian Prophetess]] traces the succession of the [[delphic-oracle|Delphic Oracle]]:

[[mother-earth|Mother Earth]] → [[themis|Themis]] → [[phoebe|Phoebe]] → [[apollo|Apollo]]

Each transfer was peaceful, "by whose own / Accepted will, with never strife nor stress." As Murray's endnotes confirm, "Aeschylus insists that there was never any strife among the gods at [[delphi|Delphi]]." (002-005) This is the model — orderly, voluntary, without violence — that Aeschylus presents as normative.

## Contrast with Violent Succession

The [[delphi|Delphi]]c model contrasts sharply with other succession myths in Greek tradition:
- **Titanomachy**: [[the-olympian-gods|The Olympians]] overthrew the Titans by force
- **Traditional [[delphi|Delphi]]c myth**: [[apollo|Apollo]] slew [[python-the-dragon|Python]] to seize the oracle
- **Ouranos-Cronus**: Cronus castrated his father Ouranos; [[zeus|Zeus]] overthrew Cronus

By deliberately revising the [[delphi|Delphi]]c foundation myth to remove all violence, Aeschylus creates a theological model for the peaceful resolution of the play's central conflict.

## The Eumenides Resolution

[[athena|Athena]]'s treatment of the [[erinyes|Furies]] follows the [[delphi|Delphi]]c model rather than the Titanomachy model. She does not destroy the old powers; she persuades and incorporates them, offering them honor in the new order. This is divine succession by consent — the theological innovation at the heart of Aeschylus' vision.
