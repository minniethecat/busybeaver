# Erichthonius

**Erichthonius** (Greek: Ἐριχθόνιος) was a legendary king of [[athens|Athens]], born from the earth when [[hephaistos|Hephaistos]]'s seed fell upon [[mother-earth|Gaia]]. He is the archetypal symbol of Athenian autochthony — the belief that the Athenians were born from the very soil of their land.

## Autochthony and Athens

The myth of Erichthonius embodies the Athenian claim to be autochthonous (literally "sprung from the earth"). This idea was central to Athenian identity: unlike other Greeks who had migrated to their lands, the Athenians had always been there. [[athena|Athena]] raised the infant Erichthonius and gave him to the daughters of Cecrops to guard, establishing her special relationship with [[athens|Athens]] from its very origins.

## Connection to The Eumenides

[[athena|Athena]]'s role in [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]] as the founder of the [[the-areopagus-court|Areopagus Court]] draws on her deep mythological connection to [[athens|Athens]]. She is not an outsider imposing order but the city's own divine patron, whose relationship to [[athens|Athens]] goes back to the very birth of its first king.

## Murray's Reference

Murray mentions Erichthonius among the kings who "had given their children to die," referring to the sacrifice of his daughters by [[erechtheus|Erechtheus]] (sometimes identified with Erichthonius). Such sacrifices, Murray argues, belong to "that primitive and barbaric world which Hellenic [[zeus|Zeus]] had swept away." (P01-016)
