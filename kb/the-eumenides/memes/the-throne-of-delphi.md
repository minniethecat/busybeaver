# The Throne of Delphi

The **Throne of [[delphi|Delphi]]** (Greek: μαντεῖος θρόνος, "prophetic throne") is the seat of prophecy at the [[delphic-oracle|Delphic Oracle]]. In the opening speech of [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]], the [[pythian-prophetess|Pythian Prophetess]] traces the sacred lineage of those who have held it.

## The Succession

The prophetess invokes "the Holders of the Throne" in order:

1. **[[mother-earth|Mother Earth]]** — the primeval prophet
2. **[[themis|Themis]]** — the Wise, who sat "on her mother's throne"
3. **[[phoebe|Phoebe]]** — the earth-born Titaness, who held it third
4. **[[apollo|Apollo (Loxias)]]** — the current holder, "fourth in use"

Each succession was peaceful, "by whose own / Accepted will, with never strife nor stress." The Throne thus embodies the model of orderly divine transition that the play extends to the [[erinyes|Furies]].
