# Menelaus

**Menelaus** (Greek: Μενέλαος) was the king of [[sparta|Sparta]], husband of Helen, and brother of [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]. Though he does not appear in [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]], his story is the background of the entire [[the-oresteia|Oresteia]].

## The Trojan War

When Helen was abducted by Paris of [[troy|Troy]], Menelaus called upon the Greek kings — bound by the Oath of [[tyndareus|Tyndareus]] — to join him in war against [[troy|Troy]]. [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]], as the most powerful Greek king, led the expedition. The ten-year war that followed is the world from which the Oresteia's characters return.

## The Brothers

Menelaus and [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] married sisters (Helen and [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]]), linking the houses of [[sparta|Sparta]] and [[argos|Mycenae]]. The contrast between the brothers is instructive: [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] returned from [[troy|Troy]] to be murdered by his wife, while Menelaus — after many wanderings — eventually returned to [[sparta|Sparta]] and ruled in relative peace. The different fates of the two brothers underscore the role of the curse on the [[the-house-of-atreus|House of Atreus]] in determining [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]'s doom.
