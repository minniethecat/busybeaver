# Delos

**Delos** is a small rocky island in the Aegean Sea, sacred in Greek mythology as the birthplace of [[apollo|Apollo]] (and his twin sister [[artemis|Artemis]]). In *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]*, Delos appears in the [[pythian-prophetess|Pythian Prophetess]]'s opening prayer as [[apollo|Apollo]]'s point of origin before his journey to [[delphi|Delphi]].

## Mythological Significance

Delos was the island where [[lete|Leto]], pursued by the jealous [[hera|Hera]], found refuge to give birth to [[apollo|Apollo]] and [[artemis|Artemis]]. According to the Homeric Hymn to [[apollo|Apollo]], Delos was a floating island until it was anchored to the seabed as a reward for providing sanctuary to [[lete|Leto]]. In Aeschylus's telling, [[apollo|Apollo]] "left his isle, he left his Delian seas" to travel to [[delphi|Delphi]] and claim the prophetic throne (001-002).

> Apollo was at Delos, his rocky isle with the "orbèd mere" in it, when he heard of the gift made to him, and set off to take possession of Delphi.
>
> (002-006)

## Role in *The Eumenides*

The Prophetess's prayer traces the peaceful succession of prophetic deities at [[delphi|Delphi]]. In this genealogy, [[apollo|Apollo]] receives [[delphi|Delphi]] as a "birthtide gift" from his grandmother [[phoebe|Phoebe]] "for that he bears her name" (001-002). The endnotes elaborate: "[[apollo|Apollo]] was at Delos, his rocky isle with the 'orbèd mere' in it, when he heard of the gift made to him, and set off to take possession of [[delphi|Delphi]]" (002-006).

Aeschylus emphasizes that unlike other versions of the myth, there was "never strife nor stress" in this transfer of power — a detail consistent with the play's broader theme of peaceful succession and institutional continuity (002-005).

## Geography

Delos is the central island of the Cyclades archipelago. In antiquity it was a major religious and political center, hosting the Delian League's treasury before it was moved to [[athens|Athens]]. The "Delian seas" mentioned by Aeschylus refer to the Aegean waters surrounding the island.
