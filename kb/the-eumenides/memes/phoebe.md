# Phoebe

**Phoebe** (Greek: Φοίβη, "Bright One") is a Titaness in Greek mythology, daughter of Uranus (Sky) and [[mother-earth|Gaia]] ([[mother-earth|Earth]]). She is significant in [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]] as part of the sacred succession of the [[delphic-oracle|Delphic Oracle]].

## In The Eumenides

The [[pythian-prophetess|Pythian Prophetess]] traces the lineage of the [[delphi|Delphi]]c oracle in her opening prayer (001-033):

[[mother-earth|Mother Earth]] → [[themis|Themis]] → **Phoebe** → [[apollo|Apollo]]

Each succession was peaceful, "with never strife nor stress." Phoebe, an earth-born Titaness, received the oracle from [[themis|Themis]] and later gave it to [[apollo|Apollo]] — who bears the name [[apollo|Phoebus]] in her honor — as a "birthtide gift."

## Aeschylus' Innovation

Aeschylus deliberately departs from the traditional myth, in which [[apollo|Apollo]] seized the oracle by violence from a dragon ([[python-the-dragon|Python]]) born of [[mother-earth|Earth]]. By emphasizing peaceful succession through Phoebe, the poet establishes a theological model of harmonious transfer of power — a model that prefigures the play's central resolution, in which the [[erinyes|Furies]] are peacefully incorporated into the Olympian order rather than destroyed.

## Genealogical Significance

In Hesiod's *Theogony*, Phoebe is the mother of [[lete|Leto]] and Asteria, making her the grandmother of [[apollo|Apollo]] and [[artemis|Artemis]]. Her gift of the oracle to [[apollo|Apollo]] thus follows a natural familial line, reinforcing Aeschylus' theme of peaceful succession within a divine order that includes both the old gods and the new.
