# The Purification at Delphi

The Purification at Delphi is the ritual act — performed by [[apollo|Apollo]] at his shrine — that cleanses [[orestes|Orestes]] of the blood-pollution (*miasma*) incurred by killing his mother. It is the essential precondition for the trial at [[athens|Athens]]: only a purified suppliant can approach [[athena|Athena]]'s statue and request justice.

## Overview

[[orestes|Orestes]] arrives at [[delphi|Delphi]] "blood on his hand yet reeking, and a naked new-drawn brand Wreathed in beseeching wool, a suppliant's weed of snow-white fleece" (001-001). He is at once a source of pollution — so contagious that the [[pythian-prophetess|Pythian Prophetess]] flees the shrine in terror — and a legitimate suppliant, entitled to [[apollo|Apollo]]'s protection. [[apollo|Apollo]] performs the ritual cleansing (*katharmos*), and the [[erinyes|Furies]], who had pursued [[orestes|Orestes]] to [[delphi|Delphi]], fall asleep, exhausted.

## Context

[[orestes|Orestes]] has killed his mother [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] at [[apollo|Apollo]]'s command, in vengeance for her murder of his father [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]. The [[erinyes|Furies]] — the ancient enforcers of the blood-feud — have pursued him relentlessly since the matricide. [[delphi|Delphi]] is his first refuge, the first place where he can stop running and seek divine intervention.

## The Event

[[apollo|Apollo]] performs the purification ritual, the details of which are not dramatized but are referenced throughout the play. He also commissions [[hermes|Hermes]] to guide [[orestes|Orestes]] on the journey to [[athens|Athens]]. [[apollo|Apollo]]'s statement at the trial — "Therefore the cleansing of his stain is mine" (001-076) — confirms that the purification has been performed and that [[orestes|Orestes]] is, from [[apollo|Apollo]]'s perspective, ritually clean.

## Aftermath

[[apollo|Apollo]] sends [[orestes|Orestes]] to [[athens|Athens]]: "On till thou come to [[acropolis|Pallas' Rock]], and fold Thine arms in prayer about her image old. In [[athens|Athens]] there be hearts to judge, there be Words that bring peace; and I shall set thee free" (001-002). The [[erinyes|Furies]], wakened by the ghost of [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]], resume their pursuit — but [[orestes|Orestes]] now travels under [[hermes|Hermes]]' protection.

## In The Eumenides

The purification establishes the play's theological framework: [[apollo|Apollo]]'s ritual cleansing is real (from the Olympian perspective) but insufficient (from the chthonian perspective). The [[erinyes|Furies]] do not recognize purification as valid — the blood once shed can never be un-shed. This disagreement is what makes the trial necessary.
