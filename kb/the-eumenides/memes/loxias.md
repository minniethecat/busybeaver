# Loxias (Apollo as Prophet)

**[[apollo|Loxias]]** (Greek: Λοξίας) is a cultic epithet of [[apollo|Apollo]] used throughout [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]] to emphasize his role as the prophetic interpreter of [[zeus|Zeus]]'s will.

## In The Eumenides

The [[pythian-prophetess|Pythian Prophetess]] uses the name [[apollo|Loxias]] when describing [[apollo|Apollo]]'s installation at [[delphi|Delphi]]:

"So [[apollo|Loxias]]' lips reveal the thought of [[zeus|Zeus]]." (001-002)

This is a crucial theological statement. [[apollo|Loxias]] is not an independent source of prophecy — he is the *Prophêtês Dios*, the revealer or interpreter of [[zeus|Zeus]]. As [[apollo|Apollo]] himself declares: "Never have I spoken on my throne of prophecy any word concerning man, woman or city, which was not commanded by [[zeus|Zeus the Father]]." (P01-011)

## Etymology and Meaning

The name "[[apollo|Loxias]]" may derive from Greek *loxos* ("oblique," "ambiguous"), referring to the famously enigmatic nature of oracular pronouncements. Alternatively, it may be connected to *legein* ("to speak"), making [[apollo|Loxias]] "the Speaker" — the one through whom [[zeus|Zeus]] speaks.

## Theological Significance

The epithet [[apollo|Loxias]] represents Aeschylus' careful theological construction. By repeatedly calling [[apollo|Apollo]] "[[apollo|Loxias]]," the poet emphasizes that [[apollo|Apollo]]'s authority derives entirely from [[zeus|Zeus]]. When [[apollo|Apollo]] commands [[orestes|Orestes]] to kill [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]], he does so as [[apollo|Loxias]] — the mouthpiece of [[zeus|Zeus]]. When he defends [[orestes|Orestes]] at the [[trial-of-orestes|trial]], he speaks with [[zeus|Zeus]]'s authority: "the oracles that are mine and the Father's." (P01-011)

This subordination of [[apollo|Apollo]] to [[zeus|Zeus]] is essential to the play's resolution. The forgiveness of [[orestes|Orestes]] is not [[apollo|Apollo]]'s innovation but the will of [[zeus|Zeus]] — the [[zeus|Zeus]] "who made a road to Thought, who established [[learning-by-suffering|Learning by Suffering]] to be an abiding law."
