# Argos (Mycenae)

Argos (and nearby Mycenae) is the kingdom of the House of Atreus — the realm of [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]], [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]], and [[orestes|Orestes]]. In *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]*, Argos is the place [[orestes|Orestes]] has fled, the site of the crimes that the trial must adjudicate, and the home to which he will return after his acquittal.

## Location

Argos lies in the Argolid plain of the northeastern Peloponnese. Mycenae, the citadel of [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]'s dynasty, is a few kilometers to the north. In the mythological geography of the Oresteia, Argos/Mycenae represents the old world of the blood-feud — a place where kings are murdered in their baths, where queens conspire with usurpers, and where sons must kill mothers to avenge fathers.

## Role in the Play

### Orestes' Homeland

[[orestes|Orestes]] identifies himself by his Argive lineage when he first speaks to [[athena|Athena]]:

> I am sprung of Argive earth; My father's name was known upon thy lips, Agamemnon, marshal of a thousand ships, With whom thou madest Troy, that city of pride, No more a city.
>
> (001-069)

The Argive identity is central to his self-presentation — he is not merely a suppliant but a king's son from a great city.

### The Proposed Argive Alliance

[[orestes|Orestes]] twice offers an alliance between Argos and [[athens|Athens]] as a reward for his acquittal. In his opening supplication he promises that Argos will be [[athena|Athena]]'s ally "without war" and "true of heart and changeless" (001-035). After his acquittal, he swears a formal oath: no Argive captain shall ever raise his spear against [[athens|Athens]] (001-132). This Argive alliance was a significant political reference for Aeschylus' Athenian audience — an Argive alliance was traditional in Athenian foreign policy.

### The Furies' Challenge

The [[erinyes|Furies]] seize on the Argos connection to challenge the acquittal. They demand to know how [[orestes|Orestes]] can return to Argos, enter his home, and participate in its religious life after shedding his mother's blood (001-107). [[apollo|Apollo]] answers by promising that Argos will "observe the pact" of loyalty to [[athens|Athens]] through future generations (001-108).
