# Sparta

**Sparta** (Greek: Σπάρτη; also Lacedaemon) was a prominent city-state in the Peloponnese, the homeland of [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] and her sister Helen. It is part of the genealogical background of [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]].

## Clytemnestra's Origins

[[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] was the daughter of [[tyndareus|Tyndareus]], king of Sparta, and [[leda|Leda]]. Her sister was Helen, whose abduction by Paris triggered the [[the-trojan-war|Trojan War]]. [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]]'s marriage to [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] of [[argos|Argos]] linked the Spartan royal house to the cursed lineage of [[the-house-of-atreus|Atreus]].

## The Spartan Connection

The Spartan connection is significant for the Oresteia's treatment of gender and power. [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] is frequently described in masculine terms — she has a "man's heart" and rules [[argos|Argos]] in her husband's absence. This characterization may draw on the Spartan reputation for strong, influential women, who in historical Sparta enjoyed greater freedom and authority than their Athenian counterparts.

## In the Background of the Trilogy

Sparta does not appear directly in *The [[erinyes|Eumenides]]*, but its royal house provides essential context for the trilogy's conflicts. The adultery of Helen (Spartan queen) with Paris, and the adultery of [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] (Spartan princess) with [[aegisthus|Aegisthus]], are parallel transgressions that drive the action of the first two plays.
