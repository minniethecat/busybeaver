# The Delian Seas

The **Delian seas** (Greek: Δηλίαι θάλασσαι) are the waters surrounding the sacred island of [[delos|Delos]] in the Aegean Sea. They are mentioned in the opening speech of [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]] by the [[pythian-prophetess|Pythian Prophetess]].

## Apollo's Departure

The prophetess describes [[apollo|Apollo]]'s journey from his birthplace at [[delos|Delos]] to his prophetic home at [[delphi|Delphi]]:

"He left his isle, he left his Delian seas, / He passed [[athena|Athena]]'s wave-worn promontories, / In haste this great [[mount-parnassus|Parnassus]] to possess."

The Delian seas are thus the starting point of [[apollo|Apollo]]'s sacred journey — the waters he must cross to claim his inheritance at [[delphi|Delphi]].

## Geography of the Sacred

The mention of the Delian seas is part of the prophetess's comprehensive geographical prayer. She maps a sacred landscape that extends from [[delos|Delos]] in the Aegean to the [[corycian-cave|Corycian Cave]] on [[mount-parnassus|Parnassus]] to the fountains of the [[pleistos-river|Pleistos River]]. This geographical breadth reflects the universal scope of [[apollo|Apollo]]'s authority and the interconnectedness of Greek sacred sites.
