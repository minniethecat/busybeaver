# Cassandra

**Cassandra** (Greek: Κασσάνδρα) was a princess of [[troy|Troy]], daughter of King Priam. She appears in the *[[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]* (the first play of the [[the-oresteia|Oresteia]]) as [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]'s captive and concubine, brought back from the [[the-trojan-war|Trojan War]].

## The Gift of Prophecy

Cassandra was granted the gift of prophecy by [[apollo|Apollo]], but when she refused his advances, he cursed her: she would always prophesy truly, but never be believed. In the *[[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]*, she foresees her own death and [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]]'s with perfect clarity, yet the chorus cannot understand her until it is too late.

## Death at Argos

Cassandra was murdered by [[clytemnestra|Clytemnestra]] alongside [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]] upon their return to [[argos|Argos]]. Her death exemplifies the indiscriminate violence of the blood-feud: she is killed not for anything she has done, but because she is associated with [[agamemnon|Agamemnon]].

## Connection to The Eumenides

Though Cassandra does not appear in [[the-eumenides|The Eumenides]], her murder is part of the chain of violence that the play must resolve. The blood-feud logic that destroys her — guilt by association — is the same logic that the [[the-areopagus-court|Areopagus Court]] is established to replace.
