# The Great Ball Game of Glooskap

## Narrative

### The Game at Piktook

When Glooskap transformed his uncle Mikchich and won him the chief's daughter for a wife, the jealousy of the young men of Piktook was kindled against the newcomer. Glooskap forewarned his uncle: "To-morrow there will be a great game at ball, and you must play. But because you have made yourself enemies of all the young men here, they will seek to slay you, by crowding all together and trampling upon you" (008-135). Glooskap gave Mikchich the power to leap high over the lodge to escape them, which he might do twice — but the third time, he warned, "will be terrible for you, and yet it must be" (008-139).

All happened as foretold. The young men indeed tried to take his life, and to escape them Mikchich jumped over the lodge, so that he seemed like a bird flying (008-142). But the third time he did this, he was caught on the top of the tent-poles and hung there dangling in the smoke (008-144). It was at this moment that Glooskap began the smoking transformation that made Mikchich the chief of the Tortoises, his skin becoming a hard shell.

### The Game on the Saco

In a later and darker episode, Glooskap confronted a family of evil magicians — three brothers and their one-eyed father — who killed every living soul they met. After Glooskap had demonstrated his power by retrieving stolen food with a wish, snapping a whale's jawbone with his fingers, and smoking a pipe of the strongest tobacco to ashes in a single pull, one of the brothers declared: "This is idle; let us go and play at ball" (018-053).

The place where they were to play was on the sandy plain of Samgadihawk, or Saco, on the bend of the river (018-054). But Glooskap discovered that the ball with which they played was a hideous skull — it was alive and snapped at his heels, and had he been as other men, it would have taken his foot off (018-057). Glooskap laughed at this treachery and declared: "So this is the game you play. Good, but let us all play with our own balls" (018-060). He broke off the end of a bough from a tree on the river-bank, and it turned into a skull ten times more terrible than the other. The magicians ran before it as a lynx chases rabbits — they were entirely beaten (018-063).

Glooskap then stamped on the sand, and the waters rose and came rushing fearfully from the mountains down the river-bed; the whole land rang with their roar (018-066). He sang a magic song which changes all beings, and the three brothers and their father became the _chinahmess_, a fish as long and large as a man, and they went headlong down on the flood to dwell in the deep sea forever (018-069).

## Significance

The ball game episodes illustrate a recurring pattern in Glooskap legends: the hero confronting and defeating adversaries at their own game, turning their weapons and tricks against them. The skull-ball on Saco River is a particularly vivid image of the magicians' malevolence — they have literally weaponized the dead. Glooskap's counter-spell — creating an even more terrible skull from a tree branch — demonstrates the Algonquin belief that a greater _m'teoulin_ (sorcerer) can always reverse the magic of a lesser one. The transformation of the magicians into fish and their permanent exile to the sea echoes the theme that Glooskap purges the land of evil beings, reshaping the world into a safe habitation for humanity.

Physically, the sandy plain at Saco has yielded artifacts — stone pestles and hominy pounders — that local tradition associates with the site of this legendary ball-play (018-056).
