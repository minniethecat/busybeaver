# How Glooskap Found the Summer

## Narrative

In the old time, the world was gripped by endless Winter. The people suffered under the tyranny of the Frost Giant, and the land knew only ice and snow. Glooskap, seeing the misery of his children, resolved to find the Summer and bring warmth to the earth (019-075).

The Master journeyed far to the North, where he came to the lodge of Winter. Winter received him with cold courtesy, and as he spoke, the very air grew frost-bound. But Glooskap was undeterred. He traveled still farther, beyond the reach of Winter's power, until he came to a land of warmth and flowering — and there he found a great lodge where the Queen of Summer dwelt among her attendants (019-088).

Summer herself received Glooskap with joy. She was bright and beautiful, and the air around her was filled with the song of birds and the scent of blossoms. Glooskap told her of the suffering of his people, and she agreed to return with him. So Glooskap took Summer and placed her in his bosom, and though he carried her away, he let the end trail behind him (019-091).

The journey back was strange and wondrous. As Glooskap came again to the lodge of Winter, the Frost Giant felt the warmth emanating from the Master and grew weak. Summer, still hidden in Glooskap's bosom, began to sing her song — and the ice upon the rivers cracked and melted, the snow retreated, and the green things of the earth began to stir (019-094).

> "Their queen was Summer. I am singing the truth: it was Summer, the most beautiful of all" (019-088)

Winter, seeing his dominion crumbling, cried out: "Great Lord, you have conquered me! I can no longer hold the land." And Glooskap, who was ever wise, did not destroy Winter entirely. Instead, he decreed that Winter and Summer should each rule the land for a portion of the year — thus establishing the cycle of seasons that endures to this day (019-096). Then Glooskap left Summer with his people, and the rivers ran free, carrying away the dead leaves (019-101).

## Significance

The finding of Summer is one of the most poetically rich and structurally significant episodes in the Glooskap cycle. It belongs to a worldwide tradition of seasonal myths — Leland notes that Winter and Summer appear as "incarnate beings" in this narrative, paralleling similar personifications in Norse and classical mythology (019-107). The episode establishes a fundamental cosmic order: that neither Winter nor Summer shall dominate permanently, but each must yield in turn. This balance, decreed by Glooskap, reflects a deep Wabanaki understanding of nature's rhythms. The image of Glooskap carrying Summer in his bosom while Winter's ice melts around him is among the most vivid in the entire Algonquin mythological corpus.
