**Keanke** is a figure mentioned in the Micmac narrative of [[Glooskap]]'s great frolic with [[Kitpooseagunow]]. He appears in the illustration accompanying the whale-spearing episode, captioned "Glooskap and Keanke spearing the whale" (011-074), suggesting his role as a companion or alternate participant in the famous whale hunt.

## Overview

The corpus contains only a single explicit reference to Keanke — the illustration caption in the Kitpooseagunow story. However, the illustration places him alongside Glooskap in the act of spearing the whale, indicating that in at least one version of the narrative (or in the visual tradition that accompanied it), Keanke rather than Kitpooseagunow was the whale-hunter who accompanied the culture hero.

This substitution — Keanke for Kitpooseagunow in the illustration — suggests either a variant tradition in which a different companion assists Glooskap, or a visual artist's independent interpretation of the story. The fact that the illustration exists at all indicates that Keanke was recognized as a figure in the Glooskap cycle, even if his narrative role was not fully recorded in Leland's text.

## Identity and Attributes

The name _Keanke_ is of uncertain etymology. The single attestation in the corpus limits what can be said about his traditional attributes, though his placement in the whale-spearing scene associates him with the prodigious feats of strength and magical power characteristic of Glooskap's companions.

## Role in Legends

### The Whale Hunt Illustration

The illustration referenced at 011-074 depicts "Glooskap and Keanke spearing the whale" — a scene that corresponds to the central episode of the Kitpooseagunow narrative, in which the culture hero and his giant companion hunt a whale of colossal size by torchlight. The substitution of Keanke for Kitpooseagunow in the illustration may indicate that Keanke was an alternate name for the giant, or that a separate tradition assigned the whale-hunt to a different companion.
