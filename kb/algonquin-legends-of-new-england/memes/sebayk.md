## Overview

**Sebayk** (also spelled Sebayik or Sipayik) was a Passamaquoddy settlement near [[campobello|Campobello Island]] at the mouth of the Bay of Fundy. In the Algonquin legends collected by Leland, Sebayk appears as one of the communities where the practice of _m'teoulin_—traditional Wabanaki shamanic magic—survived into the modern era, alongside the St. John River settlements.

## Geography

Sebayk is located near Campobello Island, at the entrance to Passamaquoddy Bay on the border between present-day Maine (United States) and New Brunswick (Canada). The name Sebayk is referenced in the legends as a known community within the Passamaquoddy territory (005-112). Its proximity to Campobello places it within the culturally rich area where Maliseet and Passamaquoddy traditions intersected.

## Role in Legends

### The M'teoulin of Sebayk

The legends record that at the time of Leland's writing, _m'teoulin_ practitioners were still active at Sebayk. One account describes "an old Passamaquoddy Indian, of Sebayk, near Campobello" (045-081) who possessed knowledge of traditional magic. The text emphasizes that "many at St. John and Sebayk are still _m'teoulin_" (045-096), indicating that the settlement was a significant center for the preservation of Wabanaki spiritual practices.

### Continuity of Tradition

Unlike many other locations in the legends that appear only in mythic narratives, Sebayk is notable for its role as a living community at the time of collection. The mention of active _m'teoulin_ at Sebayk provides a bridge between the ancient myth cycle and the living Passamaquoddy culture of the 1880s, demonstrating the persistence of traditional beliefs despite centuries of colonial pressure.

### M'teoulin Powers

The _m'teoulin_ tradition practiced at Sebayk encompassed abilities described throughout the legends: healing, shape-shifting, control over animals and weather, and communication with spiritual beings. The practitioners at Sebayk and St. John represented a living chain of knowledge connecting the mythic age of Glooskap to the present day.

## Historical Context

The Passamaquoddy people have inhabited the Fundy coastal region for thousands of years. The settlement of Sebayk (modern-day Sipayik, the Pleasant Point Passamaquoddy Reservation in Maine) remains an active Passamaquoddy community. At the time of Leland's collection in the 1880s, the Passamaquoddy were navigating the pressures of assimilation while maintaining their linguistic and spiritual heritage.
