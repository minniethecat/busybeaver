# Malsum

## Identity and Attributes

Malsum (also known as Malsum the Wolf) is the evil twin brother of Glooskap in Wabanaki creation mythology. Born together with Glooskap, Malsum represents the opposing principle of destruction, chaos, and malevolence — the necessary antagonist whose existence sets the stage for Glooskap's heroic deeds. Together, the twin brothers embody the dualistic structure of Wabanaki cosmology: the Good and Evil principles that together shape the created order (002-208, 003-148).

## Relationships

### With Glooskap

Malsum is the dark counterpart to Glooskap. Where Glooskap creates, Malsum seeks to destroy; where Glooskap frees humanity from monsters, Malsum allies with those same forces. Their fraternal conflict is the central axis around which much of Wabanaki mythology turns. Leland notes that Glooskap was born together with his brother Malsum the Wolf, who represented the opposing force of evil, and that their birth forms the foundation of the Wabanaki creation narrative (003-148).

### With Other Beings

As the embodiment of the evil principle, Malsum is associated with the hostile forces of the Wabanaki mythological world: the sorcerers, the ice-hearted Chenoo, and the various monsters that Glooskap must overcome. His wolf nature connects him to the predatory, wild aspects of the northern wilderness.

## In the Creation Narrative

The birth of the twins establishes the fundamental drama of Wabanaki mythology. From the beginning, the two brothers are set in opposition: Glooskap represents light, creation, and benevolence toward humankind, while Malsum represents darkness, destruction, and antagonism. This dualism is not merely moral but cosmological — it structures the world itself.

## Cultural Significance

The Glooskap-Malsum duality places Wabanaki mythology within a broad cross-cultural pattern of divine or semi-divine twins representing opposed cosmic principles, comparable to figures in Zoroastrian, Norse, and many indigenous American traditions. Unlike some traditions where the evil twin is ultimately destroyed, the Wabanaki narrative keeps Malsum as an ongoing presence — evil is not eliminated but perpetually contested.
