# Win-pe

## Identity and Attributes

Win-pe is a powerful sorcerer figure in the Glooskap cycle of Wabanaki mythology. His name appears in accounts of one of Glooskap's major confrontations with hostile supernatural forces: the theft of Glooskap's family and the subsequent pursuit. Win-pe represents the type of the malevolent magician — one who uses spiritual power for theft and harm rather than for the benefit of the community.

## Relationships

### With Glooskap

Win-pe is a direct adversary of Glooskap. When Win-pe stole Glooskap's family, Glooskap pursued him relentlessly. After freeing his family from the sorcerer's grasp, Glooskap confronted Win-pe directly and punished him for his misdeeds (007-026, 007-048). This narrative establishes the pattern of Glooskap as the protector of family and community against supernatural predation.

### With the M'teoulin Tradition

As a sorcerer, Win-pe represents a dark aspect of the M'teoulin tradition — the use of spiritual power for antisocial ends. He belongs to the category of beings that Leland describes as "sorcerers who become giants at will," part of the complex hierarchy of spirit beings in Wabanaki cosmology (002-130).

## In the Glooskap Cycle

The Win-pe narrative demonstrates several key themes of Wabanaki mythology: the vulnerability of even divine families to supernatural threat, the hero's willingness to pursue justice across great distances, and the ultimate triumph of benevolent power over malevolent sorcery.
