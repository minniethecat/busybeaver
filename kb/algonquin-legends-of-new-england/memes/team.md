# Team

**Team** (the Moose) appears in Passamaquoddy oral tradition as the companion and housemate of Abistanooch (the Marten). His story—preserved in a single extended narrative spanning two chapters—turns on themes of generosity, betrayal, and supernatural justice.

## Identity and Attributes

Team is portrayed as industrious and good-natured. He "hunted and worked industriously" [020-005] alongside Abistanooch, the two young men sharing a dwelling by a brook. When Team kills a bear, the narrative emphasizes his skill as a provider:

> "Now it happened that one day Team, the Moose, had killed a bear" [020-011]

His character is defined by earnestness and moral seriousness. When wronged, "Team took this sadly to heart" [020-087] and "argued earnestly" [020-089] for fair treatment, marking him as a figure of dignity rather than cunning.

## Relationships

- **Abistanooch (the Marten)**: Team's housemate and hunting partner. Their shared dwelling by a brook [020-003] establishes the domestic setting from which the narrative unfolds.
- **The Mikumwess (forest spirits)**: Team journeys to the land of the Mikumwess to seek redress, connecting his story to the broader cosmology of Wabanaki fairy lore.

## Role in Narratives

### The Betrayal and Journey to the Mikumwess

After killing a bear, Team is betrayed by a companion who scalps him with a flint-headed arrow:

> "a flint-headed arrow through Team's scalp-lock; and this" [020-096]

The wound drives Team to seek justice among the Mikumwess, the small forest-dwelling spirits. This journey narrative links Team's personal grievance to the supernatural world, where the Mikumwess possess the power to heal and to punish.

### The Return

A later fragment records Team's return from the spirit realm. In the variant catalogued by Leland, Team "(Team), have added to it another" episode [041-064], suggesting his story circulated in multiple versions among Passamaquoddy storytellers.

## Cultural Context

The Moose (Algonquian-derived _mos_) figures prominently in Wabanaki subsistence and cosmology. Team's character embodies the ethical dimension of hunting culture—the provider who expects reciprocity and suffers when it is denied.
