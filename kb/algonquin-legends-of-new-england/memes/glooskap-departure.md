# Glooskap's Departure

## Narrative

When the ways of men and beasts grew evil, they greatly vexed Glooskap. In response, he made a great feast by the shore of Lake Minas, to which all the beasts came. After the feast, the animals departed, each going their own way, and never again met together in council (010-069). Before he left, Glooskap gave gifts to Men, bestowing final blessings upon humanity (010-066). All the animals mourned for Glooskap when he departed from the world (010-077).

## Participants

The feast was a gathering of all beasts — every species of animal came together in council for the last time. The event is notable for its universal scope: the entire animal kingdom, which Glooskap had named and ordered, assembled to bid farewell to their creator.

## Variants

Glooskap departed with a specific promise: he would return to restore the Golden Age (010-088). This eschatological dimension — the promise of a future restoration — gives the departure narrative a forward-looking character distinct from simple myths of loss.

## Cultural Significance

The Departure serves multiple functions: it explains why animals no longer gather in council, provides an origin for Glooskap's final gifts to humanity, and establishes a framework of hope — the world is not permanently abandoned but awaits the hero's return. This tripartite structure (Golden Age, Decline, Promised Restoration) places Wabanaki eschatology within patterns found in many world mythologies.
