# The Stream

A network of waterways — from mountain brooks to underground channels — that flow through [[The Princess and the Goblin|the mountain]], serving as practical escape routes, symbolic boundaries between worlds, and ultimately the instrument of the [[goblins|goblins]]' defeat (007-005, 020-007, 029-005).

## Above Ground

The mountain streams form part of the daylight world of the [[miners]]. After a storm, [[irene|Irene]] sees "great brown streams tumbling down everywhere" (006-004), one forming "a watery arch" over ferns (006-005). These streams are beautiful but treacherous — swollen by rain, they become obstacles that test [[curdie|Curdie]]'s resolve.

## Below Ground

Underground, a stream flows through the [[goblins|goblin]] tunnels. [[curdie|Curdie]] discovers its source:

> A little stream rushed out of a rock (007-005).

The goblins, during their tunneling, "had broken into the channel of a small stream" (013-006) — a detail that later becomes their undoing. The underground stream leads [[curdie|Curdie]] and [[irene|Irene]] to safety, "jabber[ing] and prattl[ing]" like a living guide (020-007).

## Curdie and the Water

[[curdie|Curdie]]'s connection to water marks him as a child of [[courage-and-faith|faith]] and nature. He follows the stream's voice through darkness:

> He knew by the sound of the water that he was near the mouth of the mine (007-005).

Later, it is through water — the flooding of goblin tunnels — that the [[miners|miners]] win their final victory.

## The Dangerous Crossing

At the climax, two "roaring and dangerous streams" block [[curdie|Curdie]]'s path to [[king-papa|the king]] (029-005). The storm-swollen waters nearly prevent him from delivering his warning, testing his physical courage to its limit.

## Significance

Water in MacDonald's novel is the element of life, purity, and divine justice. It flows where it will — above ground in beauty, below ground in mystery — and ultimately becomes the agent of the goblins' defeat. The streams that once tested [[curdie|Curdie]]'s courage become the means of victory, cleansing the mountain of its ancient corruption.
