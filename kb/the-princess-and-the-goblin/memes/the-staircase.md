# The Staircase

The staircase is an ancient oak stairway in the [[the-palace|king's house]] that connects the lower domestic quarters to the upper regions of the house — most importantly, to the turret chamber of [[light|the grandmother]]. It is the novel's primary architectural symbol of the path from the ordinary to the transcendent.

## Description

The staircase is first introduced as a mysterious, ancient feature of the house:

> which opened at the foot of a curious old stair of worm-eaten oak, which looked as if it had never been used (002-004)

Its "worm-eaten" age and apparent disuse suggest it belongs to an older, half-forgotten part of the house — a passage to realms beyond ordinary domestic life. When [[king-papa|Irene]] first gets lost, she searches desperately for:

> the stairs and her safe nursery (002-005)

But the staircase she needs — the one leading up to [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]] — is not the safe passage back to [[lootie|the nurse]]ry but the uncertain way upward.

## The Ascent to the Grandmother

The staircase serves as the literal pathway to [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]. [[irene|Irene]]'s journey up it is described in detail:

> she came to the foot of the old staircase there was the moon shining in (011-004)

> As she went slowly up the stair, not quite sure that she was not dreaming (011-005)

> So up and up she went, stair after stair, until she came to the top (011-006)

The ascension is long and gradual — stair after stair — mirro[[the-ring|ring]] the progressive nature of spiritual growth and the gradual deepening of [[irene|Irene]]'s relationship with [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]].

## Narrative Role

The staircase functions on multiple symbolic levels:

1. **Physical passage**: It is the literal route between [[lootie|Irene's nurse]]ry and [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s chamber
2. **Spiritual symbol**: The upward journey represents spiritual ascent — leaving the domestic/ordinary for the divine/magical
3. **Threshold**: The staircase occupies a liminal space between two realms, neither fully in the mundane world nor in [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s magical domain
4. **Persistent mystery**: [[irene|Irene]] cannot always find the staircase (005-011), and others in the house seem unaware of its full extent — it appears only to those who seek it
