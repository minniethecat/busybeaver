# The King's Palace

The great palace of [[king-papa|the king]], built on the slopes of the [[the-mountain|mountain]] and home to [[irene|Princess Irene]]. It stands as the seat of human authority and civilization, perched above [[goblins|the goblins]]' subterranean domain. The palace is both a literal building and a symbol of the sunlit, ordered world that [[goblins|the goblins]] seek to destroy (001-002).

## Location and Layout

The palace is situated on one of the [[the-mountain|mountain]]'s slopes, with a commanding view of the surrounding country of [[the-mountain|mountain]]s and valleys (001-002). Within the palace are:

- The [[irene|princess]]'s [[lootie|nurse]]ry and living quarters
- The [[the-hall|great hall]] where [[king-papa|the king]] holds court
- Servants' quarters (including [[lootie|Lootie]], [[lootie|Irene's nurse]])
- A mysterious, largely forgotten tower at the top — where [[irene|Irene]]'s [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|great-great-grandmother]] dwells

## The Tower

The most significant feature of the palace is its ancient tower, which most inhabitants have forgotten or dismiss as uninhabited. At the top of a long, winding stair, [[irene|Irene]] discovers her [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|great-great-grandmother]]'s chamber:

> "When she came to the top, she found herself in a little square place, with three doors." (003-002)

The tower is both part of the palace and separate from it — physically connected but spiritually apart, accessible only to those who believe. This dual nature mirrors [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]] herself, who is both within and beyond the ordinary world.

## The Courtyard and Surroundings

Outside the palace walls are the courtyard, gardens, and open slopes of the [[the-mountain|mountain]]. It is in the courtyard that [[irene|Irene]] first encounters [[goblins|the goblins]]' creatures and is chased back to safety (014-008). The palace grounds are also where [[curdie|Curdie]] is later posted as a guard, connecting the world of the [[the-mines|mines]] to the royal household.

## Significance

The palace represents the human world — ordered, hierarchical, and sunlit — in contrast to [[goblins|the goblins]]' chaotic underground. Yet the palace also contains within it the spiritual realm ([[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s tower) that most of its inhabitants cannot see. This echoes MacDonald's theme that the divine is present in ordinary life but requires [[courage-and-faith|faith]] to perceive.

The palace's vulnerability — its position above the [[goblins|goblin]] tunnels — underscores that surface civilization is never truly safe from the [[darkness]] beneath. Only vigilance, [[courage-and-faith|courage]], and [[courage-and-faith|faith]] keep the palace secure.
