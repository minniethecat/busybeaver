# The Princess and the Goblin

**The Princess and the Goblin** is a children's fantasy novel by Scottish author **[[George MacDonald]]**, first published in 1872. It is one of the foundational works of modern fantasy literature, influencing authors such as C.S. Lewis and J.R.R. Tolkien.

## Plot Summary

The novel follows **[[irene|Princess Irene]]**, a young princess living in a mountain kingdom, who discovers a mysterious great-great-grandmother ([[irenes-great-great-grandmother]]) living in a hidden tower. With the help of a magical [[the-magical-thread|thread]] and a young miner named **[[curdie]]**, Irene confronts the [[goblins]] who plot against the kingdom from their underground realm.

## Themes

The novel explores themes of **faith and trust** (trusting what one cannot see), **courage in the face of darkness**, and the **power of guidance and inheritance** across generations.

## Publication and Legacy

First published in 1872 by Strahan & Co., the novel was followed by a sequel, *The Princess and Curdie* (1883). C.S. Lewis cited MacDonald's works as formative influences, and the novel remains in print and widely read today.
