# The Underground

The underground is the subterranean realm beneath the [[the-mountain|mountain]], home to the [[goblins]] and the setting for much of the novel's adventure. It represents a dark mirror of the human world above — a domain of [[darkness]], danger, and [[goblins|goblin]] civilization that lies just beneath the surface of ordinary life.

## Geography

The underground is a complex network of natural and [[goblins|goblin]]-made passages:

> a small irregular shaped opening in the rock, new in his experience of the underground regions (018-003)

It is characterized by oppressive warmth:

> it is very warm underground (007-006)

The underground contains vast [[the-cave|cavern]]s, including [[goblins|the goblins]]' [[the-hall|great hall]] with its natural [[water]] reservoir (009-006), and is [[the-magical-thread|thread]]ed with streams and passages that [[goblins|the goblins]] have engineered into a flood system.

The boundary between the human [[the-mines|mines]] and the [[goblins|goblin]] underground is porous — [[goblins|goblin]]s and miners work in overlapping territory, creating the central conflict of the novel.

## The Underground as Goblin Realm

The [[goblins|goblin]]s have shaped the underground into their [[king-papa|king]]dom. They have:

- Houses and family dwellings carved from rock (018-003)
- A great [[the-hall|palace hall]] with a raised throne platform (021-051)
- Communication tunnels between different [[goblins|goblin]] settlements (008-061–062)
- Engineered [[water]]ways for their flood weapon (009-023, 031-018)

The underground's defining feature is its total [[darkness]] (019-004), which has shaped [[goblins|goblin]] biology, psychology, and culture.

## Narrative Role

The underground functions as:
1. **The shadow realm**: A dark parallel to the sunlit human world above, embodying all that is hidden, [[fear]]ed, and dangerous
2. **The testing ground**: [[irene|Irene]] and [[curdie|Curdie]] prove their [[courage-and-faith|courage]] by descending into and traversing the underground
3. **The [[goblins|goblin]]s' home**: It gives [[goblins|the goblins]] cultural and biological coherence, ma[[king-papa|king]] them more than mere monsters
