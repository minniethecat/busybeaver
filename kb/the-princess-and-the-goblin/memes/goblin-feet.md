# Goblin Feet

Goblin feet are the distinctive anatomical feature and great vulnerability of the [[goblins]] in "The Princess and the Goblin." Soft, broad, and toeless, [[goblins|goblin]] feet are a source of profound shame to [[goblins|the goblins]], who hide them with stone shoes — a weakness that [[peter|Curdie]] exploits as the decisive combat tactic against them.

## Nature / Definition

Goblin feet are first revealed through a [[goblins|goblin]] child's conversation with his family in Chapter 8. The [[goblins|goblin]] admits:

> "It's a goblin weakness. Why they come so soft, I can't think." (008-013)

The feet are described as grotesquely soft and vulnerable:

> "the ends of her feet were wonderfully broad and could spread themselves out like a fan" (008-037)

The [[goblins|goblin]]s cannot bear even to look at their own feet:

> "they can't bear the sight of their own feet without them [shoes]" (008-039)

## Cultural Significance

The [[goblins|goblin]]s' relationship to their feet shapes their entire culture:

1. **Stone shoes**: All [[goblins|goblin]]s above a certain rank wear shoes made of stone to hide their feet:

   > "The goblin's glory is his head. To think of the feet would spoil it all." (008-015)

2. **Royal distinction**: The queen's shoes are a mark of royalty (008-018–019), setting her apart from barefoot common [[goblins|goblin]]s.

3. **Shame and denial**: The [[goblins|goblin]]s have structured their society around denying this vulnerability. They celebrate their heads (which are hard) while hiding their feet (which are soft).

## The Combat Weakness

[[curdie|Curdie]] discovers that [[goblins|goblin]] feet are their fatal weakness in battle. He learns this through his encounter with the [[goblins|goblin]] family (Ch. 8), and it becomes his signature combat tactic:

> "Stamp on their feet!" he cried as each man rose (027-013)

> "Stamp on their feet; stamp on their feet!" he shouted, and the men responded (027-019)

This tactic proves devastatingly effective:

> many more goblin feet had to go limping back on painful errands (027-020)

The human defenders' awareness of this weakness turns [[goblins|the goblins]]' greatest source of shame into their undoing.

## The Queen's Fatal Feet

The [[goblin-queen|Goblin Queen]]'s feet become her doom. When the flood overtakes [[goblins|the goblins]]:

> they found a multitude of dead goblins — among the rest the queen, with her feet crushed by her own stone shoes (032-004)

Her stone shoes — meant to hide her vulnerability — become the instrument of her death.

## Narrative Role

Goblin feet serve as:

1. **Worldbuilding detail**: They make [[goblins|the goblins]] a unique fantasy species with a coherent biological and cultural identity.

2. **Plot mechanism**: [[curdie|Curdie]]'s knowledge of the foot-weakness is the key tactical insight that defeats the [[goblins|goblin]] invasion.

3. **Thematic symbol**: The [[goblins|goblin]]s' attempt to hide their shame (feet) rather than accept or overcome it mirrors the moral failure of pride. Their vulnerability, when hidden, becomes fatal.
