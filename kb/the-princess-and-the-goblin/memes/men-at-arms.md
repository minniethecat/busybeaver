# Men-at-Arms

The men-at-arms are the household guard left by the [[king-papa|King]] to protect the [[the-palace|palace]] and its inhabitants in his absence. They appear throughout "The Princess and the Goblin" as a collective character — a body of loyal soldiers who defend against [[goblins|goblin]] threats and ultimately participate in the climactic battle.

## Role in the Story

The men-at-arms were stationed at [[the-palace|the palace]] by [[king-papa|the king]] specifically for protection:

> the room where the men-at-arms whom the king had left sat (011-002)

They maintain constant vigilance, particularly at night:

> The men-at-arms kept watching about the house, and the king's servants were on the watch (025-002)

> nothing except the tread of the men-at-arms as they marched about (025-006)

When the [[goblins|goblin]] threat escalates, their vigilance intensifies:

> The men-at-arms redoubled their vigilance (016-011)

## Appearance and Traits

As a collective, the men-at-arms are defined by their discipline and loyalty. They march on regular rounds, maintain watch, and respond to threats with coordinated action. Their captain is a distinct figure:

> the captain of the gentlemen-at-arms, walking with his great strides (024-023)

The men-at-arms identify themselves by their service:

> "We are the king's own men-at-arms," said the captain courteously (025-017)

## Combat Against the Goblins

The men-at-arms play a crucial role in the [[goblins|goblin]] war. They fight the [[goblins|goblin]] raiding parties:

> the last of the men-at-arms, the captain himself, was on the point of being overcome (027-010)

They follow [[curdie|Curdie]]'s tactical direction to stamp on [[goblins|goblin]] feet (027-013, 027-019–020). After the battle, they join [[curdie|Curdie]] in pursuit:

> Curdie gathered the men-at-arms, and took them over the garden wall (031-023)

## Key Relationships

- [[king-papa|The King]] — their commander, who stationed them at [[the-palace|the palace]]
- [[peter|Curdie]] — they fight alongside him against [[goblins|the goblins]]
- [[goblins]] — their enemy

## Significance

The men-at-arms represent the institutional human response to the [[goblins|goblin]] threat — organized, disciplined, loyal. They contrast with [[curdie|Curdie]]'s solo heroism, showing that the human world has resources beyond individual [[courage-and-faith|courage]]. Their presence makes [[the-palace|the palace]] a defended fortress rather than a vulnerable household.
