A luminous silver globe that hangs in the grandmother's tower room, serving as both a source of light and a sign of her protective presence (014-009, 031-025). Unlike the lamp that never goes out, the globe descends and moves — a mobile manifestation of the grandmother's power.

## Description

The globe is first seen hanging in the grandmother's tower room: "a great silver globe was hanging in the air" (014-009). It is not suspended by any visible means — it floats, "and as everybody knows, nothing can float in the air" (014-009), marking it as supernatural. The globe radiates a silvery light described as "a large globe of silvery light" (023-025) that descends from above.

The light is gentle, not harsh — Irene gazes "at the beautiful silvery globe until she fell asleep" (029-014). It is both a lamp and a comfort, a presence that watches over her.

## Narrative Appearances

The globe appears at pivotal moments:

| Appearance | Context | PN |
|-----------|---------|-----|
| Tower room | Hanging in the air, marking the grandmother's domain | 014-009 |
| Curdie's journey | Descends from above, guiding | 023-025 |
| Irene's sleep | She gazes at it until falling asleep | 029-014 |
| Climax | Above the king's house, shining like purest light | 031-025 |
| Final chapter | Both turn to the globe before it vanishes | 031-029, 032-002 |

At the climactic moment, "a great globe of light shining like the purest light" appears above the king's house (031-025), and "both turned their eyes to the globe of light" (031-029). The globe's final disappearance — "could see nothing of the silvery globe" (032-002) — marks the grandmother's withdrawal as the story concludes.

## Significance

The silver globe represents the grandmother's mobile, active presence — distinct from the stationary lamp. Where the lamp is a fixed beacon, the globe descends, moves, and responds to events. Its silvery light is associated with moonlight and the grandmother's realm, contrasting with the red glow of the goblins' fires underground.

The globe's appearances trace the story's arc: introduction (014-009), guidance through darkness (023-025), comfort in rest (029-014), triumph (031-025), and farewell (032-002).
