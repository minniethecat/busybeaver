# The Goblin King

[[the-goblin-king|The Goblin King]] is the ruler of the [[goblins]]' subterranean [[king-papa|king]]dom and the primary antagonist who orchestrates the [[the-goblins-plot|flood plot]] against the human inhabitants of the [[the-mountain|mountain]] in "The Princess and the Goblin."

## Identity and Status

[[the-goblin-king|The Goblin King]] presides over the great underground [[the-hall|hall]]:

> lay a huge natural reservoir of water, now the great palace hall of the goblins (009-006)

He is recognized by his royal bea[[the-ring|ring]] and the deference of his subjects. Du[[the-ring|ring]] a council meeting, [[curdie|Curdie]] observes:

> But the king uttered a strange shout, half of command and half of exultation (018-034)

The [[king-papa|king]] is paired with the [[goblin-queen|Goblin Queen]], and together they sit on a raised platform:

> a platform of rock raised a few feet from the floor and covered with rich skins (021-051)

## Leadership and Schemes

[[the-goblin-king|The Goblin King]]'s defining trait is his strategic ambition. He addresses his subjects with grand plans:

> "all to the advantage of the goblin kingdom, will be established forever" (009-018)

His scheme involves manipulating the underground [[water]] reservoirs:

> the latter plan of the goblins was to inundate the mine by breaking outlets for the water (009-023)

He rules through a combination of [[fear]] and tribal loyalty. The [[goblins|goblin]]s' previous queen ([[king-papa|the king]]'s first wife) came "from upstairs" (a human woman), which explains certain [[goblins|goblin]] customs:

> "The first queen, you see — I mean the king's first wife — wore shoes" (008-019)

This detail, revealed through a [[goblins|goblin]] family's conversation, hints at a deeper backstory of human-[[goblins|goblin]] intermarriage that shaped [[goblins|goblin]] culture.

## Narrative Role

[[the-goblin-king|The Goblin King]] functions as:
1. **Antagonist**: The primary agent of evil threatening the human characters
2. **Foil to the human [[king-papa|king]]**: While [[king-papa|Irene's father]] is a benevolent, protective ruler, the Goblin King is selfish and destructive
3. **Worldbuilding anchor**: His court, [[the-palace|palace]], and schemes flesh out the [[goblins|goblin]] civilization
