# The Tunnel

A goblin-dug passage that breaches the boundary between the underground goblin realm and the human world above. Unlike natural caves and passages, the tunnel is an act of will — deliberately engineered by [[goblins|the goblins]] to reach [[king-papa|the king]]'s house for invasion (013-006, 031-018).

## Location

The tunnel runs from the [[goblins|goblins]]' territory in the [[the-cave|caves]] upward toward [[king-papa|the king]]'s house. During excavation, the goblins "had broken into the channel of a small stream" (013-006), showing the tunnel intersected with natural waterways — a detail [[curdie|Curdie]] later exploits for navigation.

The goblins had specifically "made [the tunnel] to the [[king-papa|king]]'s house" (031-018), confirming it was not a random excavation but a targeted invasion route.

## Construction

The goblins' tunneling is described as an industrial-scale operation. After the invasion fails, the surviving goblins resume work:

> Setting all hands to the work, tunnelling here and building there (032-004).

This suggests a network of tunnels — not a single passage but a complex underground infrastructure. The goblins' engineering capability, while grotesque, is formidable. They bore through solid rock with persistence and organization.

## Curdie's Discovery

[[curdie|Curdie]] discovers the tunnel's purpose through underground listening and tracking. His knowledge of [[mining]] and his familiarity with the mountain's geology allow him to trace the goblins' path and warn [[king-papa|the king]] before the invasion succeeds.

The tunnel's intersection with the stream channel (013-006) becomes a crucial vulnerability — it is through water that [[curdie|Curdie]] and [[peter|the miners]] ultimately defeat the goblin invasion, flooding the very tunnels the goblins dug.

## Significance

The tunnel is the most concrete representation of the goblin threat. Unlike the vague menace of [[goblins|goblin]] songs or the distant danger of [[the-cave|cave]] encounters, the tunnel is a physical structure reaching upward — inch by inch — toward [[irene|the princess]] and [[king-papa|her father]]. It represents deliberate malice, planned and executed with persistence.

The ultimate irony is that the goblins' engineering becomes their undoing. The same tunnel that was to bring them victory becomes the channel through which [[miners|the miners]] flood their realm.
