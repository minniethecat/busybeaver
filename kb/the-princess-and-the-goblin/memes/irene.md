# Princess Irene

The protagonist of the novel, a young princess living in her father's [[the-mountain|mountain]] [[the-palace|palace]]. Irene begins as a sheltered child but develops into a [[courage-and-faith|courage]]ous heroine through her relationship with her mysterious [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|great-great-grandmother]] and her adventures with [[goblins|the goblins]] (001-001, 003-002).

## Role in the Story

Irene is the central character whose growth drives the narrative. At the story's opening, she is a lonely child whose mother is absent and whose father [[king-papa|the king]] is often away. Her discovery of [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]] in [[the-grandmothers-tower|the tower]] and the magical [[the-magical-thread|thread]] transforms her from a passive, frightened princess into an active rescuer.

Her defining journey is following the [[the-magical-thread|invisible thread]] through [[darkness]] to rescue [[curdie|Curdie]] from [[goblins|the goblins]]' prison (020-002–021-002), a feat that requires total [[courage-and-faith|faith]] in guidance she cannot see. She later faces the [[goblins|goblin]] horde and enters the [[the-hall|great hall]] before [[king-papa|the king]] and his court, following [[the-magical-thread|the thread]] to her father's side (031-002).

## Appearance and Traits

Irene is a "real princess" — MacDonald testifies to this by how she obeys [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s instruction immediately and without fuss (003-006). She has blue eyes with "two melted stars in them" (003-008). She is curious, persistent, and emotionally honest — she cries when hurt but also shows remarkable determination when following [[the-magical-thread|the thread]].

Her key trait is her capacity for [[courage-and-faith|faith]]: she believes in what she experiences even when others, including [[curdie|Curdie]] and her own [[lootie|nurse]], dismiss her account of [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]] as fantasy (004-039).

## Key Relationships

- [[irenes-great-great-grandmother]] — Irene's spiritual guide and protector; the relationship is one of loving [[trust]]
- [[curdie|Curdie]] — initially skeptical of Irene's stories, he comes to believe in [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]] and becomes her ally and rescuer
- [[lootie|Lootie]] — [[lootie|Irene's nurse]], who disbelieves Irene's account of [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]] and represents worldly skepticism
- [[king-papa|The King]] — [[king-papa|Irene's father]], who returns at the novel's end and witnesses the truth of [[the-magical-thread|the thread]] (031-002)

## Significance and Themes

Irene embodies the novel's central spiritual message: that [[courage-and-faith|faith]] — [[trust]]ing in guidance beyond one's understanding — is the truest form of [[courage-and-faith|courage]]. She is not physically strong, quick-witted, or worldly-wise. Her virtue is her willingness to believe and to act on that [[courage-and-faith|belief]], following a [[the-magical-thread|thread]] she cannot see.

Her name (derived from the Greek "eirene," meaning peace) suggests her role as a bringer of reconciliation — between the surface and underground worlds, between [[courage-and-faith|faith]] and doubt, between the seen and the unseen.
