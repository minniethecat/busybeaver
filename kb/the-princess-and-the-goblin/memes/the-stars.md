# The Stars

The stars are celestial bodies that appear in the novel primarily as decorative motifs in the [[light|grandmother]]'s chambers and as part of the night sky, symbolizing divine order, beauty, and [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s celestial nature.

## Nature / Definition

The stars in "The Princess and the Goblin" appear in two main contexts: the actual stars in the night sky visible from the [[the-mountain|mountain]], and the silver stars adorning the walls and ceiling of [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s turret chamber. The [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]]'s room is described as having blue walls covered with silver stars:

> All about the walls, as high as she could see, were what looked like stars of silver (011-040)

[[irene|Irene]] specifically identifies the sky, along with the moon and stars, as what [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s ceiling looks like:

> "The sky, and the moon and the stars," she answered. "It looked as if you couldn't see one separate from the rest any more." (015-028)

The stars in [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s chamber are not mere decoration — they are alive with motion and depth:

> Now she looked at the rose fire, now at the starry walls, now at the silver light (015-039)

> she looked straight into the starry herds, flashing gloriously in the dark blue (015-040)

## Symbolic Role

The stars serve as a symbol of [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s celestial and divine nature. Her chamber mirrors the heavens themselves, suggesting she is a being who dwells beyond the ordinary world. When [[irene|Irene]] first sees the chamber:

> The princess found herself in a room with blue walls and silver stars, and no end of things in it (023-015)

The completeness of the starry sky in [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s room is emphasized:

> There was the sky all out in full-blown stars — not one missing (011-002)

This perfection — not one star missing — suggests [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s complete knowledge and providential care, contrasting with the imperfect, sometimes cloud-obscured sky of the ordinary world:

> She could see nothing. Not a single star was out. (014-009)
