# Goblin Songs

Goblin [[songs]] are the grotesque [[singing-as-protection|rhymes]] and chants of the [[goblins]], appea[[the-ring|ring]] throughout "The Princess and the Goblin" as Windows into [[goblins|goblin]] psychology and culture. They are countered by [[peter|Curdie]]'s own [[singing-as-protection|singing]], which serves as a weapon and protective charm against the [[goblins|goblin]] folk.

## Goblin Rhymes

The [[goblins|goblin]]s' [[songs]] are characterized by crude humor, violence, and self-mockery. The most prominent example is the rhyme [[curdie|Curdie]] hears:

> "Gobble! gobble! goblin! There you go a wobblin'; Hobble, hobble, hobblin' —" (006-028)

This rhyme reveals several features of [[goblins|goblin]] song-culture:
1. **Onomatopoeia**: "Gobble gobble" mimics the sound of their name, suggesting their identity is inseparable from crude noise
2. **Self-deprecation**: "Wobblin'" and "hobblin'" mock their own awkward gait — a reference to their [[goblin-feet|vulnerable feet]]
3. **Rhythmic simplicity**: The [[songs]] are simple chants, not sophisticated poetry

## Songs as Cultural Expression

Goblin [[songs]] serve as vehicles for their grievances and aspirations. The [[goblins|goblin]]s' song references [[lootie|the nurse]]ry rhyme form — a corrupted version of children's verse:

> "Nine, ten, eleven, twelve — Loosely tied the shoe" (006-026–028, paraphrased)

This echoes a counting rhyme but twists it toward [[goblins|the goblins]]' obsession with shoes and feet. The [[goblins|goblin]]s appropriate human cultural forms (rhyme, counting [[songs]]) and degrade them, just as they appropriate human children.

## Curdie's Counter-Singing

In direct opposition to [[goblins|goblin]] [[songs]] stands [[curdie|Curdie]]'s [[singing-as-protection|protective singing]]. Where [[goblins|goblin]] [[songs]] are crude and moc[[king-papa|king]], [[curdie|Curdie]]'s [[songs]] are brave and defiant. His [[singing-as-protection|singing]] drives [[goblins|the goblins]] back:

> the goblins fell back a little when he began singing (018-037, paraphrased)

The juxtaposition of [[goblins|goblin]] and human song forms a minor motif: music can degrade or elevate, mock or protect.

## Narrative Role

Goblin [[songs]] serve multiple functions:
1. **Worldbuilding**: They reveal [[goblins|goblin]] psychology — crude, self-conscious, obsessed with their physical deformity
2. **Atmosphere**: The snatches of [[goblins|goblin]] song [[curdie|Curdie]] overhears underground create an eerie, folkloric texture
3. **Thematic contrast**: Goblin [[songs]] vs. [[curdie|Curdie]]'s [[songs]] = corruption vs. [[courage-and-faith|courage]], [[darkness]] vs. light
