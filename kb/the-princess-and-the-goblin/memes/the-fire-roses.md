# The Fire Roses

The fire roses are supernatural flames in the form of glowing red and white roses that burn in the hearth of [[irene|Irene]]'s [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|great-great-grandmother]]'s [[the-grandmothers-tower|tower chamber]]. They provide not only warmth and light but possess healing and restorative properties (015-007, 022-004).

## Appearance

The fire roses are first revealed to [[irene|Irene]] du[[the-ring|ring]] her second visit to her [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]]'s [[the-grandmothers-tower|tower room]]. She initially mistakes them for a bouquet:

> "She saw that what she had taken for a huge bouquet of red roses on a low stand against the wall was in fact a fire which burned in the shapes of the loveliest and reddest roses, glowing gorgeously between the heads and wings of two cherubs of shining silver." (015-007)

The room is filled with the scent of roses from the fire (015-007). In a later visit, white roses appear mingled with the red ones (022-004).

## Properties and Use

The fire roses have multiple magical functions:

- They provide **light** — the room is illuminated by their glow (015-007)
- They provide **warmth** — [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]] lights the fire for [[irene|Irene]] when she arrives cold and wet (015-006)
- They can be **handled** — [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]] stoops to the fire and takes a rose from it with her hand, unharmed (015-018)
- They have **restorative/healing power** — [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]] bathes [[irene|Irene]] in the fire, which cleanses her from [[goblins|goblin]] contamination (022-004)

The bath in the fire roses is a pivotal moment. After [[irene|Irene]] has been touched by [[goblins|the goblins]]' creatures, she feels contaminated. The [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]] tells her:

> "I am going to put you in the bath. Don't be frightened." (022-017)

The fire cleanses and restores [[irene|Irene]], removing not just physical dirt but the symbolic contamination of the [[goblins|goblin]] world.

## Significance

The fire roses embody [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s supernatural benevolence. Fire that does not burn but heals; roses that give both warmth and fragrance — these paradoxes reflect the novel's Christian allegorical underpinnings, where divine love is both purifying fire and gentle comfort. The bath in the fire of roses is a baptismal moment for [[irene|Irene]], cleansing her of [[goblins|goblin]] influence and renewing her [[courage-and-faith|courage]].

The white roses that appear among the red (022-004) may suggest increasing purity or a deeper level of [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s power being revealed as [[irene|Irene]]'s [[courage-and-faith|faith]] grows.
