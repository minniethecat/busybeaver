# Water

Water is a recur[[the-ring|ring]] natural element in "The Princess and the Goblin" that appears as rain, streams, underground reservoirs, and ultimately as a destructive flood weapon deployed by the [[goblins]] against the [[the-palace|king's palace]].

## Nature / Definition

Water pervades the novel in multiple forms. The [[the-mountain|mountain]] setting is described with water running through its passages from the very first chapter:

> and winding ways, some with water running through them, and some without (001-004)

The underground realm of [[goblins|the goblins]] contains a massive natural reservoir:

> lay a huge natural reservoir of water, now the great palace hall of the goblin nation (009-006)

A tiny stream runs through the [[goblins|goblin]] territory:

> a place where a tiny stream of water was running down the face of the rock (009-022)

The [[goblins|goblin]]s design their architecture around this water source:

> by breaking outlets for the water accumulated in the natural reservoirs (009-023)

## The Goblins' Flood Plot

The [[the-goblins-plot|goblins' plan]] to flood the [[the-mines|mines]] and destroy [[king-papa|the king]]'s house is a central plot element driven by water:

> for came a huge rush of turbid water, and almost swept them away (031-014)

The [[goblins|goblin]]s had engineered this flood as a weapon, expecting:

> expecting the water to run down into the mine, where they had hoped to drown Curdie and his party (031-018)

The flood instead endangers [[goblins|the goblins]]' own territory:

> the water was rising fast around them, spreading terror among the goblins (031-023)

## Narrative Role

Water operates on three levels in the novel:

1. **Setting/worldbuilding**: Underground streams and reservoirs define [[goblins|the goblins]]' subterranean domain and create atmosphere du[[the-ring|ring]] [[irene|Irene]]'s and [[curdie|Curdie]]'s underground journeys.

2. **Plot mechanism**: The [[goblins|goblin]]s' reservoir becomes their weapon of mass destruction. Their plan to drown the human miners backfires when the flood turns against them.

3. **Symbolic purification**: [[irene|Irene]]'s bath and submersion in [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s chamber (022-059: "she sank in the clear cool water") suggests cleansing and renewal.
