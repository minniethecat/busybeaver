# The Ring

A magical ring bestowed upon [[irene|Princess Irene]] by her mysterious [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|great-great-grandmother]]. It serves as the anchor point for [[the-magical-thread|the invisible thread]] that guides [[irene|Irene]] through [[darkness]] and danger, and is a key symbol of the bond between [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]] and child in the novel.

## Origin

The ring is given to [[irene|Irene]] by her [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|great-great-grandmother]] during a secret visit to [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s [[the-grandmothers-tower|tower chamber]]. The [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]] herself places it on [[irene|Irene]]'s finger:

> Irene held out her hand, and the old lady put the ring on the forefinger of it (015-048).

[[irene|Irene]] has possessed the ring for as long as she can remember, though she only learns its true purpose when [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]] reveals [[the-magical-thread|the thread]]. When [[lootie|Lootie]] questions where [[irene|Irene]] got the ring, [[irene|Irene]] responds:

> "Who gave me the ring, Lootie? I know I've had it a long time, but I don't remember who gave it to me" (016-013).

This suggests [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]] has watched over [[irene|Irene]] since her earliest childhood, long before [[irene|Irene]] became consciously aware of her presence.

## Properties

The ring's primary magical property is that it serves as the attachment point for [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s [[the-magical-thread|invisible thread]]. The [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]] explains:

> "I've fastened the end of it to the ring on your finger" (015-071).

The [[the-magical-thread|thread]] is invisible to all except the bearer of the ring. By keeping a finger on [[the-magical-thread|the thread]], [[irene|Irene]] can feel its guidance even in total [[darkness]]. The [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]] instructs her:

> "You must lay your finger, the same that wore the ring, upon the thread, and follow the thread wherever it leads you" (015-078).

The ring thus functions as both a physical anchor and a spiritual link — it connects [[irene|Irene]] to her [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]] across space and time, and symbolizes the unbreakable bond of love and [[trust]] between them.

The ring appears at several pivotal moments, often in conjunction with [[the-magical-thread|the thread]]:

When [[king-papa|the king]] sees the ring on [[irene|Irene]]'s finger, it serves as tangible evidence of [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s reality — something even the skeptical adults cannot easily dismiss. The ring thus bridges the novel's central tension between visible and invisible truth.

- [[the-goblin-king|The Goblin King]] — covets a sun-woman, contrasting with [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s freely given gifts

## Significance

The ring embodies the novel's central theme of [[courage-and-faith|faith]] made tangible. Unlike [[the-magical-thread|the thread]] — which is invisible and must be [[trust]]ed — the ring is solid, visible, and physical. It provides a bridge between the seen and unseen worlds, offering those who cannot perceive [[the-magical-thread|the thread]] (like [[king-papa|the king]]) a point of contact with [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s reality. The ring is a token of belonging: [[irene|Irene]] is marked as [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s own, under her protection and guidance, from her earliest days.
