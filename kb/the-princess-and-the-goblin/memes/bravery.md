# Bravery

Bravery is a virtue embodied by multiple characters in "The Princess and the Goblin," particularly [[peter|Curdie]] and [[king-papa|Irene]]. It is depicted not as the absence of [[fear]] but as the willingness to act despite it.

## Nature / Definition

[[irene|Irene]] is introduced early as possessing innate bravery:

> she was as brave as could be expected of a princess of her age (002-007)

This characterization — bravery appropriate to her age and station — establishes that bravery in the novel is developmental: [[irene|Irene]] will grow into greater [[courage-and-faith|courage]] as the story progresses.

[[curdie|Curdie]]'s bravery is more explicit and active. The [[king-papa|king]]'s servants acknowledge it:

> "He's a good boy, and a brave boy, and he has been very kind to the princess." (006-099)

The [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]] later confirms this:

> "He is a good boy, Curdie, and a brave boy. Aren't you glad you've got him for a friend?" (022-007)

## Bravery vs. Foolhardiness

The novel distinguishes true bravery from recklessness. The [[the-goblin-king|goblin king]]'s sarcastic reference reveals this distinction:

> "when our brave men have succeeded in carrying off a boy or a girl" (019-019)

Here "brave" is used ironically — [[goblins|the goblins]]' idea of bravery (abducting children) is actually cowardice. True bravery in the novel involves protecting the vulnerable, not preying on them.

## Curdie as the Brave Miner

[[curdie|Curdie]]'s bravery is tied to his identity as a miner. Wor[[king-papa|king]] underground alone requires [[courage-and-faith|courage]]. His willingness to spy on [[goblins|the goblins]], fight them with his pickaxe and [[songs]], and ultimately lead the defense of [[king-papa|the king]]'s house all demonstrate practical [[courage-and-faith|courage]] — bravery expressed through action rather than declaration.

[[irene|Irene]]'s bravery takes a different but complementary form — the [[courage-and-faith|courage]] to [[trust]] the [[the-magical-thread|invisible thread]] and follow it through total [[darkness]].
