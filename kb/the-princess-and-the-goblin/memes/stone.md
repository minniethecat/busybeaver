# Stone

A fundamental element of the novel's underground world, appearing as landscape features in the mines, weapons in goblin warfare, ornaments in goblin culture, and a magical gemstone connecting Irene to her [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|great-great-grandmother]].

## Nature / Definition

Stones are the substance of the [[the-mountain|mountain]] itself — both the [[the-mines|mines]] where the miners work and the goblins' underground realm are carved from and among stone (007-002). The novel distinguishes between ordinary stones (loose rocks, boulders), shining mineral stones, and the singular magical stone in [[irene|Irene]]'s ring, which shares its substance with [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s [[the-grandmothers-lamp|fire]]-opal crown (015-054).

## Properties

Stones in the novel have several distinctive roles:

- **Loose and movable**: Curdie finds that stones near the goblins' tunnels are loose enough to be drawn out from the wall (008-062); he uses this discovery to create an opening into the goblin passages (008-068)
- **Flashing and shining**: Mineral deposits in the caves and on the mountainside catch the light — Curdie watches stones flashing in the sun while working outside (010-004), and the [[goblins|goblin]] palace walls are full of shining minerals (018-004)
- **The ring stone**: The stone in Irene's ring is of the same substance as the fire-opal in the grandmother's crown (015-054), linking the ring to the grandmother's power

## Narrative Role

Stones serve multiple plot functions across the novel:

| Chapter | Context | PN reference |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| ch-06 | Curdie picks up a shining stone while working on the mountain | (006-005) |
| ch-08 | Curdie removes loose stones from the passage wall to access goblin territory | (008-062–008-068) |
| ch-09 | The goblins fill the opening with stones and clay to seal it | (009-024) |
| ch-10 | Curdie sees stones flashing in the sunlight from the mountain | (010-004) |
| ch-15 | The grandmother reveals the stone in Irene's ring matches her crown | (015-054) |
| ch-18 | Goblins wear stones as ornaments and hurl them as weapons at Curdie | (018-004, 018-020) |
| ch-19 | Curdie confronts a goblin standing before a heap of stones blocking a passage | (019-023) |

Stones function as both barrier and passage — Curdie removes them to enter goblin territory, and goblins pile them to block the way. They are also weapons of war (the goblins' "shower of stones" is a signature attack (018-020)) and objects of ornamentation, with higher-ranking goblins wearing stones on their heads, arms, and waists (018-004).

## Associated Characters

- [[curdie|Curdie]] — interacts with stones most directly: removes them from walls, is attacked with them, and works with them in the mines
- [[goblins]] — use stones as weapons and wear them as ornaments; their underground world is built among stone
- [[irene|Irene]] — possesses the magical stone in her ring (015-054)

## Significance

Stones embody the novel's theme of hidden value — the plain stone and the precious gem share the same substance, just as the invisible [[the-magical-thread|thread]] connects the mundane and the magical. The dual nature of stone (barrier and passage, weapon and ornament, plain rock and royal gem) mirrors the novel's broader tension between surface appearance and deeper reality.
