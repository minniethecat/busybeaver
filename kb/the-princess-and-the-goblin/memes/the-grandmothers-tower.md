# The Grandmother's Tower

A mysterious turret chamber at the highest point of [[king-papa|the king]]'s [[the-palace|palace]], occupied by [[irene|Irene]]'s [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|great-great-grandmother]]. Most [[the-palace|palace]] inhabitants cannot find it or do not believe it exists. It is a threshold space between the ordinary world and the supernatural realm of [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]] (003-002, 014-002).

## Location

The tower is at the top of [[the-palace|the palace]], reached by a long, winding stair that few ever climb. When [[irene|Irene]] first discovers it, she finds herself in "a little square place, with three doors" (003-002). The tower's location at [[the-palace|the palace]]'s highest point is symbolically significant — closer to the sky, to light, to the divine.

Inside are [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s workroom (with the spinning-wheel) and her bedchamber. The architecture is mysterious: the tower seems larger inside than it should be, and its existence is unknown to most of the household.

## Description

The tower chamber is a space of supernatural beauty and comfort:

- Moonlight streams through the windows even when the sky outside is covered with rain-clouds (011-004)
- The [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]]'s spinning-wheel hums with a "bee-like" sound (003-002)
- The [[the-fire-roses|fire of roses]] glows in the hearth (015-007)
- The [[the-grandmothers-lamp|lamp]] shines through the roof, visible from outside as a guiding star (014-009)
- Pigeons roost in the tower, coming and going freely (003-073)
- The room smells of roses from the fire (015-007)

- [[irene|Princess Irene]] — the only visitor who can find and enter the tower
- The [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]]'s pigeons — roost and nest in the tower

## Significance

The tower is a liminal space — physically part of [[the-palace|the palace]] but spiritually apart from it. It represents the divine presence within the ordinary world, accessible only to those with [[courage-and-faith|faith]]. Like [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]] herself, the tower is both ancient and eternal, hidden and central, mundane (a spinning room at the top of a stair) and miraculous (filled with [[light|supernatural light]] and fire roses).
