# The Goblins' Plot

The central antagonistic scheme of the novel: [[goblins|the goblins]]' coordinated plan to flood [[the-mines|the king's mines]], abduct [[irene|Princess Irene]] from [[the-palace|the palace]], and forcibly marry her to the [[goblins|goblin]] prince. Discovered by [[curdie|Curdie]], the plot drives the novel's climax and is ultimately defeated by [[courage-and-faith|courage]], [[courage-and-faith|faith]], and [[water]] (007-006–007-007, 026-011).

## The Plan

The [[goblins|goblin]]s work secretly for an extended period, tunneling and excavating toward their objectives:

- **Flood the [[the-mines|mines]]**: Divert the great natural reservoir of [[water]] into [[the-mines|the king's mines]], destroying the human wor[[king-papa|king]]s and possibly drowning the miners (007-006)
- **Kidnap [[irene|Irene]]**: Abduct [[irene|the princess]] from [[the-palace|the palace]] and b[[the-ring|ring]] her underground
- **Forced marriage**: Marry [[irene|Irene]] to the [[goblins|goblin]] prince, Harelip, thereby claiming a royal bloodline (026-011)

The [[goblins|goblin]]s work only at night, the sound of their picks and hammers echoing through the [[the-mountain|mountain]] (007-006).

## Discovery

[[curdie|Curdie]] discovers the plot by overhea[[the-ring|ring]] [[goblins|goblin]] conversations while wor[[king-papa|king]] in the [[the-mines|mines]] at night. His knowledge of their language and his [[courage-and-faith|courage]] in ventu[[the-ring|ring]] close to their gathe[[the-ring|ring]]s allows him to piece together their intentions:

> "Those were the sounds of the goblins. They worked only at night, for the miners' night was the goblins' day." (007-006)

He reports his findings but is initially not believed by [[the-palace|the palace]] guards and [[king-papa|the king]]'s men.

## Foiling the Plot

The plot is defeated through a combination of factors:

| Element | Contributor | PN Reference |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Intelligence | [[curdie|Curdie]] overhears the plan while hiding underground | (007-006–007-007) |
| Combat | [[curdie|Curdie]] fights [[goblins|goblin]]s using knowledge of their foot weakness | (027-010) |
| Thread | [[irene|Irene]] follows the magical [[the-magical-thread|thread]] to rescue [[curdie|Curdie]] | (020-002–021-002) |
| Water | The natural reservoir is released, flooding [[goblins|the goblins]]' domain | (009-006) |

The flooding of the [[the-cave|goblin caves]] is an ironic reversal — [[goblins|the goblins]] planned to use [[water]] as a weapon, but [[water]] becomes their own destruction when the underground reservoir is breached.

## Significance

The [[goblins|goblin]]s' plot provides the novel with its dramatic structure, uniting the various [[the-magical-thread|thread]]s: [[irene|Irene]]'s spiritual growth, [[curdie|Curdie]]'s heroism, [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s guidance, and [[goblins|the goblins]]' menace. The plot's defeat requires the cooperation of all the novel's positive forces — [[courage-and-faith|courage]], [[courage-and-faith|faith]], intelligence, and love — illustrating MacDonald's theme that evil is defeated not by single heroic acts but by the convergence of virtues.
