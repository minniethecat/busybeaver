# The Pigeons

The pigeons are a flock of predominantly white doves that dwell with [[light|the grandmother]] in her turret chamber. They serve as her messengers, gatherers of magical cobweb material, and visible symbols of her celestial nature and providential care in "The Princess and the Goblin."

## Nature / Definition

The pigeons are first introduced when [[king-papa|Irene]] discovers [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s chamber:

> a multitude of the loveliest pigeons, mostly white, but of all colours (003-073)

They are self-sustaining creatures:

> "The pigeons feed themselves. They've got plenty to eat up here." (003-079)

The [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]]'s chamber contains a purpose-built pigeon loft:

> showed a great many pigeonholes with nests, some with young ones (003-082)

The [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]] lives on their eggs:

> "And she lives upon pigeons' eggs." (004-011)

## Properties

The pigeons possess several remarkable properties:

1. **Messenger function**: They carry messages through [[darkness]] and distance. The [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]]'s [[the-moon|moon]] guides them home:

   > "If any of my pigeons are out on a message, they always see my moon and know where to fly to." (011-053)

2. **Cobweb gathe[[the-ring|ring]]**: They collect the special material for [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s magical [[the-magical-thread|thread]]:

   > "It is spiderweb — of a particular kind. My pigeons bring it me from over the great sea." (011-031)

3. **Self-sufficiency**: They feed themselves and raise their young in the turret, requi[[the-ring|ring]] no human care (003-079).

4. **Pure whiteness**: A snow-white pigeon serves as [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s close attendant:

   > At that moment a snow-white pigeon flew in at an open window and alighted on the old lady's head (010-018)

## Narrative Role

The pigeons serve several connected functions:

1. **Messengers of providence**: They bridge the gap between [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s celestial realm and the earthly world, carrying [[the-magical-thread|thread]] material from "over the great sea" (011-031).

2. **Symbol of peace and purity**: White pigeons (doves) carry strong biblical associations. Their presence in [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s chamber reinforces her identity as a divine or semi-divine figure.

3. **Visible sign of the invisible**: When [[curdie|Curdie]] can only perceive [[light|the grandmother's light]] as a pigeon (023-025), the pigeons represent the interface between the seen and unseen worlds — those without [[courage-and-faith|faith]] perceive only the lesser form.
