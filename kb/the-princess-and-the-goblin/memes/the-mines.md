# The Mines

The royal mines beneath the [[the-mountain|mountain]], worked by miners including [[curdie|Curdie]] and his father. They occupy the middle ground between the sunlit surface and [[goblins|the goblins]]' deep domain — a liminal space where human industry meets subterranean danger (006-030, 007-006, 009-002).

## Location

The mines extend into the [[the-mountain|mountain]] from the surface, forming a network of galleries and passages. They are above [[goblins|the goblins]]' domain but connect to it through natural fissures and shared rock:

> "Curdie had explored many of the goblins' galleries himself, sometimes in the company of his father." (008-062)

The mines thus serve as a borderland — not as deep as [[goblins|the goblins]]' realm but no longer fully part of the surface world. This makes the miners unique: they know both worlds and can navigate the underground.

## Description

The mines are described through [[curdie|Curdie]]'s daily experience:

- Warm work underground, hard labor at the rock face (007-006)
- Dark passages lit by miners' [[the-grandmothers-lamp|lamp]]s
- Crystalline rocks that sparkle in [[the-grandmothers-lamp|lamp]]light (006-030)
- Natural galleries connecting to [[goblins|goblin]] tunnels (008-062)
- Firedamp and other mining hazards that require careful ventilation (009-023)

The miners' world is one of honest labor and practical knowledge. They know the [[the-mountain|mountain]] from the inside and understand its dangers.

## Associated Characters

- [[peter|Curdie's father]] and fellow miners — the mining community
- [[goblins]] — tunnel upward through the mines, threatening to flood them

## Significance

The mines represent the frontier between civilization and danger. They are a place of honest, productive work — but also the point of contact with the [[goblins|goblin]] threat. The miners, especially [[curdie|Curdie]], are uniquely positioned to defend [[king-papa|the king]]dom because they live on this boundary.

The [[goblins|goblin]]s' plan to flood the mines is a direct attack on this frontier, an attempt to collapse the barrier between the two worlds. That the plan is foiled through the combined efforts of [[curdie|Curdie]] (miner), [[irene|Irene]] ([[irene|princess]]), and [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]] (divine guide) reinforces the novel's theme that protection requires cooperation across all levels of being.
