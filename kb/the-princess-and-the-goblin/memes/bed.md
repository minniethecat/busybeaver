# Bed

The bed in *The Princess and the Goblin* is more than a place of sleep — it is a threshold between the ordinary world of the palace nursery and the magical realm of the great-grandmother. Irene repeatedly leaves her bed at night to journey to the tower, and she is miraculously returned to it, blurring the boundary between waking and dreaming.

## The Bed as Starting Point

Irene's adventures often begin when she rises from bed. "So she got out of bed, without waking the nurse who slept in the same room" [011-004], she sets out into the moonlit passages of the palace. The bed is associated with illness and comfort — she is put to bed early when unwell [011-002], [011-003] — but it is also the place from which she must choose to rise when the magical thread calls her.

## The Great-Grandmother's Bed

The old lady's bed is one of the most extraordinary objects in the novel. "A large oval bed stood in the middle, with a coverlid of rose colour, and velvet curtains all round it" [011-040]. She invites Irene to sleep there, asking, "Would you like to see my bedroom?" [011-035] and "You won't be afraid, then, to go to bed with such an old woman?" [011-046]. The bed is "delicious" [011-050], and when Irene wakes, she finds herself back in her own bed [011-066], as if the journey had been a dream.

## Bed as Symbol of Safety

The bed represents domestic safety in contrast to the dangerous goblin caves. King Papa's guards "watch outside the house every night, walking round and round it" [010-033], protecting the sleeping residents. Curdie "was soon fast asleep in his bed" after his night of listening to the goblins [007-002], and later "returned laughing, got into bed again, and was fast asleep in a moment" [007-003], showing the bed as a place of secure rest after danger.

## The Miraculous Thread

Irene places the magical thread "under the pillow of your bed" [015-078] at her great-grandmother's instruction. The bed thus becomes the point of contact between the ordinary and the magical, the place where the thread begins its guiding work.
