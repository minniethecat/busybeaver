# The Cellar

The wine cellar beneath [[king-papa|the king]]'s house, where [[goblins|goblins]] tunnel through the foundation and stage their climactic invasion. It becomes the site of [[curdie|Curdie]]'s most direct physical confrontation with the goblin forces, and its breach represents the novel's peak of tension before the final resolution (026-009, 027-017).

## Location

Located under [[king-papa|the king]]'s house, the cellar lies dangerously close to the [[goblins|goblin]] tunnels:

> The huge block which lay under the cellars of the house, within the line of which they had been tunneling, was not many feet below (026-009).

This proximity makes it the thinnest point between the human world above and the goblin underworld below. [[curdie|Curdie]] estimates it is "not many feet below the cellar, that without miners and their tools they could do nothing" (030-007).

## Description

The cellar is the "king's wine cellar," containing "huge vats" of wine (026-015). It has stairs leading down from the house above (027-017). The space is large enough to hold "a multitude" of [[goblins|goblins]] when they finally break through (032-004).

The stone block beneath it forms the critical barrier. When [[goblins|the goblins]] undermine it, they "came out in the king's wine cellar" (026-010) — the invasion begins.

## Climactic Confrontation

The cellar is where the novel's tension reaches its peak. [[curdie|Curdie]], working alone in the dark, stamps on goblin feet and drives them from rafters down to the cellars (027-010). When he descends to the cellar himself, he finds:

> It was full of goblins, whom he could dimly see (027-017).

Rather than fight them all, [[curdie|Curdie]] cleverly uses his knowledge of the house to trap them:

> He bethought himself of persuading them to follow him (027-018).

## The Aftermath

After the [[miners|miners]] flood the goblin tunnels, [[king-papa|the king]]'s forces return to the cellar:

> They were soon able to get into the wine cellar, where they found a multitude of goblins — dead (032-004).

The cellar, once the goblins' point of entry, becomes their tomb.

## Significance

The cellar represents the boundary between the human world and the goblin underworld at its thinnest point. Its invasion is the literal climax of the novel — the goblins' ambition to conquer the surface reaches its peak in the king's own house. [[curdie|Curdie]]'s defense of this space demonstrates that courage, practical knowledge, and loyalty to [[king-papa|one's king]] can overcome even the most direct physical threat.
