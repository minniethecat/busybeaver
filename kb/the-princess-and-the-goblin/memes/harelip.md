# Harelip

Harelip is the [[goblins|goblin]] prince, son of the [[goblin-queen|Goblin Queen]] and [[goblin-king|Goblin King]], distinguished by a facial deformity that gives him his name. He is a key antagonist in "The Princess and the Goblin" who leads a raiding party against [[king-papa|the king]]'s house and attempts to marry the captured [[king-papa|Princess Irene]].

## Role in the Story

Harelip serves as the active field commander of [[goblins|the goblins]]' offensive against the humans. Unlike his father [[king-papa|the king]], who strategizes from the throne, Harelip leads troops in combat. His defining trait is ambition — he drives the [[goblins|goblin]] war council toward aggressive action. A [[goblins|goblin]] minister acknowledges his political power:

> "this at least is no whim of Harelip's, but a matter of State policy" (018-011)

Du[[the-ring|ring]] the climactic invasion, Harelip leads a separate assault force:

> Harelip with another company had gone up by a different route (027-018)

His most violent act is the attempted abduction of [[lootie|Lootie]]:

> Harelip rushed at them, caught up Lootie (027-022)

## Appearance and Traits

Harelip's defining physical feature is his harelip — a congenital cleft lip that gives him his name. The [[goblins|goblin]]s refer to him by this feature:

> "they were talking of their prince — Harelip, they called him" (023-047)

He is portrayed as aggressive, impulsive, and entitled. When the queen rebukes him, he responds sullenly:

> Harelip growled, and for a few moments was silent (018-018)

When [[king-papa|the king]] dismisses him to bed:

> "I'm going," said Harelip sleepily (019-042)

Despite his royal status, he is treated as a subordinate by his parents.

## Marriage Plot

Harelip's personal ambition includes marrying a human [[irene|princess]]. The [[goblins|goblin]]s discuss:

> "a wife to their horrid Harelip" (023-058)

> "for a wife to their prince Harelip" (028-023)

This attempted cross-species marriage is presented as grotesque — a violation of natural order — and drives [[goblins|the goblins]]' urgency in captu[[the-ring|ring]] [[irene|Irene]].

## Key Relationships
