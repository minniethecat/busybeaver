# Creatures

The creatures in *The Princess and the Goblin* are the goblins' domesticated or allied beasts — strange hybrid forms that Curdie encounters in the underground passages. They blur the line between animal and goblin, representing the unnaturalness of the subterranean kingdom.

## The Goblins' Beasts

Curdie first sees them beneath his own window: "a group of stumpy creatures, whom he at once recognized by their shape as goblins" [007-003]. These creatures are associated with the goblins yet distinct from them. When Curdie enters the goblin tunnels, he sees "a score of creatures" [013-003] whose exact nature remains unclear — they may be goblins, goblin animals, or something in between.

## Creatures in Irene's Path

Irene too encounters creatures in the palace passages. "Something leaped into the room. It was like a cat, with legs as long as a horse's" [014-007]. This creature pursues her through "the long avenue of silent rooms" [014-009]. Its hybrid form — cat-like but impossibly leggy — marks it as a product of the goblin world, a creature that should not exist by daylight logic.

## Symbolic Role

The goblins' creatures embody the theme of corruption and distortion. Just as the goblins themselves are twisted versions of humanity (with their sensitive feet and aversion to sunlight), their creatures are twisted versions of ordinary animals. They represent what happens when living things are cut off from the sun and the natural order: they become grotesque.
