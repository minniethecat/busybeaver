# The Nursery

The [[lootie|nurse]]ry is [[king-papa|Princess Irene]]'s childhood room in the [[the-palace|king's house]]. It features a ceiling painted with stars — a domestic detail that foreshadows the starry walls of the [[light|grandmother]]'s turret chamber and establishes the theme of celestial imagery in [[irene|Irene]]'s world.

## Description

The [[lootie|nurse]]ry is described in the novel's opening lines:

> The ceiling of her nursery was blue, with stars in it, as in the sky (001-003)

This detail immediately links [[irene|Irene]]'s domestic world to the celestial, suggesting that even her ordinary surroundings hint at the magical realm she will discover. The [[lootie|nurse]]ry's starry ceiling is a more modest reflection of [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s chamber, where:

> All about the walls, as high as she could see, were what looked like stars of silver (011-040)

## Narrative Appearances

The [[lootie|nurse]]ry appears at key moments:

1. **[[irene|Irene]]'s flight and return** (Ch. 2): After getting lost, [[irene|Irene]] frantically searches for [[lootie|the nurse]]ry:

   > She thought of the stairs and her safe nursery (002-005)

   The [[lootie|nurse]]ry represents safety and the known world.

2. **[[irene|Irene]]'s dismissal of [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]** (Ch. 3): [[irene|Irene]] contrasts her [[lootie|nurse]]ry with [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s magical room:

   > "I shouldn't like it. My nursery is much prettier." (003-047)

   This early preference for the familiar over the magical marks [[irene|Irene]]'s initial immaturity.

3. **Recovery and protection** (Ch. 6): After the [[goblins|goblin]] encounter, [[irene|Irene]] is carried:

   > carried the princess to the nursery (006-108)

4. **Return to reality** (Ch. 15): After magical experiences, [[irene|Irene]] finds herself back:

   > in her own high chair at the nursery table, with her doll's house before her (015-102)

   The [[lootie|nurse]]ry serves as the anchor point between magical adventures and ordinary life.

## Narrative Role

The [[lootie|nurse]]ry functions as:
- **A domestic counterpoint** to [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s magical chamber
- **A symbol of childhood** that [[irene|Irene]] must grow beyond to fully embrace her [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]]'s world
- **A foreshadowing device**: the starry ceiling prepares [[irene|Irene]] (and the reader) for the celestial imagery of [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s realm
