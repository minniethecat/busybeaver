# Songs

Songs and [[singing-as-protection|singing]] are a recur[[the-ring|ring]] motif in "The Princess and the Goblin," functioning both as literal protective magic ([[peter|Curdie]]'s songs repel the [[goblins]]) and as a vehicle for spiritual expression (the [[light|grandmother]]'s strange sweet song).

## Curdie's Protective Song

[[curdie|Curdie]]'s song is introduced as his primary weapon against [[goblins|the goblins]]:

> "They can't stand that song. They can't sing themselves, for they have no more voice than crows." (006-029)

The song's power lies in its effect on [[goblins|the goblins]] rather than any inherent magic:

> "They won't always mind that song even, down there." (006-041)

This qualifier — that the song may not always work — keeps the magic grounded in the novel's world. It suggests the song's power is tied to [[goblins|the goblins]]' nature (their inability to sing) rather than supernatural enchantment.

[[irene|Irene]] initially finds the song jar[[the-ring|ring]]:

> "Curdie, I don't quite like your song: it sounds to me rather rude." (006-086)

Her gentle objection highlights the difference between [[irene|Irene]]'s refined sensibility and [[curdie|Curdie]]'s practical, miner's approach to song as a tool.

## The Grandmother's Song

In contrast to [[curdie|Curdie]]'s martial song, [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s [[singing-as-protection|singing]] is ethereal and mysterious:

> "singing a strange sweet song, of which she could distinguish no word" (022-060)

The [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]]'s song is wordless in [[irene|Irene]]'s perception — it transcends language. Where [[curdie|Curdie]]'s song is a weapon, [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s is a benediction, an expression of pure beauty that [[irene|Irene]] "could not understand" but could feel.

## Narrative Role

Songs in the novel create a spectrum of musical expression:
1. **[[curdie|Curdie]]'s song** → martial, protective, practical
2. **The [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]]'s song** → numinous, mysterious, transcendent
3. **[[goblin-songs|Goblin songs]]** → crude, moc[[king-papa|king]], corrupted

This spectrum maps to a moral order: divine song ([[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]]) → human song ([[curdie|Curdie]]) → [[goblins|goblin]] noise (crude parody).
