# The Bedroom

[[irene|Princess Irene]]'s private sleeping chamber in [[king-papa|the king]]'s [[the-palace|palace]], a space that serves as both a domestic refuge and the threshold from which her nocturnal adventures begin. The bedroom is where [[irene|Irene]] keeps her most precious possession — her [[crown]] — and where the boundary between ordinary sleep and extraordinary experience becomes porous.

## The Crown in the Bedroom

The bedroom is most notably associated with [[irene|Irene]]'s [[crown]]. When asked where her crown is, [[irene|Irene]] replies simply: "In my bedroom" (003-050). This becomes a recurring motif:

> "She's got her crown in her bedroom," she went on; "but I've not been into her bedroom." (010-015)

The crown-in-the-bedroom detail establishes the bedroom as a place of hidden royalty — when [[irene|Irene]] is in her bedroom, she possesses her royal identity in private, even when her public surroundings suggest only childhood and domesticity. The [[lootie|nurse]] jokes that she "wears it in bed" (004-016), a humorous image that underscores the bedroom's role as a space where the symbolic and the practical merge.

## A Threshold for Adventure

The bedroom door is the literal starting point for [[irene|Irene]]'s night journeys. In Chapter 20, as she begins following [[the-magical-thread|the magical thread]], the outside world intrudes through the bedroom door itself:

> "The cook's great black cat, pursued by the housemaid's terrier, had bounced against her bedroom door, which had not been properly fastened." (020-003)

The unfastened door is significant — it represents the permeability of the boundary between the safe, known bedroom and the dangerous, unknown corridors beyond. Throughout the novel, the bedroom is the place from which [[irene|Irene]] departs into [[darkness]] and to which she returns, transformed by experience.

## Significance

The bedroom is a space of dual identity. In it, [[irene|Irene]] is at once a child going to sleep and a [[royal|princess]] whose [[crown]] rests nearby — a private self that contains both ordinary and extraordinary dimensions. The bedroom is where [[irene|Irene]] receives [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|her grandmother]]'s visits:

> "She came into my room—all over it—and into your bedroom, making such a row!" (028-021)

This invasion of the bedroom by the supernatural — benevolent though it is — confirms the bedroom's role as the permeable boundary between waking life and the miraculous. [[irene|Irene]]'s bedroom is never fully sealed off from either danger or grace.
