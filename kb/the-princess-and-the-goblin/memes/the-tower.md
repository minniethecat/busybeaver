# The Tower

The tower is the elevated turret of the [[the-palace|king's house]] where [[light|the grandmother]] dwells. It represents the highest point of the house — physically remote from the domestic quarters below — and functions as a symbol of the divine presence that watches over the novel's world from above.

## Location and Architecture

The tower sits at the top of the house, accessible only by the [[the-staircase|old oak staircase]]. The [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]]'s chamber occupies its summit:

> the stair of the tower (005-011)

Its architecture is only partially understood by the household — [[lootie|the nurse]] [[lootie|Lootie]] is unaware of its occupant, and the passages leading to it are described as labyrinthine:

> to ascend the three stairs, walk through the passages with their many doors (014-002)

The [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]]'s turret room is the highest habitable space, positioned above even [[king-papa|the king]]'s quarters:

> Irene saw him go up the old stair; and he did not come down again (017-013)

This suggests [[king-papa|the king]] also has access to [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s tower, identifying him as one who — unlike the servants — can reach the higher realm.

## The Tower as Sacred Space

The tower embodies the concept of sacred elevation common in religious architecture and symbolism. The [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]]'s placement at the highest point of the house reflects her transcendent nature. [[irene|Irene]] must climb to reach her — the ascent is effortful and requires persistence:

> she reached the stair of the tower they had even been (014-010)

The tower is also [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s workspace — here she spins the magical [[the-magical-thread|thread]]:

> the old lady's spinning wheel, on a stool in front of the wheel (011-029)

From [[the-grandmothers-tower|the tower]], [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]] watches over the world below, sending her [[the-pigeons|pigeons]] on messages and spinning [[the-magical-thread|thread]] that guides [[irene|Irene]] through [[darkness]].

## Narrative Role

The tower serves as:
1. **Sacred geography**: A physical space set apart from ordinary life
2. **Symbol of transcendence**: The [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]] dwells above, watching over those below
3. **Goal of the journey**: [[irene|Irene]]'s repeated ascents to [[the-grandmothers-tower|the tower]] mark her spiritual progress
4. **Hidden sanctuary**: The tower is invisible to most — only those who seek it find it
