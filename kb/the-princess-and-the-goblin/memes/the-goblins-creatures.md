# The Goblins' Creatures

Strange, degenerate animals that live with and serve [[goblins|the goblins]] in the subterranean [[the-cave|cavern]]s beneath the [[the-mountain|mountain]]. Originally ordinary surface animals such as sheep, they were brought underground generations ago and have devolved into grotesque, hybrid forms (013-004–013-006).

## Origin

The creatures are descended from ordinary surface animals that [[goblins|the goblins]]' ancestors took with them when they were driven underground:

> "The goblins kept a great many animals for their own use... whose ancestors had taken their ancestors with them when they were driven from the sunlit world." (013-004)

Over centuries of subterranean existence, these animals adapted to [[darkness]] and became as strange as their masters. The text explicitly links their degeneration to that of [[goblins|the goblins]] themselves (013-006).

## Physical Description

MacDonald describes the creatures in detail when [[curdie|Curdie]] encounters them:

- They are "hideous" and "monstrous" in appearance
- They combine features of multiple surface animals unnaturally
- Some resemble sheep but have gone wrong in various ways
- They are used by [[goblins|the goblins]] for labor, food, and riding

[[irene|Irene]]'s encounter with one of these creatures is traumatic. When a creature — perhaps a "cob" or hybrid beast — touches her, she feels profoundly contaminated, requi[[the-ring|ring]] the cleansing fire of her [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]]'s roses to restore her (022-004, 022-017).

## Relationship to Goblins

The creatures serve [[goblins|the goblins]] in several capacities:

| Role | Description | PN Reference |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Labor | Used for work and transport underground | (013-004) |
| Food | Some are kept as livestock | (013-005) |
| Companions | The [[goblins|goblin]]s interact with them as pets or beasts of burden | (013-006) |
| Children | In some cases, [[goblins|goblin]] children interact with creature young | (017-016) |

The relationship is not one of master and pet in the human sense. The creatures share in [[goblins|the goblins]]' degeneracy:

> "The goblins, as Curdie had discovered, were much more animal than human in their nature; and their creatures shared in the degeneration." (013-006)

## Narrative Role

The creatures serve to illustrate [[goblins|the goblins]]' fallen state. Their existence — warped, grotesque shadows of surface animals — mirrors [[goblins|the goblins]]' own condition as shadows of humanity. The creatures' touch is contaminating, representing the danger of extended contact with the [[goblins|goblin]] world.

When [[irene|Irene]] is touched by one of these creatures, her [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|grandmother]]'s bathing her in the [[the-fire-roses|fire of roses]] is both a literal cleansing and a symbolic purification from the [[goblins|goblin]] realm's corrupting influence (022-004–022-017).

## Significance

The creatures embody MacDonald's theme of degeneration through separation from light and goodness. Like [[goblins|the goblins]] themselves, they began as ordinary creations but have become something lesser through generations of [[darkness]]. They serve as a warning about the consequences of exile from goodness — and as a reminder that what is merely tolerated (these poor creatures did not choose their fate) can become grotesque over time.
