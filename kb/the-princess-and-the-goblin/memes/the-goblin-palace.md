# The Goblin Palace

The underground seat of [[goblins|goblin]] power, located deep within the [[the-mountain|mountain]]'s hollow places. It is the residence of the [[the-goblin-king|goblin king]] and queen, the center of [[goblins|goblin]] government and intrigue, and the destination of the tunnels [[goblins|the goblins]] dig toward the surface world (018-004, 026-011, 027-023).

## Location

The goblin palace lies in the deepest reaches of the [[the-mountain|mountain]]'s subterranean network. It is reached through labyrinthine passages and [[the-cave|cavern]]s, some natural and some excavated by [[goblins|the goblins]] themselves. Access is known only to [[goblins|goblin]]s and to [[curdie|Curdie]], who has explored their tunnels through his mining work.

> "Below sat a little group of goblins around a fire, the smoke of which vanished in the darkness far aloft." (018-004)

## Description

The [[the-palace|palace]] is described in fragments rather than as a comprehensive whole — fitting for a subterranean realm glimpsed through [[darkness]]:

- The court sits around a fire in a [[the-cave|cavern]]ous space, with smoke vanishing upward into [[darkness]] (018-004)
- The [[king-papa|king]] holds court in a [[the-hall|great hall]] of some kind, large enough for his bodyguard (027-023)
- The [[goblins|goblin]] queen has her own quarters, with attendants (027-023)
- Messengers run at "full speed to the goblin palace" with news, implying a network of passages (026-011)

## Significance

The goblin palace mirrors and inverts [[king-papa|the king]]'s [[the-palace|palace]] on the surface. Both are seats of royal authority; both are centers of plotting and power. But where the sunlit [[the-palace|palace]] is open, visible, and ordered, the goblin palace is hidden, dark, and chaotic. The contrast reinforces the novel's vertical moral geography.

The [[the-palace|palace]] is also a place of genuine community for [[goblins|the goblins]] — they gather around fires, share news, and follow their leaders. MacDonald portrays them not as mindless monsters but as a society with its own structure, however degenerate.
