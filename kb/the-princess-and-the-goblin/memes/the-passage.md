# The Passage

A network of dark, winding passages beneath [[The Princess and the Goblin|the mountain]], threading between the three-tiered world: the [[the-palace|palace]] above, [[the-grandmothers-tower|the grandmother's tower]] in between, and [[the-cave|the goblin realm]] below. Passages are the story's connective tissue and a defining feature of its geography (001-004, 009-002).

## Location and Layout

The passages span the mountain's full vertical range. The goblins' domain includes "long galleries and passages running off" from great open pits (001-004). Higher up, the palace connects to passages leading to [[the-grandmothers-tower|the tower]] and the servants' quarters (011-006, 020-002).

At the deepest level, goblin-dug passages connect to [[the-cellar|the king's cellar]], their most daring incursion into the human world (026-009).

## Variety of Passages

The passages are not uniform. Some are grand galleries that impressed even [[curdie|Curdie]]:

> None of the galleries he saw were unworthy of note (009-002).

Others are narrow and treacherous, requiring crawling:

> Through one narrow passage after another, over lumps of rock (020-008).

The palace passages are "passage after passage" of bewildering complexity — [[irene|Irene]] runs "through passage after passage and could not find her way" (005-011).

## Irene Lost in the Passages

[[irene|Irene]]'s first adventure begins when she loses her way:

> Into this she ran. It was full of doors on each side. Nothing but passages and doors everywhere! (002-006).

This confusion becomes the catalyst for her discovery of [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]. Getting lost proves to be the first step toward finding something greater.

## The Grandmother's Guidance

When [[irene|Irene]] follows [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s [[the-magical-thread|thread]], it leads her:

> Through certain narrow passages towards the kitchen, turning here and there (020-002).

The passages that once confused and frightened her become navigable through [[courage-and-faith|faith]].

## Significance

The passages embody the novel's spatial theology. The mountain is not solid but hollow — threaded with ways through. Getting lost in passages is the necessary precursor to being found. And the passages themselves, like [[the-magical-thread|the grandmother's thread]], connect everything to everything else. [[irene|Irene]] discovers that what seemed a maze is actually a network — if only one has a guide.
