The spinning wheel in the grandmother's tower room — the source of the magical thread that guides Irene, and a sensory beacon whose humming sound signals the grandmother's presence (003-021, 011-008).

## Description

The spinning wheel is the grandmother's constant companion in her tower room. It is old-fashioned, described as "such an old-fashioned lady's room" (003-021), and the wheel is always attended: "the wheel went on spinning" in the moonlight (011-029). The spider-web-like thread on the distaff glistens: "on the distaff attached to the spinning-wheel, and in the moonlight it shone like — what shall I say? it shone like the thread of a great spider" (011-029).

Irene frequently thinks of the spinning wheel as part of the grandmother's room she yearns to find again: "a room all full of pigeons and a spinning-wheel, and a lamp that never went out" (014-002).

## The Humming Sound

The spinning wheel's hum is Irene's first sensory clue to the grandmother's presence. While exploring the house, Irene hears "the strangest noise!" — "it was the hum of the spinning-wheel!" (011-008). The hum reassures her: "What a diligent grandmother to work at the wheel so late!" (011-008).

Conversely, the absence of the humming sound creates fear and uncertainty. When Irene returns to the tower and finds it dark: "there was no sound of the spinning-wheel. She grew frightened once more" (015-003). Later, when bringing Curdie: "neither could she hear any sound of the spinning-wheel, and once more her heart sank within her" (022-002).

## Narrative Role

The spinning wheel's hum serves as an auditory beacon — a sensory manifestation of the grandmother's presence visible even when the grandmother herself cannot be seen. Unlike the visual light of the lamp or the silver globe, the hum penetrates walls and doors, drawing Irene upward through the house.

The wheel is the origin point of the thread: it is from the distaff of this spinning wheel that the magical guiding thread comes (011-029). Without the wheel, there would be no thread — the wheel is the generative source, the thread its extension into the world.
