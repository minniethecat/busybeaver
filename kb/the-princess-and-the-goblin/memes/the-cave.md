# The Goblin Caves

The goblin caves are a vast subterranean network of passages, chambers, and family dwellings beneath the [[the-mountain|mountain]]. They form [[goblins|the goblins]]' entire world — a dark, labyrinthine realm that mirrors the surface [[king-papa|king]]dom above while inverting its values of light, openness, and love.

## Location

The caves extend throughout the interior of the [[the-mountain|mountain]], reaching from just below the surface to unknown depths. They connect to the [[the-mines|mines]] worked by the human miners, and in places [[goblins|the goblins]]' tunneling has broken through into the miners' galleries, creating dangerous points of contact between the two worlds (013-006).

The narrative describes them as:

> Hollow places underneath; huge caverns, and winding ways, some with water dripping down, others full of water (001-004).

The caves are accessed from the surface through hidden entrances, and from within through a maze of tunnels whose geography only [[goblins|the goblins]] fully understand. [[curdie|Curdie]] learns to navigate portions of this network du[[the-ring|ring]] his spying expeditions, but even he never maps its full extent.

## Description

The cave system contains a variety of spaces serving different functions. The outer caves serve as family dwellings:

> He passed goblin houses, caves, that is, occupied by goblin families (018-003).

Deeper within lies [[the-hall|the great hall]], the ceremonial and political center of [[goblins|goblin]] life. Beyond the [[the-hall|hall]] extend the royal apartments and storage chambers. The whole is connected by "winding ways" — narrow passages whose twists and turns disorient human intruders but provide [[goblins|the goblins]] with countless ambush points.

The caves are lit by torches and [[the-grandmothers-lamp|lamp]]s that [[goblins|the goblins]] carry or mount on walls. But the light is always partial, always threatened by the surrounding [[darkness]]. When a torch goes out, [[curdie|Curdie]] experiences the caves' true nature:

> Morning would bring no light into these regions (009-022).

The air underground is warm — "it is very warm underground" (007-006) — and the caves echo with the sounds of dripping [[water]], [[goblins|goblin]] voices, and the distant [[the-ring|ring]] of pickaxes. The [[goblins|goblin]]s' creatures — bizarre hybrid animals — roam freely through these spaces, adding to the sense of a world governed by unnatural rules.

## Significance

The goblin caves represent the novel's vision of life without light — literally and spiritually. The [[goblins|goblin]]s have retreated underground, away from the sun they cannot bear, and have built a civilization in [[darkness]]. Their caves are simultaneously a refuge and a tomb: they provide safety from the surface world [[goblins|the goblins]] [[fear]], but they also cut [[goblins|the goblins]] off from everything beautiful, good, and true.

The caves' destruction by flood at the novel's climax (Ch. 31) is the culmination of this symbolism. Water — which once filled the [[the-hall|great hall]] as a natural reservoir — returns to reclaim the underground realm, washing away [[goblins|the goblins]]' works and resto[[the-ring|ring]] the [[the-mountain|mountain]]'s interior to its original, uninhabited state. The caves, like [[goblins|the goblins]] themselves, are revealed as an intrusion — something that should not be there, and that cannot endure when divine order reasserts itself.
