# The Garden

The garden is the cultivated outdoor space belonging to the [[the-palace|king's house]] on [[the-mountain|the mountains]]ide. It serves as a setting for key scenes of familial intimacy, adventure, and homecoming in "The Princess and the Goblin."

## Location and Geography

The garden is situated on the side of the [[the-mountain|mountain]], adjacent to [[king-papa|the king]]'s house:

> she was playing on a lawn in the garden, heard the distant blast of a horn (010-003)

The garden is bordered by natural rock formations:

> Against one of these rocks was a garden seat, shadowed from the afternoon sun (010-025)

It is enclosed by a wall that separates the cultivated space from the wild [[the-mountain|mountain]] beyond:

> they can climb over the wall of the garden quite easily (023-054)

A key geographical feature connects the garden to the underground world:

> the way by which the princess left the garden for the mountain stood a great rock (023-057)

## Key Events

The garden appears at crucial narrative moments:

1. **[[irene|Irene]] and the King's Reunion** (Ch. 10): [[irene|Irene]] is playing in the garden when she hears her father's hunting horn:

   > she ran to the gate, and then out on the road (010-004)

   The [[king-papa|king]] walks with [[irene|Irene]] through the garden:

   > "we'll have a walk in the garden together," he said. (010-021)

   MacDonald provides a detailed description:

   > The garden was a very lovely place. Being on the mountainside, it climbed up and down and around (010-024)

2. **Supernatural Creature Sightings** (Ch. 13): Strange [[the-goblins-creatures|goblin creatures]] appear in the garden at night:

   > It rushed into the garden. He sent an arrow after it (013-002)

   > a third he came rushing from the garden to the other two (013-003)

3. **[[curdie|Curdie]]'s Vigil** (Ch. 25): [[curdie|Curdie]] keeps watch in the garden through the night:

   > he remained in the garden the whole night, creeping on all fours (025-006)

   The dead [[goblins|goblin]] creature's body is found:

   > the hideous body of the goblin creature lay at the foot of the rock in the garden (025-002)

4. **The Thread's Destination** (Ch. 21/23): The magical [[the-magical-thread|thread]] b[[the-ring|ring]]s [[irene|Irene]] and [[curdie|Curdie]] up into the garden:

   > they stood in her own garden, close by the seat on which she had sat with her father (021-094)

## Narrative Role

The garden functions as a liminal space — neither fully domesticated interior nor wild [[the-mountain|mountain]] exterior. It is:
- A place of **safety and reunion** ([[irene|Irene]] with her father, the endpoint of [[the-magical-thread|the thread]]'s guidance)
- A **threshold** between [[the-palace|the palace]] and the [[goblins|goblin]]-haunted [[the-mountain|mountain]]
- A site of **beauty** that reflects the goodness of the human world above
