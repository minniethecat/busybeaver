# Gold

Gold appears throughout [[The Princess and the Goblin]] as a marker of royalty, supernatural radiance, and natural beauty. Unlike [[silver]] (associated with the moon and stars), gold signals the presence of [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s divine nature and [[king-papa|the king]]'s authority.

## Royal Gold

[[king-papa|King Papa]] wears gold as a symbol of his kingship:

> He wore a narrow circle of gold set with jewels around his head (010-004).

When he prepares for battle against [[goblins|the goblins]], he and his guards put on "purple and gold" — the colours of royal authority and war (030-018).

## The Grandmother's Gold

[[irenes-great-great-grandmother|The grandmother]]'s supernatural nature manifests through gold. Her hair, having been white in her aged appearance, transforms:

> No longer white, but of a rich golden colour, streamed like a cataract over her shoulders (015-007).

This transformation echoes the [[crown]] scene — when [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]] reveals her true nature, gold signals her eternal, unageing essence.

## Gold in Nature

MacDonald extends gold beyond the explicitly supernatural. The setting sun casts "orange and gold-coloured fringes upon the air" (020-005), and a rock touched by sunset appears "like some golden creature fresh from the sky" (020-007). These natural gold appearances suggest that the divine glory of [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]] is echoed throughout creation.

## Gold in the Goblin Court

Even the [[goblins|goblins]] recognise gold's value. [[the-goblin-king|The Goblin King]]'s court hall is described as having a beauty "no lump of gold itself could have been so precious as" (018-003), despite its grotesque inhabitants — an ironic commentary on the goblins' inability to appreciate beauty they themselves create.
