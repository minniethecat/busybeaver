The kitchen of the king's house, a warm, bustling domestic space that serves as both a destination and a threshold in Irene's adventures (005-013, 020-002).

## Location

The kitchen is located on the lower floor of the king's house, connected to the servants' hall and accessible both by regular stairs and by the grandmother's hidden passages (020-002, 011-002). An underground passage leads Irene directly toward the kitchen during her escape with Curdie (020-002).

## Description

The kitchen is a lively domestic space where servants work. During Irene's first nighttime wandering, she finds herself in the kitchen instead of her intended destination (005-013). It is connected to the servants' hall, "where she was not princess merely" but a child among ordinary people (011-002). At the story's end, "the cook rushed to the kitchen and the servants to their work" upon the king's return (030-018).

## Significance

The kitchen represents domestic normalcy in a story filled with underground dangers. It is the warm, human counterpart to the cold, goblin-inhabited passages below. When Irene stumbles into it during her first nighttime wandering (005-013), the kitchen represents the ordinary world she temporarily leaves behind. Passages from the grandmother's tower lead here, making the kitchen a junction between the magical and the mundane.
