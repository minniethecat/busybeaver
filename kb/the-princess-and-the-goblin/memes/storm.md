# Storm

A violent storm descends upon [[The Princess and the Goblin|the mountain]] during the novel's climactic chapters, complicating [[curdie|Curdie]]'s escape from [[goblins|the goblins]] and [[peter|Peter]]'s desperate ride to warn [[king-papa|the king]]. The storm tests both natural endurance and [[courage-and-faith|faith]] in [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|divine protection]] (029-004, 031-010).

## Curdie's Storm

After escaping the [[goblins|goblin]] tunnels, [[curdie|Curdie]] races home through a "tremendous storm which was raging all over the mountain" (029-004). The rain-swollen streams nearly block his path, and he arrives exhausted — but alive. The storm here represents the final physical obstacle before the novel's resolution.

## Peter's Ride

[[peter|Peter]] rides through the same storm to reach [[king-papa|the king]] with [[curdie|Curdie]]'s warning. [[curdie|Curdie]] notes that had [[peter|Peter]] set out earlier, he "did not judge the storm so bad" (029-005) — an observation that suggests [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|the grandmother]]'s unseen hand arranging timing for maximum effect.

By the time [[peter|Peter]] returns, "the storm was nearly over, but the streams were still fierce" (029-012). The storm's aftermath — swollen rivers and dangerous passes — continues to threaten.

## The Second Storm

As [[king-papa|the king]]'s forces prepare to march, another storm threatens:

> "I think it must be another storm coming over the mountain," said one of the guards (031-010).

This second storm never fully materialises — perhaps another sign of [[irenes-great-great-grandmother|divine protection]] clearing the way for the final victory.

## Significance and Themes

MacDonald uses the storm as a test of character. [[curdie|Curdie]] and [[peter|Peter]] do not pray for the storm to stop — they endure it. Their perseverance through natural danger mirrors [[irene|Irene]]'s perseverance through spiritual trial. The storm, like the [[goblins|goblins]], is a real danger that cannot be wished away — but it can be survived by those who act with courage.
