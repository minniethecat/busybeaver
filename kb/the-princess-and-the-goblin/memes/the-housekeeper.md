# The Housekeeper

The housekeeper is a senior domestic servant in the [[the-palace|king's house]] who appears in several scenes involving [[king-papa|Princess Irene]]'s care and the household's response to her adventures in "The Princess and the Goblin."

## Role in the Story

The housekeeper is introduced as a figure of household authority, though subordinate to [[lootie|the nurse]] [[lootie|Lootie]] in matters concerning [[irene|Irene]]:

> sometimes to the housekeeper's room, where the housekeeper was busy with her accounts (011-002)

When [[irene|Irene]] is lost and then found, the housekeeper participates in the household's relief:

> the whole household, headed by the housekeeper, burst into the nursery to exult over their darling (016-010)

She is present at [[irene|Irene]]'s sickbed:

> her nurse bending over her, the housekeeper looking over the nurse's shoulder (024-002)

## Appearance and Traits

The housekeeper is defined by her domestic role rather than distinctive personal traits. She keeps the household accounts (011-002), manages the servants, and participates in key household events. She has a terrier dog:

> pursued by the housekeeper's terrier, had bounced against the door (020-003)

She is portrayed as subordinate to [[lootie|the nurse]] in matters concerning [[irene|Irene]]:

> the housekeeper was afraid of the nurse (024-020)

This detail reveals the household hierarchy — [[lootie|the nurse]], as [[irene|Irene]]'s direct caregiver, outranks even the senior domestic servant.

## Key Relationships

- [[king-papa|Princess Irene]] — the child she helps care for
- [[lootie|Lootie]] — [[lootie|the nurse]] who outranks her in [[irene|Irene]]'s care
- [[the-palace]] — the household she manages

## Significance

The housekeeper exemplifies the novel's attention to the household as a functioning community. She has no heroic role, but her presence — like the [[men-at-arms]] and the servants — builds [[the-palace|the palace]] as a populated, believable world.
