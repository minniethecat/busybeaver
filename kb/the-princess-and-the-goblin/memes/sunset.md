# Sunset

Sunset marks a boundary in [[The Princess and the Goblin]] — the threshold between the safe, sunlit world and the dangerous night when [[goblins|goblins]] emerge from underground. The novel's characters observe this boundary with practical vigilance (006-104, 010-033).

## Sunset as Protection Boundary

[[lootie|Lootie]] is determined to bring [[irene|Irene]] home before dark:

> She would not let the princess be out after sunset (006-104).

This rule is not superstition but practical wisdom. [[goblins|The goblins]], who "live away from the [[sun]], in cold and wet and dark places" (001-005), are creatures of darkness. Sunset is their signal to emerge.

## The Palace Guard's Vigil

[[king-papa|The king]]'s guards patrol "round and round [[the-palace|the palace]] from sunset to [[sunrise]]" (010-033). The rhythm of their watch — sunset to sunrise — structures the temporal world of the novel around the opposition of light and dark, safety and danger.

## Sunset and Natural Beauty

MacDonald does not portray sunset solely as a boundary of fear. The evening light creates beauty, casting "orange and [[gold]]-coloured fringes upon the air" (020-005). Nature itself is good even as darkness falls — it is the [[goblins|goblins]] who corrupt the night.
