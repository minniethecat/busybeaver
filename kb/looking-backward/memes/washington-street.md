# Washington Street

The principal commercial street of 19th-century Boston, which Julian West revisits in a nightmare vision and finds so absurd in its chaotic proliferation of competing shops that he bursts into laughter.

## Overview

Washington Street represents everything the utopian distribution system has eliminated: competition, waste, advertising, and the conversion of public space into commercial display. When West reaches it at "the busiest point," he is overwhelmed by "the interminable rows of stores on either side" (028-014). Each store independently owned and stocked — a cacophony of duplication that, from the perspective of the efficient utopian system, appears genuinely insane.

## Key Passages

> "I reached Washington Street at the busiest point, and there I stood and laughed aloud, to the scandal of the passersby. For my life I could not have helped it, with such a mad humor was I moved at sight of the interminable rows of stores on either side."
> — Julian West, (028-014)
