# International Council

The **International Council** is a supranational governing body in Bellamy's utopian future that regulates international commerce, settles financial accounts between nations, and coordinates joint policy toward less-developed regions. It represents the extension of the principle of national industrial organization to the global stage.

## Role and Functions

The International Council serves as the coordinating authority for the world's industrialized nations. Dr. Leete explains that "the great nations of Europe as well as Australia, Mexico, and parts of South America, are now organized industrially like the United States" and that their "peaceful relations ... are assured by a loose form of federal union of worldwide extent" (013-011). The Council's primary functions include:

- **Trade supervision**: All international commerce is conducted as "a function of the nations as units," with the Council supervising the "dozen or so merchants in the world" who manage inter-nation trade through "a simple system of book accounts" (013-013).

- **Financial settlement**: At each year's end, the Council examines national accounts, clearing balances between nations. "The balances that remain after the accounts have been cleared by the international council should not be large under our system," and the Council "requires them to be settled every few years" to prevent large debts that could "engender" ill will (013-021).

- **Quality inspection**: The Council "inspects the commodities interchanged by the nations, to see that they are of perfect quality" (013-021).

- **Credit exchange**: Travelers can exchange their national credit cards at "the local office of the international council" in foreign cities (013-027).

- **Joint policy toward less-developed regions**: The Council coordinates "joint policy toward the more backward races, which are gradually being educated up to civilized institutions" (013-011).

## Membership

The Council is composed of representatives from member nations. In the United States, the [[the-president|President]], after completing a successful five-year term approved by Congress, is typically elected "to represent the nation for five years more in the international council" (017-022).
