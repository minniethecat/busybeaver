# West's Hypnotic Sleep

On the evening of May 30, 1887 (Decoration Day), Julian West submitted to his nightly hypnotic treatment by Dr. Pillsbury (002-009). He instructed his servant Sawyer to wake him at nine the next morning (002-010), but during the night, his house burned to the ground (004-022). West's sealed underground sleeping chamber preserved him until Dr. Leete discovered it while excavating for a new laboratory in the year 2000 (003-050). The sleep is both a plot device and a metaphor for the gulf between the old world and the new.
