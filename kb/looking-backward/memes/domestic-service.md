# Domestic Service

## Overview

Domestic service — the employment of servants for household work — was a major institution of 19th-century life and a marker of class distinction. In Bellamy's utopian vision, the problem of domestic service is solved not by finding willing servants but by eliminating the need for them entirely.

## The 19th-Century Problem

Julian West, accustomed to having a servant (Sawyer), asks Dr. Leete: "How have you disposed of the problem of domestic service? Who are willing to be domestic servants in a community where all are social equals?" (011-030). West notes that 19th-century ladies "found it hard enough to find such even when there was little pretense of social equality" (011-030).

## The Utopian Solution

Dr. Leete responds that because all citizens are social equals and because all service is honorable, the society could easily provide domestic servants if needed — but they are not needed (011-031). Mrs. Leete elaborates: "Our washing is all done at public laundries at excessively cheap rates, and our cooking at public kitchens. The making and repairing of all we wear are done outside in public shops. Electricity, of course, takes the place of all fires and lighting" (011-031).


> “You spoke of paying for service to take care of your houses,” said I; “that suggests a question I have several times been on the point of asking. How have you disposed of the problem of domestic service? Who are willing to be domestic servants in a community where all are social equals? Our ladies found it hard enough to find such even when there was little pretense of social equality.”

## The Cooperative Principle

The elimination of domestic service reflects the broader cooperative reorganization of society. Bellamy identifies "the vast waste of labor and materials which resulted from domestic washing and cooking, and the performing separately of innumerable other tasks" as "a very important cause of former poverty" (014-1422). By socializing these functions, the utopian society achieves both efficiency and the dignity of all citizens.
