# The House Fire of 1887

The fire that consumed West's ancestral home is the proximate cause of his 113-year sleep. The house 'had been occupied by three generations of the family' (002-007). The fire destroyed the above-ground structure but left the underground chamber intact (004-022), and the secret of West's location died with those who knew of Dr. Pillsbury's treatments (002-010). The fire thus functions as the narrative's catalyst — without it, West would have been awakened the next morning, and the utopian future would never have been witnessed.
