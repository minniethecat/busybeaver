# Criminal Justice in Utopia

## Overview

In Edward Bellamy's vision of the year 2000, the criminal justice system has been radically transformed. With the elimination of economic inequality — the root cause of most crime — the justice system is simplified to a fraction of its 19th-century complexity.

## The Elimination of Crime's Root Cause

Dr. Leete explains that in the 19th century, "fully nineteen twentieths of the crime, using the word broadly to include all sorts of misdemeanors, resulted from the inequality in the possessions of individuals; want tempted the poor, lust of greater gains, or the desire to preserve former gains, tempted the well-to-do" (014-1259). The desire for money was "the taproot of a vast poison growth, which the machinery of law, courts, and police could barely prevent from choking your civilization outright" (014-1259).

By making the nation "the sole trustee of the wealth of the people, and guaranteed to all abundant maintenance," this root was cut, "and the poison tree that overshadowed your society withered, like Jonah's gourd, in a day" (014-1259).

## Crime as Atavism

In the utopian society, crime is understood as atavism — the recurrence of an ancestral trait. Dr. Leete explains: "It is because nearly all forms of crime known to you are motiveless now, and when they appear can only be explained as the outcropping of ancestral traits" (014-1259). The attitude toward criminals is "an attitude of compassion and firm but gentle restraint" (014-1259).

Julian West notes the disappearance of the old state prison in Charlestown (013-1239).

## The Simplified Judicial System

The judicial system is drastically reduced. "With no private property to speak of, no disputes between citizens over business relations, no real estate to divide or debts to collect, there must be absolutely no civil business at all for them" (014-1261). In criminal cases, "the plea of the accused is not a mere formality with us, as with you. It is usually the end of the case" (014-1267) — most defendants plead guilty.

When a not-guilty plea is entered, the judge appoints two colleagues to state the case from opposite sides. "Unless both agree that the verdict found is just, the case is tried over" (014-1275). The system relies on universal honesty and the absence of motive for deception.

## Disciplinary Measures

For workers who refuse to perform their duties in the industrial army, the penalty is "solitary imprisonment on bread and water till he consents" (008-797). This is the primary disciplinary mechanism, replacing the complex penal system of the 19th century.

> We leave no possible ground for any complaint of injustice by requiring precisely the same measure of service from all.
> (009-037)
