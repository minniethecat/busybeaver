# The Central Warehouse

The logistical nerve center of the utopian distribution system: a vast facility where goods are received directly from producers, orders from sample stores are aggregated and sorted, and goods are dispatched to citizens' homes.

## Overview

The central warehouse is the behind-the-scenes infrastructure that makes the sample store possible. "They receive the orders from the different sample houses all over the city and sort them and send them out," Edith Leete explains (010-033). The warehouse represents the rationalization of distribution — the elimination of wholesalers, jobbers, and stockrooms that characterized 19th-century commerce.

## The Machinery of Distribution

Dr. Leete takes West to "inspect the central warehouse and observe actually in operation the machinery of distribution" (016-028). The visit reveals the full scale of the system: goods from every producer in the nation arrive directly, are sorted by ward and individual order, and dispatched through pneumatic tubes and delivery vehicles. "All our stores are sample stores, except as to a few classes of articles. The goods, with these exceptions, are all at the great central warehouse of the city, to which they are shipped directly from the producers" (010-031). Edith describes how the same system scales to villages, where "the village sample shops are connected by transmitters with the central county warehouse, which may be twenty miles away" (010-035). The transmission is so swift that the village customer experiences no delay compared to the city counterpart.
