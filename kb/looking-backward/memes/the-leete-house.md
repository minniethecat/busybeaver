# The Leete House

The residence of the Leete family, where Julian West awakens from his 113-year sleep and spends the weeks of his introduction to utopian society (004-005).

## Overview

The Leete house is the reader's primary window into utopian domestic life. It is comfortable and well-appointed but not ostentatious — economic equality means that every citizen's home is similarly adequate. The house contains the music room with its telephonic audio system, a garden where West reflects, and bedrooms whose comfort West finds perfectly satisfactory. It is here that Dr. Leete explains the institutions of the new society, here that Edith demonstrates the music room, and here that West struggles with his growing feelings for Edith (004-011).


> “And now,” he added, extending his hand with an air of irresistible cordiality, “let me give you a hearty welcome to the Boston of the twentieth century and to this house. My name is Leete, Dr. Leete they call me.”
