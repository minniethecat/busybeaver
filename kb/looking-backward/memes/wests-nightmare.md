# West's Nightmare Return

In the novel's emotional climax, West dreams that he has returned to 1887 Boston (028-008). But his months in the utopia have transformed his perception: the poverty, inequality, and suffering he once accepted as normal now appear horrific. He walks Washington Street and is overwhelmed by the waste and misery of competitive commerce (028-010). When he awakens to find himself still in the year 2000, his conversion to the utopian vision is complete (028-048). The nightmare is the moral engine of the novel — the moment when intellectual understanding becomes visceral conviction.
