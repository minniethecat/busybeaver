[P03-001]The "Life and Times of [[red-jacket|Red Jacket]]" by Colonel William L. Stone, has been

[P03-002]before the public for many years. The industry and ability of the author

[P03-003]have made it a work of great value, and his extensive researches have left

[P03-004]but little room for anything new to be said, by one coming after him. Yet

[P03-005]the fact need not be concealed that many, who were intimately acquainted

[P03-006]with Red Jacket, were disappointed when they came to read his biography.

[P03-007]If it had been prepared under the direct influence and superintendence of

[P03-008][[joseph-brant|Thayendanegea]], or Brant, it could not have reflected more truly the animus

[P03-009]of that distinguished character. Red Jacket in his day was the subject, at

[P03-010]different times of much angry feeling, and jealousy. The author has not

[P03-011]taken pains to embalm it, in these memorials of the great orator of the

[P03-012][[seneca-nation|Senecas]]. Much that was the subject of criticism during his life, admits of

[P03-013]a more charitable construction, and the grave should become the receptacle

[P03-014]of all human resentments.

[P03-015]The author acknowledges his indebtedness to the labors of Col. Stone, and

[P03-016]by an honorable arrangement, liberty was obtained for the use made of

[P03-017]them, in the following pages. Acknowledgments are due also to others,

[P03-018]whose names will appear in the course of this work.

[P03-019]TRACY, CAL., *April 12th*, 1885.
