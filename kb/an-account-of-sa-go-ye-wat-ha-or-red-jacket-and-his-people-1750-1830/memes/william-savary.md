# William Savary

## Biography

William Savary was one of the Quakers who formed the deputation present at the treaty negotiations held at [[canandaigua|Canandaigua]] in 1794, having been invited to the Indians' councils during the deliberations. (010-259) (010-260) He kept a journal of his observations, and made of the occasion an interesting note of what he saw. (010-260) (010-261)

Writing in his Journal while attending the Indian treaty held at Canandaigua in 1794, he spoke of the children of [[red-jacket|Red Jacket]] in terms of high regard. (020-333) (020-334)

## Role and Influence

Savary's chief significance for the account is as a firsthand witness whose written record preserved scenes that would otherwise have been lost. After dinner he and John Parrish rode out to view [[farmer-s-brother|Farmer's Brother's]] encampment, which contained about five hundred Indians located by the side of a brook in the woods, with seventy or eighty huts built of bark and boughs of trees. (010-262) (010-263) (010-264)

He described the camp as one of plenty and cheerfulness, the vast number of deer they had killed hung round the huts to dry, together with the daily rations of beef; and he judged it the most pleasant visit he had yet made to the Indians. (010-270) (010-273) (010-277) (010-278)

## Key Events

- **The 1794 Canandaigua council**: Returning to their quarters, Savary and his companion passed by the Indian council where Red Jacket was displaying his oratory to his brother chiefs on the subject of Colonel [[timothy-pickering|Pickering's]] proposals. (010-281) (010-282) (010-283)

- **Red Jacket's family**: Savary further observed that Red Jacket visited the Quakers with his wife and five children, whom he had brought to see them, exceedingly well clad and, in his estimate, the best behaved and prettiest Indian children he had ever met with. (010-284) (010-285) (010-286)

- **A source for the account**: Savary's journal entries were preserved and passed into the record as quoted by Colonel Stone, from whom the author in turn drew them. (010-287)
