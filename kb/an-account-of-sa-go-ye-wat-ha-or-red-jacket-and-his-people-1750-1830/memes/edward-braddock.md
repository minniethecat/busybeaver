# Edward Braddock

## Biography

Edward Braddock was the British general whose defeat in 1755 stands, in the account, as an early landmark in the military history of the Iroquois and their French alliance. The heading of the chapter that recalls it lists "the defeat of General Braddock in 1755" among the recollections of [[cornplanter|Cornplanter's]] youth. (014-003)

The same event is noted in the book's contents among the incidents of the period, "the Indians who defeated Gen. Braddock in 1755." (P04-063)

## Role and Influence

The significance the account attaches to Braddock's defeat lies in the composition of the force that overcame him.

[[cornplanter|Cornplanter]] was with his people when they fought in alliance with the French in the year 1755. (014-117) The principal part of the force which met and defeated the English under General Braddock was Indian, and it was through their prowess mainly, if not entirely, that the victory was gained. (014-119)

What part Cornplanter himself took in that engagement is not known, but in the war of the Revolution he was a war-chief, and ranked high in the estimation of his people. (014-121)

## Key Events

- **The defeat of Braddock (1755)**: Braddock's English army was defeated by a force chiefly of Indians allied with the French, the victory being credited mainly to the Indians' prowess. (014-119)

- **Cornplanter in the field**: The young [[cornplanter|Cornplanter]] was among his people when they fought with the French that year, though his precise part is unrecorded. (014-117) (014-121)

## Legacy and Historical Significance

In Hubbard's account Edward Braddock is remembered not for himself but for the Indians who defeated him: his 1755 disaster is cited as evidence of the martial power of the Six Nations and their allies in the generation before the Revolution, and as the earliest campaign in which [[cornplanter|Cornplanter]] appears on the historical stage. (014-119) (014-117)

The event frames the long alliance with the French that the account traces down to the coming of the English and the era of [[red-jacket|Red Jacket]]. (014-003)
