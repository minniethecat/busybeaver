## Biography

General Peter B. Porter resided at Black Rock, New York, near the Buffalo Creek Reservation of the Seneca Nation. During the War of 1812, he commanded the Indian warriors who fought alongside American forces on the Niagara frontier. "From this cause; from his high character; his intimate acquaintance with the chiefs; and his known attachment to these interesting people, he had great influence over them." (019-080)

In 1821, Porter hosted Red Jacket at his Black Rock residence, providing an occasion for the Seneca orator to meet with visitors who sought to become acquainted with him. It was at Porter's home that one visitor recorded his first opportunity "of seeing that deservedly celebrated Indian chief, Red Jacket." (019-080)

Porter's wife was noted for her kindness toward the Seneca, having "won the rugged hearts of all their leading men" through her benevolence. (019-080)

## Role and Influence

Porter's influence among the Seneca derived from his military leadership during the War of 1812 and his personal relationships with the chiefs. His position at Black Rock placed him in close proximity to the [[seneca-nation|Seneca]] communities, and his home served as a meeting place between Indian leaders and American officials.
