# Oliver Phelps

## Biography

Oliver Phelps was one of the two men whose names, with that of [[nathaniel-gorham|Nathaniel Gorham]], are attached to the great land purchase in the Genesee country. (005-168) They were the acknowledged representatives of a considerable body of men who were ambitious of securing an interest in what was regarded as the most desirable region in the country. (005-169)

Phelps and Gorham were the leading spirits of the company. (005-180) It had purchased the pre-emptive right of Massachusetts in the spring of 1788. (005-181)

## Role and Influence

In 1788 Mr. Phelps went on to the ground and was successful in convening a council of the Indians for the sale of their lands. (005-182) The council met at [[buffalo-creek|Buffalo creek]] during the month of July of the same year. (005-183)

His success in obtaining this council and securing a sale was owing in a large degree to his policy in paying court to the powerful faction of the Leasees residing in [[canada|Canada]], and giving them an interest in the purchase. (005-184)

## Key Events

- **The Buffalo Creek council (1788)**: The Indians at this treaty strenuously resisted the sale of any of their land west of the [[genesee-river|Genesee river]]:

  > "The Indians at this treaty strenuously resisted the sale of any of their land west of the Genesee river." (005-188)

  Yet, with a view of furnishing "a piece of ground for a mill yard" at the Genesee Falls, they were finally persuaded to assent to a boundary that included a tract twelve miles square west of that river. (005-190)

- **The Phelps and Gorham Purchase**: The lands thus ceded came to be called "The Phelps and Gorham Purchase." (005-201) It contained by estimation two million six hundred thousand acres, for which they agreed to pay the Indians five thousand dollars and an annuity of five hundred dollars forever. (005-203)

- **Sale to Robert Morris**: [[robert-morris|Robert Morris]], the distinguished financier of the Revolution, afterward acquired an interest in these western lands. (005-205)
