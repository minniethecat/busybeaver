# Captain Pollard

## Biography

Captain Pollard was a Seneca chief who, alongside Young King, led the Christian party within the Seneca Nation during the period of religious factionalism that divided the Senecas in the 1820s. Hubbard records the moment of division: "There was a Christian, and a Pagan party. The former led by Young King, Captain Pollard, and others; the latter recognized Red Jacket as its head." (021-103)

Pollard's role in the Christian party was that of a lieutenant to Young King — a leader whose institutional position within the missionary-supported Christian community gave him standing to challenge the pagan party's dominance. Unlike Young King, whose name appears more frequently in Hubbard's narrative, Pollard's specific actions are less individually documented; his significance lies in his collective leadership of the faction that successfully deposed Red Jacket in 1827. (021-157)

## Role and Influence

Pollard represented the organizational strength of the Christian party. Where Red Jacket's influence was personal and charismatic, the Christian party's power was institutional — rooted in congregations, missionary schools, and the networks of Christian Seneca families. Leaders like Pollard did not need to match Red Jacket's oratory; they needed only to mobilize the growing Christian population to vote as a bloc in tribal councils.

## Key Events

- **Leadership of the Christian Party (1820s)**: Served as a principal leader, alongside Young King, of the faction that opposed Red Jacket's pagan party. (021-103)
- **Deposition of Red Jacket (1827)**: Participated in the council that deposed Red Jacket from his chieftaincy. (021-157)
