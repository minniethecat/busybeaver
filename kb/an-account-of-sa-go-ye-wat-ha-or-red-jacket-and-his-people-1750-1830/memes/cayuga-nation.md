# Cayuga Nation

## Overview

One of the Six Nations of the Iroquois Confederacy — the Cayuga occupied territory in the Finger Lakes region between the Seneca to the west and the Onondaga to the east, and their lands were among those targeted in the post-Revolutionary land cessions.

## Structure and Governance

Like all Iroquois nations, the Cayuga Nation were organized into matrilineal clans that determined inheritance, political office, and social identity. Chiefs were selected by clan mothers and represented the nation at the Grand Council of the Iroquois Confederacy, where each nation held a specified number of hereditary sachemships.

## Historical Role

The Cayuga were among the original Five Nations. (002-039) Their lands in central New York were included in the treaties that reshaped Iroquois territory. (005-094) Along with the Onondagas and a part of the Senecas, they were involved in councils of the period. (010-114)

The nation's experience during and after the Revolutionary War reflected the broader history of the Iroquois Confederacy — the devastation of war, the loss of territory through successive treaties, and the challenge of maintaining cultural identity under the pressures of American expansion and missionary activity.
