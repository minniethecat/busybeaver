# Oneida Nation

## Overview

One of the Six Nations of the Iroquois Confederacy — the Oneida occupied territory between the Mohawk and Onondaga and were, alongside the Tuscarora, the only Iroquois nation to support the American cause during the Revolutionary War.

## Structure and Governance

Like all Iroquois nations, the Oneida Nation were organized into matrilineal clans that determined inheritance, political office, and social identity. Chiefs were selected by clan mothers and represented the nation at the Grand Council of the Iroquois Confederacy, where each nation held a specified number of hereditary sachemships.

## Historical Role

Hubbard notes the Oneida were among the original Five Nations. (002-039) Their lands were acquired by New York State through treaty negotiations. (005-094) Missionary efforts among the Oneidas paralleled those among the Seneca. (010-176)

The nation's experience during and after the Revolutionary War reflected the broader history of the Iroquois Confederacy — the devastation of war, the loss of territory through successive treaties, and the challenge of maintaining cultural identity under the pressures of American expansion and missionary activity.
