# Little Billy

## Biography

Little Billy was a chief of some note among the Senecas who was frequently in the orator's company. (003-301) Hubbard introduces him chiefly through his association with [[red-jacket]], with whom he shared both the trails of the Seneca country and the debates of its councils.

One episode preserves that relationship. Little Billy, with Red Jacket and one or two others, was once passing from their settlement on Canandaigua lake to the old Seneca Castle near the foot of Seneca lake, when they encountered a large grizzly bear. Little Billy and the others were frightened and began to run. (003-305)

Red Jacket, distinguished as a hunter and an excellent marksman, drew up his rifle and brought the monster to the ground. (003-307) (003-308)

## Role and Influence

The memory of that day gave rise to a famous retort. On a later occasion Little Billy was very pertinacious in calling Red Jacket a coward; the orator at first took no notice, but, finding him persistent, turned and answered:

> "Well, if I am coward I never run unless it's for something bigger than a bear." (003-313)

Nothing more was heard from Little Billy concerning his cowardice on that day. (003-315) The anecdote, though told at his expense, marks him as one of the chiefs close enough to Red Jacket to provoke and be answered by him.

Beyond the anecdote, Little Billy took his place among the Seneca leadership. He appears among the chiefs — with [[farmer-s-brother]], [[cornplanter]], [[little-beard]] and Red Jacket — who took part in the discussions of the nation, the chief burden of which fell upon Red Jacket. (011-258)

## Key Events

- **Council debates**: Little Billy was among the Seneca chiefs, alongside Farmer's Brother, Cornplanter, and Little Beard, who joined the meetings and speeches on the affairs of the nation. (011-258)

- **War of 1812**: In the frontier fighting near Buffalo, Little Billy was numbered among the principal chiefs who led the Seneca warriors, together with Farmer's Brother, Red Jacket, Pollard, and others. (016-267)
