# Henry Knox

## Biography

Henry Knox was the secretary of war of the United States during the administration of President [[george-washington|Washington]], and in that office he became one of the government's principal agents in its dealings with the [[iroquois-confederacy|Six Nations]]. When a deputation of the Iroquois waited upon the president, Washington commended them, for the further explanation of his views and wishes, to "General Knox, the secretary of war, and Colonel [[timothy-pickering|Pickering]]." (008-174)

## Role and Influence

Concluding his address to the deputation, the president delivered a white belt of wampum as an evidence of the sincerity of the United States' desire for perfect peace and friendship. (008-177) Having thus appointed Colonel Pickering and General Knox to attend the further conferences with the Indians, [[red-jacket|Red Jacket]]'s reply to the president was made to them. (008-179) In that reply the Senecas welcomed the appointment, reminding Knox that though a soldier he came to speak of peace:

> "We have rejoiced in your appointment, for you are specially appointed with General Knox, to confer with us on our peace and happiness. We hope the great warrior will remember, that though a *warrior*, he is to converse with us about *peace*." (008-310)

## Key Events

- **The conferences with the Six Nations**: As secretary of war Knox was joined with Colonel Pickering to conduct the government's formal conferences with the Iroquois deputation, the business of the [[washington-address-to-iroquois|president's address]] passing into their hands. (008-179)

- **Gifts to the deputation**: Knox directed that a military suit of clothes, including a cocked hat such as worn by officers of the United States army, be given to each member of the deputation. (009-035) When [[red-jacket|Red Jacket]]'s suit was presented, he requested the bearer to inform Knox that it would hardly become him, as he was not a war-chief but a [[sachem-chieftainship|sachem]] — the sachems being civil rather than military officers. (009-039)
