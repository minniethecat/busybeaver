# Wampum

## Definition

Wampum refers to belts and strings of beads made from the purple and white shells of the quahog clam. Among the Iroquois and other Eastern Woodland nations, wampum served far more than decorative purposes: it was the primary medium for recording treaties, conveying diplomatic messages, and commemorating significant events. (002-035)

Each belt of wampum was woven with a specific pattern encoding the terms of an agreement or the substance of a message. A belt could confirm a treaty, summon a council, or serve as a credential for an envoy. When a nation received strings of wampum, they were receiving a formal communication that demanded acknowledgment. (005-140)

## Context in Iroquois Culture

In Iroquois diplomacy, wampum was inseparable from the spoken word. A belt of wampum was presented while the speaker delivered his message, and the pattern of dark and light beads served as a mnemonic device — when the belt was later read, it would recall the exact terms of the agreement. (018-062)

Red Jacket himself, as an orator and diplomat, understood the authority that wampum carried. When the United States presented belts and strings of wampum to the Iroquois, it signified recognition of the confederacy as a sovereign entity in diplomatic exchange. (010-182) Hubbard records that wampum confirmed the treaty at the close of the Revolutionary War, when "here is the belt of wampum that confirmed that treaty." (018-191)

## Role in the Narrative

Wampum appears throughout Hubbard's account at pivotal diplomatic moments. At the Treaty of Canandaigua, wampum was exchanged as a tangible record of the peace established between the United States and the Six Nations. (008-178) When councils were convened — at Fort Stanwix, at Tioga Point, at Buffaloe Creek — wampum belts and strings were the currency of diplomacy, as essential as the spoken word in the orator's repertoire. (020-146)

The exchange of wampum in Hubbard's narrative consistently marks moments when the Iroquois engaged as equals with the United States — the material embodiment of a covenant relationship that Red Jacket spent his life defending.
