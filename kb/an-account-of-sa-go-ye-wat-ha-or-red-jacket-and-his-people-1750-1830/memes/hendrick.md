# Hendrick (Theyanoguin)

## Biography

Hendrick, also known as Theyanoguin or Tiyanoga, was a Mohawk chief and diplomat who played a central role in the Anglo-Iroquois alliance during the early and mid-eighteenth century. He was present at the Albany Congress of 1754, where Benjamin Franklin proposed his Plan of Union, and became widely known to the British public through published accounts of his speeches and diplomatic missions.

Hendrick's most famous exchange appears in Hubbard's narrative — the "dreaming" episode with Sir William Johnson. When Johnson gave Hendrick an elegant suit of clothes, the chief declared he had "dreamed a dream" in which Johnson gave him such a gift. Johnson, understanding the protocol, could not refuse what a dream commanded. (003-128) (003-129) A while later, Johnson turned the game around, telling Hendrick he too had dreamed — that Hendrick had given him a certain tract of Mohawk land. Hendrick, caught by his own logic, conceded the point with good humor, declaring "Sir William, I will never dream with you again." (003-142) The episode captures the diplomatic wit and the cross-cultural negotiation of power that characterized Iroquois-British relations.

Hendrick died at the Battle of Lake George in 1755, fighting alongside British forces against the French. His death was a significant loss to the British-Iroquois alliance, removing a leader whose personal credibility had been essential to maintaining Mohawk support for the Crown.

## Role and Influence

As a Mohawk chief and spokesman, Hendrick occupied a position comparable to the one Red Jacket would later hold among the Seneca. He was a diplomat who could navigate British protocol without being captured by it, and an orator whose words traveled beyond the council fires where they were spoken.

## Key Events

- **Albany Congress (1754)**: Represented the Mohawks at the intercolonial congress where Franklin proposed colonial union.
- **The Dreaming Episode**: His famous exchange with Sir William Johnson over gifts and land claims. (003-128) (003-142)
- **Battle of Lake George (1755)**: Fought and died alongside British forces against the French.
