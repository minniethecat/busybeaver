# Robert Morris

## Biography

Robert Morris appears in Hubbard's narrative as the distinguished financier of the Revolution who became a central figure in the disposal of the Seneca lands. He afterward became owner of the greater part of the Phelps and Gorham Purchase, as well as of the pre-emptive right of Massachusetts to the remaining part of western New York. (005-205)

His interest in the Genesee country ran through his family as well as his fortune. His father, the elder Robert Morris of Philadelphia, having purchased of Massachusetts in 1790 the pre-emptive right to that part of western New York not sold to Phelps and Gorham, sent his son as preparatory to the negotiations he desired to make with the Indians. (006-150)

That son, [[thomas-morris]], would carry the family's land ambitions to the Genesee in person.

## Role and Influence

Morris's holdings made him, for a time, the proprietor of an immense stretch of Iroquois territory. The remainder of the western lands was bought by Robert Morris in the spring of 1791. He resold soon after to a company of gentlemen in Holland — the association later known as the [[holland-land-company]] — pledging himself to extinguish the Indian title. (011-112)

That pledge is what set the stage for the treaty councils of the 1790s. The obligation was discharged through the Treaty of Big Tree. In September 1797 the Seneca executed a conveyance to Robert Morris of Philadelphia, transferring the title to the greater part of their remaining domain west of the [[genesee-river]]. (012-294)

The sale was pressed on his behalf by his son Thomas and resisted at every step by [[red-jacket]]. Morris, though he never appeared in council himself, became one of the decisive off-stage figures in the loss of the Seneca homeland.

## Key Events

- **Purchase of the western lands (1791)**: Morris bought the remainder of the western New York lands in the spring of 1791 and resold to a Holland association, binding himself to extinguish the Indian title. (011-112)

- **Treaty of Big Tree (1797)**: In September 1797 the Seneca conveyed to Robert Morris the title to most of their remaining lands west of the Genesee, the cession his son Thomas negotiated over Red Jacket's opposition. (012-294)
