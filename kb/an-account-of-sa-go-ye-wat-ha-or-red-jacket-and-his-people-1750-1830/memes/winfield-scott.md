# Winfield Scott

## Biography

Winfield Scott commanded one of the two American brigades in the Niagara campaign of 1814, serving under [[jacob-brown|General Brown]]. (017-034) In the plan against Fort Erie his brigade was to cross the Niagara river through a difficult pass in the Black Rock Rapids and make a landing below the fort, so that the two brigades, enclosing it, could prevent the escape of the garrison. (017-034)

## Role and Influence

Scott was noted for the promptness and discipline of his command. He made good his landing with his usual promptness, was on the ground at the hour appointed, and by the aid of a few Indians and volunteers who accompanied him as guides, invested the fort so as to secure its garrison. (017-039) His steadiness became the mainstay of the American line at Chippewa, and other commanders looked to the support of his brigade in the crisis of the action. (017-087)

## Key Events

- **Advance to Chippewa (July 1814)**: On the evening of the same day as the capture of Fort Erie, Scott with his brigade and Towson's artillery proceeded down the river toward Chippewa, and on the morning of the fourth encamped in the open field on the south side of Street's creek. (017-062) He was joined there by General Brown with Ripley's brigade, which took post in the same field in his rear. (017-068)

- **Battle of Chippewa (July 1814)**: Nothing could exceed the coolness and order with which Scott's brigade crossed the bridge and formed its line, under the galling fire of the enemy's artillery and the headlong approach of his infantry. (017-173) When the British were only fifty yards distant, they were received by a tremendous discharge of musketry from the American line, which forced them to fall back.

- **Pursuit of the British**: Scott pursued the retreating British around the point of woods, beyond which he could only advance in face of their batteries, and, unable to reach them across the intervening river, he deployed to the left and formed a line in the open field in front of Chippewa. (017-182)
