## Biography

Colonel William L. Stone was the author of *Life and Times of Red Jacket*, which Hubbard acknowledges had "been before the public for many years" by the time he prepared his own work in 1886. (P03-001) Stone was a journalist and editor in New York, serving as editor of the *New York Commercial Advertiser*.

Hubbard praised Stone's industry and ability, noting that "his extensive researches have left but little room for anything new to be said, by one coming after him." (P03-001)

## Role and Influence

Stone's biography of Red Jacket served as the foundational modern account of the orator's life. However, Hubbard observed that Stone's work reflected a bias in favor of [[joseph-brant]], noting: "If it had been prepared under the direct influence and superintendence of Thayendanegea, or Brant, it could not have reflected more truly the animus of that distinguished character." (P03-001) This perceived partiality was one motivation for Hubbard's own, more sympathetic treatment of Red Jacket.
