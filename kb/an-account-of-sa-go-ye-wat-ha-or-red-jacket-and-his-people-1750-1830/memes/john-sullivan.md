# John Sullivan

## Biography

John Sullivan was the American general whose army marched into the Indian country of the Iroquois in 1779, during the Revolutionary War. (005-172) His advance through the Genesee country was long remembered among the Senecas, and the account of [[red-jacket|Red Jacket]] repeatedly returns to "the march of Sullivan, and his army, into the Indian country." (003-260)

The expedition passed through what the book calls the very finest portion of Western New York, at a season of the year when vegetation was in its highest perfection. (005-173)

The beauty and fertility of these lands became the theme of praise on the part of every soldier that beheld them, and their fame was thus carried eastward as later settlers were drawn to the region. (005-175)

## Role and Influence

Sullivan's campaign of 1779 was directed against the Indian towns of the Genesee country, and the warriors of the Six Nations took the field to oppose it. The Indians were on the war-path with [[joseph-brant|Brant]] during the campaign of General Sullivan against the Indian towns in the Genesee country in 1779. (014-150)

Considerable preparations for defense had been made, particularly at Newtown, where the decisive engagement of the expedition was fought. (003-261)

At the time of the invasion Red Jacket was still a young man, not yet raised to the rank of chief; Colonel Stone is quoted in the account as fixing his age:

> "Sa-go-ye-wat-ha was then twenty-nine years old, and though it does not appear that he had yet been created a [chief]..." (003-262)

After the battle at Newtown the Indians retreated until they found protection from the guns of the British at [[fort-niagara|Fort Niagara]]. (013-152)

## Key Events

- **The Sullivan campaign (1779)**: The [[sullivan-campaign|expedition]] marched into the Genesee country, and from its advent "in 1779" its route through Western New York was fixed in Seneca memory. (005-172) [[joseph-brant|Brant]] and the confederated warriors resisted the advance. (014-150)

- **Battle of Newtown**: Considerable preparations for defense were made at Newtown, (003-261) but the Indians were afterward compelled to retreat to the shelter of the British guns at Fort Niagara. (013-152)

- **Retaliation in the Mohawk valley**: Under the command of [[joseph-brant|Brant]] and [[sir-john-johnson|Sir John Johnson]], the Indians subsequently avenged the invasion of Sullivan by the destruction they wrought in the valley of the Mohawk. (014-153)

- **Opening of the Genesee lands**: In later years settlers pursued "the route taken by Sullivan in '79," reaching the Genesee region by the road his army had cut. (011-191)
