# Frank Rambotham

Frank Rambotham is Laura's youngest brother, appearing in the novel's opening chapters as part of the domestic world she leaves behind for the Ladies' College. Though a minor figure, he serves as a marker of the childhood innocence and family intimacy that Laura progressively loses through the course of the novel.

## Role in the Novel

Frank appears exclusively in the early chapters, before Laura's departure for Melbourne. He is introduced among the siblings in the garden, crying "Oh, parrakeets!" (PN [[001-009]]) and later begging Laura for more stories: "Wondrous Fair," begged little Frank (PN [[001-014]]). His vulnerability is emphasized — Laura's mother worries "if Frank treads on Maggy again" (PN [[002-031]]), and at the railway station "little Frank showed signs of beginning" to cry (PN [[003-003]]). During the long journey, "little Frank slept" (PN [[003-039]]), his exhaustion contrasting with Laura's alert, anxious watchfulness. A small chorus of "Leppie and Frank in weak chorus" (PN [[001-066]]) emphasizes his place among the younger children.

## Character Traits

Frank is defined by his **youth and vulnerability** — he is "little Frank" throughout, the baby of the family whose needs and fears require constant management. His **imaginative engagement** with Laura's stories ("parrakeets!") reveals the sibling world of shared fantasy that Laura commands. His **tendency to cry** — at the railway station, at the prospect of Laura's departure — makes him the family's emotional barometer, registering the disruption Laura's leaving causes.

## Significance

Frank functions as a figure of the childhood world Laura must leave behind. His small cries, his sleep during the journey, and his place in the "weak chorus" of younger siblings all mark him as belonging to a realm of innocence that the Ladies' College will systematically dismantle in Laura. The novel never returns to Frank after the opening chapters; his disappearance from the narrative mirrors Laura's own progressive detachment from her family.
