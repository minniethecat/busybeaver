# Maisie Shepherd (Mrs. Shepherd)

Maisie Shepherd is the wife of the curate Robby Shepherd in "The Getting of Wisdom," introduced in Chapter XVI when Laura Rambotham visits the Shepherd household during a monthly holiday. She is not a schoolgirl — despite her family's use of her girlhood nickname — but a married woman of at least seven years' standing, older than her husband and the exhausted centre of a household organised around his comfort and moods.

## Role in the Novel

Maisie appears only in Chapter XVI, but her presence illuminates a domestic world entirely distinct from the Ladies' College. She is first described indirectly: the curate's wife is "a thin, elderly lady with a tired face" — "much older than he" (PN [[016-001]]). When Laura arrives, Mrs. Shepherd "policeman-like, raised a warning hand" (PN [[016-014]]) to protect Robby's rest, whispering: "Robby has just lain down for a few minutes" (PN [[016-015]]).

The nickname "Maisie" is used exclusively by Robby — "Maisie!" he shouts impatiently from upstairs (PN [[016-018]]) — and by Isabella in admonishment: "Really, Maisie!" (PN [[016-030]]), "Do try to get it right tonight, Maisie" (PN [[016-042]]). The name carries a note of condescension, the diminutive used to address a woman whose household management is constantly found wanting by her husband and sister-in-law.

Isabella explains to Laura that her "sister-in-law is nothing of a manager" and that "Robby's patience is angelic" for tolerating her — a judgement that Laura, fresh from her own mother's capable example, finds perplexing, wondering "why, considering the dearth of time, and the distress of the ladies at each fresh contretemps, they did not jump up and fetch the missing articles themselves — as Mother would have done" (PN [[016-031]]).

## Character Traits

- **Gentle and ineffectual**: Mrs. Shepherd is described as having "so little spirit that Laura felt quite sorry for her" (PN [[016-029]]). Her attempts to manage the household are perpetually undermined by Robby's demands and Isabella's corrections.
- **Devoted but weary**: Her quiet "With mutton, Robby dear?" (PN [[016-024]]) — "with the utmost humbleness" — captures a marriage of asymmetrical power, seven years in.
- **A foil to Mother**: Laura instinctively contrasts Maisie's household management with her own mother's brisk competence, the comparison silently affirming the domestic capability of the world Laura has left behind.

## Key Passages

> "A thin, elderly lady with a tired face" — [[016-001]], the narrator's initial description of the curate's wife

> Mrs. Shepherd, "policeman-like, raised a warning hand. 'Hssh... ssh... sh!' she breathed... 'Robby has just lain down for a few minutes.'" — [[016-014]], [[016-015]], the household's anxious attendance to Robby

> "With mutton, Robby dear?" ventured Mrs. Shepherd, with the utmost humbleness. — [[016-024]]

> "Really, Maisie!" said Miss Isabella. "And when the poor boy's so rushed, too." — [[016-030]], the household's rhetorical structure: Robby's needs, Maisie's failures

> "My sister-in-law is nothing of a manager... Robby's patience is angelic." — [[016-032]], Isabella's condescending assessment

## Relationships

- **Robby Shepherd**: Her husband of seven years (PN [[016-032]]), whose moods govern the household and whose nickname for her — "Maisie" — is used by no one else in the novel with quite the same proprietary impatience.
- **Isabella Shepherd**: Her sister-in-law, who corrects and admonishes her constantly (PN [[016-030]], [[016-042]]), viewing her as a failed household manager whose improvement can only be achieved through relentless correction.
- **Laura Rambotham**: The visiting schoolgirl whose silent comparison of Mrs. Shepherd with Mother (PN [[016-031]]) is one of the novel's quiet affirmations of the domestic competence Laura has been raised to take for granted.
