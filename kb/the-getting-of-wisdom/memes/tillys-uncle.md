# Tilly's Uncle

Tilly's Uncle is one of the more vividly comic characters in "The Getting of Wisdom," dominating Chapter XIV during Laura Rambotham's holiday visit to Tilly's family. With eleven mentions in the chapter — nine unique PN paragraphs — he is a substantial presence whose eccentric authority and recurring catchphrase create a memorable portrait of colonial family life.

## Role in the Novel

Appearing exclusively in Chapter XIV, Tilly's Uncle governs the household with a combination of patriarchal authority and comic eccentricity. His catchphrase — "let's call in the cats!" — punctuates the chapter as a signature of his personality, a phrase whose meaning may be obscure but whose ritual repetition establishes his role as the household's presiding spirit.

For Laura, the visit to Tilly's home — and her encounter with this formidable uncle — is another lesson in the variety of family life beyond her own experience. Where her own household is governed by Mother's brisk competence, Tilly's is organised around the uncle's whims and pronouncements. The contrast silently expands Laura's understanding of the social world.

## Character Traits

- **Overbearing and patriarchal**: The uncle's authority structures the household, his wishes determining everyone else's movements.
- **Comically eccentric**: His catchphrase and mannerisms mark him as a character type — the colonial paterfamilias rendered with Richardson's characteristic ironic precision.
- **Memorable in repetition**: His frequent appearances and recurring verbal tics make him the chapter's dominant presence, even though his significance to the broader narrative is limited.
