# Joey

Joey is the suitor of Georgina, the Godmother's elder daughter, in "The Getting of Wisdom." Appearing in Chapter VIII during Laura Rambotham's monthly holiday at Prahran, Joey's courtship of Georgina — conducted through loud telephone arguments and comic excursions — provides Laura with an unvarnished introduction to the unromantic realities of adult romantic negotiation.

## Role in the Novel

Joey appears in Chapter VIII as part of the Godmother's extended social circle. Described as a "fat, lazy-walking young man" (PN [[008-029]]), his relationship with Georgina is marked by audible quarrels that reverberate through the household. When the party visits Marlborough Tower, Joey drives the group in a carriage — a comic set piece in which class, gender, and courtship anxieties converge.

For Laura, Joey and Georgina's relationship is an education in how little romance resembles the stories she tells. The narrator's ironic presentation — neither condemning nor sentimentalising — allows the reader to see what Laura cannot yet articulate: that courtship, in this colonial world, is as much about practical negotiation as about feeling.

## Character Traits

- **Physically unprepossessing**: Described as fat with a lazy walk, Joey is no romantic hero.
- **Loud and quarrelsome**: His telephone arguments with Georgina suggest a relationship conducted at high volume.
- **Socially ordinary**: Joey represents the unremarkable suitor — adequate but uninspiring — that a young woman of Georgina's class and temperament might reasonably expect.
