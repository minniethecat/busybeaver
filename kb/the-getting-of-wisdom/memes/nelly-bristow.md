# Nelly Bristow

Nelly Bristow is a student at the Ladies' College in Melbourne. She appears
only briefly in the novel, mentioned in a passage where [[laura-rambotham]]
reflects on the contrast between intellectual curiosity and trivial personal
observations.

## At the College

Nelly is a fellow pupil of Laura's at the [[ladies-college]]. The sole detail
recorded about her is a distinctive physical trait: when she spoke, "little
bubbles came out of her mouth, and she needed to swallow twice as often as other
people" [009-014].

This observation occurs during Laura's period of attempted intellectual reform,
after her encounter with [[miss-hicks]]. Laura notes that despite her efforts to
cultivate genuine intellectual curiosity, she finds herself more naturally
interested in such personal peculiarities than in geographical or scientific facts.

## Significance

Nelly Bristow's brief mention serves a thematic purpose in the narrative: she
represents the kind of vivid, personal detail that naturally attracts Laura's
attention, in contrast to the dry factual knowledge that the school system values.
Her presence in the text illustrates the tension between Laura's innate
observational gifts and the institutional demand for abstract learning.
