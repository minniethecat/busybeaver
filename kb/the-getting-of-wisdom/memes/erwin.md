# Erwin

Erwin is one of the Godmother's sons, introduced in Chapter VIII of "The Getting of Wisdom" as part of the unruly Prahran household that hosts Laura Rambotham during a monthly holiday. Together with his brother Marmaduke, he represents the "wild boys" whose teasing makes Laura's visit an ordeal of social discomfort.

## Role in the Novel

Erwin appears in Chapter VIII as one of the "half a dozen boys" in the Godmother's chaotic household (PN [[008-026]]). Described as "pug-nosed," he participates with Marmaduke in terrorising Laura during her visit — the brothers' rough play and mockery serving as a counterpoint to the more decorous world of the Ladies' College. Their teasing of Laura's purple dress — which Marmaduke dubs "the purple parrot" — is one of the chapter's set-piece humiliations.

## Character Traits

- **Pug-nosed and mischievous**: His physical description and behaviour mark him as a comic type — the ugly, unruly boy whose energy overwhelms Laura's attempts at dignity.
- **Part of a pack**: Erwin operates in tandem with Marmaduke, the brothers forming a miniature mob that terrorises visitors.

## Key Passages

> Erwin, the pug-nosed son, joined his brother in tormenting Laura. — [[008-026]]

## Relationships

- **Marmaduke**: His brother and partner in mischief.
- **Godmother**: His mother, the formidable head of the Prahran household.
- **Georgina and Marina**: His sisters.
- **Laura Rambotham**: The visiting schoolgirl who endures his teasing.
