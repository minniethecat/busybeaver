# Jim Chapman

Jim Chapman is the brother of Miss Chapman, one of the mistresses at the Ladies' College. A peripheral figure in "The Getting of Wisdom," his name appears across several chapters — always in connection with his sister — as part of the novel's web of family relationships that extend beyond the school's walls.

## Role in the Novel

Jim Chapman appears across three mentions in the novel's later chapters, always in connection with Miss Chapman. His existence is significant primarily for what it reveals about Miss Chapman herself — that she has a family, a brother, a life beyond the College. In a novel attentive to the hidden dimensions of its characters' lives, Jim Chapman represents one of the many offstage presences that flesh out the world beyond Laura's immediate experience.

## Relationships

- **Miss Chapman**: His sister, a mistress at the Ladies' College.
- **Sandy**: Another young man mentioned in connection with Jim Chapman.
