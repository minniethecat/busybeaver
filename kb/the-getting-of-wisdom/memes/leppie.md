# Leppie

Leppie is one of Laura Rambotham's younger brothers, introduced in the opening chapters of "The Getting of Wisdom" as part of the sibling world Laura inhabits before her departure for the Ladies' College in Melbourne. Though a minor figure, his distinctive curiosity and participation in the family's rituals mark him as more than a background presence.

## Role in the Novel

Leppie appears in the novel's first three chapters, during the family scenes that establish Laura's pre-school identity. He is distinguished from his siblings by his questioning intelligence — when Laura tells an elaborate story about a prince and a lady, it is Leppie who interrupts with a logical objection: "But what about the marks of travel?" (PN [[001-007]]), puncturing Laura's narrative revision. Later, he joins Pin in begging Laura to continue: "Oh, yes, please do, Wondrous Fair! Tell what happened next" (PN [[001-012]]), showing his engagement with Laura's imaginative world.

In the farewell scene, Leppie participates in the family's ritual of seeing Laura off: "Leppie was crying" (PN [[003-003]]), his grief part of the emotional landscape of departure that Laura carries into her new life.

## Character Traits

- **Curious and questioning**: Leppie's first significant line — "But what about the marks of travel?" (PN [[001-007]]) — reveals a mind attentive to narrative consistency, a trait that distinguishes him from the more accepting Pin.
- **Emotionally present**: His participation in the farewell scenes shows a child fully engaged in the family's emotional life, neither detached like Frank nor managing like Pin.
- **Part of the sibling constellation**: Leppie, Pin, and Frank together form the domestic world that Laura is leaving — each sibling representing a different register of childhood response to Laura's departure.

## Key Passages

> "But what about the marks of travel?" asked Leppie. — [[001-007]], his characteristic logical intervention

> "Oh, yes, please do, Wondrous Fair! Tell what happened next," begged Pin and Leppie. — [[001-012]]

> "Please, Wondrous Fair," begged little Frank. — [[001-014]], the youngest brother's echo

## Relationships

- **Laura Rambotham**: His elder sister, the "Wondrous Fair" of the opening scene, whose storytelling captivates him.
- **Pin**: His sister, with whom he shares the role of Laura's audience and companion.
- **Frank**: His younger brother, the baby of the family.
- **Mother**: The authority figure whose summons ("Laura, I want you. Come here." — [[001-017]]) ends the storytelling.
