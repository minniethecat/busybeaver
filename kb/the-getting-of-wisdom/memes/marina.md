# Marina

**Marina** is Godmother's younger daughter, a sober and capable young woman of about twenty who lives at the family home in Prahran. Though she appears predominantly in a single chapter, she represents the practical continuity of the Rambotham family's dependence on Godmother's household.

## Appearance and Character

Marina is introduced as a plain, efficient young woman whose appearance matches her no-nonsense personality:

> "Marina, a short, sleek-haired, sober-looking girl of about twenty, had Godmother's brisk, matter-of-fact manner." (PN [[008-007]])

She possesses a "neat watch" (PN [[008-016]]) and moves through her day with methodical efficiency. In contrast to Laura's excitable temperament, Marina is the embodiment of capable self-possession — she manages household tasks, shopping orders, and the escorting of Laura with equal competence.

## Escort and Holiday Visit

Marina's primary role in the narrative occurs when she collects Laura from the Ladies' College for a monthly holiday at Godmother's home. However, she arrives late, causing Laura considerable distress:

> "It was half-past nine, then ten, then half-past; it struck eleven, the best of the day was passing, and still Marina did not come." (PN [[008-003]])

When Laura, humiliated at being the last girl left, is finally collected, Marina's matter-of-fact greeting contrasts with Laura's emotional turmoil.

On the walk through the city, Marina treats Laura to sweets along the way — a small gesture of kindness that Laura records with characteristic exaggeration:

> "Laura thought she would have danced, as she walked at Marina's side." (PN [[008-010]])

Marina's own appetite remains unaffected by the treats: "off whose own appetite the edge had long since been taken" (PN [[008-014]]).

## Family Role

Marina is deeply integrated into the household economy. Her mother relies on her for domestic management:

> "'I hope, Marina, you told Graves about those ducks. I was going to myself this morning, but I declare I forgot it.' Marina, without lifting her eyes from her plate, answered that she had." (PN [[008-023]]–[[008-024]])

She manages orders for the household and spends her Saturday afternoon at a draper's shop while Laura visits the College gardens with Georgy (PN [[008-049]]–[[008-050]]). Her competence is taken for granted by the family.

## Relationship to Laura

Marina serves as a conduit of practical assistance from Godmother to Laura. It is from Marina that Laura inherits her schoolbooks (PN [[008-002]]), a hand-me-down arrangement that underscores the family's financial dependence on Godmother's household.

Marina is also Laura's bedfellow during the holiday visit, sharing her bedroom which contains "a small bookcase with a broken hinge" (PN [[008-051]]) — a detail that quietly signals the family's modest means. When Laura wakes screaming from a nightmare in a later chapter, it is Marina, her bedfellow, who "rose on one elbow" to attend to her (PN [[018-045]]).

## Role Model

Laura's mother explicitly holds Marina up as an example for Laura to emulate:

> "I hope when you grow up you'll be as much of a help to me as Marina is to her mother." (PN [[009-003]])
