# Georgina

Georgina, called "Georgy" by her family, is the elder daughter of Laura Rambotham's Godmother, appearing in Chapter VIII during Laura's monthly holiday at Prahran. She is characterised by her brusque manner, her loud arguments with her suitor Joey, and her comic vanity — particularly her insistence on drapery around her legs when ascending the Marlborough Tower.

## Role in the Novel

Georgina appears in Chapter VIII as part of the Godmother's chaotic household. She is "dumpier and more brusque" than her sister Marina (PN [[008-026]]), and her courtship with Joey — conducted largely via telephone arguments — provides Laura with a comic education in adult romance. When the party visits Marlborough Tower, Georgina's insistence on modesty ("she would not climb the tower unless a rug was draped around her legs") becomes a set piece of colonial feminine anxiety.

Her relationship with Joey is marked by loud quarrels and reconciliations — a pattern that the narrator presents with characteristic irony, neither condemning nor sentimentalising the limited romantic options available to a young woman of her class and temperament.

## Character Traits

- **Brusque and unpolished**: Unlike Marina, Georgina lacks the social graces that might soften her sharp edges.
- **Comically vain**: Her demand for leg-drapery at Marlborough Tower reveals a self-consciousness about her physical presentation.
- **Emotionally volatile**: Her telephone rows with Joey suggest a relationship conducted at high volume, with little of the restraint expected of young ladies.

## Key Passages

> Georgina, the elder daughter, was "dumpier and more brusque than Marina." — [[008-026]]

> Her loud telephone arguments with Joey were audible throughout the house. — [[008-029]]

> She refused to ascend Marlborough Tower without a rug draped around her legs. — [[008-042]]

## Relationships

- **Joey**: Her suitor, a fat, lazy-walking young man with whom she conducts a loudly quarrelsome courtship.
- **Marina**: Her younger sister, whose greater delicacy sets off Georgina's brusqueness.
- **Godmother**: Her mother, the formidable matriarch of the Prahran household.
- **Laura Rambotham**: The visiting schoolgirl who observes Georgina's romantic drama with keen, unspoken interest.
