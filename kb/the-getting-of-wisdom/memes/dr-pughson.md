# Dr. Pughson

Dr. Pughson is the Headmaster of Ladies' College, the highest institutional authority in the school. His "red-rimmed eyes" [024-038], stern disciplinary presence, and notoriously difficult examination papers make him a figure of dread among the students.

## Role in the Novel

Dr. Pughson occupies the apex of the Ladies' College hierarchy. He appears at moments of institutional ceremony — standing "next Mr. Strachey's desk" during formal proceedings [012-009] — and moments of discipline, as when he catches Laura in an infraction and deals her a sharp rebuke [017-004]. His most significant role in the plot concerns the annual examinations: his papers are "said to be far and away the most difficult" [024-035], and his reputation for "catchy" questions drives much of the anxiety in the examination chapters [024-037].

## Character Traits

The narrator provides little direct characterization of Dr. Pughson's personality, but his physical description — "red-rimmed eyes" raised from examination papers [024-038] — suggests a weary, possibly overworked man. His pedagogical approach is severe: he sets questions designed to catch students out rather than assess their knowledge [024-037]. He is also capable of leniency, as when Miss Blount expresses "extreme surprise at Dr. Pughson's leniency" [024-044]. His professional relationship with Mr. Strachey is that of colleagues sharing institutional authority [024-048].

## Key Scenes

Dr. Pughson first appears when Laura is brought before him shortly after her arrival at the College [007-021]. He presides over the formal examination proceedings, his presence dominating the room [012-009]. When he catches Laura in a minor infraction, he deals her "one of his cuts" — a sharp verbal or physical reprimand [017-004]. During the examinations, his questions are the subject of intense student anxiety [024-035], and his papers are rumoured to be exceptionally difficult [024-037]. In the final examination scene, Mr. Strachey strides into the room and Dr. Pughson "blinked up from the stacks of papers" [024-048].

## Relationship to Laura

Laura encounters Dr. Pughson as an intimidating authority figure. She is brought before him as a new student [007-021] and later caught by him in an unlucky moment [017-004]. His examination papers represent the ultimate academic challenge Laura must face, and her prayer for help with his "catchy" questions reveals her anxiety [024-037]. He embodies the institutional power against which Laura must define herself.

## Significance

Dr. Pughson represents institutional authority in its most abstract and impersonal form. Unlike Mrs. Gurley or Mr. Strachey, who interact with students socially as well as administratively, Dr. Pughson is defined entirely by his official functions: discipline, examination, hierarchy. His red-rimmed eyes and his stacks of papers suggest the bureaucratic machinery behind the school's genteel facade. He is the system made flesh.
