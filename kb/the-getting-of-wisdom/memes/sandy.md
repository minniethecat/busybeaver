# Sandy

Sandy is a young man mentioned in connection with Jim Chapman and the Chapman family in "The Getting of Wisdom." His appearances are brief — three mentions across the novel's later chapters — and he functions primarily as a name in the novel's wider social landscape, one of the offstage figures whose existence suggests a world of masculine sociability parallel to the feminine world of the Ladies' College.

## Role in the Novel

Sandy appears in three brief mentions, always in connection with Jim Chapman. Together, they represent the kind of young men — brothers, family friends — who exist at the periphery of the schoolgirls' consciousness: figures about whom the girls might speculate, but who remain essentially unknown, glimpsed rather than known.

## Relationships

- **Jim Chapman**: His companion in the novel's brief mentions.
- **Miss Chapman**: Connected through the Chapman family network.
