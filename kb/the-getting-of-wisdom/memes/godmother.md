# Godmother

**Godmother** is Laura Rambotham's elderly guardian and benefactor, a brusque but devoted figure who makes Laura's attendance at the Ladies' College possible. She lives at Prahran, on the outskirts of Melbourne, and serves as Laura's primary external support system throughout her school years.

## Character and Manner

Godmother is a woman of contradictions — sharp-tongued yet deeply loyal, snappish yet unfailingly generous. Laura's first impression captures this duality:

> "Godmother was good to them all in a brusque, sharp-tongued fashion; but Pin was her especial favourite and she made no secret of it." (PN [[004-001]])

She is described as plump and short in stature, yet possesses an unshakeable confidence that makes her unintimidated by authority figures:

> "Beside her, Godmother was nothing but a plump, short, elderly lady. But a plump, short, elderly lady who was not afraid." (PN [[004-022]]–[[004-023]])

Her manner is brisk and matter-of-fact — a trait inherited by her daughter Marina, who shares her "brisk, matter-of-fact manner" (PN [[008-007]]). She is not given to sentimentality, dismissing Laura's worries with characteristic bluntness: "Tut, tut!" (PN [[004-011]]).

## Role as Benefactor

Godmother's practical support is essential to Laura's education. She supplies Laura with schoolbooks through her daughter Marina, who passes them down as hand-me-downs (PN [[008-002]]). Without this material assistance, as Laura's mother acknowledges, the family could not afford to send Laura to school at all:

> "If it hadn't been for her supplying you with books and things I couldn't have sent you to school at all." (PN [[009-003]])

She also provides Laura with a home during monthly holidays — a crucial respite from the boarding school that many other girls take for granted. When Laura is one of the few students with nowhere to go, Godmother's invitation brings her spirits from despair to elation (PN [[008-001]]).

## At the College

On the day of Laura's admission to the Ladies' College, Godmother accompanies her and meets Mrs. Gurley, the formidable Lady Superintendent. While Laura shrinks before Mrs. Gurley's imposing presence, Godmother stands her ground:

> "Godmother, it was true, was not afraid of Mrs. Gurley. She looked the Lady Superintendent squarely in the face and did not lower her voice." (PN [[004-023]])

Godmother even intercedes on Laura's behalf, asking Mrs. Gurley to be gentle with the new pupil: "Godmother now asked if she might say a word in private" (PN [[004-024]]). Later, Cousin Grace speculates: "I bet Godmother's asking her not to wallop you too hard" (PN [[004-031]]).

## Family Life at Prahran

| Family Member | Relationship | Description |
|--------------|-------------|-------------|
| Marina | Daughter | Sleek-haired, sober young woman of about twenty; supplies Laura's schoolbooks |
| Georgy | Daughter | Attends Bible class; shares household duties |
| Eldest son | Son | Bank clerk, brusque manner similar to his mother |

Godmother runs an active household, driving her own pony-carriage (PN [[004-006]]). Her home, while not wealthy, is comfortable enough for Laura to feel out of place as "a mere negligible null" compared to her consequential status at school (PN [[009-001]]). A daughter of Godmother's had previously attended the Ladies' College, giving the family some standing there (PN [[005-053]]).

## Relationship with Laura

Godmother's relationship with Laura is complex. While she provides essential care, her brusque manner sometimes wounds Laura's sensitive nature. After one visit, Laura writes home with such a lack of warmth that her mother scolds her for ingratitude (PN [[009-003]]). Yet Godmother remains unfailingly reliable — writing to Laura's mother about Laura's arrival, driving in to collect her, and hosting her regularly.

Her optimistic assessment of Laura's prospects proved well-founded: "Godmother was equally optimistic. From what she had seen of Laura, she thought the child would get on well enough" (PN [[008-021]]).
