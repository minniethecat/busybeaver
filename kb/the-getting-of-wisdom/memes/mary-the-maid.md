# Mary (the Maid)

Mary is a maid at the Ladies' College in "The Getting of Wisdom," one of the servant figures who constitute the background domestic economy of the institution. Though she never speaks in the novel, her recurring presence — noted for her "pleasant smile" — represents the unseen labour that sustains the school's genteel surface.

## Role in the Novel

Mary appears across multiple chapters as part of the College's domestic infrastructure. She is first noted for her "pleasant smile" (PN [[008-003]]), a detail that distinguishes her from the more formidable figures of institutional authority. Her presence is part of the rhythm of school life — attending to students' needs, maintaining the physical environment — without ever intruding into the narrative foreground.

In a novel preoccupied with class and social performance, Mary represents the working-class world that the Ladies' College both depends upon and renders invisible. Laura, who comes from a household with a servant (Sarah), would recognise Mary's position — but the novel gives Mary no interiority, no voice, reflecting the period's assumptions about servant-class characters.

## Relationships

- **Ladies' College**: Her employer and the setting for her appearances.
- **Laura Rambotham**: One of the students she serves, whose own family employs a servant.
