# Ballarat

Ballarat is a city in regional Victoria, Australia, referenced once in "The Getting of Wisdom" through a memory of Laura Rambotham's: a theatrical performance of Hamlet that she witnessed before her arrival at the Ladies' College in Melbourne.

## In the Novel

Ballarat appears in Chapter XII during the dramatic scene of Annie Johns' public expulsion. As the Principal thunders his condemnation, Laura experiences "an ecstatic quiver" — a sensation she recognises as having felt only once before, "when a beautiful, golden-haired Hamlet, who had held a Ballarat theatre entranced for a whole evening, fell dead by Laertes' sword, to the rousing plaudits of the house" (PN [[012-021]]). The parallel Richardson draws between these two experiences — theatrical death and real social expulsion — reveals Laura's tendency to aestheticise the events around her, filtering schoolroom drama through the lens of remembered spectacle.

## Key Passage

> Laura was shot through by an ecstatic quiver, such as she had felt once only in her life before; and that was when a beautiful, golden-haired Hamlet, who had held a Ballarat theatre entranced for a whole evening, fell dead by Laertes' sword, to the rousing plaudits of the house. — [[012-021]]

## Significance

The Ballarat reference serves a narrative and characterological purpose rather than a geographical one. It establishes Laura's pre-Melbourne life as containing moments of cultural exposure — she has seen Shakespeare performed — while also highlighting her naivety: she equates the gravity of a classmate's expulsion with the staged death of a Hamlet actor, treating both as theatre. This moment encapsulates the gap between Laura's imaginative inner world and the social realities of the College. For Richardson's contemporary readers, Ballarat would have been recognised as a prosperous gold-rush city capable of attracting touring theatrical companies, making the reference geographically plausible.
