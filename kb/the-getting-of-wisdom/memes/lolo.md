# Lolo

Lolo is a minor schoolmate of Laura Rambotham at the Ladies' College in "The Getting of Wisdom," appearing in Chapter XXIII. The narrator's brief but vivid description of her — "a merry little lump of a girl, who darted her head from side to side when she spoke, with the movements of a watchful bird" — is one of the novel's small triumphs of comic portraiture.

## Role in the Novel

Lolo appears in Chapter XXIII in a moment of schoolgirl sociability, calling to Evelyn Souttar: "Evelyn, come here, I want to tell you something" (PN [[023-007]]). Her birdlike head movements and merry disposition place her among the more genial figures in Laura's school world — a contrast to the sharper-edged personalities that dominate the College's social hierarchy.

Though she has only three PN mentions, Lolo's vivid physical description makes her memorable. She represents the minor characters whom Richardson renders with a few quick strokes — sufficient to suggest a personality, not enough to develop one — populating the novel's social landscape with plausible, glimpsed lives.

## Character Traits

- **Merry and birdlike**: Her "watchful bird" head movements and cheerful disposition are her defining features.
- **Socially unthreatening**: Unlike the novel's more formidable personalities, Lolo seems genial and approachable.
