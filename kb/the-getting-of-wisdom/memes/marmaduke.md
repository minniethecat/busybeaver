# Marmaduke

Marmaduke is one of the Godmother's sons, introduced in Chapter VIII of "The Getting of Wisdom." He is remembered chiefly for his mockery of Laura Rambotham's purple dress, which he dubs "the purple parrot" — a moment of casual childhood cruelty that Laura experiences as acute humiliation.

## Role in the Novel

Appearing in Chapter VIII as part of the Godmother's rowdy Prahran household, Marmaduke operates in partnership with his brother Erwin — the "wild boys" whose teasing makes Laura's holiday visit an ordeal. His naming of the purple dress is an act of social aggression that resonates with Laura's broader anxieties about her appearance and her family's limited means.

## Character Traits

- **Cruel and observant**: Marmaduke's mockery of the purple dress shows a sharp eye for vulnerability — and a boy's instinct to exploit it.
- **Part of a pair**: He and Erwin function as a unit, their combined energy amplifying each individual act of mischief.
