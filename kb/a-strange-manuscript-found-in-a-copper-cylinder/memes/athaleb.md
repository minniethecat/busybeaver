# Athaleb

## Definition

The [[athaleb-class|athaleb]] is an enormous flying reptile native to the subterranean world of the Kosekin, described by the narrator as a creature resembling a prehistoric pterodactyl. Possessing a vast wingspan and a formidable beak, the [[athaleb-class|athaleb]] is tamed by the Kosekin and used for aerial transport, making it one of the most striking elements of the novel's prehistoric ecosystem (018-032). The creature's docility when domesticated and its crucial role in the escape plot make it as much a narrative device as a zoological curiosity.

The name "[[athaleb-class|athaleb]]" is itself a Kosekin word, part of the linguistic system that [[oxenden|Oxenden]] and his companions later analyze through Grimm's Law to connect the Kosekin language to Hebrew (026-028). The narrator records that the Kosekin apply this name to "these monsters," suggesting that the [[athaleb-class|athaleb]] is one of several giant creatures inhabiting the cavern world (018-032).

## Physical Characteristics

The [[athaleb-class|athaleb]] is described as a creature of immense size and power. When [[layelah|Layelah]] first identifies one approaching, the narrator is filled with terror, but she reassures him: "it's only an [[athaleb-class|athaleb]]" (021-009) — a remark that reveals both the creature's familiarity to the Kosekin and its formidable appearance to outsiders. A closer view reveals the [[athaleb-class|athaleb]] to be a winged reptile with a massive beak, though the narrator's initial terror quickly transforms into fascination once he observes its behavior (021-016).

The creature's jaws can be muzzled when it is under control, and unmuzzled only "when they are used in war" (021-013), indicating that the [[athaleb-class|athaleb]] serves a military function in addition to its role as transportation. In combat, the [[athaleb-class|athaleb]]'s natural weapons — beak and talons — make it a devastating force.

## Domestication and Use

[[kosekin-race|The Kosekin]] have mastered the art of taming the [[athaleb-class|athaleb]], a skill the narrator finds remarkable: "Man can tame anything. The [[athaleb-class|athalebs]] are very docile when they are taken young" (021-015). [[layelah|Layelah]] demonstrates this domestication by stroking the creature's breast, causing it to lie down submissively (021-023). Once mounted, the narrator discovers that the experience is surprisingly comfortable: "the fact of actually being on the back of the [[athaleb-class|athaleb]] than all fear left me. I perceived that the creature was perfectly under control" (021-031).

The [[athaleb-class|athaleb]] serves as the Kosekin's primary means of long-distance aerial transportation across the vast cavern system. It is also the vehicle of choice for the annual migration to [[magones|Magones]], the volcanic [[magones|Island of Fire]], where the creatures feed on fish (021-035, 021-040). This seasonal migration provides the mechanism by which [[adam-more|Adam More]] and [[layelah|Layelah]] execute their escape from the subterranean world.

## Role in the Escape Plot

The [[athaleb-class|athaleb]] is central to the novel's climactic escape sequence. [[layelah|Layelah]], who has been secretly planning to flee [[kosekin-culture|Kosekin society]], reveals to [[adam-more|Adam More]] that the [[athaleb-class|athaleb]] can carry them both "far away to the land of light" (021-032). Her intimate knowledge of the creature's habits — including its seasonal journey to [[magones|Magones]] and its feeding patterns — makes the escape possible. The flying sequence, in which the [[athaleb-class|athaleb]] carries the two refugees over the [[underground-sea|polar sea]], represents one of the novel's most vivid and fantastical passages.

The escape by [[athaleb-class|athaleb]] also serves a thematic function: it literalizes the ascent from darkness to light, from the inverted values of the Kosekin world to the conventional world the narrator seeks to rejoin. The creature that embodies the prehistoric strangeness of the subterranean realm becomes the vehicle of liberation from it.

## Prehistoric Context

The [[athaleb-class|athaleb]] is one of several prehistoric creatures inhabiting the subterranean ecosystem, supporting the novel's conceit that the cavern world preserves forms of life extinct on the surface. This element of the narrative reflects nineteenth-century scientific interests in paleontology and the contemporary excitement surrounding the discovery of dinosaur fossils. De Mille integrates these scientific threads into his adventure narrative, using the [[athaleb-class|athaleb]] both as spectacle and as plot mechanism.
