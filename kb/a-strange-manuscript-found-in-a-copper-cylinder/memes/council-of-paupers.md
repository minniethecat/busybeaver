## Overview

The **Council of Paupers** (also called the Council of State or Grand Council of Paupers) is the supreme governing body of the Kosekin. Composed of the most honored members of the pauper class, it serves as both legislature and high court — a "secret tribunal" that deliberates in the darkness of the caverns and whose decisions are absolute and without appeal.

> The Chief Pauper was the highest magistrate in the land, from whose opinion there was no appeal, and the other paupers here formed the Kosekin senate. Here, in imprisonment and darkness, they formed a secret tribunal and controlled everything. [029-002]

## Composition and Status

The Council consists of paupers — the highest social class in the inverted Kosekin hierarchy. Membership in the Council is the ultimate object of Kosekin ambition:

> They were objects of envy to all. All looked forward to this position as the highest object of human ambition, and the friends and relatives of those here rejoiced in their honor. [029-002]

The paradox of the Council's power is that, in a society that despises power and honors service, those who rule are by definition the lowest. The councilors exercise authority precisely because authority is considered degrading — an inversion that makes governance possible in a society whose values would seem to preclude it.

## Powers and Functions

### Deliberative Authority

The Council's powers are described as deliberative rather than executive. Executive functions are delegated to the Meleks and Athons, but always subordinated to the Council's will:

> Their powers were not executive, but deliberative. To the Meleks and Athons was left the exercise of authority, but their acts were always in subordination to the will of the paupers. [029-002]

### Judicial Function

The Council serves as the highest tribunal, as demonstrated when it examines the case of Adam More and Almah's escape attempt:

> On the next jom I had a visit from the Kohen Gadol. He informed me that the paupers had held a Council of State, in which they had made a special examination of our late flight. [028-008]

### Sentencing Authority

The Council has the power to decree life and death — or, in Kosekin terms, to bestow honors and blessings. It is the Council that adds the Mista Kosek sacrifice to More and Almah's sentence:

> "…by an exercise of unparalleled liberality, the Grand Council of Paupers have added this, as the last and best, to the high honors and rewards which they have decreed for you and Almah." [028-038]

## Narrative Function

The Council represents the institutional face of Kosekin society — the mechanism by which inverted values are translated into binding decisions. Its portrayal as a "secret tribunal" operating in darkness carries Gothic overtones while simultaneously satirizing the opacity of Victorian governance.
