# Lord Featherstone

## Role in the Frame Narrative

Lord Featherstone is one of the four English gentlemen aboard the yacht Falcon who discover and debate [[adam-more|Adam More]]'s manuscript. As the owner of the Falcon and host of the expedition, Featherstone provides the social and financial infrastructure for the narrative frame. 'Being weary of life in England, had taken a few congenial friends for a winter's cruise' (001-001). His languid curiosity — 'Paper boats! By Jove!' (001-006) — sets the tone of gentlemanly dilettantism that characterizes the frame.

## Key Passages

> This yacht Falcon was the property of Lord Featherstone, who, being weary of life in England, had taken a few congenial friends for a winter's cruise in these southern latitudes. (001-001)
