# Oxenden

## Role in the Frame Narrative

Oxenden is one of the four English gentlemen aboard the yacht Falcon who discover and debate [[adam-more|Adam More]]'s manuscript. As the most intellectually ambitious of the four readers, Oxenden develops the elaborate theory that the Kosekin language is related to Hebrew through Grimm's Law (017-065). His scholarly enthusiasm leads him to conclude that the Kosekin are 'a Semitic people' — specifically, the lost Ten Tribes of Israel (017-069). In Chapter XXVII, he preaches a sermon drawing moral lessons from More's narrative.

## Key Passages

> Oxenden was now thoroughly aroused, and seemed bent on making out the whole language to be Semitic. (017-069)
