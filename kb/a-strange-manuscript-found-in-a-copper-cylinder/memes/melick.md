# Melick

## Role in the Frame Narrative

Melick is one of the four English gentlemen aboard the yacht Falcon who discover and debate [[adam-more|Adam More]]'s manuscript. As the skeptic and humorist of the group, Melick questions the authenticity of More's manuscript and provides comic relief. His occupation with folding paper boats (001-006) signals his detachment from the earnest debates of his companions. As the voice of skepticism, Melick anticipates the reader's own doubts about the fantastic narrative.

## Key Passages

> "Paper boats! By Jove!" said Featherstone. "What for?" (001-006)
