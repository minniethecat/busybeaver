# Pierre Gassendi

**Pierre Gassendi** (1592–1655) was a French philosopher who revived materialist philosophy in its Epicurean form during the seventeenth century. His influence extended to England through his friendship with [[thomas-hobbes|Thomas Hobbes]], who came under Gassendi's philosophical influence (006-060).

## Revival of Epicurean Materialism

Gassendi played a key role in the seventeenth-century rehabilitation of [[epicurus|Epicurean]] philosophy, which had been largely suppressed during the Christian Middle Ages due to its association with atheism (006-060).

By recovering Epicurean atomism, Gassendi helped create an intellectual framework in which matter, rather than spirit, could be understood as the fundamental explanatory principle of natural phenomena (006-060).

## Influence on Hobbes

[[thomas-hobbes|Hobbes]], perhaps the most brilliant English thinker of the seventeenth century, was a freethinker and materialist who came under Gassendi's influence (006-060).

Gassendi's revival of materialism in its Epicurean shape provided Hobbes with philosophical resources for his own more radical system. However, while Gassendi sought to reconcile atomism with Christian faith, Hobbes became a champion of coercion rather than freedom of conscience (006-060).
