# Peter Annet

**Peter Annet** (1693–1769) was an English schoolmaster and deist writer who attempted to popularize freethought among the general public in the mid-18th century.

## Relation to Freedom of Thought

In J.B. Bury's "A History of Freedom of Thought", Annet is noted as a significant case in the history of persecution for freethought. Bury observes that among the earlier English rationalists, "apart from the case of Woolston) the only one who was punished was Peter Annet, a schoolmaster, who tried to popularize freethought and was sentenced for diffusing 'diabolical' opinions to the pillory and hard labor" [006-858]–[006-859].

Annet's case illustrates that while many deist writers escaped prosecution by addressing educated audiences, attempts to bring rationalist ideas to the broader public were met with harsher legal response. His punishment marked a limit-case in the relative tolerance of early 18th-century England.
