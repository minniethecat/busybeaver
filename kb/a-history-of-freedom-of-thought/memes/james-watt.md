# James Watt

**James Watt** (1736–1819) was a Scottish engineer and inventor whose improvements to the steam engine were central to the Industrial Revolution.

## Relation to Freedom of Thought

In J.B. Bury's "A History of Freedom of Thought", Watt is referenced in the context of late 18th-century natural theology. Bury cites Leslie Stephen's critique of William Paley's argument from design: Paley's conception of God had become "scientific and ingenious; he is superior to Watt or Priestley in devising mechanical and chemical contrivances, and is therefore made in the image of that generation of which Watt and Priestley were conspicuous lights" [006-760]–[006-762]. This illustrates how the mechanistic worldview, exemplified by figures like Watt, influenced even theological arguments of the period.
