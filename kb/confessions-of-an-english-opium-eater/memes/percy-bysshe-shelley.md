# Percy Bysshe Shelley

**Percy Bysshe Shelley** (1792–1822) was one of the major English Romantic poets, known for works such as *Ozymandias*, *[[ode-to-dejection|Ode to the West Wind]]*, and *Prometheus Unbound*. A radical in both politics and personal life, Shelley was a contemporary and acquaintance of [[thomas-de-quincey|Thomas De Quincey]], though the two were not close.

## Mention in the *Confessions*

De Quincey invokes Shelley's authority on the psychology of ageing in [Section 3](Section-3):

> "Certainly, Mr. Shelley is right in his notions about old age: unless powerfully counteracted by all sorts of opposite agencies, it is a miserable corrupter and blighter to the genial charities of the human heart."
>
> (003-158)

This citation appears in the context of De Quincey's recollection of receiving hospitality in [[wales|Wales]] from "sexagenarian Welsh Methodists" — a kindness that, he feared, would degenerate into mere charity once the novelty wore off.

## Literary Significance

Shelley's radical philosophy and visionary poetics placed him at the forefront of the [[romantic-autobiography|Romantic movement]]. His untimely death by drowning at age 29 cut short a career of extraordinary promise.
