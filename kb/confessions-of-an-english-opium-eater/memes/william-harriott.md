# William Harriott

**William Harriott** (fl. early 19th century) was a London magistrate and the author of the memoir *Struggles through Life*. He is mentioned in De Quincey's *Confessions* solely for his experiment with laudanum as a treatment for gout.

## Mention in the *Confessions*

In [Section 17](Section-17), De Quincey cites Harriott's published account as evidence of the extreme variability of opium's effects on different constitutions:

> "A London magistrate (Harriott's *Struggles through Life*, vol. iii. p. 391, third edition) has recorded that, on the first occasion of his trying laudanum for the gout he took *forty* drops, the next night *sixty*, and on the fifth night *eighty*, without any effect whatever; and this at an advanced age."
>
> (017-079)

De Quincey notes that even this remarkable tolerance is "sunk into a trifle" by another anecdote he withholds for a "projected medical treatise on opium."
