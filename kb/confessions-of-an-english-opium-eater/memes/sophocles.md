# Sophocles

**Sophocles** (c. 497–406 BCE) was one of the three great tragedians of classical Athens, along with Aeschylus and Euripides. His surviving plays — including *Oedipus Rex*, *Antigone*, and *Electra* — are masterpieces of dramatic structure and psychological depth, exploring themes of fate, free will, and the limits of human knowledge.

## Reference in the *Confessions*

De Quincey cites Sophocles in [Section 2](Section-2) as part of his argument about the philosophical treatment of suffering in classical literature. For De Quincey, the Athenian tragedians — Sophocles and Euripides — established a literary tradition in which extreme human agony could be examined with dignity and intellectual seriousness, a tradition he saw his own confessional narrative as extending into the modern era.
