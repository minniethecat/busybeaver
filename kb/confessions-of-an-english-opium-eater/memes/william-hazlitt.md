# William Hazlitt

**William Hazlitt** (1778–1830) was an English essayist, literary critic, and philosopher of the Romantic period. He is considered one of the greatest English critics, known for works such as *The Spirit of the Age* (1825) and *Table-Talk* (1821–22).

Hazlitt was a contemporary of De Quincey, Lamb, Coleridge, and Wordsworth, and moved in the same London literary circles. His prose style — direct, vigorous, and personal — influenced the development of the familiar essay in English literature.
