# English Mountains

## Overview

The rural setting of De Quincey's cottage where the Malay incident occurred — a space of domestic retreat contrasted with the urban intensity of London. De Quincey resided in a cottage situated amongst these mountains during the period when the Malay visitor arrived at his door. (010-089)

## The Malay Incident

The mountains serve as the backdrop for one of the most memorable episodes in the *Confessions*: the unexpected arrival of a Malay traveller at De Quincey's remote cottage. The contrast between the English mountain setting and the Asiatic visitor is central to the scene's strangeness:

> One day a Malay knocked at my door. What business a Malay could have to transact amongst English mountains I cannot conjecture; but possibly he was on his road to a seaport about forty miles distant.
>
> (010-088)(010-089)(010-090)(010-091)

The servant who answered the door was a young girl "born and bred amongst the mountains, who had never seen an Asiatic dress of any sort; his turban therefore confounded her not a little." (010-092)(010-093) The language barrier proved equally absolute: "his attainments in English were exactly of the same extent as hers in the Malay language." (010-094)(010-095)

## Narrative Significance

The English Mountains setting creates a deliberate juxtaposition in De Quincey's narrative — the remote, pastoral English landscape invaded by an emissary of the Orient. This geographical contrast prefigures the later opium dreams in which Asiatic imagery overwhelms De Quincey's consciousness. (010-088)(010-093)
