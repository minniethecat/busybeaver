# Jonas Hanway

**Jonas Hanway** (1712–1786) was an English philanthropist, traveller, and writer. He is remembered for his travel writings, his advocacy for social reforms (including the protection of chimney-sweeps and the improvement of infant welfare), and for being one of the first Englishmen to carry an umbrella in London — an eccentricity for which he was famously mocked.

## Mention in the *Confessions*

In [Section 11](Section-11), De Quincey invokes Hanway as the target of Dr. Johnson's polemical defense of tea:

> "I would have joined Dr. Johnson in a *bellum internecinum* against Jonas Hanway, or any other impious person, who should presume to disparage it."
>
> (011-054)

De Quincey aligns himself with Johnson's love of tea as a beverage "always the favourite beverage of the intellectual," contrasting it with wine and opium.
