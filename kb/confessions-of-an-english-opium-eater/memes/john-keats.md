# John Keats

**John Keats** (1795–1821) was one of the principal poets of the English Romantic movement. Despite his short life — he died of tuberculosis at the age of 25 — Keats produced a body of work that places him among the greatest English poets. His major works include the odes "Ode to a Nightingale," "Ode on a Grecian Urn," and "To Autumn."

Keats was a contemporary of De Quincey, though they moved in somewhat different literary circles. Keats's exploration of beauty, mortality, and the imagination shares thematic ground with the Romantic sensibilities evident in De Quincey's prose.
