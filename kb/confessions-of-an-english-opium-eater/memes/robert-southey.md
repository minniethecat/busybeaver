# Robert Southey

**Robert Southey** (1774–1843) was an English poet of the Romantic school, a prolific historian and biographer, and Poet Laureate from 1813 until his death. He was one of the "Lake Poets," along with William Wordsworth and Samuel Taylor Coleridge.

## Relation to De Quincey's Circle

Southey was a contemporary of De Quincey and moved in the same Lake District literary circle. While not a central figure in the *Confessions*, Southey's name appears in the wider network of Romantic-era literary figures connected to De Quincey, Wordsworth, and Coleridge. He was known for his epic poems (*Thalaba the Destroyer*, *The Curse of Kehama*), his prose works (*The Life of Nelson*), and his conservative political views, which later put him at odds with the more radical Romantics.
