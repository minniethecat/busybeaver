# Charles Baudelaire

**Charles Baudelaire** (1821–1867) was a French poet, critic, and translator, best known for his collection of poems *Les Fleurs du mal* (The Flowers of Evil). He was a key figure in the transition from Romanticism to Symbolism and is often considered the first modern urban poet.

## Connection to De Quincey

Baudelaire's *Les Paradis Artificiels* (Artificial Paradises, 1860) was profoundly influenced by De Quincey's *Confessions of an English Opium-Eater*. The second part of Baudelaire's book, "Un Mangeur d'Opium" (An Opium-Eater), is largely a translation and free adaptation of De Quincey's text, interwoven with Baudelaire's own philosophical commentary on drugs, creativity, and the human condition. Through Baudelaire's mediation, De Quincey's opium confessions reached a wide Continental audience and influenced subsequent French literary treatments of altered consciousness.
