# Thomas Hope

**Thomas Hope** (1769–1831) was a British writer, philosopher, art collector, and interior designer. He is best known as the author of the novel *Anastasius* (1819), a picaresque tale of a Greek adventurer that was initially published anonymously and widely attributed to Lord Byron.

De Quincey held *Anastasius* in high regard, discussing it in his writings and comparing its confessional mode to his own autobiographical project. Hope's novel shares with De Quincey's *Confessions* an interest in cosmopolitan experience, psychological depth, and the darker aspects of human nature.
