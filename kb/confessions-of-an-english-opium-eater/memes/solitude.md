# Solitude

## Definition

**Solitude** occupies a paradoxical position in the *Confessions*: it is simultaneously the opium-eater's natural habitat and the condition that accelerates his ruin. De Quincey presents solitude as a predisposition — "they court privacy and solitude" (001-032) — but also as an effect of the drug: opium "does not move me to seek solitude, and much less to seek inactivity" (008-138) in its early phases, yet in its later stages the opium-eater "seeks solitude and silence, as indispensable conditions" (009-076). This self-reinforcing cycle — solitude enabling consumption, consumption deepening solitude — is one of the text's most psychologically acute observations.

## In the *Confessions*

### Solitude as Predisposition

De Quincey opens the *Confessions* by characterising the opium-eater as one who naturally inclines toward solitude: "they court privacy and solitude: and even in their choice of a wife they show the same disposition" (001-032). This is presented not as pathology but as temperament — the opium-eater is constitutionally different from the convivial drinker.

### Solitude as Drug Effect

Initially, De Quincey insists, opium does not isolate: "it does not move me to seek solitude, and much less to seek inactivity" (008-138). This claim supports his broader argument that opium, unlike alcohol, does not degrade social functioning. But the trajectory of the narrative belies this early optimism. By Section 9, he acknowledges that the confirmed opium-eater "seeks solitude and silence, as indispensable conditions of his daily existence" (009-076). The circle closes: solitude enables the habit, the habit demands solitude.

### Solitude and the Dreams

The opium dreams themselves are solitary experiences — visions that cannot be shared, that isolate the dreamer even from those who share his bed. The beloved M., who "defrauds herself of sleep" (007-056), can witness the external signs of the nightmares but cannot enter the dreamer's private hell. Solitude thus becomes not merely social but existential.
