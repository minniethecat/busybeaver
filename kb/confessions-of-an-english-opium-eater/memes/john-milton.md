# John Milton

**John Milton** (1608–1674) was an English poet, polemicist, and civil servant, best known for his epic poem *Paradise Lost* (1667) and its sequel *Paradise Regained* (1671). Blind for the last two decades of his life, Milton dictated his greatest works to amanuenses.

## References in the *Confessions*

De Quincey invokes Milton on several occasions, particularly in connection with the architecture of his opium dreams. In [Section 12](Section-12), De Quincey compares his dream-visions to Milton's descriptions of Pandemonium in *Paradise Lost*, noting the "vast Gothic halls" and endless architectural spaces that characterized both Milton's hell and his own opium-induced reveries.

> Of late, if I have felt moved by anything it has been by the grand lamentations of Samson Agonistes, or the great harmonies of the Satanic speeches in Paradise Regained, when read aloud by myself.
>
> (012-017)

Milton's *Paradise Regained* (006-018) is also cited in [Section 15](Section-15) as De Quincey reflects on themes of temptation, endurance, and the conquest of suffering — motifs that run through both Milton's sequel and De Quincey's own narrative of opium's trials.
