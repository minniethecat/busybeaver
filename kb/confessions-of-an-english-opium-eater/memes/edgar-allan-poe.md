# Edgar Allan Poe

**Edgar Allan Poe** (1809–1849) was an American writer, poet, editor, and literary critic. He is best known for his tales of mystery and the macabre, and is widely regarded as a central figure of Romanticism in the United States and of American literature as a whole.

Poe was significantly influenced by De Quincey's *Confessions of an English Opium-Eater*. The psychological intensity, first-person confessional voice, and exploration of altered consciousness in Poe's tales — particularly "The Imp of the Perverse" and "The Black Cat" — owe a direct debt to De Quincey's pioneering work.
