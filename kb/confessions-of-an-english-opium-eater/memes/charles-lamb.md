# Charles Lamb

**Charles Lamb** (1775–1834) was an English essayist and poet, best known for his *Essays of Elia* and his tales from Shakespeare co-authored with his sister Mary Lamb. A central figure in the Romantic literary circle of early nineteenth-century London, Lamb was acquainted with many of the major writers of his era, including Coleridge, Wordsworth, and De Quincey.

Lamb's essays are noted for their wit, warmth, and nostalgic charm. His personal life was marked by tragedy — his sister Mary suffered from mental illness and killed their mother in 1796, after which Charles became her guardian for life.
