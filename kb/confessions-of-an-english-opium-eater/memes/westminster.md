# Westminster

**Westminster** is a central area of London, the seat of the British Parliament and government. In the geography of the *Confessions*, Westminster represents the world of public life and respectability from which De Quincey's opium-eating self has been exiled — or has exiled himself.

## The Public and the Private

De Quincey's London is divided between the monumental spaces of power (Westminster, the Houses of Parliament) and the hidden spaces of poverty and intoxication (Oxford Street at night, the Soho druggist's shop, the attic rooms where he takes his opium). Westminster appears in the narrative not as a place De Quincey inhabits but as a symbol of the world he observes from a distance — the world of "normal" life, civic duty, and public reputation that the confessional mode simultaneously courts and subverts.
