# Lord Byron

**George Gordon Byron, 6th Baron Byron** (1788–1824) was the most celebrated and controversial of the English Romantic poets. His works — including *Childe Harold's Pilgrimage*, *Don Juan*, and *Manfred* — combined passionate lyricism with biting satire and created the archetype of the "Byronic hero": brooding, rebellious, and haunted by a dark past.

## Byron, De Quincey, and Opium

Byron and De Quincey moved in overlapping circles but were not close. Byron experimented with opium recreationally and referenced it in his poetry, most notably in *Don Juan* and *The Corsair*. His attitude toward the drug was more casual than De Quincey's tormented relationship, though both writers contributed to the Romantic mythology of opium as a gateway to heightened consciousness.

Byron's early death in 1824 — from fever while fighting for Greek independence — made him a Romantic martyr and left De Quincey as one of the surviving voices of the opium experience in English letters.
