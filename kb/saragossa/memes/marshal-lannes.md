# Marshal Lannes

## Overview

Marshal Jean Lannes (1769–1809), Duke of Montebello, was one of [[napoleon|Napoleon]]'s most trusted commanders. He took over [[siege-warfare|the siege]] of Saragossa from [[marshal-moncey|Marshal Moncey]] and pressed the assault with renewed vigor (012-001)(020-004), ultimately forcing the city's surrender in February 1809 after epidemic and [[hunger|starvation]] had broken the defenders' capacity to resist.

## Role in the Narrative

Lannes appears briefly in the novel as the ultimate antagonist—the commander whose arrival signals the end of any hope of [[hope|relief]]. He represents the full weight of Napoleonic military power directed against a city that refused to surrender (031-026).

## Historical Context

Lannes was one of [[napoleon|Napoleon]]'s original marshals and fought in most of the Empire's major campaigns. He was mortally wounded at the Battle of Aspern-Essling in May 1809 (031-028), just months after the fall of Zaragoza.
