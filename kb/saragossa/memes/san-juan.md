## San Juan de los Panetes in the Siege of Saragossa

San Juan de los Panetes was a church in Saragossa that, like many religious buildings during the siege, was repurposed for military use. Located near the bank of the Ebro River, it became a critical site for ammunition production.

### Powder Mill and Cartridge Factory

At nightfall during the siege, Araceli found a key figure in the powder mill near San Juan de los Panetes, indicating the church had been converted into a facility for producing gunpowder. (013-001) The site also served as a cartridge-making center, where friars, canons, and civil officials worked alongside one another to supply the defenders. (014-001)

The church's location near the riverbank made it a natural gathering point for troops awaiting orders: "We went to the bank of the river near San Juan de los Panetas, and took up our place on a mound, awaiting orders." (006-016)

### Dual Purpose

Like La Seo and the Pilar, San Juan de los Panetes served a dual role during the siege — it remained a place of worship while simultaneously functioning as an ammunition workshop. The wounded were carried to La Seo and the Pilar, while those capable of work made cartridges at San Juan de los Panetes, reflecting the total mobilization of Saragossa's population and infrastructure.
