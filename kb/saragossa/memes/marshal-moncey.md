# Marshal Moncey

## Overview

Marshal Bon Adrien Jeannot de Moncey (1754–1842) was the initial French commander at the second [[siege-warfare|siege]] of Saragossa. Galdós records his deployment with stark numerical simplicity, conveying the overwhelming force arrayed against the city.

## Role in the Narrative

Moncey appears in a single but memorable passage:

> Marshal Moncey, who was the general in command, placed himself, with three divisions, near the canal, and on both sides of the Huerva. Forty thousand men besieged us. (004-013)

His replacement by the more aggressive [[marshal-lannes|Lannes]] signals [[napoleon|Napoleon]]'s impatience with [[siege-warfare|the siege]]'s slow progress.
