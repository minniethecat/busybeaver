# Calle del Rincón

## Overview

**Calle del Rincón** was a street in the [[las-tenerias|Las Tenerías]] quarter of Saragossa, mentioned alongside the [[calle-quemada|Calle Quemada]] as one of the streets reduced to ruins during the siege:

> The Calle Quemada and the Calle del Rincón, both in ruins, as far as the Seminary. (001-004)

## Location

The street lay in the Las Tenerías quarter, extending from the area of the Calle Quemada toward the [[seminary|Seminary]]. Its proximity to the Seminary — a key defensive position during the siege — placed it in one of the most heavily contested zones of the city.

## Role in the Siege

Calle del Rincón appears in the narrative as part of the devastated eastern approach to Saragossa. Along with the Calle Quemada, it was among the streets most thoroughly destroyed by the fighting, reflecting the intensity of the French assault through Las Tenerías and the desperate house-to-house defense mounted by the Saragossans.

The destruction of entire streets was characteristic of the siege: both sides burned buildings they could not hold, and artillery fire leveled large sections of the suburb. The mention of Calle del Rincón alongside Calle Quemada places it at the heart of this devastation.
