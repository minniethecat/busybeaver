# Hope

## Overview

Hope in *Saragossa* functions as a double-edged sword. Rumors of approaching Spanish relief armies sustain the defenders' morale during the darkest days of [[siege-warfare|the siege]]. But when those hopes prove groundless—invented by journalists or born of wishful thinking—the psychological letdown is devastating. The novel traces hope as a fragile collective emotion, easily manufactured and easily shattered.

## Manifestation in the Novel

The most dramatic episode of hope occurs when the city celebrates rumors of approaching Spanish armies:

> The people crowded into the Plaza de la Seo, and in front of the Magdalene arch, waiting until the Gazette should appear; and at last it came out, cheering everybody's spirits, and making all hearts palpitate with hope. (011-006)

The deflation is equally dramatic:

> After a few days had passed, it was known that the hoped-for forces and the powerful armies that were coming to free us were all mists of our imaginations, and especially of that of the journalist who invented them. There were no such armies of any sort roaming about to help us. (012-002)

## Key Passages

Even in the face of constant bombardment, defenders clung to hope through the physical resilience of their fortifications:

> Yet, in the meantime, the fortress had not been reduced to powder, and, dead or alive, its defenders had hope. Fresh troops were sent there, because the battalions working there since morning were decimated. (010-015)

Hope also operates at the personal level. [[augustine-montoria|Augustine]]'s commission to [[gabriel-araceli|Gabriel]]—to tell [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla]] of his fate if he dies—is prefaced with "I hope you will fulfil carefully like a good friend" (005-009), showing how hope permeates even intimate relationships.

## Historical Context

The manipulation of hope through official gazettes and rumors was a recognized feature of [[siege-warfare|siege]] warfare. Commanders on both sides understood that morale—sustained by hope of relief—was as critical as ammunition. The eventual recognition that no relief was coming marked a turning point in [[siege-warfare|the siege]].
