# Trinitarios District

The Trinitarios District was a quarter of Saragossa situated near the [[carmen-college|Carmen College]] and the Portillo gate. Its position in the western sector of the city placed it within the zone of operations during the siege.

## Location and Context

The district is mentioned in connection with the broader geography of besieged Saragossa. It lay near the orchard that bordered Carmen College, one of several religious institutions that ringed the old city and became critical military positions during the [[siege-warfare|siege]].

## The Defensive Line

The Convent of the Trinitarios formed the anchor of a critical defensive line that stretched from [[santa-engracia|Santa Engracia]] to the Trinitarios convent, described as "a line which seemed the least exposed" of the French approaches (004-015). A straight wall extended from the adjoining building to the convent itself, creating a continuous fortified front (003-013).
> From this building to the Convent of the Trinitarios extended a straight
wall, with battlements along all its length and with a good pathway in
the centre. This was protected by a deep moat that reached to the famous
field of Las Eras, scene of the heroic deeds of the fifteenth of June.
>
> (003-013)


Within this perimeter, artillery stood guard alongside the defenders: at the Trinitarios position, as at other strongpoints, "the artillery stood guard with the same determination" as elsewhere along the line (006-006). During the height of the siege, a long procession of men and women stretched from San José all the way to the Trinitarios, carrying supplies and reinforcing the defensive positions (008-021).

The French recognized the sector's importance and mounted attacks on the Trinitarios convent, hoping to breach the Spanish line (021-024). The district's position astride the route to Santa Engracia made it one of the approaches through which defenders moved during sorties and redeployments (016-032).

## The Convent as Supply Hub

Beyond its military role, the Convent of Trinitarios served as a logistical center. Sacks of flour were transported "from the Convent of Trinitarios to the headquarters of supply" (012-014), making it a node in the city's precarious supply network during the encirclement.

## During the Siege

Like other districts of Saragossa, Trinitarios was drawn into the urban combat that characterized the [[second-siege-of-saragossa|Second Siege]]. Its proximity to the Portillo gate — one of the main defensive bastions — meant it lay within the path of French advances from the western approaches. The neighborhood, like much of the city, suffered under [[french-artillery-bombardment|French bombardment]] and the privations of encirclement.
