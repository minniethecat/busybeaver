Mallorca is the largest island of the Balearic archipelago in the Mediterranean Sea. In the context of the [[second-siege-of-saragossa|siege of Saragossa]], Mallorca is significant as the birthplace of [[don-jeronimo-candiola|Don Jerónimo de Candiola]], a wealthy moneylender and one of the most controversial figures in the city.

## Candiola's Mallorcan Origin

Candiola's Mallorcan origin set him apart from the native Aragonese citizens. As [[augustine-montoria|Augustine Montoria]] noted, Candiola was "a native of Mallorca, if I am not mistaken" (005-030),
> "His name is Don Jerónimo de Candiola; he is a native of Mallorca, if
I am not mistaken."
>
> (005-030)
 a detail that underscored his status as an outsider in [[saragossa|Saragossa]].

His foreign origins, combined with his avarice and refusal to contribute to the city's defense, contributed to the widespread contempt in which he was held. The label "Mallorcan" became a term of abuse in the mouths of his enemies.

## The "Mallorcan Jew" Epithet

Candiola's Mallorcan origin became fused with anti-Semitic prejudice in the epithet "the Mallorcan Jew," repeatedly hurled at him by his detractors. The mob accused him: "You have the blood of a Mallorcan Jew. You are no son of this noble city" (012-052).

This slur followed the Candiola family even to the next generation. [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla Candiola]], Don Jerónimo's daughter, lamented that others saw in her nothing but "the daughter of the one whom they call the Mallorcan Jew," and wished she could go away from Saragossa where no one would know her father (016-009).

The epithet was weaponized by figures like [[don-jose-montoria|Don José de Montoria]], who laughed noisily and declared, "We shall see how the Mallorcan Jew keeps them off, if he is still alive till he puts his money in a place of safety" (016-036).

## Strategic Context

The island of Mallorca itself remained outside the main theater of the [[peninsular-war|Peninsular War]], though the Balearic Islands served as an important strategic base for Spanish forces and a refuge for those displaced by the French occupation of mainland Spain.
