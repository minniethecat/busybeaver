# La Seo

**La Seo** (the Cathedral of Saragossa) is one of the city's two great churches, alongside the [[Basilica of El Pilar]]. During the Second Siege, it served as a refuge for the wounded and displaced, and was a prominent landmark in the urban geography of the conflict.

## Role During the Siege

As the bombardment intensified and the [[Hospital of Saragossa]] became untenable, the sick and wounded were evacuated to the city's two great churches:

> "The women ran to care for the sick, who were all carried off to Del Pilar and to La Seo." (002-023)

The cathedral thus became an improvised hospital, its vast interior providing shelter from the French artillery bombardment that rained down on the city.

## Urban Landmark

La Seo stood as one of the defining reference points of Saragossa's ecclesiastical geography. Together with El Pilar, it represented the spiritual heart of the city. The two cathedrals anchored the urban fabric around which the siege unfolded, and their survival — while surrounding buildings were reduced to rubble — was seen as providential by the defenders.

## Historical Significance

The Cathedral of the Savior dates from the 12th century and stands on the site of the former main mosque of Saraqusta (Islamic Zaragoza). Its architectural style blends Romanesque, Gothic, Mudéjar, and Baroque elements, reflecting the city's layered history. During the Napoleonic era, it was one of the most prominent religious edifices in Aragón.
