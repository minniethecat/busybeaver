# Don Mariano Cerezo

## Overview

**Don Mariano Cerezo** (also spelled **Cereso**) was a citizen of Saragossa who took part in the city's defense during the [[siege-warfare|siege]]. He is mentioned alongside [[don-santiago-sas|Don Santiago Sas]] among the defenders of the [[castle-aljaferia|Castle Aljafería]], and his name appears repeatedly in the narrative among the notable families of the resisting city:

> If Don Mariano Cereso had not defended the Castle Aljafería. (002-023)

## Role in the Siege

Cerezo was associated with the defense of the [[castle-aljaferia|Castle Aljafería]], the ancient Moorish fortress that served as a key strongpoint in Saragossa's defensive system. His role there placed him among the prominent citizen-defenders — men who were not professional soldiers but who took up arms to resist the French assault.

He appears among a list of Saragossa's most distinguished defenders:

> Don Santiago Sas, Don Mariano Cereso, Don Lorenzo Calvo, Don Marcos Simonó — all brave warriors of the resistance. (002-025)

## Among the Defending Families

The Cerezo family is mentioned alongside other notable Saragossan houses — the Montorias, the Sases, the Salameros — as families who contributed to the city's defense:

> The Montorias, the Ceresos, the Sases, the Salameros — all took up arms in the city's defense. (018-014)

Cerezo's participation reflects the broad mobilization of Saragossan society: clergy, merchants, laborers, and gentlemen like him all served in the improvised defense that held off two French sieges. Later, his name appears again in a list of civilians who bore arms, alongside [[don-santiago-sas|Sas]], La Casa, Pidrafita, and Escorigüela:

> Cereso, Sas, La Casa, Pidrafita, Escorigüela — all took up arms. (028-001)
