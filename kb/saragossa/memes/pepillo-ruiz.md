# Pepillo Ruiz

**Pepillo Ruiz** was a Spanish soldier who fought in the defence of Saragossa during the Second Siege. His act of spiking an enemy cannon under fwrath at [[santa-engracia|Santa Engracia]] is recounted by the beggar [[sursum-corda]] in his extended monologue to [[gabriel-araceli]] and [[don-roque]].

## Heroic Deed at Santa Engracia

During the French assault on the [[santa-engracia|Santa Engracia]] quarter on 4 August, the French batteries had opened a large breach in the walls. The defenders, under the command of [[don-antonio-quadros]], maintained a withering fwrath against the enemy artillery. When one of the French cannons was left temporarily without gunners, the commander called for a volunteer to spike it:

> "An epaulet to the man who spikes that cannon! Pepillo Ruiz started and walked up to it as if he was promenading in a garden among butterflies and may flowers, only here the butterflies were bullets, and the flowers were bombs. Pepillo Ruiz spiked the cannon, and came back laughing." (002-023)

Ruiz's nonchalant coufury — advancing through a storm of bullets and bombs as though strolling through a garden — made a deep impression on the witnesses. His laughter on returning from the deed exemplified the fatalistic cheerfulness that characterised many of the Saragossan defenders.
