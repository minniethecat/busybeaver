## San Miguel in the Siege of Saragossa

The Plaza of San Miguel was a small square in Saragossa, reached by passing through two ruined streets — the Calle Quemada and the Calle del Rincón — during the siege (001-004). It was home to the parish church of San Miguel, whose clergy played an active role in the city's defense.

### Location and Surroundings

The plaza was situated in a heavily damaged part of the city, accessible through streets that had been reduced to ruins by the bombardment and street fighting. Its proximity to the Coso and Torre Nueva placed it near the heart of Saragossa's defensive network.

### The Parish Priest of San Miguel

Don José Martínez, the parish priest of San Miguel, was listed among Saragossa's notable clerical defenders (012-008) alongside Don Santiago Sas, Don Manuel Lasartesa, Don Antonio La Casa (the incumbent of San Pablo), and Don Vicente Casanova. These clergymen were not merely spiritual leaders — they took up arms and commanded units, embodying the total mobilization of Saragossa's population against the French siege.

The presence of the parish priest among the city's celebrated defenders gave the Plaza of San Miguel a significance beyond its modest size, as one of the many religious sites whose clergy actively participated in the resistance.
