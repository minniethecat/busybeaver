# San Diego

The **College of San Diego** was a religious college in Saragossa located opposite the [[Convent of San Francisco]]. Its orchard and underground passages made it a significant position in the subterranean warfare of the siege's final phase.

## Position and Layout

San Diego stood opposite San Francisco, with the [[Casa de los Duendes]] between them. The college had "its orchard surrounded by high mud walls which opened upon irregular and narrow alleys" (022-019). These alleys served as patrol routes for sentinels and troop movements between the picket lines.

## Underground Passages

A critical feature of San Diego was its underground connection to the Convent of San Francisco. The passage ran beneath the Casa de los Duendes:

> "They have a part of that building. Until now they have not been able to reach the wine-cellars of the Convent of San Francisco. If, by bad luck, they have discovered that the passage from San Diego to San Francisco is easy by the way under this house, it is probable that this is the passage that is being opened now." (022-021)

[[augustine-montoria]] voiced this concern to [[gabriel-araceli]], who heard "muffled blows under us, in the depths of the earth" (022-020) — the sound of French miners advancing from San Diego towards San Francisco.

## French Occupation

The French held at least part of San Diego, using it as a base for mining operations. The underground galleries they dug from the college threatened to bypass the Spanish defences at San Francisco, creating a constant state of alert for the defenders.
