# Assault on Santa Mónica

## Overview

The French assault on the Convent of Santa Mónica, vigorously defended by the volunteers of Huesca.

## Context

This event is part of the Peninsular War (1807–1814) and specifically [[siege-warfare|the siege]] of Saragossa as depicted in Galdós's *Saragossa: A Story of Spanish Valor*.

## Narrative Account

> "The French worked also to gain Santa Mónica, the convent situated in a line with Las Tenerías, a little to the north of the Calle de Pabostre; but its walls offered a strong resistance. The volunteers of Huesca defended it vigorously; and, after repeated attacks, the besiegers left the assault for another day."
>
> (018-003)
