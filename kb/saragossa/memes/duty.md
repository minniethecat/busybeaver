# Duty

## Overview

Duty in *Saragossa* is the invisible force that transforms civilians into soldiers and cowards into heroismes. It is both externally imposed—by commanders' sabre-cuts, by social [[hope|expectation]]—and internally felt, as an inexplicable impulse that "all feel without anyone's knowing whence the mysterious force emanates." The novel traces how duty operates at the threshold between coercion and conscience.

## Manifestation in the Novel

The most vivid depiction of duty comes during the [[resistance|defense]] of the redoubt on January 11, when [[manuela-sancho|Manuela Sancho]]'s example triggers a collective transformation:

> After Manuela Sancho, first one, then another, then many hurried, then all, urged on by the leaders whose sabre-cuts had prodded us to the point of duty. This portentous transformation came about by the impulse of every man's heart obeying sentiments which all feel without anyone's knowing whence the mysterious force emanates. (010-024)

The passage captures the dual nature of duty: external compulsion (sabre-cuts) merged with internal motivation (the heart's impulse). [[gabriel-araceli|Gabriel]]'s honest confession—"I do not know why we were cowards, nor why we were brave a few moments later"—refuses to romanticize duty as a fixed character trait.

## Key Passages

Duty is also enforced through public shaming. An old man berates young men who shirk their responsibilities:

> "Here you can see what we have been getting ready this evening. Look! There's where those who do not do their duty will be entertained. On with you! I who am old never get tired, but you young healthy men act as if you were made of putty." (014-022)

The language of duty permeates even personal relationships. [[augustine-montoria|Augustine]] and [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla]] use "friends on duty" as a cover story to escape paternal supervision:

> "We will go now. My father will march himself off. Go and tell him that we are going to relieve two friends on duty who have a sick one in their family and wish us to see him." (014-039)

[[don-jeronimo-candiola|Don Jerónimo Candiola]] inverts the concept, demanding that the authorities do *their* duty to protect his property:

> "If the authorities would send a company of engineers here, as it is their duty to do—" (019-016)

## Related Characters

- [[don-jeronimo-candiola]] — character who demands others' duty while evading his own

## Historical Context

The concept of duty in the Peninsular War context drew on both classical republican virtue and emerging Romantic ideals of [[sacrifice|self-sacrifice]] for the nation. The [[resistance|defense]] of Saragossa, in which civilians bore military duties without formal obligation, exemplified this fusion of traditional and modern notions of civic duty.
