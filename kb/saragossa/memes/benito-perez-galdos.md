**Benito Pérez Galdós** (1843–1920) was the preeminent Spanish novelist of the 19th century, often compared to Balzac and Dickens for the scope and realism of his work. He authored *Saragossa* (*Zaragoza*) (P01-002), the novel from which this wiki draws its source material. The book is the sixth in his monumental *Episodios Nacionales* series.

## Literary Context

Galdós wrote the *Episodios Nacionales* as a series of 46 historical novels chronicling Spanish history from the Battle of Trafalgar (1805) through the Restoration period (1880s). The first series, of which *Saragossa* is a part, covers the Peninsular War against [[napoleon|Napoleon]]. Published in 1874, *Saragossa* draws on eyewitness accounts to recreate the desperate [[second-siege-of-saragossa|Second Siege of Saragossa]] of 1808–1809.

## Narrative Technique

Galdós employed a first-person narrator — [[gabriel-araceli|Gabriel de Araceli]], an aged veteran recounting his youthful adventures — to give the historical events an intimate, personal dimension. This technique allowed Galdós to blend documented history with fictional characters, weaving figures like [[augustine-montoria|Augustine Montoria]] and [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla Candiola]] into the historical record alongside real personages such as [[palafox|General Palafox]].

## Historical Method

Galdós was known for his meticulous research, incorporating eyewitness accounts and historical documents into his fiction. His portrayal of the siege draws on the collective memory of the event, presenting both the [[heroism]] and the [[suffering]] of the besieged city with unflinching realism. The novel captures not only military events but also the social tensions within Saragossa — the courage of the [[aragon|Aragonese]] people, the [[sacrifice]] of the clergy and women, and the darker currents of greed and betrayal exemplified by [[don-jeronimo-candiola|Don Jerónimo de Candiola]].
