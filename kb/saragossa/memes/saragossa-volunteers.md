The **Saragossa Volunteers** were a local militia composed of citizens of Saragossa who served in the city's garrison during the second siege. They represent the popular resistance that characterized the defense.

## Composition

The volunteers were drawn from the civilian population of Saragossa: "Numbers of Saragossan volunteers were with us in the garrison" (009-003). They fought alongside regular army units and other regional volunteer battalions.

## Notable Members

The second company included a figure of some local importance: "in the second company of Saragossa volunteers, an important man" (009-012), indicating that men of social standing served alongside common citizens.

## Role in the Garrison

The volunteers formed part of the mixed force defending Saragossa, which included regular regiments, regional volunteer battalions, and local militias like this unit. Their presence in the garrison alongside the narrator's battalion underscores the city-wide mobilization.
