# Fortifications and Defensive Positions

Saragossa was not a purpose-built fortress. Its defense rested on improvised fortifications: convent walls loopholed for musketry, street barricades, earthen batteries, and the ancient Roman and medieval walls that still ringed parts of the city.
This page indexes every defensive position mentioned in the narrative.

## Notes

The city's key defensive points included the Portillo and Carmen gates, the Aljafería Castle, the Torrero heights, and the fortified convents of San José and Santa Engracia.
Many of these positions changed hands multiple times during the second siege.
