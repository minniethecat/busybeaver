The **Bombardment of Saragossa** was the sustained French artillery campaign that opened the second siege, raining shells and bombs across the city.

## Onset

The bombardment began without warning. Don José de Montoria announced its commencement: "I believe that the bombardment is going to begin" (010-002). Moments later the shells were falling: "Here it comes. One bomb! Another!" (010-008), capturing the sudden transition to siege conditions.
> "I believe that the bombardment is going to begin," he said to me.
"Here it comes. One bomb! Another!"
>
> (010-002, 010-008)


## Impact on the City

The bombardment damaged buildings across Saragossa. Families fled the harder-hit areas: "of those least damaged by the bombardment, many families had left it" (015-003). Churches, convents, and civilian houses all suffered from the artillery fire that preceded the French infantry assaults.

## Defensive Counter-Fire

The Spanish defenders responded with their own batteries positioned at key points around the city perimeter. The exchange of fire between the French attackers and Spanish positions continued throughout the siege's opening phase.
