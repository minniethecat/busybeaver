The **Botanical Garden** of Saragossa was the site of a defensive battery during the second siege. It served as an auxiliary artillery position behind the main defensive lines.

## Battery Position

The garden housed one of the inner defensive batteries. Alongside Los Mártires and the Torre del Pino, it was described as one of the batteries that "were less important than the two bodies holding the advanced positions, and served as auxiliaries" (009-002).

## Combat Operations

French forces targeted the garden's defenses: "they concentrated their fire on the batteries of Los Mártires and the Botanical Garden, so they continued operations with greater vigor on that side" (011-001). This suggests the garden's battery drew significant enemy attention during assaults.

## Strategic Role

As an auxiliary position, the Botanical Garden battery provided supporting fire for the front-line defenses. Its location within the city meant it served as a fallback position and a source of reinforcement fire for the outer batteries.

## Flanking Battery

> "They could not establish themselves there because they were flanked by the batteries of Los Mártires and the Botanical Garden, so they continued operations by mining, in order to possess themselves of those two points."
