# Monte Torrero

**Monte Torrero** was a strategic hill outside the southern walls of Saragossa. Its elevated position made it essential for any force seeking to dominate the city. The hill was the site of one of the opening engagements of the Second Siege and, once captured by the French, became their principal artillery platform.

## Strategic Importance

The French recognised that neither Monte Torrero nor the Arrabal suburb could be bypassed. Both were "points without whose possession it was impossible to dream of conquering the valiant city" (004-005). The hill commanded the southern approaches and offered direct lines of fire into the urban centre.

## The Assault of 21 December

On the morning of 21 December 1808, the French "attacking simultaneously and with great vigor Monte Torrero, and the Arrabal, the suburb on the left of the Ebro" (004-005). Ten thousand Spanish defenders under [[general-saint-march]] and General O'Neill held the Torrero positions. Forces included "the volunteers of Bourbon, Castile, Campo Segorbino, of Alicante, and of Soria, the sharpshooters of Fernando VII, the Murcia regiment, and other bodies" (007-006).

Despite the numerical strength, the position could not be held without the occupation of supporting points like Buena Vista and Casa Blanca. The defending troops "retired slowly and in the best order, retreating by the Puente de América, and carrying with them all the pieces of artillery except one" (007-006).

## Abandonment by Calculation

The loss of Torrero was a calculated sacrifice rather than a defeat. As the narrative observes:

> "If Torrero was abandoned, it was by calculation and not on account of weakness. Alone, embarrassed, deserted, without external defences, without forces or forts, Saragossa renewed again her earthworks, her defences of bricks, her bastions of mud heaped up the evening before." (007-009)

## French Use

Once in French hands, Monte Torrero became the enemy's artillery base. Cannon from the Torrero bombarded the city centre throughout the siege. The French also used it as a staging area for reinforcements, as during the Spanish sortie of 31 December when "they beat the drum in Monte Torrero" to summon cavalry (009-017).

## The Sortie of 31 December

[[Renovales]] led a sortie against French positions from "the Torrero to the Muela road" while [[general-butron]] simultaneously attacked on the French left at the Bernardona. The operation succeeded in destroying French entrenchments and capturing prisoners before the defenders withdrew in the face of French cavalry reinforcements.
