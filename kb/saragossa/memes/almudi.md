# Almudí

## Overview

The Almudí was the public granary of Saragossa, a civic building that stored grain reserves for the city. During the Second Siege of Saragossa (1808–1809), it served as a landmark and a point where food was distributed to the besieged population and defenders. Located near the archway of Cineja, it stood in the zone between the city center and the defensive positions at [[las-tenerias|Las Tenerías]].

## Location

The Almudí was situated near the arch of Cineja, a landmark archway in Saragossa. Its position placed it along the route between the central city and Las Tenerías, the tanneries district that became a key defensive sector.

> "As we arrived at the arch of Cineja, we stumbled upon Don José de Montoria, who, followed by some of his friends, was running towards the Almudí." (016-032)

## Role in the Siege

As a grain storehouse, the Almudí was a vital resource during the siege. Food shortages became acute as the siege wore on, and locations where provisions were stored or distributed took on heightened importance. The narrator records receiving "a little hot food" in front of the Almudí — a rare moment of sustenance amid the privations of the siege.

> "I was returning to Las Tenerías, and at last, in front of Almudí, they gave me a little hot food." (024-017)

The Almudí also served as a navigational landmark. [[don-jose-montoria|Don José de Montoria]] was seen running toward it with his friends during a critical moment of the battle, suggesting it was a known rallying point.
