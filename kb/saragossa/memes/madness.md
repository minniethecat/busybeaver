**Madness** is a prominent theme in the siege narrative, appearing at both individual and collective levels. The extreme conditions of urban warfare blur the line between rationality and insanity.

## Tactical Madness

The defenders' actions are described as bordering on madness from a conventional military perspective: "It was madness to try to fill that terrible fosse" (010-018). Yet this very irrationality became a tactical asset, as the French could not predict or counter the defenders' desperate measures.

## Individual Madness

Madness also appears as a personal response to trauma. Characters express anguish in terms that recall mental breakdown: "What madness, what absurdity!" (016-017). The psychological toll of the siege pushed individuals beyond normal limits.
