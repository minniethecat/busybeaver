# Virgin del Pilar

## Overview

The **Virgin del Pilar** is the patroness of [[el-pilar|Saragossa]] and of Spain, whose image in the [[el-pilar|Basilica of El Pilar]] was the spiritual center of the city's [[resistance|defense]] during the Napoleonic siege. Throughout Galdós's account, the Virgin is invoked by nearly every character — from generals to peasants — as a source of courage, consolation, and divine protection.

## Location

The revered image of the Virgin is housed in the chapel of El Pilar in the [[el-pilar|Basilica of El Pilar]], Saragossa. There is "an aperture in the wall, by which the devout, descending two or three steps, approach to kiss the pedestal which sustains the revered image" (007-035). Both [[augustine-montoria|Augustine Montoria]] and [[gabriel-araceli|Gabriel Araceli]] made this pilgrimage.

## Role in the Siege

### The Spiritual Heart of Resistance

The Virgin del Pilar served as the unifying symbol of Saragossan resistance. Her name was invoked as a battle cry by commanders and soldiers alike. [[palafox|Palafox]] himself used her name to rally the defenders:

> "Forward, sons of the Virgin del Pilar! Do you see those women over there? They are saying that if you do not go, they will go themselves." (023-015)

Another cry recorded in the narrative:

> "The indestructible redoubt of our Lady of the Pillar. Saragossans! Die for the Virgin del Pilar or conquer!" (009-020)

### Collective Devotion

The chapel of the Virgin became the site of intense, spontaneous worship. The people of Saragossa treated her not as a distant saint but as a familiar, beloved mother:

> The prayers, the entreaties, and the demonstrations of rejoicing did not seem like the prayers of any class of the faithful. The prayers were like talks mingled with tears, groans, the most tender words. Those who could not come near her were talking to her from afar off, waving their arms wildly about. (007-003)

### Personal Devotion

Characters across the narrative express intimate, personal relationships with the Virgin. Don José de Montoria "loved the Virgin del Pilar with a fanatical sort of family affection" (004-003). [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla Candiola]] kept an image of the Virgin in her room and "looked at the image of the Virgin del Pilar which is in my room, and I question it in thought, and it answers me" (016-019). Even Don Jerónimo Candiola, the miser, "prayed with fervor to the Virgin del Pilar" (019-011).

### In Battle

Soldiers invoked her name at moments of crisis. When panic threatened to break the defenders at the redoubt of El Pilar, the chronicle records: "We forgot honor, the fatherland, the glory of death, the Virgin del Pilar, whose name adorned the bridge and the 'unconquerable' defences" (010-018).

The soldier [[juan-pirli|Pirli]] made her name a refrain of his battle cries: "Viva the second artillery woman! Viva Manuela Sancho and the Virgin del Pilar!" (009-036)
