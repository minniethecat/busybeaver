The **Swiss Regiment of Aragón** was a Swiss mercenary regiment in Spanish service during the Peninsular War, commanded by Don Mariano Walker. It fought in the defense of Saragossa during the second siege.

## Command and Composition

The regiment was led by Don Mariano Walker and composed of Swiss professional soldiers in Spanish service. Augustine Montoria describes it alongside other units mustering for the siege: "the Swiss regiment of Aragón, which Don Mariano Walker commands" (006-002). Swiss regiments were common in European armies of the period, valued for their discipline and professional training.

## Role in the Defense

The regiment was part of the Spanish defensive force assembled by General Palafox. It is mentioned in the context of the initial troop dispositions as the French forces approached Saragossa. The unit's professional soldiers fought alongside Spanish volunteers and militia.

## Late Siege Service

Captain Don Carlos Lindener, a Swiss officer, is noted as having been present "last year in Saragossa" (027-008), indicating the Swiss contingent remained engaged through the siege period. Lindener is described as the "Swiss Captain" associated with the regiment.
> "There is the Brigadier Don José Manso," said Augustine to me, "with
the Swiss regiment of Aragón, which Don Mariano Walker commands, the
volunteers of Huesca, of whom Don Pedro Villacampa is leader, the
volunteers of Catalonia, and other valiant corps."
>
> (006-002)
