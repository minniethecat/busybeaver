The **Relief of Saragossa** refers to a French relief force that arrived during the second siege to reinforce the besieging army, further tipping the balance (002-025) against the exhausted defenders.

## Arrival

The relief force arrived as the siege dragged on beyond French expectations. The stubborn resistance of Saragossa had tied down French troops and required reinforcement to maintain the encirclement and continue offensive operations.

## Strategic Impact

The arrival of fresh French troops made the defenders' position increasingly untenable. Already weakened by famine, disease, and constant combat, the Spanish garrison faced a reinforced enemy with fresh supplies and manpower.

## Context

The relief force arrival is distinct from the events of the first siege when the French were forced to [[french-retreat|retreat]] (002-013). In the second siege, French reinforcements ensured no such outcome for the defenders.
