# Calle de la Parra

**Calle de la Parra** was a street in Saragossa that achieved a place in the history of the siege due to the extraordinary actions of Vicente Codé, a farmer who operated a cannon alone at its entrance against advancing French forces.

## Codé and the Cannon

The beggar [[sursum-corda]] recounted the story to [[gabriel-araceli]] and [[don-roque]] in his meandering narration of the siege's heroic episodes:

> "But much better was what Codé did, the farmer of the parish of La Magdalena, with the cannon of the Calle de la Parra. When he was going to fire the gun, the French surrounded him, everybody ran away; but Codé got under the cannon, and the French passed by without seeing him. Afterwards, helped by an old woman who brought him some rope, he pulled that big piece of artillery as far as the entrance of the street." (002-035)

This episode exemplified the improvisational character of the Saragossan defence, where civilians — farmers, women, and children — joined soldiers in manning the barricades.

## Urban Context

The street was part of the dense urban fabric where the house-to-house combat of the siege unfolded. Like [[calle-de-pabostre]] and the [[calle-de-anton-trillo]], it became a battlefield where every doorway and corner could conceal a defender.
