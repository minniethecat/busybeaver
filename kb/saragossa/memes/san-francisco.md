# San Francisco

The **Convent of San Francisco** was a vast religious edifice in Saragossa that served as a critical defensive position for the Spanish during the Second Siege. Its extensive subterranean wine-cellars made it a focal point of the underground mine warfare that characterised the final phase of the siege.

## Strategic Position

San Francisco occupied a key location in the urban geography of the siege. French forces were established in the [[convent-of-jerusalem]], and San Francisco offered "good positions for our guns against the French" (010-012). The building stood near the [[Casa de los Duendes]] and opposite the college of [[san-diego]], with its orchard "surrounded by high mud walls which opened upon irregular and narrow alleys" (022-019).

## Underground Warfare

The convent's most significant military feature was its "vast subterranean wine-cellars under its choir" (020-025). Both Spanish and French forces dug galleries to place explosive mines. The underground combat took on a nightmarish quality:

> "We built long galleries, working ceaselessly like the worm that builds his house in the darkness of earth, shaping it like his own body. Between the blows of our picks, we heard, like a muffled echo, the picks of the French." (020-025)

When the opposing galleries met, the combatants confronted each other in the darkness of the cellars. Spanish defenders used hand-grenades to drive the French back through their own tunnels — a struggle described as "one of those dreadful struggles which at times we all wage with the abhorrent figures that people the terrible caverns of our dreams" (020-026).

## Role in the Siege

The convent served as a barracks for troops including [[gabriel-araceli]] and [[augustine-montoria]], who were ordered to guard it after the fighting at [[las-tenerias]]. The [[Casa de los Duendes]] behind San Francisco was also held by the Spanish, providing a linked defensive network. The convent cloister was a place of assembly where civilians — including [[don-jeronimo-candiola]] — sought shelter.

## Modern Site

Today the Palace of Deputies for the Province was constructed on the site of the Convent of San Francisco [Footnote, Chapter 32].
