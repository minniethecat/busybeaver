The **Capitulation of Saragossa** was the formal surrender agreement that ended the second siege in February 1809. The terms were considered honorable, acknowledging the defenders' extraordinary resistance.

## Terms

The capitulation was described as having been conducted with dignity: "They say that the capitulation has been honorable" (030-018). This phrase captures the respect that even the French had for the defenders after months of brutal urban combat.

## After the First Siege

The capitulation of the second siege should be distinguished from the aftermath of the first siege. After the first siege ended in 1808, some participants found it "convenient after the capitulation to rid themselves of such a form" (003-005), suggesting that the earlier events carried different political implications.
