# Volunteers of Aragon

The **Volunteers of Aragon** (Voluntarios de Aragón) were a local volunteer regiment that fought in the defence of Saragossa during the Second Siege. They were part of the broader mobilisation of Aragonese civilians into military units that sustained the city's resistance against the French.

## Role in the Defence

The volunteers of Aragon were among the patchwork of regional and local units — alongside the [[volunteers-of-huesca]], the volunteers of Bourbon, Castile, and other provinces — that defended the city. While specific engagements of the Aragon volunteers are not detailed individually in the narrative, they formed part of the ten thousand defenders at [[monte-torrero]] under [[general-saint-march]] (006-001).

## The Volunteer Tradition

The volunteer units of the siege represented a unique fusion of civilian and military identity (006-001)(006-002). Farmers, artisans, students, and clergy took up arms alongside regular soldiers. The Aragonese volunteers drew on a deep regional tradition of self-defence — the *fueros* of Aragon had long granted the region the right and duty to defend itself — and their presence exemplified the popular character of the Saragossan resistance.
