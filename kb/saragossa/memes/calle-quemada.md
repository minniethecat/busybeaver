# Calle Quemada

## Overview

**Calle Quemada** ("Burnt Street") was a street in the [[las-tenerias|Las Tenerías]] quarter of Saragossa, named for its devastated condition during the [[siege-warfare|siege]]. The Puerta Quemada ("Burnt Gate") at its end was a critical point in the defensive perimeter — its possession allowed artillery to command the street all the way to the Plaza de la Magdalena.

## Location

Calle Quemada ran through Las Tenerías toward the Plaza de la Magdalena. The Puerta Quemada formed the terminus of the street, and the defensive line of the suburb began at this point:

> This line, running from the Puerta Quemada, met first the battery of Palafox, then the Molino, the mill, in the city, then the garden of San Augustine. (013-012)

## Role in the Siege

### Strategic Importance

The Puerta Quemada was one of the most important defensive points in the Las Tenerías line. The French recognized that capturing it would give them a decisive advantage:

> The enemy tried to take the Calle de Pabostre in order to get possession of Puerta Quemada, an important point whence they could rake with their artillery the street of the same name towards the Plaza de la Magdalena. (023-001)

### The Fighting

As French forces pushed through Las Tenerías, the defenders fought to hold the Puerta Quemada and prevent the enemy from advancing into the Calle Quemada. The street and its gate appear repeatedly in accounts of the desperate street fighting that characterized the Second Siege.

The gate's position connected directly to the [[calle-de-pabostre|Calle de Pabostre]], another key artery, and its loss would expose the Plaza de la Magdalena to direct attack. The combined advance from [[san-augustine|San Augustine]], [[las-monicas|Las Mónicas]], and the Calle de Pabostre toward Puerta Quemada represented the French plan to encircle and overwhelm the defenders of Las Tenerías.

### Destruction

The street's name itself — "Burnt" — testifies to the destruction wrought by the siege. Houses were burned by both sides to deny cover to the enemy:

> The French burned down the houses that they could not defend, and the Saragossans did the same. (002-029)
