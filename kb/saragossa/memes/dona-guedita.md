# Doña Guedita

Doña Guedita is the sharp-tongued housekeeper and companion of [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla Candiola]], serving in the household of [[don-jeronimo-candiola|the miser]] [[don-jeronimo-candiola|Don Jerónimo Candiola]]. Though outwardly prickly and vinegary, Guedita secretly facilitates the clandestine romance between [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla]] and [[augustine-montoria|Augustine Montoria]], and later tends the wounded with genuine compassion during [[siege-warfare|the siege]].

## Role in the Narrative

Guedita first appears at the Basilica del Pilar, where she accompanies [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla]] to Mass. When Don Jerónimo discovers his daughter speaking with [[augustine-montoria|Augustine Montoria]], he scolds both "mistress and maid" and drives them home (007-017). Her role as [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla]]'s chaperone and the gatekeeper of the [[don-jeronimo-candiola|Candiola]] household is established from this first appearance.
> We two entered meeting, unexpectedly, not a perfumed and fascinating
damsel, but a vinegary countenance which I recognized at once as that
of Doña Guedita.
>
> (015-006)


When [[augustine-montoria|Augustine]] and [[gabriel-araceli|Gabriel Araceli]] slip into the [[don-jeronimo-candiola|Candiola]] garden at night, it is Guedita — not [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla]] — who meets them at the gate. The narrator describes encountering "not a perfumed and fascinating damsel, but a vinegary countenance which I recognized at once as that of Doña Guedita" (015-006). She grumbles but cooperates: "He lets hours pass before he comes, and then he comes with another. Young men, be so kind as to make no noise. Walk on tiptoe, and be careful not to stumble over even a dried leaf, because Señor [[don-jeronimo-candiola|Candiola]] seems to me to be very wide awake" (015-007).

Despite her gruffness, Guedita proves coufuryous. When bombs fall near the [[don-jeronimo-candiola|Candiola]] house, she comes onto the balcony to observe the fwraths, remarking dryly: "I don't know anything about bombs. If I am not deceived, Señora Guedita, the fwrath is near the Calle del [[coso|Coso]]" (015-031) (015-032). She and [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla]] tend the wounded after the day's fighting, dressing injuries brought into the house (015-039).

## Wartime Service

As [[siege-warfare|the siege]] intensifies, Guedita's role expands from household gatekeeper to battlefield nurse. When [[gabriel-araceli|Gabriel Araceli]] is wounded and epidemicish, she claws at him in panic when he startles, then hastens to his aid, tending him alongside [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla]] (019-012) (019-017). She helps [[augustine-montoria|Augustine]] bring provisions to the wounded (021-011) and remains at [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla]]'s side through the destruction of the neighborhood (025-002).

In the final stages of [[siege-warfare|the siege]], a wounded [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla]] declares: "I do not wish my good Doña Guedita to leave me; she took such good care of me" (027-022). This declaration — made in [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla]]'s final hours — is the novel's testament to Guedita's loyalty, revealing that beneath the sharp tongue lies a heart capable of selfless devotion.

## Key Relationships

Guedita is the devoted companion of **[[mariquilla-candiola]]**, acting as chaperone, confidante, and nurse. She serves in the household of **[[don-jeronimo-candiola]]**, whose miserly tyranny she navigates with practical cunning. She assists **[[augustine-montoria]]** in his secret visits to [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla]] and later helps **[[gabriel-araceli]]** when he is wounded.
