# Los Mártires

**Los Mártires** was a church in Saragossa that served as a redoubt — a fortified observation post — during the Second Siege. Its elevated position allowed Spanish defenders to observe French movements around [[monte-torrero]].

## Observation Post

From "the redoubt of Los Mártires," [[augustine-montoria]] and [[gabriel-araceli]] watched the opening engagement of the siege:

> "From the redoubt of Los Mártires we saw the beginning of the action, and the French columns which extended the length of the canal and flanked the Torrero." (007-005)

## During the Fighting

The redoubt was also a vantage point from which the defenders celebrated their early successes. When the French assault on [[san-jose]] was repulsed, those "watching the onset from the battery of Los Mártires broke out into exclamations, applause, cries, and huzzas" (007-008) — a reminder of the fierce morale that sustained the defence even in its grimmest hours.

## Defensive Position

Los Mártires formed part of the southern defensive line, positioned to monitor enemy activity in the direction of the Torrero heights and the Huerva valley. Its proximity to San José and the southern walls made it a natural observation and artillery position.
