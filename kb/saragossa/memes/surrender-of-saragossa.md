The **Surrender of Saragossa** in February 1809 marked the end of the second siege and the city's fall to French forces. The capitulation followed months of intense urban combat that had devastated the city.

## The Final Days

The surrender came after the city's defenses had been systematically reduced. The defenders, weakened by famine and disease, could no longer maintain resistance. As the narrative describes, "They say that the capitulation has been honorable" (030-018), suggesting the terms acknowledged the defenders' valor.

## Aftermath

The fall of Saragossa ended one of the most notable urban defenses of the Peninsular War. After the capitulation, French control was established under Marshal Suchet, who became governor of Aragon. The city had been reduced to rubble — "the shapeless structure" (001-005) of ruins bore witness to the destruction.
