**Marshal Louis-Gabriel Suchet** (1770–1826) was a French military commander who became governor of Aragon after the fall of Saragossa. He was one of Napoleon's most capable marshals in the Peninsular War.

## Role at Saragossa

Suchet commanded forces encamped on the right above the suburb during the siege. (004-013) He participated in the second siege and subsequently administered the conquered region. Unlike some French commanders in Spain, he gained a reputation for relatively effective governance.

## Later Career

After Saragossa, Suchet continued to serve in the Peninsular campaign, eventually being created Duke of Albufera by Napoleon.
