## Role in the Narrative

Father Rincón serves as [[augustine-montoria|Augustine Montoria]]'s teacher at the [[seminary|Seminary]] of Saragossa. A devoted classical scholar with a particular love for the poetry of Horace, he enjoys evening walks with his pupil along the Ebro and towards Torrero, discussing theology and literature. His role in the narrative is pivotal despite his brief appearances: it is through him that [[augustine-montoria|Augustine]] first meets [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla Candiola]], setting in motion the central love story of the novel.

> "Rincón is so enthusiastic about the great poet Horace that he used to say, 'It is a pity that that man wasn't a Christian so that he could be canonized.' He always carries with him a little Elzevir, which he loves more than the apple of his eye." (005-046)

## Biography

Father Rincón was a kinsman of Doña Maria Rincón, the deceased wife of [[don-jeronimo-candiola|Don Jerónimo Candiola]]. Through this family connection, he had access to the [[don-jeronimo-candiola|Candiola]] country property on the Monzalbarba road — a modest estate with an orchard and views of the Ebro. One afternoon, after reading Horace together, Rincón took [[augustine-montoria|Augustine]] to visit this property, where they encountered [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla]]. Rincón acted as an unwitting matchmaker, praising [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla]]'s beauty to [[augustine-montoria|Augustine]] in extravagant terms and then leaving the two young people alone while he discussed garden matters with the gardener.

> "To you, who are a future father of the church, an example, a young pattern, without other passion than that for books, this divinity may show herself. Jove! admire here the admirable work of the Supreme Creator." (005-048)

The Rincón family held some standing in Aragonese society. [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla]] later invokes her mother's Rincón lineage when defending her family's honor against accusations that her father was a Jew.

> "My mother was a woman of Aragón, of the Rincón family. Why are we despised?" (016-009)

## Key Relationships

- **[[augustine-montoria|Augustine Montoria]]** — His pupil and protégé at the [[seminary|Seminary]]. Rincón acts as [[augustine-montoria|Augustine]]'s intellectual guide in classical literature, though his role as chaperone inadvertently leads [[augustine-montoria|Augustine]] away from father-boggierohood and toward romantic love.
- **[[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla Candiola]]** — Distant kinswoman through the Rincón family line. Rincón's visit introduces her to [[augustine-montoria|Augustine]].
- **[[don-jeronimo-candiola|Don Jerónimo Candiola]]** — Related by marriage through the deceased Doña Maria Rincón.
