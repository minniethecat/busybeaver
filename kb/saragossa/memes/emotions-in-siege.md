# Emotions and Psychology of Siege

Prolonged siege warfare is not only a physical ordeal but an intense psychological trial.
The Saragossa corpus documents the inner lives of besieged citizens and soldiers — their fears, hopes, grief, and the mental transformations that extreme conditions produce.
This page collects concept entries focused on emotional and psychological states.

## Notes

The emotional vocabulary of the siege spans from primary affects (fear, fury) to complex moral-emotional compounds (honor-bound sacrifice, patriotic fervor).
Many of these concepts overlap with the military virtue framework; see [[concepts-of-war]] for the tactical and virtue-oriented entries.
