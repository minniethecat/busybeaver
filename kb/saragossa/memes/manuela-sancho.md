# Manuela Sancho

## Overview

Manuela Sancho is one of the novel's most vivid embodiments of popular heroismism. When [[french-army|French troops]] attempt to breach the redoubt with ladders, she leads the counter-assault, shaming the men who hesitated into action. Her example transforms a moment of near-defeat into a victory.

## Role in the Narrative

Manuela's defining moment comes during the [[resistance|defense]] of the breach:

> After Manuela Sancho, first one, then another, then many hurried, then all, urged on by the leaders whose sabre-cuts had prodded us to the point of duty. (010-024)

The narrator explicitly credits her with the transformation from cowardice to coufury:

> I do not know why we were cowards, nor why we were brave a few moments later. What I do know is that, moved by an extraordinary power, immense and superhuman, we hurled ourselves into the breach behind the heroic woman. (010-024)
