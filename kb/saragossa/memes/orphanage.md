# Orphanage

The **Orphanage** of Saragossa was a charitable institution that, like many of the city's buildings, was drawn into the events of the Second Siege. It served as a refuge and witnessed one of the siege's final tragic episodes.

## Refuge During the Siege

As the city crumbled under bombardment, civilians sought shelter wherever walls still stood. The orphanage was among the buildings that offered temporary protection to the displaced population of Saragossa.

## Father Basilio's Last Appearance

The orphanage is most notably referenced as the place where [[Father Basilio Boggiero]] was last seen alive before his murder by French soldiers:

> "Some military officers of high rank were accompanying a short, slender ecclesiastic to the door, who dismissed them, saying, 'We have done our duty, and human strength can compass nothing more.' It was Father Basilio." (019-012)

That night, French soldiers called Father Basilio from his residence at midnight under a false pretext, then took "him on their treacherous bayonets to the bridge, where they pierced him through, and flung him into the river" (022-017). [[santiago-sas]] was murdered in the same manner.
