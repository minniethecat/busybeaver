# Don Pedro Ric

## Overview

Don Pedro Ric is a citizen of Saragossa who appears among General [[palafox|Palafox]]'s council of [[resistance|defense]] during the [[second-siege-of-saragossa|Second Siege]]. Though a minor figure in the novel's vast cast, his house serves as a meeting place for the city's military and civilian leadership, placing him at the intersection of private hospitality and public [[resistance|defense]].

## Role in the Narrative

Don Pedro Ric enters the narrative through his home, which functions as an informal command post. [[gabriel-araceli|Gabriel Araceli]] first mentions him when describing where strategic discussions take place:

> And this in the house of Don Pedro Ric. Here you have a couple of sleepers… (009-013)

The house of Don Pedro Ric becomes a recurring setting where the [[resistance|defense]] council convenes. The narrator's offhand mention of "sleepers" — likely exhausted defenders grabbing rest between assaults — reveals the domestic spaces repurposed by [[siege-warfare|the siege]]. Ric's willingness to open his home to the city's defenders marks him as a patriot of the civilian class, contributing material resources to a conflict that consumed everything.

Later, during the relentless [[french-artillery-bombardment|bombardment]], Ric is listed among the council members gathered to coordinate the city's response:

> General O'Neill, San Genís, and Don Pedro Ric. There was also there the brave Renovales… (010-012)

His inclusion alongside military commanders and the charismatic [[renovales|Renovales]] confirms his status as a trusted civic figure within [[palafox|Palafox]]'s extended leadership network.

## Council Membership

Ric's role on the council of [[resistance|defense]] illustrates how [[siege-warfare|the siege]] dissolved the boundary between military and civilian spheres in Saragossa. The council included:

| Member | Role |
|--------|------|
| [[palafox|General Palafox]] | Commander-in-chief |
| General O'Neill | Military commander |
| San Genís | Military officer |
| **Don Pedro Ric** | Civilian representative |
| [[renovales|Renovales]] | Guerrilla leader |

## Historical Context

While Ric is likely a composite or fictional character, Galdós's inclusion of a civilian on the [[resistance|defense]] council accurately reflects the historical reality of [[siege-warfare|the siege]]. The [[resistance|defense]] of Saragossa was famously a popular uprising, with ordinary citizens fighting alongside — and sometimes in place of — regular troops. Houses like Ric's became de facto headquarters, armories, and hospitals throughout the two-month ordeal.
