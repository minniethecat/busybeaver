The **Huerva Bridge** was a strategic crossing over the Huerva River on the eastern perimeter of Saragossa's defenses during the second siege. A redoubt was built at its outer end, and it was defended by a barricade.

## Defensive Position

The bridge anchored the eastern section of Saragossa's defensive wall. The fortification line ran "from there the battlements of the wall as far as the Huerva bridge, defended by a barricade" (004-015). Beyond the bridge, the defenses "deflected afterwards towards the west" toward the Torre del Pino (004-015).

## Garrison

Gabriel de Araceli's battalion was stationed at the bridge: "Our battalion remained in the redoubt raised at the outer end of the Huerva bridge" (009-002). From this position, the defenders could observe and engage French movements across the river.

## Combat Significance

The bridge was a critical chokepoint during the siege. During French advances, "these people, obstructed the bridge and obliged my battalion to look for an easier way" (006-015). The fighting around the bridge was intense, with the narrator noting "close to the bridge the little monastery of Altabás" (006-016) as a nearby landmark.

## Surrounding Area

The bridge area connected several key defensive positions. Near it stood the monastery of Altabás, and it formed the boundary between the Huerva River front and the Torre del Pino redoubt.
