# Sharpshooters of Fernando VII

The **Sharpshooters of Fernando VII** (Tiradores de Fernando VII) were an elite Spanish light infantry unit that fought at the defence of [[monte-torrero]] during the opening engagement of the Second Siege of Saragossa on 21 December 1808.

## Monte Torrero

The sharpshooters were among the ten thousand defenders who held the fortified positions of the Torrero against the initial French assault:

> "The volunteers of Bourbon, Castile, Campo Segorbino, of Alicante, and of Soria, the sharpshooters of Fernando VII, the Murcia regiment, and other bodies that I do not remember, answered the fire." (007-006)

Though the Torrero position was ultimately abandoned — a strategic calculation rather than a defeat — the sharpshooters conducted an orderly fighting retreat across the Puente de América alongside the other defending units, saving all but one of the artillery pieces.

## Unit Character

Named for the Spanish king [[Fernando VII]], the sharpshooters represented the fusion of royalist loyalty with guerrilla-style light infantry tactics that characterised much of the Spanish resistance to Napoleon. As marksmen, they were trained for precision fire rather than mass volley, making them particularly effective in the broken terrain around the Torrero heights.
