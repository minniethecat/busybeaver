## San Pedro in the Siege of Saragossa

The Calle de San Pedro was a significant street in Saragossa during the siege, running near the bridge and forming part of the city's defensive network. It gave its name to the battalion of Las Peñas de San Pedro, one of the key defensive units that defended Saragossa against the French.

### The Battalion of Las Peñas de San Pedro

The battalion of Las Peñas de San Pedro was one of the volunteer units that defended Saragossa during both sieges. Gabriel de Araceli, the narrator, served in this battalion after enlisting. The battalion had been severely weakened during the first siege and was reinforced with new volunteers for the second siege.

As Araceli recounts: "They gave me a place in the battalion of volunteers of the Peñas of San Pedro, which had been badly weakened in the first siege, and I received a uniform and a gun. (004-012)"

Augustine Montoria, the son of Don José de Montoria, was unable to serve under the Sas banner and enlisted in this same battalion alongside Araceli.

### Defensive Position

From four o'clock in the morning, the battalion of Las Peñas de San Pedro guarded the front of the fortifications from Santa Engracia to the Convent of Trinitarios (004-015) — a line that seemed the least exposed in the entire defensive circuit of the city. This position placed the battalion along a critical stretch of Saragossa's perimeter.

The streets of San Gil, San Pedro, and La Cuchillería, which led to the bridge, were described as almost impassable during the height of the siege, reflecting the intensity of combat in the area.
