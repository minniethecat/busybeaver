**General Juan (John) O'Neill** was an Irish-born Spanish general who served in the defense of Saragossa. He was mentioned alongside [[general-butron]] and [[general-saint-march]] as part of the Spanish command (023-018).

## Irish in Spanish Service

O'Neill was one of many Irish military men who served in the Spanish Army during the 18th and 19th centuries. The "Wild Geese" tradition of Irish soldiers in continental European armies was well-established, and Spain was a common destination.

## Role

O'Neill held a senior command in the Saragossa garrison, contributing to the defense alongside other generals (006-001). His Irish background was not unusual — Spain's military included officers from across Catholic Europe.

## Actions in the Siege

O'Neill participated in several key actions during the defense of Saragossa:

- **Defense of Torrero**: O'Neill jointly commanded the defense of the Torrero positions with Don Philip Saint March, leading ten thousand men against the French assault. Both were described as "generals of great merit" (006-001).
- **Sally from the Suburb**: On the twenty-fifth, Don John O'Neill led a sally from the suburb with volunteers of Aragón and Huesca, achieving tactical surprise and inflicting casualties on the enemy (008-007).
- **Governing Council**: In the siege command structure, while Captain-General Palafox nominally commanded, the defense was effectively directed by Father Basilio alongside O'Neill, Saint March, and Butron — the latter three described as "noted generals" (023-018).

O'Neill was also among the distinguished personages who visited the front lines in the early moments of the siege, alongside Don Mariano Cereso, San Genís, and Don Pedro Ric (010-012).

> Don John O'Neill made a sally from the suburb on the twenty-fifth with the volunteers of Aragón and Huesca, taking the chance of advantage from the enemy's lack of preparation, and killing many of the enemy's men.
>
> (008-007)
