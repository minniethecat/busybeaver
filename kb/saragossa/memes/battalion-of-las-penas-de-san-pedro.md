The **Battalion of Las Peñas de San Pedro** was a Spanish volunteer battalion that defended Saragossa during the second siege (1808–1809). It appears frequently in the narrative as the unit to which the protagonist Gabriel de Araceli belongs.

## Formation and Composition

The battalion was composed of volunteers from the Peñas of San Pedro region. It had been "badly weakened in the first siege" (004-012) and was reinforced with new recruits including the narrator Gabriel himself. The battalion accepted young men rejected by other units — "for this reason Augustine Montoria himself, Don José's son, could not serve under the Sas banner, and enlisted like myself in the battalion of Las Peñas de San Pedro" (004-012).

## Defensive Role

From the start of the second siege, the battalion guarded a critical section of the fortifications: "from four o'clock, from day-dawn, the battalion of Las Peñas de San Pedro guarded the front of the fortifications, from Santa Engracia to the Convent of Trinitarios, a line which seemed the least exposed in all the circuit of the city" (004-015).

## Casualties and Decimation

The battalion suffered severe losses throughout the siege. By late in the defense, it "was reduced to four sergeants, a lieutenant, a captain, and two hundred men" (021-028). The unit maintained cohesion despite overwhelming casualties.

## Notable Members

Gabriel de Araceli, the narrator, served in this battalion and eventually rose to the rank of captain: "Here am I, Gabriel, captain of the battalion of Las Peñas de San Pedro" (029-026). Augustine Montoria, son of the wealthy merchant Don José de Montoria, also served in the battalion alongside Gabriel.

## Reputation

The battalion was well-regarded among the defenders. A civilian character addresses its soldiers with respect: "You are soldiers of the Estremadura and las Peñas de San Pedro; you are all good fellows" (026-073).

## In the Narrative

> "They gave me a place in the battalion of volunteers of the Peñas of San Pedro, which had been badly weakened in the first siege, and I received a uniform and a gun. I did not enter the lines, as my protector had said, in the company of the clergyman of Santiago Sas, because this valiant company was composed exclusively of residents of the parish of San Pablo."
