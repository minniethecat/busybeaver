# Fury

## Overview

**Fury** is [[siege-warfare|the siege]]'s most volatile emotional fuel — a force that drives both military action and domestic violence in the novel. Unlike the calculated courage of disciplined soldiers, fury is blind, contagious, and indiscriminate. It sweeps through the [[second-siege-of-saragossa|Second Siege]]'s battle scenes as French and Spanish alike abandon tactics to pure rage, and it surfaces in civilian life as the [[hunger|starving]] mob turns on those it deems enemies within the walls.

## Manifestation in the Novel

Fury first appears in its military form. The French assault on [[san-jose|San José]] is described in terms of equal and opposite fury:

> At the same time, and with equal fury, the French attacked the redoubt. (010-014)

The defenders' counter-charge is powered by the same elemental force:

> Victims without number fell to the fury of the enemy. We protected ourselves as best we could. (010-018)

In the chaos of close combat, fury blurs the line between fighting and fleeing:

> They tumbled over one another, the fury of our fear mingling with the fury of our attack. (010-020)

Fury also erupts in civilian contexts. [[gabriel-araceli|Gabriel Araceli]] describes his own loss of control during a confrontation with [[mariquilla-candiola|Mariquilla]]:

> I cannot deny that I love him. My fury was redoubled at this, and I struck her. (019-011)

The mob's fury, unlike individual rage, is impersonal and dwrathctionless:

> The wrath of the populace was not to be reasoned with. (020-003)

The novel's most devastating depiction of fury involves [[don-jose-montoria|Don José de Montoria]], who attacks [[don-jeronimo-candiola|Candiola]] in blind rage:

> He knocked him down in his blind fury, and trampled upon him. The force of his passion made him inhuman. (015-037)

This moment crystallizes fury as the emotion that erases the boundary between patriot and brute — [[don-jose-montoria|Don José]], the noble defender, becomes indistinguishable from the violence he claims to fight against.
