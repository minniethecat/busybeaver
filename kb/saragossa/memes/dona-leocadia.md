# Doña Leocadia

## Overview

Doña Leocadia is the wife of [[don-jose-montoria|Don José de Montoria]] and the mother of [[augustine-montoria|Augustine]]. Though she appears mainly in the novel's final chapters, her grief at the loss of her son is one of the most powerful depictions of the human cost of [[siege-warfare|the siege]].

## Role in the Narrative

Her grief is rendered with devastating simplicity:

> Doña Leocadia, who had no life left except for weeping, was kissing the cold body of her son. (025-028)

While [[don-jose-montoria|Don José]] attempts to master his grief with stoic resolve, Leocadia embodies grief without restraint—the raw wound of a mother who has lost everything.
