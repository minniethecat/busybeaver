**Superstition** appears in the siege narrative as an element of Spanish popular culture and religious sensibility. It coexists with formal Catholic faith and influences characters' behavior.

## In Character Psychology

The narrative notes how a character's mind was governed by "the love and the superstitions which held him in thrall" (016-018), showing how superstition intertwined with deeper emotional bonds. For some characters, superstitious belief provided comfort or explanation for the chaos of war.

## Popular Religion

The line between superstition and popular Catholicism is deliberately blurred in the narrative. Belief in divine intervention, omens, and the protective power of the Virgin of El Pilar exists alongside, and sometimes merges with, what educated characters might dismiss as mere superstition.
