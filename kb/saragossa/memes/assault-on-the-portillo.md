The **Assault on the Portillo** was an attack on the Portillo gate of Saragossa led by French General Lefebre Desnouettes during the second siege.

## The Attack

General Lefebre Desnouettes directed an assault against the Portillo gate, one of the key entry points to the city. The narrator records that "Lefebre Desnouettes because he assaulted the plaza by the Portillo" (010-004), indicating this was a notable action in the early phase of the siege.

## Defensive Position

The Portillo gate was heavily fortified: "There was in the Portillo gate a large semicircular battery that joined the walls" (003-013). This defensive work made direct assault costly for the French.

## Outcome

The assault on the Portillo was one of several French attempts to breach the city's defenses at different points. Like many early French attacks, it encountered prepared Spanish positions and determined resistance.

## In the Narrative

> "Do you know that Napoleon in Paris, knowing the resistance shown by this city in the first siege, was furious with Lefebre Desnouettes because he assaulted the plaza by the Portillo and the Castle Aljafería?"
>
> (010-004)
