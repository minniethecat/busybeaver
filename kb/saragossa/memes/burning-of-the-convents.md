The **Burning of the Convents** was a desperate defensive measure taken by the defenders of Saragossa during the second siege. They set fire to convents and other buildings to deny the advancing French forces cover and positions.

## Tactical Rationale

As French forces pushed into the city, convents became contested strongpoints. By burning these buildings, the defenders denied the French fortified positions within the urban perimeter. The measure reflected the extreme lengths to which the defenders would go to maintain resistance.

## Extent

The burning affected multiple religious houses that had been incorporated into the defensive network. This included buildings that had served as hospitals, supply depots, and defensive positions during the first siege. The destruction of sacred buildings underscored the total nature of the conflict.

## Contemporary Account

> In our position we were all prepared for an energetic defence. In the redoubt del Pilar, in the battery of Los Mártires, in the tower of Del Pino, the same as in the Trinitarios, the artillery stood guard with burning matches, and the infantry waited behind the parapets in positions that seemed to us quite secure, ready to fire if any columns should attempt to assault us. It was cold, and most of us were shivering. One might almost have believed that it was from fear; but no, it was cold, and anybody who had said the contrary would have lied.
>
