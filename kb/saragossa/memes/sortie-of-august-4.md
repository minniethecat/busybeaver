The **Sortie of August 4** was a successful Spanish counter-attack launched from Saragossa against the besieging French forces. The sortie caught the French by surprise and inflicted significant disruption.

## Planning and Execution

The defenders organized the sortie to break the siege's passivity. "They decided, to give an outlet to this ardor, to arrange a sortie, and so in effect it was done" (008-004). The Spanish used their superior numbers of eager volunteers to overwhelm French forward positions.

## Success

The sortie achieved tactical surprise: "the French did not expect that sortie, and that, taken completely unaware, did not know how to respond" (008-012). The success boosted defender morale and demonstrated that the besieged garrison retained offensive capability.

## Timing

The sorties were judged well-timed by the narrator: "of this excess of people, the sorties were timely" (008-005), suggesting that the Spanish leadership, likely General Palafox, chose moments when French forces were overextended.
