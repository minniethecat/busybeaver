The **Torre del Pino** ("Tower of the Pine") was a defensive redoubt and narrow street in Saragossa that formed part of the city's fortifications during the second siege. It served as both a defensive position and an observation point.

## Position in the Defenses

The Torre del Pino was integrated into the western fortification line. The defenses ran "joining another redoubt built in the Torre del Pino; it continued in a straight line as far as the Convent of Trinitarios, and enclosed the Puerta del Carmen" (004-015). This placed it between the Huerva bridge sector and the Trinitarios convent.

## Military Role

The Torre del Pino hosted a defensive battery. It was described as one of the batteries that "were less important than the two bodies holding the advanced positions, and served as auxiliaries" (009-002), alongside the batteries of Los Mártires and the Botanical Garden.

## Combat Observation

From the Torre del Pino, defenders could observe French movements: "From the Torre del Pino we saw the retreat of the forty at about eight o’clock in the evening, after they had met the invaders with bayonet-thrusts; they retreated fighting bravely" (011-004).

## Civilian Association

Don Andrés Guspide, a merchant and friend of Don José de Montoria, "on the fourth of August was firing from near the narrow street of the Torre del Pino" (002-017), indicating civilians fought from positions near the tower.
