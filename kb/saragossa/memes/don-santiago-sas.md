# Don Santiago Sas

Don Santiago Sas was a clergyman of Saragossa who, like [[father-basilio-boggiero|Father Basilio Boggiero]], exchanged his clerical robes for a musket during the siege. He is mentioned among the churchmen who took up military roles in defense of the city.

## Role in the Siege

Sas appears in the narrative as one of several clergymen pressed into military service. The beggar who guides [[gabriel-araceli]] through Saragossa describes him in a catalogue of fighting priests:

> "Here we have great warriors among the clergy. Don Santiago Sas is a fusilier commander." (012-008)

This brief mention places Sas within the broader phenomenon of clerical militarization that characterized the [[siege-warfare|defense of Saragossa]], where even men of the church abandoned their pastoral duties to fight.

## Company Command

Sas raised and led a company of fusiliers drawn from the clergy and citizens of Saragossa. [[gabriel-araceli]] was nominally attached to this unit, describing it as "the company of fusiliers of Don Santiago Sas, of which I am captain" (003-006). The company gained a reputation for valor in the street fighting, referred to as "this valiant company" of the clergyman Santiago Sas (004-012).

Sas is first named among a group of citizens who "fearlessly attacked the French" near the hospital and the Convent of San Francisco, alongside Don Mariano Cereso, Don Lorenzo, Vicente Codé, and Don Vicente Marraco (002-025). By the final phase of the siege, Sas commanded the fusiliers under General [[lannes|Lannes]], described as "commander of those brave musketeers" (031-026).

His trajectory — from street skirmishes at the hospital to command under Lannes in the final defense — mirrors the escalating intensity of the siege itself.

## Significance

Don Santiago Sas represents the total mobilization of Saragossan society during the siege. His presence among the armed clergy illustrates how the [[second-siege-of-saragossa|Second Siege]] blurred traditional social boundaries, drawing even men of peace into active combat roles.
