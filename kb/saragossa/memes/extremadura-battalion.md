# Battalion of the Estremadura

The **Battalion of the Estremadura** was an Aragonese infantry unit that served throughout the Second Siege of Saragossa. [[gabriel-araceli]] and [[augustine-montoria]] were both incorporated into its ranks, and the battalion fought in many of the siege's most desperate engagements.

## Key Engagements

### San Augustine

When the [[Convent of San Augustine]] came under assault after the fall of [[las-monicas]], the Estremadura battalion was ordered in to reinforce the defenders:

> "Since we have not the luck to be in Las Mónicas, we will, today, give ourselves the pleasure of defending until death the four walls of Saint Augustine. As the Estremadurans are not sufficient to defend it, we are ordered in, too." (021-001)

The battalion defended the church choir against French forces already occupying the nave, exchanging fire in the darkened interior of the embattled convent.

### San Francisco and the Underground War

After San Augustine, the battalion was sent to guard the [[Convent of San Francisco]], where its men dug counter-mines against French sappers advancing from the [[convent-of-jerusalem]]. The battalion was so depleted by this point that "what was left of both was scarcely three companies" (020-030). Augustine Montoria was promoted to captain, and Gabriel Araceli to lieutenant within the battalion.

### Las Tenerías

Incorporated into the Estremadura, the two friends fought through the streets from the Calle de Palomar to the Plaza de la Magdalena, resisting the French advance towards the [[puerta-quemada]].

## Composition and Character

Like most Spanish units of the siege, the Estremadura battalion was a mix of regular soldiers and local volunteers. Despite its name suggesting origins in the Extremadura region, it was integrated into the Aragonese defence and fought alongside units like the [[volunteers-of-huesca]] and the [[walloon-guards]].
