**Marraco** is a minor character referenced in the narrative of the [[second-siege-of-saragossa|Second Siege of Saragossa]]. He appears among the many citizens and soldiers who participated in the city's defense against the [[french-army|French]] besiegers.

## In the Narrative

Marraco is mentioned briefly in the chronicle of the siege, one of the numerous participants whose individual stories are not fully developed in the narrative. His presence, along with other minor figures like [[pepillo-ruiz|Pepillo Ruiz]] and [[don-antonio-la-casa|Don Antonio la Casa]], contributes to the tapestry of ordinary citizens who formed the backbone of Saragossa's resistance.

## At the Hospital Fighting

Marraco appears in a single but vivid passage describing the street fighting near the Hospital of Saragossa and the Convent of San Francisco. He is named among a group of citizens — including [[don-santiago-sas|Don Santiago Sas]], Don Mariano Cereso, Don Lorenzo, and Vicente Codé — who "fearlessly attacked the French" from behind a barricade (002-025). This places him in the thick of the early siege fighting, part of the spontaneous civilian resistance that characterized Saragossa's defense before organized military units fully took over the city's protection.

## Minor Figures in the Narrative

The brief mention of characters like Marraco reflects [[benito-perez-galdos|Galdós's]] narrative technique of populating his historical novel with a wide cast of minor figures, creating a sense of collective endeavor in which even the least distinguished defenders played their part. These passing references help convey the scale of popular participation in the [[siege-warfare|siege]], where the entire city — from prominent leaders to obscure citizens — rose in defense.
