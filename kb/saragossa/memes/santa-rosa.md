# Santa Rosa

**Santa Rosa** was a church in Saragossa that the French occupied during the Second Siege. Its strategic significance lay in its underground connections to the [[Casa de los Duendes]] and the [[Convent of San Francisco]].

## Secret Passage

During the mine warfare under Saragossa, the French discovered a route from Santa Rosa towards the Spanish positions:

> "It is supposed that they have found a secret passageway between Santa Rosa and the house los Duendes. Being in possession of the cellars of that, they could, by digging a short gallery, get under the chapter house, which is quite near." (020-009)

This secret passage allowed the French to bypass the Spanish defensive galleries and threaten San Francisco from an unexpected direction. [[father-luengo]], the engineer-priest, acknowledged that the defenders had not mined the chapter house area, "believing that it was a safe place" (020-008).

## French Position

Santa Rosa served as a forward base for French mining operations. Together with [[san-diego]] and the [[convent-of-jerusalem]], it formed a network of French-held religious buildings from which they could strike at the Spanish defensive network through subterranean approaches.
