# Regiment of Murcia

The **Regiment of Murcia** was a Spanish infantry regiment that fought at the defence of [[monte-torrero]] during the Second Siege of Saragossa.

## Monte Torrero

The regiment was among the ten thousand Spanish defenders who held the Torrero positions on 21 December 1808:

> "The volunteers of Bourbon, Castile, Campo Segorbino, of Alicante, and of Soria, the sharpshooters of Fernando VII, the Murcia regiment, and other bodies that I do not remember, answered the fire." (007-006)

The regiment conducted an orderly retreat when the Torrero position became untenable, withdrawing across the Puente de América and saving their artillery pieces — a disciplined performance that contrasted with the chaos that might have ensued from a less professional force.

## Origins

As its name suggests, the regiment was raised in the province of Murcia in southeastern Spain. Its presence at Saragossa reflected the national character of the resistance: troops from across Spain — Extremadura, Murcia, Valencia, Castile, Catalonia, and Aragon — converged to defend the city.
