# Casa de González

## Overview

The **Casa de González** was a fortified house in the Las Tenerías quarter of Saragossa that served as an advanced point on the Spanish defensive line during the Second Siege. It belonged to Don Victoriano González and gained a place in the historical record of the siege.

## Position in the Defensive Line

The house was one of the key strongpoints anchoring the eastern defenses. The defensive line ran from the Puerta Quemada through the battery of [[palafox|Palafox]], the Molino of the city, the garden of [[san-augustine|San Augustine]], the mill of Goicoechea, and the orchard of [[las-monicas|Las Mónicas]], culminating at "a great battery and the house of González" (013-012).

As described by the narrator Gabriel Araceli, this line had two advanced points: the mill of Goicoechea and the Casa de González — both extending forward from the main defensive perimeter into contested ground.

## Combat

During the French assaults on Las Tenerías, the Casa de González was targeted by assault columns attempting to break the Spanish line:

> "Other columns equally powerful were trying to take the Casa de González, which I have before mentioned. But from the neighboring houses, and the towers of the wall, came such a terrible fire of rifles and cannons that they desisted from their attempt." (017-006)

The house's survival under direct assault testified to the effectiveness of the Spanish defensive network — fortified buildings mutually supporting each other with overlapping fields of fire.

## The Sepulcro

The house stood near a church and area called the Sepulcro, a name that proved grimly appropriate. As the narrator observed: "More than one portion of the suburb, indeed, deserved the name of sepulchre" (013-012).
