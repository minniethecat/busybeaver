# Minna Caroline Smith

## Overview

**Minna Caroline Smith** was the translator who rendered [[benito-perez-galdos|Benito Pérez Galdós]]'s novel *Saragossa* into English. Her translation, completed in 1899, brought the story of the city's heroic defense to an English-speaking audience.

## Translation of Saragossa

Smith's translation of *Saragossa* was published in 1899 and remains the principal English version of the work. The novel forms part of Galdós's *Episodios Nacionales* series, chronicling episodes from Spanish history through the eyes of fictional and historical characters.

The translation preserves Galdós's vivid battle scenes and his portrayal of the diverse characters who populate besieged Saragossa — from the aristocratic [[countess-de-bureta|Countess of Bureta]] to the common soldiers and [[resistance|civilian defenders]] who held the city against Napoleon's forces.

## Modern Edition

Smith's translation entered the digital age through the Standard Ebooks project, which produced a carefully formatted ebook edition. The project notes that readers can access the text and contribute corrections at the Standard Ebooks website (033-031).

## Legacy

As the translator who first made *Saragossa* available in English, Minna Caroline Smith played a foundational role in the novel's reception in the Anglophone world. Her work has enabled generations of English-language readers to encounter Galdós's account of one of the Peninsular War's most dramatic episodes.
