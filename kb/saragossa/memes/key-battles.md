# Key Battles and Engagements

The two sieges of Saragossa were not a single continuous battle but a series of discrete engagements — assaults on fortified positions, sorties from the city, and street-by-street fighting through burning buildings.
This page indexes the individual combat actions that together constitute the siege narrative.

## Notes

The distinction between "battle," "assault," and "sortie" reflects the military terminology of the period: assaults were French offensive operations against the city; sorties were Spanish counterattacks beyond the walls.
