## San Gil in the Siege of Saragossa

The Calle de San Gil was a strategically important street in Saragossa during the siege. Located near the bridge, it formed a critical chokepoint in the city's defenses.

### A Defensive Gateway

At the narrow entrance of the Calle de San Gil, and in the archway of Cineja, cannon were positioned to restrain the enemy's advance. (028-002) This placement reflected the street's tactical importance as a narrow passage through which the French forces would need to pass to penetrate deeper into the city.

As Araceli recounts: "But we had to go around by the Calle San Gil, because we could not get through directly" — suggesting that the street was heavily contested or barricaded, forcing defenders to take alternate routes. (028-040)

### The Bridge Corridor

Together with the Calle de San Pedro and La Cuchillería, the Calle de San Gil formed one of the main arteries leading toward the bridge. (006-014) During the heaviest fighting, these streets became almost impassable — choked with rubble, barricades, and the casualties of street-to-street combat.

The proximity to the bridge made this area a natural focal point for French assaults, and the cannon at the San Gil entrance served as one of the inner defensive rings protecting the city's core.
