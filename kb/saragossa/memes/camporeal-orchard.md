# Camporeal Orchard

## Overview

The **Camporeal Orchard** (Huerto de Camporeal) was an orchard on the eastern side of Saragossa's defensive perimeter in the Las Tenerías quarter. During the Second Siege, it became a contested zone as French forces attempted to break through the Spanish eastern defenses.

## Battlefield Role

The orchard lay adjacent to the [[battery-of-los-martires|batteries of Los Mártires]] and formed part of the irregular terrain that characterized the fighting in Las Tenerías. In the later stages of the siege, French assault columns pushed toward the orchard and the nearby batteries:

> "Other attacks took place, with better results for them, at our right, toward the orchard of Camporeal and the batteries of Los Mártires." (017-006)

The French success on this flank — advancing through the orchard toward the batteries — forced the defenders to contend with an increasingly precarious position as the siege wore on.

## Las Tenerías Defensive Line

The orchard was situated within a dense network of defensive positions in Las Tenerías. The line included the [[botanical-garden|Botanical Garden]], the Molino of the city, [[casa-de-gonzalez|Casa de González]], and the batteries of Los Mártires, all connected by fortified houses and walls. The orchard's open ground between these positions allowed French infantry to maneuver, making it a point of vulnerability that the Spanish defenders could not fully secure.
