# Trinitarios

The **Convent of the Trinitarios** (Trinitarios Descalzos) was a religious house in Saragossa that became embroiled in the defensive struggle of the Second Siege. Like many of the city's ecclesiastical buildings, it was pressed into service as a military position.

## Role in the Siege

As the French advanced through the city, no religious building was spared from the fighting (001-004). The convents and monasteries of Saragossa became fortresses by necessity. Trinitarios was among the network of religious houses — alongside [[san-francisco]], [[san-diego]], [[las-monicas]], and others — that anchored the Spanish defensive positions.

The French conducted mining operations against Trinitarios, digging towards the convent in their systematic campaign to undermine the city's defences from below. The underground galleries threatened to bring explosives directly beneath the building's foundations.

## Urban Context

Trinitarios stood among a dense cluster of religious edifices in Saragossa. During the siege, the proximity of these buildings — "the churches and convents and hospitals" — became both a strength and a vulnerability, as underground passages between them could be exploited by either side.
