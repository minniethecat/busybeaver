# Casa de los Duendes

## Overview

The **Casa de los Duendes** ("House of the Goblins") was a notable building in Saragossa, referenced during the Napoleonic [[siege-warfare|siege]] in Galdós's account. Its distinctive name suggests a house with local legend or unusual architectural character.

## Location

The Casa de los Duendes was situated in Saragossa, appearing in the later chapters of the siege narrative. It is mentioned in connection with the street-level fighting that characterized the final phase of the Second Siege.

## In the Narrative

The house appears in the context of the desperate street fighting that consumed Saragossa during the closing stages of the siege. As the French advanced house by house through the city (002-025), buildings like the Casa de los Duendes became waypoints in the urban battlefield. The defenders used existing structures — houses, convents, and towers — as improvised defensive positions.

Its striking name reflects the Spanish tradition of naming houses after local stories or distinctive features. The "goblins" referenced in the name suggest a building known to the neighborhood for unusual history or appearance.

## Context

The Casa de los Duendes was one of many houses across Saragossa that feature in the dense urban geography of the siege narrative. Buildings acquired tactical significance based on position, defensibility, and proximity to contested zones. The house-to-house nature of the fighting meant that even individual structures could become battlegrounds.
