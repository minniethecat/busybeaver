# Convent de los Fecetas

## Overview

The **Convent de los Fecetas** was a religious house in Saragossa whose walls were integrated into the city's defensive perimeter. It was physically connected to the [[san-augustine|Augustine friary]] by linking walls, forming part of the network of convent-fortresses that defined the Saragossan defense:

> The walls that joined the walls of the Convent de los Fecetas with those of the Augustine friary. (003-013)

## Location and Structure

The convent stood adjacent to the [[san-augustine|Augustine friary]], with connecting walls between the two religious houses. This physical linkage was significant: during the [[siege-warfare|siege]], the defenders turned convents and monasteries into interconnected strongpoints, using their thick stone walls as natural fortifications.

## Role in the Siege

The Convent de los Fecetas was part of the dense network of fortified religious buildings that made Saragossa so difficult to capture. The defenders connected adjacent convent walls with barricades and earthworks, creating a continuous defensive line that forced the French to take each building individually.

Like other convents in the city — [[las-monicas|Las Mónicas]], [[santa-engracia|Santa Engracia]], [[san-jose|San José]] — the Convent de los Fecetas was pressed into military service. Its walls became part of the bulwark against French assault, and its connection to the Augustine friary allowed defenders to move between positions without exposing themselves to fire.
