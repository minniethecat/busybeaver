# Walloon Guards

The **Walloon Guards** (Guardia Valona) were an elite infantry unit of the Spanish army that fought with distinction during the Second Siege of Saragossa. Originally recruited from the Walloon region of the Spanish Netherlands, the guards were among the most professional troops available to the Spanish defenders.

## The Sortie of 31 December

The Walloon Guards played a key role in the successful sortie of 31 December, where they fought alongside other elite units:

> "While the volunteers of Huesca, the grenadiers of Palafox, and the Walloon guards defeated the French infantry, the squadron of Numancia and Olivenza cavalry cautiously emerged through the Puerta de Sancho." (009-017)

Their action on the French left flank allowed the cavalry to emerge and complete the encirclement of the retreating French forces.

## Defence of San José

The Walloon Guards also served at the redoubt of [[san-jose]], fighting under [[Renovales]] alongside the [[volunteers-of-huesca]], the Valencia volunteers, and the militia of Soria. The fortress came under intense artillery bombardment, and though its walls crumbled, the defenders held through four hours of "incessant fire by powerful artillery" (011-008).

## Historical Significance

The Walloon Guards were one of the few professional guard units available to the Spanish forces during the Peninsular War. Their presence at Saragossa elevated the military credibility of the defence, providing a core of disciplined troops around which volunteer and militia units could rally.
