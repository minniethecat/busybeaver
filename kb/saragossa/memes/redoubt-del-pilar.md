# Redoubt del Pilar

## Overview

The **Redoubt del Pilar** was a defensive fortification in Saragossa, part of the outer ring of strongpoints that protected the city during the [[siege-warfare|siege]]. It is mentioned alongside the battery of Los Mártires as one of the positions where the defenders mounted a spirited resistance:

> In the redoubt del Pilar, in the battery of Los Mártires, a heroic defense was made. (006-006)

## Location and Structure

The redoubt stood near the [[battery-of-los-martires|battery of Los Mártires]], forming part of the interconnected defensive network. Redoubts were typically small, enclosed fortifications — often improvised from earthworks, rubble, and stone — designed to provide protected firing positions for infantry and light artillery.

## Role in the Siege

The Redoubt del Pilar was one of many forward positions manned by the Saragossan defenders. Along with the battery of Los Mártires, it helped form the first line of resistance against French assaults. These forward works were critical: they slowed the French advance, inflicted casualties, and bought time for the defenders to reinforce positions deeper in the city.

The redoubt's association with the [[virgen-del-pilar|Virgin del Pilar]] in name reflects the religious character of the Spanish resistance, where sacred symbols were woven into military language and fortified positions often took the names of saints and religious figures.

Redoubts and batteries like this were typically held by mixed forces of regular soldiers, volunteers, and armed citizens — the same improvisational force structure seen at the [[huerva|Huerva]] bridge redoubt under [[domingo-larripa|Don Domingo Larripa]].
