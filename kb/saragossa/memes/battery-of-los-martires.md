# Battery of Los Mártires

## Overview

The **Battery of Los Mártires** was a Spanish defensive artillery position located near the [[los-martires|Los Mártires]] area in the Las Tenerías quarter of Saragossa. It served as an auxiliary battery supporting the city's front-line defenses during the Second Siege.

## Combat Role

The battery was part of the inner defensive ring, operating alongside the [[botanical-garden|Botanical Garden]] battery and the [[torre-del-pino|Torre del Pino]]. As described in the narrative, these positions "were less important than the two bodies holding the advanced positions, and served as auxiliaries" (009-002).

During intensified French assaults, the batteries of Los Mártires and the Botanical Garden became focal points of enemy fire:

> "They concentrated their fire on the batteries of Los Mártires and the Botanical Garden, so they continued operations with greater vigor on that side." (011-001)

## Position in the Defensive Line

The battery was situated on the eastern side of the city's defenses, near the orchard of [[camporeal-orchard|Camporeal]]. In the later stages of the siege, French columns advancing through this sector attempted to break through toward the batteries of Los Mártires, while the defenders fired from fortified houses and wall towers to repel them (017-006).
