# Plaza de la Magdalena

## Overview

The Plaza de la Magdalena (also called the Plazuela de la Magdalena) was a central plaza in the Arrabal district of Saragossa. During the Second Siege of Saragossa (1808–1809), it became a critical rallying point and defensive stronghold for Spanish forces. The plaza was fortified with cannon defending its street entrances, and it served as the hub around which the defense of the Arrabal was organized.

## Location

The Plaza de la Magdalena was situated in the Arrabal, the suburb on the left bank of the [[ebro|Ebro River]]. It stood at the convergence of several key streets: the [[calle-de-palomar|Calle de Palomar]], the [[calle-de-pabostre|Calle de Pabostre]], and the street leading toward [[puerta-quemada|Puerta Quemada]]. From the plaza, defenders could hear the roar of battle at the far end of the Calle de Puerta Quemada, underscoring its position at the heart of the besieged suburb.

The parish of La Magdalena encompassed the surrounding area, and its residents — including the farmer Codé — were among the civilian defenders who manned the barricades and cannon.

## Role in the Siege

The Plaza de la Magdalena was one of the most heavily contested positions in the Arrabal. After the fall of key positions like [[san-augustine|San Augustine]] and [[las-monicas|Las Mónicas]], Spanish forces regrouped at the plaza, which they had fortified with artillery. The street entrances feeding into the plaza were defended with cannon, enabling the defenders to repel multiple French advances.

> "The street entrances which we were holding in the Plazuela de la Magdalena were defended with cannon, and repulsed the enemy's two advances from the Calle de Palomar and the Calle de Pabostre." (026-004)

The plaza was the destination for the [[extremadura-battalion|Battalion of the Estremadura]] as they moved through the Calle de Palomar to reinforce the defense. The French objective of capturing Puerta Quemada was tied to the Plaza de la Magdalena — from Puerta Quemada they could rake the street with artillery fire toward the plaza itself.

> "The enemy tried to take the Calle de Pabostre in order to get possession of Puerta Quemada, an important point whence they could rake with their artillery the street of the same name towards the Plaza de la Magdalena." (023-001)

The fighting at the Plaza de la Magdalena was notably fierce. The battle that began in the afternoon continued with undiminished intensity into the night.

> "If the battle was bloody until three, the hour when we centred in the Plaza de la Magdalena, it was not less bloody there until night." (024-001)

## Narrative Appearances

- **(002-037)**: The beggar Sursum Corda recounts the story of Codé, a farmer from the parish of La Magdalena, who hid under a cannon on the Calle de la Parra to escape advancing French troops.
- **(023-001)**: The Battalion of Estremadura marches along the Calle de Palomar and enters the Plaza de la Magdalena as the battle intensifies.
- **(024-001)**: The battle reaches the Plaza de la Magdalena at three o'clock and continues fiercely until nightfall.
- **(025-038)**: A call for reinforcements reports that men are needed at the Magdalena position.
- **(026-004)**: The cannon defending the Plazuela de la Magdalena repulse two French advances from the surrounding streets.

## Notable Figures

The parish of La Magdalena was home to **Codé**, a farmer who became a local legend for his actions with a cannon on the Calle de la Parra. When the French surrounded his position, Codé hid beneath the cannon itself while the enemy passed by, and later returned to continue firing upon them.
