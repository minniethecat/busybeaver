The **Entry of the French** into Saragossa occurred in December 1808 when French forces broke through the outer defenses and entered the city proper, marking the beginning of the intense urban combat phase of the second siege.

## The Breach

After French forces overran the outer fortifications, they pushed into the city streets. The entry into Saragossa was not an occupation but the beginning of a house-to-house struggle — "the French entered the city" only to find the defenders contesting every building. Unlike a typical siege where breaching the walls meant capitulation, Saragossa's defense continued block by block.

## Urban Warfare

The French entry transformed the siege into one of history's most brutal urban battles. The defenders continued to resist from convents, houses, and barricades erected across every street. "A barricade made by herself" (002-025) characterizes the improvised nature of the defense.

## Contemporary Account

> When we entered the city, some by the Puerta del Carmen, some by the Portillo, all the pieces of the redoubts and forts of Mediodía poured a fire against the columns which were coming after us. The two sorties combined had done damage enough to the French. In addition to losing many men, a small part of their entrenchments had been made useless to them, and we had possessed ourselves of a considerable number of their tools. Besides this, the official engineers that Butron took with him on that daring venture had had time to examine the works of the besiegers, and measure them, and could give descriptions of them to the commanding general.
>
