# Calle de Palomar

## Overview

The Calle de Palomar was a street in the Arrabal (suburb) district of Saragossa that became a significant defensive line during the Second Siege of Saragossa (1808–1809). Running parallel to other key streets of the suburb, it served as both a fighting position for Spanish defenders and a route of movement between critical points such as the Plaza de la Magdalena and the area around the Convent of San Augustine.

## Location

The Calle de Palomar was located in the Arrabal, the densely built suburb of Saragossa. It ran parallel to the Calle de Pabostre and connected to the Plaza de la Magdalena at one end, with its southern reach approaching the area held by the Convent of San Augustine. From the Calle de Palomar, defenders could hear the roar of battle at the end of the Calle de Puerta Quemada, indicating its proximity to that key defensive gate.

## Role in the Siege

During the Second Siege, the Calle de Palomar was a contested defensive position. Spanish troops, including men of the [[battalion-of-las-penas-de-san-pedro|Battalion of Las Peñas de San Pedro]] and the [[extremadura-battalion|Battalion of the Estremadura]], occupied houses along the street and used them as firing positions against French detachments.

> "During this fight we were in the houses about the Calle de Palomar, firing upon the French detachment sent to assault the convent." (020-008)

The French attempted to advance through the Calle de Palomar toward the Plazuela de la Magdalena, where the street entrances were defended with cannon. The defenders repulsed two French advances — one from the Calle de Palomar and another from the [[calle-de-pabostre|Calle de Pabostre]].

> "The street entrances which we were holding in the Plazuela de la Magdalena were defended with cannon, and repulsed the enemy's two advances from the Calle de Palomar and the Calle de Pabostre." (026-004)

After the French captured [[san-augustine|San Augustine]] and [[las-monicas|Las Mónicas]], they considered themselves masters of the suburb, as these positions gave them direct access to threaten the Calle de Palomar — a route through which they could push deeper into the Arrabal.

> "As the possession of San Augustine and Las Mónicas permitted them to threaten that central point by the easy way to the Calle de Palomar, they already considered themselves masters of the suburb." (023-001)
