# Puerta del Carmen

The **Puerta del Carmen** was one of the principal gates of Saragossa. During the Second Siege, it was among the entry points through which French forces penetrated the city's outer defences (002-025).

## Role in the Siege

Like the [[puerta-de-sancho]], [[puerta-quemada]], and the [[Portillo]], the Puerta del Carmen was a critical node in the city's defensive perimeter. The gates were the narrow passages through which the besiegers sought to force their way into Saragossa, and each became the scene of repeated assaults and desperate defences.

## After the Breach

Once the French had breached the walls and entered through the gates, the fighting moved into the streets. The Puerta del Carmen, like other entry points, marked the boundary between the outer defensive line and the urban battlefield of [[house-to-house-combat]] that followed.
