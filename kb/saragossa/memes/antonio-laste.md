# Antonio Laste

**Antonio Laste** was the first sergeant of the Fourth Company of Regulars in the Spanish defence of Saragossa during the Second Siege. His house and deeds are mentioned by the beggar [[sursum-corda]] in his extended monologue to [[gabriel-araceli]] and [[don-roque]].

## Deeds During the Siege

[[sursum-corda|Sursum Corda]] points out Laste's house among the streets of Saragossa and summarises his notable actions:

> "Do you see this house? Well, here lives Antonio Laste, first sergeant of the Fourth Company of Regulars, and you must know he saved from the treasury sixteen thousand, four hundred pesos, and took from the French the candles that they stole from the church." (002-031)

The beggar was prevented from giving further details by his impatient listeners, but references "the details of the heroismism of Antonio Laste" (002-032), suggesting the sergeant's reputation was well established among the defenders.

Laste's actions combined financial stewardship — rescuing a substantial sum from the treasury — with religious zeal in recovering church property desecrated by the [[french-army|French forces]].
