## San Lazarus in the Siege of Saragossa

The monastery of San Lazarus was a religious site on the outskirts of Saragossa that became a key defensive position during the siege. Located near the Ebro River, it formed part of a network of fortified positions protecting the approaches to the city (006-016).

### Strategic Crossfire Position

San Lazarus was a critical defensive node. From its position, defenders could engage the French Twelfth regiment as it advanced along the Barcelona road. Together with the position called El Marcelo, San Lazarus created a crossfire zone that challenged the French advance with "impetuous intrepidity" (006-017).

Araceli describes the view from the riverbank: "We saw... close to the bridge the little monastery of Altabas, yonder that of San Lazarus, and further on the Monastery of Jesus." This line of monasteries along the river formed an outer defensive perimeter, each one a fortified position guarding one approach to the city.

### Part of the Monastic Defense Line

San Lazarus was one of several monasteries pressed into military service during the siege. Along with Altabas and the Monastery of Jesus, it formed a chain of fortified religious houses that guarded the Ebro's approaches, demonstrating how thoroughly Saragossa's ecclesiastical infrastructure was integrated into the city's defense.
