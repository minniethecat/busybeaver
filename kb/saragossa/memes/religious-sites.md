# Religious Sites of Saragossa

Saragossa's religious houses played a decisive role in the city's defense.
Convents were fortified and connected by barricades; churches became hospitals, arsenals, and rallying points.
This page groups the religious sites that appear in the narrative.

## Notes

During the siege, many convents were incorporated into the defensive perimeter.
The Convent of San José, Convent of Jerusalem, and Convent of Santa Engracia became famous strongpoints in their own right.
See also [[fortifications-and-defenses]] for the secular defensive positions of the city.
