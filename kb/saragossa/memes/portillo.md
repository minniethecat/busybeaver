# Portillo

## Overview

One of the main gates of Saragossa, site of a large semicircular battery that joined the walls of the Convent de los Fecetas with those of the [[augustine-montoria|Augustine]] friars' convent.

## Location

Portillo is situated in Western Saragossa. It is one of the key locations in Galdós's *Saragossa*, appearing repeatedly as the narrative traces the geography of the [[siege-warfare|besieged city]].

## Role in the Siege

> There was in the Portillo gate a large semicircular battery that joined the walls of the Convent de los Fecetas with those of the Augustine friars' convent. (003-013)

As a defensive_position, Portillo played a significant role in the [[resistance|defense]] of Saragossa. Its first appearance at PN (003-013) establishes its presence in the novel's spatial imagination.
