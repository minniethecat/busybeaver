# Huesca

## Overview

**Huesca** is the capital city of the province of the same name in [[spain|Aragon]], northern Spain. During the [[siege-warfare|sieges of Saragossa]], Huesca contributed a notable body of volunteer militia — the [[volunteers-of-huesca]] — who became one of the most frequently mentioned fighting units in Galdós's account.

## Location

Huesca lies in the region of Aragon, northeast of Saragossa. Its strategic position made it one of the nearby cities from which volunteers could travel to reinforce the besieged capital. The city and its surrounding region had strong cultural and economic ties to Saragossa.

## Role in the Siege

The principal contribution of Huesca to the [[resistance|defense]] of Saragossa was its volunteer battalion. These volunteers are mentioned in action across multiple engagements:

### At the Redoubt of El Pilar

The volunteers of Huesca fought at the redoubt of Our Lady of the Pillar:

> While the volunteers of Huesca, the grenadiers of Palafox, and the Walloon guards defeated the French infantry, the squadron of Numancia and Olivenza cavalry cautiously emerged through the Puerta de Sancho. (009-017)

### At the Fortress of San José

Under the command of [[renovales|Renovales]], the Huesca volunteers defended the fortress of [[san-jose|San José]] alongside the [[valencia-volunteers]] and [[walloon-guards]]:

> Renovales defended the fortress of San José with the Huesca and Valencia volunteers, the Walloon Guards, and militia of Soria. It was assaulted by four hours of incessant fire by powerful artillery. (011-008)

### Night Sorties

The volunteers of Huesca participated in night operations after being relieved from guard duty:

> Augustine Montoria and I stood guard with our battalion in the Molino until after nightfall, the hour when we were relieved by the Huesca volunteers. (014-001)

### Command

General [[villacampa]] commanded the volunteers of Huesca, appearing among the military leaders directing the city's defense.
