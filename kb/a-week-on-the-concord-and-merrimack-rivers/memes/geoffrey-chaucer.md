# Geoffrey Chaucer

## Biography

Geoffrey Chaucer (~1343–1400) was a English poet and writer. English poet, author of The Canterbury Tales — praised by [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] for homely truth and naturalness.

## Works / Significance

Geoffrey Chaucer's literary achievements place them among the most influential figures in Western literature. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] engages with their work extensively throughout *A Week*, treating them as essential voices in the tradition of great poetry.

## In Thoreau's Text

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] references and discusses Geoffrey Chaucer at multiple points during [[the-1839-boat-journey|the river journey]], drawing on their work to illustrate his arguments about poetry, truth, history, and the human condition. Their presence in *A Week* reflects [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s deep engagement with the literary tradition and his conviction that the greatest poetry transcends its [[history|historical]] moment to speak with enduring authority.
