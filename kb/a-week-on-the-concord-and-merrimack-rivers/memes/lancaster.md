## Description

Lancaster is a town in Worcester County, Massachusetts, situated along the [[nashua-river|Nashua River]], one of the largest tributaries of the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]]. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] describes the [[nashua|Nashua]] as flowing "from [[mount-wachusett|Wachusett Mountain]], through Lancaster, Groton, and other towns, where it has formed well-known elm-shaded meadows." (004-1192)

## On the River Route

Although not directly visited during the 1839 boat journey, Lancaster appears in [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s [[thought|reflection]]s as a place of personal memory.

He recalls a previous excursion: "Far away from here, in Lancaster, with another companion, I have crossed the broad valley of the [[nashua|Nashua]]." (004-1196) This reminiscence illustrates [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s deep familiarity with the rivers and towns of the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]] watershed.
