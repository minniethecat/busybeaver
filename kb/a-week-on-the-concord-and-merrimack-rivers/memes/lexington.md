## Description

Lexington is a town in Middlesex County, Massachusetts, located near [[concord-mass|Concord]]. It is famous as the site of the first battle of the [[american-revolution|American Revolution]] on April 19, 1775.

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] pairs Lexington with [[concord-mass|Concord]] as twin symbols of American independence: "And Lexington and [[concord-mass|Concord]] stand / By no Laconian rill." (002-100) The reference contrasts the American towns with ancient Sparta, suggesting that American liberty springs from its own soil.

## On the River Route

Lexington lies within the broader [[concord-mass|Concord]] region but is not directly on the river route. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] invokes Lexington as a [[history|historical]] and patriotic reference rather than a geographic waypoint.

He later alludes to the Battle of Lexington when describing colonial heroes who, "when the news of the massacre at Lexington arrived, and straightway left their ploughs in the furrow, and repaired to the scene of action." (006-549)
