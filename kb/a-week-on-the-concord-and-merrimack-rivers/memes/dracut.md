## Overview

Dracut is a town in Middlesex County, Massachusetts, situated on the north
bank of the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack River]], downstream from [[lowell-mass|Lowell]]. The brothers' boat
passed Dracut on Sunday at midday, when the river was about a quarter of a
mile wide at that point.

## In Thoreau's Text

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] describes the approach to Dracut during the Sunday chapter. The
broad river at this reach carried the sound of their oars across the water
to the shore:

> (003-1020) and Dracut, at noon, here a quarter of a mile wide, the
> rattling of our oars was echoed over the water to those villages, and
> their slight sounds to us. Their harbors lay as smooth and fairy-like
> as the Lido, or Syracuse, or Rhodes, in our imagination, while, like
> some strange roving craft, we flitted past what seemed the dwellings
> of noble home-staying men, seemingly as conspicuous as if on an
> eminence, or floating upon a tide which came up to those villagers'
> breasts.

The passage captures the [[silence|quiet]], almost dreamlike quality of the river at
midday, with the brothers gliding [[history|past]] the settled shore and hearing the
distant sound of children repeating their catechism from a cottage near
the water.

## Geography

Dracut lies on the north bank of the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]], across the river from
the city of [[lowell-mass|Lowell]]. The river here, at a quarter of a mile wide, is broad
and placid, with the south bank dominated by [[lowell-mass|Lowell]]'s mills and the north
bank retaining a more rural character of cottages and farmland.
