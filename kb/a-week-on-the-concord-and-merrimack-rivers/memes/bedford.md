## Description

Bedford is a town in Middlesex County, Massachusetts, located near the [[concord-river-mass|Concord River]]. The river flows between Bedford and its neighboring towns, forming part of the natural boundary. In [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s time, Bedford was a rural farming community whose meadows and fields bordered the [[concord-river-mass|Concord River]]. (001-019)

## On the River Route

As the [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] brothers set out on the [[concord-river-mass|Concord River]] at the beginning of their voyage, they passed by Bedford's riverbanks. From the bridge between [[carlisle|Carlisle]] and Bedford, [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] observed men haying far off in the meadows, a [[history|past]]oral scene characteristic of the [[concord-mass|Concord]] countryside in late summer. (002-652)

Bedford is listed among the towns bordering the [[concord-river-mass|Concord River]], alongside Framingham, [[sudbury|Sudbury]], [[carlisle|Carlisle]], and Billerica. (003-295) The [[concord-river-mass|Concord River]] meanders "all through the [[concord-mass|Concord]], Bedford, and Billerica" meadows. (003-510)

Further downriver on the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]], [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] notes another "Bedford" — Bedford, New Hampshire — where there were more falls in the town. (005-1552) He later camped on the west bank in the town of Bedford, New Hampshire. (005-1568) Rowing between [[manchester-nh|Manchester]] and Bedford, New Hampshire, they passed a boat anchored in the middle of the stream. (006-045) "From Bedford and [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]] have been boated" goods down the river. (006-048) One Moore, of Bedford, having clay on his farm, was among those who used the river for transportation. (006-050)

## Historical Context

Bedford, Massachusetts was incorporated in 1729. Its location along the [[concord-river-mass|Concord River]] placed it within the network of small farming communities that characterized the region in [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s era. The town's meadows and agricultural lands formed part of the [[nature|landscape]] [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] knew intimately from his [[concord-mass|Concord]] home.

## Thoreau's Observations

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] invokes Bedford [[poetry|poetical]]ly through [[william-shakespeare|Shakespeare]]: "Bedford, most noble Bedford, I must not blot thee from my memory." (003-231) This literary allusion elevates the humble Massachusetts town by associating it with the Duke of Bedford from [[william-shakespeare|Shakespeare]]'s histories. The men haying in the meadows between [[carlisle|Carlisle]] and Bedford represent the enduring rural character of the [[concord-river-mass|Concord River]] valley. (002-652)
