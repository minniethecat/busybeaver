# Ossian

## Biography

Ossian (3rd century–3rd century) was a Scottish poet and writer. Legendary Gaelic [[poetry|bard]] — [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s model of older, grander, elemental poetry.

## Works / Significance

Ossian's literary achievements place them among the most influential figures in Western literature. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] engages with their work extensively throughout *A Week*, treating them as essential voices in the tradition of great poetry.

## In Thoreau's Text

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] references and discusses Ossian at multiple points during [[the-1839-boat-journey|the river journey]], drawing on their work to illustrate his arguments about poetry, truth, history, and the human condition. Their presence in *A Week* reflects [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s deep engagement with the literary tradition and his conviction that the greatest poetry transcends its [[history|historical]] moment to speak with enduring authority.
