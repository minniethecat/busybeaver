## Description

Moosehillock is a mountain in the western [[white-mountains|White Mountains]] region of New Hampshire. The name, combining "moose" and "hillock," evokes the wild character of the northern New England [[nature|landscape]]. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] mentions it in the Sunday chapter of *A Week*, grouping it with other peaks visible from the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]]'s broad waters.

## On the River Route

On Sunday, as [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] surveyed the mountainous horizon from the river, he described the peaks "slumbering like tumuli of Titans, with the peaks of Moosehillock, the Haystack, and [[kearsarge|Kearsarge]] reflected in its waters" (003-1128). The description casts Moosehillock among a row of slumbering giants, their forms doubled in the still surface of the great ponds.

The image is characteristic of [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s Sunday [[thought|reflection]]s — the [[nature|landscape]] takes on a dreamlike, mythic quality as the brothers drift through the widened river.
