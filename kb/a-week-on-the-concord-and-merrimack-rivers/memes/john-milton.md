# John Milton

## Biography

John Milton (1608–1674) was a English figure of lasting significance. English poet, author of Paradise Lost — referenced by [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] in discussions of epic poetry.

## Works / Significance

John Milton's contributions to literature and Western intellectual tradition are foundational. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] draws on their work and example at pivotal moments in *A Week*, finding in them models of intellectual integrity and spiritual [[wisdom|insight]].

## In Thoreau's Text

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] references John Milton during his [[thought|reflection]]s on [[the-1839-boat-journey|the river journey]], weaving their ideas and example into his meditations on truth, virtue, and the examined life. Their presence in the text reflects [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s characteristic synthesis of classical learning with immediate, personal experience — the ancient philosopher or poet speaks across centuries to illuminate the [[concord-river-mass|Concord River]] in 1839.
