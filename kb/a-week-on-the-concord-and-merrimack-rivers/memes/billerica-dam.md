## Description

The Billerica Dam on the [[concord-river-mass|Concord River]] in Billerica, Massachusetts, was a crucial structure along the river route. It marked the transition from the free-flowing [[concord-river-mass|Concord River]] to the [[middlesex-canal|Middlesex Canal]] system, which bypassed the falls and connected to the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]].

## On the River Route

The brothers reach Billerica after traveling about seven miles down the [[concord-river-mass|Concord River]]. (002-660) At Billerica Falls, they leave the [[concord-mass|Concord]] and enter the canal. (003-507, 003-525) The falls cause the river to contract. (003-507) The dam is noted in the context of the tension between nature and industry. (002-613)

## Historical Context

Billerica, settled behind the woods, is "ancient Billerica, (Villarica?) now in its dotage." (003-190, 003-195) Its Indian name was Shawshine. (003-196) The dam was built to power mills and feed the [[middlesex-canal|Middlesex Canal]]. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] reflects that nature will have leveled it eventually. (002-523)

## Thoreau's Observations

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] observes that "nature will have leveled the Billerica dam," suggesting natural forces prevail over human works. (002-523) He notes the fish migrations affected by the dam, particularly the shad. "Billerica dam?" is posed as a challenge not to despair when whole races leave no memorial. (002-613) The vastness of the Billerica night and its wild [[music|musical]] sound also capture him. (003-1979)
