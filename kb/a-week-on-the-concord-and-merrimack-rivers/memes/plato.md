# Plato

## Biography

Plato (~428 BCE–~348 BCE) was a Greek figure of lasting significance. Greek philosopher — [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] engages with Platonic ideas about truth, beauty, and the ideal.

## Works / Significance

Plato's contributions to philosophy and Western intellectual tradition are foundational. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] draws on their work and example at pivotal moments in *A Week*, finding in them models of intellectual integrity and spiritual [[wisdom|insight]].

## In Thoreau's Text

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] references Plato during his [[thought|reflection]]s on [[the-1839-boat-journey|the river journey]], weaving their ideas and example into his meditations on truth, virtue, and the examined life. Their presence in the text reflects [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s characteristic synthesis of classical learning with immediate, personal experience — the ancient philosopher or poet speaks across centuries to illuminate the [[concord-river-mass|Concord River]] in 1839.
