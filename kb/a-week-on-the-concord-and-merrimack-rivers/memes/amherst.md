## Overview

Amherst is a town in Hillsborough County, New Hampshire, located west of the
[[merrimack-river|Merrimack River]]. Settled in the early 18th century, it was one of the towns
along the broader [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]] Valley region that [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] and his brother John
passed near during their 1839 boat journey, though Amherst itself lies
several miles west of the river.

## In Thoreau's Text

Amherst appears in a passage where [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] quotes from local histories to
sketch the lives of early settlers along the river. The town is named as the
home of Robert Means, an Irish immigrant who established himself in New
Hampshire before the Revolution:

> (006-527) Hon. Robert Means, who died Jan. 24, 1823, at the age of 80, was
> for a long period a resident in Amherst. He was a native of Ireland. In
> 1764 he came to this country, where, by his industry and application to
> business, he acquired a large property, and great respect.

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] includes this biographical sketch among a series of similar notices
drawn from town histories, illustrating his interest in the individual lives
that shaped the communities along the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]].
