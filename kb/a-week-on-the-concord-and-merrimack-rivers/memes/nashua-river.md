## Description

The Nashua River is one of the largest tributaries of the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]], flowing from [[mount-wachusett|Wachusett Mountain]] through [[lancaster|Lancaster]] and other towns before joining the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]] at [[nashua|Nashua]], New Hampshire. (004-1192) Its mouth now resounds with the noise of manufacturing. (004-1184)

## On the River Route

The brothers reach the [[nashua|Nashua]] on Monday, their third day. They row up into it to see early settlements. (004-1145) They cross its broad valley, which they had long observed from a distance. (004-1197) The [[nashua|Nashua]] is among the tributaries they explore on their journey up the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]].

## Historical Context

The [[nashua|Nashua]] valley was the site of early conflicts between Native Americans and settlers. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] records that hostilities were pursued as far as the [[nashua|Nashua]], where they "fought across the stream." (004-1376) Settlers planted along its banks. (004-1157) Later, the river valley became a center of manufacturing.

## Thoreau's Observations

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] describes the [[nashua|Nashua]] as "one of the largest tributaries" of the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]] (004-1192), noting its origin at [[mount-wachusett|Wachusett Mountain]]. He observes the "hazy [[nashua|Nashua]] valley" (004-1319) and the village at its mouth. (004-1459) The river is mentioned alongside Baker's, Mad, [[souhegan|Souhegan]], and other tributaries. (003-1152)
