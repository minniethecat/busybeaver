## Description

The Contoocook River is a tributary of the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack River]] in south-central New Hampshire. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] listed it alongside the [[piscataquoag|Piscataquoag]], [[suncook-river|Suncook]], and Soucook rivers as one of the streams whose waters "mingled in" with the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]]. (003-1153) The river flows southward from the Contoocook Lake area through the towns of Henniker and Contoocook before joining the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]] near [[penacook|Penacook]].

## On the River Route

On the return journey, approaching the end of their voyage, the brothers came upon "an island at the mouth of the Contoocook before daybreak." (007-602) This island marked a notable feature of the lower [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]] as they neared the end of their week on the river. The brief mention in the narrative captures the [[silence|quiet]], pre-dawn moments of the final day of travel, with the Contoocook's mouth serving as a waypoint on the homeward leg.
