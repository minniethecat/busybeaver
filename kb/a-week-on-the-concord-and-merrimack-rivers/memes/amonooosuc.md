## Description

The Amonoosuc River (also spelled Amonoosuck) is a wild mountain stream in the [[white-mountains|White Mountains]] of New Hampshire. It forms part of the headwaters of the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack River]] system. As the [[pemigewasset|Pemigewasset River]] ascends toward its source, it becomes the Amonoosuck.

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] describes crossing it on "prostrate trees" and calls it the "Wild Amonoosuck, whose puny channel was crossed at a stride." (007-429)

## On the River Route

The Amonoosuck appears in the "Thursday" chapter as [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] traces the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]] to its sources. After the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]] becomes the [[pemigewasset|Pemigewasset]], and the [[pemigewasset|Pemigewasset]] in turn diminishes, the brothers crossed the Amonoosuck on fallen trees.

There they "breathed the free air of Unappropriated Land." (007-425) This remote mountain stream represents the farthest reach of the river system [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] set out to explore.
