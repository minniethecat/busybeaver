## Biography

Marcus Porcius Cato Uticensis (95–46 BCE), known as Cato the Younger, was a Roman statesman and Stoic philosopher. The great-grandson of Cato the Elder, he was renowned for his incorruptible moral integrity, his opposition to Julius Caesar, and his commitment to the Roman Republic. After Caesar's victory in the civil war, Cato took his own life at Utica rather than accept pardon — a death that made him a symbol of republican virtue and resistance to tyranny.

## Works / Significance

Cato left no philosophical treatises, but his life itself became a philosophical argument. Seneca, Cicero, and later writers celebrated him as the embodiment of Stoic principles. His refusal to compromise with Caesar, and his choice of suicide over submission, made him an enduring symbol in Western political thought. The Founding Fathers of the United States, particularly George Washington, drew inspiration from Cato's example. Joseph Addison's play *Cato* (1713) was widely performed and read in 18th-century America.

## In Thoreau's Text

Cato appears once in *A Week*, near the end of the *Friday* chapter, as [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] reflects on the [[imagination|poetic imagination]]'s power to ennoble its subjects:

> It is the imagination of poets which puts those brave speeches into the mouths of their heroes. They may feign that Cato's last words were: "The earth, the air, and seas I know, and all / The joys and horrors of their peace and wars; / And now will view the Gods' state and the stars." (008-1227)

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s reference is layered: he acknowledges that the words attributed to Cato are poetic invention, not [[history|historical]] record. Yet the choice of Cato as the exemplar is significant — he selects the figure most associated with principled self-destruction for a meditation on how imagination transforms [[history|historical]] figures into moral archetypes. The passage occurs in the context of [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s [[thought|reflection]]s on departing mountain-top [[dream|vision]]s, a fitting backdrop for Cato's final gaze toward the stars.
