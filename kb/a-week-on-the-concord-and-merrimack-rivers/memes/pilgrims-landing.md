# Pilgrims' Landing at Plymouth

## Overview

The landing of the Mayflower Pilgrims at [[plymouth|Plymouth]] in November 1620 established the first permanent English settlement in New England. Driven by [[religion|religious]] persecution, a group of Separatists crossed the Atlantic aboard the *Mayflower*, settling at what became Plymouth Colony. This event forms the deep [[history|historical]] backdrop against which [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s river journey unfolds.

## Relevance to the Journey

Though [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] does not explicitly narrate the Pilgrims' landing, the [[nature|landscape]] of his journey was shaped by the pattern of settlement that [[plymouth|Plymouth]] initiated. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] notes how the first settlers were attracted to the [[concord-river-mass|Concord River]] valley, drawn by the promise of fertile meadows and waterpower (001-003).

He draws on Edward Johnson's *Wonder-working Providence of Sions Saviour in New England*, a chronicle of the first generation of settlers, to evoke the determination of those who established these towns (001-146). The early customs of Puritan towns, including the use of a "cage" for offenders, speak to the strict social order the Pilgrims imposed (003-580).

The frontier condition in which these settlers lived persisted for generations (004-1328). [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] also references [[john-smith|Captain John Smith]]'s earlier exploration of the New England coast, a precursor to the Pilgrims' landing (003-1268). The Pilgrims' story represents for [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] the intersection of [[religion|faith]], endurance, and the transformation of the [[nature|landscape]].

## Historical Background

This subject belongs to the rich and complex history of New England. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s treatment of [[history|historical]] themes is distinguished by his refusal to accept conventional narratives at face value. The Pilgrims' landing, often romanticized in American memory, becomes in [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s hands part of a deeper meditation on what it means to make a home in a new world.
