Where’er thou sail’st who sailed with me,

Though now thou climbest loftier mounts,

And fairer rivers dost ascend,

Be thou my Muse, my Brother—.

I am bound, I am bound, for a distant shore,

By a lonely isle, by a far Azore,

[P01-001]There it is, there it is, the treasure I seek,

[P01-002]On the barren sands of a desolate creek.

[P01-003]I sailed up a river with a pleasant wind,

[P01-004]New lands, new people, and new thoughts to find;

[P01-005]Many fair reaches and headlands appeared,

[P01-006]And many dangers were there to be feared;

[P01-007]But when I remember where I have been,

[P01-008]And the fair landscapes that I have seen,

[P01-009]THOU seemest the only permanent shore,

[P01-010]The cape never rounded, nor wandered o’er.

[P01-011]Fluminaque obliquis cinxit declivia ripis;

[P01-012]Quæ, diversa locis, partim sorbentur ab ipsa;

[P01-013]In mare perveniunt partim, campoque recepta

[P01-014]Liberioris aquæ, pro ripis litora pulsant.

[P01-015]OVID, Met. I. 39

[P01-016]He confined the rivers within their sloping banks,

[P01-017]Which in different places are part absorbed by the earth,

[P01-018]Part reach the sea, and being received within the plain

[P01-019]Of its freer waters, beat the shore for banks.
