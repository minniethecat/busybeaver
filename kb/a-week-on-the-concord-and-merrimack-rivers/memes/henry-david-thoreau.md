## Biography

**Henry David Thoreau** (July 12, 1817 – May 6, 1862) was an American naturalist, essayist, poet, and philosopher. Born in [[concord-massachusetts|Concord, Massachusetts]], he attended [[harvard-college|Harvard College]] (class of 1837).

Returning to [[concord-mass|Concord]] after [[harvard-college|Harvard]], he became a central figure in the Transcendentalist movement along with [[ralph-waldo-emerson|Ralph Waldo Emerson]], [[nathaniel-hawthorne|Nathaniel Hawthorne]], and [[william-ellery-channing|William Ellery Channing]].

A leading transcendentalist, Thoreau is best known for his book *Walden* (1854), a [[thought|reflection]] upon simple living in natural surroundings, and his essay "Civil Disobedience" (1849), an argument for disobedience to an unjust state.

## In *A Week on the Concord and Merrimack Rivers*

*A Week on the [[concord-mass|Concord]] and [[merrimack-river|Merrimack River]]s* (1849) is Thoreau's first published book, recounting a two-week boat trip he took with his brother **John Thoreau Jr.** in 1839. The brothers departed from [[concord-massachusetts|Concord, Massachusetts]], traveling up the [[concord-river-mass|Concord River]] and down the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack River]] into New Hampshire, before returning by foot.

Though John is never named in the text, the book is an elegy to him — John died of lockjaw in 1842, and Thoreau wrote the book in part as a memorial. The narrative blends travel writing with philosophical [[thought|reflection]]s, literary digressions, and meditations on [[friendship|Friendship]], [[nature|Nature]], poetry, religion, and history.

Thoreau references dozens of classical and contemporary writers throughout the text — [[homer|Homer]], [[william-shakespeare|Shakespeare]], [[john-milton|Milton]], [[plato|Plato]], [[goethe|Goethe]], and many others — creating a rich intertextual tapestry that reflects his wide reading and philosophical engagement.

The journey covers:

- **Saturday** — Departure from [[concord-mass|Concord]] on the [[concord-river|Concord River]]
- **Sunday** — Arrival on the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack River]]

- **Monday** through **Thursday** — Navigation downstream, with stops at towns like [[nashua|Nashua]] and [[thought|reflection]]s on history and nature
- **Friday** — Return journey home
