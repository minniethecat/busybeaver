## Description

Chelmsford is a town in Middlesex County, Massachusetts, located on the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack River]], adjacent to [[lowell-mass|Lowell]] and Billerica. It was settled in 1655 and is known for its granite quarries, which supplied building stone throughout the region. (003-1828) Chelmsford lies between [[tyngsborough|Tyngsborough]] and Billerica along the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]]'s south bank.

## On the River Route

The brothers pass through Chelmsford on their journey. They travel over the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]] between Chelmsford and [[bedford|Bedford]] after entering the larger river. (003-1019) The town is mentioned as one of the New Angle-land communities along their route. (003-296) They note the Glass-house village in Chelmsford (003-1273) and pass through it on their return journey. (008-704, 008-883)

## Historical Context

Chelmsford was founded in 1655 as a farming community. During [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s time, it was known for its granite quarries, which produced the distinctive "Chelmsford granite." (003-1828) Chelmsford suffered during [[king-philips-war|King Philip's War]] (006-493) and later developed as a mill town alongside its larger neighbor, [[lowell-mass|Lowell]]. The town was also the site of an early glass factory.

## Thoreau's Observations

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] notes the Chelmsford granite, "which is quarried in the vicinity" (003-1828), and observes the [[nature|landscape]] between Chelmsford and [[tyngsborough|Tyngsborough]]. (003-1830) He mentions people lounging upon a bridge in Chelmsford (003-543) and records the town's role in colonial history, including the minister of Chelmsford visited by one of the early settlers. (006-491)
