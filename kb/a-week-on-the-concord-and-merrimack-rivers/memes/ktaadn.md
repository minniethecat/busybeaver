## Overview

Mount Ktaadn (now commonly spelled Katahdin) is the highest mountain in
Maine, rising to 5,269 feet at the northern terminus of the Appalachian
Trail. It lies in Baxter State Park in Piscataquis County. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] would
later make Ktaadn the subject of his celebrated essay "Ktaadn," published
posthumously in *The Maine Woods* (1864), based on his 1846 ascent of the
mountain.

## In Thoreau's Text

In *A Week*, Ktaadn appears only in a passing naturalist's observation
during the Saturday chapter. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]], discussing the fish of New England
rivers, notes the distribution of the white chivin at the mountain's base:

> (002-419) Coppermine River. I have caught white chivin of great size in
> the Aboljacknagesic, where it empties into the Penobscot, at the base of
> (002-421) Mount Ktaadn, but no red ones there. The latter variety seems
> not to have been sufficiently observed.

This brief mention — distinguishing the white chivin from the "red ones"
absent at Ktaadn — is characteristic of [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s eye for the natural
history of every place he names. The reference to the Aboljacknagesic
stream and the Penobscot River places Ktaadn in the watershed of Maine's
largest river system.

## Thoreau's Connection

Though Ktaadn receives only a sentence in *A Week*, [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] had already
climbed the mountain in 1846, three years before the book's publication.
His essay "Ktaadn" would later become one of his most important works of
[[nature|wilderness]] writing, containing the famous passage on the "vast, Titanic,
inhuman [[nature|Nature]]" encountered on the mountain's bare summit.
