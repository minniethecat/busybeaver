## Biography

Moses is the central prophetic figure of the Hebrew Bible (Old Testament) and is traditionally regarded as the author of the Torah (the first five books of the Bible). According to the Book of Exodus, he was born to Hebrew parents in Egypt during a time of oppression, hidden in a basket among the reeds of the Nile to escape Pharaoh's decree, and discovered by Pharaoh's daughter. Raised in the Egyptian court, he later fled to Midian, where he encountered God in the burning bush and was commanded to return to Egypt to lead the Israelites to freedom. After the Exodus through the Red Sea, he received the Ten Commandments on Mount Sinai and led the people through forty years in the [[nature|wilderness]].

## Works / Significance

Moses is traditionally credited with the authorship of the Torah — Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, and Deuteronomy — the foundational texts of Judaism. His laws and teachings also form a significant part of [[jesus-christ|Christ]]ian and Islamic traditions. In Western literature and thought, Moses has served as the archetype of the lawgiver and liberator.

## In Thoreau's Text

Moses appears briefly but vividly in the "Sunday" chapter of *A Week on the [[concord-mass|Concord]] and [[merrimack-river|Merrimack River]]s*. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] alludes to the infant Moses hidden among the reeds of the Nile, using the image to evoke the ancient Egyptian [[nature|landscape]] along which the travelers [[imagination|imaginative]]ly journey (003-944):

> Moses in the bulrushes, startling a multitude of storks and alligators basking in the sun. (003-944–945)

This allusion is part of a broader passage in which [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] ranges across biblical and classical [[history|antiquity]], juxtaposing the Nile's ancient associations with the present-day [[concord-river-mass|Concord River]]. The brief reference serves [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s characteristic method of layering [[history|historical]] and scriptural memory onto the immediate [[nature|landscape]] he navigates.
