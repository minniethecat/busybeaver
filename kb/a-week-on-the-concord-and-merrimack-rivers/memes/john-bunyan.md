# John Bunyan

## Biography

John Bunyan (1628–1688) was a English figure. English writer and preacher, author of The Pilgrim''s [[civilization|Progress]] — referenced by [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]].

## Works / Significance

John Bunyan's contributions have had lasting influence. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] engages with their work and ideas in *A Week*, finding in them resources for his own [[thought|reflection]]s on life, art, and truth.

## In Thoreau's Text

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] references John Bunyan during [[the-1839-boat-journey|the river journey]], drawing on their example to illuminate his arguments. As with all the figures [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] cites, John Bunyan appears not as an academic reference but as a living presence — someone whose thought and example bear directly on the experience of floating down the [[concord-mass|Concord]] and [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]] rivers.
