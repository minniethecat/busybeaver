# Dunstable Settlement

## Overview

[[dunstable|Dunstable]] was one of the earliest English settlements on the upper [[merrimack-river|Merrimack River]], founded in the mid-1600s. Its original boundaries were vast — encompassing what are now the towns of [[dunstable|Dunstable]], [[tyngsborough|Tyngsborough]], Pepperell, and others. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] refers to [[dunstable|Dunstable]] repeatedly as a microcosm of New England's frontier history.

As the northernmost English settlement on the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]] for much of its early history, [[dunstable|Dunstable]] was a borderland where conflicts, raids, and skirmishes were frequent.

The peaceful farms lining the river in his time were built on ground that witnessed bloodshed. ## Historical Background

The town of [[dunstable|Dunstable]] was first settled in the 1650s and incorporated in 1673, named after [[dunstable|Dunstable]] in [[bedford|Bedford]]shire, England.

**Colonial Politics.** The settlement was part of Massachusetts Bay Colony's expansion to control the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]] Valley's resources — timber, fish, and fertile meadowland.

**Frontier Life.** Early [[dunstable|Dunstable]] was fortified with garrison houses, a watchtower, and a stockade. Despite precautions, the town suffered heavily during King William's War (1688–1697) and Queen Anne's War (1702–1713).

**[[john-lovewell|John Lovewell]].** The Indian fighter [[john-lovewell|John Lovewell]] was from [[dunstable|Dunstable]], and his 1725 fight against the Pequawket (see [[lovewells-fight]]) stemmed from [[dunstable|Dunstable]]'s frontier tensions.

**[[jonathan-tyng|Jonathan Tyng]].** Colonel [[jonathan-tyng|Jonathan Tyng]], one of [[dunstable|Dunstable]]'s most prominent settlers, commanded the garrison and gave his name to [[tyngsborough|Tyngsborough]], which separated from [[dunstable|Dunstable]] in 1678.

**Di[[dream|vision]].** The original town was gradually divided: [[tyngsborough|Tyngsborough]] (1678), [[nashua|Nashua]] (1741), Pepperell (1775). Modern [[dunstable|Dunstable]], Massachusetts and [[dunstable|Dunstable]], New Hampshire are the remaining fragments.
