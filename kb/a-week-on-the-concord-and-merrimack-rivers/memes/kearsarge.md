## Description

Mount Kearsarge is a prominent peak in [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]] County, New Hampshire, rising to 2,937 feet. It is a notable landmark visible from the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack River]] and its associated lakes. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] mentions Kearsarge once in the Sunday chapter of *A Week*, in the context of the mountain [[thought|reflection]]s seen on the water during the brothers' journey.

## On the River Route

On Sunday morning, as the brothers rowed through a section of the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]] bordered by great ponds, [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] described the mountainous horizon reflected in the still water. Among the peaks he named were "Haystack, and Kearsarge reflected in its waters" (003-1129). The image situates Kearsarge within a panoramic mountain skyline visible from the river, alongside Haystack Mountain and [[moosehillock|Moosehillock]].

Kearsarge lies east of the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]], its rounded summit a familiar landmark for travelers on the river in [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s day as in ours.
