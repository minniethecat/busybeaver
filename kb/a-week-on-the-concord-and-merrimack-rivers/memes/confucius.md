# Confucius

## Biography

Confucius (551 BCE–479 BCE) was a Chinese figure. Chinese philosopher — [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] compares Eastern and Western wisdom traditions.

## Works / Significance

Confucius's contributions have had lasting influence. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] engages with their work and ideas in *A Week*, finding in them resources for his own [[thought|reflection]]s on life, art, and truth.

## In Thoreau's Text

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] references Confucius during [[the-1839-boat-journey|the river journey]], drawing on their example to illuminate his arguments. As with all the figures [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] cites, Confucius appears not as an academic reference but as a living presence — someone whose thought and example bear directly on the experience of floating down the [[concord-mass|Concord]] and [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]] rivers.
