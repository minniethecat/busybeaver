## Description

Squam Lake is a lake in central New Hampshire, part of the state's celebrated Lakes Region. It is situated among the "mountains of Sandwich and of Squam" and lies within the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack River]] watershed. (003-1127) [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] identified Squam as one of the headwater sources whose waters eventually reached the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]], listing it alongside [[newfound|Newfound Lake]] and Lake Winnipesaukee. The lake drains via the Squam River into the [[pemigewasset|Pemigewasset River]], which joins with the Winnipesaukee River at [[benjamin-franklin|Franklin]] to form the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]].

## On the River Route

The brothers were conscious throughout their voyage that the waters carrying them downstream originated far to the north in the lakes and mountains of New Hampshire. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] reflected that "it was already the water of Squam and [[newfound|Newfound Lake]] and Winnipiseogee" that bore their boat along the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]]. (003-1150) He returned to this idea later, again invoking "Squam, and Winnipiseogee, and [[newfound|Newfound]]" as the remote sources whose mingled waters made the river navigable. (003-1206) The brothers did not visit Squam Lake directly, but they were carried by its outflow — a re[[thought|mind]]er of how all the waters of the watershed were interconnected.
