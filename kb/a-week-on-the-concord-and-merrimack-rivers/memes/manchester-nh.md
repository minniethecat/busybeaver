## Description

Manchester is a city on the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack River]] in south-central New Hampshire at the site of the [[amoskeag-falls|Amoskeag Falls]]. In [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s time, it was a rapidly growing industrial center, built of bricks made from local clay. (006-049) It was known as the "Manchester of America" for its textile manufacturing. (003-1105)

## On the River Route

The brothers pass through Manchester, rowing between Manchester and [[bedford|Bedford]]. (006-045) They observe the city being built of bricks made on site. (006-058) Manchester is named alongside [[nashua|Nashua]] and [[concord-mass|Concord]], NH, as gleaming towns along the river. (003-1214) They note the canals of the Manchester [[menu|Manu]]facturing Company. (006-274)

## Historical Context

Manchester was originally an Abenaki fishing camp called [[amoskeag-falls|Amoskeag]] ([[amoskeag-falls|Namaskeak]]), meaning "great fishing place." (006-305) It was settled in the early 18th century and incorporated in 1846. In [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s time, it was a village of about two thousand people. (006-287) It was also the residence of John Stark, the Revolutionary hero. (006-496)

## Thoreau's Observations

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] notes Manchester "is being built of bricks made of the clay on the spot" (006-058) and reads of a "Manchester Athenaeum and Gallery of the Fine [[art|Art]]s." (006-297) He observes the artificial falls where canals drop water "into one mass of foam." (006-274, 006-279)
