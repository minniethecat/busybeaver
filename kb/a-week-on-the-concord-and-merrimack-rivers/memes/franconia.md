## Overview

Franconia is a town in Grafton County, New Hampshire, located in the White
Mountains region. In [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s time and earlier, the area was known for its
iron deposits, which supplied ore to the forges and foundries of the
[[merrimack-river|Merrimack]] Valley.

## In Thoreau's Text

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] references Franconia in a passage describing the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack River]]'s
origins and its fitness for manufacturing. The river, he says, flows from
the iron-rich uplands of Franconia, carrying the raw material that would
fuel the industrial cities along its course:

> (003-1204) Issuing from the iron region of Franconia, and flowing
> through still uncut forests, by inexhaustible ledges of granite, with
> Squam, and Winnipiseogee, and Newfound, and Massabesic Lakes for its
> mill-ponds, it falls over a succession of natural dams, where it has
> been offering its _privileges_ in vain for ages, until at last the
> Yankee race came to _improve_ them.

The passage places Franconia at the headwaters of the industrial river
system, providing the iron that would be worked in the mills of [[lawrence|Lawrence]],
[[lowell-mass|Lowell]], and [[nashua|Nashua]].

## Geography

Franconia lies in the [[pemigewasset|Pemigewasset River]] watershed, which becomes the
[[merrimack-river|Merrimack River]] at its confluence with the Winnipesaukee River in [[benjamin-franklin|Franklin]],
New Hampshire. The town is situated near Franconia Notch, a mountain pass
through the [[white-mountains|White Mountains]] that was an important early transportation
route.
