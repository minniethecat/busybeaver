## Description

Staten Island is the southernmost of the five boroughs of New York City, situated in New York Harbor at the entrance to the [[hudson|Hudson]] River. In the early nineteenth century, it was known for its Huguenot settlements and its military fortifications. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] references Staten Island twice in *A Week*, in both the Tuesday and Wednesday chapters.

## On the River Route

Staten Island is not on the [[concord-mass|Concord]]-[[merrimack-river|Merrimack]] route itself, but [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] invokes it as a point of comparison during his [[thought|reflection]]s on the river [[nature|landscape]]. On Tuesday, passing the tree-lined banks of the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]], he remarks that the scene "re[[thought|mind]]ed me of the homesteads of the Huguenots, on Staten Island" (005-059). The allusion connects the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]]'s [[history|past]]oral shoreline to the historic French Protestant settlements on the New York island.

In the Wednesday chapter, [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] reflects on watching vessels from an old ruined fort on Staten Island (006-105). The image of harbor-watching connects to his broader meditation on rivers, travel, and the lure of distant shores.
