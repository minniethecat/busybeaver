## Description

Coos Falls is a location on the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack River]] featuring locks that allowed river passage. During [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s 1839 journey, the brothers encountered a lock-keeper who guided them through "with a sort of [[silence|quiet]] enthusiasm, telling us that we were at Coos Falls."

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] notes that they "could still distinguish the strokes of his chisel for many sweeps after we had left him," (005-1563) suggesting the man was a stone-worker or sculptor. The falls later served as a landmark for their encampment.

## On the River Route

The brothers passed Coos Falls during the "Tuesday" chapter on their outward journey up the [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]]. On the return journey, recounted in "Friday," [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] describes having "already passed by broad daylight the scene of our encampment at Coos Falls."

They then "pitched our camp on the west bank, in the northern part of [[merrimack-river|Merrimack]]." (007-884) Coos Falls is one of the few places mentioned in both the outward and return narratives.
