# Johann Wolfgang von Goethe

## Biography

Johann Wolfgang von Goethe (1749–1832) was a German poet and writer. German poet, dramatist, and philosopher — cited by [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] as exemplifying universal genius.

## Works / Significance

Johann Wolfgang von Goethe's literary achievements place them among the most influential figures in Western literature. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] engages with their work extensively throughout *A Week*, treating them as essential voices in the tradition of great poetry.

## In Thoreau's Text

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] references and discusses Johann Wolfgang von Goethe at multiple points during [[the-1839-boat-journey|the river journey]], drawing on their work to illustrate his arguments about poetry, truth, history, and the human condition. Their presence in *A Week* reflects [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s deep engagement with the literary tradition and his conviction that the greatest poetry transcends its [[history|historical]] moment to speak with enduring authority.
