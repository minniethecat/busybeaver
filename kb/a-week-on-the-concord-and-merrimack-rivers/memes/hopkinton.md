## Description

Hopkinton is a town in Middlesex County, Massachusetts, situated approximately 25 miles southwest of [[concord-mass|Concord]]. It is the location where one of the principal sources of the [[concord-river-mass|Concord River]] rises. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]], quoting the historian of [[concord-mass|Concord]], notes that the river "rises in the south part of Hopkinton." (001-013)

## On the River Route

Hopkinton marks the ultimate source region of the [[concord-river-mass|Concord River]], the waterway that frames [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s narrative. In the opening chapter, [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] traces how the river flows from Hopkinton and Southborough, through Framingham, and between [[sudbury|Sudbury]] and [[wayland|Wayland]], where it is sometimes called the [[concord-river-mass|Sudbury River]], before entering [[concord-mass|Concord]]. (001-015)

The headwaters also include "Hopkinton pond and Westborough swamp," (002-526) which [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] mentions as source waters feeding the river system.
