# Bacon's Rebellion

## Overview

Colonial uprising referenced by [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] in discussions of authority and resistance.

## Relevance to the Journey

[[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]] references this event in *A Week*, using it to illuminate the character of New England's [[history|past]] and its continuing influence on the present. His treatment of [[history|historical]] events is never merely antiquarian — each story serves a philosophical or moral purpose within the larger architecture of [[the-1839-boat-journey|the river journey]].

## Historical Background

The colonial and early American history of New England is marked by encounters — and often violent conflicts — between European settlers and Native peoples, between different [[religion|religious]] and political factions, and between the claims of authority and the assertions of individual conscience. [[henry-david-thoreau|Thoreau]]'s engagement with this history reflects his characteristic independence of judgment.
