# Town near Dragoman Pass

Shaw never names the town where the Petkoffs live — it is simply "a small town near the Dragoman Pass"（001-001）— but its location is dramatically precise. Situated in western [[bulgaria|Bulgaria]], near the Servian border, it is close enough to the front lines of the [[serbo-bulgarian-war|Serbo-Bulgarian War]] that enemy fugitives can reach its rooftops.

## Dramatic Function

The town's strategic position makes the play's action plausible. [[captain-bluntschli|Bluntschli]] is a Servian officer separated from his unit during the retreat. That he should find himself in a Bulgarian town near the border — climbing a water pipe to escape pursuing Bulgarian soldiers — is dramaturgically sound. The town's proximity to the battlefield collapses the distance between the domestic and military spheres that is the play's central comic premise.

## The Unnamed Town

Shaw's refusal to name the town is characteristic of his method. The specific geographical reference (the Dragoman Pass) establishes documentary authenticity; the unnamed town preserves universality. The Petkoffs' town could be any bourgeois household in any small town near any battlefield. The comedy is located in a specific place, but its argument is general.
