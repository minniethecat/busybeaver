# Professional Class

[[captain-bluntschli|Captain Bluntschli]] represents an emerging social type at the end of the nineteenth century: the professional — a man whose social value is determined not by birth or wealth but by competence. He is a "professional officer"（001-488）, a soldier who fights for pay and prizes expertise over glory. His inheritance — the hotels — represents the commercial foundation of his independence. His marriage to [[raina-petkoff|Raina]] represents the alliance of professional competence with bourgeois respectability.

## The Professional Ethos

The professional ethos that Bluntschli embodies has several features:
- Competence over display: what matters is whether you can do the job, not whether you look the part
- Honesty over performance: the professional admits his limits; the aristocrat pretends to have none
- Commerce over glory: the professional works for pay, and this is more honest than fighting for honor

## Professionalism vs. Aristocracy

The play stages the conflict between professional and aristocratic values through the contrast between Bluntschli and [[sergius-saranoff|Sergius]]. Sergius, the Byronic cavalry officer, embodies aristocratic display — the [[uniform]], the charge, the "higher love." Bluntschli, the Swiss artillery officer, embodies professional competence — the correct cartridges, the rational retreat, the honest proposal. The play's outcome — Bluntschli wins, Sergius is relegated to Louka — is Shaw's wager on which class will inherit the future.
