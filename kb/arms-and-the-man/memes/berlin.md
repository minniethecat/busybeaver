# Berlin

Berlin, the capital of the German Empire, was the site of the Congress of 1878 that produced the [[treaty-of-berlin|Treaty of Berlin]] — the diplomatic settlement that created the political [[bulgaria|Bulgaria]] in which *Arms and the Man* takes place. Though never mentioned in the dialogue, Berlin is the invisible metropole whose decisions echo through the Petkoff household.

## The Congress

The Congress of Berlin (June–July 1878) was convened by the Great Powers to revise the Treaty of San Stefano, which had ended the Russo-Turkish War. Under the chairmanship of Otto von Bismarck, the Congress dismantled "Greater Bulgaria," the large Bulgarian state created by the San Stefano treaty, and divided it into three parts. The settlement was designed to limit Russian influence in the [[balkans|Balkans]] and to maintain the European balance of power.

## Berlin's Shadow

The shadow of Berlin falls across the play in subtle ways. The Bulgarian state whose army [[major-petkoff|Major Petkoff]] serves was created by the Berlin treaty. The [[serbo-bulgarian-war|Serbo-Bulgarian War]] was a response to Bulgaria's undoing of the Berlin settlement. The Great Power rivalries that the Congress attempted to manage would continue to destabilize the Balkans for decades — culminating in the assassination in Sarajevo in 1914 that began the Great War Shaw had been warning against for twenty years.
