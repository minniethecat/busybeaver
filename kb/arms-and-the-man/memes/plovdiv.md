# Plovdiv

Plovdiv (known historically as Philippopolis), the largest city in southern [[bulgaria|Bulgaria]], was the capital of [[eastern-rumelia|Eastern Rumelia]] under the [[treaty-of-berlin|Treaty of Berlin]] settlement. On September 6, 1885, Bulgarian nationalists in Plovdiv proclaimed the union of Eastern Rumelia with the Principality of Bulgaria — the event that triggered the [[serbo-bulgarian-war|Serbo-Bulgarian War]] and created the political context for *Arms and the Man*.

## Historical Significance

Plovdiv's role in the [[bulgarian-unification|Bulgarian unification]] made it a symbol of Bulgarian national aspiration. The bloodless coup in which the provincial governor was deposed and union with Bulgaria proclaimed was a model of nationalist efficiency — and a provocation to [[servia|Servia]], which saw Bulgarian expansion as a threat. The city's ancient history (it was founded by Philip of Macedon and ruled successively by Romans, Byzantines, and Ottomans) gave the Bulgarian nationalist claim a deep historical resonance.

## In the Play

Plovdiv is never mentioned in the play, but its significance as the site of unification is the historical fact that makes the [[serbo-bulgarian-war|war]] possible. The characters' celebration of Bulgarian victory over Servia is, implicitly, a celebration of the unification that Plovdiv represented — the fulfillment of the nationalist dream that the [[treaty-of-berlin|Treaty of Berlin]] had deferred.
