# Servian Army

The Servian Army of 1885 was the military force of the Kingdom of [[servia|Servia]], commanded by [[king-milan|King Milan I]] and defeated by [[bulgaria|Bulgaria]] in the two-week [[serbo-bulgarian-war|Serbo-Bulgarian War]]. It is the army for which [[captain-bluntschli|Captain Bluntschli]], a Swiss mercenary, served as an artillery officer — and from which he fled when the battle turned.

## The Defeat

The Servian Army invaded Bulgaria in November 1885 expecting an easy victory. The Bulgarian army, recently stripped of its [[russian-empire|Russian]] officers, was assumed to be disorganized and vulnerable. The assumption was wrong. At the [[battle-of-slivnitza|Battle of Slivnitza]], the Bulgarians defeated the Servian invasion force and pursued it across the border. The war lasted only two weeks; Servia's borders were preserved only by Austria-Hungary's diplomatic intervention.

## The Army in the Play

The Servian army appears in the play only through its absent soldiers — the fugitives hunted through the streets, the artillery officer climbing the water pipe, the machine-gun crews who had the wrong cartridges. Shaw's treatment of the Servian army is structurally symmetrical to his treatment of the [[bulgarian-army|Bulgarian army]]: both are comic institutions, staffed by men who are either incompetent or indifferent to the causes for which they fight.

## Bluntschli's Service

Bluntschli's service in the Servian army is significant precisely because he has no stake in Servian victory. "I have no interest in the politics of the war"（001-489）, he tells [[raina-petkoff|Raina]]. He fights for Servia as he would fight for any employer — professionally, competently, and without the romantic patriotism that makes soldiers willing to die. His flight from the battlefield is consistent with his professional ethic: a dead mercenary is a failed mercenary.
