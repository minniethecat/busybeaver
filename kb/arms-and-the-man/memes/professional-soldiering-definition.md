# Professional Soldiering – Definition

Professional soldiering means fighting as a trade, not a vocation. The professional soldier
serves whoever pays, evaluates tactics by their probability of success, and regards war as
logistics rather than poetry. Bluntschli's Swiss nationality is central: Switzerland, a neutral
nation famously associated with mercenary service, supplies soldiers who have no national stake
in Balkan conflicts. "I am only a Swiss," he tells Raina, "fighting merely as a professional
soldier. I joined Servia because it came first on the road from Switzerland"（001-362）. This
is not cynicism — it is clarity. The professional admits what the romantic conceals: that war
is work, not worship.

The concept is defined by three interlocking commitments. First, **competence** — the professional
knows his trade. Bluntschli's fourteen years of service（001-441）give him authority to distinguish
old soldiers from young fools. Second, **efficiency** — the professional conserves resources.
Chocolates sustain a soldier; empty cartridges do not（001-396）. Third, **honesty** — the
professional does not dress self-interest in patriotic costume. He fights for pay, and says so.
