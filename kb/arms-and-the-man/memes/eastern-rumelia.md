# Eastern Rumelia

Eastern Rumelia was an autonomous province of the [[ottoman-empire|Ottoman Empire]], created by the [[treaty-of-berlin|Treaty of Berlin]] (1878) from territory south of the Balkan Mountains. Its predominantly Bulgarian population chafed under nominal Ottoman sovereignty, and its union with the Principality of [[bulgaria|Bulgaria]] in September 1885 — the event that triggered the [[serbo-bulgarian-war|Serbo-Bulgarian War]] — was the culmination of years of nationalist agitation.

## The Province

Eastern Rumelia was an artificial creation of Great Power diplomacy. The Berlin Congress, determined to prevent the emergence of a large Bulgarian state under Russian influence, separated the territory south of the Balkans from the Bulgarian principality and placed it under a Christian governor appointed by the Sultan. The arrangement satisfied no one: the inhabitants wanted union with Bulgaria, the Ottomans resented the loss of full sovereignty, and Russia resented the thwarting of its ambitions.

## The Unification

The bloodless coup of September 6, 1885, in which Bulgarian nationalists in Plovdiv (the provincial capital) proclaimed union with Bulgaria, was the spark that ignited the [[serbo-bulgarian-war|war]]. [[servia|Servia]], fearing Bulgarian expansion, declared war in November; [[bulgaria|Bulgaria]], despite the withdrawal of its Russian officers, won decisively. The unification was internationally recognized in 1886, and Eastern Rumelia ceased to exist as a separate entity.

## In the Play

Eastern Rumelia is never mentioned in the dialogue, but its absorption into Bulgaria is the political fact that makes the [[serbo-bulgarian-war|war]] possible. The play's Bulgarian characters — living in a town near the Dragoman Pass, celebrating a victory over Servia — are citizens of a nation that has just doubled its territory through an act of nationalist defiance. This context — the intoxication of national success — is the invisible backdrop to their romantic posturing.
