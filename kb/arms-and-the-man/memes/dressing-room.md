# Dressing Room

The dressing room adjoining [[raina-petkoff|Raina]]'s bedchamber is mentioned only briefly in the stage directions, but its symbolic function is significant. It is the room where Raina's mother and the maid Louka enter and exit — a space of domestic labor and female community that contrasts with the bedchamber's solitary romanticism.

## The Threshold

The dressing room door, along with the open window, is one of two thresholds in the bedchamber. If the window is the threshold between the domestic and the military ([[captain-bluntschli|Bluntschli]]'s route of entry), the dressing room door is the threshold between the private self and the social self, between the woman who eats chocolate creams alone and the woman who performs the [[noble-attitude|noble attitude]] for her family. Raina moves between these spaces — between her romantic solitude and her domestic obligations — throughout Act I.

## The Dressing Room as Female Space

The dressing room, accessible to [[catherine-petkoff|Catherine]] and [[louka|Louka]] but not to the male intruder Bluntschli, represents the female community that surrounds and supports Raina. It is the space of practical domesticity — of dressing, grooming, the management of appearances — that contrasts with the bedchamber's space of romantic fantasy.
