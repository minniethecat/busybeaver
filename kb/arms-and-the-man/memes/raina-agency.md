# Raina's Agency Through Awakening

Raina Petkoff's arc is the play's central trajectory of growth. She begins performing femininity
as Byronism — the adoring woman on a balcony, awaiting her hero's return. The stage directions
present her as a self-conscious picture: "intensely conscious of the romantic beauty of the
night, and of the fact that her own youth and beauty is a part of it"（001-030 — 001-032）.
Her agency at this stage is entirely vicarious: "How I have envied you, Sergius! You have been
out in the world, on the field of battle, able to prove yourself there worthy of any woman in
the world; whilst I have had to sit at home inactive — dreaming — useless"（002-451 — 002-454）.

## The Turning Point

Her transformation begins with a lie. When she hides Bluntschli from the Russian officer, she
discovers competence in deception:

> Do you know, sir, that though I am only a woman, I think I am at heart as brave as you.
> （001-420 — 001-421）

This declaration is pivotal. Raina asserts her courage not by denying her gender ("only a woman")
but by claiming a deeper equivalence of character that transcends gender categories. The word
"heart" is crucial — it separates inner reality from social performance, a distinction the play
will develop systematically.

## Completion

By Act III, Raina has completed her arc. When Bluntschli calls her a "schoolgirl of seventeen,"
she corrects him with authority: "Next time I hope you will know the difference between a
schoolgirl of seventeen and a woman of twenty-three"（003-1110 — 003-1111）. The word "woman"
here is earned: Raina has moved from performing womanhood on romantic terms to claiming it on
her own.

Her final verdict on Bluntschli — "You are a romantic idiot"（003-1109）— is simultaneously an
insult and a term of endearment. She has learned to see romanticism clearly and still choose
affection, but on clear-eyed terms. Bluntschli's reply — "I am your protege, my dear" — seals
the new equilibrium: a marriage of equals built on mutual honesty rather than romantic illusion.
