[002-001]The sixth of March, 1886. In [[garden|the garden]] of major [[major-petkoff|Petkoff]]’s house. It is

[002-002]a fine spring morning; and the garden looks fresh and pretty. Beyond

[002-003]the paling the tops of a couple of minarets can be seen, shewing that

[002-004]there is a valley there, with the little town in it. A few miles

[002-005]further the Balkan mountains rise and shut in the view. Within the

[002-006]garden the side of the house is seen on the right, with a garden door

[002-007]reached by a little flight of steps. On the left the stable yard, with

[002-008]its gateway, encroaches on the garden. There are fruit bushes along the

[002-009]paling and house, covered with washing hung out to dry. A path runs by

[002-010]the house, and rises by two steps at the corner where it turns out of

[002-011]the right along the front. In the middle a small table, with two bent

[002-012]wood chairs at it, is laid for breakfast with Turkish coffee pot, cups,

[002-013]rolls, etc.; but the cups have been used and the bread broken. There is

[002-014]a wooden garden seat against the wall on the left.

[002-015][[louka|Louka]], smoking a cigaret, is standing between the table and the house,

[002-016]turning her back with angry disdain on a man-servant who is lecturing

[002-017]her. He is a middle-aged man of cool temperament and low but clear and

[002-018]keen intelligence, with the complacency of the servant who values

[002-019]himself on his rank in servility, and the imperturbability of the

[002-020]accurate calculator who has no illusions. He wears a white Bulgarian

[002-021]costume jacket with decorated border, sash, wide knickerbockers, and

[002-022]decorated gaiters. His head is shaved up to the crown, giving him a

[002-023]high Japanese forehead. His name is [[nicola|Nicola]].

[002-024]NICOLA.

[002-025]Be warned in time, Louka: mend your manners. I know the mistress. She

[002-026]is so grand that she never dreams that any servant could dare to be

[002-027]disrespectful to her; but if she once suspects that you are defying

[002-028]her, out you go.

[002-029]LOUKA.

[002-030]I do defy her. I will defy her. What do I care for her?

[002-031]NICOLA.

[002-032]If you quarrel with the family, I never can marry you. It’s the same as

[002-033]if you quarrelled with me!

[002-034]LOUKA.

[002-035]You take her part against me, do you?

[002-036]NICOLA.

[002-037](_sedately_). I shall always be dependent on the good will of the

[002-038]family. When I leave their service and start a shop in Sofia, their

[002-039]custom will be half my capital: their bad word would ruin me.

[002-040]LOUKA.

[002-041]You have no spirit. I should like to see them dare say a word against

[002-042]me!

[002-043]NICOLA.

[002-044](_pityingly_). I should have expected more sense from you, Louka. But

[002-045]you’re young, you’re young!

[002-046]LOUKA.

[002-047]Yes; and you like me the better for it, don’t you? But I know some

[002-048]family secrets they wouldn’t care to have told, young as I am. Let them

[002-049]quarrel with me if they dare!

[002-050]NICOLA.

[002-051](_with compassionate superiority_). Do you know what they would do if

[002-052]they heard you talk like that?

[002-053]LOUKA.

[002-054]What could they do?

[002-055]NICOLA.

[002-056]Discharge you for untruthfulness. Who would believe any stories you

[002-057]told after that? Who would give you another situation? Who in this

[002-058]house would dare be seen speaking to you ever again? How long would

[002-059]your father be left on his little farm? (_She impatiently throws away

[002-060]the end of her cigaret, and stamps on it._) Child, you don’t know the

[002-061]power such high people have over the like of you and me when we try to

[002-062]rise out of our poverty against them. (_He goes close to her and lowers

[002-063]his voice._) Look at me, ten years in their service. Do you think I

[002-064]know no secrets? I know things about the mistress that she wouldn’t

[002-065]have the master know for a thousand levas. I know things about him that

[002-066]she wouldn’t let him hear the last of for six months if I blabbed them

[002-067]to her. I know things about [[raina-petkoff|Raina]] that would break off her match with

[002-068][[sergius-saranoff|Sergius]] if—

[002-069]LOUKA.

[002-070](_turning on him quickly_). How do you know? I never told you!

[002-071]NICOLA.

[002-072](_opening his eyes cunningly_). So that’s your little secret, is it? I

[002-073]thought it might be something like that. Well, you take my advice, and

[002-074]be respectful; and make the mistress feel that no matter what you know

[002-075]or don’t know, they can depend on you to hold your tongue and serve the

[002-076]family faithfully. That’s what they like; and that’s how you’ll make

[002-077]most out of them.

[002-078]LOUKA.

[002-079](_with searching scorn_). You have the soul of a servant, Nicola.

[002-080]NICOLA.

[002-081](_complacently_). Yes: that’s the secret of success in service.

[002-082](_A loud knocking with a whip handle on a wooden door, outside on the

[002-083]left, is heard._)

[002-084]MALE VOICE OUTSIDE.

[002-085]Hollo! Hollo there! Nicola!

[002-086]LOUKA.

[002-087]Master! back from the war!

[002-088]NICOLA.

[002-089](_quickly_). My word for it, Louka, the war’s over. Off with you and

[002-090]get some fresh coffee. (_He runs out into the stable yard._)

[002-091]LOUKA.

[002-092](_as she puts the coffee pot and the cups upon the tray, and carries it

[002-093]into the house_). You’ll never put the soul of a servant into me.

[002-094](_Major Petkoff comes from the stable yard, followed by Nicola. He is a

[002-095]cheerful, excitable, insignificant, unpolished man of about 50,

[002-096]naturally unambitious except as to his income and his importance in

[002-097]local society, but just now greatly pleased with the military rank

[002-098]which the war has thrust on him as a man of consequence in his town.

[002-099]The fever of plucky patriotism which the Servian attack roused in all

[002-100]the Bulgarians has pulled him through the war; but he is obviously glad

[002-101]to be home again._)

[002-102]PETKOFF.

[002-103](_pointing to the table with his whip_). Breakfast out here, eh?

[002-104]NICOLA.

[002-105]Yes, sir. The mistress and Miss Raina have just gone in.

[002-106]PETKOFF.

[002-107](_sitting down and taking a roll_). Go in and say I’ve come; and get me

[002-108]some fresh coffee.

[002-109]NICOLA.

[002-110]It’s coming, sir. (_He goes to the house door. Louka, with fresh

[002-111]coffee, a clean cup, and a brandy bottle on her tray meets him._) Have

[002-112]you told the mistress?

[002-113]LOUKA.

[002-114]Yes: she’s coming.

[002-115](_Nicola goes into the house. Louka brings the coffee to the table._)

[002-116]PETKOFF.

[002-117]Well, the Servians haven’t run away with you, have they?

[002-118]LOUKA.

[002-119]No, sir.

[002-120]PETKOFF.

[002-121]That’s right. Have you brought me some cognac?

[002-122]LOUKA.

[002-123](_putting the bottle on the table_). Here, sir.

[002-124]PETKOFF.

[002-125]That’s right. (_He pours some into his coffee._)

[002-126](_[[catherine-petkoff|Catherine]] who has at this early hour made only a very perfunctory

[002-127]toilet, and wears a Bulgarian apron over a once brilliant, but now half

[002-128]worn out red dressing gown, and a colored handkerchief tied over her

[002-129]thick black hair, with Turkish slippers on her bare feet, comes from

[002-130]the house, looking astonishingly handsome and stately under all the

[002-131]circumstances. Louka goes into the house._)

[002-132]CATHERINE.

[002-133]My dear Paul, what a surprise for us. (_She stoops over the back of his

[002-134]chair to kiss him._) Have they brought you fresh coffee?

[002-135]PETKOFF.

[002-136]Yes, Louka’s been looking after me. The war’s over. The treaty was

[002-137]signed three days ago at Bucharest; and the decree for our army to

[002-138]demobilize was issued yesterday.

[002-139]CATHERINE.

[002-140](_springing erect, with flashing eyes_). The war over! Paul: have you

[002-141]let the Austrians force you to make peace?

[002-142]PETKOFF.

[002-143](_submissively_). My dear: they didn’t consult me. What could _I_ do?

[002-144](_She sits down and turns away from him._) But of course we saw to it

[002-145]that the treaty was an honorable one. It declares peace—

[002-146]CATHERINE.

[002-147](_outraged_). [[peace|Peace]]!

[002-148]PETKOFF.

[002-149](_appeasing her_).—but not friendly relations: remember that. They

[002-150]wanted to put that in; but I insisted on its being struck out. What

[002-151]more could I do?

[002-152]CATHERINE.

[002-153]You could have annexed Servia and made Prince Alexander Emperor of the

[002-154]Balkans. That’s what I would have done.

[002-155]PETKOFF.

[002-156]I don’t doubt it in the least, my dear. But I should have had to subdue

[002-157]the whole Austrian Empire first; and that would have kept me too long

[002-158]away from you. I missed you greatly.

[002-159]CATHERINE.

[002-160](_relenting_). Ah! (_Stretches her hand affectionately across the table

[002-161]to squeeze his._)

[002-162]PETKOFF.

[002-163]And how have you been, my dear?

[002-164]CATHERINE.

[002-165]Oh, my usual sore throats, that’s all.

[002-166]PETKOFF.

[002-167](_with conviction_). That comes from washing your neck every day. I’ve

[002-168]often told you so.

[002-169]CATHERINE.

[002-170]Nonsense, Paul!

[002-171]PETKOFF.

[002-172](_over his coffee and cigaret_). I don’t believe in going too far with

[002-173]these modern customs. All this washing can’t be good for the health:

[002-174]it’s not natural. There was an Englishman at Phillipopolis who used to

[002-175]wet himself all over with cold water every morning when he got up.

[002-176]Disgusting! It all comes from the English: their climate makes them so

[002-177]dirty that they have to be perpetually washing themselves. Look at my

[002-178]father: he never had a bath in his life; and he lived to be

[002-179]ninety-eight, the healthiest man in [[bulgaria|Bulgaria]]. I don’t mind a good wash

[002-180]once a week to keep up my position; but once a day is carrying the

[002-181]thing to a ridiculous extreme.

[002-182]CATHERINE.

[002-183]You are a barbarian at heart still, Paul. I hope you behaved yourself

[002-184]before all those Russian officers.

[002-185]PETKOFF.

[002-186]I did my best. I took care to let them know that we had a library.

[002-187]CATHERINE.

[002-188]Ah; but you didn’t tell them that we have an electric bell in it? I

[002-189]have had one put up.

[002-190]PETKOFF.

[002-191]What’s an electric bell?

[002-192]CATHERINE.

[002-193]You touch a button; something tinkles in the kitchen; and then Nicola

[002-194]comes up.

[002-195]PETKOFF.

[002-196]Why not shout for him?

[002-197]CATHERINE.

[002-198]Civilized people never shout for their servants. I’ve learnt that while

[002-199]you were away.

[002-200]PETKOFF.

[002-201]Well, I’ll tell you something I’ve learnt, too. Civilized people don’t

[002-202]hang out their washing to dry where visitors can see it; so you’d

[002-203]better have all that (_indicating the clothes on the bushes_) put

[002-204]somewhere else.

[002-205]CATHERINE.

[002-206]Oh, that’s absurd, Paul: I don’t believe really refined people notice

[002-207]such things.

[002-208](_Someone is heard knocking at the stable gates._)

[002-209]PETKOFF.

[002-210]There’s Sergius. (_Shouting._) Hollo, Nicola!

[002-211]CATHERINE.

[002-212]Oh, don’t shout, Paul: it really isn’t nice.

[002-213]PETKOFF.

[002-214]Bosh! (_He shouts louder than before._) Nicola!

[002-215]NICOLA.

[002-216](_appearing at the house door_). Yes, sir.

[002-217]PETKOFF.

[002-218]If that is Major Saranoff, bring him round this way. (_He pronounces

[002-219]the name with the stress on the second syllable—Sarah-noff._)

[002-220]NICOLA.

[002-221]Yes, sir. (_He goes into the stable yard._)

[002-222]PETKOFF.

[002-223]You must talk to him, my dear, until Raina takes him off our hands. He

[002-224]bores my life out about our not promoting him—over my head, mind you.

[002-225]CATHERINE.

[002-226]He certainly ought to be promoted when he marries Raina. Besides, the

[002-227]country should insist on having at least one native general.

[002-228]PETKOFF.

[002-229]Yes, so that he could throw away whole brigades instead of regiments.

[002-230]It’s no use, my dear: he has not the slightest chance of promotion

[002-231]until we are quite sure that the peace will be a lasting one.

[002-232]NICOLA.

[002-233](_at the gate, announcing_). Major Sergius Saranoff! (_He goes into the

[002-234]house and returns presently with a third chair, which he places at the

[002-235]table. He then withdraws._)

[002-236](_Major Sergius Saranoff, the original of the portrait in Raina’s room,

[002-237]is a tall, romantically handsome man, with the physical hardihood, the

[002-238]high spirit, and the susceptible imagination of an untamed mountaineer

[002-239]chieftain. But his remarkable personal distinction is of a

[002-240]characteristically civilized type. The ridges of his eyebrows, curving

[002-241]with a ram’s-horn twist round the marked projections at the outer

[002-242]corners, his jealously observant eye, his nose, thin, keen, and

[002-243]apprehensive in spite of the pugnacious high bridge and large nostril,

[002-244]his assertive chin, would not be out of place in a Paris salon. In

[002-245]short, the clever, imaginative barbarian has an acute critical faculty

[002-246]which has been thrown into intense activity by the arrival of western

[002-247]civilization in the Balkans; and the result is precisely what the

[002-248]advent of nineteenth-century thought first produced in England: to-wit,

[002-249]Byronism. By his brooding on the perpetual failure, not only of others,

[002-250]but of himself, to live up to his imaginative ideals, his consequent

[002-251]cynical scorn for humanity, the jejune credulity as to the absolute

[002-252]validity of his ideals and the unworthiness of the world in

[002-253]disregarding them, his wincings and mockeries under the sting of the

[002-254]petty disillusions which every hour spent among men brings to his

[002-255]infallibly quick observation, he has acquired the half tragic, half

[002-256]ironic air, the mysterious moodiness, the suggestion of a strange and

[002-257]terrible history that has left him nothing but undying remorse, by

[002-258]which Childe Harold fascinated the grandmothers of his English

[002-259]contemporaries. Altogether it is clear that here or nowhere is Raina’s

[002-260]ideal hero. Catherine is hardly less enthusiastic, and much less

[002-261]reserved in shewing her enthusiasm. As he enters from the stable gate,

[002-262]she rises effusively to greet him. Petkoff is distinctly less disposed

[002-263]to make a fuss about him._)

[002-264]PETKOFF.

[002-265]Here already, Sergius. Glad to see you!

[002-266]CATHERINE.

[002-267]My dear Sergius!(_She holds out both her hands._)

[002-268]SERGIUS.

[002-269](_kissing them with scrupulous [[chivalry|gallantry]]_). My dear mother, if I may

[002-270]call you so.

[002-271]PETKOFF.

[002-272](_drily_). Mother-in-law, Sergius; mother-in-law! Sit down, and have

[002-273]some coffee.

[002-274]SERGIUS.

[002-275]Thank you, none for me. (_He gets away from the table with a certain

[002-276]distaste for Petkoff’s enjoyment of it, and posts himself with

[002-277]conscious grace against the rail of the steps leading to the house._)

[002-278]CATHERINE.

[002-279]You look superb—splendid. The campaign has improved you. Everybody here

[002-280]is mad about you. We were all wild with enthusiasm about that

[002-281]magnificent [[cavalry|cavalry charge]].

[002-282]SERGIUS.

[002-283](_with grave irony_). Madam: it was the cradle and the grave of my

[002-284]military [[scandal|reputation]].

[002-285]CATHERINE.

[002-286]How so?

[002-287]SERGIUS.

[002-288]I won the battle the wrong way when our worthy Russian generals were

[002-289]losing it the right way. That upset their plans, and wounded their

[002-290]self-esteem. Two of their colonels got their regiments driven back on

[002-291]the correct principles of scientific warfare. Two major-generals got

[002-292]killed strictly according to military etiquette. Those two colonels are

[002-293]now major-generals; and I am still a simple major.

[002-294]CATHERINE.

[002-295]You shall not remain so, Sergius. The women are on your side; and they

[002-296]will see that justice is done you.

[002-297]SERGIUS.

[002-298]It is too late. I have only waited for the peace to send in my

[002-299]resignation.

[002-300]PETKOFF.

[002-301](_dropping his cup in his amazement_). Your resignation!

[002-302]CATHERINE.

[002-303]Oh, you must withdraw it!

[002-304]SERGIUS.

[002-305](_with resolute, measured emphasis, folding his arms_). I never

[002-306]withdraw!

[002-307]PETKOFF.

[002-308](_vexed_). Now who could have supposed you were going to do such a

[002-309]thing?

[002-310]SERGIUS.

[002-311](_with fire_). Everyone that knew me. But enough of myself and my

[002-312]affairs. How is Raina; and where is Raina?

[002-313]RAINA.

[002-314](_suddenly coming round the corner of the house and standing at the top

[002-315]of the steps in the path_). Raina is here. (_She makes a charming

[002-316]picture as they all turn to look at her. She wears an underdress of

[002-317]pale green silk, draped with an overdress of thin ecru canvas

[002-318]embroidered with gold. On her head she wears a pretty Phrygian cap of

[002-319]gold tinsel. Sergius, with an exclamation of pleasure, goes impulsively

[002-320]to meet her. She stretches out her hand: he drops chivalrously on one

[002-321]knee and kisses it._)

[002-322]PETKOFF.

[002-323](_aside to Catherine, beaming with parental pride_). Pretty, isn’t it?

[002-324]She always appears at the right moment.

[002-325]CATHERINE.

[002-326](_impatiently_). Yes: she listens for it. It is an abominable habit.

[002-327](_Sergius leads Raina forward with splendid gallantry, as if she were a

[002-328]queen. When they come to the table, she turns to him with a bend of the

[002-329]head; he bows; and thus they separate, he coming to his place, and she

[002-330]going behind her father’s chair._)

[002-331]RAINA.

[002-332](_stooping and kissing her father_). Dear father! Welcome home!

[002-333]PETKOFF.

[002-334](_patting her cheek_). My little pet girl. (_He kisses her; she goes to

[002-335]the chair left by Nicola for Sergius, and sits down._)

[002-336]CATHERINE.

[002-337]And so you’re no longer a soldier, Sergius.

[002-338]SERGIUS.

[002-339]I am no longer a soldier. Soldiering, my dear madam, is the coward’s

[002-340]art of attacking mercilessly when you are strong, and keeping out of

[002-341]harm’s way when you are weak. That is the whole secret of successful

[002-342]fighting. Get your enemy at a disadvantage; and never, on any account,

[002-343]fight him on equal terms. Eh, Major!

[002-344]PETKOFF.

[002-345]They wouldn’t let us make a fair stand-up fight of it. However, I

[002-346]suppose soldiering has to be a trade like any other trade.

[002-347]SERGIUS.

[002-348]Precisely. But I have no ambition to succeed as a tradesman; so I have

[002-349]taken the advice of that bagman of a captain that settled the exchange

[002-350]of prisoners with us at Peerot, and given it up.

[002-351]PETKOFF.

[002-352]What, that Swiss fellow? Sergius: I’ve often thought of that exchange

[002-353]since. He over-reached us about those horses.

[002-354]SERGIUS.

[002-355]Of course he over-reached us. His father was a hotel and livery stable

[002-356]keeper; and he owed his first step to his knowledge of horse-dealing.

[002-357](_With mock enthusiasm._) Ah, he was a soldier—every inch a soldier! If

[002-358]only I had bought the horses for my regiment instead of foolishly

[002-359]leading it into danger, I should have been a field-marshal now!

[002-360]CATHERINE.

[002-361]A Swiss? What was he doing in the Servian army?

[002-362]PETKOFF.

[002-363]A volunteer of course—keen on picking up his profession. (_Chuckling._)

[002-364]We shouldn’t have been able to begin fighting if these foreigners

[002-365]hadn’t shewn us how to do it: we knew nothing about it; and neither did

[002-366]the Servians. Egad, there’d have been no war without them.

[002-367]RAINA.

[002-368]Are there many Swiss officers in the Servian Army?

[002-369]PETKOFF.

[002-370]No—all Austrians, just as our officers were all Russians. This was the

[002-371]only Swiss I came across. I’ll never trust a Swiss again. He cheated

[002-372]us—humbugged us into giving him fifty able bodied men for two hundred

[002-373]confounded worn out chargers. They weren’t even eatable!

[002-374]SERGIUS.

[002-375]We were two children in the hands of that consummate soldier, Major:

[002-376]simply two innocent little children.

[002-377]RAINA.

[002-378]What was he like?

[002-379]CATHERINE.

[002-380]Oh, Raina, what a silly question!

[002-381]SERGIUS.

[002-382]He was like a commercial traveller in uniform. Bourgeois to his boots.

[002-383]PETKOFF.

[002-384](_grinning_). Sergius: tell Catherine that queer story his friend told

[002-385]us about him—how he escaped after [[battle-of-slivnitza|Slivnitza]]. You remember?—about his

[002-386]being hid by two women.

[002-387]SERGIUS.

[002-388](_with bitter irony_). Oh, yes, quite a romance. He was serving in the

[002-389]very battery I so unprofessionally charged. Being a thorough soldier,

[002-390]he ran away like the rest of them, with our cavalry at his heels. To

[002-391]escape their attentions, he had the good taste to take refuge in the

[002-392]chamber of some patriotic young Bulgarian lady. The young lady was

[002-393]enchanted by his persuasive commercial traveller’s manners. She very

[002-394]modestly entertained him for an hour or so and then called in her

[002-395]mother lest her conduct should appear unmaidenly. The old lady was

[002-396]equally fascinated; and the fugitive was sent on his way in the

[002-397]morning, disguised in an old coat belonging to the master of the house,

[002-398]who was away at the war.

[002-399]RAINA.

[002-400](_rising with marked stateliness_). Your life in the camp has made you

[002-401]coarse, Sergius. I did not think you would have repeated such a story

[002-402]before me. (_She turns away coldly._)

[002-403]CATHERINE.

[002-404](_also rising_). She is right, Sergius. If such women exist, we should

[002-405]be spared the knowledge of them.

[002-406]PETKOFF.

[002-407]Pooh! nonsense! what does it matter?

[002-408]SERGIUS.

[002-409](_ashamed_). No, Petkoff: I was wrong. (_To Raina, with earnest

[002-410]humility._) I beg your pardon. I have behaved abominably. Forgive me,

[002-411]Raina. (_She bows reservedly._) And you, too, madam. (_Catherine bows

[002-412]graciously and sits down. He proceeds solemnly, again addressing

[002-413]Raina._) The glimpses I have had of the seamy side of life during the

[002-414]last few months have made me cynical; but I should not have brought my

[002-415]cynicism here—least of all into your presence, Raina. I—(_Here, turning

[002-416]to the others, he is evidently about to begin a long speech when the

[002-417]Major interrupts him._)

[002-418]PETKOFF.

[002-419]Stuff and nonsense, Sergius. That’s quite enough fuss about nothing: a

[002-420]soldier’s daughter should be able to stand up without flinching to a
