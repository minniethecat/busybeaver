# Duty

Duty — the moral obligation to fulfill one's assigned role — is invoked by virtually every character in the play, but each invocation reveals a different understanding of what duty means. [[sergius-saranoff|Sergius]] feels duty to his ideals, his engagement, his reputation. [[captain-bluntschli|Bluntschli]] feels duty to his profession, his competence, his survival. [[nicola|Nicola]] feels duty to his masters, his position, his future. Shaw's treatment of duty is characteristically revisionist: the play asks not whether characters fulfill their duties but whether the duties they have accepted are worth fulfilling.

## Competing Duties

The play stages a competition between three conceptions of duty:

1. **Romantic duty:** The obligation to live up to ideals — to be brave, honorable, faithful. This is [[sergius-saranoff|Sergius]]'s duty, and the play demonstrates its impossibility. No human being can consistently fulfill the demands of romantic idealism; the attempt produces either hypocrisy (Sergius) or self-deception ([[raina-petkoff|Raina]]).

2. **Professional duty:** The obligation to perform one's job competently. This is Bluntschli's duty, and the play endorses it. The professional who knows his cartridges and serves his employer is, in Shaw's universe, more admirable than the idealist who serves abstractions.

3. **Servile duty:** The obligation to serve one's masters. This is Nicola's duty, and the play treats it as a strategic adaptation to an unjust system — understandable, even admirable in its way, but ultimately a compromise.

## Shaw's Position

Shaw's treatment of duty reflects his broader critique of social obligations that demand individual sacrifice for collective abstractions. The soldier who dies for duty is, in Shaw's analysis, a victim of the rhetoric of duty — a man persuaded to sacrifice himself for ideals that serve not him but his rulers. The professional who survives for competence is, by implication, a freer and more moral figure.

- **Target of satire**: The romantic conception of duty that demands self-sacrifice for abstractions
- **Alternative proposed**: A professional conception of duty based on competence and rational self-interest
