# Ride with the Despatches

The Ride with the Despatches is a pivotal early episode in *When Charles the First Was King*, occupying Chapter 24 ("Of My Ride with the Despatches"). The narrator [[william-dale|William Dale]] undertakes a midnight ride from Dale's Field to York to deliver urgent military despatches entrusted to him by the wounded [[captain-trevor|Captain Trevor]], a Royalist courier shot on the highway. The ride marks the narrator's reluctant entry into active participation in the Civil War.

## Overview

The episode serves as the narrator's initiation into the wider conflict. Until this point, William Dale has been an observer of events — sympathetic to the Royalist cause but not actively engaged. When a wounded courier collapses at his doorstep, Dale volunteers to complete the mission, undertaking a dangerous night ride through contested territory. The ride transforms Dale from a farmer into a participant in the war, setting in motion his subsequent military adventures.

## The Episode

### The Wounded Courier

The episode begins with William Dale and [[rose-lisle|Rose Lisle]] walking through the meadows near Dale's Field on a July evening. In the darkness, they hear a horseman approaching: "Tired indeed the horse was, from the jaded way it stepped out. But what was the matter with the rider, who reeled in his saddle like a drunken man, clinging to it with one hand" ((024-016)). The rider collapses as he reaches them: "I started forward just in time to catch the man as he rolled heavily from his saddle. He sank into my arms and I felt something wet and warm as my hand touched his breast" ((024-019)).

Rose runs ahead to alert the household while Dale carries the wounded man inside. Dale's mother dresses the gunshot wound — "a shot in the left shoulder, just high enough to have missed the heart" ((024-025)). Despite his injury, the rider insists he must continue: "Rest I cannot, for I must on and away at once. If only I had another horse!" ((024-028)).

### The Mission Accepted

When Dale assures the stranger that they are Royalists, the wounded man — Captain Trevor — reveals his mission: "I am carrying despatches to the Marquis of Newcastle at York. He must have them tonight or 'tis too late" ((024-039)). Without hesitation, Dale volunteers: "I will carry the despatches to Lord Newcastle. Tell me what to do, and give me the packet and let me go. It is now close upon eleven o'clock: I shall be in York by two" ((024-041)).

Trevor gives the packet and final instructions before fainting: "Say that Captain Trevor was bringing it and was shot on the highway" ((024-042)).

### The Ride

Dale's account of the night ride is one of the novel's most vivid passages. Mounted on his great horse Captain, he gallops through the darkness: "How we rode along that night! The great horse might have known what mission he was upon. I can still feel the grand sweep of his legs as he went forward, the regular, smooth movement of his gallop" ((024-047)). His route takes him "past Monk Frystone and Sherburn, through Barkston and Saxton, through Towton and Stretton, and so into Tadcaster ere yet it was an hour past midnight" ((024-048)). He delivers the despatches at York and returns safely.

## Significance

The Ride with the Despatches has several important narrative functions:

1. **Initiation**: It marks Dale's transition from civilian to active participant in the Royalist cause.
2. **Introduction of Captain Trevor**: The wounded courier becomes a major character whose entanglement with the Lisle and Dale families drives much of the subsequent plot.
3. **The Despatches**: The packet Dale delivers to [[marquis-of-newcastle|the Marquis of Newcastle]] establishes connections with the wider military context.
4. **Character revelation**: Dale's willingness to undertake the dangerous ride reveals his courage and loyalty — traits that define him throughout the novel.

## Key Participants

- [[william-dale|William Dale]] — the narrator, who volunteers for the ride
- [[captain-trevor|Captain Trevor]] — the wounded Royalist courier
- [[rose-lisle|Rose Lisle]] — present at the scene, witnesses the courier's collapse
- [[marquis-of-newcastle|Marquis of Newcastle]] — recipient of the despatches at York
