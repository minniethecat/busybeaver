# Darrington

Darrington is a fictional Yorkshire village that serves as the primary setting for the domestic portions of *When Charles the First Was King*. Described as lying within four miles of Pontefract, it is the home of the narrator [[william-dale|William Dale]] and his family, and the location of Dale's Field — the farmstead that is the novel's emotional and geographical anchor.

## Overview

Though Darrington itself is fictional, its location and character are drawn with sufficient precision to place it in the real geography of the West Riding of Yorkshire. The village lies in the countryside between Pontefract and Wentbridge, near Went Vale, within the historic honour of Pontefract. Its description — a small farming community with a parish church, a vicarage, and scattered farmsteads — is typical of the rural settlements that dotted the Yorkshire landscape in the seventeenth century.

## Geographic Context

The narrator describes Darrington's position with care: "It was within four miles of the ancient and historic market-town of Pontefract, where kings have been imprisoned and done to death, that I, William Dale, yeoman, was born in the year of grace 1621" ((001-005)). The proximity to Pontefract — a royalist stronghold during the Civil War — ensures that the war's events reach Darrington directly, most notably during the sieges of Pontefract Castle.

The surrounding landscape includes Went Hill, Went Vale, and the road to Wentbridge — the same road where Dale first meets [[rose-lisle|Rose Lisle]] and [[philip-lisle|Philip Lisle]]. The countryside of meadow, woodland, and farm is presented as a pastoral ideal, a world of honest yeoman labour that the war threatens to destroy.

## In the Narrative

Darrington appears throughout the novel as the place the narrator always returns to. Key events set in or near Darrington include:

- Dale's childhood and his first meetings with the Lisle family
- The recruitment of Royalist soldiers in the marketplace (Chapter 14)
- The narrator's midnight ride to York, which begins from Dale's Field (Chapter 24)
- Captain Trevor's convalescence at Dale's Field (Chapter 29)
- The kidnapping of Rose Lisle and the subsequent search (Chapters 33–37)
- The narrator's final return from London (Chapters 44–46)

The Vicar of Darrington, [[parson-drumbleforth|Parson Drumbleforth]], is a significant character whose letters provide news from the front. His daughter [[rose-lisle|Rose Lisle]] (née Drumbleforth) is one of the novel's central figures.

## Historical Significance

While Darrington is fictional, the novel draws on a real Yorkshire location of the same name. The historical Darrington is a village approximately four miles from Pontefract, with a church dating from the Norman period. The author's choice of this real place-name lends authenticity to the fictional setting while allowing creative freedom in describing the Dales' farmstead and its inhabitants.

## Notable Features

- **Dale's Field** — the narrator's farmstead, described as lying "removed but little space from the head of Went Hill" ((001-007))
- **The Vicarage** — home of Parson Drumbleforth
- **The Marketplace** — scene of the disturbance in Chapter 14, where Philip Lisle recruits for the King
- **The Road to Wentbridge** — where the narrator first encounters the Lisle children

## Appearances

Darrington and its surroundings are referenced throughout the novel, particularly in the opening and closing chapters. The household at Dale's Field — William, his mother, his sister Lucy, and the farmhand Jacob Trusty — forms the stable centre around which the novel's adventures revolve.
