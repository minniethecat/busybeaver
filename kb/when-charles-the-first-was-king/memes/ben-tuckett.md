# Ben Tuckett

Ben Tuckett is a loyal friend and ally of the Dale family, one of the network of local farmers and yeomen whose solidarity sustains the narrator through the dangers of the Civil War. His role, though modest, is essential — he provides warnings of approaching danger and practical assistance when the Dales are threatened.

## Overview

Ben Tuckett appears at critical moments as a bearer of news and warnings. In a time when information travelled slowly and official intelligence was unreliable, men like Tuckett — who knew the land, the people, and the signs of trouble — were indispensable. His loyalty to the Dales reflects the bonds of kinship and neighbourhood that the Civil War tested but did not break.

## Role in the Narrative

Tuckett serves as the narrator's eyes and ears when William himself cannot be everywhere. He brings warnings of approaching Parliamentarian forces, alerts the Dales to the movements of their Watson rivals, and provides the practical support — a horse, a hiding place, a timely word — that keeps the family safe. His character represents the quiet, unglamorous loyalty that sustained communities through the war.

## Biographical Details

A farmer of the Darrington district, Ben Tuckett is part of the close-knit rural community centred on Dale's Field. His exact circumstances are not elaborated, but his reliability and his knowledge of local affairs mark him as a man of standing among his neighbours. He is trusted by William Dale's family and reciprocates that trust with steadfast loyalty.

## Historical Context

Men like Ben Tuckett — the local allies, the bringers of warnings, the keepers of community knowledge — were essential to survival in Civil War England. Formal armies could not be everywhere, and the war was fought as much through local networks of information and assistance as through pitched battles. Tuckett embodies this informal infrastructure of loyalty that sustained families and communities through years of disruption.
