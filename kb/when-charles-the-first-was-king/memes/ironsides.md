# Ironsides

The Ironsides were the elite heavy cavalry of the Parliamentarian army during the English Civil War, personally raised and trained by Oliver Cromwell. Their discipline, religious conviction, and tactical effectiveness made them the decisive military instrument of the Parliamentary cause.

## Definition

The term "Ironsides" was originally a nickname for Cromwell himself — "Old Ironsides" — before being applied to his regiment. Unlike the Cavalier cavalry, who were often gallant but undisciplined, the Ironsides were notable for their strict discipline, sober Puritan character, and ability to regroup after a charge rather than scattering in pursuit. This tactical discipline proved decisive at Marston Moor, where the Ironsides routed the Royalist cavalry.

## Historical Context

Cromwell raised his first cavalry troop in 1643, selecting men "of a spirit" who would "stick to him" in battle. He favoured religious zealots — Independents and other Puritan sectaries — over social rank, creating a meritocratic officer corps. The Ironsides became the nucleus of the New Model Army, whose victories at Marston Moor (1644) and Naseby (1645) ended Royalist military power.

## In the Novel

The Ironsides appear as a formidable and almost mythic force in the novel's battle scenes. During the fighting at Marston Moor, the narrator describes how "the Ironsides broke our ranks" ((026-019)), forcing the Royalist cavalry to regroup. The encounter conveys both the military power and the dread that Cromwell's disciplined troopers inspired in their opponents.
