# Kush

Kush is the northernmost of the [[Black Kingdoms]] in Robert E. Howard's Conan stories, situated south of the great empire of [[Stygia]]. It is mentioned across multiple stories as a source of fierce warriors and as the first of the black kingdoms encountered when traveling south from the Hyborian world.

## Geography

Kush occupies the grasslands and savannahs immediately south of Stygia, forming a transitional zone between the Stygian empire and the deeper Black Kingdoms to the south. Its territory includes the coastal regions where Stygian and Kushite interests collide, as well as interior grasslands that support a pastoral and warrior culture:

> ...cruising along the coasts of Kush. The sea and the ways of the sea you know, and there's no better man to have at the helm when we sight a Stygian war-galley. [003-027]

The kingdom's proximity to Stygia makes it a frontier zone, subject to Stygian raids and slave-taking expeditions. The coasts of Kush are regularly traversed by ships from the Hyborian kingdoms, and the sea routes along Kush's shores connect the northern world with the mysterious southern lands:

> I am bound for Kush, to trade beads and silks and sugar and brass-hilted swords to the black kings for ivory and copra and copper ore and slaves and pearls. [003-013]

## Inhabitants

The Kushites are a black people known throughout the Hyborian world for their martial qualities and physical prowess. They appear in the Conan stories as warriors, slaves, pirates, and traders. Kushite slaves are a common presence in the Hyborian kingdoms, and Kushite warriors serve in the armies of various nations:

> ...dark-haired Zamorians, ebon Kushites, olive-skinned Shemites. [004-016]

In "Shadows in Zamboula," the cannibalistic slaves who terrorize the city at night are identified as being from [[Darfar]], a region associated with but distinct from Kush proper. Kush itself is portrayed with greater nuance as a kingdom with its own political structure, ruled by "black kings" who trade with the northern powers.

The Kushite character is depicted as fierce and warlike, qualities that make Kushite soldiers valued as mercenaries but also feared by their northern neighbors. The kingdom's proximity to Stygia has produced a culture hardened by centuries of conflict with the northern empire.

## History

Kush's history in the Conan stories is defined by its relationship with Stygia. As the immediate southern neighbor of the Stygian empire, Kush has borne the brunt of Stygian expansionism for centuries. Stygian raids into Kushite territory for slaves and plunder are a recurring background element in Howard's world-building.

In "Queen of the Black Coast," Conan's career as a pirate along the Kushite coasts is mentioned. The sea routes off Kush are described as dangerous but profitable, with Stygian war-galleys and Kushite raiders both posing threats to northern traders.

In the broader history of the Hyborian Age, Kush is one of the ancient black kingdoms that have existed since before the rise of the Hyborian civilizations. Its kings maintain diplomatic and trade relations with the northern powers while defending their territory against Stygian encroachment.

## Appearances

Kush is mentioned or referenced across multiple Conan stories:
- "The Phoenix on the Sword" — referred to in the context of southern campaigns
- "Queen of the Black Coast" — coastal trade and piracy along Kushite shores
- "Shadows in Zamboula" — contextual reference to the southern black kingdoms
- "Jewels of Gwahlur" — geographic context for the Black Kingdoms
- "The Hyborian Age" essay — historical and geographic context
