## Overview

Khemsa, also called the Rakhsha, is a sorcerer in the service of the [[Black Seers of Yimsha]], who appears in *The People of the Black Circle*. Initially acting as an agent of the Seers alongside the Turanian spy Kerim Shah [005-023], Khemsa is a wielder of dark arts who betrays his masters when the servant girl [[Gitara]] persuades him to seize power for himself. His rebellion — driven by love and ambition — briefly makes him a formidable independent force, but ultimately leads to his destruction at the hands of the very masters he served. Before his death, he provides [[Conan]] with the Stygian girdle that proves crucial to the assault on Yimsha [005-374].

> "And I the Black Seers of Yimsha," said Khemsa; "and my masters are greater than yours, for they have accomplished by their arts what Yezdigerd could not with a hundred thousand swords." [005-024]

## Agent of the Black Seers

Khemsa enters the story as an operative of the [[Black Seers of Yimsha]], dispatched to aid Kerim Shah — who is secretly a spy for King Yezdigerd of Turan — in the plot to assassinate King Bhunda Chand of Vendhya [005-023]. Khemsa is the one who explains to Kerim Shah the mechanics of the death-magic: a stolen lock of the king's hair, carried by camel caravan up the Zhaibar Pass to Yimsha, serves as the point of contact through which the Seers draw the king's soul from his body across "gulfs of echoing space" [005-019][005-021].

Khemsa's manner is cold and emotionless. When the servant girl [[Gitara]] reports that Conan has abducted the Devi [[Yasmina]], Khemsa shows no reaction beyond a nod [005-106]. He is a disciplined instrument of his masters' will — or appears to be.

> "Even the arts you call sorcery are governed by cosmic laws," answered the man in the green turban. "The stars direct these actions, as in other affairs. Not even my masters can alter the stars." [005-017]

Khemsa's role as agent also involves more direct applications of power. He murders the seven Afghuli headmen held in the governor's prison — a massacre that severs [[Conan]]'s leverage and sets the Afghuli tribes against their own chief [005-143][005-144]. He does this as part of his and Gitara's scheme to seize [[Yasmina]] for themselves and extort a ransom from Vendhya [005-114].

## Betrayal and Love for Gitara

Khemsa's transformation from servant to rebel is catalyzed by [[Gitara]], Yasmina's maid, who has already betrayed her mistress for love of the sorcerer. On the rooftop after Conan's abduction of Yasmina, Gitara throws herself upon Khemsa, urging him to abandon his masters and use his powers for their own ambition.

> "I love you!" she cried fiercely, writhing her body against his, almost strangling him in her wild embrace, shaking him in her abandon. "I will make a king of you! For love of you I betrayed my mistress; for love of me betray your masters! Why fear the Black Seers? By your love for me you have broken one of their laws already! Break the rest! You are as strong as they!" [005-116]

Khemsa resists at first — his whole body turns cold at the thought of disobedience — but Gitara's passion overwhelms him [005-111]. He yields, intoxicated by the combination of love and the sudden prospect of wielding his power for his own ends: "The arts they have taught me shall work for me, not for my masters. We shall be rulers of the world" [005-118].

What follows is a brief but terrifying demonstration of Khemsa's power unleashed without restraint. He mesmerizes the prison guard into impaling himself on his own spear [005-138], snaps a warrior's neck with a casual touch [005-139], and shatters a bronze-bound prison door with his bare palm — "A thousand-pound battering-ram with forty men to swing it could have shattered the barrier no more completely" [005-140]. The narrative notes that "Khemsa was drunk with freedom and the exercise of his power, glorying in his might and flinging his strength about as a young giant exercises his thews with unnecessary vigor in the exultant pride of his prowess" [005-140].

## Magical Abilities

Khemsa's sorcery is rooted in hypnotism, "as is the case with most Eastern magic" [005-319]. His power over the minds of others is formidable — he can compel men to kill themselves, walk as automatons, or follow his commands without question — but it is a power built on the cultural conditioning of the East, where generations have lived in "the firm conviction of the reality and power of hypnotism" [005-319].

> Khemsa's sorcery was based on hypnotism, as is the case with most Eastern magic. The way has been prepared for the hypnotist for untold centuries of generations who have lived and died in the firm conviction of the reality and power of hypnotism, building up, by mass thought and practise, a colossal though intangible atmosphere against which the individual, steeped in the traditions of the land, finds himself helpless. [005-319]

This is his critical limitation. Against [[Conan]], a barbarian from [[Cimmeria]] where "hypnotism was not even a myth," Khemsa's mesmerism proves ineffective — the Cimmerian feels it only as "a vague impulsion, a tugging and pulling that he could shake off as a man shakes spiderwebs from his garments" [005-320][005-321].

However, hypnotism is not all of Khemsa's magic. He possesses physical combat arts of uncanny effectiveness, felling Conan with a single blow to the neck "delivered with an art forgotten of men before Atlantis sank" — a strike that would have killed a lesser man instantly [005-323][005-325]. He can conjure a "horse-of-air" to transport himself and Gitara across vast distances [005-145], and his penetrating gaze and commanding presence are unnerving even to hardened men like Kerim Shah, who "feared Khemsa as a man fears a poisonous reptile" [005-122].

The Stygian girdle Khemsa wears — woven of a woman's black hair and set with strange jewels, with a buckle in the form of a golden serpent-head — carries its own power. He gives it to [[Conan]] as he dies, and it proves instrumental in protecting the Cimmerian against the Seers' magic during the assault on Yimsha [005-374][005-524]. The girdle's power is mysterious; Khemsa himself did not understand why it aided Conan but not its former owner [005-555].

## Death on the Mountain

Khemsa's rebellion is short-lived. When he confronts [[Conan]] and [[Yasmina]] on the trail to Afghulistan, intent on seizing the Devi, the Black Seers themselves descend from Yimsha in a whirling crimson cloud [005-327]. Four vulture-headed men in black robes, Khemsa's former masters, face him in a battle of wills — "a war of souls, of frightful brains steeped in lore forbidden to men for a million years" [005-332].

The conflict is agonizing. Khemsa, driven by "frantic fear and desperation," holds his ground against the four Seers longer than anyone expected — because his love for Gitara anchors his soul against their psychic assault [005-333]. But the Seers recognize this and turn their gaze upon Gitara instead. She is irresistibly compelled to walk backward over the precipice, and Khemsa crawls after her, groaning, unable to save her. On the very brink, the Seers restore her reason in a final act of cruelty, so that she dies fully aware of her fate.

> On the very brink she paused, standing stiffly, her heels on the edge, and he fell on his knees and crawled whimpering toward her, groping for her, to drag her back from destruction. And just before his clumsy fingers touched her, one of the wizards laughed, like the sudden, bronze note of a bell in hell. The girl reeled suddenly and, consummate climax of exquisite cruelty, reason and understanding flooded back into her eyes, which flared with awful fear. She screamed, clutched wildly at her lover's straining hand, and then, unable to save herself, fell headlong with a moaning cry. [005-335]

Khemsa, now utterly broken, charges at his tormentors with a knife. One of the Seers stamps his foot, and the mountain itself answers — the ledge splits, and an avalanche carries Khemsa screaming into the abyss [005-336][005-337].

Yet Khemsa is not quite dead. Later, Conan finds him on the trail — "a broken, shredded, bloody heap that gibbered and gnashed splintered teeth" [005-365]. Dying, Khemsa raves of revenge: "They killed Gitara; I will kill them — the acolytes, the Four of the Black Circle, the Master himself! Kill — kill them all!" [005-369]. He gives Conan the Stygian girdle with his final cryptic instructions: "Follow the golden vein through the abyss. Wear the girdle. I had it from a Stygian priest. It will aid you, though it failed me at last. Break the crystal globe with the four golden pomegranates. Beware of the Master's transmutations — I am going to Gitara — she is waiting for me in hell" [005-374].

The Master of Yimsha later dismisses Khemsa with contempt: "My foolish Khemsa thought to make himself great — poor fool, bursting material doors and hurtling himself and his mistress through the air from hill to hill! Yet if he had not been destroyed his power might have grown to rival mine" [005-422].
