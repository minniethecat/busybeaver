## Overview

Constantius, called "the Falcon," is the Kothic voivode (military commander) of the Free Companies and the primary antagonist of *A Witch Shall Be Born*. A mercenary leader of Shemitish soldiers, Constantius plots with the witch [[Salome]] to overthrow Queen [[Taramis]] of [[Khauran]] and install Salome as a puppet ruler. For seven months he rules Khauran as Salome's consort, his reign marked by systematic cruelty, extortion, and the violent oppression of the Hyborian populace — until [[Conan]] returns at the head of a Zuagir army and breaks him on the plain.

> "Constantius, whom men called Falcon, was tall, broad-shouldered, slim-waisted, lithe and strong as pliant steel. He was handsome in an aquiline, ruthless way." [006-055]

## The Kothic Voivode

Constantius commands the Free Companies, a body of professional mercenaries recruited from the Shemitish cities of the west — Pelishtim, Anakim, and Akkharim. His soldiers number ten thousand, described as "blue-bearded devils" by the people of Khauran [006-086]. At the opening of the story, Constantius has already positioned his army outside the walls of Khauran under the pretense of requesting passage to Turan, with himself held as a hostage to guarantee his men's good behavior [006-041].

This pose of good faith is entirely fraudulent: Constantius is already in league with Salome, admitted into the palace at night through her machinations. His physical description underscores his predatory nature — "his dark eyes were penetrating and alert, the hardness of his thin lips not softened by his thin black mustache" [006-055]. Every detail conveys a man whose calculating intelligence is matched only by his capacity for ruthlessness.

> "When I decided to take the throne of Khauran, I cast about for a man to aid me, and chose the Falcon, because of his utter lack of all characteristics men call good." — Salome [006-061]

## The Conspiracy with Salome

The usurpation of Khauran is achieved through a conspiracy between Constantius and Salome, the witch-born twin sister of Queen Taramis. Salome enters the city in Taramis's guise, orders Constantius's guards dismissed, and has the real queen seized and imprisoned in the dungeons [006-066]. Constantius serves as Salome's enforcer, personally dragging the struggling Taramis to her cell while Salome commands that all jailers be murdered to ensure no witness survives [006-076].

Constantius's relationship with Salome is purely transactional — two predators exploiting each other's strengths. She provides legitimacy (in her false persona as Taramis) and the authority to disband Khauran's native army; he provides the military force necessary to hold the throne. His sardonic remarks throughout the story reveal a man entirely unburdened by conscience or loyalty. When Salome informs him that she chose him for his complete lack of virtue, he responds with an elaborately ironic bow: "I am overwhelmed, princess" [006-062].

> "Constantius smiled with a glint of strong white teeth under his thin mustache" [006-077]

## The Tyranny of Khauran

For seven months after the coup, Constantius reigns as Salome's consort and the effective ruler of Khauran. The scholar Astreas, an eyewitness, records the conditions in a letter: "The wretched kingdom groans under exorbitant taxation, the farms are stripped to the bone" [006-151]. Constantius and his Shemites extract wealth systematically, "their fairest daughters taken to glut the insatiable lust of Constantius and his mercenaries" [006-152].

His governance is distinguished by calculated terror. A program of persecution targets young Khaurani men capable of bearing arms — "many have ruthlessly been slaughtered, others sold as slaves to the Turanians" [006-153]. The only armed force in the kingdom is Constantius's own mercenaries, who ride through the fields "with whips in their hands, like the slave-drivers of the black serfs who toil in the plantations of southern Zingara" [006-151]. When a palace guard revolt breaks out, Constantius crushes it decisively [006-158]. Valerius, a young soldier who believes the real Taramis is imprisoned, is hunted by Constantius's spies and disappears [006-161][006-162].

Constantius also crucifies his most dangerous opponent. After the initial coup, Conan — captain of the queen's guard — is captured alive at Constantius's explicit order [006-100]. Constantius personally oversees the crucifixion, watching the Cimmerian nailed to a cross in the desert, mockingly saluting him before riding away [006-103][006-107]. This act of cruelty ultimately proves his undoing, as Conan survives the cross through sheer barbarian vitality and returns to exact vengeance.

> "Constantius saluted him mockingly" — the voivode's gesture as he leaves Conan to die on the cross [006-103]

## The Fate of the Falcon

When Conan returns at the head of a Zuagir army augmented by exiled Khaurani soldiers and desert tribesmen, Constantius is forced to march out and meet him in the open plain. His scouts have reported siege engines that prove to be "mere frames of palm trunks and painted silk" — a ruse devised by Conan to draw him out [006-276]. Constantius orders the charge, but his disciplined Shemitish ranks are broken by the Zuagir horsemen and the vengeful Khaurani exiles. His lieutenant Khumbanigash falls to Conan's sword, and Constantius's army is routed [006-276][006-280].

Conan, having given orders that no prisoners be taken except Constantius, captures him alive. The symmetry of revenge is complete: seven months after Constantius crucified Conan on a cross in the desert, Conan crucifies Constantius on the same spot. "You are more fit to inflict torture than to endure it," Conan tells him. "You civilized men are soft; your lives are not nailed to your spines as are ours" [006-335]. Constantius, unable to endure the torment that he inflicted on others, is dead before sundown — left to the vultures whose wings "swept across the sands as they wheeled overhead" [006-338].

> "From the lips of Constantius came an inhuman cry of despair and horror" [006-338]
