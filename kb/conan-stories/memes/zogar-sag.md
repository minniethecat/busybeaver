# Zogar Sag

## Overview

Zogar Sag is a Pictish wizard and war-leader who serves as the primary antagonist in Howard's **Beyond the Black River**. Unlike ordinary Pictish warriors, Zogar Sag commands supernatural powers derived from the swamp demons and beasts of the Pictish Wilderness. He is the unifying force that transforms the traditionally fragmented Pictish clans into a coordinated military threat against the Aquilonian frontier (008-024).

## Powers and Influence

Zogar Sag's power is rooted in the dark magic of the wilderness. He is described as a "Pictish wizard" whose name is spoken with fear even by hardened frontiersmen (008-048). His connection to the swamp demons grants him abilities beyond mortal warfare — he commands beasts, reads omens, and strikes terror into his enemies through supernatural means. The Aquilonian settlers recognize that a Pictish wizard is far more dangerous than an ordinary war chief, for he wields powers that steel cannot counter (008-069)(008-379).

## The Gathering of the Clans

The central threat Zogar Sag poses is his ability to unite the Pictish clans. Conan explains that "the Picts are divided into small clans" who "have always fought each other," but warns that a unifying force like Zogar Sag could bring "forty clans" against the frontier (008-024). Through a combination of supernatural authority and strategic acumen, Zogar Sag achieves what no Pictish leader had accomplished in generations — turning scattered raiding parties into an army.

## Methods

Zogar Sag's tactics combine Pictish stealth with psychological warfare. The story opens with a Pict warrior killing an Aquilonian settler near the stockade — an assassination attributed to Zogar Sag's orchestration (008-011). The wizard employs "Pictish devils" — supernatural agents or summoned creatures — to sow terror among the settlers (008-069). His attacks are designed to demoralize as much as to kill, wearing down the frontier's defenders through fear as much as through steel.
