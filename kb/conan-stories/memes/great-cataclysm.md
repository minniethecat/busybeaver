# Great Cataclysm

## Overview

The Great Cataclysm was the geological catastrophe that marked the transition from the Pre-Cataclysmic Age to the Hyborian Age. In a single world-shaking event, "Atlantis and Lemuria sank, and the Pictish Islands were heaved up to form the mountain peaks of a new continent" (017-005). Sections of the Thurian Continent "vanished under the waves, or sinking, formed great inland lakes and seas," while volcanoes and earthquakes "shook down the shining cities of the empires" and "whole nations were blotted out" (017-005).

## Prelude

Before the Cataclysm, the world was dominated by the Thurian civilization — the kingdoms of Kamelia, Valusia, Verulia, Grondar, Thule, and Commoria. This civilization was already in decline; its "armies were composed largely of barbarian mercenaries," with Picts, Atlanteans, and Lemurians serving as "their generals, their statesmen, often their kings" (017-004). The Thurian world was ripe for transformation.

## The Event

The Cataclysm was not a single event but a cascade of disasters: continents sank beneath the waves while new mountain ranges were thrust upward from the sea floor; "volcanoes broke forth and terrific earthquakes shook down the shining cities of the empires" (017-005). The destruction was comprehensive but not uniform — some regions were devastated while others were comparatively untouched.

## Aftermath

The barbarian races fared better than the civilized ones. The Pictish Islands were destroyed, but a colony of Picts serving as a buffer on the Valusian frontier survived (017-006). The Continental kingdom of the Atlanteans "likewise escaped the common ruin," receiving thousands of refugees in ships from the sinking continent. Many Lemurians escaped to the eastern coast of the Thurian Continent, though they were "enslaved by the ancient race which already dwelt there" for thousands of years (017-006). The surviving peoples entered a new stone age, beginning the long climb that would eventually produce the Hyborian kingdoms.
