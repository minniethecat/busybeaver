# Aquilonia

Aquilonia is the most powerful of the Hyborian kingdoms in Robert E. Howard's Conan stories, a rich and expansionist empire that dominates the western world during the height of the Hyborian Age. It is the kingdom most frequently encountered in Conan's adventures—its glittering cities, armored knights, and imperial ambitions form the backdrop against which many of his exploits unfold. Conan himself eventually becomes king of Aquilonia, ruling from its capital at [[Tarantia]].

## Geography

Aquilonia is the westernmost of the Hyborian kingdoms, bordered by [[Pictish Wilderness]] to the west, [[Cimmeria]] to the north, the [[Bossonian Marches]] to the northwest, and the other Hyborian kingdoms—[[Nemedia]], [[Ophir]], and [[Argos]]—to the east. Howard's essay "The Hyborian Age" provides the definitive geographic description:

> The kingdoms of the Hyborians—Aquilonia, Nemedia, Brythunia, Hyperborea, Koth, Ophir, Argos, Corinthia, and one known as the Border Kingdom—dominate the western world. [017-023]

> North of Aquilonia, the westernmost Hyborian kingdom, are the Cimmerians, ferocious savages, untamed by the invaders, but advancing rapidly because of contact with them. [017-023]

Its southern province of Poitain is known for its mixed population, where "black hair and brown eyes are the dominant type" due to intermarriage with the Zingarans [017-027]. The northern province of Gunderland is the one region where "the pure Hyborian stock is found unblemished," its people keeping no slaves [017-027].

The [[Bossonian Marches]] serve as Aquilonia's crucial western buffer:

> The Bossonians are of medium height and complexion, their eyes brown or grey, and they are mesocephalic. They live mainly by agriculture, in large walled villages, and are part of the Aquilonian kingdom. [017-028]

## Inhabitants

The Aquilonians are the leading Hyborian people, described as tawny-haired and grey-eyed in their purest form, though centuries of mixing with neighboring peoples have diversified the population. The kingdom's society is feudal, with powerful barons, armored knights, and a sophisticated urban culture centered on cities like Tarantia.

Aquilonia's strength is reflected in the quality of its craftsmanship. The great Aquilonian broadsword is considered one of the finest weapons of the age:

> ...the blade at his girdle was a great Aquilonian broadsword. [003-028]

The Aquilonian character is described from the perspective of frontier settlers in "Beyond the Black River," where the frontiersman Balthus represents the new breed of Aquilonian woodsman growing up on the Pictish border.

## History

Aquilonia's history is the history of the Hyborian Age itself. Rising to dominance over five centuries, the kingdom became the most powerful state in the western world:

> Another five centuries and the Hybori peoples are the possessors of a civilization so virile that contact with it virtually snatched out of the wallow of savagery such tribes as it touched. The most powerful kingdom is Aquilonia. [017-024]

At its imperial zenith, Aquilonia annexed neighboring kingdoms and reduced great powers to tributary status:

> Wishing to extend their empire, her kings made war on their neighbors. Zingara, Argos and Ophir were annexed outright, with the western cities of Shem... Koth itself, with Corinthia and the eastern Shemitish tribes, was forced to pay Aquilonia tribute and lend aid in wars. [017-030]

The kingdom's military record was impressive but not unblemished. Major campaigns against Nemedia failed, an army sent into Cimmeria was routed, and "almost complete destruction of an army by the Æsir" occurred in the snow countries [017-045].

The fall of Aquilonia came not from internal decay but from external pressure—the united Pictish tribes armed with iron weapons and the Hyrkanian invasions from the east combined to overwhelm the Hyborian kingdoms "while their vigorous culture was in its prime" [017-029].

Conan's own reign as king of Aquilonia, chronicled in "The Phoenix on the Sword," "The Scarlet Citadel," and "The Hour of the Dragon," represents the kingdom at its peak of power, under a ruler who combined barbarian vitality with civilized authority.

## Notable Locations

- **Tarantia** — The capital city and seat of the Aquilonian throne
- **Poitain** — The southern province, known for its mixed Zingaran-Aquilonian population
- **Gunderland** — The northern province where pure Hyborian stock is preserved
- **Bossonian Marches** — The western frontier buffer against the Picts and Cimmerians
- **Thunder River** — The frontier in "Beyond the Black River," site of the failed Conajohara colony

## Appearances

Aquilonia appears across the majority of Conan stories:
- "The Phoenix on the Sword" — Conan as king, Aquilonian court intrigue
- "The Scarlet Citadel" — Conan's Aquilonian wars
- "The Hour of the Dragon" — the Nemedian invasion of Aquilonia
- "Beyond the Black River" — the Aquilonian frontier on Thunder River
- "The Hyborian Age" essay — extensive history of the kingdom
