## Overview

Derketa is a Shemitish goddess, identified as the Queen of the Dead. She is worshipped among the peoples of [[Shem]] and recognized by the black peoples of [[kush]], who name the deadly Apples of Derketa after her. Alongside [[ishtar|Ishtar]], Ashtoreth, [[bel|Bel]], and [[Adonis]], Derketa belongs to the pantheon of Shemitish gods recognized across the Hyborian world.

> "Above all are the gods of the Shemites — Ishtar and Ashtoreth and Derketo and Adonis. Bel, too, is Shemitish." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Nature and Attributes

Derketa's domain is death itself, earning her the title "Queen of the Dead." She is a goddess of the darker aspect — not a deity of comfort or afterlife, but a figure associated with the finality and terror of mortality. Her name is invoked in contexts of both reverence and fear.

> "Derketa is the Queen of the Dead." [[2]](#ref-2)

## The Apples of Derketa

The [[apples-of-derketa|Apples of Derketa]] are a deadly fruit known to the black peoples of Kush, named for the goddess of death. The juice of the Apples of Derketa is lethally poisonous — drinking even a small amount, or spilling it on bare flesh, brings death within moments. Conan used the fruit's poison to coat his spear when facing supernatural threats, demonstrating his resourcefulness with the dangerous flora of the southern regions.

> "That's what the black people of Kush call the Apples of Derketa. Drink a little of the juice, or spill it on your flesh, and you'd be dead before you could tumble to the foot of this crag." [[2]](#ref-2)

The Apples grow on a plant resembling a giant overspreading tree or vine, their purple fruit hanging heavy among the leaves. The juice stains steel a dull purplish crimson — Conan pierced one with his spear blade to coat it, drawing aside to avoid the falling drops.

> "Moving down to the edge of the leaves, he reached the spear up and carefully thrust the blade through one of the Apples of Derketa, drawing aside to avoid the darkly purple drops that dripped from the pierced fruit." [[2]](#ref-2)

## The Sanctuary of Dagon and Derketo

In the black kingdoms, a significant religious site was jointly dedicated to [[dagon|Dagon]] and Derketo. The sanctuary was located somewhere in or near [[keshan|Keshan]], and it became the focus of a political and religious conspiracy in "[[Jewels of Gwahlur]]."

The Stygian agent [[Thutmekri]] sought to have the [[teeth-of-gwahlur|Teeth of Gwahlur]] removed from the temple at [[Alkmeenon]] and placed in the sanctuary of Dagon and Derketo, using a falsified oracle to pressure the Keshani priests into compliance. This plot — which Conan ultimately foiled — reveals the sanctuary as a place of sufficient importance that possessing its sacred treasures would confer significant political and religious authority.

> "Take the Teeth of Gwahlur from the crypt where they were placed so long ago. Give the Teeth of Gwahlur into the hands of Thutmekri, the Stygian, to place in the sanctuary of Dragon and Derketo." [[3]](#ref-3)

The pairing of Dagon (referred to as "Dragon" in some translations) with Derketo as co-deities of a sanctuary in the black kingdoms confirms that Derketa's cult had spread far beyond Shem into the southern lands, likely through [[stygia|Stygian]] influence and the trade routes along the southern coasts.

## Worship

Derketa holds a place among the major deities of the Shemitish pantheon — specifically named alongside [[ishtar|Ishtar]], Ashtoreth, [[bel|Bel]], and [[Adonis]] as gods that are "real" and "above all." This suggests that Derketa's cult was widespread enough across the Hyborian world that even those outside Shem recognized her name and power.

> "But the gods are real. And above all are the gods of the Shemites." [[1]](#ref-1)

Her worship extended from the great cities of Shem to the black kingdoms of the south, where the Kushites named their most feared poison after her, and where her sanctuary with Dagon drew pilgrims and political intrigue in equal measure.
