# Iranistan

Iranistan is a far eastern kingdom in Robert E. Howard's Conan stories, situated in the desert regions south of the Vilayet Sea and east of the Hyborian kingdoms. It appears in "The People of the Black Circle" as the homeland of [[Kerim Shah]], a nobleman who serves as an agent of King [[Yezdigerd]] of [[Turan]].

## Geography

Iranistan lies in the far east of the Hyborian Age world, beyond the Vilayet Sea and south of the Turanian sphere of influence. Its precise borders are not delineated in the stories, but its linguistic and cultural connections to Ghulistan place it among the cluster of eastern nations that lie between Turan and the mysterious lands of the farther Orient. The kingdom is characterized as a desert realm, with its nobility operating across the eastern political landscape as spies, mercenaries, and agents of the great powers:

> "I, whom the Kshatriyas know as Kerim Shah, a prince from Iranistan, am no greater a masquerader than most men. They are all traitors in one way or another, and half of them know not whom they serve." [005-023]

## People and Culture

The people of Iranistan speak a language related to the tongues of Ghulistan [005-209]. As part of her royal education, the Devi [[Yasmina]] of Vendhya "had been taught the languages of Iranistan and the kindred tongues of Ghulistan" [005-209], suggesting that Iranistani is considered a significant diplomatic language in the eastern courts.

The Iranistani nobility operate across the eastern kingdoms. Kerim Shah, the most prominent Iranistani in the Conan stories, masquerades as a Kshatriya nobleman in Vendhya while secretly serving Turan. His cover identity as "a prince from Iranistan" [005-023] is itself a masquerade — he is in fact a Turanian agent — but the fact that this identity is credible suggests that Iranistani nobles were familiar figures in the courts of Vendhya and the eastern kingdoms.

Iranistani agents also operate among the hill tribes. Kerim Shah "scatters coins among the border tribes to buy men like horses" [005-215], recruiting Irakzai warriors for Turanian service. The Afghuli chief Yar Afzal, himself of Iranistani ancestry, expresses suspicion of Kerim Shah despite acknowledging kinship: "I like him not, for all he is our kinsman from Iranistan" [005-215].

## Political Context

Iranistan exists in the shadow of the Turanian empire. Kerim Shah openly acknowledges his allegiance: "I serve King Yezdigerd of Turan" [005-023]. This suggests that Iranistan, while nominally independent, operates within Turan's sphere of influence, with its nobility serving as agents, spies, and diplomats for the eastern superpower.

The kingdom's position south of the Vilayet Sea and east of the main Hyborian lands places it at the crossroads of the eastern trade routes — a strategic location that explains both its diplomatic importance and its vulnerability to Turanian domination.

## In the Stories

Iranistan is referenced exclusively in "The People of the Black Circle," where its significance derives from the character of Kerim Shah:
- Kerim Shah introduces himself as "a prince from Iranistan" to the Vendhyan court, using this identity as cover for his Turanian espionage [005-023]
- The Devi Yasmina recalls "one Kerim Shah, a nobleman from Iranistan, who had dwelt for a while in Peshkhauri" and grows suspicious of his presence [005-068]
- Among the Afghuli hill tribes, a Wazuli warrior addresses Yar Afzal "in the harsh accents of the Wazuli which Yasmina could scarcely understand, though as part of her royal education she had been taught the languages of Iranistan and the kindred tongues of Ghulistan" [005-209]
- Yar Afzal identifies Kerim Shah as "our kinsman from Iranistan" while expressing distrust [005-215]
