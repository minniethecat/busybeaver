## Overview

**"The Devil in Iron"** is a [[conan|Conan]] story by Robert E. Howard, first published in the August 1934 issue of *Weird Tales*. [[set|Set]] during [[conan|Conan]]'s time as a [[kozaki]] hetman on the Vilayet Sea, the story involves a doomed city on an uncharted island, a demon of living iron, and a plot by a [[koth|Kothic]] warlord to capture [[conan|Conan]]. The tale introduces [[Khosatral Khel]], one of the most formidable supernatural opponents in the [[conan|Conan]] canon — an iron-bodied being from a pre-human epoch.

> He was a demon, too, a devil in iron, who ruled a forgotten city on a nameless island. [004-xxx]

## Plot Summary

### The Island of Xapur

While scouting the Vilayet Sea, [[conan|Conan]] is lured to the island of [[xapur|Xapur]], where the ancient city of [[dagon|Dagon]] once stood. The island had sunk beneath the waves millennia ago but has inexplicably resurfaced. There, he encounters [[khosatral-khel|Khosatral Khel]] — a demon of living iron who ruled [[dagon|Dagon]] in the pre-human age before being defeated by a hero of the Hybori. [[khosatral-khel|Khosatral Khel]] has been restored to life and seeks to reclaim his dominion [004-xxx].

### Jehungir's Trap

[[conan|Conan]]'s arrival on [[xapur|Xapur]] is not coincidental. Jehungir Agha, a [[turan|Turan]]ian lord, has schemed to trap [[conan|Conan]] on the island by spreading rumors of treasure. Jehungir plans to capture [[conan|Conan]] and deliver him to [[yezdigerd|King Yezdigerd]] of [[Turan]]. However, the scheme goes awry when [[khosatral-khel|Khosatral Khel]] proves to be far more than the "legend" Jehungir believed him to be [004-xxx].

### The Fisherman and the Dagger

On the island, [[conan|Conan]] encounters a mysterious fisherman who is later revealed to be a survivor of the original [[dagon|Dagon]], preserved by supernatural means. The fisherman possesses a dagger forged from a mysterious metal — the only weapon capable of harming [[khosatral-khel|Khosatral Khel]], as ordinary steel shatters against the demon's iron body [004-xxx].

### Final Confrontation

[[conan|Conan]] fights [[khosatral-khel|Khosatral Khel]] in a desperate battle. His sword useless against the iron demon, [[conan|Conan]] resorts to the magical dagger. In the climactic duel, he drives the blade into [[khosatral-khel|Khosatral Khel]]'s heart, destroying the ancient being and freeing the island from its iron grip. The city of [[dagon|Dagon]] sinks once more beneath the waves [004-xxx].

## Setting

The story takes place on the **Vilayet Sea**, a vast inland sea east of the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kingdoms, and specifically on the island of **[[xapur|Xapur]]**, which holds the ruins of the pre-human city of **[[dagon|Dagon]]**. The setting bridges the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kingdoms with the [[turan|Turan]]ian empire, reflecting [[conan|Conan]]'s eastward travels. The siege of [[Fort Ghori]] is referenced as a past event in the [[kozaki|kozak]]i's history.

## Themes

- **Ancient Evil Resurgent**: [[khosatral-khel|Khosatral Khel]] embodies the horror of pre-human epochs returning to threaten the human age — a recurring Howard theme shared with the Cthulhu Mythos.
- **Iron and Flesh**: The story contrasts the cold, unyielding nature of [[khosatral-khel|Khosatral Khel]]'s iron body against [[conan|Conan]]'s fierce vitality and adaptability.
- **Treachery and Honor**: Jehungir's scheming represents the decadent duplicity of civilization, while [[conan|Conan]]'s directness represents barbarian honor.

## Publication

*The Devil in Iron* was first published in *Weird Tales*, August 1934. It was later collected in *The Coming of [[conan|Conan the Cimmerian]]* (Del Rey, 2003) alongside other early [[conan|Conan]] tales.

## Connections

- [[conan|Conan]]'s service among the [[kozaki]] provides the backdrop; this story occurs in the same period as events referenced in *[[the-people-of-the-black-circle|The People of the Black Circle]]*.
- The pre-human civilization of [[dagon|Dagon]] connects to Howard's broader mythos of pre-Thurian epochs, also explored in *[[the-hyborian-age-essay|The Hyborian Age]]* essay.
- [[Khosatral Khel]] is one of the few Howard villains to be explicitly described as a demonic entity rather than a sorcerer or warlord.
