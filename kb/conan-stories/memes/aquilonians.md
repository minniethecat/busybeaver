## Overview

The Aquilonians are the Hyborian people who inhabit [[aquilonia|Aquilonia]], the mightiest and most culturally advanced of the Hyborian kingdoms. They represent the peak of Hyborian civilization — a proud, warlike race possessed of great military power, imperial ambition, and a rich cultural tradition centered on the worship of [[mitra|Mitra]].

> "Always a rich kingdom, untold wealth had been rolled in by conquest, and sumptuous splendor had taken the place of simple and hardy living. But degeneracy had not yet sapped the kings and the people; though clad in silks and cloth-of-gold, they were still a vital, virile race." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Physical and Cultural Traits

Aquilonians are described as tall, fair-skinned, and powerfully built, with the tawny hair characteristic of the Hyborian stock. They are a martial people, producing renowned heavy cavalry and disciplined infantry. Their society is stratified, with a powerful nobility, a merchant class, and a free peasantry. The Aquilonian language serves as a common tongue across much of the Hyborian kingdoms.

Conan the Cimmerian, though not Aquilonian himself, interacts extensively with Aquilonians throughout his career — from serving as a mercenary in their armies to briefly claiming the Aquilonian throne. His perspective often contrasts the hardiness of barbarians with the relative softness of civilized Aquilonians.

> "The people on the Tauran were closer to the primitive than most Aquilonians; superstitions persisted, whose sources were lost in antiquity." [[2]](#ref-2)

## Empire and Conquest

At the height of their power, the Aquilonians built an empire that stretched across the western world. Their kings annexed Zingara, Argos, and Ophir outright, and reduced Koth, Corinthia, and the eastern Shemitish tribes to tributary status.

> "Wishing to extend their empire, her kings made war on their neighbors. Zingara, Argos and Ophir were annexed outright, with the western cities of Shem, which had, with their more eastern kindred, recently thrown off the yoke of Koth." [[1]](#ref-1)

Their imperial ambitions, however, sowed the seeds of their downfall. The Aquilonians' treatment of their [[bossonians|Bossonian]] auxiliaries — taxing them heavily, conscripting them for distant wars, and mocking their simpler ways — led to a catastrophic betrayal.

## The Aquilonian-Pictish Frontier

In the western reaches of the Aquilonian realm, along the Black River, civilization met the savage Pictish wilderness. The story "[[Beyond the Black River]]" vividly depicts life on this frontier, where Aquilonian settlers and soldiers faced constant threat from the [[picts|Pictish]] tribes.

> "There, at the fort, civilization ended. Fort Tuscelan was the last outpost of a civilized world; it represented the westernmost thrust of the dominant Hyborian races." [[2]](#ref-2)

This frontier produced a distinctive breed of Aquilonian — hardened woodsmen, scouts, and rangers who had more in common with barbarians than with the silk-clad nobles of the imperial cities.

## Religion

The Aquilonians worship Mitra as their primary deity. Mitra's worship is characterized by its opposition to the darker cults of [[set|Set]] and other demon-gods. The Mitraic faith is described as a religion of light, justice, and civilization.

> "Mitra of the Hyborians must be a strong god, because his people have builded their cities over the world." [[3]](#ref-3)

## Fall of the Empire

The Aquilonian empire fell through a chain of provocations: their arrogance toward the Bossonians led to desertion on the eve of a crucial battle against Nemedia. In retaliation, the Aquilonians massacred the Bossonian leadership and quartered Shemitish mercenaries in Bossonian villages. When the Picts invaded, the Bossonian buffer was gone, and the frontier collapsed.

> "This time there were no strong walled villages in their path, manned by sturdy archers, to hold back the rush until the imperial troops could be brought up. The remnants of the Bossonians were swept out of existence." [[1]](#ref-1)

The Pictish tide, combined with a massive Hyrkanian invasion from the east, destroyed the Aquilonian empire and ended the Hyborian Age.
