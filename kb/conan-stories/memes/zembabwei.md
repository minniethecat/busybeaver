# Zembabwei

Zembabwei is a great hybrid trading kingdom in Robert E. Howard's Conan story "Jewels of Gwahlur," situated east of the southern Black Kingdoms. Ruled by twin kings, Zembabwei is described as a powerful political and mercantile entity whose ambitions drive much of the political intrigue in the story's plot.

## Geography and Character

Zembabwei lies "many days' march to the east" of [[Keshan]] [007-016], on the far periphery of the region known as the Black Kingdoms. It is described as a "great hybrid trading kingdom" [007-016], suggesting a cosmopolitan urban center where multiple races and cultures converge. The kingdom's hybrid character implies admixture between the black peoples of the region and traders and settlers from the Hyborian lands to the north or the Shemitish territories.

The kingdom is significant enough to command substantial military resources. When Thutmekri pledges Zembabwei's support to the king of Keshan, he promises "a host of black spearmen, Shemitish archers, and mercenary swordsmen" [007-017], indicating a multi-ethnic military capable of invading Punt from the east.

## Government and Religion

Zembabwei is ruled by "twin kings" [007-016], an unusual dual monarchy that suggests a complex political structure possibly reflecting the fusion of two distinct cultural traditions. The kings of Zembabwei are described as "wary" — they demand proof of the Teeth of Gwahlur's existence before committing to military action [007-042].

The kingdom maintains a major temple complex that houses "the squat gold idols of Dagon and Derketo" [007-017]. The worship of these deities — Dagon associated with the demon [[Khosatral Khel]] of Xapur, and Derketo a dark goddess of the southern world — places Zembabwei within the broader religious landscape of Howard's Hyborian Age mythology. The temple serves as the proposed destination for the Teeth of Gwahlur, which Thutmekri promises to install there as "sacred guests in the holy shrine of the kingdom, to seal the covenant between Keshan and Zembabwei" [007-017].

## Political Machinations

Zembabwei's role in "Jewels of Gwahlur" is that of a scheming imperial power. The Zembabwan plan, as deduced by Conan:

> Punt would be invaded simultaneously from the east and the west, but the Zembabwans would see to it that the Keshani did most of the fighting, and then, when both Punt and Keshan were exhausted from the struggle the Zembabwans would crush both races, loot Keshan and take the treasure by force, if they had to destroy every building and torture every living human in the kingdom. [007-043]

This reveals Zembabwei's true ambition: not trade or alliance, but territorial expansion through proxy warfare. The conquest of Punt is "but one move toward their real goal — the capture of the Teeth of Gwahlur" [007-042]. The twin kings' strategic thinking extends beyond the immediate prize to the elimination of potential rivals.

The Zembabwan embassy to Keshan includes not only the Stygian adventurer Thutmekri but also Zargheba, a Shemitish rogue who brings a veiled slave woman to impersonate the oracle of Alkmeenon [007-107] — demonstrating the kingdom's willingness to employ deceit, religious manipulation, and foreign agents to achieve its goals.

## In the Stories

Zembabwei is referenced exclusively in "Jewels of Gwahlur," where it serves as the hidden power behind the story's central conflict:
- Zembabwei's embassy arrives in Keshan led by Thutmekri, who offers an alliance against Punt [007-015]
- The Zembabwan plan to seize the Teeth of Gwahlur by proxy warfare is exposed by Conan [007-042, 007-043]
- Zembabwei's temple cult of Dagon and Derketo connects the southern kingdoms' religion to the broader Hyborian mythological world [007-017]
