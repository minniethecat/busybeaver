# Nemedia

Nemedia is one of the most prominent kingdoms of the Hyborian Age in Robert E. Howard's Conan stories. Located in the central region of the Hyborian world, it is the perennial rival of [[Aquilonia]] and one of the great powers of the age. The kingdom is noted in the framing device of the Conan stories as the source of the Nemedian Chronicles—the fictional historical document from which Howard purports to have drawn the tales of Conan.

## Geography

Nemedia occupies a central position among the Hyborian kingdoms, bordered by Aquilonia to the west and [[Brythunia]] to the northeast. Its territory encompasses fertile river valleys, rolling plains, and forested highlands. The capital city, Belverus, is one of the great metropolises of the Hyborian Age, a center of learning, trade, and political power.

The kingdom's port cities on the western coast—including [[Khrosha]], the home of [[Sergius]]—provide Nemedia with access to the Western Ocean and maritime trade routes. The Nemedian heartland is densely populated and highly cultivated, supporting a civilization that rivals Aquilonia in wealth and sophistication.

## Inhabitants

The Nemedians are a Hyborian people, closely related to the Aquilonians and other central Hyborian groups. They are characterized by a sophisticated urban culture, a powerful aristocracy, and a tradition of scholarly and priestly learning. The Nemedian Chronicles—the fictional historical chronicle that serves as the narrative frame for Howard's Conan tales—are a product of Nemedian scholarship:

> Of that epoch known by the Nemedian chroniclers as the Pre-Cataclysmic Age, little is known. [017-001]

The Nemedian priesthood produced figures of great historical significance. [[Arus]], a Nemedian priest, played a pivotal role in the evolution of the Pictish race:

> This man was Arus, a Nemedian priest, a natural-born reformer. [017-032]

The Nemedian nobility is portrayed as politically ambitious and frequently at odds with Aquilonia. Individual Nemedians who appear in the stories include [[Octavia]], the noblewoman held captive on Xapur in "The Devil in Iron," and [[Sergius]], the corsair captain of Khrosha who confronts Conan in "Shadows in the Moonlight." The priest [[Tiberias]] (not to be confused with the merchant of Velitrium in "Beyond the Black River") is another Nemedian figure of note.

## History

Nemedia's history in the Conan stories is defined primarily by its rivalry with Aquilonia. The two kingdoms are the dominant powers of the Hyborian Age, and their competition for supremacy drives much of the political background of the later Conan tales. Howard's essay "The Hyborian Age" describes Nemedia as one of the original Hyborian kingdoms:

> ...the kingdoms of the Hyborians—Aquilonia, Nemedia, Brythunia, Hyperborea, Koth, Ophir, Argos, Corinthia, and the Border Kingdom. [017-023]

Nemedia's resilience against external threats is remarkable. When the Hyrkanian invasions swept across the western world, Nemedia stood unconquered:

> Nemedia, unconquerable by Hyborians, reeled beneath their strokes. [017-055]

In "The Hour of the Dragon," Nemedia is the primary antagonist power. The Nemedian king, in alliance with other Hyborian states and the sorcerer [[Xaltotun]], orchestrates the invasion and conquest of Aquilonia. Conan, deposed from the Aquilonian throne, must fight his way back across the Hyborian world to reclaim his crown. The Nemedian conspiracy represents the most coordinated threat that Aquilonia faces in Howard's canon.

The Nemedian Chronicles are the in-universe source for the Conan stories. This framing device—that the tales are translations from a Nemedian historical text—positions Nemedia as the literate, record-keeping civilization whose scholars preserved the memory of Conan's exploits for later ages.

## Notable Locations

- **Belverus** — The capital city, center of Nemedian political and cultural life
- **Khrosha** — A port city on the western coast, home of the corsair Sergius
- **Nemedian plains** — The fertile agricultural heartland that sustains the kingdom's population

## Appearances

Nemedia is mentioned or appears in multiple Conan stories:
- "The Phoenix on the Sword" — referenced as Aquilonia's rival
- "The Scarlet Citadel" — Nemedian political machinations
- "The Hour of the Dragon" — the primary antagonist power
- "Shadows in the Moonlight" — via the character of Sergius of Khrosha
- "The Devil in Iron" — via the character of Octavia
- "The Hyborian Age" — historical overview of Nemedian civilization
