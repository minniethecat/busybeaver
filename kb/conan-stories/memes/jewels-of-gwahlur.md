## Overview

**"Jewels of Gwahlur"** is a [[conan|Conan]] story by Robert E. Howard, first published in the March 1935 issue of *Weird Tales* (as "The Teeth of Gwahlur"). [[set|Set]] in the black kingdom of [[keshan|Keshan]], it follows [[conan|Conan]] as he pursues the fabled Teeth of Gwahlur — a treasure of immense value hidden in the abandoned oracle-city of Alkmeenon. The story is notable for its African-inspired setting, its elaborate deception plot, and its exploration of [[conan|Conan]]'s growing renown across the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] world.

> Conan's fame had preceded him, even into distant Keshan; his exploits as a chief of the black corsairs, those wolves of the southern coasts, had made his name known, admired and feared throughout the black kingdoms. [007-011]

## Plot Summary

### The Oracle of Alkmeenon

The story opens with [[conan|Conan]] arriving in [[keshan|Keshan]], a black kingdom south of [[stygia|Stygia]]. The [[keshan|Keshan]]i believe that a divine oracle dwells in the ancient, ruined city of Alkmeenon, speaking through a living goddess. The Teeth of Gwahlur — a cache of priceless jewels — are kept in the oracle's sanctuary. [[conan|Conan]] plans to steal them [007-xxx].

### Rival Claimants

[[conan|Conan]] is not alone in his pursuit. [[thutmekri|Thutmekri]], a [[shem|Shem]]itish adventurer, and his [[zembabwei|Zembabwei]] allies also seek the treasure, intending to use it to finance a political coup. [[conan|Conan]] plays both sides — presenting himself as a sell-sword to the [[keshan|Keshan]]i while secretly searching for the jewels. His reputation as "a chief of the black corsairs" gives him credibility among the [[keshan|Keshan]]i lords [007-011].

### The False Oracle

[[conan|Conan]] discovers that the oracle is a fraud: the "goddess" is a [[keshan|Keshan]]i woman named [[muriela|Muriela]], coerced by the priests into playing the divine role. [[conan|Conan]] rescues her and learns the location of the Teeth from her insider knowledge. However, complications arise when the real oracle — an ancient, mummified creature — stirs to life in the depths of Alkmeenon [007-xxx].

### Treachery and Escape

[[thutmekri|Thutmekri]]'s forces arrive, and a three-way conflict erupts between [[conan|Conan]], the [[zembabwei|Zembabwei]] mercenaries, and the awakening horror in the temple. [[conan|Conan]] secures a portion of the treasure and escapes with [[muriela|Muriela]], though not before witnessing the destruction of the oracle and the collapse of the temple's deepest chambers. The full hoard of Gwahlur remains hidden — a classic Howard ending where the hero wins, but the ultimate prize slips away [007-xxx].

## Setting

The story takes place in **[[keshan|Keshan]]**, one of the black kingdoms south of [[stygia|Stygia]], and specifically in the ruined city of **Alkmeenon** — an abandoned oracle-site in the jungle. The setting is one of Howard's most vividly realized "lost city" locations, with crumbling temples, dark corridors, and a sense of ancient, decaying power. [[keshan|Keshan]] is bordered by [[Zembabwei]] to the south and [[Darfar]] to the north.

## Themes

- **Fame and Reputation**: Throughout the story, [[conan|Conan]]'s reputation as a corsair chief precedes him, demonstrating how far his fame has spread and how it can be weaponized as a form of power.
- **Fraud and Authenticity**: The false oracle plot explores the tension between manufactured religion and genuine supernatural horror — the fake goddess is replaced, in the climax, by a real monster.
- **Greed and its Limits**: [[conan|Conan]], [[thutmekri|Thutmekri]], and the [[zembabwei|Zembabwei]] all pursue the treasure, but the story suggests that some treasures are guarded by forces beyond human comprehension.

## Publication

*Jewels of Gwahlur* was first published in *Weird Tales*, March 1935, under the title "The Teeth of Gwahlur." It was collected in *The Bloody Crown of [[conan|Conan]]* (Del Rey, 2004).
