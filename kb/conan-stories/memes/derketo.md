## Overview

Derketo is a goddess in the Shemitish pantheon of the Hyborian Age, worshipped alongside [[ishtar|Ishtar]], [[bel|Bel]], and other deities of the eastern and southern lands. She represents a synthesis of the real-world ancient Near Eastern goddesses Atargatis (also called Derketo) and Astarte, adapted by Robert E. Howard for his fictional world.

In the Conan stories, Derketo is mentioned as one of the principal gods of the Shemitish peoples, and her worship extends into the Black Kingdoms and Stygia through cultural exchange and trade routes.

## Worship

Bêlit, the pirate queen in "Queen of the Black Coast," describes the Shemitish pantheon with reverence, naming Derketo among the principal deities:

> "But the gods are real," she said, pursuing her own line of thought. "And above all are the gods of the Shemites — Ishtar and Ashtoreth and Derketo and Adonis. Bel, too, is Shemitish, for he was born in ancient Shumir, long, long ago and went forth laughing, with curled beard and impish wise eyes, to steal the wisdom of the gods." (PN 003-087)

This places Derketo as a major deity in the Shemitish pantheon, co-equal with [[ishtar|Ishtar]], Ashtoreth, and Adonis.

## Sanctuary of Dragon and Derketo

In "Jewels of Gwahlur," a sanctuary dedicated jointly to Dagon and Derketo is mentioned as a significant religious site in the Black Kingdoms:

> "Take the Teeth of Gwahlur from the crypt where they were placed so long ago. Alkmeenon is no longer holy, because it has been desecrated by blasphemers. Give the Teeth of Gwahlur into the hands of Thutmekri, the Stygian, to place in the sanctuary of Dragon and Derketo. Only this can save Keshan from the wrath of the gods." (PN 007-205)

The pairing of "Dragon" and Derketo as co-deities of a sanctuary in the Black Kingdoms suggests that Derketo's cult extended beyond Shem into the southern lands, likely through Stygian influence and trade along the southern coasts.

## Real-World Origins

The name Derketo comes from classical sources. In ancient Syria, Atargatis (Greek: Ἀταργάτις) was a fertility goddess sometimes referred to as Derketo (Δερκετώ). According to Diodorus Siculus and other classical writers, Derketo was a mermaid-like goddess associated with fish and water, worshipped at Ascalon and Hierapolis-Bambyce.

Howard incorporated this historical goddess into the Hyborian Age as part of the Shemitish pantheon, consistent with his practice of adapting real-world ancient mythology and placing it in a fictional pre-historical context. The Shemites in Howard's world correspond loosely to the Semitic peoples of the ancient Near East, and their pantheon reflects the syncretic religious practices of the region.
