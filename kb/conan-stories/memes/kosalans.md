## Overview

The Kosalans are a people of the far east, hailing from the kingdom of Kosala. They appear in the Conan stories primarily through the character of Baal-pteor, a Kosalan priest and strangler in the service of the dark god Yajur.

> "The priests of Kosala, the stranglers of Yajur, are strong beyond the belief of men." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Baal-pteor the Strangler

The most prominent Kosalan in the Conan canon is Baal-pteor, a servant of the priest Totrasmek in the city of [[Zamboula]]. A large, powerful man, Baal-pteor served as a strangler for the cult of Yajur, having fled his homeland of Kosala — or more specifically, the city of Yota-pong — to become Totrasmek's servant.

> "Why I fled from Yota-pong to become Totrasmek's servant is no concern of yours." [[1]](#ref-1)

Baal-pteor boasted of having broken hundreds of necks in the service of Yajur, claiming that the stranglers of Kosala possessed strength beyond ordinary men. He met his end at the hands of [[Conan]], who proved stronger — crushing Baal-pteor's throat and snapping his neck in a brutal reversal.

> "With my hands, barbarian, I shall break your neck!" [[1]](#ref-1)

## Relationship with the Afghulis

The Kosalans are noted to have favored relations with the [[Afghulis]], the fierce hill tribes who dwell in the Himelian mountains above the borders of [[vendhya|Vendhya]]. Conan himself, who once served as a war-chief of the Afghulis, made reference to this relationship when encountering Kosalan architecture and artwork in the far western regions of the Hyborian world.

> "I was a war-chief of the Afghulis who live in the Himelian mountains above the borders of Vendhya. These people favor the Kosalans." [[2]](#ref-2)

## Physical Characteristics

Kosalan men and women are described as slender and olive-skinned, with finely chiseled, exotic features. They are depicted in their art wearing filmy robes and delicate jeweled ornaments, and are often shown in attitudes of feasting, dancing, or lovemaking — suggesting a refined, pleasure-oriented culture.

> "The figures portrayed were those of slender, olive-skinned men and women, with finely chiseled, exotic features." [[2]](#ref-2)

## Cultural References

A Kosalan city of considerable sophistication appears to have been built far to the west of their homeland, puzzling observers. The existence of Kosalan-influenced structures so distant from Kosala suggests either extensive trade networks, colonization, or the movement of Kosalan artisans and builders across the Hyborian world.
