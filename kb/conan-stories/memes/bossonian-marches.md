# Bossonian Marches

The Bossonian Marches are the fortified border region that separates the civilized kingdom of [[Aquilonia]] from the barbarian lands of [[Cimmeria]] and the [[Pictish Wilderness]] in Robert E. Howard's Conan stories. The Bossonians, a mixed-race people descended from aboriginal stock and early Hyborian settlers, are renowned throughout the Hyborian world as master archers and stubborn defensive fighters.

## Geography

The Bossonian Marches stretch from the [[Border Kingdom]] in the north to [[Zingara]] in the southwest, forming a bulwark for Aquilonia against both the Cimmerians and the Picts:

> Between Aquilonia and the Pictish wilderness lie the Bossonian marches, peopled by descendants of an aboriginal race, conquered by a tribe of Hyborians, early in the first ages of the Hyborian drift. [017-028]

The territory consists of fortified villages, agricultural lands, and the walled settlements that give the Bossonians their defensive strength. The contrast between the Bossonian way of life and the frontier beyond is stark, as noted by the frontiersman in "Beyond the Black River":

> The Bossonians have sheltered you Aquilonians from the outer wildernesses for too many centuries. [008-035]

## Inhabitants

The Bossonians are a distinct people within the Aquilonian kingdom, physically and culturally different from the pure Hyborian stock:

> The Bossonians are of medium height and complexion, their eyes brown or grey, and they are mesocephalic. They live mainly by agriculture, in large walled villages, and are part of the Aquilonian kingdom. [017-028]

Their mixed ancestry—aboriginal stock combined with early Hyborian settlers—produced a people who "never attained the civilization of the purer Hyborians" [017-028], but who developed exceptional military capabilities. The Bossonian archers are legendary:

> They are stubborn defensive fighters, and centuries of warfare against northern and western barbarians have caused them to evolve a type of defense almost impregnable against direct attack. [017-028]

The Bossonians appear in the Conan stories as soldiers, archers, and frontier guards:

> The rangy figures of Aquilonian pikemen and forest runners mingled with the shorter, stockier forms of Bossonian archers. [008-101]

## History

The Bossonian Marches have been part of the Aquilonian kingdom for centuries, serving as the critical buffer that shields the civilized heartland from barbarian incursions. The Bossonians' walled villages and fortified positions have proven "almost impregnable against direct attack" [017-028], allowing Aquilonia to develop its civilization behind their defensive screen.

The frontier beyond the Marches—Conajohara, on Thunder River—represents Aquilonia's attempt to extend beyond the traditional Bossonian buffer. The colonization effort described in "Beyond the Black River" ends in disaster, validating the wisdom of relying on the Bossonian defensive line:

> "No; Conajohara is lost to Aquilonia. The frontier has been pushed back. Thunder River will be the new border." [008-416]

The Bossonian role is underappreciated by the Aquilonians they protect—a frontier resentment that surfaces in the stories—but their military value is undeniable.
