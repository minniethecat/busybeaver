## Overview

Baal-pteor is a Kosalan priest and servant of [[Totrasmek]] in Zamboula, encountered in the story *Shadows in Zamboula*. A towering, brown-skinned man of inhuman strength, he was trained from infancy by the priests of Yajur in the art of ritual strangulation. In Zamboula he serves as Totrasmek's enforcer, helping to drug and control [[Jungir Khan]] through the hypnotic power of a crystal sphere. He meets his end when [[Conan]] — impervious to the illusions Baal-pteor conjures — overpowers him in a brutal hand-to-hand struggle and snaps his neck.

> "I am Baal-pteor," the man answered. "Once, long ago and in another land, I had another name. But this is a good name, and why Totrasmek gave it to me, any temple wench can tell you." [009-146]

## Background and Origins

Baal-pteor was born in Kosala, a Hyborian Age kingdom of the East. As he himself recounts, he was chosen by the priests of Yajur in his infancy and raised from childhood as a strangler — a ritual executioner trained to kill sacrifices with his bare hands on the altar of Yota-pong [009-159]. His training was methodical and lifelong: "When I was a child they gave me infants to throttle; when I was a boy I strangled young girls; as a youth, women, old men and young boys. Not until I reached my full manhood was I given a strong man to slay on the altar of Yota-pong" [009-159].

At some point he left — or was exiled from — Kosala and entered the service of Totrasmek, the high priest of the Temple of Hanuman in Zamboula. Totrasmek gave him the name "Baal-pteor," a title whose significance "any temple wench can tell you" [009-146] — implying a religious role of degraded or obscene connotation.

> "I am a strangler of Yota-pong. I was chosen by the priests of Yajur in my infancy, and throughout childhood, boyhood and youth I trained in the art of slaying with the naked hands" [009-159]

## Role in the Conspiracy

In Zamboula, Baal-pteor serves as Totrasmek's right hand in the conspiracy to control [[Jungir Khan]], the city's nominal ruler. He assists Totrasmek in drugging Jungir Khan, keeping him pliable and dependent [009-174]. When [[Zabibi]] comes to the temple seeking help for the drugged khan, Baal-pteor is the one who drags her through a hidden panel in the wall, delivering her to Totrasmek.

Baal-pteor's primary weapon is a hypnotic crystal sphere, which he uses to trap [[Conan]] when the Cimmerian comes to rescue Zabibi. The sphere creates vivid illusions — Conan experiences himself transported to a marsh where a buffalo charges him, only to discover the dying buffalo transforms into Baal-pteor himself, whose severed head snaps at Conan's throat with beast-like tusks [009-152][009-154]. Throughout the ordeal, Baal-pteor lounges on a divan, "shaking with silent laughter" [009-153], delighting in the torment his illusions cause.

> Baal-pteor was laughing at him, and that laugh was edged with menace that made the hackles rise on Conan's neck and sent a red wave of murder-lust driving across his vision. [009-150]

## Appearance and Abilities

Baal-pteor is physically imposing — "slightly taller than Conan, and much heavier" — with "unnaturally long" arms and great hands that "opened and closed, twitching convulsively" [009-158]. His skin is brown, marking his eastern origin. He possesses both the supernatural power of illusion-casting through the crystal sphere and the mundane but formidable skill of a trained strangler whose grip can crush the life from even the strongest opponent.

Despite his boasts, however, Baal-pteor has never faced a man like [[Conan]]. When the Cimmerian's throat proves too thickly muscled to crush, and Conan's own grip begins to pulverize Baal-pteor's throat muscles, the Kosalan experiences genuine fear — "his black eyes widened as he felt the thick cords of muscles that protected the barbarian's throat" [009-161], and then "fear flooded his eyes" as he realized his strength was insufficient [009-162].

## Death

Baal-pteor's death is one of the most brutally physical in the Conan stories. After Conan resists the illusions, the two men engage in a strangling contest. Baal-pteor's supernatural-strength grip cannot overcome the "masses of woven iron cords" of the Cimmerian's neck [009-162], while his own throat yields under Conan's fingers. With a savage wrench, Conan twists Baal-pteor's head around "until the ghastly face leered over the left shoulder, and the vertebrae snapped like a rotten branch" [009-167].

Even in the aftermath, Conan bears the marks of the struggle — "blood trickled down his broad breast from the wounds Baal-pteor's finger nails had torn in the skin of his neck" [009-168] — acknowledging that "he had almost met his match in the inhuman Kosalan" [009-168].

> And with a savage wrench he twisted Baal-pteor's head around until the ghastly face leered over the left shoulder, and the vertebrae snapped like a rotten branch. [009-167]
