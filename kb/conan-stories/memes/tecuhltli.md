# Tecuhltli

The Tecuhltli are one of the two warring clans inhabiting the lost city of [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]] in Robert E. Howard's [[conan|Conan]] story "[[red-nails-story|Red Nails]]." They dwell by the western gate of the enclosed city and are locked in a generations-long blood feud with the [[Xotalancas]], who occupy the eastern quarter. When [[Valeria]] and [[Conan]] enter [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]], they ally themselves with the Tecuhltli, becoming key figures in the final chapter of the centuries-old war.

## Origins and Identity

The Tecuhltli trace their lineage to the Tlazitlans, a mixed [[stygia|Stygian]]-eastern people who built [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]]. [[conan|Conan]] recognizes their type: "They live on the shores of Lake Zuad, near the border of [[kush|Kush]]. They're a sort of mongrel [[stygia|Stygians]], mixed with another race that wandered into [[stygia|Stygia]] from the east some centuries ago" [011-089].

Like the [[xotalancas|Xotalancas]], the Tecuhltli bear the physical traits of their ancestry — lean, muscular frames, lank blue-black hair, and the wild eyes of people who have known nothing but war for generations. The warrior [[Techotl]], born in the castle of Tecuhltli, has "never set foot outside the city" [011-103] in his thirty-five years of life — a testament to the total isolation of [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]]'s inhabitants.

## The Castle of Tecuhltli

The Tecuhltli dwell in a fortress-like quarter at the western end of [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]]. It is described as a great castle that forms one end of the enclosed city, mirroring the [[xotalancas|Xotalanc]] stronghold at the eastern gate. The Halls of Silence — the disputed no-man's-land between the two quarters — are where the daily ambushes and skirmishes of the feud take place. [[techotl|Techotl]] explains the geography:

> "The halls and chambers between Tecuhltli and Xotalanc are a disputed region, owned by no man. We call it the Halls of Silence." [011-111]

## The Blood Feud

The war between Tecuhltli and [[xotalancas|Xotalanc]] has been grinding on for so long that the original cause is barely remembered. The feud is conducted through guerrilla warfare in the Halls of Silence — warriors from both sides slip through the corridors, setting ambushes and counting their kills. Each Tecuhltli kill is recorded as a crimson nail driven into a black pillar, a tally that absorbs the meaning of existence itself:

> "Five dead dogs! Five slain! Five crimson nails for the black pillar! The gods of blood be thanked!" [011-072]

The exultation with which [[techotl|Techotl]] greets the death of five [[xotalancas|Xotalancas]] — spitting on their corpses, stamping on their faces, "dancing in his ghoulish glee" [011-072] — reveals how the feud has dehumanized both sides. There is no negotiation, no truce, no concept of mercy. Warriors fight with "the fury of desperation" [011-049] because the alternative is torture.

## Olmec and Tascela

The Tecuhltli are ruled by Prince [[Olmec]], a powerful warrior and political leader. Olmec's rule, however, is shadowed by the presence of [[Tascela]], a figure from the clan's deeper past whose beauty and cruelty are legendary. [[tascela|Tascela]]'s true nature — and her connection to the ancient magic of [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]] — becomes the decisive factor in the story's climax.

## Alliance with Conan and Valeria

When [[techotl|Techotl]] encounters [[valeria|Valeria]] in the Halls of Science and witnesses her killing a [[xotalancas|Xotalanc]] warrior, he immediately claims her as an ally: "You are our friend, or you would not have slain my enemy" [011-031]. He promises that "My people will welcome you! They will honor you!" [011-081], recognizing that the aid of two powerful outsiders — one a [[cimmeria|Cimmerian]] warrior, the other an [[aquilonia|Aquilonian]] pirate — could tip the balance of the eternal feud.

[[conan|Conan]] and [[valeria|Valeria]] fight alongside the Tecuhltli through the climactic battle, but they ultimately become embroiled in the clan's internal politics, particularly the machinations of [[tascela|Tascela]].

## Final Fate

The destruction of [[xotalancas|Xotalanc]] does not bring peace to Tecuhltli. The feud ends not through victory but through catastrophe — and the revelation of [[tascela|Tascela]]'s true identity and powers forces both [[conan|Conan]] and [[valeria|Valeria]] to fight against the very people they had aided. In the story's aftermath, both clans are effectively destroyed, leaving the sealed city of [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]] as a tomb for the last remnants of the Tlazitlan people.
