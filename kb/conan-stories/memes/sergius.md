# Sergius

Sergius of Khrosha is a Nemedian corsair captain who appears as a secondary antagonist in Robert E. Howard's Conan story "Shadows in the Moonlight" (published in *Weird Tales*, September 1934). He is the leader of a band of pirates who encounter Conan on an island off the Vilayet Sea. Sergius bears a personal grudge against Conan stemming from a past encounter, and his vendetta drives the conflict of the story's middle act.

## Rivalry with Conan

Sergius's first appearance in the story immediately establishes his history with Conan. When he recognizes the Cimmerian, his response is instantaneous and filled with the weight of an old grievance:

> "Sergius of Khrosha, by Crom!" [002-193]

The recognition stuns Sergius, and he wastes no time in expressing his intent:

> "Did you think I had forgot? Ha! Sergius never forgets an enemy." [002-194]

The nature of their prior conflict is implied rather than narrated in detail. Sergius's hatred runs deep enough that he would eagerly seize the opportunity to settle the score, and the encounter on the island gives him exactly that chance.

## Combat with Conan

The fight between Sergius and Conan is brief but fierce. As captain, Sergius leads his men against the Cimmerian and his companion [[Olivia]]. The combatants engage directly:

> The combatants engaged, Sergius coming in with a rush... [002-198]

Sergius is a capable fighter, but Conan's superior strength and battle-hardened skill prove decisive. The clash ends with Sergius's death, cut down by the Cimmerian in single combat [002-199]. His fall sends shockwaves through his crew, who had expected their captain to prevail.

## Aftermath

Sergius's death has immediate consequences for the pirates under his command. His lieutenants are uncertain how to proceed:

> Evidently these men had been Sergius's lieutenants... [002-212]

Following the laws of the [[Red Brotherhood]]—or the custom of corsairs in this region—Conan's victory over Sergius grants him the right to claim leadership of the band. However, the issue is not so easily resolved. [[Aratus]], another member of the pirate crew, challenges this succession, leading to a further confrontation.

Sergius's body is left where he fell on the plateau [002-213]. His followers eventually drag the corpse to the western rim and cast it into the sea—a rough sea-burial fitting for a corsair [002-215].

## Significance

Sergius serves a dual narrative function in "Shadows in the Moonlight." As a secondary antagonist, he provides a combat set-piece that demonstrates Conan's prowess and establishes the pirate band's dynamics. More importantly, his death creates the power vacuum that drives the story's middle act, as different factions within the pirate crew vie for leadership.

The Nemedian origin of Sergius is characteristic of Howard's world-building, where the civilized kingdoms of the Hyborian Age produce some of the most dangerous—and most personally motivated—antagonists Conan faces.
