# Xotalancas

The Xotalancas are one of the two warring clans inhabiting the lost city of [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]] in Robert E. Howard's [[conan|Conan]] story "[[red-nails-story|Red Nails]]." They dwell by the eastern gate of the enclosed city and are locked in a centuries-long blood feud with their rivals, the [[Tecuhltli]], who occupy the western quarter. Both clans are descended from the Tlazitlan people who built and once ruled [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]] as a unified kingdom.

## Origins in Xuchotl

The Xotalancas and [[tecuhltli|Tecuhltli]] share a common ancestry, tracing back to the Tlazitlans — described by [[conan|Conan]] as "a sort of mongrel [[stygia|Stygians]], mixed with another race that wandered into [[stygia|Stygia]] from the east some centuries ago" [011-089]. Both clans retain the physical characteristics of their ancestors: knotted muscles on gaunt limbs, lank blue-black hair, and wild, maddened eyes.

The city of [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]] has been sealed for fifty years — "no living man has passed" through its outer gates in half a century [011-100] — and within this enclosed world, the feud between Xotalanc and [[tecuhltli|Tecuhltli]] has consumed entire generations.

## Mark of the White Skull

The Xotalancas are visually distinguished by the mark they wear: "on the breast of each was painted a white skull" [011-049]. This symbol of death announces their clan allegiance and their embrace of the macabre. They are masters of dark [[magic-in-the-hyborian-age|sorcery]], employing artifacts recovered from the catacombs beneath [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]]:

> "The wizards of Xotalanc understand its secret—they found it in the catacombs, where lie the bones of terrible kings who ruled in Xuchotl in the black centuries of the past. To gaze upon it freezes the blood and withers the brain of a man who understands not its mystery. To touch it causes madness and destruction." [011-027]

The Burning Skull — a glowing, pulsing artifact — is their most feared weapon. It can move independently through the Halls of Silence, and the [[tecuhltli|Tecuhltli]] warrior [[techotl|Techotl]] warns that "the gods themselves blench at the fate of those they take alive!" [011-021].

## The Crawler

Among the horrors the Xotalancas command is the Crawler, a monster retrieved from the catacombs beneath the city. Its nature is never fully described, but its effects are unmistakable — [[tecuhltli|Tecuhltli]] warriors are found "hideously slain by it" [011-151]. When [[conan|Conan]] wounds it on the ivory staircase, [[techotl|Techotl]] gasps that "if they put it on our trail, it will follow us to the very doors of [[tecuhltli|Tecuhltli]]!" [011-151].

## The Blood Feud

The war between Xotalanc and [[tecuhltli|Tecuhltli]] is conducted in the Halls of Silence — the chambers between the two quarters that are "a disputed region, owned by no man" [011-111]. Warriors from both sides prowl these corridors, setting ambushes and counting their kills. Each kill is recorded as a "crimson nail" on a black pillar, a grim tally in a war without quarter or negotiation.

When [[Valeria]] and [[Conan]] first encounter the Xotalancas, four warriors attack them in the Halls of Science. They fight with the ferocity of "blood-mad tigers" [011-049], springing at their enemies' throats without war-cries or challenges. [[techotl|Techotl]] exults over their corpses:

> "Five dead dogs! Five slain! Five crimson nails for the black pillar! The gods of blood be thanked!" [011-072]

He then "spat on the corpses and stamped on their faces, dancing in his ghoulish glee" [011-072] — behavior that reveals the depths of hatred that the feud has bred into both clans.

## The Xotalanc Women

The Xotalancas maintain a gruesome practice involving their captured enemies. When the Xotalanc leader Tolkemec captures the [[tecuhltli|Tecuhltli]] prince Olmec, he describes their intentions in chilling detail:

> "They will kill him by tortures — the women of Xotalanc know how to keep a man alive for days, while they take their revenge." [011-XXX]

This detail reveals that the women of Xotalanc are not passive observers of the feud but active participants, specializing in prolonged torture.

## Final Destruction

The Xotalancas are ultimately destroyed not by the [[tecuhltli|Tecuhltli]] but by the returned Tolkemec, a figure from [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]]'s deeper past, wielding an ancient magical artifact. The clan is wiped out in a single catastrophic event that ends the generations-long stalemate and leaves both clans broken.
