Turan is the dominant eastern power of the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]], a sprawling empire whose cavalry armies and expansionist kings threaten the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kingdoms of the west. With its capital at Aghrapur on the shores of the Vilayet Sea, Turan represents the eastern pole of power in the Hyborian world, counterbalancing [[aquilonia]] in the west.

## Geography and Reach

Turan's empire stretches from the Vilayet Sea in the west to the borders of Khitai in the far east, and from the steppes of Hyrkania in the north to the borders of [[shem|Shem]] and Iranistan in the south. Its strategic position gives it control over the major east-west trade routes.

> The Turanian empire was the colossus of the eastern world, its mailed fist poised to hammer the divided Hyborian kingdoms into submission. [002-039, 003-025]

Key territories within the Turanian sphere include:

| Region | Relationship | Strategic Value |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Vilayet Sea coast | Imperial core | Maritime trade, naval power |
| Hyrkanian steppes | Vassal/tributary | Cavalry recruitment |
| Zamora | Tributary kingdom | Buffer against Hyborian west |
| Eastern satrapies | Direct imperial rule | Silk Road trade revenue |

## Military Power

Turan's military strength rests on its cavalry—the Turanian horse-archers and heavy lancers are feared across the Hyborian world. Conan himself served in the Turanian army at various points in his career:

- **Horse-archers**: Turan's steppe-nomad auxiliary cavalry, using composite bows with deadly accuracy
- **Heavy cavalry**: armored lancers drawn from the Turanian nobility
- **Imperial guard**: the king's personal retinue, equipped with the finest arms and armor

> Turan's horsemen were the terror of the western marches, striking deep into Hyborian territory before the ponderous feudal levies could muster a response. [006-091 through 006-101]

## Politics and Expansion

Turanian kings are portrayed as ambitious, calculating rulers who view the Hyborian kingdoms as a prize to be taken. The empire's westward pressure is a recurring source of conflict in the Conan stories:

- Turan maintains a network of spies and agents throughout the Hyborian kingdoms
- The kingdom of Zamora serves as a Turanian client state, providing intelligence and forward bases
- Turan periodically launches major campaigns westward, testing the defenses of [[aquilonia]] and its allies

## Turan in Conan's Life

Conan's relationship with Turan is complex—he has served in its armies as a mercenary, raided its caravans as a thief, and fought its soldiers as an enemy:

> "By Crom, I've served in the Turanian army and I've fled from it. I've stolen Turanian gold and I've spent it in Turanian taverns." [005-076, 005-089]

This personal history gives Conan an intimate knowledge of Turanian military tactics and court politics, which serves him well in his later career as a commander and eventual king of Aquilonia.

## Significance

Turan functions in the Conan stories as a foil to the Hyborian kingdoms—a disciplined, centralized empire whose military efficiency contrasts with the fractious feudalism of the west. The Turanian threat is one of the few forces capable of uniting the normally warring Hyborian kingdoms, and the empire's ambition provides the geopolitical tension that drives much of the series' larger-scale conflict.
