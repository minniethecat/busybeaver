## Overview

The Tlazitlans are a tribe of mixed [[stygians|Stygian]] ancestry, originally from the shores of Lake Zuad near the border of Kush. They are described as "a sort of mongrel Stygians, mixed with another race that wandered into Stygia from the east some centuries ago." The Tlazitlans play a central role in the history of Xuchotl, which they conquered and inhabited for half a century.

> "They're a sort of mongrel Stygians, mixed with another race that wandered into Stygia from the east some centuries ago and were absorbed by them. They're called Tlazitlans." [[1]](#ref-1)

## The Rebellion and Flight

More than half a century before Conan's arrival at Xuchotl, the Tlazitlans rebelled against the Stygian king. Defeated in battle, they fled southward with a thousand fighting-men, their women, and children. For many weeks they wandered through grasslands, desert, and hills before reaching the great southern forest.

> "More than half a century ago a tribe of the Tlazitlans rebelled against the Stygian king, and, being defeated in battle, fled southward. For many weeks they wandered over grasslands, desert and hills, and at last they came into the great forest." [[1]](#ref-1)

At the forest's heart they discovered Xuchotl — an ancient walled city whose inhabitants initially resisted them, shooting arrows from the walls. The Tlazitlans were trapped between the city and the deadly forest. Survival forced them to lay siege.

## The Conquest of Xuchotl

The Tlazitlans, led by the brothers [[tecuhltli|Tecuhltli]] and [[xotalancas|Xotalanc]], along with a Stygian named Tolkemec who knew the ways of the [[xuchotlans|Xuchotlans]], eventually breached the city walls. They slew many defenders and took the rest captive, settling into the enormous enclosed city.

For a time they lived in peace, but the seeds of destruction were already sown. A dispute over a woman — the witch Tascela, who was already ancient when the Tlazitlans left Lake Zuad — ignited a feud between the brothers.

## The Great Feud

The Tlazitlans split into two warring factions: the Tecuhltli and the Xotalancas. The ensuing blood-feud consumed the city for decades, reducing its population from a thousand warriors to a handful of survivors. The feud's savage logic — "blood called for blood, and vengeance followed swift on the heels of atrocity" — ensured that neither side could retreat.

> "Anger and resentment and jealousy blossomed into bloodshed and rape and murder. Once the sword was drawn there was no turning back; for blood called for blood, and vengeance followed swift on the heels of atrocity." [[1]](#ref-1)

By the time Conan and Valeria entered Xuchotl, the Tlazitlans had nearly exterminated themselves through their endless civil war.

## Significance

The Tlazitlans embody Howard's recurring theme of civilization turning inward upon itself and decaying into fratricidal violence. Their story is a microcosm of the larger Hyborian Age narrative: a once-vigorous people, cut off from the outside world, consumed by internal hatred. Conan himself observes that the city-dwellers have become pale, silent wraiths, having "absorbed into their souls the silence of the empty halls."
