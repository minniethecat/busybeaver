# Afghulis

The Afghulis are a warlike hill tribe inhabiting Afghulistan in the Himelian Mountains, featured prominently in Robert E. Howard's [[conan|Conan]] story "The People of the Black Circle." During the events of the story, the tribe is led by [[Conan]] the [[cimmeria|Cimmerian]], who has become their chief — a foreigner from the west who commands their loyalty through his martial prowess.

## Conan the Afghuli Chief

At the time of "The People of the Black Circle," [[conan|Conan]] has established himself as the chief of the Afghulis, a remarkable feat for an outsider in the tribal politics of the Himelians. The [[vendhya|Vendhya]]n governor Chunder Shan recognizes this:

> "These men are of the tribe of Afghulis, but he is a foreigner from the west, and he is called Conan." [005-050]

[[conan|Conan]]'s foreignness is apparent even in his bearing. The text notes that "He did not walk like an Afghuli, and was bluntly direct where the East is subtle" [005-037] — a [[cimmeria|Cimmerian]] directness that sets him apart from the hill tribes he leads, even as his martial prowess earns their respect.

## The Seven Headmen

The central political dynamic of the Afghuli arc in "The People of the Black Circle" revolves around seven Afghuli headmen imprisoned by the [[vendhya|Vendhya]]n governor Chunder Shan in the fortress at Peshkhauri. [[conan|Conan]] describes the gravity of the situation:

> "They are headmen among the Afghulis." [005-059]

The tribe's pressure on [[conan|Conan]] is relentless — "The tribe is baying like wolves at my heels because I have not procured their release" [005-061] — and this pressure drives the entire plot of the story. [[conan|Conan]]'s plan involves capturing the Devi [[yasmina|Yasmina]] to exchange for the headmen's lives. When the headmen are killed by [[khemsa|Khemsa]]'s [[magic-in-the-hyborian-age|sorcery]] at [[gitara|Gitara]]'s urging:

> "Destroy them, so Chunder Shan can not use them to buy back the Devi. Then let us go into the mountains and take her from the Afghulis." [005-115]

[[conan|Conan]]'s belief that the governor has executed the headmen triggers his fury — "I went to fetch my Afghulis, but they've turned on me" [005-371] — though in truth the headmen were killed by [[khemsa|Khemsa]], not by Chunder Shan, a misunderstanding that nearly costs [[conan|Conan]] his life.

## Chunder Shan's Hostage Gambit

Governor Chunder Shan of Peshkhauri uses the seven Afghuli headmen as leverage to negotiate with [[conan|Conan]]. The governor's strategy involves offering a ransom beyond what hill tribes normally see:

> "Well, more than an Afghuli ever saw," amended Chunder Shan. "And it is but a drop of all the treasure of Vendhya. Why should we desire gold? It would be more to our advantage to hang these seven thieves." [005-051]

The governor's contempt for Afghuli wealth is matched by [[conan|Conan]]'s threat to escalate: "I'll raise a horde and lead it to the very gates of Peshkhauri!" [005-061]. The governor does not believe any hill-horde could take the fortified city, but he fears a "devastated countryside" — a realistic acknowledgment of the Afghulis' capacity for raiding warfare.

## Culture and Character

The Afghulis are characterized by their fierce independence and warrior ethos. They are described as operating outside the authority of the [[vendhya|Vendhyan kingdom]], inhabiting the rugged mountain territory of Afghulistan. A "chief of the Afghulis" is considered a figure of significant martial standing in the world of the eastern kingdoms:

> "A rare prize, Conan; fit mate for a chief of the Afghulis." [005-180]

The Afghulis are distinct from their neighbors the [[wazuli|Wazulis]], with whom they maintain a complex relationship of rivalry and occasional alliance. Yar Afzal, chief of the [[wazuli|Khurum Wazulis]], acknowledges the Afghulis as a separate and powerful tribe: "Afghulistan lies a good many miles farther on to the west" [005-199] from Wazuli territory.

## Tribal Rivalries and Warfare

The Afghulis exist in a state of endemic warfare with neighboring tribes and the settled kingdoms. The Wazuli warrior who encounters [[conan|Conan]] expresses the inter-tribal resentment plainly:

> "They wish me to murder you and take the girl to hold for ransom. They say why should the Afghuli dogs profit by her, when it is the people who take the risk of guarding her?" [005-205]

When [[conan|Conan]]'s Afghuli warriors believe he has betrayed them by failing to rescue their headmen, their fury is absolute — "working himself into a frothing frenzy" [005-353] — and they turn on their own chief, demonstrating that tribal loyalty is conditional on results. The pursuing Afghuli band is a constant threat throughout the story, driving [[conan|Conan]] toward Yimsha and forcing [[kerim-shah|Kerim Shah]]'s [[irakzai|Irakzai]] to choose the sorcerers' mountain over facing "the Afghulis who are following this man" [005-291].

The Afghulis' reputation for ferocity extends beyond the mountains. When Chunder Shan rides in pursuit of the abducted Devi, he notes: "It will take many riders to smoke out that hive. There will be squadrons riding up the Zhaibar by dawn" [005-165] — an acknowledgment that the Afghuli villages are a formidable military target.

## In the Stories

The Afghulis feature centrally in "The People of the Black Circle":
- [[conan|Conan]]'s seven Afghuli headmen are imprisoned in Peshkhauri, driving the hostage dynamic [005-050]
- Chunder Shan negotiates with [[conan|Conan]] over the headmen's ransom [005-041 through 005-061]
- [[gitara|Gitara]] persuades [[khemsa|Khemsa]] to destroy the seven headmen, eliminating Chunder Shan's leverage [005-115]
- [[conan|Conan]]'s plan involves returning to Afghulistan with the captive Devi [[yasmina|Yasmina]] [005-172]
- The Afghulis turn on [[conan|Conan]] when they believe the headmen have been executed [005-353]
- Afghulistan serves as the destination for [[conan|Conan]]'s flight through the mountains [005-265]
