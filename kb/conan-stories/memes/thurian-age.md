# Thurian Age

## Overview

The Thurian Age is the prehistoric era that preceded the [[Hyborian Age]], centered on a vast landmass known as the Thurian Continent. It was dominated by a group of civilized kingdoms — [[valusia|Valusia]], Commoria, Grondar, Verulia, Kamelia, and Thule — whose peoples "spoke a similar language, arguing a common origin" (017-001). The Thurian civilization was eventually destroyed by the [[Great Cataclysm]], which reshaped the entire world and ushered in the age that would become the setting for [[conan|Conan]]'s adventures.

## The Thurian Continent

The Thurian Continent was the primary landmass of the pre-cataclysmic world. According to Howard's fictional history, the continent was enormous, though its civilized kingdoms "occupied a comparatively small portion of the whole planet" (017-003). [[valusia|Valusia]] was the westernmost kingdom, while Grondar marked the eastern boundary — beyond which "stretched a wild and barren expanse of deserts" inhabited by "scattered clans and tribes of primitive savages" (017-003).

> Valusia was the westernmost kingdom of the Thurian Continent; Grondar the easternmost. East of Grondar, whose people were less highly cultured than those of their kindred kingdoms, stretched a wild and barren expanse of deserts. (017-003)

The continent was also home to a "mysterious civilization, unconnected with the Thurian culture, and apparently pre-human in its nature" far to the south, and a "human, but mysterious and non-Thurian" race on the far-eastern shores (017-003). These enigmatic cultures remained outside the sphere of Thurian influence and survived [[great-cataclysm|the Cataclysm]] in varying forms.

## The Barbarian Peoples

Three major barbarian races surrounded the Thurian kingdoms during this age:

- **[[picts|Picts]]** — Inhabitants of islands far out on the western ocean (017-002). A colony of [[picts|Picts]] settled in [[valusia|Valusia]]'s southern mountains as a buffer against foreign invasion and survived [[great-cataclysm|the Cataclysm]] — these became the ancestors of the [[Picts|Pictish]] peoples of the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]] (017-006).
- **[[Atlanteans]]** — Dwellers on a small continent between the [[picts|Pictish]] Islands and the Thurian mainland (017-002). They "founded a kingdom on the mainland" through conquest, and a Continental kingdom of [[atlanteans|Atlanteans]] survived [[great-cataclysm|the Cataclysm]] to become ancestors of the [[Cimmerians]] (017-006).
- **[[Lemurians]]** — Inhabitants of a chain of large islands in the eastern hemisphere (017-002). Those who escaped to the eastern coast of the Thurian Continent after [[great-cataclysm|the Cataclysm]] were enslaved by an ancient race and endured "thousands of years" of "brutal servitude" (017-006), eventually rising to become the [[Hyrkanians]].

## Decline and Collapse

The Thurian civilization entered a fatal decline before [[great-cataclysm|the Cataclysm]] struck. Howard describes a society that had grown dependent on outsiders for its military strength:

> The Thurian civilization was crumbling; their armies were composed largely of barbarian mercenaries. Picts, Atlanteans and Lemurians were their generals, their statesmen, often their kings. (017-004)

The internal decay was chronic rather than acute: "Of the bickerings of the kingdoms, and the wars between [[valusia|Valusia]] and Commoria, as well as the conquests by which the [[atlanteans|Atlanteans]] founded a kingdom on the mainland, there were more legends than accurate history" (017-004). The civilization was already dissolving from within when [[great-cataclysm|the Cataclysm]] delivered the final blow.

## The Great Cataclysm

The end of the Thurian Age came with the [[Great Cataclysm]], a world-shattering geological event:

> Then the Cataclysm rocked the world. Atlantis and Lemuria sank, and the Pictish Islands were heaved up to form the mountain peaks of a new continent. Sections of the Thurian Continent vanished under the waves, or sinking, formed great inland lakes and seas. Volcanoes broke forth and terrific earthquakes shook down the shining cities of the empires. Whole nations were blotted out. (017-005)

Only a few pockets of Thurian-descended peoples survived: a "remnant of one of the non-[[valusia|Valusian]] nations" known as the Zhemri dwelt in the southeastern low mountains, while "scattered clans of apish savages, entirely ignorant of the rise and fall of the great civilizations" persisted here and there (017-009).

## Legacy

The Thurian Age is the foundation of Howard's prehistoric chronology. Its remnants shaped the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]]: the survivors of its barbarian peoples became the major races of [[conan|Conan]]'s world, its destroyed continent provided the geography of the new era, and its legend — particularly the story of [[valusia|Valusia]] and the Serpent Men — remained a source of tales and terrors for millennia afterward.
