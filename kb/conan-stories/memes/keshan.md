# Keshan

Keshan is a southern kingdom of the Hyborian Age located in the region known as the [[Black Kingdoms]], in Robert E. Howard's Conan story "Jewels of Gwahlur" (published in *Weird Tales*, March 1935). The kingdom is notable for housing the legendary Temple of Gwahlur, which contains the Treasure of Gwahlur—a cache of jewels that draws fortune hunters from across the Hyborian world. It is also the seat of the high priest [[Gorulga]], who leads the expedition to recover the treasure.

## Geography

Keshan is situated in the southern reaches of the Hyborian world, south of [[Stygia]] and east of the southern coastline. The kingdom encompasses a diverse landscape that includes fertile river valleys suitable for agriculture and the mountainous highlands where the Temple of Gwahlur is hidden.

The temple itself occupies a remote mountain valley, accessible only by treacherous paths through the highlands. The isolation of the temple site—far from the kingdom's populated centers—is a key plot point: its remoteness allowed it to be abandoned and "lost" for generations, and its rediscovery by Gorulga sets the story's events in motion.

The capital of Keshan—also called Keshan in the text—is a walled city that serves as the center of political and religious authority. The kingdom's rivers provide transportation routes and sustain the agricultural base that supports the Keshani population.

## Inhabitants

The Keshani people are described as one of the peoples of the Black Kingdoms, with physical and cultural characteristics that differentiate them from the Hyborian peoples of the north. Their society is organized around a monarchy supported by a powerful priesthood, with the high priest Gorulga wielding influence that rivals that of the king.

The priesthood of Keshan is the guardian of the Temple of Gwahlur and the oracle Yelaya, whose preserved body sits in state within the temple. The priests maintain the rituals and prophecies associated with the oracle, though their devotion is tempered by the political and material ambitions that drive Gorulga and his acolytes.

Keshan's material wealth—particularly its gold and ivory—makes it a target for foreign interests. [[Thutmekri]], the Stygian emissary, has established himself in the Keshani court, attempting to manipulate the treasure expedition for his own benefit. The kingdom's political dynamics are shaped by this intersection of religious tradition and foreign intrigue.

## History

Keshan's known history in Howard's writings centers on the events of "Jewels of Gwahlur." The Temple of Gwahlur was established in an earlier era, when the oracle Yelaya was a living prophetess whose pronouncements guided the kingdom's rulers. After Yelaya's death, her body was preserved and placed on a golden dais within the temple, where it remained as an object of veneration.

Over time, the temple was abandoned as the kingdom's center of gravity shifted. The exact location of the temple and its treasure was lost to living memory, persisting only in legend and priestly tradition. When Gorulga, the current high priest, succeeded in locating the temple site, he organized an expedition to recover the treasure—the event that draws Conan into Keshani territory.

The temple's rediscovery sets off a chain of deceptions. [[Muriela]], a Corinthian dancing-girl coerced by Thutmekri, impersonates Yelaya in an attempt to manipulate the priests. The treasure itself proves to be guarded by an ancient entity—a massive ape-like creature that kills Gorulga and drives the intruders from the temple.

## Notable Locations

- **Keshan (city)** — The capital and seat of royal and priestly authority
- **The Temple of Gwahlur** — The abandoned oracle temple in the mountain highlands, containing the Treasure of Gwahlur and Yelaya's preserved body
- **The Oracle Chamber** — The inner sanctum where Yelaya's body sits on its dais

## Appearances

Keshan appears exclusively in "Jewels of Gwahlur," which is the sole source for the kingdom's depiction in Howard's canon.
