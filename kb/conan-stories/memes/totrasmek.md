## Overview

Totrasmek is the high priest of Hanuman in the desert trading-town of Zamboula, and one of the most feared figures in the city. From his temple-shrine that "stood alone in the midst of a square surrounded by a stone wall" [009-124], Totrasmek wields power that rivals the political authority of [[Jungir Khan|Jungir Khan]], the Turanian satrap. His death at the hands of [[Conan]] during the events of *Shadows in Zamboula* breaks the cult's stranglehold on the city.

> "Totrasmek, the priest of Hanuman, who for years had spun his web of power about the kings of Zamboula" [009-171]

## The Temple of Hanuman

The temple of Hanuman occupies an isolated square in Zamboula, a place shunned by the general populace. "The people shunned the temple of Hanuman as hares shun the lair of the serpent" [009-129], an indication of the terror the cult inspires. Within the temple, the idol of Hanuman leers down from its carved mask in the uncertain light [009-131], presiding over rites that include human sacrifice — "so maidens danced in the sacrifice to Hanuman centuries ago" [009-194].

Totrasmek's chambers are enclosed within a labyrinthine series of passages and traps. He is served by [[Baal-pteor]], a strangler from Yota-pong who fled his homeland to become the priest's servant [009-159][009-160]. The temple provides cover for the cult's darkest practice: the procurement of victims for the cannibal slaves of Darfar who roam the streets by night, a system of terror Totrasmek controlled and exploited [009-051][009-052].

## Control Over Zamboula

Totrasmek's influence extends far beyond his shrine. "All in Zamboula fear him, even Jungir Khan and Nafertari" [009-127] — the satrap of Zamboula and his mistress are as much in his shadow as the common people. The Hyrkanians harbor no love for him, and the priests of Set fear him [009-106], yet none dare move against him openly.

His power rests on a combination of religious dread and direct manipulation through drugs and sorcery. When [[Zabibi|Nafertari]] spurned his advances, Totrasmek drugged the satrap Jungir Khan, driving him into madness — "to spite me, Totrasmek gave him a drug that drove him mad" [009-075]. This act of personal vengeance against the satrap's mistress demonstrates the reach and impunity of the high priest's power. Totrasmek "desired me, and because I repulsed him, he made me a slave" [009-094], Nafertari explains, revealing the personal cruelty beneath his priestly facade.

> "They fear Totrasmek, as all in Zamboula fear him" [009-127]

## Cult of Hanuman

The Hanuman cult under Totrasmek centers on worship of the ape-god, with practices that include ritual dance, human sacrifice, and cannibalism. The Darfar slaves — black cannibals notorious for their appetites — operate under the cult's protection, harvesting victims from the streets of Zamboula by night [009-051][009-055]. "Conan knew now why people locked themselves in at sunset in Zamboula" [009-052] — fear of the cult's predations governed the rhythms of life in the city.

Totrasmek himself possesses arcane knowledge. He is aware of intruders in his temple through supernatural means: "He might have known Totrasmek would know when anyone entered the temple" [009-138]. He also holds the [[Star of Khorala]], a magical ring with a jewel stolen from a secret temple in the east [009-183], which he refuses to surrender even under threat.

## Encounter with Conan and Death

Totrasmek's downfall begins when [[Conan]] enters Zamboula and is drawn into Nafertari's scheme against the priest. Nafertari, disguised as the dancing-girl [[Zabibi]], seeks both the cure for her lover Jungir Khan's madness and the recovery of the Star of Khorala [009-183]. Conan agrees to accompany her to the temple of Hanuman.

Within the temple, Totrasmek confronts the intruders with sardonic confidence. When Conan threatens him, Totrasmek orders [[Baal-pteor]] to "slay him in the square chamber before the altar where the victims are given to Hanuman" [009-174], then retreats with Nafertari as a captive. "You came like a fool, creeping through the dark," Totrasmek taunts Conan [009-179].

Conan survives the encounter with Baal-pteor and fights his way to Totrasmek's inner sanctum. There, Totrasmek attempts to force himself on Nafertari, laughing as she dances before him in humiliation — "so maidens danced in the sacrifice to Hanuman centuries ago—but never with a single rope from wall to wall" [009-194]. But Conan's arrival strikes terror: "Totrasmek screamed. His eyes dilated and his brown skin turned ashy" [009-195]. The high priest sprawls from his divan, and Conan kills him [009-196][009-197].

> "He knew he had killed Totrasmek" [009-197]

In his death, Totrasmek's grip still clenches the Star of Khorala — "Totrasmek still grasped it in his stiffening fingers" [009-200] — but Nafertari recovers the phial containing the cure for Jungir Khan's madness [009-199][009-215]. With the priest dead, Nafertari reveals her true identity and plans to march a squad to the temple of Hanuman to destroy what remains of the cult [009-224].
