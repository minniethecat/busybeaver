The Hyrkanian Invasions were the cataclysmic series of cavalry wars that ended the hegemony of the [[aquilonia|Aquilonian Empire]] and reshaped the political landscape of the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]]. Hordes of dark, steel-clad riders from the eastern steppes — the descendants of the ancient Lemurians, now known as [[turan|Hyrkanians]] — swept westward in wave after wave, shattering kingdoms that had stood for centuries.

## Origins

The Hyrkanians originated from the Lemurian survivors who were driven eastward after the [[great-cataclysm|Great Cataclysm]]. Over millennia, they evolved into a horse-borne warrior culture on the vast steppes east of the Vilayet Sea, their blood mixed with that of aboriginal mountain peoples conquered during their westward drift:

> "Now the Lemurians enter history again as Hyrkanians. Through the centuries they have pushed steadily westward, and now a tribe skirts the southern end of the great inland sea — Vilayet — and establishes the kingdom of Turan on the southwestern shore." [017-026]

The kingdom of Turan, established on the shores of Vilayet, became the launching point for the invasions that would eventually topple empires. Under monarchs like Yezdigerd, Turan grew into the mightiest power in the world:

> "Yezdigerd, king of Turan, was the mightiest monarch in the world. In his palace in the great port city of Aghrapur was heaped the plunder of empires. His fleets of purple-sailed war galleys had made Vilayet an Hyrkanian lake." [004-016]

## The First Great Thrust

The first major Hyrkanian assault on the western kingdoms came during the height of Aquilonian power, when the western empire was distracted by wars of conquest against its neighbors. The timing was devastating:

> "But before their armies could join battle, a new enemy appeared in the east, as the Hyrkanians made their first real thrust at the western world. Reinforced by adventurers from east of Vilayet, the riders of Turan swept over Zamora, devastated eastern Corinthia, and were met on the plains of Brythunia by the Aquilonians who defeated them and hurled them flying eastward." [017-030]

Though Aquilonia repelled this first invasion, the cost was severe. The anti-Aquilonian alliance of Nemedia, Brythunia, and Koth was shattered — not by Aquilonian arms but by the Hyrkanian onslaught that forced all parties to reckon with a threat from the east far greater than their internecine rivalries.

The pattern would repeat: three more invasions burst upon the Zamorian borders and the Lands of Shem, each larger than the last:

> "The Hyrkanians were not convinced; three more invasions burst upon the Zamorian borders, and the Lands of Shem, and were hurled back by the Aquilonians, though the Turanian armies grew larger as hordes of steel-clad riders rode out of the east, skirting the southern extremity of the inland sea." [017-030]

## The Great Sweep

The decisive wave came later, after the weakening of Aquilonia's imperial grip. With the imperial legions withdrawn from Zamora, the Hyrkanians struck again — and this time, there was no Aquilonian army to stop them:

> "Then again the Hyrkanians rode from the blue east. The withdrawal of the imperial legions from Zamora was their incitement. Zamora fell easy prey to their thrusts, and the Hyrkanian king established his capital in the largest city of the country." [017-050]

Simultaneously, a second, more savage Hyrkanian thrust came from the north — riders who had looped around the northern end of Vilayet, traversed icy deserts, and poured into the steppes driving aborigines before them. These two Hyrkanian hosts, initially rivals, eventually united under a great chief from the shores of the eastern ocean:

> "With no Aquilonian armies to oppose them, they were invincible. They swept over and subjugated Brythunia, and devastated southern Hyperborea, and Corinthia. They swept into the Cimmerian hills, driving the black-haired barbarians before them, but among the hills, where cavalry was less effectual, the Cimmerians turned on them, and only a disorderly retreat, at the end of a whole day of bloody fighting, saved the Hyrkanian hosts from complete annihilation." [017-050]

## Cimmerian Resistance

The Hyrkanian advance was nearly unstoppable — until it reached the hills of [[cimmeria]]. Cavalry, the Hyrkanians' decisive advantage on the open plains, became a liability in the broken terrain of the Cimmerian highlands. The [[cimmerians]], fighting on their own ground with the same ferocity that had destroyed Venarium, inflicted the first major defeat on the Hyrkanian horde:

> "Among the hills, where cavalry was less effectual, the Cimmerians turned on them, and only a disorderly retreat, at the end of a whole day of bloody fighting, saved the Hyrkanian hosts from complete annihilation." [017-050]

This battle — unnamed in the chronicles but pivotal in Hyborian history — proved that the Hyrkanian tide had limits. Cimmeria became the wall against which the eastern flood broke, preserving a fragment of the older world from the Hyrkanian conquest.

## Aftermath and Legacy

The Hyrkanian Invasions did not merely conquer territories — they dissolved an entire geopolitical order. The Aquilonian Empire, already weakened by imperial overreach, never recovered. In its place arose a patchwork of smaller, squabbling kingdoms — the world of Conan's era. The Hyrkanians themselves, their momentum spent, settled into the territories they had conquered, their kingdoms eventually fragmenting into the Turanian-dominated states that Conan would encounter in his wanderings.

The invasions left a permanent mark on the Hyborian racial landscape. Centuries of intermarriage between Hyrkanian conquerors and Hyborian subjects produced the mixed populations described in the chronicles:

> "The Hyrkanians are dark and generally tall and slender, though a squat slant-eyed type is more and more common among them, resulting from mixture with a curious race of intelligent, though stunted, aborigines, conquered by them among the mountains east of Vilayet, on their westward drift." [017-027]

## In Conan's Era

By [[conan|Conan]]'s time, the Hyrkanian threat has receded into a tense frontier standoff. Turan remains a mighty empire, but its days of sweeping westward conquest are past. Conan himself fought alongside Hyrkanian kozaki — steppe mercenaries — and learned archery among them, testimony to the complex relationship between the Hyborian kingdoms and their former conquerors:

> "I was with the kozaki, as the Hyrkanian dogs called us." [002-037]

The memory of the invasions, however, lingers in Hyborian consciousness as a warning: the east is always watching, and the riders may come again.
