# Nordic Drift

## Overview

The Nordic Drift was the great southward migration of the northern barbarian tribes — [[Nordheimir]], [[Cimmerians]], Æsir and Vanir — driven by advancing glaciers at the close of the [[Hyborian Age]]. This mass movement of peoples swept away both the [[picts|Pictish]] empire and the [[Hyrkanians|Hyrkanian]] empire, obliterated the last remnants of [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] civilization, and through subsequent migrations and intermixing, laid the ethnic foundations of the peoples who would become the modern Europeans.

## The Coming of the Ice

The Nordic Drift began with a climatic catastrophe. As Howard records: "Then began the glacier ages, and the great Nordic drift. Before the southward moving ice-fields the northern tribes drifted, driving kindred clans before them" (017-028). The advancing ice rendered the northern homelands uninhabitable, creating an irresistible pressure that cascaded southward across the known world.

## The Cascade of Peoples

The drift unfolded as a chain reaction of displaced populations. The Æsir were the first wave, "blotting out the ancient kingdom of [[Hyperborea]]" and "across its ruins, coming to grips with the [[hyrkanians|Hyrkanians]]" (017-028). [[Nemedia]] had already fallen to Nordic domination, ruled by descendants of Æsir mercenaries who had served [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kings.

Behind the Æsir came the [[Cimmerians]], pushed from their hills by the same forces:

> Driven before the onrushing tides of Nordic invasion, the Cimmerians were on the march, and neither army nor city stood before them. They surged across and completely destroyed the kingdom of Gunderland, and marched across ancient Aquilonia, hewing their irresistible way through the Pictish hosts. (017-028)

The [[cimmerians|Cimmerian]] host defeated the Nordic-Nemedians, sacked their cities, overthrew a [[hyrkanians|Hyrkanian]] army on the borders of Brythunia, and continued their irresistible advance. Behind them, "hordes of Æsir and Vanir swarmed into the lands" (017-030).

## Collapse of the Old Order

The Nordic Drift annihilated the geopolitical order of the late [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]]. Howard summarizes the transformation:

| Power | Before the Drift | After the Drift |
|-------|-----------------|-----------------|
| [[picts|Pictish]] Empire | Dominated western world from Vanaheim to Zingara | Staggering, reduced to western coastal holdings |
| [[hyrkanians|Hyrkanian]] Empire | Ruled from Hyperborea to [[shem|Shem]] | Retreating, pushed back toward Vilayet |
| [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] civilization | Already fallen to [[picts|Picts]]/[[hyrkanians|Hyrkanians]] | Vanished entirely, "scarcely a trace of their blood" remaining |
| Nordic tribes | Contained in the far north | Dominant across the western world |

The Nordic barbarians became the new masters: "The western world was now dominated by Nordic barbarians" (017-036). Yet they built no empire — "there was no such thing, at that time, as a consolidated Nordic empire. As always, the tribes had each its own chief or king, and they fought savagely among themselves" (017-038).

## The Great Cataclysm and Aftermath

The Nordic Drift was followed by another geological convulsion that reshaped the continents into their modern form. "Great strips of the western coast sank; Vanaheim and western Asgard vanished beneath the waves" (017-038). The ocean formed the North Sea and Baltic Sea; the British Isles rose from the mountains of western [[cimmeria|Cimmeria]]; and the Mediterranean washed over the lands of Argos, Koth, and [[shem|Shem]].

The Nordic tribes who survived these upheavals settled on the western shores of the shrinking inland sea (the Caspian), "beginning a pastoral existence, living in more or less peace with the [[cimmerians|Cimmerians]], and gradually mixing with them" (017-040).

## The Aryan Migrations

As populations grew, "migration became an economic necessity." The descendants of the Nordic Drift moved "southward, northward and westward, into those lands now known as India, Asia Minor and central and western Europe" (017-042) — entering history as the Aryans. Howard provides a detailed ethnic genealogy:

| Modern/Historical People | Ancestral Stock |
|--------------------------|-----------------|
| Achaians, Gauls, Britons | Pure-blooded Æsir |
| Danes | Pure-blooded Vanir |
| Goths, Germans, Anglo-Saxons | Mixed Vanir-Æsir-[[cimmerians|Cimmerian]] |
| Gaels (Irish, Highland Scots) | Pure-blooded [[cimmerians|Cimmerian]] |
| Cymric tribes, Cimbri, Gimmerai | Mixed Nordic-[[cimmerians|Cimmerian]] |
| Scythians | [[cimmerians|Cimmerian]]-[[hyrkanians|Hyrkanian]] mix |
| Tatars, Huns, Mongols, Turks | [[hyrkanians|Hyrkanian]] descendants |

This genealogy represents Howard's attempt to connect his fictional prehistory to recorded history, positioning the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]] as a lost epoch between the sinking of [[atlanteans|Atlantis]] and the dawn of civilization.

## Legacy

The Nordic Drift is the culminating event of the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]] — the mechanism by which Howard's fictional world transforms into the historical one. It swept away the ancient races and established the ethnic stocks that, after further cataclysms and millennia of migration, became the peoples of Europe, the Near East, and Central Asia.
