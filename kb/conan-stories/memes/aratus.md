# Aratus

Aratus is a minor antagonist who appears in Robert E. Howard's Conan story "Shadows in the Moonlight" (published in *Weird Tales*, September 1934). He is a corsair serving in the crew of [[Sergius]] of Khrosha, and after his captain's death at [[Conan]]'s hands, Aratus challenges Conan's claim to leadership and questions the legitimacy of the succession by combat.

## Challenge to Conan

After Conan kills Sergius in single combat, the pirate crew stands uncertain. By the unwritten law of the [[Red Brotherhood]], a captaincy can be won by slaying the previous captain. However, Aratus disputes this principle:

> "What, Aratus, would you break the law of the trade?" [002-204]

When Conan asserts his right to command, Aratus shouts a counter-argument:

> "Nay!" shouted Aratus. "He was not of our band, but an outsider!" [002-207]

This objection—that an outsider cannot claim a corsair captaincy by right of combat—carries weight with some of the crew. A clamor arises as different factions within the pirate band take sides [002-209].

The dispute threatens to escalate into violence. Aratus tries to press his case by advancing on Conan:

> At that the clamor rose anew, Aratus trying to get at the senseless form... [002-212]

## Fate

The power struggle following Sergius's death is resolved when Conan demonstrates his strength and willingness to defend his claim. Aratus's challenge fails to rally enough support, and his defiance is noted but ultimately not tolerated. Conan later demands:

> "Where is that dog Aratus?" demanded Conan. [002-272]

The implication is that Aratus has already been dealt with—either killed or driven off by Conan after the events of the power struggle. His attempt to use legalistic arguments to block Conan's ascension reflects the tension between the codes of the civilized Hyborian kingdoms and the simpler, force-based customs of the barbarians of the North.

## Significance

Aratus functions as a plot device that tests Conan's leadership after his triumph over Sergius. By raising an objection grounded in corsair custom, he creates a dramatic moment where Conan must prove not only his physical dominance but also his command presence. The unsuccessful challenge cements Conan's position among the pirates and demonstrates that the Cimmerian's authority rests on more than brute force—it also depends on the respect and fear he commands.
