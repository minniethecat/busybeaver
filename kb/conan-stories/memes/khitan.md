## Overview

The Khitan are a mysterious people of the far east, beyond even [[Turan]] and [[vendhya|Vendhya]]. Though geographically remote from the central Hyborian kingdoms, Khitan goods — particularly their silk and jade — reach across the known world through extensive caravan networks.

> "I've looted enough from the Khitan caravans to know what I'm talking about. That's jade!" [[1]](#ref-1)

## Trade and Material Culture

The Khitan are renowned for two distinctive products that appear throughout the Conan stories. The first is Khitan silk, described as practically indestructible — rugs of this silk, found in ancient and abandoned cities, remain intact after centuries of neglect.

> "Here and there were rugs of that silk known as Khitan which is practically indestructible." [[2]](#ref-2)

The second is jade, a precious stone that the Khitan trade in significant quantities. Conan, who had raided Khitan caravans during his years as a mercenary and pirate, recognized jade immediately when he encountered it in the lost city of Xuchotl, far to the west.

In Xuchotl, the ancient city's chambers contained furniture of marble, jade, and carnelian, with Khitan silk rugs scattered about — a testament to the extraordinary reach of Khitan craftsmanship and trade even in a city isolated from the outside world for centuries.

> "Cobwebs hung in the corners, but there was no perceptible accumulation of dust on the floor, or on the tables and seats of marble, jade or carnelian which occupied the chambers." [[2]](#ref-2)

## Caravan Networks

Khitan caravans traverse the vast distances of the Hyborian world, bringing luxury goods from the far east to the markets of the west. These caravans were tempting targets for raiders and mercenaries — Conan among them — who recognized the immense value of their cargoes.

> "I've looted enough from the Khitan caravans to know what I'm talking about." [[1]](#ref-1)

The presence of Khitan silk in the abandoned city of [[xuchotlans|Xuchotl]], located in the remote jungles far south of the Styx, speaks to the extraordinary reach of Khitan trade networks — their goods traveled farther than the people themselves ever did.

## The Eastern World

The Khitan occupy the furthest reach of the known Hyborian world. Moving eastward from the central Hyborian kingdoms, one passes through [[Turan]] and [[vendhya|Vendhya]], then the [[Himelian Mountains]] and lands of the [[afghulis|Afghulis]] and [[kosalans|Kosalans]], before reaching the mysterious realms of which the Khitan are the only named representatives in the Conan canon. The [[yuet-shi|Yuet-shi]], mentioned as a far eastern desert people, represent another fragment of this barely-glimpsed eastern world.

The sparse references to Khitan in Howard's stories mirror the historical European perception of China during antiquity and the medieval period — a land of legendary wealth and sophistication, known primarily through the luxury goods that traveled the Silk Road, but whose people remained shadowy and half-mythical to western observers.

## Geographic Location

The Khitan homeland lies in the extreme east of the Hyborian world, beyond the Himelian mountains, Turan, and Vendhya. They are mentioned only in passing in the Conan stories, suggesting that even by the standards of the Hyborian Age's global trade networks, the Khitan remained a remote and exotic people whose land lay at the very fringe of the known world.
