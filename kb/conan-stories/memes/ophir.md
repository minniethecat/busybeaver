# Ophir

Ophir is a wealthy Hyborian kingdom in Robert E. Howard's Conan stories, located in the central region of the Hyborian world south of [[Nemedia]] and west of [[Corinthia]]. It is renowned for its gold mines at Akbitana and the fabulous wealth of its ruling dynasty.

## Geography

Ophir occupies the fertile highlands of the central Hyborian world, a region rich in mineral wealth, particularly gold. The kingdom's gold mines—especially the legendary workings at Akbitana—are the source of its prosperity and the envy of its neighbors. Ophir's position between the great powers of Aquilonia, Nemedia, and Koth makes it a frequent target of political intrigue.

The kingdom's terrain is characterized by rolling hills, river valleys, and the fortified mining settlements that dot its landscape. Ophir's capital is a city of considerable splendor, reflecting the wealth extracted from its mines.

## Inhabitants

The Ophireans are a Hyborian people whose culture has been shaped by their kingdom's vast gold wealth. The Ophirean nobility is described as rich and politically connected, with Ophirean princesses featuring in the marriage alliances of the Hyborian world:

> I am a daughter of the King of Ophir. My father sold me to a Shemitish chief because I would not marry a prince of Koth. [002-039]

Ophirean silk and craftsmanship are renowned throughout the Hyborian kingdoms:

> The tunic that clothed his arms and legs was of Nemedia; the blade at his girdle was of Ophir. [003-028]

## History

Ophir is one of the original Hyborian kingdoms listed in "The Hyborian Age." Its history is closely tied to its gold wealth, which has made it both prosperous and a target for conquest. Throughout the Hyborian Age, Ophir has navigated between the ambitions of its larger neighbors, using its wealth to maintain a degree of independence.

In "The Phoenix on the Sword," an Ophirean princess plays a role in the political machinations surrounding Conan's throne. The kingdom's gold and strategic position ensure that Ophir is perpetually entangled in the power struggles of the Hyborian Age.
