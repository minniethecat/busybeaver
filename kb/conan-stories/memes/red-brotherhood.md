The Red Brotherhood is the loose confederacy of pirates that dominates the southern sea-lanes of the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]], operating from hidden coves and lawless port cities along the coasts of [[zingara|Zingara]], [[argos|Argos]], and the Barachan Isles. Neither a formal kingdom nor a unified fleet, the Brotherhood is bound by a shared code — the "laws of the Trade" — and a brutal meritocracy in which leadership is won and lost at sword-point.

## Structure

The Brotherhood is not a hierarchical organization in the conventional sense. Each ship operates independently under its own captain, who holds command only so long as they can defend it:

> "Then if I am one of the Brotherhood," he grunted, "the laws of the Trade apply to me; and since I killed your chief in fair fight, then I am your captain!" [002-280]

This principle — leadership by combat — is the foundation of Brotherhood governance. A captain who cannot defeat challengers is no captain at all. The system ensures that only the most dangerous fighters rise to command, though it also means Brotherhood ships are perpetually one mutiny away from chaos.

The only check on this raw individualism is the "law of the Red Brotherhood" itself — a code that even the most ruthless pirate hesitates to break:

> "What, Aratus, would you break the law of the Brotherhood, you dog?" [002-204]

## Notable Members

- [[arus]] ([[valeria|Valeria of the Red Brotherhood]]) — [[aquilonia|Aquilonian]] pirate whose deeds are celebrated in song and ballad wherever seafarers gather. Her membership in the Brotherhood defines her identity throughout "[[red-nails-story|Red Nails]]"
- [[conan]] — briefly served with Brotherhood crews and once became captain by killing the previous chief in single combat
- [[belit|Bêlit]] — [[belit|the Shemite pirate queen]], though she operated with her own black corsair crew on the Black Coast rather than under the Brotherhood banner directly

The Brotherhood's most famous member is [[valeria|Valeria]], who is introduced in "[[red-nails-story|Red Nails]]" with her Brotherhood identity as her defining characteristic:

> Against the background of somber, primitive forest she posed with an unconscious picturesqueness, bizarre and out of place. She should have been posed against a background of sea-clouds, painted masts and wheeling gulls. There was the color of the sea in her wide eyes. And that was as it should have been, because this was Valeria of the Red Brotherhood, whose deeds are celebrated in song and ballad wherever seafarers gather. [010-005]

## The Law of the Trade

The Brotherhood operates under an unwritten but strictly enforced code. Key provisions, revealed through the stories, include:

- **Leadership by combat**: Any member may challenge the captain; the winner takes command
- **Division of plunder**: Shares are distributed according to rank, with the captain receiving a commander's portion
- **No quarter for deserters**: Those who abandon their crew face death if recaptured
- **Parley is sacred**: Even among cutthroats, a truce called for negotiation is honored

[[valeria|Valeria]] reflects on the Brotherhood's culture of violence as a familiar comfort compared to the alien terrors of the [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]] jungle:

> She had proved her reckless courage a thousand times in wild battles on sea and land, on the blood-slippery decks of burning warships, in the storming of walled cities, and on the trampled sandy beaches where the desperate men of the Red Brotherhood bathed their knives in one another's blood in their fights for leadership. [010-080]

## Appearances

The Red Brotherhood appears or is referenced in multiple [[conan|Conan]] stories:

- **The Tower of the Elephant** — [[conan|Conan]]'s encounter with a Brotherhood crew in [[zamora|Zamora]], where he kills their captain and claims leadership
- **[[red-nails-story|Red Nails]]** — [[valeria|Valeria]] is consistently identified as "of the Red Brotherhood," and her identity as a Brotherhood pirate shapes her actions throughout

## Significance

The Red Brotherhood represents Howard's vision of organized lawlessness — a society that exists in the margins of empire, governed not by kings or priests but by the only authority that matters on the open sea: strength, cunning, and the willingness to kill. It is the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]]'s answer to the historical Barbary corsairs, the buccaneers of the Spanish Main, and the Viking raiders — a reminder that beyond the walls of every civilized kingdom, there are those who answer to no law but their own.
