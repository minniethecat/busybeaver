## Overview

Magic in the Hyborian Age is not a unified system but a diverse collection of traditions, ranging from the cosmic sorcery of ancient empires to the war-magic of tribal shamans. Howard's world treats sorcery as a real and dangerous force — governed by cosmic laws rather than arbitrary whims — but one that exacts a terrible price from those who wield it. Across the [[conan|Conan]] stories, magic serves as both a tool of power and a source of corruption, wielded by priests, kings, and outcasts who have bargained with forces beyond mortal comprehension.

## Nature and Cosmic Laws

[[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]] sorcery operates according to fundamental principles that its practitioners must respect. As one character observes, "Even the arts you call sorcery are governed by cosmic laws" [005-017]. This distinguishes Howard's magic from mere superstition — it is a science of the supernatural, with rules, hierarchies, and consequences. The most powerful sorcerers are those who have delved into "the deeper gulfs of cosmic sorcery" [006-034], accessing forces that lesser practitioners can only glimpse.

Magic in this world is inextricably linked to the ancient past. The pre-[[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] empires — particularly [[acheron|Acheron]] — mastered arts that later ages would forget, and much of the sorcery encountered in the [[conan|Conan]] tales is residual, lingering in ruins, artifacts, and bloodlines from those elder civilizations [012-068]. The [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] tribes themselves, being a barbarian people, had no native magical tradition; the sorcery they encountered was almost always of foreign or ancient origin.

> "Even the arts you call sorcery are governed by cosmic laws" [005-017]

## Traditions and Practitioners

### Cosmic Sorcery

The highest tier of Hyborian magic involves direct manipulation of cosmic forces. Practitioners of this art — such as the magicians of [[acheron|Acheron]] and the [[black-seers-of-yimsha|Black Seers of Yimsha]] — command powers that transcend physical weapons. They can summon entities from other planes, bind supernatural beings to their will, and work spells that affect entire kingdoms [005-052].

> "Secrets of ancient kings and wizards, long forgotten by the degenerate descendants who now huddle in their ruins" [012-068]

### Hypnotism and Mental Magic

A significant portion of [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] sorcery relies on hypnotism and mental domination. Khemsa, a notable practitioner in *The People of the Black Circle*, used hypnotism as the foundation of his power, augmented by genuine sorcerous abilities [005-319]. This blend of psychological manipulation and supernatural force was common among the lesser sorcerers who served greater masters.

### Necromancy and Dark Magic

The blackest arts — necromancy, death-magic, and spells that draw power from the Abyss — were practiced by the most feared sorcerers of the age. These practitioners, including the Black Circle and the [[stygia|Stygian]] priesthood of [[set|Set]], trafficked with demonic entities and commanded the dead [012-075]. Their magic left a palpable taint on the physical world — lands where black magic had been worked became hostile to normal life.

> "Black magic and wizardry conjured out of the cosmic blackness" [012-075]

### Tribal War-Magic

Not all Hyborian magic was cosmic in scale. Tribal shamans like [[zogar-sag|Zogar Sag]] practiced a more earthy form of sorcery rooted in their connection to the natural world. [[zogar-sag|Zogar]]'s power derived from his lineage as a "child of [[Jhebbal Sag]]" [008-387], giving him dominion over beasts and the forest. This tradition represented a different magical genealogy — older than civilization, predating the cosmic sorcery of the empires.

### The Price of Power

Magic in Howard's world consistently demands payment. Sorcerers age prematurely, become physically corrupted, or lose their humanity. The [[black-seers-of-yimsha|Black Seers of Yimsha]], despite their immense power, were physically frail and dependent on their acolytes for defense [005-467]. This theme — that sorcery consumes its practitioners — runs through all the [[conan|Conan]] tales, reinforcing the superiority of [[conan|Conan]]'s barbarian vitality over the withered power of magicians.
