# Pictish-Atlantean Wars

## Overview

The Pictish-Atlantean Wars were the series of stone-age conflicts that occurred in the centuries following the Great Cataclysm. In these wars, "the outnumbered Atlanteans were hurled back into a state of savagery, and the evolution of the Picts was halted" (017-008). The outcome shaped the racial landscape of the Hyborian Age for millennia to come.

## Prelude

After the Cataclysm, both the Picts and the Atlanteans had reverted to stone-age technology. The Atlanteans, "robbed of metals and ores, they became workers in stone like their distant ancestors, and had attained a real artistic level" (017-007). The Picts "had also reverted to flint, but had advanced more rapidly in the matter of population and war-science." Howard contrasts the two: the Picts had "none of the Atlanteans' artistic nature; they were a ruder, more practical, more prolific race. They left no pictures painted or carved on ivory, as did their enemies, but they left remarkably efficient flint weapons in plenty" (017-007).

## The Wars

When the two stone-age kingdoms clashed, the outcome was determined by demographics rather than artistry. The Picts "had the advantage of numbers and unity, whereas the Atlanteans had fallen into loosely knit clans" (017-008). In a series of bloody wars, the Atlanteans were systematically driven back. Their artistic refinement counted for nothing against the Picts' superior numbers and practical warfare.

## Aftermath

Five hundred years after the Cataclysm, the barbaric kingdoms had vanished, replaced by "a nation of savages — the Picts — carrying on continual warfare with tribes of savages — the Atlanteans" (017-008). The Atlanteans would eventually descend into the total savagery of ape-men, forgetting their former existence as humans, before beginning the long climb upward that would produce the Cimmerians (017-012)(017-023).
