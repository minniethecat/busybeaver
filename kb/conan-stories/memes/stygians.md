## Overview

The Stygians are the ancient, dusky-skinned people of [[stygia|Stygia]], a kingdom that predates the [[hyborians|Hyborian]] kingdoms and whose civilization is steeped in dark sorcery, serpent-worship, and arcane knowledge. The Stygians worship [[set|Set]], the Old Serpent, a demon-god feared even by the Hyborians, and their priest-kings are among the most powerful sorcerers of the age.

> "Set, the Old Serpent, arch-demon of the Hyborians but god of the Stygians." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Civilization and Culture

Stygia is one of the oldest continuously inhabited civilizations of the Hyborian Age. While the Hyborian kingdoms rose and expanded, Stygia remained untouched by foreign invasion for millennia. The Stygian civilization is built around the worship of Set, with a powerful priestly caste that dominates every aspect of life.

The Stygian capital includes the massive black castles of Khemi, situated where the Styx River empties into the ocean. Stygian sorcerers are the most feared practitioners of dark magic in the known world, capable of necromancy, divination, and the weaving of spells that can bind or kill.

> "Ships did not put unasked into this port, where dusky sorcerers wove awful spells in the murk of sacrificial smoke mounting eternally from bloodstained altars where naked women screamed." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Religion and Sorcery

The Stygian religion centers on Set, the serpent-god, to whom human sacrifices are offered on bloodstained altars. The Stygian priesthood guards ancient secrets and magical artifacts of terrible power — the Black Ring, the Serpent Ring of Set, and various necromantic tomes including the fabled Book of Skelos.

Stygian sorcerers such as [[thoth-amon|Thoth-Amon]] wield influence far beyond Stygia's borders. Their magic often involves serpentine themes — transformations into serpents, control over venomous creatures, and the summoning of demonic entities.

## Relations with Other Peoples

The Stygians maintain an isolated, xenophobic culture. Their kingdom has never been conquered, and they view the younger Hyborian races with contempt born of ancient superiority. The Shemites, once under Stygian dominion, were driven south by the expansion of Koth, but Stygia itself remained untouched.

> "Far to the south sleeps Stygia, untouched by foreign invasion, but the peoples of Shem have exchanged the Stygian yoke for the less galling one of Koth." [[2]](#ref-2)

Stygian galleys patrol the southern seas, and encounters between Stygian ships and those of other nations often end in bloodshed. Conan himself fought Stygian warships during his time with [[belit|Bêlit]], the Queen of the Black Coast.

## Physical Characteristics

Stygians are described as dark-skinned, with features distinct from the northern Hyborian races. They are tall and slender, with an elegance that masks their capacity for cruelty. Stygian priests are distinguished by their shaven heads and serpent-motif regalia.

## Significance

The Stygians represent the ancient, decadent, sorcery-ridden civilization that stands in contrast to the vigorous young Hyborian kingdoms. Their dark wisdom and serpent-haunted temples embody Howard's fascination with the intersection of ancient civilizations and supernatural horror. Stygia functions as both a source of arcane knowledge and a wellspring of evil — a land whose sorcerers are the perennial antagonists of the Hyborian heroes.
