## Overview

**"Shadows in the Moonlight"** (also known as "Iron Shadows in the Moon") is a [[conan|Conan]] story by Robert E. Howard, first published in the April 1934 issue of *Weird Tales*. The story follows [[conan|Conan]] and [[olivia|Olivia]], a captive noblewoman he rescues from a pirate crew, as they are stranded on an island in the Vilayet Sea. There, they must survive not only the elements and wild beasts but also a supernatural menace: a circle of iron statues that awaken under the full moon.

> "They were the demons of the isle, which took the forms of molten images, to befool us." [004-xxx]

## Plot Summary

### Escape from the Pirates

The story opens with [[conan|Conan]] fleeing a mutiny among the [[Red Brotherhood]] corsairs of the Vilayet. He rescues [[olivia|Olivia]], a captive [[ophir|Ophirean]] noblewoman, from the pirates and they escape together in a small boat. After a storm, they are cast ashore on an uncharted island covered in jungle and scattered with ruins of an unknown, ancient civilization [004-xxx].

### The Island of Statues

Exploring the island, [[conan|Conan]] and [[olivia|Olivia]] discover a circle of life-sized iron statues arranged in frozen postures of combat and flight — as if a moment of terror had been captured in metal. The statues are cold, inert, and inexplicable. [[conan|Conan]]'s barbarian pragmatism clashes with [[olivia|Olivia]]'s growing dread; she senses that the statues are not merely works of art [004-xxx].

### The Moonlit Awakening

When night falls and the full moon rises, the statues come to life. They move with an unholy, mechanical grace — iron demons who were once men, perhaps, or beings of living metal akin to [[khosatral-khel|Khosatral Khel]]. The creatures hunt [[conan|Conan]] and [[olivia|Olivia]] through the ruined city, and [[conan|Conan]] discovers that ordinary weapons are useless against them. Only fire and the breaking of the dawn can drive them back [004-xxx].

### Survival and Escape

[[conan|Conan]] and [[olivia|Olivia]] survive the night through a combination of [[conan|Conan]]'s ferocity and resourcefulness. At dawn, the statues return to their frozen state. The pair salvage a boat from the island and escape, leaving the mystery of the iron statues unsolved. The story ends with [[olivia|Olivia]]'s perspective transformed — she has witnessed something beyond the civilized world's comprehension and survived [004-xxx].

## Setting

The story takes place on an **uncharted island in the Vilayet Sea**, several days' sail from the nearest shore. The island contains the ruins of a lost city, its architecture alien to any known [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] culture. The island's isolation and the iron statues' nature suggest it is a remnant of a pre-human civilization — possibly related to the same epoch that produced [[khosatral-khel|Khosatral Khel]]'s city of [[dagon|Dagon]] in *[[the-devil-in-iron|The Devil in Iron]]*.

## Themes

- **Isolation and Terror**: The confined island setting intensifies the horror — there is nowhere to flee, and help is impossible.
- **Living Metal**: The iron statues are a variation on Howard's recurring motif of metal-bodied beings from lost ages, connecting this story to the broader mythos of pre-human civilizations.
- **Moonlight as Catalyst**: The moon as the trigger for supernatural awakening is a classic horror trope that Howard deploys with particular effectiveness, the moonlight transforming inert metal into predatory life.

## Publication

*Shadows in the Moonlight* was first published in *Weird Tales*, April 1934. It has been collected in *The Coming of [[conan|Conan the Cimmerian]]* (Del Rey, 2003) under the title "Iron Shadows in the Moon."

## Connections

- The iron statues share thematic DNA with [[Khosatral Khel]] from *[[the-devil-in-iron|The Devil in Iron]]*, both being metal-bodied entities from pre-human epochs.
- [[conan|Conan]]'s involvement with the [[Red Brotherhood]] connects this story to *[[queen-of-the-black-coast|Queen of the Black Coast]]*, where he also served among pirates.
- The isolated island motif recurs in other [[conan|Conan]] stories, including the island of [[xapur|Xapur]] in *[[the-devil-in-iron|The Devil in Iron]]*.
