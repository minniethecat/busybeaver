# Conan

## Overview

Conan of [[cimmeria|Cimmeria]] is the central protagonist of Robert E. Howard's [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]] stories. A black-haired, blue-eyed barbarian from the gloomy hills of [[cimmeria|Cimmeria]], Conan embodies the archetype of the sword-and-sorcery hero: fierce in battle, wily in survival, and possessed of a barbaric code of honor that sets him apart from both the decadent civilization he despises and the savage brutality he abhors. He identifies himself simply: "I am Conan, of Cimmeria" (002-037). Across Howard's original tales, Conan is introduced as King of [[aquilonia|Aquilonia]], and his earlier adventures are told in retrospect or through flashback, forming a non-chronological mosaic of a life lived across the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age|Hyborian]] world.

## Origins and Character

Conan is a [[cimmerians|Cimmerian]], one of the "ferocious savages, untamed by the invaders, but advancing rapidly because of contact with them" — the descendants of the [[atlanteans|Atlanteans]] (017-023). The [[cimmerians|Cimmerians]] are "tall and powerful, with dark hair and blue or grey eyes" (017-027). Howard describes the Cimmerian's nature in vivid, predatory imagery: he was born in a wild hill country and moves through both the wilderness and the courts of civilization with the instincts of a wolf — as comfortable scaling sheer cliffs as navigating the treacherous politics of kings (002-035)(002-112). His barbarian upbringing, which Howard admired as a counterweight to over-refinement, gave him keener senses, greater physical endurance, and a directness that cuts through civilized deception (017-027).

Physically, Conan is "poised like a bronze statue" (002-115), a towering figure among the Hyborian races. His barbarian physique marks him as an outsider in any civilized land: broad-shouldered, deep-chested, with panther-like agility. Howard frequently contrasts Conan's raw vitality against the softness of the city-dwellers around him, a theme that anchors the entire series' critique of civilization (002-035)(017-023).

## Career

Conan's career spans virtually every role available to a man of action in the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]]. In **Shadows in the Moonlight**, he is a wandering adventurer, shipwrecked on an island of ancient ruins and fighting for survival against both man and monster (002-037). In other tales: a thief scaling the Tower of the Elephant in [[zamora|Zamora]], a corsair sailing with [[belit|Bêlit]] the Queen of the [[black-coast|Black Coast]] (003-049), a mercenary captain leading the Free Companions, a general in the armies of Khoraja, a commander of the [[kozaki]] outlaw bands of the [[zaporoska|Zaporoska]] steppes, and ultimately King of [[aquilonia|Aquilonia]] — the barbarian who seized the throne of the greatest [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kingdom by his own hand. His versatility is a central part of his legend: the barbarian who masters every trade of the civilized world while remaining, in essence, untamed.

The circularity of Conan's arc is deliberate. Howard begins with Conan as king (in *The Phoenix on the Sword*, the first published Conan story), then progressively reveals how a Cimmerian hill-warrior came to wear a crown — a life that encompasses the entire social hierarchy of the Hyborian Age, from the bottom to the top.

## Relationships

Conan's relationships define much of the narrative arc of the stories. With **[[belit|Bêlit]]**, the [[shem|Shemite]] pirate queen who commands the *Tigress*, he found a partnership of equals rare in the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] world — she calls him "no soft Hyborian" in recognition of his ferocity and a barbarism that matched her own (003-055). Their time together sailing the [[black-coast|Black Coast]] produced some of Conan's most formative experiences. With **[[valeria]]** of the [[red-brotherhood|Red Brotherhood]], a woman warrior as ruthless and capable as Conan himself, he shared an alliance built on mutual respect and survival (002-035). His encounters with antagonists such as the [[picts|Pictish]] wizard **[[zogar-sag|Zogar Sag]]** tested his ability to face supernatural threats armed only with steel, will, and barbarian instinct (008-048). His complicated relationship with civilization — at once drawn to its treasures and contemptuous of its softness — is the central tension of his character (017-027).

## Appearances

Conan appears in all of Howard's original Hyborian Age tales. Key appearances include:

- *The Phoenix on the Sword* (1932) — Conan as King of Aquilonia, first published story
- *The Scarlet Citadel* (1933) — Conan imprisoned by the wizard Tsotha-lanti
- *The Tower of the Elephant* (1933) — Young Conan in Zamora as a thief
- *Black Colossus* (1933) — Conan as mercenary general
- *Shadows in the Moonlight* (1934) — Shipwrecked on an island of ruins
- *Queen of the Black Coast* (1934) — Conan with Bêlit, the pirate queen
- *The Devil in Iron* (1934) — Conan vs. [[khosatral-khel|Khosatral Khel]] on the island of [[xapur|Xapur]]
- *The People of the Black Circle* (1934) — Conan vs. the [[black-seers-of-yimsha|Black Seers of Yimsha]] in [[vendhya|Vendhya]]
- *A Witch Shall Be Born* (1934) — Conan crucified by [[constantius|Constantius]], returns to reclaim his throne
- *Beyond the Black River* (1935) — Conan fighting [[picts|Picts]] on the [[aquilonia|Aquilonian]] frontier
- *Red Nails* (1936) — Conan and [[valeria|Valeria]] in the dying city of [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]]

## Quotations

> "I am Conan, of Cimmeria. And I have lived as no man before me has lived — thief, pirate, mercenary, general, and king — and I have never broken my word to any man."

> "He was a Cimmerian — one of those ferocious savages, untamed by the invaders, but advancing rapidly because of contact with them" (017-023)

> "I have known many gods. He who denies them is as blind as he who trusts them too deeply. I seek not beyond death." (002-037)

> "Barbarism is the natural state of mankind. Civilization is unnatural. It is a whim of circumstance. And barbarism must always ultimately triumph." (017-027)

## Legacy

Conan stands as one of the most influential characters in fantasy literature and popular culture. Howard created in him the template for the sword-and-sorcery hero: physically powerful, morally pragmatic, and alienated from civilization. The "[[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]]" setting, with its blend of pseudo-historical grounding and dark fantasy, gave Conan's adventures a distinctive texture that set them apart from the high fantasy that would follow. His influence extends through comic books (Marvel's long-running *Conan the Barbarian* series), film adaptations (notably Arnold Schwarzenegger's 1982 portrayal), and an entire subgenre of fantasy fiction that prizes gritty realism over pastoral moralism.

Howard's Conan is not the "noble savage" of Romantic tradition but something more radical: a barbarian who is neither inferior to civilized men nor their moral superior, but simply *different* — endowed with instincts and capacities that civilization has bred out of its citizens, and whose conquest of the civilized world is presented not as tragedy but as a kind of natural correction (017-023)(017-027).
