## Overview

Set, the Old Serpent, is the arch-demon of the [[Stygia|Stygian]] pantheon and one of the most fearsome supernatural entities in the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]]. Worshipped primarily in [[Stygia]] — the ancient, decadent kingdom sprawling along the river Styx — Set's cult centers on serpent-worship, human sacrifice, and the pursuit of forbidden knowledge. Unlike the more benevolent [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] gods such as [[Mitra]] or [[Ishtar]], Set is actively malevolent, a demon-god whose power is wielded through terror, [[magic-in-the-hyborian-age|sorcery]], and ancient pacts that bind the living and the dead.

> "Where Set, the Old Serpent, arch-demon of the Stygian pantheon, was said to writhe his monstrous head among the stars" [003-025]

## Nature and Attributes

Set is consistently described with serpentine imagery, reinforcing his identity as the "Old Serpent." His temples feature serpent iconography — golden serpents coiling about altars, serpent-prowed vessels, and images of the god as a monstrous, scaled entity. The serpent, in Howard's [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] world, carries a specific theological weight: it represents not merely danger but a primordial, chthonic evil that predates human civilization and will outlast it.

> "The old Serpent, whose worshippers had wreathed his altars with the bones of a thousand victims" — the reputation of Set's cult throughout the Hyborian world [014-088]

Set's worshippers believe their god is one of the elder powers of the universe, existing before the rise of the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kingdoms and destined to endure after their fall. The god demands blood sacrifice, and his temples are said to reek of the accumulated death of centuries. Yet Set also rewards devotion with tangible power — his priests command genuine sorcerous abilities, and his most favored servants are granted unnaturally extended life.

## The Cult of Set

### Priesthood and Practices

The priesthood of Set is centered in [[stygia|Stygia]], where it functions as the state religion. [[stygia|Stygian]] priests of Set are feared throughout the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] world for their mastery of dark [[magic-in-the-hyborian-age|sorcery]] and their willingness to employ any means to advance their god's interests. They wear distinctive serpent-decorated vestments and carry the authority of their god in diplomatic as well as religious matters [007-114].

> "The Stygian and his Shemitish dogs" — referring to a priest of Set and his followers operating far from Stygia's borders, demonstrating the cult's extensive reach [007-114]

Set's altars are typically constructed of black stone and decorated with golden serpents [005-511]. The golden serpents are not merely decorative — they are ensorcelled objects, capable of animating to defend the altar against intruders [005-512]. This blending of religious symbolism with practical sorcerous defense is characteristic of Set-worship: the boundary between devotion, magic, and self-protection is deliberately blurred.

### Serpent Symbolism

The serpent is the central symbol of Set's cult. [[stygia|Stygian]] vessels are built with serpent-prowed bows [003-026], and the god himself is envisioned as a cosmic serpent whose body coils through the darkness between stars. This imagery connects Set to the broader mythological tradition of the world-serpent — a primordial entity whose body encompasses the known universe and whose awakening would herald the end of all things.

> The golden serpents on Set's altar "suddenly vibrant with awful life" — the animation of serpent imagery as a manifestation of the god's power [005-512]

### Geographic Reach

While centered in [[stygia|Stygia]], the cult of Set maintains a presence far beyond its borders. Priests of Set operate in [[Keshan]] and [[Punt]] in the Black Kingdoms [007-114], and [[stygia|Stygian]] sorcerers influenced by Set-worship appear as far north as the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kingdoms. The god's reputation precedes his agents — even barbarians like [[Conan]] recognize and respect the power of Set's priesthood, even as they despise everything the cult represents.

## Set in the Cosmic Hierarchy

Within Howard's theological framework, Set occupies a position of particular significance. He is not merely one god among many but an "arch-demon" [003-025] — suggesting a being of power that transcends the conventional distinction between god and demon. This classification places Set closer to the cosmic horrors of the outer gulfs than to the more human-centered gods of the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] pantheon.

[[conan|Conan]]'s observation that "Some gods are strong to harm, others, to aid" [003-080] is exemplified by Set, who represents the extreme of the "strong to harm" category. Unlike [[Crom]], who is indifferent to human affairs, or [[Mitra]], who represents order and light, Set is actively engaged in the world through his priesthood, and his engagement is uniformly destructive.

> "Ah, Set, how far I have crawled in my..." — the despairing cry of a character who has suffered under Set's power [014-088]

## Relationship to Other Deities

Set exists within a complex theological ecosystem in Howard's world. His primary rival is [[Mitra]], the god of light worshipped in the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kingdoms, representing the eternal opposition between darkness and light, [[magic-in-the-hyborian-age|sorcery]] and civilization, the serpent and the sun. Set-worshippers view [[mitra|Mitra]] worshippers as enemies by nature, and vice versa.

Within [[stygia|Stygia]] itself, Set co-exists with other dark deities including [[Dagon]], but he is unquestionably preeminent. Lesser gods and demons of the [[stygia|Stygian]] pantheon — including [[Hanuman]] the [[hanuman|ape-god]] — serve as subsidiary powers beneath Set's overarching dominion.
