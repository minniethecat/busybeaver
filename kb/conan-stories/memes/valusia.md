## Overview

Valusia was one of the great civilized kingdoms of the [[thurian-age|Thurian Age]], the era that preceded the [[Great Cataclysm]] and the rise of the [[Hyborian Age]]. Alongside its sister kingdoms — Kamelia, Verulia, Grondar, and Commoria — Valusia represented the apex of pre-Cataclysmic civilization, a realm of settled monarchy, organized agriculture, and sophisticated culture that dominated the Thurian continent for millennia. Like all the Thurian kingdoms, Valusia was destroyed or fundamentally transformed by the [[great-cataclysm|Great Cataclysm]], its survivors scattered among the mountains and islands of the reshaped world.

## Valusia in the Thurian Age

Valusia was one of seven major kingdoms that constituted the civilized world of the Thurian era. As described in the [[the-hyborian-age-essay|Hyborian Age essay]], "the civilized kingdoms, though enormous in extent, occupied a comparatively small portion of the planet" [017-003], concentrated in the temperate zones of the Thurian continent while vast stretches of the world remained unexplored and inhabited only by scattered tribes of primitive humans.

The kingdom had a "southern frontier" [017-006] where the [[atlanteans|Atlanteans]] — a hardy race of barbarians — were settled as a buffer against the savage [[picts|Pictish]] tribes. This arrangement, in which Valusia employed barbarian clients to guard its borders against more primitive peoples, would be replicated millennia later by the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kingdoms that succeeded Valusia. The pattern of employing barbarians to guard the marches — later used by [[Aquilonia]] with the [[Bossonian Marches]] — originated in Valusia's strategic use of the [[atlanteans|Atlantean]] settlement.

> "Settled among the mountains of Valusia's southern frontier to serve as a buffer against the Picts" — the Atlanteans, placed by Valusia to guard its borders [017-006]

## Civilizational Characteristics

As one of the major Thurian kingdoms, Valusia possessed the hallmarks of advanced pre-Cataclysmic civilization: organized government under a hereditary monarchy, established cities connected by roads and trade routes, a complex social hierarchy, and a military capable of defending its borders against both barbarian incursions and the ambitions of rival kingdoms. The Valusians, like the other Thurian peoples, were fair-skinned — part of a racial group that dominated the civilized world before [[great-cataclysm|the Cataclysm]] [017-001].

The wars between Valusia and its rival kingdom Commoria were a recurring feature of Thurian politics — "the bickerings of the kingdoms, and the wars between Valusia and Commoria" are noted as characteristic of the era [017-004]. These conflicts followed patterns that would be familiar to any student of [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] history: territorial disputes, dynastic marriages, shifting alliances, and the constant effort to maintain the balance of power among competing civilized states.

## The Great Cataclysm and Aftermath

The [[Great Cataclysm]] that ended the [[thurian-age|Thurian Age]] struck Valusia with devastating force. The geological convulsions that reshaped the Thurian continent — sinking some kingdoms beneath the waves and raising new mountain ranges from the sea floor — destroyed the physical infrastructure of Valusian civilization. Its cities were toppled, its farmland flooded or buried, and its population scattered across the transformed landscape.

The [[atlanteans|Atlanteans]] who had served as Valusia's frontier guards survived [[great-cataclysm|the Cataclysm]] on their island refuge, while the [[picts|Picts]] who had been Valusia's enemies likewise endured. But the Valusian civilization itself — the cities, the monarchy, the literate culture, the organized religion — did not survive the transition to the new age. The survivors regressed to a primitive state, losing the arts of civilization within a few generations. By the time the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] tribes began their southward migration, Valusia was remembered only as a name in the oldest legends [017-009].

> The Valusian survivors were reduced to "toiling as slaves of their ancient masters" in the post-Cataclysmic chaos [017-009]

## Legacy and Historical Significance

Valusia's primary legacy lies in the institutional patterns it established — patterns that the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kingdoms two ages later would unconsciously replicate. The strategic use of barbarian buffer states, the complex diplomacy of rival civilized kingdoms, and the tension between civilization and barbarism that defines the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]] all have their origins in Valusia's history.

Valusia also exemplifies a broader theme in Howard's world-building: the cyclical nature of civilization. The Thurian kingdoms rose, flourished, and fell; the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kingdoms that followed them did the same; and the pattern promises to repeat indefinitely. Valusia stands at the beginning of this cycle, the first known example of the civilized kingdom whose achievements and failures would be echoed by every civilization that followed.

> The wars between Valusia and Commoria prefigure the later rivalries between [[Aquilonia]] and [[Nemedia]] [017-004]
