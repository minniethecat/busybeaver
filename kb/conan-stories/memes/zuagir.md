# Zuagirs

The Zuagirs are a tribe of [[shem|Shem]]itish desert nomads in Robert E. Howard's [[conan|Conan]] stories, dwelling in the eastern deserts beyond the river from [[Khauran]]. They feature most prominently in "A Witch Shall Be Born," where under [[Conan]]'s leadership they become a formidable military force that challenges the power of the settled kingdoms.

## The Eastern Desert Tribes

The Zuagirs are part of the larger division between the western and eastern [[shem|Shem]]itish peoples. As described in "A Witch Shall Be Born":

> The countries of these barbarians are divided into the western meadowlands which stretch to the distant ocean, and in which rise the cities of the town-dwellers, and the eastern deserts, where the lean nomads hold sway; there is incessant warfare between the dwellers of the cities and the dwellers of the desert. [006-153]

The Zuagirs represent the desert-dwelling faction — lean, white-robed horsemen who live by raiding caravans and warring against the cities of the western [[shem|Shem]]ites. Their traditional enmity with the city-dwellers makes them natural allies for [[conan|Conan]] when he seeks revenge against [[constantius|Constantius]], the [[shem|Shem]]itish usurper of [[khauran|Khauran]].

## Conan as Chief of the Zuagirs

After escaping crucifixion outside [[khauran|Khauran]], [[conan|Conan]] finds his way to the Zuagir camps, where he rises from the right-hand man of [[Olgerd Vladislav]] to become the true chief of the tribe. The pivotal confrontation between [[conan|Conan]] and Olgerd reveals the nature of tribal loyalty:

> "I didn't have to," answered Conan. "You lied when you said I had nothing to do with bringing in the new recruits. I had everything to do with it. They took your orders, but they fought for me. There is not room for two chiefs of the Zuagirs. They know I am the stronger man. I understand them better than you, and they, me; because I am a barbarian too." [006-226]

[[conan|Conan]]'s understanding of the Zuagirs runs deeper than the [[kozaki|kozak]] adventurer Olgerd's. [[conan|Conan]] breaks Olgerd's arm in a contest of raw strength, but does not kill him — instead, he sends him eastward into the desert, stripped of his chieftainship. The symbolic power of this moment is captured in the aftermath:

> In Olgerd's eyes grew a recognition of defeat. In his red dreams of empire he had missed what was going on about him. Happenings and events that had seemed meaningless before now flashed into his mind, with their true significance, bringing a realization that Conan spoke no idle boast. The giant black-mailed figure before him was the real chief of the Zuagirs. [006-229]

Under [[conan|Conan]]'s leadership, the Zuagir band increases vastly in numbers and capability. [[conan|Conan]] applies the military knowledge he gained among the Western nations to equip them with siege engines — a crucial advantage that allows them to threaten the walled city of [[khauran|Khauran]]:

> "...the Zuagirs have siege engines, built, undoubtedly, according to the instructions of Conan, who learned all the arts of war among the Western nations." [006-243]

## The Raid on Khauran

The Zuagir assault on [[khauran|Khauran]] is a pivotal military campaign in "A Witch Shall Be Born." Alcemides, a councilor of [[constantius|Constantius]], reports on the threat:

> "The Zuagirs have fought with and raided Khauran for centuries, without success, but they resent its conquest by their western kin. It is rumored that their natural antagonism is being fomented by the man who was formerly the captain of the queen's guard, and who, somehow escaping the hate of Constantius, who actually had him upon the cross, fled to the nomads. He is called Conan, and is himself a barbarian, one of those gloomy Cimmerians whose ferocity our soldiers have more than once learned to their bitter cost." [006-197]

The strategic calculus is carefully weighed — [[constantius|Constantius]] must march out to meet the Zuagirs in the open plain because the city lacks supplies for a siege, and the Zuagirs under [[conan|Conan]]'s tutelage now possess siege engines. The raid succeeds, [[constantius|Constantius]] is overthrown, and the true [[taramis|Queen Taramis]] is restored. In the aftermath, [[conan|Conan]] declines to remain in [[khauran|Khauran]]:

> "No, lass, that's over with. I'm chief of the Zuagirs now, and must lead them to plunder the Turanians, as I promised." [006-331]

## Shadows in Zamboula

The Zuagirs appear in a different role in "Shadows in [[zamboula|Zamboula]]," where a Zuagir tribesman encounters [[conan|Conan]] in the [[zamboula|city of Zamboula]] and recognizes him as a former brother:

> "Be warned! You have dwelt for many moons in the tents of the Zuagirs, and you are our brother! Go not to the house of Aram Baksh!" [009-047]

This encounter reveals the enduring bond between [[conan|Conan]] and the desert nomads. The Zuagir warns [[conan|Conan]] about the dangers of [[aram-baksh|Aram Baksh]]'s tavern — where travelers vanish in the night and human bones, "charred and blackened" [009-040], are found in a desert pit outside the city. The tribesman's loyalty to his former chief transcends the passage of time: "you have dwelt for many moons in the tents of the Zuagirs, and you are our brother" [009-047].

## Relationship with Olgerd Vladislav

The Zuagir chieftainship passes from Olgerd Vladislav to [[conan|Conan]] in a decisive confrontation. Olgerd, the [[kozaki|kozak]] who "wandered down from the northern steppes and made himself chief of a band of Zuagirs" [006-154], had dreams of empire but had "missed what was going on about him" [006-229]. When [[conan|Conan]] reveals that the warriors now fight for him rather than Olgerd, the former chief accepts defeat and rides away into the eastern desert:

> Slowly, without a word, he turned and stalked across the tent... unspeaking, with his broken arm thrust in the bosom of his khalat, he reined the steed about and rode eastward into the open desert, out of the life of the people of the Zuagir. [006-236]

## Military Character

The Zuagirs are described as lean, hawk-faced warriors — "lean, white-robed hawks, Zuagir tribesmen without a doubt, nomads from beyond the river" [006-120]. Their traditional fighting style relies on mobility and raiding rather than siege warfare, but under [[conan|Conan]]'s tutelage they adapt to a more conventional military approach, enabling the conquest of [[khauran|Khauran]].

Their encampment is vividly described: "lines of camelhair tents that stretched before him, and the white-robed figures that moved among them, arguing, singing, mending bridles or whetting tulwars" [006-238] — a scene of nomadic life on the cusp of war.

## Appearances Across Stories

- **"A Witch Shall Be Born"** (primary appearance) — [[conan|Conan]] becomes chief of the Zuagirs, leads them against [[khauran|Khauran]] to overthrow [[constantius|Constantius]], and defeats Olgerd Vladislav's authority [006-120 through 006-331]
- **"Shadows in [[zamboula|Zamboula]]"** — A Zuagir tribesman warns [[conan|Conan]] about the dangers of the house of [[aram-baksh|Aram Baksh]], and [[conan|Conan]] is recognized as "our brother" who "dwelt for many moons in the tents of the Zuagirs" [009-009 through 009-047]
- **"[[red-nails-story|Red Nails]]"** — [[conan|Conan]] mentions his past: "I was a chief of the Zuagirs once—desert men who live by plundering the caravans" [011-009]
