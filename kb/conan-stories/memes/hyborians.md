## Overview

The Hyborians are the dominant race of the Hyborian Age, a tawny-haired, warlike people whose tribes migrated southward from the northern steppes and built the great kingdoms of the western world. Their rise from nomadic barbarians to builders of civilization is one of the central narratives of the pre-cataclysmic era, and the Conan stories are set during the peak and early decline of their power.

> "The tale of the next thousand years is the tale of the rise of the Hyborians, whose warlike tribes dominate the western world. Rude kingdoms are taking shape. The tawny-haired invaders have encountered the Picts, driving them into the barren lands of the west." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Origins and Early History

The Hyborians began as northern barbarians, kin to the Hyperboreans, whose first kingdom — Hyperborea — arose when one tribe discovered the use of stone in building and abandoned nomadic life for settled fortresses.

> "Another factor has added to the impetus of Hyborian drift. A tribe of that race has discovered the use of stone in building, and the first Hyborian kingdom has come into being—the rude and barbaric kingdom of Hyperborea, which had its beginning in a crude fortress of boulders heaped to repel tribal attack." [[1]](#ref-1)

As the Hyperborean kingdom grew strong, other Hyborian tribes migrated southward, driven by population pressure and the harsh northern climate. Over centuries they displaced the older races and established the Hyborian kingdoms.

## The Hyborian Kingdoms

At their zenith, the Hyborian kingdoms covered the western world, extending from the frozen north to the borders of Stygia in the south. Their major states included:

- **[[aquilonians|Aquilonia]]** — the greatest and most powerful Hyborian kingdom
- **[[nemedians|Nemedia]]** — a rival to Aquilonia, known for its scholarship and intrigue
- **[[brythunians|Brythunia]]** — a northern Hyborian kingdom
- **[[bossonians|Bossonia]]** — the western march, known for its archers
- **Koth** — a southern kingdom on the borders of Shem
- **Ophir** — a wealthy kingdom south of Aquilonia
- **Argos** — a maritime kingdom on the western sea
- **Corinthia** — a mountainous region of independent city-states
- **The Border Kingdom** — the northern frontier between civilization and barbarism

> "The kingdoms of the Hyborians—Aquilonia, Nemedia, Brythunia, Hyperborea, Koth, Ophir, Argos, Corinthia, and one known as the Border Kingdom—dominate the western world." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Characteristics

The Hyborians are described as tawny-haired, with fair complexions and powerful physiques. They are characterized by their restless energy, martial prowess, and drive to expand and build. Unlike the older, decadent races they displaced, the Hyborians possess a vigorous, pragmatic culture in its prime.

Conan himself, though a [[cimmerians|Cimmerian]] — descended from the older Atlantean stock — often remarks on the softness of civilized Hyborians compared to barbarians. Yet even he acknowledges their strength. The Hyborian kingdoms produce heavily armored cavalry, disciplined infantry, and formidable fortifications.

> "Mitra of the Hyborians must be a strong god, because his people have builded their cities over the world." [[2]](#ref-2)

## Worship and Religion

The principal deity of the Hyborians is [[mitra|Mitra]], a god of light and justice whose worship is centered in Aquilonia and spread throughout the western kingdoms. In the eastern reaches of Hyborian influence, [[ishtar|Ishtar]] is worshiped, while in Koth the Shemitish gods hold sway.

> "These eastern Hyborians worship [Ishtar] (and which, inferior as it is to the true religion of Mitra which we Western nations recognize, is still superior to the devil-worship of the Zuagirs)." [[3]](#ref-3)

## Fall of the Hyborian Civilization

The Hyborian Age ended not through internal decay but through external pressure. The growing power of the barbarian nations — the [[picts|Picts]] in the west, the [[hyrkanians|Hyrkanians]] from the east, and the Nordheimr from the north — overwhelmed the Hyborian kingdoms.

> "Five hundred years later the Hyborian civilization was swept away. Its fall was unique in that it was not brought about by internal decay, but by the growing power of the barbarian nations and the Hyrkanians. The Hyborian peoples were overthrown while their vigorous culture was in its prime." [[1]](#ref-1)

The Picts, strengthened by contact with frontier cultures like the [[bossonians|Bossonians]], pushed eastward. The Hyrkanians, migrating from the steppes around the [[vilayet-sea|Vilayet Sea]], swept westward. Together they shattered the Hyborian kingdoms, bringing the age to a violent close.

## Significance

The Hyborians represent Howard's conception of a vigorous, expansionist civilization at its height — strong, pragmatic, and unsoftened by decadence. Their fall, coming from without rather than within, subverts the classical trope of civilizational decline through internal corruption. In the Conan stories, Hyborian civilization serves as both backdrop and foil, with its kings, knights, and intriguers contrasted against the raw vitality of the barbarian hero.
