## Overview

The Book of Skelos is a legendary tome of forbidden knowledge in the Hyborian Age, associated with the darkest arts of sorcery. Its name is invoked as both a source of power and a curse, and it is sought after by those who would wield the forces of darkness — from Stygian priests to the royalty of [[vendhya|Vendhya]].

> "I have read the Book of Skelos, and talked with nameless hermits in the caves below Jhelai." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Content and Nature

The exact contents of the Book of Skelos are never fully described, but it is clearly a work of profound magical significance. The Devi [[Yasmina]] of Vendhya consulted it in her quest to understand and destroy the [[black-seers|Black Seers of Mount Yimsha]], who had murdered her brother the king through sorcery.

The book's knowledge is not without cost — those who delve into its secrets court madness, death, or worse. It is associated with Stygian sorcery, the most ancient and terrible magical tradition of the Hyborian Age.

## Notable Readers

Queen Yasmina of Vendhya read the Book of Skelos as part of her extensive research into the occult, alongside consultations with hermits and mystics in the remote reaches of her kingdom. Her study of the book enabled her to learn the identity and methods of her brother's murderers, though the knowledge came at great personal risk.

> "I learned how, and by whom, he was destroyed. His enemies were the Black Seers of Mount Yimsha." [[1]](#ref-1)

## The Name Skelos in Sorcerous Invocations

The name "Skelos" appears not only in reference to the book but also as a dark invocation. The sorcerer [[Khemsa]], dying from wounds sustained in magical combat, cried out to Skelos with his final breath:

> "I am going to Gitara — she is waiting for me in hell — aie, ya Skelos yar!" [[2]](#ref-2)

This suggests that Skelos is more than an author — the name may refer to a demonic entity, a god of sorcery, or the embodiment of magical power itself. The girdle Khemsa wore, obtained from a Stygian priest, was similarly tied to the power of Skelos, though it ultimately failed to save him.

> "Wear the girdle. I had it from a Stygian priest. It will aid you, though it failed me at last." [[2]](#ref-2)

## Legacy

The Book of Skelos stands alongside other legendary magical works of the Hyborian Age as a symbol of forbidden knowledge — power that can destroy as readily as it empowers. Its connection to [[stygia|Stygia]], the oldest and most sorcery-soaked civilization of the age, places it at the apex of Hyborian occult literature.
