# Khauran

Khauran is a small Hyborian kingdom that serves as the primary setting of Robert E. Howard's Conan story "A Witch Shall Be Born" (published in *Weird Tales*, December 1934). It is ruled by Queen [[Taramis]] and located in the Kothian region of the Hyborian world, a satellite state caught between the larger powers of [[Koth]] and the desert lands to the east.

## Geography

Khauran is a compact kingdom situated in the transitional zone between the Hyborian kingdoms and the eastern desert regions. Its capital city—also called Khauran—is a walled settlement that serves as the seat of the monarchy. The kingdom's modest size belies its strategic importance, as it controls routes between the more powerful Hyborian states and the eastern trade networks. As described in a letter to the Nemedian scholar Alcemides:

> Khauran is the most southeasterly of these principalities, bordering on the very deserts of eastern Shem. The city of Khauran is the only city of any magnitude in the realm, and stands within sight of the river which separates the grasslands from the sandy desert, like a watchtower to guard the fertile meadows behind it. The land is so rich that it yields three and four crops a year, and the plains north and west of the city are dotted with villages. [006-150]

The landscape is fertile enough to support a settled population but vulnerable to desert raiders from the east. The city itself is a typical Hyborian urban center with a palace, a temple district, and a marketplace that draws merchants from across the region.

## Inhabitants

Khauran is populated by Hyborian-descended peoples who share cultural and linguistic affinities with the broader Kothian region. The population is a mix of urban dwellers in the capital, farmers in the surrounding countryside, and a small but influential noble class. The social structure is described in detail:

> The people of the city are Khauran's ruling caste, predominantly Hyborian, and valorous and warlike. [006-152]

The ruling dynasty is represented by Queen Taramis, who inherited the throne and governs with a reputation for justice and benevolence. Her twin sister [[Salome]], born with a mark that was interpreted as a sign of witchcraft, was cast out at birth—a decision that would later bring catastrophe upon the kingdom. The curse of the Askhaurian dynasty is described by Salome herself:

> "The curse of the kings of Khauran! Aye, they tell the tale in the marketplaces, with wagging beards and rolling eyes, the pious fools! They tell how the first queen of our line had traffic with a fiend of darkness and bore him a daughter who lives in foul legendry to this day. And thereafter in each century a girl baby was born into the Askhaurian dynasty, with a scarlet half-moon between her breasts, that signified her destiny." [006-026]

The military of Khauran includes a guard force under the command of officers like [[Valerius]], captain of the queen's guard, and a citizen militia that can be raised in times of crisis.

## History

Khauran's known history in Howard's writings centers entirely on the events of "A Witch Shall Be Born." The kingdom was ruled peacefully by Queen Taramis until the arrival of [[Constantius]], a usurper backed by the witch Salome. Acting as an agent of her sister's vengeance, Salome orchestrated a coup: she used her sorcery to assume Taramis's appearance while Constantius and his Shemitish mercenaries seized military control of the capital. Salome reveals her scheme with chilling frankness:

> "Enough of this farce; let us on to the next act in the comedy. Listen, dear sister: it was I who sent Constantius here. When I decided to take the throne of Khauran, I cast about for a man to aid me, and chose the Falcon, because of his utter lack of all characteristics men call good." [006-061]

During Constantius's brief reign of terror, Khauran suffered under a regime of extortion, arbitrary executions, and the systematic dismantling of the kingdom's institutions. The citizens were taxed into poverty, the queen's loyal supporters were purged, and the guard was replaced with foreign mercenaries. The scope of the oppression is documented:

> Their wealth is stripped from them, their fairest daughters taken to glut the insatiable lust of Constantius and his mercenaries. These men are utterly without mercy or compassion, possessed of all the characteristics our armies learned to abhor in our wars against the Shemitish allies of Argos—inhuman cruelty, lust, and wild-beast ferocity. [006-152]

Conan, serving as captain of the queen's guard at the time of the coup, was captured and crucified outside the city walls—a fate from which he miraculously survived. The Khaurani loyalists who fled the kingdom after the coup became the nucleus of Conan's liberation force:

> "Three thousand men of Khauran camp at the oasis of Akrel awaiting my orders." [006-213]

> "Aye. Men who had fled from the tyranny of Constantius. Most of them have been living the lives of outlaws in the deserts east of Khauran, and are gaunt and hard and desperate as man-eating tigers." [006-215]

The liberation saw the death of Constantius at Conan's hands and the exposure of Salome's deception. Taramis was restored to her throne, and the kingdom began the slow process of rebuilding.

## Appearances

Khauran appears exclusively in "A Witch Shall Be Born," which comprises the entirety of the kingdom's narrative role in the Conan canon. It is mentioned or referenced in passages throughout the story, serving as both the geographical and political center of the plot's conflict.
