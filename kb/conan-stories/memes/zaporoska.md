# Zaporoska

The Zaporoska (or Zaporoskan) is a river in the eastern steppes of the Hyborian Age, in Robert E. Howard's Conan stories. It is the homeland and base of operations for the [[Kozaki|kozaki]], the "masterless devils" — a brotherhood of Free Companions who live by raiding and mercenary service under their elected hetmans.

## Geography

The Zaporoska River flows through the eastern steppes, with its lower reaches emptying into the [[Vilayet Sea]] or its vicinity. The terrain around the river's lower course is described as a challenging wilderness:

> ...a wilderness of reeds, a swampy jungle in which our last expedition was cut to pieces by those masterless devils. [004-027]

The kozak war camp is typically pitched on the lower reaches of the Zaporoska, where the reeds and swamp provide natural defense against expeditions sent from the settled kingdoms. This geography makes the kozaki virtually unassailable in their home territory.

## The Kozaki of the Zaporoska

The Zaporoska is synonymous with the kozaki — the Free Companions of the eastern steppes. These "masterless devils" [004-027] are a brotherhood of warriors, outlaws, and adventurers who elect their own leaders (hetmans) and live outside the authority of any kingdom or empire.

Conan himself was once a hetman among the kozaki on the Zaporoska, a fact that carries significant weight in the world of Hyborian Age mercenaries. He boasts of this to the Devi Yasmina:

> "I was a hetman among the Free Companions before I rode southward. I'll make you a queen on the Zaporoska River!" [005-573]

The role of Zaporoskan hetman carries considerable prestige in the Hyborian Age mercenary world. [[Olgerd Vladislav]], a Zuagir desert chief in "A Witch Shall Be Born," was formerly a hetman on the Zaporoska, and his "jeweled goblet" and "crimson wine" suggest that Zaporoskan hetmans could accumulate substantial wealth through raiding [006-192]. Olgerd reflects on his past status with nostalgia:

> "Once I was a hetman on the Zaporoska; now I'm a desert chief." [006-196]

## In the Stories

The Zaporoska appears in multiple Conan stories as a reference point for Conan's past and the world of the eastern steppes:

- **"The Devil in Iron"** — The kozak war camp is pitched on the lower Zaporoska, and Jehungir Agha schemes to lure Conan away from the camp to the island of [[Xapur]]. After his encounter with Khosatral Khel, Conan flees across the Vilayet Sea back to the Zaporoska camp [004-027, 004-070].
- **"The People of the Black Circle"** — Conan, in conversation with the Devi Yasmina, offers to make her "a queen on the Zaporoska River," invoking his past status as a kozak hetman [005-573].
- **"A Witch Shall Be Born"** — Olgerd Vladislav, the Zuagir desert chief, identifies Conan as "once a hetman of the kozaki of the Zaporoskan River" [006-128] and reflects on his own past as a Zaporoskan hetman [006-196].
