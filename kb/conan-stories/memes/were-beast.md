## Overview

The **were-beast** is a shape-shifting abomination encountered by [[conan|Conan]] during his adventures on the [[black-coast|Black Coast]]. Alongside the [[winged-one|winged ones]], these creatures formed part of a pack of unnatural horrors produced through sorcery "blacker than the Well of Skelos" [003-161]. The were-beast is described as a "great hairy shape" with blazing eyes, possessing both the physical power of a beast and a hideously human-like quality in its cries of pain.

## Nature and Origin

Like the [[winged-one|winged ones]], the were-beasts are not natural creatures. [[conan|Conan]] recognized that "it was not merely in their unnatural size that he sensed a blasphemous difference" [003-161] — these beings possessed an aura of diabolism, the product of "godless alchemy" that perverted the natural order. Their existence represents one of the darkest applications of sorcery in the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] world: the creation of hybrid monsters that combine human cunning with bestial fury.

## Encounter on the Black Coast

The were-beast attacked atop a pyramid, part of a pack that had already been reduced in number by the spears of Conan's pirate allies. The creatures assaulted with "blazing eyes and dripping fangs" in "a nightmare rush" [003-163]. The were-beast specifically "soared up at his throat" [003-162], a leaping attack that demonstrated the creature's extraordinary agility and ferocity.

[[conan|Conan]] killed the were-beast with an arrow described in striking imagery: "The arrow was a flying beam of moonlight that flashed onward with but a blur in its course, but the were-beast plunged convulsively in midair and crashed headlong, shot through and through" [003-162]. The arrow, likened to moonlight, suggests a possible vulnerability of such creatures to silver or moon-blessed weapons — a theme consistent with lycanthropic lore across many cultures.

## Human-Like Qualities

One of the most disturbing aspects of the were-beast is its quasi-human nature. When Conan broke the foreleg of a related creature in the same battle, it emitted "a short yelp, the only cry in that grim battle, and hideously humanlike" [003-161]. This human quality — the cry of pain that sounds like a man's voice coming from a beast's body — underscores the blasphemous nature of the creatures: they exist in the uncanny valley between human and animal, a perversion of both.

## Combat and Destruction

The battle with the were-beasts was a desperate close-quarters fight. [[conan|Conan]] exhausted his arrows, then fought with sword and bare hands: "He crushed a narrow skull with the pommel of his hilt, feeling the bone splinter and blood and brains gush over his hand" [003-163]. Only his mail saved him from being "ripped to ribbons in an instant" [003-163]. The fight demonstrated both the creatures' lethal danger and the [[cimmerians|Cimmerian]]'s unmatched combat prowess.
