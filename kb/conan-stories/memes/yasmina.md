## Overview

Yasmina Devi is the reigning queen of Vendhya, one of the golden kingdoms south of the Himelian Mountains. When her brother, King Bunda Chand, is murdered through black sorcery, she embarks on a relentless campaign of revenge against the Black Seers of Yimsha — the dark wizards responsible for his death. Her quest brings her to Peshkhauri, the northern frontier fortress, where she becomes entangled with [[Conan]], the Cimmerian chieftain of the Afghuli hill tribes. Kidnapped by Conan as a bargaining chip, she gradually transforms from captive to ally as the two face the shared threat of the Seers.

> "The rulers of the golden kingdoms south of the Himelians were considered little short of divine; and she was the Devi of Vendhya!" [005-148]

## Devi of Vendhya

Yasmina's identity is inseparable from her royal station. As the Devi of Vendhya, she occupies a position of near-divine authority in her homeland. The narrative emphasizes the profound gap between her regal existence and the violent frontier world she enters — "she, Yasmina, to be carried on the saddlebow of a hill chief, like a common wench of the marketplace" [005-148]. This clash of worlds defines her early experience: a sovereign accustomed to absolute deference confronting a reality where her title carries no weight.

Her brother's death is the inciting event of her story. Bunda Chand is murdered through sorcery, his soul attacked by wizards who "drew my soul through the windblown darkness" [005-028]. In a moment that defines her character, Yasmina must perform an act of terrible mercy — "Sobbing wildly, Yasmina plucked a jeweled dagger from her girdle and plunged it to the hilt in his breast" [005-034] — ending his suffering and sealing her vow of vengeance.

> "The king of Vendhya was destroyed by magic. I have devoted my life to the destruction of his murderers." [005-052]

## Capture by Conan

Yasmina's abduction by Conan is a violent rupture from her royal life into the brutal world of the Himelian hill tribes. The Cimmerian, acting as war chief of the Afghulis, seizes her as a hostage against seven tribesmen imprisoned by the Vendhyan governor. Her initial response is one of "furious rage and shame" [005-148] at being manhandled — yet she is pragmatic enough to recognize the reality of her position.

Their journey through the mountains forces an uneasy familiarity. "Yasmina was trying to sit erect on the rocking saddlebow, clutching her captor. She was conscious of a recognition of shame that she should not find unpleasant the feel of his muscular flesh under her fingers" [005-264]. The narrative charts her gradual shift from haughty captive to reluctant dependent, and eventually to a woman who sees the strategic value in her captor's strength.

Disguised in hill-girl clothing at Conan's insistence, she undergoes a symbolic transformation. Conan himself remarks on the change: "In those smoky, mystic robes you were aloof and cold and far off as a star! Now you are a woman of warm flesh and blood!" [005-288].

## Alliance Against the Black Seers

The turning point of Yasmina's story comes with the intervention of the Black Seers themselves, who tear her from Conan's protection in a vortex of sorcery — "she felt herself enveloped in that crimson whirl and torn from her protector with appalling force" [005-396]. Imprisoned in the castle of Yimsha, she confronts the Master of the Seers, who torments her by forcing her to relive the sufferings of countless women throughout history — "She knew the agonies of childbirth, and the bitterness of love betrayed" [005-438] — yet she endures, her consciousness as Yasmina remaining unbroken.

When Conan fights his way into the Seers' fortress to rescue her, Yasmina rushes to him with desperate relief: "He wheeled back into the chamber just in time to catch the Devi of Vendhya in his arms as she rushed across the room and threw herself upon him, catching him about the neck with a frantic clasp, half hysterical with terror and gratitude and relief" [005-534]. Their escape from the collapsing sorcerous stronghold cements their bond, forged in shared peril.

In the final battle against the Turanian forces in the valley, Yasmina holds the power to abandon Conan and his hill-men to their fate — but she keeps her word. Her decision to stand with him marks her moral culmination: a ruler who chooses honor over political expediency.

## Character Traits

Yasmina is defined by a fierce and calculating intelligence. She is "a truly remarkable Devi" [005-068] who does not wait for others to act on her behalf. She studies the Book of Skelos, consults hermits, and personally travels to the dangerous frontier to pursue her vengeance — actions demonstrating both intellectual curiosity and physical courage unusual for a monarch of her era.

Her pride is immense and central to her character. Even under the Black Seers' psychic assault, her royal identity persists: "like the flick of a fiery whip throughout was the consciousness she retained of her Devi-ship" [005-438]. This pride, while initially a barrier between her and Conan, ultimately proves to be the source of her resilience.

Unlike many of Conan's female companions, Yasmina is a political actor in her own right. She has her own agenda — the destruction of the Black Seers — and Conan is, at least initially, a tool she intends to use: "She had plotted to hurl against the masters of Yimsha the man in whose arms she was now carried" [005-309]. This agency distinguishes her from characters who exist primarily in relation to Conan.
