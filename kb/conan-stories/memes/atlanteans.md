# Atlanteans

## Overview

The Atlanteans were one of the barbarian races of the [[pre-cataclysmic-age|Pre-Cataclysmic Age]], dwelling on a small continent between the [[picts|Pictish]] Islands and the [[thurian-age|Thurian Continent]] (017-002). When the [[great-cataclysm|Great Cataclysm]] destroyed Atlantis and Lemuria, a Continental kingdom of the Atlanteans on the Thurian mainland survived, and to it came thousands of refugees in ships from the sinking land (017-006). From these survivors, the [[cimmerians|Cimmerians]] — and thus [[conan|Conan]] himself — would eventually descend.

## The Pre-Cataclysmic Age

Before [[great-cataclysm|the Cataclysm]], the Atlanteans were among the barbarian peoples whose warriors served as mercenaries, generals, statesmen, and even kings in the crumbling [[thurian-age|Thurian civilization]]s (017-004). This period saw Atlanteans founding a kingdom on the mainland through conquest, though more legends than accurate history remain of these events (017-004). The Atlanteans were evidently capable seafarers, able to traverse the ocean between their continent and the Thurian mainland.

## After the Cataclysm

Following [[great-cataclysm|the Cataclysm]], the Atlanteans on the mainland faced a desperate struggle for survival. "Forced to battle continually for their lives" against swarming beasts and ape-men from sunken areas, "they yet managed to retain vestiges of their former state of highly advanced barbarism" (017-007). Robbed of metals and ores, they became workers in stone like their distant ancestors, and "had attained a real artistic level" before their struggling culture encountered the powerful [[picts|Pictish]] nation (017-007).

Unlike the [[picts|Picts]], the Atlanteans possessed an artistic nature. They "left pictures painted or carved on ivory" — artifacts testifying to a sensibility that their more practical rivals wholly lacked (017-007). But this artistic refinement proved no advantage in war.

## The Pictish-Atlantean Wars

The stone-age kingdoms clashed, and in "a series of bloody wars, the outnumbered Atlanteans were hurled back into a state of savagery, and the evolution of the [[picts|Picts]] was halted" (017-008). The [[picts|Picts]] had the advantages of "numbers and unity, whereas the Atlanteans had fallen into loosely knit clans" (017-008). Five hundred years after [[great-cataclysm|the Cataclysm]], the barbaric kingdoms had vanished, replaced by a nation of [[picts|Pictish]] savages warring against scattered Atlantean tribes.

## Descent into Savagery and Rebirth

A thousand years after a second, lesser cataclysm, the descendants of the Atlanteans had become "wandering bands of ape-men, without human speech, or the knowledge of fire or the use of implements" (017-012). They had sunk "back into the squalling chaos of jungle-bestiality from which ages ago their ancestors so laboriously crawled" (017-012).

Yet from this nadir, the Atlanteans began the long climb upward once more. They "completed the cycle of existence; they have long forgotten their former existence as men; unaware of any other former state, they are starting the climb unhelped and unhindered by human memories" (017-018). These reborn Atlanteans eventually became the [[cimmerians|Cimmerians]], described as "ferocious savages, untamed by the invaders, but advancing rapidly because of contact with them" (017-023). Thus [[conan|Conan the Cimmerian]] is the ultimate descendant of the Atlanteans — a lineage spanning the rise from apedom through savagery to barbarism, and in [[conan|Conan]] himself, something approaching heroic stature.
