# Zingara

Zingara is a western maritime kingdom of the Hyborian Age in Robert E. Howard's Conan stories, occupying the southwestern coast of the Hyborian continent. It is known for its seafaring tradition, plantation economy, and rivalry with the neighboring kingdom of [[Argos]].

## Geography

Zingara occupies the southwestern coastline of the Hyborian kingdoms, with its western border facing the Western Ocean and its southern territories bordering [[Pictish Wilderness]]. The kingdom's coastal position has shaped its identity as a naval and trading power, with Zingaran ships plying the waters:

> ...which ply between the ports of Zingara and Argos and the southern coasts. [003-022]

The interior of Zingara includes extensive agricultural lands dedicated to plantation farming. The plantation system of Zingara is noted in the Conan stories, with references to large estates worked by laborers whose conditions parallel the agricultural economies of the ancient Mediterranean. The contrast between the rich coastal trading cities and the plantation interior defines Zingara's economic geography.

Zingara's proximity to the Pictish Wilderness makes its southern frontier a contested zone, vulnerable to Pictish raids from across the border.

## Inhabitants

The Zingarans are a Hyborian people with strong maritime and trading traditions. Their culture is shaped by their coastal position and plantation economy, producing a society divided between a seafaring merchant class, a landed planter aristocracy, and agricultural laborers.

Zingaran ships are a common sight in the ports of the southern coasts, and Zingaran traders carry goods from the southern lands to the Hyborian markets. In "The Tower of the Elephant," the kothian/corinthian thief who accompanies Conan mentions Zingara among the lands where slavery is practiced on a large scale:

> ...on the plantations of southern Zingara. [006-151]

Zingara's culture places it firmly among the civilized Hyborian kingdoms, though its southern frontier location gives it a rougher edge than the more settled central kingdoms. The Zingarans appear in "Queen of the Black Coast" as naval opponents of [[Bêlit]]:

> The Zingarans sank my last ship off the Shemitish coast. [010-038]

## History

Zingara is one of the original Hyborian kingdoms that emerged during the Hyborian Age. Its history is dominated by maritime competition with Argos and the constant threat from the Pictish frontier. The kingdom's plantation economy made it a destination for slave labor, a practice that was common enough in the Hyborian world to be referenced in multiple stories.

In "The Tower of the Elephant," Zingara is mentioned in connection with the slave trade. The kingdom's maritime power and plantation wealth made it a significant player in Hyborian Age geopolitics, though less frequently featured directly than the great central kingdoms of Aquilonia, Nemedia, and Koth.
