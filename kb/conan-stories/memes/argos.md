# Argos

Argos is a maritime kingdom of the western Hyborian coast in Robert E. Howard's Conan stories, occupying the coastline between [[Zingara]] to the south and [[Aquilonia]] to the northeast. It is known for its bustling ports, merchant fleet, and naval rivalry with Zingara. The "Argossean" master-shipman and seafaring traditions feature in several Conan tales.

## Geography

Argos occupies the western coastal region of the Hyborian continent, with its western border facing the Western Ocean. Its ports serve as hubs for trade routes:

> ...which ply between the ports of Zingara and Argos and the southern coasts, hugging the shores, rarely venturing out of sight of land. [003-022]

The kingdom's coastal geography has shaped its identity as a trading and naval power. Argossean ships carry goods between the Hyborian kingdoms and the southern lands, including [[Kush]] and [[Stygia]]. The interior of Argos includes agricultural regions, but the kingdom's wealth derives primarily from maritime commerce:

> I am a master-shipman of the ports of Argos. I am bound for Kush, to trade beads and silks and sugar and brass-hilted swords to the black kings... [003-013]

## Inhabitants

The Argosseans are a Hyborian people with a strong maritime culture. Their society is organized around the port cities, with a merchant aristocracy and a large class of sailors, shipwrights, and traders. Argossean sailors are a common presence in ports throughout the Hyborian world.

Argossean culture emphasizes commercial skill and nautical expertise over the martial values of the inland kingdoms. Conan himself passed through Argos seeking employment:

> I came into Argos seeking employment, but with a war galley's master who was recruiting for the Stygian trade. [003-015]

## History

Argos is an ancient kingdom, one of the original Hyborian states. Its maritime competition with Zingara is a recurring element in Howard's world-building. The kingdom's history is less dramatically chronicled than that of Aquilonia or Nemedia, but its ports and ships are essential infrastructure in the Hyborian world's economy and military logistics.

In "Queen of the Black Coast," Argos serves as the launching point for Conan's voyage south—a voyage that ends with his encounter with [[Bêlit]], the pirate queen who becomes his companion. The naval traditions of Argos make it a natural point of departure for adventures along the southern coasts.
