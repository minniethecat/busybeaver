# Zamboula

Zamboula is a desert city that serves as the setting of Robert E. Howard's Conan story "Shadows in Zamboula" (published in *Weird Tales*, November 1935). Located on the western frontier of the [[Turanian Empire]], it is a hybrid, multi-ethnic trading center where eastern and western cultures collide under the rule of a Turanian satrap.

## Geography

Zamboula lies amidst a ring of oases in the eastern desert, serving as the westernmost outpost of the Turanian empire. The city's position on the caravan routes makes it a crucial waypoint for trade between the western Hyborian kingdoms and the eastern powers:

> Zamboula was but a small trading-town then, lying amidst a ring of oases, and inhabited by descendants of nomads. [009-023]

The surrounding landscape is harsh desert, with date palms and oases providing the only relief from the arid expanse. The city itself is a walled settlement with minarets and towers rising above the desert sands, its architecture reflecting successive waves of Stygian and Turanian colonization. The Sword-Makers' Bazaar, the temple of [[Hanuman]], and the tavern of [[Aram Baksh]] are among its notable landmarks.

A road runs westward from the city into the open desert, and palm groves fringe the outskirts. The streets are described as a vibrant but dangerous maze, where:

> the many-tongued, many-colored stream of the Zamboula streets, which is exotic, hybrid, flamboyant and clamorous [009-003]

flows past markets, temples, and taverns.

## Inhabitants

Zamboula's population is extraordinarily diverse, the product of centuries of conquest and trade. The city was originally a small trading settlement inhabited by nomads before the Stygians conquered it and built it into a city, settling it with their own people alongside Shemite and [[Kushite]] slaves. Later, the Turanians conquered it, and for a generation before the story's events, Zamboula has been ruled by a Turanian satrap:

> The babel of a myriad tongues smote on the Cimmerian's ears as the restless pattern of the Zamboula streets weaved about him—cleft now and then by a squad of clattering horsemen, the tall, supple warriors of Turan, with dark hawk-faces, clinking metal and curved swords. [009-024]

The ruling elite consists of the Turanian military aristocracy, represented by the satrap [[Jungir Khan]] and his mistress [[Nafertari]]. Beneath them, the Stygian population—"tall, somber Stygians, standing back in the shadows"—remembers its lost dominion. The mixed-race majority—descendants of nomads, slaves, traders, and settlers from across the Hyborian world—forms the commercial and laboring classes.

A darker element of Zamboula's population consists of cannibalistic slaves from [[Darfar]], who prowl the streets at night hunting for human prey. The innkeeper Aram Baksh operates a grisly trade with these slaves, selling unwary travelers to them:

> Cannibalism was not tolerated openly in Zamboula. But Conan knew now why people locked themselves in so securely at night, and why even beggars shunned the open alleys and doorless ruins. [009-052]

## History

Zamboula was originally a small trading settlement of desert nomads before being conquered by [[Stygia]], whose armies carved an empire out of the eastern desert centuries ago. The Stygians built it into a proper city and settled it with their colonists. Later, the Turanians conquered the city and made it their westernmost outpost, ruled by a satrap appointed from [[Aghrapur]].

The events of "Shadows in Zamboula" center on Conan's arrival in the city and his entanglement in the web of intrigue spun by the priest [[Totrasmek]] of Hanuman. Totrasmek, a powerful sorcerer-priest, plots the destruction of Jungir Khan and Nafertari. Conan becomes involved through his encounter with Nafertari—disguised as the dancing-girl Zabibi, a figure well known in Zamboula:

> "I am not Zabibi. I am Nafertari. And he is not Alafdhal, a poor captain of the guardsmen. He is Jungir Khan, satrap of Zamboula." [009-219]

Conan ultimately defeats Totrasmek and kills Aram Baksh, ending the cannibal innkeeper's murderous enterprise.

## Notable Locations

- **The Sword-Makers' Bazaar** — The central marketplace where Conan first enters the narrative
- **The Temple of Hanuman** — Totrasmek's seat of power, a center of dark sorcery
- **Aram Baksh's Tavern** — An inn on the western edge of the city, notorious for its guests disappearing in the night
- **The Satrap's Palace** — Residence of Jungir Khan and Nafertari

## Appearances

Zamboula appears exclusively in "Shadows in Zamboula," where it serves as the complete setting for the story's events.
