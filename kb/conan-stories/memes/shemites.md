## Overview

The Shemites are the inhabitants of [[shem|Shem]], the pastoral and trading lands that lie between the southern borders of the [[hyborians|Hyborian]] kingdoms and the northern frontier of [[stygia|Stygia]]. A dark-skinned people with Semitic cultural roots, the Shemites are renowned as traders, archers, and fervent worshippers of a rich pantheon of gods.

> "Above all are the gods of the Shemites—Ishtar and Ashtoreth and Derketo and Adonis. Bel, too, is Shemitish, for he was born in ancient Shumir, long, long ago." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Physical Characteristics and Culture

Shemites are described as dark-skinned, often bearded, of medium height and muscular build. They are a passionate people with a love of wealth and material splendor, prone to fierce rages and deep loyalties. Shemitish mercenaries serve in armies across the Hyborian world, and Shemitish traders ply the caravan routes from the Western Sea to the borders of Turan.

> "The Shemite soul finds a bright drunkenness in riches and material splendor, and the sight of this treasure might have shaken the soul of a sated emperor of Shushan." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Religion

The Shemitish pantheon is one of the most diverse of the Hyborian Age, encompassing a wide range of deities:

- **[[ishtar|Ishtar]]** — the great goddess of love and war
- **[[bel|Bel]]** — the ancient god of thieves, born in Shumir
- **[[derketo|Derketo]]** — the Shemitish goddess
- **Ashtoreth** — another aspect of the fertility/war goddess
- **Adonis** — a dying-and-rising god

Some of these gods are also worshipped by the Hyborians in adapted forms. Mitra worship competes with and sometimes supplants the Shemitish gods in the western kingdoms.

## Historical Role

The Shemites were once under the yoke of Stygia, but were freed when Koth expanded southward, exchanging one master for another — though the Kothic yoke was less harsh. Later, the western Shemitish cities fell under [[aquilonians|Aquilonian]] dominion, while the eastern Shemitish lands bowed to [[turanian-empire|Turan]].

> "Far to the south sleeps Stygia, untouched by foreign invasion, but the peoples of Shem have exchanged the Stygian yoke for the less galling one of Koth." [[2]](#ref-2)

Shemitish mercenaries figure prominently in the armies of the age. The cruel Shemite soldiers of Constantius in Khauran, the black-bearded desert raiders, and the Shemite pirates of the southern seas all appear in the Conan tales. Shemitish bows — powerful recurved weapons — are prized across the Hyborian world.

## Economic Life

The Shemites are pastoralists and traders, their land serving as a crossroads between the Hyborian kingdoms to the north, Stygia to the south, and the eastern empires. Shemitish caravans carry goods across the deserts, and Shemitish merchants are found in every major port.

Shemitish slippers with their distinctive upturned toes, silken pantaloons, and curved swords are markers of Shemitish fashion and craftsmanship, referenced throughout the Conan stories.

## In the Stories

The most prominent Shemite character in the Conan tales is [[belit|Bêlit]], the Queen of the Black Coast — a Shemite woman who led black corsairs in piratical raids across the southern seas. Bêlit exemplifies the passionate, fierce Shemitish spirit: "the wildest she-devil unhanged."
