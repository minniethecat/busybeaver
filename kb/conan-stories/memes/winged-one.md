## Overview

The **winged ones** are flying abominations that [[conan|Conan]] encountered during his time on the [[black-coast|Black Coast]]. Described as creatures "not beasts" but products of "godless alchemy blacker than the Well of Skelos" [003-161], they attack with blazing eyes and dripping fangs, possessing unnatural size and an aura of diabolism that sets them apart from any natural predator. The term is also associated with the "black-winged, sharp-beaked Children of Jhil" [008-385], a related or identical species of airborne monstrosity.

## Nature and Origin

The winged ones are not natural creatures. [[conan|Conan]] sensed "a blasphemous difference" in them beyond their mere size — "they exuded an aura tangible as the black mist rising from a corpse-littered swamp" [003-161]. Their origin is explicitly tied to sorcery: "By what godless alchemy these beings had been brought into existence, he could not guess; but he knew he faced diabolism blacker than the Well of Skelos" [003-161].

This unnatural origin places the winged ones within the broader category of sorcerously created monsters that populate the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] world — creatures bred or summoned through forbidden knowledge, akin to the horrors associated with the [[book-of-skelos|Book of Skelos]] and other repositories of dark sorcery.

## Encounter on the Black Coast

The winged one attacked [[conan|Conan]] atop a pyramid structure during the events of *Queen of the Black Coast*. It came in a "headlong rush" that "had not wavered" [003-172], forcing the [[cimmerians|Cimmerian]] into a desperate aerial battle. The creature attacked repeatedly — "the winged one came on again, and Conan sprang to meet it, his veins on fire with madness" [003-175]. Despite its unnatural nature, Conan ultimately prevailed through sheer barbarian fury and combat prowess.

## Children of Jhil

A related reference appears in the story of *The Vale of Lost Women*, where a threat is made that one's body will "be eaten by the black-winged, sharp-beaked Children of Jhil" [008-385]. Whether the Children of Jhil are identical to the winged ones encountered on the Black Coast, or represent a distinct but related species of airborne predator, is not entirely clear. Both share the characteristics of black wings, sharp beaks, and a connection to dark, primordial forces.
