## Overview

Valeria of the Red Brotherhood is an Aquilonian pirate and one of the most formidable female characters in the Conan stories. A member of the [[Red Brotherhood]] — the loose confederation of buccaneers that operates along the southern coasts — Valeria has earned her place among hardened killers through sheer competence with a blade. Her defining adventure occurs in *Red Nails*, where she flees into the unexplored southern jungles after killing a fellow pirate who attempted to assault her, and there encounters [[Conan]] and the dying city of Xuchotl.

> "This woman is Valeria of the Red Brotherhood, an Aquilonian" — Conan's introduction of Valeria to the people of Xuchotl [012-028]

## Life as a Pirate

Valeria's career with the Red Brotherhood established her reputation as a fighter who demanded respect through skill rather than intimidation. Unlike [[Bêlit]], who commanded through force of personality and a near-supernatural aura, Valeria operated as a professional among professionals — respected rather than feared, followed rather than worshipped.

Her status as a woman in the exclusively male world of Hyborian piracy was sustained solely by her willingness to fight. "Again she was Valeria of the Red Brotherhood, and the wild frenzy of battle had her in its burning grip" [011-118] — the narrator makes clear that Valeria's identity as a pirate was not merely a role she played but a fundamental aspect of her character.

> "Again she was Valeria of the Red Brotherhood, and the wild frenzy of battle had her in its burning grip" [011-118]

## Character and Skills

Valeria is defined by her fierce independence and refusal to submit to any authority — male, institutional, or supernatural. When a fellow pirate attempts to violate her, she kills him without hesitation, accepting the consequences (exile into the deadly southern jungles) rather than compromise her autonomy [010-005]. This uncompromising self-possession is what draws [[Conan]] to her; he recognizes in Valeria a kindred spirit, a barbarian soul in a civilized body.

As a fighter, Valeria is quick, decisive, and lethal. Her combat style emphasizes speed and precision over brute strength — necessary adaptations for a woman fighting in a world of physically larger opponents. She wields a dagger with particular expertise, demonstrating her skill in the claustrophobic corridors of Xuchotl [016-059].

## Valeria and Conan

The relationship between Valeria and Conan is one of equals. Unlike Conan's relationships with [[Bêlit]] — which was intense, romantic, and ended in tragedy — or with [[Yasmina]] — which was entangled in politics and sorcery — his bond with Valeria is based on mutual respect between two professional adventurers who happen to be extraordinarily competent.

Their meeting in the jungle is casual rather than dramatic: Conan recognizes her as a fellow predator in a world of prey. He makes no attempt to dominate her, and she makes no attempt to use him — a dynamic unique among Conan's female companions. When danger comes, they fight side by side, each trusting the other's competence without needing to articulate it.

## In Xuchotl

Valeria's defining moments occur in the ruined city of Xuchotl, where she and Conan become entangled in the bizarre, centuries-long feud between the city's two surviving clans. In Xuchotl, Valeria demonstrates the full range of her capabilities: she fights, negotiates, investigates, and ultimately survives in an environment that has consumed everyone else who entered it.

Her encounter with the Black Lotus in Xuchotl tests her will — "The black lotus!" said Valeria between clenched teeth [013-011] — but she resists its influence, demonstrating the same iron self-control that defines Conan himself.

> "The black lotus!" said Valeria between clenched teeth — instant recognition of the deadly drug and determination to resist it [013-011]
