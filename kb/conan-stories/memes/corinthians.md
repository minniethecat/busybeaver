## Overview

The Corinthians are the [[hyborians|Hyborian]] inhabitants of Corinthia, a mountainous region of the western world whose rugged terrain fostered a culture of fiercely independent city-states rather than a unified kingdom. Listed among the nine major Hyborian kingdoms in the Hyborian Age essay, Corinthia is unique in its fragmented political structure.

> "The kingdoms of the Hyborians—Aquilonia, Nemedia, Brythunia, Hyperborea, Koth, Ophir, Argos, Corinthia, and one known as the Border Kingdom—dominate the western world." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Geography and Political Structure

Unlike the centralized monarchies of [[aquilonians|Aquilonia]] or [[nemedians|Nemedia]], Corinthia consists of independent city-states nestled among mountain valleys. This fragmentation made Corinthia vulnerable to the larger powers that surrounded it, yet also allowed individual cities to maintain their autonomy through shifting alliances and mercenary traditions.

During the height of Aquilonia's empire, Corinthia (along with Koth and the eastern Shemitish tribes) was forced to pay tribute. Yet like all the subject peoples, Corinthians maintained their distinct cultural identity.

## Characteristics

Corinthians appear in the Conan stories as typical Hyborians — tawny-haired, fair-skinned, and martial. They are often encountered as mercenaries, adventurers, and wanderers far from their homeland. The Corinthian Ivanos in "[[Iron Shadows in the Moon]]" and the Corinthian dancing-girl Muriela in "[[Jewels of Gwahlur]]" both exemplify the far-flung presence of Corinthians across the Hyborian world.

The [[corinthian-accent|Corinthian accent]] is distinctive enough to be recognized across the Hyborian kingdoms, marking Corinthians even when they claim other origins.

## In the Stories

In "Iron Shadows in the Moon," the Corinthian Ivanos serves as one of Sergius's lieutenants among the pirates, notable for his lean build and his willingness to defy the rat-faced Brythunian Aratus. His loyalty is pragmatic rather than principled — he stands for Conan not out of friendship but out of opposition to Aratus.

In "Jewels of Gwahlur," the Corinthian dancing-girl Muriela is pressed into impersonating the oracle Yelaya, her Corinthian accent nearly betraying the deception at the crucial moment.

## Fate

During the fall of the Hyborian Age, Corinthia was overrun by the [[picts|Pictish]] advance, its cities falling like the rest of the Hyborian world. Later, when the Hyrkanians swept eastward again, the Corinthians — like all Hyborians — were scattered into the diaspora that marked the end of the age.
