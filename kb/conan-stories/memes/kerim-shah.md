## Overview

Kerim Shah is a Turanian operative dispatched by [[Yezdigerd|King Yezdigerd]] to the kingdom of Vendhya, posing as an Iranistani nobleman among the Kshatriyas [005-023]. His true mission is to destabilize Vendhya and capture Devi [[Yasmina]], serving Turan's expansionist ambitions in the Hyborian East. Throughout *The People of the Black Circle*, Kerim Shah operates as a foil to [[Conan]] — equally determined, comparably dangerous, but driven by political calculation rather than personal loyalty.

> "Kerim Shah!" muttered Conan, red glinting in his eyes — the Cimmerian's recognition of a worthy adversary [005-377]

## Agent of Turan

Kerim Shah's cover as a nobleman from Iranistan places him at the Vendhyan court, where he works to manipulate the kingdom's internal politics [005-023]. His reputation among the Vendhyan elite is one of wealth and influence — he scatters coins among the border tribes to purchase loyalty and intelligence [005-215]. But beneath this diplomatic facade, Kerim Shah is a field agent of [[Turan]], answering directly to King Yezdigerd and serving the empire's designs on its eastern neighbor.

His methods are pragmatic rather than cruel. Unlike the [[Black Seers of Yimsha]], who traffic in supernatural terror, Kerim Shah relies on gold, deception, and calculated force. When necessary, he fights — demonstrating skill with both sword and bow — but he prefers to achieve his objectives through manipulation rather than open violence.

> "My masters sent me with Kerim Shah to aid him in every way," Khemsa acknowledged the chain of command that bound him to the Turanian agent [005-109]

## Mission in Vendhya

Kerim Shah's primary objective in Vendhya is the capture of Devi [[Yasmina]], the kingdom's ruler. He pursues her across the Afghulistan borderlands in a race against [[Conan]], who has taken Yasmina under his protection for his own purposes [005-378].

When he finally confronts Conan, Kerim Shah attempts negotiation before conflict. "We both want the Devi," he tells the Cimmerian, proposing an alliance of convenience [005-389]. His willingness to pivot from rivalry to partnership — and his readiness to abandon the partnership when it ceases to serve his interests — marks him as a professional rather than a zealot.

> "Where is the Devi?" demanded Kerim Shah — his singular focus on his mission, confronting Conan directly [005-378]

## Alliance and Rivalry with Khemsa

Kerim Shah operates alongside [[Khemsa]], the acolyte of the Black Seers who serves as his complement in the Vendhyan mission [005-109]. Where Kerim Shah represents secular power — the gold and steel of Turan — Khemsa represents supernatural force, drawing on the sorcery of [[Mount Yimsha]].

Their relationship is one of parallel service rather than friendship. Khemsa acknowledges Kerim Shah's authority but pursues his own agenda, and as the Black Seers' influence grows, the bond between the two agents frays. Khemsa's masters sent him to aid Kerim Shah, but the sorcerer's ambitions extend beyond the Turanian mission — a divergence that reshapes the story's latter half.

## The Assault on Yimsha

In the story's climax, Kerim Shah joins Conan and the Irakzai tribesmen in the assault on the stronghold of the Black Seers [005-461]. His archery proves crucial against the supernatural defenders — he sends shaft after shaft into the billowing spheres of the Seers' magic, demonstrating that mortal skill can counter immortal power [005-457].

Kerim Shah's presence in the final battle is an act of survival rather than heroism. He has been drawn into a conflict that exceeds his original mission parameters, and he adapts because adaptation is his nature. Alongside [[Conan]], he fights his way into the heart of Yimsha, and when the Seers fall, Kerim Shah is among those who still stand [005-491].

> "They're no adepts, to wield magic against us," grunted Conan to Kerim Shah — the Cimmerian acknowledging the Turanian as a fellow soldier in the fight against the Black Seers [005-478]
