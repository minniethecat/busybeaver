# Zamora

Zamora is an eastern kingdom of the Hyborian Age in Robert E. Howard's Conan stories, known for its dark-skinned people and a culture where thievery is elevated to an art form. It is the setting of Conan's youth as a thief, most notably in "The Tower of the Elephant," and serves as the eastern threshold of Hyborian civilization.

## Geography

Zamora lies east of [[Koth]] and the central Hyborian kingdoms, occupying a position between the Hyborian world and the eastern satrapies. Its capital, Shadizar, is notorious throughout the Hyborian world as the "City of Wickedness." The kingdom's terrain includes arid plateaus, ancient cities, and the mysterious temples that dot its landscape.

Zamora's position as a border kingdom between west and east gives it a distinct cultural character, blending Hyborian traditions with eastern influences. The kingdom serves as a gateway for eastern trade and migration into the Hyborian kingdoms.

## Inhabitants

The Zamorians are described as a dark-skinned people distinct from the paler Hyborian races:

> The dark-skinned people of Zamora paid him tribute, as did the ancient kingdom of Koth. [004-016]

Zamorian society is notable for its tolerance—even celebration—of thievery. Young Conan spent his formative years in Zamora learning the thief's trade:

> When I was a thief in Zamora I learned of him. [003-080]

The worship of [[Bel]], the ancient god whose jewel-crowned elephant tower dominates the capital, is a central feature of Zamorian religious life. The sorcerer [[Yara]], who dwells in the [[Tower of the Elephant]], is the most powerful figure in Zamora, feared by the populace and respected by the criminal underworld alike.

Zamorians appear frequently as soldiers in the mixed armies of the Hyborian Age:

> The men he had gathered about him were made up of men from many nations: Kothians, Zamorians, Brythunians, Corinthians, Shemites. [002-187]

## History

Zamora is one of the ancient kingdoms of the Hyborian world. It was at various times a tributary of Koth and a contested territory between Koth, [[Turan]], and other eastern powers. Its history is defined by its position at the cultural boundary between the Hyborian west and the eastern empires.

The kingdom's most famous historical figure is Yara, the sorcerer who seized control of the Tower of the Elephant and used its alien power—derived from the being Yag-kosha—to dominate Zamoran politics and extract tribute from surrounding kingdoms.
