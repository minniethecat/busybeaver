# Fall of the Hyborian Kingdoms

## Overview

The fall of the Hyborian kingdoms was "unique in that it was not brought about by internal decay, but by the growing power of the barbarian nations and the Hyrkanians" (017-029). Unlike Rome or other historical empires, the Hyborian civilization was "overthrown while their vigorous culture was in its prime" — a victim of external forces it had inadvertently strengthened (017-029).

## Prelude

Aquilonia's imperial ambitions set the stage for disaster. Wishing to extend their empire, Aquilonia's kings "made war on their neighbors," annexing Zingara, Argos, and Ophir outright and reducing Koth and the Shemitish tribes to tributary status (017-030). This aggressive expansion bred resentment among the conquered peoples and overextended Aquilonia's military. Meanwhile, in the west, the Pictish chief Gorm had used iron-working knowledge provided by the Nemedian priest Arus to transform scattered clans into a unified military power (017-036)(017-040).

## The Event

The collapse came from two directions simultaneously. From the east, the Hyrkanians "made their first real thrust at the western world," their riders sweeping over Zamora and devastating eastern Corinthia (017-030). Aquilonia managed to defeat this first Hyrkanian wave, demonstrating the western kingdom's military might, but the effort drained resources and revealed strategic vulnerabilities.

From the west came the decisive blow. The Picts, now armed with iron weapons, burst upon the Bossonian frontiers "in scale-mail, wielding weapons of keen steel" (017-042). Treachery from within — contentions between the Aquilonians and their Bossonian auxiliaries — broke the frontier defenses (017-046). A vast Pictish empire eventually stretched "from the coasts of Vanaheim in the north to the southernmost shores of Zingara," encompassing all Aquilonia except its northernmost province of Gunderland (017-053).

## Aftermath

The fall of the Hyborian kingdoms was followed by a short age in which "Pict and Hyrkanian snarled at each other over the ruins of the world they had conquered" (017-055). Then the glacier ages began and the great Nordic drift pushed the Æsir and Vanir southward, sweeping away the Pictish empire in its turn and laying the ethnic foundations of modern Europe (017-055)(017-056).
