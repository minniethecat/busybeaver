# Khrosha

Khrosha is a port city in the Hyborian kingdom of [[Nemedia]], mentioned in Robert E. Howard's Conan story "Shadows in the Moonlight" (also published as "Iron Shadows in the Moon"). It is known in the story as the home of [[Sergius]], the captain of the pirate band that captures Conan on an unnamed island in the Vilayet Sea.

## Location and Character

Khrosha is a Nemedian city, placing it among the civilized Hyborian kingdoms west of the Vilayet Sea. The fact that Sergius is "of Khrosha" [002-193] — and that he commands a pirate ship on the great inland sea — suggests that Khrosha is a coastal or near-coastal settlement with access to the maritime world of the Hyborian Age. Nemedia's geography places it between Aquilonia to the west and the eastern marches, with access to the sea routes that connect the Hyborian kingdoms to the Vilayet.

## Sergius of Khrosha

The sole direct mention of Khrosha in Howard's stories occurs when Conan recognizes the pirate captain:

> "Sergius of Khrosha, by Crom!" [002-193]

Sergius is described as a "massive" man who fights "quick on his feet as a giant cat, for all his bulk" [002-198]. He leads a band of pirates — the Red Brotherhood — operating on the Vilayet Sea. Conan kills Sergius in single combat on the island where the pirate band has landed, driving his sword through the captain's body [002-199].

Sergius's death triggers a succession struggle between his lieutenants, [[Aratus]] the Kothian and [[Ivanos]] the Corinthian, revealing the internal rivalries within the pirate band. The body of "Sergius of Khrosha" is unceremoniously dragged to the western cliffs and cast into the sea [002-215].

## In the Stories

Khrosha is referenced in a single line in "Shadows in the Moonlight," identified only as the origin of the pirate captain Sergius. The city itself is never visited or described in the story, and no other characters from Khrosha appear. Despite this brevity, the epithet "of Khrosha" carries narrative weight — it marks Sergius as a man of the civilized Hyborian world, in contrast to the barbarian Conan who eventually defeats him.
