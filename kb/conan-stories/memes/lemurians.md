# Lemurians

## Overview

The Lemurians were one of three major barbarian races of the [[Thurian Age]], alongside the [[Picts]] and [[Atlanteans]]. They inhabited "a chain of large islands in the eastern hemisphere" (017-002) and their history traces one of the most dramatic arcs in Howard's fictional chronology — from island-dwelling barbarians to enslaved survivors of the [[Great Cataclysm]], and ultimately to the conquerors who became the [[Hyrkanians]] and built the empire of [[Turan]].

## In the Thurian Age

During the [[thurian-age|Thurian Age]], the Lemurians occupied a defined place in the pre-cataclysmic world order. Howard places them alongside the [[picts|Picts]] and [[atlanteans|Atlanteans]] as the three barbarian peoples who surrounded the civilized kingdoms of the [[thurian-age|Thurian Continent]]:

> The barbarians of that age were the Picts, who lived on islands far out on the western ocean; the Atlanteans, who dwelt on a small continent between the Pictish Islands and the main, or Thurian Continent; and the Lemurians, who inhabited a chain of large islands in the eastern hemisphere. (017-002)

The Lemurians occasionally came into contact with a "mysterious and non-Thurian" race that lived on the far-eastern shores of the [[thurian-age|Thurian Continent]], suggesting that the Lemurian Islands served as a waypoint between the Thurian world and an even more remote civilization (017-003). Like the [[picts|Picts]] and [[atlanteans|Atlanteans]], the Lemurians served as mercenaries in the crumbling Thurian armies — they "were their generals, their statesmen, often their kings" as the civilization decayed from within (017-004).

## The Great Cataclysm

The [[Great Cataclysm]] devastated the Lemurian homeland. Their island chains, like [[atlanteans|Atlantis]], sank beneath the waves — "[[atlanteans|Atlantis]] and Lemuria sank, and the [[picts|Pictish]] Islands were heaved up to form the mountain peaks of a new continent" (017-005). However, a substantial Lemurian population survived:

> Many Lemurians escaped to the eastern coast of the Thurian Continent, which was comparatively untouched. There they were enslaved by the ancient race which already dwelt there, and their history, for thousands of years, is a history of brutal servitude. (017-006)

## Millennia of Enslavement

The post-Cataclysm fate of the Lemurians represents one of the darkest chapters in Howard's fictional history. Cut off from the rest of the world "by the heaving up of gigantic mountains and the forming of a chain of vast lakes," the Lemurians spent thousands of years "toiling as slaves of their ancient masters" (017-009). The enslavement was so complete and brutalizing that Howard describes them as "levelled almost to a bestial plane themselves by the brutishness of their slavery" (017-013).

## Rise and Transformation

Eventually, the Lemurians rose against their oppressors. In an act of apocalyptic revenge, they "destroyed their masters" and became "savages stalking among the ruins of a strange civilization" (017-013). The survivors of their masters' civilization fled westward and founded [[Stygia]], carrying with them the dark sorcerous traditions that would define that kingdom.

Meanwhile, the freed Lemurians began their own cultural evolution. A thousand years after their uprising, they were "evolving a strange semi-civilization of their own" (017-022). This process culminated in their transformation into the [[hyrkanians|Hyrkanians]]:

> Now the Lemurians enter history again as Hyrkanians. Through the centuries they have pushed steadily westward, and now a tribe skirts the southern end of the great inland sea — Vilayet — and establishes the kingdom of Turan on the southwestern shore. (017-026)

## Legacy

The Lemurian story is central to the grand narrative of the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]]. Their trajectory — from island barbarians to brutalized slaves to world-shaking conquerors — mirrors and amplifies the broader theme of cyclical rise and fall that defines Howard's fictional history. The dark-skinned descendants who "retreated into the steppes, pushed irresistibly back toward Vilayet" at the end of the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]] (017-056) are the final echo of this ancient people.
