## Overview

The **Apples of Derketa** are deadly poisonous fruits that grow in the jungles of [[kush|Kush]] and other tropical regions of the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] world. Named for [[derketa|Derketa]], the Queen of the Dead, these large, darkly crimson globes contain a juice so toxic that even a drop on the skin is instantly fatal. [[conan|Conan]] described their lethality with characteristic bluntness: "If we ate that we wouldn't need the bite of a dragon" [010-094].

## Appearance and Properties

The fruits grow on trees with "broad leaves" of "a peculiarly rich and vivid green" [010-091], the crimson globes suspended from the boughs providing splashes of vivid color among the foliage. When pierced, the Apples release "darkly purple drops" [010-116] — the poisonous juice that makes them so feared.

The lethal mechanism of the Apples is extreme. According to [[conan|Conan]]: "Drink a little of the juice, or spill it on your flesh, and you'd be dead before you could tumble to the foot of this crag" [010-094]. This near-instantaneous fatality, whether ingested or merely contacting the skin, marks the Apples as one of the most potent natural poisons in the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] world.

## Connection to Derketa

The Apples are sacred to or named for [[derketa|Derketa]], the Shemitish goddess identified as "the Queen of the Dead" [010-094]. In the [[shem|Shem]]itish pantheon, Derketa occupies a position alongside [[ishtar|Ishtar]], Ashtoreth, Adonis, and [[bel|Bel]]. The association of the death-goddess with the deadly fruit is thematically consistent — the Apples serve as her earthly agents, delivering death as swiftly and irrevocably as the goddess herself.

The people of [[kush|Kush]] are credited with naming the fruit, indicating that knowledge of the Apples and their connection to [[derketa|Derketa]] extended across the cultural and geographical boundaries separating the [[shem|Shem]]itish lands from the black kingdoms of the south.

## Use Against Dragons

[[conan|Conan]] speculated about the Apples' potential as a weapon against dragons. In *The Pool of the Black One*, he remarked: "If your ancestors had thought to dip their spears in the poisonous juice of Derketa's Apples and jab them in the eyes or mouth or somewhere like that, they'd have seen that dragons are not more immortal than any other chunk of beef" [012-047]. This observation reflects the [[cimmerians|Cimmerian]]'s characteristic pragmatism — even the most terrifying creatures have vulnerabilities that can be exploited with sufficient ingenuity and the right poison.

## Collection and Handling

The extreme danger of the Apples requires equally extreme care in handling. When [[conan|Conan]] harvested one, he used a spear to pierce it from a distance, "drawing aside to avoid the darkly purple drops that dripped from the pierced fruit" [010-116]. The need for such precautions underscores the fruit's lethality — even the smallest splash of juice would be fatal.
