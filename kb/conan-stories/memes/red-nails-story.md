**Red Nails** is the final complete [[conan]] story written by Robert E. Howard, serialized in *Weird Tales* across the July, August–September, and October 1936 issues. Widely regarded as one of the finest entries in the [[conan|Conan]] canon, it is a claustrophobic masterpiece of pulp horror set entirely within the walls of [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]] — a sealed city whose inhabitants have spent five decades exterminating each other in a feud started by a stolen woman.

## Plot Summary

The story opens with [[arus|Valeria of the Red Brotherhood]], an [[aquilonia|Aquilonian]] pirate, fleeing through a hostile jungle after killing a superior officer who attempted to assault her. She encounters [[conan]], who has been tracking her, and the two form an uneasy partnership as they push deeper into the wilderness, pursued by a dragon-like creature.

They discover [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]], a walled city of strange architecture whose gates open at their approach. Inside, they find not a thriving civilization but a charnel house — a vast, labyrinthine metropolis inhabited by the last remnants of two tribes, the [[zogar-sag|Tecuhltli]] and the [[xotalancas|Xotalancas]], locked in a blood feud that has raged for fifty years.

The [[tecuhltli|Tecuhltli]], led by Prince Olmec, welcome the newcomers as potential allies. [[conan|Conan]] and [[valeria|Valeria]] meet [[techotl]], a warrior of the [[tecuhltli|Tecuhltli]] who serves as their guide, and [[tascela]], the immortal princess whose stolen status as the wife of [[xotalancas|Xotalanc]] was the original cause of the feud. As the story unfolds, the true horror of [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]] emerges: [[tascela|Tascela]] is not merely a princess but a centuries-old [[stygia|Stygian]] sorceress who sustains her youth by draining the life-force of young women. [[valeria|Valeria]] becomes her next intended victim.

The climax is a cascade of violence: the [[xotalancas|Xotalancas]] launch a final assault that annihilates both tribes almost entirely; Olmec, driven by lust, attempts to claim [[valeria|Valeria]] for himself; [[tascela|Tascela]] poisons Olmec and prepares [[valeria|Valeria]] for her ritual of rejuvenation; and [[conan|Conan]], presumed dead, smashes through a bronze door to confront [[tascela|Tascela]] in her sanctum — a scene that gave the story its title, as the door is decorated with twenty red nails driven into its surface, one for each enemy slain.

## Setting

The entire story takes place within and immediately around [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]], a sealed city on the [[darfar|Darfar border]]. Key locations within the city include:

- **The Halls of Science** — where the Burning Skull, a sorcerous relic, guards the approach to [[tecuhltli|Tecuhltli]] territory
- **The Jade Throne Room** — Prince Olmec's seat of power
- **The Chamber of the Dragon** — Olmec's secret worship chamber, connected to the dragon pit
- **The Throne of Ivory** — [[tascela|Tascela]]'s private sanctum, where she performs her rituals

[[xuchotl|Xuchotl]] itself is one of Howard's most fully realized settings — a city-as-character whose corridors, traps, and creeping decay mirror the psychological deterioration of its inhabitants.

## Themes

### The Death of Civilization

[[xuchotl|Xuchotl]] is Howard's most explicit meditation on civilizational decay. The [[tecuhltli|Tecuhltli]] and [[xotalancas|Xotalancas]] have not been destroyed by barbarians or natural disaster but by their own hatred — a feud so all-consuming that it has reduced a once-great city to a handful of survivors living in a mausoleum of their own making.

### Immortality as Horror

[[tascela|Tascela]] subverts the fantasy of eternal life. Her immortality is parasitic — she survives by consuming the young, generation after generation, turning her own people into livestock. Howard presents eternal youth not as a gift but as a curse that strips away humanity, leaving only hunger.

### The Barbarian as Renewal

[[conan|Conan]] and [[valeria|Valeria]] enter [[xuchotl|Xuchotl]] as outsiders and leave as the only survivors. Their barbarian vitality — untainted by the decadence that has consumed the city's original inhabitants — is what allows them to navigate the nightmare and escape. It is the clearest expression of Howard's thesis that civilization inevitably corrupts, and only the barbarian remains clean.

## Significance

"Red Nails" represents the apex of Howard's development as a writer. The prose is tighter than in earlier [[conan|Conan]] stories, the horror elements are more psychologically sophisticated, and the thematic complexity exceeds anything in the series. [[valeria|Valeria]] is arguably Howard's most fully realized female character — not a damsel but a co-protagonist who fights, kills, and survives on equal terms with [[conan|Conan]].

The story's unrelenting darkness — the annihilation of both tribes, the revelation of [[tascela|Tascela]]'s immortality, the sheer waste of human life consumed by a meaningless feud — makes it one of the most tonally mature works of pulp fiction ever written.
