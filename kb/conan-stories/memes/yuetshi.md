# Yuetshi

The Yuetshi are a strange, primitive people dwelling in rude fishing huts along the southern shore of the Vilayet Sea in Robert E. Howard's [[conan|Conan]] stories. Featured most prominently in "The [[khosatral-khel|Devil in Iron]]," they are characterized as a race "whose origin is lost in the gray dawn of the past" [004-XXX], having inhabited their coastal territory since time immemorial.

## Origins and Antiquity

The Yuetshi represent a prehistoric survival — a people who predate the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]] kingdoms and even the rise of the great civilizations of the south. Their origins are unknown even to themselves:

> The fisherman was typical of his race, that strange people whose origin is lost in the gray dawn of the past, and who have dwelt in their rude fishing huts along the southern shore of the Sea of Vilayet since time immemorial. [004-XXX]

Dim legends preserved among the Yuetshi "half intelligibly suggested a connection of immeasurable antiquity between the fishers and the unknown island kingdom" of [[xapur|Xapur]] [004-XXX]. But these legends have degraded over the centuries into meaninglessness — "it had been a thousand years since any Yuetshi had understood the import of these tales; they repeated them now as a meaningless formula, a gibberish framed by their lips by custom" [004-XXX].

## Physical Characteristics

The Yuetshi are distinctly non-[[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] in appearance:

> He was broadly built, with long apish arms and a mighty chest, but with lean loins and thin bandy legs. His face was broad, his forehead low and retreating, his hair thick and tangled. A belt for a knife and a rag for a loincloth were all he wore in the way of clothing. [004-XXX]

Their crude appearance and primitive culture place them among the most "savage" peoples of the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] world, yet their ancient lineage gives them a connection to lost ages that more advanced races have forgotten.

## Weapons

The Yuetshi carry distinctive knives — "the saw-edged crescent blade of the Yuetshi that could disembowel a man with an upward stroke" [004-XXX]. This weapon, unique to their culture, suggests a long tradition of close-quarters combat adapted to their fishing lifestyle.

## The Xapur Fisherman

The Yuetshi fisherman who ventures to [[xapur|Xapur]] is described as "less dully incurious than most of his people" [004-XXX] — an observation that paints the Yuetshi as a generally incurious race, content with the narrow horizons of their fishing villages. This particular fisherman is driven to explore the [[xapur|isle of Xapur]] after a storm wrecks his boat on its cliffs and he witnesses a lightning strike that seems to have shattered part of the ancient green stone dome.

His encounter with the resurrected sorcerer [[khosatral-khel|Khosatral Khel]] is fatal — his saw-edged knife "splintered against the stranger's corded belly as against a steel column" [004-XXX], and his neck is broken "like a rotten twig in the giant hands" [004-XXX].

## Service to Jehungir Agha

The Yuetshi serve as spies and messengers for those who pay for their services. Jehungir Agha employs Yuetshi fishermen as agents in his plot against [[conan|Conan]]:

> "Then we will send a spy—a Yuetshi fisherman will do—to the *kozak* camp, who will tell Conan that Octavia is hiding on Xapur. If I know my man, he will go straight to that place." [004-XXX]

This episode reveals the Yuetshi's willingness to serve any master for pay, their intimate knowledge of the Vilayet waters, and their expendable status in the eyes of the region's power players.

## Culture

The Yuetshi live in "rude fishing huts" along the southern Vilayet shore. Their culture is one of bare subsistence — fishing, boating, and occasional mercenary work for the settled powers. They are described as "dully incurious" [004-XXX], a people whose intellectual horizons are as narrow as their material circumstances.

Despite their primitiveness, the Yuetshi possess a stubborn resilience. No Yuetshi had visited the [[xapur|isle of Xapur]] for a century before the fisherman's storm-driven landing, and the adjacent mainland coast is described as uninhabited marsh "given over to the grim beasts that haunted it" [004-XXX] — yet the Yuetshi persist in their coastal villages, enduring where other peoples would have been driven out.
