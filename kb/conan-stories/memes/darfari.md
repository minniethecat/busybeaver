## Overview

The Darfari are a black people from the southern region of Darfar, whose territory borders [[stygia|Stygia]] on the south. They are most infamous for their practice of ritual cannibalism — not merely a dietary habit, but an integral element of their religious cult, accompanied by nocturnal drum ceremonies and communal feasting on human flesh.

> "Cannibalism was more than a perverted appetite with the black men of Darfar; it was an integral element of their ghastly cult." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Physical Characteristics

The Darfari are described as powerfully built black men, distinguishable from other black races by their filed teeth — a mark of their cannibal cult. Conan, having encountered them in Zamboula, notes the telltale sign: "His teeth were unfiled" marks a man as not being from Darfar.

> "A giant black man stood framed against the soft glow of a copper lamp. A quick glance showed Conan the man was not from Darfar. His teeth were unfiled." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Culture and Ritual

The Darfari cannibal cult operates by night, with the beat of a black drum serving as the signal for their gatherings. In cities like Zamboula, where Darfari slaves and immigrants live among other races, they hold secret conclaves in palm groves beyond the city walls. Humans are prey, lured or captured and brought to roasting pits where "strange meat might be roasted under the stars, while black beasts squatted about to glut a hideous hunger."

Their cult is sufficiently organized that accomplices like Aram Baksh in Zamboula — innkeepers who drug and deliver guests to the cannibals — operate as an established criminal network.

## Darfar Region

The land of Darfar itself lies south of Stygia, a region of grasslands that rises up in "a red wave" of raids against the Stygian frontier. Mercenary companies — the Free Companions under Zarallo — are hired to guard the Darfar border from these incursions.

> "the camp of the mercenaries by the border town of Sukhmet amidst the level grasslands, where desperate adventurers of many races guard the Stygian frontier against the raids that come up like a red wave from Darfar." [[2]](#ref-2)

The "suns of Darfar" are proverbial for their intensity — capable of burning white skin brown, a mark of the region's equatorial climate.

## In the Stories

Darfari cannibals feature prominently in "[[Shadows in Zamboula]]," where Conan must navigate the nighted streets of a city infested with them. His knowledge of their habits — and his ability to distinguish friend from foe by their teeth — saves his life.

Conan and Valeria both served as mercenaries on the Darfar border before deserting to seek their fortunes elsewhere, establishing the region as a backdrop to multiple tales.
