# Bêlit

## Overview

Bêlit is the Shemite pirate queen who dominates the waters of the Black Coast in Howard's tale **Queen of the Black Coast**. Captain of the galley *Tigress*, she commands a crew of black corsairs who worship her with a reverence bordering on religious awe. She is Conan's equal in ferocity and his partner in one of the most intense relationships in the Conan canon — a union of two fierce spirits who recognize in each other a kindred wildness.

## The Queen of the Black Coast

Bêlit's reputation precedes her. She is "a wild Shemite woman, who has made herself a queen among the black corsairs" (003-067). Her name is spoken with terror by those who sail those waters. She appears before Conan not as a captive or supplicant but as a force of nature — lithe, commanding, and utterly fearless. When Conan first encounters her, she stands poised on the deck of the Tigress, her gaze measuring him as an equal rather than prey.

Bêlit recognizes Conan's nature immediately. "You are no soft Hyborian!" she exclaims, a declaration that is both recognition and invitation (003-055). Their bond is forged in this mutual recognition — two barbarians adrift in a world of softer peoples.

## Relationship with Conan

The relationship between Bêlit and Conan is unique in the Conan stories. She is not a damsel to be rescued but a partner who fights beside him, commands beside him, and loves him with a ferocity matching his own. Her crew, who "worship her as a goddess" (003-067), follow Conan only because she commands it. Their romance is elemental — set against the dark waters of the Black Coast, the ancient ruins, and the ever-present threat of death.

## Legacy

Bêlit represents one of Howard's most fully realized female characters. In a genre often criticized for passive heroines, Bêlit is an active agent — a queen by her own making, a warrior by her own hand, and a lover by her own choice. Her death marks one of the most memorable passages in the Conan stories, and her spirit continues to haunt Conan's memory long after. Survivors of ships she butchered "named Bêlit with curses" (003-067), a testament to the terror she inspired in life.
