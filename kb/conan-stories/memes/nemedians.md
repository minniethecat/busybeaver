## Overview

The Nemedians are the [[hyborians|Hyborian]] people of Nemedia, the kingdom that stood as the great rival to [[aquilonians|Aquilonia]] throughout the Hyborian Age. While Aquilonia represented military might and imperial ambition, Nemedia was renowned for its scholars, its intricate politics, and its fierce, stubborn independence.

> "But Nemedia the western empire had never been able to subdue, although the latter's triumphs were of the defensive sort, and were generally attained with the aid of Hyperborean armies." [[1]](#ref-1)

## The Rivalry with Aquilonia

The feud between Aquilonia and Nemedia is an ancient one, stretching back into the early centuries of the Hyborian drift. While Aquilonia annexed Zingara, Argos, Ophir, and reduced Koth to tributary status, Nemedia alone among the Hyborian kingdoms consistently resisted Aquilonian domination.

The Nemedian victories were generally defensive, often achieved with the aid of Hyperborean armies. Despite being surrounded by the expanding Aquilonian empire, Nemedia maintained its sovereignty through a combination of military skill, diplomatic cunning, and strategic alliances.

> "During this period Aquilonia's only defeats were: her failure to annex Nemedia; the rout of an army sent into Cimmeria; and the almost complete destruction of an army by the Æsir." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Culture and Scholarship

Nemedia is the intellectual center of the Hyborian world. Nemedian scholars maintain the great libraries and universities, and Nemedian Chroniclers record the histories of the age. This scholarly tradition gives Nemedia a sophistication that contrasts with Aquilonia's martial culture.

In "The Hour of the Dragon," the Nemedian sorcerer [[xaltotun|Xaltotun]] — resurrected from the ancient past — leads a conspiracy to overthrow the Aquilonian king. The plot reflects Nemedian strengths: cunning, ancient knowledge, and the willingness to use forbidden arts.

## Physical Characteristics

As Hyborians, the Nemedians share the tawny hair and grey eyes of their racial stock, though centuries of intermixing have diversified their appearance. Nemedian nobles are known for their rich attire, their sophistication, and their taste for intrigue.

## Fate

Nemedia survives the fall of Aquilonia longer than most Hyborian kingdoms, but ultimately succumbs to the same forces — the [[picts|Pictish]] invasion from the west and the [[hyrkanians|Hyrkanian]] migration from the east. The Nemedian cities fall, and their people are scattered in the great migrations that end the Hyborian Age.
