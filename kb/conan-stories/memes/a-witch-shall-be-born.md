## Overview

**"A Witch Shall Be Born"** is a [[conan|Conan]] story by Robert E. Howard, first published in the December 1934 issue of *Weird Tales*. One of the most dramatic entries in the series, it opens with [[conan|Conan]] already on the throne of [[Aquilonia]] and follows his crucifixion, survival against all odds, and return to power. The story introduces [[Salome]], [[salome|the witch-queen]] of [[khauran|Khauran]] and [[conan|Conan]]'s long-lost twin sister substitute" — actually a malevolent doppelgänger who usurps the throne while [[conan|Conan]] is crucified outside the city gates.

> "I am the woman you know as Taramis, queen of Khauran. But I am not Taramis. I am Salome, her twin sister — the witch who was cast out at birth!" [010-xxx]

## Plot Summary

### The Usurpation

[[conan|Conan]], now the [[aquilonia|Aquilonian]] king, is overthrown in a coup orchestrated by his own court. Captured by [[constantius|Constantius]] the [[koth|Kothic]] and his [[shem|Shem]]itish mercenaries, [[conan|Conan]] is sentenced to crucifixion. He is nailed to a cross outside the city walls and left to die under the desert sun — one of the most iconic images in the entire [[conan|Conan]] canon [010-xxx].

### Survival

Against all probability, [[conan|Conan]] survives. An [[aquilonia|Aquilonian]] loyalist, Olgerd Vladislav, finds him and cuts him down. [[conan|Conan]]'s survival — after what would kill any ordinary man — reinforces his nearly superhuman constitution. Recovering slowly in hiding, [[conan|Conan]] begins planning his counter-strike [010-xxx].

### Zarallo's Free Companions

[[conan|Conan]]'s past as a mercenary becomes crucial. Having served among [[Free Companions]] on the [[darfar|Darfar border]], [[conan|Conan]] knows the mercenary networks of the south. He rallies a new force, leveraging old alliances and his fearsome reputation. The story provides flashback material about [[conan|Conan]]'s time with Zarallo's company, where he met [[Valeria]] of the [[red-brotherhood|Red Brotherhood]] [010-038].

### The Witch Revealed

The usurper on the [[aquilonia|Aquilonian]] throne is not a human pretender but [[salome|Salome]], a witch whose supernatural abilities include illusion-casting and mental domination. She is revealed to be the twin sister of [[taramis|Taramis]], the rightful queen — cast out at birth for the dark powers she manifested. [[conan|Conan]] must defeat not just a political rival but a supernatural threat [010-xxx].

### Retaking the Throne

[[conan|Conan]] returns to [[aquilonia|Aquilonia]] at the head of a mercenary army. The climax involves a confrontation with [[salome|Salome]], whose witch-powers prove formidable but ultimately no match for [[conan|Conan]]'s direct, physical approach to problem-solving. He reclaims his throne and restores order to the kingdom [010-xxx].

## Setting

The story takes place in the kingdom of [[Aquilonia]] during [[conan|Conan]]'s reign as king, with flashback sequences to the [[darfar|Darfar border]] and [[free-companions|Zarallo's Free Companions]]' camp at Sukhmet. This is one of the few stories set during [[conan|Conan]]'s kingship, bridging his mercenary past with his royal present.

## Themes

- **Death and Resurrection**: [[conan|Conan]]'s crucifixion and survival is a symbolic death and rebirth, reinforcing his status as a figure who transcends mortal limitations.
- **The Nature of Kingship**: The story explores what it means for a barbarian to rule a civilized kingdom, and the vulnerability of a king who governs by personal strength rather than institutional safeguards.
- **Identity and Doubles**: The twin motif ([[salome|Salome]]/[[taramis|Taramis]]) plays on themes of identity theft and the horror of being replaced by one's own mirror image.
- **Loyalty and Betrayal**: [[conan|Conan]] is betrayed by his own nobles but saved by a loyal soldier, illustrating the fragility of political alliances.

## Publication

*A Witch Shall Be Born* was first published in *Weird Tales*, December 1934. It was collected in *The Bloody Crown of [[conan|Conan]]* (Del Rey, 2004). The story's opening crucifixion scene is one of the most famous in pulp fiction.

## Connections

- [[conan|Conan]]'s history with the [[Free Companions]] and Zarallo is detailed in flashback, connecting to his broader mercenary past.
- [[Valeria]] appears in flashback at Zarallo's camp, linking this story to *[[red-nails-story|Red Nails]]* where she later becomes [[conan|Conan]]'s companion.
- The story's focus on [[conan|Conan]] as king of [[aquilonia|Aquilonia]] connects to the broader arc of his rise from barbarian to monarch.
