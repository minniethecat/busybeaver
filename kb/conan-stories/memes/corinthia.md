# Corinthia

Corinthia is a region of the central Hyborian kingdoms in Robert E. Howard's Conan stories, characterized by a loose federation of city-states rather than a unified monarchy. It lies in the mountainous territory between the great powers of [[Aquilonia]], [[Nemedia]], [[Ophir]], and [[Koth]], and its people are known as capable mercenaries and traders.

## Geography

Corinthia occupies the mountainous highlands of the central Hyborian world, a region of rugged terrain, fortified city-states, and narrow trade routes. Its position between the great powers makes it strategically significant, as control of the Corinthian passes offers military advantage to whichever kingdom can secure them. The mountainous geography has historically prevented the region from being consolidated under a single ruler, fostering the city-state political structure that defines Corinthia.

The city-states of Corinthia are typically walled settlements built on defensible hilltops, each controlling the surrounding valleys and trade routes. The region's natural defenses have preserved its independence despite the ambitions of its larger neighbors.

## Inhabitants

The Corinthians are a Hyborian people known for their martial prowess and political fractiousness. The city-state structure produces a culture of intense local patriotism and rivalry between neighboring cities. Corinthians frequently serve as mercenaries in the armies of the larger kingdoms, and their presence is noted in the forces that gather around Conan during his wars:

> The men he had gathered about him were made up of men from many nations: Kothians, Zamorians, Brythunians, Corinthians, Shemites. [002-187]

Individual Corinthians who appear in the stories include [[Aratus]], a soldier who fights alongside Conan in "The Scarlet Citadel." The Corinthian character is depicted as lean, quick, and politically sharp—qualities honed by life in the competitive city-state environment. In "The Scarlet Citadel," a Corinthian named Ivanos appears among Conan's band:

> ...some with the Corinthian, whom they called Ivanos. [002-209]

## History

Corinthia's history is defined by its position as a buffer region between the great powers. Never unified under a single king, the Corinthian city-states have survived by playing larger powers against each other and by exporting their surplus military population as mercenaries.

Corinthia is listed among the original Hyborian kingdoms in Howard's historical essay "The Hyborian Age," though its political fragmentation distinguishes it from the centralized monarchies that surround it. The region's independence has been maintained despite repeated attempts by Koth and Aquilonia to dominate it.

In "The Phoenix on the Sword," Corinthia is mentioned among the kingdoms whose representatives scheme against Conan's throne. In "The Scarlet Citadel," Corinthian mercenaries feature among the forces of both Conan and his enemies, reflecting the mercenary tradition that defines Corinthian military culture.
