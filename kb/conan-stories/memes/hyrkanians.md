# Hyrkanians

## Overview

The Hyrkanians are a major race of the [[Hyborian Age]], descended from the [[Lemurians]] who survived the [[Great Cataclysm]] on the eastern coast of the [[Thurian Age|Thurian Continent]]. Through centuries of westward migration, they established the powerful empire of [[Turan]] and became the dominant power of the eastern world — the great rival to the western [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kingdoms. [[conan|Conan]] encounters Hyrkanians throughout his adventures, from the pirate-haunted Vilayet Sea to the frontier forts of the steppes.

## Origins and Descent

The Hyrkanians trace their ancestry to the [[lemurians|Lemurians]] of the [[thurian-age|Thurian Age]] — inhabitants of a chain of large islands in the eastern hemisphere. After the [[great-cataclysm|Great Cataclysm]], thousands of [[lemurians|Lemurians]] "escaped to the eastern coast of the [[thurian-age|Thurian Continent]], which was comparatively untouched" and were "enslaved by the ancient race which already dwelt there" (017-006). Their history for "thousands of years" was "a history of brutal servitude" (017-006).

Eventually, the [[lemurians|Lemurians]] rose and overthrew their masters. Howard records that "the [[lemurians|Lemurians]] enter history again as Hyrkanians" — having pushed "steadily westward" for centuries (017-020). A tribe settled the southwestern shore of the Vilayet Sea and established the kingdom of [[turan|Turan]], while other clans skirted north of the inland sea and clashed with the [[Hyperborea|Hyperboreans]] (017-020).

## Physical Characteristics

Howard provides a detailed physical description of the Hyrkanian race:

> The Hyrkanians are dark and generally tall and slender, though a squat slant-eyed type is more and more common among them, resulting from mixture with a curious race of intelligent, though stunted, aborigines, conquered by them among the mountains east of Vilayet, on their westward drift. (017-027)

[[conan|Conan]] himself is frequently mistaken for a Hyrkanian due to his dark complexion, but the text makes clear he "was not an Hyrkanian" and "did not resemble the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] races" (002-035). This distinction underscores the Hyrkanians' position as an eastern, non-[[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] people.

## The Empire of Turan

By [[conan|Conan]]'s time, the Hyrkanian-founded kingdom of [[turan|Turan]] had become the mightiest power in the known world. The chronicle describes its reach in sweeping terms:

> Yezdigerd, king of Turan, was the mightiest monarch in the world. In his palace in the great port city of Aghrapur was heaped the plunder of empires. His fleets of purple-sailed war galleys had made Vilayet an Hyrkanian lake. The dark-skinned people of Zamora paid him tribute, as did the eastern provinces of Koth. The Shemites bowed to his rule as far west as Shushan. His armies ravaged the borders of Stygia in the south and the snowy lands of the Hyperboreans in the north. (004-016)

The [[turan|Turan]]ian capital, Aghrapur, along with Sultanapur, Khawarizm, Shahpur, and Khorusun, held glutted slave markets where "women were sold for three small silver coins" (004-016). The empire sustained itself through conquest, tribute, and the slave trade.

## In the Stories

### The Eastern Seas

[[conan|Conan]]'s first encounters with Hyrkanians come in "The Devil in Iron" and "Shadows in the Moonlight," where he sails the Vilayet Sea — "an Hyrkanian pond" (002-049). The southwestern shore is "held by the Hyrkanians for hundreds of miles" (002-052). Ships of purple-sailed war galleys and merchant vessels ply between Hyrkanian ports, harried by pirates of the same breed as the steppe-dwelling kozaki (004-019).

### The Hyrkanian Invasions

The Hyrkanians were instrumental in the fall of the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] civilization. Howard explicitly states that the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kingdoms fell "not through internal decay but through the growing power of barbarian nations and the Hyrkanians" (017-029). Their invasions from the east — sweeping through Zamora, devastating eastern Corinthia, and striking at the heart of the western kingdoms — are chronicled in the [[Hyrkanian Invasions]].

### Conan Among the Hyrkanians

[[conan|Conan]] spent time "with the kozaki, as the Hyrkanian dogs called us" (002-037) and "learned archery among the Hyrkanians" (003-040). His experiences in Hyrkanian lands gave him familiarity with their language, customs, and fighting styles. In "A Witch Shall Be Born," he confronts a Hyrkanian spy of King Yezdigerd, snarling "Do you think I'd trust you, you Hyrkanian dog?" — a recurring epithet throughout the tales (008-065).

## Culture and Warfare

The Hyrkanians were renowned as horsemen and archers. Their military tradition centered on cavalry: "hordes of steel-clad riders" swept out of the east in wave after wave of invasion (017-030). The Hyrkanian lords "parleyed with the warriors of the steppes" in frontier camps, maintaining a complex relationship with the kozaki — steppe-dwelling free warriors of much the same stock who both raided the [[turan|Turan]]ian frontier and traded with its ports (004-055).

Their religion centered on Erlik — the [[turan|Turan]]ians "bow to Erlik" (010-046), a distinct cult from the [[stygia|Stygian]] worship of [[Set]] or the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] veneration of [[Mitra]].

## Legacy

The Hyrkanians represent one of Howard's most fully realized non-[[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] cultures — a dark mirror to the western kingdoms, equally vigorous but fundamentally alien. Their role as the external force that toppled the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] civilization makes them pivotal to the arc of the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]], and their empire of [[turan|Turan]] persists as a looming presence throughout [[conan|Conan]]'s adventures.
