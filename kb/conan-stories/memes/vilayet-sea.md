## Overview

The Vilayet Sea (also called the Sea of Vilayet) is the great inland sea of the Hyborian Age, a vast body of water separating the western Hyborian kingdoms from the eastern realms of Turan and Hyrkania. It serves as a major geographical feature, trade route, and theatre of piracy and naval conflict in the Conan stories.

In "The Hyborian Age" essay, Howard describes how the Lemurians (later Hyrkanians) skirted the southern end of Vilayet as they pushed westward through the centuries (PN 017-026), establishing the sea as a critical boundary between east and west.

## Geography

The Vilayet Sea lies east of the Hyborian kingdoms and west of the Turanian steppes. Its shores are inhabited by diverse peoples, including a strange race of fishermen whose origin is "lost in the gray dawn of the past":

> The fisherman was typical of his race, that strange people whose origin is lost in the gray dawn of the past, and who have dwelt in their rude fishing huts along the southern shore of the Sea of Vilayet since time immemorial. He was broadly built, with long apish arms and a mighty chest, but with lean loins and thin bandy legs. (PN 004-003)

The islands of Vilayet are notable for their peculiar "ironlike green stone" used in ancient constructions:

> Among the trees reared a broken dome-like structure, built of gigantic blocks of the peculiar ironlike green stone found only on the islands of Vilayet. It seemed incredible that human hands could have shaped and placed them, and certainly it was beyond human power to have overthrown the structure that had been reared in the dawn of time. (PN 004-007)

Storms are rare on Vilayet during certain seasons, making it relatively navigable (PN 002-056).

## Strategic Significance

Over the centuries, the Vilayet Sea has been a contested waterway. Under King Yezdigerd of Turan, the Turanians achieved naval dominance:

> Yezdigerd, king of Turan, was the mightiest monarch in the world. In his palace in the great port city of Aghrapur was heaped the plunder of empires. His fleets of purple-sailed war galleys had made Vilayet an Hyrkanian lake. (PN 004-016)

Conan himself observed that "Vilayet is an Hyrkanian pond" (PN 002-049), reflecting the eastern dominance of this inland sea.

## In the Stories

Vilayet features prominently in several Conan tales:

* **Shadows in the Moonlight** — Conan, as a Kozaki outlaw, speaks of Vilayet and describes being pursued by Turanian horsemen across its shores (PN 002-045, 002-056).

* **The Devil in Iron** — The story opens along the southern shore of Vilayet, where the fisherman of the strange coastal race lives. The ironlike green stone from Vilayet's islands figures in the ancient structure where Khosatral Khel is imprisoned (PN 004-007).

* **The People of the Black Circle** — The Turanian-Vendhyan conflicts reference the maritime power of the Turanians and their control of Vilayet, which they have made an "Hyrkanian lake" (PN 004-016).

* **Beyond the Black River** — Conan mentions having seen ancient symbols "in the uninhabited mountains beyond the Sea of Vilayet, half a world away from this spot" (PN 008-230).
