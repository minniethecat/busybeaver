## Overview

The Turanian Empire (also known simply as Turan) is the mightiest empire of the Hyborian Age eastern world, dominant across the steppes east of the [[vilayet-sea|Vilayet Sea]] and controlling the inland sea itself. Under King Yezdigerd, Turan reached the height of its power, its fleets making "Vilayet an Hyrkanian lake" (PN 004-016).

In "The Hyborian Age," Howard traces the Turanians' origins to the Hyrkanians — descendants of the ancient Lemurians who pushed westward over centuries, establishing kingdoms around Vilayet and eventually dominating the region (PN 017-026).

## Capital and Governance

The empire is ruled from Aghrapur, the great port city on Vilayet where King Yezdigerd maintains his palace:

> Yezdigerd, king of Turan, was the mightiest monarch in the world. In his palace in the great port city of Aghrapur was heaped the plunder of empires. His fleets of purple-sailed war galleys had made Vilayet an Hyrkanian lake. The dark-skinned people of the southern shores called him emperor, and when his name was whispered among the hills, the barefoot tribesmen of the Himelians reached for their long bows. (PN 004-016)

The mention of "plunder of empires" indicates Turan's wealth comes from conquest and tribute from subjugated peoples.

## Military Power

The Turanian military is formidable, combining naval dominance on Vilayet with land forces capable of threatening the Hyborian kingdoms and the mountain tribes. Their "purple-sailed war galleys" control the Vilayet Sea, while their armies campaign against Vendhya across the Himelian Mountains:

> "Aye," muttered Chunder Shan. "You speak the truth there. It would be fight every step of the way, with hairy hill-men hurling down boulders from every height, and rushing us with their long knives in every valley. The Turanians fought their way through the Himelians once, but how many returned to Khurusun?" (PN 005-057)

This suggests that while Turanian military power is vast, operations in the mountains are costly and dangerous.

## Frontier and Steppe Conflict

The steppes between Vilayet and the easternmost Hyborian kingdoms are a zone of constant conflict. The Kozaki — freebooters of the steppes — harass the Turanian frontier:

> Ceaselessly they raided the Turanian frontier, retiring in the steppes when defeated; with the pirates of Vilayet, men of much the same breed, they harried the coast. (PN 004-019)

The Turanians' use of agents to manipulate tribal politics is also noted, with "emissaries from the Turanians" (PN 005-064) stirring trouble among the hill tribes bordering Vendhya.

## Relations with Vendhya

Turan shares a contentious border with Vendhya, the Indian-flavored kingdom to the south, separated by the Himelian Mountains. The Turanians have historically fought through these mountains:

> "The Turanians fought their way through the Himelians once, but how many returned to Khurusun?" (PN 005-057)

Agents like Kerim Shah operate in the borderlands, suggesting a complex relationship of espionage, proxy warfare, and occasional open conflict between the two powers.

## In the Stories

* **The Devil in Iron** — Opens near the southern shore of Vilayet, in territory under Turanian influence, with the empire's naval power a backdrop
* **The People of the Black Circle** — The Turanian-Vendhyan conflict features heavily, with Turanian agents (Kerim Shah, Khemsa) operating in the Himelians
* **Shadows in the Moonlight** — Conan, as a Kozaki, discusses Vilayet and recounts fleeing Turanian horsemen
* **The Hyborian Age** — Howard traces the historical development of the Turanian/Hyrkanian empire from Lemurian origins
