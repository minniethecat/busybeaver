## Overview

The Zamorians are the dark-complexioned people of Zamora, a kingdom lying east of the [[hyborians|Hyborian]] kingdoms. Ethnically distinct from the Hyborian races, the Zamorians share exotic habits with the [[zingarans|Zingarans]] of the southwest but are otherwise unrelated. Zamora is a land of ancient cities, shadowy temples, and a culture where thievery is elevated almost to an art form.

> "Zamora lies to the east, and Zingara to the southwest of these kingdoms—people alike in darkness of complexion and exotic habits, but otherwise unrelated." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Culture and Society

Zamorian civilization is ancient, predating the rise of the Hyborian kingdoms. Its cities — such as Arenjun, the "City of Thieves" — are labyrinths of winding streets, towering spires, and hidden courtyards. The Zamorians are known for their cunning, their skill at deception, and their tolerance for practices that more puritanical Hyborian cultures would condemn.

The cult of the [[elephant-god|Elephant-God]] in the Tower of the Elephant, and the spider-god worship of the priesthood in the Maul, reflect the darker currents in Zamorian religion. Zamorian thieves are legendary throughout the Hyborian world — Conan himself apprenticed in thievery in the streets of Arenjun.

> "Bel, god of thieves, is a good god. When I was a thief in Zamora I learned of him." [[2]](#ref-2)

## Physical Characteristics

Zamorians are dark-skinned, with features distinct from the fair Hyborian races. They are generally shorter and slighter than the heavily-built northern peoples, with quick, clever hands and watchful eyes. Their dark complexion is shared with the Zingarans, though the two peoples are separated by the entire breadth of the Hyborian kingdoms.

## Relations with Hyborians

Zamora has long been a target of Hyborian expansion. During the height of the Aquilonian empire, Zamora was annexed and its wealth flowed west. Later, when the Hyrkanians swept in from the east, Zamora fell easily and the Hyrkanian king established his capital in its largest city.

In the post-Hyborian period, the eastern Brythunians intermarried extensively with the Zamorians, creating a mixed population along the borders of the two lands.

## Significance

Zamora represents the exotic, decadent East in Howard's Hyborian geography — a counterpoint to the vigorous, straightforward Hyborian kingdoms. It is the land where Conan learned the arts of stealth and subtlety, complementing the brute force of his Cimmerian upbringing. The shadowed streets of Zamorian cities are the setting for some of Conan's most famous adventures.
