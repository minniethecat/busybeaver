# Koth

Koth is one of the great Hyborian kingdoms in Robert E. Howard's Conan stories, occupying a central position among the civilized nations of the age. It is a powerful and expansionist state that frequently clashes with its western rival [[Aquilonia]] and exerts considerable influence over the smaller eastern principalities. The Kothian empire once encompassed many of the minor kingdoms to its east, including [[Khauran]], before those territories regained their independence.

## Geography

Koth occupies the southeastern quadrant of the Hyborian kingdoms, stretching from the borders of [[Shem]] in the west to the eastern desert regions. Its central position makes it a crossroads of trade and military movement, with caravan routes passing through its territory from the western kingdoms to the eastern satrapies and [[Turan]]. The terrain varies from fertile agricultural plains in the heartland to arid borderlands in the east, where Koth's territory merges into the semi-desert that separates the Hyborian world from the eastern powers.

The kingdom's extensive borders bring it into contact with multiple neighboring powers, contributing to Koth's reputation as a militarily active and politically volatile state. Its geographic position ensures that Koth is perpetually involved in the power struggles that define Hyborian Age politics.

## Inhabitants

The Kothians are a Hyborian people with a distinct cultural identity shaped by centuries of imperial ambition and contact with eastern civilizations. They are characterized as militarily capable, politically sophisticated, and culturally influenced by both the western Hyborian traditions and the eastern civilizations with which they have long interacted.

Kothian society is hierarchical, dominated by a warrior aristocracy and a powerful monarchy. The kingdom's long history of imperial rule over the eastern principalities has produced a culture that values martial prowess and political cunning. Individual Kothians who appear in the stories include [[Constantius]], the mercenary captain who usurps the throne of Khauran, and various Kothian soldiers and nobles who populate Howard's tales.

The Kothian character—ambitious, expansionist, and ruthless in pursuit of power—is a recurring motif in the Conan stories, making Koth one of the most frequently referenced nations in the canon despite rarely serving as the primary setting.

## History

Koth's history is that of an imperial power in gradual decline, clinging to the memory of past glories. At its height, the Kothian empire controlled a vast swath of territory east of Shem, encompassing many of the smaller kingdoms that would later become independent. Over centuries, these territories gradually broke away:

> Khauran is a kingdom of no great size, one of the many principalities which at one time formed the eastern part of the empire of Koth, and which later regained the independence which was theirs at a still earlier age. [006-149]

Koth's rivalry with Aquilonia is a constant undercurrent in the Conan stories. In "The Phoenix on the Sword," Koth is described as a traditional adversary of the Aquilonian throne, and Kothian agents and interests frequently oppose Conan during his reign as king of Aquilonia. The kingdom's hostility is exemplified by the willingness of Kothian mercenaries and nobles to participate in plots against Aquilonia.

The kingdom also plays a significant role in "A Witch Shall Be Born," where [[Constantius]]—a Kothian exile and mercenary leader—uses Shemitish troops to seize the throne of Khauran. Conan's friendship with Kothian soldiers who join his band is depicted in "The Scarlet Citadel":

> The men he had gathered about him were made up of men from many nations: Kothians, Zamorians, Brythunians, Corinthians, Shemites. [002-187]
