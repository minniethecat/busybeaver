## Overview

The Black Kingdoms are the collective southern realms of the Hyborian Age, inhabited predominantly by black-skinned peoples. They lie south of Stygia and the Hyborian kingdoms, stretching into the unknown lands beyond the known world. The term encompasses several named kingdoms including [[kush|Kush]], [[keshan|Keshan]], [[punt|Punt]], [[darfar|Darfar]], and [[zembabwei|Zembabwei]].

In "The Hyborian Age," Howard maps the end of the Hyborian Age as a period when Stygia, already weakened by internal decay, was "staggering under the thrusts of the black kingdoms to the south" (PN 017-058), indicating their growing power as the Hyborian civilization declined.

## Geography and Peoples

The Black Kingdoms occupy a vast territory south of the known Hyborian world. Their northernmost reaches border Stygia along the River Styx, while the southern extent stretches into unexplored regions. Conan describes having "roamed in the unknown countries south of the black kingdoms of Kush, and east of Keshan" (PN 008-258), suggesting an African-scale continent.

The kingdoms vary in character:

* **Kush** — The northernmost and most frequently mentioned black kingdom, with strong connections to the southern coast where the Black Corsairs operate
* **Keshan** — A barbaric kingdom in the eastern hinterlands of Kush, ruled by a dusky nobility claiming descent from white conquerors (PN 007-012)
* **Punt** — A kingdom neighboring Keshan, targeted for conquest by Zembabwei's kings (PN 007-017)
* **Darfar** — A western kingdom known for its cannibalistic practices
* **Zembabwei** — A prosperous southern kingdom whose "benevolent kings" desired a monopoly on trade routes

## In the Stories

The Black Kingdoms feature in multiple Conan tales:

> Conan's fame had preceded him, even into distant Keshan; his exploits as a chief of the black corsairs, those wolves of the southern coasts, had made his name known, admired and feared throughout the black kingdoms. (PN 007-015)

In "Jewels of Gwahlur," Conan ventures into the black kingdoms seeking the legendary Teeth of Gwahlur, navigating the political intrigues between Keshan, Punt, and Zembabwei. His reputation as a chief of the Black Corsairs gives him credibility among these southern powers.

## The Black Corsairs

The Black Corsairs were "wolves of the southern coasts" (PN 007-015), pirates operating along the shores of the Black Kingdoms. They were men of mixed origins — "men of many crimes and countries, some born slaves, some escaped criminals" (PN 002-045) — who established themselves as a feared naval power. Conan served as a chief among them, an experience that earned him lasting fame in the region.

## Decline of the Hyborian Age

During the decline of the Hyborian Age, the Black Kingdoms played a significant role. As the Hyborian civilizations collapsed under pressure from Picts in the west and Hyrkanians in the east:

> A tribe of Vanir adventurers had passed along the Pictish coast southward, ravaged ancient Zingara, and come into Stygia, which, oppressed by a cruel aristocratic ruling class, was staggering under the thrusts of the black kingdoms to the south. (PN 017-058)

This suggests the Black Kingdoms were ascending powers in the post-Hyborian world, contributing to the fall of ancient Stygia.
