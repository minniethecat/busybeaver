# Galzai

The Galzai are a hill tribe dwelling in the Himelian Mountains, featured briefly but memorably in Robert E. Howard's [[conan|Conan]] story "The People of the Black Circle." Their village lies along a narrow mountain trail between the Wazuli territory and the Afghuli border, serving as a waypoint in [[conan|Conan]]'s flight through the mountains with the captive Devi [[Yasmina]].

## The Well Trail Encounter

The Galzai appear in the story through a single vivid encounter. Fleeing from the Kshatriya soldiers and the [[wazuli|Wazulis]] after Yar Afzal's death, [[conan|Conan]] and [[yasmina|Yasmina]] cross a mountain trail where [[conan|Conan]] knows a Galzai woman will be fetching water:

> "A Galzai village is at one end of this trail. Their women follow it to a well, for water. You need new garments." [005-XXX]

[[yasmina|Yasmina]]'s court attire — "cloth-of-gold slippers in tatters, her robes and silken undergarments torn to shreds that scarcely held together decently" — is unsuitable for the mountain crags. [[conan|Conan]]'s solution is characteristically direct: buy the clothes off a Galzai villager.

[[conan|Conan]] reassures the anxious [[yasmina|Yasmina]]: "I don't kill women ordinarily; though some of the hill-women are she-wolves" [005-XXX] — a remark that acknowledges both the hardiness of Himelian women and the [[cimmeria|Cimmerian]]'s own code of conduct.

## The Galzai Woman

The Galzai woman who encounters [[conan|Conan]] and [[yasmina|Yasmina]] is described as "a tall, slim Galzai girl, straight as a young sapling, bearing a great empty gourd" [005-XXX]. Her reaction to finding a giant barbarian and a half-naked noblewoman on the trail is one of pure pragmatism. When [[conan|Conan]] offers a gold coin for her garments:

> The response was instant. The girl smiled broadly with surprise and delight, and, with the disdain of a hill-woman for prudish conventions, promptly yanked off her sleeveless embroidered vest, slipped down her wide trousers and stepped out of them, twitched off her wide-sleeved shirt, and kicked off her sandals. Bundling them all in a bunch, she proffered them to Conan, who handed them to the astonished Devi. [005-XXX]

The "disdain of a hill-woman for prudish conventions" captures the cultural gulf between the courtly [[vendhya|Vendhya]]n Devi and the practical mountain tribes. For the Galzai girl, a gold coin — more wealth than she has ever seen — is worth far more than modesty.

## Aftermath and Deception

[[conan|Conan]]'s instructions to the Galzai woman reveal his understanding of tribal psychology:

> "Go back to her village and tell her people that a stranger attacked her. She'll have them on our track, all right. But she had to go on and get the water first; if she dared go back without it, they'd whip the skin off her. That gives us a long start. They'll never catch us." [005-XXX]

This passage reveals two facts about Galzai culture: the severe discipline enforced within the village (a woman who returns without water faces whipping), and the fierce protectiveness that would send warriors pursuing anyone who attacked one of their own.

Later, the Galzai woman encounters [[kerim-shah|Kerim Shah]]'s band and provides crucial intelligence: "we overtook a naked Galzai girl bearing a gourd of water, who told us a tale of having been stripped and ravished by a giant foreigner in the garb of an Afghuli chief" [005-XXX] — repeating the story [[conan|Conan]] planted and confirming the [[cimmeria|Cimmerian]]'s westward route.

## Culture and Position

The Galzai are typical of the smaller Himelian hill tribes — village-dwellers whose women fetch water from mountain springs, whose warriors respond swiftly to threats against their own, and whose existence is shaped by the harsh realities of mountain life. They are minor players in the power politics of the region, overshadowed by the [[afghuli|Afghulis]] and [[wazuli|Wazulis]], but they command their own stretch of mountain territory and maintain their own codes of honor and discipline.
