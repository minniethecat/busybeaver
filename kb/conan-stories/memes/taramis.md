## Overview

Taramis is the legitimate queen of [[Khauran]], a small Hyborian kingdom situated on the borders of the civilized lands. A woman of dignity, beauty, and noble character, she is beloved by her people — a fact that makes her usurpation all the more tragic. Her story is one of the most dramatic identity-theft narratives in the Conan saga: she is replaced by her own twin sister, the witch [[Salome]], and must endure imprisonment while her kingdom is plundered under her stolen name.

> "Taramis, queen of Khauran, awakened from a dream-haunted slumber to a silence that seemed more like the stillness of nighted catacombs than the normal quiet of a sleeping palace." [006-002]

## The Twin Sisters

Taramis was born a twin, but was raised believing her sister had died in infancy. In truth, the second child was [[Salome]] — marked at birth with the crimson crescent, the witch-mark that signaled her cursed nature under the ancient royal line of Khauran [006-025]. The curse of the kings of Khauran dictates that when twin daughters are born to the royal house, one shall be marked as a witch and left to die in the desert [006-026]. Salome was taken by a desert chief instead, and grew to womanhood consumed by hatred for the sister who enjoyed the life she was denied.

> "'The mark of the witch!' cried Taramis, recoiling." [006-025]

Where Taramis embodies all the virtues of a rightful queen — justice, compassion, dignity — Salome is her dark mirror: lustful, cruel, and driven by a consuming desire to destroy the sister who received everything she was denied. Taramis's own face, seen in Salome, is transformed: "the features were her own! She might have been looking into a mirror which subtly altered her reflection, lending it a tigerish gleam of eye, a vindictive curl of lip" [006-003].

## The Usurpation

The doom of Khauran is set in motion when Taramis, acting in good faith, permits [[Constantius]] — a Kothic voivode commanding a company of Shemitish mercenaries — to pass along the borders of Khauran on his way to Turan [006-041]. Constantius had previously asked for Taramis's hand in marriage, a proposal she indignantly refused [006-047]. Unknown to her, Constantius was an agent sent by Salome to position his forces near Khauran.

> "Certainly I refused!" exclaimed Taramis angrily. "Do you, an Askhaurian yourself, suppose that the Queen of Khauran could treat such a proposal with anything but disdain?" [006-047]

On the night of the blood-red crescent moon, Salome uses sorcery to drug Taramis's ladies-in-waiting and dismiss the palace guards — who mistake her for their beloved queen [006-014][006-016]. She confronts Taramis in her bedchamber, revealing their kinship through a display of malevolent magic:

> "Call me Taramis," answered Salome. "We must become accustomed to it." [006-071]

Taramis is given no chance to warn her people. The palace gates are thrown open to Constantius's mercenaries, who had been encamped near the capital [006-061][006-063]. With cruel mockery, Salome flings her sister into the arms of the Kothic soldiers:

> With a wicked laugh Salome flung her sister into the Kothic captain's arms. [006-079]

## The Stolen Throne

Salome rules Khauran in Taramis's name. To the bewildered people of Khauran, their queen has inexplicably transformed — becoming cruel, debauched, and possessed of a strange demonic energy. The real Taramis is imprisoned in the dungeons beneath the palace, while her impostor issues decrees that strip Khauran of its wealth and fighting men. The army of Khauran, on the false queen's orders, is disbanded — leaving the kingdom defenseless before Constantius and his mercenaries [006-094].

> "It's like a nightmare! Taramis, whom all Khauran loves, betraying her people to that Kothic swine!" [006-084]

The people suffer terribly under the occupation. Constantius and his men "glut the insatiable lust" of their greed, plundering a helpless population [006-152]. A reign of terror settles over Khauran, with the false Taramis standing on the palace steps, watching impassively as Khauran's loyal defenders are massacred [006-099].

> "Fright widened Taramis's lovely eyes, her supple figure rigid against Constantius." [006-080]

## Imprisonment and Endurance

In her dungeon cell, the real Taramis endures months of captivity with extraordinary dignity. When Salome visits her prison to gloat, Taramis refuses to give her sister the satisfaction of tears: "You will get no more tears from me," answered Taramis. "Too often you have reveled in the spectacle of the queen of Khauran weeping in her chains" [006-173].

Salome torments Taramis with the knowledge that the people hate her — believing their beloved queen has become a monster. When Taramis learns that [[Conan]] publicly proclaimed that she was an impostor, she is momentarily heartened: "But he knew! He knew the truth when he said I was not Taramis. How would the people rise against Constantius and his wolves?" [006-180]. Yet Salome cruelly extinguishes this hope by revealing that Conan had been captured and crucified [006-178].

> "Taramis cried out as if a blade had pierced her heart." [006-178]

Despite the psychological and physical torment, Taramis remains unbroken. When Salome visits her one last time — bearing news that Conan is dead and that she intends to sacrifice Taramis to her demon-god — Taramis faces her with quiet defiance: "Taramis met that tigerish glare without flinching" [006-171].

## The Rescue

The turning point comes when Conan, who miraculously survived his crucifixion, returns to Khauran at the head of a band of desert raiders — the Zuagirs. He has forged an alliance with the outlaws and refugees who fled Constantius's tyranny, promising them the plunder of the palace as payment for their aid [006-215][006-219]. Conan's plan is twofold: assault the city to draw Constantius's forces into battle, while a small party infiltrates the dungeon to rescue the true queen [006-243][006-245].

> "This is what we must do: rescue Taramis while the battle rages!" [006-243]

Valerius, a loyal young Khauran soldier, leads the rescue party into the dungeon. They find Taramis in the most secret of the underground chambers, a pit whose only entrance is a trapdoor hidden beneath the altar of Salome's forbidden temple. The light of a torch rouses "Taramis, Queen of Khauran, from the slumber of exhaustion" [006-283].

> "Taramis! Oh, my Queen!" — Valerius, upon finding the imprisoned queen [006-284]

But the rescue is intercepted. Salome and the priest of her dark cult seize Taramis and drag her toward the altar — "She means to give Taramis to the devil she worships!" [006-301]. In a desperate struggle, Conan bursts in and hurls the priest into the sacrificial pit. He then seizes Salome and casts her after her servant. Taramis faints from the ordeal, and Conan carries her from the dungeon:

> "Taramis had fainted. She hung like a limp figure of ivory over the Cimmerian's shoulder." [006-317]

## Aftermath

With the true Taramis restored and Constantius's forces broken, Khauran is liberated. In the final confrontation, as the people see both Taramises — the real queen and her dead twin — there is a moment of utter bewilderment:

> "By Crom, what is this? The real Taramis! But who is that yonder?" [006-324]

Conan, having broken Constantius and avenged his own crucifixion, departs as suddenly as he arrived. Taramis resumes her throne, her ordeal having proven her mettle — she who was wronged never broke, and whose people never truly stopped loving her. Valerius escorts her across the square to begin the rebuilding of her shattered kingdom [006-332].

## Character and Traits

Taramis is defined by her innate nobility — a quality so fundamental that it survives imprisonment, torture, and the theft of her identity. She is compassionate toward her people and fiercely protective of her kingdom's sovereignty, as demonstrated by her refusal to allow foreign mercenaries to occupy Khauran [006-047]. Her courage is not the battlefield valor of a [[Valeria]] or the wild ferocity of a [[Bêlit]]; it is the quiet, enduring courage of a woman who faces the worst and refuses to be diminished by it.

Physically, Taramis is described as beautiful — "Taramis was still beautiful, in spite of her imprisonment and hard usage" [006-168] — with lustrous black hair and a delicately molded face that her sister shares but twists into cruelty. Her beauty is the beauty of grace, not seduction; the narrator emphasizes her dignity even in the most degrading circumstances.

## Relationships

- [[Salome]] — twin sister and mortal enemy; the witch who usurps Taramis's throne, torments her in prison, and ultimately attempts to sacrifice her to a demon
- [[Conan]] — the Cimmerian who recognizes Salome's deception, is crucified for it, survives, and returns to rescue the true queen and liberate Khauran
- [[Constantius]] — the Kothic voivode whose mercenaries occupy Khauran; Taramis refuses his marriage proposal, which sets the plot in motion
- [[Valerius]] — the loyal young Khauran soldier who leads the rescue party into the dungeon and unfailingly serves his true queen
- [[Khauran]] — the small Hyborian kingdom over which Taramis rules as legitimate queen; its people love her deeply
- [[Ishtar]] — the goddess Taramis invokes in her moments of deepest distress
