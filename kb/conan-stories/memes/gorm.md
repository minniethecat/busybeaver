# Gorm

## Overview

Gorm was the Pictish chief who transformed his people from scattered, feuding clans into a unified military power capable of challenging — and eventually overthrowing — the Hyborian kingdoms. Howard explicitly compares him to history's great barbarian conquerors: "Gorm cannot be explained, any more than Genghis Khan, Othman, Attila, or any of those individuals, who, born in naked lands among untutored barbarians, yet possess the instinct for conquest and empire-building" (017-034).

## Rise to Power

When the Nemedian priest Arus arrived among the Picts, Gorm was already a chief of "more than usual intelligence" (017-034). Howard describes him as "the black-haired chief, in his tiger-skins and necklace of human teeth, squatting on the dirt floor of the wattle hut" — an image of raw barbarism, yet beneath it lay a mind of extraordinary strategic capacity. Gorm listened intently to Arus's teachings, but "with the unerring instinct of the barbarian, passed over his words regarding gods and their teachings, and fixed on the material powers thus vividly described" (017-035).

Where Arus saw moral uplift, Gorm saw military potential. The iron-working that Arus taught as a tool of civilization became, in Gorm's hands, the foundation of empire. "There in that mud-floored wattle hut, with the silk-robed priest on the mahogany block, and the dark-skinned chief crouching in his tiger-hides, was laid the foundations of empire" (017-035).

## Unification of the Picts

Gorm's genius was as much political as military. "By virtue of this new craft, he began to assert his dominance over other clans, partly by war, partly by craft and diplomacy, in which latter art he excelled all other barbarians" (017-038). He sent Pictish warriors to serve in Aquilonia's mercenary armies, where they "returned with more information as to armor-forging and sword-making" and "good ideas of civilized warfare, and that contempt for civilization which arises from familiarity with it" (017-039)(017-040).

"By intrigues and forays too numerous and devious to enumerate, Gorm became chief of chiefs, the nearest approach to a king the Picts had had in thousands of years" (017-040). He had waited long — past middle age — but when he finally moved, it was not in trade but in war.

## The Conquest

Under Gorm's leadership, the Picts burst upon the Bossonian frontiers "in scale-mail, wielding weapons of keen steel" (017-042). The irony of Arus's mission was complete: Gorm wore "a corselet of silvered mail now, instead of the tiger-skin, but underneath he was unchanged — the everlasting barbarian, unmoved by theology or philosophy, his instincts fixed unerringly on rapine and plunder" (017-041). The campaign Gorm began would, over generations, culminate in the overthrow of the entire Hyborian civilization.
