# Muriela

Muriela is a Corinthian dancing-girl who appears in Robert E. Howard's Conan story "Jewels of Gwahlur" (published in *Weird Tales*, March 1935). She is coerced by the Stygian agent Zargheba into impersonating the oracle Yelaya as part of a scheme to steal the legendary Treasure of Gwahlur from the temple of [[Keshan]]. Her role as a reluctant imposter places her at the center of the story's web of deceptions.

## Background and Arrival at the Temple

Muriela enters the narrative as Zargheba's unwilling agent. She is a Corinthian dancing-girl—a performer of the taverns and courts of the Hyborian world—who has been pressed into service. Conan recognizes her on sight, connecting her to a figure from the criminal underworld:

> "I've seen you somewhere. You're Muriela, Zargheba's Corinthian dancing-girl." [007-085]

Her association with Zargheba marks her as a participant in the Stygian's scheme, but her role is that of a pawn, not a willing conspirator. She has been brought to the abandoned temple in the mountains of Keshan to impersonate Yelaya, the long-dead oracle whose preserved body sits on a golden dais.

## The Oracle Deception

The central conceit of Muriela's role is that she must convince the priests of Keshan—and their rival [[Gorulga]]—that Yelaya has returned to life to speak prophecies. She is placed in the oracle chamber, where she sits in the darkness behind the oracle's dais:

> Muriela sat upright on the dais, her eyes wide and staring... [007-140]

The strain of the impersonation is immense. Conan, watching from a hidden niche, recognizes the danger:

> Terrific would be the strain on Muriela. If she snapped— [007-141]

Muriela delivers the false prophecies under the guidance of Zargheba's whispered instructions, her voice carrying to the assembled priests [007-143]. The deception works for a time, but the pressure of maintaining the illusion under the scrutiny of the priests takes its toll.

## Crisis and Unmasking

The impersonation begins to unravel when the situation grows chaotic. Conan must physically intervene to protect Muriela from discovery:

> He grasped the terrified Muriela by the throat, choking her efforts to scream. [007-152]

Her collapse is dramatic—she throws her arms convulsively [007-155], her body appears to lie placid on the dais while terror consumes her [007-163]. The deception is ultimately exposed:

> Muriela!... It's not Muriela! It's Yelaya! [007-166]

The cry from the priests reveals the confusion between the impostor and the dead oracle. A "demoniac scream" had burst from Muriela's lips, signaling the moment the impersonation collapsed [007-167]. Conan shouts her name, trying to reach her through the chaos [007-168].

## Disappearance and Rescue

In the confusion that follows, Muriela vanishes from the oracle chamber. A shred of her torn skirt near a metal door is the only clue to her disappearance [007-170]. Conan realizes that Muriela has been taken captive—or worse—through a hidden passageway.

> On the other side of that metal door Muriela was a captive, if she still lived. [007-176]

Conan's search for her is hampered by his unfamiliarity with the temple's hidden passages [007-185], but he remains committed to finding her. The jewel chest—the Treasure of Gwahlur itself—becomes intertwined with her fate:

> Muriela was on her knees clutching Conan's legs... [007-246]

In the climactic moments, Muriela is reunited with Conan. She carries the jewel chest under her arm as they flee the collapsing temple [007-260]. Her final scream coincides with the temple's destruction:

> Muriela's scream rang terribly above the roar... [007-262]

The story ends with Muriela having survived, though her ordeal leaves her traumatized. She clings to Conan as they escape across the bridge [007-263], one of the fortunate few who walk away with both their lives and some share of the Treasure of Gwahlur.

## Significance

Muriela represents a distinctive character type in Howard's Conan corpus: the reluctant participant in a deception who finds herself in over her head. Unlike the proud noblewomen or fierce warrior women who populate other Conan tales, Muriela is a commoner performer thrust into a supernatural crisis. Her vulnerability and her resourcefulness under pressure make her viewpoint central to the human stakes of "Jewels of Gwahlur." Through Muriela, Howard dramatizes the cost of ambition—both hers and Zargheba's—in a world where ancient treasures are guarded by forces beyond human comprehension.
