# Irakzai

The Irakzai are a hill tribe dwelling in the lower villages of the Himelian Mountains in Robert E. Howard's [[conan|Conan]] story "The People of the Black Circle." They serve as mercenaries and followers of [[Kerim Shah]], the [[turan|Turan]]ian agent, who recruits them with coin for the perilous assault on the sorcerers' stronghold of [[Yimsha]].

## Mercenary Character

The Irakzai are defined by their willingness to fight for pay. [[Yar Afzal]], chief of the [[wazuli|Wazulis]], dismisses them with contempt:

> "These Irakzai are jackals that follow a lion for crumbs. They have been lapping up the coins Kerim Shah scatters among the border tribes to buy men like horses." [005-215]

This characterization paints the Irakzai as mercenaries rather than independent warriors — men who sell their swords to the highest bidder in the complex tribal economy of the Himelians. [[kerim-shah|Kerim Shah]]'s practice of scattering "coins among the border tribes to buy men like horses" [005-215] exploits the economic needs of tribes like the Irakzai, who inhabit the lower villages and lack the wealth and autonomy of the more powerful [[afghuli|Afghulis]].

## First Appearance: The Search for Conan

The Irakzai are first introduced when "thirty Irakzai of the lower villages" ride with the [[dagozai|Dagozai]] scout into the hills to search for [[conan|Conan]] and his captive [005-214]. Their lean, gaunt appearance is characteristic of the hill frontier:

> "Kerim Shah!" muttered Conan, red glints smoldering luridly in his eyes. The Turanian did not struggle; they sat their horses almost breast to breast, Conan's fingers locking the other's sword-arm. Behind Kerim Shah filed a group of lean Irakzai on gaunt horses. They glared like wolves, fingering bows and knives, but rendered uncertain because of the narrowness of the path and the perilous proximity of the abyss that yawned beneath them. [005-263]

The encounter in the narrow mountain path — with the Irakzai "glared like wolves" [005-263] — captures their predatory hunger and battlefield readiness. Even when cornered by [[conan|Conan]]'s superior position, they finger their weapons, weighing the odds.

## The Choice: Follow or Die

When [[kerim-shah|Kerim Shah]] turns to his Irakzai followers and offers them the choice between the supernatural terror of Yimsha and the mundane death awaiting them from the pursuing [[afghuli|Afghulis]], their response is the fatalism of men already marked for death:

> Kerim Shah turned to the silent Irakzai and spoke briefly: "This chief and I are going to Yimsha to fight the wizards. Will you go with us, or stay here to be flayed by the Afghulis who are following this man?"
>
> They looked at him with eyes grimly fatalistic. They were doomed and they knew it—had known it ever since the singing arrows of the ambushed Dagozai had driven them back from the pass of Shalizah. The men of the lower Zhaibar had too many reeking bloodfeuds among the crag-dwellers. They were too small a band to fight their way back through the hills to the villages of the border, without the guidance of the crafty Turanian. They counted themselves as dead already, so they made the reply that only dead men would make: "We will go with thee and die on Yimsha." [005-291]

This passage reveals the Irakzai's tragic position — caught between the bloodfeuds of the hill tribes, the Afghuli pursuit, and the sorcerous defenses of Yimsha, they are doomed regardless of their choice.

## The Gauntlet of Sorcery

The Irakzai endure terrors during the ascent of Yimsha that transcend normal warfare. Each death strips away another warrior until only a handful remain:

### The Steel-Winged Hawk

> But they were not within bowshot of the tower when something shot down out of the sky without warning. It passed so close to Conan that he felt the wind of rushing wings, but it was an Irakzai who staggered and fell, blood jetting from a severed jugular. A hawk with wings like burnished steel shot up again, blood dripping from the scimitar-beak, to reel against the sky as Kerim Shah's bowstring twanged. It dropped like a plummet, but no man saw where it struck the earth. [005-331]

The first Irakzai death is swift and inexplicable — a hawk that hunts men.

### The Mad Dog

> It dashed past him and leaped at Kerim Shah. The Turanian leaped aside, and the great dog flung itself upon the Irakzai behind him. The man yelled and threw up his arm, which was torn by the brute's fangs as it bore him backward, and the next instant half a dozen tulwars were hacking at the beast. Yet not until it was literally dismembered did the hideous creature cease its efforts to seize and rend its attackers. [005-319]

A mad village dog, found inexplicably on the barren mountain slopes, attacks the Irakzai with unnatural ferocity.

### The Puffballs of Flame

> One of the Irakzai reached out and thrust his sword into the unstable mass. Instantly a sharp report shook the mountainside. There was a burst of blinding flame, and then the puffball had vanished, and the too-curious warrior remained only a heap of charred and blackened bones. The crisped hand still gripped the ivory sword-hilt, but the blade was gone—melted and destroyed by that awful heat. Yet men standing almost within reach of the victim had not suffered except to be dazzled and half blinded by the sudden flare. [005-336]

This death — the warrior reduced to charred bones, his blade melted — is perhaps the most horrifying, demonstrating the [[black-seers-of-yimsha|Black Seers]]' command of diabolical forces.

### The Bellowing Horn

> The Irakzai screamed, reeling like drunken men on the shuddering slope, and Conan, eyes glaring, charged recklessly up the incline, knife in hand, straight at the door that showed in the tower-wall. Above him the great horn roared and bellowed in brutish mockery. [005-343]

The horn of the acolytes shakes the very mountainside, disorienting the Irakzai warriors who have no defense against sonic [[magic-in-the-hyborian-age|sorcery]].

## The Final Four

Despite these terrors, the Irakzai fight with "vengeful" determination, their "fatalism momentarily submerged by temporary triumph" [005-345] as they pursue the acolytes up the mountain. They cross the shimmering mist-filled chasm on the golden thread, engage in the knife-fight on the precipice lip, and press on to the castle of the [[black-seers-of-yimsha|Black Seers]].

Of the original thirty Irakzai mentioned earlier in the story [005-214], only four survive the assault [005-349]:

> Then the conquerors shook blood and sweat from their eyes, and looked at one another. Conan and Kerim Shah still stood upright, and four of the Irakzai. [005-349]

The four survivors stand at the gates of the [[black-seers-of-yimsha|Black Seers]]' castle — "dumb, like men marching to a certain doom" [005-350] — having lost twenty-six of their comrades to [[magic-in-the-hyborian-age|sorcery]] and steel. Their survival is testimony to a courage that transcends their mercenary origins.
