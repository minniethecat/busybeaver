## Overview

The Xuchotlans are the mysterious original builders of the walled city of Xuchotl, an ancient metropolis hidden deep in the southern jungles. Long extinct by the time of the Conan stories, the Xuchotlans left behind a vast enclosed city of cyclopean architecture, a sealed world whose streets and chambers ran beneath a single great roof.

> "The whole city is built like one giant palace under one great roof. The nearest approach to a street is the Great Hall which traverses the city from the north gate to the south gate." [[1]](#ref-1)

## The Lost City

Xuchotl was an architectural wonder — a single enormous structure with no open streets, squares, or courtyards. Its only doors to the outside world were the city gates, through which no living person had passed in over fifty years by the time Conan and Valeria discovered it.

The catacombs beneath Xuchotl held the bones of terrible kings who ruled in the "black centuries of the past," and the wizards who once inhabited the city left behind artifacts of dreadful power — including a crystal skull that could freeze the blood and wither the brain of any who gazed upon it unprepared.

> "Madness and death lurk in it. The wizards of Xotalanc understand its secret—they found it in the catacombs, where lie the bones of terrible kings who ruled in Xuchotl in the black centuries of the past." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Fate

The original Xuchotlan civilization had fallen long before the Conan era. The city lay mostly empty and silent when the [[tlazitlans|Tlazitlans]] — a refugee tribe of mongrel Stygian stock — discovered it after fleeing a failed rebellion against the Stygian king. The Tlazitlans conquered the remaining Xuchotlans, took their women, and settled in the city.

The Xuchotlan descendants who survived were absorbed into the Tlazitlan population, creating a hybrid culture divided into warring factions — the [[tecuhltli|Tecuhltli]] and the [[xotalancas|Xotalancas]] — whose endless feud eventually consumed the city.

## Significance

The Xuchotlans represent one of Howard's themes of lost civilizations and fallen races. Their cyclopean architecture and mysterious artifacts evoke the sense of a forgotten age, and their sealed, labyrinthine city serves as the setting for one of Conan's most claustrophobic adventures. The Xuchotlan legacy — particularly their sorcerous artifacts — drives the bloody feud that destroys the last inhabitants of Xuchotl.
