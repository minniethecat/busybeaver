## Overview

The Zembabwans are the inhabitants of Zembabwei, a great hybrid trading kingdom far to the east of Keshan. Described as a powerful and ambitious people, the Zembabwans feature prominently in the geopolitical maneuvering surrounding the legendary treasure known as the [[teeth-of-gwahlur|Teeth of Gwahlur]].

> "The kings of Zembabwei would move. Punt would be invaded simultaneously from the east and the west." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Role in the Teeth of Gwahlur

The Zembabwans are central to the conspiracy in "Jewels of Gwahlur." Their kings, advised by the Stygian rogue [[Thutmekri]], proposed an alliance with [[Keshan]] to jointly conquer [[punt|Punt]]. However, their true goal was not Punt itself but the fabled [[teeth-of-gwahlur|Teeth of Gwahlur]] — a treasure hidden in the lost city of Alkmeenon.

> "The conquest of Punt was but one move toward their real goal — the capture of the Teeth of Gwahlur." [[1]](#ref-1)

The Zembabwans' strategy was calculated and ruthless: they intended to let the Keshani do most of the fighting against Punt, and then, when both Keshan and Punt were exhausted, crush both races and seize the treasure by force.

> "The Zembabwans would see to it that the Keshani did most of the fighting, and then, when both Punt and Keshan were exhausted from the struggle the Zembabwans would crush both races, loot Keshan and take the treasure by force." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Diplomatic Maneuvering

The Zembabwan embassy to Keshan was a carefully orchestrated deception. Their agent Thutmekri, a Stygian adventurer and rogue, represented the twin kings of Zembabwei. The embassy brought with it a veiled oracle — actually a girl named Muriela — who was instructed to manipulate the king of Keshan into handing over sacred jewels as a pledge, giving the Zembabwans direct access to the treasure.

> "Thutmekri was a Stygian, an adventurer and a rogue whose wits had recommended him to the twin kings of the great hybrid trading kingdom." [[2]](#ref-2)

## Relationship with Surrounding Peoples

Before the events of "Jewels of Gwahlur," the Zembabwans had already demonstrated their expansionist nature. Their traders had established fortresses in Punt, which the Puntians subsequently expelled and burned, setting the stage for the proposed invasion.

> "Punt — which kingdom, incidentally, lying east of Keshan, had recently expelled the Zembabwan traders and burned their fortresses." [[2]](#ref-2)

The Zembabwans are described as a "hybrid" people, reflecting the racial mixing characteristic of the southern and eastern regions of the Hyborian world, much like the mixed populations of the [[black-kingdoms]].
