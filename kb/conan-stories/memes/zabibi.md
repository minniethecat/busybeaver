## Overview

Zabibi presents herself to [[Conan]] as a dancing-girl in the streets of Zamboula, pleading for his protection and aid. Her tale of a lover driven mad by the priest [[Totrasmek]] is a carefully constructed deception — for "Zabibi" is in truth Nafertari, the mistress of the satrap [[Jungir Khan]] himself, and the mad lover she seeks to rescue is no mere Turanian soldier but the satrap of Zamboula, drugged by Totrasmek's sorcery [009-219]. Her dual identity drives the intrigue of "Shadows in Zamboula," making her both damsel in distress and political schemer.

> "I am not Zabibi. I am Nafertari. And he is not Alafdhal, a poor captain of the guard. He is Jungir Khan, satrap of Zamboula." [009-219]

## The False Dancing-Girl

Conan first encounters Zabibi in the nighted streets of Zamboula, after he has escaped the cannibal trap of Aram Baksh's tavern. She has just been rescued from Darfar cannibals who seized her in the darkness, and she clings to the Cimmerian for protection [009-064]. Introducing herself as "Zabibi, a dancing-girl" [009-094], she tells Conan a story of her lover Alafdhal, a young Turanian officer driven mad by a drug given to her by Totrasmek, the high priest of Hanuman.

> "Totrasmek desired me, and because I repulsed him, he made me the innocent tool of his vengeance against Alafdhal. I asked a love potion of Totrasmek... He gave me a drug to mix with my lover's wine... But having drunk, my lover went raving mad" [009-094]

The story is a clever fabrication. It contains enough truth — Totrasmek's desire for Nafertari, the drug that drove her lover mad — to be convincing, while concealing the fact that her lover is not a nameless soldier but the satrap of Zamboula. By posing as a common dancing-girl, she ensures Conan's help without revealing the political stakes of her situation [009-094].

## A Plea to the Cimmerian

Zabibi's plea is both desperate and calculated. She needs a man of Conan's strength and recklessness to enter Totrasmek's temple and recover the antidote that will cure her lover's madness. She knows that the people of Zamboula fear Totrasmek and will not act against him — even the Pelishtim mercenaries and the priests of Set would hesitate to cross the high priest of Hanuman [009-127].

> "You are a man! Help me! Totrasmek must die! Slay him for me!" [009-104]

She leads Conan to Totrasmek's chambers through a secret route, demonstrating her intimate knowledge of the city's hidden ways — a knowledge befitting the satrap's mistress rather than a common dancing-girl. She presses close to him in the darkness, her trembling body betraying genuine terror even as her words manipulate [009-126].

## In Totrasmek's Trap

Zabibi's plan goes awry when she and Conan are captured in Totrasmek's chambers. The priest has anticipated their coming, and his servant [[Baal-pteor]] drags Zabibi through a secret opening while Conan is left to fight for his life [009-171]. She becomes Totrasmek's captive, and the priest forces her to dance for his amusement — a cruel irony for a woman who had posed as a dancing-girl to mask her true station [009-193].

> "Sick with shame and terror Zabibi heard the hateful mirth of her captor" as she was forced to perform [009-193]

Conan's timely intervention saves her. He kills Totrasmek by hurling a heavy stool that strikes the priest down mid-ritual [009-196], then slays Baal-pteor in brutal hand-to-hand combat. With both of her enemies dead, Zabibi recovers the antidote from Totrasmek's body and administers it to her lover [009-215].

## The Truth Revealed

Only after the crisis has passed does Zabibi reveal her true identity. With Totrasmek dead and the antidote in hand, she has no further need of deception.

> "The truth," she said. "I am not Zabibi. I am Nafertari. And he is not Alafdhal, a poor captain of the guard. He is Jungir Khan, satrap of Zamboula." [009-219]

The revelation reframes the entire preceding adventure. The "dancing-girl" who pleaded in the streets was the most powerful woman in Zamboula — the mistress whose influence over Jungir Khan was whispered to rival that of the satrap himself. Totrasmek's plot had aimed not merely at petty revenge against a dancing-girl's lover, but at destabilizing the entire satrapy: by driving Jungir Khan mad, the priest planned to provoke revolt and rioting that would bring down Turanian rule in Zamboula [009-221].

## The Cure and Aftermath

Zabibi administers the antidote — the black lotus — to the unconscious Jungir Khan, and the satrap awakens cured of his madness [009-215]. The crisis averted, she resumes her true identity as Nafertari and returns to the palace with her restored lover. Conan, having been used as an unwitting pawn in the politics of Zamboula, departs the city — but not before Jungir Khan, recognizing the debt owed, sees to it that the Cimmerian is well-provisioned for his journey [009-251].

Zabibi — Nafertari — has played a dangerous game, risking her life and her identity to save the man she loves and, by extension, the stability of Zamboula itself.

## Relationships

- [[Conan]] — rescuer and unwitting pawn in Nafertari's scheme; she manipulates him through a false identity to secure his aid against Totrasmek
- [[Jungir Khan]] — her lover, satrap of Zamboula, whom she poses as "Alafdhal, a poor captain of the guard" to conceal his true identity; she risks everything to cure his drug-induced madness
- [[Totrasmek]] — high priest of Hanuman who desires her and, when repulsed, drugs Jungir Khan to madness as part of a plot to overthrow the satrapy
- [[Baal-pteor]] — Totrasmek's Kosalan servant who captures Zabibi in the priest's trap; slain by Conan
