## Overview

The Teeth of Gwahlur are a legendary treasure said to be held in the lost city of Alkmeenon in the kingdom of [[keshan|Keshan]]. They are sacred jewels of great value and religious significance, guarded by the Oracle of Alkmeenon — the mummified body of Princess Yelaya, the last ruler of the ancient city, who was believed to speak prophecies.

The treasure is the central MacGuffin of the story "Jewels of Gwahlur," driving the plot through multiple competing factions — Conan, the Stygian agent Thutmekri, the priests of Keshan, and the kings of Zembabwei — all seeking to possess them.

## Location: Alkmeenon

The Teeth are stored in the crypt of Alkmeenon, an abandoned city in the hills of Keshan:

> "Take the Teeth of Gwahlur from the crypt where they were placed so long ago. Alkmeenon is no longer holy, because it has been desecrated by blasphemers. Give the Teeth of Gwahlur into the hands of Thutmekri, the Stygian, to place in the sanctuary of Dragon and Derketo. Only this can save Keshan from the wrath of the gods." (PN 007-205)

The city of Alkmeenon itself has not been visited by priests for a century before the events of the story, and the Teeth were placed there "so long ago" that their exact nature is shrouded in legend.

## Political Significance

The Teeth of Gwahlur are more than a treasure — they are a political bargaining chip. Thutmekri, the Stygian agent, sought to obtain them as a pledge to convince the kings of Zembabwei to support his plans:

> Conan could predict Thutmekri's plans from his knowledge of the man. He knew that it had been Thutmekri who had proposed the conquest of Punt to the kings of Zembabwei, which conquest was but one move toward their real goal — the capture of the Teeth of Gwahlur. (PN 007-042)

The Zembabwei kings desired "a monopoly of the trade routes" and the Teeth served as leverage in the multi-sided diplomatic intrigue involving Keshan, Punt, and Zembabwei.

## The Oracle of Alkmeenon

The Teeth are guarded by the Oracle, believed to be Princess Yelaya, the last ruler of Alkmeenon:

> This was an awesome thing, and it caused tongues to wag excitedly in palace and beehive hut. Not for a century had the priests visited the silent city. The oracle, men said, was the Princess Yelaya, the last ruler of Alkmeenon, who had died in the full bloom of her youth and beauty, and whose body had been preserved by the arts of her priests. (PN 007-020)

In the story, Conan discovers that the oracle is a fraud — an elaborate scheme by a man calling himself Bît-Yakin to deceive the priests of Keshan and exploit the legend for his own purposes.

## In the Story

In "Jewels of Gwahlur," Conan travels to Keshan to find the Teeth, using his reputation as a commander of the Black Corsairs to gain employment with the local ruler. Muriela, a slave girl whom Conan enlists to impersonate the oracle Yelaya, becomes key to his plan. The story follows Conan as he navigates the oracle's chamber, the crypts, and a rival scheme by the priest Gorulga, culminating in the discovery of the truth behind the oracle and the fate of the treasure.
