# Wazulis

The Wazulis are a hill tribe dwelling in the village of Khurum in the Himelian Mountains, featured in Robert E. Howard's [[conan|Conan]] story "The People of the Black Circle." Led by their chief [[Yar Afzal]], the Wazulis occupy a strategic position in the mountain passes between [[vendhya|Vendhya]] and the farther eastern lands, making them important players in the political intrigues of the region.

## The Khurum Wazulis

The Wazulis inhabit the village of [[Khurum]], situated in the mountains between the [[vendhya|Vendhya]]n frontier and [[afghuli|Afghulis]]tan. Their chief, Yar Afzal, rules "the Khurum Wazulis" [005-199], a specific branch of the broader Wazuli tribal grouping. The village serves as a waypoint for travelers crossing the Himelians.

The Wazulis are distinct from the [[Afghulis]], their neighbors to the west. Yar Afzal acknowledges this separation explicitly, noting that "[[afghuli|Afghulis]]tan lies a good many miles farther on to the west" [005-199] from Wazuli territory. Despite the geographical separation, Yar Afzal identifies the [[afghuli|Afghulis]] as kinsmen — "he is our kinsman from [[iranistan|Iranistan]]" [005-215] — suggesting shared ancestry among the Himelian hill tribes.

## Hosting Conan and the Devi

When [[Conan]] arrives in Khurum with the captive Devi [[Yasmina]], Yar Afzal receives them with a measure of tribal hospitality. The chief chambers the Devi in his own hut, and the Wazuli warriors gather to assess the prize their chief has acquired:

> "A rare prize, Conan; fit mate for a chief of the Afghulis." [005-180]

The scene in Yar Afzal's hut illustrates the formal semicircle gathering of tribal decision-making, where warriors squat on their haunches to debate the fate of captives and the approach of enemies. This gathering is illuminated by torchlight, with the warriors pressing close to examine the captive — "There was a sucking intake of breath about the ring" [005-183] when her identity is revealed.

## Tribal Politics

The Wazulis occupy a precarious position in the mountain power structure. They are not strong enough to challenge the [[vendhya|Vendhyan kingdom]] directly, but their control of mountain passes gives them leverage over travelers and fugitives. When [[conan|Conan]] arrives in Khurum with the captive Devi [[Yasmina]], the Wazulis see an opportunity for ransom:

> "They wish me to murder you and take the girl to hold for ransom. They say why should the Afghuli dogs profit by her, when it is the people who take the risk of guarding her?" [005-205]

This tension between Wazuli and Afghuli interests reflects the broader tribal dynamics of the mountains, where kin groups compete for the spoils of raiding and hostage-taking. The Wazulis' relationship with the [[vendhya|Vendhyan kingdom]] is equally fraught — they are not loved by the Zhaibari and fear that rival tribes will aid the Kshatriya soldiers hunting for the Devi:

> "Are we to squat on our haunches until they smoke us out? They will learn at last in which Wazuli village the wench is held. We are not loved by the Zhaibari; they will help the Kshatriyas hunt us out." [005-233]

Yar Afzal's response — "Let them come. We can hold the defiles against a host" [005-234] — reflects the confidence of hill tribes in their mountain terrain, where narrow passes nullify the numerical advantages of the settled kingdoms' armies.

## Yar Afzal and Conan

The relationship between the Wazulis and [[conan|Conan]] is mediated through Yar Afzal, who hosts the [[cimmeria|Cimmerian]] in his hut. Yar Afzal acknowledges [[conan|Conan]]'s standing and treats him with a measure of respect, but the alliance is fragile. When Yar Afzal is killed by the sorcerer [[Khemsa]], the Wazulis' political calculus shifts, and they become a threat to [[conan|Conan]] rather than an ally.

After Yar Afzal's death, [[conan|Conan]] tells [[yasmina|Yasmina]]: "Now that Yar Afzal is dead, those damned Wazulis will be on our heels" [005-265], indicating that the chief's death dissolves whatever protection the Wazulis had offered.

## The Sole Survivor

A haunting episode in the Wazuli arc involves a lone survivor of the band that pursued [[conan|Conan]] through the gorge behind Khurum village. This man, driven mad by whatever befell his companions, wanders the hills and is eventually discovered by [[Kerim Shah]]:

> "This morning we came upon a Wazuli wandering through the hills. He was quite mad, but I learned much from his incoherent gibberings before he died. I learned that he was the sole survivor of a band which followed a chief of the Afghulis and a captive Kshatriya woman into a gorge behind Khurum village. He babbled much of a man in a green turban whom the Afghuli rode down, but who, when attacked by the Wazulis who pursued, smote them with a nameless doom that wiped them out as a gust of wind-driven fire wipes out a cluster of locusts." [005-270]

This account — of [[khemsa|Khemsa]]'s [[magic-in-the-hyborian-age|sorcery]] annihilating the pursuing Wazuli band — is the only testimony to the massacre, relayed secondhand through a madman's dying words. It underscores the vulnerability of tribal warriors against the supernatural forces at play in the mountains, and provides [[kerim-shah|Kerim Shah]] with the intelligence that sets him on [[conan|Conan]]'s trail toward Yimsha.

## Language and Culture

The Wazuli language belongs to the broader family of Himelian tongues. The Devi [[yasmina|Yasmina]], as part of her royal education, "had been taught the languages of [[iranistan|Iranistan]] and the kindred tongues of Ghulistan" [005-209], and a Wazuli warrior is heard "addressing his chief in the harsh accents of the Wazuli which [[yasmina|Yasmina]] could scarcely understand" [005-209] — suggesting that the Wazuli dialect is distinct enough from court languages to challenge even a linguistically educated monarch.

## In the Stories

The Wazulis appear in "The People of the Black Circle":
- [[conan|Conan]] and [[yasmina|Yasmina]] take shelter in the hut of Yar Afzal in Khurum [005-199]
- The Wazuli warriors pressure Yar Afzal to kill [[conan|Conan]] and ransom [[yasmina|Yasmina]] [005-205]
- Yar Afzal is killed by [[khemsa|Khemsa]]'s [[magic-in-the-hyborian-age|sorcery]], and the surviving Wazulis pursue [[conan|Conan]] through the mountains [005-265]
- The pursuing band is wiped out by [[khemsa|Khemsa]]'s [[magic-in-the-hyborian-age|sorcery]] — a massacre attested only by the mad sole survivor [005-270]
- A sole surviving Wazuli, driven mad, provides [[kerim-shah|Kerim Shah]] with crucial intelligence [005-380]
