## Overview

The **Free Companions** were loose confederations of mercenary soldiers who roamed the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]], selling their swords to the highest bidder across the kingdoms of [[Koth]], [[Zamora]], [[Turan]], and beyond. Numbering as many as five thousand warriors drawn from "a score of races and tribes," they fought as professional sell-swords and, when employment failed, turned to plundering the border territories they once guarded. [[Conan]] himself served among multiple Free Companion bands, rising to the rank of *hetman* (captain) before his later adventures as a corsair and [[kozaki]].

> "I was one of those dissolute rogues, the Free Companions, who burned and looted along the borders. There were five thousand of us, from a score of races and tribes." [002-045]

## Structure

The Free Companions operated as decentralized warbands rather than a unified organization. Each company was led by a *voivode* (warlord) or hetman, typically a [[koth|Kothic]] or [[shem|Shem]]itish commander who contracted the band's services to warring princes and satraps. Rank-and-file membership was open to fighting men of any nationality, and the bands swelled or contracted based on available contracts. When a contract ended and no new patron appeared, the Free Companions routinely turned to brigandage, "burning and looting along the borders" until the next war provided legitimate employment [002-045].

## History

[[conan|Conan]]'s earliest recorded service with the Free Companions was in eastern [[koth|Koth]], where he joined a large company — five thousand strong — hired by a rebel prince. When the prince made peace with his sovereign, the Free Companions found themselves unemployed and turned to raiding the borderlands of [[koth|Koth]], [[zamora|Zamora]], and [[turan|Turan]] simultaneously. This drew the attention of [[shah-amurath|Shah Amurath]] of [[turan|Turan]], who trapped the company near the Ilbars River with fifteen thousand cavalry. In the ensuing massacre, [[conan|Conan]] was one of the few survivors, escaping eastward into the marshes bordering the Vilayet Sea [002-045].

> "A week ago Shah Amurath trapped us near the banks of Ilbars with fifteen thousand men. Mitra! The skies were black with vultures. When the lines broke, after a whole day of fighting, some tried to break through to the north, some to the west. I doubt if any escaped." [002-045]

Later, [[conan|Conan]] joined Zarallo's Free Companions, a company that had marched south to guard the [[darfar|Darfar border]] from the frontier outpost of Sukhmet. The posting was unpopular: "The pay was poor and the wine was sour," [[conan|Conan]] recalled [010-038]. It was at Sukhmet that [[conan|Conan]] encountered [[valeria|Valeria]] of the [[Red Brotherhood]], who had fled there after jumping ship off the [[kush|Kushite]] coast.

Another branch, the Free Companies under the [[koth|Kothic]] voivode [[constantius|Constantius]], operated along the [[koth|Koth]]–[[shem|Shem]] frontier with a force of [[shem|Shem]]itish mercenaries. These men were described as "utterly without mercy or loyalty," typical of the hardened professional soldiers who filled the Free Companion ranks [006-038].

## Notable Members

- **[[Conan]]** — Served as a common soldier and later rose to hetman (captain) among the Free Companions before riding south. His experience commanding mercenary bands would later serve him as a [[kozaki]] hetman and eventually as king of [[Aquilonia]] [005-573].
- **[[valeria|Valeria]]** — Though not a Free Companion herself, the [[aquilonia|Aquilonian]] swordswoman encountered [[conan|Conan]] while he served with Zarallo's company at Sukhmet, where she had fled after abandoning her previous employer [010-039].
- **Zarallo** — Commander of the Free Companions stationed on the [[darfar|Darfar border]]. His company guarded the [[kush|Kushite]] frontier from the outpost of Sukhmet [010-039].
- **[[constantius|Constantius]]** — [[koth|Kothic]] voivode of the Free Companies operating along the [[koth|Koth]]–[[shem|Shem]] borderlands, commanding [[shem|Shem]]itish mercenaries [006-038].

## Rivals and Allies

The Free Companions had a natural rivalry with settled military forces like the [[turan|Turan]]ian cavalry (who destroyed one of their largest companies) and with the standing armies of [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kingdoms who viewed them as undisciplined rabble. Individual companies occasionally coordinated with tribal groups such as the [[Afghuli]] hillmen, though these alliances were opportunistic and short-lived.

The Free Companions occupied a similar niche to the [[kozaki]] of the [[zaporoska|Zaporoska]] steppes — both were multi-ethnic freebooter bands led by elected hetmen — and [[conan|Conan]]'s experience as a Free Companion hetman directly prepared him for his later leadership among the [[kozaki|kozak]]i.

## Real-World Parallels

The Free Companions draw on the historical tradition of *condottieri* (Italian mercenary captains of the Renaissance) and the *free companies* of the Hundred Years' War — bands of professional soldiers who sold their services to warring states and, when peace broke out, became marauding brigands. The title *voivode* is of Slavic origin (meaning "warlord" or "duke"), reflecting the pseudo-historical Eurasian setting of the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]].
