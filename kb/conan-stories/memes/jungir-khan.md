## Overview

Jungir Khan is the Turanian satrap who rules the desert trading-town of Zamboula with his mistress [[Zabibi|Nafertari]]. Though a figure of considerable political authority, Jungir Khan spends most of "Shadows in Zamboula" incapacitated — drugged into raving madness by the high priest [[Totrasmek]], who seeks to destabilize the satrapy and provoke a revolt. His rescue by [[Conan]] through the intervention of Nafertari, disguised as the dancing-girl [[Zabibi]], drives the central intrigue of the story.

> "He is Jungir Khan, satrap of Zamboula." — Nafertari reveals her lover's true identity to Conan [009-219]

## The Dual Identity: Alafdhal and Jungir Khan

Throughout the first half of "Shadows in Zamboula," Jungir Khan is known to Conan — and to the reader — only as "Alafdhal," a supposedly poor Turanian captain of the guardsmen [009-094]. This is a deliberate fiction crafted by Nafertari to conceal the political stakes of her situation: by presenting her lover as a low-ranking soldier, she ensures Conan's aid without revealing that the man driven mad by Totrasmek's drug is in fact the satrap of Zamboula [009-094].

The name "Alafdhal" is a mask, a protective lie that shields Jungir Khan's true identity while Nafertari navigates the dangerous politics of a city where even the satrap fears the priest of Hanuman. Only once Conan has killed Totrasmek and recovered the antidote does Nafertari drop the pretense, revealing the supposedly poor captain as the most powerful man in Zamboula [009-219].

## Captured and Drugged by Totrasmek

Jungir Khan's predicament is the result of Totrasmek's ambitious plot against the satrapy. The high priest of Hanuman desired Nafertari, and when she repulsed him, he gave her a drug to administer to her lover under the pretense of a love potion [009-094]. Jungir Khan drank the mixture and was immediately driven into raving madness — a state that rendered him incapable of governing and threatened to plunge Zamboula into chaos.

> "All in Zamboula fear him, even Jungir Khan and Nafertari." — the narrator on Totrasmek's power over the city [009-127]

Totrasmek's objective went beyond personal revenge: by incapacitating Jungir Khan, he planned to provoke rioting and revolt that would bring down Turanian rule in Zamboula [009-221]. The satrap, drugged and helpless, became a pawn in the priest's scheme — and his only hope lay in the deception Nafertari wove to bring Conan into the conflict.

Jungir Khan's ring — a token of his office — was stolen by Totrasmek or his servant [[Baal-pteor]] during the drugging [009-202], further stripping the satrap of the symbols of his authority.

## His Relationship with Nafertari

Jungir Khan's relationship with Nafertari defines his role in the story. She is his mistress, and her influence over the satrap was whispered to rival that of Jungir Khan himself [009-094]. It is Nafertari who takes charge when the satrap is incapacitated: she adopts the false identity of Zabibi, braves the cannibal-haunted streets of Zamboula, and enlists Conan's aid against Totrasmek — all to save the man she loves.

Her devotion is unmistakable. When Conan observes her during their journey to Totrasmek's chambers, the narrator notes that "she obviously loved Alafdhal sincerely" [009-113] — a love that drives a woman of high station to risk disgrace, capture, and death among the city's dangers. Jungir Khan may be the satrap, but Nafertari is the agent of his rescue, and the dynamic between the powerful ruler reduced to helplessness and the mistress who becomes his protector is a key thread in the story's fabric.

## Rescue and Restoration

Jungir Khan's rescue is achieved entirely through the actions of others. While he lies raving and unconscious, Nafertari guides Conan to Totrasmek's chambers, endures capture herself, and ultimately recovers the antidote — the black lotus — from the dead priest's body [009-215]. She administers the cure to her lover, and Jungir Khan awakens restored to sanity.

> "I will have Jungir Khan make you captain of his guard" — Nafertari promises Conan, now that the satrap's true identity is revealed [009-224]

As a satrap, Jungir Khan is a man who understands debts and the value of capable swords. Upon learning of Conan's role in his rescue, he ensures the Cimmerian is "sent on his way with a pouch of gold and the satrap's own thanks" — a reward fitting for a ruler who has been pulled back from the brink of madness and political catastrophe [009-251].
