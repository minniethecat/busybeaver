## Overview

**"Shadows in Zamboula"** (also published as "The Man-Eaters of Zamboula") is a [[conan|Conan]] story by Robert E. Howard, first published in the November 1935 issue of *Weird Tales*. [[set|Set]] in the decadent desert city of [[Zamboula]] on the edge of the [[turan|Turan]]ian empire, the story follows [[conan|Conan]] through a single night of escalating violence as he contends with cannibal slavers, treacherous satraps, and a seductive woman whose motives are not what they appear. The tale is one of Howard's tightest — a noir-inflected survival story compressed into a few dark hours.

> "In Zamboula, the hand of every man is against his neighbor, and death lurks in every shadow." [009-xxx]

## Plot Summary

### The Desert City

[[conan|Conan]] arrives in [[zamboula|Zamboula]], a squalid city on the southern frontier of the [[turan|Turan]]ian empire, seeking rest and supplies. He takes a room at a caravanserai but is warned by the innkeeper that the streets are unsafe at night. The city's satrap, Akter Khan, permits the cannibalistic [[darfar|Darfari]] slavers to hunt the streets after dark — a policy that keeps the slavers loyal but terrorizes the populace [009-xxx].

### The Woman in the Night

A mysterious woman comes to [[conan|Conan]]'s room, begging for his help. She claims to be threatened by the [[darfar|Darfari]] slavers and offers [[conan|Conan]] a reward to escort her safely through the city. [[conan|Conan]], ever susceptible to a beautiful woman in distress, agrees — but remains suspicious. His barbarian instincts detect something amiss in her story [009-xxx].

### The Darfari Cannibals

As [[conan|Conan]] escorts the woman through [[zamboula|Zamboula]]'s dark streets, they are ambushed by a band of [[darfar|Darfari]] — towering black cannibals from the southern jungles who have been given license by Akter Khan to prey upon the city's inhabitants. [[conan|Conan]] fights them off in a series of brutal street skirmishes, his sword cutting through the darkness. The [[darfar|Darfari]] are depicted as a genuinely terrifying threat: strong, cunning, and utterly without mercy [009-xxx].

### Betrayal and Vengeance

The woman's true nature is revealed: she is not a victim but an agent of Akter Khan, sent to lure [[conan|Conan]] into a trap. She had intended to deliver him to the [[darfar|Darfari]] as a sacrifice to maintain the satrap's dark bargain. [[conan|Conan]] turns the tables — he fights his way into Akter Khan's palace, kills the satrap, and breaks the [[darfar|Darfari]]'s hold over the city. By dawn, [[zamboula|Zamboula]] is free of both its corrupt ruler and its cannibal hunters [009-xxx].

## Setting

The story takes place entirely within **[[Zamboula]]**, a frontier city on the desert edge of the [[turan|Turan]]ian empire. [[zamboula|Zamboula]] is depicted as a lawless, multicultural sink — a place where [[darfar|Darfari]] cannibals, [[turan|Turan]]ian soldiers, [[shem|Shem]]itish merchants, and Zuagir raiders all intersect in an atmosphere of mutual predation. The setting has the feel of a border town in a Western, transposed onto the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]].

## Themes

- **Urban Horror**: Unlike the wilderness settings of most [[conan|Conan]] stories, *Shadows in Zamboula* finds its horror in the city itself — the darkness of streets ruled by cannibals and corrupt officials.
- **Trust and Betrayal**: The woman's duplicity is the story's central twist, exploring [[conan|Conan]]'s vulnerability to feminine deception and his ability to overcome it through instinct.
- **Corruption from Within**: Akter Khan represents a civilization rotting from the inside, sacrificing its own citizens to maintain power — a microcosm of the decadence Howard saw in all civilized states.
- **Night as Transformation**: The city after dark becomes a different world, where normal rules are suspended and the predators emerge.

## Publication

*Shadows in Zamboula* was first published in *Weird Tales*, November 1935, as "The Man-Eaters of Zamboula." It was collected in *The Conquering Sword of [[conan|Conan]]* (Del Rey, 2005).

## Connections

- The [[Darfari]] cannibals connect this story to the broader black kingdoms setting, also explored in *[[jewels-of-gwahlur|Jewels of Gwahlur]]* and *[[a-witch-shall-be-born|A Witch Shall Be Born]]* ([[free-companions|Zarallo's Free Companions]] were stationed on the [[darfar|Darfar border]]).
- The city of [[Zamboula]] is visited by [[conan|Conan]] in multiple tales, serving as a crossroads between the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kingdoms, [[turan|Turan]], and the southern black kingdoms.
- The story's compressed timeframe (one night) is unusual for a [[conan|Conan]] tale, giving it a pacing closer to Howard's horror stories than his adventure narratives.
