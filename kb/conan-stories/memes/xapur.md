# Xapur

Xapur, known as "Xapur the Fortified," is an uninhabited island on the Vilayet Sea in Robert E. Howard's Conan story "The Devil in Iron." The island is distinguished by its sheer cliffs, ancient ruins of a forgotten prehistoric kingdom, and its dark secret — it is the site of the resurrected city of Dagon, ruled by the demonic being [[Khosatral Khel]].

## Geography

Xapur rises dramatically from the Vilayet Sea with no natural shoreline, its cliffs towering a hundred and fifty feet above the water:

> There is an uninhabited island near the mainland, known as Xapur, the Fortified, because of some ancient ruins upon it. There is a peculiarity about it which makes it perfect for our purpose. It has no shoreline, but rises sheer out of the sea in cliffs a hundred and fifty feet tall. Not even an ape could negotiate them. [004-029]

The only means of ascent is a narrow path on the western side, worn into the solid rock in the form of a crude stairway. The adjacent mainland coast is an uninhabited reedy marsh inhabited by wild beasts, and the nearest settlement — a Yuetshi fishing village — lies some distance to the south [004-005]. The island is one among "myriad isles which dotted the great inland sea" [004-004].

## The Ruins of Xapur

Before the events of "The Devil in Iron," Xapur was known only for its crumbling ruins — remnants of a prehistoric kingdom that predated even the Hyborian migrations:

> Men called it Xapur, the Fortified, because of its ruins, remnants of some prehistoric kingdom, lost and forgotten before the conquering Hyborians had ridden southward. None knew who reared those stones, though dim legends lingered among the Yuetshi which half intelligibly suggested a connection of immeasurable antiquity between the fishers and the unknown island kingdom. [004-004]

The Yuetshi fisherfolk, who once understood the significance of the ruins, had long since reduced their tales to "a meaningless formula, a gibberish framed by their lips by custom" [004-005]. No Yuetshi had visited Xapur for a century before the events of the story.

## Dagon and the Resurrection

During the course of "The Devil in Iron," the ruins of Xapur are supernaturally restored into the living city of Dagon through the sorcery of Khosatral Khel — an ancient demonic being who had originally built the city and ruled it as a god in ages past. The city's sudden reappearance confounds Conan, who had visited the island only a month before:

> He had been on Xapur before. Less than a month ago he had held a secret conclave here with a pirate crew. He knew that he was approaching a point where he could see the mysterious ruins which gave the island its name, and he wondered if he would find the girl hiding among them. Even with the thought he stopped as though struck dead. [004-066]

Night after night, Dagon and its inhabitants rise with the setting sun — "folk who have tasted death are only partly alive" [004-121] — moving in an enchanted haze, dimly aware of their unnatural existence. With the dawn they return to sleep.

## The Fall of Dagon

When Conan slays Khosatral Khel with a knife forged from a meteor — "that unearthly knife" against which the demon's magic was "impotent" [004-117] — the entire city dissolves:

> With the passing of the inhuman intellect which had given them rebirth, they had faded back into the dust which they had been for ages uncounted. Only the stumps of broken columns rose above crumbling walls and broken paves and shattered dome. Conan again looked upon the ruins of Xapur as he remembered them. [004-183]

The city of Dagon, its walls and towers, its bronze gates, its gold and ivory, its dark-haired women and shaven-headed priests — all returned to the rubble from which the demon's will had summoned them.

## In the Stories

Xapur and the city of Dagon are central to the events of "The Devil in Iron," where the island serves as both trap and battleground. Jehungir Agha and the Kushite merchant Ghaznavi use the island as bait, sending a false message that Octavia is hiding there to lure Conan into an ambush [004-040]. Conan instead discovers the resurrected Dagon, fights Khosatral Khel, and ultimately destroys both the demon and his city.
