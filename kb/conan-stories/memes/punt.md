# Punt

Punt is a southern Black Kingdom in Robert E. Howard's Conan stories, situated east of [[Keshan]] in the far south of the Hyborian Age world. It appears most prominently in "Jewels of Gwahlur" as Keshan's hereditary enemy and the target of competing military schemes by [[Conan]] and the Stygian adventurer [[Thutmekri]].

## Geography

Punt lies east of Keshan, one of the cluster of Black Kingdoms in the far south. Its highlands are home to burial customs that figure indirectly in the plot of "Jewels of Gwahlur," where a subterranean river carries the dead from the Puntish highlands through the caverns beneath the palace of Alkmeenon:

> ...the lake where the people of the Puntish highlands throw their dead. That river runs under this palace. They have ladders hung over the water where they can hang and fish for the corpses that come floating through. [007-225]

The kingdom's rivers yield gold, which is collected by the Puntish using wicker baskets [007-271]. Punt is also known for its green firestones — gemstones that glow in the dark, which the people of Punt call "the petrified eyes of those prehistoric snakes the ancients called Golden Serpents" [011-036].

## People and Culture

The Puntish worship an ivory woman as a deity [007-271], a detail that resonates with the plot of "Jewels of Gwahlur," where the oracle of the ivory goddess Yelaya is central to the story. Gold-washing in the rivers is a significant cultural and economic activity, and the kingdom evidently possesses sufficient wealth and military organization to pose a credible threat to its neighbors.

Punt's trade relations are complex: the kingdom had previously hosted traders from [[Zembabwei]] but had expelled them and burned their fortresses [007-016], creating the political opening that Thutmekri exploits in his scheme.

## History

Punt's history in the Conan stories is defined by its rivalry with Keshan. As Keshan's "hereditary enemy" [007-014], Punt had recently achieved military successes against Keshan that had "aroused the fury of Keshan's irascible king" [007-014].

The political situation at the start of "Jewels of Gwahlur" places Punt at the center of a three-way power struggle:

- **Conan** offers to train the armies of Keshan and lead them against Punt in exchange for employment and access to the temple of Gwahlur [007-014]
- **Thutmekri**, representing the twin kings of Zembabwei, pledges to invade Punt from the east with "a host of black spearmen, Shemitish archers, and mercenary swordsmen" to aid Keshan in annexing the kingdom [007-017]
- **The kings of Zembabwei** secretly plan to let Keshan and Punt exhaust each other, then crush both and seize the Teeth of Gwahlur [007-043]

The Zembabwan scheme to use Punt's conquest as a pretext for acquiring the Teeth of Gwahlur reveals Punt's strategic position in the politics of the southern kingdoms.

## In the Stories

Punt is referenced across two Conan stories:
- **"Jewels of Gwahlur"** — Punt is Keshan's hereditary enemy, and both Conan and Thutmekri compete to offer the king of Keshan military aid against Punt as a means to access the treasures of Gwahlur. At the story's conclusion, Conan declares his intention to go to Punt with Muriela, planning to install her as a substitute for Punt's ivory goddess and fleece the kingdom of its gold [007-271].
- **"Red Nails"** — Punt is referenced as the source of the green firestones that glow in the dark halls of Xuchotl [011-036].
