# Hyborian Age

## Overview

[[the-hyborian-age-essay|The Hyborian Age]] is the fictional historical period created by Robert E. Howard as the backdrop for his [[conan|Conan]] stories. Howard prepared a detailed "history" of this age and its peoples "in order to lend him and his sagas a greater aspect of realness," adhering to its "facts" and spirit as closely as a writer of actual historical fiction follows actual history (017-001). The Age spans thousands of years, from the fall of the Pre-Cataclysmic [[thurian-age|Thurian civilization]] through the rise and eventual collapse of the Hyborian kingdoms.

## Historical Eras

[[the-hyborian-age-essay|The Hyborian Age]] encompasses several distinct eras. It begins with the [[pre-cataclysmic-age|Pre-Cataclysmic Age]], dominated by the civilized kingdoms of Kamelia, [[valusia|Valusia]], Verulia, Grondar, Thule, and Commoria, whose peoples spoke similar languages suggesting a common origin (017-001). The barbarians of that time were the [[picts|Picts]] on western islands, the [[atlanteans|Atlanteans]] on a small continent, and the [[lemurians|Lemurians]] on eastern islands (017-002). The [[thurian-age|Thurian civilization]] crumbled as its armies became largely composed of barbarian mercenaries (017-004).

The [[great-cataclysm|Great Cataclysm]] then reshaped the world: [[atlanteans|Atlantis]] and Lemuria sank, the [[picts|Pictish]] Islands were heaved up into mountain peaks, and sections of the [[thurian-age|Thurian Continent]] vanished beneath the waves (017-005). In the aftermath, stone-age kingdoms of [[picts|Picts]] and [[atlanteans|Atlanteans]] clashed for five hundred years before both descended into savagery (017-007)(017-008).

A thousand years after a lesser cataclysm, the Hyborians — or Hybori — emerged in the north. Their god was [[bori|Bori]], a great chief whom legend elevated as the king who led them north during [[great-cataclysm|the Cataclysm]] (017-014). Over centuries, these tawny-haired, grey-eyed people spread southward and westward, conquering and absorbing scattered clans (017-015)(017-017).

## The Hyborian Kingdoms

At their zenith, the Hyborian kingdoms dominated the western world: [[aquilonia|Aquilonia]], Nemedia, Brythunia, Hyperborea, Koth, Ophir, Argos, Corinthia, and the Border Kingdom (017-023). [[aquilonia|Aquilonia]] was the most powerful, though others vied with it in strength. The Hyborian civilization was described as "so virile that contact with it virtually snatched out of the wallow of savagery such tribes as it touched" (017-024).

Despite this vigor, the Hyborian civilization fell not through internal decay but through the growing power of barbarian nations and the [[hyrkanians|Hyrkanians]]. "The Hyborian peoples were overthrown while their vigorous culture was in its prime" (017-029). [[aquilonia|Aquilonia]]'s imperial greed, the unification of the [[picts|Picts]] under [[gorm|Gorm]] through iron-working learned from the priest [[arus|Arus]], and the [[hyrkanians|Hyrkanian]] invasions from the east all contributed to the collapse (017-029)(017-030)(017-032).

## Races and Peoples

[[the-hyborian-age-essay|The Hyborian Age]] is populated by a complex tapestry of races. The dominant Hyborians mixed over time with other peoples — [[shem|Shem]]itish, [[stygia|Stygian]], Zingaran, and Zamorian strains entered their bloodlines, with only the province of Gunderland preserving pure Hyborian stock (017-027). The barbarian races — [[cimmerians|Cimmerians]] (descendants of [[atlanteans|Atlanteans]]), [[nordheim|Nordheimir]] (golden-haired Æsir and red-haired Vanir), and [[picts|Picts]] — maintained their racial purity through isolation (017-027). To the east, the [[hyrkanians|Hyrkanians]] (descendants of [[lemurians|Lemurians]]) built the empire of [[turan|Turan]], while the ancient kingdom of [[stygia|Stygia]] slumbered in the far south (017-026).

## Howard's Fictional History

Howard explicitly positioned the Hyborian Age within a framework of real-world prehistory, presenting it as an epoch between the sinking of [[atlanteans|Atlantis]] (circa 20,000 BC) and the beginning of recorded history. The essay traces the arc from the [[pre-cataclysmic-age|Pre-Cataclysmic Age]] through the Hyborian ascendancy to the eventual Nordic drift — the migration of Æsir and Vanir southward before advancing ice fields — which swept away the [[picts|Pictish]] empire and laid the foundations of modern European peoples (017-055)(017-056). The essay concludes by noting that the "tall, vigorous, blue-eyed, fair-haired" race of the north "was to survive and overlap the world," forming the racial stock of modern Europe (017-056).

Howard's pseudohistorical essay "The Hyborian Age" (published posthumously in 1938) was unusual for a pulp writer: rather than merely hinting at a backstory, he wrote a full-scale fictional history modeled on the declining-and-falling-empires narrative of Gibbon and Spengler. The essay's cyclical view of civilization — barbarism rising to civilization, then being overthrown by fresh barbarism — was Howard's own interpretation of history, informed by his reading in archaeology, anthropology, and the racial theories of his day (017-001). Though Howard's racial categories reflect now-outdated scientific frameworks, the essay's ambition — to provide a complete, internally consistent prehistory for a fantasy world — set a standard later adopted by J.R.R. Tolkien and virtually every subsequent world-builder in fantasy literature (017-001)(017-056).
