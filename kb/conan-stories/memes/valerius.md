## Overview

Valerius is a young Khaurani nobleman and soldier who remains fiercely loyal to Queen [[Taramis]] during the usurpation of [[Khauran]] by the witch [[Salome]] and the Shemitish mercenary captain [[Constantius]]. When most of the Khaurani army is deceived into believing Salome (disguised as Taramis) is the legitimate queen, Valerius alone suspects the truth and becomes a key figure in the loyalist resistance [006-161]. Despite being gravely wounded by Constantius's mercenaries, he survives, escapes, and ultimately storms the palace dungeon to rescue the real Taramis, playing a decisive role in the restoration of Khauran's rightful ruler.

> "A young soldier, Valerius, had another belief. He believed that the woman who sat on the throne was not Queen Taramis, but a witch who had taken her shape." [006-161]

## Loyalty to Taramis

Valerius's defining trait is his unwavering loyalty to Queen Taramis. While the Khaurani army and the general populace are deceived by Salome's magical impersonation, Valerius refuses to accept the impostor. He is one of the very few in Khauran who perceives that something is deeply wrong with the woman on the throne — a conviction born not of evidence but of intuition and genuine devotion to his queen [006-161].

His loyalty is not merely political; it carries a personal, almost romantic dimension. He is the young nobleman who once dared to confess his feelings to the queen, a confession she remembers even in her darkest hour of imprisonment [006-287]. This personal bond gives his loyalty a depth that mere duty could not provide, driving him to take risks that a purely political allegiance would not justify.

## Wounding and Survival

When Constantius and his Shemitish mercenaries seize power in Khauran, Valerius is among those who resist. In the ensuing violence, he is badly wounded — a cut across the forehead and a pierced thigh — and left for dead [006-085]. Discovered by a loyal serving girl who secretly nurses him back to health, Valerius survives against the odds.

> "Lie still, Valerius," begged the girl who was washing his wounds. "Your forehead is cut and your thigh is pierced." [006-085]

His recovery is slow and painful, but his determination to rescue Taramis sustains him. Even as his wounds still trouble him, he resolves to act — a testament to his courage and the strength of his devotion [006-088].

## The Rescue of Taramis

Valerius's finest moment comes when he infiltrates the palace dungeon to free the captive queen. Taramis, broken by weeks of imprisonment and torture, initially does not recognize her rescuer. But Valerius's voice — and his confession of love — breaks through her despair, and she remembers him [006-287, 006-288].

> "You! Do you not remember me, Valerius? Once with your own lips you swore to serve me." [006-287]

Despite his injuries, Valerius lifts the weakened Taramis and carries her through the dungeon corridors [006-290]. When discovered by guards, he fights with desperate fury, cutting down an executioner who threatens the queen with a sacrificial knife [006-307]. His combat skill, though that of a young soldier rather than a hardened veteran like [[Conan]], is sufficient — driven by righteous fury and the urgency of his mission [006-308].

The escape is harrowing. Valerius carries Taramis across the temple courtyard, through a hail of arrows, and ultimately presents the true queen before the assembled Khaurani people just as Salome's deception is unraveling [006-311, 006-312, 006-316]. His declaration — "Here is your queen!" — is the turning point that rallies Khauran against the usurpers [006-311].

## Courage

Valerius's courage is not the battle-hardened confidence of a veteran warrior but the more fragile, more human bravery of a young man who acts despite his fear and weakness. He fights while still recovering from wounds, carries the queen when he can barely walk himself, and races through arrow fire without hesitation [006-290, 006-292, 006-316].

His cry for Conan in the heat of battle — "Conan! Conan!" — reveals his awareness of his own limitations [006-321]. He knows he is not the fighter Conan is; what makes him heroic is that he acts anyway. Even Conan acknowledges this, telling Taramis after the battle that Valerius will make her a better captain than any of his own veterans could [006-331].
