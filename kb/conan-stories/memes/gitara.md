## Overview

Gitara is a servant girl in the kingdom of [[Vendhya]], serving as a maid to the Devi [[Yasmina]]. Though her role in *The People of the Black Circle* is brief, her presence is pivotal: her love for the sorcerer [[Khemsa]] provides the emotional foundation for his defiance against the [[Black Seers of Yimsha]]. Gitara embodies the collision between ordinary human devotion and the cold, predatory world of Hyborian sorcery — a world in which she has no power and no protection.

> "I will follow them! They killed Gitara; I will kill them — the acolytes, the Masters, all!" — Khemsa's anguished vow after Gitara's death [005-369]

## Her Love for Khemsa

Gitara's relationship with [[Khemsa]] is one of genuine affection between two people trapped within the machinations of greater powers. Khemsa, an acolyte of the Black Seers, was sent to serve as a spy and agent among the Vendhyans; instead, he fell in love with a servant girl. "That girl was my maid, Gitara" [005-268] — [[Yasmina]]'s recognition of Gitara confirms that the servant girl was known to those in power, yet invisible enough that no one suspected she would become the fulcrum upon which the Seers' plans would pivot.

Gitara followed [[Khemsa]] willingly when he chose to defy his masters. She accompanied him to the slopes of Mount Yimsha [005-324], placing herself in the shadow of the very power that Khemsa sought to escape. Her devotion was absolute: she trusted Khemsa not as a sorcerer but as a man.

## The Betrayal of the Black Seers

The [[Black Seers of Yimsha]] operated through fear and supernatural dominance, controlling their acolytes through terror and the promise of power. Khemsa's love for Gitara introduced a variable the Seers had not accounted for: human emotion. Faced with a choice between his masters and the woman who loved him, Khemsa chose Gitara.

This decision set in motion the chain of events that brought [[Conan]] — then a captive of the Seers — an unexpected ally. Khemsa's rebellion, born of his love for Gitara, weakened the Seers' position and provided the opening through which Conan and [[Yasmina]] could challenge the Master of Yimsha.

> "Gitara glide out from among the rocks... she recognized her" [005-324] — the moment Gitara appears on the mountain, making herself known to Yasmina and linking the Devi's fate to that of the servant girl

## Death on Yimsha

Gitara's courage could not protect her from the power of the Master of Yimsha. During the confrontation on the mountain, the Master turned his killing gaze upon her — "the Rakhsha full upon Gitara" [005-334] — and she fell. There was no struggle, no defense: against the supernatural might of the Black Seers, an ordinary girl had no recourse.

Her death unmade [[Khemsa]]. In the space of moments, the sorcerer who had dared to defy the Black Seers was reduced to a man howling for vengeance. His final words — "I am going to Gitara — she is waiting for me in hell" [005-374] — speak not of regret but of reunion. Even in death, Gitara exerted a force that the Seers could not comprehend: love that outlasted mortality.

> "I am going to Gitara — she is waiting for me in hell" [005-374] — Khemsa's dying words, choosing damnation with Gitara over any fate without her
