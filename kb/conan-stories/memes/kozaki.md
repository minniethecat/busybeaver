# Kozaki

The kozaki (singular: kozak) are a brotherhood of [[free-companions|Free Companions]] dwelling on the broad steppes between the Vilayet Sea and the easternmost [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kingdoms in Robert E. Howard's [[conan|Conan]] stories. Formed of outlaws, escaped slaves, deserters, and adventurers from many nations, the kozaki elect their own leaders — called hetmans — and live by raiding the settled kingdoms, particularly [[Turan]].

## Origins and Character

The kozaki are a relatively recent phenomenon in the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]]:

> On the broad steppes between the Sea of Vilayet and the borders of the easternmost Hyborian kingdoms, a new race had sprung up in the past half-century, formed originally of fleeing criminals, broken men, escaped slaves, and deserting soldiers. They were men of many crimes and countries, some born on the steppes, some fleeing from the kingdoms in the west. They were called kozak, which means wastrel. [004-018]

They dwell "in huts of hardened mud and brushwood, surrounded by high palisades" [004-018], making their camps along the lower reaches of the [[Zaporoska]] River. Their territory — a "wilderness of reeds, a swampy jungle" [004-027] — provides natural defense against expeditions sent by the kingdoms they raid.

The kozaki are not a tribe but a multi-ethnic military brotherhood. "Men of many crimes and countries" [004-018] flock to their camps, where status is determined by martial prowess rather than birth. They elect their own hetmans — a term for their elected war-chiefs — and maintain a fierce independence from all settled authority.

## Conan as Kozak Hetman

[[Conan]] the [[cimmeria|Cimmerian]] rose to leadership among the kozaki through his wits and his sword alone:

> This was Conan, who had wandered into the armed camps of the kozaks with no other possession than his wits and his sword, and who had carved his way to leadership among them. [004-061]

He identifies himself proudly: "I am [[conan|Conan]], a hetman among the kozaks" [004-085]. His status as a former kozak hetman is a recurring marker of his martial reputation throughout the stories. In "Shadows in the Moonlight," when [[shah-amurath|Shah Amurath]] taunts him, [[conan|Conan]] clarifies: "[[shah-amurath|Shah Amurath]] called you a kozak; were you of that band?" / "I am [[conan|Conan]], of [[cimmeria|Cimmeria]]. I was with the kozaki, as the [[hyrkanians|Hyrkanian]] dogs called us." [002-036, 002-037]

In "A Witch Shall Be Born," his old enemy identifies him as "once a hetman of the kozaki of the [[zaporoska|Zaporoskan River]]" [006-128], and [[conan|Conan]] himself reflects on this past when facing the Devi [[yasmina|Yasmina]]:

> "I will ride northward with you to the steppes of the kozaki. I was a hetman among the Free Companions before I rode southward. I'll make you a queen on the Zaporoska River!" [005-573]

## Military Capability

The kozaki are formidable fighters. [[shah-amurath|Shah Amurath]], the [[hyrkanians|Hyrkanian]] lord, styles himself "the conqueror of the miserable kozaki" [002-008] — a boast that reveals both the threat the kozaki posed to the eastern powers and the prestige attached to defeating them.

The kozaki's guerrilla warfare in the reed-choked [[zaporoska|Zaporoska]] makes them nearly invulnerable in their home territory. An expedition sent against them was "cut to pieces by those masterless devils" [004-027], and the settled kingdoms prefer to negotiate with them for the ransom of captives rather than attempt direct military conquest.

## Kozak Fashion and Culture

The kozaki have a distinctive style that influences mercenary fashion across the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]]. [[Olgerd Vladislav]], the kozak who became chief of the [[Zuagirs]], wears his curved Cherkees knife "koz ak fashion" [006-193] — a mark of his origins on the steppes. His "apparel would have satisfied the vanity of any [[zaporoska|Zaporoskan]] hetman" [006-192], suggesting that kozak hetmans cultivated a recognizable style of ostentatious display.

The kozak identity carries weight beyond the steppes. When Olgerd Vladislav is described as "the *kozak* adventurer who wandered down from the northern steppes and made himself chief of a band of [[zuagir|Zuagirs]]" [006-154], the term carries connotations of both dangerous capability and rootlessness — a man without a nation who rises by force of will alone.

## Olgerd Vladislav: Kozak to Zuagir Chief

[[Olgerd Vladislav]] represents the kozak archetype in its most ambitious form — the Free Companion who leaves the [[zaporoska|Zaporoska]] to seek fortune in the broader world. His transition from kozak hetman to Zuagir chief demonstrates the portability of the Free Companion's skills: the ability to command multi-ethnic warriors through personal dominance rather than hereditary right.

However, Olgerd's kozak background ultimately limits him. Unlike [[conan|Conan]] — who is "a barbarian too" [006-226] — Olgerd's steppe-brotherhood origins do not grant him the innate understanding of tribal loyalty that [[conan|Conan]] possesses. He "had missed what was going on about him" [006-229], failing to perceive that the Zuagir warriors had transferred their allegiance from him to [[conan|Conan]] until it was too late.

The confrontation between Olgerd and [[conan|Conan]] is a clash of two kozak leadership models: the charismatic adventurer who rules through spectacle and ostentation (Olgerd, whose "apparel would have satisfied the vanity of any [[zaporoska|Zaporoskan]] hetman" [006-192]) versus the barbarian chieftain who rules through shared identity and raw strength ([[conan|Conan]]).

## Across the Stories

The kozaki appear in multiple [[conan|Conan]] stories, serving as a touchstone for [[conan|Conan]]'s past and the world of the eastern steppes:
- **"Shadows in the Moonlight"** — [[shah-amurath|Shah Amurath]] claims to have conquered the kozaki; [[conan|Conan]] is identified as a kozak [002-008, 002-036]
- **"The [[khosatral-khel|Devil in Iron]]"** — The kozak origin story is told, and [[conan|Conan]] serves as their hetman during the story's events [004-018 through 004-189]
- **"The People of the Black Circle"** — [[conan|Conan]] recalls his kozak past and offers to bring [[yasmina|Yasmina]] to the [[zaporoska|Zaporoska]] [005-573]
- **"A Witch Shall Be Born"** — Olgerd Vladislav is identified as a kozak adventurer; [[conan|Conan]]'s past as [[zaporoska|Zaporoskan]] hetman is noted [006-128, 006-154]
