## Overview

The Vendhyans are the inhabitants of [[vendhya|Vendhya]], an ancient and opulent kingdom in the far east of the Hyborian world. Ruled by a Devi (queen), Vendhya is a land of great wealth, elaborate court ceremony, and dark mysticism. The Vendhyans feature prominently in "The People of the Black Circle," where their kingdom becomes a battleground between imperial politics and supernatural forces.

> "Outside, the moan of the tortured thousands shuddered up to the stars which crusted the sweating Vendhyan night." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Political Structure

Vendhyan society is monarchical, ruled by a Devi — the title used by Queen Yasmina, the central figure of Vendhyan politics in the Conan stories. The kingdom's noble-born fighting-men, arrayed in polished helmets and gold-chased corselets, guard the royal palace and its many gates.

> "All the noble-born fighting-men of Ayodhya were gathered in the great palace or about it, and at each broad-arched gate and door fifty archers stood on guard." [[1]](#ref-1)

The capital city of Ayodhya serves as the seat of Vendhyan power, a place of torches, gardens, and the constant threat of assassination — for even within the heavily guarded palace, death could stalk unchecked.

## Magic and the Black Seers

The Vendhyan kingdom was plagued by the [[black-seers|Black Seers of Mount Yimsha]], a cabal of sorcerers who murdered the Devi's brother — the king of Vendhya — through magical means. This act set in motion the events of "[[The People of the Black Circle]]," as Yasmina devoted herself to destroying her brother's murderers.

> "The king of Vendhya was destroyed by magic. I have devoted my life to the destruction of his murderers." [[2]](#ref-2)

Yasmina's quest led her to study forbidden texts, including the [[book-of-skelos|Book of Skelos]], and to consult with nameless hermits in the caves below Jhelai, demonstrating the deep entanglement of Vendhyan royalty with the occult.

## Relations with Neighboring Peoples

The Vendhyans shared a contentious border with the [[afghulis|Afghuli]] hill tribes, who raided from their Himelian mountain strongholds. A Vendhyan gibbet awaited any Afghuli caught within the kingdom's borders, reflecting the bitter enmity between the settled Vendhyans and the hill peoples.

> "Yes, on a Vendhyan gibbet." [[3]](#ref-3)

The kingdom also maintained complex diplomatic relations with neighboring powers, including [[Turan]] and the various kingdoms of the Himelian frontier.
