# Hyperborea

Hyperborea is the northernmost of the Hyborian kingdoms in Robert E. Howard's Conan stories and the essay "The Hyborian Age." It is the oldest of the Hyborian states, described as rude, barbaric, and the first kingdom established by the Hyborian people during their southward migration.

## Geography

Hyperborea occupies the far northern regions of the Hyborian world, a land of snow, ice, and harsh winters:

> ...the snowy lands of the Hyperboreans in the north. [004-016]

The kingdom borders [[Nordheim]] to the north and west, and [[Brythunia]] to the south. Its harsh climate has produced a hardy, warlike people whose society remains more primitive than the southern Hyborian states. The terrain is dominated by frozen plains, pine forests, and mountain ranges that isolate Hyperborea from the more civilized kingdoms to the south.

## Inhabitants

The Hyperboreans are a Hyborian people who have retained the more barbaric characteristics of their ancestors. They are described as tall, fair-haired warriors whose culture has not advanced to the level of sophistication seen in Aquilonia or Nemedia. Howard describes Hyperborea's origins:

> The rude and barbaric kingdom of Hyperborea, which had its beginning in a crude fortress of boulders heaped together by the Hyborian tribe which had first drifted into those territories. [017-020]

Despite their barbarism, the Hyperboreans are formidable warriors. Their armies, composed of hardened northern fighters, march south periodically to raid the richer kingdoms. Hyperborean warriors are mentioned among the forces that challenge Conan's rule.

## History

Hyperborea holds the distinction of being the first Hyborian kingdom, established when the Hyborian tribes first settled in the northern regions after the Cataclysm. From this crude beginning, the Hyborian people spread southward, founding the kingdoms that would eventually dominate the known world.

Hyperborea's historical significance lies in its role as the ancestral homeland of the Hyborian civilization. The kingdom was eventually overthrown during the period of barbarian invasions that reshaped the Hyborian world:

> The ancient kingdom of Hyperborea is overthrown by one of these invading tribes. [017-022]

Despite its early founding, Hyperborea remained a relatively minor power compared to the great southern kingdoms. Its isolation and harsh climate prevented the development of the sophisticated urban culture that characterized Aquilonia, Nemedia, and Koth.
