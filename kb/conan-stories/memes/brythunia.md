# Brythunia

Brythunia is a northern-central kingdom of the Hyborian Age in Robert E. Howard's Conan stories, located between [[Nemedia]] to the west and the eastern kingdoms. It is frequently mentioned as a source of mercenary soldiers and is one of the original Hyborian kingdoms.

## Geography

Brythunia occupies the northeastern quadrant of the Hyborian kingdoms, with a landscape of rolling plains, scattered woodlands, and a climate that is colder than the southern kingdoms but milder than Hyperborea. The kingdom's borders touch Nemedia to the west, [[Hyperborea]] to the north, and extend eastward toward the territories of [[Zamora]].

The kingdom's geographic position—between the great powers of Nemedia and the eastern frontier—makes it a crossroads for military movements and trade routes. Brythunian soldiers are a common sight in armies throughout the Hyborian world, reflecting the kingdom's role as a major exporter of military manpower.

## Inhabitants

The Brythunians are a Hyborian people known primarily as soldiers and mercenaries. They appear frequently in the mixed armies of the Hyborian Age:

> The men he had gathered about him were made up of men from many nations: Kothians, Zamorians, Brythunians, Corinthians, Shemites. [002-187]

Individual Brythunians who appear in the stories are typically characterized as capable but unremarkable fighters—professional soldiers who serve in the armies of various kingdoms. In "The Scarlet Citadel," a "rat-faced Brythunian" appears among Conan's band:

> Before anyone could answer, a rat-faced Brythunian, standing behind his fellows, squeaked: "No law is broken." [002-205]

Brythunian society is less extensively developed in Howard's writings than the great central kingdoms, with the kingdom serving primarily as a geographic reference point and a source of military characters.

## History

Brythunia is listed among the original Hyborian kingdoms in Howard's essay "The Hyborian Age." Its history is less eventful than that of the southern powers, with the kingdom maintaining its independence through a combination of geographic isolation and the export of its military-age population as mercenaries.

The kingdom's role in Hyborian Age politics is primarily as a buffer state between Nemedia and the eastern kingdoms. Brythunian kings have generally avoided the expansionist ambitions that characterize Koth and Aquilonia, maintaining a more defensive posture.
