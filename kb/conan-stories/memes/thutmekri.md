## Overview

Thutmekri is a Stygian adventurer and schemer who serves as Conan's principal rival in the hunt for the fabled Jewels of Gwahlur. Arriving in the barbaric kingdom of Keshan with a delegation of archers at his back, he presents himself as an ambassador with a diplomatic proposition — but his true purpose is the capture of the legendary treasure hidden in the lost city of [[Alkmeenon]]. Unlike Conan, who relies on his physical prowess and direct action, Thutmekri operates through intrigue, corruption, and manipulation, making him a distinct type of antagonist in the Conan canon.

> "Thutmekri was a Stygian, an adventurer" — the narrator's succinct introduction to the schemer who would become Conan's rival for the Jewels of Gwahlur [007-016]

## A Stygian Schemer

Thutmekri embodies the archetype of the Stygian intriguer — operating not through force of arms but through webs of influence and deceit. As a son of [[Stygia]], the ancient and sorcery-haunted kingdom south of the Styx, Thutmekri carries the heritage of a land whose priests and nobles have elevated manipulation to an art form. He arrives in [[Keshan]] not as a lone wanderer but "at the head of" a company of men [007-015], projecting the image of a man of consequence.

His method is persuasion dressed as diplomacy. He presents himself to the king of Keshan with a proposition that concerns the country's welfare — or so he claims — while secretly maneuvering to secure the Jewels of Gwahlur for himself [007-016]. This dual-layered approach, presenting a public face while working toward a hidden end, marks Thutmekri as a quintessential Stygian operator. He conveys to the suspicious king that some of the Teeth of Gwahlur "would be put to no base usage" [007-017], cloaking his greed in the language of noble purpose.

## The Quest for the Jewels of Gwahlur

The Jewels of Gwahlur — also known as the Teeth of Gwahlur — are the legendary treasure of the dead kingdom whose capital was [[Alkmeenon]], a deserted palace-city hidden in the cliffs of southwestern Keshan [007-010] [007-012]. Tales of this treasure had drifted across the Hyborian world, drawing fortune-seekers like moths to a flame.

Thutmekri's plan for capturing the treasure was characteristically indirect. Recognizing that the superstitious Keshani priests would never willingly surrender the sacred jewels, he orchestrated a scheme that would compel them to bring the treasure out of Alkmeenon themselves. By corrupting the high priest [[Gorulga]] [007-041], Thutmekri ensured that the oracle of Alkmeenon would pronounce in his favor — the priests would be told to take the Teeth of Gwahlur and give some of them to Thutmekri "as a pledge, as he desired" [007-105]. Conan, himself deep in the intrigue, recognized the elegance of the design: "It was all Thutmekri's plan. Knowing the Keshani would obey the oracle" [007-107].

> "It was all Thutmekri's plan. Knowing the Keshani would obey the oracle" — Conan perceives the full architecture of Thutmekri's scheme [007-107]

## Rivalry with Conan

The contest between Thutmekri and Conan is a battle of opposed methods. Where Conan moves, fights, and seizes, Thutmekri whispers, bribes, and waits. Their rivalry plays out entirely through proxies and positioning — the two men never directly confront one another in physical combat, a testament to Thutmekri's ability to keep himself insulated from the consequences of his plots.

Conan understood his adversary clearly. Based on his own experience as a leader of warriors, he "could predict Thutmekri's plans from his knowledge of" the Stygian's methods [007-042]. The Cimmerian recognized that Thutmekri "never for a moment doubted" that the priests would do his bidding [007-045], and he understood the Stygian's strategy — to get the treasure "where he" could control it [007-106], using the oracle as his puppet.

Thutmekri's Shemitish partner, [[Zabibi]], adds another layer to his network of intrigue, suggesting a man who builds alliances across racial and national lines when it serves his purposes [007-018]. His readiness to arm the Pelishtim against Keshan — threatening "to enslave them" [007-271] — reveals the ruthlessness beneath his diplomatic surface.

> "Conan could predict Thutmekri's plans from his knowledge of" the Stygian's character — the Cimmerian, no stranger to intrigue, sized up his rival accurately [007-042]

## Fate

Thutmekri's scheme ultimately fails, unraveled by Conan's intervention and the unpredictable forces awakened within Alkmeenon itself. Yet Thutmekri himself escapes the climactic events. Unlike many of Conan's antagonists — who tend to meet violent and definitive ends — Thutmekri survives the collapse of his plans and withdraws from Keshan. His fate beyond "Jewels of Gwahlur" is unknown, but his escape leaves open the possibility that his path may cross Conan's again.

His survival is itself a measure of the man: a schemer who knows when the game is lost and has the wisdom to withdraw rather than fight to the death. The Stygian had gambled on controlling events from behind the scenes, and when those events spiraled beyond anyone's control, he simply vanished — leaving behind the wreckage of his plans but taking his life with him.
