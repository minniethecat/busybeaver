# Pre-Cataclysmic Age

## Overview

The Pre-Cataclysmic Age is the term used by the Nemedian chroniclers to describe the era before the [[Great Cataclysm]] — essentially synonymous with the [[Thurian Age]], but reflecting a specific historiographical tradition. Howard introduces the term in the opening of "[[the-hyborian-age-essay|The Hyborian Age]]" essay:

> Of that epoch known by the Nemedian chroniclers as the Pre-Cataclysmic Age, little is known except the latter part, and that is veiled in the mists of legendry. (017-001)

## Nemedian Historiography

The use of "Pre-Cataclysmic" rather than "Thurian" is significant to Howard's framing. The Nemedian chroniclers viewed history through the lens of [[great-cataclysm|the Cataclysm]] — the world-shattering event that divided all time into "before" and "after." Their name for the era emphasizes this rupture rather than the geographic identifier (the [[thurian-age|Thurian Continent]]). This historiographical choice mirrors real-world historical nomenclature, where events like "pre-Columbian" or "pre-dynastic" are defined by what came after rather than the era's own characteristics.

## What Is Known

Despite the veil of legendry, Howard provides a clear picture of the late Pre-Cataclysmic Age. The era was "dominated by the kingdoms of Kamelia, [[valusia|Valusia]], Verulia, Grondar, Thule and Commoria" — peoples who "spoke a similar language, arguing a common origin" (017-001). Alongside these, "other kingdoms, equally civilized, but inhabited by different, and apparently older races" existed, hinting at the deep and complex history that preceded even this ancient epoch.

The barbarian peoples of the Pre-Cataclysmic Age — [[Picts]], [[Atlanteans]], and [[Lemurians]] — surrounded the civilized kingdoms, eventually infiltrating their armies and political structures as the [[Thurian Age|Thurian civilization]] crumbled from within.

## Relationship to the Thurian Age

The Pre-Cataclysmic Age and the [[thurian-age|Thurian Age]] refer to the same historical period but from different perspectives. "[[thurian-age|Thurian Age]]" is a geographic-cultural designation (the era of the [[thurian-age|Thurian Continent]] and its civilization), while "Pre-Cataclysmic Age" is a chronological designation (everything before the world-changing Cataclysm). Howard uses both terms — the Nemedian name in the essay's opening and "Thurian" throughout — suggesting they were interchangeable in his conception, with the Nemedian term representing the scholarly tradition preserved by later chroniclers.

## The End of the Age

The Pre-Cataclysmic Age ended with the [[Great Cataclysm]] — the geological catastrophe that sank [[atlanteans|Atlantis]] and Lemuria, heaved up new continents, and "blotted out" whole nations (017-005). The term "Pre-Cataclysmic" has meaning only in relation to this event: it defines an age by its ending rather than its content, a framing that reflects the catastrophe's overwhelming importance in [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]] historiography.
