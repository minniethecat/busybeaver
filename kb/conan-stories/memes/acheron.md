## Overview

Acheron was an ancient empire that dominated the western regions of the Thurian continent in the pre-[[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] era. It was a civilization built on dark [[magic-in-the-hyborian-age|sorcery]] and necromancy, ruled by powerful magicians who commanded forces beyond mortal understanding. Even millennia after its fall, Acheronian ruins and the residual [[magic-in-the-hyborian-age|sorcery]] within them haunt the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]] landscape.

## History and Dominion

At its height, Acheron controlled vast territories including what would later become Nemedia, [[aquilonia|Aquilonia]], and portions of Koth [017-045]. The empire's power rested on the sorcerous arts of its ruling class, who mastered cosmic forces that subsequent ages would forget [012-068]. Acheronian kings wielded magic as their primary instrument of rule, and their monuments — golden towers and crystal chambers — were built through enslaved supernatural entities [006-033].

The empire's decline came when the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] tribes, migrating from the north, overwhelmed its borders. The [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]]s, a hardy barbarian people with no tradition of [[magic-in-the-hyborian-age|sorcery]], proved resistant to many of the psychological and magical weapons that had kept Acheron's subject peoples in thrall [017-027].

## Sorcery and the Dark Arts

Acheronian [[magic-in-the-hyborian-age|sorcery]] was distinct from later [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]] magic in both scope and potency. The empire's magicians commanded cosmic laws that governed reality itself [005-017], and their spells could bind beings from the outer gulfs [006-034]. Acheronian magic left lasting traces — the land itself remained tainted with sorcerous residue, manifesting in the form of ancient curses that continued to work their evil centuries after the empire's destruction [006-027].

> "Even the arts you call sorcery are governed by cosmic laws," — a description of the systematic nature of Acheronian magical knowledge [005-017]

The empire's sorcerers were not mere witch-doctors but practitioners of a sophisticated magical science. They preserved their knowledge in secret repositories, and some of their secrets — terrible magic conjured from the blackness of cosmic space — survived in fragments even into [[conan|Conan]]'s time [012-075].

## Ruins and Legacy

The physical remnants of Acheron dot the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] landscape. Ancient ruins on the fortified island of Xapur, including a peculiar wall of green stone, bear the unmistakable mark of Acheronian craftsmanship [004-029]. These ruins are often avoided by local populations who whisper of the dark powers that still linger within them.

The most feared of Acheron's surviving entities is Khosatral Khel, described in [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] legendry as "the ancient from the Abyss, the god of [[dagon|Dagon]]" [004-130]. Such beings represent the enduring threat of Acheronian [[magic-in-the-hyborian-age|sorcery]] — even in ruin, the empire's magic could give form to entities that remembered the age when Acheron ruled the world.

> "They had forgot their ancient necromancy" — describing how later peoples lost the magical knowledge that Acheron once commanded [012-060]
