## Overview

The Golden Serpents are magical entities of unknown origin and nature, serving as guardians for the [[black-seers-of-yimsha|Black Seers of Mount Yimsha]]. They are associated with the crystal that contains the Lords of the Black Circle — demonic beings summoned from a far realm by the Master of Yimsha, who alone can command them.

> "You have seen the Lords of the Black Circle — it would blast your soul to hear from what far realm I summoned them and from what doom I guard them with ensorcelled crystal and golden serpents." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Nature and Purpose

The Golden Serpents function as a protective ward — guarding the "ensorcelled crystal" that holds the Lords of the Black Circle at bay. Without the serpents, the crystal would fail, and the demonic entities within would be released upon the world. This suggests the serpents are not merely decorative but active magical constructs, imbued with the sorcerous power of the [[black-seers-of-yimsha|Master of Yimsha]].

The serpents' power is tied to the crystal itself — a symbiosis of containment and guardianship. Only the Master, who summoned the Lords from their far realm and shaped the crystal prison, can command both the serpents and their charges.

## The Crystal Globe

The protective apparatus of the Master of Yimsha extended beyond the Golden Serpents to include additional magical artifacts. On his deathbed, the sorcerer [[Khemsa]] — a former acolyte who had defied the Master — gave Conan instructions for confronting the Black Seers. He spoke of a "crystal globe with the four golden pomegranates," an object tied to the same magical containment system as the Golden Serpents.

> "Break the crystal globe with the four golden pomegranates. Beware of the Master's transmutations." [[2]](#ref-2)

The crystal globe and its four golden pomegranates formed a complementary magical device — related to, but distinct from, the Golden Serpents and their ensorcelled crystal. Where the serpents guarded the Lords themselves, the crystal globe with golden pomegranates appeared to govern access to the Master's sanctum or the shifting, illusory passages of Mount Yimsha.

## The Master's Claim

The Master of Yimsha — an immortal adept who was once a common man, steeped in dark arts over "numberless eons of adeptship" — claimed sole dominion over the Golden Serpents. His power, he boasted, rivaled that of demons, and the serpents were one of the instruments of that power: part of a containment system so terrible that merely learning the full nature of the Lords and their crystal prison would "blast" a mortal soul.

> "But only I can rule them. My foolish Khemsa thought to make himself great — poor fool, bursting material doors and hurtling himself and his mistress through the air from hill to hill! Yet if he had not been destroyed his power might have grown to rival mine." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Khemsa's Defiance

The Master's mention of Khemsa reveals the peril of attempting to command the Black Circle's power without the Master's sanction. Khemsa, who had learned enough of the dark arts to burst material doors and transport himself and his lover [[Gitara]] through the air, believed he could make himself great. His destruction — crushed by a landslide summoned by the Masters as they passed overhead, leaving his body "splintered bones hung together only by torn tissue" — served as a warning of the price of defiance.

> "I will follow them! They killed Gitara; I will kill them — the acolytes, the Four of the Black Circle, the Master himself! Kill — kill them all!" [[2]](#ref-2)

## Context

The Golden Serpents appear in "[[The People of the Black Circle]]," one of the most magic-rich Conan stories. They exist within the larger framework of the Black Seers' supernatural apparatus — a hierarchy of demonic entities, ensorcelled crystals, magical bindings, and the crystal globe with golden pomegranates, all maintained by the ageless sorcerer who rules from [[Mount Yimsha]].
