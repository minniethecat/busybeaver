## Overview

The Brythunians are the [[hyborians|Hyborian]] inhabitants of Brythunia, a northeastern kingdom listed among the nine major Hyborian realms. Situated on the border of [[zamorians|Zamora]], Brythunia is a frontier kingdom where Hyborian and Zamorian blood have intermixed over centuries.

> "The eastern Brythunians have intermarried with the dark-skinned Zamorians." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Characteristics

Brythunians are known as skilled slingers — one of the most deadly missile troops of the Hyborian Age. In "[[Iron Shadows in the Moon]]," a rat-faced Brythunian slinger named Aratus nearly kills Conan with a single well-placed stone to the head, demonstrating the lethal effectiveness of their art.

> "Before any could answer, a rat-faced Brythunian, standing behind his fellows, whirled a sling swiftly and deadly. Straight as an arrow sped the stone to its mark, and Conan reeled and fell as a tall tree falls to the woodsman's ax." [[2]](#ref-2)

## Cultural Mixing

The eastern reaches of Brythunia have experienced extensive intermarriage with the dark-skinned Zamorians, blurring the racial boundaries that define the purer Hyborian kingdoms to the west. This mixing reflects Brythunia's position as a transitional zone between the Hyborian world and the older, more exotic civilizations of the east.

## Political Position

Brythunia occupies the northeastern corner of the Hyborian world, bordered by Hyperborea to the north, Nemedia to the west, Corinthia and Zamora to the south, and the wild eastern steppes beyond. It is one of the nine major Hyborian kingdoms, though less prominent than Aquilonia or Nemedia in the chronicles of the age.

Brythunian warriors serve in mercenary companies across the known world, their slings a distinctive weapon in an age dominated by swords and bows.

## In the Stories

The most notable Brythunian character in the Conan tales is Aratus, the rat-faced lieutenant in "Iron Shadows in the Moon." His sling-shot felling of Conan — and his subsequent eagerness to finish the unconscious barbarian — establish him as a cunning and ruthless antagonist.
