## Overview

**"The People of the Black Circle"** is a [[conan|Conan]] novella by Robert E. Howard, serialized across three issues of *Weird Tales* (September, October, November 1934). It is the longest continuous [[conan|Conan]] narrative and one of the most ambitious, featuring an intricate plot of political intrigue, Eastern [[magic-in-the-hyborian-age|sorcery]], and mounting supernatural horror. [[conan|Conan]], now a chief of the [[Afghuli]] hillmen, becomes entangled in the vendetta of [[yasmina|Yasmina]], queen of [[Vendhya]], against the [[Black Seers of Yimsha]], a circle of powerful sorcerers who murdered her brother the king.

> "The king of Vendhya was destroyed by magic. I have devoted my life to the destruction of his murderers." [005-052]

## Plot Summary

### The Vendhyan Queen's Quest

[[yasmina|Yasmina]], the Devi (queen) of [[vendhya|Vendhya]], has dedicated herself to avenging her brother Bunda Chand, who was slain by the [[black-seers-of-yimsha|Black Seers of Yimsha]]. Learning that [[conan|Conan]], now chief of the [[Afghuli]] tribesmen, holds seven [[vendhya|Vendhya]]n nobles captive, she travels to the Afghuli hills to negotiate their release. Her offer: the heads of the [[black-seers-of-yimsha|Black Seers]] in exchange for her countrymen [005-052].

### Khemsa's Ambition

[[khemsa|Khemsa]], a rogue acolyte of the [[black-seers-of-yimsha|Black Seers]], seeks to use the situation for his own gain. He is a sorcerer of considerable power himself — capable of mesmerism and death-magic — but he covets the greater power of the [[black-seers-of-yimsha|Black Seers]]' master, the Master of Yimsha. [[khemsa|Khemsa]] manipulates events to bring [[conan|Conan]], [[yasmina|Yasmina]], and the [[black-seers-of-yimsha|Black Seers]] into conflict [005-024].

### The Black Seers of Yimsha

The [[black-seers-of-yimsha|Black Seers]] dwell atop [[Mount Yimsha]], a remote peak in the Himelian Mountains north of [[vendhya|Vendhya]]. Their leader is a being of immense psychic power, capable of telepathy, illusion-casting, and killing from a distance. The Seers serve a higher order whose motives are never fully revealed — they are agents of a cosmic or supernatural faction rather than mere mortal sorcerers [005-421].

### Conan's Ordeal

[[conan|Conan]] is captured by the [[black-seers-of-yimsha|Black Seers]] and subjected to prolonged psychic torture. He endures through sheer barbarian willpower, refusing to break. This ordeal sequence is one of Howard's most sustained depictions of [[conan|Conan]]'s mental fortitude — the [[cimmeria|Cimmerian]] has no defense against magic except his primal stubbornness [005-xxx].

### Climax

[[conan|Conan]] eventually turns the tables: [[khemsa|Khemsa]]'s ambition leads to a confrontation with the Master of Yimsha, and [[conan|Conan]] exploits the chaos to rescue [[yasmina|Yasmina]] and engineer the destruction of the [[black-seers-of-yimsha|Black Seers]]' stronghold. The story ends with [[conan|Conan]] refusing [[yasmina|Yasmina]]'s offer of a throne in [[vendhya|Vendhya]], choosing the freedom of the hills over the constraints of kingship [005-xxx].

## Setting

The story spans the Afghuli hills, the [[vendhya|Vendhya]]n borderlands, and the Himelian Mountains, with the climactic scenes taking place at the [[black-seers-of-yimsha|Black Seers]]' fortress on Mount Yimsha. [[vendhya|Vendhya]] is Howard's fantasy analog of ancient India, complete with devis, rajput warriors, and a complex political landscape. The setting explores the easternmost reaches of the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] world.

## Themes

- **Magic vs. Will**: The central conflict is between the [[black-seers-of-yimsha|Black Seers]]' psychic domination and [[conan|Conan]]'s indomitable barbarian will. Magic in this story is overwhelmingly powerful, yet [[conan|Conan]]'s refusal to submit proves a match for it.
- **Ambition and Overreach**: [[khemsa|Khemsa]]'s trajectory — from acolyte to would-be master — illustrates the dangers of sorcerous ambition seeking power that mortal minds cannot control.
- **East vs. West**: The story explores Howard's fascination with the "mysterious East," presenting [[vendhya|Vendhya]] as a land of ancient wisdom, decadent intrigue, and terrible [[magic-in-the-hyborian-age|sorcery]].

## Publication

*The People of the Black Circle* was serialized in *Weird Tales* across three issues (September, October, November 1934). At approximately 35,000 words, it is the longest [[conan|Conan]] story Howard ever wrote. It was collected in *The Bloody Crown of [[conan|Conan]]* (Del Rey, 2004).

## Connections

- The [[Black Seers of Yimsha]] appear in this story as the primary antagonists; their mention in other tales establishes the continuity of the [[conan|Conan]] universe's occult underground.
- [[conan|Conan]]'s leadership among the [[Afghuli]] people is established here, connecting to his broader pattern of leading tribal and mercenary groups.
- The story's Eastern setting complements the Western European tone of *[[queen-of-the-black-coast|Queen of the Black Coast]]* and *[[the-devil-in-iron|The Devil in Iron]]*, showing [[conan|Conan]]'s range across the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] world.
