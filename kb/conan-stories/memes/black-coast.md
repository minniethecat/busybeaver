## Overview

The Black Coast is a stretch of the western shore of the [[vilayet-sea|Vilayet Sea]] — or perhaps the Western Ocean — infamous as the domain of pirates and raiders. It is most famously associated with [[Bêlit]], the Shemitish woman who styled herself the Queen of the Black Coast and led a crew of black raiders in a reign of terror over the shipping lanes.

> "She is called the queen of the black coast. She is a Shemite woman, who leads black raiders." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Bêlit's Domain

The Black Coast achieved its notoriety through Bêlit and her crew. A Shemite by birth, Bêlit commanded a company of black warriors who descended from the jungles of the coast to harry passing ships. Her butchers were said to destroy entire villages along the bay, and many a good tradesman had been sent to the bottom by her swift, murderous raids.

> "They harry the shipping and have sent many a good tradesman to the bottom." [[1]](#ref-1)

When [[Conan]] first encountered Bêlit, it was along the Black Coast, where his ship the Argus was attacked by her war galley, the Tigress. The meeting on that blood-soaked shore changed both their fates — Conan joined Bêlit's crew and became her lover, and together they terrorized the coast for months, their names becoming a byword for lawless violence.

> "I am Bêlit, queen of the black coast. Oh, tiger of the North, you are cold as the snowy mountains which bred you." [[2]](#ref-2)

## Geography

The Black Coast is characterized by dense jungle running down to the sea, with scattered villages and the ruins of more ancient civilizations dotting the shoreline. The coast's geography — with its hidden coves, treacherous reefs, and thick jungle cover — made it ideal for piracy, as raiders could strike without warning and vanish back into the green wilderness.

## Legacy

The Black Coast represents the wild frontier of the Hyborian world — a place beyond the reach of kings and laws, where only strength and cunning determined survival. Its name is inextricably linked with Bêlit and Conan's time together, one of the most passionate and violent chapters of the Cimmerian's life.
