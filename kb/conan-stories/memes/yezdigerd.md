## Overview

King Yezdigerd is the sovereign of [[Turan]], the great eastern empire of the Hyborian Age and one of the most powerful political forces in Howard's world. From his throne in Aghrapur, Yezdigerd directs a network of spies, agents, and sorcerers toward the conquest of neighboring kingdoms — most prominently [[Vendhya]], whose queen [[Yasmina]] he seeks to depose or control. Unlike direct antagonists who face [[Conan]] in personal combat, Yezdigerd operates as a distant but pervasive political force, his ambitions shaping events that draw heroes, villains, and sorcerers into his orbit.

> "Yezdigerd, king of Turan, was the mightiest monarch in the world" — the narrator establishes Yezdigerd's stature as the preeminent temporal ruler of the age [004-016]

## King of Turan

Turan under Yezdigerd is the dominant imperial power of the Hyborian Age east of Zamora. The narrator describes him in terms that position him above all other monarchs — his authority is continental in scale, his reach extending from the Vilayet Sea southward toward the Himelian Mountains and the kingdoms beyond [004-016]. Yezdigerd is not merely a regional chieftain or city-king; he commands an empire whose wealth, armies, and diplomatic apparatus rival anything in the western kingdoms.

The king is described as a "Hyrkanian" by his enemies, reflecting the Turanian ruling class's steppe-nomad origins [005-216]. This heritage makes him the inheritor of a conquering tradition — the Hyrkanian riders who swept down from the eastern steppes to carve an empire out of the older settled kingdoms. Yezdigerd represents the mature, institutionalized form of that conquest: a king seated on a throne, backed by bureaucracy and intelligence networks, rather than a khan on horseback.

## Expansionist Ambitions

Yezdigerd's defining characteristic is his drive for territorial expansion. "King Yezdigerd has long looked southward with greedy lust" [005-064] — the southern hill tribes that lie between Turan and Vendhya are seen as obstacles to be overcome, gateways to the rich kingdom beyond. His ambition creates the central political tension of *The People of the Black Circle*: the fate of [[Vendhya]] and its queen hangs on whether Yezdigerd's schemes succeed.

This expansionism is not merely conventional conquest. Yezdigerd is willing to employ methods that a simpler warlord would reject. He combines military pressure with sorcerous manipulation — "accomplished by their arts what Yezdigerd could not with a hundred thousand" [005-024] — acknowledging that certain obstacles can only be overcome through the Black Circle's magic. This pragmatic hybridization of political power and dark sorcery makes him a more dangerous adversary than a conventional king.

> "Accomplished by their arts what Yezdigerd could not with a hundred thousand" — the combination of sorcery and state power defines Yezdigerd's approach to conquest [005-024]

## Agents and Intelligence

Rather than leading armies personally, Yezdigerd governs through agents. [[Kerim Shah]] serves as his principal operative in Vendhya, dispatched to eliminate or neutralize [[Yasmina]] and disrupt the kingdom's resistance [005-023]. Kerim Shah acts with the confidence of royal backing: "I serve King Yezdigerd of Turan," he declares without hesitation or equivocation [005-023].

This reliance on agents reveals Yezdigerd's governing style: he is a strategist, not a warrior. The king delegates enforcement to proxies while retaining control of policy and objectives. His relationship with the Black Seers of Yimsha — the sorcerous circle led by a Master who has his own designs — illustrates the risks of this approach. The sorcerers serve Yezdigerd's aims only as far as they align with their own: "I am weary of Yezdigerd," the Master declares, revealing that the king's hold over his supernatural allies is tenuous at best [005-415].

> "I am weary of Yezdigerd," said the Master. "I have turned to other matters" — the Black Seers' loyalty is provisional, illustrating the fragility of Yezdigerd's coalition of convenience [005-415]
