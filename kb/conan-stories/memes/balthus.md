## Overview

Balthus is a young Aquilonian settler who travels to the embattled frontier province of [[Conajohara]] to seek his fortune — whether by taking up land or entering fort-service. He is the viewpoint character for much of *Beyond the Black River*, a narrative device that allows Howard to reveal the savage world of the Pictish frontier through the eyes of a newcomer. Balthus encounters [[Conan]] on the road to Fort Tuscelan, and what begins as a chance meeting becomes a trial by fire as the young frontiersman is thrust into the brutal realities of the border war.

> "My name is Balthus," he offered. "I was at Velitrium last night. I haven't decided whether I'll take up a hide of land, or enter fort-service." [008-020]

## Arrival on the Frontier

Balthus comes from the [[Tauran]], the settled region east of Thunder River where the comforts of civilization still hold sway [008-034]. He represents a type common among the newcomers to Conajohara — more settler than woodsman, brave but untested, carrying frontier ambitions without yet possessing frontier instincts.

> Most of the frontiersmen were like Balthus — more of the settler than the woodsman type. [008-036]

His first encounter with [[Conan]] establishes the gulf between civilized naivete and barbarian hard-won knowledge. When Balthus insists that the Picts are divided and can never unite, Conan grimly corrects him — the Picts are gathering under the war-drum of [[Zogar Sag]], and the border is about to erupt [008-024].

The conversation reveals Balthus's family connection to the frontier's violent history: his uncle was at the [[Siege of Venarium]], one of the few who escaped the Cimmerian slaughter of the Aquilonian fort. Balthus has grown up hearing tales of that red disaster — and is stunned to learn that the man walking beside him, Conan, was one of the barbarians who poured over those very walls [008-026][008-028].

## Trial by Fire

Balthus's education in frontier survival begins almost immediately. He and [[Conan]] discover the mutilated body of the merchant Tiberias on the trail, slain by an unknown horror that is not — as Balthus assumes — a Pict, but something darker summoned by [[Zogar Sag]]'s sorcery [008-043][008-044]. What follows is a harrowing flight through the forest as the same creature stalks them, culminating in the decapitation of Tiberias's corpse on the litter they carried [008-086].

> "But what thing is it that can cry like a woman and laugh like a devil, and shines like witch-fire as it glides through the trees?" gasped Balthus, mopping the sweat from his pale face. [008-088]

At Fort Tuscelan, Balthus volunteers eagerly for Conan's scouting expedition across Black River into Pictish territory. His hunting experience on the Tauran gives him some competence — he can paddle a canoe quietly, a skill that earns him a measure of respect from the hardened borderers [008-133][008-139]. But Tauran deer-hunts have not prepared him for the silent lethality of the Pictish wilderness.

## Captivity and Horror

After Conan and his raiders leave the canoes to strike at the Pictish village, Balthus waits with one companion. The Picts strike from the water with lethal silence, cutting his companion's throat and taking Balthus captive [008-144]. He wakes bound to a stake in the village of Gwawela, surrounded by hundreds of warriors — far more than a single village should hold. Clans have gathered from across the wilderness, united by [[Zogar Sag]]'s dark authority [008-151][008-154].

What follows is among the most harrowing sequences in the Conan canon. Balthus witnesses Zogar Sag summon a saber-tooth tiger — a creature of primordial legend — which destroys the other captive woodsman in a single spring [008-164][008-168][008-169]. Then a second horror: the Ghost Snake, a pale serpent of ancient terror, glides through the gate to claim Balthus himself [008-174][008-175].

> Balthus became incapable of emotion. The horror of his fate paralysed him. That was the reptile that the ancients called Ghost Snake, the pale, abominable terror that of old glided into huts by night to devour whole families. [008-176]

Conan's well-aimed throwing-spear transfixes the serpent's neck, and Balthus is freed in the chaos that follows [008-178][008-181]. The two fight their way out of the village, scaling the stockade and fleeing into the forest with the enraged Picts in pursuit [008-186][008-188].

## Character and Growth

Balthus is defined by his decency and courage rather than by any extraordinary skill. He is not a warrior of Conan's caliber — he struggles to keep pace, blunders through undergrowth, and his stamina fails under prolonged exertion. Yet he never breaks. When his companion's throat is cut beside him in the canoe, he stabs back blindly with his knife before being overcome [008-145]. When captured and facing unspeakable death, he endures the terror without losing his mind.

His development across the story traces the hardening of a settler into something closer to a frontiersman. The Tauran deer-hunter who boasted that the Picts could never unite learns, in the fires of Gwawela, exactly what the unified clans are capable of. He emerges alive, but not unchanged.

## Relationships

- [[Conan]] — mentor, rescuer, and companion; Balthus's admiration for the Cimmerian shifts from apprehension to awe as he witnesses Conan's woodcraft, combat prowess, and unshakeable nerve
- [[Zogar Sag]] — the Pictish shaman whose sorcery and ambition drive the events of the story; Balthus's captor and would-be executioner
- [[Valannus]] — the governor of Conajohara, whose wine Balthus shares at Fort Tuscelan before the scouting mission [008-122]
- [[Tiberias]] — the murdered merchant whose corpse Balthus helps carry, only to see it decapitated by the swamp demon
