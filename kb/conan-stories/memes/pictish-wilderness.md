# Pictish Wilderness

## Overview

The Pictish Wilderness is the vast, trackless region west of the Bossonian marches, stretching from the borders of Vanaheim in the north to the southernmost shores of Zingara. It is the homeland of the Picts, a fierce barbarian race whose clans have inhabited these lands since before the Great Cataclysm (017-002). The Wilderness represents the untamed edge of the Hyborian world — a dark forest of perpetual threat to the civilized kingdoms.

## Geography

The Wilderness is a country of thick forests, torrential rivers, and rugged hills. The Black River forms its eastern boundary, separating Pictish territory from the Bossonian marches and Aquilonia (008-021). The land is rich in iron ore, discovered and exploited by the Picts only after the Nemedian priest Arus taught them the art of mining and smelting (017-036). Before this, the Picts traded "hides, whale's teeth, walrus tusks and such few things as savages have to trade" with their neighbors (017-033).

## The Frontier

The boundary between the Pictish Wilderness and Aquilonian civilization is the central setting of **Beyond the Black River**. Fort Tuscelan, an Aquilonian outpost on the Black River, represents the easternmost thrust of the Hyborian races — "Beyond the river the primitive still reigned, with its geometry of blood and violence and stark, naked survival" (008-093). Conan, who has ventured deep into the Wilderness more than once, understands the Picts better than the settlers who live in constant fear of them.

## Strategic Importance

The Pictish Wilderness is strategically critical to Hyborian history. It is from these forests that the Pictish hordes under Gorm eventually burst forth to overwhelm the Bossonian lines and conquer Aquilonia (017-042). The Wilderness serves as both a barrier and a breeding ground — a buffer that protects civilization from what lies beyond, and simultaneously the source of the force that will eventually destroy it.
