# Picts

## Overview

The Picts are a barbarian race in Robert E. Howard's fictional [[hyborian-age|Hyborian Age]], dwelling in the trackless [[pictish-wilderness|western wilderness]] beyond the [[bossonian-marches|Bossonian]] marches. They are one of the oldest continuous peoples in Howard's fictional history, surviving the [[great-cataclysm|Great Cataclysm]] that destroyed [[atlanteans|Atlantis]] and Lemuria, and persisting through millennia of struggle against more advanced civilizations. At the time of [[conan|Conan]], they are fierce tribal warriors locked in a centuries-long border conflict with the [[aquilonia|Aquilonian]] empire (017-032).

## Description

Physically, the Picts are a white race of short stature, very dark complexion, with black eyes and black hair (008-070). Unlike the tall, tawny-haired [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]]s or the fair-skinned [[nordheim|Nordheimir]], the Picts have maintained their distinct racial type unchanged for thousands of years (017-027). They are a practical, prolific people, lacking the artistic sensibilities of their ancient [[atlanteans|Atlantean]] rivals but excelling in the production of effective flint and later iron weaponry (017-007).

The origin of the Picts stretches back to the [[pre-cataclysmic-age|Pre-Cataclysmic Age]], when they inhabited islands far out on the western ocean. When [[great-cataclysm|the Cataclysm]] reshaped the world — sinking [[atlanteans|Atlantis]] and Lemuria — the Pictish Islands were heaved up to form the mountain peaks of a new continent (017-005). A colony of Picts settled on the [[valusia|Valusian]] southern frontier survived the disaster and became the seed of the later Pictish nation (017-006). Through the millennia that followed, the Picts remained savages, "apparently defying the laws of Nature by neither progressing nor retrogressing" (017-018).

Their culture is clan-based, with tribes frequently at feud with one another (017-033). They live by hunting, supplemented by crude agriculture learned from neighboring peoples. Their customs are bloodthirsty and include human sacrifice, blood-feud, and the burning alive of captives — practices that the Nemedian priest [[arus|Arus]] would later attempt to reform (017-034). Before contact with civilization, the Picts worked crudely in copper and tin; they later learned iron-working from [[arus|Arus]], which transformed them into a far more formidable military power (017-033)(017-036).

## In the Stories

The Picts feature most prominently in the story **Beyond the Black River**, where they are the primary antagonists. The tale is set on the [[aquilonia|Aquilonian]] frontier at Fort Tuscelan on the Black River, the boundary between civilized [[aquilonia|Aquilonia]] and the [[pictish-wilderness|Pictish Wilderness]]. The Picts, under the leadership of the wizard [[zogar-sag|Zogar Sag]], are gathering for war against the outnumbered [[aquilonia|Aquilonian]] settlers (008-021)(008-048). [[conan|Conan]] describes the Picts as divided into small clans who "have always fought each other" — but warns that a unifying force like [[zogar-sag|Zogar Sag]] could unite "forty clans" against the frontier (008-024).

The Picts' mode of warfare relies on stealth and ambush rather than open battle. The story opens with a Pict warrior silently killing an [[aquilonia|Aquilonian]] settler near the stockade (008-011). The Pictish wizard [[zogar-sag|Zogar Sag]] commands supernatural powers derived from the swamp demons of the wilderness, adding a magical dimension to the Pictish threat (008-048)(008-379). The grim reality of Pictish warfare is underscored by references to prisoners being tortured at the "Pictish stake" (008-220).

In **[[the-hyborian-age-essay|The Hyborian Age]]**, an expository essay that serves as Howard's fictional history, the full arc of Pictish civilization is traced. Under the leadership of the chief [[gorm|Gorm]], and with the unintended aid of the Nemedian priest [[arus|Arus]], the Picts learned iron-working and united into a confederation capable of challenging the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kingdoms (017-036)(017-040). This culminated in the eventual overthrow of the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] civilization, marking one of the great historical ironies of Howard's world (017-029).

## Related Entities

- **[[zogar-sag|Zogar Sag]]** — A Pictish wizard and war-leader who unites the clans for an assault on the [[aquilonia|Aquilonian]] frontier in *Beyond the Black River* (008-048)
- **[[gorm|Gorm]]** — The Pictish chief who, with [[arus|Arus]]'s unwitting aid, transformed the Picts from scattered clans into a unified military power (017-034)(017-040)
- **[[arus|Arus]]** — A Nemedian priest of [[mitra|Mitra]] who taught the Picts iron-working in an attempt to civilize them, only to enable their conquest of the [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kingdoms (017-032)(017-041)
- **[[bossonian-marches|Bossonian Marches]]** — The fortified frontier region between [[aquilonia|Aquilonia]] and the [[pictish-wilderness|Pictish Wilderness]], settled by a mixed-race people who serve as a buffer against Pictish raids (017-028)(017-033)
- **[[atlanteans|Atlanteans]]** — The ancient rivals of the Picts during the Stone Age following [[great-cataclysm|the Cataclysm]]; the Picts' superior numbers and unity eventually drove the [[atlanteans|Atlanteans]] back into savagery (017-007)(017-008)
- **[[aquilonia|Aquilonia]]** — The mightiest [[hyborian-age|Hyborian]] kingdom, whose western borders face constant Pictish pressure and whose kings employ Picts as mercenaries (017-039)
