# Darfar

Darfar is one of the southern [[Black Kingdoms]] in Robert E. Howard's Conan stories, known throughout the Hyborian world for its cannibalistic cults and the notorious thievery of its people. Situated south of [[Stygia]] and [[Kush]], Darfar represents the darker and more savage periphery of Howard's Hyborian Age map, where the raids of its inhabitants threaten the frontier settlements of the northern kingdoms.

## Geography

Darfar occupies the grasslands and savannahs of the far southern Black Kingdoms, beyond Kush and south of the Stygian frontier. Its proximity to the mercenary outpost of [[Sukhmet]] makes it a constant threat to the northern settlements along the Stygian border:

> ...desperate adventurers of many races guard the Stygian frontier against the raids that come up like a red wave from Darfar. [010-014]

The border regions of Darfar are patrolled by mercenary companies such as Zarallo's Free Companions, employed by Stygia and the northern powers to hold back Darfari incursions. The landscape is described as level grasslands (the "steppes" or savannahs of central Africa), with the border town of Sukhmet serving as the primary defensive outpost against Darfari raids:

> "I saw I'd been stung when we marched to the Darfar border. The pay was poor and the wine was sour, and I don't like black women." [010-038]

## Inhabitants

The Darfari are a black people distinct from their northern neighbors. They are characterized in Howard's stories by two prominent traits: ritual cannibalism and compulsive thievery. Both traits are treated as deeply embedded in their culture rather than individual vices.

### Cannibalistic Cult

Cannibalism among the Darfari is not merely a survival practice but an integral part of their religious or cultic observances:

> Cannibalism was more than a perverted appetite with the black men of Darfar; it was an integral element of their ghastly cult. [009-055]

In "Shadows in Zamboula," the Darfari cannibal slaves who inhabit the city's underworld gather at night to the sound of a black drum, roasting human flesh in pits beyond the palm groves. The innkeeper Aram Baksh collaborates with them, selling human victims in exchange for stolen goods:

> The slaves from Darfar were notorious thieves; there was no doubt that some of their pilfered loot found its way into the hands of Aram Baksh. And in return he sold them human flesh. [009-052]

The Darfari file their teeth as a cultural marker, distinguishing them from other black peoples such as the Wadai, whose teeth are unfiled and whose hair is "cropped close to the skull" [009-115]. This dental modification serves as a visual signifier of their otherness throughout the stories.

### Mercenary Activity

Despite their fearsome reputation, Darfari also appear in more conventional roles. Conan encounters a Darfari cannibal slave in Zamboula [009-051], and Darfari women are among those who frequent the mercenary camps at Sukhmet — though Conan notes disdainfully that they are "the only kind that came to our camp at Sukhmet—rings in their noses and their teeth filed" [010-038].

## History

Darfar's history in the Conan stories is defined by its position as a persistent threat on the Stygian frontier. The kingdom lies beyond the immediate reach of Stygian control, and its raids northward have been a factor in the military organization of the border regions for generations.

The Stygian empire maintains mercenary companies specifically to guard against Darfari incursions. Zarallo's Free Companions were brought south for this purpose, as the Shemite trader informs Valeria:

> "...Zarallo had brought his Free Companies south to guard the Darfar border. No better employment offered." [010-039]

Conan himself served in these border forces, and he and Valeria both desert from the army stationed on the Darfar border, fleeing "far to the north" toward the coast [012-028].

## In the Stories

Darfar features primarily as a background element — a place of origin for threatening figures and a source of menace on the frontier — rather than a setting visited directly by the protagonists:

- **"Shadows in Zamboula"** — Darfari cannibal slaves terrorize the night-time streets of Zamboula; Conan discovers the connection between Aram Baksh and the cannibals, and identifies a Darfari by his filed teeth [009-051, 009-052, 009-055, 009-114, 009-115]
- **"A Witch Shall Be Born"** — Valeria flees through the grasslands from the Darfari border region, and Conan recounts his service with Zarallo's Free Companions at the Darfar border [010-014, 010-038, 010-039]
- **"Red Nails"** — Valeria is mistaken for a Darfari woman until it is noted that "the suns of Darfar never burnt your white skin brown" [011-016]; Conan and Valeria identify themselves as deserters from the Darfar border [012-028]
