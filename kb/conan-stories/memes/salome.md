## Overview

Salome is the witch-twin of Queen [[Taramis]] of [[Khauran]], and one of the most purely villainous figures in the Conan cycle. Born with the witch-mark — a crescent-shaped birthmark that in Khauran signified sorcerous destiny — she was cast out as an infant on the word of the priests [006-022]. She survived, grew into a woman of terrible beauty and darker power, and returned to Khauran to claim what she considered her birthright. Her reign over Khauran, conducted under her sister's stolen name, lasted seven months and reduced the kingdom to near-ruin before [[Conan]] arrived with his Zuagir army to end it.

> "I am Salome. I too am Salome. It was always Salome, even before the name of Taramis was ever spoken." [006-027]

## The Witch-Marked Twin

Salome and Taramis were born as twin daughters to the royal house of Khauran. The priests of [[Ishtar]] examined the infants and found upon one the unmistakable crescent-shaped witch-mark — a birthmark that in Khauran's tradition foretold a life of sorcery and evil. Acting on the priests' decree, the child was taken from the palace and abandoned in the desert to die [006-022].

She did not die. Found and raised beyond the borders of civilization, Salome survived into adulthood, and the dark potential the priests feared was fully realized. She became a sorceress of considerable power, mastering arts of illusion, necromancy, and torment [006-177]. All the while, she nursed a cold hatred for the sister who had been permitted to live and reign while she had been thrown away.

> "They carried me into the desert and left me to die. But I did not die." [006-022]

## The Usurpation

Salome's return to Khauran was swift and surgical. Disguising herself as a wandering noblewoman, she infiltrated the palace and confronted Taramis in the queen's own chambers [006-020]. The resemblance between the twins was uncanny — only the witch-mark on Salome's body, hidden beneath her garments, distinguished them physically. Within hours, Taramis had been overpowered by Salome's henchmen, dragged to the dungeons of the south tower, and replaced at court by her identical twin [006-081].

The coup was executed in concert with [[Constantius]], the Kothic voivode known as the Falcon. Salome had promised him a share of Khauran's wealth and the title of royal consort in exchange for the military muscle of his Shemitish Free Companies [006-073]. The palace guards, deceived by Salome's perfect impersonation, opened the gates to Constantius's mercenaries without resistance.

> "Call me Taramis. We must become accustomed to the name." [006-071]

## Reign of the Witch-Queen

For seven months Salome ruled Khauran — not as herself, but wearing Taramis's face and name. Her reign was a calculated campaign of cruelty, driven partly by the strategic demands of paying Constantius's mercenaries and partly by a deeper, more personal sadism. The citizens of Khauran, bewildered by their beloved queen's sudden transformation into a tyrant, suffered systematic extortion, torture, and execution [006-169].

Constantius served as her consort and enforcer, his Shemitish soldiers lording over the Hyborian populace. Salome herself oversaw the torture of her imprisoned sister, visiting Taramis in the dungeons to gloat over her suffering. She did not simply want Taramis dead — she wanted Taramis to watch as everything she had loved was destroyed under the mask of her own identity [006-174].

> "So far it is only your hand that has suffered. Wait until I have finished — you will envy the dead." [006-174]

## Sorcery and Dark Arts

Salome's power extended beyond political manipulation into genuine sorcery. She practiced necromancy, demonstrated when she summoned the severed head of a dead man to speak prophecy to her — a grisly ritual that revealed Conan's approach with the Zuagir host [006-269]. She wielded illusions and protective wards around the palace, and her chambers were filled with the apparatus of dark magic: crystal globes, serpentine sigils, and forbidden texts [006-260].

Her sorcery was practical rather than philosophical — she used it as a tool for power, intelligence-gathering, and intimidation. Unlike the cosmic horrors summoned by other Howard antagonists, Salome's magic was intimate and personal, rooted in the twisted bond between twins and the ancient fear of the witch-marked child [006-177].

> "Salome!" shrieked the bloody head. "Salome!" — the severed head, compelled to speak by her necromancy [006-270]

## The Fall

Salome's reign ended when [[Conan]], who had served as captain of Taramis's guard before the usurpation, returned to Khauran at the head of a Zuagir desert army. Conan had learned the truth of the identity swap from Olgerd Vladislav and had rallied the desert tribes with promises of plunder [006-241]. The Battle of Khauran broke Constantius's forces on the plain, and Conan led the assault into the city itself.

Salome did not flee. In the final moments, cornered in her tower chambers, she faced Conan directly. The confrontation was brief and lethal — Conan's sword ended the witch-queen as it had ended so many other horrors. Her death marked the restoration of the true Taramis to the throne and the end of Khauran's nightmare [006-283].
