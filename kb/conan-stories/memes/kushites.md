## Overview

The Kushites are the black inhabitants of Kush, a vast territory lying south of [[stygia|Stygia]] along the eastern coast of the southern continent. Kushites appear throughout the Conan stories in many roles — as slaves, mercenaries, eunuch servants, and free raiders — reflecting both the reach of the Stygian empire and the vitality of the Kushite people.

## Kush and Its People

Kush is one of the [[black-kingdoms|Black Kingdoms]] that lie south of Stygia. While Stygian rule once extended into Kushite territory, the black kingdoms eventually rose against their northern masters. In the late Hyborian period, the Stygian aristocracy was overthrown in part due to the "thrusts of the black kingdoms to the south."

Kushite warriors were prized as slaves and soldiers. Their physique — tall, powerful, and muscular — made them valued as elite guards and eunuchs in the courts of the powerful. Kushite eunuchs, "images of shining ebony in silken pantaloons," served in the households of Turanian lords.

> "an impassive Kushite eunuch, an image of shining ebony in silken pantaloons." [[1]](#ref-1)

## Kushite Coast and Trade

The Kushite coast along the Western Ocean was a stopping point for ships trading between the Hyborian kingdoms and the far south. Shemitish traders frequented the port of Zabhela on the Kushite coast, exchanging news and goods between the northern and southern worlds.

> "Off Zabhela, it was. There a Shemite trader told me that Zarallo had brought his Free Companies south to guard the Darfar border." [[2]](#ref-2)

## Kushites in the Slave Trade

Kushites feature prominently in the slave economy of the Hyborian Age. The city of Zamboula, built by the Stygians as a trading post, became a hub for the slave trade, with its population a heterogeneous mix of Stygian colonists, Shemitish traders, Kushite slaves, and [[darfari|Darfari]] cannibals.

Kushite slaves fill roles ranging from palace eunuchs to plantation laborers. Their treatment varies from the gilded cages of imperial courts to the brutal conditions of agricultural slavery.

## Significance

The Kushites represent the black civilizations of the south in Howard's Hyborian geography — peoples who, while often subjugated by the northern empires, retain their own kingdoms, cultures, and capacity for rebellion. Their presence throughout the Conan stories, in roles ranging from the abject to the powerful, reflects the multi-ethnic reality of the Hyborian Age.
