# Khosatral Khel

Khosatral Khel is an ancient demonic being who appears as the primary antagonist in Robert E. Howard's Conan story "The Devil in Iron" (published in *Weird Tales*, August 1934). He is a creature of living iron, a survivor of the [[Pre-Cataclysmic Age]] who ruled the city of Dagon on the island of [[Xapur]]. His body is impervious to conventional weapons, and only a magical Yuetshi knife can break the spell that animates him.

## Origins and Nature

Khosatral Khel is described as a being from the Abyss who "crawled up from Nergal's dark realm" in an age before the rise of men [004-113]. He belongs to the same order of pre-human entities that populated [[Thuria]] during the Pre-Cataclysmic Age. Howard describes his essence in cosmic terms:

> The terrific essence that was Khosatral Khel... stood up in the shape and likeness of a man... [004-114]

He established dominion over the primitive human tribes of the region, demanding worship and sacrifice. His city, Dagon, was built on the island of Xapur, where he reigned as a living god:

> On the altars of Khosatral they died... [004-116]

Only a magical Yuetshi knife could harm him—"against the unearthly knife the magic of Khosatral was impotent" [004-117]. A rebellion led by the Yuetshi priest used this artifact to break Khosatral's power, leaving him lying "upon the golden dais" in the depths of the fortress [004-118].

## Reawakening

When Conan and the Turanians explore the ruined fortress of Dagon, Khosatral Khel is revived after millennia of dormancy. His reanimation begins when an intruder inadvertently touches the magical knife embedded in his body [004-120]. Conan's first sight of the awakened demon is memorable:

> And Conan knew that this was Khosatral Khel, the ancient from the Abyss... [004-130]

The demon spreads his great arms—a gesture of primordial menace—and no words are exchanged [004-131]. He immediately attacks Conan, who finds that his steel sword shatters against Khosatral's iron body [004-134].

The demon is described as possessing enormous physical strength. His body, "the dusky metal of Khosatral's body," is nearly invulnerable [004-180]. The shock of encountering a creature immune to weapons drives Conan to retreat through the fortress.

## Battle with Conan

The confrontation between Conan and Khosatral Khel unfolds across the fortress of Dagon. Khosatral pursues Conan and [[Octavia]] relentlessly, smashing through walls and doors:

> With a crash of bending boughs Khosatral Khel was upon them. [004-178]

Conan's desperate fight involves multiple encounters across different levels of the fortress. At one point, Khosatral looms over the Cimmerian in the darkness [004-133]; at another, Conan's sword shatters uselessly against the demon's chest [004-134]. Conan manages to temporarily blind Khosatral by throwing a heavy object at his head [004-135], buying moments to retreat.

Eventually, Conan recovers the magical Yuetshi knife—the only weapon that can harm the demon. With it, he strikes a decisive blow, and "Khosatral reeled and fell" [004-183]. The demon's body, no longer sustained by its protective magic, is vulnerable to the ancient weapon:

> Under its edge the dusky metal of Khosatral's body gave way like common flesh. [004-180]

## Significance

Khosatral Khel is one of the most formidable supernatural enemies Conan faces in Howard's original stories. Unlike human antagonists who rely on political power or physical combat, Khosatral represents a remnant of a fundamentally alien and more powerful age—a theme Howard returns to across the Conan corpus. The demon's combination of physical invulnerability and ancient malevolence makes him a memorable example of Howard's cosmic horror blended with sword-and-sorcery.

The character draws on Howard's recurring interest in pre-human civilizations and the "survivals" that emerge into the Hyborian Age. Khosatral Khel is the last remnant of Dagon—a civilization that predates even the rise of the [[Atlanteans]].
