## Appears in

- [[Chapter-1|Chapter 1: An Habitation Enforced]] — recurring presence, arbiter of local social standards (001-504 through 001-1046)

## Role

Lady Conant is a local aristocrat and social [[order|authority]] figure in the [[england|English countryside]] where the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s settle. She serves as a moral and social touchstone — her opinions carry weight in the community, and other characters invoke her name to signal what is proper or scandalous. She takes a direct interest in [[sophie-chapin|Sophie Chapin]] after meeting her at lunch, discussing the history of Friars Pardon and the controversial [[earth|land]] purchases by [[sangres|Mr. Sangres]] (001-673).

## Characteristics

Lady Conant is portrayed as direct, authoritative, and attuned to local affairs. Her voice is described as clear and [[order|command]]ing (001-637). She has strong opinions about propriety — the state of Friars Pardon before the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s is declared a scandal (001-531). Yet she is also capable of apology and warmth, expressing regret when she has overstepped (001-651).

## Key actions

- Declares the state of Friars Pardon a scandal, signaling her role as guardian of local standards (001-531)
- Speaks to [[sophie-chapin|Sophie Chapin]] at lunch, explaining the history of Friars Pardon and [[sangres|Mr. Sangres]]'s dealings (001-673)
- Apologizes to [[sophie-chapin|Sophie]] after a social misstep (001-651)
- Sends a gift to the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s at Friars Pardon after they settle (001-1046)

## Relationships

- **[[Sophie Chapin]]**: Takes an interest in her and tutors her in local affairs
- **[[George Chapin]]**: Acknowledges his transformation from American financier to English [[earth|land]]holder
- **[[Sangres|Mr. Sangres]]**: Disapproves of his [[earth|land]] dealings around Friars Pardon

## Significance

Lady Conant embodies the established social order of the [[england|English countryside]]. Her approval represents the community's acceptance of the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s as more than outsiders — it marks their integration into the fabric of rural English life. Her narrative function is to validate [[sophie-chapin|Sophie]]'s growing connection to the [[earth|land]].
