## Location

Burnt House is one of the five farms of the [[friars-pardon|Friars Pardon]] estate in the [[england|English]] Sussex Weald (001-321). It lies "behind High Pardons, where that brook is all blocked up" (001-286), and "under High Pardons Wood, before you come to Gale Anstey" (001-287).

## Description

Burnt House is situated in a wooded, rural setting under High Pardons Wood. The farm is part of the working landscape of the Friars Pardon estate, where the [[mr-cloke|Cloke]] family serves as the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s' intermediaries with the land and its tenants. [[sophie-chapin|Sophie Chapin]] delights in the possession of "all the farms" including Burnt House (001-520).

The farm has practical significance: [[mr-cloke|Mr. Cloke]] is "due at Burnt House to see about the new well" (001-836), showing the ongoing maintenance and improvement that the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s' stewardship brings to the estate.

## Significance

Burnt House, like the other Friars Pardon farms, represents the decentralised, working character of the English country estate. It is not merely property but a place of labour and inhabitation — the site of wells, brooks, and the daily life that the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s must learn to understand and manage.
