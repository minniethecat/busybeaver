The Sheshaheli is a tribal country in Africa, significant to the narrative of "[[A Deal in Cotton]]" (Chapter 9). It is the region where [[Strickland]] and his associates conduct political and commercial operations involving the local tribe.

## The Region

The Sheshaheli country was known for its fierce and independent tribal inhabitants. The Sheshahelis were described as a people with whom outsiders had difficulty establishing peaceful relations [009-???].

## In "A Deal in Cotton"

The Sheshaheli became central to the cotton scheme when Strickland and the Infant planned to use the region's political instability for commercial advantage. The region's chiefs were involved in treaty negotiations [009-503], and the threat of burning villages before lunch was casually discussed as a colonial tactic [009-148].

## The Sheshaheli People

The Sheshahelis were known for practices including cannibalism, though this was difficult to prove in courts [009-???]. The remark that "there's no market for Sheshaheli" [009-???] underscored the region's marginal position in colonial commerce, with locals being compared unfavorably to crocodiles as trading commodities.
