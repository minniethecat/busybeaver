## Location

Gale Anstey is one of the five farms belonging to the [[friars-pardon|Friars Pardon]] estate in the [[england|English]] Sussex Weald (001-321). It lies within the network of farms — Rocketts, Burnt House, Gale Anstey, and the Home Farm — that surround the main house.

## Description

Gale Anstey is an old farm with its own history and residents. [[george-chapin|George Chapin]] contemplates laying out a golf links over its land: "Lay out a Morristown links over Gale Anstey. I dare say you could hire a man to do it" (001-340). The farm's track is visible from the surrounding countryside — a landmark in the local geography (001-706).

The farm is home to Old [[whybarne|Whybarne]], "of Gale Anstey" (001-776), an elder who has seen seventy-eight years and embodies the deep-rooted rural perspective. When fever threatens, [[sophie-chapin|Sophie Chapin]] is warned to "keep away from Gale Anstey till I make sure" (001-840), reflecting the farm's place in the community's network of concern and care.

## Significance

Gale Anstey represents the decentralised structure of the Friars Pardon estate — the [[earth|land]] is not a single property but a constellation of farms, each with its own character and inhabitants. Through Gale Anstey, Kipling shows how the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s' ownership extends beyond the main house into the working life of the countryside.
