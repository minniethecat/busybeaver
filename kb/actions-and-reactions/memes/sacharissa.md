## Appears in

- [[Chapter-5]]: The [[mother-hive|Mother Hive]] (005-181 through 005-503)

## Role

Sacharissa is the antagonist of "The [[mother-hive|Mother Hive]]" — a [[wax-moth|Wax-moth]] who infiltrates [[mother-hive|the hive]] in disguise, laying eggs at an alarming rate and spreading corruption throughout the colony. She operates through charm, manipulation, and misdirection, presenting herself as a harmless visitor while systematically undermining [[mother-hive|the hive]]'s foundations. Her presence precipitates the crisis that forces the loyal bees to swarm and seek a new home.

## Characteristics

Sacharissa is seductive, cunning, and utterly self-serving. She caresses the bees she meets, using physical [[love|affection]] to disarm suspicion (005-030, 005-097). She presents a facade of piety — [[melissa|Melissa]] is told "you should see her saintly life" (005-077) — while her egg-laying threatens [[mother-hive|the hive]]'s survival. Her note changes instantly when she realizes a cell is sealed (005-198), revealing the predator beneath the charm. She is also ambitious and dismissive of [[mother-hive|the hive]]'s traditions, lowering her voice to say "I wish she'd go!" about the Queen (005-218). Most tellingly, when the crisis culminates, Sacharissa speaks of her destructive work with satisfaction: "My work... I'm glad Man does not see" (005-483).

## Key actions

- Approaches [[melissa|Melissa]] at the Gate under the guise of a harmless visitor, immediately beginning her seduction (005-026 through 005-030)
- Claims to "devote herself to rearing young" while secretly laying wax-moth eggs (005-077)
- Reveals her true predatory nature when frustrated by a sealed cell (005-194 through 005-198)
- Lowers her voice to express contempt for the Queen: "I wish she'd go!" (005-218)
- Continues laying eggs throughout [[mother-hive|the hive]], contributing to the colony's decline (005-104, 005-207)
- During the Great Burning, watches her work unfold: "My work... I'm glad Man does not see" (005-483)
- Witnesses the destruction of the old hive: "Oh, see! The Great Burning that Our..." (005-503)

## Relationships

- **[[melissa|Melissa]]**: Sacharissa's first target within [[mother-hive|the hive]]. She attempts to charm and manipulate the young worker bee, but [[melissa|Melissa]]'s sound instincts prove resistant to her influence
- **The Queen**: Sacharissa views the Queen as an obstacle and speaks dismissively of her behind her back, wishing for her departure
- **The Hive**: Sacharissa operates as a parasite within [[mother-hive|the hive]] organism, exploiting its social structures and trust to destroy it from within

## Significance

Sacharissa represents the threat of internal subversion — the enemy who does not attack from outside but infiltrates and corrupts from within. Kipling uses the Wax-moth as an allegory for ideological or political infiltration: she arrives unthreatening, speaks the language of the community, and uses its institutions against it. Her name (Sacharissa) and her seductive behavior suggest a critique of certain social types in Edwardian [[england|England]]. The fact that [[mother-hive|the hive]]'s old guard cannot recognize her threat — only young bees like [[melissa|Melissa]] grow suspicious — underscores the story's theme that institutions blind to internal decay are doomed.
