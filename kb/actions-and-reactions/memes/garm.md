## Appears in

- [[Chapter-3|Chapter 3: Garm — a Hostage]] — central figure throughout the chapter (003-016 through 003-999)

## Role

Garm is a bull-terrier of exceptional quality — "one of the finest bull-terriers... of the old-fashioned breed, two parts bull and one terrier" (003-017). He is given to the narrator by a soldier friend named [[stanley|Stanley]] as repayment for being rescued from military discipline. [[garm-as-hostage|Garm's transfer]] between owners — the soldier who raised him, the narrator who receives him — forms the emotional core of the chapter and explores themes of loyalty, love, and the bond between humans and animals.

## Characteristics

Garm is described as pure white with a fawn-coloured saddle marking (003-019). He is highly trained, capable of performing elaborate tricks including standing upright, shaking hands, and feigning death (003-027). Beyond his physical and trained qualities, Garm possesses an emotional intelligence that sets him apart: the narrator describes a dog like him as "a patient, temperate, humorous, wise soul, who knows your moods before you know them yourself" (003-062).

## Key actions

- Given by [[stanley|Stanley]] to the narrator as repayment for a rescue (003-017)
- Performs elaborate trained tricks: standing, saluting, feigning burial (003-027)
- Grieves openly for his former master on his first night with the narrator (003-073)
- Gradually accepts the narrator as his new master

## Relationships

- **[[stanley|Stanley]]**: Garm's original owner, a soldier who raised and trained him
- **The Narrator**: Garm's new owner, who receives him as a gift
- **[[vixen|Vixen]]**: The narrator's fox-terrier, jealous of Garm's arrival

## Significance

Garm embodies a central question in the story: whether a dog's loyalty can be transferred, and what it means to love an animal that has loved another. The soldier's anguished note — "please do not give him back to me, for I'm not going to take him, if you will keep him" (003-050) — underscores the sacrificial nature of the gift and elevates Garm beyond a mere pet into a symbol of love given at great cost.
