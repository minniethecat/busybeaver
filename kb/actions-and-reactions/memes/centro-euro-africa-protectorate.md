## Type

A colonial protectorate — a territory under partial British control, administered through a combination of British officials and local structures. Kipling's Centro-Euro-Africa Protectorate appears to be a fictional composite representing the newer African colonial territories of the early 20th century.

## Appears in

- [[Chapter-9|Chapter 9: A Deal in Cotton]] — the posting of Adam [[strickland|Strickland]] (009-025)

## Description

The Protectorate is where Adam [[strickland|Strickland]], son of [[Strickland]] of the [[punjab-police|Punjab Police]], serves as Assistant-Commissioner at a place called Dupé (009-026). The Infant's confusion about its location — "Somali[[earth|land]], ain't it?" — and [[stalky|Stalky]]'s scornful correction — "You're only three thousand miles out" (009-028) — suggests it is a remote, little-understood posting even among Anglo-Indians familiar with colonial geography. The Protectorate represents the newer, less romanticized phase of British imperial expansion in Africa.

## Members

- **Adam [[strickland|Strickland]]** — Assistant-Commissioner at Dupé (009-026)

## Role in story

The Protectorate serves as the next-generation counterpart to the [[punjab-police|Punjab Police]] — where the father served in established British India, the son serves in the newer, rawer African territories. This generational shift from India to Africa mirrors the historical evolution of British imperialism in the early 20th century. The Protectorate's fictional nature allows Kipling to comment on imperial administration without being tied to a specific colony.
