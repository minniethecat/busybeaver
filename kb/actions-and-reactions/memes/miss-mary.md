## Appears in

- [[Chapter-15]]: The [[house-surgeon|House Surgeon]] (015-346 through 015-733)

## Role

Miss Mary Moultrie is the eldest of the three Moultrie sisters and the most assertive figure in the family. Living with her younger sister [[miss-elizabeth|Miss Elizabeth]] at the Hydro near [[house-surgeon|Holmescroft]] (015-439), she becomes a central participant in the narrator's investigation of the house's mysterious depressive [[madness|affliction]]. Her [[order|command]]ing personality drives the action forward, and she ultimately voices the crucial discovery that the house's evil influence caused her youngest sister [[miss-aggie|Agnes]]'s death (015-708).

## Characteristics

Miss Mary is imperious, sharp-tongued, and fiercely determined. She "ordered him about like a" servant (015-454) and shows "genuine rage" (015-543) against her sister and cousin. Yet beneath her severity lies genuine grief and a hunger for truth — she alone among the sisters actively presses for answers about what happened to [[miss-aggie|Agnes]]. Her hand going "at her own throat" (015-484) reveals the physical manifestation of the house's oppressive presence on her.

## Key actions

- [[order|Order]]s Baxter about imperiously at the Hydro (015-454)
- Wrestles with Miss Elizabeth near the open window during a crisis (015-482, 015-485)
- Lurches toward the open window as if compelled (015-501)
- Writhes and fights for breath under the house's influence (015-507)
- Breaks free and applies herself to a task with fierce determination (015-514, 015-527)
- Calls Baxter an "hireling" in genuine rage at her sister (015-538)
- Declares the critical revelation: "[[miss-aggie|Aggie]] didn't do it!" — that [[miss-aggie|Agnes]] did not commit suicide (015-708)
- Speaks of saying a prayer for her dead sister (015-710)

## Relationships

- **[[miss-elizabeth|Miss Elizabeth]]**: Miss Mary's younger sister, with whom she wrestles and argues yet ultimately protects. Their physical struggles (015-482) embody the tension the house creates between them
- **[[miss-aggie|Agnes Moultrie]]**: The deceased youngest sister, whose death drives Miss Mary's quest for truth. Miss Mary initially appears to resent [[miss-aggie|Agnes]] but ultimately clears her name (015-708)
- **[[john-baxter|John Baxter]]**: The solicitor whom Miss Mary treats as an "hireling" (015-538) but who assists the sisters through their crises
- **The Narrator**: Miss Mary leans toward him with urgent communication (015-651), recognizing him as an ally in uncovering the truth

## Significance

Miss Mary Moultrie embodies the intersection of [[order|authority]] and vulnerability. As the eldest sister, she [[order|command]]s those around her with imperious certainty, yet the house's [[madness|affliction]] strips her of control — she writhes, lurches, and fights for breath like those she [[order|command]]s. Kipling uses her to show that no strength of personality can withstand the psychological poison the house exudes. Her final declaration exonerating [[miss-aggie|Agnes]] represents the triumph of truth over the house's insidious lies.
