## Summary

A soldier named [[stanley|Stanley]], rescued by the narrator from a disciplinary incident at [[mian-mir|Mian Mir cantonment]], repays the debt by giving his prize bull-terrier [[garm|Garm]] to the narrator. The transfer is emotionally devastating for both dog and former owner — [[stanley|Stanley]] runs away immediately after delivering the dog and his note, while [[garm|Garm]] howls and grieves. The event explores the profound cost of such a gift and the question of whether a dog's loyalty can be transferred.

## Participants

- **[[stanley|Stanley]]** — the soldier who gives [[garm|Garm]] away as repayment

- **[[Garm]]** — [[garm|the bull-terrier]] at the center of the transaction
- **[[vixen|Vixen]]** — the narrator's fox-terrier, immediately jealous

## Cause

Three days before the gift, the narrator rescued [[stanley|Stanley]] from a drunken incident at [[mian-mir|Mian Mir]] — driving him home, hiding him from the military guard, and covering for him with his lieutenant (003-008). [[stanley|Stanley]]'s gift of [[garm|Garm]] is explicitly framed as repayment: "I give you the dog because of what you got me out of" (003-047).

## Consequence

The transfer creates lasting emotional bonds and tensions. [[garm|Garm]] grieves openly for his former master (003-073), while [[vixen|Vixen]] becomes fiercely jealous of the newcomer (003-068). The narrator must navigate the complex dynamics of receiving a gift that carries such emotional weight, ultimately accepting the [[duty|responsibility]] of becoming [[garm|Garm]]'s new master.
