[010-001]Who gives him the Bath?

[010-002]“I,” said the wet,

[010-003]Rank Jungle-sweat,

[010-004]“I'll give him the Bath!”

[010-005]Who'll sing the psalms?

[010-006]“We,” said the Palms.

[010-007]“Ere the hot wind becalms,

[010-008]We'll sing the psalms.”

[010-009]Who lays on the sword?

[010-010]“I,” said the Sun,

[010-011]“Before he has done,

[010-012]I'll lay on the sword.”

[010-013]Who fastens his belt?

[010-014]“I,” said Short-Rations,

[010-015]“I know all the fashions

[010-016]Of tightening a belt!”

[010-017]Who buckles his spur?

[010-018]“I,” said his Chief,

[010-019]Exacting and brief,

[010-020]“I'll give him the spur.”

[010-021]Who'll shake his hand?

[010-022]“I,” said the Fever,

[010-023]“And I'm no deceiver,

[010-024]I'll shake his hand.”

[010-025]Who brings him the wine?

[010-026]“I,” said Quinine,

[010-027]“It's a habit of mine,

[010-028]I'll come with his wine.”

[010-029]Who'll put him to proof?

[010-030]“I,” said All Earth,

[010-031]“Whatever he's worth,

[010-032]I'll put to the proof.”

[010-033]Who'll choose him for Knight?

[010-034]“I,” said his Mother,

[010-035]“Before any other,

[010-036]My very own knight!”

[010-037]And after this fashion, adventure to seek,

[010-038]Was Sir Galahad made--as it might be last week!
