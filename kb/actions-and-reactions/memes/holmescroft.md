Holmescroft is a country residence in England, built in 1863, and home to the M'Leod family. It serves as the primary setting of "[[The House Surgeon]]" (Chapter 15), where a mysterious depression afflicts its inhabitants.

## Description

Holmescroft was a large, two-storied, low, creeper-covered residence [015-079]. The house was surrounded by grounds including a tennis court and gravel walks [015-150]. It featured a south verandah from which the entire property could be illuminated [015-157].

## The Depression

The house was notable for a peculiar affliction experienced by its residents — a profound emotional depression that affected anyone who stayed there [015-015]. The depression was so pervasive that it spoiled all social enjoyment [015-041]. Notably, there had been no death in the house since it was built [015-018], ruling out a conventional haunting.

## Residents

The house was purchased by [[Mr. M'Leod|Mr. Mcleod]], who lived there with his wife [[Mrs M'Leod|Mrs. Mcleod]] and their daughter [[Miss M'Leod|Miss Mcleod]]. The family's experience of the house's strange affliction forms the core of the story.
