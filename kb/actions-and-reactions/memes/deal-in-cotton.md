## Summary

"A Deal in Cotton" brings together old Anglo-Indian friends — the narrator, Colonel [[stalky|Corkran]] ([[stalky|Stalky]]), [[strickland|Strickland]], and the Infant — at the Infant's English estate. The gathering frames a tale-within-a-tale: Adam [[strickland|Strickland]], the son of [[strickland|Strickland of the Punjab Police]] and now an Assistant-Commissioner in Africa, recounts a negotiation over cotton that tests his resourcefulness and character in the colonial service. The story bridges the Indian and African spheres of British imperialism through the [[strickland|Strickland]] family.

## Cause

The reunion is a semi-regular gathering called by the Infant: "The Infant called me up by wire" (009-016). The [[strickland|Strickland]]s arrive with their son Adam and his native servant, creating the occasion for storytelling and reflection on careers spanning India and Africa.

## Consequence

The gathering illustrates the continuity of Anglo-Indian service across generations — from [[strickland|Strickland]]'s [[punjab-police|Punjab Police]] career to Adam's African posting in the [[centro-euro-africa-protectorate|Centro-Euro-Africa Protectorate]] (009-025). The cotton deal itself, told in the embedded narrative, tests Adam's abilities in the newer imperial sphere and affirms the values passed from father to son.
