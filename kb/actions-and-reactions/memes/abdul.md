## Appears in

- [[Chapter-13|Chapter 13: Little Foxes]] — Mr. [[mr-groombride|Groombride]]'s interpreter throughout the village visit, from the steamer [[earth|land]]ing to the riot and its aftermath (013-552 through 013-593)

## Role

Abdul is the interpreter engaged by Mr. [[mr-groombride|Groombride]] for his mission to the Taremabad district. Ostensibly a servant, he is in fact an agent of the local intelligence network, working with Farag to orchestrate a farcical reception for his employer. His translation "services" systematically convert Mr. [[mr-groombride|Groombride]]'s earnest philanthropic speeches into comedy for the villagers, while feeding the local authorities reports on [[mr-groombride|Groombride]]'s movements.

## Characteristics

A "yellowish boy" who has eaten and drunk well with Farag before the mission begins (013-552), Abdul presents the surface of an obliging servant while exercising deft control over events. He is quick-witted and opportunistic — when asked whether he stole [[mr-groombride|Groombride]]'s perfume bottles, he replies simply, "I am his servant. I took two" (013-585). He displays no guilt or divided loyalty, treating his employer's mission with cheerful contempt.

## Key actions

- Eats and drinks with Farag before the village visit, receiving his instructions (013-552)
- Translates [[mr-groombride|Groombride]]'s opening address to the villagers, omitting the substance and converting it to entertainment (013-567 through 013-579)
- Announces [[mr-groombride|Groombride]] as "the afflicted of God" who will "make sport" and "consume you with mirth" (013-579)
- Reveals to the assembled elders that [[mr-groombride|Groombride]] "knows less than a buffalo" about [[earth|land]] tenure (013-591)
- Declares [[mr-groombride|Groombride]] was driven out of his own [[earth|land]] by "Demah-Kerazi which is a devil inhabiting crowds and assemblies" (013-591)

## Relationships

- **[[Mr. Groombride]]**: His employer, whom he systematically undermines while maintaining a veneer of servile obedience
- **Farag**: His handler in the local intelligence network; they coordinate the village farce together
- **The Inspector**: The colonial official who monitors [[mr-groombride|Groombride]]'s activities through Abdul's reports

## Significance

Abdul embodies Kipling's satirical point in "[[little-foxes|Little Foxes]]": the would-be reformer, arriving with abstract principles, is no match for the local knowledge networks he cannot perceive. Abdul functions as [[mr-groombride|Groombride]]'s blindfold — the more loyally he "interprets," the more completely his employer is made a figure of fun. His casual admission of theft ("I took two") underscores the story's thesis that moral earnestness without local understanding is merely a spectacle.
