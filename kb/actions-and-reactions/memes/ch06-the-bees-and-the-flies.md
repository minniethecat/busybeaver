[006-001]A farmer of the Augustan age

[006-002]Perused in Virgil's golden page,

[006-003]The story of the secret won

[006-004]From Proteus by Cyrene's son

[006-005]How the dank sea-god sowed the swain

[006-006]Means to restore his hives again

[006-007]More briefly, how a slaughtered bull

[006-008]Breeds honey by the bellyful.

[006-009]The egregious rustic put to death

[006-010]A bull by stopping of its breath:

[006-011]Disposed the carcass in a shed

[006-012]With fragrant herbs and branches spread.

[006-013]And, having thus performed the charm,

[006-014]Sat down to wait the promised swarm.

[006-015]Nor waited long... The God of Day

[006-016]Impartial, quickening with his ray

[006-017]Evil and good alike, beheld

[006-018]The carcass--and the carcass swelled!

[006-019]Big with new birth the belly heaves

[006-020]Beneath its screen of scented leaves;

[006-021]Past any doubt, the bull conceives!

[006-022]The farmer bids men bring more hives

[006-023]To house the profit that arrives;

[006-024]Prepares on pan, and key and kettle,

[006-025]Sweet music that shall make 'em settle;

[006-026]But when to crown the work he goes,

[006-027]Gods! What a stink salutes his nose!

[006-028]Where are the honest toilers? Where

[006-029]The gravid mistress of their care?

[006-030]A busy scene, indeed, he sees,

[006-031]But not a sign or sound of bees.

[006-032]Worms of the riper grave unhid

[006-033]By any kindly coffin lid,

[006-034]Obscene and shameless to the light,

[006-035]Seethe in insatiate appetite,

[006-036]Through putrid offal; while above

[006-037]The hissing blow-fly seeks his love,

[006-038]Whose offspring, supping where they supt,

[006-039]Consume corruption twice corrupt.
