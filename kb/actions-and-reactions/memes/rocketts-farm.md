## Location

Rocketts is a farm in the southern counties of [[england|England]], "four miles from a station" and remote enough to feel isolated from modern life (001-079). It is the home of Mr. and [[mrs-cloke|Mrs. Cloke]], who host the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s at the recommendation of [[mrs-shonts|Mrs. Shonts]]. The farm sits amid trees, pastures, and barns — a [[garden|landscape]] that embodies the "genuine [[england|England]] of folklore and [[music|song]]" (001-078).

## Appears in

- [[Chapter-1|Chapter 1: An Habitation Enforced]] — the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s' rural retreat (001-077 through 001-999)

## Description

The [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s arrive at Rocketts in darkness, seeing only the shadowy outlines of "trees, kine, and the outlines of barns" (001-081). The farmhouse itself is ancient, with "a deep stone-floored kitchen" (001-083) and an attic bedroom with "a wavy whitewashed ceiling" (001-084). A wood fire burns in "an iron basket on a brick [[home|hearth]]" (001-085). Morning reveals "the noises of birds, the smell of box lavender, and fried bacon, mixed with an elemental smell they had never met before" (001-087).

## Significance

Rocketts is the physical manifestation of [[the-recall|The Recall]] — the [[england|English countryside]] that draws the displaced American [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s back to ancestral roots. Its ancient, unchanging character contrasts with the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s' urban American life, and the farm becomes the agent of George's healing and transformation. [[sophie-chapin|Sophie]]'s observation — "I feel farther away from anywhere than I've ever felt in my life" (001-093) — captures the farm's power to disconnect visitors from their former lives.
