## Appears in

- [[Chapter-11|Chapter 11: The Puzzler]] — [[lord-lundie|Lord Lundie]]'s Italian servant who operates the barrel-organ during the monkey-catching expedition (011-177 through 011-274, 011-347)

## Role

Giuseppe is an Italian servant on [[lord-lundie|Lord Lundie]]'s country estate. His principal contribution to "[[the-puzzler|The Puzzler]]" is practical rather than verbal: he owns and operates the barrel-organ that becomes the central instrument in [[sir-christopher-tomling|Sir Christopher Tomling]]'s scheme to catch a monkey. When the monkey escapes into the house, Giuseppe carries the organ inside on his shoulders — an Orpheus-like figure whose music is meant to charm the animal back to captivity.

## Characteristics

Giuseppe is described visually through his "dark face between flashing earrings, a twisted rag of red and yellow silk round his throat" (011-238). He is a man of few words but decisive action — when [[jimmy|Jimmy]] the interpreter explains the plan, Giuseppe simply unships the organ mechanism from its wheels, slings the strap round his shoulders, and leads the procession into the house (011-274). He possesses the Italian organ-grinder's sentimental [[love|attachment]] to his instrument, taking it to his room at night to play for his own pleasure (011-198).

## Key actions

- Reports on the monkey's condition and habits to [[jimmy|Jimmy]] (011-177)
- Takes the barrel-organ mechanism off its wheels at [[jimmy|Jimmy]]'s instruction (011-194)
- Carries the organ into the house on his shoulders to lure the monkey out of the bathroom (011-267 through 011-274)
- Leads the musical procession through the house in the role of "Orpheus" (011-274)
- Sings at his work, his voice mixing with the organ as it plays through the night to keep the monkey entertained at the Foreign Office (011-347)

## Relationships

- **[[Lord Lundie]]**: His employer, who recoils from the idea of entering another man's house with a hurdy-gurdy (011-267)
- **[[Jimmy]]**: The interpreter who relays Giuseppe's observations and directs his actions with the organ
- **[[Sir Christopher Tomling]]**: The expedition's [[order|command]]er, who orders Giuseppe to unchain the monkey and later carry the organ inside

## Significance

Giuseppe represents the practical, unreflective competence that enables the schemes of the great men around him. While the judges and statesmen debate ethics ("Isn't this burglary or something?"), Giuseppe simply shoulders the organ and walks into the house. His barrel-organ music — sentimental, Italian, popular — provides the story's comic soundtrack and becomes the literal instrument of statecraft when the monkey (and later the Gyascutis) is installed at the Foreign Office.
