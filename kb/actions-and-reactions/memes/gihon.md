The Gihon is a great river in Africa, central to the events of "[[Little Foxes]]" (Chapter 13). Its cataracts and surrounding landscape provide the dramatic backdrop for the Gihon Hunt, a fox-hunting expedition that reveals the tensions of colonial administration.

## Geography

The Gihon is described as a powerful river whose roar of cataracts in flood dominates the surrounding landscape [013-667]. The river valley forms a natural corridor through the tawny African terrain, with a carpet of vegetation between the river and the desert line [013-021].

## The Gihon Hunt

The [[Gihon Hunt|The Gihon Hunt]] was a fox-hunting event organized along the river valley, where the whole procession of the Hunt was silhouetted against the landscape [013-222]. The hunt was led by [[Lord Lundie]] and involved colonial administrators using the sport to maintain morale and English traditions in a remote outpost.

## Colonial Context

The region around the Gihon was part of British colonial administration in Africa. The river served both as a geographical landmark and as a boundary between cultivated lands and wilderness. Local villages along the river, such as those visited by [[Mr. Groombride]] [013-527], marked the frontier of colonial influence.
