## Role

Mrs. Cloke is the wife of the farmer at [[rocketts-farm|Rocketts]], the countryside farm where George and [[sophie-chapin|Sophie Chapin]] settle during George's recovery from nervous exhaustion (001-082). She and her husband serve as the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s' hosts, bridging the gap between the American outsiders and rural English life. Through her tales and local knowledge, Mrs. Cloke becomes one of the primary channels through which [[sophie-chapin|Sophie]] learns the history and tensions of the [[earth|land]] around Friars Pardon.

## Characteristics

Mrs. Cloke is warm, maternal, and possessed of a rich store of local stories. Kipling describes her as a compelling narrator: she throws incident and interpretation over her tales in a way that captivates [[sophie-chapin|Sophie]] (001-270). She is physically [[love|affection]]ate, patting [[sophie-chapin|Sophie]]'s shoulder when she is unwell (001-415). She is also practical and efficient — when [[lady-conant|Lady Conant]] calls for tea, Mrs. Cloke runs and returns swiftly (001-458). Her manner with her social superiors is deferential but not servile; she murmurs and smooths her apron in the presence of [[lady-conant|Lady Conant]] (001-274, 001-453).

## Key actions

- Welcomes the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s at the door of [[rocketts-farm|Rocketts]] farm (001-082)
- Tells [[sophie-chapin|Sophie]] the serial tale of Friars Pardon, its history and scandals (001-264)
- Comforts [[sophie-chapin|Sophie]] after she is frightened by the fowlhouse incident (001-412 through 001-415)
- Serves [[lady-conant|Lady Conant]] tea at a moment's notice (001-458)

## Relationships

- **[[George Chapin]]**: Her American guest, whom she hosts with country hospitality
- **[[Sophie Chapin]]**: Develops a warm relationship with her, sharing local stories and comfort
- **[[Lady Conant]]**: Defers to her as the local aristocrat, addressing her as "my lady" (001-458)
- **[[mr-cloke|Mr. Cloke]]**: Her husband, with whom she shares the storytelling duties (001-264)

## Significance

Mrs. Cloke serves as the human face of the [[england|English countryside]] that works its transformative power on the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s. Through her stories, [[sophie-chapin|Sophie]] comes to understand the deep history and social fabric of the [[earth|land]] she and George will eventually make their own. Mrs. Cloke embodies Kipling's theme of rootedness — she belongs to the [[earth|land]] in a way the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s initially cannot, and her quiet wisdom helps them begin to belong as well.
