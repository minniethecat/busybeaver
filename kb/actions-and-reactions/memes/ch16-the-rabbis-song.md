[016-001]If thought can reach to Heaven,

[016-002]On Heaven let it dwell,

[016-003]For fear that Thought be given

[016-004]Like power to reach to Hell.

[016-005]For fear the desolation

[016-006]And darkness of thy mind,

[016-007]Perplex an habitation

[016-008]Which thou hast left behind.

[016-009]Let nothing linger after--

[016-010]No whispering ghost remain,

[016-011]In wall, or beam, or rafter,

[016-012]Of any hate or pain:

[016-013]Cleanse and call home thy spirit,

[016-014]Deny her leave to cast,

[016-015]On aught thy heirs inherit,

[016-016]The shadow of her past.

[016-017]For think, in all thy sadness,

[016-018]What road our griefs may take;

[016-019]Whose brain reflect our madness,

[016-020]Or whom our terrors shake.

[016-021]For think, lest any languish

[016-022]By cause of thy distress

[016-023]The arrows of our anguish

[016-024]Fly farther than we guess.

[016-025]Our lives, our tears, as water,

[016-026]Are spilled upon the ground;

[016-027]God giveth no man quarter,

[016-028]Yet God a means hath found;

[016-029]Though faith and hope have vanished,

[016-030]And even love grows dim;

[016-031]A means whereby His banished

[016-032]Be not expelled from Him!
