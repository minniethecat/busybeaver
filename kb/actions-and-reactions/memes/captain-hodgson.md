## Appears in

## Role

Captain Hodgson is the relief captain of the General Post Office [[dirigible|airship]] "162," sharing bridge duties with [[captain-purnall|Captain Purnall]] on the night mail run. He functions as the narrator's primary guide to the technical and philosophical dimensions of transatlantic flight. Where Purnall is the active pilot, Hodgson is the explainer — he opens portholes, points out [[earth|land]]marks, delivers observations on aviation history, and comments wryly on the realities of the mail service.

## Characteristics

Physically, Hodgson is "large and red" (007-018), a contrast to the small, dark Purnall. He shares with his fellow captain the "brooding sheathed glance characteristic of eagles and aeronauts" — the thousand-yard stare of men who navigate naked space (007-019). His temperament is reflective and slightly sardonic. He observes that the inventor of the bow-rudder "went out of his mind because he said he couldn't serve his country any more," adding, "I wonder if any of us ever know what we're really doing" (007-060). He maintains professional composure in emergencies but is unafraid to call a situation "a filthy business" when the job requires it (007-265).

## Key actions

- Introduces himself as relief captain of "162" at the G.P.O. Captains' Room (007-018)
- Explains the history of Magniac's rudder and Castelli's gullwing curve to the narrator (007-055 through 007-061)
- Opens the great colloid underbody porthole for the narrator to watch [[london|London]] recede (007-087)
- Identifies the late-running Bombay Mail and checks his watch: "She's forty minutes late" (007-093)
- Monitors the approach to Cork and the interaction with The Shamrock liner (007-144)
- Participates in the rescue operation when the Lundt & Bleamers boat is sighted in distress, muttering about "Owner's risk" (007-170 through 007-265)

## Relationships

- **[[Captain Purnall]]**: His fellow captain on "162"; they share bridge duties with practiced coordination
- **[[Mr. Geary]]**: The G.P.O. official who sees them off from the despatching towers
- **The Engineer**: Hodgson works closely with him, shunting gas and controlling trim during the rescue operation
- **The Narrator**: Hodgson serves as the narrator's patient explainer of aeronautical technology and postal routine

## Significance

Captain Hodgson embodies the professional competence and philosophical detachment of the G.P.O. aeronaut. His throwaway remark about Magniac — "I wonder if any of us ever know what we're really doing" — is the story's quietest and most resonant statement of its technological sublime. Hodgson and his colleagues operate at the edge of human capability, where a three-eighths-inch adjustment of a rudder plate means five miles of yaw and where rescuing a foundering dirigible is just another night's work. His "large and red" physical presence and unflappable commentary [[earth|ground]] the story's futuristic wonder in recognizably human character.
