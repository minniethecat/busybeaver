## Appears in

- [[Chapter-7|Chapter 7: With the Night Mail]] — central figure, [[order|command]]ing the [[dirigible|airship]] throughout the night flight (007-017 through 007-593)

## Role

Captain Purnall is the [[order|command]]ing officer of the [[aerial-board-of-control|Aerial Board of Control]] [[dirigible|airship]] designated "162." He pilots the vessel on its night-mail run across the Atlantic, managing the complex systems of the dirigible while navigating through turbulent weather. He is the operational center of the story: every maneuver, every altitude change, and every encounter with other craft passes through his hands.

## Characteristics

Purnall is calm, decisive, and physically adept — he moves with a speed Kipling compares to a cat (007-593). He [[order|command]]s without bluster, issuing quiet orders that his crew obeys instantly. When the weather turns dangerous, he becomes more focused rather than agitated, giving instructions in an undertone during an emergency rescue (007-298). He prefers a steep climb to the stars rather than hovering at intermediate altitudes (007-181).

## Key actions

- Launches [[dirigible|airship]] "162" on the night-mail transatlantic route (007-030)
- Dives inside the vessel to check instruments when a storm approaches (007-036)
- Takes manual control of the wheel during difficult conditions (007-100)
- Rings up the engines and adjusts speed at five-fathom level (007-114)
- Executes a rescue maneuver for a sinking vessel, coordinating with a lone operator (007-298 through 007-306)
- Brings "162" down for [[earth|land]]ing, closing the gaslocks (007-253)

## Relationships

- **[[George Chapin]]**: Noted as lacking Purnall's catlike swiftness, though still a capable man (007-593)
- **The relief captain**: Works alongside Purnall during the flight (007-017)
- **The crew of "162"**: Responds instantly to his [[order|command]]s

## Significance

Captain Purnall represents the ideal of technical mastery and calm professionalism in Kipling's futuristic vision of aerial transport. He embodies the [[aerial-board-of-control|Aerial Board of Control]]'s values: competence, discipline, and complete [[order|command]] of complex machinery. His rescue of the sinking vessel demonstrates the ethical dimension of this professionalism — technical skill in service of human life.
