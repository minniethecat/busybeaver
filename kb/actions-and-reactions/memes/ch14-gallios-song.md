[014-001]All day long to the judgment-seat

[014-002]The crazed Provincials drew--

[014-003]All day long at their ruler's feet

[014-004]Howled for the blood of the Jew.

[014-005]Insurrection with one accord

[014-006]Banded itself and woke:

[014-007]And Paul was about to open his mouth

[014-008]When Achaia's Deputy spoke

[014-009]“Whether the God descend from above

[014-010]Or the man ascend upon high,

[014-011]Whether this maker of tents be Jove

[014-012]Or a younger deity--

[014-013]I will be no judge between your gods

[014-014]And your godless bickerings,

[014-015]Lictor, drive them hence with rods--

[014-016]I care for none of these things!

[014-017]“Were it a question of lawful due

[014-018]Or a labourer's hire denied,

[014-019]Reason would I should bear with you

[014-020]And order it well to be tried

[014-021]But this is a question of words and names

[014-022]And I know the strife it brings,

[014-023]I will not pass upon any your claims.

[014-024]I care for none of these things.

[014-025]“One thing only I see most clear,

[014-026]As I pray you also see.

[014-027]Claudius Caesar hath set me here

[014-028]Rome's Deputy to be.

[014-029]It is Her peace that ye go to break

[014-030]Not mine, nor any king's,

[014-031]But, touching your clamour of 'conscience sake,'

[014-032]I care for none of these things!”
