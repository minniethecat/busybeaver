[004-001]There is sorrow enough in the natural way

[004-002]From men and women to fill our day;

[004-003]But when we are certain of sorrow in store,

[004-004]Why do we always arrange for more?

[004-005]Brothers and sisters, I bid you beware

[004-006]Of giving your heart to a dog to tear.

[004-007]Buy a pup and your money will buy

[004-008]Love unflinching that cannot lie--

[004-009]Perfect passion and worship fed

[004-010]By a kick in the ribs or a pat on the head.

[004-011]Nevertheless it is hardly fair

[004-012]To risk your heart for a dog to tear.

[004-013]When the fourteen years which Nature permits

[004-014]Are closing in asthma, or tumour, or fits,

[004-015]And the vet's unspoken prescription runs

[004-016]To lethal chambers or loaded guns,

[004-017]Then you will find--it's your own affair

[004-018]But... you've given your heart to a dog to tear.

[004-019]When the body that lived at your single will

[004-020]When the whimper of welcome is stilled (how still!)

[004-021]When the spirit that answered your every mood

[004-022]Is gone--wherever it goes--for good,

[004-023]You will discover how much you care,

[004-024]And will give your heart to a dog to tear!

[004-025]We've sorrow enough in the natural way,

[004-026]When it comes to burying Christian clay.

[004-027]Our loves are not given, but only lent,

[004-028]At compound interest of cent per cent.

[004-029]Though it is not always the case, I believe,

[004-030]That the longer we've kept 'em, the more do we grieve:

[004-031]For, when debts are payable, right or wrong,

[004-032]A short-time loan is as bad as a long

[004-033]So why in Heaven (before we are there!)

[004-034]Should we give our hearts to a dog to tear?
