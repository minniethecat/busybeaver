## Type

A financial/business combine — a corporate entity formed through the merger or coordination of multiple firms. In the context of early 20th-century American [[money|finance]], a "combine" implied an aggressive consolidation of market power, often through hostile tactics.

## Appears in

- [[Chapter-1|Chapter 1: An Habitation Enforced]] — the business entity [[george-chapin|Chapin]] was dismantling (001-008)

## Description

The Holz and Gunsberg Combine is the corporate target that [[george-chapin|George Chapin]] was pursuing when he collapsed from overwork. It is described as having "shivered beneath his knife" (001-016) — [[george-chapin|Chapin]] was in the process of dismantling or acquiring it through aggressive financial tactics. After [[george-chapin|Chapin]]'s collapse, Gunsberg himself "full of condolences, came to the steamer and filled the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s' suite of cabins with overwhelming flower-works" (001-017), a gesture that mixes genuine respect with competitive triumph.

## Members

- **Gunsberg** — one of the principals, who sends flowers to the departing [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s (001-017)

## Role in story

The Combine serves as the symbol of the [[earth|world]] [[george-chapin|Chapin]] leaves behind — the arena of American [[money|finance]] where his identity was forged and then broken. Its defeat at [[george-chapin|Chapin]]'s hands, followed by his own physical collapse, establishes the story's central tension between ambition and health, work and life. The Combine's presence in the opening paragraphs also situates the story in the [[earth|world]] of transatlantic business, before the narrative shifts to the [[england|English countryside]].
