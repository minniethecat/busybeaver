## Summary

A grey [[wax-moth|Wax-moth]] infiltrates an overcrowded beehive weakened by heat and social tension. She dodges the senior Guard bees by slipping into the brood-frame where young, inexperienced bees tolerate strangers. There she begins laying eggs while posing as a "working-sister" who sympathizes with the young bees' grievances against the harsh Guard. Her infiltration represents the entry of parasitic ideology into a stressed community.

## Chapter

[[Chapter-5|Chapter 5: The Mother Hive]] — the inciting event (005-013 through 005-040)

## Participants

- **The [[wax-moth|Wax-moth]]** — the infiltrator, an allegorical parasite
- **[[melissa|Melissa]]** — a young worker bee returning from her first honey-flight, the moth's first target (005-006)
- **The Guard** — the harsh gate-keeper whose rudeness creates the grievance the moth exploits (005-009)
- **Young bees** — the naive audience in the brood-frame who tolerate the stranger (005-016)

## Cause

The hive was "old and overcrowded" with "sickness or parasites" inevitable in such conditions (005-001). The heat of the June honey-flow had stressed the entire colony (005-003). The Guard's rude treatment of [[melissa|Melissa]] — "Buzz in, and be foul-brooded to you! Next!" (005-009) — created the atmosphere of grievance [[sacharissa|the Wax-moth]] exploits.

## Consequence

The [[wax-moth|Wax-moth]] begins systematically undermining [[mother-hive|the hive]]: laying eggs among the young, sowing discord between generations, and framing herself as a martyr for "principles" (005-033). The event sets in motion [[mother-hive|the hive]]'s gradual corruption, which the story traces through to its eventual purging and renewal.
