## Appears in

- [[Chapter-5]]: The [[mother-hive|Mother Hive]] (005-020 through 005-463)

## Role

Melissa is one of the central protagonists of "The [[mother-hive|Mother Hive]]," a young worker bee whose practical skepticism and innate loyalty to the Hive drive the story's action. She is the first to directly encounter [[sacharissa|the Wax-moth]] ([[sacharissa|Sacharissa]]) and gradually becomes aware of the existential threat facing [[mother-hive|the hive]]. Despite her initial bewilderment, Melissa's courage and resourcefulness make her instrumental in organizing the loyal swarm that preserves [[mother-hive|the hive]]'s future.

## Characteristics

Melissa embodies the sound, no-nonsense temperament of a good worker bee. She has "all a sound bee's" instincts (005-054) and a cheerful directness — when [[sacharissa|the Wax-moth]] tells her not to take offense, she answers "None taken... I shall be" (005-048). Melissa is curious but [[earth|ground]]ed, questioning the stranger's egg-laying with practical indignation: "But why do you lay eggs? You lay 'em like a Queen" (005-104). Her defining trait is her unwavering work ethic — when asked what she does, her reply is simply: "I work with the Hive" (005-099). She is also capable of decisive action when the crisis demands it, urging the Princess onward with a roar: "On! Out! Up!" (005-445).

## Key actions

- Engages the disguised [[wax-moth|Wax-moth]] in conversation at the Gate, becoming the first bee to sense something amiss (005-020 through 005-038)
- Questions [[sacharissa|the Wax-moth]]'s excessive egg-laying, showing early suspicion of the infiltrator (005-104)
- Suggests sending out a swarm as a solution to [[mother-hive|the hive]]'s mysterious decline: "Suppose we sent out a swarm?" (005-228)
- Discovers the truth about the drones' fate, whispering "Is it possible?" (005-254)
- As the experienced Melissa, finds a far-off location for the loyal swarm to settle (005-362)
- Helps the Princess escape through the chaos of the Great Burning (005-418 through 005-445)
- Rallies the Princess with the cry "The old legends have come true!" as they face the fire (005-450)

## Relationships

- **[[sacharissa|Sacharissa]] ([[sacharissa|the Wax-moth]])**: Melissa is the first bee [[sacharissa|the Wax-moth]] encounters and attempts to manipulate. Their relationship begins with cryptic conversation and ends in open [[war|conflict]] as [[sacharissa|the Wax-moth]]'s true nature is revealed
- **The Queen**: Melissa is devoted to the Hive's Queen, checking on her wellbeing during the crisis: "How's our Queen?" (005-349)
- **The Princess**: During the swarm's escape, Melissa becomes the Princess's protector and guide, physically guiding her past obstacles and through the flames
- **Fellow worker bees**: Melissa is respected among her peers — a companion asks her "What is the [[earth|world]] like, Melissa?" (005-020), and she later educates young bees during the confusion (005-371)

## Significance

Melissa represents the ideal of loyal, productive citizenship in Kipling's allegorical hive. Her progression from bewildered worker to decisive leader mirrors the theme of ordinary individuals rising to meet existential threats. Unlike the older, more rigid bees who cannot accept change, Melissa adapts — she questions [[order|authority]] when necessary but never abandons her duty. Her character arc — from "I work with the Hive" to leading the Princess through the fire — embodies Kipling's argument that survival depends on the courage and initiative of the common worker, not just queens and leaders.
