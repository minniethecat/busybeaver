## Appears in

- [[An Habitation Enforced]] (001-077, 001-082, 001-257, 001-264, 001-289, 001-297, 001-335, 001-342, 001-559, 001-564, 001-586)

## Role

Mr. Cloke is the tenant farmer at [[rocketts-farm|Rocketts]], the farm in the southern counties where George and [[sophie-chapin|Sophie Chapin]] first alight after leaving America. He and his wife [[Mrs. Cloke]] serve as the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s' first hosts in [[england|England]], providing not only lodging but also a deep connection to the [[earth|land]] and its history. Cloke is a storyteller — Kipling notes that local tales were "told serially by Cloke in the barn, or his wife in the house" (001-264) — and his slow, careful observations help the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s understand the social fabric of their new surroundings.

## Characteristics

Cloke is deliberate in speech and manner. He speaks "slowly" (001-559), rubbing his knees as he talks, and offers warnings about the transience of outsiders with the [[order|authority]] of long experience (001-297). [[sophie-chapin|Sophie]] refers to him as "old man Cloke" (001-289), a phrase that captures both his age and his rootedness in the local community. Despite his humble station, Cloke possesses a quiet wisdom — his observations about [[earth|land]] ownership and inheritance prove prescient as the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s begin their transformation into English [[gentleman|gentry]].

## Key actions

- Welcomes the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s at the door of [[rocketts-farm|Rocketts]] alongside his wife (001-082).
- Tells tales in the barn that educate [[sophie-chapin|Sophie]] about local history and tensions (001-264).
- Observes the frequency of the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s' visits and draws conclusions about their intentions (001-559).
- Flanks the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s during the pivotal moment when they are "swept forward irresistibly" into their new life (001-586).

## Relationships

- **[[Mrs. Cloke]]**: His wife and partner in storytelling. Together they form the human bridge between the American outsiders and rural English life.
- **[[Mary Cloke]]**: His daughter, a sewing maid at Pardons who assists [[sophie-chapin|Sophie]].
- **[[George Chapin]]**: The American financier who becomes Cloke's [[earth|land]]lord and neighbour.
- **[[Sophie Chapin]]**: George's wife; Cloke's tales help her fall in love with the [[england|English countryside]].

## Significance

Cloke embodies the rooted, pre-industrial [[england|England]] that Kipling contrasts with the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s' American energy and [[money|wealth]]. He belongs to the [[earth|land]] in a way the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s cannot initially comprehend — and his storytelling, alongside his wife's, becomes one of the primary channels through which they absorb the history and [[duty|obligation]]s that come with [[earth|land]] ownership. In the story's thematic economy, Cloke represents continuity: the human link between generations of English rural life that the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s must learn to respect if they are to truly belong.
