[012-001]The Celt in all his variants from Builth to Ballyhoo,

[012-002]His mental processes are plain--one knows what he will do,

[012-003]And can logically predicate his finish by his start:

[012-004]But the English--ah, the English!--they are quite a race apart.

[012-005]Their psychology is bovine, their outlook crude and rare;

[012-006]They abandon vital matters to be tickled with a straw;

[012-007]But the straw that they were tickled with--the chaff that

[012-008]they were fed with--

[012-009]They convert into a weaver's beam to break their foeman's head

[012-010]with.

[012-011]For undemocratic reasons and for motives not of State,

[012-012]They arrive at their conclusions--largely inarticulate.

[012-013]Being void of self-expression they confide their views to none;

[012-014]But sometimes, in a smoking-room, one learns why things were

[012-015]done.

[012-016]In telegraphic sentences, half swallowed at the ends,

[012-017]They hint a matter's inwardness--and there the matter ends.

[012-018]And while the Celt is talking from Valencia to Kirkwall,

[012-019]The English--ah, the English!--don't say anything at all!
