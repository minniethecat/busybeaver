## Appears in

- [[With the Night Mail]] (007-014, 007-063, 007-079, 007-082)

## Role

Mr. L.L. Geary is the Second Despatcher aboard the General Post Office's night mail dirigible in "With the Night Mail" (ch07). As a "fully learned official" (007-014), he is responsible for processing the mail consignments that are transferred mid-flight. When the mail dirigible meets its sister ship, Geary handles the formalities of receipt and delivery alongside the two captains.

## Characteristics

Geary is portrayed as a competent and methodical civil servant. The narrator introduces him with the epithet "fully learned official" (007-014), suggesting both professional expertise and a certain bureaucratic character. He moves through the transfer procedures with practised ease — entering "through the door amiably" (007-063) and efficiently processing receipts while the captains attend to other matters (007-082). Kipling sketches him as a representative type: the reliable functionary who keeps the vast machinery of imperial communication running smoothly.

## Relationships

- **[[Captain Purnall]]**: Senior officer aboard the night mail; Geary works alongside him during the mail transfer operations.
- **[[Captain Hodgson]]**: The relief captain who shares the bridge duties with Purnall.

## Significance

As Second Despatcher, Geary represents the anonymous but essential civil servants who sustain the imperial infrastructure that Kipling celebrates in "With the Night Mail." The story's vision of a technologically advanced postal network depends on men like Geary — competent, unflappable, and wholly dedicated to the efficient movement of mail across the globe.
