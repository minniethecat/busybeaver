Jimmy appears throughout the central comic set-piece of "[[the-puzzler|The Puzzler]]" — the attempt to make [[giuseppe|Giuseppe]]'s monkey climb the [[the-puzzler|monkey-puzzler]] tree. He is the "light-weight" (011-208) of the party, agile in body and quick in tongue, whose linguistic gifts and artistic temperament drive both the experiment's success and its chaotic aftermath.

## Role

Jimmy's principal function in the story is as interpreter: he is "the only one who can talk Neapolitan" (011-154), which makes him indispensable to the communication between [[giuseppe|Giuseppe]] the Italian organ-grinder and the English party. Without Jimmy's linguistic bridge, the monkey-climbing experiment — and thus the resolution of the diplomatic puzzle — could not proceed.

> "The ram caught in the thicket, and — I'm the only one who can talk Neapolitan! Leggo my collar!" (011-153)

Beyond translation, Jimmy is an active participant in the scheme. He climbs onto the organ to impale wires, capers "like a springbuck" (011-210), and directs the operation with theatrical flair. His role is that of the artist-as-enabler: the man whose apparently frivolous talents turn out to be precisely what the situation demands.

## Characteristics

Jimmy is defined by three traits: his "high voice" (011-147), his physical lightness ("you're the light-weight; jump up on the organ" — 011-208), and what Kipling calls "the Artistic Temperament" (011-288). This last quality manifests most vividly at the story's close, when Jimmy is discovered "stricken speechless" with "inopportune mirth" at the bottom of a staircase, reduced to helpless laughter by the chaos he has helped unleash.

> Jimmy the artist sitting at the bottom of a newly-cleaned staircase. He waggled his hands at us, and when we entered we saw that the man was stricken speechless. (011-281)

He is also characterised by a certain ethical fastidiousness: "But consider the ethics of the case," he protests mid-experiment (011-253). This combination of irreverence and scruple marks Jimmy as the temperamental opposite of the political [[penfentenyou|Penfentenyou]] — where [[penfentenyou|Penfentenyou]] thunders, Jimmy squeaks and capers.

## Significance

Jimmy is more than comic relief. In a story that is fundamentally about the limits of political and legal frameworks, Jimmy represents a third mode of intelligence: the artistic, the improvisational, the linguistically agile. Where [[lord-lundie|Lord Lundie]] provides judicial wisdom and [[penfentenyou|Penfentenyou]] supplies political energy, Jimmy offers the gift of translation — literal and figurative — that makes communication between different [[earth|world]]s possible.

His final condition — speechless with laughter at the bottom of a staircase, cursed as an "unspeakable blackguard" and a "coward" by [[lord-lundie|Lord Lundie]] (011-296) — is the story's last word on the Artistic Temperament: it sees the comedy that others, too involved, cannot.
