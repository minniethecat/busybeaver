## Appears in

- [[Chapter-9|Chapter 9: A Deal in Cotton]] — visiting the Infant, reuniting with old friends (009-017)

## Role

Colonel A.L. Corkran, known by his school nickname "Stalky," is a veteran officer who has [[order|command]]ed "a native Infantry regiment on the border" (009-020). He appears in "[[deal-in-cotton|A Deal in Cotton]]" as part of a reunion of old Anglo-Indian friends at the Infant's estate. Stalky embodies the competent, understated military man — knowledgeable about Africa, scornful of others' geographical ignorance, and physically fit.

## Characteristics

Stalky is sharp, pragmatic, and physically capable — the Infant calls on him to "be an athlete" and arrange accommodations (009-032). He corrects the Infant's geographical errors about Africa with scornful precision: "You're only three thousand miles out. Look at the atlas" (009-028). His nickname suggests a cunning, resourceful nature carried from school days into military [[order|command]].

## Key actions

- Commands a native Infantry regiment on the Indian frontier (009-020)
- Reunites with the narrator and the Infant at the estate (009-017)
- Arranges logistics for the visiting [[strickland|Strickland]] party (009-032)

## Relationships

- **The Infant**: Close friend, baronet who hosts the gathering
- **[[strickland|Strickland]]**: Fellow Anglo-Indian, longtime friend (009-021)
- **The Narrator**: Old companion, "praised Allah, who had not yet terminated the Delights, nor separated the Companions" (009-018)

## Significance

Stalky represents the military arm of the Anglo-Indian experience. His presence at the reunion in "[[deal-in-cotton|A Deal in Cotton]]" reinforces the story's theme of enduring friendship forged in imperial service. The nickname, carried from boyhood into middle age, suggests a continuity of character across time and continents.
