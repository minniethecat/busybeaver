## Appears in

- [[An Habitation Enforced]] (001-099, 001-101, 001-833, 001-870, 001-912, 001-913)

## Role

Mary Cloke is the daughter of [[Mr. Cloke]] and [[Mrs. Cloke]], the farming couple at [[rocketts-farm|Rocketts]]. She works as a sewing maid at Pardons, the estate that George and [[sophie-chapin|Sophie Chapin]] eventually come to inhabit. Mary serves as a helper to [[sophie-chapin|Sophie]], providing a practical connection between the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s and the working [[earth|world]] of the [[england|English countryside]]. She is first encountered laying breakfast for the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s on their arrival morning (001-099).

## Characteristics

Mary is practical, hardworking, and unpretentious. She has a working knowledge of the local [[garden|landscape]] — it is she who directs [[sophie-chapin|Sophie]] to "the top o' the Barn field" to look across the countryside (001-101). Her friendship with the "five-foot housemaid" at Pardons, who teaches her to trim hats (001-913), suggests a young woman integrated into the servant community of the great house. [[mrs-cloke|Mrs. Cloke]] is protective of Mary, reacting with concern when [[sophie-chapin|Sophie]] mentions her doings (001-870).

## Relationships

- **[[Mrs. Cloke]]**: Her mother, who watches over Mary with protective care.
- **[[Mr. Cloke]]**: Her father, the farmer at [[rocketts-farm|Rocketts]].
- **[[Sophie Chapin]]**: Her mistress at Pardons; Mary assists [[sophie-chapin|Sophie]] as she settles into English country life.

## Significance

Mary Cloke represents the younger generation of the rural working class that serves the [[gentleman|gentry]]. Her trajectory — from a farmer's daughter to a sewing maid at the great house — mirrors the larger social patterns that the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s are entering. While a minor figure, she helps complete the portrait of the [[mr-cloke|Cloke]] family as a microcosm of the rooted English rural community that Kipling contrasts with the [[george-chapin|Chapin]]s' American rootlessness.
