[019-079] He was at peace within himself. Now at last he knew his wife, now at last he understood her, and it was all right. Error, not evil. Something inimical and proud in her had been destroyed. He was tired, emotionally drained, but he could let his smoke curl up to the stars and feel the cold air penetrate his blanket, calmly, while he thought and knew his own mind. He had a feeling, without any specific reason, that he should keep a vigil over [[slim-girl|Slim Girl]], but he became so sleepy. He went in by the fire, pulled sheepskins about himself, and slept.

[019-080] In the morning he brought her food and tended her wound. After they had eaten and smoked, he spoke.

[019-081] “You have lived in a terrible world that I do not know. I cannot judge you by my world. I think I understand. You have deceived me, but you have not been untrue to me, I think. Life without you would be a kind of death. Now I know that I do not have to do what I thought I had to, and I am glad for it. Now I know you, and there is no more of this secret thing that has been a river between us.

[019-082] “As soon as you are able, we shall go North. If there is a place where you have relatives, we can go there. If not, we can go to T’o Tlakai, or some place where your clan is strong, or wherever you wish. We shall get the sheep that my mother is keeping for me, and we shall buy others, and we shall live among The People. That is the only way, I think.

[019-083] “Understand, if we go on together, it is in my world, The People’s world, and not this world of Americans who have lost their way.”

[019-084] They kissed.

[019-085] “I shall be happy with you anywhere that you wish to take me. As you have said, there is nothing between us now. You have made up to me, and revenged me, for everything the Americans have done to me, My Slayer of Enemy Gods.”

[019-086] “You must not call me that; it is wicked to call a human being by such a name.”

[019-087] She answered him with another kiss. He thought he had never seen her look so happy. For the first time since he had known her, she looked as young as she was, a year or so younger than himself. Her face was full of peace.

[019-088] They fell to planning. Reckoning their resources, they concluded that they had amassed the astounding sum of three thousand dollars in money, goods, and horses. He did not want to take what came through her lover, but she said:

[019-089] “No; I took it like spoils in war. It was war I made with him. And you made it yours when your arrow struck him. And we both paid for it, I think.”

[019-090] “Perhaps when he gets well he will send policemen after us.”

[019-091] “No, I know him. He will say nothing; he will be ashamed, I think.”
