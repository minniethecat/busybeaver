# Yellow Singer

A respected Navajo singer — a ceremonial practitioner who possesses the knowledge of songs, prayers, and rituals that Laughing Boy aspires to learn. Yellow Singer represents the spiritual tradition at its fullest and most authoritative.

## Identity

Yellow Singer is one of the ceremonial authorities who preside over the Tsé Lani gathering. As a singer, he holds the specialized knowledge of major ceremonies — the songs, sand-paintings, prayers, and ritual actions that constitute Navajo religious practice. Laughing Boy's aspiration "to be a singer as well, to express all these things through the prayers... to learn to conduct the Mountain Chant" (001-021) looks toward figures like Yellow Singer as its goal.

## Role in the Narrative

Yellow Singer appears primarily in the ceremonial context of the Tsé Lani dance. His authority is demonstrated through his control of ritual participation: the community enforces his decisions about who may and may not participate in ceremony. His role is structural rather than personal — he represents the institution of Navajo ceremonial practice.

## Cultural Context

The singer in Navajo culture combines the roles of priest, healer, and keeper of traditional knowledge. Years of apprenticeship are required to learn the complex song cycles of ceremonies like the Mountain Chant and the Night Chant. Yellow Singer represents the apex of this tradition — the fully realized singer whose authority is unquestioned.
