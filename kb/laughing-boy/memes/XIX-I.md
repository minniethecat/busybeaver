[019-001] There were her tracks, wind-blurred in the sand. She must have come straight home, arriving before he reached the high place. With dread he entered the door, grateful for the half-darkness inside. She had got back into Navajo clothes, moccasins, skirt, and sash, but her blouse was only pulled over the right shoulder, leaving the left arm and breast bare. Did she think—? He saw her as an enemy.

[019-002] “I am going away.”

[019-003] “All right. But first pull this out; I am not strong enough.” She held out her arm with the arrow through it.

[019-004] He stared at it, and it made him feel sick. He was frankly avoiding her face, but he knew that the blood was gone from beneath the bronze surface, leaving it yellow-white with a green tinge under it. He kept on looking at the arrow, his arrow, with his marks on it.

[019-005] “You must come out to the light.”

[019-006] She rose with difficulty, steadying herself against the wall. He supported her to the door.

[019-007] The arrow had passed through the flesh of the under side of her arm, just missing the artery and the bone. The shaft stood out on both sides. From the barbed, iron head to the wound there was blood in the zigzag lightning grooves. The roundness of her arm was caked with dried blood and already somewhat swollen. To the one side was the barbed point, to the other were the eagle feathers and the wrappings. He took out his knife.

[019-008] “I shall try not to make it wiggle,” he said.

[019-009] “What are you going to do?”

[019-010] “Cut it off just by the hole; I can’t pull all that through your arm.”

[019-011] “It is a good arrow. Pull it through.”

[019-012] There was never another woman like this one. “Do you think I would use this again?”

[019-013] He held her arm very carefully, he cut with all possible gentleness, but the shaft moved and moved again. He heard her take in her breath and looked quickly to see her teeth clenched on her lower lip. She should have been a man. Every dart of pain in her arm went doubly through his heart. The wood was cut short, just above the wound.

[019-014] “Now,” he said, “are you ready?”

[019-015] “Pull.”

[019-016] He jerked it out. She had not moved. She was rigid and her eyes were almost glassy, but she had not made a sign. He still knelt, staring at her, at the fresh blood welling, and at the red stump of the arrow in his hand. She was brave, brave.

[019-017] She whispered, “Get me some of the whiskey.”

[019-018] He gave her a stiff dose in a cup. She emptied it at once, and sighed. A little colour came back.

[019-019] “It will be dark soon. You had better go now. I can take care of myself. But before you go, know this: whatever you have seen, I love you and you only and altogether. Goodbye.”

[019-020] She handed him back the cup. As he took it, their fingers touched, and he looked into her eyes. Something snapped inside of him. He fell forward, his head close to his knees, and began sobbing. She laid her hand on his shoulder.

[019-021] “You have been hasty, I think. One should not turn up a new trail without looking around. And you have not eaten, you are tired. This has been hard for you. In a minute I shall heat some coffee, and we can talk straight about this.”
