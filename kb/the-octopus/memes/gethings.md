## Role in the Novel

Gethings of the San Pablo rancho is one of the established wheat ranchers of the San Joaquin Valley who joins [[magnus-derrick|Magnus Derrick]]'s League to resist [[the-railroad|the Railroad]]'s oppressive freight rates. He is consistently present at the novel's key political gatherings — [[annixter|Annixter]]'s barn dance, the League meetings at [[los-muertos|Los Muertos]], the confrontation at the irrigating ditch — functioning as part of the collective body of ranchers rather than as a sharply individuated character. His defining trait is his practical, businesslike focus on wheat economics; even during the jack[[jackrabbit-hunt|rabbit drive]], a festive community event, Gethings keeps up "a prolonged and serious conversation on an expected rise in the price of wheat" [0001-5260].

## Character Analysis

Norris presents Gethings as part of a type: the "elderly men, for the most part, bearded, slow of speech, deliberate, dressed in broadcloth" who constitute the established rancher class [0001-2265]. He is pragmatic and business-minded, rising at a League meeting to propose that "routine business could be dispensed with and the schedule read at once" rather than waste time on parliamentary procedure [0001-4478]. When [[lyman-derrick|Lyman Derrick]] presents [[the-railroad|the Railroad]]'s new grain tariff with reduced rates for certain districts, Gethings is quick to note the practical irrelevance: "Where not a carload of wheat is shipped in a year" [0001-4502]. His sharpness in this exchange reveals an experienced rancher who can immediately detect [[the-railroad|the Railroad]]'s bad-faith maneuvering. Along with [[osterman|Osterman]], Gethings is among those who demand accountability from [[lyman-derrick|Lyman]]: "Yes, what did we elect you for?" [0001-4520].

## Relationships

Gethings moves almost exclusively within the circle of wheat ranchers. He is frequently paired with [[garnett]] of the Ruby Rancho — the two are described as "both on horseback" at the jack[[jackrabbit-hunt|rabbit drive]], "preoccupied, bearded men, smoking black cigars" [0001-4452]. He is allied with [[magnus-derrick]] and the League leadership, and participates alongside [[annixter]], [[osterman]], [[broderson]], and [[dabney]] in the Executive Committee meetings. During the climactic confrontation with the marshal's posse at the irrigating ditch, Gethings is one of the three men — along with [[magnus-derrick|Magnus Derrick]] and [[garnett|Garnett]] — who step forward unarmed to negotiate, marking him as one of the senior, respected ranchers chosen for this delicate role [0001-5616]. His relationship to his rancho, San Pablo, defines his identity; he is almost never named without this territorial designation.

## Key Scenes

**[[barn-dance|Annixter's Barn Dance]]**: Gethings arrives with the other ranchers at [[annixter|Annixter]]'s celebration at [[quien-sabe|Quien Sabe]]. He discusses the possibility of a worldwide wheat shortage with [[magnus-derrick|Magnus Derrick]] and later, in the harness room, participates in the drinking, storytelling, and increasing boisterousness. At supper, he is among those who "occupied themselves with eating as much as they could before the supper gave out" [0001-2545]. When [[the-railroad|the Railroad]]'s rate increase letters arrive, Gethings receives one of the sealed envelopes and reads it with the others, his silence marking the abrupt end of the festivities [0001-2628].

**League Executive Committee Meeting**: At the [[los-muertos|Los Muertos]] ranch house, Gethings sits on the Executive Committee. He is the one who moves to dispense with routine business and proceed directly to the grain tariff schedule, showing his preference for practical action over ceremony [0001-4478]. His skepticism toward [[lyman-derrick|Lyman Derrick]]'s rosy projections about the new rate schedule's benefits marks him as one of the shrewder Committee members.

**The Irrigating Ditch Showdown**: When [[magnus-derrick|Magnus Derrick]] decides to confront the marshal's posse unarmed, he selects Gethings and [[garnett|Garnett]] to accompany him — the three oldest ranchers present, apart from [[broderson|Broderson]]. As the parley proceeds, the posse gradually surrounds the three ranchers, and "by now, everybody was talking at once," the situation spiraling beyond the control of the dignified elder statesmen [0001-5636].

**The Jackrabbit Drive**: Gethings and [[garnett|Garnett]] ride side by side during the community [[jackrabbit-hunt|rabbit drive]], "ignoring the drive and the spirit of the occasion" to discuss wheat prices, a detail that economically captures his single-minded focus on the business of farming [0001-5260].

## Thematic Significance

Gethings represents the established, respectable face of agrarian resistance to [[the-railroad|the Railroad]]. Unlike the more colorful figures — [[annixter|Annixter]] with his combative energy, [[osterman|Osterman]] with his manic scheming, [[dyke|Dyke]] with his tragic arc — Gethings is sober, deliberate, and institutional. His presence on the Executive Committee and his selection as one of the unarmed negotiators suggest that Norris intends him to embody the legitimacy of the ranchers' cause: these are not hot-headed rebels but solid, practical men driven to desperate measures by systematic economic oppression. His fate after [[mussel-slough-tragedy|the shootout]] is left unresolved, like that of many of the minor ranchers, subsumed into the larger catastrophe that Norris depicts as the inevitable collision between the wheat and the railroad.
