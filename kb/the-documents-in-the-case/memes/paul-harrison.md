# Paul Harrison

## Overview

Paul Harrison is the son of [[george-harrison|George Harrison]] by an earlier marriage, a civil engineer by profession [PN=047-057]. He is the compiler and editor of the documentary evidence that forms [[the-documents-in-the-case-novel|the novel]] — his investigative notes frame the epistolary collection. After his father's suspicious death, Paul becomes the driving force behind [[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] investigation.

## Role in the Case

Paul Harrison is absent from the household during the critical period — posted overseas (in Africa) — but his [[george-harrison-letters|father's letters]] keep him informed of domestic developments. Upon learning of his father's death, he returns to England and launches his own investigation.

His "[[paul-harrison-statement|Statement by Paul Harrison]]" [PN=049-001] and its continuation [PN=051-001] form the prosecution's case. He compiles letters, telegrams, statements, and newspaper extracts into the dossier that becomes [[the-documents-in-the-case-novel|the novel]]. His investigative notes provide crucial editorial context: "[[paul-harrison-note|Note by Paul Harrison]]" sections appear throughout [PN=025-001] [PN=029-001] [PN=053-001].

Paul's theory is that [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]] and [[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]] conspired to murder his father. He presents evidence that [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] was "[[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]]'s lover" [PN=049-252] and that [[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] was committed "at [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]]'s instigation" [PN=049-257].

## Character

Paul Harrison is methodical and determined — qualities befitting both an engineer and an investigator. His letters to his father are lost (George references them), but his investigative voice is clear: analytical, persistent, and personally motivated. He is not a dispassionate observer — he is seeking justice for his father's murder.

His relationship with [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]], his stepmother, is fraught. He feels she manipulated his father and conspired with [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]. Yet his investigation is thorough enough to withstand legal scrutiny.
