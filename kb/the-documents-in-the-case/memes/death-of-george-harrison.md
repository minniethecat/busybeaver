# Death of George Harrison

## Summary

[[george-harrison|George Harrison]], aged 56, Head of the Accountants' Department, died at [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] near [[manaton|Manaton]], [[manaton|Devon]], in late March 1929 [PN=053-013]. Initially attributed to natural causes, his death was subsequently determined to be murder by [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] poisoning — extracted from [[amanita-muscaria|Amanita muscaria]] mushrooms and administered by [[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]].

## Context

The death occurred during a weekend excursion to [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]], [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s artist's studio in [[manaton|Devon]]. [[george-harrison|Harrison]] had traveled there with [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] and possibly others from the Bayswater household. He collapsed after eating a mushroom dish prepared by [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] — a dish [[harwood-lathom|the artist]] had claimed as his specialty [PN=002-012].

The death initially appeared natural: "The appearances suggested heart failure" [PN=047-050]. But the peculiar circumstances — a healthy man dying suddenly after eating mushrooms prepared by someone with mycological expertise — aroused suspicion that led to the adjournment of the inquest for chemical analysis.
