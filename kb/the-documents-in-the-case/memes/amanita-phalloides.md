# Amanita Phalloides

_Amanita phalloides_, commonly known as the death cap, is a highly poisonous basidiomycete fungus and one of the most deadly mushrooms known. It is mentioned in connection with the broader mycological and toxicological framework of the case, alongside [[amanita-muscaria|Amanita muscaria]].

## Distinction from Amanita Muscaria

While [[amanita-muscaria|Amanita muscaria]] (fly agaric) contains [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] and ibotenic acid, _Amanita phalloides_ contains amatoxins — a different class of compounds that inhibit RNA polymerase II and cause irreversible liver and kidney damage. The distinction between these two _Amanita_ species is central to the forensic investigation: symptoms, treatment, and prognosis differ dramatically between muscarine poisoning and amatoxin poisoning.

## Forensic Significance

The presence or absence of _Amanita phalloides_ in the toxicological analysis helps establish whether a poisoning was accidental (misidentification of edible mushrooms) or deliberate. The [[chemical-analysis-report|chemical analysis report]] in the case documents makes reference to the differential diagnosis between these species.
