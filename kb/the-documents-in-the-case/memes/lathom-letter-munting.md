# Lathom's Letter to Munting

## Summary

[[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]]'s letter to [[john-munting|John Munting]] [PN=026-001] is the only direct document from the prime suspect in the dossier. Unlike the voluminous correspondence of [[agatha-milsom|Agatha Milsom]] or the letters of the [[george-harrison|Harrison]] family, [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s documentary footprint is minimal — making this single letter disproportionately significant.

## Key Content

The chapter heading records the letter's existence: "Harwood Lathom to John Munting" [PN=026-001]. Its content, as described through [[john-munting|Munting]]'s subsequent letters to [[elizabeth-drake|Elizabeth Drake]], reveals [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s charm and manipulative skill — qualities that would later be interpreted as evidence of his capacity for calculated violence.

## Analysis

The scarcity of [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s own words in the dossier is itself a narrative choice. While other characters pour their thoughts into letters, [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] is documented primarily through what others say about him. This single letter is the exception — a moment when the suspect speaks for himself.

The imbalance — dozens of letters from Agatha, multiple from [[john-munting|Munting]], but only one from [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] — reinforces his role as the object of investigation rather than its subject. He is the figure everyone else writes about.
