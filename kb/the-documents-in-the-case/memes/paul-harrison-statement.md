# Paul Harrison's Statement

## Summary

Paul Harrison's Statement (Chapters 49 and 51) is the central prosecutorial document in *[[the-documents-in-the-case-novel|The Documents in the Case]]*. Compiled after his [[paul-harrison-returns-from-africa|return from Africa]], it systematically presents the evidence implicating [[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]] in [[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] of [[george-harrison|George Harrison]], with [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]] as co-conspirator.

## Key Content

[[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]] presents a detailed chronology of events at [[whittington-terrace|15 Whittington Terrace]], drawing on letters, telegrams, and personal observations. His key arguments include:

- [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s affair with [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]] during February 1929, the month before [[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] [PN=049-006]
- [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s knowledge of mushrooms and poisons, demonstrated in conversations with [[john-munting|Munting]] [PN=051-014]
- The synthetic origin of the [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]], proving deliberate preparation [PN=049-303]
- [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]]'s motive: "Plenty of motive for murder there" [PN=049-277]
- Evidence that [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] was "very much under the influence of [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]]" [PN=049-283]

## Analysis

[[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]]'s statement is both evidence and narrative frame. As the compiler of the dossier, [[paul-harrison|Paul]] selects and arranges documents to build a case. His perspective is not neutral — he is [[george-harrison|the victim]]'s son, seeking justice. Yet his engineering-trained mind produces a methodical, evidence-based argument.

The statement is notably explicit in its accusation: "deliberately murdered by [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] at [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]]'s instigation" [PN=049-257]. This clarity of accusation, supported by documentary evidence, is what distinguishes the case from mere suspicion.
