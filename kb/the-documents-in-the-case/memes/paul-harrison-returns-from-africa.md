# Paul Harrison Returns from Africa

## Summary

[[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]] was in Africa — working as a civil engineer — when his father was murdered [PN=049-002]. His return to England transforms the investigation: where others had suspicions, [[paul-harrison|Paul]] has determination. He becomes the driving force behind the documentary dossier and the prosecution case.

## Context

"I was in Africa when the news of my father's death reached me" [PN=049-002]. [[paul-harrison|Paul]]'s physical absence during the critical period means he approaches the case as an investigator rather than a witness. He is not compromised by personal involvement in the household dynamics — though his relationship with [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]], his stepmother, gives him a personal stake.

Upon returning, [[paul-harrison|Paul]] systematically gathers evidence:
- Interviews all witnesses
- Collects letters, telegrams, and newspaper extracts
- Compiles the documentary dossier
- Writes his own comprehensive statement

His engineering-trained mind produces a methodical, evidence-based case. The dossier is his creation — selected, arranged, and annotated to build an irrefutable argument.
