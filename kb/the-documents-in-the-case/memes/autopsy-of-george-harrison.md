# Autopsy of George Harrison

The autopsy of [[george-harrison|George Harrison]] was performed by [[dr-hughes|Dr. Hughes]] of [[bovey-tracey|Bovey Tracey]], acting in collaboration with the police authorities. The post-mortem examination provided the medical evidence that established the cause of death as muscarine poisoning [PN=047-049].

## The Examination

After George Harrison's body was removed to Bovey Tracey for the purpose of the autopsy, Dr. Hughes conducted the examination [PN=047-049]. His findings were subsequently presented at [[the-inquest|the inquest]] before [[dr-pringle|Dr. Pringle]], the coroner.

Dr. Hughes testified that he had found the heart to be in a condition consistent with muscarine poisoning [PN=048-023]. The medical evidence he provided was central to the coroner's determination of the cause of death.

## Police Involvement

[[sergeant-warbeck|Sergeant Warbeck]] of the Bovey Tracey police received the message that initiated the official investigation and communicated with Dr. Hughes [PN=047-044]. The collaboration between the police surgeon and the investigating officers established the chain of medical evidence that underpinned the legal proceedings.

## Significance

The autopsy findings established that George Harrison died of muscarine poisoning, but they did not — and could not — distinguish between natural and [[synthetic-muscarine|synthetic]] muscarine. This limitation of routine post-mortem toxicology became the crucial gap that [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]] later exploited in his investigation.

The autopsy thus represents both the foundation of the official case (establishing cause of death) and its fundamental incompleteness (failing to determine the origin of the poison).
