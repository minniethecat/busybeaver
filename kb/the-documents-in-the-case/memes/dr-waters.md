# Dr. Waters

Dr. Waters was a chemist introduced at the dinner party hosted by [[professor-hoskyns|Professor Hoskyns]] in the final chapter of the narrative, where he explained the critical distinction between synthetic and natural muscarine [PN=052-010].

## At the Dinner Party

Waters was described as younger than the rest of the company and was introduced as "the coming man in chemistry" [PN=052-010]. He joined a distinguished gathering that included [[professor-hoskyns|Professor Hoskyns]], [[professor-matthews|Professor Matthews]], [[professor-alcock|Professor Alcock]], and [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]].

During the dinner conversation, Waters observed that modern scientists preferred up-to-date chemical formulae [PN=052-020]. He noted the human tendency to find patterns and assign blame, remarking that "unfortunately, the sinner is only too anxious to cooperate" [PN=052-027].

## The Synthetic Muscarine Explanation

When the conversation turned to synthetic poisons, Waters delivered a detailed exposition on how synthetic compounds are produced in the laboratory [PN=052-059]. He explained the principles of stereochemistry and how they relate to the vital processes of living organisms [PN=052-061].

The critical moment came when Waters examined the substance that had been presented to the company. He identified it as "a synthetic preparation in racemic form" [PN=052-183] — a determination that proved decisive in understanding how [[george-harrison|George Harrison]] had been poisoned.

## Role in the Resolution

Waters' chemical expertise provided [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]] with the crucial insight needed to understand the mechanism of his father's death. The distinction between natural muscarine (from [[amanita-muscaria|Amanita muscaria]]) and synthetic muscarine (produced in a laboratory) became the key to unraveling the crime [PN=052-067].
