# Manaton

Manaton is a remote village in Devon, England, situated on the edge of Dartmoor. It was the nearest settlement to [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] and served as the local communication and administrative hub throughout the events of the case [PN=047-005].

The village was small enough that the arrival of outsiders was noted. Its post-office, constable, and meeting-room became key nodes in the investigation, while the journey between Manaton and London — a thread running through the entire documentary record — made the village the anchor point for reconstructing movements and alibis.

## The Remote Village

[[john-munting|Munting]] described the scene vividly: "the small room in the remote village of Manaton in Devon was crowded" during the inquest proceedings [PN=047-005]. The village's isolation was part of its character — "Manaton was well behind us now, and the road began to climb" describes the approach from the railway [PN=045-045].

> "The small room in the remote village of Manaton in Devon was crowded today, when the adjourned inquest on the body of George Harrison was resumed." [PN=047-005]

Visitors approaching [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] would pass through Manaton. [[george-harrison|Harrison]]'s directions to the cottage were simply "[[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]], Near Manaton, Devon" — the village being the only landmark needed [PN=015-002].

## Communication Hub

The post-office at Manaton was a critical link. Letters and telegrams "despatched from the post-office at Manaton by anybody who happened to be staying at [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]]" connected the isolated cottage to London [PN=047-013].

Telephone communication existed "between [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] and Manaton," and when [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] needed to summon help after discovering [[george-harrison|Harrison]]'s body, this line was his only means [PN=047-044]. A call "was put through to him from Manaton at about nine o'clock" on a key date in the investigation [PN=049-372].

The village constable was another point of contact with authority. When [[george-harrison|Harrison]]'s body was discovered, [[john-munting|Munting]] "summoned the police from [[bovey-tracey|Bovey Tracey]], the village constable being, it appeared, away from home at the time" — a detail that underscores both Manaton's small size and its role as first responder [PN=049-361].

## The Resumed Inquest

The inquest into [[death-of-george-harrison|Harrison's death]] was resumed at Manaton, bringing investigators, witnesses, and journalists to the remote village [PN=048-005]. This proceeding produced much of the documentary evidence that forms the record of the case.

The public interest was such that newspapers carried headlines reading "Manaton Murderer Hanged" when the case reached its conclusion [PN=053-012]. The village's name became inseparable from the crime in the public imagination.
