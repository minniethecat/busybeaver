# The Dossier: Complete Documentary Record

## Summary

The dossier — "[[the-documents-in-the-case-novel|The Documents in the Case]]" — is the meta-evidence: the complete collection of documents compiled by [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]] in the aftermath of his father's murder. It is both the evidence and [[the-documents-in-the-case-novel|the novel]] itself. Understanding the dossier as a curated artifact is essential to understanding the case.

## Key Content

The dossier comprises:

- **Letters**: [[agatha-milsom|Agatha Milsom]] to [[olive-farebrother|Olive Farebrother]] (primary narrative thread); [[john-munting|John Munting]] to [[elizabeth-drake|Elizabeth Drake]] (secondary thread); [[george-harrison|George Harrison]] to [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]] (victim's voice); [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]] to [[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]] (affair evidence)
- **Statements**: [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]]'s prosecution case (Chapters 49, 51); [[john-munting|John Munting]]'s testimony (Chapters 37, 45, 52)
- **Telegrams**: [[john-munting|Munting]] to Drake (February 26, 1929 — timeline marker)
- **Newspaper extracts**: [[morning-express|Morning Express]] coverage of inquest, forensic testimony, and conviction
- **Editorial notes**: [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]]'s contextual annotations

## Analysis

The dossier is not a neutral collection. [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]] selected, arranged, and annotated every document. His editorial hand shapes the reader's understanding — what is included, what is excluded, what is emphasized. The reader must constantly ask: what is not in the dossier?

The novel's title — *[[the-documents-in-the-case-novel|The Documents in the Case]]* — is itself the dossier's label. The book presents itself as evidence, not fiction. This conceit is central to the epistolary detective genre: the reader becomes the juror, weighing documents and reaching conclusions.
