# Agatha Milsom

## Overview

Agatha Milsom is the primary narrator of *[[the-documents-in-the-case-novel|The Documents in the Case]]*. A middle-aged spinster living at [[whittington-terrace|15 Whittington Terrace, Bayswater]], she writes long, analytical letters to her confidante [[olive-farebrother|Olive Farebrother]]. Her letters form the backbone of [[the-documents-in-the-case-novel|the novel]]'s epistolary structure, providing psychological insight into every character in the [[george-harrison|Harrison]] household.

## Role in the Case

Miss Milsom is a resident observer at [[whittington-terrace|15 Whittington Terrace]], the [[george-harrison|Harrison]] household where [[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] takes place. Her letters to [[olive-farebrother|Olive Farebrother]] document the events leading up to [[george-harrison|George Harrison]]'s death and the subsequent investigation. She is present during key moments — dinner conversations, the discovery of the body, and the investigation.

Her psychological acuity, informed by her consultations with [[dr-trevor|Dr. Trevor]] (a psychoanalyst), shapes her interpretation of events. She frequently applies Freudian concepts — sublimation, repression, psychological types — to explain the behavior of those around her [PN=001-005] [PN=001-006] [PN=001-007].

## Character

Miss Milsom describes herself with self-deprecating humor. She has "sharp eyes" [PN=001-005] for human behavior and an ironic sensibility. [[dr-trevor|Dr. Trevor]] advises her to "learn to detach" herself [PN=001-007] and prescribes a rest-cure [PN=001-005]. She is acutely aware of social class and propriety, yet also deeply curious about the psychological undercurrents beneath suburban respectability.

Her relationship with [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] is complex — she is initially charmed by him ("he often drops in of an evening" [PN=010-008]) but grows increasingly suspicious. She is protective of [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]] while also perceiving the impropriety of [[margaret-harrison|Margaret]]'s relationship with [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]].
