# Munting Discovers the Poison Evidence

## Summary

[[john-munting|John Munting]]'s realization that [[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]]'s mushroom expertise and the earlier conversation about synthetic poisons [PN=051-014] pointed to murder rather than accident was a crucial turning point in the investigation. As a chemist, [[john-munting|Munting]] was uniquely qualified to connect [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s demonstrated knowledge with the forensic findings.

## Context

[[john-munting|Munting]] had been [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s friend and flatmate — he shared the maisonnette at 15a [[whittington-terrace|Whittington Terrace]]. He had participated in the conversation about synthetic drugs where [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] had displayed "knowledge of poisons" [PN=031-010]. He had watched [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] study mushrooms "with scientific value" [PN=027-006].

After [[death-of-george-harrison|Harrison's death]], these memories coalesced into a pattern. [[john-munting|Munting]]'s subsequent statement [PN=052-001] documents his reconstruction of events: the conversation about synthetic poisons, [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s mycological expertise, the mushroom dinner, and the tragic [[the-shack-weekend|weekend at The Shack]].
