# The Forensic Investigation

## Summary

The forensic investigation conducted during the inquest's adjournment period — the fortnight from late October to early November 1929 [PN=047-008] — is the scientific pivot of the case. [[james-lubbock|Sir James Lubbock]]'s analysis of [[george-harrison|George Harrison]]'s organs and the contents of [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] transformed a suspicious death into a confirmed homicide.

## Context

The investigation proceeded along two parallel tracks:
- **Official**: [[james-lubbock|Sir James Lubbock]] at the Home Office laboratory, analyzing stomach contents, organs, and food items
- **Unofficial**: [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]] compiling documentary evidence, interviewing witnesses, and building the prosecution case

Lubbock's key findings were presented at the resumed inquest in early November and reported in the [[morning-express|Morning Express]] under the dramatic headline "[[james-lubbock|Sir James Lubbock]] on [[morning-express-lubbock|Shack Poison Drama]]" [PN=048-002]. His testimony established:
- Muscarine present in "considerable quantity" [PN=048-009]
- The source was [[amanita-muscaria|Amanita muscaria]] — "the [[amanita-muscaria|Fly Agaric]]" [PN=048-019]
- All food items at [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] were analyzed [PN=048-021]
- The poison was of synthetic origin [PN=049-303]
