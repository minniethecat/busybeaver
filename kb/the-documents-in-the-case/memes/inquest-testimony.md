# Coroner's Inquest Testimony

## Summary

The [[the-inquest|coroner's inquest]], opened by Dr. Pringle at the district court [PN=047-005], is the first official proceeding into [[george-harrison|George Harrison]]'s death. The testimony recorded here — identification evidence, the widow's appearance, the adjournment for analysis — establishes the legal framework within which all subsequent evidence is gathered.

## Key Content

The inquest proceedings include:

- Formal evidence of identification of the deceased [PN=047-009]
- Testimony from the widow, [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]]: "fashionably dressed" [PN=047-056]
- [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]]'s professional status noted: "a civil engineer, now absent" [PN=047-057]
- The coroner's decision to adjourn for a fortnight "to enable a chemical analysis to be made of certain organs" [PN=047-008]
- Medical evidence of the circumstances of death

## Analysis

The inquest is the hinge between private suspicion and public investigation. Before the inquest, [[death-of-george-harrison|Harrison's death]] was a family tragedy. After the adjournment and the chemical analysis, it becomes a criminal case.

The detail of [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]] appearing "fashionably dressed" [PN=047-056] is a loaded observation — the widow's demeanor scrutinized for signs of grief or its absence. It exemplifies how the inquest transforms private behavior into public evidence.
