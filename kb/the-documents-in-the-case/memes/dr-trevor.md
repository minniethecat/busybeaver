# Dr. Trevor

## Overview

Dr. Trevor is [[agatha-milsom|Agatha Milsom]]'s psychoanalyst — a character who never appears directly in the narrative but whose influence permeates it. Through Agatha's letters to [[olive-farebrother|Olive Farebrother]], Dr. Trevor's diagnoses, prescriptions, and psychological frameworks become the lens through which Agatha interprets the people around her. He is the unseen authority whose Freudian concepts give [[the-documents-in-the-case-novel|the novel]] much of its psychological vocabulary.

## Role in the Case

Dr. Trevor has no direct involvement in [[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] or its investigation. His significance is as an intellectual and psychological influence on the primary narrator. Agatha quotes or paraphrases him constantly, applying his concepts to the [[george-harrison|Harrison]] household:

- "Dr. Trevor says that rest-cures only..." [PN=001-005]
- "Dr. Trevor calls the direct way" — a reference to confronting problems directly versus indirectly [PN=001-006]
- "Dr. Trevor says it is very important to learn to detach myself" [PN=001-007]
- "Dr. Trevor thinks it is very promising" — approving Agatha's writing [PN=004-008]
- "Dr. Trevor says that that kind of adolescent..." [PN=018-007]

Agatha visits him for regular consultations, sharing her observations of the [[george-harrison|Harrison]]s: "I have been to Dr. Trevor and put the whole situation very clearly before him" [PN=010-005].

## Character

Dr. Trevor embodies the early 20th-century psychoanalytic establishment. His prescriptions — rest-cures, detachment, tonics — are standard practice of the era. His vocabulary ("sublimation," "psychological makeup" [PN=002-005], "adolescent" phases) is recognizably Freudian.

Agatha's relationship with him is complex. She is both patient and collaborator — bringing him case material from the [[george-harrison|Harrison]] household and receiving psychological tools in return. She insists she does not like him "simply because he is a man" [PN=002-005], suggesting an awareness of transference dynamics.
