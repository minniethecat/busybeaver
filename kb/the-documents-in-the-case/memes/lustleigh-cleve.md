# Lustleigh Cleave

## Overview

Lustleigh Cleave is a geographical feature in Devon, England — a deep valley with granite tors — located near [[manaton|Manaton]] and visible from [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]]. It is mentioned in [[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]]'s letters describing his stay at The Shack with [[george-harrison|George Harrison]].

## Description

Lathom describes it in a letter to [[john-munting|John Munting]]: "This is a pretty place — miles away from everywhere, of course, stuck down on a circumbendible lane which runs down from Manaton (half a dozen houses and a pub) to the deep valley which separates the Manaton Ridge and Becky Falls from Lustleigh Cleave" [PN=032-004].

Later, [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]] describes the view from The Shack: "I wandered to the edge of the slope, where the valley plunged down, gorse and heath and bracken all jumbled among the grey boulders, and looked across to where the huge tors humped their granite shoulders over the heights of Lustleigh Cleave. They looked grim enough" [PN=045-128].

## Significance

Lustleigh Cleave serves as part of the isolated, wild setting of the final act. The remote Dartmoor landscape — with its deep valleys, granite tors, and lonely cottages — provides the physical backdrop against which the tragedy unfolds. Paul Harrison's description of the tors as "grim" reflects his state of mind as he investigates his father's death.
