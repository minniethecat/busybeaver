# The Shack, Manaton

The Shack is a small cottage near [[manaton|Manaton]], [[manaton|Devon]], where [[george-harrison|George Harrison]] died on a Saturday night in March 1929 [PN=047-005]. It is the primary crime scene of *[[the-documents-in-the-case-novel|The Documents in the Case]]* — the location where [[george-harrison|Harrison]] consumed the fungus dish containing synthetic [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] and where his body was discovered the following morning. The isolation of The Shack, its limited facilities, and the circumstances of the weekend gathering there are central to the forensic reconstruction of [[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]].

## Location and Description

The Shack is described as "a little cottage" near [[manaton|Manaton]], [[manaton|Devon]] [PN=047-005]. Its precise address is not given in formal terms; the letters in the documentary record refer to it simply as "The Shack, Manaton, [[manaton|Devon]]" [PN=045-002] or "The Shack, Near [[manaton|Manaton]], [[manaton|Devon]]" [PN=015-002].

The cottage was modest in its facilities. [[john-munting|Munting]]'s account notes that "a tin of milk, presumably condensed milk, was kept in The Shack" and that "there was no delivery of newspapers" — details establishing the rustic, self-catering nature of the accommodation [PN=047-013]. Telephone communication existed "between The Shack and [[manaton|Manaton]]" [PN=047-044], providing a link to the outside world that would prove significant when [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] needed to summon help.

## The Fatal Weekend

The weekend gathering at The Shack brought together the key figures in the tragedy:

- **[[george-harrison|George Harrison]]** — [[george-harrison|the victim]], who died at The Shack on Saturday night [PN=047-005]
- **[[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]]** — who had been staying with [[george-harrison|Harrison]] and who discovered the body [PN=047-006]
- **[[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]]** — present at The Shack during the weekend
- **[[john-munting|John Munting]]** — who arrived at The Shack at about eleven o'clock on the night of [[death-of-george-harrison|Harrison's death]] [PN=047-042]

The fatal meal — a dish of cooked fungus prepared from mushrooms gathered in the surrounding woodland — was consumed at The Shack. A portion of the dish remained "on the table in The Shack at the time of his death" [PN=047-007], and it was this remnant that [[james-lubbock|Sir James Lubbock]] would later analyze, finding "a considerable quantity" of [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] [PN=048-009].

## Forensic Significance

The Shack's isolation was forensically significant. The absence of regular deliveries, the limited cooking facilities, and the fact that [[george-harrison|Harrison]] was living "a bachelor existence" during his stay [PN=047-012] narrowed the field of possible poison vectors. The [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] could only have entered [[george-harrison|Harrison]]'s system through food or drink prepared at The Shack — and the only meal he consumed that evening was the fungus dish.

[[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s movements on the evening of the death were scrutinized. He "returned to The Shack late on Saturday night, bringing with him some mushrooms he had gathered" [PN=047-041]. [[john-munting|Munting]] arrived later, at about eleven o'clock, by which time [[george-harrison|Harrison]] was already dead or dying [PN=047-042]. The timeline of arrivals and departures, centered on The Shack, became a critical element of the prosecution's circumstantial case.
