# The Shack Weekend

## Summary

The weekend at The Shack — [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s artist's studio near [[manaton|Manaton]], [[manaton|Devon]] — is the scene of [[george-harrison|George Harrison]]'s death. What was intended as a country excursion became a murder investigation. [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]], described as a rustic studio with a good view [PN=045-041], was where [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] prepared his "special mushroom dish" [PN=002-012] that contained the fatal dose of [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]].

## Context

The group traveled from London to [[manaton|Devon]], a journey that passed through [[manaton|Manaton]] and into the countryside [PN=045-045]. [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] itself was a modest structure — an artist's retreat where [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] painted and, apparently, conducted mycological experiments.

The sequence of events at [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] is reconstructed from multiple testimonies:
- [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] prepared a mushroom dish, claiming it as his specialty
- [[george-harrison|Harrison]] ate the dish and subsequently became violently ill
- His death appeared to be from heart failure [PN=047-050]
- The police were called from [[bovey-tracey|Bovey Tracey]] [PN=047-044]
- The body was examined, and the investigation began
