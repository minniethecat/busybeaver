# Christmas 1928 at Whittington Terrace

## Summary

The Christmas season of 1928 marks the arrival of [[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]] at [[whittington-terrace|15 Whittington Terrace, Bayswater]] — the event that sets the entire tragedy in motion. [[the-dossier|Paul Harrison's dossier]] notes that [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] "joined them on or about the 2nd of December 1928" [PN=025-003], renting the ground-floor maisonnette adjacent to the [[george-harrison|Harrison]] household.

## Context

[[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s arrival transforms the household dynamic. He is a young, penniless artist — an "incorrigible extrovert" [PN=037-004] whose charm and bohemian ways captivate [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]] and unsettle her husband. [[john-munting|Munting]] describes [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s belongings spread everywhere [PN=005-005] and notes his immediate effect on the household atmosphere.

The Christmas period establishes the key relationships: [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s growing intimacy with [[margaret-harrison|Margaret]], his friendship with [[john-munting|Munting]], his tension with [[george-harrison|George Harrison]]. By Christmas Day, the seeds of [[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] are already planted — though no one yet recognizes them.

The [[paris-trip-december-1928|Paris trip]] follows immediately after Christmas: George and [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]] travel to Paris from December 15-28 [PN=025-002], a brief removal from the pressure cooker of Bayswater.
