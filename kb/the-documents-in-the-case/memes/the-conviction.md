# Conviction of Harwood Lathom

## Summary

[[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]] was convicted in October 1930 of "[[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] of [[george-harrison|George Harrison]] at [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack, Manaton]], by poisoning with [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]]" [PN=053-013]. The conviction, reported in the [[morning-express|Morning Express]] of November 30, 1930 [PN=053-011], brings the documentary dossier to its conclusion.

## Context

The conviction represents the formal closure of the case — the legal system's validation of [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]]'s documentary evidence. After a year of investigation, inquest, arrest, and trial, the jury accepted the prosecution's case: that [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] had deliberately prepared synthetic [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] and administered it to [[george-harrison|George Harrison]].

[[morning-express|The Morning Express]] report of the conviction [PN=053-011] is the final document in the dossier. [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]] includes it as the capstone — the public record confirming what his private investigation had established.

The conviction also marks the end of [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]]'s legal jeopardy. Though [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]] had accused her of instigating [[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] [PN=049-257], she was not convicted. Her fate after the trial is not recorded in the dossier.
