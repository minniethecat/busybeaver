# The Affair: Margaret Harrison and Harwood Lathom

## Summary

The affair between [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]] and [[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]] is the emotional core of [[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] case. Developing during February 1929 — the month before [[george-harrison|George Harrison]]'s death [PN=029-003] — it provided the motive that [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]] would later articulate: "Plenty of motive for murder there" [PN=049-277].

## Context

The affair unfolded within the claustrophobic confines of [[whittington-terrace|15 Whittington Terrace]]. [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]], the young artist renting the ground-floor maisonnette, and [[margaret-harrison|Margaret]], the restless wife of a middle-aged accountant, were thrown together by proximity and mutual attraction. [[the-dossier|Paul Harrison's dossier]] documents "the connection between my stepmother and [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] during February" [PN=029-003].

Key developments:
- [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s growing intimacy with [[margaret-harrison|Margaret]], observed by [[john-munting|Munting]] and [[agatha-milsom|Miss Milsom]]
- [[george-harrison|George Harrison]]'s awareness and unusual tolerance: "was never jealous of [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]], as he was of every other man" [PN=037-020]
- [[margaret-harrison|Margaret]]'s letter to [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] "clearly indicating the affair was over" [PN=049-273] — a declaration whose timing and sincerity became critical
- The prosecution's claim that [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] was "very much under the influence of [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]]" [PN=049-283]
