# Warty Caps

## Overview

Warty Caps (*Amanita rubescens*) is an edible fungus that closely resembles the poisonous Fly Agaric (*[[amanita-muscaria|Amanita muscaria]]*). This resemblance is central to the case: the defence argued that [[george-harrison|George Harrison]] accidentally gathered the poisonous Fly Agaric while intending to collect Warty Caps.

## Identification

Warty Caps (*Amanita rubescens*) is described as "an edible fungus" which the Fly Agaric "very closely resembled" [PN=048-014]. The distinguishing features that separate the two species are subtle enough that an experienced forager might plausibly confuse them — a fact exploited by both the prosecution and defence at [[the-inquest|the inquest]].

## Role in the Case

### Harrison's Intention

George Harrison stated his intention to gather Warty Caps on the morning of 17 October 1929, the day before his death. In a letter to [[paul-harrison|his son]], Harrison wrote: "tomorrow morning I am going out in search of *Amanita rubescens*, which I intend to try very slowly and delicately stewed in beef broth" [PN=015-006].

### At the Inquest

At the inquest, Sir James Lubbock's testimony confirmed the dangerous similarity: the Fly Agaric "was liable to be eaten in mistake for another member of the same family, *Amanita rubescens*, or Warty Caps" [PN=048-014].

[[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]] confirmed that Harrison "had spoken to him on the Wednesday evening about his intention of gathering fungi the next day, and had mentioned a name resembling 'Warty Hats' or 'Warty Caps'" [PN=048-016].

### Paul Harrison's Investigation

[[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]], investigating his father's death, questioned whether Warty Caps were actually found where his father was seen gathering. He noted: "For Lathom's purpose it was necessary that Harrison should have had the intention of gathering Warty Caps, and no other kind of fungus, since this is the only variety which could reasonably be confused with *Amanita muscaria*" [PN=052-112].

He asked pointedly: "Are Warty Caps (*Amanita rubescens*) actually plentiful in the spot where Harrison was seen by Coffin?" [PN=052-113] — questioning whether the accidental poisoning theory was viable.

## Significance

The Warty Caps/Fly Agaric distinction is the hinge on which the case turns. If Harrison knew the difference, his death was murder. If he confused them, his death was a tragic accident. The botanical facts — the appearance, habitat, and season of these two fungi — are thus central to determining guilt or innocence.
