# Motive in the Harrison Case

The question of motive — why [[george-harrison|George Harrison]] was murdered — is one of the central puzzles of the case. The correspondence reveals multiple possible motives, none of which is conclusively established, leaving the reader to weigh the evidence.

## The Romantic Motive

[[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]]'s relationship with [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]] provided the most obvious motive. Their [[the-affair|affair]] was known or suspected by multiple correspondents. The prospect of marriage was constrained by the existing Harrison marriage — and a divorce would have been scandalous for a woman in Margaret Harrison's position [PN=040-004].

Margaret herself acknowledged the impossibility of obtaining a timely divorce, writing to Lathom that "even if we got a divorce, it wouldn't be in time" [PN=044-005]. The implication was clear: only the death of George Harrison could free the lovers to marry.

## The Financial Motive

As executor under his father's will, [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]] stood to benefit from George Harrison's death. The narrative acknowledges this directly: "Plenty of motive for murder there" [PN=049-277]. Paul's own investigation was partly motivated by the need to clear himself of suspicion on this ground.

## The Escape Motive

[[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]]'s letters to Lathom reveal a woman desperate to escape an intolerable domestic situation. George Harrison was portrayed as an eccentric, controlling husband whose obsessions with fungi and hedgehogs made life unbearable. Margaret wrote of the "stain of the divorce courts" [PN=040-004] — marriage to Harrison was a prison from which only his death could provide release without social ruin.

## Paul Harrison's Assessment

Paul Harrison, in compiling [[the-dossier|his dossier]], identified motive as the critical gap in the case. While he could establish the means ([[synthetic-muscarine|synthetic muscarine]]) and the opportunity, the question of who had the most compelling reason to wish George Harrison dead remained the subject of competing interpretations.
