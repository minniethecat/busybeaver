# Elizabeth Drake

## Overview

Elizabeth Drake, affectionately nicknamed "Bungie" by her fiancé [[john-munting|John Munting]], is one of the key recipients in the epistolary network of *[[the-documents-in-the-case-novel|The Documents in the Case]]*. [[john-munting|Munting]]'s letters to her form the second major narrative thread, providing a grounded, scientific counterpoint to [[agatha-milsom|Agatha Milsom]]'s psychological observations. She writes at least one letter directly (Chapter 34) and receives a telegram at a critical moment [PN=035-001].

## Role in the Case

Elizabeth Drake is physically absent from the Bayswater household but emotionally present throughout. [[john-munting|Munting]]'s letters to her document the unfolding events at [[whittington-terrace|15 Whittington Terrace]] — [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s arrival, the growing tensions, the dinner parties, and ultimately the death of [[george-harrison|George Harrison]] [PN=005-001] [PN=027-001].

Her role is that of the trusted confidante. [[john-munting|Munting]] shares with her details he would share with no one else — his opinion of [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]], his observations about [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]], his chemical knowledge. She also receives a rare letter from [[agatha-milsom|Agatha Milsom]] [PN=033-001], suggesting she is well-regarded by the broader social circle.

The telegram from [[john-munting|Munting]] to Drake dated February 26, 1929 [PN=035-001] marks a critical moment in the timeline — likely relating to [[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] plan or its discovery.

## Character

Elizabeth Drake remains largely off-stage, her personality refracted through [[john-munting|Munting]]'s affectionate letters. He addresses her as "Bungie" and writes to her with warmth and openness. She is a stabilizing presence in [[john-munting|Munting]]'s life — the future he is working toward while caught up in the [[george-harrison|Harrison]] household drama.
