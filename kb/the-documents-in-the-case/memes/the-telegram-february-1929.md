# The Telegram (February 26, 1929)

## Summary

On February 26, 1929, [[john-munting|John Munting]] sent a telegram to his fiancée [[elizabeth-drake|Elizabeth Drake]] containing the cryptic message: "A little mad and quite mistaken" [PN=035-001]. This terse communication marks a critical turning point in the timeline — the moment [[john-munting|Munting]] recognized something was deeply wrong at [[whittington-terrace|15 Whittington Terrace]].

## Context

The telegram falls within the crucial February 1929 window — the month when [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] and [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]]'s affair was developing [PN=029-003]. [[john-munting|Munting]]'s message suggests a moment of realization: he had been "mistaken" about something significant, and the situation was "mad" — beyond normal expectations.

The brevity of the telegram (six words) contrasts with [[john-munting|Munting]]'s usual lengthy letters to [[elizabeth-drake|Elizabeth]]. The urgency of the medium — a telegram rather than a letter — suggests the information was time-sensitive. In retrospect, this may have been [[john-munting|Munting]]'s first articulation of suspicion about [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] and the poison.
