# 15 Whittington Terrace, Bayswater

15 Whittington Terrace, Bayswater, is the [[george-harrison|Harrison]] family home and the domestic stage upon which the relationships, tensions, and preparations leading to [[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] of [[george-harrison|George Harrison]] unfolded. The address — appearing in the heading of nearly every early letter in *[[the-documents-in-the-case-novel|The Documents in the Case]]* [PN=001-002] through [PN=010-002] — anchors the documentary record in a specific London geography: a respectable Bayswater terrace where a respectable accountant lived with his wife, his lodgers, and, ultimately, his murderer.

## The Household

The building at 15 Whittington Terrace accommodated three distinct living units:

| Occupant | Unit | Relationship to Case |
|----------|------|---------------------|
| George and [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]] | Main residence | Victim and victim's wife |
| [[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]] | Ground-floor maisonnette | Lodger; future accused murderer |
| [[john-munting|John Munting]] | Flat above | Lodger; chemist and key witness |

The arrangement meant that [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] and [[john-munting|Munting]] — the accused and the expert witness whose testimony would help convict him — lived mere feet apart, sharing the same building, the same kitchen access, and the same intimate exposure to the [[george-harrison|Harrison]]s' domestic life. This proximity was forensically critical: [[john-munting|Munting]] observed [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s behavior, expertise, and relationship with [[margaret-harrison|Margaret]] from a vantage point that no outside investigator could replicate.

The address variations in the letters — sometimes "15, Whittington Terrace" [PN=001-002], sometimes "15a, Whittington Terrace" [PN=005-002] — reflect the subdivided nature of the property, with separate postal designations for the main house and the flats.

## The Mushroom Dinner Party

The most significant event at Whittington Terrace was the mushroom dinner party at which [[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]] prepared his "special mushroom dish with his own fair hand" [PN=002-012]. The dinner took place in the [[george-harrison|Harrison]] kitchen — [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s unrestricted access to which would later become a key element of the prosecution's case. The meal demonstrated:

- [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s mycological expertise in practice
- His accepted role as the household's culinary specialist in fungi
- The trust that the [[george-harrison|Harrison]]s placed in food he prepared
- The social dynamics — [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] at the center, [[margaret-harrison|Margaret]] drawn to him, [[george-harrison|Harrison]] watching from the periphery

In retrospect, the [[the-mushroom-dinner-party|dinner at Whittington Terrace]] was the dress rehearsal for the fatal meal at [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]].

## Domestic Tensions

The letters written from Whittington Terrace trace the arc of domestic tension that provides the motive for murder. [[george-harrison|George Harrison]]'s letters to his son [[paul-harrison|Paul]] — all headed from this address — move from cheerful domestic chronicle to concerned reports about [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s influence on [[margaret-harrison|Margaret]] [PN=036-009].

The spatial arrangement of the building reinforced the psychological geometry: [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] in the ground-floor flat, directly beneath the [[george-harrison|Harrison]]s' living quarters; [[john-munting|Munting]] above, observing. The physical proximity that made the household function also made it impossible for any resident to be unaware of the others' movements, relationships, and tensions.
