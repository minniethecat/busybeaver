# Morning Express Lubbock Testimony

A newspaper report published in the _Morning Express_ during the autumn of 1929, containing testimony or statements related to the Harrison poisoning case. The report forms part of the press coverage that the case attracted once it became a matter of public record.

## Context

The _[[morning-express|Morning Express]]_ was one of the London newspapers that reported on the inquest and subsequent proceedings. The Lubbock testimony represents a specific deposition or witness account carried by the paper, likely relating to evidence presented at [[the-inquest|the inquest]] into the death of [[george-harrison|George Harrison]].

## Relationship to Other Evidence

This press report should be read alongside the other _Morning Express_ extracts in the case file, particularly the reports of October 21st and November 5th, 1929. Newspaper coverage provides an external perspective on the investigation, complementing the private correspondence that makes up the bulk of the case documents.
