# Hedgehog Cookery

Hedgehog cookery was one of [[george-harrison|George Harrison]]'s most distinctive and frequently remarked-upon eccentricities. His practice of preparing and eating hedgehogs recurs throughout the correspondence as a symbol of his unconventional approach to domestic life.

## A Culinary Peculiarity

Harrison's interest in cooking hedgehogs was mentioned by multiple correspondents. [[agatha-milsom|Agatha Milsom]] observed wryly that Harrison appeared capable of feeding "the entire nation on boiled hedgehogs or some such nastiness" [PN=016-005]. She later complained that Harrison talked "interminably about his eternal fungi and hedgehogs" [PN=018-006].

The practice was sufficiently notable to be raised during the inquest proceedings. A witness testified that "one evening we had swealed hedgehog," drawing laughter from the courtroom [PN=047-015]. The unconventional dish was presented as evidence of Harrison's eccentric domestic arrangements.

## In the Press

The *Morning Express* coverage of the inquest reported that Harrison "had a mania for cooking unconventional dishes of hedgehogs, mushrooms and other natural products" [PN=048-006]. The newspaper's characterisation linked hedgehog cookery with Harrison's wider interest in foraging and natural foods.

## Connection to Fungi

Harrison's hedgehog cookery was thematically linked to his passion for fungi collecting. [[john-munting|John Munting]] described Harrison acquiring "a taste for hedgehog-broth and stewed toadstools" [PN=045-032] — a combination that neatly encapsulated both the eccentricity and the potential danger of Harrison's culinary experiments.

## Narrative Function

The hedgehog motif serves multiple functions in the narrative. It establishes Harrison's character as an obsessive hobbyist who pursued his interests regardless of social convention. It provides moments of dark comedy — as when Agatha imagines the nation fed on boiled hedgehogs. And it foreshadows the tragedy: a man who ate unconventional wild foods was susceptible to unconventional poisoning.
