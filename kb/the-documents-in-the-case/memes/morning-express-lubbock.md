# Morning Express: Lubbock Testimony (November 5, 1929)

## Summary

The *[[morning-express|Morning Express]]* of Wednesday, November 5th, 1929, published the sensational forensic testimony of [[james-lubbock|Sir James Lubbock]] under the headline "[[james-lubbock|Sir James Lubbock]] on Shack Poison Drama" [PN=048-002]. This is the key scientific evidence document in the case — it establishes that [[george-harrison|George Harrison]] was murdered by deliberate [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] poisoning.

## Key Content

[[james-lubbock|Sir James Lubbock]], [[james-lubbock|the Home Office Analyst]], testifies:

- "I obtained by analysis a considerable quantity of a substance known as [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]]" from the dish of fungus [PN=048-009]
- He estimates the amount of [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] present and describes its lethal effects [PN=048-010]
- The poison "would paralyze" cardiac function [PN=048-013]
- He identifies the source as [[amanita-muscaria|Amanita muscaria]] — "the [[amanita-muscaria|Fly Agaric]]" [PN=048-019]
- All food items and diet found at [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] were analyzed [PN=048-021]
- Specimens were passed among the jury [PN=048-020]

## Analysis

[[james-lubbock|Sir James Lubbock]]'s testimony transforms the case from a suspicious death to a confirmed homicide. The detail of specimens being "passed round among the jury" [PN=048-020] is a vivid courtroom moment — the physical evidence made tangible.

The newspaper format is significant. Unlike the private letters that form [[the-documents-in-the-case-novel|the novel]]'s core, this is public testimony — recorded, reported, and subject to scrutiny. It represents the intersection of forensic science and popular journalism in 1920s Britain.
