# Bovey Tracey

Bovey Tracey is a market town in [[manaton|Devon]], England. It served as the administrative and logistical centre of the investigation into [[george-harrison|George Harrison]]'s death [PN=047-035].

The town's railway station, police station, and the medical practice of [[dr-hughes|Dr. Hughes]] made it the hub through which most official activity in the case was routed. Its role spanned from the discovery of [[george-harrison|Harrison]]'s body through to the [[the-inquest|inquest]] proceedings.

The town lies on the branch railway line connecting [[newton-abbot|Newton Abbot]] to the Moretonhampstead line, with intermediate stations that included the stop nearest to [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack at Manaton]] [PN=049-375].

## Transportation Hub

Bovey Tracey was the rail gateway to [[manaton|Manaton]]. Travellers from London would take the Great Western Railway to [[newton-abbot|Newton Abbot]], change to the local branch line, and arrive at Bovey Tracey station [PN=045-031]. From there they proceeded by road to the scattered hamlets of Dartmoor.

[[george-harrison|Harrison]] himself made this journey on multiple occasions. On the weekend of his death, he "went up by the 8:13 from Bovey Tracey on the Thursday morning," travelling to London before returning to [[manaton|Devon]] [PN=047-035].

> "I went up by the 8:13 from Bovey Tracey on the Thursday morning. I had been staying at The Shack with Mr. Harrison." — John Munting [PN=047-035]

The station's booking-clerk would later prove a key witness. He "had clearly remembered issuing" a ticket that helped establish the timeline of [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s movements [PN=049-385].

The return journey was equally significant to the investigation. [[john-munting|Munting]] calculated that a train "would land him back at Bovey Tracey at about half-past six" — a timing critical to reconstructing possible alibis [PN=049-386].

## The Inquest and Autopsy

Bovey Tracey was the site of the official inquest into [[death-of-george-harrison|Harrison's death]]. The proceedings drew together the medical, police, and witness testimony that formed the documentary record of the case [PN=048-005].

[[dr-hughes|Dr. Hughes of Bovey Tracey]] was a central medical figure. Described as "a brisk and confident young man," he was the local physician who attended the case from its earliest stages [PN=045-139].

> "Dr. Hughes of Bovey Tracey, who performed the autopsy, stated that death was due to heart failure following upon acute congestion of the lungs." [PN=048-023]

The post-mortem examination was conducted after the body had been "removed to Bovey Tracey for the purpose of an autopsy" [PN=047-049]. This made the town's medical facilities the locus of the forensic investigation.

## Investigation Centre

The police investigation was coordinated from Bovey Tracey. When [[george-harrison|Harrison]]'s body was discovered at [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]], [[john-munting|Munting]] "himself summoned the police from Bovey Tracey, the village constable being, it appeared, away from home" [PN=049-361].

> "Sergeant Warbeck, who received me at the police-station at Bovey Tracey, was a sensible, stolid-looking man." — John Munting [PN=047-044]

[[sergeant-warbeck|Sergeant Warbeck]] oversaw the initial investigation. [[john-munting|Munting]], after interviewing witnesses in the area, "next day departed to Bovey Tracey to check [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s movements" — using the town as his base of operations [PN=049-368].

The town also had a commercial printer whose evidence became relevant. [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] claimed he had been "on the train for a printer at Bovey Tracey" — an alibi that investigators sought to verify [PN=049-374].
