# Sergeant Warbeck

## Overview

Sergeant Warbeck is the police officer stationed at [[bovey-tracey|Bovey Tracey]], the nearest police station to [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] in [[manaton|Manaton]]. He receives the initial report of [[george-harrison|George Harrison]]'s death and participates in the first phase of the investigation before the case is escalated to higher authorities.

## Role in the Case

Sergeant Warbeck is the first police responder. "Sergeant Warbeck, who received the message, communicated with headquarters" [PN=047-044] — initiating the official investigation. He is present at [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] during the initial examination of the scene, directed by a superior officer: "Oh, yes. Here, Sergeant — you'd better take charge of this" [PN=045-142].

He is instructed to "hold the fort" at [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] until the Superintendent arrives [PN=045-151], and later told the evidence "will all have to go to London" [PN=045-149] — the investigation quickly escalates beyond local police jurisdiction.

## Character

Sergeant Warbeck is a functionary in the police hierarchy — competent, dutiful, but not central to the investigation's intellectual work. He represents the machinery of law enforcement: the local officer who initiates the process that eventually leads to Scotland Yard involvement and the [[chemical-analysis-report|Home Office analysis]].

His brief appearances in the narrative provide procedural realism — [[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] investigation is not solved by brilliant amateurs alone but involves the full apparatus of the British justice system.
