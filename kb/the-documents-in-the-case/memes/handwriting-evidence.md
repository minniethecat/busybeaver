# Handwriting Evidence

Handwriting evidence played a crucial role in [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]]'s investigation into his father's death. His examination of questioned documents revealed that certain papers — ostensibly written by one person — were in fact in [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]]'s handwriting.

## The Discovery

Paul Harrison, while compiling [[the-dossier|the dossier]] of evidence for his investigation, examined documents that had been represented to him as originating from various parties. He recognised documents in his stepmother's handwriting that had been presented under different authorship [PN=049-213].

His method was direct: he paid a lad to obtain genuine samples of his stepmother's handwriting, and these confirmed his suspicions that certain contested documents were indeed hers [PN=049-247].

## George Harrison's Notes

Among the documents Paul examined were notes in his father's handwriting. He found that [[george-harrison|George Harrison]] had used certain papers as scratch paper, annotating them with notes in his distinctive hand [PN=049-366].

## Forensic Significance

The handwriting evidence formed part of Paul Harrison's larger reconstruction of the events leading to his father's death. The forged documents suggested a pattern of deception that extended beyond the immediate circumstances of the poisoning — pointing to a broader conspiracy of misrepresentation within the household.
