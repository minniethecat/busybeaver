# Morning Express: Inquest Report (October 21, 1929)

## Summary

The *[[morning-express|Morning Express]]* of Tuesday, October 21st, 1929, published the first major press report on the death of [[george-harrison|George Harrison]], under the headline "Mushroom Death Mystery Inquest" [PN=047-001]. This extract, preserved in [[the-dossier|Paul Harrison's dossier]], provides the public record of the inquest's opening and the first formal testimony.

## Key Content

The report covers the opening of the inquest by Dr. Pringle, the coroner for the district [PN=047-005]. Key testimony includes:

- Formal evidence of identification [PN=047-009]
- The inquest's adjournment for a fortnight "to permit of an analysis of the contents of the viscera" [PN=047-008]
- The widow [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]]'s appearance in court: "fashionably dressed" [PN=047-056]
- [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]]'s status: "a civil engineer, now absent" [PN=047-057]

## Analysis

The newspaper coverage serves multiple functions in the narrative. It provides the official public version of events — the record against which the private letters and statements can be measured. The sensationalist headline ("Mushroom Death Mystery") contrasts with the sober forensic details of [[james-lubbock|Sir James Lubbock]]'s later testimony.

The fact that [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]] preserves these extracts in his dossier suggests he saw value in the public record — either as unbiased corroboration or as evidence of how the case was perceived.
