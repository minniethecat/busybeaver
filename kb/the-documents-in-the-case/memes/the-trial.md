# The Trial of Harwood Lathom

## Summary

The trial of [[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]] for [[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] of [[george-harrison|George Harrison]] represents the culmination of the documentary evidence compiled in the dossier. The proceedings, reported in the [[morning-express|Morning Express]] [PN=053-011], drew on the letters, statements, telegrams, and forensic analyses assembled by [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]].

## Context

The trial is the arena where private documents become public evidence. [[agatha-milsom|Agatha Milsom]]'s letters about household tensions, [[john-munting|Munting]]'s testimony about synthetic poisons, [[james-lubbock|Sir James Lubbock]]'s forensic analysis — all enter the legal record. The dossier that the reader holds is, in effect, the trial evidence.

Key testimony included:
- [[james-lubbock|Sir James Lubbock]]'s identification of synthetic [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] [PN=049-303]
- [[john-munting|John Munting]]'s account of [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s mushroom expertise and chemical knowledge
- [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]]'s comprehensive statement compiling documentary evidence
- Evidence of [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s affair with [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]] [PN=049-252]

The trial concluded with [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s conviction for murder [PN=053-013].
