# Brimley Halt

## Overview

Brimley Halt is a small railway stop on the local line between Newton Abbot and [[bovey-tracey|Bovey Tracey]] in Devon. It appears in the case as a point on [[john-munting|John Munting]]'s journey to Manaton and, more significantly, as a possible point from which [[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]] could have secretly returned to [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]].

## John Munting's Journey

Munting describes passing through Brimley Halt on his journey to visit Lathom at The Shack: "At Newton Abbot we changed into the local, and dawdled through Teigngrace, Heathfield and Brimley Halt, taking over half an hour about it, till we were turned out, twenty minutes late, on the platform at Bovey Tracey" [PN=032-003].

## Forensic Significance

[[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]], in his investigation, questioned whether Lathom could have staged his departure to London and secretly returned to The Shack to poison his father. He wrote: "By hiring a motor-bicycle or car, he might easily have made his way back from Bovey Tracey or (if this might appear too obvious) from Brimley Halt, Heathfield, Teigngrace or Newton Abbot" [PN=052-115].

Brimley Halt's significance lies in its obscurity — a small, unstaffed stopping point where a passenger might arrive or depart without attracting notice. If Lathom wished to return to The Shack unobserved, a small halt like Brimley would offer better cover than the larger station at Bovey Tracey.
