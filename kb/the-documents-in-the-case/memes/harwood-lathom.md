# Harwood Lathom

Harwood Lathom is the accused murderer at the center of *[[the-documents-in-the-case-novel|The Documents in the Case]]* — a young, penniless artist and expert amateur mycologist whose knowledge of [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] and access to the [[george-harrison|Harrison]] kitchen form the forensic backbone of the prosecution's case. Lathom boards at [[whittington-terrace|15 Whittington Terrace, Bayswater]], where his affair with [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]] and his growing tensions with her husband [[george-harrison|George Harrison]] create the motive that, combined with means and opportunity, leads to his arrest and trial. His eventual conviction for murder by synthetic [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] poisoning rests on a chain of circumstantial evidence in which his mycological expertise — genuinely scientific yet fatally applied — is the decisive element [PN=048-008].

## Character and Personality

Lathom is described by his flatmate [[john-munting|John Munting]] as "an incorrigible extrovert" who survived the rigors of public school education at Winchester "by force of personality and complete inability to know when he was beaten" [PN=037-004] [PN=037-003]. He possesses what [[john-munting|Munting]] calls "a robust contempt for all theories and formulae" [PN=022-009] — an artistic temperament that sets him apart from the conventional [[george-harrison|Harrison]]s.

His charm is his primary social asset, but it masks a darker volatility. When contradicted, he "scowled frightfully" [PN=009-021], and his relationship with [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]] reveals a man who, according to [[john-munting|Munting]]'s careful observation, had been encouraged rather than having taken the initiative [PN=037-025].

> "He is an incorrigible extrovert — one of those people who have survived the brutalities of public school life by force of personality and complete inability to know when he was beaten." [PN=037-004]

## Arrival at Whittington Terrace

Lathom joined the [[george-harrison|Harrison]] household shortly before [[christmas-1928-whittington-terrace|Christmas 1928]], renting the ground-floor maisonnette at [[whittington-terrace|15 Whittington Terrace]] [PN=025-003]. His arrival transformed the domestic atmosphere. [[john-munting|Munting]], occupying the flat above, observed the new dynamic with a scientist's detachment: Lathom at the center, [[margaret-harrison|Margaret]] drawn into his orbit, [[george-harrison|Harrison]] watching with growing unease.

The social reconfiguration of the household occurred rapidly. By February 1929, Lathom and [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]] had begun an affair [PN=029-003] — a relationship conducted within the confines of the house where both [[margaret-harrison|Margaret]]'s husband and the watchful [[john-munting|Munting]] lived mere feet away.

## Mycological Expertise

Lathom's knowledge of fungi is the forensic keystone of the case. Unlike a casual enthusiast, his expertise was described by [[john-munting|Munting]] as having genuine "scientific value" [PN=027-006]. He was preparing a "fungus-book for the press" — a serious scholarly work complete with illustrations [PN=027-005] [PN=027-011].

This expertise manifested in several ways that would later prove legally significant:

| Domain | Evidence | Implication |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Field identification | Lathom knew precisely where and when to find specific fungi species, including toxic varieties [PN=049-325] | Demonstrated ability to source poisonous specimens |
| Culinary preparation | He prepared his "special mushroom dish with his own fair hand" for the household dinner [PN=002-012] | Proved access to the [[george-harrison|Harrison]] kitchen and skill in mushroom cookery |
| Toxicological knowledge | He understood the distinction between edible and deadly species, including the visual similarity between Warty Caps and toxic *[[amanita-muscaria|Amanita]]* variants [PN=048-019] | Established that any misidentification would be willful, not accidental |
| Chemical extraction | [[john-munting|Munting]]'s experiments later proved that synthetic [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] could be produced through simple laboratory methods — chemistry within Lathom's demonstrated capabilities [PN=051-048] | Closed the gap between mycological knowledge and homicidal application |

[[john-munting|Munting]] noted that Lathom "really cares about mushrooms and takes trouble to study them" [PN=042-006] — an observation that, read in retrospect, carries the weight of forensic implication.

## The Affair with Margaret Harrison

Lathom's relationship with [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]] developed during the winter of 1928–1929. [[the-affair|The affair]] was an open secret within the household, acknowledged if not discussed. [[margaret-harrison|Margaret]], significantly older than the young artist, was described as having actively encouraged the relationship [PN=037-025].

The letters between [[margaret-harrison|Margaret]] and Lathom, reprinted in the documentary record, reveal the emotional intensity of their connection [PN=038-001] [PN=046-001]. These letters became prosecution exhibits, establishing the motive: Lathom and [[margaret-harrison|Margaret]], in love, stood to gain from [[death-of-george-harrison|Harrison's death]].

## Chemical Knowledge and the Synthetic Poison

The most damning aspect of the case against Lathom was his demonstrated understanding of synthetic chemistry. [[john-munting|Munting]] recalled a conversation at the dinner table where Lathom displayed detailed knowledge of "synthetic drugs — all made on the premises" [PN=031-010]. This conversation, combined with [[john-munting|Munting]]'s later demonstration that "you couldn't distinguish artificial [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] from the natural product by chemical analysis" [PN=051-064], placed Lathom in a uniquely incriminating position: he possessed both the botanical knowledge to identify toxic fungi and the chemical knowledge to extract and purify their active alkaloids.

> "You couldn't distinguish artificial muscarine from the natural product by chemical analysis, once it's in the body." [PN=051-064]

This forensic blind spot — the impossibility of chemically distinguishing synthetic from natural [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] — became both the prosecution's challenge and the defense's opportunity [PN=051-064].

## Arrest and Trial

Lathom was arrested following the inquest adjournment and the chemical analysis findings. His trial attracted significant press coverage, with the [[morning-express|Morning Express]] providing detailed reporting of the proceedings. The prosecution's case rested on the convergence of motive (the affair), means (mycological and chemical expertise), and opportunity (access to the [[george-harrison|Harrison]] kitchen and the mushroom-gathering expedition at [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]]).

[[the-trial|The trial]] explored the central question: could Lathom have introduced synthetic [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] into the mushroom dish consumed by [[george-harrison|Harrison]] at [[the-shack-weekend|The Shack]]? The prosecution argued that the convergence of Lathom's expertise, access, and motive made any other explanation implausible. The defense confronted the forensic limitation head-on: if synthetic and natural [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] could not be distinguished chemically, reasonable doubt remained.
