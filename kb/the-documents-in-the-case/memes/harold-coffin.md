# Harold Coffin

## Overview

Harold Coffin is a labourer from Manaton, Devon, who gave evidence at [[the-inquest|the inquest]] into [[george-harrison|George Harrison]]'s death. His testimony established that Harrison was seen searching for mushrooms and other wild plants in the valley below [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] on the morning of 17 October 1929, two days before his death.

## Evidence at the Inquest

Coffin testified that he met Harrison on the morning of 17 October at about 8 a.m. Harrison had "a satchel slung over his shoulder, and appeared to be searching for something on the ground" [PN=048-015]. Harrison was then entering a small wood situated in the valley below Manaton.

The witness stated that he had "frequently seen deceased wandering about the country, sometimes with a sketching-easel and sometimes gathering plants and roots" [PN=048-015]. He further testified that Harrison had "sometimes conversed with the witness about making a meal of unnatural things, such as nettles and toadstools" [PN=048-015]. Coffin added that he had "always supposed him to be a little peculiar in his head" [PN=048-015].

## Significance

Coffin's testimony was significant for two reasons:

1. **Timeline**: It placed Harrison actively gathering wild fungi on the morning of 17 October — two days before his body was discovered on 19 October. This establishes that Harrison had been foraging shortly before his death.

2. **Character Evidence**: Coffin's description of Harrison as "a little peculiar in his head" — based on Harrison's habits of gathering and eating "unnatural things" — contributed to the picture of Harrison as an eccentric whose unconventional dietary experiments could plausibly have led to accidental poisoning.

## Later Interview by Paul Harrison

[[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]], investigating his father's death, later sought out Coffin. Following directions from Coffin's wife, Paul found him "carting some timber which had fallen in the recent gale" on the outskirts of a small wood [PN=052-103]. Coffin "was very ready to tell me all he knew, and led me at once to the spot, not very far away, where he had last seen my father" [PN=052-103].
