# Arrest of Harwood Lathom

## Summary

[[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]]'s arrest followed the presentation of [[james-lubbock|Sir James Lubbock]]'s forensic evidence at the resumed inquest. The identification of synthetic [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] in [[george-harrison|George Harrison]]'s remains transformed suspicion into probable cause, and [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] — the mushroom expert with motive and opportunity — was taken into custody.

## Context

The arrest occurred after the inquest jury brought in an initial [[the-conviction|verdict]] of "Accidental Death, due to poisoning by [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]]" [PN=048-028]. However, the evidence of synthetic origin — and the accumulating documentary case compiled by [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]] — led to [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] being charged with murder.

The arrest separated [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] from [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]], triggering the sequence of letters, statements, and legal proceedings that form the latter third of the dossier. His letter from Paris [PN=037-065] suggests he may have fled briefly before being apprehended.
