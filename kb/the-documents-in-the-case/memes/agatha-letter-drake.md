# Milsom's Letter to Elizabeth Drake

## Summary

[[agatha-milsom|Agatha Milsom]]'s letter to [[elizabeth-drake|Elizabeth Drake]] [PN=033-001] is a rare cross-correspondence — the primary narrator reaching out to another character in [[john-munting|Munting]]'s epistolary circle. It demonstrates the interconnectedness of the documentary network and provides a fresh perspective on Agatha's role as observer and informant.

## Key Content

This letter represents Agatha stepping outside her usual correspondence with [[olive-farebrother|Olive Farebrother]] to communicate with [[elizabeth-drake|Elizabeth Drake]]. The content likely concerns the situation at [[whittington-terrace|15 Whittington Terrace]] — Agatha sharing her observations with someone who, through [[john-munting|Munting]]'s letters, already has partial knowledge of events.

## Analysis

The letter is significant for what it represents: the epistolary network is not isolated threads but a web. Agatha writes to [[elizabeth-drake|Elizabeth]]; [[john-munting|Munting]] writes to [[elizabeth-drake|Elizabeth]]; [[elizabeth-drake|Elizabeth]] writes back to [[john-munting|Munting]] [PN=034-001].

Information circulates; perspectives cross-reference.

Agatha's choice to write to [[elizabeth-drake|Elizabeth]] suggests either concern (warning her about the situation her fiancé is entangled in) or a desire for additional confirmation ([[elizabeth-drake|Elizabeth]] as another pair of eyes on the [[george-harrison|Harrison]] household).
