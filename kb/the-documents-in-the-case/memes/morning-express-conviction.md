# Morning Express: Conviction Report (November 30, 1930)

## Summary

The *[[morning-express|Morning Express]]* of November 30, 1930 [PN=053-011], records the final outcome of the [[george-harrison|Harrison]] murder case. After a year of investigation and legal proceedings, the conviction brings closure to the documentary dossier. [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]] includes this extract as the final piece of public evidence.

## Key Content

The report covers the trial's conclusion:
- [[the-conviction|The conviction]] for "[[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] of [[george-harrison|George Harrison]] at [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack, Manaton]], by poison" [PN=053-013]
- The method: [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] extracted from [[amanita-muscaria|Amanita muscaria]] mushrooms
- The sentence and its implications

## Analysis

This final newspaper extract closes the public narrative. Where the first [[morning-express|Morning Express]] report [PN=047-001] opened with a mystery ("[[morning-express-inquest|Mushroom Death Mystery Inquest]]"), this final report closes with certainty — a conviction. The arc from mystery to resolution is complete.

[[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]]'s inclusion of this extract serves a dual purpose: it provides the official validation of his private investigation, and it marks the end of the documentary record. The case is closed; the dossier is complete.
