# Paul Harrison's Editorial Notes

## Summary

[[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]]'s editorial notes punctuate the documentary dossier at key transitions. Appearing in Chapters 25, 29, and 53, these notes provide the editorial frame that transforms a collection of letters and statements into a coherent narrative. They are the meta-documents — the compiler explaining his choices.

## Key Content

[[paul-harrison|Paul]]'s notes serve multiple functions:

- **Context**: Explaining gaps in the record: "no references to my father's domestic affairs during this period" [PN=029-002]
- **Chronology**: Establishing the timeline: [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] "joined them on or about the 2nd of December 1928" [PN=025-003]
- **Attribution**: Identifying sources and acknowledging limitations
- **Conclusion**: Summarizing the case in the final note [PN=053-001]

## Analysis

The editorial notes are crucial to [[the-documents-in-the-case-novel|the novel]]'s structure. They remind the reader that this is a curated collection — not raw documents but evidence selected and arranged for a purpose. [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]] is both investigator and editor, and his choices shape the reader's understanding.

The notes also serve as a bridge between the epistolary form and the detective genre. They provide the analytical voice — the detective explaining his deductions — that the letters alone cannot offer.
