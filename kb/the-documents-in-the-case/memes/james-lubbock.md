# Sir James Lubbock

## Overview

Sir James Lubbock is the Home Office Analyst whose forensic testimony provides the scientific foundation for [[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] case. His chemical analysis of [[george-harrison|George Harrison]]'s stomach contents and organs identifies [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] — the alkaloid poison extracted from [[amanita-muscaria|Amanita muscaria]] mushrooms — as the cause of death. His evidence transforms what might have been recorded as a natural death into a homicide investigation.

## Role in the Case

Sir James Lubbock's testimony is central to the forensic case. He conducts the analysis of [[george-harrison|the victim]]'s organs after the inquest is adjourned for a fortnight to permit chemical examination [PN=047-008]. His findings are reported in the press under the headline "Sir James Lubbock on [[morning-express-lubbock|Shack Poison Drama]]" [PN=048-002].

His key findings:
- "I obtained by analysis a considerable quantity of a substance known as [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]]" from the dish of fungus [PN=048-009]
- He estimated the amount of [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] present and its lethal potential [PN=048-010]
- He analyzed all items of food and diet found at [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] [PN=048-021]
- He stated on oath that the poison was of synthetic origin [PN=049-303]

The distinction between natural and synthetic [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] was crucial — it proved the poisoning was deliberate, not accidental ingestion of wild mushrooms.

## Character

Sir James Lubbock appears only through his official testimony, reported in newspaper extracts and trial records. His professional authority is unquestioned — he is the Home Office Analyst, the highest scientific authority available to the court. His testimony is precise, measured, and devastating to the defense.
