# Newton Abbot

Newton Abbot is a market town in [[manaton|Devon]], larger than nearby [[bovey-tracey|Bovey Tracey]], that served as the mainline railway junction on the route between London and [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack at Manaton]] [PN=049-376].

As the Great Western Railway stop where passengers changed to the local branch line, Newton Abbot was the gateway to Dartmoor. Every journey to and from [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] passed through its station, making it a natural focus for [[john-munting-statement|John Munting]]'s investigation into the movements of suspects and witnesses.

## Railway Junction

The journey from London to [[manaton|Manaton]] followed a fixed route: Great Western Railway to Newton Abbot, then change to the local branch line serving the [[bovey-tracey|Bovey Tracey]] and Moretonhampstead line [PN=045-031].

[[george-harrison|Harrison]]'s final journey followed this pattern. He "reached Newton Abbot dead on time at 9:15," after a journey he described as being made in "stuffy comfort" [PN=045-031]. From there, the connection was straightforward:

> "At Newton Abbot we changed into the local, and a few minutes late, on the platform at Bovey Tracey." — John Munting [PN=045-033]

The distance from Newton Abbot to [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] was significant — "ten or twelve miles" by road — making the rail connection essential for access to the isolated cottage [PN=049-323].

## Munting's Investigation

[[john-munting|Munting]] recognised Newton Abbot's strategic importance to the case. After his initial inquiries at [[bovey-tracey|Bovey Tracey]], he "decided to stay that night in Newton Abbot and interview the ticket-collector" [PN=049-385].

> "Newton Abbot is a larger place, and I anticipated obtaining a full and complete statement there." — John Munting [PN=049-376]

His expectation was well-founded. The ticket-collector at Newton Abbot could confirm or deny whether particular individuals had passed through the station on the day in question — evidence that was critical to verifying [[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]]'s alibi.

[[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] himself had "thought about changing at Newton Abbot" on the day of [[death-of-george-harrison|Harrison's death]], according to his own account [PN=049-374]. The station's role as a bottleneck — every London-bound passenger had to pass through it — made it impossible to reach [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] without leaving a trace at Newton Abbot.

## Geographic Context

Newton Abbot lies in the Teignbridge district of [[manaton|Devon]]. The area "Heathfield, Teigngrace of Newton Abbot" is referenced in the documentary record, placing the town within a network of smaller settlements [PN=049-322].

The intermediate stations between Newton Abbot and [[bovey-tracey|Bovey Tracey]] were significant to the investigation. [[john-munting|Munting]] noted that "as was natural, the memory of the railway staff at Newton Abbot was more reliable" than at the smaller intermediate stops [PN=049-375].
