# Five-Acre Wood

Five-Acre Wood is a woodland area near [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack at Manaton]], locally known for its abundance of toadstools and fungi [PN=049-363]. It features in the investigation as the probable site where the mushrooms used in the fatal dish were gathered.

## The Foraging Site

The wood was "locally known as Five-Acre Wood" and described as "a great place for toadstools" [PN=049-363]. Its proximity to [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] made it a natural location for anyone seeking wild fungi.

> "The wood, locally known as Five-Acre Wood, was a great place for toadstools and fungi." [PN=049-363]

[[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s presence in the wood was attested by a witness who "had never seen him in the Five-Acre Wood but once, and that was with mushrooms" [PN=049-364]. This placed [[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]] at the likely source of the fungi shortly before the fatal meal.

## Evidentiary Significance

The wood's location was marked on a map used during the investigation. [[john-munting|Munting]] noted that "Five-Acre Wood was clearly shown, and upon it were marked the spots where various species of fungi were to be found" [PN=049-366].

The presence of both edible and toxic species in the same wood was central to the question of whether the poisoning was accidental or deliberate — a forager familiar with the wood would know which species to collect, and which to avoid.
