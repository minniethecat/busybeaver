# The Morning Express

The *Morning Express* was a daily newspaper that provided contemporary press coverage of the [[george-harrison|George Harrison]] murder case. Three extracts from its pages form key components of the documentary record in *[[the-documents-in-the-case-novel|The Documents in the Case]]*, offering a public, journalistic perspective that complements the private letters and statements.

The newspaper's coverage spanned the full arc of the case — from the initial inquest through to the final execution — making it the most consistent external source in the documentary record.

## Published Extracts

### October 21st, 1929 — The Inquest Opens

The first extract appeared on Tuesday, October 21st, 1929, covering the opening of the inquest into [[death-of-george-harrison|Harrison's death]] [PN=047-001]. This report introduced the basic facts of the case to the public: the remote [[manaton|Devon]] cottage, the fatal fungus dish, and the key figures — [[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]], [[john-munting-statement|John Munting]], and the deceased.

The report identified [[john-munting|Munting]] by his literary credential: "Mr. [[john-munting|John Munting]], author of *[[i-to-hercules|I to Hercules]]*" [PN=048-006]. This detail reflects how the newspaper framed the story for its readership — a bestselling author involved in a mysterious death in a Dartmoor cottage.

### November 5th, 1929 — The Autopsy Evidence

The second extract, published on Wednesday, November 5th, 1929, reported on the resumed inquest and the medical evidence [PN=048-001]. This instalment included [[dr-hughes|Dr. Hughes]]'s autopsy findings and [[james-lubbock|Sir James Lubbock]]'s chemical analysis identifying [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] as the cause of death.

The reporting at this stage shifted from human interest to forensic detail, reflecting the investigation's turn toward the scientific evidence that would prove decisive.

### November 30th, 1930 — The Verdict

The final extract appeared on November 30th, 1930, more than a year after the initial reports [PN=053-011]. It carried the headline "[[manaton|Manaton]] Murderer Hanged" and reported on the conclusion of the case — the conviction and execution of the perpetrator [PN=053-012].

The span of coverage — from October 1929 to November 1930 — encompasses the full duration of the legal process, from inquest to final sentence.

## Role in the Documentary Record

The Morning Express extracts serve a structural function within *[[the-documents-in-the-case-novel|The Documents in the Case]]*. They appear at key junctures, providing a public-facing summary of events that the private letters and statements approach from more intimate angles.

The newspaper's tone — factual, journalistic, addressed to a general readership — contrasts with the personal voice of the letters and the formal register of the [[the-inquest|inquest]] testimony. This variety of perspective is essential to [[the-documents-in-the-case-novel|the novel]]'s documentary method.
