# Mrs. Cutts

## Overview

Mrs. Cutts is the charwoman at [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] in [[manaton|Manaton]], [[manaton|Devon]] — the cottage where [[george-harrison|George Harrison]] died of [[muscarine-poisoning|muscarine]] poisoning. She is a minor but vivid character whose brief appearances provide local color and incidental testimony during the police investigation.

## Role in the Case

Mrs. Cutts is present at [[the-shack-manaton|The Shack]] when the police arrive to investigate [[death-of-george-harrison|Harrison's death]]. She provides tea to the visiting gentleman who turns out to be [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]]: "Cutts is my name, Mrs. Cutts. They all know me here" [PN=049-071]. She is protective of her domain, warning [[paul-harrison|Paul]] not to touch anything: "I nearly tripped over Mrs. Cutts, who was washing the linoleum" [PN=049-136].

Her role is that of the ordinary person caught up in extraordinary events. She represents the working-class perspective on [[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] — practical, unpretentious, and slightly suspicious of the gentlemen investigating.

## Character

Mrs. Cutts is practical and no-nonsense. She takes pride in her work ("a cup of tea that gentlemen like" [PN=049-071]) and speaks with the directness of someone who has seen life. Her brief exchanges with [[paul-harrison|Paul Harrison]] and the police offer moments of human warmth amid the grim investigation.

She is described washing the linoleum [PN=049-136] — a detail that places her firmly in her working role while also suggesting the cleaning-away of evidence or the attempt to return to normalcy after a death.
