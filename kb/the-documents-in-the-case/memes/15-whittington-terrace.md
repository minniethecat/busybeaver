# 15 Whittington Terrace

A boarding establishment in Bayswater, London, comprising at least two addresses — No. 15 and No. 15a. The building housed two of the central figures in the epistolary narrative: [[agatha-milsom|Agatha Milsom]] at No. 15, and [[john-munting|John Munting]] at No. 15a.

## As a Setting in the Correspondence

Whittington Terrace serves as the return address for a substantial portion of the letters in the case. Agatha Milsom wrote many of her observations about the Harrison household from No. 15, addressing them to [[olive-farebrother|Olive Farebrother]]. John Munting, occupying the neighboring flat at No. 15a, corresponded separately with [[elizabeth-drake|Elizabeth Drake]].

The proximity of the two addresses is significant: Agatha and John Munting were neighbors who could observe each other's comings and goings. This physical closeness adds a layer of dramatic irony to their parallel correspondences — each writing about matters the other might have witnessed firsthand.

## Location Context

Bayswater in the late 1920s was a respectable but not fashionable district of London, consistent with the circumstances of both residents.

The address appears throughout the letters dating from September 1928 through early 1929.

## Source References

The address appears throughout the correspondence, most notably in letters from [[agatha-milsom|Agatha Milsom]] (written from 15 Whittington Terrace) and [[john-munting|John Munting]] (written from 15a Whittington Terrace). The shared residence is established early in the correspondence and provides the domestic setting for key social interactions including the Christmas 1928 gathering and the mushroom dinner party.
