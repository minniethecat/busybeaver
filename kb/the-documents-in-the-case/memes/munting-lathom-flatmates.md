# Munting and Lathom as Flatmates

## Summary

[[john-munting|John Munting]] and [[harwood-lathom|Harwood Lathom]] shared the ground-floor maisonnette at 15a [[whittington-terrace|Whittington Terrace]] — the flat adjacent to the [[george-harrison|Harrison]] household. Their cohabitation, lasting from [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s arrival in December 1928 until [[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] in March 1929, created a friendship that made [[john-munting|Munting]]'s subsequent testimony against [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] both more credible and more painful.

## Context

[[john-munting|Munting]] describes the arrangement in detail: "I had tried to play up to [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] better" [PN=037-013] — acknowledging the effort required to live with such a forceful personality. [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]] "woke me up" [PN=037-022], coming to [[john-munting|Munting]]'s room late at night to discuss his affair with [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]]. These nocturnal confessions made [[john-munting|Munting]] an unwilling confidant.

The flat-sharing arrangement gave [[john-munting|Munting]] unique access to [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s character and activities. He observed [[harwood-lathom|Lathom]]'s study of mushrooms [PN=042-006], his chemical knowledge, and his growing entanglement with [[margaret-harrison|Margaret Harrison]]. When [[death-of-george-harrison|the murder]] occurred, [[john-munting|Munting]] was the person best positioned to connect the dots.
