# II

[054-001]Upstairs it was certainly more cheerful than the basement, it also smelt less of stopped sink. The restaurant was a long, low, well-lighted room, with a stand of aspidistras in the center. Here and there, in a pot tied up in pink paper, a fern was trying not to die; there were many little tables, and the one good-sized window was embellished with red cotton curtains.

[054-002]“Some day they will be silk,” thought [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] when he saw them, remembering [[mario-boselli|Mario]]’s words.

[054-003]“Ah!” exclaimed [[the-padrone|the Padrone]], jumping up from a table at which he had been drinking vermouth. “You are late as usual, accursedly late. I am sick of you and your lateness!”

[054-004]Mario’s eyes goggled: “I am sorry—” he faltered.

[054-005]“My apron delayed us,” piped Gian-Luca.

[054-006]The Padrone stared. “And who may you be? Ah, yes, I remember, the new *piccolo*.”

[054-007]“At what time would you wish me to arrive?” inquired Gian-Luca, assuming the air of a man.

[054-008]“Half-past nine and not a minute later,” he was told.

[054-009]“I will come,” said Gian-Luca calmly.

[054-010]“That is well,” growled the Padrone, rather taken aback, “that is well. Time is money at [[the-capo|the Capo]]. And now go and wash the glass in those doors; after that you must sweep out the restaurant. Come here you, [[schmidt|Schmidt]], and give him a baize apron and show him the buckets and brooms!” he bellowed. “*Corpo di Bacco!* Where is the fool? *Santa Madonna!* where is he?”
