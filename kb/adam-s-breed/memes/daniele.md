## Biography

Daniele is a young Italian waiter employed at the Doric restaurant in London. [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] considers him a competent and reliable member of the staff (134-004). His duties include preparing the spirit-lamps for the tables, pinching the wicks "firmly between finger and thumb, then spreading out their tips as though they were petals" to assist in the making of Crêpe Suzette (134-005). When an unfamiliar name came up among the clientele, [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] turned to Daniele for information about the diners, reflecting his role as a knowledgeable member of the floor staff (134-008).

During the preparations for [[ugo-doria|Ugo Doria]]'s visit, [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s physician had ordered him to rest at luncheon. [[franco-millo|Franco Millo]] suggested that Daniele take [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s place in the restaurant during the midday service (138-013). [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] accepted reluctantly, though he spent the morning giving Daniele exhaustive final directions and rousing the whole kitchen to a state of alertness (140-001). When [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] questioned him repeatedly about the luncheon bookings, Daniele confirmed he could manage the responsibility, though he was "much aggrieved" by [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s anxious questioning (141-005, 141-007).

> "I can surely manage, Signor Gian-Luca," Daniele replied, much aggrieved. (141-007)

As the luncheon guests began to arrive, Daniele was ready and smiling, a reassuring sight for the anxious [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] (142-001).

Unlike [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]], who had lost his appetite amid the stress of Doria's impending visit, Daniele "who was still young, enjoyed the fine fare" served at the Doric, exclaiming "e molto buono!" while [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] forced himself to eat and murmured "Buonissimo!" in reply, hoping Daniele would not notice his lack of enthusiasm (147-024, 147-025).

As [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s health and focus declined in the weeks that followed, his orders to the staff became increasingly vague, so that Daniele found himself having to ask [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] to repeat his instructions (160-001). When [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] decided to take a day away from the restaurant, he informed Daniele that he could manage quite well in his absence (160-014).

Daniele was present among the staff towards the end of [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s tenure at the Doric, alongside [[roberto|Roberto]], [[giovanni|Giovanni]], and several others (175-011). At the gathering where [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s departure was discussed, Daniele spoke in his defense, saying of [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s management: "he has always been perfectly just" (175-018).

## Role at the Doric

Daniele functioned as [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s informal deputy among the waiters, capable of managing service during important occasions. [[franco-millo|Franco Millo]] trusted Daniele enough to suggest he cover [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s duties when [[ugo-doria|Ugo Doria]] visited — a significant vote of confidence given the importance of Doria's custom to the restaurant (138-013).

Daniele's competence and youth represented both the continuity of the Doric's Italian staffing tradition and the generational succession that [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] himself had embodied when he first rose to headwaiter. The preparation rituals Daniele performed — tending the spirit-lamps, pinching the wicks for Crêpe Suzette — echoed the meticulous craft that [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] had once taken pride in (134-005).

Daniele's role also illuminated [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s own decline: the contrast between Daniele's youthful enjoyment of food and [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s forced eating dramatized the older waiter's loss of appetite for the life he had built (147-024).

## Relationships

- **[[[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca Boselli]]](gian-luca-boselli.md)**: Daniele's direct supervisor. [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] initially praised him as "a competent fellow" (134-004) but later snapped at him sharply as his own health deteriorated (135-005). Their relationship evolved from mentorship to role-reversal as Daniele increasingly had to manage [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s lapses.
- **[[[franco-millo|Franco Millo]]](franco-millo.md)**: [[the-doric|The Doric]]'s director who trusted Daniele to take over [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s responsibilities during Doria's visit.
- **[[[roberto|Roberto]]](roberto.md)** and **[[[giovanni|Giovanni]]](giovanni.md)**: Fellow waiters who worked alongside Daniele at the Doric.
