# VI

[049-001][[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] still went to the Free Library, and this surprised the Librarian. “I should think you’d have no time for reading,” he remarked, “now that you’re such a man of business. I should think that you must feel rather bored with books, considering there’s no money in them—”

[049-002]“What has that got to do with it?” inquired Gian-Luca, staring at him in surprise; for at this time he was very much a Latin; he kept two distinct Gian-Lucas, one for beauty, one for business, and so far they had never collided.

[049-003]“I can’t make you out,” said the puzzled Librarian; “it’s so queer that you like books at all.”

[049-004]“[[rocca|Rocca]] likes music,” Gian-Luca told him, “he goes to the gallery at Covent Garden; [[signora-rocca|Rocca]] is mad about opera!”

[049-005]“And who is Rocca?” inquired the Librarian. “He sounds very hard, somehow.”

[049-006]“Rocca’s our butcher, he used to be a soldier. When he was a little boy he knew Garibaldi,” said Gian-Luca, defending Rocca.

[049-007]“Ah, well,” smiled the Librarian, “it’s a very large world, there must surely be room for us all—Come and see me some day, I live in Putney; perhaps you could come to tea?”

[049-008]“It would have to be a Sunday,” Gian-Luca told him gravely. “I am very busy all the week—”

[049-009]“That will do; you can come next Sunday at four—I will show you my own special books. What would you like for tea—Chelsea buns? Or do you prefer Swiss roll?”

[049-010]“I prefer Swiss roll, apricot,” said Gian-Luca.

[049-011]“I will tell my wife,” promised the Librarian.
