# II

[036-001]They walked as far as Piccadilly Circus, [[mario-boselli|Mario]] carrying the hamper. At the Circus they climbed to the top of a bus bound for Hammersmith Broadway.

[036-002]“Be careful! Be careful!” shrieked [[rosa-varese|Rosa]] to [[geppe|Geppe]], who was trying to swing himself up by the handrail.

[036-003]“He will hit me in the face with his heels,” fussed [[berta|Berta]]; “I wish you would make him behave!”

[036-004]Mario was stopped at the bottom of the steps by a kindly but firm conductor. “You must leave that ’amper with me,” said the conductor; “no ’ampers allowed on top.”

[036-005]“Not so,” retorted Mario; “I will take him on my lap—I will nurse him—he cannot be left!”

[036-006]The conductor pulled the cord and the bus moved off slowly behind the stout, sweating horses.

[036-007]“I will not leave him!” cried Mario, still clutching his hamper; “you see, I take him on my lap.”

[036-008]“Oh, all right.” The conductor stood aside for him to pass, and Mario struggled up the steps.

[036-009]The bus was very full—Geppe sat on top of Rosa. “Here we are!” shrilled Berta from her seat beside [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]. “You will have to go over there and sit beside Mamma.” Mario squeezed in with the hamper on his knees; it grew heavier every moment. “*Dio!*” he groaned, “what is living in this hamper? Is it perchance a giant?”

[036-010]“You take Geppe and give it to me,” suggested Rosa; “I will now hold it for a little.”

[036-011]Their burdens exchanged, they began to mop their faces—it was growing exceedingly hot.

[036-012]At Hammersmith Broadway the crowd was enormous, and most of it was waiting for the bus to Kew Gardens.

[036-013]“You had better carry Geppe,” said Rosa to Mario; “I fear that he may get lost.”

[036-014]Geppe objected, beginning to cry: “I want to walk with Gian-Luca!”

[036-015]As his father picked him up, he beat with his heels: “Put me down, put me down, I tell you!”

[036-016]Rosa dragged Berta along by the hand, while Gian-Luca struggled with the hamper.

[036-017]“No more room on top!” yelled the harassed conductor. “Inside only, please.”

[036-018]By dint of superhuman exertion, they managed at last to get in.

[036-019]“Now then, young ’un, don’t gouge out my eyes with that basket!” protested a voice to Gian-Luca.

[036-020]There were several other children on the knees of their parents, all fretful and on the verge of tears.

[036-021]“It’s this terrible ’eat,” said a mother to Rosa; “it do try ’em, don’t it, the ’eat?”

[036-022]The smell in the bus suggested that it did—the sun was blazing through the windows.

[036-023]“Phew! Ain’t it awful!” a lady complained, clinking her black jet bugles.

[036-024]Berta sat scratching her nettle-rash, and Geppe’s nose required attention. Gian-Luca peered over the top of the hamper, his collar was feeling rather tight.

[036-025]“May I undo my collar?” he whispered to Rosa.

[036-026]But Rosa shook her head: “No, no, *caro*, you look so nice as you are; you cannot undo your collar.”

[036-027]He subsided behind the hamper again, so as not to see Berta who was making him itch. He wished that Mario would observe Geppe’s nose—it really did require blowing. Rosa’s fringe had begun to come out of curl—Gian-Luca noticed that too—one long, black strand was gradually uncoiling; very soon it would be in her eye. Her hat—last summer’s—looked rather jaded, the roses no longer very red; however, by contrast, Berta’s headgear was a triumph; a yellow poke bonnet trimmed with cornflowers and daisies and tied under the chin with white ribbon. Berta’s hair stuck out in a bush behind; her eyes stared, inquisitive and greedy. “I am hungry,” she was saying; “how long does it take?”

[036-028]“We are nearly there,” consoled Rosa.
