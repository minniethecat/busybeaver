## Overview

Schmidt is a Swiss waiter at [[the-capo|the Capo]] di Monte restaurant who serves as [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca Boselli]]'s first workplace colleague. A temperamental German-speaker who "spoke no Italian" (053-028), Schmidt is at once comical, competitive, and unexpectedly warm. His interactions with [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] provide both friction and camaraderie in the novel's early chapters, illustrating the polyglot, multi-ethnic character of London's immigrant restaurant workforce.

## Biography

Schmidt was introduced by [[mario-boselli|Mario]] on [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s first tour of [[the-capo|the Capo]] di Monte: "There is yet another waiter. His name is Schmidt, and moreover he is Swiss, a thing which you could never mistake" (053-027). His greeting — "'Good Morgen,' said Schmidt" — immediately established his linguistic and cultural distance from the Italian staff (053-028). He grinned easily, responding to warnings about the pace of service with: "'Do not hurry? Vell, I prefer to serve at zese tables, and I do not wish to spoil zem'" (053-030).

Schmidt's working style was brisk and demanding. He ordered his colleagues about — "'You hurry und bring me at once zis coffee und fasti'" (056-012) — and could be quick to anger, his "face was now red with temper" (055-006) during altercations. Yet he was also capable and efficient: unlike [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s early clumsiness, "Schmidt, on the other hand, could carry well nigh as many plates as [[mario-boselli|Mario]]" (056-002). His tables were zones of order: "There was peace at Schmidt's tables, comparative peace" (056-004).

His relationship with [[mario-boselli|Mario Boselli]] was marked by professional competition and verbal sparring: "'Vot, never you be ready?' he heard Schmidt grumbling at [[mario-boselli|Mario]]" (055-002). Their most notable conflict occurred when Schmidt accused [[mario-boselli|Mario]] of spitting on plates: "'I know how I set the table!' Schmidt laughed. 'You not spit on the plates?'" (055-003), an incident that escalated until "Schmidt went back to the pantry, muttering" (055-008).

As [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] settled into the restaurant, Schmidt grew friendlier. "At about this time Schmidt grew very friendly to [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]" (062-013), and he confided in [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] about his admiration for [[gemma|the Padrona]]: "'You admire our [[gemma|Padrona]]?' — 'Ja, I do,' said Schmidt simply" (062-014). He also mused on the nature of work: "'Jawohl,' agreed Schmidt, 'one must begin. I am nur here to make a start'" (057-004). When [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] struggled with heavy loads, it was Schmidt who shared the burden: he "shared them with Schmidt and the now sulky [[mario-boselli|Mario]]" (056-018).

## Relationships

With **[[mario-boselli|Mario Boselli]]**, Schmidt shared a combative but functional working relationship. They bickered over duties and plates, but their mutual dependence in [[the-capo|the Capo]] di Monte's small team created a grudging camaraderie.

With **[[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca Boselli]]**, Schmidt became an unlikely friend. His growing warmth toward [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] — sharing confidences and practical burdens — softened his initially brusque impression. Schmidt's openness about his admiration for [[gemma|the Padrona]] foreshadowed the romantic entanglements that would later reshape [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s life.

With **[[the-padrone|Il Padrone]]**, Schmidt operated as an employee under the same paternalistic authority as the Italian waiters, though his non-Italian background may have given him a slightly different position in the restaurant's hierarchy.

## Significance

Schmidt represents the cosmopolitan character of London's immigrant service economy — a Swiss German-speaker working in an Italian restaurant in Soho, navigating a workplace defined by multiple languages, temperaments, and national loyalties. His rough German-accented English and volatile moods provide moments of comic relief while also illustrating the genuine frictions of polyglot immigrant labour. Schmidt's trajectory from gruff competitor to friendly confidant mirrors [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s own initiation into the restaurant world, where initial hostility often masks underlying solidarity among workers who share the same precarious circumstances.
