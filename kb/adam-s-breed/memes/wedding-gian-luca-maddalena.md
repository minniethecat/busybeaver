## Summary

[[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca Boselli]] and [[maddalena-boselli|Maddalena]] are married in July at [[st-peters-italian-church|St. Peter's Italian Church]] in [[hatton-garden|Hatton Garden]] [093-001]. The wedding is arranged through the church [091-001] with [[ottavia|Aunt Ottavia]]'s enthusiastic involvement [091-003]. [[maddalena-boselli|Maddalena]] appears "pale and lovely in her simple wedding dress" [093-002], and [[teresa-boselli|Teresa]] prepares a "magnificent" wedding breakfast at the family home [094-001]. The event cements [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s adult commitments — to [[maddalena-boselli|Maddalena]], to the community, and to the immigrant Catholic identity that St. Peter's embodies — even as the seeds of his later spiritual crisis are already present.

## Detailed Account

### Arrangements

[[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] goes "down to the Italian church — St. Peter's in [[hatton-garden|Hatton Garden]]" [091-001] to arrange the marriage. The specificity of the church's national identity — "the Italian church" — underscores that this is not merely a religious ceremony but an assertion of communal belonging. [[ottavia|Aunt Ottavia]] becomes "very voluble" about the marriage being performed at St. Peter's [091-003], her enthusiasm reflecting the communal investment in the union.

### The Ceremony

The wedding takes place in July at St. Peter's, "that quaint little lump of Italy dumped down in [[hatton-garden|Hatton Garden]]" [093-001]. [[maddalena-boselli|Maddalena]], "pale and lovely in her simple wedding dress, stood serene and undisturbed" [093-002] — her composure contrasting with the ornate Catholic setting of "frescoes blurred and peeling" [093-001]. The juxtaposition of [[maddalena-boselli|Maddalena]]'s simplicity with the church's faded grandeur captures the novel's ambivalent relationship to immigrant religious identity: sacred yet worn, beautiful yet diminished by transplantation.

### The Wedding Breakfast

[[teresa-boselli|Teresa]] prepares the wedding breakfast at home, a "magnificent repast" [094-001] that channels her formidable energy into hospitality. The domestic celebration — [[food-and-cuisine|Italian food]], family gathered, [[teresa-boselli|Teresa]]'s authority over the table — complements the ecclesiastical ceremony: church and home together seal the marriage within the immigrant community's twin institutions.
