# Themes of Adam's Breed

Radclyffe Hall's *Adam's Breed* (1926) explores the collision of faith and modernity, the immigrant's divided self, the sacrificial logic of service, and the restaurant as a theatre of class and culture. This page maps the novel's major thematic currents through its concept pages.

## About This Page

Each concept page traces a single thematic thread across the novel's chapters and characters — from Catholic sacramental theology to the semiotics of macaroni. Together they form a map of the ideas that give *Adam's Breed* its intellectual weight.
