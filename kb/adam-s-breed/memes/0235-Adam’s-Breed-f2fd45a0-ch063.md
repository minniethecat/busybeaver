# III

[061-001]In the course of a week came the beautiful [[the-padrona|Padrona]] to pay her respects to [[teresa-boselli|Teresa]]. Teresa surveyed her with critical eyes, not at all allured by her beauty.

[061-002]“She is Venetian, she is false, she is stupid,” said Teresa; “and moreover she has a wanton eye on [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]].”

[061-003]“*Ma Dio!*” gasped [[fabio-boselli|Fabio]]; “he is yet but a child.”

[061-004]“He will not remain always a child,” she reminded.

[061-005]“But what of the husband? I am certain he is firm, I am sure he is a lion among men.”

[061-006]“When the lion lies down with the lamb,” smiled Teresa, “I have heard that he loses his strength.”

[061-007]Fabio groaned loudly. “Madonna!” he complained; “and we have but now completed that large order.”

[061-008]“That is why,” said Teresa. “Her eye is on Gian-Luca, so she sends her lion here to spend money.”

[061-009]“What must be done?” inquired Fabio weakly, beginning to fidget with the things about the room.

[061-010]“Nothing,” said Teresa. “If we take him away, we do but make him think the more.”

[061-011]“But supposing—”

[061-012]“We will not suppose,” she said firmly. “He is handsome and young, but he is also ambitious; he will not stay long at [[the-capo|the Capo]] di Monte. Meanwhile he will probably meet some young girls, one of whom he can marry later on—there is [[berta|Berta]], for instance; it is true she is plain, still, she will probably improve.”

[061-013]“Alas!” exclaimed Fabio, wringing his hands. “What a terrible danger is youth! When I think of our [[olga-boselli|Olga]]—”

[061-014]But Teresa’s face stopped him.

[061-015]“I wish you would weigh up those hams,” she said quickly; “I feel sure they are under weight again.”
