# II

[032-001]It was spring, and even in [[old-compton-street|Old Compton Street]] the spring is apt to be exciting. [[nerone|Nerone]]’s new skylark sang and jumped and sang. Nerone had made a felt top for its cage, the better to shut out the sky.

[032-002]“*Ma guarda!* Is he not joyous?” said Nerone. “It appears that he too feels the spring.”

[032-003]The skylark felt the spring, [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] felt the spring, and [[mario-boselli|Mario]] felt the spring and grew restless. Gian-Luca looked at his motto every morning: “I have got myself,” and the words, lit up by sunshine, seemed to glow with a new and exciting meaning, until, what with youth and the spring in his bones, he almost forgot his troubles.

[032-004]Mario, perspiring at [[the-capo|the Capo]] di Monte, broke a dish, for which he had to pay. He grew less subtle in dealing with the clients; when he cleared their tables now he would whisk with his napkin until the crumbs flew into their laps.

[032-005]“*Sapristi!*”—[[the-padrone|the Padrone]] was proud of his French—“*Sapristi!* You imbecile, what are you doing?”

[032-006]And Mario would laugh—yes, actually laugh, right in the teeth of the Padrone!

[032-007]In the mornings, when he shaved, he did so with vigor, cutting his chin in the process. The blood might drip on to the rim of his collar, but Mario did not care; he would go to the washstand and rub off the stain with an old flannel washrag—after which his collar would look limp.

[032-008]“I will not have a clean one, it costs so much, the washing,” he would say. “We must save, we must put by the pennies for when we can go into the country.”

[032-009][[berta|Berta]] got nettle-rash and cried a good deal; she got it every spring and autumn.

[032-010]“[[geppe|Geppe]] has given me *pidocchi*!” she insisted, whenever [[rosa-varese|Rosa]] would listen.

[032-011]Geppe pretended to grope in his head and to throw the results at Berta.

[032-012]“Oh! Oh!” shrieked Berta in a panic of fear. “Oh! Oh! He is throwing me *pidocchi*!”

[032-013]Rosa scrubbed up the house, and forgetting it was London, she put the bedding out in the sun. When she brought it in it was covered with smuts.

[032-014]“Accursed country!” said Nerone, and he swore: “We live in an accursed country!”

[032-015][[teresa-boselli|Teresa]] was constantly busy in the cash-desk, the money went jingling through her fingers. Whenever a sunbeam touched a golden coin she smiled and stroked it gently with her thumb. For the habit of thrift had persisted in Teresa; now she saved for the pleasure of saving. Every Saturday morning she went off to the bank with a little bag hidden in her cloak.

[032-016]As for [[fabio-boselli|Fabio]], his halo looked wilder than ever, but the pains in his back had disappeared. He served in the shop without coat, or even waistcoat, his shirt bulging out between his braces.

[032-017]“God be praised,” smiled Fabio, well content with the gastronomical effects of the fine weather. “God be praised; we are selling more this spring than we have done for many years past.”

[032-018]Even [[rocca|Rocca]] grew poetical, a mood which found expression in his garnishing and laying out of meat. [[signora-rocca|Rocca]] bought a calf’s head to which he pinned rosettes, then he thrust a spring of parsley in its mouth.

[032-019]The Signora Rocca said very many prayers; it was May, the month of Our Lady.

[032-020]“Oh loving, oh clement, oh sweet Virgin Mary,” she murmured, and then, bethinking her of Michael the Archangel, and, via him, of Satan: “May God rebuke him—and do thou, Prince of all the heavenly host, by the power of God, thrust down into hell Satan and all wicked spirits.”
