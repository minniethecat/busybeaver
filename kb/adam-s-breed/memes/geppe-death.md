## Summary

[[geppe|Geppe]], the "turbulent creature" [026-004] and son of [[rosa-varese|Rosa]] and [[mario-boselli|Mario]], is conscripted into an Italian infantry regiment during World War I [098-001] and killed in combat. His death — communicated indirectly through the news of comrades' fates [116-005] — represents the war's most intimate wound to the immigrant community. As [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]'s childhood companion and a figure of vigorous, untroubled Italian identity, [[geppe|Geppe]]'s loss removes a vital presence and deepens the novel's meditation on sacrifice, belonging, and the cost of being claimed by a homeland one has never truly known.

## Detailed Account

### The Conscripted Youth

[[geppe|Geppe]] is introduced as a "turbulent creature of six" [026-004], full of appetite and life — "greedy but he took without" guile [028-007]. His vitality makes him a natural foil to the more introspective [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]]: where [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] questions, [[geppe|Geppe]] acts; where [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] doubts, [[geppe|Geppe]] simply is Italian. [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] envies this: "I wish to be as [[geppe|Geppe]] is — Italian" [027-058].

### The Call to War

[[geppe|Geppe]]'s conscription into "an infantry regiment" [098-001] transforms him from exuberant child into soldier. The narrator's comment — "Poor [[geppe|Geppe]], the life would be" hard [098-001] — carries the weight of foreknowledge. [[geppe|Geppe]] must learn to "dig trenches" [098-001], a skill that reduces his vitality to a grim utility. He receives the order to "rejoin his regiment at once" [111-001] and departs, his fate sealed in a single line.

### The News Arrives

[[geppe|Geppe]]'s death is not depicted directly but arrives through the report of [[riccardo|Riccardo]]'s fate: "the news that [[riccardo|Riccardo]] had been killed" [116-005]. The indirection is telling — in war, news travels circuitously through immigrant networks, and [[geppe|Geppe]]'s death is one loss among many. For [[rosa-varese|Rosa]] and [[mario-boselli|Mario]], the loss of their son is catastrophic; for [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]], the death of the companion he wished to emulate removes a model of untroubled identity, intensifying his own sense of dislocation.

### Community Impact

[[geppe|Geppe]]'s death crystallises the war's meaning for the immigrant community: Italy, the mother country that called them to fight, has taken their children. The sacrifice asked of immigrants — to die for a homeland they left — is the novel's most searing critique of the claims that nations make on those who cross their borders.
