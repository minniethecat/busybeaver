# VII

[094-001]The service being over, [[francesco-millo|Millo]] went back to the Doric, [[the-padrone|the Padrone]] and [[the-padrona|Padrona]] to [[the-capo|the Capo]]. [[the-librarian|The Librarian]] and his wife had gently disappeared—but the rest of them all hurried off to [[fabio-boselli|Fabio]]’s shop, where [[teresa-boselli|Teresa]] had prepared the wedding breakfast. A magnificent repast it was, quite worthy of the house, of the celebrated Casa Boselli. There was much Asti Spumante, and it went to [[mario-boselli|Mario]]’s head—he made reminiscent love to patient [[rosa-varese|Rosa]]. [[rocca|Rocca]] drank so many toasts to the army and the bride that [[signora-rocca|Signora Rocca]] had to interfere; [[aunt-ottavia|Aunt Ottavia]], very merry, as she always was with men, asked [[nerone|Nerone]] how he got his wooden leg. But Nerone was invariably sulky in his cups, and, moreover, he was thinking of that clock, so instead of being gallant to the little [[ottavia|Aunt Ottavia]], he stuffed his mouth with food and would not tell her.

[094-002]They feasted, they made speeches, they sang patriotic songs: “*Avanti Bersagliere!*” caroled Rocca with emotion, “*Avanti-vanti-vanti-Bersagliere!*” Only the bride and bridegroom were shy and rather silent, not doing proper justice to their food. And presently the time came when the bride must change her dress, while the bridegroom waited for her in the hall. Young [[geppe|Geppe]] fetched a taxi, into which the victims hurried amid a shower of rice and confetti. They would go straight home to Millman Street; there would be no honeymoon, for [[gian-luca-boselli|Gian-Luca]] had not wished to worry [[franco-millo|Millo]].

[094-003]As they drove he leant towards her: “You do not mind, my [[maddalena-boselli|Maddalena]]? You do not mind about our honeymoon?”

[094-004]She was silent for a moment and her eyes were rather wistful, but she said: “I am contented. Let it be just as you wish—so long as you are happy, *amore*—”
