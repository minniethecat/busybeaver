[001-001] “ARE you for a walk,” said [[montraville|Montraville]] to his companion, as they arose

[001-002] from table; “are you for a walk? or shall we order the chaise and

[001-003] proceed to [[portsmouth|Portsmouth]]?” [[belcour|Belcour]] preferred the former; and they sauntered

[001-004] out to view the town, and to make remarks on the inhabitants, as they

[001-005] returned from church.

[001-006] Montraville was a Lieutenant in the army: Belcour was his brother

[001-007] officer: they had been to take leave of their [[friendship|friends]] previous to their

[001-008] departure for [[america|America]], and were now returning to Portsmouth, where the

[001-009] troops waited orders for embarkation. They had stopped at [[chichester|Chichester]]

[001-010] to dine; and knowing they had sufficient time to reach the place of

[001-011] destination before dark, and yet allow them a walk, had resolved, it

[001-012] being Sunday afternoon, to take a survey of the Chichester ladies as

[001-013] they returned from their devotions.

[001-014] They had gratified their curiosity, and were preparing to return to the

[001-015] inn without honouring any of the belles with particular notice, when

[001-016] [[madame-du-pont|Madame Du Pont]], at the head of her school, descended from the church.

[001-017] Such an assemblage of youth and innocence naturally attracted the [[youth|young]]

[001-018] soldiers: they stopped; and, as the little cavalcade passed, almost

[001-019] involuntarily pulled off their hats. A tall, elegant girl looked at

[001-020] Montraville and blushed: he instantly recollected the features of

[001-021] [[charlotte-temple|Charlotte Temple]], whom he had once seen and danced with at a ball at

[001-022] Portsmouth. At that time he thought on her only as a very lovely child,

[001-023] she being then only thirteen; but the improvement two years had made in

[001-024] her person, and the blush of recollection which suffused her cheeks as

[001-025] she passed, awakened in his bosom new and pleasing ideas. Vanity led him

[001-026] to think that pleasure at again beholding him might have occasioned the

[001-027] emotion he had witnessed, and the same vanity led him to wish to see her

[001-028] again.

[001-029] “She is the sweetest girl in the world,” said he, as he entered the inn.

[001-030] Belcour stared. “Did you not notice her?” continued Montraville: “she

[001-031] had on a blue bonnet, and with a pair of lovely eyes of the same colour,

[001-032] has contrived to make me feel devilish odd about the heart.”

[001-033] “Pho,” said Belcour, “a musket ball from our friends, the Americans, may

[001-034] in less than two months make you feel worse.”

[001-035] “I never think of the future,” replied Montraville; “but am determined

[001-036] to make the most of the present, and would willingly compound with any

[001-037] kind Familiar who would inform me who the girl is, and how I might be

[001-038] likely to obtain an interview.”

[001-039] But no kind Familiar at that time appearing, and the chaise which they

[001-040] had ordered, driving up to the door, Montraville and his companion were

[001-041] obliged to take leave of Chichester and its fair inhabitant, and proceed

[001-042] on their journey.

[001-043] But Charlotte had made too great an impression on his mind to be easily

[001-044] eradicated: having therefore spent three whole days in thinking on her

[001-045] and in endeavouring to form some plan for seeing her, he determined

[001-046] to set off for Chichester, and [[trust]] to chance either to favour or

[001-047] frustrate his designs. Arriving at the verge of the town, he dismounted,

[001-048] and sending the servant forward with the horses, proceeded toward the

[001-049] place, where, in the midst of an extensive pleasure ground, stood the

[001-050] mansion which contained the lovely Charlotte [[mr-temple|Temple]]. Montraville leaned

[001-051] on a broken gate, and looked earnestly at the house. The wall which

[001-052] surrounded it was high, and perhaps the Argus's who guarded the

[001-053] Hesperian fruit within, were more watchful than those famed of old.

[001-054] “'Tis a romantic attempt,” said he; “and should I even succeed in seeing

[001-055] and conversing with her, it can be productive of no good: I must of

[001-056] necessity leave [[england|England]] in a few days, and probably may never return;

[001-057] why then should I endeavour to engage the affections of this lovely

[001-058] girl, only to leave her a prey to a thousand inquietudes, of which at

[001-059] present she has no idea? I will return to Portsmouth and think no more

[001-060] about her.”

[001-061] The evening now was closed; a serene stillness reigned; and the

[001-062] chaste Queen of Night with her silver crescent faintly illuminated the

[001-063] hemisphere. The mind of Montraville was hushed into composure by the

[001-064] serenity of the surrounding objects. “I will think on her no more,” said

[001-065] he, and turned with an intention to leave the place; but as he turned,

[001-066] he saw the gate which led to the pleasure grounds open, and two women

[001-067] come out, who walked arm-in-arm across the field.

[001-068] “I will at least see who these are,” said he. He overtook them, and

[001-069] giving them the compliments of the evening, begged leave to see them

[001-070] into the more frequented parts of the town: but how was he delighted,

[001-071] when, waiting for an answer, he discovered, under the concealment of a

[001-072] large bonnet, the face of Charlotte Temple.

[001-073] He soon found means to ingratiate himself with her companion, who was a

[001-074] French teacher at the school, and, at parting, slipped a letter he had

[001-075] purposely written, into Charlotte's hand, and five guineas into that of

[001-076] Mademoiselle, who promised she would endeavour to bring her young charge

[001-077] into the field again the next evening.
