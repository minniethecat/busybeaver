[022-001] WHEN [[charlotte-temple|Charlotte]] got home she endeavoured to collect her thoughts, and

[022-002] took up a pen in order to address those dear parents, whom, spite of her

[022-003] errors, she still loved with the utmost tenderness, but vain was every

[022-004] effort to write with the least coherence; her tears fell so fast

[022-005] they almost blinded her; and as she proceeded to describe her unhappy

[022-006] situation, she became so agitated that she was obliged to give over the

[022-007] attempt and retire to bed, where, overcome with the fatigue her mind had

[022-008] undergone, she fell into a slumber which greatly refreshed her, and she

[022-009] arose in the morning with spirits more adequate to the painful task she

[022-010] had to perform, and, after several attempts, at length concluded the

[022-011] following letter to her mother--

[022-012] TO MRS. TEMPLE. NEW-YORK.

[022-013] “Will my once kind, my ever [[love|beloved]] mother, deign to receive a letter

[022-014] from her [[guilt|guilty]], but repentant child? or has she, justly incensed at my

[022-015] [[gratitude|ingratitude]], driven the unhappy Charlotte from her remembrance? Alas!

[022-016] thou much injured mother! shouldst thou even disown me, I dare not

[022-017] complain, because I know I have deserved it: but yet, believe me, guilty

[022-018] as I am, and cruelly as I have disappointed the [[hope|hopes]] of the fondest

[022-019] parents, that ever girl had, even in the moment when, forgetful of my

[022-020] duty, I fled from you and happiness, even then I loved you most, and my

[022-021] heart bled at the thought of what you would [[suffering|suffer]]. Oh! never, never!

[022-022] whilst I have existence, will the agony of that moment be erased from my

[022-023] memory. It seemed like the separation of soul and body. What can I plead

[022-024] in excuse for my conduct? alas! nothing! That I loved my [[seduction|seducer]] is

[022-025] but too true! yet powerful as that [[love|passion]] is when operating in a

[022-026] [[youth|young]] heart glowing with sensibility, it never would have conquered my

[022-027] affection to you, my beloved parents, had I not been encouraged, nay,

[022-028] urged to take the fatally imprudent step, by one of my own sex, who,

[022-029] under the mask of friendship, drew me on to ruin. Yet think not your

[022-030] Charlotte was so lost as to voluntarily rush into a life of infamy; no,

[022-031] my dear mother, deceived by the specious appearance of my betrayer, and

[022-032] every suspicion lulled asleep by the most solemn promises of marriage,

[022-033] I thought not those promises would so easily be forgotten. I never once

[022-034] reflected that the man who could stoop to seduction, would not hesitate

[022-035] to forsake the wretched object of his passion, whenever his capricious

[022-036] heart grew weary of her tenderness. When we arrived at this place, I

[022-037] vainly expected him to fulfil his engagements, but was at last fatally

[022-038] convinced he had never intended to make me his wife, or if he had once

[022-039] thought of it, his mind was now altered. I scorned to claim from his

[022-040] humanity what I could not obtain from his love: I was conscious of

[022-041] having forfeited the only gem that could render me respectable in the

[022-042] eye of the world. I locked my sorrows in my own bosom, and bore my

[022-043] injuries in silence. But how shall I proceed? This man, this cruel

[022-044] [[montraville|Montraville]], for whom I sacrificed honour, happiness, and the love of my

[022-045] [[friendship|friends]], no longer looks on me with affection, but scorns the credulous

[022-046] girl whom his art has made miserable. Could you see me, my dear parents,

[022-047] without society, without friends, stung with [[repentance|remorse]], and (I feel the

[022-048] burning blush of shame die my cheeks while I write it) tortured with the

[022-049] pangs of disappointed love; cut to the soul by the indifference of him,

[022-050] who, having deprived me of every other comfort, no longer thinks it

[022-051] worth his while to sooth the heart where he has planted the thorn of

[022-052] never-ceasing regret. My daily employment is to think of you and weep,

[022-053] to pray for your happiness and deplore my own folly: my nights are

[022-054] scarce more [[happiness|happy]], for if by chance I close my weary eyes, and hope

[022-055] some small forgetfulness of [[melancholy|sorrow]], some little time to pass in sweet

[022-056] oblivion, fancy, still waking, wafts me home to you: I see your beloved

[022-057] forms, I kneel and hear the blessed words of peace and [[forgiveness|pardon]]. Extatic

[022-058] joy pervades my soul; I reach my arms to catch your dear embraces; the

[022-059] motion chases the illusive dream; I wake to real [[distress|misery]]. At other times

[022-060] I see my father angry and frowning, point to horrid caves, where, on the

[022-061] cold damp ground, in the agonies of death, I see my dear mother and my

[022-062] revered grand-father. I strive to raise you; you push me from you, and

[022-063] shrieking cry--'Charlotte, thou hast murdered me!' Horror and despair

[022-064] tear every tortured nerve; I start, and leave my restless bed, weary and

[022-065] unrefreshed.

[022-066] “Shocking as these reflexions are, I have yet one more dreadful than the

[022-067] rest. Mother, my dear mother! do not let me quite break your heart when

[022-068] I tell you, in a few months I shall bring into the world an innocent

[022-069] witness of my guilt. Oh my bleeding heart, I shall bring a poor little

[022-070] helpless creature, heir to infamy and shame.

[022-071] “This alone has urged me once more to address you, to interest you in

[022-072] behalf of this poor unborn, and beg you to extend your protection to the

[022-073] child of your lost Charlotte; for my own part I have wrote so often, so

[022-074] frequently have pleaded for forgiveness, and entreated to be received

[022-075] once more beneath the paternal roof, that having received no answer, not

[022-076] even one line, I much fear you have cast me from you for ever.

[022-077] “But sure you cannot refuse to protect my innocent infant: it partakes

[022-078] not of its mother's guilt. Oh my father, oh beloved mother, now do I

[022-079] feel the [[grief|anguish]] I inflicted on your hearts recoiling with double force

[022-080] upon my own.

[022-081] “If my child should be a girl (which heaven forbid) tell her the unhappy

[022-082] fate of her mother, and teach her to avoid my errors; if a boy, teach

[022-083] him to lament my miseries, but tell him not who inflicted them, lest in

[022-084] wishing to revenge his mother's injuries, he should wound the peace of

[022-085] his father.

[022-086] “And now, dear friends of my soul, kind guardians of my infancy,

[022-087] farewell. I feel I never more must hope to see you; the anguish of my

[022-088] heart strikes at the strings of life, and in a short time I shall be

[022-089] at rest. Oh could I but receive your blessing and forgiveness before I

[022-090] died, it would smooth my passage to the peaceful grave, and be a blessed

[022-091] foretaste of a happy eternity. I beseech you, curse me not, my adored

[022-092] parents, but let a tear of [[compassion|pity]] and pardon [[ruin|fall]] to the memory of your

[022-093] lost

[022-094] “CHARLOTTE.”
