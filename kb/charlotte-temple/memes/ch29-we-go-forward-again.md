[029-001] THE strength of [[charlotte-temple|Charlotte]]'s constitution combatted against her disorder,

[029-002] and she began slowly to recover, though she still laboured under a

[029-003] violent depression of spirits: how must that depression be encreased,

[029-004] when, upon examining her little store, she found herself reduced to

[029-005] one solitary guinea, and that during her illness the attendance of an

[029-006] apothecary and nurse, together with many other unavoidable expences,

[029-007] had involved her in debt, from which she saw no method of extricating

[029-008] herself. As to the faint hope which she had entertained of hearing from

[029-009] and being relieved by her parents; it now entirely forsook her, for

[029-010] it was above four months since her letter was dispatched, and she had

[029-011] received no answer: she therefore imagined that her conduct had either

[029-012] entirely alienated their [[love|affection]] from her, or broken their hearts, and

[029-013] she must never more hope to receive their blessing.

## Section 16

[029-014] Never did any human being wish for death with greater fervency or

[029-015] with juster cause; yet she had too just a [[reason|sense]] of the [[duty|duties]] of the

[029-016] Christian religion to attempt to put a period to her own existence. “I

[029-017] have but to be patient a little longer,” she would cry, “and nature,

[029-018] fatigued and fainting, will throw off this heavy load of [[death|mortality]], and

[029-019] I shall be released from all my [[suffering|sufferings]].”

[029-020] It was one cold stormy day in the latter end of December, as Charlotte

[029-021] sat by a handful of fire, the low state of her finances not allowing her

[029-022] to replenish her stock of fuel, and prudence teaching her to be careful

[029-023] of what she had, when she was surprised by the entrance of a farmer's

[029-024] wife, who, without much ceremony, seated herself, and began this curious

[029-025] harangue.

[029-026] “I'm come to see if as how you can pay your rent, because as how we hear

[029-027] Captain Montable is gone away, and it's fifty to one if he b'ant killed

[029-028] afore he comes back again; an then, Miss, or Ma'am, or whatever you may

[029-029] be, as I was saying to my husband, where are we to look for our money.”

[029-030] This was a stroke altogether unexpected by Charlotte: she knew so little

[029-031] of the ways of the world that she had never bestowed a thought on the

[029-032] payment for the rent of the house; she knew indeed that she owed a

[029-033] good deal, but this was never reckoned among the others: she was

[029-034] thunder-struck; she hardly knew what answer to make, yet it was

[029-035] absolutely necessary that she should say something; and judging of the

[029-036] [[kindness|gentleness]] of every female disposition by her own, she thought the best

[029-037] way to interest the woman in her favour would be to tell her candidly to

[029-038] what a situation she was reduced, and how little probability there was

[029-039] of her ever paying any body.

[029-040] Alas poor Charlotte, how confined was her knowledge of [[nature|human nature]], or

[029-041] she would have been convinced that the only way to insure the friendship

[029-042] and assistance of your surrounding acquaintance is to convince them you

[029-043] do not require it, for when once the petrifying aspect of distress and

[029-044] [[poverty|penury]] appear, whose qualities, like Medusa's head, can change to stone

[029-045] all that look upon it; when once this Gorgon claims acquaintance with

[029-046] us, the phantom of friendship, that before courted our notice, will

[029-047] vanish into unsubstantial air, and the whole world before us appear a

[029-048] barren waste. Pardon me, ye dear spirits of [[charity|benevolence]], whose benign

[029-049] smiles and cheerful-giving hand have strewed sweet flowers on many a

[029-050] thorny path through which my wayward fate forced me to pass; think not,

[029-051] that, in condemning the unfeeling texture of the human heart, I forget

[029-052] the spring from whence flow an the comforts I enjoy: oh no! I look up

[029-053] to you as to bright constellations, gathering new splendours from the

[029-054] surrounding darkness; but ah! whilst I adore the benignant rays that

[029-055] cheered and illumined my heart, I mourn that their influence cannot

[029-056] extend to all the sons and daughters of [[adversity|affliction]].

[029-057] “Indeed, Madam,” said poor Charlotte in a tremulous accent, “I am at a

[029-058] loss what to do. [[montraville|Montraville]] placed me here, and promised to defray all

[029-059] my expenses: but he has forgot his promise, he has forsaken me, and I

[029-060] have no [[friendship|friend]] who has either power or will to relieve me. Let me hope,

[029-061] as you see my unhappy situation, your [[benevolence|charity]]--”

[029-062] “Charity,” cried the woman impatiently interrupting her, “charity

[029-063] indeed: why, Mistress, charity begins at home, and I have seven children

[029-064] at home, HONEST, LAWFUL children, and it is my duty to keep them; and do

[029-065] you think I will give away my property to a nasty, impudent hussey, to

[029-066] maintain her and her bastard; an I was saying to my husband the other

[029-067] day what will this world come to; honest women are nothing now-a-days,

[029-068] while the harlotings are set up for fine ladies, and look upon us no

[029-069] more nor the dirt they walk upon: but let me tell you, my fine spoken

[029-070] Ma'am, I must have my money; so seeing as how you can't pay it, why you

[029-071] must troop, and leave all your fine gimcracks and fal der ralls behind

[029-072] you. I don't ask for no more nor my right, and nobody shall dare for to

[029-073] go for to hinder me of it.”

[029-074] “Oh heavens,” cried Charlotte, clasping her hands, “what will become of

[029-075] me?”

[029-076] “Come on ye!” retorted the unfeeling wretch: “why go to the barracks and

[029-077] work for a morsel of bread; wash and mend the soldiers cloaths, an cook

[029-078] their victuals, and not expect to live in idleness on honest people's

[029-079] means. Oh I wish I could see the day when all such cattle were obliged

[029-080] to work hard and eat little; it's only what they deserve.”

[029-081] “Father of [[compassion|mercy]],” cried Charlotte, “I acknowledge thy correction just;

[029-082] but prepare me, I beseech thee, for the portion of [[distress|misery]] thou may'st

[029-083] please to lay upon me.”

[029-084] “Well,” said the woman, “I shall go an tell my husband as how you can't

[029-085] pay; and so d'ye see, Ma'am, get ready to be packing away this very

[029-086] night, for you should not stay another night in this house, though I was

[029-087] sure you would lay in the street.”

[029-088] Charlotte bowed her head in silence; but the [[grief|anguish]] of her heart was

[029-089] too great to permit her to articulate a single word.
