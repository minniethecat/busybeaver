Liberty in Susanna Rowson's *[[charlotte-temple|Charlotte Temple]]* (1791) is a concept the novel treats with deep ambivalence. In its political [[reason|sense]] — freedom from unjust imprisonment — [[independence|liberty]] is an unambiguous good, as when [[captain-eldridge|Captain Eldridge]] speaks of his longing to "attain my [[independence|liberty]]" from debtor's prison ([004-010]). But the novel also exposes [[independence|liberty]]'s darker face: the freedom from parental supervision that enables [[charlotte-temple|Charlotte]]'s seduction, the [[independence|liberty]] of the boarding school that leaves [[youth|young]] women unprotected. Rowson's implicit argument is that [[independence|liberty]], for the [[youth|young]] and in[[experience]]d, is a danger dressed as a blessing.

## Definition

Rowson presents two distinct forms of [[independence|liberty]]. Physical [[independence|liberty]] — freedom from wrongful confinement — is treated as a fundamental right whose violation constitutes injustice. [[captain-eldridge|Captain Eldridge]]'s imprisonment for debt is the novel's central example of [[independence|liberty]] wrongly denied, and [[mr-temple|Mr. Temple]]'s efforts to secure his "[[independence|liberty]]" ([004-010]) model the proper use of power and wealth. But Rowson also critiques the [[independence|liberty]] afforded to [[youth|young]] women in fashionable education: [[charlotte-temple|Charlotte]]'s boarding school grants her a [[independence|liberty]] she is not equipped to manage, and that [[independence|liberty]] becomes the occasion of her ruin. The novel thus draws a crucial distinction: [[independence|liberty]] is a right for the mature and [[experience]]d but a snare for the [[youth|young]] and unguarded.

## Key Passages

> "Deprived of my liberty I cannot be free from anxiety" — Captain Eldridge on the psychological burden of imprisonment ([002-074])

> "I shall soon hear the delightful sound of his voice, and attain my liberty" — Eldridge anticipating Mr. Temple's rescue ([004-010])

> "The excessive felicity of seeing him at liberty" — on Eldridge's release ([004-096])

## Characters Who Embody / Challenge This Concept

- [[captain-eldridge]]: Liberty denied and restored. His imprisonment for debt il[[passion|lust]]rates [[independence|liberty]] as a right whose violation demands redress, and his eventual release models [[independence|liberty]] as the proper object of charitable action.
- [[charlotte-temple]]: Liberty's victim. The freedom afforded by her boarding-school education — freedom from parental supervision, freedom to receive clandestine visits — is [[independence|liberty]] that destroys rather than liberates.
- [[mr-temple]]: Liberty's agent. His efforts to free [[captain-eldridge|Eldridge]] and to rescue [[charlotte-temple|Charlotte]] position him as the novel's instrument of righteous liberation.

## Narrative Function

Liberty serves as the novel's political-moral pivot. By contrasting [[captain-eldridge|Eldridge]]'s unjust imprisonment with [[charlotte-temple|Charlotte]]'s dangerous freedom, Rowson makes a subtle argument about the appropriate scope of [[independence|liberty]]: it is a right for those who can exercise it responsibly, but a peril for those — particularly [[youth|young]] women — whose in[[experience]] makes freedom indistinguishable from exposure. This conservative treatment of [[independence|liberty]] aligns with the novel's broader didactic project: the protection of female virtue requires constraint, and the removal of constraint is the first step toward ruin.

## Related Concepts

- [[education]]: The institution that should prepare the [[youth|young]] for [[independence|liberty]] but, in [[charlotte-temple|Charlotte]]'s case, fails to do so.
- [[independence]]: Liberty's social form. The independence that [[charlotte-temple|Charlotte]] lacks is the independence of [[reason|judgment]] that would have enabled her to use her [[independence|liberty]] wisely.
- [[duty]]: Liberty's moral limit. The exercise of [[independence|liberty]] must be bounded by duty; [[independence|liberty]] without duty is licence.
