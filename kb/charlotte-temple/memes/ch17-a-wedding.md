[017-001] ON the day before their arrival at [[new-york|New-York]], after dinner, [[colonel-crayton|Crayton]] arose

[017-002] from his seat, and placing himself by Mademoiselle, thus addressed the

[017-003] company--

[017-004] “As we are now nearly arrived at our destined port, I think it but my

[017-005] duty to inform you, my [[friendship|friends]], that this lady,” (taking her hand,) “has

[017-006] placed herself under my protection. I have seen and severely felt the

[017-007] [[distress|anguish]] of her heart, and through every shade which cruelty or malice

[017-008] may throw over her, can discover the most amiable qualities. I thought

[017-009] it but necessary to mention my esteem for her before our disembarkation,

[017-010] as it is my fixed resolution, the morning after we land, to give her

[017-011] an undoubted title to my favour and protection by [[honour|honourably]] uniting my

[017-012] fate to hers. I would wish every gentleman here therefore to remember

[017-013] that her honour henceforth is mine, and,” continued he, looking at

[017-014] [[belcour|Belcour]], “should any man presume to speak in the least disrespectfully

[017-015] of her, I shall not hesitate to pronounce him a scoundrel.”

[017-016] Belcour cast at him a smile of contempt, and bowing profoundly low,

[017-017] wished Mademoiselle much joy in the proposed union; and assuring

[017-018] the Colonel that he need not be in the least apprehensive of any one

[017-019] throwing the least odium on the [[reputation|character]] of his lady, shook him by the

[017-020] hand with ridiculous gravity, and left the cabin.

[017-021] The truth was, he was glad to be rid of [[mademoiselle-la-rue|La Rue]], and so he was but freed

[017-022] from her, he cared not who fell a victim to her infamous arts.

[017-023] The inexperienced [[charlotte-temple|Charlotte]] was astonished at what she heard. She

[017-024] thought La Rue had, like herself, only been urged by the force of her

[017-025] attachment to Belcour, to quit her friends, and follow him to the feat

[017-026] of war: how wonderful then, that she should resolve to marry another

[017-027] man. It was certainly extremely wrong. It was indelicate. She mentioned

[017-028] her thoughts to [[montraville|Montraville]]. He laughed at her simplicity, called her a

[017-029] little idiot, and patting her on the cheek, said she knew nothing of

[017-030] the world. “If the world sanctifies such things, 'tis a very bad world I

[017-031] think,” said Charlotte. “Why I always understood they were to have been

[017-032] married when they arrived at New-York. I am sure Mademoiselle told me

[017-033] Belcour promised to marry her.”

[017-034] “Well, and suppose he did?”

[017-035] “Why, he should be obliged to keep his word I think.”

[017-036] “Well, but I suppose he has changed his mind,” said Montraville, “and

[017-037] then you know the case is altered.”

[017-038] Charlotte looked at him attentively for a moment. A full [[reason|sense]] of her

[017-039] own situation rushed upon her mind. She burst into tears, and remained

[017-040] silent. Montraville too well understood the cause of her tears. He

[017-041] kissed her cheek, and bidding her not make herself uneasy, unable to

[017-042] bear the silent but keen remonstrance, hastily left her.

[017-043] The next morning by sun-rise they found themselves at anchor before

[017-044] the city of New-York. A boat was ordered to convey the ladies on shore.

[017-045] Crayton accompanied them; and they were shewn to a house of public

[017-046] entertainment. Scarcely were they seated when the door opened, and the

[017-047] Colonel found himself in the arms of his daughter, who had landed a few

[017-048] minutes before him. The first transport of meeting subsided, Crayton

[017-049] introduced his daughter to Mademoiselle La Rue, as an old friend of her

[017-050] mother's, (for the artful French woman had really made it appear to the

[017-051] credulous Colonel that she was in the same convent with his first wife,

[017-052] and, though much younger, had received many tokens of her esteem and

[017-053] regard.)

[017-054] “If, Mademoiselle,” said [[mrs-beauchamp|Mrs. Beauchamp]], “you were the friend of

[017-055] my mother, you must be worthy the esteem of all good hearts.”

[017-056] “Mademoiselle will soon honour our [[family]],” said Crayton, “by supplying

[017-057] the place that valuable woman filled: and as you are married, my dear, I

[017-058] think you will not blame--”

[017-059] “Hush, my dear Sir,” replied Mrs. Beauchamp: “I know my duty too well to

[017-060] scrutinize your conduct. Be assured, my dear father, your happiness

[017-061] is mine. I shall rejoice in it, and sincerely [[affection|love]] the person who

[017-062] contributes to it. But tell me,” continued she, turning to Charlotte,

[017-063] “who is this lovely girl? Is she your sister, Mademoiselle?”

[017-064] A blush, deep as the glow of the carnation, suffused the cheeks of

[017-065] Charlotte.

[017-066] “It is a [[youth|young]] lady,” replied the Colonel, “who came in the same vessel

[017-067] with us from [[england|England]].' He then drew his daughter aside, and told her in

[017-068] a whisper, Charlotte was the mistress of Montraville.

[017-069] “What a [[compassion|pity]]!” said Mrs. Beauchamp softly, (casting a most compassionate

[017-070] glance at her.) “But surely her mind is not depraved. The [[kindness|goodness]] of

[017-071] her heart is depicted in her ingenuous countenance.”

[017-072] Charlotte caught the word [[compassion|pity]]. “And am I already fallen so low?” said

[017-073] she. A sigh escaped her, and a tear was ready to start, but Montraville

[017-074] appeared, and she checked the rising emotion. Mademoiselle went with the

[017-075] Colonel and his daughter to another apartment. Charlotte remained with

[017-076] Montraville and Belcour. The next morning the Colonel performed his

[017-077] promise, and La Rue became in due form Mrs. Crayton, exulted in her

[017-078] own good [[fate|fortune]], and dared to look with an eye of contempt on the

[017-079] unfortunate but far less [[guilt|guilty]] Charlotte.
