[018-001] “AND am I indeed fallen so low,” said [[charlotte-temple|Charlotte]], “as to be only pitied?

[018-002] Will the voice of approbation no more meet my ear? and shall I never

[018-003] again possess a [[friendship|friend]], whose face will wear a smile of joy whenever I

[018-004] approach? Alas! how thoughtless, how dreadfully imprudent have I been! I

[018-005] know not which is most painful to endure, the sneer of contempt, or the

[018-006] glance of [[charity|compassion]], which is depicted in the various countenances

[018-007] of my own sex: they are both equally humiliating. Ah! my dear parents,

[018-008] could you now see the child of your affections, the daughter whom you so

[018-009] dearly loved, a poor solitary being, without society, here wearing out

[018-010] her heavy hours in deep regret and [[distress|anguish]] of heart, no kind friend of

[018-011] her own sex to whom she can unbosom her griefs, no [[love|beloved]] mother, no

[018-012] woman of [[reputation|character]] will appear in my company, and low as your Charlotte

[018-013] is fallen, she cannot associate with infamy.”

## Section 10

[018-014] These were the painful reflections which occupied the mind of Charlotte.

[018-015] [[montraville|Montraville]] had placed her in a small house a few miles from [[new-york|New-York]]:

[018-016] he gave her one female attendant, and supplied her with what money she

[018-017] wanted; but business and pleasure so entirely occupied his time, that

[018-018] he had little to devote to the woman, whom he had brought from all her

[018-019] connections, and robbed of innocence. Sometimes, indeed, he would steal

[018-020] out at the close of evening, and pass a few hours with her; and then so

[018-021] much was she attached to him, that all her sorrows were forgotten while

[018-022] blest with his society: she would enjoy a walk by moonlight, or sit

[018-023] by him in a little arbour at the bottom of the garden, and play on the

[018-024] harp, accompanying it with her plaintive, harmonious voice. But often,

[018-025] very often, did he promise to renew his visits, and, forgetful of his

[018-026] promise, leave her to mourn her disappointment. What painful hours

[018-027] of expectation would she pass! She would sit at a window which looked

[018-028] toward a field he used to cross, counting the minutes, and straining her

[018-029] eyes to catch the first glimpse of his person, till blinded with tears

[018-030] of disappointment, she would lean her head on her hands, and give free

[018-031] vent to her sorrows: then catching at some new hope, she would again

[018-032] renew her watchful position, till the shades of evening enveloped every

[018-033] object in a dusky cloud: she would then renew her complaints, and, with

[018-034] a heart bursting with disappointed love and wounded sensibility, retire

[018-035] to a bed which [[repentance|remorse]] had strewed with thorns, and court in vain that

[018-036] comforter of weary nature (who seldom visits the unhappy) to come and

[018-037] steep her senses in oblivion.

[018-038] Who can form an adequate idea of the [[melancholy|sorrow]] that preyed upon the mind of

[018-039] Charlotte? The wife, whose breast glows with [[love|affection]] to her husband,

[018-040] and who in return meets only indifference, can but faintly conceive her

[018-041] [[grief|anguish]]. Dreadfully painful is the situation of such a woman, but she

[018-042] has many comforts of which our poor Charlotte was deprived. The duteous,

[018-043] faithful wife, though treated with indifference, has one solid pleasure

[018-044] within her own bosom, she can reflect that she has not deserved

[018-045] neglect--that she has ever fulfilled the [[duty|duties]] of her station with the

[018-046] strictest exactness; she may hope, by constant assiduity and unremitted

[018-047] attention, to recall her wanderer, and be doubly [[happiness|happy]] in his returning

[018-048] affection; she knows he cannot leave her to unite himself to another: he

[018-049] cannot cast her out to poverty and contempt; she looks around her,

[018-050] and sees the smile of friendly welcome, or the tear of affectionate

[018-051] [[comfort|consolation]], on the face of every person whom she favours with her

[018-052] esteem; and from all these circumstances she gathers comfort: but the

[018-053] poor girl by thoughtless passion led astray, who, in parting with

[018-054] her honour, has forfeited the esteem of the very man to whom she has

[018-055] sacrificed every thing dear and valuable in life, feels his indifference

[018-056] in the fruit of her own folly, and laments her [[poverty|want]] of power to recall

[018-057] his lost affection; she knows there is no tie but honour, and that, in

[018-058] a man who has been [[guilt|guilty]] of seduction, is but very feeble: he may leave

[018-059] her in a moment to shame and want; he may marry and forsake her for

[018-060] ever; and should he, she has no redress, no friendly, soothing companion

[018-061] to pour into her wounded mind the balm of consolation, no benevolent

[018-062] hand to lead her back to the path of [[virtue|rectitude]]; she has disgraced her

[018-063] friends, forfeited the good opinion of the world, and undone herself;

[018-064] she feels herself a poor solitary being in the midst of surrounding

[018-065] multitudes; shame bows her to the earth, [[regret|remorse]] tears her distracted

[018-066] mind, and guilt, poverty, and disease close the dreadful scene: she

[018-067] sinks unnoticed to oblivion. The finger of contempt may point out to

[018-068] some passing daughter of youthful mirth, the humble bed where lies this

[018-069] frail sister of [[death|mortality]]; and will she, in the unbounded gaiety of her

[018-070] heart, exult in her own unblemished fame, and triumph over the silent

[018-071] ashes of the dead? Oh no! has she a heart of sensibility, she will stop,

[018-072] and thus address the unhappy victim of folly--

[018-073] “Thou had'st thy faults, but sure thy [[suffering|sufferings]] have expiated them:

[018-074] thy errors brought thee to an early grave; but thou wert a

[018-075] fellow-creature--thou hast been unhappy--then be those errors forgotten.”

[018-076] Then, as she stoops to pluck the noxious weed from off the sod, a tear

[018-077] will [[ruin|fall]], and consecrate the spot to Charity.

[018-078] For ever honoured be the sacred drop of humanity; the angel of [[compassion|mercy]]

[018-079] shall record its source, and the soul from whence it sprang shall be

[018-080] immortal.

[018-081] My dear Madam, contract not your brow into a frown of disapprobation. I

[018-082] mean not to extenuate the faults of those unhappy women who fall victims

[018-083] to guilt and folly; but surely, when we reflect how many errors we are

[018-084] ourselves subject to, how many secret faults lie hid in the recesses of

[018-085] our hearts, which we should blush to have brought into open day (and yet

[018-086] those faults require the lenity and [[compassion|pity]] of a benevolent judge, or

[018-087] awful would be our prospect of futurity) I say, my dear Madam, when we

[018-088] consider this, we surely may pity the faults of others.

[018-089] Believe me, many an unfortunate female, who has once strayed into the

[018-090] thorny paths of vice, would gladly return to virtue, was any generous

[018-091] friend to endeavour to raise and re-assure her; but alas! it cannot be,

[018-092] you say; the world would deride and scoff. Then let me tell you, Madam,

[018-093] 'tis a very unfeeling world, and does not deserve half the blessings

[018-094] which a bountiful Providence showers upon it.

[018-095] Oh, thou benevolent giver of all good! how shall we erring mortals

[018-096] dare to look up to thy mercy in the great day of [[revenge|retribution]], if we now

[018-097] uncharitably refuse to overlook the errors, or alleviate the miseries,

[018-098] of our fellow-creatures.
