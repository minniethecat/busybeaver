[008-001] “I THINK, my dear,” said Mrs. [[mr-temple|Temple]], laying her hand on her husband's

[008-002] arm as they were walking together in the garden, “I think next Wednesday

[008-003] is [[charlotte-temple|Charlotte]]'s birth day: now I have formed a little scheme in my own

[008-004] mind, to give her an agreeable surprise; and if you have no objection,

[008-005] we will send for her home on that day.” Temple pressed his wife's hand

[008-006] in token of approbation, and she proceeded.--“You know the little alcove

[008-007] at the bottom of the garden, of which Charlotte is so fond? I have an

[008-008] inclination to deck this out in a fanciful manner, and invite all her

[008-009] little [[friendship|friends]] to partake of a collation of fruit, sweetmeats, and other

[008-010] things suitable to the general taste of [[youth|young]] guests; and to make it

[008-011] more pleasing to Charlotte, she shall be mistress of the feast, and

[008-012] entertain her visitors in this alcove. I know she will be delighted; and

[008-013] to complete all, they shall have some music, and finish with a dance.”

[008-014] “A very fine plan, indeed,” said Temple, smiling; “and you really

[008-015] suppose I will wink at your indulging the girl in this manner? You will

[008-016] quite spoil her, [[lucy|Lucy]]; indeed you will.”

[008-017] “She is the only child we have,” said [[mrs-temple|Mrs. Temple]], the whole tenderness

[008-018] of a mother adding animation to her fine countenance; but it was withal

[008-019] tempered so sweetly with the meek [[love|affection]] and submissive duty of the

[008-020] wife, that as she paused expecting her husband's answer, he gazed at her

[008-021] tenderly, and found he was unable to refuse her request.

[008-022] “She is a good girl,” said Temple.

[008-023] “She is, indeed,” replied the fond mother exultingly, “a grateful,

[008-024] affectionate girl; and I am sure will never lose sight of the duty she

[008-025] owes her parents.”

[008-026] “If she does,” said he, “she must forget the example set her by the best

[008-027] of mothers.”

[008-028] Mrs. Temple could not reply; but the delightful sensation that dilated

[008-029] her heart sparkled in her intelligent eyes and heightened the vermillion

[008-030] on her cheeks.

[008-031] Of all the pleasures of which the human mind is sensible, there is

[008-032] none equal to that which warms and expands the bosom, when listening to

[008-033] commendations bestowed on us by a [[love|beloved]] object, and are conscious of

[008-034] having deserved them.

[008-035] Ye giddy flutterers in the fantastic round of dissipation, who eagerly

[008-036] seek pleasure in the lofty dome, rich treat, and midnight revel--tell

[008-037] me, ye thoughtless daughters of folly, have ye ever found the phantom

[008-038] you have so long sought with such unremitted assiduity? Has she not

[008-039] always eluded your grasp, and when you have reached your hand to take

[008-040] the cup she extends to her deluded votaries, have you not found the

[008-041] long-expected draught strongly tinctured with the bitter dregs of

[008-042] disappointment? I know you have: I see it in the wan cheek, sunk

[008-043] eye, and air of chagrin, which ever mark the children of dissipation.

[008-044] Pleasure is a vain illusion; she draws you on to a thousand follies,

[008-045] errors, and I may say vices, and then leaves you to deplore your

[008-046] thoughtless credulity.

[008-047] Look, my dear friends, at yonder lovely Virgin, arrayed in a white robe

[008-048] devoid of ornament; behold the meekness of her countenance, the

[008-049] modesty of her gait; her handmaids are Humility, [[filial-piety|Filial Piety]], Conjugal

[008-050] Affection, Industry, and [[benevolence|Benevolence]]; her name is CONTENT; she holds

[008-051] in her hand the cup of true [[happiness|felicity]], and when once you have formed an

[008-052] intimate acquaintance with these her attendants, nay you must admit them

[008-053] as your bosom friends and chief counsellors, then, whatever may be your

[008-054] situation in life, the meek eyed Virgin wig immediately take up her

[008-055] abode with you.

[008-056] Is poverty your portion?--she will lighten your labours, preside at your

[008-057] frugal board, and watch your quiet slumbers.

[008-058] Is your state mediocrity?--she will heighten every blessing you enjoy,

[008-059] by informing you how grateful you should be to that bountiful Providence

[008-060] who might have placed you in the most abject situation; and, by teaching

[008-061] you to weigh your blessings against your deserts, show you how much more

[008-062] you receive than you have a right to expect.

[008-063] Are you possessed of [[wealth|affluence]]?--what an inexhaustible fund of happiness

[008-064] will she lay before you! To relieve the distressed, redress the injured,

[008-065] in short, to perform all the good works of peace and [[compassion|mercy]].

[008-066] Content, my dear friends, will blunt even the arrows of [[misfortune|adversity]], so

[008-067] that they cannot materially harm you. She will dwell in the humblest

[008-068] cottage; she will attend you even to a prison. Her parent is [[religion|Religion]];

[008-069] her sisters, Patience and [[hope|Hope]]. She will pass with you through life,

[008-070] smoothing the rough paths and tread to earth those thorns which every

[008-071] one must meet with as they journey onward to the appointed goal. She

[008-072] will soften the pains of sickness, continue with you even in the

[008-073] cold gloomy hour of death, and, cheating you with the smiles of her

[008-074] heaven-born sister, Hope, lead you triumphant to a blissful eternity.

[008-075] I confess I have rambled strangely from my story: but what of that? if

[008-076] I have been so lucky as to find the road to happiness, why should I be

[008-077] such a niggard as to omit so good an opportunity of pointing out the way

[008-078] to others. The very basis of true peace of mind is a benevolent wish to

[008-079] see all the world as happy as one's Self; and from my soul do I [[compassion|pity]] the

[008-080] selfish churl, who, remembering the little bickerings of anger, envy,

[008-081] and fifty other disagreeables to which frail [[death|mortality]] is subject, would

[008-082] wish to revenge the affront which pride whispers him he has received.

[008-083] For my own part, I can safely declare, there is not a human being in

[008-084] the universe, whose prosperity I should not rejoice in, and to whose

[008-085] happiness I would not contribute to the utmost limit of my power: and

[008-086] may my offences be no more remembered in the day of general [[revenge|retribution]],

[008-087] than as from my soul I forgive every offence or injury received from a

[008-088] fellow creature.

[008-089] Merciful heaven! who would exchange the rapture of such a reflexion for

[008-090] all the gaudy tinsel which the world calls pleasure!

[008-091] But to return.--Content dwelt in Mrs. Temple's bosom, and spread a

[008-092] charming animation over her countenance, as her husband led her in, to

[008-093] lay the plan she had formed (for the celebration of Charlotte's birth

[008-094] day,) before [[captain-eldridge|Mr. Eldridge]].
