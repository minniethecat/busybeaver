[033-001] WHEN [[mrs-beauchamp|Mrs. Beauchamp]] entered the apartment of the poor sufferer, she

[033-002] started back with horror. On a wretched bed, without hangings and but

[033-003] poorly supplied with covering, lay the emaciated figure of what still

[033-004] retained the semblance of a lovely woman, though sickness had so altered

[033-005] her features that Mrs. Beauchamp had not the least recollection of her

[033-006] person. In one corner of the room stood a woman washing, and, shivering

[033-007] over a small fire, two healthy but half naked children; the infant

[033-008] was asleep beside its mother, and, on a chair by the bed side, stood

[033-009] a porrenger and wooden spoon, containing a little gruel, and a tea-cup

[033-010] with about two spoonfulls of wine in it. Mrs. Beauchamp had never

[033-011] before beheld such a scene of poverty; she shuddered involuntarily, and

[033-012] exclaiming--“heaven preserve us!” leaned on the back of a chair ready to

[033-013] sink to the earth. The doctor repented having so precipitately brought

[033-014] her into this affecting scene; but there was no time for apologies:

[033-015] [[charlotte-temple|Charlotte]] caught the sound of her voice, and starting almost out of bed,

[033-016] exclaimed--“Angel of peace and [[compassion|mercy]], art thou come to deliver me? Oh,

[033-017] I know you are, for whenever you was near me I felt eased of half my

[033-018] sorrows; but you don't know me, nor can I, with all the recollection I

[033-019] am mistress of, remember your name just now, but I know that benevolent

[033-020] countenance, and the softness of that voice which has so often comforted

[033-021] the wretched Charlotte.”

[033-022] Mrs. Beauchamp had, during the time Charlotte was speaking, seated

[033-023] herself on the bed and taken one of her hands; she looked at her

[033-024] attentively, and at the name of Charlotte she perfectly conceived

[033-025] the whole shocking affair. A faint sickness came over her. “Gracious

[033-026] heaven,” said she, “is this possible?” and bursting into tears, she

[033-027] reclined the burning head of Charlotte on her own bosom; and folding her

[033-028] arms about her, wept over her in silence. “Oh,” said Charlotte, “you are

[033-029] very good to weep thus for me: it is a long time since I shed a tear for

[033-030] myself: my head and heart are both on fire, but these tears of your's

[033-031] seem to cool and refresh it. Oh now I remember you said you would send

[033-032] a letter to my poor father: do you think he ever received it? or perhaps

[033-033] you have brought me an answer: why don't you speak, Madam? Does he say I

[033-034] may go home? Well he is very good; I shall soon be ready.”

[033-035] She then made an effort to get out of bed; but being prevented, her

[033-036] frenzy again returned, and she raved with the greatest wildness and

[033-037] incoherence. Mrs. Beauchamp, finding it was impossible for her to be

[033-038] removed, contented herself with ordering the apartment to be made more

[033-039] comfortable, and procuring a proper nurse for both mother and child; and

[033-040] having learnt the particulars of Charlotte's fruitless application

[033-041] to [[mademoiselle-la-rue|Mrs. Crayton]] from honest John, she amply rewarded him for his

[033-042] [[charity|benevolence]], and returned home with a heart oppressed with many

[033-043] painful sensations, but yet rendered easy by the reflexion that she had

[033-044] performed her duty towards a distressed fellow-creature.

## Section 18

[033-045] Early the next morning she again visited Charlotte, and found her

[033-046] tolerably composed; she called her by name, thanked her for her

[033-047] [[kindness|goodness]], and when her child was brought to her, pressed it in her

[033-048] arms, wept over it, and called it the offspring of disobedience. Mrs.

[033-049] Beauchamp was delighted to see her so much amended, and began to hope

[033-050] she might recover, and, spite of her former errors, become an useful and

[033-051] respectable member of society; but the arrival of the doctor put an end

[033-052] to these delusive [[hope|hopes]]: he said nature was making her last effort, and

[033-053] a few hours would most probably consign the unhappy girl to her kindred

[033-054] dust.

[033-055] Being asked how she found herself, she replied--“Why better, much

[033-056] better, doctor. I hope now I have but little more to [[suffering|suffer]]. I had last

[033-057] night a few hours sleep, and when I awoke recovered the full power of

[033-058] recollection. I am quite sensible of my weakness; I feel I have but

[033-059] little longer to combat with the shafts of [[adversity|affliction]]. I have an humble

[033-060] confidence in the [[compassion|mercy]] of him who died to save the world, and [[trust]]

[033-061] that my sufferings in this state of [[death|mortality]], joined to my unfeigned

[033-062] [[penitence|repentance]], through his mercy, have blotted my offences from the sight

[033-063] of my offended maker. I have but one care--my poor infant! Father of

[033-064] mercy,” continued she, raising her eyes, “of thy infinite goodness,

[033-065] grant that the sins of the parent be not visited on the unoffending

[033-066] child. May those who taught me to despise thy laws be forgiven; lay not

[033-067] my offences to their charge, I beseech thee; and oh! shower the choicest

[033-068] of thy blessings on those whose pity has soothed the afflicted heart,

[033-069] and made easy even the bed of pain and sickness.”

[033-070] She was exhausted by this fervent address to the throne of mercy, and

[033-071] though her lips still moved her voice became inarticulate: she lay for

[033-072] some time as it were in a doze, and then recovering, faintly pressed

[033-073] Mrs. Beauchamp's hand, and requested that a clergyman might be sent for.

[033-074] On his arrival she joined fervently in the pious office, frequently

[033-075] mentioning her [[gratitude|ingratitude]] to her parents as what lay most heavy at her

[033-076] heart. When she had performed the last solemn duty, and was preparing to

[033-077] lie down, a little bustle on the outside door occasioned Mrs. Beauchamp

[033-078] to open it, and enquire the cause. A man in appearance about forty,

[033-079] presented himself, and asked for Mrs. Beauchamp.

[033-080] “That is my name, Sir,” said she.

[033-081] “Oh then, my dear Madam,” cried he, “tell me where I may find my poor,

[033-082] [[ruin|ruined]], but repentant child.”

[033-083] Mrs. Beauchamp was surprised and affected; she knew not what to say; she

[033-084] foresaw the agony this interview would occasion [[mr-temple|Mr. Temple]], who had just

[033-085] arrived in search of his Charlotte, and yet was sensible that the [[forgiveness|pardon]]

[033-086] and blessing of her father would soften even the agonies of death to the

[033-087] daughter.

[033-088] She hesitated. “Tell me, Madam,” cried he wildly, “tell me, I beseech

[033-089] thee, does she live? shall I see my darling once again? Perhaps she is

[033-090] in this house. Lead, lead me to her, that I may bless her, and then lie

[033-091] down and die.”

[033-092] The ardent manner in which he uttered these words occasioned him to

[033-093] raise his voice. It caught the ear of Charlotte: she knew the [[love|beloved]]

[033-094] sound: and uttering a loud shriek, she sprang forward as Mr. Temple

[033-095] entered the room. “My adored father.” “My long lost child.” [[nature|Nature]]

[033-096] could support no more, and they both sunk lifeless into the arms of the

[033-097] attendants.

[033-098] Charlotte was again put into bed, and a few moments restored Mr. Temple:

[033-099] but to describe the agony of his sufferings is past the power of

[033-100] any one, who, though they may readily conceive, cannot delineate the

[033-101] dreadful scene. Every eye gave testimony of what each heart felt--but

[033-102] all were silent.

[033-103] When Charlotte recovered, she found herself supported in her father's

[033-104] arms. She cast on him a most expressive look, but was unable to speak.

[033-105] A reviving cordial was administered. She then asked in a low voice,

[033-106] for her child: it was brought to her: she put it in her father's arms.

[033-107] “Protect her,” said she, “and bless your dying--”

[033-108] Unable to finish the sentence, she sunk back on her pillow: her

[033-109] countenance was serenely composed; she regarded her father as he pressed

[033-110] the infant to his breast with a steadfast look; a sudden beam of joy

[033-111] passed across her languid features, she raised her eyes to heaven--and

[033-112] then closed them for ever.
