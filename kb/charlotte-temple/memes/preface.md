[P01-001] PREFACE.

[P01-002] FOR the perusal of the [[youth|young]] and thoughtless of the fair sex, this Tale

[P01-003] of Truth is designed; and I could wish my fair readers to consider it as

[P01-004] not merely the effusion of Fancy, but as a reality. The circumstances

[P01-005] on which I have founded this novel were related to me some little time

[P01-006] since by an old lady who had personally known [[charlotte-temple|Charlotte]], though she

[P01-007] concealed the real names of the characters, and likewise the place where

[P01-008] the unfortunate scenes were acted: yet as it was impossible to offer a

[P01-009] relation to the public in such an imperfect state, I have thrown over

[P01-010] the whole a slight veil of fiction, and substituted names and places

[P01-011] according to my own fancy. The principal characters in this little tale

[P01-012] are now consigned to the silent tomb: it can therefore hurt the feelings

[P01-013] of no one; and may, I flatter myself, be of service to some who are so

[P01-014] unfortunate as to have neither [[friendship|friends]] to advise, or understanding to

[P01-015] direct them, through the various and unexpected evils that attend a

[P01-016] young and unprotected woman in her first entrance into life.

[P01-017] While the tear of [[charity|compassion]] still trembled in my eye for the fate of

[P01-018] the unhappy Charlotte, I may have children of my own, said I, to

[P01-019] whom this recital may be of use, and if to your own children, said

[P01-020] [[benevolence|Benevolence]], why not to the many daughters of [[misfortune|Misfortune]] who, deprived

[P01-021] of [[nature|natural]] friends, or spoilt by a mistaken education, are thrown on an

[P01-022] unfeeling world without the least power to defend themselves from the

[P01-023] snares not only of the other sex, but from the more dangerous arts of

[P01-024] the profligate of their own.

[P01-025] Sensible as I am that a novel writer, at a time when such a variety

[P01-026] of works are ushered into the world under that name, stands but a poor

[P01-027] chance for fame in the annals of literature, but conscious that I wrote

[P01-028] with a mind anxious for the happiness of that sex whose morals and

[P01-029] conduct have so powerful an influence on mankind in general; and

[P01-030] convinced that I have not wrote a line that conveys a wrong idea to

[P01-031] the head or a corrupt wish to the heart, I shall rest satisfied in the

[P01-032] [[innocence|purity]] of my own intentions, and if I merit not applause, I feel that I

[P01-033] [[fear|dread]] not censure.

[P01-034] If the following tale should save one hapless fair one from the errors

[P01-035] which [[ruin|ruined]] poor Charlotte, or rescue from impending [[distress|misery]] the heart

[P01-036] of one anxious parent, I shall feel a much higher gratification in

[P01-037] reflecting on this trifling performance, than could possibly result

[P01-038] from the applause which might attend the most elegant finished piece

[P01-039] of literature whose tendency might deprave the heart or mislead the

[P01-040] understanding.
