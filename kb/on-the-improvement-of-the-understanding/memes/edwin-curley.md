# Edwin Curley

Edwin Curley (b. 1937) is an American philosopher and professor emeritus at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. He is one of the foremost Spinoza scholars of the late 20th and early 21st centuries, best known for his authoritative English translation of Spinoza's collected works.

## Biographical Sketch

Edwin M. Curley received his Ph.D. from Duke University and taught philosophy at the University of Michigan for much of his career. His research focuses on early modern philosophy, particularly Spinoza, Descartes, and Hobbes. His two-volume *The Collected Works of Spinoza* (Princeton University Press, 1985 and 2016) is widely regarded as the definitive English edition of Spinoza's writings, superseding the 19th-century Elwes translation in scholarly contexts.

## Connection to the Treatise

Curley's translation of the *Tractatus de Intellectus Emendatione* is the source of the paragraph-numbering system used throughout this wiki's source text. The numbers shown in brackets (e.g., [1], [48]) are taken from Curley's edition, and Spinoza's endnotes are marked according to his scheme. The Notice to the Reader and endnote annotations explicitly credit Curley's editorial work.

The Curley translation is now the standard reference for Anglophone Spinoza scholarship, offering more precise renderings of Spinoza's technical Latin vocabulary than the older Elwes translation. His edition includes extensive scholarly apparatus — notes, introductions, and cross-references — that situate the *Tractatus* within Spinoza's broader philosophical project.

## Notes

Curley's translation is particularly valued for its fidelity to Spinoza's Latin and its avoidance of the archaic diction that sometimes obscures Elwes's Victorian rendering. Scholars working with the *Tractatus* today typically cite Curley's paragraph numbers, which have become the de facto standard for cross-referencing Spinoza's texts across editions. For readers consulting this wiki, the Curley-derived paragraph numbers ensure compatibility with the broader scholarly literature on Spinoza.
