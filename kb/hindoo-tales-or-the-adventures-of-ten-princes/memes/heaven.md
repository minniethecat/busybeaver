# Heaven

## Definition

Heaven in the Hindoo Tales is the divine realm — the abode of the gods and celestial beings — and is invoked primarily as a source of supernatural agency. Heavenly beings such as the Apsaras (celestial females) inhabit this realm, and punishment from heaven is understood as a just response to wrongdoing.

## In the Narrative

Heaven appears episodically but with narrative weight. A certain mortal is described as having a heavenly nature (003-144). A character interprets misfortune as a just punishment from heaven (015-055). Another judgment from heaven is referenced in connection with a character's fate (024-107). The index provides the gloss that Apsaras are heavenly females (026-045).

> "…his is a just punishment from heaven; so may all…" (015-055)

> "…ravarma was a punishment from heaven." (024-107)
