## Biography

Sumittra was one of the three sons of [[dharmapala|Dharmapâla]], a minister in the court of [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]]. (001-106) He was the brother of [[sumantra|Sumantra]] and [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]]. (001-106) When the infant [[arthapala|Arthapâla]] was discovered, the king, surprised that [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]] should have married a Yaksha, sent for Sumittra and committed the boy to his care, giving him the name of [[arthapala|Arthapâla]]. (002-149)

## Role

Sumittra served as the guardian and caretaker of Prince [[arthapala|Arthapâla]], the son of his brother [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]], after the child was brought to the court. (002-149)

## Relationships

- **[[dharmapala|Dharmapâla]]**: His father, a minister of [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]]. (001-106)
- **[[sumantra|Sumantra]]**: His brother. (001-106)
- **[[kamapala|Kâmapâla]]**: His brother, father of [[arthapala|Arthapâla]]. (001-106)
- **[[arthapala|Arthapâla]]**: His nephew, whom he raised as guardian. (002-149)

## Quotations

> The first of these had three sons, Sumantra, Sumittra, and Kâmapâla; (001-106)

> The king, greatly surprised, especially that Kâmapâla should have married a Yaksha, sent for the child's uncle, Sumittra, and committed the boy to his care, giving him the name of Arthapâla. (002-149)
