# Vâmadeva

Vâmadeva was a celebrated rishi and holy man, well acquainted with all past,
present, and future events. He served as a counsellor to King [[Râjahansa]] of
[[magadha|Magadha]], foretelling the birth of his son [[Râjavâhana]] and orchestrating
the delivery of several foundling children to the royal court.
(002-013) (002-021) (026-153)

## Biography

When [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]] was driven from his throne and sought to regain his
fortunes through religious penance, he went to consult Vâmadeva, intending
to engage in such austerities under the holy man's direction.
(002-013) (002-014)

> "Having been well received by the holy man, he said to him: 'O father,
> having heard of your great piety and wisdom, I have come hither for
> the favour of the gods, and be restored to my former power.'"
> (002-015)

Vâmadeva, who knew all things past, present, and to come, answered the king
that there was no need of penance. He prophesied that a son would be born
to [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]] who would crush all his enemies and restore his fortunes.
(002-021) (002-023)

Soon afterwards, a disciple of Vâmadeva brought a beautiful boy to the king.
The disciple explained that he had found the child with an old woman during a
pilgrimage to [[ramatirtha|Râmatirtha]], and the holy man had directed him to bring it to
the court.
(002-094) (002-095)

> "After searching for the woman some time in vain, I took the child to
> my master, the great muni Vâmadeva, and I have now brought it to you
> by his command."
> (002-132) (002-133)

The king, astonished at the preservation of the child under adverse
circumstances, named him [[arthapala|Arthapâla]] and entrusted him to his ministers.
(002-134) (002-150)

Not long afterwards, another disciple of Vâmadeva brought a second beautiful
child to the king, having discovered the infant during a pilgrimage to several
holy places.
(002-151) (002-152)

Later, Vâmadeva himself came to visit [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]]. Seeing the young prince
[[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] perfect in beauty, strength, and accomplishments, and surrounded
by his band of companions, the sage declared to the king that his wish was
fulfilled.
(003-028) (003-029) (003-031)

## Role in the Narrative

Vâmadeva serves as the spiritual patron and prophetic anchor of the
narrative's opening. His foreknowledge of events sets the story in motion,
and his disciples' deliveries of foundling children to the court of
[[rajahansa|Râjahansa]] provide the companions who become the ten princes. The rishi
functions as a divine intermediary, ensuring that destiny unfolds as ordained.

## Relationships

Vâmadeva was the spiritual counsellor of [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]], and his disciples
were entrusted with the delivery of children to the royal court. Two of
these children — [[Arthapâla]] and others — were brought to the king at his
command, becoming part of the company of young princes that surrounded
[[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]].
(002-094) (002-132) (002-151)
