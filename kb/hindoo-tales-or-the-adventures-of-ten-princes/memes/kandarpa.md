# Kandarpa

**Kandarpa** is the God of Love in Hindoo mythology, synonymous with [[Kâma]]. He appears in the glossary of proper names as one of the recognized deities of the Hindoo pantheon. (026-087)

## Biography

Kandarpa is identified in the text as identical to [[kama|Kâma]], the Hindoo [[kama|god of love]]. In the glossary of proper names appended to the work, he is listed as "_[[kama|Kâma]]_ or _Kandarpa_, the God of Love." (026-087) His name is invoked in classical simile throughout the narrative.

## Role in the Narrative

Kandarpa appears in the text as a poetic reference point for beauty and allure. During a public dance performance witnessed by the narrator, a female performer is described with "a glancing look like that of Kandarpa when first descended to earth." (025-166) The comparison draws on the mythological image of the love-god's descent, associating the dancer's glance with divine charm.

> "Then she being caused to have a glancing look like that of Kandarpa when first descended to earth, corresponding therewith having her gracefully-curved creeper eyebrows sportively playing."
> (025-166)
