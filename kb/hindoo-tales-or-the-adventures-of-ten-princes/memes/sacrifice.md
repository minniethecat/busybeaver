# Sacrifice

## Definition

Sacrifice (Yajna) is a religious ritual central to Hindoo worship, involving offerings made to the gods for their propitiation. The practice ranges from daily prayers and libations to grand communal ceremonies conducted by brahmans, and extends in the narrative to the mention of human sacrifice — a darker dimension of the ritual spectrum.

## In the Narrative

Sacrifices appear throughout the narrative as markers of religious devotion and moments of narrative significance. In the opening chapter, the king's prayers and sacrifices for the welfare of his subjects establish the ritual framework of righteous kingship (001-095). A dramatic sacrifice scene in Chapter 13 uses the ringing of a bell to signal the gravity of the moment (013-099). In Chapter 23, a great sacrifice is proposed as the proper response to divine displeasure (023-095). The reference to human sacrifices in the index (026-078) hints at a more sombre register of the practice, though this appears only in passing.

> "prayers and sacrifices to the gods, for the welfare…" (001-095)

> "…ted her, went to the place of sacrifice, and rang a bell hanging…" (013-099)

> "propitiated; let a great sacrifice be made to-day. The brahmans…" (023-095)
