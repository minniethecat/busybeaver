# Justice

## Definition

Justice in the context of the *Daśakumāracarita* denotes the fair and lawful administration of right and wrong, dispensed by kings, elders, and learned authorities. It encompasses both judicial procedure — the hearing of cases, weighing of evidence, and giving of verdicts — and the broader moral duty of a ruler to protect subjects through equitable governance.

## In the Narrative

The concept of justice appears primarily as an attribute of kingship and learned authority. A king is expected to be both "learned in the Scriptures" and "a protector of his subjects," fusing religious knowledge with the practical dispensation of law (023-002).
