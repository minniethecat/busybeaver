## Biography

Vimardaka was the keeper of a gaming house who was employed by [[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]] in his various schemes. (026-162) He was directed to enter the service of [[arthapati|Arthapati]] and use all possible means to excite his master against [[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]]. (009-171)

Following [[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]]'s instructions, Vimardaka carried out his role by publicly abusing and insulting [[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]] in a large assembly. [[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]], as if much astonished, played along with the ruse. (010-046)

When asked whether he had a confidential servant named Vimardaka, the answer came without hesitation: "Certainly; he is a very trustworthy man, entirely..." (010-061)

## Role

Vimardaka functions as an operative in [[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]]'s schemes. His position as a gaming-house keeper gave him access to useful information and social circles, making him a valuable asset in the intrigues against [[arthapati|Arthapati]] and [[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]].
