# Valabhi

Valabhi is a city in the *Daśakumāracarita*, home to [[grihagupta|Grihagupta]] and his
daughter, who becomes the lawful wife of a character in [[mitragupta|Mitragupta]]'s
narrative. The city appears in Chapter 20 as a reference point in a legal
dispute over marriage and identity. (020-053)

## Description

Valabhi is introduced in the narrative as the residence of [[grihagupta|Grihagupta]],
the father of a woman whose identity becomes the subject of a courtroom
dispute. A merchant, accused of abducting the daughter of
[[niddhipatidatta|Niddhipatidatta]], defends himself by claiming that the woman is in fact
his lawful wife, the daughter of [[grihagupta|Grihagupta]] of Valabhi. (020-053)

> "This is my own lawful wife, the daughter of Grihagupta, who lives at
> Valabhi. She was married to me with the proper ceremony and with the full
> consent of her parents."
> (020-053)

The merchant offers Valabhi as a verifiable reference point, proposing
that messengers be sent to the city to confirm his account with his
father-in-law. (020-056)

> "if you will not believe my assertion, send to Valabhi, to my wife's
> father, and hear what he will say."
> (020-056)

The city functions in the narrative as an anchor of truth — a distant
place whose inhabitants can corroborate or refute the merchant's claim,
underscoring the importance of verifiable origins in the text's legal
and social framework. (020-053) (020-056)

## Significance in the Narrative

Valabhi's role in the *Daśakumāracarita* is modest but revealing. It
appears in [[mitragupta|Mitragupta]]'s account of a legal proceeding where a woman's
identity and marital status are contested. The merchant's appeal to
Valabhi as a source of verification demonstrates the narrative's concern
with questions of legitimacy, proof, and social standing. (020-053)

The city is tied to [[grihagupta]], a minor figure who nevertheless
plays a decisive role in the resolution of the dispute — his daughter's
identity is the key fact in question, and his testimony, accessible
through messengers to Valabhi, is the proposed means of establishing
the truth. (020-056)

[[mitragupta|Mitragupta]]'s narrative of a legal dispute. The city is not mentioned
elsewhere in the text.
