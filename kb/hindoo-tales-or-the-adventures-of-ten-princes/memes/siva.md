# Siva

Siva (Shiva) is one of the principal deities invoked and worshipped throughout
the *Daśakumāracarita*. He appears in visions, grants boons to devotees, and
his temple at [[mahakala|Mahâkâla]] serves as a key setting for encounters between characters.
(001-159)
(026-032)
(026-120)

## Biography

Siva is described as the mighty god whose temple is at [[mahakala|Mahâkâla]]. King [[manasara|Mânasâra]]
undertook severe penance to propitiate him:

> "The haughty Mânasâra ... has been for a long time endeavouring to propitiate
> with very severe penance the mighty Siva, whose temple is at Mahâkâla, and he
> has so far succeeded that the god has given him a magic club, very destructive
> of life and conducive to victory."
> (001-159)
> (001-160)
> (001-161)

Siva is said to grant weapons and favour to those who worship him, making them
feel invincible:

> "Through this weapon, and the favour of Siva, he now thinks himself a"
> (001-162)

Siva appears in a vision to the sage [[matanga|Matanga]], rewarding his devotion:

> "O prince, last night, during sleep, Siva appeared to me and addressed me
> thus: 'Matanga, I am pleased with your devotions; they shall now have their
> reward.'"
> (003-094)
> (003-095)

His temple is a destination for pilgrims seeking blessings and re[[marriage-in-the-tales|union]]s:

> "I am on my way to the temple of Mahâkâla, to worship Siva there. I have
> stopped at this place, hoping, at a festival so much frequented, I might at
> least hear some tidings of you."
> (004-098)
> (004-099)

His wife is the goddess [[durga|Kâli]] (Durga):

> "_Durga_ or _Kâli_, wife of Siva, a terrific goddess, delighting in"
> (026-077)

## Role in the Narrative

Siva functions as a divine force who intervenes in human affairs through boons,
visions, and sacred spaces. His temple at [[mahakala|Mahâkâla]] is a recurring location where
characters converge, creating opportunities for recognition and re[[marriage-in-the-tales|union]].

He is one of the three principal gods of the Hindu pantheon, alongside [[brahma|Brahma]]
and [[vishnu|Vishnu]], who are sometimes regarded as forming a divine triad.
(026-120)
(026-148)

[[worship|Worship]] of Siva is described as a proper religious practice, and characters who
pay due worship to him receive favourable outcomes:

> "after paying due worship to Siva"
> (016-104)
