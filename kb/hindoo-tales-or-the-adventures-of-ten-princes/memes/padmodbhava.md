# Padmodbhava

Padmodbhava was one of the three chief ministers of King [[Râjahansa]] of
[[magadha|Magadha]], serving alongside [[Dharmapâla]] and [[Sitavarma]]. He was the
father of [[susruta|Susruta]], who appears in the chronicles of the court.

## Biography

Padmodbhava was named among the three trusted ministers of [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]], the
council that formed the administrative backbone of the [[magadha|Magadha]]n kingdom.
(001-105)

> "Their names were, Dharmapâla, Padmodbhava, and Sitavarma."
> (001-105)

He was the father of [[susruta|Susruta]], who is recorded in the character index of the
*Daśakumāracarita* as the son of Padmodbhava.
(026-122) (026-151)

> "_Padmodbhava_, one of Râjahansa's ministers."
> (026-122)

As one of the three ministers, Padmodbhava was part of the trusted circle
that advised [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]] on matters of state and the upbringing of the young
prince [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]]. Together with his fellow ministers, he helped guide the
kingdom through both peace and the aftermath of war.
(001-105)

The ministers collectively counselled Queen [[Vasumati]] on matters
concerning the young prince [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] after his illness, showing that
Padmodbhava's role extended beyond statecraft into the intimate affairs
of the royal household.
(002-002)

## Role in the Narrative

Padmodbhava represents the stable institutional foundation of the [[magadha|Magadha]]n
monarchy. Along with his fellow ministers, he provided continuity of
governance and wise counsel to the royal family.

Though his individual actions are less elaborated than those of some other
characters, his presence as one of the "three ministers" signals that
[[rajahansa|Râjahansa]]'s court was built on collective wisdom rather than the counsel
of a single advisor. This distributed model of ministerial governance
allowed the kingdom to withstand the crises of war and the king's eventual
decline.

His son [[susruta|Susruta]] continued the family line, appearing in the character index
as a recognized figure, though the narrative does not give him a prominent
role in the adventures of the princes.
(026-151)

## Relationships

Padmodbhava served King **[[Râjahansa]]** as one of his three principal
ministers, alongside **[[Dharmapâla]]** and **[[Sitavarma]]**.
(001-105)

He was the father of **[[susruta|Susruta]]**, who is recognized in the character index
of the narrative.
(026-151)

Through his ministerial role, Padmodbhava was connected to the royal
household of **[[Vasumati]]** and the young prince **[[Râjavâhana]]**.
(002-002)
