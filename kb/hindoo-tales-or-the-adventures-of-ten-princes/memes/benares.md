# Benâres

Benâres is a sacred city in the *Daśakumāracarita*, associated with both
princely governance and religious significance. It was ruled at different
times by King [[sinhaghosha]] — who was later deposed — and by Prince
[[kamapala|Kâmapâla]], who served as its lord. The city is the setting for
a key episode in [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]]'s narrative.
(007-184) (014-022) (026-143)

## Description

Benâres held the status of a sacred city, a destination for travellers and
pilgrims. [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]] arrived there during his wanderings, describing it as
"this city, Benâres" — a place where significant encounters occurred.
(014-022) (014-073)

> "one day at the sacred city of Benâres"
> (014-022)

The city had a complex political history. [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]], one of the ten princes,
was established as lord of Benâres, indicating the city's importance within
the political restoration that the princes sought to achieve. Earlier, it had
been ruled by King [[sinhaghosha|Sinhaghosha]], who was deposed from his throne.
(007-184) (026-143)

> "Kâmapâla lord of Benâres"
> (007-184)

> "Sinhaghosha, the deposed King of Benâres"
> (026-143)

[[mantragupta|Mantragupta]] came to Benâres and, while there, heard a child's cry that led
him into a significant encounter — one of the many chance events in the city
that shaped the princes' adventures. (014-073) (014-103)

> "One day I came to this city, Benâres, and"
> (014-073)

> "at Benâres, I heard the cry of a child"
> (014-103)

## Significance in the Narrative

Benâres serves as the stage for part of [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]]'s adventure in Chapter 14.
His arrival in the sacred city and the encounter triggered by a child's cry
form the opening of his personal narrative, demonstrating how the princes'
wanderings brought them into the affairs of the cities they visited.
(014-022) (014-073) (014-103)

The city's dual identity — sacred site and political prize — reflects the
narrative's blending of religious and temporal power. The transfer of lordship
from the deposed [[sinhaghosha|Sinhaghosha]] to [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]] illustrates the broader theme of
restoration that runs through the *Daśakumāracarita*.
(007-184) (026-143)

## Appearances

Benâres appears in Chapters 7, 14, and the proper names index of Chapter 26.
Chapter 7 identifies it as [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]]'s domain, while Chapter 14 contains
[[mantragupta|Mantragupta]]'s narrative set within the city.
