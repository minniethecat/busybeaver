# Niddhipatidatta

**Niddhipatidatta** is the father of [[Kanakavati]], mentioned during the legal proceedings brought against [[Balabhadra]] in [[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]]'s narrative in Chapter 20.

## Biography

Niddhipatidatta is referenced only indirectly: a woman falsely claims to be his daughter [[kanakavati|Kanakavati]] in order to bring a legal accusation against [[balabhadra|Balabhadra]]. The true identity of the accuser and her relationship to Niddhipatidatta are contested in the proceedings. (020-052)

## Role in the Narrative

Niddhipatidatta appears in the story of [[balabhadra|Balabhadra]] through the claim of a woman who asserts she is his daughter. [[balabhadra|Balabhadra]]'s wife counters the accusation by stating that the woman "is not [[kanakavati|Kanakavati]], the daughter of Niddhipatidatta," but is instead [[balabhadra|Balabhadra]]'s own lawful wife, the daughter of [[Grihagupta]]. (020-052–053) Niddhipatidatta thus serves as a point of reference in the contested identity at the heart of the legal drama.

> "This is not Kanakavati, the daughter of Niddhipatidatta; this is my own lawful wife, the daughter of Grihagupta, who lives at Valabhi."
> (020-052–053)
