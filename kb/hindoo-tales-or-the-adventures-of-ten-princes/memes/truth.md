# Truth

## Definition

Truth in the Hindoo Tales functions as both an epistemological value (the discovery and verification of facts) and a moral standard (truthfulness in speech). Characters invoke truth when demanding honest testimony, when verifying claims, and when establishing the credibility of narratives within narratives.

## In the Narrative

A speaker attests that "truth, and that the prince well deserved" his reputation (005-124), linking truth to merit and desert. A dramatic demand for honesty appears when a character says "Tell me the truth; you cannot" conceal it (014-165), treating truth-telling as a compelled obligation. A vow to discover facts is expressed as "I find out the truth, and I will place myself under" an obligation (016-069), presenting truth as something to be actively sought. Finally, a reflective passage considers "how they prove the truth of" assertions (018-163), engaging with the verification of claims — a meta-narrative concern.

> "…truth, and that the prince well deserved…" (005-124)

> "…sadness, saying, 'Tell me the truth; you cannot…'" (014-165)

> "…I find out the truth, and I will place myself under…" (016-069)

> "…were, and how they prove the truth of…" (018-163)
