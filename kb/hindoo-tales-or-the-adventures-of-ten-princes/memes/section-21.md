[021-001]Then laying my burden on the soft grass in a shady place, and

[021-002]sprinkling her with water, I soon had the happiness of seeing her open

[021-003]her eyes, and of recognising the beloved of my heart, the Princess

[021-004]Kandukavati, who was equally delighted on finding who was her

[021-005]deliverer.

[021-006]When sufficiently recovered, she said to me: "On returning home after

[021-007]the ball dance, longing to see you, and sad with the thought that we

[021-008]might never meet again, I was filled with great happiness by the

[021-009]report which Chandrasena brought me of your love; but when I heard

[021-010]that you had been bound and thrown into the sea by my wicked brother,

[021-011]I fell into the deepest despair, and wished for death. Wandering in

[021-012]this state of mind about the gardens, I was espied by that vile

[021-013]Rakshas, who, having assumed a human form, first made love to me, and

[021-014]then, when rejected, forcibly carried me off. He is, happily, now

[021-015]dead, and all that I have suffered is as nothing now that I am with

[021-016]you; let us return as soon as possible to my parents, who will have

[021-017]been greatly distressed at my disappearance."

[021-018]Without delay I carried her down to the shore, embarked, set sail at

[021-019]once, and the wind being favourable, we soon reached Damalipta. Here

[021-020]we found great confusion and grief among the people, and were told on

[021-021]inquiring: "The king and queen, utterly broken down by the loss of

[021-022]their son and daughter, have determined to abandon life, and have just

[021-023]set out for a holy place on the bank of the Ganges, with the intention

[021-024]of fasting to death there; and several of the old citizens have

[021-025]accompanied them with the same purpose."

[021-026]On hearing this I immediately went after them, and having soon

[021-027]overtaken them, was able to give them great happiness, by telling them

[021-028]of all that had occurred, and how both their son and daughter were

[021-029]safely returned; and they went back with me to the city, to the great

[021-030]joy of the people. The king treated me with great honour, and not long

[021-031]afterwards the princess became my wife. Her brother was reconciled to

[021-032]me, and at my request, though very reluctantly, gave up all further

[021-033]attention to Chandrasena, who was happily united with her lover.

[021-034]When King Sinhavarma was attacked as you know, I marched with an army

[021-035]to his assistance; and have thus the great pleasure of meeting with

[021-036]you.

[021-037]The prince having heard this story said "Your adventures have indeed

[021-038]been strange, and your escape from death wonderful. Great is the power

[021-039]of fate, but excellent also is courage and presence of mind such as

[021-040]you have shown." Then turning to Mantragupta, he desired him to relate

[021-041]his adventures, which he immediately began to do:--

[021-042]* * * * *

[021-043]ADVENTURES OF MANTRAGUPTA.

[021-044]My Lord, I also, in my anxiety to find you, wandered about like the

[021-045]others.

[021-046]Late one evening I came to a wood, a few miles from the city of

[021-047]Kalinga, and very near a public cemetery. Seeing no dwelling near, I

[021-048]made myself a bed of leaves, and lay down under a large tree, where I

[021-049]was soon asleep. About midnight, when evil spirits are wont to roam,

[021-050]and everything was quiet around me, I awoke, and fancied I heard a

[021-051]whispering conversation going on among the branches of the tree

[021-052]immediately above me. Listening very attentively, I was able to

[021-053]distinguish these words: "We are powerless to resist that vile Siddha

[021-054]whenever he chooses to command us; could not some person be found

[021-055]powerful enough to counteract the designs of that vile magician?"

[021-056]After this the voices ceased, and I thought I could hear a rustling

[021-057]among the branches as if the speakers were moving from tree to tree.

[021-058]This strange occurrence greatly excited my curiosity. I said to

[021-059]myself: "Who are these creatures whose voices I have heard? who can

[021-060]that magician be, and what dreadful thing is it which he is about to

[021-061]do?" With these thoughts, I determined if possible to discover the

[021-062]mystery, and followed, as well as I was able, the direction which the

[021-063]demons, or whatever they were whom I had heard conversing, had taken.

[021-064]Guided by the rustling sound which I still heard above me, I made my

[021-065]way through the darkness, till at last I thought I saw a light in the

[021-066]distance, and going a little further, I perceived a fire shining

[021-067]through the thick foliage. Approaching very cautiously, I saw a Siddha

[021-068]standing near it, his head covered with a large mass of tangled hair,

[021-069]his body begrimed with the dust of charcoal, and a girdle of human

[021-070]bones round his waist. He was throwing at intervals handfuls of

[021-071]sesamum and mustard-seed into the fire, causing flickering flames to

[021-072]rise up and dispel the surrounding darkness. Before him, in humble

[021-073]attitude, stood two Rakshas, male and female, whom I supposed to be

[021-074]those whose voices I had heard in the tree. They said to him, "We

[021-075]await your commands. What are we now to do?"

[021-076]"Go," he answered in a stern voice, "immediately to the palace of the

[021-077]King of Kalinga, and bring here his daughter Kanakalekha." This they

[021-078]did in an incredibly short time. As soon as she was brought he seized

[021-079]her by the hair, and disregarding her tears and entreaties and screams

[021-080]for help, was about to cut off her head with a sword.

[021-081]Meanwhile I had cautiously crept nearer, and perceiving the danger of

[021-082]the princess, I made a sudden rush at him, snatched the sword from his

[021-083]hand and cut off his head.

[021-084]Seeing this, the two Rakshas approached me, and showing great delight

[021-085]at the death of their cruel master, said to me: "That wicked man has

[021-086]for a long time had power over us; we have continually been compelled

[021-087]to go on his vile errands, and have had no rest night or day. You have

[021-088]done a truly good deed in killing him; your valour has freed us from

[021-089]this slavery; he is gone to the kingdom of Yama, where he will receive

[021-090]the reward of his evil deeds, and we are ready to serve you; say only

[021-091]what is to be done."

[021-092]I thanked them for their grateful offer, and said: "I have only done

[021-093]what every good man would have done under the circumstances; but if

[021-094]you are willing to serve me, all that I require of you is to carry

[021-095]this lady again to her father's house, from which she was so cruelly

[021-096]taken."

[021-097]The princess hearing this, stood for a moment irresolute, with her

[021-098]head bent down, her eyes half closed, her eyebrows quivering, her

[021-099]bosom agitated by hurried breathing and wetted by tears of joy,

[021-100]restlessly moving one foot, as if scratching the ground, and betraying

[021-101]the struggle between bashfulness and love by alternate blushes and

[021-102]paleness. Then, in a low sweet gentle voice, she uttered these words:

[021-103]"O gracious sir, why do you, having just delivered me from a terrible

[021-104]death, now overwhelm me in a sea of love whose waves are the

[021-105]agitations of anxiety driven by the wind of passion? My life, saved by

[021-106]you, is entirely at your disposal. Take pity on me; regard me as your

[021-107]own. Let me be your servant, your slave; I would endure anything

[021-108]rather than separation from you. Come with me to my father's palace;

[021-109]you need not fear discovery; all my friends and attendants are

[021-110]faithful and devoted to me; they will carefully keep the secret."

[021-111]Pierced to the heart by the arrows of Kâma, tied and bound by her

[021-112]looks and words as if with chains of iron, I had no power to refuse,

[021-113]and turning to the two Rakshas, I said: "I have no choice here.

[021-114]Whatever this fair lady commands must be done. Take us both,

[021-115]therefore, to the place from which you brought her."

[021-116]Bowing down in submission, they lifted us from the ground, carried us

[021-117]through the air, and placed us while it was yet night in the

[021-118]apartments of the princess. There she introduced me to her attendants,

[021-119]assigned me a room in the upper story where I might most easily escape

[021-120]detection, and appointed them to keep watch so that no one might enter

[021-121]her apartments without notice. I had thus abundant opportunities of

[021-122]being with the princess; but though my love daily increased, I made no

[021-123]further advances to her.

[021-124]One day some of her women came with tears in their eyes, and bowing

[021-125]down to my feet, said, with whispering timid voice, "O gracious sir,

[021-126]our lady is doubly yours, since she was gained by your own valour

[021-127]when you rescued her from death, and is assigned to you by the

[021-128]all-powerful God of Love. Do not let her languish in vain. Make her

[021-129]your wife without delay." With this request I could not refuse to

[021-130]comply, and taking the hand of the princess, I declared our solemn

[021-131]union.

[021-132]For a time we enjoyed the greatest happiness. It was destined,

[021-133]however, to be of no long duration; our separation was at hand, for

[021-134]now was the time of spring, when the trees were covered with blossoms

[021-135]bent down by the eager bees, and the song of birds was resounding

[021-136]among their branches waved by the soft south wind, bearing perfume

[021-137]from the sandal groves of Malaya; at which season the king was

[021-138]accustomed to go with all his court to the sea-shore, and there, in

[021-139]tents under the shade of lofty trees, to enjoy the cool sea breezes.

[021-140]My bride of course went with the rest; and as there was no possibility

[021-141]of concealing me in such a place, I was obliged, though reluctantly,

[021-142]to let her depart alone, consoling myself by looking forward to her

[021-143]return.

[021-144]The royal party had not long been gone, when news was brought to the

[021-145]city that the king and all his court, thinking only of enjoyment, and

[021-146]unsuspicious of danger, had been captured by Jayasinha, King of

[021-147]Andhra, who, sailing with a large fleet, had suddenly landed and taken

[021-148]them by surprise.

[021-149]This news caused me the greatest consternation. "Jayasinha," I

[021-150]thought, "will certainly be captivated by the beauty of the princess;

[021-151]she will take poison rather than submit to his embraces; and I could

[021-152]not long survive her, for how could I live without her?"

[021-153]While perplexed with this thought, and not knowing what to do, I heard

[021-154]of a brahman just arrived from Andhra, who was full of a strange event

[021-155]which had lately happened there.

[021-156]"The King of Andhra," he said, "has long been a bitter enemy of the

[021-157]King of Kalinga, and having taken him prisoner, was about to kill him,

[021-158]but he has fallen in love with the princess Kanakalekha, and wishing

[021-159]to marry her, not only spares her father's life, but treats him with

[021-160]kindness for her sake.

[021-161]"An unexpected obstacle to the accomplishment of his wishes has,

[021-162]however, arisen; the lady has suddenly become possessed by an evil

[021-163]spirit, whose rage is greatest whenever the king visits her.

[021-164]"Anxious for her recovery, he has offered a large reward to any one

[021-165]who shall succeed in driving out the demon, but as yet no one has been

[021-166]able to effect her cure."

[021-167]This information filled me with hope, for I was well aware of the

[021-168]nature of the princess's disease, and knew that no one but myself

[021-169]could cure it. I was able, therefore, to form a plan for her

[021-170]deliverance, and quickly decided on the disguise to be adopted. At the

[021-171]time when I killed the magician, I had taken off his scalp, with all

[021-172]the mass of tangled hair, and had hid it in a hollow tree. I now went

[021-173]to the place, and taking out this scalp, fitted it on my own head;

[021-174]then rubbing over my whole body with dirt and charcoal dust, and

[021-175]dressing myself in old rags, I was completely disguised as an

[021-176]ascetic--and when I went into the neighbouring villages I was regarded

[021-177]as a very holy devotee, and had many applications from persons

[021-178]wishing for advice or seeking to be cured of diseases. This belief I

[021-179]encouraged to the utmost, and took care to keep up my credit by means

[021-180]of various tricks and contrivances.
