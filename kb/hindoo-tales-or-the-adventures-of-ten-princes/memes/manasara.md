# Mânasâra

Mânasâra was the haughty and ambitious King of [[Mâlwa]], the principal
antagonist of King [[Râjahansa]]. His defeat and subsequent conquest of
[[magadha|Magadha]] set the political backdrop for the adventures of the ten princes in
the *Daśakumāracarita*.
(026-108)

## Biography

Mânasâra ruled the kingdom of [[malwa|Mâlwa]], adjoining the realm of [[Râjahansa]].
Driven by ambition and pride, he marched against [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]] with a formidable
army, but was soundly defeated.
(001-120) (001-121)

> "…the king of the adjoining country of Mâlwa, the haughty and ambitious
> Mânasâra, whom he marched to encounter with a numerous army, making the
> earth tremble with the tread of his elephants."
> (001-121)

Despite his defeat, Mânasâra brooded over his disgrace and spent a long time
preparing to avenge himself upon his conqueror.
(001-156) (001-157)

> "The haughty Mânasâra, brooding over his defeat, unmindful of your generous
> forbearance, and only anxious to wipe off his disgrace."
> (001-156)

Mânasâra eventually succeeded in overcoming [[Râjahansa]] and seized the
kingdom of [[magadha|Magadha]], forcing the rightful king to seek guidance from a sage.
(002-017) (002-018)

> "Mânasâra, King of Mâlwa, has overcome me, and now holds the kingdom which
> ought to be mine."
> (002-017)

Later, Mânasâra came with his attendants to a park, where he encountered the
heroes of the tale. Having heard of [[Râjavâhana]]'s great skill, he was
desirous of witnessing it himself.
(006-057) (006-156)

## Role in the Narrative

Mânasâra functions as the primary political antagonist whose conquest of
[[magadha|Magadha]] displaces the rightful king and scatters the ten princes. Yet the
antagonism is ultimately resolved through marriage rather than further warfare:
his daughter [[avantisundari|Avantisundari]] becomes the wife of [[Râjavâhana]], uniting the two
rival houses.
(026-053)

His character arc moves from haughty conqueror to a king who recognises and
seeks out the skill of the young prince, suggesting a potential for
reconciliation and respect.

## Relationships

Mânasâra was the father of [[Avantisundari]], who became the wife of
[[Râjavâhana]], the hero of the narrative.
(026-053)

He was the ruler of the kingdom of [[Mâlwa]], which he governed as a
sovereign monarch.
(026-106)

He was the conqueror and rival of [[Râjahansa]], King of [[magadha|Magadha]].
(026-108)
