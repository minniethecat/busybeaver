# Târâvali

## Biography

Târâvali was a Yaksha lady, the daughter of a chief Yaksha named [[manibhadra|Manibhadra]].
> "Târâvali. I am the daughter of a chief Yaksha."
> (014-100)

She visited a friend living on the [[malaya|Malaya Mountains]], and during her travels became entangled in the affairs of mortals and celestial beings.
> "I am the daughter of a chief Yaksha. A short time ago I went to visit a friend, living on the Malaya Mountains."
> (014-100)

She is recorded in the genealogical summary as a Yaksha lady and the wife of [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]], one of the Ten Princes.
> "_Târâvali_, a Yaksha lady, wife of Kâmapâla."
> (026-152)

Her father [[manibhadra|Manibhadra]] is identified as a Yaksha.
> "_Manibhadra_, a Yaksha, father of Târâvali."
> (026-109)

## Role in the Narrative

Târâvali remained with the narrator's wife and became great friends with her fellow-wife, sharing the story of her origins and how her son had been lost, showing the bonds that formed between the women.
> "Târâvali remained with me, became great friends with her fellow-wife, told her the story which she had related to me, and how her son had been lost."
> (014-135)

Her fellow-wife later revealed that Târâvali had taken something of importance, creating tension between the two women who had previously been close companions.
> "'my friend and fellow-wife, Târâvali, has taken it.'"
> (014-167)

When [[kuvera|Kuvera]], the god of wealth, returned [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]] to the mortal world, Târâvali — who had received him from [[kuvera|Kuvera]] — did not bring him back at once, causing further complication. The narrator attributes this to the nature of a rival wife.
> "Târâvali, who, when she had received you again from Kuvera, did not bring you at once to me; but what could I expect from her? It is the nature of a rival wife."
> (015-089)

## Relationships

- **[[manibhadra|Manibhadra]]** — Father, a chief Yaksha (026-109).
- **[[kamapala|Kâmapâla]]** — Husband, one of the Ten Princes (026-152).
- **[[kuvera|Kuvera]]** — The god of wealth, involved in the celestial narrative connecting Târâvali to the mortal realm (015-089).
- **Fellow-wife** — The narrator's other wife, with whom Târâvali formed a close friendship that later became strained by rivalry (014-135).
