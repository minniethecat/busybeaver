# Mantragupta

Mantragupta is one of the ten princes and companions of [[Râjavâhana]]
in the *Daśakumāracarita*. He was one of the four sons of [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]]'s
ministers, raised alongside the prince.
(002-032)

## Biography

Mantragupta was born to one of the four ministers of [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]]. He
was named Mantragupta alongside [[pramati|Pramati]], [[mitragupta|Mitragupta]], and [[visruta|Visruta]], and
raised together with Prince [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]].
(002-032)

He was among the companions who reunited with [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] after the great
dispersal, arriving in the company of [[upaharavarma|Upahâravarma]], [[pramati|Pramati]], and [[mitragupta|Mitragupta]].
(007-183)

Mantragupta married two wives: [[ambalika|Ambâlika]], the daughter of [[sinhavarma|Sinhavarma]], and
[[kanakalekha|Kanakalekha]], the daughter of the King of [[Kalinga]].
(026-040)
(026-091)

## Role in the Narrative

Mantragupta's tale is recounted to his companions after the re[[marriage-in-the-tales|union]]. When
his turn came, [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] addressed him:

> "Then turning to Mantragupta, he desired him to relate his adventures."
> (021-040)

His story constitutes the chapter "ADVENTURES OF MANTRAGUPTA."
(021-043)

## Relationships

Mantragupta was the foster-brother of [[pramati|Pramati]], [[mitragupta|Mitragupta]], and [[visruta|Visruta]].
(002-032)

His first wife was [[ambalika|Ambâlika]], daughter of [[sinhavarma|Sinhavarma]].
(026-040)

His second wife was [[kanakalekha|Kanakalekha]], daughter of the King of [[kalinga|Kalinga]].
(026-091)
