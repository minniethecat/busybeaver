# Prayer

## Definition

Prayer in the Hindoo Tales is the act of addressing the gods — through petitions, praises, lamentations, and offerings — as a fundamental mode of religious communication. It is frequently paired with sacrifice and penance as one of the core practices of piety, and is presented as a direct channel through which mortals may obtain divine favour, have their wishes granted, or appease divine displeasure.

## In the Narrative

Prayer appears throughout the narrative in diverse forms. The king's prayers and sacrifices for the welfare of his subjects establish the ritual framework of righteous rule (001-095). In times of distress, characters make many prayers (001-137) and passionate lamentations that take the form of prayer (001-199). Prayer is prescribed as a discipline alongside meditation (002-011). A muni is found engaged in prayer (008-020). A character's prayer to a local deity is heard and answered (016-093). A goddess grants a prayer for children after many prayers and offerings are made (017-138, 017-140). [[penance|Penance]] and prayer are jointly recommended as the path to appease the gods (019-175), and prayer with penance is said to obtain what is sought (023-041).

> "…devote yourself to prayer and meditation." (002-011)

> "and said: 'Your prayer is granted; your wife shall b…" (017-140)

> "obtained by prayer and penance;" (023-041)
