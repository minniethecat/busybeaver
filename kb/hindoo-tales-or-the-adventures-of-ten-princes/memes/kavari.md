# Kâvari

The Kâvari is a river in ancient India, mentioned in the second chapter of
the *Daśakumāracarita*. It is the setting where a [[brahman|Brahman]] disciple
discovered a woman carrying an infant who would become
[[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]], one of the Ten Princes.
(002-154)

## Geography

The text describes the Kâvari as a river whose banks served as a path of
travel for pilgrims. The precise location of the river is not given,
though the narrative context places it within reach of a pilgrimage route
connecting several holy places.
(002-152) (002-153) (002-154)

> "My lord, I have lately been on a pilgrimage to several holy places,
> and on my way back, happening to be on the bank of the river Kâvari,
> I saw a woman carrying this child, and evidently in great distress."
> (002-152) (002-153) (002-154) (002-155)

## Role in the Narrative

The river Kâvari is the location where [[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]],
still an infant, was found by a disciple of [[vamadeva|Vâmadeva]]. The
woman carrying him, questioned by the disciple, told the story of how
[[satyavarma|Satyavarma]] — the youngest son of [[sitavarma|Sitavarma]], a minister of
the King of [[magadha|Magadha]] — had entrusted the child to her care.
(002-154) (002-155) (002-156) (002-157)
