## Biography

Navamâlika was the princess of [[sravasti|Sravasti]], daughter of the King of [[sravasti|Sravasti]]. She appears in the narrative of [[pramati|Pramati]], who was carried to her sleeping apartment. (016-103)

[[pramati|Pramati]], transported through the air by a goddess, was placed in Navamâlika's sleeping chamber while she slept. The goddess assured him that no one would disturb him there. When [[pramati|Pramati]] awoke and found himself beside the princess, he was struck by her beauty. (016-102)

Upon discovering that the lady he had seen was the Princess Navamâlika, [[pramati|Pramati]] fell deeply in love with her and set out for [[sravasti|Sravasti]], determined to see her again and win her hand. (016-119)

## Role

Navamâlika is the romantic interest in [[pramati|Pramati]]'s narrative, the fifth tale of the collection. Her significance lies in being the object of [[pramati|Pramati]]'s divinely arranged encounter and subsequent quest. She represents the princess-as-prize motif found throughout the tales, where love is sparked by a supernatural or chance meeting.

## Quotations

> "Having thus found the supposed dream to be a reality, and that the lady whom I had seen was the Princess Navamâlika, I was confirmed in my love, and set out for Sravasti, determined, if possible, to see her again." (016-119)
