# Kalindí

**Kalindí** is the Queen of [[patala|Pâtâla]], the daughter of a chief of the Asuras, and the wife of the brahman [[Matanga]]. She appears in the narrative of [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]]'s descent into the subterranean world.

## Biography

Kalindí was the daughter of a chief of Asuras who ruled the subterranean world of [[Pâtâla]]. Her father was slain by [[vishnu|Vishnu]] after offending him, and having no son, Kalindí was left as his heir and successor, suffering great distress and perplexity. (003-140)

She consulted a holy Siddha who told her that a mortal bearing a heavenly body would descend from the upper world, become her husband, and relieve her difficulties. This prophecy was fulfilled when the brahman [[Matanga]] arrived in [[patala|Pâtâla]]. Kalindí received him with great honour and they were married. (003-140)

When [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] departed from [[patala|Pâtâla]] to return to the upper world, Kalindí gave him a magic jewel that had the power to ward off hunger, thirst, fatigue, and other discomforts from its possessor. (003-158) [[matanga|Matanga]] accompanied the prince for part of the way before returning to Kalindí.

## Role

Kalindí serves as a benefactor figure in the [[patala|Pâtâla]] episode. Her gift of the magic jewel to [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] is a key narrative element that aids the prince on his subsequent journey. She represents the alliance between the mortal world and the subterranean realm of the Asuras, bridged through her marriage to the brahman [[matanga|Matanga]].
