# Conquest in the Tales

## Summary

Conquest in the *Adventures of the Ten Princes* is the martial vocation to which princes are born and the prize that draws allied kings into war. It is presented both as a young man's prescribed career and as the motive for coalition invasion.

## Context

The counsellor's charge frames conquest as destiny: it is "time for him to go out into the world and prepare himself for the career of conquest" (003-034). The same hunger drives the political plot of the twenty-fourth chapter, where a king is persuaded to join "the King of [[asmaka|Asmaka]], who had already obtained several other allies eager to have a share in the expected conquest and plunder" (024-013).

> "...the King of Asmaka, who had already obtained several other allies eager to have a share in the expected conquest and plunder."
> (024-013)

The consequence is invasion and defeat: "when the country was actually invaded, no effectual resistance was made; [[anantavarma|Anantavarma]] was easily defeated" (024-014). Conquest, once set in motion, consumes the rulers it enlists.

## Key Participants

- [[asmaka|Asmaka]] — the kingdom whose king rallies allies for the expected conquest (024-012)
- [[anantavarma|Anantavarma]] — defeated when the coalition invades (024-015)
- The muni — who charges the prince to pursue "the career of conquest" (003-034)
