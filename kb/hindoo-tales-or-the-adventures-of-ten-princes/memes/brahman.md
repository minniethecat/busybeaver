# Brahman

## Definition

The Brahman (or Brahmin) is the highest of the four castes in traditional Hindoo society — the priestly and learned class entrusted with the performance of religious rites, the preservation of sacred knowledge, and the counsel of kings. Brahmans are characterised by their piety, learning, and ritual purity, and command deep respect across all levels of society.

## In the Narrative

Brahmans appear throughout the narrative as figures of moral and intellectual authority. In Chapter 1, the learned and pious brahmans are continually employed in prayers and sacrifices for the welfare of the kingdom, and the old brahmans serve as faithful counsellors who perceive the queen's purpose (001-094, 001-189). Chapter 2 introduces the social power of brahman status — a character exploits the universal respect owed to brahmans to gain safe passage and hospitality (002-070, 002-075). Chapter 3 provides a more critical lens, describing nominal brahmans who have abandoned their sacred studies for worldly pursuits, and contrasting them with the true brahman ideal of learning and self-discipline (003-043, 003-049).
