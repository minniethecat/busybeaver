## Biography

Gaurí is a goddess in Hindu mythology, identified as the wife of [[siva|Siva]] and the sister of [[durga|Kâli]] (also known as [[durga|Durgâ]]). (026-079) She appears in the narrative when a [[brahman|Brahman]], having no children by his first wife [[durga|Kâli]], took Gaurí as his second wife, and by her he had a son. (002-161) Her footprints were said to be visibly marked upon a glittering crystal rock on the bank of a river flowing through the [[dandaka|Dandaka forest]]. (003-098)

## Role

Gaurí is the consort of [[siva|Siva]] and the mother of a child by a [[brahman|Brahman]] in the narrative. (002-161) She represents a gentler aspect of the divine feminine compared to her sister [[durga|Kâli]], who is described as a terrific goddess [[pleasure|delight]]ing in human sacrifices. (026-079)

## Relationships

- **[[siva|Siva]]**: Her husband. (026-079)
- **[[durga|Kâli]] ([[durga|Durgâ]])**: Her sister, also wife of [[siva|Siva]]. (002-161) (026-079)
- **A [[brahman|Brahman]]**: Took her as second wife after [[durga|Kâli]] bore no children. (002-161)
