# Family

## Definition

Family in the Hindoo Tales is the foundational social structure, carrying a network of duties, obligations, and attachments. Characters act to protect their families, return to their families, and bear consequences on behalf of their families. The concept encompasses both the immediate household and the broader kinship network that defines social identity.

## In the Narrative

The king is advised to "retire with your family and treasure to a strong" place for safety (001-169), treating family as the core social unit requiring protection. A character "with his family, had gone to" a distant place (002-039), showing family as a mobile social formation. The pull of kinship is felt when someone is "desirous of visiting his family" (002-103). Family duties are enumerated alongside "religious obligations" (003-050), placing domestic responsibility on par with spiritual duty. The stakes of family honour are stark: "all her family will be reduced if she persists" (008-054), and disgrace threatens the "whole family, as well as on myself" (015-070).

> "…retire with your family and treasure to a strong…" (001-169)

> "…of Mithila — that he, with his family, had gone to…" (002-039)

> "…religious obligations, and family duties, are devoted to all…" (003-050)

> "…all her family will be reduced if she persists…" (008-054)

> "…whole family, as well as on myself." (015-070)
