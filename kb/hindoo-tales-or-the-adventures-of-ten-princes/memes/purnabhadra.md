# Purnabhadra

Purnabhadra was a reformed robber who became a loyal servant and trusted companion of Prince [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]]. His timely assistance proved instrumental in [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]]'s plan to rescue his father, King [[sinhaghosha|Sinhaghosha]], from execution.

## Biography

Purnabhadra had been a servant of the minister of King [[sinhaghosha|Sinhaghosha]] of [[benares|Benâres]]. When [[sinhaghosha|Sinhaghosha]], longing to be freed from restraint, caused his minister to be arrested and proclaimed a traitor, Purnabhadra lost his only friend and protector. Despairing, he was in the act of fastening his sash to hang himself when [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]] interrupted him (015-001–006).

> When Purnabhadra had finished this story, I said to him, "I am that child who was exposed in the cemetery, and saved by the fairy. My coming here is indeed opportune, and with your assistance I will engage to deliver my father." (015-007–010)

[[kamapala|Kâmapâla]] revealed his true identity and enlisted Purnabhadra's help. While they were discussing plans, a serpent emerged from a hole nearby. [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]], skilled in charming serpents, secured it and devised a bold scheme:

> Then I said to Purnabhadra: "O friend, this is just what I wanted. I will mix with the crowd when my father is led round, let this serpent fall on him as if by chance, and then run up to him and say that I am skilled in charms, and can save his life." (015-017–020)

Purnabhadra was [[pleasure|delight]]ed with the plan:

> Purnabhadra, delighted with the plan which I proposed, no longer wished to destroy himself. He set out at once to do as I had directed. (015-032–033)

After the successful execution of the plan, Purnabhadra also prepared [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]]'s mother for the news of her husband's apparent death:

> Meanwhile, my mother, who had been prepared by Purnabhadra to hear of her husband's death, went immediately to the king... (015-062)

## Role

Purnabhadra transitioned from a despairing outcast to a trusted agent of [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]]. He is remembered as a reformed robber who served [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]] faithfully, embodying the theme of redemption through loyal service.

## Relationships

- **[[kamapala|Kâmapâla]]** — The prince who saved him from suicide and became his master and friend (015-007–033)
- **[[sinhaghosha|Sinhaghosha]]** — King of [[benares|Benâres]]; Purnabhadra's former master's arrest by [[sinhaghosha|Sinhaghosha]] had driven Purnabhadra to despair, yet he ultimately helped rescue the king (015-001–006)
- **[[kamapala|Kâmapâla]]'s Mother** — Purnabhadra prepared her for the news of her husband's death according to [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]]'s plan (015-062)
