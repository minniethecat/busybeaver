# Andhra

Andhra is a kingdom in the southern region of India, appearing in the
*Daśakumāracarita* as the destination of [[mantragupta]]'s wanderings. It is
a land of maritime trade, brahman travellers, and royal courts, where the
prince encounters the political dynamics of the Deccan.
(021-147) (021-154)

## Description

Andhra was a coastal kingdom with a significant maritime presence. Its king
commanded a large fleet, sailing with forces that projected Andhra's power
across the eastern seas. This naval capability distinguished Andhra from the
landlocked kingdoms of northern India and made it a notable power in the
southern regions.
(021-147)

> "Andhra, who, sailing with a large fleet."
> (021-147)

The kingdom was connected to the wider world through travellers and merchants.
A brahman recently arrived from Andhra brought news and strange events from
the kingdom, serving as a conduit of information between the southern lands
and the princes wandering in other regions. This brahman's account revealed
the political situation in Andhra and set the stage for [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]]'s
intervention.
(021-154)

> "of a brahman just arrived from Andhra, who was full of a strange event."
> (021-154)

The King of Andhra was a figure of romantic and political interest. He had
long been a suitor, pursuing a marriage alliance that would extend his
influence. His prolonged courtship became a matter of regional politics,
drawing the attention of the wandering princes who sought to navigate the
complex web of royal relationships across India.
(021-156)

> "The King of Andhra," he said, "has long been a suitor."
> (021-156)

Near the city of Andhra lay a lake where [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]] built himself a dwelling
— a hermitage or retreat from which he observed the kingdom and planned his
next moves. The lake served as the prince's base of operations during his
southern campaign.
(022-006)

> "lake not far from the city of Andhra, built myself a"
> (022-006)

## Significance in the Narrative

Andhra represents the southern frontier of the princes' reach. While the main
narrative centres on the Gangetic plain kingdoms of [[magadha|Magadha]] and
[[malwa|Mâlwa]], Andhra extends the geographical scope of the tale into the
Deccan, demonstrating the breadth of the princes' wanderings and the
pan-Indian scale of their eventual restoration of righteous rule.
(021-147) (021-156)

The kingdom's maritime character — its fleet, its coastal trade, its
connections to travellers — adds a distinctive element to the narrative's
geography. Unlike the forest hermitages and riverbank cities of the north,
Andhra is a place of ships and seaborne power, enriching the *Daśakumāracarita*'s
portrait of classical India's diverse political landscape.
(021-147)

adventures in the southern kingdoms and establishes his base near the
city of Andhra.
