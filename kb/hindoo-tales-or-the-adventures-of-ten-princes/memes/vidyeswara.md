# Vidyeswara

Vidyeswara was a celebrated conjurer who travelled from court to court
exhibiting his magical skill for the amusement of kings and nobles. He
played a pivotal role in the narrative by using his arts to arrange the
marriage of Prince [[Râjavâhana]] to Princess [[avantisundari|Avantisundari]].
(006-128) (026-160)

## Biography

Vidyeswara arrived at the city of [[avanti|Oujein]] announcing himself as a famous
conjurer who travelled about exhibiting his skill for the amusement of
kings and nobles. He declared that he had come to [[avanti|Oujein]] to show his
skill before the king.
(006-128) (006-129) (006-130)

When the prince approached him for help in winning the hand of a princess,
Vidyeswara replied with confidence:

> "To such as you, with me for an ally, nothing is impossible. I will,
> through my skill, contrive that you shall marry the princess in the
> very presence of all the suitors."
> (006-141) (006-142) (006-143)

The following morning, Vidyeswara gathered a large troop of followers and
went to the palace. He announced himself to the doorkeeper with the words,
"Tell the king the great conjurer is arrived," setting in motion his plan
to secure the princess for [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]].
(006-153) (006-154) (006-155)

Through his arts, Vidyeswara succeeded in arranging the marriage of
[[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] to [[avantisundari|Avantisundari]], an event recorded in the index of characters.
(026-160)

## Role in the Narrative

Vidyeswara functions as a magical enabler, using his conjuring skills to
overcome obstacles that would otherwise prevent the hero's marriage. His
confidence and theatrical flair — arriving with a troop of followers and
announcing himself grandly — add a touch of spectacle to the narrative.
He represents the theme of ingenuity and illusion overcoming social
barriers.

## Relationships

Vidyeswara allied himself with [[Râjavâhana]], to whom he promised his
conjuring skill would make the impossible possible. He presented himself
at the court of the king of [[avanti|Oujein]], where he performed before the
assembled nobles and suitors.
(006-141) (006-153) (026-160)
