## Biography

Kosadâsa appears in the narrative as a merchant who befriends the narrator. He stood near the protagonist throughout a public event and afterwards invited him to his house, where the narrator was most hospitably entertained. (018-050)

After a woman left their company, Kosadâsa and the narrator engaged in conversation about matters that greatly concerned them both, so much so that they became deeply interested in each other's affairs. (018-074)

Kosadâsa was originally named Arthadâsa by his parents, but was known by his enemies as Vèsadâsa — meaning "slave of a girl" — owing to his excessive devotion. (025-117)

## Role

Kosadâsa is a merchant figure who serves as a companion and informant to the narrator in Chapter 18. His character also functions as the lover of [[chandrasena|Chandrasena]]. (026-096)
