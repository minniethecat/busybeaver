## Biography

Vikatavarma was the nephew of [[praharavarma|Prahâravarma]], [[praharavarma|King of Mithila]]. When [[praharavarma|Prahâravarma]] was returning to his kingdom after being liberated from imprisonment, he discovered that a rebellion had broken out, headed by his brother's son, Vikatavarma. (012-024)

Vikatavarma seized the throne and imprisoned his uncle [[praharavarma|Prahâravarma]] together with his queen, ruling as a usurper. He was described in highly unflattering terms by those who knew him. (012-147)

He was married to [[kalpasundari|Kalpasundari]], the beautiful and accomplished daughter of the sovereign of Kumâra, but she despised him for his ugliness. Vikatavarma is characterised as ugly, stupid, ignorant of all the arts of pleasing, brutal, rebellious, cruel, boastful, and false. (012-090) (012-149)

## Role

Vikatavarma serves as an antagonist in [[upaharavarma|Upahâravarma]]'s narrative. His rebellion against [[praharavarma|Prahâravarma]] sets the stage for [[upaharavarma|Upahâravarma]]'s mission to infiltrate the palace and restore the rightful king. His negative portrayal contrasts with the noble qualities of the princes, and his mistreatment of the beautiful [[kalpasundari|Kalpasundari]] underscores the injustice of his rule.

## Quotations
