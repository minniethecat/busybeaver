# Somadatta

Somadatta is one of the ten princes whose adventures form the core narrative
of the *Daśakumāracarita*. He was the son of [[satyavarma|Satyavarma]], a minister of
[[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]], and was among the first companions to reunite with
[[Râjavâhana]] after the great dispersal.
(003-013)

## Biography

Somadatta was the son of [[satyavarma|Satyavarma]], a minister of [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]].
He was given his name by [[satyavarma|Satyavarma]] and committed to the care of his guardians.
(003-013)

## Role in the Narrative

Somadatta is one of the ten princes whose adventures form the core of the narrative.
His story is recounted to his companions after they are reunited, and he tells them
of all that befell him during their separation. His adventures are noted alongside
those of [[pushpodbhava|Pushpodbhava]] as particularly significant.
(003-176)
(003-180)

Somadatta later married Vâmalochana, the daughter of [[viraketu|Víraketu]], in one of the
many [[marriage|marriages]] that conclude the princes' tales.
(026-154)

His father [[satyavarma|Satyavarma]] served as a minister of [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]].
(026-138)

## Relationships

- **Father:** [[satyavarma|Satyavarma]], minister of [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]] (026-138)
- **Wife:** Vâmalochana, daughter of [[viraketu|Víraketu]] (026-154)
- **Leader:** [[Râjavâhana]], hero of the ten princes (003-173)
- **Companions:** [[Pushpodbhava]], [[Dhanamittra]], and the other princes (007-190)
