# Alliance in the Tales

## Summary

An alliance in the *Adventures of the Ten Princes* is a deliberate pact between parties who bind their interests together—typically to gain assistance in a conflict and typically sealed by a betrothal. The tales treat it as a political instrument distinct from both the marriage that ratifies it and the feud that motivates it.

## Context

The clearest instance is the pact between Vírasekhara and Darpasárá. Vírasekhara, a Vidyâdhara of partly human descent, "thinking he might get assistance from him in a feud in which he was involved, had made an alliance with him, and engaged to marry his sister, the Princess [[avantisundari|Avantisundari]]" (007-120). The alliance here folds three acts into one: the seeking of aid, the forging of the pact, and the betrothal that guarantees it.

> "Thinking he might get assistance from him in a feud in which he was involved, had made an alliance with him, and engaged to marry his sister, the Princess Avantisundari."
> (007-120)

Alliance in the tales is thus the political hinge between [[battle-in-the-tales|Battle]] and [[marriage-in-the-tales|Marriage]]—the means by which a feud is supplied with an ally and a marriage is supplied with a motive.

## Key Participants

- Vírasekhara — the Vidyâdhara who seeks an ally to assist him in a feud (007-120)
- Darpasárá — the penitent with whom the alliance is struck, and whose sister is betrothed as its seal (007-120); see [[marriage-in-the-tales|Marriage in the Tales]]
- Princess [[avantisundari|Avantisundari]] — whose betrothal ratifies the alliance (007-120)
