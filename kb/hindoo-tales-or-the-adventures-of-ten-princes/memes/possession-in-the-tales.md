# Possession in the Tales

## Summary

Possession in the *Adventures of the Ten Princes* is the supernatural event in which an evil spirit or demon enters and takes hold of a mortal—most often a woman—driving her to rage and frenzy. The tales treat it as both a punitive curse and a dramatic obstacle to marriage, distinct from the casting of spells and from divine visitations.

## Context

Possession appears first as a divine punishment. The fairy who abandoned her husband [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]] confesses that "for that sin I was cursed by [[durga|Durgâ]], who condemned me to be possessed by an evil spirit for a year" (016-087). Here possession is the instrument of a curse—a measured, time-bound affliction rather than a permanent condition.

In the embedded tale of the twenty-first and twenty-second chapters, possession becomes the central dramatic impediment. A king wishes to marry a lady, but "the lady has suddenly become possessed by an evil spirit, whose rage is greatest whenever the king visits her" (021-162). The affliction is parasitic and reactive—the demon's fury spikes in the presence of the beloved—so that desire itself becomes the trigger of suffering.

> "The lady has suddenly become possessed by an evil spirit, whose rage is greatest whenever the king visits her."
> (021-162)

The condition demands exorcism by stratagem rather than ritual alone. The adviser explains that "the evil spirit by whom the princess is possessed cannot bear the sight of you in your present form" (022-054), and proposes a change of body as the means of driving the demon out—possession here being counteracted by a further supernatural transformation.

## Key Participants

- The cursed fairy — condemned by [[durga|Durgâ]] to be "possessed by an evil spirit for a year" as punishment for abandoning [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]] (016-087); see [[forgiveness-in-the-tales|Forgiveness in the Tales]]
- The possessed princess — whose sudden affliction obstructs the king's marriage, her rage greatest in his presence (021-162); see [[marriage-in-the-tales|Marriage in the Tales]]
- The adviser — who diagnoses the demon's weakness and prescribes a bodily transformation to expel it (022-054); see [[spell-in-the-tales|Spell in the Tales]]
