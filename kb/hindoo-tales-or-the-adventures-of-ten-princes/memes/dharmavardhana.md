# Dharmavardhana

Dharmavardhana was the King of the Sravastans, whose character was said to
correspond with his name — meaning "protector of righteousness." He is the
father of a beautiful daughter, who becomes the object of the narrator's
affection in Chapter 17.

## Biography

Dharmavardhana was the ruler of the Sravastans. His name, meaning
"protector of righteousness," was said to match his virtuous character.
(026-076)

> "The King of the Sravastans, Dharmavardhana, whose character corresponds
> with his name."
> (017-025) (017-026)

The King had a very beautiful daughter. The narrator of Chapter 17 had,
by an extraordinary chance, seen and fallen in love with her, and sought
assistance to contrive a meeting between them.
(017-026) (017-029)

> "The King of the Sravastans, Dharmavardhana, has a very beautiful
> daughter. By an extraordinary chance, I have seen and fallen in love
> with her."
> (017-026) (017-028)

## Role in the Narrative

Dharmavardhana appears in Chapter 17 within the narrative of
[[mantragupta|Mantragupta]]'s adventures. He serves primarily as the
father of the princess with whom the narrator falls in love, setting
the stage for [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]]'s schemes to win her hand.
