# Accusation in the Tales

## Summary

False accusation appears repeatedly in the *Tales of Ten Princes* as a mechanism of plot and character testing. Rivals and enemies bring fabricated charges against protagonists, creating crises that must be resolved through wit, evidence, or the intervention of truth-tellers. The motif underscores the vulnerability of reputation in a world of political intrigue.

## Context

In the courtly and mercantile settings of the tales, reputation is currency, and a false accusation can destroy a life. The tales depict accusation as a tool of the cunning — wielded by antagonists like [[kantaka|Kantaka]] — but also as a crucible that reveals the true character of the accused.

> [011-122] false accusation made by Kantaka.

> [013-122] to bring a false accusation against him, and by that mean…

> [020-055] This woman's accusation is altogether false; but if y…

## Key Participants

- [[kantaka|Kantaka]] — brings false accusation against a protagonist
- The accused — those who must defend themselves against fabricated charges
