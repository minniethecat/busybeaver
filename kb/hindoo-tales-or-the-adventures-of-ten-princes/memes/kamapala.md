# Kâmapâla

Kâmapâla was the son of one of [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]]'s ministers, and the father of
[[arthapala|Arthapâla]], who became one of the nine companions of Prince [[Râjavâhana]].
(001-106) (026-047)

## Biography

Kâmapâla was one of three sons born to the first of [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]]'s ministers,
along with his brothers [[sumantra|Sumantra]] and [[sumittra|Sumittra]].
(001-106)

> "The first of these had three sons, Sumantra, Sumittra, and Kâmapâla."
> (001-106)

Unlike his brothers, Kâmapâla was described as having an opposite character —
he thought only of present pleasures rather than future consequences.
(001-113)

He married Yaksha, the daughter of [[manibhadra|Manibhadra]], through an arrangement that
surprised the king.
(002-142) (002-148)

Kâmapâla rose to become the lord of [[benares|Benâres]], a position of considerable
power and influence.
(007-184)

## Role in the Narrative

Kâmapâla's story takes a dramatic turn in the later chapters, where he is
portrayed as a complex and ultimately tragic figure. Described as wise and
prudent, he nevertheless endeavoured secretly to excite prejudice against
the ruling authority and intended to make himself king.
(014-149) (014-154)

> "This Kâmapâla intends to make himself king; he..."
> (014-154)

His ambitions led to a public denunciation:

> "Know all men that this traitor, Kâmapâla, has not only poisoned the..."
> (015-042)

After his death, his body was permitted to be taken to his own house.
(015-074)

His story concludes with the note that his son [[arthapala|Arthapâla]] was nevertheless
counted among the nine companions of [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]].
(026-047)

## Relationships

Kâmapâla was the son of the first minister of [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]] and the father of
[[arthapala|Arthapâla]], one of the nine companions of Prince [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]]. He was married
to Yaksha, daughter of [[manibhadra|Manibhadra]]. His brothers were [[sumantra|Sumantra]] and [[sumittra|Sumittra]].
(001-106) (002-142) (026-047)

He served alongside [[visruta|Visruta]] and [[praharavarma|Prahâravarma]] in his official capacity as
lord of [[benares|Benâres]].
(007-184)
