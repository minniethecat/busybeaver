# Dhanamittra

Dhanamittra is one of the nine companions of [[Râjavâhana]] and a central figure
in the tales of the ten princes. The son of a very rich man, he is renowned for
his cleverness, his resourcefulness in adversity, and his possession of a magic
purse that figures prominently in his adventures.
(007-173)

## Biography

Dhanamittra was the son of a wealthy merchant, raised in comfort and prosperity.
He was one of the childhood companions of [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]], educated alongside the
other princes and trained in the arts and sciences befitting a young nobleman.
(009-130)

In his youth, Dhanamittra became engaged to the daughter of another rich man,
a match arranged between their families. However, fate intervened, and he was
swept into the great dispersal of the princes that scattered the companions
across the land.
(009-045)

During his wanderings, Dhanamittra acquired a magic purse — a wondrous object
that would replenish itself with wealth, providing him with unlimited resources.
This purse became both his greatest asset and a source of peril, drawing the
envy and machinations of those who learned of its power.
(010-041)

> "Dhanamittra, the son of a very rich man. During my prosperity, I was..."
> (009-130)

## Role in the Narrative

Dhanamittra's tale exemplifies the theme of cleverness triumphing over adversity.
When his magic purse attracted the covetous attention of a powerful king, he
faced imprisonment and the threat of execution. Rather than succumbing to
despair, he employed wit and stratagem to turn the situation to his advantage.
(010-047) (010-058)

> "Then Dhanamittra went to the king, and declaring..."
> (010-058)

His adventures involve a web of intrigue: a lady he encounters, a jealous master
who seeks to destroy him, and the loyal friend who stands by him. Throughout,
Dhanamittra demonstrates the qualities of patience, quick thinking, and
eloquence that mark him as one of the most resourceful of the ten princes.
(009-172) (009-179)

> "After this, Dhanamittra, making no secret of his acquisition..."
> (009-161)

At the re[[marriage-in-the-tales|union]] of the companions, Dhanamittra was warmly received. [[somadatta|Somadatta]]
himself introduced him, saying "This is my dear friend Dhanamittra, well worthy
of your respect," a testimony to the esteem in which he was held.
(007-173)

Ultimately, through his intercession and diplomacy, Dhanamittra not only
recovered his magic purse but also secured the release and reconciliation of
those who had wronged him — a demonstration of the magnanimity that
distinguished him from lesser men.
(010-100) (010-106)

> (010-100)
