# Kandukavati

Kandukavati was a princess of [[mahishmati|Mahishmati]], the daughter of the king and sister
of [[Bhimadhanwa]], who performed the ball-dance festival to propitiate the
goddess [[durga|Durgâ]].

## Biography

Kandukavati was the princess of [[mahishmati|Mahishmati]], and on the day of the festival,
she came forth to propitiate the goddess [[durga|Durgâ]] through a ceremonial dance
with a ball.
(017-152)

> "To-day the princess, whose name is Kandukavati, will perform the dance
> with the ball in honour of the goddess Durgâ."
> (017-152)

Princess Kandukavati had a dear friend and foster-sister, [[chandrasena|Chandrasena]], who
was her constant companion and confidante within the palace.
(017-156)

When she was reunited with those who knew of her, Kandukavati was equally
[[pleasure|delight]]ed on finding who was before her, acknowledging the bonds that
connected the characters across their separate paths.
(021-004)

> "Kandukavati, who was equally delighted on finding who was her visitor,
> received him with great honour."
> (021-004)

She was described as a damsel who came to propitiate the goddess having
performed her vows, a figure of devotion and grace in the ceremonial life
of the kingdom.
(025-099)

> "A damsel, called Kandukavati, will to-day propitiate the goddess having
> performed her vow."
> (025-099)

The character index records her as the princess who performed the ball-dance.
(026-092)

## Role in the Narrative

Kandukavati's primary role in the narrative is as the central figure of the
ball-dance festival, an event that brings together multiple characters at the
temple of [[durga|Durgâ]]. The festival creates the setting for important encounters
and developments.
(025-099)

She is connected to the broader network of princes and nobles through her
brother **[[Bhimadhanwa]]** and her foster-sister **[[Chandrasena]]**. Her
father, the King of [[mahishmati|Mahishmati]], sends her to propitiate [[durga|Durgâ]], indicating
her role in the religious and ceremonial life of the kingdom.
(025-131)

> "My lord's daughter Kandukavati is come to propitiate Durgâ with dancing
> and music."
> (025-131)

Her re[[marriage-in-the-tales|union]] with characters who had known her earlier ties her story to the
wider narrative of the ten princes and their associates.
(021-004)

## Relationships

Kandukavati was the daughter of the **King of [[mahishmati|Mahishmati]]** and sister of
**[[Bhimadhanwa]]**, connecting her to the royal house.
(026-060)

Her closest companion was her foster-sister **[[Chandrasena]]**, who served as
her confidante and intermediary in palace affairs.
(017-156) (026-071)

She was associated with the worship of the goddess **[[Durgâ]]**, for whom she
performed the ceremonial ball-dance.
(025-099)
