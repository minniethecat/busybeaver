# Assembly in the Tales

## Summary

The public assembly in the *Adventures of the Ten Princes* is the arena in which disputes are aired, worth is judged, and conspiracies are staged. Far from mere background, the assembly is the occasion for rivalry, insult, and even murder.

## Context

Assemblies host the tales' staged rivalries. "One day, in a large assembly, having got up a dispute between us, they said: 'It is not beauty or wealth, but the approbation of the ladies, which stamps the worth of a man'" (008-162). The same device serves deliberate provocation: "[[vimardaka|Vimardaka]], by my directions, in a large assembly, began to abuse and insult [[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]]" (010-046), staging a public quarrel to advance a plot.

> "One day, in a large assembly, having got up a dispute between us, they said: 'It is not beauty or wealth, but the approbation of the ladies, which stamps the worth of a man.'"
> (008-162)

Most darkly, the assembly furnishes the setting for an assassination disguised as a rite: "On the appointed day, in the presence of a numerous assembly, she took a small leafy branch, and dipping it in what appeared to be water, but which really contained a deadly poison, struck him gently" (024-097). The crowd that legitimises the assembly thus becomes the unwitting witness to crime.

## Key Participants

- [[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]] — the target of the staged assembly insult (010-046)
- [[vimardaka|Vimardaka]] — who engineers the public quarrel by direction (010-046)
- The poisoner — who strikes her victim before "a numerous assembly" (024-097)
