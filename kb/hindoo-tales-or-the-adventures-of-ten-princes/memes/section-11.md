[011-001]"Meanwhile, with the help of gifts furnished by my mistress, I have

[011-002]formed an intimacy with the nurse of the Princess Ambâlika, and have

[011-003]been introduced by her to the princess, whose favour I have gained by

[011-004]telling her amusing stories, and whom I have induced to feel an

[011-005]interest in the misfortune of my mistress.

[011-006]"One day, when I was standing near her in the gallery round the

[011-007]court-yard of the palace, Kantaka, having some business or other,

[011-008]passed through below us. Picking up a flower which the princess had

[011-009]dropped, I let it fall on his head; and when he looked up to see from

[011-010]whose hand it came, I managed to make the princess laugh at something

[011-011]which I said; and the conceited fool, thinking that it was she who had

[011-012]dropped it to attract his attention, went away looking quite pleased

[011-013]and confused.

[011-014]"That same evening I received a present for my mistress, a small

[011-015]basket marked with the signet of the princess, and containing articles

[011-016]of no great value. This I took to Kantaka; and begging him to observe

[011-017]the strictest secrecy, made him believe that the princess had sent it

[011-018]to him. He was even delighted when, another day, I brought him a dirty

[011-019]dress, telling him that she had worn it.

[011-020]"Finding him quite ready to believe this, and convinced that she was

[011-021]in love with him, I kept up an imaginary correspondence, bringing

[011-022]very loving messages from her, which I invented, and receiving many

[011-023]from him in return, which I took care not to deliver. His presents, of

[011-024]course, I kept for myself.

[011-025]"In this manner I have raised his hopes very high; and to encourage

[011-026]him still further, I said: 'I have heard from a learned astrologer,

[011-027]with whom I am acquainted, that you have certain marks upon you which

[011-028]indicate that you will one day be a king. This love on the part of the

[011-029]princess tends to the fulfilment of the prediction. You are therefore

[011-030]on the high road to fortune. If you have spirit enough to pursue it,

[011-031]all you have to do now is to obtain a secret interview with the lady;

[011-032]the rest will follow in due time.'

[011-033]"'But how can I manage this?' he asked. 'The wall of the garden,' I

[011-034]replied, 'communicating with the princess's apartments, is separated

[011-035]from those of the gaol by a space of a few yards only. You could not

[011-036]get over these walls; but you might make an underground passage, and

[011-037]slip in unobserved; and I will take care that there shall be some one

[011-038]to receive and conduct you to the princess. When once with her, you

[011-039]are safe; for all her attendants are attached to her; not one would

[011-040]betray the secret.'

[011-041]"'But how can I make this underground passage?' he asked. 'I cannot

[011-042]dig it myself, or employ workmen.'

[011-043]"'Have you no clever thief here,' I replied, 'accustomed to such

[011-044]work?'

[011-045]"'Well suggested,' he answered. 'I have just the right man.'

[011-046]"'Who is he?' I said.

[011-047]"'That man who has stolen the magic purse,' said he. 'If he will set

[011-048]to work with a good will he will soon dig his way through.'

[011-049]"'Very good,' I answered. 'You must persuade him by promising to let

[011-050]him go when the work is done. But it would never do for him to be in

[011-051]the secret; therefore, when he has finished, put on his fetters again,

[011-052]and report to the king that he is exceedingly obstinate; that you have

[011-053]tried all other means to make him confess, and that nothing remains

[011-054]but to put him to torture. No doubt the king will give orders

[011-055]accordingly; and you can easily manage so to inflict it that he shall

[011-056]die under it. When he is dead, your secret will be safe; you can visit

[011-057]the princess as often as you like; and, doubtless, in the end the

[011-058]king, rather than disgrace his daughter, will consent to your

[011-059]marriage; and as he has no other child, will make you his successor.'

[011-060]"With this proposal he was quite delighted; and has been treating you

[011-061]well, that you may have strength for the work. He intends to ask you

[011-062]to begin to-night; and has sent me to persuade you, believing me to be

[011-063]devoted to his interests, and looking forward to some great reward

[011-064]when he has got his wish."

[011-065]Having heard this from the old woman, I gave her great praise, and

[011-066]said: "Lose no time. Tell him I am quite ready to do the work."

[011-067]After this, Kantaka came to me, told me what he wanted, and swore a

[011-068]solemn oath that I should be liberated when the work was done; and I,

[011-069]in return, swore to keep his secret.

[011-070]Then he took off my fetters; I got a bath and a good dinner, and

[011-071]presently set to work in a dark corner, under the wall. Soon after

[011-072]midnight the work was done, and an opening made into the courtyard of

[011-073]the women's apartments.

[011-074]Before returning, I thought to myself "This man has sworn an oath

[011-075]which he intends to break: for the preservation of my own life,

[011-076]therefore, I shall be justified in killing him."

[011-077]Having formed this resolution, I went back to the prison, where

[011-078]Kantaka was waiting for me. He told me it was necessary to replace my

[011-079]fetters for the present; and I appeared to acquiesce. But as he was

[011-080]stooping to fasten them, I gave him a violent kick; and before he

[011-081]could recover himself, I had snatched a short sword which he wore, and

[011-082]cut off his head.

[011-083]I then returned to Sringâlika, who had remained in the prison, and

[011-084]said to her: "I am not disposed to have had all this toil for nothing.

[011-085]Tell me the way into the ladies' rooms. I will go there and steal

[011-086]something before I make my escape."

[011-087]Having received her directions, I passed again through the tunnel

[011-088]which I had made, came up into the court-yard; and from thence entered

[011-089]a large, lofty room lighted by jewelled lamps, where a number of women

[011-090]were sleeping.

[011-091]There, on a couch ornamented with beautifully carved flowers and

[011-092]resting on lions' feet, I saw the princess, covered only by a thin

[011-093]silken petticoat, half sunk into a soft white feather-bed, like

[011-094]lightning on an autumn cloud.

[011-095]Fast asleep, as if wearied by much play, she lay in a very graceful

[011-096]attitude, with her delicate ancles crossed, her knees slightly drawn

[011-097]up; one lovely hand laid loosely on her side, the other beneath her

[011-098]head; her full bosom, slowly heaved by gentle breathing, illuminated

[011-099]by the ruby necklace strung on burnished gold; the top-knot of her

[011-100]loosened hair hanging down like some graceful flower; her lips so

[011-101]bright that the opening of the mouth could hardly be distinguished;

[011-102]her features in calm repose, shaded by her lovely ringlets.

[011-103]I had entered so softly that no one was disturbed; and I stood gazing

[011-104]for some time lost in admiration of her beauty, quite forgetting the

[011-105]purpose for which I had come.

[011-106]I thought, she is, after all, the lady of my heart. If I do not obtain

[011-107]her, Kâma will not suffer me to live; but how can I make known my love

[011-108]to her? Were I now to wake her, she would start up with a cry of

[011-109]alarm, and I should probably lose my life. I must think of some other

[011-110]way of letting her know my love.

[011-111]Then, looking round, I saw laid on a shelf a thin board prepared for

[011-112]painting, and a box of paints and brushes. With these I made a hasty

[011-113]sketch of the princess as she lay, and of myself kneeling at her feet,

[011-114]and underneath it I wrote this verse:--

[011-115]"Of thee thy slave in humble attitude thus prays:

[011-116]Sleep on, not worn like me by pervading love."

[011-117]I then painted on the wall near her a pair of chakravâkas in loving

[011-118]attitude, gently took off her ring, replacing it with mine, and

[011-119]slipped out without disturbing any of the sleepers.

[011-120]There was at that time among the prisoners a man named Sinhaghosha,

[011-121]formerly a chief officer of police, but now imprisoned through a

[011-122]false accusation made by Kantaka.

[011-123]With this man I had already made acquaintance, and I now went to him

[011-124]and told him how I had killed Kantaka. With his consent I went forth

[011-125]from the prison, and walked away with Sringâlika. We had not gone far

[011-126]when we fell in with a patrol. I thought to myself I could easily run

[011-127]away from them; but what would become of the poor old woman? she would

[011-128]certainly be caught. Hastily determining, therefore, on what was best

[011-129]to be done, I walked right up to them with unsteady gait and idiotic

[011-130]look, and said: "Sirs, if I am a thief kill me, but you have no right

[011-131]to touch this old woman."

[011-132]She, perceiving my intention, came up, and very humbly said: "Honoured

[011-133]sirs, this young man is my son. He has been for some time confined as

[011-134]a lunatic; but was supposed to be cured, and I brought him home

[011-135]yesterday. In the middle of the night, however, he started up, and

[011-136]calling out: 'I will kill Kantaka and make love to the king's

[011-137]daughter,' rushed out into the street. I have at last overtaken him,

[011-138]and am trying to take him home. Will you be so good as to help me, and

[011-139]tie his hands behind him that he may not get away again?"

[011-140]As she said this, I called out: "O old woman, who ever bound a god or

[011-141]the wind, Shall these crows catch an eagle?" and started off at full

[011-142]speed. She, renewing her entreaties, begged them to pursue me; but

[011-143]they only laughed at her, and said: "Do you think we have nothing to

[011-144]do but to run after madmen? You must be as mad as he is to have taken

[011-145]him out;" and so they went on their way.

[011-146]I stopped when I found I was not pursued. She soon overtook me, and

[011-147]we went to my house, to the great joy of my wife, who had scarcely

[011-148]hoped for my deliverance.

[011-149]In the morning I saw Dhanamittra, told him all that had happened, and

[011-150]thanked him for following my directions so punctually.

[011-151]After this I went to the forest, to see Mâríchi. I found him restored

[011-152]to his former condition, and able to give me the desired information.

[011-153]From him I learnt that you would be here about this time.

[011-154]In the morning after my escape, Sinhaghosha informed the king of what

[011-155]had happened, and how Kantaka had been killed when about to enter the

[011-156]princess's apartments. Being found to be innocent of the crime of

[011-157]which he was accused, he was appointed governor of the prison in

[011-158]Kantaka's place.

[011-159]Before the underground passage was filled up, he permitted me to pass

[011-160]through it more than once to the princess, who was favourably disposed

[011-161]towards me through the picture and verse, and still more by all that

[011-162]Sringâlika had said in my favour.

[011-163]No great search was made after me, and by keeping quiet and going out

[011-164]only at night I escaped further arrest.

[011-165]You know how Chandavarma besieged Champa, and how Sinhavarma was

[011-166]defeated and taken prisoner. When I heard this, and how the conqueror

[011-167]intended to force the princess to marry him, I went to Dhanamittra and

[011-168]said: "Do you go about among the ministers and officers of the

[011-169]imprisoned king and the principal citizens, and tell them to be ready

[011-170]to attack the enemy as soon as they hear of the death of Chandavarma.

[011-171]I will engage to kill him to-morrow."

[011-172]How Dhanamittra has performed his part you have just seen. As to

[011-173]myself, I put on a dress suitable for the occasion, and, as many

[011-174]persons were going in and out of the palace, managed to slip in

[011-175]unobserved and get very near the intending bridegroom. Suddenly

[011-176]stretching out my arm as he was about to take the hand of the

[011-177]princess, I gave him a mortal wound with a sword; then saying a few

[011-178]hasty words of encouragement to her, I defended myself against those

[011-179]who endeavoured to seize me, till I heard your welcome voice, deep as

[011-180]the sound of thunder, and had the happiness of embracing you.
