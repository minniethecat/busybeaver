## Biography

Chandasinha was a king, the maternal grandfather of a young princess. (015-139) His eldest son died before him, leaving a wife pregnant with a daughter. The wife died in childbirth, and the infant girl was committed to the care of a nurse. The king intended for the child, when grown, to be given in marriage. (015-139)

## Role

Chandasinha appears in the narrative through his familial connections — his granddaughter's lineage traces back to him, establishing a royal pedigree for a character in the story of Prince [[arthapala|Arthapâla]]. (015-139)

## Quotations

> Whom you have just seen is the granddaughter of your maternal grandfather, Chandasinha. The eldest son of that king died before his father, leaving his wife pregnant, and she lost her life in giving birth to this daughter. (015-139)
