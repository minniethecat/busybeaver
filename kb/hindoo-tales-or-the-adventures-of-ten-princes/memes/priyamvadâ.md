# Priyamvadâ

Priyamvadâ was the Queen of [[Prahâravarma]] and the mother of sons whose
loss and the subsequent marriage engagement form part of the narrative of
the twelfth chapter.
(012-146) (026-127)

## Biography

Priyamvadâ was the queen consort of [[praharavarma|Prahâravarma]]. She was praised as a
fortunate mother, but later suffered the loss of her sons, a tragedy that
interrupted a marriage engagement already under way.
(012-069) (012-146)

> "Engaged for marriage; but when the Queen Priyamvada had lost her sons,
> the matter was for the time relinquished."
> (012-146)

Her story is closely tied to the reign of [[praharavarma|Prahâravarma]], whose adventures
form the twelfth chapter of the narrative.
(012-069) (026-127)

## Role in the Narrative

Queen Priyamvadâ appears in the twelfth chapter as the consort of
[[praharavarma|Prahâravarma]]. Her personal tragedy — the loss of her sons — caused a
marriage engagement to be temporarily abandoned, reflecting the
interweaving of personal and political fortunes in the tales.
(012-069) (012-146)
