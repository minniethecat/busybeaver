# Madhura

Madhura is a city in ancient India, mentioned in the twentieth chapter of
the *Daśakumāracarita* as the dwelling-place of
[[kalahakantaka|Kalahakantaka]], a man of great strength and violent
disposition.
(020-068)

## Geography

The text describes Madhura simply as a city (*nagara*), giving no
geographical details of its location, size, or surrounding region. It
serves as an urban backdrop for a tale of desire and seduction.
(020-068) (020-069) (020-070)

> "In a city called Madhura, there dwelt a man named Kalahakantaka, of
> great strength and vigour, ready at any time to take up the quarrel of
> a friend, famed for deeds of violence, and devoted to pleasures and
> enjoyments of every kind."
> (020-068) (020-069) (020-070)

## Role in the Narrative

Madhura is the setting for the story of [[nitambavati|Nitambavati]], told by
[[mantragupta|Mantragupta]] to the Rakshas. It is here that
[[kalahakantaka|Kalahakantaka]] sees a portrait of the beautiful
[[nitambavati|Nitambavati]] and becomes consumed with desire for her.
(020-068) (020-072) (020-073)
