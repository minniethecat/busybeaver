# Vindhya

The Vindhya is a mountain range and forested region in central India, prominent
in the *Daśakumāracarita* as a wilderness setting for encounters, refuge, and
divine presence. It is the abode of the goddess [[durga|Durgâ]], who is known as
[[durga|Vindhyavâsiní]] — the dweller of the Vindhya.
(001-176) (025-087)

## Description

The Vindhya mountains form one of the major geographical features of the
narrative's landscape. The forest of Vindhya serves as a place of concealment
and passage for the wandering princes. It contained a strong fortress, guarded
and hidden within its dense woods. (001-176)

> "a strong fortress in the forest of Vindhya, guarded by"
> (001-176)

The princes entered the forest of Vindhya during their wanderings, finding it
a place of both danger and refuge. The dense wilderness provided cover for
travellers and exiles, while also harbouring threats. (003-040)

> "they entered the forest of Vindhya"
> (003-040)

The region is most closely associated with the goddess [[durga|Durgâ]], who bears the
epithet [[durga|Vindhyavâsiní]] — "the inhabitant of Vindhya." Her presence sanctified
the mountains and drew devotees into the forest seeking her favour.
(025-087) (025-088)

> "from the feet of Durgâ, called Vindhyavâsiní"
> (025-087)

> "her love for the abode in Vindhya"
> (025-088)

## Significance in the Narrative

The Vindhya forest is the setting for a significant encounter in Chapter 22,
where a traveller meets another figure while roaming the woods. These forest
encounters often become turning points in the individual adventures of the
princes, leading to new alliances, quests, or revelations.
(022-159)

> "wandering one day in the forest of Vindhya, I met"
> (022-159)

The Vindhya region also features in the broader mythological and geographical
framework of the narrative. As the home of [[durga|Durgâ]], it links the worldly
adventures of the princes to the divine realm, reinforcing the theme of
celestial favour that runs through the story.
(025-087) (025-088) (026-194)

## Appearances

The Vindhya appears in Chapters 1, 3, 22, and 25, spanning the full arc of the
narrative. It is referenced in the proper names index of Chapter 26, where the
footnote explains the epithet [[durga|Vindhyavâsiní]].
