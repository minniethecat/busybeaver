# Beauty

## Definition

Beauty in the Hindoo Tales is a highly valorised aesthetic quality attributed primarily to persons — both male and female — and treated as a mark of distinction, divine favour, and narrative importance. Characters of exceptional beauty are singled out for extended description, and beauty consistently functions as a driver of romantic attraction, social admiration, and even metaphysical transformation.

## In the Narrative

Beauty is established early as a defining attribute of significant characters. The queen's beauty is described as so great that it seemed beyond mortal measure (001-098). A prince is seen as perfect in beauty, strength, and accomplishments (003-030). A supernatural transformation produces a new body full of youth and beauty (003-131). The great beauty of [[vâmalochanâ|Vâmalochanâ]] drives a marriage quest (004-012). Extended descriptions of female beauty appear throughout Chapter 5 — a princess of grace and beauty (005-163), exceeding beauty that seems divinely formed (005-167), beauty that [[pleasure|delight]]s the eyes (005-181). Beauty is also attributed to males: a character's beauty is admired by a woman who becomes enamoured (007-009). The consistent pattern is that beauty signals narrative importance: the beautiful are those whose stories the text wishes to tell.

> "… temper, and so great was her beauty that it seemed as…" (001-098)

> "Seeing the prince perfect in beauty, strength, and accomplishment…" (003-030)

> "her exceeding beauty. She seemed to him as if form…" (005-167)

> "… eyes are delighted with such beauty! happy the mother who has…" (005-181)
