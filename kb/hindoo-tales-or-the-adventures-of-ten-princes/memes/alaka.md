# Alaka

Alaka is a mythical mountain mentioned in the index of proper names of the
*Daśakumāracarita* as the dwelling-place of [[kuvera|Kuvera]], the god of
wealth, and his attendants, the Yakshas.
(026-039)

## Geography

In Hindu mythology, Alaka (also known as Alakâpurî) is the celestial city
and mountain abode of [[kuvera|Kuvera]]. It is situated in the [[himalaya|Himâlaya]]
range, near [[kailasa|Kailâsa]], and is described as a place of splendour
and riches, fitting for the lord of treasures and his semi-divine retinue.
(026-039)

> "_Alaka_, a mountain inhabited by Kuvera and the Yakshas."
> (026-039)

## Role in the Narrative

Alaka does not appear directly in the narrative action of the
*Daśakumāracarita*; it is recorded only in the glossary of proper names
appended to the tales. Its presence in the index, however, attests to the
text's deep grounding in the mythological geography of classical India.
The Yakshas who dwell on Alaka are semi-divine nature-spirits associated
with wealth and fertility, often appearing in Sanskrit literature as
guardians of hidden treasures.
(026-039)
