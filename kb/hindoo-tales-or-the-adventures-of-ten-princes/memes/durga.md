# Durgâ

Durgâ (also known as Kâli) is a powerful goddess in the narrative, described
as the wife of [[siva]] and a deity who appears in visions to guide and protect
her devotees. She is associated with temples, ritual dances, and divine
intervention in the affairs of mortals.
(026-077)

## Biography

Durgâ is described in the character index as the wife of [[siva|Siva]], a terrific
goddess who [[pleasure|delight]]s in divine power.
(026-077)

[[worship|Worship]] of Durgâ features prominently throughout the narrative. Her temples
serve as sites of important encounters, including a small temple where a party
of travellers gathered.
(002-062)

Devotees made prayers and offerings to the goddess, seeking her favour and
intervention in their affairs.
(017-138)

> "The goddess Durgâ appeared to me in a vision, and said: 'Your child is...'"
> (024-117)

The goddess's power extended to appointing guardians and protectors. A young
king was appointed under her divine authority, and she declared she would
protect him as her own son.
(025-022) (025-028)

> "Power of the glorious Durgâ! happy the country of which you are the..."
> (025-030)

## Role in the Narrative

Durgâ serves as a divine intervener at critical moments in the story. She
appears in visions to queens and devotees, curses sinners, and rewards the
faithful. Her power is invoked to legitimize royal authority and to protect
the innocent.

When a queen went secretly to the Temple of Durgâ, her prayers were answered
with a vision and a promise.
(024-168)

The goddess also executed divine justice, cursing a sinner to be possessed
by an evil spirit as punishment for transgression.
(016-087)

> "According to the word of Durgâ, the princess is to be allowed free..."
> (018-064)

Her worship included the performance of sacred ball dances for her
propitiation, showing the ritual devotion she commanded.
(017-153)

## Relationships

Durgâ is the wife of [[siva|Siva]] and is also identified with Kâli. She is worshipped
by multiple characters across the narrative, including queens seeking children
and those seeking protection. Her divine authority extends over mortals, whom
she may curse or bless according to their deeds.
(026-077)
