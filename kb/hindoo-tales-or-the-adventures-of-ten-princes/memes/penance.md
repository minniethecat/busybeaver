# Penance

## Definition

Penance (Tapas) is a fundamental religious practice in the Hindoo world — a form of severe self-discipline, austerity, and bodily mortification undertaken to propitiate the gods, acquire spiritual power, or atone for wrongdoing. The belief in the power of penance to confer supernatural boons and alter one's destiny is pervasive throughout the narrative.

## In the Narrative

The power of penance is established from the very beginning of the tales, where it is described as a force capable of conferring extraordinary spiritual authority. Characters throughout the narrative resort to penance at critical moments: [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]] undertakes severe penance to propitiate the mighty [[siva|Siva]] for a son (001-159); a character is advised to perform penance as the path to accomplishing his desires (002-014); and holy men practise lifelong penance in the forests (003-087, 022-123). The practice appears across all social levels — from kings to wandering ascetics — and across the full span of the narrative, making it one of the most consistently invoked religious concepts in the text.

> "The belief in the power of penance, which was supposed to confer…" (001-029)

> "propitiate with very severe penance the mighty Siva, whose temple…" (001-159)

> "…O friend, there is no need of penance in your…" (002-022)

> "have lived a life of penance and meditation in this forest…" (003-087)
