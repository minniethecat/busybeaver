# Disguise

## Definition

Disguise is one of the most pervasive narrative devices in the Hindoo Tales — the deliberate concealment or transformation of identity through altered appearance, costume, or assumed role. Characters resort to disguise to escape danger, to approach otherwise inaccessible persons or places, to test the loyalty of others, and to execute elaborate plans. The frequency and variety of disguises across the narrative make the device a structural feature of the storytelling.

## In the Narrative

Disguise is employed from the opening chapters. [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]] adopts a safe disguise to move unrecognised among his enemies (001-154). A prince accompanies another disguised as a woman to gain access to a closely guarded space (005-077), with careful preparation of all things required for the disguise (005-099). A piece of acting serves as cover for a deeper deception (006-176). The prince staying in disguise is recognised by those who know him (012-132). Disguise as a mendicant enables passage and information-gathering (020-081), while a quick decision on the disguise to be adopted drives a pivotal scene (021-170). Across seven chapters, disguise consistently serves as the mechanism through which characters navigate the gap between appearance and reality.

> "…ar, for I will accompany you, disguised as a woman," (005-077)

> "…ce everything required for my disguise. At the proper time," (005-099)

> "…there, having disguised himself as a mendicant, got …" (020-081)
