# Grihagupta

**Grihagupta** is the father of [[Balabhadra]]'s lawful wife, a resident of [[valabhi|Valabhi]], mentioned during the legal proceedings in [[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]]'s narrative in Chapter 20.

## Biography

Grihagupta is a resident of [[valabhi|Valabhi]], where he raised his daughter. His daughter was married to [[balabhadra|Balabhadra]] "with the proper ceremony and with the full consent of her parents." (020-053–055) When [[balabhadra|Balabhadra]] faces a legal accusation, his wife invokes her father Grihagupta as a witness to her true identity and lawful marriage.

## Role in the Narrative

Grihagupta serves as a crucial reference point in [[balabhadra|Balabhadra]]'s legal defence. [[balabhadra|Balabhadra]]'s wife challenges her accusers to verify her identity by sending messengers to [[valabhi|Valabhi]] to consult her father Grihagupta, or to the town where [[balabhadra|Balabhadra]] formerly lived. (020-056–058) His name thus stands as a guarantor of truth and lawful marriage in the narrative.

> "She was married to me with the proper ceremony and with the full consent of her parents. This woman's accusation is altogether false; but if you will not believe my assertion, send to Valabhi, to my wife's father, and hear what he will say."
> (020-053–057)
