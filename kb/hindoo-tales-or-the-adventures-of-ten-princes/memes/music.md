# Music

## Definition

Music in the Hindoo Tales is a hallmark of refined education and courtly pleasure. It appears consistently as one of the accomplishments expected of princes and cultivated persons, alongside drawing, poetry, and dancing. The text treats musical skill as integral to the ideal of the well-rounded nobleman.

## In the Narrative

Prince [[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]] is described as "accomplished in drawing and music, skilled in" various arts (003-022), establishing music as part of the princely curriculum. A courtly entertainment catalogue lists "musical instruments, singing, painting" among the pleasures of a well-appointed life (008-041). King [[prachandavarma|Prachandavarma]] is "skilled in dancing, music, and singing; quick" in his accomplishments (023-032), and the narrator of his tale is invited to "enjoy drinking, music, and dancing" (023-122). A musical metaphor extends to archery: an arrow is loosed "in middling slow and quick musical time" (025-153), associating rhythm with martial skill.

> "…accomplished in drawing and music, skilled in…" (003-022)

> "…musical instruments, singing, painting…" (008-041)

> "…skilled in dancing, music, and singing; quick…" (023-032)

> "…enjoy drinking, music, and dancing, and trouble you…" (023-122)
