# Trigarta

Trigarta is a country in ancient India, named in the eighteenth chapter of
the *Daśakumāracarita* as the homeland of three wealthy brothers whose
harrowing story of famine and survival is recounted by
[[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]].
(018-166)

## Geography

Trigarta was an agricultural country whose prosperity depended on the
seasonal rains. The narrative describes it as a land of considerable
wealth — the three brothers possessed many wives, servants, slaves, and
numerous flocks and herds. However, this prosperity proved fragile: when
a drought struck and no rain fell for several years, the country was
utterly devastated. Streams and fountains ceased to flow, pools and lakes
turned to mud, plants burned up, and trees withered.
(018-166) (018-167) (018-169) (018-170) (018-171)

> "There were formerly in the country of Trigarta three brothers, all wealthy, having several wives, many servants and slaves, and numerous flocks and herds."
> (018-166) (018-167)

## Role in the Narrative

The story of Trigarta serves as one of the tales-within-tales that
[[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]] tells to prove a point about human nature under extreme
duress. The three brothers, once prosperous, were reduced by famine to
eating their slaves, their wives, and their children, until only the
three brothers and their three favourite wives remained. The tale
culminates in the escape of the youngest brother and his wife [[dhumini|Dhumini]],
who fled into a forest and survived — an illustration of how catastrophe
strips away civilisation and reveals the raw instinct for survival.
(018-175) (018-178) (018-182) (018-183)
