## Biography

Kâlayavana was the ruler of an island that bore his name. The narrator visited the island of Kâlayavana and waited on his daughter [[suvritta|Suvritta]]. (002-099) It was there that a young merchant named [[ratnodbhava|Ratnodbhava]], son of a minister, entered the narrative.

The island of Kâlayavana was also the destination of another voyager who, years earlier, travelled there and married a merchant's daughter. (004-131)

Kâlayavana's daughter [[suvritta|Suvritta]] became the wife of [[ratnodbhava|Ratnodbhava]]. While crossing the sea with her husband, a great storm arose, the ship sank, and the lady was cast adrift. (004-155)

## Role

Kâlayavana is a peripheral ruler figure whose island serves as a geographical waypoint in the travels of the princes and their companions. His significance lies primarily in being the father of [[suvritta|Suvritta]], whose marriage to [[ratnodbhava|Ratnodbhava]] connects the island to the broader narrative of the Ten Princes.
