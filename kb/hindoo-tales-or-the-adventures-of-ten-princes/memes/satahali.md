# Satahali

**Satahali** is the governor of a district, engaged by Prince [[Mantragupta]] to execute a scheme against a wealthy man named Anantasíra.

## Biography

Satahali served as governor of a district under the influence of [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]]. [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]], wishing to eliminate a wealthy and influential man named Anantasíra, engaged Satahali to bring a false accusation against him, intending to stir up the people and cause Anantasíra's death in a popular tumult. (013-121–123)

When the political situation later changed and [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]] became reconciled with his uncle, he sent for Satahali again and informed him that the scheme against Anantasíra was no longer necessary, cancelling the plan. (013-159–161)

> "I have engaged a certain person, named Satahali, the governor of the district, to bring a false accusation against him, and by that means to stir up the people, and so cause his death in a popular tumult." (013-121–123)

> "I sent also for Satahali, and said: 'You know that I wished to get rid of Anantasíra, because he was suspected of being a partisan of the deposed king. Now that I am reconciled to my uncle, there is no further need.'" (013-159–161)
