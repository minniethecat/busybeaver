# Brahma

Brahma is one of the three principal deities of the Hindu pantheon, named
alongside [[vishnu]] and [[siva]] as forming the divine triad.
(026-120)

## Biography

Brahma is invoked in the *Daśakumāracarita* as one of the three principal gods:

> "the three principal gods, Brahma, Vishnu, Siva."
> (026-120)

His role in the narrative is primarily as part of the divine framework that
underpins the religious world of the characters.

## Role in the Narrative

Brahma is referenced as part of the Hindu triad (trimurti) alongside [[vishnu|Vishnu]] and
[[siva|Siva]]. While he does not appear directly in visions or grant boons in the
narrative — unlike [[vishnu]] and [[siva]] — his presence as one of the three
principal gods establishes the theological framework of the text.

The narrative's references to divine beings reflect the religious culture of
ancient India, where the trimurti of Brahma (creator), [[vishnu|Vishnu]] (preserver), and
[[siva|Siva]] (destroyer) represented the cosmic functions.
