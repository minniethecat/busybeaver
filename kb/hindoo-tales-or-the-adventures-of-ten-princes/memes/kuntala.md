# Kuntala

Kuntala is a kingdom in ancient India, mentioned in the twenty-fourth
chapter of the *Daśakumāracarita*. The King of Kuntala was persuaded by
large donations to abandon a celebrated actress and dancer in favour of
joining a military alliance against [[malwa|Mâlwa]].
(024-009)

## Geography

The text identifies Kuntala as one of the nations (*deśa*) that bordered
or lay within reach of the kingdom of [[malwa|Mâlwa]]. Its king was among
the rulers recruited into the coalition assembled by the King of
[[asmaka|Asmaka]] for the invasion and plunder of [[malwa|Mâlwa]].
(024-009) (024-011) (024-012)

> "…persuaded her, by large donations, to leave the King of Kuntala, with
> whom she was a great favourite."
> (024-009)

## Role in the Narrative

Kuntala appears in the political-military summary of Chapter 24, which
recounts how the enemies of King [[manasara|Mânasâra]] of [[malwa|Mâlwa]] gathered
allies for an invasion. The King of Kuntala was brought into the coalition
after being deprived of his favourite actress — an insult that, combined
with bribes, convinced him to join the hostile league against [[malwa|Mâlwa]].
