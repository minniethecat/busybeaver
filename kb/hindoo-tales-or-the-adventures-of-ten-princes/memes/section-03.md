[003-001]"After trying in vain the power of charms, I went to look for some

[003-002]herb which might serve as an antidote; but when I returned the poor

[003-003]creature was dead.

[003-004]"I was much perplexed at this occurrence, especially as she had not

[003-005]told me the name of the village from which she came, nor could I

[003-006]conjecture how far off it might be, so that I was unable to take the

[003-007]child to its father.

[003-008]"Therefore, after collecting wood and burning the body, I have brought

[003-009]the child to you, thinking that he will be best taken care of under

[003-010]your protection."

[003-011]The king, astonished that so many children should have been brought in

[003-012]such a wonderful manner, and distressed at not knowing where to find

[003-013]Satyavarma, gave the child the name of Somadatta, and committed him to

[003-014]the care of his uncle, Sumati, who received him with great affection.

[003-015]These nine boys, thus wonderfully collected together, became the

[003-016]associates and play-fellows of the young prince, and were educated

[003-017]together with him.

[003-018]When they were all nearly seventeen, their education was regarded as

[003-019]complete, for they had not only been taught the vedas and the

[003-020]commentaries on them, several languages, grammar, logic, philosophy,

[003-021]&c., but were well acquainted with poetry, plays, and all sorts of

[003-022]tales and stories; were accomplished in drawing and music, skilled in

[003-023]games, sleight of hand and various tricks, and practised in the use of

[003-024]weapons. They were also bold riders and drivers of horses and

[003-025]elephants; and even clever thieves, able to steal without detection;

[003-026]so that Râjahansa was exceedingly delighted at seeing his son

[003-027]surrounded by a band of such brave, active, clever companions and

[003-028]faithful followers. One day about this time Vâmadeva came to visit

[003-029]the king, by whom he was received with great respect and reverence.

[003-030]Seeing the prince perfect in beauty, strength, and accomplishments,

[003-031]and surrounded by such companions, he said to Râjahansa: "Your wish

[003-032]for a son has indeed been fully gratified, since you have one who is

[003-033]all that you could desire. It is now time for him to go out into the

[003-034]world and prepare himself for the career of conquest to which he is

[003-035]destined.".

[003-036]The king listened respectfully to the advice of the muni, and

[003-037]determined to be guided by it; having therefore given his son good

[003-038]advice, he sent him forth at a propitious hour, to travel about in

[003-039]search of adventure, accompanied by his nine friends.

[003-040]After travelling for some days, they entered the forest of Vindhya,

[003-041]and when halting there for the night they saw a rough-looking man,

[003-042]having all the appearance of a Bheel, but wearing the sacred cord

[003-043]which is the characteristic of a brahman.

[003-044]The prince, surprised at such an incongruity, asked him who he was,

[003-045]how he came to be living in such a wild place, and how, with all the

[003-046]appearance of a forester, he was wearing the brahminical cord.

[003-047]The man, seeming to be aware that his questioner was a person of

[003-048]importance, answered respectfully, "O prince, there are in this forest

[003-049]certain nominal brahmans, who, having abandoned the study of the

[003-050]vedas, religious obligations, and family duties, are devoted to all

[003-051]sorts of sinful practices, and act as leaders of robber bands,

[003-052]associating with their followers and living as they live.

[003-053]"I, Matanga by name, am the son of one of these, and was brought up

[003-054]to be a robber like them. Since I have been grown up I have often

[003-055]assisted in plundering expeditions, when they would fall suddenly on

[003-056]some defenceless village, and carry away not only all the property on

[003-057]which they could lay their hands, but several of the richest of the

[003-058]inhabitants, whom they would keep prisoners till a ransom had been

[003-059]paid, or till, compelled by torture, they confessed where their money

[003-060]was concealed.

[003-061]"On one of these occasions, when my companions were ill-treating a

[003-062]brahman, I was seized by a sudden feeling of compassion and

[003-063]remonstrated with them. Finding words of no avail, I stood before him,

[003-064]and was killed by my own men while fighting on his behalf.

[003-065]"After death I went down to the regions below, and was taken before

[003-066]Yama, the judge of the dead, sitting on a great throne inlaid with

[003-067]jewels.

[003-068]"When the god saw me prostrate before him he called one of his

[003-069]attendants and said: 'The time for this man's death is not arrived,

[003-070]and moreover, he was killed in defending a brahman; therefore, after

[003-071]showing him the tortures of the wicked, let him return to his former

[003-072]body, in which he will in future lead a holy life.'

[003-073]"By him I was shown some sinners tied to red-hot iron bars, some

[003-074]thrown into great tubs of boiling oil, some beaten with clubs, some

[003-075]cut to pieces with swords; after which my spirit re-entered the body,

[003-076]and I awoke to consciousness, lying alone, grievously wounded, in the

[003-077]forest.

[003-078]"In this state I was found by some of my relations, who carried me

[003-079]home and took care of me till my wounds were healed.

[003-080]"Shortly after this I met with the brahman whom I had rescued, and he,

[003-081]grateful for the service which I had rendered him, read to me some

[003-082]religious books, and taught me the due performance of religious rites,

[003-083]especially the proper way of worshipping Siva.

[003-084]"When he considered me sufficiently instructed, he quitted me, giving

[003-085]me his blessing, and receiving many thanks from me for his kindness.

[003-086]"Since then I have separated myself from all my former associates, and

[003-087]have lived a life of penance and meditation in this forest,

[003-088]endeavouring to atone for my past sins, and especially seeking, to

[003-089]propitiate the mighty deity who has the half-moon for his crest; and

[003-090]now, having told you my history, I have something to communicate

[003-091]which concerns you alone, and beg you to withdraw with me to hear it

[003-092]in private."

[003-093]The two then went aside from the rest of the party, and the stranger

[003-094]said, "O prince, last night, during sleep, Siva appeared to me and

[003-095]addressed me thus: 'Matanga, I am pleased with your devotions; they

[003-096]shall now have their reward. North of this place, on the bank of the

[003-097]river which flows through the Dandaka forest, there is a remarkable

[003-098]rock, glittering with crystal and marked with the footsteps of Gaurí.

[003-099]Go thither; in the side of the rock you will see a yawning chasm,

[003-100]enter it and search till you find a copper plate with letters engraved

[003-101]on it; follow the directions therein contained, and you will become

[003-102]King of Pâtâla. That you may know this not to be a mere dream, a

[003-103]king's son will come to this place to-morrow, and he will be your

[003-104]companion in the journey.'

[003-105]"I have in consequence anxiously awaited your coming, and now entreat

[003-106]you to go with me to the place pointed out in the vision."

[003-107]The curiosity of the prince was much excited by Matanga's story, and

[003-108]he readily promised to be his companion; fearing, however, that his

[003-109]friends would be opposed to his purpose, he did not on his return tell

[003-110]them anything of what he had heard, and at midnight, when they were

[003-111]all fast asleep, he slipped away without disturbing them, and went to

[003-112]join Matanga, who was waiting for him at a place which had been agreed

[003-113]on, and the two walked on till they came to the rock indicated by Siva

[003-114]in the vision.

[003-115]Meanwhile, the rest of the party, uneasy at the disappearance of the

[003-116]prince, sought for him all over the forest, and not finding him,

[003-117]determined to disperse, and continue the search in different

[003-118]countries; and having arranged where to meet again, took leave of each

[003-119]other, and set out separately in different directions.

[003-120]Matanga, entirely believing the vision, and rendered still more

[003-121]confident by the companionship of the prince, fearlessly entered the

[003-122]cavern, found the copper plate and read the words engraved on it.

[003-123]Following the directions therein contained, they went on in darkness,

[003-124]groping their way through long passages, till at last they saw light

[003-125]before them and arrived at the subterranean country of Pâtâla.

[003-126]After walking some distance further, they came to a small lake,

[003-127]surrounded by trees, with a city in view.

[003-128]Here they stopped, and Matanga begging the prince to watch and guard

[003-129]against interruption, collected a quantity of wood and lighted a large

[003-130]fire, into which he threw himself with many charms and incantations,

[003-131]and presently came forth with a new body full of youth, beauty, and

[003-132]vigour, to the great astonishment of his companion.

[003-133]Hardly was this change effected, when they saw coming towards them

[003-134]from the city a procession, headed by a beautiful young lady

[003-135]splendidly dressed, and adorned with very costly jewels. Approaching

[003-136]Matanga, she made a low obeisance, and, without speaking, put a very

[003-137]precious gem into his hand. Being questioned by him, she answered,

[003-138]with tears in her eyes and in a soft musical voice, "O excellent

[003-139]brahman, I am the daughter of a chief of Asuras, and my name is

[003-140]Kalindí; my father, the ruler of this subterranean world, was slain

[003-141]by Vishnu whom he had offended, and as he had no son, I was left his

[003-142]heir and successor, and suffered great distress and perplexity.

[003-143]"Some time ago I consulted a very holy Siddha, who had compassion on

[003-144]me, and told me, 'After a time, a certain mortal, having a heavenly

[003-145]body, will come down here from the upper world; he will become your

[003-146]husband, and reign prosperously with you over all Pâtâla'.

[003-147]"Trusting to this prophecy, I have waited impatiently, longing for

[003-148]your coming as a Châtaka longs for rain, and am now come, with the

[003-149]consent of my ministers and people, to offer you my hand and kingdom."

[003-150]Matanga, delighted at such a speedy fulfilment of the promise given in

[003-151]the vision, gladly accepted her offer, and with the approbation of

[003-152]his companion, was soon afterwards married to her amid great

[003-153]festivity.

[003-154]Râjavâhana was treated with great respect and kindness by Matanga and

[003-155]his bride; but after seeing all the wonders of the place, his

[003-156]curiosity was satisfied, and he was desirous of returning to the upper

[003-157]world.

[003-158]At his departure, a magic jewel was given him by Kalindí, which had

[003-159]the power of keeping off from the possessor of it hunger, thirst,

[003-160]fatigue, and other discomforts; and Matanga accompanied him for a part

[003-161]of the way. Walking through darkness as before, the prince at last

[003-162]reached the mouth of the cavern and came forth into the open air.

[003-163]Having missed all his companions, he was uncertain where to direct his

[003-164]steps, and wandered on till he came to a large park, outside a city,

[003-165]where a great concourse of people was assembled, and he there sat down

[003-166]to rest.

[003-167]As he sat watching the various groups, he saw a young man enter the

[003-168]park, accompanied by a lady and followed by a numerous retinue, and

[003-169]they both got into one of the swings placed there for the amusement of

[003-170]the festal crowd.

[003-171]Presently the eye of the new-comer rested on the prince; with signs of

[003-172]great joy he jumped down, exclaiming, "O what happiness! That is my

[003-173]lord Râjavâhana," and, running to him, bowed down to his feet, saying

[003-174]"Great is my good fortune in meeting you again." Râjavâhana, affected

[003-175]by equal pleasure, warmly embraced him, saying, "O my dear friend

[003-176]Somadatta, how happy I am to see you once more!"

[003-177]Then they sat down together under a shady tree, and the prince

[003-178]inquired: "What have you been doing all this time? Where have you

[003-179]been? Who is this lady? And how did you get all these attendants?"

[003-180]Somadatta, thus questioned, began the recital of what he had done and

[003-181]seen.

[003-185]various countries. One day, when stooping to drink from a cool, clear

[003-186]stream, near a forest, I saw something bright under the water, and

[003-187]having taken it up, found it to be a ruby of very great value.
