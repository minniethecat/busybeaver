# Lâta

Lâta is a region in the *Daśakumāracarita*, ruled by Lord [[mattakala|Mattakâla]],
whose aggressive pursuit of [[vâmalochanâ|Vâmalochanâ]] sets off a chain of conflict in
Chapter 4. The kingdom's political entanglements involve siege warfare,
attempted assassination, and a tense standoff between rival rulers.
(004-011)

## Description

Lâta was governed by [[mattakala|Mattakâla]], a powerful lord who, hearing of the great
beauty of [[vâmalochanâ|Vâmalochanâ]], daughter of [[viraketu|Víraketu]], the sovereign of Pâtali,
demanded her in marriage. When his suit was refused, he raised an army
and laid siege to Pâtali. (004-011)

> "The Lord of Lâta, Mattakâla by name, hearing again and again of the great
> beauty of Vâmalochana, daughter of Víraketu, sovereign of this country,
> asked her in marriage, and was refused."
> (004-011)

The conflict drew in other actors. [[manapala|Mânapâla]], whose own interests were
threatened by the Lord of Lâta, sent assassins to kill him. The prisoners
taken in this attempt told the story of the King of Lâta to
[[apahâravarma]]. (004-045)

> "Those prisoners then told me the story of the King of Lâta."
> (004-045)

After the failed assassination, men sent by the King of Lâta came to
[[manapala|Mânapâla]]'s camp demanding the return of the escaped prisoners, threatening
retaliation if they were not surrendered. (004-066)

> "men sent by the King of Lâta came to Mânapâla, and said: 'Some robbers,
> who were caught after breaking into the king's dwelling, have made their
> escape.'"
> (004-066)

[[manapala|Mânapâla]] responded with contempt, his anger at the insulting demand visible
in his reddened eyes. His defiant reply escalated the conflict between the
two rulers. (004-072)

> "Who is the King of Lâta, that I should bow down to him? What have I to do
> with that low fellow? Begone!"
> (004-072)

## Significance in the Narrative

Lâta and its ruler feature centrally in Chapter 4, where [[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]]
learns of the political situation involving the siege of Pâtali. The
region's aggressive expansionism under [[mattakala|Mattakâla]] provides the backdrop
for one of the prince's early encounters with the tangled politics of
the kingdoms he visits. The conflict between Lâta and [[manapala|Mânapâla]]
illustrates the fractured political landscape that the ten princes must
navigate and eventually restore to righteous order. (004-011) (004-072)

## Appearances

Lâta appears exclusively in Chapter 4 (004-011, 004-045, 004-066,
004-072), where [[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]]'s narrative recounts the conflict
surrounding the Lord of Lâta's siege of Pâtali. The kingdom is not
mentioned again in subsequent chapters.
