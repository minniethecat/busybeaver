# Duty

## Definition

Duty in the Hindoo Tales represents the moral obligation to perform one's prescribed role and responsibilities. The concept carries significant weight — the failure to perform one's duty is treated as a source of disgrace, and the discharge of duty is a mark of virtuous character.

## In the Narrative

A speaker declares "I have done my duty, and have carefully prepared" (008-036), presenting duty as a completed moral obligation linked to proper preparation and conduct. In another instance, the stakes of neglecting duty are starkly stated: "If I do not perform this duty, disgrace would fall on you" (015-069), revealing that failure in duty brings collective dishonour, not merely personal failing. Duty functions as a binding social and ethical force.
