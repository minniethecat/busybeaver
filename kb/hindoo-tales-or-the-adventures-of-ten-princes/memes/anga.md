# Anga

Anga is an eastern kingdom in the *Daśakumāracarita*, ruled by King
[[sinhavarma]], a devoted friend and ally of King [[râjahansa]] of [[magadha|Magadha]].
The kingdom plays a supporting role in the narrative as a loyal ally that
suffers aggression from hostile powers.
(016-007) (026-144)

## Description

Anga was situated in eastern India, a kingdom bound to [[magadha|Magadha]] by ties of
loyalty and friendship. King [[sinhavarma|Sinhavarma]] was a devoted friend of [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]],
and this alliance made Anga a target for the enemies of [[magadha|Magadha]]. The kingdom
faced military threats that required the intervention of the ten princes.
(007-055) (016-007)

> "Sinhavarma, King of Anga, father of Ambâlika."
> (026-144)

The princess [[ambalika|Ambâlika]], daughter of [[sinhavarma|Sinhavarma]], became a significant figure
in the narrative when the King of [[malwa|Mâlwa]] sought her hand for his son
[[darpasara|Darpasâra]]. Her refusal and the subsequent conflict drew the princes into
Anga's affairs.
(007-064)

> "daughter of Sinhavarma, King of Anga, and, indignant at a refusal..."
> (007-064)

## Significance in the Narrative

Anga enters the story when a hostile king marches against it, prompting
the princes to intervene. [[sinhavarma|Sinhavarma]] was imprisoned by his enemies, and one
of the princes pledged to liberate him and re-establish his rule.
(007-055) (007-175)

> "being about to march against the King of Anga"
> (007-055)

The liberation of Anga and its king became one of the collective achievements
of the ten princes. An army was raised to assist the King of Anga, and the
princes' commitment to his cause demonstrated their network of political
alliances and their dedication to restoring righteous rule across India.
(017-121) (022-150)

> (007-175)

> (022-150)

## Appearances

Anga appears in Chapters 7, 16, 17, 22, and the proper names index in Chapter
26. It serves as an ally-kingdom in the political landscape, its fate
intertwined with the princes' mission of restoration.
