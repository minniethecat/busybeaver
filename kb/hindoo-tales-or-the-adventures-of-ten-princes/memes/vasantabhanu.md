## Biography

Vasantabhânu is mentioned in a single passage of Section 24, where an assassin—after killing a king—shouts "Long live Vasantabhânu!" to make it appear that the murder was committed on Vasantabhânuʼs orders. The name is used as a political decoy, suggesting Vasantabhânu was a rival or enemy of the slain king.

## Role

Vasantabhânu does not appear directly in the narrative. His name is invoked instrumentally—the assassin frames him for the murder to divert suspicion and escape. This brief mention implies Vasantabhânu was a known political figure, likely a rival king, whose enmity with the victim made the ruse plausible.

## Quotations

> "...pierced him to the heart, and I shouted out at the same time, 'Long live Vasantabhânu!' that it might be supposed I had been sent by him." (024-160)
