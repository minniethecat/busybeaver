# Pâtâla

Pâtâla is a fabulous subterranean country in the mythical geography of the
*Daśakumāracarita*. Ruled by King [[matanga]], it is the destination of
[[râjavâhana]]'s descent into the underworld, where he received a magic
jewel and won the kingdom for his companion.
(003-102) (026-123)

## Description

Pâtâla is described as a subterranean region — one of the fabulous nether
worlds of Hindu cosmology — yet treated in the narrative with the same
geographical concreteness as earthly kingdoms. It is ruled by King [[matanga|Matanga]],
a [[brahman|Brahman]] who had gone down to Pâtâla and established his dominion there.
(003-125) (026-114)

> "Pâtâla, a fabulous subterranean country."
> (026-123)

The country is accessible from the surface world, and [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] journeyed
there together with [[matanga|Matanga]]. Within Pâtâla, [[matanga|Matanga]] presided as king, and
his queen was [[kalindi|Kalindí]], who ruled beside him over the entire subterranean
realm.
(003-102) (026-085)

> "arrived at the subterranean country of Pâtâla."
> (003-125)

## Significance in the Narrative

Pâtâla plays a crucial role in the adventures of [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]]'s companion
[[ratnodbhava|Ratnodbhava]] (also known as [[pushpodbhava|Pushpodbhava]]). A [[brahman|Brahman]] named [[matanga|Matanga]] went
down to Pâtâla, and [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] accompanied him, receiving a magic jewel
that would later prove instrumental in the princes' quests.
(003-102) (007-061)

> "King of Pâtâla. That you may know this not to be a mere dream..."
> (003-102)

The magic jewel given in Pâtâla became a key object in the narrative,
used by one of the princes to achieve his aims. The episode represents
the mythical dimension of the *Daśakumāracarita*, where earthly
adventures intersect with supernatural realms.
(007-061)

> "the magic jewel given to him in Pâtâla, and which he had contrived to..."
> (007-061)

The promise that [[matanga|Matanga]] would "reign prosperously with you over all Pâtâla"
marks the subterranean kingdom as a domain won through the efforts and
friendship of the princes — a parallel to their restoration of earthly
kingdoms.
(003-146) (026-017)

## Appearances

Pâtâla appears in Chapters 3, 7, and the proper names index in Chapter 26.
Its role is concentrated in the early and middle portions of the narrative,
providing a mythical backdrop to the princes' earthly adventures.
