# Pushpodbhava

Pushpodbhava is one of the ten princes whose adventures are recounted in the
Daśakumāracarita. His name — meaning "born of a flower" — was given to him
when he was discovered as an infant. He was raised alongside the other princes
and educated together with them.
(002-137) (003-016)

## Biography

Pushpodbhava was found as an infant and given his name by those who discovered
him. He was raised alongside the other princes and educated together with them,
becoming one of the trusted companions of [[Râjavâhana]].
(002-137) (003-016)

He was accompanied into the city of [[Avanti]], where he witnessed the
momentous events surrounding the [[Festival of Kâma]] and the fateful
encounter between [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] and [[avantisundari|Avantisundari]].
(005-137)

## Role in the Narrative

Pushpodbhava's adventures are recounted alongside those of [[somadatta|Somadatta]]. The
narrator notes that the adventures of [[somadatta|Somadatta]] and Pushpodbhava are of
particular significance among the tales of the ten princes.
(007-190)

He was present among the companions who reunited after their separate wanderings,
and his story forms part of the central narrative cycle. At the re[[marriage-in-the-tales|union]],
Pushpodbhava was warmly received and his tale added to the rich tapestry
of adventures that constitute the epic.
(004-108) (004-110)

During the stay at [[avanti|Avanti]], Pushpodbhava remained in the city with [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]],
sharing in the pleasures of the spring season and the [[festival-of-kama|great festival]].
(005-162)

> "here is Pushpodbhava." Then there were mutual embraces..."
> (004-108)
