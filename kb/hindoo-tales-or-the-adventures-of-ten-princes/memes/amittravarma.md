# Amittravarma

Amittravarma was the King or Governor of [[mahishmati|Mahishmati]], a half-brother of the late king whose widow and son sought refuge in his court. His story is recounted by [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]] in his narrative of how he recovered the kingdom for the rightful heir, Prince [[prachandavarma|Prachandavarma]].

## Biography

When the queen of [[mahishmati|Mahishmati]] fled with her infant son after her husband's death, she arrived at [[mahishmati|Mahishmati]] and was well received by Amittravarma, a half-brother of her deceased husband. She devoted herself to the education of her son, hoping he might one day recover his father's kingdom (024-031–033).

> The queen was well received by the king Amittravarma, a half-brother of her husband, and where she devoted herself to the education of her son, hoping that he might one day recover his father's kingdom. (024-031–033)

After a time, however, Amittravarma sought to marry his brother's widow. When she rejected his advances, he determined to take revenge by killing her son, forcing the queen to seek help from [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]] (024-034–037).

Later, Amittravarma attempted to reconcile with his sister-in-law by arranging a good marriage for her daughter, Princess [[manjuvadini|Manjuvâdiní]], to Prince [[prachandavarma|Prachandavarma]]. [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]] saw through this scheme, recognizing that Amittravarma intended to lure the son back into his power (024-078–082).

## Death

Amittravarma met his end through the queen's ingenious use of poison. During a confrontation, the queen had applied poison to her face. She struck him, saying:

> "If you are acting right, this will not injure you; if you are sinning in taking me, your brother's wife, and I am faithful to my husband, may this be like the blow of a sword to you." (024-100–102)

Such was the strength of the poison that Amittravarma fell dead almost instantaneously (024-103–104). His death was widely seen as divine punishment:

> The death of Amittravarma was a punishment from heaven. (024-107)

After Amittravarma was struck dead, the account of the queen's vision was readily believed, and the way was cleared for Prince [[prachandavarma|Prachandavarma]] to assume the throne (024-127–128).

## Relationships

- **The Queen** — His sister-in-law; he first gave her refuge, then sought to marry her, and finally attempted to kill her son
- **[[prachandavarma|Prachandavarma]]** — The rightful prince whose marriage Amittravarma promoted as a political maneuver
- **[[mantragupta|Mantragupta]]** — The prince who ultimately orchestrated his downfall
- **[[nalijangha|Nâlijangha]]** — [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]]'s agent who reported on Amittravarma's schemes (024-079)
