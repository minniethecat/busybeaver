# Víraketu

Víraketu was the King of [[Pâtali]] and the father of [[Vâmalochanâ]].
His reign was disrupted by the invasion of [[Mattakâla]], the Lord of
[[lata|Lâta]], and he ultimately became the father-in-law of Prince [[Somadatta]],
whom he declared his successor to the throne.
(004-012) (026-163)

## Biography

Víraketu ruled as the sovereign of the country whose capital was Pâtali.
His daughter [[vâmalochanâ|Vâmalochanâ]] was renowned throughout the land for her
extraordinary beauty, a fame that would bring both suitors and invaders
to his kingdom.
(004-012) (026-163)

> "...again of the great beauty of Vâmalochana, daughter of Víraketu,
> sovereign of this country..."
> [004-012]

When [[mattakala|Mattakâla]], the Lord of [[lata|Lâta]], heard of [[vâmalochanâ|Vâmalochanâ]]&#39;s beauty, he
asked for her hand in marriage. Víraketu refused the proposal, prompting
[[mattakala|Mattakâla]] to raise an army and besiege Pâtali, the capital city.
(004-011) (004-013) (004-014)

> "Víraketu finding himself unable to resist the enemy, purchased peace
> by giving up his daughter..."
> [004-015]

Unable to withstand the siege, Víraketu was forced to surrender his
daughter to [[mattakala|Mattakâla]] in order to secure peace. The princess was placed
under the guard of [[Mânapâla]], one of Víraketu&#39;s own officers, who
was said to be very indignant at the surrender of the lady.
(004-015) (004-016) (004-022)

After Prince [[somadatta|Somadatta]] killed [[mattakala|Mattakâla]] in battle and rescued
[[vâmalochanâ|Vâmalochanâ]], Víraketu received the prince with great honour. Having
received an account of the victory through a messenger sent from
[[manapala|Mânapâla]], he came forth to meet the returning party as they entered
the city.
(004-090) (004-091) (004-092)

As [[somadatta|Somadatta]] continued daily to increase in favour with the king,
Víraketu bestowed upon him the hand of his daughter and declared him
his successor, securing both the dynasty and a lasting alliance with
one of the ten princes.
(004-094) (004-095)

## Role in the Narrative

Víraketu appears in the fourth chapter as the beleaguered king whose
daughter&#39;s beauty draws the unwanted attention of [[mattakala|Mattakâla]]. His
inability to resist the besieging army sets in motion the chain of
events that leads to [[somadatta|Somadatta]]&#39;s heroic intervention, the defeat of
[[mattakala|Mattakâla]], and the eventual marriage alliance that concludes this
episode of the princes&#39; adventures.
(004-012) (004-015) (004-095)

His role is brief but pivotal: as the father of a famed beauty, his
predicament provides the occasion for [[somadatta|Somadatta]]&#39;s display of valour
and the establishment of one of the many marriage bonds that close the
ten princes&#39; tales.
(026-154) (026-163)
