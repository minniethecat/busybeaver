# Dandaka

The Dandaka forest is a wilderness region appearing in the third chapter of
the *Daśakumāracarita*, where the god [[siva|Siva]] revealed to [[matanga|Matanga]] the location
of a sacred rock that would lead him to become King of [[patala|Pâtâla]].
(003-097)

## Geography

The Dandaka forest lay north of the place where [[matanga|Matanga]] had been
performing his devotions to [[siva|Siva]]. A river flowed through the forest, and on
its bank stood a remarkable rock glittering with crystal and bearing the
footprints of the goddess [[gauri|Gaurí]]. The forest was remote and wild enough to
conceal a yawning chasm in the rock that contained a copper plate with
engraved instructions — the key to [[matanga|Matanga]]'s destined kingship.
(003-096) (003-097) (003-098)

> "North of this place, on the bank of the river which flows through the Dandaka forest, there is a remarkable rock, glittering with crystal and marked with the footsteps of Gaurí."
> (003-097) (003-098)

## Role in the Narrative

The Dandaka forest serves as the setting for a divine revelation. [[siva|Siva]]
appeared to [[matanga|Matanga]] in a dream and directed him to the forest, where the
rock and its hidden copper plate awaited. This episode marks the turning
point in [[matanga|Matanga]]'s story — the moment his ascetic penance is rewarded with
a concrete path to earthly power. The forest thus functions as a threshold
between [[matanga|Matanga]]'s life of wandering devotion and his future as a king.
(003-094) (003-100)
