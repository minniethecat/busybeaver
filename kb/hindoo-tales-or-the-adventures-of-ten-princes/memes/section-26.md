[026-003]After that, a certain damsel, adorned with a quantity of ornaments,

[026-004]made of jewels, who had become the chief of the whole race of women in

[026-005]the world, attended by a numerous train of modest female friends,

[026-006]having the gait of a swan, having come up softly, having made an

[026-007]offering to the most excellent brahman, of one jewel of the form

[026-008](colour) of flame, being asked by him: "Who art thou?"

[026-009]Sorrowfully, with a low murmuring voice, very gently, in a submissive

[026-010]attitude, said: "O excellent brahman, I am the daughter of a chief of

[026-011]Asuras, Kalindi by name. My father, the ruler of this world, great in

[026-012]dignity, in a battle in which the immortals were removed to a

[026-013]distance, was made a guest of the city of Yama by Vishnu, impatient of

[026-014]his own valour. Me, immersed in an ocean of grief at separation from

[026-015]him, a certain compassionate perfected devotee told: 'Damsel, a

[026-016]certain mortal, bearing a divine body, having become thy new husband,

[026-017]shall rule over the whole of Pâtâla.'"

[026-018]* * * * *

[026-019]PAGE 309.

[026-020]Having propitiated with clasped hands, put together in the form of the

[026-021]red lotus; the mass of rays coloured by the red sandalwood body of the

[026-022]thousand-eyed elephant of the eastern quarter having a thousand

[026-023]flames, the witness of things (which ought) to be done and not to be

[026-024]done, the unique sea-monster leaping over the row of cloud-waves of

[026-025]the celestial ocean, the graceful actor dancing on the stage of the

[026-026]golden rock, the one lion the tearer of the scented elephant of

[026-027]nocturnal darkness, the jewel arranged at the top of the pearl

[026-028]necklace the canopy of the stars; I went to my own dwelling. And three

[026-029]days being gone, when the lord of day had a splendour of colour common

[026-030]to it with the red chalk side of the peak of the western mountain, and

[026-031]was looking like the orb of one bosom of the Goddess of Twilight,

[026-032]united with the body of Siva, under the name of atmosphere, for the

[026-033]disparagement of the daughter of the king of mountains; that king also

[026-034]having come, stood in humble attitude, having his diadem eclipsed by

[026-035]the rays from the nails of the feet of this person placed on the

[026-036]ground; and he was thus addressed:--

[026-038]PROPER NAMES, ETC., OCCURRING IN THE TALES.

[026-039]_Alaka_, a mountain inhabited by Kuvera and the Yakshas.

[026-040]_Ambâlika_, the daughter of Sinhavarma, wife of Mantragupta.

[026-041]_Amittravarma_, King or Governor of Mahishmati.

[026-042]_Anantavarma_, King of Vidarba.

[026-043]_Apahâravarma_, son of Prahâravarma, and one of the nine companions of

[026-044]Râjahansa.

[026-045]_Apsaras_, heavenly females, nearly corresponding with the houris of

[026-046]the Mahometans.

[026-047]_Arthapâla_, son of Kâmapâla, one of the nine companions of Râjahansa.

[026-048]_Arthapati_, a merchant at Champa, who wished to marry Kulapâlika.

[026-049]_Aryaketu_, a minister and friend of Visruta.

[026-050]_Asura_, a general term for various supernatural beings not regarded

[026-051]as gods, but in general hostile to them, nearly the same as the jins

[026-052]or genii of the "Arabian Nights."

[026-053]_Avantisundari_, daughter of Mânasâra, wife of Râjavâhana.

[026-054]_Balabhadra_, a merchant, husband of Ratnavati.

[026-055]_Bâlachandrika_, wife of Pushpodbhava, and friend of Avantisundari.

[026-056]_Bandhupâla_, a merchant, father of Bâlachandrika.

[026-057]_Betel and pawn_, a mixture for chewing, frequently offered in

[026-058]politeness, as snuff with us.

[026-059]_Bheels_, savages, wild tribes, robbers.

[026-060]_Bhimadhanwa_, brother of Kandukavati.

[026-061]_Buddhist_, a disciple of Buddha. Buddha was a Hindoo reformer, whose

[026-062]followers were once very numerous in India, but at the date of these

[026-063]stories had been much diminished in number, through the persecutions

[026-064]of the brahmans. They still, however, form a large part of the

[026-065]population of Ceylon, Thibet, China, and some other countries, though

[026-066]the comparatively pure religion of the founder has for the most part

[026-067]degenerated into gross idolatry and unmeaning ceremonies.

[026-068]_Chakravâka_, name of a bird quoted for affection, as turtle-doves by

[026-069]us.

[026-070]_Chandâla_, a pariah, outcast.

[026-071]_Chandrasena_, foster-sister of the Princess Kandukavati.

[026-072]_Châtaka_, a bird supposed to be very fond of rain, and to make a loud

[026-073]noise at its approach.

[026-074]_Dhanamittra_, husband of Kulapâlika, friend of Apahâravarma.

[026-075]_Dharmapâla_, one of Râjahansa's ministers.

[026-076]_Dharmavardhana_, King of Sravasti.

[026-077]_Durga_ or _Kâli_, wife of Siva, a terrific goddess, delighting in

[026-078]human sacrifices.

[026-079]_Gaurí_, wife of Siva.

[026-080]_Ghee_, liquid butter, or butter which has been liquefied.

[026-081]_Indra_, the chief of the inferior gods, presiding over the clouds,

[026-082]rain, thunder, &c.

[026-083]_Kailâsa_, a mountain, part of the Himâlaya chain.

[026-084]_Kalahakantaka_, the man who fell in love with a portrait.

[026-085]_Kalindí_, Queen of Pâtâla, wife of Matanga.

[026-086]_Kalpasundari_, wife of Vikatavarma, afterwards of Upahâravarma.

[026-087]_Kâma_ or _Kandarpa_, the God of Love.

[026-088]_Kâmamanjari_, the actress who seduced the Muni.

[026-089]_Kâmapâla_, son of Dharmapâla, minister and son-in-law of the King of

[026-090]Benâres.

[026-091]_Kanakalekha_, daughter of the King of Kalinga, wife of Mantragupta.

[026-092]_Kandukavati_, the princess who performed the ball-dance.

[026-093]_Kantaka_, the gaoler killed by Upahâravarma.

[026-094]_Kantimati_, the wife of Kâmapâla, mother of Arthapâla.

[026-095]_Kirâta_, a savage, forester, Bheel.

[026-096]_Kosadâsa_, lover of Chandrasena.

[026-097]_Kusa-grass_, a scented grass, much used at sacrifices for laying

[026-098]offerings on, &c.

[026-099]_Kuvera_, the God of Wealth, whose attendants were the Yakshas.

[026-100]_Magadha_, the kingdom of Râjahansa.

[026-101]_Mahâkâla_, a famous temple of Siva, the object of many pilgrimages.

[026-102]_Mahishmati_, name of a city.

[026-103]_Malaya_, a mountain, or range of mountains, having many sandal trees,

[026-104]the perfume from which was supposed to be carried a long distance by

[026-105]the wind.

[026-106]_Mâlwa_, the kingdom of Mânasâra.

[026-107]_Mânapâla_, the officer who guarded Vâmalochana.

[026-108]_Mânasâra_, King of Mâlwa, conqueror of Râjahansa.

[026-109]_Manibhadra_, a Yaksha, father of Târâvali.

[026-110]_Manjuvâdiní_, daughter of Anantavarma, wife of Visruta.

[026-111]_Mantra_, a verse or chapter in the vedas, any prayer or words recited

[026-112]as a charm.

[026-113]_Mâríchi_, a great muni seduced by Kâmamanjari.

[026-114]_Matanga_, a brahman who went down to Pâtâla together with Râjavâhana.

[026-115]_Mithila_, a city or country, called also Videha.

[026-116]_Mitragupta_, one of the nine companions of Râjavâhana.

[026-117]_Muni_, a holy man devoted to study, meditation, and penance.

[026-118]_Nâlijangha_, the old man whom Visruta rescued from the well.

[026-119]_Nârâyana_, a name of Vishnu, an incarnation of the three principal

[026-120]gods, Brahma, Vishnu, Siva.

[026-121]_Navamâlika_, daughter of the King of Sravasti, wife of Pramati.

[026-122]_Padmodbhava_, one of Râjahansa's ministers.

[026-123]_Pâtâla_, a fabulous subterranean country.

[026-124]_Prachandavarma_, King or Governor of Mahishmati, killed by Visruta.

[026-125]_Prahâravarma_, King of Mithila, father of Apahâravarma and

[026-126]Upahâravarma.

[026-127]_Priyamvada_, Queen of Prahâravarma.

[026-128]_Purnabhadra_, the reformed robber, servant of Kâmapâla.

[026-129]_Pushpapuri_, the capital of Magadha.

[026-130]_Râgamanjari_, an actress, sister of Kâmamanjari.

[026-131]_Râjahansa_, king of Magadha, father of Râjavâhana, the hero of the

[026-132]story.

[026-133]_Rakshas_ or _Rakshasas_, evil spirits or ogres, hostile to men, whom

[026-134]they used to devour.

[026-135]_Rati_, a goddess, wife of Kâma.

[026-136]_Rishi_, nearly the same as Muni, a holy man retired from the world,

[026-137]devoted to prayer and meditation.

[026-138]_Satyavarma_, son of a minister of Râjahansa, and father of Somadatta.

[026-139]_Savara_, fem. _Savari_, a savage, not a Hindoo.

[026-140]_Siddha_ (literally perfected), a very holy devotee.

[026-141]_Simanta_, a religious ceremony performed on behalf of a woman at a

[026-142]certain period of pregnancy.

[026-143]_Sinhaghosha_, the deposed King of Benâres.

[026-144]_Sinhavarma_, King of Anga, father of Ambâlika.

[026-145]_Sitavarma_, one of Râjahansa's ministers.

[026-146]_Sringâlika_, the nurse of Râgamanjari.

[026-147]_Siva_, one of the three chief gods or triad of the Hindoos, Brahma,

[026-148]Siva, and Vishnu, who are sometimes regarded as one, sometimes

[026-149]confounded with each other.

[026-150]_Sumantra_, son of Dharmapâla.

[026-151]_Susruta_, son of Padmodbhava.

[026-152]_Târâvali_, a Yaksha lady, wife of Kâmapâla.

[026-153]_Vâmadeva_, a holy man consulted by Râjahansa.

[026-154]_Vâmalochana_, daughter of Víraketu, wife of Somadatta.

[026-155]_Vasumati_, Queen of Râjahansa.

[026-156]_Vasundhara_, Queen of Anantavarma the King of Vidarba.

[026-157]_Vidarba_, name of a country.

[026-158]_Videha_, a country called also Mithila.

[026-159]_Vidyâdhara_, one of the numerous demigods.

[026-160]_Vidyeswara_, the conjuror who married Râjavâhana to Avantisundari.

[026-161]_Vikatavarma_, King of Mithila, husband of Kalpasundari.

[026-162]_Vimardaka_, a keeper of a gaming house, employed by Apahâravarma.

[026-163]_Víraketu_, King of Pâtali, father of Vâmalochana.

[026-164]_Yaksha_, a sort of demigod or fairy, a servant of Kuvera.

[026-165]_Yama_, God and Judge of the Infernal Regions.

[026-166]_Yati_, an ascetic, a devotee.

[026-167]_Yavana_, a Greek, an Arabian--any foreigner.
