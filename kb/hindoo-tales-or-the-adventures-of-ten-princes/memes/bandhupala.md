# Bandhupâla

Bandhupâla was a merchant of [[malwa|Mâlwa]] and the father of [[balachandrika|Bâlachandrika]]. He played
a significant role in the early chapters of the narrative, providing aid and
shelter to a traveller and using his skill in interpreting the voices of birds
to gather information.
(005-024) (026-056)

## Biography

When a traveller arrived in [[malwa|Mâlwa]] seeking refuge, it was through the
intercession of Bandhupâla that he obtained permission from the King of
[[malwa|Mâlwa]] to reside in the city. The traveller took a house, safely deposited
his money, and established his parents there.
(005-024) (005-025) (005-026)

Bandhupâla observed the traveller's restless desire to continue his search
and offered practical counsel:

> "You have already spent much time in searching for your friend, and
> may spend much more in the same manner to no purpose, if you have no
> clue to guide you. Now I am going to the court of the King of Mâlwa,
> where I may be able to obtain some information."
> (005-028) (005-030)

One day, Bandhupâla, wishing to obtain intelligence by listening to the
voices of birds — a traditional art of divination — went with the traveller
into a park near the city. While he waited under the trees, interpreting
the bird calls, the traveller walked abroad.
(005-041) (005-042) (005-043)

Later, Bandhupâla was introduced to a young brahman who was, in reality, a
prince in disguise. Bandhupâla gave out in the city that the newcomer was a
young brahman worthy of all honour for his learning and ability, and the
prince remained for some time in that guise.
(005-139) (005-140) (005-141)

## Role in the Narrative

Bandhupâla serves as a helpful intermediary in the early chapters, providing
hospitality, practical advice, and intelligence-gathering skills to the
protagonists. His knowledge of bird omens adds a touch of traditional
wisdom and divination to the narrative, while his role as a merchant
father connects him to the broader network of characters through his
daughter [[balachandrika|Bâlachandrika]].

## Relationships

Bandhupâla was the father of [[Bâlachandrika]]. He maintained connections
with the King of [[malwa|Mâlwa]]'s court, through which he was able to assist
travellers and gather intelligence. He extended hospitality to a
disguised prince whom he presented to the city as a learned brahman.
(026-056) (005-024) (005-139)
