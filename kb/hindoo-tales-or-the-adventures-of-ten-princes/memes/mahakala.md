# Mahâkâla

Mahâkâla is a renowned temple of [[siva|Siva]] in the *Daśakumāracarita*,
a destination for pilgrims seeking the god's blessings through penance and
devotion. The temple is significant enough to be listed in the proper names
index of Chapter 26, which identifies it as "a famous temple of [[siva|Siva]], the
object of many pilgrimages." (026-101)

## Description

The temple of Mahâkâla was one of the most important [[siva|Siva]] shrines in the
world of the *Daśakumāracarita*. It drew worshippers from across the
kingdoms, who undertook arduous journeys to pay their devotions there.
(001-160)

> "the mighty Siva, whose temple is at Mahâkâla, and he has so far succeeded
> that the god has given him a magic club, very destructive of life and
> conducive to victory."
> (001-160)

The temple was a place where serious penance was performed, and [[siva|Siva]] was
believed to grant powerful boons to those who propitiated him there. A
devotee who performed severe penance at Mahâkâla received a magic club
from the god, making him nearly invincible. (001-160)

> "I am on my way to Mahâkâla, to worship Siva there."
> (004-098)

[[somadatta|Somadatta]], one of the princes' companions, stopped at a festival while
travelling to Mahâkâla, hoping to hear news of his friend [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]].
His journey to the temple became the occasion for the re[[marriage-in-the-tales|union]] of the
princes. (004-098)

## Significance in the Narrative

Mahâkâla serves as a pivot for character movement and re[[marriage-in-the-tales|union]]s in the
narrative. [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]]'s journey to the temple after completing certain
adventures creates the occasion for instructing [[somadatta|Somadatta]] to join him
there after his own devotions. (005-135)

> "Come to me as soon as possible, when you have paid your devotions at
> Mahâkâla."
> (005-135)

The temple's prestige as a pilgrimage site makes it a natural meeting
point. Characters travelling "to Mahâkâla" signals both religious devotion
and narrative convergence — journeys to the shrine bring characters
together and advance the frame story. (005-135)

## Appearances

Mahâkâla appears in Chapters 1 (001-160), 4 (004-098), 5 (005-135), and
the index in Chapter 26 (026-101). The temple is referenced as a
destination of pilgrimage in the early chapters and catalogued in the
proper names appendix.
