## Biography

Susruta was the son of [[padmodbhava|Padmodbhava]], one of [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]]'s three ministers. (026-151) He is listed among the companions of the princes: the second group had two companions, Susruta and [[ratnodbhava|Ratnodbhava]], while the last group had [[sumati|Sumati]] and [[satyavarma|Satyavarma]]. (001-107)

After the shipwreck, Susruta was sent for to take charge of his brother's child, to whom he gave the name of [[pushpodbhava|Pushpodbhava]]. (002-136) This act established Susruta as a guardian figure within the extended network of the princes' companions.

## Role

Susruta is a secondary companion figure in the narrative. His most significant action is taking charge of [[pushpodbhava|Pushpodbhava]] after the shipwreck, serving as a guardian and naming the child, which connects him to the broader network of princely companions and their families.
