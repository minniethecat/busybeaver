# Sinhavarma

Sinhavarma was the King of [[anga|Anga]], father of [[Ambâlika]], and an old friend of
[[Arthapâla]]. His impatient disposition led him into a disastrous war against
a neighbouring power, resulting in his capture.

## Biography

Sinhavarma was the ruler of the kingdom of [[anga|Anga]]. His daughter was [[ambalika|Ambâlika]],
who became the wife of [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]], one of the ten princes.
(007-064)

> "The daughter of Sinhavarma, King of Anga, and, indignant at a refusal, was
> determined to revenge herself upon her father."
> (007-064)

Sinhavarma was of a very impatient and impetuous disposition, a trait that
would prove to be his undoing. His temper drove him into conflicts that he
was not prepared to win.
(007-068)

> "Sinhavarma, being of a very impatient and impetuous disposition, would
> never brook the slightest opposition."
> (007-068)

Despite his valour in battle, Sinhavarma was eventually taken prisoner, and
his army suffered a crushing defeat.
(007-073)

> "At last Sinhavarma was taken prisoner, and his army so completely
> defeated that he was left without resources."
> (007-073)

A letter later arrived from Sinhavarma to [[Arthapâla]], addressing him as an
old friend and seeking his aid.
(014-004)

> "Soon afterwards, a letter arrived from Sinhavarma, an old friend of my
> father."
> (014-004)

When King Sinhavarma was attacked, allies marched with an army to his
assistance, acknowledging the bonds of friendship between kingdoms.
(021-034)

The character index records Sinhavarma as the King of [[anga|Anga]] and the father of
[[ambalika|Ambâlika]].
(026-144)

## Role in the Narrative

Sinhavarma appears primarily as a figure whose downfall creates narrative
tension and motivates the actions of others. His war and capture are
referenced in the tale of [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]], whose marriage to Sinhavarma's
daughter [[ambalika|Ambâlika]] is a key plot point.
(007-064)

The account of how [[chandavarma|Chandavarma]] besieged [[champa|Champa]] and how Sinhavarma was drawn
into the conflict connects him to the wider network of princes and kingdoms.
(011-165)

His letter to [[Arthapâla]] marks a turning point, calling upon old alliances
and bringing the prince into the affairs of [[anga|Anga]].
(014-004)

## Relationships

Sinhavarma was the father of **[[Ambâlika]]**, who became the wife of
**[[Mantragupta]]**, one of the ten princes.
(007-064) (026-040)

He was an old friend of **[[Arthapâla]]** and of his father, maintaining ties
between the kingdom of [[anga|Anga]] and the court of [[magadha|Magadha]].
(014-004)

His kingdom of **[[anga|Anga]]** bordered on the territories of the other princes,
making his political alliances of strategic importance.
