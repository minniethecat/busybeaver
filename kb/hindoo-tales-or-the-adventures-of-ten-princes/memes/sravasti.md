# Sravasti

Sravasti is a city and kingdom in the *Daśakumāracarita*, ruled by King
[[dharmavardhana]]. It is the setting for a key episode in [[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]]'s
narrative, where he arrives in the city searching for the actress [[râgamanjari|Râgamanjari]]
and becomes entangled in the affairs of its royal family.
(016-120) (017-015)

## Description

Sravasti was a royal seat, governed by King [[dharmavardhana|Dharmavardhana]], whose daughter
was given in marriage to [[pramati]]. The city served as a destination for
travellers and adventurers drawn by its political and social significance.
(016-100) (026-121)

> "Dharmavardhana, King of Sravasti."
> (026-076)

[[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]] arrived at Sravasti in the course of his wanderings after being
separated from the other princes. He was conducted to the palace of the King of
Sravasti, where he became involved in the intrigues and relationships of the
royal household. (016-100) (016-120)

> "the daughter of the King of Sravasti, wife of Pramati"
> (026-121)

The city appears in the narrative as a place of both refuge and entanglement.
After a long journey, [[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]] reached Sravasti exhausted, lying down to
sleep in an arbour where he witnessed events that drew him deeper into the
lives of its inhabitants. (016-143)

> "very tired at Sravasti, I lay down to sleep in an arbour"
> (016-143)

## Significance in the Narrative

Sravasti is the location where [[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]]'s personal quest — his search for
[[râgamanjari|Râgamanjari]] — converges with the broader political web of the ten princes. He
came to the city solely from the hope of finding her, but his presence there
led to encounters that shaped his subsequent adventures.
(017-015)

> "had come to Sravasti solely from the hope of finding her"
> (017-015)

The city is tied to the royal lineage through King [[dharmavardhana|Dharmavardhana]] and his
daughter, who married [[pramati|Pramati]]. This connection integrates Sravasti into the
network of kingdoms that feature in the princes' restoration of righteous
rule across India. (016-100) (026-121)
