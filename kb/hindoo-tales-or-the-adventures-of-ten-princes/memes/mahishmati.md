# Mahishmati

Mahishmati is a city in the *Daśakumāracarita*, governed by King
[[satahali|Satahali]]. It serves as a waypoint in the later chapters of the
narrative, where the ten princes regather and exchange intelligence before
their final campaigns.
(024-030) (026-102)

## Description

Mahishmati was a notable city with its own governor, appearing in the
climactic chapters of the story. After a series of adventures, [[arthapala|Arthapâla]] and
his companions arrived safely at Mahishmati, making it a place of re[[marriage-in-the-tales|union]] and
strategic planning. (024-030) (024-072)

> "the rest arrived safely at Mahishmati"
> (024-030)

The city is identified in the proper names index of Chapter 26 as a
significant location. Its governor, [[satahali|Satahali]], was a figure connected to the
network of rulers and officials that the ten princes encountered and, in some
cases, opposed. (026-041) (026-102)

> "Mahishmati, name of a city."
> (026-102)

The political climate at Mahishmati was a matter of interest to the princes.
They made efforts to understand the situation there, inquiring about what was
transpiring in the city as part of their broader intelligence-gathering across
the kingdoms. (024-072)

> "anything of what is going on at Mahishmati?"
> (024-072)

## Significance in the Narrative

Mahishmati appears in Chapter 24 as part of the princes' coordinated movement
toward their final objectives. The city was a point of convergence where the
travellers could rest, regroup, and assess the political landscape before
continuing their campaigns. (024-030) (024-089)

> "it was generally understood at Mahishmati"
> (024-089)

The governance of Mahishmati also features in the background of the narrative's
political struggles. One record notes that a previous ruler of Mahishmati was
killed by [[visruta|Visruta]], indicating that the city had witnessed violent transitions
of power before coming under [[satahali|Satahali]]'s governance. (026-124)

> "King or Governor of Mahishmati, killed by Visruta"
> (026-124)

## Appearances

Mahishmati is concentrated in Chapter 24, which describes the princes' arrival
and their intelligence-gathering in the city. It is also listed in the proper
names index of Chapter 26.
