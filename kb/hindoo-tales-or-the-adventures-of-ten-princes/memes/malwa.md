# Mâlwa

Mâlwa is a powerful kingdom in central India, ruled by the ambitious and
haughty King [[mânasâra]]. It is one of the principal antagonistic realms in
the *Daśakumāracarita*, whose conquest of [[magadha|Magadha]] sets the entire
narrative of the ten princes in motion.

## Description

Mâlwa was a prosperous and militarily formidable kingdom. Its king, [[manasara|Mânasâra]],
possessed a magic weapon that made him nearly invincible in battle. The capital
city of Mâlwa served as the seat of his power and the destination of nobles and
travellers from across the region. [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] himself resided there for a time,
observing the kingdom that had overthrown his father.
(002-042) (005-025)

> "Mânasâra, King of Mâlwa, has... furnished with a magic weapon,"
> (002-017)

The kingdom's influence extended over neighbouring territories, and its court
was the centre of political intrigue. [[manasara|Mânasâra]] arranged the marriage of his
son [[darpasara|Darpasâra]] to forge alliances, using Mâlwa's wealth and military strength
as bargaining power.
(015-143)

> "Mâlwa, the kingdom of Mânasâra."
> (026-106)

## Significance in the Narrative

Mâlwa enters the story at its very beginning as the source of catastrophe for
the house of [[magadha|Magadha]]. King [[manasara|Mânasâra]] of Mâlwa conquered [[magadha|Magadha]], took King
[[rajahansa|Râjahansa]] prisoner, and laid the entire kingdom open to his forces. This act
of aggression scattered the infant princes and set them on their individual
paths of adventure.
(001-120) (001-130)

> "...the king of the adjoining country of Mâlwa, the haughty and... their king taken prisoner, and all Mâlwa lay open to the conqueror."
> (001-120) (001-130)

The conquest of [[magadha|Magadha]] is the defining event that connects Mâlwa to every
prince's story. [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]], driven from his dominions by the King of Mâlwa,
became a wandering exile, and the ten princes grew up in hiding, awaiting the
day they could reclaim their birthright.
(012-019)

> "conquered and driven from his dominions by the King of Mâlwa"
> (012-019)

Later in the narrative, the princess [[ambalika|Ambâlika]] was given in marriage to
[[darpasara|Darpasâra]], son of the King of Mâlwa — a [[marriage-in-the-tales|union]] that wove the fates of
multiple kingdoms together and created the conditions for the princes'
eventual restoration.
(015-143)

## Appearances

Mâlwa is referenced throughout the narrative, appearing in Chapters 1, 2, 5,
12, 15, and the index of proper names in Chapter 26. The kingdom is mentioned
both as a geographical entity and as the seat of [[manasara|Mânasâra]]'s power.
