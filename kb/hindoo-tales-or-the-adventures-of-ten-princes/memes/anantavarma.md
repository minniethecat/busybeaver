# Anantavarma

## Biography

Anantavarma was the King of [[vidarba|Vidarba]], inheriting the kingdom from his father, a ruler remarkable for wisdom and justice, learned in the Scriptures and a protector of his subjects.
> "There was formerly a King of Vidarba remarkable for wisdom and justice, learned in the Scriptures, a protector of his subjects."
> (023-001)

Anantavarma was a young man of great abilities, but he cared more for the mechanical arts, music, and poetry than for the governance of his kingdom.
> "kingdom to his son Anantavarma, a young man of great abilities, but caring more for the mechanical arts, music, and poetry, than for his kingdom."
> (023-007)

His reign was undermined by secret enemies who worked to weaken his authority and diminish his resources, while preventing him from recognizing the danger.
> "in secret to weaken the authority of Anantavarma, and diminish his resources; and, lest he should perchance see the error of his ways."
> (023-145)

He maintained a splendid court attended by a numerous retinue.
> "the court of Anantavarma, attended by a numerous retinue."
> (023-150)

## Role in the Narrative

Anantavarma's reign ended in catastrophe when the King of [[asmaka|Asmaka]], having long schemed to conquer [[vidarba|Vidarba]], launched an invasion. Through the machinations of an emissary who had infiltrated the court, the kingdom was weakened and ill-prepared for war. Anantavarma was easily defeated and fell into the power of his cruel enemy.
> "was made; Anantavarma was easily defeated, and fell into the power of his cruel enemy."
> (024-015)

After his defeat and death, an old and faithful minister escaped with the queen, [[vasundhara|Vasundhara]], and her two children — a son and daughter named [[manjuvadini|Manjuvâdini]] — together with a few faithful followers. The old minister fell ill and died on the road, but the rest arrived safely at [[mahishmati|Mahishmati]], where the queen was received by King [[amittravarma|Amittravarma]], a half-brother of her husband. The queen devoted herself to the education of her son, hoping that he might one day recover his father's kingdom.
> "After the defeat and death of Anantavarma, an old and faithful minister escaped with the queen and her two children, this boy and his elder sister Manjuvâdini, together with a few faithful followers; and though the old minister was taken ill and died on the road, the rest arrived safely at Mahishmati."
> (024-026)

## Relationships

- **[[vasundhara|Vasundhara]]** — Queen of Anantavarma, who survived him and devoted herself to the education of their son, hoping he might one day recover his father's kingdom (026-156).
- **[[manjuvadini|Manjuvâdini]]** — Daughter of Anantavarma, later wife of [[visruta|Visruta]] (026-110).
- **[[amittravarma|Amittravarma]]** — Half-brother of Anantavarma, King of [[mahishmati|Mahishmati]], who received the queen and her children after the fall of [[vidarba|Vidarba]] (024-031).
- **King of [[asmaka|Asmaka]]** — The enemy who orchestrated Anantavarma's downfall through intrigue and invasion (024-004).
