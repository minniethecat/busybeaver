# Famine in the Tales

## Summary

A devastating famine strikes the land in Chapters 18–19 of the *Tales of Ten Princes*, creating a crisis of survival that tests the bonds of family and society. As the famine intensifies, men resort to unthinkable acts, and migration becomes the only path to survival.

## Context

The famine functions as a narrative pressure that forces characters into motion and moral extremity. It provides the backdrop against which human nature is revealed — both its capacity for self-preservation at any cost, and its resilience in seeking better circumstances.
