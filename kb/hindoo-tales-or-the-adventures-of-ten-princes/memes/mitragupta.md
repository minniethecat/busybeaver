# Mitragupta

Mitragupta is one of the ten princes and companions of [[Râjavâhana]]
in the *Daśakumāracarita*. He was noted as one of the nine companions
of the hero, raised alongside him from birth.
(026-116)

## Biography

Mitragupta was one of the four sons born to the ministers of King
[[rajahansa|Râjahansa]], named alongside [[pramati|Pramati]], [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]], and [[visruta|Visruta]]. The
four were raised together with Prince [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]].
(002-032)

After the princes were scattered, he was among those who reunited with
[[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]], arriving in the company of [[upaharavarma|Upahâravarma]], [[pramati|Pramati]], and
[[mantragupta|Mantragupta]].
(007-183)

His adventures form their own chapter in the narrative.
(017-129)

## Role in the Narrative

Mitragupta's tale is one of the early adventures recounted. When his turn
came, [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] commended his approach before calling upon him:

> "Then turning to Mitragupta, he said: 'It is now your turn to speak.'"
> (017-126)

His story is recorded in the chapter "ADVENTURES OF MITRAGUPTA."
(017-129)

## Relationships

Mitragupta was the foster-brother of [[pramati|Pramati]], [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]], and [[visruta|Visruta]],
all sons of [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]]'s ministers.
(002-032)

He was one of the nine companions of [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]].
(026-116)
