# Râmatirtha

Râmatirtha is a holy pilgrimage site (*tirtha*) in ancient India, mentioned
in the second chapter of the *Daśakumāracarita*. A disciple of
[[vamadeva|Vâmadeva]] was returning from Râmatirtha when he encountered an
old woman carrying the infant [[arthapala|Arthapâla]].
(002-095)

## Geography

The text describes Râmatirtha as a destination for pilgrimage, implying it
was a recognized sacred site. No further geographical details of its
location or features are provided in the narrative.
(002-095) (002-096)

> "As I was returning from a pilgrimage to Râmatirtha, I saw an old woman
> carrying this child, and asked her how she came to be wandering there."
> (002-095) (002-096)

## Role in the Narrative

Râmatirtha is the starting-point of the journey that brings
[[arthapala|Arthapâla]] to the court of King [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]]. A
disciple of [[vamadeva|Vâmadeva]], returning from this pilgrimage, found an old woman
carrying a beautiful boy. The woman revealed herself as the servant of
[[kalagupta|Kâlagupta]], entrusted with the child [[suvritta|Suvritta]] — later known as
[[arthapala|Arthapâla]], one of the Ten Princes.
