# Kailâsa

Kailâsa is a mountain in the [[himalaya|Himâlaya]] chain, mentioned in the
*Daśakumāracarita* as the abode of the god [[kuvera|Kuvera]] and as the
origin of a critical letter that altered the course of events during the
siege of [[avanti|Avanti]].
(007-081) (026-083)

## Geography

Kailâsa is described in the index of proper names as "a mountain, part of the
[[himalaya|Himâlaya]] chain." In Hindu cosmology, it is the fabled residence of [[kuvera|Kuvera]], the
god of wealth, and his attendant Yakshas. Its remote, elevated position makes
it a fitting seat for a divine being who watches over the affairs of mortals
from afar.
(026-083)

> "_Kailâsa_, a mountain, part of the Himâlaya chain."
> (026-083)

## Role in the Narrative

Kailâsa appears in the story at a moment of crisis. [[chandavarma|Chandavarma]] had captured
the city of [[avanti|Avanti]], imprisoned the king, and prepared to marry the princess
against her will. As the [[marriage-in-the-tales|wedding]] was about to be celebrated, a messenger
arrived from Kailâsa bearing a letter from [[darpasara|Darpasâra]], whose
harsh commands changed the course of events. The mountain thus serves as the
source of an intervention that complicates the political situation, showing
how even remote divine abodes are connected to the unfolding drama of the
princes.
(007-081) (007-082)

> "Just then a messenger arrived from Kailâsa, bringing a letter from Darpasâra."
> (007-081)
