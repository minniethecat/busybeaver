# Kalahakantaka

## Biography

Kalahakantaka dwelt in the city of [[madhura|Madhura]], a man of great strength and vigour, ready at any time to take up the quarrel of anyone who employed him.
> "In a city called Madhura, there dwelt a man named Kalahakantaka, of great strength and vigour, ready at any time to take up the quarrel of anyone who employed him."
> (020-068)

He is remembered in the character summary as "the man who fell in love with a portrait."
> "_Kalahakantaka_, the man who fell in love with a portrait."
> (026-084)

## Role in the Narrative

Kalahakantaka's story is recounted as part of a larger narrative of intrigue and deception in [[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]]'s tale. Upon hearing of a beautiful woman's predicament, he took another name and journeyed to [[avanti|Oujein]] ([[avanti|Ujjayini]]), where he disguised himself as a mendicant and gained admission to a household.
> "On hearing this, Kalahakantaka, taking another name, went to Oujein; and there, having disguised himself as a mendicant, got admission to the house."
> (020-080)

When the woman he pursued was taken away from him with angry words, Kalahakantaka was not deterred and continued his pursuit of her.
> "woman away with angry words. Kalahakantaka, however, was not discouraged."
> (020-091)

He went at night to an appointed meeting place where he found the woman. In the climactic scene, he discovered her bound with cords in a state of distress. He cut the cords that fastened her and, falling at her feet, confessed all that he had done.
> "In this state she was found by Kalahakantaka, who cut the cords which fastened her, and, falling at her feet, confessed all that he had done."
> (020-152)
