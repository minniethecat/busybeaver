# Mattakâla

**Mattakâla** is the Lord of [[Lâta]], who waged war against King [[Víraketu]] to obtain his daughter [[Vâmalochanâ]] in marriage.

## Biography

Mattakâla, hearing repeatedly of the great beauty of [[vâmalochanâ|Vâmalochanâ]], daughter of King [[viraketu|Víraketu]] of Pâtali, asked for her hand in marriage and was refused. (004-011–012) Determined to obtain her, he raised an army and besieged Pâtali, the capital city.

King [[viraketu|Víraketu]], finding himself unable to resist Mattakâla's forces, purchased peace by giving up his daughter. (004-016) Mattakâla accepted the arrangement but deferred the marriage celebration, thinking it could be conducted with greater magnificence in his own country of [[lata|Lâta]], and departed with his army. (004-016–018)

> "The Lord of Lâta, Mattakâla by name, hearing again and again of the great beauty of Vâmalochana, daughter of Víraketu, sovereign of this country, asked her in marriage, and was refused. Being determined to obtain her, he raised an army and besieged Pâtali, the capital city." (004-011–013, 004-015)

> "Víraketu finding himself unable to resist the enemy, purchased peace by giving up his daughter, and Mattakâla, thinking that the marriage can be celebrated with greater magnificence in his own country, has deferred it till his return." (004-016–018)
