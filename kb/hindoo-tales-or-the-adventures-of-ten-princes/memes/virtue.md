# Virtue

## Definition

Virtue (Dharma) represents righteous conduct, moral duty, and ethical living. In the Hindoo philosophical framework, it is counted among the three great aims of human life — alongside [[wealth|Wealth]] ([[wealth|Artha]]) and [[pleasure|Pleasure]] ([[kama|Kama]]) — and is the subject of sustained discussion in Chapter 8 of the narrative.

## In the Narrative

The most concentrated treatment of Virtue appears in Chapter 8, where the characters debate the relative superiority of the three aims. Virtue is presented as essential to a well-ordered life, though its primacy over wealth and pleasure is contested. One character argues that virtue alone is the true aim, since without it both wealth and pleasure become meaningless. Another counters that virtue without wealth is powerless, being unable to support itself in the world. The debate frames virtue not merely as abstract morality but as a practical guide to conduct in a world where the other aims compete for attention.
