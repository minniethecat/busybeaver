# Pramati

Pramati is one of the ten princes whose adventures form the core narrative
of the *Daśakumāracarita*. He was the son of [[sumati|Sumati]] and one of the four
minister-sons raised alongside Prince [[Râjavâhana]], later marrying
[[navamalika|Navamâlika]], the daughter of the King of [[Sravasti]].
(026-184)

## Biography

Pramati was born to [[sumati|Sumati]], one of the ministers of [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]]. He
was named Pramati alongside his three foster-brothers — [[mitragupta|Mitragupta]],
[[mantragupta|Mantragupta]], and [[visruta|Visruta]] — and raised together with the young prince
[[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]].
(002-032)

Pramati was among the companions who reunited with [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] after the
great dispersal. He arrived accompanied by [[upaharavarma|Upahâravarma]], [[mitragupta|Mitragupta]],
and [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]].
(007-183)

His adventures are distinct from those of the other princes. In the
introductory chapter, it is noted that Pramati's adventures and those
of [[arthapala|Arthapâla]] are separate narratives.
(026-187)

Pramati eventually married [[navamalika|Navamâlika]], the daughter of the King of
[[sravasti|Sravasti]].
(026-121)

## Role in the Narrative

When his turn came to recount his tale, [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] addressed him with
warmth:

> "Then turning with a kind look to Pramati, he said: 'Do you now relate your adventures.'"
> (016-018)

Pramati's adventures constitute their own chapter — "ADVENTURES OF
PRAMATI" — which is paired with the story of [[arthapala|Arthapâla]].
(016-022)

His narrative is part of the broader tapestry of the ten princes'
wanderings and re[[marriage-in-the-tales|union]]s.

## Relationships

Pramati was the son of [[sumati|Sumati]], a minister of [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]].
(026-184)

He was the foster-brother of [[mitragupta|Mitragupta]], [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]], and [[visruta|Visruta]].
(002-032)

His wife was [[navamalika|Navamâlika]], daughter of the King of [[sravasti|Sravasti]].
(026-121)

He was a companion of [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] and the other princes.
(007-183)
