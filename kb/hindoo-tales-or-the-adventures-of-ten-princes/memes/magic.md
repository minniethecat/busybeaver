# Magic

## Definition

Magic in the Hindoo Tales refers to supernatural objects, powers, and artefacts that operate beyond natural laws and serve as key plot devices. These range from destructive weapons to protective jewels, transformative ointments, and enchanted objects with specific wondrous properties. Magic is presented matter-of-factly — as a known and accepted dimension of the world — rather than as something requiring explanation or disbelief.

## In the Narrative

Magic objects appear throughout the tales as pivotal narrative mechanisms. The magic club (001-161) is a destructive weapon wielded with devastating effect. A magic jewel given in [[patala|Pâtâla]] (003-158) reappears as a significant token in later adventures (007-061). A magic ointment enables concealment by altering appearance (005-012). The magic candle and wooden man's head (009-033) are among the wondrous objects that drive the plot of the magic purse narrative, each with its own specific power (009-166). Magic thus functions as both a world-building element and a practical narrative tool, enabling transformations, escapes, and revelations that would otherwise be impossible.
