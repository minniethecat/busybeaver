# Vidarba

Vidarba is a kingdom in the *Daśakumāracarita*, ruled by King [[anantavarma]],
a monarch remarkable for wisdom and virtue. It is the homeland of one of the
narrating princes and the setting for a story of royal succession and prophecy.
(023-001) (026-042)

## Description

Vidarba was governed by King [[anantavarma|Anantavarma]], who is described as "remarkable for
wisdom and virtue." His reign established Vidarba as a kingdom of just rule,
but it also became the target of those who sought to do mischief within its
borders. (023-001) (023-184)

> "There was formerly a King of Vidarba remarkable for wisdom and virtue"
> (023-001)

The kingdom is listed in the proper names index as "a country," confirming its
status as a distinct sovereign territory. [[anantavarma|Anantavarma]]'s queen, the mother of
the narrator, is also recorded in the index, indicating the importance of the
royal lineage to the narrative. (026-042) (026-156)

> "Anantavarma, King of Vidarba"
> (026-042)

> "Queen of Anantavarma the King of Vidarba"
> (026-156)

A prophecy concerning the future King of Vidarba plays a pivotal role in the
narrative. It was foretold that a particular boy would ascend to the throne of
Vidarba, setting in motion events that shaped the ambitions and actions of
those who heard it. (024-124)

> "That boy will become King of Vidarba"
> (024-124)

## Significance in the Narrative

Vidarba is the opening setting of Chapter 23, where a narrator prince — the son
of King [[anantavarma|Anantavarma]] — begins his tale. The kingdom's affairs, including
political intrigue and the struggle against those who would "do as much
mischief as possible," form the backdrop of this prince's adventures.
(023-001) (023-184)

> "do as much mischief as possible in the country of Vidarba"
> (023-184)

The prophecy of a future King of Vidarba creates narrative tension, as the
prince's destiny is bound to the throne. The interplay of fate, ambition, and
righteous rule in Vidarba echoes the broader themes of the *Daśakumāracarita*,
where the ten princes work to restore legitimate kingship across India.
(024-124) (026-157)

## Appearances

Vidarba appears primarily in Chapters 23 and 24, where the king and the
prophecy concerning his successor are central to the narrator's story. It is
listed in the proper names index of Chapter 26 as "a country."
