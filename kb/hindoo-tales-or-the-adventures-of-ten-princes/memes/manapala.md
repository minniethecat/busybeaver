# Mânapâla

Mânapâla was an officer in the service of the King of [[malwa|Mâlwa]], entrusted with
the guardianship of the princess [[Vâmalochanâ]]. He was a capable military
commander who guarded the princess at a camp and engaged in battle with those
who threatened his charge.
(004-022) (026-107)

## Biography

Mânapâla was one of the officers of the King of [[malwa|Mâlwa]], and the princess
[[vâmalochanâ|Vâmalochanâ]] was placed under his care after her intended marriage was
interrupted. He was described as being very indignant at the surrender of
the lady, and maintained a camp at no great distance from the other forces.
(004-022)

> "The princess is not with her intended husband, but under the care of
> Mânapâla, one of her father's officers, who is said to be very
> indignant at the surrender of the lady."
> (004-022)

When travellers reached Mânapâla's camp after escaping from prison, they
found him to be a commander who was always prepared for conflict. He
received the fugitives and listened to their account of events.
(004-065) (004-067)

Mânapâla was provoked into battle by those who raided his camp. The
attackers broke into the place where the prisoners were held, providing
Mânapâla with the excuse he sought for open conflict.
(004-047) (004-070)

> "Mânapâla, who only wanted an excuse for a quarrel, having heard this
> report, prepared to attack."
> (004-070)

When the battle was joined, Mânapâla was fully prepared. The encounter was
fierce, and he proved himself a capable and ready commander.
(004-077) (004-078)

Later, through a messenger sent from Mânapâla, terms were arranged and the
opposing parties came forth to meet in a more peaceful manner, showing his
willingness to resolve conflicts through diplomacy when appropriate.
(004-092)

## Role in the Narrative

Mânapâla appears in the fourth chapter as the guardian of [[vâmalochanâ|Vâmalochanâ]],
serving as both protector and antagonist. His indignation at the princess's
surrender makes him a reluctant custodian, and his camp becomes a flashpoint
for conflict. His readiness for battle and eventual diplomatic resolution
mark him as a competent military officer whose actions drive the plot forward.
(004-022) (004-077)

## Relationships

Mânapâla served the King of [[malwa|Mâlwa]] and was responsible for guarding
[[vâmalochanâ|Vâmalochanâ]], the princess who was initially betrothed to another but
subsequently surrendered. He commanded soldiers and dealt with the
fugitives who sought refuge at his camp.
(026-107)
