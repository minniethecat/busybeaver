# Manjuvâdini

Manjuvâdini was a princess, the elder sister of a young prince and
daughter of [[anantavarma|Anantavarma]]. She fled with her brother and
the queen after her father's kingdom was overthrown, and later married
the narrator as a reward for his loyal services.

## Biography

Manjuvâdini was the daughter of King [[anantavarma|Anantavarma]].
(026-110)

When her father's kingdom fell, the minister escaped with the queen and her
two children — Manjuvâdini and her younger brother — together with a few
faithful followers. Though the old minister fell ill and died on the road,
the rest arrived safely at [[mahishmati|Mahishmati]], where the queen was well received by
the king [[amittravarma|Amittravarma]], a half-brother of her late husband.
(024-027) (024-031)

> "The minister escaped with the queen and her two children, this boy and
> his elder sister Manjuvâdini, together with a few faithful followers."
> (024-028)

Later, a marriage was arranged between Manjuvâdini and the Prince
[[prachandavarma|Prachandavarma]], the king's younger brother, and rejoicings were held on
this account.
(024-076)

> "The Prince Prachandavarma, the king's younger brother, is about to
> marry the Princess Manjuvâdini, and the rejoicings are on this account."
> (024-076)

Ultimately, the narrator himself received the hand of the lovely
Manjuvâdini as the reward of his services and in obedience to the commands
of the goddess [[durga|Durgâ]].
(025-040)

## Role in the Narrative

Manjuvâdini appears in the narrative of [[visruta|Visruta]]'s adventures (Chapters 24
and 25). She is initially betrothed to Prince [[prachandavarma|Prachandavarma]], but the
narrator — who had faithfully served her family during their exile —
ultimately marries her as a reward for his loyalty.
