# Meditation

## Definition

Meditation (Dhyâna) is a spiritual discipline of concentrated contemplation, frequently mentioned alongside penance and prayer as one of the core religious practices in the Hindoo world. It involves withdrawal from worldly affairs, silent absorption, and the turning of the mind toward religious objects. Characters undertake meditation as a life-path, as a preparatory discipline for laying curses, and as a private practice of piety.

## In the Narrative

Meditation appears across the full span of the narrative — from Chapter 1, where a character devotes himself to religious meditation and leaves home (001-110), to Chapter 22, where a character withdraws for private meditation (022-040). The practice is prescribed as a remedy: a friend is advised to devote himself to prayer and meditation (002-011). A life of penance and meditation is lived in the forest (003-087). The power of meditation is such that a curse laid during meditation carries special force (006-031). In Chapter 8, a character is interrupted during meditation (008-021) and another waits unobtrusively upon one engaged in it (008-073). Pious meditation is described as the highest duty (016-017), and characters who have passed many years in meditation are regarded with reverence (020-094). The practice is consistently associated with spiritual authority and moral seriousness.

> "…e lived a life of penance and meditation in this forest," (003-087)

> "…gaged in meditation, I lay my curse upon…" (006-031)
