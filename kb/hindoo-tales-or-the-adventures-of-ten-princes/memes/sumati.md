# Sumati

Sumati was a minister of King [[Râjahansa]] and the father of Prince
[[Pramati]], one of the ten princes whose adventures form the central
narrative of the *Daśakumāracarita*.
(001-108) (026-184)

## Biography

Sumati served as one of the ministers in the court of [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]]. He
was named alongside [[Satyavarma]] among the king's trusted advisors.
(001-108)

> "He was sent to the care of his uncle, Sumati, who received him with
> great affection."
> (003-014)

Sumati acted as an uncle and guardian, receiving a young charge with great
affection and raising him alongside the other minister-sons in the royal
household.
(003-014)

His son [[pramati|Pramati]] grew up to become one of the ten princes, later marrying
[[navamalika|Navamâlika]], the daughter of the King of [[sravasti|Sravasti]].
(026-184)

## Role in the Narrative

Sumati appears in the introductory chapter as a minister of [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]]'s
court, and his role as guardian in the third chapter establishes the
upbringing of the young princes. His son [[pramati|Pramati]]'s adventures are paired
with those of [[Arthapâla]] in the latter half of the work.
(001-108) (003-014)
