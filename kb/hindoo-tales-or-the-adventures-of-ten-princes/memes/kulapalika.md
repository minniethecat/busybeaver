# Kulapâlika

Kulapâlika was the daughter of a wealthy merchant and the central figure in
the magic purse affair recounted by [[Apahâravarma]]. Her betrothal to
[[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]] and the scheming of her father form a key subplot in the
*Daśakumāracarita*.
(009-043) (026-074)

## Biography

Kulapâlika was born into a rich merchant family in the city. From childhood,
she was engaged to marry [[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]], the son of another wealthy merchant.
(009-043) (009-044)

> "My name is Kulapâlika; I am the daughter of a rich merchant in this city,
> and was from childhood engaged to the son of another rich man, named
> Dhanamittra."
> (009-043)

However, her father sought to marry her instead to [[arthapati|Arthapati]], a merchant at
[[champa|Champa]], who wished to marry her.
(026-048)

[[arthapati|Arthapati]] proved willing to take Kulapâlika without a dowry and even made
presents to her father, settling that the marriage should take place at the
end of a month.
(009-120) (009-121)

> "…the latter was not only willing to take Kulapâlika without a dowry, but
> even made presents to her father."
> (009-120)

The father of Kulapâlika, hearing of [[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]]'s reputation and the magic
purse, had no difficulty in reconsidering his decision.
(009-173)

Ultimately, through the efforts of [[Apahâravarma]] and the magic purse,
[[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]] and Kulapâlika were married.
(010-107)

> "…the earnest entreaty of Dhanamittra, who got back his purse, and shortly
> afterwards married Kulapâlika."
> (010-107)

## Role in the Narrative

Kulapâlika's story is embedded within the adventure of [[Apahâravarma]], who
encounters her in distress and learns of her situation. Her predicament — a
forced marriage to the wrong suitor — becomes the catalyst for [[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]]'s
cunning intervention using the magic purse, demonstrating the theme of
friendship and loyalty that runs through the tales.

Her marriage to [[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]] represents the happy resolution that the ten
princes strive to bring about for those they encounter.

## Relationships

Kulapâlika was the wife of [[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]], who was a close friend of
[[Apahâravarma]].
(026-074)

She was the daughter of a rich merchant who initially opposed her marriage
to [[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]].
(009-043) (009-173)

She was the intended bride of [[arthapati|Arthapati]], a merchant at [[champa|Champa]], before
[[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]] secured her hand.
(026-048)
