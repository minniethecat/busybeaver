[013-001]When the proper time arrived, I put on a close-fitting dark dress,

[013-002]girded on my sword, and set out on the dangerous enterprise.

[013-003]Concealed at the edge of the ditch, I found a long bamboo, which the

[013-004]old woman had procured for me. This I laid across, and so got to the

[013-005]bottom of the wall. Then, cautiously raising it, I climbed to the top,

[013-006]just where a large heap of bricks had been piled up inside. Using

[013-007]these as steps, I got safely to the ground, and walked northward,

[013-008]through an avenue of champaka trees, where, as a favourable omen, I

[013-009]heard the low murmuring cry of a pair of chakravâkas. Taking an

[013-010]almost opposite direction, I saw before me what appeared to be a great

[013-011]building, and it was only by touching it that I found it to be a clump

[013-012]of trees. Going eastward, and turning once more to the south, I passed

[013-013]through some mango trees, and saw the light of a lantern shining among

[013-014]the leaves. I then knew that I was right, and went straight up to the

[013-015]bower, inside of which was a summer-house, with steps leading up to

[013-016]it, and spread with soft twigs and flowers for a carpet. The room was

[013-017]furnished with a handsome couch, a golden water-jar, trays of flowers,

[013-018]fans, &c. After I had been seated a short time, I heard the tinkling

[013-019]of ornaments and smelt a powerful perfume. Rising up hastily, I

[013-020]slipped out, and stood concealed by the shrubs outside. Presently I

[013-021]saw the lady enter; she looked about her, and not seeing me, was

[013-022]evidently disappointed and distressed. I heard her say, with a sad low

[013-023]voice, "Alas! I am deceived, he is not coming; O my heart, how can

[013-024]this be borne? O adorable Kâma, what have I done to offend thee, that

[013-025]thou thus burnest me and dost not reduce me to ashes?"

[013-026]Having heard this, I made my appearance, and said: "O lovely lady, do

[013-027]you ask how you have offended Kâma? You have given him great offence,

[013-028]since you disparage his beloved Rati by your form, his bow by your

[013-029]arched eyebrows, his arrows by your glances, his great friend, the

[013-030]perfumed wind of Malaya, by your sweet breath, the notes of his

[013-031]favourite bird by your voice. For all this Kâma justly torments you.

[013-032]But I have done nothing to offend him; why should he so distress me?

[013-033]Have pity on me, and cure the wound inflicted by the serpent of love,

[013-034]with the life-giving antidote of an affectionate look."

[013-035]Delighted at seeing me, she required no entreaty on my part, and

[013-036]readily yielded to my embrace; and, sitting down on the couch, we

[013-037]conversed as though we had been long acquainted.

[013-038]At last the time for separation arrived, and I rose up to go; but she

[013-039]with tears detained me, saying: "When you depart, my life seems to

[013-040]follow. If you go, let me go with you."

[013-041]I answered: "O my beloved, that is impossible. If you love me, be

[013-042]guided by me, and we shall soon meet again, not to be parted."

[013-043]This she readily promised, and I told her exactly what was to be done.

[013-044]Then quitting her with reluctance, I returned safely by the way I had

[013-045]come, and she went back to the palace.

[013-046]The next day she showed the picture to the king, who greatly admired

[013-047]it, and asked her where she had got it. She told him: "I have lately

[013-048]made acquaintance with a very wonderful old woman, who has travelled

[013-049]over many countries and seen many strange things; she is very skilful

[013-050]in charms, and has brought me this picture, saying: 'It has very great

[013-051]magical powers, and so confident am I in their efficacy that I ask for

[013-052]no payment or reward until you have fully proved them.' She tells me

[013-053]that if certain ceremonies are performed, and mantras which she has

[013-054]taught me, are recited in a retired spot at midnight, I shall be

[013-055]changed to a person exactly resembling the portrait, and shall have

[013-056]the power of transferring that form to you while I regain my own

[013-057]shape. I have thought it right to tell you this; but do not act

[013-058]hastily: show the picture to your ministers and consult them."

[013-059]The king, greatly astonished, but very desirous of obtaining such a

[013-060]handsome body, asked the opinion of his counsellors and younger

[013-061]brothers, and they saw no reason why the experiment should not be

[013-062]tried.

[013-063]The hour of midnight on the day of full moon was therefore appointed

[013-064]for the ceremony, and there was much talk in the city about it.

[013-065]"O the wonderful power of magic! Through the skill of the queen, the

[013-066]king will obtain a new body fit for a god."

[013-067]"But is there no danger?"

[013-068]"How can there be danger when the ceremony is to be performed by his

[013-069]own queen, in his own private gardens, where no stranger can enter?

[013-070]Besides, have not the learned and clever ministers and counsellors

[013-071]approved of it, and is it likely that they would be deceived?"

[013-072]The city was full of such talk as this, and the people awaited with

[013-073]impatience the night appointed for the working of the miracle.

[013-074]When the time arrived a great heap was made in a part of the garden

[013-075]where four roads met, not far from the summer-house, with large

[013-076]quantities of sandal-wood, lignaloes, and other sweet-smelling woods,

[013-077]camphor, silk dresses, sesamum, saffron, and various spices; and

[013-078]several animals, duly slaughtered by the priests, were laid upon it;

[013-079]and the fire having been lighted, every one withdrew except the king

[013-080]and queen. She then said to him: "You know how faithless you have been

[013-081]to me, and with this handsome body you will be a much greater

[013-082]attraction to other women. I know the fickleness of your disposition.

[013-083]Can you expect that I will confer on you this beauty for the sake of

[013-084]my rivals?"

[013-085]Then he threw himself at her feet, and said "O my darling, forgive my

[013-086]transgressions. I swear by everything solemn that in future I will

[013-087]keep to you only, and not even think of any other woman."

[013-088]After these and many other protestations, she appeared to be

[013-089]satisfied, and said: "Now withdraw to that clump of trees, and stay

[013-090]there till I ring the bell; then you may come again to the fire and

[013-091]see the wonderful change in me."

[013-092]Meanwhile, under cover of the thick smoke arising from the burning of

[013-093]all those substances, I had climbed the wall as before, and was

[013-094]standing in the summer-house when the queen came in. She said:

[013-095]"Everything is ready. I regard myself now as entirely yours; nothing

[013-096]shall part us any more;" and, throwing her arms round my neck, she

[013-097]kissed me again and again.

[013-098]Saying to her, "Stay here concealed while I finish the work," I

[013-099]quitted her, went to the place of sacrifice, and rang a bell hanging

[013-100]on a neighbouring tree; and the sound summoned the king, like a

[013-101]messenger of death.

[013-102]He found me standing by the fire, throwing on it more sandal-wood,

[013-103]lignaloes, and other precious things; and as he stood gazing in fear

[013-104]and astonishment, and hardly believing his eyes, I said to him:

[013-105]"Remember what you have promised, and now swear to me again, taking

[013-106]this sacred fire as a witness, that you will renounce all other women,

[013-107]and keep to me only."

[013-108]He answered: "O queen, there is no deceit in me. I will do all that I

[013-109]have promised," and he repeated his former oaths.

[013-110]But as if not satisfied with this, I said: "I must have some other

[013-111]proof of your sincerity. Tell me some of your state secrets."

[013-112]Then he told me: "My father's brother, Prahâravarma, has been for a

[013-113]long time in prison; with the consent of my ministers, I intend to

[013-114]poison him, and give out that he has died of old age and infirmities.

[013-115]"I am preparing an army, to be commanded by my brother, for the

[013-116]invasion of Pundra without any declaration of war.

[013-117]"There is a merchant here possessed of a diamond of immense value. I

[013-118]'am contriving a plan by which I shall get it from him at a tenth of

[013-119]its worth.

[013-120]"There is a man of wealth and influence very displeasing to me. I have

[013-121]engaged a certain person, named Satahali, the governor of the

[013-122]district, to bring a false accusation against him, and by that means

[013-123]to stir up the people, and so cause his death in a popular tumult,

[013-124]which will take away all blame or suspicion from me."

[013-125]When I had heard all these things, saying, "Die the death which your

[013-126]wicked deeds deserve," I suddenly seized him by the throat, stabbed

[013-127]him in a moment to the heart, and threw the body into the great fire,

[013-128]where it was quickly consumed; after which I went back to the queen,

[013-129]who was anxiously awaiting me. Though much agitated, she was more

[013-130]relieved at having got rid of that wretch than shocked at the manner

[013-131]of his death; and having quieted and consoled her without much

[013-132]difficulty, I went at once with her to her apartments.

[013-133]On seeing him, whom they believed to be the king, so changed, the

[013-134]women and attendants who met us were evidently much astonished, but so

[013-135]much had been said beforehand about the wonderful transformation to be

[013-136]expected, that no one seemed to doubt that I was really the king with

[013-137]a new body; and having said a few words of encouragement to them, I

[013-138]was received with great respect.

[013-139]The rest of the night was passed in hearing from the queen as much as

[013-140]possible about the court, the ministers, &c., so that I might not

[013-141]appear to be ignorant of what the king must have known, when I should

[013-142]meet them on the morrow.

[013-143]In the morning, after the performance of due worship of the gods, I

[013-144]met the ministers in council, and they also were so convinced of the

[013-145]power of magic that they did not hesitate to acknowledge me as their

[013-146]master, expressing their delight at the happy change.

[013-147]Then I said to them: "With this new body I have new feelings and

[013-148]purposes. I repent of my cruelty to my uncle, and instead of getting

[013-149]rid of him as I had intended, it is my pleasure that he shall be taken

[013-150]from prison and treated with all proper respect.

[013-151]"That diamond, of which I had intended to get possession, must not be

[013-152]obtained by fraudulent means. If I should decide on having it, I will

[013-153]pay the full price."

[013-154]To the brother who had been appointed to command the army, I said:

[013-155]"Dear brother, our purpose is changed with regard to that invasion.

[013-156]You will only watch the frontier; and if there is any beginning of war

[013-157]on the part of the Pundras, attack them vigorously; but not

[013-158]otherwise."

[013-159]I sent also for Satahali, and said: "You know that I wished to get

[013-160]rid of Anantasíra, because he was suspected of being a partisan of the

[013-161]deposed king. Now that I am reconciled to my uncle, there is no

[013-162]occasion for anything to be done to him; you will therefore take no

[013-163]further steps in that affair."

[013-164]When the ministers heard all this, and perceived me to be acquainted

[013-165]with secrets known only to the king and themselves, they were quite

[013-166]confirmed in their first impression; and while congratulating me and

[013-167]the queen, were loud in their praise of the power of magic.

[013-168]My parents were immediately liberated from prison; and having been

[013-169]informed by the old nurse of what had been done by me, were quite

[013-170]prepared when I went to them in public; and afterwards, when we met

[013-171]in private, were able to give way to their feelings of affection and

[013-172]delight at seeing me again.
