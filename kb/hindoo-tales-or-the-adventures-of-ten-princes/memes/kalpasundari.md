## Biography

Kalpasundari was the queen of [[vikatavarma|Vikatavarma]], the usurper who imprisoned his uncle [[praharavarma|Prahâravarma]] and seized his kingdom. She was the daughter of the sovereign of Kumâra and was described as exceedingly beautiful and accomplished. (012-090)

Despite her beauty and talents, Kalpasundari was married to [[vikatavarma|Vikatavarma]], whom she despised for his ugliness and brutish nature. Though unkindly treated and neglected by her husband, she remained faithful to him. (012-092)

After the rebellion, [[upaharavarma|Upahâravarma]] gained access to her palace through a mendicant's daughter who had entered Kalpasundari's service. Through this attendant, [[upaharavarma|Upahâravarma]] learned the palace's arrangements and eventually encountered the queen herself. (012-055) (012-089)

## Role

Kalpasundari appears in [[upaharavarma|Upahâravarma]]'s narrative as the queen of the usurper who had overthrown [[praharavarma|Prahâravarma]]. She is a key figure in [[upaharavarma|Upahâravarma]]'s scheme to infiltrate the palace by entering her service. Her unhappy marriage to the ugly [[vikatavarma|Vikatavarma]] serves as a point of sympathy and forms part of the tale's commentary on ill-matched [[marriage-in-the-tales|union]]s.
