# Funeral in the Tales

## Summary

Funeral practices in the *Tales of Ten Princes* centre on the ritual of the funeral pyre, where the body of the deceased is consigned to flames. The most dramatic funeral sequence occurs in Chapter 15, where a widow resolves to ascend the funeral pyre alongside her dead husband in the practice of sati — a scene that evokes both horror and admiration within the narrative.

## Context

The funeral pyre serves as the ultimate test of wifely devotion in the tales. The widow's decision to join her husband in death is portrayed with solemn gravity, and the preparations for the rite are described in ritual detail. This episode reflects the cultural values of the society Daṇḍin depicts, where a wife's loyalty extends beyond death.

## Key Participants

- The widow who resolves to perform sati — central figure of the funeral sequence
- The deceased husband — whose body lies upon the funeral pyre

## Outcome

The funeral sequence culminates in the widow's self-immolation, a act treated within the narrative as the ultimate demonstration of marital fidelity. The scene leaves a lasting impression on witnesses within the story.
