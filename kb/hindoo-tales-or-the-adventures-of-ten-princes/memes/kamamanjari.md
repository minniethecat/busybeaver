# Kâmamanjari

Kâmamanjari was a clever and beautiful actress and dancer who appears in the
eighth chapter of the narrative. She is remembered for seducing the great muni
Mârîchi and for her involvement in the complex affair of the magic purse in
the following chapters.
(008-022) (026-088)

## Biography

Kâmamanjari was an actress and dancer who, together with her mother and
companions, approached the muni Mârîchi under the pretence of renouncing
worldly life. With dishevelled hair and tearful eyes, she threw herself at
his feet and pleaded for spiritual guidance.
(008-022) (008-026)

> "An actress and dancer, called Kâmamanjari, who, with dishevelled hair
> and eyes full of tears, threw herself at his feet."
> (008-022)

The muni, deceived by her apparent piety, agreed to teach her. Over time,
Kâmamanjari seduced him entirely, persuading him to put on gay dress and
abandon his ascetic vows.
(008-106) (026-088)

Kâmamanjari had a sister named [[râgamanjari|Râgamanjari]], who was also an actress and
made her first appearance as a dancer during the narrative.
(009-177) (026-153)

> "On that day Kâmamanjari persuaded the muni to put on a gay dress and
> accompany her to the festival."
> (008-106)

Kâmamanjari later became entangled in the affair of the magic purse. She
received small presents through an intermediary and was suspected of having
obtained the purse through unlawful means.
(010-033) (010-077)

When the purse was recovered, a portion of the confiscated property was
given to Kâmamanjari as a reward for her role in the resolution.
(010-105)

## Role in the Narrative

Kâmamanjari serves as an example of worldly temptation overcoming spiritual
discipline. Her seduction of Mârîchi, a great muni, illustrates the narrative's
recurring theme of the tension between ascetic ideals and earthly desires.

> "I said to Kâmamanjari: 'No doubt you are suspected of having the
> purse; but if you follow my advice, all may yet be well.'"
> (010-083)

She also functions as a clever operator in the intrigue surrounding the magic
purse, demonstrating quick thinking and resourcefulness in navigating the
dangers of being implicated in theft.
(010-083) (010-105)

## Relationships

Kâmamanjari was the daughter of an old woman who managed the troupe of
performers. Her sister was [[râgamanjari|Râgamanjari]], likewise an actress. Her most
notable relationship was with the muni Mârîchi, whom she seduced and
controlled. She also dealt with the prince who sought the return of the
magic purse.
(026-088) (026-113) (026-153)
