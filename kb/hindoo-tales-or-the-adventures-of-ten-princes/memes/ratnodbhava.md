# Ratnodbhava

Ratnodbhava is one of the companions of Prince [[Râjavâhana]], the son of a
minister of the King of [[magadha|Magadha]] who became a merchant and travelled widely by
land and sea.
(001-107)
(001-116)

## Biography

Ratnodbhava was born the son of a minister of the King of [[magadha|Magadha]], one of four
sons assigned as companions to Prince [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]]:

> "the second, two, Susruta and Ratnodbhava; and the last had also two, Sumati
> and Satyavarma."
> (001-107)

After completing his education, Ratnodbhava took up the life of a merchant:

> "Ratnodbhava became a merchant, and in the way of traffic made many long
> journeys by land and sea."
> (001-116)

He later arrived on an island where he met fellow companion [[Dhanamittra]]:

> "one day a young merchant, named Ratnodbhava, son of a minister of the King
> of Magadha, arrived in the island"
> (002-100)

During his travels he introduced himself to another companion:

> "My name is Ratnodbhava; I am the son of a minister of the King of Magadha;
> travelling about as a merchant"
> (004-129)

Ratnodbhava married the daughter of [[kalayavana|Kâlayavana]]. While crossing the sea together,
their ship was struck by a great storm and sank, but after many trials he was
eventually reunited with his wife.
(004-155)

## Role in the Narrative

Ratnodbhava's story is one of the adventures recounted among the companions of
[[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]]. His narrative illustrates the perils of maritime travel in ancient
India and the theme of lovers separated and reunited — a recurring motif in the
*Daśakumāracarita*.

After the companions were scattered by fate, Ratnodbhava was presumed lost, and
his fellow companions hoped that he "might have escaped" the dangers that befell
them.
(002-135)

## Relationships

Ratnodbhava was the foster-brother and companion of Prince [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]], appointed
to him by the King of [[magadha|Magadha]].
(001-107)

His fellow companions included [[susruta|Susruta]], who was paired with him as one of the
two sons of the second minister.
(001-107)

He met and befriended [[Dhanamittra]] during his merchant travels.
(002-100)

He married the daughter of [[kalayavana|Kâlayavana]], who was shipwrecked with him.
(004-155)
