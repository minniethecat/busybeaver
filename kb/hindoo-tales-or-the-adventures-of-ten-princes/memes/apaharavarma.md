# Apahâravarma

Apahâravarma was one of the ten princes and the son of [[Prahâravarma]], King of
[[Mithila]]. He was a close companion of [[Râjavâhana]] and narrated his own
adventures in Chapter 8 of the *Daśakumāracarita*.
(026-043)

## Biography

When still an infant, Apahâravarma was brought to the court of [[Râjahansa]]
and given to the queen to raise alongside her own son.
(002-092)

> "…giving him the name of Apahâravarma, and begging her to bring him up
> with her own son."
> (002-093)

He grew up as a trusted companion of Prince [[Râjavâhana]], and his father
[[praharavarma|Prahâravarma]] was the [[praharavarma|King of Mithila]].
(026-125)

As an adult, Apahâravarma encountered his friend [[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]] during a battle,
and after recognising each other, they turned the tide of the conflict together.
(007-155) (007-157)

> "Is it possible? Is it really you, my dear friend Apahâravarma, who have
> done this deed?"
> (007-155)

Apahâravarma later introduced [[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]] to Prince [[Râjavâhana]], vouching
for his bravery and cleverness.
(007-172) (007-173)

He employed [[vimardaka|Vimardaka]], the keeper of a gaming house, in one of his schemes.
(026-162)

## Role in the Narrative

Apahâravarma is the first of the ten princes to tell his story when the
companions reunite. His tale constitutes the whole of Chapter 8, titled
"Adventures of Apahâravarma," in which he recounts his exploits and the
cunning stratagems he employed in the service of the prince.
(008-001) (008-003)

During the war, Apahâravarma played a crucial role in rallying the allies and
defeating the enemy forces, working alongside [[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]] and the other
companions.
(007-168) (007-170)

## Relationships

Apahâravarma was the son of [[Prahâravarma]], the [[praharavarma|King of Mithila]].
(026-125)

He was a close friend of [[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]], the husband of [[Kulapâlika]].
(026-074)

He was allied in friendship with the other nine companions of [[Râjavâhana]],
particularly [[upaharavarma|Upahâravarma]], [[pramati|Pramati]], [[mitragupta|Mitragupta]], and [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]], with whom he
fought in battle.
(007-183)

> "Then Apahâravarma introduced the stranger to the prince, saying, 'This is
> my dear friend Dhanamittra, well worthy of your respect and consideration;
> for he is as brave and clever as he is handsome.'"
> (007-172)
