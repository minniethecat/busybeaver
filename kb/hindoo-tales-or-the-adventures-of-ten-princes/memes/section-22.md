[022-001]In this manner I was soon able to collect a number of disciples, glad

[022-002]to live in idleness on the offerings continually brought to me, fully

[022-003]believing in my sanctity, entirely devoted to me, and ready to obey

[022-004]all my commands.

[022-005]Having got together this troop of followers, I went to the side of a

[022-006]tank or small lake not far from the city of Andhra, built myself a

[022-007]hut, and made known that I intended to stay there for a time.

[022-008]The news of my arrival was soon spread abroad by my disciples, who

[022-009]were loud in their praises of my miraculous powers, and the wonderful

[022-010]cures which I had effected; and great numbers of people came from the

[022-011]city to see me, either from curiosity or from the hope of receiving

[022-012]some benefit.

[022-013]In a very short time wonderful stories about me were brought to the

[022-014]Râja. "There is now a very holy devotee sleeping on the ground near

[022-015]the lake; he is possessed of the most marvellous knowledge. There is

[022-016]no question which he cannot answer, no difficulty which he cannot

[022-017]solve. His power of healing is beyond belief; a few grains of dust

[022-018]fallen from his feet, when sprinkled on the head of the sick, are more

[022-019]efficacious than any medicine; and water in which his feet have been

[022-020]washed has cured in a moment diseases, and driven out evil spirits

[022-021]which have resisted for a long time all the efforts of physicians and

[022-022]exorcists. Yet with all this he is exceedingly kind and

[022-023]condescending, and free from pride."

[022-024]The king, hearing all this, thought: "This is just the person I am in

[022-025]need of; no doubt he will be able to cure the princess." He therefore

[022-026]determined to apply to me; but so great was his respect for my dignity

[022-027]and supernatural powers, that he did not venture to send for me, but

[022-028]came several times to see me, distributing each time money among my

[022-029]followers, before mentioning his request that I would drive out the

[022-030]evil spirit from the princess.

[022-031]After hearing his statement, I looked very grave, and appeared for

[022-032]some time to be wrapped in profound meditation. At last I said: "Sir,

[022-033]you have done very right to apply to me; I will undertake that the

[022-034]lady shall be cured, but it would be useless for me to see her at

[022-035]present. The case is a very peculiar one, and the cure requires much

[022-036]thought and consideration; wait therefore for three days, then come

[022-037]again, and I will tell you what is to be done." On receiving this

[022-038]answer, the king went away very well satisfied.

[022-039]That night, as soon as it was dark, telling my followers on no account

[022-040]to disturb me, I went, as if for private meditation, to one side of

[022-041]the tank, at some distance from the steps, and there dug a large hole

[022-042]in the bank sloping upwards, with the opening partly under water and

[022-043]concealed by loose stones above; taking care to throw the excavated

[022-044]earth into the tank.

[022-045]On the third day, at dawn, I rearranged my dress as before, and having

[022-046]worshipped the all-seeing sun as he rose, returned to my followers.

[022-047]I had not long been settled in my usual place when the king made his

[022-048]appearance, and bowing down to my feet, he awaited my pleasure.

[022-049]Having kept him a short time in suspense, I thus addressed him:

[022-050]"Success does not come to the careless, but all advantages are

[022-051]attainable by the energetic; being devoted to your service, I have

[022-052]given my whole mind to the consideration of this difficult affair, and

[022-053]can now point out a certain way to success.

[022-054]"The evil spirit by whom the princess is possessed cannot bear the

[022-055]sight of you in your present form, and therefore breaks out into fury

[022-056]when you appear. If your body can be changed, he will no longer be

[022-057]offended, and will immediately depart; there is no other way by which

[022-058]he can be driven out. I have therefore so prepared this lake that if

[022-059]you bathe in it in accordance with my directions, you will acquire a

[022-060]new and beautiful body acceptable to the lady, and she will no more be

[022-061]troubled with the evil spirit.

[022-062]"You must therefore come here at midnight, and having stripped

[022-063]entirely, swim out into the middle of the tank, and there float on

[022-064]your back as long as possible. Presently a rushing noise will be

[022-065]heard, and the water will be troubled, and dash against the bank. As

[022-066]soon as the commotion has subsided, come forth; you will find that

[022-067]your body has become younger, stronger, and improved in every respect;

[022-068]and when you return to the palace there will be no further difficulty

[022-069]or obstacle on the part of the princess, who will immediately undergo

[022-070]a change in her feelings, and will long for your society as much as

[022-071]she now abhors it. All this is quite certain; you need not have the

[022-072]smallest doubt; but if you think proper, before deciding, consult your

[022-073]ministers, and be guided by their advice. If they consent, first

[022-074]worship the gods and propitiate them with offerings, make large

[022-075]donations to the brahmans and the poor, and come here to-night at the

[022-076]appointed time. That there may be no danger from alligators or

[022-077]concealed enemies, let the tank be thoroughly dragged with nets by a

[022-078]hundred fishermen, and place a line of soldiers all round it with

[022-079]torches in their hands a few steps from the water; with these

[022-080]precautions no possible harm can happen to you."

[022-081]The enamoured king, very anxious for the expulsion of the supposed

[022-082]demon, and fully believing that I had the power to perform what I had

[022-083]promised, went away well pleased, and immediately consulted his

[022-084]ministers. They seeing how eager he was, and not anticipating any

[022-085]possibility of danger, readily approved of the proceeding.

[022-086]Having obtained their consent the king returned to me, and finding

[022-087]that I was about to depart, earnestly entreated me to stay, saying

[022-088]that half the pleasure of success would be taken away if I were not

[022-089]there to witness it; but I answered that there were urgent reasons for

[022-090]my immediate departure, and that I had already remained longer than I

[022-091]had intended to do, solely on his account. I assured him that I had so

[022-092]prepared everything that my presence was now quite unnecessary, that I

[022-093]was about to disappear from the world, and that he would see me no

[022-094]more. Finding me quite determined, he took leave of me with many

[022-095]expressions of respect, and went back to his palace to give orders for

[022-096]the performance of all that I had directed.

[022-097]Accordingly, a large number of fishermen with nets were engaged, by

[022-098]whom the lake was thoroughly dragged, and large donations were made to

[022-099]the brahmans and the poor. Towards evening, soldiers with torches were

[022-100]placed, all round the tank, and at midnight the king, attended by a

[022-101]numerous retinue, and followed by a great crowd anxious to witness the

[022-102]expected miracle, came to the steps leading down to the water, and

[022-103]having undressed there in a tent which had been pitched for that

[022-104]purpose, plunged in and swam out to the middle.

[022-105]Meanwhile I had said to my followers: "I have no further need of you;

[022-106]I am about to retire to a lonely place to practise meditation; you may

[022-107]now leave me; go, and my blessing be upon you." Well satisfied with

[022-108]the gifts they had received, they departed; and when they were gone I

[022-109]slipped unobserved into the lake, and entered the hole which I had

[022-110]prepared. There I remained till I heard the noise of the crowd who

[022-111]came with the king, and perceived him floating on the surface. Diving

[022-112]cautiously under him, I pulled him down, strangled him, and dragged

[022-113]the body into the hole; then swimming to the steps, I boldly came

[022-114]forth, to the astonishment of the attendants, who, though they had

[022-115]expected a miracle, were scarcely prepared for such a great change. No

[022-116]one, however, doubted that I was really their sovereign, and having

[022-117]dressed and mounted an elephant, I entered the city, escorted by the

[022-118]soldiers and followed by a great crowd of people, who had come forth

[022-119]from curiosity, and were loud in their praises of the pious man who

[022-120]had wrought such a miracle.

[022-121]That night I was unable to sleep. In the morning I summoned all the

[022-122]ministers and counsellors, and said: "Behold the power of piety and

[022-123]penance. That holy man has performed a great miracle, and bestowed on

[022-124]me this new body, which you see, by means of the tank which he has

[022-125]consecrated, and through the favour of the gods, whom he had long

[022-126]propitiated; after such a manifestation, who shall doubt their power?

[022-127]Let the faces of all unbelievers be bowed down by shame; let a great

[022-128]and solemn festival be made with song and dance in honour of Brahma,

[022-129]Siva, Yama, and the other deities, the rulers of the world, and

[022-130]distribute much money among the poor."

[022-131]This speech was received with great approbation, and all,

[022-132]congratulating me and praising the gods, performed the duties imposed

[022-133]upon them.

[022-134]After this I went to the women's apartments, and there the first

[022-135]person whom I met was a very devoted servant of the princess, who had

[022-136]been especially attentive to me. She, not imagining what had occurred,

[022-137]would have let me pass without especial notice; but I called her, and

[022-138]said: "Have you never seen me before?"

[022-139]Then indeed she opened her eyes wide with joy and astonishment,

[022-140]saying: "Can it be possible? is not this a delusion? Tell me what it

[022-141]all means."

[022-142]I gave her a brief account of what had happened, and sent her to

[022-143]prepare my wife. How glad she was to see me you may well imagine.

[022-144]So well did we manage, that the secret was kept, no suspicion even

[022-145]arose, and all the people were rejoiced at the favourable change, not

[022-146]only in the person, but in the temper and disposition of their

[022-147]sovereign.

[022-148]In due time I was publicly married to the princess, and reinstated her

[022-149]father in his kingdom.

[022-150]I have now come here with an army to assist the King of Anga, and have

[022-151]thus obtained the great happiness of seeing you again.

[022-152]The prince, having heard this story, said "Your cleverness has indeed

[022-153]been great, and your personation of the Siddha wonderful. May you

[022-154]long continue to possess such wisdom and prudence, combined with wit

[022-155]and cheerfulness." Then, looking at Visruta, he said: "It is now your

[022-156]turn;" and he forthwith began:--

[022-158]ADVENTURES OF VISRUTA.

[022-159]My Lord, as I was wandering one day in the forest of Vindhya, I met

[022-160]with a very handsome boy, standing by the side of a well, crying

[022-161]bitterly. When I asked what was the matter, he said: "The old man who

[022-162]was with me, when trying to get water from this well, fell in, and I

[022-163]am unable to help him. What will become of me?"

[022-164]Hearing this, I looked down the well, which was not very deep, and saw

[022-165]the old man standing at the bottom, the water not being sufficient to

[022-166]cover him. By means of a long and tough stem of a creeper, I pulled

[022-167]him up safely; then using it again as a rope, with a cup made from

[022-168]the hollow stem of a bamboo, I drew water for the poor child, who was

[022-169]half dead with thirst; and finding that he was suffering from hunger

[022-170]also, I knocked down some nuts from the top of a high tree with a

[022-171]well-aimed blow of a stone.
