# Kantaka

Kantaka was the gaoler of the King of [[magadha|Magadha]] who attempted to ruin
[[Upahâravarma]] by falsely accusing him, and was killed by the prince
in retaliation.

## Biography

Kantaka had lately succeeded to the office of gaoler by the death of his
father, a man named Viraketu, whom he had followed in the post.
(010-150)

> "There was in the court-yard of the palace a man, named Kantaka, who had
> lately succeeded to the office by the death of his father, a man named
> Viraketu."
> (010-150)

He encountered [[Upahâravarma]] in the court-yard of the palace, where Kantaka
had some business or other and noticed the prince.
(011-007)

Kantaka came to [[upaharavarma|Upahâravarma]] and told him what he wanted, swearing a solemn
oath of secrecy. He offered to assist the prince in exchange for a share of
whatever was gained.
(011-067)

> "After this, Kantaka came to me, told me what he wanted, and swore a
> solemn oath that he would not tell his secret to anyone."
> (011-067)

Kantaka was waiting for [[upaharavarma|Upahâravarma]] and told him it was necessary to
replace his worn-out clothes, taking him to a suitable place.
(011-078)

## Role in the Narrative

Kantaka plays the role of an antagonist in the story of **[[Upahâravarma]]**.
He made a false accusation against the prince, which led to a direct
confrontation before the King.
(011-122)

> "I told the king the false accusation made by Kantaka."
> (011-122)

[[upaharavarma|Upahâravarma]] killed Kantaka in response to the false accusation, with the
consent of the King. The prince then went forth from the palace.
(011-124)

Kantaka was killed when about to enter the inner chambers, and his position
was subsequently taken over by the prince's associates.
(011-155)

> "I related all that had happened, and how Kantaka had been killed when
> about to enter the inner palace."
> (011-155)

The character index records Kantaka simply as the gaoler killed by
[[upaharavarma|Upahâravarma]].
(026-093)

## Relationships

Kantaka served in the court of the **King of [[magadha|Magadha]]**, where he held the
office of gaoler, succeeding his father Viraketu in the post.
(010-150)

His primary antagonist was **[[Upahâravarma]]**, one of the ten princes, whom
he falsely accused and by whose hand he was ultimately killed.
(011-122)
