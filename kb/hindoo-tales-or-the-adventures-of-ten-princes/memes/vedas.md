# Vedas

## Definition

The Vedas are the sacred scriptures of the Hindoo tradition — the foundational texts of religious knowledge, ritual obligation, and philosophical inquiry. In the narrative, they represent the highest form of learning, studied by princes and brahmans alike, and serve as a marker of proper education and religious orthodoxy.

## In the Narrative

The Vedas appear in contexts of education and royal duty. A prince is described as having been taught the Vedas (003-019), and a broader reference to the Vedas, religious obligations, and philosophy frames the curriculum of the learned (003-050). A character is praised as advanced in the Vedas and commentaries (017-088). In a discussion of kingly duties, the Vedas are listed among what is proper for kings to study (023-055). A footnote glosses a verse or chapter in the Vedas as a prayer or recited words (026-111).

> "… had not only been taught the vedas and the…" (003-019)

> "vedas, religious obligations, and f…" (003-050)

> "the vedas and commentaries, advanced in…" (017-088)
