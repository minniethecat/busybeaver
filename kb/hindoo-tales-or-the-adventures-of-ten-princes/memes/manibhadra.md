# Manibhadra

**Manibhadra** is a [[Yaksha]], a nature spirit, and the father of [[Târâvali]], who became the wife of [[Kâmapâla]].

## Biography

Manibhadra was a Yaksha whose daughter [[taravali|Târâvali]] appeared before Queen [[Vasumati]] in a vision. The Yaksha lady, holding an infant child, revealed herself to the queen, saying she was the daughter of Manibhadra and the wife of [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]]. By command of the god [[Kuvera]], she had brought the child to enter the service of the queen's son. (002-140–143) This child was [[Râjavâhana]], the future crown prince.

Manibhadra is listed in the character index of Chapter 26, confirming his identity as a Yaksha and the father of [[taravali|Târâvali]]. (026-109)

> "O queen, I am a Yaksha, daughter of Manibhadra, and wife of Kâmapâla, the son of your husband's late minister, Dharmapâla; by command of Kuvera, I have brought this my child to you, that he may enter the service of your son." (002-142–143)
