# Pilgrimage

## Definition

Pilgrimage (Tîrtha-yâtrâ) is a journey to a sacred place undertaken for religious purposes. In the Hindoo world, pilgrimage is both an act of devotion and a means of purification — characters travel to holy rivers, mountain shrines, and temples to pay homage to deities, to fulfil vows, and to acquire spiritual merit. The practice is widespread across all social classes and is woven into the rhythm of religious life.

## In the Narrative

Pilgrimage appears across the narrative as a common and significant religious practice. In Chapter 2, a character recounts returning from a pilgrimage to [[ramatirtha|Râmatirtha]], a sacred bathing-place (002-095), while another describes undertaking pilgrimages to "several holy places" as part of a wandering life (002-153). In Chapter 5, the prince [[darpasara|Darpasâra]] is said to have departed on a pilgrimage to the [[himalaya|Himâlaya]] mountains — a destination associated with [[siva|Siva]] and ascetic power (005-050). Later in the narrative, a character who has passed many years in pilgrimages to holy sites is regarded with reverence for his accumulated spiritual authority (020-094). The [[siva|Siva]] temple at [[mahakala|Mahâkâla]] is described as "the object of many pilgrimages," indicating that pilgrimage destinations were well-established and widely recognised (026-101).

> "As I was returning from a pilgrimage to Râmatirtha…" (002-095)

> "…a pilgrimage to several holy places, and…" (002-153)

> "…Darpasâra, who is now gone on a pilgrimage to the Himâlaya…" (005-050)

> "…have passed so many years in pilgrimages to holy…" (020-094)

> "…the object of many pilgrimages." (026-101)
