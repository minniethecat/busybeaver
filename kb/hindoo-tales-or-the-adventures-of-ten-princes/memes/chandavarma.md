# Chandavarma

Chandavarma was a regent and antagonist in the narrative, known for his siege
of [[champa|Champa]] and his pursuit of Princess [[ambalika|Ambâlika]]. His story ends with his dramatic
murder by an assassin.
(007-029)

## Biography

Chandavarma served as regent and had significant military and political power.
He asked in marriage [[ambalika|Ambâlika]], the sister of a rival ruler, pressing his suit
with determination.
(007-063)

> "Chandavarma had some time before this asked in marriage Ambâlika, the..."
> (007-063)

Having gained control of the princess, Chandavarma determined to press his
advantage and solidify his rule.
(007-076)

He intended to march against the allies who were advancing against him,
showing his military ambition and aggressive stance.
(007-089)

## Role in the Narrative

Chandavarma serves as one of the key antagonists in the story of the princes.
He besieged the city of [[champa|Champa]], an act remembered and referenced in later
chapters.
(011-165)

> "You know how Chandavarma besieged Champa, and how Sinhavarma was..."
> (011-165)

His story reaches its climax with his violent death:

> "Chandavarma is murdered! killed by an assassin, who stabbed him as he..."
> (007-138)

The slayer of Chandavarma came out of the palace after the deed, and news
of the regent's death emboldened his enemies to attack.
(007-150) (011-170)

Chandavarma was also referenced in the context of marching to attack another
character, showing his persistent aggression throughout the narrative.
(014-006)

## Relationships

Chandavarma pursued [[ambalika|Ambâlika]] in marriage and held her in his power. He was
opposed by [[sinhavarma|Sinhavarma]] during the siege of [[champa|Champa]]. His death at the hands
of an assassin removed a major obstacle for the allied forces.
(007-063) (007-076) (011-165)
