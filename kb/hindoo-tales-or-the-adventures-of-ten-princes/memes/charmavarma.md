# Charmavarma

Charmavarma was the son of the king's sister in [[Mâlwa]] and was appointed
joint regent alongside his brother [[Dâruvarma]] by their cousin
[[Darpasâra]] while the prince went on pilgrimage to the [[himalaya|Himâlaya Mountains]].
(005-050) (005-051) (005-052)

## Biography

Charmavarma was one of the two sons of the king's sister, the other being
his brother [[dâruvarma|Dâruvarma]]. When [[darpasara|Darpasâra]], the son of the King of [[malwa|Mâlwa]],
abdicated temporarily to perform penance on the great mountain, he appointed
Charmavarma and [[dâruvarma|Dâruvarma]] as joint regents of the kingdom.
(005-050) (005-052)

> "The former of these two alone has the management of affairs; for the
> latter, given up to evil deeds, makes use of his power only for the
> indulgence of his licentious passions."
> (005-053) (005-055)

Unlike his brother, who abused his authority to pursue licentious passions,
Charmavarma alone carried out the duties of governance and managed the
affairs of state responsibly.
(005-053) (005-054) (005-055)

## Role in the Narrative

Charmavarma appears in the fifth chapter as the responsible co-regent who
bore the actual burden of administration. His presence in the narrative
serves as a foil to his brother [[dâruvarma|Dâruvarma]]: while [[dâruvarma|Dâruvarma]] pursued
[[Bâlachandrika]] and ultimately met his death through the schemes of
[[Pushpodbhava]], Charmavarma's character demonstrates the contrast between
dutiful governance and corrupt misrule.
(005-052) (005-053) (005-055)

## Relationships
