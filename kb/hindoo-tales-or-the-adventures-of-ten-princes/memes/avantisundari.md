# Avantisundari

Avantisundari is the princess of [[Avanti]], daughter of King [[manasara|Mânasâra]],
and the beloved wife of [[Râjavâhana]], the hero of the
*Daśakumāracarita*. Her name — meaning "the beauty of [[avanti|Avanti]]" — reflects
her status as the jewel of her father's kingdom.
(026-053)

## Biography

Avantisundari was born to King [[manasara|Mânasâra]], the ruler of [[avanti|Avanti]]. As a princess,
she was attended by her women and enjoyed the companionship of her dear
friend [[balachandrika|Bâlachandrika]].
(005-146)

She first encountered [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] when he saw her during his attendance
upon the princess. They fell in love, and their meeting on a sandy spot
under a large tree became a turning point in both their lives.
(005-079)

Her love for [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] was so profound that both suffered greatly from
their separation, unable to meet. Their plight moved the conjuror
[[vidyeswara|Vidyeswara]], who intervened to bring them together.
(026-160)

Ultimately, Avantisundari's brother engaged her to marry [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]],
sealing the alliance between their houses.
(012-056)

## Role in the Narrative

Avantisundari serves as the primary romantic interest in the epic. Her
love story with [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] is one of the central threads that unites the
tales of the ten princes.

The princess is introduced in a vivid scene at a festival, sitting on a
sandy spot under a great tree:

> "Among them was the Princess Avantisundari, who was sitting on a sandy spot, under a large tree, attended by her women, especially by her dear friend Bâlachandrika."
> (005-146)

Her love at first sight with [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] is told by a companion:

> "Not long ago, he, the son of a king, met the Princess Avantisundari on this very spot, and they fell in love with each other. From the impossibility of meeting, both are suffering."
> (005-155)

The alliance was sealed when her brother betrothed her to [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]]:

> "He was engaged to marry his sister, the Princess Avantisundari."
> (012-056)

Through her [[marriage]], Avantisundari united the kingdom of [[avanti|Avanti]] with
the house of [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]].

## Relationships

Avantisundari was the daughter of King [[manasara|Mânasâra]] of [[avanti|Avanti]].
(026-053)

She was the beloved wife of [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]], the hero of the ten princes.
(026-053)

Her dear friend was [[balachandrika|Bâlachandrika]], who later married [[pushpodbhava|Pushpodbhava]].
(026-055)

The conjuror [[vidyeswara|Vidyeswara]] played a key role in uniting her with [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]].
(026-160)
