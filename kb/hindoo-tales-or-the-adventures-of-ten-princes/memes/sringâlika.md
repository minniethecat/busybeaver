# Sringâlika

Sringâlika was the nurse of [[Râgamanjari]], an actress of a wandering
troupe managed by her mother. A remarkably quick-witted old woman, Sringâlika
served as a vital go-between for [[Râgamanjari]]'s husband, the gambler,
during his imprisonment, and later helped him gain the favour of Princess
[[ambalika|Ambâlika]]. She also appears in connection with [[Kâmamanjari]]'s household and
the circle around [[Dhanamittra]].
(010-130) (026-146)

## Biography

Sringâlika was the nurse of [[Râgamanjari]], the younger daughter of an old
woman who managed a company of actresses and dancers. Her sister was
[[Kâmamanjari]], likewise a performer in the same troupe.
(009-177) (026-146)

Sringâlika first appears when she follows the narrator — the gambler who
had married [[Râgamanjari]] — as he was arrested by the police after a
drunken rampage. The gambler called out to her, pretending to curse his wife
and [[Dhanamittra]], but Sringâlika, described as "remarkably quick-witted,"
immediately understood that this was a coded message meant to protect them all.
(010-130) (010-134)

> "The old woman being remarkably quick-witted, at once understood my object
> in speaking thus, and very humbly accosting the police said: 'Worthy sir, I
> entreat you to wait a moment, while I ask your prisoner where he has hid the
> ornaments of my mistress.'"
> (010-134)

Playing along with the ruse, she approached the prisoner and received hurried
instructions, which she relayed faithfully to [[Dhanamittra]]. Following the
plan, [[dhanamittra|Dhanamittra]] went to the king and procured a stay of execution for the
gambler, saving his life.
(010-146) (010-167) (010-179)

Later, when the gambler had recovered his strength in prison, Sringâlika
visited him, "well dressed, and with cheerful countenance," and was permitted
to speak with him in private. She joyfully reported that the plan had succeeded
and that his life was safe for the present.
(010-164) (010-166)

## Role in the Narrative

Sringâlika's role in the narrative extends well beyond that of a mere nurse.
She functions as a trusted ally and go-between, enabling the gambler's
communication with the outside world during his imprisonment, and later
facilitating his access to the royal palace.

After the gambler killed [[kantaka|Kantaka]], the governor of the prison, he returned
to Sringâlika, who had remained waiting in the prison. She provided him with
directions to the ladies' rooms in the palace, enabling him to pass through a
tunnel and steal valuables before their escape.
(011-083) (011-087)

Sringâlika then escaped from the prison together with the gambler. When they
encountered a patrol shortly after leaving, the gambler feared for her safety
and devised a stratagem to protect her, knowing that "the poor old woman" would
certainly be caught if he simply fled.
(011-125) (011-127)

> "I then returned to Sringâlika, who had remained in the prison, and said to
> her: 'I am not disposed to have had all this toil for nothing. Tell me the
> way into the ladies' rooms. I will go there and steal something before I make
> my escape.' Having received her directions, I passed again through the tunnel
> which I had made."
> (011-083)

Most notably, Sringâlika had already cultivated an intimacy with the nurse of
Princess [[ambalika|Ambâlika]], using gifts furnished by her mistress. Through this
connection, she spoke favourably of the gambler to the princess, paving the
way for his eventual access to the royal women's apartments.
(011-001) (011-161)

> "Meanwhile, with the help of gifts furnished by my mistress, I have formed
> an intimacy with the nurse of the Princess Ambâlika, and have been introduced
> by her to the princess, whose favour I have gained by telling her amusing
> stories."
> (011-001)
