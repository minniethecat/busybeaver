# Prahâravarma

Prahâravarma was the King of Mithila and the father of [[Apahâravarma]], one
of the nine companions of Prince [[Râjavâhana]]. He was a sovereign of noble
character who fought in a great battle against the King of [[malwa|Mâlwa]] alongside
his allies.
(002-039) (026-125)

## Biography

Prahâravarma ruled as the King of Mithila, a kingdom neighbouring [[magadha|Magadha]]
and other realms of the narrative. He was a contemporary and ally of
[[Râjahansa]], the King of [[magadha|Magadha]].
(002-039) (026-125)

The king, together with his family, travelled to [[pushpapuri|Pushpapuri]] to attend the
Sîmanta festival of the queen, staying there for some time after the
departure of the other guests.
(002-039)

> "Prahâravarma, King of Mithila — that he, with his family, had gone to
> Pushpapuri, to be present at the Sîmanta festival of the queen."
> (002-039)

While Prahâravarma was away, the King of [[malwa|Mâlwa]], armed with a magic weapon,
invaded his country. Prahâravarma assisted his friend with the few soldiers
who accompanied him, and a great battle ensued.
(002-043) (012-021)

> "Prahâravarma was formerly king of this country. His queen was a very
> beautiful woman."
> (012-016)

Prahâravarma was eventually conquered and lost his kingdom. His brother
related the story of his downfall years later, confirming the tragic end
to his reign.
(013-112)

His son [[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]] grew up to become one of the nine companions of Prince
[[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]], a testament to the enduring bond between the royal houses.
(026-043)

## Role in the Narrative

Prahâravarma serves as an example of a noble king brought low by the fortunes
of war. His story establishes the political landscape of the narrative,
showing the network of alliances and conflicts among the kingdoms. His defeat
at the hands of the King of [[malwa|Mâlwa]] provides the backdrop for his son
[[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]]'s later adventures as one of the companions.
(002-039) (026-043)

The king is listed among the sovereigns who attended important events,
alongside [[visruta|Visruta]] and [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]], indicating his standing among the regional
rulers.
(007-184)

## Relationships

Prahâravarma was married to Queen [[priyamvadâ|Priyamvadâ]]. He was the father of
[[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]] and [[upaharavarma|Upahâravarma]]. His brother's son continued the family
line and told the tale of Prahâravarma's fall. He was allied with the
kings of neighbouring realms, including the King of [[magadha|Magadha]].
(026-125) (026-127) (013-112)
