# Bhimadhanwa

Bhimadhanwa is the brother of Princess [[kandukavati|Kandukavati]] and an antagonist in the
adventures of the ten princes. He is described as a prince who persecutes his
own sister with unwanted advances and is eventually overcome by her allies.
(017-158)
(026-060)

## Biography

Bhimadhanwa was the son of a king and the brother of the princess [[kandukavati|Kandukavati]].
His story emerges through the narrative of those who opposed him:

> "Of late, Bhimadhanwa, the brother of the princess, has cast his eyes on her,
> and persecuted her with his importunities."
> (017-158)

Despite his threatening behaviour, those who protected the princess determined
that he could be managed:

> "there will be no further danger, since Bhimadhanwa will be subject to you,
> and you will be able easily to protect me."
> (018-070)

Bhimadhanwa later introduced himself directly to those he had been opposing,
appearing in disguise as a captain. He was eventually bound with an iron chain
and taken prisoner by the very man he had sought to harm.
(018-081)
(018-087)
(018-117)

## Role in the Narrative

Bhimadhanwa serves as an antagonist figure in the [[kandukavati|Kandukavati]] episode. His
persecution of his sister creates the dramatic tension that drives her to seek
allies. His eventual capture and binding represent a moral lesson on the
consequences of unchecked desire and abuse of familial authority.

The episode involving Bhimadhanwa is recounted as part of the wider adventures
of the princes' companions in the later sections of the narrative.
(025-103)

## Relationships

Bhimadhanwa is the brother of [[kandukavati|Kandukavati]] (Kandukavat), the princess whom he
persecuted.
(017-158)
(026-060)

He was opposed and eventually captured by a captain who turned out to be one of
the companions of [[Râjavâhana]].
(018-117)

His father was the king who ruled over the region where these events took place.
(025-103)
