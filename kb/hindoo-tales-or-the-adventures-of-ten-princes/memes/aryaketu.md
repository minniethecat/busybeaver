## Biography

Aryaketu was an old and much-respected minister who served in the court visited by [[visruta|Visruta]]. He was known for his wisdom, integrity, and loyalty. (025-048)

When [[visruta|Visruta]] sought to make allies in the court, he identified Aryaketu as the most trustworthy figure, believing that if he could gain Aryaketu's confidence, the minister would be of great assistance to him. (025-048)

When others in the court spoke against [[visruta|Visruta]], arguing that a foreigner should not hold such high office, Aryaketu defended him vigorously. He praised [[visruta|Visruta]]'s wonderful ability, great courage, generosity, and kindness, declaring that so many good qualities were rarely found in one person. (025-057)

## Role

Aryaketu serves as a moral anchor in [[visruta|Visruta]]'s narrative, representing loyalty and fair judgment in a court rife with jealousy and intrigue. His defence of [[visruta|Visruta]] against xenophobic detractors demonstrates the theme of recognising merit regardless of origin, and his support proves crucial to [[visruta|Visruta]]'s standing at court.

## Quotations

> "To all this Aryaketu answered: 'Do not speak against so good a man, and one of such wonderful ability, endowed with such great courage, generosity, and kindness. So many good qualities are rarely found...'" (025-057)
