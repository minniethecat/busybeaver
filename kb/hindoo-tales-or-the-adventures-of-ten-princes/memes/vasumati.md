# Vasumati

Vasumati was the Queen of [[magadha|Magadha]], wife of King [[Râjahansa]], and mother of
[[Râjavâhana]], the central hero of the *Daśakumāracarita*. Her noble lineage
and gentle character made her a model queen in the narrative.

## Biography

The Queen Vasumati was worthy of her royal husband. She was of high birth and
of a sweet temper, and so great was her beauty that it seemed as if she had
been fashioned by the gods themselves.
(001-097)

> "The queen Vasumati was worthy of such a husband. She was of high birth
> and of a sweet temper."
> (001-097)

She was a great friend of the queen consort of the [[praharavarma|King of Mithila]], with whom
she maintained a close companionship that spanned years.
(001-145)

When [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] fell ill, the charming Vasumati personally attended to his
recovery. Once he was sufficiently recovered, she was instructed by the
ministers on how to proceed with the young prince's future.
(002-002)

> "When he was sufficiently recovered, the charming Vasumati, instructed
> by the ministers, said to him..."
> (002-002)

Queen Vasumati took on the care of her son and also received other children
entrusted to her by the court. A child was brought to her that he might be
raised together with [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]], providing companionship for the future
monarch.
(014-114) (014-137)

> "Him to the Queen Vasumati, that he might be brought up together with
> her son, who will one day become a great monarch."
> (014-114)

One night, a fairy appeared to Queen Vasumati and placed the infant [[arthapala|Arthapâla]]
in her arms, revealing his parentage and requesting her protection.
(016-082)

The character index records Vasumati as the Queen of [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]], cementing her
place as the matriarch of the central royal house.
(026-155)

## Role in the Narrative

Vasumati serves as the nurturing center of the royal household. While
[[rajahansa|Râjahansa]] governs the kingdom, Vasumati oversees the upbringing of the
young princes and companions who will go on to have the adventures that
form the core of the narrative.

Her role as foster mother to several of the young companions, including
[[arthapala|Arthapâla]] who was brought to her by a fairy, demonstrates the trust
placed in her by both the mortal court and the divine realm.
(016-082)

She was the intermediary between the ministers' counsel and the young
prince, guiding [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]]'s early development with wisdom and care.
(002-002)

## Relationships

Vasumati was the beloved queen of **[[Râjahansa]]**, King of [[magadha|Magadha]], and the
mother of **[[Râjavâhana]]**, the foremost of the ten princes. Her royal
household became the nurturing ground for the companions of the prince.

She maintained a deep friendship with the queen of **[[mithila|Mithila]]**, who visited
her at the [[magadha|Magadha]]n court, strengthening the bond between the two kingdoms.
(001-145)

Through the fairy's visitation, Vasumati became the guardian of
**[[Arthapâla]]**, one of the nine companions, raising him alongside her
own son. The ministers of the court, including **[[Dharmapâla]]** and
**[[Padmodbhava]]**, advised her on matters concerning the young prince's
education and future.
(014-114) (016-082)
