# Arthapâla

Arthapâla was one of the ten princes, the son of the minister [[Kâmapâla]]
and the Princess [[kantimati|Kantimati]]. He was counted among the nine companions of
[[Râjavâhana]] and his adventures form a distinct narrative thread in the
*Daśakumāracarita*.
(026-047)

## Biography

Arthapâla was born under extraordinary circumstances. His mother [[kantimati|Kantimati]], the
wife of [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]], married a Yaksha, and the child was entrusted to his uncle
[[sumittra|Sumittra]], who gave him the name Arthapâla.
(002-149) (002-150)

In his youth, Arthapâla was engaged in an underground passage from his father's
house to the palace, when he unexpectedly encountered another party who
recognised him.
(015-132)

> "I am Arthapâla, the son of the minister Kâmapâla and the Princess
> Kantimati, and have come thus unexpectedly on you while making an
> underground passage from my father's house to the palace."
> (015-132)

A supernatural revelation later disclosed his true origin: a fairy appeared to
[[vasumati|Queen Vasumati]], placing the infant Arthapâla in her arms and revealing his
parentage, before disappearing.
(016-083) (016-084)

> "I am that fairy—your mother."
> (016-085)

Arthapâla's mother was [[kantimati|Kantimati]], who was herself of divine or semi-divine
origin.
(026-094)

## Role in the Narrative

When the ten princes reunited, [[Râjavâhana]] turned to Arthapâla and invited
him to relate his adventures.
(014-016)

> "Then turning to Arthapâla, he said: 'Do you relate your adventures.'"
> (014-016)

Arthapâla's story occupies several chapters and is noted in the index as being
"quite distinct" from that of [[pramati|Pramati]], another companion.
(026-187)

## Relationships

Arthapâla was the son of [[Kâmapâla]], a minister at the court of
[[Râjahansa]], and [[kantimati|Kantimati]], a princess of supernatural lineage.
(026-047) (026-094)

He was entrusted as a child to his uncle [[sumittra|Sumittra]], who raised him.
(002-149)

He was one of the nine companions of Prince [[Râjavâhana]], alongside
[[Apahâravarma]], [[Upahâravarma]], [[pramati|Pramati]], [[mitragupta|Mitragupta]], [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]], and
others.
(007-183) (026-047)
