# Dâruvarma

Dâruvarma was a prince, the son of the king's sister, who served as joint
regent alongside his brother [[Charmavarma]]. His unwanted pursuit of
[[Bâlachandrika]] led to a fatal scheme devised by [[Pushpodbhava]].
(005-052) (005-094)

## Biography

Dâruvarma was the son of the king's sister in the region of the [[vindhya|Vindhya]]
Mountains. Together with his brother [[charmavarma|Charmavarma]], he was appointed as joint
regent, though [[charmavarma|Charmavarma]] alone managed the affairs of state.
(005-051) (005-052)

He became infatuated with [[balachandrika|Bâlachandrika]], the beautiful daughter of the
merchant [[Bandhupâla]], and persistently pursued her despite her clear
reluctance.
(005-036) (005-094)

> "If Dâruvarma, on hearing this, shall be alarmed, and abstain from
> further annoyance, so much the better; if, on the other hand, he
> should persist, I will come to you as before."
> (005-070)

[[balachandrika|Bâlachandrika]] and [[pushpodbhava|Pushpodbhava]] spread a rumour that she was guarded by a
demon, hoping to deter him. When Dâruvarma remained undeterred by the
report, [[balachandrika|Bâlachandrika]] consented to go to his house — unaware that it was
a trap that would lead to his death at the hands of [[pushpodbhava|Pushpodbhava]].
(005-084) (005-094)

## Role in the Narrative

Dâruvarma appears in the fifth chapter as an antagonist in [[pushpodbhava|Pushpodbhava]]'s
adventure. As joint regent with [[charmavarma|Charmavarma]], he pursued [[balachandrika|Bâlachandrika]]
unwillingly, and his persistence despite warnings set in motion the scheme
that culminated in his death.
(005-052) (005-094)
