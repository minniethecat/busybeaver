# Compassion

## Definition

Compassion (Karunâ or Dayâ) is the moral sentiment of pity for the suffering of others, coupled with the impulse to relieve that suffering. In the Hindoo ethical framework, compassion is a mark of spiritual refinement — it distinguishes the holy man from the indifferent, the virtuous character from the callous. It is frequently exercised by sages, ascetics, and those who have undergone suffering themselves, who are moved to assist strangers and unfortunates.

## In the Narrative

Compassion pervades the narrative as a recurring ethical motif, appearing across nine chapters. In Chapter 2, a compassionate cowherd discovers an abandoned infant and takes care of him, an act of spontaneous kindness that sets a life in motion (002-056). The same cowherd reappears in Chapter 12, where the rescued child recalls being "found and taken care of by a compassionate cowherd" (012-033). A character in Chapter 3 is "seized by a sudden feeling of compassion" upon witnessing distress (003-062), and a holy Siddha "had compassion on" a supplicant, demonstrating that spiritual attainment and compassion are intertwined (003-143).

The pattern of being "moved by compassion" recurs throughout: a narrator sits beside a distressed stranger, "moved by compassion," and inquires into his suffering (008-150); another character alights from his celestial vehicle "moved with compassion" at the sight of human misery (014-104). A "strong feeling of compassion" prompts a character to intervene in another's plight (014-107). In a more worldly context, a woman is urged to "take compassion on her" — a plea for mercy toward an unfortunate lady (010-142). The virtue is not universal: in Chapter 18, a character who "was a foreigner, had not much compassion," implying that compassion is an expected norm whose absence is noted (018-103). A poor wretch becomes "the object of much compassion, and praise," suggesting that compassion carries social recognition (019-021). In the final chapter, a "compassionate perfected devotee" delivers a prophecy, linking compassion to spiritual attainment (026-015).
