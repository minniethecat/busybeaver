# Surat

Surat is a country in ancient India, mentioned in the nineteenth chapter of
the *Daśakumāracarita* as the home of a wealthy ship-captain and his
daughter [[ratnavati|Ratnavati]].
(019-150)

## Geography

The narrative mentions "a town in the country of Surat" where a rich
ship-captain resided. No further geographical details are provided, though
the reference to a ship-captain suggests Surat was a coastal or riverine
settlement engaged in maritime trade.
(019-150)

> "There was, in a town in the country of Surat, a rich ship-captain who
> had a daughter named Ratnavati."
> (019-150)

## Role in the Narrative

Surat appears as the setting for the backstory of [[ratnavati|Ratnavati]],
whose troubled marriage to [[balabhadra|Balabhadra]] forms one of the
exemplary tales told by [[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]] to illustrate his
third answer — concerning the proper conduct of a householder.
(019-149) (019-150) (019-151)
