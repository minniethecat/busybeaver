# Dharmapâla

Dharmapâla was one of the three chief ministers of King [[Râjahansa]] of
[[magadha|Magadha]], alongside [[Padmodbhava]] and [[Sitavarma]]. A pious and loyal
servant of the crown, his descendants played significant roles in the
narrative of the ten princes.

## Biography

Dharmapâla was noted among three ministers whose names were Dharmapâla,
[[padmodbhava|Padmodbhava]], and [[sitavarma|Sitavarma]] — the trusted council of [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]].
(001-105)

> "Their names were, Dharmapâla, Padmodbhava, and Sitavarma."
> (001-105)

He was the father of three sons: [[sumantra|Sumantra]], [[sumittra|Sumittra]], and [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]]. Of these,
[[kamapala|Kâmapâla]] would rise to become a minister and son-in-law of the King of
[[benares|Benâres]], while [[sumantra|Sumantra]] also appears in the chronicles of the court.
(001-105) (026-075)

Dharmapâla is described as a pious man, one of the king's ministers, whose
eldest son followed in his virtuous footsteps.
(014-067)

> "A pious man, named Dharmapâla, one of the king's ministers."
> (014-067)

After Dharmapâla's death, his connection to the divine realm was revealed
when a celestial being appeared, declaring: "By command of [[kuvera|Kuvera]], I have
brought this my child to you, that he may enter the service of your son."
This messenger identified herself as the widow of Dharmapâla.
(002-143)

The character index of the *Daśakumāracarita* records Dharmapâla simply as
one of [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]]'s ministers, while also noting his son [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]] as minister
and son-in-law of the King of [[benares|Benâres]].
(026-075) (026-089)

## Role in the Narrative

Dharmapâla represents the loyal ministerial class that supports the royal
house of [[magadha|Magadha]]. Though he himself is not a central character in the
adventures, his legacy lives on through his sons, particularly [[kamapala|Kâmapâla]],
who becomes deeply entangled in the affairs of princes and kingdoms.

His posthumous connection to the god [[kuvera|Kuvera]] — through his widow who served
the deity — adds a supernatural dimension to his otherwise earthly role as
a minister. This divine link ultimately placed his son into the circle of
[[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]]'s companions.
(002-143)

The piety attributed to Dharmapâla set a standard that his eldest son
emulated, while his youngest son diverged from the path, illustrating the
varied fates of a minister's children in the narrative.
(014-067)

## Relationships

Dharmapâla served King **[[Râjahansa]]** alongside his fellow ministers
**[[Padmodbhava]]** and **[[Sitavarma]]**, forming the three pillars of
the [[magadha|Magadha]]n council.
(001-105)

He was the father of **[[Kâmapâla]]**, who became a distinguished minister
and son-in-law of the King of [[benares|Benâres]], and of **[[sumantra|Sumantra]]**, who also appears
in the chronicles.
(026-089) (026-150)

After his passing, his widow entered the service of **[[Kuvera]]**, the God
of [[wealth|Wealth]], creating a bridge between the mortal court of [[magadha|Magadha]] and the
celestial realm.
(002-143)
