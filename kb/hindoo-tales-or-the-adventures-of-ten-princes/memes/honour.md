# Honour

## Definition

Honour in the Hindoo Tales is the social recognition of worth — the respect, esteem, and due treatment accorded to persons of status, virtue, or distinction. It is expressed through formal obeisance, generous hospitality, public recognition, and deferential conduct. Honour functions both as a marker of social hierarchy and as a moral currency: to honour someone is to acknowledge their place in the moral order.

## In the Narrative

Honour appears across the social spectrum of the tales. A character is treated with great honour by a host and reciprocates the respect (004-080, 004-093). A young brahman is deemed worthy of all honour for his learning and ability (005-140). Obeisance in honour of a goddess is made as a ritual gesture (018-028, 018-046). A wife is enjoined to treat her husband with all due honour (019-140). A king treats a guest with great honour (021-030). Song and dance are made in honour of [[brahma|Brahma]] (022-128). Honour is contrasted with its opposites — dishonesty and slander — as a positive social value (023-034).

> "… young brahman, worthy of all honour for his…" (005-140)

> "…t she made a low obeisance in honour of the goddess…" (018-028)

> "…reat her husband with all due honour and…" (019-140)
