# Journey in the Tales

## Summary

Journey is the narrative backbone of the *Adventures of the Ten Princes*. The ten princes are separated by war and each embarks on long wanderings through forests, cities, and kingdoms before their eventual re[[marriage-in-the-tales|union]].

Journeys take many forms: mercantile travel by sea, solitary pilgrimage through wilderness, and the fated journey of a companion foretold in a vision.

> "Ratnodbhava became a merchant, and in the way of traffic made many long journeys by land and sea."
> (001-117)

## Context

The dispersal of the princes after [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]]'s defeat transforms each prince into a wanderer. Journey becomes the frame within which adventures unfold.

A supernatural dimension enters when a journey is foretold: "a king's son will come to this place to-morrow, and he will be your companion in the journey" (003-104). This prophecy links journey to destiny.

Solitary travel also tests character: "After he had left me, I continued my journey; and arriving late and very tired at [[sravasti|Sravasti]], I lay down to sleep" (016-142).

## Key Participants

- [[ratnodbhava]] — becomes a merchant and makes long journeys by land and sea (001-117)

- [[somadatta]] — journeys through the forest, encounters visions and companions

- [[apahâravarma]] — journeys to rejoin the king after his escape

- [[arthapâla]] — his journey takes him through subterranean palaces

## Outcome

The journey motif structures the entire work. Each prince's individual journey generates a distinct narrative thread; the re[[marriage-in-the-tales|union]] of all ten princes closes the frame. Journey is both literal movement across the geography of ancient India and a metaphor for fortune's path.
