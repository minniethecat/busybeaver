## Biography

Ambâlika was the daughter of [[sinhavarma|Sinhavarma]], King of [[anga|Anga]]. She was a princess of high standing, sought in marriage by [[chandavarma|Chandavarma]], a powerful figure who, when refused, marched against her father to take vengeance. (007-063)

[[chandavarma|Chandavarma]]'s demand for Ambâlika's hand and his subsequent military campaign against [[anga|Anga]] upon refusal form a significant plot point. Her father [[sinhavarma|Sinhavarma]]'s refusal of the match provoked a conflict that drew other characters into the narrative. (007-064)

Later, [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]], one of the ten princes, formed an intimacy with Ambâlika's nurse and through her gained access to the princess, eventually winning her favour by telling her amusing stories. (011-002)

## Role

Ambâlika appears in two separate narrative threads: first as the casus belli of [[chandavarma|Chandavarma]]'s attack on [[anga|Anga]], and later as the romantic interest of [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]]. Her story illustrates how marriage negotiations among royal houses could spark conflicts, and how the princes used ingenuity and intermediaries to win the favour of princesses.

## Quotations

> "Chandavarma had some time before this asked in marriage Ambâlika, the daughter of Sinhavarma, King of Anga, and, indignant at a refusal, was now marching against him, to take vengeance for the insult." (007-063)

> "formed an intimacy with the nurse of the Princess Ambâlika, and have been introduced by her to the princess, whose favour I have gained by telling her amusing stories." (011-002)
