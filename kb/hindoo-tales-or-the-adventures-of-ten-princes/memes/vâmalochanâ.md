# Vâmalochanâ

Vâmalochanâ was the daughter of [[Víraketu]], King of Pâtali, and the wife
of Prince [[Somadatta]], one of the ten princes. She was renowned for her
great beauty.
(004-012) (026-154)

## Biography

Vâmalochanâ was born to [[viraketu|Víraketu]], the King of Pâtali. Her extraordinary
beauty was widely spoken of, drawing the attention of those who heard
of her.
(004-012) (026-163)

> "Again of the great beauty of Vâmalochana, daughter of Víraketu."
> (004-012)

She was kept under the guard of [[Mânapâla]], an officer appointed to
watch over her. She later married Prince [[somadatta|Somadatta]], one of the ten
princes whose adventures form the central narrative.
(026-107) (026-154)

## Role in the Narrative

Vâmalochanâ appears in the fourth chapter as the famed beauty of Pâtali,
and her marriage to [[somadatta|Somadatta]] is recorded in the concluding chapter among
the [[marriage-in-the-tales|union]]s that close the princes' tales.
(004-012) (026-154)
