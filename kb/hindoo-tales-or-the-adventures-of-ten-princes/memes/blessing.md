# Blessing

## Definition

Blessing in the Hindoo Tales is a spiritual benediction — a verbal or ritual conferral of divine favour, good fortune, or grateful acknowledgment. Characters exchange blessings in moments of parting, gratitude, and spiritual transaction. The blessing holds performative power: to bless is to invoke a higher order of well-being upon the recipient.

## In the Narrative

A sage, after receiving kindness, responds by "blessing them for their kindness" (002-076), establishing blessing as a reciprocal exchange for good conduct. Another holy figure "gave me his blessing, and receiving many thanks" (003-085), pairing the blessing with gratitude in a ritual of mutual acknowledgement. A female character departs "with many blessings on me, and thanks to" her host (010-147), the blessing serving as parting spiritual currency. The most formal benediction appears when a figure declares "go, and my blessing be upon you" (022-107), using the imperative form that marks the conferral of spiritual authority.

> "…and, blessing them for their kindness, took…" (002-076)

> "…gave me his blessing, and receiving many thanks from…" (003-085)

> "…She went away, with many blessings on me, and thanks to…" (010-147)

> "…now leave me; go, and my blessing be upon you." (022-107)
