# Simanta

## Definition

Simanta is a religious ceremony performed during pregnancy, a traditional Hindu rite that marks a significant milestone in the gestational period. In the Hindoo Tales, it is presented as an occasion of great pomp and royal celebration.

## In the Narrative

The ceremony appears in connection with royal households. When [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]] held the Simanta ceremony for his queen [[vasumati|Vasumati]], it was performed "with great magnificence" (001-143). Later, King [[manasara|Mânasâra]] of [[malwa|Mâlwa]] travelled to [[pushpapuri|Pushpapuri]] to attend the Simanta festival of the queen (002-040). The index entry confirms the cultural role of the rite: a religious ceremony performed during pregnancy (026-141).

> "…Simanta with great magnificence, a…" (001-143)

> "…Pushpapuri, to be present at the Simanta festival of the queen, and…" (002-040)
