# Chandrasena

Chandrasena was the foster-sister of Princess [[Kandukavati]] and the beloved
of [[kosadasa|Kosadâsa]]. She played a key role in the romantic intrigue within the palace
of [[mahishmati|Mahishmati]].

## Biography

Chandrasena was first seen leaning on the arm of her foster-sister, the
Princess [[kandukavati|Kandukavati]], and followed by attendants as they moved through the
palace.
(018-047)

> "Leaning on the arm of her foster-sister Chandrasena, and followed by a
> train of attendants, the princess came forth."
> (018-047)

In the evening, Chandrasena spoke with the newcomer to whom she had been
introduced, acting as an intermediary between the palace and those outside
its walls.
(018-052)

Chandrasena brought a report of love from one party to another, serving as
the messenger of affection in the palace's hidden affairs.
(021-009)

> "The report which Chandrasena brought me of your love; but when I heard
> it, I could scarcely believe it."
> (021-009)

The text records that Chandrasena was happily united with her lover, finding
a resolution to the romantic complications she had helped to navigate.
(021-033)

The character index identifies Chandrasena as the foster-sister of the
Princess [[kandukavati|Kandukavati]].
(026-071)

## Role in the Narrative

Chandrasena serves as a crucial intermediary in the romantic plot of the
[[mahishmati|Mahishmati]] palace. As the foster-sister of **[[Kandukavati]]**, she has
privileged access to the princess and uses this position to facilitate
communications between lovers.
(018-047)

She functions as a messenger, carrying reports of affection between parties
who could not openly communicate. Her role bridges the gap between the
inner world of the princess and the outer world of suitors.
(021-009)

Her own story concludes with her happy [[marriage-in-the-tales|union]] with her lover **[[kosadasa|Kosadâsa]]**,
a resolution that mirrors the successful outcome of the love affair she
helped to arrange.
(021-033)

## Relationships

Chandrasena was the foster-sister and close companion of Princess
**[[Kandukavati]]** of [[mahishmati|Mahishmati]]. Their bond allowed her to move freely
between the princess's private chambers and the outer palace.
(018-047) (025-101)

She was beloved of **[[kosadasa|Kosadâsa]]**, and the two were eventually happily united.
(021-033) (026-096)

She interacted with the princes who came to the palace, acting as a trusted
confidante and messenger.
(018-052)
