# Vasundhara

Vasundhara was the queen of King [[anantavarma|Anantavarma]] of [[vidarba|Vidarba]].
Following her husband's overthrow, she escaped to [[mahishmati|Mahishmati]] with her two
children, and later acted under the guidance of the goddess [[durga|Durgâ]] to
secure her family's position.

## Biography

Vasundhara was the queen consort of King [[anantavarma|Anantavarma]], the
ruler of the [[vidarba|Vidarba]]ns.
(026-156)

After her husband's kingdom was overthrown, Vasundhara escaped with her two
children — her son and her daughter [[manjuvadini|Manjuvâdini]] — along
with the minister and a few faithful followers. The party arrived safely at
[[mahishmati|Mahishmati]], where they were received by King [[amittravarma|Amittravarma]], a half-brother
of her late husband.
(024-027) (024-031)

Under the influence of the goddess [[durga|Durgâ]] — who had appeared to her in a
vision — Vasundhara sent for some of the late king's ministers and the
elders of the city, enlisting their support against Prince [[prachandavarma|Prachandavarma]].
(024-114) (024-117)

> "Then the Queen Vasundhara (also by my directions) sent for some of the
> late king's ministers, and of the elders of the city, whom she knew to be
> ill-affected towards Prachandavarma."
> (024-114) (024-116)

## Role in the Narrative

Vasundhara appears in Chapter 24 as part of [[visruta|Visruta]]'s narrative. She is
portrayed as a protective mother who, guided by divine vision, works to
restore her son's position and counter the schemes of the usurping Prince
[[prachandavarma|Prachandavarma]].
