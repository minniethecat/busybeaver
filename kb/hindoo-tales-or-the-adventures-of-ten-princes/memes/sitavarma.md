# Sitavarma

Sitavarma was one of the three chief ministers of King [[Râjahansa]] of
[[magadha|Magadha]], alongside [[Dharmapâla]] and [[Padmodbhava]]. His youngest son
[[satyavarma|Satyavarma]] became a figure of note in the narrative.

## Biography

Sitavarma was named among the three trusted ministers of [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]] — a
council composed of [[dharmapala|Dharmapâla]], [[padmodbhava|Padmodbhava]], and Sitavarma.
(001-105)

> "Their names were, Dharmapâla, Padmodbhava, and Sitavarma."
> (001-105)

His youngest son was [[satyavarma|Satyavarma]], who is described as a brahman. [[satyavarma|Satyavarma]],
after travelling about a long time visiting all holy places, entered the
service of the King of [[magadha|Magadha]], continuing his father's legacy of ministerial
service to the crown.
(002-157)

> "The brahman, Satyavarma, the youngest son of Sitavarma, a minister of the
> King of Magadha, after travelling about a long time, visiting all holy
> places..."
> (002-157)

The character index confirms Sitavarma's position simply: one of [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]]'s
ministers.
(026-145)

## Role in the Narrative

Though Sitavarma himself does not appear extensively as an active character,
his significance lies in his role as a foundational figure of the [[magadha|Magadha]]n
court. As one of the three ministers who served [[rajahansa|Râjahansa]], he was part of
the administrative backbone of the kingdom.

His most important contribution to the narrative is through his son,
**[[Satyavarma]]**, who inherited the family tradition of service to the
crown and became connected to the wider circle of the ten princes. The
fact that [[satyavarma|Satyavarma]] is explicitly identified as a brahman suggests that
Sitavarma's family belonged to the priestly class, combining religious
learning with political office.
(002-157)

## Relationships

Sitavarma served King **[[Râjahansa]]** as one of his three principal
ministers, alongside **[[Dharmapâla]]** and **[[Padmodbhava]]**.
(001-105)

He was the father of **[[Satyavarma]]**, his youngest son, who undertook
extensive pilgrimages before entering the service of the King of [[magadha|Magadha]].
(002-157)
