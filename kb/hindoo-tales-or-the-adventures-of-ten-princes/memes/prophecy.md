# Prophecy

## Definition

Prophecy in the Hindoo Tales is a narrative device of supernatural foreknowledge, typically delivered by a muni (sage) or other holy figure. Characters orient their lives around prophecies — believing them, waiting for their fulfilment, and acting in reliance upon them. Prophecy bridges the divine and human realms, providing a framework of destiny within which the tales unfold.

## In the Narrative

The motif first appears when "The king, fully believing the prophecy of the muni" acts upon a prediction concerning his lineage (002-026). A character declares "Trusting to this prophecy, I have waited impatiently" (003-147), showing how prophecy shapes temporal patience. Another passage describes "a sign of the fulfilment of the prophecy" (004-140) as evidence that the predicted events are unfolding. A queen "relying on this prophecy, she consented to wait" (004-163), demonstrating prophecy's power to sustain hope. The index records "the first part of the prophecy of" a sage as a structural reference point (024-175), confirming the motif's recurrence across the text.

> "…sign of the fulfilment of the prophecy, I could…" (004-140)

> "Relying on this prophecy, she consented to wait, and…" (004-163)
