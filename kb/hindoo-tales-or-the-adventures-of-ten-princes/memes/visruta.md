# Visruta

Visruta is one of the ten princes whose adventures form the core narrative
of the *Daśakumāracarita*. He was one of the four sons of the ministers of
[[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]], raised alongside Prince [[Râjavâhana]].
(002-032)

## Biography

Visruta was born to one of the four ministers of [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]], at about
the same time as the birth of the king's own son, [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]]. He was named
Visruta, and brought up together with his three foster-brothers and the
young prince.
(002-032)

After [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]]'s companions were scattered by fate, Visruta wandered
alone. He eventually had his own adventure in the [[vindhya|Vindhya]] forest, where
he rescued a young boy and an old man named [[nalijangha|Nâlijangha]] from a well.
(026-118)

During his wanderings, Visruta also encountered and killed [[prachandavarma|Prachandavarma]],
the King of [[mahishmati|Mahishmati]].
(026-124)

His adventures concluded with his marriage to [[manjuvadini|Manjuvâdiní]], the daughter
of [[anantavarma|Anantavarma]], and he was reunited with his companions at the court.
(026-110)

## Role in the Narrative

Visruta's tale is one of the later adventures recounted among the ten princes.
When called upon by [[rajavahana|Râjavâhana]] to tell his story:

> "Then, looking at Visruta, he said: 'It is now your turn;' and he forthwith began."
> (022-155)

His adventure begins in the [[vindhya|Vindhya]] forest, where he meets a boy by a well:

> "My Lord, as I was wandering one day in the forest of Vindhya, I met with a very handsome boy, standing by the side of a well, crying bitterly."
> (022-159)
> (022-160)

When Visruta asked the cause of his distress, the boy explained that his
elderly companion had fallen into the well while trying to draw water.
Visruta looked down and saw the old man, [[nalijangha|Nâlijangha]], at the bottom.
(022-162)

His adventure also involved a confrontation with [[prachandavarma|Prachandavarma]], whom he
ultimately killed.
(026-124)

## Relationships

Visruta was the foster-brother of [[pramati|Pramati]], [[mitragupta|Mitragupta]], and [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]],
all sons of [[rajahansa|King Râjahansa]]'s four ministers.
(002-032)

His friend and ally was [[aryaketu|Aryaketu]], a minister.
(026-049)

He married [[manjuvadini|Manjuvâdiní]], daughter of [[anantavarma|Anantavarma]].
(026-110)
