# Balabhadra

Balabhadra was a merchant, the son of a merchant, who became the husband of [[ratnavati|Ratnavati]]. His story illustrates the cunning of women, as recounted in the adventures connected with [[kalahakantaka|Kalahakantaka]].

## Biography

Balabhadra was the son of a merchant living in another town. On the very day of his [[marriage-in-the-tales|wedding]], he took a sudden and unexplained dislike to his bride, [[ratnavati|Ratnavati]], and thereafter neglected her entirely (019-152).

A clever scheme was devised by [[kalahakantaka|Kalahakantaka]] and an old woman to remedy the situation. The old woman brought a pretended message of love from "a merchant's daughter" to Balabhadra, who was [[pleasure|delight]]ed at having attracted such attention. She arranged for him to meet the young lady in a garden, where he saw his neglected wife playing at ball. By a contrived accident, she threw the ball towards him, and an intimacy began (020-016–022).

> The old woman brought a pretended message of love from the merchant's daughter to Balabhadra, who was delighted at having attracted the attention of such a charming young lady. (020-016–017)

Balabhadra fell deeply in love — not realizing the woman was his own wife. When he discovered the truth, he was ashamed to acknowledge it after having so long neglected her, and therefore went to live in another place where he was not known (020-030–034).

In his new town, Balabhadra prospered:

> Meanwhile Balabhadra went to a town at some distance, and there by his skill and energy, though beginning with a small capital, amassed in a few years a considerable fortune, and was much respected. (020-035–037)

Later, a false accusation was brought against Balabhadra, causing him great alarm. His wife [[ratnavati|Ratnavati]] stood by him, advising him to put a good face on the matter and face the accusation calmly (020-050–051).

## Relationships

- **[[ratnavati|Ratnavati]]** — His wife, whom he initially neglected but later came to love through the clever scheme of [[kalahakantaka|Kalahakantaka]] (019-152, 020-016, 026-054)
- **[[kalahakantaka|Kalahakantaka]]** — The man who devised the scheme that reunited Balabhadra with his wife (020-010–014)
