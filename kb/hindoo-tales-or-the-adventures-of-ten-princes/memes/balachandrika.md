# Bâlachandrika

Bâlachandrika was the beautiful daughter of the merchant [[Bandhupâla]] and
the wife of [[Pushpodbhava]]. She played a crucial role in the fifth chapter,
assisting [[pushpodbhava|Pushpodbhava]] in a daring scheme that freed her from the unwelcome
attentions of Prince [[dâruvarma|Dâruvarma]].
(005-036) (026-055)

## Biography

Bâlachandrika was the daughter of [[bandhupala|Bandhupâla]], a merchant of [[pushpapuri|Pushpapuri]].
She was renowned for her beauty and drew the unwanted attention of Prince
[[dâruvarma|Dâruvarma]], who persisted in his importunities despite her clear reluctance.
(005-036) (026-056)

[[pushpodbhava|Pushpodbhava]] fell in love with Bâlachandrika while observing her from afar,
and she reciprocated his feelings, though their passion was undeclared at
first.
(005-036)

> "Bâlachandrika was delighted with my plan, and promised to do her best
> to carry it out. She had full confidence in my courage and skill."
> (005-085)

Together, Bâlachandrika and [[pushpodbhava|Pushpodbhava]] devised a plan involving a
pretended demon that guarded her, which allowed [[pushpodbhava|Pushpodbhava]] to accompany
her to [[dâruvarma|Dâruvarma]]'s house in disguise.
(005-065) (005-085)

When [[dâruvarma|Dâruvarma]], undeterred by the rumour of the demon, continued his
pursuit, Bâlachandrika consented to go to his house, setting the trap
that would lead to his death.
(005-094)

> "After a few days, Bâlachandrika informed me that Dâruvarma, undeterred
> by the report which was now spread about the city, that she was haunted
> by a demon, had continued his importunities."
> (005-094)

After [[dâruvarma|Dâruvarma]] was slain, Bâlachandrika escaped and was later married to
[[pushpodbhava|Pushpodbhava]], with whom she lived happily thereafter.
(005-125) (026-055)

## Role in the Narrative

Bâlachandrika's role centres on the fifth chapter, where her predicament as
the target of [[dâruvarma|Dâruvarma]]'s unwanted advances provides the catalyst for
[[pushpodbhava|Pushpodbhava]]'s daring exploits. Her quick discrimination and courage made her
an active participant in the scheme to eliminate [[dâruvarma|Dâruvarma]], rather than a
passive victim.

> "Meanwhile Bâlachandrika, quick in discrimination, perceived the
> situation and acted accordingly."
> (006-001)

In the sixth chapter, Bâlachandrika continued to serve as confidante and
friend to Princess [[avantisundari|Avantisundari]], relaying messages and facilitating secret
meetings.
(006-072) (006-117)

She was also a dear friend who comforted the princess in times of distress,
proving her loyalty and resourcefulness beyond the initial plot against
[[dâruvarma|Dâruvarma]].
(006-080)

## Relationships

Bâlachandrika was the daughter of [[Bandhupâla]], a merchant of [[pushpapuri|Pushpapuri]].
She became the wife of [[Pushpodbhava]], one of the companions, after he
rescued her from [[dâruvarma|Dâruvarma]]. She was a close friend and confidante of
[[avantisundari|Avantisundari]], the princess whom she served faithfully.
(026-055) (026-056) (005-160)
