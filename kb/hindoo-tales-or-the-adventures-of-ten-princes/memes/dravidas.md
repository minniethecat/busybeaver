# Dravidas

The country of the Dravidas is a region in southern India that serves as the
setting for an embedded tale in the nineteenth chapter of the
*Daśakumāracarita*, where [[mantragupta]] recounts the story of a young
brahman's quest for a wife.
(019-050)

## Geography

The Dravidas country is associated with the Dravidian-speaking regions of
southern India. In the narrative, it is depicted as a land of brahman
communities where traditional customs of marriage and caste governed
social life. A young brahman of this country, dissatisfied with arranged
matches, set out alone to travel through the land seeking a bride who
would suit him — a journey that took him to many towns and households
across the region.
(019-050) (019-051) (019-056)

> "There was formerly in the country of the Dravidas a young brahman of ... Somehow he was not married when a mere boy, as is often the case."
> (019-050) (019-051)

## Role in the Narrative

The Dravidas country is the origin-point of a story-within-a-story. While
recounting his adventures to a Rakshas, [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]] tells of a brahman from
this region whose unconventional method of wife-seeking — offering rice to
maidens and asking if they could make him a good dinner — led him through
many towns before he found a suitable match. The tale illustrates the social
customs of the Dravidian country and serves as a narrative diversion within
[[mantragupta|Mantragupta]]'s larger account of his southern wanderings.
(019-050) (019-059) (019-061)
