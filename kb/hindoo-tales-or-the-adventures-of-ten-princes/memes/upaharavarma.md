# Upahâravarma

Upahâravarma was one of the ten princes and a companion of [[Râjavâhana]].
He was rescued from death as an infant and raised at the court of [[Râjahansa]],
later becoming one of the trusted allies of the prince.
(026-126)

## Biography

As an infant, Upahâravarma was saved from a premature death and brought to the
court, where he was given his name and raised as one of the prince's
companions.
(002-080)

> "…the child was saved from such a death; and giving him the name of
> Upahâravarma, had him brought up as his own son."
> (002-081)

He grew up within the circle of the ten princes and accompanied
[[Râjavâhana]] during critical moments. When the companions reunited with the
prince, Upahâravarma was among those who arrived together, alongside [[pramati|Pramati]],
[[mitragupta|Mitragupta]], [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]], and others.
(007-183)

Upahâravarma later married [[kalpasundari|Kalpasundari]], who had previously been the wife of
[[vikatavarma|Vikatavarma]].
(026-086)

During his adventures, Upahâravarma killed [[kantaka|Kantaka]], a gaoler, demonstrating
the decisive action that characterised the princes.
(026-093)

## Role in the Narrative

When the companions gathered to tell their stories, [[Râjavâhana]] praised the
previous narrator's courage and then turned to Upahâravarma, saying, "It is now
your turn."
(012-002)

> "Râjavâhana, having heard this story, said 'You have indeed shown wonderful
> ingenuity and courage;' then he turned to Upahâravarma, and said: 'It is
> now your turn.'"
> (012-002)

Upahâravarma then recounted his own adventures, which form a significant
portion of the narrative and showcase the resourcefulness of the ten princes
in overcoming adversity.

## Relationships

Upahâravarma was the husband of [[kalpasundari|Kalpasundari]], who had previously been married
to [[vikatavarma|Vikatavarma]].
(026-086)

He fought alongside the other companions: [[Apahâravarma]], [[pramati|Pramati]],
[[mitragupta|Mitragupta]], [[mantragupta|Mantragupta]], [[visruta|Visruta]], [[praharavarma|Prahâravarma]] [[praharavarma|King of Mithila]], and
[[Kâmapâla]] lord of [[benares|Benâres]].
(007-183)

He was closely allied with [[Râjavâhana]] and the circle of the ten princes.

> "accompanied by Upahâravarma, Pramati, Mitragupta, Mantragupta, Visruta,
> Prahâravarma King of Mithila, Kâmapâla lord of Benâres…"
> (007-183)
