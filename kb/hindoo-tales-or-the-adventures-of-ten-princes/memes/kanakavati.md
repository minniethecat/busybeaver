# Kanakavati

**Kanakavati** is the daughter of [[Niddhipatidatta]], whose name is invoked in the legal proceedings against [[Balabhadra]] in [[apaharavarma|Apahâravarma]]'s narrative in Chapter 20.

## Biography

Kanakavati is known only by name. A woman brings an accusation against [[balabhadra|Balabhadra]], claiming to be Kanakavati, the daughter of [[niddhipatidatta|Niddhipatidatta]]. [[balabhadra|Balabhadra]]'s wife refutes this claim, asserting that the accuser is not Kanakavati at all. (020-052)

## Role in the Narrative

Kanakavati's name appears at a crucial moment in the legal drama surrounding [[balabhadra|Balabhadra]]. The false claim of identity—that the accuser is Kanakavati, daughter of [[niddhipatidatta|Niddhipatidatta]]—forms the basis of the legal complaint. The exposure of this falsehood by [[balabhadra|Balabhadra]]'s wife, who herself claims to be "the daughter of [[Grihagupta]], who lives at [[valabhi|Valabhi]]," is central to the defence. (020-052–053)

> "This is not Kanakavati, the daughter of Niddhipatidatta; this is my own lawful wife, the daughter of Grihagupta, who lives at Valabhi."
> (020-052–053)
