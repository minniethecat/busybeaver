# Worship

## Definition

Worship is the formal act of religious devotion and propitiation directed toward deities, particularly [[siva|Siva]], the sun, and the [[kama|god of love]] ([[kama|Kâma]]). It encompasses ritual offerings, prayers, and ceremonial observances performed at temples, riverbanks, and other sacred sites. In the tales, worship is a daily practice for the devout and a means of securing divine favour — the gods respond to sincere worship by granting boons, protection, and guidance to their worshippers.

## In the Narrative

Worship is woven into the fabric of daily life throughout the tales. Characters perform worship upon waking — "after worshipping the gods" is a recurring phrase that marks the start of a new day's activities (012-077). The proper way of worshipping [[siva|Siva]] is treated as specialized knowledge, taught and learned (003-083). Temples serve as focal points for worship: the temple of [[mahakala|Mahâkâla]] is a destination for those seeking to worship [[siva|Siva]], and characters make deliberate journeys to reach it (004-098).

The efficacy of worship is demonstrated through divine response. The god favours his worshipper, and through worship, characters receive supernatural aid (004-100). Worship also has a seasonal dimension — the spring season is specifically a time prepared for the worship of the [[kama|god of love]] (005-153). In Chapter 22, worship of the rising sun is depicted as an act of reverence toward the all-seeing deity, and characters are urged to worship and propitiate the gods (022-046, 022-074).

The practice extends across social boundaries: kings, princes, brahmans, and commoners all perform worship. Remorse can drive a character to worship as an act of atonement (009-004), and due worship is performed before significant undertakings, such as crossing a river (014-023).

> "especially the proper way of worshipping Siva." (003-083)

> "Mahâkâla, to worship Siva there. I have stopped at…" (004-098)

> "now the god has favoured his worshipper, and through this" (004-100)

> "worship the gods and propitiate them …" (022-074)
