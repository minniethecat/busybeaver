# Miracle in the Tales

## Summary

Miracles in the *Adventures of the Ten Princes* function as dramatic public spectacles that authenticate spiritual authority and transform social perception. Concentrated primarily in Chapter 22, the miracle motif presents supernatural intervention as both a test of faith and a catalyst for communal recognition.

> "That holy man has performed a great miracle, and bestowed on us this blessing."
> (022-123)

The miracle is not a private vision but a witnessed event—the crowd gathers in expectation and reacts collectively to the supernatural display.

## Context

The miracle narrative unfolds with dramatic pacing. A night is "appointed for the working of the miracle" (013-073), establishing anticipation. The crowd gathers at the designated place: they "came to the steps leading down to the water" (022-102) in expectation of witnessing something extraordinary.

When the miracle occurs, the effect is one of collective astonishment. The witnesses "were scarcely prepared for such a great change" (022-115), their expectations exceeded by the reality of the supernatural event. The scale of the transformation prompts the recognition that a holy man "had wrought such a miracle" (022-120).

The public nature of the miracle distinguishes it from private visionary experiences. It serves a social function—authenticating the holy man's status and redirecting communal belief. The declaration that the holy man "has performed a great miracle" (022-123) cements this social validation.

## Key Participants

- The holy man — the performer of the miracle, whose spiritual authority is publicly authenticated (022-120, 022-123)
- The gathered crowd — witnesses who "expected a miracle" and whose collective response validates the event (022-102, 022-115)

## Outcome

The miracle narrative pattern in the tales reinforces the providential worldview: supernatural power manifests publicly, validates spiritual authority, and transforms social understanding. Unlike private visions or personal revelations, the miracle's public dimension makes it a communal event that reshapes collective belief.
