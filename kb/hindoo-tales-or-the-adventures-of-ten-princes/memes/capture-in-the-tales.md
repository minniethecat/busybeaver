# Capture in the Tales

## Summary

Capture is a pivotal narrative device in the *Adventures of the Ten Princes*. Characters are seized by enemies, imprisoned by rival kings, or taken by surprise—and each capture sets in motion escapes, rescues, and reversals of fortune.

Episodes of capture include the princes seized after their hiding-place is discovered, the capture of a trespasser by a princess, and the surprise capture of an entire royal court.

> "active pursuit, our hiding-place was discovered, we were all captured,"
> (004-051)

## Context

The frame narrative begins with political defeat, and capture becomes a recurring peril. In Chapter 4, the princes' forest refuge is discovered and they are taken prisoner (004-050–052).

In a different register, capture also takes the form of a romantic ruse: "I had captured him, and how I had discovered the princess in the subterranean palace" (015-171).

The most dramatic capture occurs when King Jayasinha ambushes an unsuspecting court: "the king and all his court, thinking only of [[pleasure|enjoyment]], and unsuspicious of danger, had been captured by Jayasinha, King of [[andhra|Andhra]]" (021-146).

## Key Participants

- [[rajavahana]] — narrator-prince who recounts episodes of capture and escape

- [[apahâravarma]] — captured and later escapes to rejoin the king

- [[somadatta]] — among the princes captured in the forest (004-051)

## Outcome

Capture episodes serve as narrative catalysts, each one leading to an escape, a rescue, or a shift in political alignments. Captured characters demonstrate resilience through wit, allies, or the intervention of fortune.
