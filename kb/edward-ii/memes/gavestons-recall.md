# Gaveston's Recall from Exile

[[edward-ii|Edward II]]'s first act as king — the recall of [[piers-gaveston|Piers Gaveston]] from the exile imposed by his dying father — is the spark that ignites the play's political conflagration. In a single decision, Edward declares his priorities: [[love-and-desire|personal desire]] over political prudence, [[royal-patronage|royal favour]] over the [[honour-code|honour code]] of the nobility. Every subsequent event — from [[gavestons-execution|Gaveston's execution]] to [[edwards-deposition|Edward's deposition]] at [[berkeley-castle|Berkeley Castle]] — flows from this moment.

## The Decision

Edward's father had banished Gaveston on pain of death. Edward's first royal act is to reverse that banishment:

> "Come, Gaveston, / And share the kingdom with thy dearest friend" (001-003)

The speed and totality of the reversal signal that Edward's reign will be governed by personal attachment rather than political calculation. A king who shares his kingdom is a king who does not understand what a kingdom is.

## Political Consequences

The recall immediately alienates the barons who had supported the late king's policy. Edward compounds the offense by bestowing the earldom of Cornwall on Gaveston — a title normally reserved for royalty — and treating the favourite with a deference he denies his nobles:

> "That naturally would love and honour you, / But for that base and obscure Gaveston" (001-030)

The Archbishop of Canterbury's words reveal the baronial perspective: loyalty to the king is natural and automatic; disloyalty is an aberration caused by Gaveston's corrupting presence. The recall is not merely a personnel decision but a declaration of governing philosophy.

## Dramatic Function

As inciting incident, the recall establishes the terms of the entire subsequent action. The causal chain is clear and inexorable:

### The Recall's Consequences

1. **Gaveston returns** → Baronial opposition crystallizes
2. **Baronial pressure** → Edward retreats with Gaveston to [[tynemouth|Tynemouth]]
3. **Retreat fails** → [[gavestons-execution|Gaveston is captured and executed]]
4. **Edward's grief** → Vengeance against the barons, civil war
5. **Civil war** → [[queen-isabella|Isabella]] and [[younger-mortimer|Mortimer]] depose Edward
6. **Deposition** → Edward murdered at [[berkeley-castle|Berkeley Castle]]
