[020-001] The Royal Palace, [[westminster-london|London]].

[020-002] |  | *Enter **[[queen-isabella|Queen Isabella]]** and the **[[younger-mortimer|Younger Mortimer]]**.* |

[020-003] | Younger Mortimer | Fair Isabel, now have we our [[love-and-desire|desire]]; The proud corrupters of the light-brained king Have done their homage to the lofty gallows, And he himself lies in captivity. Be ruled by me, and we will rule the realm: In any case take heed of childish fear, For now we hold an old wolf by the ears, That, if he slip, will seize upon us both, And gripe the sorer, being griped himself. Think therefore, madam, that imports us much To erect your son with all the speed we may, And that I be protector over him: For our behoof, ’twill bear the greater sway Whenas a king’s name shall be under-writ. |

[020-004] | Queen Isabella | Sweet Mortimer, the life of Isabel, Be thou persuaded that I love thee well; And therefore, so the prince my son be safe, Whom I esteem as dear as these mine eyes, Conclude against his father what thou wilt, And I myself will willingly subscribe. |

[020-005] | Younger Mortimer | First would I hear news he were [[deposition|deposed]], And then let me alone to handle him. |

[020-006] |  | *Enter **Messenger**.* |

[020-007] |  | Letters! from whence? |

[020-008] | Messenger | From [[kenilworth|Killingworth]], my lord. |

[020-009] | Queen Isabella | How fares my lord the king? |

[020-010] | Messenger | In health, madam, but full of pensiveness. |

[020-011] | Queen Isabella | Alas, poor soul, would I could ease his [[grief]]! |

[020-012] |  | *Enter the **[[bishop-of-winchester|Bishop of Winchester]]** with the [[sovereignty|crown]].* |

[020-013] |  | Thanks, gentle Winchester.— Sirrah, be gone. |

[020-014] |  | *Exit **Messenger**.* |

[020-015] | Bishop of Winchester | The king hath willingly resigned his crown. |

[020-016] | Queen Isabella | O, happy news! send for the prince my son. |

[020-017] | Bishop of Winchester | Further, or this letter was sealed, Lord [[berkeley-castle|Berkeley]] came, So that he now is gone from Killingworth; And we have heard that [[earl-of-kent|Edmund]] laid a plot To set his brother free; nor more but so. The Lord of Berkeley is so pitiful As [[leicester|Leicester]] that had charge of him before. |

[020-018] | Queen Isabella | Then let some other be his guardian. |

[020-019] | Younger Mortimer | Let me alone; here is the privy-seal. |

[020-020] |  | *Exit the **Bishop of Winchester**.* |

[020-021] |  | *To Attendants within.* Who’s there? Call hither, [[gurney|Gurney]] and [[matrevis|Matrevis]].— To dash the heavy-headed Edmund’s drift, Berkeley shall be discharged, the king removed, And none but we shall know where he lieth. |

[020-022] | Queen Isabella | But, Mortimer, as long as he survives, What safety rests for us or for my son? |

[020-023] | Younger Mortimer | Speak, shall he presently be despatched and die? |

[020-024] | Queen Isabella | I would he were, so ’twere not by my means! |

[020-025] |  | *Enter **Matrevis** and **Gurney**.* |

[020-026] | Younger Mortimer | Enough.—Matrevis, write a letter presently Unto the Lord of Berkeley from ourself, That he resign the king to thee and Gurney; And, when ’tis done, we will subscribe our name. |

[020-027] | Matrevis | It shall be done, my lord. *Writes.* |

[020-028] | Younger Mortimer | Gurney— |

[020-029] | Gurney | My lord? |

[020-030] | Younger Mortimer | As thou intend’st to rise by Mortimer, Who now makes [[fortune|Fortune]]’s wheel turn as he please, Seek all the means thou canst to make him droop, And neither give him kind word nor good look. |

[020-031] | Gurney | I warrant you, my lord. |

[020-032] | Younger Mortimer | And this above the rest: because we hear That Edmund casts to work his liberty, Remove him still from place to place by night, Till at the last he come to Killingworth, And then from thence to Berkeley back again; And by the way, to make him fret the more, Speak curstly to him; and in any case Let no man comfort him, if he chance to weep, But amplify his grief with bitter words. |

[020-033] | Matrevis | Fear not, my lord; we’ll do as you command. |

[020-034] | Younger Mortimer | So, now away! post thitherwards amain. |

[020-035] | Queen Isabella | Whither goes this letter? to my lord the king? Commend me humbly to his majesty, And tell him that I labour all in vain To ease his grief and work his liberty; And bear him this as witness of my love. *Gives ring.* |

[020-036] | Matrevis | I will, madam. *Exit with **Gurney**.* |

[020-037] | Younger Mortimer | Finely dissembled! do so still, sweet queen. Here comes the young prince with the [[kent|Earl of Kent]]. |

[020-038] | Queen Isabella | Something he whispers in his childish ears. |

[020-039] | Younger Mortimer | If he have such access unto the prince, Our plots and stratagems will soon be dashed. |

[020-040] | Queen Isabella | Use Edmund friendly, as if all were well. |

[020-041] |  | *Enter **[[prince-edward|Prince Edward]]**, and **Kent** talking with him.* |

[020-042] | Younger Mortimer | How fares my honourable Lord of Kent? |

[020-043] | Kent | In health, sweet Mortimer.—How fares your grace? |

[020-044] | Queen Isabella | Well, if my lord your brother were enlarged. |

[020-045] | Kent | I hear of late he hath deposed himself. |

[020-046] | Queen Isabella | The more my grief. |

[020-047] | Younger Mortimer | And mine. |

[020-048] | Kent | *Aside.* Ah, they do dissemble! |

[020-049] | Queen Isabella | Sweet son, come hither; I must talk with thee. |

[020-050] | Younger Mortimer | You, being his uncle and the next of blood, Do look to be protector o’er the prince. |

[020-051] | Kent | Not I, my lord: who should protect the son, But she that gave him life? I mean the queen. |

[020-052] | Prince Edward | Mother, persuade me not to wear the crown: Let him be king; I am too young to reign. |

[020-053] | Queen Isabella | But be content, seeing ’tis his highness’ pleasure. |

[020-054] | Prince Edward | Let me but see him first, and then I will. |

[020-055] | Kent | Ay, do, sweet nephew. |

[020-056] | Queen Isabella | Brother, you know it is impossible. |

[020-057] | Prince Edward | Why, is he dead? |

[020-058] | Queen Isabella | No, God forbid! |

[020-059] | Kent | I would those words proceeded from your heart! |

[020-060] | Younger Mortimer | Inconstant Edmund, dost thou favour him, That wast a cause of his imprisonment? |

[020-061] | Kent | The more cause now have I to make amends. |

[020-062] | Younger Mortimer | *Aside to **Queen Isabella**.* I tell thee, ’tis not meet that one so false Should come about the person of a prince.— My lord, he hath betrayed the king his brother, And therefore trust him not. |

[020-063] | Prince Edward | But he repents, and sorrows for it now. |

[020-064] | Queen Isabella | Come, son, and go with this gentle lord and me. |

[020-065] | Prince Edward | With you I will, but not with Mortimer. |

[020-066] | Younger Mortimer | Why, youngling, ’sdain’st thou so of Mortimer? Then I will carry thee by force away. |

[020-067] | Prince Edward | Help, uncle Kent! Mortimer will wrong me. |

[020-068] | Queen Isabella | Brother Edmund, strive not; we are his friends; Isabel is nearer than the Earl of Kent. |

[020-069] | Kent | Sister, Edward is my charge; redeem him. |

[020-070] | Queen Isabella | Edward is my son, and I will keep him. |

[020-071] | Kent | *Aside.* Mortimer shall know that he hath wronged me. Hence will I haste to Killingworth Castle, And rescue aged Edward from his foes, To be revenged on Mortimer and thee. |

[020-072] |  | *Exeunt, on the one side, **Queen Isabella**, **Prince Edward**, and the **Younger Mortimer**; on other other, **Kent**.* |
