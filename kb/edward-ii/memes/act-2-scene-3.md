[007-001] Near [[tynemouth|Tynemouth Castle]].

[007-002] |  | *Enter **[[kent|Kent]]**, **[[lancaster|Lancaster]]**, the **[[younger-mortimer|Younger Mortimer]]**, **[[warwick|Warwick]]**, **[[pembroke|Pembroke]]**, and others.* |

[007-003] | Kent | My lords, of [[love-and-desire|love]] to this our native land, I come to join with you, and leave the king; And in your quarrel, and the realm’s behoof, Will be the first that shall adventure life. |

[007-004] | [[earl-of-lancaster|Lancaster]] | I fear me, you are sent of policy, To undermine us with a show of love. |

[007-005] | Warwick | He is your brother; therefore have we cause To cast the worst, and doubt of your [[rebellion|revolt]]. |

[007-006] | Kent | Mine [[honour-code|honour]] shall be hostage of my truth: If that will not suffice, farewell, my lords. |

[007-007] | Younger Mortimer | Stay, [[earl-of-kent|Edmund]]: never was Plantagenet False of his word; and therefore trust we thee. |

[007-008] | Pembroke | But what’s the reason you should leave him now? |

[007-009] | Kent | I have informed the Earl of Lancaster. |

[007-010] | Lancaster | And it sufficeth. Now, my lords, know this, That [[piers-gaveston|Gaveston]] is secretly arrived, And here in Tynmouth frolics with the king. Let us with these our followers scale the walls, And suddenly surprise them unawares. |

[007-011] | Younger Mortimer | I’ll give the onset. |

[007-012] | Warwick | And I’ll follow thee. |

[007-013] | Younger Mortimer | This tattered ensign of my ancestors, Which swept the desert shore of that Dead Sea Whereof we got the name of Mortimer, Will I advance upon this castle’s walls— Drums, strike alarum, raise them from their sport, And ring aloud the knell of Gaveston! |

[007-014] | Lancaster | None be so hardy as to touch the king; But neither spare you Gaveston nor his friends. |

[007-015] |  | *Exeunt.* |
