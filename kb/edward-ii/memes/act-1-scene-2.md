[002-001] [[westminster-london|Westminster]].

[002-002] |  | *Enter on the one side the two **Mortimers**; on the other, **[[warwick|Warwick]]** and **[[lancaster|Lancaster]]**.* |

[002-003] | Warwick | ’Tis true, the [[ecclesiastical-power|bishop]] is in [[tower-of-london|the Tower]], And goods and body given to [[piers-gaveston|Gaveston]]. |

[002-004] | [[earl-of-lancaster|Lancaster]] | What! will they tyrannise upon the church? Ah, wicked King! accursed Gaveston! This ground, which is corrupted with their steps, Shall be their timeless sepulchre or mine. |

[002-005] | [[younger-mortimer|Younger Mortimer]] | Well, let that peevish Frenchman guard him sure; Unless his breast be sword-proof, he shall die. |

[002-006] | [[elder-mortimer|Elder Mortimer]] | How now! why droops the Earl of Lancaster? |

[002-007] | Younger Mortimer | Wherefore is Guy of Warwick discontent? |

[002-008] | Lancaster | That villain Gaveston is made an earl. |

[002-009] | Elder Mortimer | An earl! |

[002-010] | Warwick | Ay, and besides Lord-chamberlain of the realm, And Secretary too, and Lord of Man. |

[002-011] | Elder Mortimer | We may not nor we will not suffer this. |

[002-012] | Younger Mortimer | Why post we not from hence to levy men? |

[002-013] | Lancaster | “My Lord of Cornwall” now at every word; And happy is the man whom he vouchsafes, For vailing of his bonnet, one good look. Thus, arm in arm, the king and he doth march: Nay, more, the guard upon his lordship waits, And all the court begins to [[flattery|flatter]] him. |

[002-014] | Warwick | Thus leaning on the shoulder of the king, He nods, and scorns, and smiles at those that pass. |

[002-015] | Elder Mortimer | Doth no man take exceptions at the slave? |

[002-016] | Lancaster | All stomach him, but none dare speak a word. |

[002-017] | Younger Mortimer | Ah, that bewrays their baseness, Lancaster! Were all the earls and [[nobility|barons]] of my mind, We’d hale him from the bosom of the king, And at the court-gate hang the peasant up, Who, swollen with [[poison|venom]] of ambitious [[pride]], Will be the ruin of the realm and us. |

[002-018] | Warwick | Here comes my Lord of Canterbury’s grace. |

[002-019] | Lancaster | His countenance bewrays he is displeased. |

[002-020] |  | *Enter the **[[archbishop-of-canterbury|Archbishop of Canterbury]]** and an Attendant.* |

[002-021] | Archbishop of Canterbury | First, were his sacred garments rent and torn; Then laid they violent hands upon him; next, Himself imprisoned, and his goods asseized: This certify the Pope: away, take horse. *Exit Attendant.* |

[002-022] | Lancaster | My lord, will you take arms against the king? |

[002-023] | Archbishop of Canterbury | What need I? God himself is up in arms When violence is offered to the church. |

[002-024] | Younger Mortimer | Then will you join with us, that be his peers, To [[exile|banish]] or behead that Gaveston? |

[002-025] | Archbishop of Canterbury | What else, my lords? for it concerns me near; The bishoprick of Coventry is his. |

[002-026] |  | *Enter **[[queen-isabella|Queen Isabella]]**.* |

[002-027] | Younger Mortimer | Madam, whither walks your majesty so fast? |

[002-028] | Queen Isabella | Unto the forest, gentle Mortimer, To live in [[grief]] and baleful discontent; For now my lord the king regards me not, But dotes upon the [[love-and-desire|love]] of Gaveston: He claps his cheeks, and hangs about his neck, Smiles in his face, and whispers in his ears; And, when I come, he frowns, as who should say, “Go whither thou wilt, seeing I have Gaveston.” |

[002-029] | Elder Mortimer | Is it not strange that he is thus bewitched? |

[002-030] | Younger Mortimer | Madam, return unto the court again: That sly inveigling Frenchman we’ll exile, Or lose our lives; and yet, ere that day come, The king shall lose his [[sovereignty|crown]]; for we have power, And courage too, to be revenged at full. |

[002-031] | Archbishop of Canterbury | But yet lift not your swords against the king. |

[002-032] | Lancaster | No; but we will lift Gaveston from hence. |

[002-033] | Warwick | And war must be the means, or he’ll stay still. |

[002-034] | Queen Isabella | Then let him stay; for, rather than my lord Shall be oppressed with civil mutinies, I will endure a melancholy life, And let him frolic with his [[favoritism|minion]]. |

[002-035] | Archbishop of Canterbury | My lords, to ease all this, but hear me speak: We and the rest, that are his counsellors, Will meet, and with a general consent Confirm his banishment with our hands and seals. |

[002-036] | Lancaster | What we confirm the king will frustrate. |

[002-037] | Younger Mortimer | Then may we lawfully [[rebellion|revolt]] from him. |

[002-038] | Warwick | But say, my lord, where shall this meeting be? |

[002-039] | Archbishop of Canterbury | At the New Temple. |

[002-040] | Younger Mortimer | Content. |

[002-041] | Archbishop of Canterbury | And, in the meantime, I’ll entreat you all To cross to Lambeth, and there stay with me. |

[002-042] | Lancaster | Come, then, let’s away. |

[002-043] | Younger Mortimer | Madam, farewell. |

[002-044] | Queen Isabella | Farewell, sweet Mortimer, and, for my sake, Forbear to levy arms against the king. |

[002-045] | Younger Mortimer | Ay, if words will serve; if not, I must. |

[002-046] |  | *Exeunt.* |
