[024-001] The Royal Palace, [[westminster-london|London]].

[024-002] |  | *Enter the **[[younger-mortimer|Younger Mortimer]]** and **[[matrevis|Matrevis]]**.* |

[024-003] | Younger Mortimer | Is’t done, Matrevis, and the murderer dead? |

[024-004] | Matrevis | Ay, my good lord: I would it were undone! |

[024-005] | Younger Mortimer | Matrevis, if thou now grow’st penitent, I’ll be thy ghostly father; therefore choose, Whether thou wilt be secret in this, Or else die by the hand of Mortimer. |

[024-006] | Matrevis | [[gurney|Gurney]], my lord, is fled, and will, I fear, Betray us both; therefore let me fly. |

[024-007] | Younger Mortimer | Fly to the savages! |

[024-008] | Matrevis | I humbly thank your [[honour-code|honour]]. *Exit.* |

[024-009] | Younger Mortimer | As for myself, I stand as Jove’s huge tree, And others are but shrubs compared to me: All tremble at my name, and I fear none: Let’s see who dare impeach me for his [[mortality|death]]! |

[024-010] |  | *Enter **[[queen-isabella|Queen Isabella]]**.* |

[024-011] | Queen Isabella | Ah, Mortimer, the king my son hath news, His father’s dead, and we have murdered him! |

[024-012] | Younger Mortimer | What if he have? the king is yet a child. |

[024-013] | Queen Isabella | Ay, but he tears his hair, and wrings his hands, And vows to be revenged upon us both. Into the [[council|council-chamber]] he is gone, To crave the aid and succour of his peers. Ay me, see where he comes, and they with him! Now, Mortimer, begins our tragedy. |

[024-014] |  | *Enter **[[edward-ii|King Edward]] III**, **Lords**, and Attendants.* |

[024-015] | First Lord | Fear not, my lord; know that you are a king. |

[024-016] | King [[prince-edward|Edward III]] | Villain!— |

[024-017] | Younger Mortimer | Ho, now, my lord! |

[024-018] | King Edward III | Think not that I am frighted with thy words: My father’s murdered through thy [[treason|treachery]]; And thou shalt die, and on his mournful hearse Thy hateful and accursed head shall lie, To witness to the world that by thy means His kingly body was too soon interred. |

[024-019] | Queen Isabella | Weep not, sweet son. |

[024-020] | King Edward III | Forbid not me to weep; he was my father; And had you loved him half so well as I, You could not bear his death thus patiently: But you, I fear, conspired with Mortimer. |

[024-021] | First Lord | Why speak you not unto my lord the king? |

[024-022] | Younger Mortimer | Because I think scorn to be accused. Who is the man dares say I murdered him? |

[024-023] | King Edward III | Traitor, in me my loving father speaks, And plainly saith, ’twas thou that murderedst him. |

[024-024] | Younger Mortimer | But hath your grace no other proof than this? |

[024-025] | King Edward III | Yes, if this be the hand of Mortimer. *Showing letter.* |

[024-026] | Younger Mortimer | *Aside to **Queen Isabella**.* False Gurney hath betrayed me and himself. |

[024-027] | Queen Isabella | I feared as much: murder can not be hid. |

[024-028] | Younger Mortimer | It is my hand; what gather you by this? |

[024-029] | King Edward III | That thither thou didst send a murderer. |

[024-030] | Younger Mortimer | What murderer? bring forth the man I sent. |

[024-031] | King Edward III | Ah, Mortimer, thou know’st that he is slain! And so shalt thou be too.—Why stays he here? Bring him unto a hurdle, drag him forth; Hang him, I say, and set his quarters up: And bring his head back presently to me. |

[024-032] | Queen Isabella | For my sake, sweet son, pity Mortimer! |

[024-033] | Younger Mortimer | Madam, entreat not: I will rather die Than sue for life unto a paltry boy. |

[024-034] | King Edward III | Hence with the [[treason|traitor]], with the murderer! |

[024-035] | Younger Mortimer | Base [[fortune|Fortune]], now I see, that in thy wheel There is a point, to which when men aspire, They tumble headlong down: that point I touched, And, seeing there was no place to mount up higher, Why should I grieve at my declining fall?— Farewell, fair queen: weep not for Mortimer, That scorns the world, and, as a traveller, Goes to discover countries yet unknown. |

[024-036] | King Edward III | What, suffer you the traitor to delay? |

[024-037] |  | *Exit the **Younger Mortimer** with **First Lord** and some of the Attendants.* |

[024-038] | Queen Isabella | As thou receivest thy life from me, Spill not the blood of gentle Mortimer! |

[024-039] | King Edward III | This argues that you spilt my father’s blood, Else would you not entreat for Mortimer. |

[024-040] | Queen Isabella | I spill his blood! no. |

[024-041] | King Edward III | Ay, madam, you; for so the rumour runs. |

[024-042] | Queen Isabella | That rumour is untrue: for loving thee, Is this report raised on poor Isabel. |

[024-043] | King Edward III | I do not think her so unnatural. |

[024-044] | Second Lord | My lord, I fear me it will prove too true. |

[024-045] | King Edward III | Mother, you are suspected for his death And therefore we commit you to [[tower-of-london|the Tower]], Till further trial may be made thereof. If you be guilty, though I be your son, Think not to find me slack or pitiful. |

[024-046] | Queen Isabella | Nay, to my death; for too long have I lived, Whenas my son thinks to abridge my days. |

[024-047] | King Edward III | Away with her! her words enforce these tears, And I shall pity her, if she speak again. |

[024-048] | Queen Isabella | Shall I not mourn for my beloved lord? And with the rest accompany him to his grave. |

[024-049] | Second Lord | Thus, madam, ’tis the king’s will you shall hence. |

[024-050] | Queen Isabella | He hath forgotten me: stay; I am his mother. |

[024-051] | Second Lord | That boots not; therefore, gentle madam, go. |

[024-052] | Queen Isabella | Then come, sweet death, and rid me of this [[grief]]! *Exit with **Second Lord** and some of the Attendants.* |

[024-053] |  | *Reenter **First Lord**, with the head of the **Younger Mortimer**.* |

[024-054] | First Lord | My lord, here is the head of Mortimer. |

[024-055] | King Edward III | Go fetch my father’s hearse, where it shall lie; And bring my funeral robes. |

[024-056] |  | *Exeunt Attendants.* |

[024-057] |  | Accursed head, Could I have ruled thee then, as I do now, Thou hadst not hatched this monstrous treachery!— Here comes the hearse: help me to mourn, my lords. |

[024-058] |  | *Reenter Attendants, with the hearse and funeral robes.* |

[024-059] |  | Sweet father, here unto thy murdered ghost I offer up the wicked traitor’s head; And let these tears, distilling from mine eyes, Be witness of my grief and innocency. |

[024-060] |  | *Exeunt.* |
