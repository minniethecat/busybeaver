# Leicester

**Leicester** (historically Henry, 3rd Earl of Lancaster) appears in the final act of [[edward-ii]] as the agent of Queen Isabella's commission, charged with taking custody of the deposed [[edward-ii|king]] and escorting him to imprisonment. Unlike the more brutal [[Matrevis]] and [[Gurney]], Leicester performs his task with a measure of reluctant civility that makes his complicity in the deposition all the more troubling.

## The Queen's Commission

Leicester's first words in the play establish his role as an instrument of the new regime:

> The queen's commission, urged by Mortimer (018-013)

The passive construction—the commission belongs to the queen but is "urged" by [[younger-mortimer|Mortimer]]—reveals Leicester's awareness that he serves not Isabella but the power behind her. His question "What would your grace have done with such a man?" frames the deposition as a rational act rather than a violent usurpation.

## Custody and Conscience

When Edward asks what will become of him, Leicester's reply is both honest and evasive:

> Your majesty must go to Killingworth (018-023)

| Action | PN |
|--------|-----|
| Delivers queen's commission | (018-013) |
| Escorts Edward from Kenilworth | (018-022) |
| Provides a litter for the deposed king | (018-025) |
| Counsels patience at Berkeley | (019-003) |

Leicester provides Edward with a litter (018-025), a small gesture of comfort that distinguishes him from the outright cruelty of Matrevis and Gurney. Yet this very civility makes his role more insidious—Leicester demonstrates that the machinery of deposition does not require monsters, only dutiful nobles who follow orders with decorum.

## The Limits of Decency

At Berkeley, Leicester counsels Edward to "Be patient, good my lord" (019-003). The advice is kindly meant but politically vacant—patience will not save a deposed king. Leicester's decency is real but ultimately powerless against the forces he serves. He is the face of a regime that prefers to cloak its violence in procedure, and his civility is the velvet glove on Mortimer's iron fist.
