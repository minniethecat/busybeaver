[013-001] Near [[tower-of-london|the Tower]] of [[westminster-london|London]].

[013-002] |  | *Enter **[[kent|Kent]]**.* |

[013-003] | Kent | Fair blows the wind for [[france-as-setting|France]]: blow, gentle gale, Till [[earl-of-kent|Edmund]] be arrived for England’s good! Nature, yield to my country’s cause in this! A brother? no, a butcher of thy friends! Proud Edward, dost thou [[exile|banish]] me thy presence? But I’ll to France, and cheer the wronged queen, And certify what Edward’s looseness is. Unnatural king, to slaughter nobleman And cherish flatterers! Mortimer, I stay Thy sweet escape. Stand gracious, gloomy night, To his device. |

[013-004] |  | *Enter the **[[younger-mortimer|Younger Mortimer]]** disguised.* |

[013-005] | Younger Mortimer | Holla! who walketh there? Is’t you, my lord? |

[013-006] | Kent | Mortimer, ’tis I. But hath thy portion wrought so happily? |

[013-007] | Younger Mortimer | It hath, my lord: the warders all asleep, I thank them, gave me leave to pass in peace. But hath your grace got shipping unto France? |

[013-008] | Kent | Fear it not. |

[013-009] |  | *Exeunt.* |
