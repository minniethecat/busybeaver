[010-001] The open country.

[010-002] |  | *Enter **[[piers-gaveston|Gaveston]]** [[grief|mourning]], **[[james|James]]**, and other Attendants of **[[pembroke|Pembroke]]**.* |

[010-003] | Gaveston | O treacherous [[warwick|Warwick]], thus to wrong thy friend! |

[010-004] | James | I see it is your life these arms pursue. |

[010-005] | Gaveston | Weaponless must I fall, and die in bands? O, must this day be period of my life, Centre of all my bliss? And ye be men, Speed to the king. |

[010-006] |  | *Enter **Warwick** and Soldiers.* |

[010-007] | Warwick | My Lord of Pembroke’s men, Strive you no longer: I will have that Gaveston. |

[010-008] | James | Your lordship doth dishonour to yourself, And wrong our lord, your honourable friend. |

[010-009] | Warwick | No, James, it is my country’s cause I follow.— Go, take the villain: soldiers, come away; We’ll make quick work.—Commend me to your master, My friend, and tell him that I watched it well.— Come, let thy shadow parley with [[edward-ii|King Edward]]. |

[010-010] | Gaveston | Treacherous earl, shall I not see the king? |

[010-011] | Warwick | The king of heaven perhaps, no other king.— Away! |

[010-012] |  | *Exeunt **Warwick** and Soldiers with **Gaveston**.* |

[010-013] | James | Come, fellows: it booted not for us to strive: We will in haste go certify our lord. |

[010-014] |  | *Exeunt.* |
