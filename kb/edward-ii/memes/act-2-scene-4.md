[008-001] In [[tynemouth|Tynemouth Castle]].

[008-002] |  | *Enter severally **[[edward-ii|King Edward]]** and the **[[young-spenser|Younger Spenser]]**.* |

[008-003] | King Edward | O, tell me, Spenser, where is [[piers-gaveston|Gaveston]]? |

[008-004] | Younger Spenser | I fear me he is slain, my gracious lord. |

[008-005] | King Edward | No, here he comes; now let them spoil and kill. |

[008-006] |  | *Enter **[[queen-isabella|Queen Isabella]]**, King Edward’s **Niece**, **Gaveston**, and Nobles.* |

[008-007] |  | Fly, fly, my lords; the earls have got the hold; Take shipping, and away to Scarborough: Spenser and I will post away by land. |

[008-008] | Gaveston | O, stay, my lord! they will not injure you. |

[008-009] | King Edward | I will not trust them. Gaveston, away! |

[008-010] | Gaveston | Farewell, my lord. |

[008-011] | King Edward | Lady, farewell. |

[008-012] | Niece | Farewell, sweet uncle, till we meet again. |

[008-013] | King Edward | Farewell, sweet Gaveston; and farewell, niece. |

[008-014] | Queen Isabella | No farewell to poor Isabel thy queen? |

[008-015] | King Edward | Yes, yes, for Mortimer your lover’s sake. |

[008-016] | Queen Isabella | Heavens can witness, I [[love-and-desire|love]] none but you. |

[008-017] |  | *Exeunt all except **Queen Isabella**.* |

[008-018] |  | From my embracements thus he breaks away. O, that mine arms could close this isle about, That I might pull him to me where I would! Or that these tears, that drizzle from mine eyes, Had power to mollify his stony heart, That, when I had him, we might never part! |

[008-019] |  | *Enter **[[lancaster|Lancaster]]**, **[[warwick|Warwick]]**, the **[[younger-mortimer|Younger Mortimer]]**, and others. Alarums within.* |

[008-020] | [[earl-of-lancaster|Lancaster]] | I wonder how he scaped! |

[008-021] | Younger Mortimer | Who’s this? the queen! |

[008-022] | Queen Isabella | Ay, Mortimer, the miserable queen, Whose pining heart her inward sighs have blasted, And body with continual [[grief|mourning]] wasted: These hands are tired with haling of my lord From Gaveston, from wicked Gaveston; And all in vain; for, when I speak him fair, He turns away, and smiles upon his [[favoritism|minion]]. |

[008-023] | Younger Mortimer | Cease to lament, and tell us where’s the king? |

[008-024] | Queen Isabella | What would you with the king? is’t him you seek? |

[008-025] | Lancaster | No, madam, but that cursed Gaveston: Far be it from the thought of Lancaster To offer violence to his sovereign! We would but rid the realm of Gaveston: Tell us where he remains, and he shall die. |

[008-026] | Queen Isabella | He’s gone by water unto Scarborough: Pursue him quickly, and he cannot scape; The king hath left him, and his train is small. |

[008-027] | Warwick | Forslow no time, sweet Lancaster; let’s march. |

[008-028] | Younger Mortimer | How comes it that the king and he is parted? |

[008-029] | Queen Isabella | That thus your army, going several ways, Might be of lesser force, and with the power That he intendeth presently to raise, Be easily suppressed: therefore be gone. |

[008-030] | Younger Mortimer | Here in the river rides a Flemish hoy: Let’s all aboard, and follow him amain. |

[008-031] | Lancaster | The wind that bears him hence will fill our sails; Come, come, aboard! ’tis but an hour’s sailing. |

[008-032] | Younger Mortimer | Madam, stay you within this castle here. |

[008-033] | Queen Isabella | No, Mortimer; I’ll to my lord the king. |

[008-034] | Younger Mortimer | Nay, rather sail with us to Scarborough. |

[008-035] | Queen Isabella | You know the king is so suspicious As, if he hear I have but talked with you, Mine [[honour-code|honour]] will be called in question; And therefore, gentle Mortimer, be gone. |

[008-036] | Younger Mortimer | Madam, I cannot stay to answer you: But think of Mortimer as he deserves. |

[008-037] |  | *Exeunt all except **Queen Isabella**.* |

[008-038] | Queen Isabella | So well hast thou deserved, sweet Mortimer, As Isabel could live with thee forever. In vain I look for love at Edward’s hand, Whose eyes are fixed on none but Gaveston. Yet once more I’ll importune him with prayer: If he be strange, and not regard my words, My son and I will over into [[france-as-setting|France]], And to the king my brother there complain How Gaveston hath robbed me of his love: But yet, I hope, my sorrows will have end, And Gaveston this blessed day be slain. *Exit.* |
