[017-001] Near Bristol.

[017-002] |  | *Enter **[[edward-ii|King Edward]]**, **[[baldock|Baldock]]**, and the **[[young-spenser|Younger Spenser]]**.* |

[017-003] | Younger Spenser | Fly, fly, my lord! the queen is overstrong; Her friends do multiply, and yours do fail. Shape we our course to Ireland, there to breathe. |

[017-004] | King Edward | What! was I born to fly and run away, And leave the Mortimers conquerors behind? Give me my horse, and let’s reinforce our troops. And in this bed of [[honour-code|honour]] die with fame. |

[017-005] | Baldock | O, no, my lord! this princely resolution Fits not the time: away! we are pursued. |

[017-006] |  | *Exeunt.* |

[017-007] |  | *Enter **[[kent|Kent]]**, with a sword and target.* |

[017-008] | Kent | This way he fled, but I am come too late. Edward, alas, my heart relents for thee! Proud [[treason|traitor]], Mortimer, why dost thou chase Thy lawful king, thy sovereign, with thy sword? Vile wretch, and why hast thou, of all unkind, Borne arms against thy brother and thy king? Rain showers of [[revenge|vengeance]] on my cursed head, Thou God, to whom in justice it belongs To punish this unnatural [[rebellion|revolt]]! Edward, this Mortimer aims at thy life! O, fly him, then! But, [[earl-of-kent|Edmund]], calm this [[wrath|rage]]; Dissemble, or thou diest; for Mortimer And Isabel do kiss, while they conspire: And yet she bears a face of [[love-and-desire|love]], forsooth. Fie on that love that hatcheth [[mortality|death]] and hate! Edmund, away! Bristow to Longshanks’ blood Is false; be not found single for suspect: Proud Mortimer pries near into thy walks. |

[017-009] |  | *Enter **[[queen-isabella|Queen Isabella]]**, **[[prince-edward|Prince Edward]]**, the **[[younger-mortimer|Younger Mortimer]]**, and **[[sir-john-of-hainault|Sir John of Hainault]]**.* |

[017-010] | Queen Isabella | Successful battle gives the God of kings To them that fight in right, and fear in wrath, Since, then, successfully we have prevailed, Thanked be heaven’s great architect, and you! Ere farther we proceed, my noble lords, We here create our well-beloved son, Of love and care unto his royal person, Lord Warden of the realm; and, sith the Fates Have made his father so infortunate, Deal you, my lords, in this, my loving lords, As to your wisdoms fittest seems in all. |

[017-011] | Kent | Madam, without offence if I may ask How will you deal with Edward in his fall? |

[017-012] | Prince Edward | Tell me, good uncle, what Edward do you mean? |

[017-013] | Kent | Nephew, your father; I dare not call him king. |

[017-014] | Younger Mortimer | My Lord of Kent, what needs these questions? ’Tis not in her controlment nor in ours; But as the [[parliament|realm and parliament]] shall please, So shall your brother be disposed of.— *Aside to the Queen.* I like not this relenting mood in Edmund: Madam, ’tis good to look to him betimes. |

[017-015] | Queen Isabella | My lord, the Mayor of Bristow knows our mind. |

[017-016] | Younger Mortimer | Yea, madam; and they scape not easily That fled the field. |

[017-017] | Queen Isabella | Baldock is with the king: A goodly chancellor, is he not, my lord? |

[017-018] | Sir John | So are the Spensers, the father and the son. |

[017-019] | Younger Mortimer | This Edward is the ruin of the realm. |

[017-020] |  | *Enter **[[rice-ap-howel|Rice ap Howel]]** with the **[[elder-spenser|Elder Spenser]]** prisoner, and Attendants.* |

[017-021] | Rice ap Howel | God save Queen Isabel and her princely son! Madam, the Mayor and citizens of Bristow, In sign of love and duty to this presence, Present by me this [[treason|traitor]] to the state, Spenser, the father to that wanton Spenser, That, like the lawless Catiline of Rome, Revelled in England’s wealth and treasury. |

[017-022] | Queen Isabella | We thank you all. |

[017-023] | Younger Mortimer | Your loving care in this Deserveth princely favours and rewards. But where’s the king and the other Spenser fled? |

[017-024] | Rice ap Howel | Spenser the son, created [[earl-of-gloucester|Earl of Gloucester]], Is with that smooth-tongued scholar Baldock gone, And shipped but late for Ireland with the king. |

[017-025] | Younger Mortimer | *Aside.* Some whirlwind fetch them back, or sink them all!— They shall be started thence, I doubt it not. |

[017-026] | Prince Edward | Shall I not see the king my father yet? |

[017-027] | Kent | *Aside.* Unhappy Edward, chased from England’s bounds! |

[017-028] | Sir John | Madam, what resteth? why stand you in a muse? |

[017-029] | Queen Isabella | I rue my lord’s ill-[[fortune]]: but, alas, Care of my country called me to this war! |

[017-030] | Younger Mortimer | Madam, have done with care and sad complaint: Your king hath wronged your country and himself, And we must seek to right it as we may.— Meanwhile have hence this rebel to the block. |

[017-031] | Elder Spenser | Rebel is he that fights against the prince: So fought not they that fought in Edward’s right. |

[017-032] | Younger Mortimer | Take him away; he prates. |

[017-033] |  | *Exeunt Attendants with the **Elder Spenser**.* |

[017-034] |  | You, Rice ap Howel, Shall do good service to her majesty, Being of countenance in your country here, To follow these rebellious runagates.— We in mean while, madam, must take advice. How Baldock, Spenser, and their complices, May in their fall be followed to their end. |

[017-035] |  | *Exeunt.* |
