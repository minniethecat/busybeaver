# Mahdist War

## Overview

[[the-mahdi|The Mahdi]]st War (1881–1899) is the historical conflict that frames the novel's action. When [[harry-feversham]]'s regiment receives orders to deploy to [[cairo|Egypt]] for the Sudan campaign, [[harry-feversham|Harry]] resigns his commission — triggering the central crisis of the narrative. The war thus serves as both the **precipitating event** of the plot and the **arena of redemption** where [[harry-feversham|Harry]] must prove his courage.

The war was fought between the [[the-mahdist-state|Mahdist State]], established by [[the-mahdi|Muhammad Ahmad]] (the self-proclaimed Mahdi), and Anglo-[[cairo|Egypt]]ian forces. It included the [[the-fall-of-khartoum|fall of Khartoum]] (1885), where [[general-feversham|General]] Gordon was killed, and culminated in the [[battle-of-omdurman|Battle of Omdurman]] (1898).

## Narrative Function

In the novel, [[the-mahdi|the Mahdi]]st War operates on two levels:

1. **Historical backdrop**: Real engagements — [[tokar|Tokar]], [[tamai|Tamai]], [[tamanieb|Tamanieb]], the fall of [[khartoum|Khartoum]] — provide the military geography that the novel's veteran characters inhabit.
2. **Moral testing ground**: The Sudan is the place where courage can be demonstrated. For [[jack-durrance]], [[captain-trench]], and [[willoughby]], service in the Sudan proves their honour. For [[harry-feversham|Harry]], the Sudan becomes the only place where he can construct a counter-narrative to the accusation of [[cowardice|coward]]ice.

[[the-mahdi|The Mahdi]]st policy of forcing conversion is referenced directly — prisoners who refuse to embrace [[the-mahdi|the Mahdi]]st faith are starved or executed [027-011] — establishing the genuine danger that [[harry-feversham|Harry]] faces when he enters the Sudan under a false identity.

> "He had sworn conversion to the Mahdist faith, or starved with such patience as he might." [027-011]

## The Eastern Sudan Campaign

The eastern theatre of the war, around [[suakin]] and [[red-sea|the Red Sea]] coast, is where the novel's British [[the-british-officer-corps|officers]] see action. Battles at [[tokar|Tokar]], [[tamai]], and [[tamanieb]] form a litany of place-names that recur in the memories of [[jack-durrance|Durrance]] and [[captain-trench|Trench]] — a shared vocabulary of tested courage that [[harry-feversham|Harry]] cannot speak.

## Key Engagements in the Novel

- [[the-fall-of-khartoum]] — the 1885 disaster that kills [[general-feversham|General]] Gordon
- [[battle-of-omdurman]] — the 1898 battle that ends Mahdist power
- Eastern Sudan engagements ([[tokar|Tokar]], [[tamai|Tamai]], [[tamanieb|Tamanieb]]) — the battles that define the novel's veteran characters
