# The House of Stone

## Description
The House of Stone is the novel's darkest and most significant setting. It is the literal prison where [[captain-trench|Trench]], [[willoughby|Willoughby]], and other British [[the-british-officer-corps|officers]] are held by Mahdist forces, and where [[harry-feversham|Harry]] — operating under a false identity — plans their escape. The prison is redemption's crucible: it is here that 'the [[cowardice|coward]]ice was atoned' (021-056), here that [[captain-trench|Trench]] dies asking [[willoughby|Willoughby]] to return his feather.

## Role in the Novel
[[the-mahdi|The Mahdi]]st prison in [[omdurman|Omdurman]] where British [[the-british-officer-corps|officers]] are held captive — the site where [[harry-feversham|Harry Feversham]], himself a prisoner, engineers the escape of the very men who sent him the [[four-feathers|white feathers]], transforming the accusation into its opposite.

## Key Scenes
The imprisonment (chapters 24–27): [[harry-feversham|Harry]], [[captain-trench|Trench]], and [[willoughby|Willoughby]] held in [[omdurman|Omdurman]]. The escape planning: [[harry-feversham|Harry]]'s secret work to free his former accusers. [[captain-trench|Trench]]'s death: his request that [[willoughby|Willoughby]] carry his feather back as proof that 'he withdrew his charge of [[cowardice|coward]]ice against [[harry-feversham|Harry Feversham]]' (025-026).
