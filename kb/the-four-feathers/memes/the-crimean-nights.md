# The Crimean Nights at Broad Place
The annual gatherings at [[broad-place|Broad Place]] where [[general-feversham|General Feversham]]'s old comrades tell war stories — the setting for young [[harry-feversham|Harry]]'s fateful exposure to tales of military [[cowardice|coward]]ice. During one such night, 'two [[fear|dread]]ful stories were told — one of an officer, the other of a surgeon, who had both shirked' (004-068). [[harry-feversham|Harry]] takes these stories to bed: 'They never left my memory; they became a part of me.'
