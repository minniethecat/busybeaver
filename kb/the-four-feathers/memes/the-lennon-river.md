# The Lennon River
The river that flows past [[lennon-house|Lennon House]] and through [[ramelton|Ramelton]] — "swift and black under overarching boughs" (004-032) — is the setting for the novel's most peaceful interlude. On the afternoon before the ball, [[harry-feversham|Harry]] and [[ethne-eustace|Ethne]] sit by the river, "talking at times, but for the most part listening to the roar of the water and watching its perpetual flow" (004-032). It is the last moment of untroubled happiness before the [[the-white-feather|feathers]] arrive, and its memory haunts the novel's remainder — a paradise lost that the final [[forgiveness|reconciliation]] can only approximate.
