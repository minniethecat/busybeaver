# Battle of the Redan

## Overview

The Battle of the Redan (June 18, 1855) was an engagement during the Siege of Sevastopol in [[crimean-war|the Crimea]]n War. In the novel, it is the specific battle whose anniversary structures the annual gatherings at [[broad-place|Broad Place]] — the "[[crimean-war|Crimean nights]]" that [[general-feversham]] hosts for his fellow veterans.

The Redan is never described as a military event; it is evoked as a memory and a ritual. The anniversary dinner on June 15 (or as [[lieutenant-sutch|Sutch]] corrects, June 18) [001-066] [034-039] is the occasion on which the novel opens, and the Redan represents the shared experience that binds the older generation of [[the-british-officer-corps|officers]].

> "This day next week will be the anniversary of our attack upon the Redan." [034-039]

## Narrative Significance

The Redan anniversary serves as the novel's **establishing scene**. It is at this dinner that [[lieutenant-sutch|Lieutenant Sutch]] first observes [[harry-feversham]]'s discomfort — the "look of fear" [001-018] that [[lieutenant-sutch|Sutch]] recognizes from recruits before their first battle. The juxtaposition is precise: veterans celebrating a past victory while the heir to their tradition sits among them paralyzed by anticipation of his own future failure.

The battle itself was fought at the Redan fortification, one of the defensive works protecting Sevastopol. British and French forces assaulted the position; the British suffered heavy casualties. For [[general-feversham|General Feversham]] and his comrades, the Redan is the defining action of their military lives — the moment when courage was tested and proven.

> "He had seen it on the faces of recruits during their first battle." [001-018]

## The Anniversary Ritual

The anniversary dinner follows a fixed script: the veterans gather on June 15, [[general-feversham|General Feversham]] presides, and the clock is watched for the exact moment when the attack began. The ritual precision mirrors the military discipline the gathering celebrates — and contrasts sharply with [[harry-feversham|Harry]]'s undisciplined fear.

When [[harry-feversham|Harry]] resigns his commission and disappears, [[crimean-war|the Crimea]]n nights cease. The Redan anniversary becomes one more casualty of [[harry-feversham|Harry]]'s decision — a silence that measures the rupture he has caused in the [[harry-feversham|Feversham]] tradition.
