# Glenalla
The house in [[devonshire|Devonshire]] where [[ethne-eustace|Ethne Eustace]] lives during the later chapters, and where the novel's final [[forgiveness|reconciliation]] takes place. Glenalla is where [[jack-durrance|Durrance]] courts [[ethne-eustace|Ethne]], where [[willoughby|Willoughby]] returns his feather, and where, in the final chapter, [[harry-feversham|Harry]] and [[ethne-eustace|Ethne]] find their way back to each other. As the setting for the novel's resolution, Glenalla represents the domestic peace that [[broad-place|Broad Place]] and [[lennon-house|Lennon House]] — sites of [[justice|judgment]] and crisis — could not provide.
