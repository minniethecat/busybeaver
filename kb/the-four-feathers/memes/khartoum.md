# Khartoum

## Location

Khartoum (spelled "Khartum" throughout the novel) lies at the confluence of the Blue Nile and White Nile in central Sudan. By the time of the novel's main action, it has fallen to [[the-mahdi|the Mahdi]]st forces and become the seat of Mahdist power, its palm trees a recurring visual motif in the novel's desert passages.

## Description

Mason evokes Khartoum primarily through its silhouette — "the tall palm trees of Khartum" standing "against the cloudless sky" [027-003] [028-038]. The city is less a physical place rendered in detail than a symbol of what was lost: the "Kauneesa" (church) where Gordon once ruled [028-048] is now in enemy hands. The recurring image of palm trees serves as a visual shorthand, visible from a distance across the desert, marking the city as both a landmark and a reminder of imperial failure.

## Narrative Significance

Khartoum is the gravitational center of the novel's Sudanese geography. Its fall to [[the-mahdi|the Mahdi]] — "before Khartum had fallen, before [[berber|Berber]] had fallen" [007-050] — is the defining event that shapes the political landscape in which the characters move. [[general-feversham|General]] Gordon's death there haunts the British characters, and the city's occupation by Mahdist forces makes it a symbol of the threat against which men like [[jack-durrance|Durrance]] and [[harry-feversham|Feversham]] measure themselves. The fugitive who appears in the narrative is "a fugitive from Khartum" [022-023], his story a living testament to the city's fall. For [[the-mahdi|the Mahdi]]sts, Khartoum represents their greatest victory — the letter summoning Gordon "to surrender Khartum" [011-042] having been refused, the city was taken by force.

## Key Scenes

- **Gordon's reign recalled** (Chapter 7): The narrative reaches back to the period when Gordon governed from Khartoum, before its fall [007-039] [007-043].
- **[[the-mahdi|The Mahdi]]'s summons** (Chapter 11): A retelling of [[the-mahdi|the Mahdi]]'s demand that Gordon surrender Khartoum, and Gordon's refusal [011-042].
- **The fugitive's tale** (Chapter 22): The story of a man who escaped from Khartoum, told amidst the "drowsy afternoon" [[disgrace|ruin]]s of [[sinkat|Sinkat]] Fort [022-023].
- **[[jack-durrance|Durrance]]'s captivity** (Chapter 28): [[jack-durrance|Durrance]] is held in the "Kauneesa" of Khartoum, where Gordon once ruled — a bitter irony [028-048] [028-074].

## Associated Characters

- **[[general-feversham|General]] Gordon** — made his last stand and died at Khartoum, his presence still felt in the city's spaces
- **[[jack-durrance|Jack Durrance]]** — imprisoned in the Kauneesa of Khartoum during his captivity
- **The Fugitive** — escaped from Khartoum and carries the story of its fall
