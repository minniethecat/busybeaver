# Mr. Calder

## Role in the Novel

Calder is one of the novel's investigators — a minor figure who, like Durrance, pieces together the truth about [[Harry Feversham]] from scattered evidence. His role is structurally significant: he represents the process by which Harry's secret heroism gradually becomes public knowledge. Without characters like Calder, Harry's [[redemption]] would remain invisible — known only to the men he saved.

## Biography

Calder appears in the novel's later chapters, searching for information about Feversham's whereabouts and activities in the Sudan. His investigation — which, like Durrance's discovery that "Harry has redeemed his [[honour]]" (026-008), helps bring hidden truth to light — parallels Durrance's own piecing-together of "the cause of Harry Feversham's [[disgrace]] and disappearance" (022-045), though Calder approaches the mystery from a different angle.

## Relationships

**Harry Feversham**: The object of his investigation, though Calder never meets him directly.

**[[Jack Durrance]]**: Their parallel investigations converge on the same truth: that Harry Feversham, far from being a coward, has performed sustained acts of heroism in the Sudan.

## Key Scenes

Calder's investigation in Chapter 25 contributes to the gradual revelation of Harry's redemption. Like Durrance, who learns that "the [[cowardice]] was atoned" (021-056), Calder pieces together fragments of testimony from the men who witnessed Harry's heroism. His role as one of several conduits through which the truth travels from the Sudan back to [[England]] makes him, however briefly, an agent of Harry's vindication.
