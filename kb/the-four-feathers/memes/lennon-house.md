# Lennon House

## Description
Lennon House is the novel's other great house, a counterpoint to [[broad-place|Broad Place]]. Where [[broad-place|Broad Place]] is stern, English, and military, Lennon House is warm, Irish, and eccentric under [[dermod-eustace|Dermod Eustace]]'s 'ancient and tyrannous hospitality' (004-031). It is here, during the ball in Chapter 4, that the [[four-feathers|white feathers]] arrive — the Irish setting making the English military [[justice|judgment]] feel like a foreign intrusion.

## Role in the Novel
The Eustace family estate in [[donegal|Donegal]] — [[dermod-eustace|Dermod Eustace]]'s home, where the ball takes place during which three [[four-feathers|white feathers]] arrive in a jeweller's box, transforming [[harry-feversham|Harry Feversham]]'s private fear into public disgrace.

## Key Scenes
The ball (Chapter 4): 'Three [[four-feathers|white feathers]] fluttered out of the box, swayed and rocked for a moment in the air, and then, one after another, settled gently down upon the floor' (004-038). [[ethne-eustace|Ethne]]'s sitting room: the confession scene, where [[harry-feversham|Harry]] tells her 'All my life I have been afraid' (004-067). The afternoon by the [[the-lennon-river|Lennon River]]: 'an afternoon restful as a Sabbath' (004-032) — the last peace before the crisis.
