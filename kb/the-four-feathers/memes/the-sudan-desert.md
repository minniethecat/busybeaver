# The Sudan Desert
The vast desert landscape where [[harry-feversham|Harry Feversham]] spends years in disguise, recovering the Gordon letters and rescuing imprisoned [[the-british-officer-corps|officers]] — a place where '[[fear]] always' (010-018) accompanies every venture, and where [[harry-feversham|Harry]]'s hidden heroism unfolds far from English eyes. Unlike the ordered estates of [[england|England]] and Ireland, the Sudan is a place of anonymity and danger — precisely the conditions [[harry-feversham|Harry]] needs to prove what he could not prove at home. [[The desert]] is the novel's transformative space, where identity can be shed and remade.
