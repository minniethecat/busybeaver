# Tamanieb

## Location

Tamanieb is located in eastern Sudan, near [[red-sea|the Red Sea]] coast, in the region between [[suakin|Suakin]] and [[tokar|Tokar]]. It sits among a string of battle sites — [[tokar|Tokar]], [[tamai]] — that mark the eastern theatre of the [[the-mahdist-state|Mahdist]] campaigns. In the novel's geography, Tamanieb belongs to the same military landscape as [[suakin]] and the [[red-sea|Red Sea]] littoral, where British expeditionary forces operated against Osman Digna's forces.

## Description

The novel does not describe Tamanieb directly as a physical place. Instead, it appears in lists of battle names — "[[tokar|Tokar]], [[tamai|Tamai]], Tamanieb" [007-052] — that function as a kind of shorthand for the experience of the Sudan campaign. The name itself carries the weight of memory for the veteran characters, a metonym for combat, danger, and the testing of courage under fire.

> "Tokar, Tamai, Tamanieb — the crowded moments came vividly to his mind." [007-052]

## Narrative Significance

Tamanieb serves a specific narrative function: it is one of the names that separates those who have seen combat from those who have not. When [[harry-feversham]] imagines the accusations that will be made against him, the battle names of the eastern Sudan — Tamanieb among them — represent the world of tested courage from which his resignation has excluded him.

The name recurs at critical moments of psychological pressure. As [[jack-durrance]] lies blinded in the Sudan, he too recalls Tamanieb as part of the litany of battles that define his generation of [[the-british-officer-corps|officers]]. For [[captain-trench]], the eastern Sudan battles — including Tamanieb — represent both the danger he has survived and the proof of his courage that [[harry-feversham|Harry]] must match.

> "Perhaps, at Tokar, or Tamai, or Tamanieb, just as Trench had done!" [027-044]

When the novel catalogues the engagements of the British Army in Sudan, Tamanieb appears alongside Tel-el-Kebir, [[tamai|Tamai]], and the attack on McNeil's zeriba [034-044] — a roll call of imperial battles that form the backdrop against which individual character is measured.

## Key Scenes

- **Chapter 7** (PN 007-052): Tamanieb first appears in a flashback catalogue of eastern Sudan battles, establishing the military geography that defines the novel's veteran characters.
- **Chapter 27** (PN 027-044): [[jack-durrance|Durrance]], blind and imprisoned in the [[the-house-of-stone|House of Stone]], recalls Tamanieb as part of his own military past — a memory that contrasts his present helplessness with his former capability.
- **Chapter 34** (PN 034-044): Tamanieb appears in the novel's retrospective catalogue of British military engagements in Sudan, part of the historical texture that grounds the fiction in real campaigns.
