# Captain Trench

## Role in the Novel

Captain Trench is the first of the three accusers whose feather is returned. His trajectory — from sending [[the white feather]] in judgment to depending on [[Harry Feversham]] for survival in the Sudan — embodies the novel's reversal of moral positions. Dying in [[the House of Stone]] in Omdurman, he asks Willoughby to carry his feather back to England as proof that "he withdrew his charge of [[cowardice]] against Harry Feversham" (025-026). His deathbed recantation is the first instalment of Harry's [[redemption]].

## Biography

Trench serves with Harry in the [[Royal Surrey Regiment]]. He is one of the officers dining with Harry when the telegram arrives — Ethne reads his name from the visiting cards: "Captain Trench, [[Mr. Castleton]], [[Mr. Willoughby]]. Do you know these men?" (004-047). He is one of the three who subsequently send the white feathers. Posted to the Sudan, he is captured and imprisoned in the House of Stone in Omdurman, where Harry — now operating under a false identity — engineers the prisoners' escape. Trench dies during the rescue, his last act being the return of his feather.

## Relationships

**Harry Feversham**: From accuser to beneficiary — the man he condemned becomes his rescuer. His deathbed withdrawal of the accusation is the novel's first formal reversal.

**Willoughby**: Fellow officer who carries Trench's feather back to England and delivers it to Ethne.

## Key Scenes

The dinner at which the telegram arrives (Chapter 2). The sending of the feathers (implied, Chapter 4). The House of Stone (Chapter 27): Trench's death and the return of the first feather. When Durrance later learns of it, he understands that "the cowardice was atoned, or in one particular at all events" (021-056).
