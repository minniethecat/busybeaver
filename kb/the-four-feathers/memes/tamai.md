# Tamai

## Location

Tamai is a location in eastern Sudan, situated inland from [[red-sea|the Red Sea]] coast. It sits within the contested region between [[suakin|Suakin]] and the interior, part of the theater of operations during [[the-mahdi|the Mahdi]]st War. The novel places it in sequence with other battle sites — "[[tokar|Tokar]], Tamai, [[tamanieb|Tamanieb]]" [007-052] — marking it as one of the series of engagements that defined the British campaign in the Sudan.

## Description

Mason does not provide a detailed physical description of Tamai itself. It exists in the novel as a name heavy with consequence — a place defined entirely by the violence that occurred there. The phrase "when the square was broken at Tamai" [016-037] is the key descriptor, evoking the iconic British infantry formation shattered by Mahdist assault. The square, a defensive formation of disciplined soldiers, was overrun — a rare and traumatic event in British military memory of the period.

## Narrative Significance

Tamai carries outsized narrative weight as a place of death and sacrifice. For the characters, it is where [[comradeship|fellow officer]]s fell: "He was killed at Tamai" [021-043] is the stark epitaph for at least one character, and another reference confirms "Barton is dead — he was killed at Tamai" [025-075]. The battle at Tamai becomes a marker of [[honour|honor]] and loss — those who died there are remembered with the particular reverence reserved for men who fell against overwhelming odds. The recurring invocation of Tamai alongside other battle names ("perhaps, at [[tokar|Tokar]], or Tamai, or [[tamanieb|Tamanieb]]" [027-044]) underscores its role as a reference point in the collective memory of the surviving [[the-british-officer-corps|officers]].

## Key Scenes

- **The rushing memory** (Chapter 7): Tamai appears in a litany of battle names — "the crowded moments came vividly to his mind" — as a veteran recalls the Sudan campaign [007-052].
- **The broken square** (Chapter 16): A reference to the catastrophic moment "when the square was broken at Tamai" [016-037].
- **[[death|Death]] of Barton** (Chapter 21 and 25): Two separate confirmations that Barton was killed at Tamai [021-043] [025-075].
- **[[captain-trench|Trench]]'s imagined end** (Chapter 27): The narrative imagines [[captain-trench|Trench]] meeting his death "at [[tokar|Tokar]], or Tamai, or [[tamanieb|Tamanieb]]" — the uncertainty about the exact location reflecting the chaos of the campaign [027-044].
- **The campaign roll-call** (Chapter 34): Tamai appears in a retrospective list of battle [[honour|honor]]s stretching from "Kassassin, Tel-el-Kebir, Tamai, [[tamanieb|Tamanieb]]" to "the attack upon McNeil's zeriba" [034-044].

## Associated Characters
