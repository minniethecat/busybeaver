# Mr. Willoughby

## Role in the Novel

Willoughby serves as the mechanism of Harry's public vindication. He is the officer who carries Trench's dying request back to England and who personally returns his own feather to [[Ethne Eustace]]. His reappearance in [[Devonshire]] (Chapter 14) is the novel's turning point: the first external confirmation that the accusation was wrong. When he holds out "a tiny, soiled, white feather" (014-069) as proof, the adjective "soiled" carries the weight of Harry's [[suffering]] in the Sudan — evidence made material.

## Biography

An officer of the [[Royal Surrey Regiment]], Willoughby is one of the three who send the feathers. Like Trench, he is captured in the Sudan and imprisoned in [[the House of Stone]]. Rescued by Harry (whom he does not initially recognize), he carries Trench's feather back to England and seeks out Ethne to deliver it. His own feather he returns personally — an act that transforms him from accuser to witness.

## Relationships

**[[Harry Feversham]]**: From accuser to beneficiary — saved by the man he condemned. His return of the feather is the novel's first public act of vindication.

**[[Captain Trench]]**: Fellow officer whose dying wish Willoughby honours.

**Ethne Eustace**: He delivers both feathers to her, making her the recipient of proof that "Harry Feversham had redeemed his honour" (014-079).

## Key Scenes

The return of the feathers to Ethne (Chapter 14): "three separate accusations of [[cowardice]], were sent to Feversham by three separate men. This was the answer to the first of them" (014-073). His account of the House of Stone rescue and Harry's heroism.
