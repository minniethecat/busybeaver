# Broad Place

## Description
Broad Place is the physical embodiment of the [[harry-feversham|Feversham]] [[honour|family honour]]. Its hall, lined with portraits of military ancestors, is where young [[harry-feversham|Harry]] internalized the stories of [[cowardice|coward]]ice that would haunt him. The terrace where [[general-feversham|General Feversham]] sits — 'that lonely iron man' (004-067) — overlooks the Sussex Downs, a landscape of quiet order that contrasts with the [[the-desert|Sudanese desert]] where [[harry-feversham|Harry]] must earn his redemption.

## Role in the Novel
The [[harry-feversham|Feversham]] family estate in Sussex — [[general-feversham|General Feversham]]'s home, where the portraits of military ancestors line the hall and annual Crimean nights gather old comrades to tell war stories.

## Key Scenes
The Crimean nights (Chapter 1): old comrades gather and young [[harry-feversham|Harry]] hears the stories of two men who 'had both shirked' (004-068). [[jack-durrance|Durrance]]'s visit (Chapter 26): he tells [[general-feversham|the General]] that '[[harry-feversham|Harry]] never was a [[cowardice|coward]]' (026-008), and the old man experiences a 'joy at the reconquest of honour by his son' (026-034).
