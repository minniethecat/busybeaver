# Durrance's Visit to General Feversham
[[jack-durrance|Jack Durrance]]'s visit to [[broad-place|Broad Place]] to tell [[general-feversham|General Feversham]] the truth about his son: 'He wants to tell him how [[harry-feversham|Harry]] has redeemed his honour, how he is still redeeming it' (026-008). The [[general-feversham|General]], nearly eighty, learns that '[[harry-feversham|Harry]] never was a [[cowardice|coward]]' (026-008), experiencing a 'joy at the reconquest of honour by his son' (026-034).
