# Harry Feversham's Resignation
[[harry-feversham|Harry Feversham]]'s decision to resign his commission upon learning that his [[regiment]] is ordered to [[cairo|Egypt]] — the act that triggers the novel's entire plot. Receiving [[castleton|Castleton]]'s telegram, [[harry-feversham|Harry]] 'thought the matter over with the telegram in front of me' (004-061), then 'threw the telegram into the fire, and that night sent in my papers' (004-061).
