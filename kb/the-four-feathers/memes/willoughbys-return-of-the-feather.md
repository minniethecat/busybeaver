# Willoughby's Return of the Feather
The scene in [[devonshire|Devonshire]] where [[willoughby|Willoughby]] returns his [[the-white-feather|white feather]] to [[ethne-eustace|Ethne Eustace]] — the novel's turning point, in which the accusation against [[harry-feversham|Harry]] begins its formal reversal. [[willoughby|Willoughby]] holds out 'a tiny, soiled, [[the-white-feather|white feather]]' (014-069) as proof that 'three separate accusations of [[cowardice|coward]]ice, were sent to [[harry-feversham|Feversham]] by three separate men. This was the answer to the first of them' (014-073).
