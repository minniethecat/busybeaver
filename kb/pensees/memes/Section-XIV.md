[014-001] SECTION XIV

[014-002] APPENDIX: POLEMICAL FRAGMENTS

[014-003] 856

[014-004] Clearness, [[evidence|obscurity]].—There would be too great darkness, if

[014-005] truth had not visible signs. This is a wonderful one, that it

[014-006] has always been preserved in one Church and one visible

[014-007] assembly [of men]. There would be too great clearness, if

[014-008] there were only one opinion in this Church. But in order to

[014-009] recognise what is true, one has only to look at what has always

[014-010] existed; for it is certain that truth has always existed, and

[014-011] that nothing false has always existed.

[014-012] 857

[014-013] The history of the Church ought properly to be called the

[014-014] history of truth.

[014-015] 858

[014-016] There is a pleasure in being in a ship beaten about by a

[014-017] storm, when we are sure that it will not founder. The persecutions

[014-018] which harass the Church are of this nature.

[014-019] 859

[014-020] In addition to so many other signs of piety, they[359] are also

[014-021] persecuted, which is the best sign of piety.

[014-022] 860

[014-023] The Church is in an excellent state, when it is sustained by

[014-024] [[god|God]] only.

[014-025] 861

[014-026] The Church has always been attacked by opposite errors,

[014-027] but perhaps never at the same time, as now. And if she suffer

[014-028] more because of the multiplicity of errors, she derives this

[014-029] advantage from it, that they destroy each other.

[014-030] She complains of both, but far more of the Calvinists, because

[014-031] of the schism.

[014-032] It is certain that many of the two opposite sects are deceived.

[014-033] They must be disillusioned.

[014-034] [[faith|Faith]] embraces many [[truth|truths]] which seem to contradict each

[014-035] other. There is a time to laugh, and a time to weep,[360] etc. Responde.

[014-036] Ne respondeas,[361] etc.

[014-037] The source of this is the union of the two natures in [[jesus-christ|Jesus]]

[014-038] Christ; and also the two worlds (the creation of a new heaven

[014-039] and a new earth; a new life and a new death; all things double,

[014-040] and the same names remaining); and finally the two natures

[014-041] that are in the righteous, (for they are the two worlds, and a

[014-042] member and image of Jesus Christ. And thus all the names

[014-043] suit them: righteous, yet sinners; dead, yet living; living, yet

[014-044] dead; elect, yet outcast, etc.).

[014-045] There are then a great number of truths, both of faith and of

[014-046] morality, which seem contradictory, and which all hold good

[014-047] together in a wonderful system. The source of all heresies is

[014-048] the exclusion of some of these truths; and the source of all the

[014-049] objections which the heretics make against us is the ignorance

[014-050] of some of our truths. And it generally happens that, unable

[014-051] to conceive the connection of two opposite truths, and believing

[014-052] that the admission of one involves the exclusion of the other,

[014-053] they adhere to the one, exclude the other, and think of us as

[014-054] opposed to them. Now exclusion is the cause of their heresy;

[014-055] and ignorance that we hold the other truth causes their objections.

[014-056] 1st example: Jesus Christ is God and man. The Arians,

[014-057] unable to reconcile these things, which they believe incompatible,

[014-058] say that He is man; in this they are Catholics. But they deny

[014-059] that He is God; in this they are heretics. They allege that we

[014-060] deny His humanity; in this they are ignorant.

[014-061] 2nd example: On the subject of the Holy Sacrament. We

[014-062] believe that, the substance of the bread being changed, and

[014-063] being consubstantial with that of the body of our Lord, Jesus

[014-064] Christ is therein really present. That is one truth. Another

[014-065] is that this Sacrament is also a type of the cross and of glory,

[014-066] and a commemoration of the two. That is the Catholic faith,

[014-067] which comprehends these two truths which seem opposed.

[014-068] The heresy of to-day, not conceiving that this Sacrament

[014-069] contains at the same time both the presence of Jesus Christ

[014-070] and a type of Him, and that it is a sacrifice and a commemoration

[014-071] of a sacrifice, believes that neither of these truths can be admitted

[014-072] without excluding the other for this reason.

[014-073] They fasten to this point alone, that this Sacrament is typical;

[014-074] and in this they are not heretics. They think that we exclude

[014-075] this truth; hence it comes that they raise so many objections to

[014-076] us out of the passages of the Fathers which assert it. Finally,

[014-077] they deny the presence; and in this they are heretics.

[014-078] 3rd example: Indulgences.

[014-079] The shortest way, therefore, to prevent heresies is to instruct

[014-080] in all truths; and the surest way to refute them is to declare

[014-081] them all. For what will the heretics say?

[014-082] In order to know whether an opinion is a Father's ...

[014-083] 862

[014-084] All err the more dangerously, as they each follow a truth.

[014-085] Their fault is not in following a falsehood, but in not following

[014-086] another truth.

[014-087] 863

[014-088] Truth is so obscure in these times, and falsehood so established,

[014-089] that unless we love the truth, we cannot know it.

[014-090] 864

[014-091] If there is ever a time in which we must make profession of

[014-092] two opposite truths, it is when we are reproached for omitting

[014-093] one. Therefore the Jesuits and Jansenists are wrong in concealing

[014-094] them, but the Jansenists more so, for the Jesuits have

[014-095] better made profession of the two.

[014-096] 865

[014-097] Two kinds of people make things equal to one another, as

[014-098] feasts to working days, Christians to priests, all things among

[014-099] them, etc. And hence the one party conclude that what is

[014-100] then bad for priests is also so for Christians, and the other that

[014-101] what is not bad for Christians is lawful for priests.

[014-102] 866

[014-103] If the ancient Church was in error, the Church is fallen. If

[014-104] she should be in error to-day, it is not the same thing; for she

[014-105] has always the superior maxim of tradition from the hand of the

[014-106] ancient Church; and so this submission and this conformity to

[014-107] the ancient Church prevail and correct all. But the ancient

[014-108] Church did not assume the future Church, and did not consider

[014-109] her, as we assume and consider the ancient.

[014-110] 867

[014-111] That which hinders us in comparing what formerly occurred

[014-112] in the Church with what we see there now, is that we generally

[014-113] look upon Saint Athanasius,[362] Saint Theresa, and the rest, as

[014-114] crowned with glory, and acting towards us as gods. Now that

[014-115] time has cleared up things, it does so appear. But at the time

[014-116] when he was persecuted, this great saint was a man called

[014-117] Athanasius; and Saint Theresa was a nun. "Elias was a man

[014-118] subject to like passions as we are," says Saint James, to disabuse

[014-119] Christians of that false idea which makes us reject the

[014-120] example of the saints, as disproportioned to our state. "They

[014-121] were saints," say we, "they are not like us." What then

[014-122] actually happened? Saint Athanasius was a man called Athanasius,

[014-123] accused of many crimes, condemned by such and such

[014-124] a council for such and such a crime. All the bishops assented

[014-125] to it, and finally the Pope. What said they to those who

[014-126] opposed this? That they disturbed the peace, that they

[014-127] created schism, etc.

[014-128] Zeal, light. Four kinds of persons: zeal without knowledge;

[014-129] knowledge without zeal; neither knowledge nor zeal; both zeal

[014-130] and knowledge. The first three condemned him. The last

[014-131] acquitted him, were excommunicated by the Church, and yet

[014-132] saved the Church.

[014-133] 868

[014-134] If [[saint-augustine|Saint Augustine]] came at the present time, and was as

[014-135] little authorised as his defenders, he would accomplish nothing.

[014-136] God directs His Church well, by having sent him before with

[014-137] authority.

[014-138] 869

[014-139] God has not wanted to absolve without the Church. As she

[014-140] has part in the offence, He desires her to have part in the pardon.

[014-141] He associates her with this [[force|power]], as kings their parliaments.

[014-142] But if she absolves or binds without God, she is no longer the

[014-143] Church. For, as in the case of parliament, even if the king

[014-144] have pardoned a man, it must be ratified; but if parliament

[014-145] ratifies without the king, or refuses to ratify on the order of

[014-146] the king, it is no longer the parliament of the king, but a

[014-147] rebellious assembly.

[014-148] 870

[014-149] The Church, the Pope. Unity, plurality.—Considering the

[014-150] Church as a unity, the Pope, who is its head, is as the whole.

[014-151] Considering it as a plurality, the Pope is only a part of it. The

[014-152] Fathers have considered the Church now in the one way, now

[014-153] in the other. And thus they have spoken differently of the Pope.

[014-154] (Saint Cyprian: Sacerdos Dei.) But in establishing one of these

[014-155] truths, they have not excluded the other. Plurality which is

[014-156] not reduced to unity is confusion; unity which does not depend

[014-157] on plurality is tyranny. There is scarcely any other country

[014-158] than France in which it is permissible to say that the Council

[014-159] is above the Pope.

[014-160] 871

[014-161] The Pope is head. Who else is known of all? Who else is

[014-162] recognised by all, having power to insinuate himself into all the

[014-163] body, because he holds the principal shoot, which insinuates

[014-164] itself everywhere? How easy it was to make this degenerate

[014-165] into tyranny! That is why Christ has laid down for them this

[014-166] precept: Vos autem non sic.[363]

[014-167] 872

[014-168] The Pope hates and fears the learned, who do not submit

[014-169] to him at will.

[014-170] 873

[014-171] We must not judge of what the Pope is by some words of the

[014-172] Fathers—as the Greeks said in a council, important rules—but

[014-173] by the acts of the Church and the Fathers, and by the canons.

[014-174] Duo aut tres in unum.[364] Unity and plurality. It is an error

[014-175] to exclude one of the two, as the papists do who exclude plurality,

[014-176] or the Huguenots who exclude unity.

[014-177] 874

[014-178] Would the Pope be dishonoured by having his knowledge

[014-179] from God and tradition; and is it not dishonouring him to

[014-180] separate him from this holy union?

[014-181] 875

[014-182] God does not perform [[miracle|miracles]] in the ordinary conduct of

[014-183] His Church. It would be a strange miracle if infallibility

[014-184] existed in one man. But it appears so natural for it to reside

[014-185] in a multitude, since the conduct of God is hidden under nature,

[014-186] as in all His other works.

[014-187] 876

[014-188] Kings dispose of their own power; but the Popes cannot

[014-189] dispose of theirs.

[014-190] 877

[014-191] Summum jus, summa injuria.

[014-192] The majority is the best way, because it is visible, and has

[014-193] strength to make itself obeyed. Yet it is the opinion of the

[014-194] least able.

[014-195] If men could have done it, they would have placed might in

[014-196] the hands of justice. But as might does not allow itself to be

[014-197] managed as men want, because it is a palpable quality, whereas

[014-198] justice is a spiritual quality of which men dispose as they please,

[014-199] they have placed justice in the hands of might. And thus that

[014-200] is called [[justice|just]] which men are forced to obey.

[014-201] Hence comes the right of the sword, for the sword gives a

[014-202] true right. Otherwise we should see violence on one side and

[014-203] justice on the other (end of the twelfth Provincial). Hence

[014-204] comes the injustice of the Fronde,[365] which raises its alleged justice

[014-205] against power. It is not the same in the Church, for there is a

[014-206] true justice and no violence.

[014-207] 878

[014-208] Injustice.—Jurisdiction is not given for the sake of the judge,

[014-209] but for that of the litigant. It is dangerous to tell this to the

[014-210] people. But the people have too much faith in you; it will not

[014-211] harm them, and may serve you. It should therefore be made

[014-212] known. Pasce oves meas,[366] non tuas. You owe me pasturage.

[014-213] 879

[014-214] Men like certainty. They like the Pope to be infallible in

[014-215] faith, and grave doctors to be infallible in morals, so as to have

[014-216] certainty.

[014-217] 880

[014-218] The Church teaches, and God inspires, both infallibly. The

[014-219] work of the Church is of use only as a preparation for grace or

[014-220] condemnation. What it does is enough for condemnation, not

[014-221] for inspiration.

[014-222] 881

[014-223] Every time the Jesuits may impose upon the Pope, they will

[014-224] make all Christendom perjured.

[014-225] The Pope is very easily imposed upon, because of his occupations,

[014-226] and the confidence which he has in the Jesuits; and the

[014-227] Jesuits are very capable of imposing upon him by means of

[014-228] calumny.

[014-229] 882

[014-230] The wretches who have obliged me to speak of the basis

[014-231] of religion.

[014-232] 883

[014-233] Sinners purified without penitence; the righteous justified

[014-234] without love; all Christians without the grace of Jesus Christ;

[014-235] God without power over the will of men; a predestination

[014-236] without mystery; a redemption without [[certainty|certitude]]!

[014-237] 884

[014-238] Any one is made a priest, who wants to be so, as under

[014-239] Jeroboam.[367]

[014-240] It is a horrible thing that they propound to us the discipline

[014-241] of the Church of to-day as so good, that it is made a crime to

[014-242] desire to change it. Formerly it was infallibly good, and it was

[014-243] thought that it could be changed without sin; and now, such as

[014-244] it is, we cannot wish it changed! It has indeed been permitted

[014-245] to change the custom of not making priests without such great

[014-246] circumspection, that there were hardly any who were worthy;

[014-247] and it is not allowed to complain of the custom which makes so

[014-248] many who are unworthy!

[014-249] 885

[014-250] Heretics.—[[ezekiel|Ezekiel]]. All the heathen, and also the Prophet,

[014-251] spoke evil of [[jacob|Israel]]. But the Israelites were so far from having

[014-252] the right to say to him, "You speak like the heathen," that he

[014-253] is most forcible upon this, that the heathen say the same as he.

[014-254] 886

[014-255] The Jansenists are like the heretics in the reformation of

[014-256] morality; but you are like them in evil.

[014-257] 887

[014-258] You are ignorant of the [[prophecy|prophecies]], if you do not know that

[014-259] all this must happen; princes, prophets, Pope, and even the

[014-260] priests. And yet the Church is to abide. By the grace of God

[014-261] we have not come to that. Woe to these priests! But we hope

[014-262] that God will bestow His mercy upon us that we shall not be

[014-263] of them.

[014-264] [[saint-peter|Saint Peter]], ii: false prophets in the past, the image of future

[014-265] ones.

[014-266] 888

[014-267] ... So that if it is true, on the one hand, that some lax

[014-268] monks, and some corrupt casuists, who are not members of

[014-269] the hierarchy, are steeped in these corruptions, it is, on the

[014-270] other hand, certain that the true pastors of the Church, who

[014-271] are the true guardians of the Divine Word, have preserved it

[014-272] unchangeably against the efforts of those who have attempted

[014-273] to destroy it.

[014-274] And thus true believers have no pretext to follow that laxity,

[014-275] which is only offered to them by the strange hands of these

[014-276] casuists, instead of the sound doctrine which is presented to them

[014-277] by the fatherly hands of their own pastors. And the ungodly

[014-278] and heretics have no ground for publishing these abuses as

[014-279] evidence of imperfection in the providence of God over His

[014-280] Church; since, the Church consisting properly in the body of

[014-281] the hierarchy, we are so far from being able to conclude from

[014-282] the present state of matters that God has abandoned her to

[014-283] corruption, that it has never been more apparent than at the

[014-284] present time that God visibly protects her from corruption.

[014-285] For if some of these men, who, by an extraordinary vocation,

[014-286] have made profession of withdrawing from the world and

[014-287] adopting the monks' dress, in order to live in a more perfect

[014-288] state than ordinary Christians, have fallen into excesses which

[014-289] horrify ordinary Christians, and have become to us what the

[014-290] false prophets were among the Jews; this is a private and personal

[014-291] misfortune, which must indeed be deplored, but from which

[014-292] nothing can be inferred against the care which God takes of

[014-293] His Church; since all these things are so clearly foretold, and it

[014-294] has been so long since announced that these temptations would

[014-295] arise from people of this kind; so that when we are well instructed,

[014-296] we see in this rather evidence of the care of God than

[014-297] of His forgetfulness in regard to us.

[014-298] 889

[014-299] Tertullian: Nunquam Ecclesia reformabitur.

[014-300] 890

[014-301] Heretics, who take advantage of the doctrine of the Jesuits,

[014-302] must be made to know that it is not that of the Church [the

[014-303] doctrine of the Church], and that our divisions do not separate

[014-304] us from the altar.

[014-305] 891

[014-306] If in differing we condemned, you would be right. Uniformity

[014-307] without diversity is useless to others; diversity without

[014-308] uniformity is ruinous for us. The one is harmful outwardly;

[014-309] the other inwardly.

[014-310] 892

[014-311] By showing the truth, we cause it to be believed; but by

[014-312] showing the injustice of ministers, we do not correct it. Our

[014-313] [[spirit|mind]] is assured by a proof of falsehood; our purse is not made

[014-314] secure by proof of injustice.

[014-315] 893

[014-316] Those who love the Church lament to see the corruption of

[014-317] morals; but laws at least exist. But these corrupt the laws.

[014-318] The model is damaged.

[014-319] 894

[014-320] Men never do evil so completely and cheerfully as when they

[014-321] do it from religious conviction.

[014-322] 895

[014-323] It is in vain that the Church has established these words,

[014-324] anathemas, heresies, etc. They are used against her.

[014-325] 896

[014-326] The servant knoweth not what his lord doeth, for the master

[014-327] tells him only the act and not the intention.[368] And this is why

[014-328] he often obeys slavishly, and defeats the intention. But Jesus

[014-329] Christ has told us the object. And you defeat that object.
