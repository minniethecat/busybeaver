[[Contradiction|Contradiction]], in Pascal's method, is not an embarrassment to be explained away but a signal to be read. Where [[reason|reason]] sees only incoherence, [[faith-and-reason|faith]] discerns a pattern: contradiction is the form in which divine truth presents itself to a corrupted human understanding.

> (007-064) ...contradictions, esteem Scripture.

This single line encapsulates Pascal's reversal of the rationalist critique: the very feature that the [[scepticism|sceptic]] takes as grounds for despising [[Scripture|Scripture]] — its apparent contradictions — is, for Pascal, precisely what should command esteem. A book that did not contain contradictions would be suspect as a merely human production.

## In Pascal's Pensées

### Contradiction as Hermeneutic Principle

Pascal's treatment of contradiction is not apologetic special pleading but a coherent [[hermeneutics|hermeneutics]]. Scripture contains contradictions because it addresses human beings in their contradictory condition. A God who spoke without contradictions would be a God who had not taken the full measure of human [[corruption|corruption]].

> (007-074) ...for these astonishing contradictions.

> (007-127) ...the cause of those contradictions which have astonished all men...

The astonishment that contradictions provoke is, for Pascal, pedagogically necessary. Contradiction unsettles the complacent reader, breaks the habit of superficial reading, and drives the seeker toward a deeper engagement with the text. The [[hiddenness-of-god|hidden God]] speaks in contradictions because a God who spoke plainly would compel belief rather than inviting [[faith|faith]].

### The Anthropological Contradiction

The most important contradiction in Pascal's system is not textual but anthropological: man himself is a contradiction.

> (007-322) ...monster, what a chaos, what a contradiction, what a prodigy!

Man is "a chaos, a contradiction, a prodigy" because he is at once [[greatness-and-misery|great and miserable]], capable of the highest [[thought|thought]] and the lowest [[concupiscence|concupiscence]], made for [[truth|truth]] yet incapable of attaining it by his own powers. This anthropological contradiction is the starting point of Pascal's [[apologetics|apologetics]]: only [[christianity|Christianity]], with its twin doctrines of creation in the [[image-of-god|image of God]] and the [[fall|Fall]], can account for a being who is simultaneously so elevated and so debased.

> (002-402) ...is never this contradiction in the feelings towards a cri...

The contradiction is not merely intellectual but affective. Human beings feel contradictory things — [[attraction|attraction]] and [[repulsion|repulsion]], [[love|love]] and [[hatred|hatred]] — toward the same objects. This affective contradiction is, for Pascal, a trace of the Fall: the [[will|will]] is divided against itself because it is turned away from its true [[sovereign-good|good]].

### Contradiction as Apologetic Method

Pascal's own writing enacts the method of contradiction. He presents opposites — [[scepticism|scepticism]] and [[dogmatism|dogmatism]], [[greatness-and-misery|greatness]] and [[wretchedness|wretchedness]], [[reason|reason]] and [[faith-and-reason|faith]] — and refuses to resolve them prematurely. The tension itself is the argument.

> (007-193) ...to reconcile these contradictions, I intend to make you see...

The "reconciliation" Pascal offers is not a dissolution of contradiction but a [[re-framing|re-framing]] that reveals it as the necessary form of a truth too large for unaided reason. Contradiction is the shape that [[mystery|mystery]] takes in the language of fallen understanding.

### The Cross as Source of Contradictions

> (012-234) Source of contradictions. A God humiliated, even to the death of the cross...

The [[cross|cross]] is the "source of contradictions" because it is the point where all human expectations are inverted. A God who dies; glory in humiliation; strength in weakness; victory in defeat. The cross is not merely one contradiction among others — it is the originating contradiction from which all others derive their structure. The crucified God is the ultimate instance of Pascal's principle: truth appears in the form of its opposite.

## Analysis

Pascal's use of contradiction operates on three distinct but interrelated planes.

On the **textual plane**, contradiction in Scripture is a sign of divine authorship: a merely human book would have been smoothed of its inconsistencies. The [[Scripture|Scripture]] that "has no order" (004-342) testifies to an author who is not constrained by human canons of consistency — and who deliberately uses obscurity to enlighten some and [[blind|blind]] others.

On the **anthropological plane**, contradiction is the inescapable datum of human self-experience. Pascal's inventory of contradictions — man judges everything yet is "a clod of earth" (002-082); man desires truth yet flees from it into [[diversion|diversion]]; man seeks happiness yet cannot find it — is not a counsel of despair but a diagnosis. Contradiction is the symptom; [[original-sin|original sin]] is the aetiology; [[grace|grace]] is the cure.

On the **apologetic plane**, contradiction is a method. Pascal does not argue from premises to conclusions but from tensions to their resolution. His procedure is to heighten contradiction to the point where only one resolution remains possible — and then to show that this resolution is precisely the Christian [[doctrine|doctrine]] that reason alone would have dismissed.
