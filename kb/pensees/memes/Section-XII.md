[012-001] SECTION XII

[012-002] PROOFS OF JESUS CHRIST

[012-003] 736

[012-004] ... Therefore I reject all other religions. In that way I find

[012-005] an answer to all objections. It is right that a [[god|God]] so pure

[012-006] should only reveal Himself to those whose hearts are purified.

[012-007] Hence this religion is lovable to me, and I find it now sufficiently

[012-008] justified by so divine a morality. But I find more in it.

[012-009] I find it convincing that, since the memory of man has lasted,

[012-010] it was constantly announced to men that they were universally

[012-011] corrupt, but that a Redeemer should come; that it was not one

[012-012] man who said it, but innumerable men, and a whole nation

[012-013] expressly made for the purpose, and prophesying for four

[012-014] thousand years. This is a nation which is more ancient than

[012-015] every other nation. Their books, scattered abroad, are four

[012-016] thousand years old.

[012-017] The more I examine them, the more [[truth|truths]] I find in them:

[012-018] an entire nation foretell Him before His advent, and an entire

[012-019] nation worship Him after His advent; what has preceded and

[012-020] what has followed; in short, people without idols and kings, this

[012-021] synagogue which was foretold, and these wretches who frequent

[012-022] it, and who, being our enemies, are admirable witnesses of the

[012-023] truth of these [[prophecy|prophecies]], wherein their wretchedness and even

[012-024] their blindness are foretold.

[012-025] I find this succession, this religion, wholly divine in its

[012-026] authority, in its duration, in its perpetuity, in its morality,

[012-027] in its conduct, in its doctrine, in its effects. The frightful

[012-028] darkness of the Jews was foretold: Eris palpans in meridie.[276]

[012-029] Dabitur liber scienti literas, et dicet: Non possum legere.[277] While

[012-030] the sceptre was still in the hands of the first foreign usurper,

[012-031] there is the report of the coming of [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]].

[012-032] So I hold out my arms to my Redeemer, who, having been

[012-033] foretold for four thousand years, has come to suffer and to die

[012-034] for me on earth, at the time and under all the circumstances

[012-035] foretold. By His grace, I await death in peace, in the hope of

[012-036] being eternally united to Him. Yet I live with joy, whether

[012-037] in the prosperity which it pleases Him to bestow upon me, or

[012-038] in the adversity which He sends for my good, and which He has

[012-039] taught me to bear by His example.

[012-040] 737

[012-041] The prophecies having given different signs which should

[012-042] all happen at the advent of the Messiah, it was necessary that

[012-043] all these signs should occur at the same time. So it was

[012-044] necessary that the fourth monarchy should have come, when

[012-045] the seventy weeks of [[daniel|Daniel]] were ended; and that the sceptre

[012-046] should have then departed from Judah. And all this happened

[012-047] without any difficulty. Then it was necessary that the Messiah

[012-048] should come; and Jesus Christ then came, who was called the

[012-049] Messiah. And all this again was without difficulty. This indeed

[012-050] shows the truth of the prophecies.

[012-051] 738

[012-052] The prophets foretold, and were not foretold. The saints

[012-053] again were foretold, but did not foretell. Jesus Christ both

[012-054] foretold and was foretold.

[012-055] 739

[012-056] Jesus Christ, whom the two Testaments regard, the Old as

[012-057] its hope, the New as its model, and both as their centre.

[012-058] 740

[012-059] The two oldest books in the world are those of [[moses|Moses]] and

[012-060] [[job|Job]], the one a Jew and the other a Gentile. Both of them

[012-061] look upon Jesus Christ as their common centre and object:

[012-062] Moses in relating the promises of God to [[abraham|Abraham]], [[jacob|Jacob]], etc.,

[012-063] and his prophecies; and Job, Quis mihi det ut,[278] etc. Scio enim

[012-064] quod redemptor meus vivit, etc.

[012-065] 741

[012-066] The Gospel only speaks of the virginity of the Virgin up to

[012-067] the time of the birth of Jesus Christ. All with reference to

[012-068] Jesus Christ.

[012-069] 742

[012-070] Proofs of Jesus Christ.

[012-071] Why was the book of Ruth preserved?

[012-072] Why the story of Tamar?

[012-073] 743

[012-074] "Pray that ye enter not into temptation."[279] It is dangerous

[012-075] to be tempted; and people are tempted because they do not pray.

[012-076] Et tu conversus confirma fratres tuos. But before, conversus

[012-077] Jesus respexit Petrum.

[012-078] [[saint-peter|Saint Peter]] asks permission to strike Malchus, and strikes

[012-079] before hearing the answer. Jesus Christ replies afterwards.

[012-080] The word, Galilee, which the Jewish mob pronounced as if

[012-081] by chance, in accusing Jesus Christ before [[pilate|Pilate]], afforded

[012-082] Pilate a reason for sending Jesus Christ to [[herod|Herod]]. And thereby

[012-083] the mystery was accomplished, that He should be judged by

[012-084] Jews and Gentiles. Chance was apparently the cause of the

[012-085] accomplishment of the mystery.

[012-086] 744

[012-087] Those who have a difficulty in believing seek a reason in the

[012-088] fact that the Jews do not believe. "Were this so clear," say

[012-089] they, "why did the Jews not believe?" And they almost

[012-090] wish that they had believed, so as not to be kept back by the

[012-091] example of their refusal. But it is their very refusal that is the

[012-092] foundation of our faith. We should be much less disposed to

[012-093] the faith, if they were on our side. We should then have a

[012-094] more ample pretext. The wonderful thing is to have made the

[012-095] Jews great lovers of the things foretold, and great enemies of

[012-096] their fulfilment.

[012-097] 745

[012-098] The Jews were accustomed to great and striking [[miracle|miracles]],

[012-099] and so, having had the great miracles of the Red Sea and of the

[012-100] land of Canaan as an epitome of the great deeds of their Messiah,

[012-101] they therefore looked for more striking miracles, of which

[012-102] those of Moses were only the patterns.

[012-103] 746

[012-104] The carnal Jews and the heathen have their calamities, and

[012-105] Christians also. There is no Redeemer for the heathen, for they

[012-106] do not so much as hope for one. There is no Redeemer for the

[012-107] Jews; they hope for Him in vain. There is a Redeemer only

[012-108] for Christians. (See Perpetuity.)

[012-109] 747

[012-110] In the time of the Messiah the people divided themselves.

[012-111] The spiritual embraced the Messiah, and the coarser-minded

[012-112] remained to serve as witnesses of Him.

[012-113] 748

[012-114] "If this was clearly foretold to the Jews, how did they not

[012-115] believe it, or why were they not destroyed for resisting a fact

[012-116] so clear?"

[012-117] I reply: in the first place, it was foretold both that they

[012-118] would not believe a thing so clear, and that they would not be

[012-119] destroyed. And nothing is more to the glory of the Messiah;

[012-120] for it was not enough that there should be prophets; their

[012-121] prophets must be kept above suspicion. Now, etc.

[012-122] 749

[012-123] If the Jews had all been converted by Jesus Christ, we should

[012-124] have none but questionable witnesses. And if they had been

[012-125] entirely destroyed, we should have no witnesses at all.

[012-126] 750

[012-127] What do the prophets say of Jesus Christ? That He will be

[012-128] clearly God? No; but that He is a God truly hidden; that He

[012-129] will be slighted; that none will think that it is He; that He will

[012-130] be a stone of stumbling, upon which many will stumble, etc.

[012-131] Let people then reproach us no longer for want of clearness,

[012-132] since we make profession of it.

[012-133] But, it is said, there are obscurities.—And without that, no

[012-134] one would have stumbled over Jesus Christ, and this is one

[012-135] of the formal pronouncements of the prophets: Excæca[280] ...

[012-136] 751

[012-137] Moses first teaches the Trinity, [[sin|original sin]], the Messiah.

[012-138] [[king-david|David]]: a great witness; a king, good, merciful, a beautiful

[012-139] soul, a sound [[spirit|mind]], powerful. He prophesies, and his wonder

[012-140] comes to pass. This is infinite.

[012-141] He had only to say that he was the Messiah, if he had been

[012-142] vain; for the prophecies are clearer about him than about Jesus

[012-143] Christ. And the same with [[saint-john|Saint John]].

[012-144] 752

[012-145] Herod was believed to be the Messiah. He had taken away

[012-146] the sceptre from Judah, but he was not of Judah. This gave

[012-147] rise to a considerable sect.

[012-148] Curse of the Greeks upon those who count three periods of

[012-149] time.

[012-150] In what way should the Messiah come, seeing that through

[012-151] Him the sceptre was to be eternally in Judah, and at His coming

[012-152] the sceptre was to be taken away from Judah?

[012-153] In order to effect that seeing they should not see, and hearing

[012-154] they should not understand, nothing could be better done.

[012-155] 753

[012-156] Homo existens te Deum facit.

[012-157] Scriptum est, Dii estis, et non potest solvi Scriptura.

[012-158] Hæc infirmitas non est ad vitam et est ad mortem.

[012-159] Lazarus dormit, et deinde dixit: Lazarus mortuus est.[281]

[012-160] 754

[012-161] The apparent discrepancy of the Gospels.[282]

[012-162] 755

[012-163] What can we have but reverence for a man who foretells

[012-164] plainly things which come to pass, and who declares his intention

[012-165] both to blind and to enlighten, and who intersperses obscurities

[012-166] among the clear things which come to pass?

[012-167] 756

[012-168] The time of the first advent was foretold; the time of the

[012-169] second is not so; because the first was to be obscure, and the

[012-170] second is to be brilliant, and so manifest that even His enemies

[012-171] will recognise it. But, as He was first to come only in [[evidence|obscurity]],

[012-172] and to be known only of those who searched [[scripture|the Scriptures]] ...

[012-173] 757

[012-174] God, in order to cause the Messiah to be known by the good

[012-175] and not to be known by the wicked, made Him to be foretold

[012-176] in this manner. If the manner of the Messiah had been clearly

[012-177] foretold, there would have been no obscurity, even for the

[012-178] wicked. If the time had been obscurely foretold, there would

[012-179] have been obscurity, even for the good. For their [goodness

[012-180] of heart] would not have made them understand, for instance,

[012-181] that the closed mem signifies six hundred years. But the time

[012-182] has been clearly foretold, and the manner in types.

[012-183] By this means, the wicked, taking the promised blessings

[012-184] for material blessings, have fallen into error, in spite of the

[012-185] clear prediction of the time; and the good have not fallen in

[012-186] error. For the understanding of the promised blessings depends

[012-187] on the heart, which calls "good" that which it loves; but the

[012-188] understanding of the promised time does not depend on the

[012-189] heart. And thus the clear prediction of the time, and the

[012-190] obscure prediction of the blessings, deceive the wicked alone.

[012-191] 758

[012-192] [Either the Jews or the Christians must be wicked.]

[012-193] 759

[012-194] The Jews reject Him, but not all. The saints receive Him,

[012-195] and not the carnal-minded. And so far is this from being

[012-196] against His glory, that it is the last touch which crowns it.

[012-197] For their argument, the only one found in all their writings, in

[012-198] the Talmud and in the Rabbinical writings, amounts only to

[012-199] this, that Jesus Christ has not subdued the nations with sword

[012-200] in hand, gladiumt uum, potentissime.[283] (Is this all they have to

[012-201] say? Jesus Christ has been slain, say they. He has failed. He

[012-202] has not subdued the heathen with His [[force|might]]. He has not

[012-203] bestowed upon us their spoil. He does not give riches. Is

[012-204] this all they have to say? It is in this respect that He is lovable

[012-205] to me. I would not desire Him whom they fancy.) It is evident

[012-206] that it is only His life which has prevented them from accepting

[012-207] Him; and through this rejection they are irreproachable

[012-208] witnesses, and, what is more, they thereby accomplish the

[012-209] prophecies.

[012-210] [By means of the fact that this people have not accepted

[012-211] Him, this miracle here has happened. The prophecies were

[012-212] the only lasting miracles which could be wrought, but they

[012-213] were liable to be denied.]

[012-214] 760

[012-215] The Jews, in slaying Him in order not to receive Him as the

[012-216] Messiah, have given Him the final proof of being the Messiah.

[012-217] And in continuing not to recognise Him, they made themselves

[012-218] irreproachable witnesses. Both in slaying Him, and

[012-219] in continuing to deny Him, they have fulfilled the prophecies

[012-220] (Isa. lx; Ps. lxxi).

[012-221] 761

[012-222] What could the Jews, His enemies, do? If they receive Him,

[012-223] they give proof of Him by their reception; for then the guardians

[012-224] of the expectation of the Messiah receive Him. If they reject

[012-225] Him, they give proof of Him by their rejection.

[012-226] 762

[012-227] The Jews, in testing if He were God, have shown that He was

[012-228] man.

[012-229] 763

[012-230] The Church has had as much difficulty in showing that Jesus

[012-231] Christ was man, against those who denied it, as in showing that

[012-232] he was God; and the probabilities were equally great.

[012-233] 764

[012-234] Source of [[contradiction|contradictions]].—A God humiliated, even to the

[012-235] death on the cross; a Messiah triumphing over death by his

[012-236] own death. Two natures in Jesus Christ, two advents, two

[012-237] states of man's nature.

[012-238] 765

[012-239] Types.—Saviour, father, sacrificer, offering, food, king, wise,

[012-240] law-giver, afflicted, poor, having to create a people whom He

[012-241] must lead and nourish, and bring into His land....

[012-242] Jesus Christ. Offices.—He alone had to create a great people,

[012-243] elect, holy, and chosen; to lead, nourish, and bring it into the

[012-244] place of rest and holiness; to make it holy to God; to make it

[012-245] the temple of God; to reconcile it to, and save it from, the

[012-246] wrath of God; to free it from the slavery of sin, which

[012-247] visibly reigns in man; to give laws to this people, and engrave

[012-248] these laws on their heart; to offer Himself to God for them, and

[012-249] sacrifice Himself for them; to be a victim without blemish, and

[012-250] Himself the sacrificer, having to offer Himself, His body, and

[012-251] His blood, and yet to offer bread and wine to God ...

[012-252] Ingrediens mundum.[284]

[012-253] "Stone upon stone."[285]

[012-254] What preceded and what followed. All the Jews exist still,

[012-255] and are wanderers.

[012-256] 766

[012-257] Of all that is on earth, He partakes only of the sorrows, not

[012-258] of the joys. He loves His neighbours, but His love does not

[012-259] confine itself within these bounds, and overflows to His own

[012-260] enemies, and then to those of God.

[012-261] 767

[012-262] Jesus Christ typified by [[joseph|Joseph]], the beloved of his father,

[012-263] sent by his father to see his brethren, etc., innocent, sold by

[012-264] his brethren for twenty pieces of silver, and thereby becoming

[012-265] their lord, their saviour, the saviour of strangers, and the

[012-266] saviour of the world; which had not been but for their plot to

[012-267] destroy him, their sale and their rejection of him.

[012-268] In prison Joseph innocent between two criminals; Jesus

[012-269] Christ on the cross between two thieves. Joseph foretells

[012-270] freedom to the one, and death to the other, from the same

[012-271] omens. Jesus Christ saves the elect, and condemns the outcast

[012-272] for the same sins. Joseph foretells only; Jesus Christ acts.

[012-273] Joseph asks him who will be saved to remember him, when

[012-274] he comes into his glory; and he whom Jesus Christ saves asks

[012-275] that He will remember him, when He comes into His kingdom.

[012-276] 768

[012-277] The conversion of the heathen was only reserved for the grace

[012-278] of the Messiah. The Jews have been so long in opposition to

[012-279] them without success; all that [[solomon|Solomon]] and the prophets said

[012-280] has been useless. Sages, like [[plato|Plato]] and Socrates, have not

[012-281] been able to persuade them.

[012-282] 769

[012-283] After many persons had gone before, Jesus Christ at last

[012-284] came to say:[286] "Here am I, and this is the time. That which

[012-285] the prophets have said was to come in the fullness of time, I tell

[012-286] you My apostles will do. The Jews shall be cast out. Jerusalem

[012-287] shall be soon destroyed. And the heathen shall enter into the

[012-288] knowledge of God. My apostles shall do this after you have

[012-289] slain the heir of the vineyard."

[012-290] Then the apostles said to the Jews: "You shall be accursed,"

[012-291] (Celsus laughed at it); and to the heathen, "You shall enter into

[012-292] the knowledge of God." And this then came to pass.

[012-293] 770

[012-294] Jesus Christ came to blind those who saw clearly, and to give

[012-295] sight to the blind; to heal the sick, and leave the healthy to die;

[012-296] to call to repentance, and to justify sinners, and to leave the

[012-297] righteous in their sins; to fill the needy, and leave the rich empty.

[012-298] 771

[012-299] [[holiness|Holiness]].—Effundam spiritum meum.[287] All nations were in

[012-300] unbelief and lust. The whole world now became fervent with

[012-301] love. Princes abandoned their pomp; maidens suffered martyrdom.

[012-302] Whence came this influence? The Messiah was come.

[012-303] These were the effect and sign of His coming.

[012-306] gentes venient et adorabunt eum.[288] Parum est ut,[289] etc. Postula a

[012-309] 773

[012-310] Jesus Christ for all, Moses for a nation.

[012-311] The Jews blessed in Abraham: "I will bless those that bless

[012-312] thee."[295] But: "All nations blessed in his seed."[296] Parum est ut, etc.

[012-313] Lumen ad revelationem gentium.[297]

[012-314] Non fecit taliter omni nationi,[298] said David, in speaking of the

[012-315] [[law|Law]]. But, in speaking of Jesus Christ, we must say: Fecit

[012-316] taliter omni nationi. Parum est ut, etc., [[isaiah|Isaiah]]. So it belongs

[012-317] to Jesus Christ to be universal. Even the Church offers sacrifice

[012-318] only for the faithful. Jesus Christ offered that of the cross for all.

[012-319] 774

[012-320] There is heresy in always explaining omnes by "all," and

[012-321] heresy in not explaining it sometimes by "all." Bibite ex hoc

[012-322] omnes;[299] the Huguenots are heretics in explaining it by "all." In

[012-323] quo omnes peccaverunt;[300] the Huguenots are heretics in excepting

[012-324] the children of true believers. We must then follow the Fathers

[012-325] and tradition in order to know when to do so, since there is

[012-326] heresy to be feared on both sides.

[012-327] 775

[012-328] Ne timeas pusillus grex.[301] Timore et tremore.—Quid ergo?

[012-329] Ne timeas [modo] timeas. [[fear|Fear]] not, provided you fear; but

[012-330] if you fear not, then fear.

[012-331] Qui me recipit, non me recipit, sed eum qui me misit.[302]

[012-332] Nemo scit, neque Filius.

[012-333] Nubes lucida obumbravit.

[012-334] Saint John[303] was to turn the hearts of the fathers to the children,

[012-336] 776

[012-337] The effects in communi and in particulari. The semi-Pelagians

[012-338] err in saying of in communi what is true only in

[012-339] particulari; and the Calvinists in saying in particulari what is

[012-345] 778

[012-346] If men knew themselves, God would heal and pardon them.

[012-348] 779

[012-349] Jesus Christ never condemned without hearing. To Judas:

[012-350] Amice, ad quid venisti?[308] To him that had not on the wedding

[012-352] 780

[012-353] The types of the completeness of the [[redemption|Redemption]], as that the

[012-354] sun gives light to all, indicate only completeness; but [the types]

[012-355] of exclusions, as of the Jews elected to the exclusion of the

[012-356] Gentiles, indicate exclusion.

[012-357] "Jesus Christ the Redeemer of all."—Yes, for He has offered,

[012-358] like a man who has ransomed all those who were willing to come

[012-359] to Him. If any die on the way, it is their misfortune; but, so

[012-360] far as He was concerned, He offered them redemption.—That

[012-361] holds good in this example, where he who ransoms and he who

[012-362] prevents death are two persons, but not of Jesus Christ, who

[012-363] does both these things.—No, for Jesus Christ, in the quality of

[012-364] Redeemer, is not perhaps Master of all; and thus, in so far as it is

[012-365] in Him, He is the Redeemer of all.

[012-366] When it is said that Jesus Christ did not die for all, you

[012-367] take undue advantage of a fault in men who at once apply this

[012-368] exception to themselves; and this is to favour despair, instead

[012-369] of turning them from it to favour hope. For men thus accustom

[012-370] themselves in inward virtues by outward customs.

[012-372] The victory over death. "What is a man advantaged if he

[012-373] gain the whole world and lose his own soul?[309] Whosoever will

[012-375] "I am not come to destroy the law, but to fulfil."[311]

[012-380] 782

[012-381] ... Then Jesus Christ comes to tell men that they have no

[012-382] other enemies but themselves; that it is their passions which

[012-383] keep them apart from God; that He comes to destroy these,

[012-384] and give them His grace, so as to make of them all one Holy

[012-385] Church; that He comes to bring back into this Church the

[012-386] heathen and Jews; that He comes to destroy the idols of the

[012-387] former and the superstition of the latter. To this all men are

[012-388] opposed, not only from the natural opposition of lust; but,

[012-389] above all, the kings of the earth, as had been foretold, join

[012-390] together to destroy this religion at its birth. (Proph.: Quare

[012-391] fremuerunt gentes ... reges terræ ... adversus Christum.)[314]

[012-392] All that is great on earth is united together; the learned, the

[012-393] wise, the kings. The first write; the second condemn; the last

[012-394] kill. And notwithstanding all these oppositions, these men,

[012-395] simple and weak, resist all these powers, subdue even these

[012-396] kings, these learned men and these sages, and remove idolatry

[012-397] from all the earth. And all this is done by the power which

[012-398] had foretold it.

[012-399] 783

[012-400] Jesus Christ would not have the testimony of devils, nor of

[012-401] those who were not called, but of God and John the Baptist.

[012-402] 784

[012-403] I consider Jesus Christ in all persons and in ourselves: Jesus

[012-404] Christ as a Father in His Father, Jesus Christ as a Brother in

[012-405] His Brethren, Jesus Christ as poor in the poor, Jesus Christ as

[012-406] rich in the rich, Jesus Christ as Doctor and Priest in priests,

[012-407] Jesus Christ as Sovereign in princes, etc. For by His glory He

[012-408] is all that is great, being God; and by His mortal life He is all

[012-409] that is poor and abject. Therefore He has taken this unhappy

[012-410] condition, so that He could be in all persons, and the model of

[012-411] all conditions.

[012-412] 785

[012-413] Jesus Christ is an obscurity (according to what the world

[012-414] calls obscurity), such that historians, writing only of important

[012-415] matters of states, have hardly noticed Him.

[012-416] 786

[012-417] On the fact that neither Josephus, nor Tacitus, nor other historians

[012-418] have spoken of Jesus Christ.—So far is this from telling against

[012-419] Christianity, that on the contrary it tells for it. For it is certain

[012-422] is plain that they purposely concealed it, or that, if they did

[012-423] speak of it, their account has been suppressed or changed.

[012-432] Jesus would not be slain without the forms of justice; for it is

[012-434] 790

[012-435] The false justice of Pilate only serves to make Jesus Christ

[012-436] suffer; for he causes Him to be scourged by his false justice,

[012-437] and afterwards puts Him to death. It would have been better

[012-438] to have put Him to death at once. Thus it is with the falsely

[012-439] [[justice|just]]. They do good and evil works to please the world, and to

[012-440] show that they are not altogether of Jesus Christ; for they are

[012-441] ashamed of Him. And at last, under great temptation and on

[012-443] 791

[012-444] What man ever had more renown? The whole [[jews|Jewish people]]

[012-445] foretell Him before His coming. The Gentile people worship

[012-446] Him after His coming. The two peoples, Gentile and Jewish,

[012-447] regard Him as their centre.

[012-448] And yet what man enjoys this renown less? Of thirty-three

[012-449] years, He lives thirty without appearing. For three years He

[012-450] passes as an impostor; the priests and the chief people reject

[012-451] Him; His friends and His nearest relatives despise Him. Finally,

[012-452] He dies, betrayed by one of His own disciples, denied by another,

[012-453] and abandoned by all.

[012-454] What part, then, has He in this renown? Never had man so

[012-455] much renown; never had man more ignominy. All that renown

[012-456] has served only for us, to render us capable of recognising Him;

[012-457] and He had none of it for Himself.

[012-458] 792

[012-459] The infinite distance between body and mind is a symbol of

[012-460] the infinitely more infinite distance between mind and charity;

[012-461] for charity is supernatural.

[012-462] All the glory of greatness has no lustre for people who are in

[012-463] search of understanding.

[012-464] The greatness of clever men is invisible to kings, to the rich,

[012-465] to chiefs, and to all the worldly great.

[012-466] The greatness of wisdom, which is nothing if not of God, is

[012-467] invisible to the carnal-minded and to the clever. These are

[012-468] three orders differing in kind.

[012-469] Great geniuses have their power, their glory, their greatness,

[012-470] their victory, their lustre, and have no need of worldly greatness,

[012-471] with which they are not in keeping. They are seen, not by the

[012-472] eye, but by the mind; this is sufficient.

[012-473] The saints have their power, their glory, their victory, their

[012-474] lustre, and need no worldly or intellectual greatness, with which

[012-475] they have no affinity; for these neither add anything to them,

[012-476] nor take away anything from them. They are seen of God

[012-477] and the angels, and not of the body, nor of the curious mind.

[012-478] God is enough for them.

[012-479] Archimedes,[316] apart from his rank, would have the same

[012-480] veneration. He fought no battles for the eyes to feast upon;

[012-481] but he has given his discoveries to all men. Oh! how brilliant

[012-482] he was to the mind!

[012-483] Jesus Christ, without riches, and without any external

[012-484] exhibition of knowledge, is in His own order of holiness. He

[012-485] did not invent; He did not reign. But He was humble, patient,

[012-486] holy, holy to God, terrible to devils, without any sin. Oh! in

[012-487] what great pomp, and in what wonderful splendour, He is come

[012-488] to the eyes of the heart, which perceive wisdom!

[012-489] It would have been useless for Archimedes to have acted

[012-490] the prince in his books on geometry, although he was a

[012-491] prince.

[012-492] It would have been useless for our Lord Jesus Christ to

[012-493] come like a king, in order to shine forth in His kingdom of

[012-494] holiness. But He came there appropriately in the glory of His

[012-495] own order.

[012-496] It is most absurd to take offence at the [[humility|lowliness]] of Jesus

[012-497] Christ, as if His lowliness were in the same order as the greatness

[012-498] which He came to manifest. If we consider this greatness in

[012-499] His life, in His passion, in His obscurity, in His death, in the

[012-500] choice of His disciples, in their desertion, in His secret resurrection,

[012-501] and the rest, we shall see it to be so immense, that we

[012-502] shall have no reason for being offended at a lowliness which is

[012-503] not of that order.

[012-504] But there are some who can only admire worldly greatness,

[012-505] as though there were no intellectual greatness; and others who

[012-506] only admire intellectual greatness, as though there were not

[012-507] infinitely higher things in wisdom.

[012-508] All bodies, the firmament, the stars, the earth and its kingdoms,

[012-509] are not equal to the lowest mind; for mind knows all

[012-510] these and itself; and these bodies nothing.

[012-511] All bodies together, and all minds together, and all their

[012-512] products, are not equal to the least feeling of charity. This

[012-513] is of an order infinitely more exalted.

[012-514] From all bodies together, we cannot obtain one little thought;

[012-515] this is impossible, and of another order. From all bodies and

[012-516] minds, we cannot produce a feeling of true charity; this is

[012-517] impossible, and of another and supernatural order.

[012-523] If Jesus Christ had only come to sanctify, all Scripture and

[012-524] all things would tend to that end; and it would be quite easy

[012-525] to convince unbelievers. If Jesus Christ had only come to blind,

[012-526] all His conduct would be confused; and we would have no

[012-527] means of convincing unbelievers. But as He came in sanctificationem

[012-528] et in scandalum,[317] as Isaiah says, we cannot convince

[012-529] unbelievers, and they cannot convince us. But by this very

[012-530] fact we convince them; since we say that in His whole conduct

[012-531] there is no convincing proof on one side or the other.

[012-537] Proofs of Jesus Christ.—Jesus Christ said great things so

[012-538] simply, that it seems as though He had not thought them great;

[012-539] and yet so clearly that we easily see what He thought of them.

[012-540] This clearness, joined to this simplicity, is wonderful.

[012-541] 797

[012-542] The style of the gospel is admirable in so many ways, and

[012-543] among the rest in hurling no invectives against the persecutors

[012-544] and enemies of Jesus Christ. For there is no such invective

[012-545] in any of the historians against Judas, Pilate, or any of the

[012-546] Jews.

[012-547] If this moderation of the writers of the Gospels had been

[012-548] assumed, as well as many other traits of so beautiful a character,

[012-549] and they had only assumed it to attract notice, even if they had

[012-550] not dared to draw attention to it themselves, they would not

[012-551] have failed to secure friends, who would have made such

[012-552] remarks to their advantage. But as they acted thus without

[012-553] pretence, and from wholly disinterested motives, they did not

[012-554] point it out to any one; and I believe that many such facts have

[012-555] not been noticed till now, which is evidence of the natural

[012-556] disinterestedness with which the thing has been done.

[012-566] how to paint a resolute death? Yes, for the same Saint Luke

[012-574] [[proof|Proof]] of Jesus Christ.—The supposition that the apostles

[012-575] were impostors is very absurd. Let us think it out. Let us

[012-576] imagine those twelve men, assembled after the death of Jesus

[012-577] Christ, plotting to say that He was risen. By this they attack

[012-578] all the powers. The heart of man is strangely inclined to

[012-582] lost. Let us follow up this thought.
