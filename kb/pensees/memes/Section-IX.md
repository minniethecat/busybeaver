[009-048] 596

[009-049] Against Mahomet.—The Koran is not more of Mahomet than

[009-050] the Gospel is of [[saint-matthew|Saint Matthew]], for it is cited by many authors

[009-051] from age to age. Even its very enemies, Celsus and Porphyry,

[009-052] never denied it.

[009-053] The Koran says Saint Matthew was an honest man.[216] Therefore

[009-054] Mahomet was a false prophet for calling honest men wicked,

[009-055] or for not agreeing with what they have said of Jesus Christ.

[009-056] 597

[009-057] It is not by that which is obscure in Mahomet, and which

[009-058] may be interpreted in a mysterious sense, that I would have

[009-059] him judged, but by what is clear, as his paradise and the rest.

[009-060] In that he is ridiculous. And since what is clear is ridiculous,

[009-061] it is not right to take his obscurities for mysteries.

[009-062] It is not the same with the [[scripture|Scripture]]. I agree that there are

[009-063] in it obscurities as strange as those of Mahomet; but there are

[009-064] admirably clear passages, and the [[prophecy|prophecies]] are manifestly

[009-065] fulfilled. The cases are therefore not on a par. We must not

[009-066] confound, and put on one level things which only resemble each

[009-067] other in their [[evidence|obscurity]], and not in the clearness, which requires

[009-068] us to reverence the obscurities.

[009-069] 598

[009-070] The difference between Jesus Christ and Mahomet.—Mahomet

[009-071] was not foretold; Jesus Christ was foretold.

[009-072] Mahomet slew; Jesus Christ caused His own to be slain.

[009-073] Mahomet forbade reading; the Apostles ordered reading.

[009-074] In fact the two are so opposed, that if Mahomet took the way

[009-075] to succeed from a worldly point of view, Jesus Christ, from the

[009-076] same point of view, took the way to perish. And instead of

[009-077] concluding that, since Mahomet succeeded, Jesus Christ [[force|might]]

[009-078] well have succeeded, we ought to say that since Mahomet

[009-079] succeeded, Jesus Christ should have failed.

[009-080] 599

[009-082] no [[miracle|miracles]], he was not foretold. No man can do what Christ

[009-264] 612

[009-265] Perpetuity.—That religion has always existed on earth, which

[009-266] consists in believing that man has fallen from a state of glory

[009-267] and of communion with God into a state of sorrow, penitence,

[009-268] and estrangement from God, but that after this life we shall be

[009-269] restored by a Messiah who should have come. All things have

[009-270] passed away, and this has endured, for which all things are.

[009-271] Men have in the first age of the world been carried away into

[009-272] every kind of debauchery, and yet there were saints, as Enoch,

[009-273] Lamech, and others, who waited patiently for the Christ promised

[009-274] from the beginning of the world. [[noah|Noah]] saw the wickedness of

[009-275] men at its height; and he was held worthy to save the world in

[009-276] his person, by the hope of the Messiah of whom he was the type.

[009-277] Abraham was surrounded by idolaters, when God made known

[009-278] to him the mystery of the Messiah, whom he welcomed from

[009-279] afar.[219] In the time of Isaac and Jacob abomination was spread

[009-280] over all the earth; but these saints lived in faith; and Jacob,

[009-281] [[death|dying]] and blessing his children, cried in a transport which made

[009-282] him break off his discourse, "I await, O my God, the Saviour

[009-283] whom Thou hast promised. Salutare taum expectabo, Domine."[220]

[009-284] The Egyptians were infected both with idolatry and magic; the

[009-285] very people of God were led astray by their example. Yet Moses

[009-286] and others believed Him whom they saw not, and worshipped

[009-287] Him, looking to the eternal gifts which He was preparing for

[009-288] them.

[009-289] The Greeks and Latins then set up false deities; the poets

[009-290] made a hundred different theologies, while the philosophers

[009-291] separated into a thousand different sects; and yet in the heart of

[009-292] Judæa there were always chosen men who foretold the coming

[009-293] of this Messiah, which was known to them alone.

[009-294] He came at length in the fullness of time, and time has since

[009-295] witnessed the birth of so many schisms and heresies, so many

[009-296] political revolutions, so many changes in all things; yet this

[009-297] Church, which worships Him who has always been worshipped,

[009-298] has endured uninterruptedly. It is a wonderful, incomparable,

[009-299] and altogether divine fact that this religion, which has always

[009-300] endured, has always been attacked. It has been a thousand

[009-301] times on the eve of universal destruction, and every time it has

[009-302] been in that state, God has restored it by extraordinary acts of

[009-303] His power. This is astonishing, as also that it has preserved

[009-304] itself without yielding to the will of tyrants. For it is not

[009-305] strange that a State endures, when its laws are sometimes made

[009-306] to give way to necessity, but that ... (See the passage indicated

[009-307] in [[montaigne|Montaigne]].)

[009-308] 613

[009-309] States would perish if they did not often make their laws give

[009-310] way to necessity. But religion has never suffered this, or practised

[009-311] it. Indeed, there must be these compromises, or miracles.

[009-312] It is not strange to be saved by yieldings, and this is not strictly

[009-313] self-preservation; besides, in the end they perish entirely. None

[009-314] has endured a thousand years. But the fact that this religion

[009-315] has always maintained itself, inflexible as it is, proves its divinity.

[009-316] 614

[009-317] Whatever may be said, it must be admitted that the Christian

[009-318] religion has something astonishing in it. Some will say, "This

[009-319] is because you were born in it." Far from it; I stiffen myself

[009-320] against it for this very reason, for fear this prejudice bias me.

[009-321] But although I am born in it, I cannot help finding it so.

[009-322] 615

[009-323] Perpetuity.—The Messiah has always been believed in. The

[009-324] tradition from [[adam|Adam]] was fresh in Noah and in Moses. Since

[009-325] then the prophets have foretold him, while at the same time

[009-326] foretelling other things, which, being from time to time fulfilled

[009-327] in the sight of men, showed the truth of their mission, and

[009-328] consequently that of their promises touching the Messiah.

[009-329] Jesus Christ performed miracles, and the Apostles also, who

[009-330] converted all the heathen; and all the prophecies being thereby

[009-331] fulfilled, the Messiah is for ever proved.

[009-332] 616

[009-333] Perpetuity.—Let us consider that since the beginning of the

[009-334] world the expectation of worship of the Messiah has existed

[009-335] uninterruptedly; that there have been found men, who said that

[009-336] God had revealed to them that a Redeemer was to be born, who

[009-337] should save His people; that Abraham came afterwards, saying

[009-338] that he had had a revelation that the Messiah was to spring from

[009-339] him by a son, whom he should have; that Jacob declared that,

[009-340] of his twelve sons, the Messiah would spring from Judah; that

[009-341] Moses and the prophets then came to declare the time and the

[009-342] manner of His coming; that they said their law was only

[009-343] temporary till that of the Messiah, that it should endure till

[009-344] then, but that the other should last for ever; that thus either

[009-345] their law, or that of the Messiah, of which it was the promise,

[009-346] would be always upon the earth; that, in fact, it has always

[009-347] endured; that at last Jesus Christ came with all the circumstances

[009-348] foretold. This is wonderful.

[009-349] 617

[009-350] This is positive fact. While all philosophers separate into

[009-351] different sects, there is found in one corner of the world the

[009-352] most ancient people in it, declaring that all the world is in error,

[009-353] that God has revealed to them the truth, that they will always

[009-354] exist on the earth. In fact, all other sects come to an end, this

[009-355] one still endures, and has done so for four thousand years.

[009-356] They declare that they hold from their ancestors that man

[009-357] has fallen from communion with God, and is entirely estranged

[009-358] from God, but that He has promised to redeem them; that this

[009-359] doctrine shall always exist on the earth; that their law has a

[009-360] double signification; that during sixteen hundred years they

[009-361] have had people, whom they believed prophets, foretelling both

[009-362] the time and the manner; that four hundred years after they were

[009-363] scattered everywhere, because Jesus Christ was to be everywhere

[009-364] announced; that Jesus Christ came in the manner, and at the

[009-365] time foretold; that the Jews have since been scattered abroad

[009-366] under a curse, and nevertheless still exist.

[009-367] 618

[009-368] I see the Christian religion founded upon a preceding religion,

[009-369] and this is what I find as a fact.

[009-370] I do not here speak of the miracles of Moses, of Jesus Christ,

[009-371] and of the Apostles, because they do not at first seem convincing,

[009-372] and because I only wish here to put in evidence all

[009-373] those foundations of the Christian religion which are beyond

[009-374] [[skepticism|doubt]], and which cannot be called in question by any person

[009-375] whatsoever. It is certain that we see in many places of the

[009-376] world a peculiar people, separated from all other peoples of the

[009-377] world, and called the Jewish people.

[009-378] I see then a crowd of religions in many parts of the world

[009-379] and in all times; but their morality cannot please me, nor can

[009-380] their proofs convince me. Thus I should equally have rejected

[009-381] the religion of Mahomet and of China, of the ancient Romans

[009-382] and of the Egyptians, for the sole reason, that none having more

[009-383] marks of truth than another, nor anything which should necessarily

[009-384] persuade me, reason cannot incline to one rather than the

[009-385] other.

[009-386] But, in thus considering this changeable and singular variety

[009-387] of morals and beliefs at different times, I find in one corner of

[009-388] the world a peculiar people, separated from all other peoples

[009-389] on earth, the most ancient of all, and whose histories are earlier

[009-390] by many generations than the most ancient which we possess.

[009-391] I find, then, this great and numerous people, sprung from a

[009-392] single man, who worship one God, and guide themselves by a

[009-393] law which they say that they obtained from His own hand.

[009-394] They maintain that they are the only people in the world to

[009-395] whom God has revealed His mysteries; that all men are corrupt

[009-396] and in disgrace with God; that they are all abandoned to their

[009-397] senses and their own imagination, whence come the strange

[009-398] errors and continual changes which happen among them, both

[009-399] of religions and of morals, whereas they themselves remain

[009-400] firm in their conduct; but that God will not leave other nations

[009-401] in this darkness for ever; that there will come a Saviour for all;

[009-402] that they are in the world to announce Him to men; that they

[009-403] are expressly formed to be forerunners and heralds of this great

[009-404] event, and to summon all nations to join with them in the

[009-405] expectation of this Saviour.

[009-406] To meet with this people is astonishing to me, and seems to

[009-407] me worthy of attention. I look at the law which they boast of

[009-408] having obtained from God, and I find it admirable. It is the

[009-409] first law of all, and is of such a kind that, even before the term

[009-410] law was in currency among the Greeks, it had, for nearly a

[009-411] thousand years earlier, been uninterruptedly accepted and

[009-412] observed by the Jews. I likewise think it strange that the

[009-413] first law of the world happens to be the most perfect; so that the

[009-414] greatest legislators have borrowed their laws from it, as is

[009-415] apparent from the law of the Twelve Tables at Athens,[221] afterwards

[009-416] taken by the Romans, and as it would be easy to prove,

[009-417] if Josephus[222] and others had not sufficiently dealt with this

[009-418] subject.

[009-419] 619

[009-420] Advantages of the Jewish people.—In this search the Jewish

[009-421] people at once attracts my attention by the number of wonderful
