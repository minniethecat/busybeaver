[013-001] SECTION XIII

[013-002] THE MIRACLES

[013-003] 802

[013-004] The beginning.—Miracles enable us to judge of doctrine, and

[013-005] doctrine enables us to judge of [[miracle|miracles]].

[013-006] There are false miracles and true. There must be a distinction,

[013-007] in order to know them; otherwise they would be useless.

[013-008] Now they are not useless; on the contrary, they are fundamental.

[013-009] Now the rule which is given to us must be such, that it does not

[013-010] destroy the proof which the true miracles give of the truth,

[013-011] which is the chief end of the miracles.

[013-012] [[moses|Moses]] has given two rules: that the prediction does not come

[013-013] to pass (Deut. xviii), and that they do not lead to idolatry

[013-014] (Deut. xiii); and [[jesus-christ|Jesus Christ]][318] one.

[013-015] If doctrine regulates miracles, miracles are useless for doctrine.

[013-016] If miracles regulate....

[013-017] Objection to the rule.—The distinction of the times. One

[013-018] rule during the time of Moses, another at present.

[013-019] 803

[013-020] Miracle.—It is an effect, which exceeds the natural [[force|power]]

[013-021] of the means which are employed for it; and what is not a miracle

[013-022] is an effect, which does not exceed the natural power of the

[013-023] means which are employed for it. Thus, those who heal by

[013-024] invocation of the devil do not work a miracle; for that does not

[013-025] exceed the natural power of the devil. But ...

[013-026] 804

[013-027] The two fundamentals; one inward, the other outward; grace

[013-028] and miracles; both supernatural.

[013-029] 805

[013-030] Miracles and truth are necessary, because it is necessary to

[013-031] convince the entire man, in body and soul.

[013-032] 806

[013-033] In all times, either men have spoken of the true [[god|God]], or the

[013-034] true God has spoken to men.

[013-035] 807

[013-036] Jesus Christ has verified that He was the Messiah, never in

[013-037] verifying His doctrine by [[scripture|Scripture]] and the [[prophecy|prophecies]], but

[013-038] always by His miracles.

[013-039] He proves by a miracle that He remits sins.

[013-040] Rejoice not in your miracles, said Jesus Christ, but because

[013-041] your names are written in heaven.[319]

[013-042] If they believe not Moses, neither will they believe one risen

[013-043] from the dead.

[013-044] Nicodemus recognises by His miracles that His teaching is of

[013-045] God. Scimus quia venisti a Deo magister; nemo enim potest

[013-046] hæc signa facere quæ tu facis nisi Deus fuerit cum eo.[320] He does

[013-047] not judge of the miracles by the teaching, but of the teaching

[013-048] by the miracles.

[013-049] The Jews had a doctrine of God as we have one of Jesus Christ,

[013-050] and confirmed by miracles. They were forbidden to believe

[013-051] every worker of miracles; and they were further commanded to

[013-052] have recourse to the chief priests, and to rely on them.

[013-053] And thus, in regard to their prophets, they had all those

[013-054] reasons which we have for refusing to believe the workers of

[013-055] miracles.

[013-056] And yet they were very sinful in rejecting the prophets, and

[013-057] Jesus Christ, because of their miracles; and they would not have

[013-058] been culpable, if they had not seen the miracles. Nisi fecissem

[013-059] ... [[sin|peccatum]] non haberent.[321] Therefore all [[faith|belief]] rests upon

[013-060] miracles.

[013-061] Prophecy is not called miracle; as [[saint-john|Saint John]] speaks of the

[013-062] first miracle in Cana, and then of what Jesus Christ says to the

[013-063] woman of Samaria, when He reveals to her all her hidden life.

[013-064] Then He heals the centurion's son; and Saint John calls this

[013-065] "the second miracle."[322]

[013-070] suppose the second.

[013-082] However it may be, the Church is without proofs if they are

[013-083] right.

[013-085] Montaigne against miracles.

[013-086] Montaigne for miracles.

[013-087] 814

[013-088] It is not possible to have a reasonable belief against miracles.

[013-089] 815

[013-090] Unbelievers the most credulous. They believe the miracles

[013-091] of Vespasian, in order not to believe those of Moses.

[013-092] 816

[013-093] Title: How it happens that men believe so many liars, who

[013-094] say that they have seen miracles, and do not believe any of those

[013-095] who say that they have secrets to make men immortal, or restore

[013-096] youth to them.—Having considered how it happens that so great

[013-097] credence is given to so many impostors, who say they have

[013-098] remedies, often to the length of men putting their lives into

[013-099] their hands, it has appeared to me that the true cause is that

[013-100] there are true remedies. For it would not be possible that

[013-101] there should be so many false remedies, and that so much faith

[013-102] should be placed in them, if there were none true. If there

[013-103] had never been any remedy for any ill, and all ills had been

[013-104] incurable, it is impossible that men should have imagined that

[013-105] they could give remedies, and still more impossible that so many

[013-106] others should have believed those who boasted of having

[013-107] remedies; in the same way as did a man boast of preventing

[013-108] death, no one would believe him, because there is no example of

[013-109] this. But as there were a number of remedies found to be true

[013-110] by the very knowledge of the greatest men, the belief of men is

[013-111] thereby induced; and, this being known to be possible, it has

[013-112] been therefore concluded that it was. For people commonly

[013-113] reason thus: "A thing is possible, therefore it is"; because the

[013-114] thing cannot be denied generally, since there are particular

[013-115] effects which are true, the people, who cannot distinguish which

[013-116] among these particular effects are true, believe them all. In

[013-117] the same way, the reason why so many false effects are credited

[013-118] to the moon, is that there are some true, as the tide.

[013-119] It is the same with prophecies, miracles, divination by dreams,

[013-120] sorceries, etc. For if there had been nothing true in all this,

[013-121] men would have believed nothing of them; and thus, instead

[013-122] of concluding that there are no true miracles because there are

[013-123] so many false, we must, on the contrary, say that there certainly

[013-124] are true miracles, since there are false, and that there are false

[013-125] miracles only because some are true. We must reason in the

[013-126] same way about religion; for it would not be possible that men

[013-127] should have imagined so many false religions, if there had not

[013-128] been a true one. The objection to this is that savages have a

[013-129] religion; but the answer is that they have heard the true spoken

[013-130] of, as appears by the deluge, circumcision, the cross of Saint

[013-131] Andrew, etc.

[013-132] 817

[013-133] Having considered how it comes that there are so many false

[013-134] miracles, false revelations, sorceries, etc., it has seemed to me

[013-135] that the true cause is that there are some true; for it would not

[013-136] be possible that there should be so many false miracles, if there

[013-137] were none true, nor so many false revelations, if there were

[013-138] none true, nor so many false religions, if there were not one

[013-139] true. For if there had never been all this, it is almost impossible

[013-140] that men should have imagined it, and still more impossible

[013-141] that so many others should have believed it. But as there

[013-142] have been very great things true, and as they have been believed

[013-143] by great men, this impression has been the cause that nearly

[013-144] everybody is rendered capable of believing also the false. And

[013-145] thus, instead of concluding that there are no true miracles,

[013-146] since there are so many false, it must be said, on the contrary,

[013-147] that there are true miracles, since there are so many false; and

[013-148] that there are false ones only because there are true; and that

[013-149] in the same way there are false religions because there is one

[013-150] true.—Objection to this: savages have a religion. But this is

[013-151] because they have heard the true spoken of, as appears by the

[013-152] cross of Saint Andrew, the deluge, circumcision, etc.—This

[013-153] arises from the fact that the human [[spirit|mind]], finding itself inclined

[013-154] to that side by the truth, becomes thereby susceptible of all the

[013-155] falsehoods of this ...

[013-156] 818

[013-157] [[jeremiah|Jeremiah]] xxiii, 32. The miracles of the false prophets. In

[013-158] the Hebrew and Vatable[324] they are the tricks.

[013-159] Miracle does not always signify miracle. I Sam. xiv, 15;

[013-160] miracle signifies fear, and is so in the Hebrew. The same

[013-161] evidently in [[job|Job]] xxxiii, 7; and also [[isaiah|Isaiah]] xxi, 4; Jeremiah xliv,

[013-162] 12. Portentum signifies simulacrum, Jeremiah l, 38; and it is

[013-163] so in the Hebrew and Vatable. Isaiah viii, 18. Jesus Christ

[013-164] says that He and His will be in miracles.

[013-165] 819

[013-166] If the devil favoured the doctrine which destroys him, he

[013-167] would be divided against himself, as Jesus Christ said. If

[013-168] God favoured the doctrine which destroys the Church, He

[013-169] would be divided against Himself. Omne regnum divisum.[325]

[013-170] For Jesus Christ wrought against the devil, and destroyed his

[013-171] power over the heart, of which exorcism is the symbolisation,

[013-172] in order to establish the kingdom of God. And thus He adds,

[013-173] Si in digito Dei ... regnum Dei ad vos.[326]

[013-174] 820

[013-175] There is a great difference between tempting and leading

[013-176] into error. God tempts, but He does not lead into error. To

[013-177] tempt is to afford opportunities, which impose no necessity;

[013-178] if men do not love God, they will do a certain thing. To lead

[013-179] into error is to place a man under the necessity of inferring and

[013-180] following out what is untrue.

[013-181] 821

[013-182] [[abraham|Abraham]] and Gideon are above revelation. The Jews

[013-183] blinded themselves in judging of miracles by the Scripture.

[013-184] God has never abandoned His true worshippers.

[013-185] I prefer to follow Jesus Christ than any other, because He

[013-186] has miracle, prophecy, doctrine, perpetuity, etc.

[013-187] The Donatists. No miracle which obliges them to say it is the

[013-188] devil.

[013-189] The more we particularise God, Jesus Christ, the Church ...

[013-190] 822

[013-191] If there were no false miracles, there would be certainty. If

[013-192] there were no rule to judge of them, miracles would be useless,

[013-193] and there would be no reason for believing.

[013-194] Now there is, humanly speaking, no human certainty, but

[013-195] we have reason.

[013-196] 823

[013-197] Either God has confounded the false miracles, or He has

[013-198] foretold them; and in both ways He has raised Himself above

[013-199] what is supernatural with respect to us, and has raised us to it.

[013-204] Reasons why we do not believe.

[013-206] in eum, ut sermo Isayæ impleretur. Excæcavit, etc.

[013-207] Hæc dixit Isaias, quando vidit gloriam ejus et locutus est de eo.

[013-208] Judæi signa petunt et Græci sapientiam quærunt, nos autem

[013-210] vos autem Christum non crucifixum et religionem sine miraculis

[013-211] et sine sapientia.[328]

[013-212] What makes us not believe in the true miracles, is want of

[013-213] love. John: Sed vos non creditis, quia non estis ex ovibus.[329]

[013-214] What makes us believe the false is want of love. II Thess. ii.

[013-215] The foundation of religion. It is the miracles. What then?

[013-216] Does God speak against miracles, against the foundations of

[013-217] the faith which we have in Him?

[013-218] If there is a God, faith in God must exist on earth. Now the

[013-219] miracles of Jesus Christ are not foretold by Antichrist, but the

[013-220] miracles of Antichrist are foretold by Jesus Christ. And so if

[013-221] Jesus Christ were not the Messiah, He would have indeed led

[013-222] into error. When Jesus Christ foretold the miracles of Antichrist,

[013-223] did He think of destroying faith in His own miracles?

[013-224] Moses foretold Jesus Christ, and bade to follow Him. Jesus

[013-225] Christ foretold Antichrist, and forbade to follow him.

[013-226] It was impossible that in the time of Moses men should keep

[013-227] their faith for Antichrist, who was unknown to them. But it

[013-228] is quite easy, in the time of Antichrist, to believe in Jesus

[013-229] Christ, already known.

[013-230] There is no reason for believing in Antichrist, which there

[013-231] is not for believing in Jesus Christ. But there are reasons for

[013-232] believing in Jesus Christ, which there are not for believing in

[013-233] the other.

[013-247] 827

[013-248] Opposition.—Abel, Cain; Moses, the Magicians; Elijah, the

[013-249] false prophets: Jeremiah, Hananiah; Micaiah, the false prophets;

[013-253] 828

[013-254] Jesus Christ says that the Scriptures testify of Him. But

[013-255] He does not point out in what respect.

[013-256] Even the prophecies could not prove Jesus Christ during

[013-257] His life; and so, men would not have been culpable for not

[013-258] believing in Him before His death, had the miracles not sufficed

[013-259] without doctrine. Now those who did not believe in Him,

[013-260] when He was still alive, were sinners, as He said Himself, and

[013-261] without excuse. Therefore they must have had proof beyond

[013-262] doubt, which they resisted. Now, they had not the prophecies,

[013-263] but only the miracles. Therefore the latter suffice, when the

[013-264] doctrine is not inconsistent with them; and they ought to be

[013-265] believed.

[013-266] John vii, 40. Dispute among the Jews as among the Christians

[013-267] of to-day. Some believed in Jesus Christ; others believed Him

[013-268] not, because of the prophecies which said that He should be born

[013-269] in Bethlehem. They should have considered more carefully

[013-270] whether He was not. For His miracles being convincing, they

[013-271] should have been quite sure of these supposed [[contradiction|contradictions]]

[013-272] of His teaching to Scripture; and this [[evidence|obscurity]] did not excuse,

[013-273] but blinded them. Thus those who refuse to believe in the

[013-274] miracles in the present day on account of a supposed contradiction,

[013-275] which is unreal, are not excused.

[013-276] The Pharisees said to the people, who believed in Him,

[013-277] because of His miracles: "This people who knoweth not the

[013-278] law are cursed. But have any of the rulers or of the Pharisees

[013-279] believed on him? For we know that out of Galilee ariseth no

[013-280] prophet." Nicodemus answered: "Doth our law judge any

[013-281] man before it hear him, [and specially, such a man who works

[013-282] such miracles]?"

[013-283] 829

[013-284] The prophecies were ambiguous; they are no longer so.

[013-285] 830

[013-286] The five propositions were ambiguous; they are no longer so.

[013-287] 831

[013-288] Miracles are no longer necessary, because we have had them

[013-289] already. But when tradition is no longer minded; when the

[013-290] Pope alone is offered to us; when he has been imposed upon;

[013-291] and when the true source of truth, which is tradition, is thus

[013-292] excluded; and the Pope, who is its guardian, is biased; the

[013-293] truth is no longer free to appear. Then, as men speak no longer

[013-294] of truth, truth itself must speak to men. This is what happened

[013-295] in the time of Arius. (Miracles under Diocletian and under

[013-296] Arius.)

[013-297] 832

[013-301] must be strict, and opposed to exception. But yet, as it is

[013-306] Those who follow Jesus Christ because of His miracles honour

[013-307] His power in all the miracles which it produces. But those

[013-308] who, making profession to follow Him because of His miracles,

[013-309] follow Him in fact only because He comforts them and satisfies

[013-310] them with worldly blessings, discredit His miracles, when they

[013-311] are opposed to their own comforts.

[013-312] John ix: Non est hic homo a Deo, quia sabbatum non custodit.

[013-313] Alii: Quomodo potest homo peccator hæc signa facere?

[013-314] Which is the most clear?

[013-315] This house is not of God; for they do not there believe that

[013-316] the five propositions are in Jansenius. Others: This house is of

[013-317] God; for in it there are wrought strange miracles.

[013-318] Which is the most clear?

[013-319] Tu quid dicis? Dico quia propheta est. Nisi esset hic a Deo,

[013-321] 834

[013-322] In the [[old-testament|Old Testament]], when they will turn you from God.

[013-323] In the New, when they will turn you from Jesus Christ. These

[013-324] are the occasions for excluding particular miracles from belief.

[013-325] No others need be excluded.

[013-326] Does it therefore follow that they would have the right to

[013-327] exclude all the prophets who came to them? No; they would

[013-328] have sinned in not excluding those who denied God, and would

[013-333] 835

[013-334] There is a great difference between not being for Jesus Christ

[013-335] and saying so, and not being for Jesus Christ and pretending

[013-336] to be so. The one party can do miracles, not the others. For

[013-337] it is clear of the one party, that they are opposed to the truth,

[013-339] 836

[013-340] That we must love one God only is a thing so evident, that it

[013-341] does not require miracles to prove it.

[013-342] 837

[013-343] Jesus Christ performed miracles, then the apostles, and the

[013-344] first saints in great number; because the prophecies not being

[013-345] yet accomplished, but in the process of being accomplished by

[013-346] them, the miracles alone bore witness to them. It was foretold

[013-347] that the Messiah should convert the nations. How could this

[013-348] prophecy be fulfilled without the conversion of the nations?

[013-349] And how could the nations be converted to the Messiah, if they

[013-350] did not see this final effect of the prophecies which prove Him?

[013-351] Therefore, till He had died, risen again, and converted the

[013-352] nations, all was not accomplished; and so miracles were needed

[013-353] during all this time. Now they are no longer needed against

[013-354] the Jews; for the accomplished prophecies constitute a lasting

[013-355] miracle.

[013-356] 838

[013-357] "Though ye believe not Me, believe at least the works."[331] He

[013-358] refers them, as it were, to the strongest proof.

[013-359] It had been told to the Jews, as well as to Christians, that they

[013-360] should not always believe the prophets; but yet the Pharisees

[013-361] and Scribes are greatly concerned about His miracles, and try

[013-362] to show that they are false, or wrought by the devil. For they

[013-363] must needs be convinced, if they acknowledge that they are

[013-364] of God.

[013-365] At the present day we are not troubled to make this distinction.

[013-366] Still it is very easy to do: those who deny neither

[013-367] God nor Jesus Christ do no miracles which are not certain.

[013-368] Nemo facit virtutem in nomine meo, et cito possit de me male

[013-369] loqui.[332]

[013-370] But we have not to draw this distinction. Here is a sacred

[013-371] relic.[333] Here is a thorn from the crown of the Saviour of the

[013-372] world, over whom the prince of this world has no power, which

[013-373] works miracles by the peculiar power of the blood shed for us.

[013-374] Now God Himself chooses this house in order to display conspiciously

[013-375] therein His power.

[013-376] These are not men who do miracles by an unknown and

[013-377] doubtful virtue, which makes a decision difficult for us. It is

[013-378] God Himself. It is the instrument of the Passion of His only

[013-379] Son, who, being in many places, chooses this, and makes men

[013-380] come from all quarters there to receive these miraculous

[013-381] alleviations in their weaknesses.

[013-382] 839
