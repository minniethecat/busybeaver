# French Parlement

The French Parlements, the sovereign courts of the ancien régime that Rousseau criticized as usurping legislative authority under the guise of judicial review — an example of the confusion of sovereignty with government

## Historical Context

French Parlement was a key institution of early modern Europe, operating within the political context that Rousseau both drew upon and criticized.

## Role in Rousseau's Theory

Rousseau references French Parlement in his analysis of political institutions, using it as an example of the institutional forms that government — as distinct from sovereignty — can take. The institution illustrates his broader argument that the forms of political organization must be suited to the character and size of the State.
