# France

France, the primary political context of Rousseau's critique — the archetype of a corrupt modern state where inequality and absolutism have destroyed legitimate political order

## Historical Significance

Rousseau wrote The Social Contract in and against the context of the French ancien régime. France exemplifies for Rousseau everything wrong with modern politics: absolute monarchy, extreme inequality, the replacement of citizenship with subjecthood, and the corruption of the general will by particular interests.

## Rousseau's Treatment

### As Political Warning

Rousseau uses France as a counterexample in his political theory — a demonstration of what happens when the social compact is corrupted by inequality, absolutism, and the replacement of the general will by particular interests.

### Key References

Rousseau's references to France appear throughout The Social Contract and his other political writings, serving as concrete illustrations of his abstract principles of political right.
