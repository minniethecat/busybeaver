# Roman Senate

The Roman Senate, the aristocratic council of the Roman Republic that Rousseau discusses as an example of government — an intermediate body between the sovereign people and the magistrates

## Historical Context

Roman Senate was a key institution of the classical republican world that Rousseau studied as a source of political wisdom.

## Role in Rousseau's Theory

Rousseau references Roman Senate in his analysis of political institutions, using it as an example of the institutional forms that government — as distinct from sovereignty — can take. The institution illustrates his broader argument that the forms of political organization must be suited to the character and size of the State.
