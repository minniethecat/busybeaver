# Founding of Rome

The legendary founding of Rome, which Rousseau discusses as an example of how a new political community is constituted — the act by which a multitude becomes a people with a common identity and purpose

## Historical Account

Founding of Rome represents a pivotal moment in the political history of the classical world — one of the events that shaped the republican tradition Rousseau draws upon throughout The Social Contract.

## Rousseau's Use

### As Political Exemplar

Rousseau treats Founding of Rome as an exemplar — a historical instance that reveals universal truths about political founding, legislation, and the conditions under which legitimate political order can be established.

### Theoretical Significance

By analyzing Founding of Rome, Rousseau develops his broader argument that political institutions are not arbitrary but must conform to principles of political right — principles that can be discovered through the study of both successful and failed political communities.
