# Comitia

The Roman Comitia, the popular assemblies of the Roman Republic that Rousseau analyzes as an exemplar of how a sovereign people can exercise its legislative power through institutional forms

## Historical Context

Comitia was a key institution of the classical republican world that Rousseau studied as a source of political wisdom.

## Role in Rousseau's Theory

Rousseau references Comitia in his analysis of political institutions, using it as an illustration of how sovereign legislative authority can be institutionally organized. The institution illustrates his broader argument that the forms of political organization must be suited to the character and size of the State.
