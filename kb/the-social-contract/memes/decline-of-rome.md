# Decline of Rome

The decline of the Roman Republic, which Rousseau analyzes as the paradigmatic case of governmental usurpation of sovereignty — the process by which the general will is replaced by particular interests

## Historical Account

Decline of Rome represents a pivotal moment in the political history of the classical world — one of the events that shaped the republican tradition Rousseau draws upon throughout The Social Contract.

## Rousseau's Use

### As Political Warning

Rousseau treats Decline of Rome as a cautionary example — a historical case that illustrates the structural tendencies that threaten every political community: the usurpation of sovereignty by government, the replacement of the general will by particular interests, and the dissolution of the social compact.

### Theoretical Significance

By analyzing Decline of Rome, Rousseau develops his broader argument that political institutions are not arbitrary but must conform to principles of political right — principles that can be discovered through the study of both successful and failed political communities.
