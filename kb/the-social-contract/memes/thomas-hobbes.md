# Thomas Hobbes

Thomas Hobbes (1588–1679), English philosopher whose Leviathan (1651) founded modern social contract theory — Rousseau's primary foil in developing his own distinct doctrine of the compact

## Life

### Early Years and Background

Thomas Hobbes was born in 1588, into a period of profound political and intellectual transformation in Europe. Their early education and intellectual formation laid the groundwork for their later contributions to political philosophy.

### Major Works and Career

Thomas Hobbes's major writings established them as one of the foundational thinkers of the social contract tradition. Their work addressed the fundamental questions of political philosophy: the origin of legitimate authority, the nature of law, and the basis of political obligation.

## Thought and Influence

Thomas Hobbes's political thought centers on the relationship between individual liberty and political authority. Their work developed a distinctive account of how legitimate political institutions can be constructed — an account that Rousseau both draws upon and transforms in The Social Contract.

## Relationship to The Social Contract

### Rousseau's Engagement

Rousseau engages Hobbes as his main antagonist. Against Hobbes's claim that the state of nature is a war of all against all, Rousseau argues that war is a social, not natural, phenomenon (006-009). Against Hobbes's identification of the Sovereign with the government, Rousseau insists on their radical separation — the Sovereign is the people, the government merely its agent (P01-036).

### Points of Divergence

Where Rousseau departs from Thomas Hobbes, he does so not by rejecting the social contract framework but by radicalizing it — pushing the logic of consent, generality, and popular sovereignty further than his predecessor was willing to go.

## Key Passages

> Rousseau's engagement with Thomas Hobbes appears throughout The Social Contract, most notably in his discussion of the foundations of political right and the nature of legitimate sovereignty.
