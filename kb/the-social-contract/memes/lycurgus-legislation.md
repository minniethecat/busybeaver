# Legislation of Lycurgus

The legislation of Lycurgus for Sparta — the archetypal act of the great Legislator in Rousseau's political theory, who transformed a people by giving them institutions that shaped their character

## Historical Account

Legislation of Lycurgus represents a pivotal moment in the political history of the classical world — one of the events that shaped the republican tradition Rousseau draws upon throughout The Social Contract.

## Rousseau's Use

### As Political Exemplar

Rousseau treats Legislation of Lycurgus as an exemplar — a historical instance that reveals universal truths about political founding, legislation, and the conditions under which legitimate political order can be established.

### Theoretical Significance

By analyzing Legislation of Lycurgus, Rousseau develops his broader argument that political institutions are not arbitrary but must conform to principles of political right — principles that can be discovered through the study of both successful and failed political communities.
