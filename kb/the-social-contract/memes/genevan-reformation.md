# Genevan Reformation

The establishment of the Reformed Republic of Geneva in the 16th century, which created the Calvinist city-state that Rousseau would later cite as a model of popular sovereignty

## Historical Account

Genevan Reformation represents a pivotal moment in the political development of early modern Europe — part of the historical context against which Rousseau developed his critique of existing political institutions.

## Rousseau's Use

### As Political Exemplar

Rousseau treats Genevan Reformation as an exemplar — a historical instance that reveals universal truths about political founding, legislation, and the conditions under which legitimate political order can be established.

### Theoretical Significance

By analyzing Genevan Reformation, Rousseau develops his broader argument that political institutions are not arbitrary but must conform to principles of political right — principles that can be discovered through the study of both successful and failed political communities.
