# Rome

Rome, the exemplar par excellence in Rousseau's political theory — both as the model of a well-constituted republic and as a cautionary tale of imperial decline

## Historical Significance

Rousseau draws extensively on Roman history throughout The Social Contract. The Roman Republic exemplifies the principles of popular sovereignty, the Comitia as assemblies of the people, and the tribunate as a preservative institution. Rome's decline illustrates the tendency of government to usurp sovereignty.

## Rousseau's Treatment

### As Political Model

Rousseau uses Rome as a exemplar in his political theory — an illustration of how well-constituted political institutions can sustain civic virtue and the general will.

### Key References

Rousseau's references to Rome appear throughout The Social Contract and his other political writings, serving as concrete illustrations of his abstract principles of political right.
