# Sparta

Sparta, the ancient Greek city-state Rousseau admires as the achievement of a great legislator — Lycurgus — who gave it laws and institutions that shaped its citizens' character

## Historical Significance

Rousseau repeatedly invokes Sparta as the achievement of the Legislator's art. Lycurgus 'gave iron and Sparta to his fellow-citizens' — not material wealth but civic virtue. Sparta represents the possibility of a polity organized around the common good rather than private interest.

## Rousseau's Treatment

### As Political Model

Rousseau uses Sparta as a exemplar in his political theory — an illustration of how well-constituted political institutions can sustain civic virtue and the general will.

### Key References

Rousseau's references to Sparta appear throughout The Social Contract and his other political writings, serving as concrete illustrations of his abstract principles of political right.
