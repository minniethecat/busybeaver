# Geneva

Geneva, Rousseau's birthplace and the model republic whose institutions shaped his political thought. The Social Contract is dedicated 'To the Republic of Geneva.'

## Historical Significance

Geneva was a Calvinist city-state governed by a General Council of citizens — a living example of popular sovereignty that deeply influenced Rousseau's political imagination. He always signed his works 'Citizen of Geneva.'

## Rousseau's Treatment

### As Political Model

Rousseau uses Geneva as a exemplar in his political theory — an illustration of how well-constituted political institutions can sustain civic virtue and the general will.

### Key References

Rousseau's references to Geneva appear throughout The Social Contract and his other political writings, serving as concrete illustrations of his abstract principles of political right.
