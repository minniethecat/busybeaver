# Genevan General Council

The General Council of the Republic of Geneva, the assembly of all citizens that exercised sovereign legislative power — the living model of popular sovereignty that shaped Rousseau's political thought

## Historical Context

Genevan General Council was a key institution of early modern Europe, operating within the political context that Rousseau both drew upon and criticized.

## Role in Rousseau's Theory

Rousseau references Genevan General Council in his analysis of political institutions, using it as an illustration of how sovereign legislative authority can be institutionally organized. The institution illustrates his broader argument that the forms of political organization must be suited to the character and size of the State.
