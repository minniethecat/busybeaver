## Introduction

St. Pancras — specifically the clock of St. Pancras — is a recurring London landmark in _Deadlock_, appearing across at least four chapters. Its striking clock marks significant moments of consciousness and transition for Miriam Henderson.

## Appearances

### Chapter I

Outside in the darkness, St. Pancras clock strikes, marking the passage of time as Miriam reflects on her evening (001-097).

### Chapter III

The route between Wimpole Street and St. Pancras becomes a geographical anchor for Miriam's imaginative life, "holding in imagination worlds" between these two poles (003-002).

### Chapter V

St. Pancras clock strikes nine as Miriam pours herself into the day's activities (005-004).

### Chapter VII

The clock strikes midnight — a moment of culmination and threshold — as Miriam returns from the eventful evening of McHibbert's lecture and her meeting with Lucie Duclaux (007-041).

## Significance

St. Pancras clock functions as more than a geographical marker. Its regular striking punctuates Miriam's consciousness, marking thresholds between day and night, solitude and society, reflection and action. As a London landmark, it anchors the novel's geography in the area between Wimpole Street and the railway terminus.
