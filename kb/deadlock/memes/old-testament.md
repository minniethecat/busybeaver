# Old Testament

The Old Testament appears as a cultural and religious reference point in *Deadlock*, invoked in discussions that touch on faith, identity, and the relationship between Jewish and Christian traditions.

## In the Novel

The Old Testament is mentioned in the context of conversations about religious identity and heritage. As the shared scripture of Judaism and Christianity, it serves as a common reference point even as characters navigate the differences between religious traditions. Its mention connects to the novel's broader treatment of religion — not as a doctrinal system but as a cultural inheritance that shapes identity and conversation.

## Context

In the early twentieth-century intellectual milieu of the novel, biblical references were part of the common cultural vocabulary, even among characters who had moved away from religious observance. The Old Testament in particular carried associations with Jewish identity that are relevant to characters like Mr. Shatov, as well as with the broader literary and moral education of the period.
