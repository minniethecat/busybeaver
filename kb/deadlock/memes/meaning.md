# Meaning

The question of **meaning** — how words carry significance, how experience yields understanding, and whether meaning is inherent or constructed — runs throughout [[deadlock|Deadlock]].

Richardson's stream-of-consciousness technique does not merely describe this question; it enacts the process by which meaning accumulates, disperses, and sometimes refuses to cohere across successive moments of perception.

## Word and Sense

[[miriam-henderson|Miriam]] reflects on the relationship between words and their meanings with a precision that is at once linguistic and philosophical. Learning [[german|German]], she discovers that one can absorb the sound of a [[language]] without attending to its semantic content:

> You need not attend to the meaning. [001-060]

This separation of sound from sense suggests a phenomenological approach to language, where the materiality of words — their rhythm, texture, weight — precedes and partly exceeds the propositions they carry. Meaning, in this view, is not the only thing language does.

## Precision and Difference

Miriam's attention to linguistic precision reflects a broader concern with the accuracy of perception. She observes that superficially similar phrases carry different implications:

> Those two phrases have different meanings. The first indefinite because of its grammatical construction. [001-064]

The recognition that grammar itself shapes meaning — that the architecture of a sentence determines what it can say — is characteristic of the novel's alertness to form. How something is said is inseparable from what is said.

## The Resistance to Connected Meaning

The novel's most radical treatment of meaning may be its willingness to let experience resist coherence:

> Moving on, without connected meaning. The strange thing that had happened was simply there. [001-144]

This passage — describing a moment when the flow of perception refuses to resolve into a connected narrative — captures the novel's commitment to the texture of experience as it is lived, before the mind imposes pattern. Meaning, in [[deadlock|Deadlock]], is not always connected. Sometimes it is simply present: a strangeness that resists interpretation and asks only to be registered.
