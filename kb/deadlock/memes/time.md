## Overview

Time in _Deadlock_ operates not as a chronological sequence of events but as a quality of consciousness — stretched, compressed, and layered through [[memory]] and immediate perception. Richardson's stream-of-consciousness method renders time as lived duration rather than measured interval.

## In the Novel

The opening pages establish time's peculiar texture in the novel's world. The boardinghouse routines create a rhythm of association that temporal measurement cannot capture:

> "… by boarders to sustain their times of association with Mrs. Bai…" [001-003]

Moments of shared stillness acquire a temporal weight independent of clock time:

> "… They had sat like that many times before. They were grouped to…" [001-010]

The Latin phrase _sic transit gloria mundi_ — "thus passes the glory of the world" — is invoked and dismissed, the novel refusing the consolations of conventional temporal wisdom:

> "…y, not to the dreary-weary sentiment. Sic transit. Almost everyo…" [001-016]

## Analysis

Richardson's treatment of time aligns with the modernist rejection of linear narrative. Consciousness does not unfold in a straight line — it doubles back, leaps forward, and suspends itself in moments of pure sensation. The novel's form embodies this temporal philosophy, replacing plot with the accumulation of perceptual instants.
