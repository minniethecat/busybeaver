## Introduction

Dr. Veslovski is a Polish medical student introduced in Chapter I of _Deadlock_. He is a friend and chess partner of [[mr-shatov|Mr. Shatov]], who brings him to Tansley Street.

## First Appearance

Dr. Veslovski is introduced by Mr. Shatov with formal deference: "I present you Dr. Veslovski" (001-164). He is described as graceful, with the bearing of a Polish doctor, and is immediately associated with chess — Shatov mentions having sat up all night playing chess with him (001-164).

## Character and Role

Veslovski represents the cosmopolitan, intellectual circle that Shatov inhabits outside of Tansley Street. His Polish nationality connects to the network of Eastern European émigrés in London. As a medical student and chess player, he embodies the rational, scientific temperament that contrasts with Miriam's more intuitive approach to knowledge.

## Relationship with Shatov

Veslovski appears as Shatov's chess companion, suggesting a shared intellectual life that Miriam glimpses only partially. The all-night chess games indicate a friendship of serious engagement.
