## Introduction

Lucie Duclaux is a Frenchwoman introduced in Chapter VII of _Deadlock_. She meets Miriam Henderson at Dr. McHibbert's lecture at the Furthermore Settlement and quickly becomes an important presence — intelligent, warm, and intellectually engaged.

## First Meeting

Miriam first notices Lucie Duclaux as her seatmate at the lecture. Lucie is described with striking physical detail — her bright eyes beneath "richly marked eyebrows," her "mobile, expressive mouth," and her direct, unselfconscious manner (007-022). She introduces herself with the simple declaration: "My name is Lucie Duclaux" (007-036).

## Character

Lucie is characterized by her intellectual liveliness and emotional warmth. She recommends Haldane's _Pathway to Reality_ to Miriam (007-032), signaling her engagement with contemporary philosophy. Her French background sets her apart from the English characters, and her "Lucie Duclaux cloak" is noted as part of her distinctive presence (007-037).

## Relationship with Miriam

Lucie's immediate connection with Miriam contrasts with the more formal and guarded relationships Miriam typically has with women. Lucie speaks directly and warmly, offering friendship and intellectual companionship without the social constraints Miriam experiences with the Baileys and others at Tansley Street.
