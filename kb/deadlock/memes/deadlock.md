## Overview

*Deadlock* was published in 1921 as the fifth volume of Dorothy M. Richardson's thirteen-volume novel sequence *Pilgrimage*. The novel continues the story of Miriam Henderson, now working as a dental secretary at Wimpole Street in London, living in a boardinghouse at Tansley Street. The novel's title names both the narrative's central metaphor and the condition of impasse that structures Miriam's experience — caught between English restraint and Russian expansiveness, between work and intellectual aspiration, between solitude and the claims of others.

The novel is set in a London boardinghouse world populated by a cast of recurring characters including Mr. Shatov, a Russian Jewish émigré who becomes Miriam's intellectual companion; the Orlys, her employers at the dental practice; and the various boarders at Tansley Street. *Deadlock* is notable for Richardson's development of stream-of-consciousness narrative, rendering Miriam's inner experience with radical immediacy.

## Structure

*Deadlock* consists of thirteen chapters. The narrative moves through Miriam's daily life at the dental practice, her philosophical conversations with Mr. Shatov, the tensions and affections of boardinghouse life, and her growing awareness of the limitations placed on women's lives. Unlike conventional novels, the plot is driven not by external events but by the movement of Miriam's consciousness — her perceptions, memories, and shifting judgments of the people and ideas she encounters.
