# Elgin Marbles

The Elgin Marbles — a collection of classical Greek marble sculptures from the Parthenon, housed in the British Museum — are referenced in *Deadlock* as a cultural landmark and an object of aesthetic contemplation.

## In the Novel

The Elgin Marbles appear in the context of London's cultural landscape as a point of reference for aesthetic experience. As one of the British Museum's most famous holdings, they represent the encounter with classical art that was part of the educated Londoner's cultural repertoire. Their mention situates the novel's characters within a world where museum-going and aesthetic discussion are part of ordinary intellectual life.

## Context

In the early twentieth century, the Elgin Marbles were already a subject of debate — their acquisition by Lord Elgin in the early nineteenth century was controversial, and arguments about their repatriation had begun. For the characters in *Deadlock*, however, they function primarily as an aesthetic touchstone, part of the fabric of London's cultural institutions alongside the [[british-museum]] itself.
