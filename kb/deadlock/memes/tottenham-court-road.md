# Tottenham Court Road

Tottenham Court Road is a major London thoroughfare running north from Oxford Street toward Camden, referenced in *Deadlock* as part of the city's street geography.

## In the Novel

Tottenham Court Road appears as a geographical marker in the London landscape through which characters move. Connecting the Bloomsbury district — with its intellectual and literary associations — to the commercial West End, the street sits at the intersection of several of the novel's key settings. Its mention anchors the narrative in the specific, walkable geography of central London.

## Context

In the early twentieth century, Tottenham Court Road was known for its furniture and electronics shops, a more workaday street than the fashionable West End thoroughfares nearby. Its appearance in the novel contributes to the textured, non-monumental London that Richardson's characters inhabit — a city of ordinary streets and daily movement rather than landmarks.
