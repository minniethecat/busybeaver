# Oxford Street

Oxford Street is one of London's principal commercial thoroughfares, referenced in *Deadlock* as part of the urban geography through which the characters move.

## In the Novel

Oxford Street appears in passing as a point of reference in the London landscape. As a major east-west artery connecting Marble Arch to Tottenham Court Road, it anchors the novel's geography in the recognizable topography of central London. Like [[euston-road]] and [[wimpole-street]], it is one of the streets that ground the narrative in a specific urban setting.

## Context

In the early twentieth-century setting of the novel, Oxford Street was already established as London's premier shopping destination, lined with department stores. Its appearance in the novel, however brief, situates the characters within a modern, commercial London — a city of movement, consumption, and social display.
