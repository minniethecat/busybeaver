# England

England (Britain) was the center of the empirical-sensationalist tradition in 18th-century aesthetics that Croce treats as an important but ultimately inadequate alternative to the idealist tradition.

## In Croce's System

Croce discusses the British tradition in his historical survey: "The English and French aestheticians" (P02-191) and "Eclecticism and sensationalism" (P02-217). **Edmund Burke**'s *Philosophical Enquiry*, **David Hume**'s "Of the Standard of Taste," and **Henry Home** (Lord Kames)'s *Elements of Criticism* represent the attempt to ground aesthetics in empirical psychology rather than metaphysics.

Croce's critique is that this tradition conflates the aesthetic (theoretical) with the psychological (empirical): beauty is reduced to the feeling of pleasure, and the sublime to the feeling of terror. The lasting contribution of the British tradition is its attention to the actual experience of art; its limitation is its inability to provide a philosophical account of aesthetic autonomy.
