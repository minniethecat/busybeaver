# Greece

Ancient Greece (*Hellas*) is the origin point of Western aesthetic thought. From **Plato**'s theory of beauty as a Form to **Aristotle**'s *Poetics*, Greek philosophy established the foundational concepts and problems that Croce's *History of Æsthetic* traces through two millennia of development.

## In Croce's System

Croce discusses "Græco-Roman antiquity" (ch19-graeco-roman-antiquity) as the first phase of aesthetic thought. Plato's treatment of beauty in the *Symposium* and *Phaedrus* — beauty as the ladder to the Forms — and Aristotle's analysis of tragedy in the *Poetics* — *katharsis*, *mimesis*, plot and character — provided the vocabulary for all subsequent aesthetic theory.

Croce's own system breaks decisively with the Greek heritage. Where Plato subordinates art to philosophy (art as imitation of imitation) and Aristotle treats art as craft (*technē*), Croce asserts the complete autonomy of intuition-expression. The Greek legacy is thus both foundational and fundamentally revised in Crocean aesthetics.
