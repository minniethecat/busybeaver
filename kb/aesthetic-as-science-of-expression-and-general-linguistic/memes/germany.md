# Germany

Germany (*Deutschland*) is the birthplace of modern philosophical aesthetics. From **Alexander Baumgarten** coining the term "aesthetic" to **Immanuel Kant**'s *Critique of Judgment* to **G. W. F. Hegel**'s systematic idealism, German philosophy established the framework within which Croce's aesthetics operates.

## In Croce's System

Croce's *History of Æsthetic* is in large part a history of German aesthetic thought. The arc from Baumgarten through Kant, Schiller, Schelling, Hegel, Herbart, and Schopenhauer constitutes the central narrative of aesthetic philosophy. Croce inherits German idealism's insistence that art is a spiritual activity, while rejecting its tendency to subordinate intuition to concept.

The German university system — Königsberg, Jena, Berlin, Göttingen, Heidelberg — was the institutional matrix of this development. Croce's own philosophy, though rooted in Italian thought, engages German Idealism as its primary interlocutor.
