# Italy

Italy (*Italia*) is the central geographical locus of Benedetto Croce's aesthetic philosophy. As home to **Giambattista Vico** in Naples, **Francesco De Sanctis** in the south, and Croce himself, Italy is the origin point of the tradition that identified aesthetics as an autonomous philosophical science.

## In Croce's System

Croce's philosophy is deeply Italian. His intellectual lineage — Vico's anti-Cartesianism, De Sanctis's immanent criticism, the Neapolitan Hegelian tradition — is inseparable from Italian intellectual history. Croce's *History of Æsthetic* traces Italian contributions from the Renaissance through the 19th century, documenting the "influence of Vico — Italian writers: A. Conti" (P02-209) and the subsequent development of aesthetic thought.

Croce himself was a central figure in Italian cultural life, serving as Minister of Public Education and shaping Italian intellectual culture for decades. The city of Naples, where Croce lived and worked, was home to the **Accademia Pontaniana** and a vibrant philosophical community.
