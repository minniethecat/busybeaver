# Robert Zimmermann

Robert Zimmermann (1824–1898) was an Austrian philosopher who developed the **Herbartian formalist aesthetics** into a systematic doctrine. Croce discusses the Herbartian tradition including Zimmermann as part of the formalist alternative to idealism.

## Biography

Zimmermann studied under Bernard Bolzano and became professor of philosophy at the University of Vienna. His major work, *General Aesthetics as Science of Form* (1865), is the most comprehensive statement of Herbartian aesthetic formalism.

## Contributions to Æsthetic

- **Aesthetics as Science of Form**: Zimmermann systematized Herbart's formalism into a complete aesthetic theory, analyzing art works in terms of elementary formal relations — symmetry, proportion, rhythm, harmony — that produce aesthetic pleasure independent of content
- **Anti-Idealist Aesthetics**: Zimmermann opposed Hegelian aesthetic idealism, arguing that beauty is a quality of formal configurations, not the sensuous appearance of metaphysical Ideas
- **Influence on Hanslick**: Zimmermann's formalism influenced Eduard Hanslick's musical aesthetics (*On the Musically Beautiful*), which argued that music's content is its form — "sounding forms in motion"
