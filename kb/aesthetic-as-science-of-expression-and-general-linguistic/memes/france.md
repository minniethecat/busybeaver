# France

France played a crucial role in the development of aesthetic thought that Croce analyzes. The Cartesian tradition — with its sharp separation of reason from imagination — provided the intellectualist framework against which Vico and subsequent aesthetic theory reacted.

## In Croce's System

Croce discusses "Cartesianism and imagination — Crousaz and André — The English and French aestheticians" (P02-191) as representing the rationalist challenge to which Vico's theory of poetic imagination was the answer. French aesthetic thought in the 17th and 18th centuries — from **Nicolas Boileau**'s neoclassical poetics through **Jean-Baptiste Dubos**'s empirical aesthetics to **Denis Diderot**'s art criticism — contributed to the gradual recognition of aesthetic autonomy.

The French Enlightenment's emphasis on reason, clarity, and rules — embodied in the Académie Française — provided both a stimulus and a foil for the development of Romantic and idealist aesthetics in Germany and Italy.
