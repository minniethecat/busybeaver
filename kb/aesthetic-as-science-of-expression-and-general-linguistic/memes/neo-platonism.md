# Neo-Platonism

Neo-Platonism was the philosophical movement that transmitted and transformed Plato's theory of beauty through late antiquity and the Renaissance. Croce's *History of Æsthetic* traces its influence from **Plotinus** through **Marsilio Ficino** to the 17th century.

## Significance for Æsthetic

**Plotinus** (3rd century CE) developed Plato's theory of beauty in the *Enneads*, arguing that beauty is the radiance of the One through material form. The sculptor does not copy nature but realizes in stone the Form that nature imperfectly embodies. This elevation of the artist's vision over mere imitation profoundly influenced Renaissance aesthetics.

**Marsilio Ficino**'s 15th-century translations of Plato and Plotinus into Latin revived Neoplatonic aesthetics for the Renaissance. Ficino's *Commentary on Plato's Symposium* made beauty a central category of Renaissance thought — beauty as the splendor of divine goodness, mediated through the hierarchy of being from matter to God.

Croce treats Neo-Platonism as an important but ultimately metaphysical theory of beauty — one that locates beauty in a transcendent source rather than in the immanent activity of spirit.
