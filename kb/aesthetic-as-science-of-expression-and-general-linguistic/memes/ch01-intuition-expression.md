[001-001] This volume is composed of a theoretical and of a historical part,

[001-002] which form two independent but complementary books.

[001-003] The nucleus of the theoretical part is a memoir, bearing the title

[001-004] *Fundamental Theses of an Æsthetic as Science of [[expression|Expression]] and General

[001-005] Linguistic,* which was read at the Accademia Pontaniana of Naples

[001-006] during the sessions of February 18 and May 6, 1900, and printed in vol.

[001-007] xxx. of its *Acts.* The author has added few substantial variations,

[001-008] but not a few additions and amplifications in rewriting it, also

[001-009] following a somewhat different sequence with a view to rendering the

[001-010] exposition more plain and easy. The first five chapters only of the

[001-011] historical portion were inserted in the Neapolitan review *Flegrea*

[001-012] (April 1901), under the title *[[giambattista-vico|Giambattista Vico]], First Discoverer of

[001-013] Æsthetic Science,* and these also reappear amplified and brought into

[001-014] harmony with the rest.

[001-015] The author has dwelt, especially in the theoretical part, upon general

[001-016] questions which are side-issues in respect to the theme that he has

[001-017] treated. But this will not seem a digression to those who remember

[001-018] that, strictly speaking, there are no particular philosophical

[001-019] sciences, standing by themselves. Philosophy is unity, and when we

[001-020] treat of Æsthetic or of Logic or of Ethics, we treat always of the

[001-021] whole of philosophy, although illustrating for didactic purposes only

[001-022] one side of that inseparable unity. In like manner, owing to this

[001-023] intimate connexion of all the parts of philosophy, the uncertainty and

[001-024] misunderstanding as to the æsthetic activity, the representative and

[001-025] [[imagination|productive imagination]], this firstborn of the spiritual activities,

[001-026] mainstay of the others, generates everywhere else misunderstandings,

[001-027] uncertainties and errors: in Psychology as in Logic, in History as

[001-028] in the Philosophy of Practice. If language is the first spiritual

[001-029] manifestation, and if the æsthetic form is language itself, taken in

[001-030] all its true scientific extension, it is hopeless to try to understand

[001-031] clearly the later and more complicated phases of the life of the

[001-032] spirit, when their first and simplest moment is ill known, mutilated

[001-033] and disfigured. From the explanation of the æsthetic activity is also

[001-034] to be expected the correction of several concepts and the solution

[001-035] of certain philosophic problems which generally seem to be almost

[001-036] desperate. Such is precisely the spirit animating the present work. And

[001-037] if the present attempt and the historical illustrations which accompany

[001-038] it may be of use in winning friends to these studies, by levelling

[001-039] obstacles and indicating paths to be followed; if this happen,

[001-040] especially here in [[italy|Italy]], whose æsthetic traditions (as has been

[001-041] demonstrated in its place) are very noble, the author will consider

[001-042] that he has gained his end, and one of his keenest desires will have

[001-043] been satisfied.

[001-044] NAPLES, *December* 1901.

[001-045] In addition to a careful literary revision, (in which, as well as in

[001-046] the revision of the notes, I have received valuable help from my friend

[001-047] Fausto Nicolini) I have in this third edition made certain alterations

[001-048] of theory, especially in Chapters X. and XI. of Part I., suggested by

[001-049] further reflexion and self-criticism.

[001-050] But I have refrained from introducing corrections or additions of such

[001-051] a kind as to alter the original plan of the book, which was, or was

[001-052] meant to be, a complete but brief æsthetic theory set in the framework

[001-053] of a general sketch of a Philosophy of the Spirit.

[001-054] The reader who desires a complete statement of the general or

[001-055] collateral doctrines or a more particular exposition of the other parts

[001-056] of philosophy (*e.g.* the lyrical nature of art) is now referred to the

[001-057] volumes on *Logic* and the *Philosophy of Practice,* which together

[001-058] with the present work compose the *Philosophy of the Spirit* which in

[001-059] the author's opinion exhausts the entire field of Philosophy. The three

[001-060] volumes were not conceived and written simultaneously; if they had

[001-061] been, some details would have been differently arranged. When I wrote

[001-062] the first I had no idea of giving it, as I have now done, two such

[001-063] companions; and I therefore designed it to be, as I say, complete in

[001-064] itself. In the second place, the present state of the study of Æsthetic

[001-065] made it desirable to append to the theoretical exposition a somewhat

[001-066] full history of the science, whereas for the other parts of Philosophy

[001-067] I was able to restrict myself to brief historical notes merely designed

[001-068] to show how, from my point of view, such a history would best be

[001-069] composed. Lastly, there are many things which now, after a systematic

[001-070] exposition of the various philosophical sciences, I see in closer

[001-071] connexions and in a clearer, or at least a different, light; a certain

[001-072] hesitation and even some doctrinal errors visible here and there in the

[001-073] *Æsthetic,* especially where subjects foreign to Æsthetic itself are

[001-074] being treated, would now no longer be justified. For all these reasons

[001-075] the three volumes, in spite of their substantial unity of spirit and of

[001-076] aim, have each its own physiognomy, and show marks of the different

[001-077] periods of life at which they were written, so as to group themselves,

[001-078] and to demand interpretation, as a progressive series according to

[001-079] their dates of publication.

[001-080] With what may be called the minor problems of Æsthetic, and the

[001-081] objections which have been or might be brought against my theory, I

[001-082] have dealt and am continuing to deal in special essays, of which I

[001-083] shall shortly publish a first collection which will form a kind of

[001-084] explanatory and polemical appendix to the present volume.

[001-085] *November* 1907.

[001-086] In revising this book once more for a fourth edition, I take the

[001-087] opportunity of announcing that the supplementary volume of essays

[001-088] promised above was published in 1910 under the title *Problems of

[001-089] Æsthetic and Contributions to the History of Æsthetic in Italy.*

[001-090] B. C.

[001-091] *May* 1911.
