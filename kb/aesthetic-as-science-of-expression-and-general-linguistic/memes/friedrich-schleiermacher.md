# Friedrich Schleiermacher

Friedrich Schleiermacher (1768–1834) was a German theologian, philosopher, and hermeneuticist. Croce devotes a chapter to him — "Schleiermacher" (ch29-schleiermacher) — examining his contribution to the aesthetics of individual expression.

## Biography

Schleiermacher was a central figure in early German Romanticism, associated with the Berlin salon of Henriette Herz and the Schlegel brothers. He became professor of theology at the University of Berlin and is considered the father of modern hermeneutics.

## Contributions to Æsthetic

- **Individual Expression**: Schleiermacher emphasized the irreducibly individual character of artistic expression, anticipating themes in Croce's own theory of intuition as the cognition of individuals
- **Hermeneutics and Aesthetics**: His theory of understanding (*Verstehen*) as the re-creation of another's mental act parallels Croce's account of taste as the reproduction of the artist's intuition
- **Religion and Art**: Schleiermacher's treatment of religion as a distinct sphere of experience — the feeling of absolute dependence — influenced the Romantic conception of art as a mode of transcendence
