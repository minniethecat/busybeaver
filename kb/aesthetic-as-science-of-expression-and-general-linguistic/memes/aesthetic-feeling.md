# Aesthetic Feeling

The concept of feeling occupies a troubled place in Croce's aesthetic theory. Croce devotes an entire chapter to "Æsthetic Feelings and the Distinction Between the Beautiful and the Ugly," yet his ultimate position is that feeling is not the essence of art — it is the pre-aesthetic material that art transforms.

## Definition

The word "feeling" is, Croce acknowledges, "one of the richest in meaning" (011-007). It can refer to the mere passivity of sensation, to the economic activity of the spirit, or to the content that intuition shapes into expression. Croce's task is to distinguish these senses and to locate the proper place of aesthetic feeling within the system of spiritual activities.

The key claim is that feeling is not a distinct form of spiritual activity but the content presupposed by intuition: "the spirit does not know anything except by making it the content of representations" (011-018). Feeling becomes aesthetic only when it is formed — that is, only when it becomes the content of an intuition-expression.

## In Croce's System

### Relation to Other Forms of Spirit

Croce distinguishes several senses of feeling:

1. **Feeling as economic activity** — the practical activity of desiring and willing. "Identification of feeling with economic activity" is one of the "various significations of the word feeling" (P02-082, P02-083). This is not aesthetic.

2. **Feeling as content of intuition** — the impressions, sensations, and emotions that constitute the material of art. The artist "does nothing but make himself his impressions and feelings, but only so far as he is able to express them" (002-259).

3. **Feeling as the aesthetic fact itself** — the erroneous claim "that art is feeling" (003-190). Croce rejects this: art is expression, not mere feeling.

### Key Distinctions

Croce is careful to distinguish "true æsthetic feelings" from "concomitant and accidental feelings" (P02-089, P02-090). True aesthetic feelings are those that arise from the contemplation of the aesthetic fact itself; accidental feelings are those that happen to accompany it — the pleasure of recognition, the comfort of familiarity, the excitement of novelty. The former are integral to art; the latter are extraneous.

The beautiful and the ugly are themselves forms of aesthetic feeling: "the beautiful is the successful expression, the ugly the unsuccessful" — but this success or failure is not a feeling added to expression; it is the character of the expression itself.

## Historical Context

### Predecessors

The reduction of art to feeling has a long history. The Romantic tradition, in particular, identified artistic expression with the outpouring of emotion. Croce engages with this tradition critically: "Poetry is the language of feeling, prose the language of the intellect" — but since "the intellect is also feeling," this distinction collapses (004-127, 004-128). The theory that art is "a fact of feeling, not a fact of knowledge" is one of the principal errors Croce sets out to refute (003-174, 003-175).

### Croce's Innovation

Croce's innovation is to dissolve the question of aesthetic feeling into the more fundamental question of expression. Art is not feeling but the expression of feeling; feeling is the matter, expression is the form. This move allows Croce to maintain the emotional richness of art while preserving its cognitive character as a form of theoretic activity. Even "the colour of a feeling" is already an intuition — already formed (002-114).

## Criticism and Debate

> These who have claimed that art is feeling the reason is that they have found in the vital impulse, in its feeling, that is to say again, pure immediacy (003-190, 003-194).

Critics have argued that Croce's treatment of feeling is too intellectualist — that by making feeling mere content for intuition, he strips it of its distinctive character. If feeling is only the raw material of expression, what becomes of the ineffable, non-cognitive dimensions of aesthetic experience? Croce's response is that there is nothing ineffable: whatever cannot be expressed is not yet aesthetic.
