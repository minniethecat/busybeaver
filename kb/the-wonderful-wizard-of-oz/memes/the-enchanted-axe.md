## Overview

The Enchanted Axe is the magical weapon used by the [[wicked-witch-of-the-east|Wicked Witch of the East]] to prevent the young woodchopper (later the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]]) from marrying the [[munchkin-maiden|Munchkin Maiden]]. Through repeated, deliberate slippages, the axe severed each of the woodchopper's natural limbs and ultimately his entire body, forcing him to replace every part with tin prosthetics crafted by [[the-tinsmith|the Tinsmith]].

## Origin and Enchantment

The axe originally belonged to the young woodchopper himself — an ordinary tool he used to earn his living. It became enchanted when the [[wicked-witch-of-the-east|Wicked Witch of the East]] intervened at the request of the [[munchkin-maiden|Munchkin Maiden]]'s lazy guardian. The old woman "promised her two sheep and a cow if she would prevent the marriage," and the Witch responded by enchanting the woodchopper's axe [005-045].

## The Dismemberments

The enchanted axe attacked its owner in a series of escalations [005-045, 005-046, 005-047]:

1. **Left leg** — The first slip, occurring while the woodchopper was "chopping away at [his] best."
2. **Right leg** — The second injury, after the woodchopper had replaced his left leg with tin.
3. **Left arm** — "After this the enchanted axe cut off my arms, one after the other."
4. **Right arm** — The fourth limb lost.
5. **Head** — "The Wicked Witch then made the axe slip and cut off my head."
6. **Body** — The final and most devastating cut, "splitting me into two halves."

Each time, the [[the-tinsmith|tinsmith]] crafted a tin replacement. After the body was split, the tinsmith created a complete tin torso, joining all the tin limbs and head with mechanical joints [005-047].

## Aftermath

Once the woodchopper was entirely made of tin, the enchanted axe could no longer harm him. He observed: "it did not matter now if my axe slipped, for it could not cut me" [005-048]. The axe's power was neutralized by the very transformation it had caused. However, the transformation stripped the woodchopper of his [[heart]], and with it his capacity to love the [[munchkin-maiden|Munchkin Maiden]] [005-047].

## Significance

The Enchanted Axe serves as the instrument of the [[wicked-witch-of-the-east|Wicked Witch of the East]]'s cruelty and is the direct cause of the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]]'s unique condition. Without it, there would be no Tin Woodman — merely an ordinary Munchkin woodchopper who married and lived an uneventful life. The axe thus represents one of the story's central paradoxes: destruction that enables a kind of rebirth.
