## Overview

The Attack of the Wild Crows is the second of three assaults sent by the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] against the travelers. After her [[wolves-of-the-wicked-witch|wolves]] were destroyed, the Witch dispatched her flock of wild crows to "peck out their eyes and tear them to pieces" [012-027]. This attack showcases the [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]]'s unique ability to counter a threat that would overwhelm any flesh-and-blood fighter.

## The Attack

When the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] learned that forty of her wolves lay dead, "she was even angrier than before" [012-027]. She summoned her flock of [[wild-crows|wild crows]], described as "forty crows with great sharp beaks," and commanded them to "fly out at once and peck out their eyes and tear them to pieces" [012-027].

The crows descended on the travelers in a dark cloud. The [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]] told his companions: "This is my battle. Lie down on the ground and I will fight them." As the crows swooped down, the Scarecrow "seized each one by the neck and wrung its neck until it died" [012-028]. He killed all forty crows, one by one.

## Aftermath

The Witch "was so angry that she trembled from head to foot" [012-029] upon learning her crows were dead. In desperation, she sent her [[black-bees|black bees]] — a swarm with stingers "that could kill a man" [012-029] — in a third attempt. The companions were prepared: the [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]] removed his straw and covered Dorothy, Toto, and the Lion, while the bees broke their stingers on the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]]'s tin body and died.

## Significance

The Scarecrow's victory over the crows demonstrates that his lack of flesh — which he had always considered a deficiency — is actually an asset. He cannot be pecked, clawed, or wounded, making him uniquely suited to face swarming attackers. This inversion — weakness becoming strength — is characteristic of the story's treatment of each companion's perceived limitations.
