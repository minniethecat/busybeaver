## Overview

Dorothy Enslaved refers to the period during which [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] was held captive by the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] and forced to work as a kitchen slave in the Witch's castle. This episode tests Dorothy's resilience and ultimately leads to the Witch's destruction and the liberation of the [[wickie-country|Winkie Country]].

## Capture

After the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] used the [[golden-cap|Golden Cap]] to summon the [[winged-monkey-band|Winged Monkeys]], they captured [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] and brought her to the Witch's castle [012-040]. The Witch was delighted, "for she knew at once that the girl was the owner of the powerful [[silver-shoes|Silver Shoes]]" [012-042]. However, she could not take the shoes from Dorothy by force, as Dorothy wore them constantly and the Witch "was as much afraid of the dark as Dorothy was, and she dared not touch the shoes at night" [012-043].

## Life as a Slave

The Witch made Dorothy her slave, assigning her "all the hardest work in the kitchen to do" [012-044]. Dorothy was forced to "carry wood and water, and sweep the floors, and wash the pots" [012-044]. The Witch deliberately made Dorothy's life miserable, hoping the girl would remove the [[silver-shoes|Silver Shoes]] out of weariness or carelessness.

Meanwhile, the [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]] was kept in a separate part of the castle. The Witch attempted to starve him into submission so she could "harness him like a horse" [012-044], but Dorothy secretly brought him food each night.

## The Theft of the Silver Shoe

After weeks of frustration, the Witch devised a trick. She placed an invisible iron bar across the kitchen floor, and when Dorothy stumbled over it, one of the [[silver-shoes|Silver Shoes]] came off. The Witch "grabbed up the shoe and put it on her own skinny foot" [012-047]. In her fury at losing one shoe, Dorothy seized a bucket of water and "dashed it over the Witch, wetting her from head to foot" [012-048].

## Aftermath

The water caused the Witch to melt "away like brown sugar" [012-048]. Dorothy's enslavement ended abruptly with the Witch's death, and the [[winkies|Winkies]] — themselves long enslaved — celebrated their liberation. This event directly leads to the [[liberation-of-the-winkies|liberation of the Winkies]] and Dorothy acquiring the [[golden-cap|Golden Cap]].
