The **King of Beasts** is the traditional title borne by lions, carrying with it the expectation of courage, strength, and leadership over all other animals. In the story of the [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]], this title serves as the emotional core of his character arc: he is called the King of Beasts by reputation, but knows himself to be a coward, and his quest is to earn the title in truth.

## The Title and the Lion's Shame

When Dorothy first meets the Lion, he confesses that the title of King of Beasts is a source of shame rather than pride. Despite his fearsome roar, which frightens every living thing, he knows he lacks the courage the title demands:

> "All the other animals in the forest naturally expect me to be brave, for the Lion is everywhere thought to be the King of Beasts. I learned that if I roared very loudly every living thing was frightened and got out of my way. Whenever I've met a man I've been awfully scared; but I just roared at him, and he has always run away as fast as he could go." [006-023]

The Scarecrow voices the moral crux of the problem:

> "But that isn't right. The King of Beasts shouldn't be a coward." [006-025]

The Lion fully agrees, calling his cowardice "my great sorrow" that "makes my life very unhappy." [006-023]

## The Quest for Worthiness

When the Lion presents himself before [[wizard-of-oz|Oz]], he frames his request directly in terms of the title:

> "I am a Cowardly Lion, afraid of everything. I came to you to beg that you give me courage, so that in reality I may become the King of Beasts, as men call me." [011-084]

Oz later gives the Lion a green liquid from a bottle, telling him that drinking it will give him courage — though Oz privately acknowledges that the courage was already inside the Lion all along. [015-061]

## Earning the Title

The Lion's true coronation as King of Beasts occurs in the southern forest when the animals of the wood are threatened by a [[lion-slaying-the-spider|Giant Spider]]. Recognizing the Lion, a tiger bows and declares:

> "Welcome, O King of Beasts! You have come in good time to fight our enemy and bring peace to all the animals of the forest once more." [021-011]

The Lion negotiates his kingship:

> "If I put an end to your enemy, will you bow down to me and obey me as King of the Forest?" [021-017]

The beasts roar their assent, and the Lion hunts down and kills the Giant Spider with a single spring and blow. Returning victorious, he receives their fealty:

> The beasts bowed down to the Lion as their King, and he promised to come back and rule over them as soon as Dorothy was safely on her way to Kansas. [021-026]

At this moment, the Cowardly Lion finally becomes, in deed as well as in name, the King of Beasts.
