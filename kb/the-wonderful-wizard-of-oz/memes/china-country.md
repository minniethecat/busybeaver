## Introduction

The Dainty China Country is a strange and miniature land located in the southern part of Oz, discovered by Dorothy and her companions during their journey to the Country of the Quadlings. In this peculiar region, every inhabitant, animal, and building is made entirely of china — fragile, small, and beautifully painted in bright colors. The tallest inhabitant is no higher than Dorothy's knee [020-009].

## Geography and Layout

The China Country sits behind a high wall. The floor of the country is described as being as "smooth and shining and white as the bottom of a big platter" [020-008]. Scattered across this white expanse are many small houses made of china and "painted in the brightest colors." The houses are so tiny that the largest reaches only to Dorothy's waist. There are also small china barns with china fences, and all manner of livestock — cows, sheep, horses, pigs, and chickens — all made of china [020-008].

Dorothy and her companions must cross the entire country to continue their southward journey. The country is bounded by a second china wall on the far side, lower than the first [020-023].

## Inhabitants

The people of the China Country are diverse and colorful:

- **Milkmaids** and **shepherdesses** in brightly colored bodices with golden spots on their gowns
- **Princesses** wearing "most gorgeous frocks of silver and gold and purple"
- **Shepherds** in knee breeches with striped stockings and golden buckles on their shoes
- **Princes** with jeweled crowns, ermine robes, and satin doublets
- **Clowns** in ruffled gowns with round red spots on their cheeks and tall pointed caps [020-009]

All these people — including their clothes — are made of china, and they are capable of speech, movement, and emotion [020-009].

## Fragility and Mending

A defining characteristic of the China Country is the extreme fragility of its inhabitants. When Dorothy accidentally startles a china milkmaid's cow, the cow kicks over the stool and breaks its leg; the milkmaid suffers a nick in her elbow [020-012]. The milkmaid explains she must take the cow "to the mender's shop and have it glued on again" [020-013].

The china people can be repaired when broken, but as a china Princess explains to Dorothy, "one is never so pretty after being mended" [020-017]. Mr. Joker the clown serves as a cautionary example — he has been mended in so many places that he is "completely covered with cracks" [020-018].

## Encounter with the China Princess

Dorothy meets a beautifully dressed young Princess and, charmed by her appearance, offers to take her back to Kansas to stand on Aunt Em's mantelpiece. The Princess refuses, explaining a crucial fact about her kind:

> "Here in our country we live contentedly, and can talk and move around as we please. But whenever any of us are taken away our joints at once stiffen, and we can only stand straight and look pretty." [020-022]

This reveals that the china people are living, conscious beings who value their freedom of movement over being admired as decorative objects. Dorothy, respecting this, bids the Princess farewell rather than make her unhappy [020-022].

## The Lion's Mishap

As the travelers exit the China Country, the Cowardly Lion accidentally smashes a china church with his tail while jumping onto the wall [020-024]. Dorothy remarks that they were lucky not to have done more harm "than breaking a cow's leg and a church" given how brittle everything is [020-025].
