## Overview

The **Tin Woodman's axe** is both the instrument of his tragedy and the tool of his heroism. Originally an ordinary woodchopper's axe, it was enchanted by the [[wicked-witch-of-the-east|Wicked Witch of the East]] to sever its owner's limbs, leading to the woodchopper's transformation into the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]]. Once the Woodman's body was entirely tin, the axe became his primary tool and weapon, used to clear forest paths, kill the [[wolves-of-the-wicked-witch|Wicked Witch's wolves]] and the [[wildcat|Wildcat]], fell trees to cross ravines, and build the truck that rescued the [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]] from the [[poppy-field|Deadly Poppy Field]].

## The Enchantment

The Wicked Witch of the East enchanted the axe at the behest of a lazy old woman who wished to prevent her servant girl from marrying the woodchopper. The old woman "promised her two sheep and a cow if she would prevent the marriage," and the Witch "enchanted my axe" [005-045].

The enchantment caused the axe to slip during the woodchopper's work, systematically severing his left leg, right leg, both arms, his head, and finally splitting his body in two [005-046–005-047]. Each lost part was replaced with tin by a [[tinsmith|tinsmith]], until the woodchopper's body was entirely artificial and the axe could no longer harm him [005-048].

## As a Tool of Heroism

After the Woodman's transformation, the enchanted axe—now harmless to its tin owner—became an instrument of construction and protection:

- **Path-clearing**: When trees and branches blocked the yellow brick road, "the Tin Woodman set to work with his axe and chopped so well that soon he cleared a passage for the entire party" [005-034]
- **Bridge-building**: When the travelers encountered the great ditch, the Woodman "chopped down the tree" to create a bridge [007-026]
- **Wolf-slaying**: The Woodman killed all forty of the Wicked Witch's wolves with his axe, decapitating each with a single blow [012-020–012-022]
- **Wildcat rescue**: He killed the pursuing Wildcat with "a quick blow that cut the beast's head clean off from its body" [009-004]
- **Truck construction**: He built the truck used by the [[field-mice|field mice]] to pull the sleeping Lion from the poppy field [009-029]
- **Firewood**: He chopped the wood that heated the [[the-balloon|Wizard's balloon]] [017-022]

## Significance

The axe embodies the double-edged nature of the Woodman's story. The enchantment that destroyed his human body also made him physically invulnerable. The axe that severed his limbs became the tool with which he saved his companions again and again. The Woodman's relationship to his axe—once victim, now master—mirrors Dorothy's relationship to Oz: what was once hostile becomes instrumental to survival and success.
