# Wolves of the Wicked Witch

## Overview

The [[wolves-of-the-wicked-witch|Wolves of the Wicked Witch]] are a pack of forty great wolves under the command of the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]]. When the Witch sees [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] and her companions approaching through the Winkie Country, her first wave of attack employs these wolves — but the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] dispatches the entire pack in a single battle, demonstrating both his martial prowess and the Witch's ruthless willingness to sacrifice her servants (012-013–012-022).

## Description

The wolves are described as "a pack of great wolves" with "long legs and fierce eyes and sharp teeth" (012-013). They are summoned by the Witch's silver whistle — the first of three enchanted summons she possesses, the others calling crows and bees. The Witch commands forty wolves, all of whom obey without question when ordered to "go to the strangers and tear them to pieces" (012-015).

The leader of the wolves is singled out in the narrative — he questions the Witch about whether she intends to enslave the travelers, suggesting a degree of independent thought. When she answers that they are "not of her race," the wolves "dashed away at full speed" to carry out their mission (012-015–012-016).

## Role in the Story

### The Attack

The [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]] and the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]], keeping watch while the others sleep, hear the wolves coming. The Tin Woodman positions himself in front of the sleeping party and, as the leader of the wolves charges, "swung his arms, and snip went the axe — the wolf's head was cut off and it fell dead at his feet" (012-020).

The remaining wolves attack one by one, and the Tin Woodman dispatches each in turn: "as soon as they came the Tin Woodman swung his axe, and another wolf fell dead. There were forty wolves, and forty times a wolf was killed, so that at last they all lay dead in a heap before the Woodman" (012-021–012-022).

### Aftermath

When the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch]] wakes the next morning, she looks out "as far as she could see" and beholds "all her wolves lying dead" (012-023). Her fury drives her to summon her second weapon — the wild crows — which the [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]] subsequently dispatches in a parallel demonstration of competence. The wolves' destruction signals the gradual erosion of the Witch's power as the travelers prove themselves more than her minions can handle.

## Quotes

## Notes

The wolf pack's annihilation is the first of three sequential tests (wolves → crows → bees) that serve to establish the complementary strengths of Dorothy's companions — the Tin Woodman's physical prowess (wolves), the Scarecrow's strategic thinking (crows), and the group's resourcefulness (bees). The wolves' willing complicity — the leader asks whether the travelers will be enslaved, and accepts the Witch's dismissal of them as "not of her race" — introduces a subtle moral dimension to the story's treatment of enchanted obedience.
