# Boq

## Overview

Boq is a wealthy Munchkin landowner who provides Dorothy with her first hospitality in the Land of Oz. On the night after her house lands in Munchkin Country, he hosts a feast and dance in her honor, gives her a room for the night, and in the morning offers cautious advice about the dangerous road to the Emerald City (003-016). Though his appearance is brief, Boq serves an important narrative function: his reaction to Dorothy's silver shoes establishes the Munchkins' reverence for the Witch-slaying stranger, and his warning about the journey ahead frames the peril to come.

## Description

Boq is described simply as "the rich Munchkin himself" (003-016). Like all Munchkins, he wears blue — the Munchkin color — and he notes that Dorothy's blue-and-white gingham dress marks her as a friend: "Blue is the color of the Munchkins, and white is the witch color. So we know you are a friendly witch" (003-021). He speaks gravely and with authority, reflective of his status in the community. His home is well-appointed, with a dining area for feasts and private rooms with "pretty bed[s]" dressed in blue cloth (003-023).

## Role in the Story

Boq first appears after the Witch of the North's council with the Munchkins. He waits upon Dorothy at supper, and when he sees her silver shoes — taken from the feet of the dead Wicked Witch of the East — he immediately addresses her as "a great sorceress" (003-017). Dorothy's protestations that she is only an ordinary girl do not shake his conviction; the silver shoes are, in Munchkin eyes, irrefutable proof of magical status.

The next morning, after Dorothy has eaten breakfast and watched a Munchkin baby play with Toto, Boq delivers his warning about the journey ahead: "It is better for people to keep away from Oz, unless they have business with him. But it is a long way to the Emerald City, and it will take you many days. The country here is rich and pleasant, but you must pass through rough and dangerous places before you reach the end of your journey" (003-026). He himself has never been to the Emerald City — an admission that underscores how isolated Munchkin Country is from the rest of Oz.

## Relationships

Boq's relationship with **[[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]]** is one of respectful deference, bordering on awe. He treats her as a sorceress despite her denials. He has no direct relationship with the **[[witch-of-the-north|Witch of the North]]** beyond being part of the Munchkin community she protects. The text does not show him interacting with **[[toto|Toto]]**, though a Munchkin baby plays with the dog under his roof (003-024), suggesting his household's general hospitality extends to Dorothy's animal companion.

## Notes

Boq is one of the few named Munchkins in the novel. His name appears only once in the text (003-016), after which he is referred to by his role. His character demonstrates how Baum uses minor figures to build the world of Oz through accumulation of detail: Boq's blue sheets, his feast, his grave warning, and the Munchkin baby all contribute to making Munchkin Country feel like a real, inhabited place rather than a mere backdrop for Dorothy's adventure.
