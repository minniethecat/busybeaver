# Melting of the Wicked Witch of the West

## Overview

In a desperate act of self-defense, Dorothy throws a bucket of water over the Wicked Witch of the West, causing the witch to melt and freeing the Winkies from slavery. Taking place in **[[witchs-castle|Witchs Castle]]** (Chapter 012), this event marks a significant moment in Dorothy's journey through the Land of Oz.

## Background

The event occurs within the context of the journey through Oz.

## Notes

This event reflects key themes in Baum's narrative: the triumph of resourcefulness over brute force, the power of belief and perception, and the importance of companionship. In the context of American children's literature at the turn of the century, such episodes emphasize moral growth and self-discovery through adventure.
