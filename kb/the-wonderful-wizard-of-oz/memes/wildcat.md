The **Wildcat** was a great yellow predatory beast encountered by Dorothy and her companions near the yellow brick road. It pursued a small gray field mouse — later revealed to be the Queen of the Field Mice — and was killed by the Tin Woodman in an act of instinctive kindness, an event that proved pivotal to the travelers' journey.

> It was, indeed, a great yellow Wildcat, and the Woodman thought it must be chasing something, for its ears were lying close to its head and its mouth was wide open, showing two rows of ugly teeth, while its red eyes glowed like balls of fire. [009-003]

## Appearance

The Wildcat was described as a large yellow feline with features that emphasized its ferocity. Its ears lay flat against its head in a hunting posture, its mouth gaped wide to reveal "two rows of ugly teeth," and its red eyes "glowed like balls of fire." [009-003] The Woodman immediately recognized the creature as a threat to the small field mouse it was pursuing.

## Death

As the Wildcat ran past the Tin Woodman in pursuit of the field mouse, the Woodman raised his axe and delivered a single swift blow:

> So the Woodman raised his axe, and as the Wildcat ran by he gave it a quick blow that cut the beast's head clean off from its body, and it rolled over at his feet in two pieces. [009-004]

The Woodman acted despite having no heart — a deliberate irony in the narrative — reasoning that "it was wrong for the Wildcat to try to kill such a pretty, harmless creature." [009-003]

## Aftermath

The field mouse that the Wildcat had been chasing stopped and approached the Woodman to thank him, revealing herself to be the Queen of all the Field Mice. When her subjects arrived moments later, they were astonished at her survival:

> "Oh, your Majesty, we thought you would be killed! How did you manage to escape the great Wildcat?" They all bowed so low to the little Queen that they almost stood upon their heads. [009-012]

The Queen credited the Tin Woodman:

> "This funny tin man," she answered, "killed the Wildcat and saved my life. So hereafter you must all serve him, and obey his slightest wish." [009-013]

This command directly led to the field mice later rescuing the [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]] from the [[deadly-poppy-field|Deadly Poppy Field]], making the Wildcat's death a critical turning point in the narrative.
