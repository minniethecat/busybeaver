# Silver Shoes

## Overview

The [[silver-shoes|Silver Shoes]] are a powerful magical item that originally belonged to the [[wicked-witch-of-the-east|Wicked Witch of the East]] and later came into the possession of [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy Gale]]. They are the central magical artifact of the story — their true power is revealed only at the end, when [[glinda|Glinda the Good Witch]] explains that they could have returned [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] to [[kansas]] from the very first day she arrived in [[land-of-oz|Oz]] (023-025). The shoes are described as silver with pointed toes, and they possess the extraordinary ability to transport the wearer to any place in the world in three steps (023-030). Their loss in the [[kansas|Deadly Desert]] at the story's end is one of the novel's most poignant moments — the power to return was always within reach, and the moment it is used, it vanishes forever.

## Properties

The Silver Shoes are described as shining silver with pointed toes (002-012). They are sized perfectly for a child's feet and fit [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] as though made for her (003-008). The shoes tinkle merrily when walking on hard surfaces (003-012).

The shoes' magical properties include:

- **Instantaneous travel**: The wearer can travel to any place in the world in three steps, with each step made in the wink of an eye (023-030).
- **Heel-click activation**: The shoes are activated by knocking the heels together three times and commanding them to carry the wearer to a desired destination (023-030).
- **Desert-safe passage**: The shoes can carry the wearer over the Deadly Desert that surrounds the [[land-of-oz|Land of Oz]], something no other means of travel can accomplish (023-025).
- **Protective charm**: The shoes bear a powerful protective charm that even the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] feared, knowing that neither she nor the [[winged-monkey-band|Winged Monkeys]] could harm the wearer (012-059).

| Property | Mechanism | Limitation |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| Teleportation | Three heel-clicks + spoken command | Falls off during flight over the Deadly Desert |
| Protection | Inherent charm on the shoes | Cannot prevent the Witch from tripping Dorothy with an invisible bar |
| Recognition | Other Oz inhabitants recognize the shoes' power | Dorothy herself does not know until the very end |
| Transfer | Given by the Witch of the North | Lost forever after the single return journey |

## Role in the Story

The Silver Shoes first appear when [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy's]] house lands on and kills the [[wicked-witch-of-the-east|Wicked Witch of the East]] in [[munchkin-country|Munchkin Country]]. Dorothy sees the dead Witch's feet sticking out from under the house, "shod in silver shoes with pointed toes" (002-012). The [[witch-of-the-north|Witch of the North]] gives the shoes to Dorothy, explaining simply that they are hers now (002-035). The [[council-of-munchkins|Munchkins]] note that the Witch of the East was proud of the shoes and that "there is some charm connected with them; but what it is we never knew" (002-036).

As Dorothy journeys along the [[road-of-yellow-brick]] toward the [[emerald-city]], she wears the silver shoes and they "tinkle merrily on the hard, yellow roadbed" (003-012). Their appearance causes others to believe she is a great sorceress — [[boq|Boq]] of the Munchkins is awed by them (003-017), and when the soldier at the [[emerald-city|Emerald City]] gate mentions the silver shoes to the [[wizard-of-oz|Wizard]], Oz becomes "very much interested" and agrees to grant [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] an audience (011-023).

The [[wizard-of-oz|Wizard of Oz]] demands that Dorothy kill the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] as the price of her return to [[kansas]]. When he asks her where she obtained the silver shoes (011-032), he acknowledges their powerful charm and makes his demand contingent on her eliminating the last Wicked Witch in Oz (011-047).

The [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] covets the Silver Shoes above all else. Seeing them on [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy's]] feet, she trembles with fear, knowing the powerful charm they carry (012-059). The Witch tries to steal the shoes, but Dorothy never removes them except at night and for bathing — and the Witch is too afraid of the dark to enter at night, and too terrified of water to approach during bathing (012-070). The Witch finally succeeds in stealing one shoe using an invisible iron bar to trip Dorothy (012-071). After Dorothy melts the Witch with a bucket of water in the [[melting-of-the-wicked-witch-of-the-west|melting]], she recovers the silver shoe from the melted remains (012-084).

> "The Silver Shoes have wonderful powers. And one of the most curious things about them is that they can carry you to any place in the world in three steps, and each step will be made in the wink of an eye." (023-030)

At the story's climax, [[glinda]] reveals the true power of the Silver Shoes: they could have taken [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] home at any time. She instructs Dorothy to knock the heels together three times and command the shoes to carry her to [[kansas]] (023-030). Dorothy does so, and the shoes transport her across the desert in three steps (023-037). However, the shoes fall off during the flight and are lost forever in the Deadly Desert (023-041).

## Notes

The Silver Shoes serve as both a literal plot device and a thematic symbol. Their presence on [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy's]] feet from the beginning suggests that the power to return home was always within her reach — she simply did not know it. In the original illustrations by W. W. Denslow, the shoes are depicted as ornate silver slippers with pointed, curled toes. In the famous 1939 MGM film adaptation, the shoes were changed to ruby slippers to take advantage of the Technicolor process, a significant departure from Baum's original text. The shoes' loss in the desert at the end is thematically resonant: the magic that brings you home is not something you get to keep — it is something you use once, and then it is gone.
