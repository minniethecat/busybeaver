## Overview

Gayelette is a princess and sorceress who lived in a ruby palace in the northern lands of Oz long before the events of the main story. She appears only in the Winged Monkey King's flashback narrative in Chapter XIV, but her actions shaped the magical law binding the Winged Monkeys to the Golden Cap — a mechanism that proves crucial to Dorothy's journey. She is remembered as a good ruler who used her magic only to help others.

## Description

Gayelette is described as both a "beautiful princess" and a "powerful sorceress" whose magic was dedicated entirely to helping her people [014-035]. She lived in a magnificent palace built from great blocks of ruby [014-035]. Despite being loved by everyone, she carried the sorrow of loneliness — she could find no one to love who measured up to her standards of beauty and wisdom [014-035]. She was patient in her search, eventually finding a boy who was "handsome and manly and wise beyond his years," whom she resolved to marry when he came of age [014-035].

## Role in the Story

Gayelette's story is told entirely in flashback by the King of the Winged Monkeys in Chapter XIV, as he explains to Dorothy the origin of the Golden Cap and the Winged Monkeys' servitude.

After finding Quelala and bringing him to her ruby palace, Gayelette used all her magical powers to make him "as strong and good and lovely as any woman could wish" [014-035]. When Quelala reached manhood, she hastened to prepare their wedding [014-035].

Just before the wedding, the King of the Winged Monkeys — the grandfather of the current King — played a prank on Quelala, dropping him into a river [014-036]. Quelala swam to safety, laughing at the mishap, but Gayelette was furious when she found his silks and velvet ruined [014-037].

As punishment, Gayelette ordered that the Winged Monkeys' wings be tied and they be dropped into the river as they had done to Quelala [014-038]. However, Quelala spoke kindly on their behalf, and the old King pleaded for mercy. Gayelette relented, but imposed a binding magical condition: the Winged Monkeys would forever after perform three commands for whoever possessed the Golden Cap [014-038]. The Golden Cap itself had been crafted as a wedding present for Quelala, said to have cost the princess "half her kingdom" [014-038].

This magical compact, born of Gayelette's wrath and mercy, becomes central to the plot: the Wicked Witch of the West uses the Cap to command the Winged Monkeys against Dorothy and her friends, and Dorothy later uses it herself before finally handing it to Glinda.

## Relationships

- **Quelala** — Gayelette chose Quelala as her husband when he was still a boy, took him to her ruby palace, and used her magic to make him the "best and wisest man in all the land" [014-035]. Her love for him was great, and his kind intervention on behalf of the Winged Monkeys tempered her punishment.
- **[[the-king-of-the-winged-monkeys]]** — Gayelette bound the Winged Monkeys to servitude through the magic of the Golden Cap after the old King's prank on Quelala [014-038]. The current King of the Winged Monkeys recounts her story with grudging respect.
- **[[wicked-witch-of-the-west]]** — Through Gayelette's creation of the Golden Cap and its binding spell, the Wicked Witch gained the power to command the Winged Monkeys.

## Quotes

## Notes

Gayelette and Quelala belong to an earlier generation of Oz — a time before the Wizard's arrival, before the Wicked Witches consolidated power, when sorceress-princesses ruled from ruby palaces. Their story is the foundation myth of the Winged Monkeys' servitude, providing the magical mechanism (the Golden Cap) that drives the action of Chapters XII–XVIII. Gayelette represents a kind of benevolent autocracy: her magic serves the people, but her punishments are severe, and her mercy is conditional.
