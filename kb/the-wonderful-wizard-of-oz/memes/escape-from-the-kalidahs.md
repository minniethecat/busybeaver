# Escape from the Kalidahs

## Overview

Pursued by the monstrous Kalidahs, the Tin Woodman chops down a tree spanning a second ditch, allowing the group to cross — then the tree falls, plunging the Kalidahs into the chasm. Taking place in **[[dark-forest|Dark Forest]]** (Chapter 007), this event marks a significant moment in Dorothy's journey through the Land of Oz.

## Background

The event occurs within the context of the journey through Oz. It was precipitated by: [[crossing-the-great-ditch|Crossing The Great Ditch]].

## Notes

This event reflects key themes in Baum's narrative: the triumph of resourcefulness over brute force, the power of belief and perception, and the importance of companionship. In the context of American children's literature at the turn of the century, such episodes emphasize moral growth and self-discovery through adventure.
