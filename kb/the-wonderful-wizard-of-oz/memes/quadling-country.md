# Quadling Country

## Overview

Quadling Country is the southern quadrant of the Land of Oz, characterized by the color red and ruled by **[[glinda|Glinda the Good Witch]]**. It is the final destination in Dorothy's journey — after the Wizard departs and the Scarecrow and Tin Woodman take up their respective thrones, Dorothy, Toto, and the Cowardly Lion travel south through Quadling Country to seek Glinda's help in returning Dorothy to Kansas. It is here, at Glinda's palace, that the **[[silver-shoes|Silver Shoes]]**'s true power is revealed and Dorothy's quest reaches its conclusion.

## Geography

Dorothy and her companions enter Quadling Country from the south gate of the Emerald City. The journey south is the most hazardous stretch of their travels, passing through the **[[fighting-trees|Fighting Trees]]**, the **[[dainty-china-country|Dainty China Country]]**, and the hill of the **[[hammer-heads|Hammer-Heads]]** before reaching Glinda's palace. Quadling Country is also bordered by a great desert that separates Oz from the rest of the world — a desert that proves to be the only barrier the Silver Shoes cannot cross (023-030, paraphrased).

Glinda's palace is a magnificent structure set in a garden where "the trees bore rich fruits and the flowers were of the brightest hues" (023-001).

## Inhabitants

The Quadlings are the people of the South, associated with the color red. Unlike the Munchkins and Winkies, who have extended scenes of celebration, the Quadlings appear only briefly: they are "good natured and kind" (023-015) and host the travelers at a farmhouse and castle. **[[glinda|Glinda the Good Witch]]** rules from her palace, attended by "a bevy of beautiful maidens" (023-016). Glinda is described as "young and beautiful" with "rich red hair" (023-018), and her palace reflects the region's red color scheme.

## Role in the Story

Quadling Country serves as the setting for the novel's resolution. After the Wizard's departure, the Scarecrow rules the Emerald City and the Tin Woodman rules the Winkies, but Dorothy still has no way home. The soldier with the green whiskers advises her to seek Glinda's help (017-012). The journey south comprises three chapters (XVIII–XX) featuring the last obstacles Dorothy faces in Oz: the Fighting Trees, the Dainty China Country, and the Hammer-Heads.

At Glinda's palace, Dorothy learns that the Silver Shoes have always had the power to take her home: they can carry the wearer "any place in the world in three steps" (023-024). Glinda also arranges the future for the Cowardly Lion, who is invited to rule the beasts of the forest. Dorothy clicks her heels three times, and in three steps she is back on the Kansas prairie.

## Notable Features

- **Glinda's Palace**: A luxurious red-themed palace set amid fruitful gardens, where the novel's resolution takes place.
- **The Deadly Desert**: The impassable desert at Oz's southern border, referenced in discussions of the Winged Monkeys' service.
- **The Forest of the Beasts**: A great forest where the Lion defeats a monstrous spider and is made King of the Beasts.

## Quotes

> "Glinda the Good Witch of the South is the most powerful of all the Witches, and rules over the Quadlings." (017-012, paraphrased)

> "Your Silver Shoes will carry you over the desert. If you had known their power you could have gone back to your Aunt Em the very first day you came to this country." — Glinda (023-023)

## Notes

Quadling Country is the least geographically developed of the four Oz quadrants, but it contains the highest concentration of the novel's most fantastical obstacles — Fighting Trees, China people, and Hammer-Heads — suggesting that the farther one travels from the Emerald City, the stranger and more perilous Oz becomes. Glinda's role as the dispenser of final wisdom (revealing the shoes' power) establishes her as the true authority figure of the novel, in contrast to the fraudulent Wizard.
