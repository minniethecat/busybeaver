## Overview

The Winkie Goldsmith is a skilled Winkie craftsman who, alongside the Winkie tinsmiths, helped restore the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] after he was battered and dented in battle. While the tinsmiths repaired the Woodman's body, the goldsmith crafted a magnificent new axe-handle of solid gold.

## Role

After [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] melted the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]], the grateful [[winkies|Winkies]] offered their services to the companions. When Dorothy asked if any Winkies were tinsmiths who could repair the damaged [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]], they responded enthusiastically [013-013].

The tinsmiths "worked for three days and four nights" [013-015] straightening dents and soldering joints. "Now while the tinsmiths had been at work mending the Woodman himself, another of the Winkies, who was a goldsmith, had made an axe-handle of solid gold and fitted it to the Woodman's axe, instead of the old broken handle" [013-016]. Other Winkies polished the blade "until all the rust was removed and it glistened like burnished silver" [013-016].

## The Golden Axe-Handle

The golden axe-handle represents the Winkies' gratitude for their liberation. It is a mark of honor and a permanent reminder of the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]]'s role in defeating the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]]. The Woodman later used this axe as the ruler of the [[winkie-country|Winkie Country]], where he became their king.

## Relationships

- **[[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]]** — The recipient of the goldsmith's finest work. The golden axe-handle was a gift of gratitude for the Woodman's part in liberating the Winkies.
- **[[winkie-tinsmiths|Winkie Tinsmiths]]** — Fellow craftsmen who worked alongside the goldsmith to restore the Tin Woodman.

## Notes

The Winkie Goldsmith appears only briefly, in a single passage [013-016], but his contribution is symbolically rich. The transformation of the Woodman's simple woodcutter's axe — the very tool that was enchanted by the [[wicked-witch-of-the-east|Wicked Witch of the East]] — into an instrument with a solid gold handle represents the Woodman's own transformation from victim of witchcraft to honored king.
