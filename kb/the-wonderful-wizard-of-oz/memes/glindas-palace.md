# Glinda's Palace

## Overview

Glinda's Palace is the castle of Glinda the Good Witch of the South, situated on the very edge of the Deadly Desert in Quadling Country [018-033]. It serves as the final destination of Dorothy's journey across Oz, where she and her companions seek the wisdom of the most powerful of the four Witches. The castle is richly appointed, with Glinda seated upon a throne of rubies, and its red décor matches the signature color of the Quadlings [023-003].

## Geography

The castle stands at the southernmost border of the Land of Oz, perched on the edge of the great desert that encircles the country [018-033]. The surrounding land is the country of the Quadlings — a region of ripening grain fields, well-paved roads, rippling brooks, and strong bridges, with fences, houses, and bridges all painted bright red [022-032]. The Quadlings themselves are short, fat, chubby, and good-natured people, dressed all in red [022-032].

To reach the castle, travelers must cross the hill of the Hammer-Heads — a barrier that deters casual visitors and explains why few Quadlings ever venture to the Emerald City [018-031]. Dorothy's party is carried over this obstacle by the Winged Monkeys [022-013].

## Architecture

The castle is a formidable structure approached through a guarded entrance. A girl soldier in a red uniform receives visitors and carries their names to Glinda before granting admission [022-036]. Inside, guests are shown to rooms where they may wash, comb their hair, and make themselves presentable before meeting the Witch [023-001].

The great audience chamber is the castle's centerpiece: a big room where Glinda sits upon a throne of rubies [023-003]. The ruby throne — a seat carved from precious gemstones — mirrors the red theme of the South just as the emerald throne does for the Emerald City [023-033]. Though the castle is a place of power and authority, its atmosphere is warm and kindly: Glinda leans forward to kiss Dorothy's forehead, demonstrating that beauty and goodness coexist here [023-014].

## Inhabitants

The castle is staffed by Quadling soldiers, including a girl soldier who serves as gatekeeper, receiving visitors and delivering their names to Glinda [022-036]. Glinda herself is the ruler of the castle — a beautiful woman who appears young despite her many years, with rich red hair in flowing ringlets, pure white dress, and kindly blue eyes [023-003]. She is described as kind to everyone and is thought by the Quadlings to be good [018-032].

## Role in the Story

### Seeking Glinda's Help

After the Wizard of Oz departs in his balloon, Dorothy's companions suggest seeking Glinda's aid for her return to Kansas. The soldier with the green whiskers advises that Glinda is "the most powerful of all the Witches, and rules over the Quadlings" and that "her castle stands on the edge of the desert, so she may know a way to cross it" [018-033]. He adds that Glinda "is a beautiful woman, who knows how to keep young in spite of the many years she has lived" [018-035].

### The Audience

Glinda receives Dorothy and her companions with warmth and kindness. When the party enters the great hall, they find her seated on her ruby throne — "both beautiful and young to their eyes" [023-003]. She listens to Dorothy's story of the cyclone, her adventures, and her longing to return to Kansas, then leans forward to kiss the little girl's upturned face [023-014].

### The Golden Cap Bargain

Glinda agrees to help Dorothy, but asks in exchange for the Golden Cap — a bargain Dorothy gladly accepts [023-014]. With the cap's three remaining commands, Glinda arranges the futures of all three companions:

- The Winged Monkeys will carry the Scarecrow back to the Emerald City, where he rules as Oz's successor [023-020]
- They will carry the Tin Woodman to the Country of the Winkies, where he will rule [023-022]
- They will carry the Cowardly Lion to the grand old forest where he is King of Beasts [023-026]

After using the cap's three commands, Glinda intends to give the Golden Cap to the King of the Winged Monkeys so they may thereafter be free forevermore [023-028].

### The Secret of the Silver Shoes

It is here in the castle that Glinda reveals the secret Dorothy has carried all along: the Silver Shoes can carry her anywhere in the world in three steps, each step made in the wink of an eye [023-034]. Had Dorothy known their power from the start, she could have returned to Kansas on her first day in Oz — but then the Scarecrow would still be in his cornfield, the Tin Woodman rusted in the forest, and the Lion a coward forever [023-037].

### Farewell

The final farewell takes place in Glinda's audience chamber. Dorothy kisses the Lion, the Tin Woodman, and hugs the Scarecrow. Glinda the Good steps down from her ruby throne to give the little girl a goodbye kiss [023-033]. Dorothy claps the heels of the Silver Shoes together three times — and is instantly whirled through the air, the shoes falling off in flight and lost forever in the desert [023-038].
