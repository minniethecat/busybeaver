# Winkies

## Overview

The [[winkies|Winkies]] are the inhabitants of the western quadrant of the [[land-of-oz|Land of Oz]], known as Winkie Country. They are visually identified by their yellow clothing — "yellow in the country of the Winkies" (022-023). For many years, the Winkies were enslaved by the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]], who "made the Winkies her slaves, and set herself to rule over their country" (012-040). Their liberation by [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] after she destroys the Witch is a turning point in the narrative, and the Winkies repay their gratitude by helping the travelers and eventually choosing the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] as their new ruler.

## Characteristics

The Winkies are described as industrious and skilled craftsmen — when asked to rescue and repair the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] after his encounter with the [[winged-monkey-band|Winged Monkeys]], "the Winkies lifted him tenderly in their arms" and a Winkie tinsmith and goldsmith repaired his body and gave him a new golden axe handle (013-008, 013-016–013-018). However, they are not a warrior people: "The Winkies were not a brave people, but they had to do as they were told" (012-038).

After the Witch's death, the Winkies celebrated their freedom: "There was great rejoicing among the yellow Winkies, for they had been made to work hard during the night as well as the day, and their cruel treatment by the Witch had made them very miserable" (013-003).

## Role in the Story

### Under Enslavement

The [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] ruled the Winkies with an iron fist. She used them as guards — "the yellow Winkies, who were the slaves of the Wicked Witch and too afraid of her not to do as she told them" (012-067) — and as forced labor. The Witch once "called a dozen of her slaves, who were the Winkies, and gave them sharp spears" to attack [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] and her companions (012-037). The [[winged-monkey-band|Winged Monkeys]] had been instrumental in the Witch's initial conquest: the Wicked Witch "made us enslave the Winkies" (014-041).

### Liberation

After [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] melted the Witch with a bucket of water, her "first act was to call all the Winkies together and tell them that they were no longer slaves" (013-002). The grateful Winkies then helped the travelers: they retrieved and repaired the [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]], brought the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] to their finest craftsmen for repair, and gathered the [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]] from his prison. When the travelers departed, the Winkies "gave them three cheers and many good wishes" (013-030).

### The Tin Woodman as Ruler

After the travelers leave the Winkie Country, the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] later returns to rule them: "The Winkies were very kind to me, and wanted me to rule over them after the Wicked Witch died" (023-018). This sets up the Tin Woodman's eventual fate as the monarch of the Winkies.

## Quotes

## Notes

The Winkies are one of the four color-coded peoples of Oz (yellow for the West). Unlike the Munchkins, who were already freed at the story's opening, the Winkies' liberation occurs within the main narrative, giving the reader a direct view of an enslaved people's reaction to freedom. The Tin Woodman's selection as their ruler is a natural outcome — he killed the Witch's wolves and the Winkies witnessed both his courage and kindness.
