## Overview

The **wild crows** are the second wave of attack deployed by the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] against [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] and her companions as they journey through the Winkie Country. Summoned by the Witch's silver whistle after the [[wolves-of-the-wicked-witch|wolves]] are slain, the flock of forty crows is dispatched by the [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]], whose scarecrow nature makes him immune to their fearlessness. The crows' destruction is the second of three sequential defeats that exhaust the Witch's lesser powers before she resorts to the [[golden-cap|Golden Cap]].

## Summoning

After discovering her wolves lying dead, the Wicked Witch blows her silver whistle twice, summoning the crows:

> Straightway a great flock of wild crows came flying toward her, enough to darken the sky. [012-024]

The Witch commands them to attack: "Go to the strangers and peck out their eyes and tear them to pieces" [012-025].

## The Attack and Defeat

The wild crows fly in one great flock toward Dorothy and her companions. The Scarecrow, recognizing his natural advantage, takes charge of the defense. He stands up and stretches out his arms, and the crows, instinctively frightened by the figure of a scarecrow, dare not approach [012-028].

The leader, King Crow, overcomes his fear and attacks:

> But the King Crow said: "I'll soon knock you to pieces, made of straw as you are." He flew at the Scarecrow, who caught it by the head and twisted its neck until it died. [012-030]

The Scarecrow then dispatches the remaining crows one by one: "There were forty crows, and forty times the Scarecrow twisted a neck, until at last all were lying dead beside him" [012-030].

## Aftermath

When the Witch looks out and sees all her crows lying in a heap, she flies into a terrible rage and blows her silver whistle three times, summoning the [[black-bees|black bees]] as her third and final conventional weapon [012-031].

The crows' destruction, together with the wolves and bees, leaves the Witch with no choice but to use the [[golden-cap|Golden Cap]] to summon the [[winged-monkey-band|Winged Monkeys]]:

> But now that her fierce wolves and her wild crows and her stinging bees were gone, and her slaves had been scared away by the Cowardly Lion, she saw there was only one way left to destroy Dorothy and her friends. [012-040]

## Significance

The wild crows form the middle link in the Witch's three-wave attack sequence (wolves → crows → bees), each of which is defeated by a different member of Dorothy's party. The Scarecrow's method of dispatch—twisting necks—demonstrates his physical competence in combat, contrasting with the Tin Woodman's axe-work against the wolves. The crows' instinctual fear of scarecrows, temporarily overcome by King Crow's bravado, ultimately proves fatal to the entire flock.
