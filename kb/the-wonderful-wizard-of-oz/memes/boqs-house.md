# Boq's House

## Overview

Boq's House is the home of the richest Munchkin in the eastern land of Oz, where Dorothy spends her first night after being carried by the cyclone from Kansas [002-009]. Larger than the typical round Munchkin dwelling, the house serves as a gathering place for celebration, a guest lodge for the strange girl in silver shoes, and the proper starting point of Dorothy's journey along the yellow brick road [002-020].

## Geography

The house stands in Munchkin Country, the eastern quadrant of Oz where blue is the signature color [002-020]. It lies a short walk from the place where Dorothy's farmhouse landed on the Wicked Witch of the East [002-009]. The setting is pastoral and prosperous: neat blue fences line the roads, beyond them fields of grain and vegetables grow in abundance, and the Munchkins are clearly good farmers [002-013].

Boq's house is positioned along the route Dorothy must take to reach the Emerald City — "rather larger than the rest" of the Munchkin houses along the way, marking it as the home of a person of means [002-009]. When Dorothy leaves the next morning and continues eastward, she soon reaches the cornfield where the Scarecrow stands [002-020].

## Architecture

Like all Munchkin homes, Boq's house follows the distinctive round architecture of the region: "the houses of the Munchkins were odd-looking dwellings, for each was round, with a big dome for a roof" [002-013]. All Munchkin buildings are painted blue, the favorite color of the eastern country [002-013].

What sets Boq's house apart is its size — "rather larger than the rest" [002-009] — befitting his status as the richest Munchkin in the land. The house has a green lawn before it where parties are held [002-009], and inside it contains at least one bedroom: a room with "a pretty bed" where Dorothy sleeps, its sheets made of blue cloth, with a blue rug on the floor for Toto [002-011].

The blue color scheme is consistent throughout: sheets, rug, and likely the walls and dome all follow the Munchkin tradition, creating an immersive experience of the eastern color for the Kansas girl [002-011].

## The Celebration

When Dorothy arrives, the lawn before the house is alive with festivity. Men and women are dancing while five little fiddlers play, and a big table is loaded with "delicious fruits and nuts, pies and cakes, and many other good things to eat" [002-009]. The occasion is the liberation of the Munchkins from the bondage of the Wicked Witch of the East — the very witch Dorothy's falling house has just killed [002-009].

This celebration marks Dorothy's first encounter with the people of Oz outside of the Munchkin welcoming committee. She is invited to supper, eats a hearty meal, and is waited upon by Boq himself [002-009] — a level of hospitality extended to the girl they believe to be a great sorceress.

## Boq's Recognition of the Silver Shoes

It is here that Boq first identifies the significance of Dorothy's silver shoes. Seeing them, he tells her, "You must be a great sorceress" — because she wears silver shoes, has killed the Wicked Witch, and has white in her frock (a witch color) [002-009]. He notes that her dress combines blue (the Munchkin color) with white (the witch color), concluding that "we know you are a friendly witch" [002-009].

Dorothy's protest that she is "only an ordinary little girl who had come by the chance of a cyclone into a strange land" [002-009] goes unheard — the perception of her as a witch, born in Boq's house, follows her throughout her journey.

## Morning and Departure

The next morning, Dorothy eats a hearty breakfast and watches a "wee Munchkin baby" play with Toto — the Munchkins have never seen a dog before, and Toto is a "fine curiosity" [002-011]. When Dorothy asks how far it is to the Emerald City, Boq replies gravely that he does not know — "for I have never been there" — and warns her that "it is a long way" through "rough and dangerous places" [002-016].

Despite this warning, Dorothy bids her hosts farewell and resumes her journey along the yellow brick road. In a few hours, she finds the Scarecrow in the cornfield — the next chapter of her adventure beginning where Boq's hospitality ends [002-020].
