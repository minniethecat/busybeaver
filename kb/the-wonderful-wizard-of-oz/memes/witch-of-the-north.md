# Witch of the North

## Overview

The Witch of the North — also called the Good Witch of the North — is the first magical being Dorothy meets in the Land of Oz. She explains the geography and politics of Oz, identifies the Wicked Witches, gives Dorothy the Silver Shoes of the dead Wicked Witch of the East (002-012), and bestows the protective kiss that shields Dorothy from harm throughout her journey (002-054). She is the story's first mentor figure — the one who sets Dorothy on the Yellow Brick Road.

## Description

The Witch of the North is described as "a little old woman, who wore a round white cap and a long white gown" (002-006). Her age and gentle appearance belie her authority — she speaks with calm certainty about the history and powers of Oz. She is kind without being sentimental: her kiss is a matter-of-fact protection, not an emotional gesture.

Her defining trait is clarity. She explains the Land of Oz to Dorothy with precision: four countries (Munchkin, Gillikin, Quadling, Winkie), two good witches (North and South), two wicked witches (East and West), and the Wizard at the center (002-030–002-034). She cannot send Dorothy home herself — "I am not as powerful as the Wicked Witch of the East was" (002-039) — but she provides both the means (the Silver Shoes) and the protection (the kiss) for Dorothy's journey.

## Role in the Story

### The Welcome

When Dorothy's house lands in Munchkin Country, the Witch of the North arrives to explain what has happened. She identifies the dead Wicked Witch of the East, praises Dorothy as a sorceress (which Dorothy denies), and gives her the Silver Shoes: "They are yours, and you shall have them to wear" (002-012).

### The Kiss

Before Dorothy departs, the Witch "bent down and kissed Dorothy on the forehead, where her lips touched the girl they left a round, shining mark" (002-054). The kiss is a powerful charm: "No one will dare injure a person who has been kissed by the Witch of the North" (002-055). This protection remains in effect throughout the entire story — the Wicked Witch of the West trembles at the sight of it on Dorothy's forehead (012-059).

### The Guidance

The Witch cannot send Dorothy home directly, but she provides the path: "You must walk. It is a long journey, through a country that is sometimes pleasant and sometimes dark and terrible. However, I will use all the magic arts I know of to keep you from harm" (002-053–002-054). She points Dorothy toward the Emerald City and the Wizard — a destination that, unknown to her, will lead not to the Wizard's power but to Dorothy's own.

## Relationships

### Dorothy Gale

The Witch treats Dorothy with avuncular kindness. She does not patronize the child or overwhelm her with magic — she provides information, gifts, and protection, then steps aside.

### The Wicked Witch of the East

The Witch of the North's enemy and counterpart. The East Witch's death at Dorothy's hands (or rather, at the hands of Dorothy's house) is the event that brings the Witch of the North to greet Dorothy.

### Glinda the Good Witch

The Witch of the South, and the Witch of the North's fellow Good Witch. Together they form the two poles of goodness in Oz. The Witch of the North begins the journey; Glinda ends it.

### The Wizard of Oz

The Witch of the North speaks of Oz with respect: "Oz himself is the Great Wizard" (002-032). Whether she knows he is a humbug is ambiguous — but her description is cautious: she whispers his name and notes that he is "more powerful than all the rest of us together" (002-033), which may be either genuine belief or diplomatic tact.

## Notes

The Witch of the North is one of the story's most ambiguous figures. She kisses Dorothy without asking, marks her without explaining, and sends her on a dangerous journey when she could presumably offer more direct help. Her powers are never fully defined — she is "not as powerful" as the Wicked Witches, yet her kiss is powerful enough to make the Wicked Witch of the West tremble. Like many fairy-tale mentors, she provides what she can and trusts the heroine to do the rest. Her kiss — a round, shining mark that never fades — is the story's literal and symbolic blessing, the magic that says: you are protected. Go.
