## Overview

The **black bees** are the third and final conventional weapon deployed by the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] against [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] and her companions. Summoned by three blasts on the [[silver-whistle|silver whistle]] after the failure of the [[wolves-of-the-wicked-witch|wolves]] and [[wild-crows|wild crows]], the swarm is defeated when the bees break their stings against the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]]'s tin body while Dorothy and the others are protected under the [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]]'s straw. Like bees whose stings are broken, they all perish.

## Summoning

Enraged by the destruction of both her wolves and her crows, the Wicked Witch blows her silver whistle three times:

> Forthwith there was heard a great buzzing in the air, and a swarm of black bees came flying toward her. [012-032]

She commands them: "Go to the strangers and sting them to death!" [012-033].

## The Attack and Defeat

The Scarecrow, seeing the bees coming, devises a strategy. He instructs the Tin Woodman to "take out my straw and scatter it over the little girl and the dog and the Lion, and the bees cannot sting them" [012-034]. The Woodman does so, covering Dorothy, Toto, and the Cowardly Lion entirely with straw [012-034].

The bees, finding no one but the Tin Woodman exposed, fly at him:

> The bees came and found no one but the Woodman to sting, so they flew at him and broke off all their stings against the tin, without hurting the Woodman at all. And as bees cannot live when their stings are broken that was the end of the black bees, and they lay scattered thick about the Woodman, like little heaps of fine coal. [012-035]

The swarm is annihilated, every bee dead from the loss of its sting.

## Aftermath

The Witch's fury reaches a peak when she discovers her bees destroyed:

> The Wicked Witch was so angry when she saw her black bees in little heaps like fine coal that she stamped her foot and tore her hair and gnashed her teeth. [012-037]

With her wolves, crows, and bees all dead, and her Winkie slaves frightened away by the [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]], the Witch is forced to employ her last remaining power—the [[golden-cap|Golden Cap]]—to summon the [[winged-monkey-band|Winged Monkeys]] [012-040].

In a final desperate move, she sends a dozen Winkie slaves armed with sharp spears against the travelers, but they too flee when the Lion roars [012-037–012-039].

## Significance

The black bees represent the third and cleverest of the Witch's natural weapons, defeated by the Scarecrow's strategic thinking rather than brute force. The sequence of three defenses—Tin Woodman's axe (wolves), Scarecrow's physical prowess (crows), and Scarecrow's ingenuity plus Tin Woodman's invulnerability (bees)—showcases the complementary abilities of Dorothy's companions. The bees' destruction is also thematically resonant: they are destroyed by their own weapon (their stings), turning the Witch's violent magic back upon itself.
