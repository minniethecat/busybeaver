# Golden Cap

## Overview

The [[golden-cap|Golden Cap]] is a powerful magical artifact made of gold with a circle of diamonds and rubies running around it (012-040). It is first introduced as belonging to the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]], who keeps it in her cupboard alongside other magical items. The cap grants its wearer the power to command the [[winged-monkey-band|Winged Monkeys]] three times, making it one of the most strategically significant magical items in the [[land-of-oz|Land of Oz]]. Its provenance passes through four owners — Quelala, the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]], [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]], and [[glinda]] — before [[glinda]] frees the [[winged-monkey-band|Winged Monkeys]] from its enchantment forever.

## Properties

The Golden Cap is a circlet of gold adorned with a circle of diamonds and rubies (012-040). It is stored in a cupboard when not in use and can be worn on the head like any cap. [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] finds it fits her comfortably when she tries it on (013-029).

The cap's magical properties include:

- **Three commands over the [[winged-monkey-band|Winged Monkeys]]**: Whoever owns the cap may call upon the Winged Monkeys three times to perform any task (012-040).
- **Charm words inscribed inside**: The activation charm is written on the lining of the cap, which [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] discovers when she looks inside (014-020).
- **Ritual activation**: The wearer must stand on the left foot and recite specific words to summon the monkeys (012-041).
- **Transferability**: The cap passes from owner to owner, each receiving their own allotment of three commands. The monkeys are bound to obey whoever possesses the cap (014-040).

## Role in the Story

The Golden Cap is first introduced as a possession of the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]], stored in her cupboard alongside other magical items (012-040). The Witch uses the cap to command the [[winged-monkey-band|Winged Monkeys]] to destroy the [[scarecrow]] and the [[tin-woodman]] and to capture [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] and the [[cowardly-lion]] (012-041).

After [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] destroys the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] with a bucket of water in the [[melting-of-the-wicked-witch-of-the-west|dramatic melting]], she discovers the Golden Cap while filling her basket with food for the journey back to the [[emerald-city]]. She tries it on and finds it fits perfectly, unaware of its power (013-029).

> "There was, in her cupboard, a Golden Cap, with a circle of diamonds and rubies running round it. This Golden Cap had a charm. Whoever owned it could call upon the Winged Monkeys, who would obey any order they were given. But no person could command these strange creatures more than three times." (012-040)

The [[queen-of-the-field-mice|Queen of the Field Mice]] notices [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] wearing the Golden Cap and is startled. She explains that the charm words are written inside the cap, and warns that if Dorothy summons the [[winged-monkey-band|Winged Monkeys]], the [[field-mice|mice]] must flee, for the monkeys are "full of mischief and think it great fun to chase us" (014-017).

Through the Winged Monkeys' explanation, the history of the Golden Cap is revealed: it was originally crafted for Quelala, a handsome young man who was to marry a beautiful princess. As a wedding gift, the cap was enchanted so that Quelala could command the Winged Monkeys three times (014-038). The cap eventually passed to the Wicked Witch of the West, who used the monkeys to enslave the [[wicked-witchs-winkies|Winkies]] and later to drive the [[wizard-of-oz|Wizard of Oz]] out of the Winkie Country (014-041).

Dorothy uses her first command to have the Winged Monkeys carry her and her companions back to the [[emerald-city]] (014-020). She later uses her second command to summon the monkeys to carry her across the desert to [[kansas]], but the King of the Monkeys explains they cannot cross the desert surrounding Oz (018-023). Dorothy is distraught, believing she has "wasted the charm of the Golden Cap to no purpose" (018-023).

With her final command, Dorothy summons the Winged Monkeys to carry her companions to their respective destinations after the [[wizards-departure|Wizard's departure]] (022-017).

At the story's conclusion, [[glinda|Glinda the Good Witch]] asks for the Golden Cap as payment for telling [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] how to return to [[kansas]] (023-009). Glinda uses her three commands to transport Dorothy's companions to their new homes, and then:

> "Then, having used up the powers of the Golden Cap, I shall give it to the King of the Monkeys, that he and his band may thereafter be free for evermore." (023-022)

## Notes

The Golden Cap functions as a narrative device that enables crucial plot developments while also providing an origin story for the [[winged-monkey-band|Winged Monkeys]] and their bondage. The cap's ultimate fate — being given to the King of the Winged Monkeys by [[glinda]] to free them — resolves one of the story's minor tragedies: the monkeys were never inherently wicked but were compelled to serve wicked masters. The number three, recurring throughout the novel (three companions, three commands, three steps of the [[silver-shoes]]), reinforces the fairy-tale structure of Baum's narrative. The cap is also notable as perhaps the only magical object in the story whose enchantment is intentionally *undone* — a rare happy ending for a magical artifact.
