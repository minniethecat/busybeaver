## Overview

The **Great Head** is the first of three illusory forms assumed by the [[wizard-of-oz|Wizard of Oz]] to intimidate visitors to his Throne Room. A giant disembodied Head floating without visible body or support, it is the form that greets [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] when she first requests an audience with the Great Oz. The Head is later revealed to be a projection manipulated by the Wizard through ventriloquism and a screen.

## Dorothy's Audience

When Dorothy enters the Throne Room, she is confronted by an astonishing sight:

> In the center of the chair was an enormous Head, without a body to support it or any arms or legs whatever. There was no hair upon this head, but it had eyes and a nose and mouth, and was much bigger than the head of the biggest giant. [011-026]

The Head's eyes turn slowly to look at Dorothy, and its mouth moves as it speaks in a voice "that seemed to come from somewhere near the top of the great dome" [011-027]:

> "I am Oz, the Great and Terrible. Who are you, and why do you seek me?" [011-028]

Dorothy, though frightened, manages to state her request: to return to Kansas. The Head tells her she must first kill the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] before any help will be given [011-034].

## The Companions' Audiences

Each of Dorothy's companions sees a different form of the Wizard:
- [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]] sees a lovely Lady seated upon the throne [011-050]
- [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] sees a most terrible Beast [011-074]
- [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]] sees a Ball of Fire [011-089]

The Head form is reserved for Dorothy—the Wizard tailoring his persona to each visitor's expectations and fears.

## The Revelation

When the Wizard is finally unmasked by [[toto|Toto]] knocking over a screen in the corner of the Throne Room, the mechanism of the Head is revealed: "Oz the Terrible... was a little old man, with a bald head and a wrinkled face" [015-050]. The Great Head, like all his forms, was a ventriloquist's illusion:

> "I am a humbug," said Oz, in a meek voice. [015-057]

## Significance

The Great Head is the most iconic of the Wizard's three forms. It represents authority as pure spectacle—a voice without a body, a presence without substance. The Head is Oz at his most terrible and most fraudulent, and its unveiling is the story's central demystification of power.
