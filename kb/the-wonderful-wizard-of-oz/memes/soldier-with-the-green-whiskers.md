## Overview

The soldier with the green whiskers is the palace guard of Oz, stationed before the door of the Royal Palace in the Emerald City. Distinguished by his green uniform and long green beard, he serves as the intermediary between visitors and the Great Oz, delivering messages and escorting guests to their audiences.

## Description

The soldier with the green whiskers wears a green uniform and sports a notably long green beard, from which he derives his distinguishing epithet [011-005]. Like all inhabitants of the Emerald City, his skin has a greenish tint. He carries a green whistle used to summon attendants within the Palace [011-014].

## Role in the Story

When the Guardian of the Gates first brings Dorothy and her companions to the Palace of Oz, the soldier with the green whiskers is standing guard at the door [011-005]. The Guardian informs him that the travelers demand to see the Great Oz, and the soldier relays the message, ultimately securing them an audience.

During the travelers' stay at the Palace, the soldier serves as Oz's messenger. On successive mornings, he comes to summon the Scarecrow [011-053], the Tin Woodman [011-067], and the Cowardly Lion [011-080] to their individual audiences with the Wizard, each time relaying Oz's instructions and escorting them to the Throne Room.

After Dorothy and her companions return from destroying the Wicked Witch of the West, the soldier with the green whiskers admits them to the Palace [015-013]. When the Wizard is reluctant to see them, the soldier carries the Scarecrow's threatening message demanding an audience, which frightens Oz into compliance [015-014]. On the appointed morning, the green-whiskered soldier comes for them at precisely nine o'clock [015-016].

When the Wizard prepares his hot-air balloon to depart Oz, he sends the soldier with the green whiskers to fetch a large clothes basket to serve as the balloon's passenger compartment [017-020]. Later, when the balloon accidentally launches without Dorothy, the Scarecrow suggests consulting the soldier for advice on how Dorothy might return to Kansas [018-026].

When Dorothy finally departs the Emerald City for her journey south to Glinda, the soldier with the green whiskers walks with the travelers as far as the city gate, shaking hands with each of them [019-002].

## Relationships

The soldier works alongside the [[guardian-of-the-gate]], who introduces Dorothy's party to him at the Palace [011-006]. He serves at the pleasure of the [[wizard-of-oz]], acting as messenger and palace guard. He also interacts with [[the-beautiful-green-girl]], another Palace attendant, and shows courtesy to all of Dorothy's companions.

## Notes

The soldier with the green whiskers is one of the Emerald City's few named functionaries, forming the bureaucratic infrastructure that sustains the Wizard's authority alongside the Guardian of the Gates. In later Oz books by L. Frank Baum, this character is given the personal name "Omby Amby," although that name does not appear in *The Wonderful Wizard of Oz* itself. He is a uniformly courteous and helpful figure, and his green beard serves as a memorable visual marker within the monochromatic green aesthetic of the Emerald City.
