# Munchkins

## Overview

The [[munchkins|Munchkins]] are the inhabitants of the eastern quadrant of the [[land-of-oz|Land of Oz]], known as Munchkin Country. They are described as diminutive in stature — Dorothy notes they are "about as tall as Dorothy, who was a well-grown child for her age" (002-013) — and were for many years held in bondage by the [[wicked-witch-of-the-east|Wicked Witch of the East]]. Their liberation when [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy Gale's]] house lands on and kills the Witch sets the entire story in motion (002-008). The Munchkins are characterized by their love of the color blue, their agricultural prosperity, and their grateful, kindhearted nature.

## Description

The Munchkins are a peaceful, hardworking people who, under the Witch's tyranny, "had been made to work hard during the night as well as the day" (013-003 — this passage refers to the Winkies, but sets a parallel pattern of witch-enslaved peoples). Once freed, they demonstrate their gratitude to [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]]: "the kindhearted Munchkins... immediately took out their handkerchiefs and wept as if they could not be consoled" when Dorothy began crying (002-044). The Munchkins wear peaked hats that were described as "a little higher than one's head with little bells around the brims that tinkled sweetly as they moved" and dress predominantly in blue — "blue is the color of the Munchkins" as [[boq|Boq]] explains (003-021). All the houses and fences in Munchkin Country are painted "a dainty blue" (003-013).

Evidently the Munchkins were good farmers, with fields of grain and vegetables in abundance (003-013). They kept neat fences at the sides of the road (003-013). Their farming prowess supports a settled, agricultural society. The Munchkins are also organized politically — they have a [[council-of-munchkins|Council of Munchkins]] who received Dorothy after her arrival (002-001) and sent a swift messenger to summon the [[witch-of-the-north|Good Witch of the North]] upon the Witch of the East's death (002-020).

## Notable Munchkins

- **[[boq|Boq]]**: One of the richest Munchkins in the land, who hosts Dorothy overnight at his house, where she enjoys a lavish meal and watches a "wee Munchkin baby" play with [[toto|Toto]] (003-016, 003-024).
- **[[council-of-munchkins|Council of Munchkins]]**: The civic leadership who greet Dorothy upon her arrival and verify that the Wicked Witch of the East is truly dead (002-001, 002-033).
- **[[munchkin-farmers|Munchkin Farmers]]**: The agricultural class who maintain the rich farmland — "Evidently the Munchkins were good farmers" (003-013). One particular farmer made the [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]] and was later advised by a fellow Munchkin to give the Scarecrow eyes (003-056, 004-014).
- **A beautiful Munchkin maiden**: Mentioned by the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] as the girl he once loved before the [[wicked-witch-of-the-east|Wicked Witch of the East]] enchanted his axe — "I will go back to the Munchkin maiden and marry her" (005-045–005-048).

## Quotes

> The little old woman... told her all about the Munchkins, who lived in the eastern part of the Land of Oz, and about the Wicked Witch of the East who had ruled them. (002-008)

> She was an old woman, who had lived in the land of the Munchkins for many years. She told Dorothy that the Munchkins dressed always in blue, and were a good and kindly people. (003-011)

> "Blue is the color of the Munchkins, and white is the witch color. So we know you are a friendly witch." — Boq (003-021)

## Notes

The Munchkins are one of the four color-coded peoples of Oz (blue for the East). In the 1939 film adaptation, the Munchkins were famously portrayed by the Singer Midgets and given the musical number "Ding-Dong! The Witch Is Dead."
