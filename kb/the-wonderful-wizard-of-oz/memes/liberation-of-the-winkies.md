# Liberation of the Winkies

## Overview

Following the Wicked Witch's melting, the Winkies celebrate their freedom and, at their own request, accept the Tin Woodman as their new ruler, with promises to serve him faithfully. Taking place in **[[winkie-country|Winkie Country]]** (Chapter 013), this event marks a significant moment in Dorothy's journey through the Land of Oz.

## Background

The event occurs within the context of the journey through Oz. It was precipitated by: [[melting-of-the-wicked-witch-of-the-west|Melting Of The Wicked Witch Of The West]].

## Notes

This event reflects key themes in Baum's narrative: the triumph of resourcefulness over brute force, the power of belief and perception, and the importance of companionship. In the context of American children's literature at the turn of the century, such episodes emphasize moral growth and self-discovery through adventure.
