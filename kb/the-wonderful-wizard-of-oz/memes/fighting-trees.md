The **Fighting Trees** are a defensive barrier encountered by Dorothy and her companions during their journey to the Land of the South. These animated trees line the edge of a thick wood beyond the Emerald City, using their branches to seize, throw, and repel anyone attempting to pass through.

> The Scarecrow, who was in the lead, finally discovered a big tree with such wide-spreading branches that there was room for the party to pass underneath. So he walked forward to the tree, but just as he came under the first branches they bent down and twined around him, and the next minute he was raised from the ground and flung headlong among his fellow travelers. [019-017]

## Encounter

After leaving the Emerald City and traveling south, the group reached a thick wood that could not be circumvented. The Scarecrow, taking the lead, attempted to pass under a wide-spreading tree but was immediately seized and thrown back.

> "Let me try it first," said the Scarecrow, "for it doesn't hurt me to get thrown about." He walked up to another tree, as he spoke, but its branches immediately seized him and tossed him back again. [019-019]

Dorothy was alarmed by this hostile behavior, and the Lion concluded that the trees were deliberately trying to block their path.

> "The trees seem to have made up their minds to fight us, and stop our journey," remarked the Lion. [019-022]

## Defeat

The Tin Woodman took the initiative, approaching the first tree with his axe. When a branch bent down to seize him, he chopped it in two. The tree reacted by shaking all its branches as if in pain, and the Woodman passed safely underneath.

> When a big branch bent down to seize him the Woodman chopped at it so fiercely that he cut it in two. At once the tree began shaking all its branches as if in pain, and the Tin Woodman passed safely under it. [019-021]

The group then ran through the gap. Toto was briefly caught by a smaller branch and shaken until he howled, but the Woodman swiftly freed him with another chop.

> "Come on!" he shouted to the others. "Be quick!" They all ran forward and passed under the tree without injury, except Toto, who was caught by a small branch and shaken until he howled. But the Woodman promptly chopped off the branch and set the little dog free. [019-023]

## Nature

After passing through, the travelers observed that only the first row of trees possessed this aggressive power. They speculated that these trees functioned as "policemen of the forest," endowed with their power to keep strangers out.

> The other trees of the forest did nothing to keep them back, so they made up their minds that only the first row of trees could bend down their branches, and that probably these were the policemen of the forest, and given this wonderful power in order to keep strangers out of it. [019-024]

This made the Fighting Trees one of several magical obstacles encountered on the journey south, preceding the [[china-country|Dainty China Country]] and ultimately leading toward the [[quadlings|Quadling]] lands.
