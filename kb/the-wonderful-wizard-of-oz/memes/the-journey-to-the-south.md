## Overview

The Journey to the South is the final leg of the companions' travels through the [[land-of-oz|Land of Oz]], undertaken after the [[wizard-of-oz|Wizard of Oz]] departed in his balloon without [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] [017-020]. The journey takes them through some of the most dangerous and wondrous regions of Oz on their way to find [[glinda-the-good-witch|Glinda the Good Witch]] in the [[quadling-country|Quadling Country]].

## Departure

After the Wizard's departure, the companions were despondent. The [[soldier-with-the-green-whiskers|Soldier with the Green Whiskers]] suggested that [[glinda-the-good-witch|Glinda]], the Witch of the South, "might help you to find your way home" [017-025]. "The journey to the South Country is long and dangerous," he warned, but Dorothy resolved to go.

## Obstacles Along the Way

The journey southward presented three major obstacles:

The **[[fighting-trees|Fighting Trees]]** guarded the entrance to the southern forest, lashing out with their branches to prevent passage [019-002]. The [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] chopped through them with his axe, clearing a path.

The **[[dainty-china-country|Dainty China Country]]** presented a different challenge — not danger, but the fragility of its inhabitants. When [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] accidentally broke the leg of a china cow and cracked the [[the-china-clown|China Clown]], the [[the-china-princess|China Princess]] begged them to "be careful, for you might break us all" [020-015]. The companions passed through as gently as possible.

The **[[hammer-head-hill|Hammer-Heads]]** were the most dangerous obstacle — creatures with no arms who shot their heads out on spring-like necks to knock travelers down the hill [022-005]. The [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]] was knocked unconscious before the [[winged-monkey-band|Winged Monkeys]] (summoned by Dorothy with her last use of the [[golden-cap|Golden Cap]]) carried the companions over the hill.

## Arrival

The companions finally reached [[glindas-palace|Glinda's Palace]] in the [[quadling-country|Quadling Country]], where [[glinda-the-good-witch|Glinda the Good Witch]] received them warmly and ultimately revealed the secret of the [[silver-shoes|Silver Shoes]] — that Dorothy had possessed the power to return home all along.

## Significance

The Journey to the South is the final test of the companions' bond. Each of the three obstacles requires a different strength — the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]]'s combat skill, the group's collective gentleness, and the [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]]'s bravery in first attempting the passage. The journey completes the narrative arc that began with Dorothy's arrival in Oz.
