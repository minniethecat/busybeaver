## Introduction

The Yellow Brick Road is the primary thoroughfare connecting the Munchkin Country in the East of Oz to the Emerald City at its center. It serves as the defining geographic feature for Dorothy's journey through the first part of the Land of Oz. The Witch of the North tells Dorothy that "the road to the City of Emeralds is paved with yellow brick, so you cannot miss it" [002-058].

## Route and Geography

The road begins in the Munchkin Country, the eastern region of Oz where Dorothy's house lands after the cyclone. It extends westward toward the center of the country, passing through diverse landscapes before reaching the Emerald City [002-058].

Along its course, the road grows increasingly rough and uneven. In more remote stretches, the yellow bricks are "very uneven," sometimes broken or missing altogether, leaving holes in the roadway [004-002]. The farms and settlements along the road become fewer and less prosperous the farther one travels from the Munchkin settlements [004-003].

## Dorothy's Journey

Dorothy begins her walk along the Yellow Brick Road immediately after receiving the Witch of the North's instructions and the protective kiss upon her forehead. At the start, she is optimistic:

> As she walked along, she noticed the bright yellow bricks and hoped they would lead her to Oz, the Great Wizard, who would send her back to Kansas [003-001].

The road is where Dorothy meets each of her three companions: the Scarecrow in a cornfield beside the road [003-014], the Tin Woodman standing rusted in the forest through which the road passes [005-012], and the Cowardly Lion in the darker parts of the forest [006-005]. Each joins her on the Yellow Brick Road toward the Emerald City.

## Condition and Maintenance

Not all sections of the road are well-maintained. The Scarecrow, having no brains to guide him around hazards, frequently stumbles over uneven bricks and falls into holes where bricks are missing [004-002]. Dorothy must pick him up and set him on his feet each time. The deterioration suggests that travel along the road is infrequent outside the Munchkin Country.

## Symbolic Significance

The Yellow Brick Road represents the path of adventure and self-discovery. Dorothy follows it believing it will lead her to a wizard who can send her home, but the journey itself transforms her companions. The Scarecrow gains the confidence to call himself a ruler, the Tin Woodman earns a kingdom, and the Lion proves his courage — all before any of them realize they are walking not toward a wizard with magical solutions, but toward the realization of their own capabilities.
