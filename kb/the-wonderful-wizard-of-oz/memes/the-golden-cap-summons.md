## Overview

The Golden Cap Summons is the decisive attack in Chapter XII when the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]], having exhausted her conventional forces, uses the second of her three wishes with the [[golden-cap|Golden Cap]] to command the [[winged-monkey-band|Winged Monkeys]] to capture or destroy the companions.

## The Summons

After the failure of the [[wolves-of-the-wicked-witch|wolves]], [[wild-crows|wild crows]], and [[black-bees|black bees]], the Witch realized she could not stop the travelers with ordinary means. "This is my last resource," she declared, taking the [[golden-cap|Golden Cap]] from a cupboard. Following the cap's ritual, she placed it on her head, stood on her left foot, and chanted: "Ep-pe, pep-pe, kak-ke! Hil-lo, hol-lo, hel-lo! Ziz-zy, zuz-zy, zik!" [012-031].

The sky darkened as "a band of black-winged monkeys, with the [[the-king-of-the-winged-monkeys|King of the Winged Monkeys]] at their head, came flying toward her" [012-033]. The Witch commanded them to "go to the strangers who are within my land and destroy them all, except the Lion" — whom she still hoped to harness — and "bring that girl to me, for she is the owner of the [[silver-shoes|Silver Shoes]]" [012-034].

## The Attack

The Winged Monkeys swooped upon the companions from every direction. They seized the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] and "dropped him from a great height upon sharp rocks far below" [012-036], leaving him "so battered and dented that he could neither move nor groan" [012-036]. They tore the [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]] apart, "throwing his clothes into the branches of a tall tree and scattering his straw to the winds" [012-037]. They bound the [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]] with ropes and carried him to the Witch's castle, and brought [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] — still holding [[toto|Toto]] in her arms — directly to the Witch.

The [[the-king-of-the-winged-monkeys|King of the Winged Monkeys]] looked curiously at the mark on Dorothy's forehead — the [[kiss-of-the-witch-of-the-north|kiss of the Witch of the North]] — and said to himself: "We dare not harm this little girl, for she is protected by the Power of Good, and that is greater than the Power of Evil" [012-039].

## Aftermath

This event scattered the companions — the first time they had been separated since forming their party. However, the Witch's apparent victory was short-lived. Dorothy's enslavement in the kitchen led directly to the Witch's accidental destruction and the companions' eventual reunion.

## Significance

The Golden Cap Summons demonstrates both the terrifying power of magical compulsion and its limits. Even under the Golden Cap's enchantment, the Winged Monkeys recognize and respect the protective mark of goodness on Dorothy [012-039], foreshadowing the ultimate triumph of good over evil in the story.
