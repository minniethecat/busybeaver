## Overview

The Beautiful Green Girl is a palace attendant in the Emerald City who serves as the primary intermediary between visitors and the Wizard of Oz. She escorts guests to their rooms, announces audiences with Oz, relays messages, and provides for their comfort during their stay. Her kindness and attentiveness make her one of the more welcoming presences in the Palace.

## Description

The green girl is consistently described as "pretty" and "beautiful," dressed in green attire befitting the Emerald City's color scheme [019-002]. She wears a green silk gown [011-096] and moves with practiced efficiency through the Palace's passages and stairways [011-014]. Her demeanor is courteous and helpful; she addresses visitors warmly and ensures their needs are met [011-017]. She also demonstrates practical kindness, as when she fills Dorothy's basket with provisions for the journey to the West [011-101].

## Role in the Story

The green girl first appears when Dorothy and her companions arrive at the Palace of Oz. The soldier with the green whiskers summons her with a green whistle, and she leads Dorothy through seven passages and up three flights of stairs to a luxurious green room prepared for her stay [011-014–015]. She tells Dorothy to make herself at home and explains that Oz will send for her the next morning [011-017]. She similarly escorts the Scarecrow, Tin Woodman, and Cowardly Lion to their own rooms [011-040].

The following morning, the green girl announces Oz's readiness to receive Dorothy [011-024]. She opens the door to the Throne Room, and Dorothy enters alone to meet the Wizard for the first time [011-054].

After Dorothy weeps upon learning she must kill the Wicked Witch of the West, the green girl warns her that tears will spot her green silk gown [011-096]. Before the companions depart for the West, the green girl fills Dorothy's basket with food and fastens a small bell with a green ribbon around Toto's neck [011-101].

When the travelers return to the Emerald City after destroying the Wicked Witch, the green girl greets them once more and shows them to their old rooms to rest [015-030]. Later, when Oz ignores them for days, the Scarecrow asks the green girl to carry a message threatening to summon the Winged Monkeys — a message that finally frightens Oz into granting them an audience [015-032].

On the morning Dorothy and her friends depart the Emerald City to journey south, Dorothy kisses the pretty green girl goodbye before they shake hands with the green-whiskered soldier and pass through the city gate [019-002].

## Notes

The green girl is never given a personal name in the text; she is referred to only by her description. She exemplifies the orderly, service-oriented culture of the Emerald City under Oz's rule. Her green attire, like the green spectacles locked upon visitors at the gate, reinforces the city's enforced color uniformity. Despite her subordinate role, she exercises a degree of practical authority as the gatekeeper of access to Oz.
