## Overview

The Lazy Old Woman is the guardian of the [[munchkin-maiden|Munchkin Maiden]] and the indirect cause of the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]]'s transformation. Her selfish desire to keep the maiden at home to perform household labor led her to bargain with the [[wicked-witch-of-the-east|Wicked Witch of the East]], setting in motion the events that would strip the young woodchopper of his natural body.

## Role in the Story

The old woman appears only within the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]]'s account of his origin. She lived with the [[munchkin-maiden|Munchkin Maiden]] and "did not want her to marry anyone, for she was so lazy she wished the girl to remain with her and do the cooking and the housework" [005-045].

To prevent the marriage between the maiden and the young woodchopper, the old woman approached the [[wicked-witch-of-the-east|Wicked Witch of the East]] and "promised her two sheep and a cow if she would prevent the marriage" [005-045]. The Witch accepted this bargain and enchanted the woodchopper's axe, leading to his gradual dismemberment and transformation into the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]].

After the transformation, the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] lost his [[heart]] and with it his love for the maiden. He speculated: "I suppose she is still living with the old woman, waiting for me to come after her" [005-047].

## Motivation

The old woman's motivation is purely selfish: she is "so lazy she wished the girl to remain with her and do the cooking and the housework" [005-045]. She is willing to sacrifice the young woman's happiness and future for her own convenience, offering livestock as payment to the Wicked Witch to ensure the marriage never takes place.

## Relationships

- **[[munchkin-maiden|Munchkin Maiden]]** — The old woman's ward. She exploited the maiden's labor and actively prevented her marriage.
- **[[wicked-witch-of-the-east|Wicked Witch of the East]]** — Co-conspirator. The Witch accepted payment of two sheep and a cow in exchange for enchanting the woodchopper's axe.
- **[[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]]** — The victim of her scheme. His entire transformation from flesh to tin was set in motion by her deal with the Witch.

## Notes

The Lazy Old Woman is never named in *The Wonderful Wizard of Oz*. She represents the theme of selfishness standing in opposition to love and happiness. Her bargain with the Witch — two sheep and a cow in exchange for ruining a young couple's future — underscores the petty cruelty that enables greater evil in the world of Oz.
