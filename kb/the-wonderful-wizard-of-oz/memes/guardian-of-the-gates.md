# Guardian of the Gates

## Overview

The Guardian of the Gates is the green-clad, green-whiskered soldier who controls entry to the Emerald City. He is the first resident of the city whom Dorothy and her companions encounter, and he serves as both gatekeeper and enforcer of the city's most peculiar custom: the requirement that all visitors wear locked green spectacles (010-053). Though a minor character, he appears at two critical junctures of the narrative — the travelers' initial arrival and their triumphant return — giving him a framing role in the Emerald City sequence.

## Description

The Guardian of the Gates is a small man dressed entirely in green, with a long green beard (010-052). He is initially described simply as "the green man" (010-053). He carries a large golden key for the city gate and wears around his neck a chain with a smaller key that locks the spectacles onto visitors' heads (010-056). His manner is courteous but bureaucratic; he follows the city's procedures without deviation, requiring every visitor — including Toto the dog — to don the locked green glasses before entering (010-057).

## Role in the Story

Dorothy and her companions first encounter the Guardian after crossing the poppy field and arriving at the Emerald City's outer wall. They ring the bell, and the Guardian opens a small window to inspect them. Upon learning they wish to see the Great Oz, he informs them of Oz's fearsome reputation: "to those who are not honest, or who approach him from curiosity, he is most terrible" (010-053). Nevertheless, he admits them after fitting each traveler with locked green spectacles designed to protect their eyes from the city's brilliance (010-056).

The Guardian then escorts the group through the city streets to the Palace, where he hands them over to a soldier with green whiskers — who is either the same man in a dual role or a closely related palace guard (010-058). He reappears when the travelers return after destroying the Wicked Witch of the West, again fitting them with spectacles and admitting them through the gate (015-012). The people of the Emerald City gather when they learn from him that the Witch has been destroyed (015-012).

## Relationships

The Guardian works under the authority of the **[[wizard-of-oz|Wizard of Oz]]**, carrying out the city's entry protocol without question. He interacts respectfully but distantly with **[[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]]** and her **[[scarecrow|Scarecrow]]**, **[[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]]**, and **[[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]]** companions. His relationship with the Wizard is purely administrative; he serves as the first layer of controlled access to the Palace.

## Quotes

> "I am the Guardian of the Gates, and since you demand to see the Great Oz I must take you to his Palace. But first you must put on the spectacles." (010-053)

> He opened the big box, and Dorothy saw that it was filled with spectacles of every size and shape. All of them had green glasses in them. (010-056)

> When the people heard from the Guardian of the Gates that Dorothy had melted the Wicked Witch of the West, they all gathered around the travelers and followed them in a great crowd to the Palace of Oz. (015-012)

## Notes

The Guardian of the Gates and the "soldier with the green whiskers" (015-013) who stands guard before the Palace door may be the same character or two distinct figures in identical livery. The text is ambiguous: both are green-clad, green-bearded men who carry keys, but the Guardian works at the city gate while the soldier is stationed at the Palace door. The recurrence of the green-whiskered motif reinforces the Emerald City's aesthetic uniformity, where everything and everyone must be green — a policy enforced literally through the locked spectacles.
