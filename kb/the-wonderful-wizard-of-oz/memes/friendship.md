## Overview

Friendship is one of the central themes of *The Wonderful Wizard of Oz*. The bond that forms between [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]], the [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]], the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]], and the [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]] transforms a group of strangers into a deeply loyal fellowship whose mutual protection and shared sacrifice drive the story's emotional core.

## Formation of the Bond

The friendship develops gradually as each companion joins the journey. The [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]] volunteers to accompany [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] to the [[emerald-city|Emerald City]] after she frees him from his pole [003-010], hoping the [[wizard-of-oz|Wizard]] will give him [[brain|brains]]. When they free the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] from his rusted state, he asks to join, and Dorothy replies warmly: "Come along" [005-032]. The [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]], after being scolded by Dorothy for attacking [[toto|Toto]], meekly asks to join their quest for [[courage|courage]] [006-012].

## Expressions of Loyalty

Each companion consistently places the welfare of others above their own. The [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]] repeatedly volunteers for dangerous tasks, reasoning that he cannot be hurt since he is stuffed with straw. The [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] fights fiercely, single-handedly defeating the [[wolves-of-the-wicked-witch|wolves]] [012-026] and the [[wild-crows|wild crows]] [012-028] sent by the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]]. When [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] and the [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]] are overcome by the [[deadly-poppy-field|poppy field]]'s scent, the [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]] and [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] carry the unconscious Lion to safety while the [[queen-of-the-field-mice|Queen of the Field Mice]] and her subjects rescue Dorothy [008-030].

The [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]], despite his self-proclaimed cowardice, leaps across the [[the-great-ditch|Great Ditch]] carrying each companion on his back [007-024] and later battles the [[great-spider|giant spider]] to save the [[the-beasts-of-the-forest|beasts of the forest]] [021-010].

## The Power of Friendship

The companions' friendship proves stronger than any magical threat they face. When they demand that the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] be destroyed before the [[wizard-of-oz|Wizard]] will grant their wishes, it is their combined effort that succeeds. Ultimately, [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]]'s love for her friends prevents her from leaving Oz with the Wizard in his balloon [017-020], choosing instead to stay until all her companions' wishes are fulfilled.

## Contrast with Isolation

The theme of friendship is underscored by contrast with the isolation of the antagonists. The [[wicked-witch-of-the-east|Wicked Witch of the East]] and [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] rule through fear and enslavement, with no genuine bonds. The [[wizard-of-oz|Wizard]] lives in self-imposed isolation behind his screen, afraid of discovery. The companions' victory comes from their loyalty to one another rather than individual power.
