## Introduction

Glinda the Good Witch is the benevolent ruler of the southern region of Oz, the Country of the Quadlings. She is one of the most powerful magical figures in Oz, distinguished from other witches by her kindness, wisdom, and beauty. Unlike the Wicked Witches of the East and West who use their power for cruelty, Glinda uses hers to guide, protect, and grant wishes to those with pure hearts.

## Appearance and Character

Glinda is described as both beautiful and young in appearance, with rich red hair that falls in flowing ringlets over her shoulders. She wears a pure white dress and has kind blue eyes [023-003]. Her throne is made of rubies, befitting her royal status [023-003].

She is defined not only by her magical power but by her genuine goodness. When Dorothy finishes recounting her adventures and expresses her wish to go home, Glinda leans forward and kisses the "sweet, upturned face of the loving little girl" [023-008]. Her compassion is matched by her practical wisdom — she secures the Golden Cap from Dorothy and immediately plans three commands for the Winged Monkeys to help each of Dorothy's companions [023-016].

## Role in the Story

### Granting Dorothy's Wish

Dorothy and her companions travel all the way to the Land of the South specifically to seek Glinda's help in returning Dorothy to Kansas [022-029]. Upon meeting her, Glinda explains the power of the Silver Shoes: they can carry the wearer anywhere in the world in three steps, each step completed in the wink of an eye [023-030].

### Arranging the Companions' Futures

Before sending Dorothy home, Glinda secures the Golden Cap and uses its three commands to help Dorothy's companions: the Winged Monkeys carry the Scarecrow back to rule the Emerald City, the Tin Woodman to rule the Winkie Country, and the Lion to his forest kingdom [023-010–023-018]. After using the three commands, she declares she will give the Golden Cap to the King of the Winged Monkeys so they may be "free forevermore" [023-018].

### The Final Goodbye

Glinda steps down from her ruby throne to give Dorothy a goodbye kiss, and Dorothy thanks her for all the kindness she has shown to her friends and herself [023-033].

## Powers and Abilities

As a Good Witch, Glinda possesses extensive magical knowledge. She understands the properties of enchanted objects — she knows the Silver Shoes can carry Dorothy across the desert, and she recognizes the Golden Cap's power to command the Winged Monkeys [023-030]. Her magic is protective and enabling rather than coercive or destructive.

## Residence

Glinda lives in a castle in the Land of the South, where she rules over the Quadlings. Dorothy and her companions are received there with hospitality — they are taken to a room to wash and make themselves presentable before being escorted by a soldier girl into Glinda's presence [023-001–023-003].
