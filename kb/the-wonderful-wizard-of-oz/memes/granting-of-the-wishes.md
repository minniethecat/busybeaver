# Granting of the Wishes

## Overview

After being revealed as a humbug, the Wizard gives the Scarecrow a brain of bran and pins, the Tin Woodman a sawdust-stuffed silk heart, and the Lion a green liquid courage — fulfilling their deepest desires through symbolism and belief. Taking place in **[[palace-of-oz|Palace Of Oz]]** (Chapter 016), this event marks a significant moment in Dorothy's journey through the Land of Oz.

## Notes

This event reflects key themes in Baum's narrative: the triumph of resourcefulness over brute force, the power of belief and perception, and the importance of companionship. In the context of American children's literature at the turn of the century, such episodes emphasize moral growth and self-discovery through adventure.
