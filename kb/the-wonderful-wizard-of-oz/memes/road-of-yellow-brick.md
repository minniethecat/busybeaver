# Road of Yellow Brick

## Overview

The Road of Yellow Brick is the central artery of Oz — a paved highway that begins in Munchkin Country and leads directly to the Emerald City. It is the path that Dorothy and her companions follow for most of the novel, and it serves as both a literal road and a symbol of the quest: staying on the road means staying on course, while straying from it leads to adventure and danger.

## Geography

The road begins "not far from" the spot where Dorothy's house lands in Munchkin Country (003-028). It is "paved with yellow brick" (002-041) and runs in a generally westward direction toward the Emerald City. Along the way, it passes through farmland, forests, ravines, and rivers. The road is well-known to all inhabitants — Dorothy receives directions from the **[[witch-of-the-north|Witch of the North]]** and **[[boq|Boq]]** — and its route is unambiguous: "it is paved with yellow brick and you cannot miss it" (002-042).

At one point the road forks around a large ditch, forcing the travelers to make a difficult crossing (007-009). Later, the deadly poppy field borders the road, and the travelers must circumnavigate it after the Lion falls asleep (008-019). Despite these obstacles, the road remains the travelers' anchor throughout their outward journey.

## Inhabitants

The road itself has no inhabitants, but it hosts a succession of travelers and encounters. Dorothy meets the **[[scarecrow|Scarecrow]]** at a cornfield beside the road (003-031), the **[[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]]** in the forest along the road (005-019), and the **[[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]]** blocking the path (006-015). Each companion joins the journey at a road-adjacent location, reinforcing the road's function as a gathering point.

## Role in the Story

The Road of Yellow Brick structures the first half of the novel. Dorothy's instruction — "follow the yellow brick road" (002-042) — defines the quest's geography. Each chapter from III through X tracks the travelers' progress along the road, with deviations into danger (the Kalidah forest, the river, the poppy field) and returns to the path.

When the travelers approach the Emerald City, the road's function shifts from a guide to a confirmation: its straightness and clarity assure them they are nearing their destination. After the Wizard's revelations, the road is no longer needed — the return journey is by balloon, by Winged Monkeys, and ultimately by Silver Shoes, all of which bypass the road entirely.

## Notable Features

- **Yellow Bricks**: The distinctive paving that gives the road its name and color — yellow being the Winkie color, which aligns with the road leading toward the western Emerald City.
- **Fork at the Ditch**: Where the road splits to pass around a great ditch, forcing the travelers to improvise a crossing (007-009).
- **Milestones**: The encounters along the road (Scarecrow, Tin Woodman, Lion) function as narrative milestones, each marking progression in companionship and danger.

## Quotes

> "The road to the City of Emeralds is paved with yellow brick," said the Witch, "so you cannot miss it." (002-041)

> "Follow the Yellow Brick Road." — popularized version from the 1939 film; the novel reads "you must follow the road of yellow brick" (002-041, paraphrased)

## Notes

The Yellow Brick Road has become one of the most iconic symbols in American popular culture, far exceeding its relatively understated treatment in Baum's novel. In the book, it is a practical feature of Ozian infrastructure — a "paved" road connecting regions — rather than a magical or sentient path. Its color (yellow) connects it to the Winkie Country color scheme, suggesting that the road was built as an imperial project linking the Emerald City to its eastern territories.
