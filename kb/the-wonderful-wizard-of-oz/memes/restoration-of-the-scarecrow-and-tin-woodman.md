## Overview

The Restoration of the Scarecrow and Tin Woodman is the post-victory episode in Chapter XIII where the newly freed [[winkies|Winkies]], in gratitude for their liberation from the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]], search for and meticulously restore [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]]'s destroyed companions. The restoration transforms a moment of despair into a celebration of craftsmanship, gratitude, and renewal.

## Finding the Destroyed Companions

After the Witch's death, [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] freed the [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]] from his prison and told the [[winkies|Winkies]] they were no longer slaves. The Winkies, "overjoyed at their freedom," eagerly offered to help Dorothy find her missing friends [013-005].

A party of Winkies searched the countryside and found the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] "lying in a rocky valley, battered and dented beyond recognition" [013-008]. His axe was beside him, "but the blade was rusted and the handle broken short off" [013-008]. The [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]] was even harder to recover — his clothes were found "hanging in the branches of a tall tree" and his straw "had been scattered far and wide by the wind" [013-010].

## The Restoration

The Winkies carried the Tin Woodman "tenderly in their arms" back to the castle [013-012]. Dorothy asked if any Winkies were tinsmiths, and they replied: "Oh, yes. Some of us are very good tinsmiths" [013-013]. The tinsmiths worked for three days and four nights, "hammering and soldering and polishing," until the Woodman "was straightened out into his old form, and his joints worked as well as ever" [013-015].

Simultaneously, other Winkies gathered fresh, clean straw and "carefully restuffed the Scarecrow" [013-018], returning his clothes to their proper shape.

## The Golden Axe-Handle

During the restoration, [[the-winkie-goldsmith|a Winkie goldsmith]] crafted a magnificent new axe-handle of solid gold [013-016], while others polished the blade "until it glistened like burnished silver" [013-016]. The [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] had kept a great store of precious metals in her castle, and the goldsmith was eager to use them for a worthy cause.

The [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] was so moved by the Winkies' kindness that "he wept tears of joy, and these tears ran down his face and rusted his jaws so badly" [013-019] that he had to be oiled and polished again.

## Aftermath

The [[winkies|Winkies]] were so impressed by the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] that "they asked him to become their ruler" [013-020]. He accepted, promising to return and govern them "as soon as [he] had seen [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] safely on her way to [[kansas|Kansas]]" [013-020]. This restoration thus not only repaired the companions but also established the Tin Woodman's future as King of the Winkies.
