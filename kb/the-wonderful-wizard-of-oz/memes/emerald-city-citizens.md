## Overview

The **citizens of the Emerald City** are the green-clad inhabitants of the [[emerald-city|Emerald City]], the capital of the [[land-of-oz|Land of Oz]]. They are distinguished by their green attire, greenish skin, and the locked [[green-spectacles|green spectacles]] that every citizen and visitor must wear. The spectacles create the illusion that the city is naturally green, when in fact this appearance is entirely the product of the lenses—a deception maintained by the [[wizard-of-oz|Wizard]] to preserve the city's mystique.

## Appearance and Customs

The citizens are a visually uniform population. Dorothy observes that "there were many people—men, women, and children—walking about, and these were all dressed in green clothes and had greenish skins" [011-003]. The people of the city have worn the green spectacles for so long that "most of them think it really is an Emerald City" [015-073].

The commercial life of the city reflects this monochromatic theme: shops sell "green candy and green popcorn... green shoes, green hats, and green clothes of all sorts," and purchases are made with "green pennies" [011-003]. Even the children's lemonade is green.

The citizens are curious about strangers but also cautious. The children "all ran away and hid behind their mothers when they saw the Lion" [011-003]. The adults, while equally curious, keep their distance from the travelers because of the fearsome presence of the [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]].

## Reaction to the Travelers

The citizens' attitude toward Dorothy and her companions changes dramatically after the Witch's destruction. When the [[guardian-of-the-gate|Guardian of the Gates]] informs them that "Dorothy had melted the Wicked Witch of the West, they all gathered around the travelers and followed them in a great crowd to the Palace of Oz" [015-012].

Later, when the balloon is prepared for launch, the citizens gather in the square to witness Oz's departure. Oz addresses the crowd, announcing the Scarecrow as his successor, and "everyone seemed pleased with this arrangement" [017-024].

## Under the Scarecrow

After the Wizard's departure, the Scarecrow becomes ruler of the Emerald City. He proves a popular monarch: the citizens tell [[glinda|Glinda]] that "Oz has made me its ruler and the people like me" [023-013]. The Scarecrow's rule represents a transition from governance through illusion to governance through sincerity and good sense.

## Significance

The Emerald City citizens are the largest collective of unnamed characters in the book, and their willing participation in the green-spectacles illusion raises the story's most sophisticated political theme. They have worn the glasses for so long that the illusion has become their reality—they "think it really is an Emerald City" [015-073]. The Wizard rules them through managed perception, yet they are content because the city "certainly is a beautiful place, abounding in jewels and precious metals, and every good thing that is needed to make one happy" [015-073]. The citizens thus embody Baum's ambivalent view of political authority: sometimes a well-managed illusion produces genuine happiness.
