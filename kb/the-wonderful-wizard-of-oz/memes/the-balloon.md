## Overview

The **Wizard's balloon** is a hot-air balloon constructed by the [[wizard-of-oz|Wizard of Oz]] as a means to carry himself and [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] back across the [[the-deadly-desert|Great Desert]] to the United States. Fashioned from silk and launched by hot air, the balloon is the Wizard's improvised solution to the problem of traversing the impassable desert that surrounds [[land-of-oz|Oz]]. The balloon's accidental premature launch—without Dorothy—is one of the story's most dramatic moments and forces Dorothy to seek an alternative path home via [[glinda|Glinda]].

## Construction

After the Wizard is revealed as a humbug and agrees to help Dorothy return to Kansas, he proposes building a balloon as the only viable means of crossing the Great Desert:

> "You see, when I came to this country it was in a balloon. You also came through the air, being carried by a cyclone. So I believe the best way to get across the desert will be through the air." [017-009]

The construction process is a collaborative effort over three days [017-018]. Oz cuts strips of silk in different shades of green—light green, dark green, and emerald green—and Dorothy sews them together. The resulting bag of green silk is "more than twenty feet long" [017-018]. Oz then paints the inside with a coat of thin glue to render it airtight [017-019].

For the basket, Oz sends the [[soldier-with-the-green-whiskers|soldier with the green whiskers]] for a large clothes basket, which is fastened with ropes to the bottom of the balloon [017-020].

## The Launch

On the day of the launch, the balloon is carried out in front of the Palace of Oz. The [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] chops a large pile of wood for a fire, and Oz holds the bottom of the balloon over it to capture the rising hot air:

> Gradually the balloon swelled out and rose into the air, until finally the basket just touched the ground. [017-022]

As the assembled citizens of the Emerald City watch, Oz prepares to depart. He announces the [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]] as his successor to rule the Emerald City [017-024]. But as Dorothy searches for Toto—who has run into the crowd to bark at a kitten—the balloon strains against its ropes:

> She was within a few steps of it, and Oz was holding out his hands to help her into the basket, when, crack! went the ropes, and the balloon rose into the air without her. [017-028]

The Wizard calls out to Dorothy from the ascending balloon, but she cannot reach it. He floats away over the desert, and Dorothy's hope of returning home with him vanishes.

## Aftermath

Dorothy weeps bitterly at the loss of her chance to return to Kansas, but later reflects that she is "glad she had not gone up in a balloon" [018-002]. The balloon's departure accelerates the story's turn toward the southern journey to Glinda. The Scarecrow, now ruler of the Emerald City [023-013], sends the travelers onward with the [[guardian-of-the-gate|Guardian of the Gates]]' blessing.

## The Wizard's First Balloon

The Wizard originally arrived in Oz by balloon as well—his circus balloon from Omaha was caught in a twisted rope, carried aloft by a current of air, and deposited in the Land of Oz after "a day and a night" of travel [015-069]. His second balloon thus closes the circle of his Oz journey: he arrived by balloon, ruled for many years through illusion, and departed by balloon when his deceptions were uncovered.

## Significance

The balloon embodies the Wizard's resourcefulness—even without magic, he can engineer a practical solution. Its failure to carry Dorothy home reinforces a central theme of the book: the Wizard cannot solve Dorothy's problem, and her ultimate salvation comes not from external authority but from the [[silver-shoes|Silver Shoes]] she has possessed since the second chapter.
