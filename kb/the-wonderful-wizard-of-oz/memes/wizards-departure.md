# The Wizard's Departure

## Overview

The Wizard prepares a hot-air balloon to take Dorothy back to Kansas, but as she chases Toto, the ropes break and the balloon floats away leaving Dorothy stranded in Oz. Taking place in **[[emerald-city|Emerald City]]** (Chapter 017), this event marks a significant moment in Dorothy's journey through the Land of Oz.

## Notes

This event reflects key themes in Baum's narrative: the triumph of resourcefulness over brute force, the power of belief and perception, and the importance of companionship. In the context of American children's literature at the turn of the century, such episodes emphasize moral growth and self-discovery through adventure.
