# The Four Witches of Oz

## Overview

The [[the-four-witches-of-oz|Four Witches of Oz]] are the quartet of sorceresses who ruled over the four quadrants of the [[land-of-oz|Land of Oz]] before [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy's]] arrival. Two are Wicked — the [[wicked-witch-of-the-east|Witch of the East]] and the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Witch of the West]] — and two are Good — the [[witch-of-the-north|Witch of the North]] and [[glinda|Glinda the Good Witch of the South]]. This balance-of-power structure is the political skeleton on which the novel's plot hangs: Dorothy's house kills one Witch, the [[wizard-of-oz|Wizard]] demands she kill the other, and the two Good Witches provide protection and, ultimately, the means to go home. The extinction of the Wicked Witches over the course of the story transforms Oz from a land of tyranny into a land of freedom.

## Characteristics

The Witches of Oz are not a unified council or alliance — they rule their respective territories independently, and the boundaries between their quadrants are well-defined. The [[land-of-oz|Land of Oz]] is divided into four directional countries, each historically under the dominion of one Witch:

| Quadrant | Direction | Witch | Nature | Status by Story's End |
|----------|-----------|-------|--------|----------------------|
| [[munchkin-country|Munchkin Country]] | East | [[wicked-witch-of-the-east|Wicked Witch of the East]] | Wicked | Killed by Dorothy's falling house (002-008) |
| (unnamed) | North | [[witch-of-the-north|Witch of the North]] | Good | Alive; protects Dorothy and sends her to the [[emerald-city]] |
| [[winkie-country|Winkie Country]] | West | [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] | Wicked | Melted by Dorothy's bucket of water (012-083) |
| [[quadling-country|Quadling Country]] | South | [[glinda|Glinda the Good Witch]] | Good | Alive; reveals the secret of the [[silver-shoes]] |

The Witches are mortal — they can be killed by ordinary means (a falling house, a bucket of water) — but they possess magical knowledge and artifacts that make them formidable. The [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] carries an umbrella, commands the [[winged-monkey-band|Winged Monkeys]] via the [[golden-cap|Golden Cap]], and keeps wolves, crows, and bees as servants (012-017–012-034). The [[witch-of-the-north|Witch of the North]] can place a protective [[kiss-of-the-witch-of-the-north|kiss]] that marks the recipient as inviolable. [[glinda|Glinda]] commands knowledge — she understands the true powers of the [[silver-shoes]] and the [[golden-cap|Golden Cap]], and uses this knowledge to resolve the story.

> "There were only four witches in all the Land of Oz, and two of them, those who live in the North and the South, are good witches." (002-024)

## Role in the Story

### The Old Order

The story begins in a world where the four Witches form a kind of balance. The Good Witches are "good" — meaning they do not enslave or harm the people under their protection — but they are not powerful enough to overthrow the Wicked ones. The [[wizard-of-oz|Wizard of Oz]], despite his reputation, is powerless against the Witches: he has no real magic, and the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] drove him out of the Winkie Country when he tried to establish authority there (015-074). The Witches' power is real; the Wizard's is illusion.

> "I thought all witches were wicked," said the girl, who was half frightened at facing a real witch.
> "Oh, no, that is a great mistake." (002-023–002-024)

### The Fall of the Wicked

[[dorothy-gale|Dorothy Gale]] becomes the unwitting agent of the Wicked Witches' destruction. The [[death-of-the-wicked-witch-of-the-east|death of the Witch of the East]] is entirely accidental — Dorothy's house simply lands on her (002-008). The [[melting-of-the-wicked-witch-of-the-west|death of the Witch of the West]] is also unintentional — Dorothy throws water at the Witch in anger over a stolen shoe, unaware that water will melt her (012-083). Dorothy, the most ordinary of children, becomes the destroyer of evil through accidents of fate and temper — a brilliant subversion of the heroic quest narrative.

### The New Order

With both Wicked Witches dead, the political landscape of [[land-of-oz|Oz]] is fundamentally transformed. The tyranny of the East and West is broken. The [[wizard-of-oz|Wizard]] — himself exposed as a fraud — departs in his balloon (017-008). The Good Witches remain, but they do not seek dominion: the [[witch-of-the-north|Witch of the North]] never reappears after Chapter II, and [[glinda]] uses her power only to set things right and free the [[winged-monkey-band|Winged Monkeys]] (023-022). The new order is one of distributed, benevolent rule — the [[scarecrow]] in the [[emerald-city]], the [[tin-woodman]] in the Winkie Country, and the [[cowardly-lion]] in the great forest.

| Old Order | New Order |
|-----------|-----------|
| Wicked Witch of the East rules Munchkin Country | Freed Munchkins, Boq and others prosper |
| Wicked Witch of the West rules Winkie Country | [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] rules the Winkies (023-006) |
| Witch of the North protects the North | Witch of the North (status unchanged) |
| Glinda rules the South | Glinda (status unchanged, but now the most powerful being in Oz) |
| Wizard rules Emerald City via illusion | [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]] rules via wisdom |

## Notable Members

- **[[wicked-witch-of-the-east|Wicked Witch of the East]]** — The first Witch killed, crushed by Dorothy's falling house. Former owner of the [[silver-shoes]].
- **[[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]]** — The primary antagonist, melted by Dorothy's bucket of water. Former owner of the [[golden-cap|Golden Cap]].
- **[[witch-of-the-north|Witch of the North]]** — A Good Witch who greets Dorothy in Munchkin Country, gives her the [[kiss-of-the-witch-of-the-north|protective kiss]], and sends her to the [[emerald-city]].
- **[[glinda|Glinda the Good Witch]]** — The Witch of the South, who reveals the true power of the [[silver-shoes]] and frees the [[winged-monkey-band|Winged Monkeys]].

## Notes

Baum's Four Witches invert fairy-tale conventions in several important ways. First, not all witches are wicked — a radical idea in 1900, when "witch" was synonymous with evil in children's literature. Second, the Wicked Witches are not defeated through heroic combat but through accidents — a falling house and a thrown bucket of water — emphasizing that evil is more fragile than it appears. Third, the extinction of the Wicked Witches is treated not as a cosmic victory but as a natural clearing of old orders, making way for the distributed, democratic rule of Dorothy's companions. The Four Witches are the old world of Oz; [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] and her companions usher in the new.
