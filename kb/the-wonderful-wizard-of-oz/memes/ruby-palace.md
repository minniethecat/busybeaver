# Ruby Palace

## Overview

The Ruby Palace is the magnificent ruby-block castle of the beautiful princess and sorceress Gayelette, located in the northern reaches of the Land of Oz [014-035]. Although a place of great beauty and good magic, the palace is most significant in Oz history as the location where the Winged Monkeys were first bound to the Golden Cap — a curse of servitude that ripples through the entire story of Dorothy's journey [014-035].

## Geography

The palace stands "away at the North" [014-035] in a land of forests and rivers. Near the palace runs a river where Quelala would walk, and nearby lies a great forest that was home to the Winged Monkeys [014-035]. The region is part of the northern quadrant of Oz, later known as Gillikin Country, where the color purple holds sway. The palace is remote enough that its ruler Gayelette is unknown to most inhabitants of the Emerald City and southern regions.

## Architecture

The palace is described as a "handsome palace built from great blocks of ruby" [014-035] — a structure literally constructed from precious gemstones, making it one of the most opulent dwellings in all of Oz. The ruby construction marks it as a place of extraordinary wealth (the Golden Cap given as a wedding present is said to have "cost the princess half her kingdom" [014-035]), and its red brilliance would have been a landmark in the northern forests.

Unlike the Emerald City, which relies on green spectacles to create the illusion of jeweled magnificence, the Ruby Palace's gemstone construction appears to be genuine — fitting for a sorceress whose "magic was used to help the people" and who "was never known to hurt anyone who was good" [014-035].

## Inhabitants

### Gayelette

Gayelette is a beautiful princess and powerful sorceress who uses all her magic to help the people [014-035]. She lives alone in her ruby palace, her greatest sorrow being that she can find no one to love in return — for she considers all the men too stupid and ugly to mate with one so beautiful and wise [014-035].

### Quelala

Quelala is the handsome and manly boy whom Gayelette discovers, takes to her ruby palace, and magically enhances to become "the best and wisest man in all the land" [014-035]. He grows to manhood in the palace, and Gayelette hastens to prepare their wedding — making the ruby palace the setting for both his transformation and their impending marriage [014-035].

### The Winged Monkeys

The Winged Monkeys are not inhabitants of the palace proper, but their story is inextricably tied to it. They live "in the forest near Gayelette's palace" [014-035], and it is the palace's proximity to their home that leads to the fateful encounter with Quelala.

## Historical Significance

### The Binding of the Winged Monkeys

The Ruby Palace is the stage for the most consequential event in Winged Monkey history. On the day before Gayelette and Quelala's wedding, the King of the Winged Monkeys — "the old fellow loved a joke better than a good dinner" — spotted Quelala walking beside the river in his rich costume of pink silk and purple velvet [014-035]. On a mischievous impulse, he and his band seized Quelala, carried him into the air, and dropped him into the middle of the river [014-035].

Gayelette, enraged by the ruin of Quelala's wedding silks, summoned all the Winged Monkeys before her at the palace. Her initial sentence was harsh: their wings should be tied and they should be dropped into the river to drown as they had done to Quelala [014-035]. But the King pleaded for mercy, Quelala spoke a kind word on their behalf, and Gayelette commuted the sentence — on the condition that "the Winged Monkeys should ever after do three times the bidding of the owner of the Golden Cap" [014-035].

### The Golden Cap's Origin

The Golden Cap itself originated at the Ruby Palace, crafted as a wedding present for Quelala [014-035]. Made with a circle of diamonds and rubies, the cap was enchanted with the charm of three commands over the Winged Monkeys — a magical artifact born from the palace's union of love, power, and sorcery [014-035].

### The Banishment

After the wedding, Quelala used his first wish as the Golden Cap's owner to banish the Winged Monkeys from the palace's sight — his bride "could not bear the sight of us" [014-035]. The monkeys were ordered to remain hidden in the forest, a command they were glad to follow, for they were all afraid of Gayelette [014-035]. Thus the ruby palace became a place the Winged Monkeys would never again approach.

## Legacy

Though the Ruby Palace appears in only one passage of the Winged Monkeys' origin story, its influence permeates the entire narrative. Every use of the Golden Cap — by the Wicked Witch of the West to enslave the Winkies, to fight the Great Oz, and to capture Dorothy; by Dorothy to command the monkeys; and finally by Glinda to send Dorothy's companions to their kingdoms — traces back to a prank played by a mischievous Monkey King near the ruby palace on the eve of a wedding. The palace stands as the unseen origin point of one of Oz's most powerful magical artifacts and the three-command curse that shaped the destiny of every major character.
