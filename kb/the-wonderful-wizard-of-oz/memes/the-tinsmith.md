## Overview

The Tinsmith is a Munchkin craftsman who played a crucial role in the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]]'s transformation. Each time the [[wicked-witch-of-the-east|Wicked Witch of the East]]'s enchanted axe cut off one of the woodchopper's limbs, the tinsmith fashioned a tin replacement, ultimately constructing the Tin Woodman's entire mechanical body.

## Role in the Story

The Tinsmith appears only in the Tin Woodman's account of his origin in Chapter V. After the enchanted axe cut off the woodchopper's left leg, he "went to a tinsmith and had him make me a new leg out of tin" [005-046]. When the axe took his right leg, "again I went to the tinsmith, and again he made me a leg out of tin" [005-046]. After losing both arms, he "had them replaced with tin ones" [005-046].

When the axe severed his head, "the tinsmith happened to come along, and he made me a new head out of tin" [005-046]. Finally, after the axe split his body in two, "once more the tinsmith came to my help and made me a body of tin, fastening my tin arms and legs and head to it, by means of joints" [005-047].

With this final construction, the woodchopper became the Tin Woodman entirely — a fully mechanical being whose joints would rust if not oiled.

## Skills and Craft

The Tinsmith demonstrates remarkable skill in metalworking, crafting functional prosthetic limbs, a head, and a jointed body entirely from tin. His work allowed the young woodchopper to continue working and moving despite catastrophic injuries, and the Tin Woodman reports that "the leg worked very well, once I was used to it" [005-046].

Later in the story, a group of Winkie tinsmiths in the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]]'s castle perform a similar repair on the Tin Woodman after he is damaged, "straightening out those dents" and "soldering him together where he is broken" [013-014]. These Winkie tinsmiths also worked alongside a Winkie goldsmith who crafted a new golden axe-handle for the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] [013-016].

## Relationships

- **[[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]]** — The tinsmith's most significant client. He constructed the Tin Woodman's entire tin body, piece by piece, over multiple visits.
- **[[wicked-witch-of-the-east|Wicked Witch of the East]]** — Indirect antagonist. Her enchanted axe created the injuries that the tinsmith was called upon to remedy.

## Notes

The tinsmith is never named in *The Wonderful Wizard of Oz*. His role, while brief in terms of page time, is essential to the Tin Woodman's origin — without him, the woodchopper would have died from his injuries rather than surviving as the Tin Woodman. The Winkie tinsmiths who later repair the Woodman [013-014] are a separate group of craftsmen working under the Wicked Witch of the West before her demise, though they are equally willing to help Dorothy and her companions.
