## Overview

The Forest of Wild Beasts is a great ancient wood in the southern reaches of the [[land-of-oz|Land of Oz]], home to a diverse community of wild animals. It is here that the [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]] fulfills his destiny by slaying the [[great-spider|giant spider]] that had terrorized the beasts and being crowned their [[king-of-beasts|king]].

## Description

When [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] and her companions first entered the forest after passing through the [[dainty-china-country|Dainty China Country]], they found "the trees grew bigger and closer together than ever before, and the road wound in and out among them in the most confusing way" [021-001]. The forest was "so dark and gloomy that even the Scarecrow, who could see perfectly well, stumbled over roots" [021-001].

The forest was inhabited by "all sorts of wild beasts — tigers and elephants and bears and wolves and foxes and many others" [021-005] who lived in a clearing in the heart of the wood.

## The Community of Beasts

When the companions reached the clearing, they found the [[the-beasts-of-the-forest|beasts]] assembled in council. A great tiger stepped forward and explained their desperate situation: "A strange and terrible animal has come into our forest" [021-006] — the [[great-spider|giant spider]] that was "as big as an elephant, with legs as long as a tree trunk, and a body covered with coarse black hair" [021-006]. The spider "devours an animal for its dinner every day" [021-007] and could not be fought.

## The Lion's Victory and Kingship

The [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]] volunteered to fight the monster. Finding the spider asleep, the Lion "struck the spider's head from its body with one blow of his heavy paw" [021-010] — an attack so swift the creature never woke.

In gratitude, the beasts proclaimed the Lion their ruler: "We will do whatever you say, and we will obey you in all things" [021-013]. The Lion accepted the title of [[king-of-beasts|King of Beasts]], promising to return and rule after seeing [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] safely to [[kansas|Kansas]].

## Significance

The Forest of Wild Beasts is the location of the [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]]'s final and most definitive act of bravery. Having called himself a coward throughout the journey, he here performs the most traditionally heroic deed in the story — slaying a monster and being acclaimed king. The forest represents the fulfillment of his arc from self-doubting lion to acknowledged king.
