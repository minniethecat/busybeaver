# Witch's Castle

## Overview

The Witch's Castle is the yellow fortress of the **[[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]]** in **[[winkie-country|Winkie Country]]** — the seat of her power and the site of the novel's climactic confrontation. It is here that Dorothy is enslaved, forced to work in the kitchens, and coveted for the **[[silver-shoes|Silver Shoes]]**; here that the Witch is melted by a bucket of water; and here that the Winkies celebrate their liberation and invite the **[[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]]** to become their ruler.

## Geography

The castle stands in the western reaches of Winkie Country, visible to the Witch from a great distance through her single telescopic eye (012-010). It is a yellow structure matching the Winkie color scheme, surrounded by open country where the Witch sends her minions to intercept approaching travelers. The castle contains kitchens where Dorothy works, a courtyard where the **[[winged-monkeys|Winged Monkeys]]** land, and the Witch's private chambers where she keeps the **[[golden-cap|Golden Cap]]**.

## Inhabitants

The castle is populated by **[[winkie-country|Winkie]]** slaves who serve the Witch out of fear. The Witch herself rules with absolute authority, aided by her army of wolves, crows, black bees, and the Winged Monkeys summoned through the Golden Cap. After the Witch's death, the Winkies take control of the castle and use it to host a feast in Dorothy's honor (013-020).

## Role in the Story

The Witch's Castle is where the novel's central conflict is resolved. Dorothy and the Lion are brought here as captives by the Winged Monkeys (012-035). The Witch claims the Silver Shoes by trickery but cannot remove them while Dorothy is alive. She enslaves Dorothy in the kitchen and plots to kill the Lion by starvation. Dorothy, enraged by the Witch's theft of one Silver Shoe, throws a bucket of water at her — and the Witch melts away: "I'm going to melt away, I'm going — and very soon vanished away entirely" (012-056, paraphrased).

The castle then transforms from a place of terror to a place of celebration. The Winkies hold feasts, repair the Tin Woodman, and eventually crown him as their new ruler. The travelers depart with the Golden Cap and the gratitude of the freed people.

## Notable Features

- **The Kitchens**: Where Dorothy is forced to work, scrubbing pots and pans under the Witch's supervision.
- **The Witch's Chambers**: Where the Golden Cap and other magical items are kept.
- **The Courtyard**: Where the Winkie tinsmiths repair the Tin Woodman and the Scarecrow after the Winged Monkeys' attack.

## Quotes

> "I never killed a witch in all my life, and don't know how. But please, I beg you, don't hurt Toto!" (012-021)

> With these words the Witch fell down in a brown, melted, shapeless mass and began to spread over the clean boards of the kitchen floor. (012-056)

## Notes

The Witch's Castle is the novel's most explicitly gothic location — a fortress of enslavement and terror whose power is undone not by heroic combat but by a child's impulsive act of anger. The melting of the Witch by water is one of the most analyzed scenes in American children's literature, often read as an allegory of purification, the vulnerability of evil to the mundane, or the triumph of natural elements over unnatural power.
