## Overview

The Attack of the Wolves is the first of three successive assaults sent by the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] against [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] and her companions as they travel through the [[winkie-country|Winkie Country]] toward the Witch's castle [012-025]. The attack demonstrates the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]]'s combat prowess and the Witch's escalating desperation.

## The Attack

Seeing the travelers approaching from her one telescopic eye, the [[wicked-witch-of-the-west|Wicked Witch of the West]] "at once called to her side all the wolves that were in her power" [012-025]. She commanded them: "Go to those strangers, and tear them to pieces" [012-025]. The leader of the [[wolves-of-the-wicked-witch|wolves]] asked if they should first capture the strangers, but the Witch replied sharply: "No. Tear them to pieces. They are only a few, and they are strangers to this country" [012-025].

When the wolves approached, the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] told his companions to stand back. As the wolf leader rushed at him, "the Tin Woodman swung his axe, and the head of the wolf fell from its body" [012-026]. The remaining wolves attacked in succession, and the Tin Woodman methodically "killed them all, one after the other" [012-026]. None of the wolves survived.

## Aftermath

When the Witch learned that all her wolves lay dead, "she was even angrier than before" [012-027]. She then sent her [[wild-crows|wild crows]] in a second attempt to destroy the travelers, followed by a swarm of [[black-bees|black bees]]. These successive failures eventually forced her to use the [[golden-cap|Golden Cap]] to summon the [[winged-monkey-band|Winged Monkeys]].

## Significance

The wolves' defeat establishes the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] as the party's most effective physical combatant. It also demonstrates that the Witch's conventional forces are no match for the companions, forcing her to escalate to magical means — first the [[golden-cap|Golden Cap]], and ultimately her own destruction by [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]]'s bucket of water.
