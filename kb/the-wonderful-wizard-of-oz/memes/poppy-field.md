The **Poppy Field** is a vast meadow of scarlet poppies encountered by Dorothy and her companions during their journey along the yellow brick road to the Emerald City. The flowers emit a powerful, soporific fragrance that causes any living creature who breathes it to fall into an endless sleep from which they will never awaken unless carried away.

> There were big yellow and white and blue and purple blossoms, besides great clusters of scarlet poppies, which were so brilliant in color they almost dazzled Dorothy's eyes. [008-040]

## The Deadly Fragrance

As the travelers pressed deeper into the meadow, the poppies grew denser and the other flowers disappeared. Unaware of the danger, Dorothy was overcome by the scent.

> Now it is well known that when there are many of these flowers together their odor is so powerful that anyone who breathes it falls asleep, and if the sleeper is not carried away from the scent of the flowers, he sleeps on and on forever. [008-044]

Dorothy, being a living creature, was affected by the fragrance. Her eyes grew heavy, and she collapsed among the poppies.

> Her eyes closed in spite of herself and she forgot where she was and fell among the poppies, fast asleep. [008-045]

## The Lion's Fall

The [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]], despite his great size and strength, was also vulnerable to the deadly scent. He struggled to keep up with the others but ultimately succumbed.

> On and on they walked, and it seemed that the great carpet of deadly flowers that surrounded them would never end. They followed the bend of the river, and at last came upon their friend the Lion, lying fast asleep among the poppies. [008-054]

The [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]] and [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]], being made of straw and tin respectively, were immune to the poppies' fragrance. They carried Dorothy to safety but could not move the enormous Lion.

> "Run fast," said the Scarecrow to the Lion, "and get out of this deadly flower bed as soon as you can. We will bring the little girl with us, but if you should fall asleep you are too big to be carried." [008-051]

## Rescue by the Field Mice

The Lion was rescued with the help of the [[field-mice|Field Mice]] and their [[queen-of-the-field-mice|Queen]]. The [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] built a truck, and thousands of field mice pulled the sleeping Lion out of the poppy bed to the safety of the green fields beyond.

> Soon they rolled the Lion out of the poppy bed to the green fields, where he could breathe the sweet, fresh air again, instead of the poisonous scent of the flowers. [009-037]

The Lion eventually awakened, having lain among the poppies for a long time breathing their deadly fragrance.

> It was some time before the Cowardly Lion awakened, for he had lain among the poppies a long while, breathing in their deadly fragrance; but when he did open his eyes and roll off the truck he was very glad to find himself still alive. [010-002]
