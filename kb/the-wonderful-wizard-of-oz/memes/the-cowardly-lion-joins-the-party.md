## Overview

The Cowardly Lion Joins the Party is the pivotal encounter in Chapter VI where [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] and her companions meet the [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]] and invite him to join their journey to the [[emerald-city|Emerald City]]. The event transforms the traveling party from three to four and introduces the theme of self-perceived inadequacy through the Lion's confession that he lacks courage.

## Narrative

### The Lion's Attack

As [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]], the [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]], and the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] traveled through the dark forest on the [[road-of-yellow-brick|road of yellow brick]], they heard "a terrible roar" and saw a great Lion spring onto the path [006-001]. With one blow of his paw, the Lion sent the Scarecrow "spinning over and over to the edge of the road" [006-002]. He then struck the Tin Woodman with his sharp claws, but could not dent the tin [006-003].

When the Lion turned toward [[toto|Toto]], who was "barking wickedly," [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] rushed forward. "Afraid that Toto would be killed," she "slapped the Lion upon his nose as hard as she could" [006-005] and scolded him fiercely.

### The Lion's Confession

The Lion, "ashamed of himself," admitted: "I am a coward. I was born a coward" [006-008]. He explained that "everyone thinks I am very fierce and brave" because of his roar, but "any little thing can frighten me" [006-008]. [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] told him about the [[wizard-of-oz|Wizard of Oz]], who she believed could grant the Lion [[courage|courage]].

### Joining the Party

The Lion declared: "If the great Oz can give me courage, I will go with you" [006-012]. With the addition of the [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]], the traveling party grew from three companions to four, and they continued together toward the [[emerald-city|Emerald City]].

## Significance

This event completes the assembly of the story's central quartet of companions. Each now has a specific deficiency they hope the [[wizard-of-oz|Wizard]] can remedy: the [[scarecrow|Scarecrow]] seeks [[brain|brains]], the [[tin-woodman|Tin Woodman]] desires a [[heart]], and the [[cowardly-lion|Cowardly Lion]] needs [[courage|courage]]. [[dorothy-gale|Dorothy]] herself simply wishes to return [[home]] to [[kansas|Kansas]], establishing the parallel quest structure that drives the remainder of the narrative.
