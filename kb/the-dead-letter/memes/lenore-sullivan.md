# Lenore Sullivan

A young seamstress from [[new-york|New York]] whose suspicious behavior on the night of [[henry-moreland|Henry Moreland]]'s murder makes her the primary target of [[mr-burton|Mr. Burton]]'s investigation. Her flight from [[blankville|Blankville]] and subsequent concealment drive much of the novel's detective plot, culminating in a cross-continental pursuit.

## 角色定位

Lenore Sullivan works in a fancy-store in [[new-york|New York]], making caps, collars, and sleeves for four dollars a week. She boards for two and a half dollars in what her aunt describes as "a nice, genteel place." She often visits [[blankville|Blankville]] to stay over Sunday with her aunt. At the time of [[murder-of-henry-moreland|the murder]], she is described as seeming "goin' in a decline" — pale and nervous, suffering from the effects of stooping over the needle. ## 关键事件参与

- **Train journey**: [[lenore-burton|Lenore]] was on the train from [[new-york|New York]] on the afternoon before [[murder-of-henry-moreland|the murder]] — not the train [[henry-moreland|Moreland]] arrived on, but the one before. A fellow passenger noticed she "seemed to have some trouble on her mind." [[005-025]]
- **The night of [[murder-of-henry-moreland|the murder]]**: She appeared at the [[moreland-villa|Moreland Villa]] grounds under suspicious circumstances. A handkerchief found beneath the balcony window — marked with her initials — links her to the scene. [[005-030]] [[005-044]]
- **Flight from [[blankville|Blankville]]**: When confronted, [[lenore-burton|Lenore]] fled at the mention of a specific word, disappearing over a wooded hill. [[mr-burton|Mr. Burton]] spent over a week searching for her throughout the region. [[014-001]]
- **The baby**: A critical discovery — [[lenore-burton|Lenore]] was the mother of an infant daughter named Nora, concealed in a [[new-york|New York]] tenement-house. The child became the key to tracking her movements. [[014-016]] [[015-004]]
- **The California pursuit**: The investigation ultimately traced her across the continent to California and onward to [[acapulco|Acapulco]]. [[022-015]]

## 叙事意义

Lenore Sullivan embodies the novel's exploration of [[social-class-and-status|class]] and gender in 19th-century America. As a working-[[social-class-and-status|class]] seamstress, she exists at the margins of the respectable society that the [[henry-moreland|Moreland]]s and [[james-argyll|Argyll]]s inhabit. Her flight from [[justice-and-retribution|justice]] — whether motivated by [[guilt-and-conscience|guilt]], [[fear-and-courage|fear]], or a darker secret — drives [[mr-burton|the detective]] plot forward and provides the emotional stakes that elevate the novel beyond a simple whodunit. The discovery of her child adds a dimension of maternal desperation to her [[honor-and-reputation|character]]ization. [[014-016]] [[015-004]]
