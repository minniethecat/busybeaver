# Mr. Burton Sr.

Mr. Burton Sr. — not to be confused with [[mr-burton|the detective]] of the same surname — is [[richard-redfield|Richard Redfield]]'s uncle and the head of the [[dead-letter-office-washington|Dead Letter Office]] in Washington. His role is functional but essential: he provides [[richard-redfield|Redfield]] with the position that places him in the path of the [[fate-and-providence|fate]]ful dead [[the-letter-as-device|letter]].

## Role Overview

[[mr-burton|Burton]] Sr. represents the institutional framework within which the novel's central discovery occurs. As head of the [[dead-letter-office-washington|Dead Letter Office]], he oversees the processing of [[dead-letter|undeliverable mail]] — the bureaucratic machinery that, through apparent [[fate-and-providence|chance]], delivers the crucial clue to his nephew's desk. [[014-016]]

## Key Plot Events

[[mr-burton|Burton]] Sr. secures [[richard-redfield|Redfield]]'s employment at the [[dead-letter-office-washington|Dead Letter Office]], where [[richard-redfield|Redfield]] remains for eighteen years. This institutional placement — a seemingly mundane detail — is the mechanism through which [[fate-and-providence|providence]] operates. [[014-016]]
