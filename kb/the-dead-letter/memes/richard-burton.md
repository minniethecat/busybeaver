# Richard Burton

The central investigative figure of the novel — a private detective of extraordinary perception who unravels the mystery of [[henry-moreland|Henry Moreland]]'s murder through a combination of mesmeric intuition, patient observation, and relentless pursuit. Formerly a forwarding-merchant in [[new-york|New York]], he discovered his remarkable abilities during a warehouse fire and has since dedicated himself to [[criminal-detection|detective work]].

## 角色定位

Richard Burton is the novel's primary detective and narrative driver. He is in independent circumstances, accepting no salary for his work — it is a "labor of love." He possesses personal influence and strong will, but it is his extraordinary mesmeric powers that distinguish him from ordinary investigators. His method combines rational deduction with what might be called supernatural perception — the ability to read the "magnetism" of objects and places connected to a crime. [[008-023]]

The experience shaped his future course. [[008-023]]
- **Taking the [[henry-moreland|Moreland]] case**: [[mr-burton|Burton]] is called to [[blankville|Blankville]] to investigate [[murder-of-henry-moreland|the murder]], bringing his mesmeric methods to bear on a case that has baffled local authorities. [[005-001]] [[005-022]]
- **The mesmeric sitting**: [[mr-burton|Burton]] conducts a remarkable session where the dead [[the-letter-as-device|letter]] is read under trance, revealing crucial clues about [[murder-of-henry-moreland|the murder]]. [[009-034]]
- **Pursuit of [[lenore-sullivan|Lenore Sullivan]]**: [[mr-burton|Burton]] spends over a week searching for the missing seamstress throughout [[new-york|New York]] and its environs, employing scores of detectives and her photograph. [[014-001]] [[014-016]]
- **Confrontation with [[doctor-seltzer|Dr. Seltzer]]**: In a dramatic encounter, [[mr-burton|Burton]] wrestles with [[george-thorley|the murderer]], disarming him of his knife and pressing him backward to safe ground. [[018-001]] [[018-002]]
- **The California pursuit**: [[mr-burton|Burton]] pursues the investigation across the continent, ultimately following the trail to [[acapulco|Acapulco]] and the Pacific. [[022-015]]

## 叙事意义

[[mr-burton|Burton]] represents the novel's fusion of rational [[criminal-detection|detection]] with supernatural intuition. His mesmeric abilities place him at the boundary between science and the occult — a [[honor-and-reputation|character]]istic figure of mid-19th-century American fiction, where Spiritualism and detective methodology converged. His tireless pursuit of [[justice-and-retribution|justice]], refusal of payment, and eventual triumph over [[george-thorley|the murderer]] make him the moral center of the narrative.
