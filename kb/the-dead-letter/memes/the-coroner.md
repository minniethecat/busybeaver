# The Coroner

The coroner appears briefly in the aftermath of [[henry-moreland|Henry Moreland]]'s murder, representing the official response to violent [[death-and-mourning|death]] in mid-19th-century America. His examination establishes the basic facts of the case but cannot penetrate the mystery — a limitation that motivates the hiring of [[mr-burton|Mr. Burton]] and the long private investigation that follows.

## Role Overview

The coroner's inquest is the first formal investigation of [[henry-moreland|Moreland]]'s [[death-and-mourning|death]]. Its findings — that a murder occurred, that the victim died of violence — establish the legal framework for the case. But the inquest cannot identify [[george-thorley|the murderer]], and its limitations demonstrate why private [[criminal-detection|detection]] is necessary. [[002-064]]

## Key Plot Events

The coroner examines [[henry-moreland|Moreland]]'s body and convenes an inquest. The official finding of murder sets the legal machinery in motion but produces no suspect — a gap that [[mr-burton|Burton]]'s private investigation must fill. [[002-064]]
