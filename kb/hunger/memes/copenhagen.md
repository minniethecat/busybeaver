Copenhagen (København), the capital of Denmark, was the leading cultural and publishing center of Scandinavia in the 19th century. For Norwegian writers of Hamsun's generation, Copenhagen held an importance comparable to Paris for other European literary movements.

## Hamsun and Copenhagen

Knut Hamsun's literary breakthrough is tied to Copenhagen. The first sketch of what would become *Hunger* appeared in 1888 in the Danish literary periodical *New Earth* (*Ny Jord*), published in Copenhagen. This publication brought Hamsun immediate attention in Scandinavian literary circles.

Copenhagen's dominance in Scandinavian publishing meant that many Norwegian authors — including Ibsen and Bjørnson — were first published there through the Danish publishing house Gyldendal. The city functioned as a gateway to the broader European literary marketplace.

## Cultural Significance

For a Norwegian writer seeking an audience beyond provincial Kristiania, Copenhagen represented the cosmopolitan literary world. The city's role in launching *Hunger* connects Hamsun's novel to the transnational dynamics of Scandinavian literary culture in the late 19th century.
