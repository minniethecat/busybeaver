Leo Tolstoy (1828–1910) was a Russian novelist, short story writer, and moral philosopher, widely regarded as one of the greatest authors of all time. His major works include *War and Peace* (1869) and *Anna Karenina* (1877).

## Hamsun and Tolstoy

In his introduction to Knut Hamsun's *Hunger*, Edwin Björkman observes that Hamsun's reputation "spread long ago over the rest of Europe, taking deepest roots in Russia, where several editions of his collected works have already appeared, and where he is spoken of as the equal of Tolstoy and Dostoyevski." This comparison situates Hamsun within the highest tier of European literary achievement while also noting the "characteristic symptom that throws interesting light on Russia as well as on Hamsun."

The comparison is notable given the vast differences in method: Tolstoy's omniscient realism versus Hamsun's subjective, impressionistic narration. The Russian enthusiasm suggests an appreciation for psychological depth that transcended formal differences.
