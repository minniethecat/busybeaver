# New Earth

## Overview

**New Earth** (*Ny Jord*, 1893) is a novel by Knut Hamsun, published the same year as *Editor Lynge*. Together, the two novels constitute Hamsun's contribution to the social novel genre — studies of Christiania's Bohemian circles that were, in Björkman's words, "chiefly characterized by their violent attacks on the men and women exercising the profession which Hamsun had just made his own."

## Significance to Hunger

"New Earth" holds a double connection to *Hunger*. First, the title was also the name of the Danish literary periodical in which the original 1888 sketch of *Hunger* first appeared — the sketch that "attracted immediate widespread attention to the author, both on account of its unusual theme and striking form" (P04-010). Second, as one of the two 1893 novels that followed *Hunger*'s success, it represents the phase in Hamsun's career when he transitioned from being an unknown struggling writer to an established literary figure.

## Literary Context

Björkman positions *New Earth* and *Editor Lynge* as transitional works in Hamsun's development. After the psychological intensity of *Hunger* (1890) and *Mysteries* (1892), these social studies represent a different register — satire and polemic rather than interior exploration. Hamsun would soon return to his characteristic mode with *Pan* in 1894, described as "the real Hamsun" finally revealed (P04-012).
