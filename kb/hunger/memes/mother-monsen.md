Mother Monsen is a minor character in Knut Hamsun's *Hunger* who represents the possibility of maternal care and community that the narrator consistently refuses.

## Role in the Novel

Like other figures who offer the narrator help — concerned strangers, potential benefactors — Mother Monsen embodies an alternative path the narrator will not take. His rejection of her offered assistance is not rational calculation but a function of the pride that structures his entire relationship to the social world.

## Maternal Absence

The very name "Mother Monsen" suggests a maternal function that the novel's urban world largely lacks. The narrator has no family in Kristiania, no network of care or obligation. The city is a space of atomized individuals, and figures like Mother Monsen represent the vestiges of a more communal social order that modernity is erasing.
