The Old Man is one of several unnamed elderly figures the narrator encounters during his wanderings in Knut Hamsun's *Hunger*. These brief encounters exemplify the fragmented social relations of modern urban life.

## Significance

The old man, like the lame old man, the blind man, and other aged figures in the novel, serves as a mirror in which the narrator glimpses his possible future — the endpoint of the physical and social decline he is already experiencing. These elderly figures embody the novel's preoccupation with time, decay, and the precariousness of human dignity.
