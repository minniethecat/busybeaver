Homansbyen is a residential neighborhood in Oslo (Kristiania), developed in the 1860s and 1870s as one of the city's first planned residential districts. Designed by architect Georg Andreas Bull, it represented the expansion of bourgeois Kristiania into what had previously been rural outskirts.

## In Hunger

Homansbyen and similar western neighborhoods form the implicit spatial and social counterpoint to the impoverished eastern districts — Vaterland, Groenlandsleret — where the narrator lives and wanders. The narrator's exclusion from bourgeois domesticity is spatial as well as social: the comfortable homes of Homansbyen represent a world from which he is barred.
