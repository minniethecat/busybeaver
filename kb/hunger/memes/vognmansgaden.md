## Overview

Vognmansgaden is a street in the Vaterland district of Christiania where the narrator emerges after passing through a lodging house for travellers during his delusional search for the wool-dealer Joachim Kierulf in Part III.

## In the Narrative

The narrator's encounter with Vognmansgaden occurs during one of his most disoriented episodes. Having convinced himself that he must find a man named Joachim Kierulf, a wool-dealer, he takes a cab to an address and enters a building. Finding no Kierulf there, he steps quietly through a room and opens the opposite door: "I step quietly across the room to the opposite door without any haste, without any thought of flight; open it, too, and come out in Vognmansgaden" [003-042].

On this street, the narrator experiences a moment of strange lucidity. He walks "very coolly down Vognmansgaden, without fear of being conscious of doing any wrong" [003-043]. The fixation on Kierulf — "this creature in whose existence I believe, and whom it was of vital importance that I should meet" — vanishes from his memory, "wiped out with many other mad whims which came and went in turns" [003-043]. The street thus marks a transition point: the narrator emerges from the enclosed delusional space of the lodging house into the open air of reality, and the phantom of Kierulf dissolves as he walks.
