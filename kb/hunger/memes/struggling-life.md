# Struggling Life

## Overview

**Struggling Life** is a collection of short stories and travelling sketches by [[knut-hamsun|Knut Hamsun]], published in 1905. It is listed among [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s works in the preface to *Hunger* as part of the remarkable series of volumes produced between 1897 and 1912 [P04-015].

## Description

The preface to *Hunger* provides a bibliographic catalogue of [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s works from his most productive period, noting: "'Struggling Life,' short stories and travelling sketches, 1905" [P04-015]. The work appears in a sequence that spans from "Siesta" (1897) through "The Last Joy" (1912) — a period in which [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]] moved restlessly between novels, plays, poetry, travel sketches, and short stories, testing the capacities of each genre while building the body of work that would culminate in the Nobel Prize for *[[the-growth-of-the-soil|The Growth of the Soil]]* in 1920.

## Relationship to Hunger

Struggling Life is connected to *Hunger* primarily through the arc of [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s career — it represents a stage in the development of the writer whose first major novel established the psychological method that would influence European modernism. The preface's inclusion of Struggling Life in its bibliographic catalogue [P04-015] attests to its place in [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s complete works, even if its relationship to *Hunger* specifically is one of chronology rather than direct thematic connection.

## Critical Reception

As a work from [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s middle period, Struggling Life has received less critical attention than his major novels. Its significance lies primarily in its place within the arc of [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s career — a career remarkable for its generic range and its sustained exploration of the psychology of exceptional individuals.
