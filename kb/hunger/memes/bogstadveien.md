Bogstadveien is a major street in Oslo, running through the Frogner and Majorstuen districts. In the late 19th century, when Hamsun wrote *Hunger*, the street was a significant thoroughfare connecting the city center to the western residential neighborhoods.

## Historical Context

During the 1880s–1890s, Bogstadveien was transitioning from a suburban road to an urban commercial street. The western expansion of Kristiania was reshaping the city's geography, creating new zones of bourgeois respectability that contrasted sharply with the working-class eastern districts like Vaterland. This spatial class division is implicit in the geography of *Hunger*.

## In Hunger

Like other streets in the novel, Bogstadveien appears as part of the narrator's extensive perambulations through Kristiania. The street's western, more prosperous character would have made it a site of particular social tension for the impoverished narrator, reinforcing his exclusion from bourgeois normality.
