The Clerk is a minor character in Knut Hamsun's *Hunger*, working at one of the Kristiania newspaper offices where the narrator attempts to sell his writing.

## Gatekeeper Function

The clerk represents the institutional and bureaucratic dimension of the literary marketplace. Before the narrator can reach the editor who might publish his work, he must pass through the clerk who guards access. This intermediary position — neither decision-maker nor supplicant — makes the clerk a figure of petty power, one of the many small humiliations the narrator endures in his pursuit of literary recognition.

## The Literary Marketplace

The clerk's role illuminates the economic reality of writing in late 19th-century Kristiania. Literature is not a sacred calling but a commodity traded in newspaper offices, subject to the same gatekeeping and arbitrariness as any other market. The narrator's dependence on this system — and his prideful resistance to its indignities — is one of the novel's central tensions.
