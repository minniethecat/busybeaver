Eline Kareno is a fictional character in Knut Hamsun's dramatic trilogy, consisting of *At the Gates of the Kingdom* (1895), *The Game of Life* (1896), and *Sunset Glow* (1898). She is the wife of the central character, Ivar Kareno.

## Role in the Trilogy

Edwin Björkman's introduction to *Hunger* identifies Eline as one of the two women who play the greatest part in Kareno's life, alongside Teresita. She accompanies Kareno through all three plays, witnessing his transformation from a rebellious young thinker at 29, through middle age as an acclaimed radical leader, to his final surrender to "the powers that be" at 50.

## Character Dynamics

The Eline-Teresita pairing in the drama reflects a recurrent pattern in Hamsun's fiction: the tension between domestic stability (Eline, the wife) and passionate, unpredictable vitality (Teresita). Björkman describes the women as "delightfully drawn," suggesting that despite Hamsun's reputation for solipsistic male protagonists, his secondary female characters possess considerable depth and dramatic interest.
