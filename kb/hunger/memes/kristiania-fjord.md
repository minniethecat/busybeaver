Kristiania Fjord (Oslofjorden) is the inlet of the Skagerrak strait on which Oslo (Kristiania) is situated. It stretches approximately 100 kilometers from the open sea to the city.

## In Hunger

The fjord is the geographical fact underlying the novel's harbour and dock scenes. The ships that come and go through the fjord — including the Russian barque *Copegoro* on which the narrator eventually departs — represent connection to the wider world beyond Kristiania. The fjord is thus both a geographical feature and a symbolic threshold: the passage between the enclosed urban world of the novel and the open possibilities of elsewhere.
