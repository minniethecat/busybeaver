*Peer Gynt* is a dramatic poem by Henrik Ibsen, published in 1867. It follows the adventures of the title character — a Norwegian peasant, braggart, and dreamer — through a picaresque narrative that blends folklore, fantasy, and philosophical allegory.

## Comparison to Hamsun's Vendt

Edwin Björkman's introduction to *Hunger* notes that Hamsun's verse drama *Vendt the Monk* (1903) features a hero who "has much in common with Peer Gynt without being in any way an imitation or a duplicate." Vendt, like Peer Gynt, is "a dreamer in revolt against the world's alleged injustice, a rebel against the very powers that invisibly move the universe, and a passionate lover of life who in the end accepts it as a joyful battle."

This comparison positions Hamsun's drama within the Ibsen tradition while emphasizing its originality. Both heroes embody the Scandinavian archetype of the rebellious individualist who refuses social and cosmic constraints.
