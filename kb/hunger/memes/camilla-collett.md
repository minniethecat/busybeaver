Camilla Collett (1813–1895) was a Norwegian novelist and essayist, best known for her novel *The District Governor's Daughters* (*Amtmandens Døtre*, 1854–55), which is considered the first Norwegian realist novel and an early feminist work.

## Literary Contribution

Collett's novel critiqued the institution of arranged marriage and the limited options available to women in Norwegian society. Her work anticipated the social criticism of Ibsen and the Modern Breakthrough by two decades.

## Context for Hamsun

As Henrik Wergeland's sister and a prominent literary figure in 19th-century Kristiania, Collett helped establish the intellectual environment in which later writers — Ibsen, Bjørnson, and eventually Hamsun — would work. Her feminism represents an aspect of Norwegian literary culture that Hamsun's male-centered fiction largely ignored.
