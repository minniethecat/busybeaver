# Groenlandsleret

Groenlandsleret (Grønlandsleret) is a street in Christiania where the narrator seeks employment at a grocer's shop. It appears in four passages of the novel, all connected to the narrator's attempt to secure work as a part-time book-keeper.[001-386]

## The Grocer's Advertisement

At a point of acute financial desperation, the narrator notices an advertisement for a book-keeping position:

> A grocer in Groenlandsleret wanted a man every week for a couple of hours' book-keeping; remuneration according to agreement. I noted my man's address, and prayed to God in silence for this place. I would demand less than any one else for my work; sixpence was ample, or perhaps fivepence. That would not matter in the least.[001-386]

The narrator writes an application to Christy the grocer at No. 13 Groenlandsleret, posts it, and then waits — but never follows up in person. His passivity and self-sabotage prevent him from pursuing this realistic opportunity.[001-390]

## Unfulfilled Hope

The narrator's relationship with the Groenlandsleret job offer follows the novel's pattern of near-misses and self-defeating behavior. Three times he thinks about the position, each time resolving to follow up, yet he never actually presents himself to the grocer. He even sets out for Groenlandsleret at one point, convinced that "luck often took such a devious course," but the journey is never completed.[001-580][001-582]

> There would surely be some way or another of getting help when the right time came! Now, there was the grocer in Groenlandsleret. Had I importuned him every hour in the day since I sent in my application? Had I rung the bell early and late, and been turned away? Why, I had not even applied personally to him or sought an answer! It did not follow, surely, that it must needs be an absolutely vain attempt.[001-580]

The Groenlandsleret episodes reveal the narrator's psychological paralysis: he can write a letter and dream of the job, but cannot cross the threshold into actual self-advocacy. The street becomes another node in the geography of the narrator's internal obstacles, alongside [[bernt-akers-street]] and [[pyle-street]].
