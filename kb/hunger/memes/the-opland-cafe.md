## Overview

The Opland Café is a social venue in Christiania that the narrator uses as a stakeout point in Part I, waiting outside it to catch an acquaintance from whom he hopes to borrow money.

## In the Narrative

In the depths of his hunger, the narrator remembers that a young man he knows — a theological student — is employed at the bookshop and receives his wages on a particular day. The narrator decides to intercept him: "I'd watch for my man outside the Opland Café between seven and eight" [001-287]. He positions himself outside the café and waits, hoping to waylay the acquaintance and ask for a loan.

His plan is simple: "When he had got his wages, I'd borrow a shilling from him" [001-287]. The café serves as a practical landmark — a known meeting point rather than a place the narrator enters and patronises. The location is described as being on the road "leading to the Opland" [001-290], suggesting it sits on the periphery of the city centre, a transitional zone where the narrator's hopes of survival meet the reality of his impoverishment.
