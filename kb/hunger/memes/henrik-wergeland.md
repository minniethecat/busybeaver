Henrik Wergeland (1808–1845) was a Norwegian poet, playwright, and political activist, considered one of the most important figures in the development of Norwegian national literature.

## Historical Significance

Wergeland was a central figure in the Norwegian national awakening of the early 19th century, advocating for Norwegian cultural independence from Denmark. His poetry, plays, and political writings helped establish a distinct Norwegian literary language and identity.

## Relation to Hamsun

While Wergeland died before Hamsun's birth, his legacy shaped the Norwegian literary culture into which Hamsun entered. The nationalist-romantic tradition that Wergeland inaugurated was both a foundation and — for Hamsun's more cosmopolitan, individualistic modernism — something to react against.
