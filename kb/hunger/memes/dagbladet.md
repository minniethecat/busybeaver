# Dagbladet

## Overview

**Dagbladet** (founded 1869) was a Norwegian daily newspaper published in Christiania (now Oslo). It was one of the principal newspapers in Norway during the time of *Hunger*, and is referenced in the novel as a venue where the unnamed narrator hopes to have his articles published.

## In the Narrative

The narrator of *Hunger* is an aspiring writer who regularly submits articles to Christiania's newspapers. Dagbladet represents one of the potential outlets for his work, part of the newspaper economy that the narrator navigates in his attempt to earn money from his writing. The newspaper offices he visits and the editors he encounters form a recurring motif in the novel, illustrating both his desperate need for income and his literary ambitions.

## Historical Context

Dagbladet was founded by Anthon Bang and was known for its liberal political stance. During the period of *Hunger* (set in the 1880s, published in 1890), Christiania had a vibrant newspaper culture, with several competing dailies serving as platforms for literary and political discourse. The newspaper industry was one of the few reliable sources of income for struggling writers like Hamsun himself, who had worked as a journalist and freelance writer before achieving literary success.
