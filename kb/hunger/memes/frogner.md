Frogner is an affluent residential district in Oslo (Kristiania). In the late 19th century, it was developing as a desirable neighborhood for the city's upper-middle class, with spacious apartments and proximity to the Royal Palace and Frogner Park.

## Social Geography of Hunger

Frogner and similar western districts represent everything the narrator of *Hunger* is excluded from: domestic comfort, social respectability, and the ordinary life of the bourgeoisie. The novel's geography of Kristiania is deeply class-structured, with the impoverished east (Vaterland, Groenlandsleret) and the prosperous west (Frogner, Homansbyen) mapping the city's social divisions.
