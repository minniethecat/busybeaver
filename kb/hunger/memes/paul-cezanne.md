Paul Cézanne (1839–1906) was a French Post-Impressionist painter whose work laid the foundations for the transition from 19th-century artistic conventions to 20th-century modernism. His explorations of geometric simplification and optical phenomena influenced both Cubism and Fauvism.

## Cézanne in Björkman's Analogy

Edwin Björkman's introduction to Knut Hamsun's *Hunger* describes Hamsun's new literary method as "life, but presented in the Impressionistic temper of a Gauguin or Cezanne." Cézanne's approach to painting — building form through modulated color rather than outline, seeking underlying structure in visual experience — provides a suggestive parallel to Hamsun's technique of rendering consciousness through fragmented, intense subjective impressions rather than conventional narrative exposition.

Both artists sought truth beneath surface appearance: Cézanne through geometric order underlying visual chaos, Hamsun through psychological authenticity beneath social convention.
