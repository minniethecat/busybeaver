# Editor Lynge

## Overview

**Editor Lynge** (*Redaktør Lynge*) is a novel by [[knut-hamsun|Knut Hamsun]], published in 1893 alongside *[[new-earth-periodical|New Earth]]*. The preface to *Hunger* describes both novels as "social studies of [[christiania|Christiania]]'s Bohemia" (P04-012), situating them within [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s early engagement with the literary and journalistic world of the Norwegian capital.

## Description

*Editor Lynge* takes as its central figure a newspaper editor in [[christiania|Christiania]], drawing on [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s own experience of the journalistic milieu. The novel belongs to the period immediately following *[[mysteries|Mysteries]]* (1892) and preceding *[[pan|Pan]]* (1894).

As a "social study," *Editor Lynge* represents a departure from the radical psychological interiority of *Hunger*. Where the earlier novel immerses the reader in a single [[soul|consciousness]], *Editor Lynge* observes a social milieu — [[christiania|Christiania]]'s Bohemia — from a more external vantage point. This shift prefigures [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s later development toward broader social canvases.

## Relationship to Hunger

*Editor Lynge* shares with *Hunger* a setting — [[christiania|Christiania]] — and a concern with the world of newspapers and literary production. The newspaper office that the hungry narrator visits to submit his articles, and the editor who accepts or rejects them, are the very institutions that *Editor Lynge* takes as its subject.

## Critical Reception

*Editor Lynge* has generally been read alongside *[[new-earth-periodical|New Earth]]* as part of [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s early social fiction. The preface's characterization of both as "social studies of [[christiania|Christiania]]'s Bohemia" (P04-012) has shaped their critical reception.
