# In Wonderland

## Overview

**In Wonderland** is a collection of travelling sketches by [[knut-hamsun|Knut Hamsun]], published in 1903, documenting his journey through the Caucasus. It is listed among [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s works in the preface to *Hunger* as part of the remarkable series of volumes produced between 1897 and 1912 [P04-015].

## Description

The preface to *Hunger* provides a bibliographic catalogue of [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s works from his most productive period, noting: "'In Wonderland,' travelling sketches from the Caucasus, 1903" [P04-015]. The work appears among a sequence that includes "Siesta" (1897), "Victoria" (1898), "Brushwood" (1903), and "The Wild Choir" (1904) — a period in which [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]] moved restlessly between forms, testing the capacities of each genre while building the body of work that would culminate in the Nobel Prize for *[[the-growth-of-the-soil|The Growth of the Soil]]* in 1920.

## Relationship to Hunger

In Wonderland is connected to *Hunger* primarily through the arc of [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s career — it represents a stage in the development of the writer whose first major novel established the psychological method that would influence European modernism. The preface's inclusion of In Wonderland in its bibliographic catalogue [P04-015] attests to its place in [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s complete works, even if its relationship to *Hunger* specifically is one of chronology rather than direct thematic connection.

## Critical Reception

As a work from [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s middle period, In Wonderland has received less critical attention than his major novels. Its significance lies primarily in its place within the arc of [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s career — a career remarkable for its generic range and its sustained exploration of the psychology of exceptional individuals.
