Alfred A. Knopf, Inc. is an American publishing house founded in 1915 by Alfred A. Knopf Sr. It became one of the most distinguished publishers of literary fiction in the United States, known for its commitment to quality, design, and international literature.

## Publishing Hunger

The 1921 Knopf edition of *Hunger*, translated by George Egerton with an introduction by Edwin Björkman, was based on the eighth printing. The title page notes that this edition is 'a bowdlerised version of the original 1899 translation.' Knopf's publication of Hamsun was part of a broader commitment to bringing European modernist literature to American readers.

## Significance

Knopf's publication of *Hunger* marked an important moment in Hamsun's American reception. The elegant Knopf edition, with Björkman's substantial critical introduction, positioned Hamsun as a major European literary figure worthy of serious attention.
