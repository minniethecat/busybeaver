Jonas Lie (1833–1908) was a Norwegian novelist, short story writer, and playwright, considered one of the 'Four Greats' of 19th-century Norwegian literature alongside Henrik Ibsen, Bjørnstjerne Bjørnson, and Alexander Kielland.

## Major Works

Lie's novels include *The Pilot and His Wife* (1874), *The Family at Gilje* (1883), and *The Commodore's Daughters* (1886). His work combines realistic social observation with a lyrical sensitivity to nature, particularly the sea. Like Bjørnson, Lie represents the established literary generation against which Hamsun defined his modernist rebellion.

## Relation to Hamsun

Lie belongs to the generation immediately preceding Hamsun. While not directly mentioned in Björkman's introduction, his presence in the Norwegian literary establishment formed part of the cultural context against which *Hunger*'s radical subjectivity registered as something new.
