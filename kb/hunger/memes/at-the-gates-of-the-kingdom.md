## Overview

*At the Gates of the Kingdom* (*Ved Rigets Port*) is Knut Hamsun's first drama, published in 1895. It marks his turn from novels to the dramatic form, and serves as the opening play of a trilogy that continues with *The Game of Life* (1896) and *Sunset Glow* (1898) (P04-013).

## Description

The play is set in Christiania and introduces Ivar Kareno, a student and thinker, at the age of 29. Kareno is portrayed as a Nietzschean rebel against the victorious majority, and the trilogy as a whole follows him through two more decades of his life. The drama explores the tension between youthful idealism and the compromises of age (P04-013).

## Relationship to Hunger

*At the Gates of the Kingdom* was written five years after *Hunger* (1890), during Hamsun's early period of literary experimentation. Like *Hunger*, the play reflects Hamsun's interest in the psychology of outsiders and rebels against social norms. The trilogy format prefigures the broader narrative ambitions Hamsun would later develop in novels such as *Children of the Time* and *Segelfoss Town* (P04-013).
