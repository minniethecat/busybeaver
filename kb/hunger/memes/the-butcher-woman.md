The Butcher Woman is a minor character in Knut Hamsun's *Hunger*, one of the many anonymous city-dwellers the narrator encounters during his wanderings through Kristiania.

## Role in the Novel

Like the many incidental figures who populate *Hunger* — the lame old man, the baker, the maid, the various clerks and editors — the Butcher Woman exists at the periphery of the narrator's consciousness, briefly illuminated by his attention and then absorbed back into the urban crowd. These minor characters collectively create the texture of city life against which the narrator's isolation is defined.

## The Urban Crowd

Hamsun's rendering of incidental urban encounters is an important aspect of his modernism. Unlike the 19th-century realist novel, where minor characters are often developed and return throughout the narrative, *Hunger*'s supporting figures are glimpsed and lost, reflecting the anonymity and transience of modern urban experience.
