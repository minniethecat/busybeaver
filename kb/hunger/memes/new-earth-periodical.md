# New Earth

## Overview

**New Earth** (*Ny Jord*) was a Danish literary periodical in which the first sketch of [[knut-hamsun|Knut Hamsun]]'s *Hunger* appeared in 1888. The preface to the English edition of *Hunger* describes this publication as the novel's point of origin: "In its original form, *Hunger* was merely a sketch, and as such it appeared in 1888 in a Danish literary periodical, 'New Earth'" (P04-010). The periodical thus occupies a foundational place in the history of Scandinavian modernism — the venue in which one of its seminal texts first reached the public.

## Description

*New Earth* was part of the vibrant Scandinavian literary periodical culture of the late nineteenth century, a network of journals that connected writers across Denmark, Norway, and Sweden. [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]], living in Copenhagen at the time, submitted his sketch of *Hunger* to the periodical, where it "attracted immediate wide attention" (P04-010). The sketch was not the full novel — [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]] would expand it substantially for the book publication two years later — but it contained the essential elements: the starving narrator, the urban setting, the psychological immediacy that would define the completed work.

The name *New Earth* is itself significant, suggesting the periodical's orientation toward emerging literary movements. The preface notes that the novel "[[editor-lynge|Editor Lynge]]" and a novel also titled "New Earth" (both 1893) were [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s own works — the name recurring across [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s career, suggesting its resonance for him. The periodical title and [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s later novel share the same Danish phrase, "Ny Jord," though the preface's translator renders the novel title as "New Earth" while keeping the periodical's name distinct.

## Relationship to Hunger

*New Earth* is directly responsible for the existence of *Hunger* as a published work. Without the periodical's acceptance of the sketch, the novel might never have been completed. The preface traces the path from this initial publication to the completed novel in 1890, which "was greeted as one of the chief heralds of the neo-romantic movement" (P04-010). The periodical thus stands at the origin of a literary revolution — the point at which [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s voice first reached the public ear.

## Critical Reception

The periodical's role in launching [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s career has been recognized by literary historians. The preface's account of the publication history — "It attracted immediate wide attention" (P04-010) — suggests that the sketch made an impression even in its abbreviated form. The transition from periodical sketch to completed novel demonstrates the importance of literary journals as venues for experimentation and debut in the late-nineteenth-century Scandinavian literary world.
