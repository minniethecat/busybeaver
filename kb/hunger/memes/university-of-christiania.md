# University of Christiania

The University of Christiania (now the University of Oslo) was Norway's leading institution of higher education during the period in which *Hunger* is set. Founded in 1811 as the Royal Frederick University (Det Kongelige Frederiks Universitet), it was renamed the University of Christiania and later, in 1939, the University of Oslo.

## University Street

In the novel, University Street (Universitetsgaten) appears as a major thoroughfare in Christiania. The narrator follows two acquaintances — a woman and a man he jealously observes — as they turn "the corner of University Street and up towards St. Olav's place." Later, the narrator repeatedly walks up and down University Street, which connects Carl Johann Street to St. Olav's Place, making it part of the novel's central walking geography.[001-196][001-220][003-1574]

> They turned, stopped at Cisler's music-shop, and spoke together. I stopped also. Thereupon they both came back, went the same road as they had come, passed me again, and turned the corner of University Street and up towards St. Olav's place.[001-196]

## Academic Context

While the university itself is not directly described in the novel, its presence shaped the intellectual and social landscape of Christiania in the late 19th century. The area around the university formed part of the fashionable promenading district that the narrator navigates as an outsider. His poverty and hunger place him in sharp contrast with the students, professors, and bourgeois residents who populate the university quarter.
