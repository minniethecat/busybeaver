Segelfoss Manor is a fictional estate in the Northland of Norway, created by Knut Hamsun as the setting for his later novels *Children of the Time* (1913) and *Segelfoss Town* (1915).

## In Hamsun's Fiction

Edwin Björkman describes the Segelfoss novels as marking a new phase in Hamsun's career. The setting shifts from the individual consciousness that dominated *Hunger* and *Pan* to a social panorama: 'The life pictured is the life of a certain spot of ground — Segelfoss manor, and later the town of Segelfoss — rather than that of one or two isolated individuals.'

Segelfoss represents Hamsun's turn toward broader social observation in his maturity, while maintaining his characteristic themes: the conflict between individual and group, the tension between primitive life and modernity, and the shaping power of the Northland environment.
