# Bodily Experience

Hunger is not an abstract condition in Hamsun's novel — it is lived in the body, registered in every tremor, gnawing pain, and fleeting sensory distortion. The protagonist's physical state is the novel's primary instrument, its fluctuations mapping directly onto narrative rhythm and psychological extremity.
