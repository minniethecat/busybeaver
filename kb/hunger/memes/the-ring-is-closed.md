## Overview

*The Ring Is Closed* (*Ringen sluttet*) is a novel by Knut Hamsun published in 1936. It tells the story of Abel Brodersen, the son of a lighthouse keeper, who drifts through life without ambition, drifting back to the small Norwegian coastal town where he was born.

## Description

The novel follows Abel Brodersen, who abandons opportunities abroad and returns to his hometown where he lives a marginal life, taking odd jobs and sleeping in a boathouse. Despite his apparent passivity, Abel exerts a quiet influence on those around him, particularly through his relationships with women. The novel explores themes of fate, renunciation, and the idea that life's course is predetermined — the "ring" of the title suggesting both closure and circularity.

## Relationship to Hunger

*The Ring Is Closed* returns to themes that preoccupied Hamsun throughout his career: the outsider who cannot — or will not — integrate into society, the tension between ambition and stasis, and the mystery of individual destiny. Where *Hunger*'s protagonist is driven by a desperate need to write and be recognized, Abel Brodersen represents the opposite: a man who lets go of all ambition. The two novels form a dialectic of striving and renunciation.
