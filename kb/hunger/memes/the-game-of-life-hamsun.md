*The Game of Life* (*Livets Spil*) is the second play in Knut Hamsun's dramatic trilogy, published in 1896. It follows *At the Gates of the Kingdom* (1895) and precedes *Sunset Glow* (1898).

## Setting and Plot

Unlike the first and third plays, which are set in Christiania, *The Game of Life* takes place in the Northland. Ivar Kareno, now 39, appears as a "heaven-storming truth-seeker" — the middle phase of his arc from youthful rebel to eventual renegade.

## Björkman's Assessment

Edwin Björkman's introduction to *Hunger* describes the trilogy's progression: Kareno is "first presented to us at the age of 29, then at 39, and finally at 50." The trilogy's lesson, Björkman suggests, "seems to be that every one is a rebel at 30 and a renegade at 50." In *The Game of Life*, Kareno occupies the midpoint of this arc — still a seeker, still in rebellion, but on the threshold of the compromises that await in middle age.

The Northland setting of the second play is significant. Hamsun frequently associates the region with intense, visionary states of consciousness, and Kareno's truth-seeking in this environment connects his intellectual passion to the elemental power of the northern landscape.
