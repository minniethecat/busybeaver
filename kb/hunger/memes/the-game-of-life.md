# The Game of Life

## Overview

**The Game of Life** (*Livets Spil*) is a play by [[knut-hamsun|Knut Hamsun]], published in 1896. The English-language preface to *Hunger* places it in the context of [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s dramatic output, noting that it followed his dramatic trilogy and "was followed by 'The Game of Life' in 1896 and '[[sunset-glow|Sunset Glow]]' in 1898" (P04-013). The play represents [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s sustained engagement with theatrical form during the mid-1890s, a period in which he produced several works for the stage.

## Description

The play belongs to [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s middle period, coming after the novels that established his reputation — *Hunger* (1890), *[[mysteries|Mysteries]]* (1892), *[[pan|Pan]]* (1894) — and during a phase when he turned increasingly to drama. The preface describes [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]] as producing a series of plays in these years, treating the dramatic form as a serious artistic medium rather than a diversion from fiction. The title *Livets Spil* — literally "Life's Game" — suggests thematic continuity with [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s broader concerns: the arbitrariness of fate, the struggle of the individual against society, and the irrational currents that shape human existence.

## Relationship to Hunger

While *The Game of Life* postdates *Hunger* by six years, it belongs to the same period of [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s most intense creative productivity, when his reputation as a leading figure of the neo-romantic movement was being consolidated. The novel and the play share [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s characteristic psychological intensity, though the dramatic form imposed different constraints than the first-person narrative voice that made *Hunger* revolutionary. The preface's mention of the play alongside the earlier dramatic trilogy positions it as part of [[knut-hamsun|Hamsun]]'s contribution to Scandinavian theatre in the 1890s.
