## Overview

Delusion in Knut Hamsun's *Hunger* functions as both a symptom of the narrator's physical starvation and a psychological defense mechanism against the intolerable reality of his condition. The narrator oscillates between moments of grandiose self-assurance and crushing despair, often constructing elaborate fictions about his own talents and prospects that bear no relation to his actual circumstances.

## In Hunger

The narrator's delusional thinking manifests early as a coping strategy. He acknowledges "the cherished, deluded hopes, and fresh endeavours that always resulted in nothing had done my courage to death" (001-006), yet he continues to generate new fantasies of imminent success. He tells himself, "Perhaps even this very day I might commence an article on the 'Crimes of Futurity,' 'Freedom of Will,' or what not, at any rate, something worth reading, something for which I would at least get ten shillings" (001-014), despite a long history of rejections.

His delusions often take the form of invented identities. He gives a stranger a false name and invents an entire persona on the spot, "carried away by the singular whim of the moment; I threw off this preposterous lie without premeditation, without any object" (003-031). These fictions provide temporary escape from his degraded circumstances.

Starvation intensifies the delusional state. He experiences what he calls "the strange freaks one's thoughts are guilty of when one is starving" (002-105), describing sensations of floating and dissolution. The boundary between creative imagination and pathological delusion becomes increasingly blurred as the novel progresses, with the narrator himself unsure whether his visions represent artistic inspiration or psychological deterioration.

By the novel's end, after eating, he notes a shift: "the wildest thoughts, too, surged up anew in my head" (004-142), suggesting that the relationship between food, mind, and delusion is not straightforward — nourishment itself can trigger new waves of irrational thinking.
