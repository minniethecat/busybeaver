The Blind Man is a minor figure in Knut Hamsun's *Hunger*, encountered briefly by the narrator during his wanderings through Kristiania.

## Narrative Function

In Hamsun's novel, disabled and marginal figures — the blind, the lame, the elderly — function as doubles for the narrator himself. His own condition (hunger, social exclusion, psychological instability) aligns him with society's outcasts, even as his pride resists this identification. The blind man, unable to see the narrator's shabby clothing, represents a potential encounter free from the visual judgment that so obsesses the protagonist.
