Vaterland Church was a church in the Vaterland district of Kristiania (Oslo), serving the working-class population of one of the city's poorest neighborhoods.

## In Hunger

Churches appear periodically in *Hunger* as landmarks and potential refuges, though they provide the narrator with no spiritual consolation. The Vaterland Church, situated in the district where much of the novel's action takes place, forms part of the urban topography that the narrator traverses in his wanderings.

## Context

The Vaterland district was radically redeveloped in the mid-20th century, and much of its 19th-century architecture — including the church — no longer exists. The novel thus preserves a record of a Kristiania that has since been transformed.
