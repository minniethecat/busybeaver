Akershus Fortress (Akershus festning) is a medieval castle and fortress complex that dominates the harbour of Oslo (Kristiania). Built in the late 13th century under King Håkon V, it has served as a royal residence, military stronghold, and prison over the centuries.

## Significance in Kristiania

By the late 19th century, Akershus was primarily a military installation and a prominent visual landmark. Its position on a promontory overlooking the fjord made it visible from much of the city, including the harbour area and the lower-lying eastern districts. It represented institutional power — military, governmental, and historical — in the physical landscape of the city.

## In Hunger

Akershus Fortress forms part of the visual and spatial backdrop of Hamsun's *Hunger*. While not directly described in detail, its presence on the Kristiania skyline contributes to the novel's sense of place. The fortress's associations with authority, confinement (it housed a prison), and Norwegian national identity create resonances with the novel's themes of power, imprisonment (both literal and metaphorical), and the narrator's ambiguous national belonging.
