# Gyldendal Norsk Forlag

## Overview

**Gyldendal Norsk Forlag** is Norway's largest and most influential publishing house. Founded in 1925 when the Norwegian interests of the Danish Gyldendalske Boghandel were separated into an independent company, Gyldendal became the primary publisher of Knut Hamsun's works in Norway and has maintained this role for nearly a century.

## Historical Significance

The publishing house traces its origins to the Danish Gyldendal, founded in Copenhagen in 1770 by Søren Gyldendal. Before the 1925 separation, the Danish house had already published many Norwegian authors, including Hamsun's early works. After the split, Gyldendal Norsk Forlag consolidated Norway's literary output, publishing not only Hamsun but also Henrik Ibsen, Sigrid Undset, and other major figures of Scandinavian literature.

## Relationship to Hamsun

Gyldendal published the collected works of Knut Hamsun, including the many novels, plays, and poetry collections referenced in Björkman's preface to *Hunger*. The publishing house's catalog documents the full arc of Hamsun's career: from *Hunger* (1890) through *Pan* (1894), *The Growth of the Soil* (1917, Nobel Prize 1920), *Wayfarers* (1927), and *On Overgrown Paths* (1949). Gyldendal's stewardship of Hamsun's literary legacy has been central to maintaining his place in the Norwegian and world literary canon.
