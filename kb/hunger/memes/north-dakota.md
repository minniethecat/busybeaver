North Dakota is a state in the Upper Midwest region of the United States. During the 1880s, when Knut Hamsun visited, it was part of the Dakota Territory, a frontier region undergoing rapid agricultural settlement.

## Hamsun's Experience

Hamsun worked as a farmhand on the North Dakota plains during his first American sojourn (1882–1884). The vast, flat agricultural landscape — so different from Norway's fjords and mountains — made a powerful impression. Edwin Björkman notes in his introduction to *Hunger* that Hamsun "worked chiefly on the plains of North Dakota and in the streets of Chicago."

The North Dakota experience contributed to Hamsun's sense of America as a land of raw material abundance but cultural and spiritual poverty. This critical perspective would inform *The Spiritual Life of Modern America* (1889). At the same time, the frontier's scale and elemental quality may have reinforced Hamsun's literary preoccupation with nature as a force that dwarfs and defines human consciousness — a theme central to his later novels like *Pan* and *Growth of the Soil*.
