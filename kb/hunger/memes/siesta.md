## Overview

*Siesta* is a short story collection by Knut Hamsun, published in 1897. It marks the beginning of a prolific period of literary output that continued through 1912, and is noted for containing a charming love story that embodies what critics have called "the tenderest note in his production" (P04-015).

## Description

Published one year before Hamsun's celebrated novel *Victoria* (1898), the collection forms part of a phase in which Hamsun alternated between novels, short fiction, and travel writing. The years following *Siesta* saw the rapid appearance of *Victoria*, *In Wonderland* (1903), *Brushwood* (1903), and several other volumes, demonstrating Hamsun's sustained creative energy (P04-015).

## Relationship to Hunger

*Siesta* was published seven years after *Hunger* (1890). While *Hunger* established Hamsun's reputation with its intense psychological focus on a single protagonist, *Siesta* shows him working in a more varied key, handling multiple characters and tones within the short story format. The collection prefigures the lyrical vein that would make *Victoria* one of his most beloved works (P04-015).
