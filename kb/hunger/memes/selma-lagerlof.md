Selma Lagerlöf (1858–1940) was a Swedish author and teacher who became the first woman to win the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1909. Her works blend folklore, legend, and realism in a distinctive narrative style.

## Major Works

Her debut novel *Gösta Berling's Saga* (1891) brought her immediate recognition. *The Wonderful Adventures of Nils* (1906–1907) became a classic of children's literature, originally commissioned as a geography reader for Swedish schools. *Jerusalem* (1901–1902) established her international reputation.

## In Björkman's Assessment

In his introduction to Knut Hamsun's *Hunger*, Edwin Björkman places Lagerlöf among the foremost Scandinavian writers after Ibsen and Strindberg, noting that both she and Henrik Pontoppidan "seem to have less than he [Hamsun] of that width of outlook, validity of interpretation and authority of tone that made the greater masters what they were." This assessment reflects the critical hierarchy of early 20th-century Scandinavian letters while acknowledging her significant standing.
