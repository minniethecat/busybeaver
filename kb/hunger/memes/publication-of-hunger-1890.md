The publication of the completed novel *Hunger* (*Sult*) in 1890 marked Knut Hamsun's definitive literary breakthrough and a watershed moment in European literary history.

## Publication History

A sketch of *Hunger* had appeared in 1888 in the Danish periodical *New Earth*, attracting immediate attention. The completed novel, published in Copenhagen in 1890, transformed literary attention into lasting recognition. Hamsun was, as Björkman writes, "greeted as one of the chief heralds of the neo-romantic movement then spreading rapidly through the Scandinavian north."

## Significance

The publication of *Hunger* represents the emergence of a new kind of novel — one that prioritized psychological interiority, subjective perception, and the irrational dimensions of experience over the social documentation of 19th-century realism. The novel's radical narrative technique, its refusal of conventional morality, and its intense focus on bodily and mental states established Hamsun as a major, original voice.
