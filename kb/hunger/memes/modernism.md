Modernism is the broad cultural movement that transformed the arts in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, driven by industrialization, urbanization, secularization, and new understandings of psychology and consciousness.

## Key Characteristics

Literary modernism is characterized by: a turn inward to subjective consciousness; formal experimentation and fragmentation; rejection of realist conventions; exploration of temporality and memory; engagement with urban experience and alienation; and a self-conscious relationship to literary tradition (often parodic or ironic).

## Hunger's Place in Modernism

Knut Hamsun's *Hunger* (1890) occupies a pivotal position in the emergence of modernism. Published before the canonical modernist works — before Joyce, Woolf, Kafka, Proust — it anticipates many of the movement's defining features: the intense focus on a single consciousness, the rejection of realist objectivity, the fragmentation of narrative, and the rendering of urban alienation. Its influence on later modernist writers is widely acknowledged.
