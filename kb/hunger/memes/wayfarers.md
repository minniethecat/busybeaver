## Overview

*Wayfarers* (*Landstrykere*), also translated as *Vagabonds*, is a novel by Knut Hamsun published in 1927. It is the first volume of the August trilogy, which follows the wanderer and adventurer August through his travels and schemes across Norway.

## Description

The novel introduces August, a restless wanderer and charismatic drifter who returns to his home village of Polden after years at sea. His arrival disrupts the settled routines of the community, bringing both energy and chaos. Through August's exploits, Hamsun explores the tension between rooted agrarian life and the rootless, modern spirit of adventure and commerce. The novel blends picaresque humor with a deeper meditation on belonging and change in rural Norway.

## Relationship to Hunger

*Wayfarers* extends Hamsun's exploration of the outsider figure — a theme central to *Hunger*. While *Hunger*'s protagonist is trapped in urban isolation, August embodies a different kind of outsider: the restless wanderer who cannot settle. Both novels examine the friction between individual impulse and social expectation, though *Wayfarers* does so through a broader social canvas and a more comic register.
