# Edwin Björkman

## Overview

**Edwin Björkman** (1866–1941) was the author of the critical-biographical preface to the English edition of Knut Hamsun's *Hunger*. His essay, signed at its conclusion, provides the most detailed contemporary account of Hamsun's life, literary career, and significance within Scandinavian and European letters.

## In the Narrative

Björkman's preface (section P04 of the corpus) constitutes a substantial critical essay that frames the novel for English-speaking readers. He traces Hamsun's biography from his birth in central Norway (P04-005) through his diverse occupations (P04-008) to his literary breakthrough with *Hunger* (P04-010). The preface also surveys Hamsun's subsequent works and assesses his place in world literature.

Björkman's critical judgments are embedded in his biographical narrative. He describes Hamsun as "an individualistic romanticist and a highly subjective aristocrat" (P04-003), and his assessment of Hamsun's significance — "he must perforce be placed in the very front rank of the world's living writers" (P04-018) — represents an early canonization of Hamsun's achievement.

## Relationship to the Narrator

As the author of the preface, Björkman is the first interpreter of *Hunger* for English-speaking audiences. His framing shapes how the novel is read — the biographical and literary-historical context he provides becomes inseparable from the text itself.
