## Overview

*Children of the Time* (*Børn av Tiden*) is a novel by Knut Hamsun, published in 1913. It marks a shift in Hamsun's career toward broader social novels, and is the first of the Segelfoss duology, followed by *Segelfoss Town* in 1915.

## Description

The novel is set in the fictional northern Norwegian town of Segelfoss and portrays the social changes brought by industrialization and modern capitalism. The narrative traces the decline of the old landed gentry, represented by Lieutenant Willatz Holmsen, and the rise of a new commercial class. Hamsun presents a critical, often satirical view of the modernizing forces reshaping Norwegian rural society (P04-016).

## Relationship to Hunger

*Children of the Time* represents a significant departure from *Hunger*. While *Hunger* focuses on the internal, psychological experience of a single protagonist in an urban setting, *Children of the Time* takes a panoramic view of an entire community undergoing economic transformation. The novel anticipates Hamsun's later, more explicitly social-critical works such as *The Growth of the Soil*.
