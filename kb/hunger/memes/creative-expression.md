# Creative Expression

*Hunger* is not only a novel about a starving writer — it is a novel that performs its own aesthetic theory, embodying the creative principles it names. The protagonist's identity as an artist, his struggle with form, and the text's self-conscious literary techniques make creativity itself a central theme. This index gathers pages that explore the artistic, aesthetic, and formal dimensions of Hamsun's work.
