The Caucasus is a mountainous region between the Black Sea and the Caspian Sea, divided between present-day Russia, Georgia, Armenia, and Azerbaijan. In the late 19th century, it was part of the Russian Empire.

## Hamsun's Journey

In 1899, Knut Hamsun traveled through Russia to the Caucasus. The resulting sketches were published in 1903 as *In Wonderland* (*I Æventyrland*). The journey reflects Hamsun's characteristic restlessness and his interest in regions beyond the familiar Scandinavian and Western European circuit.

The Caucasus sketches represent Hamsun's observational and travel-writing mode, distinct from his fiction but marked by the same intense subjectivity and impressionistic style that characterizes his novels.
