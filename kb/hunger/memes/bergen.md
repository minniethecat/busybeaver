Bergen is Norway's second-largest city, situated on the western coast. In the 19th century, it was a major port and commercial center, rivaling Kristiania in economic importance.

## Historical Significance

Bergen was the traditional gateway between Norway and the wider world, particularly through the Hanseatic trade network. In the late 19th century, it was also a point of departure for Norwegian emigrants bound for North America.

## Relation to Hunger

While *Hunger* is set entirely in Kristiania, Bergen forms part of the Norwegian national geography that the novel implicitly invokes. The contrast between coastal Bergen and inland Kristiania maps a broader cultural geography of Norway.
