# Asia

## Overview

Asia is a geographic region associated with pygmies or other small-statured peoples in ancient ethnographic accounts. Edward Tyson discusses this location in _A Philological Essay Concerning the Pygmies of the Ancients_ (1699) as part of his survey of classical sources.

## Ancient Descriptions

Ancient authors placed pygmies in this region. Tyson examines these geographic claims and assesses their plausibility based on what was known of the region's natural history.

## Tyson's Assessment

Tyson evaluates whether the ancient placement of pygmies in this region is consistent with known fauna, particularly the distribution of great apes. His geographic analysis is an integral part of his broader natural-historical argument.
