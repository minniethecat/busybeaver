## Biography

Paolo Mantegazza (1831–1910) was an Italian neurologist, physiologist, and anthropologist. He was one of the first Italian proponents of Darwinian evolutionary theory and established the first Museum of Anthropology in Florence. His wide-ranging work included studies of human stature, racial classification, and the physiology of emotions.

## Stature measurements cited by Tyson

In [[section-4|Section 4]], Tyson references stature data obtained by Mantegazza as part of his comparative analysis of human body sizes. The measurements are noted alongside those of [[topinard|Paul Topinard]], another anthropologist whose data Tyson consulted.

> figure which corresponds closely with that obtained by Mantegazza and quoted by Topinard. (004-027)

The stature figures contributed to Tyson's broader argument about the natural range of human height across populations, which he used to contextualize the reported diminutive size of pygmy peoples.
