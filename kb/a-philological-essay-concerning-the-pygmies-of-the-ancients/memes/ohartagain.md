## Identity

O'Hartagain (also spelled Ua hArtacáin) was an Irish bard whose poem describing the _sidhe_—the fairy folk of Irish mythology—is preserved in medieval manuscript sources. Little is known of his life beyond the poem itself, which belongs to the corpus of early Irish nature and mythological verse.

## The O'Hartagain poem in Tyson

In [[section-5|Section 5]], Tyson discusses O'Hartagain's poem as evidence for the theory—championed by [[macritchie|David MacRitchie]]—that Irish fairy lore preserves memories of an aboriginal pygmy race. The poem describes the sidhe as diminutive supernatural beings dwelling in mounds, a description MacRitchie interpreted as folk memory of Neolithic "pygmy" inhabitants.

> O'Hartagain's poem, which seems to bear in Mr. MacRitchie's favour (005-183)

The poem is quoted in [[section-5|Section 5]] with the opening line "Behold the sidhe before your eyes," and forms part of Tyson's survey of cross-cultural evidence for pygmy-related folk traditions.
