# Herodotus

## Biography

Herodotus (c. 484 BC – c. 425 BC) was a Greek Historian whose works are cited in Edward Tyson's _A Philological Essay Concerning the Pygmies of the Ancients_ (1699). Their writings on pygmies, dwarfs, or related subjects contributed to the ancient ethnographic tradition that Tyson systematically examined.

## Role in Tyson's Essay

Tyson references Herodotus's account as part of his comprehensive survey of ancient sources on pygmies. Their testimony forms part of the classical tradition that Tyson sought to reconcile with natural-historical observation.
