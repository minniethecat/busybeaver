# Pierre Gassendi

## Biography

Pierre Gassendi (1592–1655) was a French Philosopher, Scientist, and Astronomer, known for his revival of Epicurean atomism and his empirical approach to natural philosophy.

A correspondent and friend of Nicolas-Claude Fabri de Peiresc (1580–1637), the great French antiquary and patron of science, Gassendi wrote a detailed biography of Peiresc that included substantial observations on natural history.

Tyson drew upon this material for his own essay on the pygmies.

## Relevance to the Pygmy Question

Gassendi appears in Tyson's essay primarily as a source of ethnographic and natural-historical observation. In _Vita Peireskii_, Gassendi recorded accounts of ape hunting and behaviour that Tyson found relevant to his argument.

In Section 12, discussing the agility and speed of apes, Tyson notes that people of quality course them as they would stags. He adds that "this sort of hunting is the King's usual divertisement," then references Gassendi's report that apes were commonly hunted in Africa (012-126).

In Section 18, Tyson returns to Gassendi when discussing the stature and capabilities of apes. He observes that "neither _Tulpius_, nor _Gassendus_, nor any that I have hitherto met with, have adjusted the full stature of this Animal" (018-059). This acknowledges Gassendi as a key source while noting the limits of his data.

Gassendi's _Vita Peireskii_ also provided Tyson with remarkable details of ape capabilities. As Tyson relates:

> "That they will play upon a Pipe or Cittern, carry Water in a Pitcher, &c."
> (018-132)

These near-human behaviours are precisely the sort of observations that, Tyson argues, led ancient travelers to mistake apes for diminutive human beings.

At the close of the essay, Tyson cites Gassendi alongside [[dapper]] and Garcilasso de la Vega, situating him within a network of early modern observers whose empirical reports supported Tyson's conclusions about ancient pygmy lore (018-150).

## Key Works Cited by Tyson

- **_Vita Peireskii_ (Life of Peiresc)** — Book 5, containing observations on African apes, their hunting, behaviour, and physical characteristics.
