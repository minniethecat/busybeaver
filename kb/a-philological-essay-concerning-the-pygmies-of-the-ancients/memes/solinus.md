# Gaius Julius Solinus

## Biography

Gaius Julius Solinus (fl. 3rd century AD) was a Latin geographer and grammarian. His surviving work, *Collectanea Rerum Memorabilium* (also known as the *Polyhistor*), is a compendium of geographical and historical curiosities drawn primarily from Pliny the Elder and Pomponius Mela.

## Relevance to the Pygmy Question

Solinus appears in Tyson's essay as one of the Roman compilers who transmitted Greek accounts of Pygmies to later readers. Tyson notes that Solinus, along with Aelian, Pliny, Mela, and Philostratus, all reproduced material originally derived from earlier Greek authorities. (012-014)

Tyson further observes that Pliny and Solinus both mentioned Pygmies among the monstrous races described in ancient ethnography. (014-075)

Solinus is cited again later in the essay when Tyson discusses geographical arguments. Tyson notes that Pliny, Solinus, and Aethicus all contributed to the geographical tradition that located Pygmies in specific regions. (016-066)

## Key Works Cited by Tyson

- **Collectanea Rerum Memorabilium** (*Polyhistor*) — A geographical compendium heavily dependent on Pliny's *Naturalis Historia*, widely read in the Middle Ages and Renaissance
