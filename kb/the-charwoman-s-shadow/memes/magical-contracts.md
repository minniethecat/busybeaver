# Magical Contracts

[[magic|Magic]] in *[[the-charwoman|The Charwoman]]'s [[shadow|Shadow]]* is fundamentally contractual. Every act of teaching, every spell performed, every [[shadow]] taken is governed by an explicit agreement — a named price, a stated fee, a bargain struck. This transactional nature distinguishes Dunsany's [[magic]] from the innate wizardry of later fantasy traditions.

## Definition

(004-020) [[the-magician|The Magician]] is revealed as a shrewd bargainer: "he had a few more words to say first about the worthlessness of [[shadow|shadows]], and the sudden disclosure of the point was not in accordance with his plans for conducting a bargain." [[magic|Magic]] is negotiated like any commercial transaction — [[the-magician|the Magician]] softens the price by first devaluing the currency.

(007-005) The fee is standardised and non-negotiable: "The fee is your [[shadow]]." Every student of [[magic|the Art]] pays the same price. The contract is universal — there are no scholarships, no waivers, no exceptions.

(004-032) Refusal is possible but consequential: "he held to his [[shadow]] and would not part with it; and the more the [[the-magician|magician]] proved its uselessness the more stubborn he became." The contract can be declined, but the alternative is to return home "empty-handed."

## The Terms

(004-030) [[the-magician|The Magician]] explains what the [[shadow]] is used for: equipping [[supernatural-beings|spirits]] "that serve me better when equipped with a [[shadow]]." The contract includes a use clause — the [[shadow]] is not destroyed but repurposed, given to a spirit as an earthly anchor.

(003-017) The long-term consequences of the contract are demonstrated by [[the-charwoman|the charwoman]]: "He took it away and put it in his box. What did I know of the need one has of a [[shadow]]: that they would not speak to me, would not let me live?" The contract's fine print — social ostracism, eternal servitude — is not disclosed upfront.

## Contractual Obligations

(005-060) The apprentice's side of the contract includes diligent study: [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] "worked hard none the less, for he thought to be a better match for the powers of the [[the-magician|magician]] when he knew at least one of his mysteries." The apprentice receives knowledge in ex[[transmutation-principle|change]] for labour and, eventually, the [[shadow]].

(007-002) [[the-magician|The Magician]]'s control over communications enforces the contract's information asymmetry. Letters from home are intercepted, limiting the apprentice's ability to seek outside counsel about the bargain.

## Narrative Significance

[[magic|Magic]]al contracts in the novel mirror feudal and early modern economic relations — obligation, service, and payment in kind. [[the-magician|The Magician]] is landlord, employer, and creditor in one; the apprentice is tenant, worker, and debtor. This economic framing of [[magic]] is one of the novel's most distinctive features.
