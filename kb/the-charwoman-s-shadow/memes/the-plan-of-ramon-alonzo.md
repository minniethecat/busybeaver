# The Plan of Ramon Alonzo

The Plan of [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] is the central moral and narrative engine of *[[the-charwoman-s-shadow|The Charwoman's Shadow]]* — a secret scheme to rescue [[the-charwoman|the charwoman]]'s [[shadow]] from [[the-box|the magician's box]], conceived in the early chapters and carried through to the novel's climax. It represents the tension between [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]]'s avowed purpose (learning [[gold-transmutation|gold-transmutation]] for his sister's dowry) and his true vocation: a selfless quest undertaken for an old woman he barely knows.

## Origin

(005-032) The Plan is born as an interruption to [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]]'s stated purpose:

> "But the more he planned to make gold, the more another plan came jutting into his mind, perpetually pushing away his original purpose; a plan fantastic enough, a sentimental, generous, youthful plan, no less than a plan to find the magician's box, and open it and get the charwoman's shadow, and give it to her to dance once more at her heels or float away over the buttercups."

The Plan emerges unbidden — it "jutts into his mind" against his will, "perpetually pushing away" the mercenary purpose for which he was sent to [[the-house-in-the-wood|the house in the wood]]. Dunsany characterises it as "fantastic," "sentimental," "generous," and "youthful" — qualities that distinguish it sharply from the cold, calculating logic of [[magic|the Black Art]].

## Development

(005-060) The Plan coexists with [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]]'s magical studies:

> "That day as Ramon Alonzo sat at his work his mind was full of his plans to rescue the shadow, yet he worked hard none the less, for he thought to be a better match for the powers of the magician when he knew at least one of his mysteries."

He justifies his continued study of [[magic|the Black Art]] as a means to the Plan's end — only by mastering [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s own craft can he hope to outwit him.

(010-018) The Plan's first major setback:

> "And Ramon Alonzo's plans were laughed away, as in later days the Armada was broken by storm, and so he forsook his intention to return to the house in the wood."

After [[the-magician|the Magician]] reveals the fee for [[gold-transmutation|transmutation]].

## The Conspiracy

(013-028) [[the-charwoman|The charwoman]] becomes an active conspirator:

> "And then Ramon Alonzo knew that she hoped, for she had taken a part in his plan."

(013-029) The Plan's selflessness is underscored:

> "Thus they plotted together; but Ramon Alonzo thought nothing of his own shadow, planning only to rescue hers."

He is willing to risk his own [[shadow]] — and therefore his soul — for the sake of an aged charwoman.

## The Method

(019-023) The Plan takes technical shape:

> "But this plan involved writing, for he meant to write the syllables of the spell that opened the shadow-box, one by one amongst other writings, and to have the Master read them and pronounce them, learning by watching his lips."

[[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] devises an ingenious method: embedding the spell-syllables within decoy texts, tricking [[the-magician|the Magician]] into unwittingly revealing the pronunciation of the opening spell.

(020-024) The Plan is refined through sleepless strategising:

> "Plans of caution and plans of impatience came to Ramon Alonzo that night; and by the early hours he blended them, and decided to wait three days before asking the Master to read another script."

## The Magician's Awareness

(020-032) [[the-magician|The Magician]] suspects but cannot confirm:

> "Doubtless he knew not Ramon Alonzo's plan; yet the stir of a fetter upon a floor of stone may betray the hope of a slave to escape his prison."

The analogy is telling: [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] is a "slave" seeking to "escape his prison" — the Plan is not merely a scheme but an act of resistance against magical bondage.

## Significance

The Plan transforms [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] from a passive pupil into an active moral agent. Where his official purpose ([[gold-transmutation|making gold]]) serves worldly ambition, the Plan serves justice — restoring to [[the-charwoman|a poor old woman]] what was taken from her. It is the hidden plot within the plot, the counter-narrative to [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s curriculum, and the mechanism by which Dunsany elevates a fantasy novel about magic into a story about conscience.
