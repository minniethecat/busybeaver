## Definition

Doors and thresholds in the novel function as more than architectural features — they are magical boundaries that separate protected spaces from the outside world, mark transitions between ordinary and enchanted realms, and serve as sites where spells of exclusion and access are enacted. The magician's house is defined by its doors: the green door to the forest, the black studded door to the workroom, and the many "towering doors in the darkness" [005-005] that lead to unknown chambers.

## The Green Door

The magician's house announces itself through its door: "there just in front of the mossy stone that the young man had made his seat was an old green door all studded with old green knobs. The door was ajar" [002-011]. The door materializes along with the house itself, summoned — like the house — by the waving of the scroll. It mediates between the dark wood outside and the Arabian music within, a threshold between worlds.

> There just in front of the mossy stone that the young man had made his seat was an old green door all studded with old green knobs. The door was ajar. [002-011]

## The Spell Across the Lintel

Thresholds can be magically warded. The charwoman tells Ramon Alonzo that she cannot enter the magician's workroom because "He has put a spell against me across the lintel, so that I may not enter" [005-022]. This spell — placed not on the door itself but across its upper frame — demonstrates that thresholds are sites of magical control, where the magician's authority is inscribed into the architecture.

> "I know nothing in that room. He has put a spell against me across the lintel, so that I may not enter." [005-022]

## Doors to Unknown Spaces

The magician's house contains doors that open onto inexplicable spaces. Ramon Alonzo discovers that "Sometimes he came on towering doors in the darkness, and opened them and found great halls, whose blackness their narrow windows could do little to lighten" [005-005]. These doors suggest that the house's interior geography exceeds its exterior dimensions — a magical expansion of space that doors both conceal and reveal.

## The Door as Narrative Device

The novel repeatedly uses doors as structural markers of transition. The magician's lessons are interrupted by knocks at "the door to the forest" [012-005]; the green door "opened to him" upon his safe return [001-007]; and the magician summons a draught to close his door with theatrical finality [002-016]. Each door-related action signals a shift in the narrative's magical tension.
