# The Padlock

The padlock on the Magician's box of shadows is both a physical object and a magical one. It can be opened by a key (mundane) or by a spell (arcane), and the combination of both security systems — lock and spell together — represents the layered nature of magical protection in the novel.

## Description

(024-008) The padlock is first encountered as an obstacle: "Hastily Ramon Alonzo removed the padlock, and cautiously opened the box." The removal is physical — he has either found the key or picked the lock — suggesting that the padlock's primary function is mundane security.

(024-010) The magical layer is tested methodically: "he shut the box and put the padlock on, and said the spell to it; and it opened again. He did this two or three times." The spell-and-padlock combination can be operated by anyone who knows both the physical mechanism and the spoken command — magical security is reproducible, not unique to the Magician.

## Dual Security

The padlock embodies the novel's broader principle that magic operates alongside mundane systems, not in opposition to them. The Magician trusts neither lock alone nor spell alone — he uses both, layering physical and arcane security as any prudent householder might layer a lock and a watchdog.
