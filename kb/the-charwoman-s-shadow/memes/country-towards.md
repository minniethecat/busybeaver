# The Country Towards Moon's Rising

A directional region to the east of Aragona, named by its relation to the moon's rising rather than by any fixed political boundary. In the novel's folklore, it is the land from which witches are thought to originate — a supernatural borderland between the known and the unknown.

## Folk Belief

The folk of Aragona and its surrounding villages regard the Country Towards Moon's Rising with deep suspicion. When a stranger appears in a village, the instinctive assumption is that she has come from the east:

> "She is a witch-woman, for none knows her here; and hath seen our village upon starry nights riding by broom from the Country Towards Moon's Rising." (027-003)

The woman accused of witchcraft denies the charge — "not from the Country Towards Moon's Rising" (027-004) — but the suspicion itself reveals the cultural geography: east is where the unnatural comes from.

## Location & Geography

The Country Towards Moon's Rising is not mapped with precision; it exists as a directional concept. It lies "due eastwards" from Aragona, beyond the known bounds of Tower and village. The magician, in his final journey at the novel's end, strides "onward guided by dawns, always due eastwards" (030-009), making for the "earthward rampart of the Country Towards Moon's Rising" (030-010).

## Role in the Narrative

### The Witch Scare of Chapter XXVII

The title of Chapter XXVII — "They Dread That a Witch Has Ridden from the Country Towards Moon's Rising" — frames the episode's central anxiety. The folk's fear of the eastward country drives their persecution of unknown women, creating the atmosphere of supernatural dread that pervades the novel's later chapters.

### The Magician's Destination

In Chapter XXX, the magician's final journey takes him eastward toward the Country Towards Moon's Rising. The "gates that led through the earthward rampart" (030-010) suggest a mythological boundary — perhaps the edge of mortal lands. The magician "limped towards it with all his magical following" (030-011), departing the novel's familiar geography for the unknown.

## Symbolic Function

The Country Towards Moon's Rising functions as the novel's Orient — not a real place but a projection of Western European folk anxieties about the east. The moon, associated with magic, madness, and transformation, rises from this direction; to travel toward the moon's rising is to move toward the source of magical power itself. The magician's eastward departure at the novel's end echoes the tradition of the sunset journey — except he goes toward the *rising*, suggesting a cycle of renewal rather than decline.
