# The Love Potion Commission

[[mirandola|Mirandola]]'s commission of a [[love-potion|love potion]] from her brother is the event that binds the novel's [[magic]]al and social plots together. What begins as a sister's romantic scheme becomes a test of [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]]'s [[magic]]al competence and moral judgment.

## The Demand

(012-000) [[mirandola|Mirandola]]'s request is presented not as a supernatural appeal but as a family favour. She knows her brother is learning [[magic]]; she has a practical need; the connection is obvious. Her matter-of-factness about [[magic]] — treating it as a service industry — contrasts with [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s portentous ritualism.

## The Compounding

(013-003) The [[love-potion|potion]]'s preparation demonstrates [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]]'s growing skill: "When [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] entered the room that was sacred to [[magic]] he saw with a sudden joy that this opportunity was not yet to be his." The room must be accessed at the right moment — "by the palest earliest light" — when [[the-magician|the Magician]] is absent. The furtive preparation underscores the apprentice's ambiguous position.

(013-000) The compounding of the [[love-potion|potion]] is [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]]'s first independent [[magic]]al creation, marking his transition from student to practitioner.

## Consequences

(018-000) The [[love-potion|potion]]'s effects ripple outward: "The look on the face of [[duke-of-shadow-valley|the Duke]] of [[shadow-valley|Shadow Valley]] was gradually growing stranger." The [[magic]] works — but its consequences extend beyond the intended romantic outcome, entangling [[duke-of-shadow-valley|the Duke]], [[mirandola|Mirandola]], and ultimately the broader [[shadow]] economy.
