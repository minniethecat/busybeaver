# Gulvarez

Gulvarez — often styled Señor Gulvarez — is a member of the Spanish gentry whose castle serves as a crucial meeting-point in the novel's social geography. When [[duke-of-shadow-valley|the Duke]] of [[shadow-valley|Shadow Valley]] passes through his lands, Gulvarez's hospitality creates the occasion for social encounters that ripple through the plot.

## Role in the Story

(006-004) He is introduced through rumour: "they heard that [[duke-of-shadow-valley|the Duke]] of [[shadow-valley|Shadow Valley]], being upon a journey, would rest a night at his castle with Gulvarez." His role is defined by his capacity as host — his castle provides neutral ground where social status can be negotiated.

(006-029) His connection to [[lord-of-the-tower|the Lord]] of the Tower is significant enough that [[lord-of-the-tower|the Lord]] consults him on family matters: "he said that he would consult Gulvarez on this; which he had not intended to say." Gulvarez functions as a social arbiter, someone whose opinion carries weight in matters of marriage and alliance.

## Social Function

Gulvarez represents the stable, conventional world that [[magic]] disrupts. He is not [[magic]]al, not ambitious, not particularly distinctive — but his very ordinariness makes him a useful anchor in the novel's social fabric. His castle is where [[duke-of-shadow-valley|the Duke]] pauses, where news gathers, where marriages are contemplated.
