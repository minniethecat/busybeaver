Music pervades the world of *The Charwoman's Shadow* as an expression of both supernatural presence and ordinary human joy, bridging the magical and the mundane.

## Definition

Music in the novel serves multiple functions: it signals the presence of magic, expresses human emotion, and marks the passage between ordinary and enchanted spaces. When Ramon Alonzo first approaches the magician's house, "the silence that cloaked its appearance almost instantly glided away, giving place to Arabian music that haunted the air overhead and plaintive Hindu love-chants that yearned in the dark" [001-115]. The magician's wine carries "a memory and a music" [001-161], suggesting music is woven into the substance of magical experience.

## Manifestations

The most sustained musical presence is the Hindu chants hovering above the magician's house—songs lured from India over many years, sung "in youth by folk now withered with age, or by men and women long gathered to Indian tombs" [001-467]. These spectral melodies create a permanent atmosphere of otherworldliness. In the human realm, Anemone remembers walking "singing" through Aragona's evening slopes, where fairies would dance with her shadow and "play with its curls" [001-203].

Dance appears alongside song throughout the novel. The shadows in the Tower perform "slow dances with the solemn folds of the tapestry" [001-075]. Anemone's lost shadow once "danced for me as I went to bed, all over the walls and ceilings" [001-203], its movements growing nimbler as the candle flickered more wearily. The wind itself "sang on triumphantly, singing of spaces unconcerned with man, blue fields of the wind's roving, dark gardens amongst the stars" [001-113], suggesting that music in its broadest sense is the voice of a universe larger than human concerns.

## Narrative Significance

Music marks thresholds between worlds. The Arabian music and Hindu chants announce the magician's domain as a place apart from ordinary Spain. Anemone's remembered singing represents the lost joy of a life before her shadow was taken—a joy that she can never recover. The contrast between the magician's supernatural music and the charwoman's silenced song underscores the novel's theme of what is gained and lost in transactions with magic.
