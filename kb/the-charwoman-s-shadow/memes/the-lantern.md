# The Lantern

The lantern in *[[the-charwoman|The Charwoman]]'s [[shadow|Shadow]]* is a deceptively simple object — a source of light in a house that would otherwise be dark. But in a novel about [[shadow|shadows]], any source of light carries symbolic weight. The lantern is passed from [[the-magician|the Magician]] to [[the-charwoman|the charwoman]] to [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]], its illumination marking who has power and who merely borrows it.

## Description

(003-004) The lantern is first seen in [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s possession: "the [[the-art|Master of the Art]] took down from its hook on the wall." Later, "a [[the-charwoman|crone]] appeared in the door, holding the lantern that the [[the-magician|magician]] had lately carried." The transfer of the lantern from [[the-magician|master]] to servant is significant — she carries his light but does not own it.

(005-058) The lantern's candle burns to "its last half-inch," its finite fuel a metaphor for the waning [[the-golden-age|Golden Age]]. When the candle gutters, someone must replace it — but in a house of [[shadow|shadows]], even the light is provisional.

## Symbolic Function

In a story structured around [[shadow|shadows]] and their absence, the lantern is the counter-force — the thing that makes [[shadow|shadows]] possible. Every [[shadow]] cast in [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s house is cast by this lantern. Its presence reminds the reader that [[shadow|shadows]] are not independent entities but relationships between light and obstruction.
