# The Magic Book

[[the-magician|The Magician]]'s book is the textual foundation of [[magic|the Art]]. Written in an ancient script that requires dedicated study to decipher, it contains the secrets of [[magical-literacy|reading]], [[alchemy|transmutation]], [[shadow]]-manipulation, and every other [[magic]]al operation [[the-magician|the Magician]] performs. It is both textbook and talisman — the repository of a dying tradition.

## Description

(005-066) The book is distinguished by its script: "the big black script in the Master's book." The adjective "black" suggests both the ink's colour and the weight of the knowledge it encodes — this is not a book of white [[magic]] but of the older, morally ambiguous Art.

(004-044) Its contents are taught progressively: single syllables first, then multi-syllabled words, each revealing "mysteries" as the apprentice's literacy grows. The book's organisation mirrors the pedagogical method — knowledge is stratified, with deeper secrets accessible only to those who have [[the-magician|master]]ed the shallower ones.

## Significance

(005-066) Mastery of the book grants access to "the past and the dead" — the accumulated wisdom of all previous [[the-magician|master]]s. The book is a lineage object, connecting each generation of practitioners to those who came before. Its continued existence is threatened by the closing of [[the-golden-age|the Golden Age]].
