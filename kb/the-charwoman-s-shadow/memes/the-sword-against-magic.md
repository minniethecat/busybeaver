# The Sword Against Magic

The climactic confrontation in which [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] draws his sword against [[the-magician|the Magician]] to demand the [[the-shadows-release|release of the shadows]] held captive in [[the-box|the shadow-box]].

## Summary

In Chapter XXII, [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] resolves to end [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s practice of hoarding stolen [[shadow|shadows]]. Having discovered the true nature of [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s bargain — that [[shadow|shadows]] are taken as payment for instruction in [[the-art|the Art]] — [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] decides that his sword, "that he had never used yet on any serious business," must now serve a higher purpose: "he would confront the [[the-magician|magician]] with its slender point and make him open the [[the-box|shadow-box]]; its purpose was to rescue the oppressed, then why not those hapless [[shadow|shadows]] that lay with his own in [[the-box|the box]]?" (011-016).

The confrontation culminates in the moment when "the [[ramon-alonzo|young man]]'s sword was out and its point was before the face of the [[the-magician|magician]]" (022-040).

## Key Participants

- **[[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]]** — the challenger, wielding his rapier with "a pretty style with the sword" (022-042), motivated by compassion for the captive [[shadow|shadows]] and particularly for [[the-charwoman|Anemone]], whose [[shadow]] was among those taken
- **[[the-magician|The Magician]]** — the antagonist, whose weapon "was [[magic]]al, and thrilled up the steel the moment it touched the rapier, jarring the [[ramon-alonzo|young man]]'s arm as far as the shoulder, shaking his elbow and nearly wrenching his wrist" (022-042)

## Causes & Context

The confrontation is the culmination of several narrative threads. [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] has learned [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s secret: that the [[magical-contracts|fee]] for instruction in [[the-art|the Art]] is the pupil's [[shadow]], which [[the-magician|the Magician]] keeps in a [[the-box|padlocked box]]. The revelation that [[the-charwoman|the Charwoman]]'s [[shadow]] — and more importantly, [[the-charwoman|Anemone]]'s — are among those imprisoned galvanizes the [[ramon-alonzo|young man]]. His chivalric training and innate sense of justice compel him to act, despite the obvious disparity between mortal steel and [[magic|magical]] power.

## Consequences

The sword-against-[[magic]] confrontation marks a turning point in the relationship between [[the-magician|master]] and pupil. Though [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] cannot overcome [[the-magician|the Magician]] by force — the [[magic]]al weapon proves too powerful — his defiance establishes him as something more than a passive student. This act of courage sets in motion the plan that ultimately leads to [[the-shadows-release|the release of the shadows]] and the undoing of [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s hoard.

The duel also exposes the limits of [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s power: his years "had been given to other studies than those of thrusting and parrying" (022-042). While his [[magic]] provides overwhelming advantage, the confrontation reveals that he is not omnipotent — a revelation that proves crucial to the story's resolution.
