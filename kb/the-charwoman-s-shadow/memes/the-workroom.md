# The Workroom

The inner sanctum of [[the-house-in-the-wood|the house in the wood]] — the room "sacred to [[magic|the Art]]" where [[the-magician|the Magician]] instructs [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] and practices his [[magic|magical]] operations.

## Description

The workroom is introduced with deliberate ceremony. [[the-magician|The Magician]] gestures toward it, saying: "let us go to the room that is sacred to [[magic|the Art]]. Its very dust is made of books I have studied, and is indeed more redolent of lore than any dust in this wood; and if echoes die not at all, as some have taught (though others urge finality for all things), the spiders in its corners, whose ears are attuned to sounds that are lost to ours, hear still the echoes of my earlier musings whereby I unravelled mysteries that are not for the ears of man" (004-011).

The room's physical contents are catalogued in one of the novel's most vivid descriptive passages: "stuffed crocodiles lying as thick as on lonely mud-banks in Africa, dried herbs resembling plants that blossom in wonted fields, yet wearing a look that never was on any flowers of ours, great twinkling jewels out of the heads of toads, huge folios written by [[the-magician|master]]s that had followed [[magic|the Art]] in China, small parchments with spells upon them in Persian, Indian, or Arabic, the horn of a unicorn that had slain its [[the-magician|master]]; rare spices, condiments, and [[philosophers-stone|the philosopher's stone]]" (004-012).

The room's most striking feature is not its contents but its atmosphere: "the several sheets of gloom that seemed to come down from the roof through the midst of the air, across the natural dimness of the room" (004-012). These "sheets of gloom" suggest that the workroom contains a manufactured darkness — a gloom that is not merely the absence of light but a created substance, part of [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s apparatus.

## Function

The workroom serves multiple functions within [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s domain:

**A Place of Instruction** — [[the-magician|The Magician]] seats himself in "a high-backed chair at a lectern, on which a great book lay open showing columns of Chinese manuscript" (004-013), and from this throne of learning delivers his lectures on [[transmutation-principle|transmutation]], [[philosophers-stone|the philosopher's stone]], and the properties of metals.

**A Place of Secrecy** — [[the-magician|The Magician]] banishes [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] from the room each evening, "in order that, as [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] knew, he might play some secret game with his captive [[shadow|shadows]]" (009-046). The workroom is thus a divided space: a classroom by day, a site of hidden operations by night.

**A Place of Memory** — In the novel's closing chapter, [[the-magician|the Magician]] returns to "his room that was sacred to [[magic]], and took from the dust and darkness of a high shelf a volume in which he had written all he had learned about boar-hunting" (030-030). The room serves as the repository of centuries of accumulated knowledge, both [[magic]]al and mundane.
