# The Apprenticeship Begins

The apprenticeship of [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] under [[the-magician|the Magician]] is the foundational event of *[[the-charwoman-s-shadow|The Charwoman's Shadow]]*. It spans the novel's opening chapters and establishes all the key relationships, conflicts, and [[magic]]al principles that drive the story — from the economic desperation of [[the-tower|the Tower]] to the first gl[[supernatural-beings|imps]]e of the [[shadow|shadow]] economy.

## Circumstances

(001-003) The journey begins on "a summer evening sombre and sweet over Spain," with the [[ramon-alonzo|young man]] "pondering his journey and his future calling."

> "His name was Ramon Alonzo Matthew-Mark-Luke-John of the Tower and Rocky Forest. And his father had lately called to him as he played at ball with his sister, beating it back and forth to each other over a deep yew hedge."

His father, [[lord-of-the-tower|the Lord]] of the Tower, has chosen [[magic]] as a career path for his younger son — a practical decision driven by the household's financial decline. The empty dowry coffer, shown to [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] without a word, speaks louder than any speech.

(001-032) The economic motivation is explicit: "discernible [[shadow|shadows]] were cast by a want of [[gold-transmutation|gold]]." The apprenticeship is an investment, not a vocation. [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] goes to learn [[alchemy]], not to become a [[the-magician|magician]] — the distinction is crucial to understanding his later moral development.

## The House in the Wood

(003-002) The arrival at [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s house establishes the novel's central location:

> "That tall figure of magic stood opposite without moving, and spoke no more; so that the young man ate hastily and soon had finished."

[[the-magician|The Magician]]'s silence is more intimidating than any speech — his presence fills the room. [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] gains entry by waving his grandfather's scroll thrice in the night, a ritual that blends superstition with the genuine [[magic]]al power of the written word.

(003-004) That night, [[the-charwoman|the charwoman]] makes her first appearance: "a [[the-charwoman|crone]] appeared in the door, holding the [[the-lantern|lantern]] that the [[the-magician|magician]] had lately carried. Age had withered her beyond all fair seeming." Her warning about [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s prices plants the seed of what will become the novel's central quest.

(004-020) [[the-magician|The Magician]] names his terms carefully: "he had a few more words to say first about the worthlessness of [[shadow|shadows]]." The negotiation is a performance — devaluing the currency before asking for it.

(004-032) [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]]'s refusal to part with his [[shadow]] — "the more the [[the-magician|magician]] proved its uselessness the more stubborn he became" — establishes his character and creates the dramatic tension that will sustain the entire novel.

## Consequences

The apprenticeship sets in motion every subsequent event: the discovery of [[the-charwoman|the charwoman]]'s plight, the plan to rescue her [[shadow]], the study of [[alchemy]], the compounding of the [[love-potion|love potion]], and the eventual liberation of the [[shadow|shadows]] from [[the-box|the box]]. Without this initiating event, none of the novel's conflicts — personal, moral, or [[magic]]al — would exist.
