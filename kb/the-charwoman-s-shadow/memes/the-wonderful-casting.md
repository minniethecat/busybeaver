# The Wonderful Casting

The climactic magical event of Chapter XXVI in which Ramon Alonzo releases the charwoman's shadow from his keeping, and the shadow miraculously recasts its owner's body — transforming an aged crone into a young woman once more and breaking the magician's decades-old enchantment.

## Summary

As the charwoman rests on a sunlit hillside after their long journey, Ramon Alonzo sits near her, holding the young and delicate shadow he has carried. The time has come to give it up — he has pledged his word to restore her shadow, knowing that "shadow and substance must be alike in outline, as all the world knows" (026-002).

What should have been a simple return of stolen property becomes a miracle: the shadow, in a reversal of natural law, casts a youthful body for its owner rather than the body casting the shadow. "But your shadow is casting a body," Ramon Alonzo cries in amazement, "not your body a shadow" (026-022).

In a succession of transformations described by the charwoman as a series of remembered selves — wife, young girl, daughter — her withered form is restored to the beauty of youth.

## Key Participants

- **Ramon Alonzo** — The young apprentice who has protected and carried the shadow throughout the narrative, and who performs the release that triggers the transformation. He is simultaneously heartbroken (anticipating the loss of his love for the shadow) and awestruck by the miracle he witnesses.
- **The Charwoman** — The shadowless old woman whose stolen shadow has been held by the magician. As the shadow is released and recasts her body, she experiences a cascade of identity transformations, progressing through her life in reverse until she emerges as her youthful self.

## Causes & Context

The wonderful casting is the culmination of several narrative threads:

1. **The Shadow Theft**: The charwoman's shadow was stolen by the magician to serve as currency to purchase a young bride — her own shadow became her dowry (003, 006).

2. **Ramon Alonzo's Pledge**: Throughout the journey, Ramon Alonzo has promised to restore the charwoman's shadow, bonding them as traveling companions (003-020).

3. **The Magician's Scheme Exposed**: The revelation that the magician used the charwoman's shadow in his bargain for Gulvarez's daughter has already unraveled the magician's household (029).

## Consequences

The wonderful casting produces transformative outcomes across multiple levels:

1. **For the Charwoman**: Complete physical restoration from aged crone to young woman, undoing decades of magical theft. The transformation is not merely cosmetic — she passes through stages of identity, from old woman to wife to girl to daughter, in a cascade of remembered selves (026-031).

2. **For Ramon Alonzo**: His love story with the shadow — "not three hours old" (026-002) — is simultaneously ended and transfigured. The shadow he loved becomes a living woman, and his sacrifice (willing to give up the shadow for her owner's welfare) is rewarded beyond his expectations.

3. **For the Narrative**: The magician's power is symbolically broken — not by combat but by an act of restoration. The "dark house, the magician, the evil bargain, the long long corridors, and the peril of soul" all "slip away to the land of dreams accomplished" (026-033).
