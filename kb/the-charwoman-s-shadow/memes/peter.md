# Peter

Peter serves as [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s messenger, carrying letters between the isolated [[the-house-in-the-wood|house in the wood]] and the world beyond. His role is small but structurally important — he is the thread that keeps the [[magic]]al household connected to ordinary life.

## Role in the Story

(007-002) He delivers a crucial letter that distracts [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] at a key moment: "So fast the [[the-magician|magician]] came striding back to his room with the letter he had from Peter, that [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]]'s eye had scarce time to rove, and had not found the [[the-box|long thin box]] for which it began to seek." Peter's timely arrival (or [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s exploitation of it) prevents the premature discovery of the [[the-box|shadow-box]].

(019-023) He is also tasked with practical errands: "She had told Peter to take it after him, but Peter's [[the-magician|master]] had not allowed him to go until the..." [[the-magician|The Magician]] controls Peter's movements absolutely — he is not a free agent but an extension of his [[the-magician|master]]'s will.

## Narrative Function

Peter is one of several minor figures (like [[the-charwoman|Dockweed]] [[the-charwoman|the charwoman]], like the [[supernatural-beings|imps]]) who populate [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s household. His presence reinforces the sense that the [[the-house-in-the-wood|house in the wood]] is not merely a hermitage but a functioning estate with servants, messengers, and routines — a domestic infrastructure for [[magic]]al practice.
