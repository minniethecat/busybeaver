## Definition

A witch, or "witch-woman," is a feared figure in the world of Aragona, believed to ride by broom from the mysterious Country Towards Moon's Rising. The accusation of being a witch carries great weight, evoking "horror" among villagers [027-035] and marking the accused as an outsider who does not belong to the community.

> "She is a witch-woman, for none knows her here; and hath seen our village upon starry nights riding by broom from the Country Towards Moon's Rising." [027-033]

## The Country Towards Moon's Rising

The witch is inextricably linked to the Country Towards Moon's Rising, a supernatural realm that lies "not only beyond salvation, but the dooms of the Last Judgment cross not its borders either" [027-035]. Those who dwell there exist outside the scope of divine judgment, and one must reach it before death — "for then it is too late" [027-035]. The villagers' belief that a witch rides from this land on starlit nights fuels their dread of the unknown.

## The Accusation of Anemone

In Chapter XXVII, Anemone — the charwoman restored to youth — is accused of being a witch when she arrives at Aragona with Ramon Alonzo [027-033]. Her deep knowledge of Aragona's lanes, graves, yew-trees, and family pedigrees, which she recounts in loving detail, provokes suspicion rather than recognition [027-032]. The villagers, failing to recognize her youthful form, conclude that only supernatural means could account for her intimate knowledge of their village.

The chapter title itself — "They Dread That a Witch Has Ridden from the Country Towards Moon's Rising" [027-001] — frames the entire episode through the lens of folk superstition and fear of the strange.

## Social Function

The witch accusation in the novel serves as a mechanism of exclusion: when Anemone's identity cannot be reconciled with her youthful appearance, the community defaults to labeling her a supernatural threat. The dread of the witch reflects the broader tension between the known and the unknowable that runs through the novel's treatment of magic.
