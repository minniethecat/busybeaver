# Supernatural Beings

The world of *[[the-charwoman|The Charwoman]]'s [[shadow|Shadow]]* is populated not only by human practitioners of [[magic]] but by a host of supernatural beings — imps that bake bread, spirits of the void that serve [[the-magician|the Magician]], and creatures that drift in a "native void" until tethered to Earth by a stolen [[shadow]]. These beings form the hidden infrastructure of [[magic]]al power.

## Definition

(004-030) [[the-magician|The Magician]] explains the relationship between [[shadow|shadows]] and spirits explicitly: "there are those that serve me better when equipped with a [[shadow]] than when drifting vapidly in their native void. They have no other connection with Earth except these [[shadow|shadows]] I give them." Supernatural beings are native to a non-earthly realm and require a human [[shadow]] to anchor them to the material world.

(005-065) Lesser supernatural beings handle domestic labour in [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s household. [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] learns that "his food was baked by imps at a fire in the wood, little creatures of two [[magical-contracts|fee]]t high that could gambol" — impish beings that perform practical tasks while remaining largely unseen.

(004-032) The spirits are part of [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s economy: "for this purpose I have many [[shadow|shadows]] which I keep here in a box." Each [[shadow]] in [[the-box|the box]] corresponds to a spirit that has been equipped for earthly service.

## Types of Beings

(005-065) **Imps**: Small creature-servants, "two [[magical-contracts|fee]]t high," capable of baking and other domestic work. They are associated with fire and the wood outside [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s house. They appear to be benign or at least neutral — part of the household's routine operation.

(004-030) **Void-spirits**: Disembodied beings that "drift vapidly in their native void" until given a [[shadow]]. These are [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s primary servants, capable of tasks requiring more substance than imps can provide. They are described without moral character — tools rather than allies or enemies.

(003-004) **The [[shadow|Shadow]]less**: The [[the-charwoman|charwoman]]'s condition — "[[shadow]]less" — blurs the line between human and supernatural. Stripped of her [[shadow]] for centuries, she exists in a liminal state, visible but not fully present in human society.

## The Supernatural Ecology

(020-002) [[the-magician|The Magician]]'s solitude is populated by unseen presences: "the [[the-magician|magician]] sat all alone in his [[the-house-in-the-wood|house in the wood]]. He was not wholly hostile to man." The "not wholly" implies that his primary company consists of non-human beings, shaping his personality and isolation.

(005-065) The supernatural beings are governed by hierarchy and command. The imps bake, the spirits serve, and all answer to [[the-magician|the Magician]]. This mirrors feudal social structures, suggesting that the supernatural world is organised along lines familiar to the novel's human characters.

## Narrative Significance

Supernatural beings in the novel demystify [[magic]] by giving it a practical, almost bureaucratic dimension. [[the-magician|The Magician]] is not a lone wizard chanting spells but the manager of a supernatural household — a landlord of spirits, an employer of imps. His power is as much organisational as arcane.
