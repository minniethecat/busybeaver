# Mirandola

Mirandola is the sister of [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] and the daughter of the impoverished [[lord-of-the-tower|Lord of the Tower]]. Her demand for a [[love-potion|love potion]] — aimed at securing the affections of [[duke-of-shadow-valley|the Duke]] of [[shadow-valley|Shadow Valley]] — drives one of the novel's key subplots and illustrates how [[magic]]al services intersect with the social ambitions of the declining nobility.

## Role in the Story

(006-022) Her romantic interest in [[duke-of-shadow-valley|the Duke]] is established through family conversation: "You like him then," said her father. "He is not ill to look on; two women not long since have called him handsome. And he is a friend of [[duke-of-shadow-valley|the Duke]] of [[shadow-valley|Shadow Valley]]." Social climbing and genuine attraction are indistinguishable in her motivations.

(012-000) Her demand for a [[love-potion|love potion]] is the catalyst for [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]]'s first independent [[magic]]al work. She approaches him not as a supplicant seeking supernatural aid but as a sister making a reasonable request of a brother with useful skills.

(019-023) Her continued involvement in the plot is marked by small domestic details: "The [[ramon-alonzo|young man]] was wearing a fine old cloak of his father's, which Mirandola had begged for him on the day that he had gone cloakless away from the Tower." Even when not physically present, her care for her brother persists.

## Attributes & Abilities

(006-029) She possesses a sharp tongue and strong will: "[[lord-of-the-tower|The Lord of the Tower]] held up his hand to check her hasty utterance while he thought of appropriate words with which to reprove her error." Her father struggles to manage her directness — she speaks her mind in a household that expects female deference.

(012-000) Her understanding of [[magic]] is entirely practical: she does not seek wisdom or power, but a specific outcome — [[duke-of-shadow-valley|the Duke]]'s affection. This instrumental view of [[magic]] contrasts with both [[the-magician|the Magician]]'s philosophical Art and [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]]'s evolving moral purpose.

## Key Relationships

(019-023) With [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]]: She is protective and practical, securing a cloak for him when he leaves home unprepared. Their sibling bond is one of the novel's few uncomplicated relationships.

(006-022) With [[duke-of-shadow-valley|the Duke]] of [[shadow-valley|Shadow Valley]]: Her romantic ambition is the catalyst for the [[love-potion|love potion]] subplot. Whether her [[magical-contracts|fee]]lings are genuine or strategic is left ambiguous.
