# The Tower

The Tower and Rocky Forest is the ancestral home of [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] and his family, standing at the boundary between the settled lands of [[aragona|Aragona]] and the wild [[the-house-in-the-wood|forest]] beyond. It is the seat of the [[lord-of-the-tower|Lord of the Tower]] — a place of fading nobility, strained resources, and the quiet desperation that drives a father to send his son to study [[magic]].

## Location and Setting

[006-002] The Tower occupies a liminal position in the landscape — "the last house that stood in the open lands; on the one side [[the-wood|the forest]] cut it off entirely from the region beyond, and on the side towards men it was separated from them by rocks." This isolation defines the household's relationship with the wider world: rumour "came seldom," and the family's fortunes are tied to land too rocky for husbandry.

[001-038] The Tower overlooks a garden that "met the lawn on which they had lately played, a little lower down the slope where the Tower stood, and shut from the untamed earth and the rocks that were there before man by the same balustrade of marble that guarded the lawn." The garden, with its "Spanish shrubs that were like tall fountains of flowers," provides the setting for the childhood games of [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] and his sister [[mirandola|Mirandola]].

## Architecture and Interior

[001-007] The Tower is a fortified dwelling of an older age. Its great hall features "the room where the boar-spears hung" — trophies and weapons from generations of hunting in the surrounding forest. A separate "rummage-room" contains the accumulated detritus of centuries:

> [001-028] "Gladly then the Lord of the Tower and Rocky Forest went to his rummage-room where strange things lay and none interfered with the spider. And in that dim place where one scarce could have hoped to find anything, amongst heaps of old fishing nets that had become solid with dust, where worn-out boar-spears lay on the floor, and rusted bandilleros that had once pricked famous bulls, blunt knives and broken tent-pegs, and things too old for one to be able to name them at all, unless one washed them and brought them out in the light..."

It is in this rummage-room that [[lord-of-the-tower|the Lord]] of the Tower discovers the [[the-magician|Magician]]'s scroll — the object that sets the entire narrative in motion.

## Household and Economy

The Tower's household reflects its declining economic circumstances:

|-----------|------|-------|
| [[lord-of-the-tower|The Lord of the Tower]] | Master | Nobleman of declining fortune, concerned with dowry |
| The Lady of the Tower | Mistress | Mother of [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] and [[mirandola|Mirandola]] |
| [[ramon-alonzo|Ramon Alonzo]] | Son and heir | Sent to study [[alchemy]] to restore family wealth |
| [[mirandola|Mirandola]] | Daughter | Her dowry drives the plot's financial urgency |
| [[peter|Peter]] | Gardener | "nor is [[peter|Peter]] any better in the garden" |
| Emanuel and Mark | Stable-hands | "they will have their regular payment year in year out" |
| [[father-joseph|Father Joseph]] | Family [[father-joseph|priest]] | Called to the Tower to write letters and perform rites |

[001-007] The household economy is threadbare. [[lord-of-the-tower|The Lord of the Tower]] confesses to his son: "the rocky structure of our soil being unsuited to husbandry, [[gold-transmutation|gold]] is not easily wrung from it, and there is little of a worldly nature to be won from [[the-wood|the forest]]." The servants — Emanuel, Mark, [[peter|Peter]] — demand regular wages, and [[mirandola|Mirandola]]'s approaching marriageable age makes a dowry imperative.

> [006-012] "But he is a greedy man," said he, "and will demand a dowry such as a man cannot gather either from the rocks of a mountain or from the forest."

## Social and Political Role

[006-016] The Tower functions as a social centre for the scattered community of the borderlands. When [[lord-of-the-tower|the Lord]] of the Tower needs [[father-joseph|Father Joseph]]'s services, "he sent [[peter|Peter]], from the garden, with a message to a certain [[father-joseph|Father Joseph]], who dwelt not far away, asking him to come to the Tower."

[017-002] The Tower also hosts significant political events. When the [[gulvarez|Señor Gulvarez]] and the [[duke-of-shadow-valley|Duke of Shadow Valley]] visit to negotiate [[mirandola|Mirandola]]'s marriage, the household mobilises for an elaborate reception:

> [017-002] "The day dawned splendidly, and air and fields glittered all the morning with sunlight, which welled up over the world and was only stopped by the forest..."

[017-007] The arrival of [[duke-of-shadow-valley|the Duke]]'s party is observed from the Tower: "And suddenly, where a hump of the road appeared white against the blue sky, all saw two horsemen."

## The Rumour of Decline

[001-035] Throughout the novel, the Tower is haunted by economic anxiety. The Lady of the Tower "had long tried to make her husband see that need that was sending its [[shadow|shadows]] to creep through the Tower." These [[shadow|shadows]] — of poverty, of dwindling prospects — are the mundane counterpart to the [[magic]]al [[shadow|shadows]] that dominate the novel's supernatural plot.

[006-002] The Tower's isolation, once a mark of noble independence, has become a liability: "it was the last house that stood in the open lands" — beyond it lies only forest, rocks, and the fading [[the-golden-age|Golden Age]].
