## Description

The counting-house is Mr. Sherwin's commercial office in London. It is a place of business where clerks work under Sherwin's supervision. The establishment is hierarchical: Mannion enters "as the lowest clerk" (PN 024-038) and methodically rises through its ranks. The counting-house is a site of observation and calculation — both commercial and psychological — where Mannion studies Sherwin's weaknesses and consolidates his own position.

## Narrative Role

The counting-house is the setting for Mannion's professional ascent and his psychological dissection of Mr. Sherwin. Starting at the bottom, Mannion quickly rises to "the chief place in the countinghouse" (PN 024-039). From this vantage point, he observes Sherwin closely and forms a damning assessment: "I found him to be a pretty equal compound by nature, of the fool, the tyrant, and the coward" (PN 024-039). The counting-house is where Mannion establishes the commercial credibility that gives him access to the Sherwin household and, ultimately, to Margaret. It is also where Basil later works under Sherwin's employment, bringing him into Mannion's orbit.

## Associated Characters

- **mannion**: The counting-house is Mannion's domain within the novel. He enters as the lowest clerk and through "a manner that never varied" and "steadiness at his work" (PN 024-038) rises to become Sherwin's most trusted employee.
- **mr-sherwin**: Sherwin is the proprietor of the counting-house. His character is exposed there through Mannion's cold observation.
- **basil**: Basil enters Sherwin's employment and works at the counting-house, placing him in proximity to Mannion and deepening his entanglement with the Sherwin family.

## Symbolism

The counting-house represents the commercial world that stands in tension with the aristocratic values of Basil's family. It is a space of calculation, hierarchy, and observation — values that Mannion embodies and weaponises. The counting-house's system of meritocratic advancement (Mannion rising through competence alone) contrasts with the inherited status of the Basil family.
