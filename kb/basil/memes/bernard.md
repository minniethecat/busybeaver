# Bernard

Bernard is a minor figure from Basil's university days, mentioned briefly in the early chapters as part of the social world that Basil leaves behind when he enters into his secret marriage.

## Role

Bernard represents the normal social world — friendships, studies, the expected life of a young aristocrat — that Basil abandons for the risky world of cross-class romance and secrecy. His brief mention serves to establish the social baseline from which Basil departs.

## Biography

Bernard appears only in passing, as one of Basil's acquaintances during his time at university. His significance is structural rather than biographical: he is a marker of the life Basil might have led.

## Relationships

- **Basil**: School friend. Their relationship represents the conventional social bonds that Basil's passion displaces.

## Appearances

Part I, Chapter II: Mentioned among Basil's university acquaintances
