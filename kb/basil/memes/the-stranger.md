# The Stranger

The stranger is not a single character but a recurring figure in the novel's urban landscape — the anonymous person in the London crowd whose presence represents both the freedom and the danger of the modern city.

## Role

Collins's London is a space where "persons of all classes and all temperaments are so easily and so strangely intermingled" (007-004). The stranger embodies this urban condition: the person unknown and unknowable, who may be harmless or threatening, indifferent or observing. In a novel structured around secrecy and concealment, the stranger represents both the possibility of hiding and the impossibility of being sure one is hidden.

## Appearances

Part I, Chapter VII (London streets); Journal (Basil's period of concealment)
