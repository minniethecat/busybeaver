# Holyoak Square

Holyoak Square is the location of Basil's lodgings during the period of his greatest degradation — after the exposure of his secret marriage and Margaret's death, when he has been reduced to poverty and is pursued by Mannion.

## Significance

The move from the aristocratic family estate to a rented room in Holyoak Square charts Basil's social descent in spatial terms. It is here that he experiences the "penny-a lining in third-class newspapers" (024-028) existence that is the antithesis of his aristocratic origins, and it is here that he falls into the fever from which he barely recovers.
