# North Villa Servant

The servant at the Sherwin household is a background presence whose function is to mark the class position of the Sherwin family (PN 007-031) and to facilitate the domestic scenes in which the novel's central drama unfolds.

## Role

The servant represents the working-class presence that is peripheral to the novel's main concerns (PN 009-055) but structurally essential to its representation of Victorian domestic life. The Sherwins, as a linen-draper's family, employ at least one servant (PN 007-031) — a marker of their lower-middle-class respectability. The servant's presence at key domestic scenes (PN 009-003) (Basil's visits, the tense family meals) is noted but never developed (PN 017-028).

## Appearances

Part I, Chapter X; Part II (background presence in North Villa scenes)
