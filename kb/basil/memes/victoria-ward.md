## Description

Victoria Ward is a ward within a London hospital. It is described as a full ward, with multiple patients present, giving it a public and exposed character (PN 026-019). The ward is the setting for a critical moment of medical crisis when Mannion's fever "declared itself at last" with new symptoms (PN 026-036).

## Narrative Role

Victoria Ward appears in the later chapters of the novel during the fallout from Mannion's attack. Margaret Sherwin visits the ward in an agitated state, seeking Turner (Mannion), and is directed there (PN 026-019). The ward becomes the site of a significant revelation when Mr. Bernard reacts with visible shock at the mention of "Victoria Ward," and Ralph abruptly stops mid-sentence with "a very strange and sudden alteration in his expression" (PN 026-039). This suggests the ward holds some concealed significance connected to Mannion's identity or history.

## Associated Characters

- **mannion**: Occupies Victoria Ward as a patient when his fever develops; the ward is the locus of his medical crisis.
- **margaret-sherwin**: Visits the ward in agitation, seeking Mannion.
- **bernard**: Reacts with visible startlement at the mention of Victoria Ward, suggesting prior knowledge.
- **ralph**: Notices Bernard's reaction and cuts off his own remark with a sudden change of expression, indicating awareness of the ward's hidden significance.

## Symbolism

Victoria Ward may symbolise exposure and the collapse of concealment. Mannion, who operates throughout the novel through secrecy and manipulation, is laid bare in a public hospital ward where his physical and perhaps moral condition becomes visible. The ward's name itself—"Victoria"—carries ironic connotations of triumph and revelation.
