## Description

Lyons is a major French city, historically renowned as a centre of the silk trade. In the novel, it is the destination for Mr. Sherwin's silk-purchasing expeditions. Mannion is sent there as Sherwin's agent: "He's at Lyons now, buying silks for me" (PN 010-069). The city functions as a remote commercial hub, distant enough from London to allow Mannion freedom of action and privacy for his own designs.

## Narrative Role

Lyons plays a significant behind-the-scenes role in Mannion's scheme. Sent to purchase silks, Mannion uses the trip to advance his personal agenda: "My private object in going there, was to make interest secretly with Mr. Sherwin's correspondents for a situation in the house" (PN 024-052). While ostensibly managing Sherwin's business faithfully, Mannion arranges his own affairs "securely and privately" (PN 024-053), positioning himself for his eventual pursuit of Margaret Sherwin. The trip to Lyons marks the period during which Mannion consolidates his plan. Basil later notes that Mannion "had been some time at Lyons; had managed my employer's business...and had arranged my own affairs securely and privately" (PN 024-053).

## Associated Characters

- **mannion**: Lyons is the site of Mannion's covert scheming. His business trip there allows him to establish independent contacts and plan his future away from Sherwin's scrutiny.
- **mr-sherwin**: Sherwin sends Mannion to Lyons as his commercial representative, trusting him with important business transactions that he feels incapable of handling himself (PN 024-051).

## Symbolism

Lyons represents the hidden, commercial dimension of the novel's plot — the world of trade, silk, and international correspondence that operates beneath the surface of the domestic drama. It is a space of secrecy and private maneuvering, where Mannion can act unseen and unobserved.
