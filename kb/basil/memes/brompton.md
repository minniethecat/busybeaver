## Description

Brompton is a London neighbourhood, characterised in the novel as a quiet residential area. Ralph describes his intention to "live quietly, somewhere in the Brompton neighbourhood" (PN 025-009), suggesting it is a respectable but unremarkable part of the city, removed from the fashionable districts associated with the Basil family. Ralph later takes "a little place at Brompton" (PN 026-034), indicating modest suburban accommodation.

## Narrative Role

Brompton serves as the setting for Ralph's domestic arrangements with his mistress upon their return from the Continent. It is mentioned in the later chapters of the novel, during the period when Ralph and Basil are attempting to track down Mannion and Margaret Sherwin. Ralph references Brompton while planning their investigation (PN 025-093), and invites Basil and a companion to dine there once the house is furnished (PN 026-034).

## Associated Characters

- **ralph**: Ralph is the primary character associated with Brompton. He takes a residence there for himself and his mistress, and it serves as his London base during the later events of the novel.
- **basil**: Basil is invited to visit Ralph's Brompton residence to dine.

## Symbolism

None identified. Brompton functions as a practical London location for Ralph's domestic life, without carrying evident symbolic weight in the narrative.
