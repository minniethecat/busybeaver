# The Policeman

The policeman appears at a critical juncture in the novel, representing the external authority of law and social order that stands outside the private drama of passion, secrecy, and revenge.

## Role

The policeman embodies the public world of law and social order that the novel's main characters have evaded. His presence reminds the reader that the private transgressions the novel chronicles — the secret marriage, the concealed adultery, the revenge pursuit — exist within a larger framework of public law. The question of whether the private catastrophe will become a public scandal hovers over the novel's final sections.
