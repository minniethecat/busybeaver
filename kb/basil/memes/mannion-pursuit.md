# The Pursuit of Mannion

The pursuit of Mannion is the novel's extended climax — a sequence that transforms _Basil_ from a domestic tragedy into something closer to the revenge narratives that would become a hallmark of sensation fiction.

## The Pursuit

After the discovery of Margaret's betrayal and her subsequent death, Mannion becomes the object of Basil's obsessive pursuit. Mannion's persecution of Basil is driven by "the desire of revenge" (024-066) — a cold, calculated campaign that he has "first thoroughly tested" before executing. But the pursuit is two-sided: Basil pursues Mannion as relentlessly as Mannion pursues Basil.

The pursuit takes Basil through London's poorest districts — "my penny-a lining in third-class newspapers; my translating from French and German" (024-028) — and eventually into the countryside, where the final confrontation occurs.

## Mannion's End

Mannion's death is deliberately ambiguous. The novel offers multiple accounts — accident, disappearance, possible survival — refusing the reader the closure of a confirmed death. Mannion, who embodied the force of implacable, amoral persistence, cannot simply be eliminated. His end, like his life, remains opaque.
