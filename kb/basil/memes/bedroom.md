## Description

The bedroom in _Basil_ is the most private of domestic spaces, and its appearances in the narrative mark moments when the boundary between public and private life is breached. Three bedrooms structure the novel: the father's bedroom, governed by ritual and exclusion; the hotel bedroom where Basil spends his wedding night alone on the floor; and Clara's bedroom, which doubles as a sickroom during Basil's convalescence.

## Narrative Role

### The Father's Bedroom

Basil's father imposes strict order on his private space: "no servant but his own valet" is permitted to enter or "alter the arrangements of his bedroom" (004-011). This ritualized privacy is a figure for the emotional inaccessibility that defines the father-son relationship.

The one exception is Basil himself, who goes "upstairs, at night, into my bedroom" (004-013) to bid his father goodnight. These visits are dutiful rather than intimate, reinforcing the distance they are meant to bridge.

### The Wedding Night Floor

After his secret marriage to Margaret, Basil spends the night not in a marriage bed but "sleeping alone on the bedroom floor" (012-054). The detail is devastating: the marriage, withheld from consummation by Mr. Sherwin's conditions, is reduced to a parody of domesticity.

### Clara's Sickroom

During Basil's recovery from brain-fever, Clara's "sitting-room and bedroom" (027-037) become the site of his convalescence. The blending of private chamber and sickroom transforms the bedroom into a space of care.

## Associated Characters

**[[basil]]** experiences the bedroom as a site of ritual (with his father), deprivation (on his wedding night), and recovery (with Clara).

**[[clara]]** tends Basil in her bedroom-sickroom, her care transforming the private chamber into a place of restoration.

## Symbolism

The bedroom strips away social performance and exposes the self in its most unguarded state. The father's bedroom reveals rigidity; Basil's wedding-night floor reveals abjection; Clara's sickroom reveals the capacity for care.
