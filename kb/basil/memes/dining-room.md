## Description

The dining-room at North Villa is Mr. Sherwin's domain. It is here that he presides over his "renowned old port" (017-028) with the complacency of a man who measures status in bottles and vintages. Unlike the drawing-room, which serves as the family's public face, the dining-room is the site of its private rituals — meals, conversations, and the assertion of paternal authority.

Clara is glimpsed through the dining-room window (009-038), her face "looking after" Basil with an expression he cannot interpret. This moment invests the dining-room with a secondary significance as the vantage point from which Clara observes the drama unfolding between Basil and Margaret.

## Narrative Role

### Mr. Sherwin's Domain

The dining-room scenes establish Mr. Sherwin's character through his relationship to food and drink. His port, his "greater appearance of" ease (017-029) at table, and his assumption that hospitality can bridge class divisions all play out in this room.

### The Housemaid's Alarm

A moment of domestic crisis erupts when "the housemaid's voice, from the dining-room" (016-014) is heard calling for help — a rare breach in the Sherwins' carefully maintained surface of order that hints at tensions beneath.

### Clara's Vantage

Clara's watch from the dining-room window (009-038) establishes a pattern of female observation that runs through the novel and provides an alternative to Basil's obsessive male gaze.

## Associated Characters

**[[mr-sherwin]]** uses the dining-room as the stage for his performance of the genial host, his port and his solicitude masking the commercial nature of his alliance with Basil.

**[[basil]]** endures the dining-room as a space of obligation, where his connection to the Sherwins is most inescapably domestic.

**[[clara]]** watches from the dining-room window, her position as observer anticipating her later role as Basil's nurse and confidante.

## Symbolism

The dining-room functions as the symbolic heart of the Sherwin household — the space where consumption (of food, of drink, of Basil's social capital) is most literal. Mr. Sherwin's performance of hospitality masks but does not conceal the transaction at the marriage's core.
