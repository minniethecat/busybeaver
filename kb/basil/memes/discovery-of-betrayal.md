# Discovery of Margaret's Betrayal

The discovery scene is the novel's catastrophe — the moment when Basil finds his wife Margaret with Mannion and the secret structure of the plot collapses into open destruction.

## The Scene

Basil's discovery of Margaret with Mannion is rendered in the novel's most intense prose. Margaret's "eyes glared like an idiot's; guilt and terror had made her hideous" (019-044). The physical transformation of Margaret — from the beautiful object of Basil's passion to something barely human — marks the complete destruction of Basil's romantic illusions.

Mannion's response to the discovery is characteristically cold. He does not flee or apologise; he acknowledges the fact and begins, from that moment, his campaign of revenge against Basil.

## Consequences

The discovery triggers the novel's final movement: Margaret's death, Basil's confession to his father, Mannion's pursuit, and Basil's eventual collapse into fever and near-madness. It is the point of no return — after this scene, nothing can be repaired, only endured.
