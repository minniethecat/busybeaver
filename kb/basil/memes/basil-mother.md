# Basil's Mother

Basil's mother is deceased before the novel's action begins, but her absence shapes the emotional world of the Basil family. Her death leaves Clara as the sole female presence in a household otherwise dominated by the father's austere code of honour.

## Role

The mother's absence is structurally significant. The Basil household — father, two sons, one daughter — is a family without a maternal presence, and this absence may help explain both the father's emotional inaccessibility and Basil's vulnerability to passionate attachment. Clara, who "always lived with her since her marriage" (003-005) is mentioned in connection with the mother, suggesting a continuity of feminine presence that Margaret — the object of Basil's passion — cannot provide.
