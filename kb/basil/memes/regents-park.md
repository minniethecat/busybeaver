## Description

Regent's Park is a large royal park in London. Basil encounters its northern portion after visiting North Villa for the first time. The park borders an area of "unfinished squares, unfinished shops, unfinished gardens" (PN 007-022-023), a zone of suburban incompleteness that mirrors Basil's own unsettled psychological state. The park's northern portion is adjacent to the half-built neighbourhood of Holyoak Square and North Villa.

## Narrative Role

Regent's Park serves as a transitional space in the novel. After his first sight of North Villa and the disturbing glimpse of Margaret Sherwin through the garden gate, Basil walks to the park to collect himself. The park represents a boundary between the orderly, established London he knows and the raw, half-formed suburban world into which he is about to descend through his obsession with Margaret.

## Associated Characters

- **basil**: Basil walks to Regent's Park after his first reconnaissance of North Villa; the park is part of his solitary wanderings as he becomes increasingly consumed by his obsession with Margaret Sherwin.

## Symbolism

Regent's Park functions as a liminal space—a threshold between the known and the unknown, the respectable and the disreputable. Its northern edge, bordering the unfinished suburbs, marks the boundary of Basil's familiar world. His passage through and beyond the park into the raw territory of North Villa symbolises his crossing from social respectability into moral danger.
