# Masha

**Masha** is a servant in [[Nikolai-Levin]]'s household, appearing during [[Konstantin-Levin]]'s visit to his dying brother. She performs practical domestic duties — bringing supper, carrying messages — and plays a small but distinct role in the scenes of Nikolai's final decline.

## Identity & Role

Masha is a female servant (maid or housekeeper) in Nikolai Levin's provincial lodging. Her role is entirely domestic: she prepares and serves food, delivers messages, and assists the Levin brothers during Konstantin's stay. She has no surname given in the text — "Masha" is the common diminutive of Marya — and her identity is defined wholly by her service to the household. She first appears in the narrative when Konstantin visits his brother (022-049).

## Biography / Arc

Masha's appearances are confined to the chapters covering Nikolai Levin's illness. When Konstantin Levin arrives to visit his ailing brother, Masha is summoned to prepare supper (032-011). She brings food, tidies, and responds to Nikolai's commands with the deference of a servant but without subservience — she is a capable, matter-of-fact presence amid the squalor of the sickroom (032-023, 032-030).

Her most significant action comes when she helps Konstantin persuade Nikolai not to go out in his weakened condition (033-069). After the visit, Masha promises to write to Konstantin with news of Nikolai's health — a small but meaningful act that places her as a link between the brothers (033-070).

Later in the novel, she is referenced in passing in a social context at a country gathering (104-039), suggesting she may have entered the service of other households or that the name is generic enough to appear across different settings.

## Relationships

- **Nikolai Levin**: Her employer and the dying man she serves. Her role is that of a practical domestic assistant to an increasingly ill and difficult master.
- **Konstantin Levin**: She assists him during his visit, and her promise to write to him about Nikolai's condition shows a recognition of his concern for his brother.

## Key Passages

> "Masha! get us some supper and some wine if there's any left; if not, go for some." (032-011)

> Konstantin with the help of Masha persuaded him not to go out and to put on his dressing-gown again and sit down to supper. (033-069)

> Masha promised to write to Konstantin in case of need and to persuade Nikolai to come and stay with his brother. (033-070)
