# The Government Ministry

The imperial government ministry where Alexei Alexandrovitch Karenin holds "one of the most important positions" [013-001] is more than a setting — it is a symbol of the bureaucratic, rule-governed world that Tolstoy critiques throughout the novel.

## Role in the Narrative

The ministry is the world that defines Karenin. His approach to everything, including his marriage, is bureaucratic: correct procedure, proper form, attention to what people will think. When Anna's affair is discovered, Karenin's response is essentially administrative — what is the proper legal and social course of action?

Through his sister Anna's connection to Karenin, Stiva Oblonsky obtained his lucrative post as "president of one of the government boards at Moscow" [013-001]. The casual way Tolstoy notes this — and the observation that Stiva would have gotten the post anyway through "a hundred other personages — brothers, sisters, cousins, uncles, and aunts" — indicts the entire system of patronage and connection that the ministry represents.

## St. Petersburg Bureaucracy

The ministry is the institutional embodiment of St. Petersburg values: hierarchy, procedure, appearances. It is the world that Levin instinctively despises — "his official duties, which he laughed at, and regarded as trifling" [013-026] — and that Stiva treats with "complete indifference to the business in which he was engaged" [013-004].

## Symbolism

The ministry represents the impersonal power of institutions over individual lives. Karenin's tragedy is that he is not a bad man — he is a competent, rule-following man who has been trained by his institution to treat everything, including love and betrayal, as matters of proper procedure. The ministry has made him incapable of the human response that might have saved his marriage.

## Key Passages

- Karenin's position: "one of the most important positions in the ministry" [013-001]
- Stiva's post obtained through Karenin's influence [013-001]
- Levin's contempt for official duties: "trifling" [013-026]
- Stiva's "complete indifference to the business" [013-004]
