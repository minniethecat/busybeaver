# Organizations

This page indexes the institutions and formal organizations that shape the social world of *Anna Karenina*. Tolstoy's characters move within a dense institutional landscape — the government ministries where Karenin and others pursue bureaucratic careers, the cavalry regiments that define Vronsky's identity, the agricultural and zemstvo bodies through which Levin attempts meaningful reform. Each entry examines how institutional affiliation shapes character, constrains action, and reflects the novel's critique of Russian administrative society.

## Notes

Organizational entries cover both formal state institutions and less formal social bodies. Given the relatively sparse institutional detail in a novel focused on domestic and interior life, some entries draw on historical context to supplement textual evidence.
