# Places

This page catalogues the significant locations of *Anna Karenina* — from the glittering ballrooms of Moscow and St. Petersburg to the rural estates of Pokrovskoe and Vozdvizhenskoe, from the railway platforms where the novel's most fateful encounters occur to the Italian retreats where characters seek escape. Place in Tolstoy is never mere backdrop; it shapes mood, class identity, and narrative possibility.

## Notes

Location entries include both specific named places and broader geographic categories (the countryside, the railway, the city). The opposition between urban and rural space is a central organizing tension in Tolstoy's moral vision of the novel.
