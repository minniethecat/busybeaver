## Identity & Role

**Sappho Shtoltz** is a striking presence in [[Princess-Betsy-Tverskaya]]'s salon, distinguished — as her name suggests — by a sharp wit and a bold, unconventional femininity. The name "Sappho" carries deliberate associations with the ancient Greek poetess, signaling both cultural sophistication and a certain sexual ambiguity.

She is introduced in Part Three as one of the "expected guests" at Betsy's country gathering. Her entrance immediately energizes the room, and she becomes the center of attention through sheer force of personality.

## Biography / Arc

Sappho Shtoltz's most extended appearance occurs during the croquet party at Betsy's estate (chapters 094–095). She arrives accompanied by a young man — her latest admirer — who "dogged her footsteps" with devoted attention (095-009).

Her manner is described with precise contrast:

> She was as soft and enervated as Sappho was smart and abrupt. (095-010)

This comparison — likely between Sappho and another female guest — captures the electric, almost masculine energy that distinguishes her from the conventional society women around her. Sappho smokes cigarettes, laughs festively, and dominates conversation with the confidence of someone entirely at ease in her own skin.

When [[Konstantin-Levin]] observes her, he is struck by the deliberate cultivation of her persona — the studied effect of her mannerisms, the performance of originality that is itself a social convention.

## Themes & Significance

- **The performance of identity**: Sappho Shtoltz illustrates how Petersburg society rewards the cultivation of a memorable persona. Her very name is a costume — a classical allusion that signals her difference from ordinary women.

- **Gender and convention**: Sappho's "smart and abrupt" manner, her cigarette smoking, and her coterie of admirers position her at the edge of conventional femininity. She represents a type of female independence that is simultaneously transgressive and thoroughly socialized.

- **Levin's critique of artifice**: Through Levin's eyes, Sappho becomes an emblem of everything artificial about Petersburg life — the studied originality, the theatrical self-presentation, the substitution of wit for sincerity.

## Key Passages

Sappho's introduction captures her theatrical quality:

> Sappho Shtoltz, and a young man beaming with excess of health, the new admirer — they had walked in the expected guests. (095-001)

> Sappho laughed still more festively. (095-005)

> Sappho smoking a cigarette went off with the young man. (095-020)

Each detail — the festive laugh, the cigarette, the trailing admirer — builds a portrait of a woman who has mastered the art of being noticed.
