# Princess Marya Borissovna

**Princess Marya Borissovna** is an elderly aristocrat of St. Petersburg society, the godmother of [[Kitty-Shcherbatskaya]], and a fixture of the capital's highest social circles. She first appears as a personage of sufficient stature to be named alongside the political elite, and her drawing-room provides the setting for one of the novel's emotionally charged encounters.

## Identity & Role

Princess Marya Borissovna is a senior figure in St. Petersburg's aristocratic world — of such established position that she is referenced in jest as someone who might run the War Ministry if given the chance (156-002). She is referred to alternately as "Princess" and "Countess," indicating the fluidity of titles in her rank. Her primary narrative role is that of hostess and social nexus: her house is where Kitty Shcherbatskaya, now married to Levin and living in the country, encounters Vronsky upon his return to Petersburg society — a meeting that tests Kitty's emotional composure.

## Biography

Princess Marya Borissovna is "old" — elderly by the time the main action of the novel unfolds — and has been a fixture of the Petersburg social landscape for decades. As Kitty's godmother, she occupies a semi-familial position in the Shcherbatsky household, combining social authority with the intimacy of a long-standing personal connection. She has "always, always" received Kitty warmly, and Kitty approaches her with the ease of a godchild visiting a familiar elder (198-008).

When Levin and Kitty arrive in St. Petersburg during the winter season, Princess Marya Borissovna hosts a social gathering that brings together many of the novel's key figures. It is at her house that Kitty meets Vronsky for the first time since his return from abroad — a meeting that she has dreaded and that Vronsky, aware of her marriage, handles with careful formality (198-012). The godmother's drawing-room thus becomes the stage for an encounter that measures how much Kitty has changed since her earlier infatuation with Vronsky.

Stepan Arkadyevitch later describes her as "exquisite" — a term of society approval that, coming from Oblonsky, signals her status as a social arbiter (204-032).

## Relationships

- **Kitty Shcherbatskaya (Levin)**: Her goddaughter. Princess Marya Borissovna has known Kitty since childhood and receives her with consistent warmth. Kitty's visit to her godmother's house precipitates the test of her feelings for Vronsky.
- **Vronsky**: He is a guest at Princess Marya Borissovna's, and his presence there creates the conflict of the scene. She knows him as a member of the society set that circulates through her drawing-room.

## Key Passages

> "If only Countess Marya Borissovna were Minister of War, and Princess Vatkovskaya were Commander-in-Chief of the forces..." (156-002)

> The old Princess Marya Borissovna, Kitty's godmother, who had always, always been fond of her, insisted on seeing her. (198-008)

> "Ah, Princess Marya Borissovna, she's exquisite!" said Stepan Arkadyevitch. (204-032)
