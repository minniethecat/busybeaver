# Events

This page indexes the key events and episodes that structure the narrative of *Anna Karenina* — the Moscow ball where Anna and Vronsky first dance, the steeplechase that foreshadows disaster, the harvest scenes that anchor Levin's spiritual search, and the railway death that brings the novel to its devastating close. Each event entry situates the episode within the novel's architecture and traces its consequences through subsequent chapters.

## Notes

Event entries focus on discrete, narratively significant episodes rather than diffuse narrative arcs. Where an event spans multiple chapters, the entry anchors to the episode's initiating or climactic moment.
