## Role in the Stories

Kadali-Garbha is the daughter of a hermit living deep in the forest. She was only a few months old when her mother died, and she was raised entirely by her father in the seclusion of the forest hermitage (084-005). Kadali-Garbha grew up knowing nothing of the world beyond the trees and animals that surrounded her. She learnt a great deal through her father's teachings — about the natural world, about the plants and animals and all that live in it (084-015). Her innocent worldview is the heart of the story, as she embodies a purity untainted by worldly concerns.

## Key Actions

Kadali-Garbha's innocence shaped how she moved through the world. She never even thought about other people or society beyond the hermitage (085-001). Her first encounter with a human from the outside world came when she saw a young girl who asked who she was — in that moment, Kadali-Garbha experienced her first awareness of a world beyond the forest (085-020).

When Kadali-Garbha saw the king for the first time, she experienced a profound and unfamiliar emotional awakening (085-026). Her response was instinctive and pure: Kadali-Garbha clung closely to her father, seeking the safety of the only world she had ever known (087-001). Yet when Kadali-Garbha looked at the king and he smiled, something shifted within her, and she began to trust him (087-009).

The king became a regular presence in her life, visiting Kadali-Garbha every day and bringing all kinds of beautiful gifts (087-021). But Kadali-Garbha's world was threatened when a plot was devised against her. A barber and a wise woman conspired, with the barber falsely claiming Kadali-Garbha was a witch who knew dark secrets (089-006). Kadali-Garbha laughed when the wise woman told her ridiculous stories, showing her innocence even in the face of malice (087-039).

## Relationships

Kadali-Garbha's relationship with her father, the hermit, is her foundation. He taught her everything she knew, and she was content to live under his protection. When the king offered to send Kadali-Garbha away so that she might have worldly comforts, her father knew this was not in her nature — and Kadali-Garbha gladly ran away, wanting to return to her simple life (087-014). Her father consented to let her go, understanding that her soul belonged to the forest.

With the king, Kadali-Garbha formed a bond of mutual fascination. The king was captivated by her purity and came every day to see her, while she gradually opened to the first human outside her family circle. The king, named Dridha-Varman, loved Kadali-Garbha deeply — so much that the court became aware of his devotion (088-016). When someone at court suddenly remembered a wise woman who might know Kadali-Garbha, it set the plot against her in motion (088-021).

The animals of the forest were also an essential part of Kadali-Garbha's world. Unlike ordinary people, the animals trusted Kadali-Garbha completely — a natural bond rooted in her gentleness and harmlessness (084-024).

## Character Traits

Kadali-Garbha embodies innocence, trust, and a deep harmony with the natural world. Her defining characteristic is her complete lack of guile — she never even conceived of deception or malice because she had never encountered them. She was capable of great love and loyalty, as shown in her devotion to her father and her growing affection for the king. Her laughter at the wise woman's scheming demonstrates not naivety but a purity of heart that could not be corrupted by the worldly plotting of others (087-039).
