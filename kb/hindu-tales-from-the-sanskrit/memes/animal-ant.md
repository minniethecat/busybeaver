## Description

Ants appear in "The Jewelled Arrow" as countless tiny workers who come to the aid of Prince Sringa-Bhuja. When the magician Agni-Sikha orders the prince to pile up scattered seed that had just been sown, the prince returns to the field and finds "the whole vast space covered with millions and millions of ants, busily collecting the seed and piling it up against the wall of the town."[^1]

The ants work collectively and silently, completing the impossible task through their sheer numbers and coordinated effort. They are not speaking characters but function as magical helpers, their appearance orchestrated by the magician's daughter Rupa-Sikha, who uses her powers to assist the prince in overcoming her father's trials.

## Appearances

The ants feature in a single but significant episode:

- **The Jewelled Arrow** — The ants gather the scattered seed as part of the magical trials imposed on Prince Sringa-Bhuja by the magician Agni-Sikha. Their collective labour turns an impossible demand into a completed task.

## Narrative Function

The ants serve as agents of magical assistance. They represent the power of collective action — millions of tiny creatures accomplishing together what no individual could achieve alone. Their appearance is one of several supernatural interventions by Rupa-Sikha that enable the prince to survive her father's increasingly dangerous demands and ultimately win her hand in marriage.
