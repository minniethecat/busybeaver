[009-001]Directly [[person-subha-datta|the woodcutter]] heard he could have anything he asked for, he

[009-002]cried, “I will have the [[object-magic-pitcher|magic pitcher]].”

[009-003]You can just imagine what a shock this was to the fairies! You know, of

[009-004]course, that fairies always keep their word. If they could not persuade

[009-005]Subha Datta to choose something else, they would have to give him their

[009-006]beloved, their precious pitcher and would have to seek their food for

[009-007]themselves. They all tried all they could to persuade the woodcutter to

[009-008]choose something else. They took him to their own secret

[009-009]treasure-house, in an old, old tree with a hollow trunk, even the

[009-010]entrance to which no mortal had ever been allowed to see. They

[009-011]blindfolded him before they started, so that he could never reveal the

[009-012]way, and one of them led him by the hand, telling him where the steps

[009-013]going down from the tree began. When at last the bandage was taken from

[009-014]his eyes, he found himself in a lofty hall with an opening in the roof

[009-015]through which the light came. Piled up on the floor were sparkling

[009-016]stones worth a great deal of gold and silver money, and on the walls

[009-017]hung [[object-robe|beautiful robes]]. Subha Datta was quite dazed with all he saw, but

[009-018]he was only an ignorant woodcutter and did not realize the value of the

[009-019]jewels and clothes. So when the fairies, said to him, “Choose anything

[009-020]you like here and let us keep our pitcher,” he shook his head and said:

[009-021]“No! no! no! The pitcher! I will have the pitcher!” One fairy after

[009-022]another picked up the rubies and diamonds and other precious stones and

[009-023]held them in the light, that the woodcutter might see how lovely they

[009-024]were; and when he still only shook his head, they got down the robes

[009-025]and tried to make him put one of them on. “No! the pitcher! the

[009-026]pitcher!” he said, and at last they had to give it up. They bound his

[009-027]eyes again and led him back to the clearing and the pitcher.
