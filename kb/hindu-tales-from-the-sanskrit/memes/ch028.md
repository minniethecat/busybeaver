[028-001]Ill and suffering though he was, [[person-matri-datta|Matri-Datta]] did not dare [[concept-obedience|disobey]] the

[028-002]king: so he came at once. As soon as he appeared, [[person-prasnajit|Prasnajit]] asked him

[028-003]how he was, and said he was sorry to have to make him leave his home

[028-004]when he was ill, but the matter on which he wished to see him was of

[028-005]very great importance. Then he suddenly added: “When your doctor

[028-006]ordered you to take the juice of the nagaballa plant whom did you send

[028-007]to find it?”

[028-008]To this Matri-Datta replied trembling with fear: “My servant, O king,

[028-009]sought it in the [[place-forest|forest]]; and having found it, brought it to me.”

[028-010]“Go back and send that servant to me immediately,” was the reply; and

[028-011]the merchant hurried away, wondering very much why the king wanted to

[028-012]see the man, and hoping that he himself would not get into disgrace on

[028-013]account of anything he had done to make Prasnajit angry.
