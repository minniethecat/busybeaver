## Role in the Stories

Putraka is the central figure of the story "The Magic Shoes and Staff." He appears as a young king who was adopted and raised by a royal couple after they found him as a boy (035-002). Putraka was very much loved by his adoptive parents and the people of the land, and after the king died, everybody wanted Putraka to take the throne (035-015). He proved to be a wise ruler who cared deeply for his subjects, but he also harbored a strong desire for travel and adventure (036-003).

## Key Actions

One of the wise things Putraka did when he became king was to focus on good governance and the welfare of his people (036-001). Despite his responsibilities, his love of travelling persisted, and he often thought that if he were good and virtuous, a way to satisfy his wanderlust might present itself (036-003).

Putraka's life took a dramatic turn when his jealous uncles plotted against him. Having been very well brought up, Putraka was deeply shocked when his trusted servant warned him of their murderous intentions (039-006). When the servant planned to kill his uncles themselves, Putraka looked at them quietly and forgave them, refusing to respond to treachery with violence (039-036).

After this betrayal, Putraka threw himself upon the ground in despair, questioning the goodness of the world (040-001). He fled into the forest, where he discovered three magical objects: a pair of shoes that could carry him anywhere, a staff that could write anything on the ground, and a bowl that could provide any food he desired (041-021, 042-001). These magical items became the instruments through which he would ultimately find his destiny.

## Relationships

Putraka's relationship with his adoptive parents was one of deep mutual affection. His foster mother loved him so deeply that she agreed to his uncles' plan only under duress, and his nurse, who had taken care of Putraka ever since he was born, was horrified at the plot against him (039-019). His father, however, was complicit in the uncles' scheme and had deserted the family — when Putraka's father learned the truth, he knew he had wronged his son (037-008).

His uncles, instead of being grateful for all Putraka's generosity, became jealous of him and began to wish for his downfall (038-007). They schemed to take over the kingdom by assassinating him (039-014). This betrayal forms the emotional core of Putraka's journey, as he must reconcile his innate goodness with the cruelty of those closest to him.

## Character Traits

Putraka's defining characteristic is his combination of wisdom and innocence. Even as a young ruler, he astonished those around him with his intelligence (034-014). He was generous to a fault, giving freely to his uncles and family (037-013). His capacity for forgiveness — choosing not to punish his uncles even when he had the power to destroy them — demonstrates a profound commitment to compassion over vengeance (039-036). However, his despair in the temple also reveals a vulnerability, a tendency to lose faith when confronted with human cruelty (040-001).
