[044-001]The old woman grew very fond of [[person-putraka|Putraka]], caring for him and waiting on

[044-002]him as if he had been her own son. She was so anxious that he should be

[044-003][[concept-happiness|happy]] that she became afraid he would become tired of living alone with

[044-004]her. So she said to him one day: “My dear adopted son, you ought to

[044-005]have a wife to keep you company. I know the very one for you, the only

[044-006]one really worthy of you. She is a princess, and her name is [[person-patala|Patala]].

[044-007]She is so very lovely that every man who sees her falls in love with

[044-008]her and wants to carry her off. So she is most carefully guarded in the

[044-009]top rooms of a great palace, as high as the summits of the loftiest

[044-010][[place-mountain|mountains]].” When Putraka heard this he was all eagerness to see the

[044-011]princess, and at once determined to go forth to seek her. He was more

[044-012]than ever glad now that he had stolen [[object-magic-shoes|the shoes]], because he knew that

[044-013]they would carry him even to the top of the highest mountains.

[044-014]23. What qualities did the old woman show when she told Putraka about

[044-015]the Princess?

[044-016]24. What faults of character did the young king show when he decided at

[044-017]once to leave the old woman who had been so good to him?
