[040-001]When [[person-putraka|Putraka]] was alone, he threw himself upon the ground and wept very

[040-002]bitterly. He felt that he could never be [[concept-happiness|happy]] again, never trust

[040-003]anyone again. He had so loved his father and uncles. It had been such a

[040-004]joy to him to give them pleasure, and yet they hated him and wished to

[040-005]kill him. He wondered whether he was himself to blame for what had

[040-006]happened, and began to think he was not worthy to be king, if he could

[040-007]make such a mistake as he now feared he had made in being so [[concept-generosity|generous]]

[040-008]to those who could have such hard thoughts of him as to want to take

[040-009]his life. Perhaps after all it would be better for his country to have

[040-010]another king. He did not feel as if he could go back to his palace and

[040-011]meet his father and uncles again. “What shall I do? What shall I do?”

[040-012]he cried, his sobs choking his voice. Never in all his life had he

[040-013]thought it possible to be so miserable as he was now. Everything seemed

[040-014]changed and he felt as if he were himself a different person. The only

[040-015]thing that comforted him at all was the thought of his mother, whose

[040-016]love had never failed him; but even that was spoiled by the remembrance

[040-017]that it was her husband who had wished to kill him. She must never know

[040-018]that, for it would break her heart: yet how could he keep it from her?

[040-019]Then the idea came to him that the best thing he could do would be to

[040-020]go away and never see his own people again.
