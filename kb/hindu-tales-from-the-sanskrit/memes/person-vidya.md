## Description

Vidya is the wife of Hari-Sarman, the protagonist of "A Clever Thief." She shares her husband's idle disposition: while their children are sent "out in different directions to beg or steal," Hari-Sarman and Vidya "stayed at home doing nothing."[^1] The couple's poverty is self-inflicted — they live in desperate want not because work is unavailable but because neither is willing to exert effort.

When a neighbour suggests that the family should travel to a distant city where they might find better fortune, "Vidya gladly agreed to this, and the whole party set out, carrying the few possessions they had with them."[^2] This journey proves to be the turning point of their lives: Hari-Sarman's subsequent deception — pretending to be a holy man with supernatural knowledge — succeeds beyond all expectation, and Vidya benefits from her husband's newfound prosperity.

## Role in the Narrative

Vidya serves as a mirror to Hari-Sarman. She shares his laziness and his willingness to accept charity without earning it. The text asks pointedly whether "it would have been better for Hari-Sarman and Vidya if their neighbours had not helped them,"[^3] implying that the couple's dependence on others enabled their idleness.

Once Hari-Sarman's ruse succeeds and he becomes the revered "revealer of secrets" at the royal court, Vidya presumably enjoys the rewards of his deception alongside him. She is present for the important events that follow, including "the marriage of the eldest" of their children[^4] — a celebration that would have been unimaginable during their days of poverty. Her role in the story is that of the passive beneficiary: she does not drive the plot but profits from its outcome.
