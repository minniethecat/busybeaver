[087-001]What [[person-kadali-garbha|Kadali-Garbha]] did was to cling closely to her father, hiding her

[087-002]face on his arm and whispering, “I will not leave you: do not send me

[087-003]away from you, dear father.”

[087-004][[person-mana-kanaka|Mana Kanaka]] stroked her hair, and said in a gentle voice:

[087-005]“But, dear child, your father is old, and must leave you soon. It is a

[087-006]great honour for his little girl to be chosen by the king for his

[087-007]bride. Do not be afraid, but look at him and see how handsome he is and

[087-008]how kind he looks.”

[087-009]Then Kadali-Garbha looked at the king, who smiled at her and looked so

[087-010]charming that her fear began to leave her. She still clung to her

[087-011]father, but no longer hid her face; and Mana Kanaka begged

[087-012]Kadali-Garbha to let him send her away, so that he might talk with the

[087-013]king alone about the wish he had expressed to marry her. The king

[087-014]consented to this, and Kadali-Garbha gladly ran away. But when she

[087-015]reached the door of her home, she looked back, and knew in her heart

[087-016]that she already loved the king and did not want him to go away.

[087-017]It did not take long for the matter of the [[event-wedding|marriage]] to be settled. For

[087-018]Mana Kanaka, sad though he was to lose his dear only child, was [[concept-happiness|glad]]

[087-019]that she should be a queen, and have some one to take care of her when

[087-020]he was gone. After this first visit to the little house in the [[place-forest|forest]]

[087-021]the king came every day to see Kadali-Garbha, bringing all kinds of

[087-022]presents for her. She learnt to love him so much that she became as

[087-023]eager as he was for the wedding to be soon. When the day was fixed, the

[087-024]king sent several ladies of his court to dress the bride in clothes

[087-025]more beautiful that she had ever dreamt of; and in them she looked more

[087-026]lovely even than the first day her lover had seen her.

[087-027]Now amongst these ladies was a very [[concept-wisdom|wise]] woman who could see what was

[087-028]going to happen; and she knew that there would be troubles for the

[087-029]young queen in the palace, because many would be [[concept-jealousy|jealous]] of her

[087-030]happiness. She was very much taken with the beautiful innocent girl,

[087-031]and wanted to help her so much that she managed to get her alone for a

[087-032]few minutes, when she said to her: “I want you to promise me something.

[087-033]It is to take this packet of mustard seeds, hide it in the bosom of

[087-034]your dress, and when you ride to the palace with your husband, strew

[087-035]the seed along the path as you go. You know how quickly mustard grows.

[087-036]Well, it will spring up soon; and if you want to come home again, you

[087-037]can easily find the way by following the green shoots. Alas, I fear

[087-038]they will not have time to wither before you need their help!”

[087-039]Kadali-Garbha laughed when the wise woman talked about trouble coming

[087-040]to her. She was so happy, she could not believe she would want to come

[087-041]home again so soon. “My father can come to me when I want him,” she

[087-042]said. “I need only tell my dear husband to send for him.” But for all

[087-043]that she took the packet of seeds and hid it in her dress.
