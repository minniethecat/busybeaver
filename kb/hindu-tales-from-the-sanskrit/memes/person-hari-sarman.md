## Role in the Stories

Hari-Sarman is the central figure in "A Clever Thief." He lived in a little village with his wife Vidya and their children, but he was utterly lacking in industry or ambition — Hari-Sarman used to send his children out to beg for food because he was too lazy to work himself (076-001, 076-010). Desperate and starving, Hari-Sarman decided to move to a big town, where he went boldly to the chief house and presented himself under false pretenses (076-019). His story follows the consequences of a single lie that spirals into a web of deception from which he cannot easily escape.

## Key Actions

Hari-Sarman's defining action was his decision to deceive a wealthy merchant. The merchant was quite taken in by the story Hari-Sarman told him, believing the lazy man to be a person of wisdom and learning (076-023). Overjoyed at his success, Hari-Sarman hastened out to tell his wife of their good fortune (076-027). Once installed in the merchant's household, however, Hari-Sarman had no special abilities to offer and lived in constant fear of being exposed (077-013).

When the merchant's mare went missing and the merchant called on Hari-Sarman to demonstrate his supposed powers, Hari-Sarman decided to make one last grand deception before fleeing (078-001). By a stroke of luck, the mare was found while Hari-Sarman was talking to the merchant, which only reinforced the false impression of his abilities (079-028). Hari-Sarman, emboldened by this success, now thought it was time to take on even greater deceptions (080-001). He was treated as an honoured guest during the festivities that followed, enjoying the rewards of his fraud (080-011).

## Relationships

Hari-Sarman's relationship with his wife Vidya is the most significant in the story. Vidya initially helped him secure his position with the merchant, but Hari-Sarman refused to go back when she counselled caution after things began to go wrong (079-002). When she came to warn him, Hari-Sarman said he would go back with her — but only after grumbling that nobody appreciated him (079-013, 079-019). He treated his wife with a mixture of dependence and contempt, relying on her cleverness while refusing to heed her warnings.

His relationship with the wealthy merchant was purely transactional — Hari-Sarman viewed the merchant as a mark to be exploited, while the merchant saw Hari-Sarman as a genuine holy man. All went well with Hari-Sarman for a long time as he coasted on the merchant's generosity (080-016). But the fraud could not last forever. Eventually his deceptions caught up with him, and the consequences of his lies became impossible to escape.

## Character Traits

Hari-Sarman is defined by laziness, cunning, and a refusal to accept responsibility. He was not the only person to blame for the family's poverty — both he and Vidya contributed to their situation — but his unwillingness to perform honest work was the root cause of their troubles (076-034). His cleverness is of the wrong kind: it serves only to deceive others and delay the inevitable reckoning. Hari-Sarman's story illustrates a cautionary lesson about the unsustainability of a life built on lies, no matter how cunning the liar.
