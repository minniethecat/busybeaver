[039-001]After many meetings the three wicked men decided that they would pay

[039-002]some one to kill the king, first making the murderer they chose swear

[039-003]that he would never tell who had ordered him to do the terrible deed.

[039-004]It was not very difficult to find a man bad enough to take money for

[039-005]such an evil purpose, and the next thing to do was to decide where and

[039-006]when the deed was to be done. [[person-putraka|Putraka]] had been very well brought up by

[039-007]his mother, and he often went to a beautiful temple near his palace to

[039-008]pray alone. He would sometimes stop there a long time, winning fresh

[039-009]wisdom and strength to do the work he was [[concept-trust|trusted]] with, and praying not

[039-010]only for himself, but for his father, his mother, his aunts and uncles,

[039-011]and for the people he loved so much.

[039-012]The murderer was told to wait in this temple, and when the young king

[039-013]was absorbed in prayer, to fall suddenly upon him and kill him. Then,

[039-014]when Putraka was dead, he was to take his body and bury it far away in

[039-015]the [[place-forest|depths of the forest]] where it could never be found. At first it

[039-016]seemed likely that this [[concept-cruelty|cruel]] plot would succeed. To make quite sure,

[039-017]the murderer got two other men as wicked as himself to come and help

[039-018]him, promising to give them a share in the reward. But the god who had

[039-019]taken care of Putraka ever since he was born, did not forget him now.

[039-020]As the young king prayed, forgetting everything in his earnest pleading

[039-021]for those he loved, he did not see or hear the evil men drawing

[039-022]stealthily close to him. Their arms were uplifted to slay him, and the

[039-023]gleam of the weapons in the light that was always kept burning flashed

[039-024]upon him, when suddenly the heavenly guardian of the temple, who never

[039-025]left it day or night, but was generally invisible, appeared and cast a

[039-026]spell upon the wicked men, whose hands were arrested in the very act to

[039-027]strike.

[039-028]What a wonderful sight that must have been, when Putraka, disturbed in

[039-029]his prayers, looked round and saw the men who had come to kill him,

[039-030]with the shadowy form of the guardian threatening them! He knew at once

[039-031]that he had been saved from a dreadful death by a messenger from the

[039-032]god he had been worshipping. As he gazed at the men, the guardian faded

[039-033]away and he was left alone with them. Slowly the spell cast on them was

[039-034]broken, and they dropped their weapons, prostrated themselves, and

[039-035]clasped their hands in an appeal for mercy to the man they had meant to

[039-036]destroy. Putraka looked at them quietly and sadly. He felt no anger

[039-037]against them, only a great thankfulness for his escape. He spoke to the

[039-038]men very sternly, asking them why they wished to harm him; and the

[039-039]chief murderer told him who had sent them.

[039-040]The knowledge that his father wished to kill him shocked and grieved

[039-041]the young king terribly, but he controlled himself even when he learnt

[039-042]the sad truth. He told the men that he forgave them, for they were not

[039-043]the most to blame; and he made them promise never to betray who had

[039-044]bribed them to kill him. He then gave them some money and told them to

[039-045]leave him.
