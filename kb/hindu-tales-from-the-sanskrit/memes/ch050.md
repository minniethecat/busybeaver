[050-001]So the king summoned his guards and ordered them to take his wife to a

[050-002]strong [[event-imprisonment|prison]] and leave her there. She went with them without making

[050-003]any resistance, only turning once to look lovingly at her husband as

[050-004]she was led away. [[person-vira-bhuja|Vira-Bhuja]] returned to his own palace and had not

[050-005]been there very long when he got a message from Ayasolekha, begging him

[050-006]to give her an interview, for she had something of very great

[050-007]importance to tell him. The king consented at once, thinking to

[050-008]himself, “perhaps she has found out that what she told me about my dear

[050-009][[person-guna-vara|Guna-Vara]] is not true.”

[050-010]Great then was his disappointment when the wicked woman told him she

[050-011]had discovered a plot against his life. The son of Guna-Vara and some

[050-012]of the chief men of the kingdom, she said, had agreed together to kill

[050-013]him, so that [[person-sringa-bhuja|Sringa-Bhuja]] might reign in his stead. She and some of the

[050-014]other wives had overheard conversations between them, and were

[050-015]terrified lest their beloved Lord should be hurt. The young prince, she

[050-016]declared, had had some trouble in persuading the nobles to help him,

[050-017]but he had succeeded at last.

[050-018]Vira-Bhuja simply could not believe this story, for he [[concept-trust|trusted]] his son

[050-019]as much as he loved him; and he sent the mischief maker away, telling

[050-020]her not to dare to enter his presence again. For all that he could not

[050-021]get the matter out of his head. He had Sringa-Bhuja carefully watched;

[050-022]and as nothing against him was found out, he was beginning to feel more

[050-023]easy in his mind, and even to think of going to see Guna-Vara in her

[050-024]prison to ask her to confide in him, when something happened which led

[050-025]him to fear that after all his dear son was not true to him. This was

[050-026]what made him uneasy. He had a wonderful arrow, set with precious

[050-027]jewels, which had been given to him by a [[concept-magic|magician]], and had the power of

[050-028]hitting without fail whatever it was aimed at from however great a

[050-029]distance. The very day he had meant to visit his ill-treated wife, he

[050-030]missed this arrow from the place in which he kept it concealed. This

[050-031]distressed him very much; and after seeking it in vain, he summoned all

[050-032]those who were employed in the palace to his presence, and asked if any

[050-033]of them knew anything about [[object-jewelled-arrow|the arrow]]. He promised that he would

[050-034]forgive any one who helped him to get it back, even if it were the

[050-035][[event-theft|thief]] himself; but added that, if it was not found in three days, he

[050-036]would have all the servants beaten until the one who had stolen it

[050-037]confessed.
