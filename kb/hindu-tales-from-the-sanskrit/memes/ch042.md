[042-001]When [[person-putraka|Putraka]] heard the wonders which, could be done with what he had

[042-002]thought not worth having, he determined to get possession of the three

[042-003]treasures for himself; not considering that it would be very wrong to

[042-004]take what did not belong to him. “It seems a pity to fight,” he said,

[042-005]“why don’t you race for the things, and let whichever wins the race

[042-006]have them? That banyan tree over there would make a good winning post

[042-007]and I will be the umpire.”

[042-008]Instead of guessing what Putraka had in his mind, the brothers, who

[042-009]were very simple fellows, said at once: “All right. We won’t fight,

[042-010]we’ll race instead, and you can give us the start.” Putraka agreed, and

[042-011]directly they were off he [[event-disappearance|lost]] not a moment, but picked up [[object-magic-bowl|the bowl]] and

[042-012][[object-magic-staff|the staff]], put on [[object-magic-shoes|the shoes]], and flew straight up into the air with the

[042-013]treasures. When the brothers came back, disputing about which of them

[042-014]had won, there was not a sign of Putraka, the bowl, the stick, or the

[042-015]shoes. They guessed at once what had happened; and after staring up in

[042-016]the air for a long time, they went home, feeling very much enraged with

[042-017]the man who had cheated them, and ashamed of having been so stupid as

[042-018]to trust him.
