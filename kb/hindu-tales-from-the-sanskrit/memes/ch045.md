[045-001]The very evening of the day when [[person-putraka|Putraka]] heard about the princess, he

[045-002]started on his journey, taking with him his bowl and staff. The old

[045-003]woman gave him very careful instructions which way to go, and begged

[045-004]him to come back to tell her how he had got on. He promised he would,

[045-005]thanked her for all she had done for him, and flew away in a great

[045-006]state of excitement. She watched him till he was quite out of sight,

[045-007]and then went sadly into her lonely home, wondering if she would ever

[045-008]see him again.

[045-009]It was not long before Putraka came in sight of the palace. It was a

[045-010]beautiful night, and the moon was shining full upon the room in which

[045-011]the princess was asleep. It was a very big one, with costly furniture

[045-012]and priceless tapestry hung round the walls, and there were doors

[045-013]behind the tapestry leading to other apartments, in some of which the

[045-014]attendants on [[person-patala|Patala]] slept, whilst others kept watch lest anyone should

[045-015]intrude upon their mistress. No one thought of guarding the windows,

[045-016]for they were so high up that only a bird could reach them.

[045-017]The young king alighted on the ledge of the window of the princess’

[045-018]room, and looked in. There, on a golden bed, amongst soft cushions and

[045-019]embroidered coverings, lay the most lovely creature he had ever beheld,

[045-020]so lovely that he fell in love with her at once and gave a loud cry of

[045-021]delight. This woke the princess, who started up and was about to scream

[045-022]out aloud in her terror at seeing a man looking in at the window, when

[045-023]Putraka with the aid of his [[object-magic-staff|magic staff]] made himself invisible. Then,

[045-024]thinking she had been dreaming, Patala lay down again, and the king

[045-025]began talking to her in a low voice, telling her he had heard of her

[045-026]beauty and had flown from far away to see her. He begged her to allow

[045-027]him to show himself to her, and added: “I will go away again directly

[045-028]afterwards if you wish it.”

[045-029]Putraka’s voice was so gentle, and it seemed to Patala so wonderful

[045-030]that a man could fly and make himself invisible, that she was full of

[045-031]curiosity to see him and find out all about him. So she gave her

[045-032]consent, and immediately afterwards the young king stood within the

[045-033]room, looking so noble and so handsome that she too fell in love at

[045-034]first sight. Putraka told her all about his life and adventures, which

[045-035]interested her very much. She was [[concept-happiness|glad]], she said, that he was a king;

[045-036]but she would have loved him just as well, whoever he might have been.

[045-037]After a long talk, Patala begged him to leave her for fear her

[045-038]attendants should discover him and tell her father about him. “My

[045-039]father would never let me marry you,” she declared, “unless you were to

[045-040]come with many followers as a king to ask my hand; and how can you do

[045-041]that when you are only a [[event-journey|wandering]] exile?”
