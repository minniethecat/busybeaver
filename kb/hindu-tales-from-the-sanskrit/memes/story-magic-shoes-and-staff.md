## Summary

Putraka is born under extraordinary circumstances: found as an infant by a
childless couple who name him and raise him with love. The boy is remarkable
from the start, astonishing "the people round about him" with his gifts
(034-014), and when the king dies, the people demand that
Putraka inherit the throne. Wise beyond his years, he accepts the crown and
rules justly, but his heart yearns for adventure and the wider world
(036-003).

Putraka's destiny takes a magical turn when he receives three gifts: shoes
that enable flight, a staff that grants invisibility, and a bowl that
produces whatever food its owner desires. Using his magic shoes and staff
(045-023), he travels across kingdoms, finds the beautiful
princess he is fated to love, and must prove himself worthy through trials
of loyalty and courage -- all while contending with jealous relatives who
sought to deny him his rightful place (038-007).

## Characters

- **Putraka** — The protagonist, an orphan who becomes a king but whose true
  greatness lies in his wisdom, restlessness, and capacity for love. He
  embodies the ideal that the best rulers are those who feel called to something
  higher than power.
- **the Princess** — Putraka's destined love, imprisoned by a controlling father.
  She is no passive prize but an active partner in their escape.
- **The Childless Couple** — Putraka's adoptive parents, whose love and care
  shape the boy into the man he becomes.
- **the Jealous Relatives** — Those who envied Putraka and sought to deny him
  the throne (038-007), representing the corrupting influence
  of ambition.
- **the Supernatural Beings** — The various spirits who bestow Putraka's magical
  gifts, testing his worthiness at each encounter.

## Moral Themes

The story explores the proper relationship between power and character. Putraka
receives immense magical gifts -- flight, invisibility, endless food -- but his
true test is whether he will use them selfishly or for the good of others. That
he passes this test makes him a model ruler. The tale also examines jealousy
and its corrosive effect: Putraka's relatives "became jealous" of his gifts
(038-007), while the reader is asked whether "true love [is] ever
jealous" (049-057). The answer the story provides is clear:
love rooted in possession is fragile; love rooted in loyalty endures.

## Magical Elements

Three enchanted objects drive the plot: flying shoes, a staff of invisibility
("magic staff made himself invisible," 045-023), and a bowl of
plenty. These gifts are not merely plot devices but moral instruments -- they
test Putraka's character at every turn. The fact that he must earn each gift
through demonstrations of virtue reinforces the story's ethical framework.

## Setting

The story spans multiple kingdoms, from Putraka's modest origins to a distant
palace where the princess is held captive. The contrast between the wild freedom
of flight and the confining walls of royal courts underscores the tension between
adventure and responsibility.
