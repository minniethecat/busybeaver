[071-001]The mouse did not at once return to his hole when the [[animal-bird|birds]] were gone,

[071-002]but went for a little stroll, which brought him to the ground still

[071-003]strewn with rice, which he began to eat with great relish. “It’s an ill

[071-004]wind,” he said to himself, “which brings nobody any good. There’s many

[071-005]a good meal for my whole family here.”

[071-006]Presently he was joined by the [[animal-crow|old crow]], who had flown down from his

[071-007]perch unnoticed by [[animal-mouse|Hiranya]], and now addressed him in his croaky voice:

[071-008]“Hiranya,” he said, “for that I know is your name, I am called

[071-009]Laghupatin and I would gladly have you for a friend. I have seen all

[071-010]that you did for [[animal-pigeon|the pigeons]], and have come to the conclusion that you

[071-011]are a mouse of great wisdom, ready to help those who are in trouble,

[071-012]without any thought of yourself.”

[071-013]“You are quite wrong,” squeaked Hiranya. “I am not so silly as you make

[071-014]out. I have no wish to be your friend. If you were hungry, you wouldn’t

[071-015]hesitate to gobble me up. I don’t care for that sort of affection.”

[071-016]With that Hiranya whisked away to his hole, pausing at the entrance,

[071-017]when he knew the crow could not get at him, to cry, “You be off to your

[071-018]nest and leave me alone!”

[071-019]The feelings of the crow were very much hurt at this speech, the more

[071-020]that he knew full well it was not exactly love for the mouse, which had

[071-021]led him to make his offer, but self-interest: for who could tell what

[071-022]difficulties he himself might some day be in, out of which the mouse

[071-023]might help him? Instead of [[concept-obedience|obeying]] Hiranya, and going back to his nest,

[071-024]he hopped to the mouse’s hole, and putting his head on one side in what

[071-025]he thought was a very taking manner, he said:

[071-026]“Pray do not misjudge me so. Never would I harm you! Even if I did not

[071-027]wish to have you for a friend, I should not dream of gobbling you up,

[071-028]as you say, however hungry I might be. Surely you are aware that I am a

[071-029]strict vegetarian, and never eat the flesh of other creatures. At least

[071-030]give me a trial. Let us share a meal together, and talk the matter

[071-031]over.”
