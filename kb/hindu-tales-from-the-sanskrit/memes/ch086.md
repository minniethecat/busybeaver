[086-001][[person-mana-kanaka|Mana Kanaka]] knew at once that the man on the horse was the king; and a

[086-002]great fear entered his heart when he saw how [[person-dridha-varman|Dridha-Varman]] looked at

[086-003]his beloved only child.

[086-004]“Who are you, and who is that lovely girl?” asked the king. And Mana

[086-005]Kanaka answered, “I am only a humble woodcutter; and this is my only

[086-006]child, whose mother has long been dead.”

[086-007]“Her mother must have been a very lovely woman, if her daughter is like

[086-008]her,” said the king. “Never before have I seen such perfect beauty.”

[086-009]“Her mother,” replied Mana Kanaka, “was indeed what you say; and her

[086-010]soul was as beautiful as the body in which it dwelt all too short a

[086-011]time.”

[086-012]“I would have your daughter for my wife,” said the king; “and if you

[086-013]will give her to me, she shall have no wish ungratified. She shall have

[086-014]servants to wait on her and other young girls to be her companions;

[086-015]beautiful clothes to wear, the best of food to eat, horses and

[086-016]carriages as many as she will, and no work to do with her own hands.”

[086-017]5. If you had been [[person-kadali-garbha|Kadali-Garbha]], what would you have said when you

[086-018]heard all these promises?

[086-019]6. Of all the things the king said she should have, which would you

[086-020]have liked best?
