[060-001]Once more the bride and bridegroom set forth on their way, and once

[060-002]more they soon heard [[person-agni-sikha|Agni-Sikha]] coming after them. For when he got back

[060-003]to his palace, and the servants hastened out to take his horse, he

[060-004]guessed that a [[event-deception|trick]] had been played on him. He did not even dismount,

[060-005]but just turned his horse’s head round and galloped back again. “If

[060-006]ever,” he thought to himself, “I catch those two young people, I’ll

[060-007]make them wish they had obeyed me. Yes, they shall suffer for it. I am

[060-008]not going to stand being defied like this.”

[060-009]This time [[person-rupa-sikha|Rupa-Sikha]] [[concept-contentment|contented]] herself with making her husband and

[060-010]Marut invisible, whilst she changed herself into a letter-carrier,

[060-011]hurrying along the road as if not a moment was to be [[event-disappearance|lost]]. She took no

[060-012]notice of her father, till he reined up his [[animal-horse|steed]] and shouted to her:

[060-013]“Have you seen a man and woman on horseback pass by?”

[060-014]“No, indeed,” she said: “I have a very important letter to deliver, and

[060-015]could think of nothing but making all the haste possible.”

[060-016]“And what is this important letter about?” asked Agni-Sikha. “Can you

[060-017]tell me that?”

[060-018]“Oh, yes, I can tell you that,” she said. “But where can you have been,

[060-019]not to have heard the terrible news about the ruler of this land?”

[060-020]“You can’t tell me anything I don’t know about him,” answered the

[060-021][[concept-magic|magician]], “for he is my greatest friend.”

[060-022]“Then you know that he is dying from a wound he got in a battle with

[060-023]his enemies only yesterday. I am to take this letter to his brother

[060-024]Dhuma-Sikha, bidding him come to see him before the end.”

[060-025]Again Agni-Sikha wondered if he were dreaming, or if he were under some

[060-026]strange spell and did not really know who he was? Being able, as he

[060-027]was, to cast spells on other people, he was ready to fancy the same

[060-028]thing had befallen him. He said nothing when he heard that he was

[060-029]wounded, and was about to turn back again when Rupa-Sikha said to him:

[060-030]“As you are on horseback and can get to Dhuma-Sikha’s temple quicker

[060-031]than I can, will you carry the message of his brother’s approaching

[060-032]death to him for me, and bid him make all possible haste if he would

[060-033]see him alive?”

[060-034]This was altogether too much for the magician, who became sure that

[060-035]there was something very wrong about him. He knew he was not wounded or

[060-036]dying, but he thought he must be ill of fever, fancying he heard what

[060-037]he did not. He stared fixedly at his daughter, and she stared up at

[060-038]him, half-afraid he might find out who she was, but he never guessed.

[060-039]“Do your own errands,” he said at last; and slashing his poor innocent

[060-040]horse with his whip, he wheeled round and dashed home again as fast as

[060-041]he could. Again his servants ran out to receive him, and he gloomily

[060-042]dismounted, telling them to send his chief councillor to him in his

[060-043]private apartments. Shut up with him, he poured out all his troubles,

[060-044]and the councillor advised him to see his physician without any delay,

[060-045]for he felt sure that these strange fancies were caused by illness.

[060-046]The doctor, when he came, was very much puzzled, but he looked as [[concept-wisdom|wise]]

[060-047]as he could, ordered perfect rest and all manner of disagreeable

[060-048]medicines. He was very much surprised at the change he noticed in his

[060-049]patient, who, instead of angrily declaring that there was nothing the

[060-050]matter with him, was evidently in a great fright about his health. He

[060-051]shut himself up for many days, and it was a long time before he got

[060-052]over the shock he had received, and then it was too late for him to be

[060-053]revenged or the lovers.
