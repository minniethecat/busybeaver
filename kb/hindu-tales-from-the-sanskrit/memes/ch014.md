[014-001]This is the end of the story of the [[concept-magic|Magic]] Pitcher, but it was the

[014-002]beginning of a new chapter in the lives of [[person-subha-datta|Subha Datta]] and his family.

[014-003]They never forgot the wonder-working pitcher, and the children were

[014-004]never tired of hearing the story of how their father came to get it.

[014-005]They often wandered about in the [[place-forest|forest]], hoping that they too would

[014-006]meet with some wonderful adventure, but they never saw the fairies or

[014-007]found a [[object-magic-pitcher|magic pitcher]]. By slow degrees the woodcutter returned to his

[014-008]old ways, but he had learnt one lesson. He never again kept a secret

[014-009]from his wife; because he felt sure that, if he had told her the truth

[014-010]about the pitcher when he first came home, she would have helped him to

[014-011]save the precious treasure.
