[056-001]When [[person-sringa-bhuja|Sringa-Bhuja]] heard what [[person-agni-sikha|Agni-Sikha]] said, he was full of [[concept-happiness|joy]]; but

[056-002][[person-rupa-sikha|Rupa-Sikha]] knew well that her father did not mean a word of it. She

[056-003]waited quietly beside her lover, till the [[concept-magic|magician]] bade all the sisters

[056-004]but herself leave the hall. Then the magician, with a very wicked look

[056-005]on his face, said:

[056-006]“Before the ceremony there is just one little thing you must do for me,

[056-007]dear son-in-law that is to be. Go outside the [[place-city|town]], and near the most

[056-008]westerly tower you will find a team of oxen and a plough awaiting you.

[056-009]Close to them is a pile of three hundred bushels of sesame seed. This

[056-010]you must sow this very day, or instead of a bridegroom you will be a

[056-011]dead man to-morrow.”

[056-012]Great was the dismay of Sringa-Bhuja when he heard this. But Rupa-Sikha

[056-013]whispered to him, “Fear not, for I will help you.” Sadly the prince

[056-014]left the palace alone, to seek the field outside the city; the guards,

[056-015]who knew he was the accepted lover of their favourite mistress, letting

[056-016]him pass unhindered. There, sure enough, near the western tower were

[056-017]the oxen, the plough and a great pile of seed. Never before had poor

[056-018]Sringa-Bhuja had to work for himself, but his great love for Rupa-Sikha

[056-019]made him determine to do his best. So he was about to begin to guide

[056-020]the oxen across the field, when, behold, all was suddenly changed.

[056-021]Instead of an unploughed tract of land, covered with weeds, was a field

[056-022]with rows and rows of regular furrows. The piles of seed were gone, and

[056-023]flocks of [[animal-bird|birds]] were gathering in the hope of securing some of it as it

[056-024]lay in the furrows.

[056-025]As Sringa-Bhuja was staring in amazement at this beautiful scene, he

[056-026]saw Rupa-Sikha, looking more lovely than ever, coming towards him. “Not

[056-027]in vain,” she said to him, “am I my father’s daughter. I too know how

[056-028]to compel even nature to do my will; but the danger is not over yet. Go

[056-029]quickly back to the palace, and tell Agni-Sikha that his wishes are

[056-030]fulfilled.”
