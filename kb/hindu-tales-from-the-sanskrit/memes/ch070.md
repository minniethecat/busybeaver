[070-001]In a moment all [[animal-pigeon|the pigeons]], who were accustomed to [[concept-obedience|obey]] their leader,

[070-002]did as they were bid; each little bird seized a separate thread of the

[070-003]net in his beak and up, up, up, they all flew, looking very beautiful

[070-004]with the sunlight gleaming on their white wings. Very soon they were

[070-005]out of sight; and the man, who thought he had hit upon a very [[concept-cleverness|clever]]

[070-006]plan, came forth from his hiding-place, very much surprised at what had

[070-007]happened. He stood gazing up after his vanished net for a little time,

[070-008]and then went away muttering to himself, whilst the [[concept-wisdom|wise]] [[animal-crow|old crow]]

[070-009]laughed at him.

[070-010]When the pigeons had flown some distance, and were beginning to get

[070-011]exhausted, for the net was heavy and they were quite unused to carrying

[070-012]loads, the king bade them rest awhile in a clearing of the [[place-forest|forest]]; and

[070-013]as they all lay on the ground panting for breath, with the [[concept-cruelty|cruel]] net

[070-014]still hampering them, he said:

[070-015]“What we must do now is to take this horrible net to my old friend

[070-016][[animal-mouse|Hiranya]] the mouse, who will, I am quite sure, nibble through the

[070-017]strings for me and set us all free. He lives, as you all know, near the

[070-018]tree where the net was spread, deep underground; but there are many

[070-019]passages leading to his home, and we shall easily find one of the

[070-020]openings. Once there, we will all lift up our voices, and call to him

[070-021]at once, when he will be sure to hear us.” So the weary pigeons took up

[070-022]their burden once more, and sped back whence they had come, greatly to

[070-023]the surprise of the crow, who wondered at their coming back to the very

[070-024]place where misfortune had overtaken them. He very soon learnt the

[070-025]reason, and got so excited watching what was going on, that he hopped

[070-026]out of his nest and perched upon a branch where he could see better.

[070-027]Presently a great clamour arose, one word being repeated again and

[070-028]again: “Hiranya! Hiranya! Hiranya.”

[070-029]“Why, that’s the name of the mouse who lives down below there!” thought

[070-030]the crow. “Now, what good can he do? I know, I know,” he added, as he

[070-031]remembered the sharp teeth of Hiranya. “That king of the pigeons is a

[070-032]sensible fellow. I must make friends with him.”

[070-033]Very soon, as the pigeons lay fluttering and struggling outside one of

[070-034]the entrances to Hiranya’s retreat, the mouse came out. He didn’t even

[070-035]need to be told what was wanted, but at once began to nibble the

[070-036]string, first setting free the king, and then all the rest of the

[070-037][[animal-bird|birds]]. “A friend in need is a friend indeed,” cried the king; “a

[070-038]thousand thousand thanks!” And away he flew up into the beautiful free

[070-039]air of heaven, followed by the [[concept-happiness|happy]] pigeons, none of them ever likely

[070-040]to forget the adventure or to pick up food from the ground without a

[070-041]good look at it first.
