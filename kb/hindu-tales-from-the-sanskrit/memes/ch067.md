[067-001]After embracing his wife and thanking her for saving him, the vizier

[067-002]said to her: “Before we return home, let us give thanks to the great

[067-003]God who helped me in my need by putting into my head the device by

[067-004]which I escaped.” The [[concept-happiness|happy]] pair then prostrated themselves on the

[067-005]ground, and in fervent words of gratitude expressed their sense of what

[067-006]the God they worshipped had done for them. “And now,” said

[067-007][[person-dhairya-sila|Dhairya-Sila]], “the next thing we have to do is to take the dear little

[067-008][[object-beetle-thread|beetle]] which was the instrument of my rescue back to the place it came

[067-009]from.” And taking off his turban, he showed his wife the tiny creature

[067-010]lying in the soft folds.

[067-011][[person-buddhi-mati|Buddhi-Mati]] led her husband to the garden where she had found the

[067-012]beetle, and Dhairya-Sila laid it tenderly on the ground, fetched some

[067-013]food for it, such as he knew it loved, and there left it to take up its

[067-014]old way of life. The rest of the day he spent quietly in his own home

[067-015]with his wife, keeping out of sight of his servants, lest they should

[067-016]report his return to his master. “You must never breathe a word to any

[067-017]one of how I escaped,” Dhairya-Sila said, and his wife promised that

[067-018]she never would.

[067-020]betray how he got down from the tower, if any one went to look at it?
