[064-001][[person-buddhi-mati|Buddhi-Mati]] listened very attentively to these strange instructions,

[064-002]and began to ask questions about them. “Why do you want [[animal-beetle|the beetle]]? Why

[064-003]do you want the honey?” and so on. But her husband checked her. “I have

[064-004]no strength to waste in explanations,” he said. “Go home in peace,

[064-005]sleep well, and dream of me.” So the anxious wife went meekly away; and

[064-006]early the next day she set to work to [[concept-obedience|obey]] the orders she had received.

[064-007]She had some trouble in obtaining fine enough silk, so very, very thin

[064-008]it had to be, like a spider’s web; but the cotton, twine and rope were

[064-009]easily bought; and to her surprise she was not asked what she wanted

[064-010]them for. It took her a good while to choose the [[object-beetle-thread|beetle]]. For though she

[064-011]had a vague kind of idea that the silk, the cotton, twine, and rope,

[064-012]were to help her husband get down from the tower, she could not imagine

[064-013]what share the beetle and the honey were to take. In the end she chose

[064-014]a very handsome, strong-looking, brilliantly coloured fellow who lived

[064-015]in the garden of her home and whom she knew to be fond of honey.

[064-016]5. Can you guess how the beetle and the honey were to help in saving

[064-017][[person-dhairya-sila|Dhairya-Sila]]?

[064-018]6. Do you think it would have been better if the vizier had told his

[064-019]wife how all the things he asked for were to be used?
