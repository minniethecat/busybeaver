[065-001]All the time [[person-buddhi-mati|Buddhi-Mati]] was at work for her husband, she was thinking

[065-002]of him and looking forward to the [[concept-happiness|happy]] day of his return home. She had

[065-003]such faith in him that she did not for a moment doubt that he would

[065-004]escape; but she was anxious about the future, feeling sure that the

[065-005]Raja would never forgive [[person-dhairya-sila|Dhairya-Sila]] for being wiser than himself.

[065-006]Exactly at the time fixed the faithful wife appeared at the foot of the

[065-007]tower, with all the things she had been told to bring with her.

[065-008]“Is all well with my lord?” she whispered, as she gazed up through the

[065-009]darkness. “I have the [[object-beetle-thread|silken thread]] as fine as gossamer, the cotton

[065-010]thread, the twine, the rope, [[animal-beetle|the beetle]] and the honey.”

[065-011]“Yes,” answered Dhairya-Sila, “all is still well with me. I have slept

[065-012]well, feeling confident that my dear one would bring all that is needed

[065-013]for my safety; but I dread the great heat of another day, and we must

[065-014]lose no time in getting away from this terrible tower. Now attend most

[065-015]carefully to all I bid you do; and remember not to speak loud, or the

[065-016]sentries posted within hearing will take alarm and drive you away.

[065-017]First of all, tie the end of the silken thread round the middle of the

[065-018]beetle, leaving all its legs quite free. Then rub the drop of honey on

[065-019]its nose, and put the little creature on the wall, with its nose turned

[065-020]upwards towards me. It will smell the honey, but will not guess that it

[065-021]carries it itself, and it will crawl upwards in the hope of getting to

[065-022]the hive from which that honey came. Keep the rest of the silk firmly

[065-023]held, and gradually unwind it as the beetle climbs up. Mind you do not

[065-024]let it slip, for my very life depends on that slight link with you.”

[065-025]7. Which do you think had the harder task to perform—the husband at the

[065-026]top of the tower or the wife at the foot of it?

[065-027]8. Do you think the beetle was likely to imagine it was on the way to a

[065-028]hive of bees when it began to creep up the tower?
