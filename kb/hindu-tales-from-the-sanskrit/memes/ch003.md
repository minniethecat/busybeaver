[003-001][[person-subha-datta|Subha Datta]], though he certainly did love his wife and children, was so

[003-002]tempted at the idea of stopping in the [[place-forest|forest]] with the beautiful girls

[003-003]that, after hesitating a little while, he said, “Yes, I will stop with

[003-004]you, if you are quite sure all will be well with my dear ones.”

[003-005]“You need not be afraid about that,” said another of the girls. “We are

[003-006]fairies, you see, and we can do all sorts of wonderful things. It isn’t

[003-007]even necessary for us to go where your dear ones are. We shall just

[003-008]wish them everything they want, and they will get it. And the first

[003-009]thing to be done is to give you some food. You must work for us in

[003-010]return, of course.”

[003-011]Subha Datta at once replied, “I will do anything you wish.”

[003-012]“Well, begin by sweeping away all the dead leaves from the clearing,

[003-013]and then we will all sit down and eat together.”

[003-014]Subha Datta was very [[concept-happiness|glad]] that what he was asked to do was so easy. He

[003-015]began by cutting a branch from a tree, and with it he swept the floor

[003-016]of what was to be the dining-room. Then he looked about for the food,

[003-017]but he could see nothing but a great big pitcher standing in the shade

[003-018]of a tree, the branches of which hung over the clearing. So he said to

[003-019]one of the fairies, “Will you show me where the food is, and exactly

[003-020]where you would like me to set it out?”

[003-021]At these questions all the fairies began to laugh, and the sound of

[003-022]their laughter was like the tinkling of a number of bells.
