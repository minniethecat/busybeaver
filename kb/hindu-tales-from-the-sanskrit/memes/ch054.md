[054-001][[person-sringa-bhuja|Sringa-Bhuja]] loved [[person-rupa-sikha|Rupa-Sikha]] so much that he was ready to [[concept-obedience|obey]] her in

[054-002]whatever she asked. So he at once went with her to the palace. On every

[054-003]side he saw signs of the strength and power of the [[concept-magic|magician]]. Each gate

[054-004]was guarded by tall soldiers in shining armour, who saluted Rupa-Sikha

[054-005]but scowled fiercely at him. He knew full well that, if he had tried to

[054-006]pass alone, they would have prevented him from doing so. At last the

[054-007]two came to the great hall, where the magician was walking backwards

[054-008]and forwards, working himself into a rage at being kept waiting.

[054-009]Directly he looked at the prince, he knew him for the man who had shot

[054-010]the [[object-jewelled-arrow|jewelled arrow]] at him when he had taken the form of a crane, and he

[054-011]determined that he would be revenged. He was too [[concept-cleverness|cunning]] to let

[054-012]Sringa-Bhuja guess that he knew him, and pretended to be very [[concept-happiness|glad]] to

[054-013]see him. He even went so far as to say that he had long wished to find

[054-014]a prince worthy to wed his youngest and favourite daughter. “You,” he

[054-015]added, “seem to me the very man, young, handsome and—to judge from the

[054-016]richness of your dress and jewels—able to give my beloved one all she

[054-017]needs.”

[054-018]The prince could hardly believe his ears, and Rupa-Sikha also was very

[054-019]much surprised. She guessed however that her father had some evil

[054-020]purpose in what he said, and looked earnestly at Sringa-Bhuja in the

[054-021]hope of making him understand. But the prince was so overjoyed at the

[054-022]thought that she was to be his wife that he noticed nothing. So when

[054-023][[person-agni-sikha|Agni-Sikha]] added, “I only make one condition: you must promise that you

[054-024]will never disobey my commands, but do whatever I tell you without a

[054-025]moment’s hesitation,” Sringa-Bhuja, without waiting to think, said at

[054-026]once, “Only give me your daughter and I will serve you in any way you

[054-027]wish.”

[054-028]“That’s settled then!” cried the magician, and he clapped his hands

[054-029]together. In a moment a number of attendants appeared, and their master

[054-030]ordered them to lead the prince to the best apartments in the palace,

[054-031]to prepare a bath for him, and do everything he asked them.
