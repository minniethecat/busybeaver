[021-001]In one of the smaller [[place-city|cities]] of [[place-india|India]] called [[place-sravasti|Sravasti]] the people

[021-002]gathered together on a very hot day to stare at and talk about a

[021-003]stranger, who had come in to the town, looking very weary and walking

[021-004]with great difficulty because his feet were sore with tramping for a

[021-005]long distance on the [[place-road|rough roads]]. He was a Brahman, that is to say, a

[021-006]man who devoted his whole life to prayer, and had promised to give up

[021-007]everything for the sake of pleasing the god in whom he believed, and to

[021-008]care nothing for comfort, for riches, or for good food.

[021-009]This Brahman carried nothing with him but a staff to help him along,

[021-010]and a bowl in which to receive the offerings of those who thought it

[021-011]their duty to help him and hoped by doing so to win favour in the sight

[021-012]of God. He was naked, except for a cloth worn about his loins, and his

[021-013]long hair was all matted together for want of combing and brushing. He

[021-014]made his way very slowly and painfully through the crowds, till he came

[021-015]to a shady corner, and there he sank down exhausted, holding out his

[021-016]bowl for the gifts of the people. Very soon his bowl would have been

[021-017]full of all sorts of good things, but he made it clear that he would

[021-018]accept nothing to eat except rice still in the husk, and nothing to

[021-019]drink but pure water. He was however willing to take money; and when

[021-020]the people who wished to help him found that out, they brought him a

[021-021]good many silver and gold pieces. Some who had no money to spare gave

[021-022]him jewels and other things which could be sold for money.

[021-023]1. Can you explain why the Brahman would only accept such food as rice

[021-024]in the husk and water?

[021-025]2. Do you think it was right or wrong of the Brahman to take money and

[021-026]jewels?
