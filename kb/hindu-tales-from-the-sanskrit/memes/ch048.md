[048-001]Great indeed was the delight of [[person-patala|Patala]] when her beloved [[person-putraka|Putraka]] once

[048-002]more flew in at her window; but she was still trembling with fear for

[048-003]him and begged him to go away back to his own land as quickly as

[048-004]possible.

[048-005]“I will not go without you,” replied Putraka. “Wrap yourself up warmly,

[048-006]for it is cold flying through the air, and we will go away together,

[048-007]and your [[concept-cruelty|cruel]] father shall never see you again.”

[048-008]Patala wept at hearing this, for it seemed terrible to her to have to

[048-009]choose between the father she loved and Putraka. But in the end her

[048-010]lover got his own way, and just as those who were seeking him were

[048-011]heard approaching, he seized his dear one in his arms and flew off with

[048-012]her. He did not return to his own land even then, but directed his

[048-013]course to [[place-ganges|the Ganges]], the grand and beautiful river which the people of

[048-014][[place-india|India]] love and worship, calling it their Mother Ganga. By the banks of

[048-015]the sacred stream the lovers rested, and with the aid of his [[object-magic-bowl|magic bowl]]

[048-016]Putraka soon had a good and delicious meal ready, which they both

[048-017]enjoyed very much. As they ate, they consulted together what they had

[048-018]better do now, and Patala, who was as [[concept-cleverness|clever]] as she was beautiful,

[048-019]said:

[048-020]“Would it not be a good thing to build a new city in this lovely place?

[048-021]You could do it with your marvellous staff, could you not?”

[048-022]“Why, of course, I could,” said Putraka laughing. “Why didn’t I think

[048-023]of it myself?” Very soon a wonderful [[place-city|town]] rose up, which the young king

[048-024]wished to be as much as possible like the home he had left, only larger

[048-025]and fuller of fine buildings than it. When the town was made, he wished

[048-026]it to be full of [[concept-happiness|happy]] inhabitants, with [[place-temple|temples]] in which they might

[048-027]worship, priests to teach them how to be good, [[place-market|markets]] in which food

[048-028]and all that was needed could be bought, tanks and rivulets full of

[048-029]pure water, soldiers and officers to defend the gates, elephants on

[048-030]which he and his wife could ride, everything in fact that the heart of

[048-031]man or woman could desire.

[048-032]The first thing Putraka and Patala did after the rise of their own

[048-033]town, which they named Patali-Putra[1] after themselves, was to get

[048-034]married in accordance with the rites of their religion; and for many,

[048-035]many years they reigned wisely over their people, who loved them and

[048-036]their children with all their hearts. Amongst the attendants on those

[048-037]children was the old woman who had shown [[concept-generosity|kindness]] to Putraka in his

[048-038]loneliness and trouble. For when he told Patala the story of his life,

[048-039]she reproached him for his neglect of one to whom he owed so much. She

[048-040]made him feel quite ashamed of himself, and he flew away and brought

[048-041]the dear old lady back with him, to her very great delight.
