[032-001][[person-brahman-jewelled-arrow|The Brahman]], who had spent the time of waiting in prayers that his

[032-002]treasure should be given back to him, and was still determined that, if

[032-003]it were not, he would starve himself to death, was full of delight when

[032-004]he heard that it had been found. He hastened to the palace and was

[032-005]taken before the king, who said to him: “There is your treasure. Take

[032-006]it away, and make a better use of it than before. If you lose it again,

[032-007]I shall not try to recover it for you.”

[032-008]The Brahman, [[concept-happiness|glad]] as he was to have his money and jewels restored, did

[032-009]not like to be told by the king to make a better use of them. Besides

[032-010]this he wanted to have the [[event-theft|thief]] punished; and he began talking about

[032-011]that, instead of thanking [[person-prasnajit|Prasnajit]] and promising to follow his advice.

[032-012]The king looked at him much as he had looked at the thief and said:

[032-013]“The matter is ended so far as I have anything to do with it: go in

[032-014]peace.”

[032-015]The Brahman, who was accustomed to be honoured by every one from the

[032-016]king on his throne to the beggars in the street, was astonished at the

[032-017]way in which Prasnajit spoke to him. He would have said more, but the

[032-018]king made a sign to his attendants, two of whom dragged the sack to the

[032-019]entrance of the palace and left it there, so that there was nothing for

[032-020]the Brahman to do but to take it away with him. Every one who has read

[032-021]this wonderful story would, of courses like to know what became of him

[032-022]after that, but nothing more is told about him.

[032-023]23. Do you think that the Brahman learnt anything from his loss and

[032-024]recovery of his treasure?

[032-025]24. Was the Brahman more wicked than, the thief or the thief than the

[032-026]Brahman?

[032-027]25. Do you think the Brahman continued to be a [[concept-selfishness|miser]] for the rest of

[032-028]his life?

[032-029]26. What were the chief characteristics of the king—that is to say,

[032-030]what sort of man do you think he was?

[032-031]27. Which of the people who are spoken of in this story do you like and

[032-032]admire most, and which do you dislike most?
