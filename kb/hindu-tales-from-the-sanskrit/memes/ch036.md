[036-001]One of the other [[concept-wisdom|wise]] things [[person-putraka|Putraka]] did, when he became king, was to

[036-002]make great friends with his Brahman subjects. Brahmans are always very

[036-003]fond of travelling, and Putraka thought, if he were good and [[concept-generosity|generous]]

[036-004]to them, they would talk about him wherever they went, and that perhaps

[036-005]through them his father and uncles would hear about him. He felt sure

[036-006]that, if they knew he was now a king ruling over their native land,

[036-007]they would want to come back. He gave the Brahmans plenty of money, and

[036-008]told them to try and find his father and uncles. If they did, they were

[036-009]to say how anxious he was to see them, and promise them everything they

[036-010]wanted, if only they would return.

[036-011]7. Do you think it was wise of Putraka to be so anxious to get his

[036-012]father and uncles back, when he knew how [[concept-selfishness|selfish]] they had been in

[036-013]leaving his mother and aunts behind them?

[036-014]8. Can you suggest anything else Putraka might have done in the matter?
