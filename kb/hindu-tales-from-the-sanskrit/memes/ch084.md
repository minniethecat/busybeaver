[084-001]Near a [[place-city|town]] in [[place-india|India]] called [[place-ikshumati|Ikshumati]], on a beautiful wide river, with

[084-002]trees belonging to a great [[place-forest|forest]] near its banks, there dwelt a holy

[084-003]man named [[person-mana-kanaka|Mana Kanaka]], who spent a great part of his life praying to

[084-004]God. He had [[event-disappearance|lost]] his wife when his only child, a lovely girl called

[084-005][[person-kadali-garbha|Kadali-Garbha]], was only a few months old. Kadali-Garbha was a very

[084-006][[concept-happiness|happy]] girl, with many friends in the woods round her home, not children

[084-007]like herself, but wild creatures, who knew she would not do them any

[084-008]harm. They loved her and she loved them. The [[animal-bird|birds]] were so tame that

[084-009]they would eat out of her hand, and [[animal-deer|the deer]] used to follow her about

[084-010]in the hope of getting the bread she carried in her pocket for them.

[084-011]Her father taught her all she knew, and that was a great deal; for she

[084-012]could read quite learned books in the ancient language of her native

[084-013]land. Better even than what she found out in those books was what Mana

[084-014]Kanaka told her about the loving God of all gods who rules the world

[084-015]and all that live in it. Kadali-Garbha also learnt a great deal through

[084-016]her friendship with wild animals. She knew where the birds built their

[084-017]nests, where the baby deer were born, where the squirrels hid their

[084-018]nuts, and what food all the dwellers in the forest liked best. She

[084-019]helped her father to work in their garden in which all their own food

[084-020]was grown; and she loved to cook the fruit and vegetables for Mana

[084-021]Kanaka and herself. Her clothes were made of the bark of the trees in

[084-022]the forest, which she herself wove into thin soft material suitable for

[084-023]wearing in a hot climate.
