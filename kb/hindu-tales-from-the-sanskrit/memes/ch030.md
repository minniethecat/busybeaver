[030-001]When the silence had lasted about ten minutes, the [[event-theft|thief]] raised his

[030-002]head from the ground and looked at the king, who still said not a word.

[030-003]Something in his face however made the wicked servant hope that he

[030-004]would not be punished by death in spite of the great wrong he had done.

[030-005]The king looked very stern, it is true, but not enraged against him. So

[030-006]the servant rose to his feet, and clasping his hands together as he

[030-007]held them up to [[person-prasnajit|Prasnajit]], said in a trembling voice: “I will fetch the

[030-008]treasure, I will fetch the treasure.” “Go then at once,” said the king,

[030-009]“and bring it here”: and as he said it, there was a beautiful

[030-010]expression in his eyes, which made the thief more sorry for what he had

[030-011]done than he would have been if Prasnajit had said, “Off with his

[030-012]head!” or had ordered him to be beaten.
