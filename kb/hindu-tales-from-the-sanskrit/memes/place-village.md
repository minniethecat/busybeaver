# Village

## Description

The village appears in the Hindu Tales as a modest rural settlement, the kind of place from which characters originate before their stories carry them into larger worlds. Hari-Sarman *"lived in a little village"* [076-001] — the adjective "little" distinguishing it from the cities and towns that dominate the tales' geography.

The village is characterised by communal mutual aid: it is noted that if the *"poor people in the village had not helped them, they would"* [076-009] have suffered greatly. This brief reference reveals the village as a community bound by reciprocal obligation, where neighbours support one another through hardship — a value system distinct from the transactional relationships of the city and the court.
