[053-001][[person-rupa-sikha|Rupa-Sikha]] did not take long to decide what was best for her to do. She

[053-002]said to the prince, “I will give you back your golden arrow, and you

[053-003]must make all possible haste out of our country before my father

[053-004]discovers you are here.”

[053-005]“No! no! no! a thousand times no!” cried the prince. “Now I have once

[053-006]seen you, I can never, never leave you. Can you not learn to love me

[053-007]and be my wife?” Then he fell prostrate at her feet, and looked up into

[053-008]her face so lovingly that she could not resist him. She bent down

[053-009]towards him, and the next moment they were clasped in each other’s

[053-010]arms, quite forgetting all the dangers that threatened them. Rupa-Sikha

[053-011]was the first to remember her father, and drawing herself away from her

[053-012]lover, she said to him:

[053-013]“Listen to me, and I will tell you what we must do. My father is a

[053-014][[concept-magic|magician]], it is true, but I am his daughter, and I inherit some of his

[053-015]powers. If only you will promise to do exactly as I tell you, I think I

[053-016]may be able to save you, and perhaps even become your wife. I am the

[053-017]youngest of a large family and my father’s favourite. I will go and

[053-018]tell him that a great and mighty prince, hearing of his wonderful

[053-019]gifts, has come to our land to ask for an interview with him. Then I

[053-020]will tell him that I have seen you, fallen in love with you, and want

[053-021]to marry you. He will be flattered to think his fame has spread so far,

[053-022]and will want to see you, even if he refuses to let me be your wife. I

[053-023]will lead you to his presence and leave you with him alone. If you

[053-024]really love me, you will find the way to win his consent; but you must

[053-025]keep out of his sight till I have prepared the way for you. Come with

[053-026]me now, and I will show you a hiding-place.”

[053-027]Rupa-Sikha then led the prince far away into the [[place-forest|depths of the forest]],

[053-028]and showed him a large tree, the wide-spreading branches of which

[053-029]touched the ground, completely hiding the trunk, in which there was an

[053-030]opening large enough for a man to pass through. Steps cut in the inside

[053-031]of the trunk led down to a wide space underground; and there the

[053-032]magician’s daughter told her lover to wait for her return. “Before I

[053-033]go,” she said, “I will tell you my own password, which will save you

[053-034]from death if you should be discovered. It is LOTUS FLOWER; and

[053-035]everyone to whom you say it, will know that you are under my

[053-036]protection.”

[053-037]When Rupa-Sikha reached the palace she found her father in a very bad

[053-038]humour, because she had not been to ask how the wound in his breast was

[053-039]getting on. She did her best to make up for her neglect; and when she

[053-040]had dressed the wound very carefully, she prepared a dainty meal for

[053-041]her father with her own hands, waiting upon him herself whilst he ate

[053-042]it. All this pleased him, and he was in quite an amiable mood when she

[053-043]said to him:

[053-044]“Now I must tell you that I too have had an adventure. As I was

[053-045]gathering herbs in the forest, I met a man I had never seen before, a

[053-046]tall handsome young fellow looking like a prince, who told me he was

[053-047]seeking the palace of a great and wonderful magician, of whose

[053-048]marvellous deeds he had heard. Who could that magician have been but

[053-049]you, my father?” She added, “I told him I was your daughter, and he

[053-050]entreated me to ask you to grant him an interview.”

[053-051][[person-agni-sikha|Agni-Sikha]] listened to all this without answering a word. He was

[053-052]pleased at this fresh proof that his fame had spread far and wide; but

[053-053]he guessed at once that Rupa-Sikha had not told him the whole truth. He

[053-054]waited for her to go on, and as she said no more, he suddenly turned

[053-055]angrily upon her and in a loud voice asked her:

[053-056]“And what did my daughter answer?”

[053-057]Then Rupa-Sikha knew that her secret had been discovered. And rising to

[053-058]her full height, she answered proudly, “I told him I would seek you and

[053-059]ask you to receive him. And now I will tell you, my father, that I have

[053-060]seen the only man I will ever marry; and if you forbid me to do so, I

[053-061]will take my own life, for I cannot live without him.”

[053-062]“Send for the man immediately,” cried the magician, “and you shall hear

[053-063]my answer when he appears before me.”

[053-064]“I cannot send,” replied Rupa-Sikha, “for none knows where I have left

[053-065]him; nor will I fetch him till you promise that no evil shall befall

[053-066]him.”

[053-067]At first Agni-Sikha laughed aloud and declared that he would do no such

[053-068]thing. But his daughter was as obstinate as he was; and finding that he

[053-069]could not get his own way unless he yielded to her, he said crossly:

[053-070]“He shall keep his fine head on his shoulders, and leave the palace

[053-071]alive; but that is all I will say.”

[053-072]“But that is not enough,” said Rupa-Sikha. “Say after me, _Not a hair

[053-073]of his head shall be harmed, and I will treat him as an honoured

[053-074]guest_, or your eyes will never rest on him.”

[053-075]At last the magician promised, thinking to himself that he would find

[053-076]some way of disposing of [[person-sringa-bhuja|Sringa-Bhuja]], if he did not fancy him for a

[053-077]son-in-law. The words she wanted to hear were hardly out of her

[053-078]father’s mouth before Rupa-Sikha sped away, as if on the wings of the

[053-079]wind, full of hope that all would be well. She found her lover

[053-080]anxiously awaiting her, and quickly explained how matters stood. “You

[053-081]had better say nothing about me to my father at first,” she said; “but

[053-082]only talk about him and all you have heard of him. If only you could

[053-083]get him to like you and want to keep you with him, it would help us

[053-084]very much. Then you could pretend that you must go back to your own

[053-085]land; and rather than allow you to do so, he will be anxious for us to

[053-086]be married and to live here with him.”
