[049-001]In the city of [[place-city|Vardhamana]] in [[place-india|India]] there lived a powerful king named

[049-002][[person-vira-bhuja|Vira-Bhuja]], who, as was the custom in his native land, had many wives,

[049-003]each of whom had several sons. Of all his wives this king loved best

[049-004]the one named [[person-guna-vara|Guna-Vara]], and of all his sons her youngest-born, called

[049-005][[person-sringa-bhuja|Sringa-Bhuja]], was his favourite. Guna-Vara was not only very beautiful

[049-006]but very good. She was so patient that nothing could make her angry, so

[049-007][[concept-selfishness|unselfish]] that she always thought of others before herself, and so [[concept-wisdom|wise]]

[049-008]that she was able to understand how others were feeling, however

[049-009]different their natures were from her own.

[049-010]Sringa-Bhuja, the son of Guna-Vara, resembled his mother in her beauty

[049-011]and her unselfishness; he was also very strong and very [[concept-cleverness|clever]], whilst

[049-012]his brothers were quite unlike him. They wanted to have everything

[049-013]their own way, and they were very [[concept-jealousy|jealous]] indeed of their father’s love

[049-014]for him. They were always trying to do him harm, and though they often

[049-015]quarrelled amongst themselves, they would band together to try and hurt

[049-016]him.

[049-017]It was very much the same with the king’s wives. They hated Guna-Vara,

[049-018]because their husband loved her more than he did them, and they

[049-019]constantly came to him with stories they had made up of the wicked

[049-020]things she had done. Amongst other things they told the king that

[049-021]Guna-Vara did not really love him but cared more for some one else than

[049-022]she did for him. The most bitter of all against her was the wife called

[049-023]Ayasolekha, who was cunning enough to know what sort of tale the king

[049-024]was likely to believe. The very fact that Vira-Bhuja loved Guna-Vara so

[049-025]deeply made him more ready to think that perhaps after all she did not

[049-026]return his affection, and he longed to find out the truth. So he in his

[049-027]turn made up a story, thinking by its means to find out how she felt

[049-028]for him. He therefore went one day to her private apartments, and

[049-029]having sent all her attendants away, he told her he had some very sad

[049-030]news for her which he had heard from his chief astrologer. Astrologers,

[049-031]you know, are wise men, who are supposed to be able to read the secrets

[049-032]of the stars, and learn from them things which are hidden from ordinary

[049-033]human beings. Guna-Vara therefore did not doubt that what her husband

[049-034]was about to tell her was true, and she listened eagerly, her heart

[049-035]beating very fast in her fear that some trouble was coming to those she

[049-036]loved.

[049-037]Great indeed was her sorrow and surprise, when Vira-Bhuja went on to

[049-038]say that the astrologer had told him that a terrible misfortune

[049-039]threatened him and his kingdom and the only way to prevent it was to

[049-040]shut Guna-Vara up in [[event-imprisonment|prison]] for the rest of her life. The poor queen

[049-041]could hardly believe that she had heard rightly. She knew she had done

[049-042]no wrong, and could not understand how putting her in prison could help

[049-043]anybody. She was quite sure that her husband loved her, and no words

[049-044]could have expressed her pain at the thought of being sent away from

[049-045]him and her dear son. Yet she made no resistance, not even asking

[049-046]Vira-Bhuja to let her see Sringa-Bhuja again. She just bowed her

[049-047]beautiful head and said: “Be it unto me as my Lord wills. If he wishes

[049-048]my death, I am ready to lay down my life.”

[049-049]This submission made the king feel even more unhappy than before. He

[049-050]longed to take his wife in his arms and tell her he would never let her

[049-051]go; and perhaps if she had looked at him then, he would have seen all

[049-052]her love for him in her eyes, but she remained perfectly still with

[049-053]bowed head, waiting to hear what her fate was to be. Then the thought

[049-054]entered Vira-Bhuja’s mind: “She is afraid to look at me: what

[049-055]Ayasolekha said was true.”
