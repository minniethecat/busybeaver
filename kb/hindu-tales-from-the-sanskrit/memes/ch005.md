[005-001]The next few days passed away like a dream, and at first [[person-subha-datta|Subha Datta]]

[005-002]thought he had never been so [[concept-happiness|happy]] in his life. The fairies often went

[005-003]off together leaving him alone, only coming back to the clearing when

[005-004]they wanted something out of [[object-magic-pitcher|the pitcher]]. The woodcutter got all kinds

[005-005]of things he fancied for himself, but presently he began to wish he had

[005-006]his wife and children with him to share his wonderful meals. He began

[005-007]to miss them terribly, and he missed his work too. It was no good

[005-008]cutting trees down and chopping up wood when all the food was ready

[005-009]cooked. Sometimes he thought he would slip off home when the fairies

[005-010]were away, but when he looked at the pitcher he could not bear the

[005-011]thought of leaving it.
