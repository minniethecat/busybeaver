# Market

## Description

The marketplace is a space of public visibility in the Hindu Tales — a place where characters are seen, goods are exchanged, and the commercial life of the community is concentrated. A character is observed *"strolling quietly about in the market-place"* [047-038], his presence in this public arena making him visible to others in a way that a private dwelling would not.

The market also appears among the civic amenities of a well-ordered settlement, listed alongside temples and schools as part of the infrastructure of civilised life: *"markets in which food"* [048-027] and other goods could be obtained. The marketplace is thus not merely a location but a marker of urban development — a settlement without a market is incomplete.
