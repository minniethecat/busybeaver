[024-001]After roaming about in the [[place-forest|forest]] for a long time, the Brahman went

[024-002]back to the house in [[place-sravasti|Sravasti]] where some kind people had lent him a

[024-003]room, [[concept-happiness|glad]] and proud to have such a holy man, as they thought he was,

[024-004]living under their roof. He felt sure they had had nothing to do with

[024-005]the loss of his treasure, because they had given him many proofs of

[024-006]their goodness and honesty. Soon he was pouring out all his grief to

[024-007]them, and they did all they could to comfort him, telling him that he

[024-008]would very soon have plenty more money and jewels. They let him see

[024-009]however that they thought it was mean of him to hide away his riches,

[024-010]instead of using them to help the poor and suffering; and this added

[024-011]very much to his rage. At last he [[event-disappearance|lost]] all self-control and cried, “It

[024-012]is not worth while for me to live any longer. I will go to some holy

[024-013]place of pilgrimage by the banks of the river, and there I will starve

[024-014]myself to death.”

[024-015]A place of pilgrimage, you know, is one where some great event,

[024-016]generally connected with religion, has taken place, to which pilgrims

[024-017]go to pray in the hope of winning some special favour from God. The

[024-018]word pilgrim means a wanderer, but it has come in course of time to

[024-019]signify any traveller who comes from a distance to some such place.

[024-020]Benares in [[place-india|India]] is a very famous place of pilgrimage, because it is on

[024-021]the [[place-ganges|River Ganges]], which the Hindus worship and love, believing that its

[024-022]waters can wash away their sins. Hundreds and thousands of Hindus go

[024-023]there every year to bathe in it, and many who know that they have not

[024-024]long to live wait on its banks to die, so that after their bodies have

[024-025]been burnt, as is the custom with the Hindus, their ashes may be thrown

[024-026]into the sacred stream.
