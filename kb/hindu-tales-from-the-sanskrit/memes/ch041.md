[041-001]In the end the poor young king decided that he would go right away as

[041-002]his father and uncles had done; and his mind being made up, he became

[041-003]more cheerful and began to think he might meet with some interesting

[041-004]adventures in a new country, where nobody knew anything about him. As

[041-005]soon as it was light, he wandered off into the [[place-forest|forest]], feeling, it is

[041-006]true, very lonely, but at the same time taking a certain pleasure in

[041-007]being entirely his own master; which a king can never really be,

[041-008]because he has to consider so many other people and to keep so many

[041-009]rules.

[041-010]After all [[person-putraka|Putraka]] did not find the forest so very lonely; for he had

[041-011]not gone far in it before his sad thoughts were broken in upon by his

[041-012]coming suddenly to a [[place-clearing|little clearing]], where the trees had been cut down

[041-013]and two strong-looking men were wrestling together. The king watched

[041-014]them for a little while, wondering what they were fighting about. Then

[041-015]he called out, “What are you doing here? What are you quarrelling

[041-016]about?”

[041-017]The men were greatly surprised to hear Putraka’s voice, for they

[041-018]thought that they were quite alone. They stopped fighting for a minute

[041-019]or two, and one of them said: “We are fighting for three very precious

[041-020]things which were left behind him by our father.”

[041-021]“What are those things?” asked Putraka.

[041-022]“A bowl, a stick and a pair of shoes,” was the reply. “Whoever wins the

[041-023]fight will get them all. There they lie on the ground.”

[041-024]“Well, I never!” cried the king, laughing as he looked at the things,

[041-025]which seemed to him worth very little. “I shouldn’t trouble to fight

[041-026]about such trifles, if I were you.”

[041-027]“Trifles!” exclaimed one of the men angrily. “You don’t know what you

[041-028]are talking about. They are worth more than their weight in gold.

[041-029]Whoever gets [[object-magic-bowl|the bowl]] will find plenty of food in it whenever he wants

[041-030]it; the owner of the stick has only to write his wishes on the ground

[041-031]with it and he will get them; and whoever puts on [[object-magic-shoes|the shoes]] can fly

[041-032]through the air in them to any distance.”
