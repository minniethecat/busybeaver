# Jewelled Arrow

## Description

The Jewelled Arrow is a royal heirloom of the Vardhamana dynasty, described as both a weapon and a precious object. Its value is signalled by the epithet *"precious jewelled arrow"* [051-056] — the arrow is not merely functional but adorned with gems that mark its royal provenance. It belongs originally to the king, passed to his son, and becomes the object of a contest that determines the fate of multiple characters.

The arrow's physical description is sparse; the tales are more interested in what it represents than what it looks like. But the persistent association with jewels — *"the jewelled arrow belonging to your father"* [051-012] — establishes it as a marker of legitimate lineage. To hold the arrow is to claim connection to the royal house.

## Magical Properties

Unlike the magic pitcher or magic shoes, the Jewelled Arrow has no explicitly supernatural properties. Its power is symbolic and political rather than magical. It functions as a token of identity: *"holding the jewelled arrow"* [061-008] proves kinship and royal blood. In a narrative world where appearances deceive and identities are mistaken, the arrow's physical reality provides incontrovertible evidence of who someone truly is.

That said, the arrow's role in the archery contest hints at a quasi-magical quality of precision or fate. The contest is described as a test that only the true heir can pass, and the arrow becomes the instrument of that revelation.

## Story Role

The Jewelled Arrow drives the central plot of its namesake story through three pivotal moments. First, it is contested in an archery match where *"he would win the match with the jewelled arrow"* [051-023] — the outcome determines who will marry the princess. Second, the arrow is stolen or mistakenly taken, triggering a crisis: *"the king was filled"* [051-056] with anger at its loss. Third, the arrow's recovery provides the recognition scene that resolves the plot: *"his dear son, holding the jewelled arrow"* [061-008], restoring the rightful heir to his place.

The arrow's trajectory through the story — from heirloom to prize to lost object to proof of identity — traces a complete narrative arc. It is the MacGuffin that propels the plot and the token that resolves it, performing both functions with economy.
