[008-001]A month passed peacefully away in the [[place-forest|depths of the forest]], [[person-subha-datta|Subha Datta]]

[008-002]waiting on the fairies and becoming every day more [[concept-selfishness|selfish]] and bent on

[008-003]enjoying himself. Then he had another dream, in which he saw his wife

[008-004]and children in the old home with plenty of food, and evidently so

[008-005][[concept-happiness|happy]] without him that he felt quite determined to go and show them he

[008-006]was still alive. When he woke he said to the fairies, “I will not stop

[008-007]with you any longer. I have had a good time here, but I am tired of

[008-008]this life away from my own people.”

[008-009]The fairies saw he was really in earnest this time, so they consented

[008-010]to let him go; but they were kind-hearted people and felt they ought to

[008-011]pay him in some way for all he had done for them. They consulted

[008-012]together, and then one of them told him they wished to make him a

[008-013]present before he went away, and they would give him whatever he asked

[008-014]for.

[008-015]15. What do you think it was that made Subha Datta determine to go home

[008-016]when he found his wife and children could do without him?

[008-017]16. What would you have chosen if the fairies had told you you could

[008-018]have anything you liked?
