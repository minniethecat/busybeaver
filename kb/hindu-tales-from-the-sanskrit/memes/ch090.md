[090-001]Although the king said nothing to his wife about what the barber had

[090-002]told him, he could not treat her exactly as he did before he heard it,

[090-003]and she very soon began to wonder what she had done to vex him. The

[090-004]first thing she noticed was that one of the ladies of the court always

[090-005]followed her when she went into the [[place-forest|forest]]. She did not like this;

[090-006]because she so dearly loved to be alone with the wild creatures, and

[090-007]they did not come to her when any one else was near. She told the lady

[090-008]to go away, and she pretended to do so; but she only kept a little

[090-009]further off. And though the queen could no longer see her, she knew she

[090-010]was there, and so did the [[animal-bird|birds]] and [[animal-deer|the deer]]. This went on for a little

[090-011]time; and then [[person-kadali-garbha|Kadali-Garbha]] asked her husband to tell every one that

[090-012]she was not to be disturbed when she went to see her friends in the

[090-013]forest.

[090-014]“I am afraid,” said the king, “that some harm will come to you. There

[090-015]are wild beasts in the depths of the wood who might hurt you. And what

[090-016]should I do if any harm came to my dear one?”

[090-017]Kadali-Garbha was grieved when [[person-dridha-varman|Dridha-Varman]] said this, for she knew it

[090-018]was not true; and she looked at him so sadly that he felt ashamed of

[090-019]having doubted her. All would perhaps have been well even now, if he

[090-020]had told her of the story he had heard about her, because then she

[090-021]could have proved that it was not true. But he did not do that; he only

[090-022]said, “I cannot let you be alone so far from home. Why not be [[concept-contentment|content]]

[090-023]with the lovely [[place-garden|gardens]] all round the palace? If you still wish to go

[090-024]to the woods, I will send one of the game-keepers with you instead of

[090-025]the lady who has been watching you. Then he can protect you if any

[090-026]harmful creature should approach.”

[090-027]“If my lord does not wish me to be alone in the forest,” answered the

[090-028]queen, “I will be content with the gardens. For no birds or animals

[090-029]would come near me if one of their enemies were with me. But,” she

[090-030]added, as her eyes filled with tears, “will not my lord tell me why he

[090-031]no longer trusts his wife, who loves him with all her heart?”

[090-032]The king was very much touched by what Kadali-Garbha said, but still

[090-033]could not make up his mind to tell her the truth. So he only embraced

[090-034]her fondly, and said she was a good little wife to be so ready to [[concept-obedience|obey]]

[090-035]him. The queen went away very sadly, wondering to herself what she

[090-036]could do to prove to her dear lord that she loved him as much as ever.

[090-037]She took care never to go outside the palace gardens, but she longed

[090-038]very much for her old freedom, and began to grow pale and thin.

[090-039]The wicked woman who had tried to do her harm was very much

[090-040]disappointed that she had only succeeded in making her unhappy; so she

[090-041]went again to Asoka-Mala, and promised her more money if only she would

[090-042]think of some plan to get the king to send his wife away. The [[concept-wisdom|wise]]

[090-043]woman considered a long time, and then she said: “You must use the

[090-044]barber again. He goes from house to house, and he must tell the king

[090-045]that the beautiful woman, who used to roam about in the forest

[090-046]collecting herbs, has been seen there again in the dead of the night,

[090-047]when she could be sure no one would find out what she was doing.”

[090-048]Now it so happened that Kadali-Garbha was often unable to sleep because

[090-049]of her grief that the king did not love her so much as he used to do.

[090-050]One night she got so tired of lying awake that she got up very quietly,

[090-051]so as not to disturb her husband, and putting on her sari, she went out

[090-052]into the gardens, hoping that the fresh air might help her to sleep.

[090-053]Presently the king too woke up, and finding that his wife was no longer

[090-054]beside him, he became very uneasy, and was about to go and seek her,

[090-055]when she came back. He asked her where she had been; and she told him

[090-056]exactly what had happened, but she did not explain why she could not

[090-057]sleep.
