# Magic Staff

## Description

The Magic Staff is the most versatile of Putraka's three magical objects. It is introduced alongside the bowl and shoes as *"A bowl, a stick and a pair of shoes"* [041-022], the word "stick" suggesting an unremarkable appearance that belies its powers. Like the bowl, it was originally carried by a hermit who used it as an everyday walking aid: the hermit travelled *"taking his staff and bowl with him"* [043-020].

The staff's primary magical function is invisibility: *"Putraka with the aid of his magic staff made himself invisible"* [045-023]. But it has a secondary power as well — Putraka *"with his staff wrote rapidly on the ground"* [047-059] to communicate, and could make *"strange signs in the air with the staff"* [052-002], suggesting it functions as a writing or signalling instrument beyond its invisibility power.

The staff is compact enough to be held in one hand: Putraka *"had his staff in his hand and his shoes and"* bowl nearby [047-039], and he could choose whether *"to use the staff, or to put on the shoes"* [047-041] — the staff and shoes offer complementary but distinct powers that must be activated separately.

## Magical Properties

The staff's primary enchantment is invisibility — the power to render its wielder unseen. This is activated consciously: Putraka uses it when he needs to observe without being observed. The secondary power of writing signs in the air is less fully explained but appears to allow communication across distances or the manifestation of visible symbols.

Unlike the bowl (passive provision) and the shoes (active transport), the staff occupies a middle ground — it requires deliberate use but rewards its wielder with both concealment and communication, making it the most strategically valuable of the three objects.

## Story Role

The staff enables Putraka's most critical interventions. Its invisibility function allows him to move undetected through hostile spaces, and its writing function enables him to leave messages and proofs of his presence. The king himself acknowledges its power, asking Putraka if he *"could do it with your marvellous staff"* [048-021] — the staff's reputation has spread beyond its owner, marking it as an object of recognised supernatural authority.
