[035-001]The wonderful dream was fulfilled, and the mother and aunts called the

[035-002]boy [[person-putraka|Putraka]]. Every morning they found the gold pieces under his pillow,

[035-003]and they took care of the money for him, so that when he grew up he was

[035-004]the very richest man in the whole country. He had a [[concept-happiness|happy]] childhood and

[035-005]boyhood, his only trouble being that he did not like having never seen

[035-006]his father. His mother told him about the famine before he was born,

[035-007]and how his father and uncles had gone away and never come back. He

[035-008]often said, “When I am a man I will find my father and bring him home

[035-009]again.” He used his money to help others, and one of the best things he

[035-010]did was to irrigate the land; that is to say, he made canals into which

[035-011]water was made to flow in times when there was plenty of rain, so that

[035-012]there was no danger of there being another famine, such as that which

[035-013]had driven his father and uncles away. The country in which he lived

[035-014]became very fruitful; everybody had enough to eat and drink; and

[035-015]Putraka was very much loved, especially by the poor and unhappy. When

[035-016]the king who ruled over the land died, everybody wanted Putraka to take

[035-017]his place, and he was chosen at once.

[035-018]5. Will you describe the kind of man you think Putraka was?

[035-019]6. Do you know of any other country besides [[place-india|India]] in which everything

[035-020]depends on irrigation?
