[063-001]The guards were relieved to find they need not drag the vizier away;

[063-002]for they admired his [[concept-bravery|courage]] and felt sure that the Raja would soon

[063-003]find he could not get on without him. It might go hardly with them if

[063-004]he suffered harm at their hands. So they only closed in about him; and

[063-005]holding himself very upright, [[person-dhairya-sila|Dhairya-Sila]] walked to the tower as if he

[063-006]were quite [[concept-happiness|glad]] to go. In his heart however he knew full well that it

[063-007]would need all his skill to escape with his life.

[063-008]When her husband did not come home at night, [[person-buddhi-mati|Buddhi-Mati]] was very much

[063-009]distressed. She guessed at once that something had gone wrong, and set

[063-010]forth to try and find out what had happened. This was easy enough; for

[063-011]as she crept along, with her veil closely held about her lest she

[063-012]should be recognised, she passed groups of people discussing the

[063-013]terrible fate that had befallen the favourite. She decided that she

[063-014]must wait until midnight, when the streets would be deserted and she

[063-015]could reach the tower unnoticed. It was almost dark when she got there,

[063-016]but in the dim light of the stars she made out the form of him she

[063-017]loved better than herself, leaning over the edge of the railing at the

[063-018]top.

[063-019]“Is my dear lord still alive?” she whispered, “and is there anything I

[063-020]can do to help him?”

[063-021]“You can do everything that is needed to help me,” answered

[063-022]Dhairya-Sila quietly, “if you only [[concept-obedience|obey]] every direction I give you. Do

[063-023]not for one moment suppose that I am in despair. I am more powerful

[063-024]even now than my master, who has but shown his weakness by attempting

[063-025]to harm me. Now listen to me. Come to-morrow night at this very hour,

[063-026]bringing with you the following things: first, a [[object-beetle-thread|beetle]]; secondly,

[063-027]sixty yards of the finest silk thread, as thin as a spider’s web;

[063-028]thirdly, sixty yards of cotton thread, as thin as you can get it, but

[063-029]very strong; fourthly, sixty yards of good stout twine; fifthly, sixty

[063-030]yards of rope, strong enough to carry my weight; and last, but

[063-031]certainly not least, one drop of the purest bees’ honey.”
