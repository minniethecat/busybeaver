[078-001]This was what [[person-hari-sarman|Hari-Sarman]] decided to do. The merchant had a great many

[078-002]very beautiful horses, which lived in splendid stables and were taken

[078-003]the greatest possible care of. Amongst them was a lovely little Arab

[078-004]mare, the special favourite of the bride, who often went to pet it and

[078-005]give it sugar. “I’ll steal that mare and hide it away in the [[place-forest|forest]],”

[078-006]said the wicked man to himself. “Then, when every one is hunting for

[078-007]her, the master will remember the man who can reveal secrets and send

[078-008]for me. Ah! Ah! What a [[concept-cleverness|clever]] fellow I am! All the stablemen and grooms

[078-009]are [[event-feast|feasting]], I know; for I saw them myself when I tried to get hold of

[078-010]my wife. I can climb through a window that is always left open.” It

[078-011]turned out that he was right. He met no one on his way to the stables,

[078-012]which ware quite deserted. He got in easily, opened, the door from

[078-013]inside, and led out the little mare, which made no resistance; she had

[078-014]always been so kindly treated that she was not a bit afraid. He took

[078-015]the beautiful creature far into the depths of the forest, tied her up

[078-016]there, and got safely back to his own room without being seen.

[078-017]Early the next morning the merchant’s daughter, attended by her

[078-018]maidens, went to see her dear little mare, taking with her an extra

[078-019]supply of sugar. What was her distress when she found the stall empty!

[078-020]She guessed at once that a [[event-theft|thief]] had got in during the night, and

[078-021]hurried home to tell her father, who was very, very angry with the

[078-022]stablemen who had deserted their posts, and declared they should all be

[078-023]flogged for it. “But the first thing to do is to get the mare back,” he

[078-024]said; and he ordered messengers to be sent in every direction,

[078-025]promising a big reward to anyone who brought news of the mare.

[078-026][[person-vidya|Vidya]] of course heard all there was to hear, and at once suspected that

[078-027]Hari-Sarman had had something to do with the matter. “I expect he has

[078-028]hidden the mare,” she thought to herself, “and means to get the reward

[078-029]for finding it.” So she asked to see the master of the house, and when

[078-030]leave was granted to her she said to him:

[078-031]“Why do you not send for my husband, the man who can reveal secrets,

[078-032]because of the wonderful power that has been given him of seeing what

[078-033]is hidden from others? Many a time has he surprised me by what he has

[078-034]been able to do.”
