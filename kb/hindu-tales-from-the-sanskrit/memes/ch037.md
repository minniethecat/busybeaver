[037-001]Just what the young king hoped came to pass. Wherever the Brahmans went

[037-002]they talked about the country they came from and the wonderful young

[037-003]king who ruled over it. [[person-putraka|Putraka]]’s father and uncles, who were after all

[037-004]not so very far off, heard the stories about him, and asked the

[037-005]Brahmans many questions. The answers made them very eager to see

[037-006]Putraka, but they did not at first realize that he was closely related

[037-007]to them. Only when they heard the name of his mother did they guess the

[037-008]truth. Putraka’s father knew, when he deserted his wife, that God was

[037-009]going to give her a child soon; which made it even more wicked of him

[037-010]to leave her. Now, however, he forgot all about that, only thinking how

[037-011]he could make as much use as possible of the son who had become a king.

[037-012]He wanted to go back at once alone, but the uncles were not going to

[037-013]allow that. They meant to get all they could out of Putraka too; and

[037-014]the three [[concept-selfishness|selfish]] men, who were now quite old, set off together for the

[037-015]land they had left so long ago.

[037-016]They arrived safely, and made their way to the palace, where they were

[037-017]received, with great rejoicings. None of the wives said a word of

[037-018]reproach to the husbands who had deserted them; and as for Putraka, he

[037-019]was so overjoyed at having his father back, that he gave him a

[037-020]beautiful house to live in and a great deal of money. He was very good

[037-021]to his uncles too, and felt that he had now really nothing left to wish

[037-022]for.
