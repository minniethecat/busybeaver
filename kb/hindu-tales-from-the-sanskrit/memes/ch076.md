[076-001]A certain man, named [[person-hari-sarman|Hari-Sarman]], who lived in a [[place-village|little village]] in

[076-002][[place-india|India]], where there were no rich people and everyone had to work hard to

[076-003]get his daily bread, got very weary of the life he had to lead. He had

[076-004]a wife whose name was [[person-vidya|Vidya]], and a large family; and even if he had

[076-005]been very industrious it would have been difficult for him to get

[076-006]enough food for them all. Unfortunately he was not a bit industrious,

[076-007]but very lazy, and so was his wife. Neither of them made any attempt to

[076-008]teach their boys and girls to earn their own living; and if the other

[076-009]poor people in the village had not helped them, they would have

[076-010]starved. Hari-Sarman used to send his children out in different

[076-011]directions to beg or steal, whilst he and Vidya stayed at home doing

[076-012]nothing.

[076-013]One day he said to his wife: “Let us leave this stupid place, and go to

[076-014]some big city where we can pick up a living of some kind. I will

[076-015]pretend to be a [[concept-wisdom|wise]] man, able to find out secrets; and you can say

[076-016]that you know all about children, having had so many of your own.”

[076-017]Vidya gladly agreed to this, and the whole party set out, carrying the

[076-018]few possessions they had with them. In course of time they came to a

[076-019]big [[place-city|town]], and Hari-Sarman went [[concept-bravery|boldly]] to the chief house in it, leaving

[076-020]his wife and children outside. He asked to see the master, and was

[076-021]taken into his presence. This master was a very rich merchant, owning

[076-022]large estates in the country; but he cannot have been very [[concept-cleverness|clever]], for

[076-023]he was at once quite taken in by the story Hari-Sarman told him. He

[076-024]said that he would find work for him and his wife, and that the

[076-025]children could be sent to a farm he had in the country, where they

[076-026]could be made very useful.

[076-027]Overjoyed at this, Hari-Sarman hastened out to tell his wife the good

[076-028]news; and the two were at once received into the grand residence, in

[076-029]which a small room was given to them for their own, whilst the children

[076-030]were taken away to the farm, fall of eager delight at the change from

[076-031]the wretched life they had been leading.
