## Definition

The Moral Law (*Sittengesetz*) in Kant's *Critique of Judgment* is the objective, unconditionally binding principle of practical reason. While fully developed in the *Critique of Practical Reason*, the Moral Law plays a crucial bridging role in the third Critique: it is the ground of the [[Moral Feeling]] presupposed by the judgement on the [[The Sublime|Sublime]], and it provides the content for the [[Final Purpose]] that teleological judgment ascribes to nature as a whole.

## Kant's Account

In the *Critique of Judgment*, Kant invokes the Moral Law not as the primary subject of investigation but as an already-established principle that anchors several key arguments.

First, in the "Analytic of the Sublime," the Moral Law provides the objective ground for the subjective moral feeling. The feeling of the Sublime "is the consequence of the practical determination of the will by the Moral Law" (037-002). The Sublime's claim to universal communicability depends on the presupposition that all rational beings possess a susceptibility to the Moral Law.

Second, in the "Critique of the Teleological Judgment," the Moral Law serves as the basis for the [[Final Purpose]] of creation. Kant argues that only a being capable of moral action — that is, a being subject to the Moral Law — can be the ultimate end of nature. This establishes the transition from physical teleology to moral teleology.

Third, in the "Methodology of the Teleological Judgment" (§§ 79–91), Kant uses the Moral Law as the foundation for what he calls "Ethicotheology" — the argument that the existence of the Moral Law within us warrants a belief in a moral author of the world.

## Key Passages

> The need to assume the Moral Law as the guiding principle of conduct is borne in upon us by the Moral Law. (P01-020, P01-064)

> The intellectual satisfaction is the Moral Law in that might which it exercises over all and every motive of the mind antecedent to it. (029-032)

> The pleasure (in the moral feeling) is the consequence of the practical determination of the will by the Moral Law. (037-002)
