# Rive Droite Confrontation

A violent nighttime confrontation in a quiet district of the Right Bank of [[paris|Paris]] where [[the-pack|the Pack]]'s agents finally corner [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]], forcing him to abandon his last safe haven in the city.

## Overview

Chapter XIV ("Rive Droite") depicts the moment when [[the-pack|the Pack]]'s pursuit finally breaches [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s defenses. The shock of an alarm — "falling without presage upon the slumberous hush enveloping the little house marooned in that dead backwater of [[paris|Paris]]" ([014-001]) — signals the end of his refuge.

The confrontation is notable for its suddenness and the speed with which [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] must react. There is no negotiation, no warning — only the immediate threat of violence and the need for escape.

## Strategic Context

The Rive Droite confrontation is significant because it demonstrates that [[the-pack|the Pack]] has thoroughly penetrated [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s defenses. No location in [[paris|Paris]] is safe — not his [[trocadero-apartment|Trocadéro]] apartment, not the [[rue-des-acacias|rue des Acacias]] refuge, and now not this anonymous house on the Right Bank.

This event is a direct consequence of [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s refusal to join [[the-pack|the Pack]] at [[labbaye-council|L'Abbaye]]. The attack is not merely punitive but strategic: by eliminating [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]], [[the-pack|the Pack]] removes the only witness to their conspiracy who has both the skill and motive to oppose them.
