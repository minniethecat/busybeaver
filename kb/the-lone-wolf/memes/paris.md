# Paris

The city that shapes every aspect of [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]]'s life — his upbringing, his criminal career, his social ambitions, and his struggle for redemption — serving as both sanctuary and hunting ground throughout the novel.

## Overview

Paris in [[the-lone-wolf|The Lone Wolf]] is not a tourist's city of monuments and museums but a labyrinth of side-streets, obscure restaurants, and shadowy courtyards where a man might lose himself — or be found. It is the city where [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] arrived as a child, learned the arts of theft and gentility, and built the dual identity that would define his life. (001-001) (001-002)

## Significance in the Story

Paris functions as both stage and character. Its geography — the winding streets backing up from the Seine to the Luxembourg, the boulevards of St. Germain and St. Michel, the railway stations where detectives watch for fugitives — maps directly onto [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s internal landscape of concealment and exposure. (002-027)

The city is also the arena where [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s two worlds collide: the fashionable dining rooms of the [[rive-droite-confrontation|Rive Droite]] where society ladies gossip about [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]], and the hidden corners where the [[the-underworld|underworld]] conducts its business. Paris contains both, and [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] must navigate between them without being caught in either. (003-078)

## Key Locations

- [[troyons]] — the restaurant and hotel on the Left Bank that was [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s home and base of operations
- **[[rive-droite-confrontation|Rive Droite]]** — the Right Bank, home to the wealthy elite whose jewels [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] targets
- **[[gare-du-nord|Gare du Nord]]** — the railway station where [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] and French police watch for [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]] (002-008)
- **The Boulevards** — the grand avenues where [[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]] "flânnes" elegantly and [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] passes in [[disguise]] (018-015)
