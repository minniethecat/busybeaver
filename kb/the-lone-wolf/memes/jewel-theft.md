# Jewel Theft

The specialized criminal activity that defines [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]]'s career: the stealth burglary of private residences to steal jewellery and portable valuables, which are then fenced through a clandestine network of receivers.

## Overview

Jewel theft, as practised by [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]], is an art form demanding equal measures of technical skill, social intelligence, and nerves of steel. [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] confines his depredations to jewels and similar portable valuables — items easily converted to cash and readily transported across borders. His targets are exclusively the wealthy elite: those whose social circles he infiltrates as a gentleman, gathering intelligence on their possessions before striking. (003-053)

## Significance in the Story

The economics of jewel theft drive the novel's plot. [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s Amsterdam receiver provides his primary channel for converting stolen gems into currency (007-019) (007-023). When pressure forces the Amsterdam firm to discontinue their relationship, [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]]'s entire operational model collapses — forcing him to consider the [[the-underworld|underworld]]'s offer of an alternative fencing network. (007-043)

The Omber jewels serve as the novel's MacGuffin: their theft sets [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] in motion, and their eventual disposition becomes entangled with [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s struggle for freedom from both the law and the [[the-underworld|underworld]]. (013-106)
