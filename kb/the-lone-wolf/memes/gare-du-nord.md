# Gare du Nord

The major [[paris|Paris]] railway station that serves as a pivotal location in the narrative of Louis Joseph Vance's 1914 novel *[[the-lone-wolf|The Lone Wolf]]*, functioning as both an escape route and the site of a crucial missed connection.

## Overview

The Gare du Nord, one of [[paris|Paris]]'s principal railway terminals, appears at critical junctures in the novel. It is where [[the-pack|the Pack]]'s agents first attempt to track [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] upon his [[lanyards-return|return to Paris]] ([002-027]), and later becomes the destination of his desperate flight from [[the-pack|the Pack]]'s machinations ([011-045]).

## Role in the Story

The station serves a dual narrative function. Early in the novel, it is where [[the-pack|the Pack]] lies in wait for [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s arrival, marking their first attempt to surveil and control him ([002-027]). Later, it becomes [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s intended escape route — though the plan is complicated when [[lucy-shannon|Lucy Shannon]] is waiting there for her own train ([013-074]–[013-077]).

The station thus embodies the theme of pursuit and escape that runs throughout the novel. Every arrival and departure through its platforms carries the risk of discovery.

## Narrative Function

The Gare du Nord represents the permeable boundary between [[paris|Paris]] and the wider world — a threshold that [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] must cross to escape but which is always watched by his enemies. Its repeated mention in the text underscores the claustrophobic nature of [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s situation: even the means of escape is a trap.
