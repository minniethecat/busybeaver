# Port Aviation

The military aerodrome near [[paris|Paris]] that serves as the launching point for the daring Channel-crossing flight in Louis Joseph Vance's 1914 novel *[[the-lone-wolf|The Lone Wolf]]*.

## Overview

Port Aviation is introduced in Chapter XXV as the barracks where [[captain-vauquelin|Captain Vauquelin]] prepares for his nonstop [[paris|Paris]]-to-[[london|London]] flight. [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] arrives here "about half-past six" in the morning, "waddling quaintly in the heavy wind-resistant clothing" provided for the flight ([025-045]).

The aerodrome represents the threshold between [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s old life and his new one. On the ground, he is still the hunted [[the-lone-wolf|Lone Wolf]]. In the air, he becomes something else — a man in transit between identities.

## The Setting

The aerodrome is described in the cold light of early morning: "in their virgin light the aeroplane was transformed into a thing of ethereal beauty" ([025-045]). The military character of the installation underscores the gravity of [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s situation: he is no longer evading mere criminals but operating in a world of national security and military hardware.
