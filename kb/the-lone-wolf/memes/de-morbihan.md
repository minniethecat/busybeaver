# De Morbihan

A French aristocrat whose polished charm and social connections mask his role as a key figure in the international criminal [[the-underworld|underworld]], serving as both tempter and antagonist to [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]].

## Overview

Count De Morbihan moves effortlessly through the highest circles of [[social-class|Parisian society]] — dining at the best restaurants, charming American heiresses, and trading witticisms with the elite. Behind this facade, he is an operative of the "[[the-pack|International Underworld]] Unlimited," tasked with identifying and recruiting talented criminals. His pursuit of [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]] becomes one of the novel's central conflicts. (003-022) (003-028)

## Background

Little is revealed of De Morbihan's origins, fitting for a man whose entire existence is a performance. He claims cousinship with half the aristocracy of Europe and possesses the manners, wardrobe, and easy arrogance of old money — yet his wealth seems to flow from sources no one can quite identify. His reputation with women is said to be "infallibly fatal," and not a few men imitate his style. (003-022)

## Role in the Story

De Morbihan serves as the novel's bridge between two worlds: the glittering surface of [[paris|Paris]]ian high society and the shadowy depths of the criminal [[the-underworld|underworld]]. At a dinner party in the novel's early chapters, he explains [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]] legend to his American guests — all while knowing far more than he reveals. (003-051) (003-065)

His genius lies in deduction: he reasons out [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]]'s identity through logical elimination, recognizing in [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] the same polished surface that he himself presents to the world. When his recruitment attempt fails, De Morbihan becomes a persistent threat — not through violence, but through the social and criminal networks he commands. (003-067) (018-015)
