# Antwerp

The Belgian port city that functions as the nerve centre of the international criminal [[the-underworld|underworld]]'s communications network — a place from which coded telegrams direct operations and where agents embark for America.

## Overview

Antwerp appears in the novel not as a physical setting but as a node in the [[the-underworld|underworld]]'s communications network. It is the city "where the ships sail from" — a port of embarkation for criminals fleeing Europe, contrasted with Amsterdam "where the diamonds flock together." Coded telegrams from Antwerp carry news of arrests, deaths, and operational directives across the [[the-underworld|underworld]]'s European network. (019-054) (019-055)

## Significance in the Story

Antwerp represents the hidden infrastructure of international crime — the telegraph lines, coded addresses, and shadowy factors that enable the [[the-underworld|underworld]] to operate across borders. When [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] learns of "Q-2," the [[the-underworld|underworld]]'s chief factor in Antwerp, he glimpses the scale of the organization he is defying. (019-157) (019-159)

The Antwerp telegrams serve as a narrative device, delivering crucial information — arrests, suicides, movements of agents — that shapes [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s understanding of the forces arrayed against him. (019-053) (019-154)

## Key Events

- **[[greggs|Greggs]]'s arrest and suicide**: A coded telegram from Antwerp reports the apprehension and death of an [[the-underworld|underworld]] agent attempting to board a steamer for America (019-157)
- **Q-2 revealed**: [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] learns the identity of the [[the-underworld|underworld]]'s Antwerp factor (019-159)
