# The Sûreté

The **Sûreté** (formally **Sûreté Générale**) is the French national criminal police force that appears throughout *The Lone Wolf* as both an antagonist and, indirectly, an ally to [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]].

## Role in the Novel

The Sûreté is the force tasked with capturing [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] — the notorious Lone Wolf whose series of audacious jewel thefts has baffled the police of multiple nations. Yet the Sûreté's relationship with [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] is complicated by the presence of [[wertheimer|Wertheimer]], who works as an informant for [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] while simultaneously running a blackmail operation within [[the-pack|the Pack]].

## Cooperation with Ducroy

At the novel's climax, [[ducroy|Monsieur Ducroy]] arranges matters "through the Sûreté" (PN 027-169) to secure [[captain-vauquelin|Captain Vauquelin]]'s place in the aviation test at [[port-aviation|Port Aviation]] — unwittingly aiding [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s escape. The Sûreté's cooperation with the Minister of War demonstrates that, in the world of the novel, even police forces can be maneuvered by those who understand how institutions work.

## The Arrests

By the story's end, the Sûreté has arrested [[popinot|Popinot]], sending him to [[la-sante|La Santé prison]]. [[greggs|Greggs]], too, has been taken into custody — arrested "boarding steamer for America" (PN 019-157). The Sûreté thus serves as the instrument of justice that clears the way for [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s escape and transformation.
