# L'Abbaye Council

The council of [[the-pack|the Pack]]'s leadership at the exclusive gambling club L'Abbaye, where [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]] is formally introduced to the criminal syndicate and presented with an ultimatum that defines the novel's central conflict.

## Overview

Chapters VII–VIII of *[[the-lone-wolf|The Lone Wolf]]* depict [[the-pack|the Pack]]'s council at L'Abbaye, a private gambling establishment that serves as the syndicate's meeting place. Here, [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] is brought face to face with the organization's leadership — [[wertheimer|Wertheimer]], [[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]], [[popinot|Popinot]], and their associates — and given a choice that will determine his fate.

The council represents [[the-pack|the Pack]]'s most sophisticated operation: not a crude threat but a formal recruitment, complete with introductions, negotiations, and the trappings of legitimate business ([008-083]–[008-137]).

## The Ultimatum

[[the-pack|The Pack]]'s proposal is straightforward: [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] will steal [[madame-omber|Madame Omber]]'s jewels and the Huysman plans, sharing the proceeds with the organization ([008-011]). In return, he receives [[the-pack|the Pack]]'s protection and a share of its power.

[[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s refusal is equally straightforward — and delivered with contempt. He dismisses [[the-pack|the Pack]] as "rats of Belleville" and "sneak-thieves and blackmailers" unworthy of association ([008-115]).

## Consequences

The council's failure marks the transition of [[the-pack|the Pack]] from reluctant admirers of [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]] to his determined enemies. From this point forward, they pursue him with lethal intent — first through assassination attempts ([010-011]), then through betrayal to the police, and finally through direct confrontation.
