# La Tour Pointue

[[paris|Paris]]ian slang for the Préfecture de Police building — literally "The Pointed Tower" — the central police headquarters and jail on the Île de la Cité that looms in the background of the criminal [[the-underworld|underworld]] navigated by [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]].

## Overview

La Tour Pointue is the seat of [[french-police|French law enforcement]] in [[paris|Paris]], housing both the administrative offices of the Préfecture de Police and a jail where prisoners are held pending trial. To the criminal [[social-class|class]] of [[paris|Paris]] — [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] among them — it represents the ultimate threat: the place where a career in crime inevitably terminates. [[bourke|Bourke]] warned the young [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] that without his guidance, the boy "would long since have matriculated at La Tour Pointue and graduated with the canaille of the Santé" ([001-063]).

## In the Novel

[[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] passes the building on his [[lanyards-return|return to Paris]], observing it from his cab: "Over to the left, on the Quai de Marché Neuf, the façade of the Préfecture frowned portentously — La Tour Pointue, as the [[paris|Paris]]ian loves to term it" ([002-010]). His momentary shudder is the instinctive reaction of a hunted man.

The building reappears in [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s thoughts during moments of danger. When he is trapped and outnumbered at [[impasse-stanislas|Impasse Stanislas]], the prospect of La Tour Pointue sharpens his desperation ([009-018]). For a man who has spent his career evading the police, the building represents not just imprisonment but the dissolution of identity — [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]] reduced to a case number.

## Significance

In the symbolic geography of *[[the-lone-wolf|The Lone Wolf]]*, La Tour Pointue is the counterpoint to [[troyons|Troyon's]]. Where [[troyons|Troyon's]] represents warmth, belonging, and [[madame-troyon|Madame Troyon]]'s unconditional love, La Tour Pointue represents the cold machinery of the state — indifferent, implacable, and final. The novel's tension is sustained by [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s navigation between these two poles.
