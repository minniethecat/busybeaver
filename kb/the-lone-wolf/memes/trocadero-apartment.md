# Trocadéro Apartment

The ground-floor apartment near the Trocadéro in [[paris|Paris]] that serves as [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]]'s secondary residence and first destination upon his return to the city in Louis Joseph Vance's 1914 novel *[[the-lone-wolf|The Lone Wolf]]*.

## Overview

[[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s first destination upon leaving [[troyons|Troyon's]] after his return is "that convenient little *rez-de-chaussée* apartment near the Trocadéro, at the junction of the rue Roget and the [[rue-des-acacias|rue des Acacias]]" ([006-001]). This apartment, unlike his room at [[troyons|Troyon's]], is a secret — unknown to both [[the-pack|the Pack]] and the police.

The apartment's ground-floor location is a deliberate security choice: it allows [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] to come and go without passing through a concierge's scrutiny. The lock has a key which fits both doors, "of which no duplicate existed" ([006-014]).

## Strategic Design

| Feature | Purpose | PN |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Ground floor | Avoid concierge observation | [006-009] |
| Unique key | No duplicate could compromise security | [006-014] |
| Near [[rue-des-acacias|rue des Acacias]] | Quick access to backup safe house | [006-001] |
| Trocadéro district | Respectable neighbourhood, above suspicion | [006-001] |

## Compromise

The Trocadéro apartment is eventually compromised by [[the-pack|the Pack]]. The lock is forced, and [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] discovers evidence of intrusion. This violation is particularly disturbing because the apartment represented his most carefully protected space — its compromise demonstrates [[the-pack|the Pack]]'s thoroughness and resources.
