# Impasse Stanislas Escape

The nighttime escape of [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]] — [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]] — and [[lucy-shannon|Lucy Shannon]] from the [[impasse-stanislas|Impasse Stanislas]] hideout, where agents of [[the-pack|the Pack]] lay in wait outside while the two fugitives slipped away through back streets into the [[paris|Paris]]ian rain.

## Overview

The [[impasse-stanislas|Impasse Stanislas]] served as a temporary refuge for [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] after his earlier flight from [[the-pack|the Pack]]'s murder attempt. A dead-end street in a poorly lighted quarter of [[paris|Paris]], its seclusion made it both a sanctuary and a trap: Pack agents could watch the single entrance with ease ([014-009]).

The escape unfolds across two chapters: Chapter XI ("Flight"), where [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] first brings [[lucy-shannon|Lucy]] to the [[impasse-stanislas|Impasse Stanislas]], and Chapter XIV ("Rive Droit"), where they are forced to abandon it.

## The Hideout

Upon first arriving, [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] directs the driver sharply: "[[impasse-stanislas|Impasse Stanislas]]!" — a sudden decision made under the suspicious gaze of a *sergent de ville* ([011-106]). [[lucy-shannon|Lucy]], unfamiliar with the location, presses him: "what is this [[impasse-stanislas|impasse Stanislas]]?" ([011-110]).

[[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] leads her up "a narrow flight of steps" into "the huge room that was the upper storey" — a sparse, rented space that offers concealment but little comfort ([011-130]).

## Under Watch

By Chapter XIV, the refuge has been compromised. A glance through the hall window reveals the impasse "no more abandoned to the wind and rain, but tenanted visibly by one at least who lounged beneath the lonely lamppost, a shoulder against it: a featureless civilian silhouette with attention fixed to the little house" ([014-009]).

[[the-pack|The Pack]] has found them.

## The Escape

Rather than confront the watcher, [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] executes a covert exit. He steers "a roundabout course through byways to the rue de Sèvres station of the Nord-Sud subway; from which in due course they came to the surface again at the place de la Concorde, walked several blocks, took a taxicab, and in less than half an hour after leaving the [[impasse-stanislas|impasse Stanislas]] were comfortably ensconced in a cabinet particulier" ([014-062]).

The escape succeeds through misdirection and the obscuring rain — the "thick drizzle that settled slowly, lacking little of a fog's opacity" that made the poorly lighted quarter even harder to surveil ([011-023]).

## Significance

The [[impasse-stanislas|Impasse Stanislas]] escape demonstrates [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s tactical instinct: recognizing when a safe house becomes a trap and executing a clean exit without confrontation. It also marks a turning point in his relationship with [[lucy-shannon|Lucy Shannon]] — the shared danger and flight binding them closer even as external threats multiply.
