# Rue des Acacias

A quiet [[paris|Paris]]ian street off which [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]] — [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]] — maintains a secret apartment that serves as his private sanctuary and operational base away from his public residence at [[troyons|Troyon's]].

## Overview

The rue des Acacias apartment represents [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s hidden life. Unlike [[troyons|Troyon's]], which is known to both his friends and enemies, the rue des Acacias address is [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s true refuge — a place where he can be neither [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]] the gentleman nor [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]] the thief, but simply himself ([016-002]–[016-006]).

## Role in the Story

The rue des Acacias apartment features prominently in the novel's middle chapters. After the confrontation at [[labbaye-council|L'Abbaye]] and the events at his [[trocadero-apartment|Trocadéro]] apartment, [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] retreats here to regroup ([016-002]). It is from this location that he plans his counter-moves against [[the-pack|the Pack]].

The street itself is described as "dark and drowsy" ([016-006]), fitting [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s need for anonymity. Its obscurity allows him to move without being observed — a critical advantage when both the police and [[the-pack|the Pack]] are hunting him.
