# Tours

**Tours** is a city in central France, on the Loire River, that plays a crucial but offstage role in *The Lone Wolf*. It is the place where the novel's inciting crime occurred: the mysterious murder of a man named Huysman.

## The Huysman Murder

Before the events of the novel begin, a man named Huysman living in Tours was "mysteriously murdered" (PN 013-108). Huysman, it transpired, was a designer of defensive works — fortifications and military installations — whose plans had immense strategic value. His murder was not a random crime but a calculated act of espionage: his war plans were stolen.

## Significance

The Huysman murder in Tours sets everything in motion. The stolen plans pass through the criminal underworld, eventually reaching [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]. His decision to return them to the French government — rather than sell them — is the moral turning point that separates him from [[the-pack|the Pack]] and sets him on the path to redemption. The delivery of these plans to [[ducroy|Monsieur Ducroy]] at the Ministère de la Guerre is the act that secures [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s escape from France.

Without the murder in Tours, there would be no stolen plans, no bargain with [[ducroy|Ducroy]], and no dramatic escape from [[port-aviation|Port Aviation]]. Tours is the quiet origin point of the novel's entire chain of events.
