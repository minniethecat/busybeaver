# The Pack

An international criminal syndicate — self-styled as "the Pack" ([007-003]) — that operates across Europe, representing the organized, institutionalized face of crime in Louis Joseph Vance's 1914 novel *[[the-lone-wolf|The Lone Wolf]]*.

## Overview

The Pack is a shadowy consortium of criminals — thieves, blackmailers, and confidence men — who have banded together to systematize crime on an international scale. Their reach extends from [[paris|Paris]] to [[london|London]] to [[antwerp|Antwerp]], with communications coordinated through coded telegrams and a network of agents ([007-003]).

They name themselves "the Pack" with deliberate symbolism: they hunt collectively, overwhelm through numbers, and target those who refuse to run with them ([006-025]).

The Pack's governing philosophy is articulated in their ultimatum to [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]: "The Pack sends Greetings and extends its invitation to [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]] to associate himself with this organization" ([006-025]). When [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] refuses, the invitation becomes a threat.

## Methods

The Pack operates through a combination of bribery, blackmail, and violence. Their council meetings at [[labbaye-council|L'Abbaye]] ([008-083]–[008-137]) reveal an organization that mimics legitimate business structures — complete with hierarchies, territories, and profit-sharing — but applied to criminal enterprise.

[[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s contempt for the Pack is genuine and visceral: he sees them as "rats" and "sneak-thieves" unworthy of the professional standards he himself maintains ([008-115]).

## Conflict with the Lone Wolf

The Pack's central objective in the novel is to coerce [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] into their organization, primarily by forcing him to steal [[madame-omber|Madame Omber]]'s jewels and share the proceeds ([008-011]). When he refuses, they attempt to eliminate him — first through assassination ([010-011]), then through betrayal to the police.

This conflict defines the novel's second act and ultimately destroys the Pack's [[paris|Paris]] operations.
