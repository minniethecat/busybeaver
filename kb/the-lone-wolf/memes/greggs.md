# Greggs

Mr. Greggs, [[bannon|Bannon]]'s private secretary and right-hand man in [[the-pack|the Pack]], a minor operative whose physical similarity to [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] leads to a case of mistaken identity, and whose eventual arrest and suicide mark a significant setback for the criminal organization.

## Overview

Greggs is a background figure whose importance to the plot is disproportionate to his page time. He is first mentioned when [[lucy-shannon|Lucy Shannon]] mistakes [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] for him in poor light — they share a similar build and attire. [[bannon|Bannon]]'s efforts to free Greggs from the Santé prison create a window of opportunity for [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] and [[lucy-shannon|Lucy]]. His subsequent arrest and suicide aboard a steamer bound for America signals the tightening net around [[the-pack|the Pack]]. (013-050)

## Background

Greggs serves as [[bannon|Bannon]]'s private secretary, functioning as [[the-pack|the Pack]] leader's right-hand man. He is described as being "about your height and has a suit like the one you wear," with a beard — the distinguishing feature that allowed [[lucy-shannon|Lucy]] to eventually recognize [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] was not Greggs despite the poor light (013-050). His position within the organization is significant enough that [[bannon|Bannon]] devotes considerable resources to securing his release from prison.

## Role in the Story

Greggs's role begins with a case of mistaken identity: [[lucy-shannon|Lucy Shannon]], expecting to meet Greggs at [[troyons|Troyon's]], encounters [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] instead. She confesses, "I thought you were Mr. Greggs" (013-050). This chance misidentification sets in motion the alliance between [[lucy-shannon|Lucy]] and [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] that drives much of the novel's second half.

[[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] exploits Greggs's predicament to his advantage: "now, with any sort of luck, [[bannon|Bannon]] ought to be busy enough, trying to get his precious Mr. Greggs out of the Santé, to give us a chance" (013-055). The respite proves temporary.

Later, [[wertheimer|Wertheimer]] reports that "the illustrious [[bannon|Bannon]]'s" organization has been restructured — [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] has been appointed [[bannon|Bannon]]'s lieutenant "vice Greggs, deposed for bungling" (019-094). But Greggs's fate is darker than mere demotion. An [[the-underworld|underworld]] cable reports: "Greggs arrested today boarding steamer for America after desperate struggle killed himself immediately afterward poison no confession" (019-098).

[[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s involvement in Greggs's downfall does not go unnoticed. [[bannon|Bannon]] holds him responsible "for the arrest and suicide of Greggs" (021-005), adding a personal dimension to [[the-pack|the Pack]]'s vendetta against [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]]. The memory of Greggs's attack lingers: [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] recalls "Greggs' fingers tightening on his windpipe, that week-old night at [[troyons|Troyon's]]" (022-041), a visceral reminder of the violence underlying the criminal world he navigates.
