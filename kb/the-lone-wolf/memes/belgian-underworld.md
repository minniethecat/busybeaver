# Belgian Underworld

The [[antwerp|Antwerp]]-based node of the international criminal network that serves as the communications hub for [[the-pack|the Pack]]'s operations across Europe in Louis Joseph Vance's 1914 novel *[[the-lone-wolf|The Lone Wolf]]*.

## Overview

[[antwerp|Antwerp]] is described as the communications centre of the international [[the-underworld|underworld]] — the city through which coded telegrams coordinate criminal operations spanning from [[paris|Paris]] to [[london|London]] and beyond. A telegram "from [[antwerp|Antwerp]], it read: Underworld" ([019-157]) signifies the reach and organization of the network [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] is fighting.

## Communications Hub

The Belgian [[the-underworld|underworld]]'s primary function in the novel is as a communications relay. The coded messages that pass through [[antwerp|Antwerp]] allow [[the-pack|the Pack]] to coordinate across national borders, track targets, and share intelligence without exposure to any single national police force.

This distributed structure makes the [[the-underworld|underworld]] resilient: take down the [[paris|Paris]] operation, and [[antwerp|Antwerp]] still functions; arrest the [[london|London]] agents, and the network routes around them.
