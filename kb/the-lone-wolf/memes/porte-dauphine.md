# Porte Dauphine

The **Porte Dauphine** is one of the western city gates of [[paris|Paris]], situated at the edge of the [[bois-de-boulogne|Bois de Boulogne]]. In *The Lone Wolf*, it marks the threshold between the city and the wilderness of the park beyond — a boundary [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]] crosses during the most dangerous night of his career.

## The Chase

During the car chase that follows [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s rejection of [[wertheimer|Wertheimer]]'s blackmail, [[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]]'s men drive him inexorably toward the Porte Dauphine. "Only when in full course for Porte Dauphine did he appreciate De Morbihan's design" (PN 027-015). The gate is the point of no return: once through it, [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] is in the open darkness of the Bois, where his pursuers intend to run him down and kill him.

The Porte Dauphine thus serves as a narrative threshold — the passage from the relative safety of the city to the lawless darkness of the park, from which [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] must fight his way free.
