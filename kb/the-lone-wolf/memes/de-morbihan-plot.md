# De Morbihan's Plot

The scheme devised by Count Remy de Morbihan to recruit [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]] into [[the-pack|the Pack]]'s criminal enterprise — a plot that begins with courtly charm and ends with attempted murder.

## Overview

[[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]]'s plot unfolds across the novel's first half, beginning with his carefully staged encounter with [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] at [[troyons|Troyon's]]. He presents himself as a man of the world, an aristocrat with a taste for adventure — someone [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] might naturally find sympathetic. But behind the charm lies a cold calculation: [[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]] needs [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s skills for the Omber job, and he is prepared to use any means necessary to secure them.

## De Morbihan's Method

[[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]] operates through social manipulation rather than crude threats. He is described as having "an extraordinary popularity" in [[paris|Paris]], "due to the equally extraordinary extravagance of his entertainments" ([003-023]). His technique is to make the criminal enterprise seem like an adventure — a game for gentlemen rather than a conspiracy of thieves.

[[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] sees through this almost immediately: "One made up one's mind [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]] must be a certain sort of person — and immediately discovers he's not" ([003-067]). The recognition is mutual; [[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]], for his part, recognizes in [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] a fellow spirit he both admires and needs.
