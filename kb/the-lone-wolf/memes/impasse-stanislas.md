# Impasse Stanislas

A dead-end [[paris|Paris]]ian street — an impasse — where [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]] and [[lucy-shannon|Lucy Shannon]] briefly shelter during their flight from [[the-pack|the Pack]], representing one of the novel's transient sanctuaries that offers only temporary safety.

## Overview

The impasse Stanislas appears during the flight sequence as a place of brief respite. [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] and [[lucy-shannon|Lucy Shannon]] find shelter here after a narrow escape, catching their breath before the pursuit resumes. The location's name — an impasse, a dead end — is thematically significant: every refuge in the novel eventually becomes a trap.

## Symbolic Function

The impasse embodies the novel's recurring pattern of sanctuary-turned-trap. Like [[troyons|Troyon's]], like the [[trocadero-apartment|Trocadéro]] apartment, like the [[rue-des-acacias|rue des Acacias]] hideout, the impasse offers temporary safety at the cost of limited exits. When [[the-pack|the Pack]] inevitably finds them, the very feature that made the impasse a refuge — its obscurity, its limited access — makes escape more difficult.
