# Bannon

An American businessman travelling in [[paris|Paris]] with his ward Lucia Bannon ([[lucy-shannon|Lucy Shannon]]), who serves as a representative of the legitimate, law-abiding world that contrasts with [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]]'s criminal existence in Louis Joseph Vance's 1914 novel *[[the-lone-wolf|The Lone Wolf]]*.

## Overview

Bannon is a successful American businessman — "one of the richest men in the States" ([003-033]) — travelling in Europe with his ward. He has "not seen [[paris|Paris]] in twenty years" ([003-096]) and approaches the city with the innocent enthusiasm of a tourist rather than the knowing cynicism of its inhabitants.

His presence in the novel serves as a reminder of the world [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] cannot access: the world of honest wealth, legitimate business, and moral clarity. Bannon can travel openly, speak freely, and act without concealment — privileges [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] has never known.

## Relationship with Lucy Shannon

Bannon's relationship with [[lucy-shannon|Lucy Shannon]] is protective but distant. He travels with her, provides for her, and exercises a guardian's authority — but he does not understand her. The emotional distance between them makes [[lucy-shannon|Lucy]]'s connection with [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] both possible and necessary.
