# Madame Omber

A wealthy French aristocrat and owner of a famous collection of jewels that becomes the focal point of a criminal conspiracy in Louis Joseph Vance's 1914 novel *[[the-lone-wolf|The Lone Wolf]]*.

## Overview

Madame Hélène Omber is a wealthy and amiable eccentric of [[paris|Paris]]ian high society, described as a souvenir of the Second Empire ([016-007]). Her personal jewellery collection — valued at upwards of a quarter-million francs — attracts the attention of both [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]] ([[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]]) and the criminal syndicate known as [[the-pack|the Pack]], setting in motion the novel's central conflict ([008-011]).

## Role in the Plot

Madame Omber's jewels serve as the catalyst for the novel's main action. [[the-pack|The Pack]] attempts to force [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]] to steal them as part of their criminal enterprise, while [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] himself initially considers the theft before his moral reformation ([007-026], [015-002]).

During the climactic confrontation at her [[paris|Paris]] hôtel, Madame Omber demonstrates remarkable composure, facing armed intruders with calm defiance ([024-001]–[024-008]). When the police finally arrive, she notably does not send for them herself, suggesting a sophisticated understanding of the complex criminal dynamics at play ([024-019]).

## Relationship to Lanyard

Madame Omber is initially a target — [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] considers stealing her jewels, and [[the-pack|the Pack]] attempts to force him to do so ([015-002]). By the novel's climax, however, [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s reformation leads him to protect her rather than rob her, marking a turning point in his moral journey ([013-114], [024-014]).
