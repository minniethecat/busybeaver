# High Society

The aristocratic and wealthy milieu of Belle Époque [[paris|Paris]] that serves as both [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s hunting ground and his camouflage — a world he moves through with ease despite being, by birth, excluded from it.

## Overview

[[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s genius as a criminal lies not merely in his technical skill but in his social mobility. [[bourke|Bourke]]'s tutelage equipped him to move effortlessly through the drawing rooms of [[paris|Paris]]: "he came to know that a self-made millionaire, taken the right way, is as approachable as one whose millions date back even unto the third generation; he could order a dinner at Sherry's as readily as drinks at Sharkey's" ([001-060]). This social fluency is what distinguishes [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]] from a common burglar.

## The Faubourg Saint-Germain

The aristocratic quarter of [[paris|Paris]], centred on the Faubourg Saint-Germain, represents the pinnacle of the society [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] infiltrates. It is here that the Omber jewels — the prize that sets the novel's plot in motion — reside. The district's great houses, with their salons and ballrooms and private collections, are [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s preferred field of operations.

## Society as Camouflage

[[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s ability to pass as a gentleman is his most valuable tool. When he dines at [[labbaye-council|L'Abbaye]] Thélème, gambles at the tables, or converses with Comte Remy de Morbihan, he is not merely enjoying the pleasures of wealth — he is maintaining his cover. High society, with its elaborate codes of dress, speech, and behaviour, provides the perfect mask for a man whose true identity must remain hidden.

## The Double Life

The tension between [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s genuine appreciation for refinement and his criminal necessity animates much of the novel's psychological depth. He is not playing at being a gentleman — in many respects he *is* one, with tastes and sensibilities genuinely cultivated. Yet his presence in this world is predicated on theft. The contradiction is never resolved, only managed.
