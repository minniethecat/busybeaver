# La Santé Prison

**La Santé** (formally **Maison d'arrêt de la Santé**) is the Paris prison repeatedly referenced in *The Lone Wolf* as the destination for captured criminals — a symbol of the justice that awaits those who fail in the underworld.

## In the Novel

La Santé appears as a threat and a reality throughout the narrative. It is where [[greggs|Greggs]] is held — [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s manservant during his brief tenure with [[the-pack|the Pack]], arrested for bungling an assignment. [[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]]'s men spend effort "trying to get his precious Mr. Greggs out of the Santé" (PN 013-078).

After the events of the novel, [[popinot|Popinot]] — one of [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s most persistent enemies — is also sent to La Santé. [[lucy-shannon|Lucy Shannon]] confirms this to [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] at Port Aviation: Popinot "was on his way to the Santé, last I saw of him" (PN 027-169).

## Symbolism

La Santé represents the boundary between the criminal world and the world of law. For the members of [[the-pack|the Pack]], it is the ever-present consequence of failure. [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s ability to avoid its walls — despite being pursued by both criminals and police — marks him as exceptional. His ultimate escape from France, via aeroplane, is an escape from the fate that La Santé represents.
