# Bois de Boulogne

The **Bois de Boulogne** is the vast public park on the western edge of [[paris|Paris]] that serves as the stage for the climactic car chase in *The Lone Wolf*. It is here that [[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]]'s pursuit of [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]] reaches its violent conclusion.

## Location

The Bois lies beyond the [[porte-dauphine|Porte Dauphine]], one of the western gates of Paris. It is reached via the avenue du Bois (now Avenue Foch), a grand tree-lined boulevard radiating from the Arc de Triomphe. In the novel's nighttime setting, the Bois is described as dark and nearly deserted — "the midnight solitudes of the Bois de Boulogne" (PN 027-015).

## The Chase

After [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] storms out of [[wertheimer|Wertheimer]]'s blackmail proposition, [[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]] dispatches a carload of [[the-apaches|Apaches]] to eliminate him. The pursuit begins in the streets of Paris and drives inexorably toward the Bois. [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] realizes [[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]]'s design: "He was to be rushed out into the midnight solitudes of the Bois de Boulogne and there run down and slain" (PN 027-015).

The chase tears through the Porte Dauphine and into the park. [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] passes only one other motorcar — a limousine whose driver shouts something inarticulate. The freedom from traffic is a relief, but the pursuit creeps closer as he swings along the roadway bordering the eastern edge of the lake.

## The Wreck

Unable to outrun his pursuers, [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] executes a desperate maneuver. He leaps from his moving car and vanishes into the thickest part of the woods. [[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]]'s car, unable to stop in time, crashes. [[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]] survives but is "crippled for life, thanks to that affair in the Bois" (PN 027-169) — a permanent reminder of the night's violence.
