# The Huysman Plans

The **Huysman Plans** are the stolen military defense documents that drive the central plot of *The Lone Wolf*. They are the MacGuffin around which the novel's espionage intrigue revolves — the object that passes from hand to hand, triggering the events that transform [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]] from master thief to reluctant patriot.

## Origin

The plans originated with a man named Huysman, a designer of defensive works living in [[tours|Tours]], France. Huysman was "mysteriously murdered" (PN 013-108) — a calculated act of espionage rather than a random crime. His plans, detailing French military fortifications and defensive installations, were stolen and entered the criminal underworld.

## The Plans in the Underworld

By the time the plans come to [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s attention, they have become a commodity to be traded among the members of [[the-pack|the Pack]]. [[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]] and [[ekstrom|Ekstrom]], working as agents of a foreign power (implicitly Germany), seek to acquire the plans for their own purposes.

## Lanyard's Decision

[[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s possession of the plans creates a moral crisis. He could sell them for a fortune. Instead, he chooses to return them to the French government — an act that breaks his allegiance to [[the-pack|the Pack]] and marks his first step toward redemption. He breaks into [[ducroy|Monsieur Ducroy]]'s private residence and presents the photographic prints of the plans to the Minister of War.

## Consequences

Lanyard's return of the plans to [[ducroy|Ducroy]] has three consequences:
1. It makes him a traitor in the eyes of [[the-pack|the Pack]], who attempt to kill him
2. It earns him Ducroy's reluctant cooperation — the aviation test at [[port-aviation|Port Aviation]]
3. It establishes him as something more than a common thief — a man with a code
