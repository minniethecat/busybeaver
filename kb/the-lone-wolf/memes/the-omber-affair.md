# The Omber Affair

The theft of [[madame-omber|Madame Omber]]'s celebrated jewel collection — the crime that triggers [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]]'s pursuit of [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]] and sets the novel's central plot in motion.

## Overview

The Omber affair is the inciting incident of the novel: the theft that transforms [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]] from a legendary figure of dinner-party gossip into the target of an international manhunt. [[madame-omber|Madame Omber]]'s jewels were renowned — "everyone has heard of her wonderful jewels" — and the "real marvel" was that [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]] had neglected so shining a mark for as long as he had. (003-078)

## Significance in the Story

The Omber theft serves multiple narrative functions. It demonstrates [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s skill — the crime is executed with such precision that [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] requires "at least twenty-four hours to unlimber for action." (002-003) It brings the man from [[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] to [[paris|Paris]], putting [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] and his pursuer in the same restaurants, the same railway stations, the same social circles. (002-008)

Most importantly, the affair attracts the attention of [[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]], who uses it as the basis for his deduction of [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]]'s identity. The dinner-party conversation about the Omber jewels — where [[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]] explains [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]] legend to his American guests while [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]] himself sits listening — is the novel's first great dramatic irony. (003-051) (003-065)

## Key Events

- **The theft**: [[madame-omber|Madame Omber]]'s jewels are stolen with characteristic [[the-lone-wolf|Lone Wolf]] precision (implied before the novel opens)
- **[[scotland-yard|Scotland Yard]] responds**: Detectives are dispatched to [[paris|Paris]], establishing surveillance at railway stations and ports (002-003) (002-008)
- **[[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]]'s deduction**: At a [[paris|Paris]]ian dinner party, [[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]] uses the Omber affair to reason out [[michael-lanyard|the Lone Wolf]]'s identity (003-065)
