# Rue Roget Assault

[[the-pack|The Pack]]'s assassination attempt on [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]] at his ground-floor apartment near the [[trocadero-apartment|Trocadéro]], spanning Chapters IX–X of the novel. The assault — by darkness, strangulation, and a hypodermic needle — marks [[the-pack|the Pack]]'s escalation from manipulation to murder.

## The Ambush

Returning to his *rez-de-chaussée* apartment at the junction of the rue Roget and the avenue de l'Alma, [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] enters in darkness. "The door had closed behind him, swiftly but gently, eclipsing the faint light from the hall, leaving what amounted to stark darkness" ([009-096]).

He has no time to react. "So unexpected, so swift and sudden fell the assault, that he was caught completely off guard: between the shutting of the door and an onslaught whose violence sent him reeling to the wall, the elapsed time could have been measured by the fluttering of an eyelash" ([009-099]).

## The Attack

Two powerful arms pinion him. His feet are tripped; he is thrown "with a force that fairly jarred his teeth, half-stunning him" ([009-100]). The attacker climbs on top of him, "bearing down heavily with a knee on either of [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s forearms, hands encircling his neck, murderous thumbs digging into his windpipe" ([009-101]).

The strangulation is methodical. "The pain grew agonizing; [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s breath was almost completely shut off; he gasped vainly, with a rattling noise in his gullet; his eyeballs started; a myriad coruscant lights danced and interlaced blindingly before them" ([009-104]).

## The Drugging

Just as consciousness fades, the attack shifts. The hands leave his throat. One forces his head to the floor; the other produces "a needle driven into the flesh beneath his ear" ([009-106]) — a hypodermic injection meant to render him helpless or dead.

## Turn About

The needle's sting galvanizes [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] "to life again in a twinkling, animate with threefold strength and cunning. The man on his chest was thrown off as by a young earthquake; and [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s right arm was no sooner free than it shot out with blind but deadly accuracy to the point of his assailant's jaw" ([009-107]).

The attacker collapses with "a click of teeth... followed by a sickish grunt" ([009-107]). [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] scrambles to his feet and seizes his pistol, turning the tables completely.

## The Attackers

[[popinot|Popinot]] is identified as the likely orchestrator — "a vindictive animal, that [[popinot|Popinot]]; and the creatures he controlled, a murderous lot, drug-ridden, drink bedevilled, vicious little rats of Belleville, who'd knife a man for the price of an absinthe" ([009-004]). But the actual assailant in the darkness remains faceless — an anonymous instrument of Pack violence.

## Significance

The rue Roget assault marks a turning point in [[the-pack|the Pack]]'s strategy: from manipulation ([[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]]'s invitation) to open murder. It demonstrates that [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s refusal to join the syndicate carries a death sentence. It also showcases his physical resilience and combat instinct — the "threefold strength" that reasserts itself when death is imminent.

The event's second chapter title, "Turn [[About]]," encapsulates its meaning: the hunter becomes the hunted, then reverses the roles again.
