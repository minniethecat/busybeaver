# Popinot

A [[paris|Paris]]ian criminal and enforcer for [[the-pack|the Pack]] who commands [[the-apaches|the Apaches]] — the street gangs of Belleville — and serves as the violent muscle behind the syndicate's campaign against [[michael-lanyard|Michael Lanyard]] in Louis Joseph Vance's 1914 novel *[[the-lone-wolf|The Lone Wolf]]*.

## Overview

Popinot is described as a tough, unrefined operator — a man who handles the physical side of [[the-pack|the Pack]]'s operations. He is the one who deploys [[the-apaches|the Apaches]] to watch the stations and exits of [[paris|Paris]], ensuring that [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] cannot escape by conventional means: "Popinot's [[the-apaches|Apaches]] are patrolling every conceivable exit from [[paris|Paris]]" ([013-076]).

Unlike [[wertheimer|Wertheimer]]'s smooth negotiation or [[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]]'s aristocratic charm, Popinot's approach is direct and violent. He is [[the-pack|the Pack]]'s fist — necessary to their operations but held in contempt by the leadership.

| Function | Description |

## Relationship with Lanyard

[[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]]'s contempt for Popinot is absolute and unmediated. Where he engages with [[wertheimer|Wertheimer]] as a professional rival and with [[de-morbihan|De Morbihan]] as a social equal, he dismisses Popinot as merely a thug. This dismissal is mutual: Popinot would happily see [[michael-lanyard|Lanyard]] dead and collects his payment regardless of whose side he is on.
