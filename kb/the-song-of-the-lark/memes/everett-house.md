# Everett House

## Overview

The Everett House is a hotel on Union Square in New York City that serves as a social hub and meeting place in the later chapters of the novel. Dr. Archie stays here during his New York visits, and it is in the dusty parlor of the Everett House that he and Fred Ottenburg have crucial conversations about Thea Kronborg's artistic trajectory (046-036).

## Location & Geography

The Everett House stands at Union Square in Manhattan. In the novel, the stationery itself bears the printed heading "Everett House, Union Square" (046-036). The hotel's parlor is described as "dusty" (047-002), suggesting a respectable if somewhat faded establishment—suitable for a Western doctor visiting the city but not one of New York's most fashionable addresses.

## Role in the Novel

The Everett House functions as a neutral ground where Dr. Archie and Fred Ottenburg meet to discuss Thea. These conversations, held in the hotel's parlor, advance the plot by revealing Fred's continued investment in Thea's career and Dr. Archie's protective concern. The hotel is a liminal space—neither Thea's world of performance nor the men's private lives—where the business of managing an artist's career takes place.

## Key Scenes

- **Fred's Letter**: Fred Ottenburg writes to Dr. Archie on stationery headed "Everett House, Union Square," inviting him to meet (046-036).
- **Parlor Meeting**: Dr. Archie meets Fred "in the dusty parlor of the Everett House," where Fred's manner reveals his eagerness to discuss Thea (047-002).
- **Awkward Encounter**: In the same parlor, "the doctor had been awkward," suggesting the emotional tension underlying these conversations about Thea's future (049-018).
