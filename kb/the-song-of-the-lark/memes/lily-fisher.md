# Lily Fisher

## Overview

Lily Fisher is Thea Kronborg's first rival—a Baptist girl in Moonstone whose musical pretensions and social standing place her in direct competition with Thea for local recognition. She is "the most stuck-up doll in town," and their rivalry over who will sing at a local event represents Thea's first encounter with the competitive dimension of musical life (008-102).

## Childhood Rivalry

The rivalry between Thea and Lily Fisher is treated with the seriousness it deserves in a child's world. Who will sing at the church event? Whose performance will be judged superior? These questions, trivial in themselves, matter intensely to the children involved—and the novel respects that intensity.

Lily Fisher, "the most stuck-up doll in Moonstone," represents a kind of social advantage Thea lacks. Her father is "a prominent Baptist," giving her a position in the community that the Lutheran minister's daughter cannot quite match. Their rivalry is thus as much social as musical (008-116).

## Significance

Lily Fisher appears only briefly in the novel but serves an important narrative function: she establishes the competitive context of Thea's early musical life and foreshadows the more serious rivalries to come. The girl who must compete for the right to sing in Moonstone will later compete for stages in Chicago, New York, and Europe.
