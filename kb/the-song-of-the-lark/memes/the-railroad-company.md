# The Railroad Company

## Overview

The railroad company that employs Ray Kennedy and defines the economic character of Moonstone—the iron link between the small desert town and the wider world.

## Role in the Novel

This organization plays a significant role in the world of *The Song of the Lark*, contributing to the institutional landscape against which Thea Kronborg's individual artistic journey unfolds. Organizations in the novel range from the humble to the grand, but each serves as a context in which character is formed, tested, or expressed.

## Members & Associated Characters

The organization's members and associates include figures who intersect with Thea Kronborg's life at various points in her development. These institutional connections form part of the social fabric that supports, constrains, and shapes the artist's progress from provincial obscurity to international acclaim.
