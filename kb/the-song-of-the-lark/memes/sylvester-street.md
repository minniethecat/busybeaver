# Sylvester Street

## Overview

Sylvester Street is a residential street in Moonstone, Colorado, described as "the third parallel with Main Street" (005-008). It represents the quieter, more domestic side of the town, where respectable families live in modest houses set back behind neat yards. Tillie Kronborg, Thea's aunt, lives on Sylvester Street, and it is through her that the street's social life is most vividly rendered.

## Location & Geography

Sylvester Street runs parallel to Main Street, three blocks removed from the commercial bustle of Moonstone's primary thoroughfare. The houses on Sylvester Street "nestled modestly behind" their yards, marked by a quiet respectability (005-027). It is part of Moonstone's social geography—"Sylvester Street scarcely knew that this part" of town existed across the social divide (005-032), marking it as a street of established, churchgoing families who keep to their own.

## Role in the Novel

Sylvester Street functions as a marker of Moonstone's social stratification. It is Tillie Kronborg's domain, the place where she gathers and disseminates the town's gossip. In the Epilogue, Aunt Tillie, now an old woman, still lives on Sylvester Street, still telling her stories—though "when the people on Sylvester Street tire of Tillie's stories, she" simply turns to a new audience (062-176). The street thus bookends the novel, appearing early as a detail of Moonstone's geography and late as the setting of Tillie's enduring life.

## Key Scenes

- **Social Geography**: The narrator situates Sylvester Street as "the third parallel with Main Street," establishing Moonstone's grid and its social hierarchy in a single detail (005-008).
- **Respectable Domesticity**: The houses on Sylvester Street "nestled modestly behind" their front yards, an image of the street's quiet, unassuming character (005-027).
- **Tillie's Enduring Presence**: In the Epilogue, Aunt Tillie still lives on Sylvester Street, still telling her stories—a note of continuity that closes the novel (062-176).
