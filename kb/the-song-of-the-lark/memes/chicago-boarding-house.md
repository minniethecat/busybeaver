# The Chicago Boarding House

## Overview

The modest boarding house where Thea Kronborg lives during her Chicago student years—a space of privation and independence that marks her first extended experience of life away from family.

## Location & Geography

Illinois is an important setting in the geography of Thea Kronborg's life. Each location in *The Song of the Lark* serves both narrative and symbolic functions, marking stages in the protagonist's journey from provincial origins to international artistic acclaim.

## Role in the Novel

This place represents one of the thresholds Thea must cross—geographical, psychological, or professional—in her development as an artist. Cather's locations are never mere backdrop; they embody the tensions, possibilities, and costs of the artistic life.
