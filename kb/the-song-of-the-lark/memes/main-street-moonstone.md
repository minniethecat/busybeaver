## Overview

Main Street is the central commercial artery of Moonstone, Colorado, running north–south through the heart of the small railroad town. It serves as both the economic hub and an informal social boundary: to the west live the town's "society" families along Sylvester Street, while to the east live "all the humbler citizens, the people who voted but did not run for office" [005-023]. The street is the setting for the novel's opening scene, establishing Moonstone's character as a tight-knit, hierarchical frontier community on the high plains.

## Location & Geography

Main Street runs through the center of Moonstone. The town's businesses — a drug store, a saloon, merchants' shops — line its course. To the west, Sylvester Street runs parallel as the third street over, hosting "the best dwellings" including Dr. Archie's house at the far north end, nearly a mile from the court-house and its cottonwood grove [005-008]. To the east, the land slopes toward a deep ravine that winds south toward Mexican Town. The street's board sidewalks are flanked by "breastworks" of shoveled snow in winter, and the town's few lights — including the drug store's — mark its length after dark [001-062].

## Role in the Novel

Main Street appears throughout Part I as the organizing axis of Moonstone life. The novel opens with Dr. Archie and the town's men gathering there on a cold night after a medical emergency, establishing the street as the town's civic stage. It is the site of commercial bustle on Saturdays, when "the merchants on Main Street" do their busiest trade [018-006], and its emptiness on Sundays underscores the Kronborg family's religious rhythms. The street's role as social divider is crucial: the Kronborgs live on the west side (respectable), while the Mexican settlement and humbler citizens occupy the east side, "toward the deep ravine" [005-023].

## Key Scenes

- **Opening scene** [001-062]: After the sick child crisis, Dr. Archie and the townsmen emerge from the drug store into the dark street. "Every other light on Main Street was out," and the town looks "small and black" under the winter night — the novel's first image of Moonstone as a fragile outpost on the plains.

- **The social geography of Moonstone** [005-008]: The narrator maps the town's social layout: Main Street at the center, "society" to the west along Sylvester Street (the "third parallel with Main Street"), humbler folk to the east toward the ravine where Mexican Town lies [005-023].

- **Saturday commerce** [018-006]: The narrator notes that for the Kronborg family, Sunday is "the hard day of the week," just as "Saturday was the busy day with the merchants on Main Street" — drawing an implicit parallel between religious devotion and commercial labor.

- **Thea's aimless walk** [031-162]: After a painful emotional experience, Thea walks "down through the empty main street," all stores closed and blinds drawn, the silence amplifying her inner turmoil.
