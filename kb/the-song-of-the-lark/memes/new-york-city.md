# New York City

## Overview

New York City represents the summit of Thea Kronborg's professional ascent—the home of the Metropolitan Opera and the center of American musical culture. Her arrival in New York marks the fulfillment of the artistic destiny that began at an upright piano in a Colorado parsonage, and the city's scale and sophistication provide the appropriate setting for her mature artistry.

## The Metropolitan Opera

The Metropolitan Opera House, where Thea eventually performs, represents the institutional pinnacle of American musical life. To sing at the Met is to have arrived—to be recognized not merely as talented but as belonging to the highest rank of international performers. Thea's Metropolitan debut is the culmination of years of sacrifice and training.

## Artistic Center

New York's concentration of wealth, patronage, and cultural institutions makes it the natural destination for an artist of Thea's caliber. Here she finds audiences sophisticated enough to appreciate Wagnerian opera, patrons wealthy enough to support it, and a musical community competitive enough to push her to her highest level.
