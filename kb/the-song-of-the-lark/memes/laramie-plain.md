## Overview

Laramie Plain is a frontier region in Wyoming, near the town of Laramie, associated with Dr. Howard Archie's early life and career before he settled in Moonstone, Colorado. The plain is described through the memories of old railroad men and frontiersmen, evoking the era of westward expansion in the 1850s and 1860s, when wagon-trails crossed the high country and prospectors sought fortunes in the hills.

## Location & Geography

The Laramie Plain lies in the hills of southeastern Wyoming, near the settlement of Laramie — a key stop on the transcontinental railroad. The region is characterized by high plains, sage-covered hills, and the worn traces of old wagon-trails left by 49ers heading west to California [007-215]. It sits at the edge of the Rocky Mountain front, a transitional landscape between the Great Plains and the mountain ranges of Colorado.

## Role in the Novel

Laramie Plain appears in the novel primarily through the recollections of Dr. Archie and his acquaintances. It represents the raw frontier past — a time of hardship, adventure, and physical freedom that contrasts with the settled domesticity of Moonstone. The plain is invoked during a conversation between Spanish Johnny and Ray Kennedy as they reminisce about the old days of prospecting and railroad-building [007-212]. Later, Thea recalls these stories when thinking about Dr. Archie's background during her time in Chicago [025-162].

The region serves as part of Archie's psychological geography: a memory of his youth on the frontier that helps define his character as self-reliant, physically capable, and fundamentally shaped by the West.

## Key Scenes

- **Ray Kennedy's reminiscence** [007-212]: Johnny Tellamantez and Ray Kennedy discuss the old frontier days in Wyoming, near Laramie, where wagon-trails of the 49ers still mark the landscape. Ray recounts stories of prospectors and close calls with fortune — a "large Western dream of easy money" [007-215].

- **The wagon-trails of the 1850s** [007-221]: The narrative describes how parties set out from Laramie before daylight, "behind a string of oxen," crossing the high plain toward the California goldfields.

- **Thea's Chicago recollection** [025-162]: During her time in Chicago, Thea remembers Dr. Archie's stories about "Laramie; the grass-grown wagon trails" — a memory that connects her to the frontier past and to the doctor who shaped her early life.
