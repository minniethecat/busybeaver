# Cottonwood Grove

## Overview

The cottonwood grove is a modest but persistent landscape feature in Moonstone, Colorado, appearing at key moments throughout Thea Kronborg's childhood and adolescence. Located near Dr. Archie's house (005-011), it serves as a landmark of Thea's daily walks and a quiet companion to her inner life.

## Location & Geography

The grove stands on the outskirts of Moonstone, associated with "Beemer's cottonwood grove" (012-005), named for Uncle Billy Beemer, a local landowner. Dr. Archie's house sits near it, its property framed by the grove (005-011). By night, "the moon stood over the cottonwood grove" in a scene of stillness and silence (012-022).

## Role in the Novel

The cottonwood grove appears as a threshold marker—a place Thea passes through on her way between the town and her private thoughts. It is never the setting of dramatic action but always a presence at the edges of her consciousness. Before departing Moonstone for Chicago, she "passed the cottonwood grove where Thea often used to bring" her thoughts to rest (020-135). Returning in memory years later, she sees it "edged with gold" in the evening light (030-078).

## Key Scenes

- **The Doctor's House**: Ray Kennedy points out Dr. Archie's house, its property framed by the cottonwood grove, as he shows Thea the town's landmarks (005-011).
- **Moonlit Night**: Under the moon, the cottonwood grove stands still, "no wind, and no sound," a rare moment of exterior calm (012-022).
- **Farewell Walk**: Before departing Moonstone, Thea passes the cottonwood grove for what may be the last time, a quiet leave-taking from the landscape of her childhood (020-135).
- **Memory Return**: In Chicago, Thea sees the cottonwood grove in memory, "edged with gold" in a soft, rosy evening light (030-078).
