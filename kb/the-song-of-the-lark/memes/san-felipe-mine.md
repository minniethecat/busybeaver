## Overview

The San Felipe Mine is a silver mine in Colorado that plays a pivotal role in the financial transformation of [[dr-archie|Dr. Howard Archie]]. Initially a modest investment that required him to travel to Denver for stockholders' meetings, the mine eventually "began to give up that silver hoard" [051-144] and made Archie one of the wealthiest men in the state. The mine's success is the engine behind Archie's transition from Moonstone country doctor to influential Denver capitalist.

## Location & Geography

The San Felipe Mine is located in the mineral-rich mountains of Colorado, accessible from Denver. Dr. Archie's mining company maintains offices on the sixth floor of the Raton Building in Denver, from which he oversees operations while enjoying panoramic views of the mountain ranges where the mine is situated [051-013]. The mine itself is one of several silver-producing properties in Colorado's mining districts, and its output is significant enough that "San Felipe headed the list of mining quotations in every daily paper, East and West" [051-146].

## Role in the Novel

The San Felipe Mine first appears in Part IV when Dr. Archie travels to Denver for a stockholders' meeting [046-002]. At this point the mine is not yet producing its legendary wealth — Archie must borrow money from Captain Harris to cover a shortfall rather than sell his San Felipe shares [046-089]. Captain Harris advises him: "You want to hold on to San Felipe, and buy more" [046-089].

By Part V, the mine has transformed Archie's life entirely. He has left Moonstone, given up his medical practice, and become a wealthy Denver businessman. The mine's success coincides with the death of Mrs. Archie, and the narrator notes with characteristic irony that Archie "insisted upon accrediting his peace of mind to the San Felipe, to Time, to anything but his release from Mrs. Archie" [051-124].

The mine's wealth gives Archie political influence in Colorado and New Mexico, and he uses it to support the reform party in state politics [051-146].

## Key Scenes

- **Stockholders' meeting at the Brown Palace Hotel** [046-002]: Archie visits Denver in January for a San Felipe meeting. A snowstorm blankets the city, and he encounters Captain Harris at breakfast, who advises him to hold onto his shares and offers him a note rather than let him sell.

- **Captain Harris's prophecy** [046-089]: The old promoter tells Archie the mine is concealing wealth — a prophecy that proves accurate years later when the silver hoard is discovered.

- **The Denver office** [051-013]: Archie, now ten years older and "emphatically ten years more prosperous," stands at the window of his private room in the San Felipe Mining Company offices, looking at the mountains while dictating to his secretary.

- **The mine strikes silver** [051-144]: While Archie is living at the Brown Palace Hotel after leaving Moonstone, "the San Felipe mine began to give up that silver hoard which old Captain Harris had always accused it of concealing," and "San Felipe headed the list of mining quotations in every daily paper, East and West."
