# The Kohler Family

## Overview

The Kohler family represents the German immigrant presence in Moonstone—a household that maintains Old World cultural traditions while adapting to life in the American West. It is at the Kohlers' house that Thea Kronborg takes her piano lessons with Professor Wunsch, and Mrs. Kohler's supervision of these lessons provides Thea's earliest exposure to a European attitude toward music and culture (004-004).

## The Kohler Household

The Kohlers' house, located some distance from town—"more than a mile to reach the Kohlers' house"—is a space apart from the ordinary life of Moonstone. Here, Professor Wunsch holds court, and Thea enters a world where music is taken seriously, where European standards of art and culture are maintained despite the provincial setting (004-016).

Mrs. Kohler is "always present when Thea takes her lesson," a guardian of propriety whose watchful presence ensures that the instruction proceeds with appropriate formality. Her role is small but significant: she maintains the conditions in which cultural transmission can occur.

## Legacy

The Kohlers, like Professor Wunsch himself, are part of the chain of transmission that carries European musical culture to the American frontier. Their household is a way station on Thea's journey, providing the physical and cultural space in which her first serious musical training occurs.
