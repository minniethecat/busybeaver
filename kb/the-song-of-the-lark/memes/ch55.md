## V

[055-001] When Archie got back to his hotel at two o’clock in the morning, he found

[055-002] [[fred-ottenburg|Fred Ottenburg]]’s card under his door, with a message scribbled across the

[055-003] top: “When you come in, please call up room 811, this hotel.” A

[055-004] moment later Fred’s voice reached him over the telephone.

[055-005] “That you, Archie? Won’t you come up? I’m having some supper

[055-006] and I’d like company. Late? What does that matter? I won’t keep you

[055-007] long.”

[055-008] Archie dropped his overcoat and set out for room 811. He found Ottenburg in the

[055-009] act of touching a match to a chafing-dish, at a table laid for two in his

[055-010] sitting-room. “I’m catering here,” he announced cheerfully.

[055-011] “I let the waiter off at midnight, after he’d set me up.

[055-012] You’ll have to account for yourself, Archie.”

[055-013] The doctor laughed, pointing to three wine-coolers under the table. “Are

[055-014] you expecting guests?”

[055-015] “Yes, two.” Ottenburg held up two fingers,—“you, and my

[055-016] higher self. He’s a thirsty boy, and I don’t invite him often. He

[055-017] has been known to give me a headache. Now, where have you been, Archie, until

[055-018] this shocking hour?”

[055-019] “Bah, you’ve been banting!” the doctor exclaimed, pulling out

[055-020] his white gloves as he searched for his handkerchief and throwing them into a

[055-021] chair. Ottenburg was in evening clothes and very pointed dress shoes. His white

[055-022] waistcoat, upon which the doctor had fixed a challenging eye, went down

[055-023] straight from the top button, and he wore a camelia. He was conspicuously

[055-024] brushed and trimmed and polished. His smoothly controlled excitement was wholly

[055-025] different from his usual easy cordiality, though he had his face, as well as

[055-026] his figure, well in hand. On the serving-table there was an empty champagne

[055-027] pint and a glass. He had been having a little starter, the doctor told himself,

[055-028] and would probably be running on high gear before he got through. There was

[055-029] even now an air of speed about him.

[055-030] “Been, Freddy?”—the doctor at last took up his question.

[055-031] “I expect I’ve been exactly where you have. Why didn’t you

[055-032] tell me you were coming on?”

[055-033] “I wasn’t, Archie.” Fred lifted the cover of the chafingdish

[055-034] and stirred the contents. He stood behind the table, holding the lid with his

[055-035] handkerchief. “I had never thought of such a thing. But Landry, a young

[055-036] chap who plays her accompaniments and who keeps an eye out for me, telegraphed

[055-037] me that Madame Rheinecker had gone to Atlantic City with a bad throat, and Thea

[055-038] might have a chance to sing *Elsa*. She has sung it only twice here

[055-039] before, and I missed it in [[dresden|Dresden]]. So I came on. I got in at four this

[055-040] afternoon and saw you registered, but I thought I wouldn’t butt in. How

[055-041] lucky you got here just when she was coming on for this. You couldn’t

[055-042] have hit a better time.” Ottenburg stirred the contents of the dish

[055-043] faster and put in more sherry. “And where have you been since twelve

[055-044] o’clock, may I ask?”

[055-045] Archie looked rather self-conscious, as he sat down on a fragile gilt chair

[055-046] that rocked under him, and stretched out his long legs. “Well, if

[055-047] you’ll believe me, I had the brutality to go to see her. I wanted to

[055-048] identify her. Couldn’t wait.”

[055-049] Ottenburg placed the cover quickly on the chafing-dish and took a step

[055-050] backward. “You did, old sport? My word! None but the brave deserve the

[055-051] fair. Well,”—he stooped to turn the wine,—“and how was

[055-052] she?”

[055-053] “She seemed rather dazed, and pretty well used up. She seemed

[055-054] disappointed in herself, and said she hadn’t done herself justice in the

[055-055] balcony scene.”

[055-056] “Well, if she didn’t, she’s not the first. Beastly stuff to

[055-057] sing right in there; lies just on the ‘break’ in the voice.”

[055-058] Fred pulled a bottle out of the ice and drew the cork. Lifting his glass he

[055-059] looked meaningly at Archie. “You know who, doctor. Here goes!” He

[055-060] drank off his glass with a sigh of satisfaction. After he had turned the lamp

[055-061] low under the chafing-dish, he remained standing, looking pensively down at the

[055-062] food on the table. “Well, she rather pulled it off! As a backer,

[055-063] you’re a winner, Archie. I congratulate you.” Fred poured himself

[055-064] another glass. “Now you must eat something, and so must I. Here, get off

[055-065] that bird cage and find a steady chair. This stuff ought to be rather good;

[055-066] head waiter’s suggestion. Smells all right.” He bent over the

[055-067] chafing-dish and began to serve the contents. “Perfectly innocuous:

[055-068] mushrooms and truffles and a little crab-meat. And now, on the level, Archie,

[055-069] how did it hit you?”

[055-070] Archie turned a frank smile to his friend and shook his head. “It was all

[055-071] miles beyond me, of course, but it gave me a pulse. The general excitement got

[055-072] hold of me, I suppose. I like your wine, Freddy.” He put down his glass.

[055-073] “It goes to the spot to-night. She *was* all right, then? You

[055-074] weren’t disappointed?”

[055-075] “Disappointed? My dear Archie, that’s the high voice we dream of;

[055-076] so pure and yet so virile and human. That combination hardly ever happens with

[055-077] sopranos.” Ottenburg sat down and turned to the doctor, speaking calmly

[055-078] and trying to dispel his friend’s manifest bewilderment. “You see,

[055-079] Archie, there’s the voice itself, so beautiful and individual, and then

[055-080] there’s something else; the thing in it which responds to every shade of

[055-081] thought and feeling, spontaneously, almost unconsciously. That color has to be

[055-082] born in a singer, it can’t be acquired; lots of beautiful voices

[055-083] haven’t a vestige of it. It’s almost like another gift—the

[055-084] rarest of all. The voice simply is the mind and is the heart. It can’t go

[055-085] wrong in interpretation, because it has in it the thing that makes all

[055-086] interpretation. That’s why you feel so sure of her. After you’ve

[055-087] listened to her for an hour or so, you aren’t afraid of anything. All the

[055-088] little dreads you have with other artists vanish. You lean back and you say to

[055-089] yourself, ‘No, *that* voice will never betray.’ *Treulich

[055-090] geführt, treulich bewacht*.”

[055-091] Archie looked envyingly at Fred’s excited, triumphant face. How

[055-092] satisfactory it must be, he thought, to really know what she was doing and not

[055-093] to have to take it on hearsay. He took up his glass with a sigh. “I seem

[055-094] to need a good deal of cooling off to-night. I’d just as lief forget the

[055-095] Reform Party for once.

[055-096] “Yes, Fred,” he went on seriously; “I thought it sounded very

[055-097] beautiful, and I thought she was very beautiful, too. I never imagined she

[055-098] could be as beautiful as that.”

[055-099] “Wasn’t she? Every attitude a picture, and always the right kind of

[055-100] picture, full of that legendary, supernatural thing she gets into it. I never

[055-101] heard the prayer sung like that before. That look that came in her eyes; it

[055-102] went right out through the back of the roof. Of course, you get an *Elsa*

[055-103] who can look through walls like that, and visions and Grail-knights happen

[055-104] naturally. She becomes an abbess, that girl, after *Lohengrin* leaves her.

[055-105] She’s made to live with ideas and enthusiasms, not with a husband.”

[055-106] Fred folded his arms, leaned back in his chair, and began to sing

[055-107] softly:—

[055-108] “In lichter Waffen Scheine,\

[055-109] Ein Ritter nahte da.”

[055-110] “Doesn’t she die, then, at the end?” the doctor asked

[055-111] guardedly.

[055-112] Fred smiled, reaching under the table. “Some *Elsas* do; she

[055-113] didn’t. She left me with the distinct impression that she was just

[055-114] beginning. Now, doctor, here’s a cold one.” He twirled a napkin

[055-115] smoothly about the green glass, the cork gave and slipped out with a soft

[055-116] explosion. “And now we must have another toast. It’s up to you,

[055-117] this time.”

[055-118] The doctor watched the agitation in his glass. “The same,” he said

[055-119] without lifting his eyes. “That’s good enough. I can’t raise

[055-120] you.”

[055-121] Fred leaned forward, and looked sharply into his face. “That’s the

[055-122] point; how *could* you raise me? Once again!”

[055-123] “Once again, and always the same!” The doctor put down his glass.

[055-124] “This doesn’t seem to produce any symptoms in me to-night.”

[055-125] He lit a cigar. “Seriously, Freddy, I wish I knew more about what

[055-126] she’s driving at. It makes me jealous, when you are so in it and

[055-127] I’m not.”

[055-128] “In it?” Fred started up. “My God, haven’t you seen her

[055-129] this blessed night?—when she’d have kicked any other man down the

[055-130] elevator shaft, if I know her. Leave me something; at least what I can pay my

[055-131] five bucks for.”

[055-132] “Seems to me you get a good deal for your five bucks,” said Archie

[055-133] ruefully. “And that, after all, is what she cares about,—what

[055-134] people get.”

[055-135] Fred lit a cigarette, took a puff or two, and then threw it away. He was

[055-136] lounging back in his chair, and his face was pale and drawn hard by that mood

[055-137] of intense concentration which lurks under the sunny shallows of the vineyard.

[055-138] In his voice there was a longer perspective than usual, a slight remoteness.

[055-139] “You see, Archie, it’s all very simple, a natural development.

[055-140] It’s exactly what Mahler said back there in the beginning, when she sang

[055-141] *Woglinde*. It’s the idea, the basic idea, pulsing behind every bar

[055-142] she sings. She simplifies a character down to the musical idea it’s built

[055-143] on, and makes everything conform to that. The people who chatter about her

[055-144] being a great actress don’t seem to get the notion of where *she*

[055-145] gets the notion. It all goes back to her original endowment, her tremendous

[055-146] musical talent. Instead of inventing a lot of business and expedients to

[055-147] suggest character, she knows the thing at the root, and lets the musical

[055-148] pattern take care of her. The score pours her into all those lovely postures,

[055-149] makes the light and shadow go over her face, lifts her and drops her. She lies

[055-150] on it, the way she used to lie on the Rhine music. Talk about rhythm!”

[055-151] The doctor frowned dubiously as a third bottle made its appearance above the

[055-152] cloth. “Aren’t you going in rather strong?”

[055-153] Fred laughed. “No, I’m becoming too sober. You see this is

[055-154] breakfast now; kind of wedding breakfast. I feel rather weddingish. I

[055-155] don’t mind. You know,” he went on as the wine gurgled out, “I

[055-156] was thinking to-night when they sprung the wedding music, how any fool can have

[055-157] that stuff played over him when he walks up the aisle with some dough-faced

[055-158] little hussy who’s hooked him. But it isn’t every fellow who can

[055-159] see—well, what we saw tonight. There are compensations in life, Dr.

[055-160] Howard Archie, though they come in disguise. Did you notice her when she came

[055-161] down the stairs? Wonder where she gets that bright-and-morning star look?

[055-162] Carries to the last row of the family circle. I moved about all over the house.

[055-163] I’ll tell you a secret, Archie: that carrying power was one of the first

[055-164] things that put me wise. Noticed it down there in Arizona, in the open. That, I

[055-165] said, belongs only to the big ones.” Fred got up and began to move

[055-166] rhythmically about the room, his hands in his pockets. The doctor was

[055-167] astonished at his ease and steadiness, for there were slight lapses in his

[055-168] speech. “You see, Archie, *Elsa* isn’t a part that’s

[055-169] particularly suited to Thea’s voice at all, as I see her voice.

[055-170] It’s over-lyrical for her. She makes it, but there’s nothing in it

[055-171] that fits her like a glove, except, maybe, that long duet in the third act.

[055-172] There, of course,”—he held out his hands as if he were measuring

[055-173] something,—“we know exactly where we are. But wait until they give

[055-174] her a chance at something that lies properly in her voice, and you’ll see

[055-175] me rosier than I am to-night.”

[055-176] Archie smoothed the tablecloth with his hand. “I am sure I don’t

[055-177] want to see you any rosier, Fred.”

[055-178] Ottenburg threw back his head and laughed. “It’s enthusiasm,

[055-179] doctor. It’s not the wine. I’ve got as much inflated as this for a

[055-180] dozen trashy things: brewers’ dinners and political orgies. You, too,

[055-181] have your extravagances, Archie. And what I like best in you is this particular

[055-182] enthusiasm, which is not at all practical or sensible, which is downright

[055-183] Quixotic. You are not altogether what you seem, and you have your reservations.

[055-184] Living among the wolves, you have not become one. *Lupibus vivendi non lupus

[055-185] sum*.”

[055-186] The doctor seemed embarrassed. “I was just thinking how tired she looked,

[055-187] plucked of all her fine feathers, while we get all the fun. Instead of sitting

[055-188] here carousing, we ought to go solemnly to bed.”

[055-189] “I get your idea.” Ottenburg crossed to the window and threw it

[055-190] open. “Fine night outside; a hag of a moon just setting. It begins to

[055-191] smell like morning. After all, Archie, think of the lonely and rather solemn

[055-192] hours we’ve spent waiting for all this, while she’s

[055-193] been—reveling.”

[055-194] Archie lifted his brows. “I somehow didn’t get the idea to-night

[055-195] that she revels much.”

[055-196] “I don’t mean this sort of thing.” Fred turned toward the

[055-197] light and stood with his back to the window. “That,” with a nod

[055-198] toward the wine-cooler, “is only a cheap imitation, that any poor

[055-199] stiff-fingered fool can buy and feel his shell grow thinner. But take it from

[055-200] me, no matter what she pays, or how much she may see fit to lie about it, the

[055-201] real, the master revel is hers.” He leaned back against the window sill

[055-202] and crossed his arms. “Anybody with all that voice and all that talent

[055-203] and all that beauty, has her hour. Her hour,” he went on deliberately,

[055-204] “when she can say, ‘there it is, at last, *wie im Traum

[055-205] ich*—

[055-206] “‘As in my dream I dreamed it,\

[055-207] As in my will it was.’”

[055-208] He stood silent a moment, twisting the flower from his coat by the stem and

[055-209] staring at the blank wall with haggard abstraction. “Even I can say

[055-210] to-night, Archie,” he brought out slowly,

[055-211] “‘As in my dream I dreamed it,\

[055-212] As in my will it was.’

[055-213] Now, doctor, you may leave me. I’m beautifully drunk, but not with

[055-214] anything that ever grew in France.”

[055-215] The doctor rose. Fred tossed his flower out of the window behind him and came

[055-216] toward the door. “I say,” he called, “have you a date with

[055-217] anybody?”

[055-218] The doctor paused, his hand on the knob. “With Thea, you mean? Yes.

[055-219] I’m to go to her at four this afternoon—if you haven’t

[055-220] paralyzed me.”

[055-221] “Well, you won’t eat me, will you, if I break in and send up my

[055-222] card? She’ll probably turn me down cold, but that won’t hurt my

[055-223] feelings. If she ducks me, you tell her for me, that to spite me now

[055-224] she’d have to cut off more than she can spare. Good-night, Archie.”
