## Saba of Antioch

[021-001]This day I heard Saul of Tarsus preaching the [[jesus-of-nazareth|Christ]] unto the Jews of this city.

[021-002]He calls himself Paul now, the apostle to the Gentiles.

[021-003]I knew him in my youth, and in those days he persecuted the friends of the Nazarene. Well do I remember his satisfaction when his fellows stoned the radiant youth called Stephen.

[021-004]This Paul is indeed a strange man. His soul is not the soul of a free man.

[021-005]At times he seems like an animal in the forest, hunted and wounded, seeking a cave wherein he would hide his pain from the world.

[021-006]He speaks not of Jesus, nor does he repeat His words. He preaches the Messiah whom the prophets of old had foretold.

[021-007]And though he himself is a learned Jew he addresses his fellow Jews in Greek; and his Greek is halting, and he ill chooses his words.

[021-008]But he is a man of hidden powers and his presence is affirmed by those who gather around him. And at times he assures them of what he himself is not assured.

---

[021-009]We who knew Jesus and heard his discourses say that He taught man how to break the chains of his bondage that he might be free from his yesterdays.

[021-010]But Paul is forging chains for the man of tomorrow. He would strike with his own hammer upon the anvil in the name of one whom he does not know.

[021-011]The Nazarene would have us live the hour in passion and ecstasy.

[021-012]The man of Tarsus would have us be mindful of laws recorded in the ancient books.

[021-013]Jesus gave His breath to the breathless dead. And in my lone nights I believe and I understand.

[021-014]When He sat at the board, He told stories that gave happiness to the feasters, and spiced with His joy the meat and the wine.

[021-015]But Paul would prescribe our loaf and our cup.

[021-016]Suffer me not to turn my eyes the other way.
