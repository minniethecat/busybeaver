## Mary Magdalene

[041-001]His mouth was like the heart of a pomegranate, and the shadows in His eyes were deep.

[041-002]And He was gentle, like a man mindful of his own strength.

[041-003]In my dreams I beheld the kings of the earth standing in awe in His presence.

[041-004]I would speak of His face, but how shall I?

[041-005]It was like night without darkness, and like day without the noise of day.

[041-006]It was a sad face, and it was a joyous face.

[041-007]And well I remember how once He raised His hand towards the sky, and His parted fingers were like the branches of an elm.

[041-008]And I remember Him pacing the evening. He was not walking. He Himself was a road above the road; even as a cloud above the earth that would descend to refresh the earth.

[041-009]But when I stood before Him and spoke to him, He was a man, and His face was powerful to behold. And He said to me: “What would you, Miriam?”

[041-010]I would not answer Him, but my wings enfolded my secret, and I was made warm.

[041-011]And because I could bear His light no more, I turned and walked away, but not in shame. I was only shy, and I would be alone, with His fingers upon the strings of my heart.
