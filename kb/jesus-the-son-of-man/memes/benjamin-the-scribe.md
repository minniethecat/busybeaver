## Benjamin the Scribe

[046-001]It has been said that [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] was the enemy of [[romans|Rome]] and [[judea|Judea]].

[046-002]But I say that Jesus was the enemy of no man and no race.

[046-003]I have heard Him say: “The birds of the air and the mountaintops are not mindful of the serpents in their dark holes.

[046-004]“Let the dead bury their dead. Be you yourself among the living, and soar high.”

[046-005]I was not one of His disciples. I was but one of the many who went after Him to gaze upon His face.

[046-006]He looked upon [[rome|Rome]] and upon us, who are the slaves of Rome, as a father looks upon his children playing with toys and fighting among themselves for the larger toy. And He laughed from His height.

[046-007]He was greater than State and race; He was greater than revolution.

[046-008]He was single and alone, and He was an awakening.

[046-009]He wept all our unshed tears and smiled all our revolts.

[046-010]We knew it was in His power to be born with all who are not yet born, and to bid them see, not with their eyes but with His vision.

[046-011]Jesus was the beginning of a new kingdom upon the earth, and that kingdom shall remain.

[046-012]He was the son and the grandson of all the kings who builded the kingdom of the spirit.

[046-013]And only the kings of spirit have ruled our world.
