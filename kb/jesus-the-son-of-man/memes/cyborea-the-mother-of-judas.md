## Cyborea the Mother of Judas

[076-001]My son was a good man and upright. He was tender and kind to me, and he loved his kin and his countrymen. And he hated our enemies, the cursèd [[romans|Romans]], who wear purple cloth though they spin no thread nor sit at any loom; and who reap and gather where they have not ploughed nor sowed the seed.

[076-002]My son was but seventeen when he was caught shooting arrows at the Roman legion passing through our vineyard.

[076-003]Even at that age he would speak to the other youths of the glory of Israel, and he would utter many strange things that I did not understand.

[076-004]He was my son, my only son.

[076-005]He drank life from these breasts now dry, and he took his first steps in this garden, grasping these fingers that are now like trembling reeds.

[076-006]With these selfsame hands, young and fresh then like the grapes of [[lebanon|Lebanon]], I put away his first sandals in a linen kerchief that my mother had given me. I still keep them there in that chest, beside the window.

[076-007]He was my firstborn, and when he took his first step, I too took my first step. For women travel not save when led by their children.

[076-008]And now they tell me he is dead by his own hand; that he flung himself from the High Rock in remorse because he had betrayed his friend [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus of Nazareth]].

[076-009]I know my son is dead. But I know he betrayed no one; for he loved his kin and hated none but the Romans.

[076-010]My son sought the glory of Israel, and naught but that glory was upon his lips and in his deeds.

[076-011]When he met Jesus on the highway he left me to follow Him. And in my heart I knew that he was wrong to follow any man.

[076-012]When he bade me farewell I told him that he was wrong, but he listened not.

[076-013]Our children do not heed us; like the high tide of today, they take no counsel with the high tide of yesterday.

[076-014]I beg you question me no further about my son.

[076-015]I loved him and I shall love him forevermore.

[076-016]If love were in the flesh I would burn it out with hot irons and be at peace. But it is in the soul, unreachable.

[076-017]And now I would speak no more. Go question another woman more honoured than the mother of [[judas-iscariot|Judas]].

[076-018]Go to the mother of Jesus. The sword is in her heart also; she will tell you of me, and you will understand.
