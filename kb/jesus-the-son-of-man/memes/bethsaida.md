## Overview

**Bethsaida** was a fishing town on the northeastern shore of the Sea of [[galilee|Galilee]], near [[capernaum|Capernaum]]. In the gospel tradition, it was the home of several disciples including Peter, Andrew, and Philip. In Gibran's *Jesus the Son of Man*, Bethsaida appears through the voice of **Hannah**, a woman of the town who testifies in the year 73.

## Hannah of Bethsaida

The sole voice from Bethsaida in Gibran's book is **Hannah**, whose chapter is set in the year 73 — decades after the events of Jesus's ministry. Her opening line is poetic and personal:

> The sister of my father had left us in her youth to dwell in a hut beside her father's ancient vineyard. (024-001)

Hannah's testimony connects the landscape of Bethsaida — its vineyards, its proximity to the sea — to the spiritual legacy that survived in the region long after Jesus's death. Her perspective as a woman speaking decades later offers a rare glimpse of how the Jesus tradition was preserved in Galilean memory.

## A Galilean Fishing Community

Bethsaida's name means "house of fishing" in Aramaic, reflecting its primary livelihood on the Sea of Galilee. As a fishing town in close proximity to [[capernaum|Capernaum]], it shared in the dense network of Galilean villages that formed the backdrop of Jesus's early ministry. The town's location made it part of the "evangelical triangle" of Chorazin, Bethsaida, and Capernaum.
