## Peter

[060-001]Once in [[capernaum|Capernaum]] my Lord and Master spoke thus:

[060-002]“Your neighbour is your other self dwelling behind a wall. In understanding, all walls shall fall down.

[060-003]“Who knows but that your neighbour is your better self wearing another body? See that you love him as you would love yourself.

[060-004]“He too is a manifestation of the Most High, whom you do not know.

[060-005]“Your neighbour is a field where the springs of your hope walk in their green garments, and where the winters of your desire dream of snowy heights.

[060-006]“Your neighbour is a mirror wherein you shall behold your countenance made beautiful by a joy which you yourself if not know, and by a sorrow you yourself did not share.

[060-007]“I would have you love your neighbour even as I have loved you.”

[060-008]Then I asked Him saying: “How can I love a neighbour who loves me not, and who covets my property? One who would steal my possessions?”

[060-009]And He answered: “When you are ploughing and your manservant is sowing the seed behind you, would you stop and look backward and put to flight a sparrow feeding upon a few of your seeds? Should you do this, you were not worthy of the riches of your harvest.”

[060-010]When [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] had said this, I was ashamed and I was silent. But I was not in fear, for He smiled upon me.
