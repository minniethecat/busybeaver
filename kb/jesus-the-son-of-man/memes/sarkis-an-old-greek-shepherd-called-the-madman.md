## Sarkis, an Old Greek Shepherd, Called the Madman

[068-001]In a dream I saw [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] and my god Pan sitting together in the heart of the forest.

[068-002]They laughed at each other’s speech, with the brook that ran near them, and the laughter of Jesus was the merrier. And they conversed long.

[068-003]Pan spoke of earth and her secrets, and of his hoofèd brothers and his hornèd sisters; and of dreams. And he spoke of roots and their nestlings, and of the sap that wakes and rises and sings to summer.

[068-004]And Jesus told of the young shoots in the forest, and of flowers and fruit, and the seed that they shall bear in a season not yet come.

[068-005]He spoke of birds in space and their singing in the upper world.

[068-006]And He told of white harts in the desert wherein God shepherds them.

[068-007]And Pan was pleased with the speech of the new God, and his nostrils quivered.

[068-008]And in the same dream I beheld Pan and Jesus grow quiet and still in the stillness of the green shadows.

[068-009]And then Pan took his reeds and played to Jesus.

[068-010]The trees were shaken and the ferns trembled, and there was a fear upon me.

[068-011]And Jesus said: “Good brother, you have the glade and the rocky height in your reeds.”

[068-012]Then Pan gave the reeds to Jesus and said: “You play now. It is your turn.”

[068-013]And Jesus said: “These reeds are too many for my mouth. I have this flute.”

[068-014]And He took His flute and He played.

[068-015]And I heard the sound of rain in the leaves, and the singing of streams among the hills, and the falling of snow on the mountaintop.

[068-016]The pulse of my heart, that had once beaten with the wind, was restored again to the wind, and all the waves of my yesterdays were upon my shore, and I was again Sarkis the shepherd, and the flute of Jesus became the pipes of countless shepherds calling to countless flocks.

[068-017]Then Pan said to Jesus: “Your youth is more kin to the reed than my years. And long ere this in my stillness I have heard your song and the murmur of your name.

[068-018]“Your name has a goodly sound; well shall it rise with the sap to the branches, and well shall it run with the hoofs among the hills.

[068-019]“And it is not strange to me, though my father called me not by that name. It was your flute that brought it back to my memory.

[068-020]“And now let us play our reeds together.”

[068-021]And they played together.

[068-022]And their music smote heaven and earth, and a terror struck all living things.

[068-023]I heard the bellow of beasts and the hunger of the forest. And I heard the cry of lonely men, and the plaint of those who long for what they know not.

[068-024]I heard the sighing of the maiden for her lover, and the panting of the luckless hunter for his prey.

[068-025]And then there came peace into their music, and the heavens and the earth sang together.

[068-026]All this I saw in my dream, and all this I heard.
