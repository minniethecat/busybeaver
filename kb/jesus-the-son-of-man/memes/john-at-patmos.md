## John at Patmos

[059-001]Once more I would speak of Him.

[059-002]God gave me the voice and the burning lips though not the speech.

[059-003]And unworthy am I for the fuller word, yet I would summon my heart to my lips.

[059-004][[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] loved me and I knew not why.

[059-005]And I loved Him because He quickened my spirit to heights beyond my stature, and to depths beyond my sounding.

[059-006][[love|Love]] is a sacred mystery.

[059-007]To those who love, it remains forever wordless;

[059-008]But to those who do not love, it may be but a heartless jest.

[059-009]Jesus called me and my brother when we were labouring in the field.

[059-010]I was young then and only the voice of dawn had visited my ears.

[059-011]But His voice and the trumpet of His voice was the end of my labour and the beginning of my passion.

[059-012]And there were naught for me then but to walk in the sun and worship the loveliness of the hour.

[059-013]Could you conceive a majesty too kind to be majestic? And a beauty too radiant to seem beautiful?

[059-014]Could you hear in your dreams a voice shy of its own rapture?

[059-015]He called me and I followed Him.

[059-016]That evening I returned to my father’s house to get my other cloak.

[059-017]And I said to my mother: “Jesus of [[nazareth|Nazareth]] would have me in His company.”

[059-018]And she said: “Go His way my son, even like your brother.”

[059-019]And I accompanied Him.

[059-020]His fragrance called me and commanded me, but only to release me.

[059-021]Love is a gracious host to his guests though to the unbidden his house is a mirage and a mockery.

---

[059-022]Now you would have me explain the miracles of Jesus.

[059-023]We are all the miraculous gesture of the moment; our Lord and Master was the centre of that moment.

[059-024]Yet it was not in His desire that His gestures be known.

[059-025]I have heard Him say to the lame: “Rise and go home, but say not to the priest that I have made you whole.”

[059-026]And Jesus’ mind was not with the cripple; it was rather with the strong and the upright.

[059-027]His mind sought and held other minds and His complete spirit visited other spirits.

[059-028]And is so doing His spirit changed these minds and these spirits.

[059-029]It seemed miraculous, but with our Lord and Master it was simply like breathing the air of every day.

---

[059-030]And now let me speak of other things.

[059-031]On a day when He and I were alone walking in a field, we were both hungry, and we came to a wild apple tree.

[059-032]There were only two apples hanging on the bough.

[059-033]And He held the trunk of the tree with His arm and shook it, and the two apples fell down.

[059-034]He picked them both up and gave one to me. The other He held in His hand.

[059-035]In my hunger I ate the apple, and I ate it fast.

[059-036]Then I looked at Him and I saw that He still held the other apple in His hand.

[059-037]And He gave it to me saying: “Eat this also.”

[059-038]And I took the apple, and in my shameless hunger I ate it.

[059-039]And as we walked on I looked upon His face.

[059-040]But how shall I tell you of what I saw?

[059-041]A night where candles burn in space;

[059-042]A dream beyond our reaching;

[059-043]A noon where all shepherds are at peace and happy that their flock are grazing;

[059-044]An eventide, and a stillness, and a homecoming;

[059-045]Then a sleep and a dream.

[059-046]All these things I saw in His face.

[059-047]He had given me the two apples. And I knew He was hungry even as I was hungry.

[059-048]But I now know that in giving them to me He had been satisfied. He Himself ate of other fruit from another tree.

[059-049]I would tell you more of Him, but how shall I?

[059-050]When love becomes vast love becomes wordless.

[059-051]And when memory is overladen it seeks the silent deep.
