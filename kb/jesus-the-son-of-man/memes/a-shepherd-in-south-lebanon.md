## A Shepherd in South Lebanon

[017-001]It was late summer when He and three other men first walked upon that road yonder. It was evening, and He stopped and stood there at the end of the pasture.

[017-002]I was playing upon my flute, and my flock was grazing all around me. When He stopped I rose and walked over and stood before Him.

[017-003]And He asked me: “Where is the grave of Elijah? Is it not somewhere near this place?”

[017-004]And I answered Him: “It is there, Sir, underneath that great heap of stones. Even unto this day every passerby brings a stone and places it upon the heap.”

[017-005]And He thanked me and walked away, and His friends walked behind Him.

[017-006]And after three days Gamaliel, who was also a shepherd, said to me that the man who had passed by was a prophet in [[judea|Judea]]; but I did not believe him. Yet I thought of that man for many a moon.

[017-007]When spring came, [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] passed once more by this pasture, and this time He was alone.

[017-008]I was not playing on my flute that day for I had lost a sheep and I was bereaved, and my heart was downcast within me.

[017-009]And I walked towards Him and stood still before Him, for I desired to be comforted.

[017-010]And He looked at me and said: “You do not play upon your flute this day. Whence is the sorrow in your eyes?”

[017-011]And I answered: “A sheep from among my sheep is lost. I have sought her everywhere but I find her not. And I know not what to do.”

[017-012]And He was silent for a moment. Then He smiled upon me and said: “Wait here awhile and I will find your sheep.” And He walked away and disappeared among the hills.

[017-013]After an hour He returned, and my sheep was close behind Him. And as He stood before me, the sheep looked up into His face even as I was looking. Then I embraced her in gladness.

[017-014]And He put His hand upon my shoulder and said: “From this day you shall love this sheep more than any other in your flock, for she was lost and now she is found.”

[017-015]And again I embraced my sheep in gladness, and she came close to me, and I was silent.

[017-016]But when I raised my head to thank Jesus, He was already walking afar off, and I had not the courage to follow Him.
