> A disciple of Jesus whose brief but moving testimony reflects on the Teacher's humanity.

Levi the Disciple speaks in a single chapter [030-001–030-023] of Gibran's *[[titlepage|Jesus the Son of Man]]*, contributing a distinctive voice to the book's polyphonic witness.

## Testimony

Upon an eventide He passed by my house, and my soul was quickened within me. (030-001)

The chapter is one of seventy-nine testimonies that together compose Gibran's portrait of [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] through the eyes of those who knew him — or knew of him — in first-century Palestine and the wider Mediterranean world.

Jesus's response to the publicans at Levi's table reveals the scope of his mission:

> And Jesus answered: "Aye, these also we would comfort. Came we here except to share the loaf and the cup with the uncrowned and the unshod amongst you?
>
> "Few, aye, too few are the featherless who dare the wind, and many are the wingèd and full-fledged yet in the nest.
>
> "And we would feed them all with our beak, both the sluggish and the swift."
>
> (030-009)

## Significance

As one of the seventy-nine witnesses, Levi the Disciple represents a particular angle of perception — one thread in the tapestry of voices that Gibran weaves around the central figure of Jesus. Each narrator sees differently, and in the accumulation of these partial views, a fuller portrait emerges.
