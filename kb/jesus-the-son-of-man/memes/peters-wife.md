> The wife of Simon Peter, whose presence in the Gospel narrative hints at the domestic context of the early disciples.

Gibran touches on Peter's family life as part of his humanizing portrait of the apostles. Peter's wife represents the ordinary ties of family and home that the disciples navigated alongside their extraordinary calling.

Peter's wife is not named in Gibran's text, but her existence is implied through the households of Simon Peter — her mother (006-025) and daughter Petronelah attest to the domestic world from which Peter was called.
