## Rafca the Bride of Cana

[009-001]This happened before He was known to the people.

[009-002]I was in my mother’s garden tending the rosebushes, when He stopped at our gate.

[009-003]And He said: “I am thirsty. Will you give me water from your well?”

[009-004]And I ran and brought the silver cup, and filled it with water; and I poured into it a few drops from the jasmine vial.

[009-005]And He drank deep and was pleased.

[009-006]Then He looked into my eyes and said: “My blessing shall be upon you.”

[009-007]When He said that I felt as it were a gust of wind rushing through my body. And I was no longer shy; and I said: “Sir, I am betrothed to a man of [[cana|Cana]] in [[galilee|Galilee]]. And I shall be married on the fourth day of the coming week. Will you not come to my wedding and grace my marriage with your presence?”

[009-008]And He answered: “I will come, my child.”

[009-009]Mind you, He said: “My child,” yet He was but a youth, and I was nearly twenty.

[009-010]Then He walked on down the road.

[009-011]And I stood at the gate of our garden until my mother called me into the house.

[009-012]On the fourth day of the following week I was taken to the house of my bridegroom and given in marriage.

[009-013]And [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] came, and with Him His mother and His brother James.

[009-014]And they sat around the wedding-board with our guests whilst my maiden comrades sang the wedding-songs of Solomon the King. And Jesus ate our food and drank our wine and smiled upon me and upon the others.

[009-015]And He heeded all the songs of the lover bringing his belovèd into his tent; and of the young vineyard-keeper who loved the daughter of the lord of the vineyard and led her to his mother’s house; and of the prince who met the beggar maiden and bore her to his realm and crowned her with the crown of his fathers.

[009-016]And it seemed as if He were listening to yet other songs also, which I could not hear.

[009-017]At sundown the father of my bridegroom came to the mother of Jesus and whispered saying: “We have no more wine for our guests. And the day is not yet over.”

[009-018]And Jesus heard the whispering, and He said: “The cup bearer knows that there is still more wine.”

[009-019]And so it was indeed—and as long as the guests remained there was fine wine for all who would drink.

[009-020]Presently Jesus began to speak with us. He spoke of the wonders of earth and heaven; of sky flowers that bloom when night is upon the earth, and of earth flowers that blossom when the day hides the stars.

[009-021]And He told us stories and parables, and His voice enchanted us so that we gazed upon Him as if seeing visions, and we forgot the cup and the plate.

[009-022]And as I listened to Him it seemed as if I were in a land distant and unknown.

[009-023]After a while one of the guests said to the father of my bridegroom: “You have kept the best wine till the end of the feast. Other hosts do not so.”

[009-024]And all believed that Jesus had wrought a miracle, that they should have more wine and better at the end of the wedding-feast than at the beginning.

[009-025]I too thought that Jesus had poured the wine, but I was not astonished; for in His voice I had already listened to miracles.

[009-026]And afterwards indeed, His voice remained close to my heart, even until I had been delivered of my firstborn child.

[009-027]And now even to this day in our village and in the villages near by, the word of our guest is still remembered. And they say: “The spirit of Jesus of [[nazareth|Nazareth]] is the best and the oldest wine.”
