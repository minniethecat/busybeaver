## Joseph Surnamed Justus

[063-001]They say he was vulgar, the common offspring of common seed, a man uncouth and violent.

[063-002]They say that only the wind combed His hair, and only the rain brought His clothes and His body together.

[063-003]They deem Him mad, and they attribute His words to demons.

[063-004]Yet behold, the Man despised sounded a challenge and the sound thereof shall never cease.

[063-005]He sang a song and none shall arrest that melody. It shall hover from generation to generation and it shall rise from sphere to sphere remembering the lips that gave it birth and the ears that cradled it.

[063-006]He was a stranger. Aye, He was a stranger, a wayfarer on His way to a shrine, a visitor who knocked at our door, a guest from a far country.

[063-007]And because He found not a gracious host, He has returned to His own place.
