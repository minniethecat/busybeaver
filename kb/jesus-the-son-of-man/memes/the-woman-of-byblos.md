## The Woman of Byblos

[077-001]Weep with me, ye daughters of Ashtarte, and all ye lovers of Tamouz.

[077-002]Bid your heart melt and rise and run blood-tears,

[077-003]For He who was made of gold and ivory is no more.

[077-004]In the dark forest the boar overcame Him,

[077-005]And the tusks of the boar pierced His flesh.

[077-006]Now He lies stained with the leaves of yesteryear,

[077-007]And no longer shall His footsteps wake the seeds that sleep in the bosom of the spring.

[077-008]His voice will not come with the dawn to my window,

[077-009]And I shall be forever alone.

[077-010]Weep with me, ye daughters of Ashtarte, and all ye lovers of Tamouz,

[077-011]For my Belovèd has escaped me;

[077-012]He who spoke as the rivers speak;

[077-013]He whose voice and time were twins;

[077-014]He whose mouth was a red pain made sweet;

[077-015]He on whose lips gall would turn to honey.

[077-016]Weep with me, daughters of Ashtarte, and ye lovers of Tamouz.

[077-017]Weep with me around His bier as the stars weep,

[077-018]And as the moon-petals fall upon His wounded body.

[077-019]Wet with your tears the silken covers of my bed,

[077-020]Where my Belovèd once lay in my dream,

[077-021]And was gone away in my awakening.

[077-022]I charge ye, daughters of Ashtarte, and all ye lovers of Tamouz,

[077-023]Bare your breasts and weep and comfort me,

[077-024]For [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus of Nazareth]] is dead.
