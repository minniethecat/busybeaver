## Birbàrah of Yammouni

[065-001][[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] was patient with the dullard and the stupid, even as the winter awaits the spring.

[065-002]He was patient like a mountain in the wind.

[065-003]He answered with kindliness the harsh questionings of His foes.

[065-004]He could even be silent to cavil and dispute, for He was strong and the strong can be forbearing.

[065-005]But Jesus was also impatient.

[065-006]He spared not the hypocrite.

[065-007]He yielded not to men of cunning nor to the jugglers of words.

[065-008]And He would not be governed.

[065-009]He was impatient with those who believed not in light because they themselves dwelt in shadow; and with those who sought after signs in the sky rather than in their own hearts.

[065-010]He was impatient with those who weighed and measured the day and the night before they would trust their dreams to dawn or eventide.

[065-011]Jesus was patient.

[065-012]Yet He was the most impatient of men.

[065-013]He would have you weave the cloth though you spend years between the loom and the linen.

[065-014]But He would have none tear an inch off the woven fabric.
