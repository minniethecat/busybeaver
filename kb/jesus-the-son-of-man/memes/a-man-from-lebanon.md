## A Man from Lebanon

[079-001]Nineteen Centuries Afterward

[079-002]Master, master singer,

[079-003]Master of words unspoken,

[079-004]Seven times was I born, and seven times have I died,

[079-005]Since your last hasty visit and our brief welcome.

[079-006]And behold I live again,

[079-007]Remembering a day and a night among the hills,

[079-008]When your tide lifted us up.

[079-009]Thereafter many lands and many seas did I cross,

[079-010]And wherever I was led by saddle or sail

[079-011]Your name was prayer or argument.

[079-012]Men would bless you or curse you;

[079-013]The curse, a protest against failure,

[079-014]The blessing, a hymn of the hunter

[079-015]Who comes back from the hills

[079-016]With provision for his mate.

[079-017]Your friends are yet with us for comfort and support,

[079-018]And your enemies also, for strength and assurance.

[079-019]Your mother is with us;

[079-020]I have beheld the sheen of her face in the countenance of all mothers;

[079-021]Her hand rocks cradles with gentleness,

[079-022]Her hand folds shrouds with tenderness.

[079-023]And [[mary-magdalene|Mary Magdalene]] is yet in our midst,

[079-024]She who drank the vinegar of life, and then its wine.

[079-025]And [[judas-iscariot|Judas]], the man of pain and small ambitions,

[079-026]He too walks the earth;

[079-027]Even now he preys upon himself when his hunger find naught else,

[079-028]And seeks his larger self in self-destruction.

[079-029]And John, he whose youth loved beauty, is here,

[079-030]And he sings though unheeded.

[079-031]And [[simon-peter|Simon Peter]] the impetuous, who denied you that he might live longer for you,

[079-032]He too sits by our fire.

[079-033]He may deny you again ere the dawn of another day,

[079-034]Yet he would be crucified for your purpose, and deem himself unworthy of the honour.

[079-035]And [[caiaphas|Caiaphas]] and Annas still live their day,

[079-036]And judge the guilty and the innocent.

[079-037]They sleep upon their feathered bed

[079-038]Whilst he whom they have judged is whipped with the rods.

[079-039]And the woman who was taken in adultery,

[079-040]She too walks the streets of our cities,

[079-041]And hungers for bread not yet baked,

[079-042]And she is alone in an empty house.

[079-043]And [[pontius-pilate|Pontius Pilatus]] is here also:

[079-044]He stands in awe before you,

[079-045]And still questions you,

[079-046]But he dares not risk his station or defy an alien race;

[079-047]And he is still washing his hands.

[079-048]Even now [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]] holds the basin and [[romans|Rome]] the ewer,

[079-049]And betwixt the two thousand thousand hands would be washed to whiteness.

[079-050]Master, Master Poet,

[079-051]Master of words sung and spoken,

[079-052]They have builded temples to house your name,

[079-053]And upon every height they have raised your cross,

[079-054]A sign and a symbol to guide their wayward feet,

[079-055]But not unto your joy.

[079-056]Your joy is a hill beyond their vision,

[079-057]And it does not comfort them.

[079-058]They would honour the man unknown to them.

[079-059]And what consolation is there in a man like themselves, a man whose kindliness is like their own kindliness,

[079-060]A god whose love is like their own love,

[079-061]And whose mercy is in their own mercy?

[079-062]They honour not the man, the living man,

[079-063]The first man who opened His eyes and gazed at the sun

[079-064]With eyelids unquivering.

[079-065]Nay, they do not know Him, and they would not be like Him.

[079-066]They would be unknown, walking in the procession of the unknown.

[079-067]They would bear sorrow, their sorrow,

[079-068]And they would not find comfort in your joy.

[079-069]Their aching heart seeks not consolation in your words and the song thereof.

[079-070]And their pain, silent and unshapen,

[079-071]Makes them creatures lonely and unvisited.

[079-072]Though hemmed about by kin and kind,

[079-073]They live in fear, uncomraded;

[079-074]Yet they would not be alone.

[079-075]They would bend eastward when the west wind blows.

[079-076]They call you king,

[079-077]And they would be in your court.

[079-078]They pronounce you the Messiah,

[079-079]And they would themselves be anointed with the holy oil.

[079-080]Yea, they would live upon your life.

[079-081]Master, Master Singer,

[079-082]Your tears were like the showers of May,

[079-083]And your laughter like the waves of the white sea.

[079-084]When you spoke, your words were the far-off whisper of their lips when those lips should be kindled with fire;

[079-085]You laughed for the marrow in their bones that was not yet ready for laughter;

[079-086]And you wept for their eyes that yet were dry;

[079-087]Your voice fathered their thoughts and their understanding;

[079-088]Your voice mothered their words and their breath.

[079-089]Seven times was I born and seven times have I died,

[079-090]And now I live again, and I behold you,

[079-091]The fighter among fighters,

[079-092]The poet of poets,

[079-093]King above all kings,

[079-094]A man half-naked with your road-fellows.

[079-095]Every day the bishop bends down his head

[079-096]When he pronounces your name;

[079-097]And every day the beggars say:

[079-098]“For [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]]’ sake

[079-099]Give us a penny to buy bread.”

[079-100]We call upon each other,

[079-101]But in truth we call upon you,

[079-102]Like the flood tide in the spring of our want and desire,

[079-103]And when our autumn comes, like the ebb tide.

[079-104]High or low, your name is upon our lips,

[079-105]The Master of infinite compassion.

[079-106]Master, Master of our lonely hours,

[079-107]Here and there, betwixt the cradle and the coffin, I meet your silent brothers,

[079-108]The free men, unshackled,

[079-109]Sons of your mother earth and space.

[079-110]They are like the birds of the sky,

[079-111]And like the lilies of the field.

[079-112]They live your life and think your thoughts,

[079-113]And they echo your song.

[079-114]But they are empty-handed,

[079-115]And they are not crucified with the great crucifixion,

[079-116]And therein is their pain.

[079-117]The world crucifies them every day,

[079-118]But only in little ways.

[079-119]The sky is not shaken,

[079-120]And the earth travails not with her dead.

[079-121]They are crucified and there is none to witness their agony.

[079-122]They turn their face to right and left

[079-123]And find not one to promise them a station in his kingdom.

[079-124]Yet they would be crucified again and yet again,

[079-125]That your God may be their God,

[079-126]And your Father their Father.

[079-127]Master, Master Lover,

[079-128]The Princess awaits your coming in her fragrant chamber,

[079-129]And the married unmarried woman in her cage;

[079-130]The harlot who seeks bread in the streets of her shame,

[079-131]And the nun in her cloister who has no husband;

[079-132]The childless woman too at her window,

[079-133]Where frost designs the forest on the pane,

[079-134]She finds you in that symmetry,

[079-135]And she would mother you, and be comforted.

[079-136]Master, Master Poet,

[079-137]Master of our silent desires,

[079-138]The heart of the world quivers with the throbbing of your heart,

[079-139]But it burns not with your song.

[079-140]The world sits listening to your voice in tranquil delight,

[079-141]But it rises not from its seat

[079-142]To scale the ridges of your hills.

[079-143]Man would dream your dream, but he would not wake to your dawn

[079-144]Which is his greater dream.

[079-145]He would see with your vision,

[079-146]But he would not drag his heavy feet to your throne;

[079-147]Yet many have been enthroned inn your name

[079-148]And mitred with your power,

[079-149]And have turned your golden visit

[079-150]Into crowns for their head and sceptres for their hand.

[079-151]Master, Master of Light,

[079-152]Whose eye dwells in the seeking fingers of the blind,

[079-153]You are still despised and mocked,

[079-154]A man too weak and infirm to be God,

[079-155]A God too much man to call forth adoration.

[079-156]Their mass and their hymn,

[079-157]Their sacrament and their rosary, are for their imprisoned self.

[079-158]You are their yet distant self, their far-off cry, and their passion.

[079-159]But Master, Sky-heart, Knight of our fairer dream,

[079-160]You do still tread this day;

[079-161]Nor bows nor spears shall stay your steps;

[079-162]You walk through all our arrows.

[079-163]You smile down upon us,

[079-164]And though you are the youngest of us all

[079-165]You father us all.

[079-166]Poet, Singer, Great Heart,

[079-167]May our God bless your name,

[079-168]And the womb that held you, and the breasts that gave you milk.

[079-169]And may God forgive us all.
