Capernaum stands in Gibran's narrative as a city of poetic significance
and as a geographical stage for encounters between [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] and the people
of [[galilee|Galilee]].

## Geography

Capernaum lay on the northern shore of the Sea of [[galilee|Galilee]], in the
territory that in [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]]'s time was ruled by [[herod-antipas|Herod Antipas]]. The town
sat on the Via Maris, the great trade route linking [[damascus|Damascus]] with the
Mediterranean coast and [[egypt|Egypt]], which made it both a fishing village and
a crossroads. The text does not describe its physical layout, but the
town's position by the lake is central to its identity in the narrative.

## In the Narrative

Capernaum appears primarily through Peter. Recalling a moment in
the town, Peter records: "Once in Capernaum my Lord and Master
spoke thus: 'Your neighbour is your other self dwelling behind a wall'"
(060-001). The teaching — that the neighbor is "your better self
wearing another body" — is delivered in Capernaum, giving the town
the role of a place where [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]]'s ethical vision finds its voice.

## Significance

In the narrative economy, Capernaum functions as a place of utterance
rather than event. It is where [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] speaks, not where things happen
to Him. Gibran does not narrate exorcisms or healings at Capernaum, but
Peter's memory anchors a moment of moral instruction there.

## Events

A remembered sermon or teaching moment — "Once in Capernaum my Lord
and Master spoke" (060-001) — is the only event associated with the
town in the text.

## Related Places

- [[galilee]] — the region whose bride Capernaum is called to be
- [[bethsaida]] — the nearby fishing village, home to Peter and Andrew
- [[damascus]] — the trade-route city to the northeast

## Quotation

Peter recalls a teaching delivered at Capernaum:

> Once in Capernaum my Lord and Master spoke thus: "Your neighbour is your other self dwelling behind a wall. In understanding, all walls shall fall down."
>
> (060-001)
