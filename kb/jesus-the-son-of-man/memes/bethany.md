Bethany is a village on the eastern slope of the Mount of Olives, near
[[jerusalem|Jerusalem]]. In Gibran's narrative, it appears as a place of pause and
encounter — a quiet threshold before the tumultuous events in the city.

## Geography

Bethany lies a short distance from [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]], separated by the Mount
of Olives. [[john-the-baptist|John the Baptist]] recalls that [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] and his disciples
"reached the hill of Bethany" on their approach to the city,
and from there [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] said: "Let us go to [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]]" (019-032).
The village sits on the road from [[jericho|Jericho]] to [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]], a natural
stopping place for travelers from the east.

## In the Narrative

Bethany is the setting for one of the narrative's most intimate
moments of recognition. Rumanous the Greek poet recalls: "When I
stood before Him in Bethany, I knew what it is to hold an instrument
and yet be silent" (029-010) — a poet's confession that in the
presence of [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]], even the most gifted voice may choose to listen.
Susannah of [[nazareth|Nazareth]] records simply that "we were told that He had
gone to Bethany" (062-046), a passing note that places the village
on the map of [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]]'s final movements.

## Significance

Bethany serves as a liminal space in the narrative geography — neither
the rural [[galilee|Galilee]] of beginnings nor the urban [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]] of endings,
but a village on the edge where one may still turn back or press on.

## Events

[[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]]'s approach to [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]] passes through Bethany (019-032), and
his presence there is noted by those following his movements (062-046).

## Related Places

- [[jerusalem]] — the city to which Bethany is the threshold
- [[jericho]] — the city on the road that passes through Bethany

## Quotation

Joseph of Arimathea recalls the approach to Jerusalem:

> And when we reached the hill of Bethany, He said: "Let us go to Jerusalem. The city awaits us. I will enter the gate riding upon a colt, and I will speak to the multitude."
>
> (019-032)
