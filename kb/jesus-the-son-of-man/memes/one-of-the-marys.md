## One of the Marys

[028-001]His head was always high, and the flame of God was in His eyes.

[028-002]He was often sad, but His sadness was tenderness shown to those in pain, and comradeship given to the lonely.

[028-003]When He smiled His smile was as the hunger of those who long after the unknown. It was like the dust of stars falling upon the eyelids of children. And it was like a morsel of bread in the throat.

[028-004]He was sad, yet it was a sadness that would rise to the lips and become a smile.

[028-005]It was like a golden veil in the forest when autumn is upon the world. And sometimes it seemed like moonlight upon the shores of the lake.

[028-006]He smiled as if His lips would sing at the wedding-feast.

[028-007]Yet He was sad with the sadness of the wingèd who will not soar above his comrade.
