## Zacchaeus

[047-001]You believe in what you hear said. Believe in the unsaid, for the silence of men is nearer the truth than their words.

[047-002]You ask if [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] could have escaped His shameful death and saved His followers from persecution.

[047-003]I answer: He could indeed have escaped had He chosen, but He did not seek safety nor was He mindful of protecting His flock from wolves of the night.

[047-004]He knew His fate and the morrow of His constant lovers. He foretold and prophesied what should befall every one of us. He sought not His death; but He accepted death as a husbandman shrouding his corn with earth, accepts the winter, and then awaits the spring and harvest; and as a builder lays the largest stone in the foundation.

[047-005]We were men of [[galilee|Galilee]] and from the slopes of [[lebanon|Lebanon]]. Our Master could have led us back to our country, to live with His youth in our gardens until old age should come and whisper us back into the years.

[047-006]Was anything barring His path back to the temples of our villages where others were reading the prophets and then disclosing their hearts?

[047-007]Could He not have said, “Now I go east with the west wind,” and so saying dismiss us with a smile upon His lips?

[047-008]Aye, He could have said: “Go back to your kin. The world is not ready for me. I shall return a thousand years hence. Teach your children to await my return.”

[047-009]He could have done this had He so chosen.

[047-010]But He knew that to build the temple invisible He must needs lay Himself the cornerstone, and lay us around as little pebbles cemented close to Himself.

[047-011]He knew that the sap of His tree must rise from its roots, and He poured His blood upon its roots; and to Him it was not sacrifice but rather gain.

[047-012][[death|Death]] is the revealer. The death of Jesus revealed His life.

[047-013]Had He escaped you and His enemies, you would have been the conquerors of the world. Therefore He did not escape.

[047-014]Only He who desires all shall give all.

[047-015]Aye, Jesus could have escaped His enemies and lived to old age. But He knew the passing of the seasons, and He would sing His song.

[047-016]What man facing the armèd world would not be conquered for the moment that he might overcome the ages?

[047-017]And now you ask who, in very truth, slew Jesus, the [[romans|Romans]] or the priests of [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]]?

[047-018]Neither the Romans slew Him, nor the priests. The whole world stood to honour Him upon that hill.
