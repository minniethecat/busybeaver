## Overview

Tamouz (also known as Tammuz or Dumuzid) is a Babylonian dying-and-rising deity, the lover of Ishtar/Ashtarte. In *Jesus the Son of Man*, his name appears in the lament of [[the-woman-of-byblos|the Woman of Byblos]], who invokes him alongside Ashtarte in her song of mourning and longing.

The Woman of Byblos calls upon "all ye lovers of Tamouz" (077-001)(077-010)(077-016)(077-022), weaving his name into a ritual lament that echoes the ancient Near Eastern pattern of the dying-and-rising god — a motif that Gibran subtly connects to the Jesus narrative through shared imagery of death and return.
