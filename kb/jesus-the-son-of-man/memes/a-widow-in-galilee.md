## A Widow in Galilee

[031-001]My son was my first and my only born. He laboured in our field and he was contented until he heard the man called [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] speaking to the multitude.

[031-002]Then my son suddenly became different, as if a new spirit, foreign and unwholesome, had embraced his spirit.

[031-003]He abandoned the field and the garden; and he abandoned me also. He became worthless, a creature of the highways.

[031-004]That man Jesus of [[nazareth|Nazareth]] was evil, for what good man would separate a son from his mother?

[031-005]The last thing my child said to me was this: “I am going with one of His disciples to the North Country. My life is established upon the Nazarene. You have given me birth, and for that I am grateful to you. But I needs must go. Am I not leaving with you our rich land, and all our silver and gold? I shall take naught but this garment and this staff.”

[031-006]Thus my son spoke, and departed.

[031-007]And now the [[romans|Romans]] and the priests have laid hold upon Jesus and crucified Him; and they have done well.

[031-008]A man who would part mother and son could not be godly.

[031-009]The man who sends our children to the cities of the Gentiles cannot be our friend.

[031-010]I know my son will not return to me. I saw it in his eyes. And for this I hate Jesus of Nazareth who caused me to be alone in this unploughed field and this withered garden.

[031-011]And I hate all those who praise Him.

[031-012]Not many days ago they told me that Jesus once said: “My father and my mother and my brethren are those who hear my word and follow me.”

[031-013]But why should sons leave their mothers to follow His footsteps?

[031-014]And why should the milk of my breast be forgotten for a fountain not yet tasted? And the warmth of my arms be forsaken for the Northland, cold and unfriendly?

[031-015]Aye, I hate the Nazarene, and I shall hate Him to the end of my days, for He has robbed me of my firstborn, my only son.
