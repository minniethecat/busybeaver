## Claudius, a Roman Centurion

[073-001]After he was taken, they entrusted Him to me. And I was ordered by [[pontius-pilate|Pontius Pilatus]] to keep Him in custody until the following morning.

[073-002]My soldiers led Him prisoner, and He was obedient to them.

[073-003]At midnight I left my wife and children and visited the arsenal. It was my habit to go about and see all that was well with my battalions in [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]]; and that night I visited the arsenal where He was held.

[073-004]My soldiers and some of the young Jews were making sport of Him. They had stripped Him of His garment, and they had put a crown of last year’s brier-thorns upon His head.

[073-005]They had seated Him against a pillar, and they were dancing and shouting before Him.

[073-006]And they had given Him a reed to hold in His hand.

[073-007]As I entered someone shouted: “Behold, O Captain, the King of the Jews.”

[073-008]I stood before Him and looked at Him, and I was ashamed. I knew not why.

[073-009]I had fought in Gallia and in Spain, and with my men I had faced death. Yet never had I been in fear, nor been a coward. But when I stood before that man and He looked at me I lost heart. It seemed as though my lips were sealed, and I could not utter no word.

[073-010]And straightway I left the arsenal.

[073-011]This chanced thirty years ago. My sons who were babes then are men now. And they are serving Caesar and [[romans|Rome]].

[073-012]But often in counselling them I have spoken of Him, a man facing death with the sap of life upon His lips, and with compassion for His slayers in His eyes.

[073-013]And now I am old. I have lived the years fully. And I think truly that neither Pompey nor Caesar was so great a commander as that Man of [[galilee|Galilee]].

[073-014]For since His unresisting death an army has risen out of the earth to fight for Him. … And He is better served by them, though dead, than ever Pompey or Caesar was served, though living.
