## Significance

Each of the seventy-nine witnesses in Gibran's book brings a distinct angle of vision. Hannah of Bethsaida represents one such perspective — the accumulation of these partial views creates the book's unique, prismatic portrait of [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]].
