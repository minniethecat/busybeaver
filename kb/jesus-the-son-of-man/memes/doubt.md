# Doubt

## Definition

Doubt in *[[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] [[jesus-of-nazareth|the Son of Man]]* is not condemned but honored as the honest companion of faith. Its most eloquent spokesman is [[thomas-the-disciple|Thomas]], whose grandfather taught him: "Let us observe truth, but only when truth is made manifest unto us" (026-001). This is not cynicism but intellectual integrity — the refusal to profess what one has not verified. [[thomas-the-disciple|Thomas]]'s great discovery is that "doubt is a pain too lonely to know that faith is his twin brother" (026-010). Doubt and faith are not opposites but siblings, each incomplete without the other.

## In the Text

[[thomas-the-disciple|Thomas]] embodies doubt as a spiritual discipline. When [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] called him, "I heeded Him, for His command was more potent than my will; yet I kept my counsel" (026-002). While other disciples "were swayed like branches in the wind, I listened immovable" (026-003). His doubt protects him from the crowd's enthusiasm and the authorities' certainty alike. In the book's final accounting, [[thomas-the-disciple|Thomas]] is the one who walks beside [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] to [[crucifixion|Golgotha]] — his doubt having been transformed not into certainty but into presence.

## Significance

Gibran's treatment of doubt is one of his most original contributions. In a religious tradition that often demands unwavering faith, he offers a vision in which doubt is not failure but fidelity — the refusal to lie about what one has not yet seen. [[thomas-the-disciple|Thomas]]'s story suggests that the honest doubter may be closer to [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] at the end than the confident believer, because the doubter has never pretended to certainty he did not possess.

## Quotation

Thomas describes the spiritual condition of the divided heart with a seafarer's metaphor:

> Now a man who loves with his heart, yet holds a doubt in his mind, is but a slave in a galley who sleeps at his oar and dreams of his freedom, till the lash of the master wakes him.
>
> (026-007)
