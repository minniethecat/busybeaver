> A name crafted by Gibran for the daughter of Simon Peter, adding familial depth to the apostle's portrait.

Petronelah is a distinctive Gibranian invention, illustrating the author's practice of filling in the human silences of the Gospel record with imagined detail. Her presence deepens the domestic dimension of Peter's life and embodies the private cost of public discipleship.
