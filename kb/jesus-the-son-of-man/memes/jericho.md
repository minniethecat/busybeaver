Jericho is one of the oldest cities in the world, an oasis in the
Jordan Valley near the Dead Sea. In Gibran's narrative, it appears as
a waypoint on [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]]'s journeys — a place where seekers find him and
where memories of encounter linger after his death.

## Geography

Jericho lies in the Jordan Valley, northeast of [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]], surrounded
by desert and fed by springs. It sits on the ancient road that climbs
from the [[jordan|Jordan River]] up to the Mount of Olives toward [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]].

## In the Narrative

Ephraim of Jericho sought [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] out: "When he came again to Jericho
I sought Him out and said to Him" (043-001), his encounter becoming
the occasion for a question about the wedding at [[cana|Cana]]. James the
Brother of the Lord, after the crucifixion, remembers finding himself
"in a village near Jericho" (074-057), one of the scattered places
where the disciples regathered in the aftermath of loss.

## Significance

Jericho is a place of approach — the last city on the road to [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]]
from the east. To encounter [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] in Jericho is to meet him on the
way toward the capital, before the final confrontation.

## Events

Ephraim's conversation with [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] occurs in Jericho (043-001). James
finds himself near Jericho after the crucifixion (074-057).
