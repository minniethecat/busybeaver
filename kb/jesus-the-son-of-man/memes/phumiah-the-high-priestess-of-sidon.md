## Phumiah, the High Priestess of Sidon

[045-001]Take your harps and let me sing.

[045-002]Beat your strings, the silver and the gold;

[045-003]For I would sing the dauntless Man

[045-004]Who slew the dragon of the valley,

[045-005]Then gazèd down with pity

[045-006]Upon the thing He had slain.

[045-007]Take your harps and sing with me

[045-008]The lofty Oak upon the height,

[045-009]The sky-hearted and the ocean-handed Man,

[045-010]Who kissed the pallid lips of death,

[045-011]Yet quivers now upon the mouth of life.

[045-012]Take your harps and let us sing

[045-013]The fearless Hunter on the hill,

[045-014]Who marked the beast, and shot His viewless arrow,

[045-015]And brought the horn and tusk

[045-016]Down to the earth.

[045-017]Take your harps and sing with me

[045-018]The valiant Youth who conquered the mountain cities,

[045-019]And the cities of the plain that coiled like serpents in the sand.

[045-020]He fought not against pygmies but against gods

[045-021]Who hungered for our flesh and thirsted for our blood.

[045-022]And like the first Golden Hawk

[045-023]He would rival only eagles;

[045-024]For His wings were vast and proud

[045-025]And would not outwing the less wingèd.

[045-026]Take your harps and sing with me

[045-027]The joyous song of sea and cliff.

[045-028]The gods are dead,

[045-029]And they are lying still

[045-030]In the forgotten isle of a forgotten sea.

[045-031]And He who slew them sits upon His throne.

[045-032]He was but a youth.

[045-033]Spring had not yet given Him full beard,

[045-034]And His summer was still young in His field.

[045-035]Take your harps and sing with me

[045-036]The tempest in the forest

[045-037]That breaks the dry branch and the leafless twig,

[045-038]Yet sends the living root to nestle deeper at the breast of earth.

[045-039]Take your harps and sing with me

[045-040]The deathless song of our Belovèd.

[045-041]Nay, my maidens, stay your hands.

[045-042]Lay by your harps.

[045-043]We cannot sing Him now.

[045-044]The faint whisper of our song cannot reach His tempest,

[045-045]Nor pierce the majesty of His silence.

[045-046]Lay by your harps and gather close around me,

[045-047]I would repeat His words to you,

[045-048]And I would tell you of His deeds,

[045-049]For the echo of His voice is deeper than our passion.
