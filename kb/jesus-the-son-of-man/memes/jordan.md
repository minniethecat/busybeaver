The River Jordan flows from Mount Hermon through the Sea of [[galilee|Galilee]]
to the Dead Sea, forming the eastern boundary of the Holy Land. In
Gibran's narrative, it is the river of beginnings — the place of
baptism and first recognition.

## Geography

The Jordan winds southward through the Jordan Valley, passing near
[[jericho|Jericho]] before emptying into the Dead Sea. Its waters mark the
boundary between the wilderness to the east and the inhabited lands
to the west.

## In the Narrative

[[simon-peter|Simon Peter]] recalls that he had "already seen Him by the River Jordan
when [[john-the-baptist|John the Baptist]] had proclaimed the coming of the Prophet" (006-018).
The Jordan is where the forerunner and the one he foretold intersect.
[[john-the-baptist|John the Baptist]] confesses a darker vision: that he "shall be drowned
in the River Jordan" (018-008) — a premonition of his own fate, the
herald submerged in the very waters where he proclaimed.

## Significance

The Jordan is the waters of transition — where the prophet of the old
order gives way to the one he cannot fully comprehend. John's vision
of drowning in the Jordan captures the paradox of the forerunner:
to prepare the way is to be overtaken by it.

## Events

[[simon-peter|Simon Peter]]'s first sighting of [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] occurs at the Jordan (006-018).
[[john-the-baptist|John the Baptist]]'s ministry centers there, and his foreboding of his
own end is tied to its waters (018-008).

## Related Places
