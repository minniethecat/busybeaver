## Thomas

[026-001]My grandfather, who was a lawyer, once said: “Let us observe truth, but only when truth is made manifest unto us.”

[026-002]When [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] called me I heeded Him, for His command was more potent than my will; yet I kept my counsel.

[026-003]When He spoke and the others were swayed like branches in the wind, I listened immovable. Yet I loved Him.

[026-004]Three years ago He left us, a scattered company to sing His name, and to be His witnesses unto the nations.

[026-005]At that time I was called [[thomas-the-disciple|Thomas the Doubter]]. The shadow of my grandfather was still upon me, and always I would have truth made manifest.

[026-006]I would even put my hand in my own wound to feel the blood ere I would believe in my pain.

[026-007]Now a man who loves with his heart, yet holds a doubt in his mind, is but a slave in a galley who sleeps at his oar and dreams of his freedom, till the lash of the master wakes him.

[026-008]I myself was that slave, and I dreamed of freedom, but the sleep of my grandfather was upon me. My flesh needed the whip of my own day.

[026-009]Even in the presence of the Nazarene I had closed my eyes to see my hands chained to the oar.

[026-010][[doubt|Doubt]] is a pain too lonely to know that faith is his twin brother.

[026-011]Doubt is a foundling unhappy and astray, and though his own mother who gave him birth should find him and enfold him, he would withdraw in caution and in fear.

[026-012]For Doubt will not know truth till his wounds are healed and restored.

[026-013]I doubted Jesus until He made Himself manifest to me, and thrust my own hand into His very wounds.

[026-014]Then indeed I believed, and after that I was rid of my yesterday and the yesterdays of my forefathers.

[026-015]The dead in me buried their dead; and the living shall live for the Anointed King, even for Him who was the [[son-of-man|Son of Man]].

[026-016]Yesterday they told me that I must go and utter His name among the Persians and the Hindus.

[026-017]I shall go. And from this day to my last day, at dawn and at eventide, I shall see my Lord rising in majesty and I shall hear Him speak.
