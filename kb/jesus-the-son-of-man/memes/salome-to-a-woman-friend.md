## Salome, to a Woman Friend

[022-001]He was like poplars shimmering in the sun;

[022-002]And like a lake among the lonely hills,

[022-003]Shining in the sun;

[022-004]And like snow upon the mountain heights,

[022-005]White, white in the sun.

[022-006]Yea, He was like unto all these,

[022-007]And I loved Him.

[022-008]Yet I feared His presence.

[022-009]And my feet would not carry my burden of love

[022-010]That I might girdle His feet with my arms.

[022-011]I would have said to Him:

[022-012]“I have slain your friend in an hour of passion.

[022-013]Will you forgive me my sin?

[022-014]And will you not in mercy release my youth

[022-015]From its blind deed,

[022-016]That it may walk in your light?”

[022-017]I know He would have forgiven my dancing

[022-018]For the saintly head of His friend.

[022-019]I know He would have seen in me

[022-020]An object of His own teaching.

[022-021]For there was no valley of hunger He could not bridge,

[022-022]And no desert of thirst He could not cross.

[022-023]Yea, He was even as the poplars,

[022-024]And as the lakes among the hills,

[022-025]And like snow upon [[lebanon|Lebanon]].

[022-026]And I would have cooled my lips in the folds of His garment.

[022-027]But He was far from me,

[022-028]And I was ashamed.

[022-029]And my mother held me back

[022-030]When the desire to seek Him was upon me.

[022-031]Whenever He passed by, my heart ached for his loveliness,

[022-032]But my mother frowned at Him in contempt,

[022-033]And would hasten me from the window

[022-034]To my bedchamber.

[022-035]And she would cry aloud, saying:

[022-036]“Who is He but another locust-eater from the desert?

[022-037]“What is He but a scoffer and a renegade,

[022-038]A seditious riot-monger, who would rob us of sceptre and crown,

[022-039]And bid the foxes and the jackals of His accursèd land

[022-040]Howl in our halls and sit upon our throne?

[022-041]Go hide your face from this day,

[022-042]And await the day when His head shall fall down,

[022-043]But not upon your platter.”

[022-044]These things my mother said.

[022-045]But my heart would not keep her words.

[022-046]I loved Him in secret,

[022-047]And my sleep was girdled with flames.

[022-048]He is gone now.

[022-049]And something that was in me is gone also.

[022-050]Perhaps it was my youth

[022-051]That would not tarry here,

[022-052]Since the God of youth was slain.
