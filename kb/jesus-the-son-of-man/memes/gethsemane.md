Gethsemane is the final place [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] chooses to go before His arrest.
In Gibran's narrative, told through the eyes of James the brother of
the Lord, it is an olive grove beyond the city walls where [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] bids
His disciples farewell, prays alone, and is ultimately taken — while
His followers scatter in fear.

## Geography

Gethsemane lay beyond the walls of [[jerusalem|Jerusalem]], on the Mount of Olives.
[[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] says to His disciples after the last supper: "Let us go beyond
these walls. Let us go unto Gethsemane" (074-024), marking the
transition from the enclosed space of the meal to the open darkness
of the grove. James describes it as "an olive grove" (074-036), a
place of trees and shadows on the slope overlooking the city.

## In the Narrative

The account of Gethsemane unfolds across a sequence of paragraphs in
James's recollection. After the farewell meal, [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] and the disciples
walk out from the city. "When He reached an olive grove he stopped and
turned towards us saying: 'Rest here for an hour'" (074-036). The
disciples settle, but [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] does not rest: "[[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] left us and walked
by Himself in the olive grove. And I watched Him while the others
slept" (074-039).

James, the only one still awake, watches the solitary figure moving
among the trees. He recalls an earlier saying of [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] — "Heaven and
earth, and hell too, are of man" — and reflects: "I knew that He who
was pacing the olive grove was heaven made man; and I bethought me
that the womb of the earth is not a beginning nor the grave an end"
(074-042).

The scene then breaks. After the soldiers come, James confesses: "I
ran alone through the olive groves, nor had I power to be mindful, nor
did any voice speak in me except my fear" (074-056). The grove that
was the setting for [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]]'s solitude becomes the landscape of the
disciples' flight.

## Significance

Gethsemane in this telling is the place of ultimate solitude. [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]]
walks alone while His closest followers sleep; when danger comes,
they run. The olive grove is at once a garden of prayer and a theatre
of abandonment — the pivot between the intimacy of the supper and the
violence of the arrest.

## Events

The farewell at the last supper ends with the decision to go to
Gethsemane (074-024). In the grove, the disciples rest (074-036)
while [[jesus-of-nazareth|Jesus]] prays alone (074-039). The arrest follows, and the
disciples flee through the same olive groves (074-056).
