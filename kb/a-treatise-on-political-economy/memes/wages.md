# Wages

## Definition

Wages are the payment made to [[labouring-class|labourers]] in [[commerce|exchange]] for their productive services. They form one of the principal costs of production and represent the return to human industry. As Say observes, agricultural [[labouring-class|labourers]] "subsist wholly upon the wages they receive from their agricultural employers" (002-012), highlighting that wages are the means by which the labouring class participates in the product of industry.

## Wages and Capital

The payment of wages requires an advance of capital: the [[adventurer|entrepreneur]] must have sufficient funds to support [[labouring-class|labourers]] during the production process before the product is sold. "Every branch of production requires larger advances of wages, of [[raw-material|raw materials]], and of tools" (008-012). The rate of wages is thus influenced by the accumulation of capital — the more capital is available, the greater the demand for labour and the higher the wages that can be sustained.

## Wages in Production Costs

Wages enter into the cost of every commodity. Even [[raw-material|raw materials]] embody the wages of those who extracted or cultivated them. "The expence of wages alone will have been reduced" (008-013) whenever productivity improves, demonstrating that [[invention|technological progress]] benefits society by lowering the labour cost embedded in [[commodity|goods]]. This relationship between wages, productivity, and price is central to Say's theory of value.
