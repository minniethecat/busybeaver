# Right of Property

## Definition

The right of property is the legal and social recognition of exclusive [[property|ownership]] over objects of value. It is the foundation upon which production and [[saving|capital accumulation]] rest, for without secure [[property|possession]] of the fruits of one's industry, there is little incentive to produce beyond immediate subsistence. (014-001)

## Explanation

### Property as Productive Incentive

Secure property rights provide the motive for production and saving. When individuals know they will retain the benefit of their labour and investments, they are willing to defer consumption, accumulate capital, and undertake long-term productive enterprises. In societies where property is insecure — through arbitrary confiscation, theft, or excessive [[tax|taxation]] — production withers and capital is hidden or consumed.

### Property and Social Order

Say sees property rights as essential to social order and economic prosperity. "Amongst abundance of other causes of the misery and weakness of the countries subjected to the Ottoman dominion, it cannot be doubted, that one of the principal is, the vast quantity of capital remaining in a [[public-authority|state]] of inactivity. The general distrust and uncertainty of the future induce people of every rank...to withdraw a part of their property from the greedy eyes of power." (012-001)

### Property in the Productive Sources

[[property|Property]] rights extend to all three productive sources: the [[labour|work]]er's right to their labour (industry), the capitalist's right to their accumulated values (capital), and the landholder's right to their e[[public-authority|state]] (natural agents). The market prices of these productive services — [[wages|wage]]s, interest, and rent — depend on the security with which each agent can enforce their claim.

## Say's Treatment

Say devotes an entire chapter to the right of property, treating it not as a mere legal detail but as a fundamental condition of production. His analysis connects institutional security to economic outcomes, anticipating themes in institutional economics and the literature on property rights and development.

Say warns that the right of property may be violated indirectly as well as directly:

> It is to be observed, that the right of property is equally invaded, by obstructing the free employment of the objects of property, as by forcibly depriving the proprietor of them.
>
> (014-002)
