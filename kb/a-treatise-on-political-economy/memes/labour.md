# Labour

## Definition and Productive Agency

Labour is the human agency brought to bear upon natural objects to render them fit for consumption or further production. It is one of the three great agents of production — along with capital and natural agents — and "the most perfect product of labour is the one that derives nearly all its value from that source" (002-003). Say acknowledges that labour is indispensable to all production but rejects the notion that it is the sole source of value, criticising the view "that labour is the sole measure of wealth, or of value" (004-005).

## Division of Labour

The division of labour multiplies its productive power. By assigning distinct operations to different hands, society achieves a far greater output than if each individual attempted to perform every task. Say devotes an entire chapter to this principle, noting that "the division of labour increases its productive power in a very great degree" and is fundamental to industrial progress (see [[Division of Labour]]).

## Labour and Capital

Labour and capital work in concert. "Nature is commonly the fellow-labourer of man and his instruments," operating alongside human effort (004-003). Nations rich in capital can support more productive labour; those "deficient in capital labour under great disadvantage" (005-004). Where capital is scarce, labour receives lower remuneration, and national wealth grows more slowly.

## Labour, Value, and Wages

Say carefully distinguishes the labour expended in production from the value that labour creates. The value conferred by labour is measured not by the toil it cost but by the utility it produces. [[wages|Wages]] represent the price of labour, determined by the proportion between the demand for labour and the supply of [[labouring-class|labourers]]. The poor labourer "who goes to the alehouse on a Sunday" consumes unproductively, "wherefore their labour adds nothing to the total wealth of the community" (002-007).

> Man, as we have already seen, obliges natural agents, and even the products of his own previous industry, to work in concert with him in the business of production. There will, therefore, be no difficulty in comprehending the terms labour or productive service of nature, and labour or productive service of capital.
