# Banking

## Definition

Banking is the business of receiving deposits, making payments, extending credit, and facilitating the circulation of money through instruments such as bills of [[commerce|exchange]] and [[credit|letters of credit]]. Say describes the banker as one "who receives or pays on account of other people, or gives bills, orders, or [[credit|letters of credit]], payable elsewhere than at the place where they are given" (009-001). Banking is thus a specialized branch of commercial industry concerned with the management and circulation of value.

## The Banking System and Capital

Banks play a crucial role in mobilizing capital for productive use. "By an improved process of banking, by the extended use of credit" (021-055), banks enable savers to place their funds where they can be most productively employed. The banking system transforms scattered individual [[saving|savings]] into concentrated capital, available to [[adventurer|entrepreneur]]s who can put it to productive use.

## Banking and the Circulation of Money

Banking reduces the amount of money needed to conduct [[commercial-industry|commerce]]. Through instruments like bills of [[commerce|exchange]], deposits, and clearing mechanisms, banks enable transactions to be settled without the physical movement of [[coin|specie]]. The "common process of banking" (022-011) substitutes credit for coin, reducing the cost and risk of [[commercial-industry|commerce]] while accelerating the circulation of value through the economy.
