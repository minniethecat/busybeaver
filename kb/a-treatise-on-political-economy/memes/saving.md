# Saving

## Definition

Saving is the act of abstaining from immediate consumption in order to devote resources to future productive use. It is the foundation of capital accumulation and economic growth. "More savings are made, and more capital accumulated from the [[profit|profits]] of [[commerce|trade]] and [[manufacturing|manufacture]]" (002-006), linking saving directly to the expansion of a nation's productive capacity.

## Saving and Capital Formation

Saved resources, when productively employed, become capital — tools, machinery, [[raw-material|raw materials]], and the advances that support [[labouring-class|labourers]] during production. "Every saving of expenditure is so much gain to the consumer" (007-003), because reductions in the cost of production are ultimately passed on through lower prices. Saving thus benefits not only the saver but society at large, by increasing the [[capital|stock]] of capital available for productive enterprise.

## Saving versus Hoarding

Say distinguishes between productive saving and mere [[hoard|hoarding]]. Saving that is "employed productively" (011-008) — that is, invested in productive enterprises — adds to the nation's [[stock|capital stock]]. Idle savings, by contrast, withdraw resources from circulation without contributing to production. The key is the [[demand|effective demand]] generated by the productive employment of saved resources: "the saving is made with more or less advantage" (011-006) depending on how it is deployed.

> It must on no account be overlooked, that, in one way or other, a saving such as that we have been speaking of, whether expended productively or unproductively, still is in all cases expended and consumed ;
