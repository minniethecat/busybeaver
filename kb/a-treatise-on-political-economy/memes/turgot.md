# Anne Robert Jacques Turgot

## Biography

Anne Robert Jacques Turgot (1727–1781) was a French economist and [[public-authority|state]]sman, a leading figure among the Physiocrats. He served as Controller-General of Finances under Louis XVI, where he attempted sweeping economic reforms. Say's translator describes him as one of the economists whose outline has been followed in structuring the *Treatise* (P02-005).

## Major Contributions

### Theory of Capital and Saving

Turgot made significant contributions to the theory of capital formation. Say notes that Turgot agreed with [[adam-smith|Smith]] that frugality was more prevalent in modern times than formerly, citing the low rate of interest as evidence of abundant [[saving|capital accumulation]]: "Turgot falls in with [[adam-smith|Smith]]'s opinion. He expresses his belief, that frugality is more generally prevalent now than in former times." (011-016)

### Commerce and Trade Policy

Turgot also wrote on commercial policy, advocating for free [[commerce|trade]]. Say references Turgot's letters on the corn [[commerce|trade]], noting the practical difficulties of inter[[demand|vent]]ionist policies: "Turgot, in his letters on the [[commercial-industry|commerce]] of corn, observes that the procuring of exact information on this point must be still more insuperable." (017-095)

## Relationship to Say

Say treats Turgot as one of the key predecessors alongside [[adam-smith|Smith]] and [[james-steuart|Stewart]] in the development of political economy. All three are invoked together as authorities who agree on the fundamental relationship between production and the multiplication of wealth: "[[james-steuart|Stewart]], Turgot, and [[adam-smith|Smith]], all agree in thinking, that the multiplication of wealth is in exact proportion to the increase of its production." (019-006)

Say particularly values Turgot's analysis of saving and [[saving|capital accumulation]], and his critique of sumptuary laws. He observes that Turgot raised well-reasoned objections to artificial restrictions on consumption: "Turgot...has been well observed upon." (021-015) The reference is to Turgot's *Réflexions sur la Formation et la Distribution des [[wealth|Riches]]ses*.

- *Letters on the [[commerce|Commerce]] of Corn* (1770)
