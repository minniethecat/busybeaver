# Examination

In legal procedure, examination is the formal questioning of a witness under oath to elicit testimony relevant to a case. In The Leavenworth Case, the examination of witnesses at the coroner's inquest and in private investigative settings provides the primary means by which facts are established and suspects identified.

## The Coroner's Examination

The coroner conducts the first formal examination at the Leavenworth mansion. The narrator observes his skill:

> He was capable of conducting an important examination, with the necessary skill and judgment. (002-004)

The examination proceeds methodically, with the coroner calling witnesses in sequence and eliciting their testimony. Each witness's account adds to the accumulating picture of the crime:

> The interest which the examination of so important a person as the secretary naturally created. (003-118)

The coroner maintains a serious tone, preventing levity from intruding:

> Mr. Gryce put a stop to any attempt at levity or badinage into an examination the seriousness of which we were all beginning to feel. (003-128)

## Key Examinations

**Mr. Harwell's examination** — The secretary of the deceased is a key witness. His examination concludes with a detail that the coroner finds significant:

> This remark ended Mr. Harwell's examination for the time. (003-237)

**The expert examination of the pistol** — A firearms expert is examined regarding the make and origin of the murder weapon:

> After a momentary examination of the face of the cylinder... (005-029)

**Mary Leavenworth's examination** — The most charged examination involves Mary, whose composure under questioning is noted by all present. Her examination proceeds until:

> I looked to see her examination terminate, when suddenly his manner changed. (007-039)

## Examination and Truth

The novel explores examination as both a truth-seeking mechanism and a fallible human procedure. Witnesses may be truthful, evasive, or deliberately false. The examiner's skill lies in distinguishing among these possibilities.

The lawyer-narrator understands that examination is not merely about recording answers but about probing beneath them:

> He reached his conclusions by a thorough examination of the law bearing upon the facts. (019-045)

## Private Examination

Beyond the formal inquest, Detective Gryce conducts private examinations — interviews of suspects and witnesses outside the courtroom setting. These private examinations lack the legal formality of sworn testimony but are equally important to the investigation. They allow for more probing questioning and the observation of unguarded reactions.
