# Troilus and Cressida

William Shakespeare's problem play set during the Trojan War, written circa 1602. Referenced through chapter epigraphs in The Leavenworth Case, the play's treatment of faithlessness, disillusionment, and the gap between appearance and reality provides thematic resonance for Green's detective narrative.

## Relevance to the Novel

Anna Katharine Green's choice of epigraphs from Troilus and Cressida signals attention to the play's darker themes: the failure of ideals, the unreliability of appearances, and the bitterness of betrayal. These concerns map onto the novel's investigation of a crime committed within a family — a betrayal of the most intimate trust.

## Thematic Parallels

The play's cynical examination of human motives — its insistence that love, honor, and loyalty are fragile constructs — finds an echo in the detective's professional skepticism. Gryce, like Shakespeare's Thersites, sees through the surfaces that others take at face value.
