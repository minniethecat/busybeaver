# The Coroner's Inquest

The coroner's inquest is the formal legal proceeding convened at the [[leavenworth-mansion]] on the day after the discovery of [[horatio-leavenworth]]'s body. Presided over by the coroner and heard by a [[jury]], this event occupies several early chapters of *The Leavenworth Case* and establishes the factual and legal framework for the entire investigation conducted by [[ebenezer-gryce]].

## The Proceeding

The inquest convenes at the mansion, transforming the private residence into a public courtroom. The coroner presides, a [[jury]] is empaneled to hear evidence, and witnesses are called in sequence, each examined under oath:

> "The jury have just gone upstairs to view the body." (001-040)

The proceeding establishes the official finding that transforms the case:

> "A verdict of murder, Miss Leavenworth." (011-014)

## Sequence of Events

1. **Jury Views the Body**: The jurors inspect the crime scene and the deceased
2. **Staff Testimony**: Servants describe the discovery and household routine
3. **Expert Examination**: Medical and ballistic evidence presented
4. **Family Testimony**: [[mary-leavenworth]] and [[eleanore-leavenworth]] questioned
5. **Verdict**: The jury returns a finding of murder

## Witness Testimony

The inquest hears from a range of witnesses whose testimony establishes the factual record that [[ebenezer-gryce]] will later scrutinize, compare, and challenge. The Leavenworth nieces' testimony proves particularly significant — [[mary-leavenworth]]'s evasive answers are noted by Gryce and later revealed as deceptive, while [[eleanore-leavenworth]]'s composed responses create a different kind of suspicion.

The secretary's examination concludes with a significant detail that Gryce files away for later use:

> He had a quick, telling look, and after a few words decided that the inquest should proceed in the usual manner. (003-198)

## Aftermath

The inquest verdict authorizes the full criminal investigation. Immediately following the verdict, [[ebenezer-gryce]] shifts from observation to active pursuit:

> Immediately upon the delivery of the verdict, Mr. Gryce was not the man to let the grass grow under his feet. (011-011)

The testimony recorded at the inquest becomes the baseline against which all subsequent statements are measured. Gryce returns to the inquest record repeatedly throughout his investigation, comparing later accounts against the sworn testimony and finding the contradictions that ultimately break the case open.
