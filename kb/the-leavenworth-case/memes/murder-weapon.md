# Murder Weapon

The instrument or object used to cause the death of a homicide victim. In The Leavenworth Case, the murder weapon is a small pistol of a specific make — a detail that becomes crucial to the investigation and the identification of the killer.

## The Weapon

Horatio Leavenworth is killed by a single pistol shot to the back of the head. The discovery is made by the narrator:

> The pistol with which the deed was committed had been found on the floor. (001-023)

The coroner immediately focuses on the weapon as key evidence:

> He observed that he had not seen pistol or weapon lying upon the floor when he entered. (002-008)

## Identification of the Pistol

The investigation traces the pistol to its source. Through expert analysis, the coroner establishes:

> Bohn & Co. was the well-known pistol and ammunition store. (005-007)

The butler's testimony provides critical detail about the weapon's provenance:

> "Can you tell us from what make of pistol that was delivered?" (005-010)

And the expert answers:

> "Usually sold with the small pistol made by Smith & Wesson." (005-011)

The butler reacts with recognition:

> "A small pistol!" exclaimed the butler, jumping up. (005-012)

This revelation — that the weapon was a small, concealable pistol of a particular make — narrows the field of suspects and links the crime to a specific purchase.

## Physical Evidence and the Weapon

The murder weapon is not an isolated piece of evidence. It connects to other physical clues:

**The handkerchief** — Found stained with pistol grease, linking a specific household item to the weapon:

> The handkerchief found stained with pistol smoke. (010-069)

**The ammunition** — The bullet and cartridge case provide additional forensic information about the type of weapon used.

**The trajectory** — The position of the wound and the location of the body establish the physical circumstances of the shooting.

## Evidentiary Significance

In 19th-century forensic practice, the murder weapon was among the most important pieces of physical evidence. Unlike modern DNA analysis, the identification of the perpetrator relied heavily on tracing the weapon to its owner. The pistol in The Leavenworth Case demonstrates how a physical object can serve as the nexus connecting testimony, circumstances, and motive into a coherent theory of the crime.

The absence of the key from the library door — combined with the presence of the pistol — creates the locked-room dimension of the mystery.
