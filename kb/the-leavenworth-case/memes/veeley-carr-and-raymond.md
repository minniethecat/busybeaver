# Veeley, Carr & Raymond

The New York law firm where the narrator, Everett Raymond, is a junior partner. The firm's senior partner, Mr. Veeley, was Horatio Leavenworth's personal attorney and drafted the will whose provisions become central to the murder investigation.

## Role in the Case

As the Leavenworth family's legal representatives, the firm is involved in the case from the moment of the murder's discovery. Mr. Veeley dispatches Raymond to the inquest:

> As Mr. Veeley was their uncle's best friend, they naturally turned to him for guidance. (001-013)

The firm's knowledge of the will — its original provisions and any later alterations — makes them essential participants in establishing the financial motives underlying the crime.

## Professional Context

Veeley, Carr & Raymond represents the world of 19th-century New York legal practice — a world of partnerships, apprentices, and professional relationships that combined personal trust with institutional authority. The firm's role in the novel reflects the central place of law and lawyers in the social fabric of the period.
