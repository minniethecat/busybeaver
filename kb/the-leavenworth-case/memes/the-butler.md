# The Butler

The senior domestic servant of the Leavenworth household, the butler is a witness at the coroner's inquest whose dramatic recognition of the murder weapon provides one of the investigation's early breakthroughs.

## Testimony

The butler's testimony at the inquest focuses on the physical circumstances of the household and, crucially, on the identification of the weapon. When the firearms expert identifies the pistol as a small Smith & Wesson, the butler reacts with recognition:

> "A small pistol!" exclaimed the butler, jumping up. (005-012)

This spontaneous reaction — recognizing the specific type of weapon — suggests knowledge that goes beyond what a mere observer would possess. The butler's familiarity with the pistol connects it to the household in a way that more general testimony cannot.

## Evidentiary Significance

The butler's recognition of the pistol type advances the investigation by narrowing the origin of the weapon. A small Smith & Wesson is a specific and identifiable firearm — not a generic weapon that could belong to anyone. The butler's testimony helps anchor the physical evidence to the household.
