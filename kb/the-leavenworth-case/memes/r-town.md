# R— (Town)

A town outside New York whose name is partially suppressed in the narrative — a common convention in 19th-century fiction to suggest that real places and people are being protected. Mrs. Belden's connection to this town proves significant.

## Significance in the Investigation

This location plays a role in the unfolding of the Leavenworth murder investigation, contributing to the spatial and social geography of the novel's 1870s New York setting.
