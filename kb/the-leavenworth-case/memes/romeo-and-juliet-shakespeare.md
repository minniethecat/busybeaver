# Romeo and Juliet

William Shakespeare's tragedy of star-crossed lovers, written circa 1595. Referenced in The Leavenworth Case, the play's depiction of a clandestine union opposed by family authority provides a literary parallel to the secret marriage between Eleanore Leavenworth and Henry Clavering.

## Relevance to the Novel

The secret marriage plot of The Leavenworth Case echoes Shakespeare's most famous love story. Eleanore and Henry, like Romeo and Juliet, contract a union that must be concealed from the family whose approval is essential to their social standing and material prospects.

Unlike Shakespeare's lovers, Eleanore and Henry survive — but their secret marriage has consequences no less severe than the tragedy of Verona. The concealment of the marriage generates suspicion, forces Eleanore into a silence that appears incriminating, and provides a possible motive for murder.

## Thematic Parallels

- **Secret love and family opposition**: Both works explore the conflict between private affection and public obligation
- **The consequences of concealment**: Secrecy in both works generates tragedy
- **The role of intermediaries**: Friar Laurence and Mr. Raymond both serve as confidants to secret lovers
