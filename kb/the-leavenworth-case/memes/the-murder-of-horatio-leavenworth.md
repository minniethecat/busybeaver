# The Murder of Horatio Leavenworth

The fatal shooting of Horatio Leavenworth in the library of his Fifth Avenue mansion, which sets in motion the entire investigation and narrative of Anna Katharine Green's novel.

## Discovery

The body was discovered on the morning after the murder by the household staff. Horatio Leavenworth was found seated at his desk in the library, dead from a single pistol wound to the back of the head. The discovery is reported to Mr. Raymond and Mr. Veeley, who arrive to find the coroner's inquest already in progress:

> The coroner's inquest being now in session, I felt the full significance of the tragedy for the first time. (001-074)

## Circumstances

The murder took place in a locked room. The library door was locked, and the key was missing — a detail that would become central to the investigation. The weapon — a small Smith & Wesson pistol — was found at the scene.

> There was no key in the door. (003-023)

The butler's reaction to the identification of the pistol type suggests household knowledge of the weapon:

> "A small pistol!" exclaimed the butler. (005-012)

## Investigation

The murder investigation proceeds through the coroner's inquest and the subsequent detective work of Ebenezer Gryce. The investigation examines:
- The locked room and the missing key
- The pistol and its provenance through Bohn & Co.
- The handkerchief stained with pistol residue
- The movements of household members on the night of the murder
- The provisions of Horatio Leavenworth's will

## The Resolution

The murder is ultimately solved through the combination of physical evidence, witness testimony, and deductive reasoning. The resolution reveals not only the identity of the murderer but the complex web of motive — financial, personal, and circumstantial — that led to the crime.
