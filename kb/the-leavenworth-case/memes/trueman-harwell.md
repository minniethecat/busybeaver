# Trueman Harwell

The confidential secretary of Horatio Leavenworth, Trueman Harwell is a key witness in the investigation. His position gave him intimate knowledge of the household and its affairs, making his testimony at the coroner's inquest particularly significant.

## Role in the Household

As Mr. Leavenworth's private secretary, Harwell had access to the library — the room where the murder occurred — and was familiar with the deceased's business affairs. His knowledge of the household routine, the disposition of keys, and the habits of the residents makes him an important source of factual testimony.

## Testimony at the Inquest

Harwell is among the first witnesses called at the coroner's inquest. His examination provides the baseline of factual information about the discovery of the body and the circumstances of the household:

> The interest which the examination of so important a person as the secretary naturally created was great. (003-118)

He testified about the locked library door, the missing key, and the household's movements on the evening before the murder. His examination concluded with a detail that the coroner found significant:

> This remark ended Mr. Harwell's examination for the time. (003-237)

## Evidence and Relevance

Harwell's testimony establishes several key facts about the physical circumstances of the crime. His knowledge of the key — where it was kept, when it was last seen — becomes crucial to the locked-room dimension of the mystery. His recollection of the household's movements helps establish the timeline of events.
