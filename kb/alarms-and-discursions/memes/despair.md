# Despair

**Despair** appears in Chesterton's essays as a philosophical and emotional extreme. He contrasts England's weather with 'our own romantic country' where one does not settle 'down into contentment or despair' (018-001), describes 'an incredulity as strong as despair that my house was actually built' (022-003), and recounts a friend who 'with the lucidity of despair' explained a point (027-010).

## In Chesterton's Essays

Chesterton treats despair not as a psychological affliction but as a philosophical error. In "The Glory of Grey," he contrasts the English climate, which teaches resignation, with the romantic temper that refuses to "settle down into contentment or despair" (018-001). Despair, like its twin contentment, is a form of surrender.

The comic register of despair appears in "The Wings of Stone," where Chesterton confesses "an incredulity as strong as despair that my house was actually built" (022-003). The hyperbole is deliberate. Despair can be the appropriate response to the sheer improbability of ordinary achievements.

In "The Triumph of the Donkey," despair takes on a clarifying function. A friend, "with the lucidity of despair," explains a philosophical point (027-010). The paradox is characteristic: despair, which is usually thought to cloud judgement, here sharpens it. When hope is abandoned, what remains is a terrible clarity.

For Chesterton, despair is ultimately a failure of imagination. To despair is to mistake the present difficulty for the final word, forgetting that the world is still being made.

## Notable Passages

> In France you have much sun and some rain; in Italy you have hot winds and cold winds; in Scotland and Ireland you have rain, either thick or thin; in America you have hells of heat and cold, and in the Tropics you have sunstrokes varied by thunderbolts. But all these you have on a broad and brutal scale, and you settle down into contentment or despair. Only in our own romantic country do you have the strictly romantic thing called Weather; beautiful and changing as a woman.

> (018-001)
