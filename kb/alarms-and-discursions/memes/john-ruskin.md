## In the Corpus

Ruskin appears once in *Alarms and Discursions*, in "The Philosophy of Sight-seeing" (essay 12). Chesterton mentions him in connection with the proper appreciation of tombs and sepulchres:

> (012-011) ...pulchres is not the one which Ruskin was always recommending. It is the spirit of the Sabbath, and not the spirit of the Sketches.

The reference is fleeting but telling. Chesterton contrasts Ruskin's aesthetic recommendations with a deeper principle — the "spirit of the Sabbath," a mode of attention that is reverent rather than merely appreciative. For Chesterton, Ruskin represents the Victorian art-critical tradition that he both inherited and gently satirized.
