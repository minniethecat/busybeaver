## In the Corpus

Chesterton appears by name once in the collected essays, in a wry self-reference from "Ethandune":

> (030-003) ...d happen to address me "G. K. Chesterton, Poste Restante, Ethandune,"...

This passing self-mention locates the author within his own landscape — receiving mail at a Wiltshire post office while walking the ancient battlefield of Ethandune. The gesture is characteristically Chestertonian: the author is present in his essays not as a distant narrator but as a figure in the scene, a "man walking" through the English countryside.

## Editorial Note

This page serves as the author entry for the wiki. *Alarms and Discursions* is a collection of 39 essays originally published in the *Daily News* (London) and collected in book form in 1910. Chesterton does not discuss himself at length in these essays — his method is to observe the world, not to confess — so the corpus evidence for this page is necessarily thin. Readers seeking fuller biographical treatment should consult Chesterton's *Autobiography* (1936) or Maisie Ward's *Gilbert Keith Chesterton* (1944).
