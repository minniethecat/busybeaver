# Oxford

Oxford appears twice in *Alarms and Discursions*, both as incidental references. In [[The Man and His Newspaper]], Chesterton begins his journey "at a little station, which I decline to specify, somewhere between Oxford and Guildford" where he misses a connection and is left stranded with a copy of the *Daily Wire* (006-001). In [[The Gold of Glastonbury]], Chesterton uses Oxford as a shorthand for academic credibility, arguing that an educated follower of a religious prophet would be unsurprising: "no one who knows the modern world would be surprised if one of his closest followers were a Professor from Heidelberg or an M.A. from Oxford" (015-005).

Oxford receives no sustained treatment in the essays; it functions solely as a geographical reference point and a metonym for scholarly authority.
