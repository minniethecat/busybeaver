# Liana

**Lianas** (known locally as _cipós_) are long-stemmed, woody climbing plants that festoon the trees of the [[Amazon]] rainforest. In _The Giant Raft_, a liana of the cipó kind becomes the centerpiece of a jungle exploration scene when [[Benito]], [[Minha]], [[Lina]], and [[Fragoso]] follow one to its end [007-068].

## Role in the Amazon Ecosystem

Lianas are parasitic plants that twist around the trunks and branches of Amazonian trees, creating dense networks of vegetation. In the region near the [[Iquitos Fazenda]], the trees were described as "joined to each other by festoons of lianas, which here and there have made the forest almost impenetrable" [003-004]. These climbing plants, along with bromelias and other epiphytes, form an intricate layer of the rainforest canopy.

In the [[Lower Amazon]], lianas intertwine with the giant trees, curling around branches and forming natural bridges between them. The narrative notes that the forest floor is often "so overgrown with verdure, over which the liana twisted like a serpent" that passage through the jungle requires determination and a sharp blade [007-096].

## The Liana Scene (Chapter 7)

During a stop along the [[Amazon]] journey, [[Lina]] spots a particular liana of the _cipós_ kind and proposes a game:

> "You see that liana?" And [[Lina]] pointed to a liana of the cipós kind, twisted round a gigantic tree trunk. "Perhaps," said she, "that we try to follow that liana to its very end." [007-068]

[[Benito]] agrees enthusiastically: "to follow this liana, no matter what may be the obstacles!" [007-072]. [[Minha]] joins the game — "Let us follow the liana!" [007-075] — and even the reluctant [[Fragoso]] concedes: "I obey. Let us follow the liana!" [007-079].

The party follows the liana as it runs along the ground, climbs into the trees, drops back to the forest floor, and curls around high branches. At one point, it forms a natural bridge "made of bejucos, twined together" that crosses a stream [007-105]. After much effort, the liana leads them back to the riverbank where they had started — [[Lina]] had tied the other end to a tree at the camp, and the supposed discovery was a game all along [007-146].

## Lianas as Natural Bridges

The [[Amazon]] Indians use lianas (bejucos) to construct suspension bridges across rivers and streams. These "bridges of lianas" allow passage over waterways when canoes are unavailable [007-105]. The flexible, supple nature of the cipó vine makes it an ideal material for rope, cord, and temporary construction in the jungle.
