# Monte Alegre

## Overview

Monte Alegre is a Brazilian town on the left bank of the [[Lower Amazon]], situated between [[Óbidos]] and the mouth of the Xingu River. In the novel, it appears as one of the final settlements the [[Jangada]] passes on its long voyage down the great river, its banks lined with extensive cocoa plantations.

## Geography

Monte Alegre lies in the [[Lower Amazon]] region, downstream from [[Óbidos]] — the narrowest point of the [[Amazon]] — and upstream from the mouth of the Xingu River. The town sits on the left bank of the Amazon, in what is now the Brazilian state of Pará. The surrounding landscape is characterized by cocoa-tree plantations that stretch along both banks of the river in this region [040-011].

## In the Novel

Monte Alegre appears in the closing chapter of the [[Jangada]]'s voyage. After passing [[Óbidos]], the raft glides between "interminable plantations of cocoa-trees with their somber green flanked by the yellow thatch or ruddy tiles of the roofs of the huts of the settlers on both banks from Obidos up to the town of Monte Alegre" [040-011].

Shortly afterward, the [[Jangada]] passes Monte Alegre itself, then the town of Pravnha de Onteiro, and finally the mouth of the Xingu River — "frequented by Yurumas Indians, whose principal industry consists in preparing their enemies' heads for natural history cabinets" [040-020]. By this stage of the voyage, the Atlantic tide is already making itself felt, somewhat checking the raft's progress.

## Journey Role

Monte Alegre is one of the last named settlements on the [[Amazon]] before the river broadens into its vast estuary. Its passing marks the [[Jangada]]'s entry into the final leg of the journey, with the Atlantic Ocean now within reach. The cocoa plantations surrounding the town represent the agricultural colonization of the [[Lower Amazon]] in the 19th century.
