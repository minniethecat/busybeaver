# Magalhães

Magalhães was a Portuguese timber-feller and the original proprietor of the [[Fazenda]] at [[Iquitos]], on the [[Upper Amazon]]. He was the father of [[Yaquita]], the father-in-law of [[Joam Garral]] ([[Joam Dacosta]]), and the man whose act of hospitality toward a starving fugitive set the entire story of *The [[Giant Raft]]* in motion.

## Character

Magalhães was "like all the Portuguese of the old race" — hospitable, hard-working, and possessed of "an excellent heart" (003-011). He was an excellent worker, inured to fatigue, but "lacking education" — he understood the management of his few slaves and hired Indians, but showed himself "much less" capable in the business affairs that required literacy and formal training (003-011).

His most defining trait was his generosity of spirit. When he found [[Joam Garral]] — then a twenty-two-year-old fugitive — half-dead with hunger and fatigue in the neighboring forest, Magalhães "did not ask the unknown where he came from, but what he wanted" (003-014). He recognized in Joam the qualities of intelligence, education, and upright character that he himself lacked, and he asked the young man to remain at the farm.

## Role in the Story

### The Fazenda at Iquitos

Magalhães followed the trade of timber-felling, and his settlement extended for about half a mile along the bank of the [[Amazon River]] (003-010). He lived with his daughter [[Yaquita]], who after the death of her mother had taken charge of his household.

When [[Joam Garral]] arrived in 1826, the [[Fazenda]]'s affairs were "getting somewhat embarrassed" (003-014). Magalhães needed the kind of business acumen and education that Joam possessed.

### Partnership with Joam Garral

Recognizing Joam's talents, Magalhães asked him to remain at the farm, where he would "in a measure, supply that which was wanting in the worthy farmer" (003-014). Joam entered resolutely into the service of the [[Fazenda]], and Magalhães "had no cause to regret his generous action" (003-017). The business recovered, and the wood trade extended down the [[Amazon]] as far as Pará.

### Death and Marriage Blessing

The turning point came when Magalhães was superintending a clearance and was "mortally wounded by the fall of a tree" (003-023). Carried home, feeling himself to be dying, he insisted that Joam marry his daughter [[Yaquita]]. Joam at first refused, offering to remain her protector without becoming her husband: "I owe you all, Magalhães. I will never forget it, but the price you would pay for my endeavors is out of all proportion to what they are worth" (003-025).

But Magalhães was insistent. [[Yaquita]] was then twenty-two, Joam was twenty-six. They loved each other, and they were married "some hours before the death of Magalhães, who had just strength left to bless their union" (003-027). It was under these circumstances that [[Joam Garral]] became the new fazender of [[Iquitos]] in 1830.

## Legacy

Magalhães's act of hospitality created the entire world of the Garral family. By taking in the fugitive [[Joam Dacosta]] without questions, he set in motion a chain of events that would span twenty-six years — from the prosperity of the [[Iquitos Fazenda]] to the eventual journey down the [[Amazon]] on the [[Giant Raft]], and ultimately to Joam's arrest, trial, and vindication.

His daughter [[Yaquita]] proved herself, in moments of crisis, "the valiant daughter of Magalhães, the worthy wife of the fazender of [[Iquitos]]" (035-016). [[Joam Garral]], facing his past in the prison at [[Manaus]], reproached himself for never having confessed to Magalhães who he really was — "above all, at the moment when Magalhães had placed in his hand the hand of the daughter who would never have believed that he was the author of so frightful a crime" (037-009).

The [[Fazenda]] itself became known in the region by Magalhães's name, with people referring to "the farm of old Magalhães" (013-056). Even twenty-six years after his death, his reputation as a "hospitable fazender" endured in the memories of all who knew the Garral family's history (037-007).

## Relationships

- **[[Yaquita]]**: Daughter, took charge of household after her mother's death
- **[[Joam Garral]] ([[Joam Dacosta]])**: Son-in-law, partner, and successor as fazender
- **[[Benito Garral]]**: Grandson
- **[[Minha Garral]]**: Granddaughter
- **[[Cybele]]**: Longtime servant who remained in Magalhães's service after his death (003-044)
- **[[Padre Passanha]]**: The priest who later married Joam and [[Yaquita]] — he was the one who "had married the daughter of Farmer Magalhães to the clerk who had been received at the [[Fazenda]]" (009-027)
