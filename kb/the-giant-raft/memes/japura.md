# Japurá River

## Overview

The Japurá River (also spelled Japura, and historically Yapura) is a major left-bank tributary of the [[Amazon]]. In *The [[Giant Raft]]*, its mouth is described as being of immense width — six thousand six hundred feet — and the river enters the Amazon in the middle reaches, downstream of [[Tabatinga]].

## In the Novel

The Japurá region is first mentioned in connection with its dense forests [005-045]. As the [[Jangada]] descends the [[Amazon]], the Rio Japurá is identified as entering the main stream some miles downstream of another tributary [015-033]. The narrative pauses to awe at the river's mouth: "The Rio Japura, whose mouth was forty-eight hours distant, measured no less than six thousand six hundred feet across" — an extraordinary width that makes it "appear like a gulf or an arm of the sea" [015-049]. The mouth of the Japurá is noted again at six thousand six hundred feet wide near the island of Arenapo [016-071]. Downstream, the river passes the island of Yapura, after which "the river, less encumbered, opened more freely" [016-072].
