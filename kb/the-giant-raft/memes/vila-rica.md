# Vila Rica

## Overview

Vila Rica (present-day Ouro Preto) is an inland colonial town in the Brazilian province
of Minas Gerais, far from the [[Amazon River]] but central to the backstory of
*The [[Giant Raft]]*. It was here that [[Joam Dacosta]] worked as a young advocate
[023-003], defended himself against charges of [[Diamond]] smuggling from the [[Tijuco]]
district [024-069], and was condemned to death by a jury whose verdict was given
unanimously [025-040]. Dacosta escaped from the prison at Vila Rica under
mysterious conditions [020-008] [037-007], fleeing to Peru where he rebuilt his
life under the alias [[Joam Garral]]. The iniquitous judgment delivered at Vila Rica
[023-020] hung over him for decades, until his innocence was at length admitted
[039-059].

The town serves as the origin point of the [[Diamond]] convoy that the smuggler
[[Torres]] later leverages as proof of Dacosta's true identity. Though never visited
directly in the novel's present-tense [[Amazon]] journey, Vila Rica is repeatedly
invoked in the trial sequence and flashback expositions (chapters 19 through 39),
making it one of the most-referenced off-river locations in the narrative.
