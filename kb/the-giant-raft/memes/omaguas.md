# Omaguas

## Overview

The Omaguas are an indigenous people of the [[Upper Amazon]] basin whose name, meaning
"flatheads," derives from the custom of flattening the foreheads of their children
[014-032]. They appear in *The [[Giant Raft]]* among the riverine populations of the
Peruvian [[Amazon]], listed alongside the Ticunas as groups of mixed descent with
Spanish influence [003-003].

A [[Mission]] settlement, San Joachim d'Omaguas, stands where the [[Upper Amazon]] basin
narrows near the Peruvian frontier [005-026]. The village of Omaguas lies on the
[[Jangada]]'s upstream route; the great raft passes the village and the mouth of the
Ambiacu River as it navigates toward the Brazilian border [010-053]. The Omaguas'
historical territory encompasses a stretch of the upper river through which the
Garral party must travel before reaching [[Tabatinga]].

> It has been the country of the Omaguas, whose name means "flatheads," on
> account of a practice of flattening the foreheads of their children. [014-032]
