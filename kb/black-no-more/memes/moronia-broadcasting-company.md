# Moronia Broadcasting Company

The **Moronia Broadcasting Company** is a satirically named radio
network in *Black No More* that disseminates political speeches and
racial propaganda to a national audience. Its name — a portmanteau of
"moron" and the place-name suffix "-ia" — exemplifies
[[george-schuyler]]'s caustic view of mass media as an instrument of
mass manipulation. The network served as the essential broadcast
infrastructure for the [[knights-of-nordica]]'s 1934 political campaign,
enabling [[rev-givens]] to reach an audience estimated at one hundred
million citizens (009-019).

## In the Novel

The Moronia Broadcasting Company appears in the political chapters
surrounding the national election, providing the infrastructure through
which [[rev-givens]] and the [[knights-of-nordica]] reach a national
audience. Matthew Fisher, as Grand Exalted Giraw, orchestrated the radio
strategy: "First, we get Givens on the radio; national hookup, you know"
(009-005). When Bunny Brown asked "When do we start this radio racket?"
(009-016), Fisher replied that he would coordinate with Rev. Givens.

The broadcast itself was a carefully staged media event:

> The following Thursday evening at 8:15 p.m. millions of people sat before their loud speakers, expectantly awaiting the heralded address to the nation by the Imperial Grand Wizard of the Knights of Nordica. (009-018)

Mortimer K. Shanker, the announcer, opened the program from Station WHAT in Atlanta:

> "Good evening, ladies and gentlemen of the radio audience. This is Station W H A T, Atlanta, GA, Mortimer K. Shanker announcing. This evening we are offering a program of tremendous interest to every American citizen."
> "The countrywide hookup over the chain of the Moronia Broadcasting Company is enabling one hundred million citizens to hear one of the most significant messages ever delivered to the American public." (009-019)

The program featured entertainment interludes — Jack Albert singing
"Vanishing Mammy" backed by Sammy Snort's Bogalusa Babies (009-020) —
before Givens delivered his address on "The Menace of Negro Blood"
(009-028). The juxtaposition of minstrel entertainment with political
propaganda exemplified Schuyler's satirical conflation of racial
politics and commercial spectacle.

The closing announcement promised a weekly series:

> "Now friends, this is Mortimer K. Shanker again, announcing from Station W H A T, Atlanta, GA, with a nationwide hookup over the chain of the Moronia Broadcasting Company."
> "You have just heard a scholarly and inspiring address by Rev. Henry Givens, Imperial Grand Wizard of the Knights of Nordica on 'The Menace of Negro Blood.' Rev. Givens will deliver another address at this station a week from tonight." (009-028)

## Political Function

The Moronia Broadcasting Company was central to the Knights of Nordica's
political strategy. The network's nationwide reach allowed the
organization to bypass local political structures and speak directly to
millions of white voters. [[rev-givens]]'s frequent radio addresses
became a fixture of the political landscape (010-033). The Knights had
also purchased "a radio broadcasting station" as part of their
infrastructure investment (005-025), suggesting that the Moronia network
was integrated with the organization's own media apparatus. This
ownership of both the political message and its means of distribution
gave the Knights an asymmetric advantage over competing organizations
like the [[anglo-saxon-association]].

The radio campaign proved effective in shaping public opinion ahead of
the national election. By framing racial anxiety in the language of
scientific authority — borrowing Matthew Fisher's anthropological
credentials — and packaging it as mass entertainment, the broadcasts
mobilized white voters who might otherwise have remained indifferent to
the Knights' message. The campaign culminated in the election of
President Goosie, whose administration would later attempt to implement
the genealogical examination laws (010-033).

## Satirical Significance

### The Name

Schuyler's choice of "Moronia" is a characteristically direct satirical
device. The name suggests that the network's audience — the American
public — is being addressed as, and perhaps constituted as, a nation
of morons. This aligns with the novel's broader critique of how easily
the American public is manipulated by racial propaganda.

### Media as Infrastructure of Race-Making

The Moronia Broadcasting Company represents the **media
infrastructure** that makes racial politics possible at a national
scale. Without radio networks to broadcast speeches, political rallies
and racial propaganda would remain local affairs. The network is thus
an essential component of the novel's depiction of race as an
**industrialized, mass-mediated phenomenon** — not a natural fact but a
product manufactured and distributed through modern communication
technologies.

### Parallel to Real-World Media

The name satirizes real broadcasting companies of the 1930s (such as
the Columbia Broadcasting System and the National Broadcasting
Company) that played significant roles in shaping American public
opinion. Schuyler, a working journalist, was intimately familiar with
the media landscape and skeptical of its democratic pretensions. The
call letters "W H A T" — spelling out the word "what" — further
underscore the novel's suggestion that mass media content is empty
noise dressed as news.
