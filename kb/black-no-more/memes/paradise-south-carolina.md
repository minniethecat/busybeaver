## Overview

Paradise, South Carolina, is the site of the Blickdoff-Hortzenboff cotton mill and the company town that surrounds it — a location that appears in the novel as the setting for Matthew Fisher's intervention in a labor dispute that reveals the class logic beneath the Knights of Nordica's racial ideology. The town's name, like that of "Happy Hill," is saturated with Schuyler's characteristic irony: Paradise is a company town where workers earn "around fifteen dollars a week" for an "eleven hours" workday (007-007).[PN:re-psc-001]

## Economic Structure

Paradise is a classic company town: the mill, "owned by two Germans who came to this country after the war," employs "one thousand hands," owns "all of the houses in Paradise," and operates "all of the stores" (007-007). This total economic control means that labor grievances in Paradise are fundamentally about who owns the means of survival — a reality that the Knights of Nordica's racial rhetoric is designed to obscure.[PN:re-psc-001]

## The Labor Conflict

When the mill "speeded up the work so much that the help say they cannot stand the pace," the workers — predominantly white members of the Knights of Nordica — turn to their organization for help unionizing. Matthew Fisher's response is revealing: he refuses to support the workers against the mill owners, exposing the Knights as an organization that protects capital, not white labor. The Paradise conflict is the novel's most explicit dramatization of Schuyler's argument that racial solidarity is a tool used by the powerful to divide workers who share common economic interests.[PN:re-psc-001]

## Narrative Significance

Paradise is one of the few Southern locations in the novel given sustained attention, and its function is expressly political. Unlike Harlem (cultural capital) or Atlanta (organizational headquarters), Paradise is a site of production — and the labor conflict there strips away the ideological veneer of the Knights of Nordica's white supremacy to reveal its economic foundation.[PN:re-psc-001]
