# Senator Bosh

Senator Bosh of Mississippi is a minor character in [[Black No More]] who appears only in the novel's epilogue ([[Chapter 13|ch13]]). As a politician facing reelection, he exploits the racial panic triggered by [[Crookman's Monograph]] for electoral advantage.

## Political Opportunism

"Senator Bosh of Mississippi, about to run again for office, referred several times to it in the Congressional Record, his remarks interspersed with 'Applauses'" (013-120). Bosh's references to Crookman's finding — that new Caucasians were "whiter than white" — represent the standard political response to the ancestry crisis: leveraging racial anxiety for electoral gain. The stage direction "Applauses" in the Congressional Record underscores the performative nature of his outrage.

## Narrative Function

Bosh is one of several political figures whom [[Schuyler]] deploys in the epilogue to illustrate how the ancestry revelations reshape the American political landscape. Like [[Senator Kretin]], Bosh transforms scientific data into political theater, demonstrating the novel's thesis that racial ideology is primarily a tool of elite political mobilization.
