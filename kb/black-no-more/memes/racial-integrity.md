Racial integrity — the preservation of supposed racial purity against contamination — is one of the few concepts in [[black-no-more-treatment|Black No More]] that Schuyler satirizes from both directions simultaneously. White supremacists invoke it to justify segregation and anti-miscegenation laws; Black race leaders invoke it to discourage their constituents from taking the [[black-no-more-treatment|Black-No-More treatment]]. The symmetry is the point: "racial integrity" is a fiction that serves the class interests of whoever deploys it, regardless of which race they claim to be defending.

## White Racial Integrity: The Knights of Nordica

The [[knights-of-nordica|Knights of Nordica]] organize around the slogan "Join in the Fight for White Race Integrity" [009-018]. Their recruitment poster frames the threat in explicitly biological terms: "The racial integrity of the Caucasian Race is being threatened by the activities of a scientific black Beelzebub in New York" [009-018]. The language is apocalyptic — the very existence of the Caucasian race is at stake — but the novel undercuts this rhetoric by revealing the economic and political interests beneath it. Racial integrity is, for the Knights, a fundraising mechanism and a mobilizing slogan, not a genuine biological concern.

> The racial integrity of the Caucasian Race is being threatened by the activities of a scientific black Beelzebub in New York. [009-018]

## Matthew Fisher's Cynicism

[[max-disher|Matthew Fisher]] — the novel's most clear-eyed observer — immediately recognizes racial integrity discourse as instrumental: "Unlike Givens, he had no belief in the racial integrity nonsense nor any confidence in the white masses whom he thought were destined to flock to the Knights of Nordica. On the contrary he despised and hated them" [009-035]. Fisher's cynicism is the novel's own position: racial integrity is "nonsense," useful only as a tool for manipulating the credulous. That Fisher can rise to the top of an organization built on an ideology he despises is Schuyler's verdict on the relationship between belief and power in racial politics.

> Unlike Givens, he had no belief in the racial integrity nonsense nor any confidence in the white masses. [009-035]

## Black Racial Integrity: The Mirror Image

Schuyler extends the satire by creating a mirror organization: the Committee for the Preservation of Negro Racial Integrity, chaired by [[shakespeare-agamemnon-beard|Dr. Shakespeare Agamemnon Beard]] [009-051]. The committee's very existence is the joke — the same logic of racial preservation that drives the Knights of Nordica is reproduced, with reversed valence, by Black leaders who see the Black-No-More treatment as a threat to their constituency. [[santop-licorice|Santop Licorice]], head of the Back-to-Africa Society, is "always delighted when Dr. Beard met with any sort of rebuff" and is "doubly pleased in this instance because he had been overlooked in the sending out of invitations" [009-051]. Racial solidarity, Schuyler suggests, is as much about institutional competition as about genuine commitment.

## The Legal Architecture

The [[anglo-saxon|Anglo-Saxon]] Association's [[arthur-snobbcraft|Arthur Snobbcraft]] is "the genius that thought up the numerous racial integrity laws adopted in Virginia and many of the other Southern states" [009-053]. These laws — the real-world Racial Integrity Act of 1924 was indeed enacted in Virginia — form the legal infrastructure of the color line. The novel's satire gains force from its proximity to actual legislation: Schuyler is not inventing absurdity but documenting it. The racial integrity laws that Snobbcraft devises are the same ones that, in the novel's world, the Black-No-More treatment renders unenforceable — not by repeal but by making the legal category of race impossible to apply.

## The Collapse of Integrity

The novel's climax demonstrates that racial integrity was always a performative claim rather than a biological reality. Dr. Buggerie's genealogical research reveals that "as many as twenty million people in the United States who possessed some slight non-Nordic strain" [009-064] — and when his own ancestry is exposed as including Negro blood, the professional Anglo-Saxon is revealed as a fraud. Racial integrity, pursued to its logical conclusion, destroys itself: the more rigorously you search for purity, the more impurity you find. The concept that organized the novel's social world turns out to have no referent outside the power relations it sustained.
