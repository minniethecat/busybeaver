## Overview

Dr. Shakespeare Agamemnon Beard is Schuyler's most devastating satire of an entire class: the professional Negro leader whose livelihood depends on the perpetuation of racial grievance. A Harvard–Yale–Copenhagen graduate with a "haughty bearing" that "never failed to impress both Caucasians and Negroes," Beard edits *The Dilemma* and writes "scholarly and biting editorials denouncing the Caucasians whom he secretly admired and lauding the greatness of the Negroes whom he alternately pitied and despised" (005-049). When Black-No-More threatens the economic basis of Negro leadership, Beard organizes the Committee for the Preservation of Negro Racial Integrity — and when the racial order inverts at the novel's end, he resurfaces as "Karl von Beerde," founder of the Down-With-White-Prejudice-League, proving that the racial grievance industry is transferable across color lines.

The name itself is a joke: "Shakespeare Agamemnon" combines literary canon and classical epic into absurd overreach, while "Beard" suggests patriarchal wisdom. His mixed ancestry — "part-French, part-Russian, part-Indian, part-Negro" — is recited as a credential, satirizing the Talented Tenth's obsession with pedigree and proximity to whiteness.

## Biography

Beard is introduced in Chapter 5 presiding over a meeting of Negro leaders in the N.S.E.L.'s private office. His staff has already drafted the resolution to the Attorney General because "no member of it believed that the other Negro leaders possessed sufficient education to word the document effectively and grammatically" (005-049). His intellectual condescension toward his peers matches his studied distance from the black masses whose "sufferings and privations" he chronicles "in limpid prose" while remaining "totally and thankfully unfamiliar" with their actual lives (005-049).

The portrait is devastatingly precise. Beard "bitterly denounced the Nordics for debauching Negro women while taking care to hire comely yellow stenographers with weak resistance" (005-049). He is "a Pink Socialist" in peacetime who "bivouacked at the feet of Mars" when war clouds gather (005-049). Every detail — the glass-topped desk, the gold-rimmed fountain pen, the half-closed eyelids — constructs the image of a man who performs radicalism from behind a safely bourgeois barricade.

When the anti-Black-No-More meeting descends into an emotional revival, Beard watches "cold and disdainful," toying with his pen, then interrupts with a cynical proposal: the leaders should accept money from the Knights of Nordica to lecture against Black-No-More — because the organization's real aim (preserving white supremacy) is aligned with their economic interest in maintaining racial segregation. When Rev. Gronne objects — "But Doctor Beard, isn't this a rather unusual procedure?" — Beard's reply is aristocratically dismissive: "Rev. Gronne, it's not near as unusual as Black-No-More. I have probably ruffled your dignity but that's nothing to what Dr. Crookman will do" (005-074–005-075).

> They looked at Dr. Beard and each other in amazement. (005-072)

After Black-No-More devastates the Negro leadership class, Beard is among those targeted by Bunny Brown to lecture for the Knights of Nordica (010-100). His economic desperation — once the market for Negro grievance collapses — makes him a willing collaborator with the very forces he once denounced.

## Karl von Beerde: The Shape-Shifter

Beard's transformation in the epilogue is the novel's most explicit statement about the fungibility of racial politics. Resurfacing as "Karl von Beerde" — the name a Teutonic pastiche of his old persona — he founds the Down-With-White-Prejudice-League to advocate for the newly-pale minority, "whom some accused of being the same Doctor Beard who had, as a Negro, once headed the National Social Equality League" (013-120). The accusation is left unresolved, but the implication is clear: Beard's skills — scholarly rhetoric, moral posturing, institutional fundraising — are transferable across any color line. The grievance industry needs only grievance; the color of the aggrieved is a matter of market conditions.

## Satirical Function

Beard embodies the novel's critique of what Schuyler saw as a self-serving racial leadership class. His CV, his affect, his politics, his erotic preferences, and his ultimate transformation into "Karl von Beerde" — every detail satirizes a particular hypocrisy of the Talented Tenth. The final joke is that Beard survives and prospers regardless of which race is on top, because his real business is not racial justice but racial brokerage.

## Key Episodes

> In a very private inner office of the N.S.E.L. suite, Dr. Shakespeare Agamemnon Beard, founder of the League and a graduate of Harvard, Yale and Copenhagen (whose haughty bearing never failed to impress both Caucasians and Negroes) sat before a glass-topped desk, rubbing now his curly gray head, and now his full spade beard. (005-048)

> He bitterly denounced the Nordics for debauching Negro women while taking care to hire comely yellow stenographers with weak resistance. In a real way, he loved his people. In time of peace he was a Pink Socialist but when the clouds of war gathered he bivouacked at the feet of Mars. (005-049)

> Bishop Whooper was about to start up again, when Dr. Beard, who had sat cold and disdainful through this outbreak of revivalism, toying with his gold-rimmed fountain pen and gazing at the exhibition through half-closed eyelids, interrupted in sharp metallic tones. (005-070)

> "Twenty-one, aren't you?" sneered Beard. "Well, then be men enough to act like it." (005-073)

> "Rev. Gronne," the great man replied, "it's not near as unusual as Black-No-More." (005-075)

> The Down-With-White-Prejudice-League was founded by one Karl von Beerde, whom some accused of being the same Doctor Beard who had, as a Negro, once headed the National Social Equality League. (013-120)
