## Overview

On Easter Sunday, 1934, two major events cemented the Knights of Nordica's dominance in white supremacist politics: a mass meeting celebrating the organization's first anniversary and the enrollment of its one-millionth member, followed immediately by the wedding of Helen Givens — daughter of the Imperial Grand Wizard — to Matthew Fisher, the organization's newly elevated Grand Exalted Giraw (006-001). The twin events mark the peak of the Knights' institutional power in the novel.

## Context

In the months leading up to Easter 1934, the [[Black-No-More]] sanitariums operated by [[black-no-more-inc]] had processed hundreds of thousands of Black Americans into whiteness, creating widespread social panic among white supremacists. The Knights of Nordica, founded by Reverend Givens, had grown rapidly by capitalizing on this fear. Matthew Fisher — formerly Max Disher, a Black man from [[harlem]] who took the treatment and reinvented himself as a white anthropologist — had risen through the Knights' ranks. His courtship of Helen Givens paralleled the organization's ascent.

Months earlier, on the previous Easter, Max (still Black) had wandered Atlanta hoping to glimpse Helen after she rebuffed him at a New Year's Eve party with the words "I never dance with niggers" (004-001). The transformation that Black-No-More enabled now positioned him to marry into the very family that embodied white supremacist ideology.

### The Knights' Growth

Rev. Henry Givens, Imperial Grand Wizard of the Knights of Nordica, was "a short, wizened, almost-bald, bull-voiced, ignorant ex-evangelist" who had previously helped organize the Ku Klux Klan following the Great War (004-030). Recognizing Matthew Fisher as "a valuable asset to the Knights of Nordica" (004-038), Givens invited him to address a mass meeting, where Fisher's performance impressed the membership and drew the attention of Helen Givens (004-050). The advertisement that first drew Matthew's interest — "The Knights of Nordica Want 10,000 Atlanta White Men and Women to Join in the Fight for White Race Integrity" — appeared in a newspaper on the Monday after Easter the previous year (004-010).

## Narrative Account

### The Mass Meeting

The Easter Sunday program opened with a massive rally at the Knights' newly constructed reinforced concrete auditorium at Nordica Hall. The venue itself embodied the organization's rapid rise:

> The Knights of Nordica's flag-draped auditorium slowly filled. It was a bare, cavernous structure, with sawdust on the floor, a big platform at one end, row after row of folding wooden chairs and illuminated by large, white lights hanging from the rafters. On the platform was a row of five chairs, the center one being high-backed and gilded. On the lectern downstage was a bulky bible. A huge American flag was stretched across the rear wall. (004-064)

The gathering served the dual purpose of celebrating the secret society's first anniversary and announcing that it had enrolled its one-millionth member — a milestone that signaled the Knights' transformation from a fringe group into a mass political movement (006-001). The million-member mark also represented a political threat: the organization could now credibly claim to represent a mass white constituency.

### The Wedding

Immediately following the rally, Helen Givens and Matthew Fisher were married. Fisher, whose title Grand Exalted Giraw marked him as a senior officer, had fully completed his metamorphosis from Max Disher, the Harlem dilettante, into a white supremacist aristocrat (006-001). The wedding joined Fisher to the Givens dynasty and secured his position within the organization's leadership.

Earlier that spring, Fisher had addressed the Knights at Nordica Hall as a featured speaker, delivering "the most astounding news" about the racial threat — a performance that won him the admiration of the membership and the notice of Helen Givens (004-028).

> "Oh, Honey," he gurgled, "I think this here Knights of Nordica is going over big; going over big! My fame is spreading. Only today I had a long talk with a famous anthropologist from New York and he's going to address our mass meeting tonight." (004-050)

## Aftermath

### Political Consolidation

The Easter Sunday events consolidated the Knights of Nordica's political power and Matthew Fisher's personal standing. His marriage to Helen made him son-in-law to the Imperial Grand Wizard, while the million-member milestone gave the organization credible claim to represent a mass constituency. These developments set the stage for the Knights' subsequent legislative campaigns — the genealogical examination laws and anti-intermarriage statutes — that dominate the novel's middle chapters.

However, Fisher's success bred jealousy within the organization:

> Matthew's great success as an organizer and his increasing popularity was not viewed by Rev. Givens with equanimity. The former evangelist knew that everybody of intelligence in the upper circles of the order realized that the growth and prosperity of the Knights of Nordica was largely due to the industry, efficiency and intelligence of Matthew. He had been told that many people were saying that Fisher ought to be Imperial Grand Wizard instead of Grand Exalted Giraw. (006-013)

### Bunny Brown's Return

A week or two after the wedding, Matthew received a visitor at his office — Bunny Brown, his old friend from Harlem, who had also taken the Black-No-More treatment and was now passing as white. Brown whispered, "Don't recognize me, do you Max?" — reconnecting Fisher to his hidden past (006-015). Matthew immediately made Bunny his private secretary: "from now on you're private secretary to the Grand Exalted Giraw; that's me" (006-056). This arrangement gave Fisher a trusted confidant inside the Knights' headquarters while concealing the racial secret that would undo him if exposed.

### Broader Context

The Easter Sunday events occurred against the backdrop of the largest migration of African Americans in the nation's history, as Southern Blacks flooded northward to reach Black-No-More sanitariums (005-044). The Knights' million-member milestone was thus as much a reaction to demographic panic as it was an organizational achievement — the organization fed on the very fear that the whitening treatment was generating.

## Key Passages

> Two important events took place on Easter Sunday, 1934. The first was a huge mass meeting in the brand new reinforced concrete auditorium of the Knights of Nordica for the double purpose of celebrating the first anniversary of the militant secret society and the winning of the millionth member. The second event was the wedding of Helen Givens and Matthew Fisher, Grand Exalted Giraw of the Knights of Nordica. (006-001)

> Matthew Fisher, alias Max Disher, joined the Easter Sunday crowds, twirling his malacca stick and ogling the pretty flappers who passed giggling in their Spring finery. For nearly three months he had idled around the Georgia capital hoping to catch a glimpse of the beautiful girl who on New Year's Eve had told him "I never dance with niggers." (004-001)

> Rev. Henry Givens, Imperial Grand Wizard of the Knights of Nordica, was a short, wizened, almost-bald, bull-voiced, ignorant ex-evangelist, who had come originally from the hilly country north of Atlanta. He had helped in the organization of the Ku Klux Klan following the Great War and had worked with a zeal only equalled by his thankfulness to God for escaping from the precarious existence of an itinerant saver of souls. (004-030)

> Rev. Givens saw. He nodded his head as Matthew, now glorying in his newly-discovered eloquence made point after point, and concluded that this pale, dapper young fellow, with his ready tongue, his sincerity, his scientific training and knowledge of the situation ought to prove a valuable asset to the Knights of Nordica. (004-038)
