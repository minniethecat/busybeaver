## Overview

Colonel Mortimer Roberts — the title "Colonel" having been bestowed by "some white man" and sticking — is Schuyler's most concentrated satire of the accommodationist Negro leader. As principal of the Dusky River Agricultural Institute, Supreme General of the Knights and Daughters of Kingdom Come, and president of the Uncle Tom Memorial Association, Roberts accumulates titles that mock the institutional apparatus of Negro conservatism. He is described as "a great mountain of blackness with a head shaped like an upturned bucket, pierced by two pig-like eyes and a cavernous mouth equipped with large tombstone teeth" — a physical caricature that literalizes his grotesque political posture. His speech "conveyed to most white people an impression of rugged simplicity and sincerity, which was very fortunate since Colonel Roberts maintained his school through their contributions."

## Key Episodes

> Colonel Roberts was the acknowledged leader of the conservative Negroes (most of whom had nothing to conserve) who felt at all times that the white folks were in the lead and that Negroes should be careful to guide themselves accordingly. (005-049)

> His speech was a cross between the woofing of a bloodhound and the explosion of an inner tube. It conveyed to most white people an impression of rugged simplicity and sincerity, which was very fortunate since Colonel Roberts maintained his school through their contributions. (005-049)

> He spoke as usual about the cordial relations existing between the two races in his native Georgia, the effrontery of Negroes who dared whiten themselves and thus disturb the minds of white people and insinuated alliance with certain militant organizations in the South to stop this whitening business before it went too far. (005-049)
