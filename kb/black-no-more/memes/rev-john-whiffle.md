# Rev. John Whiffle

**Rev. John Whiffle** is a clergyman and political operative in
*Black No More* who participates in the white supremacist political
convention that selects the presidential and vice-presidential
candidates.

## Role in the Novel

Whiffle appears in the political negotiations of chapter 10, where
delegates from the [[knights-of-nordica]] and the
[[anglo-saxon-association]] gather to select their ticket. He is
described as a significant behind-the-scenes force:

> Rev. John Whiffle, a power in the party, gulped...
> ([010-037](010-037))

When challenged on the viability of the ticket, Whiffle adds a
pragmatic voice to the proceedings:

> "And how," Rev. Whiffle chimed in, "can you get anywhere..."
> ([010-044](010-044))

## Character Function

Whiffle represents the **clerical dimension of organized white
supremacy** in Schuyler's satire. Like [[rev-givens]] (who leads the
Knights of Nordica), Whiffle demonstrates how religious authority and
political power operate in tandem within the novel's racial
organizations. His title "Reverend" grants him moral legitimacy while
he participates in the grubby mechanics of political deal-making.

This coupling of clerical status with political manipulation is a
recurring satirical device in the novel, exposing what Schuyler saw
as the hypocrisy of religious figures who lent spiritual sanction to
white supremacist politics.

## Political Alignment

Whiffle is allied with [[simeon-dump]] and [[arthur-snobbcraft]] in
the Anglo-Saxon Association faction, opposing the Knights of Nordica's
candidates during the convention negotiations. His opposition to
[[henry-givens]]'s vice-presidential nomination reflects the
competitive internal politics of the white supremacist movement.
