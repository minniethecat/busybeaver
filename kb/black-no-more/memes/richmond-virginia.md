## Overview

Richmond, Virginia, serves as the institutional nerve center of [[anglo-saxon|Anglo-Saxon]] racial ideology in *Black No More*. It is the headquarters of the [[anglo-saxon-association|Anglo-Saxon Association of America]], the political base of [[arthur-snobbcraft|Arthur Snobbcraft]], and the site where Dr. Samuel [[christopher-jackson-buggerie|Buggerie]] conducts the genealogical research that will ultimately destroy the white supremacist project from within.

Richmond represents a different strain of American racism than the [[knights-of-nordica|Knights of Nordica]]'s Atlanta: not the populist, revival-tent nativism of [[rev-givens|Henry Givens]], but the pedigreed, institutional racism of the old Virginia aristocracy. Where Atlanta sells racial fear to the masses, Richmond manufactures racial ideology for the elite.

## In the Narrative

### Headquarters of the Anglo-Saxon Association

Richmond first enters the narrative when [[max-disher|Matthew Fisher]] and [[bunny-brown|Bunny Brown]], having built [[knights-of-nordica|the Knights]] of [[knights-of-nordica|Nordica]] into a national force, seek an alliance with the more established [[anglo-saxon-association|Anglo-Saxon Association]]. Matthew explains to [[bunny-brown|Bunny]]:

> "The Anglo-Saxon Association of America is an organization located in Virginia. The headquarters are in Richmond. It's a group of rich highbrows who can trace their ancestry back almost two hundred years." [009-042]

The distinction is crucial: [[knights-of-nordica|the Knights]] command numbers, but the [[anglo-saxon-association|Anglo-Saxon Association]] commands lineage. Matthew understands that his organization needs the legitimacy — and the money — that Richmond can provide.

### The Annual Meeting

The alliance is forged at the Association's annual meeting in Richmond. Matthew and Givens travel to the city to present their case to the Association's directors, who gather "in an old pre-Civil War mansion on a broad, tree-shaded boulevard" [009-058]. The directors, men who "had dreamed from youth of holding high political office at the national capital as had so many eminent Virginians" [009-059], see in [[knights-of-nordica|the Knights]]' numbers the electoral power they have never been able to achieve through lineage alone.

### Buggerie's Research

After the alliance is cemented, Richmond becomes the center of the novel's genealogical plot. "Back in Richmond [[arthur-snobbcraft|Mr. Snobbcraft]] and his friends were in conference with the statistician of a great New York insurance company" — Dr. Samuel [[christopher-jackson-buggerie|Buggerie]] [009-061]. From his Richmond office, [[christopher-jackson-buggerie|Buggerie]] builds the vast genealogical database that will trace the ancestry of the American population, seeking to identify and exclude those with non-white blood.

[[walter-williams|Walter Williams]], dispatched by the Democratic National Committee, travels to Richmond to gather intelligence on the Republicans' operations: "I'd like to get expenses to Richmond. I have a friend down there in Snobbcraft's office and he might have some dope we can use to our advantage" [010-080].

### The Catastrophic Revelation

Richmond is the stage for the novel's most devastating satirical reversal. [[christopher-jackson-buggerie|Buggerie]], presenting his findings to the [[anglo-saxon-association|Anglo-Saxon Association]] directors, confesses the truth his research has uncovered: "A certain percentage of these Negroes in time lightened sufficiently to be able to pass for white. They then merged with the general population." His conclusion lands with maximum force in the very heart of the [[anglo-saxon|Anglo-Saxon]] establishment:

> "Too many of our very first families are touched right here in Richmond!" [011-051]

The city that serves as the headquarters of American [[racial-integrity|racial purity]] becomes the site where [[racial-integrity|racial purity]] is revealed to be a fiction.

### Epilogue: The Country Estate

The novel's final scene unfolds at "a stately country home near Richmond, VA" [013-001], where the whitened [[max-disher|Max]] Disher — now revealed as [[max-disher|Matthew Fisher]] — faces his ultimate fate. Richmond bookends the novel's political drama: the city where the [[anglo-saxon|Anglo-Saxon]] elite plotted its ascent becomes the city where the whitened Negro elite meets its reckoning.

## Significance

Richmond functions in the novel as the institutional pole of American white supremacy — distinct from Atlanta's populist pole. The [[anglo-saxon-association|Anglo-Saxon Association]]'s Richmond headquarters, with its pre-Civil War mansion and its board of "First Families of Virginia," embodies the old-money, pedigree-obsessed racism that the novel satirizes most sharply. The fact that [[christopher-jackson-buggerie|Buggerie]]'s research — conducted in Richmond, funded by Richmond aristocrats — ultimately proves that these very aristocrats have Black ancestry is the novel's most perfect satirical justice.

Richmond also serves as a symbol of the [[democratic-party|Democratic Party]]'s Southern establishment. When the [[anglo-saxon-association|Anglo-Saxon Association]] merges with [[knights-of-nordica|the Knights]] of [[knights-of-nordica|Nordica]] and the [[democratic-party|Democratic Party]] nominates Givens and Snobbcraft at the Jackson, Mississippi convention, Richmond's ideology has been successfully exported to the national stage.
