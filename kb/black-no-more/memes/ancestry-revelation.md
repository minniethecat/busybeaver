## Overview

The Ancestry Revelation is the novel's central ironic turning point. Dr. Samuel Buggerie, a statistician hired by the Anglo-Saxon Association of America to conduct a nationwide genealogical survey intended to expose "non-Nordic strains" in the population, instead discovers that over half of white Americans — including the Association's own president, Arthur Snobbcraft — possess provable Black ancestry. The revelation, delivered privately in Snobbcraft's Richmond study, shatters the premise of white racial purity on which the entire political movement rests.

## Context

The Anglo-Saxon Association, under Arthur Snobbcraft's presidency, had been the driving force behind a wave of racial-purity legislation. Their political platform demanded mandatory genealogical examinations for all citizens, with those found to possess "Negro ancestry" stripped of citizenship and voting rights. To build the statistical case for this program, the Association's board of directors voted to fund Dr. Buggerie's nationwide research project — on condition that the work be kept "as secret as possible" (009-064).

Buggerie, a "ponderous, nervous, entirely bald" academic from a New York insurance company, was himself a "professional Anglo-Saxon" and a descendant of one of the First Families of Virginia (009-063). His preliminary estimate suggested "as many as twenty million people in the United States" possessed some non-Nordic strain and were "thus unfit for both citizenship and procreation" (009-061). The Association expected his final report to validate their legislative agenda.

## Narrative Account

### The Research Phase

Through the late summer of 1934, Buggerie and his operatives "were making great headway examining birth and marriage records throughout the United States" (010-057). By mid-September, he presented a partial report to the Board of Directors. But the full picture, as his research assistants later acknowledged, had turned "inflammatory" — far more dangerous than Snobbcraft or Buggerie anticipated (010-094).

### The Private Meeting

In late October, Buggerie summoned Snobbcraft to an urgent private meeting in the Association's Richmond headquarters. The statistician was agitated: "It would be suicidal to publish it," he told Snobbcraft (011-038). When pressed, Buggerie revealed his catastrophic finding: "over half the population has no record of its ancestry beyond five generations" (011-042).

### The Personal Blow

Buggerie then delivered the personal blow. Tracing the genealogical lines, he explained that Black Americans who had passed for white over the preceding fifteen generations had produced "close to fifty million souls" of mixed ancestry in the present day. "Too many of our very first families are touched right here in Richmond," he warned (011-046).

The climax came when Buggerie turned to Snobbcraft's own lineage:

> "Take your own family. It is true that your people descended from King Alfred... One of your maternal ancestors in the late seventeenth century was the offspring of an English serving maid and a black slave. This woman in turn had a daughter by the plantation owner. This daughter was married to a former indentured slave. Their children were all white and you are one of their direct descendants!" (011-057)

Buggerie then added, almost cheerfully, that he too had disreputable ancestry — a great-great-grandfather whose ears were cropped for debt, whose illegitimate daughter "married a free Negro who fought in the Revolutionary War" (011-057). He further disclosed that Rev. Givens, "quite a fanatic on the race question and white supremacy," was "only four generations removed from a mulatto ancestor" (011-062).

## Aftermath

The revelation triggered a crisis at the Anglo-Saxon Association headquarters. The research data — contained on index cards in the basement vault — represented dynamite: proof that the very leaders of the white supremacy movement were, by their own definitions, racially "impure." Before Buggerie and Snobbcraft could destroy the evidence, the vault was raided. When they reached the basement, "the doors of the vault were open, sagging on their hinges" and "the vault was absolutely empty" (011-069). The stolen data would later surface as a political weapon in the hands of Matthew Fisher and the Democratic Party machine.

The Ancestry Revelation is the novel's most devastating satire of American racial ideology. White supremacy is exposed not merely as morally bankrupt but as factually impossible — sustained only by willful ignorance of every family's actual genealogical history.

## Key Passages

> "I have found," continued Buggerie, "that over half the population has no record of its ancestry beyond five generations!" (011-042)

> "A certain percentage of these Negroes," continued Buggerie, quite at ease now and seemingly enjoying his dissertation, "in time lightened sufficiently to be able to pass for white. They then merged with the general population. Assuming that there were one thousand such cases fifteen generations ago — and we have proof that there were more — their descendants now number close to fifty million souls. Now I maintain that we dare not risk publishing this information. Too many of our very first families are touched right here in Richmond!" (011-046)

> "Take your own family, for instance... One of your maternal ancestors in the late seventeenth century was the offspring of an English serving maid and a black slave... Their children were all white and you are one of their direct descendants!" (011-057)

> The doors of the vault were open, sagging on their hinges. There was a litter of trash in front of the vault. They all clustered around the opening and peered inside. The vault was absolutely empty. "My God!" exclaimed Snobbcraft and Buggerie in unison, turning two shades paler. (011-069)
