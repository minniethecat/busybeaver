## Overview

The Better White League is one of the novel's culminating ironies: an organization of whitened ex-Negroes who, having crossed [[color-line|the color line]], now organize to protect their interests *as white people* against suspicion from "original" whites. The league represents the ultimate reversal of racial politics: the formerly oppressed becoming the newly anxious, organizing to defend a [[racial-identity|racial identity]] they purchased for fifty dollars.

## Role in the Narrative

The league appears in the novel's late chapters, after the treatment has become widespread and the social order has reorganized around the permanent uncertainty of racial identity. It demonstrates that racial organizing does not disappear when race disappears — it simply takes new forms, with new targets, serving new interests.

## Satirical Function

Delivers the novel's final satirical verdict: the problem is not any particular racial group but the logic of racial organization itself. Any racial category, once established, generates organizations to defend it — and those organizations always serve the economic interests of their leaders.
