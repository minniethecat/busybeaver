## Overview

Nordica Hall is the meeting place of [[knights-of-nordica|the Knights]] of [[knights-of-nordica|Nordica]], described as a "flag-draped auditorium" (004-064) that slowly fills with white men seeking to defend "racial integrity." It is here that [[knights-of-nordica|the Knights]] hold their rallies, recruit members "at five dollars per head" (004-075), and stage the political theater of white supremacy. The hall is also where [[max-disher|Max]] Disher, now [[max-disher|Matthew Fisher]], rises to prominence within the organization — an infiltrator at the heart of the white supremacist machine.

## In the Narrative

The Knights' rally at Nordica Hall occupies much of Chapter 4. The "oil cloth sign reading 'The [[knights-of-nordica|Knights of Nordica]]'" is "stretched across the front" (004-020). Inside, the [[rev-givens|Imperial Grand Wizard]] — a "short, wizened" man who privately "despised" his followers (004-041) — whips up racial hysteria while collecting membership dues. The hall is the novel's most concentrated depiction of white supremacy as theater: flags, costumes, speeches, and the carefully orchestrated production of fear.

## Significance

Nordica Hall is a space of [[jim-crow-aesthetics|racial performance]], not racial essence. Its very name — "[[knights-of-nordica|Nordica]]," evoking an imaginary Nordic past — signals the invented nature of the white racial identity it purports to defend. By setting key scenes here, [[george-schuyler|Schuyler]] suggests that white supremacy, like Black racial pride, is a performance sustained by economic interest: the dues collected at the door, the political careers launched from the podium, the fear manufactured for profit.
