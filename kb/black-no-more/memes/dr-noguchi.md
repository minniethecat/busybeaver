## Overview

Dr. Yusaburo Noguchi is a real historical figure mentioned in the novel's Preface as the inspiration for Crookman's fictional treatment. In October 1929, Noguchi "told American newspaper reporters… that as a result of fifteen years of painstaking research and experiment he was able to change a Negro into a white man" (P04-003). He claimed that "given time, I could change the Japanese into a race of tall blue-eyed blonds" and that the transformation could be achieved "by glandular control and electrical nutrition."

## Character Analysis

Noguchi bridges the novel's fiction and the real-world racial discourse of 1931. His actual newspaper statements — which Schuyler quotes — were part of a global conversation about racial science and transformation. By citing a Japanese scientist rather than a Western one, Schuyler positions racial transformation as a global rather than merely American phenomenon.
