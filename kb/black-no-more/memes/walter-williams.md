Walter Williams is a minor figure in [[black-no-more-treatment|Black No More]] whose brief appearance encapsulates one of the novel's central satirical points: the utter arbitrariness of American racial classification. Williams "had passed for a Negro for years on the strength of a part-Negro grandparent and then gone back to the white race when the National Social Equality League was forced to cease operations" [010-066]. His racial identity is not a biological fact but an institutional convenience — he was Black when it served his career in a Black organization, and he became white again when that organization collapsed.

## Racial Identity as Professional Strategy

Williams's career trajectory is a compressed version of the novel's larger argument. He worked for the National Social Equality League — an organization devoted to ending racial discrimination — while himself occupying a racial identity that was purely instrumental. When the League closes, he "went back to the white race" as casually as one might change employers. Schuyler's point is devastating: if a man can be Negro one decade and white the next based solely on professional convenience, then the entire apparatus of racial classification — the laws, the customs, the violence — is revealed as a structure maintained not by nature but by will.

## The Republican Campaign

By the novel's end, Williams is working as a Republican campaigner alongside [[joseph-bonds|Joseph Bonds]], the two of them "busily engaged in leading the other workers (who knew better) to believe that they were earning the ten dollars a day they were receiving" [010-066]. The pairing of Williams (who passed as Negro by choice) and Bonds (who became white via [[black-no-more-treatment|Black-No-More]]) demonstrates that the direction of racial transition is irrelevant — what matters is the institutional role, not the race that fills it.

> Walter Williams and Joseph Bonds, busily engaged in leading the other workers (who knew better) to believe that they were earning the ten dollars a day they were receiving. [010-066]
