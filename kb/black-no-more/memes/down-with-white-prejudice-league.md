## Overview

The Down-With-White-Prejudice-League is a satirical organization that appears in the epilogue of [[george-schuyler|George Schuyler]]'s *Black No More*. Founded in the aftermath of [[crookman-monograph|Crookman's monograph]] — which revealed that new Caucasians were "from two to three shades lighter than the old Caucasians" [013-120] — the League advocates for the civil rights of the newly-pale minority who face mounting discrimination. It is Schuyler's final, most acid demonstration that the "racial grievance industry" is entirely transferable: the same leaders who once profited from Black oppression now profit from pale oppression.

## Founding and Leadership

As anti-pale prejudice spread — "certain prejudice against all fellow workers who were exceedingly pale" [013-120] — the new Caucasians began to organize. They "wrote indignant letters to the newspapers," "protested vehemently against the effort on the part of employers to pay them less," and sent a delegation to President [[goosie|Goosie]] demanding government action [013-125].

The League was founded by one Karl von Beerde — "whom some accused of being the same Doctor Beard who had, as a Negro, once headed the [[national-social-equality-league|National Social Equality League]]" [013-125]. This is [[shakespeare-agamemnon-beard|Dr. Shakespeare Agamemnon Beard]], the Harvard–Yale–Copenhagen-educated Negro leader who had previously devoted his career to denouncing Caucasians and celebrating Negro achievement. His transformation into "Karl von Beerde" — swapping a Shakespearean name for a Germanic one — literalizes the novel's thesis: racial leadership is a profession, not a calling, and its practitioners follow the market.

## Activities

Offices were established in the [[times-square|Times Square]] district of New York, and "the mails were soon laden with releases attempting to prove that those of exceedingly pale skin were just as good as anybody else and should not, therefore, be oppressed" [013-125]. The League's rhetoric precisely mirrors the [[national-social-equality-league|National Social Equality League]]'s earlier civil rights advocacy — only the target demographic has changed.

## Satirical Parallels

The League is accompanied by a parody-infrastructure of racial pseudoscience that mirrors the earlier chapters' eugenics discourse: Dr. Cutten Prodd writes a book proving Nordic peoples "had been in savagery when Egypt and Crete were at the height of their development," and Professor Handen Moutthe announces that pale citizens are "mentally inferior" after "three entire weeks of hard work assembling his data" [013-125]. Four state legislatures immediately consider bills for "separate schools for pale children."

The League thus completes the novel's satirical arc. Where the early chapters depicted a racial hierarchy built on anti-Black discrimination, the epilogue shows the identical structure rebuilt on anti-pale discrimination — with the same leaders, the same rhetoric, and the same pseudoscientific justifications, differing only in which group occupies the bottom.
