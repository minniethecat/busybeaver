Senator Kretin is a minor political figure in [[black-no-more-treatment|Black No More]] whose name — a phonetic rendering of "cretin" — announces Schuyler's satirical intentions before the character speaks a word. He delivers the keynote speech at the [[democratic-party|Democratic]] convention where [[rev-givens|Rev. Givens]] and [[arthur-snobbcraft|Arthur Snobbcraft]] are nominated [010-035], and he appears at the novel's end among the political figures fleeing the consequences of [[christopher-jackson-buggerie|Dr. Buggerie]]'s genealogical revelations.

## The Keynote Speech

Kretin's keynote address frames the Democratic convention that nominates the white-supremacist fusion ticket. Schuyler does not reproduce the speech's content — the senator's function is purely positional, occupying a slot in the novel's institutional machinery. What matters is that the Democratic Party's most prominent voice at its national convention bears a name meaning "idiot" — a judgment on the intellectual content of the party's platform, which [[rev-givens]] himself cannot explain.

## The Flight

By the novel's climax, Kretin is among those who recognize that Dr. Buggerie's genealogical research threatens to expose the entire white political class. [[max-disher|Matthew Fisher]] notes that they "can't find old Senator Kretin and Arthur Snobbcraft now" [012-055] — the senator has vanished, presumably fleeing the consequences of the racial-exposure he helped to orchestrate. The disappearance is Schuyler's final comment on Kretin: a man whose career was built on racial demagoguery cannot survive the collapse of the racial categories that sustained it.
