## Overview

White Citizens Councils — the novel's prescient anticipation of the actual organizations that would form in the 1950s to resist desegregation — emerge as grassroots responses to the [[black-no-more-treatment|Black-No-More treatment]]. Southern newspapers call for "intelligent white men and women" to organize against the "devilish work" of [[race-science|Crookmanism]] (003-009), and local groups form to pressure politicians and enforce [[color-line|the color line]] through extralegal means.

## Role in the Narrative

These councils represent the decentralized, popular dimension of white supremacist politics — distinct from the organized [[knights-of-nordica|Knights of Nordica]] but overlapping in membership and goals. Their emergence demonstrates that the treatment's disruption of racial categories generates not liberation but new forms of surveillance and enforcement.

## Satirical Function

Pre-enacts the actual White Citizens' Councils that would form two decades later, demonstrating [[george-schuyler|Schuyler]]'s insight that racial panic always generates organizational responses — and that those organizations always serve economic as well as ideological functions.
