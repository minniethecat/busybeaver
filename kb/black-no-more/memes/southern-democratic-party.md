## Overview

The Southern [[democratic-party|Democratic Party]] machine — the one-party system that governed the [[jim-crow|Jim Crow]] [[the-southland|South]] through racial demagoguery — is the novel's target for political satire. Southern politicians had built careers on the promise to "protect white womanhood" from the Black menace; when the [[black-no-more-treatment|Black-No-More treatment]] makes that menace invisible, their electoral strategies collapse. The novel traces their desperate attempts to find new bases for political mobilization now that "the Negro" can no longer serve as a political bogeyman.

## Role in the Narrative

The Southern [[democratic-party|Democrats]]' crisis drives the novel's political plot. Unable to campaign on racial fear when race is invisible, they pivot to class-based appeals — only to discover that white workers and whitened ex-Negroes share more economic interests than they had acknowledged. The political realignment that results is the novel's most radical suggestion: that racial politics can be replaced by class politics.

## Satirical Function

Demonstrates that racial demagoguery is a political strategy, not an authentic belief — and that when the strategy stops working, politicians will find new strategies. Southern Democrats in the novel prove as adaptable as any other entrepreneurs.
