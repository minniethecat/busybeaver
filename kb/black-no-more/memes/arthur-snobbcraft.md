Mr. Arthur Snobbcraft is the president of the Anglo-Saxon Association of America and one of [[black-no-more-treatment|Black No More]]'s most memorable satirical creations. An F.F.V. — First Family of Virginia — Snobbcraft has "devoted his entire life to fighting for two things: white racial integrity and Anglo-Saxon supremacy" [009-053]. Schuyler's devastating portrait transforms the aristocratic defender of racial purity into a figure of farce, beginning with the description of Snobbcraft as "a man suspiciously swarthy for an Anglo-Saxon" [009-053] — a detail that undercuts his entire ideological project before it is even explained.

## The Professional Anglo-Saxon

Snobbcraft's career is a catalogue of racial-purity legislation. He is "the genius that thought up the numerous racial integrity laws adopted in Virginia and many of the other Southern states" [009-053] — laws modeled on the real Virginia Racial Integrity Act of 1924. His policy positions form a comprehensive program of racial authoritarianism: he is "strong for sterilization of the unfit: meaning Negroes, aliens, Jews and other riff raff," and he nurses "an abiding hatred of democracy" [009-053]. Schuyler's satire operates through accumulation: each detail is individually plausible as a historical position, but their concentration in one character reveals the totalitarian logic underlying racial-purity discourse.

> Mr. Arthur Snobbcraft, President of the Anglo-Saxon Association, an F.F.V. and a man suspiciously swarthy for an Anglo-Saxon, had devoted his entire life to fighting for two things: white racial integrity and Anglo-Saxon supremacy. [009-053]

## Political Pragmatism

Snobbcraft's commitment to Anglo-Saxon purity proves flexible when power is at stake. When the opportunity arises to ally with the [[knights-of-nordica|Knights of Nordica]] for the [[democratic-party|Democratic]] presidential campaign, Snobbcraft and his fellow aristocrats "by swallowing their pride for a season and joining with the riff raff of the Knights of Nordica, they saw an opportunity, for the first time in years to get into power; and they took it" [009-057]. The genealogical elitists ally with the populist racists they privately despise — a political marriage that Schuyler presents as the natural alliance of all white-supremacist factions when power is within reach.

## The Vice-Presidential Campaign

Snobbcraft is nominated as the Democratic vice-presidential candidate alongside [[rev-givens|Rev. Henry Givens]] [010-047]. His campaign represents the fusion ticket that the novel's political satire requires: Givens brings the mass movement, Snobbcraft brings the old-family money and respectability. Together they threaten to unseat the [[goosie|Goosie]] administration. Snobbcraft is "jovial and confident that he would ride into office on the shoulders of the Knights" [011-030] — a confidence that proves misplaced when [[christopher-jackson-buggerie|Dr. Buggerie]]'s genealogical research reveals the impurity of the entire white population, Snobbcraft included.

## The Happy Hill Fate

Snobbcraft's fate is entwined with that of Dr. Buggerie in the novel's grotesque Happy Hill episode. The two professional Anglo-Saxons — one an aristocrat, one a statistician — find themselves at the mercy of the very racial violence their careers have enabled. Their appearance in Happy Hill, "grotesque" [013-083], reduces them from political powerbrokers to bodies vulnerable to the logic they have spent their lives promoting. The swarthy Anglo-Saxon and the genealogical scientist are indistinguishable from the Negroes they have persecuted when the mob's appetite for victims exceeds its supply of actual Black people.

> As the sun approached its zenith, Mr. Arthur Snobbcraft and Dr. Samuel Buggerie, grotesque... [013-083]
