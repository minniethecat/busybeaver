## Overview

The Scimitar is a New York City tabloid newspaper that appears in Chapter 2 of [[george-schuyler|George Schuyler]]'s *Black No More*. It serves as the vehicle through which [[max-disher|Max Disher]]'s transformation story enters the public sphere, and functions as a satirical representation of the sensationalist press's appetite for racial spectacle.

## Role in the Narrative

When Max Disher emerges from the [[crookman-sanitarium|Crookman Sanitarium]] as a white man, he is immediately mobbed by reporters and photographers. A female reporter from The Scimitar intercepts him:

> "Don't be scared," she smiled. "I knew you would want to get away from that mob so I went around the corner and got a cab for you. Come along with me and I'll get everything fixed up for you. I'm a reporter from The Scimitar. We'll give you a lot of money for your story." [002-010]

At the Scimitar's office, Max — using the alias "William Small" — tells his story to a stenographer and receives "a sheaf of crisp, new bills" [002-027]. Within hours, newsboys are crying the extra edition, with a huge photograph of Max occupying the entire front page.

## Satirical Function

The Scimitar represents the symbiosis between the press and racial sensation in Schuyler's America. The paper buys Max's story not for its news value but for its commercial potential as spectacle — the "first installment of his strange tale" sells as entertainment rather than information. Max, for his part, is complicit: he takes the money and provides a false name, treating his racial transformation as a commodity to be marketed.

The paper's name — "Scimitar," a curved sword associated with the Middle East — carries an ironic Orientalist charge, suggesting exotic violence and aligning the tabloid press with the novel's broader critique of how race is packaged and consumed in American culture.
