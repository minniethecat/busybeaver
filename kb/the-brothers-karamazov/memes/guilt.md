# Guilt

Chapter V. A Laceration In The Drawing-Room (Book IV). Katerina Ivanovna, newly betrothed to Dmitri, is troubled by Dmitri's violent humiliation of Captain Snegiryov. She tasks Alyosha with delivering compensation, revealing her sense of moral responsibility for Dmitri's actions.

## Definition in Dostoevsky's Framework

(035-044) Katerina Ivanovna tells Alyosha that Dmitri 'was guilty of a hasty and unjust action' — seizing a captain by the beard and dragging him through the street — and asks Alyosha to deliver 200 roubles to the impoverished family as a gesture of sympathy.

(042-023) Ivan declares: 'there are none guilty; that cause follows effect, simply and directly; that everything flows and finds its level — but that's only Euclidian nonsense, I know that, and I can't consent to live by it!' He rejects a world without justice and 'returns his ticket' to God.

(047-033) Elder Zossima's mother asks him: 'what sin have you committed yet, that you hold yourself more guilty than all?' — a question that crystallizes Zossima's radical doctrine of universal responsibility.
