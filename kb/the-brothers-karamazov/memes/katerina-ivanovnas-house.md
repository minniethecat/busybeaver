## Location and Description

Katerina Ivanovna's house in Skotoprigonyevsk is the residence of Madame Hohlakov, where Katerina Ivanovna stays as a guest. The house is a comfortable, well-appointed dwelling reflecting the social standing of its owner. It includes a drawing room where many of the novel's tense interpersonal scenes take place, as well as Lise's room where she receives Alyosha (012-012) (015-040).

## Significance in the Novel

Katerina Ivanovna's house functions as a stage for the novel's central romantic entanglements and their moral consequences. It is here that Katerina alternates between her devotion to the unfaithful Dmitri and her growing intimacy with Ivan. The house becomes a place where pride, guilt, and self-deception play out in intimate conversations (015-051) (027-001).

The house is also where the relationship between Alyosha and Lise develops. Their conversations in Lise's room, alternating between childish playfulness and serious spiritual questioning, offer a counterpoint to the destructive passions driving the other characters. Lise's confession of love and their brief, doomed engagement take place here (034-058) (034-062).

Katerina Ivanovna's house becomes a key location in the trial narrative. Her testimony alternates between protecting Ivan and condemning Dmitri, and she produces the damning letter — written while drunk — that the prosecution uses to establish motive. The house represents the intersection of the novel's themes of love, pride, money, and truth (027-048) (032-033).

## Key Events

- Alyosha first visits Katerina Ivanovna at Madame Hohlakov's house, where Katerina reveals the complex history of her relationship with Dmitri and the money she gave him (012-012) (015-040).

- Katerina and Grushenka have their fierce confrontation in the drawing room, with Katerina's attempt to assert moral superiority collapsing into humiliation (015-051) (015-059).

- Ivan confesses his love to Katerina in this house, and Katerina struggles between her sense of duty to Dmitri and her feelings for Ivan (027-001) (027-013).

- Alyosha and Lise have their engagement discussion in Lise's room, where Lise reveals both her love and her morbid fascination with suffering (034-058) (039-014).

- During the trial, Katerina's testimony from this house — particularly the letter from Dmitri — provides crucial evidence that implicates Dmitri (027-048) (032-033).
