## Location and Description

Mokroe is a village situated at a considerable distance from Skotoprigonyevsk, reachable by posting carriage. The village is notable for its inn, owned by the peasant Trifon Borissovitch, which serves as the primary setting for the events that occur there. The inn is a substantial establishment capable of hosting large parties, with a main room for dancing and drinking, and is described as a place where Dmitri can spend money lavishly (022-026) (061-002).

## Significance in the Novel

Mokroe is the setting for two of the novel's most dramatic episodes, both involving Dmitri Karamazov. The first visit occurs early in the novel when Dmitri, having come into money, holds a wild party that leads to his first serious entanglement with Grushenka. The second visit occurs on the night of Fyodor Pavlovitch's murder, when Dmitri returns to Mokroe with Grushenka for another spree — this time believing he has irrevocably destroyed his life (061-004) (066-003).

Mokroe is where Dmitri's fate is sealed. His arrest at the inn in the middle of the night marks the turning point from personal drama to legal tragedy. The detailed inventory of Dmitri's spending during the spree — the champagne, the gypsies, the gifts — becomes evidence at his trial, used to establish both his desperate state of mind and the missing money that could have been the three thousand roubles stolen from his father (067-019) (073-001).

The village also serves as the setting for a remarkable spiritual moment. Despite the circumstances of his arrest, Dmitri experiences a dream of a suffering peasant babe that awakens his conscience. In the filthy inn at Mokroe, he has his first genuine moral awakening, declaring he will accept suffering and go to Siberia (072-013) (072-017).

## Key Events

- Dmitri's first spree at Mokroe, where he meets Grushenka and spends money recklessly, establishing the pattern of behavior that will later damn him (022-026) (022-029).

- On the night of the murder, Dmitri travels to Mokroe with Grushenka, spending wildly on champagne, gypsy singers, and gifts for the peasants, believing this is his final night of freedom (061-002) (061-070).

- The authorities arrive at Mokroe and arrest Dmitri in the middle of the night, finding incriminating evidence including the large sum of money he has been spending (067-019) (067-023).

- Dmitri experiences his transformative dream of the "wee one" — a vision of a starving peasant baby that awakens his compassion and leads to his moral conversion (072-013) (073-001).

- During the trial, witnesses from Mokroe — including the innkeeper Trifon Borissovitch and the peasants who received Dmitri's largesse — testify about his behavior, his spending, and his state of mind (102-012) (104-007).
