## Location and Description

Fyodor Pavlovitch Karamazov's house is located in the town of Skotoprigonyevsk (003-003). It is a substantial dwelling befitting a man of some means, though its atmosphere reflects the moral degradation of its owner. The house contains a main living area, private rooms, and the garden where Fyodor Pavlovitch conducts his secret nocturnal vigils. The property is also notable for its outbuildings and the enclosed area where the murder takes place (004-001) (005-001).

## Significance in the Novel

Fyodor Pavlovitch's house represents the corrupt heart of the Karamazov family. It is here that the patriarch indulges in drunken orgies, hoards his money, and lusts after Grushenka — creating the conditions that lead to his murder. The house embodies the father's spiritual poverty and the degradation he spreads to all who enter (003-003) (004-003).

The house is the scene of the novel's central crime: the murder of Fyodor Pavlovitch. The circumstances surrounding the murder — the secret signals, the envelope with three thousand roubles, the multiple people drawn to the house that night — form the foundation of the trial that dominates the novel's final section (067-002) (102-013).

The house also serves as a site of inheritance and contested legacy. Ivan and Alyosha visit their father there, each grappling with the burden of their paternity. The house is where Ivan's philosophy of "everything is permitted" meets its logical conclusion: patricide (018-001) (021-029).

## Key Events

- Fyodor Pavlovitch establishes his household in Skotoprigonyevsk after the death of his second wife, raising his youngest son Alyosha with neglect before the boy enters the monastery (003-003) (004-001).

- Dmitri bursts into the house in a violent rage, attacking his father and threatening to kill him over the disputed inheritance and rivalry for Grushenka (035-044) (044-001).

- The murder of Fyodor Pavlovitch takes place at the house — he is found with his skull crushed, and the empty envelope that had contained three thousand roubles is discovered on the floor (067-002).

- During the investigation, the house becomes a crime scene examined by the district authorities, revealing evidence of the elder Karamazov's debauched lifestyle (067-007) (067-017).

- The garden and the secret knocks at the window — the "signal" system by which Fyodor Pavlovitch expected Grushenka to announce herself — become crucial evidence at Dmitri's trial (061-187) (086-023).
