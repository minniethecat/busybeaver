# Minoret-Levrault

## Overview

Minoret-Levrault is [[cremiere|the tax-receiver of Nemours]] and [[dr-minoret|Doctor Minoret]]'s nephew
by marriage. A weak man dominated by his formidable wife [[zelie-minoret|Zélie]], he
participates in [[theft-of-the-will|the conspiracy]] against [[ursule-mirouet-p|Ursule]] more from passivity than from
active malice. He represents the type of the complacent bourgeois functionary
whose moral inertia makes him an accomplice to wrongdoing he would never have
the courage to initiate himself (001-041).

## Life and Role in the Novel

As tax-receiver, Minoret-Levrault occupies a position of modest importance
in [[nemours|Nemours]]. His professional life is defined by routine, his domestic life
by subordination to [[zelie-minoret|Zélie]]. "As a son of the Revolution" (001-010), Minoret-Levrault belongs to the generation that benefited from the upheaval of the old order yet made nothing of that inheritance beyond material comfort. The inheritance represents his one chance at
significant wealth — a chance he has been counting on for years and cannot
bear to see slip away (001-003).

His role in [[theft-of-the-will|the conspiracy]] is that of the follower. [[zelie-minoret|Zélie]] plans; Minoret-
Levrault executes. His compliance is not enthusiastic but it is also not
resisted. He lacks the moral imagination to conceive of an alternative to
following his wife's lead, and he lacks the courage to resist even if an
alternative occurred to him.

Balzac's treatment of Minoret-Levrault is characteristically precise. He is
not a villain; he is something more common and, in the novel's moral scheme,
almost as culpable: a man who knows better but lacks the will to act on what
he knows (001-004). His eventual exposure is handled without drama — he is simply one
of those who are found out when [[theft-of-the-will|the conspiracy]] collapses.

> Still, on seeing Minoret-Levrault, an artist would have left his place to sketch this country townsman; he was so original by sheer force of being common. Combine all the characteristics of the brute and you get Caliban, who certainly is a great creation.
>
> (001-006)

## Relationships

Minoret-Levrault's marriage to **[[zelie-minoret|Zélie]]** is the defining relationship of his
life. She dominates him completely, and his participation in [[theft-of-the-will|the conspiracy]]
is essentially an extension of his marital obedience (001-049).

With **[[dr-minoret|Doctor Minoret]]**, he maintains the forms of family affection while
privately calculating his share of the expected inheritance.

## Significance

Minoret-Levrault represents the moral danger of passivity — the man who does
wrong not because he is evil but because he is weak. His presence in the
novel serves as a warning that vice is not always active; sometimes it is
simply the failure to resist.
