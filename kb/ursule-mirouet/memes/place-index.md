## The Two Frances

Balzac structures *Ursule Mirouët* around the contrast between Paris and the provinces — a recurring framework in *La Comédie Humaine*. Nemours represents tradition, community, and moral clarity; Paris embodies ambition, corruption, and the allure of wealth. The movement between these two poles drives much of the novel's dramatic tension.
