# Art

Art is a central thematic concern in Ivan Turgenev's *On the Eve* (1860), embodied primarily through the character of [[pavel-shubin|Pavel Shubin]], a young sculptor living as a dependent in the [[Stahov-Household|Stahov house]]hold. Through [[pavel-shubin|Shubin]]'s art — his busts, his self-criticism, his sudden creative rages and abandonments — Turgenev explores the relationship between aesthetic creation and moral action, between the artist's eye and the citizen's [[duty]].

## Definition and Scope

Art in *On the Eve* is primarily visual and plastic — sculpture, specifically — rather than literary or [[music|musical]]. [[pavel-shubin|Shubin]]'s medium is clay, and the novel traces his attempts to capture human character in physical form: a bust of [[zoya-nikitishna|Zoya]], a figurine of [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]], a self-portrait. Each attempt reveals something about both the subject and the artist.

## Expression in the Novel

### Shubin's Sculpture

[[pavel-shubin|Shubin]]'s artistic practice is sustained throughout the novel. He models a bust of [[zoya-nikitishna|Zoya]], then "breaking up the bust of [[zoya-nikitishna|Zoya]], [[pavel-shubin|Shubin]] set hastily to modelling and kneading the clay again with an air of vexation" (004-011). This cycle — creation, dissatisfaction, destruction — is characteristic. [[pavel-shubin|Shubin]] can see what is wrong with his work but cannot complete it.

### Art as Perception

[[pavel-shubin|Shubin]]'s finest quality as an artist is his eye. He sees [[elena-nikolaevna|Elena]], [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]], [[andrei-bersenyev|Bersenyev]], and himself with a lucidity the other characters lack. His observation that [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]] has "iron in his character" is one of the novel's most penetrating character assessments (022-034). Art, in this sense, is a mode of moral perception.

### Art vs. Action

The novel's central tension — art versus action — is articulated through the contrast between [[pavel-shubin|Shubin]] and [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]]. [[pavel-shubin|Shubin]] can sculpt, describe, mock, and admire, but he cannot act. [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]], who lacks [[pavel-shubin|Shubin]]'s aesthetic sensibility, can act decisively. The novel does not wholly condemn art — [[pavel-shubin|Shubin]]'s perceptions enrich it — but it insists that art without action is insufficient.

## Key Quotations

> "If a beautiful view, if beautiful music, say nothing to your heart, I mean, if you are not in sympathy with it..." — Shubin, on the spiritual dimension of art (001-018)

> "Besides my art, the only beauty I love is in women." — Shubin, defining his narrowed aesthetic world (001-020)

> Shubin "set hastily to modelling and kneading the clay again with an air of vexation" — after destroying Zoya's bust (004-011)
