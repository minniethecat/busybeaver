# Kiev

Kiev (Kyiv) appears in Ivan Turgenev's *On the Eve* (1860) as a station in [[dmitri-insarov|Dmitri Insarov]]'s biography — the city where he passed his adolescence, living with his aunt after the [[death]] of his parents, and where he received the education that would shape his revolutionary consciousness. It is a city of preparation, not action; the novel's present tense finds [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]] in [[moscow|Moscow]], his Kiev years behind him but foundational to who he has become.

## Location

- **Country**: [[russia|Russian Empire]] (present-day Ukraine)
- **Region**: Kiev Governorate
- **In the Novel**: Referenced in Chapter 10, in the context of [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]]'s backstory

## Significance in the Novel

### Insarov's Formative Years

[[andrei-bersenyev|Bersenyev]]'s account of [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]]'s biography places Kiev at the center of the young [[bulgaria|Bulgaria]]n's development: "His aunt, his father's sister, is still living in Kiev, married to a senior history teacher" (010-014). After the [[death]] of his parents — his mother at the hands of Turkish forces, his father shot while attempting revenge — the young [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]] was taken in by this aunt. "At Kiev he lived twelve whole years" (010-019), a period during which he received the systematic education that would distinguish him from his more impulsive countrymen.

### Between Two Worlds

Kiev occupies an intermediate position in [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]]'s trajectory: it is neither [[bulgaria|Bulgaria]] (the homeland he lost) nor [[moscow|Moscow]] (where his revolutionary mission takes active shape). In Kiev, [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]] was still a dependent, a child, a student. His [[the-journey-to-venice|departure]] from Kiev — via Odessa to [[moscow|Moscow]] — marks the transition from passive victim of history to active agent in it.

### The Education of a Revolutionary

The fact that [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]]'s aunt married a history teacher is significant: it suggests the intellectual atmosphere in which [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]]'s political consciousness was formed. Kiev provided the books, the instruction, and the relative safety in which a [[bulgaria|Bulgaria]]n orphan could prepare himself to return to the struggle.

## Key Passages

> "...Insarov's aunt, is still living in Kiev, married to a senior history teacher..." — Bersenyev recounting Insarov's backstory to Elena (010-014)

> "...to Odessa, and from there to Kiev. At Kiev he lived twelve whole years..." — the arc of Insarov's youth (010-019)
