# Italy

Italy appears in Ivan Turgenev's *On the Eve* (1860) not as a physical setting the characters inhabit but as a horizon of longing — a name invoked repeatedly by the sculptor [[pavel-shubin|Pavel Shubin]] as the destination where art, freedom, and authentic life are to be found. In the novel's symbolic geography, Italy stands opposite [[russia|Russia]]: where [[russia|Russia]] is "stifling," Italy is [[freedom|liberation]]; where [[russia|Russia]] is ineffectual gentility, Italy is creative vitality.

## Location

- **Country**: Italy (Italian peninsula)
- **In the Novel**: Referenced in Chapters 2–3, primarily in conversations at [[kuntsovo|Kuntsovo]]
- **Relevance**: Never visited in the novel's present action; functions as an idealized "elsewhere"

## Significance in the Novel

### Shubin's Artistic Dream

Italy is central to [[pavel-shubin|Shubin]]'s identity as a sculptor. He speaks of it as the one place where true art is possible: "there is no salvation to be found outside of Italy!" (002-008). His repeated invocations of Italy — "Good God, when shall I go to Italy? When?" (002-004) — establish the pattern of yearning that defines his character. The sculptor has already been there once ("I went off to the [[russia|Little Russia]]ns" after exhausting his Italy fund — 002-006), but the return remains perpetually deferred.

### Symbolic Contrast

The novel sets up a chain of symbolic oppositions: [[russia|Russia]]/[[bulgaria|Bulgaria]] as political action vs. inaction, and [[russia|Russia]]/Italy as artistic authenticity vs. provincial stagnation. [[pavel-shubin|Shubin]]'s Italy-longing parallels [[elena-nikolaevna|Elena]]'s [[bulgaria|Bulgaria]]-longing — both characters seek an elsewhere that promises more meaning than the world they inhabit. But where [[elena-nikolaevna|Elena]]'s quest leads to decisive action (she follows [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]] toward the Balkans), [[pavel-shubin|Shubin]]'s remains a complaint, never transformed into movement.

## Key Passages

> "Good God, when shall I go to Italy? When—" — Shubin, in a characteristic outburst of artistic longing (002-004)

> "There is no salvation to be found outside of Italy!" — Shubin's declaration of artistic faith (002-008)

> "You will go to Italy," said Bersenyev, without turning round. — Bersenyev's calm reassurance, itself a kind of Russian immobility (002-009)

> "I am stifled here, I want to be in Italy," pursued Shubin, "there is sun, there is beauty..." — Shubin's lament at Kuntsovo (002-010)
