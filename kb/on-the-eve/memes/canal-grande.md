The Canal Grande (Grand Canal) is the main waterway of [[venice|Venice]], [[italy|Italy]]. In Turgenev's *On the Eve*, it serves as the primary setting for Chapter XXXIII, where [[elena-nikolaevna|Elena]] and [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]] spend a day together exploring [[venice|Venice]] by gondola, attending the opera, and gliding along the canal at night (033-016)(033-019)(033-030).

## In the Novel

[[elena-nikolaevna|Elena]] and [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]] arrive in [[venice|Venice]] and take a gondola along the Canal Grande. [[elena-nikolaevna|Elena]] proposes they give up the day entirely to one another, forgetting politics and war: "we will go along to the Canal Grande. We have not seen [[venice|Venice]] properly, you know, all the while we have been here" (033-016). They instruct the gondolier to take them without hurry along the canal (033-019).

After the opera, they glide along the Canal Grande again at night. The palaces appear transformed by moonlight and shadow, and the gondolas with their red lamps flit past noiselessly, creating an atmosphere of tranquil [[beauty]] (033-030).

## Symbolic Significance

The Canal Grande represents the brief, luminous interval of [[happiness]] [[elena-nikolaevna|Elena]] and [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]] share in [[venice|Venice]] before [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]]'s fatal illness. Their repeated journeys along the canal frame their last day of untroubled [[happiness|joy]]. The contrast between the bright April day and the soft, shadowy night passage mirrors the impending darkness of [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]]'s [[death]].
