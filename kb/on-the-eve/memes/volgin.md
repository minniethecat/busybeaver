## Identity and Relationships

- **Full Name**: Volgin (no first name or patronymic given in the novel)
- **Identity**: Senator, a ponderous figure in [[moscow|Moscow]] high society
- **[[family|Family]]**: A rich relation of [[anna-stahova|Anna Vassilyevna Stahov]]a on her mother's side, alongside the Princes [[prince-tchikurasov|Tchikurasov]] (003-001)
- **Social Circle**: Associated with [[prince-tchikurasov|Prince Ardalion Tchikurasov]]; moves in [[moscow|Moscow]]'s upper social strata

## Role in the Novel

### In the Stahov Family Background

Senator Volgin belongs to the wealthy branch of [[anna-stahova|Anna Vassilyevna Stahov]]a's maternal relatives. When Anna was orphaned at seven, [[prince-tchikurasov|Prince Ardalion Tchikurasov]] — of the same social circle as Volgin — was appointed her guardian and placed her in a [[moscow|Moscow]] boarding-school (003-001).

### The Patron Who Did Nothing

When [[pavel-shubin|Pavel Shubin]] was sixteen and showing artistic promise, Volgin saw one of his statuettes and "declared that he intended to protect this youthful genius." After the sudden [[death]] of [[pavel-shubin|Shubin]]'s father, however, this patronage amounted to nothing more than a plaster bust of Homer. The narrator notes drily: "The senator, the patron of genius, made him a present of a bust of Homer in plaster, and did nothing more" (003-004). [[pavel-shubin|Shubin]]'s real support came from [[anna-stahova|Anna Vassilyevna]], who helped him with money.

### A Style to Emulate

In chapter 9, [[pavel-shubin|Shubin]] mockingly describes [[prince-tchikurasov|Prince Tchikurasov]] as "a Maecenas of a Kazan-Tartar stock, after the style of Volgin" (009-036). The comparison casts Volgin as a type: the grandiloquent patron whose gestures of artistic support far exceed his actual contributions.

## Character and Themes

- **Traits**: Ponderous, self-important, superficially cultured
- **Representing**: Hollow patronage, the gap between aristocratic pretension and genuine support for the arts
- **Narrative Function**: A minor satirical figure illustrating the inadequacy of [[russia|Russia]]'s established elite as patrons of emerging talent
