# Renditch

Renditch is a Dalmatian sailor who serves as [[dmitri-insarov|Dmitri Insarov]]'s contact in [[venice|Venice]] in the final chapters of Ivan Turgenev's *On the Eve*. Though he appears only briefly, his role is critical: he is the man charged with arranging [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]] and [[elena-nikolaevna|Elena]]'s passage to [[bulgaria|Bulgaria]] — and his delayed arrival contributes to the novel's tragic climax.

## Role in the Novel

Renditch is first mentioned by [[elena-nikolaevna|Elena]], who reports that "Renditch promised to arrange everything" (033-009) for their journey. [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]], already gravely ill, grows increasingly anxious about Renditch's whereabouts. In his feverish sleep, he mutters the name: "Renditch!" (033-034). His first question upon waking is "Renditch has not come?" (034-002).

This pattern of expectation and delay structures the [[venice|Venice]] chapters. "Renditch," both [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]] and [[elena-nikolaevna|Elena]] think at once when a visitor arrives — "but a stranger it was who entered" (034-004). The repeated disappointment underscores the narrowing window of time: [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]]'s illness is advancing, and without Renditch, there can be no [[the-journey-to-venice|departure]] for [[bulgaria|Bulgaria]].

When Renditch finally appears, the scene is charged with desperate urgency. "Renditch!" cried [[elena-nikolaevna|Elena]], "it's you! Look, look, Renditch, come to us, come here!" (034-032). But by this point — "Renditch said nothing and only moved on" (034-033) — it may already be too late. The doctor is summoned from the same hotel.

> "They say the poor Dalmatian fishermen have sacrificed their dredging weights—you know the leads they weigh their nets with for letting them down to the bottom—to make bullets! They have no money, they only just live by fishing; but they have joyfully given up their last property, and now are starving. What a nation!"
>
> (033-009)

## Epilogue

In the novel's final chapter, Renditch appears once more: "the next day, in the same room, Renditch was standing at the window" (035-001). The narrator adds a brief biography: "Renditch was a Dalmatian, a sailor, with whom [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]] had become acquainted during his wanderings in his own country, and whom he had sought out in [[venice|Venice]]" (035-002). This retrospective identification transforms Renditch from a plot mechanism into a character with his own history — a fellow exile, a link in the chain of the [[bulgaria|Bulgaria]]n [[freedom|liberation]] network that extends across Europe.

> Renditch was a Dalmatian, a sailor, with whom Insarov had become acquainted during his wanderings in his own country, and whom he had sought out in Venice. He was a dry, gruff man, full of daring and devoted to the Slavonic cause. He despised the Turks and hated the Austrians.
>
> (035-002)
