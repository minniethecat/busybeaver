# Lupoyarov

Lupoyarov is a minor [[russia|Russia]]n character who appears briefly in [[venice|Venice]] in the final chapters of Ivan Turgenev's *On the Eve*. His presence — voluble, social, entirely unaware of the tragedy unfolding around him — provides a moment of jarring contrast to the desperate situation of [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]] and [[elena-nikolaevna|Elena]].

## Appearance in the Novel

Lupoyarov appears in Chapter XXXIV, where he encounters [[elena-nikolaevna|Elena]] and [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]] in [[venice|Venice]]. "Bowing politely to [[elena-nikolaevna|Elena]]," he greets them with the easy familiarity of a compatriot abroad: "Lupoyarov, do you remember, we met at M..." (034-006).

The encounter is awkward — [[elena-nikolaevna|Elena]] and [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]] are preoccupied with [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]]'s worsening illness and the critical wait for [[renditch|Renditch]] — but Lupoyarov is oblivious to their distress.

His defining characteristic is his talkativeness. "And for a long time Lupoyarov still babbled on in the same style" (034-009), the narrator notes, the verb "babbled" conveying the inconsequential [[nature]] of his conversation against the backdrop of [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]]'s life-or-[[death]] crisis.

He is a peripheral figure — a reminder that the social world continues its chatter even as private tragedy unfolds.

> And for a long time Lupoyarov still babbled on in the same way, and, as he went away, he promised to come again.
>
> (034-009)

## Contrast with Insarov

Lupoyarov's brief appearance gains its significance through contrast. Where [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]] is [[death|dying]] for a cause — burning with fever, weak, stripped of everything but his commitment to [[bulgaria|Bulgaria]]n [[freedom|liberation]] — Lupoyarov is entirely at ease, a tourist adrift in [[venice|Venice]] with nothing more pressing than idle conversation.

The encounter dramatizes the gulf between those who give their lives to history and those who merely pass through it. Turgenev uses Lupoyarov as a foil to sharpen the tragedy of [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]]'s predicament. The chatterbox babbles "on in the same way" (034-009) while [[elena-nikolaevna|Elena]] watches her husband's strength ebb.

[[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]] himself draws the contrast bitterly: "So this is your younger generation! There are plenty who show off, and give themselves airs, while at heart they are as empty chatterboxes as that worthy" (034-010).

The social world, Lupoyarov reminds us, does not pause for tragedy. His promise to "come again" (034-009) is made to people who will soon be gone — [[elena-nikolaevna|Elena]] to [[bulgaria|Bulgaria]], [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]] to his grave. The obliviousness is not cruelty but a more ordinary thing: the indifference of the everyday to the exceptional.

> Worn out by the unexpected visit, Insarov lay down on the sofa. 'So this,' he said, mournfully looking at Elena, 'is your younger generation! There are plenty who show off, and give themselves airs, while at heart they are as empty chatterboxes as that worthy.'
>
> (034-010)
