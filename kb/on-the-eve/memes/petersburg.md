# Petersburg

Petersburg (St. Petersburg) appears in Ivan Turgenev's *On the Eve* (1860) as the seat of imperial authority and the site of [[nikolai-artemyevitch|Nikolai Artemyevitch Stahov]]'s former career. It is the Petersburg appointment that established the [[family|Stahov family]]'s social position, but the novel's action takes place far from the capital — at [[kuntsovo|Kuntsovo]], near [[moscow|Moscow]] — a distance that mirrors the family's partial withdrawal from the world of official ambition.

## Location

- **Country**: [[russia|Russian Empire]]
- **City**: St. Petersburg (imperial capital)
- **In the Novel**: Referenced in Chapters 3, 7, and 22; never a direct setting
- **Contrast**: [[moscow|Moscow]] and [[kuntsovo|Kuntsovo]] — the novel's actual [[All-Places|settings]], where life is less formal and more intimate

## Significance in the Novel

### The Bureaucratic World

[[nikolai-artemyevitch|Nikolai Artemyevitch]] "received a lucrative post in Petersburg" (003-001), and this Petersburg career is the foundation of his social identity. But it is also what he has left behind: by the time of the novel's present, he lives in [[moscow|Moscow]] and summers at [[kuntsovo|Kuntsovo]], his government service a memory rather than an ongoing occupation. Petersburg in this sense stands for the world of official rank and formal society — a world the novel acknowledges but does not enter.

### Architectural Style

The Stahovs' [[moscow|Moscow]] residence is described as "built in the Petersburg style" (007-001) — a detail that marks the family's social aspirations. The Petersburg style, with its neoclassical regularity and imperial pretension, is imported into the more eclectic [[moscow|Moscow]] streetscape, just as [[nikolai-artemyevitch|Nikolai Artemyevitch]]'s courtly manners feel slightly out of place in the more relaxed atmosphere of the family dacha.

### Comme il Faut

Petersburg is associated with the social code of *comme il faut* — "to think very little about Petersburg and comme il faut" (022-034) describes [[Yegor-Kurnatovsky|Kurnatovsky]]'s values. The capital represents the world of propriety and convention against which the novel's more earnest characters ([[elena-nikolaevna|Elena]], [[dmitri-insarov|Insarov]], [[andrei-bersenyev|Bersenyev]]) define themselves.

## Key Passages

> "Nikolai Artemyevitch Stahov... received a lucrative post in Petersburg." — establishing the family's social foundation (003-001)

> "...a solid stone house, built in the Petersburg style, between Arbaty Road and..." — the Moscow house as Petersburg transplant (007-001)

> "...to think very little about Petersburg and comme il faut: he once even..." — Kurnatovsky's values, where Petersburg is shorthand for social convention (022-034)
