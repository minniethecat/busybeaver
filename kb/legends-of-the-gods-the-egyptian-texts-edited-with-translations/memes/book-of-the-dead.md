# Book of the Dead

The **Book of the Dead** (Egyptian **Pert em Hru** — "Chapters of Coming Forth by Day") is the most famous collection of ancient Egyptian funerary texts. It comprises spells, hymns, and instructions designed to guide the [[Soul|Ba]] through the [[Tuat|underworld]] and into the blessed afterlife, where the deceased would join [[Osiris]] and travel with [[Ra]] in his [[Sun Boat]].

## Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Egyptian Name** | Pert em Hru (Coming Forth by Day) |
| **Modern Name** | Book of the Dead (coined by Lepsius, 1842) |
| **Content** | ~200 spells, hymns, prayers |
| **Purpose** | Guide the deceased through the afterlife |
| **Key Chapter** | Chapter 125 — Weighing of the Heart |
| **Medium** | Papyrus scrolls, tomb walls, coffin texts |

## Meaning and Usage

The Book of the Dead contains approximately 200 spells covering protection from underworld dangers, transformation into divine forms, the Judgment of the Dead before [[Osiris]], names for gates and guardians, and solar hymns to join [[Ra]] in his boat.

The most famous spell is **Chapter 125** — the Judgment Scene, where the heart of the deceased is weighed against the feather of [[Maat]]:

> "The heart of the deceased shall be placed upon the great balance, and the feather of Maat shall be set in the other pan." *(019-001)*

## Occurrences in the Texts

The corpus edited by Budge includes several chapters of magical texts closely related to the Book of the Dead tradition. These include "The Chapter of Casting a Spell on the Cat," magical spells for protection against venomous creatures, and hymns to [[Osiris]] — all within the broader corpus of Egyptian funerary literature.

The spells were inscribed on papyri placed in tombs, and later on mummy wrappings, coffins, and tomb walls. The deceased who knew these spells could navigate the perils of the [[Tuat]] and emerge transformed — a [[Neter|netjer]] in their own right.
