# Uatchit

## Overview

Uatchit (Wadjet, Uto) is the cobra-goddess of Lower Egypt, worshipped principally at Per-Uatchit (Buto) in the Delta. Together with the vulture-goddess [[nekhebet]] of Upper Egypt, she forms the "Two Ladies" (*Nebty*), the protective pair whose emblems adorn the pharaoh's crown as the uraeus.

## Identity and Role

Uatchit is the divine guardian of the Red Crown (*deshret*) of Lower Egypt and the cobra who rears upon the brow of [[ra]] and the pharaoh, spitting fire against enemies. She embodies the fierce protective aspect of royal power — her venom and flame defend the king from all threats, both mortal and supernatural.

Her worship center is recorded in the legends:

> the worship of Uatchet, or Uatchit, was at Per-Uatchit [015-134]

She is invoked alongside her counterpart Nekhebet to protect the solar barque:

> the goddess Uatchit to be with him in the Great Boat [015-404]

> on whom the goddess Uatchit is enthroned [015-443]

## Family and Relationships

Uatchit has no extensive mythological family. Her primary relationship is her pairing with [[nekhebet]] as the Two Ladies who represent the unity of Upper and Lower Egypt under the pharaoh:

> the goddesses Uatchet and Nekhebet, who guard the South and North [002-086]

Together they flank the solar disk and protect the king's name in the royal titulary:

> the goddesses Uatchet and Nekhebet in the form of serpents with burning venom [002-209]

## Major Myths and Narratives

**The Uraeus on the Solar Barque.** Uatchit appears on the prow of the Boat of [[ra]], spitting fire at the enemies of the sun-god — particularly the serpent-fiend [[apep]]. Her venom burns the darkness-serpent as it attempts to waylay the solar barque in the underworld.

**The Two Ladies as Witnesses.** In the narrative of the Winged Disk, the twin sister-goddesses accompany Heru-Behutet in his campaign against the enemies of [[ra]]:

> Heru-Behutet on whom the goddess Uatchit is enthroned [015-443]

**The Per-Uatchit Shrine.** The Lord of the Shrines at Buto is attested in invocations:

> the Lord of the Shrines of Uatchit [017-002]

The Delta sanctuary at Buto was one of the oldest oracular shrines in Egypt, where the cobra-goddess delivered prophecies through her priestesses.

## Cult and Worship

Per-Uatchit (Greek *Buto*) in the sixth nome of Lower Egypt was the principal cult centre. The temple housed a famous oracle, and the cobra was the living embodiment of the goddess. Festivals of Uatchit celebrated the protection of the pharaoh and the renewal of his royal authority.
