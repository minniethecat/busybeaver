[017-001] In the eighteenth year of the Horus, Neter-Khat, of the King of the
[017-002] South and North, Neter-Khat, of the Lord of the Shrines of Uatchit and
[017-003] Nekhebit, Neter-Khat, of the Golden Horus Tcheser,[FN#166] when Matar
[017-004] was Ha Prince, and Erpa, and Governor of the temple-cities in the Land
[017-005] of the South, and director of the Khenti[FN#167] folk in Abtu,[FN#168]
[017-006] there was brought unto him the following royal despatch: "This is to
[017-007] inform thee that misery hath laid hold upon me [as I sit] upon the
[017-008] great throne by reason of those who dwell in the Great House.[FN#169]
[017-009] My heart is grievously afflicted by reason of the exceedingly great evil
[017-010] [which hath happened] because Hapi (i.e., the Nile) hath not come
[017-011] forth[FN#170] in my time to the [proper] height for seven years. Grain
[017-012] is very scarce, vegetables are lacking altogether, every kind of thing
[017-013] which men eat for their food hath ceased, and every man [now] plundereth
[017-014] "his neighbour. Men wish to walk, but are unable to move, the child
[017-015] waileth, the young man draggeth his limbs along, and the hearts of the
[017-016] aged folk are crushed with despair; their legs give way under them, and
[017-017] they sink down to the ground, and their hands are laid upon their bodies
[017-018] [in pain]. The shennu[FN#171] nobles are destitute of counsel, and
[017-019] [when] the storehouses which should contain supplies are opened, there
[017-020] cometh forth therefrom nothing but wind. Everything is in a state of
[017-021] ruin. My mind hath remembered, going back to former time, when I had an
[017-022] advocate, to the time of the gods, and of the Ibis-god, and of the chief
[017-023] Kher-heb priest I-em-hetep,[FN#172] the son of Ptah of his Southern
[017-024] Wall."

[FN#166] Tcheser was a king of the IIIrd Dynasty, and is famous as the
[017-025] builder of the Step Pyramid at Sakkarah. His tomb was discovered by
[017-026] Mr. J. Garstang at Bet Khallaf in Upper Egypt in 1901.

[FN#167] i.e., the people who were in front of, that is, to the South
[017-027] of Egypt, or the population of the country which lies between Dakkah
[017-028] and Aswan.

[FN#168] The ancient Egyptian name for Elephantine Island, which
[017-029] appears to have gained this name because it resembled an elephant in
[017-030] shape.

[FN#169] i.e., the palace.

[FN#170] i.e., risen.

[FN#171] i.e., the high court officials and administrators.

[FN#172] The famous priest and magician, who was subsequently deified
[017-031] and became one of the chief gods of Memphis.

[017-032] "Where is the place of birth of Hapi (the Nile)? What god, or what
[017-033] goddess, presideth (?) over it? What manner of form hath he? It is he
[017-034] who stablisheth revenue for me, and a full store of grain. I would go
[017-035] to the Chief of Het-Sekhet[FN#173] whose beneficence strengtheneth all
[017-036] men in their works. I would enter into the House of Life,[FN#174] I
[017-037] would unfold the written rolls [therein], and I would lay my hand upon
[017-038] them."

[FN#173] Hermopolis.

[FN#174] Per-ankh, or Pa-ankh, was a name given to one of the temple-
[017-039] colleges of priests and scribes.

[017-040] Then [Matar] set out on his journey, and he returned to me straightway.
[017-041] He gave me instruction concerning the increase of Hapi,[FN#175] and
[017-042] told me all things which men had written concerning it, and he revealed
[017-043] to me the secret doors (?) whereto my ancestors had betaken themselves
[017-044] quickly, the like of which has never been, to [any] king since the time
[017-045] of Ra, (?). And he said unto me: "There is a city in the middle of the
[017-046] stream wherefrom Hapi maketh his appearance; "'Abu'[FN#176] was its
[017-047] name in the beginning; it is the City of the Beginning, and it is the
[017-048] Nome of the City of the Beginning. [It reacheth] to Uaua,[FN#177]
[017-049] which is the beginning of the land. There is too a flight of
[017-050] steps,[FN#178] which reareth itself to a great height, and is the
[017-051] support of Ra, when he maketh his calculation to prolong life to
[017-052] everyone; 'Netchemtchem Ankh'[FN#179] is the name of its abode. 'The
[017-053] two Qerti'[FN#180] is the name of the water, and they are the two
[017-054] breasts from which every good thing cometh forth (?).

[FN#175] i.e., the Inundation, or Nile Flood.

[FN#176] The Elephant City, i.e., Elephantine.

[FN#177] A portion of Northern Nubia.

[FN#178] This is probably an allusion to the famous Nilometer on the  
[017-055] Island of Philae.

[FN#179] i.e., "Sweet, sweet life."

[FN#180] The Qerti were the two openings through which the Nile
[017-056] entered this world from the great celestial ocean.

[017-057] "Here is the bed of Hapi (the Nile), wherein he reneweth his youth [in
[017-058] his season], wherein he causeth the flooding of the land. He cometh
[017-059] and hath union as he journeyeth, as a man hath union with a woman. And
[017-060] again he playeth the part of a husband and satisfieth his desire. He
[017-061] riseth to the height of twenty-eight cubits [at Abu], and he droppeth
[017-062] at Sma-Behutet[FN#181] to seven cubits. The union(?) there is that of
[017-063] the god Khnemu in [Abu. He smiteth the ground] with his sandals, and
[017-064] [its] fulness becometh abundant; he openeth the bolt of the door with
[017-065] his hand, and he throweth open the double door of the opening through
[017-066] which the water cometh."

[FN#181] Diospolis of Lower Egypt, or "Thebes of the North."

[017-067] "Moreover, he dwelleth there in the form of the god Shu,[FN#182] as one
[017-068] who is lord over his own territory, and his homestead, the name of
[017-069] which is 'Aa' (i.e., the 'Island'). There he keepeth an account of the
[017-070] products of the Land of the South and of the Land of the North, "in
[017-071] order to give unto every god his proper share, and he leadeth to each
[017-072] [the metals], and the [precious stones, and the four-footed beasts],
[017-073] and the feathered fowl, and the fish, and every thing whereon they
[017-074] live. And the cord [for the measuring of the land] and the tablet
[017-075] whereon the register is kept are there.

[FN#182] The god who separated the Sky-goddess Nut from the embrace of
[017-076] her husband, the Earth-god Keb, and who holds her above him each day.

[017-077] "And there is an edifice of wood there, with the portals thereof formed
[017-078] of reeds, wherein he dwelleth as one who is over his own territory, and
[017-079] he maketh the foliage of the trees (?) to serve as a roof.

[017-080] "His God-house hath an opening towards the south-east, and Ra (or, the
[017-081] Sun) standeth immediately opposite thereto every day. The stream which
[017-082] floweth along the south side thereof hath danger [for him that
[017-083] attacketh it], and it hath as a defence a wall which entereth into the
[017-084] region of the men of Kens[FN#183] on the South. Huge mountains [filled
[017-085] with] masses of stone are round about its domain on the east side, and
[017-086] shut it in. Thither come the quarrymen with things (tools?) of every
[017-087] kind, [when] they "seek to build a House for any god in the Land of the
[017-088] South, or in the Land of the North, or [shrines] as abodes for sacred
[017-089] animals, or royal pyramids, and statues of all kinds. They stand up in
[017-090] front of the House of the God and in the sanctuary chamber, and their
[017-091] sweet smelling offerings are presented before the face of the god
[017-092] Khnemu during his circuit, even as [when they bring] "garden herbs and
[017-093] flowers of every kind. The fore parts thereof are in Abu
[017-094] (Elephantine), and the hind parts are in the city of Sunt (?).[FN#184]
[017-095] One portion thereof is on the east side[FN#185] of the river, and
[017-096] another portion is on the west side[FN#186] of the river, and another
[017-097] portion is in the middle[FN#187] of the river. The stream decketh the
[017-098] region with its waters during a certain season of the year, and it is a
[017-099] place of delight for every man. And works are carried on among these
[017-100] quarries [which are] on the edges [of the river?], "for the stream
[017-101] immediately faceth this city of Abu itself, and there existeth the
[017-102] granite, the substance whereof is hard (?); 'Stone of Abu' it is
[017-103] called.

[FN#183] Kens extended south from Philae as far as Korosko.

[FN#184] Perhaps Sunut, = the Syene of the Greeks, and the {hbw  
[017-104] SuWeNeH} of the Hebrews.

[FN#185] i.e., Syene.

[FN#186] i.e., Contra Syene.

[FN#187] i.e., the Island of Elephantine.

[017-105] "[Here is] a list of the names of the gods who dwell in the Divine
[017-106] House of Khnemu. The goddess of the star Sept (Sothis), the goddess
[017-107] Anqet, Hap (the Nile-god), Shu, Keb, Nut, Osiris, Horus, Isis, and
[017-108] Nephthys.

[017-109] "[Here are] "the names of the stones which lie in the heart of the
[017-110] mountains, some on the east side, some on the west side, and some in
[017-111] [the midst of] the stream of Abu. They exist in the heart of Abu, they
[017-112] exist in the country on the east bank, and in the country on the west
[017-113] bank, and in the midst of the stream, namely, "Bekhen-stone, Meri (or
[017-114] Meli)-stone, Atbekhab (?)-stone, Rakes-stone, and white Utshi-stone;
[017-115] these are found on the east bank. Per-tchani-stone is found on the
[017-116] west bank, and the Teshi-stone in the river.

[017-117] "[Here are] the names of the hard (or, hidden) precious stones, which
[017-118] are found in the upper side, among them being the . . . . . stone, the
[017-119] name[FN#188] of which hath spread abroad through [a space of] four atru
[017-120] measures: Gold, Silver, Copper, Iron, Lapis-lazuli, Emerald, Thehen
[017-121] (Crystal?), Khenem (Ruby), Kai, Mennu, Betka (?), Temi, Na (?). The
[017-122] following come forth from the fore part[FN#189] of the land: Mehi-
[017-123] stone, [He]maki-stone, Abheti-stone, iron ore, alabaster for statues,
[017-124] mother-of-emerald, antimony, seeds (or, gum) of the sehi plant, seeds
[017-125] (or, gum) of the amem plant, and seeds (or, gum) of the incense plant;
[017-126] these are found in the fore parts of its double city." These were the
[017-127] things which I learned therefrom (i.e., from Matar).

[FN#188] i.e., the stone was very famous.

[FN#189] The "fore part," or "front," of the land means the country
[017-128] lying to the south of Nubia, and probably some part of the modern
[017-129] Egyptian Sudan.

[017-130] Now my heart was very happy when I heard these things, and I entered
[017-131] into [the temple of Khnemu]. The overseers unrolled the documents
[017-132] which were fastened up, the water of purification was sprinkled [upon
[017-133] me], a progress was made [through] the secret places, and a great
[017-134] offering [consisting] of bread-cakes, beer, geese, oxen (or, bulls),
[017-135] and beautiful things of all kinds were offered to the gods and
[017-136] goddesses who dwell in Abu, whose names are proclaimed at the place
[017-137] [which is called], "Couch of the heart in life and power."

[017-138] And I found the God standing in front of me, and I made him to be at
[017-139] peace with me by means of the thank-offering which I offered unto him,
[017-140] and I made prayer and supplication before him. Then he opened his
[017-141] eyes, and his heart was inclined [to hear] me, and his words were
[017-142] strong [when he said], "I am Khnemu,[FN#190] who fashioned thee. My
[017-143] two hands were about thee and knitted together thy body, and "made
[017-144] healthy thy members; and it is I who gave thee thy heart. Yet the
[017-145] minerals (or, precious stones) [lie] under each other, [and they have
[017-146] done so] from olden time, and no man hath worked them in order to build
[017-147] the houses of the god, or to restore those which have fallen into ruin,
[017-148] or to hew out shrines for the gods of the South and of the North, or to
[017-149] do what he ought to do for his lord, notwithstanding that I am the Lord
[017-150] and the Creator.

[FN#190] He was the "builder of men, maker of the gods, the Father who
[017-151] was from the beginning, the maker of things which are, the creator of
[017-152] things which shall be, the source of things which exist, Father of
[017-153] fathers, Mother of mothers, Father of the fathers of the gods and
[017-154] goddesses, lord of created things, maker of heaven, earth, Tuat, water
[017-155] and mountains" (Lanzone, Dizionario, p. 957).

[017-156] "I am [he] who created himself, Nu, the Great [God], who came into being
[017-157] at the beginning, [and] Hapi, who riseth according to his will, in
[017-158] order to give health to him that laboureth for me. I am the Director
[017-159] and Guide of all men at their seasons, the Most Great, the Father of the
[017-160] Gods, Shu, the Great One, the Chief of the Earth. The two halves of the
[017-161] sky (i.e., the East and the West) are as a habitation below me. A lake
[017-162] of water hath been poured out for me, [namely,] Hap (i.e., the Nile),
[017-163] which embraceth the field-land, and his embrace provideth the [means of]
[017-164] life for "21 every nose (i.e., every one), according to the extent of
[017-165] his embrace of the field-land. With old age [cometh] the condition of
[017-166] weakness. I will make Hap (i.e., the Nile) rise for thee, and [in] no
[017-167] year shall [he] fail, and he shall spread himself out in rest upon every
[017-168] land. Green plants and herbs and trees shall bow beneath [the weight of]
[017-169] their produce. The goddess Renenet[FN#191] shall be at the head of
[017-170] everything, and every product shall increase by hundreds of thousands,
[017-171] according to the cubit of the year. The people shall be filled, verily
[017-172] to their hearts' desire, "and everyone. Misery shall pass away, and the
[017-173] emptiness of their store-houses of grain shall come to an end. The land
[017-174] of Ta-Mert (i.e., Egypt) shall come to be a region of cultivated land,
[017-175] the districts [thereof] shall be yellow with grain crops, and the grain
[017-176] [thereof] shall be goodly. And fertility shall come according to the
[017-177] desire [of the people], more than there hath ever been before."

[FN#191] The goddess of the harvest.

[017-178] Then I woke up at [the mention of] crops, my heart (or, courage) came
[017-179] [back], and was equal to my [former] despair, and I made the following
[017-180] decree in the temple of my father Khnemu:—

[017-181] The king giveth an offering to Khnemu[FN#192] the Lord of the city of
[017-182] Qebhet,[FN#193] the Governor of Ta-Sti,[FN#194] in return for those

[017-186] space of twenty measures,[FN#196] on the east side and on the west
[017-187] side, with the gardens, and the river front "everywhere throughout the
[017-188] region included in these measures. From every husbandman who tilleth
[017-189] the ground, and maketh to live again the slain, and placeth water upon

[017-192] growing crops and from every storehouse, as "thy share.
