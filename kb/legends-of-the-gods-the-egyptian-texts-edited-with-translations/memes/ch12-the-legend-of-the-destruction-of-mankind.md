### CHAPTER IV.

[012-001] IV. And the majesty of this god said, "Call to me the god Thoth," and
[012-002] one brought the god to him forthwith. And the Majesty of this god said
[012-003] unto Thoth, "Let us depart to a distance from heaven, from my place,
[012-004] because I would make light and the god of light (Khu) in the Tuat and
[012-005] [in] the Land of Caves. Thou shalt write down [the things which are]
[012-006] in it, and thou shalt punish those who are in it, that is to say, the
[012-007] workers who have worked iniquity (or, rebellion). Through thee I will
[012-008] keep away from the servants whom this heart [of mine] loatheth. Thou
[012-009] shalt be in my place (ast) ASTI, and thou shalt therefore be called, O
[012-010] Thoth, the 'Asti of Ra.' Moreover, I give thee power to send (hab)
[012-011] forth . . . . .; thereupon shall come into being the Ibis (habi) bird
[012-012] of Thoth. I moreover give thee [power] to lift up thine hand before
[012-013] the two Companies of the gods who are greater than thou, and what thou
[012-014] doest shall be fairer than [the work of] the god Khen; therefore shall
[012-015] the divine bird tekni of Thoth come into being. Moreover, I give thee
[012-016] [Power] to embrace (anh) the two heavens with thy beauties, and with
[012-017] thy rays of light; therefore shall come into being the Moon-god (Aah)
[012-018] of Thoth. Moreover, I give thee [power] to drive back (anan) the Ha-
[012-019] nebu;[FN#64] therefore shall come into being the dog-headed Ape (anan)
[012-020] of Thoth, and he shall act as governor for me. Moreover, thou art now
[012-021] in my place in the sight of all those who see thee and who present
[012-022] offerings to thee, and every being shall ascribe praise unto thee, O
[012-023] thou who art God."

[FN#64] i.e., the "North-lords," that is to say, the peoples who lived
[012-024] in the extreme north of the Delta, and on its sea-coasts, and perhaps
[012-025] in the Islands of the Mediterranean.
