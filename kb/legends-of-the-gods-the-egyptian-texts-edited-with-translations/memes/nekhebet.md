# Nekhebet

## Overview

Nekhebet (Nekhbet) is the vulture-goddess of Upper Egypt, worshipped at Nekheb (Greek *Eileithyiaspolis*, modern el-Kab). Together with the cobra-goddess [[uatchit]] of Lower Egypt, she forms the "Two Ladies" (*Nebty*) — the dual protectresses whose emblems appear on the pharaoh's crown and in the royal titulary.

## Identity and Role

Nekhebet is the divine guardian of the White Crown (*hedjet*) of Upper Egypt. Her outstretched vulture wings signify sheltering protection. She is also the patroness of childbirth, her role as divine midwife giving her the Greek identification with Eileithyia.

She is the eastern counterpart to Uatchit's northern protection:

> the goddess Nekhebet and the goddess Uatchet, who guard the South [015-072]

> the goddess Nekhebet was incarnate in a special kind of vulture [015-132]

Nekhebet's protectiveness extends to the solar barque:

> the goddesses Uatchet and Nekhebet, who guard the South and North [002-086]

They also take the form of burning serpents to defend against enemies:

> the goddesses Uatchet and Nekhebet in the form of serpents with burning venom [002-209]

## Family and Relationships

Like [[uatchit]], Nekhebet has no extensive mythological genealogy. Her primary relationship is her pairing with Uatchit as the Two Ladies. She is also associated with the pharaoh as his divine nurse — wall reliefs at her temple show her suckling the infant king. She frequently appears alongside [[horus]] in his form as the royal child.

## Major Myths and Narratives

**Guardian of the Royal Birth.** Nekhebet presides over royal births. In New Kingdom birth-houses (*mammisi*), she appears alongside other protective goddesses to safeguard the divine birth of the pharaoh and assure his legitimate rule.

**The Two Ladies in the Solar Journey.** Nekhebet and [[uatchit]] flank the solar barque of [[ra]] as it traverses the sky and underworld:

> the two sister goddesses Nekhebet and Uatchet, on the right and left, guarding the bark [005-083]

In serpent form, they spit venom against the approaching [[apep]]:

> by the goddesses Uatchet and Nekhebet in the form of serpents with burning venom [002-209]

**The Campaign of Heru-Behutet.** Nekhebet joins the forces of [[horus]] in the Winged Disk narrative:

> the goddess Nekhebet was placed at the prow [015-072]

## Cult and Worship

Nekheb (el-Kab), across the Nile from Nekhen (Hierakonpolis), was Nekhebet's principal cult centre. Her temple there was one of the oldest in Egypt, dating to the Predynastic period. The vulture, believed to be an exclusively maternal bird (the Egyptians thought all vultures were female), made Nekhebet a natural patroness of motherhood and protection.
