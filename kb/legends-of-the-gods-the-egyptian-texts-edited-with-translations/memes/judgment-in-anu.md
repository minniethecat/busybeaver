# Judgment of Horus in Anu

## Overview

The Judgment of Horus in Anu was a divine tribunal convened to determine the rightful succession to Osiris's throne. Thoth, the god of wisdom and writing, took charge of Horus's case in the Judgment Hall of Anu (Heliopolis) — the same hall where Osiris himself had been judged. Acting as Horus's advocate, Thoth promised to ensure that all accusations against Horus would be refuted and that the young god would succeed to the Throne of the Two Lands.

## Narrative

### The Setting: the Judgment Hall of Anu

The Judgment Hall of Anu (Heliopolis) was the sacred place where the gods assembled to adjudicate disputes. It was here that Osiris himself had been judged, establishing it as the preeminent venue for divine justice:

> "Osiris had been judged, and that as his advocate he would make any accusations which might be brought against Horus to recoil on him that brought them." [PN=005-227]

### Thoth's Advocacy

After Horus recovered from the scorpion sting caused by Set's agent Uhat, Thoth descended from the Boat of Ra and addressed Isis, giving her assurance of her son's legal vindication:

> "Thoth returned to the Boat of Ra... The final words of Thoth comforted her greatly, for he told her that he would take charge of the case of Horus in the Judgment Hall of Anu, wherein Osiris had been judged, and that as his advocate he would make any accusations which might be brought against Horus to recoil on him that brought them." [PN=005-1787]

### The Scope of the Judgment

Thoth's advocacy was comprehensive. He promised to secure Horus's position against all threats, both cosmic and chthonic:

> "Furthermore, he would give Horus power to repulse any attacks which might be made upon him by beings in the heights above, or fiends in the depths below, and would ensure his succession to the Throne of the Two Lands, i.e., Egypt." [PN=005-1791]

Ra himself acted as the advocate of Horus, even as he had done for Osiris, underscoring the precedent and legitimacy of Horus's claim:

> "Thoth also promised Isis that Ra himself should act as the advocate of Horus, even as he had done for his father Osiris." [PN=005-1795]

## Outcome and Significance

The Judgment of Horus in Anu had profound legal and theological implications:

1. **Precedent of Divine Justice**: The tribunal in Anu established the cosmic legal framework by which rightful inheritance was adjudicated, setting the pattern for all subsequent succession disputes.

2. **Thoth as Divine Advocate**: Thoth's role as advocate reinforced his position as the god of law, wisdom, and writing, the divine scribe who records the verdicts of the gods.

3. **Legitimacy of Horus**: The judgment affirmed Horus as the rightful heir to Osiris, undermining Set's claim to the throne based on usurpation and murder.

4. **Ra's Imprimatur**: Ra's participation as advocate provided the highest divine endorsement of Horus's succession, linking it to the cosmic order governed by the sun god.
