# Thoth

## Overview

Thoth is the god of writing, wisdom, magic, science, and the moon. He serves as the divine scribe and vizier of [[ra]], recording the judgment of the dead before [[osiris]] and mediating disputes among the gods. Thoth invented hieroglyphic writing and all the arts of civilisation.

## Identity and Role

Thoth is the "Scribe of Truth" who records the verdict at the Weighing of the Heart. He is self-created, emerging from the primordial waters as the *Tekni* bird. When [[ra]] grew old and wished to withdraw from the earth, he summoned Thoth to serve as his deputy:

> Ra next sent for the god Thoth, and when he came into the presence of the god Ra, he asked him to act as his vicar [001-169]

> Thoth, the Scribe of Truth, to write down the words of Ra [001-173]

Ra granted Thoth absolute authority to act in his stead, creating the moon as Thoth's emblem of nocturnal rule:

> he deputed to Thoth the power to deal absolutely with all the enemies of Ra [001-175]

> Thoth was to be his vicar, to take his place in the sky while he travelled through the Underworld [001-177]

All that Thoth does is deemed good (*khen*), giving rise to the *Tekni* bird (ibis), his sacred animal. Ra also gave Thoth the power to "turn back" (*anan*), creating the dog-headed baboon, Thoth's other sacred form:

> the Tekni bird of Thoth came into being. He gave Thoth power to turn back (anan) [001-181]

> therefore the dog-headed ape of Thoth came into being [001-184]

## Family and Relationships

Thoth is self-created and has no conventional parentage among the Ennead. In some traditions, his consort is [[seshat]], goddess of writing and measurement. Thoth is the close ally and advisor of [[ra]], [[isis]], and [[horus]], mediating their conflicts and lending his magical knowledge. He is vital to the resurrection of [[osiris]] and the protection of the infant [[horus]].

When the rebellious humans in the Destruction of Mankind myth threatened to overrun divine order, Thoth counselled [[ra]]:

> Thoth, seeing this, told Ra that because the gods had fled, he should not punish them further [002-052]

In the conflict between [[horus]] and [[set]], Thoth serves as judge and scribe, recording the legal arguments:

> Horus of Behutet and Thoth spearing human victims [002-173]

> Thoth decreed that Horus should be called "Heru-Behutet" for what he had already done [002-184]

## Major Myths and Narratives

**The Deputy of Ra.** When [[ra]] retired from the earth, he made Thoth his vicar. Thoth governs by night as the moon, his light a reflection of Ra's solar radiance. Ra promises:

> Thoth that he would take his place, and that everyone would see the brightness of Thoth [001-185]

**The Contendings.** Thoth serves as scribe and arbitrator in the legal battle between [[horus]] and [[set]] before the Ennead tribunal. When [[set]] uses foul language, Thoth rebukes him, calling him "Nehaha-her" (Foul-of-Face):

> Set used such foul language that Thoth called him "Nehaha-her," i.e., "Foul-of-Face" [002-130]

**The Healing of Horus.** When [[horus]] is wounded, Thoth aids [[isis]] in healing him, reciting spells and driving away the poison. His magical knowledge makes him the patron of all physicians and magicians.

**The Judgment of the Dead.** In the Hall of Two Truths, Thoth records the result of the Weighing of the Heart:

> Ra, Sekhet, Thoth, and Heka, who will blind and choke the enemies of Osiris [005-123]

## Cult and Worship

Thoth's primary cult centre was at Khmun (Hermopolis Magna), where he headed the Ogdoad — the eight primordial gods of creation. His worship extended throughout Egypt, with major temples also at Dakka in Nubia and in the Bahariya Oasis. As the patron of scribes, every scribe poured a libation from their water-pot to Thoth before beginning work each day.

Millions of mummified ibises and baboons — his sacred animals — were dedicated to him at his cult centres, particularly at Saqqara and Tuna el-Gebel. The Book of the Dead identifies Thoth as the scribe who records the verdict at the judgment and as the god who invented writing:

> Thoth, the Scribe of Truth, to write down the words [001-173]
