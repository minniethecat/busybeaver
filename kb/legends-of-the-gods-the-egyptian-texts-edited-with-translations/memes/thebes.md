# Thebes

**Thebes** (Egyptian **Uast** or **Waset**) was the great southern capital of ancient Egypt and the primary cult center of [[amen|Amen-Ra]]. Located on the west bank of the Nile in Upper Egypt, Thebes was known as the "Mighty City," renowned for its temples, its College of Magic, and its role as the setting for many of the mythological narratives preserved in the corpus.

## Overview

Thebes was one of the most magnificent cities of the ancient world. The corpus describes it as the place where Pharaoh Rameses II held court and celebrated festivals in honor of [[amen|Amen-Ra]]. The city's grandeur and religious importance made it a natural destination for foreign envoys seeking Egyptian aid — most notably in the Legend of the Possessed Princess of [[Bekhten]].

## Mythological Significance

Thebes was known as the "city of Amit" in one mythological context, where Ra's decree brought beautiful women into being there (009-130). The city was home to numerous gods and goddesses, with the texts honoring "the Company of the Gods, the Lords of Thebes, the beloved one" (016-117).

The city held a special place in the Egyptian magical tradition. Its College of Magic was the most prestigious institution of its kind, and the qenbetu (magistrate-priests) of Thebes advised the Pharaoh on matters requiring divine intervention:

> "The royal scribe was summoned, and the qenbetu officials of the great College of Magic at Thebes were consulted." *(003-052)*

## The Temple of Khensu

The **Temple of Khensu** at Thebes was central to the Legend of the Possessed Princess. When the Prince of [[Bekhten]] sought healing for his daughter Bent-enth-resh, he came to Thebes to petition the Pharaoh during a festival of Amen-Ra in the Temple of Luxor (003-063). The statue of **Khensu Nefer-hetep**, "the expeller of spirits," was dispatched from Thebes on a seventeen-month journey to Bekhten to cure the princess.

## In the Texts

The city of Thebes witnessed one of the great battles between [[Horus|Heru-Behutet]] and the followers of [[Set]]:

> "One battle took place at Tch... near Thebes." *(002-092)*

Ra commanded that his image be established to the south of Thebes, reinforcing the city's position as a divine stronghold. The texts also record that His Majesty was present "in Thebes, the Mighty city, the Mistress of cities" (016-146).

Plutarch's account, referenced in the corpus, notes that the inhabitants of Thebes were among those who abstained from certain animal sacrifices, reflecting distinct local religious customs (023-163).
