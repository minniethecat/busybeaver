## I.—INCANTATIONS AGAINST REPTILES AND NOXIOUS CREATURES IN GENERAL.

[018-001] Get thee back, Apep, thou enemy of Ra, thou winding serpent in the form
[018-002] of an intestine, without arms [and] without legs. Thy body cannot
[018-003] stand upright so that thou mayest have therein being, long is
[018-004] thy[FN#199] tail in front of thy den, thou enemy; retreat before Ra.
[018-005] Thy head shall be cut off, and the slaughter of thee shall be carried
[018-006] out. Thou shalt not lift up thy face, for his (i.e., Ra's) flame is in
[018-007] thy accursed soul. The odour which is in his chamber of slaughter is
[018-008] in thy members, and thy form shall be overthrown by the slaughtering
[018-009] knife of the great god. The spell of the Scorpion-goddess Serq driveth
[018-010] back thy might. Stand still, stand still, and retreat through her
[018-011] spell.

[FN#199] Literally, "his."

[018-012] Be vomited, O poison, I adjure thee to come forth on the earth. Horus
[018-013] uttereth a spell over thee, Horus hacketh thee in pieces, he spitteth
[018-014] upon thee; thou shalt not rise up towards heaven, but shalt totter
[018-015] downwards, O feeble one, without strength, cowardly, unable to fight,
[018-016] blind, without eyes, and with thine head turned upside down. Lift not
[018-017] up thy face. Get thee back quickly, and find not the way. Lie down in
[018-018] despair, rejoice not, retreat speedily, and show not thy face because
[018-019] of the speech of Horus, who is perfect in words of power. The poison
[018-020] rejoiced, [but] the heart[s] of many were very sad thereat. Horus hath
[018-021] smitten it with his magical spells, and he who was in sorrow is [now]
[018-022] in joy. Stand still then, O thou who art in sorrow, [for] Horus hath
[018-023] been endowed with life. He coineth charged, appearing himself to
[018-024] overthrow the Sebiu fiends which bite. All men when they see Ra praise
[018-025] the son of Osiris. Get thee back, Worm, and draw out thy poison which
[018-026] is in all the members of him that is under the knife. Verily the might
[018-027] of the word of power of Horus is against thee. Vomit thou, O Enemy, get
[018-028] thee back, O poison.
