# Tefnut

## Overview

Tefnut (Tefenet) is the goddess of moisture in the Heliopolitan creation system. She is one of the first created deities, emitted from the self-created god **Tem** (or Ra) together with her twin brother Shu. As the female principle paired with Shu (air/dryness), she represents the moisture necessary for life and is part of the Great Ennead of Heliopolis. With Shu she produced Geb (Earth) and Nut (Sky), forming the second generation of the cosmic family.

## Identity and Role

Tefnut's essential identity is tied to the **primeval act of creation**. The creator god Tem describes emitting her: "I sent forth from myself the goddess Tefnut" [007-017]. Together with Shu she constitutes the first differentiation of the primordial unity: the twin deities of air and moisture who together fill the space between earth and sky.

She is also identified with the **Eye of Ra** — the fiery, protective solar eye that acts as the god's emissary [009-010]. In magic spells she is invoked as a model of divine power: "thy right eye like Shu, and thy left eye like Tefnut" [022-053].

## Family and Relationships

Tefnut is the daughter of the self-created god **Tem** (Ra), who produced her without a consort: "I brought my own name into my mouth as a word of power, and I straightway came into being... Then I emitted Shu, and I emitted Tefnut" [008-029]. She is thus one of the earliest emanations from the creator.

Her twin brother and consort is **Shu**, god of air and light. From their union came **Geb** (Earth) and **Nut** (Sky) [007-042]: "Shu and Tefnut brought into being Geb and Nut." This makes Tefnut the grandmother of Osiris, Isis, Set, and Nephthys in the Heliopolitan genealogy.

## Major Myths and Narratives

### The Creation of Shu and Tefnut

The creation narrative in Chapter 7 describes the self-generation of Tem, who produces Shu and Tefnut as his first children. The pair emerge from the primordial waters (Nu) and rejoice: "Then Shu and Tefnut rejoiced from out of the inert mass of primeval water wherein they were" [007-028]. They are said to have remained behind the creator "for very many hen periods" [008-042] as the world was being shaped.

### The Eye of Ra

In Chapter 9, Tefnut is listed alongside Shu and other cosmic powers as part of the divine company: "the Eye, and Shu, and Tefnut, and Seb, and Nut, and the fathers and mothers" [009-010]. Her association with the Eye of Ra connects her to solar theology and the protective, sometimes destructive, power of the sun god.

## Cult and Worship

Tefnut's cult was centred in **Heliopolis** as part of the Ennead worship. While she had fewer independent temples than some other deities, her role in the creation cycle meant she was invoked in foundational rituals. Her connection to moisture made her relevant to agricultural fertility and the inundation cycle.
