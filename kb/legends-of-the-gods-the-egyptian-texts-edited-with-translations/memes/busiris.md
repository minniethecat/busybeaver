# Busiris

**Busiris** (Egyptian **Pa-Asar**, "House of Osiris") was one of the most ancient and important cult centers of [[Osiris]] in the Nile Delta. The city's very name proclaimed its devotion to the god, and it was one of the principal sites where the relics of Osiris's dismembered body were venerated.

## Overview

Located in the Delta region of Lower Egypt, Busiris was known in Egyptian as **Pa-Asar-neb-Tetut**, which the Greeks rendered as Busiris (016-013). The name "House of Osiris" signifies the city's role as one of the god's primary earthly dwellings. The city was a center of the Djed pillar cult, the symbol of Osiris's backbone and stability.

## Mythological Significance

Busiris was one of the cities where [[Isis]] was believed to have deposited a portion of Osiris's body after recovering the pieces scattered by [[Set]]. The city claimed to house the backbone (Djed) of Osiris, making it a focal point for the Djed pillar festival — one of the most important Osirian rituals celebrating the god's resurrection and stability:

> "Is Busiris, where likewise he was worshipped, and the inhabitants of Busiris and Lycopolis never use trumpets..." *(023-727)*

The city was invoked in magical healing texts, where the power of Osiris at Busiris was called upon to neutralize poisons and protect the afflicted:

> "Busiris, the poison shall not work against you." *(019-035)*

## Rituals and Practices

The inhabitants of Busiris were noted for distinctive religious observances. According to the corpus, which draws on Plutarch's account, the people of Busiris abstained from certain practices that were common elsewhere in Egypt, reflecting the specific ritual purity codes associated with the Osirian cult (023-849).

During the annual Osiris festival, the raising of the Djed pillar at Busiris symbolized the resurrection of the god and the restoration of cosmic order. The ceremony drew pilgrims from across the Delta region.

## In the Texts

The Isis-Osiris narrative records that Isis, having collected the scattered pieces of Osiris's body, traveled to Busiris to offer prayers for his safe passage to the afterlife:

> "Permit ye him to go to Busiris. Let your nostrils be closed and let not the enemies of Osiris draw nigh unto him." *(020-018)*

This passage, from a protective invocation, underscores Busiris's role as a sanctuary city where Osiris could find eternal rest and protection from the agents of [[Set]].
