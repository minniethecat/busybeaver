# Buto

**Buto** (Egyptian **Pe-Tept** or **Per-Wadjet**) was a city in the northwestern Nile Delta, the cult center of the cobra goddess **Wadjet**, patron deity of Lower Egypt. Its marshlands provided the legendary refuge where [[Isis]] concealed the infant [[Horus]] from [[Set]] after the murder of [[Osiris]].

## Overview

Buto was one of the most ancient settlements in the Delta, predating the unification of Egypt. As the cult center of Wadjet — the cobra goddess who, together with the vulture goddess Nekhebet of Upper Egypt, formed the dual uraeus that adorned the pharaoh's crown — Buto held immense symbolic importance in Egyptian kingship ideology.

## The Marshes of Buto

The city's marshlands played a crucial role in the Osiris-Horus myth cycle. After concealing the chest containing Osiris's body, Isis fled to the Delta marshes to give birth to and raise Horus in secret:

> "Horus was born in the marshes of Buto, because the vapour of damp lands strengthens the constitution." *(023-969)*

Plutarch's account emphasizes that the humid Delta environment was believed to foster the health and growth of the divine child, providing a natural explanation for why Isis chose this location as her sanctuary.

## The City of Pe-Tept

The city's Egyptian name, Pe-Tept, appears in the magical texts in connection with divine nurses:

> "The nurses who dwell in the city Pe-Tept (Buto), for they have offered..." *(022-258)*

This reference to divine nurses aligns with the mythological tradition that Isis was aided in hiding and raising Horus by local deities and protective spirits dwelling in the Buto marshes.

## In the Osiris Cycle

Buto also features in the narrative of the search for Osiris's body. Plutarch records that Isis stopped at Buto during her journey across Egypt:

> "At Buto she deposited the chest..." *(023-560)*

The city thus marks an important waypoint in Isis's journey — the place where she temporarily concealed the chest containing Osiris before continuing to other sanctuaries in the Delta.

## In the Texts

Beyond the mythological references, Buto's identity as the cult center of Wadjet placed it at the heart of Egyptian royal symbolism. The uraeus cobra worn by the pharaoh represented the goddess of Buto, and the city's temple was among the most venerable in Lower Egypt.
