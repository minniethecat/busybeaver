# Anubis

## Overview

Anubis (Egyptian *Anpu*) is the jackal-headed god of embalming, funerary rites, and guardian of the necropolis. He is the divine embalmer who prepared the body of [[osiris]] for burial — establishing the rites of mummification that all Egyptians would follow. In the judgment hall, Anubis operates the scales that weigh the heart of the deceased against the feather of [[maat]].

## Identity and Role

Anubis is "He Who Is Upon His Mountain" (*Tepy-dju-ef*), the vigilant guardian watching over the necropolis from the desert cliffs where jackals prowled. His black colour symbolizes both the discolouration of the corpse after embalming and the fertile black soil of rebirth.

He is identified as the son of Osiris in some traditions:

> the son of Osiris (Sarapis), Isis, Anubis the jackal-god [006-111]

The parentage of Anubis is discussed in comparative religion:

> their offspring was Anubis, Anpu [023-417]

> it obtained the name of Anubis, and it is thought that this name was derived from the Egyptian word for "jackal" [023-485]

## Family and Relationships

Anubis's parentage varies by tradition. In the most common account, he is the son of [[osiris]] and [[nephthys]] — Nephthys having seduced Osiris by disguising herself as [[isis]]. Isis then raised the child as her own. In other traditions, he is the son of [[ra]] and [[hesat]] (the cow-goddess), or of Set and Nephthys.

Anubis is a loyal ally of Isis in the Osiris cycle. After Set murdered and dismembered Osiris, Anubis assisted Isis in gathering the pieces and performed the first mummification — wrapping the body in linen bandages and anointing it with preservative oils.

## Major Myths and Narratives

**The First Embalming.** After Osiris was slain by [[set]], Anubis embalmed his body, establishing the funerary rites. Every subsequent mummification ritual re-enacted this primordial event, with the chief embalmer wearing the mask of Anubis. Through his craft, Osiris became the first mummy, able to resurrect in the underworld.

**The Weighing of the Heart.** In the Hall of Two Truths (*Maaty*), Anubis leads the deceased to the scales and operates the balance. He weighs the heart (*ib*) against the feather of [[maat]]. [[thoth]] records the result. If the heart balances, the deceased is declared "true of voice" (*maa-kheru*) and admitted to the realm of Osiris.

**Guardian of the Necropolis.** Anubis patrols the desert edge, protecting tombs from grave-robbers and wild animals:

> Anubis was the guardian of the necropolis [023-972]

His image as a recumbent jackal on a shrine appears on tomb seals and funerary equipment throughout Egyptian history.

## Cult and Worship

The principal cult centre of Anubis was at the necropolis of Hardai (Greek *Cynopolis*, "City of the Dog") in Upper Egypt. However, Anubis was worshipped in every necropolis across Egypt. Embalmers' workshops featured shrines to Anubis, and the *imiut* fetish — a headless stuffed animal skin on a pole — represented his presence during mummification.
