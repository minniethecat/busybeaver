[015-001] XII. In the three hundred and sixty-third year of Ra-Heru-Khuti, who

[015-003] soldiers were with him; [the enemy] did not conspire (auu) against
[015-004] their lord, and the land [is called] Uauatet unto this day. And Ra set
[015-005] out on an expedition in his boat, and his followers were with him, and
[015-006] he arrived at Uthes-Heru,[FN#76] [which lay to] the west of this nome,
[015-007] and to the east of the canal Pakhennu, which is called [ . . . . . . .
[015-008] to this day]. And Heru-Behutet was in the boat of Ra, and he said
[015-009] unto his father Ra-Heru-Khuti (i.e., Ra-Harmachis), "I see that the
[015-010] enemies are conspiring against their lord; let thy fiery serpent gain
[015-011] the mastery . . . . . over them."

[015-012] about the modern Kalabsha. In ancient days Ta-kens appears to have
