[014-001] The Chapter of the divine (or, mighty) god, who created himself, who
[014-002] made the heavens and the earth, and the breath of life, and fire, and
[014-003] the gods, and men, and beasts, and cattle, and reptiles, and the fowl
[014-004] of the air, and the fish, who is the king of men and gods, [who
[014-005] existeth] in one Form, [to whom] periods of one hundred and twenty
[014-006] years axe as single years, whose names by reason of their multitude are
[014-007] unknowable, for [even] the gods know them not. Behold, the goddess
[014-008] Isis lived in the form, of a woman, who had the knowledge of words [of
[014-009] power]. Her heart turned away in disgust from the millions of men, and
[014-010] she chose for herself the millions of the gods, but esteemed more
[014-011] highly the millions of the spirits. Was it not possible to become even
[014-012] as was Ra in heaven and upon earth, and to make [herself] mistress of
[014-013] the earth, and a [mighty] goddess—thus she meditated in her heart—by
[014-014] the knowledge of the Name of the holy god? Behold, Ra entered [heaven]
[014-015] each day at the head of his mariners, establishing himself upon the
[014-016] double throne of the two horizons. Now the divine one had become old,
[014-017] he dribbled at the mouth, and he let his emissions go forth from him
[014-018] upon the earth, and his spittle fell upon the ground. This Isis
[014-019] kneaded in her hand,[FN#66] with [some] dust, and she fashioned it in
[014-020] the form of a sacred serpent, and made it to have the form of a dart,
[014-021] so that none might be able to escape alive from it, and she left it
[014-022] lying upon the road whereon the great god travelled, according to his
[014-023] desire, about the two lands. Then the holy god rose up in the
[014-024] tabernacle of the gods in the great double house (life, strength,
[014-025] health!) among those who were in his train, and [as] he journeyed on
[014-026] his way according to his daily wont, the holy serpent shot its fang
[014-027] into him, and the living fire was departing from the god's own body,
[014-028] and the reptile destroyed the dweller among the cedars. And the mighty
[014-029] god opened his mouth, and the cry of His Majesty (life, strength,
[014-030] health!) reached unto the heavens, and the company of the gods said,
[014-031] "What is it?" and his gods said, "What is the matter?" And the god
[014-032] found [no words] wherewith to answer concerning himself. His jaws
[014-033] shook, his lips trembled, and the poison took possession of all his
[014-034] flesh just as Hapi (i.e., the Nile) taketh possession of the land
[014-035] through which he floweth. Then the great god made firm his heart
[014-036] (i.e., took courage) and he cried out to those who were in his
[014-037] following:—"Come ye unto me, O ye who have come into being from my
[014-038] members,[FN#67] ye gods who have proceeded from me, for I would make
[014-039] you to know what hath happened. I have been smitten by some deadly
[014-040] thing, of which my heart hath no knowledge, and which I have neither
[014-041] seen with my eyes nor made with my hand; and I have no knowledge at all
[014-042] who hath done this to me. I have never before felt any pain like unto
[014-043] it, and no pain can be worse than this [is]. I am a Prince, the son of
[014-044] a Prince, and the divine emanation which was produced from a god. I am
[014-045] a Great One, the son of a Great One, and my father hath determined for
[014-046] me my name. I have multitudes of names, and I have multitudes of
[014-047] forms, and my being existeth in every god. I have been invoked (or,
[014-048] proclaimed?) by Temu and Heru-Hekennu. My father and my mother uttered
[014-049] my name, and [they] hid it in my body at my birth so that none of those
[014-050] who would use against me words of power might succeed in making their
[014-051] enchantments have dominion over me.[FN#68] I had come forth from my
[014-052] tabernacle to look upon that which I had made, and was making my way
[014-053] through the two lands which I had made, when a blow was aimed at me,
[014-054] but I know not of what kind. Behold, is it fire? Behold, is it water?
[014-055] My heart is full of burning fire, my limbs are shivering, and my
[014-056] members have darting pains in them. Let there be brought unto me my
[014-057] children the gods, who possess words of magic, whose mouths are cunning
[014-058] [in uttering them], and whose powers reach up to heaven." Then his
[014-059] children came unto him, and every god was there with his cry of
[014-060] lamentation; and Isis[FN#69] came with her words of magic, and the
[014-061] place of her mouth [was filled with] the breath of life, for the words
[014-062] which she putteth together destroy diseases, and her words make to live
[014-063] those whose throats are choked (i.e., the dead). And she said, "What
[014-064] is this, O divine father? What is it? Hath a serpent shot his venom
[014-065] into thee? Hath a thing which thou hast fashioned lifted up its head
[014-066] against thee? Verily it shall be overthrown by beneficent words of
[014-067] power, and I will make it to retreat in the sight of thy rays." The
[014-068] holy god opened his mouth, [saying], I was going along the road and
[014-069] passing through the two lands of my country, for my heart wished to
[014-070] look upon what I had made, when I was bitten by a serpent which I did
[014-071] not see; behold, is it fire? Behold, is it water? I am colder than
[014-072] water, I am hotter than fire, all my members sweat, I myself quake,
[014-073] mine eye is unsteady. I cannot look at the heavens, and water forceth
[014-074] itself on my face as in the time of the Inundation."[FN#70] And Isis
[014-075] said unto Ra, "O my divine father, tell me thy name, for he who is able
[014-076] to pronounce his name liveth." [And Ra said], "I am the maker of the
[014-077] heavens and the earth, I have knit together the mountains, and I have
[014-078] created everything which existeth upon them. I am the maker of the
[014-079] Waters, and I have made Meht-ur to come into being; I have made the
[014-080] Bull of his Mother, and I have made the joys of love to exist. I am
[014-081] the maker of heaven, and I have made to be hidden the two gods of the
[014-082] horizon, and I have placed the souls of the gods within them. I am the
[014-083] Being who openeth his eyes and the light cometh; I am the Being who
[014-084] shutteth his eyes and there is darkness. I am the Being who giveth the
[014-085] command, and the waters of Hapi (the Nile) burst forth, I am the Being
[014-086] whose name the gods know not. I am the maker of the hours and the
[014-087] creator of the days. I am the opener (i.e., inaugurator) of the
[014-088] festivals, and the maker of the floods of water. I am the creator of
[014-089] the fire of life whereby the works of the houses are caused to come
[014-090] into being. I am Khepera in the morning, and Ra (at the time of his
[014-091] culmination (i.e., noon), and Temu in the evening."[FN#71]
[014-092] Nevertheless the poison was not driven from its course, and the great
[014-093] god felt no better. Then Isis said unto Ra, "Among the things which
[014-094] thou hast said unto me thy name hath not been mentioned. O declare
[014-095] thou it unto me, and the poison shall come forth; for the person who
[014-096] hath declared his name shall live." Meanwhile the poison burned with
[014-097] blazing fire and the heat thereof was stronger than that of a blazing
[014-098] flame. Then the Majesty of Ra, said, "I will allow myself to be
[014-099] searched through by Isis, and my name shall come forth from my body and
[014-100] go into hers." Then the divine one hid himself from the gods, and the
[014-101] throne in the Boat of Millions of Years[FN#72] was empty. And it came
[014-102] to pass that when it was the time for the heart to come forth [from the
[014-103] god], she said unto her son Horus, "The great god shall bind himself by
[014-104] an oath to give his two eyes."[FN#73] Thus was the great god made to
[014-105] yield up his name, and Isis, the great lady of enchantments, said,
[014-106] "Flow on, poison, and come forth from Ra; let the Eye of Horus come
[014-107] forth from the god and shine(?) outside his mouth. I have worked, and
[014-108] I make the poison to fall on the ground, for the venom hath been
[014-109] mastered. Verily the name hath been taken away from the great god.
[014-110] Let Ra live, and let the poison die; and if the poison live then Ra
[014-111] shall die. And similarly, a certain man, the son of a certain man,
[014-112] shall live and the poison shall die." These were the words which spake
[014-113] Isis, the great lady, the mistress of the gods, and she had knowledge
[014-114] of Ra in his own name. The above words shall be said over an image of
[014-115] Temu and an image of Heru-Hekennu,[FN#74] and over an image of Isis and
[014-116] an image of Horus.

[FN#66] Here we have another instance of the important part which the
[014-117] spittle played in magical ceremonies that were intended to produce evil
[014-118] effects. The act of spitting, however, was intended sometimes to carry
[014-119] a curse with it, and sometimes a blessing, for a man spat in the face
[014-120] of his enemy in order to lay the curse of impurity upon him, and at the
[014-121] present time, men spit upon money to keep the devils away from it.

[FN#67] The gods were, according to one belief, nothing more than the
[014-122] various names of Ra, who had taken the forms of the various members of
[014-123] his body.

[FN#68] Thus the god's own name became his most important talisman.

[FN#69] The position of Isis as the "great enchantress" is well
[014-124] defined, and several instances of her magical powers are recorded. By
[014-125] the utterance of her words of power she succeeded in raising her dead
[014-126] husband Osiris to life, and she enabled him by their means to beget
[014-127] Horus of her. Nothing could withstand them, because they were of
[014-128] divine origin, and she had learned them from Thoth, the intelligence of
[014-129] the greatest of the gods.

[FN#70] Or, "the period of the summer." The season Shemmu, began soon
[014-130] after the beginning of April and lasted until nearly the end of July.

[FN#71] Khepera, Rd, and Temu were the three principal forms of the  
[014-131] Sun-god according to the theological system of the priests of
[014-132] Heliopolis.

[FN#72] The name by which the Boat of Ra is generally known in  
[014-133] Egyptian texts. It was this boat which was stopped in its course when
[014-134] Thoth descended from the sky to impart to Isis the words of power that
[014-135] were to raise her dead child Horus to life.

[FN#73] i.e., the fluid of life of the sun, and the fluid of life of
[014-136] the moon. The sun and the moon were the visible, material symbols of
[014-137] the Sun god.

[FN#74] The attributes of this god are not well defined. He was a god
[014-138] of the Eastern Delta, and was associated with the cities where Temu was
[014-139] worshipped.
