### THE LEGEND OF RA AND ISIS.

[003a-001] The original text of this very interesting legend is written in the
[003a-002] hieratic character on a papyrus preserved at Turin, and was published
[003a-003] by Pleyte and Rossi in their Corpus of Turin Papyri.[FN#16] French and
[003a-004] German translations of it were published by Lefebure,[FN#17] and
[003a-005] Wiedemann[FN#18] respectively, and summaries of its contents were given
[003a-006] by Erman[FN#19] and Maspero.[FN#20] A transcript of the hieratic text
[003a-007] into hieroglyphics, with transliteration and translation, was published
[003a-008] by me in 1895.[FN#21]

[FN#16] Papyrus de Turin, pll. 31, 77, 131-138.

[FN#17] A. Z., 1883, p. 27 ff.

[FN#18] Die Religion, p. 29.

[FN#19] Aegypten, p. 359 ff.

[FN#20] Les Origines, V. 162-4.

[FN#21] First Steps in Egyptian, p. 241 ff.

[003a-009] It has already been seen that the god Ra, when retiring from the
[003a-010] government of this world, took steps through Thoth to supply mankind
[003a-011] with words of power and spells with which to protect themselves against
[003a-012] the bites of serpents and other noxious reptiles. The legend of the
[003a-013] Destruction of Mankind affords no explanation of this remarkable fact,
[003a-014] but when we read the following legend of Ra and Isis we understand why
[003a-015] Ra, though king of the gods, was afraid of the reptiles which lived in
[003a-016] the kingdom of Keb. The legend, or "Chapter of the Divine God," begins
[003a-017] by enumerating the mighty attributes of Ra as the creator of the
[003a-018] universe, and describes the god of "many names" as unknowable, even by
[003a-019] the gods. At this time Isis lived in the form of a woman who possessed
[003a-020] the knowledge of spells and incantations, that is to say, she was
[003a-021] regarded much in the same way as modern African peoples regard their
[003a-022] "medicine-women," or "witch-women." She had used her spells on men,
[003a-023] and was tired of exercising her powers on them, and she craved the
[003a-024] opportunity of making herself mistress of gods and spirits as well as
[003a-025] of men. She meditated how she could make herself mistress both of
[003a-026] heaven and earth, and finally she decided that she could only obtain
[003a-027] the power she wanted if she possessed the knowledge of the secret name
[003a-028] of Ra, in which his very existence was bound up. Ra guarded this name
[003a-029] most jealously, for he knew that if he revealed it to any being he
[003a-030] would henceforth be at that being's mercy. Isis saw that it was
[003a-031] impossible to make Ra declare his name to her by ordinary methods, and
[003a-032] she therefore thought out the following plan. It was well known in
[003a-033] Egypt and the Sudan at a very early period that if a magician obtained
[003a-034] some portion of a person's body, e.g., a hair, a paring of a nail, a
[003a-035] fragment of skin, or a portion of some efflux from the body, spells
[003a-036] could be used upon them which would have the effect of causing grievous
[003a-037] harm to that person. Isis noted that Ra had become old and feeble, and
[003a-038] that as he went about he dribbled at the mouth, and that his saliva
[003a-039] fell upon the ground. Watching her opportunity she caught some of the
[003a-040] saliva of the and mixing it with dust, she moulded it into the form of
[003a-041] a large serpent, with poison-fangs, and having uttered her spells over
[003a-042] it, she left the serpent lying on the path, by which Ra travelled day
[003a-043] by day as he went about inspecting Egypt, so that it might strike at
[003a-044] him as he passed along. We may note in passing that the Banyoro in the
[003a-045] Sudan employ serpents in killing buffaloes at the present day. They
[003a-046] catch a puff-adder in a noose, and then nail it alive by the tip of its
[003a-047] tail to the round in the middle of a buffalo track, so that when an
[003a-048] animal passes the reptile may strike at it. Presently a buffalo comes
[003a-049] along, does what it is expected to do, and then the puff-adder strikes
[003a-050] at it, injects its poison, and the animal dies soon after. As many as
[003a-051] ten buffaloes have been killed in a day by one puff-adder. The body of
[003a-052] the first buffalo is not eaten, for it is regarded as poisoned meat,
[003a-053] but all the others are used as food.[FN#22]

[FN#22] Johnston, Uganda, vol. ii., p. 584. The authority for this
[003a-054] statement is Mr. George Wilson, formerly Collector in Unyoro.

[003a-055] Soon after Isis had placed the serpent on the Path, Ra passed by, and
[003a-056] the reptile bit him, thus injecting poison into his body. Its effect
[003a-057] was terrible, and Ra cried out in agony. His jaws chattered, his lips
[003a-058] trembled, and he became speechless for a time; never before had be
[003a-059] suffered such pain. The gods hearing his cry rushed to him, and when
[003a-060] he could speak he told them that he had been bitten by a deadly
[003a-061] serpent. In spite of all the words of power which were known to him,
[003a-062] and his secret name which had been hidden in his body at his birth, a
[003a-063] serpent had bitten him, and he was being consumed with a fiery pain.
[003a-064] He then commanded that all the gods who had any knowledge of magical
[003a-065] spells should come to him, and when they came, Isis, the great lady of
[003a-066] spells, the destroyer of diseases, and the revivifier of the dead, came
[003a-067] with them. Turning to Ra she said, "What hath happened, O divine
[003a-068] Father?" and in answer the god told her that a serpent had bitten him,
[003a-069] that he was hotter than fire and colder than water, that his limbs
[003a-070] quaked, and that he was losing the power of sight. Then Isis said to
[003a-071] him with guile, "Divine Father, tell me thy name, for he who uttereth
[003a-072] his own name shall live." Thereupon Ra proceeded to enumerate the
[003a-073] various things that he had done, and to describe his creative acts, and
[003a-074] ended his speech to Isis by saying, that he was Khepera in the morning,
[003a-075] Ra at noon, and Temu in the evening. Apparently he thought that the
[003a-076] naming of these three great names would satisfy Isis, and that she
[003a-077] would immediately pronounce a word of power and stop the pain in his
[003a-078] body, which, during his speech, had become more acute. Isis, however,
[003a-079] was not deceived, and she knew well that Ra had not declared to her his
[003a-080] hidden name; this she told him, and she begged him once again to tell
[003a-081] her his name. For a time the god refused to utter the name, but as the
[003a-082] pain in his body became more violent, and the poison passed through his
[003a-083] veins like fire, he said, "Isis shall search in me, and my name shall
[003a-084] pass from my body into hers." At that moment Ra removed himself from
[003a-085] the sight of the gods in his Boat, and the Throne in the Boat of
[003a-086] Millions of Years had no occupant. The great name of Ra was, it seems,
[003a-087] hidden in his heart, and Isis, having some doubt as to whether Ra would
[003a-088] keep his word or not, agreed with Horus that Ra must be made to take an
[003a-089] oath to part with his two Eyes, that is, the Sun and the Moon. At
[003a-090] length Ra allowed his heart to be taken from his body, and his great
[003a-091] and secret name, whereby he lived, passed into the possession of Isis.
[003a-092] Ra thus became to all intents and purposes a dead god. Then Isis,
[003a-093] strong in the power of her spells, said: "Flow, poison, come out of Ra.
[003a-094] Eye of Horus, come out of Ra, and shine outside his mouth. It is I,
[003a-095] Isis, who work, and I have made the poison to fall on the ground.
[003a-096] Verily the name of the great god is taken from him, Ra shall live and
[003a-097] the poison shall die; if the poison live Ra shall die."

[003a-098] This was the infallible spell which was to be used in cases of
[003a-099] poisoning, for it rendered the bite or sting of every venomous reptile
[003a-100] harmless. It drove the poison out of Ra, and since it was composed by
[003a-101] Isis after she obtained the knowledge of his secret name it was
[003a-102] irresistible. If the words were written on papyrus or linen over a
[003a-103] figure of Temu or Heru-hekenu, or Isis, or Horus, they became a mighty
[003a-104] charm. If the papyrus or linen were steeped in water and the water
[003a-105] drunk, the words were equally efficacious as a charm against snake-
[003a-106] bites. To this day water in which the written words of a text from the
[003a-107] Kur'an have been dissolved, or water drunk from a bowl on the inside of
[003a-108] which religious texts have been written, is still regarded as a never-
[003a-109] failing charm in Egypt and the Sudan. Thus we see that the modern
[003a-110] custom of drinking magical water was derived from the ancient
[003a-111] Egyptians, who believed that it conveyed into their bodies the actual
[003a-112] power of their gods.
