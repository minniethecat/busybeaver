# Suten-Henen

**Suten-Henen** (Greek **Heracleopolis Magna**, modern **Ihnasya el-Medina**) was a major city of Middle Egypt near the Fayum, celebrated as the coronation site and residence of [[Osiris]] during his earthly reign. Its name means "City of the Henen Plant," and it ranked among the five greatest cult centers of Egypt.

## Overview

Suten-Henen held a pivotal place in Egyptian royal mythology as the city where Osiris first assumed the throne of Egypt. This event established the city as a foundational locus of legitimate kingship, and its association with Osiris's reign before his murder by [[Set]] endowed it with profound mythological significance. In later periods, Suten-Henen served as a strategic center guarding the approaches to the Fayum and the Western Desert oases.

## Coronation of Osiris

The most significant event associated with Suten-Henen was the crowning of Osiris as the first legitimate king of Egypt. The texts record this foundational moment:

> "Osiris also was crowned at Suten-henen, and in this city lived..." *(001-060–061)*

As the site of this primordial coronation, Suten-Henen became a symbol of legitimate rulership. Every subsequent pharaoh, through ritual and symbol, could trace their authority back to this original act of divine kingship. The city thus represented the mythic wellspring of Egyptian royal ideology.

## The Slaughter at Suten-Henen

In the **Legend of the Destruction of Mankind**, Suten-Henen was the starting point of the divine punishment of rebellious humanity:

> "The slaughter of men began at Suten-henen..." *(001-056)*

The goddess [[Hathor]] (or [[Sekhet]]), acting as the Eye of Ra, descended upon the rebels and began her work of destruction in this city. Blood flowed through the streets:

> "The goddess waded in their blood, beginning at Suten-henen..." *(009-049)*

This episode established Suten-Henen as a liminal site where divine wrath first manifested in the human world, a place where the boundary between divine power and mortal consequence was made visible.

## Among the Great Cult Centers

The texts list Suten-Henen among the greatest cult centers of Egypt, alongside [[Heliopolis]], [[Memphis]], [[Hermopolis]], and [[Abydos]]. This enumeration confirms the city's status as one of the foundational religious centers of Egyptian civilization, despite having fewer corpus mentions than its peers.

## In the Texts

Beyond the coronation and destruction narratives, Suten-Henen is identified as the city of the Henen plant, from which it derived its Egyptian name. The connection between the city's name and its sacred flora reflects the Egyptian practice of linking urban identity to botanical and mythological symbolism.
