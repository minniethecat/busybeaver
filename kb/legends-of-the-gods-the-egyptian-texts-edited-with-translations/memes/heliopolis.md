# Heliopolis

**Heliopolis** (Egyptian **Annu**, biblical **On**) was the most important solar cult center of ancient Egypt, serving as the earthly seat of [[Ra]] and the birthplace of the [[Ennead]] theology that shaped Egyptian religion for three millennia.

## Overview

Located near modern Cairo on the east bank of the Nile, Heliopolis housed the **Het-Aat** (Great House) — the temple complex where [[Ra]] was worshipped as the supreme god. The city was the seat of the Ennead, the company of nine gods: [[Ra]], [[Shu]], [[Tefnut]], [[Seb]], [[Nut]], [[Osiris]], [[Isis]], [[Set]], and [[Nephthys]], who held their divine council there.

The temple precinct contained the sacred **Benben stone**, a pyramid-shaped object symbolizing the primeval mound from which [[Ra]] first emerged at the moment of creation.

## Mythological Significance

Heliopolis is identified as the place where [[Ra]] first emerged from the primordial waters of [[Nu]] and where the creator god held court over both gods and mankind. According to the Egyptian texts, it was here that Ra summoned the elder gods to consult on the problem of mankind's rebellion against his authority.

The city was also the dwelling place of **Sekhet**, the fierce lioness aspect of the goddess [[Hathor]]. When Ra decreed the destruction of rebellious mankind, Sekhet went forth from Annu to carry out the slaughter:

> "The Majesty of Ra spake unto the goddess Nu, saying: 'Let the goddess Sekhet go forth to Annu (Heliopolis) to crush.' And behold, when the goddess Sekhet came to Annu, she crushed the enemies of Ra, and waded in their blood." *(009-057)*

## The Temple of Heliopolis

The temple at Heliopolis was one of the most magnificent in ancient Egypt. The corpus describes it as a structure greater than any other temple of its time:

> "The temple of Heliopolis" was celebrated throughout Egypt, being described as even greater than the famous temple of Heliopolis that was built in later dynasties. *(001-037)*

The temple housed the priests who maintained the solar cult and developed the sophisticated Heliopolitan creation theology. These priests were so dedicated that later Greek writers recorded their strict purity practices:

> "The priests of the Sun at Heliopolis never carry wine into the temple, for they hold it to be wrong to drink it in sight of their god and lord." *(023-161)*

## In the Texts

The **Destruction of Mankind** legend records that Sekhet's slaughter began from Heliopolis, where she "crushed and ground up" the enemies of Ra (001-072). The city remained a major cult center into the Ptolemaic period, referenced alongside [[Thebes]], [[Memphis]], and [[Abydos]] (023-596).

The sacred [[Persea tree]] of Heliopolis was a landmark where the names of kings were inscribed. The texts record that the god lived "by the Persea tree in Heliopolis" (021-139), and that the city was renowned for its balsam-trees (021-136).
