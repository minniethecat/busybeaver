# Khepera

## Overview

Khepera (Egyptian *ḫpr*, "to become, to come into being") is the **scarab beetle** form of the sun god, representing the **morning sun** and the principle of self-generated creation. He is the first phase of the daily solar cycle: "I am Khepera in the morning, and Ra at the culmination, and Temu in the evening" [014-090]. As the creator who brought himself into existence, he embodies the concept of spontaneous becoming.

## Identity and Attributes

Khepera's name derives from the Egyptian verb *kheper* ("to become"), and he represents the transformative, creative power of the sun at its birth. His iconographic form is the **scarab beetle** (*Scarabaeus sacer*), which the Egyptians observed rolling balls of dung — interpreted as an analogue for the sun rolling across the sky [022-217].

The god describes his own creation:

> "I came into being under the form of the god Khepera, and I am the creator of that which came into being" [007-006], [008-004]

This self-creation was not a single event but a continuous process: "I came into being from out of the primeval matter, and the things of Khepera which were made were more numerous than those which came into being" [008-026], [008-003].

## The Solar Triad

Khepera is the first of the **three principal forms of the sun god**:

| Form | Time of Day | Phase |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Khepera** | Morning / Dawn | Coming into being |
| **Ra** | Noon / Culmination | Full power |
| **Temu** | Evening / Sunset | Decline and rest |

As the Egyptian texts explain: "Khepera, Ra, and Temu were the three principal forms of the Sun-god, representing the rising sun, the midday sun, and the setting sun" [014-130], [014-091].

## Myths and Narratives

### The Creator at Dawn

Khepera features centrally in the creation accounts where the sun god describes his own origins. Before creation, he existed in the primeval waters of **Nu**, and then brought himself into being:

> "I came into being in the form of Khepera, and I am the creator of that which came into being — yea, multitudes of things came forth from my mouth before the heavens and the earth existed" [007-006], [008-004]

This cosmogonic role places Khepera at the very beginning of existence, as the first living being who initiated the process of creation.

### The Beetle Symbolism

The scarab beetle of Khepera was one of the most ubiquitous religious symbols in ancient Egypt. The beetle pushing its ball of dung across the ground was seen as a miniature reflection of Khepera pushing the sun across the sky [022-217]. This made the scarab amulet one of the most popular forms of protective jewellery, associated with resurrection and renewal.

## Cult and Worship

Khepera did not have a major independent cult centre. Instead, he was primarily honoured through:
- **Scarab amulets**: One of the most common Egyptian religious artifacts, worn for protection and rebirth
- **Solar hymns**: Invocations of the sun god in his morning form
- **Funerary texts**: Khepera's self-creation provided a model for the deceased's hoped-for rebirth

The **beetle of Khepera** was "a form of the Sun-god when he rises on this world" and was believed to bring about the renewal of life each morning [022-217].
