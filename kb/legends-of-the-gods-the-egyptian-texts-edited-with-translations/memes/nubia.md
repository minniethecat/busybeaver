# Nubia

**Nubia** (Egyptian **Uaua**, also **Ta-Kens** or **the Land of the Bow**) was the region lying immediately south of Egypt, stretching from the First Cataract into the African interior. It was a land of strategic military importance, known for its skilled archers and recurrent rebellions against Egyptian rule.

## Overview

Nubia was Egypt's southern neighbour and rival, frequently mentioned in the context of military expeditions. The region was known by several names: **Uaua** (or Uauatet) after a conspiracy against Ra, **Ta-Kens** in northern Nubia, and the "Land of the Bow" for its famed archers. The border between Egypt and Nubia was traditionally marked at the First Cataract near [[Elephantine]].

## Mythological Significance

Nubia's name, according to the texts, derives from a conspiracy. When Ra-Harmakhis was in the region, certain enemies rose against him:

> "In the 363rd year of his reign Ra-Harmakhis was in Nubia with his army, and certain men rebelled against him; because of their conspiracy (*auu*) Nubia is called 'Uaua' to this day." *(002-038–041)*

The naming of the land after an act of rebellion reflects the Egyptian mythological conflation of history, geography, and divine narrative.

## Military Campaigns

The texts record multiple military engagements in Nubia, particularly under Ra-Harmakhis. After vanquishing Set's followers in Egypt, Ra sailed south to confront further rebellion:

> "Meanwhile rebellion had again broken out in Nubia, where about one-third of the enemy forces were stationed." *(002-199)*

In a later campaign, Ra confronted the Uaua tribes:

> "His Majesty was in Ta-Kens, and his soldiers defeated their enemies in the country of Uaua, and they were uttering cries of lamentation." *(015-002, 015-400–401)*

The Nubian forces were distinguished by their archery, earning the region the title "Land of the Bow." The texts note that the nations of Nubia "fought with bows and arrows" (016-127), making them formidable adversaries.

## Geography and Extent

Northern Nubia, known as Ta-Kens, extended from the island of [[Philae]] southward to the settlement of Korosko. The texts define its boundary: "A portion of Northern Nubia. The Nome of the City of the Beginning reacheth to Uaua" (017-054, 017-048). Beyond this lay the broader territories of Nubia and lands "lying to the south of Nubia, probably some part of the modern Sudan" (017-128).

## In the Texts

The journey from Nubia formed a key episode in the **Legend of the Winged Sun Disk**. After his Nubian campaign, Ra-Harmakhis sailed down the river to [[Edfu]], where he celebrated his victories and established the cult of Horus Behutet (002-041). The region thus marked the starting point of a divine progress through Egypt that established temples and cult centers along the Nile.
