# Procreation of Horus

## Overview

The Procreation of Horus is the divine conception event in which Isis, having restored the body of the murdered Osiris to temporary life, assumed the form of a hawk and hovered over him to conceive their son Horus. This event is depicted on Plate XIV of the papyrus and represents the miraculous union that produced the rightful heir to Osiris's throne — a conception that occurred after death, through magical means, ensuring the continuity of divine kingship.

## Narrative

### The Transformation of Isis

After restoring Osiris to life through her magical spells [FN#214], Isis transformed herself into a hawk. In this form, she hovered over the body of Osiris, and by the beating of her wings she fanned the breath of life into him. The text describes the moment:

> "She made to rise up the helpless members of him whose heart was at rest, she drew from him his essence, and she made therefrom an heir." [PN=010-002]

### The Conception

Through this miraculous union, Isis conceived Horus, the son who would avenge his father. Plate XIV depicts this moment — titled "The Procreation of Horus, son of Isis" — showing Isis as a hawk hovering above the recumbent Osiris:

> "This bare statement of the dogma of the conception of Horus does not explain all the details, but the accompanying vignette makes the meaning clear." [PN=010-003]

### The Aftermath

Following the conception, Isis withdrew to the papyrus swamps of the Delta to give birth in secret. She declared:

> "I am Isis, who conceived a child by her husband, and she became heavy with Horus." [FN#229]

The conception was a closely guarded secret, known only to the gods who witnessed it. Isis kept the pregnancy hidden from Set, knowing that he would seek to destroy any heir of Osiris.

## Outcome and Significance

The Procreation of Horus was a pivotal event with far-reaching consequences:

1. **Continuity of Divine Kingship**: Without this miraculous conception, the line of Osiris would have ended with his murder. Horus became the legitimate heir who would reclaim the throne from the usurper Set.

2. **Posthumous Fatherhood**: The event established the theological possibility of posthumous conception — the dead Osiris could still produce an heir through magical means. This concept was crucial to Egyptian funerary beliefs about regeneration after death.

3. **The Hawk Symbolism**: Isis's transformation into a hawk became a powerful iconographic motif. The image of the hawk hovering over Osiris was reproduced in countless funerary contexts as a symbol of regeneration and rebirth.

4. **Magical Power of Isis**: The procreation demonstrated the supreme magical power of Isis, who could not only restore life to the dead but also conceive from that temporarily animated body. This established her as the greatest of all magician deities.
