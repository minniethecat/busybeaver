# Resurrection of Osiris

## Overview

The Resurrection of Osiris is one of the most significant events in Egyptian mythology. After Osiris was murdered by his brother Set, his sister-wife Isis, aided by Nephthys, used magical spells to restore him to life. Though Osiris could no longer dwell in the world of the living, his resurrection enabled him to become the ruler of the Duat (Underworld) and the judge of the dead. This event was annually commemorated at Abydos in a miracle play and forms the foundation of the Egyptian belief in life after death.

## Narrative

### The Murder of Osiris

Set, driven by jealousy of his brother Osiris, murdered him during a time of conflict. According to the traditions preserved in the later accounts, Set tricked Osiris into lying in a chest at a banquet, then sealed it and cast it into the Nile. The chest floated to Byblos, where it became embedded in a tree that was cut down for the palace of the king. [PN=009-001]

> "Egyptian literature is full of allusions to events which took place in the life of Osiris, and to his persecution, murder, and resurrection, and numerous texts of all periods describe the love and devotion of his sister and wife Isis, and the filial piety of Horus." [PN=009-001]

### The Search and Resurrection

Isis set out in search of Osiris's body. When she found it, she and Nephthys performed the sacred rites of lamentation. The Festival Songs of Isis and Nephthys, preserved in a papyrus, contain the words and spells used in this ritual.

> "By uttering spells Isis restored life to her husband Osiris for a season, and so became with child by him." [FN#214]

Through her magical knowledge, Isis was able to temporarily restore Osiris to life. During this brief reunion, she conceived their son Horus, who would later avenge his father.

### The Abydos Commemoration

The resurrection of Osiris was commemorated annually at Abydos in a dramatic ritual performance:

> "At Abydos a sort of miracle play, in which all the sufferings and resurrection of Osiris were commemorated, was performed annually, and the raising up of a model of his body, and the placing of his head upon it, were the culminating ceremonies." [PN=023-708]

## Outcome and Significance

The Resurrection of Osiris had profound theological consequences:

1. **Ruler of the Underworld**: Osiris could no longer dwell among the living but became king of the Duat and judge of the dead. Every deceased Egyptian hoped to be justified before Osiris.

2. **Foundation of Funerary Belief**: The resurrection established the precedent that death could be overcome through magical spells and proper ritual — the core belief behind Egyptian mummification and funerary texts.

3. **Birth of Horus**: The brief revival allowed Isis to conceive Horus, the heir who would later avenge Osiris and reclaim the throne from Set.

4. **Annual Ritual Cycle**: The event gave rise to the annual Festival of Osiris at Abydos, one of the most important religious celebrations in Egypt.
