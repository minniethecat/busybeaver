[016-001] Homage to thee, Osiris, Lord of eternity, King of the gods, whose names
[016-002] are manifold, whose transformations are sublime, whose form is hidden
[016-003] in the temples whose Ka is holy, the Governor of Tetut,[FN#119] the
[016-004] mighty one of possessions (?)in the shrine,[FN#120] the Lord of
[016-005] praises[FN#121] in the nome of Anetch,[FN#122] President of the tchefa
[016-006] food in Anu,[FN#123] Lord who art commemorated in [the town of]
[016-007] Maati,[FN#124] the mysterious (or, hidden) Soul, the Lord of
[016-008] Qerret,[FN#125] the sublime one in White Wall,[FN#126] the Soul of Ra
[016-009] [and] his very body, who hast thy dwelling in Henensu,[FN#127] the
[016-010] beneficent one, who art praised in Nart,[FN#128] who makest to rise up
[016-011] thy Soul, Lord of the Great House in the city[FN#129] of the Eight
[016-012] Gods,[FN#130] [who inspirest] great terror in Shas-hetep,[FN#131] Lord
[016-013] of eternity, Governor of Abtu (Abydos).

[FN#119] More fully Pa-Asar-neb-Tetut, the Busiris of the Greeks;  
[016-014] Busiris = Pa-Asar, "House of Osiris," par excellence. The variant
[016-015] Tataut also occurs.

[FN#120] An allusion, perhaps, to the town Sekhem, the capital of the
[016-016] second nome (Letopolites) of Lower Egypt.

[FN#121] i.e., lord whose praises are sung.

[FN#122] Letopolites.

[FN#123] Heliopolis.

[FN#124] i.e., a famous sanctuary in the Letopolite nome where Ptah
[016-017] was worshipped.

[FN#125] The region of the First Cataract, where the Nile was believed
[016-018] to rise.

[FN#126] Memphis.

[FN#127] Herakleopolis, the {hbw XaNeS} of Isaiah.

[FN#128] A name of Herakleopolis.

[FN#129] Khemenu or Hermopolis, the city of Thoth.

[FN#130] These gods were: Nu and Nut; Hehu and Hehut; Kekui and  
[016-019] Kekuit; Kerh and Kerhet.

[FN#131] The capital of Set, the eleventh nome of Upper Egypt; the
[016-020] chief local deity was Khnemu.

[016-021] Thy seat (or, domain) reacheth far into Ta-tchesert,[FN#132] and thy
[016-022] name is firmly stablished in the mouth[s] of men. Thou art the two-
[016-023] fold substance of the Two Lands[FN#133] everywhere (?), and the divine
[016-024] food (tchef) of the Kau,[FN#134] the Governor of the Companies[FN#135]
[016-025] of the Gods, and the beneficent (or, perfect) Spirit-soul[FN#136] among
[016-026] Spirit-souls. The god Nu draweth his waters from thee,[FN#137] and
[016-027] thou bringest forth the north wind at eventide, and wind from thy
[016-028] nostrils to the satisfaction of thy heart. Thy heart flourisheth, and
[016-029] thou bringest forth the splendour of tchef food.

[FN#132] A name of the Other World.

[FN#133] i.e., the two Egypts, Upper and Lower.

[FN#134] The Doubles of the beatified who are fed by Osiris in the  
[016-030] Other World.

[FN#135] Three Companies are distinguished: the gods of Heaven, the
[016-031] gods of Earth, and the gods of the Other World.

[FN#136] The indestructible, immortal Spirit-soul as opposed to the  
[016-032] Ba-soul or animal-soul.

[FN#137] Here and in other places I have changed the pronoun of the
[016-033] third person into that of the second to avoid the abrupt changes of the
[016-034] original.

[016-035] The height of heaven and the stars [thereof] are obedient unto thee,
[016-036] and thou makest to be opened the great gates [of the sky]. Thou art
[016-037] the lord to whom praises are sung in the southern heaven, thou art he
[016-038] to whom thanks are given in the northern heaven. The stars which never
[016-039] diminish are under the place of thy face,[FN#138] and thy seats are the
[016-040] stars which never rest.[FN#139] Offerings appear before thee by the
[016-041] command of Keb. The Companies of the Gods ascribe praise unto thee,
[016-042] the Star-gods of the Tuat smell the earth before thee,[FN#140] the
[016-043] domains [make] bowings [before thee], and the ends of the earth make
[016-044] supplication to thee [when] they see thee.

[FN#138] i.e., they are under thy inspection and care.

[FN#139] i.e., the stars which never set. The allusion is probably to
[016-045] certain circumpolar stars.

[FN#140] i.e., do homage.

[016-046] Those who are among the holy ones are in terror of him, and the Two
[016-047] Lands, all of them, make acclamations to him when they meet His
[016-048] Majesty. Thou art a shining Noble at the head of the nobles, permanent
[016-049] in [thy] high rank, stablished in [thy] sovereignty, the beneficent
[016-050] Power of the Company of the Gods. Well-pleasing [is thy] face, and
[016-051] thou art beloved by him that seeth thee. Thou settest the fear of thee
[016-052] in all lands, and because of their love for thee [men] hold thy name to
[016-053] be pre-eminent. Every man maketh offerings unto thee, and thou art the
[016-054] Lord who is commemorated in heaven and upon earth. Manifold are the
[016-055] cries of acclamation to thee in the Uak[FN#141] festival, and the Two
[016-056] Lands shout joyously to thee with one accord. Thou art the eldest, the
[016-057] first of thy brethren, the Prince of the Company of the Gods, and the
[016-058] stablisher of Truth throughout the Two Lands. Thou settest [thy] son
[016-059] upon the great throne of his father Keb. Thou art the beloved one of
[016-060] thy mother Nut, whose valour is most mighty [when] thou overthrowest
[016-061] the Seba Fiend. Thou hast slaughtered thy enemy, and hast put the fear
[016-062] of thee into thy Adversary.

[FN#141] One of the chief festivals of Osiris, during which the god
[016-063] made a periplus.

[016-064] Thou art the bringer in of the remotest boundaries, and art stable of
[016-065] heart, and thy two feet are lifted up (?); thou art the heir of Keb and
[016-066] of the sovereignty of the Two Lands, and he (i.e., Keb) hath seen thy
[016-067] splendid qualities, and hath commanded thee to guide the lands (i.e.,
[016-068] the world) by thy hand so long as times [and seasons] endure.

[016-069] Thou hast made this earth with thy hand, the waters thereof, the winds
[016-070] thereof, the trees and herbs thereof, the cattle thereof of every kind,
[016-071] the birds thereof of every kind, the fish thereof of every kind, the
[016-072] creeping things thereof, and the four-footed beasts thereof. The land
[016-073] of the desert[FN#142] belongeth by right to the son of Nut, and the Two
[016-074] Lands have contentment in making him to rise[FN#143] upon the throne of
[016-075] his father like Ra.

[FN#142] This may also represent the mountainous districts of Egypt,
[016-076] or even foreign countries in general.

[FN#143] To make him rise like the sun, or to enthrone him.

[016-077] Thou rollest up into the horizon, thou settest the light above the
[016-078] darkness, thou illuminest [the Two Lands] with the light from thy two
[016-079] plumes, thou floodest the Two Lands like the Disk at the beginning of
[016-080] the dawn. Thy White Crown pierceth the height of heaven saluting the
[016-081] stars,[FN#144] thou art the guide of every god. Thou art
[016-082] perfect[FN#145] in command and word. Thou art the favoured one of the
[016-083] Great Company of the Gods, and thou art the beloved one of the Little
[016-084] Company of the Gods.

[FN#144] Or, "becoming a brother to the stars," or the Star-gods.

[FN#145] Or, beneficent.

[016-085] Thy sister [Isis] acted as a protectress to thee. She drove [thy]
[016-086] enemies away, she averted seasons [of calamity from thee], she recited
[016-087] the word (or, formula) with the magical power of her mouth, [being]
[016-088] skilled of tongue and never halting for a word, being perfect in
[016-089] command and word. Isis the magician avenged her brother. She went
[016-090] about seeking for him untiringly.

[016-091] She flew round and round over this earth uttering wailing cries of
[016-092] grief, and she did not alight on the ground until she had found him.
[016-093] She made light [to come forth] from her feathers, she made air to come
[016-094] into being by means of her two wings, and she cried out the death cries
[016-095] for her brother. She made to rise up the helpless members of him whose
[016-096] heart was at rest, she drew from him his essence, and she made
[016-097] therefrom an heir. She suckled the child in solitariness and none knew
[016-098] where his place was, and he grew in strength. His hand is mighty (or,
[016-099] victorious) within the house of Keb, and the Company of the Gods
[016-100] rejoice greatly at the coming of Horus, the son of Osiris, whose heart
[016-101] is firmly stablished, the triumphant one, the son of Isis, the flesh
[016-102] and bone of Osiris. The Tchatcha[FN#146] of Truth, and the Company of
[016-103] the Gods, and Neb-er-tcher[FN#147] himself, and the Lords of Truth,
[016-104] gather together to him, and assemble therein.[FN#148] Verily those who
[016-105] defeat iniquity rejoice[FN#149] in the House of Keb to bestow the
[016-106] divine rank and dignity upon him to whom it belongeth, and the
[016-107] sovereignty upon him whose it is by right.

[FN#146] Literally, the "Heads," I.e., the divine sovereign Chiefs at
[016-108] the court of Osiris, who acted as administrators of the god, and even
[016-109] as task-masters.

[FN#147] "He who is the lord to the end (or, limit) of the world," a
[016-110] name of Osiris.

[FN#148] i.e., in the House of Keb.

[FN#149] Or perhaps "take their seats in the House of Keb."

## A LEGEND OF PTAH NEFER-HETEP AND THE PRINCESS OF BEKHTEN.

[016-111] The Horus: "Mighty Bull, the form(?) of risings[FN#150], stablished in
[016-112] sovereignty like Tem." The Golden Horus: "Mighty one of
[016-113] strength[FN#151], destroyer of the Nine Nations of the Bow."[FN#152]
[016-114] King of the South and North: "The Lord of the Two Lands, User-Maat-Ra-
[016-115] setep-en-Ra Son of Ra: Of his body, Ra-meses-meri-Amen, of Amen-
[016-116] Ra;[FN#153] the Lord of the thrones of the Two Lands, and of the
[016-117] Company of the Gods, the Lords of Thebes, the beloved one. The
[016-118] beneficent god, the son of Amen, born of Mut, begotten of Heru-khuti,
[016-119] the glorious offspring of Neb-tchert,[FN#154] begetting [as] the Bull
[016-120] of his Mother, [FN#155] king of Egypt, Governor of the deserts, the
[016-121] Sovereign who hath taken possession of the Nine Nations of the Bow;
[016-122] [who] on coming forth from the womb ordained mighty things, who gave
[016-123] commands whilst he was in the egg, the Bull, stable of heart, who hath
[016-124] sent forth his seed; the king who is a bull, [and] a god who cometh
[016-125] forth on the day of battle like Menthu,[FN#156] the mighty one of
[016-126] strength like the son of Nut."[FN#157]

[FN#150] i.e., the image who rises like the sun day by day, or the
[016-127] image of [many] crowns.

[FN#151] Or, mighty one of the thigh, i.e., he of the mighty thigh.

[FN#152] The nations of Nubia who fought with bows and arrows.

[FN#153] In this version of the protocol of Rameses II. the second
[016-128] "strong name" of the king is omitted.

[FN#154] i.e., Neb-er-tcher.

[FN#155] Ka-mut-f, the {greek kamh^fic} of the Greeks.

[FN#156] The War-god of Thebes.

[FN#157] i.e., Osiris.

[016-129] Behold, His Majesty was in the country of Neheru[FN#158] according to
[016-130] his custom every year, and the chiefs of every land, even as far as the
[016-131] swamps, came [to pay] homage, bearing offerings to the Souls of His
[016-132] Majesty; and they brought their gifts, gold, lapis-lazuli, turquoise,
[016-133] bars of wood of every kind of the Land of the God,[FN#159] on their
[016-134] backs, and each one surpassed his neighbour.

[FN#158] The "country of the rivers," the {hbw AaRam NaHaRaYim} of  
[016-135] Gen. xxiv. 10, the #### of Syrian writers.

[FN#159] A name including Western Asia and a portion of the East Coast
[016-136] of Africa.

[016-137] And the Prince of Bekhten [also] caused his gifts to be brought, and he
[016-138] set his eldest daughter at the head of them all, and he addressed words
[016-139] of praise to His Majesty, and prayed to him for his life. And the
[016-140] maiden was beautiful, and His Majesty considered her to be the most
[016-141] lovely [woman] in the world, and he wrote down as her title, "Great
[016-142] Royal Wife, Ra-neferu"; and when His Majesty arrived in Egypt, he did
[016-143] for her whatsoever was done for the Royal Wife.

[016-144] On the twenty-second day of the second month of the season of
[016-145] Shemu,[FN#160] in the fifteenth year [of his reign], behold, His
[016-146] Majesty was in Thebes, the Mighty [city], the Mistress of cities,
[016-147] performing the praises of Father Amen, the Lord of the thrones of the
[016-148] Two Lands, in his beautiful Festival of the Southern Apt,[FN#161] which
[016-149] was the seat of his heart (i.e., the chosen spot) from primaeval time,
[016-150] [when] one came to say to His Majesty, "An ambassador of the Prince of
[016-151] Bekhten hath arrived bearing many gifts for the Royal Wife."

[FN#160] The summer. The Copts called the second month of this season  
[016-152] Paoni.

[FN#161] The modern Temple of Luxor.

[016-153] And having been brought into the presence of His Majesty with his
[016-154] gifts, he spake words of adoration to His Majesty, saying, "Praise be
[016-155] unto thee, O thou Sun (Ra) of the Nine Nations of the Bow, permit us to
[016-156] live before thee!" And when he had spoken, and had smelt the earth
[016-157] before His Majesty, he continued his speech before His Majesty, saying,
[016-158] "I have come unto thee, my King and Lord, on behalf of Bent-Resht, the
[016-159] younger sister of the Royal Wife Ra-neferu. [Some] disease hath
[016-160] penetrated into her members, and I beseech Thy Majesty to send a man of
[016-161] learning to see her."

[016-162] And His Majesty said, "Bring to me the magicians (or, scribes) of the
[016-163] House of Life, and the nobles of the palace." And having been brought
[016-164] into his presence straightway, His Majesty said unto them, "Behold, I
[016-165] have caused you to be summoned [hither] in order that ye may hear this
[016-166] matter. Now bring to me [one] of your company whose heart is
[016-167] wise[FN#162], and whose fingers are deft." And the royal scribe
[016-168] Tehuti-em-heb came into the presence of His Majesty, and His Majesty
[016-169] commanded him to depart to Bekhten with that ambassador.

[FN#162] Or, a skilled craftsman.

[016-170] And when the man of learning had arrived in Bekhten, he found Bent-
[016-171] Resht in the condition of a woman who is possessed by a spirit, and he
[016-172] found 12 this spirit to be an evil one, and to be hostile in his
[016-173] disposition towards him.

[016-174] And the Prince of Bekhten sent a messenger a second time into the
[016-175] presence of His Majesty, saying, "O King, my Lord, I pray His (i.e.,
[016-176] Thy) Majesty to command that a god be brought hither to contend
[016-177] against the spirit."

[016-178] Now when the messenger came to His Majesty in the first month[FN#163]
[016-179] of the season of Shemu, in the twenty-sixth year [of his reign], on the
[016-180] day which coincided with that of the Festival of Amen, His Majesty was
[016-181] in the palace (or, temple?) of Thebes. And His Majesty spake a second
[016-182] time[FN#164] in the presence of Khensu in Thebes, [called] "Nefer-
[016-183] Hetep," saying, "O my fair Lord, I present myself before thee a second
[016-184] time on behalf of the daughter of the Prince of Bekhten." Then Khensu,
[016-185] in Thebes, [called] "Nefer-Hetep", was carried to Khensu, [called] "Pa-
[016-186] ari-sekher," the great god who driveth away the spirits which attack.
[016-187] And His Majesty spake before Khensu in Thebes, [called] "Nefer-Hetep,"
[016-188] saying, "O my fair Lord, if thou wilt give (i.e., turn) thy face to
[016-189] Khensu, [called] 'Pa-ari-sekher,' the great god who driveth away the
[016-190] spirits which attack, permit thou that he may depart to Bekhten;" [and
[016-191] the god] inclined his head with a deep inclination twice. And His
[016-192] Majesty said, "Let, I pray, thy protective (or, magical) power [go]
[016-193] with him, so that I may make His Majesty to go to Bekhten to deliver
[016-194] the daughter of the Prince of Bekhten [from the spirit]."

[FN#163] The month Pakhon of the Copts.

[FN#164] The text makes no mention of the first application to Khensu.

[016-195] And Khensu in Thebes, [called] "Nefer-Hetep," inclined his head with a
[016-196] deep inclination twice. And he made [his] protective power to pass
[016-197] into Khensu, [called] "Pa-ari-sekher-em-Uast," in a fourfold measure.
[016-198] Then His Majesty commanded that Khensu, [called] "Pa-ari-sekher-em-
[016-199] Uast," should set out on his journey in a great boat, [accompanied by]
[016-200] five smaller boats, and chariots, and a large number of horses [which
[016-201] marched] on the right side and on the left.

[016-202] And when this god arrived in Bekhten at the end of a period of one year
[016-203] and five months, the Prince of Bekhten came forth with his soldiers and
[016-204] his chief[s] before Khensu, [called] "Pa-ari-sekher," and he cast
[016-205] himself down upon his belly, saying, "Thou hast come to us, and thou
[016-206] art welcomed by us, by the commands of the King of the South and North,
[016-207] User-Maat-Ra-setep-en-Ra!"

[016-208] And when this god had passed over to the place where Bent-Resht was, he
[016-209] worked upon the daughter of the Prince of Bekhten with his magical
[016-210] power, and she became better (i.e., was healed) straightway. And this
[016-211] spirit which had been with her said, in the presence of Khensu,
[016-212] [called] "Pa-ari-sekher-em-Uast," "Come in peace (i.e., Welcome!), O
[016-213] great god, who dost drive away the spirits which attack! Bekhten is
[016-214] thy city, the people thereof, both men and women, are thy (servants,
[016-215] and I myself am thy servant. I will [now] depart unto the place whence
[016-216] I came, so that I may cause thy heart to be content about the matter
[016-217] concerning which thou hast come. I pray that Thy Majesty will command
[016-218] that a happy day (i.e., a festival, or day of rejoicing) be made with
[016-219] me, and with the Prince of Bekhten." And this god inclined his head
[016-220] [in approval] to his priest, saying, "Let the Prince of Bekhten make a
[016-221] great offering in the (presence of this spirit."

[016-222] Now whilst Khensu, [called] "Pa-ari-sekher-em-Uast," was arranging
[016-223] these [things] with the spirit, the Prince of Bekhten and his soldiers
[016-224] were standing there, and they feared with an exceedingly great fear.
[016-225] And the Prince of Bekhten made a great offering in the presence of
[016-226] Khensu, [called] "Pa-ari-sekher-em-Uast," and the spirit of the Prince
[016-227] of Bekhten, and he made a happy day (i.e., festival) on their behalf,
[016-228] and [then] the spirit departed in peace unto the place which he loved,
[016-229] by the command of Khensu, [called] "Pa-ari-sekher-em-Uast." And the
[016-230] Prince of Bekhten, and every person who was in the country of Bekhten,
[016-231] rejoiced very greatly, and he took counsel with his heart, saying, "It
[016-232] hath happened that this god hath been given as a gift to Bekhten, and I
[016-233] will not permit him to depart to Egypt."

[016-234] And [when] this god had tarried for three years and nine months in
[016-235] Bekhten, the Prince of Bekhten, who was lying down asleep on his bed,
[016-236] saw this god come forth outside his shrine (now he was in the form of a
[016-237] golden hawk), and he flew up into the heavens and departed to Egypt;
[016-238] and when the Prince woke up he was trembling. And he said unto the
[016-239] prophet of Khensu, [called] "Pa-ari-sekher-em-Uast," "This god who
[016-240] tarried with us hath departed to Egypt; let his chariot also depart to
[016-241] Egypt."

[016-242] And the Prince of Bekhten permitted [the image of] the god to set out
[016-243] for Egypt, and he gave him many great gifts of beautiful things of all
[016-244] kinds, and a large number of soldiers and horses [went with him]. And
[016-245] when they had arrived in peace in Thebes, Khensu, [called] "Pa-ari-
[016-246] sekher-em-Uast," went into the Temple of Khensu in Thebes, [called]
[016-247] "Nefer-Hetep," and he placed the offerings which the Prince of Bekhten
[016-248] had given unto him, beautiful things of all kinds, before Khensu in
[016-249] Thebes, [called] "Nefer-Hetep," and he gave nothing thereof whatsoever
[016-250] to his [own] temple.

[016-251] Thus Khensu, [called] "Pa-ari-sekher-em-Uast," arrived in his temple in
[016-252] peace, on the nineteenth day of the second month[FN#165] of the season
[016-253] Pert, in the thirty-third year of the [reign of the] King of the South
[016-254] and North, User-Maat-en-Ra-setep-en-Ra, the giver of life, like Ra, for
[016-255] ever.

[FN#165] The month Mekhir of the Copts; the season Pert is the  
[016-256] Egyptian spring.
