# Winged Disk

The **Winged Disk** (Egyptian **Behudet**) is one of the most iconic symbols of ancient Egypt. A solar disk flanked by outspread falcon wings, it represents [[Horus]] of Behutet in his triumphant form as protector of [[Ra]] and vanquisher of enemies. It appears above temple gateways, on stelae, and on royal crowns throughout the Nile Valley.

## Function and Symbolism

The Winged Disk serves three primary functions:

1. **Protection**: Placed above doorways, it wards off evil and guards sacred thresholds.
2. **Victory**: It commemorates [[Horus]]'s triumph at [[Edfu]], where he transformed into the winged disk and flew into the sky (002-045).
3. **Royal Legitimacy**: As the symbol of [[Horus]], it affirms the pharaoh's divine authority.

The disk is flanked by two uraeus serpents — **Uatchet** (Lower Egypt) and **Nekhebet** (Upper Egypt) — who accompanied [[Horus]] in battle.

## In the Texts

The Legend of Horus of Behutet records the origin of this symbol:

> "Horus of Behutet took the form of a great winged disk, and at once flew up into the sky, where he took the place of Ra." *(002-045)*

[[Ra]] decreed the disk's eternal placement:

> "Thou shalt be called Behutet, and the winged disk shall be set over every temple and every shrine as a sign of divine protection." *(002-054)*

At the temple of [[Edfu]], two Winged Disks are represented flanking the entrance — enduring symbols of [[Horus]]'s eternal guardianship of the sanctuary (015-451). The disk appears in the [[Book of the Dead]] as a protective emblem for the deceased, and above the [[Sun Boat]] in tomb paintings.
