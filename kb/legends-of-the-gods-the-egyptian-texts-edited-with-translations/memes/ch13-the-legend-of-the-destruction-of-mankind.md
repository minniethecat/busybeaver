### CHAPTER V.

[013-001] V. Whosoever shall recite the words of this composition over himself
[013-002] shall anoint himself with olive oil and with thick unguent, and he
[013-003] shall have propitiatory offerings on both his hands of incense, and
[013-004] behind his two ears shall be pure natron, and sweet-smelling salve
[013-005] shall be on his lips. He shall be arrayed in a new double tunic, and
[013-006] his body shall be purified with the water of the nile-flood, and he
[013-007] shall have upon his feet a pair of sandals made of white [leather], and
[013-008] a figure of the goddess Maat shall be drawn upon his tongue with green-
[013-009] coloured ochre. Whensoever Thoth shall wish to recite this composition
[013-010] on behalf of Ra, he must perform a sevenfold (?) purification for three
[013-011] days, and priests and [ordinary] men shall do likewise. Whosoever
[013-012] shall recite the above words shall perform the ceremonies which are to
[013-013] be performed when this book is being read. And he shall make his place
[013-014] of standing (?) in a circle (or, at an angle) . . . . . which is beyond
[013-015] [him], and his two eyes shall be fixed upon himself, all his members
[013-016] shall be [composed], and his steps shall not carry him away [from the
[013-017] place]. Whosoever among men shall recite [these] words shall be like
[013-018] Ra on the day of his birth; and his possessions shall not become fewer,
[013-019] and his house shall never fall into decay, but shall endure for a
[013-020] million eternities.

[013-021] Then the Aged One himself (i.e., Ra) embraced (?) the god Nu, and spake
[013-022] unto the gods who came forth in the east of the sky, "Ascribe ye praise
[013-023] to the god, the Aged One, from whom I have come into being. I am he
[013-024] who made the heavens, and I (set in order [the earth, and created the
[013-025] gods, and] I was with them for an exceedingly long period; then was
[013-026] born the year and . . . . . . but my soul is older than it (i.e.,
[013-027] time). It is the Soul of Shu, it is the Soul of Khnemu (?),[FN#65] it
[013-028] is the Soul of Heh, it is the Soul of Kek and Kerh (i.e., Night and
[013-029] Darkness), it is the Soul of Nu and of Ra, it is the Soul of Osiris,
[013-030] the lord of Tettu, it is the Soul of the Sebak Crocodile-gods and of
[013-031] the Crocodiles, it is the Soul of every god [who dwelleth] in the
[013-032] divine Snakes, it is the Soul of Apep in Mount Bakhau (i.e., the Mount
[013-033] of Sunrise), and it is the Soul of Ra which pervadeth the whole world."

[FN#65] There are mistakes in the text here.

[013-034] Whosoever sayeth [these words] worketh his own protection by means of
[013-035] the words of power, "I am the god Hekau (i.e., the divine Word of
[013-036] power), and [I am] pure in my mouth, and [in] my belly; [I am] Ra from
[013-037] whom the gods proceeded. I am Ra, the Light-god (Khu)." When thou
[013-038] sayest [this], stop forth in the evening and in the morning on thine
[013-039] own behalf if thou wouldst make to fall the enemies of Ra. I am his
[013-040] Soul, and I am Heka.

[013-041] Hail, thou lord of eternity, thou creator of everlastingness, who
[013-042] bringest to nought the gods who came forth from Ra, thou lord of thy
[013-043] god, thou prince who didst make what made thee, who art beloved by the
[013-044] fathers of the gods, on whose head are the pure words of power, who
[013-045] didst create the woman (erpit) that standeth on the south side of thee,
[013-046] who didst create the goddess who hath her face on her breast, and the
[013-047] serpent which standeth on his tail, with her eye on his belly, and with
[013-048] his tail on the earth, to whom Thoth giveth praises, and upon whom the
[013-049] heavens rest, and to whom Shu stretcheth out his two hands, deliver
[013-050] thou me from those two great gods who sit in the east of the sky, who
[013-051] act as wardens of heaven and as wardens of earth, and who make firm the
[013-052] secret places, and who are called "Aaiu-su," and "Per-f-er-maa-Nu."
[013-053] Moreover [there shall be) a purifying on the . . . . . day of the month
[013-054] . . . . . . .. even according to the performance of the ceremonies in
[013-055] the oldest time.

[013-056] Whosoever shall recite this Chapter shall have life in Neter-kher
[013-057] (i.e., Underworld), and the fear of him shall be much greater than it
[013-058] was formerly [upon earth] . . . . . . . and they shall say, "Thy names
[013-059] are 'Eternity' and 'Everlastingness.'" They are called, they are
[013-060] called, "Au-peh-nef-n-aa-em-ta-uat-apu," and "Rekh-kua-[tut]-en-neter-
[013-061] pui-. . . . . . en en-hra-f-Her-shefu." I am he who hath strengthened
[013-062] the boat with the company of the gods, and his Shenit, and his Gods, by
[013-063] means of words of power.
