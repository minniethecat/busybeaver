# Birth of Horus

## Overview

The Birth of Horus is a foundational event in Egyptian mythology, marking the arrival of the rightful heir to Osiris's throne. After conceiving Horus from the resurrected Osiris, Isis fled to the papyrus swamps (Ateh) of the Delta, where she secretly gave birth to and nursed her son. Hidden from his murderous uncle Set, Horus grew in strength until he could claim his inheritance and avenge his father.

## Narrative

### The Conception

Before the birth, Isis had restored Osiris to life through her magical spells. During this brief revival, she conceived their child:

> "She made to rise up the helpless members of him whose heart was at rest, she drew from him his essence, and she made therefrom an heir." [PN=010-002]

### Birth and Concealment in the Papyrus Swamps

Isis fled to the papyrus swamps of the Delta (called Ateh) to give birth in secret, far from the reach of Set:

> "She suckled the child in solitariness and none knew where his place was, and he grew in strength." [PN=010-002]

The text emphasizes the secrecy and isolation of Horus's infancy. Isis called out to those who dwelt in the papyrus swamps, and they came to her aid, bewailing her affliction and protecting her:

> "I cried then to those who dwelt in the papyrus swamps (or, Ateh), and they inclined to me straightway. And the people came forth to me from their houses, and they thronged about me at [the sound of] my voice." [PN=010-002]

### The Nursing and Growth of Horus

Isis nursed Horus by giving him her finger to suck. The child grew strong in secret:

> "His hand is mighty (or, victorious) within the house of Keb, and the Company of the Gods rejoice greatly at the coming of Horus, the son of Osiris, whose heart is firmly stablished, the triumphant one, the son of Isis, the flesh and bone of Osiris." [PN=010-002]

## Outcome and Significance

The Birth of Horus had several crucial consequences for Egyptian mythology and kingship:

1. **Legitimate Heir**: Horus was the flesh-and-blood son of Osiris, making him the rightful heir to the throne of Egypt. His birth ensured the continuation of divine kingship.

2. **Model of Kingship**: The hidden childhood and eventual triumph of Horus became the mythological model for Egyptian kingship — the pharaoh was identified with Horus as the living embodiment of divine rule.

3. **Mother-Son Bond**: The devotion of Isis to her infant son became one of the most beloved motifs in Egyptian religion, with countless images showing Isis nursing the child Horus (Harpocrates).

4. **Avenger of Osiris**: Horus's birth set in motion the eventual confrontation with Set and the restoration of rightful order (Maat).
