# Ra

**Ra** (also **Re**) is the supreme sun god of the Egyptian pantheon — the self-begotten and self-created deity who assumed sovereignty over men, women, and gods. As the primary creator in [[Heliopolis|Heliopolitan]] theology, he fathered the [[Ennead]] and ruled Egypt for nearly a thousand years before abdicating to the heavens.

## Identity and Role

Ra (also called **Re**) is the god of the sun, light, and creation. He is described as "the god who was self-begotten and self-created," the ONE god who ruled as king over Egypt in the primeval age (001-001). His primary manifestations include **Ra-Harmachis** (Ra-Horus of the Horizon), linking him with the sky god [[Horus]].

Ra embodies divine kingship — he is simultaneously the creator, the ruler, and the source of maat (cosmic order). His daily journey across the sky in the [[Sun Boat|Boat of Ra]] symbolizes the cycle of life, death, and rebirth.

## Family and Relationships

Ra emerged from [[Nu]] (the primeval waters) through self-creation. He is the father of [[Shu]] (air) and [[Tefnut]] (moisture), grandfather of [[Seb]] (earth) and [[Nut]] (sky), and great-grandfather of [[Osiris]], [[Isis]], [[Set]], and [[Nephthys]].

His Eye — personified as the goddess [[Hathor]] (and in her fierce aspect as **Sekhet**) — serves as his instrument of divine retribution.

## Major Myths and Narratives

### The Destruction of Mankind

When Ra grew old — "his bones had become like silver, and his members had turned into gold" (001-025) — mankind rebelled against him. After consulting the elder gods in [[Nu]], Ra sent his Eye in the form of [[Hathor]] to slay the rebels:

> "The Majesty of Ra spake unto the god Nu, saying: 'Behold, mankind have taken counsel against me. Tell me what thou wouldst do in this matter.'" *(001-030)*

When [[Hathor]] developed a taste for blood, Ra tricked her by flooding the fields with beer dyed red, making her drunk and preventing the total annihilation of humanity (001-050).

### Abdication and Ascent to Heaven

Weary of ruling, Ra retired from earth on the back of the goddess [[Nut]], who transformed into a great cow. Before departing, he appointed [[Thoth]] as his viceroy and decreed that [[Seb]] should guard against the serpents of the earth (003-001). He then ascended to the heavens, leaving Egypt's rule to a succession of gods.

## Cult and Worship

Ra's primary cult center was at [[Heliopolis]] (Annu), where he was worshipped as Ra-Atum. The solar theology of Heliopolis became one of the most influential religious systems in ancient Egypt. Ra was later syncretized with other major gods including Amen (as Amen-Ra) and [[Horus]] (as Ra-Harmachis).

The [[Book of the Dead]] contains numerous hymns to Ra, and his daily journey in the solar barque became the paradigm for the soul's journey through the afterlife.
