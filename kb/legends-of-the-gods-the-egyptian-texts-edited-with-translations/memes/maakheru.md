# Maakheru

## Overview

**Maakheru** (Egyptian: *mꜣꜥ-ḫrw*, "true of voice") was the declaration of justification pronounced over the deceased who passed the judgment in the Hall of [[osiris|Osiris]]. More than a verdict, *maakheru* was a performative formula of immense magical potency — the "great command of maa-kheru" was the WORD whose commands were "performed, instantly and completely, by every god, spirit, fiend, human being and by every thing, animate and inanimate." To be *maakheru* was to possess divine authority, to have one's voice carry cosmic weight.

## Core Doctrine

### The Judgment

In the Hall of the Two Truths (*Maaty*), the heart of the deceased was weighed against the feather of [[maat]]. Those whose hearts balanced true were declared *maakheru* by the [[divine-company|Divine Company]] and the Lords of Truth. The declaration transformed the deceased from a vulnerable spirit into an *akh* — a transfigured, effective being with powers in the realm of the gods.

### Magical Power

The Egyptian texts emphasize that *maakheru* was not merely a status but a source of operational magic:

> "...bearing with him the 'great command of maa-kheru,' i.e., the WORD, whose commands were performed, instantly and completely, by every god, spirit, fiend, human being and by every thing, animate and inanimate..." [005-196]

The justified dead, speaking with the *maakheru* formula, could command the beings of the [[tuat|Tuat]], navigate the gates of the underworld, and participate in the boat of [[ra]].

### The Formula in Use

The *maakheru* formula was appended to the names of the deceased in funerary inscriptions. When a text refers to "Osiris, *maakheru*" or "the Osiris N, *maakheru*," it declares the deceased's identification with the resurrected god and their justified status. The formula also appears in execration texts and magical spells, where "the great word of command of maa-kheru" compels obedience from hostile entities:

> "I am a man who is provided with the great word of command of maa-kheru, [and said:] 'O ye...'" [022-159]

## Significance

*Maakheru* represents the confluence of Egyptian ethics, magic, and funerary belief. The concept unites moral judgment (the heart-weighing), ritual efficacy (the performative formula), and divine authority (command over all beings). It is the cornerstone of Egyptian soteriology — the mechanism by which a mortal becomes a god-like being in the afterlife. Without *maakheru*, the elaborate tombs, offerings, and spells of the funerary cult were powerless.
