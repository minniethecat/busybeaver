# Heh

## Overview

Heh (Hehu, Huh) is the god of infinity and eternity — one of the eight primordial deities of the Ogdoad of Hermopolis. His name signifies "millions" and "boundlessness," and he personifies the infinite space and endless duration that existed before creation. In the Egyptian legends he appears among the company of elder gods invoked at the dawn of the world.

## Identity and Role

Heh embodies the concept of *ḥeḥ* — millions, boundlessness, eternity. As a member of the Ogdoad he is paired with his female counterpart Hehut, and together they represent the infinite expanse of the primeval waters from which the creator-god emerged. He is often depicted as a kneeling figure holding palm ribs — the hieroglyphic sign for "year" and "millions" — symbolising his dominion over unending time.

His place among the elder gods is affirmed in the creation records where he appears alongside Shu and Khnemu:

> the word of power of the god Shu, and that of Shu, Khnemu (?), Heh, etc., and then he must proclaim... [001-191]

The Ogdoad genealogy lists him among the eight primordial powers:

> These gods were: Nu and Nut; Hehu and Hehut; Kekui and Kekuit... [016-018]

## Soul and Essence

In the mystical texts Heh is identified as one of the cosmic souls — an aspect of the primeval darkness from which light emerged:

> it is the Soul of Heh, it is the Soul of Kek and Kekuit [013-028]

The "Soul of Heh" represents the animating principle of boundlessness — the living spirit that pervades the infinite darkness before creation and sustains the endless cycles of time thereafter.

## Major Myths and Narratives

**The Naming of Heh.** In the legends Ra himself bestows the name of Heh upon a sacred site, linking the god's quality of boundlessness to the created world:

> she shall be called by the name of 'Heh' [015-299]

This naming act establishes the principle of infinity as a permanent presence in the ordered cosmos — the boundless made manifest in the bounded.

**The Supporters of Heaven.** The *heh*-beings, derived from Heh's essence, appear as the divine supporters who hold up the vault of heaven. In the Cow of Heaven narrative, these beings flank the celestial cow:

> The supporters called Heh-enti shall be by her shoulder [010-002]

> The supporters called Heh-enti shall be at her side [010-003]

These Heh-enti beings are emanations of Heh himself — personified infinity bearing the weight of the sky.

**The Lake of Heh.** A ritual lake associated with the god is attested in priestly contexts:

> "Heh" is the name of the lake [015-306]

The lake, like the god, represents boundlessness in microcosm — the waters that have no visible end.

## Cult and Worship

Heh had no major independent cult centre, as he belonged to the Ogdoad — a theological system rather than a popular cult. His worship was centred at Hermopolis (Khemenu), the "City of the Eight," where the Ogdoad was venerated as the primeval company that preceded and enabled creation. Heh's symbols — the palm rib and the tadpole (for the Ogdoad's aquatic nature) — appear in temple reliefs and funerary papyri as emblems of eternal renewal.
