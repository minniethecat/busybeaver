# Sekhet

## Overview

Sekhet (Sekhmet) is the lion-headed goddess of war, pestilence, and healing, worshipped principally at Memphis as the consort of [[ptah]]. She is the fierce eye of [[ra]] — the burning power of the sun sent forth to destroy the god's enemies — and appears in the Egyptian legends as a divine agent of retribution alongside Ra, Thoth, and Heka.

## Identity and Role

Sekhet embodies the destructive heat of the sun. Her name means "the Powerful One," and she is often depicted as a lioness or a woman with a lioness's head, symbolising her untamed ferocity. In the Memphite triad she stands beside Ptah as consort, with Nefer-Tem as their son.

Her identity in the legends is recorded among the great gods who act on Ra's behalf:

> Ra, Sekhet, Thoth, and Heka — this last-named being the god of magic — accompanied the great god on his journey [005-123]

She dwells in Annu (Heliopolis), the city of the sun, where she receives offerings and executes divine commands:

> the Majesty of this god gave them to Sekhet, the goddess who dwelleth in Annu [009-056]

## Family and Relationships

Sekhet is the consort of [[ptah]], the creator-god of Memphis, and the mother of Nefer-Tem, the lotus-god of perfume and healing. Through her association with Ra she is also counted among the daughters of the sun-god — the "Eye of Ra" who executes his judgments.

Her relationship to other deities appears primarily through shared action: she stands in the company of [[ra]], [[thoth]], and [[heka]] in the journey narratives, and she is invoked alongside Ra and Thoth in protective spells:

> Ra shutteth up your mouths, Sekhet stoppeth up your throats, Thoth cutteth... [020-027]

## Major Myths and Narratives

**The Goddess in Annu.** Sekhet dwells in the temple of Annu as the divine protectress who receives offerings on behalf of the gods:

> Sekhet of the offerings waded about in the night [009-048]

As a goddess who "wades" through the darkness, she patrols the boundaries between order and chaos, destroying threats before they reach the solar barque.

**The Company of Ra.** Sekhet travels in the entourage of Ra — the select company of deities who accompany the sun-god through his daily and nightly journeys. She holds a place equal to Thoth and Heka in this company:

> Ra, Sekhet, Thoth, and Heka [005-123]

**Guardian of the Throat.** In the protective formulae she is the power that seals mouths and throats against blasphemy and hostile speech, acting in concert with Ra and Thoth:

> Ra shutteth up your mouths, Sekhet stoppeth up your throats [020-027]

## Cult and Worship

The principal cult centre of Sekhet was Memphis, where she was worshipped as part of the Memphite triad with Ptah and Nefer-Tem. Her secondary sanctuary was in Annu (Heliopolis), the city of Ra, reflecting her dual role as both Memphite consort and Heliopolitan daughter of the sun. Festivals of Sekhet involved the appeasement of her destructive aspect through offerings and music, and her priestesses were known for their knowledge of healing arts — for the goddess who sends plague also holds the power to cure it.
