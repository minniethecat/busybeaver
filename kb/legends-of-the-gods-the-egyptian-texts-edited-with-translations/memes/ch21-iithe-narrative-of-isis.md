[021-001] I am Isis, [and] I have come forth from the dwelling (or, prison)
[021-002] wherein my brother Set placed me. Behold the god Thoth, the great god,
[021-003] the Chief of Maat[FN#203] [both] in heaven and on the earth, said unto
[021-004] me, "Come now, O Isis, thou goddess, moreover it is a good thing to
[021-005] hearken,[FN#204] [for there is] life to one who shall be guided [by the
[021-006] advice] of another. Hide thou thyself with [thy] son the child, and
[021-007] there shall come unto him these things. His members shall
[021-008] grow,[FN#205] and two-fold strength of every kind shall spring up [in
[021-009] him]. [And he] shall be made to take his seat upon the throne of his
[021-010] father, [whom] he shall avenge,[FN#206] [and he shall take possession
[021-011] of] the exalted position of Heq[FN#207] of the Two Lands."[FN#208]

[FN#203] i.e., Law, or Truth.

[FN#204] Or, obey.

[FN#205] i.e., flourish.

[FN#206] He avenged his father Osiris by vanquishing Set.

[FN#207] i.e., tribal chief.

[FN#208] i.e., Upper and Lower Egypt.

[021-012] I came forth [from the dwelling] at the time of evening, and there came
[021-013] forth the Seven Scorpions which were to accompany me and to strike(?)
[021-014] for me with [their] stings. Two scorpions, Tefen and Befen, were
[021-015] behind me, two scorpions, Mestet and Mestetef, were by my side, and
[021-016] three scorpions, Petet, Thetet, and Maatet (or, Martet), were for
[021-017] preparing the road for me. I charged them very strictly (or, in a loud
[021-018] voice), and my words penetrated into their ears: "Have no knowledge of
[021-019] [any], make no cry to the Tesheru beings, and pay no attention to the
[021-020] 'son of a man' (i.e., anyone) who belongeth to a man of no account,"
[021-021] [and I said,] "Let your faces be turned towards the ground [that ye may
[021-022] show me] the way." So the guardian of the company brought me to the
[021-023] boundaries of the city of Pa-Sui,[FN#209] the city of the goddesses of
[021-024] the Divine Sandals, [which was situated] in front of the Papyrus
[021-025] Swamps.[FN#210]

[FN#209] "The House of the Crocodile," perhaps the same town as Pa-  
[021-026] Sebekt, a district in the VIIth nome of Lower Egypt (Metelites).

[FN#210] Perhaps a district in the Metelite nome.

[021-027] When I had arrived at the place where the people lived[FN#211] I came
[021-028] to the houses wherein dwelt the wives [and] husbands. And a certain
[021-029] woman of quality spied me as I was journeying along the road, and she
[021-030] shut her doors on me. Now she was sick at heart by reason of those
[021-031] [scorpions] which were with me. Then [the Seven Scorpions] took
[021-032] counsel concerning her, and they all at one time shot out their venom
[021-033] on the tail of the scorpion Tefen; as for me, the woman Taha[FN#212]
[021-034] opened her door, and I entered into the house of the miserable lady.

[FN#211] In Egyptian Teb, which may be the Tebut in the Metelite nome.

[FN#212] Taha may be the name of a woman, or goddess, or the word may
[021-035] mean a "dweller in the swamps," as Golenischeff thinks.

[021-036] Then the scorpion Tefen entered in under the leaves of the door and
[021-037] smote (i.e., stung) the son of Usert, and a fire broke out in the house
[021-038] of Usert, and there was no water there to extinguish it; [but] the sky
[021-039] rained upon the house of Usert, though it was not the season for
[021-040] rain.[FN#213]

[FN#213] i.e., it was not the season of the inundation.

[021-041] Behold, the heart of her who had not opened her door to me was
[021-042] grievously sad, for she knew not whether he (i.e., her son) would live
[021-043] [or not], and although she went round about through her town uttering
[021-044] cries [for help], there was none who came at [the sound of] her voice.
[021-045] Now mine own heart was grievously sad for the sake of the child, and [I
[021-046] wished] to make to live [again] him that was free from fault.
[021-047] [Thereupon] I cried out to the noble lady, "Come to me. Come to me.
[021-048] Verily my mouth (?) possesseth life. I am a daughter [well] known in
[021-049] her town, [and I] can destroy the demon of death by the spell (or,
[021-050] utterance) which my father taught me to know. I am his daughter, the
[021-051] beloved [offspring] of his body."

[021-052] Then Isis placed her two hands on the child in order to make to live him
[021-053] whose throat was stopped, [and she said], "O poison of the scorpion
[021-054] Tefent, come forth and appear on the ground! Thou shalt neither enter
[021-055] nor penetrate [further into the body of the child]. O poison of the
[021-056] scorpion Befent, come forth and appear on the ground! I am Isis, the
[021-057] goddess, the lady (or, mistress) of words of power, and I am the maker
[021-058] of words of power (i.e., spells), and I know how to utter words with
[021-059] magical effect.[FN#214] Hearken ye unto me, O every reptile which
[021-060] possesseth the power to bite (i.e., to sting), and fall headlong to the
[021-061] ground! O poison of the scorpion Mestet, make no advance [into his
[021-062] body]. O poison of the scorpion Mestetef, rise not up [in his body]. O
[021-063] poison of the scorpions Petet and Thetet, penetrate not [into his body].
[021-064] [O poison of] the scorpion Maatet (or, Martet), fall down on the
[021-065] ground."

[FN#214] By uttering spells Isis restored life to her husband Osiris
[021-066] for a season, and so became with child by him. She made a magical
[021-067] figure of a reptile, and having endowed it with life, it stung Ra as he
[021-068] passed through the sky, and the great god almost died. In Greek times
[021-069] it was believed that she discovered a medicine which would raise the
[021-070] dead, and she was reputed to be a great expert in the art of healing
[021-071] men's sicknesses. As a goddess she appeared to the sick, and cured
[021-072] them.

[021-073] [Here follows the] "Chapter of the stinging [of scorpions]."

[021-074] And Isis, the goddess, the great mistress of spells (or, words of
[021-075] power), she who is at the head of the gods, unto whom the god Keb gave
[021-076] his own magical spells for the driving away of poison at noon-day (?),
[021-077] and for making poison to go back, and retreat, and withdraw, and go
[021-078] backward, spake, saying, "Ascend not into heaven, through the command
[021-079] of the beloved one of Ra, the egg of the Smen goose which cometh forth
[021-080] from the sycamore. Verily my words are made to command the uttermost
[021-081] limit of the night. I speak unto you, [O scorpions] I am alone and in
[021-082] sorrow because our names will suffer disgrace throughout the nomes. Do
[021-083] not make love, do not cry out to the Tesheru fiends, and cast no
[021-084] glances upon the noble ladies in their houses. Turn your faces towards
[021-085] the earth and [find out] the road, so that we may arrive at the hidden
[021-086] places in the town of Khebt.[FN#215] Oh the child shall live and the
[021-087] poison die! Ra liveth and the poison dieth! Verily Horus shall be in
[021-088] good case (or, healthy) for his mother Isis. Verily he who is stricken
[021-089] shall be in good case likewise."

[FN#215] The island of Chemmis of classical writers.

[021-090] And the fire [which was in the house of Usert] was extinguished, and
[021-091] heaven was satisfied with the utterance of Isis, the goddess.

[021-092] Then the lady Usert came, and she brought unto me her possessions, and
[021-093] she filled the house of the woman Tah (?), for the Ka of Tah
[021-094] (?) because [she] had opened to me her door. Now the lady Usert
[021-095] suffered pain and anguish the whole night, and her mouth tasted (i.e.,
[021-096] felt) the sting [which] her son [had suffered]. And she brought her
[021-097] possessions as the penalty for not having opened the door to me. Oh
[021-098] the child shall live and the poison die! Verily Horus shall be in good
[021-099] case for his mother Isis. Verily everyone who is stricken shall be in
[021-100] good case likewise.

[021-101] Lo, a bread-cake [made] of barley meal shall drive out (or, destroy)
[021-102] the poison, and natron shall make it to withdraw, and the fire [made]
[021-103] of hetchet-plant shall drive out (or, destroy) fever-heat from the
[021-104] limbs.

[021-105] "O Isis, O Isis, come thou to thy Horus, O thou woman of the wise
[021-106] mouth! Come to thy son"—thus cried the gods who dwelt in her quarter
[021-107] of the town—"for he is as one whom a scorpion hath stung, and like
[021-108] one whom the scorpion Uhat, which the animal Antesh drove away, hath
[021-109] wounded."

[021-110] [Then] Isis ran out like one who had a knife [stuck] in her body, and
[021-111] she opened her arms wide, [saying] "Behold me, behold me, my son Horus,
[021-112] have no fear, have no fear, O son my glory! No evil thing of any kind
[021-113] whatsoever shall happen unto thee, [for] there is in thee the essence
[021-114] (or, fluid) which made the things which exist. Thou art the son from
[021-115] the country of Mesqet,[FN#216] [thou hast] come forth from the
[021-116] celestial waters Nu, and thou shalt not die by the heat of the poison.
[021-117] Thou wast the Great Bennu,[FN#217] who art born (or, produced) or; the
[021-118] top of the balsam-trees[FN#218] which are in the House of the Aged One
[021-119] in Anu (Heliopolis). Thou art the brother of the Abtu Fish,[FN#219]
[021-120] who orderest what is to be, and art the nursling of the Cat[FN#220] who
[021-121] dwelleth in the House of Neith. The goddess Reret,[FN#221] the goddess
[021-122] Hat, and the god Bes protect thy members. Thy head shall not fall to
[021-123] the Tchat fiend that attacketh thee. Thy members shall not receive the
[021-124] fire of that which is thy poison. Thou shalt not go backwards on the
[021-125] land, and thou shalt not be brought low on the water. No reptile which
[021-126] biteth (or, stingeth) shall gain the mastery over thee, and no lion
[021-127] shall subdue thee or have dominion over thee. Thou art the son of the
[021-128] sublime god 82 who proceeded from Keb. Thou art Horus, and the poison
[021-129] shall not gain the mastery over thy members. Thou art the son of the
[021-130] sublime god who proceeded from Keb, and thus likewise shall it be with
[021-131] those who are under the knife. And the four august goddesses shall
[021-132] protect thy members."

[FN#216] Mesqet was originally the name of the bull's skin in which
[021-133] the deceased was wrapped in order to secure for him the now life; later
[021-134] the name was applied to the Other World generally. {See Book of the
[021-135] Dead, Chap. xvii. 121.}

[FN#217] The Bennu who kept the book of destiny. See Book of the Dead,  
[021-136] Chap. xvii. 25.

[FN#218] These are the balsam-trees for which Heliopolis has been
[021-137] always famous. They are described by Wansleben, L'Histoire de
[021-138] l'Eglise, pp. 88-93, and by 'Abd al-Latif (ed. de Sacy), p. 88.

[FN#219] The Abtu and Ant Fishes swam before the Boat of Ra and guided
[021-139] it.

[FN#220] This is the Cat who lived by the Persea tree in Heliopolis.  
[021-140] See Book of the Dead, Chap. xvii. 18.

[FN#221] A hippopotamus goddess.
