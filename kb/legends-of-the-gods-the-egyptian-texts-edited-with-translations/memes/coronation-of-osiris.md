# Coronation of Osiris

## Overview

The Coronation of Osiris marks the establishment of divine kingship in Egypt. Osiris, the son of Keb (the earth god) and Nut (the sky goddess), was crowned king at Suten-henen (Herakleopolis). He inherited the earth from his father and the sovereignty of heaven from his mother, uniting in his person the dual heritage of terrestrial and celestial rule. His reign inaugurated a golden age of order, law, and prosperity, setting the mythological precedent for all subsequent Egyptian kingship.

## Narrative

### The Inheritance

Osiris received his kingship through inheritance from his divine parents. As the text declares, he possessed authority over both the terrestrial and celestial realms:

> "He inherited the earth from his father Keb, and the sovereignty of heaven from his mother Nut." [PN=009-028]

In his person, Osiris united endless time in the past and endless time in the future. The doors of the sky opened before him of their own accord when he appeared, and the imperishable stars were his ministers. [PN=009-027]

### The Coronation at Suten-henen

The coronation took place at Suten-henen (Herakleopolis), a city already established as a royal seat. Ra himself had risen as king for the first time in this same city:

> "Osiris also was crowned at Suten-henen, and in this city lived the great Bennu bird, or Phoenix, and the 'Crusher of Bones' mentioned in the Negative Confession." [PN=001-486]

This association with both Ra and the Bennu bird (symbol of rebirth and cyclical renewal) linked Osiris's coronation with the most ancient traditions of Egyptian kingship.

### The Golden Age of Osiris

Osiris ruled as a beneficent king who brought civilization to Egypt. He exercised his creative power in making land and water, trees and herbs, cattle and other four-footed beasts, birds, fish, and creeping things. Even the waste spaces of the desert owed allegiance to him as the creator. [PN=009-030] He rolled out the sky and set the light above the darkness.

Like Ra, he had fought Seba (Set), the monster of evil, and had defeated him, and his victory assured to him lasting authority over the gods and the dead. [PN=009-029]

## Outcome and Significance

The Coronation of Osiris established several foundational principles of Egyptian kingship:

1. **Divine Right of Kings**: The pharaoh's authority derived from Osiris, the first divine king. Every subsequent Egyptian ruler was identified with Horus, the son and rightful heir of Osiris.

2. **Dual Kingship**: Osiris's inheritance of the earth from Keb and the sky from Nut provided the mythological model for the pharaoh's rule over the Two Lands (Upper and Lower Egypt).

3. **Maat (Cosmic Order)** : As king, Osiris established law and order. The concept of Maat — truth, justice, and cosmic balance — was central to his reign.

4. **Precedent for Succession**: The coronation established the principle of legitimate inheritance from father to son, which later became crucial in the dispute between Horus and Set.
