### CHAPTER I.

[009-001] [Here is the story of Ra,] the god who was self-begotten and self-
[009-002] created, after he had assumed the sovereignty over men and women, and
[009-003] gods, and things, the ONE god. Now men and women were speaking words
[009-004] of complaint, saying:—"Behold, his Majesty (Life, Strength, and Health
[009-005] to him!) hath grown old, and his bones have become like silver, and
[009-006] his members have turned into gold and his hair is like unto real lapis-
[009-007] lazuli." His Majesty heard the words of complaint which men and women
[009-008] were uttering, and his Majesty (Life, Strength, and Health to him!)
[009-009] said unto those who were in his train:—"Cry out, and bring to me my
[009-010] Eye, and Shu, and Tefnut, and Seb, and Nut, and the father-gods, and
[009-011] the mother-gods who were with me, even when I was in Nu side by side
[009-012] with my god Nu. Let there be brought along with my Eye his ministers,
[009-013] and let them be led to me hither secretly, so that men and women may
[009-014] not perceive them [coming] hither, and may not therefore take to flight
[009-015] with their hearts. Come thou[FN#58] with them to the Great House, and
[009-016] let them declare their plans (or, arrangements) fully, for I will go
[009-017] from Nu into the place wherein I brought about my own existence, and
[009-018] let those gods be brought unto me there." Now the gods were drawn up
[009-019] on each side of Ra, and they bowed down before his Majesty until their
[009-020] heads touched the ground, and the maker of men and women, the king of
[009-021] those who have knowledge, spake his words in the presence of the Father
[009-022] of the first-born gods. And the gods spake in the presence of his
[009-023] Majesty, saying:—"Speak unto us, for we are listening to them" (i.e.,
[009-024] thy words). Then Ra spake unto Nu, saying:—"O thou first-born god
[009-025] from whom I came into being, O ye gods of ancient time, my ancestors,
[009-026] take ye heed to what men and women [are doing]; for behold, those who
[009-027] were created by my Eye are uttering words of complaint against me.
[009-028] Tell me what ye would do in the matter, and consider this thing for me,
[009-029] and seek out [a plan] for me, for I will not slay them until I have
[009-030] heard what ye shall say to me concerning it."

[FN#58] The god here addressed appears to have been Nu.

[009-031] Then the Majesty of Nu, to son Ra, spake, saying:—"Thou art the god
[009-032] who art greater than he who made thee, thou art the sovereign of those
[009-033] who were created with thee, thy throne is set, and the fear of thee is
[009-034] great; let thine Eye go against those who have uttered blasphemies
[009-035] against thee." And the Majesty of Ra, said:—"Behold, they have
[009-036] betaken themselves to flight into the mountain lands, for their hearts
[009-037] are afraid because of the words which they have uttered." Then the
[009-038] gods spake in the presence of his Majesty, saying:—"Let thine Eye go
[009-039] forth and let it destroy for thee those who revile thee with words of
[009-040] evil, for there is no eye whatsoever that can go before it and resist
[009-041] thee and it when it journeyeth in the form of Hathor." Thereupon this
[009-042] goddess went forth and slew the men and the women who were on the
[009-043] mountain (or, desert land). And the Majesty of this god said, "Come,
[009-044] come in peace, O Hathor, for the work is accomplished." Then this
[009-045] goddess said, "Thou hast made me to live, for when I gained the mastery
[009-046] over men and women it was sweet to my heart;" and the Majesty of Ra
[009-047] said, "I myself will be master over them as [their] king, and I will
[009-048] destroy them." And it came to pass that Sekhet of the offerings waded
[009-049] about in the night season in their blood, beginning at Suten-
[009-050] henen.[FN#59] Then the Majesty of Ra, spake [saying], "Cry out, and
[009-051] let there come to me swift and speedy messengers who shall be able to
[009-052] run like the wind . . . .;" and straightway messengers of this kind
[009-053] were brought unto him. And the Majesty of this god spake [saying],
[009-054] "Let these messengers go to Abu,[FN#60] and bring unto me mandrakes in
[009-055] great numbers;" and [when] these mandrakes were brought unto him the
[009-056] Majesty of this god gave them to Sekhet, the goddess who dwelleth in
[009-057] Annu (Heliopolis) to crush. And behold, when the maidservants were
[009-058] bruising the grain for [making] beer, these mandrakes were placed in
[009-059] the vessels which were to hold the beer, and some of the blood of the
[009-060] men and women [who had been slain]. Now they made seven thousand
[009-061] vessels of beer. Now when the Majesty of Re, the King of the South and
[009-062] North, had come with the gods to look at the vessels of beer, and
[009-063] behold, the daylight had appeared after the slaughter of men and women
[009-064] by the goddess in their season as she sailed up the river, the Majesty
[009-065] of Ra said, "It is good, it is good, nevertheless I must protect men
[009-066] and women against her." And Ra, said, "Let them take up the vases and
[009-067] carry them to the place where the men and women were slaughtered by
[009-068] her." Then the Majesty of the King of the South and North in the
[009-069] three-fold beauty of the night caused to be poured out these vases of
[009-070] beer which make [men] to lie down (or, sleep), and the meadows of the
[009-071] Four Heavens[FN#61] were filled with beer (or, water) by reason of the
[009-072] Souls of the Majesty of this god. And it came to pass that when this
[009-073] goddess arrived at the dawn of day, she found these [Heavens] flooded
[009-074] [with beer], and she was pleased thereat; and she drank [of the beer
[009-075] and blood], and her heart rejoiced, and she became drunk, and she gave
[009-076] no further attention to men and women. Then said the Majesty of Ra to
[009-077] this goddess, "Come in peace, come in peace, O Amit,"[FN#62] and
[009-078] thereupon beautiful women came into being in the city of Amit (or,
[009-079] Amem). And the Majesty of Ra spake [concerning] this goddess,
[009-080] [saying], "Let there be made for her vessels of the beer which
[009-081] produceth sleep at every holy time and season of the year, and they
[009-082] shall be in number according to the number of my hand-maidens;" and
[009-083] from that early time until now men have been wont to make on the
[009-084] occasions of the festival of Hathor vessels of the beer which make them
[009-085] to sleep in number according to the number of the handmaidens of Ra.
[009-086] And the Majesty of Ra spake unto this goddess, [saying], "I am smitten
[009-087] with the pain of the fire of sickness; whence cometh to me [this]
[009-088] pain?" And the Majesty of Ra said, "I live, but my heart hath become
[009-089] exceedingly weary[FN#63] with existence with them (i.e., with men); I
[009-090] have slain [some of] them, but there is a remnant of worthless ones,
[009-091] for the destruction which I wrought among them was not as great as my
[009-092] power." Then the gods who were in his following said unto him, "Be not
[009-093] overcome by thy inactivity, for thy might is in proportion to thy
[009-094] will." And the Majesty of this god said unto the Majesty of Nu, "My
[009-095] members are weak for (or, as at) the first time; I will not permit this
[009-096] to come upon me a second time." And the Majesty of the god Nu said, "O
[009-097] son Shu, be thou the Eye 'for thy father . . . . . and avenue (?) him,
[009-098] and 'thou goddess Nut, place him . . . . . … And the goddess Nut
[009-099] said, "How can this be then, O my father Nu? Hail," said Nut . . . . .
[009-100] to the god Nu, and the goddess straightway became [a cow], and she set
[009-101] the Majesty of Ra upon [her] back . . . . . And when these things had
[009-102] been done, men and women saw the god Ra, upon the back [of the cow].
[009-103] Then these men and women said, "Remain with us, and we will overthrow
[009-104] thine enemies who speak words of blasphemy [against thee.], and
[009-105] [destroy them]." Then his Majesty [Ra] set out for the Great House,
[009-106] and [the gods who were in the train of Ra remained] with them (i.e.,
[009-107] the men); during that time the earth was in darkness. And when the
[009-108] earth became light [again] and the morning had dawned, the men came
[009-109] forth with their bows and their [weapons], and they set their arms in
[009-110] motion to shoot the enemies [of Ra]. Then said the Majesty of this
[009-111] god, "Your "transgressions of violence are placed behind you, for the
[009-112] slaughtering of the enemies is above the slaughter [of sacrifice];"
[009-113] thus came into being the slaughter [of sacrifice]. And the Majesty of
[009-114] this god said unto Nut, "I have placed myself upon my back in order to
[009-115] stretch myself out." What then is the meaning of this? It meaneth
[009-116] that he united (?) himself with Nut. [Thus came into being] . . . . .
[009-117] Then said the Majesty of this god, "I am departing from them (i.e.,
[009-118] from men), and he must come after me who would see me;" thus came into
[009-119] being . . . . . Then the Majesty of this god looked forth from its
[009-120] interior, saying, "Gather together [men for me], and make ready for me
[009-121] an abode for multitudes;" thus came into being . . . . . . . And his
[009-122] Majesty (life, health, and strength be to him!) said, "Let a great
[009-123] field (sekhet) be produced (hetep);" thereupon Sekhet-hetep came into
[009-124] being. [And the god said], "I will gather herbs (aarat) therein;"
[009-125] thereupon Sekhet-aaru came into being. [And the god said], "I will
[009-126] make it to contain as dwellers things (khet) like stars of all sorts;"
[009-127] thereupon the stars (akhekha) came into being. Then the goddess Nut
[009-128] trembled because of the height.

[FN#59] Or, Henen-su, {hbw XaNeS}, i.e., Herakleopolis, Magna.

[FN#60] i.e., Elephantine, or Syene, a place better known by the  
[009-129] Arabic name ASWAN.

[FN#61] i.e., the South, North, West, and East of the sky.

[FN#62] i.e., "the fair and gracious goddess."

[FN#63] Literally, "My heart hath stopped greatly."

[009-130] And the Majesty of Ra said, "I decree that supports be to bear [the
[009-131] goddess up];" thereupon the props of heaven (heh) came into being. And
[009-132] the Majesty of Ra said, "O my son Shu, I pray thee to set thyself under
[009-133] [my] daughter Nut, and guard thou for me the supports (heh) of the
[009-134] millions (heh) which are there, and which live in darkness. Take thou
[009-135] the goddess upon thy head, and act thou as nurse for her;" thereupon
[009-136] came into being [the custom] of a son nursing a daughter, and [the
[009-137] custom] of a father carrying a son upon his head.
