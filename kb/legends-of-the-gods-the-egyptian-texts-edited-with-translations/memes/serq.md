# Serq

## Overview

Serq (Serqet, Selkis) is the scorpion-goddess of protection, healing, and magic. She is one of the four protective goddesses who guard the canopic shrine of [[osiris]] (alongside [[isis]], [[nephthys]], and [[neith]]). Her venom could both kill and cure — she was invoked in healing spells against scorpion stings and snake bites, her own power turned against the poisons that afflicted the living and the dead.

## Identity and Role

Serq embodies the dual nature of scorpion venom: as a deadly weapon against enemies, and as a healing force when ritually controlled. She protects the throne of Osiris, the canopic jars, and the solar barque. She is identified in magical spells that drive away venomous creatures:

> the spell of the Scorpion-goddess Serq driveth the poison back [018-009]

In the myth of Isis and the scorpions, it is Serq who advises [[isis]] to pray to [[ra]] for aid when the scorpion's sting afflicts the child of the woman who refused her shelter:

> the goddess Serqet advised Isis to pray to Ra for the child's life [005-190]

Her own protection is invoked in healing:

> these things would be effected by Serqet [005-098]

## Family and Relationships

Serq has no extensive mythological family. Her mythological relationships are primarily functional — she is one of the four tutelary goddesses of the dead, paired with Isis, Nephthys, and Neith. In some traditions, she is the mother of [[nehebkau]], the serpent-god who guards the entrance to the underworld.

She appears alongside Isis in the marshes:

> Serq came also and hid herself in the papyrus swamps [022-150]

## Major Myths and Narratives

**Isis and the Seven Scorpions.** When Isis fled from [[set]] with the infant [[horus]], seven scorpions — led by Serqet's scorpion-form Tefen — accompanied her as bodyguards. When a wealthy woman refused Isis shelter, the scorpions combined their venom into one sting to punish the woman. Serqet then advised Isis to pray to Ra, who sent [[thoth]] with healing spells.

**Guardian of the Canopic Shrine.** Serq is one of four goddesses who protect the canopic chest of Osiris. Her golden shrine-statue at the corners of the sarcophagus extends her protective arms over the deceased, ensuring the integrity of the body for resurrection.

**The Scorpion Spell.** In the magical corpus, Serq's name itself carries power:

> the spell of the Scorpion-goddess Serq driveth the poison back from all the limbs [018-009]

## Cult and Worship

Serq had no major independent temple, but her cult was widespread in funerary contexts. Amulets of scorpions and golden figurines of Serq were placed in tombs to protect the deceased. Her priests were also physicians (*sunu*), specializing in the treatment of venomous stings — applying the goddess's power through medical-magical practice.
