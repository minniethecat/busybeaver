# Heru-Behutet

## Overview

Heru-Behutet ("Horus of Edfu") is the winged sun-disk manifestation of [[heru|Heru (Horus)]], the divine protector who battled the forces of [[set|Set]] at the side of [[ra|Ra]] . His primary mythology is preserved in the Legend of the Winged Sun-Disk, inscribed on the temple walls at [[edfu|Edfu]], where he is the patron deity.

## Identity and Role

Heru-Behutet embodies the solar warrior aspect of Horus. He takes the form of a great winged disk—the sun-disk with outspread falcon wings—and serves as Ra's champion in battle [002-045]. His titles include "Great Protector" and "Exalted One, Son of Ra" [015-015]. He is both a celestial being (dwelling in the Boat of Ra) and a local god of Edfu, where his primary temple stands.

> "Thou art a great protector" — Ra to Heru-Behutet [015-044]

## Family and Relationships

As a form of [[heru|Heru (Horus)]], Heru-Behutet shares his divine lineage: son of [[osiris|Osiris]] and [[isis|Isis]]. He serves as the warrior champion of [[ra|Ra]], the sun-god, and stands in eternal opposition to [[set|Set]] and his confederates. In the Edfu texts, Ra addresses him as "son" in acknowledgment of their close bond [015-015].

## Major Myths and Narratives

The principal myth of Heru-Behutet is the **Legend of the Winged Sun-Disk** (Chapter 2). After Ra's enemies rebel in Nubia, Heru-Behutet enters the Boat of Ra and is commissioned to destroy them [002-042]. Taking the form of a great winged disk upon the prow of Ra's boat, he attacks the rebels with divine fury [002-045].

The battle narrative (Chapter 15) covers extensive campaigns across Egypt. Heru-Behutet pursues Set's allies through the waters and marshes, his boat bearing his name. After repeated victories, specific locations are named in commemoration of the battles [015-030]. The House of Heru-Behutet and his Boat each receive sacred names [015-045], [015-054].

> "And Heru-Behutet was in the boat of Ra, and he said unto his father Ra..." [015-008]

## Cult and Worship

Heru-Behutet was worshipped primarily at **Edfu** (ancient Djeba), where the great Temple of Horus stands as one of the best-preserved temples in Egypt. The temple's walls carry the full text of the Winged Sun-Disk legend. His palace-abode is called "Sweet Life" [015-041], and sacred place-names associated with his battles dot Upper Egypt, each marking a victory site [015-030].
