# Heru

## Overview

Heru (Horus) appears in multiple forms in Egyptian religion. This entry treats **Heru-ur** (Horus the Elder, *Haroeris*), the cosmic falcon god of the sky, distinct from [[horus]] the son of [[isis]]. Heru-ur is a primordial sky deity who defends [[ra]]'s solar barque and battles [[set]] in the form of the Winged Disk. He is often identified as **Heru-Behutet** (Horus of Edfu), the protector god of [[edfu]].

## Identity and Role

Heru-ur is the falcon god whose eyes are the sun and the moon. He is the son of [[nut]] and [[keb]], born alongside [[osiris]], [[isis]], [[set]], and [[nephthys]]:

> Nut brought forth Osiris, and Heru-khenti-an-maa, and Set, and Isis, and Nephthys [008-050]

As Heru-Behutet, he is the Winged Disk — a form he assumed to defeat the enemies of [[ra]]. When rebels threatened Ra, Harmakhis addressed Heru-Behutet and commanded him to attack:

> Harmakhis in answer addressed Heru-Behutet, saying: "You are the protector of Ra" [002-043]

> Then Heru-Behutet took the form of a great winged disk and flew up into the sky [002-045]

After his victory, Ra decreed that the Winged Disk must be placed above every temple entrance in Egypt as a protective emblem:

> because he had taken the form of a winged disk he must be called "Heru-Behutet" [002-053]

## Family and Relationships

Heru-ur is one of the five children of [[nut]] and [[keb]], alongside [[osiris]], [[isis]], [[set]], and [[nephthys]]. He is distinct from [[horus]] (Horus the Child, Harpocrates), who is the son of [[isis]] and [[osiris]].

Heru-Behutet operates as the warrior-protector of [[ra]], stationed at the prow of the solar barque or flying above it as the Winged Disk. Isis accompanies him:

> stand Isis and Heru Khenti-Khatti [002-029]

In the battles against [[set]], Heru-Behutet fights alongside or in parallel with [[horus]], the son of [[isis]]:

> fight between Heru-ur and Set, the fight between Ra and Set, the fight between Heru-Behutet and Set [002-148]

## Major Myths and Narratives

**The Winged Disk Victory.** When rebels rose against [[ra]], Heru-Behutet transformed into the great Winged Disk and flew up into the sky to attack them from above. After a series of battles, he defeated the enemies of Ra and was rewarded with eternal prominence:

> Heru-Behutet was in the boat of Ra, and he said unto his father Ra-Heru-Khuti [015-008]

> he arrived at Uthes-Heru, which lay to the west of the canal [015-006]

**Spearing the Crocodile.** Heru-Behutet and [[thoth]] spear Set's followers, who had transformed into crocodiles and hippopotami to escape:

> Horus of Behutet and Thoth spearing human victims with their spears [002-173]

> Horus of Behutet and Thoth spearing Set in the form of a crocodile [002-176]

**The Boat of Ra.** When [[ra]] entered his boat at Edfu, Heru-Behutet joined him:

> where Heru-Behutet entered his boat, and he sailed with Ra [002-042]

**Proclamation by Temu and Heru-Hekennu.** In the Underworld texts, the deceased is proclaimed by cosmic authorities:

> I am proclaimed by Temu and Heru-Hekennu [014-048]

The image of Heru-Hekennu (Horus of Acclamation) appears over the doors and images of the tomb:

> Temu and an image of Heru-Hekennu, and over an image of Ra [014-115]

## Cult and Worship

Heru-Behutet was worshipped primarily at [[edfu]] (Behutet), where the great Temple of Horus stands — the best-preserved temple in Egypt. The annual Festival of the Winged Disk re-enacted Heru-Behutet's victory over the enemies of [[ra]]. His cult also extended to Mesen (Kom Ombo area) and into Nubia.

The Winged Disk symbol — derived from Heru-Behutet's transformation — was placed above temple gateways, stele tops, and royal cartouches throughout Egyptian history. Every pharaoh adopted the Horus name as one of their five royal titles, identifying themselves as the living embodiment of Horus on earth.
