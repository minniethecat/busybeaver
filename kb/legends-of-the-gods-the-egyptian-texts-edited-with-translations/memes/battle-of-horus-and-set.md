# Battle of Horus and Set

The **Battle of Horus and Set** is the climactic conflict of the [[Osiris]] cycle. [[Horus]], son of [[Osiris]] and [[Isis]], confronts [[Set]] — the murderer of his father — in a cosmic struggle for the throne of Egypt. The battle spans the Nile valley, culminating at [[Edfu]], and establishes Horus as the divine prototype of legitimate kingship.

## Participants

- **Horus, son of Isis** — The rightful heir and avenger
- **Heru-Behutet** (Horus of Edfu) — Warrior form, armed with spear and chain
- **Set** — The murderer of [[Osiris]] and usurper of the throne
- **Isis** — Mother of Horus, magical protector
- **Ra-Harmachis** — The sun god who legitimizes Horus's victory

## Narrative

After [[Set]] murdered [[Osiris]], [[Isis]] gave birth to [[Horus]] in secret and raised him to claim his inheritance. When Horus came of age, he confronted [[Set]] before the divine tribunal.

In the form of **Heru-Behutet**, Horus took to the sky as a great winged disk and pursued Set's forces:

> "Then Heru-Behutet took the form of a great winged disk, and he set himself upon the prow of the boat of Ra. And he pursued the enemies of the god across the waters." *(002-045)*

With the goddesses **Uatchet** and **Nekhebet** transformed into protecting serpents, Horus captured hundreds of foes with chains. The decisive moment came at [[Edfu]]:

> "Horus the son of Isis cut off the head of the Enemy Set, and the gods of the train of Ra rejoiced with hymns of praise." *(002-173)*

## Outcome and Significance

The victory established the divine model of legitimate kingship triumphing over chaos (Isfet). Every pharaoh was identified with [[Horus]] in life, and the myth reinforced the principle that the throne passes from father to son. The [[Winged Disk]] became Egypt's most ubiquitous protective symbol — placed above temple entrances throughout the land to commemorate Horus's eternal vigilance.

The battle is commemorated in the Temple of [[Horus]] at [[Edfu]], where wall reliefs depict the slaughter of Set's forces and the triumph of the winged sun-disk.
