# Sun Boat

The **Sun Boat** (or **Solar Barque**) is the physical ritual counterpart of [[Ra]]'s celestial vessel — wooden boats placed in tombs and temples so that the deceased king could join [[Ra]]'s daily journey across the sky and through the [[Tuat|underworld]]. Model barques crewed by shabti figurines reenacted the divine voyage in ritual space.

## Function and Symbolism

The sun boat serves a dual function:

1. **Ritual Procession**: Cult statues were placed in portable barques and carried by priests, reenacting the solar journey on earth.
2. **Funerary Vessel**: The deceased king required a boat to navigate the waterways of the [[Tuat]] and ascend to the heavens.

The corpus records the divine archetype these models replicate:

> "The Boat of Ra — the gods who formed its crew resumed their rowing, and the Boat of Ra moved onward." *(001-008)*

## In the Texts

The iconographic model is precisely described in the Edfu texts:

> "A boat with a rudder and a double shrine shall be therein, and Aten (the Disk) shall be above it, and Ra shall be in it." *(002-001)*

This corresponds to tomb paintings showing [[Ra]] enthroned within a shrine on the deck, with the [[Winged Disk]] above. From the earliest dynasties, Egyptians built functional wooden boats buried beside royal tombs — not merely symbolic, but actual watercraft for divine navigation.
