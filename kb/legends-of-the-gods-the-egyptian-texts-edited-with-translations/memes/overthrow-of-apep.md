# Overthrow of Apep

## Overview

The Overthrow of Apep is a recurring mythological event in which the sun god Ra battles and defeats the great serpent Apep (also called Apophis) during his nightly journey through the Underworld. Apep, the embodiment of chaos and darkness, attempted each night to devour the Boat of Ra and prevent the sunrise. Ra's victory over Apep was not a single historical event but a daily cosmic struggle that had to be ritually reinforced through spells and ceremonies contained in the "Book of Overthrowing Apep."

## Narrative

### The Daily Battle

Each night, as the Boat of Ra entered the Duat (Underworld), the serpent Apep lay in wait. Apep was the great enemy of Ra, the personification of all that opposed order (Maat):

> "The chief of these was Apep, the great enemy of Ra." [PN=005-095]

### The Spells of Overthrow

The Egyptians composed powerful spells to ensure Ra's victory over Apep. These spells were collected in the "Book of Overthrowing Apep," a ritual text recited to magically assist the sun god:

> "When uttered by Horus it made Apep to vomit, and when used by [the deceased] it destroyed Apep utterly." [PN=005-101]

The spell was designed to neutralize the poison of Apep and strip the serpent of its power:

> "The Book of Overthrowing Apep was to be recited over a figure of Apep, which was to be cursed, trampled upon, and finally burned." [PN=005-100]

### The Serpent's Nature

Apep was described in the texts as the "winding serpent" who lay in the celestial waters:

> "Get thee back, Apep, thou enemy of Ra, thou winding serpent." [PN=018-001]

The serpent was also identified with the "Soul of Apep in Mount Bakhau," the mountain of sunrise where the serpent attempted to block Ra's emergence at dawn [PN=013-032].

## Outcome and Significance

The Overthrow of Apep held profound cosmic significance:

1. **Triumph of Order over Chaos**: Ra's daily victory over Apep was the mythological expression of Maat (order) triumphing over Isfet (chaos). Every sunrise was proof of Ra's victory.

2. **Ritual Reinforcement**: The Egyptians did not take Ra's victory for granted. Priests performed daily rituals reciting the spells from the Book of Overthrowing Apep, creating wax figures of the serpent that were cursed, trampled, and burned.

3. **Solar Cycle**: The battle explained the daily solar cycle — the sun's disappearance at night was its journey through the Duat, and its reappearance at dawn was proof that Apep had been defeated once again.

4. **Funerary Application**: The deceased identified themselves with Ra, hoping to share in his victory over Apep during their own journey through the Underworld.
