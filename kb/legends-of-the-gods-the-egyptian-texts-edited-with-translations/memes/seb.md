# Seb

## Overview

Seb (also known as Geb or Keb) is the earth-god of the Heliopolitan Ennead. He is the son of [[shu]] and [[tefnut]], the brother and consort of [[nut]], and the father of the four great deities [[osiris]], [[isis]], [[set]], and [[nephthys]]. As the deified earth, Seb represents the ground beneath all existence.

## Identity and Role

Seb is the personification of the earth, often depicted lying beneath the sky-goddess Nut, with Shu standing between them to keep them separated. His body is the land of Egypt itself, and earthquakes were understood as Seb's laughter. As the hereditary father of Osiris, Seb serves as an important figure in the legitimization of royal authority.

The majesty of Seb is invoked in divine councils:

> the Majesty of the god Seb, saying, 'Come, with me' [011-002]

Seb's authority as a father-god is acknowledged alongside the primordial deities:

> Eye, and Shu, and Tefnut, and Seb, and Nut, and the father-gods [009-010]

## Family and Relationships

Seb is the son of Shu (air) and Tefnut (moisture), the brother-husband of Nut (sky), and the father of Osiris, Isis, Set, and Nephthys:

> Seb and Nut brought forth [Osiris and Isis and Set and Nephthys] [007-043]

Through Osiris, Seb is the grandfather of [[horus]], establishing the divine lineage that legitimizes the pharaonic throne. In the Contendings of Horus and Set, Seb plays a judicial role, adjudicating the dispute over the succession of Osiris.

## Major Myths and Narratives

**The Separation of Earth and Sky.** Seb and Nut were originally locked in eternal embrace. At Ra's command, their father Shu separated them — lifting Nut above as the sky while Seb remained below as the earth. This separation created the space in which creation could unfold.

**The Judgment of Horus and Set.** Seb sat as judge in the tribunal to determine the rightful heir of Osiris. After hearing both sides, Seb declared Horus the legitimate successor, affirming the principle of patrilineal inheritance that underpinned Egyptian kingship.

**The Sebau Fiends.** The term *Sebau* (derived from Seb's name) appears frequently in protective spells as a designation for serpentine fiends and enemies of [[ra]]:

> overthrow the Sebiu fiends which bite. All men when they see the sun rise, praise thee [018-024]

These fiends were opposed by various gods in the solar barque:

> the Seba Fiend. Thou hast slaughtered the fiends [016-061]

## Cult and Worship

Seb had no major independent cult centre, but was honoured as part of the Heliopolitan Ennead at [[heliopolis]]. His role as the earth from which plants grow connected him to agricultural fertility and funerary beliefs — the deceased were "laid in Seb" when buried in the earth.
