# Council of the Gods

## Overview

The Council of the Gods is a pivotal event in Egyptian mythology in which the sun god Ra, faced with mankind's rebellion and blasphemy, convened the divine assembly in the Great House of Heliopolis. The council of elder gods — including Nu (the primeval waters), Shu, Tefnut, Keb, and Nut — advised Ra to send forth his Eye in the form of the goddess Hathor to destroy the rebels. This council represents the divine deliberation that preceded the Destruction of Mankind and established the precedent that even the supreme god must consult his divine counselors before taking drastic action.

## Narrative

### The Occasion

After ruling Egypt as king for many years, Ra grew old. Mankind, perceiving his weakness, conspired against him and spoke blasphemies. Ra learned of their rebellion and was filled with righteous anger.

### The Assembly in Heliopolis

Rather than acting in haste, Ra demonstrated divine wisdom by calling a council. He was unwilling to destroy the rebels without first consulting the elder gods:

> "He was unwilling to slay the rebels without hearing what his gods had to say." [PN=002-001]

The gods assembled in the Great House of Heliopolis. Among them were the most ancient deities — Nu, the personification of the primeval waters; Shu, god of air; Tefnut, goddess of moisture; Keb, the earth god; and Nut, the sky goddess.

### The Divine Counsel

Ra presented the case against mankind to the council. He explained that men had plotted against him and spoken evil of his name. The gods deliberated and gave their advice:

> "In reply the gods advised Ra to send forth his Eye to destroy the blasphemers." [PN=002-001]

### The Decision and Its Execution

Ra accepted the council's advice and sent forth his Eye, which took the form of the goddess Hathor. She descended upon mankind and began the slaughter:

> "Ra sent forth his Eye in the form of Hathor to destroy the blasphemers." [PN=002-001]

The slaughter began at Suten-henen, and Hathor pursued the rebels relentlessly across the land.

## Outcome and Significance

The Council of the Gods had multiple layers of significance:

1. **Divine Governance Model**: The council established that even Ra, the supreme god, ruled through consultation and consensus rather than arbitrary decree. This mirrored the ideal of Egyptian kingship, where the pharaoh consulted his council of advisors.

2. **Cosmic Legal Procedure**: The council functioned as a divine tribunal, with Ra presenting evidence, the gods deliberating, and a collective decision being reached. This became the model for later divine judgments, including the Judgment Hall of Anu.

3. **Theological Hierarchy**: The council affirmed the hierarchy of the Egyptian pantheon — Ra as the supreme ruler, the elder gods as counselors, and the Eye (Hathor/Sekhmet) as the executor of divine will.

4. **Precedent for Destruction**: The council's decision led directly to the Destruction of Mankind, one of the most consequential events in Egyptian mythology.
