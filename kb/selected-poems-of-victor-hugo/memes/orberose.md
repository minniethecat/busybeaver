# Orberose

**Orberose** is a central female figure in the ancient mythos of [[pingouinie|Pingouinie]], appearing in Livre II of Anatole France's *Penguin Island*. The first Penguin woman to don modest clothing and the companion of the hero [[kraken|Kraken]], Orberose embodies a complex duality — at once loyal wife and unfaithful lover, her transgressions paradoxically secure her husband's survival and the founding of the [[alca|Alcan]] kingdom. Her name, derived from *orbe* (poetic: celestial globe), marks her as a figure of luminous significance in Penguin legendary history.

## Origins and Name

Orberose first appears as a young Penguin woman who had been personally clothed by the monk [[saint-mael|Magis]], becoming the first female to wear modest garments in the post-baptismal Penguin society (015-016). Her name was bestowed upon her by the awestruck crowd who witnessed her fleeing in a dawn-colored robe:

> « en souvenir du jour où la foule émerveillée des Pingouins l'avait vue fuir glorieusement dans sa robe couleur d'aurore, cette vierge avait reçu le nom d'Orberose » [015-018]

The name derives from the poetic French *orbe*, meaning a celestial globe or spherical body — a name befitting her radiant beauty, which was acknowledged as unparalleled among the Penguins of her time (016-025).

## Marriage to Kraken

Orberose's fateful encounter with Kraken occurs on the desolate coast of the Shore of Shadows, where the hero had retreated from society (015-011). Initially terrified — believing Kraken to be a wandering spirit of the dead — she is reassured by his words and accepts his proposal:

> « Orberose, viens dans ma maison, c'est celle du plus ingénieux et du plus brave entre les fils des Pingouins. Si tu consens à me suivre, je ferai de toi ma compagne. [...] Alors, baissant les yeux, elle murmura: — Je vous suivrai, seigneur. » [015-054]

Their union was conducted without ceremony or celebration, hidden from Penguin society in Kraken's inaccessible cavern (015-060). This clandestine marriage set the stage for Orberose's pivotal role in the founding myth of the Draconid dynasty.

## The Dragon Episode

When a terrifying dragon began ravaging the villages of Alca, Orberose disappeared and was presumed devoured — the Elders of Alca reported to [[saint-mael|Maël]] that "il a broyé entre ses dents affamées la vierge Orberose, la plus belle des Pingouines" [017-029]. In truth, the "dragon" was Kraken himself, using a fearsome disguise to terrorize the population as part of his plan to seize power. Orberose's disappearance was a strategic feint; she had simply withdrawn to safety while her husband executed his scheme. The public belief in her death heightened the terror that Kraken's dragon-persona inspired, accelerating the crisis that would ultimately lead to his triumph.

## Affairs and Loyalty

Despite loving her husband, Orberose was not faithful to him alone (019-001). She conducted secret affairs, notably with a young shepherd named Marcel — to whom she presented herself under the false name "Brigide" (019-007) — and later with a cowherd from Belmont (019-028). Anatole France treats these infidelities with characteristic irony: it was precisely through her liaison with the cowherd that Orberose learned of Maël's mandate against the dragon, realizing that Kraken's disguise had become a mortal liability:

> « Et l'heureuse infidélité d'Orberose devait bientôt sauver le héros d'un grand péril et assurer à jamais sa fortune et sa gloire. » [019-026]

This "fortunate infidelity" subverts the moral expectations of legendary narrative — Orberose's transgression becomes the instrument of providence, saving the founder of the Draconid line.

## Role as Counselor

In later passages, Orberose appears in a more mature role, spinning wool by the hearth while Kraken broods (021-010). She serves as his interlocutor and counselor, asking pointed questions that reveal political intelligence. When monks begin conspiring against Kraken, it is Orberose who warns her husband of the threat, demonstrating her evolution from passive beauty to active political actor (021-054).
