# Draco and the Dragon of Alca

The **Dragon of Alca** episode is the founding political myth of [[pingouinie|Pingouinie]], occupying the latter half of Livre II in Anatole France's *Penguin Island*. The episode encompasses [[kraken|Kraken's]] dragon-disguise, his reign of terror over [[alca|Alca]], the presumed death of [[orberose|Orberose]], and the establishment of the Draconid dynasty by Kraken's son [[draco|Draco]]. It is France's satire of mythic founding narratives — the violence that founds political order, retrospectively sanitized into legend.

## The Terror

Kraken, the hero who dwelt in an inaccessible cavern on the Shore of Shadows (015-011), devised a plan to seize power over the Penguins of Alca. Disguising himself as a fearsome dragon — using a costume of hide and horn — he began ravaging the countryside. The dragon "dépeuple nos étables et ravit dans son antre la fleur de notre jeunesse" (017-027), depopulating farms and carrying off the young. The villagers of the Valley of Dalles lived in terror, and even the village of Anis suffered nocturnal attacks (016-031).

Orberose, Kraken's companion, disappeared during this period and was widely believed to have been devoured: "il a broyé entre ses dents affamées la vierge Orberose, la plus belle des Pingouines" (017-029). The Elders of Alca, in desperation, appealed to [[saint-mael|Saint Maël]] to save them from the scourge.

## The Dragon-Slayer

Maël, initially baffled by the problem — dragons were not in his theological training — ultimately issued a mandate declaring that the dragon was a demonic manifestation to be vanquished by spiritual authority. [[orberose|Orberose]], learning of this mandate through her liaison with a cowherd from Belmont (019-068), realized that Kraken could no longer safely maintain his disguise. Her warning saved him from exposure and destruction:

> « Et l'heureuse infidélité d'Orberose devait bientôt sauver le héros d'un grand péril et assurer à jamais sa fortune et sa gloire. » [019-026]

Kraken shed his dragon-skin and emerged as the people's savior — the very man who had terrorized them now presented himself as their deliverer from terror.

## Founding of the Draconids

Kraken's son, born from Orberose, was given the name Draco — the Dragon — in commemoration of the disguise that had won his father power:

> « Kraken eut un fils qui, pour cette raison, fut surnommé Draco. Il fonda la première dynastie des rois d'Alca. » [023-060]

The irony is complete: a dynasty founded on fraud and terror is memorialized through a name that celebrates the instrument of that fraud. The Draconid kings — including Draco the Great (026-006), who "atteignit à une hauteur" through endless [[war|warfare]], and Draco the Simple (027-041) — would rule Pingouinie for centuries, their legitimacy resting on a myth that reversed the roles of predator and protector.

## Satirical Function

The Dragon episode is France's miniature of all founding myths. Every nation, he implies, is built on an act of primordial violence that subsequent historiography transforms into heroic narrative. The dragon — the monster slain by the founder — is, in fact, the founder himself. Civilization does not defeat barbarism; it rebrands it.

|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| Terror | Kraken disguised as dragon ravages Alca | A monster threatens the people |
| Crisis | Elders appeal to Maël | The wise seek divine aid |
| Vanquishing | Kraken removes disguise | The dragon is slain |
| Dynasty | Kraken's son named Draco | The dragon-slayer's line rules |
