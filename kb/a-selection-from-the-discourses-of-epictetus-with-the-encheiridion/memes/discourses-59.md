[059-001]Keep this thought in

[059-002]readiness, when you lose anything external, what you acquire in place of it;

[059-003]and if it be worth more, never say, I have had a loss; neither if you have got

[059-004]a horse in place of an ass, or an ox in place of a sheep, nor a [[good]] action in

[059-005]place of a bit of money, nor in place of idle talk such tranquillity as befits

[059-006]a man, nor in place of lewd talk if you have acquired modesty. If you remember

[059-007]this, you will always maintain your character such as it ought to be. But if

[059-008]you do not, consider that the times of opportunity are perishing, and that

[059-009]whatever pains you take about yourself, you are going to waste them all and

[059-010]overturn them. And it needs only a few things for the loss and overturning of

[059-011]all—namely, a small deviation from [[reason]]. For the steerer of a ship to upset

[059-012]it, he has no need of the same means as he has need of for saving it; but if he

[059-013]turns it a little to the wind, it is lost; and if he does not do this

[059-014]purposely, but has been neglecting his duty a little, the ship is lost.

[059-015]Something of the kind happens in this case also; if you only fall a nodding a

[059-016]little, all that you have up to this time collected is gone. Attend therefore

[059-017]to the appearances of things, and watch over them; for that which you have to

[059-018]preserve is no small matter, but it is modesty and fidelity and constancy,

[059-019]freedom from the affects, a state of mind undisturbed, freedom from fear,

[059-020]tranquillity, in a word liberty. For what will you sell these things? See what

[059-021]is the value of the things which you will obtain in exchange for these.—But

[059-022]shall I not obtain any such thing for it?—See, and if you do in return get

[059-023]that, see what you receive in place of it. I possess decency, he possesses a

[059-024]tribuneship: he possesses a prætorship, I possess modesty. But I do not make

[059-025]acclamations where it is not becoming: I will not stand up where I ought not;

[059-026]for I am free, and a friend of God. and so I obey him willingly. But I must not

[059-027]claim (seek) anything else, neither body nor possession, nor magistracy, nor

[059-028]good report, nor in fact anything. For he (God) does not allow me to claim

[059-029](seek) them, for if he had chosen, he would have made them good for me; but he

[059-030]has not done so, and for this reason I cannot transgress his commands. Preserve

[059-031]that which is your own good in everything; and as to every other thing, as it

[059-032]is permitted, and so far as to behave consistently with reason in respect to

[059-033]them, content with this only. If you do not, you will be unfortunate, you will

[059-034]fail in all things, you will be hindered, you will be impeded. These are the

[059-035]laws which have been sent from thence (from God); these are the orders. Of

[059-036]these laws a man ought to be an expositor, to these he ought to submit, not to

[059-037]those of Masurius and Cassius.
