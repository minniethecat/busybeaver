[056-001]Are you not ashamed at being more cowardly and more

[056-002]mean than fugitive slaves? How do they when they run away leave their masters?

[056-003]on what estates do they depend, and what domestics do they rely on? Do they not

[056-004]after stealing a little, which is enough for the first days, then afterwards

[056-005]move on through land or through sea, contriving one method after another for

[056-006]maintaining their lives? And what fugitive slave ever died of hunger? But you

[056-007]are afraid lest necessary things should fail you, and are sleepless by night.

[056-008]Wretch, are you so blind, and don’t you see the road to which the want of

[056-009]necessaries leads?—Well, where does it lead?—to the same place to which a fever

[056-010]leads, or a stone that falls on you, to death. Have you not often said this

[056-011]yourself to your companions? have you not read much of this kind, and written

[056-012]much? and how often have you boasted that you were easy as to death?

[056-013]Learn then first what are the things which are shameful, and then tell us that

[056-014]you are a philosopher: but at present do not, even if any other man calls you

[056-015]so, allow it.

[056-016]Is that shameful to you which is not your own act, that of which you are not

[056-017]the cause, that which has come to you by accident, as a headache, as a fever?

[056-018]If your parents were poor, and left their property to others, and if while they

[056-019]live, they do not help you at all, is this shameful to you? Is this what you

[056-020]learned with the philosophers? Did you never hear that the thing which is

[056-021]shameful ought to be blamed, and that which is blamable is worthy of blame?

[056-022]Whom do you blame for an act which is not his own, which he did not do himself?

[056-023]Did you then make your father such as he is, or is it in your power to improve

[056-024]him? Is this power given to you? Well then, ought you to wish the things which

[056-025]are not given to you, or to be ashamed if you do not obtain them? And have you

[056-026]also been accustomed while you were studying philosophy to look to others and

[056-027]to hope for nothing from yourself? Lament then and groan and eat with fear that

[056-028]you may not have food to-morrow. Tremble about your poor slaves lest they

[056-029]steal, lest they run away, lest they die. So live, and continue to live, you

[056-030]who in name only have approached philosophy, and have disgraced its theorems as

[056-031]far as you can by showing them to be useless and unprofitable to those who take

[056-032]them up; you, who have never sought constancy, freedom from perturbation, and

[056-033]from passions; you who have not sought any person for the sake of this object,

[056-034]but many for the sake of syllogisms; you who have never thoroughly examined any

[056-035]of these appearances by yourself, Am I able to bear, or am I not able to bear?

[056-036]What remains for me to do? But as if all your affairs were well and secure, you

[056-037]have been resting on the third topic, that of things being unchanged, in order

[056-038]that you may possess unchanged—what? cowardice, mean spirit, the admiration of

[056-039]the rich, [[desire]] without attaining any end, and avoidance ([Greek: echchlisin])

[056-040]which fails in the attempt? [[About]] security in these things you have been

[056-041]anxious.

[056-042]Ought you not to have gained something in addition from [[reason]], and then to

[056-043]have protected this with security? And whom did you ever see building a

[056-044]battlement all around and encircling it with a wall? And what doorkeeper is

[056-045]placed with no door to watch? But you practise in order to be able to

[056-046]prove—what? You practise that you may not be tossed as on the sea through

[056-047]sophisms, and tossed about from what? Show me first what you hold, what you

[056-048]measure, or what you weigh; and show me the scales or the medimnus (the

[056-049]measure); or how long will you go on measuring the dust? Ought you not to

[056-050]demonstrate those things which make men happy, which make things go on for them

[056-051]in the way as they wish, and why we ought to blame no man, accuse no man, and

[056-052]acquiesce in the administration of the universe?
