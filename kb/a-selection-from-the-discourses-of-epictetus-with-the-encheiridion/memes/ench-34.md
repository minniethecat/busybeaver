[102-001]If you have received the [[impression]] ([Greek: phantasion]) of any pleasure,

[102-002]guard yourself against being carried away by it; but let the thing wait for

[102-003]you, and allow yourself a certain delay on your own part. Then think of both

[102-004]times, of the time when you will enjoy the pleasure, and of the time after the

[102-005]enjoyment of the pleasure, when you will repent and will reproach yourself. And

[102-006]set against these things how you will rejoice, if you have abstained from the

[102-007]pleasure, and how you will commend yourself. But if it seem to you seasonable

[102-008]to undertake (do) the thing, take care that the charm of it, and the pleasure,

[102-009]and the attraction of it shall not conquer you; but set on the other side the

[102-010]consideration, how much better it is to be conscious that you have gained this

[102-011]victory.
