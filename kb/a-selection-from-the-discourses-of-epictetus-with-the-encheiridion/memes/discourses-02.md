[002-001]To the rational

[002-002]animal only is the irrational intolerable; but that which is rational is

[002-003]tolerable. Blows are not naturally intolerable. How is that? See how the

[002-004]Lacedaemonians endure whipping when they have learned that whipping is

[002-005]consistent with [[reason]]. To hang yourself is not intolerable. When then you have

[002-006]the opinion that it is rational, you go and hang yourself. In short, if we

[002-007]observe, we shall find that the animal man is pained by nothing so much as by

[002-008]that which is irrational; and, on the contrary, attracted to nothing so much as

[002-009]to that which is rational.

[002-010]Only consider at what price you sell your own will: if for no other reason, at

[002-011]least for this, that you sell it not for a small sum. But that which is great

[002-012]and superior perhaps belongs to [[socrates|Socrates]] and such as are like him. Why then, if

[002-013]we are naturally such, are not a very great number of us like him? Is it true

[002-014]then that all horses become swift, that all dogs are skilled in tracking

[002-015]footprints? What then, since I am naturally dull, shall I, for this reason,

[002-016]take no pains? I hope not. [[epictetus|Epictetus]] is not superior to Socrates; but if he is

[002-017]not inferior, this is enough for me; for I shall never be a Milo, and yet I do

[002-018]not neglect my body; nor shall I be a Croesus, and yet I do not neglect my

[002-019]property; nor, in a word, do we neglect looking after anything because we

[002-020]despair of reaching the highest degree.
