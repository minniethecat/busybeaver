[110-001]When any person treats you ill or speaks ill of you, remember that he does this

[110-002]or says this because he thinks that it is his duty. It is not possible then for

[110-003]him to follow that which seems right to you, but that which seems right to

[110-004]himself. Accordingly if he is wrong in his opinion, he is the person who is

[110-005]hurt, for he is the person who has been deceived; for if a man shall suppose

[110-006]the true conjunction to be false, it is not the conjunction which is hindered,

[110-007]but the man who has been deceived about it. If you proceed then from these

[110-008]opinions, you will be mild in temper to him who reviles you; for say on each

[110-009]occasion, It seemed so to him.
