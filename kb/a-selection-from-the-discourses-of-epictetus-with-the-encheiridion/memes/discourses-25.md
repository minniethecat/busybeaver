[025-001]It is no common (easy) thing to do this

[025-002]only, to fulfil the promise of a man’s [[nature]]. For what is a man? The answer

[025-003]is, A rational and mortal being. Then by the rational faculty from whom are we

[025-004]separated? From wild beasts. And from what others? From sheep and like animals.

[025-005]Take care then to do nothing like a wild beast; but if you do, you have lost

[025-006]the character of a man; you have not fulfilled your promise. See that you do

[025-007]nothing like a sheep; but if you do, in this case also the man is lost. What

[025-008]then do we do as sheep? When we act gluttonously, when we act lewdly, when we

[025-009]act rashly, filthily, inconsiderately, to what have we declined? To sheep. What

[025-010]have we lost? The rational faculty. When we act contentiously and harmfully and

[025-011]passionately and violently, to what have we declined? To wild beasts.

[025-012]Consequently some of us are great wild beasts, and others little beasts, of a

[025-013]bad disposition and small, whence we may say, Let me be eaten by a lion. But in

[025-014]all these ways the promise of a man acting as a man is destroyed. For when is a

[025-015]conjunctive (complex) proposition maintained? When it fulfils what its nature

[025-016]promises; so that the preservation of a complex proposition is when it is a

[025-017]conjunction of truths. When is a disjunctive maintained? When it fulfils what

[025-018]it promises. When are flutes, a lyre, a horse, a dog, preserved? (When they

[025-019]severally keep their promise.) What is the wonder then if man also in like

[025-020]manner is preserved, and in like manner is lost? Each man is improved and

[025-021]preserved by corresponding acts, the carpenter by acts of carpentry, the

[025-022]grammarian by acts of grammar. But if a man accustoms himself to write

[025-023]ungrammatically, of necessity his art will be corrupted and destroyed. Thus

[025-024]modest actions preserve the modest man, and immodest actions destroy him; and

[025-025]actions of fidelity preserve the faithful man, and the contrary actions destroy

[025-026]him. And on the other hand contrary actions strengthen contrary characters:

[025-027]shamelessness strengthens the shameless man, faithlessness the faithless man,

[025-028]abusive words the abusive man, anger the man of an angry temper, and unequal

[025-029]receiving and giving make the avaricious man more avaricious.

[025-030]For this [[reason]] philosophers admonish us not to be satisfied with learning

[025-031]only, but also to add study, and then practice. For we have long been

[025-032]accustomed to do contrary things, and we put in practice opinions which are

[025-033]contrary to true opinions. If then we shall not also put in practice right

[025-034]opinions, we shall be nothing more than the expositors of the opinions of

[025-035]others. For now who among us is not able to discourse according to the rules of

[025-036]art about [[good]] and evil things (in this fashion)? That of things some are good,

[025-037]and some are bad, and some are indifferent: the good then are virtues, and the

[025-038]things which participate in virtues; and the bad are the contrary; and the

[025-039]indifferent are wealth, health, reputation. Then, if in the midst of our talk

[025-040]there should happen some greater noise than usual, or some of those who are

[025-041]present should laugh at us, we are disturbed. Philosopher, where are the things

[025-042]which you were talking about? Whence did you produce and utter them? From the

[025-043]lips, and thence only. Why then do you corrupt the aids provided by others? Why

[025-044]do you treat the weightiest matters as if you were playing a game of dice? For

[025-045]it is one thing to lay up bread and wine as in a storehouse, and another thing

[025-046]to eat. That which has been eaten, is digested, distributed, and is become

[025-047]sinews, flesh, bones, blood, healthy color, healthy breath. Whatever is stored

[025-048]up, when you choose you can readily take and show it; but you have no other

[025-049]advantage from it except so far as to appear to possess it. For what is the

[025-050]difference between explaining these doctrines and those of men who have

[025-051]different opinions? Sit down now and explain according to the rules of art the

[025-052]opinions of Epicurus, and perhaps you will explain his opinions in a more

[025-053]useful manner than Epicurus himself. Why then do you call yourself a Stoic? Why

[025-054]do you deceive the many? Why do you act the part of a Jew, when you are a

[025-055]Greek? Do you not see how (why) each is called a Jew, or a Syrian, or an

[025-056]Egyptian? and when we see a man inclining to two sides, we are accustomed to

[025-057]say, This man is not a Jew, but he acts as one. But when he has assumed the

[025-058]affects of one who has been imbued with Jewish doctrine and has adopted that

[025-059]sect, then he is in fact and he is named a Jew.
