# Happiness

## Definition

In Stoic philosophy, **happiness** (*eudaimonia*) is the natural end (*telos*) of human life — the state of flourishing that all human beings seek by nature. For the Stoics, happiness is not a feeling of pleasure or satisfaction but an objective condition: the perfection of one's rational nature through virtue.

The Stoic position is that virtue is both necessary and sufficient for happiness. External goods — health, wealth, reputation — may be preferred (*proēgmena*) but contribute nothing to genuine happiness. The sage can be happy even on the rack because happiness depends entirely on the quality of one's judgments, not on external circumstances.

## Epictetus's Treatment

[[epictetus|Epictetus]] follows the orthodox Stoic teaching that happiness is inseparable from virtue. "For what purpose then have we received these things?" he asks, and answers: "It is for kingship, for freedom, for happiness, for freedom from perturbation" (033-052). [[philosophy|Philosophy]] itself is the means to this end.

What is distinctive in [[epictetus|Epictetus]] is his emphasis on the immediacy of happiness. It is not a distant goal to be achieved after years of [[exercise|practice]] but a present possibility available to anyone who correctly understands what is and is not within their power.

[[epictetus|Epictetus]] constantly returns to the theme that happiness cannot be found in externals: "You have never sought [[firmness|constancy]], freedom from perturbation, and peace" (056-032). The pursuit of happiness through external means is the fundamental error that philosophy exists to correct.

## Key Passages

- **Happiness as the goal**: [[philosophy|Philosophy]] aims at "kingship, for freedom, for happiness, for freedom from perturbation" (033-052).
- **Happiness and virtue**: [[virtue|Virtue]] "promises good fortune and happiness and freedom from perturbation" (004-009).
- **The error of seeking happiness externally**: Most people have never truly sought "[[firmness|constancy]], freedom from perturbation, and peace" (056-032).

## Related Concepts

- **[[virtue|Virtue]]** (*aretē*) — The sole sufficient condition for happiness.
- **[[freedom|Freedom]]** (*[[freedom|eleutheria]]*) — Happiness and freedom are inseparable; the free person is happy.
- **[[passion|Passion]]** (*pathos*) — [[passion|Passion]]s are obstacles to happiness; *apatheia* is the happy state.
- **[[nature|Nature]]** (*[[nature|physis]]*) — Happiness is living according to nature.
