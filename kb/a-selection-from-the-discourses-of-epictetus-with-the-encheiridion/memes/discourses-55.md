[055-001]Consider as to the things

[055-002]which you proposed to yourself at first, which you have secured, and which you

[055-003]have not; and how you are pleased when you recall to memory the one, and are

[055-004]pained about the other; and if it is possible, recover the things wherein you

[055-005]failed. For we must not shrink when we are engaged in the greatest combat, but

[055-006]we must even take blows. For the combat before us is not in wrestling and the

[055-007]Pancration, in which both the successful and the unsuccessful may have the

[055-008]greatest merit, or may have little, and in truth may be very fortunate or very

[055-009]unfortunate; but the combat is for [[good]] fortune and [[happiness]] themselves. Well

[055-010]then, even if we have renounced the contest in this matter (for good fortune

[055-011]and happiness), no man hinders us from renewing the combat again, and we are

[055-012]not compelled to wait for another four years that the games at [[olympia|Olympia]] may come

[055-013]again; but as soon as you have recovered and restored yourself, and employ the

[055-014]same zeal, you may renew the combat again; and if again you renounce it, you

[055-015]may again renew it; and if you once gain the victory, you are like him who has

[055-016]never renounced the combat. Only do not through a habit of doing the same thing

[055-017](renouncing the combat), begin to do it with pleasure, and then like a bad

[055-018]athlete go about after being conquered in all the circuit of the games like

[055-019]quails who have run away.
