[073-001]Men are disturbed not by the things which happen, but by the opinions about the

[073-002]things; for example, death is nothing terrible, for if it were it would have

[073-003]seemed so to [[socrates|Socrates]]; for the opinion about death that it is terrible, is the

[073-004]terrible thing. When then we are impeded, or disturbed, or grieved, let us

[073-005]never blame others, but ourselves—that is, our opinions. It is the act of an

[073-006]ill-instructed man to blame others for his own bad condition; it is the act of

[073-007]one who has begun to be instructed, to lay the blame on himself; and of one

[073-008]whose instruction is completed, neither to blame another, nor himself.
