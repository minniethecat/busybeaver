[006-001]From everything, which is or happens in the world, it is easy to

[006-002]praise Providence, if a man possesses these two qualities: the faculty of

[006-003]seeing what belongs and happens to all persons and things, and a grateful

[006-004]disposition. If he does not possess these two qualities, one man will not see

[006-005]the use of things which are and which happen: another will not be thankful for

[006-006]them, even if he does know them. If God had made colors, but had not made the

[006-007]faculty of seeing them, what would have been their use? None at all. On the

[006-008]other hand, if he had made the faculty of vision, but had not made objects such

[006-009]as to fall under the faculty, what in that case also would have been the use of

[006-010]it? None at all. Well, suppose that he had made both, but had not made light?

[006-011]In that case, also, they would have been of no use. Who is it then who has

[006-012]fitted this to that and that to this?

[006-013]What, then, are these things done in us only? Many, indeed, in us only, of

[006-014]which the rational animal had peculiar need; but you will find many common to

[006-015]us with irrational animals. Do they then understand what is done? By no means.

[006-016]For use is one thing, and understanding is another; God had need of irrational

[006-017]animals to make use of appearances, but of us to understand the use of

[006-018]appearances. It is therefore enough for them to eat and to drink, and to

[006-019]copulate, and to do all the other things which they severally do. But for us,

[006-020]to whom he has given also the intellectual faculty, these things are not

[006-021]sufficient; for unless we act in a proper and orderly manner, and conformably

[006-022]to the [[nature]] and constitution of each thing, we shall never attain our true

[006-023]end. For where the constitutions of living beings are different, there also the

[006-024]acts and the ends are different. In those animals then whose constitution is

[006-025]adapted only to use, use alone is enough; but in an animal (man), which has

[006-026]also the power of understanding the use, unless there be the due exercise of

[006-027]the understanding, he will never attain his proper end. Well then God

[006-028]constitutes every animal, one to be eaten, another to serve for agriculture,

[006-029]another to supply cheese, and another for some like use; for which purposes

[006-030]what need is there to understand appearances and to be able to distinguish

[006-031]them? But God has introduced man to be a spectator of God and of his works; and

[006-032]not only a spectator of them, but an interpreter. For this [[reason]] it is

[006-033]shameful for man to begin and to end where irrational animals do; but rather he

[006-034]ought to begin where they begin, and to end where nature ends in us; and nature

[006-035]ends in contemplation and understanding, and in a way of life conformable to

[006-036]nature. Take care then not to die without having been spectators of these

[006-037]things.

[006-038]But you take a journey to [[olympia|Olympia]] to see the work of Phidias, and all of you

[006-039]think it a misfortune to die without having seen such things. But when there is

[006-040]no need to take a journey, and where a man is, there he has the works (of God)

[006-041]before him, will you not [[desire]] to see and understand them? Will you not

[006-042]perceive either what you are, or what you were born for, or what this is for

[006-043]which you have received the faculty of sight? But you may say, There are some

[006-044]things disagreeable and troublesome in life. And are there none at Olympia? Are

[006-045]you not scorched? Are you not pressed by a crowd? Are you not without

[006-046]comfortable means of bathing? Are you not wet when it rains? Have you not

[006-047]abundance of noise, clamor, and other disagreeable things? But I suppose that

[006-048]setting all these things off against the magnificence of the spectacle, you

[006-049]bear and endure. Well then and have you not received faculties by which you

[006-050]will be able to bear all that happens? Have you not received greatness of soul?

[006-051]Have you not received manliness? Have you not received endurance? And why do I

[006-052]trouble myself about anything that can happen if I possess greatness of soul?

[006-053]What shall distract my mind, or disturb me, or appear painful? Shall I not use

[006-054]the power for the purposes for which I received it, and shall I grieve and

[006-055]lament over what happens?

[006-056]Come, then, do you also having observed these things look to the faculties

[006-057]which you have, and when you have looked at them, say: Bring now, O [[zeus|Zeus]], any

[006-058]difficulty that thou pleasest, for I have means given to me by thee and powers

[006-059]for honoring myself through the things which happen. You do not so; but you sit

[006-060]still, trembling for fear that some things will happen, and weeping, and

[006-061]lamenting, and groaning for what does happen; and then you blame the gods. For

[006-062]what is the consequence of such meanness of spirit but impiety? And yet God has

[006-063]not only given us these faculties, by which we shall be able to bear everything

[006-064]that happens without being depressed or broken by it; but, like a [[good]] king and

[006-065]a true father, He has given us these faculties free from hindrance, subject to

[006-066]no compulsion, unimpeded, and has put them entirely in our own power, without

[006-067]even having reserved to Himself any power of hindering or impeding. You, who

[006-068]have received these powers free and as your own, use them not; you do not even

[006-069]see what you have received, and from whom; some of you being blinded to the

[006-070]giver, and not even acknowledging your benefactor, and others, through meanness

[006-071]of spirit, betaking yourselves to fault-finding and making charges against God.

[006-072]Yet I will show to you that you have powers and means for greatness of soul and

[006-073]manliness; but what powers you have for finding fault making accusations, do

[006-074]you show me.
