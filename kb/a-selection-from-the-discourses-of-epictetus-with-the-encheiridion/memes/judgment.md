# Judgment

## Definition

In Stoic psychology, **judgment** (*krisis*, *hypolēpsis*) is the cognitive act by which the rational faculty evaluates an impression and determines whether it corresponds to reality. Judgments are the building blocks of character: repeated judgments form dispositions, and dispositions constitute either virtue or vice.

For the Stoics, every emotion is ultimately a judgment. [[fear|Fear]] is the judgment that something bad is present or approaching; desire is the judgment that something good is absent. To change one's emotional life, one must change one's judgments — not through suppression but through rational examination and correction.

## Epictetus's Treatment

[[epictetus|Epictetus]] places extraordinary emphasis on the role of judgment in the moral life. His most famous teaching — "It is not things themselves that disturb men, but their judgments about things" — distills the entire Stoic therapeutic program into a single principle.

Throughout the Discourses, [[epictetus|Epictetus]] returns to the theme that our judgments are entirely within our power. No one can compel us to judge falsely; no circumstance can force us to assent to an inadequate impression. This radical freedom of judgment is the foundation of Stoic invulnerability.

Yet [[epictetus|Epictetus]] is also aware of how difficult it is to correct one's judgments. "We must do what we do in the case of the body," he writes; "when the physician prescribes, we bear the pain in hope of health." The correction of judgment requires persistent [[exercise|practice]] and the willingness to endure the discomfort of recognizing one's own errors.

## Key Passages

- **Judgments, not things**: "It is not things themselves that disturb men, but their judgments about things."
- **Judgment and the discipline of assent**: "In assent and suspension of assent, that you be not deceived" (004-029).
- **The correction of judgment**: Like medical treatment, correcting judgments requires enduring temporary discomfort "in hope of health."

## Related Concepts

- **[[assent|Assent]]** (*synkatathesis*) — Judgment is the act of giving or withholding assent to an impression.
- **[[impression|Impression]]** (*phantasia*) — The raw material upon which judgment operates.
- **[[virtue|Virtue]]** (*aretē*) — [[virtue|Virtue]] consists in correct judgment; vice consists in erroneous judgment.
- **[[passion|Passion]]** (*pathos*) — Every passion is ultimately a false judgment about the value of externals.
