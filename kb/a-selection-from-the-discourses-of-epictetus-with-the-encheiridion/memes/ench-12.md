[080-001]If you intend to improve, throw away such thoughts as these: if I neglect my

[080-002]affairs, I shall not have the means of living: unless I chastise my slave, he

[080-003]will be bad. For it is better to die of hunger and so to be released from grief

[080-004]and fear than to live in abundance with perturbation; and it is better for your

[080-005]slave to be bad than for you to be unhappy. Begin then from little things. Is

[080-006]the oil spilled? Is a little wine stolen? Say on the occasion, at such price is

[080-007]sold freedom from perturbation; at such price is sold tranquillity, but nothing

[080-008]is got for nothing. And when you call your slave, consider that it is possible

[080-009]that he does not hear; and if he does hear, that he will do nothing which you

[080-010]wish. But matters are not so well with him, but altogether well with you, that

[080-011]it should be in his power for you to be not disturbed.
