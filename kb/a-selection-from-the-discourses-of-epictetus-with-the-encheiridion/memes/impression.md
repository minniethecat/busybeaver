# Impression

## Definition

In Stoic epistemology, an **impression** (*phantasia*) is the initial presentation of an object or state of affairs to the mind. Every impression includes a propositional content — a claim about how things are — and is accompanied by a characteristic feeling of conviction that may or may not be warranted.

Impressions are not in our power: they arise automatically from our interaction with the world. What is in our power is the judgment we make about them — whether we assent to their propositional content or reject it.

## Epictetus's Treatment

[[epictetus|Epictetus]] makes the examination of [[appearances|impressions]] the central [[exercise|practice]] of Stoic philosophy. "Make it your study," he repeatedly urges, "to confront every harsh impression with the words: 'You are an impression, and not at all the thing you appear to be'" ([[enchiridion|Encheiridion]] §1).

The key distinction for [[epictetus|Epictetus]] is between the impression itself — which is involuntary — and the use we make of it — which is entirely voluntary. "It is not things themselves that disturb men, but their judgments about things" ([[enchiridion|Encheiridion]] §5). This cognitive theory of emotion grounds his entire practical ethics.

Throughout the Discourses, [[epictetus|Epictetus]] emphasizes the need for vigilance in the face of [[appearances|impressions]]. We must not allow [[appearances|impressions]] to sweep us into assent before they have been examined. "Do not be carried away by the impression," he warns; "say to it: 'Wait; let me see what you are and what you represent'" (Discourse 2).

## Key Passages

- **Confronting [[appearances|impressions]]**: "You are an impression, and not at all the thing you appear to be" ([[enchiridion|Encheiridion]] §1).
- **[[judgment|Judgment]]s, not things**: "It is not things themselves that disturb men, but their judgments about things" ([[enchiridion|Encheiridion]] §5).
- **Vigilance**: "Do not be carried away by the impression; say to it: 'Wait; let me see what you are.'"

## Related Concepts

- **[[assent|Assent]]** (*synkatathesis*) — The act by which we accept or reject an impression as true.
- **[[reason|Reason]]** (*logos*) — The faculty that examines [[appearances|impressions]] before granting or withholding assent.
- **[[passion|Passion]]** (*pathos*) — [[passion|Passion]]s arise from assenting to false [[appearances|impressions]] about the value of externals.
- **[[prohairesis|Prohairesis]]** (moral purpose) — The faculty that exercises choice in responding to [[appearances|impressions]].
