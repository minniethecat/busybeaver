[084-001]When you see a person weeping in sorrow either when a child goes abroad or when

[084-002]he is dead, or when the man has lost his property, take care that the

[084-003]appearance do not hurry you away with it, as if he were suffering in external

[084-004]things. But straightway make a distinction in your own mind, and be in

[084-005]readiness to say, it is not that which has happened that afflicts this man, for

[084-006]it does not afflict another, but it is the opinion about this thing which

[084-007]afflicts the man. So far as words then do not be unwilling to show him

[084-008]sympathy, and even if it happens so, to lament with him. But take care that you

[084-009]do not lament internally also.
