[093-001]Has any man been preferred before you at a banquet, or in being saluted, or in

[093-002]being invited to a consultation? If these things are [[good]], you ought to rejoice

[093-003]that he has obtained them; but if bad, be not grieved because you have not

[093-004]obtained them. And remember that you cannot, if you do not the same things in

[093-005]order to obtain what is not in our own power, be considered worthy of the same

[093-006](equal) things. For how can a man obtain an equal share with another when he

[093-007]does not visit a man’s doors as that other man does; when he does not attend

[093-008]him when he goes abroad, as the other man does; when he does not praise

[093-009](flatter) him as another does? You will be unjust then and insatiable, if you

[093-010]do not part with the price, in return for which those things are sold, and if

[093-011]you wish to obtain them for nothing. Well, what is the price of lettuces? An

[093-012]obolus perhaps. If then a man gives up the obolus, and receives the lettuces,

[093-013]and if you do not give up the obolus and do not obtain the lettuces, do not

[093-014]suppose that you receive less than he who has got the lettuces; for as he has

[093-015]the lettuces, so you have the obolus which you did not give. In the same way

[093-016]then in the other matter also you have not been invited to a man’s feast, for

[093-017]you did not give to the host the price at which the supper is sold; but he

[093-018]sells it for praise (flattery), he sells it for personal attention. Give then

[093-019]the price, if it is for your interest, for which it is sold. But if you wish

[093-020]both not to give the price and to obtain the things, you are insatiable and

[093-021]silly. Have you nothing then in place of the supper? You have indeed, you have

[093-022]the not flattering of him, whom you did not choose to flatter; you have the not

[093-023]enduring of the man when he enters the room.
