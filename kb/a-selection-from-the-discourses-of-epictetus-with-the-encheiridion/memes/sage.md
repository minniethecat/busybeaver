# Sage

## Definition

The **Sage** (*sophos*) is the Stoic ideal: a person who has achieved perfect virtue and complete freedom from passion (*apatheia*). The sage possesses knowledge (*epistēmē*) rather than mere opinion, and their every action is in perfect accordance with nature and reason.

The Stoics acknowledged that the sage is vanishingly rare — perhaps one appears every five hundred years, or perhaps none has ever existed. Yet the ideal of the sage serves an essential function: it provides a standard against which progress (*prokopē*) can be measured.

## Epictetus's Treatment

[[epictetus|Epictetus]] uses the figure of the sage as a [[example|model]] for aspiration rather than as an attainable goal. He frequently invokes [[socrates|Socrates]] and Diogenes as examples of those who approached the sage ideal, but he never claims the title for himself or for any living person.

"What kind of person do I wish to be?" is the question [[epictetus|Epictetus]] places before his students. The answer is implicit throughout the Discourses: one should aim to be like the sage — "erect, unterrified, free from passion and perturbation" (024-068).

[[epictetus|Epictetus]] is realistic about the difficulty. "Show me a Stoic," he challenges. "By the [[divinity|gods]], I long to see one." Yet this acknowledgment of the ideal's difficulty does not lead him to lower the standard. The sage remains the measure of genuine philosophical achievement.

## Key Passages

- **The sage's character**: "Erect, unterrified, free from passion and perturbation" (024-068).
- **Show me a Stoic**: "By the [[divinity|gods]], I long to see one" — [[epictetus|Epictetus]]'s challenge to his students.
- **[[socrates|Socrates]] as [[example|model]]**: [[socrates|Socrates]] is repeatedly invoked as one who approached the ideal of perfect wisdom.

## Related Concepts

- **[[virtue|Virtue]]** (*aretē*) — The sage possesses perfect virtue; there is no gap between knowledge and action.
- **[[passion|Passion]]** (*pathos*) — The sage is free from passion (*apatheia*).
- **[[progress|Progress]]** (*prokopē*) — Those who are not yet sages can make progress toward wisdom.
- **[[freedom|Freedom]]** (*[[freedom|eleutheria]]*) — The sage [[solitude|alone]] is truly free, having mastered all desires for externals.
