[070-001]Remember that [[desire]] contains in it the profession (hope) of obtaining that

[070-002]which you desire; and the profession (hope) in aversion (turning from a thing)

[070-003]is that you will not fall into that which you attempt to avoid; and he who

[070-004]fails in his desire is unfortunate; and he who falls into that which he would

[070-005]avoid is unhappy. If then you attempt to avoid only the things contrary to

[070-006][[nature]] which are within your power you will not be involved in any of the

[070-007]things which you would avoid. But if you attempt to avoid disease, or death, or

[070-008]poverty, you will be unhappy. Take away then aversion from all things which are

[070-009]not in our power, and transfer it to the things contrary to nature which are in

[070-010]our power. But destroy desire completely for the present. For if you desire

[070-011]anything which is not in our power, you must be unfortunate; but of the things

[070-012]in our power, and which it would be [[good]] to desire, nothing yet is before you.

[070-013]But employ only the power of moving towards an object and retiring from it; and

[070-014]these powers indeed only slightly and with exceptions and with remission.
