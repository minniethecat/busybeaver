# Assent

## Definition

In Stoic epistemology and psychology, **assent** (*synkatathesis*) is the act by which the rational faculty (*hēgemonikon*) accepts or rejects an impression (*phantasia*) as true. It is the crucial moment of judgment that stands between the passive reception of [[appearances|impressions]] and the active responses of impulse and action.

Assent is always voluntary — no impression can compel assent without the cooperation of the rational faculty. This is the foundation of moral responsibility in [[stoicism|Stoicism]]: because we [[power|control]] our assent, we [[power|control]] whether we are moved by false appearances or guided by true ones.

## Epictetus's Treatment

[[epictetus|Epictetus]] places the doctrine of assent at the center of his practical ethics. The proper management of assent constitutes one of his three disciplines — the discipline of assent — alongside the discipline of desire and the discipline of action.

"If a man should be able to assent to this doctrine as he ought," [[epictetus|Epictetus]] writes, "he would immediately become a different person" (003-001). The transformative power of correct assent is total: to consistently assent only to adequate [[appearances|impressions]] is to become a sage.

[[epictetus|Epictetus]] is particularly concerned with the suspension of assent (*epochē*) in cases of uncertainty: "in assent and suspension of assent, that you be not deceived" (004-029). The wise person does not rush to judgment but withholds assent until the impression has been examined against the standard of nature and reason.

He also warns against the stubborn refusal to assent to what is manifestly true: "when a man is resolved not to assent to what is manifestly false, he will not be deceived" (005-007). The discipline of assent requires both the courage to accept truth and the restraint to suspend judgment when evidence is insufficient.

## Key Passages

- **The transformative power of correct assent**: "If a man should be able to assent to this doctrine as he ought, he would immediately become a different person" (003-001).
- **Assent and error**: "See that you commit no error; in assent and suspension of assent, that you be not deceived" (004-028, 004-029).
- **Resisting falsehood**: "When a man is resolved not to assent to what is manifestly false, he will not be deceived" (005-007).
- **Assent and purpose**: The rational faculty acts with "purpose and design and assent" (013-005).

## Related Concepts

- **[[impression|Impression]]** (*phantasia*) — The appearance or presentation to the mind to which assent is given or withheld.
- **[[reason|Reason]]** (*logos*) — The faculty that performs the act of assent.
- **[[prohairesis|Prohairesis]]** (moral purpose) — Assent is the mechanism through which prohairesis exercises its freedom.
- **[[passion|Passion]]** (*pathos*) — [[passion|Passion]]s arise from assenting to the false impression that externals are good or bad.
