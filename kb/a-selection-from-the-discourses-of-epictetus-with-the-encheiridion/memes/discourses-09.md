[009-001]When some one asked, How may

[009-002]a man eat acceptably to the gods, he answered: If he can eat justly and

[009-003]contentedly, and with equanimity, and temperately, and orderly, will it not be

[009-004]also acceptable to the gods? But when you have asked for warm water and the

[009-005]slave has not heard, or if he did hear has brought only tepid water, or he is

[009-006]not even found to be in the house, then not to be vexed or to burst with

[009-007][[passion]], is not this acceptable to the gods? How then shall a man endure such

[009-008]persons as this slave? Slave yourself, will you not bear with your own brother,

[009-009]who has [[zeus|Zeus]] for his progenitor, and is like a son from the same seeds and of

[009-010]the same descent from above? But if you have been put in any such higher place,

[009-011]will you immediately make yourself a tyrant? Will you not remember who you are,

[009-012]and whom you rule? That they are kinsmen, that they are brethren by [[nature]],

[009-013]that they are the offspring of Zeus? But I have purchased them, and they have

[009-014]not purchased me. Do you see in what direction you are looking, that it is

[009-015]towards the earth, towards the pit, that it is towards these wretched laws of

[009-016]dead men? but towards the laws of the gods you are not looking.
