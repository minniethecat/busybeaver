[069-001]Of things some are in our power, and others are not. In our power are opinion

[069-002]([Greek: hupolaepsis]), movement towards a thing ([Greek: hormae]), [[desire]],

[069-003]aversion ([Greek: echchlisis]), turning from a thing; and in a word, whatever

[069-004]are our acts. Not in our power are the body, property, reputation, offices

[069-005](magisterial power), and in a word, whatever are not our own acts. And the

[069-006]things in our power are by [[nature]] free, not subject to restraint or hindrance;

[069-007]but the things not in our power are weak, slavish, subject to restraint, in the

[069-008]power of others. Remember then, that if you think the things which are by

[069-009]nature slavish to be free, and the things which are in the power of others to

[069-010]be your own, you will be hindered, you will lament, you will be disturbed, you

[069-011]will blame both gods and men; but if you think that only which is your own to

[069-012]be your own, and if you think that what is another’s, as it really is, belongs

[069-013]to another, no man will ever compel you, no man will hinder you, you will never

[069-014]blame any man, you will accuse no man, you will do nothing involuntarily

[069-015](against your will), no man will harm you, you will have no enemy, for you will

[069-016]not suffer any harm.

[069-017]If then you desire (aim at) such great things remember that you must not

[069-018](attempt to) lay hold of them with a small effort; but you must leave alone

[069-019]some things entirely, and postpone others for the present. But if you wish for

[069-020]these things also (such great things), and power (office) and wealth, perhaps

[069-021]you will not gain even these very things (power and wealth) because you aim

[069-022]also at those former things (such great things); certainly you will fail in

[069-023]those things through which alone [[happiness]] and freedom are secured. Straightway

[069-024]then practise saying to every harsh appearance: You are an appearance, and in

[069-025]no manner what you appear to be. Then examine it by the rules which you

[069-026]possess, and by this first and chiefly, whether it relates to the things which

[069-027]are in our power or to things which are not in our power; and if it relates to

[069-028]anything which is not in our power, be ready to say that it does not concern

[069-029]you.
