## Definition

In Stoic ethics, **roles** (*prosōpa*, Latin *[[mask|persona]]e*) are the multiple relations and stations that define what a person is at a given time — a human being, a citizen, a son, a brother, a father, a neighbor, a ruler or ruled. Each role carries its own "character" (*[[mask|prosōpon]]*, in the dramatic sense of a mask or part): a distinct set of functions the holder must discover and fulfill.

Developed by Panaetius and transmitted through Cicero's *De Officiis*, the doctrine treats moral life as the art of playing one's assigned parts well. [[epictetus|Epictetus]] makes it concrete: "you are a son. What does this character promise?" (026-023) — each role is a promise the holder has made, and the task is to keep it.

The concept is distinct from [[character]] (the moral disposition itself, which every role presupposes) and from [[duties]] (*kathēkonta*, the [[duties|obligation]]s that flow from each role). Roles name the *positions*; character names the *quality*; duties name the *actions*.

## Epictetus's Treatment

Discourse 026 is [[epictetus|Epictetus]]'s sustained treatment of roles. He walks through a person's parts in descending order of scope, beginning with the most universal: "you are a citizen of the world, and a part of it, not one of the subservient (serving), but one of the principal (ruling) parts" (026-007).

The citizen role — the broadest after that of a human being — demands that private interest be subordinated to the common good:

> What then does the character of a citizen promise (profess)? To hold nothing as profitable to himself; to deliberate about nothing as if he were detached from the community, but to act as the hand or foot would do, if they had reason and understood the constitution of nature, for they would never put themselves in motion nor desire anything otherwise than with reference to the whole. (026-010)

He then descends to the family roles. The son's part is to treat everything he has as belonging to his father, to obey him in all things, and never to injure or blame him (026-023).

The brother's part is "to make concessions; to be easily persuaded, to speak good of your brother, never to claim in opposition to him any of the things which are independent of the will" (026-028) — yielding externals to preserve the moral.

The walk continues through station-in-life: "if you are a senator of any state, remember that you are a senator; if a youth, that you are a youth; if an old man, that you are an old man" (026-034).

The fullest enumeration comes in Discourse 029, where the philosopher's work is "maintaining the relations both natural and acquired, as the relation of son, of father, of brother, of citizen, of man, of wife, of neighbor, of fellow-traveller, of ruler, of ruled" (029-023).

Two threads run through the treatment. First, every role is a *promise*: failing it is the real loss, as when one "lost the character of a man; you have not fulfilled your promise" by acting like a [[animal|beast]] (025-006). Second, role-duties concern only what is in one's power; over externals the holder concedes and keeps the good disposition [[solitude|alone]], "as good citizens do to the law" of the whole (008-017, 036-063).

## Key Passages

- (008-017) — The good man administers the whole "as good citizens do to the law" — the citizen as one who obeys the common order.
- (025-006) — "You have lost the character of a man; you have not fulfilled your promise" — the fundamental role, and what it means to fail it.
- (026-007) — "You are a citizen of the world, and a part of it... one of the principal (ruling) parts" — the cosmopolitan role.
- (026-010) — "What then does the character of a citizen promise?" — to act for the whole, like the hand or foot.
- (026-023) — "You are a son. What does this character promise?" — the son's duties to the father.
- (026-028) — "You are a brother also, and that to this character it is due to make concessions" — yielding externals, keeping the moral.
- (026-034) — Senator, youth, old man — roles extend to every station in life.
- (029-023) — "The relation of son, of father, of brother, of citizen, of man, of wife, of neighbor, of fellow-traveller, of ruler, of ruled" — the complete enumeration.
- (036-063) — "To discharge the duties of a citizen" — role-[[duties|obligation]]s as concrete actions.
