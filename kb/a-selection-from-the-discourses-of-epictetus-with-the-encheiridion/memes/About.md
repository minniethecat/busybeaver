# About This Wiki

## Source Material

This wiki is derived from **A Selection from the Discourses of [[epictetus|Epictetus]] with the [[enchiridion|Encheiridion]]**,

translated by George Long (1890). The text consists of selections from the four books of

[[epictetus|Epictetus]]'s *Discourses*, together with the complete *[[enchiridion|Encheiridion]]* (Handbook) — a concise

[[enchiridion|manual]] of Stoic ethical advice compiled by Arrian, [[epictetus|Epictetus]]'s student.

[[epictetus|Epictetus]] (c. 50–135 AD) was a Greek Stoic philosopher who taught that philosophy is a way

of life, not merely a theoretical discipline. His teachings emphasize the distinction between

what is within our [[power|control]] and what is not, the cultivation of inner freedom, and the pursuit

of virtue as the sole good.

## Usage Notes

This wiki presents [[epictetus|Epictetus]]'s teachings as structured, interconnected knowledge pages.

Each chapter of the original text is divided into numbered paragraphs (PN) for precise

citation. Cross-references link related concepts, persons, and themes throughout the

wiki, enabling both linear [[books|reading]] and non-linear exploration.

- **Chapters**: The original text of the Discourses and [[enchiridion|Encheiridion]], with paragraph-level navigation

- **Concepts**: Key Stoic ideas such as [[freedom|Freedom]], [[providence|Providence]], and the Discipline of [[assent|Assent]]

- **People**: Historical figures mentioned in the text, including [[epictetus|Epictetus]], Arrian, [[socrates|Socrates]], and others
