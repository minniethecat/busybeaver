[032-001]What is the first

[032-002]business of him who philosophizes? To throw away self-conceit ([Greek:

[032-003]oiaesis]). For it is impossible for a man to begin to learn that which he

[032-004]thinks that he knows. As to things then which ought to be done and ought not to

[032-005]be done, and [[good]] and bad, and beautiful and ugly, all of us talking of them at

[032-006]random go to the philosophers; and on these matters we praise, we censure, we

[032-007]accuse, we blame, we judge and determine about principles honorable and

[032-008]dishonorable. But why do we go to the philosophers? Because we wish to learn

[032-009]what we do not think that we know. And what is this? Theorems. For we wish to

[032-010]learn what philosophers say as being something elegant and acute; and some wish

[032-011]to learn that they may get profit from what they learn. It is ridiculous then

[032-012]to think that a person wishes to learn one thing, and will learn another; or

[032-013]further, that a man will make proficiency in that which he does not learn. But

[032-014]the many are deceived by this which deceived also the rhetorician Theopompus,

[032-015]when he blames even Plato for wishing everything to be defined. For what does

[032-016]he say? Did none of us before you use the words good or just, or do we utter

[032-017]the sounds in an unmeaning and empty way without understanding what they

[032-018]severally signify? Now who tells you, Theopompus, that we had not natural

[032-019]notions of each of these things and preconceptions ([Greek: prolaepseis])? But

[032-020]it is not possible to adapt preconceptions to their correspondent objects if we

[032-021]have not distinguished (analyzed) them, and inquired what object must be

[032-022]subjected to each preconception. You may make the same charge against

[032-023]physicians also. For who among us did not use the words healthy and unhealthy

[032-024]before Hippocrates lived, or did we utter these words as empty sounds? For we

[032-025]have also a certain preconception of health, but we are not able to adapt it.

[032-026]For this [[reason]] one says, Abstain from food; another says, Give food; another

[032-027]says, Bleed; and another says, Use cupping. What is the reason? is it any other

[032-028]than that a man cannot properly adapt the preconceptions of health to

[032-029]particulars?
