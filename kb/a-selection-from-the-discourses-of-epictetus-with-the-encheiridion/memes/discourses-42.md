[042-001]When some person asked him how it happened that since [[reason]] has

[042-002]been more cultivated by the men of the present age, the progress made in former

[042-003]times was greater. In what respect, he answered, has it been more cultivated

[042-004]now, and in what respect was the progress greater then? For in that in which it

[042-005]has now been more cultivated, in that also the progress will now be found. At

[042-006]present it has been cultivated for the purpose of resolving syllogisms, and

[042-007]progress is made. But in former times it was cultivated for the purpose of

[042-008]maintaining the governing faculty in a condition conformable to [[nature]], and

[042-009]progress was made. Do not then mix things which are different, and do not

[042-010]expect, when you are laboring at one thing to make progress in another. But see

[042-011]if any man among us when he is intent upon this, the keeping himself in a state

[042-012]conformable to nature and living so always, does not make progress. For you

[042-013]will not find such a man.

[042-014]It is not easy to exhort weak young men; for neither is it easy to hold (soft)

[042-015]cheese with a hook. But those who have a [[good]] natural disposition, even if you

[042-016]try to turn them aside, cling still more to reason.
