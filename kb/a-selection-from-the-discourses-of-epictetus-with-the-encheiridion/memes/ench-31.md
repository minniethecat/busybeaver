[099-001]As to piety towards the gods you must know that this is the chief thing, to

[099-002]have right opinions about them, to think that they exist, and that they

[099-003]administer the All well and justly; and you must fix yourself in this principle

[099-004](duty), to obey them, and to yield to them in everything which happens, and

[099-005]voluntarily to follow it as being accomplished by the wisest intelligence. For

[099-006]if you do so, you will never either blame the gods, nor will you accuse them of

[099-007]neglecting you. And it is not possible for this to be done in any other way

[099-008]than by withdrawing from the things which are not in our power, and by placing

[099-009]the [[good]] and the evil only in those things which are in our power. For if you

[099-010]think that any of the things which are not in our power is good or bad, it is

[099-011]absolutely necessary that, when you do not obtain what you wish, and when you

[099-012]fall into those things which you do not wish, you will find fault and hate

[099-013]those who are the cause of them; for every animal is formed by [[nature]] to this,

[099-014]to fly from and to turn from the things which appear harmful and the things

[099-015]which are the cause of the harm, but to follow and admire the things which are

[099-016]useful and the causes of the useful. It is impossible then for a person who

[099-017]thinks that he is harmed to be delighted with that which he thinks to be the

[099-018]cause of the harm, as it is also impossible to be pleased with the harm itself.

[099-019]For this [[reason]] also a father is reviled by his son, when he gives no part to

[099-020]his son of the things which are considered to be good; and it was this which

[099-021]made Polynices and Eteocles enemies, the opinion that royal power was a good.

[099-022]It is for this reason that the cultivator of the earth reviles the gods, for

[099-023]this reason the sailor does, and the merchant, and for this reason those who

[099-024]lose their wives and their children. For where the useful (your interest) is,

[099-025]there also piety is. Consequently he who takes care to [[desire]] as he ought and

[099-026]to avoid ([Greek: echchlinein]) as he ought, at the same time also cares after

[099-027]piety. But to make libations and to sacrifice and to offer first-fruits

[099-028]according to the custom of our fathers, purely and not meanly nor carelessly

[099-029]nor scantily nor above our ability, is a thing which belongs to all to do.
