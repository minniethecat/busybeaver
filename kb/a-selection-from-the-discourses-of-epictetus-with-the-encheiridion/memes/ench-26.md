[094-001]We may learn the wish (will) of [[nature]] from the things in which we do not

[094-002]differ from one another: for instance, when your neighbor’s slave has broken

[094-003]his cup, or anything else, we are ready to say forthwith, that it is one of the

[094-004]things which happen. You must know then that when your cup also is broken, you

[094-005]ought to think as you did when your neighbor’s cup was broken. Transfer this

[094-006]reflection to greater things also. Is another man’s child or wife dead? There

[094-007]is no one who would not say, This is an event incident to man. But when a man’s

[094-008]own child or wife is dead, forthwith he calls out, Woe to me, how wretched I

[094-009]am! But we ought to remember how we feel when we hear that it has happened to

[094-010]others.
