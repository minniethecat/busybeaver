[013-001]When a man holds his proper station in

[013-002]life, he does not gape after things beyond it. Man, what do you wish to happen

[013-003]to you? I am satisfied if I [[desire]] and avoid conformably to [[nature]], if I employ

[013-004]movements towards and from an object as I am by nature formed to do, and

[013-005]purpose and design and [[assent]]. Why then do you strut before us as if you had

[013-006]swallowed a spit? My wish has always been that those who meet me should admire

[013-007]me, and those who follow me should exclaim, O the great philosopher! Who are

[013-008]they by whom you wish to be admired? Are they not those of whom you are used to

[013-009]say that they are mad? Well, then, do you wish to be admired by madmen?
