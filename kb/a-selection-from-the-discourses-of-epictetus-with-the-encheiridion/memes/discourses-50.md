[050-001](And

[050-002]I too, by the gods; for it is a fine thing.) But consider here what precedes

[050-003]and what follows; and then, if it is for your [[good]], undertake the thing. You

[050-004]must act according to rules, follow strict diet, abstain from delicacies,

[050-005]exercise yourself by compulsion at fixed times, in heat, in cold; drink no cold

[050-006]water, nor wine, when there is opportunity of drinking it. In a word, you must

[050-007]surrender yourself to the trainer, as you do to a physician. Next in the

[050-008]contest, you must be covered with sand, sometimes dislocate a hand, sprain an

[050-009]ankle, swallow a quantity of dust, be scourged with the whip; and after

[050-010]undergoing all this, you must sometimes be conquered. After reckoning all these

[050-011]things, if you have still an inclination, go to the athletic practice. If you

[050-012]do not reckon them, observe you will behave like children who at one time play

[050-013]as wrestlers, then as gladiators, then blow a trumpet, then act a tragedy, when

[050-014]they have seen and admired such things. So you also do: you are at one time a

[050-015]wrestler (athlete), then a gladiator, then a philosopher, then a rhetorician;

[050-016]but with your whole soul you are nothing: like the ape you imitate all that you

[050-017]see; and always one thing after another pleases you, but that which becomes

[050-018]familiar displeases you. For you have never undertaken anything after

[050-019]consideration, nor after having explored the whole matter and put it to a

[050-020]strict examination; but you have undertaken it at hazard and with a cold

[050-021][[desire]]. Thus some persons having seen a philosopher and having heard one speak

[050-022]like Euphrates—and yet who can speak like him?—wish to be philosophers

[050-023]themselves.

[050-024]Man, consider first what the matter is (which you propose to do), then your own

[050-025][[nature]] also, what it is able to bear. If you are a wrestler, look at your

[050-026]shoulders, your thighs, your loins: for different men are naturally formed for

[050-027]different things. Do you think that, if you do (what you are doing daily), you

[050-028]can be a philosopher? Do you think that you can eat as you do now, drink as you

[050-029]do now, and in the same way be angry and out of humor? You must watch, labor,

[050-030]conquer certain desires, you must depart from your kinsmen, be despised by your

[050-031]slaves, laughed at by those who meet you, in everything you must be in an

[050-032]inferior condition, as to magisterial office, in honors, in courts of justice.

[050-033]When you have considered all these things completely, then, if you think

[050-034]proper, approach to philosophy, if you would gain in exchange for these things

[050-035]freedom from perturbations, liberty, tranquillity. If you have not considered

[050-036]these things, do not approach philosophy: do not act like children, at one time

[050-037]a philosopher, then a tax collector, then a rhetorician, then a procurator

[050-038](officer) of Cæsar. These things are not consistent. You must be one man either

[050-039]good or bad; you must either labor at your own ruling faculty or at external

[050-040]things; you must either labor at things within or at external things; that is,

[050-041]you must either occupy the place of a philosopher or that of one of the vulgar.

[050-042]A person said to Rufus when Galba was murdered: Is the world now governed by

[050-043]Providence? But Rufus replied: Did I ever incidentally form an argument from

[050-044]Galba that the world is governed by Providence?
