[115-001]When at a small cost you are supplied with everything for the body, do not be

[115-002]proud of this; nor, if you drink water, say on every occasion, I drink water.

[115-003]But consider first how much more frugal the poor are than we, and how much more

[115-004]enduring of labor. And if you ever wish to exercise yourself in labor and

[115-005]endurance, do it for yourself, and not for others. Do not embrace statues; but

[115-006]if you are ever very thirsty, take a draught of cold water and spit it out, and

[115-007]tell no man.
