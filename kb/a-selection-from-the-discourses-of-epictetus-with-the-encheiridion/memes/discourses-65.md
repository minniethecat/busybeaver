[065-001]The

[065-002]difficulties of all men are about external things, their helplessness is about

[065-003]external. What shall I do? how will it be? how will it turn out? will this

[065-004]happen? will that? All these are the words of those who are turning themselves

[065-005]to things which are not within the power of the will. For who says, How shall I

[065-006]not [[assent]] to that which is false? how shall I not turn away from the truth? If

[065-007]a man be of such a [[good]] disposition as to be anxious about these things I will

[065-008]remind him of this: Why are you anxious? The thing is in your own power, be

[065-009]assured; do not be precipitate in assenting before you apply the natural rule.

[065-010]On the other side, if a man is anxious (uneasy) about [[desire]], lest it fail in

[065-011]its purpose and miss its end, and with respect to the avoidance of things, lest

[065-012]he should fall into that which he would avoid, I will first kiss (love) him,

[065-013]because he throws away the things about which others are in a flutter (others

[065-014]desire) and their fears, and employs his thoughts about his own affairs and his

[065-015]own condition. Then I shall say to him: If you do not choose to desire that

[065-016]which you will fail to obtain nor to attempt to avoid that into which you will

[065-017]fall, desire nothing which belongs to (which is in the power of) others, nor

[065-018]try to avoid any of the things which are not in your power. If you do not

[065-019]observe this rule, you must of necessity fail in your desires and fall into

[065-020]that which you would avoid. What is the difficulty here? where is there room

[065-021]for the words How will it be? and How will it turn out? and Will this happen or

[065-022]that?

[065-023]Now is not that which will happen independent of the will? Yes. And the [[nature]]

[065-024]of good and of evil, is it not in the things which are within the power of the

[065-025]will? Yes. Is it in your power then to treat according to nature everything

[065-026]which happens? Can any person hinder you? No man. No longer then say to me, How

[065-027]will it be? For, however it may be, you will dispose of it well, and the result

[065-028]to you will be a fortunate one. What would Hercules have been if he said: How

[065-029]shall a great lion not appear to me, or a great boar, or savage men? And what

[065-030]do you care for that? If a great boar appear, you will fight a greater fight;

[065-031]if bad men appear, you will relieve the earth of the bad. Suppose then that I

[065-032]lose my life in this way. You will die a good man, doing a noble act. For since

[065-033]he must certainly die, of necessity a man must be found doing something, either

[065-034]following the employment of a husbandman, or digging, or trading, or serving in

[065-035]a consulship, or suffering from indigestion or from diarrhoea. What then do you

[065-036]wish to be doing when you are found by death? I, for my part, would wish to be

[065-037]found doing something which belongs to a man, beneficent, suitable to the

[065-038]general interest, noble. But if I cannot be found doing things so great, I

[065-039]would be found doing at least that which I cannot be hindered from doing, that

[065-040]which is permitted me to do, correcting myself, cultivating the faculty which

[065-041]makes use of appearances, laboring at freedom from the affects (laboring at

[065-042]tranquillity of mind); rendering to the relations of life their due. If I

[065-043]succeed so far, also (I would be found) touching on (advancing to) the third

[065-044]topic (or head) safety in forming judgments about things. If death surprises me

[065-045]when I am busy about these things, it is enough for me if I can stretch out my

[065-046]hands to God and say: The means which I have received from thee for seeing thy

[065-047]administration (of the world) and following it I have not neglected; I have not

[065-048]dishonored thee by my acts; see how I have used my perceptions, see how I have

[065-049]used my preconceptions; have I ever blamed thee? have I been discontented with

[065-050]anything that happens, or wished it to be otherwise? have I wished to

[065-051]transgress the (established) relations (of things)? That thou hast given me

[065-052]life, I thank thee for what thou hast given. So long as I have used the things

[065-053]which are thine I am content. Take them back and place them wherever thou

[065-054]mayest choose, for thine were all things, thou gavest them to me. Is it not

[065-055]enough to depart in this state of mind? and what life is better and more

[065-056]becoming than that of a man who is in this state of mind? and what end is more

[065-057]happy?
