[116-001]The condition and characteristic of an uninstructed person is this: he never

[116-002]expects from himself profit (advantage) nor harm, but from [[externals]]. The

[116-003]condition and characteristic of a philosopher is this: he expects all advantage

[116-004]and all harm from himself. The signs (marks) of one who is making progress are

[116-005]these: he censures no man, he praises no man, he blames no man, he accuses no

[116-006]man, he says nothing about himself as if he were somebody or knew something;

[116-007]when he is impeded at all or hindered, he blames himself; if a man praises him

[116-008]he ridicules the praiser to himself; if a man censures him he makes no defence;

[116-009]he goes about like weak persons, being careful not to move any of the things

[116-010]which are placed, before they are firmly fixed; he removes all [[desire]] from

[116-011]himself, and he transfers aversion ([Greek: echchlisin]) to those things only

[116-012]of the things within our power which are contrary to [[nature]]; he employs a

[116-013]moderate movement towards everything; whether he is considered foolish or

[116-014]ignorant he cares not; and in a word he watches himself as if he were an enemy

[116-015]and lying in ambush.
