[098-001]Duties are universally measured by relations ([Greek: tais schsesi]). Is a man

[098-002]a father? The precept is to take care of him, to yield to him in all things, to

[098-003]submit when he is reproachful, when he inflicts blows. But suppose that he is a

[098-004]bad father. Were you then by [[nature]] made akin to a [[good]] father? No; but to a

[098-005]father. Does a brother wrong you? Maintain then your own position towards him,

[098-006]and do not examine what he is doing, but what you must do that your will shall

[098-007]be conformable to nature. For another will not damage you, unless you choose:

[098-008]but you will be damaged then when you shall think that you are damaged. In this

[098-009]way then you will discover your duty from the relation of a neighbor, from that

[098-010]of a citizen, from that of a general, if you are accustomed to contemplate the

[098-011]relations.
