[082-001]If you would have your children and your wife and your friends to live for

[082-002]ever, you are silly; for you would have the things which are not in your power

[082-003]to be in your power, and the things which belong to others to be yours. So if

[082-004]you would have your slave to be free from faults, you are a fool; for you would

[082-005]have badness not to be badness, but something else. But if you wish not to fail

[082-006]in your desires, you are able to do that. Practise then this which you are able

[082-007]to do. He is the master of every man who has the power over the things which

[082-008]another person wishes or does not wish, the power to confer them on him or to

[082-009]take them away. Whoever then wishes to be free let him neither wish for

[082-010]anything nor avoid anything which depends on others: if he does not observe

[082-011]this rule, he must be a slave.
