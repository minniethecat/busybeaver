[092-001]Let not these thoughts afflict you, I shall live unhonored and be nobody

[092-002]nowhere. For if want of honor ([Greek: atimia]) is an evil, you cannot be in

[092-003]evil through the means (fault) of another any more than you can be involved in

[092-004]anything base. Is it then your business to obtain the rank of a magistrate, or

[092-005]to be received at a banquet? By no means. How then can this be want of honor

[092-006](dishonor)? And how will you be nobody nowhere, when you ought to be somebody

[092-007]in those things only which are in your power, in which indeed it is permitted

[092-008]to you to be a man of the greatest worth? But your friends will be without

[092-009]assistance! What do you mean by being without assistance? They will not receive

[092-010]money from you, nor will you make them Roman citizens. Who then told you that

[092-011]these are among the things which are in our power, and not in the power of

[092-012]others? And who can give to another what he has not himself? Acquire money

[092-013]then, your friends say, that we also may have something. If I can acquire money

[092-014]and also keep myself modest and faithful and magnanimous, point out the way,

[092-015]and I will acquire it. But if you ask me to lose the things which are [[good]] and

[092-016]my own, in order that you may gain the things which are not good, see how

[092-017]unfair and silly you are. Besides, which would you rather have, money or a

[092-018]faithful and modest friend? For this end then rather help me to be such a man,

[092-019]and do not ask me to do this by which I shall lose that character. But my

[092-020]country, you say, as far as it depends on me, will be without my help. I ask

[092-021]again, what help do you mean? It will not have porticos or baths through you.

[092-022]And what does this mean? For it is not furnished with shoes by means of a

[092-023]smith, nor with arms by means of a shoemaker. But it is enough if every man

[092-024]fully discharges the work that is his own: and if you provided it with another

[092-025]citizen faithful and modest, would you not be useful to it? Yes. Then you also

[092-026]cannot be useless to it. What place then, you say, shall I hold in the city?

[092-027]Whatever you can, if you maintain at the same time your fidelity and modesty.

[092-028]But if when you wish to be useful to the state, you shall lose these qualities,

[092-029]what profit could you be to it, if you were made shameless and faithless?
