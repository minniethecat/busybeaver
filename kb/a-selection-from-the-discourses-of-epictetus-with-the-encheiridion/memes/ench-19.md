[087-001]You can be invincible, if you enter into no contest in which it is not in your

[087-002]power to conquer. Take care then when you observe a man honored before others

[087-003]or possessed of great power or highly esteemed for any [[reason]], not to suppose

[087-004]him happy, and be not carried away by the appearance. For if the [[nature]] of the

[087-005][[good]] is in our power, neither envy nor jealousy will have a place in us. But

[087-006]you yourself will not wish to be a general or senator ([Greek: prutanis]) or

[087-007]consul, but a free man: and there is only one way to this, to despise (care not

[087-008]for) the things which are not in our power.
