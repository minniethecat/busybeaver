# Prohairesis

## Definition

**Prohairesis** (*prohairesis*) — often translated as "moral purpose," "choice," or "will" — is the central concept of [[epictetus|Epictetus]]'s philosophy. It is the faculty by which we choose between alternatives, particularly between right and wrong. Unlike the modern concept of free will, prohairesis is not a mysterious capacity for uncaused choice but the rational faculty considered specifically in its role of making practical decisions.

For [[epictetus|Epictetus]], prohairesis is what constitutes the self. "You are not flesh or hair, but prohairesis," he tells his students. "If you make that beautiful, then you will be beautiful." The quality of one's prohairesis determines the quality of one's entire life.

## Epictetus's Treatment

Prohairesis is [[epictetus|Epictetus]]'s most distinctive contribution to Stoic philosophy. While earlier Stoics discussed the rational faculty (*hēgemonikon*), [[epictetus|Epictetus]] places prohairesis at the absolute center of his system. Everything good and everything bad resides in prohairesis: "The good is a certain kind of prohairesis, the bad is a certain kind of prohairesis."

No one can harm or constrain prohairesis. "You may fetter my leg, but my prohairesis not even [[zeus|Zeus]] himself can overpower." This radical invulnerability of moral purpose is the source of the Stoic sage's freedom and tranquility.

The practical task of philosophy, for [[epictetus|Epictetus]], is the [[education|training]] of prohairesis — making it "elevated, free, unrestrained, unhindered, faithful, and honorable." Every exercise, every examination of [[appearances|impressions]], every moment of self-reflection serves this single end.

## Key Passages

- **The self as prohairesis**: "You are not flesh or hair, but prohairesis" (Discourse 3).
- **Good and bad in prohairesis**: "The good is a certain kind of prohairesis, the bad is a certain kind of prohairesis" (Discourse 1).
- **Invulnerable**: "My prohairesis not even [[zeus|Zeus]] himself can overpower" (Discourse 1).

## Related Concepts

- **[[virtue|Virtue]]** (*aretē*) — The perfection of prohairesis; virtue is prohairesis in its proper state.
- **[[freedom|Freedom]]** (*[[freedom|eleutheria]]*) — [[freedom|Freedom]] is the unhindered operation of prohairesis.
- **[[assent|Assent]]** (*synkatathesis*) — Prohairesis exercises itself through giving or withholding assent.
- **[[externals|Externals]]** (*ta ouk eph' hēmin*) — Everything that is not prohairesis; the domain of indifference.
