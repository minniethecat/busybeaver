[097-001]In every act observe the things which come first, and those which follow it;

[097-002]and so proceed to the act. If you do not, at first you will approach it with

[097-003]alacrity, without having thought of the things which will follow; but

[097-004]afterwards, when certain base (ugly) things have shown themselves, you will be

[097-005]ashamed. A man wishes to conquer at the Olympic games. I also wish indeed, for

[097-006]it is a fine thing. But observe both the things which come first, and the

[097-007]things which follow; and then begin the act. You must do everything according

[097-008]to rule, eat according to strict orders, abstain from delicacies, exercise

[097-009]yourself as you are bid at appointed times, in heat, in cold, you must not

[097-010]drink cold water, nor wine as you choose; in a word, you must deliver yourself

[097-011]up to the exercise master as you do to the physician, and then proceed to the

[097-012]contest. And sometimes you will strain the hand, put the ankle out of joint,

[097-013]swallow much dust, sometimes be flogged, and after all this be defeated. When

[097-014]you have considered all this, if you still choose, go to the contest: if you do

[097-015]not you will behave like children, who at one time play at wrestlers, another

[097-016]time as flute players, again as gladiators, then as trumpeters, then as tragic

[097-017]actors. So you also will be at one time an athlete, at another a gladiator,

[097-018]then a rhetorician, then a philosopher, but with your whole soul you will be

[097-019]nothing at all; but like an ape you imitate everything that you see, and one

[097-020]thing after another pleases you. For you have not undertaken anything with

[097-021]consideration, nor have you surveyed it well; but carelessly and with cold

[097-022][[desire]]. Thus some who have seen a philosopher and having heard one speak, as

[097-023]Euphrates speaks—and who can speak as he does?—they wish to be philosophers

[097-024]themselves also. My man, first of all consider what kind of thing it is; and

[097-025]then examine your own [[nature]], if you are able to sustain the character. Do you

[097-026]wish to be a pentathlete or a wrestler? Look at your arms, your thighs, examine

[097-027]your loins. For different men are formed by nature for different things. Do you

[097-028]think that if you do these things, you can eat in the same manner, drink in the

[097-029]same manner, and in the same manner loathe certain things? You must pass

[097-030]sleepless nights, endure toil, go away from your kinsmen, be despised by a

[097-031]slave, in everything have the inferior part, in honor, in office, in the courts

[097-032]of justice, in every little matter. Consider these things, if you would

[097-033]exchange for them, freedom from passions, liberty, tranquillity. If not, take

[097-034]care that, like little children, you be not now a philosopher, then a servant

[097-035]of the publicani, then a rhetorician, then a procurator (manager) for Cæsar.

[097-036]These things are not consistent. You must be one man, either [[good]] or bad. You

[097-037]must either cultivate your own ruling faculty, or external things. You must

[097-038]either exercise your skill on internal things or on external things; that is

[097-039]you must either maintain the position of a philosopher or that of a common

[097-040]person.
