# Externals

## Definition

In Stoic ethics, **externals** (*ta ektos*, *ta ouk eph' hēmin*) are everything that lies outside the sphere of moral choice — the body, property, reputation, power, and all other things that are not under our direct [[power|control]]. Externals are classified as indifferents (*adiaphora*): they are neither good nor bad in themselves, though some are naturally preferred (*proēgmena*) and others dispreferred (*apoproēgmena*).

The distinction between what is within our power and what is not is the foundation of [[epictetus|Epictetus]]'s entire philosophy. It appears in the opening lines of the [[enchiridion|Encheiridion]] and is presupposed throughout the Discourses.

## Epictetus's Treatment

[[epictetus|Epictetus]] is the Stoic who most relentlessly emphasizes the externals/internals distinction. "Of things that exist," the [[enchiridion|Encheiridion]] begins, "some are in our power, and some are not in our power." Everything that follows in [[epictetus|Epictetus]]'s teaching is an application of this single distinction.

The practical consequence is radical: we must train ourselves to be indifferent to everything external. "If you kiss your child, or your wife, say to yourself that you are kissing a human being; for thus, if either of them dies, you will not be disturbed" ([[enchiridion|Encheiridion]] §3). This is not callousness but the recognition that attachment to externals is the source of all suffering.

[[epictetus|Epictetus]] does not teach that we should neglect externals. Preferred indifferents may be pursued, but always with the reservation (*hypexairesis*) that their attainment is not up to us. "Make use of what is in your power, and take everything else as it happens" (Discourse 1).

## Key Passages

- **The fundamental distinction**: "Of things that exist, some are in our power, and some are not in our power" ([[enchiridion|Encheiridion]] §1).
- **Attachment and loss**: "If you kiss your child... say to yourself that you are kissing a human being" ([[enchiridion|Encheiridion]] §3).
- **The reservation**: Pursue preferred indifferents "with the reservation" that their attainment is not up to us.

## Related Concepts

- **[[freedom|Freedom]]** (*[[freedom|eleutheria]]*) — [[freedom|Freedom]] is independence from externals; the slave is one who desires externals.
- **[[virtue|Virtue]]** (*aretē*) — The only true good, contrasted with externals which are indifferents.
- **[[prohairesis|Prohairesis]]** (moral purpose) — What is within our power; the opposite of externals.
- **[[desire|Desire]]** (*[[desire|orexis]]*) — Misdirected desire for externals is the source of all suffering.
