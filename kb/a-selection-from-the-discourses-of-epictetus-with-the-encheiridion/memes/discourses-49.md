[049-001]As bad tragic actors cannot sing alone, but in

[049-002]company with many, so some persons cannot walk about alone. Man, if you are

[049-003]anything, both walk alone and talk to yourself, and do not hide yourself in the

[049-004]chorus. Examine a little at last, look around, stir yourself up, that you may

[049-005]know who you are.

[049-006]You must root out of men these two things, arrogance (pride) and distrust.

[049-007]Arrogance then is the opinion that you want nothing (are deficient in nothing);

[049-008]but distrust is the opinion that you cannot be happy when so many circumstances

[049-009]surround you. Arrogance is removed by confutation; and [[socrates|Socrates]] was the first

[049-010]who practised this. And (to know) that the thing is not impossible inquire and

[049-011]seek. This search will do you no harm; and in a manner this is philosophizing,

[049-012]to seek how it is possible to employ [[desire]] and aversion ([Greek: echchlisis])

[049-013]without impediment.

[049-014]I am superior to you, for my father is a man of consular rank. Another says, I

[049-015]have been a tribune, but you have not. If we were horses, would you say, My

[049-016]father was swifter? I have much barley and fodder, or elegant neck ornaments.

[049-017]If then you were saying this, I said, Be it so: let us run then. Well, is there

[049-018]nothing in a man such as running in a horse, by which it will be known which is

[049-019]superior and inferior? Is there not modesty ([Greek: aidos]), fidelity,

[049-020]justice? Show yourself superior in these, that you may be superior as a man. If

[049-021]you tell me that you can kick violently, I also will say to you, that you are

[049-022]proud of that which is the act of an ass.
