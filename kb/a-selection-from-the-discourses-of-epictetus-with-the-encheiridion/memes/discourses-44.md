[044-001]As we

[044-002]exercise ourselves against sophistical questions, so we ought to exercise

[044-003]ourselves daily against appearances; for these appearances also propose

[044-004]questions to us. A certain person’s son is dead. Answer; the thing is not

[044-005]within the power of the will: it is not an evil. A father has disinherited a

[044-006]certain son. What do you think of it? It is a thing beyond the power of the

[044-007]will, not an evil. Cæsar has condemned a person. It is a thing beyond the power

[044-008]of the will, not an evil. The man is afflicted at this. Affliction is a thing

[044-009]which depends on the will: it is an evil. He has borne the condemnation

[044-010]bravely. That is a thing within the power of the will: it is a [[good]]. If we

[044-011]train ourselves in this manner, we shall make progress; for we shall never

[044-012][[assent]] to anything of which there is not an appearance capable of being

[044-013]comprehended. Your son is dead. What has happened? Your son is dead. Nothing

[044-014]more? Nothing. Your ship is lost. What has happened? Your ship is lost. A man

[044-015]has been led to prison. What has happened? He has been led to prison. But that

[044-016]herein he has fared badly, every man adds from his own opinion. But [[zeus|Zeus]], you

[044-017]say, does not do right in these matters. Why? because he has made you capable

[044-018]of endurance? because he has made you magnanimous? because he has taken from

[044-019]that which befalls you the power of being evils? because it is in your power to

[044-020]be happy while you are suffering what you suffer? because he has opened the

[044-021]door to you, when things do not please you? Man, go out and do not complain!

[044-022]Hear how the Romans feel towards philosophers, if you would like to know.

[044-023]Italicus, who was the most in repute of the philosophers, once when I was

[044-024]present, being vexed with his own friends and as if he was suffering something

[044-025]intolerable, said: “I cannot bear it, you are killing me; you will make me such

[044-026]as that man is,” pointing to me.
