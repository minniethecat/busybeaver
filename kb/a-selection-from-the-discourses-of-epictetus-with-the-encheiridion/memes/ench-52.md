[120-001]In every thing (circumstance) we should hold these maxims ready to hand:

[120-002]Lead me, O [[zeus|Zeus]], and thou O Destiny,

[120-003]The way that I am bid by you to go:

[120-004]To follow I am ready. If I choose not,

[120-005]I make myself a wretch, and still must follow.  
  
But whoso nobly yields unto necessity,

[120-006]We hold him wise, and skill’d in things divine.

[120-007]And the third also: O Crito, if so it pleases the gods, so let it be; Anytus

[120-008]and Melitus are able indeed to kill me, but they cannot harm me.
