# The Good

## Definition

In Stoic ethics, **the good** (*to agathon*) is identified exclusively with virtue (*aretē*) and whatever participates in virtue. All other things — health, wealth, reputation, even life itself — are classified as indifferents (*adiaphora*), meaning they are neither good nor bad in themselves and contribute nothing to happiness.

This radical identification of the good with virtue is the defining feature of Stoic ethics. It entails that a person who is virtuous lacks nothing necessary for happiness, while a person who lacks virtue cannot be happy regardless of how many external advantages they possess.

## Epictetus's Treatment

[[epictetus|Epictetus]] is uncompromising in his insistence that the good is entirely within our power. "The good is a certain kind of moral purpose," he teaches; "the bad is a certain kind of moral purpose" (Discourse 1). This locates good and evil entirely in the sphere of choice — in how we judge and respond to [[appearances|impressions]].

This teaching has a liberating effect: if the good is within our power, then happiness is within our power. No tyrant, no circumstance, no external event can deprive us of the good because the good is nothing other than the right use of our own faculty of choice.

[[epictetus|Epictetus]]'s emphasis on moral purpose (*prohairesis*) as the locus of good and evil is his distinctive contribution to Stoic ethics. While earlier Stoics spoke of virtue in broader terms, [[epictetus|Epictetus]] narrows the focus to the immediate activity of choosing correctly in the face of [[appearances|impressions]].

## Key Passages

- **The good as moral purpose**: "The good is a certain kind of moral purpose; the bad is a certain kind of moral purpose" (Discourse 1).
- **[[virtue|Virtue]] as the sole good**: "Now if virtue promises good fortune and happiness, certainly progress towards virtue is progress towards each of these things" (004-009).
- **The independence of the good**: If the good is within our power, "no one can hinder you, no one can compel you" (Discourse 1).

## Related Concepts

- **[[virtue|Virtue]]** (*aretē*) — Identical with the good; the sole good in Stoic ethics.
- **[[prohairesis|Prohairesis]]** (moral purpose) — The seat of good and evil; good/bad reside in the quality of choice.
- **Indifferents** (*adiaphora*) — Everything that is neither good nor bad, including health, wealth, and life itself.
- **[[evil|Evil]]** (*kakon*) — [[vice|Vice]]; the corruption of moral purpose through false judgments.
