[067-001]When you have remitted your attention for a short time, do not

[067-002]imagine this, that you will recover it when you choose; but let this thought be

[067-003]present to you, that in consequence of the fault committed today your affairs

[067-004]must be in a worse condition for all that follows. For first, and what causes

[067-005]most trouble, a habit of not attending is formed in you; then a habit of

[067-006]deferring your attention. And continually from time to time you drive away by

[067-007]deferring it the [[happiness]] of life, proper behavior, the being and living

[067-008]conformably to [[nature]]. If then the procrastination of attention is profitable,

[067-009]the complete omission of attention is more profitable; but if it is not

[067-010]profitable, why do you not maintain your attention constant? Today I choose to

[067-011]play. Well then, ought you not to play with attention? I choose to sing. What

[067-012]then hinders you from doing so with attention? Is there any part of life

[067-013]excepted, to which attention does not extend? For will you do it (anything in

[067-014]life) worse by using attention, and better by not attending at all? And what

[067-015]else of the things in life is done better by those who do not use attention?

[067-016]Does he who works in wood work better by not attending to it? Does the captain

[067-017]of a ship manage it better by not attending? and are any of the smaller acts

[067-018]done better by inattention? Do you not see that when you have let your mind

[067-019]loose, it is no longer in your power to recall it, either to propriety, or to

[067-020]modesty, or to moderation; but you do everything that comes into your mind in

[067-021]obedience to your inclinations.

[067-022]First then we ought to have these (rules) in readiness, and to do nothing

[067-023]without them, and we ought to keep the soul directed to this mark, to pursue

[067-024]nothing external, and nothing which belongs to others (or is in the power of

[067-025]others), but to do as he has appointed who has the power; we ought to pursue

[067-026]altogether the things which are in the power of the will, and all other things

[067-027]as it is permitted. Next to this we ought to remember who we are, and what is

[067-028]our name, and to endeavor to direct our duties towards the character (nature)

[067-029]of our several relations (in life) in this manner: what is the season for

[067-030]singing, what is the season for play, and in whose presence; what will be the

[067-031]consequence of the act; whether our associates will despise us, whether we

[067-032]shall despise them; when to jeer ([Greek: schopsai]), and whom to ridicule; and

[067-033]on what occasion to comply and with whom; and finally, in complying how to

[067-034]maintain our own character. But wherever you have deviated from any of these

[067-035]rules, there is damage immediately, not from anything external, but from the

[067-036]action itself.

[067-037]What then? is it possible to be free from faults (if you do all this)? It is

[067-038]not possible; but this is possible, to direct your efforts incessantly to being

[067-039]faultless. For we must be content if by never remitting this attention we shall

[067-040]escape at least a few errors. But now when you have said, Tomorrow I will begin

[067-041]to attend, you must be told that you are saying this, Today I will be

[067-042]shameless, disregardful of time and place, mean; it will be in the power of

[067-043]others to give me pain; today I will be passionate and envious. See how many

[067-044]evil things you are permitting yourself to do. If it is [[good]] to use attention

[067-045]tomorrow, how much better is it to do so today? if tomorrow it is in your

[067-046]interest to attend, much more is it today, that you may be able to do so

[067-047]tomorrow also, and may not defer it again to the third day.
