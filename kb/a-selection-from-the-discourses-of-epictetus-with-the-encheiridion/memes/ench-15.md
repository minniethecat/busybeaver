[083-001]Remember that in life you ought to behave as at a banquet. Suppose that

[083-002]something is carried round and is opposite to you. Stretch out your hand and

[083-003]take a portion with decency. Suppose that it passes by you. Do not detain it.

[083-004]Suppose that it is not yet come to you. Do not send your [[desire]] forward to it,

[083-005]but wait till it is opposite to you. Do so with respect to children, so with

[083-006]respect to a wife, so with respect to magisterial offices, so with respect to

[083-007]wealth, and you will be some time a worthy partner of the banquets of the gods.

[083-008]But if you take none of the things which are set before you, and even despise

[083-009]them, then you will be not only a fellow banqueter with the gods, but also a

[083-010]partner with them in power. For by acting thus Diogenes and Heracleitus and

[083-011]those like them were deservedly divine, and were so called.
