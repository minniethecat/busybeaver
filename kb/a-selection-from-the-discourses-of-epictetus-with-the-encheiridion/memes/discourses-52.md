[052-001]When you make any charge against Providence, consider, and you

[052-002]will learn that the thing has happened according to [[reason]]. Yes, but the unjust

[052-003]man has the advantage. In what? In money. Yes, for he is superior to you in

[052-004]this, that he flatters, is free from shame, and is watchful. What is the

[052-005]wonder? But see if he has the advantage over you in being faithful, in being

[052-006]modest; for you will not find it to be so; but wherein you are superior, there

[052-007]you will find that you have the advantage. And I once said to a man who was

[052-008]vexed because Philostorgus was fortunate: Would you choose to lie with Sura?

[052-009]May it never happen, he replied, that this day should come? Why then are you

[052-010]vexed, if he receives something in return for that which he sells; or how can

[052-011]you consider him happy who acquires those things by such means as you

[052-012]abominate; or what wrong does Providence, if he gives the better things to the

[052-013]better men? Is it not better to be modest than to be rich? He admitted this.

[052-014]Why are you vexed then, man, when you possess the better thing? Remember then

[052-015]always and have in readiness the truth, that this is a law of [[nature]], that the

[052-016]superior has an advantage over the inferior in that in which he is superior;

[052-017]and you will never be vexed.

[052-018]But my wife treats me badly. Well, if any man asks you what this is, say, my

[052-019]wife treats me badly. Is there then nothing more? Nothing. My father gives me

[052-020]nothing. (What is this? my father gives me nothing. Is there nothing else then?

[052-021]Nothing); but to say that this is an evil is something which must be added to

[052-022]it externally, and falsely added. For this reason we must not get rid of

[052-023]poverty, but of the opinion about poverty, and then we shall be happy.
