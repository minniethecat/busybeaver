[016-001]If these things are true, and if we are not silly, and are not

[016-002]acting hypocritically when we say that the [[good]] of man is in the will, and the

[016-003]evil too, and that everything else does not concern us, why are we still

[016-004]disturbed, why are we still afraid? The things about which we have been busied

[016-005]are in no man’s power; and the things which are in the power of others, we care

[016-006]not for. What kind of trouble have we still?

[016-007]But give me directions. Why should I give you directions? Has not [[zeus|Zeus]] given

[016-008]you directions? Has he not given to you what is your own free from hindrance

[016-009]and free from impediment, and what is not your own subject to hindrance and

[016-010]impediment? What directions then, what kind of orders did you bring when you

[016-011]came from him? Keep by every means what is your own; do not [[desire]] what belongs

[016-012]to others. Fidelity (integrity) is your own, virtuous shame is your own; who

[016-013]then can take these things from you? who else than yourself will hinder you

[016-014]from using them? But how do you act? When you seek what is not your own, you

[016-015]lose that which is your own. Having such promptings and commands from Zeus,

[016-016]what kind do you still ask from me? Am I more powerful than he, am I more

[016-017]worthy of confidence? But if you observe these, do you want any others besides?

[016-018]“Well, but he has not given these orders,” you will say. Produce your

[016-019]præcognitions ([Greek: prolaepseis]), produce these proofs of philosophers,

[016-020]produce what you have often heard, and produce what you have said yourself,

[016-021]produce what you have read, produce what you have meditated on; and you will

[016-022]then see that all these things are from God.

[016-023]If I have set my admiration on the poor body, I have given myself up to be a

[016-024]slave; if on my poor possessions, I also make myself a slave. For I immediately

[016-025]make it plain with what I may be caught; as if the snake draws in his head, I

[016-026]tell you to strike that part of him which he guards; and do you be assured that

[016-027]whatever part you choose to guard, that part your master will attack.

[016-028]Remembering this, whom will you still flatter or fear?

[016-029]But I should like to sit where the Senators sit. Do you see that you are

[016-030]putting yourself in straits, you are squeezing yourself? How then shall I see

[016-031]well in any other way in the amphitheatre? Man, do not be a spectator at all,

[016-032]and you will not be squeezed. Why do you give yourself trouble? Or wait a

[016-033]little, and when the spectacle is over, seat yourself in the place reserved for

[016-034]the Senators and sun yourself. For remember this general truth, that it is we

[016-035]who squeeze ourselves, who put ourselves in straits; that is, our opinions

[016-036]squeeze us and put us in straits. For what is it to be reviled? Stand by a

[016-037]stone and revile it, and what will you gain? If then a man listens like a

[016-038]stone, what profit is there to the reviler? But if the reviler has as a

[016-039]stepping-stone (or ladder) the weakness of him who is reviled, then he

[016-040]accomplishes something. Strip him. What do you mean by him? Lay hold of his

[016-041]garment, strip it off. I have insulted you. Much good may it do you.

[016-042]This was the practice of [[socrates|Socrates]]; this was the [[reason]] why he always had one

[016-043]face. But we choose to practise and study anything rather than the means by

[016-044]which we shall be unimpeded and free. You say: “Philosophers talk paradoxes.”

[016-045]But are there no paradoxes in the other arts? And what is more paradoxical than

[016-046]to puncture a man’s eye in order that he may see? If any one said this to a man

[016-047]ignorant of the surgical art, would he not ridicule the speaker? Where is the

[016-048]wonder, then, if in philosophy also many things which are true appear

[016-049]paradoxical to the inexperienced?
