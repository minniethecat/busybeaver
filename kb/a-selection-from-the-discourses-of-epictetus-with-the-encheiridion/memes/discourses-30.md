[030-001]When

[030-002]some persons have heard these words, that a man ought to be constant (firm),

[030-003]and that the will is naturally free and not subject to compulsion, but that all

[030-004]other things are subject to hindrance, to slavery, and are in the power of

[030-005]others, they suppose that they ought without deviation to abide by everything

[030-006]which they have determined. But in the first place that which has been

[030-007]determined ought to be sound (true). I require tone (sinews) in the body, but

[030-008]such as exists in a healthy body, in an athletic body; but if it is plain to me

[030-009]that you have the tone of a frenzied man and you boast of it, I shall say to

[030-010]you, Man, seek the physician; this is not tone, but atony (deficiency in right

[030-011]tone). In a different way something of the same kind is felt by those who

[030-012]listen to these discourses in a wrong manner; which was the case with one of my

[030-013]companions, who for no [[reason]] resolved to starve himself to death. I heard of

[030-014]it when it was the third day of his abstinence from food, and I went to inquire

[030-015]what had happened. “I have resolved,” he said. “But still tell me what it was

[030-016]which induced you to resolve; for if you have resolved rightly, we shall sit

[030-017]with you and assist you to depart, but if you have made an unreasonable

[030-018]resolution, change your mind.” “We ought to keep to our determinations.” “What

[030-019]are you doing, man? We ought to keep not to all our determinations, but to

[030-020]those which are right; for if you are now persuaded that it is right, do not

[030-021]change your mind, if you think fit, but persist and say, We ought to abide by

[030-022]our determinations. Will you not make the beginning and lay the foundation in

[030-023]an inquiry whether the determination is sound or not sound, and so then build

[030-024]on it firmness and security? But if you lay a rotten and ruinous foundation,

[030-025]will not your miserable little building fall down the sooner, the more and the

[030-026]stronger are the materials which you shall lay on it? Without any reason would

[030-027]you withdraw from us out of life a man who is a friend and a companion, a

[030-028]citizen of the same city, both the great and the small city? Then while you are

[030-029]committing murder and destroying a man who has done no wrong, do you say that

[030-030]you ought to abide by your determinations? And if it ever in any way came into

[030-031]your head to kill me, ought you to abide by your determinations?”

[030-032]Now this man was with difficulty persuaded to change his mind. But it is

[030-033]impossible to convince some persons at present; so that I seem now to know what

[030-034]I did not know before, the meaning of the common saying, that you can neither

[030-035]persuade nor break a fool. May it never be my lot to have a wise fool for my

[030-036]friend; nothing is more untractable. “I am determined,” the man says. Madmen

[030-037]are also, but the more firmly they form a [[judgment]] on things which do not

[030-038]exist, the more hellebore they require. Will you not act like a sick man and

[030-039]call in the physician?—I am sick, master, help me; consider what I must do: it

[030-040]is my duty to obey you. So it is here also: I know not what I ought to do, but

[030-041]I am come to learn.—Not so; but speak to me about other things: upon this I

[030-042]have determined.—What other things? for what is greater and more useful than

[030-043]for you to be persuaded that it is not sufficient to have made your

[030-044]determination and not to change it. This is the tone (energy) of madness, not

[030-045]of health.—I will die, if you compel me to this.—Why, man? What has happened?—I

[030-046]have determined—I have had a lucky escape that you have not determined to kill

[030-047]me—I take no money. Why?—I have determined—Be assured that with the very tone

[030-048](energy) which you now use in refusing to take, there is nothing to hinder you

[030-049]at some time from inclining without reason to take money, and then saying, I

[030-050]have determined. As in a distempered body, subject to defluxions, the humor

[030-051]inclines sometimes to these parts, and then to those, so too a sickly soul

[030-052]knows not which way to incline; but if to this inclination and movement there

[030-053]is added a tone (obstinate resolution), then the evil becomes past help and

[030-054]cure.
