[007-001]I indeed think that the old man ought to be sitting here, not to

[007-002]contrive how you may have no mean thoughts nor mean and ignoble talk about

[007-003]yourselves, but to take care that there be not among us any young men of such a

[007-004]mind, that when they have recognized their kinship to God, and that we are

[007-005]fettered by these bonds, the body, I mean, and its possessions, and whatever

[007-006]else on account of them is necessary to us for the economy and commerce of

[007-007]life, they should intend to throw off these things as if they were burdens

[007-008]painful and intolerable, and to depart to their kinsmen. But this is the labor

[007-009]that your teacher and instructor ought to be employed upon, if he really were

[007-010]what he should be. You should come to him and say: [[epictetus|Epictetus]], we can no longer

[007-011]endure being bound to this poor body, and feeding it, and giving it drink and

[007-012]rest, and cleaning it, and for the sake of the body complying with the wishes

[007-013]of these and of those. Are not these things indifferent and nothing to us; and

[007-014]is not death no evil? And are we not in a manner kinsmen of God, and did we not

[007-015]come from him? Allow us to depart to the place from which we came; allow us to

[007-016]be released at last from these bonds by which we are bound and weighed down.

[007-017]Here there are robbers and thieves and courts of justice, and those who are

[007-018]named tyrants, and think that they have some power over us by means of the body

[007-019]and its possessions. Permit us to show them that they have no power over any

[007-020]man. And I on my part would say: Friends, wait for God: when he shall give the

[007-021]signal and release you from this service, then go to him; but for the present

[007-022]endure to dwell in this place where he has put you. Short indeed is this time

[007-023]of your dwelling here, and easy to bear for those who are so disposed; for what

[007-024]tyrant, or what thief, or what courts of justice are formidable to those who

[007-025]have thus considered as things of no value the body and the possessions of the

[007-026]body? Wait then, do not depart without a [[reason]].
