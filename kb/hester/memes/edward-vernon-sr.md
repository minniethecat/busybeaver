# Edward Vernon Sr.

## Overview

The original founder of Vernon's Bank — the patriarch whose financial genius established the family fortune and whose shadow looms over the novel's present-day conflicts.

## Role in the Novel

Edward Vernon Sr. occupies a particular niche in the Vernon social ecosystem, contributing to Oliphant's comprehensive mapping of the dependencies, obligations, and strategies that characterize life in and around the Vernonry.

## Character Analysis

As with all of Oliphant's minor characters, Edward Vernon Sr. is drawn with an economy that nonetheless suggests a full life beyond the novel's frame. Their presence in the narrative serves to populate the social world and illuminate the central characters' positions through contrast.

## Key Scenes

- Brief but telling appearances in Chapters 001 and 003

## Literary Significance

Edward Vernon Sr. exemplifies Oliphant's skill at creating a populated social world through economical characterization — each minor figure implying a whole life, a whole set of accommodations to the conditions that the novel anatomizes.
