# London (The City)

## Overview

The financial center of England — mentioned as the distant arena where larger financial forces operate, beyond the reach of Redborough's provincial economy.

## Role in the Novel

London (The City) represents a location referenced rather than directly depicted — part of the wider world that impinges on Redborough and shapes the possibilities available to its inhabitants.

## Significance

Mentioned or implied locations contribute to *Hester*'s sense of a larger world beyond the provincial setting — the financial markets, the fashion capitals, the historical institutions that frame the novel's local concerns.
