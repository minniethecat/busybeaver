# The Ashton Parents

## Overview

The unseen but influential parents of Roland and Emma Ashton — representatives of the genteel poverty that drives their children's social ambition.

## Role in the Novel

The Ashton Parents occupies a particular niche in the Vernon social ecosystem, contributing to Oliphant's comprehensive mapping of the dependencies, obligations, and strategies that characterize life in and around the Vernonry.

## Character Analysis

As with all of Oliphant's minor characters, The Ashton Parents is drawn with an economy that nonetheless suggests a full life beyond the novel's frame. Their presence in the narrative serves to populate the social world and illuminate the central characters' positions through contrast.

## Key Scenes

- Brief but telling appearances in Chapters minor and 005

## Literary Significance

The Ashton Parents exemplifies Oliphant's skill at creating a populated social world through economical characterization — each minor figure implying a whole life, a whole set of accommodations to the conditions that the novel anatomizes.
