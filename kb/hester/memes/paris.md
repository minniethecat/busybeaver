# Paris

## Overview

Referenced as the source of Ellen Vernon's fashionable wardrobe — representing the world of fashion and consumption that exists in tension with Redborough's provincial solidity.

## Role in the Novel

Paris represents a location referenced rather than directly depicted — part of the wider world that impinges on Redborough and shapes the possibilities available to its inhabitants.

## Significance

Mentioned or implied locations contribute to *Hester*'s sense of a larger world beyond the provincial setting — the financial markets, the fashion capitals, the historical institutions that frame the novel's local concerns.
