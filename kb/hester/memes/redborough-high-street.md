# Redborough High Street

## Overview

The commercial heart of Redborough — where the bank stands and where the town's economic life is concentrated around the Vernon family's institution.

## Role in the Novel

Redborough High Street fills in the spatial geography of Oliphant's Redborough, contributing to the novel's detailed mapping of the relationship between physical space and social position.

## Significance

Each location in *Hester* carries specific social meaning — encoding hierarchies, marking boundaries between insiders and outsiders, and materializing the economic relations that the novel anatomizes.

## Key Associations

Significant to the novel's spatial organization of power, dependence, and community.
