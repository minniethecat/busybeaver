# Mrs. Morgan

## Overview

A gentle, elderly resident of the Vernonry — a quietly observant figure whose long perspective on the Vernon family provides a counterweight to the intensity of the central conflicts.

## Role in the Novel

Mrs. Morgan occupies a particular niche in the Vernon social ecosystem, contributing to Oliphant's comprehensive mapping of the dependencies, obligations, and strategies that characterize life in and around the Vernonry.

## Character Analysis

As with all of Oliphant's minor characters, Mrs. Morgan is drawn with an economy that nonetheless suggests a full life beyond the novel's frame. Their presence in the narrative serves to populate the social world and illuminate the central characters' positions through contrast.

## Key Scenes

- Brief but telling appearances in Chapters 017 and 042

## Literary Significance

Mrs. Morgan exemplifies Oliphant's skill at creating a populated social world through economical characterization — each minor figure implying a whole life, a whole set of accommodations to the conditions that the novel anatomizes.
