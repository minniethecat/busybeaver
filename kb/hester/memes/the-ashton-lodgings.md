# The Ashton Lodgings

## Overview

The modest accommodation of Roland and Emma Ashton — representing the precarious social position of those who hover at the edge of the Vernon circle without being fully absorbed into it.

## Role in the Novel

The Ashton Lodgings fills in the spatial geography of Oliphant's Redborough, contributing to the novel's detailed mapping of the relationship between physical space and social position.

## Significance

Each location in *Hester* carries specific social meaning — encoding hierarchies, marking boundaries between insiders and outsiders, and materializing the economic relations that the novel anatomizes.

## Key Associations

Significant to the novel's spatial organization of power, dependence, and community.
