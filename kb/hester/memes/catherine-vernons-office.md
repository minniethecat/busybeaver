# Catherine Vernon's Office

## Overview

The inner office of Vernon's Bank where Catherine exercises her authority — the space where financial decisions are made and where the novel's climactic confrontations occur.

## Role in the Novel

Catherine Vernon's Office fills in the spatial geography of Oliphant's Redborough, contributing to the novel's detailed mapping of the relationship between physical space and social position.

## Significance

Each location in *Hester* carries specific social meaning — encoding hierarchies, marking boundaries between insiders and outsiders, and materializing the economic relations that the novel anatomizes.

## Key Associations

Significant to the novel's spatial organization of power, dependence, and community.
