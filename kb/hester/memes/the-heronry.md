# The Heronry

## Overview

The original name of the house that became the Vernonry — a reminder of the building's pre-history before it was converted into apartments for dependent relatives.

## Role in the Novel

The Heronry fills in the spatial geography of Oliphant's Redborough, contributing to the novel's detailed mapping of the relationship between physical space and social position.

## Significance

Each location in *Hester* carries specific social meaning — encoding hierarchies, marking boundaries between insiders and outsiders, and materializing the economic relations that the novel anatomizes.

## Key Associations

Significant to the novel's spatial organization of power, dependence, and community.
