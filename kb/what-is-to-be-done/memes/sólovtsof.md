# Sólovtsof

Sólovtsof is a minor but significant antagonist in the novel — the man who seduces and abandons Nástenka Kryukova, and whose cynical character represents everything the "new people" stand against.

## The Seducer

Kirsánof recounts Sólovtsof's treatment of Nástenka as a cautionary tale illustrating the old order's predation on vulnerable women. Sólovtsof is characterized by superficial charm and genuine viciousness — he promises marriage, takes what he wants, and then abandons his victim when she becomes inconvenient. His type is the negative image of the "new man": where Lopukhóf and Kirsánof treat women as equals whose autonomy must be respected, Sólovtsof treats them as objects to be used and discarded.

## Narrative Function

Sólovtsof serves a specific structural role: by showing the consequences of the old order's sexual morality for women without resources, he makes visible what Viéra Pavlovna escaped. Nástenka's tragedy is the road not taken — the fate that would have been Viéra's had she not encountered the "new people" and their philosophy.
