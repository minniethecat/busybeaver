# Kirsánof's Hospital

Kirsánof's hospital is the professional domain of the novel's scientific "new man" — a space where research and healing, theory and practice, coexist in productive tension.

## The Researcher's Domain

Kirsánof describes his hospital work in rigorously scientific terms: "I do not cure; I only observe, and make experiments" (051-003). The hospital is, in his account, a laboratory — a space for the production of medical knowledge rather than the restoration of individual health. Yet the hospital servants tell a different story: "Keep up good heart; it takes a tough sickness to stand up against this surgeon; he is a master, and a real father" (051-003). The hospital thus becomes the site where the tension between Kirsánof's self-presentation as pure researcher and his actual practice as healer is most visible.

## A Space of Care

The hospital is also where Kirsánof treats Nástenka Kryukova — not only her physical condition but the social and psychological damage of her betrayal by Sólovtsof. The hospital thus extends beyond the treatment of bodies to the care of persons, embodying the "new people's" refusal to separate scientific practice from human concern.
