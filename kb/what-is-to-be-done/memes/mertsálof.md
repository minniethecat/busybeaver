# Mertsálof

Alekséi Petróvitch Mertsálof is a priest who belongs to the "new people's" circle — an unusual figure whose religious office coexists with his allegiance to the rationalist, materialist philosophy of Lopukhóf and Kirsánof.

## The Believing Rationalist

Mertsálof performs the wedding ceremony for Lopukhóf and Viéra Pavlovna, and remains a trusted friend throughout the novel. His presence in the "new people's" circle serves an important narrative function: it demonstrates that the philosophy of rational egotism is not inherently anti-religious but can accommodate genuine religious conviction. The Mertsálofs are the only married couple among the Lopukhófs' regular visitors, and their household provides one of the few social spaces outside the Lopukhófs' own apartment where the "new people" gather.

## The Second Dream

Mertsálof appears in Viéra's Second Dream (as Alekséi Petróvitch), walking across the field with Lopukhóf and explaining the allegory of the soil — the distinction between "actual dirt" (composed of healthy elements that merely need proper coordination) and "putrid dirt" (rotten at its core) (044-003). His role as explicator of the dream's philosophical content aligns him with the novel's intellectual project.
