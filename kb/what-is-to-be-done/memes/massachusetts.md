# Massachusetts

Massachusetts appears in the novel's background as part of Beaumont's American biography — the New England state associated with utopian communities, abolitionism, and social experimentation.

## The Utopian Landscape

Chernyshevsky's choice of Massachusetts as Beaumont's American home is not arbitrary. In the 1840s and 1850s, Massachusetts was the center of American Fourierism — the utopian socialist movement inspired by Charles Fourier, whose ideas about cooperative communities and the transformation of labor into pleasure profoundly influenced Chernyshevsky. Brook Farm, the most famous Fourierist community, was located in Massachusetts. By placing Beaumont in Massachusetts, the novel connects the "new people's" Russian experiment to a transatlantic tradition of utopian social thought.
