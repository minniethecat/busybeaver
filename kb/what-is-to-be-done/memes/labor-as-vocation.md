# Labor as Vocation

The principle that work should be pursued for its intrinsic satisfaction rather than merely for income is one of the defining characteristics of the "new people" in Chernyshevsky's novel. It is a direct application of the theory of egotism to economic life: the highest self-interest is served by work that engages one's capacities and contributes to human welfare.

## Viéra's "Hobby"

Viéra Pavlovna's explanation to her seamstresses is the novel's most explicit statement of the principle. She describes the sewing cooperative not as a business but as a "hobby" — an activity pursued for its own sake: "My hobby happens to be this thing which I am trying to arrange with you, and I not only do not ruin myself for the sake of my hobby, but I do not even spend any money on it; I am only too glad to give up some of my time to it, and do not take any of the profit for myself" (045-011). The cooperative is not charity but the pursuit of a different kind of satisfaction — the pleasure of creating conditions where others can flourish.

## Lopukhóf's Choice

Lopukhóf embodies the same principle in his professional life. Rather than pursuing the most lucrative medical career, he chooses work that engages his intellectual interests. Viéra explains: "He does not spend his time now in doing those things which bring him in most money, but in those things which he likes best" (045-011). The choice is framed not as sacrifice but as rational preference — the satisfaction of interesting work outweighs the additional income of private practice.

## Beyond the Profit Motive

The principle of labor as vocation is the economic corollary of the theory of egotism. Just as rational egotism demonstrates that apparently selfless actions are the highest self-interest, the choice of vocation over profit demonstrates that the satisfaction of meaningful work exceeds the satisfaction of additional consumption. The novel thus anticipates a post-scarcity conception of work where economic activity is organized around human fulfillment rather than mere survival.
