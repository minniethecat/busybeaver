# Storeshnikof

Mikhaïl Ivanuitch Storeshnikof is the wealthy young man whom Marya Alekséyevna targets as a husband for Viéra. His function in the novel is to embody the marriage market that Viéra must escape — a system in which women are commodities and men are purchasers.

## The Rejected Suitor

Storeshnikof represents everything the "new people" reject: conventional social climbing, the reduction of marriage to financial transaction, and the assumption that wealth entitles a man to any woman he desires. His courtship of Viéra is orchestrated by Marya Alekséyevna as a business transaction, and his vapid character is the novel's demonstration that the old order's marriage system serves neither women's happiness nor human development. Viéra's refusal of his offer — and her choice of Lopukhóf instead — is the novel's first and most fundamental assertion of its values.
