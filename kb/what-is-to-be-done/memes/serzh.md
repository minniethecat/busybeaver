# Serzh

Serzh is a minor character — an officer and friend of Storeshnikof who participates in the young men's schemes regarding Viéra Pavlovna. He represents the old order's masculine culture of military camaraderie, casual predation, and the treatment of women as objects of competition and conquest.

## The Wager

Serzh appears in connection with Storeshnikof's pursuit of Viéra. The culture of officers' bets and dares that he represents is the masculine counterpart to Marya Alekséyevna's commodification of her daughter — both sides of the old order's reduction of marriage to transaction and women to property. His presence in the novel, though brief, establishes the social world against which the "new people" define themselves.
