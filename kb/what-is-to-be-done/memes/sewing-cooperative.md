# Sewing Cooperative

The sewing workshop founded by Viéra Pavlovna is the novel's practical demonstration of cooperative economic principles — a concrete experiment in organizing labor on the basis of profit-sharing, mutual education, and democratic self-management.

## Foundation

Viéra Pavlovna established the sewing union with deliberate simplicity. She began with just three seamstresses, paying them "a trifle more than the regular seamstresses were getting at the shops" (045-001). There were no formal rules at first — the foundation was trust: "Viéra Pavlovna was not a woman of mere words, not fickle; and therefore, without any hesitation they accepted her offer to work with her" (045-001). The initial modesty was strategic: Viéra understood that radical changes in economic relations could not be imposed but must grow from demonstrated success and earned confidence.

## The Cooperative Model

At the end of the first month, Viéra presented the seamstresses with a complete account of income and expenses, then divided the remaining profits equally among all workers. This was the moment of transformation — from employer-employee relations to cooperative ownership. Viéra explained her motivation with characteristic clarity: "I have established this sewing shop with the express purpose of letting the profits go to the very seamstresses by whose work it was earned" (045-008).

Her explanation of why she took no profit for herself introduced the novel's philosophy of work as vocation: "My hobby happens to be this thing which I am trying to arrange with you... I am only too glad to give up some of my time to it, and do not take any of the profit for myself" (045-011). The cooperative was not charity but the pursuit of a different kind of satisfaction — the pleasure of creating conditions where others could flourish.

## Evolution of Self-Governance

The cooperative's governance evolved organically over three years. Viéra's final act as "mistress" was to instruct the seamstresses to elect two representatives to share management duties: "It is your money, and not mine; therefore, you must look after it" (045-013). The women worked through several profit-sharing schemes before arriving at equal division — recognizing that "the profits was not the reward for the art of one or two of their number, but the result of the general character of the shop" (045-018). This progression from proportional to equal shares embodied the cooperative's deepening understanding of solidarity.

## Thematic Significance

The sewing cooperative is the economic counterpart to the novel's philosophical theory of egotism. Just as rational egotism demonstrates that apparently selfless actions are in fact the highest self-interest, the cooperative demonstrates that an economic order based on mutual benefit can be more productive — and more satisfying — than one based on exploitation. By the end of three years, the shop had grown to twenty workers, established a mutual bank, and proved that cooperative principles could succeed in practice.
