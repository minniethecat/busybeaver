# XX

### XX

[036-001]“It is now the twenty-eighth of April; he said that he should be through by the first of July. Let us say the tenth; but that is not the first. Well, we can take the tenth; or, so as to get nearer, I’ll suppose it’s the fifteenth. No, I’ll take the tenth, after all. Now, how many days are left? Today should not be counted; there are only five hours of it left. There are two days more in April; May, thirty-one, and two make thirty-three; June, thirty, and thirty-three makes sixty-three; in July ten days; altogether it makes seventy-three. Is that much? Only seventy-three days, and then—freedom! I shall get out of this cellar. *Akh!* how happy I am! My *[[dmitri-lopukhóf|mílenki]]*! how cleverly he thought it all out! How happy I am!”

[036-002]This was on Sunday evening. On Monday came a lesson given instead of Tuesday.

[036-003]“My dear, my beloved!16 how glad I am to be with you, if only for a minute! Do you know how many days there are left for me to be in this cellar? When will you be done? Will you be done by the tenth of July?”

[036-004]“Yes, [[viéra-pavlovna|Viérotchka]].”

[036-005]“Then I shall have to sit in this cellar only seventy-two days and this evening. One day I have marked off already. See I have made a little calendar just as boarding-school girls and boys do, and I cross off the days. How delightful it is to cross them off!”

[036-006]“My dear little Viérotchka, my dear!17 Indeed, you have not long to worry along here; two months and a half will quickly pass, and you will be free.”

[036-007]“*Akh!* how delightful it will be! Only just at present, my dearest,18 don’t always talk with me, and don’t look at me; and we must not play on the piano every time you come, either. And I shall not come out of my room every time that you come here; no, I shall not have enough strength of mind for that. I shall come out always, if only for one minute; and I shall look at you so coldly; not fondly at all. And now I am going right away to my room. Goodbye, my dear.19 When?”

[036-008]“Thursday.”

[036-009]“Three days; how long! But then there will be only sixty-eight days left.”

[036-010]“Count less; about the seventh you will be able to get away from here.”

[036-011]“The seventh? Then it is now only sixty-eight days. How happy you have made me! Goodbye, my dear.”

---

[036-012]**Thursday**.—“My dearest,20 there are only sixty-six days to stay here.”

[036-013]“Yes, Viérotchka; the time flies fast.”

[036-014]“Fast? No, my dear. *Akh!* how long the days seem! Sometimes it seemed to me as though a whole month had dragged along while these three days were passing. Goodbye, my dearest,21 we must not talk long; aren’t we shrewd? yes? Goodbye. *Akh!* only sixty-six remain for me to sit in the cellar.—Hm! hm! it is not so noticeable, of course; when one is at work, time flies. And then I am not in a cellar. Hm! hm! *da!*”

---

[036-015]**Saturday**.—“*Akh!* my dearest,22 only sixty-four days are left. *Akh!* how gloomy it is here! These two days have seemed longer than those three days. *Akh!* how gloomy! How miserable it is here; if you only realized it, my dear.23 Goodbye, my dear, my sweetheart,24 till Tuesday; and these three days will seem longer than the last five. Goodbye, my dear.—Hm! hm! *da!* hm! her eyes look badly. She does not like to weep. This is not well. Hm! *da!*”

---

[036-016]**Tuesday**.—“*Akh!* my dearest,25 I gave up counting the days. They don’t pass—they don’t pass at all.”

[036-017]“Viérotchka, my little friend, I have a favor to ask of you. We must have a nice little talk together. You are anxiously longing for freedom. Well, give yourself a little freedom; we must have a talk together.”

[036-018]“Yes, we must, *moï mílenki*, we must.”

[036-019]“Then I will ask you how this suits you. What time will it be most convenient for you tomorrow; it does not make the least difference what time, only tell me; be again on that bench of the Konno-Gvardéïsky Boulevard. Will you?”

[036-020]“I will be there, *moï mílenki*, without fail. At eleven o’clock; is that right?”

[036-021]“Very well; thank you, little friend.”

[036-022]“Goodbye, my dearest.26 *Akh!* how glad I am that you have thought about it! How was it that I, myself, foolish little thing that I am, did not think about it? Goodbye. We will talk; at all events, I shall breathe the fresh air. Goodbye, *mílenki*. At eleven o’clock, without fail.”

---

[036-023]**Friday**.—“Viérotchka, where are you going?”

[036-024]“I, *mámenka*?”

[036-025]Viérotchka blushed.

[036-026]“To the Nevsky Prospekt, *mámenka*.”

[036-027]“Then I am going with you, Viérotchka; I have an errand at the [[gostinui-dvor|Gostinui Dvor]]. What did you put on such a dress as that for, Viérotchka, when you say you are going to the Nevsky. You ought to put on a better one when you are going to the Nevsky; folks’ll see you.”

[036-028]“I like this dress. Just wait one second, *mámenka*; I want to get just one thing out of my room.”

[036-029]They start; they go. They reached the Gostinui Dvor. They were going along the block that runs parallel with Sadovaïa Street; they are not far from the Nevsky corner, and here is Ruzanof’s shop.

[036-030]“*Mámenka*, I have two words to tell you.”

[036-031]“What is the matter with you, Viérotchka?”

[036-032]“Goodbye, *mámenka*. I don’t know whether we shall meet again soon. If you don’t get angry, it’ll be tomorrow.”

[036-033]“What is it, Viérotchka? I cannot understand it, somehow?”

[036-034]“Goodbye, *mámenka*; I am going to my husband. Dmitri Sergéitch and I were married three days ago. Drive to Karavannaïa Street, *Izvoshchik*.”

[036-035]“A quarter, lady.”

[036-036]“All right; only be quick about it. He will call upon you this evening, *mámenka*; and don’t get angry with me, *mámenka*.”

[036-037]These words hardly reached [[marya-alekséyevna|Marya Alekséyevna]]’s ears.

[036-038]“Don’t drive to Karavannaïa Street; I only said so as to get away from that lady as quickly as I could. Go to the left27 down Nevsky. I must go much further than Karavannaïa Street, to the [[vasilyevsky-island|Vasilyevsky Island]], the fifth block behind the Middle Prospekt. Drive fast; I will give you a good fee.”

[036-039]“*Akh!* lady, you were pleased to fool me. You’ll have to give me half a ruble.”

[036-040]“If you drive fast.”
