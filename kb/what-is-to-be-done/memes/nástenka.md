# Nástenka

Nástenka (Nástenka Kryukova) is a young woman whose story Kirsánof tells Viéra Pavlovna as a cautionary tale. Her experience as a factory worker seduced and abandoned by the cynical Sólovtsof illustrates the vulnerability of women under the old order — and by contrast, the dignity that the "new people's" principles make possible.

## The Story

Kirsánof recounts Nástenka's story to Viéra during one of his visits. Sólovtsof, a man of superficial charm and genuine viciousness, seduced Nástenka, promised marriage, and then abandoned her when she became inconvenient. The story serves as a counterpoint to Viéra's own trajectory: where Viéra escaped the marriage market through philosophy and a fictitious marriage, Nástenka lacked the resources — intellectual, social, and economic — to protect herself. Kirsánof's care for Nástenka is presented as characteristic of his practice: "I do not cure; I only observe, and make experiments" (051-003) — yet he takes the most hopeless cases and restores them.
