# The Sewing Workshop

The sewing workshop is the physical site of Viéra Pavlovna's cooperative experiment — a space where the architecture of production is reorganized around principles of equality, education, and mutual aid.

## A Transformed Workplace

Unlike the conventional dressmaking establishments where seamstresses work in conditions of exploitation and degradation, Viéra's workshop is designed as a space of dignity. The workshop includes not only workstations but spaces for the communal meals, discussions, and educational activities that distinguish the cooperative from an ordinary business. The spatial arrangement — workers sitting together rather than isolated, accounts presented openly rather than hidden, decisions made collectively rather than imposed — embodies the cooperative's principles in physical form.

## Growth and Transformation

Over the three years of its development, the workshop expands from a modest space accommodating three seamstresses to a substantial establishment employing twenty workers with its own mutual bank. The physical growth of the workshop mirrors the growth of the cooperative's principles — from simple profit-sharing to full democratic self-governance.
