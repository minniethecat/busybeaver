# Katerina Vasílyevna

Katerina Vasílyevna Pólozova is a member of the "new people's" circle whose correspondence with Viéra Pavlovna provides one of the novel's most extended and explicit articulations of its philosophy of love, gratitude, and autonomy.

## The Correspondence

After Lopukhóf's disappearance, Katerina writes to Viéra expressing sympathy and sharing her understanding of Lopukhóf's character. Viéra's lengthy reply is one of the novel's philosophical set pieces — an analysis of why she was not to blame for the events that led to Lopukhóf's suicide, and why the very concept of "blame" is misplaced in relationships between autonomous equals. "I do not want to be dependent upon the good will of anyone, no matter who it may be" (075-006).

## Marriage to Beaumont

Katerina eventually marries Charles Beaumont — who is, in fact, Lopukhóf returned from America under a new identity. Her marriage thus completes the novel's pattern of happy resolutions achieved through the strategic manipulation of legal and social forms. She represents the extension of the "new people's" principles beyond the central triangle of Viéra, Lopukhóf, and Kirsánof.
