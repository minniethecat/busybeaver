# Fictitious Marriage

The concept of "fictitious marriage" in Chernyshevsky's novel refers to the strategic use of legal marriage as a formal mechanism to achieve substantive freedom. Lopukhóf and Viéra Pavlovna's marriage is a fiction in the sense that it is entered into not for the conventional reasons of love, property, or social status, but as a legal instrument to liberate Viéra from her parents' household.

## The Marriage as Instrument

Lopukhóf proposes marriage to Viéra not as a romantic union but as a practical solution to her imprisonment in her family's "cellar." The marriage is "fictitious" in that it uses the legal form of the old order to achieve the substantive goals of the new — individual autonomy and freedom of development. The form is hollowed out and repurposed: the legal bond exists to neutralize patriarchal authority, not to create a new form of dependency.

## The Architecture of Independence

The Lopukhófs' domestic arrangement is carefully designed to preserve autonomy within the legal framework of marriage. They occupy separate rooms connected by a "neutral room" — a spatial metaphor for their relationship as equal partners rather than fused identities. Viéra explicitly refuses the conventional language of gratitude and obligation: "I do not even feel that I owe him any gratefulness" (075-004). The marriage succeeds precisely because it makes no emotional demands beyond those freely given.

## Dissolution Without Tragedy

The novel's most radical claim about marriage is that it should be dissolvable without moral catastrophe. When Viéra's feelings shift to Kirsánof, the framework of fictitious marriage enables all three characters to navigate the transition without the conventional melodrama of betrayal and guilt. Lopukhóf's fake suicide — itself a fiction within the fiction — is the mechanism that dissolves the legal bond while preserving everyone's dignity. The novel thus argues that the value of a marriage lies in the real relations it enables, not in its legal or social permanence.
