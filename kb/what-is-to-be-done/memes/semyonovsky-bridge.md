# Semyonovsky Bridge

The Semyonovsky Bridge appears at several crucial moments in the novel as a liminal space — a threshold between worlds, associated with transitions, decisions, and the crossing of boundaries.

## A Geography of Transition

Bridges in Chernyshevsky's novel are charged with symbolic significance: they mark the passage from one condition to another. The Semyonovsky Bridge, in particular, appears at moments when characters are poised between old and new — between the Petersburg of Gorokhovaïa Street and the Petersburg of Vasilyevsky Island, between the life they have known and the life they are choosing.

## Narrative Appearances

The bridge appears in connection with Lopukhóf's movements through the city — the medical student crossing between the world of his studies and the world of the Rozálsky household, between his scientific work and his mission of liberation. As a physical feature of the Petersburg landscape, the bridge anchors the novel's geography in the actual city while investing that geography with symbolic meaning.
