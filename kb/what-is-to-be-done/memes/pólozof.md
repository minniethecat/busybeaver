# Pólozof

Pólozof is a wealthy government contractor whose fortune represents the economic system that the "new people" seek to replace. As the father of Katerina Vasílyevna, he stands in relation to his daughter as Marya Alekséyevna stands to Viéra — though his wealth gives him different instruments of control.

## The Old Order's Economy

Pólozof's wealth is derived from government contracts — the intersection of state power and private profit that characterized the Russian economy of Chernyshevsky's time. His factory is part of the system of exploitation that the sewing cooperative is designed to transcend. The novel presents him not as personally villainous but as a structural figure — his function in the narrative is to represent the economic order that the "new people's" cooperative principles challenge.
