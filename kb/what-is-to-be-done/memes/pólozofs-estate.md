# Pólozof's Estate

Pólozof's country estate represents the old order's economic geography — wealth concentrated in land, sustained by government contracts, and transmitted through inheritance rather than earned through productive labor.

## The Old Order's Wealth

The estate is the material embodiment of the economic system the "new people" challenge. Pólozof's fortune derives from government contracts — the intersection of state power and private profit that characterized the Russian economy. His estate, like his factory, represents an economic order based on extraction and hierarchy, in contrast to the cooperative's economy based on shared labor and mutual benefit.

## A World Left Behind

Katerina Vasílyevna's marriage to Beaumont/Lopukhóf represents her departure from the estate's world — not through rejection of her father but through the quiet establishment of an alternative. The estate remains in the novel's background as a reminder of what the "new people" have transcended.
