# New York

New York — and America more broadly — represents in the novel the possibility of transformation through emigration. It is the place where Lopukhóf becomes Charles Beaumont.

## The New World

Lopukhóf's years in America are narrated only in retrospect, through the figure of Beaumont. The New World functions as a blank space in the novel's geography — a place where identities can be remade, where the constraints of Russian society do not apply. America is not described in detail because its narrative function is precisely its otherness: it is the not-Russia where transformation is possible without the elaborate fictions (suicide, letters, Rakhmétof's intervention) that the same transformation required in Petersburg.
