# Sadovïa Street

Sadovïa Street (Garden Street) is one of the St. Petersburg streets that appear in the novel's detailed urban geography. Like Gorokhovaïa Street, it anchors the narrative in the actual topography of the imperial capital.

## The Street in the City

Sadovïa Street forms part of the network of streets, canals, and bridges that the "new people" traverse in their movements between the old order's Petersburg and their own emerging spaces. The novel's attention to specific streets and addresses is itself a philosophical gesture — an insistence that the transformation it describes is not utopian fantasy but practical possibility, located in the actual geography of an actual city.
