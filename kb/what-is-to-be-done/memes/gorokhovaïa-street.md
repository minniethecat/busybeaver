# Gorokhovaïa Street

Gorokhovaïa Street is the address of the Rozálsky household and the symbolic center of the old order in Chernyshevsky's novel. It is the geography of the "cellar" — the world Viéra Pavlovna must leave behind.

## The Rozálsky Household

The Rozálsky apartment on Gorokhovaïa Street is the novel's primary image of the old order's domestic space. Marya Alekséyevna rules this territory with the logic of the market: her daughter is an asset, her husband is irrelevant, and every social interaction is a transaction. The apartment's rooms — the parlor where Storeshnikof is entertained, the corner where Viéra reads forbidden books, the spaces where Lopukhóf's philosophical conversations take place under Marya Alekséyevna's eavesdropping — map the conflict between old and new within a single domestic geography.

## The Street as Symbol

Gorokhovaïa Street represents the world Viéra must transcend. Her First Dream's "damp, gloomy cellar" (028-003) is the psychological correlative of the Gorokhovaïa apartment — a space of confinement from which liberation is possible only through the intervention of forces from outside. Lopukhóf's arrival at the Rozálsky household is the arrival of the outside world into the cellar.
