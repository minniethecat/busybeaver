# Pável Konstantinuitch

Pável Konstantinuitch Rozálsky is Viéra Pavlovna's father — a minor government official whose passivity in the face of his wife Marya Alekséyevna's domineering management of the household makes him complicit in the "cellar" from which Viéra must escape.

## The Absent Father

Pável Konstantinuitch is notable primarily for his absence as a moral force in the household. While Marya Alekséyevna actively schemes to commodify their daughter, Pável Konstantinuitch drifts in the background — neither opposing his wife's plans nor endorsing them, simply failing to intervene. His passivity is presented not as neutral but as a form of complicity: by doing nothing, he enables everything. In the novel's philosophical terms, he has failed to calculate his own interest — or has calculated that comfort outweighs conscience.
