# Gostinui Dvor

Gostinui Dvor, the great merchants' arcade of St. Petersburg, appears in the novel as a site of commerce and social exchange — the economic geography of the old order that the "new people" must navigate.

## The Marketplace

The arcade represents the commercial world that Marya Alekséyevna understands and manipulates — a world where everything, including daughters, has a price. For the "new people," Gostinui Dvor is the geography of necessity: the place where materials for the sewing cooperative are purchased, where the products of labor are sold, where the economic transactions that sustain life take place. The novel's treatment of commerce is neither romantic nor dismissive — it acknowledges that even a transformed society must engage with markets while transforming the relations of production that markets encode.
