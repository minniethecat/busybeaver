# Yo Fei

**Yo Fei** ({{zh|岳飛}}, 1103–1142) was a Chinese military general of the Southern [[Sung dynasty]], celebrated in Chinese history as a paragon of loyalty and patriotism. He led a series of successful campaigns against the Jurchen [[Jin dynasty]] invaders before being recalled, imprisoned, and executed under a peace treaty engineered by the court faction led by Ch'in K'ui.

## Military Campaigns

Yo Fei rose to prominence during the Jurchen invasion that pushed the Song court south of the [[yangtze-river|Yangtze River]] in 1127. Commanding a disciplined volunteer army known as the "Yo Family Army", he won a string of victories against Jin forces and recovered significant territory in the central plains.

His military success made him the most prominent figure in the war party, which advocated continued resistance against the Jurchen rather than accepting a humiliating peace.

## Downfall and Execution

In 1141, the peace faction at the Song court, led by the chancellor Ch'in K'ui, negotiated a treaty with the Jurchen that required the Song to accept vassal status. Yo Fei's continued military operations threatened this diplomatic settlement.

> The military party, General Yo Fei, in 1141, was able to conclude peace with the Jurchen. [045-105]

Yo Fei was recalled to the capital, imprisoned, and executed on fabricated charges of treason. His death became one of the most famous miscarriages of justice in Chinese history.

## Posthumous Rehabilitation

Yo Fei's reputation was rehabilitated decades after his death. Ch'in K'ui was posthumously condemned as a traitor, while Yo Fei was elevated to the status of a national hero and symbol of loyal service.

> Later generations regarded Ch'in K'ui as a traitor and Yo Fei as a national hero. [045-113]

## Legacy

Yo Fei remains one of the most revered figures in Chinese popular culture. Temples dedicated to his memory can be found across China, and his story — of a loyal general betrayed by a corrupt court — has been retold in countless operas, novels, and films. His mother's legendary tattoo of the characters "serve the country with utmost loyalty" (精忠報國) on his back has become a cultural touchstone for patriotic devotion.
