## Geography

The Coral Sea is a marginal sea of the southwestern [[pacific-ocean|Pacific Ocean]], lying off the northeast coast of [[australia|Australia]]. It is bounded by [[bowen|Queensland]] to the west, [[new-guinea|Papua]] [[new-guinea|New Guinea]] and the Solomon Islands to the north, and New Caledonia and Vanuatu to the east. The sea is named for its extensive coral formations, including the [[great-barrier-reef|Great Barrier Reef]], which make it one of the most hazardous bodies of water for navigation. (015-028)

The Coral Sea connects to the [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]] at its northwestern edge, forming a passage between [[australia|Australia]] and [[new-guinea|New Guinea]]. Together these waters present what [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] called "nearly all the dangers of the Coral Sea and [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]], which, indeed, were not a few." (015-028)

## Role in the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] traversed the Coral Sea during the [[australia|Australia]]n leg of his [[voyage|circumnavigation]]. While at [[cooktown|Cooktown]], the owner of a clipper yacht challenged [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] to race to [[thursday-island|Thursday Island]]. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] declined, citing the unfairness of "three young yachtsmen in a clipper against an old sailor all alone in a craft of coarse build." He added: "besides that, I would not on any account race in the Coral Sea." (014-021)

After passing through the [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] considered the Coral Sea's hazards behind him. From [[thursday-island|Thursday Island]] onward, "all ahead from this point was plain [[sail|sailing]] and a straight course" down the [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]], with the trade wind blowing fresh toward Madagascar. (015-028)

> The owner, whom I had met before he sailed, wanted to race the poor old *Spray* to Thursday Island en route. I declined the challenge, naturally, on the ground of the unfairness of three young yachtsmen in a clipper against an old sailor all alone in a craft of coarse build; besides that, I would not on any account race in the Coral Sea.

## Historical Context

In the late 19th century, the Coral Sea was well known to mariners for its complex [[reefing|reef]]s and unpredictable conditions. The route through the [[great-barrier-reef|Great Barrier Reef]] and [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]] required careful navigation. For a small vessel like [[spray|the Spray]] under single-handed command, the dangers were magnified. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s successful transit demonstrated both his seamanship and [[spray|the Spray]]'s capabilities in confined, [[reefing|reef]]-strewn waters. (014-021)
