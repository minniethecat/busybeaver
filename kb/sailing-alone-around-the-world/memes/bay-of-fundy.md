# Bay of Fundy

The Bay of Fundy, between [[nova-scotia|Nova Scotia]] and New Brunswick, is famous for the highest tidal range on Earth — over 50 feet in its upper reaches. Ferocious [[tide|tide-race]]s form where the immense flow of water is constricted through narrow passages.

For [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], a [[nova-scotia|Nova Scotia]] native born on North Mountain overlooking the bay, these were home waters from his earliest days.¹ (001-002)

## Geography

The bay stretches about 170 miles from its mouth at the Gulf of Maine to its head, where it splits into Chignecto Bay and the Minas Basin. The extreme tides are a result of resonance — the natural oscillation period of the bay matches [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] tidal cycle, amplifying the range. Tidal currents can reach 8 [[knot|knots]] in narrow passages, creating violent [[tide|tide-race]]s.

To the east, the bay is overlooked by North Mountain, "overlooking the Bay of Fundy on one side and the fertile Annapolis valley on the other."² (001-002) This ridge was [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s birthplace — "I was born in a cold spot, on coldest North Mountain, on a cold February 20."² (001-002)

## In the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] navigated the Bay of Fundy early in his voyage — a deliberate choice, testing the *[[spray|Spray]]* in waters he knew to be among the most challenging on [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] coast. After departing [[gloucester|Gloucester]] on May 7 and making his way east along the Maine coast, he shaped a course for [[briars-island|Briar's Island]].³

Approaching the island on the western fishing-grounds, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] spoke to a fisherman at anchor — who gave him a wrong course. The [[spray|Spray]] then "sailed directly over the southwest ledge through the worst [[tide|tide-race]] in the Bay of Fundy, and got into [[westport|Westport harbor]] in [[nova-scotia|Nova Scotia]]."⁴ (002-013)

The [[spray|Spray]] survived without damage. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] later "tried her seams, but found that even the test of the sou'west rip had started nothing."⁵ (002-016)

> The *Spray*, heading east, stretched along the coast among many islands and over a tranquil sea. At evening of this day, May 10, she came up with a considerable island, which I shall always think of as the Island of Frogs, for the *Spray* was charmed by a million voices. From the Island of Frogs we made for the Island of Birds, called Gannet Island, and sometimes Gannet Rock, whereon is a bright, intermittent light, which flashed fitfully across the *Spray*’s deck as she coasted along under its light and shade. Thence shaping a course for Briar’s Island, I came among vessels the following afternoon on the western fishing-grounds, and after speaking a fisherman at anchor, who gave me a wrong course, the *Spray* sailed directly over the southwest ledge through the worst tide-race in the Bay of Fundy, and got into Westport harbor in Nova Scotia, where I had spent eight years of my life as a lad.
>
> (002-013)

## Westport and Briar's Island

[[westport|Westport]] was a deeply personal landfall. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had "spent eight years of my life as a lad" there, and he was "delighted to reach [[westport|Westport]]."⁴ He wrote: "The very stones on [[briars-island|Briar's Island]] I was glad to see again, and I knew them all."⁶ He found old schoolmates, visited the familiar little shop "round the corner, which for thirty-five years I had not seen," and mused on boyhood memories.⁶

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] noted the character of [[briars-island|Briar Island]]ers with pride: "It is known that a [[briars-island|Briar Island]]er, fish or no fish on his hook, never flinches from a sea."⁷ This resilience was the same quality he would draw upon across three oceans.

## Significance

The Bay of Fundy represents [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s origins — the cold, tidal waters of the Canadian Maritimes that shaped his seamanship. The [[spray|Spray]]'s passage through "the worst [[tide|tide-race]] in the Bay of Fundy" was her first serious test, and she passed it.⁴

As [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] later recalled, a fruitcake his sister made at the bay, "at the first of the voyage, kept about the same length of time, namely, forty-two days."⁸ (019-006) The bay was both the physical and emotional starting point of the [[voyage|circumnavigation]].
