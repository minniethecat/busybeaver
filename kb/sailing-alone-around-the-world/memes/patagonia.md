# Patagonia

Patagonia is the vast, windswept region encompassing the southern end of South America, shared by Argentina and [[chile|Chile]]. The *[[spray|Spray]]* sailed along the Patagonian coast during its passage from the Río de la Plata to [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]]. For [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], Patagonian waters were both the proving ground of his vessel's seaworthiness and the scene of one of the voyage's most harrowing moments.

## Geography

Patagonia stretches from the Río Colorado in the north to [[tierra-del-fuego|Tierra del Fuego]] in the south, bordered by [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] Ocean to the east and the Andes and [[chile|Chile]]an fjords to the west. It is a land of arid steppes, rugged mountains, and a notoriously [[gale|storm]]y coastline. The Patagonian coast is known among mariners for its destructive [[tide|tide-race]]s and shallow banks that extend many miles offshore, requiring vessels to give the land a wide berth (007-005).

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] passed safely by [[bahia|Bahia Blanca]], the Gulf of St. Matias, and "the mighty Gulf of St. George" while giving "all the capes a berth of about fifty miles, for these dangers extend many miles from the land" (007-005).

## Role in the Voyage

### Preparation for Patagonian Waters

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] prepared the *[[spray|Spray]]* for Patagonian waters while at [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio de Janeiro]], adding a yawl [[rigging|rig]] specifically "for the [[storm|tempest]]uous waters of Patagonia" by installing a semicircular brace on the stern to support a jigger mast (005-030). Veteran ship captains who inspected the *[[spray|Spray]]* at [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio]] contributed to her outfit, including an anchor and cable that would serve well in Patagonian [[gale|storm]]s.

### The Great Wave

While [[sail|sailing]] well off the Patagonian coast, the *[[spray|Spray]]* encountered one of the most dangerous moments of the entire voyage. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s own account captures the terror of the moment:

> "For, one day, well off the Patagonian coast, while the sloop was running under a reefed mainsail during a heavy gale of wind, a tremendous wave, the culmination, it seemed, of many waves, rolled down upon her in overwhelming darkness." (007-005)

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had only a moment to react: "I got all sail down and myself up on the peak halliards, out of danger, before the mighty crest towering [[mast|masthead]]-high had completely smothered the vessel" (007-005). From his precarious perch in the [[rigging|rig]]ging, he looked down and saw no part of the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s hull — the vessel was entirely submerged.

Yet the *[[spray|Spray]]* survived: "She shook in every timber and broke the force of the sea with her body, and rose quickly out of it, and rode grandly over the rollers that followed" (007-005). Paradoxically, this terrifying incident "reassured me against rude [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]]" — if the *[[spray|Spray]]* could survive a Patagonian wave that submerged her completely, she could survive anything (007-005).

### Aftermath and Mirage

After the great wave, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] noted a dramatic change in conditions: "From the time the great wave swept over [[spray|the Spray]] until she reached [[cape-virgins|Cape Virgins]] nothing occurred to move a pulse and [[current|set]] blood in motion. On the contrary, the weather became fine and the sea smooth and life tranquil" (007-006). The phenomenon of mirage frequently appeared on the Patagonian seas — "An albatross sitting on the water one day looked as large as a ship" (007-006).

### The Patagonian People

At [[sandy-point|Sandy Point]] ([[sandy-point|Punta Arenas]]) — "a [[chile|Chile]]an coaling-station" with about two thousand inhabitants of mixed nationality but mostly [[chile|Chile]]ans — [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] observed the native Patagonian and [[fuegians|Fuegian people]] (007-011). He described them as "fine specimens of the Patagonian race," but his observations carried a dark edge:

> "The natives, Patagonian and Fuegian, on the other hand, were, it was understood, the proprietors of all that part of the country, and the white people had no business there at all." (007-011)

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] witnessed the corrosive effect of colonial exploitation on the native population. He described how unscrupulous traders used "firewater" to exploit them — the natives looking "smart in the morning when they came into town" but reduced to being "beastly drunk" by night (007-011).

## Approach to the Strait

On February 11, 1896, the *[[spray|Spray]]* rounded [[cape-virgins|Cape Virgins]] and entered [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]]. "The scene was again real and gloomy; the wind, northeast, and blowing a gale, sent feather-white spume along the coast; such a sea ran as would swamp an ill-appointed ship" (007-007). After the gale subsided, a "smart breeze" carried the *[[spray|Spray]]* through the narrows, and she cast anchor at [[sandy-point|Sandy Point]] on February 14, 1896 (007-009).
