**Devonport** is a port city on the River Mersey on the north coast of [[australia|Tasmania]], which [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]] visited during the [[australia|Australia]]n leg of his solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]]. At the time of his arrival, it was rapidly becoming the most important port in [[tasmania|Tasmania]].

## Slocum's Visit

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sailed to Devonport from a smaller anchorage along the [[tasmania|Tasmania]]n coast. He found it "a thriving place on the river Mersey, a few hours' sail westward along the coast, and fast becoming the most important port in [[tasmania|Tasmania]]" (014-009). Large steamers already frequented the harbor, carrying away substantial cargoes of farm produce.

The *[[spray|Spray]]* was the first vessel to bring the American flag to the port. The harbormaster, Captain Murray, told [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] as much, and the fact was duly recorded in the port records. "For the great distinction the *[[spray|Spray]]* enjoyed many civilities while she rode comfortably at anchor in her port-duster awning that covered her from stem to stern" (014-009).

## Inspection of the Spray

The stay at Devonport included a thorough examination of [[sloop|the sloop]]. The *[[spray|Spray]]* was "hauled out on the marine railway at Devonport and examined carefully top and bottom, but was found absolutely free from the destructive teredo, and sound in all respects" (014-013).

To protect her for the tropical waters ahead, the bottom was "coated once more with copper paint, for she would have to sail through the Coral and [[arafura-sea|Arafura]] seas before refitting again" (014-013). The inspection was a reassuring moment: the little sloop, built of timber [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had felled himself, was proving remarkably resilient against shipworm, the bane of wooden vessels in warm waters.

## Departure

Despite the warm reception, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] felt the weight of leaving [[tasmania|Tasmania]]. He later wrote: "If there was a moment in my voyage when I could have given it up, it was there and then; but no vacancies for a better post being open, I weighed anchor April 16, 1897, and again put to sea" (014-013).

> From this snug little place I made sail for Devonport, a thriving place on the river Mersey, a few hours' sail westward along the coast, and fast becoming the most important port in Tasmania. Large steamers enter there now and carry away great cargoes of farm produce, but the *Spray* was the first vessel to bring the Stars and Stripes to the port, the harbormaster, Captain Murray, told me, and so it is written in the port records.
>
> (014-013)
