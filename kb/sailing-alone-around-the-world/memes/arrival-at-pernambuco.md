# Arrival at Pernambuco

On October 5, 1895, after a passage of forty days from [[Gibraltar]], [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]] made landfall at [[pernambuco|Pernambuco]] — modern [[pernambuco|Recife]] — on the coast of [[brazil|Brazil]].

The arrival was a significant miles[[tonnage|ton]]e: it was the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s first landfall in the [[united-states|America]]s since departing [[Boston]] five months earlier, and it brought [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] to waters he knew well from his years as a merchant captain.

The landfall also vindicated his decision, made in the wake of the [[pirate-chase-off-gibraltar|pirate chase off Gibraltar]], to sail westward across [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] rather than eastward through the [[suez|Suez Canal]]. As [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] wrote: "My determination to sail westward from [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]] not only enabled me to escape the pirates of the [[red-sea|Red Sea]], but, in bringing me to [[pernambuco|Pernambuco]], landed me on familiar shores" (005-024).

## Summary

The *[[spray|Spray]]* departed [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]] on August 25, 1895, and sailed southwest across [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]], crossing the equator on September 30. On October 5, just north of Olinda Point, she made the [[brazil|Brazilian coast]]. The passage had been largely uneventful — a contrast to the dramatic [[pirate-chase-off-gibraltar|pirate chase]] that had preceded it — and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] arrived in good spirits. At [[pernambuco|Pernambuco]], he shortened the damaged boom (broken during the escape from the pirates), refitted the jaws, and enjoyed "abundant good cheer" (005-029). He sailed for [[Rio-de-Janeiro]] on October 24, having spent nearly three weeks in the [[brazil|Brazil]]ian port.

## Context

[[pernambuco|Pernambuco]] — known today as [[pernambuco|Recife]] — was one of [[brazil|Brazil]]'s oldest and most important ports. Founded by the Portuguese in the sixteenth century, it had been a center of the sugar trade and, by the 1890s, was a major commercial hub. For [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], who had made many voyages to [[brazil|Brazil]] as a merchant captain, [[pernambuco|Pernambuco]] was familiar territory. He had been in command of the *[[aquidneck|Aquidneck]]* when she called at [[brazil|Brazil]]ian ports, and in 1893 he had delivered the torpedo-boat *[[destroyer|Destroyer]]* from [[new-york|New York]] to [[brazil|Brazil]] during the naval revolt against the government.

The decision to sail westward from [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]] was made in the immediate aftermath of the [[pirate-chase-off-gibraltar|pirate chase]]. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had initially planned to pass through the [[suez|Suez Canal]] and across the [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]] — a shorter route in miles but one that would have taken him through the pirate-infested [[red-sea|Red Sea]]. The chase on August 25 convinced him that a single-handed sailor in a slow sloop was too vulnerable to human predators. He chose instead to cross [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] and round [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]] — a route that was physically far more dangerous but involved no threat from pirates.

## Narrative

### The Atlantic Crossing

After escaping the Moorish pirates, the *[[spray|Spray]]* [[current|set]]tled into the rhythm of the open sea. The wind held steady, the sea was manageable, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] — alone in his cabin — could "hardly realize that any sea was running at all, so easy was the long, swinging motion of [[sloop|the sloop]] over the waves" (005-005). The passage was marked by small incidents: a swordfish that swam alongside, flying fish that landed [[deck|on deck]], and the daily routines of navigation and sail-trimming that gave structure to the solitary days.

The *[[spray|Spray]]* crossed the equator on September 30, 1895, in longitude 29° 30′ W:

> "September 30, at half-past eleven in the morning, the Spray crossed the equator in longitude 29° 30′ W. At noon she was two miles south of the line. The southeast trade-winds, met, rather light, in about 4° N., gave her sails now a stiff full sending her handsomely over the sea toward the coast of Brazil, where on October 5, just north of Olinda Point, without further incident, she made the land." — (005-023)

"Without further incident" — the phrase is characteristic of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s understated style. A forty-day Atlantic passage in a thirty-six-foot sloop, single-handed, was by any measure an extraordinary feat. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] describes it as though it were a routine coastal passage.

### Landfall

The coast of [[brazil|Brazil]], when it rose from the sea on October 5, was a sight of profound significance:

> "It was not at all strange in a life common to sailors that, having already crossed the Atlantic twice and being now halfway from Boston to the Horn, I should find myself still among friends. My determination to sail westward from Gibraltar not only enabled me to escape the pirates of the Red Sea, but, in bringing me to Pernambuco, landed me on familiar shores. I had made many voyages to this and other ports in Brazil." — (005-024)

The word "friends" is telling. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], alone on the sea for forty days, was now among people who knew him — or at least knew of him — from his previous career. The [[brazil|Brazilian coast]] was not a foreign shore but a familiar one, populated by fellow mariners and old acquaintances. In a voyage that would increasingly take him to remote and unfamiliar places, [[pernambuco|Pernambuco]] was a reminder of the world [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had come from.

### Repairs at Pernambuco

The stay at [[pernambuco|Pernambuco]] was productive. The *[[spray|Spray]]*'s boom, broken during the escape from the Moorish pirates off the coast of [[morocco|Morocco]], had been a persistent nuisance. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] now shortened it:

> "While at Pernambuco I shortened the boom, which had been broken when off the coast of Morocco, by removing the broken piece, which took about four feet off the inboard end; I also refitted the jaws. On October 24, 1895, a fine day even as days go in Brazil, the Spray sailed, having had abundant good cheer." — (005-029)

The shortening of the boom was more significant than it sounds. The boom is the spar at the foot of the mainsail, and its length affects the sail's shape and the vessel's balance. Removing four feet from the inboard end meant the mainsail would [[current|set]] differently for the remainder of the voyage — a permanent modification forced by the [[pirate-chase-off-gibraltar|pirate chase]] that had reshaped the entire [[voyage|circumnavigation]].

## Aftermath

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] departed [[pernambuco|Pernambuco]] on October 24 and made his way south along the [[brazil|Brazilian coast]], reaching [[Rio-de-Janeiro]] on November 5. The [[brazil|Brazil]]ian interlude — [[pernambuco|Pernambuco]] to [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio]] to [[Montevideo]] — was the last period of relatively easy [[sail|sailing]] before the *[[spray|Spray]]* entered the [[southern-ocean|Southern Ocean]] and the dangers of [[strait-of-magellan|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]].

The Pernambuco landfall also had a symbolic dimension. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had now crossed [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] from east to west — the route of [[christopher-columbus|Columbus]], of the early explorers, of the great [[sail|sailing]] ships that had connected Europe and the [[united-states|America]]s for four centuries. He had done it alone, in a small sloop, and he had done it as a deliberate alternative to the safer, shorter route through [[mediterranean-sea|the Mediterranean]]. The choice to sail westward, made in the panic of the [[pirate-chase-off-gibraltar|pirate chase]], had been vindicated.

## Significance

The arrival at [[pernambuco|Pernambuco]] marks the completion of the first major leg of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s [[voyage|circumnavigation]]. He had sailed from [[boston|Boston]] to [[gloucester|Gloucester]] to [[nova-scotia|Nova Scotia]] to the [[azores|Azores]] to [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]] — and then, in a single bold stroke, across the entire Atlantic to [[brazil|Brazil]]. He was now in the Southern Hemisphere, on the coast of [[south-america|South America]], with [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]] and [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]] still ahead.

The episode is also significant for what it reveals about [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s relationship to his past. In [[pernambuco|Pernambuco]] he was no longer simply "the man who sailed alone [[voyage|around the world]]" — that story was still being written. He was [[joshua-slocum|Captain Slocum]] of the *[[northern-light|Northern Light]]*, of the *[[aquidneck|Aquidneck]]*, of the *[[destroyer|Destroyer]]* — a known and respected figure in the maritime community of the South Atlantic. The voyage that would make him famous was built on the foundation of a career that had already earned him respect.
