# Victoria

The *Victoria* was the only one of [[ferdinand-magellan|Ferdinand Magellan]]'s five ships to complete the first [[voyage|circumnavigation]] of the globe (1519–1522). Of the approximately 270 men who [[current|set]] out, only 18 returned in the *Victoria* under Juan Sebastián Elcano. She was, in every sense, the original — the vessel that proved the world could be circled by sea. For [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], the solo inheritor of that tradition, the *Victoria* was the distant ancestor of the *[[spray|Spray]]*.

## The Voyage

[[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] departed Spain in 1519 with five ships: *Trinidad*, *San Antonio*, *Concepción*, *Victoria*, and *Santiago*. By the time the fleet entered the strait that would bear [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]]'s name, one ship had been wrecked and another had deserted. [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] was killed in the [[philippines|Philippines]] in 1521. The *Victoria*, under Elcano, sailed on alone — across the [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]], around [[cape-of-good-hope|the Cape]] of Good Hope, and back to Spain in September 1522.

## Legacy

The *Victoria* established the [[voyage|circumnavigation]] tradition. Every vessel that has circled the globe since — including [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s *[[spray|Spray]]* — [[sail|sails]] in her wake. The progress from the *Victoria*'s 85 [[tonnage|tons]] and crew of 42 (on departure) to the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s 12 [[tonnage|tons]] and crew of one is the story of maritime exploration in miniature: from empires to individuals, from great expeditions to solitary voyages.
