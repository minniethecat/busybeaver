The **Loss of the Aquidneck** was the shipwreck that ended [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]]'s career as a merchant shipmaster and indirectly led to his solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]] of the world. After commanding the *[[aquidneck|Aquidneck]]*, a bark he considered "the nearest to perfection of beauty," for several years, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was forced to abandon her when she was wrecked on the coast of Brazil. He and his family returned home in the canoe *[[liberdade|Liberdade]]*, a vessel he built himself from the wreckage (001-004).

## Summary

Sometime around 1886, after nearly twenty years as a shipmaster, [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]]'s bark *[[aquidneck|Aquidneck]]* was wrecked on the coast of Brazil. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] "quit her deck" at the scene of the wreck and, rather than take passage on another vessel, built a canoe — the *[[liberdade|Liberdade]]* — from salvaged materials and sailed home to New York with his family (001-004). The wreck marked the end of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s conventional maritime career; he would not command another large vessel again.

## Context

The *[[aquidneck|Aquidneck]]* was the vessel [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] commanded after the *[[northern-light|Northern Light]]*, his most prestigious post. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] described the *[[aquidneck|Aquidneck]]* with deep affection:

> "I owned and sailed the Aquidneck, a little bark which of all man's handiwork seemed to me the nearest to perfection of beauty, and which in speed, when the wind blew, asked no favors of steamers." — (001-004)

By the time of the wreck, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had accumulated nearly twenty years of experience as a shipmaster, [[sail|sailing]] principally to China, [[australia|Australia]], Japan, and among the Spice Islands as a [[cargo|freight]]er and trader (001-005). The loss of the vessel represented not just a financial blow but the end of an era — the age of American sail was fading, and finding another command would prove difficult.

## Narrative

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] provides few details of the wreck itself in *[[sail|Sail]]ing Alone Around the World*, treating it as a turning point rather than a story to be told in full:

> "I had been nearly twenty years a shipmaster when I quit her deck on the coast of Brazil, where she was wrecked. My home voyage to New York with my family was made in the canoe Liberdade, without accident." — (001-004)

The *[[liberdade|Liberdade]]* voyage was itself a remarkable achievement — [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] built the canoe from the wreckage or with materials at hand, and sailed it with his wife and children from Brazil to New York, a journey of over 5,000 miles. This experience of small-boat ocean voyaging foreshadowed the *[[spray|Spray]]* voyage, demonstrating that a small, well-handled vessel could safely cross oceans.

A later reference in Chapter 6 confirms that [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had called at [[maldonado|Maldonado]] ([[uruguay|Uruguay]]) during his *[[aquidneck|Aquidneck]]* days, this time "with a stricken crew" — suggesting that the bark's final voyage was already troubled before the wreck on the Brazilian coast (006-008).

## Aftermath

After the wreck, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] found himself ashore in [[boston|Boston]], "cast up from old ocean," uncertain whether to seek another command or work in a shipyard (001-006). The maritime economy had shifted: tall vessels were being cut down for coal-barges, and experienced captains competed for fewer and fewer commands. It was at this low point that [[eben-pierce|Captain Eben Pierce]] offered him the *[[spray|Spray]]* — an antiquated sloop that [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] would rebuild and eventually sail [[voyage|around the world]] (001-007).

Thus, paradoxically, the loss of the *[[aquidneck|Aquidneck]]* was the necessary precursor to [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s greatest achievement. Without the wreck, he might have continued as a merchant captain and never undertaken the voyage that made him famous.

## Significance

The loss of the *[[aquidneck|Aquidneck]]* is a hinge event in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s life — the end of his conventional maritime career and the beginning of the path that led to the *[[spray|Spray]]* and the first solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]]. It also demonstrates a pattern in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s life: his response to disaster was not defeat but creative reinvention. He built the *[[liberdade|Liberdade]]* from the wreck of the *[[aquidneck|Aquidneck]]*, and he rebuilt the *[[spray|Spray]]* from a derelict sloop. Both responses reveal the shipwright's skills and self-reliant spirit that would carry him [[voyage|around the world]].
