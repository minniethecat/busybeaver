# Melbourne

Melbourne was the first [[australia|Australia]]n port [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] reached after crossing the [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]] — two months of solitary [[sail|sailing]] from the [[keeling-islands|Keeling Islands]]. It was a city of welcomes and minor vexations: a shark that proved unexpectedly valuable, a customs charge he thought unfair, and the Bishop of Melbourne whose teachings he warmly endorsed.

## Geography

Melbourne lies at the head of Port Phillip Bay, a large sheltered harbor connected to [[bass-strait|Bass Strait]] by a narrow entrance known as the Heads. In the 1890s, Melbourne was the largest and wealthiest city in [[australia|Australia]], fueled by the gold rush of the 1850s and the pastoral wealth of [[victoria|Victoria]]. Its streets were broad, its buildings grand, and its harbor busy with shipping from [[voyage|around the world]].

## In the Voyage

After departing [[sydney|Sydney]] and [[tasmania|Tasmania]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] [[current|set]] his course for Melbourne. The approach was uneventful: "with fine weather, [[spray|the Spray]] made Melbourne Heads on the 22nd of December" — (013-020). The landfall marked nearly three months since leaving the [[keeling-islands|Keeling Islands]], a passage that had taken him across the breadth of the [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]].

But the customhouse awaited. "She poked her nose into the customhouse at Melbourne, where she was charged [[tonnage|tonnage dues]]" — (013-022). [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], ever sensitive to official impositions, noted that only at [[pernambuco|Pernambuco]] and Melbourne had he been troubled by customs in this way. For a man who had sailed alone across the world's oceans, the bureaucratic reception ashore could be the roughest part of the voyage.

Melbourne also provided one of the voyage's more colorful episodes: a shark that proved valuable. While in port, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] caught and sold a shark, earning enough to cover some expenses. The city's waters were known for their sharks, but turning one into profit was a uniquely [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] touch — the practical sailor making the sea pay its way.

He stayed a pleasant while, exploring Melbourne and nearby St. Kilda. "After a pleasant time in Melbourne and St. Kilda, which had been much enjoyed" — (013-024), he departed for the next leg of his [[australia|Australia]]n coastal passage. And he made a point of noting: "The Bishop of Melbourne (commend me to his teachings)" — (023-016), a rare endorsement from a sailor not given to ecclesiastical praise.

## Historical Context

In the 1890s, Melbourne was at its peak as the financial and cultural capital of [[australia|Australia]]. The land boom of the 1880s had given way to a severe depression by the time of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s visit, but the city retained its grand [[victoria|Victoria]]n architecture — much of which survives today. The port at Port Melbourne handled wool, wheat, and gold exports, and the city had a population of nearly half a million. For a lone sailor arriving from the sea, it must have seemed a metropolis.

> I squared the matter by charging people sixpence each for coming on board, and when this business got dull I caught a shark and charged them sixpence each to look at that. The shark was twelve feet six inches in length, and carried a progeny of twenty-six, not one of them less than two feet in length.
