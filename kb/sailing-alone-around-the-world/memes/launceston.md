# Launceston

Launceston is one of [[tasmania|Tasmania]]'s principal cities, situated at the head of the Tamar River in the north of the island. At the time of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s visit in 1896–1897, it was a thriving port and the site of [[victoria|the Victoria]] Museum, whose curator, Professor Porter, received a notable specimen from the voyage.

## Role in the Voyage

During his [[australia|Australia]]n stay, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] donated a shark he had caught — described as a "Kilda shark" — to "Professor Porter, the curator of [[victoria|the Victoria]] Museum of Launceston, which is at the head of the Tamar" (013-028).

> I carried the St. Kilda shark along, stuffed with hay, and disposed of it to Professor Porter, the curator of the Victoria Museum of Launceston, which is at the head of the Tamar. For many a long day to come may be seen there the shark of St. Kilda. Alas! the good but mistaken people of St. Kilda, when the illustrated journals with pictures of my shark reached their newsstands, flew into a passion, and swept all papers containing mention of fish into the fire; for St. Kilda was a watering-place—and the idea of a shark there! But my show went on.
>
> (013-028)

The *[[spray|Spray]]* was also physically berthed at Launceston under unusual circumstances. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] recorded: "The [[spray|Spray]] was berthed on the beach at a small jetty at Launceston while the tide driven in by the gale that brought her up the river was unusually high; and she lay there hard and fast, with not enough water around her at any time after to wet one's feet till she was ready to sail; then, to float her, the ground was dug from under her keel" (013-029). The grounded berthing and the need to dig her out show the practical challenges of navigating a small vessel into [[tasmania|Tasmania]]n tidal ports.
