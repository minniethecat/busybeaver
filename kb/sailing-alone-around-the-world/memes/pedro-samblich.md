Pedro Samblich was an Austrian sea captain of large experience who [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] met at [[sandy-point|Sandy Point]] ([[sandy-point|Punta Arenas]]) in [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]]. He became one of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s most memorable benefactors during the solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]], providing not only provisions but a famously unconventional defense — a bag of carpet-tacks — that proved decisive in the [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n waters ahead.

## Biography

Captain Pedro Samblich was an Austrian mariner who had [[current|set]]tled at [[sandy-point|Sandy Point]] in [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]]. Described by [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] as "a good Austrian of large experience," [[samblich|Samblich]] operated a sloop in the region and was well acquainted with the local waters and inhabitants (007-012).

[[samblich|Samblich]] had deep knowledge of the [[fuegians|Fuegian natives]] and knew the dangers of the strait intimately. A kinsman of [[samblich|Samblich]] had been killed by [[black-pedro|Black Pedro]], a [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n native whom [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] would later encounter (009-008), giving [[samblich|Samblich]] personal reason to understand the threat.

The port captain at [[sandy-point|Sandy Point]] had urged [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] to take armed men aboard for the strait passage, but [[samblich|Samblich]], understanding both the terrain and the natives, offered a radically different approach — the carpet-tacks.

## Role in the Voyage

When [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] arrived at [[sandy-point|Sandy Point]], he was confronting the dilemma of navigating the perilous [[strait-of-magellan|Strait of Magellan]] alone. The port captain advised him to ship extra hands to fight Indians in the strait, but [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] found only one willing man — who insisted on bringing another "mon and a doog" (007-012).

At this critical moment, [[samblich|Samblich]] stepped forward with an unusual but invaluable gift:

> "Captain Pedro Samblich, a good Austrian of large experience, coming along, gave me a bag of carpet-tacks, worth more than all the fighting men and dogs of Tierra del Fuego." — (007-012)

[[samblich|Samblich]] explained cryptically that [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] would have use for the tacks, adding, "You must use them with discretion — that is to say, don't step on them yourself" (007-012). The tacks would prove crucial: [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] scattered them on the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s deck at night to deter [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n boarders (008-011).

### Provisions and Other Gifts

Beyond the tacks, [[samblich|Samblich]]'s generosity extended further. He provided bags of hard biscuits superior to [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s ordinary sea-biscuits, a large quantity of smoked venison, and a compass better than [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s own (007-013).

[[samblich|Samblich]] even offered to unbend his [[sloop|sloop's]] mainsail and brought out a bottle of [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n gold-dust, urging [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] to take some for the voyage ahead. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] declined the gold but later reflected that "[[samblich|Samblich]]'s tacks, as it turned out, were of more value than gold" (007-013).

The provisioning was timely. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was about to enter the most dangerous stretch of the entire [[voyage|circumnavigation]]: the narrow, winding channels of [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]], with [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n tribes whose reputation for hostility was well established.

## Advice on the Fuegians

[[samblich|Samblich]]'s counsel extended beyond material gifts. Regarding [[black-pedro|Black Pedro]], the most notorious [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n in the region, [[samblich|Samblich]] was direct: he told [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] to shoot the man on sight (009-008). This advice reflected the reality of [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n waters — a lone sailor in a small sloop could easily be overwhelmed.

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] would indeed encounter [[black-pedro|Black Pedro]] later in the voyage. While he did not follow [[samblich|Samblich]]'s advice to the letter — [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was notably reluctant to use firearms — the carpet-tacks proved a more creative deterrent. Scattered [[deck|on deck]] at night, they turned the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s planking into a bed of nails for any barefoot [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n attempting to board unnoticed (008-011).

## Significance

[[samblich|Samblich]] represents the generosity [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] encountered at critical junctions of his voyage. His gift of carpet-tacks became one of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s most effective defenses in [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n waters — a creative, non-lethal deterrent that allowed the solo sailor to sleep while keeping his deck clear of unwanted visitors.

The episode illustrates the network of experienced mariners that sustained [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s voyage. At every major waypoint — the [[azores|Azores]], [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]], [[sandy-point|Sandy Point]], [[australia|Australia]], the [[keeling-cocos|Cocos Islands]], [[st-helena|St. Helena]] — local captains and officials offered advice, supplies, and encouragement. [[samblich|Samblich]]'s contribution was among the most ingenious: a bag of household tacks transformed into a strategic defense.
