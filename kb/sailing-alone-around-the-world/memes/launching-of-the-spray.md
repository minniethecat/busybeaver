The **Launching of the Spray** was the culmination of thirteen months of solitary labor by [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]], who rebuilt an antiquated sloop given to him by [[eben-pierce|Captain Eben Pierce]] of [[fairhaven|Fairhaven]], Massachusetts. The launch in 1893 marked the birth of the vessel that, two years later, would depart [[boston|Boston]] on the first single-handed [[voyage|circumnavigation]] of the globe (001-001).

## Summary

In the winter of 1892, retired sea captain [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]] was uncertain of his future — whether to seek another command or work in a shipyard — when a chance encounter with an old whaling-captain offered him a ship. The "ship" proved to be the *[[spray|Spray]]*, a derelict sloop propped up in a field in [[fairhaven|Fairhaven]]. Over thirteen months, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] single-handedly rebuilt her from keel to mast, and on the day after her painting was finished, the *[[spray|Spray]]* was launched into the waters off [[fairhaven|Fairhaven]], "sitting on the water like a swan" (001-013).

## Context

By the early 1890s, the age of American sail was in decline. Tall vessels were being cut down for coal-barges, and experienced captains found themselves without commands. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], a master mariner with nearly twenty years' experience commanding ships like the *[[northern-light|Northern Light]]* and *[[aquidneck|Aquidneck]]*, found himself ashore in [[boston|Boston]] after the wreck of the *[[aquidneck|Aquidneck]]* on the coast of Brazil (001-004).

> "Nearly all our tall vessels had been cut down for coal-barges, and were being ignominiously towed by the nose from port to port, while many worthy captains addressed themselves to Sailors' Snug Harbor." — (001-006)

It was in this climate that [[eben-pierce|Captain Eben Pierce]], an old whaling-captain acquaintance, made [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] an unusual offer: "Come to [[fairhaven|Fairhaven]] and I'll give you a ship. But she wants some repairs" (001-006). The ship turned out to be the *[[spray|Spray]]*, a sloop so old that locals joked she had been "built in the year 1" and had sat propped in a field, covered with [[sail|canvas]], for seven years (001-007).

## Narrative

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] began work in earnest in March 1892. He felled an oak tree for the keel, built a steam-box to bend the ribs, and worked day by day as the seasons turned. The work became a fixture of the [[fairhaven|Fairhaven]] community — whaling-captains visited regularly to inspect the progress and offer advice, and the neighbors debated whether the rebuilt vessel would "pay" (001-008).

> "It was a great day in the Spray shipyard when her new stem was set up and fastened to the new keel. Whaling-captains came from far to survey it. With one voice they pronounced it 'A 1,' and in their opinion 'fit to smash ice.'" — (001-008)

The construction was thorough. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] used Georgia pine planks, through-bolted to the timbers with screw-nuts — about a thousand bolts in all (001-010). White-oak stanchions, cedar-wedged bulwarks, and yellow-pine [[deck|deck beams]] went into a vessel designed to be "stout and strong" (001-011). The total cost was $553.62 for materials, plus thirteen months of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s own labor (001-015).

The actual launch came quietly, without ceremony. After the calking was finished and the hull painted with copper paint below and white lead above, "on the following day the *[[spray|Spray]]* was launched. As she rode at her ancient, rust-eaten anchor, she sat on the water like a swan" (001-013).

## Aftermath

Immediately after the launch, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] fitted a New Hampshire spruce mast and made ready for a trial trip. With [[eben-pierce|Captain Pierce]] aboard, the *[[spray|Spray]]* "flew... across [[buzzards-bay|Buzzard's Bay]] on a trial-trip — all [[rigging|rig]]ht" (001-015). [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] spent an additional season fishing on the coast — discovering, he admitted, that he "had not the cunning properly to bait a hook" — before resolving to undertake a voyage [[voyage|around the world]] (002-001).

On the morning of April 24, 1895, with a fair wind, the *[[spray|Spray]]* weighed anchor and sailed from [[boston|Boston]]. "A thrilling pulse beat high in me," [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] wrote. "My step was light [[deck|on deck]] in the crisp air. I felt that there could be no turning back" (002-002). The voyage would last over three years, cover 46,000 miles, and make [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] the first person to sail alone [[voyage|around the world]].

## Significance

The launching of the *[[spray|Spray]]* was more than a shipbuilding feat — it was the foundational event of the first solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]]. The vessel [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] created with his own hands, at a cost of just over five hundred dollars, would carry him safely through [[gale|storm]]s, pirates, groundings, and the world's most dangerous passages. The *[[spray|Spray]]* herself became a testament to [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s seamanship and the idea, radical at the time, that a small, well-built vessel could go anywhere. After the voyage, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sailed the *[[spray|Spray]]* back to [[fairhaven|Fairhaven]], "to the place of the very beginning whence I had, as I have said, renewed my age" (021-015), tying her to the same cedar spile driven into the bank to hold her when she was launched.
