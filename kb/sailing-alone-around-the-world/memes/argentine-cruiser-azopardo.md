# Azopardo

The *Azopardo* was an Argentine cruiser that shared the anchorage at [[port-angosto|Port Angosto]] with the *[[spray|Spray]]* during a gale in the [[strait-of-magellan|Strait of Magellan]]. Her arrival — seeking the same shelter as [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] — underscored the international importance of the strait, where the navies of [[chile|Chile]] and Argentina maintained a watchful presence. Named for Juan Bautista Azopardo, an early Argentine naval hero, the cruiser was one of several South American warships that [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] encountered on his passage.

## Specifications

The *Azopardo* was a cruiser — a multi-purpose warship designed for patrol, showing the flag, and, in the context of [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]], maintaining a sovereign presence in contested waters. Cruisers of this era were typically of 1,000–3,000 [[tonnage|tons]] displacement, armed with 4–6 inch guns, and capable of 15–20 [[knot|knots]] under steam. The *Azopardo* was substantial enough to impress, but the [[gale|storm]] at [[port-angosto|Port Angosto]] made her as vulnerable as any vessel.

## History

The *Azopardo* appears in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s narrative during the same gale that tested the *[[spray|Spray]]* at [[port-angosto|Port Angosto]]. "The Argentine cruiser *Azopardo* into port" — (010-006) came seeking shelter, driven by the same weather that had forced [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] to return to the anchorage. The shared experience of running for cover from a [[patagonia|Patagonia]]n [[gale|storm]] leveled all distinctions between a navy cruiser and a lone sloop.

The *Azopardo*'s presence also had a protective dimension. During an earlier incident when [[fuegians|Fuegians]] had been harassing [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] at [[port-angosto|Port Angosto]], the Argentine cruiser — though not present at that exact moment — represented the kind of naval authority that kept the region's dangers in check.

## In Slocum's Narrative

The *Azopardo* appears briefly but memorably — a ship that, like [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], was at the mercy of the strait's weather. The gale that brought her into [[port-angosto|Port Angosto]] was the same one that had tested the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s ground tackle. For [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], accustomed to being the only vessel in sight for weeks on end, the arrival of a foreign warship in the same lonely anchorage was a reminder that the strait, for all its remoteness, was a highway — and that the nations of South America were as invested in its security as any passing sailor.

> The bad weather on my last return to Port Angosto for shelter brought the Chilean gunboat *Condor* and the Argentine cruiser *Azopardo* into port. As soon as the latter came to anchor, Captain Mascarella, the commander, sent a boat to the *Spray* with the message that he would take me in tow for Sandy Point if I would give up the voyage and return — the thing farthest from my mind.
>
> (010-006)

## Significance

The *Azopardo* represents the geopolitical dimension of [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] in the 1890s. [[chile|Chile]] and Argentina both maintained naval forces in the region, a reflection of the strategic importance of the strait and the ongoing competition between the two nations for influence in [[tierra-del-fuego|Tierra del Fuego]] and [[patagonia|Patagonia]]. That [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] encountered vessels from both navies — the [[chile|Chile]]an *[[chilean-gunboat-huemel|Huemel]]* and *[[chilean-gunboat-condor|Condor]]*, and the Argentine *Azopardo* — is a window into the geopolitics of a passage that was far from empty.
