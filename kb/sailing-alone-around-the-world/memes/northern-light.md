# Northern Light

The *Northern Light* was a full-[[rigging|rig]]ged clipper ship — one of the great American [[sail|sailing]] vessels of the mid-19th century — and the finest command of [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]]'s career as a merchant captain. In his own words, she was "the finest American [[sail|sailing]]-vessel afloat" (001-004), and his pride in having commanded her colors every page of his later writing.

## Design and Construction

The *Northern Light* was built around 1853, during the twilight of the American clipper era. She was a large vessel for her type — probably around 1,500 gross [[tonnage|tons]] — with the sharp lines, lofty [[rigging|rig]], and immense sail area characteristic of the clippers that raced tea from China and wool from [[australia|Australia]]. By the time [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] took command, steam was already eroding the clippers' commercial viability, but the *Northern Light* remained a thoroughbred.

## In Slocum's Career

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] commanded the *Northern Light* during his years as a merchant captain, [[sail|sailing]] her on the long-haul routes he knew best: to China, to [[australia|Australia]], to Japan, and among the Spice Islands (001-005). The clipper represented the peak of the [[sail|sailing]] merchant marine — fast, demanding, and magnificent. To command such a vessel was to stand at the summit of the profession.

> "My best command was that of the magnificent ship *Northern Light*, of which I was part owner. I had a right to be proud of her, for at that time — in the eighties — she was the finest American sailing-vessel afloat." — (001-004)

The *Northern Light* also figured in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s network of maritime friendships. Years later, in [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]], the chief mate of the steamer *Colombia*, an old friend named Mr. Hannibal, "referred affectionately to days in [[philippines|Manila]] when we were there together, he in the *Southern Cross* and I in the *Northern Light*, both ships as beautiful as their names" (009-012).

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s affection for the *Northern Light* was evident decades later. In *[[sail|Sail]]ing Alone Around the World*, he recalled his voyages in her with pride, contrasting the great clipper's speed and power with the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s modest dimensions. The comparison was not invidious — each vessel was perfect in her own element.

## Legacy

The *Northern Light* represents [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s life before the *[[spray|Spray]]* — the career of a working merchant captain in the last great age of sail. Her memory haunts the narrative of the [[voyage|circumnavigation]], reminding the reader that the solo sailor in the 37-foot sloop had once commanded one of the finest ships afloat.
