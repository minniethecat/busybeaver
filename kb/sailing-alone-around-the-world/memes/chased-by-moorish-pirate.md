A dramatic high-speed chase by Moorish pirates in the waters off [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]], shortly after the *[[spray|Spray]]* began her Atlantic crossing. The pursuit ended abruptly when a sudden squall and heavy sea [[mast|dismasted]] the pirate felucca, saving [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] from an armed confrontation at sea.

## Summary

On August 25, 1895, the day the *[[spray|Spray]]* sailed from [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]] into [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]], a felucca carrying Moorish pirates gave chase. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], warned that the coast was "the home of pirates," found himself pursued at close quarters by a vessel crewed by men he recognized as "the sons of generations of pirates." The chase ended when a great wave and squall [[mast|dismasted]] the felucca while [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] himself was forced to [[reefing|reef]] his [[sail|sails]], narrowly escaping a violent confrontation. (005-002)

## Context

[[gibraltar|Gibraltar]] had been a welcoming port — [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] received vegetables twice a week and milk every morning from the admiralty grounds [005-intro]. However, he had been warned by "officers of vast experience in navigating these seas" that "longshore pirates on both coasts" were numerous (005-002). His planned route through the [[suez|Suez Canal]] had been abandoned on their advice in favor of the western passage around [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]], but the immediate danger lay much closer to [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]]'s shores.

## Narrative

Shortly after the *[[spray|Spray]]* cleared [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]] on August 25, the wind rose rapidly to a furious gale. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s plan was to haul well offshore, clear of the piratical coast, but he "had hardly accomplished this when I perceived a felucca making out of the nearest port, and finally following in the wake of the *[[spray|Spray]]*" (005-002).

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] changed course; the felucca did the same. Both vessels were [[sail|sailing]] very fast, "but the distance growing less and less between us" (005-002). The *[[spray|Spray]]* was doing nobly, even more than at her best, but she was carrying too much sail for safety and would broach now and then. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] realized he must [[reefing|reef]] "even if I had to grapple with him for my life" (005-002).

> "I was not long in reefing the mainsail and sweating it up — probably not more than fifteen minutes; but the felucca had in the meantime so shortened the distance between us that I now saw the tuft of hair on the heads of the crew — by which, it is said, Mohammed will pull the villains up into heaven — and they were coming on like the wind." — (005-003)

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] could clearly make out the crew and "felt them to be the sons of generations of pirates," observing by their movements that "they were now preparing to strike a blow" (005-003). But the situation changed in an instant:

> "Their craft, with too much sail on, broached to on the crest of a great wave. This one great sea changed the aspect of affairs suddenly as the flash of a gun. Three minutes later the same wave overtook the *Spray* and shook her in every timber." — (005-003)

Simultaneously, the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s sheet-strop parted, and the [[boom|main-boom]] broke short at the [[rigging|rig]]ging. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sprang to the [[halyard|jib-halyards]] and downhaul, instantly downing the jib. With the headsail off and the [[rudder|helm]] put hard down, [[sloop|the sloop]] came into the wind. He got the mainsail down and secured inboard, broken boom and all — "not a stitch of it was broken" (005-003).

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] then stepped quickly to the cabin and snatched down his loaded rifle and cartridges. "I made mental calculations that the pirate would by this time have recovered his course and be close aboard, and that when I saw him it would be better for me to be looking at him along the barrel of a gun" (005-003). But when he peered into the mist, there was no pirate within a mile.

> "The wave and squall that carried away my boom dismasted the felucca outright. I perceived his thieving crew, some dozen or more of them, struggling to recover their rigging from the sea. Allah blacken their faces!" — (005-003)

## Aftermath

The pirate threat was eliminated by the same sea that damaged the *[[spray|Spray]]*. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s vessel had lost her [[boom|main-boom]], but the felucca had been completely [[mast|dismasted]], leaving her crew helpless in the water. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s quick seamanship — [[reef|reefing]] in time and securing the broken [[rigging|rig]]ging — saved both his vessel and potentially his life. The *[[spray|Spray]]* continued southward along the African coast, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] would later compare his experience favorably with that of [[christopher-columbus|Columbus]] in the same waters (005-001).

## Significance

This encounter was one of the most dangerous moments of the entire [[voyage|circumnavigation]] — not from natural forces but from human predators. It demonstrated [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s seamanship under extreme pressure and his willingness to fight if necessary. The event also validated the advice he had received in [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]] to change his route from the [[suez|Suez Canal]] to [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]], confirming that piracy remained a real threat in these waters at the close of the nineteenth century.
