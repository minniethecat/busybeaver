## Geography

Port Denison is a natural harbor on the [[bowen|Queensland]] coast of [[australia|Australia]], situated south of [[gloucester|Gloucester]] Island. The harbor is "easy of approach, spacious and safe, and afforded excellent holding-ground." (015-003) The country surrounding the port presented "a healthful appearance," with the town of [[bowen|Bowen]] resting on a hill overlooking the anchorage. (015-002)

Port Denison was one of the primary ports along the [[great-barrier-reef|Barrier Reef]] Channel, serving as a coaling and provisioning stop for coastal steamers and [[sail|sailing]] vessels traversing the [[bowen|Queensland]] coast. (015-001)

## Role in the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] arrived at Port Denison on the morning of May 26, 1897, [[anchor|anchoring]] [[spray|the Spray]] in the evening at the harbor below [[bowen|Bowen]]. He described the town as "the future watering place and health-resort of [[bowen|Queensland]]." (015-002)

The people of [[bowen|Bowen]] came to hear about [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s voyage on the second evening of his stay: "It was quiet in [[bowen|Bowen]] when [[spray|the Spray]] arrived, and the good people with an hour to throw away on the second evening of her arrival came down to the School of Arts to talk about the voyage, it being the latest event." (015-003)

From Port Denison, [[spray|the Spray]] "ran before the constant trade-wind, and made no stop at all, night or day" until reaching [[cooktown|Cooktown]] on the [[cooktown|Endeavor River]] on May 31, 1897. (015-007) Port Denison was one of only two anchorages [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] made throughout the entire passage of the [[great-barrier-reef|Barrier Reef]] Channel — the other being at [[cooktown|Endeavor River]]. (015-015)

## Historical Context

Port Denison and the town of [[bowen|Bowen]] were established in the mid-nineteenth century as part of [[bowen|Queensland]]'s coastal development. By the 1890s, it was a modest but growing [[current|set]]tlement, promoted as a health resort for the colony. The "sweet little town" with its hilltop location offered a pleasant respite for passing mariners. (015-002)

The chapter [[course|heading]] for this leg of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s voyage reads: "Arrival at Port Denison, Queensland — A lecture — Reminiscences of [[captain-cook|Captain Cook]] — Lecturing for charity at [[cooktown|Cooktown]] — A happy escape from a coral [[reefing|reef]]." (015-001)

> On the morning of the 26th Gloucester Island was close aboard, and the Spray anchored in the evening at Port Denison, where rests, on a hill, the sweet little town of Bowen, the future watering place and health-resort of Queensland.
>
> (015-002)
