## Passage

On May 19 the Spray passed the Tweed River and was signaled from [[danger-point|Danger Point]] (014-039).

> May 19 the Spray, passing the Tweed River, was signaled from Danger Point, where those on shore seemed most anxious about the state of my health, for they asked if "all hands" were well, to which I could say, "Yes."
>
> (014-039)

## Narrative Significance

The Tweed River marks a point along Slocum's coastal passage of eastern Australia, where he recorded the date — May 19 — with the precision of a ship's log (014-039). This attention to chronological detail is characteristic of Slocum's narrative style, which blends the matter-of-fact entries of a navigation log with the broader storytelling of a travel memoir.
