# John Ericsson

John Ericsson (1803–1889) was a Swedish-American engineer and inventor, best known for designing the USS *Monitor* during the American Civil War and the torpedo boat *[[destroyer|Destroyer]]*, which [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]] delivered from New York to Brazil.

## Biography

Born in Sweden in 1803, [[destroyer|Ericsson]] showed early talent in engineering and moved to England in 1826, where he designed locomotives and the novel screw propeller. In 1839, he emigrated to the United States, where he became one of the most celebrated naval architects of the 19th century. His most famous creation, the ironclad USS *Monitor*, revolutionized naval warfare. [[destroyer|Ericsson]] died in New York in 1889, shortly before [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s famous [[voyage|circumnavigation]].

## Role in the Voyage

Although John Ericsson never appears directly in *[[sail|Sail]]ing Alone Around the World*, his legacy is present through the "famous [[destroyer|Ericsson]] ship *[[destroyer|Destroyer]]*" (005-024). [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had served as master of the *[[destroyer|Destroyer]]*, delivering the vessel from New York to Brazil. The *[[destroyer|Destroyer]]* was later "renamed the *Buffalo*" and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] noted that it "was in many ways the better ship" (005-025, 005-026).

In Brazil, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s connection to the *[[destroyer|Destroyer]]* gave him standing with local officials. The doctor at a Brazilian port "dined me often" on provisions "of which he obtained from the *[[destroyer|Destroyer]]*" (005-026). When [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] revealed "that it was I who brought the *[[destroyer|Destroyer]]* to Brazil," the response was immediate recognition: "Oh, yes" (005-027). [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] still held a financial claim, noting the "wages due me from the beloved *[[destroyer|Destroyer]]*" (005-029).

> In 1893 I was employed as master to take the famous Ericsson ship Destroyer from New York to Brazil to go against the rebel Mello and his party. The Destroyer, by the way, carried a submarine cannon of enormous length.
>
> (005-024)

## Ericsson's Naval Innovations

[[destroyer|Ericsson]]'s influence on 19th-century naval architecture extended well beyond the *[[destroyer|Destroyer]]*. His development of the screw propeller — first demonstrated on the steamship *Francis B. Ogden* in 1837 — transformed maritime propulsion, rendering paddle-[[helm|wheel]]s obsolete for ocean-going vessels. The ironclad USS *Monitor*, with its revolutionary revolving turret, introduced a design concept that shaped warship construction for generations.

The *[[destroyer|Destroyer]]* itself represented [[destroyer|Ericsson]]'s interest in torpedo warfare and small, fast attack craft. For [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], commanding the *[[destroyer|Destroyer]]* on its delivery voyage to Brazil meant handling a unique vessel at the forefront of naval technology — experience that would serve him well when he later rebuilt and sailed the *[[spray|Spray]]* alone [[voyage|around the world]].

## Significance

John Ericsson's *[[destroyer|Destroyer]]* represents an important chapter in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s pre-[[spray|Spray]] maritime career. The delivery voyage to Brazil was one of several commands [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] held before his famous solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]], and his association with [[destroyer|Ericsson]]'s innovative vessel lent him credibility in South American ports.
