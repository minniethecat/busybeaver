**Beauty Point** is a [[current|set]]tlement on the Tamar River on the north coast of [[australia|Tasmania]]. [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]] anchored the *[[spray|Spray]]* there during his [[voyage|circumnavigation]] and remembered it as a place of serene natural beauty and warm hospitality.

## Slocum's Visit

Waiting for the fair-weather season around northern [[australia|Australia]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sailed to several [[tasmania|Tasmania]]n ports "where it is fine the year round, the first of these being Beauty Point, near which are [[beacon|Beacon]]sfield and the great [[tasmania|Tasmania]] goldmine, which I visited in turn" (014-005). He toured the mine and observed "much gray, uninteresting rock being hoisted out of the mine there, and hundreds of stamps crushing it into powder," noting wryly, "People told me there was gold in it, and I believed what they said" (014-005).

What stayed with [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] most was the natural [[current|set]]ting. "I remember Beauty Point for its shady forest and for the road among the tall gum-trees" (014-006).

## A Vice-Regal Visit

While anchored at Beauty Point, an unexpected encounter occurred. The governor of [[newcastle|New South Wales]], Lord Hampden, and his family arrived on a steam-yacht. The *[[spray|Spray]]* "threw her bunting out, of course, and probably a more insignificant craft [[course|bearing]] the Stars and Stripes was never seen in those waters" (014-006).

Yet the governor's party knew of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s voyage. When [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] heard His Excellency say, "Introduce me to the captain," he "found myself at once in the presence of a gentleman and a friend, and one greatly interested in my voyage" (014-006). Lord and Lady Hampden promised to rendezvous with [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] on board the *[[spray|Spray]]* at the Paris Exposition in 1900. "If we live," they said, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] added, "Dangers of the seas excepted" (014-006).

## Departure

From Beauty Point, the *[[spray|Spray]]* "visited Georgetown, near the mouth of the river Tamar" (014-007), continuing westward along the [[tasmania|Tasmania]]n coast toward [[devonport|Devonport]].

> The season of fair weather around the north of Australia being yet a long way off, I sailed to other ports in Tasmania, where it is fine the year round, the first of these being Beauty Point, near which are Beaconsfield and the great Tasmania goldmine, which I visited in turn. I saw much gray, uninteresting rock being hoisted out of the mine there, and hundreds of stamps crushing it into powder. People told me there was gold in it, and I believed what they said.
>
> (014-005)
