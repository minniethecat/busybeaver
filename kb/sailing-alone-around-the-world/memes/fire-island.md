## Encounter with the Tornado

On June 25, 1898, as the Spray approached the coast of Long Island near the end of her circumnavigation, she encountered a severe tornado off Fire Island (021-008). The tornado had swept over New York City an hour earlier, with lightning that wrecked buildings and sent trees flying about in splinters; even ships at docks had parted their moorings and smashed into other ships (021-008).

It was the "climax storm of the voyage" (021-008). Slocum recognized its character in time to have everything snug aboard and receive it under bare poles. Even so, the sloop shivered when it struck and heeled over unwillingly on her beam ends; but rounding to, with a sea-anchor ahead, she righted and faced out the storm (021-008).

## After the Storm

When daylight came, Slocum found the sea had changed color from dark green to light. He threw the lead and got soundings in thirteen fathoms, making land some miles east of Fire Island (021-015). From there he sailed along the coast to Newport, Rhode Island, where the Spray cast anchor at 1 a.m. on June 27, 1898, completing her cruise of over forty-six thousand miles (021-015).

## Strategic Significance

The encounter off Fire Island marked Slocum's return to American waters after nearly three years at sea. The tornado forced him to change his plan of landing at New York; instead, he made for Newport to "think the matter over" (021-008).
