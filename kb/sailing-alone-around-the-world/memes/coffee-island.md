# Coffee Island

Coffee Island is the name [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]] gave to a small island in the [[patagonia|Patagonia]]n channels where he anchored during his passage through [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] region. The island, south of [[fortescue-bay|Fortescue Bay]], was not a named place on any chart — [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] named it himself after a simple act of comfort in a harsh environment.

## Role in the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] first found this anchorage on a difficult night in the [[patagonia|Patagonia]]n channels. After "hard beating in the heavy squalls and against the current," he "gained anchorage at twelve o'clock that night, under the lee of a little island, and then prepared myself a cup of coffee, of which I was sorely in need" (007-017). The small ritual in the midst of struggle prompted him to name the place: "Finding that the anchor held, I drank my beverage, and named the place Coffee Island" (007-017).

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] returned to Coffee Island months later, on his birthday. On February 20, 1896, "The [[spray|Spray]] now reached away for Coffee Island, which I sighted on my birthday" (009-006). Before departing again, he "prepared a cup of warm coffee over a smart wood fire in my great [[montevideo|Montevideo]] stove" (009-006), revisiting the very ritual that had given the island its name.

> I gained anchorage at twelve o'clock that night, under the lee of a little island, and then prepared myself a cup of coffee, of which I was sorely in need; for, to tell the truth, hard beating in the heavy squalls and against the current had told on my strength. Finding that the anchor held, I drank my beverage, and named the place Coffee Island. It lies to the south of Charles Island, with only a narrow channel between.
>
> (007-017)

## Historical Context

The naming of Coffee Island exemplifies [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s self-reliant character and his tendency to imbue the voyage's small moments with significance. In a region of unnamed islets and treacherous channels, Coffee Island became a personal landmark — a place of rest, warmth, and the simple pleasure of a hot drink after a hard day's sail.
