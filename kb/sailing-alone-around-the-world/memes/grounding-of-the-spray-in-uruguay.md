# Grounding of the Spray in Uruguay

A near-shipwreck early in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s [[voyage|circumnavigation]], when [[spray|the Spray]] was driven ashore on the sands of [[uruguay|Uruguay]] by a southwest gale in 1895.

## Summary

While [[sail|sailing]] south along the coast of South America, [[spray|the Spray]] was caught in a severe southwest gale and driven onto the [[uruguay|Uruguay]]an shore. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] narrowly escaped shipwreck and was forced to work with local inhabitants to refloat his vessel (006-001).

## Context

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had departed from Brazil and was making his way south toward [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]]. The coast of [[uruguay|Uruguay]], south of the "dividing-line of [[uruguay|Uruguay]] and Brazil," presented a dangerous lee shore when the wind blew from the southwest (006-005). The region was sparsely populated, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was entirely alone on [[spray|the Spray]].

## Narrative

The incident is recounted in Chapter VI of *[[sail|Sail]]ing Alone Around the World*. A southwest gale — described as a "pampero" — drove [[spray|the Spray]] toward the [[uruguay|Uruguayan coast]]. Despite [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s efforts to keep her off, [[sloop|the sloop]] was carried onto the sands (006-001).

Once ashore, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] encountered a local man on horseback who surveyed the situation. The "chap was not a native [[uruguay|Uruguay]]an," but like many in the region, he proved helpful (006-006). The sea "sent up the whole shore of [[uruguay|Uruguay]] before a southwest gale" (006-009), making salvage urgent. To the local "[[uruguay|Uruguay]]ans," [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s feat of [[sail|sailing]] alone "may have seemed a feat worthy of some recognition" (006-011).

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] managed to refloat [[spray|the Spray]] with local assistance and minor repairs, continuing his voyage southward toward [[patagonia|Patagonia]] and [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]].

## Aftermath

The grounding caused minimal damage to [[spray|the Spray]], a testament to her sturdy construction. The incident demonstrated both the dangers of [[sail|sailing]] alone along an unfamiliar lee shore and the resourcefulness that would characterize [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s entire [[voyage|circumnavigation]].

## Significance

This was one of the first major tests of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s seamanship on the voyage. It illustrated the constant danger faced by single-handed sailors, particularly in regions where weather could change rapidly and assistance was scarce. The successful refloating reinforced [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s confidence in his ability to handle emergencies alone.
