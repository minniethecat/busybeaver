# Danger Point

Danger Point is a coastal headland near the mouth of the [[tweed-river|Tweed River]] on [[australia|Australia]]'s eastern shore, notable in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s narrative as the place where shore-dwellers signaled the *[[spray|Spray]]* to inquire after the lone sailor's welfare.

## In the Voyage

On May 19, 1897, the *[[spray|Spray]]*, "passing the Tweed River, was signaled from Danger Point" — (014-039). Those on shore "seemed most anxious about the state of my health, for they asked if 'all hands' were well, to which I could say, 'Yes'" — (014-039). The inquiry was a simple human moment in a long voyage: strangers on a remote headland, looking out for a solitary sailor.

Five days later, [[sloop|the sloop]] had made swift progress northward. "On the 24th of May, [[sloop|the sloop]], having made one hundred and ten miles a day from Danger Point, now entered Whitsunday Pass, and that night sailed through among the islands" — (014-044). The run from Danger Point into the sheltered waters of [[whitsunday-pass|Whitsunday Pass]] marked a transition — leaving behind the open coastal passage for the intricate channels within the [[great-barrier-reef|Great Barrier Reef]].

## Geography

Danger Point sits just south of the [[bowen|Queensland]] border, near where the Tweed River meets [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]]. The point itself is unremarkable as [[australia|Australia]]n headlands go — but its place in the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s [[course|track]] gives it a quiet distinction. The [[beacon|lighthouse]] at [[danger-point|Danger Point]] was one of a chain of coastal lights — from [[cape-howe|Cape Howe]] in the south to [[great-sandy-cape|Great Sandy Cape]] in the north — that guided coasting vessels along the dangerous lee shore of eastern [[australia|Australia]].
