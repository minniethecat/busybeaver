# Briar's Island

An island at the western entrance to [[bay-of-fundy|the Bay]] of [[bay-of-fundy|Fundy]], [[nova-scotia|Nova Scotia]], where [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] prepared the *[[spray|Spray]]* for her deep-sea voyage and reconnected with childhood friends.

## Geography

Briar's Island lies near the mouth of [[bay-of-fundy|the Bay]] of [[bay-of-fundy|Fundy]], in the western fishing grounds off [[nova-scotia|Nova Scotia]]. The island sits amid treacherous tide races, including the southwest ledge — the site of the worst tide-rip in [[bay-of-fundy|the Bay]] of [[bay-of-fundy|Fundy]] (002-013). Its waters are rich fishing grounds, and the islanders were known as hardy fishermen who "never flinch from a sea" (002-014).

## Role in the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] arrived at Briar's Island early in his [[voyage|circumnavigation]], on May 13, 1895, after a rough passage from [[cape-ann|Massachusetts]]. He had shaped a course for the island from Gannet Rock but was given a wrong [[course|bearing]] by a fisherman at anchor, causing the *[[spray|Spray]]* to sail "directly over the southwest ledge through the worst [[tide|tide-race]] in [[bay-of-fundy|the Bay]] of [[bay-of-fundy|Fundy]]" before reaching [[westport|Westport harbor]] (002-013).

At Briar's Island, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] "overhauled [[spray|the Spray]] once more and tried her seams, but found that even the test of the sou'west rip had started nothing" (002-016). The sloop had proven herself seaworthy. While waiting for weather, he made "a short excursion with some friends to [[st-marys-bay|St. Mary's Bay]], an old cruising-ground" (002-016).

The island held deep personal meaning for [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]: "The very stones on Briar's Island I was glad to see again, and I knew them all" (002-015). He had spent eight years of his boyhood in [[westport|Westport]], [[nova-scotia|Nova Scotia]], and found old schoolmates still living there. He noted the "little shop round the corner, which for thirty-five years I had not seen, was the same, except that it looked a deal smaller" (002-015).

After refitting and provisioning — including filling six barrels of water — [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] departed for [[yarmouth|Yarmouth]] to continue his voyage (002-016).

## Historical Context

Briar's Island was part of the [[nova-scotia|Nova Scotia]] fishing communities that thrived in the late 19th century. The [[bay-of-fundy|Bay of Fundy]]'s extreme tides — among the highest in the world — made seamanship a demanding skill. The island had produced generations of fishermen and mariners, including [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s friend "Deacon W. D——," whom [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] recalled fondly (002-014).
