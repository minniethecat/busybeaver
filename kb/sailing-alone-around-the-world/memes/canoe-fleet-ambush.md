While anchored at [[snug-bay|Snug Bay]] after passing [[cape-froward|Cape Froward]], the *[[spray|Spray]]* was ambushed by two canoes of well-armed [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n savages. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] fired warning shots and escaped under sail, dragging his anchor through a [[reefing|reef]] of kelp, with one hand on the [[helm|tiller]] and one eye watching for pursuing canoes.

## Summary

At gray morning after passing [[cape-froward|Cape Froward]] in [[strait-of-magellan|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] anchored at [[snug-bay|Snug Bay]] — only to discover that two canoes he had eluded by [[sail|sailing]] all night were now entering the same bay "stealthily under the shadow of the high headland." Outnumbered and at risk of being flanked from the bush, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] fired a warning shot, then made a dramatic escape under full sail, dragging his anchor behind him. (009-011)

## Context

The [[strait-of-magellan|Strait of Magellan]] was the most dangerous leg of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s [[voyage|circumnavigation]] — not for its weather alone, but for the [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n tribes who inhabited its shores. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had been warned at [[sandy-point|Sandy Point]] ([[sandy-point|Punta Arenas]]) by the port captain to "shoot straight, and begin to do it in time, but to avoid killing them if possible" (009-001). Captain [[black-pedro|Pedro]] [[samblich|Samblich]] had given him a bag of carpet-tacks as an unconventional defense against night boarders. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had already encountered [[black-pedro|Black Pedro]], the notorious renegade killer, and knew he was being [[course|track]]ed through the strait.

## Narrative

After a night of [[sail|sailing]] to stay ahead of pursuing canoes, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] thought he had found refuge at [[snug-bay|Snug Bay]]. But as dawn broke, he discovered his pursuers had followed him in:

> "I anchored at gray morning after passing Cape Froward, to find, when broad day appeared, that two canoes which I had eluded by sailing all night were now entering the same bay stealthily under the shadow of the high headland. They were well manned, and the savages were well armed with spears and bows." — (009-011)

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] fired a warning shot from his rifle across the bows of the approaching canoes. Both turned aside into a small creek, out of range — but not out of the fight. Now came the greater danger: being flanked by warriors moving through the bush close aboard.

> "In danger now of being flanked by the savages in the bush close aboard, I was obliged to hoist the sails, which I had barely lowered, and make across to the opposite side of the strait, a distance of six miles." — (009-011)

But an even worse problem appeared: the windlass had jammed, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] could not [[anchor|weigh anchor]]. He was forced to [[current|set]] all sail and fill away, hauling the anchor short by hand:

> "The sloop carried her anchor away, as though it was meant to be always towed in this way underfoot, and with it she towed a ton or more of kelp from a reef in the bay, the wind blowing a wholesale breeze." — (009-011)

The escape was desperate and painful:

> "Meanwhile I worked till blood started from my fingers, and with one eye over my shoulder for savages, I watched at the same time, and sent a bullet whistling whenever I saw a limb or a twig move; for I kept a gun always at hand, and an Indian appearing then within range would have been taken as a declaration of war." — (009-012)

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s hands bled freely from sea-cuts caused by "pulling on hard, wet ropes," but "these healed when I finally got away from the strait into fine weather" (009-012).

## Aftermath

After clearing [[snug-bay|Snug Bay]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] hauled [[sloop|the sloop]] to the wind, repaired the windlass, hove the anchor to the hawse, and made across to a refuge under a high mountain about six miles away — the place he named Echo Mountain (009-012). The [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]ns did not pursue across the open strait. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s tactics — constant movement, warning shots, and refusing to anchor within bowshot of the shore — kept him one step ahead of the ambushers throughout the [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] passage.

## Significance

This event exemplifies the unique nature of the [[strait-of-magellan|Magellan Strait]] danger: the threat came not from the sea but from its shores. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s experience at [[snug-bay|Snug Bay]] illustrates his constant vigilance and the physical toll of solo [[sail|sailing]] through hostile territory. The episode also shows how the [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]ns, emboldened by numbers and knowledge of the terrain, forced [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] into ever more desperate maneuvers — yet he consistently out-sailed and out-thought them.
