# Massachusetts Bay

Massachusetts Bay is a large bay on the [[atlantic-ocean|Atlantic]] coast of [[united-states|Massachusetts]], bordered by [[boston|Boston]] to the west and [[cape-ann|Cape Ann]] to the north. It was the starting waters of [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]]'s solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]].

## In the Book

As the *[[spray|Spray]]* departed [[boston|Boston]] harbor on her trial trip, waves dancing joyously across Massachusetts Bay met her coming out, dashing into myriads of sparkling gems about her at every surge (002-004).

The bay is also referenced by way of [[buzzards-bay|Buzzard's Bay]] to the south, where [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] and [[eben-pierce|Captain Pierce]] conducted the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s trial run before her departure (001-015).

## Maritime Context

In the 1890s, Massachusetts Bay was one of the busiest waterways on [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] seaboard. [[boston|Boston]], at its western end, was a thriving port that had launched countless fishing schooners, merchant vessels, and clipper ships. The bay's waters were [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s home ground — he had sailed them since his earliest days as a seaman, and it was from these familiar shores that he would depart on the greatest voyage of his life.

> Waves dancing joyously across Massachusetts Bay met her coming out of the harbor to dash them into myriads of sparkling gems that hung about her at every surge. The day was perfect, the sunlight clear and strong. Every particle of water thrown into the air became a gem, and the *Spray*, bounding ahead, snatched necklace after necklace from the sea, and as often threw them away.
>
> (002-004)
