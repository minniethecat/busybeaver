# Thursday Island

Thursday Island — a dot of land in the [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]], barely a square mile — became one of the most memorable stops of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s voyage. It was here that he joined in the far-flung celebration of Queen [[victoria|Victoria]]'s Diamond Jubilee, where the pearling fleet gathered, and where he received a gift that would fly from the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s [[mast|masthead]] for the rest of the voyage.

## Geography

Thursday Island lies in the middle of the [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]], between [[australia|Australia]]'s Cape York and [[new-guinea|New Guinea]]. In the 1890s, it was the administrative center of the [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]] Islands, a pearling hub, and a port of call for vessels transiting the strait. The harbor was small but well-sheltered, and the population was a mix of Europeans, Japanese pearl divers, and [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]] Islanders.

## In the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] approached Thursday Island with a mix of anticipation and vigilance. Even before arriving, a steamer captain had challenged him — "he offered to race the poor old [[spray|Spray]] to Thursday Island en route. I declined the challenge" — (014-021). The *[[spray|Spray]]* was no racer, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] knew it.

He arrived in late June 1897, just in time for the Jubilee. "The 22nd was the great day on Thursday Island, for then we had not only the Jubilee" — (015-025), but also his own small celebration aboard the *[[spray|Spray]]*. A new American ensign was presented to him — "a new ensign for [[spray|the Spray]]" — (015-001), replacing the worn flag that had crossed [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] and Pacific.

An encounter with a pearl-fisherman added local color — "a pearl-fisherman at Thursday Island" — (015-001) — and the island's multicultural character left an impression. The Jubilee celebration was conducted with full colonial ceremony, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], the lone American among British subjects, was welcomed warmly.

> The 22nd was the great day on Thursday Island, for then we had not only the jubilee, but a jubilee with a grand corroboree in it.
>
> (015-025)

His departure from Thursday Island [[current|set]] a record. "Twenty-three days from Thursday Island" — (016-004) to the [[keeling-islands|Keeling Cocos Islands]] was a remarkable run, one that he noted with pride. The distance was "twenty-seven hundred miles" — (022-018), and the *[[spray|Spray]]* covered it with the same steady reliability that had brought her this far.

## Historical Context

Thursday Island was at its peak in the 1890s as the center of the [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]] pearling industry. Hundreds of luggers worked the waters, crewed by Japanese, Malay, and Islander divers. The Jubilee of 1897 was celebrated across the British Empire, and even this remote outpost — closer to [[new-guinea|New Guinea]] than to any [[australia|Australia]]n city — marked the occasion with flags, fireworks, and a sense of shared imperial occasion.
