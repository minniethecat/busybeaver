# Fiji

Fiji lay along the periphery of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s route across the [[pacific-ocean|South Pacific]]. After departing [[samoa|Samoa]] for [[australia|Australia]], he passed "north of Fiji instead of south" as he had intended — a course deviation that reflected the challenges of solo navigation across [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]] (013-008).

## Geography

The Fiji archipelago consists of more than 300 islands in the [[pacific-ocean|South Pacific]], straddling the 180th [[longitude|meridian]]. The two largest islands, Viti Levu and Vanua Levu, dominate the group. In [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s day, Fiji was a British crown colony (annexed in 1874), known for its sugar plantations, sandalwood, and strategic position on [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]] shipping routes.

## Goodbye to Vailima

The passage toward Fiji began not at sea but at Vailima, the mountain estate of [[robert-louis-stevenson|Robert Louis Stevenson]]. After weeks of generous [[samoa|Samoa]]n hospitality, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] made his farewell rounds. "Calling to say goodbye to my friends at Vailima, I met Mrs. [[robert-louis-stevenson|Stevenson]] in her [[panama|Panama]] hat, and went over the estate with her" (013-007).

The farewell included a [[samoa|Samoa]]n kava ceremony conducted in honor of the *[[spray|Spray]]*. "It was my turn first, after the custom of the country, to spill a little over my shoulder," [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] wrote, "but having forgotten the [[samoa|Samoa]]n for 'Let the gods drink,' I repeated the equivalent in Russian and Chinook, as I remembered a word in each, whereupon Mr. Osbourne pronounced me a confirmed [[samoa|Samoa]]n" (013-007).

The departure from [[samoa|Samoa]] on August 20, 1896, was bittersweet. "A sense of loneliness seized upon me as the islands faded astern, and as a remedy for it I crowded on sail for lovely [[australia|Australia]], which was not a strange land to me; but for long days in my dreams Vailima stood before the prow" (013-007).

## The Departure from Samoa

The *[[spray|Spray]]*'s passage toward Fiji began with dramatic speed. The sloop "had barely cleared the islands when a sudden burst of the [[trade-wind|trades]] brought her down to close [[reefing|reef]]s, and she reeled off one hundred and eighty-four miles the first day, of which I counted forty miles of current in her favor" (013-008). This remarkable pace underscored the power of [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]] [[trade-wind|trade winds]] that propelled [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] across the ocean.

The decision to pass north of Fiji was a pragmatic navigational choice made in rough conditions. "Finding a rough sea, I swung her off free and sailed north of [[cape-horn|the Horn]] Islands, also north of Fiji instead of south," [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] recorded, after which he "coasted down the west side of the archipelago" (013-008). From there he "sailed direct for [[newcastle|New South Wales]], passing south of New Caledonia, and arrived at [[newcastle|Newcastle]] after a passage of forty-two days, mostly of [[gale|storm]]s and gales" (013-008).

## The Forty-Two Day Passage

The voyage from [[samoa|Samoa]] to [[australia|Australia]] was not an easy one. The forty-two days at sea were described by [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] as "mostly of [[gale|storm]]s and gales" (013-008). Near New Caledonia, the *[[spray|Spray]]* encountered a particularly severe gale — the same [[gale|storm]] that "foundered the American clipper-ship *Patrician* farther south" (013-009). While a large clipper ship succumbed to the weather, the tiny *[[spray|Spray]]* rode it out.

The [[gale|storm]] was severe enough that a French mail-steamer, blown considerably off course, on arrival at [[sydney|Sydney]] "reported it an awful [[gale|storm]], and to inquiring friends said: 'Oh, my! we don't know what has become of the little [[spray|sloop Spray]]. We saw her in the thick of the [[gale|storm]]'" (013-009). But the *[[spray|Spray]]* was "all [[rigging|rig]]ht, lying to like a duck. She was under a goose's wing mainsail, and had had a dry deck throughout the gale" (013-009).

This vignette — a French steamer crew fearing for the little [[sloop|sloop's]] survival, while the *[[spray|Spray]]* was in fact perfectly comfortable — captures the deceptive seaworthiness of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s vessel and his own quiet competence as a sailor.

## Arrival at Newcastle

After passing south of New Caledonia, the *[[spray|Spray]]* approached the [[australia|Australia]]n coast. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] "arrived at [[newcastle|Newcastle]] in the teeth of a gale of wind" on a [[gale|storm]]y day (013-010). The government pilot, Captain Cumming, met him at the harbor bar and, with the assistance of a steamer, carried the vessel to a safe berth. "Nothing was too good for [[spray|the Spray]] here," [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] noted. "All government dues were remitted" (013-010).

## Role in the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s engagement with Fiji was brief. [[sail|Sail]]ing from Vailima in [[samoa|Samoa]], his intended course would have taken him south of the Fiji group. However, navigational realities — or the whims of wind and current — carried the *[[spray|Spray]]* north of the islands (013-008). [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] noted this in his departure log: "Leaving Fiji to the south" was the plan as he [[current|set]] his course for [[newcastle|Newcastle]], [[australia|Australia]] (013-001).

The Fijian waters represented one of many navigational challenges in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s Pacific crossing, where isolated [[reefing|reef]]s and unpredictable weather required constant vigilance. That [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] passed Fiji without incident speaks to his seamanship, though the islands themselves remained unseen.

## Historical Context

In the 1890s, Fiji was a British colony with a plantation economy dependent on indentured Indian labor. The islands' strategic position on the route between [[australia|Australia]] and North America made them a regular port of call for Pacific shipping. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s decision to bypass Fiji likely reflected his eagerness to reach [[australia|Australia]] before the southern summer's cyclone season.
