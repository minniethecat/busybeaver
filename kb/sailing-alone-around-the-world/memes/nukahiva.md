# Nukahiva

Nukahiva (modern spelling: [[marquesas|Nuku Hiva]]) is the largest and southernmost of the [[marquesas|Marquesas Islands]] in the [[pacific-ocean|South Pacific]]. For [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]], it was a landmark — a proof of navigation — but not a destination. He sighted it, verified his position, and sailed on.

## The Landfall

After forty-three days alone at sea from [[juan-fernandez-island|Juan Fernandez]], the sight of land was both a relief and a vindication. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had been navigating by [[navigation|dead reckoning]] and [[sextant|lunar observation]]s, and the appearance of Nukahiva confirmed his skill: "I made the island of Nukahiva, the southernmost of the [[marquesas|Marquesas]] group, clear-cut and lofty. The verified longitude when abreast was somewhere between the two reckonings; this was extraordinary" — (011-023).

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] took quiet pride in the accuracy of his navigation. The lunar distance method was notoriously difficult, especially for a lone sailor with no assistant to steady the sextant or mark the chronometer. That his sight of Nukahiva fell between his two reckonings was a small triumph — the kind that sustains a solitary voyager's confidence across thousands of miles of empty ocean.

## The Decision Not to Land

Having reached Nukahiva, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] made a deliberate choice: he would not go ashore. "Instead of my hauling in for Nukahiva, which I could have made as well as not, I kept on for [[samoa|Samoa]], where I wished to make my next landing. This occupied twenty-nine days more, making seventy-two days in all" — (012-002).

> I was not distressed in any way during that time. There was no end of companionship; the very coral reefs kept me company, or gave me no time to feel lonely, which is the same thing, and there were many of them now in my course to Samoa.
>
> (012-002)

The decision was practical. [[samoa|Samoa]] offered friends — the [[robert-louis-stevenson|Stevenson]]s at Vailima — and a known harbor. Nukahiva, however beautiful, offered only a brief interruption. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was not a tourist; he was a mariner with a course to keep. "I was not distressed in any way during that time. There was no end of companionship; the very coral [[reefing|reef]]s kept me company, or gave me no time to feel lonely, which is the same thing" — (012-002).

## Geography

Nukahiva is a volcanic island of dramatic relief — the "clear-cut and lofty" profile that [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] described is the signature of the [[marquesas|Marquesas]], where mountains rise sharply from the sea with little coastal plain. In the 1890s, it was sparsely touched by European contact compared to [[tahiti|Tahiti]] or [[samoa|Samoa]]; its isolation was precisely what made it a useful navigation mark rather than a port of call.
