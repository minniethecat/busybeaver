# Fairhaven

Fairhaven, [[cape-ann|Massachusetts]], is the town where [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]]'s historic voyage began. It was here, across the Acushnet River from the whaling port of [[new-bedford|New Bedford]], that [[eben-pierce|Captain Eben Pierce]] gave [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] the derelict sloop *[[spray|Spray]]*, and where [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] spent thirteen months rebuilding her with his own hands.

## Geography

Fairhaven sits on the eastern bank of the Acushnet River, opposite [[new-bedford|New Bedford]], [[cape-ann|Massachusetts]]. In the 1890s, the two towns formed the heart of America's whaling industry, though the trade was in decline by the time [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] arrived (001-001).

## Role in the Voyage

### The Gift of the Spray

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] arrived in Fairhaven on a winter day in 1892, following an old whaling captain's offer: "Come to Fairhaven and I'll give you a ship." What he found was both a disappointment and an opportunity:

> "The next day I landed at Fairhaven, opposite New Bedford, and found that my friend had something of a joke on me. For seven years the joke had been on him. The 'ship' proved to be a very antiquated sloop called the *Spray*, which the neighbors declared had been built in the year 1." — (001-001)

The *[[spray|Spray]]* had been propped up in a field, covered with [[sail|canvas]], for seven years. The people of Fairhaven had long wondered what [[eben-pierce|Captain Pierce]] intended to do with the old vessel. When [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] appeared and began work, the question circulating through town changed from "Will he rebuild her?" to "Will it pay?" (001-001).

### Thirteen Months of Labor

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] spent over a year in Fairhaven, rebuilding the *[[spray|Spray]]* timber by timber. The cost of materials was $553.62, and the labor — thirteen months of it — was entirely his own. He found occasional work on whale-ships fitting out farther down the harbor to support himself during the rebuild (001-002).

The people of Fairhaven were "thrifty and observant," and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] took evident pleasure in the local character, noting the walking was good across the bridge to [[new-bedford|New Bedford]], "the home of whaling-captains" (001-002).

### The Trial Trip

In the spring of 1893, the rebuilt *[[spray|Spray]]* was ready:

> "Sails were bent, and away she flew with my friend Captain Pierce and me, across Buzzard's Bay on a trial-trip — all right." — (001-003)

The question "Will it pay?" was never answered in monetary terms. But in the annals of maritime history, Fairhaven's old sloop would prove priceless.

## History

Fairhaven was incorporated in 1812. During the nineteenth century, it shared in the prosperity of the [[new-bedford|New Bedford]] whaling industry. By the 1890s, as whaling declined, Fairhaven — like many New England maritime towns — was adapting to new economic realities. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s rebuilding of the *[[spray|Spray]]* in a Fairhaven field is one of the last great stories of New England's age of sail.
