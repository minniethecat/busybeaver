# Port Tamar

Port Tamar was a brief but pivotal anchorage for [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] in the [[strait-of-magellan|Strait of Magellan]]. It was here that he found "shelter for the night" — (007-028) before making his final run for [[cape-pillar|Cape Pillar]] and the [[pacific-ocean|Pacific Ocean]]. For a single night's stay, Port Tamar occupies an outsized place in the narrative — the last sheltered water before the open sea.

## Geography

Port Tamar lies in the western reaches of [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]], near the strait's Pacific entrance. With [[cape-pillar|Cape Pillar]] visible to the west, it was the final protected anchorage available to westbound vessels before they committed to the open ocean. The anchorage was small but sufficient for a sloop of the *[[spray|Spray]]'s* size, and the surrounding country was typical of the region — rugged, sparsely vegetated, and dominated by the weather coming off [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]].

## In the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s visit to Port Tamar was brief — a single night. He anchored with "[[cape-pillar|Cape Pillar]] in sight to the west" — (007-028), knowing that the next day would bring the decisive passage. The proximity of the cape, visible from the anchorage, must have been both a promise and a warning: [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]] lay just beyond, but the [[tide|tide-race]] off the cape was known to be ferocious.

When morning came, "the *[[spray|Spray]]* sailed from Port Tamar direct for [[cape-pillar|Cape Pillar]], with the wind from the north" — (008-002). The favorable wind was a gift, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] seized it without delay. From Port Tamar to [[cape-pillar|Cape Pillar]] the distance was short by sea, measured in hours rather than days. But those hours marked the transition from the confined, sheltered world of the strait to the vast autonomy of the open ocean.

Port Tamar, for all its insignificance on the map, was the last quiet anchorage before one of the voyage's great milestones: [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]] crossing.

## Historical Context

Like many small anchorages in [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]], Port Tamar was known to local pilots and chart-makers but saw little traffic in the age of steam. For [[sail|sailing]] vessels, however, these refuges remained essential. The strait's weather could change violently, and a secure anchorage within reach could mean the difference between safe passage and disaster. Port Tamar's value lay precisely in its position — the last shelter before [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]], and therefore the last place a prudent captain could pause, rest, and wait for the [[rigging|rig]]ht wind.

> I knew now that I had put a world behind me, and that I was opening out another world ahead. I had passed the haunts of savages.
