# Olympia

The *Olympia* was one of several steamships that spoke the *[[spray|Spray]]* during [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s Atlantic passages. Her brief appearance — "the steamship *Olympia* spoken" (003-001) — represents the network of maritime traffic that made even the solitary sailor part of a larger world.

> Porpoises gamboling all about. The S.S. *Olympia* passed at 11:30 a.m., long. W. 34° 50′.
>
> (003-013)

## Significance

Vessels like the *Olympia* appear and disappear in a line of text. But for [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], each encounter was meaningful: confirmation of position, exchange of news, a reminder that the ocean was a highway, not a wilderness.

In the era before wireless telegraphy at sea, a spoken exchange between vessels was the only way to share navigational data, learn of hazards ahead, or break the monotony of an ocean passage.

## Passage Context

The *Olympia* was spoken during [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s Atlantic crossing, a leg that had already seen the *[[spray|Spray]]* pass through the [[azores|Azores]] and prove her sea-keeping worth.

Steamship traffic across the North Atlantic was dense in the 1890s; fast liners and tramp steamers plied the routes between Europe and the Americas. A small sloop like the *[[spray|Spray]]*, [[sail|sailing]] alone, was a rare sight — one that often prompted passing vessels to alter course for a closer look and a hail.

## Maritime Communication in the 1890s

Before radio, all communication between vessels at sea relied on visual signals — flags by day, lanterns by night — or on the ancient practice of "speaking": two vessels drawing close enough for officers to shout across the water through speaking trumpets.

For a single-hander like [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], bringing the *[[spray|Spray]]* alongside a steamship for a brief exchange demanded careful seamanship. Every such encounter confirmed that the *[[spray|Spray]]* and her captain were on course and on schedule — a reassurance that mattered greatly to a man [[sail|sailing]] alone [[voyage|around the world]].
