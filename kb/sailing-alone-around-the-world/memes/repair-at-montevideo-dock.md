After the *[[spray|Spray]]* limped into [[montevideo|Montevideo]] with damage from her grounding on the [[uruguay|Uruguay]] sands, the agents of the Royal Mail Steamship Company not only docked and repaired her free of expense but also gave [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] twenty pounds sterling — an act of extraordinary generosity that exemplified the kindness [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] encountered throughout his voyage.

## Summary

When the *[[spray|Spray]]* arrived at [[montevideo|Montevideo]] after her rough grounding on the [[uruguay|Uruguay]] coast, the Royal Mail Steamship Company's agents, Messrs. Humphreys & Co., immediately offered to dock and repair her at no cost. They "did it to the letter, and more besides," also providing [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] with twenty pounds sterling. Carpenters mended the keel, caulkers made [[sloop|the sloop]] tight, and the dory was painted "till I hardly knew it from a butterfly." (006-012)

## Context

The *[[spray|Spray]]* had run hard aground on the sands of [[uruguay|Uruguay]] near Castillo Chicos. The grounding cost her "no small amount of pounding on the hard sand; she lost her shoe and part of her [[keel|false keel]], and received other damage" (006-004). Although [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] managed to refloat her with local help and sail her to [[montevideo|Montevideo]] under her own power, the vessel needed professional attention before she could continue south toward [[strait-of-magellan|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]].

[[montevideo|Montevideo]] was a major port and a welcoming one for [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]. He had previously forwarded a letter of introduction from Sir Edward Hairby to Mr. Mulhall, owner of the *Standard* newspaper, and was assured of a warm reception.

## Narrative

The *[[spray|Spray]]* had barely dropped anchor at [[montevideo|Montevideo]] when extraordinary help arrived:

> "The *Spray* had barely come to anchor at Montevideo when the agents of the Royal Mail Steamship Company, Messrs. Humphreys & Co., sent word that they would dock and repair her free of expense and give me twenty pounds sterling, which, they did to the letter, and more besides." — (006-012)

The work was thorough. Caulkers at [[montevideo|Montevideo]] "paid very careful attention to the work of making [[sloop|the sloop]] tight" (006-012). Carpenters mended the damaged keel — the result of the pounding she had taken on the hard [[uruguay|Uruguay]] sand. They also repaired the lifeboat, the little dory that was [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s shore transport:

> "Carpenters mended the keel and also the lifeboat (the dory), painting it till I hardly knew it from a butterfly." — (006-012)

While the repairs were underway, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was welcomed ashore by Mr. Mulhall, who "with a prancing team, came down to the docks as soon as the *[[spray|Spray]]* was berthed, and would have me go to his house at once, where a room was waiting. And it was New Year's day, 1896" (006-013). The *Standard* had been following the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s voyage in its columns.

## Aftermath

The repairs made at [[montevideo|Montevideo]] put the *[[spray|Spray]]* in condition to face the most dangerous waters of the voyage — [[strait-of-magellan|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] and [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]]. The thorough caulking was particularly important for the southern ocean passage. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] would later recall this generosity as typical of the reception he received in South American ports. Before departing, he shortened the mast and bowsprit to make [[sloop|the sloop]] more manageable in the [[gale|heavy weather]] ahead (006-001).

From [[montevideo|Montevideo]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] also made an excursion to [[buenos-aires|Buenos Aires]], enjoying a brief respite before the challenges of [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]].

## Significance

This event highlights the extraordinary goodwill [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] encountered during his voyage. The Royal Mail Steamship Company had no obligation to help a lone American sailor in a small sloop, yet they not only repaired his vessel free of charge but gave him money. This pattern of unsolicited kindness — from Admiral Bruce at [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]] to Captain [[samblich|Samblich]] at [[sandy-point|Sandy Point]] to Humphreys & Co. at [[montevideo|Montevideo]] — was a recurring feature of the [[voyage|circumnavigation]] and contributed materially to its success.
