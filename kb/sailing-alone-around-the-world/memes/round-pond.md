## Geography

Round Pond is a small natural harbor on the coast of Maine, situated east of Pemaquid Point and south of Damariscotta. The harbor is surrounded by pine-covered shores and offers good shelter for small vessels from prevailing winds. In the 1890s it was a quiet fishing community, typical of the many small ports that dotted the Maine coast and served as havens for coasting vessels.

## The Coastal Passage Begins

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] departed [[gloucester|Gloucester, Massachusetts]], on May 7, 1895, under mild weather. (002-011) The sloop worked out of [[gloucester|Gloucester]] Bay against a light southwest wind and about noon squared away off Eastern Point, receiving a hearty salute from well-wishers ashore — "the last of many kindnesses to her at [[gloucester|Gloucester]]." (002-011) Shaping his course eastward, he planned to go north of Cashes Ledge and the Amen Rocks, all the while privately weighing whether the voyage ahead was truly wise. (002-011)

That evening, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] fished on the edge of Cashes Ledge in thirty fathoms of water, [[current|set]]ting out a [[anchor|sea-anchor]] to hold [[spray|the Spray]] head to windward while he took on a stock of fresh provisions. (002-011) It was during this night at anchor that the famous "whale dream" occurred:

> I had read somewhere of a fishing-schooner hooking her anchor into a whale, and being towed a long way and at great speed. (002-012)

This fancy, mixed with the reality of a rising gale, convinced him he had been towed by a whale in his sleep. When he awoke to [[gale|storm]] conditions, he acted decisively.

## In Slocum's Voyage

As the weather worsened, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] made for shelter:

> I reefed the sails, then hauled in my sea-anchor, and setting what canvas the sloop could carry, headed her away for Monhegan light, which she made before daylight on the morning of the 8th. The wind being free, I ran on into Round Pond harbor, which is a little port east from Pemaquid. (002-012)

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] rested at Round Pond while a gale blew through:

> Here I rested a day, while the wind rattled among the pine-trees on shore. But the following day was fine enough, and I put to sea, first writing up my log from Cape Ann, not omitting a full account of my adventure with the whale. (002-012)

The "adventure with the whale" is a delightful piece of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s storytelling — the half-dream of being towed by a whale, which he knew to be fanciful yet recorded with good humor. It [[current|set]]s the [[tonnage|ton]]e for the entire narrative: a lone sailor who maintains his composure and wit even when the sea tests him.

## Historical Context

The Maine coast in the 1890s retained much of its character from the great age of sail. Small harbors like Round Pond served local fishing fleets and provided refuge for coasting vessels caught in bad weather. The Maine island communities — Monhegan, Matinicus, and the many unnamed ledges and rocks — were familiar waypoints for mariners making the passage between New England and the Canadian Maritimes.

Round Pond's proximity to Pemaquid, one of the earliest European [[current|set]]tlement sites in North America, placed it in a region rich with maritime history. By [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s time, this stretch of the Maine coast had been navigated by fishing schooners and coasting sloops for more than two centuries. (002-012)

## Significance

Round Pond represents the intimate rhythm of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s coastal passage — pressing on when weather permitted, taking shelter when it did not. This early stop, just a day into his solo voyage, demonstrates the cautious seamanship that would carry [[spray|the Spray]] safely [[voyage|around the world]]. Rather than push through a gale on his first night alone, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sensibly sought shelter and waited for conditions to improve.

The image of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] resting while "the wind rattled among the pine-trees on shore" captures a peaceful interlude before the long ocean passages that lay ahead. (002-012) The whale dream, too, reveals [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s literary sensibility — he was already shaping his experiences into the narrative that would become *[[sail|Sail]]ing Alone Around the World*.
