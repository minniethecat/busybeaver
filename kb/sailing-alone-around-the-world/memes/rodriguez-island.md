# Rodriguez Island

Rodriguez Island (now Rodrigues) is a small volcanic island in the [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]], about 350 miles east of [[mauritius|Mauritius]], of which it is a dependency. Remote even by [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]] standards, it was one of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s most pleasant stops — a place of genuine hospitality after long ocean passages.

## Geography

The island is about 11 miles long by 5 miles wide, surrounded by coral [[reefing|reef]]s that form a shallow lagoon. The interior is hilly, rising to 1,300 feet at Mont Limon. The climate is tropical and pleasant, tempered by [[trade-wind|trade winds]]. The island was uninhabited until the 17th century; in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s time, it supported a small colonial community.

## In the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] arrived at Rodriguez after a long passage across the [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]], making the *Spray* "securely fast to a pier in Rodriguez" (016-028). The governor, [[governor-roberts|Mr. Roberts]], and his family "did everything in their power to make my stay pleasant" (016-027). The island was a revelation of colonial hospitality: fresh water ([[governor-roberts|Governor Roberts]] had built a reservoir), good food, and the company of educated people after weeks of solitude. On the first evening ashore, Slocum found himself "in a bright hall, surrounded by sparkling wit, and dining with the governor of the island" (016-028).

> My first day at this Land of Promise was to me like a fairytale. For many days I had studied the charts and counted the time of my arrival at this spot, as one might his entrance to the Islands of the Blessed, looking upon it as the terminus of the last long run, made irksome by the want of many things with which, from this time on, I could keep well supplied. And behold, here was the sloop, arrived, and made securely fast to a pier in Rodriguez.
>
> (016-028)

The stop was restorative. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] explored the island, enjoyed the Roberts family's hospitality, and left with fresh provisions and renewed spirits. The contrast with the wild waters of [[strait-of-magellan|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] could not have been greater.

## Significance

Rodriguez is the archetype of the hospitable island — remote, small, and utterly welcoming. In the geography of the voyage, it stands opposite [[tierra-del-fuego|Tierra del Fuego]]: where the one threatened, the other embraced.
