## The Vessel

During his Pacific passage, Slocum encountered a vessel named the *Southern Cross*. He recorded that "he in the *Southern Cross* and I in the *Northern Light*" — the two ships being together at that point in their respective voyages (009-012). The brief mention suggests a passing encounter typical of the era of sail, when ships would occasionally meet and exchange news on the open ocean.

## The Constellation

The Southern Cross (Crux) is one of the most prominent constellations of the southern sky and served as a vital navigational reference for Slocum. He wrote that "The Southern Cross I saw every night abeam" (011-019), indicating that the constellation was a constant companion during his southern hemisphere passages. For a solo sailor navigating by the stars, the Southern Cross was an essential celestial marker.
> For one whole month my vessel held her course true; I had not, the while, so much as a light in the binnacle. The Southern Cross I saw every night abeam. The sun every morning came up astern; every evening it went down ahead. I wished for no other compass to guide me, for these were true. If I doubted my reckoning after a long time at sea I verified it by reading the clock aloft made by the Great Architect, and it was right.
>
> (011-019)
