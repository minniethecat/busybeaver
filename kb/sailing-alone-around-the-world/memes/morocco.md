**Morocco** is a country on the northwest coast of Africa, separated from [[spain|Spain]] by [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]]. During his stay at [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]] in August 1895, [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]] was invited on a naval excursion to the Morocco shore, though the party never actually landed there.

## Gibraltar Excursion

Before [[sail|sailing]] from [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was invited on a picnic with the governor, officers of the garrison, and commanders of the warships stationed there. "Torpedo-boat No. 91, going twenty-two [[knot|knots]], carried our party to the Morocco shore and back" (004-025).

The vessel was commanded by Sublieutenant Boucher, whom [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] described as "apparently a mere lad" who nevertheless "handled his ship with the skill of an older sailor" (004-025). Despite the thrilling ride across the strait at top speed, the day did not include a landing. "The day was perfect — too fine, in fact, for comfort on shore, and so no one landed at Morocco" (004-025).

> No. 91 trembled like an aspen-leaf as she raced through the sea at top speed. Sublieutenant Boucher, apparently a mere lad, was in command, and handled his ship with the skill of an older sailor.
>
> (004-025)

## The Torpedo Boat

Torpedo Boat No. 91 represented the cutting edge of naval technology in the 1890s. Capable of twenty-two [[knot|knots]] — an extraordinary speed in an era when most [[sail|sailing]] vessels made five to [[knot|eight knots]] — these small, fast craft were designed to attack larger warships with the newly developed self-propelled torpedo.

The contrast was sharp: [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], about to embark on a solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]] under sail, was being entertained by officers of a vessel that represented the future of naval warfare. The torpedo boat's speed across the strait foreshadowed the mechanized navy that would soon relegate [[sail|sailing]] ships to obsolescence.

## Maritime Context

Morocco appears indirectly in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s narrative after he left [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]]. While at [[pernambuco|Pernambuco]], Brazil, he shortened the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s boom, "which had been broken when off the coast of Morocco" (005-029). The repair removed about four feet from the inboard end. This suggests [[sloop|the sloop]] encountered rough conditions along the African coast shortly after departing [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]].
