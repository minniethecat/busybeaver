> I am a citizen of the United States — a naturalized Yankee, if it may be said that Nova Scotians are not Yankees in the truest sense of the word.
>
> (001-002)

## Slocum's Citizenship

Joshua Slocum, though born in Nova Scotia, was a naturalized citizen of the United States, which he described himself as "a naturalized Yankee" (001-002). The *Spray* flew the American flag throughout the circumnavigation, and Slocum relied on the United States consular network for assistance at many ports of call.

## Consular Support During the Voyage

United States consuls played a vital role in Slocum's journey. At Gibraltar, the "venerable United States consul," Horatio J. Sprague, honored him (004-025). At Juan Fernandez, six young men came down in the United States consul general's boat, singing, to welcome him (012-018). At Apia, the first visitor on board was the United States consul, Mr. Brown (013-010). When the *Spray* cleared from the United States consulate at the end of her voyage, her license was recorded (021-002).

## Related to the Voyage

The United States government also intersected with Slocum's story through the Spanish-American War — the ship *Oregon*, purchased by the United States government during that conflict, "flew the American flag" (005-025). Slocum was unaware that "war with Spain had been declared" while at sea (020-004).
