## Slocum's Visit

Slocum visited Longwood during his stay on [[st-helena|St. Helena]] in April 1898. After paying his respects to Governor Sterndale at Plantation House, he "drove with Mr. Clark, a countryman of mine, to 'Longwood,' the home of Napoleon" (019-005). At the time of Slocum's visit, the French consular agent M. Morilleau was in charge of the property, keeping it "respectable and the buildings in good repair." His family "spend here days, months, and years of contentment, though they have never seen the world beyond the horizon of St. Helena" (019-005).

The excursion to Longwood was one of several activities during Slocum's stay on St. Helena, which also included delivering lectures at Garden Hall in Jamestown and at Plantation House (019-001) (019-002).

> Returning from the governor's house to Jamestown, I drove with Mr. Clark, a countryman of mine, to "Longwood," the home of Napoleon. M. Morilleau, French consular agent in charge, keeps the place respectable and the buildings in good repair. His family at Longwood, consisting of wife and grown daughters, are natives of courtly and refined manners, and spend here days, months, and years of contentment, though they have never seen the world beyond the horizon of St. Helena.
>
> (019-005)
