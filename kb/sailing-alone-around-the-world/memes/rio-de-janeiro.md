# Rio de Janeiro

Rio de Janeiro, the capital of Brazil at the time of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s visit, was an important port of call during the early Atlantic stages of the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s [[voyage|circumnavigation]]. Here [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] reprovisioned, made repairs, and investigated the fate of the *[[destroyer|Destroyer]]*, a warship he had once commanded.

## Geography

Rio de Janeiro sits on the southeastern coast of Brazil, on a spectacular natural harbor — Guanabara Bay — ringed by mountains including Sugarloaf and Corcovado. In 1895, when [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] arrived, it was the capital of the Brazilian Republic and one of the busiest ports in the Americas (004-001).

## Role in the Voyage

### Arrival and the Destroyer

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] arrived at Rio on November 5, 1895, after a pleasant coastal passage from [[pernambuco|Pernambuco]], "making about one hundred miles a day along the coast" (004-001). At Rio, he pursued a personal matter: the fate of the *[[destroyer|Destroyer]]*, a Brazilian warship he had once commanded:

> "On the following day I bestirred myself to meet the highest lord of the admiralty and the ministers, to inquire concerning the matter of wages due me from the beloved *Destroyer*. The high official I met said: 'Captain, so far as we are concerned, you may have the ship, and if you care to accept her we will send an officer to show you where she is.' I knew well enough where she was at that moment. The top of her smokestack being awash in Bahia, it was more than likely that she rested on the bottom there." — (004-001)

### Preparation and Gifts

At Rio, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] prepared the *[[spray|Spray]]* for the dangerous passage ahead. He decided to give [[sloop|the sloop]] "a yawl [[rigging|rig]] for the [[storm|tempest]]uous waters of [[patagonia|Patagonia]]" and placed "on the stern a semicircular brace to support a jigger mast" (004-002).

A group of old shipmasters inspected the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s [[rigging|rig]]ging and contributed to her outfit:

> "Captain Jones, who had acted as my interpreter at Rio, gave her an anchor, and one of the steamers gave her a cable to match it. She never dragged Jones's anchor once on the voyage." — (004-002)

### Departure and Aftermath

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sailed from Rio on November 28, 1895, "and first of all ran into a gale of wind, which tore up things generally along the coast." Shortly after, on December 11, the *[[spray|Spray]]* ran aground on the sands of [[uruguay|Uruguay]] — an accident [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] attributed to being deceived by "the false appearance of the sand-hills under a bright moon" (005-001).

## History

Rio de Janeiro was founded in 1565 and served as Brazil's capital from 1763 to 1960. In the 1890s, it was a rapidly modernizing city with a strong maritime tradition. The international community of shipmasters who assisted [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] there reflects Rio's role as a global maritime crossroads.
