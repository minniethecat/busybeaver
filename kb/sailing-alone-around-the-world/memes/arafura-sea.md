# Arafura Sea

A shallow tropical sea lying between northern [[australia|Australia]] and Indonesia, through which the *[[spray|Spray]]* sailed in mid-1897 during [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s long passage toward the [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]].

## Geography

The Arafura Sea connects the [[coral-sea|Coral Sea]] to the east with the Timor Sea and [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]] to the west. Its shallow waters, particularly in the eastern reaches, are known for dramatic color variations. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] described [[sail|sailing]] for days "in water milky white and green and purple" (015-031). The area is rich in marine life, including sea-snakes and flying fish, and forms part of the complex archipelagic waters between [[australia|Australia]] and Southeast Asia.

## Role in the Voyage

The *[[spray|Spray]]* entered the Arafura Sea after passing [[booby-island|Booby Island]] and the [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]], on the last quarter of the moon (015-031). [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] noted the advantage of dark nights at this lunar phase, as it allowed him to witness "the phosphorescent light effect at night in its greatest splendor." He wrote that "the sea, where [[sloop|the sloop]] disturbed it, seemed all ablaze, so that by its light I could see the smallest articles [[deck|on deck]], and her wake was a path of fire" (015-031).

In the shallower parts of the Arafura, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] observed sea-snakes "writhed about on the surface and tumbled over and over in the waves," noting that they disappeared entirely once [[sloop|the sloop]] reached deeper, blue ocean water (015-033).

Before entering the Arafura, the *[[spray|Spray]]* had been hauled out at [[devonport|Devonport]], where her bottom was "coated once more with copper paint, for she would have to sail through the Coral and Arafura seas before refitting again" (014-013). This was a deliberate precaution against the teredo worm, a wood-boring marine pest common in tropical waters.

> There is a light today on Booby Island, and regular packet communication with the rest of the world, and the beautiful uncertainty of the fate of letters left there is a thing of the past. I made no call at the little island, but standing close in, exchanged signals with the keeper of the light. Sailing on, the sloop was at once in the Arafura Sea, where for days she sailed in water milky white and green and purple. It was my good fortune to enter the sea on the last quarter of the moon, the advantage being that in the dark nights I witnessed the phosphorescent light effect at night in its greatest splendor. The sea, where the sloop disturbed it, seemed all ablaze, so that by its light I could see the smallest articles on deck, and her wake was a path of fire.
>
> (015-031)

## Historical Context

In the late 19th century, the Arafura Sea was part of the established [[sail|sailing]] route from [[australia|Australia]] to the [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]]. The waters were frequented by pearling luggers, trading vessels, and the occasional long-distance yachtsman. The sea's shallow depth and complex currents required careful navigation, particularly for a small vessel like the *[[spray|Spray]]*.
