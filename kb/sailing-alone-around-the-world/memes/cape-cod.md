Cape Cod is a hook-shaped peninsula extending into [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] Ocean from the southeastern corner of [[cape-ann|Massachusetts]]. It appears in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s narrative through two maritime acquaintances — [[captain-howard|Captain Howard]], who sailed with him to [[buenos-aires|Buenos Aires]], and a government sea-captain who, after seeing the *[[spray|Spray]]*, dreamed of returning to sea.

## Geography

Cape Cod extends roughly 65 miles into [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]], forming Cape Cod Bay to the north. The cape is known for its fishing grounds, sandy beaches, and a long maritime tradition that dates to the earliest colonial period. At the time of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s voyage in the 1890s, the cape was already a well-established fishing and seafaring region.

## Role in the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] never visited Cape Cod during his [[voyage|circumnavigation]], but the cape appears through the character of [[captain-howard|Captain Howard]], a friend from [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s earlier days:

> "An old friend of mine, Captain Howard of Cape Cod and of River Plate fame, took the trip in her to Buenos Aires, where she arrived early on the following day, with a gale of wind and a current so much in her favor that she outdid herself." — (006-014)

[[captain-howard|Howard]], an experienced pilot-sloop captain, was the witness to one of the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s most impressive demonstrations: [[sail|sailing]] with no living being at the [[rudder|helm]]. [[captain-howard|Howard]] watched the compass while [[sloop|the sloop]] held her course without deviating "a quarter of a point." He was so impressed by this feat that "he cried, 'I'll be stranded on Chico Bank if ever I saw the like of it!'" (006-014).

The cape appears once more at the conclusion of the voyage, when another seasoned sea-captain friend examined the *[[spray|Spray]]*:

> "My friend the government expert, and saltest of salt sea-captains, standing only yesterday on the deck of the *Spray*, was convinced of her famous qualities, and he spoke enthusiastically of selling his farm on Cape Cod and putting to sea again." — (022-018)

This unnamed captain's reaction forms the final image of the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s account — a testament to [[sloop|the sloop]]'s qualities so compelling that a man [[current|set]]tled on land would consider giving up his farm to return to the ocean.

## Maritime Context

Cape Cod in the late nineteenth century was home to a generation of deep-water sailors whose careers spanned the transition from sail to steam. [[captain-howard|Captain Howard]]'s "River Plate fame" indicates experience on the busy trade route between New England and Argentina — a route [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] himself knew well from his earlier command of merchant vessels.

The "government expert" [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] refers to represents another type: the seasoned captain who had transitioned to shore-based work but whose instincts remained at sea. His reaction to the *[[spray|Spray]]* — the urge to sell his farm and sail again — captures the pull that the ocean held over men of that generation.
