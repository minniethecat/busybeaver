## Specifications

The S.S. *[[olympia-steamship|Olympia]]* was a steamship operating in the North Atlantic during the 1890s. She was a passenger and mail steamer, of the type that maintained scheduled transatlantic service between Europe and the Americas. Her precise build date and [[tonnage|ton]]nage are not recorded in [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]]'s narrative; she appears only in a single encounter that left a lasting impression on the circumnavigator.

## Encounter with the Spray

On July 18, 1895, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was [[sail|sailing]] the *[[spray|Spray]]* through the North Atlantic toward the [[azores|Azores]] when the *[[olympia-steamship|Olympia]]* crossed his path. The steamship passed at 11:30 a.m., at longitude west 34° 50′ (003-013). Porpoises were gamboling about the *[[spray|Spray]]*, but [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] noted that none accompanied the steamship — a detail he attributed to the preference of porpoises for [[sail|sailing]] vessels.

> The S.S. Olympia passed at 11:30 a.m., long. W. 34° 50′.
> (003-013)

The *[[olympia-steamship|Olympia]]*'s captain hailed [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], giving him the exact longitude and time: it lacked three minutes of the half-hour (003-014). [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] admired the businesslike precision of the steamship, but privately reflected that such overconfidence had been the cause of the *Atlantic* disaster and other wrecks.

> "It lacks now three minutes of the half-hour," shouted the captain, as he gave me the longitude and the time. I admired the businesslike air of the Olympia; but I have the feeling still that the captain was just a little too precise in his reckoning.
> (003-014)

## Slocum's Reflection

The encounter crystallized a seamanship philosophy that ran through [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s entire voyage. The *[[olympia-steamship|Olympia]]*, with her scheduled routes and precise coordinates, represented the modern, mechanized approach to the sea. The *[[spray|Spray]]*, by contrast, sailed by feel and observation. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] did not doubt the *[[olympia-steamship|Olympia]]*'s captain knew exactly where he was — but he wondered whether so much certainty left room for the sea's unpredictability.

## A Study in Contrasts

The *[[olympia-steamship|Olympia]]* encounter appears in a chapter whose [[course|heading]] itself encapsulates the transition between worlds: "The [[olympia-steamship|steamship Olympia]] spoken — Arrival at the [[azores|Azores]]" (003-001). [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] places the steamship alongside other Atlantic encounters — the Spanish barque *La Vaguisa*, whose captain sent over a bottle of wine, and the *[[java-steamship|Java]]*, whose master provoked "a bout of words." Each vessel represented a different relationship to the sea. The *[[olympia-steamship|Olympia]]* stood for the new age of steam: punctual, precise, and entirely indifferent to the porpoises that skipped alongside the *[[spray|Spray]]* alone (003-014).

> There were no porpoises at all skipping along with the Olympia!
> (003-014)

For [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], the porpoises' preference was a small vindication. The creatures of the sea, he implied, recognized something in the *[[spray|Spray]]* that the *[[olympia-steamship|Olympia]]* — for all her precision — had lost. It was a romantic notion, but one that [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], alone on [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] with his [[helm|tiller]] in hand, was inclined to believe.

## Significance

The *[[olympia-steamship|Olympia]]* encounter serves as a foil to the *[[spray|Spray]]* in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s narrative: the great steamship versus the little sloop, precision navigation versus coastal instinct, scheduled service versus solitary wandering. This contrast runs through *[[sail|Sail]]ing Alone Around the World* as a recurring theme.
