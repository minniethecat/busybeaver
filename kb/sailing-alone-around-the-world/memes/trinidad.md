The island of Trinidad, in the southern [[west-indies|Caribbean Sea]], served as a navigational landmark for Slocum during his [[atlantic-ocean|Atlantic]] return.

## Landfall and Approach

Slocum sighted the revolving light on Trinidad, thirty miles away, which threw its beam across the sea (020-009). He described the light on Trinidad as "a charming introduction to Grenada" (020-001), indicating his passage through the Windward Islands as he neared the end of his voyage. After passing Trinidad, Slocum set his course for [[grenada]], to which he carried letters of introduction from [[south-africa]] (020-010).

> I fell back in a coil of rope, speechless and amazed, not distressed, but rejoiced. Aladdin's lamp! My fisherman's own lantern! It was the great revolving light on the island of Trinidad, thirty miles away, throwing flashes over the waves, which had deceived me! The orb of the light was now dipping on the horizon, and how glorious was the sight of it!
>
> (020-009)
