## Sighting Spain

On the morning of August 4 during his Atlantic passage toward the Mediterranean, Joshua Slocum "discovered Spain." He saw fires on shore and knew he had reached the European continent (004-016). This sighting marked a significant milestone in the early stages of his circumnavigation — his first encounter with the Old World since departing from the Americas.

## The Spanish-American War

While Slocum was at sea, the Spanish-American War broke out. He later noted: "I did not know that war with Spain had been declared" (020-004), highlighting his isolation from world events during the long ocean passages. The war also touched the narrative through the ship *Oregon*, which had been "purchased by the United States government during the Spanish war" (005-025).

> Early the next morning, August 4, I discovered Spain. I saw fires on shore, and knew that the country was inhabited.
>
> (004-016)
