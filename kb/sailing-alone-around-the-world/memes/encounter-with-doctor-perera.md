During the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s stay in [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio de Janeiro]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] renewed his acquaintance with Dr. Perera, the owner and editor of *El Commercio Jornal* and a "very enthusiastic yachtsman." The doctor came to visit the *[[spray|Spray]]* at her mooring in Upper Topsail Reach, then carried [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] to his country residence by water — an estate guarded by a remarkable fleet of small boats including a Chinese sampan, a Norwegian pram, and a [[gloucester|Cape Ann]] dory. The encounter exemplified the warm hospitality [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] found among the international community in Brazil, where old acquaintances and yachting enthusiasts alike embraced the solitary circumnavigator.

## Summary

After the *[[spray|Spray]]* was safely moored in [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio de Janeiro]]'s Upper Topsail Reach, Dr. Perera — an old acquaintance from [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s earlier visits to Brazil — came to pay a visit and brought [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] by water to his country residence (005-026). The approach to the doctor's mansion was guarded by his "armada" of small craft: a Chinese sampan, a Norwegian pram, and a [[gloucester|Cape Ann]] dory (the last obtained from the American warship *[[destroyer|Destroyer]]*) (005-026). Dr. Perera dined [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] often on good Brazilian fare, explaining that he wished the sailor to *salle gordo* — to fatten up — for the voyage ahead. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] noted with characteristic humor that "even on the best I fattened slowly" (005-026).

## Context

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was no stranger to Brazil. Having crossed [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] twice before his [[voyage|circumnavigation]], he found himself "still among friends" at [[pernambuco|Pernambuco]] and [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio]] (005-024). His decision to sail westward from [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]] — abandoning the [[suez|Suez Canal]] route on the advice of British officers — had brought him to familiar shores in South America rather than into the pirate-infested [[red-sea|Red Sea]] (005-024). [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio de Janeiro]], the capital of the Brazilian Republic, was a major port with a substantial international community, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had prior connections there from his merchant [[sail|sailing]] days.

The *[[spray|Spray]]*'s arrival in Brazil was part of the coastal passage that had included the stranding on the [[uruguay|Uruguay]]an sands and the warm reception at [[montevideo|Montevideo]]. By the time she reached [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio]], [[sloop|the sloop]] had already established her reputation as a capable blue-water vessel.

## Narrative

Dr. Perera was more than a newspaper editor — he was an enthusiast of small craft with an eclectic collection:

> "The approach to his mansion by the waterside was guarded by his armada, a fleet of boats including a Chinese sampan, a Norwegian pram, and a Cape Ann dory, the last of which he obtained from the *Destroyer*." — (005-026)

The doctor's collection reflected the cosmopolitan character of [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio]]'s port — boats from three continents assembled at one Brazilian waterside estate. The [[gloucester|Cape Ann]] dory, in particular, would have been a familiar sight to [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], who had built the *[[spray|Spray]]* himself in Massachusetts and knew the New England boatbuilding tradition intimately.

Dr. Perera's hospitality was practical as well as generous. Knowing the privations of a long single-handed voyage, he "dined me often on good Brazilian fare, that I might, as he said, *salle gordo* for the voyage" (005-026). The Portuguese-inflected Spanish phrase *salle gordo* — "go out fat" — captured the doctor's warm concern for the solitary sailor's wellbeing. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], ever lean and hardened by years at sea, observed drily that "even on the best I fattened slowly" (005-026).

## Aftermath

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s stay in [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio]] concluded on October 23, 1895, when he unmoored and made ready for departure. The departure was complicated by a bureaucratic entanglement — the collector of customs charged the *[[spray|Spray]]* [[tonnage|tonnage dues]] despite her yacht license (005-027). A merchant named Mr. Lungrin offered to help by [[cargo|freight]]ing the *[[spray|Spray]]* with a cargo of gunpowder for [[bahia|Bahia]], even offering to ship without insurance when the insurance companies refused to cover cargo on a vessel "manned by a crew of only one" (005-028). [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] described this as "perhaps paying me a greater compliment than I deserved" (005-028).

The encounter with Dr. Perera, [[current|set]] amid these practical difficulties, stood out as a moment of genuine warmth and yachting fellowship. It was one of many such encounters that punctuated the voyage — moments where [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], far from being the lonely circumnavigator, was drawn into the social life of the ports he visited.

## Significance

The encounter with Dr. Perera illustrates the international network of yachtsmen, editors, and maritime enthusiasts who recognized the significance of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s voyage and offered him hospitality. Dr. Perera's eclectic boat collection — a Chinese sampan alongside a [[gloucester|Cape Ann]] dory — also spoke to the global connections of the maritime world at the turn of the century. For [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], these moments of shore-side comfort were brief but essential: they provided the physical and moral sustenance that carried him through the solitary months at sea.
