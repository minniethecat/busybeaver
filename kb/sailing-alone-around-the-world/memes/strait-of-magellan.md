# Strait of Magellan

The Strait of Magellan is the narrow, 350-mile passage that separates the South American mainland from the islands of [[tierra-del-fuego|Tierra del Fuego]]. Discovered by [[ferdinand-magellan|Ferdinand Magellan]] in 1520, it remained for centuries the only safe (relatively speaking) passage between [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] and Pacific. For [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], navigating the strait alone in a 37-foot sloop was the supreme test of the voyage — and very nearly its end.

## Geography

The strait runs roughly east–west, connecting [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] to [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]] through a labyrinth of channels, bays, and narrows. Its width varies from two to 20 miles. The surrounding terrain is mountainous and largely barren — "a gloomy region" (005-018) in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s words. The weather is notoriously violent: williwaws (katabatic winds) roar down from the mountains without warning, currents run at several [[knot|knots]], and squalls appear from every quarter. The western entrance is particularly treacherous, with rocks, [[reefing|reef]]s, and kelp beds that have claimed many vessels.

## In the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s transit of the strait was the most harrowing leg of his [[voyage|circumnavigation]]. He entered from [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] and made his way westward against fierce currents and through violent squalls. The strait tested every aspect of his seamanship: navigation through uncharted channels, [[anchor|anchoring]] in wild coves, reading the weather in a place where it changed by the minute.

> On February 11 the Spray rounded Cape Virgins and entered the Strait of Magellan. The scene was again real and gloomy; the wind, northeast, and blowing a gale, sent feather-white spume along the coast; such a sea ran as would swamp an ill-appointed ship.
>
> (007-007)

The dangers were not only natural. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s encounter with [[black-pedro|Black Pedro]] occurred here, and the threat of [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n raiders was a constant concern. "Kelp waved forebodingly under her keel, and the wreck of a great steamer lay on the beach" (007-007) — a sobering reminder of the strait's toll.

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s first attempt to transit the strait westward failed; he was forced back by weather and currents. His second attempt succeeded, but the experience left its mark. The strait chapters are the most intense in the narrative — a solo sailor in the most dangerous waters of the voyage, relying on skill, courage, and the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s remarkable qualities.

## Significance

The Strait of Magellan is the pivot of the voyage — the passage from [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] world to [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]], from the known to the immense. Every circumnavigator since [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] had faced it, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s solitary transit belongs in that lineage. The strait tested him as nothing else did, and he emerged — barely — into [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]].
