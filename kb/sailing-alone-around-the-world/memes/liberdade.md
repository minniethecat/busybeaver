# Liberdade

The *Liberdade* was a 35-foot [[sail|sailing]] canoe — essentially a large, decked dugout with a junk-[[rigging|rig]]ged sail — built by [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]], his wife, and their two sons on a Brazilian beach after the wreck of the *[[aquidneck|Aquidneck]]*. In this improbable vessel, the [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] family sailed 5,500 miles from Brazil to Washing[[tonnage|ton]], D.C., navigating the South American coast, the Caribbean, and the eastern seaboard of the United States. The voyage of the *Liberdade* was the dress rehearsal for the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s [[voyage|circumnavigation]].

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] summarized this extraordinary chapter of his life with characteristic understatement in the opening of *[[sail|Sail]]ing Alone Around the World*:

> "My home voyage to New York with my family was made in the canoe *Liberdade*, without accident." — (001-004)

The compression is remarkable: a 5,500-mile voyage in a hand-built canoe with a wife and two young children aboard is dispatched in a single subordinate clause. The phrase "without accident" carries the weight of the entire undertaking — the [[gale|storm]]s weathered, the landfalls made, the family kept safe through a journey that, in any other age, would have been the subject of an epic.

## Design and Construction

Built from the wreckage of the *[[aquidneck|Aquidneck]]* and local Brazilian timber, the *Liberdade* was a testament to [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s resourcefulness. She was a [[sail|sailing]] canoe — long and narrow, with a shallow draft and a simple [[rigging|rig]]. The construction was crude but effective: a dugout hull, decked over, with a mast and sail stepped amidships. The family built her on the beach, launched her through the surf, and [[current|set]] out with little more than a compass and their knowledge of the sea.

## The Voyage

The voyage of the *Liberdade* — from Brazil to the Potomac River — took the [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] family along the coast of South America, through the Caribbean islands, and up [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] coast of the United States. It was a journey of 5,500 miles in a vessel smaller than most lifeboats. The family endured [[gale|storm]]s, calms, and the constant proximity of each other in a space of 35 feet. But they arrived safely, and the voyage made [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s name in the small world of maritime adventurers.

## Legacy

The *Liberdade* was the prototype for the *[[spray|Spray]]*. The lessons [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] learned — about small-boat handling, about the reliability of simple [[rigging|rig]]s, about the possibility of ocean voyaging without a crew — were directly applied to the [[voyage|circumnavigation]]. The *Liberdade* proved that a small boat, in capable hands, could cross oceans. The *[[spray|Spray]]* proved it could do so alone.

In the narrative arc of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s life, the *Liberdade* stands between the catastrophe of the *[[aquidneck|Aquidneck]]* and the triumph of the *[[spray|Spray]]*. She was built from loss and sailed toward home — a vessel of transition, literally and figuratively. That [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] could build her, command her, and bring his family safely across an ocean in her is, in its own way, as remarkable as the solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]] that followed.
