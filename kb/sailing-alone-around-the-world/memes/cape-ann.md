Cape Ann is a rocky peninsula on the northeastern coast of Massachusetts, about 30 miles northeast of [[boston|Boston]]. It appears twice in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s narrative: as the place where he began composing his voyage log, and as the namesake of the dory boat type that appeared in a Brazilian doctor's eclectic fleet.

## Geography

Cape Ann is formed primarily of granite and extends into [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] Ocean, with [[gloucester|Gloucester]] — one of America's oldest fishing ports — on its southern shore. The cape is bounded by Ipswich Bay to the north and [[massachusetts-bay|Massachusetts Bay]] to the south. At the time of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s departure in 1895, the region was a thriving center of the New England fishing industry.

## Role in the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s first reference to Cape Ann comes early in the voyage, shortly after his dramatic encounter with a whale off the New England coast. Having weathered a [[gale|storm]] overnight, he made for [[round-pond|Round Pond]] harbor to rest:

> "The following day was fine enough, and I put to sea, first writing up my log from Cape Ann, not omitting a full account of my adventure with the whale." — (002-012)

This marks Cape Ann as the place where [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] began the written record of his [[voyage|circumnavigation]] — the log that would later become *[[sail|Sail]]ing Alone Around the World*.

The cape's second appearance comes during [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s stay in Brazil. Dr. Perera, a Brazilian newspaper editor and yachting enthusiast, maintained an eclectic collection of boats at his country estate:

> "The approach to his mansion by the waterside was guarded by his armada, a fleet of boats including a Chinese sampan, a Norwegian pram, and a Cape Ann dory, the last of which he obtained from the Destroyer." — (005-026)

The Cape Ann dory was a type of small fishing boat developed on the Massachusetts coast — flat-bottomed, stable, and well suited to the rough waters of the North Atlantic. Its appearance in Dr. Perera's Brazilian collection is a testament to how widely New England boat designs had traveled.

## Narrative Significance

Cape Ann serves as the narrative's geographical starting point. Although [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s physical voyage began from [[fairhaven|Fairhaven]] (across [[buzzards-bay|Buzzards Bay]]), it was at Cape Ann that he committed his experiences to paper — transforming the journey from lived experience into a written record.

The choice of location is fitting. Cape Ann, with its granite shores and long fishing heritage, was quintessential New England maritime territory. Beginning his log there grounded [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s narrative in the region that had shaped him as a sailor.
