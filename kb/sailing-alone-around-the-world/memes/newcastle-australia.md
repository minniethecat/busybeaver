## Geography

Newcastle, Australia, is a major coastal city in [[new-south-wales|New South Wales]]. Slocum arrived at Newcastle after a forty-two day passage from New Caledonia (013-008). He arrived in the teeth of a gale (013-010).

Later in the voyage, some yachtsmen purposed making for Newcastle under jury-sails after their mast was carried away (014-024). Another vessel turned back to Newcastle, its crew having missed the port on the way out (014-026).

## Slocum's Arrival

The chapter "Fiji to the south — Arrival at Newcastle, Australia — The yachts of Sydney" (013-001) marks Newcastle as a significant waypoint in the Spray's Australian coastal passage.
> One particularly severe gale encountered near New Caledonia foundered the American clipper-ship Patrician farther south. Again, nearer the coast of Australia, a French mail-steamer from New Caledonia for Sydney, blown considerably out of her course, on her arrival reported it an awful storm, and to inquiring friends said: "Oh, my! we don't know what has become of the little sloop Spray. We saw her in the thick of the storm." The Spray was all right, lying to like a duck. She was under a goose's wing mainsail, and had had a dry deck while the passengers on the steamer, I heard later, were up to their knees in water in the saloon.
>
> (013-009)
