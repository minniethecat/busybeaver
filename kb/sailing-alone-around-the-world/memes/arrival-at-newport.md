The **Arrival at Newport** on June 27, 1898, marked the completion of [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]]'s solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]] of the globe. After three years, two months, and two days at sea — covering more than forty-six thousand miles — the *[[spray|Spray]]* cast anchor at 1 a.m. in [[newport|Newport]] harbor. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] returned younger and healthier than when he had departed, having accomplished what no one before him had done: a single-handed voyage [[voyage|around the world]] (021-001) (021-015).

## Summary

The final leg of the [[voyage|circumnavigation]] began on June 5, 1898, when the *[[spray|Spray]]* cleared from the United States consulate for the last time. After crossing the horse latitudes, surviving a gale that parted the jibstay, and enduring a violent tornado off Fire Island — "the climax [[gale|storm]] of the voyage" — the *[[spray|Spray]]* reached [[newport|Newport]] in the early hours of June 27. The guardship *Dexter* recognized the little sloop: "[[spray|Spray]], ahoy!" A friend's voice meant a friend would not fire (021-015).

## Context

By the spring of 1898, the *[[spray|Spray]]* had already rounded [[cape-of-good-hope|the Cape]] of Good Hope, crossed the South Atlantic, and reached the Caribbean. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had been at sea for over three years. The Spanish-American War had broken out while he was in the [[antigua|West Indies]], and American harbors were mined against possible attack. The final approach to [[newport|Newport]] required navigating through a minefield (021-015).

> "Reflections of the voyage so nearly finished stole in upon me now; many tunes I had hummed again and again came back once more." — (021-011)

## Narrative

### The Final Storm

After an eight-day calm in the horse latitudes — [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s "probation" before the harbor entrance — the *[[spray|Spray]]* entered the [[gulf-stream|Gulf Stream]] and immediately encountered a series of gales. On June 20, the jibstay broke at the [[mast|masthead]], and the sail fell into the sea. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] gathered it in on the first wave: "I found by the amount of work done in three minutes' or less time that I had by no means grown stiff-jointed on the voyage" (021-006).

On June 25, off Fire Island, the *[[spray|Spray]]* was struck by the same tornado that had just devastated New York City, wrecking buildings and snapping ships' moorings:

> "It was the climax storm of the voyage, but I saw the unmistakable character of it in time to have all snug aboard and receive it under bare poles. Even so, the sloop shivered when it struck her, and she heeled over unwillingly on her beam ends." — (021-009)

### The Final Approach

After the [[gale|storm]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] changed course from New York to [[newport|Newport]], [[sail|sailing]] past Montauk Point and Point Judith. The final hazard was the minefield at the entrance to [[newport|Newport]] harbor:

> "The Spray hugged the rocks along where neither friend nor foe could come if drawing much water, and where she would not disturb the guard-ship in the channel. It was close work, but it was safe enough so long as she hugged the rocks close, and not the mines." — (021-015)

As she passed the guardship *Dexter*, a voice called out: "There goes a craft!" [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] threw up a light. "[[spray|Spray]], ahoy!" came the recognition — the voice of a friend.

### Arrival

> "At last she reached port in safety, and there at 1 a.m. on June 27, 1898, cast anchor, after the cruise of more than forty-six thousand miles round the world, during an absence of three years and two months, with two days over for coming up." — (021-015)

## Aftermath

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] found himself in remarkable condition. Far from being worn down by the voyage, he had "profited in many ways" — he had gained a pound in weight, and his friends declared he looked "at least ten years younger than the day I felled the first tree for the construction of [[spray|the Spray]]" (021-015). On July 3, he sailed the *[[spray|Spray]]* back to [[fairhaven|Fairhaven]], Massachusetts, tying her to the same cedar spile driven into the bank to hold her when she was launched (021-015).

The [[voyage|circumnavigation]] was complete. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s book, *[[sail|Sail]]ing Alone Around the World*, published in 1900, would become a classic of maritime literature and inspire generations of solo sailors.

## Significance

The arrival at [[newport|Newport]] was the culminating event of the first solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]] — a feat that proved a single person, in a small, hand-built vessel, could safely sail [[voyage|around the world]]. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s understated account of the homecoming — navigating a minefield as casually as any other hazard, hearing a friend's voice in the dark — captures the quiet confidence he had earned over 46,000 miles at sea.
