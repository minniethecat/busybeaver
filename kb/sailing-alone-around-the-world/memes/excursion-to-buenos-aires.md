Rather than proceed directly on her [[voyage|circumnavigation]] after repairs at [[montevideo|Montevideo]], the *[[spray|Spray]]* made an excursion up the River Plate to [[buenos-aires|Buenos Aires]] in late December 1895. Aboard with [[captain-howard|Captain Howard]] — an old [[cape-cod|Cape Cod]] friend and River Plate veteran — [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] revisited a city that had undergone remarkable transformation, enjoying fish chowder on the [[cabin|cabin floor]] and the hospitality of newspaper proprietor Mr. Mulhall.

## Summary

After the *[[spray|Spray]]* was refitted at [[montevideo|Montevideo]] — including docking and repairs courtesy of the Royal Mail Steamship Company — [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] decided on a short excursion upriver before resuming his voyage. On December 29, 1895, with [[captain-howard|Captain Howard of Cape Cod]] aboard, the *[[spray|Spray]]* sailed to [[buenos-aires|Buenos Aires]], arriving early the following day (006-014). The visit was a blend of nostalgia and sightseeing: [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] revisited old haunts, observed the city's transformation, and received generous hospitality from Mr. Mulhall, owner of the *Standard* newspaper. The *[[spray|Spray]]* was also shortened at [[buenos-aires|Buenos Aires]] — her mast was cut down by seven feet and her bowsprit sawed off — before she departed with a strong slant of wind in her favor (006-021) (007-001).

## Context

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had a history with [[buenos-aires|Buenos Aires]] dating back years before his [[voyage|circumnavigation]]. He had once "landed from packets, in a cart" at a much more primitive waterfront (006-016). By 1895, the city had changed dramatically:

> "The place where I had once landed from packets, in a cart, was now built up with magnificent docks. Vast fortunes had been spent in remodeling the harbor; London bankers could tell you that." — (006-016)

The *[[spray|Spray]]*'s arrival at [[montevideo|Montevideo]] had been greeted with unexpected pomp ("steam-whistles till I felt embarrassed") (006-011), and the Royal Mail Steamship Company had generously offered to dock and repair her free of charge, plus giving [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] twenty pounds sterling (006-012).

## Narrative

The [[buenos-aires|Buenos Aires]] excursion began with [[captain-howard|Captain Howard]], whom [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] described as an old friend "of [[cape-cod|Cape Cod]] and of River Plate fame" (006-014). [[captain-howard|Howard]] had spent years away from his native home but had not forgotten the art of making fish chowder:

> "When the savory chowder was done, chocking the pot securely between two boxes on the cabin floor, so that it could not roll, 'all hands' sat down to dinner, and a jollier time we had not had on the voyage." — (006-015)

Arriving at [[buenos-aires|Buenos Aires]] early on the following day, the port captain assigned the *[[spray|Spray]]* a safe berth and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] found himself welcomed by Mr. Mulhall, proprietor of the *Standard* newspaper:

> "Mr. Mulhall, with a prancing team, came down to the docks as soon as the *Spray* was berthed, and would have me go to his house at once, where he had arranged for the comfort of both mind and body of the crew of the *Spray*." — (006-017)

Mulhall drove [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] around the city to see the many improvements. They went in search of old landmarks. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was delighted to find that the man who sold "lemonade" on the plaza when he first visited the city was still there, "selling lemonade still at two cents a glass; he had made a fortune by it" (006-018). But they looked in vain for the man who "once sold whisky and coffins in [[buenos-aires|Buenos Aires]]; the march of civilization had crushed him — memory only clung to his name" (006-019).

A practical improvement was also made to the *[[spray|Spray]]* during this stop: [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] "unshipped [[sloop|the sloop]]'s mast at [[buenos-aires|Buenos Aires]] and shortened it by seven feet" and "sawed off the bowsprit" — modifications that would prove beneficial in the rough southern seas ahead (006-021).

## Aftermath

The *[[spray|Spray]]* departed [[buenos-aires|Buenos Aires]] with a strong slant of wind, but the departure was marked by what [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] described as "an outburst of emotion at the mouth of the Plate" (007-001). The emotional departure underscored his attachment to the River Plate region and its people. With the mast shortened and bowsprit trimmed, the *[[spray|Spray]]* was better configured for the challenging passage around [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]] that lay ahead. The excursion — though a detour from the strict [[voyage|circumnavigation]] route — had provided [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] with both practical vessel improvements and a heartening reminder of the friendships and hospitality that the voyage was accumulating as it progressed.

## Significance

The [[buenos-aires|Buenos Aires]] excursion was a rare moment of leisure and nostalgia in an otherwise arduous voyage. It demonstrated that [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s [[voyage|circumnavigation]] was not a non-stop endurance feat but a journey punctuated by human connections — old friends like [[captain-howard|Captain Howard]], generous hosts like Mr. Mulhall, and the simple pleasure of revisiting a remembered lemonade stand. The practical modification to the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s [[rigging|rig]] — shortening the mast and bowsprit — also marked [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s pragmatic seamanship: he was continuously refining his vessel for the conditions ahead, treating the voyage as an evolving engineering problem as much as a feat of endurance.
