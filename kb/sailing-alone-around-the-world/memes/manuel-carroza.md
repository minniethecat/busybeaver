**Manuel Carroza** was a Portuguese settler on [[juan-fernandez|Juan Fernandez Island]], described by [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]] as "the king of Juan Fernandez" (011-003). When the *[[spray|Spray]]* called at the island in 1896, Carroza proved to be a formidable trader.

## Encounter with Slocum

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] found Carroza a shrewd bargainer — "the Yankee Portuguese named Manuel Carroza, who nearly traded me out of my ship" (012-006). Carroza was a family man: his wife, described as "a beautiful woman," was "now a mother" (011-015), and they formed part of the small community on the remote island.

Despite the hard bargaining, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s portrait of Carroza is warm, depicting him as a character of the remote Pacific — self-reliant, enterprising, and kingly in his own domain.
> The house of the king of Juan Fernandez, Manuel Carroza by name, besides resembling the ark, wore a polished brass knocker on its only door, which was painted green. In front of this gorgeous entrance was a flag-mast all ataunto, and near it a smart whaleboat painted red and blue, the delight of the king's old age.
>
> (011-003)
