## Passage

From [[danger-point|Danger Point]], the [[spray|Spray]] entered Whitsunday Pass and sailed through that night (014-044).

## Narrative Significance

The transit of Whitsunday Pass represents Slocum's careful navigation through the intricate inner passage of Queensland's coast. Entering the pass after clearing Danger Point (014-044), Slocum sailed through by night, demonstrating the confidence he had developed in the Spray's handling and his own coastal pilotage. The Whitsunday Islands form part of the inner route within the Great Barrier Reef, a passage that required constant vigilance from the solo mariner.
> On the 24th of May, the sloop, having made one hundred and ten miles a day from Danger Point, now entered Whitsunday Pass, and that night sailed through among the islands. When the sun rose next morning I looked back and regretted having gone by while it was dark, for the scenery far astern was varied and charming.
>
> (014-044)
