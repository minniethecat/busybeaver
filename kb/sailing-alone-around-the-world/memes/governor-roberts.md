# Governor Roberts

Governor Roberts was the British colonial administrator of [[rodriguez-island|Rodriguez Island]], a remote outpost in the [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]], where [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] made landfall after his long run from [[cape-of-good-hope|the Cape]] of Good Hope. Roberts and his family received the solo circumnavigator with warmth and generosity, making the island one of the most pleasant stops of the entire voyage.

## Biography

Roberts belonged to a family with a presence on [[rodriguez-island|Rodriguez]] — his father, the elder Mr. Roberts, had also lived on the island. As governor, Roberts oversaw the small colonial community on this isolated speck of land, hundreds of miles from the nearest continent. The governorship of such a remote dependency demanded self-reliance and hospitality — qualities Roberts possessed in abundance.

## Role in the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] arrived at [[rodriguez-island|Rodriguez]] after a passage of eight days from [[cape-of-good-hope|the Cape]] of Good Hope, and the governor's welcome was immediate and warm: "Governor Roberts and his family did everything in their power to make my stay pleasant" (016-027). The governor had undertaken practical improvements to the island's infrastructure — including "a reservoir for the wholesome water" (016-031) that supplied [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] with fresh water for his continuing voyage.

> The governor of the island, Mr. Roberts, came down immediately to see what it was all about, for the little town was in a great commotion. One elderly woman, when she heard of my advent, made for her house and locked herself in. When she heard that I was actually coming up the street she barricaded her doors, and did not come out while I was on the island, a period of eight days. Governor Roberts and his family did not share the fears of their people, but came on board at the jetty, where the sloop was berthed, and their example induced others to come also.
>
> (016-027)

The governor's hospitality extended beyond material provisions. Roberts and his family provided companionship after weeks of solitude, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s account of the stay is colored with genuine gratitude. He later met the governor's father, "the elder Mr. Roberts, father of Governor Roberts of Rodriguez, and witness of many of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s nautical exploits" — suggesting that the connection to the Roberts family was more than a passing encounter.

## Significance

Governor Roberts represents the network of colonial hospitality that made [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s [[voyage|circumnavigation]] possible. At island after island, colonial governors and officials — often starved for news and company — welcomed the solitary sailor, provided supplies, and sent him on his way with letters of introduction to the next port. Roberts stands out for the warmth of his welcome and the practical help he provided.
