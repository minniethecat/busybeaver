# Gibraltar

Gibraltar — the great limestone Rock rising 1,400 feet from the sea at the entrance to [[mediterranean-sea|the Mediterranean]] — has been a strategic maritime gateway since antiquity. For [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], it was an early port of call, a place to replenish after the first Atlantic leg, and an encounter with the Royal Navy that reminded him he sailed in a world of empires.

## Geography

Gibraltar occupies a narrow peninsula at the southern tip of Spain, commanding [[strait-of-magellan|the Strait]] of Gibraltar — the nine-mile-wide passage between [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] and [[mediterranean-sea|the Mediterranean]]. The Rock itself is a fortress: tunnels, gun batteries, and signal stations honeycomb its limestone mass. The harbor, sheltered by the Rock's eastern face, has been a British naval base since 1704.

## In the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] arrived at Gibraltar in August 1895, early in his voyage. "Monday, August 25, the *[[spray|Spray]]* sailed from Gibraltar, well repaid for whatever deviation she had made from a direct course to reach the place. A tug belonging to her Majesty towed [[sloop|the sloop]] into the steady breeze clear of the mount" (005-002). The Royal Navy's courtesy — a tug to assist the small sloop — was a mark of respect from one maritime power to another, however different in scale.

Gibraltar was also a place of strategic calculation. The African coast nearby was, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] noted, "the home of pirates" (005-002), and his course from Gibraltar southward required careful planning to avoid the dangerous shore. The Rock was the last bastion of European order before the long run down the African coast.

> The *Spray* continued on her course till well in with the land, which was that about Trafalgar. Then keeping away a point, she passed through the Strait of Gibraltar, where she cast anchor at 3 p.m. of the same day, less than twenty-nine days from Cape Sable.
>
> (004-016)

## Significance

Gibraltar represents the imperial architecture within which [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s voyage unfolded. British naval power, Spanish coastal [[current|set]]tlements, Portuguese island colonies — the world [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sailed through was shaped by empires, and Gibraltar, the quintessential imperial fortress, was a conspicuous waypoint. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s reception by the Royal Navy affirmed his status as a legitimate mariner, however small his vessel.
