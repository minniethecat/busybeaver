# Falkland Islands

A remote British archipelago in the South Atlantic, which [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] considered as a potential port of refuge during his challenging passage around [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]] and [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]].

## Geography

The Falkland Islands lie approximately 500 kilometers east of the [[patagonia|Patagonian coast]] in the South [[atlantic-ocean|Atlantic Ocean]]. The main [[current|set]]tlement, Port Stanley, served as a coaling station and port of call for vessels rounding [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]] in the late 19th century.

## Role in the Voyage

During his difficult passage through [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] and around [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] considered making for the Falklands. He noted the "distance to Port Stanley, in the Falkland Islands, where I might find materials for repairs" (008-006). The [[spray|Spray]] had been battered by gales and encounters with [[fuegians|Fuegians]], and Port Stanley offered the nearest safe harbor with supplies.

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] ultimately decided against diverting to the Falklands, pressing on through the strait and into [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]] instead. This decision reflected his characteristic determination to maintain progress on his [[voyage|circumnavigation]] rather than seek port refuge.

## Historical Context

By the 1890s, the Falkland Islands were an established British colony with a small permanent population. Port Stanley served as an important repair and resupply point for vessels making the arduous passage between [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] and [[pacific-ocean|Pacific Ocean]]s, particularly [[sail|sailing]] ships that had been damaged in the waters around [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]].

> I inspected my chart and pricked off the course and distance to Port Stanley, in the Falkland Islands, where I might find my way and refit, when I saw through a rift in the clouds a high mountain, about seven leagues away on the port beam. The fierce edge of the gale by this time had blown off, and I had already bent a square sail on the boom in place of the mainsail, which was torn to rags.
>
> (008-006)
