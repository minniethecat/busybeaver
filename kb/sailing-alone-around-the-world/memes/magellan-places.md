# Strait of Magellan Places

The [[strait-of-magellan|Strait of Magellan]] was the most hazardous passage of [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]]'s entire [[voyage|circumnavigation]]. For weeks he threaded the *[[spray|Spray]]* through its rocky channels, battling williwaws, tide-rips, and the hostile reception of the [[fuegians|Fuegian people]]. This index gathers all locations associated with that defining passage.

## The Magellan Passage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s transit of [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] was remarkable for its duration — he spent more time in those narrow waters than many larger vessels dared — and for his encounters with the indigenous [[fuegians|Fuegians]], whose reputation for hostility he found both confirmed and complicated by direct experience. The Strait marked the transition from [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] to [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]], and from the known [[sail|sailing]] routes of the North Atlantic to the uncharted (for a solo sailor) waters of the [[pacific-ocean|South Pacific]].
