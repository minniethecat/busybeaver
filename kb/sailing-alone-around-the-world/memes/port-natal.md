# Port Natal

Port Natal is the historic name for the port of [[durban|Durban]], the commercial center of what was then the British colony of [[south-africa|Natal]] on the southeastern coast of Africa. [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]] arrived here on November 17, 1897, after a difficult crossing of the [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]].

## In the Book

After enduring a succession of gales that drove the *[[spray|Spray]]* about in many directions, including a hard southwest gale in the [[mozambique-channel|Mozambique Channel]] with heavy thunder and lightning, [[sloop|the sloop]] arrived at Port Natal on the 17th of November (017-014).

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] described the place warmly: "This delightful place is the commercial center of the 'Garden Colony,' [[durban|Durban]] itself, the city, being the continuation of a garden" (017-014). The signalman from the bluff station reported the *[[spray|Spray]]* fifteen miles off and later noted with admiration that one man was doing all the work of shortening sail, [[reef|reefing]] the mainsail and [[current|set]]ting it again in ten minutes. The item was printed in a [[durban|Durban]] morning journal within three minutes (017-014).

The local press welcomed the *[[spray|Spray]]* with enthusiasm:

> As briefly noted yesterday, the Spray, with a crew of one man, arrived at this port yesterday afternoon on her cruise round the world. The Spray made quite an auspicious entrance to Natal. Her commander sailed his craft right up the channel past the main wharf, and dropped his anchor near the old Forerunner in the creek, before anyone had a chance to get on board. The Spray was naturally an object of great curiosity to the Point people, and her arrival was witnessed by a large crowd. (017-016)

## Slocum's Stay in Durban

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] found that [[durban|Durban]] supported two yacht-clubs, "both of them full of enterprise." He met all the members of both clubs and sailed in the crack yacht *Florence* of the Royal [[durban|Natal]], with Captain Spradbrow and the Right Honorable Harry Escombe, premier of the colony (017-016).

The most famous episode of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s [[durban|Natal]] stay was his encounter with three Transvaal Boers who came from [[pretoria|Pretoria]] seeking to prove that the world was flat — a belief endorsed by President [[president-kruger|Krüger]]. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] found their efforts amusing: "With the advice to call up some ghost of the dark ages for research, I went ashore, and left these three wise men poring over [[spray|the Spray]]'s [[course|track]] on a chart of the world, which, however, proved nothing to them, for it was on Mercator's projection, and behold, it was 'flat'" (017-018).

The next morning, one of the party — now dressed in a clergyman's garb and carrying a large Bible — confronted [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] again. When [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] began to question the premise, the man flew into a passion, "making as if he would run me through with an assagai." [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] noted dryly: "Had this good but misguided fanatic been armed with a real weapon, the crew of [[spray|the Spray]] would have died a martyr there and then" (017-018).

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] also visited many public schools at [[durban|Durban]] and met the explorer [[henry-m-stanley|Henry M. Stanley]], who subjected him to a friendly cross-examination about his voyage. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] described the meeting as a highlight of his African stay (017-016).

## Departure

On December 14, 1897, after "having a fine time in [[durban|Natal]]," [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] swung the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s dinghy in [[deck|on deck]] and sailed with a morning land-wind which carried her clear of the bar. Once again she was "off on her alone," bound for [[cape-of-good-hope|the Cape of Good Hope]] (017-022).

## Role in the Voyage

Port Natal was [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s first landfall on the African continent after crossing the [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]]. It provided rest, hospitality, and one of the most memorable comic episodes of the entire voyage. The contrast between the warm reception at [[durban|Durban]] and the hostile gales of the Mozambique Channel that preceded it captures the alternating rhythm of hardship and human connection that defined the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s [[voyage|circumnavigation]].
