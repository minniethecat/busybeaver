The **Philippines** is an archipelago of over 7,000 islands in the western [[pacific-ocean|Pacific Ocean]], situated between the Philippine Sea and the South China Sea. For [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]], the Philippines — particularly its capital, Manila — was a familiar port from his earlier career as a merchant ship captain, years before his solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]] in the *[[spray|Spray]]*.

## Slocum in Manila

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s connection to the Philippines dates to his service as captain of the *[[northern-light|Northern Light]]*, one of the finest American [[sail|sailing]] ships of her day. During a chance encounter with an old friend — Mr. Hannibal, chief mate of the steamer *Colombia* — off the South American coast, the two "referred affectionately to days in Manila when we were there together, he in the *Southern Cross* and I in the *[[northern-light|Northern Light]]*" (009-012). This reminiscence places [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] in Manila during his merchant service years, commanding a large vessel on Pacific trade routes.

> In the chief mate of the *Colombia*, Mr. Hannibal, I found an old friend, and he referred affectionately to days in Manila when we were there together, he in the *Southern Cross* and I in the *Northern Light*, both ships as beautiful as their names.
>
> (009-012)

## Circumnavigation History

The Philippines holds a pivotal place in the history of global [[voyage|circumnavigation]]. [[ferdinand-magellan|Ferdinand Magellan]], leading the first expedition to circle the globe, was killed at the Battle of Mactan in the Philippines on April 27, 1521. The *[[victoria|Victoria]]*, under Juan Sebastián Elcano, continued westward from the Philippines to complete the first [[voyage|circumnavigation]] in September 1522. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], as a student of maritime history, was well aware of this lineage — his own [[voyage|circumnavigation]] nearly four centuries later traced many of the same waters.

## Geographic Significance

The Philippines sits astride major Pacific trade routes linking East Asia with the Americas and [[australia|Australia]]. For nineteenth-century merchant shipping, Manila was a key port of call for vessels carrying cargo between Asia and the Americas — the very trade in which [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], as captain of the *[[northern-light|Northern Light]]*, was engaged.
