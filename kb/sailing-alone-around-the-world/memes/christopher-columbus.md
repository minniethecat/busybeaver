# Christopher Columbus

Christopher Columbus (1451–1506) was the Genoese navigator whose 1492 voyage across [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] — [[sail|sailing]] west from Spain in search of Asia — initiated the European encounter with the Americas. For [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], crossing the same ocean four centuries later, Columbus was a natural point of reference: the archetypal ocean-crosser, the navigator who had ventured into the unknown.

## Biography

Born in Genoa to a wool-weaver's family, Columbus went to sea as a young man and conceived a plan to reach the Indies by [[sail|sailing]] west. After years of seeking patronage, he secured the support of Ferdinand and Isabella of Spain. His first voyage (1492) with the ships *Niña*, *[[pinta|Pinta]]*, and *[[santa-maria|Santa María]]* made landfall in the Bahamas. He made three subsequent voyages, never fully comprehending that he had found a continent unknown to Europeans.

## Role in the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] invokes Columbus at a key moment in his own Atlantic crossing. After a severe gale, ill and exhausted, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] found that the *[[spray|Spray]]* had held her course through the night without a hand at the [[rudder|helm]]: "Columbus himself could not have held her more exactly on her course" (004-011). The comparison is deliberate and bold — the greatest navigator in history could not have done better than the *[[spray|Spray]]* [[helm|steering]] herself.

For [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], Columbus represented the navigator's ideal: the courage to [[current|set]] out across an ocean with no certainty of what lay beyond. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] knew his destination; Columbus did not. Yet Columbus had a crew, while [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had only himself. The comparison cuts both ways.

## Significance

Columbus appears in the narrative not as a historical figure to be studied but as a fellow mariner to be measured against. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s "Columbus himself could not have held her more exactly" is at once a boast, a tribute, and an assertion of the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s preternatural qualities. Columbus is the gold standard of ocean navigation; the *[[spray|Spray]]*, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] implies, has exceeded it.

> To my astonishment, I saw now at broad day that the Spray was still heading as I had left her, and was going like a racehorse. Columbus himself could not have held her more exactly on her course.
>
> (004-011)
