## Passage Through

Slocum sailed direct for New South Wales after passing New Caledonia (013-008). He noted the vigilance of the New South Wales police (013-011). While there, the governor of New South Wales, Lord Hampden, and his family visited the Spray (014-006). Slocum later encountered rough seas off the coast of New South Wales, in latitude 30° 12′ south (014-037).

> Thence I sailed direct for New South Wales, passing south of New Caledonia, and arrived at Newcastle after a passage of forty-two days, mostly of storms and gales.
>
> (013-008)

## Narrative Significance

Slocum's passage through New South Wales marked a significant phase of the Australian leg of his voyage. After a long Pacific crossing, the coast of New South Wales represented both a navigational milestone and an opportunity to reprovision (013-008). The visit by Governor Lord Hampden and his family to the Spray (014-006) underscores the curiosity and hospitality Slocum encountered from colonial officials throughout his journey. The rough seas Slocum later experienced off this coast (014-037) were characteristic of the variable conditions along Australia's eastern seaboard.
