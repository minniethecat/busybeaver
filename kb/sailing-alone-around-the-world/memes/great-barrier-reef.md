# Great Barrier Reef

The world's largest coral [[reefing|reef]] system, stretching over 2,000 kilometers along the [[bowen|Queensland]] coast of [[australia|Australia]], which [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] referenced while planning his route through the [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]].

## Geography

The Great Barrier Reef runs parallel to the northeastern coast of [[australia|Australia]], from the [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]] in the north to below the Tropic of Capricorn in the south. In [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s time, it was charted but remained a formidable navigation hazard, requiring careful pilotage through its narrow passages.

## Role in the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] considered the Great Barrier Reef while planning his route from [[australia|Australia]] to the [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]]. He wrote that he decided on a course "through [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]], by way of the Great Barrier Reef, the route I finally decided on" (013-025). He equipped himself with "sheet-charts of the coast and Barrier Reef" which gave him confidence: "I felt easy in mind" (014-018).

Captain Fisher of the steamship *Caledonia*, whom [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] met at [[newcastle|Newcastle]], advised him on the route and shared charts, noting that the Patrician had foundered on the Barrier Reef farther south (013-009). The [[spray|Spray]] successfully navigated the inner passage without incident.

## The Inner Passage Route

The inner passage between the [[australia|Australia]]n mainland and the Great Barrier Reef demanded constant vigilance. Captain Fisher, who had "steamed through the Barrier passages in H.M.S. Orlando," provided [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] with firsthand knowledge of the route (014-018). The passage was marked by [[beacon|lighthouse]]s at critical points: from the "pitch of the cape was a noble light seen twenty-seven miles," and passing from there to "Lady Elliott Light" provided a chain of navigational markers (014-041).

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s approach blended practical seamanship with a philosophical acceptance of risk. He "trusted now to the mercies of the Maker of all [[reefing|reef]]s, keeping a good lookout at the same time for perils on every hand" (014-043). The reward for this careful navigation was a spectacle he never forgot: "the Barrier Reef and the waters of many colors studded all about with enchanted islands" (014-044).

## Historical Context

The Great Barrier Reef had claimed numerous vessels by the 1890s. [[captain-cook|Captain James Cook]]'s *Endeavour* had struck the [[reefing|reef]] in 1770, and many ships thereafter met similar fates. The publication of reliable charts in the late 19th century made the inner passage safer, though it remained a test of seamanship that [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] met with characteristic calm.

> Lo! the Barrier Reef and the waters of many colors studded all about with enchanted islands! I behold among them after all many safe harbors, else my vision is astray. On the 24th of May, the sloop, having made one hundred and ten miles a day from Danger Point, now entered Whitsunday Pass, and that night sailed through among the islands. When the sun rose next morning I looked back and regretted having gone by while it was dark, for the scenery far astern was varied and charming.
>
> (014-018)
