## Biography

Sir R. A. Sterndale served as Governor of [[st-helena|St. Helena]], the remote South Atlantic island famous as Napoleon's place of exile. He held the post during the 1890s, at the time of [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]]'s solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]] aboard the *[[spray|Spray]]*.

## Hospitality to Slocum

When [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] arrived at [[st-helena|St. Helena]] in April 1898 during his solo voyage [[voyage|around the world]], [[governor-sterndale|Governor Sterndale]] received him with exceptional warmth and ceremony. The governor remarked upon landing that it was rare for a circumnavigator to pass his way (019-002). He invited [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] to recount the voyage at Garden Hall for the people of Jamestown, and again at Plantation House, the governor's hilltop residence.

> It was about noon when the Spray came to anchor off Jamestown, and "all hands" at once went ashore to pay respects to his Excellency the governor of the island, Sir R. A. Sterndale. His Excellency, when I landed, remarked that it was not often, nowadays, that a circumnavigator came his way, and he cordially welcomed me, and arranged that I should tell about the voyage, first at Garden Hall to the people of Jamestown, and then at Plantation House — the governor's residence. (019-002)

## Tour of the Island

On the second day of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s visit, [[governor-sterndale|Governor Sterndale]] personally conducted him on a carriage tour of [[st-helena|St. Helena]]. The island's steep, winding roads impressed [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], who noted the tremendous labor required to build them across ravines and spurs (019-004). The governor's escort allowed [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] to see the island beyond the port town of Jamestown.

## Gifts for the Voyage

The Sterndale family showed generous hospitality. Before the *[[spray|Spray]]* departed on April 20, [[governor-sterndale|Lady Sterndale]] sent a large fruitcake from Plantation House for the voyage — a "great high-decker" that [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] ate sparingly, the last of it consumed with his first cup of coffee at [[antigua|Antigua]] (019-006). [[governor-sterndale|Governor Sterndale]] also gave [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] a bag of coffee in the husk, a practical provision for the long Atlantic crossing ahead (019-008). A goat put aboard at the governor's suggestion proved more troublesome than useful, requiring constant restraint.

## Significance

Sir R. A. Sterndale represents the pattern of colonial hospitality that [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] encountered during his [[voyage|circumnavigation]]. His reception at [[st-helena|St. Helena]] was among the warmest of the entire voyage, combining official ceremony with genuine personal kindness.
