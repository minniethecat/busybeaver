# Black Pedro

Black Pedro was a [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n man whom [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] encountered in [[strait-of-magellan|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] — a figure who became, in the narrative, the embodiment of the dangers that lurked on the desolate shores of [[tierra-del-fuego|Tierra del Fuego]]. Their tense confrontation is one of the most dramatic episodes in *[[sail|Sail]]ing Alone Around the World*.

## Biography

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] describes Black Pedro as the "worst murderer in [[tierra-del-fuego|Tierra del Fuego]]" — a characterization based on stories he heard from local [[current|set]]tlers and sailors. Pedro was known to have plundered wrecked ships along the strait, and his fearsome reputation preceded him. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] first heard of him while preparing to transit the strait: [[fortescue-bay|Fortescue Bay]] was described as "a miss-shot for Black Pedro" (007-001) — a place where Pedro had once attempted violence and failed.

## Role in the Voyage

The encounter with Black Pedro occurred in [[strait-of-magellan|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]]. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] met him with characteristic courage — meeting the threat with calm determination rather than panic. Pedro approached the *[[spray|Spray]]* in a canoe, demanding gifts. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], recognizing the danger, dealt with the situation through a combination of firmness and psychological tactics — spreading carpet tacks on the deck to deter barefoot boarders, and demonstrating his rifle's accuracy.

> A miss was as good as a mile for Mr. "Black Pedro," as he it was, and no other, a leader in several bloody massacres. I knew by his Spanish lingo and by his full beard that he was the villain I have named, a renegade mongrel, and the worst murderer in Tierra del Fuego. The authorities had been in search of him for two years. The Fuegians are not bearded.
>
> (007-019)

The confrontation reached its climax when Pedro, seeing [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s preparedness, withdrew. Later, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] learned that Pedro had visited a passing steamer and told the crew about the solitary sailor in the strait: "they saw Black Pedro and learned that he had visited the *[[spray|Spray]]*" (010-006). The implication was clear — Pedro had assessed the solo sailor and decided he was not worth the risk.

## Significance

Black Pedro serves a dual role in the narrative. On one level, he is the most dangerous human adversary [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] faced — a reminder that the perils of the voyage were not limited to wind and wave. On another level, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s handling of Pedro demonstrates the captain's most important quality: calm under pressure. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] did not shoot Pedro, did not flee, did not panic. He prepared, stood his ground, and the threat passed. The episode is a study in the power of composure.
