# Montevideo

Montevideo, the capital and largest city of [[uruguay|Uruguay]], was the port where [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]] received an unexpectedly warm welcome after the *[[spray|Spray]]* ran aground on the [[uruguay|Uruguayan coast]]. The city's maritime community provided free repairs and generous hospitality.

## Geography

Montevideo sits on the northern shore of the [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio]] de la Plata estuary, approximately 200 kilometers east of [[buenos-aires|Buenos Aires]]. In [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s time, it was a major South American port, with a well-established British mercantile community (005-002).

## Role in the Voyage

### Arrival After Shipwreck

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] arrived in Montevideo under dramatic circumstances. The *[[spray|Spray]]* had run hard aground on the sands of [[uruguay|Uruguay]] near [[maldonado|Maldonado]] on December 11, 1895. After refloating the vessel with help from local coastguards and a German sailor, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sailed for the [[uruguay|Uruguay]]an capital (005-002).

> "Shortly after, the *Spray* sailed for Montevideo, where she arrived on the following day and was greeted by steam-whistles till I felt embarrassed and wished that I had arrived unobserved." — (005-003)

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], characteristically modest, felt the celebration was premature: "The voyage so far alone may have seemed to the [[uruguay|Uruguay]]ans a feat worthy of some recognition; but there was so much of it yet ahead, and of such an arduous nature, that any demonstration at this point seemed, somehow, like boasting prematurely" (005-003).

### Repairs and Hospitality

The British shipping agents Humphreys & Co. provided exceptional assistance:

> "The *Spray* had barely come to anchor at Montevideo when the agents of the Royal Mail Steamship Company, Messrs. Humphreys & Co., sent word that they would dock and repair her free of expense and give me twenty pounds sterling, which, they did to the letter, and more besides." — (005-004)

The calkers and carpenters at Montevideo carefully repaired the damage from the grounding, mending the keel and repainting the dory (005-004).

### Onward to Buenos Aires

From Montevideo, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] made an excursion up the [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio]] de la Plata to [[buenos-aires|Buenos Aires]]. A friend named [[captain-howard|Howard]] accompanied him part of the way, preparing fish chowder aboard the *[[spray|Spray]]* and telling "stories about the [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n cannibals" as [[sloop|the sloop]] made her way up the river (005-005).

## History

Montevideo was founded by the Spanish in 1724. By the 1890s, it was a thriving commercial port with strong European ties. The warm reception [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] received reflects both the port's maritime culture and the generosity extended to solo voyagers throughout his [[voyage|circumnavigation]].
