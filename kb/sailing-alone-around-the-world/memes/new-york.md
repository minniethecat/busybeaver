**New York** was [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]]'s home port in the years before his solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]], and the city appears repeatedly in *Sailing Alone Around the World* as a point of reference — the destination of voyages, the registry of ships, and the place where news of his achievement would eventually arrive. Although [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] never called at New York during the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s circumnavigation itself, the city was woven into the fabric of his maritime life.

## In Slocum's Earlier Career

Before the *[[spray|Spray]]*, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] commanded some of the finest American [[sail|sailing]] vessels afloat, including the magnificent ship *[[northern-light|Northern Light]]*. After one of his commands was wrecked, his "home voyage to New York with my family was made in the [[liberdade|Liberdade]]" (001-004) — the [[canoe|canoe]] he had built from the wreckage in [[brazil|Brazil]]. New York was thus the end point of one of the most remarkable voyages of his early career, the open-boat passage from South America that preceded his more famous solo circumnavigation.

## As a Maritime Hub

Throughout the voyage, New York appears as a familiar landmark in the maritime world. The Ericsson ship *Destroyer* had sailed "from New York to [[brazil|Brazil]]" (005-024), part of the naval traffic between the hemispheres. At sea in the Pacific, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] encountered the steamer *[[colombia-steamship|Colombia]]*, Captain Henderson, "from New York, bound for San Francisco" (009-012) — and aboard her found an old friend, the chief mate Mr. Hannibal.

## A Connection to the World

In the [[marquesas|Marquesas]] Islands, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] carried a message from "Mr. W—— of New York, a gentleman interested in missionary work" who had "charged me, when I sailed, to give his remembrance to the king of the Cannibal Islands" (013-004). The connection shows New York as a node in the global network of maritime and missionary endeavor that linked the South Seas to the drawing rooms of the East Coast.

## Completion of the Voyage

When the *[[spray|Spray]]* was off [[cape-of-good-hope|the Cape]] of Good Hope, the keeper of the [[cape-agulhas|Agulhas]] light exchanged signals with her, and "afterward wrote me at New York congratulations on the completion of the voyage" (018-004). The keeper, seeing the little sloop pass, assumed that by the time word reached New York, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] would be safely home. His instinct was correct — New York, the city from which so many voyages set out, was the natural place to send the news of a voyage completed.

On June 25, 1898, a violent tornado swept over New York City with lightning that wrecked buildings and sent trees flying about in splinters; even ships at docks had parted their moorings. An hour later, the *[[spray|Spray]]* encountered the same storm off [[fire-island|Fire Island]] — the climax storm of the voyage that forced [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] to divert from his intended New York landfall to [[newport|Newport]] instead (021-008).
