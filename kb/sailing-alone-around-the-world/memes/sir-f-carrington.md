## Biography

Sir F. Carrington served as Governor of [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]], the British fortress at the entrance to [[mediterranean-sea|the Mediterranean]] Sea, during the mid-1890s. He held a military rank — addressed both as "Sir F. Carrington" and "General Carrington" — reflecting [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]]'s dual role as a colony and a strategic garrison.

## Meeting Slocum

On the day after [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s arrival at [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]] aboard the *[[spray|Spray]]*, Governor Carring[[tonnage|ton]] came aboard the little sloop accompanied by high officers of the garrison and commanders of the battleships stationed there. Each visitor signed the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s logbook (004-022). The governor extended an invitation for [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] to attend an "at home" reception aboard H.M.S. *Collingwood*, though [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] declined in jest, citing his limited wardrobe.

> On the following day Sir F. Carrington, at the time governor of Gibraltar, with other high officers of the garrison, and all the commanders of the battleships, came on board and signed their names in the Spray's logbook. Again there was a hail, "Spray ahoy!" "Hello!" "Commander Reynolds's compliments. You are invited on board H.M.S. Collingwood, 'at home' at 4:30 p.m." (004-022)

## The Morocco Picnic

Before the *[[spray|Spray]]* departed [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]], Governor Carring[[tonnage|ton]] invited [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] on a picnic with the garrison officers and warship commanders. The party was carried across the strait to the [[morocco|Morocco shore]] and back aboard Torpedo-boat No. 91, racing at twenty-two [[knot|knots]] (004-025). Though no one landed at [[morocco|Morocco]] owing to the heat, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was struck by the speed of the craft and the skill of young Sublieutenant Boucher at the [[rudder|helm]]. The following day, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] lunched with General Carrington at Line Wall House.

> Before sailing I was invited on a picnic with the governor, the officers of the garrison, and the commanders of the warships at the station; and a royal affair it was. Torpedo-boat No. 91, going twenty-two knots, carried our party to the Morocco shore and back. (004-025)

## Significance

Governor Carring[[tonnage|ton]]'s hospitality marked the first major official reception of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s [[voyage|circumnavigation]]. The contrast between the *[[spray|Spray]]* — a thirty-six-foot oyster sloop — and the Royal Navy battleships anchored at [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]] underscored the singular nature of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s achievement.
