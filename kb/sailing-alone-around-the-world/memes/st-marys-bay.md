# St. Mary's Bay

A bay on the western coast of [[nova-scotia|Nova Scotia]], long familiar to [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] from his youth and revisited during the early days of his [[voyage|circumnavigation]].

## Geography

St. Mary's Bay lies along the western shore of [[nova-scotia|Nova Scotia]], between the Digby Neck peninsula and the mainland. The bay opens onto [[bay-of-fundy|the Bay]] of [[bay-of-fundy|Fundy]] and is characterized by sheltered waters, making it a traditional cruising and fishing ground for local mariners. Its proximity to [[briars-island|Briar's Island]] and [[westport|Westport]] placed it within the network of harbors and anchorages [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] knew intimately from his boyhood years in the region.

## Role in the Voyage

During the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s stopover at [[briars-island|Briar's Island]] in May 1895, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] made "a short excursion with some friends to St. Mary's Bay, an old cruising-ground, and back to the island" (002-016). The visit was a nostalgic return to waters he had sailed as a young man. Bad weather and head winds outside kept the *[[spray|Spray]]* in the area, giving [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] time to reconnect with the places of his youth before rounding [[cape-sable|Cape Sable]] and continuing south (002-016).

The excursion came during a period of final preparation and provisioning. After returning from St. Mary's Bay, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sailed for [[yarmouth|Yarmouth]] where he took in butter, a barrel of potatoes, filled six barrels of water, and acquired his famous one-dollar tin clock — the only [[chronometer|timepiece]] carried on the entire voyage (002-016).

## Historical Context

In the late 19th century, the bays and inlets of [[nova-scotia|Nova Scotia]]'s [[bay-of-fundy|Fundy]] shore were home to thriving fishing communities. St. Mary's Bay was part of the rich [[nova-scotia|Acadia]]n and maritime heritage of the region. For [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], a [[nova-scotia|Nova Scotia]] native who had spent his formative years in [[westport|Westport]], these waters represented not just geography but personal history — a last connection to home before embarking on his unprecedented solo voyage [[voyage|around the world]].

> I made a short excursion with some friends to St. Mary's Bay, an old cruising-ground, and back to the island.
