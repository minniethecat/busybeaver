## Geography

Cape Froward is the southernmost point of the mainland of the American continent, situated on the north shore of [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]]. The region around the cape is characterized by rugged, barren terrain, with few animals apart from dogs kept by the indigenous [[fuegians|Fuegians]]. Birds were scarce, though the occasional scream of a wild fowl would startle with its piercing cry. (007-029)

The strait west of Cape Froward is a labyrinth of islands and channels. The [[cockburn-channel|Cockburn Channel]] enters [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] at a point opposite the cape, near the suggestively named Thieves' Bay. (008-010)

## Role in the Voyage

> February 20 was my birthday, and I found myself alone, with hardly so much as a bird in sight, off Cape Froward, the southernmost point of the continent of America. By daylight in the morning I was getting my ship under way for the bout ahead.
>
> (007-016)

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] reached Cape Froward on February 20, 1896, which was also his birthday. He found himself alone there: "with hardly so much as a bird in sight, off Cape Froward, the southernmost point of the continent of America." (007-016)

The chapter summary for this part of the voyage lists Cape Froward among the landmarks of a harrowing passage: "Off Cape Froward — Chased by Indians from [[fortescue-bay|Fortescue Bay]]." (007-001) After passing the cape, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] anchored at [[snug-bay|Snug Bay]] at gray morning, only to discover at dawn that two canoes of well-armed savages he had eluded by [[sail|sailing]] through the night were entering the same bay stealthily under the shadow of the headland. He fired a shot from his rifle across their bows, and both canoes turned aside. (009-003)

Later, [[sail|sailing]] through the [[cockburn-channel|Cockburn Channel]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] found that [[spray|the Spray]] had entered [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] at a point opposite Cape Froward and was already passing Thieves' Bay. At night on March 8, she anchored in a snug cove at the Turn. "Every heartbeat on [[spray|the Spray]] now counted thanks." (008-010)

## Historical Context

Cape Froward was named by the English navigator Sir Thomas Cavendish in 1587, marking the southern extremity of the Americas. During the era of sail, it was a significant waypoint for vessels navigating the treacherous [[strait-of-magellan|Strait of Magellan]]. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s passage in [[spray|the Spray]] was particularly notable as he was [[sail|sailing]] alone, contending with both extreme weather and the threat of [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n raiders along the shores.
