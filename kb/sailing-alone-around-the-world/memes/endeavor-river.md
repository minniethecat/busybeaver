**Endeavor River** is a river in northern Queensland, [[australia|Australia]], flowing into the [[coral-sea|Coral Sea]] at [[cooktown|Cooktown]]. The river was named by Captain James Cook after his ship HMS *Endeavour*, which was careened there for repairs in 1770. [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]] called at Cooktown on the Endeavor River during his passage north along the Australian coast.

## Slocum's Visit

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] reached "Cooktown, on the Endeavor River" (014-034) after a passage through the [[great-barrier-reef|Great Barrier Reef]], where he found news of the Australian wool market. On his southbound passage, the *[[spray|Spray]]* called again at the Endeavor River, where "on the very night following" (015-015) her arrival, a violent storm struck the anchorage — one of the few occasions when the *[[spray|Spray]]* dragged her anchor during the voyage.

> Tacking inside of all the craft in port, I moored her at sunset nearly abreast the Captain Cook monument, and next morning went ashore to feast my eyes on the very stones the great navigator had seen, for I was now on a seaman's consecrated ground.
>
> (015-015)
