# Cooktown

Cooktown, on the Endeavor River in far north [[bowen|Queensland]], was one of the warmest stops of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s [[australia|Australia]]n coastal passage. The miners of this rough frontier town turned out to hear his story, paid for the privilege in gold, and left him with a memory of generosity that stood out even on a voyage full of kindness from strangers.

## Geography

Cooktown lies on the northeastern coast of [[bowen|Queensland]], near the mouth of the Endeavor River — named for [[captain-cook|Captain James Cook]]'s ship, which was beached here for repairs in 1770. In the 1890s, it was a gold-rush town, serving the Palmer River goldfields inland. The harbor is sheltered by a headland, but the approach through the [[great-barrier-reef|Great Barrier Reef]] demands careful navigation.

## In the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] reached Cooktown on the last day of May, after navigating the inner passage of the [[great-barrier-reef|Barrier Reef]]. "The 31st of May, when I reached Cooktown, on the Endeavor River, where I remained" — (014-034) — marks the arrival. The approach had been tense: a near miss with a coral [[reefing|reef]] kept him vigilant "night or day, till she reached Cooktown, on the Endeavor River" — (015-007).

The town's reception was enthusiastic. "The longshore daily papers of Cooktown the morning after she arrived" — (015-010) reported on the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s arrival, though there was some debate about her exact provenance. "There seemed a question in Cooktown's mind as to the exact spot" — (015-011) from which she had been reconstructed. A passing ship had put in "at Cooktown for scurvy-grass" — (015-012), a reminder of the practical concerns of long-distance [[sail|sailing]].

But what [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] remembered most warmly was the lecture he gave. "The good Cooktown people wished to hear a story" — (015-013), and they were willing to pay for it. "The miners of Cooktown" — (015-014) contributed generously, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], ever practical, noted the funds helped provision the *[[spray|Spray]]* for the next leg — through the [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]] and into the [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]].

> From Port Denison the sloop ran before the constant trade-wind, and made no stop at all, night or day, till she reached Cooktown, on the Endeavor River, where she arrived Monday, May 31, 1897, before a furious blast of wind encountered that day fifty miles down the coast.
>
> (014-034)

## Historical Context

Cooktown was founded in 1873 during the Palmer River gold rush and grew rapidly as the main supply port for the goldfields. By the 1890s, the rush had subsided, but the town remained a center for mining, pearling, and coastal trade. Its isolation — far north of any [[australia|Australia]]n city — bred a self-reliant community that appreciated a good story from a traveler, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s tale of solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]] was exactly the kind of entertainment that a frontier town craved.
