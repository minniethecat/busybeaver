## Specifications

The *[[java-steamship|Java]]* was a bark-[[rigging|rig]]ged [[sail|sailing]] vessel registered in Glasgow, Scotland. In July 1895 she was carrying cargo from Peru to Queenstown (now Cobh), Ireland, for orders — a typical tramp-cargo route of the late nineteenth century. She was a large, heavy ship, described by [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] as "foul" — her hull fouled by marine growth — which made her slow in the light winds that prevailed at the time of the encounter.

## Encounter with the Spray

On July 16, 1895, while the *[[spray|Spray]]* was crossing the North Atlantic toward the [[azores|Azores]], a large bark appeared on the lee bow, [[hull|hull down]] on the horizon. By 2:30 p.m., [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had closed the distance and spoke the stranger. She was the bark *[[java-steamship|Java]]* of Glasgow, homeward bound from South America (003-010).

The meeting became one of the comic highlights of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s narrative:

> She was the bark Java of Glasgow, from Peru for Queenstown for orders. Her old captain was bearish, but I met a bear once in Alaska that looked pleasanter. At least, the bear seemed pleased to meet me, but this grizzly old man! Well, I suppose my hail disturbed his siesta, and my little sloop passing his great ship had somewhat the effect on him that a red rag has upon a bull. (003-010)

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] described the *[[java-steamship|Java]]*'s captain as the most disagreeable mariner he met on the entire voyage. The old captain, annoyed that his afternoon siesta had been disturbed, roared across the water: "How long has it been calm about here?" [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s reply — "Dunno, cap'n. I haven't been here long" — did nothing to improve the man's mood (003-010).

The *[[spray|Spray]]*, with her great mainsail bellying to the light winds, was "just skipping along as nimbly as one could wish," while the heavy, foul-bottomed *[[java-steamship|Java]]* made poor headway. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had covered the distance from [[cape-sable|Cape Sable]] in fourteen days, and when the *[[java-steamship|Java]]*'s captain heard this, he bellowed to his mate: "Mate, come here and listen to the Yankee's yarn. Haul down the flag, mate, haul down the flag!" In the best of humor after all, the *[[java-steamship|Java]]* surrendered to the *[[spray|Spray]]* (003-010).

> The wind was light; his ship was heavy and foul, making poor headway, while the Spray, with a great mainsail bellying even to light winds, was just skipping along as nimbly as one could wish. (003-010)

## Significance

The *[[java-steamship|Java]]* encounter illustrates the social hierarchy of the sea in the 1890s. A deep-water bark captain, accustomed to command over a large crew and a substantial vessel, had little time for a lone sailor in a converted oyster sloop. The fact that the tiny *[[spray|Spray]]* was out[[sail|sailing]] the great bark only sharpened the insult.

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s humorous account of the encounter — preferring an Alaskan bear to the *[[java-steamship|Java]]*'s captain — became one of the comic [[current|set]]-pieces of his narrative. Yet beneath the humor lies a serious point: the *[[spray|Spray]]*, though small and crewed by one man, was proving herself on the open ocean. The *[[java-steamship|Java]]*'s grudging salute — hauling down her flag — was, in the language of the sea, an acknowledgment of superiority.
