## History with Alexander Hare

Prison Island, part of the Keeling Cocos atoll, gained its name from the conflict between two settlers: Alexander Hare and Captain John Clunies-Ross. Hare, a former governor of a wild colony in Borneo with "oceans of money," could not confine himself to the tame life of civilization. He retreated before Ross and his sturdy crew of eight Scotch sailors until he found himself and his harem of forty women confined to the island known to this day as Prison Island (016-008).

On the island, Hare — "like Bluebeard" — confined his wives in a castle. But the channel between the islands was narrow and the water shallow, and Ross's sailors "wore long boots" (016-008).

## Hare's Downfall

After a St. Andrew's Day celebration, Hare sent a note of complaint about the sailors trespassing in his flower garden, to which Ross famously roared across the channel: "Ahoy, there, on Prison Island! You Hare, don't you know that rum and roast pig are not a sailor's heaven?" (016-008). The lawless establishment was soon broken up when the women deserted Prison Island, putting themselves under Ross's protection. Hare then went to [[batavia|Batavia]], where he met his death (016-009).

> From this time on Hare had a hard time of it. He and Ross did not get on well as neighbors. The islands were too small and too near for characters so widely different.
>
> (016-008)
