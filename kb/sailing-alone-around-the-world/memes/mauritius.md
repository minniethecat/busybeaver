# Mauritius

Mauritius is a volcanic island in the southwestern [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]] — a green jewel of sugarcane plantations, coral [[reefing|reef]]s, and mountains, lying about 1,200 miles east of Africa. For [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], it was a welcome port of call after long ocean passages, a place to resupply and repair before the final run to [[cape-of-good-hope|the Cape]] of Good Hope.

## Geography

The island is of volcanic origin, surrounded by coral [[reefing|reef]]s that form a natural breakwater. Its highest peak, Pi[[tonnage|ton]] de la Petite Rivière Noire, rises to 2,717 feet. The climate is tropical, moderated by the surrounding ocean. Discovered by the Portuguese, colonized by the Dutch, [[current|set]]tled by the French, and captured by the British in 1810, Mauritius was a plantation economy — sugar, above all — worked by Indian and African labor.

## In the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] arrived at Mauritius (017-002) after crossing much of the [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]]. The island offered everything a weary sailor needed: a safe harbor, fresh provisions, and the hospitality of a colonial port accustomed to visiting ships. He made repairs to the *[[spray|Spray]]*, took on water and stores, and prepared for the run to [[cape-of-good-hope|the Cape]] of Good Hope.

The stop was practical rather than dramatic — no [[gale|storm]]s, no confrontations, no literary pilgrimages. But in the rhythm of a [[voyage|circumnavigation]], such stops are essential. They are the breathing spaces between the great passages.

## Significance

Mauritius represents the network of colonial ports that made [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s voyage possible — not famous, not celebrated, but vital. Without places like Mauritius, the solo circumnavigator would have had no safe harbors between [[australia|Australia]] and Africa.

> At Mauritius, where I drew a long breath, the *Spray* rested her wings, it being the season of fine weather. The hardships of the voyage, if there had been any, were now computed by officers of experience as nine tenths finished, and yet somehow I could not forget that the United States was still a long way off.
>
> (017-003)
