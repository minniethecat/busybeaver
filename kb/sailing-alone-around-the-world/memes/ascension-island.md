# Ascension Island

Ascension Island is a volcanic speck in the South Atlantic — a heap of cinders and lava rock, lying almost exactly midway between Africa and South America. A British possession since 1815, it served as a naval station and provisioning stop. For [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], nearing the end of his [[voyage|circumnavigation]], it was one of the last landfalls before [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] crossing home.¹ (019-001)

> After luncheon a royal mail was made up for Ascension, the island next on my way. Then Mr. Poole and his daughter paid the *Spray* a farewell visit, bringing me a basket of fruit. It was late in the evening before the anchor was up, and I bore off for the west, loath to leave my new friends. But fresh winds filled the sloop's sails once more, and I watched the beacon-light at Plantation House, the governor's parting signal for the *Spray*, till the island faded in the darkness astern and became one with the night, and by midnight the light itself had disappeared below the horizon.
>
> (019-007)

## Geography

The island is a mass of volcanic matter, thrown up from the bed of the ocean to the height of two thousand eight hundred and eighteen feet at the highest point above sea-level.² (019-016) It lies in 7° 35′ south latitude and 14° 25′ west longitude, in the very heart of the southeast trade-winds, about eight hundred and forty miles from the coast of Liberia.² (019-016)

The island has no natural harbor — only an open roadstead exposed to [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] swell. In the limited but rich soil at the top of the island, among the clouds, vegetation has taken root.² A little scientific farming is carried on under the supervision of a gentleman from Canada, and a few cattle and sheep are pastured there for the garrison mess.² Water storage is made on a large scale.²

## In the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] arrived at Ascension on the 27th of April, having sailed from [[st-helena|St. Helena]] after a warm farewell from [[governor-sterndale|Governor Sterndale]] and the American consul, Mr. Poole.³ (019-009) The island is garrisoned by a man-of-war crew, and the boatswain of the island came on board to greet him.³ (019-009)

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s stop at Ascension is memorable in the narrative less for geography than for an animal: the goat he had been given at [[st-helena|St. Helena]]. The creature had already devoured his straw hat, eaten his chart of the [[antigua|West Indies]], and threatened everything from [[jib|flying-jib]] to stern-davits.³ [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] called it "the worst pirate I met on the whole voyage."⁴ (019-008)

As the boatswain stepped out of his boat, the mutinous goat climbed into it, defying boatswain and crew.³ [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] hired them to land "the wretch" at once, "which they were only too willing to do, and there he fell into the hands of a most excellent Scotchman, with the chances that he would never get away."³

## The "Stone Frigate"

Ascension Island is officially called the "Stone Frigate, R.N.," and is rated "tender" to the South African Squadron.⁵ (019-016) It is a strategic point, and as [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] noted, "belonged to Great Britain before it got cold."⁵ (019-016) In his words, "this heap of cinders and lava rock is stored and fortified, and would stand a siege."⁵ (019-016)

## Significance

Ascension represents the home stretch — the final provisioning stop before [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] crossing. From here, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sailed into "the depths of solitude" once more, headed for the Caribbean and, ultimately, [[newport|Newport]]. The goat's departure marked the end of his ill luck with animal companions aboard [[spray|the Spray]].⁶
