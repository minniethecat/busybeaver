The ***North Star*** was a British merchant [[sail|sailing]] ship of [[london|London]] that [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]] encountered twice during his Atlantic crossing in late 1895 — once in mid-ocean and again at [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio de Janeiro]].

## Mid-Atlantic Encounter

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] spoke the *North Star* on September 25, 1895, near 5° N latitude and 26° 30' W longitude, in the equatorial Atlantic. At the time, the *[[spray|Spray]]* was thirty days out from [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]], making good time across [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]].

> "The great ship was out forty-eight days from Norfolk, Virginia, and was bound for Rio, where we met again about two months later." — (005-022)

The encounter occurred in the doldrums region near the equator, where [[sail|sailing]] ships often [[current|drift]]ed for days waiting for wind. For a solo sailor weeks into an ocean crossing, the sight of another vessel was a significant event — a chance to confirm position and break the isolation.

## Reunion at Rio

True to the mid-ocean expectation, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] encountered the *North Star* again at [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio de Janeiro]] about two months later. The coincidence of a small sloop and a large merchantman meeting twice — once at sea and once in port — across thousands of miles of ocean was a reminder of the shared routes and predictable rhythms of [[sail|sailing]]-ship travel in the 1890s.

The reunion also demonstrated the reliability of [[navigation|celestial navigation]] in the era before radio. Both vessels, navigating independently by sextant and chronometer, had reached the same port within weeks of each other — a routine but impressive achievement of nineteenth-century seamanship.

## Significance

The *North Star* represents one of the few named vessels [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] "spoke" (hailed and conversed with) during his Atlantic crossing. Such encounters were important to the solo sailor: they confirmed his position, connected him briefly to the larger maritime world, and demonstrated that his small sloop was keeping pace with larger merchant ships on the same routes.

The *North Star*'s passage from Norfolk to [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio]] via the equatorial Atlantic was a standard trade route of the period — American tobacco, cot[[tonnage|ton]], or manufactured goods southbound, Brazilian coffee and sugar northbound. The *[[spray|Spray]]*, by contrast, was on a voyage without commercial purpose — a distinction that [[current|set]] [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] apart from every other vessel he encountered.
