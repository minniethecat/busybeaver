## Slocum's Arrival

"It was about noon when the *Spray* came to anchor off Jamestown, and 'all hands' at once went ashore to pay respects to his Excellency the governor of the island, [[governor-sterndale|Sir R. A. Sterndale]]" (019-002). The governor welcomed Slocum warmly, remarking "that it was not often, nowadays, that a circumnavigator came his way," and arranged for him to deliver lectures about his voyage — first at Garden Hall to the people of Jamestown, and then at Plantation House for the governor and officers of the garrison (019-002).

After his stay at Plantation House, Slocum drove back down to Jamestown, making the excursion to Longwood along the way (019-005). Slocum departed St. Helena on the evening of April 20, watching the beacon-light at Plantation House until it disappeared below the horizon (019-007).

> It was about noon when the Spray came to anchor off Jamestown, and "all hands" at once went ashore to pay respects to his Excellency the governor of the island, Sir R. A. Sterndale. His Excellency, when I landed, remarked that it was not often, nowadays, that a circumnavigator came his way, and he cordially welcomed me, and arranged that I should tell about the voyage, first at Garden Hall to the people of Jamestown, and then at Plantation House.
>
> (019-002)
