## Visit

Slocum arrived at the island of Dominica on the 30th, where he stopped for water before continuing his voyage (020-012).

## Narrative Significance

Dominica was one of Slocum's final ports of call before completing his circumnavigation. Stopping for fresh water (020-012) was a routine but essential ritual of long-distance sailing — after nearly three years at sea, Slocum had refined the art of making efficient landfalls, taking only what he needed before pressing on toward his home port. The brief mention of this stop in the narrative reflects the workmanlike nature of the voyage's closing stages.
> The [[spray|Spray]] sailed from [[grenada|Grenada]] on the 28th of May, and coasted along under the lee of the Antilles, arriving at the island of Dominica on the 30th, where, for the want of knowing better, I cast anchor at the quarantine ground; for I was still without a chart of the islands, not having been able to get one even at Grenada. Here I not only met with further disappointment in the matter, but was threatened with a fine for the mistake I made in the anchorage.
>
> (020-012)
