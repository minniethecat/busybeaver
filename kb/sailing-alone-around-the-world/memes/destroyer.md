The ***Destroyer*** was an experimental warship designed by the Swedish-American inventor [[john-ericsson|John Ericsson]], carrying a submarine cannon of enormous length. [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]] commanded her on a delivery voyage from New York to Brazil in 1893, an experience that proved consequential for his later solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]].

## Specifications

The *Destroyer* was built to Ericsson's design as a vessel capable of at[[tack|tacking]] below the waterline. "The *Destroyer*, by the way, carried a submarine cannon of enormous length" (005-024). Little else is recorded of her technical specifications in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s narrative, but she was sufficiently seaworthy to cross [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] under [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s command.

[[john-ericsson|John Ericsson]] (1803–1889) was the famed inventor of the USS *Monitor* and a pioneer of naval engineering. The *Destroyer* represented a later chapter in his career, applying experimental weapons technology that was ahead of its time.

## Slocum's Delivery Voyage

In 1893 [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was employed to take the *Destroyer* from New York to Brazil to support the legal government against the rebel forces of Admiral Mello. The voyage across [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] was successful, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was owed wages for his service — wages he would never collect.

## The Brazilian Naval Rebellion

The *Destroyer* was deployed in the conflict between the Brazilian government and the Mello faction. "The *Destroyer* was in many ways the better ship of the two, but the Brazilians in their curious war sank her themselves at [[bahia|Bahia]]" (005-025).

With the ship went [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s hope of recovering the wages due him. "Within two years the whirligig of time had brought the Mello party into power, and although it was the legal government which had employed me, the so-called 'rebels' felt under less obligation to me than I could have wished" (005-025).

When [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] returned to Brazil in the *[[spray|Spray]]*, he inquired about the wages at [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio de Janeiro]]. The high official made a sardonic offer: "Captain, so far as we are concerned, you may have the ship, and if you care to accept her we will send an officer to show you where she is."

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] reflected: "I knew well enough where she was at that moment. The top of her smokestack being awash in [[bahia|Bahia]], it was more than likely that she rested on the bottom there" (005-027).

## Connection to the Spray's Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s experience commanding the *Destroyer* to Brazil gave him familiarity with Brazilian ports that proved useful during his [[voyage|circumnavigation]]. At [[pernambuco|Pernambuco]], he encountered the collector of customs — an unforgiving Mello supporter — who charged the *[[spray|Spray]]* [[tonnage|tonnage dues]] despite her yacht exemption.

> "Our consul reminded the collector of this and of the fact — without much diplomacy, I thought — that it was I who brought the *Destroyer* to Brazil." — (005-027)

The collector replied dryly: "Oh, yes; we remember it very well" (005-027). The *Destroyer* connection, far from being an as[[current|set]], had become a liability — the Mello faction remembered [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s service to their enemies.

## Legacy

The *Destroyer* episode encapsulates the political landscape [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] navigated in South America. His employment by the Brazilian government had been a professional engagement, not a political statement, but in the aftermath of civil war such distinctions carried no weight.

The unpaid wages and the sunken ship became part of the background that pushed [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] toward his solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]] — a voyage free of governments, factions, and unpaid debts.
