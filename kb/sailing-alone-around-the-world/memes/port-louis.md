# Port Louis

Port Louis is the capital and chief harbor of [[mauritius|Mauritius]], the volcanic island in the [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]] where the *[[spray|Spray]]* made an extended stop in September–October 1897. The port offered [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] rest, refit, and one of the most charming episodes of the entire [[voyage|circumnavigation]].

## Arrival and Refit

The *[[spray|Spray]]* arrived at [[mauritius|Mauritius]] on September 19, 1897, after a passage from [[rodriguez-island|Rodriguez Island]]. "At [[mauritius|Mauritius]], where I drew a long breath, [[spray|the Spray]] rested her wings, it being the season of fine weather" — (017-003). The reception was generous: "[[spray|the Spray]] was tendered the use of the military dock free of charge, and was thoroughly refitted by the port authorities" — (017-011).

## The Harbor Sail

The most memorable episode at Port Louis was an impromptu harbor cruise with a party of young Mauritian ladies and their aunt. They "just stood up and laughed at seas tumbling aboard, while I was at the [[rudder|helm]] making the worst weather of it I could, and spinning yarns to the aunt about sea-serpents and whales" — (017-007).

The excursion proved so captivating that the passengers refused to turn back. "We were fifteen miles or more at sea before they ceased the eager cry, 'Go on!' Then [[sloop|the sloop]] swung round, I still hoping to be back to Port Louis in time to keep my appointment" — (017-008). But the return was further delayed by a stop at Tombo Bay for a swim, and by the time the harbormaster's launch came out to meet them, "it was too late to get [[sloop|the sloop]] into Port Louis that night" — (017-008).

The *[[spray|Spray]]* spent the night in Tombo Bay with her passengers aboard, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] prepared "a roof for the night [[deck|on deck]] with the [[sail|sails]]" — (017-008). The event became local legend: "The voyage was the event of Port Louis; such a thing as young ladies [[sail|sailing]] about the harbor, even, was almost unheard of before" — (017-010).

## A Port of Connections

Port Louis served as a social and logistical hub for the remainder of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]] passage. He carried letters from [[mauritius|Mauritius]] to [[grenada|Grenada]] in the Caribbean: "Lady Bruce, in a note to [[spray|the Spray]] at Port Louis, said [[grenada|Grenada]] was a lovely island, and she wished [[sloop|the sloop]] might call there on the voyage home" — (020-011). This network of colonial connections — letters of introduction, dockyard hospitality, and the shared language of empire — shaped the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s reception at every subsequent port.
