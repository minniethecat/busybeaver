## Slocum's Passing

Two days after Christmas 1897, the *Spray* "passed Cape Agulhas in company with the steamship *Scotsman*, now with a fair wind" (018-004). The keeper of the lighthouse on Agulhas exchanged signals with the *Spray* as she sailed by, and afterward wrote to Slocum in New York with congratulations on the completion of his voyage. The lighthouse keeper "seemed to think the incident of two ships of so widely different types passing his cape together worthy of a place on canvas, and he went about having the picture made" (018-004).

Slocum reflected on these remote stations: "At lonely stations like this hearts grow responsive and sympathetic, and even poetic. This feeling was shown toward the *Spray* along many a rugged coast, and reading many a kind signal thrown out to her gave one a grateful feeling for all the world" (018-004).

Shortly after passing Cape Agulhas, "one more gale of wind came down upon the *Spray* from the west," but she dodged it by putting into Simons Bay (018-005). From there, Slocum sailed around the [[cape-of-good-hope|Cape of Good Hope]], and the remainder of the voyage seemed assured.

> Two days later, the Spray, having recovered the distance lost in the gale, passed Cape Agulhas in company with the steamship Scotsman, now with a fair wind.
