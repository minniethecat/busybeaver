# Charles Island

A large island in [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]], [[patagonia|Patagonia]], that served as a [[gale|storm]] refuge for the *[[spray|Spray]]* during [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s perilous transit of the strait in 1896.

## Geography

Charles Island lies in [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]], south of [[coffee-island|Coffee Island]], with only a narrow channel separating the two (007-017). The island features a cove where vessels can anchor in a bed of kelp, and a bluff point that provided some shelter from the prevailing westerly gales (007-018)(009-007). The shoreline is thickly wooded, typical of the [[patagonia|Patagonia]]n archipelago.

## Role in the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] anchored at Charles Island twice during his passage through [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]]. On his westward journey, after beating hard against contrary winds, he brought the *[[spray|Spray]]* into a cove on the island, where she "remained undisturbed two days, with both anchors down in a bed of kelp" while a fierce wind[[gale|storm]] raged outside (007-018).

On his return eastward passage, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] again sought shelter at Charles Island. He encountered "another gale, that brought her in the lee of great Charles Island for shelter" (009-007). During this second visit, he had a tense encounter with a tribe of [[fuegians|Fuegian natives]] on the bluff. A canoe carrying two squaws and an Indian — whom [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] recognized as "[[black-pedro|Black Pedro]]," a notorious figure — approached [[sloop|the sloop]] begging for food. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] kept the rest of the canoes at a distance by displaying a Martini-Henry rifle and held a revolver ready, having been warned of [[black-pedro|Pedro]]'s murderous reputation (009-007).

From Charles Island, the *[[spray|Spray]]* crossed to [[fortescue-bay|Fortescue Bay]], where [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] spent a "comfortable night under the lee of high land" (009-009).

> By daylight the next morning the *Spray* was again under way, beating hard; but she came to in a cove in Charles Island, two and a half miles along on her course. Here she remained undisturbed two days, with both anchors down in a bed of kelp.
>
> (007-018)

## Historical Context

The [[strait-of-magellan|Strait of Magellan]] was the only viable route between [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] and Pacific prior to the opening of the [[panama|Panama]] Canal in 1914. The [[fuegians|Fuegian people]]s who inhabited the shores of the strait, including Charles Island, were known to passing sailors. By the 1890s, encounters between lone yachtsmen like [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] and the indigenous population carried significant risk, exacerbated by decades of hostile contact with sealers, whalers, and convict transports.
