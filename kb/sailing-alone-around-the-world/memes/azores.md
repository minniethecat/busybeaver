# Azores

The Azores are a group of nine volcanic islands scattered across the mid-Atlantic, roughly 900 miles west of Portugal. For centuries, they have been [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] sailor's first or last landfall.

For [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], they were his first foreign port and the scene of one of the most memorable episodes of the entire voyage.

## Geography

The islands are volcanic peaks of a submerged mountain range, with steep cliffs, crater lakes, and lush vegetation. [[flores-island|Flores]], the westernmost island, rises "like a mountain of silver" from the sea, and "remained many years undiscovered after the principal group of the islands had been colonized." (003-015)

[[pico|Pico]], with its towering volcanic cone, "loomed above the clouds." (003-016) "Only those who have seen the Azores from the deck of a vessel realize the beauty of the mid-ocean picture." (003-016)

> Early on the morning of July 20 I saw Pico looming above the clouds on the starboard bow. Lower lands burst forth as the sun burned away the morning fog, and island after island came into view. As I approached nearer, cultivated fields appeared, "and oh, how green the corn!" Only those who have seen the Azores from the deck of a vessel realize the beauty of the mid-ocean picture.
>
> (003-016)

## In the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] first sighted the Azores on July 19, when "a mystic dome like a mountain of silver stood alone in the sea ahead." (003-015) Though land was hidden by a "white, glistening haze," he knew it was [[flores-island|Flores Island]]. (003-015)

The next morning, July 20, [[pico|Pico]] appeared "looming above the clouds on the [[starboard|starboard bow]]," and "lower lands burst forth as the sun burned away the morning fog, and island after island came into view." (003-016)

He cast anchor at [[fayal|Fayal]] ([[fayal|Horta]]) "exactly eighteen days from [[cape-sable|Cape Sable]]." (003-017) The American consul came alongside immediately, and a young naval officer offered his services as pilot — though after "fouling all the craft in port and sinking a lighter," [[spray|the Spray]] was moored with little additional damage. (003-017)

### Island Hospitality

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] found the Azoreans exceptionally generous. "Islanders are always the kindest people in the world," he wrote, "and I met none anywhere kinder than the good hearts of this place." (003-018) It was fruit season, and "there was soon more of all kinds of it put on board than I knew what to do with." (003-018)

The day after his arrival was a saint's feast day. Boats loaded with people came from other islands to [[fayal|Horta]], "the capital, or Jerusalem, of the Azores," and the deck of [[spray|the Spray]] "was crowded from morning till night with men, women, and children." (003-019)

A particularly moving encounter came when a native harnessed a team and drove [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] a day around [[fayal|Fayal]]. Explaining why, he spoke in broken English of his own experience in America — the difficulty of being unable to speak English, until someone listened to his story. He had made a promise to his saint: "If ever a stranger came to my country, I would try to make him happy." (003-019)

### The Pilot of the Pinta

Leaving the Azores proved more eventful than arriving. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] [[current|set]] sail from [[fayal|Horta]] on July 24, and after mending [[rigging|rig]]ging damaged in a squall, passed the island of [[pico|Pico]]. (004-003)

He had been living luxuriously on "fresh bread, butter, vegetables, and fruits of all kinds," and had eaten freely of plums and a [[pico|Pico]] white cheese. (004-003)

That night the combination laid him low. "Between cramps I got the mainsail down," he wrote, then went below and threw himself on the [[cabin|cabin floor]] in great pain. (004-003) He became delirious. When he came to, he found a strange man at the [[rudder|helm]] — a figure "in a large red cap cockbilled over his left ear," with shaggy black whiskers, who "would have been taken for a pirate in any part of the world." (004-003)

The stranger addressed him: "Señor, I have come to do you no harm. I am one of [[christopher-columbus|Columbus]]'s crew. I am the [[pinta|pilot of the Pinta]] come to aid you. Lie quiet, señor captain, and I will guide your ship [[tonnage|ton]]ight." (004-003) Then, biting off a quid of black twist, he added: "You did wrong, captain, to mix cheese with plums. White cheese is never safe unless you know whence it comes." (004-003)

## Significance

The Azores were [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s first foreign landfall and first test of small-boat ocean crossing. The pilot-of-the-[[pinta|Pinta]] episode — whether fever dream or ghost story — would become one of the most frequently recounted tales of the voyage.

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] himself later told it to [[captain-howard|Captain Howard]] of the *Colombia* during a gale off [[patagonia|Patagonia]]. (006-015) The islands represented not just a geographic milestone but a narrative one: the point where a solo [[sail|sailing]] voyage became something strange and rich.
