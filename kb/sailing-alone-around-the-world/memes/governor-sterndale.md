# Governor Sterndale

Governor Sterndale was a British colonial administrator — "[[sir-r-a-sterndale|Sir R. A. Sterndale]]" (019-002) — whom [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] encountered at an island in the [[pacific-ocean|South Pacific]]. His warm reception, and the gifts he and his wife provided, made the stop one of the voyage's most agreeable interludes.

## Biography

Sterndale served as governor of a small island territory in the [[pacific-ocean|South Pacific]]. Beyond [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s account, little is preserved of his career — he belongs to the legion of colonial administrators whose names survive only in the narratives of passing travelers. But [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s portrait is vivid: a hospitable man, proud of his island, who did everything possible to make the solitary sailor welcome.

## Role in the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was received with exceptional warmth. "His Excellency, when I landed, received me kindly" (019-002). The governor extended genuine hospitality, and "Lady Sterndale had sent a large fruitcake" (019-006) — a gift that, after months of ship's provisions, must have been received with delight. The governor also gave [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] "a bag of coffee" (019-008) — practical provisions for the continuing voyage.

> His Excellency, when I landed, remarked that it was not often, nowadays, that a circumnavigator came his way, and he cordially welcomed me, and arranged that I should tell about the voyage, first at Garden Hall to the people of Jamestown, and then at Plantation House — the governor's residence, which is in the hills a mile or two back — to his Excellency and the officers of the garrison and their friends.
>
> (019-002)

Sterndale's hospitality is notable for its completeness: food, coffee, conversation, and the comfort of civilized company after weeks of solitude. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] recorded the encounter with gratitude, the governor's kindness standing out amid the long Pacific passages.

## Significance

Governor Sterndale represents the network of colonial hospitality that supported [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s voyage. Like [[governor-roberts|Governor Roberts]] at [[rodriguez-island|Rodriguez]], Sterndale offered not just supplies but human connection — the fellowship that made the solo voyage bearable. His fruitcake and coffee, small gifts in themselves, became part of the voyage's currency of kindness.
