# Marquesas Islands

The Marquesas Islands marked a significant waypoint in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s Pacific crossing.

After departing [[juan-fernandez-island|Juan Fernandez]], he sailed westward through the [[pacific-ocean|South Pacific]] and sighted the southernmost island of the Marquesas group before continuing to [[samoa|Samoa]].

The landfall came on the forty-third day out from [[juan-fernandez-island|Juan Fernandez]] — a passage that tested [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s seamanship and his trust in intuitive navigation.

## Geography

The Marquesas are a group of volcanic islands in the [[pacific-ocean|South Pacific]], part of French Polynesia. Nuku Hiva is the largest island, and it was the southernmost island of this group — "clear-cut and lofty" — that [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sighted from the deck of the *[[spray|Spray]]* (011-023).

The islands lie roughly 3,000 miles west of [[juan-fernandez-island|Juan Fernandez]] and 2,000 miles east of [[samoa|Samoa]].

When [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] made his landfall, he experienced a moment of navigational wonder. His longitude by dead-reckoning placed him ahead of the island, while a [[sextant|lunar observation]] placed him behind it.

Yet both estimates proved nearly true: "The verified longitude when abreast was somewhere between the two reckonings; this was extraordinary" (011-023).

## Role in the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s passage to the Marquesas was swift. As he recorded, the *[[spray|Spray]]* sailed "with a bone in her mouth, toward the Marquesas, in the west" (011-018).

The [[trade-wind|trade winds]], when they finally arrived, "came with a bang, and made up for lost time; and the *[[spray|Spray]]*, under [[reefing|reef]]s, sometimes one, sometimes two, flew before a gale for a great many days" (011-018).

Between [[juan-fernandez-island|Juan Fernandez]] and the Marquesas, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] experienced shifting wind patterns that tested his seamanship across the vast Pacific (011-021).

He observed that "about every seven days the wind freshened and drew several points farther than usual from the direction of the pole," while a heavy swell from the southwest indicated gales in the higher latitudes (011-021).

> If the trades were tardy, however, when they did come they came with a bang, and made up for lost time; and the *Spray*, under reefs, sometimes one, sometimes two, flew before a gale for a great many days, with a bone in her mouth, toward the Marquesas, in the west, which, she made on the forty-third day out, and still kept on sailing. My time was all taken up those days—not by standing at the helm; no man, I think, could stand or sit and steer a vessel round the world: I did better than that; for I sat and read my books, mended my clothes, or cooked my meals and ate them in peace. I had already found that it was not good to be alone, and so I made companionship with what there was around me, sometimes with the universe and sometimes with my own insignificant self; but my books were always my friends, let fail all else. Nothing could be easier or more restful than my voyage in the trade-winds.
>
> (011-018)

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] did not land at the Marquesas, instead pressing on toward [[samoa|Samoa]]. The islands served as a navigational milestone — proof that he had crossed the broadest expanse of the [[pacific-ocean|South Pacific]].

## Navigation by the Heavens

The long Pacific passage was a testament to [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s [[navigation|celestial navigation]]. He wrote: "I sailed with a free wind day after day, marking the position of my ship on the chart with considerable precision; but this was done by intuition, I think, more than by slavish calculations" (011-019).

For an entire month, the *[[spray|Spray]]* held her course true without even a light in the [[compass|binnacle]]. He reflected: "The Southern Cross I saw every night abeam. The sun every morning came up astern; every evening it went down ahead. I wished for no other compass to guide me, for these were true" (011-019).

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s confidence in his ship was absolute. He would wake to find the sun shining into the cabin, hear water rushing past with only a thin plank between him and the depths.

He reflected: "it was all [[rigging|rig]]ht; it was my ship on her course, [[sail|sailing]] as no other ship had ever sailed before in the world" (011-020). The rushing water told him she was "going at her utmost speed" (011-020).

## The Pacific Immensity

The scale of [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]] impressed itself deeply on [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]. "To cross [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]] Ocean, even under the most favorable circumstances, brings you for many days close to nature, and you realize the vastness of the sea," he wrote (011-022).

Watching the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s [[course|track]] inch across the chart, he reflected that "slowly but surely the mark of my little ship's course on the [[course|track]]-chart reached out on the ocean and across it" (011-022).

On the forty-third day from land — "a long time to be at sea alone" — [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sighted the "bonny Marquesas" (011-022). The sighting marked the successful crossing of the world's largest ocean in a vessel barely larger than a ship's longboat.
