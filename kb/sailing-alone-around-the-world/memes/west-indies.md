## Geography

The West Indies is an extensive archipelago stretching from the southern coast of North America to the northern coast of South America, separating the Caribbean Sea from the Atlantic Ocean. The islands include the Greater Antilles (Cuba, Hispaniola, Jamaica, Puerto Rico) and the Lesser Antilles, which include Antigua and Dominica, Slocum's ports of call.

## Role in the Voyage

Slocum enjoyed his "first cup of coffee at Antigua, West Indies, which, after all, was quite" a welcome refreshment after his long Atlantic crossing (019-006). A curious incident occurred when a fish onboard the Spray "destroyed my chart of the West Indies, in the cabin, one day, while" Slocum was occupied elsewhere (019-008), a lighthearted episode that underscored both the informality of life aboard and a real navigational risk — the loss of a chart in unfamiliar waters.
> The mistake I made was that I did not chain him to the mast instead of tying him with grass ropes less securely. The old one began depredations by eating my chart of the West Indies, in the cabin, one day, while I was about my work forward, thinking that the critter was securely tied on deck by the pumps. Alas! there was not a rope in the sloop proof against that goat's awful teeth!
>
> (019-008)


## Historical Context

The West Indies were among the first regions of the Americas colonized by European powers, with a long history of sugar plantations, maritime trade, and naval conflict. By the 1890s, the islands were well-integrated into global shipping routes, and ports like Antigua offered reliable supplies for vessels completing transatlantic passages.
