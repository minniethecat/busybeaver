## Early Voyage Reference

A large schooner came out of Liverpool, Nova Scotia, one morning during the early stages of Slocum's narrative (003-004).

## Narrative Significance

Liverpool, Nova Scotia, appears early in the voyage as part of the familiar coastal waters Slocum knew from his years as a merchant captain. The sighting of a large schooner emerging from this port (003-004) anchors the opening chapters in the geography of the North Atlantic seaboard, a region where Slocum had spent much of his professional life before setting out on his solo circumnavigation.
> At noon on July 3 Ironbound Island was abeam. The [[spray|Spray]] was again at her best. A large schooner came out of Liverpool, Nova Scotia, this morning, steering eastward. The Spray put her hull down astern in five hours. At 6:45 p.m. I was in close under Chebucto Head light, near [[halifax|Halifax]] harbor.
>
> (003-004)
