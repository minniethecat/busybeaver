Westport is a small harbor town on Brier Island, [[nova-scotia|Nova Scotia]], at the entrance to [[bay-of-fundy|the Bay]] of [[bay-of-fundy|Fundy]]. It held deep personal significance for [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]], who lived there for eight years as a boy before going to sea. His return during the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s shakedown cruise was both a homecoming and a practical test of the little [[sloop|sloop's]] seaworthiness.

> I was delighted to reach Westport. Any port at all would have been delightful after the terrible thrashing I got in the fierce sou’west rip, and to find myself among old schoolmates now was charming. It was the 13th of the month, and 13 is my lucky number—a fact registered long before Dr. Nansen sailed in search of the north pole with his crew of thirteen. Perhaps he had heard of my success in taking a most extraordinary ship successfully to Brazil with that number of crew. The very stones on Briar’s Island I was glad to see again, and I knew them all. The little shop round the corner, which for thirty-five years I had not seen, was the same, except that it looked a deal smaller. It wore the same shingles—I was sure of it; for did not I know the roof where we boys, night after night, hunted for the skin of a black cat, to be taken on a dark night, to make a plaster for a poor lame man? Lowry the tailor lived there when boys were boys. In his day he was fond of the gun.
>
> (002-015)

## Geography

Westport harbor lies on the western side of Brier Island, which sits at the mouth of [[bay-of-fundy|the Bay]] of [[bay-of-fundy|Fundy]] in [[nova-scotia|Nova Scotia]]. The approach to Westport is hazardous — [[bay-of-fundy|the Bay]] of [[bay-of-fundy|Fundy]] is known for the world's highest tides and powerful [[tide|tide-race]]s.

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] entered the harbor through the "worst [[tide|tide-race]] in [[bay-of-fundy|the Bay]] of [[bay-of-fundy|Fundy]]," having been given a misleading course by a local fisherman that nearly led the *[[spray|Spray]]* onto the southwest ledge (002-013).

## Role in the Voyage

During the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s shakedown cruise in May 1895, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sailed from [[gloucester|Gloucester, Massachusetts]], across [[bay-of-fundy|the Bay]] of [[bay-of-fundy|Fundy]] to Westport. The passage was rough — he described his arrival with relief: "I was delighted to reach Westport. Any port at all would have been delightful after the terrible thrashing I got in the fierce sou'west rip" (002-015).

The date of arrival, May 13, was significant to [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], who considered 13 his lucky number. He noted this was "a fact registered long before [[fridtjof-nansen|Dr. Nansen]] sailed in search of the north pole with his crew of thirteen," adding wryly that [[fridtjof-nansen|Nansen]] "had heard of my success in taking a most extraordinary ship successfully to sea with a crew of one" (002-015).

Westport was more than a port of refuge — it was a homecoming. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] found himself "among old schoolmates" and recognized every stone on the island. "The very stones on [[briars-island|Briar's Island]] I was glad to see again, and I knew them all" (002-015).

He noted the little shop around the corner that he had not seen for thirty-five years, still wearing the same shingles. The visit was also practical: [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] overhauled the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s seams, which had "started nothing" despite the severe thrashing in [[bay-of-fundy|the Bay]] of [[bay-of-fundy|Fundy]] (002-016).

## The Fisherman of Briar's Island

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s approach to Westport was nearly disastrous thanks to misleading directions from a local fisherman. Having hailed a man at anchor on the western fishing-grounds, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] asked for a course to Brier's Island.

The fisherman's response was less than helpful: "Before he made up his mind to answer me at all, he improved the occasion of his own curiosity to know where I was from, and if I was alone, and if I didn't have 'no dorg nor no cat'" (002-014).

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] remarked dryly that "it was the first time in all my life at sea that I had heard a hail for information answered by a question" (002-014). The fisherman may have said "east-southeast," but [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] thought he said "east-northeast" and changed course accordingly — a miscommunication that nearly put the *[[spray|Spray]]* on the dangerous southwest ledge (002-014).

## Departure and Refit

After his visit, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] made a short excursion with friends to [[st-marys-bay|St. Mary's Bay]], an old cruising ground, before returning to the island to prepare for [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] crossing (002-016).

He then sailed to [[yarmouth|Yarmouth]] to take on provisions — butter, potatoes, and fresh water — the final supplies before departing North American waters for the open ocean (002-016).

Bad weather and strong head winds prevailed outside, but [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was in no hurry: the *[[spray|Spray]]* had proven herself in the worst [[bay-of-fundy|the Bay]] of [[bay-of-fundy|Fundy]] could offer, and her seams had held firm.
