# Buenos Aires

Buenos Aires was never on [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s [[sail|sailing]] plan — he arrived by steamer from [[montevideo|Montevideo]], on a whim and an invitation. But the visit left a mark on the *[[spray|Spray]]*, literally: her mast was shortened there, her bowsprit sawed off, and her captain, temporarily without a ship, pondered the march of progress over two cities he knew from an earlier era.

## Geography

Buenos Aires sits on the southern shore of the [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio]] de la Plata, the great estuary that divides Argentina from [[uruguay|Uruguay]]. In the 1890s, it was a booming port city, the commercial capital of the Argentine Republic, with a harbor crowded by steamships and [[sail|sailing]] vessels from every maritime nation. Across the river, less than a hundred miles away, lay [[montevideo|Montevideo]] — close enough for a day trip by steamer.

## In the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had no intention of visiting Buenos Aires when he anchored at [[montevideo|Montevideo]]. But the steamer captain of the *Liberty*, newly famous for rescuing the crew of a burning ship, "took the trip in her to Buenos Aires, where she arrived early on the day of the battle of flowers" — (006-014). The timing was serendipitous — the city was in festival.

He found the place transformed from his earlier memories. "I had not been in Buenos Aires for a number of years. The place which once sold whisky and coffins in Buenos Aires" — (006-019) had given way to something more urbane. "The march of civilization has made strides" — he observed wryly.

The practical work came after the holiday. Returning to [[montevideo|Montevideo]] to rejoin the *[[spray|Spray]]*, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] decided to modify [[sloop|the sloop]] for the difficult waters ahead. He "unshipped [[sloop|the sloop]]'s mast at Buenos Aires and shortened it by seven feet" — (006-021), a substantial modification to reduce windage in the [[southern-ocean|Southern Ocean]]. He also sawed off part of the bowsprit — "the bowsprit which I had sawed off at Buenos Aires" — (007-019) — which he kept aboard as a spare.

The departure from Buenos Aires marks the opening of Chapter VII: "Weighing anchor at Buenos Aires" — (007-001). The *[[spray|Spray]]*, "trimmed in every way, sailed from Buenos Aires" — (007-002) on the next leg of her journey south toward the [[strait-of-magellan|Strait of Magellan]].

## Historical Context

Buenos Aires in the mid-1890s was experiencing a period of explosive growth, driven by agricultural exports, immigration, and foreign investment. The sleepy colonial town [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] remembered had become a cosmopolitan city. Its port was one of the busiest in South America, and the contrast with the rough [[patagonia|Patagonia]]n [[current|set]]tlements he would soon encounter — [[sandy-point|Sandy Point]], for instance — was stark.
