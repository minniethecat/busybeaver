# Captain Jones

Captain Jones was an [[united-states|America]]n shipmaster who appears twice in *[[sail|Sail]]ing Alone Around the World* — first as [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s interpreter in [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio de Janeiro]], then as the captain of the pearl-fishing vessel *Tarawa* in the [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]]. He is one of the most warmly portrayed characters in the narrative, described by [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] as "the heart-yarn of a sailor."

## Biography

Jones was an [[united-states|America]]n, though "the only [[united-states|America]]n aboard" his own vessel the *Tarawa* — his crew being, as [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] noted with amusement, all expert "guessers" (016-005). He captained a pearl-fishing vessel built in California, operating in the waters of the [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]] between [[australia|Australia]] and [[new-guinea|New Guinea]].

At [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio de Janeiro]], Jones was among a group of old shipmasters who inspected the *[[spray|Spray]]* before her departure for [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]]. Each contributed something to her outfit — Jones gave her an anchor (003-002).

## Role in the Voyage

### Rio de Janeiro

When [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] prepared to leave [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio]] for the treacherous waters of [[patagonia|Patagonia]], Captain Jones acted as his interpreter and contributed essential equipment:

> "Captain Jones, who had acted as my interpreter at Rio, gave her an anchor, and one of the steamers gave her a cable to match it. She never dragged Jones's anchor once on the voyage, and the cable not only stood the strain on a lee shore, but when towed off Cape Horn helped break combing seas astern that threatened to board her." — (003-002)

The anchor proved one of the most reliable pieces of equipment aboard the *[[spray|Spray]]*, never dragging throughout the entire [[voyage|circumnavigation]] (003-002).

### Torres Strait

Months later, entering the [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]] at dusk, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] encountered Jones again in dramatically different circumstances. Jones, now captaining the pearl-fisher *Tarawa*, guided the *[[spray|Spray]]* to a safe anchorage by directing her from his deck (016-005).

The next morning, Jones demonstrated his characteristic generosity:

> "On the following morning Captain Jones brought on board two pairs of exquisite pearl shells, the most perfect ones I ever saw. They were probably the best he had, for Jones was the heart-yarn of a sailor." — (016-005)

Jones also arranged for the Jardine family — [[current|set]]tlers at nearby Somer[[current|set]] — to visit the *[[spray|Spray]]*. Mrs. Jardine, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] learned, was the niece of [[king-malietoa|King Malietoa]] of [[samoa|Samoa]] (016-005).

## Significance

Captain Jones exemplifies the network of [[united-states|America]]n seafarers [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] encountered in ports [[voyage|around the world]]. His appearances at opposite ends of the voyage — [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio]] and [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]] — illustrate the interconnected maritime world of the late nineteenth century, where an [[united-states|America]]n shipmaster might turn up as an interpreter in [[brazil|Brazil]] and a pearl fisherman in [[australia|Australia]].

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s evident affection for Jones — calling him "the heart-yarn of a sailor" and noting Jones was "never heard to guess at all" unlike his crew — marks him as one of the voyage's most memorable minor characters (016-005). The anchor Jones donated accompanied the *[[spray|Spray]]* through the worst weather of the voyage, serving as a tangible link between the goodwill [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] received and his successful [[voyage|circumnavigation]].
