# Indian Ocean

The Indian Ocean is the third-largest of the world's oceanic divisions. [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]] crossed it from east to west during the latter stages of his [[voyage|circumnavigation]], [[sail|sailing]] from [[thursday-island|Thursday Island]] in the [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]] across to [[rodriguez-island|Rodrigues]] Island and then to South Africa.

## Geography

The Indian Ocean is bounded by Asia to the north, Africa to the west, [[australia|Australia]] to the east, and the [[southern-ocean|Southern Ocean]] to the south. For [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], the Indian Ocean crossing represented the last great open-water passage before rounding [[cape-of-good-hope|the Cape]] of Good Hope and entering [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] for his homeward run (017-005).

## Role in the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] entered the Indian Ocean from the [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]] in late June, after saying farewell to the pearl fishermen at [[thursday-island|Thursday Island]]:

> "On June 24 the *Spray*, well fitted in every way, sailed for the long voyage ahead, down the Indian Ocean. Mr. Douglas gave her a flag as she was leaving his island. The *Spray* had now passed nearly all the dangers of the Coral Sea and Torres Strait, which, indeed, were not a few." — (017-005)

[[robert-louis-stevenson|Robert Louis Stevenson]]'s widow, whom [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had met in [[samoa|Samoa]], contributed a crucial navigational aid: "Mrs. [[robert-louis-stevenson|Stevenson]] also gave me a great directory of the Indian Ocean" — a gift [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] received "with a feeling of reverential awe" (013-002).

The crossing was marked by long stretches of solitude. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] noted: "[[sail|sailing]] down the last stretch of the Indian Ocean, where mosquitos came by hundreds from rainwater poured out of the heavens" — one of the few times living creatures disturbed his solitude at sea (020-003).

The *[[spray|Spray]]* reached [[rodriguez-island|Rodrigues]] Island, far out in the Indian Ocean, which "wore a hard, weather-beaten appearance there, all alone, far out in the Indian Ocean, like land a[[current|drift]]" (019-002).

## History

The Indian Ocean has been a major trade route since ancient times, connecting the spice trade of the East Indies with Africa and Europe. By [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s era, steamships increasingly dominated these routes, making his solo [[sail|sailing]] passage in a small sloop all the more remarkable (017-005).
