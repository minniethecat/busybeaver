When the *[[spray|Spray]]* ran aground on the [[uruguay|Uruguayan coast]] near Castillo Chicos, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] found himself stranded on a wild and unfamiliar shore. Among the local people who came to his aid — a boy who fetched butter and eggs, a coastguard from Fort Teresa, and neighbors from nearby ranches — was a German [[current|set]]tler who proved "of great assistance to me in many ways" (006-005).

## Summary

On November 28, 1895, shortly after departing [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio de Janeiro]], the *[[spray|Spray]]* encountered a gale and was driven ashore on the sands of [[uruguay|Uruguay]] at Castillo Chicos, about seven miles south of the Brazil–[[uruguay|Uruguay]] border (006-002) (006-005). [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] worked through the night to secure the vessel and eventually made it to shore exhausted. By morning, local people had gathered — first a boy on horseback, then a German [[current|set]]tler from a neighboring ranch, and later a coastguard from Fort Teresa. A despatch from the port captain of [[montevideo|Montevideo]] ordered all possible assistance, and a steam-tug was sent to tow the *[[spray|Spray]]* free (006-007). [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] subsequently anchored at [[maldonado|Maldonado]], where the British consul provided fresh provisions.

## Context

The stranding occurred on the first leg of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s South American coastal passage. Having left [[rio-de-janeiro|Rio de Janeiro]] on November 28, the *[[spray|Spray]]* immediately ran into a coastal gale. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had been observing that while some small vessels could outsail the *[[spray|Spray]]* by day, "they fell astern of her by night" — her self-[[helm|steering]] made her relentless (006-002). But this advantage could not prevent the grounding in heavy seas.

The coast of [[uruguay|Uruguay]] at Castillo Chicos was sparsely populated ranchland. The nearest formal authority was the coastguard station at Fort Teresa, a few miles distant (006-006). The region was Spanish-speaking, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] — a New England Yankee — was entirely a foreigner.

## Narrative

The stranding was a close call. The *[[spray|Spray]]* went ashore during the night, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] spent hours trying to get a line to shore through heavy surf:

> "After righting the dory for the fourth time, I finally succeeded by the utmost care in keeping her upright while I hauled myself into her and with one of the oars, which I had recovered, paddled to the shore, somewhat the worse for wear and pretty full of salt water." — (006-003)

Exhausted, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] threw himself on the sand above the tide to rest. But his solitude was short-lived:

> "I had not been long on the shore when I heard the patter, patter of a horse's feet approaching along the hard beach, which ceased as it came abreast of the sand where I lay." — (006-004)

The rider was a local boy. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] told him he wished to trade ship's biscuits for butter and milk, and "a broad grin lighted up his face" — the promise of biscuits made him [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s "bosom friend" (006-005). The boy "almost flew home, and returned quickly with butter, milk, and eggs" (006-005).

With the boy came others from neighboring ranches, "old and young," including "a German [[current|set]]tler, who was of great assistance to me in many ways" (006-005). A coastguard then arrived from Fort Teresa, sent to protect [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s property — though [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had already recovered his stolen revolver from a passing "merchant" by "a bold front" (006-006).

## Aftermath

The stranding ended with substantial official assistance. A despatch came from the port captain of [[montevideo|Montevideo]], commanding the coastguards to render "every assistance," and a powerful steam-tug arrived to tow the *[[spray|Spray]]* to [[montevideo|Montevideo]] (006-007). [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] anchored at [[maldonado|Maldonado]] the following day, where the British consul, his daughter, and another young lady came aboard [[course|bearing]] fresh eggs, strawberries, milk, and sweet bread (006-008).

The generosity of strangers — the German [[current|set]]tler among them — turned what could have been a disastrous shipwreck into a heartening demonstration of human kindness on a remote coast. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had been alone, exhausted, and at the mercy of the sea; by the next morning, a network of locals, officials, and consular staff had mobilized to help him.

## Significance

The stranding and rescue at Castillo Chicos illustrated a recurring theme of the [[voyage|circumnavigation]]: the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s vulnerability was matched by the goodwill of strangers. The German [[current|set]]tler — unnamed but described as "of great assistance in many ways" — represented the kind of spontaneous generosity [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] encountered repeatedly, from [[uruguay|Uruguay]] to [[keel|Keel]]ing Cocos to [[durban|Durban]]. The episode also demonstrated the reach of officialdom: within hours of the *[[spray|Spray]]* grounding, the port captain of [[montevideo|Montevideo]], over a hundred miles away, had sent orders for assistance. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s voyage, though solitary, was never truly isolated.
