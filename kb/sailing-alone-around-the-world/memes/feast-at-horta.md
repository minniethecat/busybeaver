[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s first landfall after his Atlantic crossing brought him to [[fayal|Horta]] in the [[azores|Azores]] on July 20, 1895 — the feast day of a great saint. The convergence of the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s arrival with the island's religious celebration created a memorable entrance: flags flying, shore boats racing, and islanders who mistook the little sloop for the long-awaited mail packet.

## Summary

After an eighteen-day passage from [[cape-sable|Cape Sable]], [[nova-scotia|Nova Scotia]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sighted the [[azores|Azores]] — first [[flores-island|Flores]], then [[pico|Pico]], and finally [[fayal|Fayal]] — arriving at [[fayal|Horta]] on July 20, 1895 (003-016) (003-017) (003-018). The day of his arrival coincided with the feast of a great saint, and the harbor was alive with celebration. The *[[spray|Spray]]*, flying her flags, was at first mistaken for a vessel bringing mails from Lisbon, prompting a spirited race of shore boats to be the first to reach her (003-019). The American consul and a young naval officer came aboard to welcome him, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was showered with fruit and hospitality by the islanders (003-018) (003-019).

## Context

The [[azores|Azores]], a Portuguese archipelago in the mid-Atlantic, were a natural waypoint for vessels crossing between North America and Europe. [[fayal|Horta]], on the island of [[fayal|Fayal]], was a regular port of call for transatlantic shipping. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had sailed the *[[spray|Spray]]* across the North Atlantic after departing [[yarmouth|Yarmouth, Nova Scotia]], on July 2 — his final farewell to the American coast (003-003). The passage had included lonely days in dense fog, an encounter with the bark *[[java-steamship|Java]]*, and the gradual return of good spirits as the islands approached (003-006) (003-010).

The timing of his arrival — July 20, the feast day of a saint — was pure coincidence, but it meant that [[fayal|Horta]] was in a festive mood when the *[[spray|Spray]]* appeared on the horizon.

## Narrative

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s approach to the [[azores|Azores]] was gradual and dramatic. On July 19, [[flores-island|Flores Island]] appeared as "a mystic dome like a mountain of silver" in a "white, glistening haze that shone in the sun like polished silver" (003-016). The next morning, [[pico|Pico]] loomed above the clouds, and "island after island came into view" as the fog burned away (003-017).

At 4:30 p.m. on July 20, the *[[spray|Spray]]* anchored at [[fayal|Fayal]], exactly eighteen days from [[cape-sable|Cape Sable]]. The American consul came out in a smart boat before [[sloop|the sloop]] even reached the breakwater, and a young naval officer boarded to offer his services as pilot (003-018).

The feast-day atmosphere of [[fayal|Horta]] made for a lively reception:

> "The day after my arrival at Horta was the feast of a great saint. The people were all in gala dress, and the harbor, nearly full of vessels, was decorated with flags from stem to stern. The little *Spray*, with her own flags flying, was not to be outdone, and her appearance with her 'coach' flying an American flag, and a St. George's burgee at her truck, was said to be the prettiest in the harbor." — (003-019)

The *[[spray|Spray]]*'s arrival caused an unexpected commotion. Islanders, seeing her flags and mistaking her for a vessel carrying long-awaited correspondence, raced to meet her:

> "Boats put off from every vessel in the harbor to board the stranger, there being a curiosity to know where she was from. The first to reach me said that the whole bay had been looking, since morning, for the mail packet from Lisbon, and I was taken for the packet because of the number of flags I was flying." — (003-019)

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] noted ruefully that he "could have carried the mail in his hat" — a disappointment, perhaps, but the festive mood was undimmed.

The islanders' generosity was remarkable: "It was the season for fruit when I arrived at the [[azores|Azores]], and there was soon more of all kinds of it put on board than I knew what to do with" (003-019).

## Aftermath

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] stayed at [[fayal|Horta]] for four days, departing on July 24. Before leaving, he obtained a bill of health — a document that would later prove essential at [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]], where the "fierce old port doctor" demanded to see it (004-003) (004-018). The rest and provisioning at [[fayal|Horta]] refreshed both captain and vessel for the next leg toward [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]] and [[mediterranean-sea|the Mediterranean]].

The American consul provided [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] with letters of introduction and practical assistance, and the young naval officer's concern for the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s safety was the first of many instances where [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] found that his tiny sloop inspired protective instincts in professional mariners.

## Significance

The feast-day arrival at [[fayal|Horta]] was a symbolic turning point in the voyage. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had left North America behind and was now truly in mid-ocean. The festive welcome — flags, fruit, and the racing shore boats — was a [[tonnage|ton]]ic after the lonely Atlantic crossing, and it [[current|set]] a pattern that would repeat throughout the [[voyage|circumnavigation]]: the *[[spray|Spray]]* arriving at a port to find warmth, curiosity, and generous hospitality. The [[azores|Azores]] also marked [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s first landfall on the eastern side of [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] — the beginning of the European and [[mediterranean|Mediterranean]] phases of his journey.
