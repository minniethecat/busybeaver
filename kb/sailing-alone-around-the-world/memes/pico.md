## Geography

Pico is an island in the central group of the [[azores|Azores]], dominated by its volcanic peak that rises dramatically from [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]]. The mountain is so high that [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] first saw Pico "looming above the clouds on the [[starboard|starboard bow]]" as he approached the archipelago on the morning of July 20, 1895. The lower lands of the other islands only burst forth as the sun burned away the morning fog. (003-016)

> Early on the morning of July 20 I saw Pico looming above the clouds on the starboard bow. Lower lands burst forth as the sun burned away the morning fog, and island after island came into view. As I approached nearer, cultivated fields appeared, "and oh, how green the corn!"
>
> (003-016)

The island's volcanic soil supports agriculture, and Pico is known for its dairy production. A Pico white cheese, given to [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] by the American consul-general, would prove to have memorable consequences for the lone mariner. (004-003)

## Role in the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] did not land on Pico but passed it twice. On his approach to the [[azores|Azores]], Pico was the first island he recognized, its peak visible above the clouds before any other land appeared. (003-016)

On the outbound leg from [[fayal|Fayal]], after mending his [[rigging|rig]]ging, [[spray|the Spray]] passed Pico and stretched across to leeward of St. Michael's. (004-003)

Pico is also the home of Antonio, the brother of the young woman who offered to cook for [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s voyage to Lisbon. Antonio, who served as interpreter for his sister, asked [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] if he knew John Wilson of [[boston|Boston]] and left the message: "Antonio of Pico remembers him." (003-020)

The island's most notable connection to the voyage involves its cheese. General Manning, the American consul-general, gave [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] a Pico white cheese, which he ate with plums. By nightfall he was doubled up with cramps, and in a delirious state that followed, he hallucinated the pilot of [[christopher-columbus|Columbus]]'s ship [[pinta|Pinta]] taking the [[rudder|helm]] and guiding [[spray|the Spray]] through a [[gale|storm]]. The apparition scolded him: "You did wrong, captain, to mix cheese with plums. White cheese is never safe unless you know whence it comes." (004-003)

## Historical Context

The [[azores|Azores]] islands, including Pico, have been [[current|set]]tled since the 15th century and served as a crucial provisioning point for ships crossing [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]]. The island's name comes from its distinctive volcanic peak (Ponta do Pico), the highest mountain in Portugal at 2,351 meters. In the 1890s, the American consulate at [[fayal|Horta]] provided assistance to the many American whaling and merchant vessels that called at the islands. (003-016) (004-003)
