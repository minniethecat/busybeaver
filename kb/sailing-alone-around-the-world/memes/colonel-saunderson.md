## Association with Slocum

Colonel Saunderson, a Member of Parliament, was one of Slocum's hosts during his stay in South Africa. At the Hotel Royal in Durban, Slocum dined with Colonel Saunderson, his son, and Lieutenant Tipping, where he met the explorer Henry Morton Stanley (017-022).

Saunderson invited Slocum to dine with the party again the following day, giving Slocum the pleasure of meeting Stanley a second time (017-022).

## Cape Town and Newlands Vineyard

After Slocum's arrival at Cape Town, Colonel Saunderson — described as "Mr. Krüger's friend" — invited Slocum to Newlands Vineyard, where he met many agreeable people (018-009). The governor, Sir Alfred Milner, also visited the Spray. During the visit, the colonel took snapshots of the sloop and her distinguished visitors from his dinghy with his Kodak camera, while Lady Saunderson sat by the skipper at the wheel (018-009).

## Relationship with President Krüger

Colonel Saunderson was President Krüger's "very best friend," known for advising the president to stop mounting guns (023-014).

> Soon after my arrival at the cape, Mr. Krüger's friend Colonel Saunderson, who had arrived from Durban some time before, invited me to Newlands Vineyard, where I met many agreeable people. His Excellency Sir Alfred Milner, the governor, found time to come aboard with a party.
>
> (018-009)
