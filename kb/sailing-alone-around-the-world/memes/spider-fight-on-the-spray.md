In one of the voyage's most whimsical moments, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] watched a spider from [[boston|Boston]] battle a [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n spider in the cabin of the *[[spray|Spray]]*. The [[boston|Boston]]ian won decisively, dismembering its opponent "so dexterously that in less than three minutes... the [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n spider didn't know itself from a fly." A Scots lady later named the victorious spider "Bruce."

## Summary

While gathering firewood from dead trees near Echo Mountain in [[strait-of-magellan|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] brought aboard a log harboring a [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n spider. In the cabin, it encountered a spider of the same size and species that had traveled all the way from [[boston|Boston]]. The [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n threw up its antennae for a fight, but the [[boston|Boston]]ian "downed it at once, then broke its legs, and pulled them off, one by one." A Scots lady in [[cape-town|Cape Town]] later named the [[boston|Boston]] spider "Bruce" in honor of its victory. (009-005)

## Context

The *[[spray|Spray]]* had been driven back into [[strait-of-magellan|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] after a failed attempt to round [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]] and enter [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]]. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was navigating the treacherous inland passage, constantly on guard against [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n raiders and violent williwaws. After a tense escape from [[snug-bay|Snug Bay]], where he had dragged anchor to flee canoes, he found a temporary refuge at Echo Mountain — so named because his voice echoed back from the perpendicular cliff face. It was here, in one of the few moments of relative calm, that the spider combat unfolded.

## Narrative

After clearing [[snug-bay|Snug Bay]] and repairing the windlass, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] anchored close under the face of a cliff he named Echo Mountain. Seeing dead trees farther along the broken shore, he made a landing for fuel, taking his ax and, as always on these days, his rifle:

> "I saw no living thing here, except a small spider, which had nested in a dry log that I boated to the sloop. The conduct of this insect interested me now more than anything else around the wild place." — (009-005)

What happened next in the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s cabin was a miniature battle that [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] watched with genuine fascination:

> "In my cabin it met, oddly enough, a spider of its own size and species that had come all the way from Boston — a very civil little chap, too, but mighty spry. Well, the Fuegian threw up its antennae for a fight; but my little Bostonian downed it at once, then broke its legs, and pulled them off, one by one, so dexterously that in less than three minutes from the time the battle began the Fuegian spider didn't know itself from a fly." — (009-005)

## Aftermath

The following morning, before weighing anchor, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] prepared a cup of warm coffee over a smart wood fire in his [[montevideo|Montevideo]] stove. "In the same fire was cremated the [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n spider, slain the day before by the little warrior from [[boston|Boston]], which a Scots lady at [[cape-town|Cape Town]] long after named 'Bruce' upon hearing of its prowess at Echo Mountain" (009-006).

The [[boston|Boston]] spider — now christened Bruce — continued the voyage with [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]. Much later, in the [[pacific-ocean|South Pacific]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] would remark that the only creatures aboard besides himself were "the spider and his wife, from [[boston|Boston]]" (019-014), suggesting Bruce had found a mate and established a family aboard the *[[spray|Spray]]*.

## Significance

The spider fight is one of the most charming and human moments in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s narrative. Amid the constant danger of the [[strait-of-magellan|Magellan Strait]] — the williwaws, the hostile [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]ns, the treacherous currents — [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] took time to observe and enjoy a tiny combat between two spiders. It speaks to his temperament as a solo sailor: observant, amused by small wonders, and finding companionship even in the smallest creatures. Bruce the spider became a minor celebrity among those who heard [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s story, a symbol of the unexpected passengers who shared his voyage.
