Newport, Rhode Island, was the final port of call for [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]]'s solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]] of the globe. The *[[spray|Spray]]* dropped anchor in Newport harbor at 1 a.m. on June 27, 1898, completing a voyage of more than 46,000 miles over three years and two months.

## Geography

Newport is situated on [[aquidneck|Aquidneck]] Island in Narragansett Bay, Rhode Island. Its deep-water harbor has been a significant maritime center since the colonial era.

At the time of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s arrival in 1898, the harbor was mined as a defensive measure during the Spanish-American War, requiring careful navigation close to the rocks to avoid both the mines and the guard-ship stationed in the channel (021-015).

> When daylight came I saw that the sea had changed color from dark green to light. I threw the lead and got soundings in thirteen fathoms. I made the land soon after, some miles east of Fire Island, and sailing thence before a pleasant breeze along the coast, made for Newport. The weather after the furious gale was remarkably fine. The *Spray* rounded Montauk Point early in the afternoon; Point Judith was abeam at dark; she fetched in at Beavertail next. Sailing on, she had one more danger to pass—Newport harbor was mined. The *Spray* hugged the rocks along where neither friend nor foe could come if drawing much water, and where she would not disturb the guard-ship in the channel. It was close work, but it was safe enough so long as she hugged the rocks close, and not the mines.
>
> (021-015)

## Role in the Voyage

Newport appears at pivotal moments in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s narrative. Early in the book, when describing the inhabitants of [[juan-fernandez-island|Juan Fernandez]] Island, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] notes that one [[chile|Chile]]an lady who made a [[jib|flying-jib]] for the *[[spray|Spray]]* "would be called a belle at Newport" — using the town as a standard of refinement and beauty (011-002).

Newport's true significance lies at the voyage's end. Approaching the coast after crossing [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] for the final time, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sailed before a pleasant breeze "along the coast, made for Newport" (021-015).

The approach was not without danger — Newport harbor had been mined during the Spanish-American War. The *[[spray|Spray]]* "hugged the rocks along where neither friend nor foe could come if drawing much water," navigating carefully between the shoreline and the minefield (021-015).

As the *[[spray|Spray]]* passed the guard-ship Dexter, someone on board called out, "There goes a craft!" [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] threw up a light and received the hail, "*[[spray|Spray]]*, ahoy!" — "the voice of a friend" (021-015).

At 1 a.m. on June 27, 1898, the *[[spray|Spray]]* cast anchor in Newport, having sailed over 46,000 miles round the world in an absence of three years, two months, and two days. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] recorded that he had gained a pound in weight and looked ten years younger than when he began (021-016).

## The Final Landfall

The last day at sea was a study in contrasts. At daylight, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] "saw that the sea had changed color from dark green to light" and threw the lead, getting "soundings in thirteen fathoms" (021-015).

He "made the land soon after, some miles east of Fire Island" (021-015). The weather after the previous day's furious gale "was remarkably fine" (021-015).

The *[[spray|Spray]]* rounded Montauk Point early in the afternoon, passed Point Judith later, and with lights blazing from every prominent point along the shore, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] navigated the final miles of a voyage that had carried him across every ocean.

## Homecoming

The completion of the voyage at Newport was more than a navigational achievement — it was a personal triumph. As [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] reflected: "I had profited in many ways by the voyage. I had even gained flesh, and actually weighed a pound more than when I sailed from [[boston|Boston]]" (021-016).

The physical transformation surprised even his friends, who "all said, '[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] is young again'" (021-016). [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] himself felt "at least ten years younger than the day I felled the first tree for the construction of the *[[spray|Spray]]*" (021-016).

Newport thus stands in the narrative not merely as a geographical endpoint but as the place where a solitary mariner, after circumnavigating the globe alone, returned renewed — a fitting conclusion to the first solo voyage [[voyage|around the world]].
