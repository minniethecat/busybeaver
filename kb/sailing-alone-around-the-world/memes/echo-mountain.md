## Slocum's Naming

Slocum named this place "Echo Mountain" after the echo of a gunshot from the [[spray|Spray]] reverberated from its slopes (009-005). Upon hearing of its prowess at Echo Mountain, the [[spray|Spray]] reached away from the coast (009-006).

## Narrative Significance

Echo Mountain is distinctive among the places in Slocum's narrative as one he personally named. The echo of a gunshot reverberating from its slopes inspired the name (009-005), revealing Slocum's playful engagement with the landscape he traversed. This act of naming also reflects the explorer's perspective Slocum brought to his voyage — though he followed routes charted by others, he approached the coastline with fresh eyes, ready to leave his own mark on the map. The Spray's prowess noted after this incident (009-006) adds a touch of humor to the log.

> Here my own voice answered back, and I named the place "Echo Mountain."
>
> (009-005)
