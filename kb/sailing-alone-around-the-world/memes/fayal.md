## Geography

Fayal is one of the central islands of the [[azores|Azores]] archipelago, located in the North Atlantic. It lies 174 miles east of [[flores-island|Flores Island]], the westernmost island of the group. The island is known for its beautiful roads and cultivated landscapes. (003-015)

Horta, the principal port and capital of Fayal, serves as a key harbor for vessels crossing [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]]. In the 1890s it was a regular port of call for American and European ships, with an American consul stationed there to assist visiting mariners. (003-017)

## Role in the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] arrived at Fayal on July 19, 1895, eighteen days after departing [[cape-sable|Cape Sable]]. The American consul came alongside before [[spray|the Spray]] even reached the breakwater, and a young naval officer offered his services as pilot. The piloting did not go smoothly: "after fouling all the craft in port and sinking a lighter, she was moored without much damage to herself." (003-017)

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] intended to stay only two days but remained four, detained by "the kindness of the islanders and their touching simplicity." On the day after his arrival, a local man harnessed a team and drove [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] over the beautiful roads of the island, repaying a kindness he had once received while unable to speak English in America. (003-019)

A young woman came alongside [[spray|the Spray]] one day offering to cook for the voyage to Lisbon, and her brother Antonio asked after his friend John Wilson of [[boston|Boston]]. The message he left was: "Antonio of [[pico|Pico]] remembers him." (003-020)

After departing Fayal, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] passed the Prince of Monaco's steam-yacht bound for the same island, where the prince had once "slipped his cables to escape a reception" from the local padres. (004-003)

## Historical Context

The [[azores|Azores]] have long served as a vital provisioning stop for transatlantic voyagers. In the 1890s, Horta was a bustling port where whaling ships, merchant vessels, and yachts called for supplies and repairs. The feast-day celebration [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] witnessed drew boats from neighboring islands to Horta, described as "the capital, or Jerusalem, of the [[azores|Azores]]." (003-019)

> I remained four days at Fayal, and that was two days more than I had intended to stay. It was the kindness of the islanders and their touching simplicity which detained me. A damsel, as innocent as an angel, came alongside one day, and said she would embark on the *Spray* if I would land her at Lisbon. She could cook flying-fish, she thought, but her forte was dressing *bacalao*.
>
> (003-017)
