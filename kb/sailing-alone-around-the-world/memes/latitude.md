# Latitude

## Role in Navigation

Throughout his voyage, Joshua Slocum relied on latitude as his primary means of determining his position at sea. His logbook entries routinely record the latitude of the *Spray*, often paired with [[longitude]]. For example, on September 25, he recorded being "in the latitude of 5° N., longitude 26° 30' W." (005-022). On another occasion, he noted the *Spray* was at latitude 35½° (011-012), and he "ran down the longitude" in latitude 12° S. (011-021).

## Geographic References in the Narrative

Slocum used latitude to describe the locations of the places he visited and passed. The coast of [[new-south-wales|New South Wales]] was recorded at latitude 30° 12' south (014-037). The ridge and backbone of Australia was noted on a specific "parallel of latitude" (015-007). [[st-helena|St. Helena]] lies in "7° 35' south latitude and 14° 25' west longitude" (019-016). The [[keeling-cocos-islands|Keeling Cocos Islands]] lie between the latitudes of 11° 50' and 12° 12' S. (016-005).

## Notable Entries

In one poetic logbook entry, Slocum wrote: "Tonight, in latitude 7° 13' N., for the first time in nearly three years I see the [[north-star|North Star]]" (020-009). On another day, he noted that the *Spray*'s latitude "was the same" at noon — a mark of steady sailing (021-003). The *Spray* also encountered the "horse latitudes," where her "sail flapped limp" in the doldrums (021-004).

> This is more or less the constant state of the winter trades in latitude 12° S., where I "ran down the longitude" for weeks.
>
> (011-021)
