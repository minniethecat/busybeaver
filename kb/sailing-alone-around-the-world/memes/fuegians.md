# Fuegians

The indigenous people of [[tierra-del-fuego|Tierra del Fuego]], a group [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] encountered and described extensively during his southern voyage through [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]].

## Definition

The Fuegians were the indigenous inhabitants of the [[tierra-del-fuego|Tierra del Fuego]] archipelago at the southern tip of South America. In [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s time (the 1890s), they were known to sailors and explorers as a people adapted to the harsh subantarctic climate, living in small [[current|set]]tlements along the coasts and islands of the region.

## In the Book

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s encounters with the Fuegians form some of the most vivid passages of his narrative. Before reaching [[tierra-del-fuego|Tierra del Fuego]], he had heard stories about "[[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n cannibals" from a captain's wife (006-015). Once in the region, he observed that the natives, both "[[patagonia|Patagonia]]n and [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n," were "as savage as any in the world" (007-011).

The Fuegians made several attempts to approach [[spray|the Spray]]. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] recounted how a party of "young bloods" planned to "foray a [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n [[current|set]]tlement and wipe out what they could" (007-012), and described being offered a bottle of "[[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n gold-dust from a place where it had been buried for years" (007-013). He characterized them as reaching "the very core of the savage Fuegians" (007-014).

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s strategy shifted from avoidance to deterrence. After being pursued, he decided to "be clear of the worst of the Fuegians" (007-024), and later noted that "the Fuegians, being cruel, are naturally cowardly" (008-013).

In a later encounter, he resolved to "become the aggressor, and put the Fuegians entirely on the defensive" by firing his gun, after which "the [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n threw up its antennae for a flight" (009-003, 009-005). He also observed a physical characteristic: "the Fuegians are not bearded" (007-019).

> It was not without thoughts of strange and stirring adventure beyond all I had yet encountered that I now sailed into the country and very core of the savage Fuegians.
>
> (007-014)

## Slocum's Evolving Strategy

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s approach to the Fuegians evolved across his two passages through [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]]. On the first transit, his primary goal was simple evasion — keeping [[spray|the Spray]] clear of canoes and avoiding confrontation. After a night of wakefulness on the "weird shore," he "made haste the following morning to be under way" (009-006).

On the second passage, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s posture had hardened into active deterrence. He kept his rifle "by me ready for instant use," having "seen [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n canoes at this place" on the earlier trip (010-005). His willingness to fire warning shots — and his observation that the Fuegians "threw up its antennae for a flight" when confronted — suggests he had learned that a show of force was more effective than flight.

This progression from fearful avoidance to calculated deterrence mirrors [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s broader maturation as a solo navigator. By the time he made his sixth attempt to clear the strait, he had "made up my mind after six attempts, being driven back each time, to be in no further haste to sail" (010-006). The Fuegians, once a terror, had become one more navigational variable to be managed with patience and prudence.
