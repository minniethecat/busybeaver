## Geography

The Cape Verde Islands are a volcanic archipelago located in the central Atlantic Ocean approximately 570 kilometers off the coast of West Africa. The northwesternmost island, St. Antonio, was Slocum's landfall on his eastward Atlantic crossing. The islands lie at a strategic position astride the historic sailing routes between Europe, Africa, and South America (014-019).

## Role in the Voyage

Slocum sighted the Cape Verde Islands during his Atlantic crossing from the Canary Islands. He recorded the landfall at St. Antonio, "the northwesternmost of the Cape Verdes, close aboard" (005-014). The islands served as a waypoint confirming his navigation across the Atlantic. Later in the voyage, when considering his route across the Indian Ocean, Slocum reflected on "the route which I took from Cape Verde Islands or farther back to the equator" (014-019), marking the Cape Verdes as a significant milestone in his transatlantic passage.

> On September 10 the *Spray* passed the island of St. Antonio, the northwesternmost of the Cape Verdes, close aboard. The landfall was wonderfully true, considering that no observations for longitude had been made.
>
> (005-014)

## Historical Context

The Cape Verde Islands were uninhabited until discovered and colonized by the Portuguese in the 15th century. By Slocum's time in the 1890s, they were a well-established provisioning stop for sailing vessels crossing the Atlantic, though the opening of the Suez Canal had already begun to shift global shipping patterns away from the Cape route.
