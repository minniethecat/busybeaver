# Captain Howard

Captain Howard was an American mariner from [[cape-cod|Cape Cod]] who had made a career as a pilot on the River Plate in South America. An old friend of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s, he sailed aboard the *[[spray|Spray]]* at [[buenos-aires|Buenos Aires]] and became an important witness to the vessel's most celebrated quality — her uncanny ability to steer herself.

## Biography

Howard hailed from [[cape-cod|Cape Cod]] — a region that, like [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s [[nova-scotia|Nova Scotia]], produced generations of deep-water sailors. He had "River Plate fame" (006-014), having established himself as a pilot on the great estuary between Argentina and [[uruguay|Uruguay]], a waterway notorious for its shifting shoals and strong currents. A pilot's work demanded intimate knowledge of local waters and steady nerves — qualities that made Howard a discerning judge of a vessel's behavior.

## Role in the Voyage

Howard joined [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] for a short excursion up the River Plate in late December 1895. The passage became a demonstration of the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s most remarkable characteristic. "Howard sat near the [[compass|binnacle]] and watched the compass while [[sloop|the sloop]] held her course so steadily that one would have declared that the card was nailed fast. Not a quarter of a point did she deviate from her course" (006-014).

Howard's reaction was that of a seasoned professional confronted with something he could not explain: "My old friend had owned and sailed a pilot-sloop on the river for many years, but this feat took the wind out of his [[sail|sails]] at last, and he cried, 'I'll be stranded on Chico Bank if ever I saw the like of it!'" (006-014). It is the highest testimony the *[[spray|Spray]]* ever received — a veteran pilot, who knew those waters better than any man, declaring himself amazed.

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] used Howard's testimony to make a larger point: "The point I make for the *[[spray|Spray]]* here, above all other points, is that she sailed in shoal water and in a strong current, with other difficult and unusual conditions. Captain Howard took all this into account" (006-014).

> I was glad to have a sailor of Howard's experience on board to witness her performance of sailing with no living being at the helm. Howard sat near the binnacle and watched the compass while the sloop held her course so steadily that one would have declared that the card was nailed fast. My old friend had owned and sailed a pilot-sloop on the river for many years, but this feat took the wind out of his sails at last, and he cried, "I'll be stranded on Chico Bank if ever I saw the like of it!"
>
> (006-014)

## Significance

Captain Howard serves as a witness and validator in the narrative. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] could describe the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s virtues at length, but an independent captain's astonishment carries more weight. Howard's "I'll be stranded on Chico Bank" is the seal of a professional mariner on [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s claims.
