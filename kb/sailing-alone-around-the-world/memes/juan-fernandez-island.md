**Juan Fernández Island** (now officially called Robinson Crusoe Island) is a mountainous island in the South [[pacific-ocean|Pacific Ocean]], roughly four hundred miles off the coast of [[chile|Chile]]. [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]] made landfall there on April 26, 1896, after a fifteen-day passage from [[cape-pillar|Cape Pillar]], and regarded the visit as one of the high points of his [[voyage|circumnavigation]].

## Arrival

Approaching from the south after rounding [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sighted the island with deep emotion. "The blue hills of Juan Fernandez, high among the clouds, could be seen about thirty miles off. A thousand emotions thrilled me when I saw the island, and I bowed my head to the deck" (010-019).

He arrived on April 26, having sailed from [[cape-pillar|Cape Pillar]] in fifteen days (010-018).

> The blue hills of Juan Fernandez, high among the clouds, could be seen about thirty miles off. A thousand emotions thrilled me when I saw the island, and I bowed my head to the deck. We may mock the Oriental salaam, but for my part I could find no other way of expressing myself.
>
> (010-019)

## The Island

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] found the island idyllic. "Juan Fernandez, as a place of call, is a lovely spot. The hills are well wooded, the valleys fertile, and pouring down through many ravines are streams of pure water" (011-002). He observed that "there are no serpents on the island, and no wild beasts other than pigs and goats" (011-002) and that the forty-five inhabitants lived simply: "The people lived without the use of rum or beer of any sort. There was not a police officer or a lawyer among them" (011-002).

Juan Fernandez had once been a convict station. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] noted "a number of caves in which the prisoners were kept, damp, unwholesome dens, are no longer in use, and no more prisoners are sent to the island" (011-013).

## Robinson Crusoe's Cave

The island's greatest draw was its literary association. [[alexander-selkirk|Alexander Selkirk]], the Scottish sailor whose solitary stay on the island inspired Daniel Defoe's *[[robinson-crusoe|Robinson Crusoe]]*, had lived there. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] visited the very cave: "On the morning of May 5, 1896, I sailed from Juan Fernandez, having feasted on many things, but on nothing sweeter than the adventure itself of a visit to the home and to the very cave of [[robinson-crusoe|Robinson Crusoe]]" (011-017).

He remarked: "Blessed island of Juan Fernandez! Why [[alexander-selkirk|Alexander Selkirk]] ever left you was more than I could make out" (011-002).

## Encounters

On the island [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] met Manuel Carroza, a Portuguese-American [[current|set]]tler who styled himself the king of Juan Fernandez, and whose house "besides resembling the ark, wore a polished brass knocker on its only door, which was painted green" (011-003). Carroza "nearly traded me out of my boots" (012-006), and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] later ran out of potatoes in mid-ocean as a result. During his stay, a [[chile|Chile]]an woman made a [[jib|flying-jib]] for the *[[spray|Spray]]*, "taking her pay in tallow" (011-002).
