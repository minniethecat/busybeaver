## Role in Navigation

The trade winds were vital to Slocum's voyage. Near the Equator, at the "limit of the southeast trade winds, the air was heavily charged with electricity" (005-021). Slocum navigated through the "trade-wind regions" of the South Atlantic, noting the latitude where the winds shifted (011-012). The reliability of the trade winds made them a sailor's highway across the world's oceans.

> Along the equatorial limit of the southeast trade winds the air was heavily charged with electricity, and there was much thunder and lightning. It was hereabout I remembered that, a few years before, the American ship Alert was destroyed by lightning. Her people, by wonderful good fortune, were rescued on the same day and brought to Pernambuco, where I then met them.
>
> (005-021)
