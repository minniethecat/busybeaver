# Eden

Eden is a port town on Twofold Bay in [[australia|New South Wales]], [[australia]]. [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]] passed by this location during his coastal voyage southward along the [[australia|Australia]]n coast in December 1896. The town lies at the southern head of Twofold Bay, one of the deepest natural harbors in the Southern Hemisphere.

## In the Book

On December 12, 1896, an exceptional day with a fine northeast coast wind, the *[[spray|Spray]]* passed Twofold Bay early in the morning in a smooth sea with land close aboard. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] described the scene with unusual warmth:

> The 12th of December, however, was an exceptional day, with a fine coast wind, northeast. The Spray early in the morning passed Twofold Bay and later Cape Bundooro in a smooth sea with land close aboard. The lighthouse on the cape dipped a flag to the Spray's flag, and children on the balconies of a cottage near the shore waved handkerchiefs as she passed by. There were only a few people all told on the shore, but the scene was a happy one. I saw festoons of evergreen in token of Christmas, near at hand. I saluted the merrymakers, wishing them a "Merry Christmas," and could hear them say, "I wish you the same." (013-015)

The [[beacon|lighthouse]] on nearby [[cape-bundooro|Cape Bundooro]] dipped its flag to the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s flag — a small gesture of recognition from shore that must have meant much to a solitary sailor who had been weeks at sea. Children on the balconies of a cottage near the shore waved handkerchiefs as she passed, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] saw festoons of evergreen hung in token of the approaching Christmas.

The coast in this region was largely unsettled at the time. After passing Eden and Twofold Bay, the *[[spray|Spray]]* continued southward past Cliff Island in [[bass-strait|Bass Strait]], where the wind howled and the sea broke over the light-keepers' rocky home (013-016).

## Role in the Voyage

Eden and Twofold Bay marked the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s passage along the southeastern coast of [[australia|Australia]] during the [[australia|Australia]]n summer of 1896–97. The warm reception — the dipping flag, the waving children, the Christmas decorations — stood in memorable contrast to the rough weather [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had endured along other coasts and the hard gales still to come in the [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]].

The passage of Eden also marked a transitional moment in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s [[voyage|circumnavigation]]. He was now well into the final third of his voyage, having crossed [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]] and rounded [[australia|Australia]]. The Christmas greetings exchanged with strangers on a quiet shore served as a reminder of the world of human connection that awaited him at journey's end.
