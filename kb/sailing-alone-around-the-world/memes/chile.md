# Chile

Chile played a significant role in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s [[voyage|circumnavigation]] as he navigated the treacherous southern waters of [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] and the channels of [[tierra-del-fuego|Tierra del Fuego]]. Chilean naval officers at [[sandy-point|Sandy Point]] provided critical navigational advice, Chilean gunboats shared the anchorages, and the remote island of [[juan-fernandez-island|Juan Fernandez]] — a Chilean possession — served as [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s final South American waypoint before the long Pacific crossing.

## Geography

Chile stretches along the western coast of South America, from the Atacama Desert in the north to the fjords and channels of [[patagonia|Patagonia]] in the south. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] encountered Chilean territory primarily in the far south — at [[sandy-point|Sandy Point]] ([[sandy-point|Punta Arenas]]), a "Chilean coaling-station" on [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] (007-011), at [[port-angosto|Port Angosto]] in the western channels (010-006), and at the island of [[juan-fernandez-island|Juan Fernandez]], which lies four hundred miles off the Chilean coast (011-012).

## Role in the Voyage

### Sandy Point and the Strait of Magellan

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] arrived at [[sandy-point|Sandy Point]] on February 14, 1896, after passing through the narrows of [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] (007-009). The [[current|set]]tlement then had about two thousand inhabitants "of mixed nationality, but mostly Chileans" (007-011). The economy was built on sheep-farming, gold-mining, and hunting:

> "What with sheep-farming, gold-mining, and hunting, the settlers in this dreary land seemed not the worst off in the world. But the natives, Patagonian and Fuegian, were, it was understood, the proprietors of all that part of the country, and the white people had no business there at all." (007-011)

The port was free at the time [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] visited, though a customhouse was under construction (007-012). A soldier police guarded the [[current|set]]tlement, supplemented by a vigilante force (007-012).

The port captain, a Chilean naval officer, proved instrumental to [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s progress. Initially he urged [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] to wait for a steamer escort through the dangerous [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n channels, but "finding that I was resolved to go, even alone, since there was no help for it, [[current|set]] up no further objections" (007-014). His advice was practical and grim: "in case the savages tried to surround me with their canoes, to shoot straight, and begin to do it in time, but to avoid killing them if possible" (007-014).

### Chilean Naval Presence

The Chilean navy maintained an active presence in the southern channels. At [[sandy-point|Sandy Point]], the gunboat *[[chilean-gunboat-huemel|Huemel]]* arrived while [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was ashore (007-024). Later, after six failed attempts to clear the strait's western reaches, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] returned to [[port-angosto|Port Angosto]] where he found the "Chilean gunboat *[[chilean-gunboat-condor|Condor]]* and the Argentine cruiser *[[argentine-cruiser-azopardo|Azopardo]]*" already at anchor (010-006). He procured cordage and supplies from these vessels, and "the officers of each of them mustered a supply of warm flannels, of which I was most in need" (010-007).

### The Chile-Argentina War

At [[juan-fernandez-island|Juan Fernandez]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] learned of a border conflict between Chile and Argentina — news that had not reached him while in the strait. As one islander told him: "They told me of a war between Chile and the Argentine, which I had not heard of when I was there" (010-022). This diplomatic tension colored the political atmosphere of the region, though it did not directly affect [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s voyage.

## Encounters and Observations

### Seven Attempts to Clear the Strait

[[port-angosto|Port Angosto]], though "a dreary enough place," became [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s reluctant base of operations (010-002). He made six attempts to exit the strait westward, each time driven back by fierce westerly gales. "The bad weather on my last return to [[port-angosto|Port Angosto]] for shelter brought the Chilean gunboat *[[chilean-gunboat-condor|Condor]]* and the Argentine cruiser *[[argentine-cruiser-azopardo|Azopardo]]*" — a convergence of naval powers in a remote anchorage (010-006).

On April 13, 1896, on his seventh attempt, the *[[spray|Spray]]* finally cleared [[tierra-del-fuego|Tierra del Fuego]]. It was, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] admitted, "a close shave and a narrow escape" (010-011).

At [[port-angosto|Port Angosto]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] also met Professor Dusen of a Swedish scientific expedition to South America and [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]] Islands, who was camped studying mosses by a brook at the harbor's head (010-009).

### Juan Fernandez

After rounding [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] [[current|set]] course for [[juan-fernandez-island|Juan Fernandez]], "which country it belongs to" — Chile (011-012). The islanders greeted him with enthusiasm: "They had all heard of the voyage of [[spray|the Spray]] through the papers of Valparaiso, and were hungry for news concerning it" (010-022). [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] served them coffee and doughnuts, and they towed the *[[spray|Spray]]* into the anchorage at three [[knot|knots]] (010-023).

### A Gift from Chile

In the narrative, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] noted with appreciation that "One lady there, from Chile, who made a [[jib|flying-jib]] for [[spray|the Spray]]" — a gesture of practical generosity from a Chilean resident encountered elsewhere in the Americas (011-002).

## Historical Context

In the 1890s, Chile was a growing maritime power in South America, having emerged victorious from the War of [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]] (1879–1884). Its navy maintained a strong presence in [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]], where coaling stations like [[sandy-point|Sandy Point]] were strategic assets. Tensions with Argentina over [[patagonia|Patagonia]]n borders were ongoing, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s voyage occurred during a period of active diplomatic friction (010-022).

The Chilean gunboats *[[chilean-gunboat-huemel|Huemel]]* and *[[chilean-gunboat-condor|Condor]]* were part of Chile's naval patrol in the southern channels, reflecting the nation's commitment to controlling the vital maritime passage. [[juan-fernandez-island|Juan Fernandez]], Chile's remote Pacific possession, served as a provisioning stop for vessels rounding [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]] — though by [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s time it was sparsely inhabited and administered as a quiet outpost.
