**Durban** is the principal port city of Natal (now part of South Africa), situated on the [[indian-ocean|Indian Ocean]] coast. [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]] arrived there in November 1897 after a [[gale|storm]]y passage from [[mauritius|Mauritius]] across the Mozambique Channel, and spent several weeks enjoying the hospitality of the "Garden Colony."

## Arrival

The *[[spray|Spray]]*'s approach was spotted from land well before she reached port. "The signalman from the bluff station reported the *[[spray|Spray]]* fifteen miles off. The wind was freshening, and when she was within eight miles he said: 'The *[[spray|Spray]]* is shortening sail; the mainsail was [[reefing|reefed]] and [[current|set]] in ten minutes. One man is doing all the work'" (017-015).

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] described the city with genuine appreciation: "This delightful place is the commercial center of the 'Garden Colony,' Durban itself, the city, being the continuation of a garden" (017-015).

## The City and Its Yachting

The *[[spray|Spray]]*'s arrival became local news almost instantly. A Durban morning journal had the story of her [[reef|reefing]] maneuver in print "three minutes later," in time to hand [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] a copy when he reached the wharf (017-016). The newspaper praised his seamanship in navigating the busy harbor (017-018).

Durban supported two yacht clubs, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sailed aboard the crack yacht *Florence* of the Royal Natal with Captain Spradbrow and the premier Harry Escombe (017-019). Escombe offered to sail the *[[spray|Spray]]* around [[cape-of-good-hope|the Cape]] for [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] — a jest that Spradbrow met with a cribbage warning (017-019).

The dual yacht clubs reflected Durban's unusual maritime culture. Unlike [[cape-town|Cape Town]], which was primarily a naval and commercial port, Durban had cultivated a leisure [[sail|sailing]] scene that welcomed [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] as a celebrity rather than a curiosity.

## Encounter with the Flat-Earth Boers

It was at Durban that [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] met [[henry-morton-stanley|Henry M. Stanley]], the famous explorer, who was "just from [[pretoria|Pretoria]]" (017-022). The encounter with [[henry-morton-stanley|Stanley]] was notable, but an even stranger one followed: "three Boers favored by the opinion of [[president-kruger|President Krüger]]" arrived from [[pretoria|Pretoria]] to interview [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], seeking proof that the world was flat (017-023).

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s refusal to endorse their theory annoyed them, and the next morning one of them, now dressed as a clergyman, confronted him with a Bible and an assagai-like gesture: "If you respect the Word of God, you must admit that the world is flat" (017-023). [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] dodged the imaginary weapon and recorded the encounter with his characteristic dry humor.
