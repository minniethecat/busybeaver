The **Arrival at Horta** on July 20, 1895, was the first landfall of the *[[spray|Spray]]* after crossing [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] Ocean. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] cast anchor at [[fayal|Fayal]] in the [[azores|Azores]] at 4:30 p.m., exactly eighteen days from [[cape-sable|Cape Sable]]. The four-day stay at [[fayal|Horta]] was marked by the feast of a great saint, the kindness of the islanders, and a memorable encounter with a native who had promised his "good saint" to help any stranger who came to his country (003-019).

## Summary

After eighteen days crossing from [[cape-sable|Cape Sable]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sighted [[flores-island|Flores Island]] on July 19 — "a mystic dome like a mountain of silver stood alone in the sea ahead" — and [[pico|Pico]] on the morning of July 20. As he approached [[fayal|Fayal]], "cultivated fields appeared, 'and oh, how green the corn!'" The American consul came alongside before the *[[spray|Spray]]* reached the breakwater, and a young naval officer insisted on piloting [[sloop|the sloop]], with chaotic results: "after fouling all the craft in port and sinking a lighter, she was moored without much damage to herself" (003-019).

## Context

The *[[spray|Spray]]* had departed [[nova-scotia|Nova Scotia]] and rounded [[cape-sable|Cape Sable]], [[course|heading]] into the open Atlantic for the first time. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was navigating with his "tin clock" — a dollar [[chronometer|timepiece]] with a smashed face — and had been gratified when a passing steamship confirmed his longitude exactly. The eighteen-day crossing was swift by the standards of a nine-[[tonnage|ton]] sloop, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] arrived at the [[azores|Azores]] in excellent condition (003-019).

## Narrative

### The Landfall

> "Land ho! On the morning of July 19 a mystic dome like a mountain of silver stood alone in the sea ahead. Although the land was completely hidden by the white, glistening haze that shone in the sun like polished silver, I felt quite sure that it was Flores Island." — (003-019)

The approach to the [[azores|Azores]] filled [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] with delight. "Only those who have seen the [[azores|Azores]] from the deck of a vessel realize the beauty of the mid-ocean picture" (003-019).

### Island Hospitality

The day after [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s arrival was the feast of a great saint. Boats loaded with celebrants came from other islands, and "the deck of [[spray|the Spray]] was crowded from morning till night with men, women, and children." On the day after the feast, a kindhearted native harnessed a team and drove [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] over the beautiful roads of [[fayal|Fayal]], explaining: "When I was in America and couldn't speak a word of English, I found it hard till I met someone who seemed to have time to listen to my story, and I promised my good saint then that if ever a stranger came to my country I would try to make him happy" (003-020).

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] stayed four days — two more than planned — detained by "the kindness of the islanders and their touching simplicity" (003-024).

## Aftermath

The *[[spray|Spray]]* departed [[fayal|Horta]] on July 24, [[course|heading]] for [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]]. The stay at the [[azores|Azores]] proved to be the template for [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s experience throughout the [[voyage|circumnavigation]]: wherever he went, the kindness of strangers transformed what could have been a lonely voyage into a social one. At [[gibraltar|Gibraltar]], however, he received a warning that would alter his route — British naval officers advised him that [[mediterranean-sea|the Mediterranean]] was infested with pirates, prompting him to turn back and sail around South America instead (004-025).

## Significance

The arrival at [[fayal|Horta]] was the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s first proof of concept as an ocean-crossing vessel. The eighteen-day Atlantic passage demonstrated that [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s hand-rebuilt sloop was seaworthy, and the warm reception at the [[azores|Azores]] [[current|set]] the [[tonnage|ton]]e for the hospitality he would encounter [[voyage|around the world]].
