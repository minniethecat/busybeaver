## Geography

New Caledonia is a large island group in the southwest Pacific, located approximately 1,200 kilometers east of Australia. A French colony at the time of Slocum's voyage, the main island is surrounded by one of the world's largest barrier reef systems. The island lies along the sailing route between the Pacific Islands and the eastern coast of Australia.

## Role in the Voyage

Slocum passed south of New Caledonia on his approach to Australia, sailing direct for New South Wales after clearing the island (013-008). The waters near New Caledonia proved dangerous: a "severe gale encountered near New Caledonia foundered the American clipper" that Slocum noted in his narrative (013-009), a sobering reminder of the hazards that even large, well-crewed ships faced in these waters — and which the Spray, with her single-handed crew, had to navigate alone.
> One particularly severe gale encountered near New Caledonia foundered the American clipper-ship Patrician farther south. Again, nearer the coast of Australia, a French mail-steamer from New Caledonia for Sydney, blown considerably out of her course, on her arrival reported it an awful storm, and to inquiring friends said: "Oh, my! we don't know what has become of the little sloop Spray. We saw her in the thick of the storm."
>
> (013-009)


## Historical Context

New Caledonia was annexed by France in 1853 and served as a penal colony from 1864. By the 1890s, when Slocum sailed past, it was a growing French settlement and an important waypoint for vessels traversing the Pacific. Its location near the Coral Sea route made it a navigational landmark for ships bound between the South Pacific Islands and Australia.
