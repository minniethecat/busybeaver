# Three Island Cove

Three Island Cove is a small anchorage in the [[strait-of-magellan|Strait of Magellan]], about twenty miles west of [[fortescue-bay|Fortescue Bay]]. It was one of many nameless or half-named coves where the *[[spray|Spray]]* found shelter during [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s arduous passage through the [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n channels — a region he regarded as the most dangerous part of the entire [[voyage|circumnavigation]].

## A Midnight Anchorage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] reached Three Island Cove at midnight after a day of [[gale|heavy weather]] and tense encounters with the native [[fuegians|Fuegians]]. "I came to anchor at midnight in Three Island Cove, about twenty miles along from [[fortescue-bay|Fortescue Bay]]" — (007-020). The anchorage offered more than shelter from the wind — it offered relative safety from the human dangers of the strait.

From his anchorage, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] could see signs of the [[fuegians|Fuegians]] on the far shore: "I saw on the opposite side of the strait signal-fires, and heard the barking of dogs, but where I lay it was quite deserted by natives" — (007-020). He noted his own rule of thumb for safety in those waters: "I have always taken it as a sign that where I found birds sitting about, or seals on the rocks, I should not find savage Indians. Seals are never plentiful in these waters, but in Three Island Cove I saw one on the rocks, and other signs of the absence of savage men" — (007-020).

## Riding Out the Gales

The weather in [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] was relentless. "The [[spray|Spray]] sailed from Three Island Cove in the morning after the gale went down, but was glad to return for shelter from another sudden gale" — (007-024). The pattern — depart, encounter a williwaw, retreat — was the rhythm of the strait. No vessel, however well-handled, could fight the winds that funneled through those channels.

On his second departure, the *[[spray|Spray]]* made better progress, "fetching [[borgia-bay|Borgia Bay]], a few miles on her course" — (007-024). But the respite was brief; at [[borgia-bay|Borgia Bay]], the [[chile|Chile]]an gunboat *[[chilean-gunboat-huemel|Huemel]]* arrived and its officers advised [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] to leave immediately — the [[fuegians|Fuegians]] there were particularly dangerous.

## Geography

Three Island Cove lies on the northern shore of [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]], in the maze of channels, bays, and inlets between [[port-tamar|Port Tamar]] and [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]] entrance. The cove takes its name from the three small islets that partially shelter it from the strait's notorious westerly gales. In [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s day it was uncharted in any detail — one of countless anonymous indentations on a coast that had barely been surveyed.
