## Passage Near South Africa

Slocum's course from Australia to South Africa took him across the vast Indian Ocean (012-036). Chapter 17, titled "Leaving South Africa," describes his departure from the African coast and his approach to the Atlantic. While in South Africa, Slocum took pride in finding "that American humor was appreciated" there (017-022).

> It was a matter of no small pride to me in South Africa to find that American humor was never at a discount, and one of the best American stories I ever heard was told by the premier. At Hotel Royal one day, dining with Colonel Saunderson, M.P., his son, and Lieutenant Tipping, I met Mr. Stanley.
>
> (017-022)
