# Captain Eben Pierce

Captain Eben Pierce was a retired whaling captain from [[fairhaven|Fairhaven]], [[cape-ann|Massachusetts]], and the man who [[current|set]] [[joshua-slocum|Joshua Slocum]]'s historic voyage in motion by giving him the derelict sloop *[[spray|Spray]]*. Without Pierce's gift, there would have been no [[voyage|circumnavigation]].

## Biography

Pierce was a whaling captain who lived in [[fairhaven|Fairhaven]], [[cape-ann|Massachusetts]], across the Acushnet River from [[new-bedford|New Bedford]] — the heart of America's nineteenth-century whaling industry. By the time [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] met him around 1892, Pierce was retired from the sea.

The *[[spray|Spray]]* had been propped up in a field in [[fairhaven|Fairhaven]] for seven years, covered with [[sail|canvas]], and the thrifty locals had long wondered what Pierce intended to do with the antique vessel. According to [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], "the neighbors declared had been built in the year 1" — a characteristically humorous exaggeration (001-002).

## Role in the Voyage

Pierce's role in the voyage is foundational: he gave [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] the vessel itself. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] describes the moment with dry humor:

> "The next day I landed at Fairhaven, opposite New Bedford, and found that my friend had something of a joke on me. For seven years the joke had been on him. The 'ship' proved to be a very antiquated sloop called the *Spray*." — (001-002)

What Pierce offered as a casual gift — "I'll give you a ship," he had told [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] — turned out to be a rotting hulk that required thirteen months of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s own labor and $553.62 in materials to rebuild. Yet [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] never expressed anything but gratitude for the gift (001-002).

After the *[[spray|Spray]]* was rebuilt, Pierce accompanied [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] on her trial trip:

> "Sails were bent, and away she flew with my friend Captain Pierce and me, across Buzzard's Bay on a trial-trip — all right." — (001-003)

Pierce also appears in the appendix, where [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] traces the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s history: "when Captain Eben Pierce presented her to me, at the end of her natural life, she stood, as I have already described, propped up in a field at [[fairhaven|Fairhaven]]" (021-004).

## Significance

Captain Eben Pierce occupies a unique place in maritime history as the unwitting catalyst of the first solo [[voyage|circumnavigation]]. His gift of a derelict oyster sloop — worth almost nothing in monetary terms — became, through [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s skill and determination, one of the most famous vessels in nautical history.

Pierce's story illustrates the informal economy of New England's maritime community, where retired sea captains traded in old vessels, favors, and maritime lore. The seven-year joke that the townspeople had at Pierce's expense — wondering what he would do with the old *[[spray|Spray]]* — ultimately resolved into one of [[sail|sailing]]'s greatest achievements (001-002).
