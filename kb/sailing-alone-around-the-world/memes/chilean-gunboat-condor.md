# Condor

The *Condor* was a [[chile|Chile]]an gunboat that arrived at [[port-angosto|Port Angosto]] during [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s stay, sharing the anchorage with the *[[spray|Spray]]* and the Argentine cruiser *[[argentine-cruiser-azopardo|Azopardo]]*. Her presence — alongside her Argentine counterpart — reflected the delicate balance of naval power in the [[strait-of-magellan|Strait of Magellan]] during the 1890s, where two nations' warships shared the same confined waters under an uneasy peace.

## Specifications

The *Condor* was a steam-powered gunboat of the [[chile|Chilean]] Navy, similar in design and mission to the *[[chilean-gunboat-huemel|Huemel]]*. These vessels were small by warship standards — typically 500–1,000 [[tonnage|tons]] — but well-suited to the strait's narrow channels and frequent gales. Their shallow draft allowed them to operate close to shore, and their armament — a few medium-caliber guns — was sufficient for patrol duties and showing the flag.

> The bad weather on my last return to Port Angosto for shelter brought the Chilean gunboat *Condor* and the Argentine cruiser *Azopardo* into port.
>
> (010-006)

## History

The *Condor* appears in a single but revealing sentence in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s narrative: the [[gale|storm]] that drove the *[[spray|Spray]]* back to [[port-angosto|Port Angosto]] "brought the [[chile|Chile]]an gunboat *Condor* and the Argentine cruiser *[[argentine-cruiser-azopardo|Azopardo]]* into port" — (010-006). The image is striking: a [[chile|Chilean]] gunboat and an Argentine cruiser, representing two nations with a history of territorial rivalry, both seeking shelter from the same gale as a lone American in a sloop.

## In Slocum's Narrative

The *Condor*'s appearance is brief but significant. It confirms the density of naval traffic in the strait — [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] encountered multiple warships during his passage — and underscores the international character of what might otherwise seem an empty wilderness. For the [[chile|Chile]]an Navy, the *Condor* and her sisters were the instruments of sovereignty in a region where [[chile|Chile's]] claim depended on the ability to project power.

## Significance

Like the *[[chilean-gunboat-huemel|Huemel]]* and the *[[argentine-cruiser-azopardo|Azopardo]]*, the *Condor* represents the thin but persistent presence of state authority in the world's most remote waterways. That [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] encountered three warships in a single anchorage — two [[chile|Chilean]], one Argentine — speaks to the geopolitical reality of [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] in the 1890s, a passageway simultaneously at the edge of the world and at the center of South American naval strategy.
