## Geography

Tasmania is a large island state of [[australia|Australia]], separated from the mainland by the [[bass-strait|Bass Strait]]. During [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s visit, it was already well-established with several ports and a growing economy based on farming, mining, and maritime trade.

## Slocum's Visit

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] spent considerable time in Tasmania after crossing from [[melbourne|Melbourne]]. He described Tasmania as a place "where it is fine the year round" (014-005). He visited several ports, including [[beauty-point|Beauty Point]], near the great Tasmania goldmine at [[beacon|Beacon]]sfield (014-005).

> The season of fair weather around the north of Australia being yet a long way off, I sailed to other ports in Tasmania, where it is fine the year round, the first of these being Beauty Point, near which are Beaconsfield and the great Tasmania goldmine, which I visited in turn.
> (014-005)

While in Tasmania, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] delivered his first lecture of the voyage (014-001). He also had the *[[spray|Spray]]* inspected for safety at [[devonport|Devonport]], a thriving port on the river Mersey that was fast becoming the most important port in Tasmania (014-009).

## Why Tasmania?

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s decision to spend extended time in Tasmania was strategic. Rather than "thrashing round cold and [[gale|storm]]y [[cape-leeuwin|Cape Leeuwin]]," he elected to wait in Tasmania for the favorable season to pass through [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]] by way of the [[great-barrier-reef|Great Barrier Reef]] — a route that would take advantage of the anticyclones which, as he noted, "never fail" (013-025). He had also sailed around Tasmania years before and welcomed the chance to [[current|set]] foot on its shores again.

> Therefore, instead of thrashing round cold and stormy Cape Leeuwin, I decided to spend a pleasanter and more profitable time in Tasmania, waiting for the season for favorable winds through Torres Strait, by way of the Great Barrier Reef, the route I finally decided on. To sail this course would be taking advantage of anticyclones, which never fail, and besides it would give me the chance to put foot on the shores of Tasmania, round which I had sailed years before.
> (013-025)

## Devonport and the Spray's Inspection

At [[devonport|Devonport]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] submitted the *[[spray|Spray]]* to a thorough safety inspection — a significant gesture for a sailor who had already crossed three oceans alone. The port, situated on the river Mersey, was thriving and beginning to rival the older [[current|set]]tlements of the island (014-009). [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] also visited George Town, a small [[current|set]]tlement that he believed marked "the place where the first footprints were made by whites in Tasmania, though it never grew to be more than a hamlet" (014-007).

## Whaling and Maritime Activity

Tasmania was an active whaling center during [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s time. He encountered the ketch *Secret*, a fisherman, and the *Mary* of [[sydney|Sydney]], a steam ferryboat fitted for whaling, anchored in Tasmanian waters. He noted that whenever the word "whale" was spoken, the crew were ready to pursue (013-018).

## Local Hospitality

The Tasmanians received [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] with remarkable warmth. A testimonial from a lady became one of his treasured possessions (014-001). Another gentleman charged him "to tell the glories of Tasmania in every land and on every occasion" (014-012). The lady's gift, which [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had intended to keep only as a loan, later became the subject of mild self-reproach when he discovered, to his embarrassment, that he had inadvertently invested the money (015-013).

## Significance

Tasmania represented a period of rest and preparation for [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]. While waiting for the fair-weather season to continue north toward [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]], he explored the island, gave lectures, and received hospitality from the locals. The island also marked a strategic pivot — the decision to take the inner route through the [[great-barrier-reef|Barrier Reef]] rather than the southern ocean — that would define the next phase of his [[voyage|circumnavigation]].
