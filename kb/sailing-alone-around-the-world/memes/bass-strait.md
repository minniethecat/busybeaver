## Geography

Bass Strait is the channel of water separating the [[australia|Australia]]n mainland from the island of [[tasmania|Tasmania]], connecting the Tasman Sea to the east with the Great [[australia|Australia]]n Bight to the west. Its waters contain numerous islands, including Cliff Island, where a [[beacon|lighthouse]] guided passing vessels. The strait is known for its strong and variable winds, particularly during the southern summer months. (013-016)

In the summer months — December through March — east winds are normally prevalent through Bass Strait and around [[cape-leeuwin|Cape Leeuwin]]. However, during [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s passage, a vast amount of ice [[current|drift]]ing up from the Antarctic disrupted the normal wind patterns, bringing "much bad weather." (013-025)

## Role in the Voyage

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sailed from [[sydney|Sydney]] on December 6, 1896, intending to round [[cape-leeuwin|Cape Leeuwin]] for [[mauritius|Mauritius]] on his way home, and "coasted along toward Bass Strait in that direction." (013-014) From [[cape-bundooro|Cape Bundooro]], [[spray|the Spray]] passed Cliff Island in Bass Strait, where [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] "exchanged signals with the light-keepers while [[spray|the Spray]] worked up under the island." The wind howled that day while the sea broke over their rocky home. (013-016)

After a month spent in [[melbourne|Melbourne]], [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] departed again on January 24, 1897, crossing Bass Strait toward [[tasmania|Tasmania]]. (013-024) The prevailing east winds of summer had been disrupted by Antarctic ice, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] "considered it impracticable to pursue the course farther" around [[cape-leeuwin|Cape Leeuwin]]. Instead, he decided to "spend a pleasanter and more profitable time in [[tasmania|Tasmania]]," waiting for the season of favorable winds to sail through [[torres-strait|Torres Strait]] and the [[great-barrier-reef|Great Barrier Reef]]. (013-025)

The rip off [[melbourne|Port Phillip]] Heads was rough when [[spray|the Spray]] departed, but "with sea-room and under sail, she made good weather immediately after passing it. It was only a few hours' sail to [[tasmania|Tasmania]] across the strait, the wind being fair and blowing hard." (013-028)

> Time flew fast those days in Australia, and it was December 6, 1896, when the *Spray* sailed from Sydney. My intention was now to sail around Cape Leeuwin direct for Mauritius on my way home, and so I coasted along toward Bass Strait in that direction.
>
> (013-014)

## Historical Context

Bass Strait was first crossed by Europeans in 1798, when George Bass and Matthew Flinders sailed through it, proving [[tasmania|Tasmania]] was an island. By the 1890s, it was a busy shipping route connecting [[melbourne|Melbourne]] and [[sydney|Sydney]] with [[tasmania|Tasmania]]n ports like [[launceston|Launceston]]. Lighthouses at Cliff Island and Wilson's Promontory guided vessels through its often turbulent waters. (013-016)
