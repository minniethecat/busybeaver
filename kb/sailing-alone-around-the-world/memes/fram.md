# Fram

The *Fram* was the Norwegian polar exploration vessel designed by Colin Archer for [[fridtjof-nansen|Fridtjof Nansen]]'s 1893–1896 Arctic expedition. Built to be frozen into the ice and [[current|drift]] across the polar sea, she was — in her own element — as extraordinary as the *[[spray|Spray]]* was in hers. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], navigating the southern oceans while the *Fram* explored the Arctic ice, was aware of the parallel: two small ships, each pushing the boundaries of what was possible.

## Design and Construction

Colin Archer designed the *Fram* with a rounded hull — so that when the ice pressed in, the ship would be squeezed upward rather than crushed. She was massively built, with oak frames up to 28 inches thick and an ice sheathing of greenheart. Her [[rigging|rig]] was a three-masted schooner, and she carried an auxiliary steam engine. The design was radical, and it worked: the *Fram* [[current|drift]]ed across the Arctic Ocean for three years without damage.

## In Context of the Voyage

The *Fram* and the *[[spray|Spray]]* were contemporaries, and their voyages overlapped. While [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] was [[sail|sailing]] alone through [[strait-of-magellan|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] and across [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]], the *Fram* was [[current|drift]]ing through the polar ice. Both vessels demonstrated design principles that were ahead of their time; both captains were innovators who trusted their own judgment over conventional wisdom.

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], who followed [[fridtjof-nansen|Nansen]]'s exploits in the newspapers, would have recognized a kindred spirit. The Norwegian and the American, each in his own small ship, were proving that individual enterprise could still achieve what had once required national expeditions.

## Legacy

The *Fram* holds the record for the farthest north and farthest south (under Amundsen) of any wooden ship — a distinction unique in maritime history. She is preserved in Oslo, a monument to the golden age of polar exploration, as the *[[spray|Spray]]* is remembered in the annals of solo [[sail|sailing]].
