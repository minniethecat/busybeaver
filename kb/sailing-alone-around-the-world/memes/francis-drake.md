# Sir Francis Drake

Sir Francis Drake (1540–1596) was the English sea captain, privateer, and explorer who led the second expedition to circumnavigate the globe (1577–1580) (018-014). For [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], navigating the same southern waters that Drake had sailed three centuries earlier, the Elizabethan captain was a natural predecessor — another man who had taken a small ship [[voyage|around the world]].

## Biography

Born in Tavistock, Devon, Drake went to sea as a young man and rose to become one of Elizabethan England's most feared and celebrated sailors. His [[voyage|circumnavigation]] in the *[[golden-hind|Golden Hind]]* — a vessel not much larger than the *[[spray|Spray]]* — established English naval power in [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]] and made Drake a national hero. He died of dysentery off the coast of [[panama|Panama]] and was buried at sea in a lead coffin.

## Role in the Voyage

Drake appears in the narrative as a historical presence rather than a direct reference — his name evoked by the waters [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sailed. The [[strait-of-magellan|Strait of Magellan]], which Drake had navigated, was the most dangerous passage of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s voyage. [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]], which Drake had rounded, marked the southern limit of the known world. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sailed in Drake's wake, and the comparison — the Elizabethan adventurer and [[victoria|the Victoria]]n solo sailor — is implicit throughout the southern chapters.

> One of the old circumnavigators (Sir Francis Drake, I think), when he first saw this magnificent pile, sang, " 'Tis the fairest thing and the grandest cape I've seen in the whole circumference of the earth."
>
> (018-014)

## Significance

Drake represents the continuity of maritime exploration. The challenges he faced — [[gale|storm]], current, hostile shores — were the same challenges [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] faced. The difference was the vessel: Drake had a crew of about 60; [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had himself. The progress from Drake's *[[golden-hind|Golden Hind]]* to [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s *[[spray|Spray]]* is not one of technology but of seamanship — the demonstration that one man, in a well-found boat, could do what had once required a ship's company.
