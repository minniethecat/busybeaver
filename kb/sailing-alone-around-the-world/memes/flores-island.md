## Geography

Flores Island is the westernmost island of the [[azores|Azores]] archipelago, located in the North Atlantic approximately 1,200 miles west of Portugal. The island is of volcanic origin, with a high elevation that makes it visible from a great distance at sea. It lies 174 miles from [[fayal|Fayal]], the neighboring island to the east. (003-015)

Despite its prominence, Flores remained undiscovered for many years after the principal islands of the [[azores|Azores]] had been [[current|set]]tled and colonized. (003-015) The [[azores|Azores]] as a whole present a dramatic appearance from the deck of an approaching vessel. "Only those who have seen the [[azores|Azores]] from the deck of a vessel realize the beauty of the mid-ocean picture," [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] wrote. (003-016)

## In Slocum's Voyage

Flores was the first land [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sighted after eighteen days alone on [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] since leaving [[cape-sable|Cape Sable]], [[nova-scotia|Nova Scotia]]. His first glimpse of the island came through a peculiar atmospheric phenomenon on the morning of July 19, 1895:

> On the morning of July 19 a mystic dome like a mountain of silver stood alone in the sea ahead. Although the land was completely hidden by the white, glistening haze that shone in the sun like polished silver, I felt quite sure that it was Flores Island. At half-past four p.m. it was abeam. (003-015)

The haze later cleared, confirming his identification:

> The haze in the meantime had disappeared. Flores is one hundred and seventy-four miles from Fayal, and although it is a high island, it remained many years undiscovered after the principal group of the islands had been colonized. (003-015)

The following morning, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] sighted [[pico|Pico]] looming above the clouds, and island after island came into view as he sailed into the heart of the [[azores|Azores]]. He anchored at [[fayal|Fayal]] on July 20, exactly eighteen days out from [[cape-sable|Cape Sable]]. (003-017) The passage had been swift — an average of over 120 miles per day across the open Atlantic, a testament to [[spray|the Spray]]'s seakeeping abilities.

## Slocum's Stay in the Azores

Though Flores itself was only sighted in passing, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s arrival in the [[azores|Azores]] marked the beginning of a memorable four-day stay at [[fayal|Fayal]]. (003-020) He arrived during the fruit season, and the generosity of the islanders quickly overwhelmed his provisions: "there was soon more of all kinds of it put on board than I knew what to do with." (003-018)

The timing of his visit was fortuitous, coinciding with a festa on the neighboring island of [[pico|Pico]]. "Boats loaded with people came from other islands to celebrate at [[fayal|Horta]], the capital, or Jerusalem, of the [[azores|Azores]]." (003-019) A kindhearted local, grateful for kindnesses received in America, drove [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] for a day over the beautiful roads of [[fayal|Fayal]]. (003-019)

[[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] noted the Azoreans with appreciation, though he observed they were "not a very rich community." (003-018) A personal touch came when someone asked him to relay a message: "If this reaches the [[rigging|rig]]ht John Wilson, I am told to say that 'Antonio of [[pico|Pico]] remembers him.'" (003-020) These human connections recurred throughout [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s [[voyage|circumnavigation]].

## Historical Context

The [[azores|Azores]] were colonized by the Portuguese in the 15th century, but Flores and its neighbor Corvo, being the westernmost outliers of the archipelago, were among the last islands to be discovered and [[current|set]]tled. By [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s time in 1895, all the islands were well-established Portuguese territories with strong maritime connections. Many Azoreans had emigrated to America, particularly to [[new-bedford|New Bedford]] and other New England ports, creating transatlantic family ties that benefited [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] during his stay. (003-019)

The "mystic dome" effect [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] describes — a mountain appearing to float as a silver dome above the sea — is a known atmospheric refraction phenomenon sometimes observed over these high volcanic islands when temperature inversions trap haze at lower levels.

## Significance

The sighting of Flores marks the successful completion of the first ocean crossing of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s [[voyage|circumnavigation]]. After eighteen solitary days on [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]], the appearance of land — even as a ghostly silver dome in the haze — was a profound moment of relief and validation. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s quiet confidence in identifying the island despite the obscured view speaks to his deep navigational skill and familiarity with [[atlantic-ocean|the Atlantic]] approaches to the [[azores|Azores]]. (003-015)

The landfall at Flores also inaugurated a pattern that would repeat throughout the voyage: a solitary ocean passage, followed by warm receptions from local communities, provision replenishment, and the forging of human bonds across cultural and linguistic divides. That the [[azores|Azores]] were Portuguese territory also foreshadowed [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s later encounters with Portuguese communities in ports [[voyage|around the world]] — from the Azorean potato trader he met in the [[juan-fernandez-island|Juan Fernández Island]]s to the Portuguese officials at various colonial outposts. (010-023) (012-006)
