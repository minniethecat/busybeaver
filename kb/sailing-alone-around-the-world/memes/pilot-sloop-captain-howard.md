# Captain Howard's Pilot Sloop

[[captain-howard|Captain Howard]]'s pilot-sloop was a working vessel on the River Plate — the estuary between Argentina and [[uruguay|Uruguay]] — where [[captain-howard|Howard]] had made his career as a pilot. Though unnamed in the narrative, her significance is clear: she was the benchmark against which [[captain-howard|Howard]] measured the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s extraordinary self-[[helm|steering]] ability.

## Howard's Testimony

[[captain-howard|Howard]] "had owned and sailed a pilot-sloop on the river for many years, but this feat took the wind out of his [[sail|sails]] at last, and he cried, 'I'll be stranded on Chico Bank if ever I saw the like of it!'" (006-014). The pilot-sloop was [[captain-howard|Howard]]'s standard of reference — the vessel he knew best, the yardstick of [[sail|sailing]] performance. That the *[[spray|Spray]]* exceeded her so decisively was the strongest possible testimony.

> I was glad to have a sailor of Howard's experience on board to witness her performance of sailing with no living being at the helm. Howard sat near the binnacle and watched the compass while the sloop held her course so steadily that one would have declared that the card was nailed fast. Not a quarter of a point did she deviate from her course.
>
> (006-014)
