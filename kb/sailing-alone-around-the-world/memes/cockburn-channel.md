## Geography

Cockburn Channel is a narrow waterway in the labyrinth of islands northwest of [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]], leading into the strait at a point opposite [[cape-froward|Cape Froward]], the southernmost point of the South American mainland. The channel weaves through a dense archipelago of rocky, uninhabited islands, with steep bluffs and precipices overhanging "a bold shore of wild and uninviting appearance." (008-010)

The channel was known to navigators as an alternative entrance to [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] from [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]] side, used when the more direct approaches were blocked by weather. (008-001)

## Role in the Voyage

After being driven south toward [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]] by a [[storm|violent storm]] that began on March 3, 1896, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] worked his way back toward [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]]. Navigating through Fury Island and the "Milky Way of the sea" — the white breakers over sunken rocks northwest of [[cape-horn|Cape Horn]] — he eventually reached smooth water inside small islands. (008-009) [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] called this "the greatest sea adventure of my life." (008-008)

The [[spray|Spray]]'s fortune turned: "I discovered, as she sailed along through a labyrinth of islands, that she was in the Cockburn Channel, which leads into [[strait-of-gibraltar|the Strait]] of [[ferdinand-magellan|Magellan]] at a point opposite [[cape-froward|Cape Froward]], and that she was already passing Thieves' Bay, suggestively named." (008-010) On the night of March 8, [[spray|the Spray]] anchored "in a snug cove at the Turn! Every heartbeat on [[spray|the Spray]] now counted thanks." (008-010)

Rather than sail east to the [[sandy-point|Sandy Point settlement]] for repairs, [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] turned into the northwestward reach of the strait and "[[current|set]] to work with my palm and needle at every opportunity, when at anchor and when [[sail|sailing]]," slowly repairing the mainsail torn to rags by the gale. It was slow but steady work: "little by little the squaresail on the boom expanded to the dimensions of a serviceable mainsail with a peak to it and a leech besides." (009-002)

> The *Spray*'s good luck followed fast. I discovered, as she sailed along through a labyrinth of islands, that she was in the Cockburn Channel, which leads into the Strait of Magellan at a point opposite Cape Froward, and that she was already passing Thieves' Bay, suggestively named. And at night, March 8, behold, she was at anchor in a snug cove at the Turn! Every heartbeat on the *Spray* now counted thanks.
>
> (008-010)

## Historical Context

The channels and fjords of western [[tierra-del-fuego|Tierra del Fuego]] were known to European mariners since the sixteenth century. By the 1890s, the Cockburn Channel was charted but remained a remote and hazardous passage, frequented only by vessels forced into it by the extreme weather of [[cape-of-good-hope|the Cape]] Horn region. The area was inhabited by [[tierra-del-fuego|Fuegia]]n tribes who communicated between islands by canoe and used smoke signals. (008-013)
