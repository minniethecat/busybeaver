**New Bedford** is a port city in southeastern [[cape-ann|Massachusetts]], situated on the west bank of the Acushnet River opposite [[fairhaven|Fairhaven]]. In the late nineteenth century, it was renowned as a center of the American whaling industry — a reputation reflected in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s characterization of it as "the home of whaling-captains" (001-009).

## Role in the Voyage

New Bedford figures in the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s origin story. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] landed at [[fairhaven|Fairhaven]] — "opposite New Bedford" — to inspect the vessel his friend [[eben-pierce|Captain Eben Pierce]] had offered him (001-007). The two towns were connected by a bridge, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] noted that "the walking is good" between them (001-009). The whaling culture of New Bedford left a mark on the *[[spray|Spray]]*'s design: tales of arctic whaling voyages inspired [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] "to put a double [[current|set]] of breast-hooks in the *[[spray|Spray]]*, that she might shunt ice" (001-009).

New Bedford also served as a transatlantic link. Azorean sailors who had worked on New Bedford whale-ships later assisted [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] during his Atlantic passages. At [[horta|Horta]] in the [[azores|Azores]], a local man who "had sailed out of New Bedford" in whale-ships steered a boat for him (010-023). Later, Portuguese immigrants from the [[azores|Azores]] traveling "by way of New Bedford" brought [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] fresh provisions during his stay in the [[strait-of-magellan|Strait of Magellan]] (012-006).

## Maritime Significance

New Bedford in the 1890s was at the tail end of its golden age of whaling. The city's whale-ship captains had charted oceans worldwide, and their knowledge of winds, currents, and distant ports was woven into the fabric of the community. [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]], himself a veteran of deep-water sail, moved comfortably in this world. [[eben-pierce|Captain Eben Pierce]], who gifted him the *[[spray|Spray]]*, was a New Bedford man, and [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] later recorded that his boat's papers were "directed to New Bedford" by well-wishers encountered across [[pacific-ocean|the Pacific]] (015-030).

> The next day I landed at Fairhaven, opposite New Bedford, and found that my friend had something of a joke on me. For seven years the joke had been on him. The "ship" proved to be a very antiquated sloop called the *Spray*, which the neighbors declared had been built in the year 1.
>
> (001-007)
