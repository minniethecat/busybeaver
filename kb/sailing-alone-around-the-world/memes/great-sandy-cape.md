## Passage

The [[spray|Spray]] rounded Great Sandy Cape, which Slocum described as a notable event in the log (014-040). Many thousands of miles later, near Sandy Cape, Australia, the seas fell lighter each day (017-013).

## Narrative Significance

Great Sandy Cape, on the coast of Queensland, was a notable waypoint during Slocum's Australian coastal passage (014-040). The cape's prominence in Slocum's memory is evidenced by his reference to it many thousands of miles later, near Sandy Cape, [[australia|Australia]], when the seas grew lighter (017-013). This retrospective mention illustrates how the landmarks of Slocum's voyage remained fixed in his navigator's memory, serving as reference points long after they had slipped below the horizon.

> On the following day the Spray rounded Great Sandy Cape, and, what is a notable event in every voyage, picked up the trade-winds, and these winds followed her now for many thousands of miles, never ceasing to blow from a moderate gale to a mild summer breeze, except at rare intervals.
>
> (014-040)
