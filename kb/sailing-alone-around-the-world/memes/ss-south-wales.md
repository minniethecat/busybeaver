# SS South Wales

The SS *South Wales* was a British steamship that crossed paths with the *[[spray|Spray]]* off the coast of South America. Like many passing vessels in [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s narrative, her significance lies not in her own history — which is largely unrecorded — but in the moment of contact: a great steamship and a small sloop, speaking each other on the open ocean.

## The Encounter

"The steamship *South Wales* spoke the *[[spray|Spray]]*" (006-002) — the laconic entry conceals the importance of the meeting. For a solo sailor, any contact with another vessel was an event: confirmation of position, exchange of news, perhaps the gift of provisions or newspapers. The *South Wales*, proceeding on her commercial route, represented the world [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]] had left behind — the world of schedules, cargoes, and crews.

## Significance

Vessels like the *South Wales* populate the margins of [[joshua-slocum|Slocum]]'s narrative — unnamed or barely named ships that briefly shared his ocean. Collectively, they form a counterpoint to his solitude: proof that even on the most remote stretches of sea, the sailor was never entirely alone. The *South Wales* spoke the *[[spray|Spray]]*; the *[[spray|Spray]]* answered; and each continued on its way.

> The steamship South Wales spoke the Spray and unsolicited gave the longitude by chronometer as 48° W., "as near as I can make it," the captain said.
