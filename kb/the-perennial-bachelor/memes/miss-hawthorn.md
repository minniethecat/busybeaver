# Miss Hawthorn

## Overview

Miss Hawthorn is a young woman in Wilmington society, part of the Hawthorn family that includes her mother [[Mrs. Hawthorn]] and (probably) her sister [[Lucy Hawthorn]]. She appears briefly at a social gathering and is the subject of an unexpected call by Victor Campion at the Hawthorn residence in Chapter 19, where his visit is met with the family's characteristic social ambition.

## Key Appearances

### The Social Gathering (Chapter 12)

At an evening party, Miss Hawthorn appears in fragmentary dialogue — the narrative catches her mid-conversation, snippets of party chatter about French and jersey dresses. The scene captures the texture of Wilmington social life: a swirl of overlapping voices in which individual characters surface briefly and recede.

### Victor's Call (Chapter 19)

Victor Campion, in a moment of social restlessness, calls at the Hawthorns' "big brownstone house" and is "astonished to hear himself asking the butler, 'Is Miss Hawthorn in?'" The call is less about Miss Hawthorn herself than about the gravitational pull the Hawthorn family exerts — they represent a particular kind of social ambition that Victor finds alternately attractive and suffocating. The narrative tracks his thought: he imagines himself as "the strong oak for those three ivies, Mr. and Mrs. Hawthorn and Miss Hawthorn, to cling to. He almost saw himself so, through their eyes."

The moment passes. The chapter turns to Lucy's visit to the Leafs', and Victor's attention shifts decisively to her. Miss Hawthorn, like her family, serves as a marker of the social possibilities Victor contemplates but does not pursue.

## Character and Significance

Miss Hawthorn is a minor presence defined almost entirely through her family context. She is less an individual than a component of the Hawthorn household — part of the "three ivies" Victor briefly imagines supporting. Her function is atmospheric: she populates the social world, fills out the party scenes, and provides a contrast to the richer characterization of her sister Lucy, who genuinely captures Victor's romantic imagination.

## Relationships

- **Mrs. Hawthorn**: Her mother, a figure of "sweeping violet silk and foaming lace"
- **Lucy Hawthorn**: Her sister (probable), the Hawthorn daughter who becomes the object of Victor's affection
