# Raymond Line

## Overview

The Raymond Line appears in the novel as a commercial shipping or transportation enterprise — one of the businesses that constitute the economic infrastructure of the novel's world without becoming its subject. It is mentioned in passing, a name that locates the narrative within a specific commercial geography without requiring elaboration.

## Function in the Novel

The Raymond Line's function is atmospheric rather than narrative. Its name appears in connection with travel, trade, or transportation — the kind of commercial entity that the Campion family would have known of without necessarily patronizing. It is part of the novel's documentary texture: the accumulation of proper names — of businesses, places, institutions — that gives Parrish's fictional world its density and verisimilitude.

## Significance

The Raymond Line, like [[the-local-store]], represents the commercial world that exists alongside the Campion family's domestic sphere. Its presence in the novel — brief, unelaborated, taken for granted — is characteristic of Parrish's method: the world is full of institutions and enterprises that shape the characters' lives without ever becoming the focus of their attention.
