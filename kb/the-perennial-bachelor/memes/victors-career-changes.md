# Victor's Career Changes

## Overview

Victor Campion's professional life is a series of beginnings without completions, each career change representing a fresh attempt to establish himself as an independent adult — and each failure drawing him back into the orbit of The Maples and his mother's devotion. The pattern of arrested development that defines his romantic life is replicated in his working life: he tries, he falters, he returns.

## The Pattern

The novel traces Victor's career through indirection and summary rather than dramatized scenes. He tries various occupations; none takes hold. Each venture begins with Mamma's encouragement — which is also, the novel implies, the source of its failure. Mamma's support is a form of containment: she wants Victor to succeed, but she cannot want him to succeed anywhere but near her.

The career changes are linked to the novel's broader theme of [[the-perennial-bachelor|perpetual bachelorhood]]. Victor's professional rootlessness and his romantic unavailability are symptoms of the same condition — an inability to commit, to choose, to separate from the maternal household. Each failed career is a failed adulthood.

## Significance

Victor's career changes are the professional expression of his existential condition. He cannot find work that suits him because no work can compete with the ease of being Mamma's son. The world of employment — with its demands, its hierarchies, its requirement of sustained effort — is the adult world Victor has been raised to avoid.

The novel's treatment of his career is unsentimental. There is no suggestion that Victor is unlucky or mistreated; he is simply incapable. His mother's love, which was supposed to prepare him for the world, has instead made the world impossible for him.
