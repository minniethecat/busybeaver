## Overview

Leo Schutzmacher is the most commercially honest doctor in *[[the-doctors-dilemma-play|The Doctor's Dilemma]]*. Unlike [[sir-colenso-ridgeon|Ridgeon]], [[bloomfield-bonington|B.B.]], or [[cutler-walpole|Walpole]], he makes no pretense that medicine is anything other than a trade: "I'm not a good doctor — I'm a doctor who does well" (001-003). His ethnic identity as a Jewish practitioner — noted in the stage direction — adds a dimension of outsider perspective to [[the-doctors-dilemma-play|the play]]'s critique of the English [[the-medical-profession-as-institution|medical establishment]].

## Role in the Play

Schutzmacher functions as [[the-doctors-dilemma-play|the play]]'s truth-teller about [[private-practice|medical economics]]. Where the other [[medical-profession|doctors]] wrap their commercial interests in professional rhetoric, Schutzmacher openly describes his practice as a business transaction. His presence exposes the [[poverty-and-class|class]] and ethnic hierarchies within Edwardian medicine: the Jewish GP who charges sixpence and the titled consultants who charge [[private-practice|guinea]]s serve the same market but occupy different social worlds.

## Character Analysis

Shaw uses Schutzmacher to puncture [[the-medical-profession-as-institution|the profession]]al pretensions of [[sir-colenso-ridgeon|Ridgeon]]'s circle. His cheerful admission — "I'm not a good doctor: I'm a doctor who does well" — is [[the-doctors-dilemma-play|the play]]'s most honest description of [[medical-profession|medical practice]], and it shames the other [[medical-profession|doctors]] precisely because they cannot deny its truth. His practice among the Jewish community in the East End represents a parallel medical economy that the West End consultants neither see nor acknowledge.

## Relationships

Schutzmacher's relationship with the other [[medical-profession|doctors]] is collegial but distant — he was at University College with them but has not risen in the same way. At the [[star-and-garter-hotel|Star and Garter]] dinner (002-001), he sits on the left with [[cutler-walpole|Walpole]], suggesting his marginal status within the group. His brief exchange with [[sir-colenso-ridgeon|Ridgeon]] establishes a shared professional history without shared professional values.
