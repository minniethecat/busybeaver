## Overview

Redpenny is a minor but structurally important character in *[[the-doctors-dilemma-play|The Doctor's Dilemma]]* — a medical student "surname Redpenny, Christian name unknown and of no importance" (001-001) who serves as [[sir-colenso-ridgeon|Ridgeon]]'s secretary and provides the clinical-administrative context for [[the-doctors-dilemma-play|the play]]'s opening. His presence establishes the hierarchy of the [[ridgeons-consulting-room|consulting room]] and [[the-medical-profession-as-institution|the profession]]al aspirations that drive the medical world Shaw satirizes.

## Role in the Play

Redpenny functions primarily as a dramatic device — his interruptions announce the arrival of successive [[medical-profession|doctors]], structuring the Act I parade of medical characters. His subordinate position relative to [[sir-colenso-ridgeon|Ridgeon]] mirrors the hierarchical nature of medical training, and his silent presence during the [[medical-profession|doctors]]' conversations imp[[truth-and-deception|lies]] the transmission of professional values from one generation to the next.

## Character Analysis

Shaw gives Redpenny the barest of characterizations — his Christian name is "unknown and of no importance" (001-001) — yet this very anonymity is significant. Redpenny represents the unformed medical student who will absorb [[the-medical-profession-as-institution|the profession]]al culture he observes in [[sir-colenso-ridgeon|Ridgeon]]'s [[ridgeons-consulting-room|consulting room]], including its conspiracies, its hypocrisies, and its ethical compromises. He is the future of [[the-medical-profession-as-institution|the profession]], watching the present generation fail.

## Relationships

Redpenny's relationship with [[sir-colenso-ridgeon|Ridgeon]] is that of apprentice to master — he sits at "the writing-table" (001-003) and manages the flow of patients and visitors. His relationship with Emmy, the serving-woman, is one of parallel subordination within the consulting-room hierarchy. He has no significant interaction with the other [[medical-profession|doctors]] beyond announcing their arrival.
