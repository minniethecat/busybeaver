# Servilius

## Role in the Play

Servilius is one of [[timon|Timon]]'s servants, dispatched alongside [[flaminius|Flaminius]] to solicit loans from [[timon|Timon]]'s [[friendship|friends]]. He is sent to [[lucius|Lucius]] but encounters [[lucilius|Lucilius]] instead, and their exchange reveals the cascading pattern of refusal that defines the play's second movement. (004-094)

## Key Scenes

**The Summons** (004-094–004-095): Servilius and [[flaminius|Flaminius]] are called by [[timon|Timon]] and dispatched to various lords. [[timon|Timon]]'s casual "Within there! [[flaminius|Flaminius]]! Servilius!" marks the beginning of the [[debt|borrowing]] campaign that will expose the hollowness of his [[friendship|friends]]hips. (004-094)

**The Encounter with [[lucilius|Lucilius]]** (006-010–006-022): Servilius finds [[lucilius|Lucilius]] and delivers [[timon|Timon]]'s request. [[lucilius|Lucilius]] initially assumes [[timon|Timon]] has sent a gift — "he's ever sending: how shall I thank him, thinkest thou?" (006-014) — only to learn that [[timon|Timon]] needs [[gold|money]]. Servilius's blunt "Has only sent his present occasion now, my lord" (006-015) punctures the gift-economy illusion. When pressed, he insists on the urgency: "If his occasion were not virtuous, I should not urge it half so [[trust|faith]]fully" (006-017). [[lucilius|Lucilius]]'s refusal, couched in self-laceration — "What a wicked beast was I" (006-020) — mirrors the pattern established by [[lucullus|Lucullus]].

## Relationships

**[[timon|Timon]]**: Servilius serves [[timon|Timon]] [[trust|faith]]fully, undertaking the degrading task of begging from former beneficiaries of [[timon|Timon]]'s [[bounty|generosity]].

**[[lucilius|Lucilius]]**: The former recipient of [[timon|Timon]]'s kindness who refuses to reciprocate. Their exchange demonstrates that even those who recognise the in[[law|justice]] of refusal — [[lucilius|Lucilius]] had just condemned [[lucullus|Lucullus]] for the same behaviour — replicate it when asked themselves.

**[[flaminius|Flaminius]]**: Fellow servant dispatched on a parallel mission. Their shared [[service|loyalty]] and parallel [[fortune|fate]]s reinforce the structural nature of the betrayal.

**[[lucius|Lucius]]**: The false friend to whom Servilius was originally sent. The misdirection to [[lucilius|Lucilius]] is part of the play's chain-of-refusal structure.


## Notable Quotations

Servilius delivers [[timon|Timon]]'s request with blunt clarity, cutting through the gift-economy [[hope|expectation]]s:

> Has only sent his present occasion now, my lord; requesting your lordship to supply his instant use with so many talents.
>
> (006-015)

When [[lucilius|Lucilius]] presses him, Servilius insists on the legitimacy of [[timon|Timon]]'s need — his [[service|loyalty]] making no allowance for evasion:

> But in the mean time he wants less, my lord. If his occasion were not virtuous, I should not urge it half so faithfully.
>
> (006-017)

Pressed once more, his simple affirmation carries the weight of his entire mission:

> Upon my soul, 'tis true, sir.
>
> (006-019)
