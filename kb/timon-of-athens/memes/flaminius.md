# Flaminius

## Role in the Play

Flaminius is one of [[timon|Timon]]'s servants, dispatched alongside [[servilius|Servilius]] to borrow [[gold|money]] from [[timon|Timon]]'s [[friendship|friends]] after [[timon|Timon]]'s [[wealth|fortune]] collapses. He is sent to [[lucullus|Lucullus]], where his moral rectitude is tested — and vindicated — in a scene that crystallises the play's critique of transactional [[friendship|friends]]hip. (004-094)

## Key Scenes

**The Embassy to [[lucullus|Lucullus]]** (005-002–005-019): Flaminius waits at [[lucullus|Lucullus]]'s house to request fifty talents on [[timon|Timon]]'s behalf. [[lucullus|Lucullus]], assuming Flaminius brings a gift, greets him warmly: "Flaminius, honest Flaminius; you are very respectively welcome, sir" (005-007). When Flaminius explains his errand, [[lucullus|Lucullus]]'s manner transforms instantly. He attempts to bribe Flaminius to lie to [[timon|Timon]] — "I have noted thee always wise. Here's to thee" (005-014) — offering coins in exchange for reporting that [[lucullus|Lucullus]] was not at home.

**The Moral Triumph** (005-017–005-019): Flaminius throws the [[gold|money]] back and delivers one of the play's most righteous speeches: "Is't possible the world should so much differ, And we alive that lived? Fly, damned baseness, To him that worships thee!" (005-017). His curse upon [[lucullus|Lucullus]] — "May these add to the number that may scald thee! Let moulten coin be thy damnation" (005-019) — is the moral judgment the play's structure otherwise leaves implicit.

## Relationships

**[[timon|Timon]]**: Flaminius is loyal to [[timon|Timon]], undertaking the humiliating errand of begging from false [[friendship|friends]]. His indignation on [[timon|Timon]]'s behalf — "I feel master's passion!" (005-019) — is genuine and unselfish.

**[[lucullus|Lucullus]]**: The false friend who tries to bribe Flaminius. Their interaction is a study in contrasting moral economies: [[lucullus|Lucullus]] understands every relationship as a transaction; Flaminius refuses to sell his honesty.

**[[servilius|Servilius]]**: Fellow servant, dispatched simultaneously to borrow from [[lucius|Lucius]]. Their parallel missions produce parallel outcomes, demonstrating the universality of [[timon|Timon]]'s betrayal.


## Notable Quotations

Flaminius's rejection of [[lucullus|Lucullus]]'s bribe is one of the play's few moments of unambiguous moral clarity:

> Is't possible the world should so much differ, And we alive that lived? Fly, damned baseness, To him that worships thee!
>
> (005-017)

His curse upon the false friend distils the play's moral out[[anger|rage]] into a single speech:

> May these add to the number that may scald thee! Let moulten coin be thy damnation, Thou disease of a friend, and not himself! Has friendship such a faint and milky heart, It turns in less than two nights? O you gods, I feel master's passion! this slave, Unto his honour, has my lord's meat in him: Why should it thrive and turn to nutriment, When he is turn'd to poison?
>
> (005-019)
