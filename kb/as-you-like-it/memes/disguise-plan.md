# Rosalind and [[celia|Celia]]'s Disguise Plan

## Overview

The [[disguise|Disguise]] Plan is the crucial turning point at the end of Act 1, Scene 3 of *As You Like It*. Following [[rosalinds-banishment|Rosalind's banishment]] by [[duke-frederick|Duke Frederick]], [[rosalind|Rosalind]] and [[celia|Celia]] devise their escape from the court. [[celia|Celia]] proposes they flee to [[forest-of-arden|the Forest]] of [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]] in "poore and meane attire" with smirched faces, but [[rosalind|Rosalind]] — being "more then common tall" — proposes a bolder ruse: she will disguise herself as a young man named [[ganimed|Ganymede]], carrying a "gallant curtelax" and affecting a "swashing and a marshall outside." [[celia|Celia]] will become [[aliena|Aliena]], "Something that hath a reference to my state." They recruit [[touchstone|Touchstone]] the fool to accompany them as "a comfort to our trauaile."

## Dramatic Function

The disguise plan is the engine of the play's comic plot. By adopting male attire, [[rosalind|Rosalind]] gains the [[freedom]] to move safely through [[forest-of-arden-place|the forest]] and, crucially, to conduct the "love cure" of [[orlando|Orlando]] from a position of masculine authority. Her disguise as [[ganimed|Ganymede]] — Jove's page — also creates the play's central dramatic irony: the audience knows the "[[youth]]" counseling [[orlando|Orlando]] is the very woman he loves.

[[celia|Celia]]'s disguise as [[aliena|Aliena]] ("the alien/stranger") complements [[rosalind|Rosalind]]'s: where [[rosalind|Rosalind]]'s disguise grants power, [[celia|Celia]]'s grants invisibility. And the recruitment of [[touchstone|Touchstone]], the court fool, ensures that court [[wit]] will survive the journey into the pastoral world.

## Key Passages

### [[rosalind|Rosalind]]'s Proposal (Act 1, Scene 3)

[[rosalind|Rosalind]], being tall, proposes the male disguise:

> Because that I am more then common tall, / That I did suite me all points like a man, / A gallant curtelax vpon my thigh, / A bore-speare in my hand, and in my heart / Lye there what hidden womans feare there will (003-052)

### The Naming (Act 1, Scene 3)

The new identities are chosen:

> Cel. What shall I call thee when thou art a man? / Ros. Ile haue no worse a name then Ioues owne Page, / And therefore looke you call me Ganimed. / But what will you be call'd? / Cel. Something that hath a reference to my state: / No longer Celia, but Aliena (003-054)

### Recruiting Touchstone (Act 1, Scene 3)

[[rosalind|Rosalind]] proposes taking the fool:

> Ros. But Cosen, what if we assaid to steale / The clownish Foole out of your Fathers Court: / Would he not be a comfort to our trauaile? (003-055)
