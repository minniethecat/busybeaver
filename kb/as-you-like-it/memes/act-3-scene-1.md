(011-001)Enter Duke, Lords, & [[oliver|Oliuer]].

(011-002)Du. Not see him since? Sir, sir, that cannot be:

(011-003)But were I not the better part made mercie,

(011-004)I should not seeke an absent argument

(011-005)Of my reuenge, thou present: but looke to it,

(011-006)Finde out thy brother wheresoere he is,

(011-007)Seeke him with Candle: bring him dead, or liuing

(011-008)Within this tweluemonth, or turne thou no more

(011-009)To seeke a liuing in our Territorie.

(011-010)Thy Lands and all things that thou dost call thine,

(011-011)Worth seizure, do we seize into our hands,

(011-012)Till thou canst quit thee by thy brothers mouth,

(011-013)Of what we thinke against thee

(011-014)Ol. Oh that your Highnesse knew my heart in this:

(011-015)I neuer lou'd my brother in my life

(011-016)Duke. More villaine thou. Well push him out of dores

(011-017)And let my officers of such a nature

(011-018)Make an extent vpon his house and Lands:

(011-019)Do this expediently, and turne him going.

(011-020)Exeunt.
