# Forest of Arden

## Definition

The Forest of Arden is the pastoral "green world" of As You Like It — a space of transformation where [[persons|characters]] exiled from the court undergo moral and romantic regeneration. Drawing on the pastoral convention that opposes the simplicity of rural life to the corruption of the court, Shakespeare's [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]] functions as both a real geographical location (the Ardennes region) and a symbolic space where the play's philosophical and romantic resolutions become possible.

## In As You Like It

The Forest is introduced early as the destination of the exiled [[duke-senior|Duke Senior]], who "is already in the Forrest of [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]], and a many merry men with him; and there they liue like the old Robin Hood of England" (001-043). This initial description establishes [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]] as a utopian alternative to [[duke-frederick|Duke Frederick]]'s court: a place of fellowship, [[freedom]], and restored natural order.

[[duke-senior|Duke Senior]]'s opening speech in [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]] (Act 2, Scene 1) delivers the play's most explicit pastoral manifesto. He contrasts [[forest-of-arden-place|the forest]]'s "sweet" adversity with "painted pompe" (004-005) and finds moral instruction in nature's elements: the winter wind is "no flattery" but a truthful "counsellor" (004-012). The [[green-world|forest]] life is "more free from perill then the enuious Court" (004-006).

[[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]] becomes the theater for the play's romantic transformations. [[orlando|Orlando]] hangs [[verse-and-poetry|love poems]] on its trees (012-007) (012-008); [[rosalind|Rosalind]], disguised as [[ganimed|Ganymede]], conducts her love-cure there; and four couples are united in marriage at its edge. The [[green-world|forest]] also hosts the play's philosophical set-pieces: [[jaques|Jaques]]'s "All the world's a stage" speech (010-151) and [[touchstone|Touchstone]]'s satire of courtly manners are both products of [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]]'s contemplative leisure.

Yet [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]] is not an uncomplicated paradise. It contains danger (the lioness that attacks [[oliver|Oliver]]), harsh weather, and the melancholy of [[jaques|Jaques]]. The [[green-world|forest]]'s pastoral simplicity is repeatedly punctured by the very courtly artifices the [[persons|characters]] have fled — most notably through [[touchstone|Touchstone]]'s witty deflations of pastoral convention.

## Key Passages

### The Forest Introduced (Act 1, Scene 1)

> They say hee is already in the Forrest of Arden, and a many merry men with him; and there they liue like the old Robin Hood of England (001-043)

### [[duke-senior|Duke Senior]]'s Pastoral Manifesto (Act 2, Scene 1)

> Are not these woods / More free from perill then the enuious Court? (004-006)

> This is no flattery: these are counsellors / That feelingly perswade me what I am (004-012)

### [[orlando|Orlando]]'s Arboreal Poetry (Act 3, Scene 2)

> O Rosalind, these Trees shall be my Bookes, / And in their barkes my thoughts Ile charracter (012-007)

> That euerie eye, which in this Forrest lookes, / Shall see thy vertue witnest euery where (012-008)

### [[celia|Celia]]'s Flight to Arden (Act 1, Scene 3)

> To seeke my Vncle in the Forrest of Arden (003-097)
