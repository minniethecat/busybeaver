## Scene Context

In the final scene of the play, Act 5 Scene 4, all the major [[persons|characters]] gather in the [[forest-of-arden-place|Forest of Arden]]. [[rosalind|Rosalind]], having orchestrated a complex series of promises and conditions, prepares to reveal her true identity and resolve the multiple love plots. At a crucial moment, she exits and returns accompanied by the god [[hymen|Hymen]] — a supernatural entrance that transforms the scene from a secular comedy into something approaching divine ritual.

## The Entrance

The stage direction reads: "Enter [[hymen|Hymen]], [[rosalind|Rosalind]], and [[celia|Celia]]" (022-059). [[duke-senior|Duke Senior]] immediately recognizes the mythological figure, remarking "If there be truth in sight, you are my daughter" (022-060) — his visual recognition of [[rosalind|Rosalind]] confirmed by [[hymen|Hymen]]'s presence. The god's entrance serves as a theatrical device that authorizes the multiple marriages about to take place.

[[hymen|Hymen]] speaks in verse that elevates the tone:
> "Then is there [[joy-and-celebration|mirth]] in heaven, / When earthly things made even / Atone together" [022-061–063]

## The Ceremonial Function

[[hymen|Hymen]] performs three key ceremonial actions:

1. **Identifying [[rosalind|Rosalind]]**: "Good duke, receive thy daughter, / [[hymen|Hymen]] from heaven brought her" [022-065–066] — the god authenticates [[rosalind|Rosalind]]'s true identity before the assembled company.

2. **Presenting the couples**: [[hymen|Hymen]] systematically presents each pair — [[orlando|Orlando]] and [[rosalind|Rosalind]], [[oliver|Oliver]] and [[celia|Celia]], [[phebe|Phoebe]] and [[silvius|Silvius]], [[touchstone|Touchstone]] and [[audrey|Audrey]] — declaring "You and you no cross shall part" [022-075–080].

3. **Blessing the unions**: "'Tis [[hymen|Hymen]] peoples every town, / High [[marriage|wedlock]] then be honored" [022-096–097] — the god's final blessing universalizes the four marriages as a celebration of social order restored.

## Dramatic Significance

[[hymen|Hymen]]'s entrance functions as a *deus ex machina* that resolves the play's romantic complications with supernatural authority. In a play that has consistently questioned categories of identity — gender, rank, sincerity — the appearance of an actual god offers a moment of unchallengeable certainty. The mythological frame also connects the comedy to the [[marriage|marriage]] rituals of Renaissance court masques, where such divine interventions were a familiar convention.
