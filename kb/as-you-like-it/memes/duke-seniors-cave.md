# [[duke-senior|Duke Senior]]'s Cave

## Overview

[[duke-senior|Duke Senior]]'s Cave is the exiled Duke's dwelling and the informal court of [[forest-of-arden|the Forest]] of [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]] in Acts 2 through 5 of *As You Like It*. More than a shelter, the cave functions as a symbolic counterpoint to [[duke-fredericks-court|[[duke-frederick|Duke Frederick]]'s Court]]: where the court embodies hierarchy, envy, and usurpation, the cave represents fellowship, hospitality, and the natural order restored. It is the site of [[orlando|Orlando]]'s reception into [[forest-of-arden-place|the forest]] community, [[oliver|Oliver]]'s account of his conversion, and the final gathering before the weddings.

## Dramatic Function

The cave serves as the Duke's "bower" — a rustic alternative to the stolen court. Here the exiled Duke and his "Coe-mates, and brothers in [[freedom|exile]]" (004-003) hold their informal gatherings, complete with song, philosophy, and the hospitality owed to any stranger who stumbles into [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]].

Three pivotal moments occur at or near the cave. First, the Duke welcomes [[orlando|Orlando]] — the son of his beloved Sir Rowland de Boys — with open arms, telling the old servant [[adam|Adam]] to "Go to my Caue" for rest and refreshment (010-204). Second, [[oliver|Oliver]] recounts how [[orlando|Orlando]] saved him from the lioness and "led me instantly vnto his Caue" for refuge (018-149). Third, at the play's conclusion, [[jaques|Jaques]] declares he will "stay to know, at your abandon'd caue" what becomes of [[duke-frederick|Duke Frederick]]'s conversion (022-142), marking the cave as the site where stories are told and transformations witnessed.

## Key Passages

### [[orlando|Orlando]]'s Welcome (Act 2, Scene 7)

When [[orlando|Orlando]] bursts into the Duke's camp demanding food, the Duke responds with grace rather than force, then welcomes him as Sir Rowland's son:

> Be truly welcome hither: I am the Duke / That lou'd your Father, the residue of your fortune, / Go to my Caue, and tell mee. Good old man, / Thou art right welcome, as thy masters is (010-204)

### [[oliver|Oliver]]'s Account (Act 4, Scene 3)

[[oliver|Oliver]], recounting his rescue by [[orlando|Orlando]] from the lioness, describes being led to the cave:

> Who led me instantly vnto his Caue, / There stript himselfe, and heere vpon his arme / The Lyonnesse had torne some flesh away (018-149)

### [[jaques|Jaques]]'s Farewell (Act 5, Scene 4)

As the community prepares for the wedding celebrations, [[jaques|Jaques]] alone elects to remain with the converts at the cave:

> Ile stay to know, at your abandon'd caue (022-142)
