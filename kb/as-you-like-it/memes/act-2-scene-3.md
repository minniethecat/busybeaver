(006-001)Enter [[orlando|Orlando]] and [[adam|Adam]].

(006-002)Orl. Who's there?

(006-003)Ad. What my yong Master, oh my gentle master,

(006-004)Oh my sweet master, O you memorie

(006-005)Of old Sir Rowland; why, what make you here?

(006-006)Why are you vertuous? Why do people [[love|loue]] you?

(006-007)And wherefore are you gentle, strong, and valiant?

(006-008)Why would you be so fond to ouercome

(006-009)The bonnie priser of the humorous Duke?

(006-010)Your praise is come too swiftly home before you.

(006-011)Know you not Master, to seeme kinde of men,

(006-012)Their graces serue them but as enemies,

(006-013)No more doe yours: your vertues gentle Master

(006-014)Are sanctified and holy traitors to you:

(006-015)Oh what a world is this, when what is comely

(006-016)Enuenoms him that beares it?

(006-017)Why, what's the matter?

(006-018)Ad. O vnhappie [[youth]],

(006-019)Come not within these doores: within this roofe

(006-020)The enemie of all your graces liues

(006-021)Your brother, no, no brother, yet the sonne

(006-022)(Yet not the son, I will not call him son)

(006-023)Of him I was about to call his Father,

(006-024)Hath heard your praises, and this night he meanes,

(006-025)To burne the lodging where you vse to lye,

(006-026)And you within it: if he faile of that

(006-027)He will haue other meanes to cut you off;

(006-028)I ouerheard him: and his practises:

(006-029)This is no place, this house is but a butcherie;

(006-030)Abhorre it, feare it, doe not enter it

(006-031)Ad. Why whether Adam would'st thou haue me go?

(006-032)Ad. No matter whether, so you come not here

(006-033)Orl. What, would'st thou haue me go & beg my food,

(006-034)Or with a base and boistrous Sword enforce

(006-035)A theeuish liuing on the common rode?

(006-036)This I must do, or know not what to do:

(006-037)Yet this I will not do, do how I can,

(006-038)I rather will subiect me to the malice

(006-039)Of a diuerted blood, and bloudie brother

(006-040)Ad. But do not so: I haue fiue hundred Crownes,

(006-041)The thriftie hire I saued vnder your Father,

(006-042)Which I did store to be my foster Nurse,

(006-043)When [[loyalty-and-service|seruice]] should in my old limbs lie lame,

(006-044)And vnregarded age in corners throwne,

(006-045)Take that, and he that doth the Rauens feede,

(006-046)Yea prouidently caters for the Sparrow,

(006-047)Be comfort to my age: here is the gold,

(006-048)All this I giue you, let me be your seruant,

(006-049)Though I looke old, yet I am strong and lustie;

(006-050)For in my youth I neuer did apply

(006-051)Hot, and rebellious liquors in my bloud,

(006-052)Nor did not with vnbashfull forehead woe,

(006-053)The meanes of weaknesse and debilitie,

(006-054)Therefore my age is as a lustie winter,

(006-055)Frostie, but kindely; let me goe with you,

(006-056)Ile doe the seruice of a yonger man

(006-057)In all your businesse and necessities

(006-058)Orl. Oh good old man, how well in thee appeares

(006-059)The constant seruice of the antique world,

(006-060)When seruice sweate for dutie, not for meede:

(006-061)Thou art not for the fashion of these times,

(006-062)Where none will sweate, but for promotion,

(006-063)And hauing that do choake their seruice vp,

(006-064)Euen with the hauing, it is not so with thee:

(006-065)But poore old man, thou prun'st a rotten tree,

(006-066)That cannot so much as a blossome yeelde,

(006-067)In lieu of all thy paines and husbandrie,

(006-068)But come thy waies, weele goe along together,

(006-069)And ere we haue thy youthfull wages spent,

(006-070)Weele light vpon some setled low content

(006-071)Ad. Master goe on, and I will follow thee

(006-072)To the last gaspe with truth and loyaltie,

(006-073)From seauentie yeeres, till now almost fourescore

(006-074)Here liued I, but now liue here no more

(006-075)At seauenteene yeeres, many their fortunes seeke

(006-076)But at fourescore, it is too late a weeke,

(006-077)Yet fortune cannot recompence me better

(006-078)Then to die well, and not my Masters debter.

(006-079)Exeunt.
