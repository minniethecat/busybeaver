# Patience

## Definition

Patience in As You Like It is the virtue of endurance — the capacity to bear suffering, waiting, and uncertainty without losing faith or composure. In the play's moral economy, patience is both a practical necessity (exiles must endure hardship, lovers must endure waiting) and a mark of character that distinguishes the virtuous from the merely fortunate. [[rosalind|Rosalind]]'s measured composure, [[adam|Adam]]'s long service, and [[duke-senior|Duke Senior]]'s philosophical acceptance of adversity all exemplify patience as an active moral achievement rather than passive suffering.

## In As You Like It

Patience is first invoked as a quality of the exiled [[rosalind|Rosalind]]. Her silence under [[duke-frederick|Duke Frederick]]'s unjust [[exile|banishment]] is described admiringly: "Her verie silence, and her patience, Speake to the people, and they pittie her" (003-065). Patience here is not weakness but a form of eloquence — her composed bearing wins the people's sympathy.

[[duke-senior|Duke Senior]] models patience as philosophical acceptance. Reframing [[freedom|exile]]'s hardships as moral instruction, he finds that [[forest-of-arden-place|the forest]]'s elements "feelingly perswade me what I am" (004-012). His patience is the virtue that transforms adversity into wisdom, making him the play's moral center.

[[rosalind|Rosalind]]'s patience with [[orlando|Orlando]]'s love [[verse-and-poetry|poetry]] — "O most gentle Iupiter, what tedious homilie of Loue haue you wearied your parishioners withall, and neuer cri'de, haue patience good people" (012-050) — is comic in tone but serious in substance. Her willingness to endure [[orlando|Orlando]]'s excesses while gently correcting them demonstrates patience as the discipline of love.

[[adam|Adam]] embodies patience as lifelong [[loyalty-and-service|fidelity]]. "I haue lost my teeth in your [[loyalty-and-service|seruice]]" (001-029), he tells [[orlando|Orlando]], his physical decay testifying to decades of patient [[loyalty-and-service|devotion]]. His offering of his life savings to the dispossessed [[orlando|Orlando]] is patience's final, most costly expression.
