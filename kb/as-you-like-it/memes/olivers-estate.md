# [[oliver|Oliver]]'s Estate

## Overview

Oliver's Estate is the de Boys family home, the setting for Act 1, Scene 1 of *As You Like It*. The estate — particularly its orchard — is where the [[brotherhood|fraternal conflict]] between [[oliver|Oliver]] and [[orlando|Orlando]] erupts into physical violence, setting in motion the chain of events that drives the play's [[persons|characters]] from the corrupt court world into [[forest-of-arden|the Forest]] of [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]]. The estate embodies the injustices of primogeniture: [[oliver|Oliver]] has inherited everything from their father Sir Rowland de Boys, while [[orlando|Orlando]] is kept "rustically at home" like an animal.

## Dramatic Function

The play opens here, with [[orlando|Orlando]] recounting to the old servant [[adam|Adam]] the injustices he has suffered since their master Sir Rowland's death. [[oliver|Oliver]], the eldest brother, has inherited the estate and denied [[orlando|Orlando]] both education and maintenance, keeping him "rustically at home" — stalled like an ox (001-002). The estate is not a home but a prison for [[orlando|Orlando]], a place where birthright and [[virtue-and-gentility|gentility]] are systematically denied.

The confrontation between the brothers takes place specifically in the orchard: when [[oliver|Oliver]] demands "Know you where you are sir?", [[orlando|Orlando]] answers "O sir, very well: heere in your Orchard" (001-018). The orchard — a space of cultivation and fruitfulness — becomes a site of violent fraternal strife, as [[orlando|Orlando]] seizes [[oliver|Oliver]] by the throat.

The estate is also where [[adam|Adam]], the loyal old servant of Sir Rowland, makes his last stand. After serving the de Boys family his entire life, he is dismissed by [[oliver|Oliver]] as "olde dogge" (001-028) and replies: "Is old dogge my reward: most true, I haue lost my teeth in your [[loyalty-and-service|seruice]]" (001-029). His flight with [[orlando|Orlando]] to [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]] begins from this place of betrayal.

## Key Passages

### [[orlando|Orlando]]'s Complaint (Act 1, Scene 1)

[[orlando|Orlando]] describes his condition on the estate to [[adam|Adam]]:

> he keepes me rustically at home, or (to speak more properly) staies me heere at home vnkept: for call you that keeping for a gentleman of my birth, that differs not from the stalling of an Oxe? (001-002)

### Confrontation in the Orchard (Act 1, Scene 1)

The brothers' conflict erupts in the orchard:

> Oli. Know you where you are sir? / Orl. O sir, very well: heere in your Orchard (001-018)

### [[adam|Adam]]'s Dismissal (Act 1, Scene 1)

[[oliver|Oliver]]'s treatment of the aged servant epitomizes the estate's moral corruption:

> Oli. Get you with him, you olde dogge. / Adam. Is old dogge my reward: most true, I haue lost my teeth in your seruice (001-028-001-029)
