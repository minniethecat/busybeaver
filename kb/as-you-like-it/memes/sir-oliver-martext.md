# Sir Oliver Martext

## Character Overview

Sir Oliver Martext is the vicar of the village adjacent to the [[forest-of-arden-place|Forest of Arden]]. [[touchstone|Touchstone]] summons him to perform an impromptu marriage to [[audrey|Audrey]] under a tree. His name -- "Martext" suggesting a mangled or defective text -- signals his inadequacy as a clergyman. "Sir" was a common Elizabethan title for clergymen without university degrees, marking Martext as a hedge-priest of dubious authority.

## The Interrupted Wedding

[[touchstone|Touchstone]] brings Sir Oliver Martext to marry him and [[audrey|Audrey]] in Act 3 Scene 3. The scene is pure farce: a fool seeks a sham marriage from a sham priest, with a country wench as bride. [[jaques|Jaques]] intervenes, urging Touchstone to seek a proper church wedding:

> **Jaques**: Get you to Church, and haue a good Priest / That can tell you what marriage is (013-054)

Sir Oliver Martext's incompetence and the irregularity of the ceremony underscore the play's concern with proper versus improper [[marriage|marriage]]. His presence provides the comic counterpoint to the play's final, legitimate multiple wedding.
