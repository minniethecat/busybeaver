# Rosalind and Orlando's First Meeting

## Summary

Following his victory over [[charles|Charles]] in the [[wrestling-match|wrestling match]], [[orlando|Orlando]] meets [[rosalind|Rosalind]] for the first time. She gives him a chain:

> **Rosalind**: Sir, you haue wrastled well, and ouerthrowne / More then your enemies. ... Weare this for me: one out of suites with fortune / That could giue more, but that her hand lacks meanes. (002-285 to 002-294)

Orlando is struck speechless with love:

> **Orlando**: What passion hangs these weights vpon my tongue? / I cannot speake to her, yet she vrg'd conference. / O poore Orlando! thou art ouerthrowne (002-304)

[[celia|Celia]] and [[rosalind|Rosalind]] both notice his lovestruck silence, and [[celia|Celia]] teases him gently. [[duke-frederick|Duke Frederick]]'s sudden hostility upon learning Orlando is [[sir-rowland-de-boys|Sir Rowland de Boys]]'s son darkens the moment, but the love between Orlando and Rosalind has already been kindled.

## Dramatic Significance

This is the play's romantic inciting event. Everything that follows -- [[rosalinds-banishment|Rosalind's banishment]], the flight to [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]], the Ganymede disguise, the love-cure game -- flows from this meeting. The chain Rosalind gives Orlando becomes a token that reappears in the forest scenes, linking court and pastoral worlds.
