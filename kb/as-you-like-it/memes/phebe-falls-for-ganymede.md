## Scene Context

Act 3, Scene 5 takes place in the [[forest-of-arden-place|Forest of Arden]]. [[silvius|Silvius]], the lovesick shepherd, has been pursuing the disdainful shepherdess [[phebe|Phoebe]] throughout the play. In this scene, [[rosalind|Rosalind]] — disguised as the [[youth]] [[ganimed|Ganymede]] — intervenes in their quarrel, with consequences that complicate the romantic plot further.

## The Rejection of Silvius

The scene opens with [[silvius|Silvius]] pleading: "Sweet Phoebe, do not scorn me, do not, Phoebe" (015-002). His language is earnest and conventional — the Petrarchan lover begging for his mistress's pity. Phoebe's response is merciless: she declares she would rather be "executed" than loved by him [015-008–010]. [[corin|Corin]] reminds her of [[silvius|Silvius]]'s [[loyalty-and-service|devotion]], but Phoebe remains unmoved.

## Rosalind/[[rosalind|Ganymede]]'s Intervention

[[rosalind|Rosalind]], watching from concealment with [[celia|Celia]], steps forward as [[ganimed|Ganymede]] and delivers a blistering rebuke to Phoebe. [[ganimed|Ganymede]] tells Phoebe she is no beauty — "You have no beauty" (015-035) — and that she should be grateful for any love offered to her. The irony is rich: [[rosalind|Rosalind]], herself in love with [[orlando|Orlando]], lectures on love while disguised as a boy.

## The Twist

Phoebe's response is the scene's comic reversal. Rather than being offended, she becomes instantly infatuated with [[ganimed|Ganymede]]:
> "Sweet youth, I pray you chide a year together. / I had rather hear you chide than this man woo" [015-066–067]

The shepherdess who scorned [[silvius|Silvius]]'s earnest [[loyalty-and-service|devotion]] now pursues a [[youth]] who openly insults her. Phoebe's infatuation with [[ganimed|Ganymede]] — unaware that "he" is [[rosalind|Rosalind]] in disguise — creates a love triangle (or quadrilateral) that drives the comic complications of Acts 4 and 5.

## Dramatic Significance

The scene exemplifies the play's treatment of love as both absurd and irresistible. Phoebe's sudden passion for [[ganimed|Ganymede]] mirrors the irrationality that [[touchstone|Touchstone]] and [[jaques|Jaques]] have mocked throughout — but unlike their detached commentary, Phoebe's experience is visceral and transformative. The scene also deepens the gender-performance comedy: [[rosalind|Rosalind]], disguised as [[ganimed|Ganymede]], must now manage being the object of another woman's desire while maintaining her own courtship with [[orlando|Orlando]] under the same male persona.
