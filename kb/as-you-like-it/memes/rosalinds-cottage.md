# [[rosalind|Rosalind]]'s Cottage

## Overview

Rosalind's Cottage is the sheep-cote and pasture purchased by [[rosalind|Rosalind]] and [[celia|Celia]] upon their arrival in [[forest-of-arden|the Forest]] of [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]]. Acquired from its previous owner through [[silvius|the shepherd]] [[corin|Corin]], the cottage becomes the exiled women's home and the operational base for [[rosalind|Rosalind]]'s disguised courtship of [[orlando|Orlando]]. As a piece of property bought and owned by women in a patriarchal world, the cottage represents both pastoral retreat and unexpected female agency.

## Dramatic Function

The cottage is acquired almost immediately upon the women's arrival in [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]]. When [[silvius|the shepherd]] [[corin|Corin]] mentions that his master's "Cottage, pasture, and the flocke" are for sale, [[rosalind|Rosalind]] seizes the opportunity: "Buy thou the Cottage, pasture, and the flocke, / And thou shalt haue to pay for it of vs" (007-081). [[celia|Celia]] echoes her approval: "I like this place, and willingly could / Waste my time in it" (007-084).

The purchase marks a significant shift in the women's fortunes. From fugitives dependent on disguise, they become property owners with a settled base. [[rosalinds-cottage|The cottage]]'s "removed" quality — [[orlando|Orlando]] notes [[rosalind|Rosalind]]'s accent is "something finer, then you could / purchase in so remoued a dwelling" (012-167) — underscores its distance from courtly life while paradoxically enabling [[rosalind|Rosalind]]'s most sophisticated performance: the love cure she conducts from this pastoral headquarters.

By Act 3, Scene 5, the cottage has become known property; [[silvius|Silvius]] tells [[phebe|Phebe]] that the [[youth]] [[ganimed|Ganymede]] "hath bought the Cottage and the bounds / That the old Carlot once was Master of" (015-113), confirming the transaction and establishing [[rosalind|Rosalind]]'s place in [[forest-of-arden-place|the forest]] economy.

## Key Passages

### The Purchase (Act 2, Scene 4)

[[rosalind|Rosalind]] authorizes [[corin|Corin]] to buy the property on her behalf:

> Buy thou the Cottage, pasture, and the flocke, / And thou shalt haue to pay for it of vs (007-081)

### A Removed Dwelling (Act 3, Scene 2)

[[orlando|Orlando]], meeting [[ganimed|Ganymede]] for the first time, remarks on the incongruity of refined speech in a rustic setting:

> Your accent is something finer, then you could / purchase in so remoued a dwelling (012-167)

### Confirmation of Sale (Act 3, Scene 5)

[[silvius|Silvius]] confirms the purchase to [[phebe|Phebe]]:

> And he hath bought the Cottage and the bounds / That the old Carlot once was Master of (015-113)
