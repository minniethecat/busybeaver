## Overview

Honor in As You Like It operates across multiple registers: the martial honor tested in the wrestling match, the familial honor violated by [[oliver|Oliver]]'s treatment of [[orlando|Orlando]] and [[duke-frederick|Duke Frederick]]'s usurpation, and the personal honor that [[persons|characters]] invoke in oaths and promises. [[celia|Celia]]'s pledge — "by mine honor I will" (002-006) — establishes honor as a currency of trust, while the wrestling match frames it as a public performance where reputation is staked on physical victory [^1].

## Martial Honor: The Wrestling Match

[[charles|Charles]] the wrestler fights "for my credit" (001-046), explicitly linking physical prowess to social standing. [[orlando|Orlando]]'s decision to challenge [[charles|Charles]] — despite being an untested [[youth]] — is an act of honor-reclamation: denied his gentleman's education, he seeks to prove his worth through bodily risk. [[duke-frederick|Duke Frederick]]'s response to learning [[orlando|Orlando]]'s parentage — "The world esteem'd thy father honourable" (002-123) — reveals honor as hereditary, a legacy that can elevate or condemn [^2].

## Violated Honor: Fraternal Betrayal

The play opens with honor in crisis. [[oliver|Oliver]]'s refusal to "breed mee well" (001-002) violates the testamentary honor-code binding brother to brother. [[orlando|Orlando]]'s complaint — "mines my [[virtue-and-gentility|gentility]] with my education" (001-002) — equates honor with [[virtue-and-gentility|gentility]], a birthright that proper upbringing should nurture. [[duke-senior|Duke Senior]]'s [[freedom|exile]] by his "yonger brother" (001-041) mirrors this fraternal violation at the ducal level, structuring the court/[[green-world|forest]] opposition as a consequence of dishonorable usurpation [^3].

## Honor and Oath

The women of the play invoke honor as a bond of truth. [[celia|Celia]]'s "by mine honor I will" (002-006) underwrites her promise to restore [[rosalind|Rosalind]]'s inheritance. [[rosalind|Rosalind]]'s qualification — "thou maist in honor come off againe" (002-007) — tempers comic play with a safety clause, suggesting that honor functions as a limit on behavior even within the licensed space of jest.
