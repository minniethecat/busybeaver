## Overview

Aliena is the name adopted by [[celia|Celia]], daughter of [[duke-frederick|Duke Frederick]], when she flees the court for [[forest-of-arden|the Forest]] of [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]] alongside her cousin [[rosalind|Rosalind]]. The name signals [[celia|Celia]]'s transformation from court lady to [[green-world|forest]] dweller — "Aliena" suggesting both her alienated status from court and her new identity in the pastoral world. She is accompanied by the court fool [[touchstone|Touchstone]], and her presence provides grounding and companionship for [[rosalind|Rosalind]]'s [[ganimed|Ganimed]] persona throughout [[forest-of-arden-place|the forest]] episodes.

## Role in the Play

Aliena functions as [[rosalind|Rosalind]]'s confidante and anchor in [[forest-of-arden-place|the forest]]. While [[rosalind|Rosalind]]-as-[[ganimed|Ganimed]] drives the romantic action, Aliena represents loyalty and steadfastness. Her own romantic arc — the whirlwind courtship with [[oliver|Oliver]], [[orlando|Orlando]]'s reformed brother — mirrors and contrasts with the prolonged game of courtship between [[ganimed|Ganimed]] and [[orlando|Orlando]]. Where [[rosalind|Rosalind]]'s love requires testing and education, [[celia|Aliena]]'s love erupts suddenly and decisively, leading to a swift engagement that helps precipitate the play's resolution in the quadruple wedding. Her disguise also contributes to the play's exploration of identity: she is no longer [[celia|Celia]], but Aliena, and as Aliena she finds love with a man who knows her only by that name.

> No longer Celia, but Aliena (003-119)

## Key Scenes

- **Act 2, Scene 4** — [[celia|Celia]] and [[rosalind|Rosalind]] arrive in [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]]; [[celia|Celia]] assumes the name Aliena. (003-119)
- **Act 4, Scene 3** — [[rosalind|Rosalind]] confides in Aliena about her love for [[orlando|Orlando]]. (016-093)
- **Act 5, Scene 2** — [[oliver|Oliver]] declares his love for Aliena and asks [[orlando|Orlando]]'s blessing; the couple's engagement is announced. (020-002) (020-006) (020-022)

## Relationships

- **[[celia|Celia]]** — Aliena is [[celia|Celia]]'s created identity, representing her rejection of [[duke-frederick|Duke Frederick]]'s court and her commitment to accompanying [[rosalind|Rosalind]] into [[freedom|exile]].
- **[[oliver|Oliver]]** — [[oliver|Oliver]] de Boys, [[orlando|Orlando]]'s elder brother, falls in love with Aliena at first sight. Their sudden romance is the play's most compressed love story — "on so little acquaintance" — yet it resolves [[oliver|Oliver]]'s villain-to-hero transformation and provides the counterweight that makes the four-wedding finale possible.
- **[[rosalind|Rosalind]] ([[ganimed|Ganimed]])** — Aliena is [[ganimed|Ganimed]]'s constant companion and confidante, the steady presence to whom [[rosalind|Rosalind]] can drop her performance and speak honestly.
- **[[touchstone|Touchstone]]** — The court fool accompanies [[celia|Celia]] to [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]] and remains associated with her throughout the play, providing comic commentary on the pastoral life Aliena has chosen.
