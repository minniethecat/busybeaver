# Dennis

## Character Overview

Dennis is a servant in [[oliver|Oliver]]'s household, summoned by name in Act 1 Scene 1. His appearance is brief -- Oliver calls "holla Dennis" and the stage direction reads "Enter Dennis" (001-031) -- but his presence serves a dramatic purpose. Where [[adam|Adam]] represents the old, loyal servant devoted to Sir Rowland's memory, Dennis represents the household staff who answer to the new master [[oliver|Oliver]].

## Dramatic Function

Dennis appears at the precise moment [[oliver|Oliver]]'s violent intentions crystallize. After [[orlando|Orlando]] leaves, Oliver summons Dennis as he plots to "physicke your ranckenesse" (001-031) -- to cure Orlando's defiance through Charles the wrestler's violence. Dennis's silent obedience contrasts with [[adam|Adam]]'s active loyalty: Dennis serves the current master, whatever his character; Adam serves the memory of the old master, whatever the risk.
