(021-001)Enter [[touchstone|Clowne]] and [[audrey|Audrey]].

(021-002)Clo. To morrow is the ioyfull day Audrey, to morow

(021-003)will we be married

(021-004)Aud. I do desire it with all my heart: and I hope it is

(021-005)no dishonest desire, to desire to be a woman of y world?

(021-006)Heere come two of the banish'd Dukes Pages.

(021-007)Enter two Pages.

(021-008)1. Pa. Wel met honest Gentleman

(021-009)Clo. By my troth well met: come, sit, sit, and a song

(021-010)2. Pa. We are for you, sit i'th middle

(021-011)1. Pa. Shal we clap into't roundly, without hauking, or spitting, or saying we are hoarse, which are the onely prologues to a bad voice

(021-012)2. Pa. I faith, y'faith, and both in a tune like two gipsies on a horse.

(021-013)Song.

(021-014)It was a Louer, and his lasse,

(021-015)With a hey, and a ho, and a hey nonino,

(021-016)That o're the greene corne feild did passe,

(021-017)In the spring time, the onely pretty rang time.

(021-018)When Birds do sing, hey ding a ding, ding.

(021-019)Sweet Louers [[love|loue]] the spring,

(021-020)And therefore take the present time.

(021-021)With a hey, & a ho, and a hey nonino,

(021-022)For loue is crowned with the prime.

(021-023)In spring time, &c.

(021-024)Betweene the acres of the Rie,

(021-025)With a hey, and a ho, & a hey nonino:

(021-026)These prettie Country folks would lie.

(021-027)In spring time, &c.

(021-028)This Carroll they began that houre,

(021-029)With a hey and a ho, & a hey nonino:

(021-030)How that a life was but a Flower,

(021-031)In spring time, &c

(021-032)Clo. Truly yong Gentlemen, though there was no

(021-033)great matter in the dittie, yet y note was very vntunable

(021-034)1. Pa. you are deceiu'd Sir, we kept time, we lost not

(021-035)our time

(021-036)Clo. By my troth yes: I count it but time lost to heare such a foolish song. God buy you, and God mend your voices. Come Audrie.

(021-037)Exeunt.
