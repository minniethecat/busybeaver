# Oliver's Plot Against Orlando

## Summary

After [[orlando|Orlando]] confronts [[oliver|Oliver]] about his mistreatment and denied inheritance, Oliver seizes an opportunity for murder. [[charles|Charles]] the Duke's wrestler comes to warn Oliver that Orlando plans to challenge him tomorrow. Oliver, instead of dissuading Charles, lies about Orlando's character:

> **Oliver**: Ile tell thee Charles, it is the stubbornest yong fellow of France, full of ambition, an enuious emulator of euery mans good parts, a secret and villanous contriuer against mee his naturall brother: therefore vse thy discretion, I had as liefe thou didst breake his necke as his finger (001-047)

Charles accepts the charge, and Oliver's plot is set. The scheme fails only because [[orlando|Orlando]] defeats [[charles|Charles]] in the [[wrestling-match|wrestling match]].

## Dramatic Significance

This event establishes the play's central fraternal conflict and reveals Oliver's true character. It also triggers the chain of events that drives the entire plot: Orlando's victory leads to [[rosalind|Rosalind]]'s love, [[duke-frederick|Duke Frederick]]'s hostility, Orlando's flight to [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]], and ultimately all the forest scenes.
