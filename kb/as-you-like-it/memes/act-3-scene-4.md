(014-001)Enter [[rosalind|Rosalind]] & [[celia|Celia]].

(014-002)Ros. Neuer talke to me, I wil weepe

(014-003)Cel. Do I prethee, but yet haue the grace to consider,

(014-004)that teares do not become a man

(014-005)Ros. But haue I not cause to weepe?

(014-006)Cel. As good cause as one would desire,

(014-007)Therefore weepe

(014-008)Ros. His very haire

(014-009)Is of the dissembling colour

(014-010)Cel. Something browner then Iudasses:

(014-011)Marrie his kisses are Iudasses owne children

(014-012)Ros. I'faith his haire is of a good colour

(014-013)Cel. An excellent colour:

(014-014)Your Chessenut was euer the onely colour:

(014-015)Ros. And his kissing is as ful of sanctitie,

(014-016)As the touch of holy bread

(014-017)Cel. Hee hath bought a paire of cast lips of Diana: a Nun of winters sisterhood kisses not more religiouslie, the very yce of chastity is in them

(014-018)Rosa. But why did hee sweare hee would come this

(014-019)morning, and comes not?

(014-020)Cel. Nay certainly there is no truth in him

(014-021)Ros. Doe you thinke so?

(014-022)Cel. Yes, I thinke he is not a picke purse, nor a horse-stealer,

(014-023)but for his verity in [[love|loue]], I doe thinke him as

(014-024)concaue as a couered goblet, or a Worme-eaten nut

(014-025)Ros. Not true in loue?

(014-026)Cel. Yes, when he is in, but I thinke he is not in

(014-027)Ros. You haue heard him sweare downright he was

(014-028)Cel. Was, is not is: besides, the oath of Louer is no stronger then the word of a Tapster, they are both the confirmer of false reckonings, he attends here in the forrest on the Duke your father

(014-029)Ros. I met the Duke yesterday, and had much question with him: he askt me of what parentage I was; I told him of as good as he, so he laugh'd and let mee goe. But what talke wee of Fathers, when there is such a man as [[orlando|Orlando]]? Cel. O that's a braue man, hee writes braue verses, speakes braue words, sweares braue oathes, and breakes them brauely, quite trauers athwart the heart of his louer, as a puisny Tilter, y spurs his horse but on one side, breakes his staffe like a noble goose; but all's braue that [[youth]] mounts, and folly guides: who comes heere? Enter [[corin|Corin]].

(014-030)[[corin|Corin]]. Mistresse and Master, you haue oft enquired

(014-031)After the Shepheard that complain'd of loue,

(014-032)Who you saw sitting by me on the Turph,

(014-033)Praising the proud disdainfull Shepherdesse

(014-034)That was his Mistresse

(014-035)Cel. Well: and what of him?

(014-036)Cor. If you will see a pageant truely plaid

(014-037)Betweene the pale complexion of true Loue,

(014-038)And the red glowe of scorne and prowd disdaine,

(014-039)Goe hence a little, and I shall conduct you

(014-040)If you will marke it

(014-041)Ros. O come, let vs remoue,

(014-042)The sight of Louers feedeth those in loue:

(014-043)Bring vs to this sight, and you shall say

(014-044)Ile proue a busie actor in their play.

(014-045)Exeunt.
