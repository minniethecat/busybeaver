# As You Like It

## Overview

*As You Like It* is one of Shakespeare's great pastoral comedies, following the intertwined stories of love, exile, and transformation in the [[forest-of-arden-place|Forest of Arden]]. The play contrasts the corrupt, hierarchical world of [[duke-fredericks-court|the court]] with the liberating, egalitarian space of the [[forest-of-arden|forest]], where characters shed their social identities and discover truer versions of themselves.

## Key Characters

- [[rosalind|Rosalind]] — The heroine, disguised as Ganymede in Arden
- [[orlando|Orlando]] — The hero, youngest son of Sir Rowland de Boys
- [[celia|Celia]] — Rosalind's loyal cousin, disguised as Aliena
- [[touchstone|Touchstone]] — The court fool who accompanies the women to Arden
- [[jaques|Jaques]] — The melancholy philosopher of Duke Senior's exiled court
- [[duke-senior|Duke Senior]] — The exiled rightful duke, living in Arden
- [[duke-frederick|Duke Frederick]] — The usurping duke who banishes Rosalind
- [[oliver|Oliver]] — Orlando's tyrannical elder brother
- [[phebe|Phebe]] — A shepherdess who comically falls for Ganymede
- [[silvius|Silvius]] — A shepherd hopelessly in love with Phebe
- [[audrey|Audrey]] — A country wench courted by Touchstone

## Major Themes

- [[love|Love]] — In all its varieties: romantic, fraternal, courtly, pastoral
- [[disguise|Disguise]] and [[gender-performance|Gender Performance]] — Rosalind's Ganymede persona
- [[green-world|The Green World]] — The forest as transformative space
- [[pastoralism|Pastoralism]] — The idealization of rural life
- [[reconciliation-concept|Reconciliation]] — The resolution of fraternal and political conflicts
- [[transformation|Transformation]] — Personal change through experience

## Structure

The play moves through three distinct movements:

1. **Court** (Act 1) — Conflict, tyranny, and the wrestling match
2. **Journey** (Act 2) — The flight to Arden, establishing the pastoral world
3. **Forest** (Acts 3–5) — The green world of love, disguise, and reconciliation

The resolution brings all characters together for a multiple wedding, [[duke-fredericks-conversion|Duke Frederick's conversion]], and the restoration of legitimate rule.
