(017-001)Enter [[jaques|Iaques]] and Lords, Forresters.

(017-002)Iaq. Which is he that killed the Deare?

(017-003)Lord. Sir, it was I

(017-004)Iaq. Let's present him to the Duke like a Romane Conquerour, and it would doe well to set the Deares [[cuckoldry|horns]] vpon his head, for a branch of victory; haue you no song Forrester for this purpose? Lord. Yes Sir

(017-005)Iaq. Sing it: 'tis no matter how it bee in tune, so it make noyse enough.

(017-006)Musicke, Song.

(017-007)What shall he haue that kild the Deare?

(017-008)His Leather skin, and hornes to weare:

(017-009)Then sing him home, the rest shall beare this burthen;

(017-010)Take thou no scorne to weare the horne,

(017-011)It was a crest ere thou wast borne,

(017-012)Thy fathers father wore it,

(017-013)And thy father bore it,

(017-014)The horne, the horne, the lusty horne,

(017-015)Is not a thing to laugh to scorne.

(017-016)Exeunt.
