# William

## Overview

William is a country [[youth]] who appears in a single scene of As You Like It (Act 5, Scene 1). He is [[audrey|[[audrey|Audrey]]'s]] rustic suitor, and his encounter with [[touchstone|Touchstone]] provides one of the play's most economical comic sketches. [[william|William]]'s role — though brief — illuminates [[touchstone|Touchstone]]'s character and the play's broader satire of rural life versus courtly [[wit]].

## Role in the Play

William appears in Act 5, Scene 1, where he encounters [[touchstone|Touchstone]] and [[audrey|Audrey]] in [[forest-of-arden|the Forest]] of [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]]. [[touchstone|Touchstone]], claiming superior [[wit]] and threatening violence, bullies William into renouncing his claim on [[audrey|Audrey]]. The scene is a miniature of social domination: the court fool, armed with [[wit|wordplay]] and bravado, effortlessly dismisses the tongue-tied country [[youth]].

[[william|William]]'s simple answers — "William, sir" (019-019) and "God rest you merry sir" (019-034) — are helpless against [[touchstone|Touchstone]]'s verbal assault. The scene parodies the romantic rivalry motif: instead of a duel of equals, we witness [[wit]] rout simplicity without contest.
