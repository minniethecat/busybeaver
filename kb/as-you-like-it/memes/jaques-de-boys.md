# Jaques de Boys

## Character Overview

Jaques de Boys is the middle brother of the de Boys family, mentioned early in the play and appearing only in its final scene. [[orlando|Orlando]] introduces him in Act 1: "My brother [[jaques-de-boys|Iaques]] he keepes at schoole, and report speakes goldenly of his profit" (001-002). He has been away at school throughout the action, untouched by the fraternal conflict between [[orlando|Orlando]] and [[oliver|Oliver]].

**Important**: Jaques de Boys is distinct from [[jaques|Jaques]] the melancholy courtier who attends [[duke-senior|Duke Senior]]. The shared first name is a source of potential confusion -- one is a de Boys brother, the other is a court philosopher.

## The Final Messenger

Jaques de Boys enters in Act 5 Scene 4 as "the second sonne of old Sir Rowland" (022-106) bearing the news that resolves the political plot. He reports that [[duke-frederick|Duke Frederick]], marching with an army to destroy [[duke-senior|Duke Senior]], met "an old religious man" at the edge of the forest and was converted:

> **Jaques de Boys**: Duke [[duke-frederick|Fredericke]] / hearing how that euery day men of great worth / Resorted to this Forrest, purpos'd to take / His brother heere, and put him to the sword... / meeting with an old religious man, / After some question with him, was conuerted / Both from his enterprize, and from the world (022-107 to 022-119)

His function is purely structural: he delivers the news that completes the play's resolution, restoring the dukedom to [[duke-senior|Duke Senior]] and ensuring the restoration of all de Boys lands to [[orlando|Orlando]].

## The Three Brothers Reconciled

Jaques de Boys's appearance completes the reunion of Sir Rowland's three sons. All three are present at the play's end: [[orlando|Orlando]] married to [[rosalind|Rosalind]], [[oliver|Oliver]] converted and married to [[celia|Celia]], and Jaques de Boys returned from school bearing the final piece of good news. The fracturing of the de Boys family that opened the play is fully healed.
