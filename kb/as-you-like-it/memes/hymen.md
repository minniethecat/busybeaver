# Hymen

## Overview

Hymen is the Roman god of marriage who appears in the final scene of As You Like It to sanctify the four weddings. His entrance — alongside [[rosalind|Rosalind]] (still in her wedding garments) — signals the transition from pastoral comedy to ritual celebration. [[hymen|Hymen]]'s presence elevates the multiple marriages from social contract to divine ordinance, linking the play's romantic resolution to cosmic order.

## Role in the Play

Hymen appears only in Act 5, Scene 4, but his function is essential: he transforms [[rosalind|Rosalind]]'s carefully orchestrated comic resolution into a sacred ceremony. He leads [[rosalind|Rosalind]] from her [[ganimed|Ganymede]] disguise into her revealed identity, and his blessing confers divine sanction on all four unions.

[[hymen|Hymen]]'s song — "[[marriage|Wedding]] is great Iunos crowne, O blessed bond of boord and bed" (022-094) — frames marriage as both a social and cosmic institution. His closing couplet, "Tis Hymen peoples euerie towne, high [[marriage|wedlock]] then be honored" (022-096), universalizes [[forest-of-arden|the Forest]] of [[forest-of-arden-place|Arden]]'s celebration to encompass all human communities.

## Key Passages

### The Entrance (Act 5, Scene 4)

Hymen enters with [[rosalind|Rosalind]] and [[celia|Celia]], introduced by stage direction:

> Enter Hymen, Rosalind, and Celia. (022-060)

### [[hymen|Hymen]]'s Blessing (Act 5, Scene 4)

> Then is there mirth in heauen, when earthly things made eauen: attone together, good Duke receiue thy daughter, Hymen from Heauen brought her, yea brought her hether (022-061)

### The Wedding Song (Act 5, Scene 4)

> Wedding is great Iunos crowne, O blessed bond of boord and bed: Tis Hymen peoples euerie towne, high wedlock then be honored (022-094)
