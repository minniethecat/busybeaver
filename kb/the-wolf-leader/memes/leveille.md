## Overview

L'Eveillé is a huntsman and attendant who appears in the later chapters of the narrative (Chapters 11–23). He is a named companion of [[engoulevent|Engoulevent]] [021-011] and serves as a practical, capable outdoorsman—managing horses, relays, and hounds during the hunt for the werewolf. His name, meaning "the awakened one" or "the lively one," suits his active role in the [[escape|pursuit]] scenes.

## Relationship with Engoulevent

L'Eveillé travels "in company with l'Eveillé and [[engoulevent|Engoulevent]]" during the search for [[thibault|Thibault]] [021-011]. The two huntsmen form a pair, with l'Eveillé often handling the practical logistics—horses, relays, hounds—while [[engoulevent|Engoulevent]] provides information and direction. Their partnership in Chapters 21–23 mirrors the [[hunt|hunting]] retinue structure established in Chapter 1.

## Role in the Hunt

L'Eveillé serves as a practical hunter and aide during the critical [[escape|pursuit]] of [[thibault|Thibault]] in the final chapters. When [[escape|the chase]] intensifies, he is called upon to estimate distances ("Ten leagues at least") [023-070] and to manage the hounds [023-073]. The Baron delegates relay duties to him: "You look after the relays, l'Eveillé; I want them ready whenever needed" [023-077].

His earlier appearances in the narrative are more incidental—he is confused with someone else in a comic episode [011-052], lends a horse [013-070], and is called upon during key moments [011-051, 011-056]. These scattered references establish him as a recognizable member of the [[hunt|hunting]] retinue before his more substantial role in [[the-werewolf-hunt-final|the climax]].

## Character

L'Eveillé is a secondary figure with no independent plot arc, but his practical competence and reliable presence support the [[hunt|hunting]]-world texture of the novel. He is the working huntsman—not ambitious like [[engoulevent|Engoulevent]], not brutal like [[marcotte|Marcotte]]—simply a man who knows his craft and does his job.
