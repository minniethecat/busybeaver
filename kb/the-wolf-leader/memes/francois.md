# François

## Character Overview

François (full name Auguste François Levasseur) is the valet to [[baron-of-vez|Raoul de Vauparfond]], the Lord of Vauparfond. He encounters [[thibault|Thibault]] at the Dauphin d'Or restaurant in [[ferte-milon|Ferté-Milon]], where the two share a meal and François unwittingly provides the shoemaker with valuable information about the Duke of Orleans's hunting party and the movements of the aristocracy through the region.

## Role in the Story

Thibault spots François descending the hill of [[ferte-milon|Ferté-Milon]] and recognises him immediately as "Auguste François Levasseur, valet to Raoul the Lord of Vauparfond" (015-003). Thibault calls out to him — "Halloa! François!" (015-004) — and invites him to dine:

> "Friend François, come here; I invite you to dine" (015-006).

François accepts, seating himself at Thibault's table (015-011). Over dinner, Thibault questions him, and François reveals that he is on sentry duty at the Château:

> "And now, friend François, you are going to tell me a few things I am anxious to know about" (015-012).

François, "leaning back in his chair with the most superb air of contempt" (015-013), explains his duties. He and another servant are dressed in long grey coats, a sort of household uniform:

> "He was dressed, like François, in a long grey coat" (015-031).

François discloses that the [[duke-of-orleans|Duke of Orleans]] and [[madame-de-montesson|Madame de Montesson]] are hunting nearby (015-026), news that Thibault receives with great interest despite his outward composure.

## Key Relationships

- **[[baron-of-vez|Raoul de Vauparfond]]** — François's master; Thibault later inhabits Raoul's body, making François unwittingly his own servant
- **[[thibault|Thibault]]** — The shoemaker who extracts information from François over dinner at the Dauphin d'Or
- **[[duke-of-orleans|The Duke of Orleans]]** — The supreme authority François indirectly serves as part of the ducal household's extended staff

## Key Appearances

- **Chapter 15**: François's principal scene. He meets [[thibault|Thibault]] at the Dauphin d'Or in [[ferte-milon|Ferté-Milon]], shares a meal, and reveals key information about the hunting party and household movements while Thibault plies him with food and drink.

## Quotations

François laughs off Thibault's self-importance with the easy disdain of a servant who knows his place within a great household:

> "I am not surprised at that," replied François, laughing at the shoemaker's presumptuousness (015-013).

His cheerful reply when asked about the approach of another grey-coat:

> "That's all right," said François cheerfully (015-034).
