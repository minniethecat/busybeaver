## Location Overview

Fleury is a small village situated between Dampleux and [[longpont|Longpont]] in the forested region of the Aisne [004-013]. It lies along one of the routes that [[thibault|Thibault]] traverses, both as a man and as a wolf. The village marks a waypoint in the geography of the novel's [[hunt|hunting]] landscape, connecting the settled areas with the deeper forest.

## Description in the Text

Dumas mentions Fleury in the context of [[thibault|Thibault]]'s movements through the landscape. The [[hunt|hunting]] party passes near it:

> [004-013] "the plain of Meutard, leaving Fleury and Dampleux to the left"

[[thibault|Thibault]], pursued and desperate, hopes to reach it:

> [016-011] "from the village of Fleury, and he galloped on hoping that some miracle"

The forest path [[thibault|Thibault]] knows well passes through this area:

> [020-001] "a part which he knew between Fleury and Longpont, where the trees were especially tall and dense"

## Key Events

1. **[[hunt|The Hunt]]ing Party Route** (Chapter 4): The Baron's [[hunt|hunting]] party crosses the plain of Meutard, lea[[wine|vin]]g Fleury and Dampleux to the left as they traverse the countryside [004-013].

2. **[[thibault|Thibault]]'s Flight** (Chapter 16): In a desperate moment, [[thibault|Thibault]] gallops toward the village of Fleury, hoping for a miracle to save him from his pursuers [016-011].

3. **The Wolf's Path** (Chapter 20): [[thibault|Thibault]] selects the forest path between Fleury and [[longpont|Longpont]] because of the tall, dense trees that provide cover for a wolf's movements [020-001].

## Significance

Fleury functions as a waypoint in the novel's geography — a place on the edge of the deep forest that represents safety for the human [[thibault|Thibault]] but is bypassed by the wolf [[thibault|Thibault]]. The fact that he "gallops hoping some miracle" will save him as he approaches Fleury [016-011] underscores the village's symbolic role as a potential refuge that remains just out of reach.

## Real-World Counterpart

Fleury is a real commune in the Aisne département of northern France, located approximately 10 km south of [[villers-cotterets|Villers-Cotterêts]]. It lies near [[longpont|Longpont]], consistent with the novel's geographical references. Like many of the place names in *The Wolf-Leader*, Dumas anchored his fictional narrative in the real topography of his native region.
