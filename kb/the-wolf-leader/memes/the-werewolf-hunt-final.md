# The Werewolf Hunt (Final)

## Overview

The final hunt is the novel's climax — the conclusion of the arc that began with the opening [[hunt|wolf-hunt]] and passed through the flogging and [[the-devils-pact|the pact]]. The Baron's hounds, trained to hunt wolves, now pursue a werewolf, and the distinction between animal and human, natural and supernatural, dissolves in the violence of [[escape|the chase]].

## Narrative Significance

The final hunt is structured as an inversion of [[the-wolf-hunt-opening|the opening hunt]]. In Chapter 1, the Baron hunts ordinary wolves and punishes a human poacher; in Chapters 23–24, he hunts a man who has become a wolf and [[the-flogging|the punishment]] is not judicial but cosmic. The symmetry is exact and devastating: [[thibault|Thibault]], who poached the Baron's deer, has himself become the ultimate quarry.

The hunt's violence is described with unflinching precision. The hounds catch the werewolf's scent (023-076), the struggle begins "in very earnest" (024-003), and the werewolf's skin is torn apart — only to be revealed as [[thibault|Thibault]]'s own (024-014). The physical [[death|destruction]] of the werewolf's body is also the exposure of [[thibault|Thibault]]'s true nature: the wolf-skin was not a disguise but the man himself, transformed.

## Key Moments

- The hounds catch the scent: "at once on to the scent of the werewolf, and began to give cry" (023-076)
- The struggle: "the struggle between the werewolf and the dogs" begins "in very earnest" (024-003)
- The revelation: the skin they have been [[hunt|hunting]] "was the skin of the werewolf" — and of [[thibault|Thibault]] (024-014)

## Aftermath

The hunt's conclusion is both cathartic and troubling. The werewolf is destroyed, the community is safe, and the cosmic order is restored — but [[thibault|Thibault]], the man who might have chosen differently, is beyond [[repentance|redemption]]. The novel ends not with moral satisfaction but with the somber recognition that some choices cannot be undone.
