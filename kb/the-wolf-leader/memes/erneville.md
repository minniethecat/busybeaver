## Location Overview

Erneville is a village located approximately one mile from the [[castle-of-vez|Castle of Vez]] [010-024]. It is the home of the [[magloire|Bailiff Magloire]] and his wife Suzanne, and serves as the setting for the wedding feast of [[thibault|Thibault]] and [[agnette|Agnelette]]. The village sits along the road between [[vez|Vez]] and [[villers-cotterets|Villers-Cotterêts]] [017-030], placing it within the forested region of the Aisne that Dumas knew intimately from his own childhood.

## Description in the Text

Dumas situates Erneville precisely within the geography of the novel:

> [010-024] "arrived at the village of Erneville, which is situated about a mile from the Castle of Vez"

The village has a church with a prominent clock that marks significant moments in the narrative [011-051]. The Bailiff's house is described as an "imposing" dwelling, large enough to host a wedding feast with "at least twenty guests" [011-001]. The road connecting Erneville to [[villers-cotterets|Villers-Cotterêts]] features multiple times as a route where characters travel or encounter each other [017-030][017-032].

## Key Events

1. **The Wedding Feast** (Chapters 10–11): [[thibault|Thibault]] and [[agnette|Agnelette]]'s wedding celebration takes place at the Bailiff's house in Erneville. The event draws guests from "all the villages of the district" [010-024]. The feast is marked by heavy [[wine|drinking]] and ends ominously.

2. **The Transformation** (Chapter 11): At midnight, [[village-church|the church]] clock of Erneville strikes as [[thibault|Thibault]] leaves the Bailiff's house, and the fumes of wine begin to oppress him. This marks the onset of his first wolf transformation [011-051].

3. **The Baron's Encounter** (Chapter 13): The [[baron-of-vez|Baron of Vez]] passes through Erneville on his way back from dining with his friend de Vivières, catching sight of someone on the road [013-019].

4. **The Wolf-Hunt Aftermath** (Chapter 24): [[thibault|Thibault]], in wolf form, leaves Erneville to his left as he leaps into a stream to escape pursuing hounds [024-002].

## Significance

Erneville functions as a symbolic threshold in the novel. It is the site of [[thibault|Thibault]]'s wedding — his last moment of human happiness and social integration — and simultaneously the place where his transformation into a werewolf begins. The church clock striking midnight carries Gothic overtones, marking the boundary between the human and the monstrous. The village thus represents the fragile border between civilization and the wild forest that surrounds it.

## Real-World Counterpart

Dumas situates Erneville within the real geography of the Aisne département, between the historical locations of [[villers-cotterets|Villers-Cotterêts]] and [[vez|Vez]]. While "Erneville" may be a fictionalized or renamed settlement, its placement on the road network connecting these real towns suggests Dumas drew on his intimate knowledge of the region. The name may derive from "Erneville-aux-Bois" or a similar hamlet in the area.
