# Thibault's Flogging

## Overview

The flogging of [[thibault|Thibault]] is the novel's inciting incident — the event that transforms the sabot-maker's generalized resentment into focused hatred and opens him to the [[devil|black wolf]]'s temptation. Though the punishment is legally justified ([[thibault|Thibault]] did poach the deer), its severity and public nature make it the emotional crux of the entire narrative.

## Narrative Significance

The flogging scene is structured as a judicial [[custom|ritual]]. The Baron examines the evidence (the boar-spear), pronounces sentence (thirty-six lashes, apportioned between [[oath|perjury]] and poaching), and delegates en[[violence|force]]ment to [[marcotte|Marcotte]]. Dumas notes the religious framing: "God's portion must be the largest" (003-003) — a grim parody of tithing applied to corporal punishment.

The punishment's effects radiate through the entire novel. [[thibault|Thibault]]'s physical pain heals; his psychological wound festers. The hatred that drives his wishes, the revenge that consumes his soul, the werewolf he becomes — all trace back to this clearing in the forest, this tree, these thirty-six (or thirty-seven) [[violence|blow]]s.

[[agnette|Agnelette]]'s intervention interrupts but does not undo the punishment. She saves [[thibault|Thibault]] from death but cannot save him from the hatred that the flogging plants in him. Her [[mercy|compassion]] and the Baron's cruelty are juxtaposed in the same scene, [[sacrifice|offering]] [[thibault|Thibault]] two possible responses to his [[shame|humiliation]] — and he chooses the darker path.

## Key Moments

- The Baron's sentencing: "a dozen for poaching and two dozen for perjuring himself" (003-003)
- [[thibault|Thibault]]'s futile oaths of innocence (003-005)
- [[agnette|Agnelette]]'s intervention: "In the name of the God of mercy, my Lord, have [[mercy|pity]] on that man!" (003-009)
- [[marcotte|Marcotte]]'s extra [[violence|blow]] — the thirteenth stroke after the cease order (003-031)

## Aftermath

Immediately after the flogging, [[thibault|Thibault]]'s thoughts turn to hatred and [[revenge|vengeance]] (003-056). That night, the [[devil|black wolf]] appears in his hut to offer [[the-devils-pact|the pact]] that will grant his vengeful wishes — and extract their terrible price.
