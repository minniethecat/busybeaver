## Definition

In *The Wolf-Leader*, gold and precious metals function as visible markers of the class hierarchy that defines Thibault's world. Gold is what the nobility possesses and the peasant lacks — the material embodiment of social distance. Unlike "fortune" (the abstract concept of luck), gold is tangible, wearable, exchangeable. It appears in epaulettes, buckles, rings, and coins, always indexing the wearer's position in the social order. The novel's treatment of gold reflects the material culture of the *ancien régime*, where sumptuary distinctions were legally enforced and visibly legible.

## Role in the Novel

Gold marks every social boundary Thibault cannot cross. The king's forest-guard appears "with red vest and gold epaulettes" (001-040), gold literally gilding the representatives of authority. The exchange of rings — "I have a silver ring, I have a gold one; let us exchange" (005-021) — dramatizes the material gradient of social status through jewelry. Even the wolf's supernatural ring is "made neither of gold nor of silver" (P02-330), positioning it outside the normal economy of precious metals and therefore outside human law — a fitting property for a diabolical object.

The goldsmith Monsieur Dugué, mentioned as the craftsman who "will alter it for us" (006-034), represents the legitimate path to converting material into status — a path Thibault, in his impatience, bypasses in favor of supernatural shortcuts. Gold thus operates as both temptation and accusation: the visible wealth that Thibault desires and the visible proof that he does not belong among those who possess it.

## Key Examples

> "of the king with red vest and gold epaulettes" (001-040)

The king's servant wears gold; Thibault wears sabot-maker's cloth. The visual economy of the novel encodes power in metal.

> "I have a silver ring, I have a gold one; let us exchange; the baron changed rings with his wife" (005-021)

The ring exchange between the Baron and his wife, overheard by Thibault, condenses the social world he is excluded from — gold exchanged between equals while he watches from outside.
