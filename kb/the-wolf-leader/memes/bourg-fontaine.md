## Location Overview

Bourg-Fontaine is a settlement and district situated at the low grounds where the forest gives way to cultivated land [001-030]. It is connected to the [[baisemont|Baisemont pond]]s by a brook [001-031] and features in [[thibault|Thibault]]'s route through the [[hunt|hunting]] grounds. The town itself is substantial enough that [[thibault|Thibault]] goes there specifically to look at his reflection [006-049], suggesting it has shops or public spaces with mirrors.

## Description in the Text

Dumas situates Bourg-Fontaine within the hydrological and topographical landscape of the forest:

> [001-030] "neared the low grounds of Bourg-fontaine"

The brook that joins the [[baisemont|Baisemont]] and Bourg ponds is a key landscape feature:

> [001-031] "joins the ponds of Baisemont and Bourg, then to walk against stream"

The district is part of the [[hunt|hunting]] range:

> [004-014] "Back again in the district of Bourg-Fontaine, he ranged the country"

[[thibault|Thibault]]'s [[wish|desire]] to see his reflection brings him here:

> [006-049] "he would go into the town of Bourg-Fontaine and look at himself in a glass"

The low-lying nature of the land is noted again late in the novel:

> [023-078] "his tongue in the low lands about Bourg-Fontaine"

## Key Events

1. **[[hunt|The Hunt]]ing Circuit** (Chapter 1): The narrative establishes Bourg-Fontaine as part of the landscape that [[the-wolf-hunt-opening|the Baron's hunt]]ing party traverses, with the brook connecting the ponds of [[baisemont|Baisemont]] and Bourg [001-031].

2. **Ranging the Country** (Chapter 4): After a wolf chase, the narrative notes that the hunt returns to "the district of Bourg-Fontaine" [004-014], indicating it as a central hub in the [[hunt|hunting]] geography.

3. **[[thibault|Thibault]]'s Mirror** (Chapter 6): Following his physical transformation under the influence of the wolf-pelt, [[thibault|Thibault]] goes into Bourg-Fontaine specifically to look at his reflection [006-049]. The town thus serves as a place where the transformed protagonist confronts his new appearance.

4. **The Wolf's Tongue** (Chapter 23): The low lands about Bourg-Fontaine are mentioned in connection with a wolf's tongue [023-078], part of the bestiary imagery that pervades the late chapters.

## Significance

Bourg-Fontaine represents the interface between the wild forest and human civilization. [[thibault|Thibault]] comes here to see his own reflection — a moment of self-confrontation that is central to the novel's theme of transformation and identity. The town's position at the "low grounds" suggests a liminal space between the elevated forest and the settled valleys, mirroring [[thibault|Thibault]]'s own position between the human and animal worlds.
