## Overview

Engoulevent serves as valet and second huntsman to the [[baron-of-vez|Seigneur Jean]], [[baron-of-vez|Baron of Vez]] [001-008]. His ambition to become a "pricker" (a mounted hunt servant) drives much of his scheming. A small-statured but cunning figure, he acts as a [[faith|foi]]l to [[thibault|Thibault]]—spying on the sabot-maker, reporting his infractions, and ultimately marrying [[agnette|Agnelette]] after [[thibault|Thibault]]'s downfall.

## Role and Ambition

Engoulevent aspired to become a pricker, a position of some status in the [[hunt|hunting]] hierarchy [001-008]. His eagerness to advance himself is evident in his servile manner toward his superiors: he approaches "cap in hand" when reporting to the Baron [001-024–25], and adopts "an expression of humility" when addressing lords [001-026]. This ambition makes him a ready tool for the Seigneur's purposes.

## The Flogging of Thibault

Engoulevent plays a central role in the incident that triggers [[thibault|Thibault]]'s pact with the dark [[violence|force]]s. He reports [[thibault|Thibault]]'s poaching of a goat to the Baron, boasting that with a goat carcass "our dear [[thibault|Thibault]]" will finally be caught [005-046–50]. He also tells his fellow huntsmen about the "little bone" in a goat's spine that, when broken, produces a crack like a pistol shot—a detail used to frame [[thibault|Thibault]] [005-050–52].

After [[thibault|Thibault]] is sentenced to be flogged, Engoulevent hands the Baron the weapon to carry out [[the-flogging|the punishment]] [003-001]. The narrator explicitly identifies him as "[[thibault|Thibault]]'s evil genius" [002-116], the instigator who transforms the Baron's potential leniency into harsh [[revenge|vengeance]] [002-114].

## As a Huntsman

Engoulevent appears alongside the other huntsmen throughout the [[hunt|wolf-hunt]]ing episodes of the narrative. He participates in the search for the [[devil|black wolf]] [005-044], and is later described as "the second huntsman" who "avenges" what he perceives as [[thibault|Thibault]]'s offenses against the [[hunt|hunting]] order [014-013, 014-021].

## Marriage to Agnelette

In the closing chapters, Engoulevent marries [[agnette|Agnelette]], [[thibault|Thibault]]'s former love. The marriage is portrayed as unequal: [[agnette|Agnelette]] "knew that Engoulevent would not give in" and had "made no more secret of her repugnance" toward him [022-012–14]. Engoulevent nevertheless proves himself a practical and devoted husband; he travels with [[leveille|l'Eveillé]] seeking news of [[thibault|Thibault]], whose wolf-pack threatens the countryside [021-011]. He warns [[agnette|Agnelette]] of the danger, explaining that "[[thibault|Thibault]] and his wolves had been seen and killed" near various locations [022-004].

## Appearance and Character

Engoulevent is repeatedly described as "little Engoulevent" [001-024, 005-044], suggesting small physical stature that contrasts with his ambitious nature. His behavior mixes servility toward [[power|authority]] with cruelty toward those beneath him—particularly [[thibault|Thibault]], whom he persecutes with singular fixation.
