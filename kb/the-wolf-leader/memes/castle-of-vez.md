# Castle of Vez

## Overview

The Castle of Vez is the seat of the Baron's [[power|authority]] and the architectural embodiment of the *ancien régime* social order. Perched above the surrounding forest, it is both a physical fortress against wolves and a symbolic fortress against the chaos that [[thibault|Thibault]]'s wishes unleash.

## Role in the Novel

The Castle appears most prominently in three contexts. First, as the Baron's base of operations — the place from which [[hunt|hunting]] parties depart and to which they return with their trophies. Second, as a symbol of exclusion: [[thibault|Thibault]] watches its "arched windows... lit up for some festivity" from outside, his face pressed against the glass of a world he cannot enter (003-094). Third, as a promised refuge: the Baron offers [[agnette|Agnelette]] safety within its walls, telling her that "no werewolf, or any other kind of wolf, has ever crossed the moat" (003-050).

The Castle's moat is specifically invoked as a barrier against supernatural evil — a detail that merges the practical function of medieval fortification with the folkloric [[faith|belief]] that running water stops witches and werewolves. That the Castle offers genuine protection distinguishes it from the forest, where all boundaries dissolve.

## Significance

The Castle represents order — hierarchical, unjust, but stable — against the forest's chaos. Its safety is real but conditional: it protects those inside but excludes those outside, and its walls are maintained by the same social system that produces [[thibault|Thibault]]'s resentment. The Castle is both a refuge and a provocation.
