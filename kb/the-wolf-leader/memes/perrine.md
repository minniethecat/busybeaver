## Overview

Perrine (diminutive: Perrinette) is the cook and servant of the Magloire household, appearing in the domestic scenes of Chapters 10 through 13. She is an obliging, competent servant, tasked with preparing meals [010-017, 011-002] and attending to guests. Her warm relationship with the [[magloire|Bailiff Magloire]], who calls her "my love," suggests a [[black-wolf|familiar]], [[love|affection]]ate domestic atmosphere in the Magloire home.

## Domestic Role

Perrine manages the kitchen and serves at table. The Bailiff gives her detailed instructions about the menu for a special meal [011-002], and she comes in to receive her orders [011-009]. When the household hosts guests, "savoury dishes that Mademoiselle Perrine was preparing" are noted as the centerpiece of the hospitality [012-029].

She also handles the practical duties of running the household—showing guests to their rooms [012-051, 013-061], relaying messages between family members [011-017–18], and managing the cellar (fetching out a bottle of old wine) [010-017].

## Relationship with the Magloires

The Bailiff addresses Perrine as "Perrinette, my love" [011-011], indicating an easy, [[love|affection]]ate relationship between master and servant. Her interaction with [[madame-suzanne|Madame Suzanne]] is similarly respectful but efficient—she relays her mistress's delays to the waiting guests [011-027] and conveys the Bailiff's wishes back to Madame [011-017–18].

## Role in the Narrative

Perrine is a minor character who exists entirely within the domestic sphere of the Magloire household. Her presence helps establish the warmth and normality of village life that forms the contrasting backdrop to [[thibault|Thibault]]'s supernatural transformation. The meals she prepares, the rooms she shows to guests, and the messages she carries all contribute to the texture of quotidian existence that Dumas uses to ground the novel's fantastic elements.
