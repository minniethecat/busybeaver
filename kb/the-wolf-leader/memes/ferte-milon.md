# Ferté-Milon

## Location Overview

Ferté-Milon is a town in the Aisne department, situated within the regional geography of the novel near [[villers-cotterets|Villers-Cotterêts]]. It is best known in the narrative as the location of the Dauphin d'Or, an excellent restaurant where [[thibault|Thibault]] shares a pivotal meal with the valet [[francois|François]], extracting information about the Duke of Orleans's hunting party. It also serves as a source of medical expertise when [[agnette|Agnelette]] falls fatally ill.

## Description in the Text

Thibault chooses Ferté-Milon as his destination specifically for its celebrated restaurant, whose proprietor's credentials connect it to the ducal household:

> "Thibault took the road to Ferté-Milon, where there was an excellent restaurant called the Dauphin d'Or, kept by a man who had formerly been cook for his Highness, the Duke of Orleans" (014-047).

The approach to the town is described geographically:

> "As he came down the hill of Ferté-Milon" (015-011).

During Agnelette's terminal illness, the town is listed alongside [[villers-cotterets|Villers-Cotterêts]] as a source of medical aid:

> "The doctors from Villers-Cotterets and Ferté-Milon, Agnelette became worse" (022-015).

## Key Events

1. **The Dinner at the Dauphin d'Or** (Chapter 15): [[thibault|Thibault]] encounters [[francois|François]], valet to [[baron-of-vez|Raoul de Vauparfond]], as François descends the hill of Ferté-Milon. Thibault invites him to dine at the Dauphin d'Or, where he extracts information about the [[duke-of-orleans|Duke of Orleans]]'s hunting party and the movements of the aristocracy (015-003 through 015-036).

2. **The Journey from Oigny** (Chapter 14): [[thibault|Thibault]] sets out from [[oigny|Oigny]] with the intention of reaching Ferté-Milon specifically for its restaurant, setting the stage for his encounter with François (014-047).

3. **Agnelette's Illness** (Chapter 22): During [[agnette|Agnelette]]'s final decline, doctors are summoned from both [[villers-cotterets|Villers-Cotterêts]] and Ferté-Milon, indicating the town's status as a regional centre with medical resources (022-015).

## Significance

Ferté-Milon functions as a node in the novel's geography of information exchange. The Dauphin d'Or, with its connection to the Duke's former cook, is a place where the worlds of the aristocracy and the peasantry briefly intersect — [[thibault|Thibault]], the shoemaker-poacher, gains intelligence about the Duke's household from [[francois|François]], the ducal valet, over a meal prepared by the Duke's former chef. The town thus serves as a conduit through which knowledge flows between social strata.

## Real-World Counterpart

La Ferté-Milon is a real commune in the Aisne department of northern France, approximately 20 kilometres from Villers-Cotterêts. It is known historically as the birthplace of Jean Racine (1639). In Dumas's era, as today, it was a small town within the forested region of the Valois — a plausible setting for the kind of provincial restaurant and medical services the novel attributes to it.
