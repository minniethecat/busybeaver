# Préciamont

## Overview

Préciamont is a small peasant hamlet on the edge of the [[forest|Forest]] of [[villers-cotterets|Villers-Cotterêts]], approximately three miles from the Osier-beds where [[thibault|Thibault]] lives. It is [[agnette|Agnelette]]'s home and represents the world of rural poverty, hard work, and family [[love|devotion]] that stands in contrast to both aristocratic luxury and supernatural ambition.

## Role in the Novel

Préciamont appears primarily through [[agnette|Agnelette]]'s references to it. She walks three miles from the village to gather grass for her grandmother's goats (003-038), a journey that takes her through the forest she fears. The distance is significant — close enough for daily travel, far enough to make the journey a burden.

The village is described only through implication: a church with a steeple visible from the forest's edge (003-108), a community of poor peasants who share the hardships of rural life, and a social fabric that [[agnette|Agnelette]]'s [[love|devotion]] to her grandmother exemplifies. Préciamont is not idealized — its poverty is real — but it is presented as morally intact in a way that neither the Castle nor the forest can claim.

## Significance

Préciamont embodies the world [[thibault|Thibault]] rejects in favor of supernatural ambition. It offers community, love, and honest labor — everything the [[devil|black wolf]]'s wishes cannot provide. [[agnette|Agnelette]]'s three-mile walk with a load of heath on her head is the novel's most powerful image of what [[thibault|Thibault]] throws away.
