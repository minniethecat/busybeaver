## Location Overview

Baisemont is a wooded area near the forest where [[thibault|Thibault the sabot-maker]] lives and hunts. It is characterized by a coppice and a pond fed by a crystal-clear stream, and is connected hydrologically to the low grounds of [[bourg-fontaine|Bourg-Fontaine]] [001-031]. The area lies within the forest belt surrounding [[villers-cotterets|Villers-Cotterêts]] that Dumas knew from his youth.

## Description in the Text

Dumas describes Baisemont in the context of the hunt, painting it as part of a wild landscape of ponds and streams:

> [001-031] "the brook which joins the ponds of Baisemont and Bourg"

The coppice of Baisemont serves as cover for wolves:

> [004-068] "the wolf plunged into the Baisemont coppice"

The sound of hounds echoes across the landscape:

> [005-032] "from the direction of Baisemont, he again heard the baying of hounds"

The water source that feeds the ponds is noted for its purity:

> [006-049] "clear as crystal, which fed the pond near Baisemont, and those at Bourg"

## Key Events

1. **The Initial Hunt** (Chapter 1): The ponds of Baisemont are mentioned as part of the landscape surrounding [[thibault|Thibault]]'s hut when the [[hunt|hunting]] party of the [[baron-of-vez|Baron of Vez]] passes through [001-031].

2. **The Wolf Chase** (Chapter 4): A wolf plunges into the Baisemont coppice during a hunt, with hounds pursuing it through the dense undergrowth [004-068].

3. **Baying of Hounds** (Chapter 5): [[thibault|Thibault]] hears the distant baying of hounds coming from the direction of Baisemont, indicating ongoing [[hunt|hunting]] activity in the region [005-032].

4. **[[thibault|Thibault]]'s Reflection** (Chapter 6): The crystal-clear stream that feeds the Baisemont pond is noted as [[thibault|Thibault]] plans to visit [[bourg-fontaine|Bourg-Fontaine]] to see his own reflection after his physical transformation [006-049].

## Significance

Baisemont represents the wild forest environment that is [[thibault|Thibault]]'s natural domain as a sabot-maker and hunter. Unlike the cultivated spaces of castles and villages, Baisemont is a place of instinct and [[escape|pursuit]] — the terrain where both wolves and [[hunt|wolf-hunt]]ers move. The coppice provides cover, the ponds sustain life, and the sound of hounds across the landscape signals the ongoing tension between the human and animal worlds that the novel explores.
