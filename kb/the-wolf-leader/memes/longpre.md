## Overview

Longpré is a village in the Aisne department of northern France, consistently mentioned in tandem with [[haramont|Haramont]] as a paired reference point in the novel's rural geography [P02-017, 011-001, 021-016]. It is one of several real locations Dumas uses to anchor his fantastic tale in verifiable geography.

## Geographic Context

Longpré is situated between [[berval|Berval]] and [[haramont|Haramont]]: travelers follow the road "which runs between [[berval|Berval]] and Longpré" [001-002], passing [[vez|the lordly dwelling of Vez]] on the left. The convergence of roads "leading to Longpré and [[haramont|Haramont]]" [011-001] marks the approach to the Magloire household. This network of villages—[[haramont|Haramont]], Longpré, Bonneuil, [[berval|Berval]]—constitutes the novel's densely realized rural world.

## In the Narrative

Longpré appears most significantly in Chapter 21, where the wolf-pack's depredations are described geographically: while [[thibault|Thibault]] and his men "were busy near [[haramont|Haramont]] or Longpré, the people of Corcy and Vertfeuille" enjoyed a temporary respite [021-016]. The village thus participates in the novel's mapping of danger and safety across the rural landscape.

The preface mentions a "shepherd of Longpré" [P02-062] in connection with [[mocquet|Mocquet]]'s tales, further integrating the village into the oral [[custom|tradition]] that feeds the novel's supernatural elements.
