## Character Overview

Lisette is the waiting-maid to [[countess|the Countess]] of [[mont-gobert|Mont-Gobert]], and serves as [[countess|the Countess]]'s confidante and go-between in her illicit liaison with [[thibault|Thibault]]. Described as "such a pretty girl" [017-024], she is herself the object of [[escape|pursuit]] by [[engoulevent|Engoulevent]], the jealous servant whose nocturnal prowling complicates [[countess|the Countess]]'s affairs. Lisette occupies the classic literary role of the resourceful lady's maid — loyal, discreet, and indispensable to her mistress's schemes.

## Role in the Story

Lisette first appears when [[thibault|Thibault]] arrives at [[countess|the Countess]]'s residence. She is instrumental in arranging the secret encounters: she waits for [[thibault|Thibault]] at a hut on the road [020-010], leads him through the château's passages, and brings him to [[countess|the Countess]]'s room. When the Count arrives unexpectedly, it is Lisette who "rushed in full of alarm" [017-052] to warn the lovers, and she physically guides the Baron ([[thibault|Thibault]], now elevated) to safety — "Lisette! Lisette! take the Baron through your room" [017-062].

In the tragic climax (Chapters 19–20), Lisette leads [[thibault|Thibault]] to [[countess|the Countess]]'s room a final time. "Lisette, without the slightest effort" [020-018] navigates the château, and when they reach the room she "shut the door behind him and disappeared" [020-030], lea[[wine|vin]]g [[thibault|Thibault]] to discover [[countess|the Countess]]'s body.

Afterward, [[thibault|Thibault]] "found Lisette on her knees, praying" [020-039], and she tells him the full story of [[countess|the Countess]]'s death: how [[countess|the Countess]] took poison, and how the Count died at the hand of the Baron ([[thibault|Thibault]]) with "the dagger given him by [[countess|the Countess]]" [020-043].

## Key Relationships

- **The [[countess|Countess of Mont-Gobert]]** — Lisette's mistress and confidante. Their relationship is intimate and trusting: [[countess|the Countess]] confides the details of her lethal plan to Lisette, saying "Undress me, Lisette" [020-052] and "I am going to take a sleeping-draught" [020-056]. Lisette's response — "What, nothing but that!" [020-057] — shows her awareness of the gravity of what her mistress is doing.

- **[[thibault|Thibault]] (the Baron)** — Lisette serves as the intermediary between [[thibault|Thibault]] and [[countess|the Countess]]. She guides him through the château, facilitates their meetings, and ultimately reveals to him [[countess|the Countess]]'s fate.

- **[[engoulevent|Engoulevent]]** — A fellow servant who is "jealous with regard to Lisette" [020-065] and has been "prowling about at night" near [[countess|the Countess]]'s residence. His jealousy creates a persistent danger for the secret meetings.

## Character Analysis

Lisette embodies the loyal servant archetype common in Gothic and romantic fiction. She is discreet, competent, and wholly devoted to her mistress — even when [[countess|the Countess]] is pursuing an adulterous affair and ultimately planning suicide. Her unquestioning service reflects the feudal relationship between nobility and their domestic servants: she does not judge, only facilitates.

Yet Lisette is not merely passive. She takes initiative in moments of crisis — rushing to warn the lovers of the Count's arrival, physically guiding the escape, and praying alone after the tragedy. The narrator mentions her beauty ("Lisette is such a pretty girl!" [017-024]) and her desirability ([[engoulevent|Engoulevent]]'s jealousy), but these attributes serve the plot rather than define her character independently.
