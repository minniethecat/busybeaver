# Hunger

## Definition

Hunger (*faim*) is the primal drive for consumption — the body's demand for sustenance. In *The Wolf-Leader*, hunger undergoes a qualitative transformation as [[thibault|Thibault]] changes from man to beast: it shifts from ordinary appetite to "ferocious hunger" (004-001), a supernatural craving that bypasses the social rituals of eating and demands raw, immediate satisfaction. Hunger is the body's first declaration of its new nature — before the physical transformation is complete, hunger announces what [[thibault|Thibault]] is becoming.

## Role in the Novel

Hunger appears as a marker of [[thibault|Thibault]]'s progressive dehumanization. After his first encounter with the [[black-wolf|black wolf]], "the ferocious hunger which now consumed him made him look more like a wolf than a man" (004-001). The hunger is not for ordinary food but for what wolves eat — raw flesh, blood. In his werewolf state, "when hunger seized him and he was forced to satisfy it" (009-007), the hunger becomes a compulsion that overrides human restraint. The contrast is sharp: at the wedding feast, hunger and thirst are communal, shared — "for such was their hunger and thirst, that half the carp and the whole of the other dishes disappeared" (011-030) — but after transformation, hunger isolates.

## Key Examples

> "The ferocious hunger which now consumed him made him look more like a wolf than a man" (004-001) — hunger as the first visible sign of transformation.

> "When hunger seized him and he was forced to satisfy it" (009-007) — hunger as compulsion, not choice.

> "Such was their hunger and thirst, that half the carp disappeared" (011-030) — the communal feast as counterpoint to solitary, feral hunger.

> "Fill your stomach with jealousy, and my stomach with hunger, when, with the aid of a good appetite, one digests one's wrongs" (014-046) — [[thibault|Thibault]]'s bitter reflection on hunger and emotional consumption.
