# Community

Community (*communauté*) is the social fabric of village life — the network of mutual obligation, shared custom, and collective identity that defines the pre-modern rural world. In *The Wolf-Leader*, community functions as both a protection and a constraint: it sustains its members but also surveils and judges them.

## Community as Moral Order

The village in *The Wolf-Leader* is not merely a collection of households but a moral community. It has its own norms, enforced not by law but by gossip, reputation, and the threat of exclusion. When Thibault begins to change — becoming wealthier, more arrogant, less present at communal gatherings — the community notices and judges. This judgment is not necessarily unjust: Thibault is becoming dangerous, and the community's suspicion is a form of collective self-defense.

## The Breakdown of Community

Thibault's tragedy is partly the story of a man's progressive estrangement from community. Each wish granted by the [[devil|devil]] pushes him further from the village world: his new wealth isolates him from neighbors who share poverty; his new power makes him indifferent to the reciprocal obligations that structure communal life; his transformation into a wolf is the literal culmination of his social transformation into something no longer human — no longer a member of the community.

## The Wedding as Communal Ritual

The wedding of [[agnette|Agnelette]] to [[engoulevent|Engoulevent]] is the novel's most concentrated depiction of community. The entire village gathers; roles are assigned by custom; the feast is a collective celebration. Thibault's exclusion from this event — he is not invited, and his revenge against it is directed at the principle of communal joy itself — marks the point at which his alienation becomes irreversible.

## Community and the Hunt

The [[hunt|wolf hunt]] also functions as a communal ritual. The village unites against a common enemy, and participation affirms membership. Thibault begins the novel as a marginal participant in this community of hunters — he is present but not central — and ends as its quarry. The hunt that opens the novel is a defense of community; the hunt that closes it is the community's defense against what Thibault has become.
