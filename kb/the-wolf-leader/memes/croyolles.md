## Location Overview

Croyolles is a settlement near the forest, most notable for its mill — "charmingly situated at the bottom of a valley" [007-064]. The mill is owned by a young widow, a woman between twenty-six and twenty-eight years old [006-004], who becomes the object of [[thibault|Thibault]]'s romantic [[escape|pursuit]]. The shortest route to Croyolles goes round by the rear of [[oigny|Oigny]] [006-008], and a road leads from Croyolles to [[preciamont|Préciamont]] via the [[ham|rear of Ham]] [013-130].

## Description in the Text

Dumas describes the mill with genuine [[love|affection]]:

> [007-064] "The Mill at Croyolles is charmingly situated at the bottom of a valley"

The widow who owns it is portrayed as an attractive figure, li[[wine|vin]]g independently:

> [006-004] "a young widow, owner of the mill at Croyolles, a woman between twenty-six and twenty-eight years old"

The location is recognizable from a distance:

> [013-129] "he saw the mill of Croyolles in the distance"

The approach route is clearly defined:

> [006-008] "The shortest way to Croyolles was round by the rear of Oigny"

## Key Events

1. **[[thibault|Thibault]]'s Decision to Visit** (Chapter 6): After his physical transformation gives him newfound confidence, [[thibault|Thibault]] decides to go to Croyolles to pursue the young widow [006-005]. He starts off determined to try his luck with her [006-007].

2. **The Journey** (Chapter 6): [[thibault|Thibault]] takes the shortest route past [[oigny|Oigny]], but pauses to consider whether to continue his walk to Croyolles [006-049], reflecting his ambivalence between his old life and his new powers.

3. **The Valley Route** (Chapter 7): [[thibault|Thibault]] continues along the valley in the direction of Croyolles [007-005] but is diverted by other events.

4. **The Widow's Complaint** (Chapter 13): The owner of the mill at Croyolles makes a formal complaint about damage to her property — her "wizard's garden" has been affected [013-127]. This complaint becomes part of the legal proceedings around the wolf attacks.

5. **[[the-mill|The Mill]]-Wheel Sound** (Chapter 12): [[madame-suzanne|Madame Suzanne]] hears the "click-clack of the mill-wheel at Croyolles" [012-056], a sensory detail that places the mill within earshot of [[erneville|Erneville]].

6. **The Roundabout Route** (Chapter 22): A character plans to "go round by Croyolles and Value instead of crossing the forest" [022-011], indicating Croyolles as a safe alternative to the dangerous forest path.

## Significance

Croyolles represents the world of ordinary human commerce and [[wish|desire]] that exists alongside the supernatural events of the novel. The mill — a place of work, grain, and daily life — contrasts with the wild forest where [[thibault|Thibault]] undergoes his wolf transformations. The young widow who owns it represents a possible future for [[thibault|Thibault]]: a settled, prosperous life. That he ultimately cannot reach Croyolles (he is repeatedly diverted) mirrors his inability to escape his wolf-nature.
