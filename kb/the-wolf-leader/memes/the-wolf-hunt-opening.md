# The Wolf Hunt (Opening)

## Overview

The novel opens *in medias res* with the [[baron-of-vez|Baron of Vez]] leading a [[hunt|wolf-hunt]] through the [[forest|Forest]] of [[villers-cotterets|Villers-Cotterêts]]. This opening scene establishes the novel's social world — its hierarchies, its [[custom|ritual]]s, its distribution of power — and sets in motion the events that will drive [[thibault|Thibault]] into the [[devil|black wolf]]'s pact.

## Narrative Significance

The hunt introduces every major element of the novel's world: the Baron's [[power|authority]], the forest's danger, the peasants' subordinate position, and the violence that underpins the social order. When [[thibault|Thibault]] is discovered poaching, the hunt transforms from aristocratic sport into judicial proceeding — the Baron becomes judge, [[marcotte|the pricker]] becomes executioner, and the forest clearing becomes a courtroom.

[[thibault|Thibault]]'s punishment — thirty-six lashes administered by [[marcotte|Marcotte]] — is both brutal and legal. The Baron is not acting outside his rights; he is enforcing the game laws with the full [[power|authority]] of the seigneurial system. This makes [[thibault|Thibault]]'s subsequent rage comprehensible without making it sympathetic: the system IS unjust, but [[thibault|Thibault]]'s response to in[[law|justice]] will prove more destructive than the in[[law|justice]] itself.
