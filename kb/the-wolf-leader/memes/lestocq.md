## Overview

Lestocq (J. B. Lestocq) serves as Head Keeper to the Comte de Mont-Gobert [020-004]. He is a loyal huntsman who accompanies his master during the violent episodes of Chapters 17–20, and his death becomes a notable but emotionally unremarked event in [[thibault|Thibault]]'s increasingly detached consciousness.

## Service to the Count

Lestocq is introduced alongside his master in a moment of confrontation: the Count arrives "in person, and his huntsman Lestocq, with him" [017-055]. As Head Keeper, Lestocq holds the senior position in the Count's [[hunt|hunting]] establishment—analogous to [[marcotte|Marcotte]]'s role for the [[baron-of-vez|Baron of Vez]], but with less individual characterization.

## The Confrontation and Death

When the Count is wounded in his encounter with Raoul/[[thibault|Thibault]], Lestocq is called to his aid: the Count, "on one knee, cried 'Help, Lestocq!'" [017-071]. Lestocq rushes to assist his master [017-072], but the violent chaos that follows proves fatal for the keeper as well.

Lestocq's death is recorded with chilling brevity: "Lestocq's death was not difficult to verify" [020-008]. [[thibault|Thibault]]'s reaction—or lack thereof—is significant: "He felt neither satisfaction nor [[conscience|remorse]], at Lestocq's death; all he thought was..." [020-009]. This emotional numbness marks the degree to which [[thibault|Thibault]]'s humanity has eroded under the weight of his supernatural transformation.

## Aftermath

The keeper's death leaves the Count without immediate support. The [[baron-of-vez|Baron of Vez]] later recalls Lestocq's final moments: "on one knee, crying: 'help, Lestocq!' Then the Baron remembered..." [019-065]. This echo suggests that Lestocq's death—a loyal servant falling with his master—resonates in the memory of the [[hunt|hunting]] nobility, even if [[thibault|Thibault]] himself has grown indifferent to such losses.
