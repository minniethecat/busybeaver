## Location Overview

Berval is an area in the valley that runs between Berval and [[longpre|Longpré]] [001-002], known primarily for its ponds. These ponds, along with those of Poudron, were drawn for fishing — an event that drew crowds from surrounding villages. Berval lies within the forested [[hunt|hunting]] grounds that [[thibault|Thibault]] knows intimately, part of the landscape where he moves between his [[wood|sabot-making]] hut and the wider world.

## Description in the Text

Dumas places Berval within the picturesque valley landscape at the novel's opening:

> [001-002] "the beautiful valley which runs between Berval and Longpré"

The ponds are noted as a site of communal activity:

> [010-002] "The magnificent ponds of Berval and Poudron were to be drawn"

The drawing of the ponds is a significant event that provides context for the wedding feast:

> [017-035] "They had been drawing the Berval ponds"

## Key Events

1. **The Valley Description** (Chapter 1): The narrator establishes the beautiful valley between Berval and [[longpre|Longpré]] as the setting of the novel, situating [[thibault|Thibault]]'s hut within this landscape [001-002].

2. **The Drawing of the Ponds** (Chapter 10): The magnificent ponds of Berval and Poudron are drawn, an event that attracts people from surrounding villages and serves as backdrop to the wedding festivities [010-002].

3. **The Investigation** (Chapter 17): During the inquiry into the wolf attacks, it is mentioned that the Berval ponds had been drawn, establishing the timeline of events [017-035].

## Significance

Berval serves as a geographical anchor in the novel, defining the valley landscape alongside [[longpre|Longpré]]. The drawing of the ponds represents the communal, agricultural rhythms of village life — a world of seasonal work and collective celebration that stands in contrast to the solitary, supernatural existence of the werewolf. The ponds are a place where the community gathers, where news is exchanged, and where the normal order of rural life asserts itself against the disruption caused by the wolf attacks.
