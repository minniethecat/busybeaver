## Overview

George Gissing (1857–1903) was an English novelist and the actual author of *The Private Papers of Henry Ryecroft*. In the work, he appears as "G.G." — the anonymous editor of Ryecroft's posthumous papers who provides the biographical preface and arranges the seasonal structure of the volume.

## Biography

Gissing was born in Wakefield, Yorkshire, and became one of the major English novelists of the late Victorian period. By the time he wrote *The Private Papers of Henry Ryecroft* (published 1903), Gissing had already produced numerous novels including *New Grub Street*, *The Odd Women*, and *The Nether World*. He died in December 1903, the same year this work was published.

Like his fictional creation Ryecroft, Gissing had firsthand experience of the struggles of a literary life — poverty, disappointed ambition, and ill health were themes common to both men's experiences.

## Role in the Narrative

Gissing adopts the persona of "G.G.," the friend and literary executor who discovers Ryecroft's manuscript journals after his death. In the preface, Gissing explains that he first met Ryecroft when the latter was forty, and over "several years of friendship" came to understand the man beneath the stoic exterior. It is Gissing who makes the editorial decisions — selecting passages, dividing them by season, and presenting them as *The Private Papers of Henry Ryecroft*.

The preface voice of "G.G." serves as a framing device that allows Gissing to write autobiographical reflections through the mask of a fictional memoirist.

## Key Passages

> To me, however, fell the duty of examining Ryecroft's papers; and having, in the exercise of my discretion, decided to print this little volume, I feel that it requires a word or two of biographical complement.

> I hit upon the thought of dividing the little book into four chapters, named after the seasons. Like all classifications, it is imperfect, but 'twill serve. — G. G.
