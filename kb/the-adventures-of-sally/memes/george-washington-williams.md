# George Washington Williams

## Identity

George Washington Williams is described as a "talented coloured gentleman" recruited from small-time vaudeville by Mr. Abrahams to perform at the Flower Garden. (016-097)

## Performance Act

Williams is a trap-drummer. His nightly routine begins with a period of drumming, followed by a comic tour of the tables on the edge of the dancing-floor, where he whimsically pretends to clip the hair of male patrons using a pair of drumsticks held like scissors. (016-097)

> George Washington Williams was the talented coloured gentleman who had been extracted from small-time vaudeville by Mr. Abrahams to do a nightly speciality at the Flower Garden. He was, in fact, a trap-drummer: and it was his amiable practice, after he had done a few minutes trap-drumming, to rise from his seat and make a circular tour of the tables on the edge of the dancing-floor, whimsically pretending to clip the locks of the male patrons with a pair of drumsticks held scissor-wise.
>
> (016-097)

## In the Narrative

Williams appears in Chapter 16, when he inadvertently interrupts a tender moment between Mr. Carmyle and Sally Nicholas at the Flower Garden, leaning over Carmyle and taking "quite unpardonable liberties with his back hair." (016-097)
