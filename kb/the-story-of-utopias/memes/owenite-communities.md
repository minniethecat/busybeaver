# Owenite Communities

The **Owenite Communities** were the network of cooperative settlements inspired by [[robert-owen|Robert Owen]]'s vision of industrial reform. In *[[the-story-of-utopias|The Story of Utopias]]*, [[lewis-mumford|Lewis Mumford]] treats them as a significant chapter in the history of the "industrial village" — practical attempts to realize [[eutopia|eutopian]] principles within the framework of the Industrial Revolution, and important precursors to the more successful [[garden-city-organization|Garden City movement]].

## Role in Mumford's Narrative

[[lewis-mumford|Mumford]] places the [[robert-owen|Owen]]ite communities within the broader tradition of [[utopia-of-reconstruction|reconstructive utopia]]nism that attempted to bridge the gap between industrial production and humane community life. They represent the practical dimension of nineteenth-century utopian socialism — not merely theoretical projections but actual experiments in living.

> "The industrial village, which Owen and Fourier and their followers attempted to create, was a genuine effort to provide the material basis for the good life; and if their communities failed, it was not because the end they sought was wrong but because the means they employed were inadequate" (011-033).

## Historical Significance

| Aspect | [[robert-owen|Owen]]'s Contribution | Limitation |
|--------|-------------------|------------|
| Vision | Model industrial community with humane conditions | Vision was incomplete — lacked the full [[good-life|good life]] framework |
| Method | Intentional community as strategy for transformation | Communities were isolated — lacked the regional scope that [[lewis-mumford|Mumford]] later advocated |
| Legacy | Demonstrated that the [[idolum]] could be embodied in actual institutions | Failed to scale — proved that [[utopia-of-reconstruction|reconstruction]] requires more than good intentions |

## Comparison with Later Movements

The [[robert-owen|Owen]]ite communities stand in instructive contrast to the [[garden-city-organization|Garden City Association]] founded by [[ebenezer-howard|Ebenezer Howard]]. Where [[robert-owen|Owen]]'s communities attempted to withdraw from industrial society to create islands of cooperation, [[ebenezer-howard|Howard]]'s Garden Cities sought to transform the pattern of urban development itself. This difference — between retreat and reconstruction — mirrors [[lewis-mumford|Mumford]]'s fundamental distinction between the [[utopia-of-escape|utopia of escape]] and the [[utopia-of-reconstruction|utopia of reconstruction]].

Their ultimate failure does not, in [[lewis-mumford|Mumford]]'s view, invalidate their contribution. They remain part of the accumulating eutopian tradition that runs from [[plato|Plato]]'s *[[republic-plato|Republic]]* through [[sir-thomas-more|More]]'s *[[utopia-more|Utopia]]* to the practical achievements of the twentieth century.
