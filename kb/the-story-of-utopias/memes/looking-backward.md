## Introduction

*Looking Backward: 2000–1887* is a utopian novel by the American writer [[edward-bellamy]], published in 1888. The novel tells the story of [[julius-west|Julian West]], a Bostonian who falls into a hypnotic sleep in 1887 and wakes up in the year 2000 to find a radically transformed America — one organized around a nationalized "industrial army" in which every citizen serves from age 21 to 45. Upon publication, the book became an international bestseller and inspired a political movement (the "Nationalist" movement) that advocated for the nationalization of industry (008-022).

[[lewis-mumford|Mumford]] treats Bellamy as the most influential American utopian of the nineteenth century, though his assessment is sharply critical: Bellamy perfected the "utopia of reconstruction" — a form that addresses the machinery of society without reimagining its ends (008-037).

## Summary of the Work

*Looking Backward* is structured as a romance framed by a preface dated "Historical Section [[bernard-shaw|Shaw]]mut College, Boston, December 26, 2000." The narrator, [[julius-west|Julian West]], is a wealthy young man from 1887 who suffers from insomnia and uses hypnosis to sleep. When his house burns down and his hypnotist dies, [[julius-west|West]] is presumed dead — until he is discovered and revived 113 years later by Dr. Leete, a physician of the new era (008-022).

Bellamy's utopian America is organized on the model of an army. Every able-bodied citizen serves a term of industrial service from age 21 to 45. "Now the general of this industrial army is the president of the United States. He is chosen from among the corps commanders; and it is provided that every officer in the army, from the president down to the sergeant, must work his way up from the grade of common laborer" (008-032). This system, Bellamy argues, eliminates class conflict, unemployment, and the economic motives for crime:

> The great part of *Looking Backward* is a discussion of this perfected form of industrial organization; the manner in which it is worked; and the effects of complete economic equality in doing away with the necessity for the greater part of the legal machinery of the present day, since crimes with an economic motive would almost, according to Bellamy, be unthinkable. (008-034)

The novel describes a transformed daily life: goods are ordered from central warehouses by sample, families dine in communal restaurant rooms served by young conscripts, and citizens travel internationally through a system of nationalized credit (008-035). Retired citizens over 45 enjoy a country-club existence of travel and hobbies.

## Mumford's Assessment

[[lewis-mumford|Mumford]]'s treatment of *Looking Backward* is extensive but predominantly critical. He identifies Bellamy as the culmination of a nineteenth-century tradition that reduced utopianism to machinery:

> As we pass from Hertzka to Bellamy these facts glare insistently at us. The slight air of tedium that I have not been able to disguise in dealing with these utopias arises, I believe, from our excessive familiarity with their contents. Our nineteenth century utopias, if we except those of Fourier and Spence and a few more distinguished ones which we shall presently come to, do not dream of a renovated world: they keep on adding inventions to the present one. (007-037)

[[lewis-mumford|Mumford]]'s central critique is that Bellamy confused means with ends. The industrial army and central planning are "the material bases of the [[good-life|good life]]" — but Bellamy never adequately defines the [[good-life|good life]] itself. "Are these things, as [[aristotle|Aristotle]] would have said, the material bases of the [[good-life|good life]], or are they substitutes for the [[good-life|good life]]?" (008-037). [[lewis-mumford|Mumford]] identifies a "breach between Bellamy's conception of the [[good-life|good life]] and the structure he erected to shelter it" (008-039).

At the same time, [[lewis-mumford|Mumford]] acknowledges what remains valuable:

> What remains in *Looking Backward* is the honest passion that inspired the man; the play of generous impulses; the insistence that there is no fun for an ordinarily imaginative person in dining with Dives whilst Lazarus hangs around the table. Bellamy wanted everyone to be equally educated, so that everyone might be his companion; he wanted everyone to be decently fed and sheltered; he wanted to take his share in the dirty work and to see that accidents of wealth did not keep other people from taking theirs. (008-039)

[[lewis-mumford|Mumford]] also notes Bellamy's prescience: "if Bellamy did not portray a better future he at any rate, like Mr. [[h-g-wells|H. G. Wells]], in his early romances, outlined many ports of a future that has for us, in the twentieth century, become an actuality" (008-023).

## Place in the Utopian Tradition

*Looking Backward* occupies a pivotal position in the utopian tradition as the most commercially successful utopian novel of the nineteenth century. It synthesized earlier traditions — Cabet's *[[voyage-en-icarie|Voyage en Icarie]]* in France, the socialist critiques of industrial capitalism — and presented them in an accessible narrative form (008-001). Bellamy moved utopian writing from philosophical dialogue or traveler's tale into the modern science-fiction framework.

[[lewis-mumford|Mumford]] places Bellamy in the "utopia of reconstruction" lineage alongside [[james-buckingham|James Buckingham]] and Theodor Hertzka, contrasting their emphasis on machinery and organization with the [[utopia-of-escape|utopias of escape]] and the organic utopianism of [[patrick-geddes]] and [[ebenezer-howard]]. The novel's impact is measured by its reach: it directly inspired the Nationalist political movement in America and influenced a generation of reformers.

The chief limitation [[lewis-mumford|Mumford]] finds in *Looking Backward* — its "externalism" and impersonality — became the starting point for the next wave of utopian writing, particularly H.G. Wells's *[[a-modern-utopia]]*, which attempted to balance technological organization with individual freedom (008-036).
