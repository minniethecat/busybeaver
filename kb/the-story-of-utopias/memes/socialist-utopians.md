Before Marx and Engels systematized "scientific socialism," a generation of utopian socialists — Fourier, Saint-Simon, Owen, and their followers — imagined cooperative communities, phalansteries, and planned economies. Their ideas shaped labor movements, intentional communities, and the broader socialist tradition.
