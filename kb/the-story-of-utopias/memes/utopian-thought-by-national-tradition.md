# Utopian Thought by National Tradition

Utopian thinking is shaped by the political culture, economic conditions, and intellectual climate of each nation. French utopianism gave the world Fourier and Saint-Simon; British utopianism produced More and the Fabians; American utopianism spawned hundreds of experimental communities. This page maps the geography of utopian thought as it appears in Mumford's survey.

## About This Comparative View

National traditions are not sealed containers — Fourier influenced American communitarians; British Fabians read French sociology. The groupings above highlight the primary national affiliation of each entry as tagged, but many figures and movements operated across borders.
