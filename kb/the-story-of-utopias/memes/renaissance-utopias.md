The Renaissance revival of classical learning gave birth to the modern utopian tradition. Thomas More's *Utopia* (1516) coined the term and established the genre; Campanella's *City of the Sun* and Bacon's *New Atlantis* followed, each projecting Renaissance humanism and scientific optimism onto imaginary societies.
