# British Reform and Utopian Tradition

Britain gave utopian thought its modern name ([[sir-thomas-more|Thomas More]]'s *Utopia*, 1516) and its most sustained engagement with industrial reality. From Owen's model mills to the Fabian Society's gradualist socialism, British reformers consistently tested utopian ideas against the stubborn facts of class, poverty, and power. Mumford returns repeatedly to this tradition as a counterweight to abstract system-building.
