## Overview

**Compulsory service** — military conscription — is a fault line in the political world of *William—An Englishman*. Before the war, [[william-tully|William Tully]] "would all but foam at the mouth when compulsory service in the army was the subject of conversation" [002-003], yet he had no objection to compulsory trade union membership. This selective pacifism is one of Hamilton's sharpest satirical points: the reformist's principles bend to suit his tribal affiliations.

The trajectory of compulsory service in the novel traces the arc from pre-war debate to wartime reality. What begins as a topic of Labour meeting rhetoric becomes, with the Military Service Act of 1916, a force that claims [[faraday|Faraday]]'s life and transforms William's understanding of his own political commitments.

## In the Novel

Compulsory service appears in the novel primarily as a concept of debate, not as a lived experience until the Conscription Act brings it home. William's pre-war position is defined by contradiction: "'militarism,' to him, was the blackest of all the works of the devil; but he was bitter, and violently bitter, against the blackleg who objected to compulsory service in a Trade Union, and had spoken, times without number, in hearty encouragement of that form of siege warfare which is commonly known as a Strike" [002-003].

Hamilton's narrator observes that William was "a pacifist of the type which seeks peace and ensues it by insisting firmly, and even to blood, that it is the other side's duty to give way" [002-003]. His opposition to compulsory military service is sincere but intellectually inconsistent — he opposes state coercion in one domain while enthusiastically supporting it in another.

The novel's most poignant treatment of conscription comes through [[faraday|Faraday]]. The radical journalist who held peace meetings "at considerable personal risk" and "distributed printed appeals to pacifist and anti-British sentiment" ultimately "was called up under the Conscription Act [and] went without protest, made an excellent soldier and died fighting in a night raid near Hulluch" [015-003]. Faraday's trajectory — from principled opposition to dutiful service to death in battle — encapsulates the war's power to override pre-war convictions. His quiet acceptance of conscription, after years of anti-militarist activism, is presented not as hypocrisy but as an honest reckoning with changed circumstances.

William himself avoids conscription by enlisting voluntarily after his experience in Belgium. The distinction between voluntary enlistment and compulsory service becomes, for him, a marker of his transformed identity: he is no longer the man who opposed military service on principle, but a soldier who chose his path.

## Thematic Significance

Compulsory service functions in the novel as a limit case for pre-war radical principles. The same William who denounced military conscription as the devil's work supported compulsory trade union membership with equal passion. Hamilton's point is not that one form of compulsion is better than the other, but that the pre-war radical mind was incapable of seeing the structural similarity between the two — a failure of intellectual consistency that the war exposed but did not create.

## Historical Context

Britain was unique among the major European powers in entering the First World War without conscription. The British Army in 1914 was a professional, volunteer force. As casualties mounted and voluntary enlistment declined, the government introduced the Military Service Act in January 1916, making all unmarried men aged 18–41 liable for service. The Act was extended to married men in May 1916.

Conscription was fiercely debated. The labour movement, Nonconformist churches, and the Liberal Party all had strong anti-conscription traditions. The introduction of compulsory service represented a fundamental shift in the relationship between the British state and its citizens — a shift that William's pre-war radicalism, with its suspicion of state power, would have instinctively resisted.
