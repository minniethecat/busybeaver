## Historical Context

The events of *William—An Englishman* unfold against the backdrop of the most transformative period in modern British history: the Edwardian afternoon of progressive reform, the catastrophe of the Great War, and the shattered aftermath. Each event in the novel is both personal and historical, private experience and public meaning. Hamilton's achievement is to show how history enters individual lives — not as abstraction but as experience, not as narrative but as disruption.
