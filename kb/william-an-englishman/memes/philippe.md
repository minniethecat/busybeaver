# Philippe

## Overview

Philippe is a young farm-boy who works at Madame Amberg's Belgian cottage, representing the rural innocence that the approaching war will destroy. His presence in the narrative is brief but significant: he embodies a world — agricultural, traditional, pre-modern — that exists outside the political categories that consume William, Griselda, and Madame Amberg. His youth makes him a figure of vulnerability, someone who will be swept up in events he cannot understand.

## Role in the Novel

Philippe functions as a symbol of the ordinary life that war disrupts. Unlike the novel's major characters, who are defined by their political commitments, Philippe is defined by his labour — the work of the farm, the tending of animals, the rhythms of rural life. His presence grounds the Belgian chapters in a material reality that the political drama threatens to obscure.
