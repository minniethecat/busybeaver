## About Concepts

Cicely Hamilton's *William—An Englishman* (1919) is a novel of ideas as much as characters. Its concept entries map the ideological landscape of early twentieth-century Britain: patriotism, militarism, progress, duty, freedom, and the forces that both uphold and undermine them. Each concept page traces how the novel embodies these ideas through its characters' experiences, using the fictional narrative as a lens on the political debates of the Great War era.
