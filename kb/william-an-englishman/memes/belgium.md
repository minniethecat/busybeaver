# Belgium

## Overview

The wartime setting where the novel's central drama unfolds — Madame Amberg's cottage, the flight from invasion, and Griselda's death.

## In the Novel

Belgium serves as more than setting in *William—An Englishman*. Hamilton invests locations with psychological and political significance, making them active participants in the novel's drama rather than passive backdrops. The movement between places — from London to Belgium, from domestic space to war zone — maps William Tully's psychological journey.

## Historical Context

Each location in the novel carries the weight of its historical moment. Edwardian London was a city of political ferment and class anxiety. Wartime Belgium underwent one of the most brutal occupations in modern European history. Hamilton's precise attention to place grounds her psychological drama in material reality.

## Key Passages

The novel's descriptions of place are precise and evocative, linking geography to psychology in ways characteristic of Hamilton's method.
