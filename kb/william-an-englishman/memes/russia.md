# Russia

## Overview

Referenced through Madame Amberg's putative Russian origin and the broader context of revolutionary internationalism that haunts the novel's political imagination.

## In the Novel

Russia appears in *William—An Englishman* as both geographical reality and symbolic space. Hamilton's treatment of place is never merely descriptive; locations carry ideological weight, representing political traditions, national characters, and the forces that shape individual destiny. The movement between nations — from England to Belgium to France — maps William Tully's transformation from insular Englishman to participant in European history.

## Historical Context

Russia in the Edwardian and WWI periods was defined by the political and military events that reshaped the European map. Hamilton published her novel in 1919, after the old Europe had been destroyed and before the new Europe had taken shape. Her treatment of place reflects this moment of historical suspension — places are remembered, lost, and imagined rather than simply inhabited.

## Key Passages

The novel's references to Russia are precise and purposeful, linking geography to psychology in Hamilton's characteristic manner.
