## About the Characters

Hamilton's characters are drawn with a polemicist's clarity: each represents a position in the novel's argument about war, gender, and social change. William Tully, the "Englishman" of the title, embodies dutiful, unthinking patriotism; Griselda, his wife, represents the emerging feminist consciousness; and the minor characters — the socialist Farthing, the suffragette Miss Haynes, the patriotic crowds — fill out the spectrum of early twentieth-century British political life.
