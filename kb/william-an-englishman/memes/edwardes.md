## Overview

Edwardes is a minor character in [[william-an-englishman-novel|William—An Englishman]], appearing in Chapter XV at the anti-militarist meeting convened by [[faraday|Faraday]]. As the secretary of the Central London branch, Edwardes represents the committee-bound, procedural character of pre-war socialist reformism — a world of earnest but ineffectual activism that the [[european-war|European War]] has rendered obsolete.

## At the Meeting

Edwardes is introduced at the gathering in the Bloomsbury hall:

> [015-010] Edwardes, the Central London secretary, was hugging his ankle as he always hugged it, and Mrs. Jay-Blenkinsop, the formidable treasurer, was at the table, with her receipt book and her books of tickets.

The physical detail — "hugging his ankle as he always hugged it" — conveys a man of habitual, almost ritualized postures, as predictable and unchanging as the meetings themselves.

## A "Little Bespectacled Labour Man"

When Edwardes speaks at the meeting, his character is further delineated:

> [015-014] Edwardes followed him, in response to the invitation for suggestions: a little bespectacled Labour man whose ideal was a world of committees.

The phrase "a world of committees" is a devastating capsule characterization. Edwardes, like [[edgar-jay-blenkinsop|Edgar Jay-Blenkinsop]], inhabits a universe of resolutions, agendas, and procedural motions — a universe that [[william-tully|William Tully]], transformed by his encounter with the war, can no longer take seriously.

## William's Contempt

The narrative groups Edwardes with the other attendees whom William now sees as fundamentally inadequate to the moment:

> [015-012] He was of a finer because more intelligent mould than the Edwardeses and Jay-Blenkinsops who heard him and who were still thinking of the European tragedy as a red herring drawn by the cunning politicians to divert reform.

The pluralization — "the Edwardeses and Jay-Blenkinsops" — elevates the character from an individual to a type: the well-meaning, committee-devoted reformer whose frame of reference cannot accommodate the catastrophe of total war.

## Significance

Edwardes serves as one of several foils — alongside [[edgar-jay-blenkinsop|Jay-Blenkinsop]] and his mother — through whom the novel dramatizes the inadequacy of pre-war progressive politics in the face of the European catastrophe. He is not villainous but simply irrelevant, his "world of committees" swept aside by forces beyond its comprehension.
