# Haste

## Definition

**Haste** is the quality of hurried or rushed action — movement driven by urgency, impatience, or the pressure of circumstance. In *The Mystery at Lilac Inn*, haste appears in concentrated bursts at moments of narrative acceleration, when characters must act quickly to seize an opportunity or respond to a threat (002-030). Haste is distinct from speed or efficiency: it carries an implicit note of incompleteness, of action taken before deliberation is finished.

## In the Novel

Haste in the novel is most often framed as a consequence of disorder. When Nancy arrives home to find "everything in confusion," Hannah's haste in departing has left domestic chaos in its wake (002-030). This early instance establishes haste as a disruptive force: hurried action begets disorganization, which in turn creates openings for oversight and error. The motif recurs at Lilac Inn itself, where Mrs. Willoughby's haste to investigate a disturbance leaves the household similarly disordered (005-038).

The novel also uses haste to characterize individuals. The girl who darts across the porch "in her haste to reach the street" (003-059) is seen by Nancy from a distance — the haste itself is the first piece of evidence Nancy gathers about this unknown figure, marking her as someone with something to hide or somewhere urgent to be. Haste in this context functions as a behavioral tell: it signals to the observant detective that normal rhythms have been disrupted, and disruption is the first clue that something is wrong.

Though haste appears only in a few concentrated passages, its narrative function is significant. These moments of acceleration punctuate the novel's generally measured pace, creating peaks of urgency that briefly elevate the stakes before the narrative settles back into its investigative rhythm. Haste is the exception that proves the rule of Nancy's methodical approach — she observes it in others but rarely succumbs to it herself.

## Key Instances

- **Hannah's hasty departure** (002-030): Domestic disorder caused by haste foreshadows the larger disruptions to come.
- **The fleeing figure on the porch** (003-059): Haste as a behavioral clue — someone running is someone with a reason to run.
- **Mrs. Willoughby's haste at the Inn** (005-038): a moment of crisis that reveals how haste propagates through social networks.
