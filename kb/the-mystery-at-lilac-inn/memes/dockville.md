# Dockville

Dockville is a small riverside town located up the river from Lilac Inn. It serves as an important location in Nancy Drew's investigation of the Crandall jewels theft, as it is where Mary Mason's brother resides (012-038).

## Location and Description

Dockville is described as a district situated up the river from the Lilac Inn area. Nancy questions whether it lies "up the river" (012-039), confirming its position along the same waterway that flows past the inn. The town appears to be a modest residential community with streets and houses typical of a small riverside settlement.

## Role in the Investigation

Dockville becomes a focal point of Nancy's detective work after she learns that Mary Mason has a brother living there. Determined to uncover more about Mary's background, Nancy decides: "To Dockville it is" (012-044), setting out on a drive to investigate.

Upon reaching Dockville, Nancy initially feared "that the trip to Dockville had been a wasted one" (012-052), but she persisted in gathering information. A delivery driver confirmed knowing Mary Mason: "That girl who lives down in Dockville? Sure! I just dropped off several packages there" (013-028).

Nancy later discovered "the address was a street in Dockville" (016-015) for Mary Mason, and noted there appeared to be "more than one Mason family in Dockville" (016-029). She drove to Dockville to confront Mary Mason directly, resolved to "drive to Dockville and see Mary Mason" (016-053).

## Key Events

Nancy made multiple trips to Dockville during her investigation. On one occasion, others believed "she thought you had gone to Dockville" (017-004) when she was elsewhere. She later declared, "I'll drive over to Dockville right now" (017-041), and upon "reaching Dockville, Nancy drove toward the house" (017-043) where she hoped to find answers. Nancy remained in the area "before she left Dockville" (018-003), working to resolve the mystery.
