# Courage

## Overview

Courage is a defining character trait of Nancy Drew, demonstrated throughout her investigation in *The Mystery at Lilac Inn*. Nancy repeatedly faces dangerous situations with composure and determination, refusing to be deterred by threats or intimidation. She is described as "by nature a brave girl" (018-033).

## Facing Danger

Throughout the novel, Nancy confronts numerous perilous situations. She pursues suspects into dangerous locations, hides in places where she "dared not move, scarcely breathe" (018-002), and follows suspects "as closely as she dared" (016-041). Her courage is not recklessness but calculated bravery in service of justice.

## Moral Courage

Nancy's courage extends beyond physical bravery. She demonstrates moral courage by standing up for what is right, even when it would be easier to abandon the case. When her friend Emily is distressed by accusations, Nancy provides steadfast encouragement and support (015-015). She is determined to clear the names of the innocent and expose the guilty, regardless of personal risk.

## Courage as a Theme

Courage is a recurring theme in Nancy Drew's character. Her willingness to face danger and uncertainty reflects her commitment to justice and her loyalty to those she helps. The novel portrays courage not as the absence of fear, but as the determination to act despite it. In one critical moment, a character "lacked the courage to attempt a rescue" (022-007), highlighting by contrast the exceptional bravery that Nancy consistently displays.
