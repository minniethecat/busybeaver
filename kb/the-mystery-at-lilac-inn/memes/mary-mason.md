# Mary Mason

## Overview

Mary Mason is a mysterious figure whose connection to the Crandall jewels theft makes her a key person of interest in Nancy Drew's investigation. Her strange behavior and sudden disappearance after the theft draw Nancy's attention and suspicion.

## Strange Behavior

From her first appearance, Mary Mason's conduct is peculiar. The narrative notes the "strange look" she displays (004-002), and her actions in the period leading up to the theft raise questions about her possible involvement. Nancy begins to gather evidence concerning Mary Mason, determined to learn the truth about her role.

## The Investigation

Nancy is determined "to lose no time in gathering evidence concerning Mary Mason" (016-002). As the investigation progresses, Mary Mason becomes a central suspect. She "denied the accusations made against her" (023-035), but the evidence against her appears damning. Nancy must determine whether Mary is the true culprit or another victim of circumstance.

## Resolution

Ultimately, Mary Mason's involvement in the case is resolved through Nancy's persistent investigation. The truth of her connection to the theft, whether as perpetrator or pawn, emerges as the mystery reaches its climax.
