# Patrol

The **patrol** — in the form of the government patrol and the patrol wagon — represents institutional law enforcement in *The Mystery at Lilac Inn*. It appears late in the narrative, arriving to formalize what Nancy Drew's investigation has already accomplished: the identification and capture of the criminals.

## The Government Patrol

The river patrol is mentioned as a threat by the criminals themselves: "This may belong to the government patrol. They'd jail us in a minute if they..." (022-018). The patrol represents a force beyond Nancy's personal investigation — state authority that the criminals fear more than they fear an amateur detective. The patrol's jurisdiction on the water is a constraint the criminals must navigate.

## The Patrol Wagon

The patrol wagon appears at the novel's climax. Criminals are "handcuffed and hustled into the patrol wagon" (024-052). After "the patrol wagon had departed" (024-055), the crowd disperses — the patrol's work is done, and the criminals are removed from the scene. The patrol wagon is the final step in the justice process: investigation leads to identification, identification leads to arrest, and arrest leads to the patrol wagon.

## Patrol and Nancy's Investigation

The patrol's role is significant in what it does not do. The patrol does not solve the mystery — Nancy does. The patrol does not identify the criminals — Nancy does. What the patrol provides is the legal authority to make arrests and remove dangerous criminals from the community. The novel presents a division of labor: amateur detection handles investigation; state authority handles enforcement.

## Justice Institutionalized

The patrol represents the institutionalization of justice. Nancy's personal quest for truth becomes, through the patrol, a matter of public order. The patrol wagon's arrival signals that the private phase of the investigation is over and the public phase of prosecution has begun.

## Key Passages

> "May belong to the government patrol. They'd jail us in a minute" (022-018)

The criminals' fear of the patrol reveals its power — even before it appears, the patrol constrains criminal action.

> "Handcuffed and hustled into the patrol wagon" (024-052)

The moment of arrest — swift, decisive, and institutional. The criminals who eluded Nancy's personal pursuit cannot elude the state.

> "After the patrol wagon had departed, the crowd..." (024-055)

The departure of the patrol wagon signals the end of the crisis — order is restored, and normal life resumes.
