# Crandall Jewels

## Overview

The Crandall jewels are a famous collection of valuable jewelry belonging to the Crandall family (001-041). They serve as the central plot device in *The Mystery at Lilac Inn*, with their theft and subsequent recovery forming the backbone of Nancy Drew's investigation.

## History and Significance

The jewels are described as "very quaint" and well-remembered by those familiar with the Crandall family (002-038). They represent the Crandall family inheritance, passed down through generations.

## Inheritance

Emily Crandall is set to receive the Crandall jewels as part of her inheritance (001-040). Nancy Drew is invited to see the jewels, and Emily reminds her of the upcoming viewing (002-008). Nancy, intrigued by the famous jewels, eagerly anticipates the opportunity (004-006).

## The Theft

The jewels are stolen under mysterious circumstances at Lilac Inn (007-011). At the time of the theft, the jewels were in the possession of Mrs. Willoughby, who was carrying them for safekeeping (009-023). The theft shocks the community and sets Nancy on a determined course of investigation.

## Nancy's Investigation

Nancy Drew dedicates herself to solving the mystery of the Crandall jewels (009-088). She visits the bank vault where the jewels were kept (017-028) and pursues multiple leads. Despite the difficulty of the case, Nancy remains determined to uncover the truth (012-009).

## Recovery

Through persistent investigation, Nancy ultimately confronts the thief, who admits to stealing the Crandall jewels (021-026). Nancy manages to save the jewels through quick thinking and courage (021-029).
