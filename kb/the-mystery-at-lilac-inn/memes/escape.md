# Escape

Escape and flight are significant motifs in the mystery at Lilac Inn, appearing both in the criminals' attempts to avoid capture and in Nancy Drew's own need to free herself from confinement during the investigation.

## The Criminals' Escape

The perpetrators of the jewel theft attempted to flee after committing their crime. Their escape plans involved using the river and motorboat to make a getaway, with the conspirators hoping to elude pursuit by heading downstream.

## Nancy's Escape

During her investigation, Nancy found herself held against her will. Her escape from captivity became a critical turning point in the case. Using her resourcefulness and courage, she was able to free herself and continue her pursuit of the criminals.

## Symbolic Meaning

The theme of escape runs through the narrative — escape from suspicion for the wrongly accused, escape from poverty for Mary Mason, and ultimately, escape from justice that the criminals failed to achieve. The resolution of the mystery ensures that those responsible cannot escape accountability.
