# Evidence

## Overview

Evidence is the foundation of criminal investigation in *The Mystery at Lilac Inn*. Nancy Drew must carefully gather and evaluate evidence to identify the true culprit behind the Crandall jewels theft, distinguishing between genuine proof and misleading appearances.

## Gathering Evidence

Nancy is determined "to lose no time in gathering evidence" (016-002) as she pursues the investigation. She sifts through the available information repeatedly, "over and over she sifted the evidence" (011-069), but initially finds it insufficient to draw firm conclusions.

## The Challenge of Proof

Throughout the investigation, Nancy faces the difficulty of building a case with limited evidence. She acknowledges that she does "not have a particle of evidence to back up her theory" (012-009), relying instead on intuition and persistence. The legal standard for proof is high, and "so far the evidence certainly isn't sufficient" to accuse anyone of the crime (013-034).

## Damaging Evidence

As the investigation progresses, Nancy realizes that "the evidence against her" — one of the suspects — "was extremely damaging" (015-036). This realization drives her to work harder to uncover the full truth, recognizing that appearances can be deceiving and that careful analysis of evidence is essential to justice.
