# Mr. Hodge

## Role in the Story

Mr. Hodge is the proprietor of Taylor Store, a local shop in River Heights. When Carson Drew needs information about pawned jewelry as part of his investigation into the stolen Crandall jewels, Nancy suggests her father approach Mr. Hodge, noting that "you've done favors for Mr. Hodge more than once" and that "he ought to do you a good turn once in a while" (014-053). Mr. Hodge initially proves reluctant to share information but eventually cooperates, revealing that a certain young woman has been purchasing quantities of inexpensive trinkets — a detail that advances the investigation (015-047, 015-053).

## Appearances

- **Chapter XIV (014-049–059)**: Nancy suggests Carson Drew ask Mr. Hodge for information about pawned jewelry. She urges her father to see him "the first thing in the morning."
- **Chapter XV (015-007, 015-047–053)**: Nancy reminds her father of his errand to see Mr. Hodge. Later, Carson Drew reports back: he saw Mr. Hodge, who was initially reluctant but ultimately shared that a certain young woman had been "buying scads of trinkets."

## Key Actions

- **Provides pawnshop intelligence** (015-047–053): After initial reluctance, Mr. Hodge discloses that a young woman has been making unusual jewelry purchases — a lead that helps identify the real culprits.
