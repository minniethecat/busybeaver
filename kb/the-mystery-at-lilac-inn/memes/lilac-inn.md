# Lilac Inn

## Overview

Lilac Inn is a country inn located on the main road between River Heights and Melville (001-007). It serves as a popular dining destination and becomes the central location of the mystery that Nancy Drew investigates throughout the novel.

## Location and Setting

The inn is situated on a main road connecting River Heights to Melville (002-007). Its grounds are described as a "lovely spot" (001-016), and the establishment is known for its chicken dinners (001-003). The inn features a dining room where guests are seated by the window (005-007).

## Role in the Mystery

### Jewel Incident

A critical episode unfolds at Lilac Inn when Mrs. Willoughby and Mrs. Potter stop for luncheon while carrying valuable jewelry (005-004). During their meal, Mrs. Willoughby's handbag—containing Emily Crandall's jewels—is stolen (005-007). This theft sets the central mystery in motion and draws Nancy Drew into the investigation.

### Nancy's Investigation

Nancy Drew makes multiple visits to Lilac Inn to investigate the jewel theft (009-013). Upon arrival one morning, she questions the staff about the incident (009-041). Nancy is determined to preserve the reputation of Lilac Inn while uncovering the truth (009-055). She later reflects that "the mystery of Lilac Inn was likely to prove even more" complex than she initially thought (009-088).

### The Impersonation Plot

Nancy learns that the "little scene at Lilac Inn could have been staged" as part of a larger scheme (007-039). The inn becomes the backdrop for a plot involving impersonation—specifically, someone pretending to be Nancy Drew herself (010-002).
