# Secret

**Secrets** pervade *The Mystery at Lilac Inn* — secrets kept by criminals to conceal their guilt, secrets uncovered by Nancy Drew to reveal the truth, and physical secrets like the hidden compartment that literally holds the key to the mystery.

## The Secret Compartment

The most tangible secret in the novel is physical: "the secret compartment of the motorboat" (021-027). This compartment serves the criminals' purposes perfectly — a concealed space where stolen jewels can be hidden in plain sight. Its discovery is one of Nancy's major investigative breakthroughs:

> "I put 'em back in the secret compartment of the boat" (019-026)

The compartment represents a double concealment: it hides the jewels from discovery, and its existence hides the criminals' method. When Nancy finds it, she dismantles the second concealment even before recovering the jewels themselves.

## Secrets and Character

The novel distinguishes between different kinds of secrets:

- **Criminal secrets**: Intentional concealment for illegal purposes — the criminals hide their identities, methods, and the jewels
- **Protective secrets**: Information withheld to protect someone — some characters keep secrets not from guilt but from fear
- **Investigative secrets**: What Nancy discovers but does not immediately reveal — she keeps her conclusions private until she can prove them

## The Ethics of Secrets

Mr. Drew warns about the danger of revealing secrets carelessly: "let the neighbors into all our secrets" (015-043). The line between appropriate disclosure and harmful gossip is one that Nancy must navigate throughout her investigation. Not every secret deserves to be exposed; not every exposure serves justice.

## Uncovering Secrets

Nancy's method for uncovering secrets relies on:
- **Corroboration**: Comparing one person's account against another's
- **Physical investigation**: Searching the actual locations — the motorboat, the storeroom, the inn
- **Patience**: Secrets reveal themselves over time; Nancy "secreted the purse" (007-042) of evidence, accumulating facts until the full picture emerges

## Key Passages

> "The secret compartment! I've found it!" (024-032)

Nancy's exclamation marks the climactic discovery — the physical secret that confirms her theory and locates the stolen jewels.

> "The jewels had been hidden in a secret compartment of the motorboat" (021-027)

The revelation connects the crime scene (Lilac Inn) with the hiding place (the motorboat), linking the criminals to both locations.

> "Let the neighbors into all our secrets" (015-043)

The caution against indiscriminate disclosure reflects the novel's awareness that secrets serve social as well as criminal purposes.
