# The Discovery

## Overview

The discovery of crucial evidence marks the turning point in Nancy Drew's investigation. After repeatedly sifting through the available information—"over and over she sifted the evidence" (011-069)—Nancy finally finds the connection that breaks the case open. She realizes that "the evidence against her was extremely damaging" (015-036), confirming her theory. of the Crandall jewels theft. After sifting through the available clues and following leads, Nancy uncovers the information that breaks the case open.

## The Breakthrough

Nancy's persistent detective work leads to a breakthrough in understanding the crime. She had previously acknowledged that she did "not have a particle of evidence" (012-009), but her persistence finally yields results. in understanding the true nature of the crime at Lilac Inn. She pieces together disparate clues into a coherent picture that reveals the identity of the thief and the method of the theft.
