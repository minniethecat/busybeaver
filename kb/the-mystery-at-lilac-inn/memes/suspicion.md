# Suspicion

## Overview

Suspicion permeates the investigation of the Crandall jewels theft in *The Mystery at Lilac Inn*. Multiple characters fall under suspicion, and the atmosphere of doubt complicates Nancy Drew's efforts to uncover the truth.

## The Initial Accusation

When the jewels are stolen at Lilac Inn, suspicion immediately falls on those present. Mrs. Willoughby, who was carrying the jewels at the time of the theft, attempts to avoid drawing attention, noting that "no one must know" she has the valuables (005-008). Despite her precautions, the police investigation soon focuses on her.

## Mrs. Willoughby Under Suspicion

The police "are inclined to suspect Mrs. Willoughby" of involvement in the theft (007-054). Nancy is troubled by this development, knowing that Emily Crandall will be distressed to learn "that her guardian is suspected" (007-059). Others question whether the suspicion is warranted, asking Nancy directly if she really suspects Mrs. Willoughby (008-069).

## Broader Suspicion

Beyond the primary suspects, the atmosphere of distrust extends more broadly. Nancy observes suspicious behavior at the inn, including a staff member whom she suspects "frequently took food" without authorization (012-042). The web of suspicion complicates her investigation, requiring her to distinguish genuine evidence from unfounded accusations.
