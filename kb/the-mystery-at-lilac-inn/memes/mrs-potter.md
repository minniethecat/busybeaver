# Mrs. Potter

## Overview

Mrs. Potter is the companion of Mrs. Willoughby during the critical luncheon at Lilac Inn when the Crandall jewels are stolen. She accompanies Mrs. Willoughby and provides support as the two women attempt to safeguard the valuable jewelry.

## At Lilac Inn

Mrs. Potter arrives at Lilac Inn alongside Mrs. Willoughby, the two "elegantly dressed women" alighting from a handsome coupé (005-004). She is visibly nervous about the situation, asking Mrs. Willoughby in a low tone whether it was "wise to stop here for luncheon" given that they "can't take any chances, carrying all that precious jewelry" (005-005).

## Witness to the Theft

As a witness to the events surrounding the theft, Mrs. Potter's account of the luncheon is important to Nancy's investigation. She and Mrs. Willoughby are "both eager to get away from Lilac Inn" after the incident (005-032), suggesting the traumatic nature of what transpired.
