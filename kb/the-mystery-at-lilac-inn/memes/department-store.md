The **Department Store** (Taylor's Store) is the largest retail establishment in [[River Heights]], located in the main commercial district. It serves as both a mundane social backdrop and an unexpected nexus for investigative leads in the [[the-investigation|investigation]] of [[Mary Mason]]'s circumstances.

## Role in the Narrative

### A Social Setting

[[Helen Corning]] and [[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]] visit the department store during a shopping trip to nearby [[hidelbergs|Hidelberg's]], a high-end dress shop:

> "Helen drove skillfully, and to her elation found a parking place just in front of her favorite department store." (010-035)

Outside the store, they encounter [[emily-crandall|Emily Crandall]], whose distressed appearance signals the emotional toll of the mystery:

> "Arm in arm the two girls left the department store. Just outside the door they met Emily Crandall. The girl was pale and deep circles were under her blue eyes and her face was drawn." (010-050, 010-053)

### The Taylor's Delivery Wagon

The department store's red delivery wagon becomes a crucial tracking device. [[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]] recognizes it and uses it to establish a connection between [[Mary Mason]] and the stolen-goods operation:

> "Nancy saw Mary Mason take the packages. She then closed the door and the driver went back to his wagon." (013-015)

Nancy follows the wagon and confirms its identity:

> "As she turned the corner she caught a glimpse of red far up the street and was certain that it was the Taylor delivery auto." (013-021)

> "Are you the delivery man from Taylor's?" Nancy asked, by way of an opening. (013-023)

### Employment Background

> "But you know Mr. Hodge at the Taylor Store, Dad. Isn't he one of the big men there?" (014-049)

The store later appears in a cross-reference check during the investigation — staff at both the River Heights Department Store and Hidelberg's are queried about [[Mary Mason]]'s recent purchases (015-061).

## Narrative Functions

1. **Verisimilitude** — anchors the fictional River Heights in recognizable small-city geography
2. **Employment nexus** — local employer that normalizes questions about wages and hiring
3. **Surveillance node** — the delivery wagon turns a mundane retail operation into an investigative asset
4. **Class marker** — the contrast between the department store (accessible) and [[hidelbergs|Hidelberg's]] (exclusive) maps River Heights social stratification
