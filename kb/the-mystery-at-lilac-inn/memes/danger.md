# Danger

## Overview

Danger is a constant presence in Nancy Drew's investigation in *The Mystery at Lilac Inn*. Her pursuit of the truth repeatedly places her in perilous situations that test her courage and resourcefulness.

## Physical Peril

Nancy knowingly places herself in dangerous situations to gather evidence. She enters an old house despite knowing that "it would be dangerous to enter" (018-030), driven by her determination to solve the mystery. Her curiosity overcomes her caution, even though "she realized that it was a dangerous thing to do" (018-008).

## Environmental Hazards

The danger Nancy faces extends beyond human threats to environmental hazards. During a critical voyage, Nancy knows that circumstances "added to the danger of the voyage" (021-010). Later, she recognizes that "it was dangerous to attempt a journey on the" river (020-052), yet proceeds with her investigation despite the risk.

## Calculated Risk

Nancy's approach to danger is characterized by calculated risk rather than recklessness. She assesses situations carefully, as when she determines "there was no particular danger" in a dimly lit room (019-033), proceeding only when she judges the risk to be manageable. Her ability to navigate dangerous situations is essential to her success as a detective.
