# Jennings

Jennings is an employee who served Mrs. Jane Willoughby for six years and later worked at Lilac Inn. His connection to both Mrs. Willoughby and the inn makes him a person of interest in Nancy Drew's investigation of the Crandall jewels theft.

## Employment and Description

Jennings had been in Mrs. Willoughby's employ for six years (006-032). When Nancy inquired about the Willoughby household staff, she was told: "only Jennings. He served Mrs. Willoughby" (009-061). When Nancy asked to speak with him, Jennings appeared: "He was tall, precise in gait" (009-068), suggesting a formal, disciplined manner.

## Role in the Investigation

Nancy questioned Jennings about the events surrounding the jewel theft. She began quietly: "Jennings, what do you know about this?" (009-069), seeking information about the circumstances at Lilac Inn. His position gave him insight into both Mrs. Willoughby's household and the operations at the inn.

It was later revealed that Jennings also worked at Lilac Inn as a waiter. A witness noted: "that waiter at Lilac Inn—Jennings they call him" (011-042), establishing his dual role that placed him at the scene of the theft.

## Aftermath

At the resolution of the case, Emily Crandall reflected: "And poor Jennings!" (025-024), expressing sympathy for his entanglement in the affair.
