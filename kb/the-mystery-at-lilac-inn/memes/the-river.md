# The River

## Overview

The river near Lilac Inn serves as the setting for some of the novel's most dramatic sequences. A vessel sinks on the river, leaving its occupant with "little hope of a rescue" (022-032). It is the scene of a sinking vessel, a perilous rescue operation, and moments of high tension during the story's climax.

## Dramatic Events

The river becomes the stage for life-threatening danger when a vessel begins to sink. The treacherous conditions mean that rescue is uncertain, and those aboard face desperate circumstances (022-007). The isolation of the waterway means help is far away, but Nancy is prepared "if need be, to attempt a rescue herself" (022-055), and those aboard face a desperate struggle for survival. The river's treacherous conditions test the courage of all involved.
