# The Stranger

## Overview

A mysterious stranger appears early in the novel, rushing up to Nancy Drew and eagerly crying out "Nancy Drew of all people!" (001-009). This encounter marks the beginning of Nancy's involvement with the events that will lead to the mystery at Lilac Inn.

## Mysterious Behavior

The stranger's behavior is peculiar from the start. She seats herself on the davenport in the Drew home (003-010), and her presence raises questions. Nancy later observes a stranger in town and, though she "would not have given a stranger a second glance" under normal circumstances, something about this person catches her attention (016-007).

## Nancy's Surveillance

Nancy follows the stranger through River Heights, watching as the figure walks "rapidly" down the street (016-010). She picks up "an old envelope which the stranger had dropped" (016-012), finding a potential clue. Nancy follows the stranger into the train station (016-021) and onto a train, sitting "directly behind" to observe (016-032). She hears the conductor address the stranger (016-035), gathering intelligence about this mysterious figure.
