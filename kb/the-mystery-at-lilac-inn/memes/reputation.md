# Reputation

Nancy Drew's reputation as a successful detective is an important element in the mystery at Lilac Inn. Her prior achievements have made her known in the community, lending credibility to her investigative work.

## Prior Achievements

Nancy's reputation was built on her earlier cases. Emily Crandall reminded her: "Everyone said it was a wonderful piece of detective work when you solved the mystery of the Jacob Aborn bungalow. I read glowing accounts of your cleverness in the papers. Just imagine capturing a criminal! I'd be thrilled to death!" (001-017).

## Recognition at the Inn

Nancy's reputation preceded her even at Lilac Inn. Upon entering the crowded dining room, "the head waiter bowed to Nancy in recognition" (001-022) and gave her and Emily an excellent table "to the astonishment of persons who had not received choice tables" (001-022). This preferential treatment demonstrated that Nancy was known and respected.

## Impact on the Case

Nancy's established reputation made others more willing to share information and cooperate with her investigation. Her credibility as a detective was an asset that helped her navigate the complex social dynamics of the case.
