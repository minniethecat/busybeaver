# Sedan

Mrs. Jane Willoughby's sedan is an automobile that plays a small but pivotal role in the opening of the mystery at Lilac Inn. Emily Crandall borrowed the sedan, and its breakdown set in motion the chain of events that would involve Nancy Drew in the Crandall jewels case.

## The Breakdown

Emily Crandall explained to Nancy: "Mrs. Willoughby lent me her sedan for the day; but, as luck would have it, the mean thing stalled" (001-013). The sedan's mechanical failure forced Emily to walk to Lilac Inn, where she unexpectedly encountered Nancy Drew. Without this chance meeting, Nancy might not have become involved in the jewel theft investigation.

## Significance

The stalled sedan represents one of several fortunate coincidences that positioned Nancy to take on the case. It also establishes the economic context — Mrs. Willoughby owned a sedan and could lend it to Emily, while Emily herself lacked her own vehicle.
