# Rescue

## Overview

Rescue is a critical motif in *The Mystery at Lilac Inn*, representing the moments when characters face life-threatening danger and require intervention. These episodes heighten the stakes of Nancy Drew's investigation and demonstrate the courage required to act in moments of crisis.

## The Crisis at Sea

One of the novel's most dramatic rescue scenarios unfolds on the water. A character finds themselves in a sinking vessel, cut off from help and able to "feel the boat list" with "little hope of a rescue" (022-032). The isolation and helplessness of the situation underscore the gravity of the danger.

## Failed Rescues

Not all rescue attempts succeed. In one critical moment, a bystander witnesses someone in peril but "lacked the courage to attempt a rescue" (022-007), choosing self-preservation over intervention. This moment of moral failure serves as a foil to Nancy's characteristic bravery.

## Nancy as Rescuer

Nancy Drew herself embodies the rescue impulse, willing to put herself at risk "if need be, to attempt a rescue herself" (022-055). Her willingness to intervene where others hesitate defines her heroism and distinguishes her from characters who lack the courage to act.
