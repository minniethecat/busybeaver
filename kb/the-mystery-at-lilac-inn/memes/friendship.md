# Friendship

## Overview

Friendship is a recurring theme in *The Mystery at Lilac Inn*, providing emotional grounding amidst the mystery and suspense. The bonds between characters, particularly the friendship between Nancy Drew and Emily Crandall, motivate key actions and decisions throughout the novel.

## Nancy and Emily

Emily Crandall is described as a "friend" of Nancy Drew, and their reunion is marked by genuine affection. Nancy greets Emily with "genuine delight, gripping her friend's hand" (001-012). Emily has come to River Heights specifically "to see a friend" (001-013), underscoring the importance of their relationship.

## The Bond of Trust

Emily's trust in Nancy is evident throughout the novel. She confides in Nancy about her inheritance and her fears regarding the Crandall jewels. Emily possesses a "beauty which was the envy of her friends" (001-010), yet her true value lies in the loyalty she inspires in Nancy.

## Support in Crisis

When Emily is troubled by the theft and the subsequent accusations, Nancy provides steadfast support. Emily turns to her friend in moments of distress, and Nancy resolves to protect her from those who would "coax her away from my best friend" (003-006). The friendship between the two young women forms an emotional core of the novel, motivating Nancy's determination to see justice done.
