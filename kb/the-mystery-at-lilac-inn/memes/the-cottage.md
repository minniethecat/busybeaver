# The Cottage

The lakeside residence of Emily Crandall and her guardian Mrs. Jane Willoughby, located outside River Heights and intended as the viewing site for the famous Crandall family jewels.

---

## Description

The cottage is a residence situated on a lake, reachable by road from River Heights (001-032). Emily Crandall and her guardian Mrs. Willoughby live there (001-032). The property is accessible by car, as Nancy Drew drives her roadster to visit (004-007). The cottage has a door at the front entry, and it is possible to walk around the exterior of the house (004-007).

The building is described as being within sight from the approaching road — Nancy can see the cottage as she nears it (008-081). There is at least one window or visible exterior that allows an observer to determine whether lights are on inside (008-081).

## Role in Narrative

Emily Crandall invites Nancy Drew to visit the cottage specifically to view the Crandall family jewels, which Emily has recently inherited (001-048). Nancy eagerly accepts the invitation (001-049).

When Nancy arrives at the cottage for the appointed viewing, she finds no one at home — her knocks go unanswered, and after walking around the house she confirms the cottage appears empty (004-007). Emily later apologizes, explaining she was delayed and had intended to return before Nancy arrived (004-012).

The cottage becomes significant in the jewel theft narrative. The jewels are meant to be kept at the cottage overnight before being transported (004-022). Emily half-jokingly suggests that Nancy would have to keep the jewels in the cottage all night if circumstances required it (004-022). Later events involve Nancy speeding toward the cottage on the lake (008-079), and the narrative indicates that Emily Willoughby — actually Mrs. Willoughby — is imprisoned in an abandoned cottage, though this may be a different structure (020-026).
