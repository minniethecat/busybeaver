# Detective Work

## Overview

Detective work is the systematic approach to criminal investigation practiced by Nancy Drew throughout *The Mystery at Lilac Inn*. Her methods combine careful observation, logical deduction, and persistent questioning of witnesses and suspects. The police investigation begins by asking whether there is "any real clue" to pursue (007-053).

## Nancy's Methods

Nancy Drew approaches each case with a methodical mindset. She begins by gathering facts and interviewing those involved, following leads wherever they may take her. Her father, Carson Drew, is a famous criminal and mystery-case lawyer, and Nancy has learned much from observing his work (002-021).

## Observation and Deduction

Nancy relies heavily on her powers of observation, noticing details that others overlook. She pieces together seemingly unrelated facts to form a coherent picture of the crime. Her ability to connect disparate clues is a hallmark of her detective work. She methodically traces down leads, describing her process as "tracing down a number of clues" (009-035).

## Persistence

Throughout the investigation of the Crandall jewels theft, Nancy demonstrates remarkable persistence. Despite setbacks and dangers, she refuses to abandon the case. She follows leads to Lilac Inn, interviews staff and patrons, and pursues suspects even when the trail grows cold. She investigates "every clue" available (010-029) and refuses to drop any lead "without a little investigation" (009-055).

## Courage Under Pressure

Nancy's detective work frequently places her in dangerous situations. She maintains her composure under pressure, using her wits to escape peril and continue her investigation. Her courage is as essential to her success as her deductive skills.
