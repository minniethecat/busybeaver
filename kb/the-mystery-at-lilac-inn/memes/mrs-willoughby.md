# Mrs. Willoughby

## Overview

Mrs. Willoughby is Emily Crandall's guardian, entrusted with the safekeeping of the Crandall family jewels. When the jewels are stolen from her handbag during a luncheon at Lilac Inn, she becomes a central figure—and initial suspect—in the resulting investigation.

## The Jewel Custody

Mrs. Willoughby carries the Crandall jewels in a handbag with "an ornate clasp" that draws the attention of a waiter at Lilac Inn (005-011). She is keenly aware of the risk, nervously commanding her companion Mrs. Potter to be silent: "No one must know I have it!" (005-006). Despite her precautions, the theft occurs.

## Under Suspicion

When the jewels are discovered missing, suspicion falls heavily on Mrs. Willoughby. The police "are inclined to suspect Mrs. Willoughby" (007-054), and she is directly asked: "Has your ward accused you?" (008-011). She later confesses: "I was so terribly excited" (008-038) during the incident, but maintains her innocence.

## Key Relationships

- **Emily Crandall** — Her ward and the owner of the stolen jewels
- **Mrs. Potter** — Her companion during the incident at Lilac Inn
- **Nancy Drew** — The detective investigating the theft
