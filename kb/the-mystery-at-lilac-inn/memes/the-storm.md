# The Storm

## Overview

A violent storm strikes during the events of *The Mystery at Lilac Inn*. Nancy thinks to herself: "I believe there's going to be a storm" (017-045), and her foreboding proves correct as a "terrible storm" (018-032) descends upon the area. The storm serves as both a literal and metaphorical element, heightening the novel's suspense at critical moments.

## During the Storm

The chapter titled "During the Storm" captures the intensity of the weather event, as characters realize "it's going to storm" (018-017) and must act quickly and its impact on the characters. The dangerous conditions add urgency to Nancy's investigation and create additional obstacles that test her courage and resourcefulness.

## Impact on the Investigation

The storm creates hazardous conditions for travel and communication. The sky confirms "that the storm was likely to last for several" hours (018-040), isolating characters and raising the stakes of the mystery. Nancy must navigate both the criminal conspiracy and the environmental dangers that the storm presents, demonstrating her determination to solve the case regardless of circumstances.
