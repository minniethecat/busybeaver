# Luncheon

The luncheon at Lilac Inn is the framing event for the opening of the mystery. It brings Nancy Drew and Emily Crandall together after a long separation and places Nancy at the scene when the Crandall jewels are stolen.

## Nancy's Arrival

Nancy arrived at Lilac Inn motivated by hunger. She told herself: "The thought of chicken makes me almost expire from hunger. I think I'll stop here for luncheon" (001-006). The inn's sign advertising "Chicken Dinners: Our Specialty" had caught her attention as she drove along the lake road.

## The Reunion

Once seated in the dining room, Nancy and Emily enjoyed their meal together. After they had eaten, Nancy asked Emily: "Have you had luncheon?" and Emily replied: "No, I haven't, and it's after one o'clock, too. I'm nearly starved" (001-018). Nancy then invited Emily to join her, setting in motion their renewed friendship and collaboration.

## Significance

The luncheon placed Nancy Drew at Lilac Inn at the exact time the jewel theft occurred, making her a natural person to investigate the crime. Without this meal, the entire course of the mystery might have been different.
