# XXIV: The Search


## XXIV

[024-001] The Search

[024-002] “[[nancy-drew|Miss Drew]], you may as well wait in one of the sheds where you’ll be out of the rain,” [[captain-dudley|Captain Dudley]] said, addressing Nancy. “I’ll have the motorboat dragged up to the dock where it will be safe to board her. It will take only a few minutes.”

[024-003] “Thank you,” Nancy replied quietly.

[024-004] “Since you’re through questioning me, I want to go to a hotel,” [[mary-mason|Mary Mason]] declared angrily. “My clothes are soaking wet and—”

[024-005] “If you make the slightest disturbance, we will call the [[police-department|police]] without waiting for Miss Drew to produce [[crandall-jewels|the jewels]],” the captain told her severely.

[024-006] “She can’t do it!”

[024-007] “Take the three prisoners into the boathouse,” Captain Dudley ordered.

[024-008] In spite of her protests, Mary was marched along with the others. Though no compulsion was placed upon Nancy, she followed, to get out of the rain which had chilled her to the bone. Her serene expression did not disclose that she was troubled, but in [[truth|reality]] she was in far from a comfortable state of mind. Only too well she knew that if she failed to produce the [[jewel|Crandall jewels]] it would only be a case of her word against that of Mary Mason.

[024-009] “If I just knew where to hunt!” she told herself anxiously.

[024-010] She was soberly contemplating the work before her when Captain Dudley came to the door to inform her that the motorboat had been brought to the dock.

[024-011] “You can go aboard her now,” he told her.

[024-012] “I insist upon being present when the search is made,” Mary put in angrily.

[024-013] “Very well,” the captain said, after a brief hesitation. “I’ll give you every opportunity to prove your story.”

[024-014] Soberly, Nancy Drew followed the captain to the motorboat, while Mary, guarded by the naval officer, brought up the rear.

[024-015] “Perhaps I’d better keep all of the bystanders away,” the captain suggested to Nancy.

[024-016] “By all [[circumstance|means]].”

[024-017] Not without misgiving, she stepped aboard the boat and entered [[the-cabin|the cabin]]. Fortunately, everything was as it had been left at the time of [[automobile-accident|the accident]], for the rain had extinguished the fire as quickly as it had started. Critically, Nancy Drew surveyed the room, wondering where to begin the search.

[024-018] “Well, produce the Crandall jewels if you know where they are!” Mary brought out triumphantly.

[024-019] Nancy did not make a response, but began to open the drawers of the table. As she had half-expected, she found nothing. Undaunted, she made the rounds of the walls, tapping upon them sharply with her knuckles. To her disappointment, there was no indication of a hollow behind any of the panels.

[024-020] “Didn’t I tell you?” Mary demanded of the captain. “Now will you let me go?”

[024-021] “I’m not through searching yet,” Nancy said sharply. “I know the jewels are [[secret|hidden]] here somewhere.”

[024-022] “Take all the time you need,” Captain Dudley told her kindly.

[024-023] Again Nancy Drew surveyed the room. She could not admit defeat, and yet she was at her wit’s end.

[024-024] As her eyes roamed over the walls again, her attention was attracted to a round-faced wall clock which had stopped at the hour of twelve. The timepiece was very ordinary in appearance, and Nancy might not have given it as much as a second glance had she not chanced to look toward Mary Mason.

[024-025] The girl was staring fixedly at the clock and for one fleeting instant there was an expression of stark [[fear|terror]] in her smoldering eyes. The next moment she looked away from the wall indifferently, but Nancy Drew was not to be deceived.

[024-026] She rushed eagerly over to the clock. It was far above her head, but by mounting upon the cot she found that she could reach it.

[024-027] “Well, of all the silly things!” Mary burst out, but there was a tremor to her voice.

[024-028] Quickly, Nancy reached up and opened the glass door of the clock. The cardboard face did not appear to be securely in place, and upon impulse she began to pry at it with her fingers.

[024-029] “Here’s my knife,” Captain Dudley offered.

[024-030] “Thank you, that’s just what I need.”

[024-031] Nancy took the knife and pried off two tiny screws. The face then dropped down into her hands.

[024-032] “Oh!” she cried. “The secret compartment! I’ve found it!”

[024-033] She had expected to view the “works” of the clock, but instead beheld a round metal box which fit snugly into the wall. The clock was only a clever sham. To her delight, she found that the metal box could be removed from the wall.

[024-034] Placing it on the table, Nancy surveyed Mary Mason triumphantly.

[024-035] “I guess this proves my story, doesn’t it?”

[024-036] She fumbled with the catch on the box and lifted the lid. There before her was an array of jewels such as she had never viewed before in her life. Brilliant diamonds mounted in old-fashioned rings and quaint bracelets. Pendants of rubies and broaches of sapphires. For a moment, Nancy Drew was so dazzled by the display that she could only stare open-mouthed.

[024-037] “My word!” Captain Dudley exclaimed, breaking the silence. “What a collection!”

[024-038] “They belong to [[emily-crandall|Emily Crandall]], a [[friendship|friend]] of mine,” Nancy explained. “I’m afraid some of the jewels are missing.”

[024-039] Captain Dudley turned sternly upon Mary.

[024-040] “What have you to say for yourself now, young lady?”

[024-041] Mary’s arrogant air had fallen completely from her. She looked crushed and beaten.

[024-042] “Well, I guess you have the goods on me,” she admitted with a shrug of her shoulders.

[024-043] “Do you admit that Miss Drew’s story is true?”

[024-044] “Yes, I stole the jewels.”

[024-045] “What have you done with the ones that are missing?” Nancy broke in.

[024-046] “Out with it!” Captain Dudley commanded sharply. “It will go harder with you if you try to keep anything back.”

[024-047] “We pawned several of the diamonds,” Mary admitted grudgingly.

[024-048] “Where?” Nancy demanded.

[024-049] “At a pawnshop [[tom|Tom]] Tozzle knows about in [[winchester|Winchester]]. It’s a place on Bond Street.”

[024-050] “I know the locality,” Nancy told the captain. “It’s possible that we’ll be able to recover the jewels. I believe it’s a law that pawnbrokers must not accept stolen goods.”

[024-051] “The first thing to do is to land our prisoners in jail,” the captain suggested. “If you’ll take [[accusation|charge]] of the jewels, Miss Drew, I’ll step out and call the police.”

[024-052] Within fifteen minutes [[police|the authorities]] had arrived at the dock and the three prisoners were handcuffed and hustled into the patrol wagon.

[024-053] “We’ve been on the watch for this jane nearly a year,” one of the policemen told Nancy. “She’s wanted for half a dozen smaller thefts. You’ve done a good night’s work, young lady.”

[024-054] “I live in [[river-heights|River Heights]],” Nancy returned, with a smile. “If you need me to testify, I’ll be at your service.”

[024-055] After the patrol wagon had departed, the crowd began to disperse. For the first time Nancy Drew realized that the hour was late.

[024-056] “Why, it’s after four o’clock,” she said in astonishment, as she glanced at her wrist watch.

[024-057] “Won’t you do me the honor of taking breakfast at my home?” Captain Dudley asked. “My wife will be delighted.”

[024-058] “At this hour?” Nancy laughed. She shook her head. “No, I must get back to River Heights just as quickly as I can. Poor Mrs. Willoughby is under [[suspicion]] for the [[theft]] of the Crandall jewels and the police intend to arrest her.”

[024-059] “Then of course I won’t try to hold you here. When you see your father, give him my kindest regards.”

[024-060] “I will,” Nancy promised. “And now, if someone will tell me where I can get a taxicab—”

[024-061] “I’ll be glad to drive you to River Heights in my car,” the naval officer volunteered. “If you intend to take those jewels with you, you’ll need someone to go along as a guard.”

[024-062] “Perhaps you are right. I shall be delighted to accept your kind offer.”

[024-063] During the night [[the-storm|the storm]] had blown itself out, and as Nancy Drew stepped into the automobile she noticed that the sky had cleared. Gazing toward the east, she was surprised to see that the sun was about to peep over the horizon.

[024-064] “Ho-hum,” she yawned sleepily. “It’s been a terrible night, but I wouldn’t have missed it for anything! I only hope I get home safely with these jewels. As soon as I deliver them, I’m going to bed and sleep a month!”
