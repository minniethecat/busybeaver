# Jane Willoughby

## Overview

Jane Willoughby is a young widow serving as the guardian of Emily Crandall. Described by Emily as "a dear" (001-034), Mrs. Willoughby occupies a position of significant trust: she is the custodian of the famous Crandall family jewels, held in a bank vault until Emily comes of age. Her decision to remove the jewels from the bank vault — and the theft that follows — provides the inciting incident for the novel's central mystery.

## Background

Mrs. Willoughby is characterized as a young widow who has taken on the responsibility of raising Emily Crandall. Nancy Drew is informed that Emily lives "under the guardianship of Mrs. Jane Willoughby, a young widow" (001-011). Despite the financial responsibilities she carries, Mrs. Willoughby is portrayed as someone Emily genuinely likes: "I always liked Mrs. Willoughby" (001-038).

She maintains a friendship with Mrs. Clara Potter, with whom she socializes and travels. The two are frequently mentioned together, including at the fateful luncheon at Lilac Inn.

## Role in the Mystery

Mrs. Willoughby's pivotal role stems from her management of the Crandall jewels. She removes the jewels from the bank vault, intending to transfer them to a deposit box in another town (004-019). Nancy, learning of this, reflects: "I wish now I'd told Mrs. Willoughby to leave them in the bank vault" (004-027). The decision proves consequential — the jewels are stolen during the lunch at Lilac Inn.

The narrative registers some ambivalence about Mrs. Willoughby's judgment: "it does seem to me that Mrs. Willoughby is a trifle careless" (004-030). Yet she is never portrayed as complicit in the theft; rather, she is a victim of circumstance whose moment of carelessness enables the crime.

At Lilac Inn, Mrs. Willoughby exhibits visible anxiety. She "cast a quick glance about" (005-004) upon arrival, and the narrator notes the "nervous manner in which Mrs. Willoughby clutched her handbag" (005-007). When the theft is discovered, her distress is immediate: she whispers urgently to Mrs. Potter, and the subsequent scene depicts her shock.

## Key Relationships

- **Emily Crandall** — Her ward, the heir to the jewels she was safeguarding
- **Mrs. Clara Potter** — Her close friend and companion at Lilac Inn
- **Nancy Drew** — The investigator who works to recover the stolen jewels
- **The Crandall Jewels** — The family heirlooms placed in her custody
