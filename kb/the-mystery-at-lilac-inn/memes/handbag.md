# Handbag

The handbag carried by Mrs. Jane Willoughby is a significant object in the mystery at Lilac Inn. It contained the valuable Crandall jewels and became the target of a theft that launched Nancy Drew's investigation.

## Description

Mrs. Willoughby's handbag was notably large and drew attention from those around her. Nancy "glanced curiously at the large handbag which rested upon the table" (005-021), noting its conspicuous size. Mrs. Willoughby "nervously clutched a handbag which she carried" (005-004), her anxious behavior drawing further notice.

## The Jewels

The handbag's significance lay in its contents. Nancy realized "that the jewels are in the handbag. After all, the bag is a beautiful hiding place" (005-021). Mrs. Willoughby was carrying the precious Crandall jewels inside it, making the handbag the central object of value at the inn that day.

## Suspicion and Theft

Other diners took notice of the handbag. Nancy observed a stranger: "the way he kept looking at her handbag" (005-022), suspecting criminal intent. Mrs. Willoughby herself was aware of the danger, declaring: "I shall keep my eye on that handbag every minute" (005-030).

Despite her vigilance, the theft occurred. When the commotion subsided, "there was no sign of the handbag on the floor" (005-054). A search of the area was fruitless: "the missing handbag was not revealed" (005-061). Mrs. Willoughby cried out in distress: "My handbag! Someone stole my handbag!" (006-003), explaining: "When I rushed back to the table my handbag was gone" (006-005).

## Significance

The theft of the handbag and the Crandall jewels within it is the inciting incident that drives the entire mystery. Every subsequent investigation, accusation, and discovery stems from this single act of theft at Lilac Inn.
