# Emily Crandall

## Overview

Emily Crandall is Nancy Drew's friend and the heir to the famous Crandall family jewels. She is described as having "candid blue eyes" and a beauty "which was the envy of her friends" (001-010). Her impending inheritance becomes the catalyst for the central mystery of the novel.

## Background

Emily is the daughter of a young widow and "had never had a great deal of money" (001-011) despite her family's famous jewels. She is on the verge of turning eighteen and celebrating her birthday, marking her transition to adulthood and her legal right to the Crandall inheritance (002-036).

## Role in the Mystery

Emily's inheritance of the Crandall jewels draws Nancy Drew into the case. After the jewels are stolen, Emily is devastated, having "counted on her inheritance for everything" (007-013). Nancy's determination to recover the jewels is driven in part by her loyalty to her friend.

## Key Relationships

- **Nancy Drew** — Emily's close friend and the detective investigating the theft
- **Mrs. Willoughby** — Emily's guardian, entrusted with the jewels when they were stolen
