# Telephone

The telephone is a recurring communication device in the mystery at Lilac Inn, used by Nancy Drew and other characters to share information, coordinate activities, and advance the investigation.

## Nancy's Use

Nancy frequently relied on the telephone during her detective work. At Lilac Inn, she told Emily: "Wait for me for a minute, I'll make a telephone call. I must let Dad know where I am" (001-058). She then "hurried away, sought a telephone booth and quickly put in a call" (001-059), demonstrating the importance of staying connected during her travels.

## Investigative Role

Throughout the mystery, telephone calls serve as conduits for critical information. Nancy used the telephone to relay findings to her father, Carson Drew, and to coordinate with other allies. The ability to make calls from various locations — inns, homes, public booths — allowed her investigation to proceed efficiently across the region.
