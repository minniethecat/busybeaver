# Theft

## Overview

The theft of the Crandall jewels is the central crime in *The Mystery at Lilac Inn*. The jewels are stolen from Mrs. Willoughby's handbag during a luncheon at Lilac Inn (005-004), setting in motion a complex investigation that reveals a web of deception and impersonation.

## The Crime Scene

The theft occurs at Lilac Inn, a country inn situated between River Heights and Melville. Mrs. Willoughby and Mrs. Potter stop for luncheon while carrying the valuable Crandall jewels (005-005). The theft is discovered after their meal, when Mrs. Willoughby realizes her handbag has been tampered with (005-007).

## The Investigation

Nancy Drew immediately takes up the case upon learning of the theft. She questions the inn's staff and examines the circumstances surrounding the crime. Nancy suspects that the theft may have been staged as part of a larger scheme (007-039).

## The Aftermath

The theft creates widespread concern among those connected to the Crandall family. Nancy's investigation reveals that the crime is more complex than a simple robbery, involving premeditation and possibly multiple conspirators. The pressure to recover the jewels intensifies as the investigation progresses.

## Resolution

Through persistent detective work, Nancy ultimately identifies the thief and recovers the jewels. Her success demonstrates her skill as an investigator and restores the Crandall family's prized inheritance.
