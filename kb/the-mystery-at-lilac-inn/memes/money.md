# Money

Money and financial considerations play a significant role in the mystery at Lilac Inn, from the value of the stolen Crandall jewels to the economic circumstances of various characters and the ransom demands made during the investigation.

## The Value of the Jewels

The Crandall jewels represent a substantial financial asset. References to their worth appear throughout the story, with the conspirators motivated by the prospect of converting them to money. The jewels were described as being worth a considerable sum, making their theft a serious crime.

## Economic Circumstances

Several characters' financial situations influence the plot. Emily Crandall "had never had a great deal of money" (001-011), living under the modest guardianship of Mrs. Willoughby. Mary Mason's employment circumstances and her brother's involvement in crime were driven by financial desperation.

## The Stolen Money

Beyond the jewels themselves, stolen money factored into the criminal activities. The conspirators handled transactions involving significant amounts. Nancy's investigation uncovered financial trails that helped identify the perpetrators and their motivations.
