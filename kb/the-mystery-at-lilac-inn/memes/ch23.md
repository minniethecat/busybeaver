# XXIII: Captured


## XXIII

[023-001] Captured

[023-002] As [[nancy-drew|Nancy Drew]] cried out in [[alarm]], several persons broke from the crowd on the dock and ran after [[tom|Tom]] Tozzle. They chased him across the road and were rapidly gaining on him when he slipped behind a group of old shacks and vanished.

[023-003] “Oh, I hope they get him!” Nancy murmured.

[023-004] Since her [[rescue]] from the ill-fated motorboat, events had transpired so quickly that she had not had time to think of [[mary-mason|Mary Mason]] and her brother. Her first thought had been to save the [[crandall-jewels|Crandall jewels]]. Now, as she pushed her way through the crowd, she wondered if the two had managed to escape.

[023-005] It was therefore with [[relief]] that she caught a glimpse of them as the light from a lantern fell full upon their faces. They were being questioned by a man who wore a naval officer’s uniform.

[023-006] Nancy guessed from what she had overheard while still a prisoner in [[the-cabin|the cabin]], that they had been captured the moment they leaped from the motorboat. Undoubtedly, her own cries for help had made their hasty departure appear suspicious. She must accost them before they told an improvised story which might gain them release. Eagerly, she pushed forward.

[023-007] “It may be as you say,” she heard the naval officer tell them, “but I can’t let you go until the [[police-department|police]] come.”

[023-008] “The police!” Mary fairly shrieked.

[023-009] With a vicious jerk of her arm she freed herself from the retaining grasp and broke through the crowd, striking out furiously at those who would have blocked her path. Her action was a signal for [[bud-mason|Bud]] to do likewise. Before the astonished officer realized what was going on, he wriggled free and ran in the opposite direction from that Mary had taken.

[023-010] “Stop them!” Nancy shouted. “They’re both thieves! Don’t let them get away!”

[023-011] A few of the persons on the dock made a halfhearted attempt to stop the two, but for the most part they were too astonished to realize what it all meant. Fortunately, the naval officer was quick to recover himself and darted through the crowd after Bud.

[023-012] Nancy did not stand idle. Quick as a flash, she was after Mary. The girl had the start of her by a dozen yards, but Nancy was an excellent runner, and in this instance she was spurred on by righteous anger.

[023-013] Mary cast an anxious glance over her shoulder and saw that she was being overtaken. Bending her head low, she increased her speed, throwing every iota of her strength into the mad race for freedom. Nancy ran easily, but she too doubled her efforts.

[023-014] Mary reached the road, but there she was overtaken. Nancy reached out and grasped her by the shoulder, but the girl managed to squirm away. She darted off again, but a dozen long strides brought Nancy even with her. This time she did not attempt to hold her, but, getting a trifle ahead, tripped her up. As Mary went sprawling on the ground, she emitted a cry of rage.

[023-015] “I guess the tables are turned this time,” Nancy observed dryly.

[023-016] Mary gave her a glance of hatred and tried to scramble to her feet.

[023-017] “Oh, no you won’t,” Nancy said, and promptly sat down on her.

[023-018] She was not required to maintain such strict vigilance, for just then several men ran up to take [[accusation|charge]] of the girl.

[023-019] “What’s the idea?” one of them demanded. “Has she stolen something?”

[023-020] “No, I haven’t!” Mary spit out. “Let me go or I’ll have you all arrested for this outrage!”

[023-021] “Keep a close watch on her,” Nancy directed calmly. “And will someone please call the police?”

[023-022] Assured that there was no [[danger]] of Mary’s making another break for freedom, she hurried to the dock to [[discovery|find]] out what had become of Bud. To her relief, she saw that the naval officer had collared him and was dragging him back by main force.

[023-023] “We have them all now except Tom Tozzle!” Nancy exclaimed.

[023-024] “They’ve caught him too,” someone in the crowd observed.

[023-025] A moment later two men came up with the riverman in custody. From his battered appearance it was obvious that there had been a scuffle and that he had not fared particularly well.

[023-026] “The fellow’s a tough customer,” one of the men who had captured him observed. “We cornered him in a shed.”

[023-027] “Have you charges to prefer against these three persons?” the naval officer questioned, turning politely to Nancy.

[023-028] “Indeed, I have! They are [[criminal|criminals]] of the worst sort. First they stole forty thousand dollars’ worth of jewels from a [[friendship|friend]] of mine and—”

[023-029] “It’s a lie! All a lie!” Mary Mason broke in.

[023-030] “And I happen to know that they are wanted for a number of smaller thefts,” Nancy continued, without paying the slightest attention to the interruption. “Then when I discovered what they were about, they made me a prisoner and took me aboard their boat, bound hand and foot. When we were rammed by the yacht, they left me to drown.”

[023-031] “That’s so.” The two men who had rescued Nancy confirmed this statement. “When we found her she was tied up all right.”

[023-032] “The motorboat was running without lights,” a quiet voice put in. “Otherwise my yacht wouldn’t have run her down.”

[023-033] Everyone turned and saw that an elderly, dignified man in uniform had joined the group. Nancy instantly recognized him as the captain of the yacht.

[023-034] “This is [[captain-dudley|Captain Dudley]],” the naval officer said by way of introduction. “There isn’t a more careful man on [[the-river|the river]]. You can depend on his word.”

[023-035] For the benefit of Captain Dudley, Nancy repeated her story, but when she had finished, Mary Mason again denied the accusations made against her.

[023-036] “It’s all a trumped-up story,” she repeated. “It’s true she was tied up and gagged, but for a very good reason. We caught her trying to steal our jewels!”

[023-037] “That’s right!” Bud agreed quickly.

[023-038] Nancy Drew was aghast at the statement. For a moment she was so taken aback that she could not say a word, and Mary was quick to press her advantage.

[023-039] “It’s only her word against the three of us,” she declared boldly. “Let her prove her story if she can.”

[023-040] Captain Dudley glanced at Nancy with troubled eyes.

[023-041] “Can you do that?” he asked.

[023-042] “Certainly I can if you give me time,” Nancy announced quietly. “I am sure the police will have a record—”

[023-043] “Time!” Mary fairly screamed. “She wants time so she can get away!”

[023-044] “Be calm, please,” the captain ordered. “We’ll thrash this thing out.”

[023-045] “If her story is true, let her produce the jewels she claims we stole!” Mary continued.

[023-046] She cast a triumphant glance at Nancy, feeling that she had scored heavily.

[023-047] “I’m afraid I can’t do that,” Nancy admitted reluctantly. “The jewels were in the motorboat, and it sank to the bottom of the river.”

[023-048] “That’s just an excuse,” Mary retorted. “The jewels never were in the boat.”

[023-049] “We’ll have a way of proving whether or not your story is true,” Captain Dudley said quietly. “As it happens the motorboat isn’t on the bottom of the river.”

[023-050] “What?” Mary gasped. For the moment she was completely taken aback.

[023-051] “Thanks to the timely suggestion of this young lady you are accusing, my men slung grappling hooks into the boat and we managed to keep her afloat.”

[023-052] “Oh, I’m so glad!” Nancy exclaimed in relief.

[023-053] “All right,” Mary said viciously. “Let her find the jewels if she claims they’re on board. That’s all I’ve got to say.”

[023-054] “You can do that, Miss—” the captain hesitated as he remembered that he had not heard Nancy’s name.

[023-055] “Drew,” she supplied automatically.

[023-056] “Drew!” Captain Dudley repeated in astonishment. “You’re not any relation to [[carson-drew|Carson Drew]], by any [[luck|chance]]?”

[023-057] “He is my father.”

[023-058] “Jove! I know him well!”

[023-059] He turned to the crowd with decision.

[023-060] “Gentlemen, I can vouch for this girl. Her father is a famous criminal lawyer in [[river-heights|River Heights]].”

[023-061] The captain’s words had an effect upon the bystanders, and it was obvious that they were again swinging over to Nancy’s side. “Call the police,” they began to murmur.

[023-062] “You’re not giving us a fair [[opportunity|chance]],” Mary insisted, in her most injured manner. “All we ask is that Nancy Drew produce the jewels.”

[023-063] “Perhaps the matter can be settled more quickly if you do,” the captain suggested to Nancy. “Of course you know where to find them.”

[023-064] “Yes,” Nancy murmured uncomfortably, “I think so.”

[023-065] She knew by the pleased look that Mary Mason shot her that the girl felt she had won her battle.

[023-066] “She thinks I shan’t be able to find the jewels,” she told herself grimly. “And maybe I shan’t!”

[023-067] Though she confidently believed that the [[jewel|Crandall jewels]] were [[secret|hidden]] somewhere inside the cabin of the motorboat, she was not certain that such was the case. Even if they were, she might not be able to find the hidden compartment. If so, she would stand condemned in the eyes of the crowd, and before she could prove the [[truth]] of her story, Mary Mason and her companions would manage to slip away.

[023-068] “I’ve got to find those jewels!” she told herself. “Everything depends upon it now!”
