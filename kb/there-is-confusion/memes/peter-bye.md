# Peter Bye

**Peter Bye** is the male protagonist of [[jessie-redmon-fauset|Jessie Redmon Fauset]]'s *There Is Confusion* (1924). A young Black man of striking beauty and inherited talent, Peter spends much of the novel adrift — haunted by the failed ambition of his father, [[meriwether-bye|Meriwether Bye]], and unable to commit either to a career or to the woman who [[marriage-in-there-is-confusion|love]]s him, [[joanna-marshall|Joanna Marshall]]. His eventual transformation into a distinguished surgeon is one of the novel's central arcs.

## Character and Appearance

Peter is introduced through [[joanna-marshall|Joanna]]'s appraising gaze:

> "Peter Bye, in spite of the dark arresting beauty which first drew Joanna's glance to him across the other white and pink faces in the crowded schoolroom, was undoubtedly shiftless. 'Not lazy,' Joanna said to herself, looking at him from under level brows before she dismissed him forever from her busy mind."

"Dark arresting beauty" — the phrase recurs. Peter's physical presence attracts; his lack of "fixity of purpose" repels. This tension between promise and performance defines his character throughout the novel.

## The Weight of Inheritance

Peter's story cannot be understood apart from his father. [[meriwether-bye|Meriwether Bye]] was a man of medical training who squandered his gift in drink and despair, leaving Peter with nothing but a Family Bible and a name freighted with expectation. [[isaiah-bye|Isaiah Bye]], Peter's grandfather, passed down a "tradition that had become warped, that had gone astray and had carried Peter and Peter Bye's father along in its general wreckage."

The novel traces how three generations of deferred and twisted ambition converge in Peter — and how he finally breaks the pattern.

## Joanna Marshall

[[joanna-marshall|Joanna]] is the catalyst. Her own fierce ambition — to become "the greatest singer in the world" — alternately attracts and repels Peter. She demands that he *become* someone:

> "'Both of us know it, Peter. And it's up to us, to you and me, Peter Bye, to show them we can stick to our last as well as anybody else.'"

[[joanna-marshall|Joanna]] refuses to marry a man without purpose. Her ultimatum — become a real doctor, not just a man with inherited medical knowledge — is the turning point of Peter's life.

## The War and Dr. Meriwether Bye

[[world-war-i|World War I]] sends Peter to France, where he meets the white [[meriwether-bye]], his father's namesake. Their battlefield friendship is transformative:

> "The young physician's words and ideas had exercised a singleness of concentration, of influence over Peter such as a friendship of long standing could hardly have hoped to achieve."

When [[meriwether-bye|Meriwether Bye]] is killed, Peter carries his body from No Man's Land — and in doing so, symbolically reclaims the name his father had disgraced.

## The Surgeon

After the war, Peter completes his medical training and becomes "Dr. Bye, the famous surgeon." [[philip-marshall|Phil]]adelphia society murmurs: "'That's Peter Bye, the distinguished surgeon! His wife is unusual, too, she was [[joanna-marshall|Joanna Marshall]].'" The shiftless boy has become a man of achievement — and [[joanna-marshall|Joanna]] finally consents to marry him.

At the novel's close, Peter names his son Meriwether and declares: "I'm going to try to finish up [[meriwether-bye|Meriwether Bye]]'s life, to be the man that he would have been."
