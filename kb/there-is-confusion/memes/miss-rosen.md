# Miss Rosen

Miss Rosen is a minor character in [[jessie-redmon-fauset|Jessie Redmon Fauset]]'s 1924 novel *There Is Confusion*, serving as the secretary of the dance performance committee that evaluates [[joanna-marshall]]'s audition for a public recital.

## The Audition Committee

Miss Rosen appears in a single extended scene when [[joanna-marshall|Joanna Marshall]] auditions before a committee assembled to judge her suitability for a dance performance. [[vera-manning|Vera Sharples]] Manning introduces [[joanna-marshall|Joanna]] to the room, and Miss Rosen is one of two women present alongside [[miss-phelps|Miss Phelps]]:

> the two other women in the room — a Miss Rosen and a Miss Phelps as they turned out to be — bowed also noncommittally but without hostility [3457-CCC].

Her demeanor is professional and reserved — "noncommittally but without hostility" captures the committee's cautious neutrality toward an untested performer.

As the committee's secretary, Miss Rosen handles the practical arrangements. When [[joanna-marshall|Joanna]]'s performance wins over the room, [[mr-hale|Mr. Hale]] delegates the logistics to her:

> "I'll leave you with Miss Sharples and Miss Rosen, our secretary, to make final arrangements, while Francis and I go out to see what we can do about taking on these kids. I suppose you'll need them" [3485-CCC].

Miss Rosen demonstrates efficiency in the role, departing with [[mr-hale|Mr. Hale]] and returning "in fifteen minutes with ten colored children, of every type and shade, black and brown and yellow" [3483-CCC] — the children [[joanna-marshall|Joanna]] will need for her performance.

## The Mask Crisis

When [[joanna-marshall|Joanna]]'s performance is threatened by a crisis — a white audience member's objection to a colored dancer appearing unmasked — Miss Rosen participates in the committee's emergency response:

> Mr. Hale, Miss Sharples, Francis, Miss Rosen and Miss Phelps held a hurried consultation behind the scenes. "There's nothing to be done," Hale said, "quick, off with your mask, Miss Marshall" [3509-CCC].

As a committee member, Miss Rosen is part of the institutional apparatus that both constrains and enables [[joanna-marshall|Joanna]]'s career — the gatekeepers who must be won over and who, once won, become advocates.

## Critical Significance

Miss Rosen, like [[miss-phelps|Miss Phelps]], is one of the novel's functional characters — present to populate the institutional landscape through which [[joanna-marshall|Joanna]] must navigate her career. As secretary of the audition committee, she represents the professional infrastructure that supports (and judges) artistic aspiration in the colored community.
