# Alice Graves

Alice Graves is a minor but structurally significant character in [[jessie-redmon-fauset|Jessie Redmon Fauset]]'s 1924 novel *There Is Confusion*. As the mother of [[peter-bye|Peter Bye]] — one of the novel's central characters — she links the Graves family of Gravestown, New Jersey, to the [[family-in-there-is-confusion|Bye family]] line that traces back to the Revolutionary War.

## Character

Unlike her older sister Susan, Alice Graves was "not as direct in purpose as Susan, yet in some respects curiously strong" (005-009). She rebelled against the Graves family tradition of endogamy — the expectation that one should marry within the Graves clan. "She had always considered the Graves' tradition silly: it was so unexciting marrying someone whom you had known and seen all your life. What was [[marriage-in-there-is-confusion|marriage]] for if not for a change?" (005-009).

This independence of mind led her to make choices her sister would never have contemplated. When the oldest son of Merchant Sharples of Girard Avenue married and relocated to New York, Susan went along as housekeeper — and "thither Alice Graves followed shortly to do sewing for that intricate but orderly household" (005-010).

## Marriage to Meriwether Bye

In New York, Alice met [[meriwether-bye|Meriwether Bye]], whom both sisters had known in [[philip-marshall|Phil]]adelphia through his family connections. Meriwether, "in his rare fits of loneliness, and between runs courted Alice Graves in Central Park" (005-010). The [[marriage-in-there-is-confusion|courtship]] led to [[marriage-in-there-is-confusion|marriage]], and Alice became the mother of [[peter-bye|Peter Bye]].

[[susan-graves|Susan Graves]] "resigned herself easily to the matter — for Bye belonged to old stock and must, she thought, make good eventually." However, "she developed a strong dislike for him before his death, and took Peter not only for his mother's sake but also to dispel if possible his father's doubtless harmful influence" (005-010).

## Legacy

Though Alice Graves appears only briefly in the novel's genealogy, her significance is considerable. Through her [[marriage-in-there-is-confusion|marriage]] to [[meriwether-bye|Meriwether Bye]] and her son Peter, she connects two distinguished African American family lines — the Graves family of New Jersey and the [[family-in-there-is-confusion|Bye family]] of [[philip-marshall|Phil]]adelphia. Her rejection of family endogamy in favor of romantic choice also introduces the theme of individual desire versus family tradition that recurs throughout the novel.
