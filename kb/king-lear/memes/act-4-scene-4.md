# Act 4 Scene 4: Cordelia's Search

[[cordelia|Cordelia]] returns at the head of a French army not for conquest but for restoration — to find and heal her mad father [[king-lear|Lear]]. This brief scene is the play's pivot from suffering toward the fragile possibility of redemption.

## Summary

[[cordelia|Cordelia]] enters with drum and colours, leading French soldiers onto English soil. She describes [[king-lear|Lear]] as she has heard reports of him — "mad as the vext Sea, singing alowd, / Crown'd with ranke Fenitar, and furrow weeds" (004-270). She immediately sends a centuria to search every acre of the high-grown fields and bring Lear to her. Consulting a doctor, she asks what human wisdom can do to restore Lear's "bereaved sense." The doctor prescribes rest — "Our foster Nurse of Nature, is repose" (004-275) — along with medicinal simples. Cordelia offers everything she has to whoever can heal her father. The scene closes with news of the approaching British forces, but Cordelia's purpose remains clear: she came to England not for ambition but for love.
