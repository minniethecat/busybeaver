# Act 3 Scene 5: Edmund Made Earl

[[cornwall-duke-of-cornwall|Cornwall]] rewards [[edmund|Edmund]]'s betrayal of his father by making him the new [[earl-of-gloucester|Earl of Gloucester]] — the climax of [[edmund|Edmund]]'s campaign to supplant his legitimate brother [[edgar|Edgar]] and usurp his father's title  .

## Summary

[[cornwall-duke-of-cornwall|Cornwall]] enters with [[edmund|Edmund]] and declares he will have his revenge before departing (003-303). [[edmund|Edmund]], performing reluctance, says he fears being censured for letting "Nature thus giues way to Loyaltie" — turning against his own father (003-304). Cornwall assures him it was not purely [[edgar|Edgar]]'s evil disposition that made him seek Gloucester's death, but "a prouoking merit set a-worke by a reprouable badnesse in himselfe" (003-305). [[edmund|Edmund]] presents the letter that proves [[earl-of-gloucester|Gloucester]] an "intelligent partie to the aduantages of France" (003-305). Cornwall declares that true or false, the letter has made [[edmund|Edmund]] Earl of Gloucester, and orders him to find his father for arrest. [[edmund|Edmund]], in an aside, promises to persevere in his course of "Loyalty" to [[cornwall-duke-of-cornwall|Cornwall]], though the conflict be sore between that and his blood (003-305).
