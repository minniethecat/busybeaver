# Act 5 Scene 1: Before the Battle

The final act opens in the British camp, where [[edmund-bastard-son-of-gloucester|Edmund]]'s web of sexual and political treachery tightens, [[duke-of-albany|Albany]] reveals his divided loyalties, and [[edgar|Edgar]] sets in motion the trial by combat that will determine the play's outcome.

## Summary

[[edmund-bastard-son-of-gloucester|Edmund]] sends an officer to learn [[duke-of-albany|Albany]]'s battle plans, noting Albany's tendency toward self-doubt and alteration. [[regan|Regan]] arrives and immediately questions Edmund about his relationship with [[goneril|Goneril]], demanding the truth: "Do you not loue my Sister?" Edmund denies any intimacy. [[goneril|Goneril]] enters and the two sisters trade veiled barbs. [[duke-of-albany|Albany]] enters and declares his position: he will fight the French invasion, but not against [[king-lear|Lear]] and [[cordelia|Cordelia]] unless they actively support the invader. [[edgar|Edgar]], still disguised, enters and hands Albany a letter, instructing him to sound a trumpet before the battle if he wins — a champion will appear to prove the letter's contents. After Edgar exits, [[edmund-bastard-son-of-gloucester|Edmund]] soliloquises about his plan to have Lear and Cordelia killed if the British win.
