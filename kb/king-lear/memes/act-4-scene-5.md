# Act 4 Scene 5: Regan's Jealousy

The rivalry between [[goneril|Goneril]] and [[regan|Regan]] over [[edmund-bastard-son-of-gloucester|Edmund]] surfaces openly in this tense scene, as Regan interrogates [[oswald|Oswald]] and stakes her claim — the sisters' competition for Edmund will prove as destructive as their competition for [[king-lear|Lear]]'s land.

## Summary

[[regan|Regan]] questions [[oswald|Oswald]] about [[goneril|Goneril]]'s letter to [[edmund-bastard-son-of-gloucester|Edmund]]. She reveals that Edmund has departed on serious business — hunting down the blinded [[earl-of-gloucester|Gloucester]] — but Regan believes he should instead be commanding her own forces. She openly declares that Edmund is more suited to her than to Goneril, and demands that Oswald show her Goneril's letter. When Oswald refuses, Regan gives him her own token to deliver to Edmund, warning him to advise Edmund that Goneril is a "better Souldier" but a worse choice. The scene ends with Regan urging Oswald to report that Gloucester's survival was a mistake that needs correcting.
