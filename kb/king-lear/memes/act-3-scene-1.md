# Act 3 Scene 1: Kent Finds the King

On a storm-lashed heath, [[kent-earl-of-kent|Kent]] reveals to a [[gentleman-messenger|Gentleman]] the political fracture between [[duke-of-albany|Albany]] and [[duke-of-cornwall|Cornwall]], and sends a secret token to [[cordelia|Cordelia]] — the first glimmer of hope for [[king-lear|Lear]]'s cause since the play's opening  .

## Summary

Amid a violent storm, [[kent-earl-of-kent|Kent]] meets a [[gentleman-messenger|Gentleman]] and asks after the King (003-003). The Gentleman reports that Lear is contending with the elements, bidding the wind to blow the earth into the sea, accompanied only by the [[the-fool|Fool]] who labors to out-jest his heart-struck injuries (003-006). [[kent-earl-of-kent|Kent]], trusting the Gentleman, reveals a closely guarded secret: there is a hidden division between [[duke-of-albany|Albany]] and [[duke-of-cornwall|Cornwall]], and French spies have gathered intelligence of England's state (003-015). Kent gives the Gentleman his purse and a ring, instructing him to show it to [[cordelia|Cordelia]] when he sees her — she will identify the ring's owner (003-028). The two men part to search for Lear in opposite directions, agreeing the first to find him will halloo the other.

> "Contending with the fretfull Elements; / Bids the winde blow the Earth into the Sea, / Or swell the curled Waters 'boue the Maine, / That things might change, or cease" (003-006)

> "There is diuision / (Although as yet the face of it is couer'd / With mutuall cunning) 'twixt Albany, and Cornwall" (003-015)
