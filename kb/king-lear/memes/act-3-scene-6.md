# Act 3 Scene 6: The Mock Trial

One of the most audacious scenes in Shakespeare — a mad king stages a trial of his daughters with a disguised madman and a Fool as judges — unfolds in a hovel while [[earl-of-gloucester|Gloucester]] orchestrates [[king-lear|Lear]]'s escape to [[cordelia|Cordelia]] at Dover.

## Summary

[[earl-of-gloucester|Gloucester]] brings [[king-lear|Lear]], [[kent-earl-of-kent|Kent]], [[the-fool|the Fool]], and [[edgar|Edgar]] (disguised as Poor Tom) to a farmhouse for shelter. Lear, his mind now fully unmoored, convenes an imaginary trial of [[goneril|Goneril]] and [[regan|Regan]] for filial ingratitude. [[the-fool|The Fool]] and [[edgar|Edgar]] serve as judges in this hallucinated court, with Edgar's "Fraterretto" ravings blending with Lear's fierce lucidity. The trial mixes profound insight into injustice with the pathos of madness: "Then let them anatomitze Regan, see what breeds about her heart" (003-450). Gloucester returns with urgent news — he has overheard a plot against Lear's life — and insists they flee immediately. Kent carries the sleeping Lear toward Dover and Cordelia, while Edgar, left briefly alone, reflects that witnessing Lear's suffering has lightened his own.
