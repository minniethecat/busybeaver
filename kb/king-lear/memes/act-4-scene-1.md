# Act 4 Scene 1: Edgar on the Heath

[[edgar|Edgar]], still disguised as Poor Tom, reflects on his fallen state and encounters his blinded father [[earl-of-gloucester|Gloucester]] — the scene in which the son becomes his father's guide (004-002).

## Summary

[[edgar|Edgar]] opens with a soliloquy on acceptance: "Yet better thus, and knowne to be contemn'd, / Then still contemn'd and flatter'd, to be worst" (004-002). He sees his father [[earl-of-gloucester|Gloucester]] being led by an Old Man, freshly blinded by [[cornwall|Cornwall]]'s cruelty. [[earl-of-gloucester|Gloucester]], in despair, asks to be taken to [[dover|Dover]]'s cliffs to end his life. The Old Man, bound by duty to the estate, cannot go further. [[edgar|Edgar]], still disguised as Poor Tom, agrees to guide [[earl-of-gloucester|Gloucester]] — the loyal son leading the father who does not recognize him (004-026).
