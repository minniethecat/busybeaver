[059-001]the brachet, and of his adventure by the way.

[059-002]When Sir Tor was ready, he mounted upon his horseback, and rode after the

[059-003]knight with the brachet. So as he rode he met with a dwarf suddenly that smote

[059-004]his horse on the head with a staff, that he went backward his spear length. Why

[059-005]dost thou so? said Sir Tor. For thou shalt not pass this way, but if thou joust

[059-006]with yonder knights of the pavilions. Then was Tor ware where two pavilions

[059-007]were, and great spears stood out, and two shields hung on trees by the

[059-008]pavilions. I may not tarry, said Sir Tor, for I am in a quest that I must needs

[059-009]follow. Thou shalt not pass, said the dwarf, and therewithal he blew his horn.

[059-010]Then there came one armed on horseback, and dressed his shield, and came fast

[059-011]toward Tor, and he dressed him against him, and so ran together that Tor bare

[059-012]him from his horse. And anon the knight yielded him to his mercy. But, sir, I

[059-013]have a fellow in yonder pavilion that will have ado with you anon. He shall be

[059-014]welcome, said Sir Tor. Then was he ware of another knight coming with great

[059-015]raundon, and each of them dressed to other, that marvel it was to see; but the

[059-016]knight smote Sir Tor a great stroke in midst of the shield that his spear all

[059-017]to-shivered. And Sir Tor smote him through the shield below of the shield that

[059-018]it went through the cost of the knight, but the stroke slew him not. And

[059-019]therewith Sir Tor alighted and smote him on the helm a great stroke, and

[059-020]therewith the knight yielded him and besought him of mercy. I will well, said

[059-021]Sir Tor, but thou and thy fellow must go unto [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], and yield you

[059-022]prisoners unto him. By whom shall we say are we thither sent? Ye shall say by

[059-023]the knight that went in the quest of the knight that went with the brachet.

[059-024]Now, what be your two names? said Sir Tor. My name is, said the one, Sir Felot

[059-025]of Langduk; and my name is, said the other, Sir Petipase of Winchelsea. Now go

[059-026]ye forth, said Sir Tor, and God speed you and me. Then came the dwarf and said

[059-027]unto Sir Tor, I pray you give me a gift. I will well, said Sir Tor, ask. I ask

[059-028]no more, said the dwarf, but that ye will suffer me to do you service, for I

[059-029]will serve no more recreant knights. Take an horse, said Sir Tor, and ride on

[059-030]with me. I wot ye ride after the knight with the white brachet, and I shall

[059-031]bring you where he is, said the dwarf. And so they rode throughout a forest,

[059-032]and at the last they were ware of two pavilions, even by a priory, with two

[059-033]shields, and the one shield was enewed with white, and the other shield was

[059-034]red.
