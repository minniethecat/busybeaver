[089-001]Ettard by means of the Damosel of the Lake, whom he loved ever after.

[089-002]Sir knight Pelleas, said the Damosel of the Lake, take your horse and come

[089-003]forth with me out of this country, and ye shall love a lady that shall love

[089-004]you. I will well, said Sir Pelleas, for this Lady Ettard hath done me great

[089-005]despite and shame, and there he told her the beginning and ending, and how he

[089-006]had purposed never to have arisen till that he had been dead. And now such

[089-007]grace God hath sent me, that I hate her as much as ever I loved her, thanked be

[089-008]our Lord Jesus! Thank me, said the Damosel of the Lake. Anon Sir Pelleas armed

[089-009]him, and took his horse, and commanded his men to bring after his pavilions and

[089-010]his stuff where the Damosel of the Lake would assign. So the Lady Ettard died

[089-011]for sorrow, and the Damosel of the Lake rejoiced Sir Pelleas, and loved

[089-012]together during their life days.
