[188-003]Thus as [[sir-tristram|Sir Tristram]] and La Beale Isoud were in prison, it happed a knight and

[188-004]a lady came unto them where they were, to cheer them. I have marvel, said

[188-005]Tristram unto the knight and the lady, what is the cause the lord of this

[188-006]castle holdeth us in prison: it was never the custom of no place of [[honour|worship]]

[188-007]that ever I came in, when a knight and a lady asked harbour, and they to

[188-008]receive them, and after to destroy them that be his guests. Sir, said the

[188-009]knight, this is the old custom of this castle, that when a knight cometh here

[188-010]he must needs [[battle|fight]] with our lord, and he that is weaker must lose his head.

[188-011]And when that is done, if his lady that he bringeth be fouler than our

[188-012]lord’s wife, she must lose her head: and if she be fairer proved than is

[188-013]our lady, then shall the lady of this castle lose her head. So God me help,

[188-014]said Sir Tristram, this is a foul custom and a shameful. But one advantage have

[188-015]I, said Sir Tristram, I have a lady is fair enough, fairer saw I never in all

[188-016]my life-days, and I doubt not for lack of beauty she shall not lose her head;

[188-017]and rather than I should lose my head I will fight for it on a fair field.

[188-018]Wherefore, sir knight, I pray you tell your lord that I will be ready as

[188-019]to-morn with my lady, and myself to do battle, if it be so I may have my horse

[188-020]and mine armour. Sir, said that knight, I undertake that your desire shall be

[188-021]sped right well. And then he said: Take your rest, and look that ye be up

[188-022]betimes and make you ready and your lady, for ye shall want no thing that you

[188-023]behoveth. And therewith he departed, and on the morn betimes that same knight

[188-024]came to Sir Tristram, and fetched him out and his lady, and brought him horse

[188-025]and armour that was his own, and bade him make him ready to the field, for all

[188-026]the estates and commons of that lordship were there ready to behold that battle

[188-027]and judgment.

[188-028]Then came Sir Breunor, the lord of that castle, with his lady in his hand,

[188-029]muffled, and asked Sir Tristram where was his lady: For an thy lady be fairer

[188-030]than mine, with thy sword smite off my lady’s head; and if my lady be

[188-031]fairer than thine, with my sword I must strike off her head. And if I may win

[188-032]thee, yet shall thy lady be mine, and thou shalt lose thy head. Sir, said

[188-033]Tristram, this is a foul custom and horrible; and rather than my lady should

[188-034]lose her head, yet had I liefer lose my head. Nay, nay, said Sir Breunor, the

[188-035]ladies shall be first showed together, and the one shall have her judgment.

[188-036]Nay, I will not so, said Sir Tristram, for here is none that will give

[188-037]righteous judgment. But I doubt not, said Sir Tristram, my lady is fairer than

[188-038]thine, and that will I prove and make good with my hand. And whosomever he be

[188-039]that will say the contrary I will prove it on his head. And therewith Sir

[188-040]Tristram showed La Beale Isoud, and turned her thrice about with his naked

[188-041]sword in his hand. And when Sir Breunor saw that, he did the same wise turn his

[188-042]lady. But when Sir Breunor beheld La Beale Isoud, him thought he saw never a

[188-043]fairer lady, and then he dread his lady’s head should be off. And so all

[188-044]the people that were there present gave judgment that La Beale Isoud was the

[188-045]fairer lady and the better made. How now, said Sir Tristram, meseemeth it were

[188-046][[mercy|pity]] that my lady should lose her head, but because thou and she of long time

[188-047]have used this wicked custom, and by you both have many good knights and ladies

[188-048]been destroyed, for that cause it were no loss to destroy you both. So God me

[188-049]help, said Sir Breunor, for to say the sooth, thy lady is fairer than mine, and

[188-050]that me sore repenteth. And so I hear the people privily say, for of all women

[188-051]I saw none so fair; and therefore, an thou wilt slay my lady, I doubt not but I

[188-052]shall slay thee and have thy lady. Thou shalt win her, said Sir Tristram, as

[188-053]dear as ever knight won lady. And because of thine own judgment, as thou

[188-054]wouldst have done to my lady if that she had been fouler, and because of the

[188-055]evil custom, give me thy lady, said Sir Tristram. And therewithal Sir Tristram

[188-056]strode unto him and took his lady from him, and with an awk stroke he smote off

[188-057]her head clean. Well, knight, said Sir Breunor, now hast thou done me a

[188-058]despite.
