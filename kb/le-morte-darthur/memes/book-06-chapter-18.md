[126-001][[king-arthur|Arthur]]’s Court, and how there were recounted all his noble feats and

[126-002]acts.

[126-003]Now turn we unto [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot du Lake]], that came home two days afore the Feast

[126-004]of Pentecost; and the king and all the court were passing fain of his coming.

[126-005]And when [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]], Sir Uwaine, Sir Sagramore, Sir Ector de Maris, saw Sir

[126-006]Launcelot in [[sir-kay|Kay]]’s armour, then they wist well it was he that smote them

[126-007]down all with one spear. Then there was laughing and smiling among them. And

[126-008]ever now and now came all the knights home that Sir Turquine had prisoners, and

[126-009]they all honoured and worshipped Sir Launcelot.

[126-010]When Sir Gaheris heard them speak, he said, I saw all the battle from the

[126-011]beginning to the ending, and there he told King Arthur all how it was, and how

[126-012]Sir Turquine was the strongest knight that ever he saw except Sir Launcelot:

[126-013]there were many knights bare him record, nigh three score. Then Sir Kay told

[126-014]the king how Sir Launcelot had rescued him when he should have been slain, and

[126-015]how he made the knights yield them to me, and not to him. And there they were

[126-016]all three, and bare record. And by Jesu, said Sir Kay, because Sir Launcelot

[126-017]took my harness and left me his I rode in good peace, and no man would have ado

[126-018]with me.

[126-019]Anon therewithal there came the three knights that fought with Sir Launcelot at

[126-020]the long bridge. And there they yielded them unto Sir Kay, and Sir Kay forsook

[126-021]them and said he fought never with them. But I shall ease your heart, said Sir

[126-022]Kay, yonder is Sir Launcelot that overcame you. When they wist that they were

[126-023]glad. And then Sir Meliot de Logres came home, and told the king how Sir

[126-024]Launcelot had saved him from the death. And all his deeds were known, how four

[126-025]queens, sorceresses, had him in prison, and how he was delivered by King

[126-026]Bagdemagus’ daughter. Also there were told all the great deeds of arms

[126-027]that Sir Launcelot did betwixt the two kings, that is for to say the King of

[126-028]Northgalis and King Bagdemagus. All the truth Sir Gahalantine did tell, and Sir

[126-029]Mador de la Porte and [[mordred|Sir Mordred]], for they were at that same tournament. Then

[126-030]came in the lady that knew Sir Launcelot when that he wounded Sir Belleus at

[126-031]the pavilion. And there, at request of Sir Launcelot, Sir Belleus was made

[126-032]knight of the [[round-table|Round Table]]. And so at that time Sir Launcelot had the greatest

[126-033]name of any knight of the world, and most he was honoured of high and low.
