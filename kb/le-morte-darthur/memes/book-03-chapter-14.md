[064-001]as he lay by night in a valley, and of their adventures.

[064-002]And therewith he armed him. So right even afore him there met two knights, the

[064-003]one came froward [[camelot|Camelot]], and the other from the north, and either saluted

[064-004]other. What tidings at Camelot? said the one. By my head, said the other, there

[064-005]have I been and espied the court of [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], and there is such a fellowship

[064-006]they may never be broken, and well-nigh all the world holdeth with Arthur, for

[064-007]there is the flower of chivalry. Now for this cause I am riding into the north,

[064-008]to tell our chieftains of the fellowship that is withholden with King Arthur.

[064-009]As for that, said the other knight, I have brought a remedy with me, that is

[064-010]the greatest poison that ever ye heard speak of, and to Camelot will I with it,

[064-011]for we have a friend right nigh King Arthur, and well cherished, that shall

[064-012]poison King Arthur; for so he hath promised our chieftains, and received great

[064-013]gifts for to do it. Beware, said the other knight, of [[merlin|Merlin]], for he knoweth

[064-014]all things by the devil’s craft. Therefore will I not let it, said the

[064-015]knight. And so they departed asunder. Anon after Pellinore made him ready, and

[064-016]his lady, [and] rode toward Camelot; and as they came by the well there as the

[064-017]wounded knight was and the lady, there he found the knight, and the lady eaten

[064-018]with lions or wild beasts, all save the head, wherefore he made great sorrow,

[064-019]and wept passing sore, and said, Alas! her life might I have saved; but I was

[064-020]so fierce in my quest, therefore I would not abide. Wherefore make ye such

[064-021]dole? said the lady. I wot not, said Pellinore, but my heart mourneth sore of

[064-022]the death of her, for she was a passing fair lady and a young. Now, will ye do

[064-023]by mine advice? said the lady, take this knight and let him be buried in an

[064-024]hermitage, and then take the lady’s head and bear it with you unto

[064-025]Arthur. So King Pellinore took this dead knight on his shoulders, and brought

[064-026]him to the hermitage, and charged the hermit with the corpse, that service

[064-027]should be done for the soul; and take his harness for your pain. It shall be

[064-028]done, said the hermit, as I will answer unto God.
