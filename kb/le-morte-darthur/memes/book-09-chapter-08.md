[211-001]prisoner, and after rescued by [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot]], and how Sir Launcelot overcame

[211-002]four brethren.

[211-003]This meanwhile Sir La Cote Male Taile sank right down upon the earth, what

[211-004]for-wounded and what for-bled he might not stand. Then the other knight had

[211-005][[mercy|pity]] of him, and said: Fair young knight, dismay you not, for had ye been fresh

[211-006]when ye met with me, as I was, I wot well that I should not have endured so

[211-007]long as ye have done; and therefore for your noble deeds of arms I shall show

[211-008]to you kindness and gentleness in all that I may. And forthwithal this noble

[211-009]knight, Sir Plenorius, took him up in his arms, and led him into his tower. And

[211-010]then he commanded him the wine, and made to search him and to stop his bleeding

[211-011]wounds. Sir, said La Cote Male Taile, withdraw you from me, and hie you to

[211-012]yonder bridge again, for there will meet with you another manner knight than

[211-013]ever was I. Why, said Plenorius, is there another manner knight behind of your

[211-014]fellowship? Yea, said La Cote Male Taile, there is a much better knight than I

[211-015]am. What is his name? said Plenorius. Ye shall not know for me, said La Cote

[211-016]Male Taile. Well, said the knight, he shall be encountered withal whatsomever

[211-017]he be.

[211-018]Then Sir Plenorius heard a knight call that said: Sir Plenorius, where art

[211-019]thou? either thou must deliver me the prisoner that thou hast led unto thy

[211-020]tower, or else come and do battle with me. Then Plenorius gat his horse, and

[211-021]came with a spear in his hand walloping toward Sir Launcelot; and then they

[211-022]began to feutre their spears, and came together as thunder, and smote either

[211-023]other so mightily that their horses fell down under them. And then they avoided

[211-024]their horses, and pulled out their swords, and like two bulls they lashed

[211-025]together with great strokes and foins; but ever Sir Launcelot recovered ground

[211-026]upon him, and Sir Plenorius traced to have gone about him. But Sir Launcelot

[211-027]would not suffer that, but bare him backer and backer, till he came nigh his

[211-028]tower gate. And then said Sir Launcelot: I know thee well for a good knight,

[211-029]but wit thou well thy life and death is in my hand, and therefore yield thee to

[211-030]me, and thy prisoner. The other answered no word, but struck mightily upon Sir

[211-031]Launcelot’s helm, that the fire sprang out of his eyes. Then Sir

[211-032]Launcelot doubled his strokes so thick, and smote at him so mightily, that he

[211-033]made him kneel upon his knees. And therewith Sir Launcelot leapt upon him, and

[211-034]pulled him grovelling down. Then Sir Plenorius yielded him, and his tower, and

[211-035]all his prisoners at his will.

[211-036]Then Sir Launcelot received him and took his troth; and then he rode to the

[211-037]other bridge, and there Sir Launcelot jousted with other three of his brethren,

[211-038]the one hight Pillounes, and the other hight Pellogris, and the third Sir

[211-039]Pellandris. And first upon horseback Sir Launcelot smote them down, and

[211-040]afterward he beat them on foot, and made them to yield them unto him; and then

[211-041]he returned unto Sir Plenorius, and there he found in his prison King Carados

[211-042]of Scotland, and many other knights, and all they were delivered. And then Sir

[211-043]La Cote Male Taile came to Sir Launcelot, and then Sir Launcelot would have

[211-044]given him all these fortresses and these bridges. Nay, said La Cote Male Taile,

[211-045]I will not have Sir Plenorius’ livelihood; with that he will grant you,

[211-046]my lord Sir Launcelot, to come unto [[king-arthur|King Arthur]]’s court, and to be his

[211-047]knight, and all his brethren, I will pray you, my lord, to let him have his

[211-048]livelihood. I will well, said Sir Launcelot, with this that he will come to the

[211-049]court of King Arthur and become his man, and his brethren five. And as for you,

[211-050]Sir Plenorius, I will undertake, said Sir Launcelot, at the next feast, so

[211-051]there be a place voided, that ye shall be Knight of the [[round-table|Round Table]]. Sir, said

[211-052]Plenorius, at the next feast of Pentecost I will be at Arthur’s court,

[211-053]and at that time I will be guided and ruled as King Arthur and ye will have me.

[211-054]Then Sir Launcelot and Sir La Cote Male Taile reposed them there, unto the time

[211-055]that Sir La Cote Male Taile was whole of his wounds, and there they had merry

[211-056]cheer, and good rest, and many good games, and there were many fair ladies.
