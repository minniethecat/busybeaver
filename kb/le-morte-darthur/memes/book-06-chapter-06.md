[114-001]King Bagdemagus’ daughter, and how he made his complaint to her

[114-002]father.

[114-003]And soon as [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot]] came within the abbey yard, the daughter of King

[114-004]Bagdemagus heard a great horse go on the pavement. And she then arose and yede

[114-005]unto a window, and there she saw Sir Launcelot, and anon she made men fast to

[114-006]take his horse from him and let lead him into a stable, and himself was led

[114-007]into a fair chamber, and unarmed him, and the lady sent him a long gown, and

[114-008]anon she came herself. And then she made Launcelot passing good cheer, and she

[114-009]said he was the knight in the world was most welcome to her. Then in all haste

[114-010]she sent for her father Bagdemagus that was within twelve mile of that Abbey,

[114-011]and afore even he came, with a fair fellowship of knights with him. And when

[114-012]the king was alighted off his horse he yode straight unto Sir Launcelot’s

[114-013]chamber and there he found his daughter, and then the king embraced Sir

[114-014]Launcelot in his arms, and either made other good cheer.

[114-015]Anon Sir Launcelot made his complaint unto the king how he was betrayed, and

[114-016]how his brother Sir Lionel was departed from him he wist not where, and how his

[114-017]daughter had delivered him out of prison; Therefore while I live I shall do her

[114-018]service and all her kindred. Then am I sure of your help, said the king, on

[114-019]Tuesday next coming. Yea, sir, said Sir Launcelot, I shall not fail you, for so

[114-020]I have promised my lady your daughter. But, sir, what knights be they of my

[114-021]lord [[king-arthur|Arthur]]’s that were with the King of Northgalis? And the king said it

[114-022]was Sir Mador de la Porte, and [[mordred|Sir Mordred]] and Sir Gahalantine that all

[114-023]for-fared my knights, for against them three I nor my knights might bear no

[114-024]strength. Sir, said Sir Launcelot, as I hear say that the tournament shall be

[114-025]here within this three mile of this abbey, ye shall send unto me three knights

[114-026]of yours, such as ye trust, and look that the three knights have all white

[114-027]shields, and I also, and no painture on the shields, and we four will come out

[114-028]of a little wood in midst of both parties, and we shall fall in the front of

[114-029]our enemies and grieve them that we may; and thus shall I not be known what

[114-030]knight I am.

[114-031]So they took their rest that night, and this was on the Sunday, and so the king

[114-032]departed, and sent unto Sir Launcelot three knights with the four white

[114-033]shields. And on the Tuesday they lodged them in a little leaved wood beside

[114-034]there the tournament should be. And there were scaffolds and holes that lords

[114-035]and ladies might behold and to give the prize. Then came into the [[battle|field]] the

[114-036]King of Northgalis with eight score helms. And then the three knights of

[114-037]Arthur’s stood by themselves. Then came into the field King Bagdemagus

[114-038]with four score of helms. And then they feutred their spears, and came together

[114-039]with a great dash, and there were slain of knights at the first recounter

[114-040]twelve of King Bagdemagus’ party, and six of the King of

[114-041]Northgalis’ party, and King Bagdemagus’ party was far set aback.
