[079-001]Uriens her husband, and how Sir Uwaine her son saved him.

[079-002]The meanwhile [[morgan-le-fay|Morgan le Fay]] had weened [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] had been dead. So on a day

[079-003]she espied King Uriens lay in his bed sleeping. Then she called unto her a

[079-004]maiden of her counsel, and said, Go fetch me my lord’s sword, for I saw

[079-005]never better time to slay him than now. O madam, said the damosel, an ye slay

[079-006]my lord ye can never escape. Care not you, said Morgan le Fay, for now I see my

[079-007]time in the which it is best to do it, and therefore hie thee fast and fetch me

[079-008]the sword. Then the damosel departed, and found Sir Uwaine sleeping upon a bed

[079-009]in another chamber, so she went unto Sir Uwaine, and awaked him, and bade him,

[079-010]Arise, and wait on my lady your mother, for she will slay the king your father

[079-011]sleeping in his bed, for I go to fetch his sword. Well, said Sir Uwaine, go on

[079-012]your way, and let me deal. Anon the damosel brought Morgan the sword with

[079-013]quaking hands, and she lightly took the sword, and pulled it out, and went

[079-014]boldly unto the bed’s side, and awaited how and where she might slay him

[079-015]best. And as she lifted up the sword to smite, Sir Uwaine leapt unto his

[079-016]mother, and caught her by the hand, and said, Ah, fiend, what wilt thou do? An

[079-017]thou wert not my mother, with this sword I should smite off thy head. Ah, said

[079-018]Sir Uwaine, men saith that [[merlin|Merlin]] was begotten of a devil, but I may say an

[079-019]earthly devil bare me. O fair son, Uwaine, have mercy upon me, I was tempted

[079-020]with a devil, wherefore I cry thee mercy; I will never more do so; and save my

[079-021][[honour|worship]] and discover me not. On this covenant, said Sir Uwaine, I will forgive

[079-022]it you, so ye will never be about to do such deeds. Nay, son, said she, and

[079-023]that I make you assurance.
