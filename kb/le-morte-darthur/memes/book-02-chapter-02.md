[033-001]pulled out the sword, which afterward was the cause of his death.

[033-002]Then fell it so that time there was a poor knight with [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], that had

[033-003]been prisoner with him half a year and more for slaying of a knight, the which

[033-004]was cousin unto King Arthur. The name of this knight was called Balin, and by

[033-005]good means of the barons he was delivered out of prison, for he was a good man

[033-006]named of his body, and he was born in Northumberland. And so he went privily

[033-007]into the court, and saw this adventure, whereof it raised his heart, and he

[033-008]would assay it as other knights did, but for he was poor and poorly arrayed he

[033-009]put him not far in press. But in his heart he was fully assured to do as well,

[033-010]if his grace happed him, as any knight that there was. And as the damosel took

[033-011]her leave of Arthur and of all the barons, so departing, this knight Balin

[033-012]called unto her, and said, Damosel, I pray you of your courtesy, suffer me as

[033-013]well to assay as these lords; though that I be so poorly clothed, in my heart

[033-014]meseemeth I am fully assured as some of these others, and meseemeth in my heart

[033-015]to speed right well. The damosel beheld the poor knight, and saw he was a

[033-016]likely man, but for his poor arrayment she thought he should be of no [[honour|worship]]

[033-017]without villainy or [[treason|treachery]]. And then she said unto the knight, Sir, it

[033-018]needeth not to put me to more pain or labour, for it seemeth not you to speed

[033-019]there as other have failed. Ah! fair damosel, said Balin, worthiness, and good

[033-020]tatches, and good deeds, are not only in arrayment, but manhood and worship is

[033-021]hid within man’s person, and many a worshipful knight is not known unto

[033-022]all people, and therefore worship and [[courage|hardiness]] is not in arrayment. By God,

[033-023]said the damosel, ye say sooth; therefore ye shall assay to do what ye may.

[033-024]Then Balin took the sword by the girdle and sheath, and drew it out easily; and

[033-025]when he looked on the sword it pleased him much. Then had the king and all the

[033-026]barons great marvel that Balin had done that adventure, and many knights had

[033-027]great despite of Balin. Certes, said the damosel, this is a passing good

[033-028]knight, and the best that ever I found, and most of worship without treason,

[033-029]treachery, or villainy, and many marvels shall he do. Now, gentle and courteous

[033-030]knight, give me the sword again. Nay, said Balin, for this sword will I keep,

[033-031]but it be taken from me with force. Well, said the damosel, ye are not wise to

[033-032]keep the sword from me, for ye shall slay with the sword the best friend that

[033-033]ye have, and the man that ye most love in the world, and the sword shall be

[033-034]your destruction. I shall take the adventure, said Balin, that God will ordain

[033-035]me, but the sword ye shall not have at this time, by the faith of my body. Ye

[033-036]shall repent it within short time, said the damosel, for I would have the sword

[033-037]more for your avail than for mine, for I am passing heavy for your sake; for ye

[033-038]will not believe that sword shall be your destruction, and that is great [[mercy|pity]].

[033-039]With that the damosel departed, making great sorrow.

[033-040]Anon after, Balin sent for his horse and armour, and so would depart from the

[033-041]court, and took his leave of King Arthur. Nay, said the king, I suppose ye will

[033-042]not depart so lightly from this fellowship, I suppose ye are displeased that I

[033-043]have shewed you unkindness; blame me the less, for I was misinformed against

[033-044]you, but I weened ye had not been such a knight as ye are, of worship and

[033-045]prowess, and if ye will abide in this court among my fellowship, I shall so

[033-046]advance you as ye shall be pleased. God thank your highness, said Balin, your

[033-047]bounty and highness may no man praise half to the value; but at this time I

[033-048]must needs depart, beseeching you alway of your good grace. Truly, said the

[033-049]king, I am right wroth for your departing; I pray you, fair knight, that ye

[033-050]tarry not long, and ye shall be right welcome to me, and to my barons, and I

[033-051]shall amend all miss that I have done against you; God thank your great

[033-052]lordship, said Balin, and therewith made him ready to depart. Then the most

[033-053]part of the knights of the [[round-table|Round Table]] said that Balin did not this adventure

[033-054]all only by might, but by witchcraft.
