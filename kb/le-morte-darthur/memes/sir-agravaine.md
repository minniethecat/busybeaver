# Sir Agravaine

Sir Agravaine was the second son of [[King Lot]] of Orkney and Queen Margawse, sister of [[King Arthur]]. Together with his brothers — [[Sir Gawaine]], [[Gaheris]], and [[Sir Gareth]] — he belonged to the Orkney clan, one of the most prominent family blocs in Arthur's court. Though less celebrated than Gawaine or Gareth, Agravaine appears consistently in the tournament and battle rolls of the Round Table, a reliable knight of the king's inner circle.

## The Orkney Brothers

Agravaine's lineage placed him at the centre of Arthurian power. His mother Margawse was Arthur's half-sister, making Agravaine the king's nephew. The four Orkney brothers — Gawaine, Agravaine, Gaheris, and Gareth — arrived at court as a unit, and Malory frequently lists them together as a single martial bloc. At the interment of a fallen knight, "came King Lot's wife Margawse with her four sons, Gawaine, Agravaine, Gaheris, and Gareth" (042-003), marking their first collective appearance. The brothers fought as a cohort throughout the wars and tournaments, Agravaine always named second or third among them, a position indicating both his standing and his place in the shadow of Gawaine.

## Tournament Record

Agravaine's appearances in the tournament lists establish him as a competent knight of the middle rank. At the great tournament of the Castle of Maidens, he rode in with King Arthur's party: "there came with King Arthur Sir Gawaine, Agravaine, Gaheris, his brethren" (154-003). In the melee that followed, he was struck down by Sir Safere: "Sir Safere smote down Agravaine, Sir Gawaine's brother" (154-057). This tournament record — present at the great assemblies, capable but vulnerable to the more extraordinary knights — is characteristic of Agravaine's place in the Arthurian world. At another tournament, Sir Palamides "put to the worse Sir Gawaine, Gaheris, Agravaine, Bagdemagus, Kay, Dodinas le Savage, Sagramore le Desirous" (172-039), a list that places Agravaine among the notable knights, though on the losing side.

## Marriage and Lineage

King Arthur personally arranged Agravaine's marriage to Dame Laurel, niece of Dame Lionesse, as part of the great wedding celebration that also saw Gareth marry Lionesse and Gaheris marry Dame Linet. Malory records that "King Arthur made Sir Agravaine to wed Dame Lionesse's niece, a fair lady, her name was Dame Laurel" (162-009). This triple wedding, orchestrated by the king, bound the Orkney brothers to the noble houses of the realm and strengthened the kinship networks that sustained Arthur's rule.

## Identity and Loyalty

When Gareth first arrived at court anonymously and served in the kitchens, he revealed his true identity by declaring: "Sir Gawaine is my brother, and Sir Agravaine and Sir Gaheris, and I am the youngest of them all" (140-040). Gareth's invocation of Agravaine's name as a marker of noble identity speaks to the weight the Orkney name carried — to be Agravaine's brother was to be someone of consequence. The four brothers presented a united front, rising together at court when their mother visited and kneeling to ask her blessing (152-004).
