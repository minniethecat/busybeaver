[023-001]horse and followed the Questing Beast, and how [[merlin|Merlin]] met with [[king-arthur|Arthur]].

[023-002]Sir knight, said the king, leave that quest, and suffer me to have it, and I

[023-003]will follow it another twelvemonth. Ah, fool, said the knight unto Arthur, it

[023-004]is in vain thy desire, for it shall never be achieved but by me, or my next

[023-005]kin. Therewith he started unto the king’s horse and mounted into the

[023-006]saddle, and said, Gramercy, this horse is my own. Well, said the king, thou

[023-007]mayst take my horse by force, but an I might prove thee whether thou were

[023-008]better on horseback or I.—Well, said the knight, seek me here when thou

[023-009]wilt, and here nigh this well thou shalt find me, and so passed on his way.

[023-010]Then the king sat in a study, and bade his men fetch his horse as fast as ever

[023-011]they might. Right so came by him Merlin like a child of fourteen year of age,

[023-012]and saluted the king, and asked him why he was so pensive. I may well be

[023-013]pensive, said the king, for I have seen the marvellest sight that ever I saw.

[023-014]That know I well, said Merlin, as well as thyself, and of all thy thoughts, but

[023-015]thou art but a fool to take thought, for it will not amend thee. Also I know

[023-016]what thou art, and who was thy father, and of whom thou wert begotten; King

[023-017]Uther Pendragon was thy father, and begat thee on Igraine. That is false, said

[023-018]King Arthur, how shouldest thou know it, for thou art not so old of years to

[023-019]know my father? Yes, said Merlin, I know it better than ye or any man living. I

[023-020]will not believe thee, said Arthur, and was wroth with the child. So departed

[023-021]Merlin, and came again in the likeness of an old man of fourscore year of age,

[023-022]whereof the king was right glad, for he seemed to be right wise.

[023-023]Then said the old man, Why are ye so sad? I may well be heavy, said Arthur, for

[023-024]many things. Also here was a child, and told me many things that meseemeth he

[023-025]should not know, for he was not of age to know my father. Yes, said the old

[023-026]man, the child told you truth, and more would he have told you an ye would have

[023-027]suffered him. But ye have done a thing late that God is displeased with you,

[023-028]for ye have lain by your sister, and on her ye have gotten a child that shall

[023-029]destroy you and all the knights of your realm. What are ye, said Arthur, that

[023-030]tell me these tidings? I am Merlin, and I was he in the child’s likeness.

[023-031]Ah, said King Arthur, ye are a [[marvel|marvellous]] man, but I marvel much of thy words

[023-032]that I must die in battle. Marvel not, said Merlin, for it is God’s will

[023-033]your body to be punished for your foul deeds; but I may well be sorry, said

[023-034]Merlin, for I shall die a shameful death, to be put in the earth quick, and ye

[023-035]shall die a worshipful death. And as they talked this, came one with the

[023-036]king’s horse, and so the king mounted on his horse, and Merlin on

[023-037]another, and so rode unto [[camelot|Carlion]]. And anon the king asked Ector and Ulfius how

[023-038]he was begotten, and they told him Uther Pendragon was his father and Queen

[023-039]Igraine his mother. Then he said to Merlin, I will that my mother be sent for

[023-040]that I may speak with her; and if she say so herself then will I believe it. In

[023-041]all haste, the queen was sent for, and she came and brought with her [[morgan-le-fay|Morgan]] le

[023-042]Fay, her daughter, that was as fair a lady as any might be, and the king

[023-043]welcomed Igraine in the best manner.
