[034-002]The meanwhile, that this knight was making him ready to depart, there came into

[034-003]the court a lady that hight the [[lady-of-the-lake|Lady of the Lake]]. And she came on horseback,

[034-004]richly beseen, and saluted [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], and there asked him a gift that he

[034-005]promised her when she gave him the sword. That is sooth, said Arthur, a gift I

[034-006]promised you, but I have forgotten the name of my sword that ye gave me. The

[034-007]name of it, said the lady, is [[sword-in-the-stone|Excalibur]], that is as much to say as Cut-steel.

[034-008]Ye say well, said the king; ask what ye will and ye shall have it, an it lie in

[034-009]my power to give it. Well, said the lady, I ask the head of the knight that

[034-010]hath won the sword, or else the damosel’s head that brought it; I take no

[034-011]force though I have both their heads, for he slew my brother, a good knight and

[034-012]a true, and that gentlewoman was causer of my father’s death. Truly, said

[034-013]King Arthur, I may not grant neither of their heads with my [[honour|worship]], therefore

[034-014]ask what ye will else, and I shall fulfil your desire. I will ask none other

[034-015]thing, said the lady. When Balin was ready to depart, he saw the Lady of the

[034-016]Lake, that by her means had slain Balin’s mother, and he had sought her

[034-017]three years; and when it was told him that she asked his head of King Arthur,

[034-018]he went to her straight and said, Evil be you found; ye would have my head, and

[034-019]therefore ye shall lose yours, and with his sword lightly he smote off her head

[034-020]before King Arthur. Alas, for shame! said Arthur, why have ye done so? ye have

[034-021]shamed me and all my court, for this was a lady that I was beholden to, and

[034-022]hither she came under my safe-conduct; I shall never forgive you that trespass.

[034-023]Sir, said Balin, me forthinketh of your displeasure, for this same lady was the

[034-024]untruest lady living, and by enchantment and sorcery she hath been the

[034-025]destroyer of many good knights, and she was causer that my mother was burnt,

[034-026]through her falsehood and [[treason|treachery]]. What cause soever ye had, said Arthur, ye

[034-027]should have forborne her in my presence; therefore, think not the contrary, ye

[034-028]shall repent it, for such another despite had I never in my court; therefore

[034-029]withdraw you out of my court in all haste ye may.

[034-030]Then Balin took up the head of the lady, and bare it with him to his hostelry,

[034-031]and there he met with his squire, that was sorry he had displeased King Arthur

[034-032]and so they rode forth out of the town. Now, said Balin, we must depart, take

[034-033]thou this head and bear it to my friends, and tell them how I have sped, and

[034-034]tell my friends in Northumberland that my most foe is dead. Also tell them how

[034-035]I am out of prison, and what adventure befell me at the getting of this sword.

[034-036]Alas! said the squire, ye are greatly to blame for to displease King Arthur. As

[034-037]for that, said Balin, I will hie me, in all the haste that I may, to meet with

[034-038]King Rience and destroy him, either else to die therefore; and if it may hap me

[034-039]to win him, then will King Arthur be my good and gracious lord. Where shall I

[034-040]meet with you? said the squire. In King Arthur’s court, said Balin. So

[034-041]his squire and he departed at that time. Then King Arthur and all the court

[034-042]made great dole and had shame of the death of the Lady of the Lake. Then the

[034-043]king buried her richly.
