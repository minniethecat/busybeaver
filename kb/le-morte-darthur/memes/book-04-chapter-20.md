[086-001]lady.

[086-002]And therewith he passed unto the one side of the laund; and on the other side

[086-003]saw [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]] ten knights that hoved still and made them ready with their

[086-004]shields and spears against that one knight that came by Sir Gawaine.

[086-005]Then this one knight aventred a great spear, and one of the ten knights

[086-006]encountered with him, but this woful knight smote him so hard that he fell over

[086-007]his horse’s tail. So this same dolorous knight served them all, that at

[086-008]the leastway he smote down horse and man, and all he did with one spear; and so

[086-009]when they were all ten on foot, they went to that one knight, and he stood

[086-010]stone still, and suffered them to pull him down off his horse, and bound him

[086-011]hand and foot, and tied him under the horse’s belly, and so led him with

[086-012]them. O Jesu! said Sir Gawaine, this is a doleful sight, to see the yonder

[086-013]knight so to be entreated, and it seemeth by the knight that he suffereth them

[086-014]to bind him so, for he maketh no resistance. No, said his host, that is truth,

[086-015]for an he would they all were too weak so to do him. Sir, said the damosel unto

[086-016]Sir Gawaine, meseemeth it were your [[honour|worship]] to help that dolorous knight, for

[086-017]methinketh he is one of the best knights that ever I saw. I would do for him,

[086-018]said Sir Gawaine, but it seemeth he will have no help. Then, said the damosel,

[086-019]methinketh ye have no lust to help him.

[086-020]Thus as they talked they saw a knight on the other side of the laund all armed

[086-021]save the head. And on the other side there came a dwarf on horseback all armed

[086-022]save the head, with a great mouth and a short nose; and when the dwarf came

[086-023]nigh he said, Where is the lady should meet us here? and therewithal she came

[086-024]forth out of the wood. And then they began to strive for the lady; for the

[086-025]knight said he would have her, and the dwarf said he would have her. Will we do

[086-026]well? said the dwarf; yonder is a knight at the cross, let us put it both upon

[086-027]him, and as he deemeth so shall it be. I will well, said the knight, and so

[086-028]they went all three unto Sir Gawaine and told him wherefore they strove. Well,

[086-029]sirs, said he, will ye put the matter in my hand? Yea, they said both. Now

[086-030]damosel, said Sir Gawaine, ye shall stand betwixt them both, and whether ye

[086-031]list better to go to, he shall have you. And when she was set between them

[086-032]both, she left the knight and went to the dwarf, and the dwarf took her and

[086-033]went his way singing, and the knight went his way with great mourning.

[086-034]Then came there two knights all armed, and cried on high, Sir Gawaine! knight

[086-035]of [[king-arthur|King Arthur]]’s, make thee ready in all haste and joust with me. So they

[086-036]ran together, that either fell down, and then on foot they drew their swords,

[086-037]and did full actually. The meanwhile the other knight went to the damosel, and

[086-038]asked her why she abode with that knight, and if ye would abide with me, I will

[086-039]be your faithful knight. And with you will I be, said the damosel, for with Sir

[086-040]Gawaine I may not find in mine heart to be with him; for now here was one

[086-041]knight discomfited ten knights, and at the last he was cowardly led away; and

[086-042]therefore let us two go whilst they [[battle|fight]]. And Sir Gawaine fought with that

[086-043]other knight long, but at the last they accorded both. And then the knight

[086-044]prayed Sir Gawaine to lodge with him that night. So as Sir Gawaine went with

[086-045]this knight he asked him, What knight is he in this country that smote down the

[086-046]ten knights? For when he had done so manfully he suffered them to bind him hand

[086-047]and foot, and so led him away. Ah, said the knight, that is the best knight I

[086-048]trow in the world, and the most man of [[courage|prowess]], and he hath been served so as

[086-049]he was even more than ten times, and his name hight Sir Pelleas, and he loveth

[086-050]a great lady in this country and her name is Ettard. And so when he loved her

[086-051]there was cried in this country a great [[tournament|jousts]] three days, and all the knights

[086-052]of this country were there and gentlewomen, and who that proved him the best

[086-053]knight should have a passing good sword and a circlet of gold, and the circlet

[086-054]the knight should give it to the fairest lady that was at the jousts. And this

[086-055]knight Sir Pelleas was the best knight that was there, and there were five

[086-056]hundred knights, but there was never man that ever Sir Pelleas met withal but

[086-057]he struck him down, or else from his horse; and every day of three days he

[086-058]struck down twenty knights, therefore they gave him the prize, and forthwithal

[086-059]he went thereas the Lady Ettard was, and gave her the circlet, and said openly

[086-060]she was the fairest lady that there was, and that would he prove upon any

[086-061]knight that would say nay.
