# Sir Galahad

**Sir Galahad** is the [[chivalry|knightly]] ideal of purity in Malory's *Le Morte d'Arthur* — the [[grail|Sangreal]]-achiever foretold by prophecy. Merlin declares "his first name was Galahad" (067-030), and Caxton's preface names him alongside [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot]] (002-118), framing the father and son as paired figures of earthly and heavenly [[chivalry]].

## The Prophesied Arrival

Galahad's coming is announced long before his appearance at court. The preface records that "the Thirteenth Book treateth how Galahad came first to King Arthur's court" (002-120), marking his arrival as a narrative turning-point. He is the son of [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot]], begotten with Elaine of Corbin under [[enchantment]] — a union of [[sin|sinful]] origin that produces the purest of all [[knights]].

## The Grail Achievement

Galahad's defining act is the achievement of the [[grail|Sangreal]], the supreme spiritual quest of the Arthurian world. His purity allows him to succeed where his father Launcelot could not; the prophecy foretold that "Galahad should" (050-018) come to heal what was broken. He appears at the Round Table on the feast of [[pentecost|Pentecost]], takes the Siege Perilous — the seat reserved for the Grail knight — and leads the quest that redeems the fellowship.
