[055-001]Pellinore, and how [[sir-gawaine|Gawaine]] was made knight.

[055-002]So on the morn King Pellinore came to [[camelot|the court of King Arthur]], which had great

[055-003]joy of him, and told him of Tor, how he was his son, and how he had made him

[055-004]knight at the request of the cowherd. When Pellinore beheld Tor, he pleased him

[055-005]much. So the king made Gawaine knight, but Tor was the first he made at the

[055-006]feast. What is the cause, said [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], that there be two places void in

[055-007]the sieges? Sir, said [[merlin|Merlin]], there shall no man sit in those places but they

[055-008]that shall be of most [[honour|worship]]. But in the Siege Perilous there shall no man sit

[055-009]therein but one, and if there be any so hardy to do it he shall be destroyed,

[055-010]and he that shall sit there shall have no fellow. And therewith Merlin took

[055-011]King Pellinore by the hand, and in the one hand next the two sieges and the

[055-012]Siege Perilous he said, in open audience, This is your place and best ye are

[055-013]worthy to sit therein of any that is here. Thereat sat Sir Gawaine in great

[055-014]envy and told Gaheris his brother, yonder knight is put to great worship, the

[055-015]which grieveth me sore, for he slew our father King Lot, therefore I will slay

[055-016]him, said Gawaine, with a sword that was sent me that is passing trenchant. Ye

[055-017]shall not so, said Gaheris, at this time, for at this time I am but a squire,

[055-018]and when I am made knight I will be avenged on him, and therefore, brother, it

[055-019]is best ye suffer till another time, that we may have him out of the court, for

[055-020]an we did so we should trouble this high feast. I will well, said Gawaine, as

[055-021]ye will.
