[044-001]knight which was in likewise slain, and how the damosel bled for the custom of

[044-002]a castle.

[044-003]So Balin and the damosel rode into a forest, and there met with a knight that

[044-004]had been a-hunting, and that knight asked Balin for what cause he made so great

[044-005]sorrow. Me list not to tell you, said Balin. Now, said the knight, an I were

[044-006]armed as ye be I would [[battle|fight]] with you. That should little need, said Balin, I

[044-007]am not afeard to tell you, and told him all the cause how it was. Ah, said the

[044-008]knight, is this all? here I ensure you by the faith of my body never to depart

[044-009]from you while my life lasteth. And so they went to the hostelry and armed

[044-010]them, and so rode forth with Balin. And as they came by an hermitage even by a

[044-011]churchyard, there came the knight Garlon invisible, and smote this knight,

[044-012]Perin de Mountbeliard, through the body with a spear. Alas, said the knight, I

[044-013]am slain by this traitor knight that rideth invisible. Alas, said Balin, it is

[044-014]not the first despite he hath done me; and there the hermit and Balin buried

[044-015]the knight under a rich stone and a tomb royal. And on the morn they found

[044-016]letters of gold written, how [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]] shall revenge his father’s

[044-017]death, King Lot, on [[king-arthur|the King]] Pellinore. Anon after this Balin and the damosel

[044-018]rode till they came to a castle, and there Balin alighted, and he and the

[044-019]damosel went to go into the castle, and anon as Balin came within the

[044-020]castle’s gate the portcullis fell down at his back, and there fell many

[044-021]men about the damosel, and would have slain her. When Balin saw that, he was

[044-022]sore aggrieved, for he might not help the damosel. Then he went up into the

[044-023]tower, and leapt over walls into the ditch, and hurt him not; and anon he

[044-024]pulled out his sword and would have foughten with them. And they all said nay,

[044-025]they would not fight with him, for they did nothing but the old custom of the

[044-026]castle; and told him how their lady was sick, and had lain many years, and she

[044-027]might not be whole but if she had a dish of silver full of blood of a clean

[044-028]maid and a king’s daughter; and therefore the custom of this castle is,

[044-029]there shall no damosel pass this way but she shall bleed of her blood in a

[044-030]silver dish full. Well, said Balin, she shall bleed as much as she may bleed,

[044-031]but I will not lose the life of her whiles my life lasteth. And so Balin made

[044-032]her to bleed by her good will, but her blood helped not the lady. And so he and

[044-033]she rested there all night, and had there right good cheer, and on the morn

[044-034]they passed on their ways. And as it telleth after in the Sangreal, that Sir

[044-035][[sir-percivale|Percivale]]’s sister helped that lady with her blood, whereof she was dead.
