[036-001]knight of Ireland, and how he jousted and slew him.

[036-002]And so this Lady Lile of Avelion took her this sword that she brought with her,

[036-003]and told there should no man pull it out of the sheath but if he be one of the

[036-004]best knights of this realm, and he should be hard and full of [[courage|prowess]], and with

[036-005]that sword he should slay her brother. This was the cause that the damosel came

[036-006]into this court. I know it as well as ye. Would God she had not come into this

[036-007]court, but she came never in fellowship of [[honour|worship]] to do good, but always great

[036-008]harm; and that knight that hath achieved the sword shall be destroyed by that

[036-009]sword, for the which will be great damage, for there liveth not a knight of

[036-010]more prowess than he is, and he shall do unto you, my Lord [[king-arthur|Arthur]], great honour

[036-011]and kindness; and it is great [[mercy|pity]] he shall not endure but a while, for of his

[036-012]strength and hardiness I know not his match living.

[036-013]So the knight of Ireland armed him at all points, and dressed his shield on his

[036-014]shoulder, and mounted upon horseback, and took his spear in his hand, and rode

[036-015]after a great pace, as much as his horse might go; and within a little space on

[036-016]a mountain he had a sight of Balin, and with a loud voice he cried, Abide,

[036-017]knight, for ye shall abide whether ye will or nill, and the shield that is

[036-018]to-fore you shall not help. When Balin heard the noise, he turned his horse

[036-019]fiercely, and said, Fair knight, what will ye with me, will ye joust with me?

[036-020]Yea, said the Irish knight, therefore come I after you. Peradventure, said

[036-021]Balin, it had been better to have holden you at home, for many a man weeneth to

[036-022]put his enemy to a rebuke, and oft it falleth to himself. Of what court be ye

[036-023]sent from? said Balin. I am come from [[camelot|the court of King Arthur]], said the knight

[036-024]of Ireland, that come hither for to revenge the despite ye did this day to King

[036-025]Arthur and to his court. Well, said Balin, I see well I must have ado with you,

[036-026]that me forthinketh for to grieve King Arthur, or any of his court; and your

[036-027]quarrel is full simple, said Balin, unto me, for the lady that is dead, did me

[036-028]great damage, and else would I have been loath as any knight that liveth for to

[036-029]slay a lady. Make you ready, said the knight Lanceor, and dress you unto me,

[036-030]for that one shall abide in the [[battle|field]]. Then they took their spears, and came

[036-031]together as much as their horses might drive, and the Irish knight smote Balin

[036-032]on the shield, that all went shivers off his spear, and Balin hit him through

[036-033]the shield, and the hauberk perished, and so pierced through his body and the

[036-034]horse’s croup, and anon turned his horse fiercely, and drew out his

[036-035]sword, and wist not that he had slain him; and then he saw him lie as a dead

[036-036]corpse.
