# King

The **[[kinsman|kin]]g** in [[Le Morte d'Arthur]] is both a person and a principle. [[King Arthur]] is the exemplary [[kinsman|kin]]g — the "most [[honour|renown]]ed Christian [[kinsman|kin]]g" (002-006) — but the text is populated with dozens of lesser [[kinsman|kin]]gs: rebel [[kinsman|kin]]gs, vassal [[kinsman|kin]]gs, [[kinsman|kin]]gs of small realms and distant countries. To be a [[kinsman|kin]]g in Malory is to occupy a specific office with duties, privileges, and vulnerabilities that distinguish it from mere [[baron|lord]]ship.

## The Office of Kingship

Kingship is an institution before it is a person. Malory refers to [[king-arthur|Arthur]] repeatedly as "[[kinsman|kin]]g and sovereign [[baron|lord]]" (013-024), yo[[kinsman|kin]]g the personal title to the formal role. The [[kinsman|kin]]g is the source of law, the arbiter of [[justice]], and the centre around which the ent[[wrath|ire]] chivalric order revolves:

> our king and sovereign lord (013-024)

> according unto the sovereign lord's desire (014-036)

The dual formula — "[[kinsman|kin]]g *and* sovereign [[baron|lord]]" — insists on the inseparability of person and office. [[king-arthur|Arthur]] does not merely hold the crown; he embodies it.

## Kings and Vassalage

The political world of [[Le Morte d'Arthur]] is structured by layers of kingship. [[King Arthur]] is high [[kinsman|kin]]g, but beneath him are [[kinsman|kin]]gs who rule their own territories and owe him [[fealty]]:

> King Lot of Lothian (011-004)

> King Uriens of Gore (011-005)

> King Nentres of Garlot (011-006)

These vassal [[kinsman|kin]]gs attend [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s [[feast|feasts]], contribute [[knight|knights]] to his [[battle|war]]s, and sit at the [[Round Table]]. Their independence is both a source of strength — the realm is rich in [[baron|lord]]s — and a perpetual source of instability, for each vassal [[kinsman|kin]]g is a potential rebel. The history of [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s early reign is the history of subduing [[kinsman|kin]]gs who deny his [[lordship|overlordship]].

## The King as Judge

One of the [[kinsman|kin]]g's most solemn functions is judgment. [[king-arthur|Arthur]] sits in the hall and hears [[dispute|disputes]]; he must ba[[spear|lance]] [[justice]] against [[mercy]], the claims of the powerful against the needs of the weak. The [[kinsman|kin]]g who judges poorly loses the moral authority that kingship confers:

> what is the cause, said King Arthur, that we should trouble this high feast (055-006)

Even at the feast — the ritual centre of court life — the [[kinsman|kin]]g's judicial function cannot be suspended. The feast is also a court of law, and every petition brought to the [[kinsman|kin]]g at table demands a ruling.

## The Vulnerable King

Kingship in Malory is not secure. [[king-arthur|Arthur]] is challenged from the moment of his crowning, and even after decades of rule, the threat of rebellion never disappears. The [[kinsman|kin]]g depends on the loyalty of his [[knight|knights]] — and when that loyalty fractures, as it does in the final books, kingship itself collapses.

The [[Sovereign|sovereign]]'s vulnerability is structural: a [[kinsman|kin]]g cannot enforce his will alone. He requ[[wrath|ire]]s counsel, he requ[[wrath|ire]]s arms, and above all he requ[[wrath|ire]]s the consent of those he rules. When [[Sir Mordred]] seizes the throne in [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s absence, he demonstrates how easily the office can be usurped when the person who holds it is absent.

## The King and the Emperor

[[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s kingship reaches its fullest expression when he is acclaimed emperor:

> King Arthur was emperor (013-024)

The imperial claim elevates [[king-arthur|Arthur]] above the other [[kinsman|kin]]gs of Britain and places him in the [[fellowship|company]] of the great rulers of Christian history — "the second David [[kinsman|kin]]g of Jerusalem, and the third Judas Maccabeus" (002-017). To be a [[kinsman|kin]]g in Malory's universe is to asp[[wrath|ire]] to join this [[fellowship|company]], to be remembered as more than a regional [[baron|lord]].
