# Sir Kehydius

Sir Kehydius was the son of King Howel of Brittany and the brother of Isoud la Blanche Mains, whom Sir Tristram wedded. He accompanied Sir Tristram on several adventures and fought alongside him in tournaments, but his fatal love for La Beale Isoud — Tristram's true beloved — led to his death by Tristram's sword.

## Kindred and Early Companionship

Sir Kehydius was the son of King Howel of Brittany, who had been besieged by an enemy lord. When Sir Tristram arrived in Brittany and helped King Howel win his war, he came to know Kehydius closely (199-003, 199-008). Through the great means of King Howel and Kehydius his son, Tristram was won to wed Isoud la Blanche Mains, Kehydius's sister (199-017).

Thereafter, Sir Kehydius sailed with Tristram and Isoud la Blanche Mains into Cornwall, to play them in the country (201-003, 202-056). He became a frequent companion to Tristram, alongside Sir Segwarides, riding and jousting together (202-008).

## Adventures with Tristram

Sir Kehydius asked leave of Sir Tristram to joust with a passing knight, but at their first meeting Sir Kehydius had a fall, and the stranger knight rode on (213-029, 213-030). Later, when Sir Kehydius was sore wounded in battle, Sir Tristram and Sir Lamorak together gat Sir Kehydius upon a shield betwixt them and bore him to safety (215-017).

Sir Kehydius was also present at the tournament at the Castle of Maidens, where Tristram won great honour, and continued to fight loyally in Tristram's company.

## The Fatal Love for La Beale Isoud

When Sir Kehydius saw La Beale Isoud, he was so enamoured of her that he fell into a grievous love-sickness (219-017). He began to send letters and tokens to La Beale Isoud, and she answered him courteously, though her heart remained with Tristram (219-027, 219-028).

Sir Tristram, discovering the letters that Kehydius had sent unto La Beale Isoud and the letter that she had written unto Kehydius, confronted his companion (219-027). Tristram drew out his sword and said: "Sir Kehydius, keep thee" (219-039). He smote Kehydius so grievously that he fell to the earth swooned (219-040).

Before the matter could be ended, Sir Kehydius died for the love of La Beale Isoud (219-019).

## The Tragedy of Trust

Sir Tristram had brought Kehydius out of Brittany and into Cornwall as a companion and brother by marriage (219-034). The betrayal of trust — Kehydius's pursuit of La Beale Isoud while Tristram's sworn companion — stands as one of the saddest episodes in Tristram's story, a private tragedy within the larger tragedy of Tristram and Isoud's forbidden love.
