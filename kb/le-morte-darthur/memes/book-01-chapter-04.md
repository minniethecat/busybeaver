[007-001]Pendragon.

[007-002]Then within two years King Uther fell sick of a great malady. And in the

[007-003]meanwhile his enemies usurped upon him, and did a great battle upon his men,

[007-004]and slew many of his people. Sir, said [[merlin|Merlin]], ye may not lie so as ye do, for

[007-005]ye must to the [[battle|field]] though ye ride on an horse-litter: for ye shall never have

[007-006]the better of your enemies but if your person be there, and then shall ye have

[007-007]the victory. So it was done as Merlin had devised, and they carried the king

[007-008]forth in an horse-litter with a great host towards his enemies. And at St.

[007-009]Albans there met with the king a great host of the North. And that day Sir

[007-010]Ulfius and Sir Brastias did great deeds of arms, and King Uther’s men

[007-011]overcame the Northern battle and slew many people, and put the remnant to

[007-012]flight. And then the king returned unto London, and made great joy of his

[007-013]victory. And then he fell passing sore sick, so that three days and three

[007-014]nights he was speechless: wherefore all the barons made great sorrow, and asked

[007-015]Merlin what counsel were best. There is none other remedy, said Merlin, but God

[007-016]will have his will. But look ye all barons be before King Uther to-morn, and

[007-017]God and I shall make him to speak. So on the morn all the barons with Merlin

[007-018]came to-fore the king; then Merlin said aloud unto King Uther, Sir, shall your

[007-019]son [[king-arthur|Arthur]] be king after your days, of this realm with all the appurtenance?

[007-020]Then Uther Pendragon turned him, and said in hearing of them all, I give him

[007-021]God’s blessing and mine, and bid him pray for my soul, and righteously

[007-022]and worshipfully that he claim the crown, upon forfeiture of my blessing; and

[007-023]therewith he yielded up the ghost, and then was he interred as longed to a

[007-024]king. Wherefore the queen, fair Igraine, made great sorrow, and all the barons.
