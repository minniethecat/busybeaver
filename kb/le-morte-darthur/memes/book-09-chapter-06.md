[209-002]Ah, said [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot]], that knight is my fellow, and him shall I rescue or

[209-003]else I shall lose my life therefore. And therewithal he rode fast till he came

[209-004]before the Castle of Pendragon; and anon therewithal there came six knights,

[209-005]and all made them ready to set upon Sir Launcelot at once; then Sir Launcelot

[209-006]feutred his spear, and smote the foremost that he brake his back in-sunder, and

[209-007]three of them hit and three failed. And then Sir Launcelot passed through them,

[209-008]and lightly he turned in again, and smote another knight through the breast and

[209-009]throughout the back more than an ell, and therewithal his spear brake. So then

[209-010]all the remnant of the four knights drew their swords and lashed at Sir

[209-011]Launcelot. And at every stroke Sir Launcelot bestowed so his strokes that at

[209-012]four strokes sundry they avoided their saddles, passing sore wounded; and

[209-013]forthwithal he rode hurling into that castle.

[209-014]And anon the lord of the castle, that was that time cleped Sir Brian de les

[209-015]Isles, the which was a noble man and a great enemy unto [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], within a

[209-016]while he was armed and upon horseback. And then they feutred their spears and

[209-017]hurled together so strongly that both their horses rashed to the earth. And

[209-018]then they avoided their saddles, and dressed their shields, and drew their

[209-019]swords, and flang together as wood men, and there were many strokes given in a

[209-020]while. At the last Sir Launcelot gave to Sir Brian such a buffet that he

[209-021]kneeled upon his knees, and then Sir Launcelot rashed upon him, and with great

[209-022]force he pulled off his helm; and when Sir Brian saw that he should be slain he

[209-023]yielded him, and put him in his mercy and in his grace. Then Sir Launcelot made

[209-024]him to deliver all his prisoners that he had within his castle, and therein Sir

[209-025]Launcelot found of Arthur’s knights thirty, and forty ladies, and so he

[209-026]delivered them; and then he rode his way. And anon as La Cote Male Taile was

[209-027]delivered he gat his horse, and his harness, and his damosel Maledisant.

[209-028]The meanwhile Sir Nerovens, that Sir Launcelot had foughten withal afore at the

[209-029]bridge, he sent a damosel after Sir Launcelot to wit how he sped at the Castle

[209-030]of Pendragon. And then they within the castle marvelled what knight he was,

[209-031]when Sir Brian and his knights delivered all those prisoners. Have ye no

[209-032]marvel, said the damosel, for the best knight in this world was here, and did

[209-033]this journey, and wit ye well, she said, it was Sir Launcelot. Then was Sir

[209-034]Brian full glad, and so was his lady, and all his knights, that such a man

[209-035]should win them. And when the damosel and La Cote Male Taile understood that it

[209-036]was Sir Launcelot du Lake that had ridden with them in fellowship, and that she

[209-037]remembered her how she had rebuked him and called him coward, then was she

[209-038]passing heavy.
