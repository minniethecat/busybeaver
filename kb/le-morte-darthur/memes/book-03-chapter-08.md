[058-001][[sir-gawaine|Gawaine]] and Gaheris, and how they were overcome, and their lives saved at

[058-002]request of four ladies.

[058-003]And Sir Gawaine went into the castle, and made him ready to lie there all

[058-004]night, and would have unarmed him. What will ye do, said Gaheris, will ye unarm

[058-005]you in this country? Ye may think ye have many enemies here. They had not

[058-006]sooner said that word but there came four knights well armed, and assailed Sir

[058-007]Gawaine hard, and said unto him, Thou new-made knight, thou hast shamed thy

[058-008]knighthood, for a knight without mercy is dishonoured. Also thou hast slain a

[058-009]fair lady to thy great shame to the world’s end, and doubt thou not thou

[058-010]shalt have great need of mercy or thou depart from us. And therewith one of

[058-011]them smote Sir Gawaine a great stroke that nigh he fell to the earth, and

[058-012]Gaheris smote him again sore, and so they were on the one side and on the

[058-013]other, that Sir Gawaine and Gaheris were in jeopardy of their lives; and one

[058-014]with a bow, an archer, smote Sir Gawaine through the arm that it grieved him

[058-015]wonderly sore. And as they should have been slain, there came four fair ladies,

[058-016]and besought the knights of grace for Sir Gawaine; and goodly at request of the

[058-017]ladies they gave Sir Gawaine and Gaheris their lives, and made them to yield

[058-018]them as prisoners. Then Gawaine and Gaheris made great dole. Alas! said Sir

[058-019]Gawaine, mine arm grieveth me sore, I am like to be maimed; and so made his

[058-020]complaint piteously.

[058-021]Early on the morrow there came to Sir Gawaine one of the four ladies that had

[058-022]heard all his complaint, and said, Sir knight, what cheer? Not good, said he.

[058-023]It is your own default, said the lady, for ye have done a passing foul deed in

[058-024]the slaying of the lady, the which will be great villainy unto you. But be ye

[058-025]not of [[king-arthur|King Arthur]]’s kin? said the lady. Yes truly, said Sir Gawaine.

[058-026]What is your name? said the lady, ye must tell it me or ye pass. My name is

[058-027]Gawaine, the King Lot of Orkney’s son, and my mother is King

[058-028]Arthur’s sister. Ah! then are ye nephew unto King Arthur, said the lady,

[058-029]and I shall so speak for you that ye shall have conduct to go to King Arthur

[058-030]for his love. And so she departed and told the four knights how their prisoner

[058-031]was King Arthur’s nephew, and his name is Sir Gawaine, King Lot’s

[058-032]son of Orkney. And they gave him the hart’s head because it was in his

[058-033]quest. Then anon they delivered Sir Gawaine under this promise, that he should

[058-034]bear the dead lady with him in this manner; the head of her was hanged about

[058-035]his neck, and the whole body of her lay before him on his horse’s mane.

[058-036]Right so rode he forth unto [[camelot|Camelot]]. And anon as he was come, [[merlin|Merlin]] desired of

[058-037]King Arthur that Sir Gawaine should be sworn to tell of all his adventures, and

[058-038]how he slew the lady, and how he would give no mercy unto the knight,

[058-039]wherethrough the lady was slain. Then the king and the queen were greatly

[058-040]displeased with Sir Gawaine for the slaying of the lady. And there by ordinance

[058-041]of the queen there was set a quest of ladies on Sir Gawaine, and they judged

[058-042]him for ever while he lived to be with all ladies, and to [[battle|fight]] for their

[058-043]quarrels; and that ever he should be courteous, and never to refuse mercy to

[058-044]him that asketh mercy. Thus was Gawaine sworn upon the Four Evangelists that he

[058-045]should never be against lady nor gentlewoman, but if he fought for a lady and

[058-046]his adversary fought for another. And thus endeth the adventure of Sir Gawaine

[058-047]that he did at the marriage of King Arthur. Amen.
