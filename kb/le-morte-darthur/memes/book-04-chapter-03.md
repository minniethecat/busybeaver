[069-001]overthrew them, and slew the five kings and made the remnant to flee.

[069-002]Unto this counsel these five kings assented, and so they passed forth with

[069-003]their host through North Wales, and came upon [[king-arthur|Arthur]] by night, and set upon his

[069-004]host as the king and his knights were in their pavilions. King Arthur was

[069-005]unarmed, and had laid him to rest with his [[queen-guenever|Queen Guenever]]. Sir, said [[sir-kay|Sir Kay]],

[069-006]it is not good we be unarmed. We shall have no need, said [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]] and Sir

[069-007]Griflet, that lay in a little pavilion by the king. With that they heard a

[069-008]great noise, and many cried, [[treason|Treason]], treason! Alas, said King Arthur, we be

[069-009]betrayed! Unto arms, fellows, then he cried. So they were armed anon at all

[069-010]points. Then came there a wounded knight unto the king, and said, Sir, save

[069-011]yourself and my lady the queen, for our host is destroyed, and much people of

[069-012]ours slain. So anon the king and the queen and the three knights took their

[069-013]horses, and rode toward Humber to pass over it, and the water was so rough that

[069-014]they were afraid to pass over. Now may ye choose, said King Arthur, whether ye

[069-015]will abide and take the adventure on this side, for an ye be taken they will

[069-016]slay you. It were me liefer, said the queen, to die in the water than to fall

[069-017]in your enemies’ hands and there be slain.

[069-018]And as they stood so talking, Sir Kay saw the five kings coming on horseback by

[069-019]themselves alone, with their spears in their hands even toward them. Lo, said

[069-020]Sir Kay, yonder be the five kings; let us go to them and match them. That were

[069-021]folly, said Sir Gawaine, for we are but three and they be five. That is truth,

[069-022]said Sir Griflet. No force, said Sir Kay, I will undertake for two of them, and

[069-023]then may ye three undertake for the other three. And therewithal, Sir Kay let

[069-024]his horse run as fast as he might, and struck one of them through the shield

[069-025]and the body a fathom, that the king fell to the earth stark dead. That saw Sir

[069-026]Gawaine, and ran unto another king so hard that he smote him through the body.

[069-027]And therewithal King Arthur ran to another, and smote him through the body with

[069-028]a spear, that he fell to the earth dead. Then Sir Griflet ran unto the fourth

[069-029]king, and gave him such a fall that his neck brake. Anon Sir Kay ran unto the

[069-030]fifth king, and smote him so hard on the helm that the stroke clave the helm

[069-031]and the head to the earth. That was well stricken, said King Arthur, and

[069-032]worshipfully hast thou holden thy promise, therefore I shall honour thee while

[069-033]that I live. And therewithal they set the queen in a barge into Humber; but

[069-034]always Queen Guenever praised Sir Kay for his deeds, and said, What lady that

[069-035]ye love, and she love you not again she were greatly to blame; and among

[069-036]ladies, said the queen, I shall bear your noble fame, for ye spake a great

[069-037]word, and fulfilled it worshipfully. And therewith the queen departed.

[069-038]Then the king and the three knights rode into the forest, for there they

[069-039]supposed to hear of them that were escaped; and there he found the most part of

[069-040]his people, and told them all how the five kings were dead. And therefore let

[069-041]us hold us together till it be day, and when their host have espied that their

[069-042]chieftains be slain, they will make such dole that they shall no more help

[069-043]themselves. And right so as the king said, so it was; for when they found the

[069-044]five kings dead, they made such dole that they fell from their horses.

[069-045]Therewithal came King Arthur but with a few people, and slew on the left hand

[069-046]and on the right hand, that well-nigh there escaped no man, but all were slain

[069-047]to the number thirty thousand. And when the battle was all ended, the king

[069-048]kneeled down and thanked God meekly. And then he sent for the queen, and soon

[069-049]she was come, and she made great joy of the overcoming of that battle.
