# Sir Morganor

Sir Morganor — who appears in the chronicles under the variant spellings Morganore and King Morganore — was a knight and seneschal of the Arthurian age. He served in the early campaigns of [[King Arthur]]'s reign, rallying troops and facing the enemy in the field. His career spans from the first great battles against the rebel kings to later tournament encounters with [[Sir Tristram]].

## Seneschal in Arthur's Early Wars

When [[King Arthur]] assembled his host to fight the rebellious kings, Morganore held the office of seneschal — an administrative and military position of considerable trust. After a skirmish in which [[Sir Kay]] distinguished himself, Kay came to Morganore's tent to report the outcome. The seneschal's presence at the heart of Arthur's camp indicates that he was among the trusted officers of the royal household (017-063).

In the aftermath of battle, Morganore gathered the people together — the common folk, the wounded, and the camp followers — and organised them into a defensive position. This act of stewardship reveals a knight who understood that war was not only about feats of arms but also about the care of those caught in its wake (019-013).

## The Felling of a King

During the fierce fighting of the early campaign, Morganore himself took the field. But the fortunes of war turned against him: he was felled in combat, and great slaughter followed among his men. The chronicles record that he was struck down — "King Morganore" as one account names him — suggesting that he held a royal or quasi-royal title, perhaps as a petty king allied to Arthur. His fall was a significant moment in the ebb and flow of the battle (019-026).

## The Tournament Encounter with Tristram

Morganor reappears in the chronicles much later, in the time of [[Sir Tristram]]. At a tournament, he and another knight touched shields — the formal challenge that preceded a joust. Morganor was struck down by a knight of surpassing prowess, felled alongside other worthy opponents. This later appearance confirms that Morganor survived the early wars and continued to bear arms in the chivalric contests of the age (182-019) (182-022).

## A Knight of Two Eras

Sir Morganor's career bridges the foundational wars of Arthur's reign and the later age of tournament and adventure that defined the Round Table. As seneschal, he was an organiser and steward; as a knight, he was a fighter who risked — and nearly lost — his life on the battlefield. His multiple names in the chronicles — Morganore, Morganor, King Morganore — reflect the fluid orthography of medieval romance but do not obscure the outline of a loyal knight who served across two distinct phases of the Arthurian story.
