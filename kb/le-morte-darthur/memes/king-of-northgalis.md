# King of Northgalis

The King of Northgalis was a recurring figure in the great tournaments of Sir Tristram's time, appearing frequently as a tournament leader and adversary. His kingdom lay in the northern regions of Arthur's Britain, and he commanded a significant force of knights that contended with the best warriors of the Round Table in many a tournament field.

## The Tournament of the Castle of Maidens

The King of Northgalis first appears prominently at the great tournament of the Castle of Maidens. He led a powerful party of knights, and the tournament was betwixt him and the King of Scotland (112-012). Arthur's knights that rode with the King of Northgalis included many proven warriors, for the king said it was the fellowship of the Round Table that aided him (114-021).

The King of Northgalis brought eight score helms to the field, and the knights of his party jousted fiercely against the knights of King Bagdemagus and the King of Scotland (114-036, 115-026). At that tournament, however, the knights of the King of Northgalis suffered a grievous defeat, for many of them were smitten down.

## Conflicts with Sir Tristram's Party

At the great tournament at the Castle of Hard Rock — the tournament that Sir Tristram won — the King of Northgalis was among the contending kings. His knights did fare well, and at one point they and the knights of King Carados put the King of Northgalis and his party to the worse (232-009). Sir Palomides, fighting for the King of Northgalis, did many great deeds of arms (230-048, 230-049).

Sir Palomides, returned from the King of Northgalis's party, rode against him in later rounds, as the shifting alliances of the tournament demanded (233-002). The King of Northgalis's knights were among those who espied Sir Tristram and pursued him on the field (231-019).

## The Prize of the Tournament

In the end, the King of Northgalis was awarded the prize of the tournament because Sir Tristram had departed secretly and would not be known (234-007). The King of Northgalis accepted the honour, though all acknowledged that Tristram had done best of all.

## Later Appearances

The King of Northgalis continued to appear at major gatherings. So the King of Northgalis and the King with the Hundred Knights, and the King of Scotland, and King Carados were all among the kings who assembled and contended for honour (235-002, 235-004). He also rode against Sir Launcelot du Lake in later tournaments (227-020).
