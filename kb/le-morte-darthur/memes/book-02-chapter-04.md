[035-001]damosel.

[035-002]At that time there was a knight, the which was the king’s son of Ireland,

[035-003]and his name was Lanceor, the which was an orgulous knight, and counted himself

[035-004]one of the best of the court; and he had great despite at Balin for the

[035-005]achieving of the sword, that any should be accounted more hardy, or more of

[035-006][[courage|prowess]]; and he asked [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] if he would give him leave to ride after

[035-007]Balin and to revenge the despite that he had done. Do your best, said Arthur, I

[035-008]am right wroth with Balin; I would he were quit of the despite that he hath

[035-009]done to me and to my court. Then this Lanceor went to his hostelry to make him

[035-010]ready. In the meanwhile came [[merlin|Merlin]] unto [[camelot|the court of King Arthur]], and there

[035-011]was told him the adventure of the sword, and the death of the [[lady-of-the-lake|Lady of the Lake]].

[035-012]Now shall I say you, said Merlin; this same damosel that here standeth, that

[035-013]brought the sword unto your court, I shall tell you the cause of her coming:

[035-014]she was the falsest damosel that liveth. Say not so, said they. She hath a

[035-015]brother, a passing good knight of prowess and a full true man; and this damosel

[035-016]loved another knight that held her to paramour, and this good knight her

[035-017]brother met with the knight that held her to paramour, and slew him by force of

[035-018]his hands. When this false damosel understood this, she went to the Lady Lile

[035-019]of Avelion, and besought her of help, to be avenged on her own brother.
