[098-001]York, and how he ordained the realm should be governed in his absence.

[098-002]Now leave we of Lucius the Emperor and speak we of [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], that commanded

[098-003]all them of his retinue to be ready at the utas of Hilary for to hold a

[098-004]parliament at York. And at that parliament was concluded to arrest all the navy

[098-005]of the land, and to be ready within fifteen days at Sandwich, and there he

[098-006]showed to his army how he purposed to conquer the empire which he ought to have

[098-007]of right. And there he ordained two governors of this realm, that is to say,

[098-008]Sir Baudwin of Britain, for to counsel to the best, and Sir Constantine, son to

[098-009]Sir Cador of Cornwall, which after the death of Arthur was king of this realm.

[098-010]And in the presence of all his lords he resigned the rule of the realm and

[098-011][[queen-guenever|Guenever]] his queen to them, wherefore [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot]] was wroth, for he left Sir

[098-012][[sir-tristram|Tristram]] with [[king-mark|King Mark]] for the love of Beale Isould. Then the Queen Guenever

[098-013]made great sorrow for the departing of her lord and other, and swooned in such

[098-014]wise that the ladies bare her into her chamber. Thus the king with his great

[098-015]army departed, leaving the queen and realm in the governance of Sir Baudwin and

[098-016]Constantine. And when he was on his horse he said with an high voice, If I die

[098-017]in this journey I will that Sir Constantine be mine heir and king crowned of

[098-018]this realm as next of my blood. And after departed and entered into the sea at

[098-019]Sandwich with all his army, with a great multitude of ships, galleys, cogs, and

[098-020]dromounds, sailing on the sea.
