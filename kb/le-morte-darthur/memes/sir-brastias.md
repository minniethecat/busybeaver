# Sir Brastias

Sir Brastias was originally a knight of the Duke of Tintagil's household. After the duke's death, he became one of the most trusted knights of King Uther Pendragon and later served King Arthur as warden of the north.

## The Tintagil Deception

Sir Brastias first enters the narrative through Merlin's enchantment at Tintagil. When Merlin devised his plan for King Uther to lie with Igraine, he declared that Sir Ulfius would take the likeness of Sir Brastias, a knight of the duke's household, while Merlin himself would become like Sir Jordanus (005-017). Thus Brastias, though unknowing, played a part in the night of Arthur's conception. After the duke's death, Igraine herself testified that two knights had come in the likeness of Brastias and Jordanus (007-010).

## Deeds of Arms at St. Albans

Sir Brastias proved his valour in battle. At St. Albans, when King Uther rode forth in a horse-litter against the Northern host, Sir Ulfius and Sir Brastias did great deeds of arms (007-010). King Uther's men overcame the Northern battle and slew many people, putting the remnant to flight. Brastias's prowess in this battle confirmed his worth as a knight.

## Trusted Guardian of the Young King

After Uther's death, when the barons sought to protect the young Arthur, Brastias was among the best knights they could get — those whom Uther Pendragon had loved best and most trusted in his days. He was placed about Arthur along with Sir Baudwin of Britain, Sir Kay, and Sir Ulfius, always attending the young king day and night till the feast of Pentecost (009-040).

## Warden of the North

When Arthur was crowned and had established the realm, he appointed his officers. Sir Brastias was made warden to wait upon the north from Trent forwards, for it was at that time the most party of the king's enemies (010-021). Within few years after, Arthur won all the north, Scotland, and all that were under their obeissance.

## The Battle Against the Eleven Kings

When the rebel kings challenged Arthur's authority, Brastias stood among the men of most worship who accompanied the king (012-004). In the battle that followed, Sir Baudwin, Sir Kay, and Sir Brastias slew on the right hand and on the left hand that it was marvel (012-018). He also fought as a messenger alongside Ulfius, riding forth well horsed and well armed to deliver Arthur's letters to the rebel kings.
