[096-001]came to [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] to demand truage for Britain.

[096-002]When King Arthur had after long [[battle|war]] rested, and held a royal feast and Table

[096-003]Round with his allies of kings, princes, and noble knights all of the Round

[096-004]Table, there came into his hall, he sitting in his throne royal, twelve ancient

[096-005]men, bearing each of them a branch of olive, in token that they came as

[096-006]ambassadors and messengers from the Emperor Lucius, which was called at that

[096-007]time, Dictator or Procuror of the Public Weal of Rome. Which said messengers,

[096-008]after their entering and coming into the presence of King Arthur, did to him

[096-009]their obeisance in making to him reverence, and said to him in this wise: The

[096-010]high and mighty Emperor Lucius sendeth to the King of Britain greeting,

[096-011]commanding thee to acknowledge him for thy lord, and to send him the truage due

[096-012]of this realm unto the Empire, which thy father and other to-fore thy

[096-013]precessors have paid as is of record, and thou as rebel not knowing him as thy

[096-014]sovereign, withholdest and retainest contrary to the statutes and decrees made

[096-015]by the noble and worthy Julius Cesar, conqueror of this realm, and first

[096-016]Emperor of Rome. And if thou refuse his demand and commandment know thou for

[096-017]certain that he shall make strong war against thee, thy realms and lands, and

[096-018]shall chastise thee and thy subjects, that it shall be ensample perpetual unto

[096-019]all kings and princes, for to deny their truage unto that noble empire which

[096-020]domineth upon the universal world. Then when they had showed the effect of

[096-021]their message, the king commanded them to withdraw them, and said he should

[096-022]take advice of council and give to them an answer. Then some of the young

[096-023]knights, hearing this their message, would have run on them to have slain them,

[096-024]saying that it was a rebuke to all the knights there being present to suffer

[096-025]them to say so to the king. And anon the king commanded that none of them, upon

[096-026]pain of death, to missay them nor do them any harm, and commanded a knight to

[096-027]bring them to their lodging, and see that they have all that is necessary and

[096-028]requisite for them, with the best cheer, and that no dainty be spared, for the

[096-029]Romans be great lords, and though their message please me not nor my court, yet

[096-030]I must remember mine honour.

[096-031]After this the king let call all his lords and knights of the [[round-table|Round Table]] to

[096-032]counsel upon this matter, and desired them to say their advice. Then Sir Cador

[096-033]of Cornwall spake first and said, Sir, this message liketh me well, for we have

[096-034]many days rested us and have been idle, and now I hope ye shall make sharp war

[096-035]on the Romans, where I doubt not we shall get honour. I believe well, said

[096-036]Arthur, that this matter pleaseth thee well, but these answers may not be

[096-037]answered, for the demand grieveth me sore, for truly I will never pay truage to

[096-038]Rome, wherefore I pray you to counsel me. I have understood that Belinus and

[096-039]Brenius, kings of Britain, have had the empire in their hands many days, and

[096-040]also Constantine the son of Heleine, which is an open evidence that we owe no

[096-041]tribute to Rome but of right we that be descended of them have right to claim

[096-042]the title of the empire.
