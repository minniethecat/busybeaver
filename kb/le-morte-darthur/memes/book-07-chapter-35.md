[162-001]officers were made at the feast of the wedding, and of the [[tournament|jousts]] at the

[162-002]feast.

[162-003]So it drew fast to Michaelmas; and thither came Dame Lionesse, the lady of the

[162-004]Castle Perilous, and her sister, Dame Linet, with Sir Gringamore, her brother,

[162-005]with them for he had the conduct of these ladies. And there they were lodged at

[162-006]the device of [[king-arthur|King Arthur]]. And upon Michaelmas Day the Bishop of Canterbury

[162-007]made the wedding betwixt [[sir-gareth|Sir Gareth]] and the Lady Lionesse with great solemnity.

[162-008]And King Arthur made Gaheris to wed the Damosel Savage, that was Dame Linet;

[162-009]and King Arthur made Sir Agravaine to wed Dame Lionesse’s niece, a fair

[162-010]lady, her name was Dame Laurel.

[162-011]And so when this solemnization was done, then came in the Green Knight, Sir

[162-012]Pertolepe, with thirty knights, and there he did homage and fealty to Sir

[162-013]Gareth, and these knights to hold of him for evermore. Also Sir Pertolepe said:

[162-014]I pray you that at this feast I may be your chamberlain. With a good will, said

[162-015]Sir Gareth sith it liketh you to take so simple an office. Then came in the Red

[162-016]Knight, with three score knights with him, and did to Sir Gareth homage and

[162-017]fealty, and all those knights to hold of him for evermore. And then this Sir

[162-018]Perimones prayed Sir Gareth to grant him to be his chief butler at that high

[162-019]feast. I will well, said Sir Gareth, that ye have this office, and it were

[162-020]better. Then came in Sir Persant of Inde, with an hundred knights with him, and

[162-021]there he did homage and fealty, and all his knights should do him service, and

[162-022]hold their lands of him for ever; and there he prayed Sir Gareth to make him

[162-023]his sewer-chief at the feast. I will well, said Sir Gareth, that ye have it and

[162-024]it were better. Then came the Duke de la Rowse with an hundred knights with

[162-025]him, and there he did homage and fealty to Sir Gareth, and so to hold their

[162-026]lands of him for ever. And he required Sir Gareth that he might serve him of

[162-027]the wine that day of that feast. I will well, said Sir Gareth, and it were

[162-028]better. Then came in the Red Knight of the Red Launds, that was Sir Ironside,

[162-029]and he brought with him three hundred knights, and there he did homage and

[162-030]fealty, and all these knights to hold their lands of him for ever. And then he

[162-031]asked Sir Gareth to be his carver. I will well, said Sir Gareth, an it please

[162-032]you.

[162-033]Then came into the court thirty ladies, and all they seemed widows, and those

[162-034]thirty ladies brought with them many fair gentlewomen. And all they kneeled

[162-035]down at once unto King Arthur and unto Sir Gareth, and there all those ladies

[162-036]told the king how Sir Gareth delivered them from the dolorous tower, and slew

[162-037]the Brown Knight without Pity: And therefore we, and our heirs for evermore,

[162-038]will do homage unto Sir Gareth of Orkney. So then the kings and queens, princes

[162-039]and earls, barons and many bold knights, went unto meat; and well may ye wit

[162-040]there were all manner of meat plenteously, all manner revels and games, with

[162-041]all manner of minstrelsy that was used in those days. Also there was great

[162-042]jousts three days. But the king would not suffer Sir Gareth to joust, because

[162-043]of his new bride; for, as the French book saith, that Dame Lionesse desired of

[162-044]the king that none that were wedded should joust at that feast.

[162-045]So the first day there jousted Sir Lamorak de Galis, for he overthrew thirty

[162-046]knights, and did passing marvellously deeds of arms; and then King Arthur made

[162-047]Sir Persant and his two brethren Knights of the [[round-table|Round Table]] to their

[162-048]lives’ end, and gave them great lands. Also the second day there jousted

[162-049][[sir-tristram|Tristram]] best, and he overthrew forty knights, and did there [[marvel|marvellous]] deeds

[162-050]of arms. And there King Arthur made Ironside, that was the Red Knight of the

[162-051]Red Launds, a Knight of the Table Round to his life’s end, and gave him

[162-052]great lands. The third day there jousted [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot du Lake]], and he

[162-053]overthrew fifty knights, and did many marvellous deeds of arms, that all men

[162-054]wondered on him. And there King Arthur made the Duke de la Rowse a Knight of

[162-055]the Round Table to his life’s end, and gave him great lands to spend. But

[162-056]when these jousts were done, Sir Lamorak and Sir Tristram departed suddenly,

[162-057]and would not be known, for the which King Arthur and all the court were sore

[162-058]displeased. And so they held the court forty days with great solemnity. And

[162-059]this Sir Gareth was a noble knight, and a well-ruled, and fair-languaged.

[162-060]Thus endeth this tale of Sir Gareth of Orkney that wedded Dame Lionesse of the

[162-061]Castle Perilous. And also Sir Gaheris wedded her sister, Dame Linet, that was

[162-062]called the Damosel Sabage. And Sir Agrabaine wedded Dame Laurel, a fair lady

[162-063]and great, and mighty lands with great riches gave with them King Arthur, that

[162-064]royally they might live till their lives’ end.
