# King Cradelment of North Wales

King Cradelment of North Wales was one of the eleven rebel kings who refused to acknowledge Arthur's sovereignty after his coronation. He fought against Arthur in the great battles of the early wars.

## The Rebellion Against Arthur

When Arthur was crowned king, many of the lesser kings of Britain refused to accept the rule of a beardless boy of low birth. Among them was King Cradelment of North Wales, who joined the coalition of rebel kings led by King Lot of Orkney (017-052). Together with the King of the Hundred Knights, King Carados, King Uriens, King Idres, King Brandegoris, King Clariance, King Agwisance, King Nentres, and the Duke of Cambenet, Cradelment swore to bring Arthur down.

## Struck Down by Arthur

In the great battle between Arthur's host and the rebel kings, Cradelment faced the young king himself. Arthur ran unto King Cradelment of North Wales as a lion, and smote him through the left side, that the horse and the king fell down (017-052). As Cradelment lay unhorsed, Arthur took the king's horse by the rein and led it to his old friend Sir Ulfius, saying he had great need of a horse. When the King with the Hundred Knights saw King Cradelment on foot, he rushed to aid, smiting down Sir Ector and giving his horse to Cradelment to rehorse him (017-057). But Arthur pursued, smiting the King of the Hundred Knights on the helm until a quarter of his helm and shield fell down.

## Against Arthur's Knights

Later in the same battle, Ulfius, Brastias, and Sir Ector encountered against King Cradelment and other rebel kings, forcing them to avoid the ground (018-020). Despite these setbacks, Cradelment remained steadfast in the rebel cause. When King Lot proposed that they continue the war rather than make peace, Cradelment joined the other kings in swearing they would never fail each other, neither for life nor for death — and whoso fled should be slain (019-072).
