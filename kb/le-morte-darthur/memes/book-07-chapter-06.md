[133-002]And on the morn the damosel and he took their leave and thanked the knight, and

[133-003]so departed, and rode on their way until they came to a great forest. And there

[133-004]was a great river and but one passage, and there were ready two knights on the

[133-005]farther side to let them the passage. What sayest thou, said the damosel, wilt

[133-006]thou match yonder knights or turn again? Nay, said Sir [[sir-gareth|Beaumains]], I will not

[133-007]turn again an they were six more. And therewithal he rushed into the water, and

[133-008]in midst of the water either brake their spears upon other to their hands, and

[133-009]then they drew their swords, and smote eagerly at other. And at the last Sir

[133-010]Beaumains smote the other upon the helm that his head stonied, and therewithal

[133-011]he fell down in the water, and there was he drowned. And then he spurred his

[133-012]horse upon the land, where the other knight fell upon him, and brake his spear,

[133-013]and so they drew their swords and fought long together. At the last Sir

[133-014]Beaumains clave his helm and his head down to the shoulders; and so he rode

[133-015]unto the damosel and bade her ride forth on her way.

[133-016]Alas, she said, that ever a kitchen page should have that fortune to destroy

[133-017]such two doughty knights: thou weenest thou hast done doughtily, that is not

[133-018]so; for the first knight his horse stumbled, and there he was drowned in the

[133-019]water, and never it was by thy force, nor by thy might. And the last knight by

[133-020]mishap thou camest behind him and mishappily thou slew him.

[133-021]Damosel, said Beaumains, ye may say what ye will, but with whomsomever I have

[133-022]ado withal, I trust to God to serve him or he depart. And therefore I reck not

[133-023]what ye say, so that I may win your lady. Fie, fie, foul kitchen knave, thou

[133-024]shalt see knights that shall abate thy boast. Fair damosel, give me goodly

[133-025]language, and then my care is past, for what knights somever they be, I care

[133-026]not, nor I doubt them not. Also, said she, I say it for thine avail, yet mayest

[133-027]thou turn again with thy [[honour|worship]]; for an thou follow me, thou art but slain,

[133-028]for I see all that ever thou dost is but by misadventure, and not by [[courage|prowess]] of

[133-029]thy hands. Well, damosel, ye may say what ye will, but wheresomever ye go I

[133-030]will follow you. So this Beaumains rode with that lady till evensong time, and

[133-031]ever she chid him, and would not rest. And they came to a black laund; and

[133-032]there was a black hawthorn, and thereon hung a black banner, and on the other

[133-033]side there hung a black shield, and by it stood a black spear great and long,

[133-034]and a great black horse covered with silk, and a black stone fast by.
