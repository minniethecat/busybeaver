The **spear**—or lance—is the opening weapon of knightly [[battle|combat]] in Malory's [[Le Morte d'Arthur]]. Before the [[sword]] is drawn, before the [[shield]] is shattered, the spear delivers the first and often decisive blow of the encounter. It is the weapon of the charge, the tournament, and—in its most charged symbolic form—the Bleeding Spear of the [[Sangreal]] quest. As both practical instrument and sacred object, the spear spans the full range of Malory's world, from the brutal efficiency of the battle[[battle|field]] to the mysteries of divine revelation.

## The Spear in Battle

In the massed encounters that punctuate Malory's early books, the spear is the weapon that initiates [[battle|combat]]. Knights couch their spears and ride at one another with the full weight of horse and [[arms|armour]] behind the point:

> they dressed their spears, and ran together with great Random (013-040)

The brea[[kinsman|kin]]g of spears—"a great medley of brea[[kinsman|kin]]g of spears" (018-034)—is Malory's shorthand for the intensity of the en[[challenge|gage]]ment. A spear that finds its mark can kill a duke and his horse in a single blow (017-027), while a spent spear demands that the knight reach for his sword and continue the [[battle|fight]] on closer terms.

## The Tournament Spear

In the tournament, the spear takes on a sporting dimension while remaining a lethal instrument. Knights "took new spears" between courses (019-075), refreshing their primary weapon for the next pass. The tournament spear is both a tool of display—demonstrating the knight's skill before an audience—and a means of winning [[honour]] and [[worship]] through victory. The knight who breaks the most spears in a tournament earns the day's glory, and the spear count becomes a metric of [[prowess]].

## The Short Spear

Malory distinguishes between the long lance of the mounted charge and the "short spear that was great" (018-002), a weapon used on foot or in exceptional circumstances. The short spear delivers force at closer range, and its use signals a shift from the formal cadence of the joust to the desperate improvisation of close [[battle|combat]]. It is the weapon of the knight who has been unhorsed, or who seeks to kill rather than to unhorse.

## The Symbolic Spear

Beyond its martial uses, the spear takes on symbolic weight in the Grail narrative. The Bleeding Spear—the spear that pierced Christ's side—appears in the [[Sangreal]] quest as a sacred relic, its blood a perpetual testimony to sacrifice and redemption. Where the battle[[battle|field]] spear [[wound|wounds]] and kills, the Grail spear heals and reveals. The transformation of the spear from instrument of violence to vessel of grace is one of the central symbolic movements of Malory's work, mapping the [[knight]]'s journey from worldly [[chivalry]] to spiritual [[perfection]].
