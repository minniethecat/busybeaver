# Sir Gareth
**Sir Gareth** of Orkney is [[king-arthur|King Arthur]]'s nephew and the youngest brother of [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]] in Thomas Malory's *Le Morte d'[[king-arthur|Arthur]]*. His story — the "Tale of Sir Gareth" — is one of the most self-contained romances in the Morte, and his death at [[sir-launcelot|Launcelot]]'s hands during the rescue of [[queen-guenever|Guenever]] is the event that makes the final t[[wrath|rage]]dy inevitable.

## The Fair Unknown
Gareth arrives at [[camelot|Arthur's court]] in disguise, refusing to reveal his name, and accepts a year of kitchen service — earning the moc[[kinsman|kin]]g nickname "Beaumains" (Fair Hands) from [[sir-kay|Sir Kay]]. His patient [[hardiness|endurance]] of humiliation and his subsequent proving of his worth through deeds rather than [[lineage|birth]] make him an exemplar of meritocratic chivalry.

## The Tragic Death
Gareth's death is arguably the pivotal event of the Morte's tragic finale. When [[sir-launcelot|Launcelot]] rescues [[queen-guenever|Guenever]] from the stake, he [[battle|fight]]s his way through the guards and kills — unknowingly — the unarmed Gareth, who had been ordered to attend the execution but refused to bear arms against [[sir-launcelot|Launcelot]]. [[sir-gawaine|Gawaine]]'s grief over his brother's death transforms him from [[sir-launcelot|Launcelot]]'s friend into his implacable enemy, setting in motion the chain of events that destroys the [[round-table|Round Table]].
