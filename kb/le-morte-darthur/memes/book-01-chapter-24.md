[027-002]Therewithal came [[merlin|Merlin]] and said, Knight, hold thy hand, for an thou slay that

[027-003]knight thou puttest this realm in the greatest damage that ever was realm: for

[027-007]knight, that he fell to the earth in a great sleep. Then Merlin took up King

[027-008]Arthur, and rode forth on the knight’s horse. Alas! said Arthur, what

[027-009]hast thou done, Merlin? hast thou slain this good knight by thy crafts? There

[027-010]liveth not so worshipful a knight as he was; I had liefer than the stint of my

[027-011]land a year that he were alive. Care ye not, said Merlin, for he is wholer than

[027-012]ye; for he is but asleep, and will awake within three hours. I told you, said

[027-013]Merlin, what a knight he was; here had ye been slain had I not been. Also there

[027-014]liveth not a bigger knight than he is one, and he shall hereafter do you right

[027-015]good service; and his name is Pellinore, and he shall have two sons that shall

[027-016]be passing good men; save one they shall have no fellow of [[courage|prowess]] and of good

[027-017]living, and their names shall be [[sir-percivale|Percivale]] of Wales and Lamerake of Wales, and

[027-018]he shall tell you the name of your own son, begotten of your sister, that shall

[027-019]be the destruction of all this realm.
