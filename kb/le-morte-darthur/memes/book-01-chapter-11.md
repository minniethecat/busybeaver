[014-001]and the two kings Ban and [[sir-bors|Bors]], and how they went over the sea.

[014-002]And [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] and the two kings let depart the seven hundred knights in two

[014-003]parties. And there were three hundred knights of the realm of Benwick and of

[014-004]Gaul turned on the other side. Then they dressed their shields, and began to

[014-005]couch their spears many good knights. So Griflet was the first that met with a

[014-006]knight, one Ladinas, and they met so eagerly that all men had [[marvel|wonder]]; and they

[014-007]so fought that their shields fell to pieces, and horse and man fell to the

[014-008]earth; and both the French knight and the English knight lay so long that all

[014-009]men weened they had been dead. When Lucas the butler saw Griflet so lie, he

[014-010]horsed him again anon, and they two did marvellous deeds of arms with many

[014-011]bachelors. Also [[sir-kay|Sir Kay]] came out of an ambushment with five knights with him,

[014-012]and they six smote other six down. But Sir Kay did that day marvellous deeds of

[014-013]arms, that there was none did so well as he that day. Then there came Ladinas

[014-014]and Gracian, two knights of France, and did passing well, that all men praised

[014-015]them.

[014-016]Then came there Sir Placidas, a good knight, and met with Sir Kay, and smote

[014-017]him down horse and man, where fore Sir Griflet was wroth, and met with Sir

[014-018]Placidas so hard, that horse and man fell to the earth. But when the five

[014-019]knights wist that Sir Kay had a fall, they were wroth out of wit, and therewith

[014-020]each of them five bare down a knight. When King Arthur and the two kings saw

[014-021]them begin to wax wroth on both parties, they leapt on small hackneys, and let

[014-022]cry that all men should depart unto their lodging. And so they went home and

[014-023]unarmed them, and so to evensong and supper. And after, the three kings went

[014-024]into a garden, and gave the prize unto Sir Kay, and to Lucas the butler, and

[014-025]unto Sir Griflet. And then they went unto council, and with them Gwenbaus, the

[014-026]brother unto Sir Ban and Bors, a wise clerk, and thither went Ulfius and

[014-027]Brastias, and [[merlin|Merlin]]. And after they had been in council, they went unto bed.

[014-028]And on the morn they heard mass, and to dinner, and so to their council, and

[014-029]made many arguments what were best to do. At the last they were concluded, that

[014-030]Merlin should go with a token of King Ban, and that was a ring, unto his men

[014-031]and King Bors’; and Gracian and Placidas should go again and keep their

[014-032]castles and their countries, as for [dread of King Claudas] King Ban of

[014-033]Benwick, and King Bors of Gaul had ordained them, and so passed the sea and

[014-034]came to Benwick. And when the people saw King Ban’s ring, and Gracian and

[014-035]Placidas, they were glad, and asked how the kings fared, and made great joy of

[014-036]their welfare and cording, and according unto the sovereign lords desire, the

[014-037]men of [[battle|war]] made them ready in all haste possible, so that they were fifteen

[014-038]thousand on horse and foot, and they had great plenty of victual with them, by

[014-039]Merlin’s provision. But Gracian and Placidas were left to furnish and

[014-040]garnish the castles, for dread of King Claudas. Right so Merlin passed the sea,

[014-041]well victualled both by water and by land. And when he came to the sea he sent

[014-042]home the footmen again, and took no more with him but ten thousand men on

[014-043]horseback, the most part men of arms, and so shipped and passed the sea into

[014-044]England, and landed at Dover; and through the wit of Merlin, he had the host

[014-045]northward, the priviest way that could be thought, unto the forest of

[014-046]Bedegraine, and there in a valley he lodged them secretly.

[014-047]Then rode Merlin unto Arthur and the two kings, and told them how he had sped;

[014-048]whereof they had great marvel, that man on earth might speed so soon, and go

[014-049]and come. So Merlin told them ten thousand were in the forest of Bedegraine,

[014-050]well armed at all points. Then was there no more to say, but to horseback went

[014-051]all the host as Arthur had afore purveyed. So with twenty thousand he passed by

[014-052]night and day, but there was made such an ordinance afore by Merlin, that there

[014-053]should no man of war ride nor go in no country on this side Trent water, but if

[014-054]he had a token from King Arthur, where through the king’s enemies durst

[014-055]not ride as they did to-fore to espy.
