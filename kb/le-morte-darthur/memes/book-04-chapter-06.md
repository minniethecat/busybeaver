[072-001]Accolon of Gaul, chased an hart, and of their [[marvel|marvellous]] adventures.

[072-002]Then it befell that [[king-arthur|Arthur]] and many of his knights rode a-hunting into a great

[072-003]forest, and it happed King Arthur, King Uriens, and Sir Accolon of Gaul,

[072-004]followed a great hart, for they three were well horsed, and so they chased so

[072-005]fast that within a while they three were then ten mile from their fellowship.

[072-006]And at the last they chased so sore that they slew their horses underneath

[072-007]them. Then were they all three on foot, and ever they saw the hart afore them

[072-008]passing weary and enbushed. What will we do? said King Arthur, we are hard

[072-009]bestead. Let us go on foot, said King Uriens, till we may meet with some

[072-010]lodging. Then were they ware of the hart that lay on a great water bank, and a

[072-011]brachet biting on his throat, and more other hounds came after. Then King

[072-012]Arthur blew the prise and dight the hart.

[072-013]Then the king looked about the world, and saw afore him in a great water a

[072-014]little ship, all apparelled with silk down to the water, and the ship came

[072-015]right unto them and landed on the sands. Then Arthur went to the bank and

[072-016]looked in, and saw none earthly creature therein. Sirs, said the king, come

[072-017]thence, and let us see what is in this ship. So they went in all three, and

[072-018]found it richly behanged with cloth of silk. By then it was dark night, and

[072-019]there suddenly were about them an hundred torches set upon all the sides of the

[072-020]ship boards, and it gave great light; and therewithal there came out twelve

[072-021]fair damosels and saluted King Arthur on their knees, and called him by his

[072-022]name, and said he was right welcome, and such cheer as they had he should have

[072-023]of the best. The king thanked them fair. Therewithal they led the king and his

[072-024]two fellows into a fair chamber, and there was a cloth laid, richly beseen of

[072-025]all that longed unto a table, and there were they served of all wines and meats

[072-026]that they could think; of that the king had great marvel, for he fared never

[072-027]better in his life as for one supper. And so when they had supped at their

[072-028]leisure, King Arthur was led into a chamber, a richer beseen chamber saw he

[072-029]never none, and so was King Uriens served, and led into such another chamber,

[072-030]and Sir Accolon was led into the third chamber passing richly and well beseen;

[072-031]and so they were laid in their beds easily. And anon they fell asleep, and

[072-032]slept marvellously sore all the night. And on the morrow King Uriens was in

[072-033][[camelot|Camelot]] abed in his wife’s arms, [[morgan-le-fay|Morgan le Fay]]. And when he awoke he had

[072-034]great marvel, how he came there, for on the even afore he was two days’

[072-035]journey from Camelot. And when King Arthur awoke he found himself in a dark

[072-036]prison, hearing about him many complaints of woful knights.
