[164-001]and how his mother died at his birth, wherefore she named him [[sir-tristram|Tristram]].

[164-002]It was a king that hight Meliodas, and he was lord and king of the country of

[164-003]Liones, and this Meliodas was a likely knight as any was that time living. And

[164-004]by fortune he wedded [[king-mark|King Mark]]’s sister of Cornwall, and she was called

[164-005]Elizabeth, that was called both good and fair. And at that time [[king-arthur|King Arthur]]

[164-006]reigned, and he was whole king of England, Wales, and Scotland, and of many

[164-007]other realms: howbeit there were many kings that were lords of many countries,

[164-008]but all they held their lands of King Arthur; for in Wales were two kings, and

[164-009]in the north were many kings; and in Cornwall and in the west were two kings;

[164-010]also in Ireland were two or three kings, and all were under the obeissance of

[164-011]King Arthur. So was the King of France, and the King of Brittany, and all the

[164-012]lordships unto Rome.

[164-013]So when this King Meliodas had been with his wife, within a while she waxed

[164-014]great with child, and she was a full meek lady, and well she loved her lord,

[164-015]and he her again, so there was great joy betwixt them. Then there was a lady in

[164-016]that country that had loved King Meliodas long, and by no mean she never could

[164-017]get his love; therefore she let ordain upon a day, as King Meliodas rode

[164-018]a-hunting, for he was a great chaser, and there by an enchantment she made him

[164-019]chase an hart by himself alone till that he came to an old castle, and there

[164-020]anon he was taken prisoner by the lady that him loved. When Elizabeth, King

[164-021]Meliodas’ wife, missed her lord, and she was nigh out of her wit, and

[164-022]also as great with child as she was, she took a gentlewoman with her, and ran

[164-023]into the forest to seek her lord. And when she was far in the forest she might

[164-024]no farther, for she began to travail fast of her child. And she had many grimly

[164-025]throes; her gentlewoman helped her all that she might, and so by [[marvel|miracle]] of Our

[164-026]Lady of Heaven she was delivered with great pains. But she had taken such cold

[164-027]for the default of help that deep draughts of death took her, that needs she

[164-028]must die and depart out of this world; there was none other bote.

[164-029]And when this Queen Elizabeth saw that there was none other bote, then she made

[164-030]great dole, and said unto her gentlewoman: When ye see my lord, King Meliodas,

[164-031]recommend me unto him, and tell him what pains I endure here for his love, and

[164-032]how I must die here for his sake for default of good help; and let him wit that

[164-033]I am full sorry to depart out of this world from him, therefore pray him to be

[164-034]friend to my soul. Now let me see my little child, for whom I have had all this

[164-035]sorrow. And when she saw him she said thus: Ah, my little son, thou hast

[164-036]murdered thy mother, and therefore I suppose, thou that art a murderer so

[164-037]young, thou art full likely to be a manly man in thine age. And because I shall

[164-038]die of the birth of thee, I charge thee, gentlewoman, that thou pray my lord,

[164-039]King Meliodas, that when he is christened let call him Tristram, that is as

[164-040]much to say as a sorrowful birth. And therewith this queen gave up the ghost

[164-041]and died. Then the gentlewoman laid her under an umbre of a great tree, and

[164-042]then she lapped the child as well as she might for cold. Right so there came

[164-043]the barons, following after the queen, and when they saw that she was dead, and

[164-044]understood none other but the king was destroyed.
