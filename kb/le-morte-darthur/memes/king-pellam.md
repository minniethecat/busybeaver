# King Pellam

King Pellam of Listeneise was the guardian of the Holy Grail and the most worshipful man of his time, near kin of Joseph of Arimathea. His wounding by the Dolorous Stroke brought devastation upon three countries, and he lay many years sore wounded until his healing in the Sangreal quest.

## Lord of the Grail Castle

King Pellam was of the lineage of Joseph of Arimathea, and within his castle was preserved part of the blood of Our Lord Jesus Christ that Joseph had brought into the land (047-009). Upon a rich bed within a marvellously well-dight chamber lay the sacred relics, guarded by a table of clean gold with four pillars of silver. Upon this table stood the marvellous spear — the same spear that Longius smote Our Lord to the heart (046-011). Pellam was the most worshipful man that lived in those days (047-009).

## The Feast and the Dolorous Stroke

King Pellam made a great feast in his country of Listeneise, and no knight might come unless he brought his wife or his paramour (045-014). Among those who came was Sir Balin, who had long sought the invisible knight Garlon — Pellam's own brother. At the feast, Balin found and slew Garlon.

When King Pellam learned that Balin had slain his brother, he arose fiercely and declared that Balin should die. Pellam caught in his hand a grim weapon and smote eagerly at Balin — there would be no man to have ado with Balin but Pellam himself, for the love of his brother (046-006). As Pellam pursued him, Balin's sword burst in sunder. Weaponless, Balin fled from chamber to chamber, King Pellam always after him (046-007).

At the last, Balin entered the rich chamber of the Grail and seized the marvellous spear. He turned upon King Pellam and smote him passingly sore (046-011). This was the Dolorous Stroke. The castle roof and walls brake and fell to the earth, and the most part of the castle lay upon Pellam and Balin three days (046-017).

## The Wounded King

King Pellam lay so, many years sore wounded, and might never be whole (047-004). Through the Dolorous Stroke, three countries were destroyed, and great dole, tray, and tene followed (047-016). Merlin came and told King Arthur of the dolorous stroke that Balin gave to King Pellam, marking it as one of the great tragedies of that age (047-009).

The wound could only be healed by the best knight of the world. It was prophesied that Galahad, the haut prince, would heal King Pellam in the quest of the Sangreal (047-004). In that place was part of the blood of Our Lord Jesus Christ, and Pellam was nigh of Joseph's kin — great pity it was of his hurt (047-009).
