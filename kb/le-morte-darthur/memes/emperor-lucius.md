# Emperor Lucius

Emperor Lucius, also called Lucius the Emperor and sometimes identified as Lucius Tiberius, was the [[Roman Empire|Roman]] ruler who challenged the sovereignty of [[King Arthur]] by demanding tribute from Britain. His refusal to accept Arthur's defiance led to a climactic military campaign in which the Emperor amassed a vast host only to fall before Arthur's forces, cementing the ascendancy of Arthur's Britain over the ancient Roman imperium.

## The Demand for Tribute

The conflict began when messengers arrived at [[King Arthur]]'s court bearing an ultimatum from Emperor Lucius. The Emperor commanded Arthur to acknowledge Roman supremacy and pay the tribute traditionally owed by British kings to Rome (096-006). Lucius styled himself "high and mighty Emperor" and sent his demand to the King of Britain in peremptory terms (096-010). When the messengers had delivered their message and returned to Lucius to report, the Emperor received them with the expectation of submission (097-032).

## Arthur's Defiance

Upon hearing the Roman demand, King Arthur took counsel and resolved to resist. Lucius waited for Britain's reply as Arthur prepared for war. When the Emperor understood that Arthur meant to fight rather than submit, he began marshalling his forces for the coming confrontation (097-034). Arthur sent his own messengers to Lucius, commanding him in Arthur's name to prepare for battle or withdraw from the lands he occupied (101-011). To this demand Lucius answered with defiance, setting the stage for war (101-012).

## The Great Host

Emperor Lucius assembled an enormous army drawn from across the Roman dominions. He gathered forces that included many Saracens among his ranks, and his host was said to number over a hundred thousand men (103-040). Lucius also benefited from the prior conquests of King Claudas in Gaul, incorporating those territories into his campaign (097-070). The scale of his army reflected the full military might of the Roman imperium, making the coming battle one of the largest in Arthurian chronicle.

## The Battle with King Arthur

The confrontation between Emperor Lucius and King Arthur was long and fiercely contested. Lucius fought with determination, and at one point he struck Arthur directly in combat (103-044). The battle drew in knights from both sides, and the Emperor himself proved a capable warrior in the field. Yet despite the size and strength of his host, Lucius could not overcome the combined prowess of Arthur and his knights.

## Death and Aftermath

Emperor Lucius fell in the great battle against Arthur's forces. After the fighting ended, Arthur's men went to the place where the Emperor lay dead upon the field (103-058). The King commanded that Lucius's body be treated with honour, laid in a car covered with the arms of the Roman imperium (103-079). The death of Lucius marked the decisive victory of Arthur over Rome, lifting any future obligation of British tribute and securing Arthur's reputation as a conquering sovereign equal to any emperor in Christendom.
