# Camelot

**Camelot** (also called Carlion in Malory) is the geographical and symbolic centre of the [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian world. It is the city where [[King Arthur]] holds his court, where the [[Round Table]] assembles at the great feasts, and where the fellowship is finally broken.

Caxton's preface records "Camelot, the great stones and the [[marvel|marvellous]] works of iron lying under the ground" (002-059), mar[[kinsman|kin]]g it from the outset as a place of [[marvel|wonder]] — a city whose very stones testify to a greatness that is simultaneously monumental and already buried.

## The City of the Round Table

Camelot is identified by Malory with Winchester: "the City of Camelot, that is in English Winchester" (050-022). This identification grounds the legendary city in English geography, giving the [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian world a tangible centre.

It is to Camelot that knights ride from every corner of the realm: "All the knights and gentlemen of arms should draw unto a castle called Camelot" (032-011). Every year the knights "were sworn at the [[feast-of-pentecost|high feast of Pentecost]]" (065-036), ma[[kinsman|kin]]g Camelot the place where chivalry is publicly affirmed.

## The Stage of Great Events

The defining events of the [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian cycle occur at or pass through Camelot. [[king-arthur|Arthur]] is wedded to [[queen-guenever|Guenever]] there — "the [[kinsman|kin]]g was wedded at Camelot" (056-003) — binding the [[kinsman|kin]]g's personal destiny to the city.

It is at Camelot that [[Merlin]] appears with his prophecies: "Right so rode he forth unto Camelot. And anon as he was come, [[merlin|Merlin]] des[[wrath|ire]]d of the [[kinsman|kin]]g to have his charge" (058-036).

It is at Camelot too that the [[Sangreal]] appears at the [[Feast of Pentecost]], setting in motion the quest that will crown and fracture the [[round-table|Round Table]].

The city is thus not merely backdrop but participant: every knight who rides out from Camelot carries the city's name with him, and every adventure that concludes there affirms Camelot's role as the arbiter of chivalric worth.

## A Court Under Siege

Camelot is not immune from the conflicts that define [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s reign. Wars are made upon it from without; [[treason|treachery]] festers within its walls. [[king-mark|King Mark]] of [[Cornwall]] journeys to Camelot to pay homage, yet his presence is always charged with the threat of [[treason|betrayal]]: "And so departed [[king-mark|King Mark]] unto Camelot to [[king-arthur|King Arthur]]" (039-027).

The final t[[wrath|rage]]dy — the exposure of [[Sir Launcelot]] and [[Queen Guenever]]'s affair — occurs within Camelot itself. The centre cannot hold: Camelot's fall is the fall of the [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian world, and the great stones that Caxton's preface records as already buried become a monument to what was lost.
