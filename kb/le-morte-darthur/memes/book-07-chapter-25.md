[152-001]feast of Pentecost, and [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]] and his brethren came to ask her blessing.

[152-002]So then the king and they went to meat, and were served in the best manner. And

[152-003]as they sat at the meat, there came in [[queen-guenever|the Queen]] of Orkney, with ladies and

[152-004]knights a great number. And then Sir Gawaine, Sir Agravaine, and Gaheris arose,

[152-005]and went to her and saluted her upon their knees, and asked her blessing; for

[152-006]in fifteen year they had not seen her. Then she spake on high to her brother

[152-007][[king-arthur|King Arthur]]: Where have ye done my young son [[sir-gareth|Sir Gareth]]? He was here amongst

[152-008]you a twelvemonth, and ye made a kitchen knave of him, the which is shame to

[152-009]you all. Alas, where have ye done my dear son that was my joy and bliss? O dear

[152-010]mother, said Sir Gawaine, I knew him not. Nor I, said the king, that now me

[152-011]repenteth, but thanked be God he is proved a worshipful knight as any is now

[152-012]living of his years, and I shall never be glad till I may find him.

[152-013]Ah, brother, said the Queen unto King Arthur, and unto Sir Gawaine, and to all

[152-014]her sons, ye did yourself great shame when ye amongst you kept my son in the

[152-015]kitchen and fed him like a poor hog. Fair sister, said King Arthur, ye shall

[152-016]right well wit I knew him not, nor no more did Sir Gawaine, nor his brethren;

[152-017]but sithen it is so, said the king, that he is thus gone from us all, we must

[152-018]shape a remedy to find him. Also, sister, meseemeth ye might have done me to

[152-019]wit of his coming, and then an I had not done well to him ye might have blamed

[152-020]me. For when he came to this court he came leaning upon two men’s

[152-021]shoulders, as though he might not have gone. And then he asked me three gifts;

[152-022]and one he asked the same day, that was that I would give him meat enough that

[152-023]twelvemonth; and the other two gifts he asked that day a twelvemonth, and that

[152-024]was that he might have the adventure of the damosel Linet, and the third was

[152-025]that [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot]] should make him knight when he desired him. And so I granted

[152-026]him all his desire, and many in this court marvelled that he desired his

[152-027]sustenance for a twelvemonth. And thereby, we deemed, many of us, that he was

[152-028]not come of a noble house.

[152-029]Sir, said the Queen of Orkney unto King Arthur her brother, wit ye well that I

[152-030]sent him unto you right well armed and horsed, and worshipfully beseen of his

[152-031]body, and gold and silver plenty to spend. It may be, said the King, but

[152-032]thereof saw we none, save that same day as he departed from us, knights told me

[152-033]that there came a dwarf hither suddenly, and brought him armour and a good

[152-034]horse full well and richly beseen; and thereat we all had marvel from whence

[152-035]that riches came, that we deemed all that he was come of men of [[honour|worship]].

[152-036]Brother, said the queen, all that ye say I believe, for ever sithen he was

[152-037]grown he was marvellously witted, and ever he was faithful and true of his

[152-038]promise. But I marvel, said she, that [[sir-kay|Sir Kay]] did mock him and scorn him, and

[152-039]gave him that name Beaumains; yet, Sir Kay, said the queen, named him more

[152-040]righteously than he weened; for I dare say an he be alive, he is as fair an

[152-041]handed man and well disposed as any is living. Sir, said Arthur, let this

[152-042]language be still, and by the grace of God he shall be found an he be within

[152-043]this seven realms, and let all this pass and be merry, for he is proved to be a

[152-044]man of worship, and that is my joy.
