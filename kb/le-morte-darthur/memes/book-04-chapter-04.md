[070-001]came, and how [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] founded an abbey where the battle was.

[070-002]Therewithal came one to King Arthur, and told him that King Pellinore was

[070-003]within three mile with a great host; and he said, Go unto him, and let him

[070-004]understand how we have sped. So within a while King Pellinore came with a great

[070-005]host, and saluted the people and the king, and there was great joy made on

[070-006]every side. Then the king let search how much people of his party there was

[070-007]slain; and there were found but little past two hundred men slain and eight

[070-008]knights of the [[round-table|Table Round]] in their pavilions. Then the king let rear and

[070-009]devise in the same place whereat the battle was done a fair abbey, and endowed

[070-010]it with great livelihood, and let it call the Abbey of La Beale [[adventure|Adventure]]. But

[070-011]when some of them came into their countries, whereof the five kings were kings,

[070-012]and told them how they were slain, there was made great dole. And all King

[070-013]Arthur’s enemies, as the King of North Wales, and the kings of the North,

[070-014][when they] wist of the battle, they were passing heavy. And so the king

[070-015]returned unto [[camelot|Camelot]] in haste.

[070-016]And when he was come to Camelot he called King Pellinore unto him, and said, Ye

[070-017]understand well that we have lost eight knights of the best of the Table Round,

[070-018]and by your advice we will choose eight again of the best we may find in this

[070-019]court. Sir, said Pellinore, I shall counsel you after my conceit the best:

[070-020]there are in your court full noble knights both of old and young; and therefore

[070-021]by mine advice ye shall choose half of the old and half of the young. Which be

[070-022]the old? said King Arthur. Sir, said King Pellinore, meseemeth that King Uriens

[070-023]that hath wedded your sister [[morgan-le-fay|Morgan le Fay]], and the King of the Lake, and Sir

[070-024]Hervise de Revel, a noble knight, and Sir Galagars, the fourth. This is well

[070-025]devised, said King Arthur, and right so shall it be. Now, which are the four

[070-026]young knights? said Arthur. Sir, said Pellinore, the first is [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]], your

[070-027]nephew, that is as good a knight of his time as any is in this land; and the

[070-028]second as meseemeth best is Sir Griflet le Fise de Dieu, that is a good knight

[070-029]and full desirous in arms, and who may see him live he shall prove a good

[070-030]knight; and the third as meseemeth is well to be one of the knights of the

[070-031]Round Table, [[sir-kay|Sir Kay the Seneschal]], for many times he hath done full

[070-032]worshipfully, and now at your last battle he did full honourably for to

[070-033]undertake to slay two kings. By my head, said Arthur, he is best worth to be a

[070-034]knight of the Round Table of any that ye have rehearsed, an he had done no more

[070-035][[courage|prowess]] in his life days.
