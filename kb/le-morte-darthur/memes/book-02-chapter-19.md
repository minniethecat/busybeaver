[050-001]tomb, and of Balin’s sword.

[050-002]In the morn came [[merlin|Merlin]] and let write Balin’s name on the tomb with

[050-003]letters of gold, that Here lieth Balin le Savage that was the Knight with the

[050-004]Two Swords, and he that smote the Dolorous Stroke. Also Merlin let make there a

[050-005]bed, that there should never man lie therein but he went out of his wit, yet

[050-006][[sir-launcelot|Launcelot]] de Lake fordid that bed through his noblesse. And anon after Balin

[050-007]was dead, Merlin took his sword, and took off the pommel and set on another

[050-008]pommel. So Merlin bade a knight that stood afore him handle that sword, and he

[050-009]assayed, and he might not handle it. Then Merlin laughed. Why laugh ye? said

[050-010]the knight. This is the cause, said Merlin: there shall never man handle this

[050-011]sword but the best knight of the world, and that shall be Sir Launcelot or else

[050-012]Galahad his son, and Launcelot with this sword shall slay the man that in the

[050-013]world he loved best, that shall be [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]]. All this he let write in the

[050-014]pommel of the sword. Then Merlin let make a bridge of iron and of steel into

[050-015]that island, and it was but half a foot broad, and there shall never man pass

[050-016]that bridge, nor have [[courage|hardiness]] to go over, but if he were a passing good man

[050-017]and a good knight without [[treason|treachery]] or villainy. Also the scabbard of

[050-018]Balin’s sword Merlin left it on this side the island, that Galahad should

[050-019]find it. Also Merlin let make by his subtilty that Balin’s sword was put

[050-020]in a marble stone standing upright as great as a mill stone, and the stone

[050-021]hoved always above the water and did many years, and so by adventure it swam

[050-022]down the stream to the City of [[camelot|Camelot]], that is in English Winchester. And that

[050-023]same day Galahad the haut prince came with [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], and so Galahad brought

[050-024]with him the scabbard and achieved the sword that was there in the marble stone

[050-025]hoving upon the water. And on Whitsunday he achieved the sword as it is

[050-026]rehearsed in the book of Sangreal.

[050-027]Soon after this was done Merlin came to King Arthur and told him of the

[050-028]dolorous stroke that Balin gave to King Pellam, and how Balin and Balan fought

[050-029]together the marvellest battle that ever was heard of, and how they were buried

[050-030]both in one tomb. Alas, said King Arthur, this is the greatest [[mercy|pity]] that ever I

[050-031]heard tell of two knights, for in the world I know not such two knights. Thus

[050-032]endeth the tale of Balin and of Balan, two brethren born in Northumberland,

[050-033]good knights.
