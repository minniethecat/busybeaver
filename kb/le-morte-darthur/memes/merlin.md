# Merlin

**Merlin** is the enchanter, prophet, and counselor whose [[marvel|supernatural]] agency shapes the destiny of [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] and his [[realm|kingdom]] in Thomas Malory's *Le Morte d'[[king-arthur|Arthur]]*. He is the architect of [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s conception, the guide of his early reign, and the prophet whose foreknowledge establishes the tragic fate that overshadows the ent[[wrath|ire]] cycle.

## Architect of Arthur's Rise

Merlin's role begins before [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s [[lineage|birth]]. When Uther [[dragon|Pendragon]] is stricken with love for Igraine, a knight advises him to "seek Merlin, and he shall do you remedy" (004-038). Ulfius "met Merlin in a beggar's array" (004-039), and the enchanter's disguise — appearing as a beggar — establishes his characteristic mode of operating through ind[[wrath|ire]]ction and transformation. "Merlin asked Ulfius whom he sought" (004-040), and when told, responds with knowing recognition: "I know whom thou seekest" (004-041). Merlin's knowledge is never in question; his power lies in what he chooses to reveal and when.

## Prophet of Tragedy

Merlin's prophecies establish the fatal horizon of the [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian narrative. He foretells the battle of Salisbury where [[king-arthur|Arthur]] and [[mordred|Mordred]] will destroy each other, and his prophecies are woven into the chapter headings themselves — as in the tale "of the prophecy of Merlin" (042-001). Caxton's preface notes the book in which "Merlin was assotted" (002-108), referencing the episode where Merlin is himself overcome — the enchanter enchanted, the prophet caught in his own foresight. Merlin's disappearance from the narrative (entrapped by [[lady-of-the-lake|Nimue]]) marks the moment when [[king-arthur|Arthur]] must navigate fate without [[marvel|supernatural]] guidance.

## Source and Legacy

The scholarly apparatus of the Morte traces Malory's work "by chapter to the 'Merlin' of Robert de Borron and his successors" (001-054), acknowledging Merlin as not only a character within the story but a foundational figure of the [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian [[custom|tradition]] itself. Merlin in Malory is simultaneously a character, a narrative device, and a literary [[blood|lineage]].
