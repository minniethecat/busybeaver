# Sir Nabon

Sir Nabon, called Nabon le Noire, was a great mighty giant and the lord of the Isle of Servage. He harboured a bitter hatred toward all the knights of King Arthur's court and maintained dominion over all who passed through his isle. Sir Tristram de Liones confronted and slew him, ending his tyranny and liberating the isle's inhabitants.

## Lord of the Isle of Servage

The Isle of Servage lay off the coast of Wales, and its lord was Sir Nabon le Noire, a great mighty giant (200-023). Sir Nabon hated all the knights of King Arthur, and there came never knight to that isle but he destroyed them (200-024). The isle was a woeful place for errant knights, and when Sir Tristram and his companions arrived there, they were warned that Nabon must know of their presence before they could depart (200-032).

Sir Lamorak had also been trapped upon that isle, unable to leave because Nabon would not suffer any knight of Arthur's court to pass freely (201-053).

## The Cry and the Tournament

When word came that Sir Nabon had made a cry that all the people of that country should bring their best knights to a great tournament — for on that day the son of Nabon should be made knight, and all the knights of that isle must be there to joust (202-002, 202-004) — Sir Tristram saw his opportunity.

Sir Nabon lent Sir Lamorak horse and armour, and Sir Lamorak did such deeds of arms that Nabon and all the people said there was never knight that ever they saw do such deeds (202-009, 202-010).

## The Combat with Sir Tristram

After the tournament, Sir Nabon proffered to play with Sir Tristram at a game of jousting (202-014). Tristram, knowing well that Sir Nabon would not abide a stroke with him if he knew his true identity, disguised himself (202-031).

They jousted mightily — Tristram would not rest, and at the last Sir Nabon prayed him to tell him his name (202-034). When Tristram revealed himself as the nephew of King Mark, Nabon welcomed him warmly, for of all knights he most desired to meet one of Arthur's kin (202-036). Yet Tristram pressed the combat and slew Sir Nabon, and so ended the giant's reign (202-038).

## The Liberation of Servage

After Nabon's death, Tristram advanced Sir Lamorak to the lordship of the Isle of Servage, and Sir Lamorak took governance of the land (202-051, 202-065). The son of Nabon was also dealt with, and the isle was freed from the tyranny that had long oppressed it.
