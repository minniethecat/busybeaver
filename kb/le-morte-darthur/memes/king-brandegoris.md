# King Brandegoris

King Brandegoris of Stranggore was one of the eleven kings who rose in rebellion against the young King Arthur during the early wars of his reign. He was a potent lord who brought a great host to the rebel cause and fought in the major battles of the rebellion.

## The Oath of the Eleven Kings

When the kings of the realm gathered to resist Arthur's claim to the throne, they made a solemn oath to destroy him. The Duke of Cambenet swore first, promising five thousand men of arms ready on horseback. Then swore King Brandegoris of Stranggore that he would bring five thousand men of arms on horseback, making him one of the principal contributors to the rebel host (015-011). Other kings followed with their own levies, and together they formed a mighty alliance against the new-crowned Arthur.

## The Battle against Arthur

When the eleven kings joined battle with Arthur's forces, King Brandegoris fought in the thick of the combat. In the great medley, he was one of three kings — alongside King Idres and King Agwisance — who together struck down Griflet, horse and man, and Lucas the butler, horse and man (017-033). The medley waxed passing hard on both parties as the battle raged.

Throughout the fighting, King Brandegoris remained in the company of the principal rebel kings. He was among the six kings — King Lot, King Nentres, King Idres, King Uriens, and King Agwisance — who held strong medley against the knights Lucas, Gwinas, Briant, and Bellias of Flanders (018-012). So fiercely did Arthur's knights press them that the six kings unnethe had any power to defend themselves.

## The Renewed Oath

After the initial campaigns, when King Lot asked the assembled kings to renew their resolve, King Brandegoris was among those who swore to continue the war. Alongside King Nentres, the King of the Hundred Knights, King Carados, King Uriens, King Idres, King Cradelment, the Duke of Cambenet, King Clariance, and King Agwisance, Brandegoris swore they would never fail one another, neither for life nor for death (019-071).
