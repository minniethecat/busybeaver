# Feast

The **feast** in [[Le Morte d'Arthur]] is the primary social institution of [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian [[king|kingship]] — the gathering at which oaths are sworn, [[adventure|adventures]] are proposed, and the fellowship of the [[Round Table]] is ritually renewed. Malory uses the feast as a narrative frame: the great majority of books and major episodes open or pivot upon a feast day, most often the Feast of [[Pentecost]].

## The Feast of Pentecost

[[feast-of-pentecost|Pentecost]] is the feast *par excellence* in Malory's scheme. It is the day on which [[King Arthur]] holds his most solemn court, and the narrative returns to it at the beginning of each major book:

> And at the feast of Pentecost all manner of men of worship came to that court (010-002)

At [[feast-of-pentecost|Pentecost]], [[knights]] from every realm gather to renew their [[fealty|allegiance]], and it is on this day that new adventures most often present themselves. The timing is not accidental: [[feast-of-pentecost|Pentecost]], celebrating the descent of the Holy Spirit, is the liturgical moment of collective inspiration — fitting for a [[kingship]] founded on [[chivalry]].

## Feasts as Political Assemblies

A great feast is a political act. When [[King Arthur]] "let cry a great feast" (011-002), he summons not only his own [[baron|lord]]s but the [[kinsman|kin]]gs of the surrounding realms:

> there came to the feast King Lot of Lothian and his company (011-004)

> Also there came to the feast King Uriens of Gore with four hundred knights (011-005)

> Also there came to that feast King Nentres of Garlot, with seven hundred knights (011-006)

The feast is the mechanism by which [[king-arthur|Arthur]] asserts his [[lordship|overlordship]]. Every [[kinsman|kin]]g who attends acknowledges, however grudgingly, [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s claim to be high [[kinsman|kin]]g. The [[kinsman|kin]]g who refuses attendance is declaring rebellion; the [[kinsman|kin]]g who attends but brings armed men is negotiating his terms. Malory's feasts are never merely social — they are the visible form of the political order.

## The Feast as Threshold of Adventure

Many of the work's most memorable adventures begin at a feast. The unknown [[maiden|damsel]] arrives during the meal; the challenger enters the hall; the wounded [[knight]] is carried in on a litter. The feast hall is the threshold between the ordered world of the court and the wild world of adventure:

> So after the feast and journey, King Arthur drew unto London (013-002)

The rhythm is consistent: feast, then journey, then adventure, then return. The feast marks the passage from peace to action, and from action back to peace:

> King Arthur let make a feast that lasted three days (024-029)

The three-day feast after a victory is the ritual that transforms military success into political settlement.

## Feasts as Sites of Oath and Vow

The feast is the privileged occasion for oath-ta[[kinsman|kin]]g. Knights swear their [[feast-of-pentecost|Pentecost]]al oath at the feast; [[lady|ladies]] present petitions; quarrels are either settled or formally declared. The public nature of the feast — all eyes upon the speaker — gives words spoken there a binding force that private conversation cannot match:

> what is the cause, said King Arthur, that we should trouble this high feast (055-006)

To disrupt a feast is a grave discourtesy precisely because the feast is a ritual space. [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s question recognises that the disturbance must have a serious cause to justify its intrusion upon the feast's order.

## The Feast's Narrative Function

The feast in Malory is a narrative machine. It gathers all the characters in one place so that adventures may be proposed, challenges issued, and the plot advanced. It is the mechanism by which the centrifugal forces of [[knight-errantry]] are periodically drawn back to the centre. Without the feast, there is no court; without the court, there is no [[round-table|Round Table]]; without the [[round-table|Round Table]], there is no fellowship — and without fellowship, the whole [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian world collapses.
