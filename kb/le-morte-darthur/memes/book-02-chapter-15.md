[046-002]stroke.

[046-003]Anon all the knights arose from the table for to set on Balin, and King Pellam

[046-004]himself arose up fiercely, and said, Knight, hast thou slain my brother? thou

[046-005]shalt die therefore or thou depart. Well, said Balin, do it yourself. Yes, said

[046-006]King Pellam, there shall no man have ado with thee but myself, for the love of

[046-007]my brother. Then King Pellam caught in his hand a grim weapon and smote eagerly

[046-008]at Balin; but Balin put the sword betwixt his head and the stroke, and

[046-009]therewith his sword burst in sunder. And when Balin was weaponless he ran into

[046-010]a chamber for to seek some weapon, and so from chamber to chamber, and no

[046-011]weapon he could find, and always King Pellam after him. And at the last he

[046-012]entered into a chamber that was marvellously well dight and richly, and a bed

[046-013]arrayed with cloth of gold, the richest that might be thought, and one lying

[046-014]therein, and thereby stood a table of clean gold with four pillars of silver

[046-015]that bare up the table, and upon the table stood a [[marvel|marvellous]] spear strangely

[046-016]wrought. And when Balin saw that spear, he gat it in his hand and turned him to

[046-017]King Pellam, and smote him passingly sore with that spear, that King Pellam

[046-018]fell down in a swoon, and therewith the castle roof and walls brake and fell to

[046-019]the earth, and Balin fell down so that he might not stir foot nor hand. And so

[046-020]the most part of the castle, that was fallen down through that dolorous stroke,

[046-021]lay upon Pellam and Balin three days.
