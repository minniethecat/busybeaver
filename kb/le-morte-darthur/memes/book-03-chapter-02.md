[053-001]were ordained and their sieges blessed by the Bishop of Canterbury.

[053-002]When [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] heard of the coming of [[queen-guenever|Guenever]] and the hundred knights with

[053-003]the [[round-table|Table Round]], then King Arthur made great joy for her coming, and that rich

[053-004]present, and said openly, This fair lady is passing welcome unto me, for I have

[053-005]loved her long, and therefore there is nothing so lief to me. And these knights

[053-006]with the Round Table please me more than right great riches. And in all haste

[053-007]the king let ordain for the marriage and the coronation in the most honourable

[053-008]wise that could be devised. Now, [[merlin|Merlin]], said King Arthur, go thou and espy me

[053-009]in all this land fifty knights which be of most [[courage|prowess]] and [[honour|worship]]. Within

[053-010]short time Merlin had found such knights that should fulfil twenty and eight

[053-011]knights, but no more he could find. Then the Bishop of Canterbury was fetched,

[053-012]and he blessed the sieges with great royalty and devotion, and there set the

[053-013]eight and twenty knights in their sieges. And when this was done Merlin said,

[053-014]Fair sirs, ye must all arise and come to King Arthur for to do him homage; he

[053-015]will have the better will to maintain you. And so they arose and did their

[053-016]homage, and when they were gone Merlin found in every sieges letters of gold

[053-017]that told the knights’ names that had sitten therein. But two sieges were

[053-018]void. And so anon came young [[sir-gawaine|Gawaine]] and asked the king a gift. Ask, said the

[053-019]king, and I shall grant it you. Sir, I ask that ye will make me knight that

[053-020]same day ye shall wed fair Guenever. I will do it with a good will, said King

[053-021]Arthur, and do unto you all the worship that I may, for I must by reason ye are

[053-022]my nephew, my sister’s son.
