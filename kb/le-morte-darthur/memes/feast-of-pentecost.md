# Feast of Pentecost

The **Feast of Pentecost** is the liturgical and narrative fulcrum of the [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian year in Malory's *Le Morte d'[[king-arthur|Arthur]]*. It is the day on which the knights of the [[Round Table]] assemble at [[Camelot]] to renew their oaths, on which quests are proclaimed and adventurers return to tell their tales, and on which the great spiritual events of the cycle — most decisively, the coming of the [[Sangreal]] — are ordained to occur. "Ever the [[kinsman|kin]]g had a custom that at the feast of Pentecost" (128-005) the court would gather, and this custom makes Pentecost the heartbeat of the chivalric calendar: a day that is simultaneously celebration, [[judgment|judgement]], and renewal.

## The Custom of the Court

[[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s custom of holding the high feast at Pentecost is established early in his reign and maintained throughout. At the feast, "all manner of men assayed to pull at the sword" (010-002) that would prove [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s right to the crown, and after his coronation the Pentecostal feast becomes the occasion for the great gathering of [[kinsman|kin]]gs and [[baron|lord]]s:

> At the feast of Pentecost, a great feast, and what kings and lords came to his feast. (011-001)

The custom is reiterated throughout the narrative. "Every year were they sworn at the high feast of Pentecost" (065-036) — the annual oath-ta[[kinsman|kin]]g binds the knights of the [[round-table|Round Table]] to their [[oath|vow]]s of chivalry, courtesy, and service. Pentecost is thus the day on which the chivalric order is ritually reconstituted, the fellowship reaffirmed against the erosions of time, distance, and human frailty.

## The Coming of the Sangreal

The most decisive Pentecost in Malory's narrative is the one on which the [[sangreal|Sangreal]] appears. The quest is not merely announced but prophesied to occur at this specific feast:

> Against the feast of Pentecost came the Damosel of the Lake and brought with her the four knights that achieved the Sangreal. (094-015)

The [[Damosel|Damosel of the Lake]] arrives at Pentecost bearing the tokens that identify the Grail knights, and the feast becomes the threshold between the era of earthly chivalry — the tournaments and love-affairs that occupy the earlier books — and the era of spiritual quest that will transform the [[round-table|Round Table]] forever. The timing is not incidental: Pentecost, the feast of the Holy Spirit's descent upon the apostles, is the day on which divine power enters the human world. The [[sangreal|Sangreal]]'s arrival at Pentecost repeats this pattern, ma[[kinsman|kin]]g the [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian feast a typological echo of the original Christian Pentecost.

## A Day of Beginnings and Endings

Pentecost in Malory is a day of thresholds. [[quest|Quest]]s begin at Pentecost; knights who have been absent for a year return to tell their tales. The feast that "draweth nigh" (123-056) structures the narrative calendar, providing the temporal frame within which adventures are undertaken and measured. A knight who [[oath|vow]]s to return by Pentecost has given his word on the most binding of days; a challenge issued at Pentecost carries the weight of the ent[[wrath|ire]] assembled chivalry.

Yet Pentecost is also, in the later books, a day of foreboding. As the [[round-table|Round Table]] fractures, the great feast that once affirmed the fellowship becomes the occasion on which its di[[dream|vision]]s are most starkly visible. The empty seats, the absent knights, the oaths that can no longer be sworn — Pentecost, which once gathered the chivalry, comes to measure its diminishment.
