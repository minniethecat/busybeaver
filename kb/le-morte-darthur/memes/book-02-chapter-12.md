[043-001][[king-arthur|Arthur]], and how Balin fetched him, and how that knight was slain by a knight

[043-002]invisible.

[043-003]Within a day or two King Arthur was somewhat sick, and he let pitch his

[043-004]pavilion in a meadow, and there he laid him down on a pallet to sleep, but he

[043-005]might have no rest. Right so he heard a great noise of an horse, and therewith

[043-006]the king looked out at the porch of the pavilion, and saw a knight coming even

[043-007]by him, making great dole. Abide, fair sir, said Arthur, and tell me wherefore

[043-008]thou makest this sorrow. Ye may little amend me, said the knight, and so passed

[043-009]forth to the castle of Meliot. Anon after there came Balin, and when he saw

[043-010]King Arthur he alighted off his horse, and came to the King on foot, and

[043-011]saluted him. By my head, said Arthur, ye be welcome. Sir, right now came riding

[043-012]this way a knight making great mourn, for what cause I cannot tell; wherefore I

[043-013]would desire of you of your courtesy and of your gentleness to fetch again that

[043-014]knight either by force or else by his good will. I will do more for your

[043-015]lordship than that, said Balin; and so he rode more than a pace, and found the

[043-016]knight with a damosel in a forest, and said, Sir knight, ye must come with me

[043-017]unto King Arthur, for to tell him of your sorrow. That will I not, said the

[043-018]knight, for it will scathe me greatly, and do you none avail. Sir, said Balin,

[043-019]I pray you make you ready, for ye must go with me, or else I must [[battle|fight]] with

[043-020]you and bring you by force, and that were me loath to do. Will ye be my

[043-021]warrant, said the knight, an I go with you? Yea, said Balin, or else I will die

[043-022]therefore. And so he made him ready to go with Balin, and left the damosel

[043-023]still. And as they were even afore King Arthur’s pavilion, there came one

[043-024]invisible, and smote this knight that went with Balin throughout the body with

[043-025]a spear. Alas, said the knight, I am slain under your conduct with a knight

[043-026]called Garlon; therefore take my horse that is better than yours, and ride to

[043-027]the damosel, and follow the quest that I was in as she will lead you, and

[043-028]revenge my death when ye may. That shall I do, said Balin, and that I make [[oath|vow]]

[043-029]unto knighthood; and so he departed from this knight with great sorrow. So King

[043-030]Arthur let bury this knight richly, and made a mention on his tomb, how there

[043-031]was slain Herlews le Berbeus, and by whom the [[treason|treachery]] was done, the knight

[043-032]Garlon. But ever the damosel bare the truncheon of the spear with her that Sir

[043-033]Herlews was slain withal.
