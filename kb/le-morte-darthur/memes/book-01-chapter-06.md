[009-001]divers times.

[009-002]Now assay, said Sir Ector unto [[sir-kay|Sir Kay]]. And anon he pulled at the sword with

[009-003]all his might; but it would not be. Now shall ye assay, said Sir Ector to

[009-004][[king-arthur|Arthur]]. I will well, said Arthur, and pulled it out easily. And therewithal Sir

[009-005]Ector knelt down to the earth, and Sir Kay. Alas, said Arthur, my own dear

[009-006]father and brother, why kneel ye to me? Nay, nay, my lord Arthur, it is not so;

[009-007]I was never your father nor of your blood, but I wot well ye are of an higher

[009-008]blood than I weened ye were. And then Sir Ector told him all, how he was

[009-009]betaken him for to nourish him, and by whose commandment, and by [[merlin|Merlin]]’s

[009-010]deliverance.

[009-011]Then Arthur made great dole when he understood that Sir Ector was not his

[009-012]father. Sir, said Ector unto Arthur, will ye be my good and gracious lord when

[009-013]ye are king? Else were I to blame, said Arthur, for ye are the man in the world

[009-014]that I am most beholden to, and my good lady and mother your wife, that as well

[009-015]as her own hath fostered me and kept. And if ever it be God’s will that I

[009-016]be king as ye say, ye shall desire of me what I may do, and I shall not fail

[009-017]you; God forbid I should fail you Sir, said Sir Ector, I will ask no more of

[009-018]you, but that ye will make my son, your foster brother, Sir Kay, seneschal of

[009-019]all your lands. That shall be done, said Arthur, and more, by the faith of my

[009-020]body, that never man shall have that office but he, while he and I live.

[009-021]Therewithal they went unto the Archbishop, and told him how the sword was

[009-022]achieved, and by whom; and on Twelfth-day all the barons came thither, and to

[009-023]assay to take the sword, who that would assay. But there afore them all, there

[009-024]might none take it out but Arthur; wherefore there were many lords wroth, and

[009-025]said it was great shame unto them all and the realm, to be overgoverned with a

[009-026]boy of no high blood born. And so they fell out at that time that it was put

[009-027]off till Candlemas and then all the barons should meet there again; but always

[009-028]the ten knights were ordained to watch the sword day and night, and so they set

[009-029]a pavilion over the stone and the sword, and five always watched. So at

[009-030]Candlemas many more great lords came thither for to have won the sword, but

[009-031]there might none prevail. And right as Arthur did at Christmas, he did at

[009-032]Candlemas, and pulled out the sword easily, whereof the barons were sore

[009-033]aggrieved and put it off in delay till the high feast of Easter. And as Arthur

[009-034]sped before, so did he at Easter; yet there were some of the great lords had

[009-035]indignation that Arthur should be king, and put it off in a delay till the

[009-036]feast of Pentecost.

[009-037]Then the Archbishop of Canterbury by Merlin’s providence let purvey then

[009-038]of the best knights that they might get, and such knights as Uther Pendragon

[009-039]loved best and most trusted in his days. And such knights were put about Arthur

[009-040]as Sir Baudwin of Britain, Sir Kay, Sir Ulfius, Sir Brastias. All these, with

[009-041]many other, were always about Arthur, day and night, till the feast of

[009-042]Pentecost.
