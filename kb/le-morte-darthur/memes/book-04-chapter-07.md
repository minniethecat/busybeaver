[073-003]What are ye that so complain? said [[king-arthur|King Arthur]]. We be here twenty knights,

[073-004]prisoners, said they, and some of us have lain here seven year, and some more

[073-005]and some less. For what cause? said Arthur. We shall tell you, said the

[073-006]knights; this lord of this castle, his name is Sir Damas, and he is the falsest

[073-007]knight that liveth, and full of treason, and a very coward as any liveth, and

[073-008]he hath a younger brother, a good knight of [[courage|prowess]], his name is Sir Ontzlake;

[073-009]and this traitor Damas, the elder brother will give him no part of his

[073-010]livelihood, but as Sir Ontzlake keepeth thorough prowess of his hands, and so

[073-011]he keepeth from him a full fair manor and a rich, and therein Sir Ontzlake

[073-012]dwelleth worshipfully, and is well beloved of all people. And this Sir Damas,

[073-013]our master is as evil beloved, for he is without mercy, and he is a coward, and

[073-014]great [[battle|war]] hath been betwixt them both, but Ontzlake hath ever the better, and

[073-015]ever he proffereth Sir Damas to fight for the livelihood, body for body, but he

[073-016]will not do; other-else to find a knight to fight for him. Unto that Sir Damas

[073-017]had granted to find a knight, but he is so evil beloved and hated, that there

[073-018]is never a knight will fight for him. And when Damas saw this, that there was

[073-019]never a knight would fight for him, he hath daily lain await with many knights

[073-020]with him, and taken all the knights in this country to see and espy their

[073-021]adventures, he hath taken them by force and brought them to his prison. And so

[073-022]he took us separately as we rode on our adventures, and many good knights have

[073-023]died in this prison for hunger, to the number of eighteen knights; and if any

[073-024]of us all that here is, or hath been, would have foughten with his brother

[073-025]Ontzlake, he would have delivered us, but for because this Damas is so false

[073-026]and so full of treason we would never fight for him to die for it. And we be so

[073-027]lean for hunger that unnethe we may stand on our feet. God deliver you, for his

[073-028]mercy, said Arthur.

[073-029]Anon, therewithal there came a damosel unto Arthur, and asked him, What cheer?

[073-030]I cannot say, said he. Sir, said she, an ye will fight for my lord, ye shall be

[073-031]delivered out of prison, and else ye escape never the life. Now, said Arthur,

[073-032]that is hard, yet had I liefer to fight with a knight than to die in prison;

[073-033]with this, said Arthur, I may be delivered and all these prisoners, I will do

[073-034]the battle. Yes, said the damosel. I am ready, said Arthur, an I had horse and

[073-035]armour. Ye shall lack none, said the damosel. Meseemeth, damosel, I should have

[073-036]seen you in the court of Arthur. Nay said the damosel, I came never there, I am

[073-037]the lord’s daughter of this castle. Yet was she false, for she was one of

[073-038]the damosels of [[morgan-le-fay|Morgan le Fay]].

[073-039]Anon she went unto Sir Damas, and told him how he would do battle for him, and

[073-040]so he sent for Arthur. And when he came he was well coloured, and well made of

[073-041]his limbs, that all knights that saw him said it were [[mercy|pity]] that such a knight

[073-042]should die in prison. So Sir Damas and he were agreed that he should fight for

[073-043]him upon this covenant, that all other knights should be delivered; and unto

[073-044]that was Sir Damas sworn unto Arthur, and also to do the battle to the

[073-045]uttermost. And with that all the twenty knights were brought out of the dark

[073-046]prison into the hall, and delivered, and so they all abode to see the battle.
