[039-001]best knights of the world should [[battle|fight]] there, which were Sir [[sir-launcelot|Lancelot]] and Sir

[039-002][[sir-tristram|Tristram]].

[039-003]The meanwhile as this was a-doing, in came [[merlin|Merlin]] to [[king-mark|King Mark]], and seeing all

[039-004]his doing, said, Here shall be in this same place the greatest battle betwixt

[039-005]two knights that was or ever shall be, and the truest lovers, and yet none of

[039-006]them shall slay other. And there Merlin wrote their names upon the tomb with

[039-007]letters of gold that should fight in that place, whose names were Launcelot de

[039-008]Lake, and Tristram. Thou art a [[marvel|marvellous]] man, said King Mark unto Merlin, that

[039-009]speakest of such marvels, thou art a boistous man and an unlikely to tell of

[039-010]such deeds. What is thy name? said King Mark. At this time, said Merlin, I will

[039-011]not tell, but at that time when Sir Tristram is taken with his sovereign lady,

[039-012]then ye shall hear and know my name, and at that time ye shall hear tidings

[039-013]that shall not please you. Then said Merlin to Balin, Thou hast done thyself

[039-014]great hurt, because that thou savest not this lady that slew herself, that

[039-015]might have saved her an thou wouldest. By the faith of my body, said Balin, I

[039-016]might not save her, for she slew herself suddenly. Me repenteth, said Merlin;

[039-017]because of the death of that lady thou shalt strike a stroke most dolorous that

[039-018]ever man struck, except the stroke of our Lord, for thou shalt hurt the truest

[039-019]knight and the man of most [[honour|worship]] that now liveth, and through that stroke

[039-020]three kingdoms shall be in great poverty, misery and wretchedness twelve years,

[039-021]and the knight shall not be whole of that wound for many years. Then Merlin

[039-022]took his leave of Balin. And Balin said, If I wist it were sooth that ye say I

[039-023]should do such a perilous deed as that, I would slay myself to make thee a

[039-024]liar. Therewith Merlin vanished away suddenly. And then Balan and his brother

[039-025]took their leave of King Mark. First, said the king, tell me your name. Sir,

[039-026]said Balan, ye may see he beareth two swords, thereby ye may call him the

[039-027]Knight with the Two Swords. And so departed King Mark unto [[camelot|Camelot]] to King

[039-028][[king-arthur|Arthur]], and Balin took the way toward King Rience; and as they rode together

[039-029]they met with Merlin disguised, but they knew him not. Whither ride you? said

[039-030]Merlin. We have little to do, said the two knights, to tell thee. But what is

[039-031]thy name? said Balin. At this time, said Merlin, I will not tell it thee. It is

[039-032]evil seen, said the knights, that thou art a true man that thou wilt not tell

[039-033]thy name. As for that, said Merlin, be it as it be may, I can tell you

[039-034]wherefore ye ride this way, for to meet King Rience; but it will not avail you

[039-035]without ye have my counsel. Ah! said Balin, ye are Merlin; we will be ruled by

[039-036]your counsel. Come on, said Merlin, ye shall have great worship, and look that

[039-037]ye do knightly, for ye shall have great need. As for that, said Balin, dread

[039-038]you not, we will do what we may.
