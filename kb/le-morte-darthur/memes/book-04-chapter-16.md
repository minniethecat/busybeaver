[082-001][[king-arthur|King Arthur]] from mantle that should have burnt him.

[082-002]With that came the Damosel of the Lake unto the king, and said, Sir, I must

[082-003]speak with you in privity. Say on, said the king, what ye will. Sir, said the

[082-004]damosel, put not on you this mantle till ye have seen more, and in no wise let

[082-005]it not come on you, nor on no knight of yours, till ye command the bringer

[082-006]thereof to put it upon her. Well, said King Arthur, it shall be done as ye

[082-007]counsel me. And then he said unto the damosel that came from his sister,

[082-008]Damosel, this mantle that ye have brought me, I will see it upon you. Sir, she

[082-009]said, It will not beseem me to wear a king’s garment. By my head, said

[082-010]Arthur, ye shall wear it or it come on my back, or any man’s that here

[082-011]is. And so the king made it to be put upon her, and forth withal she fell down

[082-012]dead, and never more spake word after and burnt to coals. Then was the king

[082-013]wonderly wroth, more than he was to-forehand, and said unto King Uriens, My

[082-014]sister, your wife, is alway about to betray me, and well I wot either ye, or my

[082-015]nephew, your son, is of counsel with her to have me destroyed; but as for you,

[082-016]said the king to King Uriens, I deem not greatly that ye be of her counsel, for

[082-017]Accolon confessed to me by his own mouth, that she would have destroyed you as

[082-018]well as me, therefore I hold you excused; but as for your son, Sir Uwaine, I

[082-019]hold him suspect, therefore I charge you put him out of my court. So Sir Uwaine

[082-020]was discharged. And when [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]] wist that, he made him ready to go with

[082-021]him; and said, Whoso banisheth my cousin-germain shall banish me. So they two

[082-022]departed, and rode into a great forest, and so they came to an abbey of monks,

[082-023]and there were well lodged. But when the king wist that Sir Gawaine was

[082-024]departed from the court, there was made great sorrow among all the estates.

[082-025]Now, said Gaheris, Gawaine’s brother, we have lost two good knights for

[082-026]the love of one. So on the morn they heard their masses in the abbey, and so

[082-027]they rode forth till that they came to a great forest. Then was Sir Gawaine

[082-028]ware in a valley by a turret [of] twelve fair damosels, and two knights armed

[082-029]on great horses, and the damosels went to and fro by a tree. And then was Sir

[082-030]Gawaine ware how there hung a white shield on that tree, and ever as the

[082-031]damosels came by it they spit upon it, and some threw mire upon the shield.
