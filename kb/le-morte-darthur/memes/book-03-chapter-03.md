[054-001]mare desired [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] to make his son knight.

[054-002]Forthwithal there came a poor man into the court, and brought with him a fair

[054-003]young man of eighteen years of age riding upon a lean mare; and the poor man

[054-004]asked all men that he met, Where shall I find King Arthur? Yonder he is, said

[054-005]the knights, wilt thou anything with him? Yea, said the poor man, therefore I

[054-006]came hither. Anon as he came before the king, he saluted him and said: O King

[054-007]Arthur, the flower of all knights and kings, I beseech Jesu save thee. Sir, it

[054-008]was told me that at this time of your marriage ye would give any man the gift

[054-009]that he would ask, out except that were unreasonable. That is truth, said the

[054-010]king, such cries I let make, and that will I hold, so it apair not my realm nor

[054-011]mine estate. Ye say well and graciously, said the poor man; Sir, I ask nothing

[054-012]else but that ye will make my son here a knight. It is a great thing thou

[054-013]askest of me, said the king. What is thy name? said the king to the poor man.

[054-014]Sir, my name is Aries the cowherd. Whether cometh this of thee or of thy son?

[054-015]said the king. Nay, sir, said Aries, this desire cometh of my son and not of

[054-016]me, for I shall tell you I have thirteen sons, and all they will fall to what

[054-017]labour I put them, and will be right glad to do labour, but this child will not

[054-018]labour for me, for anything that my wife or I may do, but always he will be

[054-019]shooting or casting darts, and glad for to see battles and to behold knights,

[054-020]and always day and night he desireth of me to be made a knight. What is thy

[054-021]name? said the king unto the young man. Sir, my name is Tor. The king beheld

[054-022]him fast, and saw he was passingly well-visaged and passingly well made of his

[054-023]years. Well, said King Arthur unto Aries the cowherd, fetch all thy sons afore

[054-024]me that I may see them. And so the poor man did, and all were shaped much like

[054-025]the poor man. But Tor was not like none of them all in shape nor in

[054-026]countenance, for he was much more than any of them. Now, said King Arthur unto

[054-027]the cow herd, where is the sword he shall be made knight withal? It is here,

[054-028]said Tor. Take it out of the sheath, said the king, and require me to make you

[054-029]a knight.

[054-030]Then Tor alighted off his mare and pulled out his sword, kneeling, and

[054-031]requiring the king that he would make him knight, and that he might be a knight

[054-032]of the [[round-table|Table Round]]. As for a knight I will make you, and therewith smote him in

[054-033]the neck with the sword, saying, Be ye a good knight, and so I pray to God so

[054-034]ye may be, and if ye be of [[courage|prowess]] and of worthiness ye shall be a knight of

[054-035]the Table Round. Now [[merlin|Merlin]], said Arthur, say whether this Tor shall be a good

[054-036]knight or no. Yea, sir, he ought to be a good knight, for he is come of as good

[054-037]a man as any is alive, and of kings’ blood. How so, sir? said the king. I

[054-038]shall tell you, said Merlin: This poor man, Aries the cowherd, is not his

[054-039]father; he is nothing sib to him, for King Pellinore is his father. I suppose

[054-040]nay, said the cowherd. Fetch thy wife afore me, said Merlin, and she shall not

[054-041]say nay. Anon the wife was fetched, which was a fair housewife, and there she

[054-042]answered Merlin full womanly, and there she told the king and Merlin that when

[054-043]she was a maid, and went to milk kine, there met with her a stern knight, and

[054-044]half by force he had my maidenhead, and at that time he begat my son Tor, and

[054-045]he took away from me my greyhound that I had that time with me, and said that

[054-046]he would keep the greyhound for my love. Ah, said the cowherd, I weened not

[054-047]this, but I may believe it well, for he had never no tatches of me. Sir, said

[054-048]Tor unto Merlin, dishonour not my mother. Sir, said Merlin, it is more for your

[054-049][[honour|worship]] than hurt, for your father is a good man and a king, and he may right

[054-050]well advance you and your mother, for ye were begotten or ever she was wedded.

[054-051]That is truth, said the wife. It is the less grief unto me, said the cowherd.
