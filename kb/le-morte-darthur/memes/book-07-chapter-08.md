[135-001]was slain met with [[sir-gareth|Beaumains]], and fought with Beaumains till he was

[135-002]yielden.

[135-003]Thus as they rode together, they saw a knight come driving by them all in

[135-004]green, both his horse and his harness; and when he came nigh the damosel, he

[135-005]asked her, Is that my brother the Black Knight that ye have brought with you?

[135-006]Nay, nay, she said, this unhappy kitchen knave hath slain your brother through

[135-007]unhappiness. Alas, said the Green Knight, that is great [[mercy|pity]], that so noble a

[135-008]knight as he was should so unhappily be slain, and namely of a knave’s

[135-009]hand, as ye say that he is. Ah! traitor, said the Green Knight, thou shalt die

[135-010]for slaying of my brother; he was a full noble knight, and his name was Sir

[135-011]Perard. I defy thee, said Beaumains, for I let thee wit I slew him knightly and

[135-012]not shamefully.

[135-013]Therewithal the Green Knight rode unto an horn that was green, and it hung upon

[135-014]a thorn, and there he blew three deadly motes, and there came two damosels and

[135-015]armed him lightly. And then he took a great horse, and a green shield and a

[135-016]green spear. And then they ran together with all their mights, and brake their

[135-017]spears unto their hands. And then they drew their swords, and gave many sad

[135-018]strokes, and either of them wounded other full ill. And at the last, at an

[135-019]overthwart, Beaumains with his horse struck the Green Knight’s horse upon

[135-020]the side, that he fell to the earth. And then the Green Knight avoided his

[135-021]horse lightly, and dressed him upon foot. That saw Beaumains, and therewithal

[135-022]he alighted, and they rushed together like two mighty kemps a long while, and

[135-023]sore they bled both. With that came the damosel, and said, My lord the Green

[135-024]Knight, why for shame stand ye so long fighting with the kitchen knave? Alas,

[135-025]it is shame that ever ye were made knight, to see such a lad to match such a

[135-026]knight, as the weed overgrew the corn. Therewith the Green Knight was ashamed,

[135-027]and therewithal he gave a great stroke of might, and clave his shield through.

[135-028]When Beaumains saw his shield cloven asunder he was a little ashamed of that

[135-029]stroke and of her language; and then he gave him such a buffet upon the helm

[135-030]that he fell on his knees. And so suddenly Beaumains pulled him upon the ground

[135-031]grovelling. And then the Green Knight cried him mercy, and yielded him unto Sir

[135-032]Beaumains, and prayed him to slay him not. All is in vain, said Beaumains, for

[135-033]thou shalt die but if this damosel that came with me pray me to save thy life.

[135-034]And therewithal he unlaced his helm like as he would slay him. Fie upon thee,

[135-035]false kitchen page, I will never pray thee to save his life, for I will never

[135-036]be so much in thy danger. Then shall he die, said Beaumains. Not so hardy, thou

[135-037]bawdy knave, said the damosel, that thou slay him. Alas, said the Green Knight,

[135-038]suffer me not to die for a fair word may save me. Fair knight, said the Green

[135-039]Knight, save my life, and I will forgive thee the death of my brother, and for

[135-040]ever to become thy man, and thirty knights that hold of me for ever shall do

[135-041]you service. In the devil’s name, said the damosel, that such a bawdy

[135-042]kitchen knave should have thee and thirty knights’ service.

[135-043]Sir knight, said Beaumains, all this availeth thee not, but if my damosel speak

[135-044]with me for thy life. And therewithal he made a semblant to slay him. Let be,

[135-045]said the damosel, thou bawdy knave; slay him not, for an thou do thou shalt

[135-046]repent it. Damosel, said Beaumains, your charge is to me a pleasure, and at

[135-047]your commandment his life shall be saved, and else not. Then he said, Sir

[135-048]knight with the green arms, I release thee quit at this damosel’s

[135-049]request, for I will not make her wroth, I will fulfil all that she chargeth me.

[135-050]And then the Green Knight kneeled down, and did him homage with his sword. Then

[135-051]said the damosel, Me repenteth, Green Knight, of your damage, and of your

[135-052]brother’s death, the Black Knight, for of your help I had great mister,

[135-053]for I dread me sore to pass this forest. Nay, dread you not, said the Green

[135-054]Knight, for ye shall lodge with me this night, and to-morn I shall help you

[135-055]through this forest. So they took their horses and rode to his manor, which was

[135-056]fast there beside.
