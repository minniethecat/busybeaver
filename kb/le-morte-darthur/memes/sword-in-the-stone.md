# Sword in the Stone
The **Sword in the Stone** is the foundational [[marvel|miracle]] of Thomas Malory's *Le Morte d'[[king-arthur|Arthur]]*. On New Year's Day, a sword appears set in a stone in a churchyard, with letters of gold declaring that whoever draws it is the rightful [[kinsman|kin]]g of all Eng[[realm|land]]. The event establishes [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s divine election and inaugurates the [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian age.

## The Miracle
The Archbishop calls the realm's [[baron|noble]]s to a tournament on New Year's Day, and during the gathering the sword appears. All the great [[baron|lord]]s try and fail to draw it. Young [[king-arthur|Arthur]], attending as squ[[wrath|ire]] to his foster-brother [[sir-kay|Sir Kay]], draws the sword casually when sent to fetch [[sir-kay|Kay]]'s weapon — not knowing its significance.

## Controversy and Confirmation
[[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s drawing of the sword is met with dis[[faith|belief]]. The [[baron|noble]]s demand repeated proofs, and the sword is set again in the stone at Candlemas, Easter, and [[feast-of-pentecost|Pentecost]]. Each time only [[king-arthur|Arthur]] can draw it. The repetition emphasizes that the [[marvel|miracle]] is not accident but divine will.

## From Miracle to Kingship
The sword [[test|trial]] transforms [[king-arthur|Arthur]] from obscure youth to acknowledged [[kinsman|kin]]g. The commons cry "We will have [[king-arthur|Arthur]] unto our [[kinsman|kin]]g," and the coronation follows. The Sword in the Stone is the hinge between the chaotic pre-[[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian world and the ordered chivalric [[realm|kingdom]] [[king-arthur|Arthur]] will build.
