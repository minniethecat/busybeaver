[060-002]Therewith Sir Tor alighted and took the dwarf his glaive, and so he came to the

[060-003]white pavilion, and saw three damosels lie in it, on one pallet, sleeping, and

[060-004]so he went to the other pavilion, and found a lady lying sleeping therein, but

[060-005]there was the white brachet that bayed at her fast, and therewith the lady yede

[060-006]out of the pavilion and all her damosels. But anon as Sir Tor espied the white

[060-007]brachet, he took her by force and took her to the dwarf. What, will ye so, said

[060-008]the lady, take my brachet from me? Yea, said Sir Tor, this brachet have I

[060-009]sought from [[king-arthur|King Arthur]]’s court hither. Well, said the lady, knight, ye

[060-010]shall not go far with her, but that ye shall be met and grieved. I shall abide

[060-011]what adventure that cometh by the grace of God, and so mounted upon his horse,

[060-012]and passed on his way toward [[camelot|Camelot]]; but it was so near night he might not

[060-013]pass but little further. Know ye any lodging? said Tor. I know none, said the

[060-014]dwarf, but here beside is an hermitage, and there ye must take lodging as ye

[060-015]find. And within a while they came to the hermitage and took lodging; and was

[060-016]there grass, oats and bread for their horses; soon it was sped, and full hard

[060-017]was their supper; but there they rested them all night till on the morn, and

[060-018]heard a mass devoutly, and took their leave of the hermit, and Sir Tor prayed

[060-019]the hermit to pray for him. He said he would, and betook him to God. And so

[060-020]mounted upon horseback and rode towards Camelot a long while.

[060-021]With that they heard a knight call loud that came after them, and he said,

[060-022]Knight, abide and yield my brachet that thou took from my lady. Sir Tor

[060-023]returned again, and beheld him how he was a seemly knight and well horsed, and

[060-024]well armed at all points; then Sir Tor dressed his shield, and took his spear

[060-025]in his hands, and the other came fiercely upon him, and smote both horse and

[060-026]man to the earth. Anon they arose lightly and drew their swords as eagerly as

[060-027]lions, and put their shields afore them, and smote through the shields, that

[060-028]the cantels fell off both parties. Also they tamed their helms that the hot

[060-029]blood ran out, and the thick mails of their hauberks they carved and rove in

[060-030]sunder that the hot blood ran to the earth, and both they had many wounds and

[060-031]were passing weary. But Sir Tor espied that the other knight fainted, and then

[060-032]he sued fast upon him, and doubled his strokes, and gart him go to the earth on

[060-033]the one side. Then Sir Tor bade him yield him. That will I not, said Abelleus,

[060-034]while my life lasteth and the soul is within my body, unless that thou wilt

[060-035]give me the brachet. That will I not do, said Sir Tor, for it was my quest to

[060-036]bring again thy brachet, thee, or both.
