# Sir Driant

Sir Driant was a knight of King Arthur's Round Table. He fought at tournaments alongside Sir Lamorak de Galis and participated in the rescue of Sir Launcelot from the treason of Morgan le Fay.

## Tournament Knight

Sir Driant appears among the company of knights who frequented the tournaments of the Arthurian world. At a castle beside a river where thirty knights stood ready to joust against all comers, Sir Driant arrived by fortune alongside Sir Lamorak de Galis (196-006). There Sir Driant jousted right well, but at the last he had a fall. Sir Lamorak then proffered to joust, and fared so mightily that not one of the thirty knights escaped without a fall.

## Companion of Sir Lamorak

Sir Driant accompanied Sir Lamorak on at least two significant journeys. On one occasion, Lamorak departed from a chapel with Sir Driant, and by the way they met with a knight sent from Morgan le Fay unto King Arthur (197-003). The knight bore a magical horn that would reveal any lady's unfaithfulness. By force Sir Lamorak made the knight tell the cause of the horn, and then commanded him to bear it instead to King Mark of Cornwall.

## Defender of Sir Launcelot

When Morgan le Fay plotted to have Sir Launcelot slain by thirty knights, a damosel rode forth seeking noble knights of Arthur's court to rescue him. She met with Sir Bors de Ganis, Sir Ector de Maris, and Sir Driant, and told them of the treason (225-021). The three knights promised to be near the place where Launcelot should meet the thirty knights, vowing to do rescue if they set upon him.

Sir Driant also appeared on the bridge where Sir Tristram encountered Arthur's knights. Sir Bleoberis and Sir Driant arrived, and Sir Bleoberis proffered to joust with Tristram, who smote him down (225-008). Through these appearances, Driant emerges as a reliable companion among the fellowship of the Round Table, present at important gatherings though seldom the central figure.
