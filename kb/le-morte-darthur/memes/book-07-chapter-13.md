[140-001]Sir Persant and [[sir-gareth|Beaumains]], and how he told him that his name was Sir

[140-002]Gareth.

[140-003]And so on the morn the damosel and Sir Beaumains heard mass and brake their

[140-004]fast, and so took their leave. Fair damosel, said Persant, whitherward are ye

[140-005]way-leading this knight? Sir, she said, this knight is going to the siege that

[140-006]besiegeth my sister in the Castle Dangerous. Ah, ah, said Persant, that is the

[140-007]Knight of the Red Laund, the which is the most perilous knight that I know now

[140-008]living, and a man that is without mercy, and men say that he hath seven

[140-009]men’s strength. God save you, said he to Beaumains, from that knight, for

[140-010]he doth great wrong to that lady, and that is great [[mercy|pity]], for she is one of the

[140-011]fairest ladies of the world, and meseemeth that your damosel is her sister: is

[140-012]not your name Linet? said he. Yea, sir, said she, and my lady my sister’s

[140-013]name is Dame Lionesse. Now shall I tell you, said Sir Persant, this Red Knight

[140-014]of the Red Laund hath lain long at the siege, well-nigh this two years, and

[140-015]many times he might have had her an he had would, but he prolongeth the time to

[140-016]this intent, for to have [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot du Lake]] to do battle with him, or Sir

[140-017][[sir-tristram|Tristram]], or Sir Lamorak de Galis, or [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]], and this is his tarrying so

[140-018]long at the siege.

[140-019]Now my lord Sir Persant of Inde, said the damosel Linet, I require you that ye

[140-020]will make this gentleman knight or ever he [[battle|fight]] with the Red Knight. I will

[140-021]with all my heart, said Sir Persant, an it please him to take the order of

[140-022]knighthood of so simple a man as I am. Sir, said Beaumains, I thank you for

[140-023]your good will, for I am better sped, for certainly the noble knight Sir

[140-024]Launcelot made me knight. Ah, said Sir Persant, of a more renowned knight might

[140-025]ye not be made knight; for of all knights he may be called chief of knighthood;

[140-026]and so all the world saith, that betwixt three knights is departed clearly

[140-027]knighthood, that is Launcelot du Lake, Sir Tristram de Liones, and Sir Lamorak

[140-028]de Galis: these bear now the [[honour|renown]]. There be many other knights, as Sir

[140-029]Palamides the Saracen and Sir Safere his brother; also Sir Bleoberis and Sir

[140-030]Blamore de Ganis his brother; also [[sir-bors|Sir Bors de Ganis]] and Sir Ector de Maris and

[140-031][[sir-percivale|Sir Percivale]] de Galis; these and many more be noble knights, but there be none

[140-032]that pass the three above said; therefore God speed you well, said Sir Persant,

[140-033]for an ye may match the Red Knight ye shall be called the fourth of the world.

[140-034]Sir, said Beaumains, I would fain be of good fame and of knighthood. And I let

[140-035]you wit I came of good men, for I dare say my father was a noble man, and so

[140-036]that ye will keep it in close, and this damosel, I will tell you of what kin I

[140-037]am. We will not discover you, said they both, till ye command us, by the faith

[140-038]we owe unto God. Truly then, said he, my name is Gareth of Orkney, and King Lot

[140-039]was my father, and my mother is [[king-arthur|King Arthur]]’s sister, her name is Dame

[140-040]Morgawse, and Sir Gawaine is my brother, and Sir Agravaine and Sir Gaheris, and

[140-041]I am the youngest of them all. And yet wot not King Arthur nor Sir Gawaine what

[140-042]I am.
