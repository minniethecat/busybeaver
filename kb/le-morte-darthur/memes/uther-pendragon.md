# Uther Pendragon

**Uther Pendragon** is the father of [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] and the [[king|sovereign]] whose reign precedes the Arthurian age. Caxton's preface records how "Uther Pendragon gat the noble conqueror King Arthur" (002-104), and the narrative that follows traces his [[realm|kingdom]] from its days of power to the succession crisis that brings Arthur to the throne.

## King of All England

"It befell in the days of Uther Pendragon, when he was king of all England" (004-003) — so begins the First Book, establishing Uther's dominion as the baseline from which Arthur's story departs. He is a war-king, a ruler who commands [[fealty]] from his [[baron|lords]] and wages [[battle|war]] against his enemies, and his court is the centre of political power in the realm. The narrative presents him as a figure of authority whose desires — particularly his [[love]] for [[igraine|Igraine]] — drive the early action of the cycle.

## The Begetting of Arthur

Uther's most consequential act is the begetting of Arthur upon [[igraine|Igraine]], the [[lady|Duchess of Cornwall]], accomplished through [[merlin|Merlin's]] [[enchantment]]. Merlin transforms Uther into the likeness of Igraine's husband, the Duke of Cornwall, and "after wedded her Uther Pendragon, and gat on her" (119-026) the child who would become the greatest of British kings. The [[deception]] of this union — a king using magic to take another man's wife — is the founding ambiguity of Arthur's [[lineage]], and it shapes the [[tragedy|tragic]] logic of the entire cycle.

## Death and Succession

"After the death of Uther Pendragon reigned Arthur his son" (032-002). Uther's passing creates the power vacuum that defines Arthur's early reign: the [[realm|kingdom]] fragments, rival kings rise, and the young Arthur must prove his right to rule. The barons "knew not that he was Uther Pendragon's" (052-004) son — a hidden identity that makes the [[sword-in-the-stone|sword in the stone]] the necessary proof of Arthur's [[lineage]] and divine election.
