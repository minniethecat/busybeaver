[204-001][[king-arthur|King Arthur]], and how [[sir-kay|Sir Kay]] called him in scorn La Cote Male Taile.

[204-002]At [[camelot|the court of King Arthur]] there came a young man and bigly made, and he was

[204-003]richly beseen: and he desired to be made knight of the king, but his

[204-004]over-garment sat over-thwartly, howbeit it was rich cloth of gold. What is your

[204-005]name? said King Arthur. Sir, said he, my name is Breunor le Noire, and within

[204-006]short space ye shall know that I am of good kin. It may well be, said Sir Kay,

[204-007]the Seneschal, but in mockage ye shall be called La Cote Male Taile, that is as

[204-008]much to say, the evil-shapen coat. It is a great thing that thou askest, said

[204-009]the king; and for what cause wearest thou that rich coat? tell me, for I can

[204-010]well think for some cause it is. Sir, he answered, I had a father, a noble

[204-011]knight, and as he rode a-hunting, upon a day it happed him to lay him down to

[204-012]sleep; and there came a knight that had been long his enemy, and when he saw he

[204-013]was fast asleep he all to-hew him; and this same coat had my father on the same

[204-014]time; and that maketh this coat to sit so evil upon me, for the strokes be on

[204-015]it as I found it, and never shall be amended for me. Thus to have my

[204-016]father’s death in remembrance I wear this coat till I be revenged; and

[204-017]because ye are called the most noblest king of the world I come to you that ye

[204-018]should make me knight. Sir, said Sir Lamorak and Sir Gaheris, it were well done

[204-019]to make him knight; for him beseemeth well of person and of countenance, that

[204-020]he shall prove a good man, and a good knight, and a mighty; for, Sir, an ye be

[204-021]remembered, even such one was [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot du Lake]] when he came first into

[204-022]this court, and full few of us knew from whence he came; and now is he proved

[204-023]the man of most [[honour|worship]] in the world; and all your court and all your Round

[204-024]Table is by Sir Launcelot worshipped and amended more than by any knight now

[204-025]living. That is truth, said the king, and to-morrow at your request I shall

[204-026]make him knight.

[204-027]So on the morrow there was an hart found, and thither rode King Arthur with a

[204-028][[fellowship|company]] of his knights to slay the hart. And this young man that Sir Kay named

[204-029]La Cote Male Taile was there left behind with [[queen-guenever|Queen Guenever]]; and by sudden

[204-030]adventure there was an horrible lion kept in a strong tower of stone, and it

[204-031]happened that he at that time brake loose, and came hurling afore the queen and

[204-032]her knights. And when the queen saw the lion she cried and fled, and prayed her

[204-033]knights to rescue her. And there was none of them all but twelve that abode,

[204-034]and all the other fled. Then said La Cote Male Taile: Now I see well that all

[204-035]coward knights be not dead; and therewithal he drew his sword and dressed him

[204-036]afore the lion. And that lion gaped wide and came upon him ramping to have

[204-037]slain him. And he then smote him in the midst of the head such a mighty stroke

[204-038]that it clave his head in sunder, and dashed to the earth. Then was it told the

[204-039]queen how the young man that Sir Kay named by scorn La Cote Male Taile had

[204-040]slain the lion. With that the king came home. And when the queen told him of

[204-041]that adventure, he was well pleased, and said: Upon pain of mine head he shall

[204-042]prove a noble man and a faithful knight, and true of his promise: then the king

[204-043]forthwithal made him knight. Now Sir, said this young knight, I require you and

[204-044]all the knights of your court, that ye call me by none other name but La Cote

[204-045]Male Taile: in so much as Sir Kay hath so named me so will I be called. I

[204-046]assent me well thereto, said the king.
