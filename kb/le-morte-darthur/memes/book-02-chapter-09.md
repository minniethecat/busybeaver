[040-001]counsel of [[merlin|Merlin]], took King Rience and brought him to [[king-arthur|King Arthur]].

[040-002]Then Merlin lodged them in a wood among leaves beside the highway, and took off

[040-003]the bridles of their horses and put them to grass and laid them down to rest

[040-004]them till it was nigh midnight. Then Merlin bade them rise, and make them

[040-005]ready, for the king was nigh them, that was stolen away from his host with a

[040-006]three score horses of his best knights, and twenty of them rode to-fore to warn

[040-007]the Lady de Vance that the king was coming; for that night King Rience should

[040-008]have lain with her. Which is the king? said Balin. Abide, said Merlin, here in

[040-009]a strait way ye shall meet with him; and therewith he showed Balin and his

[040-010]brother where he rode.

[040-011]Anon Balin and his brother met with the king, and smote him down, and wounded

[040-012]him fiercely, and laid him to the ground; and there they slew on the right hand

[040-013]and the left hand, and slew more than forty of his men, and the remnant fled.

[040-014]Then went they again to King Rience and would have slain him had he not yielded

[040-015]him unto their grace. Then said he thus: Knights full of [[courage|prowess]], slay me not,

[040-016]for by my life ye may win, and by my death ye shall win nothing. Then said

[040-017]these two knights, Ye say sooth and truth, and so laid him on a horse-litter.

[040-018]With that Merlin was vanished, and came to King Arthur aforehand, and told him

[040-019]how his most enemy was taken and discomfited. By whom? said King Arthur. By two

[040-020]knights, said Merlin, that would please your lordship, and to-morrow ye shall

[040-021]know what knights they are. Anon after came the Knight with the Two Swords and

[040-022]Balan his brother, and brought with them King Rience of North Wales, and there

[040-023]delivered him to the porters, and charged them with him; and so they two

[040-024]returned again in the dawning of the day. King Arthur came then to King Rience,

[040-025]and said, Sir king, ye are welcome: by what adventure come ye hither? Sir, said

[040-026]King Rience, I came hither by an hard adventure. Who won you? said King Arthur.

[040-027]Sir, said the king, the Knight with the Two Swords and his brother, which are

[040-028]two [[marvel|marvellous]] knights of prowess. I know them not, said Arthur, but much I am

[040-029]beholden to them. Ah, said Merlin, I shall tell you: it is Balin that achieved

[040-030]the sword, and his brother Balan, a good knight, there liveth not a better of

[040-031]prowess and of worthiness, and it shall be the greatest dole of him that ever I

[040-032]knew of knight, for he shall not long endure. Alas, said King Arthur, that is

[040-033]great [[mercy|pity]]; for I am much beholden unto him, and I have ill deserved it unto

[040-034]him for his kindness. Nay, said Merlin, he shall do much more for you, and that

[040-035]shall ye know in haste. But, sir, are ye purveyed, said Merlin, for to-morn the

[040-036]host of Nero, King Rience’s brother, will set on you or noon with a great

[040-037]host, and therefore make you ready, for I will depart from you.
