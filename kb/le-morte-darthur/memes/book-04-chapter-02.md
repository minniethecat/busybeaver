[068-001][[battle|war]] against [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], and what counsel Arthur had against them.

[068-002]And as King Arthur rode to [[camelot|Camelot]], and held there a great feast with mirth and

[068-003]joy, so soon after he returned unto Cardoile, and there came unto Arthur new

[068-004]tidings that the king of Denmark, and the king of Ireland that was his brother,

[068-005]and the king of the Vale, and the king of Soleise, and the king of the Isle of

[068-006]Longtains, all these five kings with a great host were entered into the land of

[068-007]King Arthur, and burnt and slew clean afore them, both cities and castles, that

[068-008]it was [[mercy|pity]] to hear. Alas, said Arthur, yet had I never rest one month since I

[068-009]was crowned king of this land. Now shall I never rest till I meet with those

[068-010]kings in a fair field, that I make mine avow; for my true liege people shall

[068-011]not be destroyed in my default, go with me who will, and abide who that will.

[068-012]Then the king let write unto King Pellinore, and prayed him in all haste to

[068-013]make him ready with such people as he might lightliest rear and hie him after

[068-014]in all haste. All the barons were privily wroth that the king would depart so

[068-015]suddenly; but the king by no mean would abide, but made writing unto them that

[068-016]were not there, and bade them hie after him, such as were not at that time in

[068-017]the court. Then the king came to [[queen-guenever|Queen Guenever]], and said, Lady, make you

[068-018]ready, for ye shall go with me, for I may not long miss you; ye shall cause me

[068-019]to be the more hardy, what adventure so befall me; I will not wit my lady to be

[068-020]in no jeopardy. Sir, said she, I am at your commandment, and shall be ready

[068-021]what time so ye be ready. So on the morn the king and the queen departed with

[068-022]such fellowship as they had, and came into the north, into a forest beside

[068-023]Humber, and there lodged them. When the word and tiding came unto the five

[068-024]kings above said, that Arthur was beside Humber in a forest, there was a

[068-025]knight, brother unto one of the five kings, that gave them this counsel: Ye

[068-026]know well that Sir Arthur hath the flower of chivalry of the world with him, as

[068-027]it is proved by the great battle he did with the eleven kings; and therefore

[068-028]hie unto him night and day till that we be nigh him, for the longer he tarrieth

[068-029]the bigger he is, and we ever the weaker; and he is so courageous of himself

[068-030]that he is come to the field with little people, and therefore let us set upon

[068-031]him or day and we shall slay down; of his knights there shall none escape.
