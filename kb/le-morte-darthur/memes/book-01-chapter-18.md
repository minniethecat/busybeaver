[021-001][[sir-bors|Bors]] rescued King Leodegrance, and other incidents.

[021-002]And then [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], and King Ban, and King Bors departed with their

[021-003]fellowship, a twenty thousand, and came within six days into the country of

[021-004]Cameliard, and there rescued King Leodegrance, and slew there much people of

[021-005]King Rience, unto the number of ten thousand men, and put him to flight. And

[021-006]then had these three kings great cheer of King Leodegrance, that thanked them

[021-007]of their great goodness, that they would revenge him of his enemies; and there

[021-008]had Arthur the first sight of [[queen-guenever|Guenever]], the king’s daughter of Cameliard,

[021-009]and ever after he loved her. After they were wedded, as it telleth in the book.

[021-010]So, briefly to make an end, they took their leave to go into their own

[021-011]countries, for King Claudas did great destruction on their lands. Then said

[021-012]Arthur, I will go with you. Nay, said the kings, ye shall not at this time, for

[021-013]ye have much to do yet in these lands, therefore we will depart, and with the

[021-014]great goods that we have gotten in these lands by your gifts, we shall wage

[021-015]good knights and withstand the King Claudas’ malice, for by the grace of

[021-016]God, an we have need we will send to you for your succour; and if ye have need,

[021-017]send for us, and we will not tarry, by the faith of our bodies. It shall not,

[021-018]said [[merlin|Merlin]], need that these two kings come again in the way of [[battle|war]], but I know

[021-019]well King Arthur may not be long from you, for within a year or two ye shall

[021-020]have great need, and then shall he revenge you on your enemies, as ye have done

[021-021]on his. For these eleven kings shall die all in a day, by the great might and

[021-022][[courage|prowess]] of arms of two valiant knights (as it telleth after); their names be

[021-023]Balin le Savage, and Balan, his brother, that be [[marvel|marvellous]] good knights as be

[021-024]any living.

[021-025]Now turn we to the eleven kings that returned unto a city that hight Sorhaute,

[021-026]the which city was within King Uriens’, and there they refreshed them as

[021-027]well as they might, and made leeches search their wounds, and sorrowed greatly

[021-028]for the death of their people. With that there came a messenger and told how

[021-029]there was come into their lands people that were lawless as well as Saracens, a

[021-030]forty thousand, and have burnt and slain all the people that they may come by,

[021-031]without mercy, and have laid siege on the castle of Wandesborow. Alas, said the

[021-032]eleven kings, here is sorrow upon sorrow, and if we had not warred against

[021-033]Arthur as we have done, he would soon revenge us. As for King Leodegrance, he

[021-034]loveth Arthur better than us, and as for King Rience, he hath enough to do with

[021-035]Leodegrance, for he hath laid siege unto him. So they consented together to

[021-036]keep all the marches of Cornwall, of Wales, and of the North. So first, they

[021-037]put King Idres in the City of Nauntes in Britain, with four thousand men of

[021-038]arms, to watch both the water and the land. Also they put in the city of

[021-039]Windesan, King Nentres of Garlot, with four thousand knights to watch both on

[021-040]water and on land. Also they had of other men of war more than eight thousand,

[021-041]for to fortify all the fortresses in the marches of Cornwall. Also they put

[021-042]more knights in all the marches of Wales and Scotland, with many good men of

[021-043]arms, and so they kept them together the space of three year, and ever allied

[021-044]them with mighty kings and dukes and lords. And to them fell King Rience of

[021-045]North Wales, the which and Nero that was a mighty man of men. And all this

[021-046]while they furnished them and garnished them of good men of arms, and victual,

[021-047]and of all manner of habiliment that pretendeth to the war, to avenge them for

[021-048]the battle of Bedegraine, as it telleth in the book of adventures following.
