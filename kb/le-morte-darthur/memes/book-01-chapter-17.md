[020-001]it was ended by [[merlin|Merlin]].

[020-002]When Sir [[king-arthur|Arthur]] and King Ban and [[sir-bors|Bors]] beheld them and all their knights, they

[020-003]praised them much for their noble cheer of chivalry, for the hardiest fighters

[020-004]that ever they heard or saw. With that, there dressed them a forty noble

[020-005]knights, and said unto the three kings, they would break their battle; these

[020-006]were their names: Lionses, Phariance, Ulfius, Brastias, Ector, [[sir-kay|Kay]], Lucas the

[020-007]butler, Griflet le Fise de Dieu, Mariet de la Roche, Guinas de Bloi, Briant de

[020-008]la Forest Savage, Bellaus, Morians of the Castle [of] Maidens, Flannedrius of

[020-009]the Castle of Ladies, Annecians that was King Bors’ godson, a noble

[020-010]knight, Ladinas de la Rouse, Emerause, Caulas, Graciens le Castlein, one Blois

[020-011]de la Case, and Sir Colgrevaunce de Gorre; all these knights rode on afore with

[020-012]spears on their thighs, and spurred their horses mightily as the horses might

[020-013]run. And the eleven kings with part of their knights rushed with their horses

[020-014]as fast as they might with their spears, and there they did on both parties

[020-015][[marvel|marvellous]] deeds of arms. So came into the thick of the press, Arthur, Ban, and

[020-016]Bors, and slew down right on both hands, that their horses went in blood up to

[020-017]the fetlocks. But ever the eleven kings and their host was ever in the visage

[020-018]of Arthur. Wherefore Ban and Bors had great marvel, considering the great

[020-019]slaughter that there was, but at the last they were driven aback over a little

[020-020]river. With that came Merlin on a great black horse, and said unto Arthur, Thou

[020-021]hast never done! Hast thou not done enough? of three score thousand this day

[020-022]hast thou left alive but fifteen thousand, and it is time to say Ho! For God is

[020-023]wroth with thee, that thou wilt never have done; for yonder eleven kings at

[020-024]this time will not be overthrown, but an thou tarry on them any longer, thy

[020-025]fortune will turn and they shall increase. And therefore withdraw you unto your

[020-026]lodging, and rest you as soon as ye may, and reward your good knights with gold

[020-027]and with silver, for they have well deserved it; there may no riches be too

[020-028]dear for them, for of so few men as ye have, there were never men did more of

[020-029][[courage|prowess]] than they have done today, for ye have matched this day with the best

[020-030]fighters of the world. That is truth, said King Ban and Bors. Also said Merlin,

[020-031]withdraw you where ye list, for this three year I dare undertake they shall not

[020-032]dere you; and by then ye shall hear new tidings. And then Merlin said unto

[020-033]Arthur, These eleven kings have more on hand than they are ware of, for the

[020-034]Saracens are landed in their countries, more than forty thousand, that burn and

[020-035]slay, and have laid siege at the castle Wandesborow, and make great

[020-036]destruction; therefore dread you not this three year. Also, sir, all the goods

[020-037]that be gotten at this battle, let it be searched, and when ye have it in your

[020-038]hands, let it be given freely unto these two kings, Ban and Bors, that they may

[020-039]reward their knights withal; and that shall cause strangers to be of better

[020-040]will to do you service at need. Also you be able to reward your own knights of

[020-041]your own goods whensomever it liketh you. It is well said, quoth Arthur, and as

[020-042]thou hast devised, so shall it be done. When it was delivered to Ban and Bors,

[020-043]they gave the goods as freely to their knights as freely as it was given to

[020-044]them. Then Merlin took his leave of Arthur and of the two kings, for to go and

[020-045]see his master Bleise, that dwelt in Northumberland; and so he departed and

[020-046]came to his master, that was passing glad of his coming; and there he told how

[020-047]Arthur and the two kings had sped at the great battle, and how it was ended,

[020-048]and told the names of every king and knight of [[honour|worship]] that was there. And so

[020-049]Bleise wrote the battle word by word, as Merlin told him, how it began, and by

[020-050]whom, and in likewise how it was ended, and who had the worse. All the battles

[020-051]that were done in Arthur’s days Merlin did his master Bleise do write;

[020-052]also he did do write all the battles that every worthy knight did of

[020-053]Arthur’s court.

[020-054]After this Merlin departed from his master and came to King Arthur, that was in

[020-055]the castle of Bedegraine, that was one of the castles that stand in the forest

[020-056]of Sherwood. And Merlin was so disguised that King Arthur knew him not, for he

[020-057]was all befurred in black sheep-skins, and a great pair of boots, and a bow and

[020-058]arrows, in a russet gown, and brought wild geese in his hand, and it was on the

[020-059]morn after Candlemas day; but King Arthur knew him not. Sir, said Merlin unto

[020-060]the king, will ye give me a gift? Wherefore, said King Arthur, should I give

[020-061]thee a gift, churl? Sir, said Merlin, ye were better to give me a gift that is

[020-062]not in your hand than to lose great riches, for here in the same place where

[020-063]the great battle was, is great treasure hid in the earth. Who told thee so,

[020-064]churl? said Arthur. Merlin told me so, said he. Then Ulfius and Brastias knew

[020-065]him well enough, and smiled. Sir, said these two knights, it is Merlin that so

[020-066]speaketh unto you. Then King Arthur was greatly abashed, and had marvel of

[020-067]Merlin, and so had King Ban and King Bors, and so they had great disport at

[020-068]him. So in the meanwhile there came a damosel that was an earl’s

[020-069]daughter: his name was Sanam, and her name was Lionors, a passing fair damosel;

[020-070]and so she came thither for to do homage, as other lords did after the great

[020-071]battle. And King Arthur set his love greatly upon her, and so did she upon him,

[020-072]and the king had ado with her, and gat on her a child: his name was Borre, that

[020-073]was after a good knight, and of the [[round-table|Table Round]]. Then there came word that the

[020-074]King Rience of North Wales made great [[battle|war]] on King Leodegrance of Cameliard, for

[020-075]the which thing Arthur was wroth, for he loved him well, and hated King Rience,

[020-076]for he was alway against him. So by ordinance of the three kings that were sent

[020-077]home unto Benwick, all they would depart for dread of King Claudas; and

[020-078]Phariance, and Antemes, and Gratian, and Lionses [of] Payarne, with the leaders

[020-079]of those that should keep the kings’ lands.
