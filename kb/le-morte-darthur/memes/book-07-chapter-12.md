[139-001]Persant of Inde, and made him to be yielden.

[139-002]With this Sir Persant of Inde had espied them as they hoved in the [[battle|field]], and

[139-003]knightly he sent to them whether he came in war or in peace. Say to thy lord,

[139-004]said [[sir-gareth|Beaumains]], I take no force, but whether as him list himself. So the

[139-005]messenger went again unto Sir Persant and told him all his answer. Well then

[139-006]will I have ado with him to the utterance, and so he purveyed him and rode

[139-007]against him. And Beaumains saw him and made him ready, and there they met with

[139-008]all that ever their horses might run, and brast their spears either in three

[139-009]pieces, and their horses rushed so together that both their horses fell dead to

[139-010]the earth; and lightly they avoided their horses and put their shields afore

[139-011]them, and drew their swords, and gave many great strokes that sometime they

[139-012]hurtled together that they fell grovelling on the ground. Thus they fought two

[139-013]hours and more, that their shields and their hauberks were all forhewen, and in

[139-014]many steads they were wounded. So at the last Sir Beaumains smote him through

[139-015]the cost of the body, and then he retrayed him here and there, and knightly

[139-016]maintained his battle long time. And at the last, though him loath were,

[139-017]Beaumains smote Sir Persant above upon the helm, that he fell grovelling to the

[139-018]earth; and then he leapt upon him overthwart and unlaced his helm to have slain

[139-019]him.

[139-020]Then Sir Persant yielded him and asked him mercy. With that came the damosel

[139-021]and prayed to save his life. I will well, for it were [[mercy|pity]] this noble knight

[139-022]should die. Gramercy, said Persant, gentle knight and damosel. For certainly

[139-023]now I wot well it was ye that slew my brother the Black Knight at the black

[139-024]thorn; he was a full noble knight, his name was Sir Percard. Also I am sure

[139-025]that ye are he that won mine other brother the Green Knight, his name was Sir

[139-026]Pertolepe. Also ye won my brother the Red Knight, Sir Perimones. And now since

[139-027]ye have won these, this shall I do for to please you: ye shall have homage and

[139-028]fealty of me, and an hundred knights to be always at your commandment, to go

[139-029]and ride where ye will command us. And so they went unto Sir Persant’s

[139-030]pavilion and drank the wine, and ate spices, and afterward Sir Persant made him

[139-031]to rest upon a bed until supper time, and after supper to bed again. When

[139-032]Beaumains was abed, Sir Persant had a lady, a fair daughter of eighteen year of

[139-033]age, and there he called her unto him, and charged her and commanded her upon

[139-034]his blessing to go unto the knight’s bed, and lie down by his side, and

[139-035]make him no strange cheer, but good cheer, and take him in thine arms and kiss

[139-036]him, and look that this be done, I charge you, as ye will have my love and my

[139-037]good will. So Sir Persant’s daughter did as her father bade her, and so

[139-038]she went unto Sir Beaumains’ bed, and privily she dispoiled her, and laid

[139-039]her down by him, and then he awoke and saw her, and asked her what she was.

[139-040]Sir, she said, I am Sir Persant’s daughter, that by the commandment of my

[139-041]father am come hither. Be ye a maid or a wife? said he. Sir, she said, I am a

[139-042]clean maiden. God defend, said he, that I should defoil you to do Sir Persant

[139-043]such a shame; therefore, fair damosel, arise out of this bed or else I will.

[139-044]Sir, she said, I came not to you by mine own will, but as I was commanded.

[139-045]Alas, said Sir Beaumains, I were a shameful knight an I would do your father

[139-046]any disworship; and so he kissed her, and so she departed and came unto Sir

[139-047]Persant her father, and told him all how she had sped. Truly, said Sir Persant,

[139-048]whatsomever he be, he is come of a noble blood. And so we leave them there till

[139-049]on the morn.
