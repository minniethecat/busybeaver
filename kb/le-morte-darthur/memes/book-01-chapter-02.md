[005-002][[king-arthur|Arthur]].

[005-003]Then Ulfius was glad, and rode on more than a pace till that he came to King

[005-004]Uther Pendragon, and told him he had met with Merlin. Where is he? said the

[005-005]king. Sir, said Ulfius, he will not dwell long. Therewithal Ulfius was ware

[005-006]where Merlin stood at the porch of the pavilion’s door. And then Merlin

[005-007]was bound to come to the king. When King Uther saw him, he said he was welcome.

[005-008]Sir, said Merlin, I know all your heart every deal; so ye will be sworn unto me

[005-009]as ye be a true king anointed, to fulfil my desire, ye shall have your desire.

[005-010]Then the king was sworn upon the Four Evangelists. Sir, said Merlin, this is my

[005-011]desire: the first night that ye shall lie by Igraine ye shall get a child on

[005-012]her, and when that is born, that it shall be delivered to me for to nourish

[005-013]there as I will have it; for it shall be your [[honour|worship]], and the child’s

[005-014]avail, as mickle as the child is worth. I will well, said the king, as thou

[005-015]wilt have it. Now make you ready, said Merlin, this night ye shall lie with

[005-016]Igraine in the castle of Tintagil; and ye shall be like the duke her husband,

[005-017]Ulfius shall be like Sir Brastias, a knight of the duke’s, and I will be

[005-018]like a knight that hight Sir Jordanus, a knight of the duke’s. But wait

[005-019]ye make not many questions with her nor her men, but say ye are diseased, and

[005-020]so hie you to bed, and rise not on the morn till I come to you, for the castle

[005-021]of Tintagil is but ten miles hence; so this was done as they devised. But the

[005-022]duke of Tintagil espied how the king rode from the siege of Terrabil, and

[005-023]therefore that night he issued out of the castle at a postern for to have

[005-024]distressed the king’s host. And so, through his own issue, the duke

[005-025]himself was slain or ever the king came at the castle of Tintagil.

[005-026]So after the death of the duke, King Uther lay with Igraine more than three

[005-027]hours after his death, and begat on her that night Arthur, and on day came

[005-028]Merlin to the king, and bade him make him ready, and so he kissed the lady

[005-029]Igraine and departed in all haste. But when the lady heard tell of the duke her

[005-030]husband, and by all record he was dead or ever King Uther came to her, then she

[005-031]marvelled who that might be that lay with her in likeness of her lord; so she

[005-032]mourned privily and held her peace. Then all the barons by one assent prayed

[005-033]the king of accord betwixt the lady Igraine and him; the king gave them leave,

[005-034]for fain would he have been accorded with her. So the king put all the trust in

[005-035]Ulfius to entreat between them, so by the entreaty at the last the king and she

[005-036]met together. Now will we do well, said Ulfius, our king is a lusty knight and

[005-037]wifeless, and my lady Igraine is a passing fair lady; it were great joy unto us

[005-038]all, an it might please the king to make her his queen. Unto that they all well

[005-039]accorded and moved it to the king. And anon, like a lusty knight, he assented

[005-040]thereto with good will, and so in all haste they were married in a morning with

[005-041]great mirth and joy.

[005-042]And King Lot of Lothian and of Orkney then wedded Margawse that was

[005-043][[sir-gawaine|Gawaine]]’s mother, and King Nentres of the land of Garlot wedded Elaine.

[005-044]All this was done at the request of King Uther. And the third sister [[morgan-le-fay|Morgan]] le

[005-045]Fay was put to school in a nunnery, and there she learned so much that she was

[005-046]a great clerk of necromancy. And after she was wedded to King Uriens of the

[005-047]land of Gore, that was Sir Ewain’s le Blanchemain’s father.
