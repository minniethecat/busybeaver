[087-003]And so he chose her for his sovereign lady, and never to love other but her,

[087-004]but she was so proud that she had scorn of him, and said that she would never

[087-005]love him though he would die for her. Wherefore all ladies and gentlewomen had

[087-006]scorn of her that she was so proud, for there were fairer than she, and there

[087-007]was none that was there but an Sir Pelleas would have proffered them love, they

[087-008]would have loved him for his noble [[courage|prowess]]. And so this knight promised the

[087-009]Lady Ettard to follow her into this country, and never to leave her till she

[087-010]loved him. And thus he is here the most part nigh her, and lodged by a priory,

[087-011]and every week she sendeth knights to [[battle|fight]] with him. And when he hath put them

[087-012]to the worse, then will he suffer them wilfully to take him prisoner, because

[087-013]he would have a sight of this lady. And always she doth him great despite, for

[087-014]sometime she maketh her knights to tie him to his horse’s tail, and some

[087-015]to bind him under the horse’s belly; thus in the most shamefullest ways

[087-016]that she can think he is brought to her. And all she doth it for to cause him

[087-017]to leave this country, and to leave his loving; but all this cannot make him to

[087-018]leave, for an he would have fought on foot he might have had the better of the

[087-019]ten knights as well on foot as on horseback. Alas, said Sir Gawaine, it is

[087-020]great [[mercy|pity]] of him; and after this night I will seek him to-morrow, in this

[087-021]forest, to do him all the help I can. So on the morn Sir Gawaine took his leave

[087-022]of his host Sir Carados, and rode into the forest; and at the last he met with

[087-023]Sir Pelleas, making great moan out of measure, so each of them saluted other,

[087-024]and asked him why he made such sorrow. And as it is above rehearsed, Sir

[087-025]Pelleas told Sir Gawaine: But always I suffer her knights to fare so with me as

[087-026]ye saw yesterday, in trust at the last to win her love, for she knoweth well

[087-027]all her knights should not lightly win me, an me list to fight with them to the

[087-028]uttermost. Wherefore an I loved her not so sore, I had liefer die an hundred

[087-029]times, an I might die so oft, rather than I would suffer that despite; but I

[087-030]trust she will have pity upon me at the last, for love causeth many a good

[087-031]knight to suffer to have his entent, but alas I am unfortunate. And therewith

[087-032]he made so great dole and sorrow that unnethe he might hold him on horseback.

[087-033]Now, said Sir Gawaine, leave your mourning and I shall promise you by the faith

[087-034]of my body to do all that lieth in my power to get you the love of your lady,

[087-035]and thereto I will plight you my troth. Ah, said Sir Pelleas, of what court are

[087-036]ye? tell me, I pray you, my good friend. And then Sir Gawaine said, I am of the

[087-037]court of [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], and his sister’s son, and King Lot of Orkney was my

[087-038]father, and my name is Sir Gawaine. And then he said, My name is Sir Pelleas,

[087-039]born in the Isles, and of many isles I am lord, and never have I loved lady nor

[087-040]damosel till now in an unhappy time; and, sir knight, since ye are so nigh

[087-041]cousin unto King Arthur, and a king’s son, therefore betray me not but

[087-042]help me, for I may never come by her but by some good knight, for she is in a

[087-043]strong castle here, fast by within this four mile, and over all this country

[087-044]she is lady of. And so I may never come to her presence, but as I suffer her

[087-045]knights to take me, and but if I did so that I might have a sight of her, I had

[087-046]been dead long or this time; and yet fair word had I never of her, but when I

[087-047]am brought to-fore her she rebuketh me in the foulest manner. And then they

[087-048]take my horse and harness and put me out of the gates, and she will not suffer

[087-049]me to eat nor drink; and always I offer me to be her prisoner, but that she

[087-050]will not suffer me, for I would desire no more, what pains so ever I had, so

[087-051]that I might have a sight of her daily. Well, said Sir Gawaine, all this shall

[087-052]I amend an ye will do as I shall devise: I will have your horse and your

[087-053]armour, and so will I ride unto her castle and tell her that I have slain you,

[087-054]and so shall I come within her to cause her to cherish me, and then shall I do

[087-055]my true part that ye shall not fail to have the love of her.
