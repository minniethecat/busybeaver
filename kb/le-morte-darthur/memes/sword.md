The **sword** is the defining weapon of the knight in Malory's [[Le Morte d'Arthur]], but its significance extends far beyond the battle[[battle|field]]. A sword may be the proof of [[king|kingship]], as when [[King Arthur]] draws the sword from the stone (008-018); it may be a named heirloom whose history is inseparable from its bearer, as with [[Excalibur]]; and it may serve as a narrative token—a sword given, lent, or refused—that drives the plot for[[battle|war]]d. The sword is at once an instrument of [[prowess]], a symbol of [[sovereign|sovereignty]], and a material link between knight and identity.

## The Sword as Proof of Sovereignty

The sword in the stone is the mechanism by which [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s [[king|kingship]] is revealed and validated. The test is simple but absolute: only the true [[kinsman|kin]]g may draw the sword, and all others fail:

> Whoso pulleth out this sword of this stone and anvil, is rightwise king born of all England (008-019)

The sword is divinely ordained as the arbiter of legitimacy. When no knight at the tournament can stir it, the sword exposes the limits of merely human [[courage|prowess]] (008-026). [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s drawing of the sword is not an act of strength but of identity—the sword recognises its rightful bearer, and in doing so reveals a [[king|kingship]] that [[lineage|birth]] alone had concealed.

## Named Swords and Their Histories

A named sword in Malory carries its own history, independent of its current wielder. [[Excalibur]], bestowed upon [[king-arthur|Arthur]] by the [[Lady of the Lake]], is not merely a superior weapon but a token of [[marvel|supernatural]] sanction. When [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s sword is broken in battle, the gift of [[sword-in-the-stone|Excalibur]] restores not only his [[battle|fight]]ing capacity but his claim to rule. The sword's eventual return to the lake at the end of [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s reign closes the arc that its bestowal opened—the sword departs when the [[king|kingship]] it sustained departs.

## The Sword in Combat

In the hand of a skilled knight, the sword is the instrument of [[prowess]]. [[king-arthur|Arthur]] himself "laid on with a sword, and did [[marvel|marvellous]] deeds of arms" on horseback in the thick of battle (012-020). The sword is the weapon of close [[battle|combat]], of the press, of the moment when [[spear|lance]] and [[shield]] have been spent and the [[battle|fight]] comes down to blade against blade. A knight's skill with the sword is the ultimate measure of his martial worth, and the brea[[kinsman|kin]]g or loss of a sword is a crisis that only a true companion—or a [[enchantment|magic]]al intervention—can resolve.

## The Sword as Narrative Token

Beyond [[battle|combat]], the sword functions as a narrative device. [[sir-kay|Sir Kay]]'s lost sword sends young [[king-arthur|Arthur]] on the [[quest|errand]] that leads him to the stone (008-040). The sword that Balin draws despite the damosel's [[battle|war]]ning sets in motion the Dolorous Stroke. A sword given in [[fealty]], a sword refused in [[shame]], a sword laid across a sleeping knight and queen—each of these gestures speaks in a language that all the characters understand. The sword is a medium of communication as much as a tool of violence.
