[161-001]they loved each other to [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], and of the appointment of their

[161-002]wedding.

[161-003]And among all these ladies she was named the fairest, and peerless. Then when

[161-004][[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]] saw her there was many a goodly look and goodly words, that all men

[161-005]of [[honour|worship]] had joy to behold them. Then came King Arthur and many other kings,

[161-006]and Dame [[queen-guenever|Guenever]], and the Queen of Orkney. And there the king asked his

[161-007]nephew, [[sir-gareth|Sir Gareth]], whether he would have that lady as paramour, or to have her

[161-008]to his wife. My lord, wit you well that I love her above all ladies living.

[161-009]Now, fair lady, said King Arthur, what say ye? Most noble King, said Dame

[161-010]Lionesse, wit you well that my lord, Sir Gareth, is to me more liefer to have

[161-011]and wield as my husband, than any king or prince that is christened; and if I

[161-012]may not have him I promise you I will never have none. For, my lord Arthur,

[161-013]said Dame Lionesse, wit ye well he is my first love, and he shall be the last;

[161-014]and if ye will suffer him to have his will and free choice I dare say he will

[161-015]have me. That is truth, said Sir Gareth; an I have not you and wield not you as

[161-016]my wife, there shall never lady nor gentlewoman rejoice me. What, nephew, said

[161-017]the king, is the wind in that door? for wit ye well I would not for the stint

[161-018]of my crown to be causer to withdraw your hearts; and wit ye well ye cannot

[161-019]love so well but I shall rather increase it than distress it. And also ye shall

[161-020]have my love and my lordship in the uttermost wise that may lie in my power.

[161-021]And in the same wise said Sir Gareth’s mother.

[161-022]Then there was made a provision for the day of marriage; and by the

[161-023]king’s advice it was provided that it should be at Michaelmas following,

[161-024]at Kink Kenadon by the seaside, for there is a plentiful country. And so it was

[161-025]cried in all the places through the realm. And then Sir Gareth sent his summons

[161-026]to all these knights and ladies that he had won in battle to-fore, that they

[161-027]should be at his day of marriage at Kink Kenadon by the sands.

[161-028]And then Dame Lionesse, and the damosel Linet with Sir Gringamore, rode to

[161-029]their castle; and a goodly and a rich ring she gave to Sir Gareth, and he gave

[161-030]her another. And King Arthur gave her a rich pair of bee of gold; and so she

[161-031]departed.

[161-032]And King Arthur and his fellowship rode toward Kink Kenadon, and Sir Gareth

[161-033]brought his lady on the way, and so came to the king again and rode with him.

[161-034]Lord! the great cheer that [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot]] made of Sir Gareth and he of him, for

[161-035]there was never no knight that Sir Gareth loved so well as he did Sir

[161-036]Launcelot; and ever for the most part he would be in Sir Launcelot’s

[161-037][[fellowship|company]]; for after Sir Gareth had espied Sir Gawaine’s conditions, he

[161-038]withdrew himself from his brother, Sir Gawaine’s, fellowship, for he was

[161-039]vengeable, and where he hated he would be avenged with murder, and that hated

[161-040]Sir Gareth.
