[107-001][[king-arthur|Arthur]] with his prisoners, and how the King won a city, and how he was crowned

[107-002]Emperor.

[107-003]Now thanked be God, said the noble King Arthur. But what manner man is he that

[107-004]standeth by himself, him seemeth no prisoner. Sir, said [[sir-gawaine|Gawaine]], this is a good

[107-005]man of arms, he hath matched me, but he is yielden unto God, and to me, for to

[107-006]become Christian; had not he have been we should never have returned, wherefore

[107-007]I pray you that he may be baptised, for there liveth not a nobler man nor

[107-008]better knight of his hands. Then the king let him anon be christened, and did

[107-009]do call him his first name Priamus, and made him a duke and knight of the Table

[107-010]Round. And then anon the king let do cry assault to the city, and there was

[107-011]rearing of ladders, breaking of walls, and the ditch filled, that men with

[107-012]little pain might enter into the city. Then came out a duchess, and Clarisin

[107-013]the countess, with many ladies and damosels, and kneeling before King Arthur,

[107-014]required him for the love of God to receive the city, and not to take it by

[107-015]assault, for then should many guiltless be slain. Then the king avaled his

[107-016]visor with a meek and noble countenance, and said, Madam, there shall none of

[107-017]my subjects misdo you nor your maidens, nor to none that to you belong, but the

[107-018]duke shall abide my judgment. Then anon the king commanded to leave the

[107-019]assault, and anon the duke’s oldest son brought out the keys, and

[107-020]kneeling delivered them to the king, and besought him of grace; and the king

[107-021]seized the town by assent of his lords, and took the duke and sent him to

[107-022]Dover, there for to abide prisoner term of his life, and assigned certain rents

[107-023]for the dower of the duchess and for her children.

[107-024]Then he made lords to rule those lands, and laws as a lord ought to do in his

[107-025]own country; and after he took his journey toward Rome, and sent Sir Floris and

[107-026]Sir Floridas to-fore, with five hundred men of arms, and they came to the city

[107-027]of Urbino and laid there a bushment, thereas them seemed most best for them,

[107-028]and rode to-fore the town, where anon issued out much people and skirmished

[107-029]with the fore-riders. Then brake out the bushment and won the bridge, and after

[107-030]the town, and set upon the walls the king’s banner. Then came the king

[107-031]upon an hill, and saw the city and his banner on the walls, by which he knew

[107-032]that the city was won. And anon he sent and commanded that none of his liege

[107-033]men should defoul nor lie by no lady, wife nor maid; and when he came into the

[107-034]city, he passed to the castle, and comforted them that were in sorrow, and

[107-035]ordained there a captain, a knight of his own country.

[107-036]And when they of Milan heard that thilk city was won, they sent to King Arthur

[107-037]great sums of money, and besought him as their lord to have [[mercy|pity]] on them,

[107-038]promising to be his subjects for ever, and yield to him homage and fealty for

[107-039]the lands of Pleasance and Pavia, Petersaint, and the Port of Tremble, and to

[107-040]give him yearly a million of gold all his lifetime. Then he rideth into

[107-041]Tuscany, and winneth towns and castles, and wasted all in his way that to him

[107-042]will not obey, and so to Spolute and Viterbe, and from thence he rode into the

[107-043]Vale of Vicecount among the vines. And from thence he sent to the senators, to

[107-044]wit whether they would know him for their lord. But soon after on a Saturday

[107-045]came unto King Arthur all the senators that were left alive, and the noblest

[107-046]cardinals that then dwelt in Rome, and prayed him of peace, and proferred him

[107-047]full large, and besought him as governor to give licence for six weeks for to

[107-048]assemble all the Romans, and then to crown him emperor with chrism as it

[107-049]belongeth to so high estate. I assent, said the king, like as ye have devised,

[107-050]and at Christmas there to be crowned, and to hold my [[round-table|Round Table]] with my

[107-051]knights as me liketh. And then the senators made ready for his enthronization.

[107-052]And at the day appointed, as the romance telleth, he came into Rome, and was

[107-053]crowned emperor by the pope’s hand, with all the royalty that could be

[107-054]made, and sojourned there a time, and established all his lands from Rome into

[107-055]France, and gave lands and realms unto his servants and knights, to everych

[107-056]after his desert, in such wise that none complained, rich nor poor. And he gave

[107-057]to Sir Priamus the duchy of Lorraine; and he thanked him, and said he would

[107-058]serve him the days of his life; and after made dukes and earls, and made every

[107-059]man rich.

[107-060]Then after this all his knights and lords assembled them afore him, and said:

[107-061]Blessed be God, your [[battle|war]] is finished and your conquest achieved, in so much

[107-062]that we know none so great nor mighty that dare make war against you: wherefore

[107-063]we beseech you to return homeward, and give us licence to go home to our wives,

[107-064]from whom we have been long, and to rest us, for your journey is finished with

[107-065]honour and [[honour|worship]]. Then said the king, Ye say truth, and for to tempt God it

[107-066]is no wisdom, and therefore make you ready and return we into England. Then

[107-067]there was trussing of harness and baggage and great carriage. And after licence

[107-068]given, he returned and commanded that no man in pain of death should not rob

[107-069]nor take victual, nor other thing by the way but that he should pay therefore.

[107-070]And thus he came over the sea and landed at Sandwich, against whom Queen

[107-071][[queen-guenever|Guenever]] his wife came and met him, and he was nobly received of all his

[107-072]commons in every city and burgh, and great gifts presented to him at his

[107-073]home-coming to welcome him with.
