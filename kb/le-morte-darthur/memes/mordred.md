# Mordred

**Mordred** (or Modred) is [[king-arthur|King Arthur]]'s illegitimate son and the agent of his destruction in Thomas Malory's *Le Morte d'[[king-arthur|Arthur]]*. [[prophecy|Merlin's prophecy]] of a son who would destroy [[king-arthur|Arthur]] finds its fulfillment in Mordred, whose usurpation of the throne and attempt to marry [[queen-guenever|Queen Guenever]] trigger the catastrophic final battle.

## The Prophesied Destroyer

Mordred's role is foretold from early in the narrative. [[merlin|Merlin]] [[battle|war]]ns [[king-arthur|Arthur]] that his son by his half-sister Morgause will bring about the [[realm|kingdom]]'s downfall — a prophecy [[king-arthur|Arthur]] attempts to th[[battle|war]]t by ordering the destruction of all infants born at the prophesied time. Mordred survives, however, and the attempt to evade fate only ensures its fulfillment.

## Usurpation and Betrayal

While [[king-arthur|Arthur]] campaigns in France against [[sir-launcelot|Launcelot]], Mordred seizes the throne and attempts to marry [[queen-guenever|Guenever]]. This act of usurpation — the son claiming the father's crown and queen — represents the ultimate violation of both feudal and familial bonds. [[king-arthur|Arthur]] must abandon his vendetta against [[sir-launcelot|Launcelot]] to confront the greater threat.

## Salisbury

The final battle between [[king-arthur|Arthur]] and Mordred at Salisbury is the apocalyptic conclusion of the Morte. Father and son meet in single [[battle|combat]]: [[king-arthur|Arthur]] kills Mordred but receives his death-wound in the act. The prophecy is fulfilled, the [[round-table|Round Table]] is destroyed, and the [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian age ends. Mordred is Malory's instrument of tragic necessity — the figure through whom fate exacts its price for the glories of [[camelot|Camelot]].
