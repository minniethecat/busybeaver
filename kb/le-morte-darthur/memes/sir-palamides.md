# Sir Palamides

Sir Palamides, known as Palamides the Saracen, was a noble knight of the [[Round Table]] whose story in [[Le Morte d'Arthur]] is defined by his unrequited love for [[La Beale Isoud]] and his enduring rivalry with [[Sir Tristram]]. A skilled tournament fighter, he earned renown as the Knight of the Black Shield and was counted among the worthiest knights of [[King Arthur]]'s court despite his Saracen origins.

## The Rivalry with Sir Tristram

Palamides's narrative is inseparable from his long rivalry with Sir Tristram, both as competitors in arms and as rivals for the love of La Beale Isoud. Palamides followed the [[Questing Beast]] after Sir Tristram's death, marking him as the inheritor of one of Arthurian legend's most enigmatic pursuits (022-040).

At the great tournament, Sir Palamides appeared bearing a black shield and proved himself the dominant knight of the day: "and many called him the Knight of the Black Shield. So that day Sir Palamides had great worship" (172-043). His prowess was such that many knights feared him, and he was widely expected to win the prize (172-027).

## Love for La Beale Isoud

Palamides's devotion to La Beale Isoud drove much of his action in the tournament fields of [[Cornwall]] and [[Ireland]]. Every day he drew near to the queen, and those who knew him recognised him as a noble knight worthy of her attention (172-009). Yet his love remained unrequited, for La Beale Isoud's heart belonged to Sir Tristram. When she told Tristram of Palamides's identity, it further complicated the tensions between the two knights (172-013).

After suffering in his unrequited passion, Palamides was eventually made to put aside his pursuit and was sent to Ireland, where he was made to bear no more harness for love of La Beale Isoud (173-001).

## Family and Brotherhood

Palamides was one of three brothers who served as knights. His brethren included Sir Safere, who followed Palamides into many adventures (140-029), and Sir Segwarides (154-055). Together they constituted a small but notable family presence among the knights of the Round Table, distinguished by their Saracen identity in a predominantly Christian Arthurian world.

## The Tournament Knight

Palamides excelled in the tournament circuit, often appearing incognito to test his skill. At one notable gathering, Sir Palamides came riding out of a castle to challenge the assembled knights (154-051), and he engaged in a strong battle against [[Sir Bleoberis]] (156-004). His combat drew the attention of many spectators, and he was known to strike down foes with formidable efficiency (172-041). On the morning after a great tournament, he made himself ready to continue the contest (173-009), demonstrating the tireless martial spirit that defined his character.

## The Saracen Knight

Palamides stands as one of the most prominent non-Christian knights in the Arthurian corpus. His epithet "the Saracen" identifies his origins, and yet he was fully integrated into the chivalric world of tournaments, quests, and courtly love. His narrative demonstrates that the code of [[knighthood]] in Malory's world transcended religious boundaries, valuing prowess and noble conduct above origin or creed.
