# Sir Percivale
**Sir Percivale** of Wales is one of the three knights who achieve the Holy Grail in Thomas Malory's *Le Morte d'[[king-arthur|Arthur]]*, alongside Sir Galahad and [[sir-bors|Sir Bors]]. Raised in isolation by his mother, Percivale's simplicity and innocence distinguish him from the courtly knights of [[camelot|Camelot]] and prove essential to his spiritual success.

## The Innocent Knight
Percivale's backstory — raised in the Welsh forest by a mother who sought to protect him from the chivalric life that killed his father and brothers — makes him an outsider to [[camelot|Arthur's court]]. His naivety is initially a source of comedy but becomes the foundation of his spiritual achievement: Percivale approaches the Grail without the worldly entanglements that hinder knights like [[sir-launcelot|Launcelot]].

## Grail Achievement
Percivale is one of the three knights who achieve the full Grail [[dream|vision]] at Corbenic. His purity — not his [[courage|prowess]] — is the key to his success, and his story represents Malory's most developed meditation on the tension between chivalric and spiritual values.
