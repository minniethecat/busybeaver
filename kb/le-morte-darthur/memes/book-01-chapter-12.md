[015-002]And so within a little space the three kings came unto the castle of

[015-003]Bedegraine, and found there a passing fair fellowship, and well beseen, whereof

[015-004]they had great joy, and victual they wanted none. This was the cause of the

[015-005]northern host: that they were reared for the despite and rebuke the six kings

[015-006]had at [[camelot|Carlion]]. And those six kings by their means, gat unto them five other

[015-007]kings; and thus they began to gather their people.

[015-008]And now they sware that for weal nor woe, they should not leave other, till

[015-009]they had destroyed Arthur. And then they made an oath. The first that began the

[015-010]oath was the Duke of Cambenet, that he would bring with him five thousand men

[015-011]of arms, the which were ready on horseback. Then sware King Brandegoris of

[015-012]Stranggore that he would bring five thousand men of arms on horseback. Then

[015-013]sware King Clariance of Northumberland he would bring three thousand men of

[015-014]arms. Then sware the King of the Hundred Knights, that was a passing good man

[015-015]and a young, that he would bring four thousand men of arms on horseback. Then

[015-016]there swore King Lot, a passing good knight, and [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawain]]’s father,

[015-017]that he would bring five thousand men of arms on horseback. Also there swore

[015-018]King Urience, that was Sir Uwain’s father, of the land of Gore, and he

[015-019]would bring six thousand men of arms on horseback. Also there swore King Idres

[015-020]of Cornwall, that he would bring five thousand men of arms on horseback. Also

[015-021]there swore King Cradelmas to bring five thousand men on horseback. Also there

[015-022]swore King Agwisance of Ireland to bring five thousand men of arms on

[015-023]horseback. Also there swore King Nentres to bring five thousand men of arms on

[015-024]horseback. Also there swore King Carados to bring five thousand men of arms on

[015-025]horseback. So their whole host was of clean men of arms on horseback fifty

[015-026]thousand, and a-foot ten thousand of good men’s bodies. Then were they

[015-027]soon ready, and mounted upon horse and sent forth their fore-riders, for these

[015-028]eleven kings in their ways laid a siege unto the castle of Bedegraine; and so

[015-029]they departed and drew toward Arthur, and left few to abide at the siege, for

[015-030]the castle of Bedegraine was holden of King Arthur, and the men that were

[015-031]therein were Arthur’s.
