# King Bors

King Bors of Gaul — that is, France — was one of the two royal brothers from across the Channel who came to the aid of [[King Arthur]] in his earliest wars. Together with his brother [[King Ban]], Bors brought a host of knights to Britain and fought alongside Arthur against [[King Lot]] and the other rebellious kings. His loyalty helped secure Arthur's throne in the critical first years of the reign.

## King of Gaul

King Bors ruled over a domain in Gaul, with his seat at Benwick. He and his brother [[King Ban]] were among the most powerful sovereigns on the Continent, commanding thousands of knights and enjoying the counsel of [[Merlin]] himself. When Arthur sent for their aid against the eleven kings, Bors and Ban answered the call without hesitation, crossing the sea with their armies to stand beside the young King of Britain (013-001) (013-020).

## The Alliance with King Arthur

The bond between Arthur and the two brothers was forged in counsel and sealed in blood. At a great gathering, [[King Arthur]], [[King Ban]], and King Bors took stock of their forces — twenty thousand men-at-arms — and planned their campaign against the rebellious kings of the north and west. Bors spoke at these councils with the authority of an experienced sovereign, and his presence lent weight to Arthur's cause (017-002) (017-008).

## The Battle Against the Rebellious Kings

When the armies met, King Bors fought in the forefront. At the great battle in the forest outside [[Bedegraine]], Bors and Ban led a flanking ambush that broke the rebel host. [[King Lot]] of Orkney, seeing the banners of Bors of Gaul among the enemy, marvelled that such distant kings should come to fight for Arthur. Bors himself engaged in single combat with King Carados, and his godson [[Sir Bleoberis]] carried his chief standard into the fray (018-008) (018-047) (018-049) (018-053).

Bors fought with distinction throughout the battle. He rallied his knights when the lines wavered, and his personal courage was noted by all who witnessed it. After the victory, Arthur honoured both Bors and Ban with great gifts, and [[Merlin]] prophesied that their line would produce knights of surpassing virtue (019-004) (019-043) (019-051).

## Family and Legacy

King Bors was the forebear of [[Sir Bors de Ganis]], one of the greatest knights of the Round Table and a key figure in the [[Sangreal]] quest. The younger Bors inherited his elder's loyalty, courage, and piety. The house of Bors thus produced one of the three knights who achieved the Holy Grail, fulfilling Merlin's prophecy that greatness would spring from this line (014-031) (014-033).

The castle of Benwick and the kingdom of Gaul remained allied to Arthur throughout his reign, a testament to the bond that Bors and Ban had established in the bloody fields of the early wars.
