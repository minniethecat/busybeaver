# Morgan le Fay
**Morgan le Fay** is [[king-arthur|King Arthur]]'s half-sister in Thomas Malory's *Le Morte d'[[king-arthur|Arthur]]*, a sorceress whose [[enchantment|magic]]al powers and shifting [[fealty|allegiance]]s make her one of the most ambiguous figures in the [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian cycle.

## Sorceress and Sister
Morgan's relationship to [[king-arthur|Arthur]] is the foundation of her ambiguous role. As his half-sister, she is bound to him by blood; as a practitioner of [[enchantment|magic]], she operates outside the chivalric norms that govern [[camelot|Arthur's court]]. Her enchantments range from hostile (imprisoning knights, attempting to destroy [[king-arthur|Arthur]]) to protective (conveying the wounded [[king-arthur|Arthur]] to [[avalon|Avalon]] in some [[custom|tradition]]s).

## The Ambiguity of Magic
Morgan embodies the ambivalent status of [[enchantment|magic]] in Malory's world. She is neither purely evil (like some [[giant|giants]] and sorcerers [[king-arthur|Arthur]] battles) nor purely beneficent (like the [[lady-of-the-lake|Lady of the Lake]] in some aspects). Her [[enchantment|magic]] is powerful but morally unaligned — a force that can threaten or aid depending on circumstance.
