[205-003]Then that same day there came a damosel into the court, and she brought with

[205-004]her a great black shield, with a white hand in the midst holding a sword. Other

[205-005]picture was there none in that shield. When [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] saw her he asked her

[205-006]from whence she came and what she would. Sir, she said, I have ridden long and

[205-007]many a day with this shield many ways, and for this cause I am come to your

[205-008]court: there was a good knight that ought this shield, and this knight had

[205-009]undertaken a great deed of arms to enchieve it; and so it misfortuned him

[205-010]another strong knight met with him by sudden adventure, and there they fought

[205-011]long, and either wounded other passing sore; and they were so weary that they

[205-012]left that battle even hand. So this knight that ought this shield saw none

[205-013]other way but he must die; and then he commanded me to bear this shield to the

[205-014]court of King Arthur, he requiring and praying some good knight to take this

[205-015]shield, and that he would fulfil the quest that he was in. Now what say ye to

[205-016]this quest? said King Arthur; is there any of you here that will take upon him

[205-017]to wield this shield? Then was there not one that would speak one word. Then

[205-018][[sir-kay|Sir Kay]] took the shield in his hands. Sir knight, said the damosel, what is

[205-019]your name? Wit ye well, said he, my name is Sir Kay, the Seneschal, that

[205-020]wide-where is known. Sir, said that damosel, lay down that shield, for wit ye

[205-021]well it falleth not for you, for he must be a better knight than ye that shall

[205-022]wield this shield. Damosel, said Sir Kay, wit ye well I took this shield in my

[205-023]hands by your leave for to behold it, not to that intent; but go wheresomever

[205-024]thou wilt, for I will not go with you.

[205-025]Then the damosel stood still a great while and beheld many of those knights.

[205-026]Then spake the knight, La Cote Male Taile: Fair damosel, I will take the shield

[205-027]and that adventure upon me, so I wist I should know whitherward my journey

[205-028]might be; for because I was this day made knight I would take this adventure

[205-029]upon me. What is your name, fair young man? said the damosel. My name is, said

[205-030]he, La Cote Male Taile. Well mayest thou be called so, said the damosel, the

[205-031]knight with the evil-shapen coat; but an thou be so hardy to take upon thee to

[205-032]bear that shield and to follow me, wit thou well thy skin shall be as well hewn

[205-033]as thy coat. As for that, said La Cote Male Taile, when I am so hewn I will ask

[205-034]you no salve to heal me withal. And forthwithal there came into the court two

[205-035]squires and brought him great horses, and his armour, and his spears, and anon

[205-036]he was armed and took his leave. I would not by my will, said the king, that ye

[205-037]took upon you that hard adventure. Sir, said he, this adventure is mine, and

[205-038]the first that ever I took upon me, and that will I follow whatsomever come of

[205-039]me. Then that damosel departed, and La Cote Male Taile fast followed after. And

[205-040]within a while he overtook the damosel, and anon she missaid him in the foulest

[205-041]manner.
