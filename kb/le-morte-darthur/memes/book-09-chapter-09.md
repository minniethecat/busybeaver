[212-001]Taile lord of the Castle of Pendragon, and after was made knight of the Round

[212-002]Table.

[212-003]And in the meanwhile came [[sir-kay|Sir Kay]], the Seneschal, and Sir Brandiles, and anon

[212-004]they fellowshipped with them. And then within ten days, then departed those

[212-005]knights of [[king-arthur|Arthur]]’s court from these fortresses. And as [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot]]

[212-006]came by the Castle of Pendragon there he put Sir Brian de les Isles from his

[212-007]lands, for cause he would never be withhold with King Arthur; and all that

[212-008]Castle of Pendragon and all the lands thereof he gave to Sir La Cote Male

[212-009]Taile. And then Sir Launcelot sent for Nerovens that he made once knight, and

[212-010]he made him to have all the rule of that castle and of that country, under La

[212-011]Cote Male Taile; and so they rode to Arthur’s court all wholly together.

[212-012]And at Pentecost next following there was Sir Plenorius and Sir La Cote Male

[212-013]Taile, called otherwise by right Sir Breunor le Noire, both made Knights of the

[212-014][[round-table|Table Round]]; and great lands King Arthur gave them, and there Breunor le Noire

[212-015]wedded that damosel Maledisant. And after she was called Beauvivante, but ever

[212-016]after for the more part he was called La Cote Male Taile; and he proved a

[212-017]passing noble knight, and mighty; and many worshipful deeds he did after in his

[212-018]life; and Sir Plenorius proved a noble knight and full of [[courage|prowess]], and all the

[212-019]days of their life for the most part they awaited upon Sir Launcelot; and Sir

[212-020]Plenorius’ brethren were ever knights of King Arthur. And also, as the

[212-021]French book maketh mention, Sir La Cote Male Taile avenged his father’s

[212-022]death.
