# Battle of Camlann
The **Battle of Camlann** (also called the Battle of Salisbury) is the culminating event of Thomas Malory's *Le Morte d'[[king-arthur|Arthur]]*. It is the battle[[battle|field]] where father and son destroy each other, where [[round-table|the Round Table fellowship]] perishes, and where the [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian age comes to its prophesied end.

## The Coming of Battle
[[prophecy|Merlin's prophecy]] foretold "a great battle beside Salisbury, and [[mordred|Mordred]] his own son should be against him." When [[mordred|Mordred]] seizes the throne during [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s French campaign, the prophecy accelerates to[[battle|war]]d fulfillment. [[king-arthur|Arthur]] returns to Britain and the armies meet at Salisbury.

## The Final Combat
In the battle's climax, [[king-arthur|Arthur]] and [[mordred|Mordred]] meet in single [[battle|combat]]. [[king-arthur|Arthur]] runs his spear through [[mordred|Mordred]], and [[mordred|Mordred]] — dying — strikes [[king-arthur|Arthur]] a mortal blow to the head. The [[kinsman|kin]]g who drew the sword from the stone falls on the [[battle|field]] of his last battle, surrounded by the dead of his fellowship.

## Aftermath
[[king-arthur|Arthur]] is borne from the [[battle|field]] to [[avalon|Avalon]] by the [[lady|ladies]] who attend him. The [[round-table|Round Table]] is destroyed, the surviving knights scatter to hermits and monasteries, and the chivalric world that [[king-arthur|Arthur]] built vanishes into legend.
