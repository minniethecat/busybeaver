[104-002]Now turn we unto [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] and his noble knights, which, after the great

[104-003]battle achieved against the Romans, entered into Lorraine, Brabant and

[104-004]Flanders, and sithen returned into Haut Almaine, and so over the mountains into

[104-005]Lombardy, and after, into Tuscany wherein was a city which in no wise would

[104-006]yield themself nor obey, wherefore King Arthur besieged it, and lay long about

[104-007]it, and gave many assaults to the city; and they within defended them

[104-008]valiantly. Then, on a time, the king called Sir Florence, a knight, and said to

[104-009]him they lacked victual, And not far from hence be great forests and woods,

[104-010]wherein be many of mine enemies with much bestial: I will that thou make thee

[104-011]ready and go thither in foraying, and take with thee [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]] my nephew, Sir

[104-012]Wisshard, Sir Clegis, Sir Cleremond, and the Captain of Cardiff with other, and

[104-013]bring with you all the beasts that ye there can get.

[104-014]And anon these knights made them ready, and rode over holts and hills, through

[104-015]forests and woods, till they came into a fair meadow full of fair flowers and

[104-016]grass; and there they rested them and their horses all that night. And in the

[104-017]springing of the day in the next morn, Sir Gawaine took his horse and stole

[104-018]away from his fellowship, to seek some adventures. And anon he was ware of a

[104-019]man armed, walking his horse easily by a wood’s side, and his shield

[104-020]laced to his shoulder, sitting on a strong courser, without any man saving a

[104-021]page bearing a mighty spear. The knight bare in his shield three griffins of

[104-022]gold, in sable carbuncle, the chief of silver. When Sir Gawaine espied this gay

[104-023]knight, he feutred his spear, and rode straight to him, and demanded of him

[104-024]from whence that he was. That other answered and said he was of Tuscany, and

[104-025]demanded of Sir Gawaine, What, profferest thou, proud knight, thee so boldly?

[104-026]here gettest thou no prey, thou mayest prove what thou wilt, for thou shalt be

[104-027]my prisoner or thou depart. Then said Gawaine, thou avauntest thee greatly and

[104-028]speakest proud words, I counsel thee for all thy boast that thou make thee

[104-029]ready, and take thy gear to thee, to-fore greater grame fall to thee.
