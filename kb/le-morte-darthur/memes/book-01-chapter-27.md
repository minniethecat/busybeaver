[030-001]that were born on May-day, and how [[mordred|Mordred]] was saved.

[030-002]Then [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] let send for all the children born on May-day, begotten of

[030-003]lords and born of ladies; for [[merlin|Merlin]] told King Arthur that he that should

[030-004]destroy him should be born on May-day, wherefore he sent for them all, upon

[030-005]pain of death; and so there were found many lords’ sons, and all were

[030-006]sent unto the king, and so was Mordred sent by King Lot’s wife, and all

[030-007]were put in a ship to the sea, and some were four weeks old, and some less. And

[030-008]so by fortune the ship drave unto a castle, and was all to-riven, and destroyed

[030-009]the most part, save that Mordred was cast up, and a good man found him, and

[030-010]nourished him till he was fourteen year old, and then he brought him to the

[030-011]court, as it rehearseth afterward, toward the end of the Death of Arthur. So

[030-012]many lords and barons of this realm were displeased, for their children were so

[030-013]lost, and many put the wite on Merlin more than on Arthur; so what for dread

[030-014]and for love, they held their peace. But when the messenger came to King

[030-015]Rience, then was he wood out of measure, and purveyed him for a great host, as

[030-016]it rehearseth after in the book of Balin le Savage, that followeth next after,

[030-017]how by adventure Balin gat the sword.
