[077-001][[morgan-le-fay|Morgan le Fay]], [[king-arthur|King Arthur]]’s sister, and how she would have done slay

[077-002]him.

[077-003]Then Sir Accolon bethought him, and said, Woe worth this sword, for by it have

[077-004]I got my death. It may well be, said the king. Now, sir, said Accolon, I will

[077-005]tell you; this sword hath been in my keeping the most part of this twelvemonth;

[077-006]and Morgan le Fay, King Uriens’ wife, sent it me yesterday by a dwarf, to

[077-007]this intent, that I should slay King Arthur, her brother. For ye shall

[077-008]understand King Arthur is the man in the world that she most hateth, because he

[077-009]is most of [[honour|worship]] and of [[courage|prowess]] of any of her blood; also she loveth me out

[077-010]of measure as paramour, and I her again; and if she might bring about to slay

[077-011]Arthur by her crafts, she would slay her husband King Uriens lightly, and then

[077-012]had she me devised to be king in this land, and so to reign, and she to be my

[077-013]queen; but that is now done, said Accolon, for I am sure of my death. Well,

[077-014]said Sir Arthur, I feel by you ye would have been king in this land. It had

[077-015]been great damage to have destroyed your lord, said Arthur. It is truth, said

[077-016]Accolon, but now I have told you truth, wherefore I pray you tell me of whence

[077-017]ye are, and of what court? O Accolon, said King Arthur, now I let thee wit that

[077-018]I am King Arthur, to whom thou hast done great damage. When Accolon heard that

[077-019]he cried aloud, Fair, sweet lord, have mercy on me, for I knew not you. O Sir

[077-020]Accolon, said King Arthur, mercy shalt thou have, because I feel by thy words

[077-021]at this time thou knewest not my person; but I understand well by thy words

[077-022]that thou hast agreed to the death of my person, and therefore thou art a

[077-023]traitor; but I wite thee the less, for my sister Morgan le Fay by her false

[077-024]crafts made thee to agree and consent to her false lusts, but I shall be sore

[077-025]avenged upon her an I live, that all Christendom shall speak of it; God knoweth

[077-026]I have honoured her and worshipped her more than all my kin, and more have I

[077-027]trusted her than mine own wife and all my kin after.

[077-028]Then Sir Arthur called the keepers of the [[battle|field]], and said, Sirs, come hither,

[077-029]for here are we two knights that have fought unto a great damage unto us both,

[077-030]and like each one of us to have slain other, if it had happed so; and had any

[077-031]of us known other, here had been no battle, nor stroke stricken. Then all aloud

[077-032]cried Accolon unto all the knights and men that were then there gathered

[077-033]together, and said to them in this manner, O lords, this noble knight that I

[077-034]have fought withal, the which me sore repenteth, is the most man of prowess, of

[077-035]manhood, and of worship in the world, for it is himself King Arthur, our alther

[077-036]liege lord, and with mishap and with misadventure have I done this battle with

[077-037]the king and lord that I am holden withal.
