[090-001]damosel, and how he came to the Duke of the South Marches.

[090-002]Now turn we unto Sir Marhaus, that rode with the damosel of thirty winter of

[090-003]age, southward. And so they came into a deep forest, and by fortune they were

[090-004]nighted, and rode long in a deep way, and at the last they came unto a

[090-005]courtelage, and there they asked harbour. But the man of the courtelage would

[090-006]not lodge them for no treatise that they could treat, but thus much the good

[090-007]man said, An ye will take the adventure of your lodging, I shall bring you

[090-008]where ye shall be lodged. What adventure is that that I shall have for my

[090-009]lodging? said Sir Marhaus. Ye shall wit when ye come there, said the good man.

[090-010]Sir, what adventure so it be, bring me thither I pray thee, said Sir Marhaus;

[090-011]for I am weary, my damosel, and my horse. So the good man went and opened the

[090-012]gate, and within an hour he brought him unto a fair castle, and then the poor

[090-013]man called the porter, and anon he was let into the castle, and so he told the

[090-014]lord how he brought him a knight errant and a damosel that would be lodged with

[090-015]him. Let him in, said the lord, it may happen he shall repent that they took

[090-016]their lodging here.

[090-017]So Sir Marhaus was let in with torchlight, and there was a goodly sight of

[090-018]young men that welcomed him. And then his horse was led into the stable, and he

[090-019]and the damosel were brought into the hall, and there stood a mighty duke and

[090-020]many goodly men about him. Then this lord asked him what he hight, and from

[090-021]whence he came, and with whom he dwelt. Sir, he said, I am a knight of King

[090-022][[king-arthur|Arthur]]’s and knight of the [[round-table|Table Round]], and my name is Sir Marhaus, and

[090-023]born I am in Ireland. And then said the duke to him, That me sore repenteth:

[090-024]the cause is this, for I love not thy lord nor none of thy fellows of the Table

[090-025]Round; and therefore ease thyself this night as well as thou mayest, for as

[090-026]to-morn I and my six sons shall match with you. Is there no remedy but that I

[090-027]must have ado with you and your six sons at once? said Sir Marhaus. No, said

[090-028]the duke, for this cause I made mine avow, for [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]] slew my seven sons

[090-029]in a recounter, therefore I made mine avow, there should never knight of King

[090-030]Arthur’s court lodge with me, or come thereas I might have ado with him,

[090-031]but that I would have a revenging of my sons’ death. What is your name?

[090-032]said Sir Marhaus; I require you tell me, an it please you. Wit thou well I am

[090-033]the Duke of South Marches. Ah, said Sir Marhaus, I have heard say that ye have

[090-034]been long time a great foe unto my lord Arthur and to his knights. That shall

[090-035]ye feel to-morn, said the duke. Shall I have ado with you? said Sir Marhaus.

[090-036]Yea, said the duke, thereof shalt thou not choose, and therefore take you to

[090-037]your chamber, and ye shall have all that to you longeth. So Sir Marhaus

[090-038]departed and was led to a chamber, and his damosel was led unto her chamber.

[090-039]And on the morn the duke sent unto Sir Marhaus and bade make him ready. And so

[090-040]Sir Marhaus arose and armed him, and then there was a mass sung afore him, and

[090-041]brake his fast, and so mounted on horseback in the court of the castle where

[090-042]they should do the battle. So there was the duke all ready on horseback, clean

[090-043]armed, and his six sons by him, and everych had a spear in his hand, and so

[090-044]they encountered, whereas the duke and his two sons brake their spears upon

[090-045]him, but Sir Marhaus held up his spear and touched none of them.
