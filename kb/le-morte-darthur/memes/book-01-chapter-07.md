[010-001]he made officers.

[010-002]And at the feast of Pentecost all manner of men assayed to pull at the sword

[010-003]that would assay; but none might prevail but [[king-arthur|Arthur]], and pulled it out afore

[010-004]all the lords and commons that were there, wherefore all the commons cried at

[010-005]once, We will have Arthur unto our king, we will put him no more in delay, for

[010-006]we all see that it is God’s will that he shall be our king, and who that

[010-007]holdeth against it, we will slay him. And therewithal they kneeled at once,

[010-008]both rich and poor, and cried Arthur mercy because they had delayed him so

[010-009]long, and Arthur forgave them, and took the sword between both his hands, and

[010-010]offered it upon the altar where the Archbishop was, and so was he made knight

[010-011]of the best man that was there. And so anon was the coronation made. And there

[010-012]was he sworn unto his lords and the commons for to be a true king, to stand

[010-013]with true justice from thenceforth the days of this life. Also then he made all

[010-014]lords that held of the crown to come in, and to do service as they ought to do.

[010-015]And many complaints were made unto Sir Arthur of great wrongs that were done

[010-016]since the death of King Uther, of many lands that were bereaved lords, knights,

[010-017]ladies, and gentlemen. Wherefore King Arthur made the lands to be given again

[010-018]unto them that owned them.

[010-019]When this was done, that the king had stablished all the countries about

[010-020]London, then he let make [[sir-kay|Sir Kay]] seneschal of England; and Sir Baudwin of

[010-021]Britain was made constable; and Sir Ulfius was made chamberlain; and Sir

[010-022]Brastias was made warden to wait upon the north from Trent forwards, for it was

[010-023]that time the most party the king’s enemies. But within few years after

[010-024]Arthur won all the north, Scotland, and all that were under their obeissance.

[010-025]Also Wales, a part of it, held against Arthur, but he overcame them all, as he

[010-026]did the remnant, through the noble [[courage|prowess]] of himself and his knights of the

[010-027][[round-table|Round Table]].
