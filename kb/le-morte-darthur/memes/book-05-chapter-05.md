[100-001]a [[marvel|marvellous]] giant, and how he fought and conquered him.

[100-002]Then came to him an husbandman of the country, and told him how there was in

[100-003]the country of Constantine beside Brittany, a great giant which had slain,

[100-004]murdered and devoured much people of the country, and had been sustained seven

[100-005]year with the children of the commons of that land, insomuch that all the

[100-006]children be all slain and destroyed; and now late he hath taken the Duchess of

[100-007]Brittany as she rode with her meiny, and hath led her to his lodging which is

[100-008]in a mountain, for to ravish and lie by her to her life’s end, and many

[100-009]people followed her, more than five hundred, but all they might not rescue her,

[100-010]but they left her shrieking and crying lamentably, wherefore I suppose that he

[100-011]hath slain her in fulfilling his foul lust of lechery. She was wife unto thy

[100-012]cousin Sir Howell, whom we call full nigh of thy blood. Now, as thou art a

[100-013]rightful king, have [[mercy|pity]] on this lady, and revenge us all as thou art a noble

[100-014]conqueror. Alas, said [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], this is a great mischief, I had liefer than

[100-015]the best realm that I have that I had been a furlong way to-fore him for to

[100-016]have rescued that lady. Now, fellow, said King Arthur, canst thou bring me

[100-017]thereas this giant haunteth? Yea, Sir, said the good man, look yonder whereas

[100-018]thou seest those two great fires, there shalt thou find him, and more treasure

[100-019]than I suppose is in all France. When the king had understood this piteous

[100-020]case, he returned into his tent.

[100-021]Then he called to him [[sir-kay|Sir Kay]] and Sir Bedivere, and commanded them secretly to

[100-022]make ready horse and harness for himself and them twain; for after evensong he

[100-023]would ride on pilgrimage with them two only unto Saint Michael’s mount.

[100-024]And then anon he made him ready, and armed him at all points, and took his

[100-025]horse and his shield. And so they three departed thence and rode forth as fast

[100-026]as ever they might till that they came to the foreland of that mount. And there

[100-027]they alighted, and the king commanded them to tarry there, for he would himself

[100-028]go up into that mount. And so he ascended up into that hill till he came to a

[100-029]great fire, and there he found a careful widow wringing her hands and making

[100-030]great sorrow, sitting by a grave new made. And then King Arthur saluted her,

[100-031]and demanded of her wherefore she made such lamentation, to whom she answered

[100-032]and said, Sir knight, speak soft, for yonder is a devil, if he hear thee speak

[100-033]he will come and destroy thee; I hold thee unhappy; what dost thou here in this

[100-034]mountain? for if ye were such fifty as ye be, ye were not able to make

[100-035]resistance against this devil: here lieth a duchess dead, the which was the

[100-036]fairest of all the world, wife to Sir Howell, Duke of Brittany, he hath

[100-037]murdered her in forcing her, and hath slit her unto the navel.

[100-038]Dame, said the king, I come from the noble conqueror King Arthur, for to treat

[100-039]with that tyrant for his liege people. Fie on such treaties, said she, he

[100-040]setteth not by the king nor by no man else; but an if thou have brought

[100-041]Arthur’s wife, dame [[queen-guenever|Guenever]], he shall be gladder than thou hadst given

[100-042]to him half France. Beware, approach him not too nigh, for he hath vanquished

[100-043]fifteen kings, and hath made him a coat full of precious stones embroidered

[100-044]with their beards, which they sent him to have his love for salvation of their

[100-045]people at this last Christmas. And if thou wilt, speak with him at yonder great

[100-046]fire at supper. Well, said Arthur, I will accomplish my message for all your

[100-047]fearful words; and went forth by the crest of that hill, and saw where he sat

[100-048]at supper gnawing on a limb of a man, baking his broad limbs by the fire, and

[100-049]breechless, and three fair damosels turning three broaches whereon were

[100-050]broached twelve young children late born, like young birds.

[100-051]When King Arthur beheld that piteous sight he had great compassion on them, so

[100-052]that his heart bled for sorrow, and hailed him, saying in this wise: He that

[100-053]all the world wieldeth give thee short life and shameful death; and the devil

[100-054]have thy soul; why hast thou murdered these young innocent children, and

[100-055]murdered this duchess? Therefore, arise and dress thee, thou glutton, for this

[100-056]day shalt thou die of my hand. Then the glutton anon started up, and took a

[100-057]great club in his hand, and smote at the king that his coronal fell to the

[100-058]earth. And the king hit him again that he carved his belly and cut off his

[100-059]genitours, that his guts and his entrails fell down to the ground. Then the

[100-060]giant threw away his club, and caught the king in his arms that he crushed his

[100-061]ribs. Then the three maidens kneeled down and called to Christ for help and

[100-062]comfort of Arthur. And then Arthur weltered and wrung, that he was other while

[100-063]under and another time above. And so weltering and wallowing they rolled down

[100-064]the hill till they came to the sea mark, and ever as they so weltered Arthur

[100-065]smote him with his dagger.

[100-066]And it fortuned they came to the place whereas the two knights were and kept

[100-067]Arthur’s horse; then when they saw the king fast in the giant’s

[100-068]arms they came and loosed him. And then the king commanded Sir Kay to smite off

[100-069]the giant’s head, and to set it upon a truncheon of a spear, and bear it

[100-070]to Sir Howell, and tell him that his enemy was slain; and after let this head

[100-071]be bound to a barbican that all the people may see and behold it; and go ye two

[100-072]up to the mountain, and fetch me my shield, my sword, and the club of iron; and

[100-073]as for the treasure, take ye it, for ye shall find there goods out of number;

[100-074]so I have the kirtle and the club I desire no more. This was the fiercest giant

[100-075]that ever I met with, save one in the mount of Araby, which I overcame, but

[100-076]this was greater and fiercer. Then the knights fetched the club and the kirtle,

[100-077]and some of the treasure they took to themselves, and returned again to the

[100-078]host. And anon this was known through all the country, wherefore the people

[100-079]came and thanked the king. And he said again, Give the thanks to God, and

[100-080]depart the goods among you.

[100-081]And after that King Arthur said and commanded his cousin Howell, that he should

[100-082]ordain for a church to be builded on the same hill in the [[honour|worship]] of Saint

[100-083]Michael. And on the morn the king removed with his great battle, and came into

[100-084]Champayne and in a valley, and there they pight their tents; and the king being

[100-085]set at his dinner, there came in two messengers, of whom that one was Marshal

[100-086]of France, and said to the king that the emperor was entered into France, and

[100-087]had destroyed a great part, and was in Burgoyne, and had destroyed and made

[100-088]great slaughter of people, and burnt towns and boroughs; wherefore, if thou

[100-089]come not hastily, they must yield up their bodies and goods.
