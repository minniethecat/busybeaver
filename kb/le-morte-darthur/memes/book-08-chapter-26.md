[189-001]Breunor, and at the last smote off his head.

[189-002]Now take thine horse: sithen I am ladyless I will win thy lady an I may.

[189-003]Then they took their horses and came together as it had been the thunder; and

[189-004][[sir-tristram|Sir Tristram]] smote Sir Breunor clean from his horse, and lightly he rose up;

[189-005]and as Sir Tristram came again by him he thrust his horse throughout both the

[189-006]shoulders, that his horse hurled here and there and fell dead to the ground.

[189-007]And ever Sir Breunor ran after to have slain Sir Tristram, but Sir Tristram was

[189-008]light and nimble, and voided his horse lightly. And or ever Sir Tristram might

[189-009]dress his shield and his sword the other gave him three or four sad strokes.

[189-010]Then they rushed together like two boars, tracing and traversing mightily and

[189-011]wisely as two noble knights. For this Sir Breunor was a proved knight, and had

[189-012]been or then the death of many good knights, that it was [[mercy|pity]] that he had so

[189-013]long endured.

[189-014]Thus they fought, hurling here and there nigh two hours, and either were

[189-015]wounded sore. Then at the last Sir Breunor rashed upon Sir Tristram and took

[189-016]him in his arms, for he trusted much in his strength. Then was Sir Tristram

[189-017]called the strongest and the highest knight of the world; for he was called

[189-018]bigger than [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot]], but Sir Launcelot was better breathed. So anon Sir

[189-019]Tristram thrust Sir Breunor down grovelling, and then he unlaced his helm and

[189-020]struck off his head. And then all they that longed to the castle came to him,

[189-021]and did him homage and fealty, praying him that he would abide there still a

[189-022]little while to fordo that foul custom. Sir Tristram granted thereto. The

[189-023]meanwhile one of the knights of the castle rode unto Sir Galahad, the haut

[189-024]prince, the which was Sir Breunor’s son, which was a noble knight, and

[189-025]told him what misadventure his father had and his mother.
