[042-001]of the prophecy of [[merlin|Merlin]], and how Balin should give the dolorous stroke.

[042-002]So at the interment came King Lot’s wife Margawse with her four sons,

[042-003][[sir-gawaine|Gawaine]], Agravaine, Gaheris, and [[sir-gareth|Gareth]]. Also there came thither King Uriens,

[042-004]Sir Ewaine’s father, and [[morgan-le-fay|Morgan le Fay]] his wife that was King

[042-005][[king-arthur|Arthur]]’s sister. All these came to the interment. But of all these twelve

[042-006]kings King Arthur let make the tomb of King Lot passing richly, and made his

[042-007]tomb by his own; and then Arthur let make twelve images of latten and copper,

[042-008]and over-gilt it with gold, in the sign of twelve kings, and each one of them

[042-009]held a taper of wax that burnt day and night; and King Arthur was made in sign

[042-010]of a figure standing above them with a sword drawn in his hand, and all the

[042-011]twelve figures had countenance like unto men that were overcome. All this made

[042-012]Merlin by his subtle craft, and there he told the king, When I am dead these

[042-013]tapers shall burn no longer, and soon after the adventures of the Sangreal

[042-014]shall come among you and be achieved. Also he told Arthur how Balin the

[042-015]worshipful knight shall give the dolorous stroke, whereof shall fall great

[042-016]vengeance. Oh, where is Balin and Balan and Pellinore? said King Arthur. As for

[042-017]Pellinore, said Merlin, he will meet with you soon; and as for Balin he will

[042-018]not be long from you; but the other brother will depart, ye shall see him no

[042-019]more. By my faith, said Arthur, they are two [[marvel|marvellous]] knights, and namely

[042-020]Balin passeth of [[courage|prowess]] of any knight that ever I found, for much beholden am

[042-021]I unto him; would God he would abide with me. Sir, said Merlin, look ye keep

[042-022]well the scabbard of [[sword-in-the-stone|Excalibur]], for ye shall lose no blood while ye have the

[042-023]scabbard upon you, though ye have as many wounds upon you as ye may have. So

[042-024]after, for great trust, Arthur betook the scabbard to Morgan le Fay his sister,

[042-025]and she loved another knight better than her husband King Uriens or King

[042-026]Arthur, and she would have had Arthur her brother slain, and therefore she let

[042-027]make another scabbard like it by enchantment, and gave the scabbard Excalibur

[042-028]to her love; and the knight’s name was called Accolon, that after had

[042-029]near slain King Arthur. After this Merlin told unto King Arthur of the prophecy

[042-030]that there should be a great battle beside Salisbury, and [[mordred|Mordred]] his own son

[042-031]should be against him. Also he told him that Bagdemegus was his cousin, and

[042-032]germain unto King Uriens.
