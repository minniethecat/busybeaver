# Sangreal

The **Quest of the Sangreal** — the Holy Grail — is the culminating spiritual adventure of the [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian world. Unlike the martial quests that occupy most of Malory's narrative, the Sangreal quest demands purity rather than [[courage|prowess]]: only three knights of the [[Round Table]] prove worthy to achieve it. The quest transforms the fellowship, revealing the gap between earthly chivalry and [[grace|divine grace]], and its aftermath marks the beginning of the [[round-table|Round Table]]'s decline.

## The Coming of the Sangreal

The Sangreal enters the court at [[Camelot]] not as a prize to be won but as a [[dream|vision]] that overwhelms the senses. It appears during the [[Feast of Pentecost]], the holiest day of the [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian calendar, and its presence silences the assembled chivalry:

> Then after the adventures of the Sangreal, that Sir Launcelot begat Galahad upon Elaine, the daughter of King Pelles. (042-013)

The knights, stirred by the [[dream|vision]], swear an oath to seek the Sangreal for a year and a day. [[King Arthur]] watches them depart with foreboding, knowing that many will never return.

## The Nature of the Quest

The Sangreal is not a physical enemy to be defeated but a state of grace to be attained. Where other quests test the arm, this one tests the soul:

> Of the Sangreal, and no knight found such tokens as Sir Galahad, for he was a clean maiden in will and in deed. (071-025)

The quest systematically eliminates those who rely on strength alone. [[Sir Launcelot]], the greatest of earthly knights, glimpses the Sangreal but cannot approach it, his sin with [[Queen Guenever]] barring the way. The quest thus becomes a [[judgment|judgement]] upon the ent[[wrath|ire]] chivalric order.

## Those Who Achieved

Only three knights reach the full mystery of the Sangreal:

| Knight | Quality | Role in the [[quest|Quest]] |
|--------|---------|-------------------|
| [[Sir Galahad]] | Perfect purity | The chosen Grail knight, born for this purpose |
| [[Sir Percival]] | Innocence | The pure-hearted seeker |
| [[Sir Bors]] | Penitent chastity | The knight who confessed and was absolved |

> The four that achieved the Sangreal, and the Damosel of the Lake made by her crafts that they should be known from all other knights. (094-029)

These three — Galahad, Percival, and [[sir-bors|Bors]] — together with Percival's sister, who gives her life to complete the quest, represent the apex of spiritual knighthood. Galahad alone beholds the full mystery before being taken up to heaven.

## Consequences for the Round Table

The Sangreal quest, though spiritually triumphant, fractures the [[round-table|Round Table]]. The year-long absence of its greatest knights weakens [[camelot|Arthur's court]]; the revelation of [[Sir Launcelot]]'s un[[worship|worthiness]] plants the seed of his eventual downfall; and the death of Galahad — the one knight who might have held the fellowship together — removes the [[round-table|Round Table]]'s spiritual anchor.

> As it telleth after in the Sangreal, that Sir Launcelot began to be estranged from the queen, and to withdraw from her company, and to eschew the fellowship of ladies. (044-034)

Even this reform proves temporary. The t[[wrath|rage]]dy of the Sangreal is that it shows what chivalry might have been — and by that light, reveals what it actually is.
