# Lady of the Lake
The **Lady of the Lake** (also called Nimue or Vivien) is a [[marvel|supernatural]] enchantress in Thomas Malory's *Le Morte d'[[king-arthur|Arthur]]*. She is the source of [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s sword [[sword-in-the-stone|Excalibur]], the foster-mother who raises [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot]], and the figure who ultimately entraps and replaces [[merlin|Merlin]] — embodying the dual nature of [[enchantment|magic]] as both beneficent and d[[wrath|anger]]ous.

## Giver of Excalibur
The [[lady|Lady]] first appears as the source of [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s enchanted sword. The sword and its scabbard — the latter protecting its bearer from [[wound|wounds]] — are gifts from the [[lady|Lady]] to [[king-arthur|Arthur]], establishing her as a benefactor to the [[realm|kingdom]]. Yet gifts from fairies in [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian [[custom|tradition]] are rarely without cost.

## Foster-Mother of Launcelot
The [[lady|Lady]] raises the infant [[sir-launcelot|Launcelot]] in her underwater realm, training him in the skills and [[virtue|virtues]] that will make him the greatest knight of the [[round-table|Round Table]]. This nurturing role — presenting [[sir-launcelot|Launcelot]] to [[camelot|Arthur's court]] when he comes of age — makes the [[lady|Lady]] a foundational figure in the chivalric world.

## Entrapper of Merlin
The [[lady|Lady]]'s most consequential act is the entrapment of [[merlin|Merlin]]. Learning his secrets, she imprisons him in a cave or under a stone, removing the great enchanter from the narrative. This act represents the transfer of [[enchantment|magic]]al authority from the masculine [[merlin|Merlin]] to the feminine [[lady|Lady]] — a transfer that both empowers the [[lady|Lady]] and deprives [[king-arthur|Arthur]] of his [[marvel|supernatural]] counselor at a critical moment.
