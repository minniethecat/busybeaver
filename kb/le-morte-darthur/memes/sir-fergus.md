# Sir Fergus

Sir Fergus was a knight of Cornwall and a loyal companion to Sir Tristram de Liones. He served as Tristram's messenger to the court, defended him in judgment, and tended him during the period of his madness in the forest.

## Messenger of Sir Tristram

When Sir Tristram fell into deep sorrow and was almost out of his mind, he sent Sir Fergus to the court to learn what tidings there were (220-022). As Fergus rode, he met a damosel who had come from Sir Palomides seeking news of Tristram. Fergus told her of Tristram's condition, and when the damosel asked where she might find him, Fergus directed her to the place (220-026). Fergus then found Queen Isoud sick in her bed, making the greatest dole that ever any earthly woman made.

## Defender in Judgment

When King Mark, in his jealousy against Tristram, called his barons to judge the knight to death, many of the barons would not assent. Sir Fergus stood among those who defended Tristram, alongside Sir Dinas the Seneschal (224-010). By their counsel, Tristram was not condemned to death but banished out of Cornwall for ten years — a mercy compared to the king's original intent.

## Keeper of the Mad Knight

During the darkest period of Tristram's life, when grief drove him to madness in the forest, it was Sir Fergus who kept him (229-008). For many years Tristram wandered, and when he was mad in the forest, Sir Fergus tended to him. This loyal service ensured that Tristram survived his period of derangement until he could be restored to himself. Dame Bragwaine, seeking Tristram, found him through his horse Passe-Brewel — for she knew that Sir Fergus had kept the horse through those years of wandering.
