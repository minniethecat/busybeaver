[130-001]and how it was granted to him, and how he desired to be made knight of Sir

[130-002][[sir-launcelot|Launcelot]].

[130-003]With these words came before the king [[sir-gareth|Beaumains]], while the damosel was there,

[130-004]and thus he said, Sir king, God thank you, I have been this twelvemonth in your

[130-005]kitchen, and have had my full sustenance, and now I will ask my two gifts that

[130-006]be behind. Ask, upon my peril, said the king. Sir, this shall be my two gifts,

[130-007]first that ye will grant me to have this adventure of the damosel, for it

[130-008]belongeth unto me. Thou shalt have it, said the king, I grant it thee. Then,

[130-009]sir, this is the other gift, that ye shall bid Launcelot du Lake to make me

[130-010]knight, for of him I will be made knight and else of none. And when I am passed

[130-011]I pray you let him ride after me, and make me knight when I require him. All

[130-012]this shall be done, said the king. Fie on thee, said the damosel, shall I have

[130-013]none but one that is your kitchen page? Then was she wroth and took her horse

[130-014]and departed. And with that there came one to Beaumains and told him his horse

[130-015]and armour was come for him; and there was the dwarf come with all thing that

[130-016]him needed, in the richest manner; thereat all the court had much marvel from

[130-017]whence came all that gear. So when he was armed there was none but few so

[130-018]goodly a man as he was; and right so as he came into the hall and took his

[130-019]leave of [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], and [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]], and Sir Launcelot, and prayed that he

[130-020]would hie after him, and so departed and rode after the damosel.
