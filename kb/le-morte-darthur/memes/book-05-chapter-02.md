[097-001][[king-arthur|King Arthur]] aid and help against the Romans.

[097-002]Then answered King Anguish of Scotland, Sir, ye ought of right to be above all

[097-003]other kings, for unto you is none like nor pareil in Christendom, of knighthood

[097-004]nor of dignity, and I counsel you never to obey the Romans, for when they

[097-005]reigned on us they distressed our elders, and put this land to great extortions

[097-006]and tallies, wherefore I make here mine avow to avenge me on them; and for to

[097-007]strengthen your quarrel I shall furnish twenty thousand good men of [[battle|war]], and

[097-008]wage them on my costs, which shall await on you with myself when it shall

[097-009]please you. And the king of Little Britain granted him to the same thirty

[097-010]thousand; wherefore King Arthur thanked them. And then every man agreed to make

[097-011]war, and to aid after their power; that is to wit, the lord of West Wales

[097-012]promised to bring thirty thousand men, and Sir Uwaine, Sir Ider his son, with

[097-013]their cousins, promised to bring thirty thousand. Then [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot]] with all

[097-014]other promised in likewise every man a great multitude.

[097-015]And when King Arthur understood their courages and good wills he thanked them

[097-016]heartily, and after let call the ambassadors to hear their answer. And in

[097-017]presence of all his lords and knights he said to them in this wise: I will that

[097-018]ye return unto your lord and Procuror of the Common Weal for the Romans, and

[097-019]say ye to him, Of his demand and commandment I set nothing, and that I know of

[097-020]no truage nor tribute that I owe to him, nor to none earthly prince, Christian

[097-021]nor heathen; but I pretend to have and occupy the sovereignty of the empire,

[097-022]wherein I am entitled by the right of my predecessors, sometime kings of this

[097-023]land; and say to him that I am delibered and fully concluded, to go with mine

[097-024]army with strength and power unto Rome, by the grace of God, to take possession

[097-025]in the empire and subdue them that be rebel. Wherefore I command him and all

[097-026]them of Rome, that incontinent they make to me their homage, and to acknowledge

[097-027]me for their Emperor and Governor, upon pain that shall ensue. And then he

[097-028]commanded his treasurer to give to them great and large gifts, and to pay all

[097-029]their dispenses, and assigned Sir Cador to convey them out of the land. And so

[097-030]they took their leave and departed, and took their shipping at Sandwich, and

[097-031]passed forth by Flanders, Almaine, the mountains, and all Italy, until they

[097-032]came unto Lucius. And after the reverence made, they made relation of their

[097-033]answer, like as ye to-fore have heard.

[097-034]When the Emperor Lucius had well understood their credence, he was sore moved

[097-035]as he had been all araged, and said, I had supposed that Arthur would have

[097-036]obeyed to my commandment, and have served you himself, as him well beseemed or

[097-037]any other king to do. O Sir, said one of the senators, let be such vain words,

[097-038]for we let you wit that I and my fellows were full sore afeard to behold his

[097-039]countenance; I fear me ye have made a rod for yourself, for he intendeth to be

[097-040]lord of this empire, which sore is to be doubted if he come, for he is all

[097-041]another man than ye ween, and holdeth the most noble court of the world, all

[097-042]other kings nor princes may not compare unto his noble maintenance. On New

[097-043]Year’s Day we saw him in his estate, which was the royalest that ever we

[097-044]saw, for he was served at his table with nine kings, and the noblest fellowship

[097-045]of other princes, lords, and knights that be in the world, and every knight

[097-046]approved and like a lord, and holdeth [[round-table|Table Round]]: and in his person the most

[097-047]manly man that liveth, and is like to conquer all the world, for unto his

[097-048]courage it is too little: wherefore I advise you to keep well your marches and

[097-049]straits in the mountains; for certainly he is a lord to be doubted. Well, said

[097-050]Lucius, before Easter I suppose to pass the mountains, and so forth into

[097-051]France, and there bereave him his lands with Genoese and other mighty warriors

[097-052]of Tuscany and Lombardy. And I shall send for them all that be subjects and

[097-053]allied to the empire of Rome to come to mine aid. And forthwith sent old wise

[097-054]knights unto these countries following: first to Ambage and Arrage, to

[097-055]Alexandria, to India, to Armenia, whereas the river of Euphrates runneth into

[097-056]Asia, to Africa, and Europe the Large, to Ertayne and Elamye, to Araby, Egypt,

[097-057]and to Damascus, to Damietta and Cayer, to Cappadocia, to Tarsus, Turkey,

[097-058]Pontus and Pamphylia, to Syria and Galatia. And all these were subject to Rome

[097-059]and many more, as Greece, Cyprus, Macedonia, Calabria, Cateland, Portugal, with

[097-060]many thousands of Spaniards. Thus all these kings, dukes, and admirals,

[097-061]assembled about Rome, with sixteen kings at once, with great multitude of

[097-062]people. When the emperor understood their coming he made ready his Romans and

[097-063]all the people between him and Flanders.

[097-064]Also he had gotten with him fifty giants which had been engendered of fiends;

[097-065]and they were ordained to guard his person, and to break the front of the

[097-066]battle of King Arthur. And thus departed from Rome, and came down the mountains

[097-067]for to destroy the lands that Arthur had conquered, and came unto Cologne, and

[097-068]besieged a castle thereby, and won it soon, and stuffed it with two hundred

[097-069]Saracens or Infidels, and after destroyed many fair countries which Arthur had

[097-070]won of King Claudas. And thus Lucius came with all his host, which were

[097-071]disperplyd sixty mile in breadth, and commanded them to meet with him in

[097-072]Burgoyne, for he purposed to destroy the realm of Little Britain.
