[166-003]And then he let ordain a gentleman that was well learned and taught, his name

[166-004]was Gouvernail; and then he sent young [[sir-tristram|Tristram]] with Gouvernail into France to

[166-005]learn the language, and nurture, and deeds of arms. And there was Tristram more

[166-006]than seven years. And then when he well could speak the language, and had

[166-007]learned all that he might learn in that country, then he came home to his

[166-008]father, King Meliodas, again. And so Tristram learned to be an harper passing

[166-009]all other, that there was none such called in no country, and so on harping and

[166-010]on instruments of music he applied him in his youth for to learn.

[166-011]And after, as he grew in might and strength, he laboured ever in hunting and in

[166-012]hawking, so that never gentleman more, that ever we heard read of. And as the

[166-013]book saith, he began good measures of blowing of beasts of venery, and beasts

[166-014]of chase, and all manner of vermin, and all these terms we have yet of hawking

[166-015]and hunting. And therefore the book of venery, of hawking, and hunting, is

[166-016]called the book of Sir Tristram. Wherefore, as meseemeth, all gentlemen that

[166-017]bear old arms ought of right to honour Sir Tristram for the goodly terms that

[166-018]gentlemen have and use, and shall to the day of doom, that thereby in a manner

[166-019]all men of [[honour|worship]] may dissever a gentleman from a yeoman, and from a yeoman a

[166-020]villain. For he that gentle is will draw him unto gentle tatches, and to follow

[166-021]the customs of noble gentlemen.

[166-022]Thus Sir Tristram endured in Cornwall until he was big and strong, of the age

[166-023]of eighteen years. And then [[king-arthur|the King]] Meliodas had great joy of Sir Tristram,

[166-024]and so had the queen, his wife. For ever after in her life, because Sir

[166-025]Tristram saved her from the fire, she did never hate him more after, but loved

[166-026]him ever after, and gave Tristram many great gifts; for every estate loved him,

[166-027]where that he went.
