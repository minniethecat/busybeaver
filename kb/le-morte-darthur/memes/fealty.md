**Fealty** is the sworn obligation of loyalty that binds a knight to his [[baron|lord]] in Malory's [[Le Morte d'Arthur]]. Together with [[homage]]—the ritual act of submission from which fealty flows—it forms the legal and moral foundation of the feudal order. Yet fealty in Malory is never simple: a knight may owe fealty to multiple [[baron|lord]]s, and the claims of fealty may conflict with the demands of [[love]], [[honour]], or [[kinsman|kinship]].

## Fealty and Homage

Fealty and homage are distinct but inseparable. [[homage|Homage]] is the ceremony—the kneeling, the clasped hands, the spoken words—while fealty is the ongoing obligation that the ceremony creates:

> yield to him homage and fealty (107-038)

Once sworn, fealty obliges the vassal to military service, counsel, and loyalty. It is a bond that cannot be lightly broken, and the knight who violates his fealty commits a form of [[treason]] that strikes at the foundations of the social order.

## The Web of Fealty

In [[custom|practice]], a knight's fealties are multiple. He owes fealty to his immediate [[baron|lord]], to the [[kinsman|kin]]g, to his [[kinsman|kin]], and—through the [[Round Table]] oath—to the fellowship of knights. These obligations may come into conflict:

> proffered him his homage and fealty at all points (137-046)

The phrase "at all points" acknowledges the comprehensiveness of the obligation while hinting at its fragility. A knight who serves two masters may find that loyalty to one requ[[wrath|ire]]s [[treason|disloyalty]] to another. The t[[wrath|rage]]dy of Sir [[Sir Launcelot|Launcelot]] is in part a t[[wrath|rage]]dy of divided fealties—to [[King Arthur]] his [[baron|lord]] and to [[Queen Guenever]] his queen.

## The Ritual of Fealty

The swearing of fealty is a public act, performed before witnesses and recorded in the memory of the court:

> Fair lords, said Beaumains, I will take your homage and fealty (145-010)

When [[Sir Gareth|Beaumains]]—the unknown kitchen knight—receives the fealty of defeated [[baron|lord]]s, the ritual transforms him from outsider to [[baron|lord]] in a single gesture. Fealty is performative: the words themselves create the bond, and the public nature of the ceremony ensures that the bond is known and enforceable.

## Fealty and the Round Table

The [[Round Table]] oath creates a form of horizontal fealty—a mutual obligation among equals that supplements the vertical bond between [[baron|lord]] and vassal. This innovation is one of [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s great achievements, binding the knights to one another as well as to the crown. But it also multiplies the potential for conflict, creating obligations that can pull a knight in incompatible d[[wrath|ire]]ctions. The dissolution of the [[round-table|Round Table]] at the end of the Morte is simultaneously the dissolution of the web of fealties that held the [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian world together.
