[037-001]Lanceor, slew herself for love, and how Balin met with his brother Balan.

[037-002]Then he looked by him, and was ware of a damosel that came riding full fast as

[037-003]the horse might ride, on a fair palfrey. And when she espied that Lanceor was

[037-004]slain, she made sorrow out of measure, and said, O Balin, two bodies thou hast

[037-005]slain and one heart, and two hearts in one body, and two souls thou hast lost.

[037-006]And therewith she took the sword from her love that lay dead, and fell to the

[037-007]ground in a swoon. And when she arose she made great dole out of measure, the

[037-008]which sorrow grieved Balin passingly sore, and he went unto her for to have

[037-009]taken the sword out of her hand, but she held it so fast he might not take it

[037-010]out of her hand unless he should have hurt her, and suddenly she set the pommel

[037-011]to the ground, and rove herself through the body. When Balin espied her deeds,

[037-012]he was passing heavy in his heart, and ashamed that so fair a damosel had

[037-013]destroyed herself for the love of his death. Alas, said Balin, me repenteth

[037-014]sore the death of this knight, for the love of this damosel, for there was much

[037-015][[love|true love]] betwixt them both, and for sorrow might not longer behold him, but

[037-016]turned his horse and looked toward a great forest, and there he was ware, by

[037-017]the arms, of his brother Balan. And when they were met they put off their helms

[037-018]and kissed together, and wept for joy and [[mercy|pity]]. Then Balan said, I little

[037-019]weened to have met with you at this sudden adventure; I am right glad of your

[037-020]deliverance out of your dolorous prisonment, for a man told me, in the castle

[037-021]of Four Stones, that ye were delivered, and that man had seen you in the court

[037-022]of [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], and therefore I came hither into this country, for here I

[037-023]supposed to find you. Anon the knight Balin told his brother of his adventure

[037-024]of the sword, and of the death of the [[lady-of-the-lake|Lady of the Lake]], and how King Arthur was

[037-025]displeased with him. Wherefore he sent this knight after me, that lieth here

[037-026]dead, and the death of this damosel grieveth me sore. So doth it me, said

[037-027]Balan, but ye must take the adventure that God will ordain you. Truly, said

[037-028]Balin, I am right heavy that my Lord Arthur is displeased with me, for he is

[037-029]the most worshipful knight that reigneth now on earth, and his love will I get

[037-030]or else will I put my life in adventure. For the King Rience lieth at a siege

[037-031]at the Castle Terrabil, and thither will we draw in all haste, to prove our

[037-032][[honour|worship]] and [[courage|prowess]] upon him. I will well, said Balan, that we do, and we will

[037-033]help each other as brethren ought to do.
