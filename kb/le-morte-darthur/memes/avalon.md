# Avalon
**Avalon** is the mythical is[[realm|land]] to which the mortally wounded [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] is taken in the final pages of Thomas Malory's *Le Morte d'[[king-arthur|Arthur]]*. It exists at the boundary between the mortal and the [[marvel|supernatural]] — a place of healing, mystery, and the enduring hope of [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s return.

## The Isle of Healing
After [[king-arthur|Arthur]] receives his death-wound from [[mordred|Mordred]] at Salisbury, he is borne away to Avalon by the [[lady|ladies]] who attend him — including [[morgan-le-fay|Morgan le Fay]]. The is[[realm|land]] is presented as a place of healing beyond mortal medicine: "some men say in many parts of Eng[[realm|land]] that [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] is not dead, but carried by the will of our Lord Jesu into another place; and men say that he shall come again."

## Threshold of Worlds
Avalon represents the boundary between the chivalric world and the realm of faerie. It is from Avalon that [[sword-in-the-stone|Excalibur]] came (given by the [[lady-of-the-lake|Lady of the Lake]]), and it is to Avalon that [[king-arthur|Arthur]] returns. This circularity — the [[kinsman|kin]]g receiving his sword from the otherworld and returning to it in death — frames the ent[[wrath|ire]] [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian narrative within a [[marvel|supernatural]] parenthesis.
