[153-001]Lionesse, and how she let cry a [[tournament|tourney]] at her castle, whereas came many

[153-002]knights.

[153-003]Then said [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]] and his brethren unto [[king-arthur|Arthur]], Sir, an ye will give us

[153-004]leave, we will go and seek our brother. Nay, said [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot]], that shall ye

[153-005]not need; and so said Sir Baudwin of Britain: for as by our advice the king

[153-006]shall send unto Dame Lionesse a messenger, and pray her that she will come to

[153-007]the court in all the haste that she may, and doubt ye not she will come; and

[153-008]then she may give you best counsel where ye shall find him. This is well said

[153-009]of you, said the king. So then goodly letters were made, and the messenger sent

[153-010]forth, that night and day he went till he came unto the Castle Perilous. And

[153-011]then the lady Dame Lionesse was sent for, thereas she was with Sir Gringamore

[153-012]her brother and [[sir-gareth|Sir Gareth]]. And when she understood this message, she bade him

[153-013]ride on his way unto King Arthur, and she would come after in all goodly haste.

[153-014]Then when she came to Sir Gringamore and to Sir Gareth, she told them all how

[153-015]King Arthur had sent for her. That is because of me, said Sir Gareth. Now

[153-016]advise me, said Dame Lionesse, what shall I say, and in what manner I shall

[153-017]rule me. My lady and my love, said Sir Gareth, I pray you in no wise be ye

[153-018]aknowen where I am; but well I wot my mother is there and all my brethren, and

[153-019]they will take upon them to seek me, I wot well that they do. But this, madam,

[153-020]I would ye said and advised the king when he questioned with you of me. Then

[153-021]may ye say, this is your advice that, an it like his good grace, ye will do

[153-022]make a cry against the feast of the Assumption of our Lady, that what knight

[153-023]there proveth him best he shall wield you and all your land. And if so be that

[153-024]he be a wedded man, that his wife shall have the degree, and a coronal of gold

[153-025]beset with stones of virtue to the value of a thousand pound, and a white

[153-026]gerfalcon.

[153-027]So Dame Lionesse departed and came to King Arthur, where she was nobly

[153-028]received, and there she was sore questioned of the king and of [[queen-guenever|the Queen]] of

[153-029]Orkney. And she answered, where Sir Gareth was she could not tell. But thus

[153-030]much she said unto Arthur: Sir, I will let cry a tournament that shall be done

[153-031]before my castle at the Assumption of our Lady, and the cry shall be this: that

[153-032]you, my lord Arthur, shall be there, and your knights, and I will purvey that

[153-033]my knights shall be against yours; and then I am sure ye shall hear of Sir

[153-034]Gareth. This is well advised, said King Arthur; and so she departed. And the

[153-035]king and she made great provision to that tournament.

[153-036]When Dame Lionesse was come to the Isle of [[avalon|Avilion]], that was the same isle

[153-037]thereas her brother Sir Gringamore dwelt, then she told them all how she had

[153-038]done, and what promise she had made to King Arthur. Alas, said Sir Gareth, I

[153-039]have been so wounded with unhappiness sithen I came into this castle that I

[153-040]shall not be able to do at that tournament like a knight; for I was never

[153-041]thoroughly whole since I was hurt. Be ye of good cheer, said the damosel Linet,

[153-042]for I undertake within these fifteen days to make ye whole, and as lusty as

[153-043]ever ye were. And then she laid an ointment and a salve to him as it pleased to

[153-044]her, that he was never so fresh nor so lusty. Then said the damosel Linet: Send

[153-045]you unto Sir Persant of Inde, and assummon him and his knights to be here with

[153-046]you as they have promised. Also, that ye send unto Sir Ironside, that is the

[153-047]Red Knight of the Red Launds, and charge him that he be ready with you with his

[153-048]whole sum of knights, and then shall ye be able to match with King Arthur and

[153-049]his knights. So this was done, and all knights were sent for unto the Castle

[153-050]Perilous; and then the Red Knight answered and said unto Dame Lionesse, and to

[153-051]Sir Gareth, Madam, and my lord Sir Gareth, ye shall understand that I have been

[153-052]at [[camelot|the court of King Arthur]], and Sir Persant of Inde and his brethren, and

[153-053]there we have done our homage as ye commanded us. Also Sir Ironside said, I

[153-054]have taken upon me with Sir Persant of Inde and his brethren to hold part

[153-055]against my lord Sir Launcelot and the knights of that court. And this have I

[153-056]done for the love of my lady Dame Lionesse, and you my lord Sir Gareth. Ye have

[153-057]well done, said Sir Gareth; but wit you well ye shall be full sore matched with

[153-058]the most noble knights of the world; therefore we must purvey us of good

[153-059]knights, where we may get them. That is well said, said Sir Persant, and

[153-060]worshipfully.

[153-061]And so the cry was made in England, Wales, and Scotland, Ireland, Cornwall, and

[153-062]in all the Out Isles, and in Brittany and in many countries; that at the feast

[153-063]of our Lady the Assumption next coming, men should come to the Castle Perilous

[153-064]beside the Isle of Avilion; and there all the knights that there came should

[153-065]have the choice whether them list to be on the one party with the knights of

[153-066]the castle, or on the other party with King Arthur. And two months was to the

[153-067]day that the tournament should be. And so there came many good knights that

[153-068]were at their large, and held them for the most part against King Arthur and

[153-069]his knights of the [[round-table|Round Table]] and came in the side of them of the castle. For

[153-070]Sir Epinogrus was the first, and he was the king’s son of Northumberland,

[153-071]and Sir Palamides the Saracen was another, and Sir Safere his brother, and Sir

[153-072]Segwarides his brother, but they were christened, and Sir Malegrine another,

[153-073]and Sir Brian de les Isles, a noble knight, and Sir Grummore Grummursum, a good

[153-074]knight of Scotland, and Sir Carados of the dolorous tower, a noble knight, and

[153-075]Sir Turquine his brother, and Sir Arnold and Sir Gauter, two brethren, good

[153-076]knights of Cornwall. There came [[sir-tristram|Sir Tristram]] de Liones, and with him Sir Dinas,

[153-077]the Seneschal, and Sir Sadok; but this Sir Tristram was not at that time knight

[153-078]of the Table Round, but he was one of the best knights of the world. And so all

[153-079]these noble knights accompanied them with the lady of the castle, and with the

[153-080]Red Knight of the Red Launds; but as for Sir Gareth, he would not take upon him

[153-081]more but as other mean knights.
