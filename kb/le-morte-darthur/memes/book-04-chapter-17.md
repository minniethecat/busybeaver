[083-001]with twelve fair damosels, and how they complained on Sir Marhaus.

[083-002]Then [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]] and Sir Uwaine went and saluted them, and asked why they did

[083-003]that despite to the shield. Sir, said the damosels, we shall tell you. There is

[083-004]a knight in this country that owneth this white shield, and he is a passing

[083-005]good man of his hands, but he hateth all ladies and gentlewomen, and therefore

[083-006]we do all this despite to the shield. I shall say you, said Sir Gawaine, it

[083-007]beseemeth evil a good knight to despise all ladies and gentlewomen, and

[083-008]peradventure though he hate you he hath some certain cause, and peradventure he

[083-009]loveth in some other places ladies and gentlewomen, and to be loved again, an

[083-010]he be such a man of [[courage|prowess]] as ye speak of. Now, what is his name? Sir, said

[083-011]they, his name is Marhaus, the king’s son of Ireland. I know him well,

[083-012]said Sir Uwaine, he is a passing good knight as any is alive, for I saw him

[083-013]once proved at a [[tournament|jousts]] where many knights were gathered, and that time there

[083-014]might no man withstand him. Ah! said Sir Gawaine, damosels, methinketh ye are

[083-015]to blame, for it is to suppose, he that hung that shield there, he will not be

[083-016]long therefrom, and then may those knights match him on horseback, and that is

[083-017]more your [[honour|worship]] than thus; for I will abide no longer to see a knight’s

[083-018]shield dishonoured. And therewith Sir Uwaine and Gawaine departed a little from

[083-019]them, and then were they ware where Sir Marhaus came riding on a great horse

[083-020]straight toward them. And when the twelve damosels saw Sir Marhaus they fled

[083-021]into the turret as they were wild, so that some of them fell by the way. Then

[083-022]the one of the knights of the tower dressed his shield, and said on high, Sir

[083-023]Marhaus, defend thee. And so they ran together that the knight brake his spear

[083-024]on Marhaus, and Marhaus smote him so hard that he brake his neck and the

[083-025]horse’s back. That saw the other knight of the turret, and dressed him

[083-026]toward Marhaus, and they met so eagerly together that the knight of the turret

[083-027]was soon smitten down, horse and man, stark dead.
