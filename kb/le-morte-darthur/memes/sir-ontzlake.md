# Sir Ontzlake

Sir Ontzlake was a knight of prowess and the younger brother of the treacherous Sir Damas. Renowned for his goodness and gentle deeds, he was well-beloved of all people and was eventually made a knight of King Arthur's Round Table.

## The Quarrel with Sir Damas

Sir Ontzlake's elder brother, Sir Damas, was the lord of a castle but was known as the falsest knight that lived, full of treason and cowardice (073-008). Damas gave Ontzlake no part of his livelihood, but through his prowess of hands Ontzlake kept a fair manor and a rich dwelling, where he lived worshipfully and was well-beloved of all people (073-010). The two brothers had great war between them, and Ontzlake ever had the better, for he continually proffered Sir Damas to fight for the livelihood body for body, but Damas would not do so (073-014).

Sir Damas, being evil-beloved and hated, could find no knight willing to fight for him. He therefore imprisoned twenty good knights errant who refused to champion his cause. These knights declared that if any of them would have fought against Ontzlake, they would have been delivered, but they would never fight for so false a knight as Damas (073-025).

## The Battle with King Arthur

When King Arthur was brought by enchantment to the castle of Sir Damas, he was compelled to fight as Damas's champion or else remain imprisoned. Hearing of the impending battle, Sir Damas sent word to his brother Ontzlake, bidding him to be ready in the field by the hour of prime to fight with a good knight (074-027).

When this word came to Sir Ontzlake, he was passing heavy, for he had been wounded a little before through both his thighs with a spear and made great dole (074-030). Though wounded, he would have taken the battle on hand. However, by the contrivance of Morgan le Fay, Sir Accolon was lodged with Ontzlake at that time (074-034). Accolon, who had been given Excalibur and its sheath by Morgan le Fay to fight the mysterious knight, offered to take the battle on Ontzlake's behalf (074-037).

Sir Ontzlake was passing glad and thanked Sir Accolon with all his heart. Therewithal he sent word to his brother Damas that he had a knight who would be ready in the field (074-039).

## Knight of the Round Table

After the battle, in which King Arthur defeated Accolon despite fighting without his true sword Excalibur, Arthur revealed his identity and passed judgment. He commanded the treacherous Sir Damas to give Ontzlake the whole manor with its appurtenances, holding it of Damas and giving yearly a palfrey to ride upon (078-011). Arthur further charged Damas never to distress knights errant and to restore the twenty imprisoned knights of all their harness.

To Sir Ontzlake, Arthur spoke with honour: because Ontzlake was named a good knight, full of prowess, true and gentle in all his deeds, Arthur charged him to come to the court and become a knight of his (078-018). Arthur promised that if Ontzlake's deeds were thereafter worthy, he would be so preferred that in short time he should live as worshipfully as his brother.

Sir Ontzlake accepted, swearing to be from henceforward at all times at Arthur's commandment. He lamented that his wound from an adventurous knight through both thighs had prevented him from fighting the battle himself (078-025). Arthur said it was great pity, for then he himself would not have been hurt as he was.

## Pursuing Morgan le Fay

When Arthur later discovered that Morgan le Fay had stolen the scabbard of Excalibur, he commanded that the best horse be found and bid Sir Ontzlake arm him in all haste and ride with him (080-029). The king and Ontzlake were well armed and rode after Morgan le Fay, pursuing her through the countryside until they came to a valley where she transformed herself by enchantment into a great marble stone (080-043).
