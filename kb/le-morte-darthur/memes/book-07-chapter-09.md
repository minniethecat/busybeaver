[136-002]boy.

[136-003]And ever she rebuked Beaumains, and would not suffer him to sit at her table,

[136-004]but as the Green Knight took him and sat him at a side table. [[marvel|Marvel]]

[136-005]methinketh, said the Green Knight to the damosel, why ye rebuke this noble

[136-006]knight as ye do, for I warn you, damosel, he is a full noble knight, and I know

[136-007]no knight is able to match him; therefore ye do great wrong to rebuke him, for

[136-008]he shall do you right good service, for whatsomever he maketh himself, ye shall

[136-009]prove at the end that he is come of a noble blood and of king’s lineage.

[136-010]Fie, fie, said the damosel, it is shame for you to say of him such [[honour|worship]].

[136-011]Truly, said the Green Knight, it were shame for me to say of him any

[136-012]disworship, for he hath proved himself a better knight than I am, yet have I

[136-013]met with many knights in my days, and never or this time have I found no knight

[136-014]his match. And so that night they yede unto rest, and all that night the Green

[136-015]Knight commanded thirty knights privily to watch Beaumains, for to keep him

[136-016]from all treason.

[136-017]And so on the morn they all arose, and heard their mass and brake their fast;

[136-018]and then they took their horses and rode on their way, and the Green Knight

[136-019]conveyed them through the forest; and there the Green Knight said, My lord

[136-020]Beaumains, I and these thirty knights shall be always at your summons, both

[136-021]early and late, at your calling and whither that ever ye will send us. It is

[136-022]well said, said Beaumains; when that I call upon you ye must yield you unto

[136-023][[king-arthur|King Arthur]], and all your knights. If that ye so command us, we shall be ready

[136-024]at all times, said the Green Knight. Fie, fie upon thee, in the devil’s

[136-025]name, said the damosel, that any good knights should be obedient unto a kitchen

[136-026]knave. So then departed the Green Knight and the damosel. And then she said

[136-027]unto Beaumains, Why followest thou me, thou kitchen boy? Cast away thy shield

[136-028]and thy spear, and flee away; yet I counsel thee betimes or thou shalt say

[136-029]right soon, alas; for wert thou as wight as ever was Wade or [[sir-launcelot|Launcelot]],

[136-030][[sir-tristram|Tristram]], or the good knight Sir Lamorak, thou shalt not pass a pass here that

[136-031]is called the Pass Perilous. Damosel, said Beaumains, who is afeard let him

[136-032]flee, for it were shame to turn again sithen I have ridden so long with you.

[136-033]Well, said the damosel, ye shall soon, whether ye will or not.
