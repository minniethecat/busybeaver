[056-001][[king-arthur|Arthur]] to [[queen-guenever|Guenever]], a white hart came into the hall, and thirty couple hounds,

[056-002]and how a brachet pinched the hart which was taken away.

[056-003]Then was the high feast made ready, and the king was wedded at [[camelot|Camelot]] unto

[056-004]Dame Guenever in the church of Saint Stephen’s, with great solemnity. And

[056-005]as every man was set after his degree, [[merlin|Merlin]] went to all the knights of the

[056-006][[round-table|Round Table]], and bade them sit still, that none of them remove. For ye shall

[056-007]see a strange and a [[adventure|marvellous adventure]]. Right so as they sat there came

[056-008]running in a white hart into the hall, and a white brachet next him, and thirty

[056-009]couple of black running hounds came after with a great cry, and the hart went

[056-010]about the Table Round as he went by other boards. The white brachet bit him by

[056-011]the buttock and pulled out a piece, wherethrough the hart leapt a great leap

[056-012]and overthrew a knight that sat at the board side; and therewith the knight

[056-013]arose and took up the brachet, and so went forth out of the hall, and took his

[056-014]horse and rode his way with the brachet. Right so anon came in a lady on a

[056-015]white palfrey, and cried aloud to King Arthur, Sir, suffer me not to have this

[056-016]despite, for the brachet was mine that the knight led away. I may not do

[056-017]therewith, said the king.

[056-018]With this there came a knight riding all armed on a great horse, and took the

[056-019]lady away with him with force, and ever she cried and made great dole. When she

[056-020]was gone the king was glad, for she made such a noise. Nay, said Merlin, ye may

[056-021]not leave these adventures so lightly; for these adventures must be brought

[056-022]again or else it would be disworship to you and to your feast. I will, said the

[056-023]king, that all be done by your advice. Then, said Merlin, let call [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]],

[056-024]for he must bring again the white hart. Also, sir, ye must let call Sir Tor,

[056-025]for he must bring again the brachet and the knight, or else slay him. Also let

[056-026]call King Pellinore, for he must bring again the lady and the knight, or else

[056-027]slay him. And these three knights shall do [[marvel|marvellous]] adventures or they come

[056-028]again. Then were they called all three as it rehearseth afore, and each of them

[056-029]took his charge, and armed them surely. But Sir Gawaine had the first request,

[056-030]and therefore we will begin at him.
