# Sir Bors
**Sir Bors** de Ganis is one of the three knights who achieve the Holy Grail in Thomas Malory's *Le Morte d'[[king-arthur|Arthur]]*, alongside Sir Galahad and [[sir-percivale|Sir Percivale]]. Unlike Galahad (defined by spiritual perfection) and [[sir-percivale|Percivale]] (defined by innocent purity), Bors is distinguished by moral judgment — the capacity to choose rightly in impossible situations.

## The Choice
Bors faces the most famous ethical dilemma in the Morte: he must choose between rescuing his brother Lionel (being beaten by attackers) and aiding a maiden being forcibly abducted. Bors chooses to save the maiden — a choice that seems to betray [[kinsman|kin]] loyalty but is later vindicated as the righteous decision. Lionel's [[wrath|rage]] at being abandoned transforms into a homicidal [[wrath|fury]] that nearly kills Bors, yet Bors refuses to [[battle|fight]] his brother.

## The Grail Knight
Bors's selection as a Grail achiever validates his moral judgment. Where [[sir-launcelot|Launcelot]] fails because of his entanglement with [[queen-guenever|Guenever]], Bors succeeds precisely because he has subordinated all earthly attachments — even brotherly love — to moral principle. He is the Grail knight most accessible to ordinary human emulation: not born perfect like Galahad, not raised in innocence like [[sir-percivale|Percivale]], but a man of the world who chooses rightly.
