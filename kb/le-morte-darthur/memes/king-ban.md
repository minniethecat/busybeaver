# King Ban

King Ban was the ruler of Benwick in France, the father of [[Sir Launcelot]], and one of [[King Arthur]]'s earliest and most powerful allies in the wars that established the young king's realm. Alongside his brother [[King Bors]], Ban provided crucial military support that proved decisive in Arthur's early campaigns.

## The Alliance with King Arthur

The alliance between King Ban and King Arthur was forged through [[Merlin]]'s diplomacy. When Arthur faced rebellion from the eleven kings, "they sent for King Ban and King Bors" (013-001), summoning the two French kings to Britain. Ban ruled over Benwick — later called Bourges (013-019) — and answered Arthur's call with his formidable army. Messengers were sent "unto King Ban and Bors sent from King Arthur" (013-037), bearing Arthur's request for aid against his rebellious vassals.

Merlin personally carried a token — King Ban's ring — to Ban's wife to confirm the authenticity of the summons, for King Claudas threatened Ban's lands in his absence (014-030). When the people "saw King Ban's ring" (014-034), they recognised the authentic seal and the mission proceeded.

## The Great Battle

King Ban's arrival on the battlefield marked a turning point in Arthur's war against the rebellious kings. He came "as fierce as a lion, with bands of green and thereupon gold" (019-001). The chroniclers noted that "such two brethren as is King Ban and King Bors are not living" (019-004), testifying to the extraordinary martial quality of the brothers.

Ban's prowess that day was devastating. "When King Ban came into the battle, he came in so fiercely" that the northern host was entirely broken by his assault (019-009). His enemies "beheld the great damage that King Ban did" (019-015) and were sore astonished. The giant-like King Rience struck Ban's horse through with his sword, but Ban "smote at the other so vigorously" (019-024) that he continued fighting on foot. When remounted, the alliance of "King Arthur, King Ban, and King Bors made their knights to draw together" (019-043) and carried the day.

## The Father of Launcelot

King Ban's most enduring legacy in the Arthurian world was as the father of Sir Launcelot, the greatest knight of the [[Round Table]]. Ban's son would far eclipse his father's fame, but the noble lineage of Benwick produced a knight whose prowess and tragedy would define the later Arthurian age. Ban's name is thus carried forward through Launcelot's own titles, which include reference to his father's kingdom.

The shadow of King Claudas, Ban's enemy who threatened Benwick during his absence in Britain, would later fall upon the young Launcelot. The Lady of the Lake took the infant Launcelot from his mother, the Queen of Benwick, to raise him in her underwater realm — a destiny set in motion by the precarious position of Ban's kingdom.

## The Loyal Ally

Throughout the early wars, Ban remained steadfast. When Arthur faced his darkest hour in battle, Ban urged him forward with the words: "Ah, Sir Arthur, they are the best fighting men that ever we saw" (019-050), and "That is truth, said King Ban and Bors" (020-030). Merlin also assured the kings that "the world is yours" (020-030), prophesying their victory.

Ban's loyalty earned him a place at Arthur's high table. Together with Bors, he received the honours of a victorious ally: "so had King Ban and King Bors" (020-067). His partnership with Arthur stood as a model of the international alliances that underpinned the early Arthurian state.

## The Father of a Legend

King Ban's narrative significance lies not only in his own martial achievements but in his role as progenitor. The king who fought lions on the battlefield fathered the knight who became the very definition of chivalric excellence — and whose forbidden love for [[Queen Guenever]] would eventually bring down the kingdom Ban helped to build. This dramatic arc, from Ban's triumphant alliance to Launcelot's tragic grandeur, spans the full breadth of Malory's Arthurian chronicle.
