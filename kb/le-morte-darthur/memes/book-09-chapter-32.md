[235-001][[sir-palomides|Palomides]], and how he jousted with [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], and other feats.

[235-002]So the King of Northgalis and the King with the Hundred Knights, they two

[235-003]encountered with King Carados and with the King of Ireland; and there the King

[235-004]with the Hundred Knights smote down King Carados, and the King of Northgalis

[235-005]smote down the King of Ireland. With that came in Sir Palomides, and when he

[235-006]came he made great work, for by his indented shield he was well known. So came

[235-007]in King Arthur, and did great deeds of arms together, and put the King of

[235-008]Northgalis and the King with the Hundred Knights to the worse. With this came

[235-009]in [[sir-tristram|Sir Tristram]] with his black shield, and anon he jousted with Sir Palomides,

[235-010]and there by fine force Sir Tristram smote Sir Palomides over his horse’s

[235-011]croup. Then King Arthur cried: Knight with the Black Shield, make thee ready to

[235-012]me, and in the same wise Sir Tristram smote King Arthur. And then by force of

[235-013]King Arthur’s knights the King and Sir Palomides were horsed again. Then

[235-014]King Arthur with a great eager heart he gat a spear in his hand, and there upon

[235-015]the one side he smote Sir Tristram over his horse. Then foot-hot Sir Palomides

[235-016]came upon Sir Tristram, as he was upon foot, to have overridden him. Then Sir

[235-017]Tristram was ware of him, and there he stooped aside, and with great ire he gat

[235-018]him by the arm, and pulled him down from his horse. Then Sir Palomides lightly

[235-019]arose, and then they dashed together mightily with their swords; and many

[235-020]kings, queens, and lords, stood and beheld them. And at the last Sir Tristram

[235-021]smote Sir Palomides upon the helm three mighty strokes, and at every stroke

[235-022]that he gave him he said: This for Sir Tristram’s sake. With that Sir

[235-023]Palomides fell to the earth grovelling.

[235-024]Then came the King with the Hundred Knights, and brought Sir Tristram an horse,

[235-025]and so was he horsed again. By then was Sir Palomides horsed, and with great

[235-026]ire he jousted upon Sir Tristram with his spear as it was in the rest, and gave

[235-027]him a great dash with his sword. Then Sir Tristram avoided his spear, and gat

[235-028]him by the neck with his both hands, and pulled him clean out of his saddle,

[235-029]and so he bare him afore him the length of ten spears, and then in the presence

[235-030]of them all he let him fall at his adventure. Then Sir Tristram was ware of

[235-031]King Arthur with a naked sword in his hand, and with his spear Sir Tristram ran

[235-032]upon King Arthur; and then King Arthur boldly abode him and with his sword he

[235-033]smote a-two his spear, and therewithal Sir Tristram stonied; and so King Arthur

[235-034]gave him three or four strokes or he might get out his sword, and at the last

[235-035]Sir Tristram drew his sword and [either] assailed other passing hard. With that

[235-036]the great press departed [them]. Then Sir Tristram rode here and there and did

[235-037]his great pain, that eleven of the good knights of the blood of King Ban, that

[235-038]was of [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot]]’s kin, that day Sir Tristram smote down; that all

[235-039]the estates marvelled of his great deeds and all cried upon the Knight with the

[235-040]Black Shield.
