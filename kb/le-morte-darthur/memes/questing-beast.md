# Questing Beast

The **Questing Beast** (_beste glatissant_) is one of the most enigmatic creatures in [[Le Morte d'Arthur]] — a hybrid monster whose appearance at [[King Arthur]]'s court sets in motion one of the earliest adventures of the [[Round Table]]. The beast emits a noise like a pack of hounds baying from within its belly, and its pursuit becomes the lifelong quest of [[Sir Pellinore]] and, later, [[Sir Palomides]].

## Appearance and Description

The beast is first seen by [[King Arthur]] himself in a dream-like [[dream|vision]] shortly after his coronation. Malory describes it as a creature of uncanny strangeness:

> he saw coming toward him the strangest beast that ever he saw or heard of (022-026)

The beast approaches a well, drinks, and as it drinks the noise in its belly ceases — only to resume once it departs:

> the beast went to the well and drank, and the noise was in the beast's belly (022-027)

> but all the while the beast drank there was no noise in the beast's belly (022-029)

The description — part natural, part [[marvel|supernatural]] — marks the beast as a creature of the border[[realm|land]] between the ordinary world and the realm of [[miracle|marvel]].

## The Noise of the Hounds

The beast's defining trait is the sound it carries within it: the baying of hounds. Malory renders this with a memorable simile:

> the noise was like unto the questing of thirty couple hounds (022-028)

This internal noise is what gives the beast its name — "questing" refers to the baying of hunting hounds on the trail. The sound is both the beast's identity and its mystery: the hounds are heard but never seen, pursuing their quarry within the beast's own body.

## Narrative Function

The Questing Beast appears at three key narrative junctures:

1. **[[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s Early Reign** — [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] sees the beast and, immediately after[[battle|war]]d, meets [[Sir Pellinore]], who has pursued it for years. This encounter establishes the [[adventure|adventure-logic]] of the early books: a [[miracle|marvel]] appears, and a [[knight]] is found to follow it.

2. **Pellinore's Vow** — Pellinore declares of the beast that it is the object of his life's quest:

> I have followed that beast long time, and killed mine horse (022-035)

The pursuit of the beast defines Pellinore's identity as a knight-errant.

3. **[[sir-palomides|Palomides]]' Obsession** — Later in the cycle, the Saracen knight [[Sir Palomides]] takes up the pursuit, ma[[kinsman|kin]]g the beast the emblem of his restless, unconverted state — forever chasing, never attaining.

## The Beast's Symbolic Meaning

The Questing Beast operates as a symbol on multiple levels. As a portent, it appears at moments of narrative transition. As a [[quest]]-object, it represents the pursuit that defines the knight-errant's life — a goal that is always receding, never fully attainable. The hounds within the beast's belly suggest a hunt that is internalised: the knight is himself both hunter and hunted.

The beast's association with water — it drinks at a well, and the noise stops only while it drinks — connects it to the broader pattern of [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian [[marvel|marvels]] that occur at springs, wells, and fords.
