[041-001]Nero and King Lot of Orkney, and how King Lot was deceived by [[merlin|Merlin]], and how

[041-002]twelve kings were slain.

[041-003]Then [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] made ready his host in ten battles and Nero was ready in the

[041-004][[battle|field]] afore the Castle Terrabil with a great host, and he had ten battles, with

[041-005]many more people than Arthur had. Then Nero had the vanguard with the most part

[041-006]of his people, and Merlin came to King Lot of the Isle of Orkney, and held him

[041-007]with a tale of prophecy, till Nero and his people were destroyed. And there Sir

[041-008][[sir-kay|Kay]] the seneschal did passingly well, that the days of his life the [[honour|worship]]

[041-009]went never from him; and Sir Hervis de Revel did [[marvel|marvellous]] deeds with King

[041-010]Arthur, and King Arthur slew that day twenty knights and maimed forty. At that

[041-011]time came in the Knight with the Two Swords and his brother Balan, but they two

[041-012]did so marvellously that the king and all the knights marvelled of them, and

[041-013]all they that beheld them said they were sent from heaven as angels, or devils

[041-014]from hell; and King Arthur said himself they were the best knights that ever he

[041-015]saw, for they gave such strokes that all men had wonder of them.

[041-016]In the meanwhile came one to King Lot, and told him while he tarried there Nero

[041-017]was destroyed and slain with all his people. Alas, said King Lot, I am ashamed,

[041-018]for by my default there is many a worshipful man slain, for an we had been

[041-019]together there had been none host under the heaven that had been able for to

[041-020]have matched with us; this faiter with his prophecy hath mocked me. All that

[041-021]did Merlin, for he knew well that an King Lot had been with his body there at

[041-022]the first battle, King Arthur had been slain, and all his people destroyed; and

[041-023]well Merlin knew that one of the kings should be dead that day, and loath was

[041-024]Merlin that any of them both should be slain; but of the twain, he had liefer

[041-025]King Lot had been slain than King Arthur. Now what is best to do? said King Lot

[041-026]of Orkney; whether is me better to treat with King Arthur or to fight, for the

[041-027]greater part of our people are slain and destroyed? Sir, said a knight, set on

[041-028]Arthur for they are weary and forfoughten and we be fresh. As for me, said King

[041-029]Lot, I would every knight would do his part as I would do mine. And then they

[041-030]advanced banners and smote together and all to-shivered their spears; and

[041-031]Arthur’s knights, with the help of the Knight with the Two Swords and his

[041-032]brother Balan put King Lot and his host to the worse. But always King Lot held

[041-033]him in the foremost front, and did marvellous deeds of arms, for all his host

[041-034]was borne up by his hands, for he abode all knights. Alas he might not endure,

[041-035]the which was great [[mercy|pity]], that so worthy a knight as he was one should be

[041-036]overmatched, that of late time afore had been a knight of King Arthur’s,

[041-037]and wedded the sister of King Arthur; and for King Arthur lay by King

[041-038]Lot’s wife, the which was Arthur’s sister, and gat on her [[mordred|Mordred]],

[041-039]therefore King Lot held against Arthur. So there was a knight that was called

[041-040]the Knight with the Strange Beast, and at that time his right name was called

[041-041]Pellinore, the which was a good man of [[courage|prowess]], and he smote a mighty stroke at

[041-042]King Lot as he fought with all his enemies, and he failed of his stroke, and

[041-043]smote the horse’s neck, that he fell to the ground with King Lot. And

[041-044]therewith anon Pellinore smote him a great stroke through the helm and head

[041-045]unto the brows. And then all the host of Orkney fled for the death of King Lot,

[041-046]and there were slain many mothers’ sons. But King Pellinore bare the wite

[041-047]of the death of King Lot, wherefore [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]] revenged the death of his

[041-048]father the tenth year after he was made knight, and slew King Pellinore with

[041-049]his own hands. Also there were slain at that battle twelve kings on the side of

[041-050]King Lot with Nero, and all were buried in the Church of Saint Stephen’s

[041-051]in [[camelot|Camelot]], and the remnant of knights and of others were buried in a great

[041-052]rock.
