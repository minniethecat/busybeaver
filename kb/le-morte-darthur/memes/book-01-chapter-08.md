[011-001]Pentecost, a great feast, and what kings and lords came to his feast.

[011-002]Then the king removed into Wales, and let cry a great feast that it should be

[011-003]holden at Pentecost after the incoronation of him at the city of [[camelot|Carlion]]. Unto

[011-004]the feast came King Lot of Lothian and of Orkney, with five hundred knights

[011-005]with him. Also there came to the feast King Uriens of Gore with four hundred

[011-006]knights with him. Also there came to that feast King Nentres of Garlot, with

[011-007]seven hundred knights with him. Also there came to the feast the king of

[011-008]Scotland with six hundred knights with him, and he was but a young man. Also

[011-009]there came to the feast a king that was called [[king-arthur|the King]] with the Hundred

[011-010]Knights, but he and his men were passing well beseen at all points. Also there

[011-011]came the king of Carados with five hundred knights. And King Arthur was glad of

[011-012]their coming, for he weened that all the kings and knights had come for great

[011-013]love, and to have done him [[honour|worship]] at his feast; wherefore the king made great

[011-014]joy, and sent the kings and knights great presents. But the kings would none

[011-015]receive, but rebuked the messengers shamefully, and said they had no joy to

[011-016]receive no gifts of a beardless boy that was come of low blood, and sent him

[011-017]word they would none of his gifts, but that they were come to give him gifts

[011-018]with hard swords betwixt the neck and the shoulders: and therefore they came

[011-019]thither, so they told to the messengers plainly, for it was great shame to all

[011-020]them to see such a boy to have a rule of so noble a realm as this land was.

[011-021]With this answer the messengers departed and told to King Arthur this answer.

[011-022]Wherefore, by the advice of his barons, he took him to a strong tower with five

[011-023]hundred good men with him. And all the kings aforesaid in a manner laid a siege

[011-024]to-fore him, but King Arthur was well victualed. And within fifteen days there

[011-025]came [[merlin|Merlin]] among them into the city of Carlion. Then all the kings were

[011-026]passing glad of Merlin, and asked him, For what cause is that boy Arthur made

[011-027]your king? Sirs, said Merlin, I shall tell you the cause, for he is King Uther

[011-028]Pendragon’s son, born in wedlock, gotten on Igraine, the duke’s

[011-029]wife of Tintagil. Then is he a bastard, they said all. Nay, said Merlin, after

[011-030]the death of the duke, more than three hours, was Arthur begotten, and thirteen

[011-031]days after King Uther wedded Igraine; and therefore I prove him he is no

[011-032]bastard. And who saith nay, he shall be king and overcome all his enemies; and,

[011-033]or he die, he shall be long king of all England, and have under his obeissance

[011-034]Wales, Ireland, and Scotland, and more realms than I will now rehearse. Some of

[011-035]the kings had marvel of Merlin’s words, and deemed well that it should be

[011-036]as he said; and some of them laughed him to scorn, as King Lot; and more other

[011-037]called him a witch. But then were they accorded with Merlin, that King Arthur

[011-038]should come out and speak with the kings, and to come safe and to go safe, such

[011-039]surance there was made. So Merlin went unto King Arthur, and told him how he

[011-040]had done, and bade him fear not, but come out boldly and speak with them, and

[011-041]spare them not, but answer them as their king and chieftain; for ye shall

[011-042]overcome them all, whether they will or nill.
