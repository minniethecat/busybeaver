[094-001]three knights with their three damosels met at the fountain.

[094-002]Right so at the twelvemonths’ end they met all three knights at the

[094-003]fountain and their damosels, but the damosel that [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]] had could say but

[094-004]little [[honour|worship]] of him so they departed from the damosels and rode through a

[094-005]great forest, and there they met with a messenger that came from [[king-arthur|King Arthur]],

[094-006]that had sought them well-nigh a twelvemonth throughout all England, Wales, and

[094-007]Scotland, and charged if ever he might find Sir Gawaine and Sir Uwaine to bring

[094-008]them to the court again. And then were they all glad, and so prayed they Sir

[094-009]Marhaus to ride with them to the king’s court. And so within twelve days

[094-010]they came to [[camelot|Camelot]], and the king was passing glad of their coming, and so was

[094-011]all the court. Then the king made them to swear upon a book to tell him all

[094-012]their adventures that had befallen them that twelvemonth, and so they did. And

[094-013]there was Sir Marhaus well known, for there were knights that he had matched

[094-014]aforetime, and he was named one of the best knights living.

[094-015]Against the feast of Pentecost came the Damosel of the Lake and brought with

[094-016]her Sir Pelleas; and at that high feast there was great [[tournament|jousting]] of knights,

[094-017]and of all knights that were at that jousts, Sir Pelleas had the prize, and Sir

[094-018]Marhaus was named the next; but Sir Pelleas was so strong there might but few

[094-019]knights sit him a buffet with a spear. And at that next feast Sir Pelleas and

[094-020]Sir Marhaus were made knights of the [[round-table|Table Round]], for there were two sieges

[094-021]void, for two knights were slain that twelvemonth, and great joy had King

[094-022]Arthur of Sir Pelleas and of Sir Marhaus. But Pelleas loved never after Sir

[094-023]Gawaine, but as he spared him for the love of King Arthur; but ofttimes at

[094-024]jousts and tournaments Sir Pelleas quit Sir Gawaine, for so it rehearseth in

[094-025]the book of French. So [[sir-tristram|Sir Tristram]] many days after fought with Sir Marhaus in

[094-026]an island, and there they did a great battle, but at the last Sir Tristram slew

[094-027]him, so Sir Tristram was wounded that unnethe he might recover, and lay at a

[094-028]nunnery half a year. And Sir Pelleas was a worshipful knight, and was one of

[094-029]the four that achieved the Sangreal, and the Damosel of the Lake made by her

[094-030]means that never he had ado with [[sir-launcelot|Sir Launcelot]] de Lake, for where Sir Launcelot

[094-031]was at any jousts or any tournament, she would not suffer him be there that

[094-032]day, but if it were on the side of Sir Launcelot.
