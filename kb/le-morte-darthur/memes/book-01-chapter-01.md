[004-001]duke of Cornwall and Igraine his wife, and of their departing suddenly

[004-002]again.

[004-003]It befell in the days of Uther Pendragon, when he was king of all England, and

[004-004]so reigned, that there was a mighty duke in Cornwall that held [[battle|war]] against him

[004-005]long time. And the duke was called the Duke of Tintagil. And so by means King

[004-006]Uther sent for this duke, charging him to bring his wife with him, for she was

[004-007]called a fair lady, and a passing wise, and her name was called Igraine.

[004-008]So when the duke and his wife were come unto the king, by the means of great

[004-009]lords they were accorded both. The king liked and loved this lady well, and he

[004-010]made them great cheer out of measure, and desired to have lain by her. But she

[004-011]was a passing good woman, and would not assent unto the king. And then she told

[004-012]the duke her husband, and said, I suppose that we were sent for that I should

[004-013]be dishonoured; wherefore, husband, I counsel you, that we depart from hence

[004-014]suddenly, that we may ride all night unto our own castle. And in like wise as

[004-015]she said so they departed, that neither the king nor none of his council were

[004-016]ware of their departing. All so soon as King Uther knew of their departing so

[004-017]suddenly, he was wonderly wroth. Then he called to him his privy council, and

[004-018]told them of the sudden departing of the duke and his wife.

[004-019]Then they advised the king to send for the duke and his wife by a great charge;

[004-020]and if he will not come at your summons, then may ye do your best, then have ye

[004-021]cause to make mighty war upon him. So that was done, and the messengers had

[004-022]their answers; and that was this shortly, that neither he nor his wife would

[004-023]not come at him.

[004-024]Then was the king wonderly wroth. And then the king sent him plain word again,

[004-025]and bade him be ready and stuff him and garnish him, for within forty days he

[004-026]would fetch him out of the biggest castle that he hath.

[004-027]When the duke had this warning, anon he went and furnished and garnished two

[004-028]strong castles of his, of the which the one hight Tintagil, and the other

[004-029]castle hight Terrabil. So his wife Dame Igraine he put in the castle of

[004-030]Tintagil, and himself he put in the castle of Terrabil, the which had many

[004-031]issues and posterns out. Then in all haste came Uther with a great host, and

[004-032]laid a siege about the castle of Terrabil. And there he pight many pavilions,

[004-033]and there was great war made on both parties, and much people slain. Then for

[004-034]pure anger and for great love of fair Igraine the king Uther fell sick. So came

[004-035]to the king Uther Sir Ulfius, a noble knight, and asked the king why he was

[004-036]sick. I shall tell thee, said the king, I am sick for anger and for love of

[004-037]fair Igraine, that I may not be whole. Well, my lord, said Sir Ulfius, I shall

[004-038]seek [[merlin|Merlin]], and he shall do you remedy, that your heart shall be pleased. So

[004-039]Ulfius departed, and by adventure he met Merlin in a beggar’s array, and

[004-040]there Merlin asked Ulfius whom he sought. And he said he had little ado to tell

[004-041]him. Well, said Merlin, I know whom thou seekest, for thou seekest Merlin;

[004-042]therefore seek no farther, for I am he; and if King Uther will well reward me,

[004-043]and be sworn unto me to fulfil my desire, that shall be his honour and profit

[004-044]more than mine; for I shall cause him to have all his desire. All this will I

[004-045]undertake, said Ulfius, that there shall be nothing reasonable but thou shalt

[004-046]have thy desire. Well, said Merlin, he shall have his intent and desire. And

[004-047]therefore, said Merlin, ride on your way, for I will not be long behind.
