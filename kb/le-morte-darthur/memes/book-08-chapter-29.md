[192-001]Beale Isoud, and of Bragwaine her maid, and of Palamides.

[192-002]And anon they were richly wedded with great noblesse. But ever, as the French

[192-003]book saith, [[sir-tristram|Sir Tristram]] and La Beale Isoud loved ever together. Then was there

[192-004]great [[tournament|jousts]] and great tourneying, and many lords and ladies were at that

[192-005]feast, and Sir Tristram was most praised of all other. Thus dured the feast

[192-006]long, and after the feast was done, within a little while after, by the assent

[192-007]of two ladies that were with Queen Isoud, they ordained for hate and envy for

[192-008]to destroy Dame Bragwaine, that was maiden and lady unto La Beale Isoud; and

[192-009]she was sent into the forest for to fetch herbs, and there she was met, and

[192-010]bound feet and hand to a tree, and so she was bounden three days. And by

[192-011]fortune, Sir Palamides found Dame Bragwaine, and there he delivered her from

[192-012]the death, and brought her to a nunnery there beside, for to be recovered. When

[192-013]Isoud the queen missed her maiden, wit ye well she was right heavy as ever was

[192-014]any queen, for of all earthly women she loved her best: the cause was for she

[192-015]came with her out of her country. And so upon a day Queen Isoud walked into the

[192-016]forest to put away her thoughts, and there she went herself unto a well and

[192-017]made great moan. And suddenly there came Palamides to her, and had heard all

[192-018]her complaint, and said: Madam Isoud, an ye will grant me my boon, I shall

[192-019]bring to you Dame Bragwaine safe and sound. And the queen was so glad of his

[192-020]proffer that suddenly unadvised she granted all his asking. Well, Madam, said

[192-021]Palamides, I trust to your promise, and if ye will abide here half an hour I

[192-022]shall bring her to you. I shall abide you, said La Beale Isoud. And Sir

[192-023]Palamides rode forth his way to that nunnery, and lightly he came again with

[192-024]Dame Bragwaine; but by her good will she would not have come again, because for

[192-025]love of the queen she stood in adventure of her life. Notwithstanding, half

[192-026]against her will, she went with Sir Palamides unto the queen. And when the

[192-027]queen saw her she was passing glad. Now, Madam, said Palamides, remember upon

[192-028]your promise, for I have fulfilled my promise. Sir Palamides, said the queen, I

[192-029]wot not what is your desire, but I will that ye wit, howbeit I promised you

[192-030]largely, I thought none evil, nor I warn you none evil will I do. Madam, said

[192-031]Sir Palamides, as at this time, ye shall not know my desire, but before my lord

[192-032]your husband there shall ye know that I will have my desire that ye have

[192-033]promised me. And therewith the queen departed, and rode home to the king, and

[192-034]Sir Palamides rode after her. And when Sir Palamides came before the king, he

[192-035]said: Sir King, I require you as ye be a righteous king, that ye will judge me

[192-036]the right. Tell me your cause, said the king, and ye shall have right.
