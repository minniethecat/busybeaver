[022-002]Then after the departing of King Ban and of King [[sir-bors|Bors]], [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] rode into

[022-003][[camelot|Carlion]]. And thither came to him, King Lot’s wife, of Orkney, in manner

[022-004]of a message, but she was sent thither to espy the court of King Arthur; and

[022-005]she came richly beseen, with her four sons, [[sir-gawaine|Gawaine]], Gaheris, Agravine, and

[022-006][[sir-gareth|Gareth]], with many other knights and ladies. For she was a passing fair lady,

[022-007]therefore the king cast great love unto her, and desired to lie by her; so they

[022-008]were agreed, and he begat upon her [[mordred|Mordred]], and she was his sister, on his

[022-009]mother’s side, Igraine. So there she rested her a month, and at the last

[022-010]departed. Then the king dreamed a [[marvel|marvellous]] dream whereof he was sore adread.

[022-011]But all this time King Arthur knew not that King Lot’s wife was his

[022-012]sister. Thus was the dream of Arthur: Him thought there was come into this land

[022-013]griffins and serpents, and him thought they burnt and slew all the people in

[022-014]the land, and then him thought he fought with them, and they did him passing

[022-015]great harm, and wounded him full sore, but at the last he slew them. When the

[022-016]king awaked, he was passing heavy of his dream, and so to put it out of

[022-017]thoughts, he made him ready with many knights to ride a-hunting. As soon as he

[022-018]was in the forest the king saw a great hart afore him. This hart will I chase,

[022-019]said King Arthur, and so he spurred the horse, and rode after long, and so by

[022-020]fine force oft he was like to have smitten the hart; whereas the king had

[022-021]chased the hart so long, that his horse lost his breath, and fell down dead.

[022-022]Then a yeoman fetched the king another horse.

[022-023]So the king saw the hart enbushed, and his horse dead, he set him down by a

[022-024]fountain, and there he fell in great thoughts. And as he sat so, him thought he

[022-025]heard a noise of hounds, to the sum of thirty. And with that the king saw

[022-026]coming toward him the strangest beast that ever he saw or heard of; so the

[022-027]beast went to the well and drank, and the noise was in the beast’s belly

[022-028]like unto the questing of thirty couple hounds; but all the while the beast

[022-029]drank there was no noise in the beast’s belly: and there with the beast

[022-030]departed with a great noise, whereof the king had great marvel. And so he was

[022-031]in a great thought, and therewith he fell asleep. Right so there came a knight

[022-032]afoot unto Arthur and said, Knight full of thought and sleepy, tell me if thou

[022-033]sawest a strange beast pass this way. Such one saw I, said King Arthur, that is

[022-034]past two mile; what would ye with the beast? said Arthur. Sir, I have followed

[022-035]that beast long time, and killed mine horse, so would God I had another to

[022-036]follow my quest. Right so came one with the king’s horse, and when the

[022-037]knight saw the horse, he prayed the king to give him the horse: for I have

[022-038]followed this quest this twelvemonth, and either I shall achieve him, or bleed

[022-039]of the best blood of my body. Pellinore, that time king, followed the Questing

[022-040]Beast, and after his death Sir Palamides followed it.
