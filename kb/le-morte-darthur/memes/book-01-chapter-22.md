[025-001]jousted with a knight.

[025-002]Thou art full young and tender of age, said [[king-arthur|Arthur]], for to take so high an

[025-003]order on thee. Sir, said Griflet, I beseech you make me knight. Sir, said

[025-004][[merlin|Merlin]], it were great [[mercy|pity]] to lose Griflet, for he will be a passing good man

[025-005]when he is of age, abiding with you the term of his life. And if he adventure

[025-006]his body with yonder knight at the fountain, it is in great peril if ever he

[025-007]come again, for he is one of the best knights of the world, and the strongest

[025-008]man of arms. Well, said Arthur. So at the desire of Griflet the king made him

[025-009]knight. Now, said Arthur unto Sir Griflet, sith I have made you knight thou

[025-010]must give me a gift. What ye will, said Griflet. Thou shalt promise me by the

[025-011]faith of thy body, when thou hast jousted with the knight at the fountain,

[025-012]whether it fall ye be on foot or on horseback, that right so ye shall come

[025-013]again unto me without making any more debate. I will promise you, said Griflet,

[025-014]as you desire. Then took Griflet his horse in great haste, and dressed his

[025-015]shield and took a spear in his hand, and so he rode a great wallop till he came

[025-016]to the fountain, and thereby he saw a rich pavilion, and thereby under a cloth

[025-017]stood a fair horse well saddled and bridled, and on a tree a shield of divers

[025-018]colours and a great spear. Then Griflet smote on the shield with the butt of

[025-019]his spear, that the shield fell down to the ground. With that the knight came

[025-020]out of the pavilion, and said, Fair knight, why smote ye down my shield? For I

[025-021]will joust with you, said Griflet. It is better ye do not, said the knight, for

[025-022]ye are but young, and late made knight, and your might is nothing to mine. As

[025-023]for that, said Griflet, I will joust with you. That is me loath, said the

[025-024]knight, but sith I must needs, I will dress me thereto. Of whence be ye? said

[025-025]the knight. Sir, I am of Arthur’s court. So the two knights ran together

[025-026]that Griflet’s spear all to-shivered; and there withal he smote Griflet

[025-027]through the shield and the left side, and brake the spear that the truncheon

[025-028]stuck in his body, that horse and knight fell down.
