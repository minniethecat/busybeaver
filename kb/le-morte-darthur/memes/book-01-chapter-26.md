[029-001]Rience had overcome eleven kings, and how he desired [[king-arthur|Arthur]]’s beard to

[029-002]trim his mantle.

[029-003]This meanwhile came a messenger from King Rience of North Wales, and king he

[029-004]was of all Ireland, and of many isles. And this was his message, greeting well

[029-005]King Arthur in this manner wise, saying that King Rience had discomfited and

[029-006]overcome eleven kings, and everych of them did him homage, and that was this,

[029-007]they gave him their beards clean flayed off, as much as there was; wherefore

[029-008]the messenger came for King Arthur’s beard. For King Rience had purfled a

[029-009]mantle with kings’ beards, and there lacked one place of the mantle;

[029-010]wherefore he sent for his beard, or else he would enter into his lands, and

[029-011]burn and slay, and never leave till he have the head and the beard. Well, said

[029-012]Arthur, thou hast said thy message, the which is the most villainous and

[029-013]lewdest message that ever man heard sent unto a king; also thou mayest see my

[029-014]beard is full young yet to make a purfle of it. But tell thou thy king this: I

[029-015]owe him none homage, nor none of mine elders; but or it be long to, he shall do

[029-016]me homage on both his knees, or else he shall lose his head, by the faith of my

[029-017]body, for this is the most shamefulest message that ever I heard speak of. I

[029-018]have espied thy king met never yet with worshipful man, but tell him, I will

[029-019]have his head without he do me homage. Then the messenger departed.

[029-020]Now is there any here, said Arthur, that knoweth King Rience? Then answered a

[029-021]knight that hight Naram, Sir, I know the king well; he is a passing good man of

[029-022]his body, as few be living, and a passing proud man, and Sir, doubt ye not he

[029-023]will make [[battle|war]] on you with a mighty puissance. Well, said Arthur, I shall ordain

[029-024]for him in short time.
