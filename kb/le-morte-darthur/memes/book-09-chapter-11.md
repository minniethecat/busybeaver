[214-001]de Galis, and how they fought, and after accorded never to [[battle|fight]] together.

[214-002]Now fair knight, he said, my name is [[sir-tristram|Sir Tristram]] de Liones. Sir, said the

[214-003]other knight, and my name is Sir Lamorak de Galis. Ah, Sir Lamorak, said Sir

[214-004]Tristram, well be we met, and bethink thee now of the despite thou didst me of

[214-005]the sending of the horn unto [[king-mark|King Mark]]’s court, to the intent to have

[214-006]slain or dishonoured my lady the queen, La Beale Isoud; and therefore wit thou

[214-007]well, said Sir Tristram, the one of us shall die or we depart. Sir, said Sir

[214-008]Lamorak, remember that we were together in the Isle of Servage, and at that

[214-009]time ye promised me great friendship. Then Sir Tristram would make no longer

[214-010]delays, but lashed at Sir Lamorak; and thus they fought long till either were

[214-011]weary of other. Then Sir Tristram said to Sir Lamorak: In all my life met I

[214-012]never with such a knight that was so big and well breathed as ye be, therefore,

[214-013]said Sir Tristram, it were [[mercy|pity]] that any of us both should here be mischieved.

[214-014]Sir, said Sir Lamorak, for your [[honour|renown]] and name I will that ye have the worship

[214-015]of this battle, and therefore I will yield me unto you. And therewith he took

[214-016]the point of his sword to yield him. Nay, said Sir Tristram, ye shall not do

[214-017]so, for well I know your proffers, and more of your gentleness than for any

[214-018]fear or dread ye have of me. And therewithal Sir Tristram proffered him his

[214-019]sword and said: Sir Lamorak, as an overcome knight I yield me unto you as to a

[214-020]man of the most noble [[courage|prowess]] that ever I met withal. Nay, said Sir Lamorak, I

[214-021]will do you gentleness; I require you let us be sworn together that never none

[214-022]of us shall after this day have ado with other. And therewithal Sir Tristram

[214-023]and Sir Lamorak sware that never none of them should fight against other, nor

[214-024]for weal nor for woe.
