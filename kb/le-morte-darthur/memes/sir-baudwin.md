# Sir Baudwin of Britain

Sir Baudwin of Britain was a trusted knight who served King Arthur from the earliest days of his reign. He was made constable of England and later appointed as joint governor of the realm during Arthur's Roman campaign.

## Trusted Knight of Uther's Choosing

Sir Baudwin was among the knights whom King Uther Pendragon loved best and most trusted in his days. After Uther's death, when Merlin and the barons sought to protect the young Arthur, Baudwin was one of the best knights they could get, placed about Arthur along with Sir Kay, Sir Ulfius, and Sir Brastias. These knights were always about Arthur, day and night, till the feast of Pentecost (009-040).

## Constable of England

When Arthur was crowned and had stablished all the countries about London, he appointed his chief officers. Sir Baudwin of Britain was made constable of England, one of the highest military offices in the realm (010-020). Sir Kay was made seneschal, Sir Ulfius was made chamberlain, and Sir Brastias was made warden of the north.

## The Battle Against the Rebel Kings

When the eleven rebel kings refused to acknowledge Arthur's sovereignty, Baudwin stood among the men of most worship who accompanied the king. Alongside the Archbishop of Canterbury, Sir Kay, and Sir Brastias, he went with Arthur to confront the rebel kings. Though there was no meekness but stout words on both sides, Baudwin and the others remained steadfast (012-018).

In the battle that followed, Sir Baudwin, Sir Kay, and Sir Brastias slew on the right hand and on the left hand, that it was marvel (012-018). Always King Arthur on horseback laid on with a sword and did marvellous deeds of arms, while his trusted knights fought beside him.

## Governor of the Realm

When King Arthur prepared to cross the sea for his Roman campaign, he ordained two governors of the realm: Sir Baudwin of Britain and Sir Constantine, son to Sir Cador of Cornwall (098-008). In the presence of all his lords, Arthur resigned the rule of the realm and Queen Guenever to their governance. Baudwin was chosen for his wisdom and loyalty, to counsel to the best (098-008).

Later, when Sir Gareth's whereabouts were unknown, Sir Launcelot opposed sending Sir Gawaine and his brethren to seek him. Sir Baudwin of Britain concurred, advising instead that the king should send a messenger to Dame Lionesse, for she might give best counsel where Gareth might be found (153-005). This prudent counsel was well received.
