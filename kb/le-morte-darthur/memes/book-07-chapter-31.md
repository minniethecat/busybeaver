[158-002]Then [[sir-gareth|Sir Gareth]] rode unto the barbican of the castle, and prayed the porter

[158-003]fair to let him into the castle. The porter answered ungoodly again, and said,

[158-004]Thou gettest no lodging here. Fair sir, say not so, for I am a knight of King

[158-005][[king-arthur|Arthur]]’s, and pray the lord or the lady of this castle to give me harbour

[158-006]for the love of King Arthur. Then the porter went unto the duchess, and told

[158-007]her how there was a knight of King Arthur’s would have harbour. Let him

[158-008]in, said the duchess, for I will see that knight, and for King Arthur’s

[158-009]sake he shall not be harbourless. Then she yode up into a tower over the gate,

[158-010]with great torchlight.

[158-011]When Sir Gareth saw that torch-light he cried on high: Whether thou be lord or

[158-012]lady, giant or champion, I take no force so that I may have harbour this night;

[158-013]and if it so be that I must needs [[battle|fight]], spare me not to-morn when I have

[158-014]rested me, for both I and mine horse be weary. Sir knight, said the lady, thou

[158-015]speakest knightly and boldly; but wit thou well the lord of this castle loveth

[158-016]not King Arthur, nor none of his court, for my lord hath ever been against him;

[158-017]and therefore thou were better not to come within this castle; for an thou come

[158-018]in this night, thou must come in under such form, that wheresomever thou meet

[158-019]my lord, by stigh or by street, thou must yield thee to him as prisoner. Madam,

[158-020]said Sir Gareth, what is your lord, and what is his name? Sir, my lord’s

[158-021]name is the Duke de la Rowse. Well madam, said Sir Gareth, I shall promise you

[158-022]in what place I meet your lord I shall yield me unto him and to his good grace;

[158-023]with that I understand he will do me no harm: and if I understand that he will,

[158-024]I will release myself an I can with my spear and my sword. Ye say well, said

[158-025]the duchess; and then she let the drawbridge down, and so he rode into the

[158-026]hall, and there he alighted, and his horse was led into a stable; and in the

[158-027]hall he unarmed him and said, Madam, I will not out of this hall this night;

[158-028]and when it is daylight, let see who will have ado with me, he shall find me

[158-029]ready. Then was he set unto supper, and had many good dishes. Then Sir Gareth

[158-030]list well to eat, and knightly he ate his meat, and eagerly; there was many a

[158-031]fair lady by him, and some said they never saw a goodlier man nor so well of

[158-032]eating. Then they made him passing good cheer, and shortly when he had supped

[158-033]his bed was made there; so he rested him all night.

[158-034]And on the morn he heard mass, and brake his fast and took his leave at the

[158-035]duchess, and at them all; and thanked her goodly of her lodging, and of his

[158-036]good cheer; and then she asked him his name. Madam, he said, truly my name is

[158-037]Gareth of Orkney, and some men call me Beaumains. Then knew she well it was the

[158-038]same knight that fought for Dame Lionesse. So Sir Gareth departed and rode up

[158-039]into a mountain, and there met him a knight, his name was Sir Bendelaine, and

[158-040]said to Sir Gareth: Thou shalt not pass this way, for either thou shalt joust

[158-041]with me, or else be my prisoner. Then will I joust, said Sir Gareth. And so

[158-042]they let their horses run, and there Sir Gareth smote him throughout the body;

[158-043]and Sir Bendelaine rode forth to his castle there beside, and there died. So

[158-044]Sir Gareth would have rested him, and he came riding to Bendelaine’s

[158-045]castle. Then his knights and servants espied that it was he that had slain

[158-046]their lord. Then they armed twenty good men, and came out and assailed Sir

[158-047]Gareth; and so he had no spear, but his sword, and put his shield afore him;

[158-048]and there they brake their spears upon him, and they assailed him passingly

[158-049]sore. But ever Sir Gareth defended him as a knight.
