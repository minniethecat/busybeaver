[047-001]and saved a knight that would have slain himself for love.

[047-002]Then [[merlin|Merlin]] came thither and took up Balin, and gat him a good horse, for his

[047-003]was dead, and bade him ride out of that country. I would have my damosel, said

[047-004]Balin. Lo, said Merlin, where she lieth dead. And King Pellam lay so, many

[047-005]years sore wounded, and might never be whole till Galahad the haut prince

[047-006]healed him in the quest of the Sangreal, for in that place was part of the

[047-007]blood of our Lord Jesus Christ, that Joseph of Arimathea brought into this

[047-008]land, and there himself lay in that rich bed. And that was the same spear that

[047-009]Longius smote our Lord to the heart; and King Pellam was nigh of Joseph’s

[047-010]kin, and that was the most worshipful man that lived in those days, and great

[047-011][[mercy|pity]] it was of his hurt, for through that stroke, turned to great dole, tray

[047-012]and tene. Then departed Balin from Merlin, and said, In this world we meet

[047-013]never no more. So he rode forth through the fair countries and cities, and

[047-014]found the people dead, slain on every side. And all that were alive cried, O

[047-015]Balin, thou hast caused great damage in these countries; for the dolorous

[047-016]stroke thou gavest unto King Pellam three countries are destroyed, and doubt

[047-017]not but the vengeance will fall on thee at the last. When Balin was past those

[047-018]countries he was passing fain.

[047-019]So he rode eight days or he met with adventure. And at the last he came into a

[047-020]fair forest in a valley, and was ware of a tower, and there beside he saw a

[047-021]great horse of [[battle|war]], tied to a tree, and there beside sat a fair knight on the

[047-022]ground and made great mourning, and he was a likely man, and a well made. Balin

[047-023]said, God save you, why be ye so heavy? tell me and I will amend it, an I may,

[047-024]to my power. Sir knight, said he again, thou dost me great grief, for I was in

[047-025]merry thoughts, and now thou puttest me to more pain. Balin went a little from

[047-026]him, and looked on his horse; then heard Balin him say thus: Ah, fair lady, why

[047-027]have ye broken my promise, for thou promisest me to meet me here by noon, and I

[047-028]may curse thee that ever ye gave me this sword, for with this sword I slay

[047-029]myself, and pulled it out. And therewith Balin stert unto him and took him by

[047-030]the hand. Let go my hand, said the knight, or else I shall slay thee. That

[047-031]shall not need, said Balin, for I shall promise you my help to get you your

[047-032]lady, an ye will tell me where she is. What is your name? said the knight. My

[047-033]name is Balin le Savage. Ah, sir, I know you well enough, ye are the Knight

[047-034]with the Two Swords, and the man of most [[courage|prowess]] of your hands living. What is

[047-035]your name? said Balin. My name is Garnish of the Mount, a poor man’s son,

[047-036]but by my prowess and hardiness a duke hath made me knight, and gave me lands;

[047-037]his name is Duke Hermel, and his daughter is she that I love, and she me as I

[047-038]deemed. How far is she hence? said Balin. But six mile, said the knight. Now

[047-039]ride we hence, said these two knights. So they rode more than a pace, till that

[047-040]they came to a fair castle well walled and ditched. I will into the castle,

[047-041]said Balin, and look if she be there. So he went in and searched from chamber

[047-042]to chamber, and found her bed, but she was not there. Then Balin looked into a

[047-043]fair little garden, and under a laurel tree he saw her lie upon a quilt of

[047-044]green samite and a knight in her arms, fast halsing either other, and under

[047-045]their heads grass and herbs. When Balin saw her lie so with the foulest knight

[047-046]that ever he saw, and she a fair lady, then Balin went through all the chambers

[047-047]again, and told the knight how he found her as she had slept fast, and so

[047-048]brought him in the place there she lay fast sleeping.
