[181-001]and abode with Sir Bleoberis, and how she desired to go to her husband.

[181-002]So when she was set betwixt them both she said these words unto [[sir-tristram|Sir Tristram]]:

[181-003]Wit ye well, Sir Tristram de Liones, that but late thou wast the man in the

[181-004]world that I most loved and trusted, and I weened thou hadst loved me again

[181-005]above all ladies; but when thou sawest this knight lead me away thou madest no

[181-006]cheer to rescue me, but suffered my lord Segwarides ride after me; but until

[181-007]that time I weened thou haddest loved me, and therefore now I will leave thee,

[181-008]and never love thee more. And therewithal she went unto Sir Bleoberis.

[181-009]When Sir Tristram saw her do so he was wonderly wroth with that lady, and

[181-010]ashamed to come to the court. Sir Tristram, said Sir Bleoberis, ye are in the

[181-011]default, for I hear by this lady’s words she before this day trusted you

[181-012]above all earthly knights, and, as she saith, ye have deceived her, therefore

[181-013]wit ye well, there may no man hold that will away; and rather than ye should be

[181-014]heartily displeased with me I would ye had her, an she would abide with you.

[181-015]Nay, said the lady, so God me help I will never go with him; for he that I

[181-016]loved most I weened he had loved me. And therefore, Sir Tristram, she said,

[181-017]ride as thou came, for though thou haddest overcome this knight, as ye was

[181-018]likely, with thee never would I have gone. And I shall pray this knight so fair

[181-019]of his knighthood, that or ever he pass this country, that he will lead me to

[181-020]the abbey where my lord Sir Segwarides lieth. So God me help, said Bleoberis, I

[181-021]let you wit, good knight Sir Tristram, because [[king-mark|King Mark]] gave me the choice of

[181-022]a gift in this court, and so this lady liked me best—notwithstanding, she

[181-023]is wedded and hath a lord, and I have fulfilled my quest, she shall be sent

[181-024]unto her husband again, and in especial most for your sake, Sir Tristram; and

[181-025]if she would go with you I would ye had her. I thank you, said Sir Tristram,

[181-026]but for her love I shall beware what manner a lady I shall love or trust; for

[181-027]had her lord, Sir Segwarides, been away from the court, I should have been the

[181-028]first that should have followed you; but sithen that ye have refused me, as I

[181-029]am true knight I shall her know passingly well that I shall love or trust. And

[181-030]so they took their leave one from the other and departed.

[181-031]And so Sir Tristram rode unto Tintagil, and Sir Bleoberis rode unto the abbey

[181-032]where Sir Segwarides lay sore wounded, and there he delivered his lady, and

[181-033]departed as a noble knight; and when Sir Segwarides saw his lady, he was

[181-034]greatly comforted; and then she told him that Sir Tristram had done great

[181-035]battle with Sir Bleoberis, and caused him to bring her again. These words

[181-036]pleased Sir Segwarides right well, that Sir Tristram would do so much; and so

[181-037]that lady told all the battle unto King Mark betwixt Sir Tristram and Sir

[181-038]Bleoberis.
