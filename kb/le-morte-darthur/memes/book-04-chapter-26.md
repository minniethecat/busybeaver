[092-002]Now turn we unto Sir Uwaine, that rode westward with his damosel of three score

[092-003]winter of age, and she brought him thereas was a tournament nigh the march of

[092-004]Wales. And at that tournament Sir Uwaine smote down thirty knights, therefore

[092-005]was given him the prize, and that was a gerfalcon, and a white steed trapped

[092-006]with cloth of gold. So then Sir Uwaine did many strange adventures by the means

[092-007]of the old damosel, and so she brought him to a lady that was called the Lady

[092-008]of the Rock, the which was much courteous. So there were in the country two

[092-009]knights that were brethren, and they were called two perilous knights, the one

[092-010]knight hight Sir Edward of the Red Castle, and the other Sir Hue of the Red

[092-011]Castle; and these two brethren had disherited the Lady of the Rock of a barony

[092-012]of lands by their extortion. And as this knight was lodged with this lady she

[092-013]made her complaint to him of these two knights.

[092-014]Madam, said Sir Uwaine, they are to blame, for they do against the high order

[092-015]of knighthood, and the oath that they made; and if it like you I will speak

[092-016]with them, because I am a knight of [[king-arthur|King Arthur]]’s, and I will entreat

[092-017]them with fairness; and if they will not, I shall do battle with them, and in

[092-018]the defence of your right. Gramercy said the lady, and thereas I may not acquit

[092-019]you, God shall. So on the morn the two knights were sent for, that they should

[092-020]come thither to speak with the Lady of the Rock, and wit ye well they failed

[092-021]not, for they came with an hundred horse. But when this lady saw them in this

[092-022]manner so big, she would not suffer Sir Uwaine to go out to them upon no surety

[092-023]nor for no fair language, but she made him speak with them over a tower, but

[092-024]finally these two brethren would not be entreated, and answered that they would

[092-025]keep that they had. Well, said Sir Uwaine, then will I [[battle|fight]] with one of you,

[092-026]and prove that ye do this lady wrong. That will we not, said they, for an we do

[092-027]battle, we two will fight with one knight at once, and therefore if ye will

[092-028]fight so, we will be ready at what hour ye will assign. And if ye win us in

[092-029]battle the lady shall have her lands again. Ye say well, said Sir Uwaine,

[092-030]therefore make you ready so that ye be here to-morn in the defence of the

[092-031]lady’s right.
