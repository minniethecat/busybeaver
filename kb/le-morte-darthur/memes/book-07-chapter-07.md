[134-002]There sat a knight all armed in black harness, and his name was the Knight of

[134-003]the Black Laund. Then the damosel, when she saw that knight, she bade him flee

[134-004]down that valley, for his horse was not saddled. Gramercy, said [[sir-gareth|Beaumains]], for

[134-005]always ye would have me a coward. With that the Black Knight, when she came

[134-006]nigh him, spake and said, Damosel, have ye brought this knight of [[king-arthur|King Arthur]]

[134-007]to be your champion? Nay, fair knight, said she, this is but a kitchen knave

[134-008]that was fed in King Arthur’s kitchen for alms. Why cometh he, said the

[134-009]knight, in such array? it is shame that he beareth you [[fellowship|company]]. Sir, I cannot

[134-010]be delivered of him, said she, for with me he rideth maugre mine head: God

[134-011]would that ye should put him from me, other to slay him an ye may, for he is an

[134-012]unhappy knave, and unhappily he hath done this day: through mishap I saw him

[134-013]slay two knights at the passage of the water; and other deeds he did before

[134-014]right [[marvel|marvellous]] and through unhappiness. That marvelleth me, said the Black

[134-015]Knight, that any man that is of [[honour|worship]] will have ado with him. They know him

[134-016]not, said the damosel, and for because he rideth with me, they ween that he be

[134-017]some man of worship born. That may be, said the Black Knight; howbeit as ye say

[134-018]that he be no man of worship, he is a full likely person, and full like to be a

[134-019]strong man: but thus much shall I grant you, said the Black Knight; I shall put

[134-020]him down upon one foot, and his horse and his harness he shall leave with me,

[134-021]for it were shame to me to do him any more harm.

[134-022]When Sir Beaumains heard him say thus, he said, Sir knight, thou art full large

[134-023]of my horse and my harness; I let thee wit it cost thee nought, and whether it

[134-024]liketh thee or not, this laund will I pass maugre thine head. And horse nor

[134-025]harness gettest thou none of mine, but if thou win them with thy hands; and

[134-026]therefore let see what thou canst do. Sayest thou that? said the Black Knight,

[134-027]now yield thy lady from thee, for it beseemeth never a kitchen page to ride

[134-028]with such a lady. Thou liest, said Beaumains, I am a gentleman born, and of

[134-029]more high lineage than thou, and that will I prove on thy body.

[134-030]Then in great wrath they departed with their horses, and came together as it

[134-031]had been the thunder, and the Black Knight’s spear brake, and Beaumains

[134-032]thrust him through both his sides, and therewith his spear brake, and the

[134-033]truncheon left still in his side. But nevertheless the Black Knight drew his

[134-034]sword, and smote many eager strokes, and of great might, and hurt Beaumains

[134-035]full sore. But at the last the Black Knight, within an hour and an half, he

[134-036]fell down off his horse in swoon, and there he died. And when Beaumains saw him

[134-037]so well horsed and armed, then he alighted down and armed him in his armour,

[134-038]and so took his horse and rode after the damosel.

[134-039]When she saw him come nigh, she said, Away, kitchen knave, out of the wind, for

[134-040]the smell of thy bawdy clothes grieveth me. Alas, she said, that ever such a

[134-041]knave should by mishap slay so good a knight as thou hast done, but all this is

[134-042]thine unhappiness. But here by is one shall pay thee all thy payment, and

[134-043]therefore yet I counsel thee, flee. It may happen me, said Beaumains, to be

[134-044]beaten or slain, but I warn you, fair damosel, I will not flee away, a nor

[134-045]leave your company, for all that ye can say; for ever ye say that they will

[134-046]kill me or beat me, but howsomever it happeneth I escape, and they lie on the

[134-047]ground. And therefore it were as good for you to hold you still thus all day

[134-048]rebuking me, for away will I not till I see the uttermost of this journey, or

[134-049]else I will be slain, other truly beaten; therefore ride on your way, for

[134-050]follow you I will whatsomever happen.
