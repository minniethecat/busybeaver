[099-001]lying in his cabin had a [[marvel|marvellous]] dream and of the exposition thereof.

[099-002]And as the king lay in his cabin in the ship, he fell in a slumbering and

[099-003]dreamed a marvellous dream: him seemed that a dreadful dragon did drown much of

[099-004]his people, and he came flying out of the west, and his head was enamelled with

[099-005]azure, and his shoulders shone as gold, his belly like mails of a marvellous

[099-006]hue, his tail full of tatters, his feet full of fine sable, and his claws like

[099-007]fine gold; and an hideous flame of fire flew out of his mouth, like as the land

[099-008]and water had flamed all of fire. After, him seemed there came out of the

[099-009]orient, a grimly boar all black in a cloud, and his paws as big as a post; he

[099-010]was rugged looking roughly, he was the foulest beast that ever man saw, he

[099-011]roared and romed so hideously that it were marvel to hear. Then the dreadful

[099-012]dragon advanced him and came in the wind like a falcon giving great strokes on

[099-013]the boar, and the boar hit him again with his grizzly tusks that his breast was

[099-014]all bloody, and that the hot blood made all the sea red of his blood. Then the

[099-015]dragon flew away all on an height, and came down with such a swough, and smote

[099-016]the boar on the ridge, which was ten foot large from the head to the tail, and

[099-017]smote the boar all to powder both flesh and bones, that it flittered all abroad

[099-018]on the sea.

[099-019]And therewith the king awoke anon, and was sore abashed of this dream, and sent

[099-020]anon for a wise philosopher, commanding to tell him the signification of his

[099-021]dream. Sir, said the philosopher, the dragon that thou dreamedst of betokeneth

[099-022]thine own person that sailest here, and the colours of his wings be thy realms

[099-023]that thou hast won, and his tail which is all to-tattered signifieth the noble

[099-024]knights of the [[round-table|Round Table]]; and the boar that the dragon slew coming from the

[099-025]clouds betokeneth some tyrant that tormenteth the people, or else thou art like

[099-026]to [[battle|fight]] with some giant thyself, being horrible and abominable, whose peer ye

[099-027]saw never in your days, wherefore of this dreadful dream doubt thee nothing,

[099-028]but as a conqueror come forth thyself.

[099-029]Then after this soon they had sight of land, and sailed till they arrived at

[099-030]Barflete in Flanders, and when they were there he found many of his great lords

[099-031]ready, as they had been commanded to wait upon him.
