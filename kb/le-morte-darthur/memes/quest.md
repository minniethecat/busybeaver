# Quest

The **quest** is the defining narrative form of Thomas Malory's *Le Morte d'[[king-arthur|Arthur]]*. Knights set forth from [[camelot|Arthur's court]] into unknown [[realm|land]]s, driven by [[oath|vow]]s, prophecies, or the demands of [[honour|honor]]. The quest structures both individual adventures and the overarching arc of the [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian cycle.

## The Narrative of Seeking

The quest is fun[[lady|dame]]ntally a journey of seeking. [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] himself "saw the questing beast" (022-001) and later hears a sound "like unto the questing of thirty couple hounds" (022-028) — the very word "questing" associated with a [[marvel|supernatural]] creature that embodies pursuit without end. The quest form links individual [[adventure|knightly adventure]]s to the larger cosmic drama of the [[king-arthur|Arthur]]ian world.

## The Sangreal Quest

The most significant quest in Malory's cycle is the quest for the [[sangreal|Sangreal]] (Holy Grail). Caxton's preface describes how the Grail "came first to [[kinsman|kin]]g [[camelot|Arthur's court]], and the quest how the [[sangreal|Sangreal]] was begun" (002-121), and the "Fourteenth Book treateth of the quest of the [[sangreal|Sangreal]]" (002-122). The Grail quest represents the apotheosis of the quest form — a spiritual journey that tests not only martial [[courage|prowess]] but moral [[worship|worthiness]], and whose completion signals both the highest achievement and the beginning of the end for [[round-table|the Round Table fellowship]].
