[052-001][[queen-guenever|Guenever]], daughter to Leodegrance, King of the Land of Cameliard, with whom he

[052-002]had the [[round-table|Round Table]].

[052-003]In the beginning of [[king-arthur|Arthur]], after he was chosen king by adventure and by grace;

[052-004]for the most part of the barons knew not that he was Uther Pendragon’s

[052-005]son, but as [[merlin|Merlin]] made it openly known. But yet many kings and lords held

[052-006]great [[battle|war]] against him for that cause, but well Arthur overcame them all, for

[052-007]the most part the days of his life he was ruled much by the counsel of Merlin.

[052-008]So it fell on a time King Arthur said unto Merlin, My barons will let me have

[052-009]no rest, but needs I must take a wife, and I will none take but by thy counsel

[052-010]and by thine advice. It is well done, said Merlin, that ye take a wife, for a

[052-011]man of your bounty and noblesse should not be without a wife. Now is there any

[052-012]that ye love more than another? Yea, said King Arthur, I love Guenever the

[052-013]king’s daughter, Leodegrance of the land of Cameliard, the which holdeth

[052-014]in his house the Table Round that ye told he had of my father Uther. And this

[052-015]damosel is the most valiant and fairest lady that I know living, or yet that

[052-016]ever I could find. Sir, said Merlin, as of her beauty and fairness she is one

[052-017]of the fairest alive, but, an ye loved her not so well as ye do, I should find

[052-018]you a damosel of beauty and of goodness that should like you and please you, an

[052-019]your heart were not set; but there as a man’s heart is set, he will be

[052-020]loath to return. That is truth, said King Arthur. But Merlin warned the king

[052-021]covertly that Guenever was not wholesome for him to take to wife, for he warned

[052-022]him that [[sir-launcelot|Launcelot]] should love her, and she him again; and so he turned his

[052-023]tale to the adventures of Sangreal.

[052-024]Then Merlin desired of the king for to have men with him that should enquire of

[052-025]Guenever, and so the king granted him, and Merlin went forth unto King

[052-026]Leodegrance of Cameliard, and told him of the desires of the king that he would

[052-027]have unto his wife Guenever his daughter. That is to me, said King Leodegrance,

[052-028]the best tidings that ever I heard, that so worthy a king of [[courage|prowess]] and

[052-029]noblesse will wed my daughter. And as for my lands, I will give him, wist I it

[052-030]might please him, but he hath lands enow, him needeth none; but I shall send

[052-031]him a gift shall please him much more, for I shall give him the Table Round,

[052-032]the which Uther Pendragon gave me, and when it is full complete, there is an

[052-033]hundred knights and fifty. And as for an hundred good knights I have myself,

[052-034]but I faute fifty, for so many have been slain in my days. And so Leodegrance

[052-035]delivered his daughter Guenever unto Merlin, and the Table Round with the

[052-036]hundred knights, and so they rode freshly, with great royalty, what by water

[052-037]and what by land, till that they came nigh unto London.
