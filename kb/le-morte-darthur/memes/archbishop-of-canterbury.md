# Archbishop of Canterbury

The Archbishop of Canterbury was the highest ecclesiastical authority in the realm during the time of [[King Uther Pendragon]] and the rise of [[King Arthur]]. As the primate of the English Church, he served as a counsellor to kings, a witness to sacred miracles, and the officiant of the royal coronation that established Arthur's legitimacy as sovereign.

## Counsel to King Uther

When [[King Uther Pendragon]] fell gravely ill, his advisors sought the spiritual authority of the Archbishop. The archbishop went to counsel the king on matters of succession and the realm's future, acting as the bridge between divine will and earthly rule during a time of uncertainty (008-004).

## The Miracle of the Sword in the Stone

After Uther's death, the realm fell into disorder, with many lords contending for the crown. It was the Archbishop of Canterbury who, by [[Merlin]]'s providence, summoned all the lords and gentlemen of arms to London for Christmas. There, in the churchyard, the miraculous sword in the stone appeared. The Archbishop let it be known that whosoever could pull the sword from the stone would be the rightful king of all England — a divine test that ultimately revealed [[King Arthur]] as the true heir (009-037).

## The Coronation of King Arthur

When Arthur drew the sword from the stone and was acknowledged as king by the commons, the Archbishop of Canterbury officiated at his coronation. He went with Arthur to the ceremony, lending the full weight of ecclesiastical authority to the young king's accession. The Archbishop's presence at this pivotal moment symbolised the union of Church and Crown that would define Arthur's reign (012-003).

## Witness to the Founding of the Round Table

The Archbishop also bore witness to the knighting of new warriors and the gathering of the great lords who would form the backbone of Arthur's military power. He stood alongside [[King Ban]] and [[King Bors]] of Gaul, [[Sir Ector]], and other noble figures at ceremonies that marked the dawn of the Arthurian age (013-081).
