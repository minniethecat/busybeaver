[105-002]Then they took their spears and ran each at other with all the might they had,

[105-003]and smote each other through their shields into their shoulders, wherefore anon

[105-004]they pulled out their swords, and smote great strokes that the fire sprang out

[105-005]of their helms. Then [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]] was all abashed, and with Galatine his good

[105-006]sword he smote through shield and thick hauberk made of thick mails, and all

[105-007]to-rushed and break the precious stones, and made him a large wound, that men

[105-008]might see both liver and lung. Then groaned that knight, and addressed him to

[105-009]Sir Gawaine, and with an awk stroke gave him a great wound and cut a vein,

[105-010]which grieved Gawaine sore, and he bled sore. Then the knight said to Sir

[105-011]Gawaine, bind thy wound or thy blee[ding] change, for thou be-bleedest all thy

[105-012]horse and thy fair arms, for all the barbers of Brittany shall not con staunch

[105-013]thy blood, for whosomever is hurt with this blade he shall never be staunched

[105-014]of bleeding. Then answered Gawaine, it grieveth me but little, thy great words

[105-015]shall not fear me nor lessen my courage, but thou shalt suffer teen and sorrow

[105-016]or we depart, but tell me in haste who may staunch my bleeding. That may I do,

[105-017]said the knight, if I will, and so will I if thou wilt succour and aid me, that

[105-018]I may be christened and believe on God, and thereof I require thee of thy

[105-019]manhood, and it shall be great merit for thy soul. I grant, said Gawaine, so

[105-020]God help me, to accomplish all thy desire, but first tell me what thou

[105-021]soughtest here thus alone, and of what land and liegiance thou art of. Sir, he

[105-022]said, my name is Priamus, and a great prince is my father, and he hath been

[105-023]rebel unto Rome and overridden many of their lands. My father is lineally

[105-024]descended of Alexander and of Hector by right line. And Duke Joshua and

[105-025]Maccabaeus were of our lineage. I am right inheritor of Alexandria and Africa,

[105-026]and all the out isles, yet will I believe on thy Lord that thou believest on;

[105-027]and for thy labour I shall give thee treasure enough. I was so elate and

[105-028]hauteyn in my heart that I thought no man my peer, nor to me semblable. I was

[105-029]sent into this [[battle|war]] with seven score knights, and now I have encountered with

[105-030]thee, which hast given to me of fighting my fill, wherefore sir knight, I pray

[105-031]thee to tell me what thou art. I am no knight, said Gawaine, I have been

[105-032]brought up in the guardrobe with the noble [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] many years, for to take

[105-033]heed to his armour and his other array, and to point his paltocks that long to

[105-034]himself. At Yule last he made me yeoman, and gave to me horse and harness, and

[105-035]an hundred pound in money; and if fortune be my friend, I doubt not but to be

[105-036]well advanced and holpen by my liege lord. Ah, said Priamus, if his knaves be

[105-037]so keen and fierce, his knights be passing good: now for the King’s love

[105-038]of Heaven, whether thou be a knave or a knight, tell thou me thy name. By God,

[105-039]said Sir Gawaine, now I will say thee sooth, my name is Sir Gawaine, and known

[105-040]I am in his court and in his chamber, and one of the knights of the Round

[105-041]Table, he dubbed me a duke with his own hand. Therefore grudge not if this

[105-042]grace is to me fortuned, it is the goodness of God that lent to me my strength.

[105-043]Now am I better pleased, said Priamus, than thou hadst given to me all the

[105-044]Provence and Paris the rich. I had liefer to have been torn with wild horses,

[105-045]than any varlet had won such loos, or any page or priker should have had prize

[105-046]on me. But now sir knight I warn thee that hereby is a Duke of Lorraine with

[105-047]his army, and the noblest men of Dolphiny, and lords of Lombardy, with the

[105-048]garrison of Godard, and Saracens of Southland, y-numbered sixty thousand of

[105-049]good men of arms; wherefore but if we hie us hence, it will harm us both, for

[105-050]we be sore hurt, never like to recover; but take heed to my page, that he no

[105-051]horn blow, for if he do, there be hoving here fast by an hundred knights

[105-052]awaiting on my person, and if they take thee, there shall no ransom of gold nor

[105-053]silver acquit thee.

[105-054]Then Sir Gawaine rode over a water for to save him, and the knight followed

[105-055]him, and so rode forth till they came to his fellows which were in the meadow,

[105-056]where they had been all the night. Anon as Sir Wisshard was ware of Sir Gawaine

[105-057]and saw that he was hurt, he ran to him sorrowfully weeping, and demanded of

[105-058]him who had so hurt him; and Gawaine told how he had foughten with that man,

[105-059]and each of them had hurt other, and how he had salves to heal them; but I can

[105-060]tell you other tidings, that soon we shall have ado with many enemies.

[105-061]Then Sir Priamus and Sir Gawaine alighted, and let their horses graze in the

[105-062]meadow, and unarmed them, and then the blood ran freshly from their wounds. And

[105-063]Priamus took from his page a vial full of the four waters that came out of

[105-064]Paradise, and with certain balm anointed their wounds, and washed them with

[105-065]that water, and within an hour after they were both as whole as ever they were.

[105-066]And then with a trumpet were they all assembled to council, and there Priamus

[105-067]told unto them what lords and knights had sworn to rescue him, and that without

[105-068]fail they should be assailed with many thousands, wherefore he counselled them

[105-069]to withdraw them. Then Sir Gawaine said, it were great shame to them to avoid

[105-070]without any strokes; Wherefore I advise to take our arms and to make us ready

[105-071]to meet with these Saracens and misbelieving men, and with the help of God we

[105-072]shall overthrow them and have a fair day on them. And Sir Florence shall abide

[105-073]still in this field to keep the stale as a noble knight, and we shall not

[105-074]forsake yonder fellows. Now, said Priamus, cease your words, for I warn you ye

[105-075]shall find in yonder woods many perilous knights; they will put forth beasts to

[105-076]call you on, they be out of number, and ye are not past seven hundred, which be

[105-077]over few to fight with so many. Nevertheless, said Sir Gawaine, we shall once

[105-078]encounter them, and see what they can do, and the best shall have the victory.
