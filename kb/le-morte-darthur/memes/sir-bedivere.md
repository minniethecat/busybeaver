# Sir Bedivere

Sir Bedivere was a [[Knight of the Round Table]] who served [[King Arthur]] in the Roman War campaign, fighting alongside [[Sir Lionel]] and [[Sir Gawaine]] in the vanguard against the forces of the Emperor Lucius. He was among the inner circle of knights whom Arthur trusted with the most critical commands.

## The King's Trusted Knight

Bedivere stood among Arthur's most trusted companions. When the king prepared for the great war against Rome, "he called to him Sir Kay and Sir Bedivere, and commanded them secretly" (100-021), entrusting them with matters of strategic importance. That Arthur summoned Bedivere alongside [[Sir Kay]] the seneschal — one of the king's oldest and closest companions — speaks to the confidence the king placed in him.

## The Roman War Campaign

When Arthur led his host across the sea, Bedivere fought in the vanguard. The king "called Sir Gawaine, Sir Bors, Sir Lionel, and Sir Bedivere" (101-002) to his war council, placing Bedivere among the foremost knights of the realm. Together with Sir Lionel, he was deployed in a position of tactical importance — "left in a bushment Sir Lionel and Sir Bedivere" (101-010) — lying in ambush to surprise the Roman forces.

The chronicle records the moment of action: "Sir Lionel and Sir Bedivere were hoving" (101-022), waiting for the signal to spring the trap. The ambush succeeded, and the Roman advance was broken upon the hidden knights.

## The Fellowship of Knights

Bedivere appears among the honoured roll of Arthur's knights in later chapters. His name is recorded alongside the greatest of the Round Table: "Sir Kay, Sir Bedivere, Sir Meliot de Logres" (154-015) and others who constituted the flower of Arthurian chivalry. His inclusion in this catalogue of worthies confirms his enduring place in the fellowship.

In tournament, Bedivere continued to prove his valour, though not without cost. The chronicle notes that "Sir Bedivere was smitten to the earth" (155-014) in a fierce encounter — a reminder that even the king's trusted commanders faced defeat as well as victory in the lists.

## A Knight of the Vanguard

Bedivere's role in the chronicle is that of the reliable commander — the knight who is called to council, entrusted with ambush, and placed in the vanguard. He was not a solitary adventurer pursuing grails and giants, but a soldier-knight whose service was rendered in the heat of battle and the councils of war. In this, he represents a distinct type of Round Table knight: the dependable companion whose worth is proved in campaign, not in solitary quest.
