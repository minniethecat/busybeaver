# Sir Kay
**Sir Kay** the [[seneschal|Seneschal]] is [[king-arthur|King Arthur]]'s foster-brother and the [[seneschal|steward]] of the [[round-table|Round Table]] in Thomas Malory's *Le Morte d'[[king-arthur|Arthur]]*. Often portrayed as boastful and occasionally churlish, Kay is nevertheless a capable and loyal servant of [[camelot|Arthur's court]] whose administrative skill enables the chivalric enterprise.

## The Seneschal
Kay's role as seneschal places him at the operational center of [[camelot|Arthur's court]]. He manages feasts, arranges tournaments, and is frequently mentioned among the knights of the [[round-table|Round Table]]. His practical competence frees [[king-arthur|Arthur]] and the greater knights to pursue quests and adventures.

## Foil and Function
Kay often serves as narrative foil — his failures in [[battle|combat]] highlight the [[courage|prowess]] of greater knights, and his sharp tongue provides comic relief. Yet Malory never presents Kay as contemptible; he is a loyal knight who does his duty, and his inclusion among [[round-table|the Round Table fellowship]] is never questioned.
