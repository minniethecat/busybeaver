# King Mark
**King Mark** is the [[kinsman|kin]]g of [[cornwall|Cornwall]] in Thomas Malory's *Le Morte d'[[king-arthur|Arthur]]*, the uncle of [[sir-tristram|Sir Tristram]], and the husband of La Beale Isoud. His [[envy|jealousy]] to[[battle|war]]d [[sir-tristram|Tristram]] — both personal and political — drives one of the longest narrative arcs in the Morte.

## The Jealous King
Mark is defined primarily by his suspicion and [[envy|resentment]] of [[sir-tristram|Tristram]]. As [[kinsman|kin]]g, he fears [[sir-tristram|Tristram]]'s military [[courage|prowess]] and popularity; as husband, he resents [[sir-tristram|Tristram]]'s love for Isoud. This dual [[envy|jealousy]] makes Mark one of Malory's most complex antagonists — a legitimate [[king|sovereign]] whose legitimate grievances curdle into [[treason|treachery]].

## Political and Personal Betrayal
Mark oscillates between reconciliation and attempted murder of [[sir-tristram|Tristram]] throughout the narrative. His inability to either trust or destroy his nephew makes him a figure of pathos as well as villainy. The [[realm|kingdom]] of [[cornwall|Cornwall]] under Mark's rule becomes a dark mirror of [[king-arthur|Arthur]]'s [[camelot|Camelot]] — a court where suspicion replaces fellowship.
