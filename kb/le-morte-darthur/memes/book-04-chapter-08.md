[074-001]and he took upon him to do battle against [[king-arthur|Arthur]].

[074-002]Now turn we unto Accolon of Gaul, that when he awoke he found himself by a deep

[074-003]well-side, within half a foot, in great peril of death. And there came out of

[074-004]that fountain a pipe of silver, and out of that pipe ran water all on high in a

[074-005]stone of marble. When Sir Accolon saw this, he blessed him and said, Jesus save

[074-006]my lord King Arthur, and King Uriens, for these damosels in this ship have

[074-007]betrayed us, they were devils and no women; and if I may escape this

[074-008]misadventure, I shall destroy all where I may find these false damosels that

[074-009]use enchantments. Right with that there came a dwarf with a great mouth and a

[074-010]flat nose, and saluted Sir Accolon, and said how he came from Queen [[morgan-le-fay|Morgan]] le

[074-011]Fay, and she greeteth you well, and biddeth you be of strong heart, for ye

[074-012]shall [[battle|fight]] to morrow with a knight at the hour of prime, and therefore she

[074-013]hath sent you here [[sword-in-the-stone|Excalibur]], Arthur’s sword, and the scabbard, and she

[074-014]biddeth you as ye love her, that ye do the battle to the uttermost, without any

[074-015]mercy, like as ye had promised her when ye spake together in privity; and what

[074-016]damosel that bringeth her the knight’s head, which ye shall fight withal,

[074-017]she will make her a queen. Now I understand you well, said Accolon, I shall

[074-018]hold that I have promised her now I have the sword: when saw ye my lady Queen

[074-019]Morgan le Fay? Right late, said the dwarf. Then Accolon took him in his arms

[074-020]and said, Recommend me unto my lady queen, and tell her all shall be done that

[074-021]I have promised her, and else I will die for it. Now I suppose, said Accolon,

[074-022]she hath made all these crafts and enchantments for this battle. Ye may well

[074-023]believe it, said the dwarf. Right so there came a knight and a lady with six

[074-024]squires, and saluted Accolon, and prayed him for to arise, and come and rest

[074-025]him at his manor. And so Accolon mounted upon a void horse, and went with the

[074-026]knight unto a fair manor by a priory, and there he had passing good cheer.

[074-027]Then Sir Damas sent unto his brother Sir Ontzlake, and bade make him ready by

[074-028]to-morn at the hour of prime, and to be in the field to fight with a good

[074-029]knight, for he had found a good knight that was ready to do battle at all

[074-030]points. When this word came unto Sir Ontzlake he was passing heavy, for he was

[074-031]wounded a little to-fore through both his thighs with a spear, and made great

[074-032]dole; but as he was wounded, he would have taken the battle on hand. So it

[074-033]happed at that time, by the means of Morgan le Fay, Accolon was with Sir

[074-034]Ontzlake lodged; and when he heard of that battle, and how Ontzlake was

[074-035]wounded, he said that he would fight for him. Because Morgan le Fay had sent

[074-036]him Excalibur and the sheath for to fight with the knight on the morn: this was

[074-037]the cause Sir Accolon took the battle on hand. Then Sir Ontzlake was passing

[074-038]glad, and thanked Sir Accolon with all his heart that he would do so much for

[074-039]him. And therewithal Sir Ontzlake sent word unto his brother Sir Damas, that he

[074-040]had a knight that for him should be ready in the field by the hour of prime.

[074-041]So on the morn Sir Arthur was armed and well horsed, and asked Sir Damas, When

[074-042]shall we to the field? Sir, said Sir Damas, ye shall hear mass. And so Arthur

[074-043]heard a mass, and when mass was done there came a squire on a great horse, and

[074-044]asked Sir Damas if his knight were ready, for our knight is ready in the field.

[074-045]Then Sir Arthur mounted upon horseback, and there were all the knights and

[074-046]commons of that country; and so by all advices there were chosen twelve good

[074-047]men of the country for to wait upon the two knights. And right as Arthur was on

[074-048]horseback there came a damosel from Morgan le Fay, and brought unto Sir Arthur

[074-049]a sword like unto Excalibur, and the scabbard, and said unto Arthur, Morgan le

[074-050]Fay sendeth here your sword for great love. And he thanked her, and weened it

[074-051]had been so, but she was false, for the sword and the scabbard was counterfeit,

[074-052]and brittle, and false.
