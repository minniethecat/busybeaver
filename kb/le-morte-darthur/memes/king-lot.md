# King Lot

King Lot of Lothian and of Orkney was one of the most powerful regional kings in the early years of [[King Arthur]]'s reign. A formidable warrior who initially opposed Arthur's claim to the throne, Lot fought among the rebel kings at the great battle of Bedegraine before ultimately swearing fealty and becoming a sworn knight of the [[Round Table]]. He was the husband of [[Morgawse]], Arthur's half-sister, and through her the father of several notable knights.

## The Rebellion Against Arthur

When Arthur was crowned king following his drawing of the sword from the stone, many of the established kings of Britain refused to acknowledge his sovereignty. Lot was among the most prominent of these dissenting monarchs. At the great feast where Arthur assembled the realm, Lot arrived with five hundred knights and openly laughed Arthur's claim to scorn, declaring the young king unworthy to rule over him and his peers (011-004).

When [[Merlin]] counselled Arthur on the coming war, Lot dismissed the prophet as a mere dream-reader, defying even the supernatural authority that backed Arthur's reign (012-012). Lot then broke out from the parley and prepared for battle against the royal forces (012-022).

## The Battle of Bedegraine

At the great battle of Bedegraine, Lot proved himself a formidable adversary. He struck down King Arthur himself in the course of the fighting, unhorsing the young sovereign with a powerful spear-thrust (012-026). He also smote down King Lot's peer King Nentres and Clariance de la Forest Savage (017-046). But Lot himself was sore wounded in the battle and struck from his horse by the same spear that had unhorsed him (017-036). Despite his ferocity, the day turned against the rebel kings. When he saw King Bors of Gaul arriving with fresh forces, Lot recognised the hopelessness of their cause: "we must be discomfited, for yonder cometh one of the most worshipful kings and best knights of the world" (019-002). Lot wept at the slaughter of his people (019-007).

## Fealty and Family

After the defeat of the rebellion, Lot swore fealty to Arthur and was recorded among the kings who pledged their service to the new sovereign. He was a "passing good knight" who took his place among the sworn lords of the Round Table (015-016). Lot's wife was Morgawse of Orkney, who came to Arthur's court after the war — a visit that would have profound consequences, for she was Arthur's half-sister, and from their union came [[Mordred]] (022-003).

## The War with Nero

Lot's loyalty to Arthur was tested again during the war with King Nero. When Merlin came to Lot and urged him to delay his advance until Arthur could bring up his main force, Lot's army was attacked in his absence. A messenger came to Lot and told him that while he tarried, his people had been assailed and his host destroyed (041-016). Lot lamented his shame at not being present with his army, recognising that had he been there with his body of knights, the outcome would have been different (041-017).
