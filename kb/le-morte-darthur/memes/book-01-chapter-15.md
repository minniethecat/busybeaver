[018-001]Then Lucas saw King Agwisance, that late had slain Moris de la Roche, and Lucas

[018-002]ran to him with a short spear that was great, that he gave him such a fall,

[018-003]that the horse fell down to the earth. Also Lucas found there on foot, Bloias

[018-004]de La Flandres, and Sir Gwinas, two hardy knights, and in that woodness that

[018-005]Lucas was in, he slew two bachelors and horsed them again. Then waxed the

[018-006]battle passing hard on both parties, but [[king-arthur|Arthur]] was glad that his knights were

[018-007]horsed again, and then they fought together, that the noise and sound rang by

[018-008]the water and the wood. Wherefore King Ban and King [[sir-bors|Bors]] made them ready, and

[018-009]dressed their shields and harness, and they were so courageous that many

[018-010]knights shook and bevered for eagerness. All this while Lucas, and Gwinas, and

[018-011]Briant, and Bellias of Flanders, held strong medley against six kings, that was

[018-012]King Lot, King Nentres, King Brandegoris, King Idres, King Uriens, and King

[018-013]Agwisance. So with the help of [[sir-kay|Sir Kay]] and of Sir Griflet they held these six

[018-014]kings hard, that unnethe they had any power to defend them. But when Sir Arthur

[018-015]saw the battle would not be ended by no manner, he fared wood as a lion, and

[018-016]steered his horse here and there, on the right hand, and on the left hand, that

[018-017]he stinted not till he had slain twenty knights. Also he wounded King Lot sore

[018-018]on the shoulder, and made him to leave that ground, for Sir Kay and Griflet did

[018-019]with King Arthur there great deeds of arms. Then Ulfius, and Brastias, and Sir

[018-020]Ector encountered against the Duke Eustace, and King Cradelment, and King

[018-021]Clariance of Northumberland, and King Carados, and against the King with the

[018-022]Hundred Knights. So these knights encountered with these kings, that they made

[018-023]them to avoid the ground. Then King Lot made great dole for his damages and his

[018-024]fellows, and said unto the ten kings, But if ye will do as I devise we shall be

[018-025]slain and destroyed; let me have the King with the Hundred Knights, and King

[018-026]Agwisance, and King Idres, and the Duke of Cambenet, and we five kings will

[018-027]have fifteen thousand men of arms with us, and we will go apart while ye six

[018-028]kings hold medley with twelve thousand; an we see that ye have foughten with

[018-029]them long, then will we come on fiercely, and else shall we never match them,

[018-030]said King Lot, but by this mean. So they departed as they here devised, and six

[018-031]kings made their party strong against Arthur, and made great [[battle|war]] long.

[018-032]In the meanwhile brake the ambushment of King Ban and King Bors, and Lionses

[018-033]and Phariance had the vanguard, and they two knights met with King Idres and

[018-034]his fellowship, and there began a great medley of breaking of spears, and

[018-035]smiting of swords, with slaying of men and horses, and King Idres was near at

[018-036]discomforture.

[018-037]That saw Agwisance the king, and put Lionses and Phariance in point of death;

[018-038]for the Duke of Cambenet came on withal with a great fellowship. So these two

[018-039]knights were in great danger of their lives that they were fain to return, but

[018-040]always they rescued themselves and their fellowship marvellously. When King

[018-041]Bors saw those knights put aback, it grieved him sore; then he came on so fast

[018-042]that his fellowship seemed as black as Inde. When King Lot had espied King

[018-043]Bors, he knew him well, then he said, O Jesu, defend us from death and horrible

[018-044]maims! for I see well we be in great peril of death; for I see yonder a king,

[018-045]one of the most worshipfullest men and one of the best knights of the world, is

[018-046]inclined unto his fellowship. What is he? said the King with the Hundred

[018-047]Knights. It is, said King Lot, King Bors of Gaul; I marvel how they came into

[018-048]this country without witting of us all. It was by [[merlin|Merlin]]’s advice, said

[018-049]the knight. As for him, said King Carados, I will encounter with King Bors, an

[018-050]ye will rescue me when myster is. Go on, said they all, we will do all that we

[018-051]may. Then King Carados and his host rode on a soft pace, till that they came as

[018-052]nigh King Bors as bow-draught; then either battle let their horse run as fast

[018-053]as they might. And Bleoberis, that was godson unto King Bors, he bare his chief

[018-054]standard, that was a passing good knight. Now shall we see, said King Bors, how

[018-055]these northern Britons can bear the arms: and King Bors encountered with a

[018-056]knight, and smote him throughout with a spear that he fell dead unto the earth;

[018-057]and after drew his sword and did [[marvel|marvellous]] deeds of arms, that all parties had

[018-058]great wonder thereof; and his knights failed not, but did their part, and King

[018-059]Carados was smitten to the earth. With that came the King with the Hundred

[018-060]Knights and rescued King Carados mightily by force of arms, for he was a

[018-061]passing good knight of a king, and but a young man.
