[080-001]sorrow for the death of Accolon, and how she stole away the scabbard from

[080-002][[king-arthur|Arthur]].

[080-003]Then came tidings unto [[morgan-le-fay|Morgan le Fay]] that Accolon was dead, and his body

[080-004]brought unto the church, and how King Arthur had his sword again. But when

[080-005]Queen Morgan wist that Accolon was dead, she was so sorrowful that near her

[080-006]heart to-brast. But because she would not it were known, outward she kept her

[080-007]countenance, and made no semblant of sorrow. But well she wist an she abode

[080-008]till her brother Arthur came thither, there should no gold go for her life.

[080-009]Then she went unto [[queen-guenever|Queen Guenever]], and asked her leave to ride into the

[080-010]country. Ye may abide, said Queen Guenever, till your brother the king come

[080-011]home. I may not, said Morgan le Fay, for I have such hasty tidings, that I may

[080-012]not tarry. Well, said Guenever, ye may depart when ye will. So early on the

[080-013]morn, or it was day, she took her horse and rode all that day and most part of

[080-014]the night, and on the morn by noon she came to the same abbey of nuns whereas

[080-015]lay King Arthur; and she knowing he was there, she asked where he was. And they

[080-016]answered how he had laid him in his bed to sleep, for he had had but little

[080-017]rest these three nights. Well, said she, I charge you that none of you awake

[080-018]him till I do, and then she alighted off her horse, and thought for to steal

[080-019]away [[sword-in-the-stone|Excalibur]] his sword, and so she went straight unto his chamber, and no man

[080-020]durst disobey her commandment, and there she found Arthur asleep in his bed,

[080-021]and Excalibur in his right hand naked. When she saw that she was passing heavy

[080-022]that she might not come by the sword without she had awaked him, and then she

[080-023]wist well she had been dead. Then she took the scabbard and went her way on

[080-024]horseback. When the king awoke and missed his scabbard, he was wroth, and he

[080-025]asked who had been there, and they said his sister, Queen Morgan had been

[080-026]there, and had put the scabbard under her mantle and was gone. Alas, said

[080-027]Arthur, falsely ye have watched me. Sir, said they all, we durst not disobey

[080-028]your sister’s commandment. Ah, said the king, let fetch the best horse

[080-029]may be found, and bid Sir Ontzlake arm him in all haste, and take another good

[080-030]horse and ride with me. So anon the king and Ontzlake were well armed, and rode

[080-031]after this lady, and so they came by a cross and found a cowherd, and they

[080-032]asked the poor man if there came any lady riding that way. Sir, said this poor

[080-033]man, right late came a lady riding with a forty horses, and to yonder forest

[080-034]she rode. Then they spurred their horses, and followed fast, and within a while

[080-035]Arthur had a sight of Morgan le Fay; then he chased as fast as he might. When

[080-036]she espied him following her, she rode a greater pace through the forest till

[080-037]she came to a plain, and when she saw she might not escape, she rode unto a

[080-038]lake thereby, and said, Whatsoever come of me, my brother shall not have this

[080-039]scabbard. And then she let throw the scabbard in the deepest of the water so it

[080-040]sank, for it was heavy of gold and precious stones.

[080-041]Then she rode into a valley where many great stones were, and when she saw she

[080-042]must be overtaken, she shaped herself, horse and man, by enchantment unto a

[080-043]great marble stone. Anon withal came Sir Arthur and Sir Ontzlake whereas the

[080-044]king might know his sister and her men, and one knight from another. Ah, said

[080-045]the king, here may ye see the vengeance of God, and now am I sorry that this

[080-046]misadventure is befallen. And then he looked for the scabbard, but it would not

[080-047]be found, so he returned to the abbey where he came from. So when Arthur was

[080-048]gone she turned all into the likeliness as she and they were before, and said,

[080-049]Sirs, now may we go where we will.
