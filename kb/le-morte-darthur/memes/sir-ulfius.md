# Sir Ulfius

Sir Ulfius was a noble knight who served under both King Uther Pendragon and King Arthur. He played a key role in the union of Uther and Igraine, and later became chamberlain of England under Arthur's reign.

## The Knight of Uther's Court

Sir Ulfius first appears at the court of King Uther Pendragon, where he served as a trusted knight. When Uther fell ill from anger and love for Igraine, the Duchess of Cornwall, it was Sir Ulfius who came to the king and learned the cause of his sickness (004-035). At the king's request, Ulfius undertook to seek Merlin, promising that nothing reasonable would be denied if the wizard could remedy the king's malady (004-037).

Ulfius departed in search of Merlin and by adventure met him in a beggar's array. Merlin revealed his identity, and Ulfius conveyed the king's offer of reward. Merlin agreed to fulfil Uther's desire on condition that the king swear to grant his wish (004-037).

## The Tintagil Deception

When Merlin devised his plan to transform Uther into the likeness of the Duke of Tintagil so the king might lie with Igraine, Ulfius was given a critical role in the enchantment. Merlin declared that Ulfius would be transformed into the likeness of Sir Brastias, a knight of the duke's household, while Merlin himself took the form of Sir Jordanus, another of the duke's knights. The scheme succeeded, and that night Arthur was conceived.

## Peacemaker and Chamberlain

After the Duke of Tintagil's death, King Uther placed all his trust in Ulfius to negotiate peace with Igraine. Ulfius mediated between them, and at the last the king and she met together (010-021). Ulfius spoke warmly of the match, saying their king was a lusty knight and wifeless, while Igraine was a passing fair lady — it would be great joy if the king made her his queen (010-021). The counsel was well received, and Uther and Igraine were married with great mirth and joy.

Ulfius also proved himself in battle. At St. Albans, he and Sir Brastias did great deeds of arms, and King Uther's men overcame the Northern battle, slaying many and putting the remnant to flight (010-021).

## Chamberlain Under King Arthur

After Arthur was crowned king and had established the realm, he appointed his chief officers. Sir Ulfius was made chamberlain of England, one of the highest offices of the royal household (010-021). He was among the knights whom Uther Pendragon had loved and trusted most in his days, placed about young Arthur along with Sir Baudwin of Britain, Sir Kay, and Sir Brastias (009-040).

When Arthur had to deal with the rebel kings, Ulfius and Brastias were made messengers, riding forth well horsed and well armed to deliver the king's letters in the pleasant wise according to Arthur's desire (017-024).
