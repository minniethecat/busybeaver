# Sir Petipase

Sir Petipase of Winchelsea was a knight who appears in the tournament narratives of [[Le Morte d'Arthur]]. His name is recorded in the lists of combatants at the great Tournament of the Castle of Maidens, where he encountered [[Sir Sadok]] in the lists.

## The Knight of Winchelsea

The chronicle first names Petipase during an encounter on the road: "my name is, said the other, Sir Petipase of Winchelsea" (059-025). Winchelsea, a port town on the Sussex coast, gives Petipase a geographical anchor — one of the few Arthurian knights associated with a specific English town rather than a kingdom, duchy, or castle. This precision of origin places him among the gentry rather than the high nobility, a knight of local rather than regional standing.

## The Tournament of the Castle of Maidens

Petipase's most substantial appearance comes late in the chronicle, at the Tournament of the Castle of Maidens. The roll-call of combatants lists him among notable company: "Sir Bedivere, Sir Meliot de Logres, Sir Petipase of Winchelsea" (154-015). To be named alongside [[Sir Bedivere]], one of the most loyal knights of the [[Round Table]], was a mark of some standing — though Petipase occupied the margins of the great lists rather than their centre.

The tournament itself was one of the grand set-pieces of the latter books, a gathering of knights from across Britain that tested the prowess of all who entered. For Petipase, it provided the stage on which his brief moment of narrative visibility unfolded.

## The Encounter with Sir Sadok

In the press of the tournament, Petipase met [[Sir Sadok]] in the lists. The chronicle records the encounter with blunt economy: "Sir Sadok encountered with Sir Petipase, and there Sir Sadok was discomfited" (155-015). The verb "discomfited" — a word that in Malory's usage carries the weight of decisive defeat — marked the end of Petipase's recorded deeds.

The encounter between Petipase and Sadok is characteristic of the minor tournament exchanges that fill the later books of [[Le Morte d'Arthur]]: brief, decisive meetings between knights whose names flicker across the page and then recede, leaving only the record of a lance broken and a rider unhorsed.

## The Minor Knight

Sir Petipase of Winchelsea belongs to the large class of Arthurian knights whose names survive in the chronicle but whose stories do not. His Winchelsea origin, his presence at the Tournament of the Castle of Maidens, and his encounter with Sir Sadok are the sum of what the chronicle preserves. Yet names like Petipase's are essential to the texture of [[Le Morte d'Arthur]]: they populate the lists, fill the tournament fields, and give the great set-pieces their sense of scale and abundance. The [[Round Table]] was not only [[Sir Launcelot]] and [[Sir Tristram]] — it was also Sir Petipase of Winchelsea, a knight of a Sussex port who rode in the lists and took his fall like all the rest.
