[012-001]and how he won the [[battle|field]].

[012-002]Then [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] came out of his tower, and had under his gown a jesseraunt of

[012-003]double mail, and there went with him the Archbishop of Canterbury, and Sir

[012-004]Baudwin of Britain, and [[sir-kay|Sir Kay]], and Sir Brastias: these were the men of most

[012-005][[honour|worship]] that were with him. And when they were met there was no meekness, but

[012-006]stout words on both sides; but always King Arthur answered them, and said he

[012-007]would make them to bow an he lived. Wherefore they departed with wrath, and

[012-008]King Arthur bade keep them well, and they bade the king keep him well. So the

[012-009]king returned him to the tower again and armed him and all his knights. What

[012-010]will ye do? said [[merlin|Merlin]] to the kings; ye were better for to stint, for ye shall

[012-011]not here prevail though ye were ten times so many. Be we well advised to be

[012-012]afeared of a dream-reader? said King Lot. With that Merlin vanished away, and

[012-013]came to King Arthur, and bade him set on them fiercely; and in the meanwhile

[012-014]there were three hundred good men, of the best that were with the kings, that

[012-015]went straight unto King Arthur, and that comforted him greatly. Sir, said

[012-016]Merlin to Arthur, fight not with the sword that ye had by [[marvel|miracle]], till that ye

[012-017]see ye go unto the worse, then draw it out and do your best. So forthwithal

[012-018]King Arthur set upon them in their lodging. And Sir Baudwin, Sir Kay, and Sir

[012-019]Brastias slew on the right hand and on the left hand that it was marvel; and

[012-020]always King Arthur on horseback laid on with a sword, and did marvellous deeds

[012-021]of arms, that many of the kings had great joy of his deeds and [[courage|hardiness]].

[012-022]Then King Lot brake out on the back side, and the King with the Hundred

[012-023]Knights, and King Carados, and set on Arthur fiercely behind him. With that Sir

[012-024]Arthur turned with his knights, and smote behind and before, and ever Sir

[012-025]Arthur was in the foremost press till his horse was slain underneath him. And

[012-026]therewith King Lot smote down King Arthur. With that his four knights received

[012-027]him and set him on horseback. Then he drew his sword [[sword-in-the-stone|Excalibur]], but it was so

[012-028]bright in his enemies’ eyes, that it gave light like thirty torches. And

[012-029]therewith he put them a-back, and slew much people. And then the commons of

[012-030][[camelot|Carlion]] arose with clubs and staves and slew many knights; but all the kings

[012-031]held them together with their knights that were left alive, and so fled and

[012-032]departed. And Merlin came unto Arthur, and counselled him to follow them no

[012-033]further.
