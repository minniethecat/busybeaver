[024-001][[king-arthur|Arthur]]’s mother, of treason; and how a knight came and desired to have

[024-002]the death of his master revenged.

[024-003]Right so came Ulfius, and said openly, that the king and all might hear that

[024-004]were feasted that day, Ye are the falsest lady of the world, and the most

[024-005]traitress unto the king’s person. Beware, said Arthur, what thou sayest;

[024-006]thou speakest a great word. I am well ware, said Ulfius, what I speak, and here

[024-007]is my glove to prove it upon any man that will say the contrary, that this

[024-008]Queen Igraine is causer of your great damage, and of your great [[battle|war]]. For, an

[024-009]she would have uttered it in the life of King Uther Pendragon, of the birth of

[024-010]you, and how ye were begotten ye had never had the mortal wars that ye have

[024-011]had; for the most part of your barons of your realm knew never whose son ye

[024-012]were, nor of whom ye were begotten; and she that bare you of her body should

[024-013]have made it known openly in excusing of her [[honour|worship]] and yours, and in like

[024-014]wise to all the realm, wherefore I prove her false to God and to you and to all

[024-015]your realm, and who will say the contrary I will prove it on his body.

[024-016]Then spake Igraine and said, I am a woman and I may not fight, but rather than

[024-017]I should be dishonoured, there would some good man take my quarrel. More, she

[024-018]said, [[merlin|Merlin]] knoweth well, and ye Sir Ulfius, how King Uther came to me in the

[024-019]Castle of Tintagil in the likeness of my lord, that was dead three hours

[024-020]to-fore, and thereby gat a child that night upon me. And after the thirteenth

[024-021]day King Uther wedded me, and by his commandment when the child was born it was

[024-022]delivered unto Merlin and nourished by him, and so I saw the child never after,

[024-023]nor wot not what is his name, for I knew him never yet. And there, Ulfius said

[024-024]to the queen, Merlin is more to blame than ye. Well I wot, said the queen, I

[024-025]bare a child by my lord King Uther, but I wot not where he is become. Then

[024-026]Merlin took the king by the hand, saying, This is your mother. And therewith

[024-027]Sir Ector bare witness how he nourished him by Uther’s commandment. And

[024-028]therewith King Arthur took his mother, Queen Igraine, in his arms and kissed

[024-029]her, and either wept upon other. And then the king let make a feast that lasted

[024-030]eight days.

[024-031]Then on a day there came in the court a squire on horseback, leading a knight

[024-032]before him wounded to the death, and told him how there was a knight in the

[024-033]forest had reared up a pavilion by a well, and hath slain my master, a good

[024-034]knight, his name was Miles; wherefore I beseech you that my master may be

[024-035]buried, and that some knight may revenge my master’s death. Then the

[024-036]noise was great of that knight’s death in the court, and every man said

[024-037]his advice. Then came Griflet that was but a squire, and he was but young, of

[024-038]the age of the king Arthur, so he besought the king for all his service that he

[024-039]had done him to give the order of knighthood.
