[151-001]demanded of them where [[sir-gareth|Sir Gareth]] was.

[151-002]Then goodly they said all at once, God forgive you, and we do, and pray you

[151-003]that ye will tell us where we may find Sir Beaumains. Fair lords, said Sir

[151-004]Ironside, I cannot tell you, for it is full hard to find him; for such young

[151-005]knights as he is one, when they be in their adventures be never abiding in no

[151-006]place. But to say the [[honour|worship]] that the Red Knight of the Red Launds, and Sir

[151-007]Persant and his brother said of Beaumains, it was marvel to hear. Well, my fair

[151-008]lords, said [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], wit you well I shall do you honour for the love of Sir

[151-009]Beaumains, and as soon as ever I meet with him I shall make you all upon one

[151-010]day knights of the [[round-table|Table Round]]. And as to thee, Sir Persant of Inde, thou hast

[151-011]been ever called a full noble knight, and so have ever been thy three brethren

[151-012]called. But I marvel, said the king, that I hear not of the Black Knight your

[151-013]brother, he was a full noble knight. Sir, said Pertolepe, the Green Knight, Sir

[151-014]Beaumains slew him in a recounter with his spear, his name was Sir Percard.

[151-015]That was great [[mercy|pity]], said the king, and so said many knights. For these four

[151-016]brethren were full well known in [[camelot|the court of King Arthur]] for noble knights,

[151-017]for long time they had holden [[battle|war]] against the knights of the Round Table. Then

[151-018]said Pertolepe, the Green Knight, to the king: At a passage of the water of

[151-019]Mortaise there encountered Sir Beaumains with two brethren that ever for the

[151-020]most part kept that passage, and they were two deadly knights, and there he

[151-021]slew the eldest brother in the water, and smote him upon the head such a buffet

[151-022]that he fell down in the water, and there he was drowned, and his name was Sir

[151-023]Gherard le Breusse; and after he slew the other brother upon the land, his name

[151-024]was Sir Arnold le Breusse.
