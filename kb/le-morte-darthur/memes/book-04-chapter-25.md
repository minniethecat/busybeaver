[091-002]Then came the four sons by couple, and two of them brake their spears, and so

[091-003]did the other two. And all this while Sir Marhaus touched them not. Then Sir

[091-004]Marhaus ran to the duke, and smote him with his spear that horse and man fell

[091-005]to the earth, and so he served his sons; and then Sir Marhaus alighted down and

[091-006]bade the duke yield him or else he would slay him. And then some of his sons

[091-007]recovered, and would have set upon Sir Marhaus; then Sir Marhaus said to the

[091-008]duke, Cease thy sons, or else I will do the uttermost to you all. Then the duke

[091-009]saw he might not escape the death, he cried to his sons, and charged them to

[091-010]yield them to Sir Marhaus; and they kneeled all down and put the pommels of

[091-011]their swords to the knight, and so he received them. And then they helped up

[091-012]their father, and so by their cominal assent promised to Sir Marhaus never to

[091-013]be foes unto [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], and thereupon at Whitsuntide after to come, he and

[091-014]his sons, and put them in the king’s grace.

[091-015]Then Sir Marhaus departed, and within two days his damosel brought him whereas

[091-016]was a great tournament that the Lady de Vawse had cried. And who that did best

[091-017]should have a rich circlet of gold worth a thousand besants. And there Sir

[091-018]Marhaus did so nobly that he was renowned, and had sometime down forty knights,

[091-019]and so the circlet of gold was rewarded him. Then he departed from them with

[091-020]great [[honour|worship]]; and so within seven nights his damosel brought him to an

[091-021]earl’s place, his name was the Earl Fergus, that after was Sir

[091-022][[sir-tristram|Tristram]]’s knight; and this earl was but a young man, and late come into

[091-023]his lands, and there was a giant fast by him that hight Taulurd, and he had

[091-024]another brother in Cornwall that hight Taulas, that Sir Tristram slew when he

[091-025]was out of his mind. So this earl made his complaint unto Sir Marhaus, that

[091-026]there was a giant by him that destroyed all his lands, and how he durst nowhere

[091-027]ride nor go for him. Sir, said the knight, whether useth he to [[battle|fight]] on

[091-028]horseback or on foot? Nay, said the earl, there may no horse bear him. Well,

[091-029]said Sir Marhaus, then will I fight with him on foot; so on the morn Sir

[091-030]Marhaus prayed the earl that one of his men might bring him whereas the giant

[091-031]was; and so he was, for he saw him sit under a tree of holly, and many clubs of

[091-032]iron and gisarms about him. So this knight dressed him to the giant, putting

[091-033]his shield afore him, and the giant took an iron club in his hand, and at the

[091-034]first stroke he clave Sir Marhaus’ shield in two pieces. And there he was

[091-035]in great peril, for the giant was a wily fighter, but at last Sir Marhaus smote

[091-036]off his right arm above the elbow.

[091-037]Then the giant fled and the knight after him, and so he drove him into a water,

[091-038]but the giant was so high that he might not wade after him. And then Sir

[091-039]Marhaus made the Earl Fergus’ man to fetch him stones, and with those

[091-040]stones the knight gave the giant many sore knocks, till at the last he made him

[091-041]fall down into the water, and so was he there dead. Then Sir Marhaus went unto

[091-042]the giant’s castle, and there he delivered twenty-four ladies and twelve

[091-043]knights out of the giant’s prison, and there he had great riches without

[091-044]number, so that the days of his life he was never poor man. Then he returned to

[091-045]the Earl Fergus, the which thanked him greatly, and would have given him half

[091-046]his lands, but he would none take. So Sir Marhaus dwelled with the earl nigh

[091-047]half a year, for he was sore bruised with the giant, and at the last he took

[091-048]his leave. And as he rode by the way, he met with [[sir-gawaine|Sir Gawaine]] and Sir Uwaine,

[091-049]and so by adventure he met with four knights of Arthur’s court, the first

[091-050]was Sir Sagramore le Desirous, Sir Osanna, Sir Dodinas le Savage, and Sir Felot

[091-051]of Listinoise; and there Sir Marhaus with one spear smote down these four

[091-052]knights, and hurt them sore. So he departed to meet at his day aforeset.
