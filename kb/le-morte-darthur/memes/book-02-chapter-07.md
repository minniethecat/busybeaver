[038-001]death of Lanceor, and how [[king-mark|King Mark of Cornwall]] found them, and made a tomb

[038-002]over them.

[038-003]Now go we hence, said Balin, and well be we met. The meanwhile as they talked,

[038-004]there came a dwarf from the city of [[camelot|Camelot]] on horseback, as much as he might;

[038-005]and found the dead bodies, wherefore he made great dole, and pulled out his

[038-006]hair for sorrow, and said, Which of you knights have done this deed? Whereby

[038-007]askest thou it? said Balan. For I would wit it, said the dwarf. It was I, said

[038-008]Balin, that slew this knight in my defence, for hither he came to chase me, and

[038-009]either I must slay him or he me; and this damosel slew herself for his love,

[038-010]which repenteth me, and for her sake I shall owe all women the better love.

[038-011]Alas, said the dwarf, thou hast done great damage unto thyself, for this knight

[038-012]that is here dead was one of the most valiantest men that lived, and trust

[038-013]well, Balin, the kin of this knight will chase you through the world till they

[038-014]have slain you. As for that, said Balin, I fear not greatly, but I am right

[038-015]heavy that I have displeased my lord [[king-arthur|King Arthur]], for the death of this knight.

[038-016]So as they talked together, there came a king of Cornwall riding, the which

[038-017]hight King Mark. And when he saw these two bodies dead, and understood how they

[038-018]were dead, by the two knights above said, then made the king great sorrow for

[038-019]the [[love|true love]] that was betwixt them, and said, I will not depart till I have on

[038-020]this earth made a tomb, and there he pight his pavilions and sought through all

[038-021]the country to find a tomb, and in a church they found one was fair and rich,

[038-022]and then the king let put them both in the earth, and put the tomb upon them,

[038-023]and wrote the names of them both on the tomb. How here lieth Lanceor the

[038-024]king’s son of Ireland, that at his own request was slain by the hands of

[038-025]Balin; and how his lady, Colombe, and paramour, slew herself with her

[038-026]love’s sword for dole and sorrow.
