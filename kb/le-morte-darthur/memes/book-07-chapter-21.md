[148-001][[sir-gareth|Beaumains]], came to the presence of his lady, and how they took acquaintance,

[148-002]and of their love.

[148-003]And then came forth Dame Lionesse arrayed like a princess, and there she made

[148-004]him passing good cheer, and he her again; and they had goodly language and

[148-005]lovely countenance together. And Sir Gareth thought many times, Jesu, would

[148-006]that the lady of the Castle Perilous were so fair as she was. There were all

[148-007]manner of games and plays, of dancing and singing. And ever the more Sir Gareth

[148-008]beheld that lady, the more he loved her; and so he burned in love that he was

[148-009]past himself in his reason; and forth toward night they yede unto supper, and

[148-010]Sir Gareth might not eat, for his love was so hot that he wist not where he

[148-011]was.

[148-012]All these looks espied Sir Gringamore, and then at-after supper he called his

[148-013]sister Dame Lionesse into a chamber, and said: Fair sister, I have well espied

[148-014]your countenance betwixt you and this knight, and I will, sister, that ye wit

[148-015]he is a full noble knight, and if ye can make him to abide here I will do him

[148-016]all the pleasure that I can, for an ye were better than ye are, ye were well

[148-017]bywaryd upon him. Fair brother, said Dame Lionesse, I understand well that the

[148-018]knight is good, and come he is of a noble house. Notwithstanding, I will assay

[148-019]him better, howbeit I am most beholden to him of any earthly man; for he hath

[148-020]had great labour for my love, and passed many a dangerous passage.

[148-021]Right so Sir Gringamore went unto Sir Gareth, and said, Sir, make ye good

[148-022]cheer, for ye shall have none other cause, for this lady, my sister, is yours

[148-023]at all times, her [[honour|worship]] saved, for wit ye well she loveth you as well as ye

[148-024]do her, and better if better may be. An I wist that, said Sir Gareth, there

[148-025]lived not a gladder man than I would be. Upon my worship, said Sir Gringamore,

[148-026]trust unto my promise; and as long as it liketh you ye shall sojourn with me,

[148-027]and this lady shall be with us daily and nightly to make you all the cheer that

[148-028]she can. I will well, said Sir Gareth, for I have promised to be nigh this

[148-029]country this twelvemonth. And well I am sure [[king-arthur|King Arthur]] and other noble

[148-030]knights will find me where that I am within this twelvemonth. For I shall be

[148-031]sought and found, if that I be alive. And then the noble knight Sir Gareth went

[148-032]unto the Dame Lionesse, which he then much loved, and kissed her many times,

[148-033]and either made great joy of other. And there she promised him her love

[148-034]certainly, to love him and none other the days of her life. Then this lady,

[148-035]Dame Lionesse, by the assent of her brother, told Sir Gareth all the truth what

[148-036]she was, and how she was the same lady that he did battle for, and how she was

[148-037]lady of the Castle Perilous, and there she told him how she caused her brother

[148-038]to take away his dwarf.
