[006-002]Then Queen Igraine waxed daily greater and greater, so it befell after within

[006-003]half a year, as King Uther lay by his queen, he asked her, by the faith she

[006-004]owed to him, whose was the body; then she sore abashed to give answer. Dismay

[006-005]you not, said the king, but tell me the truth, and I shall love you the better,

[006-006]by the faith of my body. Sir, said she, I shall tell you the truth. The same

[006-007]night that my lord was dead, the hour of his death, as his knights record,

[006-008]there came into my castle of Tintagil a man like my lord in speech and in

[006-009]countenance, and two knights with him in likeness of his two knights Brastias

[006-010]and Jordanus, and so I went unto bed with him as I ought to do with my lord,

[006-011]and the same night, as I shall answer unto God, this child was begotten upon

[006-012]me. That is truth, said the king, as ye say; for it was I myself that came in

[006-013]the likeness, and therefore dismay you not, for I am father of the child; and

[006-014]there he told her all the cause, how it was by [[merlin|Merlin]]’s counsel. Then the

[006-015]queen made great joy when she knew who was the father of her child.

[006-016]Soon came Merlin unto the king, and said, Sir, ye must purvey you for the

[006-017]nourishing of your child. As thou wilt, said the king, be it. Well, said

[006-018]Merlin, I know a lord of yours in this land, that is a passing true man and a

[006-019]faithful, and he shall have the nourishing of your child, and his name is Sir

[006-020]Ector, and he is a lord of fair livelihood in many parts in England and Wales;

[006-021]and this lord, Sir Ector, let him be sent for, for to come and speak with you,

[006-022]and desire him yourself, as he loveth you, that he will put his own child to

[006-023]nourishing to another woman, and that his wife nourish yours. And when the

[006-024]child is born let it be delivered to me at yonder privy postern unchristened.

[006-025]So like as Merlin devised it was done. And when Sir Ector was come he made

[006-026]fiaunce to the king for to nourish the child like as the king desired; and

[006-027]there the king granted Sir Ector great rewards. Then when the lady was

[006-028]delivered, the king commanded two knights and two ladies to take the child,

[006-029]bound in a cloth of gold, and that ye deliver him to what poor man ye meet at

[006-030]the postern gate of the castle. So the child was delivered unto Merlin, and so

[006-031]he bare it forth unto Sir Ector, and made an holy man to christen him, and

[006-032]named him [[king-arthur|Arthur]]; and so Sir Ector’s wife nourished him with her own pap.
