# Sir Gringamore

**Sir Gringamore** is a knight who appears in the Tale of [[Sir Gareth]] (Book VII of Malory's *Le Morte d'Arthur*). He is the brother of [[Dame Lionesse]] and initially opposes Gareth's secret courtship of his sister, but ultimately becomes a trusted ally and intermediary between the two lovers.

## Role in the Gareth Story

Sir Gringamore is introduced when [[Dame Lionesse]], having hosted Sir Gareth at her castle and fallen in love with him, calls upon her brother for assistance in managing the delicate situation (146-034). Gringamore arrives armed in all-black armor, accompanied by a dwarf (146-051, 146-054). He assumes a protective stance over his sister, treating Gareth as an intruder who has overstayed his welcome at the castle.

When Gareth, frustrated by the obstacles placed between him and Lionesse, confronts Gringamore directly and threatens him — "Thou traitor, Sir Gringamore, deliver me my love" (147-036) — Gringamore initially responds from the safety of a window (147-038). However, once he understands the sincerity of Gareth's intentions, his demeanor shifts markedly.

## Alliance with Gareth

Gringamore's turning point comes when he acknowledges Gareth's resolve. He tells Gareth, "Well, said Sir Gringamore, sithen I know now your will" (147-050), signaling his acceptance of the situation. From this moment forward, Gringamore becomes cooperative, taking Gareth by the hand (147-059) and acting as an intermediary.

During supper, Gringamore observes the romantic glances exchanged between Gareth and Lionesse (148-012), confirming the depth of their attachment. He then goes to Gareth with a message from Lionesse (148-021), and swears upon his honor to facilitate their union: "Upon my worship, said Sir Gringamore" (148-025).

## Family Ties

Sir Gringamore's primary motivation throughout the narrative is the welfare of his sister. When Lionesse cries out in distress, Gringamore hears her (149-037) and comes to her aid. He speaks to her with frankness and care: "Sister, said Sir Gringamore, and I will that ye wit" (149-044). The episode concludes with both Gringamore and Lionesse sharing in great sorrow (149-047) before the resolution, underscoring the strong sibling bond that drives his actions.

## Legacy

Though Sir Gringamore is a minor figure in the broader Arthurian cycle, his role in the Gareth story is structurally significant. He functions as the protective brother who first obstructs, then ultimately enables, the romance at the heart of the tale. His transformation from suspicious guardian to willing facilitator mirrors the narrative arc of the Castle Perilous episode itself, where obstacles give way to recognition and union.
