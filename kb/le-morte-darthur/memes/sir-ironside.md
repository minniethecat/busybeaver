# Sir Ironside

Sir Ironside, the Red Knight of the Red Launds, was a knight whose career in the Arthurian chronicle spanned the full spectrum from formidable adversary to honoured companion. He first appears as a dangerous opponent subdued by [[Sir Gareth]], then reappears as a trusted ally who rode with [[Sir Tristram]] and [[Sir Lamorak]] in the great tournament assemblies.

## The Red Knight of the Red Launds

Sir Ironside's identity was bound to his fierce epithet. When challenged, he declared: "my name is Sir Ironside; and sir, wit ye well, here I am called the Red Knight of the Red Launds" (150-047). The title carried weight — the Red Launds were a domain associated with dangerous knights who tested the worth of Arthur's fellowship.

His reputation preceded him. When [[Sir Gareth]] undertook his quest to prove himself, it was Sir Ironside who waited at the end — the final and most formidable opponent the young knight must overcome. The chronicle records that messengers carried word "unto Sir Ironside, that is the Red Knight of the Red Launds" (153-046), summoning him to account for his deeds.

## The Fellowship of Tournaments

Sir Ironside's narrative did not end with defeat. Having been bested by [[Sir Gareth]], he entered the broader fellowship of Arthurian knighthood and reappears in the great tournament assemblies where the champions of the age tested one another.

The chronicle records a notable encounter "betwixt Sir Lamorak and Sir Ironside, that was the Red Knight of the Red Launds" (156-003). On another occasion, Sir Tristram "rode unto Sir Ironside and to Sir Persant" (156-021) before a great tournament, marking Ironside as a knight whose company the greatest champions sought.

## The Bonds of Fellowship

Sir Ironside's place in the fellowship is clearest in a passage that gathers the knights in council. When Sir Tristram made a proposal, "said Sir Ironside" (156-025) in response — his voice counted among the peers. And when the company rode together, "Sir Tristram, Sir Ironside, Sir Persant, and his brother" (156-034) formed a band of proven warriors.

The chronicle elsewhere notes him as "Sir Ironside, that was the Red Knight of the Red Launds" (162-028), the epithet following him even as his place in the fellowship grew secure. He was the adversary who became an ally, the dangerous knight whose defeat opened the door to honourable companionship.

## The Tamed Champion

Sir Ironside embodies a recurring pattern in the Arthurian chronicle: the formidable adversary who, once defeated, becomes a valued ally. His trajectory from the Red Knight of the Red Launds — a title of menace — to a trusted companion of [[Sir Tristram]] illustrates the integrative logic of the [[Round Table]]. The fellowship did not destroy its enemies; it absorbed them, and in doing so, turned the fierce energies of knights like Sir Ironside toward the service of chivalric order.
