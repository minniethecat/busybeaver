Neighbours in Payment Deferred constitute an informal surveillance system that exerts continuous social pressure on the [[william-marble|Marble]] family. More than mere background characters, the neighbouring community functions as a collective [[guilt-and-conscience|conscience]] — watching, judging, and ultimately contributing to the exposure of [[william-marble|William Marble]]'s crime.

## Definition

The neighbours in Payment Deferred represent the social world beyond [[53-malcolm-road|53 Malcolm Road]] — a world that both constrains and judges the [[william-marble|Marble]] family. They are not named individuals but a collective presence, characterized by their "prying" [003-007] curiosity and their instinct for detecting deviations from suburban norms. In the novel's moral economy, the neighbours stand for the force of public opinion, the social equivalent of the legal system that [[william-marble|Marble]] so desperately [[fear-in-payment-deferred|fear]]s.

## In the Novel

The neighbours appear as a persistent, low-level threat throughout the narrative. [[william-marble|Marble]]'s earliest [[fear-in-payment-deferred|fear]]s after the [[murder-method|murder]] include "the wild nightmares of prying neighbours" [003-007], suggesting that social exposure is as terrifying to him as legal punishment. The neighbours' bafflement at the [[william-marble|Marble]]s' sudden prosperity [008-003] is a source of both comedy and suspense — they cannot prove anything, but their collective intuition senses something amiss. The [[william-marble|Marble]]s' eventual move to a better neighbourhood is, in part, an attempt to escape this scrutiny, but it proves futile. The new neighbours prove equally watchful, and [[annie-marble|Annie]]'s increasingly erratic behaviour draws fresh attention [010-001]. The phrase "committee of neighbours coming [[secrecy|secret]]ly to investigate" [008-028] captures the quasi-judicial role the community assumes.

## Thematic Role

The neighbours serve as a **social mirror** reflecting the gap between the [[william-marble|Marble]]s' performed [[social-class-and-respectability|respectability]] and their hidden [[guilt-and-conscience|guilt]]. They embody the novel's critique of suburban morality — a world where appearances are everything and where private life is subject to constant public audit. The thematic argument is ironic: the neighbours' prying is presented as vulgar and intrusive, yet their suspicions are entirely correct. The community's nosiness, however distasteful, functions as a crude but effective moral sensor.
