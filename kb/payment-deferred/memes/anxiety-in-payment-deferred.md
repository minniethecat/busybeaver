Anxiety in Payment Deferred is the dominant psychological atmosphere of the novel, functioning as both a character trait and a narrative engine. Every major character experiences [[fear-in-payment-deferred|anxiety]] in a distinct form — [[william-marble|Marble]]'s financial and criminal [[fear-in-payment-deferred|anxiety]], [[annie-marble|Annie]]'s domestic and moral [[fear-in-payment-deferred|anxiety]], and even [[the-marble-children|the children]]'s confused apprehension — creating a sustained mood of unease that persists from the opening pages to the final catastrophe.

## Definition

Anxiety, as depicted in the novel, is a state of chronic nervous tension produced by the gap between appearance and reality. For [[william-marble|Marble]] before the [[murder-method|murder]], it is the [[fear-in-payment-deferred|anxiety]] of the [[financial-desperation|debt]]or who must maintain a respectable facade while knowing he cannot pay his bills [001-004]. After the [[murder-method|murder]], it transforms into the [[fear-in-payment-deferred|anxiety]] of the criminal who [[fear-in-payment-deferred|fear]]s detection at every knock on the door. The novel's genius lies in showing that [[fear-in-payment-deferred|anxiety]] does not diminish with the removal of its ostensible cause — [[william-marble|Marble]]'s [[murder-method|murder]] solves his financial problems but only intensifies his psychological torment [003-003].

## In the Novel

[[william-marble|Marble]]'s [[fear-in-payment-deferred|anxiety]] is established early: "turning and tossing in fevered [[fear-in-payment-deferred|anxiety]]" [003-003] captures his state immediately after the [[murder-method|murder]]. This [[fear-in-payment-deferred|anxiety]] gives him "real cause" for concern as it directs his attention to concrete threats — the cellar, [[the-garden-at-53-malcolm-road|the garden]], the neighbours [003-007]. His [[fear-in-payment-deferred|anxiety]] is contagious: [[annie-marble|Annie]]'s "frothy [[fear-in-payment-deferred|anxiety]]" [013-006] mirrors his own, and even [[dr-atkinson|Dr. Atkinson]] observes that the victims of the play they attend "were depressed, or worn with [[fear-in-payment-deferred|anxiety]]" [015-005], in an ironic foreshadowing of the [[william-marble|Marble]]s' own fate. The novel's climax sees [[fear-in-payment-deferred|anxiety]] crystallize into terror as the police finally arrive.

## Thematic Role

Anxiety is the novel's **psychological signature** — it is the internal experience of [[guilt-and-conscience|guilt]] for characters who lack a developed [[guilt-and-conscience|conscience]]. [[william-marble|Marble]] does not feel [[guilt-and-conscience|remorse]] in any conventional sense, but he feels [[fear-in-payment-deferred|anxiety]] constantly. The thematic implication is that the body knows what the mind refuses to acknowledge: [[fear-in-payment-deferred|anxiety]] is the physical manifestation of moral [[guilt-and-conscience|guilt]]. The novel thus makes a subtle argument about the relationship between crime, psychology, and punishment — the criminal is punished not by the law but by his own nervous system.
