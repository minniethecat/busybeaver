# Knowledge and Ignorance in Payment Deferred

The asymmetries of knowledge — who knows what, and when — that drive the novel's suspense and define its power relationships.

## Definition

In *Payment Deferred*, knowledge is a weapon and a vulnerability. William Marble's crime creates a "terrible secret" (003-003) that only he possesses, granting him a grim form of power while simultaneously making him a prisoner of his knowledge. As other characters come to know or suspect the truth, the distribution of knowledge reconfigures the power dynamics of the household.

## In the Novel

The novel's suspense is structured around the progressive disclosure of knowledge. Initially, only Marble knows about the body in the cellar — he draws "calm in the knowledge that no one was poking about in his garden" (003-003). This solitary knowledge is his burden and his shield.

The first breach comes when Annie discovers the truth: "The secret was out now" (004-039). Marble "hotly resented her knowledge" (011-007), because shared knowledge means shared power — or shared danger. The "unvoiced menace of a secret shared" (012-001) hangs between husband and wife, transforming their marriage into a mutual hostage situation.

The knowledge spreads further: neighbours begin to suspect, the bank manager Saunders knows something is amiss, and Marble lives in constant fear that "she had guessed his secret" (013-023). The arc of the novel traces knowledge from private crime to public revelation — culminating in the hangman's knowledge that ends Marble's life.

## Thematic Role

Knowledge in the novel is a form of surveillance. Marble's original power — he knows something no one else does — becomes his torment as he realizes that knowledge spreads inexorably. The novel suggests that truth, like a buried body, will eventually surface. Ignorance is merely a temporary state; knowledge is the default condition toward which the plot inevitably moves.

## Key Passages

> "For a terrible secret to be hidden away in the garden." (003-003)

> "The secret was out now. Saunders could be told." (004-039)

> "He hotly resented her knowledge." (011-007)

> "She had guessed his secret, he was sure of it. The knowledge had troubled him little up to now." (013-023)
