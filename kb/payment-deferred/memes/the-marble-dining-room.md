The dining room at [[53-malcolm-road|53 Malcolm Road]] is the primary domestic stage of Payment Deferred — the room where the [[william-marble|Marble]] family assembles, where [[william-marble|William Marble]] sits alone with his [[alcohol-and-escapism|whisky]] after the [[murder-method|murder]], and where the routine of [[domestic-life-at-53-malcolm-road|family life]] continues to be performed even as the knowledge of the crime corrodes it from within.

## In the Novel

The dining room is established as a space of ambiguous comfort. [[william-marble|Marble]] is "happy sitting alone in the dining-room at [[53-malcolm-road|Malcolm Road]], with a full bottle of his only friend beside him" [003-003] — solitude in the dining room is preferable to the company of his family. The family assembles in the dining room for meals [006-003], maintaining the forms of domesticity even as the substance drains away. But the dining room is also the scene of [[william-marble|Marble]]'s darkest moments: "sitting in the same frowsty dining-room, from minute to minute growing more desperate" [003-006]. The room that should represent family togetherness becomes the site of [[william-marble|Marble]]'s isolation, a space where he sits alone with his [[guilt-and-conscience|guilt]] while the [[domestic-life-at-53-malcolm-road|household]] sleeps.
