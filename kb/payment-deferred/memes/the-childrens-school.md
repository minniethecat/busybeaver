The children's school in Payment Deferred is the institution that represents normality in the novel — a world of homework, scholarships, and the ordinary ambitions of lower-middle-class parents. It serves as a counterpoint to [[53-malcolm-road|the Marble house]]hold, a space where [[the-marble-children|the children]] can temporarily escape the oppressive atmosphere of [[53-malcolm-road|53 Malcolm Road]], and where the family's aspirations for social mobility are most visibly invested.

## In the Novel

The school appears primarily through [[the-marble-children|the children]]'s relationship to it — their homework, their chatter, their scholarships. [[john-marble|John]]'s "secondary school" [006-016] and the scholarships that send the [[the-marble-children|Marble children]] there [005-004] represent the family's commitment to [[social-class-and-respectability|respectability]] through education. The father's involvement in school matters — "What about school, [[john-marble|John]]?" [006-007] — is one of the few moments of normal paternal interest that [[william-marble|Marble]] displays. But the school also highlights the gap between the [[william-marble|Marble]]s' performance of normality and their hidden reality: [[the-marble-children|the children]]'s "chatter about school" at the dinner table [003-003] is a fragile ritual of ordinariness maintained against the growing pressure of the family [[secrecy|secret]].
