Escape attempts in Payment Deferred trace [[william-marble|William Marble]]'s recurring impulse to flee from the consequences of his actions — an impulse that the novel treats not as cowardice but as the rational response of a man who has calculated the odds and found them unfavourable. The pattern of contemplated escape punctuates the narrative, and each rejection of flight tightens the noose.

## Role in the Novel

[[william-marble|Marble]]'s first thought after the [[murder-method|murder]] is "flight and covering his tracks" [003-001], and this pairing — escape and [[secrecy|concealment]] — defines his strategy. But he quickly realizes that flight would be self-incriminating: "like Crippen's flight to the Continent" [005-010], it would confirm [[guilt-and-conscience|guilt]] rather than evade it. The novel presents escape not as a moral failure but as a practical calculation that [[william-marble|Marble]] repeatedly makes and rejects. Each rejection of escape is less a decision to face justice than a recognition that escape is impossible — the world is too tightly networked, the evidence too dispersed, the risks too high. The final impossibility of escape is confirmed by the police knock, which arrives not as a surprise but as the long-expected end.
