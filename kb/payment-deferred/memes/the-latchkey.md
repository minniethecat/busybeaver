The latchkey in Payment Deferred is a small object with outsized symbolic weight. The sound of a key in the door punctuates the novel's most tense moments, marking arrivals that are never innocent and departures that offer only temporary relief. The key represents the boundary between the [[william-marble|Marble]]s' private world and [[the-public-house|the pub]]lic one that threatens to expose them.

## In the Novel

Keys appear at critical moments throughout the narrative. [[william-marble|Marble]]'s "keys rattle as he unlocked" [002-002] the [[the-sideboard|sideboard]] where the poison is kept; his "latchkey" [010-048] is the instrument of his return to the domestic scene after his speculative triumphs. [[annie-marble|Annie]] is "startled to hear her husband's key in the door" [011-014] — the sound that should signal safety has become a source of [[fear-in-payment-deferred|fear]]. Most ominously, "they heard [[annie-marble|Annie Marble]]'s key in the door" [013-033] and "downstairs they heard the key turn in the lock" [014-050] — the key that once secured the home now signifies imprisonment within it. The domestic threshold, guarded by the latchkey, has become a trap.
