Prison life in Payment Deferred is depicted with the same unsentimental clarity that characterizes the rest of the novel. [[william-marble|Marble]]'s imprisonment is not a redemptive arc but the logical terminus of his criminal trajectory — a narrowing of physical and psychological space that mirrors the progressive constriction of his moral choices.

## Role in the Novel

The prison sequence occupies the novel's final chapters, following [[william-marble|Marble]]'s [[marbles-arrest|arrest]]. The condemned cell represents the ultimate failure of [[william-marble|Marble]]'s strategy of [[secrecy|concealment]]: he has moved from the freedom of [[dulwich|the suburb]]an home to the absolute constraint of the prison, from the pretense of [[social-class-and-respectability|respectability]] to the stark reality of the criminal [008-028]. The novel's attention to the "formalities of the condemned cell and of the scaffold" [008-028] emphasizes the institutional machinery that processes the criminal — a system as impersonal and inexorable as the one that governed [[william-marble|Marble]]'s clerical life. The prison does not reform [[william-marble|Marble]]; it merely contains him until the state can dispose of him.
