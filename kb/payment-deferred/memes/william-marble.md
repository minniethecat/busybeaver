# William Marble

William Marble is the protagonist of C. S. Forester's "Payment Deferred" — a bank clerk whose [[financial-desperation|financial desperation]] drives him to [[murder-method|murder]] his wife and execute a daring [[franc-speculation|currency speculation]]. He is, by any conventional measure, unremarkable: "a small man, with sparse reddish hair and a bristling moustache," "weak grey eyes," and a "worried expression" (001-004). It is precisely this ordinariness that makes his transformation into a [[murder-method|murder]]er so disturbing.

## Character

Marble is defined by the gap between his modest capabilities and his desperate circumstances. He is intelligent but not brilliant, calculating but not cold, timid but capable of extraordinary action under pressure. His most notable traits are his **financial acumen** — he can "at two minutes' notice give the rate of exchange on anywhere at any time within the last two years" (004-006) — and his **capacity for self-deception**, which allows him to live with the consequences of his actions far longer than [[guilt-and-conscience|conscience]] should permit.

## Role in the Novel

Marble's arc structures the entire novel. He begins in [[financial-desperation|financial desperation]], commits [[murder-method|murder]] to escape it, and then compounds his crimes through [[franc-speculation|currency speculation]] — turning a four-hundred-pound margin into over fifty thousand pounds. His success, however, is indistinguishable from his destruction. The novel charts his progressive psychological disintegration: from [[fear-in-payment-deferred|anxiety]] to terror to [[marbles-confession|confession]] to collapse.

The paradox at Marble's core is that the same qualities that make him a [[murder-method|murder]]er also make him a financial genius. The cold calculation required to study [[forensic-medicine-in-payment-deferred|forensic medicine]], plan a [[murder-method|poisoning]], and bury a body is indistinguishable from the cold calculation required to read currency markets and execute leveraged trades. Forester refuses to separate these capacities, suggesting uncomfortable continuities between professional competence and criminal capability.

- **[[saunders|Saunders]]** (accomplice): A wealthy public-house acquaintance used as a front for [[franc-speculation|currency speculation]]
