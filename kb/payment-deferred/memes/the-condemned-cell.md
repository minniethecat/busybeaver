The condemned cell in Payment Deferred is the novel's final setting — the narrow institutional space where [[william-marble|William Marble]] awaits execution. It represents the complete inversion of his earlier life: where he once moved freely through [[dulwich|the suburb]]s, managing appearances and maintaining a facade, he is now confined to a single room under constant observation, with nothing left to manage and no appearance to maintain.

## Role in the Novel

The condemned cell sequence strips [[william-marble|Marble]] of the last vestiges of control. The "formalities of the condemned cell and of the scaffold" [008-028] — the scheduled visits, the measured routine, the institutional courtesy — replace the domestic routine that once structured his life. The cell is the antithesis of [[53-malcolm-road|53 Malcolm Road]]: where [[dulwich|the suburb]]an house allowed [[secrecy|concealment]], the cell permits none; where the family home was a stage for performance, the cell is a space of absolute exposure. [[william-marble|Marble]]'s experience of the condemned cell is rendered with clinical precision, and the novel's refusal to sentimentalize his final days is consistent with its unsentimental treatment of his entire criminal career.
