The [[alcohol-and-escapism|whisky]] bottle in Payment Deferred is both a literal object and a barometer of the [[william-marble|Marble]] family's psychological state. It is [[william-marble|William Marble]]'s "only friend" [003-003] before the [[murder-method|murder]] and [[annie-marble|Annie Marble]]'s refuge afterward, tracing a trajectory of dependency that parallels the family's moral decay.

## In the Novel

The [[alcohol-and-escapism|whisky]] bottle is introduced early as an object of complex significance. [[william-marble|Marble]] "stared gloomily at [[the-whisky-decanter|the whisky]] bottle, which was perched, like some family god, in the exact centre of the table" [002-038] — the religious language is not incidental; [[alcohol-and-escapism|whisky]] is the [[domestic-life-at-53-malcolm-road|household]]'s true deity. When [[william-marble|Marble]] "eyed [[the-whisky-decanter|the whisky]] bottle" [002-049] after the [[murder-method|murder]], it represents the comfort he desperately needs but cannot fully permit himself. After the [[murder-method|murder]], [[the-whisky-decanter|the whisky]] bottle migrates from [[william-marble|Marble]]'s private consolation to [[annie-marble|Annie]]'s public vice: her [[alcohol-and-escapism|drinking]] becomes visible to the neighbours and contributes to the family's exposure. The "full bottle" that [[william-marble|Marble]] contemplates [002-057] is a promise of oblivion that the novel will not grant him.
