Evidence and detection in Payment Deferred trace the slow, inexorable process by which [[william-marble|William Marble]]'s crime comes to light. Unlike the detective fiction of its era, the novel presents detection not as brilliant deduction but as the accumulation of small inconsistencies — the changed spending habits, the suspicious [[the-garden-at-53-malcolm-road|garden]], the neighbours' observations — that collectively build an unanswerable case against the [[murder-method|murder]]er.

## Definition

Evidence, in the novel's epistemology, is any fact that connects the crime to the criminal. It operates at two levels: the objective evidence that would convince a court (the body in the cellar, the financial records) and the subjective evidence that convinces the community (the [[william-marble|Marble]]s' changed behaviour, [[annie-marble|Annie]]'s [[alcohol-and-escapism|drinking]]). Detection is the process by which evidence is gathered and interpreted, and the novel's insight is that detection is not a specialized skill but a universal human activity — everyone in the novel, from [[annie-marble|Annie]] to the dressmaker, is engaged in detecting the truth.

## In the Novel

[[william-marble|Marble]]'s awareness of evidence shapes his every action after the [[murder-method|murder]]. He knows that "all the evidence would point that way" [006-067] if suspicions were raised, and his efforts to manage appearances are essentially attempts at evidence suppression. The novel scatters potential evidence throughout — [[the-garden-at-53-malcolm-road|the garden]] where the body is buried, the money that appears from nowhere, the books on criminal procedure that [[william-marble|Marble]] acquires. Each piece of evidence on its own is ambiguous; together they form a pattern that even the unimaginative police can eventually read. The novel's title itself is a kind of evidence — the "[[deferred-payment|deferred payment]]" that will eventually be collected.

## Thematic Role

Evidence represents the **material persistence of truth** — the idea that actions leave traces that cannot be permanently erased. [[william-marble|Marble]] believes he can control the evidence against him, but the novel argues that evidence multiplies beyond the criminal's control. Every attempt at [[secrecy|concealment]] generates new evidence of [[secrecy|concealment]], and the very thoroughness of [[william-marble|Marble]]'s precautions becomes, in retrospect, the strongest evidence of his [[guilt-and-conscience|guilt]].
