The [[alcohol-and-escapism|whisky]] glass in Payment Deferred is one of the novel's most significant objects — the vessel through which [[william-marble|William Marble]] administers the [[poison|potassium cyanide]] to his nephew James [[medland|Medland]]. It embodies the novel's method of making the ordinary sinister: a domestic object transformed into a [[murder-method|murder]] weapon through the simple addition of poison.

## In the Novel

The [[alcohol-and-escapism|whisky]] glass appears throughout the [[murder-method|murder]] scene with deliberate prominence. [[william-marble|Marble]] "put the glass down indifferently beside him" [001-120], then "drained his glass almost without thinking" [001-145] — these mundane gestures are charged with [[fear-in-payment-deferred|dread]]ful significance for the reader who knows what the glass contains. The crucial moment comes when [[william-marble|Marble]] "reached across and drew [[medland|Medland]]'s glass from his hand" [001-147], confirming the administration of the poison. The glass remains "still in his hand" [001-151] as the [[murder-method|murder]] is accomplished, and its disposal — like the body — becomes part of [[william-marble|Marble]]'s cleanup operation.
