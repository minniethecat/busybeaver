Flight from justice in Payment Deferred is presented as a theoretical possibility that the novel systematically forecloses. [[william-marble|William Marble]], who has studied criminal cases and knows the statistical odds against successful escape, understands that flight would be an admission of [[guilt-and-conscience|guilt]] rather than an evasion of it. The novel's treatment of this theme is characteristically unsentimental: justice is not poetic but practical, and its effectiveness derives not from moral righteousness but from administrative competence.

## Role in the Novel

The possibility of flight haunts [[william-marble|Marble]] from the moment of the [[murder-method|murder]]. He considers "abandoning the whole business" [004-014], fleeing to the Continent, disappearing into the anonymous masses. But his knowledge of criminal procedure — his "trained foreign clerk's brain" — tells him that flight is a trap. The famous cases he has studied — Crippen, the [[murder-method|murder]]ers who ran and were caught — demonstrate that flight transforms suspicion into certainty. [[william-marble|Marble]]'s decision to stay, to maintain his routine, to "cover his tracks" rather than flee, is the rational choice of a man who understands the system he is up against. The [[irony-in-payment-deferred|irony]] is that staying proves equally fatal: the novel argues that crime offers no viable exit strategy, whether one stays or goes.
