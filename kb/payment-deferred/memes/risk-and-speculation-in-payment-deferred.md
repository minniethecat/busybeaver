Risk and speculation in Payment Deferred are the engine of the plot: [[william-marble|William Marble]]'s [[franc-speculation|currency speculation]] creates the [[financial-desperation|financial desperation]] that drives him to [[murder-method|murder]]. The novel uses financial gambling as a metaphor for the larger gamble of [[guilt-and-conscience|moral transgression]] — once [[william-marble|Marble]] begins speculating, he cannot stop, and each risk requires a greater one to cover it.

## Definition

Risk, in the novel, has a double meaning. In its financial sense, it refers to [[william-marble|Marble]]'s leveraged speculation on the French franc: "It isn't much good buying francs outright" [004-041] — instead he uses margin, where "a five percent rise in the franc gives you fifty percent profit" [004-045]. This is risk as calculated exposure to loss in pursuit of gain. But risk also operates in a moral register: [[william-marble|Marble]]'s decision to [[murder-method|kill]] [[medland|Medland]] is a speculation of a different order — a gamble that he can commit the perfect crime and escape detection.

## In the Novel

The novel's financial plot is meticulously detailed. [[william-marble|Marble]] works in the foreign exchange department of the County National Bank, which "dealt solely with foreign business" [004-002]. His insider knowledge convinces him that "the franc is going to rise, and now is the time to buy" [004-038]. The mechanics of margin trading are explained: a small deposit controls a large position, magnifying both gains and losses. [[william-marble|Marble]] "showed his whole hand, so sure was he" [004-041] — the confidence of the speculator who believes he has special knowledge.

The [[murder-method|murder]] itself is framed as a species of speculation. [[william-marble|Marble]] weighs the risks: "when he had been a rat in a corner he had fought like one, desperately, to the last risk" [003-001]. The language of financial risk bleeds into the language of crime. The irony the novel develops is that [[william-marble|Marble]] is a man who would have "hooted with derision at a man who proposed to back a stray acquaintance's favourite horse" [004-076] — yet he stakes everything, including his life, on a moral gamble far riskier than any financial bet.

## Thematic Role

Risk links the novel's financial and moral economies. [[william-marble|Marble]]'s tragedy is that he treats [[murder-method|murder]] as though it were a currency trade — a calculated risk with manageable downside. The novel demonstrates that some risks cannot be hedged. The franc speculation also serves a sociological function: it shows how the respectable middle-class world of bank clerks and suburban houses contains its own forms of recklessness, no less destructive than the vices it condemns. "A bookmaker earns his living by other people's gambling" [004-059] — but [[william-marble|Marble]] is gambling too, just in a more respectable idiom.
