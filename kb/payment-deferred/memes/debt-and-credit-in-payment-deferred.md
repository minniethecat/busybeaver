Debt and credit in Payment Deferred constitute more than economic background — they form the novel's foundational metaphor. [[william-marble|William Marble]]'s financial [[financial-desperation|debt]]s to his creditors parallel and ultimately lead to the moral [[financial-desperation|debt]] he incurs through [[murder-method|murder]], while the concept of "[[deferred-payment|deferred payment]]" in the title itself announces the novel's central thesis: all [[financial-desperation|debt]]s, financial or moral, come due eventually.

## Definition

Debt, in the novel's moral economy, is a state of obligation that cannot be permanently evaded. Financial [[financial-desperation|debt]] — [[william-marble|Marble]]'s chronic inability to live within his means [001-004] — is the most visible form, but the novel traces a chain of escalating [[financial-desperation|debt]]s: the social [[financial-desperation|debt]] of maintaining appearances, the psychological [[financial-desperation|debt]] of living with a [[secrecy|secret]], and ultimately the criminal [[financial-desperation|debt]] owed to justice. Credit, its opposite, is the temporary reprieve that makes [[financial-desperation|debt]] possible, and the novel treats credit as a form of moral hazard.

## In the Novel

[[william-marble|Marble]]'s relationship with [[financial-desperation|debt]] defines his character from the opening pages: he has "managed nevertheless to keep out of [[financial-desperation|debt]]" [001-004] — but only barely, and the strain is evident. His desperation crystallizes around the figure of "that devil [[evans|Evans]]" [001-074], a creditor whose demands are more pressing and personal than institutional [[financial-desperation|debt]]s. The [[murder-method|murder]] of [[medland|Medland]] is, in economic terms, a [[financial-desperation|debt]] consolidation — [[william-marble|Marble]] exchanges many small financial [[financial-desperation|debt]]s for one enormous moral one. The [[irony-in-payment-deferred|irony]] is that after paying his [[financial-desperation|debt]]s "every one of them, like a saint" [003-004], he finds himself more burdened than ever. The novel's title operates at this dual level: [[medland|Medland]]'s death is a "[[deferred-payment|deferred payment]]" on [[william-marble|Marble]]'s obligations, but the [[deferred-payment|deferral]] is temporary and the interest compounds.

## Thematic Role

Debt functions as the novel's **structural metaphor** — it gives moral weight to economic transactions and economic specificity to moral failings. The novel argues, through its plot, that no [[financial-desperation|debt]] can be permanently deferred. The financial [[financial-desperation|debt]]s that drive [[william-marble|Marble]] to [[murder-method|murder]] are replaced by a moral [[financial-desperation|debt]] that proves far more expensive: it costs him his peace of mind, his family, and ultimately his life. The economic language of "payment" and "credit" thus becomes a surprisingly precise vocabulary for describing moral causality.
