# The Wristwatch

## Definition

The platinum wristwatch is a luxury accessory worn by William Marble, the protagonist of C.S. Forester's 1926 crime novel *Payment Deferred*. Together with his diamond tiepin and gold cigarette case, it forms a trio of personal accessories that project an image of City prosperity, funded by the money Marble stole after murdering his nephew James Medland.

## In the Novel

The wristwatch appears in two key passages, both concerned with Marble's public presentation. During the police investigation, it is noted as "his platinum wristwatch bracelet" [008-015], the platinum metal signalling a higher tier of luxury than mere gold. In the novel's satirical catalogue of Marble's appearance, it is listed alongside his other accessories: "tiepin, wristwatch, cigarette case, well-cut clothes and prosperous appearance" [010-002].

The wristwatch is a period-specific detail: wristwatches were still relatively novel accessories for men in the 1920s, having gained popularity during the First World War. Marble's adoption of the fashion, in platinum rather than the more common gold, signals his keenness to project a modern, affluent image.

## Thematic Role

The wristwatch functions within the novel's systematic contrast between surface and substance. Its platinum gleam is purchased with blood money, and its function — the measurement of time — links it to the clock on the mantelpiece, the novel's central symbol of Marble's psychological torment. Where the mantelpiece clock measures the hours of dread at home, the wristwatch accompanies Marble into the City, the site of his professional ambition and criminal concealment.
