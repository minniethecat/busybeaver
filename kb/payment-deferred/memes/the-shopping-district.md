The shopping district in Payment Deferred is the stage on which [[annie-marble|Annie Marble]] performs her transformation from careful housewife to extravagant spender. The shops — [[evans|Evans]]', Mr. Richards', the dress shops — are the sites where the [[william-marble|Marble]]s' newfound wealth becomes publicly visible, and where the discrepancy between their known income and their spending patterns first attracts notice.

## In the Novel

[[annie-marble|Annie]]'s visits to [[rye-lane|the shops]] mark her psychological transformation after the [[murder-method|murder]]. Where once she "had to go on obtaining credit at [[rye-lane|the shops]]" [001-121], she now enters them with cash and confidence. Her visit to "[[evans|Evans]]' shop" [002-024] to pay off [[financial-desperation|debt]]s is her first taste of the power that money brings. The "shop windows" [007-042] become objects of desire rather than exclusion, and [[annie-marble|Annie]]'s leisurely browsing — "their eyes on the shop windows" [007-042] — signals her entry into a consumer economy she had previously only observed from outside. The dress shop [007-044], where she makes her most conspicuous purchases, becomes a key location in the chain of evidence that eventually leads to the [[william-marble|Marble]]s' exposure.
