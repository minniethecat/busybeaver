The gold cigarette case in Payment Deferred is the most visible emblem of [[william-marble|William Marble]]'s transformation after the [[murder-method|murder]] — from a struggling bank clerk who carried cigarettes in "a battered yellow packet" [001-067] to a man of means who displays a gold case. The case is a performance of prosperity, and like all performances in the novel, it is scrutinized by observers who find it inconsistent with what they know of the performer.

## In the Novel

The cigarette case's trajectory tracks [[william-marble|Marble]]'s social ascent and moral descent. Before the [[murder-method|murder]], he smokes from a cheap packet [001-067]; afterward, he takes "a gold cigarette case out of his pocket" [007-018] with the casualness of a man to whom such objects are familiar. The case becomes part of his public persona — "tiepin, wristwatch, cigarette case, well-cut clothes" [010-002] — the accessories of the gentleman he is pretending to be. But the performance is too thorough, too studied, and it registers as false to those who knew him before. The gold cigarette case does not conceal [[william-marble|Marble]]'s origins; it advertises the gap between what he was and what he has become.
