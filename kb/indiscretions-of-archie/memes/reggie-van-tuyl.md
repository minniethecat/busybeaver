# Reggie van Tuyl

Reggie van Tuyl is a friend of [[archie-moffam|Archie Moffam]]'s in New York — a sporting young American who introduces Archie to the pleasures and perils of baseball wagering. The heir to the van Tuyl millions, Reggie combines inherited wealth with an amiable but unreliable character that makes him one of the novel's more memorable supporting figures.

## Character and Background

Reggie is the heir to a substantial American fortune — "the van Tuyl millions" (010-182) — though the narrative suggests the burden of expectation sits uneasily on his shoulders. Archie encounters him at Madison Square Garden (003-091) and later at the Hotel Cosmopolis, where Reggie becomes a regular presence in Archie's social circle.

Archie's fondness for Reggie is genuine but qualified. When Reggie enters the grill-room, Archie "waved cheerily to him to join his table. He liked Reggie" (010-172–173). Yet the narration also notes that "the burden of the van Tuyl millions was more than his friend could bear" (010-182), and Archie is described as being "such a wobbly reed as Reggie van Tuyl" (011-002) — a friend of undoubted goodwill but uncertain reliability.

## The Baseball Bet

Reggie's most consequential action in the novel is persuading Archie to wager on the [[new-york-giants]]: "Reggie van Tuyl assured Archie that the New York Giants would win the opening game of the series" (017-009). This seemingly casual piece of sporting advice launches Archie into the baseball subplot that occupies Sections 17 through 20 — a sequence of escalating comic complications involving wagering, debt, and Archie's fraught relationship with his father-in-law.

When the Giants lose, Reggie's advice proves costly, and Archie finds himself in financial difficulty. The episode illustrates Reggie's characteristic combination of confidence and unreliability — he means well, but his assurances should never be taken at face value.
