# J. B. Wheeler

J. B. Wheeler is the novel's representative artist — a painter whose work becomes entangled in the commercial dealings and social comedy that drive the plot. His presence introduces the theme of artistic creation into a novel otherwise dominated by commerce and consumption.

## Role in the Novel

Wheeler's primary narrative function is to provide the artistic content around which business negotiations revolve. When he invites Archie to "come and pose for a picture I'm doing" (003-113), he initiates a sequence of events that connects the novel's art-world episodes to its central characters. His painting becomes an object of negotiation, its aesthetic value inseparable from its commercial potential.

As a character, Wheeler represents a type — the American artist, serious about his work but not immune to the commercial pressures of his environment. His interactions with Archie highlight the contrast between creative labor and gentlemanly leisure.

## Biography

Wheeler is an American painter of some reputation. His studio serves as a setting for key encounters, and his work — particularly the painting that becomes the subject of business negotiations (010-118) — functions as both art object and plot device. His professional status as an artist places him outside the hotel world that dominates the novel, providing a different perspective on the themes of value and worth.

## Personality and Traits

Wheeler is characterized by professional seriousness leavened with American practicality. He takes his art seriously but is not above the commercial transactions that sustain it. His interactions with Archie reveal a man who can appreciate English charm while maintaining his own artistic standards.
