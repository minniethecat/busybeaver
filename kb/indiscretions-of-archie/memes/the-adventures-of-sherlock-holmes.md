# The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes

*The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes* is a collection of twelve detective stories by Sir Arthur Conan Doyle, first published in 1892. In [[indiscretions-of-archie-the-novel|the novel]], it makes a brief but telling appearance as Squiffy's bedtime reading — and the narrator's gentle mockery of his literary choice.

## Overview

The book surfaces in Chapter VIII, when Archie's friend [[lord-seacliff|Lord Seacliff]] ("Squiffy") is described as having settled in for the evening with a volume of detective fiction:

> His book was *The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes*, and the particular story which he selected for perusal was the one entitled, "The Speckled Band." He was not a great reader, but, when he read, he liked something with a bit of zip to it. (008-151–008-154)

The narrator frames this with wry affection: the "consensus of opinion" holds that "a gentle, slow-moving story makes the best opiate," and by that standard Squiffy's choice was "rather injudicious" — a story with "a bit of zip" being exactly the wrong prescription for settling down to sleep (008-149–008-150).

## In the Novel

The reference functions as quick character portraiture. Squiffy, a young English aristocrat temporarily displaced to New York, reveals himself through his reading habits: he is not intellectual or contemplative, but he craves excitement. His choice of Sherlock Holmes — specifically "The Speckled Band," one of the collection's most atmospheric and suspenseful tales — tells the reader that Squiffy is a man of action-adjacent tastes, even when supine.

The moment is also a small instance of the novel's broader pattern of referencing real-world books and publications to ground its fictional world. Like the [[new-york-chronicle]] that appears at the McCall breakfast table or Homer's [[the-iliad|Iliad]] invoked by the narrator, Conan Doyle's detective stories place the novel's 1920s New York within a recognizable cultural landscape.

## Associated Characters

- **[[lord-seacliff]]** — Reads the book in his room at the Cosmopolis Hotel, preferring a story "with a bit of zip" (PN 008-151)

## Real-World Context

*The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes* was published in October 1892 by George Newnes Ltd, collecting the first twelve Sherlock Holmes short stories that had appeared in *The Strand Magazine* between July 1891 and June 1892. "The Adventure of the Speckled Band" is among the most famous of the Holmes stories, known for its Gothic atmosphere and locked-room mystery. By the time of *Indiscretions of Archie* (1921), the Holmes stories were firmly established as popular reading on both sides of the Atlantic.
