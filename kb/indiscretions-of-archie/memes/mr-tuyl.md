# Mr. van Tuyl

Mr. van Tuyl is the father of [[reggie-van-tuyl|Reggie van Tuyl]] and the husband of the Florida hostess ("a Mrs. van Tuyl") who invited Archie to her Miami house-party (001-149–150). A wealthy member of New York society, he appears in several social episodes across the novel, most notably as Archie's companion on a jewellery-shopping expedition.

## In the Novel

Archie first encounters the van Tuyl family through Reggie, who is introduced to Archie at a Fifth Avenue club as "son of his Florida hostess" (003-092). The Florida connection — Mrs. van Tuyl's Miami house-party — is the initial link between Archie and the van Tuyl family.

Mr. van Tuyl's most substantial appearance occurs in Section 17, when Archie drags him to a jeweller's shop to help select a birthday present for Lucille. "He gathered in Mr. van Tuyl, of whose taste in these matters he had a high opinion, and dragged him off to a jeweller's" (017-013). At the shop, Mr. van Tuyl murmurs approvingly at a bracelet priced at eight hundred and fifty dollars (017-027), displaying the understated connoisseurship of the wealthy.

Later, Archie introduces him to another character with the casual "My friend, Mr. van Tuyl" (022-154). Mr. van Tuyl also appears near the novel's climax, where his murmured commentary (035-145) suggests his continued presence in Archie's social orbit.

## Key Relationships

- **[[reggie-van-tuyl]]** — His son, heir to the van Tuyl millions
- **[[archie-moffam]]** — Consulted him on Lucille's birthday gift
- **Mrs. van Tuyl** — His wife, the Florida hostess who invited Archie to Miami

## Character

Mr. van Tuyl is characterized primarily through his speech — or rather, its absence. In both of his speaking appearances, he "murmurs" (017-027, 035-145), suggesting a man of few words and understated manner. His "taste in these matters" (017-013) — jewellery selection — implies cultivated discernment and the quiet confidence of old money, in contrast to Archie's impulsive enthusiasm and Reggie's languid unreliability.
