# Cigar-Stand Girl

An unnamed employee working at the cigar counter of the Hotel Cosmopolis, strategically positioned "adjoined the coat-and-[hat]" area of the lobby (018-005). She functions as Archie Moffam's intelligence source, providing him with crucial gossip about the love life of the New York Giants pitcher Gus "Looney" Biddle.

## In the Novel

Archie first approaches her when he "crossed to the cigar-stand to buy a few" items (017-133) while gathering information. During their conversation, "the cigar-stand girl laughed amusedly" (017-145) at his questions, and she proves remarkably forthcoming about hotel gossip. Her assessment of Gus Biddle is blunt and colloquial: "Funny about that guy. He's a nut!" (017-158), she tells Archie, establishing her role as an unfiltered conduit of lobby chatter.

When Archie presses for more details about the Biddle relationship drama, the cigar-stand girl provides crucial intelligence: she and the Girl Friend had attended "a picture last week" (017-190), during which Gus insulted the film star Bryant Washburn — the incident that triggered the couple's quarrel.

During Archie's encounter with the Girl Friend in the lobby, the cigar-stand girl acts as an informal chorus. When the Girl Friend denounces Washburn as "a pill," the "shocked cigar-stand girl" exclaims "Never!" (018-057). She "gave unqualified approval" (018-062) to Archie's peacemaking efforts and was "in favour of a fortn[ight]" (018-069) of reconciliation. Eventually, professional duty calls: "The cigar-stand girl turned to attend to an i[ncoming]" customer (018-073), her role in the drama complete.

## Role in the Novel

The Cigar-Stand Girl exemplifies Wodehouse's use of minor hotel employees as narrative facilitators. Like Parker the valet, she occupies a position that gives her access to private information, which she dispenses freely to Archie. Her function is to bridge the gap between Archie's ignorance and the knowledge he needs to act — in this case, to save both a baseball bet and a romantic relationship.
