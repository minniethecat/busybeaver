# Springfield, Ohio

Springfield, Ohio is a Midwestern American city that appears in the novel as the recovered memory of the Sausage Chappie — the amnesiac war veteran whose identity had been erased by a shrapnel wound. It is the one biographical fact that surfaces through the fog of his memory loss, the anchor point from which his full identity is eventually reconstructed.

## In the Novel

The Sausage Chappie's memory of Springfield emerges during his shift as a waiter in the Cosmopolis grill-room. As his mind begins to clear after weeks of blankness, fragments of his past start to return. The first concrete detail that surfaces is geographical: "I remembered was that I was born at Springfield" (026-103). The recognition comes slowly, "like a mist starting to lift" (026-104), and the full name crystallizes: "Springfield, Ohio" (026-104).

This single recovered fact — a place name — represents the first crack in the wall of amnesia that has separated the Sausage Chappie from his past. It leads eventually to the restoration of his full identity: his name is John Smith, he was an actor before the war, and he has a wife and child who have believed him dead.

The choice of Springfield, Ohio — a quintessentially ordinary Midwestern city — is characteristic of Wodehouse's comic sensibility. The Sausage Chappie's identity is anchored not in romance or grandeur but in a place so unremarkable that its very ordinariness becomes the point.
