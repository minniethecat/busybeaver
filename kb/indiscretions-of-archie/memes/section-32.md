## Section 32

[032-001] “I look on you as a son, laddie, a favourite son!”

[032-002] “If I had a father like you—!”-“Ah, but you haven’t,

[032-003] young-feller-me-lad, and that’s the trouble. If you had, everything

[032-004] would be simple. But as your actual father, if you’ll allow me to say

[032-005] so, is one of the finest specimens of the human vampire-bat in

[032-006] captivity, something has got to be done about it, and you’re dashed

[032-007] lucky to have me in your corner, a guide, philosopher, and friend, full

[032-008] of the fruitiest ideas. Now, if you’ll kindly listen to me for a

[032-009] moment—”

[032-010] “I’ve been listening to you ever since you came in.”

[032-011] “You wouldn’t speak in that harsh tone of voice if you knew all!

[032-012] William, I have a scheme!”

[032-013] “Well?”

[032-014] “The scheme to which I allude is what Maeterlinck would call a

[032-015] lallapaloosa!”

[032-016] “What a little marvel he is!” said [[lucille-brewster|Lucille]], regarding her husband

[032-017] affectionately. “He eats a lot of fish, Bill. That’s what makes him so

[032-018] clever!”

[032-019] “Shrimps!” diagnosed Bill, churlishly.

[032-020] “Do you know the leader of the orchestra in the restaurant downstairs?”

[032-021] asked [[archie-moffam|Archie]], ignoring the slur.

[032-022] “I know there _is_ a leader of the orchestra. What about him?”

[032-023] “A sound fellow. Great pal of mine. I’ve forgotten his name—”

[032-024] “Call him [[spectatia-huskisson|Pootles]]!” suggested Lucille.

[032-025] “Desist!” said [[indiscretions-of-archie-the-novel|Archie]], as a wordless growl proceeded from his stricken

[032-026] brother-in-law. “Temper your hilarity with a modicum of reserve. This

[032-027] girlish frivolity is unseemly. Well, I’m going to have a [[conversation-and-dialogue|chat]] with this

[032-028] chappie and fix it all up.”

[032-029] “Fix what up?”

[032-030] “The whole jolly business. I’m going to kill two birds with one stone.

[032-031] I’ve a composer chappie popping about in the background whose one

[032-032] ambish. is to have his pet song sung before a discriminating audience.

[032-033] You have a singer straining at the leash. I’m going to arrange with

[032-034] this egg who leads the orchestra that your female shall sing my

[032-035] chappie’s song downstairs one night during [[dining-and-social-ritual|dinner]]. How about it? Is it

[032-036] or is it not a ball of fire?”

[032-037] “It’s not a bad idea,” admitted Bill, brightening visibly. “I wouldn’t

[032-038] have thought you had it in you.”

[032-039] “Why not?”

[032-040] “Well—”

[032-041] “It’s a capital idea,” said Lucille. “Quite out of the question, of

[032-042] course.”

[032-043] “How do you mean?”

[032-044] “Don’t you know that the one thing Father hates more than anything else

[032-045] in the world is anything like a cabaret? People are always coming to

[032-046] him, suggesting that it would brighten up the dinner hour if he had

[032-047] singers and things, and he crushes them into little bits. He thinks

[032-048] there’s nothing that lowers the tone of a place more. He’ll bite you in

[032-049] three places when you suggest it to him!”

[032-050] “Ah! But has it escaped your notice, lighting system of my soul, that

[032-051] the dear old dad is not at present in residence? He went off to fish at

[032-052] Lake What’s-its-name this morning.”

[032-053] “You aren’t dreaming of doing this without asking him?”

[032-054] “That was the general idea.”

[032-055] “But he’ll be furious when he finds out.”

[032-056] “But will he find out? I ask you, will he?”

[032-057] “Of course he will.”

[032-058] “I don’t see why he should,” said Bill, on whose plastic mind the plan

[032-059] had made a deep impression.

[032-060] “He won’t,” said Archie, confidently. “This wheeze is for one night

[032-061] only. By the time the jolly old guv’nor returns, bitten to the bone by

[032-062] mosquitoes, with one small stuffed trout in his suit-case, everything

[032-063] will be over and all quiet once more along the Potomac. The scheme is

[032-064] this. My chappie wants his song heard by a publisher. Your girl wants

[032-065] her voice heard by one of the blighters who get up concerts and all

[032-066] that sort of thing. No doubt you know such a bird, whom you could

[032-067] invite to the hotel for a bit of dinner?”

[032-068] “I know Carl Steinburg. As a matter of fact, I was thinking of writing

[032-069] to him about Spectatia.”

[032-070] “You’re absolutely sure that _is_ her name?” said Archie, his voice

[032-071] still tinged with incredulity. “Oh, well, I suppose she told you so

[032-072] herself, and no doubt she knows best. That will be topping. Rope in

[032-073] your pal and hold him down at the table till the finish. Lucille, the

[032-074] beautiful vision on the sky-line yonder, and I will be at another table

[032-075] entertaining [[maxie-blumenthal|Maxie Blumenthal]].”

[032-076] “Who on earth is Maxie Blumenthal?” asked Lucille.

[032-077] “One of my boyhood chums. A music-publisher. I’ll get him to come

[032-078] along, and then we’ll all be set. At the conclusion of the performance

[032-079] Miss—” Archie winced—“Miss Spectatia Huskisson will be signed up for a

[032-080] forty weeks’ tour, and jovial old Blumenthal will be making all

[032-081] arrangements for publishing the song. Two birds, as I indicated before,

[032-082] with one stone! How about it?”

[032-083] “It’s a winner,” said Bill.

[032-084] “Of course,” said Archie, “I’m not urging you. I merely make the

[032-085] suggestion. If you know a better ’ole go to it!”

[032-086] “It’s terrific!” said Bill.

[032-087] “It’s absurd!” said Lucille.

[032-088] “My dear old partner of joys and sorrows,” said Archie, wounded, “we

[032-089] court criticism, but this is mere abuse. What seems to be the

[032-090] difficulty?”

[032-091] “The leader of the orchestra would be afraid to do it.”

[032-092] “Ten dollars—supplied by William here—push it over, Bill, old man—will

[032-093] remove his tremors.”

[032-094] “And Father’s certain to find out.”

[032-095] “Am I afraid of Father?” cried Archie, manfully. “Well, yes, I am!” he

[032-096] added, after a moment’s reflection. “But I don’t see how he can

[032-097] possibly get to know.”

[032-098] “Of course he can’t,” said Bill, decidedly. “Fix it up as soon as you

[032-099] can, Archie. This is what the doctor ordered.”

[032-100] CHAPTER XXIV.

[032-101] THE MELTING OF MR. CONNOLLY

[032-102] The main dining-room of the [[hotel-cosmopolis-place|Hotel Cosmopolis]] is a decorous place. The

[032-103] lighting is artistically dim, and the genuine old tapestries on the

[032-104] walls seem, with their mediaeval calm, to discourage any essay in the

[032-105] riotous. Soft-footed waiters shimmer to and fro over thick, expensive

[032-106] carpets to the music of an orchestra which abstains wholly from the

[032-107] noisy modernity of jazz. To Archie, who during the past few days had

[032-108] been privileged to hear Miss Huskisson rehearsing, the place had a sort

[032-109] of brooding quiet, like the ocean just before the arrival of a cyclone.

[032-110] As Lucille had said, Miss Huskisson’s voice was loud. It was a powerful

[032-111] organ, and there was no doubt that it would take the cloistered

[032-112] stillness of [[hotel-cosmopolis|the Cosmopolis]] dining-room and stand it on one ear. Almost

[032-113] unconsciously, Archie found himself bracing his muscles and holding his

[032-114] breath as he had done in France at the approach of the zero hour, when

[032-115] awaiting the first roar of a barrage. He listened mechanically to the

[032-116] conversation of Mr. Blumenthal.

[032-117] The music-publisher was talking with some vehemence on the subject of

[032-118] Labour. A recent printers’ strike had bitten deeply into Mr.

[032-119] Blumenthal’s soul. The working man, he considered, was rapidly landing

[032-120] God’s Country in the soup, and he had twice upset his glass with the

[032-121] vehemence of his gesticulation. He was an energetic right-and-left-hand

[032-122] talker.

[032-123] “The more you give ’em the more they want!” he complained. “There’s no

[032-124] pleasing ’em! It isn’t only in my business. There’s your father, Mrs.

[032-125] Moffam!”

[032-126] “Good God! Where?” said Archie, starting.

[032-127] “I say, take your father’s case. He’s doing all he knows to get this

[032-128] new hotel of his finished, and what happens? A man gets fired for

[032-129] loafing on his job, and Connolly calls a strike. And the building

[032-130] operations are held up till the thing’s settled! It isn’t right!”

[032-131] “It’s a great shame,” agreed Lucille. “I was reading about it in the

[032-132] paper this morning.”

[032-133] “That man Connolly’s a tough guy. You’d think, being a personal friend

[032-134] of your father, he would—”

[032-135] “I didn’t know they were friends.”

[032-136] “Been friends for years. But a lot of difference that makes. Out come

[032-137] the men just the same. It isn’t right! I was saying it wasn’t right!”

[032-138] repeated Mr. Blumenthal to Archie, for he was a man who liked the

[032-139] attention of every member of his audience.

[032-140] Archie did not reply. He was staring glassily across the room at two

[032-141] men who had just come in. One was a large, stout, square-faced man of

[032-142] commanding personality. The other was Mr. [[daniel-brewster|Daniel Brewster]].

[032-143] Mr. Blumenthal followed his gaze.

[032-144] “Why, there is Connolly coming in now!”

[032-145] “Father!” gasped Lucille.

[032-146] Her eyes met Archie’s. Archie took a hasty drink of ice-water.

[032-147] “This,” he murmured, “has torn it!”

[032-148] “Archie, you must do something!”

[032-149] “I know! But what?”

[032-150] “What’s the trouble?” enquired Mr. Blumenthal, mystified.

[032-151] “Go over to their table and talk to them,” said Lucille.

[032-152] “Me!” Archie quivered. “No, I say, old thing, really!”

[032-153] “Get them away!”

[032-154] “How do you mean?”

[032-155] “I know!” cried Lucille, inspired, “Father promised that you should be

[032-156] manager of the new hotel when it was built. Well, then, this strike

[032-157] affects you just as much as anybody else. You have a perfect right to

[032-158] talk it over with them. Go and ask them to have dinner up in our suite

[032-159] where you can discuss it quietly. Say that up there they won’t be

[032-160] disturbed by the—the music.”

[032-161] At this moment, while Archie wavered, hesitating like a diver on the

[032-162] edge of a spring-board who is trying to summon up the necessary nerve

[032-163] to project himself into the deep, a bell-boy approached the table where

[032-164] the Messrs. Brewster and Connolly had seated themselves. He murmured

[032-165] something in Mr. Brewster’s ear, and the proprietor of the Cosmopolis

[032-166] rose and followed him out of the room.

[032-167] “Quick! Now’s your chance!” said Lucille, eagerly. “Father’s been

[032-168] called to the telephone. Hurry!”

[032-169] Archie took another drink of ice-water to steady his shaking

[032-170] nerve-centers, pulled down his waistcoat, straightened his tie, and

[032-171] then, with something of the air of a Roman gladiator entering the

[032-172] arena, tottered across the room. Lucille turned to entertain the

[032-173] perplexed music-publisher.

[032-174] The nearer Archie got to Mr. Aloysius Connolly the less did he like the

[032-175] [[appearance-and-first-impressions|looks]] of him. Even at a distance the Labour leader had had a formidable

[032-176] aspect. Seen close to, he looked even more uninviting. His face had the

[032-177] appearance of having been carved out of granite, and the eye which

[032-178] collided with Archie’s as the latter, with an attempt at an

[032-179] ingratiating smile, pulled up a chair and sat down at the table was

[032-180] hard and frosty. [[mr-connolly|Mr. Connolly]] gave the impression that he would be a

[032-181] good man to have on your side during a rough-and-tumble fight down on

[032-182] the water-front or in some lumber-camp, but he did not look chummy.

[032-183] “Hallo-allo-allo!” said Archie.

[032-184] “Who the devil,” inquired Mr. Connolly, “are you?”

[032-185] “My name’s Archibald Moffam.”

[032-186] “That’s not my fault.”

[032-187] “I’m jolly old Brewster’s son-in-law.”

[032-188] “Glad to meet you.”

[032-189] “Glad to meet _you_,” said Archie, handsomely.

[032-190] “Well, good-bye!” said Mr. Connolly.

[032-191] “Eh?”

[032-192] “Run along and sell your papers. Your [[fatherhood-and-authority|father-in-law]] and I have business

[032-193] to discuss.”

[032-194] “Yes, I know.”

[032-195] “Private,” added Mr. Connolly.

[032-196] “Oh, but I’m in on this binge, you know. I’m going to be the manager of

[032-197] the new hotel.”

[032-198] “You!”

[032-199] “Absolutely!”

[032-200] “Well, well!” said Mr. Connolly, noncommittally.

[032-201] Archie, pleased with the smoothness with which matters had opened, bent

[032-202] forward winsomely.

[032-203] “I say, you know! It won’t do, you know! Absolutely no! Not a bit like

[032-204] it! No, no, far from it! Well, how about it? How do we go? What? Yes?

[032-205] No?”

[032-206] “What on earth are you talking about?”

[032-207] “Call it off, old thing!”

[032-208] “Call what off?”

[032-209] “This festive old strike.”

[032-210] “Not on your—hallo, Dan! Back again?”

[032-211] Mr. Brewster, looming over the table like a thundercloud, regarded

[032-212] Archie with more than his customary hostility. Life was no pleasant

[032-213] thing for the proprietor of the Cosmopolis just now. Once a man starts

[032-214] building hotels, the thing becomes like dram-drinking. Any hitch, any

[032-215] sudden cutting-off of the daily dose, has the worst effects; and the

[032-216] strike which was holding up the construction of his latest effort had

[032-217] plunged Mr. Brewster into a restless gloom. In addition to having this

[032-218] strike on his hands, he had had to abandon his annual fishing-trip just

[032-219] when he had begun to enjoy it; and, as if all this were not enough,

[032-220] here was his son-in-law sitting at his table. Mr. Brewster had a

[032-221] feeling that this was more than man was meant to bear.
