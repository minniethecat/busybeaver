# British Army

The British Army is the military force in which [[archie-moffam|Archie Moffam]] served as a second lieutenant during the First World War. This biographical detail, though mentioned only in passing, is essential to Archie's character: it establishes his social class (officer rank), his generation (the war-shadowed young men of the 1910s), and his post-war situation — a gentleman of modest means navigating civilian life after the great disruption.

## In the Novel

Archie's military service surfaces at several points in the narrative. A reference to his rank — "Oh, second lieutenant" — suggests the casual manner in which his war record is acknowledged, both by himself and by others. The war is part of the texture of his world rather than a subject of sustained reflection.

The narrative voice also deploys military imagery for comic effect. The "entire German army" (019-032) appears in a simile, while a character is said to possess "the nerve of an army mule" (027-012) — both instances using the army as a rhetorical measuring stick for exaggeration and absurdity, in keeping with the novel's comic mode.

## Real-World Context

The British Army of the First World War was one of the largest military organisations in history, mobilising millions of men between 1914 and 1918. Commissioned officers — second lieutenants being the most junior rank — were drawn disproportionately from the upper and middle classes. For a character like Archie Moffam, whose creator P. G. Wodehouse placed him squarely in the tradition of the well-meaning but hapless young English gentleman, military service was both a biographical given and a source of comic incongruity.
