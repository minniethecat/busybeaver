# Percy the Bulldog

**Percy** is a bulldog who appears in several chapters of *Indiscretions of Archie*, owned by a woman involved in theatrical productions at the hotel. He is described as a "fine bulldog" with a formidable appearance, but his temperament proves more amenable than his looks suggest when properly approached.

## Description

Percy is introduced as "a beastly great whacking brute of a bulldog" (014-111), brought to rehearsals by his owner. When Archie first encounters the dog, it is described in impressive terms: "massive chin resting on the edge of the wicker-work, lay a fine bulldog" (015-068–069). Despite his intimidating appearance, Percy responds well to Archie's experienced handling — Archie, himself a dog-lover who "knew all about dogs, their points, their manners, their customs, and their treatment in sickness and in health" (015-079–081).

## Encounters with Archie

Archie first meets Percy under surprising circumstances, returning to his hotel room to find "somebody else's dressing-gowns and bulldogs" (015-095). The encounter begins with mutual wariness — "There was a lengthy pause in which the bulldog looked earnestly at Archie and Archie looked earnestly at the bulldog" (015-072–073) — but Archie's natural affinity with dogs quickly transforms the situation.

## The Illness Episode

In a later chapter, Percy falls suddenly ill. His owner cries out "Percy! Percy, come to muzzer!" (016-096), but the dog proves unable to move properly: "He seemed quite unable to drag his limbs across the room. There was a curious arch in his back, and, as his mistress touched him, he cried out plaintively" (016-106–108). Archie, drawing on his veterinary knowledge, diagnoses the problem by passing his hand over the dog's back (016-127), demonstrating his practical expertise with animals.
