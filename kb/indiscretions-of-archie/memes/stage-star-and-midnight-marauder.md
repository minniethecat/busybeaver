# "Stage Star and Midnight Marauder"

Two imagined newspaper headlines murmured by [[miss-silverton|Miss Silverton]] — a small but revealing window into her theatrical ambitions and her habit of seeing herself as the heroine of a dramatic story.

## The Quote

> "I did think that for once my guardian angel had gone back to work and was doing something for me. 'Stage Star and Midnight Marauder,'" murmured Miss Silverton, wistfully. "'Footlight Favourite Foils Felon.'" (016-002)

## Context

[[miss-silverton|Miss Silverton]], an actress at the Hermitage Theatre, speaks these lines after an incident involving [[archie-moffam|Archie]] and a would-be criminal. The incident, she reflects, should have been her moment of glory — a real-life drama in which she, the stage star, foiled a felon and earned a headline. The reality, she laments, fell short of this vision.

The headlines are character-revealing in their specificity. [[miss-silverton|Miss Silverton]] does not simply want recognition; she wants the p[[art-and-collecting|art]]icular kind of recognition that comes from tabloid newspaper headlines — alliterative, breathless, and slightly absurd. Her wistful delivery ("murmured... wistfully") suggests she has spent considerable time composing these headlines in her imagination.

## Speaker

- **[[miss-silverton]]** — The actress who dreams of headlines. Her profession and her personality converge in the gap between the dramatic recognition she imagines and the modest reality she inhabits.

## Significance

[[p-g-wodehouse|Wodehouse]] uses these imagined headlines to do what he does best: puncture pretension with affection. [[miss-silverton|Miss Silverton]]'s headlines are genuinely funny — the overblown alliteration, the self-casting as both "star" and "favourite" — but they are also genuinely human. Everyone, [[p-g-wodehouse|Wodehouse]] suggests, is the hero of their own story; [[miss-silverton|Miss Silverton]] has simply gone to the trouble of writing the headlines in advance.

The headlines also serve as a gentle satire of newspaper sensationalism. "[[plucky-actress-captures-burglar|Footlight Favourite Foils Felon]]" is exactly the kind of alliterative excess that tabloid sub-editors of the era favored. [[p-g-wodehouse|Wodehouse]], who knew the newspaper world well, is winking at a familiar convention.
