# Rallying Round Percy

**Rallying Round Percy** is the title of Chapter XIII of *Indiscretions of Archie*, and the event it describes: a comic episode in which [[archie-moffam|Archie Moffam]] discovers a bulldog named [[percy-the-bulldog|Percy]] occupying his hotel room, navigates a series of awkward confrontations, and ultimately finds himself "rallying round" the animal when it falls ill.

## The Discovery

The episode begins with Archie returning to his room at the [[hotel-cosmopolis|Hotel Cosmopolis]] to find it unexpectedly inhabited. "Archie's heart jumped sideways and upwards with a wiggling movement, turning two somersaults, and stopped beating" (015-096–097) as he realises his room contains "somebody else's dressing-gowns and bulldogs" (015-095). The bulldog — Percy — eyes him from a basket: "There was a lengthy pause in which the bulldog looked earnestly at Archie and Archie looked earnestly at the bulldog" (015-072–073).

## Archie and Dogs

The narrative takes care to establish Archie's credentials: "Normally, Archie was a dog-lover. His hurry was never so great as to prevent him stopping, when in the street, and introducing himself to any dog he met" (015-074–076). Though denied a veterinary career "by the years," Archie "knew all about dogs, their points, their manners, their customs, and their treatment in sickness and in health" (015-079–081). This expertise proves critical when the episode continues.

## The Crisis

The situation escalates when Percy's owner — [[miss-silverton|Miss Silverton]] — enters and accuses Archie of intruding. In a later chapter, Percy falls suddenly ill: "He seemed quite unable to drag his limbs across the room. There was a curious arch in his back, and, as his mistress touched him, he cried out plaintively" (016-106–108). Archie's veterinary knowledge allows him to diagnose and treat the dog, transforming him from suspected intruder to valued ally.

## The Reconciliation

With Percy treated, Archie seizes the moment to mediate between Miss Silverton and [[george-benham|George Benham]], the theatrical manager she has abandoned after the bulldog incident. Miss Silverton's grievance is visceral:

> "I'll never forgive that man for kicking Percy!" (016-161)

Archie, armed with Benham's version of events, mounts a diplomatic defence:

> "Now listen, dear old soul. You've got the story all wrong. As a matter of fact, jolly old Benham told me himself that he has the greatest esteem and respect for Percy, and wouldn't have kicked him for the world. And, you know it was more a sort of push than a kick. You might almost call it a light shove. The fact is, it was beastly dark in the theatre, and he was legging it sideways for some reason or other, no doubt with the best motives, and unfortunately he happened to stub his toe on the poor old bean." (016-162–169)

When Miss Silverton protests that Benham never explained himself, Archie counters: "As far as I could make out, you didn't give him a chance" (016-171). He seals the argument with a blend of flattery and practical concern — "Besides, think of all the coves thrown out of work—the thingummabobs and the poor what-d'you-call-'ems!" (016-182–183) — and Miss Silverton agrees to return to New York, restoring harmony to the theatrical company and completing Archie's transformation from intruder to indispensable peacemaker.

## Narrative Significance

The episode exemplifies the novel's farcical structure: a misunderstanding (wrong room), an intimidating obstacle (the bulldog), comic escalation (the owner's arrival), and eventual resolution through Archie's unexpected competence. The chapter title's pun — "rallying round" suggesting both support and the round physique of a bulldog — is characteristic of Wodehouse's wordplay.
