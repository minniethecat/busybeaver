# Plucky Actress Captures Burglar

> "On the front page, with photographs: 'Plucky Actress Captures Burglar.'" (015-175)

## The Quote

"Plucky Actress Captures Burglar" is an imagined newspaper headline — a publicity fantasy dreamed up by [[miss-silverton|Miss Silverton]], the moving-picture actress, during the chaotic hotel-room mix-up in Chapter 15. Having caught sight of a leg protruding from under her bed, she briefly believes she has cornered a genuine burglar and begins mentally composing the front-page story that would launch her career. The actual headline never appears in print; it exists only in [[miss-silverton|Miss Silverton]]'s wishful imagination, making it all the more revealing of her character.

[[miss-silverton|Miss Silverton]] also produces a second, equally florid variant moments later: "'[[stage-star-and-midnight-marauder|Stage Star and Midnight Marauder]],' murmured [[miss-silverton|Miss Silverton]], wistfully. 'Footlight Favourite Foils Felon.'" (015-178–180)

## Context

The headline occurs during the extended hotel-room comedy of Chapter 15, in which [[archie-moffam|Archie Moffam]], through a series of misunderstandings, finds himself occupying the room that [[miss-silverton|Miss Silverton]] customarily reserves at the [[hotel-cosmopolis|Hotel Cosmopolis]] (015-070–015-090). When the actress returns unexpectedly and discovers evidence of an intruder, her first reaction is not fear but professional calculation:

> "Nothing breaks right for me in this darned world," she said, regretfully. "When I caught sight of your leg sticking out from under the bed, I did think that everything was all lined up for a real find and, at last, I could close my eyes and see the thing in the papers. On the front page, with photographs: 'Plucky Actress Captures Burglar.' Darn it!" (015-175–180)

Her disappointment when she discovers the "burglar" is merely [[archie-moffam|Archie]] — a harmless, apologetic Englishman — is palpable. She had been hoping for career-making publicity, and instead got a room-service mix-up. The scene climaxes with [[miss-silverton|Miss Silverton]] producing an [[miss-silvertons-pistol|automatic pistol]] and the arrival of the hotel detective, all stemming from her thw[[art-and-collecting|art]]ed headline fantasy.

## Speaker

- **[[miss-silverton]]** — A moving-picture actress staying at the [[hotel-cosmopolis|Hotel Cosmopolis]] who is perpetually frustrated by her press agent's incompetence. She complains that her agent "eats well and sleeps well and has just enough intelligence to cash his monthly cheque" but generates no meaningful publicity (015-080–085). The "Plucky Actress" headline is her own PR instinct at work — she knows exactly what kind of story sells newspapers.

## Significance

The "Plucky Actress Captures Burglar" moment is [[p-g-wodehouse|Wodehouse]] at his most economically satirical. In a single imagined headline, he skewers the machinery of early Hollywood publicity, the hunger of minor celebrities for front-page attention, and the gap between theatrical fantasy and mundane reality. [[miss-silverton|Miss Silverton]]'s instant mental composition of her own heroic headline — complete with photographs — is a miniature portrait of the publicity-driven culture that [[archie-moffam|Archie]], with his [[gentlemanly-conduct|gentleman]]ly obliviousness, blunders through without ever quite understanding.

The headline's florid tabloid style ("Plucky Actress," the alliterative "Footlight Favourite Foils Felon") also parodies the journalistic conventions of the 1920s, when [[p-g-wodehouse|Wodehouse]] himself was writing about Broadway and Hollywood for American magazines.
