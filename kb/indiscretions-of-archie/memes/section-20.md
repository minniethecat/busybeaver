## Section 20

[020-001] [[archie-moffam|Archie]] meditated. The future was black enough as it was. He shrank from

[020-002] the prospect of being confronted next day, at the height of his misery,

[020-003] with these appalling reach-me-downs.

[020-004] An idea struck him.

[020-005] “Yes, send ’em,” he said.

[020-006] “What’s the name and address?”

[020-007] “[[daniel-brewster|Daniel Brewster]],” said [[indiscretions-of-archie-the-novel|Archie]], “[[hotel-cosmopolis-place|Hotel Cosmopolis]].”

[020-008] It was a long time since he had given his [[fatherhood-and-authority|father-in-law]] a present.

[020-009] Archie went out into the street, and began to walk pensively down a now

[020-010] peaceful Ninth Avenue. Out of the depths that covered him, black as the

[020-011] pit from pole to pole, no single ray of [[hope-and-optimism|hope]] came to cheer him. He

[020-012] could not, like the poet, thank whatever gods there be for his

[020-013] unconquerable soul, for his soul was licked to a splinter. He felt

[020-014] alone and friendless in a rotten world. With the best intentions, he

[020-015] had succeeded only in landing himself squarely amongst the ribstons.

[020-016] Why had he not been content with his wealth, instead of risking it on

[020-017] that blighted bet with [[reggie-van-tuyl|Reggie]]? Why had he trailed the Girl Friend, dash

[020-018] her! He might have known that he would only make an ass of himself.

[020-019] And, because he had done so, Looney Biddle’s left hand, that priceless

[020-020] left hand before which opposing batters quailed and wilted, was out of

[020-021] action, resting in a sling, careened like a damaged battleship; and any

[020-022] chance [[new-york-giants|the Giants]] might have had of beating [[pittsburg-pirates|the Pirates]] was

[020-023] gone—gone—as surely as that thousand dollars which should have bought a

[020-024] birthday present for [[lucille-brewster|Lucille]].

[020-025] A birthday present for Lucille! He groaned in bitterness of spirit. She

[020-026] would be coming back to-night, dear girl, all smiles and happiness,

[020-027] wondering what he was going to give her tomorrow. And when to-morrow

[020-028] dawned, all he would be able to give her would be a kind smile. A nice

[020-029] state of things! A jolly situation! A [[good-egg|thoroughly good egg]], he did _not_

[020-030] think!

[020-031] It seemed to Archie that Nature, contrary to her usual custom of

[020-032] indifference to human suffering, was mourning with him. The sky was

[020-033] overcast, and the sun had ceased to shine. There was a sort of

[020-034] sombreness in the afternoon, which fitted in with his mood. And then

[020-035] something splashed on his face.

[020-036] It says much for Archie’s pre-occupation that his first thought, as,

[020-037] after a few scattered drops, as though the clouds were submitting

[020-038] samples for approval, the whole sky suddenly began to stream like a

[020-039] shower-bath, was that this was simply an additional infliction which he

[020-040] was called upon to bear, On top of all his other troubles he would get

[020-041] soaked to the skin or have to hang about in some doorway. He cursed

[020-042] richly, and sped for shelter.

[020-043] The rain was setting about its work in earnest. The world was full of

[020-044] that rending, swishing sound which accompanies the more violent summer

[020-045] storms. Thunder crashed, and lightning flicked out of the grey heavens.

[020-046] Out in the street the raindrops bounded up off the stones like fairy

[020-047] fountains. Archie surveyed them morosely from his refuge in the

[020-048] entrance of a shop.

[020-049] And then, suddenly, like one of those flashes which were lighting up

[020-050] the gloomy sky, a thought lit up his mind.

[020-051] “By Jove! If this keeps up, there won’t be a ball-game to-day!”

[020-052] With trembling fingers he pulled out his watch. The hands pointed to

[020-053] five minutes to three. A blessed vision came to him of a moist and

[020-054] disappointed crowd receiving rain-checks up at the Polo Grounds.

[020-055] “Switch it on, you blighters!” he cried, addressing the leaden clouds.

[020-056] “Switch it on more and more!”

[020-057] It was shortly before five o’clock that a young man bounded into a

[020-058] jeweller’s shop near the [[hotel-cosmopolis|Hotel Cosmopolis]]—a young man who, in spite of

[020-059] the fact that his coat was torn near the collar and that he oozed water

[020-060] from every inch of his drenched clothes, appeared in the highest

[020-061] spirits. It was only when he spoke that the jeweller recognised in the

[020-062] human sponge the immaculate youth who had looked in that morning to

[020-063] order a bracelet.

[020-064] “I say, old lad,” said this young man, “you remember that jolly little

[020-065] what-not you showed me before [[dining-and-social-ritual|lunch]]?”

[020-066] “The bracelet, sir?”

[020-067] “As you observe with a manly candour which does you credit, my dear old

[020-068] jeweller, [[lucilles-bracelet|the bracelet]]. Well, produce, exhibit, and bring it forth,

[020-069] would you mind? Trot it out! Slip it across on a lordly dish!”

[020-070] “You wished me, surely, to put it aside and send it to the Cosmopolis

[020-071] to-morrow?”

[020-072] The young man tapped the jeweller earnestly on his substantial chest.

[020-073] “What I wished and what I wish now are two bally separate and dashed

[020-074] distinct things, friend of my college days! Never put off till

[020-075] to-morrow what you can do to-day, and all that! I’m not taking any more

[020-076] chances. Not for me! For others, yes, but not for Archibald! Here are

[020-077] the doubloons, produce the jolly bracelet. Thanks!”

[020-078] The jeweller counted the notes with the same unction which Archie had

[020-079] observed earlier in the day in the proprietor of the second-hand

[020-080] clothes-shop. The process made him genial.

[020-081] “A nasty, wet day, sir, it’s been,” he observed, chattily.

[020-082] Archie shook his head.

[020-083] “Old friend,” he said, “you’re all wrong. Far otherwise, and not a bit

[020-084] like it, my dear old trafficker in gems! You’ve put your finger on the

[020-085] one aspect of this blighted p.m. that really deserves credit and

[020-086] respect. Rarely in the experience of a lifetime have I encountered a

[020-087] day so absolutely bally in nearly every shape and form, but there was

[020-088] one thing that saved it, and that was its merry old wetness! Toodle-oo,

[020-089] laddie!”

[020-090] “Good evening, sir,” said the jeweller.

[020-091] CHAPTER XVI.

[020-092] ARCHIE ACCEPTS A SITUATION

[020-093] Lucille moved her wrist slowly round, the better to examine the new

[020-094] bracelet.

[020-095] “You really are an angel, angel!” she murmured.

[020-096] “Like it?” said Archie complacently.

[020-097] “_Like_ it! Why, it’s gorgeous! It must have cost a [[wealth-and-money|fortune]].”

[020-098] “Oh, nothing to speak of. Just a few hard-earned pieces of eight. Just

[020-099] a few doubloons from the old oak chest.”

[020-100] “But I didn’t know there were any doubloons in the old oak chest.”

[020-101] “Well, as a matter of fact,” admitted Archie, “at one point in the

[020-102] proceedings there weren’t. But an aunt of mine in [[england|England]]—peace be on

[020-103] her head!—happened to send me a chunk of the necessary at what you

[020-104] might call the psychological moment.”

[020-105] “And you spent it all on a birthday present for me! Archie!” Lucille

[020-106] gazed at her husband adoringly. “Archie, do you know what I think?”

[020-107] “What?”

[020-108] “You’re the perfect man!”

[020-109] “No, really! What ho!”

[020-110] “Yes,” said Lucille firmly. “I’ve long suspected it, and now I know. I

[020-111] don’t think there’s anybody like you in the world.”

[020-112] Archie patted her hand.

[020-113] “It’s a rummy thing,” he observed, “but your father said almost exactly

[020-114] that to me only yesterday. Only I don’t fancy he meant the same as you.

[020-115] To be absolutely frank, his exact expression was that he thanked God

[020-116] there was only one of me.”

[020-117] A troubled look came into Lucille’s grey eyes.

[020-118] “It’s a shame about father. I do wish he appreciated you. But you

[020-119] mustn’t be too hard on him.”

[020-120] “Me?” said Archie. “Hard on your father? Well, dash it all, I don’t

[020-121] think I treat him with what you might call actual brutality, what! I

[020-122] mean to say, my whole idea is rather to keep out of the old lad’s way

[020-123] and curl up in a ball if I can’t dodge him. I’d just as soon be hard on

[020-124] a stampeding elephant! I wouldn’t for the world say anything

[020-125] derogatory, as it were, to your jolly old pater, but there is no

[020-126] getting away from the fact that he’s by way of being one of our leading

[020-127] man-eating fishes. It would be idle to deny that he considers that you

[020-128] let down the proud old name of Brewster a bit when you brought me in

[020-129] and laid me on the mat.”

[020-130] “Anyone would be lucky to get you for a son-in-law, precious.”

[020-131] “I fear me, light of my life, the dad doesn’t see eye to eye with you

[020-132] on that point. No, every time I get hold of a daisy, I give him another

[020-133] chance, but it always works out at ‘He loves me not!’”

[020-134] “You must make allowances for him, darling.”

[020-135] “Right-o! But I hope devoutly that he doesn’t catch me at it. I’ve a

[020-136] sort of idea that if the old dad discovered that I was making

[020-137] allowances for him, he would have from ten to fifteen fits.”

[020-138] “He’s worried just now, you know.”

[020-139] “I didn’t know. He doesn’t confide in me much.”

[020-140] “He’s worried about that waiter.”

[020-141] “What waiter, queen of my soul?”

[020-142] “A man called [[salvatore|Salvatore]]. Father dismissed him some time ago.”

[020-143] “Salvatore!”

[020-144] “Probably you don’t remember him. He used to wait on this table.”

[020-145] “Why—”

[020-146] “And father dismissed him, apparently, and now there’s all sorts of

[020-147] trouble. You see, father wants to build this new hotel of his, and he

[020-148] thought he’d got the site and everything and could start building right

[020-149] away: and now he finds that this man Salvatore’s mother owns a little

[020-150] newspaper and tobacco shop right in the middle of the site, and there’s

[020-151] no way of getting him out without buying the shop, and he won’t sell.

[020-152] At least, he’s made his mother promise that she won’t sell.”

[020-153] “A boy’s best friend is his mother,” said Archie approvingly. “I had a

[020-154] sort of idea all along—”

[020-155] “So father’s in despair.”

[020-156] Archie drew at his cigarette meditatively.

[020-157] “I remember a chappie—a policeman he was, as a matter of fact, and

[020-158] incidentally a fairly pronounced blighter—remarking to me some time ago

[020-159] that you could trample on the poor man’s face but you mustn’t be

[020-160] surprised if he bit you in the leg while you were doing it. Apparently

[020-161] this is what has happened to the old dad. I had a sort of idea all

[020-162] along that old friend Salvatore would come out strong in the end if you

[020-163] only gave him time. Brainy sort of feller! Great pal of

[020-164] mine.”—Lucille’s small face lightened. She gazed at Archie with proud

[020-165] affection. She felt that she ought to have known that he was the one to

[020-166] solve this difficulty.

[020-167] “You’re wonderful, darling! Is he really a friend of yours?”

[020-168] “Absolutely. Many’s the time he and I have chatted in this very

[020-169] grill-room.”

[020-170] “Then it’s all right. If you went to him and argued with him, he would

[020-171] agree to sell the shop, and father would be happy. Think how grateful

[020-172] father would be to you! It would make all the difference.”

[020-173] Archie turned this over in his mind.

[020-174] “Something in that,” he agreed.

[020-175] “It would make him see what a pet lambkin you really are!”

[020-176] “Well,” said Archie, “I’m bound to say that any scheme which what you

[020-177] might call culminates in your father regarding me as a pet lambkin

[020-178] ought to receive one’s best attention. How much did he offer Salvatore

[020-179] for his shop?”

[020-180] “I don’t know. There is father.—Call him over and ask him.”

[020-181] Archie glanced over to where Mr. Brewster had sunk moodily into a chair

[020-182] at a neighbouring table. It was plain even at that distance that Daniel

[020-183] Brewster had his troubles and was bearing them with an ill grace. He

[020-184] was scowling absently at the table-cloth.

[020-185] “_You_ call him,” said Archie, having inspected his formidable

[020-186] relative. “You know him better.”

[020-187] “Let’s go over to him.”

[020-188] They crossed the room. Lucille sat down opposite her father. Archie

[020-189] draped himself over a chair in the background.

[020-190] “Father, dear,” said Lucille. “Archie has got an idea.”

[020-191] “Archie?” said Mr. Brewster incredulously.

[020-192] “This is me,” said Archie, indicating himself with a spoon. “The tall,

[020-193] distinguished-looking bird.”

[020-194] “What new fool-thing is he up to now?”

[020-195] “It’s a splendid idea, father. He wants to help you over your new

[020-196] hotel.”

[020-197] “Wants to run it for me, I suppose?”

[020-198] “By Jove!” said Archie, reflectively. “That’s not a bad scheme! I never

[020-199] thought of running an hotel. I shouldn’t mind taking a stab at it.”
