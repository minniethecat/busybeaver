## Section 5

[005-001] There followed a brief council of war, which, as it took place in the

[005-002] bedroom, was inaudible to [[archie-moffam|Archie]] except as a distant growling noise. He

[005-003] could distinguish no words, but, as it was succeeded by a general

[005-004] trampling of large boots in the direction of the door and then by

[005-005] silence, he gathered that the pack, having drawn the studio and found

[005-006] it empty, had decided to return to other and more profitable duties. He

[005-007] gave them a reasonable interval for removing themselves, and then poked

[005-008] his head cautiously over the settee.

[005-009] All was peace. The place was empty. No sound disturbed the stillness.

[005-010] [[indiscretions-of-archie-the-novel|Archie]] emerged. For the first time in this morning of disturbing

[005-011] occurrences he began to feel that God was in his heaven and all right

[005-012] with the world. At last things were beginning to brighten up a bit, and

[005-013] life might be said to have taken on some of the aspects of a good egg.

[005-014] He stretched himself, for it is cramping work lying under settees, and,

[005-015] proceeding to the bedroom, picked up the tweed trousers again.

[005-016] Clothes had a fascination for Archie. Another man, in similar

[005-017] circumstances, might have hurried over his toilet; but Archie, faced by

[005-018] a difficult choice of ties, rather strung the thing out. He selected a

[005-019] specimen which did great credit to the taste of [[elmer-m-moon|Mr. Moon]], evidently one

[005-020] of our snappiest dressers, found that it did not harmonise with the

[005-021] deeper meaning of the tweed suit, removed it, chose another, and was

[005-022] adjusting the bow and admiring the effect, when his attention was

[005-023] diverted by a slight sound which was half a cough and half a sniff;

[005-024] and, turning, found himself gazing into the clear blue eyes of a large

[005-025] man in uniform, who had stepped into the room from the fire-escape. He

[005-026] was swinging a substantial club in a negligent sort of way, and he

[005-027] looked at Archie with a total absence of bonhomie.

[005-028] “Ah!” he observed.

[005-029] “Oh, _there_ you are!” said Archie, subsiding weakly against the chest

[005-030] of drawers. He gulped. “Of course, I can see you’re thinking all this

[005-031] pretty tolerably weird and all that,” he proceeded, in a propitiatory

[005-032] voice.

[005-033] The policeman attempted no analysis of his emotions, He opened a mouth

[005-034] which a moment before had looked incapable of being opened except with

[005-035] the assistance of powerful machinery, and shouted a single word.

[005-036] “[[officer-cassidy|Cassidy]]!”

[005-037] A distant voice gave tongue in answer. It was like alligators roaring

[005-038] to their mates across lonely swamps.

[005-039] There was a rumble of footsteps in the region of the stairs, and

[005-040] presently there entered an even larger guardian of the Law than the

[005-041] first exhibit. He, too, swung a massive club, and, like his colleague,

[005-042] he gazed frostily at Archie.

[005-043] “God save Ireland!” he remarked.

[005-044] The words appeared to be more in the nature of an expletive than a

[005-045] practical comment on the situation. Having uttered them, he draped

[005-046] himself in the doorway like a colossus, and chewed gum.

[005-047] “Where ja get him?” he enquired, after a pause.

[005-048] “Found him in here attimpting to disguise himself.”

[005-049] “I told Cap. he was hiding somewheres, but he would have it that he’d

[005-050] beat it down th’ escape,” said the gum-chewer, with the sombre triumph

[005-051] of the underling whose sound advice has been overruled by those above

[005-052] him. He shifted his wholesome (or, as some say, unwholesome) morsel to

[005-053] the other side of his mouth, and for the first time addressed Archie

[005-054] directly. “Ye’re pinched!” he observed.

[005-055] Archie started violently. The bleak directness of the speech roused him

[005-056] with a jerk from the dream-like state into which he had fallen. He had

[005-057] not anticipated this. He had assumed that there would be a period of

[005-058] tedious explanations to be gone through before he was at liberty to

[005-059] depart to the cosy little [[dining-and-social-ritual|lunch]] for which his interior had been sighing

[005-060] wistfully this long time past; but that he should be arrested had been

[005-061] outside his calculations. Of course, he could put everything right

[005-062] eventually; he could call witnesses to his character and the purity of

[005-063] his intentions; but in the meantime the whole dashed business would be

[005-064] in all the papers, embellished with all those unpleasant flippancies to

[005-065] which your newspaper reporter is so prone to stoop when he sees half a

[005-066] chance. He would feel a frightful chump. Chappies would rot him about

[005-067] it to the most fearful extent. Old [[daniel-brewster|Brewster]]’s name would come into it,

[005-068] and he could not disguise it from himself that his [[fatherhood-and-authority|father-in-law]], who

[005-069] liked his name in the papers as little as possible, would be sorer than

[005-070] a sunburned neck.

[005-071] “No, I say, you know! I mean, I mean to say!”

[005-072] “Pinched!” repeated the rather larger policeman.

[005-073] “And annything ye say,” added his slightly smaller colleague, “will be

[005-074] used agenst ya ’t the trial.”

[005-075] “And if ya try t’escape,” said the first speaker, twiddling his club,

[005-076] “ya’ll getja block knocked off.”

[005-077] And, having sketched out this admirably clear and neatly-constructed

[005-078] scenario, the two relapsed into silence. Officer Cassidy restored his

[005-079] gum to circulation. [[officer-donahue|Officer Donahue]] frowned sternly at his boots.

[005-080] “But, I say,” said Archie, “it’s all a mistake, you know. Absolutely a

[005-081] frightful error, my dear old constables. I’m not the lad you’re after

[005-082] at all. The chappie you want is a different sort of fellow altogether.

[005-083] Another blighter entirely.”

[005-084] [[new-york-city|New York]] policemen never laugh when on duty. There is probably

[005-085] something in the regulations against it. But Officer Donahue permitted

[005-086] the left corner of his mouth to twitch slightly, and a momentary

[005-087] muscular spasm disturbed the calm of Officer Cassidy’s granite

[005-088] features, as a passing breeze ruffles the surface of some bottomless

[005-089] lake.

[005-090] “That’s what they all say!” observed Officer Donahue.

[005-091] “It’s no use tryin’ that line of [[conversation-and-dialogue|talk]],” said Officer Cassidy.

[005-092] “Babcock’s squealed.”

[005-093] “Sure. Squealed ’s morning,” said Officer Donahue.

[005-094] Archie’s memory stirred vaguely.

[005-095] “Babcock?” he said. “Do you know, that name seems familiar to me,

[005-096] somehow. I’m almost sure I’ve read it in the paper or something.”

[005-097] “Ah, cut it out!” said Officer Cassidy, disgustedly. The two constables

[005-098] exchanged a glance of austere disapproval. This hypocrisy pained them.

[005-099] “Read it in th’ paper or something!”

[005-100] “By Jove! I remember now. He’s the chappie who was arrested in that

[005-101] bond business. For goodness’ sake, my dear, merry old constables,” said

[005-102] Archie, astounded, “you surely aren’t labouring under the impression

[005-103] that I’m the Master-Mind they were talking about in the paper? Why,

[005-104] what an absolutely priceless notion! I mean to say, I ask you, what!

[005-105] Frankly, laddies, do I look like a Master-Mind?”

[005-106] Officer Cassidy heaved a deep sigh, which rumbled up from his interior

[005-107] like the first muttering of a cyclone.

[005-108] “If I’d known,” he said, regretfully, “that this guy was going to turn

[005-109] out a ruddy Englishman, I’d have taken a slap at him with m’ stick and

[005-110] chanced it!”

[005-111] Officer Donahue considered the point well taken.

[005-112] “Ah!” he said, understandingly. He regarded Archie with an unfriendly

[005-113] eye. “I know th’ sort well! Trampling on th’ face av th’ poor!”

[005-114] “Ya c’n trample on the poor man’s face,” said Officer Cassidy,

[005-115] severely; “but don’t be surprised if one day he bites you in the leg!”

[005-116] “But, my dear old sir,” protested Archie, “I’ve never trampled—”

[005-117] “One of these days,” said Officer Donahue, moodily, “the Shannon will

[005-118] flow in blood to the sea!”

[005-119] “Absolutely! But—”

[005-120] Officer Cassidy uttered a glad cry.

[005-121] “Why couldn’t we hit him a lick,” he suggested, brightly, “an’ tell th’

[005-122] Cap. he resisted us in th’ exercise of our jooty?”

[005-123] An instant gleam of approval and enthusiasm came into Officer Donahue’s

[005-124] eyes. Officer Donahue was not a man who got these luminous inspirations

[005-125] himself, but that did not prevent him appreciating them in others and

[005-126] bestowing commendation in the right quarter. There was nothing petty or

[005-127] grudging about Officer Donahue.

[005-128] “Ye’re the lad with the head, Tim!” he exclaimed admiringly.

[005-129] “It just sorta came to me,” said Mr. Cassidy, modestly.

[005-130] “It’s a great idea, Timmy!”

[005-131] “Just happened to think of it,” said Mr. Cassidy, with a coy gesture of

[005-132] self-effacement.

[005-133] Archie had listened to the dialogue with growing uneasiness. Not for

[005-134] the first time since he had made their acquaintance, he became vividly

[005-135] aware of the exceptional physical gifts of these two men. The New York

[005-136] police force demands from those who would join its ranks an extremely

[005-137] high standard of stature and sinew, but it was obvious that jolly old

[005-138] Donahue and Cassidy must have passed in first shot without any

[005-139] difficulty whatever.

[005-140] “I say, you know,” he observed, apprehensively.

[005-141] And then a sharp and commanding voice spoke from the outer room.

[005-142] “Donahue! Cassidy! What the devil does this mean?”

[005-143] Archie had a momentary impression that an angel had fluttered down to

[005-144] his rescue. If this was the case, the angel had assumed an effective

[005-145] disguise—that of a police captain. The new arrival was a far smaller

[005-146] man than his subordinates—so much smaller that it did Archie good to

[005-147] look at him. For a long time he had been wishing that it were possible

[005-148] to rest his eyes with the spectacle of something of a slightly less

[005-149] out-size nature than his two companions.

[005-150] “Why have you left your posts?”

[005-151] The effect of the interruption on the Messrs. Cassidy and Donahue was

[005-152] pleasingly instantaneous. They seemed to shrink to almost normal

[005-153] proportions, and their manner took on an attractive deference.

[005-154] Officer Donahue saluted.

[005-155] “If ye plaze, sorr—”

[005-156] Officer Cassidy also saluted, simultaneously.

[005-157] “’Twas like this, sorr—”

[005-158] The captain froze Officer Cassidy with a glance and, leaving him

[005-159] congealed, turned to Officer Donahue.

[005-160] “Oi wuz standing on th’ fire-escape, sorr,” said Officer Donahue, in a

[005-161] tone of obsequious respect which not only delighted, but astounded

[005-162] Archie, who hadn’t known he could talk like that, “accordin’ to

[005-163] instructions, when I heard a suspicious noise. I crope in, sorr, and

[005-164] found this duck—found the accused, sorr—in front of the mirror,

[005-165] examinin’ himself. I then called to Officer Cassidy for assistance. We

[005-166] pinched—arrested um, sorr.”

[005-167] The captain looked at Archie. It seemed to Archie that he looked at him

[005-168] coldly and with contempt.

[005-169] “Who is he?”

[005-170] “The Master-Mind, sorr.”

[005-171] “The what?”

[005-172] “The accused, sorr. The man that’s wanted.”

[005-173] “You may want him. I don’t,” said the captain. Archie, though relieved,

[005-174] thought he might have put it more nicely. “This isn’t Moon. It’s not a

[005-175] bit like him.”

[005-176] “Absolutely not!” agreed Archie, cordially. “It’s all a mistake, old

[005-177] companion, as I was trying to—”

[005-178] “Cut it out!”

[005-179] “Oh, right-o!”

[005-180] “You’ve seen the photographs at the station. Do you mean to tell me you

[005-181] see any resemblance?”

[005-182] “If ye plaze, sorr,” said Officer Cassidy, coming to life.

[005-183] “Well?”

[005-184] “We thought he’d bin disguising himself, the way he wouldn’t be

[005-185] recognised.”

[005-186] “You’re a fool!” said the captain.

[005-187] “Yes, sorr,” said Officer Cassidy, meekly.

[005-188] “So are you, Donahue.”

[005-189] “Yes, sorr.”

[005-190] Archie’s respect for this chappie was going up all the time. He seemed

[005-191] to be able to take years off the lives of these massive blighters with

[005-192] a word. It was like the stories you read about lion-tamers. Archie did

[005-193] not despair of seeing Officer Donahue and his old college chum Cassidy

[005-194] eventually jumping through hoops.

[005-195] “Who are you?” demanded the captain, turning to Archie.

[005-196] “Well, my name is—”

[005-197] “What are you doing here?”

[005-198] “Well, it’s rather a longish story, you know. Don’t want to bore you,

[005-199] and all that.”

[005-200] “I’m here to listen. You can’t bore _me_.”

[005-201] “Dashed nice of you to put it like that,” said Archie, gratefully. “I

[005-202] mean to say, makes it easier and so forth. What I mean is, you know how

[005-203] rotten you feel telling the deuce of a long yarn and wondering if the

[005-204] party of the second part is wishing you would turn off the tap and go

[005-205] home. I mean—”

[005-206] “If,” said the captain, “you’re reciting something, stop. If you’re

[005-207] trying to tell me what you’re doing here, make it shorter and easier.”

[005-208] Archie saw his point. Of course, time was [[wealth-and-money|money]]—the modern spirit of

[005-209] hustle—all that sort of thing.
