# "What Ho"

[[archie-moffam|Archie Moffam]]'s buoyant greeting — an Edwardian exclamation that he deploys on friend and stranger alike, expressing a [[hope-and-optimism|cheerfulness]] impervious to circumstance.

## The Quote

> "Halloa, General! What ho, what ho! What on earth are you doing over here?" (007-046)

## Context

"What ho" is one of [[archie-moffam|Archie]]'s three great exclamations, alongside "[[old-bean|old bean]]" and "jolly old." It appears throughout [[indiscretions-of-archie-the-novel|the novel]] as his default greeting. In Section 7, [[archie-moffam|Archie]] encounters General Mannister and greets him with characteristic exuberance: "Halloa, General! What ho, what ho!" (007-046). The doubling of the phrase — "What ho, what ho!" — is typical of [[archie-moffam|Archie]]'s speech: whatever can be said once can be said twice, with added enthusiasm.

The phrase appears elsewhere in more casual contexts. "I said 'What ho!'" (023-027, 023-028) captures the reflexive nature of the greeting — it is not a considered choice of words but an automatic response, as natural to [[archie-moffam|Archie]] as breathing.

## Speaker

- **[[archie-moffam]]** — The phrase's owner. "What ho" encapsulates his approach to life: greet everything — even disaster — with a cheerful exclamation.

## Significance

"What ho" belongs to a specific era of English speech — the Edwardian public-school vernacular that [[p-g-wodehouse|Wodehouse]] immortalized. For [[archie-moffam|Archie]], it is a fragment of home carried into American exile. The phrase never quite translates: Americans in [[indiscretions-of-archie-the-novel|the novel]] do not say "what ho," and [[archie-moffam|Archie]]'s [[failure-and-resilience|persistence]] in using it marks him as perpetually, charmingly foreign.

Like [[old-bean|"old bean"]], "what ho" is p[[art-and-collecting|art]] of [[archie-moffam|Archie]]'s linguistic armor — a way of meeting the world on his own terms. No situation is so dire that it cannot be ad[[appearance-and-first-impressions|dress]]ed with a "what ho." The phrase is the verbal equivalent of [[archie-moffam|Archie]]'s indestructible [[hope-and-optimism|optimism]].
