# Lucille's Bracelet

Lucille's Bracelet is a piece of jewelry that plays a recurring role in the novel's financial subplots. Along with a ring, it is pawned by [[archie-moffam|Archie]] and [[lucille-brewster|Lucille]] to raise funds during one of their periodic financial crises. The bracelet embodies both the couple's precarious economic position and their willingness to sacrifice personal possessions for each other — themes that run throughout the novel's treatment of love and money.

## In the Novel

The bracelet first appears in the context of financial necessity. Lucille offers it — along with a ring — to Archie as collateral: "You can pawn that ring and that bracelet of mine" (010-079). The gesture is characteristically generous, reflecting Lucille's steady willingness to support Archie's schemes even when they involve liquidating her personal property.

The business negotiations that follow involve "the bracelet and the ring" (010-118) as objects of transaction. Archie, ever the optimist, enters these negotiations with more charm than commercial acumen. The bracelet and ring together "had fetched nearly twelve hundred dollars" (011-175) — a sum substantially larger than anything Archie had managed to earn through his own efforts, and a quiet testament to the value of what Lucille has contributed to the marriage.

Later in the novel, Archie returns to the theme: he visits a jeweller to "order a bracelet" (020-063) — presumably to replace the one that was pawned. The exchange with the jeweller ("The bracelet, sir?" / "Well, produce, exhibit, and... produce the jolly bracelet. Thanks!" 020-066) captures Archie's characteristic blend of impatience and charm.

## Thematic Significance

The bracelet is more than a plot device. It represents the material sacrifice that Lucille makes for her marriage — giving up personal possessions to fund her husband's ventures. At the same time, Archie's later purchase of a replacement bracelet (020-063) represents his desire to restore what was lost, to make good on the debts — both financial and emotional — that his indiscretions have incurred.
