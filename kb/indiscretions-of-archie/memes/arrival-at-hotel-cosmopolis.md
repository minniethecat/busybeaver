# Arrival at the Hotel Cosmopolis

Archie Moffam's arrival at the Hotel Cosmopolis is the novel's inciting event. He enters the lobby carrying letters of introduction (001-148), a young Englishman with "neither money nor occupation" (001-035). His meeting with Daniel Brewster — and his casual criticism of the hotel — establishes the central conflict that will drive every subsequent episode.

## Summary

Archie arrives as a guest, unaware that the hotel's proprietor is also his future father-in-law. His conversation with Brewster, in which he calls the Cosmopolis "a bally rotten hotel" (001-135), is a masterpiece of comic miscommunication: Archie intends only casual English exaggeration, while Brewster hears a mortal insult to his life's work.

## Key Passages

> "The Cosmopolis is a bally rotten hotel!" (001-135)

> "A remark like that about his hotel, and war was definitely declared." (001-140)
