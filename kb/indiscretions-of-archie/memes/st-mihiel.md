# Battle of St. Mihiel

The World War I engagement near St. Mihiel, France, referenced in Section 23 as the setting for a pivotal moment of battlefield camaraderie between Archie Moffam and the soldier later known as the Sausage Chappie.

## In the Novel

The St. Mihiel episode appears in the novel not as a depicted battle scene but as a shared memory between two veterans. When Archie encounters the Sausage Chappie after the war, the latter recalls the incident: "I remember you giving me a bit of sausage outside St. Mihiel" (PN: 023-018). The memory is expanded upon: "It was just outside St. Mihiel, about five in the evening. The quartermaster-sergeant had brought you your rations, and you'd only got a bit of bread and a nob of cheese" (PN: 023-025).

The episode functions as a recognition scene — the shared war experience is what allows the Sausage Chappie, who suffers from amnesia, to remember Archie and their bond. The gift of sausage becomes a symbol of battlefield generosity that transcends the chaos of war and the fog of memory loss.

## The Sausage Chappie Reunion

The emotional weight of St. Mihiel is realised in Section 23, where Archie — now at the Hotel Cosmopolis — recognises a disoriented stranger as his wartime comrade. The Sausage Chappie, suffering from memory loss caused by a wartime head injury, initially cannot place Archie at all:

> "I don't know you from Adam. But I like your conversation — especially the part about eating — and I'm taking a chance." (023-020–022)

Archie, undeterred, reconstructs their first meeting with characteristic Wodehousian dialogue:

> "Listen, old bean. Make an effort. You must remember that sausage episode? It was just outside St. Mihiel, about five in the evening. Your little lot were lying next to my little lot, and we happened to meet, and I said 'What ho!' and you said 'Halloa!' and I said 'What ho! What ho!' and you said 'Have a bit of sausage?' and I said 'What ho! What ho! What _ho!_' " (023-024–029)

The reunion culminates in a confrontation with [[daniel-brewster|Daniel Brewster]], who discovers the stranger in his bedroom. Archie's defence is simple and unreasoning — "He gave me a bit of sausage outside St. Mihiel" (023-090) — a phrase that enrages Brewster but perfectly captures the novel's ethos of honouring personal debts, however small.

## Historical Context

The Battle of Saint-Mihiel (September 12–15, 1918) was a major World War I engagement and the first solo offensive by the American Expeditionary Forces under General John J. Pershing. The battle resulted in the reduction of the Saint-Mihiel salient, a German-held bulge in the Western Front lines that had existed since 1914. American and French forces successfully captured the area, taking over 15,000 German prisoners.

P. G. Wodehouse's reference to the battle grounds Archie's backstory in authentic World War I history, consistent with his characterisation as a returning British officer adjusting to civilian life in America.
