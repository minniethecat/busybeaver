# Salvatore

Salvatore is an Italian immigrant whose mother owns a small newspaper and tobacco shop on Seventh Avenue in New York. He works as a waiter in the Cosmopolis Hotel grill-room, where Archie Moffam — who "had always lacked that reserve which characterises many Britons" — takes a personal interest in him. Through a series of friendly chats, Salvatore shares details about his home in Italy and his mother's business; Archie's "insatiable curiosity about his fellow-man" makes him one of the few people at the Cosmopolis who treats Salvatore as a person rather than a function (010-131–010-137). His poor treatment by Daniel Brewster — summarily firing him when he complains about working conditions — leads to an ironic reversal: Salvatore's mother's shop occupies the exact site where Brewster plans to build his new hotel, and the vengeful Salvatore ensures she refuses to sell.

## Role in the Novel

Salvatore's subplot exemplifies the novel's recurring theme of the underdog's revenge. After Archie encourages him to present his grievances to Brewster, Salvatore picks the worst possible moment — when Brewster is already enraged — and is fired on the spot. However, he emerges triumphant when it is revealed that his mother's shop is the linchpin of Brewster's development plans. Archie, who had championed Salvatore from the beginning, is called upon to mediate. (PN 010-124–010-169, 013-057–013-081, 020-142–020-178)

His story arc directly recalls Officer Donahue's warning from Chapter V: "you could trample on the poor man's face but you mustn't be surprised if he bit you in the leg while you were doing it." Archie explicitly connects these dots in Chapter XX. (PN 020-157–020-164)

## Personality and Traits

Salvatore is physically described as "the dark, sinister-looking waiter" — his appearance so unsettling that when he walks into Brewster's office, the hotel proprietor sees "something connected with the executive staff of the Black Hand." His manner is furtive, and his English is heavily accented (rendered as "Sare?" for "Sir?"). (PN 010-126, 013-057–013-059, 010-139, 010-152)

Despite his intimidating exterior, Salvatore is portrayed sympathetically as a downtrodden worker. When Archie first inquires about his troubles, he shrugs "as if indicating an unwillingness to inflict his grievances on one of the tipping classes." Only after Archie's persistent encouragement does he lay bare his "sad story of excessive hours and insufficient pay." (PN 010-154–010-161)

His most formidable quality is his persistence. When he finally gets his chance to speak to Brewster, he proves "a strong, ambidextrous talker, whom it was hard to interrupt" — and Brewster, "though not a linguist," understands enough to realize the waiter is criticizing his hotel. (PN 013-073–013-079)

After being fired, Salvatore displays a calculating vindictiveness: he makes his mother promise not to sell her shop, effectively holding Brewster's development plans hostage. Archie admiringly calls him a "brainy sort of feller." (PN 020-150–020-151, 020-163)

## Key Relationships

- **[[archie-moffam]]** — Salvatore's sole ally at the Cosmopolis. Archie takes a personal interest in him, learns his life story, and champions his cause with Brewster. Archie later mediates the shop dispute, hoping to restore himself to Brewster's good graces. (PN 010-124–010-169, 020-167–020-178)

- **[[daniel-brewster]]** — Salvatore's employer and antagonist. Brewster fires Salvatore on the spot for criticizing the hotel, but later finds himself at Salvatore's mercy when the waiter's mother's shop blocks Brewster's new hotel construction. (PN 013-057–013-081, 020-142–020-155)

- **[[lucille-brewster]]** — Lucille tells Archie about Salvatore's revenge, setting up the mediation plot. She sees Archie's friendship with Salvatore as the key to solving her father's problem. (PN 020-140–020-166)

- **Salvatore's Mother** — The unseen owner of the newspaper and tobacco shop on Seventh Avenue. Salvatore's influence over her ("he's made his mother promise that she won't sell") is his primary leverage. (PN 010-135, 020-149–020-151)
