# Mawson

Mawson is [[daniel-brewster|Daniel Brewster]]'s art agent, responsible for scouting and acquiring collectibles in New York. He discovered a china warrior figurine in a small shop on the east side of the city and was subsequently tasked with finding its missing pair — a commission that proves unexpectedly difficult.

## Role in the Novel

Mawson appears only in the opening chapter, but his discovery of the china warrior figurine sets in motion one of the novel's running subplots. Brewster, an art collector of more enthusiasm than expertise, takes immediate pride in the acquisition and becomes obsessed with completing the pair.

The figurine is first introduced through Mawson's report: "Mawson, my agent, found it in a little shop on the east side" (001-197). Brewster's satisfaction with the find is immediately undercut by his frustration at its incompleteness: "Mawson's looking for the other one now" (001-201). The search for the missing pair — which Brewster assumes will be straightforward — proves elusive, and the figurine becomes a symbol of Brewster's restless dissatisfaction with his collection.

## Character and Function

Mawson functions as a narrative catalyst rather than a developed character. He appears only in reported speech — Brewster's recounting of his activities — and never speaks or acts directly on the page. His role is to introduce the china warrior figurine into the novel's inventory of objects, triggering Brewster's acquisitive anxiety and providing a thread that connects to broader themes about the limits of wealth and the elusiveness of completion.

As an art agent, Mawson represents the professional infrastructure behind Brewster's collecting — the network of scouts, dealers, and intermediaries who feed the American appetite for European and Asian art objects in the post-World War I period.
