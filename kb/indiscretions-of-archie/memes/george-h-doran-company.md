# George H. Doran Company

The George H. Doran Company was a New York-based publishing house that issued the first American edition of *Indiscretions of Archie* in 1921. The copyright page of the novel's first edition records the imprint: "NEW YORK GEORGE H. DORAN COMPANY COPYRIGHT, 1921, BY GEORGE H. DORAN" (P01-003).

## In the Novel

As the publisher of the first American edition, the George H. Doran Company is the entity through which Archie Moffam's story first reached American readers. The firm was a significant force in early twentieth-century publishing, with a catalogue that included both British and American authors. P. G. Wodehouse had an established relationship with Doran, who published several of his works in the American market during the 1910s and 1920s.

## Key Passages

## Real-World Context

George H. Doran (1869–1956) founded his eponymous publishing company in 1908. The firm merged with Doubleday, Page & Company in 1927 to form Doubleday, Doran & Company, which became one of the largest publishing houses in the English-speaking world. Doran was known for publishing British authors in the American market, including Wodehouse, Arnold Bennett, and Somerset Maugham.
