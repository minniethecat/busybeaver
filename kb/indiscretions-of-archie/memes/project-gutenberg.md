# Project Gutenberg

Project Gutenberg is the digital library that produced the public domain electronic text of *Indiscretions of [[indiscretions-of-archie-the-novel|Archie]]*, which serves as the source corpus for this wiki. The Project Gutenberg trademark and legal apparatus appear in the final paragraphs of [[indiscretions-of-archie-the-novel|The Novel]]'s e-text, reflecting the organization's careful stewardship of its digital publishing mission.

## In the Novel

Project Gutenberg's role is entirely external to the narrative — it is the means by which the novel entered the digital public domain. The e-text's production note explains that the work is "not protected by U.S. copyright law" because it was published before 1928, making it eligible for free distribution through the Project Gutenberg platform. The extensive legal apparatus appended to the e-text — concerning the "PROJECT GUTENBERG™ concept and trademark" (036-185–036-187) — reflects the organization's commitment to maintaining the integrity of its digital publishing mission.

## Key Passages

> "Project Gutenberg™ electronic works to protect the PROJECT GUTENBERG™ concept and trademark. Project Gutenberg is a registered trademark." (036-185–036-187)

## Real-World Context

Founded in 1971 by Michael S. Hart, Project Gutenberg is the oldest digital library and the first producer of free electronic books. It has digitized tens of thousands of public domain works, making them freely available to readers worldwide. The *[[indiscretions-of-archie-the-novel|Indiscretions Of Archie]]* e-text is part of this vast collection, representing the [[1921|1921]] novel's entry into the digital age.
