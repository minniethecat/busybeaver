# Miami

Miami is referenced in [[indiscretions-of-archie-the-novel|The Novel]] as a destination for [[leisure-and-sports|Leisure]] travel, representing the broader American geography of resorts and seasonal migration that extends beyond New York City. It is mentioned in connection with a house-party (001-150), suggesting the social circuits of wealthy Americans that [[indiscretions-of-archie-the-novel|Archie]]'s marriage to [[lucille-brewster|Lucille]] potentially opens to him.

## In the Novel

Miami appears as a name on the horizon — a place of sun and leisure that contrasts with the urban intensity of the [[hotel-cosmopolis|Hotel Cosmopolis]]. Its mention expands the novel's geographic imagination beyond Manhattan, reminding readers that the characters inhabit a nation of movement and possibility.
