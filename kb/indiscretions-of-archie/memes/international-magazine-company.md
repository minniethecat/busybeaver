# International Magazine Company

The International Magazine Company was the corporate entity that held the 1920 copyright for the original magazine serialization of *Indiscretions of Archie*. The copyright notice appears on the verso of the first edition's title page: "COPYRIGHT, 1920, BY INTERNATIONAL MAGAZINE COMPANY" (P01-003).

## In the Novel

The International Magazine Company is referenced solely in the copyright notice of the first American edition. As the parent company of [[cosmopolitan-magazine|Cosmopolitan Magazine]], it represents the first venue in which the novel's episodes reached readers — before their collection into the 1921 George H. Doran Company edition. The magazine serialization was the standard publishing model for Wodehouse's work in this period.

## Key Passages

> "COPYRIGHT, 1920, BY INTERNATIONAL MAGAZINE COMPANY" (P01-003)

## Real-World Context

The International Magazine Company was the publishing corporation behind *Cosmopolitan*, one of the leading American general-interest magazines of the early twentieth century. Under the editorship of Ray Long, *Cosmopolitan* serialized fiction by many of the era's prominent authors, including Wodehouse, whose work appeared in the magazine alongside that of Sinclair Lewis, Theodore Dreiser, and Edna Ferber.
