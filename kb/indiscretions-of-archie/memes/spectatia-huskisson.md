## Overview

Spectatia Huskisson is a young woman from Snake Bite, Michigan, who comes to New York's Greenwich Village aspiring to be a singer. She becomes the romantic interest of Bill Brewster and, through Archie's matchmaking scheme, performs Wilson Hymack's song at the Hotel Cosmopolis dining room, creating both a musical spectacle and a near-catastrophe when Daniel Brewster returns unexpectedly (031-197–033-162).

## Character

### Background and appearance

Spectatia comes from Snake Bite, Michigan, a fact that anchors her identity throughout. Her unusual name provokes Archie's disbelief: he "gravely doubt[s] whether there is such a name." Bill defends it as "a darned sight better name than Archibald Moffam," while Lucille explains it as "a good old Middle West name" — "everybody knows the Huskissons of Snake Bite, Michigan" (031-196–031-207). Archie continues to wince at the name throughout (032-079). Bill Brewster affectionately calls her "Pootles" (031-208–031-209).

Lucille describes Spectatia as a girl from the Middle West "trying to be twice as Bohemian as the rest of the girls down in Greenwich Village," wearing bobbed hair and a kimono. Despite her attempts at bohemian sophistication, Lucille notes that "you can see Hicks Corners sticking out of her all the time" — her small-town origins are unmistakable (031-143–031-148).

### Voice: a powerful instrument

Spectatia possesses a notably loud singing voice — the trait that drives her entire role in the plot. Lucille describes it as simply "loud" (031-176), and later observation confirms that "Miss Huskisson's voice was loud. It was a powerful organ, and there was no doubt that it would take the cloistered stillness of the Cosmopolis dining-room and stand it on one ear" (032-108–032-115). Archie, awaiting her performance, finds himself "bracing his muscles and holding his breath as he had done in France at the approach of the zero hour, when awaiting the first roar of a barrage" (032-113–032-115).

## Role in the story

### Bill Brewster's love interest

Spectatia is introduced when Lucille tells Archie that Bill is "in love again" with a girl from Greenwich Village (031-127–031-148). Bill declares that "this time it is the real thing" (031-157). Lucille anticipates that Daniel Brewster will not approve, continuing Bill's pattern of falling for inappropriate women (031-165–031-169).

### The concert scheme

Archie devises a plan to help both his composer friend Wilson Hymack and his brother-in-law Bill: Spectatia will sing Hymack's song "It's a Long Way Back to Mother's Knee" during dinner at the Hotel Cosmopolis, with music publisher Maxie Blumenthal in the audience (032-030–032-081). The scheme takes advantage of Daniel Brewster's absence — he has gone fishing (032-051–032-052).

### The performance

The performance takes place in the main dining room of the Hotel Cosmopolis. Miss Huskisson "[begins] the first verse" of Hymack's masterpiece from the dais (033-043). She reaches "the high note at the end of the refrain" and begins the second verse (033-070–033-074). She "bowed, retired, returned, bowed, and retired again" at the conclusion (033-091).

The performance is dramatically interrupted by Daniel Brewster's unexpected return — he had been called back from his fishing trip (032-142–032-153). The concert complicates into farce as Archie tries to manage both the musical spectacle and his father-in-law's fury.
