# Cosmopolitan Magazine

*Cosmopolitan Magazine* was the American periodical that originally serialized *Indiscretions of Archie* in 1920, before the novel's collection and publication as a single volume by the [[george-h-doran-company|George H. Doran Company]] in 1921. The magazine is acknowledged in the first edition's copyright notice: "(COSMOPOLITAN MAGAZINE)" (P01-003).

## In the Novel

The magazine serialization model meant that the individual episodes of Archie's misadventures first reached readers as discrete magazine installments — a format that shaped the novel's episodic structure. Each chapter functions as a self-contained comic unit, a narrative architecture ideally suited to the rhythms of monthly magazine publication.

## Key Passages

> "(COSMOPOLITAN MAGAZINE)" (P01-003)

## Real-World Context

*Cosmopolitan* was founded in 1886 as a family magazine and evolved into a leading outlet for literary fiction in the early twentieth century. Under editor Ray Long, the magazine published serializations by P. G. Wodehouse and many other prominent authors of the period. It was not until the 1960s, under Helen Gurley Brown's editorship, that the magazine transformed into the women's lifestyle publication known today.
