## Section 2

[002-001] “She returns to-day?”

[002-002] “Yes, Been at [[miami|Miami]].” [[daniel-brewster|Mr. Brewster]], having dwelt at adequate length on

[002-003] the contents of the chafing-dish, adjusted his glasses and took up the

[002-004] envelope. “I shall be glad—Great Godfrey!”

[002-005] He sat staring at the telegram, his mouth open. His friend eyed him

[002-006] solicitously.

[002-007] “No bad news, I [[hope-and-optimism|hope]]?”

[002-008] Mr. Brewster gurgled in a strangled way.

[002-009] “Bad news? Bad—? Here, read it for yourself.”

[002-010] [[professor-binstead|Professor Binstead]], one of the three most inquisitive men in [[new-york-city|New York]],

[002-011] took the slip of paper with gratitude.

[002-012] “‘Returning New York to-day with darling [[archie-moffam|Archie]],’” he read. “‘Lots of

[002-013] love from us both. [[lucille-brewster|Lucille]].’” He gaped at his host. “Who is [[indiscretions-of-archie-the-novel|Archie]]?” he

[002-014] enquired.

[002-015] “Who is Archie?” echoed Mr. Brewster helplessly. “Who is—? That’s just

[002-016] what I would like to know.”

[002-017] “‘Darling Archie,’” murmured the professor, musing over the telegram.

[002-018] “‘Returning to-day with darling Archie.’ Strange!”

[002-019] Mr. Brewster continued to stare before him. When you send your only

[002-020] daughter on a visit to Miami minus any entanglements and she mentions

[002-021] in a telegram that she has acquired a darling Archie, you are naturally

[002-022] startled. He rose from the table with a bound. It had occurred to him

[002-023] that by neglecting a careful study of his mail during the past week, as

[002-024] was his bad habit when busy, he had lost an opportunity of keeping

[002-025] abreast with current happenings. He recollected now that a letter had

[002-026] arrived from Lucille some time ago, and that he had put it away

[002-027] unopened till he should have [[leisure-and-sports|leisure]] to read it. Lucille was a dear

[002-028] girl, he had felt, but her [[letters-and-correspondence|letters]] when on a vacation seldom contained

[002-029] anything that couldn’t wait a few days for a reading. He sprang for his

[002-030] desk, rummaged among his papers, and found what he was seeking.

[002-031] It was a long letter, and there was silence in the room for some

[002-032] moments while he mastered its contents. Then he turned to the

[002-033] professor, breathing heavily.

[002-034] “Good heavens!”

[002-035] “Yes?” said Professor Binstead eagerly. “Yes?”

[002-036] “Good Lord!”

[002-037] “Well?”

[002-038] “Good gracious!”

[002-039] “What is it?” demanded the professor in an agony.

[002-040] Mr. Brewster sat down again with a thud.

[002-041] “She’s married!”

[002-042] “Married!”

[002-043] “Married! To an Englishman!”

[002-044] “Bless my soul!”

[002-045] “She says,” proceeded Mr. Brewster, referring to the letter again,

[002-046] “that they were both so much in love that they simply had to slip off

[002-047] and get married, and she hopes I won’t be cross. Cross!” gasped Mr.

[002-048] Brewster, gazing wildly at his friend.

[002-049] “Very disturbing!”

[002-050] “Disturbing! You bet it’s disturbing! I don’t know anything about the

[002-051] fellow. Never heard of him in my life. She says he wanted a quiet

[002-052] wedding because he thought a fellow looked such a chump getting

[002-053] married! And I must love him, because he’s all set to love me very

[002-054] much!”

[002-055] “Extraordinary!”

[002-056] Mr. Brewster put the letter down.

[002-057] “An Englishman!”

[002-058] “I have met some very agreeable Englishmen,” said Professor Binstead.

[002-059] “I don’t like Englishmen,” growled Mr. Brewster. “[[parker|Parker]]’s an

[002-060] Englishman.”

[002-061] “Your valet?”

[002-062] “Yes. I believe he wears my shirts on the sly,’” said Mr. Brewster

[002-063] broodingly, “If I catch him—! What would you do about this, Binstead?”

[002-064] “Do?” The professor considered the point judicially. “Well, really,

[002-065] Brewster, I do not see that there is anything you can do. You must

[002-066] simply wait and meet the man. Perhaps he will turn out an admirable

[002-067] son-in-law.”

[002-068] “H’m!” Mr. Brewster declined to take an optimistic view. “But an

[002-069] Englishman, Binstead!” he said with pathos. “Why,” he went on, memory

[002-070] suddenly stirring, “there was an Englishman at this hotel only a week

[002-071] or two ago who went about knocking it in a way that would have amazed

[002-072] you! Said it was a rotten place! _My_ hotel!”

[002-073] Professor Binstead clicked his tongue sympathetically. He understood

[002-074] his friend’s warmth.

[002-075] CHAPTER III.

[002-076] MR. BREWSTER DELIVERS SENTENCE

[002-077] At about the same moment that Professor Binstead was clicking his

[002-078] tongue in Mr. Brewster’s sitting-room, Archie Moffam sat contemplating

[002-079] his bride in a drawing-room on the express from Miami. He was thinking

[002-080] that this was too good to be true. His brain had been in something of a

[002-081] whirl these last few days, but this was one thought that never failed

[002-082] to emerge clearly from the welter.

[002-083] Mrs. Archie Moffam, nee Lucille Brewster, was small and slender. She

[002-084] had a little animated face, set in a cloud of dark hair. She was so

[002-085] altogether perfect that Archie had frequently found himself compelled

[002-086] to take the marriage-certificate out of his inside pocket and study it

[002-087] furtively, to make himself realise that this miracle of good [[wealth-and-money|fortune]]

[002-088] had actually happened to him.

[002-089] “Honestly, [[old-bean|old bean]]—I mean, dear old thing,—I mean, darling,” said

[002-090] Archie, “I can’t believe it!”

[002-091] “What?”

[002-092] “What I mean is, I can’t understand why you should have married a

[002-093] blighter like me.”

[002-094] Lucille’s eyes opened. She squeezed his hand.

[002-095] “Why, you’re the most wonderful thing in the world, precious!—Surely

[002-096] you know that?”

[002-097] “Absolutely escaped my notice. Are you sure?”

[002-098] “Of course I’m sure! You wonder-child! Nobody could see you without

[002-099] loving you!”

[002-100] Archie heaved an ecstatic sigh. Then a thought crossed his mind. It was

[002-101] a thought which frequently came to mar his bliss.

[002-102] “I say, I wonder if your father will think that!”

[002-103] “Of course he will!”

[002-104] “We rather sprung this, as it were, on the old lad,” said Archie

[002-105] dubiously. “What sort of a man _is_ your father?”

[002-106] “Father’s a darling, too.”

[002-107] “Rummy thing he should own that hotel,” said Archie. “I had a frightful

[002-108] row with a blighter of a manager there just before I left for Miami.

[002-109] Your father ought to sack that chap. He was a blot on the landscape!”

[002-110] It had been settled by Lucille during the journey that Archie should be

[002-111] broken gently to his [[fatherhood-and-authority|father-in-law]]. That is to say, instead of bounding

[002-112] blithely into Mr. Brewster’s presence hand in hand, the happy pair

[002-113] should separate for half an hour or so, Archie hanging around in the

[002-114] offing while Lucille saw her father and told him the whole story, or

[002-115] those chapters of it which she had omitted from her letter for want of

[002-116] space. Then, having impressed Mr. Brewster sufficiently with his luck

[002-117] in having acquired Archie for a son-in-law, she would lead him to where

[002-118] his bit of good fortune awaited him.

[002-119] The programme worked out admirably in its earlier stages. When the two

[002-120] emerged from Mr. Brewster’s room to meet Archie, Mr. Brewster’s general

[002-121] idea was that fortune had smiled upon him in an almost unbelievable

[002-122] fashion and had presented him with a son-in-law who combined in almost

[002-123] equal parts the more admirable characteristics of Apollo, Sir Galahad,

[002-124] and Marcus Aurelius. True, he had gathered in the course of the

[002-125] [[conversation-and-dialogue|conversation]] that dear Archie had no occupation and no private means;

[002-126] but Mr. Brewster felt that a great-souled man like Archie didn’t need

[002-127] them. You can’t have everything, and Archie, according to Lucille’s

[002-128] account, was practically a hundred per cent man in soul, [[appearance-and-first-impressions|looks]],

[002-129] manners, amiability, and breeding. These are the things that count. Mr.

[002-130] Brewster proceeded to the lobby in a glow of optimism and geniality.

[002-131] Consequently, when he perceived Archie, he got a bit of a shock.

[002-132] “Hullo—ullo—ullo!” said Archie, advancing happily.

[002-133] “Archie, darling, this is father,” said Lucille.

[002-134] “Good Lord!” said Archie.

[002-135] There was one of those silences. Mr. Brewster looked at Archie. Archie

[002-136] gazed at Mr. Brewster. Lucille, perceiving without understanding why

[002-137] that the big introduction scene had stubbed its toe on some

[002-138] unlooked-for obstacle, waited anxiously for enlightenment. Meanwhile,

[002-139] Archie continued to inspect Mr. Brewster, and Mr. Brewster continued to

[002-140] drink in Archie.

[002-141] After an awkward pause of about three and a quarter minutes, Mr.

[002-142] Brewster swallowed once or twice, and finally spoke.

[002-143] “Lu!”

[002-144] “Yes, father?”

[002-145] “Is this true?”

[002-146] Lucille’s grey eyes clouded over with perplexity and apprehension.

[002-147] “True?”

[002-148] “Have you really inflicted this—_this_ on me for a son-in-law?” Mr.

[002-149] Brewster swallowed a few more times, Archie the while watching with a

[002-150] frozen fascination the rapid shimmying of his new relative’s

[002-151] Adam’s-apple. “Go away! I want to have a few words alone with

[002-152] this—This—_wassyourdamname?_” he demanded, in an overwrought manner,

[002-153] addressing Archie for the first time.

[002-154] “I told you, father. It’s Moom.”

[002-155] “Moom?”

[002-156] “It’s spelt M-o-f-f-a-m, but pronounced Moom.”

[002-157] “To rhyme,” said Archie, helpfully, “with Bluffinghame.”

[002-158] “Lu,” said Mr. Brewster, “run away! I want to speak to-to-to—”

[002-159] “You called me _this_ before,” said Archie.

[002-160] “You aren’t angry, father, dear?” said Lucilla.

[002-161] “Oh no! Oh no! I’m tickled to death!”

[002-162] When his daughter had withdrawn, Mr. Brewster drew a long breath.

[002-163] “Now then!” he said.

[002-164] “Bit embarrassing, all this, what!” said Archie, chattily. “I mean to

[002-165] say, having met before in less happy circs. and what not. Rum

[002-166] coincidence and so forth! How would it be to bury the jolly old

[002-167] hatchet—start a new life—forgive and forget—learn to love each

[002-168] other—and all that sort of rot? I’m game if you are. How do we go? Is

[002-169] it a bet?”

[002-170] Mr. Brewster remained entirely unsoftened by this manly appeal to his

[002-171] better feelings.

[002-172] “What the devil do you mean by marrying my daughter?”

[002-173] Archie reflected.

[002-174] “Well, it sort of happened, don’t you know! You know how these things

[002-175] _are!_ Young yourself once, and all that. I was most frightfully in

[002-176] love, and Lu seemed to think it wouldn’t be a bad scheme, and one thing

[002-177] led to another, and—well, there you are, don’t you know!”

[002-178] “And I suppose you think you’ve done pretty well for yourself?”

[002-179] “Oh, absolutely! As far as I’m concerned, everything’s topping! I’ve

[002-180] never felt so braced in my life!”

[002-181] “Yes!” said Mr. Brewster, with bitterness, “I suppose, from your

[002-182] view-point, everything _is_ ‘topping.’ You haven’t a cent to your name,

[002-183] and you’ve managed to fool a rich man’s daughter into marrying you. I

[002-184] suppose you looked me up in [[bradstreet|Bradstreet]] before committing yourself?”

[002-185] This aspect of the matter had not struck Archie until this moment.

[002-186] “I say!” he observed, with dismay. “I never looked at it like that

[002-187] before! I can see that, from your point of view, this must look like a

[002-188] bit of a wash-out!”

[002-189] “How do you propose to support Lucille, anyway?”

[002-190] Archie ran a finger round the inside of his collar. He felt

[002-191] embarrassed, His father-in-law was opening up all kinds of new lines of

[002-192] thought.

[002-193] “Well, there, old bean,” he admitted, frankly, “you rather have me!” He

[002-194] turned the matter over for a moment. “I had a sort of idea of, as it

[002-195] were, working, if you know what I mean.”

[002-196] “Working at what?”

[002-197] “Now, there again you stump me somewhat! The general scheme was that I

[002-198] should kind of look round, you know, and nose about and buzz to and fro

[002-199] till something turned up. That was, broadly speaking, the notion!”

[002-200] “And how did you suppose my daughter was to live while you were doing

[002-201] all this?”

[002-202] “Well, I think,” said Archie, “I _think_ we rather expected _you_ to

[002-203] rally round a bit for the nonce!”

[002-204] “I see! You expected to live on me?”

[002-205] “Well, you put it a bit crudely, but—as far as I had mapped anything

[002-206] out—that WAS what you might call the general scheme of procedure. You

[002-207] don’t think much of it, what? Yes? No?”

[002-208] Mr. Brewster exploded.

[002-209] “No! I do not think much of it! Good God! You go out of my hotel—_my_

[002-210] hotel—calling it all the names you could think of—roasting it to beat

[002-211] the band—”

[002-212] “Trifle hasty!” murmured Archie, apologetically. “Spoke without

[002-213] thinking. Dashed tap had gone _drip-drip-drip_ all night—kept me

[002-214] awake—hadn’t had [[dining-and-social-ritual|breakfast]]—bygones be bygones—!”

[002-215] “Don’t interrupt! I say, you go out of my hotel, knocking it as no one

[002-216] has ever knocked it since it was built, and you sneak straight off and

[002-217] marry my daughter without my knowledge.”

[002-218] “Did think of wiring for blessing. Slipped the old bean, somehow. You

[002-219] know how one forgets things!”

[002-220] “And now you come back and calmly expect me to fling my arms round you

[002-221] and kiss you, and support you for the rest of your life!”

[002-222] “Only while I’m nosing about and buzzing to and fro.”
