# Seventh Avenue

Seventh Avenue is a major north-south thoroughfare in Manhattan, New York City, running through the heart of the city's commercial and fashion districts. In *Indiscretions of Archie*, it appears as the location of a small newspaper and tobacco shop owned by the mother of Salvatore, the Italian immigrant waiter at the Hotel Cosmopolis.

## In the Novel

Salvatore tells Archie about the shop during one of their friendly conversations in the Cosmopolis grill-room. Archie learns of "his mother owned down on Seventh Avenue, and a hundred other personal" (010-136) details about the waiter's life — information that later proves crucial.

The shop's location becomes the novel's most satisfying ironic reversal. When Daniel Brewster plans to build a new hotel, the construction site includes the Seventh Avenue property. Brewster, who had summarily fired Salvatore for complaining about working conditions, now finds his ambitious plans held hostage by the very man he dismissed. Salvatore "made his mother promise that she won't sell" (020-149–020-151), and the shop — a modest commercial establishment on a busy avenue — becomes the fulcrum upon which Brewster's entire development scheme balances.
