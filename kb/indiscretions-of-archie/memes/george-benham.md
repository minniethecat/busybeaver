# George Benham

A playwright and theatre director at the Hermitage resort, described as "a grave young man" (014-069) whose professional seriousness is constantly undermined by the chaotic personalities he must manage — including the pistol-wielding actress Miss Silverton and her rheumatism-afflicted bulldog Percy.

## In the Novel

Archie first recalls Benham as an acquaintance: "Benham — I remember meeting George Benham" (013-156), and soon discovers that "his friend, George Benham, the playwright, had" (014-066) a production in rehearsal at the Hermitage. This connection draws Archie into the theatrical subplot.

Benham takes his craft seriously, reacting with genuine passion to the frustrations of rehearsal. "Good Lord!" cried George Benham, with honest warmth" (014-081) when confronted with yet another complication. Over a meal, "Mr. Benham helped himself to fish-pie" (014-104) while recounting his theatrical woes.

The central crisis of Benham's production involves Miss Silverton's bulldog Percy. During a dark rehearsal, Benham accidentally kicked (or shoved) the dog, triggering Miss Silverton's walkout. As Benham recalls with visible distress: "brings it to rehearsal. Mr. Benham's eyes filled with tears" (014-112) at the memory of the ensuing chaos.

Throughout his troubles, Benham maintains a brittle dignity. When Archie fails to pay attention to his story, he responds "reproachfully. I told you" (014-123), and when Archie suggests a course of action, Benham replies with a single, devastating word: "Push" (014-132), delivered "austerely." The cumulative effect of his theatrical misadventures is captured in a small, telling moment: "Mr. Benham gave a slight shiver" (014-136).

## Role in the Novel

George Benham represents the serious artist trapped in the machinery of Wodehousian farce. His gravity as a playwright contrasts with the absurdity of his circumstances — a leading lady armed with a pistol, a bulldog with rheumatism — creating the comic tension that defines the Hermitage subplot. He is less a fully realized character than a straight man, the suffering professional around whom chaos swirls.
