# Officer Donahue

Officer Donahue is a New York City policeman who partners with Officer Cassidy in Chapters V and VI. Together they form a comic duo, arresting Archie Moffam and engaging in banter that showcases their Irish-American identity and dim-witted but earnest approach to police work.

## Role in the Novel

Officer Donahue serves as a comic foil to his partner Cassidy. While Cassidy is the "brains" of the pair — modestly generating ideas like hitting the suspect and claiming he resisted — Donahue is the appreciative audience, quick to praise his partner's inspiration. Together they arrest Archie on the street, escort him to the station, and later appear before the captain. (PN 005-036–005-193, 006-038–006-084)

Donahue's role is small but memorable. His gloomy pronouncements about Irish grievances and his visible admiration for Cassidy's schemes create some of the early chapters' most sustained comic dialogue. (PN 005-111–005-138)

## Personality and Traits

Donahue speaks with a heavy Irish accent, using expressions like "sorr," "ye're the lad with the head," and "th' face av th' poor." His dialogue is colored by Irish nationalist sentiment: he darkly predicts that "one of these days the Shannon will flow in blood to the sea" and declares he knows "th' sort well! Trampling on th' face av th' poor!" (PN 005-112–005-118, 006-069)

Unlike Cassidy, who generates ideas, Donahue is an appreciator and amplifier. When Cassidy suggests hitting Archie and lying to the captain, "an instant gleam of approval and enthusiasm came into Officer Donahue's eyes." There is "nothing petty or grudging about Officer Donahue" — he freely bestows praise where it is due. (PN 005-123–005-127, 005-128)

He also functions as a deadpan commentator. His flat observation "That's what they all say!" when Archie protests his innocence, and his dismissal "Ah!" when Archie is identified as English, establish the world-weary cop persona. (PN 005-090, 005-112)

## Key Relationships

- **Officer Cassidy** — Donahue's partner and "old college chum." The two patrolmen are inseparable in their scenes, with Donahue serving as the enthusiastic straight man to Cassidy's modest schemer. Donahue calls Cassidy "Tim" and praises him with "Ye're the lad with the head, Tim!" (PN 005-128, 005-138, 005-193)

- **[[archie-moffam]]** — Donahue participates in Archie's arrest and regards him with the automatic suspicion that New York policemen extend to Englishmen. He frowns sternly at his boots while Cassidy does the talking. (PN 005-079, 005-111–005-113)

- **The Police Captain** — Donahue is summoned along with Cassidy by the captain's commanding voice ("Donahue! Cassidy! What the devil does this mean?") and is reduced to submissive "Sorr!" responses. (PN 005-142, 006-069, 006-081)
