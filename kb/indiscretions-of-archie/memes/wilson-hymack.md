## Overview

Wilson Hymack is a composer and friend of Archie Moffam from their time in France during the war, specifically in the neighbourhood of Armentières (030-194). An aspiring songwriter working unhappily in his uncle's office, Hymack writes "It's a Long Way Back to Mother's Knee," a sentimental ballad that — through Archie's scheme to have it performed by Spectatia Huskisson at the Hotel Cosmopolis — becomes an international hit, selling three million copies in the United States alone (030-177–030-194, 033-044).

## Character

### Appearance and temperament

Hymack has "a distinctly artistic look," wearing a bow-tie, with trousers that "bagged at the knees" and hair that "fell about his ears in luxuriant disarray" — a marked contrast to his military days when it "had been pruned to the roots" (031-016–031-020). When frustrated about his stalled musical career, he "snorted fiercely," pouring out "the pent-up emotion of many days" (031-084–031-085).

### Day job and artistic frustrations

Hymack works in his uncle's office, a job he despises. He tells Archie it gives him "a pain in the thorax," expressing his true ambition: "I want to be a composer" (031-013–031-014).

Hymack is deeply frustrated by the music industry. He complains that "you write the biggest thing in years and you go round trying to get someone to sing it, and they say you're a genius and then shove the song away in a drawer and forget about it" (031-085–031-088). He refuses to play his song in a publisher's office "with his hired gang of Tin-Pan Alley composers listening at the keyhole and taking notes" (031-096–031-098).

### The song: "It's a Long Way Back to Mother's Knee"

Hymack's masterpiece is "It's a Long Way Back to Mother's Knee," which he wrote both lyrics and melody for (031-023–031-024). He describes the title as "a lallapaloosa" (031-025). The song needs a powerful female vocalist to reach "that last high note" — Hymack explains that "you need Tetrazzini or someone who would just pick that note off the roof and hold it till the janitor came round to lock up the building for the night" (031-077–031-081).

When he performs it for Archie in his "weak, high, composer's voice," his voice "cracked on the final high note, which was of an altitude beyond his powers" (031-068–031-069). All composers, the narrative notes, "sing exactly alike, and they have to be heard to be believed" (031-039–031-040).

The song's lyrics begin:

> One night a young man wandered through the glitter of Broadway:
> His money he had squandered. For a meal he couldn't pay. (031-041–031-042)

The refrain:

> It's a long way back to Mother's knee,
> Mother's knee, Mother's knee:
> It's a long way back to Mother's knee,
> Where I used to stand and prattle
> With my teddy-bear and rattle:
> Oh, those childhood days in Tennessee,
> They sure look good to me! (031-052–031-067)

## Role in the story

### The concert scheme and performance

Archie, learning that his wife Lucille's friend (Spectatia Huskisson) is an aspiring singer with a powerful voice, devises a plan to help both Hymack and his brother-in-law Bill Brewster: Spectatia will sing "Mother's Knee" during dinner at the Hotel Cosmopolis, with music publisher Maxie Blumenthal in the audience (032-030–032-081).

### Unintended consequences

The performance has dramatic unintended effects. Labour leader Aloysius Connolly, dining at the Cosmopolis, is so moved by the song — particularly the line about returning to mother — that he ends a construction strike against Daniel Brewster's new hotel on the spot: "That song was sent to guide me, Dan! It was meant! Thirty years ago last October me dear old mother—" (033-127–033-128).

Meanwhile, Spectatia Huskisson herself is so affected by performing the song that she abandons her New York career and returns home to Snake Bite, Michigan, to be with her mother — inadvertently freeing Bill Brewster from his infatuation (033-165–033-170).

### Success

Music publisher Maxie Blumenthal pronounces the song "sure-fire": "The words were gooey enough to hurt, and the tune reminded him of every other song-hit he had ever heard" (033-141–033-144). He concludes the business arrangements with Hymack by telephone, and the song goes on to sell millions of copies (033-139–033-145).

The song becomes a phenomenon. As the narrative records: "'Mother's Knee' went through the world like a pestilence. Scots elders hummed it on their way to kirk; cannibals crooned it to their offspring in the jungles of Borneo; it was a best-seller among the Bolshevists. In the United States alone three million copies were disposed of" (030-179–030-183). Archie, reflecting on the evening's cascade of events — the strike settled, Bill freed from unsuitable romance, Hymack published, Blumenthal with a hit — counts the "birds" killed with his single stone (033-174).
