# Mr. McCall

Mr. Lindsay McCall is a small, hunted-looking man dominated by his wife, Mrs. Cora Bates McCall, the eminent lecturer on Rational Eating. He is the nominal plaintiff in a lawsuit against [[daniel-brewster|Daniel Brewster]] over a Westchester property dispute, though the suit is actually driven by his wife.

## Role in the Novel

Mr. McCall appears primarily as part of the McCall family subplot, which intersects with the main narrative through the McCalls' antagonistic relationship with Daniel Brewster. The McCalls register at the Hotel Cosmopolis — Brewster's own hotel — which Brewster interprets as a deliberate provocation. The lawsuit over Westchester property forms part of the broader conflict. (PN 027-135, 027-151)

The McCall family breakfast scene in Chapter 29 provides extended comic characterization, revealing Mr. McCall's subservience to his wife and his suffering under her dietary regime. His appearance later in the chapter, during the pie-eating sequence at the hotel restaurant, underscores his powerlessness within his marriage. (PN 029-071, 029-140)

## Biography / Backstory

Mr. McCall owns property adjacent to land Daniel Brewster purchased in Westchester. He is involved in a lawsuit against Brewster over a boundary dispute, claiming a portion of the land belongs to him. According to Lucille Brewster, Mr. McCall and her father were originally fond of each other until Mrs. McCall forced her husband to initiate the lawsuit. (PN 027-151, 027-165)

He is married to Cora Bates McCall, a prominent lecturer on Rational Eating who uses her "platform voice" even in private. They have a teenage son named Washington ("Washy"), a long, thin boy of about sixteen with pale red hair. (PN 029-071, 029-102)

## Personality and Traits

Mr. McCall is described as "a little hunted-looking man" with a distinctive pair of semicircular glasses "like half-moons with the horns turned up." His eyes play "a perpetual game of peekaboo" behind these glasses, "now peering over them, anon ducking down and hiding behind them." (PN 029-072, 029-075)

He is the quintessential henpecked husband. Lucille characterizes him as "one of those little, meek men" dominated by "one of those big, bullying women." She notes that "he does whatever his wife tells him to." (PN 027-163, 027-155)

His daily comfort is entirely dependent on how well newspaper reporters cover his wife's lectures. Mr. McCall monitors her expression anxiously as she reads the morning paper, knowing that a poorly reported lecture means a miserable day for him. (PN 029-140, 029-145)

He suffers silently under his wife's Rational Eating regime. The breakfast cereal — "an old straw hat that had been run through a meat chopper" — and the "more than usually offensive brand of imitation coffee" compete for the title of most loathed food item in the McCall household. (PN 029-083, 029-086)

## Key Relationships

- **Mrs. Cora Bates McCall** — His domineering wife, a lecturer on Rational Eating. She makes all decisions and he lives in fear of her moods. (PN 029-071, 029-140)

- **[[daniel-brewster]]** — Former friend, now legal adversary. The lawsuit between them was instigated by Mrs. McCall. Brewster is outraged when the McCalls register at his hotel. (PN 027-135, 027-165)

- **Washington McCall ("Washy")** — His teenage son, who shares his father's suffering under Mrs. McCall's dietary regime. Father and son bond over their mutual loathing of health food. (PN 029-078, 029-089)
