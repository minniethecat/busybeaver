Camões frames the Portuguese voyages not merely as commercial enterprise but as a continuation of the medieval Reconquista — a holy war carried across the seas against Muslim powers. ## About This Index

The `crusade` tag identifies episodes, persons, and concepts that embody the militant Christian ideology Camões uses to justify Portuguese imperialism. From the legendary Battle of Ourique to King Sebastian's doomed North African expedition, the crusade motif links Portugal's medieval past to its oceanic present, casting the conflict with Muslim powers in India and Africa as a sacred duty.
