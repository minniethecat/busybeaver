# Trade and Commerce

The pursuit of trade — above all the spice trade — is the driving economic force behind Vasco da Gama's voyage in *Os Lusíadas*. Camões depicts the Indian Ocean as a vast commercial theater, where Portuguese ships enter a world of established port cities, merchant networks, and lucrative commodities. The `trade` tag is applied to ports, regions, and commercial hubs that feature in the epic's geography of commerce. Camões frames the Portuguese mission as both a spiritual crusade and a commercial enterprise — trade is not incidental to the poem but one of its central justifications for empire.
