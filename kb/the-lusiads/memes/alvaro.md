# Álvaro

## Overview

Álvaro was one of [[vasco-da-gama|Vasco da Gama]]'s men who remained ashore at [[calicut|Calicut]] to guard the Portuguese merchandise after it had been landed as samples and gifts for the [[samorim|Samorim]]. His role, though brief in the poem, illustrates one of the recurring themes of The Lusiads: the vulnerability of Portuguese sailors in hostile foreign ports and their steadfastness in the face of obstruction.

## In The Lusiads

### The Guard at Calicut (Canto VIII)

Álvaro appears in Canto VIII, stanza 94, after Gama has landed merchandise as a display of Portuguese wealth and a gift to the Samorim. The [[catual|Catual]], Governor of Calicut, seizes the goods — using them as leverage to detain Gama and his crew. Álvaro and his companion Diogo are left to guard the store:

> Álvaro and Diogo guard the store, / with leave to truck or vend as best they can.

Despite being outnumbered and powerless to prevent the Catual's appropriation, their presence represents Portuguese resolve. They remain at their post even as the diplomatic situation deteriorates, a small but telling detail in Camões' portrayal of Portuguese honor under duress.

### Prophetic Reference (Canto X)

A second Álvaro appears in the prophetic vision of Canto X, where [[tethys|Tethys]] reveals future Portuguese glories. In stanza 70, an Álvaro is described as a future navigator who "opens the waters 'spite what risks oppose." This may be a different figure — possibly Álvaro de Caminha or another Portuguese captain — or the same name used to suggest the continuity of Portuguese maritime courage across generations.
