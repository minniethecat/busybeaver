# Prophecy and Fate

Prophecy is one of the most important structural devices in *Os Lusíadas*. From Adamastor's curse at the Cape of Good Hope to Jove's promise of eastern empires, prophetic speeches project the narrative forward and backward across centuries — binding Portugal's past (the Reconquista, the founding of the kingdom) to its imperial future in Asia, Africa, and Brazil. ## About This Index

The `prophecy` tag marks entities where the epic voice shifts from narration to foresight. Camões uses prophecy to telescope history — a device inherited from Virgil and Homer — so that events centuries apart (the Battle of Ourique, the conquest of India, the discovery of Brazil) appear as a single unfolding of divine providence. These passages are among the most frequently cited in the wiki, reflecting their narrative and ideological weight.
