# Mythological Themes

The mythological apparatus of *Os Lusíadas* draws heavily on Greco-Roman antiquity — Olympian gods intervene in the voyage, nymphs populate the seas, and classical myths are reworked into the Portuguese imperial narrative. ## About This Index

The `mythology` tag is applied to entities drawn from or directly referencing classical myth. Camões uses these figures both as narrative machinery (the council of the gods, Venus's patronage, Bacchus's opposition) and as literary ornament — weaving the Portuguese voyage into the fabric of classical epic tradition.
