# Voyages and Exploration

The voyage of Vasco da Gama is the central narrative thread of *Os Lusíadas*, but it is set within a wider culture of Portuguese maritime exploration that Camões celebrates throughout the poem. From the legendary travels of classical heroes to the expeditions of Prince Henry's captains, the epic weaves together multiple voyaging traditions. ## About This Index

The `voyage` and `exploration` tags identify episodes, persons, and concepts directly connected to maritime travel and discovery. Camões presents the Portuguese as the heirs to a long tradition of seafaring — from Odysseus and Aeneas to the navigators of the Age of Discovery — and the voyage itself becomes a metaphor for human endeavor, knowledge, and the expansion of empire.
