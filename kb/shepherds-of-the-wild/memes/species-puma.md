# Puma

The puma — also called cougar or mountain lion — is the most feared and respected predator in *Shepherds of the Wild*. It represents the untamable violence at the heart of the wilderness.

## Definition

The puma is presented as the ultimate test of vigilance for shepherds and their flocks. "Even the great puma or the terrible grizzly, unless they had the advantage of ambush, treated Spread Horn with respect" (030-001), establishing the puma as a creature that commands fear even from the mightiest prey. The species is personified most fully in Broken Fang, the legendary cougar known across the Idaho wilderness.

## Role in the Ecosystem

As an apex predator, the puma sits at the top of the food chain in Smoky Land. Its presence shapes the behavior of every creature below it — from the bighorn sheep that must remain vigilant to the coyotes that scavenge its kills. The puma's hunting method — "glaring of eye and hushed of foot" (031-002) — is described as a "dreadful business" (032-003), emphasizing predation as the central drama of the wild.

## Named Individuals

### Broken Fang

Broken Fang is "the great Broken Fang, the monarch of the cougars" (033-004), a legendary puma whose name is known across a thousand square miles of Idaho forest. Hugh, a hunter, comes to Smoky Land specifically to slay Broken Fang, framing the puma not just as a predator but as a worthy adversary — a creature whose death would carry meaning.

## Significance in the Novel

The puma embodies the novel's central tension between civilization and wilderness. It is magnificent but merciless, a creature that the shepherds both admire and fear. The novel's descriptions of puma cries — "one of the most diabolical sounds in the world" (034-005) — reinforce the gothic horror that the wilderness can evoke. Yet the puma is not villainized; it simply is what the wild demands it be.

## Key Passages

- (031-002): The puma described as "glaring of eye and hushed of foot" — the predator's essence.

- (034-005): The puma's cry described as diabolical — the sound of wild terror.
