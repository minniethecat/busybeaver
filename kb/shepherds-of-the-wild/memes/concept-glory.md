# Glory

Glory in *Shepherds of the Wild* is not human triumph but the fierce and terrible magnificence of nature itself — the hour when "fang and talon and strength and stealth come into their own."

## Definition

The novel defines glory through the predator's victory: "It is the hour of glory when fang and talon and strength and stealth come into their own" (039-001). Glory belongs to the hunt, to the successful kill, to the creature that proves its fitness through violence. This is not moral glory but natural glory — the splendor of the world operating as it was designed.

## Manifestations

Glory appears in several recurring forms:

- **The predatory hour**: Twilight and dawn are the hours of glory, when predators emerge and the test of survival begins (039-001).
- **The sun's glory**: The shepherd remembers "the sun breaking forth in glory to the east" (040-002), a vision of natural beauty that city spires could not wholly obscure.
- **The glory of strength**: An animal experiencing "the joy of victory, the glory of strength" (041-003) represents glory as physical triumph.
- **The prophecy of glory**: "There is a prophecy that some time such an hour of glory will come again" (042-004), suggesting glory as a promised return to wildness.
- **The buzzard's glory**: Even scavengers have their moment — "If all the streams and all the springs should dry up, the buzzards would be in their glory" (043-005), a dark reminder that glory is relative.

## Key Characters

Glory belongs primarily to the predators — the puma, the grizzly, the wolf — whose strength and stealth make them masters of their hour. The shepherd experiences glory vicariously, witnessing the sun and the wilderness rather than conquering it.

## Philosophical Significance

The novel's concept of glory challenges human-centered morality. Glory is not awarded for goodness but for fitness — the ability to survive, to hunt, to endure. This aligns with the novel's broader philosophy that the wilderness has its own moral order, indifferent to human judgments. The prophecy of glory's return suggests that the wild, though diminished, will one day reclaim its proper dominion.

## Key Passages

- (039-001): The hour of glory when fang and talon come into their own — the predator's moment.
- (040-002): The sun breaking forth in glory — natural splendor as spiritual experience.
- (041-003): The joy of victory and the glory of strength — physical triumph as glory.
- (042-004): The prophecy of an hour of glory to come again — hope for the wild's return.
- (043-005): The buzzards' glory in drought — glory's dark relativity.
