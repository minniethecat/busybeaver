# Dominance

## Definition

Dominance in *Shepherds of the Wild* operates on two levels: the natural hierarchies of the animal kingdom, where predators and prey occupy fixed positions in a chain of power, and the human struggle for dominance over land, resources, and other people. The novel draws explicit parallels between these two orders, suggesting that human conflicts over range rights are merely the civilized expression of the same dominance drive that animates the cougar and the wolf.

## Manifestations

In the animal world, dominance is clean and impersonal. Broken Fang the puma is the apex predator of Smoky Land — he kills when he is hungry, and lesser predators yield to him. The hierarchy is maintained by violence but governed by necessity; no animal kills for sport or spite. In the human world, dominance is corrupted by greed and cruelty. Landy Fargo's campaign to drive out the sheepmen is an exercise in dominance that serves no survival need — it is dominance as an end in itself, the will to power divorced from the natural order that contains it.

## Key Characters/Events

**Landy Fargo** represents dominance in its degenerate human form — using violence and intimidation not to survive but to eliminate competition and expand control. His pack of bear dogs (013-038) is the instrument of his dominance, turning natural predation into a tool of economic warfare. **Broken Fang** represents dominance in its natural form — fearsome, lethal, but morally neutral. He kills because he is a killer; Fargo kills because he chooses to.

## Philosophical Significance

The novel uses dominance to explore the difference between natural power and human tyranny. In nature, dominance serves ecological function; the predator keeps prey populations healthy, and the hierarchy prevents wasteful conflict. In human society, dominance becomes an addiction — Fargo doesn't need the Crowson range to survive, but his desire for control has become an end in itself. The novel's implicit argument is that human dominance, divorced from natural constraints, is more dangerous than any predator.
