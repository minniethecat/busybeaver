# Fang

## Definition

The fang is the predator's signature weapon in *Shepherds of the Wild*, embodying the killing power that maintains the wilderness order. From the "deadly, white-fanged mink" to the mighty grizzly and the great cougar Broken Fang himself, fangs are the instruments through which the food chain enforces its hierarchy (006-032). The novel uses "fang" both literally — as anatomy — and symbolically, as a measure of predatory identity.

## Manifestations

Fangs appear across the full spectrum of predation. The mink follows "a rabbit's trail beside the river" with deadly white fangs (006-032). Broken Fang the cougar derives his very name from this feature — he is not merely a cougar but "the great Broken Fang, the monarch of the cougars" (006-039), a creature "that had already won a name through a thousand square miles of Idaho forest" (003-015). The fang is what transforms stealth into death: "stealth and strength, fang and claw, the stalk in the shadows, the leap, the blow" (006-032) — the complete sequence of the kill.

## Key Characters/Events

Broken Fang is the novel's principal embodiment of fang-power. Hugh comes to Smoky Land to hunt him, imagining "the tawny cougar, Broken Fang, whom he had come to slay" responding to the hunting hour (004-006). The locals ask with knowing respect: "Maybe old Broken Fang himself?" (004-012). Even the wolf acknowledges Broken Fang's supremacy — when "Broken Fang himself had spoken" (005-050), the forest listens. The cougar's discouraging night of failed hunting (007-024) humanizes the predator without diminishing the lethal potential of his fangs.

## Philosophical Significance

Fangs represent the irreducible violence at the heart of nature. They are not evil — they are necessary. The novel presents predation without sentimentality: the fang is simply how the wilderness works. When Broken Fang hunts, he does so as "no ordinary cougar" but as one who "had ranged through Smoky Land, killing as he went" for more years than anyone can remember (007-025). The fang is the tool of survival for the predator and the symbol of death for the prey — an amoral fact of wild existence.

## Key Passages
