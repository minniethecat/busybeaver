# Old Surefoot

Old Surefoot is one of the two greatest bighorn rams of the Smoky Land high peaks, alongside his rival Old Argali. Though mentioned only briefly, his name captures the defining trait of the mountain sheep — their mastery of terrain that no predator can match.

## Overview

The sole direct reference to Old Surefoot comes in Chapter 18: "Old Surefoot and Argali, the two greatest of the bighorn rams, fought their battles in the fall" (018-009). This single sentence establishes him as Argali's equal and rival — a patriarch of the high country.

The bighorn sheep are described as "the finest game, the richest trophies, perhaps the most interesting wild animals in all North American fauna" (018-009), and Surefoot shares in this elevated status. His name — Surefoot — emphasizes the trait that sets his kind apart: the ability to navigate "narrow passes and steep precipices" (020-012) where predators cannot follow.

## Character Arc

Unlike Spot and Argali, Surefoot has no individual arc — he is a type rather than a character, representing the collective identity of the bighorn band. His significance lies in the world he represents: "Far above timber line, in the land where the great snow banks endured through the centuries" (018-009).

## Relationships

Surefoot's only relationship in the text is with Old Argali, his rival in the autumn battles. This rivalry — the competition between patriarchs — is part of the natural order of the high peaks, a ritual as old as the mountains themselves.

## Thematic Significance

Old Surefoot, like Argali, represents the wild in its elevated form. His name suggests not only physical agility but spiritual certainty — the bighorn knows his place in the world in a way that the domestic sheep (and the human characters) do not. He is the wild that needs no redemption because he has never been lost.

His inclusion alongside Argali — "the two greatest" — suggests that wildness is not singular but plural, not one patriarch but a community of equals who fight and coexist in the eternal cycle of the high country.

## Key Passages

> Old Surefoot and Argali, the two greatest of the bighorn rams, fought their battles in the fall. (018-009)
