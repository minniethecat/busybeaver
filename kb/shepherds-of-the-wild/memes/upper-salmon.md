# Upper Salmon

The Upper Salmon is a mountain region in Idaho, referenced as "the Upper Salmon country" (002-032) — a remote area inhabited by "that hairy old breed of mountaineers" who know the wilderness intimately.

## Overview

The Old Colonel mentions the Upper Salmon when persuading Hugh to hunt Broken Fang: "I know something about that hairy old breed of mountaineers in the Upper Salmon country. They don't take the trouble to give a puma a name unless he's a moose" (002-032). The region is characterized by its isolation and the self-reliance of its inhabitants.

The Upper Salmon watershed includes the high mountain terrain that forms the eastern boundary of Smoky Land, connecting the sheep country to the broader Idaho wilderness.

## Real-World Reference

The Salmon River in central Idaho — known as "The River of No Return" — is a real geographic feature. Marshall's "Upper Salmon country" corresponds to the upper reaches of this river system, one of the longest free-flowing rivers in the contiguous United States.
