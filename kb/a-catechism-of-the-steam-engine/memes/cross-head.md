The **cross-head** is a crucial component in [[steam|steam engine]]s that connects the [[piston]] rod to the [[connecting-rod]], converting the reciprocating motion of the piston into the rotary motion needed to drive machinery. It absorbs the lateral (side) thrust that would otherwise be transmitted to the piston rod, ensuring smooth linear motion.

## Construction and dimensions

[[john-bourne|Bourne]] provides detailed specifications for cross-head dimensions relative to the cylinder diameter:

> [029-102] What are the dimensions of the cross head? If the length of the cross head be taken at 1.4 times the diameter of the cylinder, the dimensions of the cross head will be as follows:—the diameter of the cross head journal will be 0.0358 times the cube root of the pressure on the piston in lbs. multiplied by the diameter of the cylinder in inches.

> [029-111] The diameter of each cross head journal will be 0.01716 times the square root of the pressure on the piston in lbs. multiplied by the diameter of the cylinder in inches.

The cross head features an eye (hole) for receiving the piston rod, and journals at each end for connection to the side rods. The web of the cross head experiences significant strain:

> [029-124] In these rules for the cross head, the strain upon the web is 1/2.25 times the elastic force.

## Function in the engine

The cross head serves as the connection point between the vertical motion of the piston and the angular motion of the side rods or [[connecting-rod|connecting rod]]:

> [009-056] A cross head, extends from the bottom of the piston rod to the side rods, transmitting the force from the piston to the side levers.

> [010-009] A strong gudgeon, called the cross head gudgeon, connects the cross head to the side rods.

In [[marine-engine|marine engine]]s, the cross head also provides an attachment point for the air-pump rod:

> [009-074] The air pump rod is suspended from a cross head, passing through a stuffing box in the bottom of the hot well.

## Types and variations

Cross heads vary by engine type. In side-lever [[marine-engine|marine engine]]s, the cross head connects to side rods that run parallel to the cylinder. In direct-acting engines, it connects directly to the [[connecting-rod]]. The [[stuffing-box]] beneath the cylinder must accommodate the cross head's vertical travel while maintaining a steam-tight seal.
