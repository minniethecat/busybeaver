# Cabrey

**Mr. Cabrey** (fl. early 19th century) was an engineer and inventor who devised an arrangement for working steam expansively on [[locomotive|locomotive engine]]s. His mechanism was one of several expedients developed in the early railway era to achieve variable expansion without the use of cams, which [[john-bourne|Bourne]] notes were "inadmissible" in [[locomotive|locomotive engine]]s (016-072).

## Career and Contributions

Cabrey's [[expansion-valve|expansion gear]] operated on the principle of varying the throw of the [[slide-valve|slide valve]] to accomplish the requisite variations of expansion (016-072). His specific arrangement made the eccentric rod terminate in a pin which worked into a straight slotted lever, furnished with jaws similar to those on the eccentric rods of locomotives. By raising the pin of the eccentric rod in this slot, the travel of the valve would be varied, and expansive action would be the result (016-094).

Cabrey's arrangement was listed alongside those of [[george-stephenson|Stephenson]], [[fenton|Fenton]], [[dodds|Dodds]], [[farcot|Farcot]], [[edwards|Edwards]], [[lavagrian|Lavagrian]], [[rudolf-bodmer|Bodmer]], [[meyer|Meyer]], [[robert-hawthorn|Hawthorn]], [[gonzenbach|Gonzenbach]], and [[john-bourne|Bourne]] himself as among the numerous forms of apparatus contrived for [[expansion-valve|expansive working]] (069-055).

## Legacy

Cabrey's expansion valve design represents one of the many independent attempts during the early nineteenth century to solve the problem of variable expansion in [[steam|steam engine]]s. His work contributed to the broader development of [[expansion-valve|expansion valve]] technology, though his specific mechanism did not achieve the lasting adoption of [[george-stephenson|Stephenson]]'s [[link-motion|link motion]].
