## Overview

Messrs. Rennie was a British engineering firm associated with George Rennie (1791–1866) and his brother [[John Rennie|Sir John Rennie]] (1794–1874). The firm was known for manufacturing marine steam engines, mill machinery, and other heavy engineering products during the 19th century.

## Marine Engine Construction

The firm manufactured marine steam engines of substantial size. Bourne records that Messrs. Rennie constructed engines with cylinders 61 inches in diameter. (055-071) Their engines employed a distinctive configuration of the eduction passage, with the eduction area being a specific proportion of the cylinder dimensions. (055-069)

## Friction Experiments

Mr. G. Rennie of the firm conducted experiments on the friction of shafts under different pressures. Bourne cites these experiments, noting that with a particular pressure the friction was found to be a specific fraction of the weight. (005-065)
