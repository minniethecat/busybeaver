## Overview

Miller, Ravenhill & Co. (also known as Messrs. Miller, Ravenhill and Co. and Miller & Ravenhill) were British marine engine builders. The firm constructed steam engines for several notable vessels during the mid-19th century.

## Engineering Work

The firm built large marine steam engines. For the *Indus* and *Ripon*, they constructed engines with cylinders 76 inches in diameter. (055-081) Miller, Ravenhill & Co. developed a distinctive method of fixing the piston in oscillating engines: instead of using a single central bolt, they employed four bolts, which they found to be more secure and less liable to work loose. (038-041) Their mode of fixing the piston was documented by Bourne as a notable practice in marine engine construction. (069-320)

## Vessels

The firm supplied engines for HMS *Indus*, described as having cylinders 76 inches in diameter with an engine of the oscillating type. (028-046)

Earlier in its history, operating as Messrs. Ravenhill & Salked, the firm built engines for the Holyhead packet service. (010-070)
