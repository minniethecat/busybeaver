# Robert Hawthorn

**Robert Hawthorn** (1796–1867) was an English [[locomotive|locomotive engine]]er and manufacturer. His firm, R. & W. Hawthorn of Newcastle upon Tyne, was one of the leading locomotive builders of the early railway era, supplying engines to numerous British and overseas railways.

## Career and Contributions

Hawthorn's locomotives were noted for their robust construction and reliable performance. [[john-bourne|Bourne]] takes Hawthorn's locomotive boiler design as a representative example when explaining boiler dimensioning rules: the boiler contained a [[fire-box]] with a specific grate area, a barrel of given length and diameter, and a defined number of tubes — all proportions that illustrated sound design practice (011-030).

The firm's engines were built with attention to accessible maintenance, a consideration that made them popular with railway companies operating in remote locations where skilled repair staff were scarce.

## Legacy

R. & W. Hawthorn grew to become one of Britain's largest locomotive exporters. The firm's designs influenced locomotive practice throughout the British Empire, and the company continued building engines into the twentieth century.
