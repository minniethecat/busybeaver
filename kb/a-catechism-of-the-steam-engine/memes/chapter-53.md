[053-001] CHAPTER X.

[053-002] EXAMPLES OF ENGINES.

[053-003] *       *       *       *       *

[053-004] OSCILLATING PADDLE ENGINES.

[053-005] 618. _Q._--Will you describe the structure of an oscillating engine as made

[053-006] by Messrs. Penn?

[053-007] _A._--To do this it will be expedient to take an engine of a given power,

[053-008] and then the sizes may be given as well as an account of the configuration

[053-009] of the parts: we may take for an example a pair of engines of 21-1/2 inches

[053-010] diameter of cylinder, and 22 inches stroke, rated by Messrs. Penn at 12

[053-011] horses power each. The cylinders of this oscillating engine are placed

[053-012] beneath the cranks, and, as in all Messrs. Penn's smaller engines, the

[053-013] piston rod is connected to the crank pin by means of a brass cap, provided

[053-014] with a socket, by means of which it is cuttered to the piston rod. There is

[053-015] but one air pump, which is situated within the condenser between the

[053-016] cylinders, and it is wrought by means of a crank in the intermediate

[053-017] shaft--this crank being cut out of a solid piece of metal as in the

[053-018] formation of the cranked axles of locomotive engines. The steam enters the

[053-019] cylinder through the outer trunnions, or the trunnions adjacent to the

[053-020] ship's sides, and enters the condenser through the two midship trunnions--a

[053-021] short three ported valve being placed on the front of the cylinder to

[053-022] regulate the flow of steam to and from the cylinder in the proper manner.

[053-023] The weight of this valve on one side of the cylinder is balanced by a

[053-024] weight hung upon the other side of the cylinder; but in the most recent

[053-025] engines this weight is discarded, and two valves are used, which balance

[053-026] one another. The framing consists of an upper and lower frame of cast iron,

[053-027] bound together by eight malleable iron columns: upon the lower frame the

[053-028] pillow blocks rest which carry the cylinder trunnions, and the condenser

[053-029] and the bottom frame are cast in the same piece. The upper frame supports

[053-030] the paddle shaft pillow blocks; and pieces are bolted on in continuation of

[053-031] the upper frame to carry the paddle wheels, which are overhung from the

[053-032] journal.

[053-033] 619. _Q._--What are the dimensions and arrangement of the framing?

[053-034] _A._--The web, or base plate of the lower frame is 3/4 of an Inch thick,

[053-035] and a cooming is earned all round the cylinder, leaving an opening of

[053-036] sufficient size to permit the necessary oscillation. The cross section of

[053-037] the upper frame is that of a hollow beam 6 inches deep, and about 3-1/2

[053-038] inches wide, with holes at the sides to take out the core; and the

[053-039] thickness of the metal is 13/16ths of an inch. Both the upper and the lower

[053-040] frame is cast in a single piece, with the exception of the continuations of

[053-041] the upper frame, which support the paddle wheels. An oval ring 3 inches

[053-042] wide is formed in the upper frame, of sufficient size to permit the working

[053-043] of the air pump crank; and from this ring feathers run to the ends of the

[053-044] cross portions of the frame which supports the intermediate shaft journals.

[053-045] The columns are 1-1/2 inches in diameter; they are provided with collars at

[053-046] the lower ends, which rest upon bosses in the lower frame, and with collars

[053-047] at the upper ends for supporting the upper frame; but the upper collars of

[053-048] two of the corner columns are screwed on, so as to enable the columns to be

[053-049] drawn up when it is required to get the cylinders out. The cross section of

[053-050] the bottom frame is also of the form of a hollow beam, 7 inches deep,

[053-051] except in the region of the condenser, where it is, of course, of a

[053-052] different form. The depth of the boss for the reception of the columns is a

[053-053] little more than 7 inches deep on the lower frame, and a little more than 6

[053-054] inches deep on the upper frame; and the holes through them are so cored

[053-055] out, that the columns only bear at the upper and lower edges of the hole,

[053-056] instead of all through it--a formation by which the fitting of the columns

[053-057] is facilitated.

[053-058] 620. _Q._--What are the dimensions of the condenser?

[053-059] _A._--The condenser, which is cast upon the lower frame, consists of an

[053-060] oval vessel 22-1/2 inches wide, by 2 feet 4-1/4 inches long, and 1 foot

[053-061] 10-1/2 inches deep; it stands 9 inches above the upper face of the bottom

[053-062] frame, the rest projecting beneath it; and it is enlarged at the sides by

[053-063] being carried beneath the trunnions.

[053-064] 621. _Q._--What are the dimensions of the air pump?

[053-065] _A._--The air pump, which is set in the centre of the condenser, is 15-1/4

[053-066] inches in diameter, and has a stroke of 11 inches. The foot valve is

[053-067] situated in the bottom of the air pump, and its seat consists of a disc of

[053-068] brass, in which there is a rectangular flap valve, opening upwards, but

[053-069] rounded on one side to the circle of the pump, and so balanced as to enable

[053-070] the valve to open with facility. The balance weight, which is formed of

[053-071] brass cast in the same piece as the valve itself, operates as a stop, by

[053-072] coming into contact with the disc which constitutes the bottom of the pump;

[053-073] the disc being recessed opposite to the stop to enable the valve to open

[053-074] sufficiently. This disc is bolted to the barrel of the pump by means of an

[053-075] internal flange, and before it can be removed the pump must be lifted out

[053-076] of its place. The air pump barrel is of brass to which is bolted a cast

[053-077] iron mouth piece, with a port for carrying the water to the hot well;

[053-078] within the hot well the delivery valve, which consists of a common flap

[053-079] valve, is situated. The mouth piece and the air pump barrel are made tight

[053-080] to the condenser, and to one another, by means of metallic joints carefully

[053-081] scraped to a true surface, so that a little white or red lead interposed

[053-082] makes an air tight joint. The air pump bucket is of brass, and the valve of

[053-083] the bucket is of the common pot lid or spindle kind. The injection water

[053-084] enters through a single cock in front of the condenser--the jet striking

[053-085] against the barrel of the air pump. The air pump rod is maintained in its

[053-086] vertical position by means of guides, the lower ends of which are bolted to

[053-087] the mouth of the pump, and the upper to the oval in the top frame, within

[053-088] which the air pump crank works; and the motion is communicated from this

[053-089] crank to the pump rod by means of a short connected rod. The lower frame is

[053-090] not set immediately below the top frame, but 2-1/2 inches behind it, and

[053-091] the air pump and condenser are 2-1/2 inches nearer one edge of the lower

[053-092] frame than the other.

[053-093] 622. _Q._--What are the dimensions of the cylinder?

[053-094] _A._--The thickness of the metal of the cylinder is 9/16ths of an inch; the

[053-095] depth of the belt of the cylinder is 9-1/2 inches, and its greatest

[053-096] projection from the cylinder is 2-1/2 inches. The distance from the lower

[053-097] edge of the belt to the bottom of the cylinder is 11-1/2 inches, and from

[053-098] the upper edge of the belt to the top flange of the cylinder is 9 inches.

[053-099] The trunnions are 7-1/4 inches diameter in the bearings, and 3-1/2 inches

[053-100] in width; and the flanges to which the glands are attached for screwing in

[053-101] the trunnion packings are 1-1/2 inch thick, and have 7/8ths of an inch of

[053-102] projection. The width of the packing space round the trunnions is 5/8ths of

[053-103] an inch, and the diameter of the pipe passing through the trunnion

[053-104] 4-5/8ths, which leaves 11/16ths for the thickness of the metal of the

[053-105] bearing. Above and below each trunnion a feather runs from the edge of the

[053-106] belt or bracket between 3 and 4 inches along the cylinder, for the sake of

[053-107] additional support; and in large engines the feather is continued through

[053-108] the interior of the belt, and cruciform feathers are added for the sake of

[053-109] greater stiffness. The projection of the outer face of the trunnion flange

[053-110] from the side of the cylinder is 6-1/2 inches; the thickness of the flange

[053-111] round the mouth of the cylinder is 3/4 of an inch, and its projection 1-3/8

[053-112] inch; the height of the cylinder stuffing box above the cylinder cover is

[053-113] 4-1/8 inches, and its external diameter 4-3/8 inches--the diameter of the

[053-114] piston rod being 2-1/8 inches. The thickness of the stuffing box flange is

[053-115] 1-1/8 inch.

[053-116] 623. _Q._--Will you describe the nature of the communication between the

[053-117] cylinder and condenser?

[053-118] _A._--The pipe leading to the condenser from the cylinder is made somewhat

[053-119] bell mouthed where it joins the condenser, and the gland for compressing

[053-120] the packing is made of a larger internal diameter in every part except at

[053-121] the point at which the pipe emerges from it, where it accurately fits the

[053-122] pipe so as to enable the gland to squeeze the packing. By this construction

[053-123] the gland may be drawn back without being jammed upon the enlarged part of

[053-124] the pipe; and the enlargement of the pipe toward the condenser prevents the

[053-125] air pump barrel from offering any impediment to the free egress of the

[053-126] steam. The gland is made altogether in four pieces: the ring which presses

[053-127] the packing is made distinct from the flange to which the bolts are

[053-128] attached which force the gland against the packing, and both ring and

[053-129] flange are made in two pieces, to enable them to be got over the pipe. The

[053-130] ring is half checked in the direction of its depth, and is introduced

[053-131] without any other support to keep the halves together, than what is

[053-132] afforded by the interior of the stuffing box; and the flange is half

[053-133] checked in the direction of its thickness, so that the bolts which press

[053-134] down the ring by passing through this half-checked part, also keep the

[053-135] segments of the flange together. The bottom of the trunnion packing space

[053-136] is contracted to the diameter of the eduction pipe, so as to prevent the

[053-137] packing from being squeezed into the jacket; but the eduction pipe does not

[053-138] fit quite tight into this contracted part, but, while in close contact on

[053-139] the lower side, has about 1/32nd of an inch of space between the top of the

[053-140] pipe and the cylinder, so as to permit the trunnions to wear to that extent

[053-141] without throwing a strain upon the pipe. The eduction pipe is attached to

[053-142] the condenser by a flange joint, and the bolt holes are all made somewhat

[053-143] oblong in the perpendicular direction, so as to permit the pipe to be

[053-144] slightly lowered, should such an operation be rendered necessary by the

[053-145] wear of the trunnion bearings; but in practice the wear of the trunnion

[053-146] bearings is found to be so small as to be almost inappreciable.

[053-147] 624. _Q._--Will you describe the valve and valve casing?

[053-148] _A._--The length of the valve casing is 16-1/2 inches, and its projection

[053-149] from the cylinder is 3-1/2 inches at the top, 4-1/4 inches at the centre,

[053-150] and 2-1/2 inches at the bottom, so that the back of the valve casing is not

[053-151] made flat, but is formed in a curve. The width of the valve casing is 9

[053-152] inches, but there is a portion of the depth of the belt 1-1/2 inch wider,

[053-153] to permit the steam to enter from the belt into the casing. The valve

[053-154] casing is attached to the cylinder by a metallic joint; the width of the

[053-155] flange of this joint is 1-1/4 inch, the thickness of the flange on the

[053-156] casing 1/2 inch, and the thickness of the flange on the cylinder 5/8ths of

[053-157] an inch. The projection from the cylinder of the passage for carrying the

[053-158] steam upwards, and downwards, from the valve to the top and bottom of the

[053-159] cylinder, is 2-1/4 inches, and its width externally 8-5/8 inches. The valve

[053-160] is of the ordinary three ported description, and both cylinder and valve

[053-161] faces are of cast iron.
