The **[[stuffing-box|gland]]** (sometimes called a junk ring) is a metal ring fitted into the mouth of a [[stuffing-box]] that compresses the [[packing]] material against the reciprocating rod passing through it. By tightening the [[stuffing-box|gland]] bolts, the packing is squeezed radially inward against the rod, creating a [[steam]]-tight seal while still permitting the rod to slide freely. The [[stuffing-box|gland]] is an essential component of every [[piston]] rod, [[valve]] spindle, and pump rod penetration through a pressure boundary.

## Construction and materials

The [[stuffing-box|gland]] is typically made of cast iron or brass, with brass being preferred where corrosion resistance is important:

> [041-020] Where the stuffing box is below the water line the gland must be of brass, but for a waste water pipe a cast iron gland will answer.

The depth of the [[stuffing-box|gland]] and the number of securing bolts are critical to achieving uniform compression:

> [040-065] The gland should be made of adequate depth, and the gland should be secured by bolts passing through it, which pull it down equally upon the packing.

## How the gland works

The [[stuffing-box|gland]] forces the packing to expand radially inward against the rod:

> [039-031] The gland compresses the packing ring, and the packing also aids in confining the steam by its own elasticity, independently of the pressure of the gland.

> [041-019] The packing is kept in by means of a gland secured with bolts passing through the stuffing box flange. By tightening these bolts the packing is compressed, and a steam-tight joint is the result.

## Marine and large-engine glands

In larger engines and marine applications, the [[stuffing-box|gland]] design is more elaborate to accommodate thermal expansion and misalignment:

> [053-122] The pipe is made slightly smaller than the hole through the condenser, so as to enable the gland to squeeze the packing. By this means the gland may be drawn back without disturbing the pipe.

> [053-126] The gland is made altogether in four pieces, to enable it to be removed without taking off the flange.

## Gland types by application

| Application | Gland material | Special features |
|------------|---------------|-----------------|
| [[piston|Piston]] rod (land engine) | Cast iron | Simple bolted flange |
| [[piston|Piston]] rod (marine, below WL) | Brass | Corrosion-resistant |
| Waste water pipe | Cast iron | Simplified design |
| [[condenser|Condenser]] penetration | Brass or gunmetal | Split (multi-piece) for easy removal |
| Trunnion ([[oscillating-engine|oscillating engine]]) | Brass-bushed | Deep [[stuffing-box|gland]] for extra packing depth |

> [055-086] The intermediate space is occupied with a very deep gland, bushed with brass. The internal part of the gland is formed with a recess to receive a ring of India rubber.
