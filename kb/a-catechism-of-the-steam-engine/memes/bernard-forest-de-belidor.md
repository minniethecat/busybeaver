# Bernard Forest de Bélidor (1698–1761)

Bernard Forest de Bélidor was a French military and hydraulic engineer whose work on water wheels, pumps, and valves influenced mechanical engineering practice across Europe. Though he died before the steam age fully matured, his valve designs found application in [[steam|steam engine]] [[feed-pump|feed pumps]] and other auxiliary machinery.

## Career and Contributions

Bélidor served as a professor of artillery and as a military engineer in the French army. His major work, *Architecture Hydraulique* (1737–1753), was a comprehensive treatise on hydraulic engineering that covered water wheels, pumps, sluices, and valve mechanisms. This work became a standard reference for engineers throughout Europe.

In the context of [[steam|steam engine]] auxiliaries, Bélidor's valve designs were adapted for [[feed-pump|feed pump]] delivery valves. [[john-bourne|Bourne]] notes that delivery valves in [[feed-pump|feed pump]]s "might be made on Belidor's plan" (068-314), and that foot valves might similarly follow his design. (069-101) The valves operated on a simple hinged-flap principle — "valves on Belidor's plan" (039-013) — and were considered suitable for forcing pumps, though [[john-bourne|Bourne]] observes they could be objectionable "in many ways" for certain applications.

Bélidor's principle was to use a leather-faced hinged flap that opened under pressure from below and closed by its own weight and back-pressure. While simple, these valves were less reliable than the later [[slide-valve|slide valves]] and equilibrium valves developed for high-pressure steam service.

## Legacy

Bélidor's engineering treatises bridged the gap between seventeenth-century hydraulic knowledge and the industrial revolution's demands for reliable pumping machinery. His valve designs, though eventually superseded by more sophisticated mechanisms, represent an important stage in the evolution of [[steam|steam engine]] feed-water systems. The continued reference to "Belidor's plan" in an 1840s [[steam|steam engine]]ering textbook demonstrates the longevity of his influence on practical machine design.
