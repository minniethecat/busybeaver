# Governor

The **governor**, also known as the centrifugal governor or conical pendulum, is a device that automatically regulates the speed of a [[steam|steam engine]] by controlling the admission of steam. (003-037) (008-089)

It consists of two heavy balls attached to arms hinged on a vertical spindle, which is driven by the engine. As the spindle speed increases, the balls fly outward by centrifugal force, and through a system of levers or bell-cranks they partially close the [[valve|throttle valve]], reducing the steam supply. When the speed falls, the balls collapse inward and the [[throttle-valve|throttle]] opens. (008-090) (008-092)

## Operating Principle

The governor is fundamentally a conical pendulum. (004-029) Its operation is governed by the laws of centrifugal force: the height of the cone that the balls describe determines the speed at which they will maintain equilibrium. (004-054)

If the vertical height of the cone is fixed, then the speed of the governor must remain constant; conversely, if the speed varies, the height of the cone changes and the [[throttle-valve|throttle]] position is altered accordingly. (004-054)

> "The velocity with which the balls of the governor revolve being inconsiderable, the ordinary arrangement can only be used in engines making a moderate number of revolutions." (008-095)

The governor does not act instantaneously; a certain range of speed change is required before the [[throttle-valve|throttle]] moves appreciably. (004-068) This tendency for the balls to resist change gives the governor a degree of stability, preventing it from over-correcting minor speed fluctuations.

## Construction

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Balls | Heavy spheres, typically of iron, attached to the arms (004-085) |
| Arms | Fixed upon the upright shaft, carry the heavy balls (008-093) (008-094) |
| Spindle | Upright shaft kept in revolution by a cord from the fly wheel (008-093) (008-094) |
| Collar | Sliding collar on the spindle, moved by the arms (008-090) |
| Bell-cranks | Levers connecting the collar to the [[valve|throttle valve]] (008-092) |
| Throttle valve | The [[valve|steam valve]] whose opening is regulated |

Heavy balls are only required when there is considerable friction in the [[throttle-valve|throttle]] mechanism to overcome. (004-085) For well-made engines, small balls suffice, which allows the governor to respond more quickly to speed changes.

## High-Speed Governors

In engines running at higher speeds, such as [[Locomotive|locomotives]], the standard governor arrangement is unsuitable because the governor spindle cannot be driven fast enough from the main shaft to provide adequate centrifugal force with a moderate ball size. (008-095)

For high-speed applications, very small balls are employed, and the spindle is geared up to rotate at a higher speed than the engine. (058-121) (058-141) The governor spindle of a high-speed governor is finished bright on all parts except the bracket, and the arms and spindle are of polished steel. (058-137)

## Practical Considerations

The [[james-watt|James Watt]] governor was one of the earliest and most successful feedback control mechanisms in engineering history. It enabled engines to run unattended at constant speed without continuous human adjustment of the [[throttle-valve|throttle]].

The governor works on the [[valve|throttle valve]], but in engines with [[Valve|variable expansion gear]] it may also be connected to alter the point of cut-off, providing a more efficient means of speed regulation than simple throttling.
