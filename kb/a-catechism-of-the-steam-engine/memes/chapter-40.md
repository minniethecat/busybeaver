[040-001] 467. _Q._--To what do you attribute the circumstance of a better vacuum

[040-002] being got at low speeds than at high speeds?

[040-003] _A._--It is difficult to assign the precise reason, but it appears to be a

[040-004] consequence of the largeness of the vacant space between the valve plates.

[040-005] When the piston of the air pump is drawn back, the air contained in this

[040-006] large collection of water will cause it to boil up like soda water; and

[040-007] when the piston of the pump is forced forward, this air, instead of being

[040-008] expelled, will be again driven into the water. There will consequently be a

[040-009] quantity of air in the pump which cannot be got rid of at all, and which

[040-010] will impair the vacuum as a matter of course.

[040-011] 468. _Q._--What expedient did you adopt to improve the vacuum in the engine

[040-012] to which you have referred?

[040-013] _A._--I put blocks of wood on the air pump piston, which at the end of its

[040-014] stroke projected between the valve plates and forced the water out. I also

[040-015] introduced a cock of water at each end of the pump between the valve

[040-016] plates, to insure the presence of water at each end of the pump to force

[040-017] the air out. With these ameliorations the pump worked steadily, and the

[040-018] vacuum obtained became as good as in the old pump. I had previously

[040-019] introduced an injection cock into each end of the air pump in steam

[040-020] vessels, from which I had obtained advantageous results; and in all

[040-021] horizontal air pumps I would recommend the piston and valve plates to be so

[040-022] constructed that the whole of the water will be expressed by the piston. I

[040-023] would also recommend an injection cock to be introduced at each end of the

[040-024] pump.

[040-025] PUMPS, COCKS, AND PIPES.

[040-026] 469. _Q._--Will you explain the arrangement of the feed pump?

[040-027] _A._--In steam vessels, the feed pump plunger is generally of brass, and

[040-028] the barrel of the pump is sometimes of brass, but generally of cast iron.

[040-029] There should be a considerable clearance between the bottom of the plunger

[040-030] and the bottom of the barrel, as otherwise the bottom of the barrel may be

[040-031] knocked out, should coal dust or any other foreign substance gain

[040-032] admission, as it probably would do if the injection water were drawn at any

[040-033] time from the bilge of the vessel, as is usually done if the vessel springs

[040-034] a leak. The valves of the feed pump in marine engines are generally of the

[040-035] spindle kind, and are most conveniently arranged in a chest, which may be

[040-036] attached in any accessible position to the side of the hot well. There are

[040-037] two nozzles upon this chest, of which the lower one leads to the pump, and

[040-038] the upper one to the boiler. The pipe leading to the pump is a suction pipe

[040-039] when the plunger ascends, and a forcing pipe when the plunger descends. The

[040-040] plunger in ascending draws the water out of the hot well through the lowest

[040-041] of the valves, and in descending forces it through the centre valve into

[040-042] the space above it, which communicates with the feed pipe. Should the feed

[040-043] cock be shut so as to prevent any feed water from passing through it, the

[040-044] water will raise the topmost valve, which is loaded to a pressure

[040-045] considerably above the pressure of the steam, and escape into the hot well.

[040-046] This arrangement is neater and less expensive than that of having a

[040-047] separate loaded valve on the feed pipe with an overflow through the ship's

[040-048] side, as is the more usual practice.

[040-049] 470. _Q._--Will you describe what precautions are to be observed in the

[040-050] construction of the cocks used in engines?

[040-051] _A._--All the cocks about an engine should be provided with bottoms and

[040-052] stuffing boxes, and reliance should never be placed upon a single bolt

[040-053] passing through a bottom washer for keeping the plug in its place, in the

[040-054] case of any cock communicating with the boiler; for a great strain is

[040-055] thrown upon that bolt if the pressure of the steam be high, and if the plug

[040-056] be made with much taper; and should the bolt break, or the threads strip,

[040-057] the plug will fly out, and persons standing near may be scalded to death.

[040-058] In large cocks, it appears the preferable plan to cast the bottoms in; and

[040-059] the metal of which all the cocks about a marine engine are made, should be

[040-060] of the same quality as that used in the composition of the brasses, and

[040-061] should be without lead, or other deteriorating material. In some cases the

[040-062] bottoms of cocks are burnt in with hard solder, but this method cannot be

[040-063] depended upon, as the solder is softened and wasted away by the hot salt

[040-064] water, and in time the bottom leaks, or is forced out. The stuffing box of

[040-065] cocks should be made of adequate depth, and the gland should be secured by

[040-066] means of four strong copper bolts. The taper of blow-off cocks is an

[040-067] important element in their construction; as, if the taper be too great, the

[040-068] plugs will have a continual tendency to rise, which, if the packing be

[040-069] slack, will enable grit to get between the faces, while, if the taper be

[040-070] too little, the plug will be liable to jam, and a few times grinding will

[040-071] sink it so far through the shell that the waterways will no longer

[040-072] correspond. One eighth of an inch deviation from the perpendicular for

[040-073] every inch in height, is a common angle for the side of the cock, which

[040-074] corresponds with one quarter of an inch difference of diameter in an inch

[040-075] of height; but perhaps a somewhat greater taper than this, or one third of

[040-076] an inch difference in diameter for every inch of height, is a preferable

[040-077] proportion. The bottom of the plug must be always kept a small distance

[040-078] above the bottom of the shell, and an adequate surface must be left above

[040-079] and below the waterway to prevent leakage. Cocks formed according to these

[040-080] directions will be found to operate satisfactorily in practice, while they

[040-081] will occasion perpetual trouble if there be any malformation.

[040-082] 471. _Q._--What is the best arrangement and configuration of the blow-off

[040-083] cocks?

[040-084] _A._--The blow-off cocks of a boiler are generally placed some distance

[040-085] from the boiler; but it appears preferable that they should be placed quite

[040-086] close to it, as there are no means of shutting off the water from the pipe

[040-087] between the blow-off cock and the boiler, should fracture or leakage there

[040-088] arise. Every boiler must be furnished with a blow-off cock of its own,

[040-089] independently of the main blow-off cocks on the ship's sides, so that the

[040-090] boilers may be blown off separately, and may be shut off from one another.

[040-091] The preferable arrangement appears to be, to cast upon each blow-off cock a

[040-092] bend for attaching the cock to the bottom of the boiler, and the plug

[040-093] should stand about an inch in advance of the front of the boiler, so that

[040-094] it may be removed, or re-ground, with facility. The general arrangement of

[040-095] the blow-off pipes is to run a main blow-off pipe beneath the floor plates,

[040-096] across the ship, at the end of the engines, and into this pipe to lead a

[040-097] separate pipe, furnished with a cock, from each boiler. The main blow-off

[040-098] pipe, where it penetrates the ship's side, is furnished with a cock: and in

[040-099] modern steam vessels Kingston's valves are also used, which consist of a

[040-100] spindle or plate valve, fitted to the exterior of the ship, so that if the

[040-101] internal pipe or cock breaks, the external valve will still be operative.

[040-102] Some expedient of this kind is almost necessary, as the blow-off cocks

[040-103] require occasional regrinding, and the sea cocks cannot be re-ground

[040-104] without putting the vessel into dock, except by the use of Kingston's

[040-105] valves, or some equivalent expedient.

[040-106] 472. Q.--What is the proper construction and situation of the injection

[040-107] cocks, and waste water valves?

[040-108] A.--The sea injection cocks are usually made in the same fashion as the sea

[040-109] blow-off cocks, and of about the same size, or rather larger. The injection

[040-110] water is generally admitted to the condenser by means of a slide valve, but

[040-111] a cock appears to be preferable, as it is more easily opened, and has not

[040-112] any disposition to shut of its own accord. In paddle vessels the sea

[040-113] injection pipes should be put through the ship's sides in advance of the

[040-114] paddles, so that the water drawn in may not be injuriously charged with

[040-115] air. The waste water pipe passing from the hot well through the vessel's

[040-116] side is provided with a stop valve, called the discharge valve, which is

[040-117] usually made of the spindle kind, so as to open when the water coming from

[040-118] the air pump presses against it. In some cases this valve is a sluice

[040-119] valve, but the hot well is then almost sure to be split, if the engine be

[040-120] set on without the valve having been opened. The opening of the waste water

[040-121] pipe should always be above the load water line, as it will otherwise be

[040-122] difficult to prevent leakage through the engine into the ship when the

[040-123] vessel is lying in harbor.

[040-124] 473. Q.--What is the best arrangement of gauge cocks and glass gauges?

[040-125] A.--Gauge cocks are generally very inartificially made, and occasion

[040-126] needless annoyance. They are rarely made with bottoms, or with stuffing

[040-127] boxes, and are consequently, for the most part, adorned with stalactites of

[040-128] salt after a short period of service. The water discharged from them, too,

[040-129] from the want of a proper conduit, disfigures the front of the boiler, and

[040-130] adds to the corrosion in the ash pits. It would be preferable to combine

[040-131] the gauge cocks appertaining to each boiler into a single upright tube,

[040-132] connected suitably with the boiler, and the water flowing from them could

[040-133] be directed downward into a funnel tube communicating with the bilge. The

[040-134] cocks of the glass tubes, as well as of the gauge cocks, should be

[040-135] furnished with stuffing boxes and with bottoms, unless the water enters

[040-136] through the bottom of the plug, which in gauge cocks is sometimes the case.

[040-137] The glass gauge tubes should always be fitted with a cock at each neck

[040-138] communicating with the boiler, so that the water and steam may be shut off

[040-139] if the tube breaks; and the cocks should be so made as to admit of the

[040-140] tubes being blown through with steam to clear them, as in muddy water they

[040-141] will become so soiled that the water cannot be seen. The gauge cocks

[040-142] frequently have pipes running up within the boiler, to the end that a high

[040-143] water level may be made consistent with an easily accessible position of

[040-144] the gauge cocks themselves. With the glass tubes, however, this species of

[040-145] arrangement is not possible, and the glass tubes must always be placed in

[040-146] the position of the water level.

[040-147] 474. Q.--What is the proper material of the pipes in steam vessels?

[040-148] A.--Most of the pipes of marine engines should be made of copper. The steam

[040-149] pipes may be of cast iron, if made very strong, but the waste water pipes

[040-150] should be of copper. Cast iron blow-off pipes have in some cases been

[040-151] employed, but they are liable to fracture, and are dangerous. The blow-off

[040-152] and feed pipes should be of copper, but the waste steam pipe may be of

[040-153] galvanized iron. Every pipe passing through the ship's side, and every pipe

[040-154] fixed at both ends, and liable to be heated and cooled, should be furnished

[040-155] with a faucet or expansive joint; and in the case of the cast iron pipes,

[040-156] the part of the pipe fitting into the faucet should be turned. In the

[040-157] distribution of the faucets of the pipes exposed to pressure, care must be

[040-158] taken that they be so placed that the parts of the pipe cannot be forced

[040-159] asunder, or turned round by the strain, as serious accidents have occurred

[040-160] from the neglect of this precaution.
