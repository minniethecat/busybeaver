# Maudslay Sons and Field

**Maudslay Sons and Field** was a prominent British engineering firm specializing in marine [[Steam|steam]] engines during the nineteenth century. [[john-bourne|Bourne]] compares their practice extensively with that of [[boulton-and-watt|Boulton and Watt]] and [[john-penn|John Penn]]. (020-056)

The firm originated with [[henry-maudslay|Henry Maudslay]], a pioneering machine-tool builder, and later became one of the principal suppliers of engines for the Royal Navy and early steamship lines. (020-172)

## About

The firm traces its origins to [[Henry Maudslay]] (1771–1831), who established his works in Lambeth, London. [[henry-maudslay|Maudslay]]'s innovations in precision engineering — including the slide-rest lathe and standardized [[screw-propeller|screw]] threads — provided the manufacturing foundation for the firm's engine work.

After [[henry-maudslay|Maudslay]]'s death, the firm continued under his sons and his partner Joshua Field. As Maudslay Sons and Field, they became a principal builder of [[marine-engine|marine steam engine]]s for the Royal Navy.

## Key Contributions

The firm made significant contributions to [[marine-engine|marine engine]] development:

- **Marine boiler design**: Their boilers for HMS Retribution and SS Great Western were noted by [[john-bourne|Bourne]] for their large proportions and generous heating surface. (020-172)

- **Disc valve for [[air-pump|air pump]]s**: The firm designed an efficient disc valve for [[air-pump|air pump]] applications, discussed by [[john-bourne|Bourne]] as an example of thoughtful valve engineering. (020-056)

- **Self-feeding furnace**: [[henry-maudslay|Maudslay]] devised [[grate|fire bars]] that formed a self-feeding furnace, allowing coal to descend automatically and reducing stoking labor. (013-090)

- **Paddle shaft centering**: The firm developed accurate methods for centering [[paddle-wheel|paddle]] shafts and boring out shaft centers. (020-056)

> "Messrs. Maudslay's boilers for the Retribution and the Great Western were of larger proportions than those of Boulton and Watt, the heating surface being increased in relation to the fire-grate area." (020-172)

## Legacy

[[henry-maudslay|Maudslay]]'s precision standards — first established for machine tools, then applied to [[marine-engine|marine engine]]s — raised the quality floor of British manufacturing. The slide-rest lathe alone is considered a foundational invention of the machine age.

The firm's [[marine-engine|marine engine]]s powered important steamships of the early Victorian era, and their boiler and valve designs influenced subsequent builders. [[john-bourne|Bourne]]'s detailed attention to [[henry-maudslay|Maudslay]]'s work confirms their standing among the top tier of nineteenth-century engine manufacturers.
