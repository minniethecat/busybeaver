# Rudolf Bodmer

**Rudolf Bodmer** (fl. 1841) was a Swiss engineer who contributed to the development of variable [[expansion-valve|expansion valve]] gear for [[steam|steam engine]]s. In 1841, he patented a design that employed two sliding blocks fitted over apertures in the ordinary [[slide-valve|slide valve]].

## Career and Contributions

Bodmer's 1841 expansion valve design, developed contemporaneously with [[meyer|Meyer]]'s 1842 mechanism, employed two slides or blocks fitted over apertures in the ordinary [[slide-valve|slide valve]]. These blocks were approximated or set apart by a right- and left-handed [[screw-propeller|screw]], allowing the point of [[cut-off|steam cut-off]] to be adjusted while the engine was in motion (016-109).

[[john-bourne|Bourne]] listed Bodmer among the principal contributors to expansion valve technology, alongside [[cabrey|Cabrey]], [[fenton|Fenton]], [[dodds|Dodds]], [[farcot|Farcot]], [[edwards|Edwards]], [[lavagrian|Lavagrian]], [[meyer|Meyer]], [[robert-hawthorn|Hawthorn]], [[gonzenbach|Gonzenbach]], and [[john-bourne|Bourne]] himself (069-056). This places Bodmer within a cohort of European engineers — including both British and Continental practitioners — who advanced the practical application of [[expansion-valve|expansive working]] during the 1840s.

## Legacy

Bodmer's double-block design represents one of the early Continental European contributions to [[steam|steam engine]] valve gear, predating [[meyer|Meyer]]'s similar 1842 design by one year. Both mechanisms belong to the class of expansion valves that achieved [[cut-off|variable cut-off]] through adjustable apertures rather than through variable eccentric throw.
