The **damper** is a pivoted or sliding metal plate positioned in the [[chimney]] uptake or [[flue]] passage, used to regulate the draft through a [[boiler]] [[furnace]]. By partially obstructing the gas passage, the damper reduces the flow of combustion air through the [[grate|fire bars]], thereby moderating the rate of combustion and, consequently, the production of [[steam]].

## Principle of operation

The damper controls the furnace by throttling the chimney draft. Closing the damper restricts the flow of hot gases, which in turn reduces the supply of fresh air drawn through the fire:

> [007-145] A plate of metal or a damper, as it is called, which slides up and down in the flue, and this damper is closed more or less when the pressure of the steam is too high, to check the draught and moderate the fire.

Conversely, opening the damper wide increases the draft, accelerating combustion when more steam is required:

> [007-153] If the pressure of the steam rises too high, the damper is closed, whereas, if the pressure falls, the damper is opened.

## Self-acting dampers

A significant improvement was the self-acting or automatic damper, which uses steam pressure itself to regulate the furnace draft:

> [058-109] There is a balanced damper, c, in the chimney. The weight, d, is attached to a lever, e, which is moved by a rod connected with a piston in a small cylinder.

The principle is simple: a small piston exposed to boiler steam pressure moves a lever connected to the damper plate. When steam pressure rises, the piston moves, closing the damper and checking the fire. When pressure falls, the weight opens the damper, increasing the draft:

> [058-112] When the pressure of steam in the boiler rises too high, the weight is raised, the lever rises, and the damper begins to close, and to check the draught, and therefore the intensity of the fire.

## Construction and placement

| Damper type | Location | Control method |
|------------|---------|---------------|
| Sliding damper | [[flue|Flue]] passage | Manual rod or chain |
| Balanced damper | [[chimney|Chimney]] base | Self-acting (pressure piston + lever) |
| Venetian damper | [[chimney|Chimney]] | Multi-leaf, balanced |
| Swinging damper | [[flue|Flue]] uptake | Weight and lever |

> [058-027] There is a damper, formed like a Venetian blind, 28 inches wide and 34 inches diameter.

> [031-126] Dampers should not be in the chimney in marine boilers, but in the uptake, as the heat in the chimney is apt to buckle them.

## Marine boiler dampers

In marine boilers the damper arrangement receives careful attention:

> [030-052] A damper to be fixed in that situation is exposed to great heat, and is therefore liable to be burnt out.

> [030-155] What is the arrangement of the furnace bridges and dampers of marine boilers?

Marine dampers were typically placed in the uptake (the passage between boiler and [[chimney|funnel]] base) rather than directly in the chimney, to protect them from the most intense heat.
