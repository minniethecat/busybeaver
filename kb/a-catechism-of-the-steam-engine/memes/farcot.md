# Farcot

Farcot was a French engineer who contributed to [[expansion-valve|expansion valve]] development in the 1840s. Together with [[Edwards]] and [[Lavagrian]], he developed a design employing a supplementary valve at the back of the ordinary [[slide-valve]].

## Career and Contributions

Farcot's expansion valve mechanism was part of a collaborative effort with [[edwards|Edwards]] and [[lavagrian|Lavagrian]]. [[john-bourne|Bourne]] describes their approach: "Farcot, [[edwards|Edwards]], and [[lavagrian|Lavagrian]] cut off the steam by the application of a supplementary valve at the back of the ordinary valve, which supplementary valve is moved by tappets fixed to the valve casing." (016-106)

The supplementary valve technique placed an additional sliding element behind the main [[slide-valve|slide valve]], actuated by tappets — fixed mechanical projections on the valve casing that triggered the supplementary valve at precise positions in the piston stroke. This arrangement allowed the point of [[cut-off|steam cut-off]] to be adjusted independently of the main valve motion, enabling variable expansion for improved [[steam]] efficiency.

Farcot is indexed under "Farcot, expansion valve by" (069-068) and listed among the notable expansion valve inventors: "expansion valves, by [[cabrey|Cabrey]], [[fenton|Fenton]], [[dodds|Dodds]], Farcot, [[edwards|Edwards]], [[lavagrian|Lavagrian]], [[rudolf-bodmer|Bodmer]], [[meyer|Meyer]], [[robert-hawthorn|Hawthorn]], [[gonzenbach|Gonzenbach]], and [[john-bourne|Bourne]]." (069-055)

## Legacy

Farcot's work, along with that of his collaborators, represents the French contribution to the international race to perfect [[cut-off|variable cut-off]] mechanisms in the 1840s. The tappet-actuated supplementary valve approach, while eventually superseded by link motion and [[george-corliss|Corliss]] gear, demonstrated the viability of mechanically-timed independent expansion control.
