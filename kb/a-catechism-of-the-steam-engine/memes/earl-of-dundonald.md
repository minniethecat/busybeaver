# Earl of Dundonald (1775–1860)

Thomas Cochrane, 10th Earl of Dundonald, was a British naval officer and inventor who held a patent for an early form of [[screw-propeller]]. His 1843 design was one of several competing [[screw-propeller|screw]] [[screw-propeller|propeller]] configurations evaluated during the transition from [[paddle-wheel|paddle wheels]] to [[screw-propeller|screw]] propulsion in steam navigation.

## Career and Contributions

The Earl of Dundonald's most notable contribution to [[steam|steam engine]]ering was his patented [[screw-propeller|screw]] [[screw-propeller|propeller]] form. [[john-bourne|Bourne]] describes it in the context of alternative [[screw-propeller|propeller]] geometries: "One of these forms of [[screw-propeller|screw]] is that patented by the Earl of Dundonald in 1843" (051-097). The design was one of many [[screw-propeller|screw]] [[screw-propeller|propeller]] configurations explored during a period of intense experimentation, as engineers sought to determine the optimal shape and pitch for marine propulsion.

The Dundonald [[screw-propeller|screw]] took the form of a cylinder rather than the more conventional frustum of a cone, representing a different approach to the geometry of the [[screw-propeller|propeller]]. This design was contemporary with other patent [[screw-propeller|propeller]]s, including the form patented by [[hodgson|Mr. Hodgson]] in the following year (051-098).

The Catechism's index records "Dundonald, Earl of, [[screw-propeller|screw]] by" (068-349), confirming his place in the documented history of [[screw-propeller|screw]] propulsion development.

## Legacy

Dundonald's [[screw-propeller|propeller]] design was one episode in the rapid evolution of [[screw-propeller|marine screw]] propulsion during the 1840s. While his cylindrical form did not become the dominant configuration, the period of experimentation to which he contributed helped establish the [[screw-propeller]] as the standard means of marine propulsion, eventually replacing the [[paddle-wheel]].
