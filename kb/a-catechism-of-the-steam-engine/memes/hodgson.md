# Hodgson

Mr. Hodgson was a nineteenth-century inventor who patented an innovative form of [[screw-propeller]] in 1844. His design featured a distinctive parabolic curvature of the [[screw-propeller|propeller]] arms, distinguishing it from other contemporary [[screw-propeller|propeller]] configurations.

## Career and Contributions

Hodgson's 1844 patent came one year after the [[earl-of-dundonald|Earl of Dundonald]]'s 1843 [[screw-propeller|screw]] [[screw-propeller|propeller]] patent, during a period of intense experimentation with [[screw-propeller|marine screw]] propulsion. [[john-bourne|Bourne]] describes Hodgson's design in direct comparison with [[earl-of-dundonald|Dundonald]]'s: "Another is the form of [[screw-propeller|screw]] already represented in fig. 48, and which was patented by Mr. Hodgson in 1844. Mr. Hodgson bends the arms of his [[screw-propeller|propeller]]s backward, not into the form of a triangle, but into the form of a parabola" (051-099). The parabolic form was intended "to the end that the impact of the [[screw-propeller|screw]] on the water might be more uniform" (051-100), reflecting the contemporary understanding that [[screw-propeller|propeller]] geometry significantly affected efficiency and smoothness of operation.

The Catechism's index records "Hodgson's [[screw-propeller|screw]]" (069-182) as a distinct entry, confirming its recognition as a notable variant in the history of [[screw-propeller|screw]] [[screw-propeller|propeller]] development. Hodgson's design was one of many competing geometries evaluated as the maritime industry transitioned from [[paddle-wheel]] propulsion to [[screw-propeller|screw]] [[screw-propeller|propeller]]s.

## Legacy

Hodgson's parabolic [[screw-propeller|screw]] design represents one of the many incremental experiments in [[screw-propeller|propeller]] geometry during the 1840s. While the specific commercial success of his design is not detailed in the Catechism, his inclusion alongside other [[screw-propeller|propeller]] patent holders demonstrates the breadth of inventive activity that ultimately led to the widespread adoption of [[screw-propeller|screw]] propulsion in steam navigation.
