# Daniel Gooch

**Daniel Gooch** (1816–1889) was an English railway [[locomotive|locomotive engine]]er. As the first locomotive superintendent of the Great Western Railway (GWR), he established the Swindon works as a leading centre of locomotive design and construction. His express engine designs and the Gooch valve gear were widely adopted.

## Career and Contributions

Gooch joined the Great Western Railway under [[Isambard Kingdom Brunel]] and was responsible for designing the railway's early locomotive fleet. His express engine, designed for the broad-gauge GWR, was among the fastest and most efficient locomotives of its day. The engine used a large boiler with a domed [[fire-box|firebox]] and was adapted for burning coke rather than coal to comply with early smoke-abatement regulations (011-059).

Gooch's designs set standards for express passenger service, demonstrating that locomotives could achieve both high speed and fuel economy on regular scheduled runs.

## Key Inventions

### Gooch Link Motion

The Gooch link motion is a valve gear design in which the link is suspended from a fixed point and the die block slides within a curved link. Unlike [[george-stephenson|Stephenson]]'s link motion, which raises and lowers the link itself, Gooch's design keeps the link stationary and moves the radius rod. This arrangement provides accurate valve events for both forward and reverse running, making it well-suited for express passenger locomotives where precise steam distribution at high speed is essential.

## Legacy

The GWR's reputation for speed and reliability was built on Gooch's locomotives. His broad-gauge express engines remained in service for decades, and the Gooch link motion was adopted by other railways for its simplicity and precision. The Swindon works he founded became one of the world's foremost locomotive manufacturing centres.
