The **hot well** is a small cistern or tank that receives the warm water discharged from the [[air-pump]] of a condensing [[steam]] engine. The water, having been heated by the condensation of [[eduction|exhaust]] steam, is fed from the hot well back into the [[boiler]] by the [[feed-pump|feed pump]]. By returning water already close to boiling point, the hot well significantly improves the thermal efficiency of the engine cycle.

## Position in the engine cycle

The hot well sits between the [[condenser]] and the boiler in the water circuit of a [[condensation|condensing engine]]. The flow path is: [[eduction|exhaust]] steam → [[condenser]] (collapsed by cold injection water) → [[air-pump]] (extracts the water and any air) → hot well (receives and holds the warm water) → [[feed-pump|feed pump]] → boiler:

> [008-155] A small cistern S, called the hot well, through a valve opening outwards and called the delivery valve. A pump M, called the hot water pump, lifts hot water out of the hot well to feed the boiler.

> [009-015] The hot water pump M, pumps a small quantity of this hot water into the boiler, to compensate for the water carried off in the form of steam.

## Optimum temperature

[[james-watt|James Watt]] determined through experiment that the most efficient temperature for the hot well was approximately 100°F (38°C):

> [027-021] Mr. Watt found that the most beneficial temperature of the hot well of his engines was 100 degrees. If, therefore, the temperature of the steam be 212°, and the latent heat 1,000°, then 1,212° may be taken to represent the heat contained in the steam, or 1,112° if we deduct the temperature of the hot well.

This temperature represents a balance: warm enough to save fuel in reheating the feed water, but cool enough to maintain an adequate vacuum in the [[condensation|condenser]] (since vacuum quality depends on the condensation temperature being low).

## Construction and placement

The hot well is typically a cast-iron tank located below the level of the [[air-pump|air pump]] delivery valve:

> [040-036] There are a great number of Improved constructions of the delivery or discharge valves of air pumps. In some cases the valve is situated at the top of the air pump barrel; in other cases it is placed at the side of the hot well.

The delivery valve at the hot well inlet opens outward, allowing water to pass from the [[air-pump|air pump]] into the hot well but preventing backflow:

> [053-077] The delivery valve at the top of the air pump barrel for carrying the water to the hot well.
