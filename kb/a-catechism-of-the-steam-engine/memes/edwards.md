# Edwards

Edwards was a British engineer who contributed to [[expansion-valve|expansion valve]] technology in the 1840s, working alongside [[Farcot]] and [[Lavagrian]] on a design that employed a supplementary valve at the back of the ordinary [[slide-valve|slide valve]].

## Career and Contributions

Edwards, together with [[farcot|Farcot]] and [[lavagrian|Lavagrian]], developed an expansion valve mechanism that "cut off the steam by the application of a supplementary valve at the back of the ordinary valve, which supplementary valve is moved by tappets fixed to the valve casing." (016-106)

This design differed from the approaches taken by other contemporary inventors. While [[Dodds]] used a spiral feather on the [[crank-shaft|crank axle]], and [[Bodmer]] and [[Meyer]] employed two sliding blocks with a right-and-left-handed [[screw-propeller|screw]], the Edwards–[[farcot|Farcot]]–[[lavagrian|Lavagrian]] design used an auxiliary valve activated by mechanical tappets — a relatively simple and robust arrangement for achieving [[cut-off|variable cut-off]].

The expansion valve is listed in the *Catechism* index among the innovators of the period: "expansion valves, by [[cabrey|Cabrey]], [[fenton|Fenton]], [[dodds|Dodds]], [[farcot|Farcot]], Edwards, [[lavagrian|Lavagrian]], [[rudolf-bodmer|Bodmer]], [[meyer|Meyer]], [[robert-hawthorn|Hawthorn]], [[gonzenbach|Gonzenbach]], and [[john-bourne|Bourne]]." (069-055) Edwards also receives an individual index entry: "Edwards, expansion valve by" (069-010).

## Legacy

The Edwards–[[farcot|Farcot]]–[[lavagrian|Lavagrian]] supplementary valve design contributed to the rapid evolution of expansion valve technology during the 1840s. The use of tappets — mechanical projections that actuate the valve at precise points in the stroke — prefigured the positive valve actuation methods used in later high-speed [[steam|steam engine]]s.
