[057-002] 651. _Q._--What is the diameter of the screw shaft?

[057-003] _A._--The screw shaft is 7-1/2 inches diameter, but the bearings on each

[057-004] side of the disc are 8-1/2 inches diameter, and 16 inches long. Between the

[057-005] side of the disc and the side of the contiguous bearings there is a short

[057-006] neck extending 4-3/4 inches in the length of the shaft, and hollowed out

[057-007] somewhat to permit the passage of the piston rod; for one piston rod passes

[057-008] immediately above the shaft on the one side of the discs, and the other

[057-009] piston rod passes immediately below the shaft on the other side of the

[057-010] discs. A short piece of one piston rod is shown in fig. 54.

[057-013] 652. _Q._--How is the thrust of the screw shaft received?

[057-014] _A._--The thrust of the screw shaft is received upon 7 collars, each 1 inch

[057-015] thick, and with 1 inch of projection above the shaft. The plummer block for

[057-016] receiving the thrust of the shaft is shown in fig. 55, and the coupling to

[057-017] enable the screw propeller to be disconnected from the engine, so that it

[057-018] may revolve freely when the vessel is under sail, is shown in fig. 56. When

[057-019] it is required to disengage the propeller from the engine, the pins passing

[057-020] through the opposite eyes shown fig. 56, are withdrawn by means of screws

[057-021] provided for that purpose, and the propeller and the engine are thenceforth

[057-022] independent of one another.

[057-024] 653. _Q._--Will you describe the arrangement of the valve gearing?

[057-025] _A._--The end of the screw shaft, after emerging from the bearing beside

[057-026] the disc, is reduced to a diameter of 4 inches, and is prolonged for 4-1/2

[057-027] inches to give attachment to the cam or curved plate which gives motion to

[057-028] the expansion valve. This plate is 3-1/2 inches thick, and a stud 3-1/2

[057-029] inches diameter is fixed in the plate at a distance of 5 inches from the

[057-030] centre of the shaft. To this stud an arm is attached which extends to a

[057-031] distance of 2 inches from the centre of the shaft in the opposite

[057-032] direction, and the end of this arm carries a pin of 2-1/2 inches diameter.

[057-033] From the pin most remote from the centre of the shaft, a rod 2-1/2 inches

[057-034] broad and 1 inch thick extends to the upper end of the link of the link

[057-035] motion; and from the pin least remote from the centre of the shaft, a

[057-036] similar rod extends to the lower end of the link of the link motion. This

[057-037] link, which is represented in fig. 57, is 2-1/4 inches broad, 1 inch thick,

[057-038] and is capable of being raised or lowered 25 inches in all. In the open

[057-039] part of the link is a brass block, which, by raising or lowering the link,

[057-040] takes either the position in which it is represented at the centre of the

[057-041] link, or a position at either end of it. Through the hole in the brass

[057-042] block a pin passes to attach the brass to the end of a lever fixed on the

[057-043] valve shaft; so that whatever motion is imparted to the brass block is

[057-044] communicated to the valve through the medium of this lever. If the brass

[057-045] block be set in the middle of the link, no motion is communicated to it,

[057-046] and the valve being consequently kept stationary and covering both ports,

[057-047] the engine stops. If the link be lowered until the brass block comes to the

[057-048] upper end of the link, the valve receives the motion of the eccentric for

[057-049] going ahead, and the engine moves ahead; whereas if the link be raised

[057-050] until the brass block comes to the lower end of the link, the valve

[057-051] receives the motion of the backing eccentric, and the engine moves astern.

[057-052] Instead of eccentrics, however, pins at the end of the shaft are employed

[057-053] in this engine, the arrangement partaking of the nature of a double crank;

[057-054] but the backing pin has less throw than the going ahead pin, whereby the

[057-055] efficient length of the link for going ahead is increased; and the

[057-056] operation of backing, which does not require to be performed at the highest

[057-057] rate of speed, is sufficiently accommodated by about half the throw being

[057-058] given to the valve that is given in going ahead. A valve shaft extends

[057-059] across the end of the cylinder with two levers standing up, which engage

[057-060] horizontal side rods extending from a small cross head on the end of the

[057-061] valve rod. A lever extends downwards from the end of the valve shaft, which

[057-062] is connected by a pin to the brass block within the link; and the link is

[057-063] moved up or down by the starting handle, which, by means of a spring bolt

[057-064] shooting into a quadrant, holds the starting handle at any position in

[057-065] which it may be set.

[057-066] 654. _Q._--What is the diameter and pitch of the screw propeller?

[057-067] _A._--The diameter is 7 feet and the pitch 14 feet. The propeller is Holm's

[057-068] conchoidal propeller. Its diameter is smaller than is advisable, being

[057-069] limited by the draught of water of the vessel; and the vessel was required

[057-070] to have a small draught of water to go over a bar. This engine makes, under

[057-071] favorable circumstances, 100 strokes per minute. The speed of piston with

[057-072] this number of strokes is 700 feet per minute, and the engine works

[057-073] steadily at this speed, the shock and tremor arising from the arrested

[057-074] momentum of the moving parts being taken away by the counterbalance applied

[057-075] at the discs.

[057-076] LOCOMOTIVE ENGINE.

[057-077] 655. _Q._--Will you describe the principal features of a modern locomotive

[057-078] engine?

[057-079] _A._--I will take for this purpose the locomotive Snake, constructed by

[057-080] John V. Gooch for the London and South Western Railway, as an example of a

[057-081] modern locomotive of good construction, adapted for the narrow gauge. The

[057-082] length of the wheel base of this engine is 12 feet 8-1/2 inches. There are

[057-083] two cylinders, each 14-1/4 inches diameter and 21 inches stroke. The total

[057-084] weight of the engine is 19 tons; and this weight is so distributed on the

[057-085] wheels as to throw 8 tons on the leading wheels, 6 tons on the driving

[057-086] wheels, and 5 tons on the hind wheels. The engine is made with outside

[057-087] cylinders, and the cylinders are raised somewhat out of the horizontal line

[057-088] to enable them better to clear the leading wheels.

[057-089] 656. _Q._--What are the dimensions of the boiler?

[057-090] _A._--The interior of the fire box is 3 feet 7-1/4 inches wide by 3 feet

[057-091] 5-1/2 inches long, measuring in the direction of the rails. The area of the

[057-092] fire grate is consequently 12.4 square feet. The bars are somewhat lower on

[057-093] the side next the fire door than at the side next the tubes, and the mean

[057-094] height of the crown of the fire box above the bars is 3 feet 10 inches. The

[057-095] top edge of the fire door is about 7 inches lower than the crown of the

[057-096] fire box. The fire box is divided transversely by a corrugated feather or

[057-097] bridge of plate iron, containing water, about 3-1/2 inches wide, and of

[057-098] about one-third of the height of the fire box in the centre of the feather,

[057-099] and about two-thirds the height of the fire box at the sides where it joins

[057-100] the sides of the fire box. The internal shell of the fire box tapers

[057-101] somewhat upwards to facilitate the disengagement of the steam. It is about

[057-102] 2 inches narrower and shorter at the top than at the bottom; the water

[057-103] space between the external and internal shell of the fire box being 2

[057-104] inches at the bottom and 3 inches at the top.

[057-105] 657. _Q._--Of what material is the fire box composed?

[057-106] _A._--The external shell of the fire box is formed of iron plates 3/8ths of

[057-107] an inch thick, and the internal shell is formed of copper plates 1/4 inch

[057-108] thick, but the tube plate is 3/4 inch thick. The fire grate is rectangular,

[057-109] and the internal and external shells are tied together by iron stay bolts

[057-110] 3/4 inch diameter, and pitched about 4 inches apart. The roof of the fire

[057-111] box is stiffened by six strong bars extending from side to side of the fire

[057-112] box like beams, and the top of the fire box is secured to these bars, so

[057-113] that it cannot be forced down without breaking or bending them.

[057-114] 658. _Q._--What are the dimensions of the barrel of the boiler?

[057-115] _A._--The barrel of the boiler is 3 feet 7-1/2 inches in diameter, and 10

[057-116] feet long. It is formed of iron plates 3/8ths of an inch thick, riveted

[057-117] together. It is furnished with 181 brass tubes 1-7/8 inch diameter and 10

[057-118] feet long, secured at the ends by ferules. The tube plate at the smoke box

[057-119] end is 5/8ths of an inch thick, and the tube plates above the tubes are

[057-120] tied together by eight iron rods 7/8ths of an inch thick, extending from

[057-121] end to end of the boiler. The metal of the tubes is somewhat thicker at the

[057-122] end next the fire, being 13 wire gauge at fire box end, and 14 wire gauge

[057-123] at smoke box end. The rivets of the boiler are 3/4 inch diameter and 1-1/2

[057-124] inch pitch. The plating of the ash pan is 5/16ths of an inch thick, and the

[057-125] plating of the smoke box is 3/16ths of an inch thick.

[057-126] 659. _Q._--Will you describe the structure of the framework on which the

[057-127] boiler and its attachments rest, and in which the wheels are set?

[057-128] _A._--The framework or framing consists of a rectangular structure of plate

[057-129] iron circumscribing the boiler, with projecting lugs or arms for the

[057-130] reception of the axles of the wheels. In this engine the sides of the

[057-131] rectangle are double, or, as far as regards the sides, there are virtually

[057-132] two framings, one for the reception of the driving axles, and the other for

[057-133] the reception of the axles not connected with the engine. The whole of the

[057-134] parts of the outer and inner framings are connected together by knees at

[057-135] the corners, and the double sides are elsewhere connected by intervening

[057-136] brackets and stays, so as to constitute the whole into one rigid structure.

[057-137] The whole of the plating of the inside frame is 3/4 inch thick and 9 inches

[057-138] deep. The plating of the outside frame is of the same thickness and depth

[057-139] at the fore part, until it reaches abaft the position of the cylinders and

[057-140] guides, where it reduces to 1/2 inch thick. The axle guard of the leading

[057-141] wheels is formed of 3/4 plate bolted to the frame with angle iron guides.

[057-142] The axle guards of the trailing wheels are formed of two 1/2 inch plates,

[057-143] with cast iron blocks between them to serve as guides. The ends of the

[057-144] rectangular frame are formed of plates 3/4 thick, and at the front end

[057-145] there is a buffer beam of oak 4-1/2 inches thick and 15 inches deep. The

[057-146] draw bolt is 2 inches diameter. There are two strong stays on each side,

[057-147] joining the barrel of the boiler to the inside framing, and one angle iron

[057-148] on each side joining the bottom of the smoke box to the inside framing.

[057-149] 660. _Q._--Of what construction are the wheels?

[057-150] _A._--The wheels and axles are of wrought iron, and the tires of the wheels

[057-151] are of steel. The driving wheels are 6 feet 6-1/2 inches in diameter, and

[057-152] the diameter of crank pin is 3-1/2 inches. The diameter of the smaller

[057-153] wheels is 48-1/2 inches. The axle boxes are of cast iron with bushes of

[057-154] Fenton's metal, and the leading axle has four bearings. The springs are

[057-155] formed of steel plates, 3 feet long, 4 inches broad, and 1\2 inch thick.

[057-156] The axle of the driving wheel has two eccentrics, forged solid upon it, for

[057-157] working the pumps.
