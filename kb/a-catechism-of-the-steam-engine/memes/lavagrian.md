# Lavagrian

Lavagrian was an engineer who, together with [[Farcot]] and [[Edwards]], developed an [[expansion-valve|expansion valve]] design in the 1840s employing a supplementary valve at the back of the ordinary [[slide-valve]].

## Career and Contributions

Lavagrian's contribution to [[steam|steam engine]] technology was the co-development of a supplementary valve mechanism for [[cut-off|variable cut-off]]. [[john-bourne|Bourne]] writes: "[[farcot|Farcot]], [[edwards|Edwards]], and Lavagrian cut off the steam by the application of a supplementary valve at the back of the ordinary valve, which supplementary valve is moved by tappets fixed to the valve casing." (016-106)

The supplementary valve design placed an additional sliding element on the back of the ordinary [[slide-valve|slide valve]]. Tappets — fixed projections on the valve casing — actuated the supplementary valve at the desired point in the piston stroke, cutting off steam admission independently of the main valve motion. This was one of several competing approaches to achieving variable expansion developed during the 1840s, alongside the designs of [[Dodds]], [[Bodmer]], [[Meyer]], [[Hawthorn]], and [[Gonzenbach]].

Lavagrian appears in the *Catechism* index in two entries: within the group listing of expansion valve inventors (069-055, 069-060) and under the individual entry "Lavagrian, expansion valve by" (069-226).

## Legacy

While relatively little biographical information about Lavagrian survives, the expansion valve design he co-developed with [[farcot|Farcot]] and [[edwards|Edwards]] represents one thread in the dense fabric of 1840s [[cut-off|variable cut-off]] innovation. The collective work of these inventors established the technical foundation upon which later, more refined [[expansion-valve|expansion gear]] was built.
