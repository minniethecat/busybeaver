# Feed Pipe

The **feed pipe** is the conduit that carries feed water from the [[Feed-Pump|feed pump]] into the [[Boiler|boiler]]. It runs between the pump discharge and the boiler inlet, and is fitted with protective devices to guard against excessive pressure. (033-136) (040-042)

## Construction and Material

Feed pipes were typically made of copper for durability and resistance to corrosion from hot water. (040-152) The diameter of the feed pipe depended on the size of the engine and its [[feed-pump|feed pump]]; for example, the feed pipe of a [[marine-engine|marine engine]] with 4-inch diameter pump plungers and 3-1/4 inches stroke was specified as 2 inches in diameter. (058-036)

In [[Locomotive|locomotive]] engines the feed pipe enters the boiler near the bottom and approximately at the middle of the boiler's length, delivering water into the cooler region away from the steam space above. (046-002) (046-003) The flow into the boiler is controlled by a cock on the feed pipe, allowing the engine-man to regulate the water supply independently. (059-061)

## Safety Arrangements

The feed pipe is subject to the full boiler pressure. If the feed cock were shut while the [[Feed-Pump|feed pump]] continued to operate, the pressure in the feed pipe would rise until the pipe burst. (033-135) To prevent this, a safety or escape valve is fitted on the feed pipe between the engine and the boiler. (033-136) (068-177)

> "Should the feed cock be shut so as to prevent any feed water from passing through it, the water will raise the topmost valve, which is loaded to a pressure considerably above the pressure of the steam, and escape into the hot well." (040-042) (040-045)

This arrangement — a loaded valve on the feed pipe with an overflow — provides a neater and less expensive safety solution than a separate external [[safety-valve|safety valve]]. (040-047) The escape valve on the feed pipe is listed by [[john-bourne|Bourne]] as a standard fitting for marine boilers. (069-026)

## Role in Boiler Safety

The reliable operation of the feed pipe and its [[safety-valve|safety valve]]s is critical to boiler integrity. A blocked feed pipe prevents water from reaching the boiler, leading to a falling water level and the risk of uncovering the boiler crown — one of the principal causes of boiler explosion in early steam practice. Conversely, a feed pipe without adequate pressure relief can itself become a rupture hazard under the high pressures generated by positive-displacement [[feed-pump|feed pump]]s.
