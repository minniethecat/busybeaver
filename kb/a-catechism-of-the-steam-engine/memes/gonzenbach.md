# Gonzenbach

Gonzenbach was an inventor who contributed to the development of [[expansion-valve|expansion valve]] mechanisms for [[steam|steam engine]]s in the nineteenth century. His patent for a supplementary [[slide-valve|slide valve]] arrangement was one of many competing designs during the period of intensive experimentation with [[expansion-valve|expansive working]].

## Career and Contributions

Gonzenbach's principal contribution was an expansion valve patented in the same year as a competing design by another inventor. [[john-bourne|Bourne]] describes the context: a particular expansion apparatus uses a frame "lying on the ordinary cylinder face upon the outside of the valve, and working up against the steam side of the valve at each end so as to cut off the steam. In the same year Gonzenbach patented an arrangement which consists of an additional [[slide-valve|slide valve]] and valve casing placed on the back of the ordinary [[slide-valve|slide valve]] casing, and through this supplementary valve the steam must first pass (016-115). The supplementary valve was worked by a double-ended lever, slotted at one end for the reception of a pin on the valve link, the position of which in the slot determined the point of cut-off.

Gonzenbach is listed in the Catechism's index among notable expansion valve designers, alongside [[cabrey|Cabrey]], [[fenton|Fenton]], [[dodds|Dodds]], [[farcot|Farcot]], [[edwards|Edwards]], [[lavagrian|Lavagrian]], [[rudolf-bodmer|Bodmer]], [[meyer|Meyer]], [[robert-hawthorn|Hawthorn]], and [[john-bourne|Bourne]] (069-056). This places him within the active community of engineers and inventors working to improve the efficiency of the [[steam engine]] through better control of steam admission and cut-off.

## Legacy

While the specific details of Gonzenbach's commercial success are not documented in the Catechism, his inclusion in the index of expansion valve designers indicates that his work was recognized among the engineering community of his time. His supplementary [[slide-valve|slide valve]] design represents one of the many incremental improvements that collectively advanced [[steam|steam engine]] efficiency during the nineteenth century.
