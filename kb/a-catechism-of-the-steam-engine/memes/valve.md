# Valve

A **valve** in a [[steam|steam engine]] is any device that regulates the flow of steam — admitting it to the [[Cylinder]], cutting off its supply at the appropriate point, and opening the [[eduction|exhaust]] path to the [[Condensation|condenser]] or atmosphere. The [[Piston]] is driven by the pressure of steam which is admitted and withdrawn by the action of the valves. (008-117) (008-059)

## Function and Importance

The valves control every phase of the engine cycle. As the piston commences its motion, suitable valves admit steam on one side while [[eduction|exhaust]]ing it from the other. (008-060) The timing, speed, and extent of valve opening directly determine the engine's efficiency, power, and smoothness of operation.

The steam which impels the piston is admitted and withdrawn by the action of the valves, and the perfection of the engine's operation depends substantially upon the punctuality and precision with which the valves perform their functions. (008-117) Poor valve timing causes waste of steam, irregular motion, and loss of power.

> "The piston is pushed by the steam alternately up and down, being admitted alternately above and below the piston by suitable valves provided for that purpose." (008-059) (008-060)

## Valve Types

### Slide Valve

The **[[slide-valve|slide valve]]** is the most common type, consisting of a flat plate that slides over ports in the cylinder face, alternately opening them to steam and [[eduction|exhaust]]. The [[eccentric|Eccentric]] which works the [[slide-valve|slide valve]] is placed upon the [[crank-shaft|crankshaft]]. (010-016) If the connection between the eccentric and the [[slide-valve|slide valve]] is broken by lifting the end of the eccentric rod, the engine can be stopped instantly. (010-025)

There are many varieties of the [[slide-valve|slide valve]], which may include an additional [[slide-valve|slide valve]] and valve casing placed on the back of the ordinary [[slide-valve|slide valve]] casing. (015-017) (016-116) The purpose of these variations is to control the point of [[cut-off|steam cut-off]] with greater precision.

> "Of the slide valve there are many varieties; but they all operate on the principle of admitting steam to the cylinder during a portion of the stroke and then cutting it off, the remaining portion of the stroke being performed by the expansion of the steam." (015-017)

### Throttle Valve

The **throttle valve** in the steam pipe regulates the total quantity of steam admitted to the engine. By partially closing the throttle valve, the supply of steam is reduced and the engine's speed is thereby controlled. (002-105) The friction of the throttle valve which shuts off the steam contributes to the total resistance of the engine. (004-086)

The throttle valve is controlled by the [[governor|Governor]] through suitable rods and linkages: when the engine speed increases, the governor communicates motion to the throttle valve and it will be partly closed. (008-100) (008-105)

### Safety Valve

The **[[safety-valve|safety valve]]** is a protective device that opens automatically when steam pressure exceeds a safe limit. The pressure shown by the [[safety-valve|safety valve]] on the boiler indicates the working pressure of the engine. (001-224) [[steam|Steam]] which escapes through the [[safety-valve|safety valve]] prevents the boiler from bursting under excessive pressure. (007-125)

The [[safety-valve|safety valve]] is set in a chest standing on the boiler, weighted to a pressure slightly above the normal working pressure so that it remains closed during normal operation but opens promptly if pressure rises dangerously. (007-126)

### Atmospheric Valve

The **atmospheric valve** is a small inward-opening valve fitted to the boiler. Its purpose is to admit air when the internal pressure falls below atmospheric, preventing the boiler from collapsing under external pressure when steam is condensed internally. The valve will open and admit air when the internal pressure falls below the pressure of the atmosphere. (007-117) (007-120)

## Valve Gear and Timing

| Component | Function |
|-----------|---------|
| [[eccentric|Eccentric]] | Converts rotary crank motion to reciprocating valve motion (010-016) |
| [[eccentric|Eccentric]] rod | Connects eccentric to valve spindle |
| Link motion | Allows reversal of valve timing (for reversing engines) |
| Expansion gear | Adjusts cut-off point independently of main valve |

The valves are connected together so that they operate in synchrony with the piston. The four valves — upper steam, upper [[eduction|exhaust]], lower steam, and lower [[eduction|exhaust]] — are connected by suitable levers so that when one set opens the other closes. (008-133) (008-135)
