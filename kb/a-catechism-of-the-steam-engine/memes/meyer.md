# Meyer

**Meyer** (fl. 1842) was an engineer who, in 1842, patented an [[expansion-valve|expansion valve]] design employing two slides or blocks fitted over apertures in the ordinary [[slide-valve|slide valve]].

## Career and Contributions

Meyer's 1842 design, developed one year after [[rudolf-bodmer|Rudolf Bodmer]]'s closely similar 1841 mechanism, employed two slides or blocks fitted over apertures in the ordinary [[slide-valve|slide valve]]. These blocks were approximated or set apart by a right- and left-handed [[screw-propeller|screw]], enabling the point of steam [[cut-off|cut-off]] to be adjusted while the engine was in motion (016-109).

[[john-bourne|Bourne]] catalogued Meyer among the numerous inventors of expansion valve apparatus, listing him alongside [[rudolf-bodmer|Bodmer]], [[cabrey|Cabrey]], [[fenton|Fenton]], [[dodds|Dodds]], [[farcot|Farcot]], [[edwards|Edwards]], [[lavagrian|Lavagrian]], [[robert-hawthorn|Hawthorn]], [[gonzenbach|Gonzenbach]], and [[john-bourne|Bourne]] himself in the index of expansion valve contributors (069-056). Meyer's entry in the index of the volume records his expansion valve by name (069-319).

## Legacy

Meyer's double-slide mechanism exemplifies the burst of inventive activity around variable expansion during the early 1840s. Together with [[rudolf-bodmer|Bodmer]]'s nearly identical 1841 design, Meyer's valve represents a distinct class of [[expansion-valve|expansion gear]] in which cut-off was regulated by adjustable apertures rather than by variable eccentric motion — a fundamentally different approach from the mechanisms of [[george-stephenson|Stephenson]] and [[cabrey|Cabrey]].
