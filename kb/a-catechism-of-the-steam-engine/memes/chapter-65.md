[065-001] 721. _Q._--How do you ascertain the accuracy of the parallel motion?

[065-002] _A._--The parallel motion when put in its place should be tested by raising

[065-003] and lowering the piston by means of the crane. First, set the beams level,

[065-004] and shift in or out the motion shaft plummer blocks or bearings, until the

[065-005] piston rod is upright. Then move the piston to the two extremes of its

[065-006] motion. If at both ends the cross head is thrown too much out, the stud in

[065-007] the beam to which the motion side rod is attached is too far out, and must

[065-008] be shifted nearer to the main centre; if at the extremities the cross head

[065-009] is thrown too far in, the stud in the beam is not out far enough. If the

[065-010] cross head be thrown in at the one end, and out equally at the other, the

[065-011] fault is in the motion side rod, which must be lengthened or shortened to

[065-012] remedy the defect.

[065-013] 722. _Q._--Will you describe the method pursued in erecting oscillating

[065-014] engines?

[065-015] _A._--The columns here are of wrought iron, and in the case of small

[065-016] engines there is a template made of wood and sheet iron, in which the holes

[065-017] are set in the proper positions, by which the upper and lower frames are

[065-018] adjusted; but in the case of large engines, the holes are set off by means

[065-019] of trammels. The holes for the reception of the columns are cast in the

[065-020] frames, and are recessed out internally: the bosses encircling the holes

[065-021] are made quite level across, and made very true with a face plate, and the

[065-022] pillars which have been turned to a gauge are then inserted. The top frame

[065-023] is next put on, and must bear upon the collars of the columns so evenly,

[065-024] that one of the columns will not be bound by it harder than another. If

[065-025] this point be not attained, the surfaces must be further scraped, until a

[065-026] perfect fit is established. The whole of the bearings in the best

[065-027] oscillating engines are fitted by means of scraping, and on no other mode

[065-028] of fitting can the same reliance be placed for exactitude.

[065-029] 723. _Q._--How do you set out the trunnions of oscillating engines, so that

[065-030] they shall be at right angles with the interior of the cylinder?

[065-031] _A._--Having bored the cylinder, faced the flange, and bored out the hole

[065-032] through which the boring bar passes, put a piece of wood across the mouth

[065-033] of the cylinder, and jam it in, and put a similar piece in the hole through

[065-034] the bottom of the cylinder. Mark the centre of the cylinder upon each of

[065-035] these pieces, and put into the bore of each trunnion an iron plate, with a

[065-036] small indentation in the middle to receive the centre of a lathe, and

[065-037] adjusting screws to bring the centre into any required position. The

[065-038] cylinder must then be set in a lathe, and hung by the centres of the

[065-039] trunnions, and a straight edge must be put across the cylinder mouth and

[065-040] levelled, so as to pass through the line in which the centre of the

[065-041] cylinder lies. Another similar straight edge, and similarly levelled, must

[065-042] be similarly placed across the cylinder bottom, so as to pass through the

[065-043] central line of the cylinder; and the cylinder is then to be turned round

[065-044] in the trunnion centres-the straight edges remaining stationary, which will

[065-045] at once show whether the trunnions are in the same horizontal plane as the

[065-046] centre of the cylinder, and if not, the screws of the plates in the

[065-047] trunnions must be adjusted until the central point of the cylinder just

[065-048] comes to the straight edge, whichever end of the cylinder is presented. To

[065-049] ascertain whether the trunnions stand in a transverse plane, parallel to

[065-050] the cylinder flange, it is only necessary to measure down from the flange

[065-051] to each trunnion centre; and if both these conditions are satisfied, the

[065-052] position of the centres may be supposed to be right. The trunnion bearings

[065-053] are then turned, and are fitted into blocks of wood, in which they run

[065-054] while the packing space is being turned out. Where many oscillating engines

[065-055] are made, a lathe with four centres is used, which makes the use of

[065-056] straight edges in setting out the trunnions superfluous.

[065-057] 724. _Q._--Will you explain how the slide valve of a marine engine is set?

[065-058] _A._--Place the crank in the position corresponding to the end of the

[065-059] stroke, which can easily be done in the shop with a level, or plumb line;

[065-060] but in a steam vessel another method becomes necessary. Draw the transverse

[065-061] centre line, answering to the centre line of the crank shaft, on the sole

[065-062] plate of the engine, or on the cylinder mouth if the engine be of the

[065-063] direct action kind; describe a circle of the diameter of the crank pin upon

[065-064] the large eye of the crank, and mark off on either side of the transverse

[065-065] centre line a distance equal to the semi-diameter of the crank pin. From

[065-066] the point thus found, stretch a line to the edge of the circle described on

[065-067] the large eye of the crank, and bring round the crank shaft till the crank

[065-068] pin touches the stretched line; the crank may thus be set at either end of

[065-069] its stroke. When the crank is thus placed at the end of the stroke, the

[065-070] valve must be adjusted so as to have the amount of lead, or opening on the

[065-071] steam side, which it is intended to give at the beginning of the stroke;

[065-072] the eccentric must then be turned round upon the shaft until the notch in

[065-073] the eccentric rod comes opposite the pin on the valve lever, and falls into

[065-074] gear: mark upon the shaft the situation of the eccentric, and put on the

[065-075] catches in the usual way. The same process must be repeated for going

[065-076] astern, shifting round the eccentric to the opposite side of the shaft,

[065-077] until the rod again falls into gear. In setting valves, regard must of

[065-078] course be had to the kind of engine, the arrangements of the levers, and

[065-079] the kind of valve employed; and in any general instructions it is

[065-080] impossible to specify every modification in the procedure that

[065-081] circumstances may render advisable.

[065-082] 725. _Q._--Is a similar method of setting the valve adopted when the link

[065-083] motion is employed.

[065-084] _A._--Each end of the link of the link motion has the kind of motion

[065-085] communicated to it that is due to the action of the particular eccentric

[065-086] with which that end is in connection. In that form of the link motion in

[065-087] which the link itself is moved up or down, there is a different amount of

[065-088] lead for each different position of the link, since to raise or lower the

[065-089] link is tantamount to turning the eccentric round on the shaft. In that

[065-090] form of the link motion in which the link itself is not raised or lowered,

[065-091] but is susceptible of a motion round a centre in the manner of a double

[065-092] ended lever, the lead continues uniform. In both forms of the link motion,

[065-093] as the stroke of the valve may be varied to any required extent while the

[065-094] lap is a constant quantity, the proportion of the lap relatively to the

[065-095] stroke of the valve may also be varied to any required extent, and the

[065-096] amount of the lap relatively with the stroke of the valve determines the

[065-097] amount of the expansion. In setting the valve when fitted with the link

[065-098] motion, the mode of procedure is much the same as when it is moved by a

[065-099] simple eccentric. The first thing is to determine if the eccentric rods are

[065-100] of the proper length, and this is done by setting the valve at half stroke

[065-101] and turning round the eccentric, marking each extremity of the travel of

[065-102] the end of the rod. The valve attachment should be midway between these

[065-103] extremes; and if it is not so, it must be made so by lengthening or

[065-104] shortening the rod. The forward and backward eccentric rods are to be

[065-105] adjusted in this way, and this being done, the engine is to be put to the

[065-106] end of the stroke, and the eccentric is to be turned round until the amount

[065-107] of lead has been given that is desired. The valve must be tried by turning

[065-108] the engine round to see that it is right at both centres, for going ahead

[065-109] and also for going astern. In some examples of the link motion, one of the

[065-110] eccentric rods is made a little longer than the other, and the position of

[065-111] the point of suspension or point of support powerfully influences the

[065-112] action of the link in certain cases, especially if the link and this point

[065-113] are not in the same vertical line. To reconcile all the conditions proper

[065-114] to the satisfactory operation of the valve in the construction of the link

[065-115] motion, is a problem requiring a good deal of attention and care for its

[065-116] satisfactory solution; and to make sure that this result is attained, the

[065-117] engine must be turned round a sufficient number of times to enable us to

[065-118] ascertain if the valve occupies the desired position, both at the top and

[065-119] bottom centres, whether the engine is going ahead or astern. This should

[065-120] also be tried with the starting handle in the different notches, or, in

[065-121] other words, with the sliding block in the slot or opening of the link in

[065-122] different positions.

[065-123] MANAGEMENT OF MARINE BOILERS.

[065-124] 726. _Q._--You have already stated that the formation of salt or scale in

[065-125] marine boilers is to be prevented by blowing out into the sea at frequent

[065-126] intervals a portion of the concentrated water. Will you now explain how the

[065-127] proper quantity of water to be blown out is determined?

[065-128] _A._--By means of the salinometer, which is an instrument for determining

[065-129] the density of the water, constructed on the principle of the hydrometer

[065-130] for telling the strength of spirits. Some of the water is drawn off from

[065-131] the boiler from time to time, and the salinometer is immersed in it after

[065-132] it has been cooled. By the graduations of the salinometer the saltness of

[065-133] this water is at once discovered; and if the saltness exceeds 8 ounces of

[065-134] salt in the gallon, more water should be blown out of the boiler to be

[065-135] replenished with fresher water from the sea, until the prescribed limit of

[065-136] freshness is attained. Should the salinometer be accidentally broken, a

[065-137] temporary one may be constructed of a phial weighted with a few grains of

[065-138] shot or other convenient weight. The weighted phial is first to be floated

[065-139] in fresh water, and its line of floatation marked; then to be floated in

[065-140] salt water, and its line of floatation marked; and another mark of an equal

[065-141] height above the salt water mark will be the blow off point.

[065-142] 727. _Q._--HOW often should boilers be blown off in order to keep them free

[065-143] from incrustation?

[065-144] _A._--Flue boilers generally require to be blown off about twice every

[065-145] watch, or about twice in the four hours; but tubular boilers may require to

[065-146] be blown off once every twenty minutes, and such an amount of blowing off

[065-147] should in every case be adopted, as will effectually prevent any injurious

[065-148] amount of incrustation.

[065-149] 728. _Q._--In the event of scale accumulating on the flues of a boiler,

[065-150] what is the best way of removing it?

[065-151] _A._--If the boilers require to be scaled, the best method of performing

[065-152] the operation appears to be the following:--Lay a train of shavings along

[065-153] the flues, open the safety valve to prevent the existence of any pressure

[065-154] within the boiler, and light the train of shavings, which, by expanding

[065-155] rapidly the metal of the flues, while the scale, from its imperfect

[065-156] conducting power, can only expand slowly, will crack off the scale; by

[065-157] washing down the flues with a hose, the scale will be carried to the bottom

[065-158] of the boiler, or issue, with the water, from the mud-hole doors. This

[065-159] method of scaling must be practised only by the engineer himself, and must

[065-160] not be intrusted to the firemen who, in their ignorance, might damage the

[065-161] boiler by overheating the plates. It is only where the incrustation upon

[065-162] the flues is considerable that this method of removing it need be

[065-163] practised; in partial cases the scale may be chipped off by a hatched faced

[065-164] hammer, and the flues may then be washed down with the hose in the manner

[065-165] before described.
