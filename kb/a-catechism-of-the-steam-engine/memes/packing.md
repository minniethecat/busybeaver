**Packing** is the sealing material compressed around a reciprocating rod — such as a [[piston]] rod, [[valve]] spindle, or air-pump rod — to prevent the escape of [[steam]] or the ingress of air where the rod passes through the [[cylinder]] cover or other pressure boundary. The packing is housed in a [[stuffing-box]] and compressed by a [[gland]].

## Hemp packing

The traditional and most common packing material is plaited hemp, driven tightly into the annular space around the rod:

> [008-112] It is made steam tight by a packing of hemp driven tightly into a recess in the cylinder cover called the stuffing box.

> [009-023] A packing of plaited hemp is forced, which is compressed by a gland or ring tightened down by screws.

The hemp is typically plaited (braided) square and then hammered firmly into place. [[tallow|Tallow]] is commonly applied to lubricate and preserve the hemp:

> [035-103] Apply a hand mallet, pour some melted tallow all around, then pack in a layer of plaited hemp, and drive it down with a wooden mallet.

> [035-108] Abundance of tallow should be allowed, especially at first, to diminish the friction of the packing against the rod and prevent the rod from being scored.

## Metallic packing

For higher-pressure engines or where maintenance access is difficult, metallic packing offers greater durability:

> [035-111] A metallic packing, consisting for the most part of an India-rubber ring, lying in a groove of a brass bush, which bush is formed with a junk ring in the usual manner for enabling the application of hemp packing behind it.

The metallic packing uses a soft anti-friction metal (often a [[isaac-babbitt|Babbitt]]-like alloy or brass ring) that bears against the rod, backed by hemp packing to provide the necessary elasticity:

> [035-114] The elasticity of the India-rubber ring is sufficient to maintain a steam-tight contact with the rod, while it obviates the necessity for the frequent screwing down of the junk ring.

## Packing dimensions

| Component | Proportion | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Packing space depth | ~4× packing ring thickness | Allows room for multiple rings |
| Packing ring thickness | 0.25–0.5 inch | Depends on rod diameter |
| Junk ring clearance | ~0.015 inch | Snug but not binding |

> [035-104] The packing ring is about a quarter of an inch thick, so that the whole packing may have the required depth. The packing should be beat solid, but not too hard, or it will grip the rod too tightly.

## Trunnion packings

In [[oscillating-engine|oscillating engine]]s the steam passes through trunnions (hollow pivots), which also require packing:

> [036-170] The trunnion packings are not liable to leak when the packing is good, and also when the trunnions are well supported so that the wear is uniform.
