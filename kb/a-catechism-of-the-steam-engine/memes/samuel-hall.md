# Samuel Hall (1781–1863)

Samuel Hall was a British engineer and inventor best known for Hall's [[condensation|condenser]], a surface [[condenser]] for [[marine-engine|marine steam engines]] that operated on the principle of external cooling through small copper tubes.

## Career and Contributions

Hall's major innovation was the [[condenser|surface condenser]], which addressed a critical problem in [[marine-engine|marine steam engine]]ering: the contamination of boiler feed water by salt when using [[condenser|jet condenser]]s that mixed seawater directly with [[eduction|exhaust]] steam. Hall's design used indirect heat exchange — steam condensed on the outside of cold copper tubes through which seawater circulated, keeping the condensate fresh and suitable for re-use in the [[boiler]].

[[john-bourne|Bourne]] notes that "some few years ago, a good number of steam vessels were fitted with Hall's [[condensation|condenser]]s, which operated on the principle of external cold, and which consisted of a faggot of small copper tubes surrounded by water." (027-097) The term "faggot of small copper tubes" describes the tube bundle that maximized heat transfer surface area — the same principle used in modern shell-and-tube heat exchangers.

The [[condenser|surface condenser]] was especially beneficial for ocean-going vessels where fresh water make-up was scarce. [[john-bourne|Bourne]] also notes that "in steam vessels provided with Hall's [[condensation|condenser]]s" (033-021), the arrangement allowed continuous re-use of the same fresh water, dramatically reducing boiler scaling and [[blow-off|blow-down]] requirements.

Hall's [[condensation|condenser]] is listed among numerous patented [[condensation|condenser]] designs: "Hall's, Coupland's, Godson's, Robinson's, Stevens's, Hazeldine's, Indie's, Bristow and Attwood's, and a great number of others." (013-102)

## Legacy

The Hall [[condenser|surface condenser]] established the principle of indirect condensation that became standard in [[marine-engine|marine engine]]ering and later in stationary power plants. The transition from [[condenser|jet condenser]]s to [[condenser|surface condenser]]s was one of the key enabling technologies for long-distance ocean steam navigation, as it solved the problem of boiler [[incrustation]] from salt-water feed. Modern power station [[condensation|condenser]]s are direct descendants of Hall's design.
