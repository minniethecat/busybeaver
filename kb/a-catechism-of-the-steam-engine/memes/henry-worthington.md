# Henry R. Worthington (1817–1880)

Henry Rossiter Worthington was an American inventor and manufacturer best known for developing the Worthington duplex steam pump, a widely used feed-water pump design for [[boiler]]s and a significant advance in pumping machinery reliability.

## Career and Contributions

Worthington's key innovation was the duplex steam pump, which employed two parallel steam-driven pumping cylinders operating in alternation. This design ensured a continuous flow of water without the pulsations characteristic of single-cylinder pumps, making it particularly suitable for feeding [[boiler]]s where steady water supply was critical for safe operation.

[[john-bourne|Bourne]] includes a detailed longitudinal section of "the Worthington [[steam|Steam]] Pump, the first of which was constructed about the year 1840" (058-164), noting its historical significance. He also provides "a perspective view of H. R. Worthington's Duplex [[steam|Steam]] Pump" (059-022), describing its construction and operation in detail.

The Worthington pump was manufactured "of various size according to the quantity of water required" (059-033), making it adaptable to boilers of different capacities — from small stationary engines to large [[marine-engine|marine]] power plants. Its self-regulating feature, where the pump automatically adjusted its speed to match the boiler's water consumption, was a key advantage over earlier [[feed-pump|feed pump]] designs.

By the mid-nineteenth century, Worthington pumps were installed in steamships, factories, and waterworks across America and Europe. The design's reliability and simplicity contributed to the broader adoption of high-pressure steam systems, which required dependable feed-water delivery.

## Legacy

The Worthington Pump Company, founded by Henry Worthington, became one of the largest pump manufacturers in the world, producing equipment for [[steam|steam power]] plants, municipal water systems, and industrial processes well into the twentieth century. The duplex principle — using two alternating cylinders for smooth, continuous delivery — influenced pump design across multiple industries. In the *Catechism*, Worthington's pump is presented as a standard against which other [[feed-pump|feed pump]] designs were measured.
