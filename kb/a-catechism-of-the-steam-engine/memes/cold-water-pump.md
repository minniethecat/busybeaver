# Cold Water Pump

The **cold water pump** is a pump in a condensing [[steam|steam engine]] that delivers a continuous supply of cold water — drawn from the sea, river, or a cooling pond — to the [[condenser]], where it mixes with the [[eduction|exhaust]] steam, causing it to condense and thereby maintaining the vacuum (027-148). It is an essential component of the condensing apparatus in both [[marine-engine]]s and land engines.

## Operation

When the [[eduction|exhaust]] steam enters the [[condensation|condenser]], it meets a jet or shower of cold water delivered by the cold water pump. The cold water absorbs the latent heat of the steam, condensing it back into liquid. This condensation creates the partial vacuum that reduces back-pressure on the piston and improves the engine's efficiency. The resulting mixture of condensed steam and injection water is then removed by the [[air-pump]] and discharged into the [[hot-well]].

The quantity of cold water required depends on the initial temperature of the cooling water and the volume of steam to be condensed. In temperate climates, about 24 to 28 times the volume of the cylinder is required per stroke for condensing purposes (027-155).

## Dimensions

The capacity of the cold water pump must be proportioned to the cylinder size and the degree of condensation required. For land engines, the usual dimensions of the cold water pump are determined by rule: if a certain number of cubic inches of condensing water are required per stroke, the cold water pump ought to be sized accordingly to deliver that volume against the pressure head in the [[condensation|condenser]] (027-148, 027-151). For a [[double-acting|double-acting engine]], the cold water pump in cubic inches is computed proportionally to the cylinder volume (027-160).

In [[marine-engine|marine engine]]s, the cold water pump draws directly from the sea through a sea cock and suction pipe. In land engines, the pump draws from a cold well, canal, or cooling pond. The pump is typically of the reciprocating or centrifugal type and is driven either directly from the engine's cross-head or by a separate eccentric on the [[crank-shaft]].
