William Henry Beveridge (1879–1963), later created 1st Baron Beveridge, was a British economist and social [[social-reform|reform]]er whose 1942 report _[[national-insurance|Social Insurance]] and Allied Services_ — universally known as the **Beveridge Report** — laid the intellectual foundations of the British post-war [[welfare-state|welfare]] state.

## The Beveridge Report

Commissioned by the wartime coalition government in 1941, the Beveridge Report proposed a comprehensive system of [[welfare-state|social insurance]] covering every citizen "from the cradle to the grave." It identified **five giants** on the road to reconstruction: Want (poverty), Disease (ill health), Ignorance (lack of education), Squalor (poor housing), and Idleness (unemployment). The report proposed universal flat-rate contributions and benefits, a national health service, and a commitment to full employment.

The report became an immediate publishing sensation, selling over 600,000 copies, and its proposals were largely implemented by the post-war [[labour-party|Labour]] government under Clement Attlee (1945–1951). The [[nation|Nation]]al Health Service, family allowances, and expanded national insurance were direct outcomes of Beveridge's framework.

## In Liberalism

Beveridge represents the culmination of the **social liberal** tradition that [[leonard-trelawny-hobhouse]] articulated and that thinkers like [[john-maynard-keynes]] advanced. [[leonard-trelawny-hobhouse|Hobhouse]]'s synthesis of liberal individualism with social responsibility — his argument that liberty required a minimum of economic security — found its practical expression in Beveridge's [[welfare-state|welfare]] architecture.

The Beveridge Report demonstrated that comprehensive social provision need not mean state socialism. Beveridge insisted that his system preserved individual freedom and initiative while protecting citizens against risks they could not manage alone — a distinctly liberal vision of the [[welfare-state|welfare]] state, rooted in the same tradition that produced [[old-age-pensions|old-age pension]]s, labour exchanges, and national insurance in the pre-1914 Liberal governments.
