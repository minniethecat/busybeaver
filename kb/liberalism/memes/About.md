# About This Wiki

This wiki is a structured knowledge base on **liberalism**, covering its intellectual history, key thinkers, foundational concepts, major movements, and historical events.

## Source Material

The primary source for this wiki is **[[leonard-trelawny-hobhouse|L. T. Hobhouse]]**, _[[liberalism|Liberalism]]_ (1911, Williams & Norgate, [[london|London]]). This classic work of political philosophy traces the development of [[liberalism|liberal]] thought from its origins through the early 20th century, covering the core principles of [[liberty]], [[equality]], [[rights]], [[individualism]], and [[democracy]].

The source text is organized into nine chapters:

| Chapter | Title |
|---------|-------|
| I | [[ch01-before-liberalism|Before Liberalism]] |
| II | [[ch02-the-elements-of-liberalism|The Elements of Liberalism]] |
| III | [[ch03-the-movement-of-theory|The Movement of Theory]] |
| IV | [[ch04-laissez-faire|Laissez-Faire]] |
| V | [[ch05-gladstone-and-mill|Gladstone and Mill]] |
| VI | [[ch06-the-heart-of-liberalism|The Heart of Liberalism]] |
| VII | [[ch07-the-state-and-the-individual|The State and the Individual]] |
| VIII | [[ch08-economic-liberalism|Economic Liberalism]] |
| IX | [[ch09-the-future-of-liberalism|The Future of Liberalism]] |

Additional materials include a **Colophon** and an **Uncopyright** notice from the original publication.
