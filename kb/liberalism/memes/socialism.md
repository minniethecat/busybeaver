## Overview

Socialism in [[leonard-trelawny-hobhouse|Hobhouse]]'s *[[liberalism|Liberalism]]* is treated not as liberalism's opposite but as its critical interlocutor. [[leonard-trelawny-hobhouse|Hobhouse]] distinguishes between forms of socialism that liberalism must reject — "mechanical" and "official" socialism — and those socialist insights that a fully developed liberalism must incorporate.

> There are two forms of Socialism with which Liberalism has nothing to do. These I will call the mechanical and the official. Mechanical Socialism is founded on a false interpretation of history. Official Socialism is founded on a false interpretation of human nature. (008-001)

## Mechanical and Official Socialism

**Mechanical socialism** — the [[karl-marx|Marx]]ist doctrine of historical materialism and class struggle — is rejected by [[leonard-trelawny-hobhouse|Hobhouse]] for its determinism, which denies the role of ideas, moral choice, and individual agency in historical change.

**Official socialism** — the bureaucratic, state-centered vision of collective ownership and central planning — is rejected for its "contempt for ideals of liberty" (008-002) and its confusion of liberty with mere competition.

> Official Socialism is a creed of different brand. Beginning with a contempt for ideals of liberty based on a confusion between liberty and competition, it proceeds to a scheme of social organization which would depersonalize the individual and turn the State into a vast bureaucratic machine. (008-002)

## The Convergence of Liberalism and Socialism

Despite these rejections, [[leonard-trelawny-hobhouse|Hobhouse]] argues that a consistent liberalism, when it "grapples with the facts," is driven a considerable distance along what might be called socialist lines:

> Pursuing the economic rights of the individual we have been led to contemplate a Socialist organization of industry. But this, it will be said, is not Liberalism but Socialism. (007-019)

[[leonard-trelawny-hobhouse|Hobhouse]]'s response is that the label does not matter. What matters is whether the policy genuinely serves the liberal end of individual freedom. If state intervention in economic life is necessary to secure the conditions of liberty for all, then such intervention is liberal, not socialist:

> It is evident that these conceptions embody many of the ideas that go to make up the framework of Socialist teaching, though they also emphasize elements of individual right and personal independence, which are the distinctive characteristics of Liberalism. (008-028)

## The Distinguishing Principle

The line between liberalism and socialism, for [[leonard-trelawny-hobhouse|Hobhouse]], is ultimately drawn at the question of liberty. Socialism that pursues equality at the expense of liberty is illiberal. But social [[social-reform|reform]] that expands the sphere of [[positive-liberty|effective liberty]] for ordinary [[nation|people]] is entirely consistent with — indeed, required by — liberal principles.
