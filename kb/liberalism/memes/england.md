## Overview

England occupies a distinctive position in [[leonard-trelawny-hobhouse|Hobhouse]]'s _[[liberalism|Liberalism]]_ as both the setting and a significant part of the argument. British liberalism developed through a characteristic method — constitutional, incremental, practical — that [[leonard-trelawny-hobhouse|Hobhouse]] contrasts favorably with both French revolutionary abstraction and German authoritarianism. "In England, it is true, where men are apt to be shy and unhandy in the region of theory, the Liberal movement has often sought to dispense with general principles" (003-002).

## Significance to Liberalism

The English liberal tradition's preference for practice over theory is, for [[leonard-trelawny-hobhouse|Hobhouse]], both a strength and a limitation. It gave British liberalism a rootedness in actual institutions and historical experience that made its gains durable. The seventeenth-century parliamentary victories — the [[petition-of-right|Petition of Right]], the [[habeas-corpus|Habeas Corpus]] Act, the Bill of [[rights|Rights]] — established constitutional protections that no subsequent government could easily undo.

But the English aversion to theory also constrained liberalism's development. The demand for universal principles — rights, equality, democracy — came from French and [[united-states|America]]n sources, and English liberals were sometimes slow to embrace their full implications. The task [[leonard-trelawny-hobhouse|Hobhouse]] sets himself — providing liberalism with a coherent philosophical foundation — is in part a response to this English theoretical deficit.

England's global position also shaped its liberalism. As the world's leading industrial and imperial power through much of the nineteenth century, England experienced the tensions between liberal principle and imperial practice in acute form. [[gladstone|Gladstone]]'s struggle with the [[ireland|Irish Question]] and his commitment to "national liberty and international fair dealing" exemplified the effort to extend liberal principles beyond England's borders (005-002).
