# University of Utopia

The universities of the Modern Utopia are monumental institutions, central to the intellectual life of the World State. The university at the Utopian capital is described as "a mighty University here, with thousands of professors and tens of thousands of advanced students," where "great journals of thought and speculation, mature and splendid books of philosophy and science, and a glorious fabric of literature will be woven and shaped." (Chapter 26, "The World State in Being")

## Architecture and Physical Presence

The university buildings themselves are architectural statements. The narrator describes "a tall white mass of University buildings that rises with a free and fearless gesture, to lift saluting pinnacles against the clear evening sky." The design reflects a deliberate departure from traditional styles: "It lacks the self-complacent unreasonableness of Board of Works classicism. There's something in its proportions—as though someone with brains had taken a lot of care to get it quite right, someone who not only knew what metal can do, but what a University ought to be." (Chapter 28)

College quadrangles follow the Oxford model—"this particular inn is a quadrangle after the fashion of an Oxford college; it is perhaps forty feet high, and with about five stories of bedrooms above its lower apartments"—but rendered in modern materials and with Utopian sensibilities. (Chapter 24)

## Research and Innovation

Universities serve as the primary research institutions of the Utopian world. "Great systems of laboratories attached to every municipal" centre drive scientific progress. Inventors receive royalties, with a portion going "partly to the research institution that had produced him." In literature and the sciences, "every higher educational establishment carried its studentships, its fellowships, its occasional lectureships," and producing a significant work would attract "a generous competition between rival Universities." (Chapter 29, "The Samurai in Utopian Society")

Authors of sufficient merit may accept "a University endowment and conceding his copyright to the University press." The system ensures that intellectual production is both supported and disseminated through institutional channels, with "grants in the hands of committees of the most varied constitution" supplementing academic resources. (Chapter 29)

## Role in Samurai Training

The university system is integral to samurai formation. College completion is required for samurai candidacy, and the intellectual standard is maintained by the Canon—"very much of it is read and learnt in the schools." Every university in the world contributes to the central intellectual project: "Every university in the world will be urgently working for priority in this aspect of the problem or that." (Chapter 8)
