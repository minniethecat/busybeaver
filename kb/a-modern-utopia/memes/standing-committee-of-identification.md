# Standing Committee of Identification

The Standing Committee of Identification is the Utopian administrative body responsible for processing unidentified persons—strangers, visitors, and anomalous arrivals—within the World State's registration system. When the narrator and the botanist arrive in Utopia without proper documentation, it is this committee that assumes jurisdiction over their case. (Chapter 25, "The Standing Committee")

## Function and Procedure

The committee's role is demonstrated when the narrator encounters the Utopian official at Wassen. The official, consulting a memorandum, announces: "The Standing Committee of Identification has remitted your case to the Research Professor of Anthropology in the University of London, and they want you to go there, if you will, and talk to him." The committee does not itself make final determinations about unidentified individuals; rather, it routes cases to the appropriate specialist authority—in this instance, an anthropological expert. (Chapter 25)

The official's dry, procedural tone—"There's no positive compulsion, but your work here will probably cease"—reflects the committee's bureaucratic character. It operates within a framework of voluntary compliance rather than coercive detention, though the practical consequences of non-compliance (cessation of work permits) provide strong incentive for cooperation. (Chapter 25)

## The Identification System

The committee's work rests on the broader Utopian identification infrastructure. The World State maintains a central index of all citizens, classified "primarily by some unchanging physical characteristic, such as we are told the thumb-mark and finger-mark afford." Wells envisions "each human being could be given a distinct formula, a number or 'scientific name,' under which he or she could be docketed." (Chapter 19, "The Central Index")

The central index is described as potentially "housed in a vast series of buildings at or near Paris," with the French capital serving as the administrative hub of the World State's record-keeping apparatus. The classification of "thumb-marks and of inalterable physical characteristics goes on steadily," forming the bureaucratic backbone against which the Standing Committee checks unidentified arrivals. (Chapter 19)

## Institutional Context

The committee operates within the public office system. The official who communicates the committee's decision is encountered at the public office in Wassen—the same integrated administrative centre that houses the labour bureau, post office, and other public services. This integration ensures that identification status feeds directly into employment eligibility, travel authorisation, and other administrative functions. (Chapter 18)
