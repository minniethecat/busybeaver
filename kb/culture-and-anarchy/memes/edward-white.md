# Rev. Edward White

The Reverend Edward White (1819–1898) was an English Nonconformist minister and author who wrote against Church Establishments. [[matthew-arnold]] engages with White's arguments at length in the Preface to *Culture and Anarchy*, treating him as a representative voice of Nonconformist disestablishmentarianism [P03-009].

## Identity

White was a Congregationalist minister who served congregations in London. He wrote a temperate and carefully argued pamphlet against the principle of state-established churches — a work that Arnold took seriously enough to engage with in detail. Arnold describes him as having written "a temperate and well-reasoned pamphlet against Church Establishments" [P03-009].

## Role in Arnold's Argument

Arnold uses White's pamphlet as a vehicle to examine the Nonconformist case against church establishments. While Arnold respects White's moderation, he ultimately finds the argument provincial and insufficient:

> "Edward White... says that 'the unendowed and unestablished communities of England exert full as much moral and ennobling influence upon the conduct of statesmen as that Church which is both established and endowed.'" [P03-009]

Arnold responds that the question is not whether Nonconformist communities exert *as much* influence but whether *any* of the existing religious bodies in England exert *enough* influence for the higher purposes of national life.

## Key Passages

White's central claim, as Arnold reports it, is that religious equality would produce better outcomes:

> "When all good men alike are placed in a condition of religious equality, and the whole complicated iniquity of Government Church patronage is swept away, more of moral and ennobling influence than ever will be brought to bear upon the action of statesmen." [P03-013]

Arnold challenges this as a kind of provincialism, asking the same question about America that White asks about England, and finding that the removal of establishments does not in itself produce the higher national life [P03-019].

Arnold also critiques White's language of "sweeping away the whole complicated iniquity of Government Church patronage" as "language which has been forced upon him by his position, but which is... devoid of any real solidity" [P03-021].

## Relationship to Arnold's Framework

White represents for Arnold the best face of Nonconformist [[dissent]] — temperate, reasoned, and well-intentioned — and yet still trapped within what Arnold calls the "provincialism" of sectarian thinking. White's proposals, in Arnold's view, address the machinery of religion rather than its [[culture|cultural]] and spiritual substance.

White connects to Arnold's broader argument about the inadequacy of both Establishment and Dissent when each is absorbed in institutional questions rather than the pursuit of human [[perfection]].
