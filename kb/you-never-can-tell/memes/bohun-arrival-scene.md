# Bohun's Arrival

## Definition

The dramatic entrance of Bohun Q.C. — his immediate domination of the scene, transforming emotional chaos into legal order through sheer professional ferocity.

## Context

The late Victorian period of the 1890s was an era of rapid social and intellectual change. Victorian certainties were being challenged by Darwinism, socialism, feminism, and the emergence of new professional classes. Shaw's *You Never Can Tell* captures this transitional moment at an English seaside resort in August 1896.

## In the Play

Bohun's arrival is one of the great theatrical entrances in Shaw. He enters not as a character but as a force — his professional manner so extreme that it becomes therapeutic. His first action: taking command, reducing chaos to legal categories, transforming drama into case law. Shaw's point: professional competence, genuinely practiced at the highest level, solves problems that sentiment cannot. Bohun is the modern deus ex machina — a distinctly professional god.

## Shaw's Commentary

Shaw uses the seaside resort as a liminal space where social conventions are suspended. The characters who represent the future are attractive and life-affirming; those representing the past are rigid and isolated until transformed. The play's comic form is itself the argument: laughter dissolves outdated convention.
