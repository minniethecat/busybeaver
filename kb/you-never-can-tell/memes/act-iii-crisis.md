## Definition

Act III of *You Never Can Tell* is the play's crisis point, where the romantic tension between Valentine and Gloria and the familial tension between Crampton and his children both reach their peak. Shaw structures the act as a series of confrontations — Valentine and Gloria, Gloria and her mother, Crampton and Mrs. Clandon — each building on the last, until Bohun's entrance transforms the emotional impasse into a legal negotiation. The act demonstrates Shaw's mastery of dramatic rhythm: acceleration through conflict, resolution through reason. [004-004]

## In the Play

The central scene between Valentine and Gloria crystallizes the play's philosophical argument. Gloria challenges Valentine to prove his love is genuine and not merely conventional — and Valentine, characteristically, responds with honesty rather than romance. He acknowledges that his conscience would prevent him from marrying without means, and that his love, while real, does not exempt him from practical considerations. This anti-romantic honesty paradoxically convinces Gloria of his sincerity, and their understanding forms the emotional foundation for the legal settlement Bohun will arrange.
