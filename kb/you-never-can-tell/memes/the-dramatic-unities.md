## Definition

*You Never Can Tell* observes the classical unities of time and place with unusual fidelity for a modern play. The action unfolds within a single day (unity of time), primarily in two locations — the dental office and the Marine Hotel — that are in close proximity (unity of place). Shaw's adherence to these classical constraints reflects his dramatic discipline: by compressing the action temporally and spatially, he intensifies its emotional and intellectual pressure.

## In the Play

The compressed timeline is made plausible by the seaside setting — a holiday atmosphere in which encounters can be more rapid and relationships more quickly developed than in everyday life. The spatial concentration (dental office → hotel) mirrors the dramatic movement from private to public, from professional to personal, from estrangement to reconciliation. Shaw's respect for the unities demonstrates his understanding that dramatic intensity often emerges from constraint rather than freedom.
