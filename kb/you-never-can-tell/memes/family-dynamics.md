## Definition

Family dynamics in *You Never Can Tell* center on the Clandon family's unusual structure: a mother who has raised three children alone, a father who has been erased from family memory, and children who have never known the constraints of conventional domestic life. Shaw uses this arrangement to explore fundamental questions about family: What obligations do parents and children owe one another? Can family bonds survive eighteen years of separation? Is the traditional nuclear family a natural form or a social construction? [001-004]

## In the Play

The play traces a movement from fragmentation toward reintegration. At the outset, the Clandons are a family defined by absence — no father, no shared surname for the children, no fixed home (they have just arrived from Madeira). The twins' demand to know their father's identity precipitates a crisis that forces all family members to renegotiate their relationships. By the final act, a new family configuration has emerged: Crampton is acknowledged as father, Mrs. Clandon's long resentment has softened, and Gloria's engagement promises the formation of a new family unit.
