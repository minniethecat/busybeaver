## Definition

English society as depicted in *You Never Can Tell* is the late-Victorian social world of a seaside resort — a milieu defined by class anxiety, status display, and rigid codes of respectability. Shaw presents this society through the eyes of outsiders (the Clandon children, raised in Madeira) and marginal insiders (Valentine, the struggling professional), whose perspectives expose its absurdities. The play's comedy depends on the gap between English social pretension and the human realities it attempts to regulate. [001-004]

## In the Play

The Marine Hotel functions as a microcosm of English society — a place where strangers encounter one another under the governance of social convention. The question that structures social access — "do you have a father?" — reveals the arbitrary nature of English respectability. Valentine's precarious position as a tradesman who must appear to be a gentleman captures the anxiety of a society in which status is both essential and unstable.
