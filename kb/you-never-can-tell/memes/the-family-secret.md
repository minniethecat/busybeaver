## Definition

The family secret in *You Never Can Tell* — that Fergus Crampton is the father of the Clandon children, and that Mrs. Clandon left him because of domestic violence — functions as the play's central plot mechanism. Shaw deploys the secret not for melodramatic effect but as a device for exploring larger themes: the ethics of concealment, the rights of children to know their origins, and the tension between privacy and honesty in family life.

## In the Play

The secret is maintained through an unspoken pact among the adults — Mrs. Clandon, McComas, and Crampton himself. The twins' demand to know their father's identity breaks this pact and sets the plot in motion. Shaw handles the revelation with restraint: the secret is exposed in layers, with Mrs. Clandon's confession of Crampton's violence coming only after the twins have forced the issue. The secret's function is thus structural rather than sensational — it organizes the play's movement from ignorance to knowledge, estrangement to reconciliation.
