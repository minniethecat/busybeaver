## Definition

Legal mediation in *You Never Can Tell* represents Shaw's argument that rational, professional intervention can resolve conflicts that emotional confrontation cannot. Bohun Q.C., as the legal professional who transforms a family quarrel into a negotiated settlement, embodies the play's faith in institutional reason. His brusque, unsentimental manner is presented not as a character flaw but as a professional virtue — he treats the Clandon family's emotional crisis as a case to be settled, and in doing so, succeeds where sentiment would fail. [004-004]

## In the Play

Bohun's mediation takes place largely offstage, reported by characters who marvel at his efficiency. He arranges the financial settlement between Crampton and his family, establishes the terms of Gloria's engagement, and effectively reconstructs the family unit on a contractual basis. Shaw's point is not that family relationships should be reduced to legal contracts, but that legal frameworks can provide the structure within which emotional relationships can be rebuilt on fair terms.
