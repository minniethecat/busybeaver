# Love at First Sight

## Definition

Valentine's instant infatuation with Gloria — "Love at first sight. You can add his scalp to your collection, Gloria" (001-004) — as the comic embodiment of Shaw's Life Force theory.

## Context

The late Victorian period of the 1890s was an era of rapid social and intellectual change. Victorian certainties were being challenged by Darwinism, socialism, feminism, and the emergence of new professional classes. Shaw's *You Never Can Tell* captures this transitional moment at an English seaside resort in August 1896.

## In the Play

Valentine falls in love with Gloria the moment he sees her, and Shaw makes no attempt to disguise the artifice. Philip immediately diagnoses the condition: "Love at first sight. You can add his scalp to your collection, Gloria" (001-004). The instantaneity of the attraction is Shaw's dramatization of the Life Force — the evolutionary imperative that, in Shaw's philosophy, drives humans toward reproduction and improvement of the species. Love is not a sentiment but a biological command.

## Shaw's Commentary

Shaw uses the seaside resort as a liminal space where social conventions are suspended. The characters who represent the future are attractive and life-affirming; those representing the past are rigid and isolated until transformed. The play's comic form is itself the argument: laughter dissolves outdated convention.
