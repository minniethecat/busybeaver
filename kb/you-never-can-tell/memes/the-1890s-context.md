## Definition

*You Never Can Tell* is set in August 1896, a moment poised between the Victorian certainties of the nineteenth century and the modernist disruptions of the twentieth. The play's title itself gestures toward this temporal ambiguity — "you never can tell" what the new century will bring. The 1890s were a decade of accelerating social change in Britain: the New Woman movement challenged gender roles, the professions were expanding, and the old aristocratic order was giving way to a more fluid class structure based on education and expertise.

## In the Play

The play's awareness of its historical moment is explicit. Mrs. Clandon's treatises are titled for the coming century — *Twentieth Century Cooking*, *Twentieth Century Children* — suggesting that Victorian methods are obsolete. Valentine's dental practice represents the new professional economy. The twins' freedom from Victorian inhibition embodies the generational change already underway. Even the setting — a seaside resort where strangers of different classes mingle — reflects the social fluidity characteristic of the fin de siècle.
