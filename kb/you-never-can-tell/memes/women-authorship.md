## Definition

Mrs. Clandon's identity as the author of the *Twentieth Century Treatises* places women's authorship at the center of *You Never Can Tell*. Her writing is not merely a backdrop but the economic and philosophical foundation of the Clandon family's independence — it has supported three children and enabled their escape from an abusive marriage. Shaw presents female authorship as a vehicle of both financial autonomy and intellectual authority, challenging the Victorian assumption that women's proper sphere is domestic and non-commercial.

## In the Play

The *Twentieth Century Treatises* — covering cooking, creeds, clothing, conduct, children, and parents — are simultaneously a source of family pride and embarrassment. The twins rattle off their titles with practiced ease, half-mocking and half-celebrating their mother's achievement. Mrs. Clandon's writing places her among the "New Women" of the 1890s — women who claimed public voices and professional identities. Yet Shaw also suggests the limits of her authorial authority: the treatises' rational prescriptions have not fully prepared her children for the emotional complexities they encounter in England.
