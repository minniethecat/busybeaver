## Definition

The modern professional in *You Never Can Tell* represents Shaw's portrait of a new social type: the man (and potentially woman) who earns status through expertise rather than inheritance. Valentine the dentist and Bohun the barrister exemplify different stages of this professional trajectory — one struggling to establish a practice, the other at the apex of his field. Between them, they embody Shaw's belief that professional competence, not birth, should determine social standing. [001-001]

## In the Play

Valentine's "five-shilling dentist" system is both a business model and a philosophical statement — a flat fee regardless of procedure, honest and transparent. His professional struggles (six weeks without a patient, unpaid rent) reflect the precariousness of the self-made professional, but his commitment to his system even under financial pressure demonstrates integrity. Bohun, by contrast, has so thoroughly mastered the legal profession that he can afford to dispense with its social graces — his brusqueness is the luxury of unquestioned competence.
