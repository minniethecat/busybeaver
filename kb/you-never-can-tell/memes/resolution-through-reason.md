## Definition

Resolution through reason is Shaw's signature method for achieving comic closure. Rather than relying on the conventional devices of romantic comedy — sudden conversions, providential coincidences, changes of heart — Shaw constructs resolutions in which characters think their way to agreement. The happy ending is earned through intellectual effort: positions are articulated, objections are addressed, and terms are negotiated. This method reflects Shaw's faith in reason as a tool for resolving human conflicts.

## In the Play

Bohun's legal mediation embodies resolution through reason in its purest form. The family's emotional crisis is translated into a set of negotiable issues — financial support, acknowledgment of paternity, terms of engagement — and each issue is resolved through rational discussion rather than emotional appeal. Even Valentine and Gloria's romantic understanding is achieved through intellectual honesty rather than sentimental declaration. The resolution satisfies because it has been earned through the exercise of reason, not granted by dramatic fiat.
