## Definition

Lincoln Cathedral appears in *You Never Can Tell* not as a physical setting but as a social credential — the fact that the Clandon children's grandfather is "a canon of Lincoln Cathedral" is sufficient to restore their social standing when their lack of a known father threatens it. The cathedral thus functions as a symbol of institutional respectability, the hereditary social capital that compensates for the deficit in personal family history.

## In the Play

When Valentine learns that Dolly and Philip have a grandfather who is a canon of Lincoln, his social hesitation vanishes instantly: "What! Have you a grandfather? ... A canon of Lincoln! That makes it all right, of course" [001-150]. Shaw's irony is sharp — the same social system that demands a father will accept a grandfather as substitute, provided he occupies a sufficiently respectable institutional position. The cathedral, never seen but repeatedly invoked, represents the established order whose authority the play both acknowledges and undermines.
