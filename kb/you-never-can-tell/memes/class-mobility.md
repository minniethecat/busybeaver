## Definition

Class mobility in *You Never Can Tell* is presented as a defining feature of modern English society — both a promise and a source of anxiety. Valentine's position as a dentist occupies an ambiguous class location: he is a professional, therefore a gentleman, but a tradesman, therefore not quite. His struggle to establish himself reflects the precariousness of achieved (rather than inherited) status. The play suggests that class mobility is real but costly, demanding constant performance and offering no security. [002-003]

## In the Play

Valentine's social position is the play's most sustained meditation on class mobility. His "five-shilling dentist" system is both a business strategy and a class statement — it refuses the hierarchy of fees that would align medical treatment with social rank. The Clandon children, raised outside the English class system, embody a different kind of mobility: they move through English society with a freedom born of ignorance of its rules. Their grandfather — "a canon of Lincoln Cathedral" — provides the inherited status that their father's absence threatens to erase.
