## Definition

Anagnorisis, the Aristotelian term for the moment of recognition or discovery in drama, describes the structural pivot of *You Never Can Tell*: the moment when Fergus Crampton recognizes his children at the Marine Hotel, and they begin to recognize him as their father. Shaw adapts this classical device to comic purposes, transforming what could be a tragic recognition (Oedipus discovering his parentage) into a gradual, negotiated, and ultimately redemptive process of mutual acknowledgment. [001-004]

## In the Play

The recognition unfolds in stages rather than as a single dramatic climax. Crampton first encounters his children as charming strangers at the hotel; he gradually realizes their identity through accumulating clues; the formal revelation is mediated through McComas and Bohun rather than delivered in a single explosive confession. This distributed recognition reflects Shaw's dramatic method — he prefers rational unfolding to melodramatic shock. The anagnorisis is complete only when all parties have acknowledged not merely the fact of their relationship but its emotional and moral implications.
