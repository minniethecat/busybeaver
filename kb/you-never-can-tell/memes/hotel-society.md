## Definition

The Marine Hotel in *You Never Can Tell* functions as a social laboratory — a contained environment in which characters from different backgrounds are thrown together under the governance of commercial hospitality rather than domestic hierarchy. Unlike the private home, where access is controlled by family and class, the hotel is open to anyone who can pay. This openness creates the conditions for the play's central encounters: estranged family members meeting as fellow guests, a struggling dentist lunching with strangers who become his future in-laws. [001-001]

## In the Play

The hotel's public spaces — dining room, terrace, corridors — are the stages for the play's key confrontations. Walter the Waiter presides over the dining room as its interpreter and guardian, translating between the guests' social codes and the hotel's commercial logic. The hotel's neutrality — it belongs to no one, it judges no one — makes it the ideal setting for a play about the reconstruction of family bonds on new terms.
