## Definition

The name "Clandon" in *You Never Can Tell* is a floating signifier, its meaning contested by different characters. For Valentine, it suggests landed gentry — "Mr. Densmore Clandon of Newbury Hall." For Dolly and Philip, it means their mother's celebrity — "the celebrated Mrs. Lanfrey Clandon, an authoress of great repute — in Madeira." For Crampton, it is the name of the family he lost. Shaw uses the contested meaning of the family name to explore broader questions about identity, inheritance, and the gap between social perception and personal reality.

## In the Play

The name's ambiguity is established in Act I, when Valentine's assumption about the Clandons' social position is immediately corrected by the twins' comic recitation of their mother's book titles. The name means one thing from the outside (gentry, status, Newbury Hall) and another from the inside (authorship, exile, Madeira). The play's resolution involves a kind of settlement of the name's meaning — Crampton is acknowledged as father, but on terms that reflect the family's actual history rather than social convention.
