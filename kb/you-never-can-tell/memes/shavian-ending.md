## Definition

The Shavian ending, as exemplified by *You Never Can Tell*, rejects the conventional comic closure of marriage-as-destiny in favor of marriage-as-negotiation. Shaw's endings are intellectually satisfying rather than emotionally overwhelming — characters reach agreement through reasoned discussion, and the final arrangement reflects justice rather than merely desire. This method reflects Shaw's conviction that comedy should model the rational resolution of human conflicts, not merely provide escape from them.

## In the Play

The play's ending brings all parties to the luncheon table — now transformed from a site of awkwardness and recognition into one of genuine family conviviality. The legal and financial arrangements have been settled offstage; what remains is the emotional acknowledgment that the legal machinery has made possible. The final image — the family at lunch — mirrors the opening situation but transforms it: what was a gathering of strangers has become a gathering of relations, bound not by coercion but by choice.
