## Definition

Social performance in *You Never Can Tell* refers to Shaw's insight that all social interaction involves elements of theatrical performance. Characters adopt roles — the respectable dentist, the charming young lady, the eminent barrister, the perfect waiter — and their success in navigating the social world depends on their ability to perform these roles convincingly. Shaw's comedy exposes the gap between role and self, but unlike more cynical dramatists, he suggests that this gap can be narrowed through honesty and self-awareness.

## In the Play

Valentine is the play's most self-conscious performer, acutely aware that his professional persona is a construction: his "professional manner evidently strikes him as being a joke" [001-040]. Bohun performs professional eminence with such conviction that the performance has become indistinguishable from the self. Walter the Waiter performs service as an art form. The twins perform charm and wit with such natural grace that their performances read as authenticity. The play's resolution suggests that the healthiest social performance is one that acknowledges its own constructedness.
