## Definition

The modern family, as Shaw imagines it in *You Never Can Tell*, is a voluntary association based on mutual respect rather than a compulsory institution based on biological kinship and patriarchal authority. The Clandon family's journey from fragmentation to reintegration models this new conception: Crampton is accepted as father not because biology demands it but because his children choose to accept him; Mrs. Clandon's maternal authority is respected not because convention requires it but because she has earned it. The modern family, in Shaw's vision, is a family reconstructed on democratic principles.

## In the Play

The play's resolution establishes the template for the modern family. Crampton's relationship with his children is reestablished on contractual rather than authoritarian terms. Gloria's marriage to Valentine will be a partnership of equals, not a transfer of patriarchal authority from father to husband. Even Walter the Waiter, the childless professional, is included in the family circle — suggesting that the modern family extends beyond biological boundaries to include those bound by affection and respect.
