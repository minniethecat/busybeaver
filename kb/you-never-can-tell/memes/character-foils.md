## Definition

Shaw structures the character system of *You Never Can Tell* around a series of contrasting pairs — foils whose differences illuminate each other and the play's themes. Valentine and Bohun contrast as emerging versus established professionals. Gloria and Dolly contrast as principled intensity versus irreverent play. Mrs. Clandon and Crampton contrast as progressive motherhood versus traditional fatherhood. These pairings create a dramatic economy in which character is revealed through comparison rather than exposition. [001-004]

## In the Play

The Valentine-Bohun pairing is particularly rich. Both are professionals whose identity is defined by their work; both are honest to the point of social discomfort; both play crucial roles in the play's resolution. Their differences — youth versus age, precariousness versus security, warmth versus brusqueness — illuminate the trajectory of the modern professional life. Similarly, the Dolly-Gloria contrast explores the range of possible responses to their mother's progressive upbringing: Dolly's playful irreverence and Gloria's principled intensity represent complementary rather than competing possibilities.
