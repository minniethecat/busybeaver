## Definition

Act I of *You Never Can Tell*, set in Valentine's dental office on a "fine August morning in 1896," is one of Shaw's most efficient opening acts. In a single location and within roughly an hour of stage time, Shaw introduces all the principal characters (save Bohun and Crampton), establishes the central comic situation, and sets in motion every major plot thread. The dental setting — with its uncomfortable chair, its "gas pump and cylinder," its professional apparatus — provides a fitting metaphor for the extraction of uncomfortable truths that the play will perform. [001-001]

## In the Play

The act proceeds through a series of escalating encounters: Dolly's tooth extraction, the arrival of Philip, the appearance of Mrs. Clandon and Gloria, and finally the entrance of McComas with news of Crampton. Each entrance adds a new layer of complication. Valentine's role shifts from dentist to potential suitor to reluctant lunch guest, his professional identity increasingly entangled with his personal destiny. The act closes with the Clandons' departure for the Marine Hotel, having successfully extracted not only a tooth but also a lunch companion — and, inadvertently, the first thread in the unraveling of their family secret.
