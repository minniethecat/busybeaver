## Definition

The marriage settlement in *You Never Can Tell* represents Shaw's distinctive approach to the comic ending. Rather than concluding with a romantic declaration or a sentimental embrace, the play resolves its central romance through a legal and financial arrangement negotiated by Bohun. This anti-romantic resolution embodies Shaw's conviction that marriage is as much an economic and legal institution as an emotional one, and that happy endings require material foundations.

## In the Play

Bohun's offstage negotiation of the settlement is reported rather than dramatized, a technique that emphasizes the matter-of-factness of the arrangement. The settlement addresses the practical obstacles to the marriage — Valentine's lack of means, Crampton's obligations to his children, the terms under which the young couple can establish a household. By making the settlement explicit, Shaw refuses the convention that love conquers all — love, in his view, needs legal and economic support to flourish.
