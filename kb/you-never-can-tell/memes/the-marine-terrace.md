## Definition

The Marine Hotel terrace in *You Never Can Tell* functions as a liminal space — neither fully public (like the dining room) nor fully private (like a domestic interior). It is on the terrace that characters encounter one another as social equals, where conversations that would be inappropriate indoors can unfold in the neutral space between hotel and sea. Shaw uses the terrace as a dramatic device to stage encounters that require a degree of openness and informality.

## In the Play

Several of the play's most significant conversations occur on or near the terrace. Its position between the hotel's interior and the open sea symbolizes the characters' position between social constraint and natural freedom — the governing tension of the play. The terrace is where characters go to think, to speak privately, to escape the formalities of the dining room while remaining within the hotel's social orbit.
