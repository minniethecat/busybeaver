## Definition

In *You Never Can Tell*, conversation is not merely the medium through which action is reported — conversation IS the action. Characters advance the plot, transform relationships, and resolve conflicts not through physical deeds but through talk. This reflects Shaw's conviction that in modern society, the most consequential human actions are verbal: negotiations, confessions, arguments, reconciliations. The play's dramatic tension derives from what characters say to one another and how they say it. [001-004]

## In the Play

Every significant plot development occurs through conversation: the twins' demand to know their father, Mrs. Clandon's revelation of Crampton's violence, Valentine's declaration of love, Bohun's negotiation of the settlement. Even the physical events (the tooth extraction, the luncheon) are significant primarily as occasions for conversation. Shaw's mastery of dialogue as action represents a distinctly modern dramatic technique — the replacement of melodramatic incident with intellectual and emotional exchange.
