# Crampton's Hidden Sensitivity

## Definition

The deep emotional vulnerability beneath Fergus Crampton's harsh exterior revealed by Shaw's stage directions describing his "wistful, wounded eyes" (001-298) and "painful want of confidence in his welcome."

## Context

The late Victorian period of the 1890s was an era of rapid social and intellectual change. Victorian certainties were being challenged by Darwinism, socialism, feminism, and the emergence of new professional classes. Shaw's *You Never Can Tell* captures this transitional moment at an English seaside resort in August 1896.

## In the Play

Shaw's stage directions for Crampton establish a character whose severity is a defense mechanism. His "atrociously obstinate, ill tempered, grasping mouth" (001-298) is immediately qualified: "his consequent capacity for suffering acutely from all the dislike that his temper and obstinacy can bring upon him is proved by his wistful, wounded eyes" (001-298). The sensitivity explains the harshness — Crampton is not cruel by nature but wounded by experience, and his obduracy is the scar tissue.

## Shaw's Commentary

Shaw uses the seaside resort as a liminal space where social conventions are suspended. The characters who represent the future are attractive and life-affirming; those representing the past are rigid and isolated until transformed. The play's comic form is itself the argument: laughter dissolves outdated convention.
