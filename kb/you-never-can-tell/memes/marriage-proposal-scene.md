# The Marriage Proposal

## Definition

Valentine's proposal to Gloria at the Marine Hotel — conducted through legal negotiation rather than romantic declaration, exemplifying Shaw's argument that marriage should be a rational contract.

## Context

The late Victorian period of the 1890s was an era of rapid social and intellectual change. Victorian certainties were being challenged by Darwinism, socialism, feminism, and the emergence of new professional classes. Shaw's *You Never Can Tell* captures this transitional moment at an English seaside resort in August 1896.

## In the Play

The proposal in *You Never Can Tell* is characteristically Shavian. Valentine does not kneel or declaim; instead, the proposal emerges from practical discussion facilitated by Bohun's legal machinery. The marriage settlement — with provisions for separate property and mutual independence — is the true declaration of love. Shaw's point is that a marriage based on clear terms and mutual respect is more genuinely romantic than one based on sentimental illusion.

## Shaw's Commentary

Shaw uses the seaside resort as a liminal space where social conventions are suspended. The characters who represent the future are attractive and life-affirming; those representing the past are rigid and isolated until transformed. The play's comic form is itself the argument: laughter dissolves outdated convention.
