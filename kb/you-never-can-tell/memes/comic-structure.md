## Definition

The comic structure of *You Never Can Tell* follows the classical pattern of complication, crisis, and resolution, but Shaw inflects each phase with his characteristic intellectual content. The play begins with a chance encounter (the tooth extraction), escalates through a series of revelations and confrontations, and resolves through legal and emotional negotiation rather than the conventional marriage proposal. Shaw's innovation lies in making the resolution intellectually satisfying — the characters earn their happy ending by thinking through their positions rather than simply feeling their way to it. [001-004]

## In the Play

Act I establishes the complications: the Clandons' lack of a father, Valentine's professional precarity, the attraction between Valentine and Gloria. Act II brings all parties to the Marine Hotel and stages the recognition between Crampton and his children. Act III develops the romantic crisis between Valentine and Gloria while advancing the family negotiations. The final act resolves all threads through Bohun's legal machinery — a deliberately anti-romantic resolution that satisfies both the emotional and intellectual demands of the drama.
