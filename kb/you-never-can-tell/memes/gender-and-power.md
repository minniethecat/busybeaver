## Definition

Gender and power in *You Never Can Tell* is examined through the play's systematic contrast between patriarchal and egalitarian models of male-female relations. Crampton embodies the patriarchal model — his power over his wife was absolute, enforced by violence and law. Mrs. Clandon embodies resistance to that model — her flight, her career, her independent household all represent female power achieved through separation. The younger generation — Valentine and Gloria, the twins — represents the possibility of a new arrangement: gender relations based on equality rather than hierarchy. [001-004]

## In the Play

Valentine's courtship of Gloria models egalitarian gender relations. He does not pursue, conquer, or command — he states his feelings honestly and awaits her response. Gloria's acceptance is an act of autonomy, not submission. The twins' relationship with each other models gender equality in its most natural form — Dolly and Philip operate as equals, neither deferring to nor dominating the other. The play's vision of reformed gender relations is one of its most explicitly progressive elements.
