## Definition

The dialogue of *You Never Can Tell* exemplifies Shaw's mature comic style — rapid, intellectually dense, and structured like musical exchange. The twins' verbal duets, Valentine's self-deprecating candor, Walter's philosophical monologues, and Bohun's brusque legal pronouncements each represent a distinct verbal register, and Shaw's skill lies in orchestrating their interaction. The play's wit is not ornamental but structural: ideas are advanced, tested, and refined through conversation.

## In the Play

The twins' dialogue is the play's most technically demanding verbal performance. Their exchanges operate at a speed that disorients other characters — Valentine "bewildered by the leaps and bounds with which their acquaintanceship is proceeding, gasps" [001-145]. Their wit is not malicious but liberating: it punctures pretension, exposes absurdity, and creates space for honest exchange. Shaw's dialogue, unlike Wilde's epigrammatic style, aims at truth-telling rather than display — the joke is always in service of the idea.
