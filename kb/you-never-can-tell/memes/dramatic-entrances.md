## Definition

Dramatic entrances in *You Never Can Tell* are moments of heightened theatrical significance, carefully timed and characterized. Shaw understands that a character's first appearance on stage establishes expectations that the subsequent action will fulfill, subvert, or complicate. Each major character's entrance is accompanied by a detailed stage description that functions as a miniature character sketch, ensuring that the audience sees the character before they speak.

## In the Play

The play's most memorable entrances include Philip's arrival — "a handsome man in miniature, obviously the young lady's twin" [001-100] — which energizes the scene and establishes the twin dynamic; Gloria's entrance, which renders Valentine speechless with love at first sight; and Crampton's entrance at the hotel, where his failure to recognize his own children creates the play's central dramatic irony. Each entrance is both a plot event and a character revelation.
