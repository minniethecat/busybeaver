## Definition

Comic timing in *You Never Can Tell* refers to Shaw's precise control of dramatic rhythm — the speed of dialogue, the placement of entrances and exits, the buildup and release of tension. The twins' rapid-fire exchanges, Valentine's hesitant declarations, Walter's measured monologues, and Bohun's brusque pronouncements each operate at a distinct tempo, and Shaw's art lies in orchestrating these different rhythms into a coherent comic whole.

## In the Play

The play's timing is most virtuosic in the Act I exchanges between Dolly, Philip, and Valentine. The twins speak at a speed that disorients their interlocutor — their dialogue overlapping, completing each other's sentences, shifting subjects without transition. Valentine's slower, more considered tempo creates a comic counterpoint. Later acts introduce Walter's flowing, meditative rhythm and Bohun's staccato efficiency, expanding the play's temporal palette. The resolution achieves its satisfying effect partly through the gradual slowing of the play's tempo — from the twins' frenetic energy to Bohun's measured legal cadences.
