## Definition

Act IV of *You Never Can Tell* brings the play's multiple plot threads to resolution through a combination of legal negotiation and emotional reconciliation. Bohun's offstage mediation transforms the family's emotional crisis into a structured settlement, while Valentine and Gloria's understanding deepens into an engagement. Shaw's resolution is deliberately anti-sentimental — the happy ending is achieved through contracts and settlements, not through grand gestures or sudden conversions — yet it satisfies both the emotional and intellectual demands the play has established. [004-004]

## In the Play

The act's structure is retrospective: much of the decisive action (Bohun's negotiations) occurs offstage, reported by characters who enter with news of settlements reached. This technique, borrowed from classical drama, allows Shaw to maintain the play's conversational texture while advancing the plot efficiently. The final moments bring all characters together in a configuration that mirrors the opening scene — a family lunch, now transformed from an awkward gathering of strangers into a genuine family occasion.
