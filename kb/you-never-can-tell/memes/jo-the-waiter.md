## Role

Jo is a young waiter at the Marine Hotel, assisting Walter the Waiter. Though a minor character with few lines, Jo contributes to the play's depiction of the service world that sustains the social drama of the hotel. Jo appears briefly in Act II, receiving instructions from Walter and providing a glimpse of the younger generation of service professionals.

## Biography

Little is known of Jo beyond his occupation as assistant waiter. He is described as young, presumably learning the trade under Walter's tutelage. His presence reinforces the hotel's status as a workplace as well as a social setting, reminding the audience that the elegance enjoyed by the Clandons and their guests depends on labor performed by people whose lives remain largely invisible.

## Key Scenes

Jo appears in Act II at the Marine Hotel, where he assists Walter in preparing for and serving the luncheon party. His interactions with Walter reveal the hierarchy and professional standards of hotel service.
