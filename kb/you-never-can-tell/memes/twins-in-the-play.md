## Definition

Dolly and Philip Clandon, the twin children of Mrs. Clandon, function as a composite dramatic device in *You Never Can Tell*. Their rapid-fire dialogue, instinctive coordination, and shared irreverence create a chorus-like perspective on the adult world they observe and disrupt. As twins, they represent natural vitality unspoiled by Victorian convention — Shaw's vision of what young people might become if freed from the "family life of punishments and lies" their mother fled.

## In the Play

The twins drive the play's action from its opening moments. It is Dolly whose toothache brings the family to Valentine's dental office; it is Philip who demands that their mother reveal their father's identity. Their dialogue operates in a kind of verbal tennis, with exchanges so rapid that Valentine — and the audience — struggle to keep pace. This stylistic choice reflects Shaw's thematic point: the twins embody a new generation whose speed of thought and freedom of manner leave their elders bewildered.
