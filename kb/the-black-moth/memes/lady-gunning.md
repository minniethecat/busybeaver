# Lady Gunning

Miss Elizabeth Gunning, later Lady Gunning, is one of the celebrated "beautiful Gunnings" — a pair of sisters whose arrival in [[bath|Bath]] society causes a stir among the fashionable world. She is the talk of [[the-pump-room|the Pump Room]], the subject of gentlemen's admiration and ladies' envy, the kind of beauty around whom the social currents of the novel swirl.(019-080)(019-115)

## Role in the Novel

Gunning appears as a figure of fashionable beauty — she is "the newest beauty" of Bath, discussed in the same breath as the arrival of her sister. The gentlemen of the novel's world take note of her; the ladies, including [[mr-devereux|Lady Devereux]], feel the subtle sting of comparison. She passes by on the arm of an admirer, and her impending marriage to a Mr. Coventry is reported as news of social significance.(019-080)(019-115)(019-117)(030-012)

Her function in the novel is atmospheric: she exemplifies the [[bath|Bath]] [[marriage-and-matrimony|marriage market]], the economy of beauty and fortune that drives so much of the social action. She is not a driver of plot; she is a feature of the landscape through which the central characters move.
