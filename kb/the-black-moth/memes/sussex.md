## Description

Sussex in *The Black Moth* is less a precisely mapped geography than a landscape of the heart — a county of quiet lanes, rolling downs, and small villages where the pace of life is slow and the world of [[london|London]] fashion seems very far away. It is here that [[diana-beauleigh|Diana Beauleigh]] lives, here that [[john-carstares|Carstares]] rides his mare Jenny through the twilight, and here that the novel's central love story takes root in the gardens of [[horton-house|Horton House]]. (006-038)(008-003)

The novel's Sussex is a working landscape: "typical Sussex lane[s], where he trotted between uneven hedges, sweet with blossom and with May, and placid fields rolling away on either side, upwards until they merged into the undulating hills, barely discernible in the gloom, that are the downs." It is a place of labourers returning home after a long day in the fields, of ostlers chewing straws at country inns, and of evenings so calm that "there was nothing beyond the sound of the mare's hoofs to break the beautiful stillness of it all." (008-035)(008-036)

## In The Black Moth

**Diana's Home**: It is in Sussex that the Beauleigh family resides — at [[horton-house|Horton House]], near the village of Littledean, seven miles west of Midhurst. When [[tracy-belmanoir|the Duke of Andover]] asks his sister where his mysterious beauty lives, [[lavinia-carstares|Lady Lavinia]] receives the curt answer: "She lives in Sussex." The single sentence, delivered without elaboration, marks the county as a world apart from the fashionable society of [[bath|Bath]] and London. (006-038)(020-107)

**Carstares's Territory**: Sussex is also the territory that Carstares patrols as a [[highway-robbery|highwayman]] — "the west side of Sussex, beyond Midhurst" — not because the pickings are rich, but because "he knew and loved the country." His robberies in these parts are notably unsuccessful as crimes: he apologises to ladies, duels with sporting gentlemen for half their jewels, and is so charmed by the landscape that he often forgets he is supposed to be working. The Sussex lanes are where he rides Jenny at dusk, thinking of his penniless days in Paris, and where the beauty of the evening can momentarily lift even his carefully guarded melancholy. (008-003)(008-035)

**The Duke's Intrigue**: When the Duke of Andover sets his machinations in motion, Sussex becomes the theatre of his scheme. He summons [[harper|Harper]] and begins with a single question — "Do you know Sussex?" — before laying out the precise geography of his plan: Littledean, Midhurst, The Pointing Finger inn, and the household he means to infiltrate. The county, which has been the setting for Carstares's freedom and Diana's innocence, is now to be the hunting-ground of the predator. (020-099)(020-107)

**The Return**: At the novel's close, after the drama of the abduction and rescue, "O'Hara travelled back to Sussex" with Carstares — a quiet journey that signals the restoration of the natural order. Sussex, which had been the setting for the story's gentlest scenes, reclaims its characters from the violence that threatened them. (028-186)
