# Honour System in The Black Moth

The [[honour|honour] system is the invisible architecture of Georgette Heyer's *[[john-carstares|The Black Moth]]*. Every [[john-carstares|major plot ee-card-party|ven]]t — John ]]Carstares's self-exile, the card-party scandal, the climactic duel, Diana's final choice — turns on the question of what honour demands and what it forbids. This page aggregates the novel's core honour-related concepts and characters, presenting the system as a unified social logic rather than as isolated themes.

## The Card Party — Honour's Test

The [[the-card-party|card party at Andover's house]] is the novel's honour-event — the scene where the entire system is put under pressure. [[richard-carstares|Richard Carstares]] cheats; [[john-carstares|Jack]] takes the blame; [[tracy-belmanoir|Tracy Belmanoir]] exploits the situation. Each man's response reveals his relationship to the honour code.

## Resolution — Love Over Honour

The novel's resolution is a quiet revolution in the honour system. [[diana-beauleigh|Diana Beauleigh]]'s question — "And she was willing to share your disgrace?" — and her answer — yes — overturns the code's absolute condemnation. Love, in Heyer's world, does not abolish honour; it transforms it from a social verdict into a personal choice.（014-095）
