# Jim Davenant

Jim (James) Davenant is a minor character in Georgette Heyer's *The Black Moth*. He is a gentleman of [[john-carstares|John Carstares]]'s generation and was among the circle of friends who turned their backs on Jack after the card-party scandal.

## The Scandal

Davenant is mentioned in [[richard-carstares|Richard Carstares]]'s anguished recollection of the fateful night at the card party. When Jack defiantly confirmed the accusation of cheating, all his friends recoiled — and Davenant, who had been one of Jack's closest companions, was no exception:

> "Dare sprang forward, catching him by the shoulder — crying out that it could not be true! When Jack laughed — he fell away from him as from the plague. And all of them! My God, to see them drawing away — not looking at Jack! And Jack's face — growing paler and harder … every moment. … All his friends … turning their backs to him. Davenant — even Jim Davenant walked away to the fireplace with Evans." (003-103)

The emphasis on "even [[jim-salter|Jim]] Davenant" underscores how complete Jack's social abandonment was — not even his closest friends stood by him.

## At Lord Avon's

Davenant reappears briefly at [[lord-avon|Lord Avon]]'s card party, looking across at the [[tracy-belmanoir|Duke of Andover]] inquiringly during a moment of tension.

## Redemption

At the novel's conclusion, Davenant is among the old friends bidden to a re[[marriage-and-matrimony|union]] dinner — a sign that the social breach of seven years is finally being healed:

> "Well, what does it mean? Fortescue is bidden, and Davenant. 'Tis very curious." (024-062)

His inclusion in the reconciliation gathering signals the restoration of the social order that Jack's sacrifice had shattered — and the quiet vindication of both [[john-carstares|Carstares]] brothers.
