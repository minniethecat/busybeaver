# The Highway Rescue

The rescue of Diana [[mr-beauleigh|Beauleigh]] from [[highway-robbery|highwaymen]] by [[john-carstares|John Carstares]] is the novel's pivotal romantic event — the moment when the disguised hero and the heroine first meet, and the attraction that will drive the novel's central relationship is kindled. It is a scene of action, danger, and sudden intimacy that establishes the terms of the love story to come.(011-019)

## The Event

[[diana-beauleigh|Diana Beauleigh]], [[coach|travel]]ling with her aunt, is waylaid by [[highway-robbery|highwaymen]] on the [[coach|road]]. [[john-carstares|Carstares]], who happens upon the scene — and who is, ironically, himself a [[highway-robbery|highwayman]] — intervenes. He fights off the attackers with skill and [[courage-and-recklessness|courage]], and in the aftermath, [[diana-beauleigh|Diana]] is captivated by her rescuer. "The [[duelling|duel]] had set her every nerve tingling; she was filled with admiration for her preserver, and the sight of his sensitive, handsome countenance did nothing to dispel that admiration."(011-019)(011-079)

## Consequences

The rescue sets the romantic plot in motion. [[diana-beauleigh|Diana]] knows her rescuer only as "[[john-carstares|Sir Anthony Ferndale]]" — an alias that conceals [[john-carstares|Carstares]]'s true identity as the [[disgrace-and-exile|disgrace]]d Lord [[john-carstares|John]]. Her fascination with him, and her gradual discovery of his secret, drives the novel's central relationship. The rescue also establishes [[john-carstares|Carstares]]'s character: he is brave, skilled, and protective — a gentleman even when acting outside the law.
