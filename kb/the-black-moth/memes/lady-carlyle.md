# Lady Carlyle

Lady Carlyle is a member of [[london|London]] society who serves as [[lavinia-carstares|Lavinia Carstares]]'s fashionable rival. She appears at the theatre and is referenced in connection with a social invitation.

## Role in the Novel

Lady Carlyle appears in a theatre box during a performance attended by [[lavinia-carstares|Lavinia Carstares]]. At any other time, Lavinia would have been annoyed to see that the occupant was Lady Carlyle, "her pet rival in all matters of fashion," but on this occasion Lavinia is too preoccupied by personal concerns to care. She merely remarks that Lady Carlyle's garlands of roses on her dress appear "quite grotesque" (023-006).

Lady Carlyle is also referenced indirectly when [[lady-gunning|Lady Gunning] asks Lavinia whether she has "the Carlyles' invitation," suggesrvant|ting the ]]Carlyle household is a fixture in London social circles (024-007).

## Relationships

- **[[lavinia-carstares]]** — Rival in fashion. Lavinia considers Lady Carlyle her "pet rival" in matters of dress and style (023-006)
- **[[lady-gunning]]** — Connected through London social circles; Lady Gunning references "the Carlyles' invitation" (024-007)
