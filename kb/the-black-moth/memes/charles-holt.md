# Charles Holt

Charles Holt is a very minor character in Georgette Heyer's *The Black Moth*. A young kinsman of the Fleming family, he attends the theatre with [[lavinia-carstares|Lavinia]] and [[richard-carstares|Richard Carstares]] on the night of Lavinia's planned elopement.

## Appearance and Role

Holt is [[fashion|fashion]]ably — indeed, somewhat garishly — dressed for a young man about town:

> Mr. Holt's rather garish mixture of apple-green and pink, with waistcoat of yellow. (023-002)

At the theatre, he points out notable figures in the audience to [[william-fleming|Mr. Fleming]], demonstrating his familiarity with London society. His knowledge of who everyone is provides Lavinia with a moment of wry amusement — her young relative's social expertise contrasts with her own distracted state of mind on this pivotal evening.
