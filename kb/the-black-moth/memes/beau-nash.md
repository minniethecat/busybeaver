# Beau Nash

Richard "Beau" Nash (1674–1761) was the most famous master of ceremonies in Georgian England, credited with transforming Bath into the most fashionable resort in the country. In *The Black Moth*, he appears as a background figure whose presence defines the rigid social etiquette of Bath society.

## In the Novel

Beau Nash is glimpsed strutting "about among the ladies" at a Bath social function, observed by Lady Lavinia's party with a hint of wistfulness (007-013). His authority over Bath's social life is such that even the boisterous [[lord-avon|Lord Avon]] is measured against his standards: Avon's noisy laugh "had more than once caused Mr. Nash to shudder and to close his august eyes" (007-059).

Nash's role in the novel is atmospheric rather than active — he represents the formal, rule-bound Georgian social order against which the novel's passions and scandals play out. His presence at Bath anchors the story in a specific historical moment when that city, under his governance, was the beating heart of English fashion.
