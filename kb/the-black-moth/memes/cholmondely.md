# Jack Cholmondely

Jack Cholmondely is a minor character in *The Black Moth*, part of the London gambling and duelling set. He is present at White's club and is later mentioned in connection with a duel fought in Bath against [[lord-falmouth|Lord Falmouth]].

## In the Novel

Cholmondely appears in Chapter VIII among the gentlemen at White's. When Andrew Belmanoir asks about Lord Falmouth's whereabouts, it is noted that "Jack Cholmondely was there — he would know" (007-045). Cholmondely looks up when addressed, confirming his presence in the fashionable gaming circle (007-046).

Later, in Chapter XXIII, [[lord-avon|Lord Avon]] brings news to Lady Lavinia of a scandal: "How Cholmondely and Falmouth had dared to fight a duel in Crescent Fields, and had been arrested" (023-025). This brief mention places Cholmondely among the novel's habitual duellists — gentlemen willing to risk legal consequences for the sake of honour.
