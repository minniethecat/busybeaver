# Lady Fanny

Lady Fanny is a minor character in Georgette Heyer's *The Black Moth*. She appears in Chapter XVIII, where she assists [[lavinia-carstares|Lady Lavinia Carstares]] in dressing for an evening engagement, addressing her with affectionate familiarity.

## In The Black Moth

Lady Fanny's single scene places her in the intimate setting of [[lavinia-carstares|Lavinia]]'s dressing room. She exclaims with warmth as she helps her friend prepare:

> "But, my angel!" exclaimed Lady Fanny, stepping back to view her, "… (018-115)

Her use of the endearment "my angel" suggests a close, affectionate relationship with [[lavinia-carstares|Lavinia]], placing her among the confidantes of the novel's heroine.
