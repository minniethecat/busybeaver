# The Chequers Inn

The Chequers Inn at Fallowfield is the novel's opening setting — the place where the [[disgrace-and-exile|disgrace]]d [[john-carstares|John Carstares]] is living under an assumed name, and where the family solicitor [[warburton|Warburton]] arrives to find him. It is a vivid, warmly drawn portrait of a provincial English inn: Tory, comfortable, and presided over by the pompous, lovable [[chadber|Mr. Chadber]].(001-001)

## Description

The inn has been in the [[chadber|Chadber]] family since 1667, when the "jovial Stuart King sat on the English throne, and the Hanoverian Electors were not yet dreamed of." Its loyalties are Jacobite — [[chadber|Mr. Chadber]] boasts of having sheltered fugitives of the 'Forty-Five — and its atmosphere is one of comfortable, self-satisfied provincialism. It is the perfect setting for a novel that begins with a [[disgrace-and-exile|disgrace]]d aristocrat hiding in plain sight among the middling sort.(001-001)(001-002)

## In The Black Moth

The Chequers serves as the novel's starting point — literally and thematically. It is from the inn that [[warburton|Warburton]] sets out to find Jack; it is at the inn that Jack's double life as "[[john-carstares|Sir Anthony Ferndale]]" is first revealed to the reader. The inn is the threshold between the world Jack has lost and the world he has made for himself on the [[coach|road]].(001-037)
