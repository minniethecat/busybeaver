# O'Hara Residence

The O'Hara home is the novel's happiest [[servant|domestic]] setting — the place where [[miles-ohara|Miles]] and [[molly-ohara|Molly]]'s companionate [[marriage-and-matrimony|marriage]] plays out in scenes of breakfast comedy and warm hospitality. It is here that "Cousin Harry" ([[john-carstares|Carstares]] in disguise) finds his first safe harbour in society, and here that the novel's ideal of friendship is most fully realised.(009-001)(010-025)

## Description

The O'Hara household is rendered in warm, affectionate detail. The breakfast table where [[molly-ohara|Molly]] plies [[miles-ohara|Miles]] with coffee and eggs; the drawing room where "Cousin Harry" is welcomed without question; the easy, unpretentious comfort of an Irish gentleman's home in England. It is the novel's middle ground — neither the grandeur of [[wyncham|Wyncham]] nor the provincial cosiness of the [[chequers-inn|Chequers]], but a place where title and warmth coexist without tension.(009-001)(010-001)

## In The Black Moth

The O'Hara home is the setting for the novel's most joyful recognition scene — [[miles-ohara|Miles]] seeing through Jack's disguise and embracing his old friend without hesitation — and for some of its warmest [[servant|domestic]] comedy. It represents the possibility of a social world that judges men by what they are rather than by what they appear to be, and its warmth provides the emotional baseline against which the novel's darker settings are measured.(010-025)
