# The Queensberry Rout

The [[fashion]]able rout or masquerade — associated with Lady Davenant — is one of the novel's key social events, a gathering where the titled and the [[fashion]]able converge, where [[tracy-belmanoir|Tracy Belmanoir]] exercises his dangerous charm, and where the romantic and social plots intertwine in the crowded rooms of the beau monde.(020-049)

## The Event

Georgian [[london-season|rout]]s were large evening parties —[[london-season| les]]s formal than balls, more crowded and chaotic. Guests came and went, card tables were set up in side rooms, and the press of bodies made intimate conversation possible even under the eye of society. The [[the-masquerade-ball|masquerade]] eleme[[masquerade|nt added]] the frisson of disguise — a theme that resonates throughout the novel.

## In The Black Moth

The rout is the setting for crucial social encounters. It is at such gatherings that [[tracy-belmanoir|Belmanoir]] is most dangerous — his charm most effective, his menace most veiled. It is here that [[scandal|reputation]]s are made and destroyed in an evening's gossip, that glances across a crowded room carry the weight of social judgement, and that the novel's characters must navigate the treacherous waters of [[fashion]]able society.
