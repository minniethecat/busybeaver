# Harper

Harper is a [[servant|servant]] employed by [[tracy-belmanoir|Tracy Belmanoir,]] the Duke of Andover, who later transfers to the Beauleigh household under the auspices of [[sir-hugh-grandison|Sir Hugh Grandison]] (Tracy's alias). A simple, nervous man, he functions as a minor plot device connecting Tracy's London life to his pursuit of Diana in the countryside.

## Role in the Novel

Harper serves as [[tracy-belmanoir|Tracy Belmanoir]]'s groom and general servant at St. James's Square (020-097). When Tracy adopts the alias of [[sir-hugh-grandison|Sir Hugh Grandison]] to pursue Diana Beauleigh, Harper is dispatched to the Beauleigh household in the country, where he provides character references under the false identity. His presence enables Tracy to track Diana's movements and eventually orchestrate her abduction (025-041).

## Key Actions and Events

[[tracy-belmanoir|Tracy]] summons Harper to deliver confidential instructions at his [[london|London]] residence (020-097). Harper, described as dim-witted and easily flustered, struggles to comprehend his master's schemes but obediently carries them out (020-110), (020-116).

A few days later, a "groom" (Harper) arrives at [[whites-chocolate-house|St. James's]] Square bearing a missive from the countryside — a barely legible letter signed "M. Harper" (022-017), (022-021). Tracy subsequently provides a false character reference for Harper under the name of [[sir-hugh-grandison|Sir Hugh Grandison]], describing him as "exceeding honest and trustworthy" (022-024), (026-037).

Harper enters the [[servant|service]] of the [[beauleigh-residence|Beauleigh]] household, where Diana commends his skill with horses: "You would be surprised how excellently well Harper manages the horses" (025-004). When Diana goes riding in search of berries near Ashley, Harper accompanies her and her aunt (025-026).

During the berry-picking excursion, Harper deliberately keeps pace with [[diana-beauleigh|Diana]], drawing nearer as they approach a wooded bend in the road (025-041), (025-042). He assists Diana in dismounting her horse and ties both horses to a tree (025-046). Moments later, Harper's murmuring voice is heard at the door of a coach — and [[tracy-belmanoir|Tracy Belmanoir]] enters, revealing Harper's role as an accomplice in the abduction plot (025-059).

## Relationships

- **[[tracy-belmanoir]]** — Employer. Harper serves Tracy as groom and messenger, carrying out instructions however nervously (020-097), (022-017)
- **[[diana-beauleigh]]** — Serves Diana under false pretences, praised for his horsemanship, but ultimately aids in her abduction (025-004), (025-046)
