# Andrew Belmanoir

Lord Andrew [[tracy-belmanoir|Belmanoir]] is the younger Belmanoir brother — a "mad, reckless rakehell" who embodies the family wildness in its most impulsive, least controlled form.[[tracy-belmanoir| Unli]]ke his brother Tracy, whose cold intelligence turns rakery into an instrument of power, Andrew is simply reckless: brave, hot-blooded, and incapable of calculating the cost of his actions.(018-059)

## Role in the Novel

Andrew functions as a minor counterpoint to [[tracy-belmanoir|Tracy]]. He has all the [[tracy-belmanoir|Belmanoir tra]]its — pride, courage, wildness, charm — but none of the cold calculation that makes his elder brother genuinely dangerous. He is a hot-headed boy rather than a cold-hearted man, and the novel treats him with a mixture of exasperation and affection that it never extends to Tracy.

His presence also helps establish [[belmanoir-family|the Belmanoir family]] dynamic: Tr[[lavinia-carstares|acy the]] cold eldest, Lavinia the proud sister, Andrew the reckless youngest. Together they form a portrait of aristocratic character in its three most characteristic modes.(004-077)(022-009)

## Characterisation

Andrew's defining trait is [[courage-and-recklessness|recklessness]] without restraint. He is described as "the same mad, reckless rakehell as ever" — a phrase that captures both the continuity of his character and its fundamental immaturity. He does not grow, does not learn, does not calculate; he simply acts, and the consequences are what they are.(018-059)

His relationship with [[tracy-belmanoir|Tracy]] is one of admiring emulation. He wants to be what his brother is — feared, desired, magnificent — but he lacks the intelligence and the coldness that make Tracy's rakery effective. He is, in effect, [[pride|Belmanoir pride]] without Belmanoir skill.
