[030-001][[tracy-belmanoir|His Grace]] of [[andover-estate|Andover]] sat at the window of his lodgings at Venice, looking down at a letter in his hand. The writing was his sister’s. After a moment he drew a deep breath and broke the seal, spreading the sheets out upon the broad sill.

[030-002]> “My very dear [[tracy-belmanoir|Tracy]],

[030-003]> “So you have gone again with no Farewell to yr. poor Sister, sir! I am indeed very offended, but I understand yr. Reason. As soon as I sett mine eyes on [[diana-beauleigh|Diana]] I knew the Truth and recognised yr. dark Beauty. I am monstrous grieved for you, dear. I quite love her myself, altho’ she is very tiresomely lovely, but perhaps as she is dark and I am fair, we shall not clash.

[030-004]> “The Homecoming was prodigious exciting. [[andrew-belmanoir|Andrew]] was present, [[richard-carstares|Dicky]], of course, and me. Mrs. [[isabella-fanshawe|Fanshawe]], too, was there, for she knew Jack Abroad, and a monstrous queer Old Man, who was vastly fidgetty and overcome to see Jack. Then Sir [[miles-ohara|Miles]] and his wife came, who I thought quite agreeable nice People, and Diana’s Father and Aunt, rather Bourgeois, but, on the whole, presentable.

[030-005]> “Everyone knows the Truth now, but most People have been prodigious kind and I scarce notice a difference in our Reception. Dearest [[richard-carstares|Dicky]] is gayer than he was wont to be and more darling, and I almost enjoy being a Social Outcast.

[030-006]> “When Diana is properly gowned, as should suit her position (but I grieve to say that she prefers to [[fashion|dress]] plainly), she will make a prodigious Elegantt Countess. I have promised to connduct her to my own Mantua Maker, which is very sacrificing, as I am sure You will agree. I know [[london|London]] will go Crazy about her, and, indeed, those who have allready seen her, which is [[lord-avon|Avon]] and Falmouth, are positively Foolish. I make no doubtt ’twill be very mortifying, but I suppose it must be borne.

[030-007]> “She and Jack are prodigious happy together; it is most Unfashionable, but so am I happy with Dick, so there are a Pair of us, and we had best sett Fashion.

[030-008]> “Pray, return soon, my dear Tracy, you cannot conceive how I miss you. I was surprised you went away with Mr. [[frank-fortescue|Fortescue]], I had no Notion you were so friendly.

[030-009]> With dearest Love,

[030-010]> Yr. Sister

[030-011]> [[lavinia-carstares|Lavinia]].

[030-012]> P.S.—’Twill interest you to hear that [[lady-gunning|Miss Gunning]] is to marry Coventry. ’Tis all over Town this last Week.”

[030-013]Slowly his Grace put the sheets together and handed them to [[john-fortescue|Fortescue]], who had just come into the room.

[030-014]“These, from my sister, may possibly interest you, F[[class|rank]].”

[030-015][[john-fortescue|Fortescue]] read the letter through, and at the end folded it and handed it back in silence. Tracy laid it down on the table at his elbow.

[030-016]“I began—wrongly,” he said.

[030-017]“Yes,” assented his friend. “She was not—that kind of girl.”

[030-018]“But having begun wrongly—I could not undo the wrong.”

[030-019]“So you made it worse,” said [[john-fortescue|Fortescue]] gently.

[030-020]“I would have married her in all [[honour|honour]]—”

[030-021]“In your own arrogant [[fashion|fashion]], Tracy.”

[030-022]“As you say—in my own arrogant fashion, Frank. If I could go back a year—but where’s the use? I am not whining. Presently I shall return to England and make my bow to—the Countess of [[wyncham|Wyncham]]. Possibly, I shall not feel one jealous qualm. One never knows. At all events—I’ll make that bow.”

[030-023]“You will?” F[[class|rank]] looked sharply down at him. “Nothing more, [[tracy-belmanoir|Tracy]]! You do not purpose—”

[030-024]“Nothing more. You see, F[[class|rank]]—I love her.”

[030-025]“I crave your pardon. Yes—she would not take you, but she has, I think, made you. As I once told you, when love came you would count yourself as nought, and her happiness as everything.”

[030-026]For a moment [[tracy-belmanoir|his Grace]] was silent, and then back came the old smile, still cynical, yet with less of the sneer in it.

[030-027]“How very pleasant it must be, F[[class|rank]], to have one’s prophecies so happily verified!” he purred. “Allow me to felicitate you!”
