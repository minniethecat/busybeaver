# Beauleigh Residence

The Beauleigh home is the novel's [[servant|domestic]] sanctuary — the place where the wounded [[john-carstares|Carstares]] is brought to recover, where [[miss-betty-beauleigh|Miss Betty]] nurses him in defiance of the doctor's bloodletting, and where [[diana-beauleigh|Diana]] comes to know the man beneath the [[highway-robbery|highwayman]]'s mask. It is the novel's quietest and warmest setting.(013-001)

## Description

The [[mr-beauleigh|Beauleigh]] residence is a comfortable country house — not grand like [[wyncham|Wyncham]] or magnificent like [[andover-court|Andover]], but warm, well-run, and full of the steady affection that [[miss-betty-beauleigh|Miss Betty]] provides. It is a gentlewoman's establishment: respectable, unpretentious, and exactly the kind of place where a wounded [[highway-robbery|highwayman]] can convalesce without awkward questions.

## In The Black Moth

The Beauleigh home is where the novel's romantic plot develops from rescue to relationship. It is here that [[john-carstares|Carstares]], bedridden and dependent, reveals more of himself than he ever does on the [[coach|road]]; here that [[diana-beauleigh|Diana]]'s admiration deepens into love; here that [[miss-betty-beauleigh|Miss Betty]]'s shrewd warmth provides the [[servant|domestic]] ground for emotions that the novel's more theatrical settings could not sustain.(013-001)(014-001)
