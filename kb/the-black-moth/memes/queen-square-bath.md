# Queen Square, Bath

Queen Square is the [[bath|Bath]] address where [[lavinia-carstares|Lavinia Carstares]] stays during the season — the [[servant|domestic]] headquarters from which the novel's social plots radiate. It is here that [[richard-carstares|Richard]] and [[lavinia-carstares|Lavinia]]'s [[marriage-and-matrimony|marriage]] plays out its tensions, here that [[tracy-belmanoir|Tracy Belmanoir]] visits his sister, here that the novel's intersecting worlds of [[fashion]], family, and danger converge.(006-008)

## Description

Queen Square was one of [[bath|Bath]]'s most fashionable addresses in the 1750s, part of the Georgian architectural expansion that transformed the city into England's premier spa resort. In the novel, it represents the elegance and constraint of fashionable life — a world of morning calls, afternoon promenades, and evening routs, where every conversation carries social weight.

## In The Black Moth

The Queen Square setting hosts some of the novel's most psychologically acute scenes. It is here that [[tracy-belmanoir|Tracy]] visits [[lavinia-carstares|Lavinia]] and delivers his cutting verdict — "like all Belmanoirs, you care first for yourself" — and here that the [[john-carstares|Carstares]] [[marriage-and-matrimony|marriage]] is tested by [[richard-carstares|Richard]]'s secret guilt. The elegance of the setting makes the emotional drama within all the more striking.(005-086)
