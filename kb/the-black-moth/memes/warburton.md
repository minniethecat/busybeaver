# Warburton

The family solicitor Warburton is the novel's first point of contact between the worlds of [[wyncham|Wyncham]] and the highway. A "little green-clad lawyer" with "an air of mystery," he arrives at [[chequers-inn|the Chequers Inn]] in Chapter I, charged by [[richard-carstares|Richard Carstares]] with the delicate task of finding his disgraced brother and persuading him to come home.(001-007)(001-056)

## Role in the Novel

Warburton functions as the emissary between the two [[john-carstares|Carstares]] brothers. His loyalty is to the family, not to any individual member, and his careful, lawyerly approach to the task of finding Jack — asking quiet questions, observing closely, never pushing too hard — establishes him as one of the novel's few genuinely prudent [[characters-of-the-black-moth|characters]]. When he finally meets Jack, his emotion — "Master Jack! I implore you!" — breaks through his professional reserve, revealing the depth of his attachment to the family he serves.(001-032)(001-077)

## Characterisation

Warburton is defined by professional competence married to personal loyalty. He is a lawyer by trade, and his approach to the mission [[richard-carstares|Richard]] gives him is methodical, careful, and discreet. But he is also a man who has served the [[carstares-household|Carstares family]] for years, and his devotion to Jack — the disgraced son the family has officially erased — is both moving and significant. It is the first indication that Jack's disgrace, however absolute in theory, is not absolute in the hearts of those who know him.
