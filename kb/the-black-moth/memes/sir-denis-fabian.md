# Sir Denis Fabian

Sir Denis Fabian is a minor figure in [[the-black-moth|The Black Moth]], mentioned only once as a potential suitor for [[lavinia-carstares|Lavinia Carstares]]. His character is defined entirely through Lavinia's contemptuous dismissal.

## Role in the Novel

Sir Denis is a baronet whom [[richard-carstares|Richard Carstares]] has been inviting to the house in hopes of arranging a match for his sister Lavinia. Lavinia, who is in love with [[john-carstares|John Carstares]] (living under the alias "Mr. Everard"), rejects the idea with scorn, calling Sir Denis "that great gaby" (016-105).

## Characterisation

The single reference to Sir Denis — "that great gaby" — suggests a foolish or awkward man, at least in [[lavinia-carstares|Lavinia]]'s estimation. The term "gaby" is 18th-century slang for a si[[richard-carstares|mpleton]] or fool. That Richard keeps "forever inviting [him] to the house" suggests Sir Denis is persistent in his courtship or that Richard is persistent in his matchmaking (016-105).
