# The Jacobite Cause

The Jacobite cause — the movement to restore the [[disgrace-and-exile|exile]]d House of Stuart to the British throne — is the political force that shapes the loyalties of *[[john-carstares|The Black Moth]]*'s world. Though the 'Forty-Five rising has been defeated by the time the novel opens, its memory is fresh, and the political allegiances it forged still define the novel's characters.(001-002)

## Historical Background

The Jacobites (from *Jacobus*, Latin for James) supported the claim of James II and his Stuart descendants to the British throne, against the Hanoverian succession established after the Glorious Revolution of 1688. The movement drew support from Tories, Catholics, and Highland clans, and mounted major risings in 1715 and 1745. After the defeat at Culloden (1746), the cause was effectively dead as a military force, but survived as a political sentiment — particularly among Tory families like the [[john-carstares|Carstares]].

## In The Black Moth

The Jacobite cause is present in the novel as atmosphere rather than plot. [[chadber|Mr. Chadber]]'s Tory politics and his pride in having sheltered Jacobite fugitives establish the political texture of Fallowfield. The [[john-carstares|Carstares]]'s traditional Toryism — "true to their rightful king" — places them on the losing side of history, a position that aligns with Jack's own status as an [[disgrace-and-exile|exile]] and outsider. The cause provides the novel's political depth without ever becoming its subject.(001-002)(001-131)
