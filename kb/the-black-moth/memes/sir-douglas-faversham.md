# Sir Douglas Faversham

Sir Douglas Faversham is a minor figure in [[the-black-moth|The Black Moth]], appearing in a single brief reference as one of several gentlemen who call upon [[lavinia-carstares|Lavinia Carstares]] at her London home.

## Role in the Novel

Sir Douglas is announced at Lavinia's door alongside [[gregory-markham|Sir Gregory Markham]], "Moosso le Chevalier," and [[harold-lovelace|Captain Lovelace]] (019-044). His name and title mark him as a member of London's fashionable society, one of the many gentlemen who pay their respects to the eligible Miss Carstares.

## Characterisation

Sir Douglas exists only as a name — no dialogue, no description, no action. He is a piece of social furniture, serving to demonstrate the breadth of [[lavinia-carstares|Lavinia]]'s circle of admirers and the constant stream of callers at a fashionable young lady's door.
