# London

London is the distant centre of *[[john-carstares|The Black Moth]]*'s world — the metropolis from which the [[john-carstares|Carstares]] and [[tracy-belmanoir|Belmanoir]]s draw their power and to which the novel's social currents ultimately flow. It is less a setting than a gravitational force: the place where [[scandal|reputation]]s are made and destroyed, where [[duelling|duels]] are fought at [[barn-elms|Barn Elms]], where the Season determines who is in favour and who is out.(001-003)(024-126)

## Description

Heyer's London is glimpsed rather than described — mentioned in passing as the place [[chadber|Mr. Chadber]] once visited ("staying there for ten days and setting eyes on no less a person than the great Duke of Marlborough"), the destination of [[coach]]es, the source of [[fashion]]s and [[scandal]]s. It is the novel's capital in every sense: political, social, and moral.(001-003)

## In The Black Moth

London is where the [[the-card-party|card party]] took place — the scene of [[richard-carstares|Richard]]'s cheating and Jack's self-sacrifice. It is where [[tracy-belmanoir|Belmanoir]] moves with supreme confidence through [[gaming|gaming house]]s and routs. It is the place of ultimate judgement, where society's verdict on a man's honour is rendered and enforced.(003-076)(024-126)
