# Fittering

Fittering is a village in Sussex near the Carstares family estate. It appears in *The Black Moth* primarily through the activities of [[jim-salter|Jim Salter]], Jack Carstares's devoted servant.

## In the Novel

Fittering is first mentioned when Jim Salter proudly tells his master that he bought his mare Jenny "at Fittering today from the naughty ruffian who owned her" (002-161). The horse-fair at Fittering is clearly a familiar landmark in the neighbourhood.

The village is also home to Mary, Salter's sweetheart. [[miles-ohara|Sir Miles O'Hara]] teasingly asks Salter "what about that little girl at Fittering?" suggesting a romance that Salter is bashful about (015-061). Later, during the crisis when Jack rides to rescue Diana, Salter asks leave "to visit his Mary at Fittering" — a brief moment of domestic warmth amid the novel's climactic action (026-035).

By the novel's end, Salter's loyalty is rewarded. After returning from Fittering to find the household in turmoil, he proves his devotion to Jack once again (029-003). O'Hara later advises Salter to "marry that nice girl at Fittering," promising that Mary "shall maid my lady" — a happy resolution for the faithful servant (029-055).
