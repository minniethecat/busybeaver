# Colonel Shepherd

Colonel Shepherd is a military man living at an ad[[fashion|dress]] on the Strand in [[london|London]]. The [[tracy-belmanoir|Duke of Andover]] visits him and departs the half-hour interview with visible satisfaction, suggesting the Colonel provided information or assistance useful to the Duke's schemes.

## Role in the Novel

The [[tracy-belmanoir|Duke of Andover]] rises early one mor[[wigs-and-grooming|ning]] and driv[[fashion|es by]] chair to an address on the Strand, "where lodged a certain Colonel Shepherd." He spends half an hour with the Colonel and, upon emerging, "the curl of his lip betokened satisfaction" (024-058). The nature of their business is not disclosed, but the timing and Tracy's pleased reaction imply the Colonel is a source of useful information.

Later, [[tracy-belmanoir|Tracy]] reports to [[john-carstares|John Carstares]]: "You may be relieved to hear it. I have seen Shepherd." Carstares responds with an interrogative "Shepherd?" suggesting he is aware of the Colonel's significance to Tracy's plans (024-071), (024-072).
