# Lady Elizabeth Beauleigh

Lady Elizabeth Beauleigh is the deceased mother of [[diana-beauleigh|Diana Beauleigh]]. She appears in the novel only as a name — a figure whose absence defines Diana's situation as a young woman without a mother, the beloved daughter of a mother who has passed, now under the care of her aunt.(007-001)

## Role in the Novel

Lady Elizabeth is never seen alive in the novel. She is mentioned in the context of Miss Elizabeth Beauleigh (her sister-in-law, or possibly a namesake relation) staying at an inn with Diana. Her death has left Diana "motherless" — a condition that shapes Diana's vulnerability and the protective instincts she inspires in those around her, particularly [[mr-beauleigh|Mr. Beauleigh]] and later [[john-carstares|Jack Carstares]].(007-001)

The single reference to her is brief — she is invoked only to establish [[diana-beauleigh|Diana]]'s family situation at the opening of the story.
