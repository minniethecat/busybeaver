# Lord Falmouth

Lord Falmouth is a minor character in [[the-black-moth|The Black Moth]], appearing as a member of London's fashionable society. He is a friend of [[frank-fortescue|Frank Fortescue]] and an associate of [[tracy-belmanoir|the Duke of Andover]], for whom he once served as a second in a duel.

## Role in the Novel

Falmouth appears in Chapter 7 at a [[london|London]] card-party, where he engages in gaming and conversation with [[john-fortescue|Fortescue]], Andover, [[tom-wilding|Tom Wilding]], and others. He reminisces about a duel fought two years earlier between Fortescue and the [[tracy-belmanoir|Duke of Andover]] ("Devil") in Hyde Park, in which Falmouth served as Devil's second (007-069). He participates enthusiastically in the dice game that follows, egging Belmanoir on to accept a wager (007-078).

> "Gad, yes!" exclaimed his partner, Lord Falmouth. "Why, I was Devil's second! But it was ages ago!"
>
> (007-069)

Falmouth is later mentioned in Chapter 23, where [[lord-avon|Lord Avon]] tells Lavinia of a scandal: "How Cholmondely and Falmouth had dared to fight a duel in Crescent Fields, and had been arrested" (023-025). This suggests Falmouth is a habitual duellist, willing to risk legal consequences.

In the [[Epilogue]], Falmouth is listed among the gentlemen who have become "positively foolish" over Lavinia's beauty (030-006).

## Characterisation

Falmouth is a typical Georgian gentleman of fashion — fond of gaming, willing to duel, and deeply embedded in London's aristocratic social circle. His dialogue ("Gad, yes!") and the fact that he fought a duel in Bath's Crescent Fields suggest a man of action rather than reflection, comfortable with the era's codes of honour and ready to hazard both his money and his life.
