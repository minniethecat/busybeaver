# Lady Charlwood

Lady Charlwood is a minor figure in [[london|London]] society who appears at a [[masquerade|masquerade ball]]. She is notable only for being briefly mistaken for Diana Beauleigh by Tracy Belmanoir, the Duke of Andover.

## Role in the Novel

Lady Charlwood appears entirely through a case of mistaken identity. At a [[the-masquerade-ball|masquerade]], the [[tracy-belmanoir|Duke of Andover]] approaches a lady he believes to be Diana Beauleigh, only to realise his error and excuse himself: "Far more probable, my dear. I thought you Lady Charlwood! Pray give me leave" (019-190). She has no dialogue, no described appearance, and no further role in the narrative. Her sole function is to illustrate the confusion and anonymity of the masquerade setting.
