# The 'Forty-Five Rising

The Jacobite rising of 1745 — the attempt by Charles Edward Stuart ("Bonnie Prince Charlie") to reclaim the British throne for the House of Stuart — is the historical event that forms the political backdrop to *[[john-carstares|The Black Moth]]*. It shapes the loyalties of the novel's characters, colours the atmosphere of Tory Fallowfield, and provides the historical texture against which the fictional drama plays out.(001-002)

## The Historical Event

In 1745, Charles Edward Stuart landed in Scotland and raised an army of Highland clansmen. He advanced as far south as Derby before turning back, and the rising was crushed at Culloden in April 1746. The aftermath was brutal: Jacobite sympathisers were executed, imprisoned, or [[disgrace-and-exile|exile]]d, and the Highland clan system was systematically dismantled.

## In The Black Moth

The 'Forty-Five is recent history in the novel (set in the 1750s). [[chadber|Mr. Chadber]] boasts of having sheltered Jacobite fugitives; the [[john-carstares|Carstares]] are staunch Tories "true to their rightful king"; the political atmosphere of Fallowfield is still charged with the memory of the rising. The event provides the novel's historical depth, anchoring its fictional aristocrats in the real political conflicts of their era.(001-002)(001-131)
