# Crescent Fields, Bath

Crescent Fields is the [[duelling]] ground near [[bath|Bath]] where Cholmondely and Falmouth fought, and one of the locations where the novel's culture of honour finds its most dangerous expression. Like [[barn-elms|Barn Elms]] near [[london|London]], it is a place where gentlemen come to settle their differences at sword-point.(023-025)

## Description

The [[duelling]] grounds of Georgian England — [[barn-elms|Barn Elms]], Chalk Farm, Crescent Fields — were semi-secret locations, known to the gentlemen who used them and the authorities who tacitly permitted them. They existed in a legal grey zone: everyone knew what happened there, but the fiction of ignorance was maintained so long as the participants were gentlemen and the outcomes were discreet.

## In The Black Moth

Crescent Fields is mentioned in passing — [[lavinia-carstares|Lady Lavinia]] hears gossip of the [[duelling|duel]] — but its significance is structural. It is one of the network of locations that made the culture of [[duelling]] possible, a physical infrastructure of honour that paralleled the novel's moral infrastructure of pride, [[courage-and-recklessness|courage]], and [[disgrace-and-exile|disgrace]].(023-025)
