# Lord Fotheringham

Lord Fotheringham is a minor character in Georgette Heyer's *The Black Moth*. He appears at a social gathering in Chapter XIX, where he approaches [[lavinia-carstares|Lady Lavinia Carstares]] to claim a promised dance.

## In The Black Moth

Lord Fotheringham's sole [[fashion|appearance]] [[london|occurs]] at a London social event. [[lavinia-carstares|Lavinia Carstares]] points out his approach to her companion, noting that she had previously promised him a dance:

> "And I see Lord Fotheringham approaching. I am promised to…" (019-105)

His brief mention contributes to the novel's depiction of [[lavinia-carstares|Lavinia]]'s lively social circle and the constant attention she receives from eligible gentlemen of the *ton*.
