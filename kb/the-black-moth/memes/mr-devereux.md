# Mr. Devereux

Mr. Devereux is a minor gentleman of [[bath|Bath]] society who appears at the fashionable gatherings of the Beau Monde. His name surfaces in two contexts: as one of the gentlemen entering the theatre box alongside [[gregory-markham|Markham]], Wilding, and [[john-fortescue|Sir John Fortescue]], and in connection with Lady Devereux, who may be his wife or a relation.(018-111)(023-011)

## Role in the Novel

Devereux appears only briefly — he is named among the company at the theatre, one of the gentlemen who make up the social world that surrounds the novel's central [[characters-of-the-black-moth|characters]].(023-011)

Lady Devereux provides a more vivid, if still brief, presence: she comes "tripping up to them" at a social gathering, her manner light and flirtatious, receiving compliments with the practised ease of a woman accustomed to admiration.(018-111)(018-117)

Whether Mr. and Lady Devereux are husband and wife, or connected by some other family tie, the novel does not specify. Their appearances, though brief, contribute to the texture of the Bath social world — a world of boxes at the theatre, compliments exchanged, and the endless circulation of fashionable people.
