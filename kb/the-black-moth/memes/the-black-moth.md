# The Black Moth

*The Black Moth* (1921) is Georgette Heyer's first novel, a Georgian-era romance and adventure story centered on John Carstares, the disgraced eldest son of the Earl of Wyncham, who turns highwayman after taking the blame for his brother's cheating at cards.

## Characters

The novel features a cast of aristocratic families, their households, and the society figures of Georgian London and Bath:

- [[John Carstares]] — protagonist, the disgraced Earl turned highwayman
- [[Richard Carstares]] — his brother, burdened by guilt and debt
- [[Tracy Belmanoir]] — the Duke of Andover, antagonist and rival
- [[Diana Beauleigh]] — the heroine, caught between Jack and Tracy
- [[Lavinia Carstares]] — Richard's wife, central to the marital drama
- [[Harold Lovelace]] — a charming interloper in the Carstares marriage
- [[Andrew Belmanoir]] — Tracy's younger brother

## Places

The action moves between country estates, London society, and the fashionable spa town of Bath:

- [[Wyncham]] — the Carstares family estate
- [[Andover]] — the Duke of Andover's seat
- [[Beauleigh]] — Diana's home
- [[Chequers Inn]] — where the novel opens
- [[London]] — the hub of society, gaming, and scandal
- [[Bath]] — where much of the romance unfolds
