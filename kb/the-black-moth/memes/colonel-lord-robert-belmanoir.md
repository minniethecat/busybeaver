# Colonel Lord Robert Belmanoir

Colonel Lord Robert Belmanoir is a minor member of the Belmanoir family, brother to [[Lavinia Carstares|Lady Lavinia]] and kinsman to [[Tracy Belmanoir|the Duke of Andover]]. He appears in London and Bath as part of the fashionable world through which his sister moves, and his presence contributes to the atmospheric depiction of the Belmanoir family's influence.

## Family Connection

Robert is one of Lavinia's brothers, along with Andrew Belmanoir. Both brothers are "in town" during the London season and provide "casual chaperonage" that Lavinia "much preferred to Richard's solicitous care." This preference speaks to the gulf between Lavinia's world — easy, aristocratic, careless — and her husband [[Richard Carstares|Richard]]'s more anxious, attentive style of marriage.(006-006)

## Characterisation

Robert is depicted as "very richly clad and rakish in [[fashion|appearance]]," with a "florid face" that registers surprise upon seeing his sister at the opening of the [[bath|Pavi]]lion in Bath. His description aligns him with the Belmanoir style — showy, confident, and slightly disreputable.(018-034)

[[richard-carstares|Richard Carstares]] "disliked [[lord-robert-belmanoir|Colonel Lord Robert Belmanoir]], but the Duke he detested." This gradation is significant: Robert is objectionable [[tracy-belmanoir|enoug]]h to be disliked, but he lacks his brother Tracy's capacity for inspiring genuine hatred. He is a Belmanoir, but a lesser one — guilty by association rather than his own wickedness.(006-006)

## In the Novel

Robert serves principally as a figure in the background of [[lavinia-carstares|Lavinia]]'s world. He escorts her to routs and [[the-masquerade-ball|masquerade]]s, appears at the opening of the Pavilion, and generally fills the role of the rakish aristocratic brother — part of the family machinery that produces both Lavinia's social confidence and her vulnerability.

His relationship to [[tracy-belmanoir|Tracy]] is never explored in depth; he exists as one more [[tracy-belmanoir|Belmanoir]] in a novel that is interested primarily in the most extreme member of that family.
