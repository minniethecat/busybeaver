# Catcher (Baseball)

> "Chiefly on Al Heaton, the stalwart catcher, did the eyes of the multitude rest with favor." (002-008)

## Definition

The catcher in 19th-century baseball stood behind home plate to receive pitches from the pitcher. Unlike modern baseball where the catcher wears extensive protective gear, catchers of this era played with minimal equipment. The position was considered one of the most important on the field — the catcher was often the team's most prominent player and a natural leader.

## The Catcher's Role

The catcher was central to a team's identity and success. Al Heaton, the Catalpa catcher, was "a tall, shapely young fellow, with a ruddy and oval face, bright brown eyes, a keen glance, and a sinewy length of limb that gave him preeminence in the field" (002-008). His physical description emphasizes the athletic qualities valued in a catcher of the period.

A good catcher was a scarce and valuable resource. Dr. Selby noted that Al Heaton was "a first-rate catcher" and that there was "nobody like him" (004-007), while acknowledging that the only comparable talent in the region was Larry Boyne of the downriver nine, who was called "the famous catcher" (002-073).

## Catching Skill and Reputation

The reputation of a catcher extended beyond his own town. Sam Ellis of Jonesville was described as "the champion catcher of the whole region" (002-009), demonstrating how individual players could become known across a wide geographic area. Teams built their strategies around the strengths and weaknesses of their catchers. Catchers needed to be alert for foul tips, as when Morrison "went out on a foul tip to Al Heaton, catcher" (002-066).

During games, the catcher's role included fielding foul tips (002-066) and managing the defensive alignment. In the social hierarchy of the baseball club, the catcher was typically one of the most respected members.

## Notable Catchers in the Novel

- **Al Heaton**: Catcher for the Catalpa Nine, "stalwart" athlete
- **Sam Ellis**: "Champion catcher" of the Jonesville club (002-009)
- **Larry Boyne**: "Famous catcher of the downriver nine" (002-073)
- **Bill Van Orman**: Catcher for the Dean County Nine (003-004, 004-022)
