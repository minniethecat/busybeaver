# Judge Howell

> "I don't know enough about what we may call the etiquette of the game to decide what is an amateur and what a professional club." (003-011)

## Civic Leadership

Judge Howell was one of the most respected figures in [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]]. He lived in a fine residence on the north side of Stone River with his daughter [[alice-howell|Alice]], and his opinions carried weight throughout the community. When the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa club]] needed to reorganize after its humiliating defeat, it was Judge Howell whose endorsement mattered most.

## Baseball Patron

Despite claiming limited knowledge of [[national-game|baseball]]'s finer points, the Judge became a key patron of the reorganized club. His decisive statement — "if we are in earnest in this proposition to organize a really creditable [[national-game|baseball]] club in [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]]...we may as well make up our minds to put our hands into our pockets and help the boys a little, otherwise we shall go down again" (003-011) — committed the community's resources to the effort.

As "the oracle of the grocery store" noted, Howell "spoke the sentiments of the community" (003-013), and his advocacy gave the club legitimacy. His role was that of a 19th-century booster — providing financial backing and civic imprimatur rather than on-field expertise.

## Family

Judge Howell was the father of [[alice-howell|Alice Howell]], whose passion for [[national-game|baseball]] both amused and occasionally exasperated him. When [[alice-howell|Alice]] quoted [[national-game|baseball]] slang, the Judge "shuddered" (001-009) at the mixing of "the slang of the game with her mother's mode of speech." His affectionate tolerance of [[alice-howell|Alice]]'s [[national-game|baseball]] obsession humanized the stern figure of the town judge.
