# Wheeler

> "Deputy Sheriff Wheeler, forgetting for the time his official dignity" (002-070)

## Role in the Club

Wheeler played center field for the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville Nine]] and also served as Deputy Sheriff. His dual role as both a player and a law enforcement official added a distinctive character to the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville club]]. He was an enthusiastic supporter whose emotions were visibly on display during the game.

## Key Appearances

Wheeler appeared in the opening [[baseball-match|match]] against the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpas]] in Chapter 2. As the game progressed and "the prospects of the visitors visibly waned," Wheeler "went next to the [[baseball-equipment|bat]]" (002-033) in an attempt to rally his team. He later "made a capital safe hit to center field" (002-047) and "made first base on a ground [[baseball-equipment|ball]]" (002-050), contributing to the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville]] offense.

Defensively, Wheeler covered center field, and late in the game he was "put out on first base" (002-063) after reaching safely. His enthusiasm for the game was evident — at one point he "hurried up" and cried "Gosh all hemlock!" (011-057), and the narrator notes that "Deputy Sheriff Wheeler, forgetting for the time his official dignity" (002-070) became wholly absorbed in the [[baseball-match|contest]].

Wheeler also appeared as a spectator at the [[state-championship-catalpa-calumets|championship game]] in later chapters, positioned "far around on the other side" of the diamond (013-011). In the final game, he remarked of a player that "he died at first base" (017-012), a colorful piece of commentary typical of his expressive character.
