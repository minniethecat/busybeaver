# Alice Howell

> "Alice was an ardent lover of the American game, and her heart was set on the great match." (001-001)

## A Female Baseball Enthusiast

Alice Howell was the daughter of [[judge-howell|Judge Howell]] of [[north-catalpa|North Catalpa]] and one of the novel's central characters. She is introduced on the morning of the [[championship-baseball|championship]] [[baseball-match|match]], "flattening her pretty nose against the window pane as she looked ruefully out into the misty atmosphere" (001-001) — worried that bad weather might postpone the game.

Her passion for [[national-game|baseball]] was genuine and knowledgeable. "Alice was an ardent lover of the [[national-game|American game]]" (001-001), and she followed the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa club]]'s fortunes with keen interest. She was able to "mingle the slang of the game with her mother's mode of speech" (001-009), quoting [[ben-burton|Ben Burton]]'s colorful phrase about "a basket of goose eggs" (001-016).

## The Spectator's Perspective

Alice provides the reader's emotional connection to the games. Her reactions track the fortunes of the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]] — she bit her nails with anxiety when the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville]]s [[scoring-baseball|score]]d (002-032) and rejoiced when the home club rallied. Her father noted with affectionate exasperation that she knew "everything worth knowing about the game" (001-009).

She was not alone among women in her [[national-game|baseball]] knowledge. The Introduction notes that "even among the gentler sex, who may be regarded as [[baseball-spectators|spectators]] only of the game, there is to be found much sound information and an intelligent acquaintance with the details of [[national-game|baseball]] playing" (P01-001). Alice embodies this educated female spectator.

## Character and Conviction

Alice was not merely a passive observer — she had strong opinions about the club and its players. She ardently hoped for the [[championship-baseball|championship]] (006-019) and worried when the team's prospects dimmed: "I think we should have no chance for the [[championship-baseball|championship]], I am afraid" (006-020). Her emotional investment in the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]]'s success made her a representative of the community's hopes.
