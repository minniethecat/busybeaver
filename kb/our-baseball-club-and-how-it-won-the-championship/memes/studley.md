# Studley

> "Studley now made his second base" (002-047)

## Role in the Club

Studley played second base for the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville Nine]], the visiting club that faced the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpas]] in the novel's opening [[national-game|baseball]] [[baseball-match|match]]. He [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ted in the middle of the order and was an active contributor both offensively and defensively.

## Key Appearances

Studley's entire known performance comes from Chapter 2, during the game between the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville Nine]] and the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpas]]. In the field, he "put out" [[hart-stirling|Hart Stirling]] at second base "in excellent style" (002-036) and "beautifully caught" a [[baseball-equipment|ball]] on second base "amidst murmurs of applause" (002-041), demonstrating capable defensive skills.

At the plate, Studley made his way to "second base by a" hit (002-047) and later "sent a good [[baseball-equipment|ball]] to center field" (002-055). However, he also committed a [[fielding-baseball|fielding]] error, allowing a runner to reach "first base through the muffing of Studley" (002-049). He was eventually "caught out by" [[lew-morris|Lew Morris]] (002-061).

Studley [[scoring-baseball|score]]d a run when he and [[alexander|Alexander]] "were on the second and third bases" and were "brought home" by a teammate's hit (002-062). Late in the game, a [[baseball-equipment|ball]] "sent by Studley to [[sam-morrison|Morrison]]" at first base cut off a [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] runner (002-069).
