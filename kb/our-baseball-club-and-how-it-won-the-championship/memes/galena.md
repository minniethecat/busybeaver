The town of **Galena**, in Northern Illinois, was the home of the [[red-stockings|Red Stockings]] [[national-game|baseball]] club and the destination of the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]] for the [[championship-baseball|championship series]] of games. It was the most significant away-game location in the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa club]]'s season, hosting the [[baseball-match|match]]es that would determine the [[championship-baseball|championship]] of Northern Illinois.

## The Red Stockings and the Championship

Galena's [[national-game|baseball]] club, the [[red-stockings|Red Stockings]], were the ultimate opponents in the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]]'s campaign. The season was structured as a progression toward this decisive [[baseball-match|contest]]:

> From Sandy Key the Nine were to go to Bluford to play the famous "Zoo-zoo Nine," as they called themselves, of that city, and then they were to begin a struggle for the championship of Northern Illinois with the Red Stockings of Galena. How much depended on the result of the meeting of the Black Hawks and the Catalpas, you who have followed the career of a baseball nine can best reckon. (007-001)

The [[state-championship-catalpa-calumets|championship game]] at Galena was the culmination of the club's efforts and was watched with intense interest back in [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] (009-005).

## Quapaw House

While in Galena, the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] players lodged at the [[quapaw-house|Quapaw House]], a hotel that served as their headquarters during the [[championship-baseball|championship series]]:

> "The Judge is a brick!" said Larry Boyne, when this message was read to the members of the club, as they lounged in one of the bedrooms of Quapaw House, in Galena, where the boys were waiting to begin the championship series of games with the Red Stockings. (008-009)

## Galena Baseball Grounds

The [[state-championship-catalpa-calumets|championship game]]s were played at the Galena Baseball Grounds, a field placed at the disposal of the visiting [[catalpa-nine|Catalpas]]:

> By general consent, the meeting was broken up for the more important business of practice on the Galena Baseball Grounds, placed at the disposal of the visitors by the managers of the championship series. (008-018)

## News from Galena

The people of [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] followed the events in Galena with breathless attention, relying on telegrams and dispatches for news of the [[championship-baseball|championship]] [[baseball-match|contest]]. [[alice-howell|Alice Howell]]s received a telegram from [[al-heaton|Al Heaton]] reporting the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpas]]' strong start:

> Alice had received a telegram from Galena, and as Rough and Ready climbed the slope by the Judge's house, a sunny head was popped from one of its upper windows and Alice's cheerful voice cried, "Oh, Roughy—excuse me for calling you Roughy, but I'm so glad!—Albert Heaton has telegraphed to me that the Catalpas have made ten runs in the first three innings and the Galenas only one!" (009-013)

The [[scoring-baseball|score]]board from the [[championship-baseball|championship series]] recorded the final tally: "Galenas | 0 | 1 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0" (010-035), confirming the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]]'s triumph on their opponents' home grounds.
