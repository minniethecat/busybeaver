# Sam Ellis

> "Sam Ellis, the champion pitcher of the Jonesvilles, cannot hold a candle to Bill Van Orman." (003-004)

## Champion Pitcher

Sam Ellis was the ace [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]] for the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville Nine]] and the "champion [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]]" of the region (003-004). His reputation extended across multiple counties, and opposing clubs measured their own [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]]s against him. When the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa club]] de[[baseball-equipment|bat]]ed its [[reorganization-catalpa-club|reorganization]], [[neddie-ellis|Ellis]]'s name was invoked as the standard of excellence that [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] must [[baseball-match|match]].

## Key Appearances

[[neddie-ellis|Ellis]] featured prominently in the Chapter 2 [[baseball-match|match]] against [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]]. His pitching kept the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ters off balance, and the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville]] [[fielding-baseball|fielding]] backed him up effectively. "[[neddie-ellis|Ellis]], the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville]] [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]], made a muff with his [[baseball-equipment|ball]]" (002-049), showing that even the champion could make errors — but his overall performance was strong enough that [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville]] won decisively.

"[[neddie-ellis|Ellis]] shared his fate" (002-063) with [[sam-morrison|Morrison]] in being caught out on a [[foul-ball|foul fly]], showing that [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]]s of the era were expected to [[baseball-equipment|bat]] as well as pitch. His dual role as [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]] and [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ter was typical of 19th-century [[national-game|baseball]].
