**Charlie Webb** was the catcher for the [[calumets|Calumets]] [[national-game|baseball]] club, forming the [[baseball-equipment|bat]]tery with [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]] [[sam-morse|Sam Morse]] in the [[championship-baseball|championship]] [[baseball-match|contest]] against the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]]. His strong and accurate throwing arm was one of the [[calumets|Calumets]]' most effective defensive weapons, deterring and defeating stolen base attempts.

## Identity

Webb occupied the catcher's position for the [[calumets|Calumets]] and [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ted in the lineup (011-029). As [[catcher-baseball|catcher]], he was responsible for receiving Morse's intricate out-curves, preventing passed [[baseball-equipment|ball]]s, and controlling the opposition's running game. He was known among the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] players by the nickname "the catcher" (013-022).

## Defensive Prowess

Webb's throwing arm was his defining asset. His ability to throw out baserunners attempting to steal was a constant threat that shaped the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]]'s offensive strategy:

> Larry Boyne was the first man to the bat for the Catalpas in the sixth inning, and he secured his base on called balls, but fell before Charlie Webb's throwing, while trying to steal to the second bag. (011-052)

This caught-stealing was a pivotal moment — [[larry-boyne|Larry Boyne]], one of [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]]'s fastest runners, was erased from the basepaths by Webb's throw.

## In the Story

Webb was the first Calumet to [[baseball-equipment|bat]] in an early inning (011-047) and contributed to his team's offense. He also hit a [[baseball-equipment|ball]] that was fielded for a play at first base (011-053). His most memorable moment came when he "gayly faced 'tricky [[charlie-king|Charlie]]'" (011-062), approaching his at-[[baseball-equipment|bat]] with visible confidence despite the pressure of the [[championship-baseball|championship]] [[baseball-match|contest]].

As the [[calumets|Calumets]]' [[catcher-baseball|catcher]], Webb shared responsibility for the passed [[baseball-equipment|ball]]s that occasionally allowed [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] runners to advance. Yet his overall performance behind the plate earned him the respect of opponents who learned to think twice before testing his arm.
