# Catalpa Nine

> "The baseball club of Catalpa was made up of some of the finest young fellows in the town." (001-011)

## Founding and Identity

The Catalpa [[national-game|baseball]] club was founded by Civil War veterans — "survivors of the company that went out and came back, decimated as to numbers and not all sound in body" (001-011) — who returned to Catalpa and established the region's first [[national-game|baseball]] club. The members called themselves "The Catalpas" after their town.

The club was based on the north side of Stone River, the more affluent section of Catalpa. Their players were "students in the Seminary, young fellows in the law and county offices of the town, and sons of gentlemen of leisure" (001-013). This social composition led their rivals on the south side to call them "Aristocrats" (001-013), while the Catalpas retorted by calling their rivals "[[dean-county-nine|Stalwarts]]."

## Playing Style and Strengths

The Catalpas were "well chosen with an eye to symmetry and uniformity. They were all brawny and athletic young fellows" (002-008). Their players, "mostly men of leisure," had "plenty of time to [[baseball-training|practice]]" (002-008), giving them an advantage in preparation. [[al-heaton|Al Heaton]], the "stalwart [[catcher-baseball|catcher]]," was their star player and drew "the eyes of the multitude" (002-008).

## Competitive Record

The club achieved early success. They had "challenged and played two games" (001-014) against the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville club]] for the [[championship-baseball|championship]] of northern Illinois. But their record was mixed — the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville Nine]] had beaten them "by a [[scoring-baseball|score]] of 24 to 13 — an overwhelming defeat" (001-015) that precipitated a crisis and eventual [[reorganization-catalpa-club|reorganization]].

After the [[reorganization-catalpa-club|reorganization]], the Catalpas embarked on a tour "to begin a struggle for the [[championship-baseball|championship]] of Northern Illinois with the clubs down the river" (007-001). The team faced off against the [[black-hawks|Black Hawks]], the [[calumets|Calumets]], and other regional clubs in a [[championship-baseball|championship series]].

## Reorganization

Following the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville]] defeat, the community rallied behind the club. [[judge-howell|Judge Howell]] led the effort, declaring "if we are in earnest in this proposition to organize a really creditable [[national-game|baseball]] club in Catalpa...we may as well make up our minds to put our hands into our pockets and help the boys a little" (003-011). The "project took definite shape at once" (003-013), and a new, more competitive Catalpa Nine emerged.

## Rivalries

- **[[Jonesville-Nine]]**: Primary rival; [[baseball-match|contest]]ed the northern Illinois [[championship-baseball|championship]]
- **[[Dean-County-Nine]]**: Cross-town rival in Catalpa — the "[[dean-county-nine|Stalwarts]]" to Catalpa's "Aristocrats"
- **[[Calumets]]**: Downriver rival club
- **[[Black-Hawks]]**: Experienced club with semi-professional players
