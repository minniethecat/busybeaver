**[[sam-ellis|Sam]]uel "[[sam-ellis|Sam]]" Morse** was the [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]] for the [[calumets|Calumets]] [[national-game|baseball]] club and a central figure in the [[championship-baseball|championship]] [[baseball-match|contest]] against the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]]. Known for his deceptive curves and competitive spirit, Morse was an old schoolmate of several [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] players, adding a personal dimension to the rivalry between the two clubs.

## Identity

Morse was "an old school chum" of the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] players (011-006), a connection that made the [[championship-baseball|championship]] [[baseball-match|contest]] as much a reunion as a rivalry. He pitched for the [[calumets|Calumets]], the club representing the [[state-championship-catalpa-calumets|state championship]] claimants from the southern part of the region.

## Role as Pitcher

As the [[calumets|Calumets]]' [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]], Morse was the cornerstone of their [[fielding-baseball|defense]]. His signature pitch was the out-curve, a delivery that moved away from the [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ter and proved nearly impossible to hit:

> He was retired without hitting the ball, Sam Morse's out-curves being more intricate than anything that he had yet encountered. (011-046)

His pitching was described as "renowned," and [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ters approached the plate against him with visible apprehension (011-034). In the [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ting order, Morse occupied the ninth position in the lineup (011-023).

## In the Story

Morse's duel with [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]]'s [[charlie-king|Charlie King]] was the pitching centerpiece of the [[state-championship-catalpa-calumets|championship game]]. He faced the heart of the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ting order and held his own through the critical middle innings. At the plate, Morse contributed a base hit to the right field (011-041) but also [[strikeout-baseball|struck out]] at a crucial moment, leaving a runner stranded (011-053).
