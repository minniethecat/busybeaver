**Rob Peabody** (also called Robert) was the right fielder for the [[calumets|Calumets]] [[national-game|baseball]] club (011-025). He was recognized as "a good base runner" whose speed on the basepaths added a dimension to the [[calumets|Calumets]]' offense beyond his contributions at the plate and in the field.

## Identity

Peabody occupied the right field position for the [[calumets|Calumets]] and [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ted in their lineup. His reputation as a skilled baserunner meant that whenever he reached base, the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] [[fielding-baseball|defense]] had to be alert to the threat of a stolen base or an extra base taken on a hit (011-052).

## In the Story

Peabody was active throughout the [[state-championship-catalpa-calumets|championship game]]s. At the plate, he "secured first base on called [[baseball-equipment|ball]]s" (011-044) — demonstrating patience — and followed with a memorable hit:

> Rob Peabody followed and hit a liner to Neddie Ellis who misjudged the distance, and the ball went over his head and allowed Rob to make two bases, while Handy got safely home. (011-050)

This line drive was one of the key hits of the game, driving in a run and putting Peabody in scoring position.

In later games, Peabody "sent up a short fly" to the outfield (013-014), and his right field [[fielding-baseball|defense]] supported the [[calumets|Calumets]]' pitching staff. He was also the [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ter who benefited from a "sharp [[foul-ball|foul tip]]" by [[glenn-otto|Glenn Otto]] that kept him at the plate (011-051).
