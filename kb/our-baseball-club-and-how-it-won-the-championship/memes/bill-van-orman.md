# Bill Van Orman

> "Bill Van Orman, the big pitcher of the Dean County Nine." (002-034)

## A Rival Talent

Bill Van Orman was the primary [[catcher-baseball|catcher]] for the [[dean-county-nine|Dean County Nine]] and, by some accounts, the only [[catcher-baseball|catcher]] in the region who could compare to [[al-heaton|Al Heaton]]. [[dr-selby|Dr. Selby]] noted that "nobody like him, unless it is Bill Van Orman, the [[dean-county-nine|Dean County Nine]]'s [[catcher-baseball|catcher]]" (003-004) could [[baseball-match|match]] [[al-heaton|Heaton]]'s ability. This made Van Orman both a rival and a benchmark — the standard against which the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] star was measured.

Van Orman was physically imposing, described as "the big [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]]" (002-034), though he primarily caught for [[dean-county-nine|Dean County]]. His versatility — able to both pitch and catch — made him especially valuable to his club.

## In the Club Reorganization

When the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa club]] reorganized, Van Orman's name came up in discussions about the [[reorganization-catalpa-club|new nine]]. Someone noted that "Bill is to be [[catcher-baseball|catcher]] in the [[reorganization-catalpa-club|new nine]]" (004-026), suggesting that the reorganized [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa club]] sought to recruit him. Comparisons between Van Orman and [[al-heaton|Heaton]] were inevitable: "You are a great sight better [[catcher-baseball|catcher]] than Bill Van Orman" (004-022), one supporter told [[al-heaton|Al Heaton]], underscoring the rivalry between the two clubs' star [[catcher-baseball|catcher]]s.
