# Dean County Nine

> "The club on the south side of the river...arrogated to themselves the title of 'Catalpa's Champions.'" (001-011)

## The Town Rival

The Dean County Nine was the other [[national-game|baseball]] club in [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]], based on the south (business) side of Stone River. While the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]] drew from the affluent north side, the Dean County club represented "the men of more moderate means" (001-013) — working men, tradesmen, and those connected to the mills and lumberyards of the town's commercial district.

This social divide created a "chasm as wide as Stone River" (001-013) between the clubs. The Dean County players called the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpas]] "Aristocrats," and the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpas]] retorted with "Stalwarts" — the nickname by which the Dean County Nine became known.

## Notable Players

[[bill-van-orman|Bill Van Orman]] was the club's star — "the big [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]] of the Dean County Nine" (002-034) and also its [[catcher-baseball|catcher]]. [[dr-selby|Dr. Selby]] considered [[bill-van-orman|Van Orman]] the only [[catcher-baseball|catcher]] in the region comparable to [[al-heaton|Al Heaton]] (003-004). [[hank-jackson|Hank Jackson]], the "burly shortstop" (007-030), provided power and presence in the infield.

## Competitive Relationship

Despite the rivalry, the clubs were "drawn together by a common interest in the national game" (001-014). Friendly [[baseball-match|match]]es between them were regular occurrences, though the social tensions added edge to every [[baseball-match|contest]]. When the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa club]] reorganized for the [[championship-baseball|championship]] campaign, there was discussion of recruiting Dean County players into the unified team.
