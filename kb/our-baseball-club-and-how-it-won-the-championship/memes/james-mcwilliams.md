# James McWilliams

> "Mac had fire in his eye" (011-047)

## Role in the Club

James McWilliams played left field for the [[calumets|Calumets]], the Chicago-based club that faced the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]] in the [[championship-baseball|championship]] [[baseball-match|contest]]. He [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ted in the lower part of the order and was known by his teammates as "Mac" (011-047). His [[fielding-baseball|fielding]] was described as "wonderfully fine" in key moments of the game (013-052).

## Key Appearances

McWilliams featured prominently in the [[calumets|Calumets]]–[[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] [[state-championship-catalpa-calumets|championship game]], spanning chapters 11 through 17. His first at-[[baseball-equipment|bat]] ended in a strikeout against [[charlie-king|Charlie King]], the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]] whom McWilliams had vowed to "take the conceit out of" (011-039). He [[strikeout-baseball|struck out]] again in his second appearance, retiring on [[strikeout-baseball|strikes]] "after six [[baseball-equipment|ball]]s were charged against him" (011-047), having boasted he would "eat clover for a week" if he failed to hit safely.

In the field, McWilliams was more successful. He "captured" a skyscraper hit by [[hiram-porter|Hiram Porter]] in "wonderfully fine style" (013-052) and made a difficult running catch described as the "lithe and agile McWilliams" capturing the [[baseball-equipment|ball]] "after a hard run" (013-055). He also caught fly [[baseball-equipment|ball]]s from [[al-heaton|Al Heaton]] (017-019) and handled several other defensive chances.

In the seventh inning, McWilliams hit a short [[ground-ball|grounder]] to [[hart-stirling|Hart Stirling]] at second base, resulting in "a very pretty double play" by [[hart-stirling|Stirling]] and [[hiram-porter|Hiram Porter]] (011-053). Late in the game, McWilliams "went out on a [[foul-ball|foul fly]] to 'The Lily'" ([[bill-van-orman|Bill Van Orman]]) (017-018), and was retired at first base alongside [[charlie-webb|Charlie Webb]] (017-023).
