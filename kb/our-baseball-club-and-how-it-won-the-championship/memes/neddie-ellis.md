# Neddie Ellis

> "Neddie is as good a pitcher as there is in the country." (004-013)

## Role in the Club

Neddie Ellis was a [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]] for the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]] and a versatile contributor to the club. [[dr-selby|Dr. Selby]] declared him "as good a [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]] as there is in the country" (004-013), a strong endorsement of his ability. Ellis's pitching complemented [[al-heaton|Al Heaton]]'s catching, forming a capable [[baseball-equipment|bat]]tery for the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa club]].

Ellis also contributed with the [[baseball-equipment|bat]]. In the [[the-great-match|Jonesville match]], he ended one inning when he "went out on a [[foul-ball|foul fly]]" (002-063), but his overall value to the team was never in question. When the club reorganized, Ellis was among the players retained for the [[reorganization-catalpa-club|new nine]].

## Key Appearances

Ellis featured in the Chapter 2 [[baseball-match|match]] against [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville]] and continued with the club through its reformation and [[championship-baseball|championship]] campaign. He was part of the traveling squad that toured the downriver clubs, and his pitching was counted among the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa club]]'s strengths.

## Playing Style

As a [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]] in 1880s [[national-game|baseball]], Ellis would have thrown underhand or sidearm, focusing on control and placement rather than velocity. The fact that he was described as "as good a [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]] as there is in the country" (004-013) indicates that even in an era when pitching was less specialized, exceptional talent was recognized and valued.
