# Simpson

> "Simpson now made first base on a hit" (002-066)

## Role in the Club

Simpson played third base for the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville Nine]], the visiting team that faced the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpas]] in the novel's opening [[national-game|baseball]] [[baseball-match|match]]. He [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ted in the lower portion of the order and contributed both offensively and defensively to his club's effort.

## Key Appearances

Simpson's known performance is confined to Chapter 2. His first at-[[baseball-equipment|bat]] came when he "struck the [[baseball-equipment|ball]] straight into the" field and reached base (002-043). In a later at-[[baseball-equipment|bat]], "after two [[strikeout-baseball|strikes]]," he advanced a runner before going "out on a [[foul-ball|foul tip]] leaving Simpson on the base and closing the inning" (002-049).

He struck again in a later inning but was "put out on first base" (002-061). However, Simpson returned in a later inning and "now made first base on a hit" (002-066), which contributed to the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville Nine]]'s scoring as the inning brought home [[neddie-ellis|Ellis]] and [[wheeler|Wheeler]].

Defensively, at third base, Simpson handled a [[baseball-equipment|ball]] hit by [[will-sprague|Will Sprague]] and [[john-brubaker|John Brubaker]] — the [[baseball-equipment|ball]] went "first by [[martin|Martin]] and then by Simpson" (002-060), suggesting either a sequence of [[fielding-baseball|fielding]] chances or a [[baseball-equipment|ball]] that got past both infielders.
