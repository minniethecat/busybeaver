**Jamie Kennedy** was an infielder for the [[calumets|Calumets]] [[national-game|baseball]] club who distinguished himself through his study of opposing [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]]s and his ability to share tactical intelligence with his teammates. His understanding of [[charlie-king|Charlie King]]'s curves made him a valuable asset to the [[calumets|Calumets]] beyond his contributions in the field and at [[baseball-equipment|bat]].

## Identity

Kennedy played in the infield for the [[calumets|Calumets]] and was regarded as a thinking player — someone who "assumed to know a little about the mysteries of [[charlie-king|King]]'s curves" (011-036). His [[national-game|baseball]] intelligence complemented the physical skills of his teammates.

## Baseball Intelligence

Kennedy's most notable contribution to the [[calumets|Calumets]] was his study of [[charlie-king|Charlie King]]'s pitching. Before the [[calumets|Calumets]]' second inning, he shared observations that helped his teammates prepare for [[charlie-king|King]]'s deceptive deliveries:

> The Calumets now went to work with a will at the beginning of their second inning, and, after receiving some hints from Jamie Kennedy, who assumed to know a little about the mysteries of King's curves. (011-036)

## In the Story

Kennedy was active throughout the [[championship-baseball|championship]] [[baseball-match|contest]]s. He benefited from a passed [[baseball-equipment|ball]] that allowed a runner to advance (011-037) and handled defensive chances cleanly, including [[fielding-baseball|fielding]] a [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ted [[baseball-equipment|ball]] readily at his infield position (011-049).

At the plate, Kennedy tried his best to produce for the [[calumets|Calumets]] (011-052) but experienced the same frustrations as his teammates against the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpas]]' pitching. In later games, he made a remark about the opposing team's performance: "I am sure they would get a good lead on them" (013-031), reflecting his analytical approach to the game.

Defensively, he made a critical error — muffing a thrown [[baseball-equipment|ball]] (013-034) — but also handled difficult plays, including [[fielding-baseball|fielding]] a sharply hit [[baseball-equipment|ball]] "finely" (013-021), demonstrating the inconsistency that marked the [[calumets|Calumets]]' play under pressure.
