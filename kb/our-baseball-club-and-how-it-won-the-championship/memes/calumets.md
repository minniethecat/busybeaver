# Calumets

> "The Calumets are the champions of the State." (007-046)

## A Famous Chicago Nine

The Calumets were a prominent [[national-game|baseball]] club from Chicago and the "champions of the State" (007-046). Their reputation extended throughout Illinois — when their captain visited the Stone River region, the news "evoked much enthusiasm among the [[national-game|baseball]] connoisseurs of [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]]" (007-045). The Calumets represented the pinnacle of amateur [[national-game|baseball]] excellence, the standard against which all regional clubs measured themselves.

## The State Championship

The Calumets served as the ultimate test for the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa club]]. A [[baseball-match|match]] between them and the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]], scheduled as "the State Championship" (010-042), was the climax of the novel's competitive arc. The prospect of facing the "famous Chicago nine" (007-047) was both thrilling and terrifying for the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] players and their supporters.

[[alice-howell|Alice Howell]] captured the excitement: "I am so happy that our nine will play the Calumets, papa" (010-043). For a small-town club to challenge the state champions was the achievement of a dream.

## Reputation

The Calumets' status as "famous" (007-045, 007-047) made them a measuring stick. When their captain scouted talent in the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] region, it was a sign that the small-town clubs were on the [[national-game|baseball]] map. The Calumets' players — including [[sam-ellis|Sam]]uel Morse, their [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]] (011-006) — were seasoned competitors who provided the stiffest test the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpas]] would face.
