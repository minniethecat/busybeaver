**Tom "Tommy" Shoff** was an outfielder for the [[calumets|Calumets]] [[national-game|baseball]] club who played a significant role in the [[championship-baseball|championship]] [[baseball-match|contest]] against the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]]. He contributed both in the field and at [[baseball-equipment|bat]], handling fly [[baseball-equipment|ball]]s and delivering key hits for his club.

## Identity

Shoff played in the outfield for the [[calumets|Calumets]] and [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ted in their lineup. His [[fielding-baseball|fielding]] was reliable — he handled difficult fly [[baseball-equipment|ball]]s and made plays that kept the [[calumets|Calumets]] in contention during the [[championship-baseball|championship series]] (011-043). He was active throughout the games, appearing in multiple innings and situations.

## In the Story

At the plate, Shoff had mixed results. He "sent the [[baseball-equipment|ball]] in the direction of [[ben-burton|Ben Burton]] at shortstop," reaching base when the [[baseball-equipment|ball]] was fumbled (011-037). He went to the [[baseball-equipment|bat]]sman's square with runners on base (013-014) and contributed a single that sent a runner in, making "the [[scoring-baseball|score]] even at three and three" (013-016) — a crucial hit that kept the [[calumets|Calumets]] in the game.

In the field, Shoff caught fly [[baseball-equipment|ball]]s for outs, including retiring [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ters on "flies to Tom Shoff" (011-053). His outfield play complemented the [[calumets|Calumets]]' infield [[fielding-baseball|defense]] and supported [[sam-morse|Sam Morse]]'s pitching.
