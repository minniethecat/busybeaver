# Agricultural Fair Grounds

> "A dead level plain, admirably adapted for a baseball field." (002-001)

## From Fair Ground to Ball Field

The Agricultural Fair Grounds, located just above [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] and "lying to the southward" (002-001), was originally built for agricultural exhibitions. But the "Northern District fair had gone farther North, and the grounds were left to chance comers — a travelling circus, or an occasional amateur racing [[baseball-match|match]]" (002-001). When the [[national-game|baseball]] clubs of [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] began to [[baseball-training|practice]] within the enclosure, the grounds found a new purpose.

The location was ideal for [[national-game|baseball]]: "one reaches a lovely stretch of undulating ground skirted by a dead level plain, admirably adapted for a [[national-game|baseball]] field" (002-001). A "high board fence bounded the grassy lawn" (011-004), enclosing the field of play.

## The Great Matches

The Fair Grounds hosted the most important [[baseball-match|match]]es in the novel. On game days, [[baseball-spectators|spectators]] arrived by "strings of buggies, lumber-wagons and other vehicles" (002-001), transforming the quiet grounds into a bustling social center. The [[championship-baseball|championship]] [[baseball-match|match]] between the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]] and the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville club]] was played here, and the grounds witnessed both triumph and humiliation for the home club.

## Community Hub

More than just a [[national-game|baseball]] venue, the Fair Grounds served as [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]]'s public gathering place. "The mass of the [[baseball-spectators|spectators]] were grouped together in the enclosure" (002-002), and the grounds became the town's de facto commons. When "the [[baseball-equipment|ball]] clubs of [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] began to [[baseball-training|practice]] within the enclosure" (002-001), the grounds transitioned from agricultural exhibition space to the heart of the community's sporting life.
