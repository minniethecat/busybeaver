**John Brubaker** was the right fielder for the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]], occupying a key outfield position throughout the club's season. He contributed both at the plate and in the field, hitting multiple [[home-run|home runs]] and making important defensive plays during the [[championship-baseball|championship]] campaign.

## Identity

Brubaker held down the right field position for the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpas]], [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ting in the lower portion of the lineup. He was one of the steady contributors whose all-around play — hitting, [[fielding-baseball|fielding]], and base running — helped carry the club through the season [002-016, 004-036].

## Role on the Nine

As right fielder, Brubaker patrolled the outfield and was responsible for catching fly [[baseball-equipment|ball]]s and [[fielding-baseball|fielding]] hits to his territory:

> He sent a magnificent air ball so high that it seemed lost in the clouds before it began its descent to earth, and then fell straight into the hands of John Brubaker in the right field. (002-063)

His strong and accurate throwing arm made him a defensive asset, and opposing baserunners respected his ability to cut down advances.

## In the Story

Brubaker was one of the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]]'s most productive hitters. He hit a home run during a key game alongside [[hiram-porter|Hiram Porter]] (002-064), demonstrating power that belied his position in the [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ting order. He also contributed a hit that drove in [[hart-stirling|Hart Stirling]] (002-036).

On the basepaths, Brubaker showed aggression, though not always successfully:

> John Brubaker, in attempting to steal from first to second base, was put out. (002-065)

He reached base on multiple occasions and was involved in several scoring rallies for the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpas]]. His consistency in the field and at the plate made him a reliable presence throughout the season.
