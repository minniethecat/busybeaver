# Hart Stirling

> "Thus closed the innings — two runs for the visiting Nine, by Hart Stirling, the second baseman of the Catalpas." (002-033)

## Role in the Club

Hart Stirling played second base for the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]], forming the middle infield alongside shortstop [[ben-burton|Ben Burton]]. His name carries a sense of solidity — "Stirling" suggesting steadiness — and his role as second baseman required quick hands to turn double plays and field ground [[baseball-equipment|ball]]s to the right side of the infield.

Stirling was involved in key defensive moments. In a later [[baseball-match|match]], [[tom-shoff|Shoff]] "hit a [[ground-ball|grounder]] to Hart Stirling, at second base" (011-044), testing his [[fielding-baseball|fielding]] under pressure. His position between first and second base made him critical to the infield [[fielding-baseball|defense]].

## Key Appearances

Stirling appears in the game accounts, most notably in the Chapter 2 [[baseball-match|match]] against [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville]]. The closing of the innings is narrated with reference to his play (002-033). He also participated in the scoring — the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpas]] "[[scoring-baseball|score]]d three [[scoring-baseball|runs]] in their inning, Hart Stirling crossing the home plate" (002-060), confirming his offensive contributions.

## In the Narrative

Stirling represents one of the "brawny and athletic young fellows" (002-008) who comprised the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa club]]. He was not a star like [[al-heaton|Heaton]] or a charismatic figure like [[larry-boyne|Boyne]], but his consistent presence was part of what made the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]] competitive. His grounding out to first and scoring [[scoring-baseball|runs]] in the same game captures the rhythm of a player's day in 19th-century [[national-game|baseball]].
