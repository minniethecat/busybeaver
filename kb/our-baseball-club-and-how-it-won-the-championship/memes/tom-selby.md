## Identity

Tom Selby was the son of [[dr-selby|Dr. Selby]], the town apothecary, and one of the rising young athletes of [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]]. A versatile sportsman — a [[national-game|baseball]] player, the best oarsman on Stone River, and strong enough to lift a barrel of flour — [[tom-shoff|Tom]] embodied the physical vigor that [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]]'s [[national-game|baseball]] culture celebrated. He played for the [[dean-county-nine|Dean County Nine]], the club from which the unified [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]] drew several of its key players.

> PN: (003-003)

> Tom Selby was not only a wiry and agile player in the field, but he was the best oarsman on the river, and he could lift a barrel of flour, properly shook down, with one hand — a feat that gave him an honorable distinction in that neighborhood.

## Role

### Athlete and Strongman

[[tom-shoff|Tom]]'s reputation rested on multiple physical accomplishments. On the [[national-game|baseball]] field, he was known for his agility and wiry build. On Stone River, he was the preeminent oarsman — a distinction that carried social weight in a river town like [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]]. And his feat of strength — lifting a barrel of flour with one hand — had earned him "honorable distinction in that neighborhood," marking him as a local strongman of note.

> PN: (012-027)

### Son of the Doctor

[[tom-shoff|Tom]]'s relationship with his father was one of mutual pride. [[dr-selby|Dr. Selby]] defended his son's athletic endeavors when others criticized the less aristocratic [[dean-county-nine|Dean County club]], and [[tom-shoff|Tom]]'s successes reflected well on the apothecary's household. The Doctor spoke of his son's accomplishments with evident satisfaction.

> PN: (014-004)

### Dean County Nine Player

As a member of the [[dean-county-nine|Dean County Nine]], [[tom-shoff|Tom]] was part of the rival local club that eventually contributed players to the unified [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]]. His presence on the [[dean-county-nine|Dean County]] roster represented the broader [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]]-area talent pool from which the [[championship-baseball|championship]] team was assembled.

## In the Story

Tom Selby appears throughout the novel as both a participant in and a symbol of [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]]'s athletic culture. He is mentioned as a rising player when [[dr-selby|Dr. Selby]] defends the [[dean-county-nine|Dean County club]] against criticism. He is invoked as a threat when Dan is told to "go and tell Tom Selby that I want him to lick the first fellow that says anything against our nine" — suggesting [[tom-shoff|Tom]]'s reputation as an enforcer of local loyalty.

[[tom-shoff|Tom]]'s most memorable appearance comes during the [[championship-baseball|championship]] celebration, when he shouts exultantly to his father as [[judge-howell|the Judge]]'s carriage arrives:

> "And now for the championship of the State, dad?" shouted Tom Selby, exultingly, as his father descended from the carriage of the Judge.

In this moment, [[tom-shoff|Tom]] represents the younger generation of [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] — those for whom the [[national-game|baseball]] [[championship-baseball|championship]] was not merely entertainment but a defining event, a source of pride that transcended the ordinary rhythms of small-town life.
