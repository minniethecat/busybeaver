# The Catalpa Club's First Road Trip

> "It was an impressive occasion when the Catalpa club started on their first pilgrimage abroad." (007-001)

## Departure

The Catalpa Nine's first road trip was a milestone in the club's history. After the reorganization, the club embarked on a tour to "begin a struggle for the championship of Northern Illinois with the clubs down the river" (007-001). The departure was "an impressive occasion" — the entire town turned out to see the players off, and the sense of embarking on a great adventure was palpable.

The tour was described as "their first pilgrimage abroad" (007-001), the use of "pilgrimage" conveying both the seriousness of the undertaking and the almost religious devotion the community felt toward its baseball club.

## The Competition

The road trip brought the Catalpa club into contact with teams they had only heard about. The Black Hawks of Sandy Key were the most formidable opponent — players "renowned as fielders" with "a reputation that inspired terror among the baseball players of the region" (007-002). These were "experienced amateurs and professional players" (007-002) who represented a higher level of competition than the Catalpa Nine had faced before.

Other matches on the tour tested the club's depth and resilience. The players encountered different playing styles, different field conditions, and the pressures of performing away from their home crowd.

## Growth and Learning

The road trip was a formative experience for the Catalpa club. Win or lose, each match taught lessons that could not be learned in practice games at the Fair Grounds. Hank Jackson, the "burly shortstop of the Dean County Nine" (007-030), was among those who watched the Catalpa players with new respect after their road performances.

The trip also produced memorable individual performances. Ben Burton, at shortstop, "had many opportunities to do himself credit" (007-044), and the despatches sent home by Al Heaton and the other players were eagerly consumed by the Catalpa faithful.
