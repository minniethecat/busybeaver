# John Handy

> "Every eye was fixed on John Handy, as he went to the bat for the Calumets." (013-054)

## Role in the Club

John Handy played third base for the [[calumets|Calumets]], the Chicago-based rival of the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]]. He had a reputation as "a slugger" and was known for his aggressive approach at the plate (011-042). Handy [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ted in a critical spot in the [[calumets|Calumets]]' lineup and fielded the hot corner at third base.

## Key Appearances

Handy's most significant action came in the fifth and sixth innings of the [[state-championship-catalpa-calumets|championship game]]. In the fifth inning, he "hit the [[baseball-equipment|ball]], the first that was delivered him, and it went like a rocket to [[larry-boyne|Larry Boyne]] at third base," resulting in a double play when [[larry-boyne|Boyne]] made "a difficult one-hand catch" (011-042). The play was notable because Ayres had vacated third base "without once dreaming that [[larry-boyne|Larry]] would be able to capture the [[baseball-equipment|ball]]" (011-042).

In the sixth inning, [[darius-ayres|Captain Darius]] Ayres told Handy, "This is our lucky inning. Here, take my [[baseball-equipment|bat]] for luck" (011-050). Using Ayres' [[baseball-equipment|bat]], Handy "called for a high [[baseball-equipment|ball]], caught [[charlie-king|King]] off his guard as he struck, and so secured a good hit on the very first [[baseball-equipment|ball]], and made first base" (011-050). He then [[scoring-baseball|score]]d when [[rob-peabody|Rob Peabody]] hit a liner that [[neddie-ellis|Neddie Ellis]] misjudged, putting the [[calumets|Calumets]] ahead 3–2 (011-050).

Handy's [[fielding-baseball|fielding]] at third base was less consistent. He "failed to master" a sharp [[ground-ball|grounder]] from [[hiram-porter|Hiram Porter]] (013-021) and committed an error that "left the three bases occupied" (013-029). However, he also made a "lightning throw to first base" on a ground [[baseball-equipment|ball]] from [[sam-morrison|Sam Morrison]] (013-047). In the late innings, Handy "struck three times the unsubstantial air" (013-051) and later hit a skyscraper to [[neddie-ellis|Neddie Ellis]] (017-009).
