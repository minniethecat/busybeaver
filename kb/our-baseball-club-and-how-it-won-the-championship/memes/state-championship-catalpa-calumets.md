# State Championship: Catalpa vs. Calumets

> "I am so happy that our nine will play the Calumets, papa." (010-043)

## The Dream Match

The state championship match between the Catalpa Nine and the Chicago Calumets was the realization of a dream for the small-town club. The Calumets were "the champions of the State" (007-046), a "famous Chicago nine" (007-047) whose reputation extended throughout Illinois. For the Catalpa club to earn the right to challenge them was itself a triumph.

Alice Howell captured the sentiment of the entire community: "I am so happy that our nine will play the Calumets, papa" (010-043). The prospect filled the town with nervous anticipation — this was not merely a game but a chance to prove that a small-town club could compete at the highest level.

## The Match

The championship contest played out over two chapters. "It was arranged between the Catalpa nine and the Calumet club for the State Championship" (010-042), with the Black Hawks providing the umpire (011-010) — Sam Ayres, who "took his place as umpire" (011-010).

The game was a tense, closely fought affair. The Calumets' pitcher Samuel Morse (011-006) delivered "puzzling curves" (011-035) that challenged the Catalpa batters. "A trifling error would tie the game" (011-055), and the pressure mounted with every inning.

The Catalpa players rose to the occasion. Ben Burton, playing shortstop, was a defensive anchor. Al Heaton, behind the plate, managed the pitchers and contributed at bat. The game featured spectacular fielding — a Calumet infielder "fielded it quickly and caught Neddie at second" (011-043) — and dramatic strikeouts: "Sam Morrison struck out, and Neddie Ellis ended the inning" (011-052).

## The Victory

The Catalpa Nine prevailed in a close contest, securing the state championship and completing their journey from humiliated also-rans to champions. The victory validated the community's investment in the club, Judge Howell's leadership, and the players' dedication through the long season.

The novel's final chapters celebrate the triumph, with the club's faithful — including Mike Costigan, "who had a holiday" (017-027) — joining in the celebration. The state championship was the culmination of the arc that began with the humiliating loss to Jonesville and passed through reorganization, training, road trips, and the exposure of the conspiracy.
