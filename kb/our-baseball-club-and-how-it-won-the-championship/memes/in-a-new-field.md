**In a New Field** refers to the Catalpa Nine's first game of the championship series in Chicago, played in late October. The event marked the club's transition from small-town matches to a professional-caliber contest in an unfamiliar urban setting, testing both their skills and their composure.

## Arrival in Chicago

The Catalpa players arrived in Chicago with a mixture of anxiety and determination. Larry Boyne was particularly unsettled by the presence of so many hometown spectators:

> "I wish so many of the Catalpa folks had not come in to see the game, today," said Larry Boyne, discontentedly, on the morning of the first of the championship series of games in Chicago, late in the following October. "It is bad enough to feel like a cat in a strange garret as I do here, without the added embarrassment of being watched by the townspeople." (011-001)

Captain Hiram Porter attempted to lift his spirits, hinting at the encouraging presence of a certain young lady in the stands (011-002).

## The Professional Venue

The Chicago grounds were unlike anything the Catalpa players had experienced. The enclosed professional park featured a grandstand, uncovered seating, and a high board fence surrounding the field — a far cry from the open agricultural fairgrounds where they usually played (011-004).

> When the nine, with beating hearts and quickened pulses, entered the grounds on the day so fraught with importance to them, they were a little dumbfounded by the size of the crowd and the professional character of the surroundings. (011-005)

The scale and formality of the venue represented a new level of competition for the club.

## A Test of Character

Playing "in a new field" was both literal and metaphorical. The Catalpas faced not only their opponents on the diamond but also the psychological challenge of performing in an intimidating environment before a crowd that included their own townspeople, whose expectations weighed heavily upon them. The game in Chicago tested whether the club could rise above its small-town origins and compete at a higher level.
