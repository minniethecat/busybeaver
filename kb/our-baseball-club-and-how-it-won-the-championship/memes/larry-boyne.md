# Larry Boyne

> "Larry Boyne, a fresh-faced and laughing young fellow." (002-009)

## Role on the Field

Larry Boyne was [[charlie-webb|the catcher]] for the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville Nine]] during the [[championship-baseball|championship]] [[baseball-match|match]] against [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]]. His reputation preceded him — bystanders remarked that "nobody on the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville]]rs, 'cept it is Larry Boyne, that can catch a [[baseball-equipment|ball]]" (002-004). Later in the narrative, he appears as "the famous [[catcher-baseball|catcher]] of the downriver nine" (002-073), suggesting he moved between clubs or that his reputation had spread.

As [[catcher-baseball|catcher]], Boyne was involved in critical defensive plays. When he "attempted to throw [[ben-burton|Burton]] out at second base" (002-049), his powerful arm was on display. He also [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ted effectively, sending up "a winged [[baseball-equipment|ball]] which he sent flying to the right field" (002-050) to drive in two [[scoring-baseball|runs]] and reach second base.

## Key Appearances

Boyne's most active appearance is in Chapter 2 during the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville]]-[[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] [[baseball-match|match]]. He went to [[baseball-equipment|bat]] multiple times: "Larry Boyne, smiling, but keenly alert, went to the [[baseball-equipment|bat]]" (002-047), and later "Larry Boyne went to the [[baseball-equipment|bat]] in the sixth" (002-063). His at-[[baseball-equipment|bat]]s were consequential — his "grand hit to the center field" (002-062) advanced baserunners.

In the field, Boyne was involved in several plays. Though his throw to second base didn't retire [[ben-burton|Burton]], his overall performance demonstrated why he was considered one of the premier [[catcher-baseball|catcher]]s in the region. He even showed his defensive range by catching [[foul-ball|foul tip]]s — "Larry tipped a [[foul-ball|foul fly]] which [[al-heaton|Al Heaton]] caught" (002-055) — though this instance shows him as a [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ter, it confirms his [[national-game|baseball]] instincts.

## Reputation

Boyne's style was described in contrast to the more serious [[al-heaton|Al Heaton]]. He was "fresh-faced and laughing" (002-009), suggesting a more easygoing demeanor that nevertheless concealed competitive intensity. His fame as "the famous [[catcher-baseball|catcher]] of the downriver nine" made him a known quantity throughout the [[national-game|baseball]] circles of northern Illinois.
