# Martin

> "Martin cleverly made his home run" (002-046)

## Role in the Club

Martin played left field for the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville Nine]], the visiting team that faced the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpas]] in the novel's opening [[national-game|baseball]] [[baseball-match|match]]. He [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ted in the middle of the order and was noted as "the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville]] left fielder" (002-043) when the second inning began.

## Key Appearances

Martin's performance is recorded entirely in Chapter 2. He started the second inning for [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville]], coming to [[baseball-equipment|bat]] as "the second inning began with Martin, the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville]] left fielder" (002-043). He reached "first base on called [[baseball-equipment|ball]]s" and then "cleverly made his home run, scoring" on a teammate's hit, demonstrating heads-up [[base-running|baserunning]] (002-046).

In a later inning, Martin "got to first base on a slow [[baseball-equipment|ball]]" (002-048) and then "sent a slow [[baseball-equipment|ball]] to center field" (002-050) as a hitter. He also contributed to the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville]] scoring when, "on a center field [[baseball-equipment|ball]] hit," runners came home (002-066).

Defensively, Martin had a difficult moment when a [[baseball-equipment|ball]] was "muffed shamefully, first by Martin and then by [[simpson|Simpson]]" (002-060), a sequence of two [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville]] errors that allowed [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] baserunners to advance. Despite this miscue, Martin remained in the game. He was eventually "put out" after [[wheeler|Wheeler]] had reached base ahead of him (002-061).
