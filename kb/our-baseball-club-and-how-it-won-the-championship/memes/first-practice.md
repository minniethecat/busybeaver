The **First Practice** of the Catalpa Baseball Club marked the formal opening of the club's outdoor season. The event encompassed both the initial outdoor training sessions beginning in early April and the first practice game — an away match against the Black Hawk Nine of Sandy Key — that served as the inaugural test of the newly reorganized Catalpa Nine. This practice game was the first stop on a three-city road trip that would define the early-season trajectory of the club and set the tone for their championship pursuit.

## Background

The Catalpa club had spent the winter months organizing and preparing for the summer campaign. By early April, outdoor practice was sanctioned, though active match play would not begin until the first of May (006-001). The club's players — mostly men of leisure with ample time to train — had been carefully selected "with an eye to symmetry and uniformity," and the townspeople of Catalpa held high expectations for the season ahead (002-008).

The club's primary motivation was not financial. As the narrative records:

> They were burning to retrieve the reputation of "Old Catalpa" as they called their town, albeit it was one of the youngest in Northern Illinois. (006-001)

Judge Howell, whose opinions commanded wide respect in Catalpa, had publicly articulated the prevailing philosophy: "What we want, gentlemen, is muscle and training. I am confident that in this good town of Catalpa there are more than nine young men who can give time to the practice necessary for the purpose, and who are endowed by nature with the requisite powers for the development of first-rate baseball players." (003-005)

## The Practice Game at Sandy Key

### Significance

The practice match against the [[black-hawks|Black Hawk Nine]] of Sandy Key was felt to be one of the most important contests of the early season. It was "partly for practice, and partly to test the material of the new nine" (007-001). The game launched a three-city tour:

| Stop | Opponent | Location | Purpose |
|------|----------|----------|---------|
| 1 | Black Hawk Nine | Sandy Key | Practice game — test new lineup |
| 2 | Zoo-zoo Nine | Bluford | Exhibition match |
| 3 | Red Stockings | Galena | Championship series opener |

> It was an impressive occasion when the Catalpa club started on their first pilgrimage. They had arranged a practice game with the Black Hawk Nine, of Sandy Key, in the central part of the State, to begin the season with. (007-001)

### Venue: The Agricultural Fair Grounds

The Catalpa club held its outdoor practice sessions at the [[agricultural-fair-grounds|Agricultural Fair Grounds]], a repurposed exhibition space just above the town. The grounds lay on a "lovely stretch of undulating ground skirted by a dead level plain, admirably adapted for a baseball field" (002-001). The Northern District fair had moved farther north, leaving the enclosure available for baseball clubs and the occasional traveling circus or amateur racing match.

## Key Personnel

The practice period was crucial for establishing the team's lineup and assessing player readiness:

- **[[al-heaton|Al Heaton]]**, catcher — the stalwart around whom the team's defense was built; described as "a tall, shapely young fellow, with a ruddy and oval face, bright brown eyes, a keen glance, and a sinewy length of limb that gave him preeminence in the field" (002-008)
- **[[charlie-king|Charlie King]]**, pitcher — whose "wonderfully clever" pitching would prove decisive in close contests
- **[[lew-morris|Lew Morris]]**, captain — who "went among the boys and impressed on them something of his own cheerful courage" (002-055)
- **[[hiram-porter|Hiram Porter]]**, first baseman — an honor student at Ann Arbor whose powerful batting could "retrieve the failing fortunes" of the club (002-041)

## Aftermath

The practice game at Sandy Key initiated a sequence of away matches that tested the Catalpa Nine's readiness for championship play. How much depended on the result of this opening contest — as the narrator observes — "you who have followed the career of a baseball nine can best reckon" (007-001). The experience gained during these early practice outings would prove essential when the club later faced its rivals, the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville Nine]], [[dean-county-nine|Dean County Nine]], and the [[calumets|Calumets]], in the championship campaign.
