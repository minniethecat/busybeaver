# Jonesville Nine

> "The Jonesvillians play a much better fielding game than the Catalpas." (002-059)

## A Formidable Rival

The Jonesville Nine was the principal rival of the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa club]] and the holder of the northern Illinois [[championship-baseball|championship]]. Their "[[fielding-baseball|fielding]]...was far superior to what their previous encounters had shown" (002-009), indicating a club that raised its level of play for important [[baseball-match|match]]es. The club boasted two of the region's premier players: [[sam-ellis|Sam Ellis]], the "champion [[pitcher-baseball|pitcher]]" (003-004), and [[larry-boyne|Larry Boyne]], "the famous [[catcher-baseball|catcher]] of the downriver nine" (002-073).

## Championship Pedigree

The Jonesville club had "won the [[championship-baseball|championship]] of the Northern District" (002-039), a title they defended against challengers including the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpas]]. Their victory over [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] by a [[scoring-baseball|score]] of "24 to 13 — an overwhelming defeat" (001-015) demonstrated their superiority at the opening of the novel. The team's success was built on superior [[fielding-baseball|fielding]] and the pitching-catching combination of [[neddie-ellis|Ellis]] and [[larry-boyne|Boyne]].

## Playing Style

The Jonesvillians were known for their defensive excellence. "The Jonesvillians play a much better [[fielding-baseball|fielding game]]" (002-059) became the simple explanation for their success. Their [[fielding-baseball|fielding]] was described as "far superior" (002-009), and their ability to catch [[foul-ball|foul]] flies and convert defensive chances put constant pressure on opposing [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ters.
