# The Conspiracy

> "The Conspiracy Laid Open" — Chapter 16 title

## A Threat to the Club

As the Catalpa Nine pursued the state championship, a conspiracy emerged that threatened to derail their campaign. The nature of the plot is revealed across Chapters 15 and 16, involving attempts to undermine the club from within and to manipulate the outcome of critical matches.

## Mike Costigan's Discovery

The conspiracy was uncovered through the sharp eyes of Mike Costigan, the telegraph messenger (015-004). His position gave him access to communications passing through Catalpa, and what he discovered was serious enough to warrant the chapter title "Mike Costigan's Discovery" (Chapter 15). The telegraph operator's alertness proved crucial to the club's fortunes.

## The Conspiracy Exposed

Chapter 16, titled "The Conspiracy Laid Open," details the unraveling of the plot. The exposure came at a critical moment — had the conspiracy succeeded, the Catalpa Nine's championship hopes would have been destroyed. Ben Burton and the other club leaders took action based on Costigan's information, and the conspirators' scheme collapsed.

The episode demonstrated that the baseball championship involved stakes high enough to inspire underhanded tactics, and that the Catalpa club's enemies extended beyond the playing field. It also showed how ordinary citizens like Costigan could become heroes through vigilance and loyalty.

## Aftermath

The exposure of the conspiracy cleared the path for the Catalpa Nine to compete fairly for the championship. By the novel's conclusion, Costigan appears as a "faithful satellite" (017-027) granted recognition for his service.
