**Darius Ayres** was the captain and first baseman of the [[calumets|Calumets]] [[national-game|baseball]] club, the primary antagonists in the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]]'s [[championship-baseball|championship]] campaign. As the leader of the opposing nine, Ayres embodied the competitive spirit of the [[calumets|Calumets]] — skilled, proud, and unwilling to concede defeat easily.

## Identity

Ayres served as captain of the [[calumets|Calumets]] and played first base, a position typically reserved for a steady, reliable fielder with leadership qualities (011-022). His dual role as player and leader made him the central figure in the [[calumets|Calumets]]' dugout and on the field.

## Leadership

As captain, Ayres set the tone for his club's approach to the [[championship-baseball|championship]] [[baseball-match|contest]]. When a close call went against the [[calumets|Calumets]], his reaction revealed his investment in the game:

> The decision was received with a groan by the umpire; and Captain Darius Ayres, with a look of vengeful determination on his face, stepped to the home plate. (011-039)

He was a hands-on leader who led by example, both in the field and at [[baseball-equipment|bat]]. His instructions to his players about "hugging the bases" demonstrated his tactical awareness (011-062).

## In the Story

Ayres was a consistent contributor throughout the [[championship-baseball|championship series]]. He hit safely to the left field (011-048) and secured his base on called [[baseball-equipment|ball]]s (011-053). In the field, he participated in a double play that halted a [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] rally (011-053).

At the plate, Ayres experienced the full range of outcomes — from clean hits to frustrating outs. He was thrown out at first base (013-036) and hit a liner that was "very cleverly caught by [[sam-morrison|Sam Morrison]]" to end an inning (013-048). Despite these setbacks, his leadership never wavered.
