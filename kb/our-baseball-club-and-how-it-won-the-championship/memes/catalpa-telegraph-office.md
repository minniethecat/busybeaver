The **Catalpa Telegraph Office** was the communications hub of [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]], located on the second story of Niles's Building on the main street. During the [[national-game|baseball]] season, it became the focal point of the town's hopes and anxieties, as [[baseball-spectators|crowd]]s gathered below its windows to hear the latest game results transmitted by wire.

## Location and Setting

The office occupied a distinctive position in the town's geography:

> The telegraph office in Catalpa was in the second story of Niles's building, a brick structure on the main street of the town and chiefly occupied by lawyers and doctors. The narrow stairway was found with difficulty by strangers. (007-016)

The building's professional character — housing lawyers and doctors — made the telegraph office a natural meeting place for the town's leading citizens during moments of public excitement.

## The Crowd and the Bulletins

During away games, the telegraph office became the scene of intense communal anticipation. [[hank-jackson|Hank Jackson]], the burly shortstop of the [[dean-county-nine|Dean County Nine]], was among those who kept vigil:

> "Another bulletin!" cried Hank Jackson, the burly shortstop of the Dean County Nine, as the tall form of Mr. Heaton emerged from the telegraph office. (007-030)

The bulletins from the telegraph office were the town's only connection to the far-away [[baseball-match|contest]]s. Each update was greeted with outbursts of emotion from the waiting [[baseball-spectators|crowd]] below (007-026).

## The Climactic Moment

The telegraph office played its most dramatic role when Mr. [[al-heaton|Heaton]], unable to contain his joy at the good news, leaned out of the window to announce the result to the [[baseball-spectators|crowd]] below:

> So when Mr. Heaton, radiant with joy, and without waiting to come down the stairs of the telegraph office, put his leg and his head out of the window of the building and cried "Good news again!" every voice in the crowd took up the cry. (007-037)

The resulting cheer was so loud it reached the jurors deliberating in the nearby courthouse (007-038). In this moment, the telegraph office transcended its mundane function and became the symbolic heart of the town's shared [[national-game|baseball]] passion.
