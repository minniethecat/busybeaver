# Hiram Porter

> "Hiram Porter, the first baseman of the home nine, and an honor man in his class at the seminary." (002-041)

## Role in the Club

Hiram Porter manned first base for the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]]. His academic distinction — "an honor man in his class at the seminary" (002-041) — marked him as one of the educated young men who comprised the north-side club. As first baseman, Porter handled throws from across the infield and was a key defensive anchor.

Porter [[baseball-equipment|bat]]ted in the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] order and contributed a "safe hit" (002-049) in the [[the-great-match|Jonesville match]]. He also "hit a [[ground-ball|swift grounder]] to [[john-handy|Handy]] who failed to master it" (013-021), showing his ability to put the [[baseball-equipment|ball]] in play under pressure.

## Key Appearances

Porter appears in the box [[scoring-baseball|score]]s and game accounts throughout the novel. In the [[baseball-match|match]] against the [[black-hawks|Black Hawks]], his [[fielding-baseball|fielding]] at first base was part of the defensive effort. Despatches from the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] tour mentioned Porter alongside [[al-heaton|Heaton]] and the other regulars, confirming his place in the traveling squad (007-040).

The Porter family held "a first-rate social position" (005-011) in [[dean-county-nine|Dean County]], suggesting that Hiram's background connected him to both the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] and [[dean-county-nine|Dean County]] communities.

## Playing Style

As first baseman, Porter needed to handle throws from the infielders — shortstop [[ben-burton|Ben Burton]] and third baseman [[larry-boyne|Larry Boyne]] relied on him to complete putouts. His "safe hit" in the [[jonesville-nine|Jonesville]] game demonstrated his offensive contribution.
