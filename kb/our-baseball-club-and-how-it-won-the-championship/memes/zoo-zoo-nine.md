The **Zoo-Zoo Nine** were the [[national-game|baseball]] club of [[bluford|Bluford]], Illinois, and the second opponent on the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]]'s three-city [[catalpa-first-road-trip|road trip]]. The club occupied a unique place in Illinois [[national-game|baseball]], drawing its identity and playing style from a distinguished Civil War lineage.

## Civil War Heritage

The Zoo-Zoos traced their origins to military rather than athletic tradition:

> The "Zoo-Zoos" were picked players, the lineal descendants of a company of Illinois Zouaves renowned in the Civil War for their bravery, dash, and skill as skirmishers. (008-001)

This martial heritage was reflected in their disciplined, aggressive style of play — a stark contrast to the more informal approach of many [[amateur-baseball-club|amateur club]]s. The original Zouave company had distinguished itself in the war, and their [[national-game|baseball]]-playing descendants carried that legacy onto the diamond.

## The Bluford Defeat

The Zoo-Zoos inflicted a crushing defeat on the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]]:

> Defeat, utter and overwhelming, followed the Catalpas to Bluford, where they played the "Zoo-Zoo Nine" of that city. (008-001)

This loss was one of the lowest points of the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa club]]'s season, compounded by [[al-heaton|Al Heaton]]'s illness and the team's struggles away from home. The Zoo-Zoos' disciplined play proved too much for the visiting [[catalpa-nine|Catalpas]].

## Place in the Road Trip

The Zoo-Zoo [[baseball-match|match]] was the middle [[baseball-match|contest]] of the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]]'s three-city tour, sandwiched between the [[baseball-training|practice]] game against the [[black-hawks|Black Hawks]] of [[sandy-key|Sandy Key]] and the [[championship-baseball|championship series]] against the [[red-stockings|Red Stockings of Galena]] (007-001). While not a [[championship-baseball|championship]] [[baseball-match|contest]], the [[bluford|Bluford]] game was a crucial test of the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa club]]'s ability to compete against organized, well-drilled opposition on the road.
