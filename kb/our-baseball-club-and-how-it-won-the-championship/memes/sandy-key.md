The town of **Sandy Key**, situated on the Illinois Central Railroad in the central part of Illinois, was the home of the [[black-hawks|Black Hawk Nine]] and the destination of the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa club]]'s first road game. This opening [[baseball-match|contest]] was framed as a test of the newly formed [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]] against an established and feared opponent.

## The First Road Game

The [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]]'s season began with a [[baseball-training|practice]] game against the [[black-hawks|Black Hawks]], a [[baseball-match|match]] that carried outsized importance for the young club:

> They had arranged a practice game with the Black Hawk Nine, of Sandy Key, in the central part of the State, to begin the season with. Other games were arranged for later work, but this match, which was partly for practice, and partly to test the material of the new nine, was felt to be one of the most important. (007-001)

Two or three of the more active promoters of the [[national-game|baseball]] scheme traveled to Sandy Key to witness the [[baseball-match|contest]] (007-002). [[al-heaton|Al Heaton]] went down to Sandy Key to keep the home town informed of the game's progress (007-019).

## The Contest

The [[black-hawks|Black Hawks]] were renowned for their [[fielding-baseball|fielding]] and had "acquired a reputation that inspired terror among the [[national-game|baseball]] players of the southern portion of the state" (007-002). For a new and unknown nine from [[dean-county-nine|Dean County]] to challenge them was considered audacious.

As the game unfolded in Sandy Key, the people of [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] gathered at the telegraph office to await news. [[judge-howell|Judge Howell]], presiding in the courthouse, smiled to himself "to think that the home nine was undoubtedly doing well in Sandy Key" (007-033).

The final [[scoring-baseball|score]] confirmed the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpas]]' victory: [[catalpa-nine|Catalpas]] 17, [[black-hawks|Black Hawks]] 0 (007-040). The triumph was announced by Mr. [[al-heaton|Heaton]] at the telegraph office with the triumphant bulletin, "Good news from the boys!" which sparked celebrations throughout [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa]] (007-025).

## Aftermath

The victory at Sandy Key established the [[catalpa-nine|Catalpa Nine]]'s credibility, though the club's subsequent loss at [[bluford|Bluford]] tempered the enthusiasm. The Sandy Key game remained a bright moment in the club's early season, demonstrating that the untested [[dean-county-nine|Dean County Nine]] could compete with established clubs.
