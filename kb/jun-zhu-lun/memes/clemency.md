# Clemency (Clemenza)

Clemency (clemenza) in Machiavelli's political vocabulary is an ambivalent virtue. While every prince wishes to be considered clement, Machiavelli warns that clemency poorly applied causes far greater harm than measured cruelty. The prince who is too merciful allows disorders to spread, ultimately requiring far harsher measures than would have sufficed at the outset.

## Machiavelli 的论述

Machiavelli addresses clemency primarily in Chapters 17 and 28, always in tension with cruelty. His treatment is characteristically unsentimental: the moral quality of clemency matters less than its political consequences.

### 核心论点

- A prince must take care not to misuse clemency — the example of Cesare Borgia, considered cruel, shows that his severity restored order to the Romagna and was ultimately more merciful than Florentine inaction, which allowed Pistoia to be destroyed by faction (017-003)
- Clemency, when it prevents a prince from acting decisively against disorders at their onset, allows evils to grow until they require extreme remedies. True clemency is the severity that prevents greater suffering.
- A prince who wishes to be considered merciful must sometimes accept the reputation of cruelty, because the mercy that permits disorder is the greatest cruelty to the people as a whole
- Clemency and liberality are virtues a prince should display when first coming to power — Castruccio Castracani seized the opportunity to show his clemency and liberality upon taking control of Lucca (028-322)

### 历史例证

- **Cesare Borgia** — the paradigmatic case of cruelty that was more merciful than excessive clemency. His severity in the Romagna brought peace where Florentine leniency had allowed faction to destroy Pistoia (017-003)
- **Castruccio Castracani** — upon his elevation in Lucca, seized the opportunity to demonstrate clemency and liberality, showing that these virtues have their place in winning initial favor (028-322)
- **Scipio Africanus** — his excessive clemency toward his mutinous army in Spain nearly cost him his command; his "merciful nature" was a defect that required others to correct

## 概念辨析

### 与其他概念的关系

- **Clemency vs. Cruelty**: These are not opposites but complementary tools. Well-measured cruelty produces the conditions in which clemency can be genuinely practiced. False clemency that tolerates disorder produces the necessity for true cruelty.
- **Clemency vs. Fear**: Excessive clemency undermines fear and invites contempt. The prince must balance mercy with the fear that maintains order.
- **Clemency vs. Hatred**: A prince who is too clement may inadvertently breed the conditions (disorder, faction, violence) that lead the people to hate him for his weakness.

### 在 Machiavelli 思想体系中的位置

Clemency illustrates Machiavelli's core methodological principle: judge actions by their effects, not their appearances. What looks merciful (tolerating disorder) may be crueler than what looks cruel (swift punishment). The prince must see through the surface of virtue to its political consequences.

## 后世影响

Machiavelli's treatment of clemency influenced early modern debates about the royal prerogative of mercy and the relationship between justice and mercy in sovereign power. His inversion — that cruelty can be mercy — remains a provocative contribution to the ethics of statecraft.
