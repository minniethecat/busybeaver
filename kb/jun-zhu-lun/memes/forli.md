## Overview

Forli was a city in the Romagna region of northern Italy during the Renaissance. It was ruled by the Riario and later Sforza families, most notably by Catherina Sforza, the "Countess of Forli" and "my lady of Forli" to whom Machiavelli himself was sent as a Florentine envoy. The city's fate under Cesare Borgia's conquest illustrates the weakness of relying on mercenary forces for defense.

## Political Context

Forli formed part of the patchwork of small signorie in the Romagna during the late 15th century. Catherina Sforza, the illegitimate daughter of Galeazzo Maria Sforza of Milan, ruled Forli after her husband Girolamo Riario's death. Known for her determined and resourceful character, she defended Forli against multiple threats, though ultimately the city fell to Cesare Borgia's French-backed forces in 1500.

> "The Countess of Forli, when her husband Girolamo was killed, defended herself against the attacking forces and awaited aid from Milan." (020-121)

## Role in The Prince

Forli appears in The Prince primarily as an illustration of the vulnerability of mercenary-reliant defenses. In Chapter 13, Machiavelli recounts how Borgia "entered the Romagna with French troops, and with them he captured Imola and Forli. But afterwards, such forces not appearing trustworthy to him, he turned to mercenaries" and eventually to his own troops (013-045). This narrative arc—from reliance on foreign auxiliaries to mercenaries to native arms—is central to Machiavelli's military thesis.

In Chapter 20, Machiavelli references his own diplomatic mission to Catherina Sforza, noting that "to the Countess of Forli... Machiavelli was sent as envoy from the Florentine Republic" (020-121), lending personal authority to his political analysis. Forli also appears in the list of Romagna powers in Chapter 3, where "my lady of Forli" is counted among the regional lords who would resist or accommodate foreign invasion (003-174).

> "The cities of the Romagna, among whom were the Lord of Imola and the Lord of Forli, at once threw themselves into the arms of the Duke Valentino." (027-091)

## Related Events

- **The Siege of Forli (1499–1500)**: Cesare Borgia captured Forli after a determined defense by Catherina Sforza, who reportedly fought from the citadel while the city fell.
- **Machiavelli's Mission**: In 1499, Machiavelli was sent as Florentine envoy to negotiate with Catherina Sforza at Forli (020-121).
