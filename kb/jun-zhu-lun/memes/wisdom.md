# Wisdom (Sapienza)

Wisdom (sapienza) in Machiavelli's political thought is not an abstract virtue but a practical capacity: the ability to select good counselors, listen to them effectively, and reach sound decisions. Machiavelli inverts the conventional relationship between wisdom and counsel — good counsel does not make a prince wise; rather, a wise prince makes counsel good.

## Machiavelli 的论述

Machiavelli's most direct discussion of wisdom appears in Chapters 22–23, where he treats the selection of ministers and the avoidance of flatterers as tests of princely wisdom.

### 核心论点

- The first opinion formed of a prince's intelligence comes from observing the men he has around him — when they are capable and faithful, the prince is reputed wise (023-044)
- Good counselors, whoever they may be, are born of the wisdom of the prince, and not the wisdom of the prince from good counselors (023-057, 023-058)
- Human wisdom is scanty — men enter into affairs with good intentions but cannot foresee the consequences, making the quality of counsel even more critical (013-093)
- The scanty wisdom of man is a constant theme — even the most prudent princes cannot foresee all outcomes, but a wise prince recognizes this limitation and seeks diverse counsel

### 历史例证

- **The Wise Prince** — Machiavelli's ideal: a ruler who selects a small number of capable men, gives them liberty to speak truth, consults them on specific matters, and decides independently
- **Pope Julius II** — all his wisdom could not rid him of the annoyances caused by his impetuosity, showing that even wise rulers have blind spots (011-035)

## 概念辨析

### 与其他概念的关系

- **Wisdom vs. Prudence**: Prudence is the faculty of choosing well among alternatives; wisdom is the broader capacity that includes the selection and use of counselors. Prudence operates in the moment; wisdom builds the institutional framework for good decision-making.
- **Wisdom vs. Flatterers**: Flatterers are the enemy of wisdom. A prince who listens to flatterers cannot be wise; a wise prince cannot be surrounded by flatterers.
- **Wisdom vs. Virtù**: Wisdom is the cognitive dimension of virtù, concerned specifically with decision-making and counsel.
