# Prudence (Prudenza)

Prudence (prudenza) in Machiavelli's lexicon is the intellectual capacity that enables a prince to recognize the character of the times, choose the right course among imperfect alternatives, and adapt his conduct to circumstances. Unlike classical or Christian prudence — which emphasizes moral deliberation — Machiavellian prudence is strictly instrumental: it judges actions by their outcomes, not their conformity to virtue.

## Machiavelli 的论述

Machiavelli invokes prudence throughout The Prince, but the most concentrated discussions appear in Chapters 17, 21, and 28.

### 核心论点

- Prudence consists in knowing how to recognize the nature of difficulties and to choose the less bad course as the good one — there are no perfectly safe courses (P01-302)
- A prince must use cruelty in a temperate manner with prudence — knowing when severity serves order and when it breeds hatred (017-023)
- Prudence in alliance is essential: a prince must never ally with a stronger power to attack another, because victory leaves one at the ally's mercy; true prudence avoids this trap (021-069)
- Prudence consists in knowing how to distinguish between kinds of difficulties and choosing the least harmful — a prince who recognizes evils before they grow can remedy them, but one who waits too long finds the disease incurable (021-086)
- Prudence drives everything before it; time waits for no one, and the prince who lacks prudence is swept away by events (003-148)

### 历史例证

- **Castruccio Castracani** — gave ample proof of his prudence and military skill throughout his career; his ability to read circumstances and act decisively made him the master of Lucca (028-105, 028-671)
- **Cesare Borgia** — his prudence in recognizing when to switch from French dependence to his own arms exemplifies the practical application of prudenza
- **The Romans** — their prudence in sending colonies and anticipating future problems made their empire durable

## 概念辨析

### 与其他概念的关系

- **Prudence vs. Virtù**: Prudence is the cognitive dimension of virtù — the ability to perceive and judge, where virtù also encompasses the will and capacity to act.
- **Prudence vs. Fortune**: Prudence is the faculty that recognizes fortune's patterns and seizes opportunity. Without prudence, virtù cannot effectively oppose fortune.
- **Prudence vs. Cruelty**: Prudent cruelty serves the state; imprudent cruelty breeds hatred. The distinction between well-used and poorly-used cruelty is itself an exercise of prudence.
- **Prudence vs. Occasione**: Prudence recognizes the occasion (occasione) when it appears; without prudence, the opportunity passes unseen.

### 在 Machiavelli 思想体系中的位置

Prudence is the intellectual nerve of Machiavellian statecraft. Every other princely quality — cruelty, liberality, faith, force — must be deployed with prudence to be effective. Without prudence, virtù becomes recklessness and force becomes tyranny.

## 后世影响

Machiavelli's instrumentalization of prudence — divorcing it from moral virtue and attaching it to political effectiveness — marks a turning point in the history of political thought. The concept influenced Hobbes's notion of "prudence" as the foresight based on experience and the broader tradition of political realism.
