# Lombardy

**Lombardy** was the richest and most contested region of northern Italy in Machiavelli's time — a prize that drew French kings across the Alps and exposed the fatal errors of Italian statecraft.

## Overview

Lombardy in the early 16th century was a patchwork of duchies, marquisates, and city-states anchored by the Duchy of [[milan|Milan]], which dominated the region's political economy. The Po Valley's agricultural wealth, combined with Lombardy's strategic position as the gateway between France and the rest of Italy, made it the primary theater of the Italian Wars.

Machiavelli uses Lombardy throughout *The Prince* as the concrete object of foreign ambition. King Louis XII "obtained half the state of Lombardy by his intervention" (003-165) — a gain that proved temporary precisely because Louis violated the political principles Machiavelli lays out.

## Political Context

### Gateway to Italy

Lombardy's geography determined its fate. Any power seeking to dominate Italy had to control the Lombard plain first. The French under Charles VIII and Louis XII, the Spanish under Ferdinand, the Venetians, and the Swiss all contested the region. Machiavelli notes that "the Venetians had a design on Lombardy, to which the Venetians would have consented" (003-220) — a classic case of Italians inviting foreign intervention to settle local scores.

### The Milanese Axis

The Sforza dukes of Milan were Lombardy's nominal masters, but their hold was fragile. Francesco Sforza "turned his ambition to Lombardy" (012-083) after securing the Milanese duchy through his own arms. The region's fate was tied to Milan's — when Milan fell, Lombardy fell with it. Machiavelli records how Venice "hoped to obtain half the state of Lombardy" through alliance with France, only to find that French power once entrenched in Milan "regained at once the authority which Charles had lost" (003-171).

## Role in The Prince

### Louis XII's Errors

Lombardy serves as the case study for one of Machiavelli's most famous political analyses: the five errors of Louis XII. Chapter 3 enumerates them: Louis destroyed the minor powers that could have checked Venice, increased the Church's strength, and brought Spain into Italy — all errors committed in pursuit of Lombard territory. "King Louis lost Lombardy by not having followed any of those conditions observed by others" (003-241) who successfully held acquired provinces.

### The French Invasion Model

Machiavelli uses Lombardy to demonstrate how a foreign power should properly hold an acquired territory — and how failure follows when the correct policies are abandoned. The French "secured two towns in Lombardy" (003-178) for Venice as payment for Venetian support, then alienated their ally by depriving them of expected gains.

> "King Louis was brought into Italy by the ambition of the Venetians, who desired to obtain half the state of Lombardy by his intervention. I will not blame this resolution of the king; for, wishing to get a footing in Italy, and having no friends there — seeing rather that every door was shut to him owing to the conduct of Charles — he was forced to accept those friendships which he could get." (003-164)

### Ravaging and Recovery

In the closing exhortation of Chapter 26, Machiavelli invokes "the ravaging and plundering of Lombardy" (026-022) as proof of Italy's desperate condition — a land so abused by foreign armies that even its richest province lay despoiled. This rhetorical framing turns Lombardy from a mere territorial prize into a symbol of national degradation requiring redemption.

## Related Events

- The French conquest of Milan (1499): Louis XII seized the duchy, establishing French control over Lombardy
- The Battle of Novara (1513): Swiss forces drove the French out of Lombardy, restoring the Sforza
- The Spanish takeover: after Pavia (1525), Lombardy passed under Spanish domination
