# Colonies

Colonies represent Machiavelli's recommended strategy for governing newly acquired territories. In Chapter 3 of *The Prince*, he argues that sending colonists is more economical, causes less resentment, and produces more lasting control than maintaining armed forces in the conquered region. This advice reflects his broader principle of minimizing the cost of rule while maximizing stability.

## Machiavelli 的论述

Machiavelli's discussion of colonies is concentrated in Chapter 3, where he analyzes the Roman approach to governing conquered provinces and contrasts it with the French failures in Italy.

### 核心论点

- The best and most effective course is to send colonies to one or two places that serve as "keys" to the state (003-073)
- Colonies cost the prince very little — with minimal expense, the prince establishes loyal outposts that secure the territory (003-076)
- Colonies are not costly, they are more faithful, and they injure only those displaced from their lands — a small and dispersed group that cannot harm the prince (003-083)
- By contrast, maintaining armed men in place of colonies costs vastly more and creates widespread resentment (003-090)
- The Romans understood this principle well: they sent colonies and maintained friendly relations with minor powers without increasing their strength (003-119)

### 历史例证

- **The Romans** — the primary exemplar, whose colonial policy enabled them to govern a vast empire with minimal military occupation. They sent colonies, maintained friendly relations with lesser powers, and kept down the great powers (003-119)
- **Louis XII of France** — his errors in Italy included failing to send colonies and instead relying on armed intervention; these errors cost him the state (003-214)

## 概念辨析

### 与其他概念的关系

- **Colonies vs. Armed Garrisons**: Colonies cost little and offend few; garrisons cost much and offend many. The former is the prudent choice.
- **Colonies vs. Conquest**: Conquest acquires territory; colonies consolidate it. A prince who conquers without colonizing holds a house built on straw.
- **Colonies vs. Foundations**: Colonies are one method of laying foundations in newly acquired territories — physical extensions of the prince's power.

### 在 Machiavelli 思想体系中的位置

Colonies exemplify Machiavelli's preference for economical, low-friction governance. They reflect his insight that the most durable forms of control are those that impose the least visible burden on the governed — a principle that extends beyond military occupation to all forms of princely rule.

## 后世影响

Machiavelli's defense of colonization as a cost-effective method of territorial control influenced early modern imperial policy, particularly in the English and French colonial traditions. His economic calculus — comparing the costs of colonies versus garrisons — anticipates modern cost-benefit analyses of military occupation.
