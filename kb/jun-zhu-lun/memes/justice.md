# Justice (Giustizia)

Justice (giustizia) occupies an ambivalent position in Machiavelli's thought. While he acknowledges justice as a princely virtue — particularly for maintaining the people's support — he subordinates it to necessity and the security of the state. The Prince does not reject justice; it redefines it in instrumental terms.

## Machiavelli 的论述

Machiavelli addresses justice most directly in Chapters 19, 21, and 26, always in relation to political outcomes rather than moral absolutes.

### 核心论点

- A prince should be seen as a man of modest life, a lover of justice, an enemy to cruelty, humane, and benign — these qualities win the people's support and make the prince secure (019-143)
- In alliances, a prince must show regard especially to justice — but only to the extent that it serves his interests. Betraying an ally for advantage brings infamy, but standing by an ally who is losing is folly (021-064)
- A just war is one that is necessary — the final chapter declares that "with us there is great justice, because that war is just which is necessary, and those arms are pious where no hope is left save in arms" (026-040)
- Justice in rule means respecting property and women — these are the violations that provoke hatred, and avoiding them is the prince's first justice to his subjects

### 历史例证

- **The "just war" against the barbarians** — Machiavelli's exhortation to liberate Italy frames the enterprise as just because it is necessary, a radical redefinition of just war theory (026-040)
- **Roman Emperors** — Machiavelli analyzes which emperors maintained a reputation for justice and which did not, showing that justice is part of the package of princely virtues that secure rule

## 概念辨析

### 与其他概念的关系

- **Justice vs. Necessity**: Necessity defines justice. What is necessary for the preservation of the state is just. This instrumental view breaks decisively with classical and Christian natural law.
- **Justice vs. Hatred**: Injustice that violates property or women breeds hatred. The prince's primary "obligation" is to avoid this kind of injustice.
- **Justice vs. Law**: Justice is the outcome; law is the instrument. Good laws produce just outcomes, but only when backed by good arms.
