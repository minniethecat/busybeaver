# Glory (Gloria)

Glory (gloria) in Machiavelli's thought is the highest aim of political action. Unlike Christian virtue, which defers reward to the afterlife, Machiavellian glory is won in this world through the successful founding, reforming, or defending of states. It is both the motivation and the measure of princely virtù.

## Machiavelli 的论述

Machiavelli presents glory as the natural objective of the ambitious prince. Chapters 6, 7, 21, 24, and 26 contain the most significant discussions.

### 核心论点

- Glory is won not by inheriting a state but by founding a new one or radically reforming an old one — new principalities demand greater virtù and thus offer greater glory (024-009)
- Necessary wars are just wars, and arms carried in such wars bring glory where no other recourse exists — the translator's introduction frames this as a core Machiavellian principle (P01-305)
- Glory and riches are the two common goals of men; the ambitious pursue both through different means (025-044)
- Imitation of great men — particularly ancient exemplars — is the path to glory (014-065)
- A prince who rises from humble origins to found a state achieves double glory: first for establishing order, second for sustaining it with good laws and good arms (021-004)

### 历史例证

- **Cesare Borgia** — sought security and glory through his own arms rather than relying on fortune (007-008)
- **Agathocles of Syracuse** — gained empire through wicked means but not glory, because his cruelty was excessive and his methods dishonorable (008-040)
- **Scipio Africanus** — his chastity, affability, and military success brought him enduring fame and glory (017-081, 017-083)
- **Hierarchs and Founders** — Moses, Cyrus, Romulus, and Theseus are the archetypes of glory through founding (Ch. 6)

## 概念辨析

### 与其他概念的关系

- **Glory vs. Virtù**: Virtù is the capacity for effective action; glory is its public recognition. A prince may possess virtù without achieving glory (if fortune is adverse), but glory without virtù is impossible.
- **Glory vs. Fortune**: Fortune provides the opportunity (occasione) for glory, but virtù must seize it. Glory is what virtù extracts from fortune.
- **Glory vs. Riches**: Machiavelli pairs these as the two universal human goals (025-044), but true princely glory is distinct from mere wealth — it requires lasting institutional achievement.
- **Glory vs. Reputation**: Reputation is tactical (being perceived a certain way); glory is strategic and enduring (being remembered for great deeds).

### 在 Machiavelli 思想体系中的位置

Glory is the telos of Machiavelli's political vision. The final chapter's exhortation to liberate Italy is framed as an appeal to glory — a rare opportunity for a new prince to achieve immortal fame. Glory transforms the instrumental advice of The Prince into a higher calling.

## 后世影响

Machiavelli's secularization of glory — separating it from Christian salvation and attaching it to political founding — influenced the Renaissance cult of the statesman and later nationalist ideologies. The idea that "necessary wars are just" entered early modern international law through Gentili and Grotius.
