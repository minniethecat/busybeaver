# Ambition (Ambizione)

Ambition (ambizione) in Machiavelli's political psychology is a double-edged force. It motivates the founding of states and the pursuit of glory, but it also drives the invasions, rebellions, and usurpations that destroy them. The Prince is, in a sense, a manual for channeling ambition — both the prince's own and that of his rivals — toward stable rule.

## Machiavelli 的论述

Machiavelli treats ambition as an ineradicable feature of human nature, particularly among the great. His references to ambition span the work, particularly in Chapters 1–4 and the dedicatory letter.

### 核心论点

- The ambition of great men is a constant source of political instability — states are disrupted either through the excess of ambition of their own nobles or through the ambition of foreign powers (003-103, 004-007)
- The ambitions of Pope Alexander VI and his son Cesare Borgia exemplify how personal ambition, when combined with virtù, can reshape the political map of Italy (P01-117)
- The Venetians brought the French into Italy through their own ambition, unleashing forces they could not control — the classic case of ambition overreaching (003-164, 003-193)
- A wise prince must manage the ambitions of his subjects: satisfy the moderate, contain the excessive, and redirect the energies of the ambitious toward state service
- Ambition without virtù is mere appetite; ambition with virtù is the engine of political creation

### 历史例证

- **Pope Alexander VI** — his ambition to aggrandize his son drove decades of Italian political maneuvering (P01-117)
- **The Venetians** — their ambition to expand in the Romagna led them to invite French intervention, a classic case of ambition exceeding prudence (003-164)
- **Cesare Borgia** — the ambition to found his own state drove his entire career; his virtù nearly succeeded in channeling that ambition into lasting foundations

## 概念辨析

### 与其他概念的关系

- **Ambition vs. Virtù**: Virtù is the disciplined channeling of ambition toward achievable ends. Ambition without virtù is destructive; virtù without ambition is sterile.
- **Ambition vs. Glory**: Ambition is the desire; glory is the reward. Ambition pursues glory but may also seek mere power or wealth.
- **Ambition vs. Fortune**: Fortune gives ambition its opportunities but also its temptations — the ambitious prince who overreaches is fortune's victim.
