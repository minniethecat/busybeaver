# Romagna

The Romagna is a region of northern Italy, historically part of the Papal States, that figures prominently in *The Prince* as the territory conquered and pacified by Cesare Borgia.

## Political Context

In the late 15th century, the Romagna was a patchwork of petty lordships — Rimini, Forlì, Faenza, Pesaro, and others — nominally subject to the Pope but effectively independent. The region was notorious for lawlessness and factional violence.

## Cesare Borgia's Pacification

Machiavelli uses the Romagna as his case study of how a new prince should govern a newly acquired territory. Cesare Borgia, having conquered the Romagna with French assistance, found it "full of robberies, quarrels, and every kind of violence" (007-068). He appointed Remirro de Orco, "a swift and cruel man," as governor with plenipotentiary powers.

De Orco restored order through brutal repression. Once peace was established, Cesare judged that "such excessive authority was no longer necessary" and had de Orco executed, his body "cut in two pieces and placed on a stake in the public square at Cesena" (007-074). The spectacle "caused the people to be at once satisfied and stupefied" — satisfying their desire for justice while deflecting hatred from Cesare himself.

## What Machiavelli drew from this

The Romagna episode illustrates Machiavelli's principle that cruelty should be "well-used" — inflicted once, for necessity, and not repeated. It also exemplifies his advice that a prince should delegate harsh measures to lieutenants while personally dispensing favors.
