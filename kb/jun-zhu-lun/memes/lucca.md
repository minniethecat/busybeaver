## Overview

Lucca was an independent city-state in Tuscany during the Renaissance, located approximately 20 kilometers northeast of Pisa. In the early 16th century, it maintained its republican government amid the turbulence of Italian politics, though it fell under the influence of larger powers including Florence and the Papal States.

## Political Context

Lucca was governed as a republic, with powerful families such as the Guinigi playing dominant roles in its political life (028-064). The city experienced ongoing factional conflict between Guelph and Ghibelline parties, which shaped its internal politics throughout the 14th and early 15th centuries. During the period Machiavelli describes, Lucca balanced between the major Italian powers—Florence, Milan, and the Papal States—while maintaining a degree of autonomy.

> The Guinigi family held considerable influence: "There lived in the city of Lucca a gentleman of the Guinigi family, named Messer Francesco, whose profession of arms and his courtesy and valour excelled all other men in Lucca." (028-064)

## Role in The Prince

Lucca appears in two distinct contexts within The Prince. In Chapter 7, Machiavelli notes that after Cesare Borgia "pounced down upon Pisa," Lucca "yielded at once, partly through hatred and partly through fear of the Florentines" (007-164). This illustrates how a rising power can swiftly cow smaller states through a combination of fear and existing rivalries.

The majority of Lucca's presence in the work, however, comes through the Appendix on Castruccio Castracani, the Lucchese military leader whom Machiavelli holds up as an exemplar of virtù. Castruccio's rise from foundling to de facto prince of Lucca serves as a case study in how personal ability can overcome humble origins—a central theme of The Prince.

> "Castruccio Castracani of Lucca was one of those men who did great deeds... born of a most humble origin, he achieved such a name that he became almost a prince in Lucca." (028-016)

## Related Events

- **Castruccio's Rise**: Castruccio Castracani rose from being a foundling raised by a canon of San Michele (028-027) to becoming the military leader of Lucca through his service in the wars against other Tuscan cities.
- **The Florentine Expedition**: When the Florentines attempted to restore exiled Guelphs to Lucca, Castruccio led the Lucchese defense, eventually securing his dominance over the city (028-155).
- **Pistoia's Annexation**: Castruccio extended Lucchese influence by manipulating the factional divisions within Pistoia, ultimately bringing it under his control (028-344).
