## Overview

Genoa was one of the great maritime republics of Renaissance Italy, alongside Venice. Located on the Ligurian coast of northwestern Italy, it was a major commercial and naval power whose wealth derived from Mediterranean trade. During the period Machiavelli describes, Genoa oscillated between independence and submission to France, its strategic position making it a frequent prize in the Italian Wars.

## Political Context

Genoa's republican government was chronically unstable, riven by factional conflicts between noble families and between Guelph and Ghibelline factions. This internal weakness, combined with Genoa's strategic importance as a port city, made it a frequent target of French ambitions. During the reign of Louis XII, Genoa fell under French control, an arrangement that had significant consequences for the balance of power in Italy.

> "Genoa yielded; the Florentines became his friends." (003-172)

## Role in The Prince

Genoa appears in The Prince primarily as an example of the cascading effects of foreign intervention in Italy. In Chapter 3, Machiavelli describes how Louis XII of France, having established his authority in Milan, quickly saw "Genoa yield" to his power (003-172). This is part of Machiavelli's broader analysis of Louis XII's errors in Italy—the king gained territory rapidly but failed to maintain it through proper alliances.

Genoa also appears in the military context of Chapter 26, the exhortation to liberate Italy, where Machiavelli lists the French military victories that demonstrated the vulnerability of Italian states: "Alessandria, Capua, Genoa, Vaila, Bologna, Mestri" (026-070). The inclusion of Genoa in this litany of defeats underscores Machiavelli's argument that Italian states could not resist organized foreign armies. Machiavelli also connects events in Genoa to Medici political fortunes, noting that some "Florentine merchants at Genoa" were involved in Medici financial operations (P01-228).

> "Afterwards Alessandria, Capua, Genoa, Vaila, Bologna, Mestri." — listing French military victories over Italian states (026-070)

## Related Events

- **The French Submission (1499)**: Genoa submitted to Louis XII during his Italian campaign, becoming a French protectorate (026-072).
- **The Genoese Revolt**: Genoa periodically revolted against French rule, demonstrating the instability of foreign-dominated governance.
