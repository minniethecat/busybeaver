## THE PRINCE

## CHAPTER I. HOW MANY KINDS OF PRINCIPALITIES THERE ARE, AND BY WHAT MEANS THEY ARE ACQUIRED

[001-001]All states, all powers, that have held and hold rule over men have been

[001-002]and are either republics or principalities.

[001-003]Principalities are either hereditary, in which the family has been long

[001-004]established; or they are new.

[001-005]The new are either entirely new, as was Milan to Francesco Sforza, or they

[001-006]are, as it were, members annexed to the hereditary state of the prince who

[001-007]has acquired them, as was the kingdom of Naples to that of the King of

[001-008]Spain.

[001-009]Such dominions thus acquired are either accustomed to live under a prince,

[001-010]or to live in freedom; and are acquired either by the arms of the prince

[001-011]himself, or of others, or else by fortune or by ability.
