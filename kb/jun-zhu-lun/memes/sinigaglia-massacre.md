# Sinigaglia Massacre

The Sinigaglia Massacre (31 December 1502) was the trap by which Cesare Borgia (Duke Valentino) eliminated his chief rivals — Vitellozzo Vitelli, Oliverotto da Fermo, the Signor Pagolo Orsini, and the Duke of Gravina Orsini. Machiavelli presents it as the supreme example of political ruthlessness executed with precision.

## Background

After Cesare's conquest of the Romagna, his mercenary captains — the Orsini and their allies — grew suspicious of his growing power and conspired against him at La Magione. Cesare feigned reconciliation, promising them new honors and territories while secretly planning their destruction.

## The Trap

Cesare arranged to meet the conspirators at Sinigaglia, a town on the Adriatic coast. He received them with false cordiality, then had them arrested as they entered his quarters. That same night, Vitellozzo Vitelli and Oliverotto da Fermo were strangled. The two Orsini were executed shortly after.

## In The Prince

Machiavelli presents the massacre as Cesare's masterstroke. Having "secured himself against his chief enemies," Cesare "laid very good foundations for his future power" (007-065). The episode illustrates the principle that a prince should eliminate his enemies decisively: injuries "should be done all at once, so that being less tasted, they will give less offense" (008-015).
