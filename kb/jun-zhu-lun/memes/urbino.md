## Overview

Urbino was a duchy in the Marche region of central Italy, ruled by the Montefeltro dynasty during the Renaissance. Under Duke Federico da Montefeltro, it became a renowned center of Renaissance culture and learning. By Machiavelli's time, the duchy had passed to Guidobaldo da Montefeltro and become a strategic target in the power struggles of Cesare Borgia and the Papal States.

## Political Context

The Duchy of Urbino occupied a strategic position between the Papal States and the Adriatic coast. Its rulers, particularly Federico da Montefeltro, had built Urbino into one of the most admired courts of Renaissance Italy, known for its library, architecture, and patronage of the arts. However, by the early 16th century, the duchy's military weakness made it vulnerable to the expansionist ambitions of Cesare Borgia, who was consolidating the Romagna under papal authority.

> "After taking the Duchy of Urbino, Borgia attacked Tuscany, and the king of France compelled him to desist." (007-086)

## Role in The Prince

Urbino features prominently in Machiavelli's analysis of Cesare Borgia's career in Chapter 7. Borgia's conquest of the duchy is presented as part of his broader campaign to establish a unified state in central Italy. After securing the Romagna, Borgia "attacked Tuscany" from his new base in Urbino, only to be stopped by French intervention (007-086). This illustrates the limits of even a highly capable prince's power when opposed by a greater external force.

The duchy becomes a more complex case when Machiavelli describes the rebellion that broke out there. The people of Urbino, along with other Romagna subjects, "took hope of being able to effect a change" during Borgia's illness (027-023). Some men of Urbino conspired to "seize the fortress of San Leo," planning a broader uprising (027-026). This rebellion demonstrates the fragility of newly acquired territories and the difficulty of maintaining control over populations that remember their former liberty.

> "The people of Urbino, among whom were certain men of Urbino who planned to seize the fortress of San Leo, took hope of being able to effect a change." (027-023)

## Related Events

- **Borgia's Conquest**: Cesare Borgia seized the Duchy of Urbino as part of his Romagna campaign, displacing Duke Guidobaldo da Montefeltro (007-086).
- **The Urbino Rebellion**: During Borgia's illness, the people of Urbino rose in rebellion alongside other Romagna subjects, temporarily threatening his conquests (027-023).
- **Guidobaldo's Return**: Machiavelli notes that Duke Guidobaldo, "on returning to his dominion" after Borgia's fall, found that the people welcomed him back—a testament to the residual loyalty of subjects to their legitimate prince (020-093).
