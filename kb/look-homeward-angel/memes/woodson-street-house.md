# Woodson Street House

## Description

Woodson Street House is a key location in the world of *Look Homeward, Angel*. Gant's first house — 'the picture of his soul, the garment of his will' — where the older Gant children grew up

The place is rendered with Wolfe's characteristic sensory intensity — every detail contributes to the novel's immersive reconstruction of a lost world:

> (001-026) This town of Altamont had been settled soon after the Revolutionary War. It had been a convenient stopping-off place for cattledrovers and farmers in their swing eastward from Tennessee into South Carolina.

## Significance

This location serves as more than backdrop — it is an active presence in the novel, shaping the characters who inhabit it and embodying the themes of the narrative.

> (020-007) They came out at ten-thirty or eleven o'clock, on cold ringing pavements, into a world frozen bare — a dead city of closed shops, dressed windows, milliners' and clothiers' models posturing with waxen gaiety at congealed silence.

## Connections

- **Characters**: Various members of the Gant family and townspeople
- **Themes**: Home and belonging, time and memory, death and mortality
