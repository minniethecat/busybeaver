## Synopsis

Dr. Cardiac is one of Wolfe's most economical character sketches — a physician who appears in a single, memorable line during the account of Eugene Gant's infancy. His defining moment is a question delivered with "shocking and medical coarseness," inquiring after the infant Eugene with a rudely affectionate epithet. The briefness of his appearance is itself characteristic of Wolfe's method: minor characters are often rendered in a single brushstroke, their vividness deriving from a telling detail rather than sustained development.

## Biography

Dr. Cardiac appears only in the context of Eugene's earliest childhood, one of the doctors who attended the Gant household:

> [004-022] "How is the little bastard?" inquired Dr. Cardiac, referring thus, with shocking and medical coarseness, to this most royal imp.

The line captures in miniature the rough, familiar world of Altamont's professional men — doctors whose bedside manner was seasoned with profanity and irreverence, whose affection for their patients was expressed through gruff humor rather than sentiment. Cardiac belongs to the same medical fraternity as Dr. McGuire and Dr. Coker, though he appears only in this single flash of Wolfean portraiture.
