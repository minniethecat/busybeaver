# Eugene Gant

## Synopsis

Eugene Gant is one of the central figures in *Look Homeward, Angel*. The protagonist — Oliver's youngest son, the hungry listener and future writer whose coming-of-age is the novel's central narrative.

## Biography

The life of Eugene Gant unfolds across the novel's sweeping narrative of the Gant family in Altamont.

> (001-001) A destiny that leads the English to the Dutch is strange enough; but one that leads from Epsom into Pennsylvania, and thence into the hills that shut in Altamont over the proud coral cry of the cock, and the soft stone smile of an angel, is touched by that dark miracle of chance which makes new magic in a dusty world.

The family's story is one of love and loss, hunger and creation:

> (002-009) Eliza came through stolidly to victory. As she marched down these enormous years of love and loss, stained with the rich dyes of pain and pride and death, and with the great wild flare of his alien and passionate life, her limbs faltered in the grip of ruin, but she came on.

The town of Altamont shapes every character who lives there:

> (001-026) This town of Altamont had been settled soon after the Revolutionary War. It had been a convenient stopping-off place for cattledrovers and farmers in their swing eastward from Tennessee into South Carolina.

## Relationships

Eugene Gant's relationships with other members of the Gant family define their role in the novel. The web of family connections — love, resentment, duty, and rebellion — forms the novel's emotional core.

> (020-001) Gant, during these years in which Helen and Luke, the two for whom he felt the deepest affection, were absent a large part of the time, lived a splintered existence at home and at Eliza's.
