# Luke Gant

## Synopsis

Luke Gant plays a significant role in the Gant family's story. Gant's second son — the salesman with enormous personality and Rabelaisian humor

## Biography

Luke Gant's life intersects with the central narrative of the Gant family in Altamont.

> (001-001) A destiny that leads the English to the Dutch is strange enough; but one that leads from Epsom into Pennsylvania, and thence into the hills that shut in Altamont over the proud coral cry of the cock, and the soft stone smile of an angel, is touched by that dark miracle of chance.

The family's collective experience encompasses joy, conflict, and transformation:

> (002-009) Eliza came through stolidly to victory. As she marched down these enormous years of love and loss, stained with the rich dyes of pain and pride and death, she came on, through sickness and emaciation, to victory.

## Relationships

Luke Gant's connections to the Gant family and the town of Altamont shape their role in the narrative.

> (020-001) Gant, during these years in which Helen and Luke, the two for whom he felt the deepest affection, were absent a large part of the time, lived a splintered existence at home and at Eliza's.
