## Synopsis

Jim Trivett is a student at Pulpit Hill who befriends Eugene Gant during a "bitter and desperate autumn" and initiates him into a world of masculine bravado, drinking, and sexual adventure. The son of a wealthy tobacco farmer from the eastern part of the State, Trivett is a "good tempered young tough of twenty years" whose loose grin and easy confidence mark him as a type Wolfe renders with characteristic economy — the provincial big man on a small campus, at home in the rough-and-tumble world of young men testing boundaries. His role in the novel is concentrated in a single long episode in Chapter 29, where he leads Eugene to a brothel in what becomes the younger boy's first sexual experience.

## Biography

Eugene meets Jim Trivett during an autumn of emotional desolation at Pulpit Hill:

> [029-019] It was during this bitter and desperate autumn that Eugene first met Jim Trivett.

Trivett is introduced as the son of a rich tobacco farmer, living with fellow students from his home region in a lodging-house near the university's west gate:

> [029-020] Jim Trivett, the son of a rich tobacco farmer in the eastern part of the State, was a good tempered young tough of twenty years.

Their connection forms around the familiar rituals of student life — complaints about schoolwork, casual profanity, and the posturing of young men. Trivett's speech is a steady stream of grinning profanity and rough humor:

> [029-025] "I've been working like a son-of-a-bitch."

The pivotal episode of Trivett's role comes when he proposes an expedition to a local brothel. His needling of Eugene — alternating between challenge and protective bravado — captures the dynamics of adolescent masculinity. When Eugene hesitates, Trivett alternately cajoles and vouches for him:

> [029-089] "He's going on eighteen," said Jim Trivett loyally. "Don't you worry about him."

After the encounter, Trivett's parting words — "Stay with them, son" — combine rough camaraderie with a note of paternalism, marking the moment as a rite of passage in Eugene's coming of age.
