# Jannadeau

## Synopsis

Jannadeau is a character in the world of Altamont. Gant's fellow stonecutter — the Swiss craftsman who works beside him in the shop

## Biography

Jannadeau's presence in the novel adds to the rich tapestry of Altamont life that Wolfe renders in exhaustive detail.

> (001-026) This town of Altamont had been settled soon after the Revolutionary War. It had been a convenient stopping-off place for cattledrovers and farmers.

The town and its inhabitants form the world that shapes Eugene's consciousness:

> (020-004) He remained at Dixieland as long as there was anyone to listen to him, and to the bleak little group of winter boarders he brought magic.

## Relationships

Jannadeau's interactions with the Gant family contribute to the novel's dense social fabric.

> (020-001) Gant, during these years in which Helen and Luke, the two for whom he felt the deepest affection, were absent a large part of the time, lived a splintered existence at home and at Eliza's.
