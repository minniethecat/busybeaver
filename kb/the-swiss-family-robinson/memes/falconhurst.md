# Falconhurst

Falconhurst (Falcon's Nest) is the Robinson family's primary dwelling on the island — a remarkable treehouse constructed in the branches of an enormous tree. It represents the family's transition from mere survival to settled domesticity, and stands as the architectural symbol of their island civilization.

## Construction

The father designs and the family collectively builds Falconhurst after determining that the initial tent encampment at Safety Bay is unsuitable for long-term habitation. The treehouse is elevated for safety from wild animals and flooding, and is constructed with materials salvaged from the wreck.

## Significance

Falconhurst embodies the novel's central theme of civilization emerging from wilderness through industry and ingenuity. It is not merely a shelter but a home — the domestic center around which the family's life revolves. Its name (Falcon's Nest) reflects the family's sense of themselves as elevated above the brute conditions of mere survival.
