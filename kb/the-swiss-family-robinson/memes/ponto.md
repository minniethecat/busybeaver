# Ponto

Ponto is one of the Robinson family's two dogs who survive the shipwreck. Alongside Turk, he serves as guard, hunter, and companion throughout the family's island life. The two dogs together form the family's first line of defense and their most constant animal companions.

## Role in the Narrative

Ponto appears alongside Turk in hunting scenes and settlement defense. The father notes his confidence that "my valiant dogs would do their duty" (005-040). Ponto's presence, like Turk's, domesticates the wilderness and maintains continuity with European life.

## Significance

Together, Turk and Ponto represent the domestication of nature — wild canines tamed into loyal service. They are also narrative devices: their barking warns of approaching danger, their hunting skills provide food, and their companionship comforts the children.
