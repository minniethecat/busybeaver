[002-001] The life which was as vivid all about him as if caught by the latest

[002-002] instantaneous process made the same comparatively ineffective appeal.

[002-003] The operatic spectacle was still there. The people, with their cloaks

[002-004] statuesquely draped over their left shoulders, moved down the street, or

[002-005] posed in vehement dialogue on the sidewalks; the drama of bargaining,

[002-006] with the customer's scorn, the shopman's pathos, came through the open

[002-007] shop door; the handsome, heavy-eyed ladies, the bare-headed girls,

[002-008] thronged the ways; the caffès were full of the well-remembered figures

[002-009] over their newspapers and little cups; the officers were as splendid as

[002-010] of old, with their long cigars in their mouths, their swords kicking

[002-011] against their beautiful legs, and their spurs jingling; the dandies,

[002-012] with their little dogs and their flower-like smiles, were still in front

[002-013] of the confectioners' for the inspection of the ladies who passed; the

[002-014] old beggar still crouched over her scaldino at the church door, and the

[002-015] young man with one leg, whom he thought to escape by walking fast, had

[002-016] timed him to a second from the other side of the street. There was the

[002-017] wonted warmth in the sunny squares, and the old familiar damp and stench

[002-018] in the deep narrow streets. But some charm had gone out of all this. The

[002-019] artisans coming to the doors of their shallow booths for the light on

[002-020] some bit of carpentering, or cobbling, or tinkering; the crowds swarming

[002-021] through the middle of the streets on perfect terms with the wine-carts

[002-022] and cab horses; the ineffective grandiosity of the palaces huddled upon

[002-023] the crooked thoroughfares; the slight but insinuating cold of the

[002-024] southern winter, gathering in the shade and dispersing in the sun, and

[002-025] denied everywhere by the profusion of fruit and flowers, and by the

[002-026] greenery of gardens showing through the grated portals and over the tops

[002-027] of high walls; the groups of idle poor, permanently or temporarily

[002-028] propped against the bases of edifices with a southern exposure; the

[002-029] priests and monks and nuns in their gliding passage; the impassioned

[002-030] snapping of the cabmen's whips; the clangour of bells that at some hours

[002-031] inundated the city, and then suddenly subsided and left it to the

[002-032] banging of coppersmiths; the open-air frying of cakes, with its

[002-033] primitive smell of burning fat; the tramp of soldiery, and the fanfare

[002-034] of bugles blown to gay measures--these and a hundred other

[002-035] characteristic traits and facts still found a response in the

[002-036] consciousness where they were once a rapture of novelty; but the

[002-037] response was faint and thin; he could not warm over the old mood in

[002-038] which he once treasured them all away as of equal preciousness.

[002-039] Of course there was a pleasure in recognising some details of former

[002-040] experience in [[florence|Florence]] as they recurred. [[theodore-colville|Colville]] had been met at once

[002-041] by a _festa_, when nothing could be done, and he was more than consoled

[002-042] by the caressing sympathy with which he was assured that his broken

[002-043] trunk could not be mended till the day after to-morrow; he had quite

[002-044] forgotten about the festas and the sympathy. That night the piazza on

[002-045] which he lodged seemed full of snow to the casual glance he gave it;

[002-046] then he saw that it was the white Italian moonlight, which he had also

[002-047] forgotten....

[002-048] II

[002-049] Colville had readied this point in that sarcastic study of his own

[002-050] condition of mind for the advantage of his late readers in the

[002-051] _[[post-democratic-republican|Post-Democrat-Republican]]_, when he was aware of a polite rustling of

[002-052] draperies, with an ensuing well-bred murmur, which at once ignored him,

[002-053] deprecated intrusion upon him, and asserted a common right to the

[002-054] prospect on which he had been dwelling alone. He looked round with an

[002-055] instinctive expectation of style and poise, in which he was not

[002-056] disappointed. The lady, with a graceful lift of the head and a very

[002-057] erect carriage, almost Bernhardtesque in the backward fling of her

[002-058] shoulders and the strict compression of her elbows to her side, was

[002-059] pointing out the different bridges to the little girl who was with her.

[002-060] "That first one is the Santa Trinità, and the next is the Carraja, and

[002-061] that one quite down by the [[cascine|Cascine]] is the iron bridge. The Cascine you

[002-062] remember--the park where we were driving--that clump of woods there----"

[002-063] A vagueness expressive of divided interest had crept into the lady's

[002-064] tone rather than her words. Colville could feel that she was waiting for

[002-065] the right moment to turn her delicate head, sculpturesquely defined by

[002-066] its toque, and steal an imperceptible glance at him: and he

[002-067] involuntarily afforded her the coveted excuse by the slight noise he

[002-068] made in changing his position in order to be able to go away as soon as

[002-069] he had seen whether she was pretty or not. At forty-one the question is

[002-070] still important to every man with regard to every woman.

[002-071] "Mr. Colville!"

[002-072] The gentle surprise conveyed in the exclamation, without time for

[002-073] recognition, convinced Colville, upon a cool review of the facts, that

[002-074] the lady had known him before their eyes met.

[002-075] "Why, [[lina-bowen|Mrs. Bowen]]!" he said.

[002-076] She put out her round, slender arm, and gave him a frank clasp of her

[002-077] gloved hand. The glove wrinkled richly up the sleeve of her dress

[002-078] half-way to her elbow. She bent on his face a demand for just what

[002-079] quality and degree of change he found in hers, and apparently she

[002-080] satisfied herself that his inspection was not to her disadvantage, for

[002-081] she smiled brightly, and devoted the rest of her glance to an electric

[002-082] summary of the facts of Colville's physiognomy; the sufficiently good

[002-083] outline of his visage, with its full, rather close-cut, drabbish-brown

[002-084] beard and moustache, both shaped a little by the ironical self-conscious

[002-085] smile that lurked under them; the non-committal, rather weary-looking

[002-086] eyes; the brown hair, slightly frosted, that showed while he stood with

[002-087] his hat still off. He was a little above the middle height, and if it

[002-088] must be confessed, neither his face nor his figure had quite preserved

[002-089] their youthful lines. They were both much heavier than when Mrs. Bowen

[002-090] saw them last, and the latter here and there swayed beyond the strict

[002-091] bounds of symmetry. She was herself in that moment of life when, to the

[002-092] middle-aged observer, at least, a woman's looks have a charm which is

[002-093] wanting to her earlier bloom. By that time her character has wrought

[002-094] itself more clearly out in her face, and her heart and mind confront you

[002-095] more directly there. It is the youth of her spirit which has come to the

[002-096] surface.

[002-097] "I should have known you anywhere," she exclaimed, with friendly

[002-098] pleasure in seeing him.

[002-099] "You are very kind," said Colville. "I didn't know that I had preserved

[002-100] my [[youth-and-age|youthful beauty]] to that degree. But I can imagine it--if you say so,

[002-101] Mrs. Bowen."

[002-102] "Oh, I assure you that you have!" she protested; and now she began

[002-103] gently to pursue him with one fine question after another about himself,

[002-104] till she had mastered the main facts of his history since they had last

[002-105] met. He would not have known so well how to possess himself of hers,

[002-106] even if he had felt the same necessity; but in fact it had happened that

[002-107] he had heard of her from time to time at not very long intervals. She

[002-108] had married a leading lawyer of her Western city, who in due time had

[002-109] gone to Congress, and after his term was out had "taken up his

[002-110] residence" in Washington, as the newspapers said, "in his elegant

[002-111] mansion at the corner of & Street and Idaho Avenue." After that he

[002-112] remembered reading that Mrs. Bowen was going abroad for the education of

[002-113] her daughter, from which he made his own inferences concerning her

[002-114] marriage. And "You knew [[mr-bowen|Mr. Bowen]] was no longer living?" she said, with

[002-115] fit obsequy of tone.

[002-116] "Yes, I knew," he answered, with decent sympathy.

[002-117] "This is my little Effie," said Mrs. Bowen after a moment; and now the

[002-118] child, hitherto keeping herself discreetly in the background, came

[002-119] forward and promptly gave her hand to Colville, who perceived that she

[002-120] was not so small as he had thought her at first; an effect of infancy

[002-121] had possibly been studied in the brevity of her skirts and the

[002-122] immaturity of her corsage, but both were in good taste, and really to

[002-123] the advantage of her young figure. There was reason and justice in her

[002-124] being dressed as she was, for she really was not so old as she looked by

[002-125] two or three years; and there was reason in Mrs. Bowen's carrying in the

[002-126] hollow of her left arm the India shawl sacque she had taken off and hung

[002-127] there; the deep cherry silk lining gave life to the sombre tints

[002-128] prevailing in her dress, which its removal left free to express all the

[002-129] grace of her extremely lady-like person. Lady-like was the word for Mrs.

[002-130] Bowen throughout--for the turn of her head, the management of her arm

[002-131] from the elbow, the curve of her hand from wrist to finger-tips, the

[002-132] smile, subdued, but sufficiently sweet, playing about her little mouth,

[002-133] which was yet not too little, and the refined and indefinite perfume

[002-134] which exhaled from the ensemble of her silks, her laces, and her gloves,

[002-135] like an odorous version of that otherwise impalpable quality which women

[002-136] call style. She had, with all her flexibility, a certain charming

[002-137] stiffness, like the stiffness of a very tall feather.

[002-138] "And have you been here a great while?" she asked, turning her head

[002-139] slowly toward Colville, and looking at him with a little difficulty she

[002-140] had in raising her eyelids; when she was younger the glance that shyly

[002-141] stole from under the covert of their lashes was like a gleam of

[002-142] sunshine, and it was still like a gleam of paler sunshine.

[002-143] Colville, whose mood was very susceptible to the weather, brightened in

[002-144] the ray. "I only arrived last night," he said, with a smile.

[002-145] "How glad you must be to get back! Did you ever see Florence more

[002-146] beautiful than it was this morning?"

[002-147] "Not for years," said Colville, with another smile for her pretty

[002-148] enthusiasm. "Not for seventeen years at the least calculation."

[002-149] "Is it so many?" cried Mrs. Bowen, with lovely dismay. "Yes, it is," she

[002-150] sighed, and she did not speak for an appreciable interval.

[002-151] He knew that she was thinking of that old love affair of his, to which

[002-152] she was privy in some degree, though he never could tell how much; and

[002-153] when she spoke he perceived that she purposely avoided speaking of a

[002-154] certain person, whom a woman of more tact or of less would have insisted

[002-155] upon naming at once. "I never can believe in the lapse of time when I

[002-156] get back to Italy; it always makes me feel as young as when I left it

[002-157] last."

[002-158] "I could imagine you'd never left it," said Colville.

[002-159] Mrs. Bowen reflected a moment. "Is that a compliment?"

[002-160] "I had an obscure intention of saying something fine; but I don't think

[002-161] I've quite made it out," he owned.

[002-162] Mrs. Bowen gave her small, sweet smile. "It was very nice of you to try.

[002-163] But I haven't really been away for some time; I've taken a house in

[002-164] Florence, and I've been here two years. [[palazzo-pinti|Palazzo Pinti]], [[lungarno|Lung' Arno]] della

[002-165] Zecca. You must come and see me. Thursdays from four till six."

[002-166] "Thank you," said Colville.

[002-167] "I'm afraid," said Mrs. Bowen, remotely preparing to offer her hand in

[002-168] adieu, "that Effie and I broke in upon some very important cogitations

[002-169] of yours." She shifted the silken burden off her arm a little, and the

[002-170] child stirred from the correct pose she had been keeping, and smiled

[002-171] politely.

[002-172] "I don't think they deserve a real dictionary word like that," said

[002-173] Colville. "I was simply mooning. If there was anything definite in my

[002-174] mind, I was wishing that I was looking down on the Wabash in Dos Vaches,

[002-175] instead of the [[arno-river|Arno]] in Florence."
