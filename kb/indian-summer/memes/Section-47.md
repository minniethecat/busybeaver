[047-001] His instinct of forbearance had served him better than the subtlest art.

[047-002] His submission was the best defence. He rose with a real dignity, and

[047-003] she rose also. "Remember," he said, "that I confess all you accuse me

[047-004] of, and that I acknowledge the justice of what you do--because you do

[047-005] it." He put out his hand and took the hand which hung nerveless at her

[047-006] side. "You are quite right. Good-bye." He hesitated a moment. "May I

[047-007] kiss you, Lina?" He drew her to him, and she let him kiss her on the

[047-008] lips.

[047-009] "Good-bye," she whispered. "Go--"

[047-010] "I am going."

[047-011] [[effie-bowen|Effie Bowen]] ran into the room from the kitchen.

[047-012] "Aren't you going to take--" She stopped and turned to her mother. She

[047-013] must not remind Mr. [[theodore-colville|Colville]] of his invitation; that was what her

[047-014] gesture expressed.

[047-015] Colville would not say anything. He would not seize his advantage, and

[047-016] play upon the mother's heart through the feelings of her child, though

[047-017] there is no doubt that he was tempted to prolong the situation by any

[047-018] means. Perhaps [[lina-bowen|Mrs. Bowen]] divined both the temptation and the

[047-019] resistance. "Tell her," she said, and turned away.

[047-020] "I can't go with you to-night, Effie," he said, stooping toward her for

[047-021] the inquiring kiss that she gave him. "I am--going away, and I must say

[047-022] good-bye."

[047-023] The solemnity of his voice alarmed her. "Going away!" she repeated.

[047-024] "Yes--away from [[florence|Florence]]. I'm afraid I shall not see you again."

[047-025] The child turned from him to her mother again, who stood motionless.

[047-026] Then, as if the whole calamitous fact had suddenly flashed upon her, she

[047-027] plunged her face against her mother's breast. "I can't _bear_ it!" she

[047-028] sobbed out; and the reticence of her lamentation told more than a storm

[047-029] of cries and prayers.

[047-030] Colville wavered.

[047-031] "Oh, you must stay!" said Lina, in the self-contemptuous voice of a

[047-032] woman who falls below her ideal of herself.

[047-033] XXIV

[047-034] In the levities which the most undeserving husbands permit themselves

[047-035] with the severest of wives, there were times after their marriage when

[047-036] Colville accused Lina of never really intending to drive him away, but

[047-037] of meaning, after a disciplinary ordeal, to marry him in reward of his

[047-038] tested [[sacrifice-and-renunciation|self-sacrifice]] and obedience. He said that if the appearance of

[047-039] Effie was not a _coup de théâtre_ contrived beforehand, it was an

[047-040] accident of no consequence whatever; that if she had not come in at that

[047-041] moment, her mother would have found some other pretext for detaining

[047-042] him. This is a point which I would not presume to decide. I only know

[047-043] that they were married early in June before the syndic of Florence, who

[047-044] tied a tricolour sash round his ample waist for the purpose, and never

[047-045] looked more paternal or venerable than when giving the sanction of the

[047-046] Italian state to their union. It is not, of course, to be supposed that

[047-047] Mrs. Colville was contented with the civil rite, though Colville may

[047-048] have thought it quite sufficient. The religious ceremony took place in

[047-049] the English chapel, the assistant clergyman officiating in the absence

[047-050] of the incumbent, who had already gone out of town.

[047-051] The [[mr-waters|Rev. Mr. Waters]] gave away the bride, and then went home to Palazzo

[047-052] Pinti with the party, the single and singularly honoured guest at their

[047-053] wedding feast, for which Effie Bowen went with Colville to Giacosa's to

[047-054] order the ices in person. She has never regretted her choice of a step

[047-055] father, though when Colville asked her how she would like him in that

[047-056] relation she had a moment of hesitation, in which she reconciled herself

[047-057] to it; as to him she had no misgivings. He has sometimes found himself

[047-058] the object of little jealousies on her part, but by promptly deciding

[047-059] all questions between her and her mother in Effie's favour he has

[047-060] convinced her of the groundlessness of her suspicions.

[047-061] In the absence of any social pressure to the contrary, the Colvilles

[047-062] spent the summer in [[palazzo-pinti|Palazzo Pinti]]. Before their fellow-sojourners

[047-063] returned from the _villeggiatura_ in the fall, however, they had turned

[047-064] their faces southward, and they are now in Rome, where, arriving as a

[047-065] married couple, there was no inquiry and no interest in their past.

[047-066] It is best to be honest, and own that the affair with [[imogene-graham|Imogene]] has been

[047-067] the grain of sand to them. No one was to blame, or very much to blame;

[047-068] even Mrs. Colville says that. It was a thing that happened, but one

[047-069] would rather it had not happened.

[047-070] Last winter, however, Mrs. Colville received a letter from Mrs. Graham

[047-071] which suggested, if it did not impart, consolation. "[[mr-morton|Mr. Morton]] was here

[047-072] the other day, and spent the morning. He has a parish at Erie, and there

[047-073] is talk of his coming to Buffalo."

[047-074] "Oh, Heaven grant it!" said Colville, with sudden piety.

[047-075] "Why?" demanded his wife.

[047-076] "Well, I wish she was married."

[047-077] "You have nothing whatever to do with her."

[047-078] It took him some time to realise that this was the fact.

[047-079] "No," he confessed; "but what do you think about it?"

[047-080] "There is no telling. We are such simpletons! If a man will keep on long

[047-081] enough--But if it isn't Mr. Morton, it will be some one else--some

[047-082] _young_ person."

[047-083] Colville rose and went round the breakfast table to her. "I hope so," he

[047-084] said. "_I_ have married a young person, and it would only be fair."

[047-085] This magnanimity was irresistible.

[047-086] THE END.
