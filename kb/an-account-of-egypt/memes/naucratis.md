## Overview

Naucratis was a Greek trading settlement in the western Nile Delta, founded under the patronage of the Saite pharaohs and granted special status by Amasis as the exclusive port of entry for Hellenic commerce. Herodotus identifies Naucratis as the channel through which Greek knowledge of Egypt became reliable: "we who are Hellenes know by intercourse with them the certainty of all that which happened in Egypt beginning from king Psammetichos and afterwards." (014-008) (016-005)

## Location / Geography

Naucratis was situated on the Canobic branch of the Nile, near Sais, approximately seventy kilometres southeast of Alexandria (which did not yet exist in Herodotus's time). It was accessible to Greek shipping from the Mediterranean and well connected by river to the Egyptian interior.

## Historical Significance

The establishment of Naucratis marked a turning point in Egyptian-Hellenic relations. Amasis, who favoured the Greeks, granted the city to the Hellenes for permanent settlement and commerce — a privilege that gave Greek merchants, mercenaries, and travellers a secure base within Egypt. (016-005) From this settlement, knowledge of Egyptian customs, history, and geography flowed into the Greek world.
