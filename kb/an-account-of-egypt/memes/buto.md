## Overview

Buto was an ancient city in the Nile Delta, sacred to the cobra-goddess Wadjet (identified by Herodotus with Leto). It was the site of one of the two most important oracles in Egypt — the oracle of Leto — which Herodotus describes as the most truthful of all Egyptian oracles. (014-008) The oracle of Buto plays a pivotal role in the history of the 26th Dynasty, providing the prophecy of "bronze men from the sea" that foretold Psammetichos's rise to sole rule. (014-008)

## Location / Geography

Buto was situated in the marshy fen-country of the northwestern Delta, near the Canobic branch of the Nile. The city comprised two neighbouring settlements — Pe and Dep — which the Greeks collectively called Buto. Its location in the fen-country is significant: it was from this region that the oracular voice was said to emanate, and it was here that Psammetichos took refuge during his exile. (014-008) The temple of Leto at Buto was described by Herodotus as one of the most remarkable in Egypt, featuring a monolithic shrine chamber transported from Elephantine.

## Historical Significance

The oracle of Leto at Buto was consulted by kings and commoners alike. Its most historically consequential pronouncement was the prophecy given to Psammetichos during his exile: that vengeance would come when men of bronze appeared from the sea. (014-008) When Ionian and Carian raiders landed on the Egyptian shore wearing bronze armour, Psammetichos recognised the fulfilment of the oracle and enlisted the Greeks to his cause — the event that launched the Saite Dynasty and transformed Egypt's relationship with the Hellenic world. (014-008) (015-001)

The oracle also appears in the story of Mykerinos, who received from Buto the prophecy that he had only six years to live — a doom that he attempted to defy by turning nights into days through lamp-light. (012-005)
