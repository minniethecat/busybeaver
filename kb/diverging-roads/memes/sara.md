## Overview

Sara is a miniature painter and a member of the circle of professional women who gather at [[helen-davies|Helen Davies]]'s cottage on Russian Hill. Distinguished by her "long, sensitive hands" and "slender, fastidious fingers," she represents the artistic temperament within the group — dreamy, romantic, and quietly insistent on the primacy of love [024-3139, 024-3177].

## Character

### The Artist

Sara is introduced on the [[the-railroad|train]] to the beach excursion:

> And Sara was a miniature painter, Dodo executive secretary of an important California commission. [024-3133]

Her artistic sensibility marks her apart from the more politically engaged Anne or the professionally driven [[dodo|Dodo]]. On the beach, she sits apart, her "eyes full of dreams," responding to the beauty of the night:

> "How beautiful it is!" she said at intervals, lifting her face to the dark sky full of stars, or indicating with a nod the lights flung over the Berkeley hills like handfuls of jewels. [024-3139]

She is also the only one to react with pure emotion to the group's discussion of the war: "Oh, it's all too horrible!" Sara cried [024-3143].

### On Love

In the group's extended debate about [[marriage-and-courtship|marriage]], money, and modern [[gender-roles|womanhood]], Sara alone defends the romantic ideal:

> "I don't know what you're all talking about," Sara ventured softly. "I should think — love — would be all that mattered." [024-3221]

When [[dodo|Dodo]] and Anne dissect the economic realities of [[marriage-and-courtship|marriage]], Sara insists:

> "Just the same," Sara insisted softly, "you leave out everything that's important when you leave out love." [024-3255]

Anne later singles Sara out as the one among them who might marry: "The real reason you don't marry is the reason none of us'll marry, except perhaps Sara" [024-3195].

### The Farewell

On [[helen-davies|Helen]]'s last morning, Sara is one of the two friends (with [[willetta|Willetta]]) who help with the frantic preparations:

> Sara had made coffee and toast and pressed them upon Helen while she was dressing. [024-3427]
