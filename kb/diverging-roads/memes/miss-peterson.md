## Overview

Miss Peterson is a stenographer from the [[san-francisco|San Francisco]] *Post* who appears briefly in the novel's closing pages, among the crowd of well-wishers seeing [[helen-davies|Helen Davies]] off at the steamer.

## Appearance

Miss Peterson's sole appearance is poignant in its brevity:

> Miss Peterson, the stenographer from the *Post*, was pressing a white package into her hands; two little girls from Telegraph Hill had come down to bring a hot, wilted bunch of weed-flowers; Mary O'Brien, from the settlement house she had written about, and others, acquaintances she had hardly remembered. [026-3437]

The "white package" she presses into [[helen-davies|Helen]]'s hands is an unremarked gift, a gesture of farewell from the working world [[helen-davies|Helen]] is leaving behind. As a colleague of [[marian-marcy|Marian Marcy]] at the newspaper, Miss Peterson represents the professional network [[helen-davies|Helen]] built during her [[san-francisco|San Francisco]] years.

## Thematic Significance

Miss Peterson's cameo is part of a closing montage that gathers the threads of [[helen-davies|Helen]]'s life — colleagues, neighbors, acquaintances — for a final farewell. Her presence, along with the girls from Telegraph Hill and the settlement-house worker, testifies to the breadth of [[helen-davies|Helen]]'s connections across [[san-francisco|San Francisco]]'s social spectrum. That [[helen-davies|Helen]] "had hardly remembered" some of these well-wishers underscores a central irony: she has been more woven into the fabric of [[san-francisco|the city]] than she knew.
