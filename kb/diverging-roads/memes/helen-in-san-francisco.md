# Helen's Business Trip to San Francisco

Helen's business trip to San Francisco represents the furthest point of her journey from Masonville. She walks "briskly through the groups in the hotel lobby," takes her room key, and leaves a call for "the early San Francisco train" (018-013) — a woman in command of her professional life, moving through urban space with competence and purpose.

## Summary

In San Francisco, Helen closes a sale and plans to "get the final contracts" before taking them to San José (018-013). She buys the morning papers, reads the headlines, and navigates the offices of Clark & Hayward — "wide, clean-looking" spaces where businessmen conduct the commerce that powers the California economy.

The trip is interrupted by news of war, which momentarily destabilizes her business plans. But the essential fact remains: Helen Davies, the farm girl from Masonville, has become a person who closes contracts in San Francisco.

## Significance

This event marks the completion of one of Helen's roads — the professional path. But the novel does not end here; professional success does not resolve the question of what kind of life Helen wants. San Francisco is a destination, but not necessarily an answer.
