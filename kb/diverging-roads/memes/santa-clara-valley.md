# Santa Clara Valley

A fertile agricultural valley south of San Francisco, where Helen Davies worked her territory as a land saleswoman — a landscape of orchards, trolley-lines, and bungalows that represented the promise of California's agrarian prosperity.

## Description

The Santa Clara Valley in the early twentieth century was a patchwork of orchards and small ranches, threaded with interurban trolley lines and dotted with bungalows. Helen, driving through the territory, saw "the miles of orchard around it, interlaced with trolley-lines, for the thousands of bungalows on ranches no larger than gardens" (023-005). The valley's scale was intimate rather than industrial — the ranches were small enough to be called gardens, and the communities were connected by trolley rather than highway.

The region's history stretched back much further than its recent agricultural development: "Here, within the memory of men yet living, fields of wild mustard had hidden hundreds of grazing cattle and vaqueros, riding down to them from the foothills, had vanished in seas of yellow bloom; here the padres had trudged patiently on the road from Santa Clara to Mission San José; here pioneers had broken the raw soil and lined the cup of the valley with golden wheat fields" (028-010).

## Significance

The Santa Clara Valley represents the agricultural promise of California — the fertile land that drew settlers and speculators alike. For Helen, it was her professional territory: Hutchinson "got a small advance on commission, and the Santa Clara Valley territory" (023-005). Working this valley, she developed a genuine affection for the place and its communities, thinking of San José as "like a sturdy old geranium plant, woody-stemmed, whose roots were thick in every foot of the Santa Clara Valley" (023-005).

The valley also appears as part of a larger historical layering: Spanish missionaries, Mexican vaqueros, American pioneers, and finally orchardists and land speculators all left their mark on the same ground. The landscape Helen sold was not blank but thick with previous lives.
