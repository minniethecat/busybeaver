## Overview

Telegraph Hill is a San Francisco neighborhood whose steep, "crazy stairs that were the streets" are home to a diverse community of Italian, Chinese, and Spanish immigrants. Helen Davies's encounters on Telegraph Hill broaden her understanding of the city beyond the professional circles of the business district and the domestic sphere of Russian Hill.

## Helen's Encounters

### The Neighborhood

Helen's walks through Telegraph Hill reveal a San Francisco far removed from both the land-sales offices and the women's circle gatherings:

> She loved her encounters with many sides of life; the talk of the Italian waiter in a quaint Latin Quarter café; her curious friendship with a tiny Chinese mother who lived in the Wong "family house," the shadowy corridors of which were filled with a constant whispering shuffle of sandaled feet; the hordes of ragged, adorable Spanish children who ran to her for cakes when she climbed the crazy stairs that were the streets of Telegraph Hill. [024-3171]

The passage is one of the novel's richest evocations of urban life — the "whispering shuffle of sandaled feet," the "ragged, adorable Spanish children," the Italian café — each detail contributing to a portrait of a city whose vitality lies in its immigrant communities.

### The Wong Family House

Helen's "curious friendship" with a Chinese mother in the Wong family house represents a crossing of racial and cultural boundaries that was unusual in early twentieth-century American fiction. The house's "shadowy corridors" suggest both mystery and density — a lived-in space far from the airy openness of Helen's Russian Hill cottage.

## The Farewell

At Helen's departure, the Telegraph Hill connection is affirmed in a final gesture:

> Two little girls from Telegraph Hill had come down to bring a hot, wilted bunch of weed-flowers. [026-3437]

The "hot, wilted bunch of weed-flowers" — offered by children who climbed down from the hill to see her off — is among the novel's most poignant images of unremarked connection and departure.
