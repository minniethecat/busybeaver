## Overview

Anne Lester is an economics teacher and a member of the circle of professional women who gather around [[helen-davies|Helen Davies]]. "High-bred, imperious, with something of untamed freedom in every gesture," Anne represents the politically conscious wing of the group, engaging passionately with labor issues and social questions of her era [024-3133].

## Character

### Political Passion

Anne is introduced in the context of her profession and her political [[marriage-and-courtship|engagement]]:

> Anne, high-bred, imperious, with something of untamed freedom in every gesture — Anne was a teacher of economics! [024-3133]

At the beach picnic, she engages in heated argument with [[dodo|Dodo]] over labor unions and the I.W.W., her "red glimmer of her cigarette" sketching her gestures upon the darkness [024-3139].

### Intellectual Breadth

Anne's intellectual interests span contemporary social questions and the history of ideas. She is described reading a *History of the Warfare of Science and Religion in Christendom* while listening to [[willetta|Willetta]]'s stories [024-3177].

### On Women and War

Anne articulates an internationalist perspective on women's changing roles when [[dodo|Dodo]] argues that career women are "freaks":

> "In America!" Anne retorted. "You won't find them in their homes any more in France or England. The girls aren't marrying in their teens over there, not since the war. They're going to work — just as we did. They're going into business. Already French women are increasing the exports of France — *increasing* them! We may be freaks, Dodo, but we're going to have lots of company." [024-3251]

This ex[[change-and-progress|change]] captures the novel's [[marriage-and-courtship|engagement]] with the transformative effect of World War I on women's social and economic roles.
