# Grant Avenue

San Francisco's principal commercial boulevard, known for its suave buildings — a street Helen Davies passed on her final journey through the city, its polished storefronts a sharp contrast to her inner turmoil.

## Description

Grant Avenue ran through the heart of San Francisco, its "suave buildings" representing the city's commercial sophistication (027-034). The avenue bordered Chinatown, whose "colorful vegetable markets and glittering shops" offered a vivid counterpoint to Grant Avenue's more restrained elegance. Together, Chinatown and Grant Avenue formed the commercial core of the neighborhood through which Helen's taxi carried her to the steamer.

In Helen's final glimpse, Grant Avenue appeared as part of a sequence: "San Francisco's hilly streets, Chinatown's colorful vegetable markets and glittering shops, Grant Avenue's suave buildings" (027-034). The progression from the rough cobblestones of the residential hills, through the exotic bustle of Chinatown, to the polished facades of Grant Avenue, traced a map of the city's social geography in a single taxi ride.

## Significance

Grant Avenue appears only in the departure scene, but its placement in the sequence of final images gives it weight. "Suave" is an unusual adjective for buildings — it suggests smoothness, polish, a certain worldly sophistication. Grant Avenue, in Helen's eyes, was the face San Francisco presented to the world: confident, commercial, modern.

The contrast between Grant Avenue's composure and Helen's own state — clutching her bag, her roses, her steamer-rug, feeling "nothing but a sense of unreality" — underscores the gulf between the city's steady rhythms and the upheaval in her own life. The "suave buildings" would remain, indifferent to her departure.
