# Monsieur Gillenormand

Luc-Esprit Gillenormand is the novel's most fully realized portrait of the old order — a royalist, a bourgeois, a man of the eighteenth century who has outlived his era but refuses to admit it. His relationship with his grandson Marius is Hugo's study in how love can bridge the generational chasm that politics cannot.

## Identity

- **Full Name**: Luc-Esprit Gillenormand
- **Role**: Representative of the old order — the generation that must yield
- **First Appearance**: Volume III, Book II, Chapter I — "A Bourgeois of Ninety" (147-001)
- **Social Position**: Wealthy bourgeois, former habitué of the salons of the ancien régime, father-in-law of Colonel Pontmercy (whom he despises), grandfather of Marius
- **Key Relationships**: Marius (grandson, estranged then reconciled), Georges Pontmercy (son-in-law, despised Bonapartist), Mademoiselle Gillenormand (daughter)

## Character Arc

Gillenormand's transformation is the novel's most modest — not a conversion but a capitulation of love. He begins as a rigid royalist who has forbidden Marius from seeing his Bonapartist father; he ends as a grandfather who would rather have a living republican grandson than a dead royalist one.

## Key Actions

- **Alienation of Marius** (Book III) — Gillenormand's political rigidity drives Marius from his house into poverty
- **Reconciliation at the barricade** (Book XIV) — Gillenormand, learning Marius may be dead, offers to embrace any politics if only his grandson will return
