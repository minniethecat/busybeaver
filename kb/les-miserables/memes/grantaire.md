# Grantaire

Grantaire is the novel's most sophisticated minor character — a drunk, a cynic, a mocker of every ideal, who nevertheless loves Enjolras with a devotion that surpasses any political commitment. His death — asking to be shot beside the revolutionary leader he could never believe in — is Hugo's most compressed argument that love transcends ideology.

## Identity

- **Full Name**: Grantaire (often called "R")
- **Role**: Skeptic and foil — doubt redeemed by love
- **First Appearance**: Volume III, Book IV, Chapter I
- **Social Position**: Student, drunk, hanger-on of the Friends of the ABC
- **Key Relationships**: Enjolras (the only thing he believes in)

## Character Arc

Grantaire does not change — he is consistent in his skepticism from first to last. What changes is the context: at the barricade, his habitual drunken sleep becomes a state from which he wakes to choose death. "Long live the Republic! I am one of them," he says, and the soldiers shoot him beside Enjolras. It is the novel's most concentrated image of grace — belief granted at the last moment, not through argument but through love.
