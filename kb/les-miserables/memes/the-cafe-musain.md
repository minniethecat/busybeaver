# The Cafe Musain

The Cafe Musain is the novel's most important interior — the back room where the Friends of the ABC gather to debate philosophy, plan revolution, and forge the friendships that will be tested at the barricade.

## In the Narrative
The Musain appears throughout Volumes III and IV as the group's headquarters. Hugo devotes an entire chapter to describing its atmosphere — the smoke, the arguments, the camaraderie — and it is here that the novel's political philosophy is most directly articulated in dialogue.

## Hugo's Description
> The cafe is described as a space of intellectual ferment — students arguing over maps, Grantaire drinking in the corner, Enjolras speaking in the low voice of absolute conviction.

## Symbolic / Thematic Significance
The Musain embodies **intellectual revolution** — the back room where ideas are forged before they become barricades.
