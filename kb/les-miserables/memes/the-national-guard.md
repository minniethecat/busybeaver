# The National Guard

The National Guard (*Garde Nationale*) is the citizen militia that represents, in Hugo's treatment, the armed defense of a social order built on injustice. They are not villains — Hugo acknowledges their courage and discipline — but they are the instruments of a regime that the novel's revolutionary characters oppose.

## Organization
- **Type**: Citizen militia — originally a revolutionary institution (founded 1789), by 1832 a force for order
- **Period**: Active throughout the novel's era; the key engagement is June 5–6, 1832
- **Key Members**: Unnamed — Hugo treats the Guard as a collective, not as individuals

## In the Narrative
The National Guard appears as the force besieging the barricade. Hugo's description of their assault is professional and respectful — they are soldiers doing their duty — but the narrative's sympathy lies with the insurgents. The Guard's bullets kill Gavroche, execute Enjolras, and wound Marius.

## Hugo's Commentary
Hugo's treatment of the Guard is characteristic of his historical method: he acknowledges their professionalism while insisting that the order they defend is unjust. The Guard is not evil; it is a tool, and the hand that wields it belongs to a regime that Hugo, in exile, opposed.
