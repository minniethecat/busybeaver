# Valjean Spares Javert

Valjean's sparing of Javert at the barricade is the novel's supreme act of mercy — the moment when the chain of compassion that began with the Bishop's pardon of Valjean achieves its final, symmetrical expression. The convict spares the policeman. The hunted releases the hunter. And Javert's world collapses.

## In the Narrative
The scene (Book XIV, Chapter VII) is brief and wordless. Valjean, who has been given Javert to execute, instead cuts his bonds and tells him: "You are free." Javert, unable to process this — a convict showing mercy to a policeman is a category error in his universe — walks away into the night.

## Key Moments
- Valjean cutting Javert's bonds — the convict as agent of mercy
- "You are free" — the words that destroy Javert's worldview
- Javert walking away — the beginning of his unraveling

## Symbolic / Thematic Significance
The sparing embodies **mercy's circuit** — the chain from the Bishop to Valjean to Javert, from the sacred to the criminal to the policeman. It is the novel's definitive argument that mercy is more powerful than law.
