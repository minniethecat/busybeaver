# The Patron-Minette Gang

The Patron-Minette gang is the novel's portrait of organized crime — the dark twin of the Friends of the ABC. Both groups are products of poverty and social exclusion; the Friends respond with revolutionary idealism, the Patron-Minette with predatory violence.

## Organization
- **Type**: Criminal organization
- **Period**: Active c. 1820–1832
- **Headquarters**: The criminal underworld of Paris (no fixed address)
- **Key Members**: Patron-Minette (leader and namesake), Babet (the intellectual), Claquesous (the invisible), Gueulemer (the brute), Montparnasse (the dandy-murderer)

## In the Narrative
The gang appears most prominently in the Gorbeau ambush, where they are hired by Thénardier (as Jondrette) to extort money from Valjean. Hugo describes each member with the same individuating care he gives to the Friends — the parallel is deliberate. The difference is not in social origin but in moral response.

## Hugo's Commentary
The Patron-Minette are not romanticized — Hugo describes their violence with unflinching clarity — but they are contextualized. They are what the social order produces when it offers neither education, employment, nor hope. The Friends choose the barricade; the Patron-Minette choose the knife. The society that made both groups possible is the defendant.
