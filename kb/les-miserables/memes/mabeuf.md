# Monsieur Mabeuf

Monsieur Mabeuf is the novel's quietest tragedy — an elderly scholar and botanist whom poverty destroys not dramatically but incrementally, book by book, until he has nothing left but his life, which he gives to the revolution he does not understand.

## Identity

- **Full Name**: Mabeuf (first name unknown)
- **Role**: Symbol of old France destroyed by economic forces
- **First Appearance**: Volume III, Book V, Chapter I
- **Social Position**: Former churchwarden → amateur botanist → destitute → barricade martyr
- **Key Relationships**: Colonel Pontmercy (whom he knew and whose garden he tended), Marius (whom he helps)

## Character Arc

Mabeuf's arc is a straight descent. He sells his beloved botanical plates one by one to buy bread; each sale is a small death. His appearance at the barricade, where he raises the flag and is shot, is the novel's image of apolitical martyrdom — a man destroyed by the old order dying, almost accidentally, for the new.

## Key Actions

- **Selling his books** (Book VIII) — each volume of his botanical library sold for subsistence
- **Death at the barricade** (Book XIV) — Mabeuf raises the flag, is shot, and becomes an accidental revolutionary martyr
