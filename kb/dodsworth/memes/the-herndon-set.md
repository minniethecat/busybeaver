# The Herndon Set

The social circle orbiting Lord Herndon — the English aristocrats, politicians, and cultivated hangers-on who populate his London house and country estate — represents the most exclusive version of the European social world the Dodsworths encounter. Unlike the De Pénable circle, which Fran can aspire to join through performance and persistence, the Herndon set is closed by birth: it can be visited but never entered.

## Description

Lewis renders the Herndon set through its effects on Sam rather than through direct description. Its members are effortlessly superior, quietly assured, indifferent to the American visitor not out of hostility but out of an absolute certainty that they need nothing from him. Their manners are exquisite and their assumptions unexamined; their conversation is cultivated and their curiosity, about anything beyond their own world, is nil.

The set's most striking characteristic is its impenetrability. Sam can visit Herndon's country house, can dine at his table, can be the object of his diagnostic attention — but he can never be of the set. It is a circle drawn by birth, and Sam was born outside it. The recognition of this fact — which Fran never fully accepts and Sam gradually comes to appreciate — is one of the novel's key lessons about the limits of social ambition.

## Role in the Novel

The Herndon set provides the novel's purest case study in aristocratic exclusivity. It is not hostile (the De Pénable circle, in its way, is more dismissive of Sam); it is simply closed. Lord Herndon's genuine interest in Sam — his careful, courteous, devastating diagnosis of the Dodsworth marriage — takes place across an unbridgeable social distance. The set will observe Sam; it will not admit him.

This experience teaches Sam something Fran cannot learn: that the European world she pursues does not want her, and that the wanting itself — the performance, the aspiration, the anxiety — is what ensures she will never be admitted. The Herndon set, by its very indifference, liberates Sam from the ambition that consumes his wife.

## Key People

Lord Herndon presides. Major Lockert mediates between the English and American worlds. Sam Dodsworth visits and withdraws. Fran Dodsworth aspires and is not admitted.
