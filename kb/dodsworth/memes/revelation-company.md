# Revelation Motor Company

The Revelation Motor Company is the industrial enterprise that defines Sam Dodsworth's American life. He built it, led it, and was formed by it — and its absorption by the larger Unit Automotive Company at the novel's opening is the event that propels the Dodsworths toward Europe and the novel's entire subsequent action.

## Description

The Revelation Motor Company offices occupy "an immense glass and marble building on Constitution Avenue, North, above Court House Square, opposite the flashing new skyscraper of the Plymouth National Bank" (002-003). The executive floor is "like the lobby of a pretentious hotel — waiting-room in brocade and tapestry and Grand Rapids renaissance; then something like an acre of little tables with typists and typists and typists" (002-003). Lewis renders the building as both impressive and faintly absurd — a monument to American industrial ambition that mistakes scale for significance.

The private offices feature "enormous desks in imitation of refectory tables, covered with enormous sheets of plate glass, and fanatically kept free of papers and all jolly disorder" (002-003). The description captures the Revelation Company's essence: prosperous, energetic, and slightly naive about its own importance.

## Significance in the Novel

Sam's identity is inseparable from the Revelation Company. When Alec Kynance of the U.A.C. arrives to finalize the acquisition, Sam confronts the question that will define the rest of the novel: who is he without the company he built? The sale, which makes Sam wealthy but purposeless, is the novel's inciting event — the economic fact that makes the European journey possible and the existential crisis that makes it necessary.

The Revelation building also embodies the world Fran is fleeing. Its typists, its plate-glass desks, its earnest commercial energy — these are precisely what she hopes to leave behind in Europe.

## Key Events

**The U.A.C. acquisition (Chapter II)**: Alec Kynance's arrival and the final transfer of holdings — the business transaction that ends Sam's industrial career.

**Sam's departure from the office**: Leaving the building he built for the last time, Sam begins the journey from industrialist to traveler to something not yet defined.

## Characters Associated

Sam Dodsworth built and led the company. Alec Kynance, president of the U.A.C., appears as the agent of its absorption. Tub Pearson, as a Zenith banker, is connected to its financial life.
