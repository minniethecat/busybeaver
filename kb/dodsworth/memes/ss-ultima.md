# SS Ultima

The S.S. *Ultima* is the ocean liner on which the Dodsworths cross the Atlantic — a thirty-two-thousand-ton floating world that serves as the novel's threshold between American and European life. Aboard the Ultima, Sam discovers his capacity for seafaring, Fran discovers her talent for transatlantic flirtation, and the dynamics that will define their European journey are rehearsed in miniature.

## Description

Lewis renders the Ultima with the appreciation of a mechanic: "the most sure and impressive mechanism he had ever seen; more satisfying than a Rolls, a Delauney-Belleville" (005-004). Sam marvels at "the authoritative steadiness with which the bow mastered the waves" and "the powerful sweep of the lines of the deck" (005-004). The ship is at once a triumph of engineering and a self-contained society: "the roseate music room, the smoking-room with its Tudor fireplace — solid and terrestrial as a castle — and the swimming-pool, green-lighted water washing beneath Roman pillars" (005-004).

For Sam, the Ultima awakens "some never-realized desire for seafaring" (005-004). The ship's "long straight lines of the decking planks, rigid as bars of music, divided by seams of glistening tar" (005-012) become a kind of poem — the novel's most sustained passage of physical description, rendered through a sensibility discovering its capacity for wonder.

## Significance in the Novel

The Ultima is the novel's transitional space — neither America nor Europe, a place where old identities are suspended and new ones not yet formed. It is aboard the Ultima that the Dodsworth marriage begins to reveal its essential shape: Fran collecting admirers, Sam observing from the margins, the pattern of their European life already emerging before they reach Southampton.

The ship also marks a threshold in Sam's interior life. His pleasure in its mechanical perfection, his solitary tramps around the deck, his moment of loneliness in the smoking-room — these are the first stirrings of the self he will become in Europe. The Ultima teaches him that he can be alone and not diminished by it.

## Key Events

**The first hours at sea**: Sam's unease at the after rail — "the rearing mass of the sea loomed over the ship" (005-001) — gives way to exhilaration as he discovers the pleasure of shipboard life.

**Fran and Lockert**: Aboard the Ultima, Fran's pattern of collecting male admirers crystallizes with Major Lockert, who "assumed that he was her patron" (012-001).

**The gala dance**: Under Japanese lanterns on the starboard deck, Sam remembers the Kennepoose Canoe Club — the Ultima becoming, briefly, a floating Zenith.

## Characters Associated

Sam Dodsworth discovers seafaring and solitude. Fran Dodsworth discovers the shipboard society that prefigures her European social life. Major Lockert appears as Fran's first European-style admirer, establishing the dynamic that will persist through London and Paris.
