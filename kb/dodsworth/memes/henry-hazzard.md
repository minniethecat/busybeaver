Dr. Henry Hazzard is a heart specialist in Zenith and a member of [[Sam Dodsworth]]'s intimate circle of friends. Along with [[Tub Pearson]], he represents the solid, professional Zenith society that Sam leaves behind on his European journey—and to which he eventually contemplates returning.

## Biographical Sketch

Hazzard is a distinguished physician in Zenith, a heart specialist who has achieved sufficient prominence to move in the city's elite business and professional circles. He is introduced as one of Sam's monthly dinner companions, alongside "Tub Pearson, his humorous classmate who was now the gray and oracular president of the Centaur State Bank, Dr. Henry Hazzard, the heart specialist, Judge Turpin, and Wheeler, the manufacturer of steel office furniture" (003-039).

By the time Sam returns from Europe, Hazzard has become the center of the weekly poker game that Sam's friends maintain. "It was to the house of Dr. Henry Hazzard that Tub looked now for his weekly drug of poker. Sam saw that Hazzard was at least as necessary to Tub as himself" (019-002)—a realization that both comforts Sam (his friends have not been lonely without him) and slightly wounds him (he is replaceable).

## Role in the Novel

Hazzard appears only briefly but carries significant thematic weight. He represents the continuity of Zenith life—the professional class that runs the city, the friendships built over decades, the poker games that provide reliable masculine companionship. When Sam is adrift in Europe, thinking of home, Hazzard's name recurs as a touchstone.

> While lolling in his Paris hotel room, Sam "thought of answering Henry Hazzard's last letter. But—oh, thunder, there was no news" (025-002). The unmailed letter symbolizes Sam's growing disconnection from Zenith life.

In the novel's final chapters, when Sam contemplates returning to America, the thought of seeing Hazzard figures among his anticipated pleasures: "the next steamer and see the baby, Emily, Harry, Brent, Tub, Henry Hazzard" (025-009). Hazzard is listed among the people who make Zenith feel like home.

## Key Scenes

**The monthly dinner (Chapter 3)** — Hazzard appears as one of the five men who form Sam's core Zenith circle. This scene establishes the professional and social world Sam is leaving behind.

**The shifted poker game (Chapter 19)** — Upon Sam's return to Zenith for a visit, he discovers that the poker game has migrated to Hazzard's house. This small detail signals the larger truth that life in Zenith has continued without Sam—his friends have adapted, and his place in their world is no longer central.

## Character Analysis

Hazzard is a background figure, but Lewis uses him with characteristic economy. As a heart specialist, his profession carries a gentle irony—he treats physical hearts while the novel explores the emotional heartbreaks of Sam's midlife crisis. The weekly poker game at Hazzard's house represents a form of masculine community that Sam loses in Europe, where social life is dominated by Fran's preferences.

> Sam's reflection that "Hazzard was at least as necessary to Tub as himself" (019-002) is a moment of mature self-awareness. It acknowledges that friendships are not exclusive possessions but part of a larger social fabric—a theme that resonates with Sam's eventual acceptance of change.
