# Renée de Pénable

Renée de Pénable is a French woman of society who appears in the Paris chapters. She represents the kind of European femininity that Fran simultaneously admires and envies: effortlessly sophisticated, securely placed in a social world that Fran can only visit.

## Biographical Sketch

Renée moves in the Parisian circles that Fran aspires to join. She is described with the economy Lewis reserves for minor characters who function as social markers: her name, her nationality, and her social position are her identity. She is not developed psychologically — she does not need to be. Her role is to represent the achieved cosmopolitanism that Fran can only pursue.

## Role in the Novel

Renée functions as a mirror in which Fran sees both her aspiration and her limitation. Renée IS what Fran wants to become — and her very existence demonstrates that Fran will always be an arriviste in the world Renée inhabits by birth. The Paris chapters use characters like Renée to dramatize the gap between the Europe Fran imagines and the Europe that actually exists.
