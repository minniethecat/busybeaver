# Centaur State Bank

The Centaur State Bank, presided over by Thomas J. Pearson — Sam Dodsworth's Yale roommate and closest friend — is the financial institution that anchors Zenith's commercial establishment. Through Tub Pearson's prominence, the bank represents the intersection of personal loyalty and institutional power that characterizes the Midwestern world Sam inhabits.

## Description

The Centaur State Bank is known through its president rather than through direct description. Tub Pearson — "former state-senator, honorary LL.D. of Winnemac University, president of the Centaur State Bank, director in twelve companies, trustee of the Loring Grammar School and of the Zenith Art Institute, chairman of the Mayor's City Planning Commission" (003-012) — embodies the bank's character: prosperous, civic-minded, and unreflectively confident in the virtues of Midwestern capitalism.

The bank's name — Centaur — suggests the slightly absurd classicism of American institutional naming, a habit Lewis frequently satirizes without quite condemning. The centaur is a creature of myth, half-man and half-horse; the bank is entirely mundane, but its name gestures toward a grandeur it does not need and cannot quite achieve.

## Role in the Novel

The Centaur State Bank functions primarily as an extension of Tub Pearson's character: it is the institutional expression of the loyalty, prosperity, and cheerful conventionality that Tub represents. Sam's friendship with Tub is mediated through their shared institutional world — the bank, the university, the club — and the bank is one of the pillars of that world.

When Sam returns to America for his Yale reunion, the Centaur State Bank is part of the familiar landscape that welcomes him home. It is, like Zenith itself, a world he has outgrown but cannot entirely leave behind.

## Key People

Tub Pearson — "the Hon. Thos. J. Pearson" — is the bank's president and public face. His progression from Yale class jester to bank president to civic eminence traces the arc of the successful Midwestern establishment figure.
