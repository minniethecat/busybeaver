# Ross Ireland

Ross Ireland is an American journalist working in Europe — a man who, like Sam, is an American abroad, but who navigates the transatlantic world with the ease of long practice. He offers Sam a different model of American cosmopolitanism: critical of Europe without being defensive, appreciative of America without being boastful.

## Biographical Sketch

Ireland is a journalist — the novel does not specify his publication — who has spent enough time in Europe to know its codes without being captured by them. He is observant, articulate, and possessed of the journalist's gift for extracting insight from experience. Unlike Sam, who is bewildered by Europe, and Fran, who is seduced by it, Ireland treats Europe as a beat: interesting, worth understanding, but not a source of identity.

## Role in the Novel

Ireland appears in the later European chapters as a counterweight to the dominant cultural dynamic. Where Lockert represents the European perspective on America, Ireland represents the American perspective on Europe that is neither chauvinistic nor self-hating. His conversations with Sam offer the possibility of an American engagement with Europe that does not require choosing between Zenith and Venice.

## Key Scenes

**Encounters in Europe**: Ireland's observations on the expatriate scene, on European politics, and on the Americans he encounters — delivered with the journalist's detachment and the compatriot's sympathy.
