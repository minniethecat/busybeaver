# Ford Motor Company

The Ford Motor Company is the unseen giant of the American automobile industry in *Dodsworth* — a real corporation whose presence shadows the fictional Revelation Motor Company and the entire world of independent auto manufacturing that [[sam-dodsworth|Sam Dodsworth]] inhabits. Ford is never depicted directly, but its influence is felt in every business decision Sam makes, every consolidation that threatens his independence, and every conversation about the future of the industry he loves.

## The Industrial Context

By the 1920s, when the novel's central action takes place, Ford has transformed automobile manufacturing from a craft into a science. The moving assembly line, the Model T's unprecedented affordability, the five-dollar day — these innovations have made Ford synonymous with American industrial might. For independent manufacturers like the Revelation Motor Company, Ford represents both an inspiration and an existential threat. The logic of mass production that Ford pioneered is the same logic that, in its later form as financial consolidation, will absorb Sam's company into the Unit Automotive Company.

Sam is not a Ford-like figure. He is not Henry Ford — the solitary genius imposing his will on an industry. Sam is a builder, a craftsman who cares about "a new kind of beauty for autos" with "long straight lines." His vision is aesthetic and democratic — "the largest profits would lie in selling automobiles as cheaply as possible to as many customers as possible" (001-067) — but his methods are those of the pre-Ford era: personal, intuitive, small-scale. The novel chronicles what happens when that kind of industrialist encounters the forces of consolidation that Ford's revolution set in motion.

## Ford as Symbol

Ford functions in the novel as a metonym for American capitalism itself — creative, ruthless, democratic in its effects and autocratic in its methods. When characters discuss the automobile industry, Ford is the unspoken benchmark:

| Company | Role in Novel | Real/Fictional |
|---------|--------------|----------------|
| Ford Motor Company | The industry standard; mass production pioneer | Real |
| Revelation Motor Company | Sam's company; absorbed by U.A.C. | Fictional |
| Unit Automotive Company (U.A.C.) | The consolidator; "seven makes of motors, billion dollars of capital" | Fictional |
| Zenith Locomotive Works | Sam's first employer | Fictional |

The fact that Sam builds his career in the shadow of Ford — and that his company is absorbed not by Ford but by the financial logic Ford's success enabled — is one of the novel's quiet ironies. The industrial revolution that Ford represents creates the conditions for its own supersession by finance capital.
