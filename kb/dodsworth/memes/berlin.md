# Berlin

Berlin is the city where the Dodsworth marriage reaches its breaking point. It is here that Fran's pursuit of European aristocracy crystallizes into a specific object — Kurt von Obersdorf — and here that Sam finally recognizes that his marriage is beyond repair.

## Description

Lewis's Berlin is a city of social possibility and moral exposure. It offers Fran the access to titled Europeans she has been seeking since the journey began, and it offers Sam the clarity he has been avoiding: the recognition that Fran has chosen a future in which he has no place. Berlin in the 1920s was a city of cultivated decadence, of postwar reckoning and prewar elegance, and Lewis captures both its glamour and its capacity to expose what travelers bring with them.

## Significance in the Novel

Berlin functions as the novel's point of no return. The preceding cities — London, Paris — offered opportunities for reconciliation that neither Sam nor Fran was willing to take. Berlin forecloses those opportunities. Fran's declaration that she intends to leave Sam for von Obersdorf is the crisis the entire novel has been building toward, and Berlin — sophisticated, amoral, indifferent — is its appropriate setting.

The irony is that Berlin, for all its glamour, cannot deliver what Fran seeks. Von Obersdorf may be charmed by her, but the Austrian aristocracy is no more likely to accept an American divorcée than the English aristocracy was. Berlin exposes not just the Dodsworth marriage but Fran's entire project: the European life she has been pursuing does not exist, or exists only on terms she cannot meet.

## Key Events

**Fran's pursuit of von Obersdorf**: The relationship that precipitates the marital crisis.

**Sam's recognition**: The moment when Sam stops hoping for reconciliation and begins to imagine a life beyond Fran.

## Characters Associated

Fran finds in Berlin the titled European she has been seeking; Sam finds the clarity he has been avoiding. Kurt von Obersdorf is the object of Fran's ambition and the catalyst for Sam's liberation.
