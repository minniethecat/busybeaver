# London

London is the Dodsworths' first port of call in Europe, and Lewis uses it to stage the novel's initial transatlantic encounters. It is here that Sam first feels the weight of European history and the indifference of European society to American achievement.

## Description

Lewis's London is a city of "traditional, unsmiling stateliness" (008-009) — Pall Mall clubs, country house weekends, and the quiet assumption of superiority that the English wear as naturally as their clothing. Sam, accustomed to being Someone in Zenith, finds himself invisible: "anything to assure him that he was someone and meant something, here in this city... among these unhurried, well-dressed people who so thoroughly ignored him" (008-009).

The city triggers both defensive patriotism and genuine curiosity in Sam. He buys five hats in a single afternoon — "beginning the Europeanization which he wasn't going to begin" (008-016) — and finds himself moved by the unexpected discovery of his own ancestral connections: "Mother England! Land of his ancestors" (006-020).

## Significance in the Novel

London is the novel's threshold: the point at which the European journey ceases to be an abstraction and becomes a concrete, disorienting experience. Sam's reactions to London — by turns defensive, curious, homesick, and unexpectedly moved — establish the emotional register of the entire European section.

## Key Events

**First morning in London**: Sam's disorientation — the hotel, the breakfast, the unreadable social codes — crystallizes the American experience of Europe.

**The Pall Mall walk (Chapter VIII)**: Sam, invisible among the English, seeks the reassurance of an American bank window — the novel's emblematic image of transatlantic dislocation.

**The country house weekend (Chapter X)**: Sam confronts the English aristocracy en masse and finds the experience "too rich for my blood."

## Characters Associated

Sam experiences London most intensely; Fran navigates it most skillfully. Lord Herndon appears here as host and diagnostician. The Hurd, manager of the London Revelation agency, provides Sam's thin connection to his American life.
