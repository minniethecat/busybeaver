# Pearson's Bank

Pearson's Bank — formally the Centaur State Bank of Zenith — is the financial institution owned by the family of [[tub-pearson|Tub Pearson]], Sam Dodsworth's closest friend. It represents the established financial order of [[zenith|Zenith]]: conservative, provincial, and deeply embedded in the social networks that define Sam's pre-European world.

## Role in the Novel

The bank is primarily significant as the institutional embodiment of Tub Pearson's social position. Tub, "teller and future president of his father's bank," belongs to the hereditary financial elite of Zenith — a world of inherited position rather than entrepreneurial risk. Where Sam builds the Revelation Motor Company through years of struggle and innovation, Tub inherits his place in the Zenith order.

This contrast between the builder and the inheritor is central to the novel's social analysis. Sam creates value through manufacturing; Tub's bank circulates value created by others. The difference in their temperaments — Sam's restless energy versus Tub's comfortable, witty complacency — reflects the difference between industrial and financial capitalism as Lewis understood them.

## Tub as Banker

Tub Pearson is introduced in the novel's opening scene as "round and short and jolly, class jester and class dandy at Yale" — and already marked for a life of inherited ease. His position at the bank is not something he has earned but something he has been born into. This does not make Tub unsympathetic — he is, throughout the novel, Sam's most loyal friend — but it does make him a representative of a social order that Sam's industrial energy has, ironically, helped to undermine.

When Sam returns to Zenith midway through the novel, he finds that the world of Tub's bank, the Zenith country club, and the established commercial order no longer has a place for him. The bank is still there, Tub is still there, but the industrialist who once gave that world its dynamism has become a tourist in his own city.
