The **British Embassy at Constantinople** is the diplomatic mission where Orlando serves as British Ambassador to the Ottoman Empire, a position of extraordinary prestige for one so young. This posting marks a decisive turn in the novel: it is in Constantinople that Orlando undergoes the mysterious gender transformation that reshapes the remainder of the narrative.

## The Embassy in Orlando

Orlando arrives in Constantinople with great ceremony and takes up residence at the Embassy. Woolf describes the diplomatic life — the levees, the dispatches, the ceremonial visits — with the same blend of historical detail and fantastical embellishment that characterises the novel. The Embassy provides the backdrop for Orlando's growing detachment from the masculine world of power and diplomacy.

The Embassy scenes culminate in the extraordinary events surrounding Orlando's transformation: the insurrection in the city, the mysterious trance that lasts seven days, and the eventual awakening as a woman. The Embassy thus functions as the threshold between Orlando's male and female lives.

## Historical Context

British diplomatic presence in Constantinople (Istanbul) dated from the late 16th century, with the Levant Company maintaining official representation. By the 18th century, the Embassy was a key posting in British foreign policy, managing relations with the Ottoman Porte.
