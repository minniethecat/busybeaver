The **Constantinople Insurrection** is a popular uprising that breaks out in the Ottoman capital during Orlando's tenure as British Ambassador. The insurrection provides the tumultuous backdrop against which Orlando undergoes the novel's central event: a seven-day trance followed by a physical transformation from man to woman.

## Role in the Novel

Woolf describes the insurrection with characteristic detachment — crowds surge through the streets, fires blaze, and the diplomatic community retreats behind its walls. Meanwhile, Orlando lies in a deep sleep within the Embassy, oblivious to the chaos outside. The juxtaposition of political upheaval and personal metamorphosis is deliberate: the insurrection externalises the revolutionary change occurring within Orlando's body.

When Orlando awakens after seven days, the insurrection has subsided and the transformation is complete. The city's unrest and Orlando's transformation are presented as parallel upheavals — one public and political, the other private and ontological.
