**Orlando's Return to England** is the journey by ship from Constantinople back to England, undertaken after the transformation. The voyage is a liminal passage: Orlando boards the ship as one person and arrives in England prepared to live as another.

## Role in the Novel

The return voyage dramatises the disorientation of Orlando's new condition. On board, Orlando reflects on the differences between living as a man and as a woman — the ship, with its all-male crew, provides a concentrated version of the gendered world Orlando must now navigate differently. The voyage is also the occasion for Orlando's first experience of being seen and treated as a woman by men.

Upon landing in England, Orlando faces the practical consequences of the transformation: legal challenges to identity and property, the resumption of social life in a new gender, and the dawning realisation of how profoundly society's expectations differ for women.
