The **Frost Fair** is the temporary carnival city erected on the frozen surface of the River Thames during the Great Frost of 1608. The fair provides the magical, liminal setting in which Orlando first encounters the Russian princess Sasha and falls passionately in love.

## The Fair in Orlando

Woolf's description of the Frost Fair is one of the novel's most celebrated set pieces. The frozen river becomes a city in miniature — tents and booths, ox roasts, puppet shows, archery contests, and skating parties. Social distinctions are temporarily suspended: the King mingles with commoners, and the Russian embassy's exotic visitors move through the crowd like figures from a fairy tale.

Orlando spots Sasha skating on the ice and is immediately captivated. Their courtship unfolds against the backdrop of the fair — a world of ice and firelight, music and carnival, that feels outside ordinary time. The fair is a space of enchantment that cannot last, and its dissolution with the thaw mirrors the dissolution of Orlando's romance.

## Historical Basis

Historical Frost Fairs were held on the Thames during exceptionally cold winters, most notably in 1608, 1683–84, and 1814. Woolf draws on these historical events to create a setting that is at once documentable and fantastic.
