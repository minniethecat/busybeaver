The **Gipsy Camp near Brusa** is the temporary home of the gipsy tribe that Orlando joins after departing Constantinople. Situated in the hills of Anatolia, the camp provides a setting radically different from the English estates and London drawing-rooms that otherwise dominate the novel.

## Significance in the Novel

The camp serves as a transitional space where Orlando, newly transformed, can exist outside the rigid categories of English society. The gipsies' relationship with the natural world — direct, practical, unmediated by aesthetic sentiment — offers a counterpoint to Orlando's cultivated sensibility. The camp is where Orlando must confront the question of what it means to belong: to a people, to a gender, to a landscape.

The episode ends when Orlando feels the pull of England and civilisation, departing the camp to return to a society that will now receive Orlando as a woman.
