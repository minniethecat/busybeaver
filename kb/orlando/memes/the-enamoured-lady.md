The ***Enamoured Lady*** is the merchant sailing vessel on which Marmaduke Bonthrop Shelmerdine serves, making voyages around Cape Horn. The ship is the primary locus of Shelmerdine's life and, by extension, represents the world of masculine adventure that both attracts and separates him from Orlando.

## Significance in the Novel

The ship's name — *Enamoured Lady* — is richly suggestive: it links the vessel to the novel's concern with love and gender, feminising the object of Shelmerdine's devotion. Just as Orlando is devoted to "The Oak Tree" (both the poem and the physical tree), Shelmerdine is devoted to his ship, creating a symmetry between the two characters' passions — one literary and rooted, the other maritime and mobile.

The voyages around Cape Horn become a recurring motif: Shelmerdine's departures and returns structure the later chapters, and the ship's perilous journeys mirror the emotional risks of the couple's unconventional marriage.
