# Sir Adrian Scrope

**Sir Adrian Scrope** was a British naval officer who appears in Chapter 3 of *Orlando*, arriving in Constantinople at the end of Ramadan bearing Orlando's patent of nobility and the insignia of the Order of the Bath.

## Role in Novel

Sir Adrian Scrope commanded the frigate that brought the honours from England to the British Embassy in Constantinople. The narrator records that "the Order of the Bath and the patent of nobility arrived in a frigate commanded by Sir Adrian Scrope" [009-008], and Orlando marked the occasion with "an entertainment more splendid than any that has been known before or since in Constantinople" [009-008].

At the investiture ceremony, Scrope appears in "the full dress of a British Admiral" [009-010], placing the Collar of the Most Noble Order of the Bath around Orlando's neck and pinning the Star to his breast. The narrator then notes that "another gentleman of the diplomatic corps advancing in a stately manner placed on his shoulders the ducal robes" [009-010].

## Historical Context

The historical Sir Adrian Scrope (or Scroope) belonged to a prominent family with connections to the Cromwellian and Restoration periods. In Woolf's novel, his presence serves as a formal link between the British Crown and its distant ambassador — a reminder of the ceremonial machinery of empire, even at its furthest outpost.

## Constantinople and the Insurrection

Scrope's presence takes on a more practical significance during the insurrection that follows. The narrator remarks that "what might not have happened, had it not been for the presence of Sir Adrian Scrope and a squad of British bluejackets, nobody can say" [009-011], suggesting that naval force provided crucial protection during the uprising that coincided with Orlando's seven-day trance and subsequent transformation.
