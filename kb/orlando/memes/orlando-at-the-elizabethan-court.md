**Orlando at the Elizabethan Court** refers to the period of Orlando's youth when he first gains the favour of Queen Elizabeth I and begins his life as a courtier. This episode opens the novel and establishes the template of favour, ambition, and precarious fortune that will characterise Orlando's subsequent lives.

## The Episode

Orlando, a boy of sixteen from a noble family, is presented at court and immediately catches the Queen's eye. His beauty and innocence charm the aging monarch, who grants him estates, wealth, and the Order of the Garter. The Queen's favour transforms Orlando's fortunes overnight, but the novel hints at the fragility of such preferment: the Queen's death, when it comes, will leave Orlando exposed to the less forgiving regime of King James I.

## Significance

This opening establishes the novel's fairy-tale logic — the beautiful youth, the granting of gifts, the magical transformation of fortune — while grounding it in the historical reality of the Elizabethan court. The episode also introduces the theme of Orlando's beauty as both gift and vulnerability.
