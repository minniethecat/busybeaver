**Sasha Abandons Orlando** at the close of the Great Frost — a moment of romantic devastation that reverberates through the rest of the novel. Orlando, having fallen deeply in love with the Russian princess during the Frost Fair, plans to elope with her. Instead, Sasha sails away without him.

## The Event

The Great Frost provides a dreamlike setting for Orlando's first great love: the frozen Thames becomes a carnival city, and Sasha — skating, speaking French, utterly unlike anyone Orlando has known — captivates him completely. Their romance unfolds with the intensity of first love, heightened by the strangeness of the frozen river and the exoticism of the Russian entourage.

The betrayal is devastating. Orlando waits for Sasha at the appointed hour; she does not come. He watches her ship depart down the thawing Thames. The novel registers this moment as a psychic wound — Orlando's first encounter with the unreliability of love, and perhaps with the treachery that Orlando, in later life, will learn to expect from the world.

## Significance

The abandonment by Sasha is the emotional template for several later losses and disappointments. It introduces the theme of romantic idealism colliding with worldly reality — a pattern that will be repeated and transformed as Orlando moves through subsequent lives and loves.
