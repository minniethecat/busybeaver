The **Countess of R.** is one of the aristocratic women who feature in Orlando's London social life after the return from Constantinople. Her pursuit of Orlando — and Orlando's complex response to that pursuit — dramatises the novel's exploration of gender, desire, and social expectation in the 18th century.

## Role in the Novel

The Countess represents the heterosexual romantic conventions that Orlando, now living as a woman, must navigate. Her attentions place Orlando in a position of being courted rather than courting, forcing a recalibration of the social scripts Orlando had learned as a man. Woolf uses these encounters to expose the asymmetry of gender roles: the same behaviours that Orlando once exercised as a male suitor now appear very different when experienced from the other side.
