**Reading and Interpretation** are acts that *Orlando* both performs and theorises. The novel is itself a reading of English literary history, interpreting the canon through the lens of Orlando's lived experience. At the same time, the biographer's struggles to interpret Orlando's life model the reader's own activity.

## In the Novel

The biographer — the novel's narrator — is constantly reading Orlando: reading Orlando's actions, Orlando's poem, Orlando's face for signs of gender, age, and emotion. The biographer's interpretations are often provisional, uncertain, and contested, dramatising the hermeneutic problem that every reader faces.

Orlando too is a reader — of books, of people, of historical periods. The novel suggests that reading is not passive reception but active creation: Orlando reads the Elizabethan age into being just as Woolf reads literary history into being through Orlando.
