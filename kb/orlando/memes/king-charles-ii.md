# King Charles II

**King Charles II** (1630–1685) reigned during the Restoration period, which Orlando experienced upon returning to England after her transformation in Constantinople. The Restoration court represented a dramatic shift from the Jacobean and Commonwealth periods that had intervened during Orlando's absence abroad.

## Orlando's Encounter with the Restoration

When Orlando returned to England as a woman, she found London transformed. The narrator notes that during her absence there had been "a plague and a fire" — the Great Plague of 1665 and the Great Fire of 1666 — and that St. Paul's Cathedral, "which Mr. Wren had built during her absence," now dominated the skyline [049-058].

The Restoration court under Charles II was markedly different from the court of James I that Orlando had known as a young man. The shift from Puritan Commonwealth to the "Merry Monarch's" court reflected the larger themes of transformation and the passage of time that structure the novel.

## Social World

Orlando navigated the Restoration social scene as a noblewoman, attending receptions and encountering the literary figures — Addison, Dryden, and Pope — who defined the era's cultural life. The narrator mentions her seeing these figures at the Cocoa Tree coffeehouse as her ship sailed up the Thames: "there he was — one might see Mr. Addison taking his coffee; the other two gentlemen... were Mr. Dryden and Mr. Pope" [049-062].

## Historical Context

Charles II's restoration to the throne in 1660 ended the Interregnum that had followed the execution of his father, Charles I. His reign was characterised by the reopening of theatres, the flourishing of Restoration comedy, and the establishment of the Royal Society — marking a period of cultural renaissance that Orlando observed from her position in fashionable society.
