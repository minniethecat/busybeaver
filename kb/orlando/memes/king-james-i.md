# King James I

**King James I** (1566–1625) was the monarch of England during a significant portion of Orlando's early life at court. Orlando returned to the Court of King James after a period of slumming among common people in London's beer gardens and docks, resuming his place as a favoured young nobleman [049-048].

## Role in the Narrative

King James's court serves as the backdrop for Orlando's life as a young nobleman in the early seventeenth century. After growing weary of "the primitive manners of the people" in Wapping and the beer gardens, Orlando "hung his grey cloak in his wardrobe, let his star shine at his neck and his garter twinkle at his knee, and appeared once more at the Court of King James. He was young, he was rich, he was handsome. No one could have been received with greater acclamation than he was" [049-048].

## The Great Frost and the Embassy

During James's reign, England experienced the Great Frost, described by the narrator as "the most severe that has ever visited these islands" [049-058]. The Frost Fair on the frozen Thames, held during this period, became the setting for Orlando's fateful encounter with Sasha, the Russian princess.

It was also under James's authority that Orlando was appointed ambassador to Constantinople, a posting that would lead to Orlando's mysterious transformation and the novel's pivotal turn.

## Historical Context

James I was the first Stuart king of England, succeeding Elizabeth I in 1603. As James VI of Scotland, he had already ruled Scotland since 1567. His reign united the English and Scottish crowns and saw the production of the King James Bible, though the novel makes only indirect reference to these historical achievements, focusing instead on the courtly world Orlando inhabited.
