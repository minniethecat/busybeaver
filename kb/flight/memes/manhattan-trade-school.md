## Overview

The Manhattan Trade School is a vocational training institution in [[new-york-city|New York City]] that provided industrial education — particularly in sewing and dressmaking — to young women in the early 20th century. For [[mimi-daquin|Mimi Daquin]], it represents the pathway to economic self-sufficiency after leaving the South. The school's training program, focused on practical skills rather than academic learning, reflects the educational philosophy of the era for working-class and minority women.

Mimi's training at Manhattan Trade School gives her the technical skills she needs to secure employment after her separation from [[mrs-rogers|Aunt Sophie]]. The school's curriculum also shapes her professional identity, preparing her for the exacting standards of the garment industry and ultimately for her work as a finisher at [[francines-dress-shop|Francine's]] (017-020).

## In the Novel

Mimi's training is referenced when she seeks work: she calls "to her aid the knowledge gained from the training she had received at the Manhattan Trade School" (017-020). This training proves essential in securing her position at [[francines-dress-shop|Francine's]] despite her "diffidence" and lack of direct work experience.

The school also connects Mimi to a broader world of working women. Another employee at [[francines-dress-shop|Francine's]] reveals that she too attended Manhattan Trade School, telling Mimi: "Take me, for instance. I went to Manhattan Trade, too. My real name's Bernstein" (017-037). This shared educational background creates a bond between the women, demonstrating how the school formed a network among [[new-york-city|New York]]'s female garment workers.

Mimi's Manhattan Trade School education distinguishes her from other applicants and gives her the confidence to pursue the competitive position at [[francines-dress-shop|Francine's]], the most prestigious dress shop in the city. The school thus functions as both a practical and psychological foundation for her new life passing as white.
