Ivy Street is a thoroughfare in [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta, Georgia]]. In the novel *Flight*, it is mentioned in connection with the city's slum district, paired with [[decatur-street|Decatur Street]] as marking the most impoverished and dangerous part of [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]].

## The Slum District

Ivy Street is identified alongside [[decatur-street|Decatur Street]] as [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]]'s slum area. A cab driver describes the route: he "would have carried you through Decatur and Ivy Streets — that's the slum district" ([001-007]). The pairing of the two streets establishes them as the symbolic heart of [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]]'s poverty.

> He'd have carried you through Decatur and Ivy Streets — that's the slum district — saloons and dives and cheap boarding houses. [001-007]

## Significance

Though mentioned only once in the novel, Ivy Street's association with the slum district helps establish the geography of racial and economic segregation in early twentieth-century [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]]. Together with [[decatur-street|Decatur Street]], it represents the conditions that the Daquin family sought to escape by leaving the South.
