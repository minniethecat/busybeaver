## Overview

The Atlanta Police Department's behavior during the [[atlanta-race-riot-1906|1906 race riot]] is one of the novel's most damning indictments of institutional racism. Rather than protecting Black citizens from white mobs, the police stood aside — and in some cases participated in the violence. The department's failure to maintain order revealed that the state's monopoly on legitimate violence was, in the [[jim-crow-era|Jim Crow]] South, effectively reserved for whites.

This institutional betrayal shapes Mimi's subsequent relationship to authority. Her distrust of institutions — whether the police, the courts, or the social arbiters of [[harlem-new-york|Harlem]] — is rooted in the lesson of [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]]: that the institutions that claim to protect all citizens in fact protect only some.
