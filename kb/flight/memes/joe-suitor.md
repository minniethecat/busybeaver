## Overview

Joe is a young Black man in [[philadelphia-pennsylvania|Philadelphia]] who courts Mimi during her time in the city. He represents the path not taken — a life of respectability within the Black community, with marriage, family, and the modest security that a working-class Black man could offer in the early 20th century. Joe is earnest, hardworking, and devoted — everything that respectability politics would prescribe as a suitable partner for Mimi.

But Mimi cannot choose Joe. The novel suggests that her Creole upbringing, her experience of the [[atlanta-race-riot-1906|Atlanta riot]], and her light skin have made her unfit for the conventional Black respectability that Joe represents. She has seen too much, been wounded too deeply, to find meaning in the life Joe offers. Her refusal of Joe is both a rejection of respectability politics and a recognition that the racial order offers Black people no truly safe choices.
