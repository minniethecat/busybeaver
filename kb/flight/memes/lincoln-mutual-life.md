## Overview

The Lincoln Mutual Life Insurance Company is a Black-owned insurance business in [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta, Georgia]]. Owned by [[mr-hunter|Mr. Hunter]], it provides economic opportunity for the city's African American community during the [[jim-crow-era|Jim Crow]] era. The company symbolizes the Black business class that flourished on [[auburn-avenue|Auburn Avenue]].

> "He's goin' to work down to the Lincoln Mutual Life Insurance Company—that's Mr. Hunter's company." (001-028)

[[jean-daquin|Jean Daquin]] takes employment at Lincoln Mutual through [[mr-hunter|Mr. Hunter]]. The company represents [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]]'s Black economic pragmatism, which Jean finds both impressive and alienating. His work reflects the tension between old Creole culture and the [[new-south-ideology|New South]] (004-033).

## In the Novel

[[jean-daquin|Jean Daquin]] joins through [[mr-hunter|Mr. Hunter]], the company's president, introduced as "the president of the Lincoln Mutual Life Insurance Company" (003-004).

Jean's reaction is ambivalent. He tells Mimi, "They're a fine bunch—very much in earnest—and I suppose I'll have to admit Mary and her father are right—they do put things over. Mr. Hunter's the best of the lot" (004-033). Yet he struggles with the pace of the business world.

> "I feel as if some power has pulled me out of a quiet pool where I was lying on my back looking up at the sky—and has thrown me head first into a deadly revolving whirlpool." (004-035)

After Jean's death, Mimi purchases stock in the Lincoln Mutual Insurance Company with part of her inheritance. "One of the first things Mimi did with the money she received from the sale of the piano was to invest it judiciously in purchasing stock in the Lincoln Mutual Insurance Company" (012-007). This investment proves wise as the company prospers.
