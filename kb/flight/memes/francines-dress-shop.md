## Overview

Francine's is an exclusive women's dress shop in [[new-york-city|New York City]], known "wherever the best women's clothes were sold" (017-011). The establishment is run by [[madame-francine|Madame Francine]], a shrewd businesswoman who understands that presenting an air of French exclusivity — whether genuine or constructed — is essential to attracting wealthy clientele.

> "Francine's name was greeted with that tone of respectful homage granted only to the names of Paquin and Lucile and Worth." (017-012)

For [[mimi-daquin|Mimi Daquin]], Francine's is the first stop in her quest for employment after deciding to pass as white. Her training at the [[manhattan-trade-school|Manhattan Trade School]] and her natural skill earn her a position. Francine's becomes both Mimi's livelihood and a stage on which she performs her new identity, observing wealthy white patrons while hiding her true racial identity.

## In the Novel

Mimi applies to Francine's as "the only place to which she applied" (017-011), recognizing that its reputation would give her the best chance at advancement. [[madame-francine|Madame Francine]] is described as "the high priestess of the temple of feminine adornment" (017-013), a figure who "knew the value of a stage setting" and carefully constructed the shop's aura of exclusivity (017-014).

Mimi "began work as a finisher at Francine's" (017-019). She "desperately wanted work, and work at Francine's" (017-020). The shop floor operates under strict discipline: "save when the eyes of the fitter or a very rare visit from Francine herself hushed their whispered chatter" (017-021).

Mimi's experience reveals both her skill and ambition. She observes "the manners of the patrons of Francine's whom they watched" (017-045), learning the social codes of wealthy white women. She begins to imagine her own future: "she saw herself sitting as Francine now did in a delicately beautiful little office" (017-047). The shop becomes the center of her new life (018-001).

> "Deftly, quickly, she did the work assigned her. From eight-thirty each weekday morning until five-thirty she plied her needle, head bent low at the table given her in the long workroom on the fourth floor." (017-021)
