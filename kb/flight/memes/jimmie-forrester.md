## Overview

James H. Forrester, known as Jimmie, is a New York stockbroker who enters the narrative of *Flight* in Chapter 19. He is introduced to Mimi Daquin through Mrs. Crosby during Mimi's Paris business trip. Forrester works at a brokerage house that handles the investments of Horace Crosby, Mrs. Crosby's husband, and is described as "frightfully smart" and "bright as a steel trap" [019-029] by the matchmaking Mrs. Crosby.

Forrester is physically distinctive rather than classically handsome. When he smiles, however, he "became an individual" — his smile transforms his otherwise mismatched features into something engaging [019-032]. He pursues Mimi with persistent zeal through the later chapters, eventually becoming her husband, though the marriage brings Mimi into a world far removed from her own people and past.

## In the Story

### Meeting Mimi (Chapter 19)

Forrester arrives at Mrs. Crosby's hotel suite as Mimi is attempting to leave. Their introduction, orchestrated by Mrs. Crosby, is brief but charged. Despite Forrester's self-deprecating apology — "didn't know I was such an ogre" [019-033] — an "undefinable bond" springs into being between them within minutes [019-038]. He quietly mocks Mrs. Crosby to Mimi, referring to her as "The Holy Behemoth" [019-035], establishing a conspiratorial rapport.

### Pursuit and Courtship (Chapters 20–21)

Forrester's pursuit of Mimi intensifies upon her return to New York. He brings gifts and attention, and "would not be denied" [020-029]. His zeal shows "no sign of abating" [020-031]. The Forrester family home sits on the north side of a described New York neighborhood [021-001]. Mimi finds herself increasingly drawn into his orbit despite her ambivalence about the world he represents — a world of wealth, whiteness, and distance from her Black identity and community.

### Marriage and Its Implications

Forrester becomes Mimi's husband, representing her final step into "passing" and away from her racial identity. Their marriage embodies the central tension of the novel's later chapters: Mimi's flight from her own people into a white world where she must constantly guard her secret.
