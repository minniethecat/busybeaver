## Overview

[[chicago-defender|The Chicago Defender]] was the most influential Black newspaper of the early 20th century. Founded by Robert S. Abbott in 1905, the paper grew from a four-page handbill to a national institution with a circulation of over 200,000. Its most significant historical contribution was its role in encouraging the [[great-migration-event|Great Migration]]: [[chicago-defender|the Defender]] published train schedules, job listings, and vivid accounts of northern freedom that inspired tens of thousands of Black southerners to move north.

In *Flight*, [[chicago-defender|the Defender]] would have been part of the information ecosystem that connected Mary Robertson's [[chicago-illinois|Chicago]] to the broader Black world. The paper's celebration of northern opportunity — and its exposés of southern racial violence — shaped the consciousness of a generation of Black migrants.
