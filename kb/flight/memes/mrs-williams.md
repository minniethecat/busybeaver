Mrs. Williams is [[mimi-daquin|Mimi Daquin]]'s landlady in Philadelphia. Despite her harsh words, she proves to be a crucial source of practical help during the most terrifying crisis of Mimi's early motherhood — the near-fatal illness of her son Petit Jean.

## In the Story

When Mimi needed to work, Mrs. Williams volunteered to care for Jean, "despite her words of the night before" — suggesting a gruff exterior that masked genuine concern [014-016]. Her true value emerged when Petit Jean fell gravely ill.

It was Mrs. Williams who recognized the severity of the situation and called for [[dr-newton|Dr. Newton]]. She reported the symptoms with clinical precision: "He said somepin about 'fectious diarrhea," she told the doctor, her earlier fright subsiding with the physician's arrival [014-022].

When Dr. Newton diagnosed intussusception — a life-threatening intestinal blockage requiring immediate surgery — Mrs. Williams shared Mimi's dismay at the dire prognosis [014-024]. Throughout the crisis, she remained a steady presence.

After the surgery, Mimi owed Mrs. Williams back rent along with mounting medical bills [014-040]. The debt weighed on Mimi, but she was able to settle part of it once she found work recommended by Dr. Newton [014-050].
