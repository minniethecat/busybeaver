Westchester County is a suburban area north of [[new-york-city|New York City]]. In the novel *Flight*, it is the location where [[mimi-daquin|Mimi Daquin]] places her young son Jean for care.

## Jean's Placement

Unable to raise her son while working in [[new-york-city|New York City]], Mimi makes the difficult decision of "placing him with a family in Westchester County where she could see him" ([019-008]). The choice of Westchester — distant enough to require travel but close enough for regular visits — reflects the practical compromises forced upon Mimi by her circumstances.

> She had compromised by placing him with a family in Westchester County where she could see him occasionally. [019-008]

## Significance

Westchester County represents the separation between Mimi's professional life in Manhattan and her maternal responsibilities. The distance between the city and the county mirrors the emotional distance Mimi must maintain, visiting her son only occasionally while building her career as a dressmaker. This arrangement is one of the central tensions of Mimi's adult life — the conflict between independence and motherhood.
