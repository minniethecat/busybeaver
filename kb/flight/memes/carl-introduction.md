# Mimi's First Meeting with Carl Hunter

[[mimi-daquin|Mimi Daquin]]'s brief, cool first encounter with [[carl-hunter|Carl Hunter]] at one of the small parties that form the social life of [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]]'s Black elite, an introduction that plants the seed of a relationship with devastating consequences.

## Overview

Mimi meets [[carl-hunter|Carl Hunter]] for the first time at a small social gathering, "the room ringed with fat, chatting mothers whose conversation did not so absorb them that they failed to notice any action, however insignificant" (005-001). [[hilda-adams|Hilda Adams]], who is already infatuated with Carl — "he's the most thrilling boy in [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]]" (004-025) — introduces the two with the simple words: "Mimi, this is Carl."

The meeting is brief and disappointing. Carl "giving her a quick glance that seemed to her to take in not only her physical self but to pry down deep into her innermost thoughts, paused a minute before speaking. When he did speak his greeting was only a cool 'Hello'" (005-001). Mimi is unimpressed: on the way home she tells Hilda, "I don't think much of your friend, Hilda. He didn't say anything and he seems to have an awfully good opinion of himself" (005-002).

## Narrative Context

The meeting occurs in chapter 5, during the period of relative calm before the [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]] Race Riot. Carl is on the verge of [[atlanta-departure|leaving Atlanta]] — his father is "going to let him go away and work this summer and then enter school up North next fall" (005-005). Hilda is despondent at his departure, confiding her unrequited love to Mimi.

Mimi's initial dismissal of Carl will prove dramatically ironic. The same qualities she criticizes — his reserve, his self-possession — will later attract her. And Hilda's warning — "you just wait until you know him and you'll think differently" (005-003) — will prove prophetic in ways neither girl can anticipate.

The meeting is notable for what does not happen: no spark, no romantic interest, no memorable exchange. The inauspiciousness of this first encounter makes the novel's later developments — Mimi's passionate involvement with Carl, her pregnancy, his betrayal — all the more pointed. The relationship that will transform Mimi's life begins with a dismissive "Hello" that she barely registers.

## Aftermath

Carl reappears in the novel after Mimi returns to [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]] from college, and their relationship develops through the period of Jean's decline. The relationship culminates in Mimi's pregnancy and the discovery of Carl's infidelity, which drives her from [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]] altogether. The first meeting, in retrospect, becomes a dark portent — a meeting that in a different novel might have been the beginning of a courtship but that here plants the seeds of catastrophe.
