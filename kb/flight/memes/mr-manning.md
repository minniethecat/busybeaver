Mr. Manning is an elderly caterer in Philadelphia who, together with his wife [[mrs-manning|Mrs. Manning]], provides shelter and support to [[mimi-daquin|Mimi Daquin]] after the birth of her son Petit Jean.

## Relationship with Mimi

Mr. Manning arranged for Mimi to stay with friends in New Jersey after she left the public ward [013-016]. He and his wife had no children of their own, and they grew deeply fond of Mimi during her months with them. Mr. Manning devised plans to help Mimi earn a living as a seamstress, though his schemes were often impractical:

> Mr. Manning scratched his white head and pursed his lips as he gazed at the ceiling. [013-020]

The Mannings deferred Mimi's start of work on various pretexts, reluctant to push her into independence before she was ready [013-023]. Having always been thrifty, they had saved some money, but their catering business was declining as younger competitors captured the market [013-025].

## Accident and Decline

In early November, Mr. Manning slipped on ice while returning from a shopping trip and broke his hip [013-028]. The injury confined him to bed for many weeks. His age worked against recovery — his bones did not knit as rapidly as hoped [014-013]. This accident forced Mimi to leave the Manning household and seek independent lodging and work, accelerating her journey toward self-sufficiency.

## Later Years

After Mimi moved out, she occasionally visited the Mannings, telling them optimistic accounts of her new life [013-030]. She later corresponded with them from New York, though her interest in maintaining contact waned over time [018-039]. Mr. Manning's kindness, along with his wife's, represented one of the few periods of genuine care and stability Mimi experienced in Philadelphia.
