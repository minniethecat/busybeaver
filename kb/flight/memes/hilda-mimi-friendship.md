## Overview

At [[fleur-de-lis-club|the Fleur-de-Lis]] Club meeting, Mimi meets [[hilda-adams|Hilda Adams]] — the daughter of the woman being gossiped about — and the two girls form an immediate, intense bond. Hilda and Mimi stood looking at each other, each caught and held fast by some power, neither of them knew what nor even that there was such a power holding them (004-011). The friendship is one of the novel's few depictions of uncomplicated human connection.

The friendship between Hilda and Mimi is remarkable for its innocence in a novel otherwise dominated by social calculation. While the older women gossip and perform, the girls sit on the steps and talk, sharing intimacies that weave between them a gossamer band of friendship. Mimi, through all of her fourteen years, had had no confidante other than her father, and she feels a tender affection surge through her for this newfound friend (004-014).
