## Overview

Dr. Adams is a Black physician practicing in Atlanta, Georgia, who appears in Chapters 10 through 12 of *Flight*. He serves as the Daquin family's doctor, attending to Jean Daquin during his fatal illness and later diagnosing Mimi Daquin's pregnancy. His crisp, professional demeanor accompanies grave medical news at critical turning points in Mimi's life.

Dr. Adams is the father of Hilda Adams and the husband of Mrs. Adams, who is a member of the Fleur-de-Lis Club, an Atlanta social organization. Through these family connections, the Adams family is woven into the social fabric of Atlanta's Black middle class. His professional role places him at the intersection of personal tragedy and medical crisis in Mimi's story.

## In the Story

### Jean's Final Illness (Chapters 10–11)

Dr. Adams is called to treat Jean Daquin during the illness that proves fatal. After emerging from Jean's room with another doctor, both "haggard from their work, anxiety and loss of sleep," Dr. Adams delivers the grave prognosis to the family [010-056]. Mimi feels "as though she were a condemned criminal standing before a judge" as Dr. Adams utters his "crisp, staccato sentences" informing her that Jean is dangerously ill [010-058]. He promises to send his wife and daughter to help [010-059].

Dr. Adams makes his last call at midnight on the night Jean dies, and when he returns at seven the next morning, he confirms that Jean had been dead about three hours [011-004]. He gently tries to pull Mimi away from Jean's bedside, advising her not to stay too long.

### The Pregnancy Diagnosis (Chapter 12)

Months after Jean's death, when Mimi feels unwell, she visits Dr. Adams at her stepmother's suggestion. After an examination, Dr. Adams looks at her gravely and delivers the news: Mimi is pregnant [012-017]. Mimi's reaction — a face shining "with a great light, the light of contentment and love" — puzzles Dr. Adams, whose face remains grave and worried [012-019]. Mimi cannot understand his concern, viewing her pregnancy as the sacred result of her night with Carl, not a source of shame. Later, Mimi confirms the news to Carl: "I'm going to have a baby — Dr. Adams told me so this afternoon" [012-029].
