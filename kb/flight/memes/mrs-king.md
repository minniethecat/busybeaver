## Overview

Mrs. Sophronisba King is a minor but recurring character in *Flight*, appearing alongside [[mrs-plummer|Mrs. Plummer]] as part of a gossipy pair of [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]] neighbors who observe and judge the moral conduct of those around them. Described as "lean, acidulous, suspicious of all humans and their motives save her own," she is "as curveless as a young sapling" [001-016].

Mrs. King functions as a narrative device representing the close-knit, judgmental social fabric of [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]]'s Black community on [[auburn-avenue-district|Auburn Avenue]]. Her suspicions about the Daquin family — particularly [[mimi-daquin|Mimi]] — help convey the social pressures and surveillance that define life in the neighborhood.

## In the Story

### First Impressions (Chapter 1)

Mrs. King is introduced in the opening chapter as [[mrs-plummer|Mrs. Plummer]]'s companion, busy "giving quick little jabs at the furniture with an oiled cloth, pursuing relentlessly bits of dust" when she eagerly shares gossip about [[lizzie-stone|Lizzie Stone]] and [[jerry-reed|Jerry Reed]] [001-016][001-017]. She prides herself on being first to know scandal: "The possession of a juicy morsel which had not yet come to her friend's ears caused Mrs. King's skinny frame to swell with prideful importance" [001-017].

When the Daquin family arrives, Mrs. King helps [[mrs-plummer|Mrs. Plummer]] prepare their house, scanning the new arrivals with "one swift, critical — very critical — glance" [001-036]. She immediately notes the family dynamics, whispering to [[mrs-plummer|Mrs. Plummer]] about Jean: "'Tain't hard to see who wears the pants in this family" [001-041].

### Monitoring Mimi (Chapters 7 and 11)

Mrs. King reappears in Chapter 7, when she and [[mrs-plummer|Mrs. Plummer]] spot [[mimi-daquin|Mimi]] leaving a social hall with [[carl-hunter|Carl Hunter]]. She confirms the sighting with certainty: "It's her, sho's you born. I told you she'd bear watching. Playing off respectable and going in such places —" [007-027]. The two women eagerly follow to see where the couple goes next [007-029].

In Chapter 11, during [[jean-daquin|Jean Daquin]]'s wake, Mrs. King and [[mrs-plummer|Mrs. Plummer]] linger in the house discussing the probable price of the casket and furniture in low voices, provoking Mimi's fury [011-026][011-031]. When Mimi's anger erupts, the two women "thoroughly frightened, hastily edged around the room towards the door" [011-034].

## Relationships

- **[[mrs-plummer|Mrs. Plummer]]**: Mrs. King's constant companion in gossip and moral surveillance. The two form an inseparable pair, appearing together in every scene.
- **[[mimi-daquin|Mimi Daquin]]**: A target of Mrs. King's suspicion and judgment, especially regarding Mimi's relationship with [[carl-hunter|Carl Hunter]].
- **[[mary-robertson|Mary Robertson Daquin]]**: Mrs. King treats Mary with outward respect while privately dissecting the Daquin [[home|household]] dynamics.
