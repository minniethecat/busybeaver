## Overview

Madame Francine is the owner and namesake of [[francines-dress-shop|Francine's]], the exclusive [[new-york-city|New York]] modiste where Mimi works as a finisher while passing for white. She appears only briefly in the novel — her rare visits to the workrooms hush the chatter of the girls — but her presence pervades the establishment. She understands what most theatrical producers do not: the value of a stage setting, the importance of maintaining the illusion that her customers are not engaged in anything so vulgar as buying and selling (017-014).

[[francines-dress-shop|Francine's]] identity is ambiguous in ways that serve the novel's themes. Her name is French, her establishment models itself on [[paris-france|Paris]]ian fashion houses, and her employees presumably believe she is a white Frenchwoman. But the novel never confirms this — [[francines-dress-shop|Francine]] exists in the same racial ambiguity that Mimi exploits. The possibility that [[francines-dress-shop|Francine]] herself might be passing is left tantalizingly unexplored.
