## Overview

Mr. Robertson is the father of [[mary-robertson|Mary Robertson Daquin]] and a significant secondary figure in the early chapters of *Flight.* A sharp, incisive businessman from [[chicago-illinois|Chicago]], he embodies the money-driven ethos that stands in stark contrast to the Creole sensibilities of his son-in-law [[jean-daquin|Jean Daquin]].

> Money — money — money — how much is it worth? — how much can I make out of it? — these were the first, last and intermediate stages of Mr. Robertson's every thought, every statement, every action. [001-015]

When Mary marries Jean, Mr. Robertson orchestrates the Daquin family's move from [[new-orleans-louisiana|New Orleans]] to [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]], arranging housing and employment for Jean at [[lincoln-mutual-life|Lincoln Mutual Life Insurance Company]] [001-035].

## In the Story

### Arrival in Atlanta (Chapter 1)

Mr. Robertson meets the Daquin family at [[atlanta-union-station|Union Station]] upon their arrival from [[new-orleans-louisiana|New Orleans]]. He greets them with characteristic brusqueness and immediately takes charge, directing the driver to avoid Decatur and [[ivy-street|Ivy Street]]s, which he dismisses as the "slum district" [001-006] [001-007].

> Though he disliked Mr. Robertson intensely Jean was happy to receive the news. He felt bewildered, lost, a malady bordering on nausea at the hubbub around him. [001-005]

He reveals his priorities: rushing back to [[chicago-illinois|Chicago]] for an election, but insisting on seeing the family "started off right" in Atlanta's Negro society [001-010]. He warns Mary bluntly: "Got to get in right — or you'll never get in" [001-013].

### A Divisive Presence

Jean's hostility toward Mr. Robertson intensifies. He cringes at being familiarly called "Gene" and resents his father-in-law's interference [001-014]. Jean resolves privately never to let his soul "be turned into a moneygrubber's."

### The New Orleans Marriage (Chapter 2, Flashback)

When Jean met Mary Robertson during a Mardi Gras visit to [[new-orleans-louisiana|New Orleans]], Mr. Robertson's materialistic influence had already shaped Mary's outlook.

> Mary Robertson had no recollection of a time when she did not hear from morning to night endless discussions of money and of politics. [002-032]

After the marriage, Mr. Robertson rushed to [[new-orleans-louisiana|New Orleans]] amid the storm of protest. Mary ultimately saw her father's prediction vindicated.

> His judgment vindicated, Mr. Robertson used his influence, and Jean received a flattering offer to associate himself with the Atlanta company. [002-055]

## Relationships

- **[[mary-robertson|Mary Robertson Daquin]]** (daughter): Mr. Robertson shapes her worldview and arranges the family's transition to [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]].

- **[[jean-daquin|Jean Daquin]]** (son-in-law): Deeply antagonistic. Jean despises Mr. Robertson's materialism; Robertson views Jean as impractical.

- **[[mimi-daquin|Mimi Daquin]]** (granddaughter): Interactions are formal and distant. Mimi dislikes his coarse mannerisms [001-045].

- **[[mrs-plummer|Mrs. Plummer]]**: Employed by Mr. Robertson to prepare the Daquins' new home.
