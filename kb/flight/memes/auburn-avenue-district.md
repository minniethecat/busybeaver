## Overview

The Auburn Avenue Business District — known as [[auburn-avenue|Sweet Auburn]] — was the economic heart of Black [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]] in the early 20th century. Stretching east from downtown [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]], the district housed banks, insurance companies, real estate offices, drugstores, newspapers, and professional offices — all Black-owned and serving a Black clientele. The district's success made [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]] a symbol of Black economic achievement and a destination for aspiring entrepreneurs like [[jean-daquin|Jean Daquin]].

In *Flight*, [[auburn-avenue|Auburn Ave]]nue is the world of [[mr-hunter|Mr. Hunter]]'s insurance company and the economic elite who patronize [[fleur-de-lis-club|the Fleur-de-Lis]] Club. [[jean-daquin|Jean Daquin]]'s judgment that a Negro-owned insurance company could succeed in [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]] was vindicated by the evidence of [[auburn-avenue|Auburn Ave]]nue (002-055). But the district also revealed the limits of Black capitalism under [[jim-crow-era|Jim Crow]]: its prosperity depended on segregation, and its residents were powerless to prevent the white violence that erupted during the [[atlanta-race-riot-1906|1906 race riot]].
