Washington Square is a park and surrounding neighborhood in the Greenwich Village section of Manhattan, [[new-york-city|New York City]]. In the novel *Flight*, it serves as a setting for [[mimi-daquin|Mimi Daquin]]'s encounters with [[new-york-city|New York]]'s social and cultural elite.

## The Washington Square Residence

In the later chapters of the novel, a house "sat on the north side of Washington Square" ([021-001]) with "high-ceiled walls" that convey the elegance and refinement of the neighborhood. This residence becomes a significant social venue where Mimi interacts with a cross-section of [[new-york-city|New York]] society.

> The house sat on the north side of Washington Square. Its high-ceiled walls and handsome furnishings spoke of a cultivated taste and a substantial income. [021-001]

## Seasonal Beauty

The square itself is described with poetic attention: "Washington Square lay in restful beauty" ([023-001]) beneath winter snow, "free from the encrustations of snow" in spots where "the verdant hue of" grass showed through. This careful description of the square's physical beauty mirrors Mimi's growing appreciation for the refined world she has entered.

## Social Crossroads

The Washington Square gatherings bring together an eclectic mix of guests — relatives from Iowa, representatives from [[broadway-nyc|Broadway]], bankers from small Midwestern towns. In this setting, Mimi navigates between different social worlds, using her beauty and poise to move through circles that would otherwise be closed to her.
