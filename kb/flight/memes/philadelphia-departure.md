## Overview

Mimi's departure from [[philadelphia-pennsylvania|Philadelphia]] for [[new-york-city|New York]] is the last in the series of geographic moves that structure her life. [[philadelphia-pennsylvania|Philadelphia]] had represented a middle ground — a city where she could pursue respectability after the traumas of [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]]. But the city's limitations became clear, and [[new-york-city|New York]] to her seemed the only field large enough for her ambitions (014-052).

The departure is also a departure of a different kind: the beginning of the end of Mimi's life as a recognized Black woman. [[philadelphia-pennsylvania|Philadelphia]] was where she gave birth to [[petit-jean|Petit Jean]] and where she attempted to build a respectable Black life. Leaving Philadelphia means leaving that attempt behind and opening herself to the possibilities — and the deceptions — of [[new-york-city|New York]].
