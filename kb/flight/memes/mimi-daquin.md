## Overview

Mimi Daquin, christened Annette Angela Daquin, is the central character of Walter White's novel *Flight*. She is born in [[new-orleans-louisiana|New Orleans]] around 1892, the daughter of [[jean-daquin|Jean Daquin]], a Creole tailor.

Her life is shaped by the departure of her birth mother, the arrival of her stepmother Mary Robertson from [[chicago-illinois|Chicago]], and the family's eventual move to [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]].

Mimi grows up in the Creole quarter of [[new-orleans-louisiana|New Orleans]], inheriting a lineage traced to a San Domingan quadroon who served under Andrew Jackson (002-099). As the novel progresses, she makes the fateful decision to pass for white, severing herself from her family and community.

## In the Story

### Early Life in New Orleans

Mimi arrives in [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]] as a young girl with her father [[jean-daquin|Jean Daquin]] and stepmother [[mary-robertson|Mary Robertson Daquin]].

On the train ride from [[new-orleans-louisiana|New Orleans]], Jean awakens her with the words, "We're there, petite Mimi" (001-003). She was about eleven when Mary Robertson entered Jean Daquin's life (002-042).

The Creole quarter where she spent her childhood was a world with "the romance and languorous charm" of its own social order (002-036).

### Life in Atlanta and the Fleur-de-Lis Club

In [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]], Mimi enters the elite Black society of [[fleur-de-lis-club|the Fleur-de-Lis]] Club, referred to by Mr. Hunter as "the eating brigade" (003-022).

She forms a close friendship with [[hilda-adams|Hilda Adams]] (004-013). Through Hilda, Mimi learns of the ejection of [[mrs-adams|Mrs. Adams]] from a white theatre for passing (004-020).

### Philadelphia and Motherhood

After a personal crisis, Mimi leaves for [[philadelphia-pennsylvania|Philadelphia]], where she gives birth to a son she calls "Petit Jean" (013-020).

When Petit Jean falls seriously ill, she summons Dr. Adams, one of the ranking colored physicians of [[philadelphia-pennsylvania|Philadelphia]], who comes promptly (014-020).

### The Decision to Pass

In [[philadelphia-pennsylvania|Philadelphia]], Mimi meets a young woman from an old Philadelphia colored family who is passing for white (014-050). This plants the seed of possibility.

Driven by despair after her son is taken away and her failed struggle, she decides to cross the color line permanently, leaving "coloured people for good" (016-062).

### Working at Francine's

As a white-passing woman, Mimi secures work at [[francines-dress-shop|Francine's]], an exclusive dress shop in [[new-york-city|New York]] (017-011).

There she befriends [[sylvia-smith|Sylvia Smith]], a white coworker (017-022). She lives with constant vigilance. When a coworker mentions the previous fitter was "a coloured girl," Mimi asks with alarm creeping into her voice (017-030).

## Relationships

Mimi's most defining relationship is with her father, [[jean-daquin|Jean Daquin]]. Her stepmother, Mary Robertson, represents the intrusion of Anglo-American racial categories into the Creole world.

Her friendship with [[hilda-adams|Hilda Adams]] gives Mimi her first peer companionship. Her connection to [[petit-jean|Petit Jean]] anchors her to her Black identity. Through her aunt [[mrs-rogers|Mrs. Rogers]], Mimi finds refuge in [[harlem-new-york|Harlem]].

## Key Moments

**Arrival and Childhood:** Arrives in [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]] by train (001-002/001-003). Hears [[mr-robertson|Mr. Robertson]]'s remarks about "lowest kind of Negroes" (001-007).

**Friendship and Society:** Friends with [[hilda-adams|Hilda Adams]] (004-013). Learns of [[mrs-adams|Mrs. Adams]]'s ejection from a white theatre (004-020). Meets [[carl-hunter|Carl Hunter]], "the most thrilling boy in [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]]" (005-001).

**Motherhood and Exile:** Leaves for [[philadelphia-pennsylvania|Philadelphia]] (012-067). Gives birth to [[petit-jean|Petit Jean]] (013-020). Dr. Adams saves Petit Jean (014-020).

**Passing:** Decides to leave "coloured people for good" (016-062). Works at [[francines-dress-shop|Francine's]] (017-019). Befriends [[sylvia-smith|Sylvia Smith]] (017-022).
