Mrs. Bennett is a wealthy New York socialite and customer at Madame Francine's dress shop. Her demanding nature makes her the shop's most challenging client, but her eventual approval of [[mimi-daquin|Mimi Daquin]]'s design work becomes a turning point in Mimi's career.

## In the Story

Of all the customers at Francine's shop, "there was none so hard to satisfy as the queenly querulous Mrs. Bennett, wife of him whose name was synonymous with wealth and social position" [018-008]. Because of her difficulty, Madame Francine assigned Mimi exclusively to attend to Mrs. Bennett's wishes.

When no submitted design met her standards, Mimi took the initiative, asking Mrs. Bennett to leave the matter in her hands. The older woman's response carried a veiled threat: "If it isn't — well —" and her voice "trailed off into a silence many times as expressive as any spoken words might have been" [018-009].

Mimi's design — a creation of green charmeuse and silvery ornaments — proved a triumph. At a dinner honoring a visiting British nobleman, Mrs. Bennett was "beyond all doubt the most strikingly gowned woman" [018-010]. She basked in the attention of the Englishman and "enjoyed no less the poorly concealed envious looks which came from her friends" [018-010].

True to her word, Mrs. Bennett told Francine of her satisfaction and recommended Mimi to all her friends, significantly advancing Mimi's reputation as a designer [018-010].
