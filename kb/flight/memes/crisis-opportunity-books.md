## Overview

Crisis & Opportunity Books is the publisher responsible for producing the transcription of Walter White's *Flight* that served as the basis for the [[standard-ebooks|Standard Ebooks]] digital edition used by this wiki. The name evokes the two most important magazines of the early 20th-century Black intellectual tradition: *The Crisis*, the official magazine of the [[naacp|NAACP]] founded by W.E.B. Du Bois, and *Opportunity*, the journal of the [[national-urban-league|National Urban League]].

The preservation and republication of *Flight* represents an ongoing effort to maintain access to significant works of African American literature. Walter White's novel, originally published in 1926 by Alfred A. Knopf, had gone out of print and was not widely available until these transcription and republication efforts brought it back to readers and scholars.

## In the Novel

Crisis & Opportunity Books does not appear in the novel itself. It is a present-day publisher whose work enables this wiki's engagement with *Flight*. The name's dual reference to *The Crisis* and *Opportunity* connects the preservation effort to the broader tradition of Black intellectual and political activism that the novel both documents and participates in.

The transcription work done by this publisher, and subsequently refined by [[standard-ebooks|Standard Ebooks]], makes possible the structured analysis presented in this wiki — the PN citation system, the character mapping, the event classification, and all other systematic treatments that depend on a clean, reliable text.
