## Overview

The National Urban League was founded in 1910 to assist African Americans migrating from the rural South to northern cities. While the [[naacp|NAACP]] focused on legal challenges to segregation, the Urban League concentrated on employment, housing, and social services — the practical needs of Black communities adapting to urban industrial life.

The Urban League's existence contextualizes the world Mimi enters in [[new-york-city|New York]]. The organization's employment services, its training programs, and its research on urban conditions all addressed the challenges that Mimi navigates alone. Her decision to pass for white at [[francines-dress-shop|Francine's]] rather than seek help through Black community institutions reflects both her personal desperation and the limited reach of organizations like the Urban League.
