## Overview

Black Baptist churches like Wheat Street Baptist were the institutional anchors of [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]]'s African American community in the early 20th century. Churches served not only as places of worship but as centers of political organizing, mutual aid, education, and social life. The pastors of these churches were among the most influential leaders in the community, and church networks provided the infrastructure for everything from burial societies to civil rights activism.

In *Flight*, the church appears primarily through Mary Robertson's Baptist faith, which marks her difference from the Creole Catholic world of Jean's [[new-orleans-louisiana|New Orleans]]. The religious landscape of [[atlanta-georgia|Atlanta]] — with its proliferation of Black Baptist and AME congregations — represents a form of institutional power distinct from the economic power of [[auburn-avenue|Auburn Ave]]nue's insurance companies.
