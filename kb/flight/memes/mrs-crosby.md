## Overview

Mrs. Horace Crosby is a wealthy socialite originally from Chicago, where her husband Horace Crosby made a fortune manipulating grain markets as a Rotary Club member. She appears in Chapter 19 of *Flight*, set in Paris, where she encounters the protagonist Mimi Daquin on the Rue de la Paix. Mrs. Crosby is a patron of Francine's dress shop, where Mimi works as second in command. Her primary narrative function is as a matchmaker: she introduces Mimi to Jimmie Forrester, who eventually becomes Mimi's husband.

Mrs. Crosby is physically described as rotund and much bepowdered, with "a succession of chins joggling with jellylike quiverings above her ample breast" [019-015]. Despite her ludicrous exterior, she is recognized by those who know her as possessing a simple, jovial soul beneath the surface. Her wealth enables an aggressive hospitality that makes her difficult to evade, as Mimi discovers when Mrs. Crosby insists on hosting her for luncheon [019-017].

## In the Story

### The Paris Encounter (Chapter 19)

Mrs. Crosby intercepts Mimi on the Rue de la Paix and insists on taking her to luncheon at her hotel. Despite Mimi's attempts to decline, Mrs. Crosby's persistence — backed by wealth "far greater than she had ever dreamed as a girl the world possessed" [019-017] — proves irresistible. During the luncheon, Mrs. Crosby displays for Mimi's approval an assortment of loot acquired through her husband's checkbook: statuettes, laces, oils, and trinkets, most of which Mimi privately judges as junk [019-023].

### The Introduction

After lunch, as Mimi tries to leave, Mrs. Crosby launches into an effusive biography of Jimmie Forrester, describing him as coming from "a very good family in New York" and being "frightfully smart" and "bright as a steel trap" [019-029]. She orchestrates the meeting between Mimi and Forrester, beaming "with maternal benedictions upon them" [019-030]. The introduction proves consequential: an immediate bond forms between Mimi and Forrester, setting in motion the relationship that leads to their eventual marriage.
