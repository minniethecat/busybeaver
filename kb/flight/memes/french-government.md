## Overview

The French Third Republic governed France and its colonial empire during the period of *Flight*. French racial ideology differed significantly from the American model — France's colonial approach emphasized cultural assimilation rather than strict racial segregation, and French law did not recognize the one-drop rule that structured American racial classification. This difference was particularly significant in Louisiana, where French and Spanish colonial law had established a tripartite racial system markedly different from the Anglo-American binary.

The French government's racial policies help explain both the distinctiveness of [[new-orleans-louisiana|Creole New Orleans]] and the attraction that French identity held for Mimi. When she passes as French rather than white, she is drawing on a cultural tradition that understood race differently — more fluidly, more culturally — than the American system she was fleeing.
