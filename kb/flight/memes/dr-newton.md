## Overview

Dr. Newton is a prominent Black physician and surgeon practicing in Philadelphia, who appears in Chapter 14 of *Flight*. When Mimi Daquin's infant son, Petit Jean, falls critically ill with what is diagnosed as infectious diarrhea, Mimi summons Dr. Newton — a doctor she knows by sight from church and by reputation as one of "eminence among the coloured physicians of Philadelphia" [014-020]. His professional competence and quiet compassion prove vital in saving Jean's life.

Dr. Newton is described as "trim, alert, intelligent" with a composed demeanor. His examination technique is methodical and confident: "long brown fingers probed the baby's abdomen, methodically, confidently he measured pulse and temperature" [014-020]. Beyond his medical role, he shows personal kindness by discreetly recommending Mimi for dressmaking work to help her pay the hospital bills.

## In the Story

### The Emergency Call (Chapter 14)

When Petit Jean falls violently ill, Mimi immediately thinks of Dr. Newton and asks Mrs. Williams to summon him [014-020]. He comes at once and conducts a thorough examination, coolly reciting the symptoms. His presence alone brings reassurance: Mrs. Williams's fright vanishes "with the coming of Dr. Newton" [014-022]. Upon learning that another doctor had administered castor oil to the infant, Dr. Newton reacts with professional outrage — "The blithering fool!" [014-023] — recognizing the treatment as dangerously inappropriate.

### The Operation

Dr. Newton arranges for Jean to be operated on at the "coloured hospital" in Philadelphia. Despite his assurance that the condition stems from the thinness of infant intestinal walls — a condition that "most frequently occurred in apparently perfectly healthy and well-cared-for children" — Mimi cannot shake her guilt [014-032]. Jean survives the operation and spends four weeks recovering in the hospital.

### Quiet Generosity

After the operation, Mimi speaks frankly with Dr. Newton about her poverty, though "it galled her to be forced to confess" her financial situation [014-045]. Unbeknownst to Mimi, Dr. Newton subsequently recommends her to a wealthy Philadelphia family needing a trousseau — work that enables her to pay the hospital bill and settle part of her debt [014-050].
