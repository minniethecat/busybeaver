Broadway is a major thoroughfare in [[new-york-city|New York City]], running the length of Manhattan. In the novel *Flight*, Broadway appears as a symbol of the entertainment industry and a site of economic opportunity for [[mimi-daquin|Mimi Daquin]].

## Role in the Novel

[[mimi-daquin|Mimi Daquin]] first encounters the glamour of Broadway during her early days in [[new-york-city|New York]]. As a young woman, she sees "blonde chorus girls on Broadway swaying their shoulders" ([007-017]), a vision of the theatrical world that both fascinates and contrasts with her own life.

> Mimi saw blonde chorus girls on Broadway swaying their shoulders and beckoning, beckoning to eager boys who had come to Broadway to find life. [007-017]

## Mimi's Shop

Later, after establishing herself as a dressmaker, Mimi decides "to open a shop in upper Broadway, somewhere in the Nineties" ([018-004]). This shop becomes the foundation of her independent professional identity, placing her at the intersection of [[new-york-city|New York]]'s commercial and cultural life.

## Broadway and the Social World

Broadway serves as a meeting point between different social spheres. At a [[washington-square-nyc|Washington Square]] gathering, the guests ranged from Iowa relatives to "those from Broadway" ([022-005]), representing a spectrum from provincial America to the cosmopolitan entertainment world.

The Broadway theater scene also provides a backdrop for Mimi's observations of the changing cultural landscape, as "the vogue of Negro shows on Broadway" ([023-023]) reflects shifting racial dynamics in American entertainment. Mimi watches performers "come up here and get stuff for Broadway" ([023-029]), observing the creative economy from her position as a businesswoman.
