Aunt Sophie (Mrs. Rogers) is [[mimi-daquin|Mimi Daquin]]'s aunt, a widow living in New York who runs a beauty parlor in Harlem. She is one of the few consistently supportive figures in Mimi's life, offering both practical refuge and emotional stability across decades.

## In the Story

### Atlanta Funeral and First Invitation

Aunt Sophie first appears when she travels to Atlanta for the funeral of Mimi's stepmother. She takes Mimi in her arms, offering immediate comfort, and Mimi "felt instantly the presence of a friend and ally" [011-039]. Before returning to New York, Aunt Sophie extends an invitation for Mimi to come live with her — an offer that Mimi declines due to her attachment to Carl Hunter [011-048].

### Refuge in New York

When Mimi finally leaves Atlanta, Aunt Sophie's invitation becomes "the one stable thing in a sea of uncertainties" [014-055]. Her letter of reply is "short, but warm and sincere" [014-059]. Upon Mimi's arrival in New York, Aunt Sophie greets her with overwhelming affection. Mimi soon discovers her aunt possesses "a fund of hard common sense" combined with "a warmth of feeling" [015-024].

### Harlem Life

Aunt Sophie ushers Mimi into a new social world in Harlem, introducing her to an interesting circle of friends who frequent her house [015-041]. Together they enjoy modest excursions to theaters and restaurants downtown [015-046]. Aunt Sophie's home becomes the foundation from which Mimi builds her New York life, including her eventual career at Madame Francine's dress shop.

### Later Years

After Mimi marries Jimmie Forrester and moves away from Harlem, she pays occasional visits to her aunt [018-001]. Six years after leaving Aunt Sophie's household, Mimi reflects on their relationship [019-002]. Near the end of Mimi's journey, she learns her aunt has returned to New Orleans, and she feels the old pull of Harlem — "the old attraction which had come over her that first night in New York when her Aunt Sophie" had welcomed her [023-024].
