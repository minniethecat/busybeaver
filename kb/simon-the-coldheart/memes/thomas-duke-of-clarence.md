# Thomas, Duke of Clarence

**Thomas of Lancaster, Duke of Clarence** (1387–1421), is [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] second son [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] King [[henry-iv-bolingbroke|Henry IV]] and younger bro[[raoul-the-terrible|the]]r to [[king-henry-v|Henry V]]. He appears as a royal commander during [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] [[normandy|Normandy]] campaign, serving alongside his bro[[raoul-the-terrible|the]]r [[king-henry-v|the King]] and [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] assembled [[knighthood|knight]]s [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] realm.

## Royal Lineage

Thomas was born into [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Lancastrian royal house as [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] second surviving son [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[henry-iv-bolingbroke|Henry Bolingbroke]]. When his fa[[raoul-the-terrible|the]]r seized [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] throne from [[richard-ii|Richard II]] in 1399, Thomas became a prince [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] blood. His elder bro[[raoul-the-terrible|the]]r succeeded as [[king-henry-v|Henry V]] in 1413, and Thomas served as one [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] his principal military lieutenants. In [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] text, he is referred to simply as "[[king-henry-v|the King]]'s bro[[raoul-the-terrible|the]]r" (012-006), a title that captures both his proximity to power and his subordinate role within it.

At [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] muster before [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] [[belremy|Belrémy]] siege, Thomas Duke of Clarence is listed among [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] notable commanders present (012-006).
