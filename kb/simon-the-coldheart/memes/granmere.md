## Location & Geography

Granmere is an English [[barony|estate]] centred on Granmere Hall, [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] residence [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] Lord [[charles-of-granmere|Charles of Granmere]], cousin to [[fulk-of-montlice|Fulk of Montlice]]. [[the-chevalier|The]] hall is described as magnificent, with a courtyard where riders are received and a hall-room where [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] lord entertains guests.

## Significance in the Story

Granmere Hall serves as a waypoint in [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon of Beauvallet]]'s journey from [[montlice|Montlice]] to [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] French campaign. [[fulk-of-montlice|Fulk of Montlice]] sends [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] to his cousin [[charles-of-granmere|Charles]], instructing him to seek assistance if needed. [[the-chevalier|The]] stay at Granmere provides a moment [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] hospitality and political connection before [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] narrative plunges into [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] siege [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[belremy|Belrémy]].

[[charles-of-granmere|Charles of Granmere]] receives [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] warmly, and [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] two men establish a friendly rapport. Granmere's easy manner — "his eyes twinkled anew" — provides a contrast to [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] martial severity [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[montlice|Montlice]].
