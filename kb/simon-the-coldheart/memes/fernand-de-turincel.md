# Fernand de Turincel

**Fernand de Turincel** was [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Duke [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[turincel|Turincel]], a Norman nobleman whose [[stronghold|castle]] served as a pivotal location in [[the-lady-margaret|the Lady Margaret's]] flight from [[belremy|Belrémy]] and whose eventual submission to Lancastrian authority marked one [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] final acts [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] [[belremy|Belrémy]] campaign.

## Margaret's Hope

During her escape, [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] [[the-lady-margaret|Lady Margaret]] repeatedly identified Fernand as her hoped-for saviour:

> "If I could reach it, Fernand de Turincel would aid me! Aid me to throw off this hated yoke!" (017-032)

Her confidence was absolute: "I would go to Fernand de Turincel" (019-049). [[the-chevalier|The]] journey to [[turincel|Turincel]] became her single objective — "Even an I walk to [[turincel|Turincel]] I can reach it within three days" (019-049). She imagined rallying his forces: "I wonder what he will say when I bring [[turincel|Turincel]] about his ears?" (020-043).

## The Reception at Turincel

When Margaret and her servant [[jeanne|Jeanne]] arrived at [[turincel|Turincel]], [[raoul-the-terrible|the]]y found Fernand absent at first. Margaret asked: "Is my Lord Fernand within?" (021-041). When he appeared, his reception was formally correct:

> "I am Fernand, Duke of Turincel, at your service, Countess." (021-056)

[[the-chevalier|The]] formal address — "Countess" ra[[raoul-the-terrible|the]]r than any warmer term — was [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] first hint that Fernand's allegiance might not match Margaret's expectations.

## The Turn

Within two days, Margaret's hopes were shattered:

> Margaret whispered. "Fernand turned traitor. ... What shall I do?" (021-075)

Fernand's "treason" — from Margaret's perspective — was his decision not to commit his forces against [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]. Whe[[raoul-the-terrible|the]]r this was a true betrayal or a pragmatic assessment [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] military situation is left ambiguous. Fernand's title, "Duke of Turincel," suggests a lord [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] consequence; his refusal to fight suggests a lord who understood [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] balance [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] power.

When [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] and [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|Geoffrey]] arrived at [[turincel|Turincel]] in pursuit, [[raoul-the-terrible|the]]y found Fernand abroad (023-095). [[the-chevalier|The]] news that "[[turincel|Turincel]] has submitted" (023-081) confirmed that [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] duke had chosen accommodation over defiance.

## Character

Fernand appears in [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] novel only through [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] reports and impressions [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] o[[raoul-the-terrible|the]]rs. He is never directly portrayed in action, only in consequence. His role is that [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] pivot: [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] ally Margaret counted on, who chose differently. His "treason" — submission to Lancastrian authority — was prudent from [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] perspective [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] realpolitik but catastrophic for Margaret's plans.
