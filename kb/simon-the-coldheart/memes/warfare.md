Warfare in the novel is not romanticised. It is a world of sieges, campaigns, and cold strategic calculation — the arena in which [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon of Beauvallet]] proves his military genius. The battles and conflicts depicted reflect the historical reality of the early 15th century, when the Hundred Years' War shaped the political landscape of England and France.

## In the Novel

The novel presents warfare through Simon's pragmatic lens. His approach to conflict is methodical and disciplined: he deploys siege engines with precision, enforces strict order among his troops, and values results over glory. When Simon surveys [[belremy|Belrémy]] before the siege, his assessment is purely tactical: "So that I may weaken their fortifications, and, by hunger, weaken the soldiers" (013-018).

[[king-henry-v|King Henry V]]'s campaign in [[normandy|Normandy]] provides the historical framework for the military action. The [[siege-of-belremy|Siege of Belrémy]] demonstrates Simon's tactical skill, while the [[campaign-in-normandy|Campaign in Normandy]] shows warfare at the strategic level. Simon's declaration that "with grace and might of chivalry" order shall prevail over pillaging (014-038) articulates the disciplined approach to war that sets him apart from freebooters and brigands.

The novel captures the transitional nature of 15th-century warfare where the professionalisation of armies increasingly challenged traditional chivalric ideals — a tension embodied in Simon's cold, calculating character.

## Historical Background

Early 15th-century warfare was in transition. The traditional dominance of heavy cavalry was being challenged by the growing importance of archers (English longbowmen) and professional infantry. Siege warfare remained central to territorial conquest, and the novel accurately depicts the use of siege engines and the strategic importance of fortified positions. [[king-henry-v|Henry V]]'s 1415 campaign in France, culminating at Agincourt, represented the high point of English military success in the Hundred Years' War.
