## Overview

[[the-chevalier|The]] House of Granmere is an English landed family based at [[granmere|Granmere Hall]], headed by Lord [[charles-of-granmere|Charles of Granmere]]. [[the-chevalier|The]] house holds kinship ties to [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] [[house-of-montlice|House of Montlice]] through [[charles-of-granmere|Charles]]'s relationship as [[fulk-of-montlice|Fulk of Montlice]]'s cousin.

## Key Members

- **[[charles-of-granmere|Charles of Granmere]]** — lord [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[granmere|Granmere Hall]], described as a man [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] middle age with a genial manner. He provides hospitality to [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]'s party during [[raoul-the-terrible|the]]ir journey to [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] French campaign.

## Relationship to Other Houses

[[the-chevalier|The]] House of Granmere's primary political connection is to [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] [[house-of-montlice|House of Montlice]] via [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] cousin relationship between [[charles-of-granmere|Charles]] and [[fulk-of-montlice|Fulk]]. When [[fulk-of-montlice|Fulk]] sends [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] to [[charles-of-granmere|Charles]], it is with [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] assurance that [[charles-of-granmere|Charles]] will assist any friend [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[montlice|Montlice]]:

> "It would be my pleasure to assist any friend of Fulk's."

[[the-chevalier|The]] [[granmere|Granmere]]s represent [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] network [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] minor noble houses that sustain [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] greater houses through kinship, hospitality, and mutual obligation — [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] social infrastructure [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] 15th-century English nobility.

## Role in the Narrative

[[granmere|Granmere Hall]] serves as a waypoint in [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]'s journey from [[montlice|Montlice]] to [[normandy|Normandy]]. [[the-chevalier|The]] house's brief appearance in [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] narrative illustrates [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] web [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] aristocratic connections — cousins, hosts, allies — that surrounds [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] novel's central military action.
