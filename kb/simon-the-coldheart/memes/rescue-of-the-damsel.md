# Rescue of the Damsel

[[the-chevalier|The]] **Rescue of the Damsel** was an event in [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] year 1404 in which [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon of Beauvallet]] rescued a kidnapped girl in [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] woodlands near [[montlice|Montlice]], capturing her abductor and uncovering evidence [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] a political [[plot|conspiracy]] against [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Lancastrian crown.

## The Encounter

While riding through [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] countryside with his squire [[roger|Roger]], [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] approached a region known as Fair Pastures (005-021). [[the-chevalier|The]] name — "a fair [[barony|baron]]y" (005-024) — belied [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] violence about to unfold.

Hearing cries, [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] discovered a girl being forcibly taken by a man. [[the-chevalier|The]] girl attempted to express her gratitude prematurely:

> The girl gave a little glad cry and fell upon her knees with intent to kiss Simon's hand. (005-050)

## The Struggle

[[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] engaged [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] abductor, a man [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] considerable physical strength:

> He realised that he could not throw this fair young giant and he twisted suddenly and cunningly so that he broke away. (005-053)

[[the-chevalier|The]] girl crouched by her bucket, "hiding her face in her hands and weeping" (005-056), while [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] subdued [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] man. After [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] capture, [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] had her fetch his wallet: "[[the-chevalier|The]] girl dragged herself up and went with lagging steps to where [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] wallet lay" (005-063).

Meanwhile, [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]'s squire [[roger|Roger]] arrived: "[[roger|Roger]] came racing round [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] bend to his master's assistance" (005-068). After a quick command, "[[roger|Roger]] turned and ran back through [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] wood" (005-070). [[roger|Roger]] was [[raoul-the-terrible|the]]n tasked with comforting [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] girl (005-076), and toge[[raoul-the-terrible|the]]r [[raoul-the-terrible|the]]y carried [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] unconscious abductor to an inn at [[gountray|Gountray]].

## Discovery of the Plot

At [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] inn, [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] searched [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] captive and found a letter:

> The letter was couched in fair terms, and it assured my lord the Baron of Crowburg, faithful adherent to the true king, Richard by the Grace of God, lately escaped into Scotland, that despite the lying reports of his death, set about by the usurper ... (005-075)

This letter revealed a conspiracy among Lancastrian loyalists who still supported [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] deposed [[king-henry-v|Richard II]], believing [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] reports [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] his death to be false. [[the-chevalier|The]] discovery transformed a simple rescue into a matter [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] state security. [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] preserved [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] evidence carefully, folding it toge[[raoul-the-terrible|the]]r with o[[raoul-the-terrible|the]]r papers into a pouch inside his own tunic (005-076).

## Aftermath

[[the-chevalier|The]] rescue and [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] discovery [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] plot set in motion a chain [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] events: [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] dispatched [[roger|Roger]] to deliver a report to [[fulk-of-montlice|Fulk of Montlice]] (005-104), detained [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] prisoner, and subsequently rode "hotfoot" to [[london|London]] to present his findings to [[king-henry-v|the King]] (Chapter VI). [[the-chevalier|The]] entire episode demonstrated [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] interconnected nature [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] personal violence and political conspiracy in [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] medieval world.
