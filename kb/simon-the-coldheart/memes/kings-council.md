# The King's Council

**The King's Council** was [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] royal advisory body serving [[king-henry-v|King Henry V of England]] during [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] early fifteenth century. In [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] novel, [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Council appears as [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] setting for critical military and political deliberations during [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] [[campaign-in-normandy|Campaign in Normandy]] and [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] events at [[shrewsbury|Shrewsbury]].

## The Council at Shrewsbury

Before [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] [[battle-of-shrewsbury|Battle of Shrewsbury]], [[king-henry-v|the King]] convened councils to plan [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] campaign:

> Within an hour a council was summoned from which Fulk did not return until well into the night, when Simon lay sleeping peacefully and dreamlessly upon his hard pallet. (003-164)

[[the-chevalier|The]] Council's deliberations were intense and prolonged:

> The King held another council, and when Fulk at last rejoined Simon his eyes were kindling with the lust for battle, and his mouth smiled grimly. (003-166)

[[the-chevalier|The]]se passages convey [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Council's function: a war council where military strategy was debated and decided. [[the-chevalier|The]] effect on [[fulk-of-montlice|Fulk of Montlice]] — returning with kindling eyes and grim smile — shows how Council decisions translated directly into battlefield action.

## The Council at Westminster

When [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] brought intelligence [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] [[plot|Crowburg conspiracy]] to [[london|London]], he was summoned before [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Council:

> As before, he was conducted to the King's closet, but this time he found some six or seven gentlemen of the Council there beside the King. (007-036)

[[the-chevalier|The]] composition — "six or seven gentlemen" — suggests a working council ra[[raoul-the-terrible|the]]r than a formal Great Council [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] realm. [[the-chevalier|The]]se were [[king-henry-v|the King]]'s closest advisors, [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] inner circle [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] Lancastrian government. That [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]], a relatively obscure [[knighthood|knight]], was admitted to this chamber marks his elevation into [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] sphere [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] royal confidence.

## The Council and Royal Authority

[[the-chevalier|The]] Council served [[king-henry-v|Henry V]] as an instrument [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] royal will. Its members advised, but [[king-henry-v|the King]] decided. Henry's style — informal, direct, "quizzical" (007-043) — set [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] tone for [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Council's proceedings. [[the-chevalier|The]] Lancastrian council was not a parliament [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] equals but a body [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] trusted servants.

[[the-chevalier|The]] Council's role in [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] novel is primarily military: it plans campaigns, receives intelligence, and dispatches commanders. This reflects [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] historical reality [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[king-henry-v|Henry V]]'s reign, dominated as it was by warfare in France and on [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Scottish marches.
