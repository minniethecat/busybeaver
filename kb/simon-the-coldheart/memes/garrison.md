## Definition

A garrison in medieval military practice is a detachment [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] soldiers assigned to hold and defend a fortified position — a [[stronghold|castle]], walled town, or strategic strongpoint. Unlike a field army, which moves and fights battles, a garrison is a static force whose duty is endurance and vigilance. In [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] novel, garrisons represent [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] institutionalisation [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] military control, transforming conquered places into permanently held positions.

English garrisons in [[normandy|Normandy]] during [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Hundred Years' War were a critical instrument [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] territorial control. Towns such as Harfleur, Caen, and [[cherbourg|Cherbourg]] housed English garrisons that served as bases for fur[[raoul-the-terrible|the]]r operations and as deterrents to French reconquest.
