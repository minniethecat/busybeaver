Razincourt is a French village encountered by [[the-lady-margaret|the Lady Margaret]] and [[jeanne|Jeanne]] during their escape from [[belremy|Belrémy]]. It lies further along the road from [[balderin|Balderin]].

## In the Novel

After leaving Balderin, Margaret and Jeanne arrive at Razincourt around eleven o'clock (020-012). They eat dinner at the village tavern, where Margaret drinks sack — some of which she manages to spill — and endures the rough accommodations. The tavern is described as unpleasant, with a drunken peasant who nearly strikes Margaret, thinking her a saucy pageboy.
