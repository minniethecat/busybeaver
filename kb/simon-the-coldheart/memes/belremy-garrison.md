## Overview

[[the-chevalier|The]] Belrémy Garrison was [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] defending force stationed in [[belremy|Belrémy]] during [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Lancastrian campaign in [[normandy|Normandy]]. Under [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] command [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Count [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[belremy|Belrémy]], [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] garrison held [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] city against [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon of Beauvallet]]'s besieging army.

## Composition and Command

[[the-chevalier|The]] garrison included both regular soldiers and [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Sheriff's men. [[ferdinand-de-valme|Ferdinand de Valmé]], as Sheriff, held authority over [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] city's defences and was [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] designated keeper [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] city keys. [[the-chevalier|The]] garrison's strength is not enumerated in [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] text, but [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Count's confidence in holding [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] city — and his willingness to threaten [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] execution [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[alan-of-montlice|Alan of Montlice]] — suggests a substantial defensive force.

## Role in the Siege

During [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] [[siege-of-belremy|siege of Belrémy]], [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] garrison faced [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]'s methodical assault. [[the-chevalier|The]] Count's ultimatum — threatening [[alan-of-montlice|Alan]]'s life unless [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] city keys were surrendered to [[ferdinand-de-valme|Ferdinand de Valmé]] — reflects [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] garrison's role not merely as a military force but as an instrument [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Count's political will.

> "If ye depart not from this my city within the space of twelve hours, surrendering the keys unto Ferdinand de Valmé, my Sheriff, the knight, Sir Alan of Montlice, swings from the ramparts in thy sight."

> — [015-017]

## Outcome

[[the-chevalier|The]] garrison ultimately surrendered with [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] [[fall-of-belremy|fall of Belrémy]]. After [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] surrender, [[walter-of-santoy|Walter of Santoy]] set a guard [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]'s own men at [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] gates, replacing [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] former garrison with Lancastrian troops.
