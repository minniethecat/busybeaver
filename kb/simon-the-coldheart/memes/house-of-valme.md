## Overview

[[the-chevalier|The]] House of Valme (or Valmé) is a minor noble family in [[belremy|Belrémy]], [[normandy|Normandy]], known through [[ferdinand-de-valme|Ferdinand de Valmé]], who served as Sheriff [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[belremy|Belrémy]] under [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Count. [[the-chevalier|The]] house appears in only a single passage [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] narrative but carries administrative weight: when [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Count demands [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] surrender [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] city, [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] keys are to be delivered to [[ferdinand-de-valme|Ferdinand de Valmé]] personally.

## Political Alignment

[[the-chevalier|The]] House of Valme owes its allegiance to [[house-of-belremy|House of Belrémy]], [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] ruling house [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] city. As Sheriff, [[ferdinand-de-valme|Ferdinand de Valmé]] represents [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] administrative arm [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Count's authority. [[the-chevalier|The]] house's fate is tied to [[belremy|Belrémy]]'s — when [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] city falls to Lancastrian forces, [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Sheriff's role in [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] old order dissolves.

## Relationship to Other Houses

House of Valme serves [[house-of-belremy|House of Belrémy]] in an administrative capacity. Its authority is delegated, not independent — [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Sheriff holds [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] city keys on [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Count's behalf, not his own. This distinguishes minor administrative houses like Valme from [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] greater noble houses ([[montlice|Montlice]], [[malvallet|Malvallet]], [[beauvallet|Beauvallet]]) that command [[raoul-the-terrible|the]]ir own military forces.
