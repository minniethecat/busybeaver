## The Journey

[[the-chevalier|The]] novel opens with [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon of Beauvallet]] walking from Bedford into Cambridge on a May morning. A young man [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] unknown parentage and uncertain future, he arrives at [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] [[stronghold|castle]] [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[fulk-of-montlice|Fulk of Montlice]] seeking service. [[the-chevalier|The]] journey is [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]'s first step from obscurity toward [[barony|lordship]].

> He came walking from Bedford into Cambridge one May morning, when the sun was still young and the dew scarce gone from the grass.
>
> — [001-002]

At Cambridge, [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] encounters [[alan-of-montlice|Alan of Montlice]], [[fulk-of-montlice|Fulk]]'s young son, and through a series [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] events — including a tavern brawl that demonstrates [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]'s physical prowess — he gains an introduction to [[fulk-of-montlice|Fulk]] himself. When asked who he is, [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] gives no family name, answering simply that he is "Nobody."

## Significance

[[the-chevalier|The]] journey from Bedford to Cambridge is [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] novel's foundational event. Everything that follows — [[knighthood|knight]]hood, siege [[battle|warfare]], [[barony|baron]]ial elevation — begins with this walk. [[the-chevalier|The]] journey establishes [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]'s character: physically powerful, proud, nameless, determined to earn his place. [[fulk-of-montlice|Fulk]]'s decision to take [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] into his service, detecting "[[raoul-the-terrible|the]] [[malvallet|Malvallet]] in him" despite [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]'s silence about his birth, is [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] first [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] several acts [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] recognition that drive [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] plot.
