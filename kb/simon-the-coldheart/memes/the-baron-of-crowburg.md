## Role & Significance

The Baron of Crowburg is a minor Lancastrian nobleman, identified as a "faithful adherent to [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] true cause" in a single reference within [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] narrative. His mention serves to illustrate [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] network [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[barony|baron]]ial support underpinning [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Lancastrian faction during [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] political and military conflicts depicted in [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] novel.

> "…and it assured my lord the Baron of Crowburg, faithful adherent to the true cause…"
>
> — [005-075]

His brief appearance in [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] text situates him among [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] lesser nobility who backed [[richard-ii|Richard II]] and later [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Lancastrian succession, forming part [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] political landscape within which [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon of Beauvallet]] rises to prominence.
