# Edmund of Fenton

**Edmund of Fenton** was the Marshal [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[montlice|Montlice]] before [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon of Beauvallet]]'s assumption [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[barony|lordship]]. His tenure was marked by incompetence and neglect, and his dismissal by [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] symbolised [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] transition from [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] old regime to [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] new.

## The Summons

When [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] assumed [[barony|lordship]] at [[montlice|Montlice]], he summoned Edmund along with [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] secretary, [[maurice|Master Hubert]]:

> He had the audacity to summon also Edmund, the Marshal, that aged fool! What said he to Edmund, Master Secretary? (009-029)

[[the-chevalier|The]] secretary's indignation — "that aged fool" — reveals [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] contempt in which the Marshal was held even by his colleagues.

## The Dismissal

[[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]'s [[justice|judgment]] was delivered with characteristic directness, but also with a measure [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] gentleness that surprised [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] onlookers:

> My lord had the full sum from him, and at the end he said with great gentleness, "Edmund of Fenton, it seems that ye grow too old for your task, since rogues thrive under your rule and ye are either too weary or too fearful to check their arrogance." (009-030)

[[the-chevalier|The]] phrasing is significant: [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] did not accuse Edmund [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] corruption but [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] weakness. "Too weary or too fearful" — [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] diagnosis was incapacity born [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] age, not malice. This gentleness, coming from [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] notoriously stern [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]], made [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] dismissal more cutting, not less.

## Aftermath

[[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] appointed a replacement immediately:

> Therefore I say that I will make ye Marshal in Edmund's room, where ye shall prove yourself worthy of my trust. (009-180)

[[the-chevalier|The]] new Marshal's identity spread quickly through [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] [[stronghold|castle]]:

> It is rumoured that Maurice of Gountray is Marshal in Edmund's room. (010-006)

[[the-chevalier|The]] phrase "in Edmund's room" — replacing him in his very chamber — underscores [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] completeness [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] transition. Edmund represented [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] old [[montlice|Montlice]], [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] lax governance [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[fulk-of-montlice|Fulk]]'s later years that allowed "rogues" to "thrive." His removal was a necessary step in [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]'s reformation [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] [[barony|estate]].

Edmund of Fenton appears only briefly, but his dismissal serves as a narrative hinge. It demonstrates [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]'s willingness to remove even long-established [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]]ficers and his capacity for delivering hard [[justice|judgment]]s without unnecessary cruelty.
