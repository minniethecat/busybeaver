## Role & Significance

Malcolm Clayton serves as squire to [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon of Beauvallet]]. Assigned to [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] by [[fulk-of-montlice|Fulk of Montlice]], Malcolm accompanies [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] throughout his campaigns — from [[montlice|Montlice]] to [[belremy|Belrémy]]. His role as squire places him in close proximity to [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] protagonist, attending to [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]'s armour, weapons, and personal needs.

Malcolm is initially disparaged as a "dolt" by [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]'s companion, who protests that Malcolm "would not serve you as well as I." [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] insists on taking Malcolm none[[raoul-the-terrible|the]]less, recognising [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] squire's [[allegiance|loyalty]] and steadiness.

> "Malcolm will serve me better," said Simon evenly. "Send me Malcolm in thy stead. He will maybe serve me better than you suppose."
>
> — [005-111]

Malcolm is characterised by quiet dependability. Unlike [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] more vocal companions around [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]], Malcolm serves without complaint. [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]'s choice [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] Malcolm over a more aggressive squire reflects [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] protagonist's preference for substance over show — a [[raoul-the-terrible|the]]me that recurs throughout [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] narrative. Malcolm's presence at [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] front [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] column in Chapter 14 suggests he has earned his place not through birth but through faithful service, mirroring [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]'s own trajectory.
