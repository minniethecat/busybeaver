## Overview

Bayeux is a town in Normandy where King Henry V held his court during the events of the story. It served as a destination for [[simon-of-beauvallet]] and his retinue when summoned by the king, and was the site of royal audiences and military assemblies.

## Role in the Narrative

Simon travelled to Bayeux to meet with [[king-henry-v]], who was holding court there. The journey to Bayeux marked a significant turning point in Simon's campaign, as the king's summons brought him before the royal authority. The Lady [[the-lady-margaret|Margaret]] reacted with visible distress upon learning that Simon was bound for Bayeux:

> "You—you are going—to Bayeux?" [028-033]

## Royal Court

King Henry V referred to Bayeux as "our town of Bayeux," indicating its status as a royal holding:

> ...to join us here in our town of Bayeux, bringing with you the men of... [031-034]

Simon rode into Bayeux at the head of his men, accompanied by [[alan-of-montlice]], and later departed the following week after completing his business with the king [029-003][029-056].
