## Location & Geography

Santoy is an English region or [[barony|estate]] whose name is borne by its captain, [[walter-of-santoy|Walter of Santoy]]. Its precise location is not described in [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] text, but it falls within [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] Lancastrian sphere and provides men-at-arms to [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon of Beauvallet]]'s campaigns.

## Significance in the Story

Santoy enters [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] narrative through [[walter-of-santoy|Walter of Santoy]], appointed captain under [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]] at [[montlice|Montlice]]. While [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] location itself is not described in detail, its importance lies in [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] men it provides: six [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] Santoy's men are chosen for [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] perilous task [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] holding [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] tower during [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] siege [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[belremy|Belrémy]].

> "Six of Santoy's men went up to hold the tower — a desperate duty, but dauntless."
>
> — [014-134]

[[walter-of-santoy|Walter of Santoy]] proves himself "an admirable commander" in [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]'s service, drilling [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] garrison and leading scouting parties. [[the-chevalier|The]] Santoy contingent becomes a reliable element [[geoffrey-of-malvallet|of]] [[simon-of-beauvallet|Simon]]'s forces throughout [[raoul-the-terrible|the]] [[belremy|Belrémy]] campaign.
