# Madame Suarez's Buffet Flat
The most exclusive private nightlife venue in *Home to [[harlem|Harlem]]*, run by Madame Adeline Suarez, who "catered to sporty colored persons of consequence only and certain groups of downtown whites" (008-006).
## Location
Madame Suarez's [[buffet-flat|buffet flat]] features a "magnificent red carpet," "colored cushions," and "soft, shaded lights" (008-009). It is the venue where [[jake-brown|Jake]] — "the first [[longshoreman]], colored or white, to tread that magnificent red carpet" (008-006) — crosses class boundaries through sheer personal charm.

## Quotations

McKay describes the exclusive clientele of Madame Suarez's:

> Madame Suarez catered to sporty colored persons of consequence only and certain groups of downtown whites that used to frequent Harlem in the good old pre-prohibition days.
>
> (008-006)

Madame Suarez's own words on the class boundaries of her establishment:

> "Ain't got no time for cheap-no-'count niggers," Madame Suarez often said. "Gimme their room to their company any time, even if they've got money to spend."
>
> (008-007)
