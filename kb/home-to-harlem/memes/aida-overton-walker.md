**Aida Overton Walker** (1880–1914), also referred to as Adah Overton, was a celebrated [[africa|Africa]]n American vaudeville performer, choreographer, and singer, known as the "Queen of the [[cakewalk|Cakewalk]]." She is referenced in [[claude-mckay|Claude McKay]]'s *[[home-to-harlem|Home to Harlem]]* under both names.

## In the Novel

Walker appears in two passages. In Chapter VI, a character recalls knowing "[[bert-williams|Bert Williams]] and Walker and Adah Overton" in his sporting days. (006-051) Later, in a [[harlem|Harlem]] social scene, characters "gossiped reminiscently of [[bert-williams|Bert Williams]], [[george-walker|George Walker]] and Aida Overton Walker, [[anita-patti-brown|Anita Patti Brown]] and [[cole-and-johnson|Cole and Johnson]]." (021-007) The variant spellings of her name — Adah in the earlier passage, Aida in the later one — may reflect McKay's rendering of oral recollection, where exact names were subject to the vagaries of memory. Adah Walker also appears earlier in the same chapter among "our big niggers: [[jack-johnson|Jack Johnson]], [[james-reese-europe|James Reese Europe]], Adah Walker, Buddy" (006-049), listed as one of the [[the-sporting-world|sporting world]]'s luminaries.
