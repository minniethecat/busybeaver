**Lodgers** are boarding house residents in [[claude-mckay|Claude McKay]]'s *[[home-to-harlem|Home to Harlem]]*, whose daily lives, gossip, and social interactions form part of the novel's depiction of Harlem's working-class community.

## In the Novel

The term appears in Mrs. Thurlow's description of her tenants as "her gentlemen lodgers." (017-015) The lodgers inhabit a shared social world — when a sensational event occurs in the neighborhood, "those lodgers whose windows gave on the street" gather to watch with excitement. (020-048) "The women lodgers cackled gayly" over neighborhood dramas. (020-049) The lodgers include a diverse cast: elevator runners, a porter who works on Ellis Island, and other working-class Harlem residents. (017-063) They represent the broader community beyond the novel's main characters — the ordinary people of Harlem whose lives intersect in the close quarters of boarding houses and tenements.
