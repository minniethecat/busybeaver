**Leroy's Cabaret** is one of the historic Harlem nightclubs referenced in Claude McKay's *Home to Harlem*. Listed among the great cabarets of the era, Leroy's represents the glamour and musical innovation that made Harlem nightlife legendary during the 1920s.

## In the Novel

Leroy's appears in the novel's recurring catalogues of Harlem cabarets:

> "Leroy's Cabaret!" (001-030)

This exclamation, embedded in Jake's thoughts upon returning to Harlem, positions Leroy's alongside Barron's as a landmark of the neighborhood. The cabaret is also referenced alongside other venues:

> "All the old cabarets were going still… Leroy's…" (004-003)

The description of Leroy's as among the "old cabarets" suggests it was a well-established institution with a history in the neighborhood.

## Historical Leroy's

The historical Leroy's was located at 135th Street and Fifth Avenue. It was one of Harlem's most famous cabarets, known for its floor shows and jazz performances. Like other Harlem nightclubs of the era, it attracted both Black patrons and white "slummers" from downtown, creating the racially mixed spaces that both fascinated and troubled observers of the Renaissance.

## Legacy

> "Leroy's…" (018-059)

The persistence of Leroy's as a named reference throughout the novel — from Jake's first excited thoughts of Harlem to later narrative mentions — establishes it as a fixture of the neighborhood's social geography.
