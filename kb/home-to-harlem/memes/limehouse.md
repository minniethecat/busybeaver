**Limehouse** is a district in London's East End that appears in [[claude-mckay|Claude McKay]]'s *[[home-to-harlem|Home to Harlem]]* as the neighborhood where [[jake-brown|Jake Brown]] lived while working as a docker at the [[west-india-docks|West India Docks]].

## Location

### In the Novel's World

Limehouse is presented as part of [[jake-brown|Jake]]'s experience of [[london|London]]'s docklands. After deserting from the U.S. Army in Brest during World War I, Jake crossed to [[london|London]] and found work at the nearby [[west-india-docks|West India Docks]]. "He liked the West India Docks. He liked Limehouse." (001-024) The pubs of Limehouse were places where local men "gave him their friendly paws and called him 'darky'" (001-024) — a racial appellation Jake found novel and not unwelcome. In Limehouse he "made friends. He found a woman. He was happy in the East End." (001-024)

## Significance to the Narrative

Limehouse represents Jake's time abroad — a period of freedom and self-discovery between his desertion from the military and his return to [[harlem|Harlem]]. Unlike the racial tensions of the United States, [[london|London]] offered Jake a different social experience, one where he felt accepted and at ease. Later in the novel, Jake sings "a sailor song he had picked up in Limehouse" (019-069) at Aunt Hattie's, briefly revisiting his London memories through music.

## From the Novel

The simple happiness Jake finds in London's Limehouse is rendered with unadorned directness:

> He liked the West India Docks. He liked Limehouse. In the pubs men gave him their friendly paws and called him "darky." He liked how they called him "darky." He made friends. He found a woman. He was happy in the East End.
>
> (001-024)

## Historical Context

Limehouse was historically London's first Chinatown and a working-class docklands district, associated with the West India Docks (opened 1802) and immigrant communities. In the early twentieth century, it was known for its pubs, sailors, and cosmopolitan character — a fitting setting for Jake's sojourn abroad.

Limehouse appears or is referenced in chapters 1 and 19. (001-024, 019-069)
