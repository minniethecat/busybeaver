**Gordon Dry** is a brand of [[gin-whisky|gin]] referenced in [[claude-mckay|Claude McKay]]'s 1928 novel *[[home-to-harlem|Home to Harlem]]*. It appears as a popular drink among the novel's characters, consumed in [[harlem|Harlem]]'s [[cabaret-culture|cabarets]] and [[buffet-flat|buffet flats]].

## In the Novel

Gordon Dry [[gin-whisky|gin]] appears in multiple social scenes across the novel. In Chapter IV, [[zeddy-plummer|Zeddy]] "tossed down the third glass of Gordon gin and became aware of Rose, the Congo entertainer." (004-078) Shortly after, [[zeddy-plummer|Zeddy]] slips away and "Another Scotch and Gordon Dry. The glasses kissed. Like a lean lazy leopard the mulattress reclined against Jake." (004-085) — the brand woven into the novel's sensual, syncopated prose rhythm.

In Chapter VI, a character's appraisal is blunt: "She's sho one ugly spade, but she's right there with her Gordon Dry." (006-017), the gin serving as a redeeming quality. The brand's cultural weight is clearest when it becomes an oath:

> "I ain't. Honest-to-Gawd Gordon Dry, and moh — ef you're the goods, all F.O.C."
>
> (006-033)

Invoking Gordon Dry as a truth-pledge — "Honest-to-God Gordon Dry" — suggests the brand carried a reputation for authenticity and quality among Harlem's working class. In Chapter VII, [[susy|Susy]] declares hospitality through the brand: "Ise got plenty o' good Gordon Dry foh eve'body." (007-034), positioning it as a staple of [[harlem|Harlem]] social gatherings. The repeated mentions across Chapters IV, VI, and VII — in scenes of romance, barroom banter, and domestic hosting — establish Gordon Dry as part of the novel's detailed portrait of [[harlem|Harlem]]'s drinking culture during the [[prohibition|Prohibition]] era.

## Historical Note

Gordon's Dry Gin was (and remains) a real commercial product, first produced in [[london|London]] in 1769 by Alexander Gordon. Its appearance in McKay's novel reflects the brand's genuine popularity and availability in 1920s [[harlem|Harlem]], where imported liquors circulated despite [[prohibition|Prohibition]].
