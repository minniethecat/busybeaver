**Alabama** is referenced in Claude McKay's *Home to Harlem* as part of the Southern geography that shapes the backgrounds of Harlem's Black migrants. During the Great Migration, Alabama was a significant source state for African Americans moving north to cities like New York, Chicago, and Philadelphia.

## In the Novel

Alabama appears in brief but meaningful references that locate characters within the broader geography of Black America:

> "…Alabama…" (012-007)

> "…Alabama…" (020-043)

These passing mentions situate Alabama within the novel's unspoken map of the South — a place of origin for characters who have made the journey to Harlem seeking economic opportunity and greater personal freedom.

## Great Migration Significance

Like other Deep South states, Alabama represented both the oppression of Jim Crow and the cultural roots that migrants carried north. The blues, religious traditions, and folkways that migrants brought from Alabama and neighboring states enriched Harlem's cultural landscape during the Renaissance period.
