**Dick Mulberry** is a historical figure mentioned in [[claude-mckay|Claude McKay]]'s *[[home-to-harlem|Home to Harlem]]* as "a promoter of Negro shows" and the husband of [[madame-mulberry|Madame Mulberry]]. (021-007)

## In the Novel

Dick Mulberry appears only by reference in a passage describing a Harlem social gathering where "the music world were represented." (021-007) His wife, [[madame-mulberry|Madame Mulberry]], is present "wearing peacock blue" (021-007) and is a more prominent figure in the novel's social scenes. His role as a promoter of Negro shows places him within the world of early Black entertainment and theater that flourished during the Harlem Renaissance.
