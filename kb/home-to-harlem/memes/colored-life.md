**Colored Life** was a Negro newspaper referenced in [[claude-mckay|Claude McKay]]'s *[[home-to-harlem|Home to Harlem]]*.

## In the Novel

The newspaper is mentioned in a single passage describing a fashionable gathering at a Harlem cabaret: "The sports editor of Colored Life was also there, with a prominent Negro politician." (021-009) The scene depicts a convergence of Harlem's music world, press, and political figures. The presence of the sports editor at such an event suggests the newspaper's role in covering the social and cultural life of Harlem's Black community.

Colored Life was part of a vibrant ecosystem of Black newspapers in the 1920s, alongside publications such as [[the-chicago-defender|The Chicago Defender]], [[the-negro-world|The Negro World]], and the [[amsterdam-news|Amsterdam News]]. These newspapers chronicled Black social life, politics, and cultural achievements during the Harlem Renaissance. (010-014)
