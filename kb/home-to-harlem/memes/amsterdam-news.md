The **Amsterdam News** (also known as the *New York Amsterdam News*) is a Negro newspaper referenced in [[claude-mckay|Claude McKay]]'s *[[home-to-harlem|Home to Harlem]]* as part of the reading material of Harlem residents.

## In the Novel

The newspaper appears in two scenes. A character is found "reading the Negro newspaper, The Amsterdam News, with a pail of beer beside him." (014-022) Later, another character "glanced carelessly through The Amsterdam News" and spots a social notice: "I saw Madame Laura in Fairmount Park." (015-025) The Amsterdam News was one of the most influential Black newspapers of the Harlem Renaissance, alongside publications like [[the-chicago-defender|The Chicago Defender]], [[the-negro-world|The Negro World]], and [[colored-life|Colored Life]]. Founded in 1909, it chronicled the social, political, and cultural life of Harlem's Black community. (010-014)
