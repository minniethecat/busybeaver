# Madame Laura

A [[harlem|Harlem]] woman who hosts one of the novel's most atmospherically dense party scenes — the [[buffet-flat|buffet flat]] gathering in Chapter XIII where [[jake-brown|Jake]] brings [[ray]], the evening dissolves into music and coupling, and a smiling white policeman raids the premises. Madame Laura presides over this episode with the authority of a veteran nightlife operator, embodying the informal economic agency of Black women in [[harlem|Harlem]]'s [[the-sporting-world|sporting world]].

## Role in the Novel

Madame Laura appears in Chapter XIII as the host of a [[buffet-flat|buffet flat]] gathering to which [[jake-brown|Jake]] brings [[ray]]. [[jake-brown|Jake]] introduces [[ray]] casually: "Here, Laura, this is mah friend" (013-005). She commands them: "Bring him over here and sit down" (013-008) — her tone establishing her as a woman accustomed to authority within her domain.

The party unfolds under her management. A pianist plays [[blues]]. Her young son — "a boy of dull-gold complexion," about eleven, "clever kid, too. He loves books" (013-022) — appears at her side, a startling domestic detail in a room full of drinking and flirtation. The boy goes to the piano and converses with the pianist, while Madame Laura turns her attention to [[jake-brown|Jake]].

McKay captures the evening's sensual atmosphere with economy: "Madame Laura had set the pace. There were four other couples making love" (013-031). A "big-built, very black man" amuses himself with two attractive girls, one brown-skinned and one yellow. "A bottle of [[muscatel|Muscatel]] stood on the table. The man was well [[fashion-and-style|dress]]ed in nigger-brown and he wore an expensive diamond ring on his little finger" (013-031). The card-playing stags continue their game; the "happy-faced black woman" manages the room, "as Madame Laura was otherwise engaged" (013-043).

The evening's climax is abrupt and theatrical: "Raided!" — a white policeman in full uniform stands at the rear door, "smilingly contemplated the spectacle" (013-048). The old-timers shrug and stay seated; newcomers scramble. And then, with the practiced ease of someone who has navigated this ritual many times before, "Madame Laura pushed aside the policeman" (013-048). He twirls his baton and sits down at a table with her — the raid resolved into negotiation, the law domesticated by a woman who knows how to handle it.

## Characterization

McKay characterizes Madame Laura through action rather than description. She is defined by what she does: commands, hosts, flirts, manages, and ultimately faces down a policeman with a push and a negotiation. Her "Madame" title — shared with [[madame-suarez|Madame Suarez]], [[madame-mulberry|Madame Mulberry]], and others — places her among [[harlem|Harlem]]'s female entrepreneurs of [[pleasure-seeking|pleasure]], women who converted their apartments into [[buffet-flat|buffet flats]] and their social skills into income.

The presence of her son at the party is McKay's most striking detail. An eleven-year-old boy, book-loving and clever, drifting between the piano and his mother's side while couples make love and [[gin-whisky|gin]] flows — the image captures the domestic-sensual blur that defines the [[buffet-flat|buffet flat]] as a social institution.

## Themes

Madame Laura embodies the **informal economy of Black women's entrepreneurship** in [[harlem|Harlem]]. Like [[madame-suarez|Madame Suarez]], she operates a space — part social club, part [[speakeasy]], part brothel — that exists outside formal legality but inside the community's economic and social logic.

The raid scene encapsulates the **ambiguous relationship between [[harlem|Harlem]] nightlife and the law**. The policeman is white and smiling; the old-timers are unsurprised. Madame Laura does not flee or plead — she pushes past the officer and negotiates, and the raid dissolves into a drink at a table. This is [[prohibition|Prohibition]]-era policing as McKay observed it: a ritual of control that was also, in practice, a negotiation, with Black women like Madame Laura as the skilled intermediaries.
