**George Walker** (c. 1873–1911) was a pioneering [[africa|Africa]]n American vaudeville performer, best known as one half of the comedy duo [[bert-williams|Williams and Walker]], referenced in [[claude-mckay|Claude McKay]]'s *[[home-to-harlem|Home to Harlem]]*.

## In the Novel

Walker is mentioned in two contexts. In a passage set in [[harlem|Harlem]], characters "gossiped reminiscently of [[bert-williams|Bert Williams]], George Walker and [[aida-overton-walker|Aida Overton Walker]]" (021-007), recalling the golden age of Black vaudeville. Earlier, a character boasts of knowing "[[bert-williams|Bert Williams]] and Walker and [[aida-overton-walker|Adah Overton]]" in his sporting days. (006-051) These references position Walker among the revered figures of early Black entertainment whose legacy was still felt in 1920s [[harlem|Harlem]].
