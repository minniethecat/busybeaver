**Palm Beach** is a resort town in Florida, referenced in [[claude-mckay|Claude McKay]]'s 1928 novel *[[home-to-harlem|Home to Harlem]]*.

The town is part of the novel's wider geography of [[great-migration|Black migration]] and travel, which spans from [[harlem|Harlem]] to [[pittsburgh|Pittsburgh]], [[philadelphia|Philadelphia]], [[london|London]], and the American South.

## References in the Novel

Palm Beach appears in a conversation between [[jake-brown|Jake Brown]] and [[felice|Felice]], a young woman he meets at the Sheba Palace cabaret. After their first encounter, Jake searches for her at the cabaret for many nights. When they finally reunite, Felice explains her absence:

> "Why, honey, I don't lives in cabarets all mah nights 'cause Ise got to work. Furthermore, I done went away that next week to Palm Beach—"
>
> "Palm Beach! What foh?"
>
> (020-014)(020-015)

The passage establishes Palm Beach as a destination for seasonal service work. Felice's trip there is part of the pattern of Black labor migration that links Florida's resorts to Harlem's working world.

Later in the novel, another character recalls a visit, noting "It was jest when I got back from Palm Beach" (021-085).
