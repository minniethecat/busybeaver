**Liver-lip** is a minor character in [[claude-mckay|Claude McKay]]'s *[[home-to-harlem|Home to Harlem]]*, a fellow [[longshoreman]] encountered at [[dixie-red|Dixie Red]]'s [[poolroom]] who has been injured fighting strikebreakers.

## Description

He is described as an "iron-heavy, blue-black lad" whose nickname derives from "the plankiness of his lips." (005-022) He appears at [[dixie-red|Dixie Red]]'s [[poolroom]] with his arm in a sling, among other injured [[longshoreman|longshoremen]], and recounts to [[jake-brown|Jake]] the story of how he was attacked.

## In the Narrative

Liver-lip tells [[jake-brown|Jake]] that strikebreakers "jumped on me soon as I turned mah black moon on that li'l saloon tha's catering to us niggers." (005-022) He fought back with a [[razor]] — "I got out mah shaving steel and draws it down the goosey flesh o' one o' them, and, buddy, you shoulda heah him squeal." (005-022) He notes that the police "tunned me loose by'n'by" because "they's with us this time," (005-022) suggesting a rare moment of police tolerance toward Black strikers. His brief appearance illustrates the labor militancy and street violence that were part of [[harlem|Harlem]] [[longshoreman|longshoremen]]'s lives.
