# 369th Infantry Regiment (Harlem Hellfighters)
The all-Black U.S. Army regiment that served under French command during WWI, spending more time in combat than any other American unit. Their military band, led by [[james-reese-europe|James Reese Europe]], introduced syncopated Black music to Europe.
## Role in the Novel
The Hellfighters are invoked indirectly through the figure of [[james-reese-europe|James Reese Europe]], whose "celebrated band" had "just returned from France, full of honors" (021-010). The regiment's legacy — Black martial valor and cultural achievement — frames the novel's concern with Black masculinity and its expressions.

## Quotations

McKay's description of James Reese Europe, the Hellfighters' bandleader, at the height of his post-war fame:

> James Reese Europe, the famous master of jazz, was among a group of white admirers. He had just returned from France, full of honors, with his celebrated band. New York had acclaimed him and America was ready to applaud.
>
> (021-010)
