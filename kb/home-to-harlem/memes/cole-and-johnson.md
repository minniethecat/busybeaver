**Cole and Johnson** refers to the pioneering African American songwriting and performing partnership of Bob Cole (1868–1911) and J. Rosamond Johnson (1873–1954), referenced in [[claude-mckay|Claude McKay]]'s *[[home-to-harlem|Home to Harlem]]*.

## In the Novel

The team is mentioned in a single passage where Harlem characters "gossiped reminiscently of [[bert-williams|Bert Williams]], [[george-walker|George Walker]] and [[aida-overton-walker|Aida Overton Walker]], [[anita-patti-brown|Anita Patti Brown]] and Cole and Johnson." (021-007) Their inclusion in this roll call of distinguished Black performers places them among the most celebrated figures in early African American theater. Cole and Johnson were known for their sophisticated musical comedies, including *A Trip to Coontown* (1898) and *The Shoo-Fly Regiment* (1906), and their song "Under the Bamboo Tree" became a major hit.
