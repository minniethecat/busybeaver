**Pittsburgh** is a [[pennsylvania-railroad|Pennsy]]lvania city that appears in [[claude-mckay|Claude McKay]]'s *Home to [[harlem|Harlem]]*, primarily in chapter 11 ("Snowstorm in Pittsburgh"), where [[jake-brown|Jake]] experiences the city during a railroad layover. It is portrayed as unwelcoming and hostile — a cold, industrial counterpoint to [[harlem|Harlem]]'s warmth.

## Location

### In the Novel's World

Pittsburgh is a waypoint on [[jake-brown|Jake]]'s railroad travels, reached after the train passes through Ohio and "rolled slowly into the station of Pittsburgh." (010-096) It is a city of transit, not destination — a place where railroad men stop but never linger by choice.

## Significance to the Narrative

Pittsburgh's most sustained treatment comes in chapter 11, titled "Snowstorm in Pittsburgh." The chapter opens with [[jake-brown|Jake]] stepping off the bridge into a snowstorm, his first impression of the city one of bitter cold and urban desolation. (011-001)(011-002)

The city is deeply disliked by the railroad workers: "The railroad men hated the Pittsburgh run. They hated the town, the life, the people." (011-014) [[jake-brown|Jake]] himself is unenthusiastic: "There's nothing ever in Pittsburgh for me. I'm in no mood for the burg." (011-021) The only positive association is with the weekly newspaper, the *Pittsburgh Courier*, one of the leading Black newspapers of the era. (011-024)

## From the Novel

The railroad men's disdain for Pittsburgh is bluntly stated:

> The railroad men hated the Pittsburgh run. They hated the town, they hated Wiley Avenue and their wretched free quarters that were in it.
>
> (011-014)

## Historical Context

Pittsburgh was a major destination for Black migrants during the Great Migration, drawn by jobs in the steel industry and on the railroads. The *Pittsburgh Courier*, founded in 1907, was one of the most widely circulated Black newspapers in America. McKay's portrayal of the city as bleak and inhospitable may reflect its industrial, working-class character compared to the cultural vibrancy of [[harlem|Harlem]].

## Appearances

Pittsburgh appears in chapters 10–11 and 18. (010-051)–(018-002)
