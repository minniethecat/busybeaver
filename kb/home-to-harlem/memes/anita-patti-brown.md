**Anita Patti Brown** is a historical figure, described in [[claude-mckay|Claude McKay]]'s *[[home-to-harlem|Home to Harlem]]* as a "celebrated Negro singer" mentioned in a passage recalling the luminaries of early Black entertainment. (021-007)

## In the Novel

Brown is referenced in a single sentence in which characters "gossiped reminiscently" about the greats of Black vaudeville and theater — "[[bert-williams|Bert Williams]], [[george-walker|George Walker]] and [[aida-overton-walker|Aida Overton Walker]], Anita Patti Brown and [[cole-and-johnson|Cole and Johnson]]." (021-007) Her inclusion in this list of distinguished performers establishes her as part of the generation of Black artists who built the foundation of [[africa|Africa]]n American theater and music before and during the [[harlem-renaissance|Harlem Renaissance]].
