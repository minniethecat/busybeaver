# Aunt Hattie

The proprietress of a [[harlem|Harlem]] cookshop — a small, home-based restaurant — whose fried chicken, candied sweet potatoes, and maternal presence offer the novel's characters (and readers) respite from the frenetic energy of [[cabaret-culture|cabarets]] and [[buffet-flat|buffet flats]].

## Role in the Novel

Aunt Hattie's cookshop is one of the few spaces in *Home to [[harlem|Harlem]]* defined by food rather than music, drink, or sex. It is where [[jake-brown|Jake]], [[ray|Ray]], and their friends eat "fried chicken" and "candied sweet potatoes sweeter than honey" (018-034) — meals that ground the characters in domestic comfort. Aunt Hattie herself is portrayed with warmth: she touches [[jake-brown|Jake]]'s glass twice in a toast and laughs with her guests, embodying a maternal presence absent elsewhere in the novel's nightlife-dominated social world.

## Key Scenes

**The dinner (Chapter XVIII):** Aunt Hattie hosts [[jake-brown|Jake]], [[ray|Ray]], and [[billy-biasse|Billy Biasse]] for a meal that provides one of the novel's few scenes of uncomplicated communal pleasure. "Glasses clinked and Aunt Hattie touched [[jake-brown|Jake]]'s twice and closed her eyes as with trembling hand she guzzled" (018-032) — her participation in the drinking, not just the cooking, makes her part of the social circle rather than merely its server.

## Quotations

McKay's vivid description of Aunt Hattie and her establishment:

> Aunt Hattie's was renowned among the lowly of Harlem's Black Belt. It was a little basement joint, smoke-colored. And Aunt Hattie was weather-beaten dark-brown, cheery-faced, with two rusty-red front teeth sticking together conspicuously out of her twisted, spread-away mouth. She cooked delicious food — home-cooked food they called it.
>
> (003-028)

Billy Biasse's tribute to Aunt Hattie's nurturing role:

> "Always in luck, Jakey," said Billy. "It's no wonder you nevah see niggers in the bread line. And you'll nevah so long as theah's good black womens like Aunt Hattie in Harlem."
>
> (018-024)
