***The Negro World*** was the official newspaper of Marcus Garvey's Universal Negro Improvement Association (UNIA), referenced in [[claude-mckay|Claude McKay]]'s *[[home-to-harlem|Home to Harlem]]* as part of [[ray|Ray]]'s reading in Pittsburgh.

## In the Novel

[[ray|Ray]] buys a copy of *The Negro World* in Pittsburgh alongside other weekly Negro newspapers: *The Pittsburgh Courier*, *The Baltimore American*, and *[[the-chicago-defender|The Chicago Defender]]*. The narrator notes that in Pittsburgh Ray "found a big assortment of all the Negro publications that he never could find in Harlem." (011-021) The presence of *The Negro World* among Ray's reading material connects him to the broader currents of Black nationalist and pan-Africanist thought that Garvey's movement represented.

## Historical Context

*The Negro World* was founded in 1918 by Marcus Garvey as the official organ of the UNIA. At its peak in the early 1920s, it had a circulation of over 200,000 and was distributed internationally, with editions in English, Spanish, and French. The paper promoted Garvey's message of Black pride, economic self-sufficiency, and the "Back to Africa" movement. Its presence in McKay's novel is significant given that [[claude-mckay|Claude McKay]] himself had complex engagements with Garveyism — admiring its racial militancy while remaining skeptical of its nationalist politics.

## Quotations

Ray's purchase of the Negro press in Pittsburgh, situating The Negro World within the broader landscape of Black journalism:
