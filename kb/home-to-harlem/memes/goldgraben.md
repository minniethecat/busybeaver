**Goldgraben's** is a cabaret on Lenox Avenue referenced in Claude McKay's *Home to Harlem*. It appears in the novel's enumeration of Harlem nightlife venues, specifically located on Lenox Avenue — one of the neighborhood's primary commercial and entertainment thoroughfares.

## In the Novel

Goldgraben's is mentioned alongside Barron's, Connor's, and Leroy's as part of Harlem's cabaret ecosystem:

> "Goldgraben's on Lenox Avenue…" (004-004)

> "…was leading all the Negro cabarets a cruel dance. The big-spiri…" (004-004)

Its location on Lenox Avenue places it at the heart of Harlem's entertainment district. Lenox Avenue was the rival thoroughfare to Seventh Avenue, with its own collection of cabarets, speakeasies, and nightclubs that competed for the patronage of Harlem's diverse population.

## Lenox Avenue Context

Lenox Avenue (now also known as Malcolm X Boulevard) was, along with Seventh Avenue, one of the two main north-south arteries of Harlem's commercial and social life. During the 1920s, Lenox Avenue was lined with cabarets, dance halls, and speakeasies that defined the Harlem Renaissance's nightlife. Goldgraben's, as a Lenox Avenue establishment, was situated at the epicenter of this cultural ferment.
