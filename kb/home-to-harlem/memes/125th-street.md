**125th Street** is a major east–west thoroughfare in [[harlem|Harlem]], New York City, referenced in [[claude-mckay|Claude McKay]]'s *[[home-to-harlem|Home to Harlem]]* as the location of a German delicatessen where characters sometimes received meals.

## In the Novel

The street is mentioned in a single passage in which a character recalls that "from the German delicatessen in 125th Street... sometimes they asked me to eat with them." (017-010) The reference is brief but locates the action within the real geography of Harlem, where 125th Street has long been the neighborhood's principal commercial artery. During the 1920s, 125th Street was a hub of Harlem's business, entertainment, and social life, lined with theaters, speakeasies, and shops serving the growing Black community.
