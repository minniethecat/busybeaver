The **express subway** is referenced in [[claude-mckay|Claude McKay]]'s *[[home-to-harlem|Home to Harlem]]* as the mode of transit that brings [[jake-brown|Jake Brown]] to Harlem upon his return to New York City. Also referred to simply as the **subway**, it represents the modern urban infrastructure connecting the disparate worlds of the novel.

## In the Novel

After arriving at [[south-ferry|South Ferry]] with fifty-nine dollars, Jake "took an express subway train for Harlem." (002-002) The express subway — as distinct from local trains — was a key element of New York City's transit infrastructure, enabling rapid movement between Lower Manhattan and Harlem. Jake's use of the express subway signals both his familiarity with the city and the physical distance between the docks of Lower Manhattan and the Black neighborhood uptown where he belongs.

References to the subway recur throughout the novel as characters navigate the city: [[strawberry-lips|Strawberry Lips]] leaves "the subway train at Borough Hall" (006-037); characters urge one another to "git the subway" (006-086); and Jake and others regularly "caught the subway train for Harlem" (007-085) or Brooklyn (007-161). The subway is woven into the fabric of the characters' daily lives, an unremarked but essential part of the modern urban landscape McKay depicts.
