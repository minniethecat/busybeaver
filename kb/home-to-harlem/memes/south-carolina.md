**South Carolina** appears in Claude McKay's *Home to Harlem* as one of the Southern states from which Black characters migrated to Harlem during the Great Migration. The state is referenced in the context of character backgrounds and regional identities that shape the novel's social landscape.

## The Great Migration Context

South Carolina was a major source state for the Great Migration, the mass movement of African Americans from the rural South to Northern cities between 1916 and 1930. Characters in the novel who hail from or reference South Carolina bring with them Southern speech patterns, cultural practices, and memories that contrast with Harlem's urban sophistication.

## Corpus References

The novel references South Carolina in character dialogue and description:

> "…South Carolina…" (006-008)

> "…South Carolina…" (006-020)

> "…South Carolina…" (017-002)

These passing references situate the state within the novel's geography of migration, where the South represents both origin and memory for Harlem's Black population.

## Regional Identity in the Novel

The novel distinguishes between characters of Southern origin and those born in Northern cities, with South Carolina and other Southern states representing a cultural and experiential divide. The tensions between rural Southern migrants and established Harlem residents form a recurring theme in the novel's social dynamics.
