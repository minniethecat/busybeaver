John Milton (1608–1674) appears in Chesterton's "[[section-12|The Wrong Incendiary]]" as the representative of a lost Puritan comprehensiveness.

Chesterton's concern is not Milton's poetry but his social meaning: Milton stands at one end of a spectrum that has been narrowed into something smaller and more merely conventional.

## The Old Puritan Unity

Chesterton's argument concerns "large monochrome patches" in modern society — broad uniformities that have replaced older, more variegated forms of community.

He offers this as a personal regret: "Thus I, for one, regret the supersession of the old Puritan unity, founded on theology, but embracing all types from Milton to the grocer, by that newer Puritan unity which is founded rather on certain social habits, certain common notions, both permissive and prohibitive, in connection with Particular social pleasures" (011-108–011-112).

The phrase "from Milton to the grocer" is the key. The old Puritan unity was theological in its foundation and could hold together the poet and the shopkeeper in a single community of belief.

The new Puritan unity, by contrast, is merely social. It coheres around "certain social habits" and "common notions" about pleasure and propriety. It is narrower, shallower, and for Chesterton, a clear loss.

Milton's function is to represent the high end of the spectrum — the intellectual and poetic summit of a tradition that could also accommodate the ordinary tradesman.

Chesterton does not discuss Milton's poetry, politics, or theology in any detail. He simply invokes the name as a cultural marker: the old Puritanism could contain a Milton; the new Puritanism can only contain a social convention.

The reference reveals a recurring Chestertonian theme: the preference for an older, more capacious social order over a modern one that is ostensibly more liberal but in practice more constraining.

The Puritanism that produced *Paradise Lost* was capable of containing multitudes; the Puritanism of Chesterton's own day was capable only of enforcing a narrow code of social respectability.
