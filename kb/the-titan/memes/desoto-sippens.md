# Henry De Soto Sippens

Henry De Soto Sippens is [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]]'s first recruit in the [[chicago|Chicago]] gas wars — a veteran engineer whose earlier experience with "a franchise to manufacture and sell gas in the downtown districts" (007-020) makes him the ideal practical lieutenant. Energetic, contentious, and hungry for a second [[fate|chance]], Sippens represents the technical competence that [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] needs to transform [[finance|financial]] ambition into physical infrastructure.

## Biography

### The Early Failure

Sippens's earlier gas company had "once been a smaller organization" (007-020) that "had entered and actually secured, by some hocus-pocus, a franchise" before being absorbed by the larger [[south-side|South Side]] company. This experience — the technical knowledge gained and the franchise lost — leaves Sippens bitter but hungry, the perfect raw material for [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]]'s purposes.

### The Recruitment

[[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]]'s approach is characteristically direct. "Mr. Sippens, you were once in the gas manufacturing and distributing business here in [[chicago|Chicago]], weren't you?" (007-023). Sippens's response is immediate and emphatic: "I think I know as much about the manufacture of gas as anyone. I worked at it for a number of years" (007-024). The offer that follows — six thousand a year and a share in the company — is more than Sippens has seen in a decade. His reaction is explosive: "Yes, I do, Mr. [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]]!" exclaimed Sippens, jumping to his feet, putting on his hat and shoving it far back on his head. He looked like a chest-swollen bantam rooster" (007-033).

### The Campaign

Sippens becomes [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]]'s general practical adviser across the franchise companies. "[[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]], once the franchises had been secured, had installed Sippens in his own office, giving him charge for the time being" (009-007). He oversees the physical plant-building, the endless legal suits to "enjoin, annul, and restrain," and the daily operations that transform legislative victories into working gas lines. He works alongside [[general-van-sickle|Van Sickle]] and [[kent-mckibben|McKibben]] but occupies a distinct role — the engineer rather than the fixer or the front man.

## Relationships

Sippens's relationship with [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] is one of fierce loyalty born of gratitude. After a decade of obscurity, the [[finance|financier]] has given him not just employment but vindication. "Oh, the wonder of this, and after ten years! Now he would show those people" (007-036) — Sippens's ecstasy on leaving [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]]'s office captures the emotional economy of mentorship and dependency that runs through the novel.

## Character Analysis

Sippens is the novel's archetype of the competent man rescued from obscurity — a figure who appears repeatedly in Dreiser's world. His "almost contentious" (007-024) manner and his "chest-swollen bantam rooster" energy are products of talent denied its outlet. Once given scope, Sippens repays [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] with unquestioning service, exemplifying the reciprocal obligations that underpin the [[finance|financier]]'s network.
