# Harold Hand

Harold Hand is one of the "great manipulators, like [[arneel|Arneel]], Hand, [[norman-schryhart|Schryhart]] and others" (007-002) who dominate [[chicago|Chicago]] finance when [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] arrives. Conservative, established, and protective of his position, Hand sees [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] not as a colleague but as a threat — an outsider whose "buccaneering methods" threaten the stability of the [[finance|financial]] order he has helped build.

## Biography

### The Establishment Man

Hand is a product of the [[chicago|Chicago]] establishment, with deep roots in the city's banking and industrial networks. His opposition to [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] is partly business — he sees the street-railway consolidation as reckless overreach — and partly personal. He is "an eminent investor" (031-007) whose [[fate|fortune]] and reputation give him the standing to challenge [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] publicly.

### The Personal Dimension

The business rivalry between Hand and [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] is complicated by a personal betrayal. [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] "ventured to establish a liaison with Mrs. [[hosmer-hand|Hosmer Hand]], wife of an eminent investor" (031-007). This affair transforms what [[power|might]] have been a routine business competition into something more intimate and more dangerous. Hand's subsequent attacks on [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] are motivated by vengeance as much as by [[finance|financial]] calculation.

### The Panic

During the [[finance|financial]] panic, Hand is among those who refuse to extend credit or protection to [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]]. "No, no, no! I can't risk another dollar in this venture, and I won't!" (047-016) — Hand's declaration over the phone captures the calculus of self-preservation that governs the [[finance|financial]] elite's response to crisis.

## Relationships

Hand's relationship with [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] is the central axis of his character — a rivalry that begins in the boardroom and escalates into personal war. His marriage to Mrs. [[hosmer-hand|Hosmer Hand]] becomes a battlefield in that war, with [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]]'s affair turning a business opponent into a cuckolded husband bent on revenge.

## Character Analysis

Hand represents the conservative wing of [[chicago|Chicago]] finance — men who have made their [[fate|fortune]]s and now seek to protect them. His opposition to [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] is not hypocritical (he knows the system is corrupt) but territorial: [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] threatens to disrupt an order that has served Hand well.
