# Arneel

Arneel is one of the foundational figures of the [[chicago|Chicago]] [[finance|financial]] establishment — "portly, conservative, aggressive" (002-022). Like [[norman-schryhart|Schryhart]] and [[harold-hand|Hand]], he is a "great manipulator" (007-002) whose position at the apex of [[chicago|Chicago]] finance gives him both the means and the inclination to resist [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]]'s ascent. Where [[norman-schryhart|Schryhart]] is emotional and [[harold-hand|Hand]] is personal, Arneel is institutional — his opposition reflects the collective judgment of the city's moneyed class.

## Biography

### The Establishment Figure

Arneel first meets [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] at the [[union-club|Union Club]], in "a private dining-room of heavily carved oak" (002-022), the mise-en-scene of [[finance|financial]] power in Gilded Age [[chicago|Chicago]]. He approves of [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]]'s shrewdness but never fully trusts him — the outsider from [[philadelphia|Philadelphia]] brings methods and ambitions that unsettle the conservative order Arneel has spent a lifetime building.

### The Anti-Cowperwood Coalition

As [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]]'s power grows, Arneel emerges as a coordinating figure in the opposition. He is "sour" (034-017) when [[judah-addison|Addison]] notifies the board of the [[lakeview|Lake View]] Council about [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]]'s maneuverings, and he joins [[norman-schryhart|Schryhart]] and [[harold-hand|Hand]] in the [[politics|political]] campaign against their common enemy. The Arneel-[[harold-hand|Hand]]-[[norman-schryhart|Schryhart]] combination becomes "a really unprecedented antagonism" (054-017), a unified front of established wealth against the upstart.

### The Turning Point

At the Arneel house conference, when [[hosmer-hand|Hosmer Hand]] declares "I must give in," Arneel is "silent and dour" (050-004), having been "cavalierly affronted by a man" who refuses to play by the rules of deference that the [[finance|financial]] aristocracy expects. His silence is the silence of a class that has lost its power to command obedience.

## Relationships

Arneel's primary relationships are with his fellow [[finance|financier]]s — [[harold-hand|Hand]] and [[norman-schryhart|Schryhart]] form the triumvirate at the center of opposition to [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]]. His relationship with [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] himself is cool and correct, a clash of temperaments as much as interests. Where [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] is daring and improvisational, Arneel is methodical and precedent-bound.

## Character Analysis

Arneel embodies the paradox of conservative finance: he knows the system is built on [[power|influence]] and insider dealing, yet he condemns [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] for practicing what the system rewards. His objection is not to [[bribery|corruption]] but to the disruption of an equilibrium that has served him well. He represents a world where "those who know anything concerning the [[finance|financial]] world and its great adventures know how precious is that reputation for probity, solidarity, and conservatism" (034-002) — a reputation that [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] threatens not by being less honest but by being less careful about appearances.
