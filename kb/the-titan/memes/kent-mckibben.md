# Kent McKibben

Kent McKibben is a [[chicago|Chicago]] lawyer whose social connections make him doubly valuable to [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]]: as a legal operative in the franchise-securing machinery and as a social bridge for the struggling [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] household. "Intensely pleased" (008-014) by [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]]'s approach, McKibben becomes one of the network of competent men whose careers are advanced by association with the [[finance|financier]].

## Biography

### The Recruitment

[[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] encounters McKibben while assembling the organizational infrastructure for his gas franchise campaign. His casual inquiry — "What sort of business is it you handle, Mr. McKibben?" (008-013) — is followed by the equally casual suggestion: "You [[power|might]] come and see me some time next week." In another man, McKibben "would have resented this remote suggestion of future aid. Now, instead, he was intensely pleased. The man before him gripped his imagination."

### The Franchise Campaign

McKibben becomes one of [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]]'s trusted dummy officers. "So a second company, officered by dummies of McKibben's selection, was organized" (008-017) — the lawyer's knowledge of reliable fronts complements [[general-van-sickle|Van Sickle]]'s [[politics|political]] experience and [[desoto-sippens|Sippens]]'s technical expertise. McKibben understands the legal architecture of deception that franchise acquisition requires.

### The Social Dimension

McKibben's value extends beyond the legal realm. At the [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] housewarming on [[north-shore-drive|Michigan Avenue]], "Kent McKibben, smug in his afternoon frock" (010-011), appears as one of the few genuinely connected society figures in attendance. His judgment, along with that of [[taylor-lord|Taylor Lord]], functions as a social credential: "From McKibben, who was in society, and with Lord, another 'in' standing by, this was like wine to Aileen. She beamed joyously" (010-013). For a couple struggling for social acceptance, McKibben's presence is a form of currency.

## Relationships

McKibben's defining relationship is with [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]], who recognizes his dual value — legal and social — and deploys him accordingly. His connection to [[chicago|Chicago]] society makes him a useful intermediary between the [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]] household and the world of the [[anson-merrill|Merrill]]s, though even McKibben's endorsement cannot overcome the deeper social resistance Aileen faces.

## Character Analysis

McKibben represents the class of ambitious professionals who gravitate toward power. He is not a principal — he does not initiate or command — but his competence and connections make him an essential instrument. In the novel's economy, men like McKibben are the connective tissue between the great predators and the institutions they seek to [[power|control]].
