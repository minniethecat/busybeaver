# City Hall

**City Hall** is the institutional nexus of *The Titan*'s political drama — the place where franchise applications are filed, council votes are bought, and the line between governance and commerce dissolves entirely.

## The Franchise Factory

The building houses the council chambers where applications for franchises are debated and where "each ordinance granting him a franchise" must pass through the aldermanic gauntlet. City Hall of *The Titan* is not a temple of democracy but a marketplace. Legislation is a commodity, and [[michael-mckenty|McKenty]] is its principal broker.

## McKenty's Domain

City Hall is Michael McKenty's headquarters, the physical manifestation of the Democratic machine. From its offices, he controls the votes that make [[frank-cowperwood|Cowperwood]]'s empire possible. The casual confidence of McKenty's assurances reflects his absolute command of the building's operations.

## The Aldermanic Machine

Cowperwood studied the aldermen, identified their vulnerabilities, and systematically built a network of influence. "The reporters around the City Hall and the council-chamber, who were in touch with Alderman Thomas Dowling, McKenty's leader on the floor," became his intelligence sources [026-021].

Schryhart, one of Cowperwood's rivals, relied on "certain reputable but somewhat slow-going lawyers" for his City Hall dealings. Cowperwood was more direct [026-031].

His political man reported that "the ways of the City Hall are greased straight up to the mayor and McKenty" [026-035]. The implication was clear: Cowperwood had mastered the municipal machinery.

## The Franchise Battleground

City Hall was the arbiter of the [[capital|capital]]-intensive [[franchise|franchise]] contests that defined Cowperwood's Chicago career. Gas companies needed council-approved operating rights. Street-railway franchises in [[lakeview|Lakeview]] and [[hyde-park|Hyde Park]] required aldermanic votes.

The "bucaneering forces at the City Hall" were, in Dreiser's phrase, the engines of commercial expansion [013-001]. Chicago was a Democratic city in a Republican state — a tension Cowperwood exploited with characteristic agility.
