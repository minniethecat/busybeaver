# Captain Hull

## Overview

The Pilgrim's master is a trusted servant of her owner: James W. Weldon "had for several years intrusted the command of it to Captain Hull" (DSCF-001-003).

He is "a good seaman, and also one of the most skilful harpooners of the flotilla" (DSCF-001-005), a master mariner who "knew how to disentangle himself... from among those icebergs" of the southern seas (DSCF-001-004).

## Role in the Story

When the whaling season fails, a passenger's plea diverts his course — "a request for a passage was made which he could not refuse" (DSCF-001-012) — and the young apprentice already owes him everything, for "it was, when he was a cabin-boy on board a merchant vessel, that Dick Sand was remarked by Captain Hull" (DSCF-002-029).

Sighting a derelict, he orders the rescue: "Howik," said Captain Hull... "heave to, and lower the small boat" (DSCF-003-045), and boarding it "Captain Hull discovered the bodies of five negroes" (DSCF-003-083) — the survivors he then shelters, assuring them "you have not compromised your liberty in coming on board of the American brig" (DSCF-004-024).

## Character & Traits

His humanity is deliberate; knowing the cruelties of the trade, "he was obliged to tell Mrs. Weldon that such facts, monstrous as they might be, were unhappily not rare" (DSCF-004-018).

His confidence at sea shades into fatal pride. He assures her, "More than once it has been my lot to hunt the whale with a single boat, and I have always finished by taking possession of it" (DSCF-007-005), yet prudently he "was forced to put on Dick Sand the care of guarding the 'Pilgrim'" (DSCF-007-012) before setting out.

The hunt turns disastrous: he cries that "the beast is going to take a spring and throw herself on us" (DSCF-008-076), and his last act is heroic — "he was seen for a moment hoisting the boatswain on a wreck" (DSCF-008-109) before the sea claims them all.

## Relationships

- [[Mrs. Weldon]] — his passenger, taken aboard on her own responsibility, for whose comfort he yields his own cabin.
- [[Dick Sand]] — the cabin-boy he singled out and made ship's novice, on whom command devolves at his death.
- Negoro — the cook of doubtful antecedents whom he engaged, and whose true nature he never lives to uncover.
