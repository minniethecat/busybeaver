# Snowy River

## Overview

The Snowy River is the real Australian river that forms the last great obstacle on the overland search in *In Search of the Castaways*. The travellers hope "they might camp that same night on the banks of the Snowy River, an important river which throws itself" toward the sea. (SC-041-054) As evening falls, "a white mist on the horizon marked the course of the Snowy River." (SC-041-055)

Crossing it is the final barrier between the party and their goal at Twofold Bay.

## In the Narrative

The river's crossing dominates the closing march. Ayrton warns "that the principal difficulties are not the obstacles in the road, but the Snowy River has to be crossed." (SC-042-061) The travellers anxiously ask "is this Snowy River wide?" (SC-042-064)

The distance still to cover is reckoned against it: "the courageous ladies did not shrink, if necessary, from walking the whole distance between the Snowy River and Twofold Bay." (SC-042-048) After determining their position, Paganel confirms the river's place on their route with "his customary rigor." (SC-042-042)

## Geography & Features

The Snowy River runs full and dangerous in the storm season. There is "reason to fear that if the storm lasted longer the Snowy River would overflow its banks, which would be a very unlucky" turn for the encamped party. (SC-042-001) Its threatened flood forces the searchers to halt and take stock of their exact position on the 37th parallel. (SC-042-042)

Beyond the river lies the coast: it is the last named watercourse before Twofold Bay, and the party's plans hinge on fording it before pressing on to the sea.
