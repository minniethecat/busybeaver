## Overview

Adelaide is the capital of South Australia in *[[In Search of the Castaways]]*, the chief town of its province. When Glenarvan's party is wrecked, it is noted that "the nearest port was Adelaide, the Capital of Southern Australia" (SC-032-004), the city standing as the first refuge on that stretch of coast.

It ranks among the great cities of the continent. The wandering natives, Verne writes, "often go to the large towns, such as Adelaide, Sydney and Melbourne" (SC-038-034), the port set beside [[Sydney]] and [[Melbourne]] as one of the three chief Australian cities.

## In the Narrative

Adelaide is a place of repair and refuge for the shipwrecked. The damage to the vessel is judged "so serious as to require more skilful workmen than could be found in Adelaide" (SC-032-005), the city's resources measured against the wreck's needs.

It is also a landfall for the poor and hopeful. One settler "came with his family to Australia, landed at Adelaide, where, refusing employment as a miner, he got engaged on a farm" (SC-032-037), the port serving as the gateway through which new arrivals entered the southern colony.

## Geography & Features

Adelaide gives its name to the whole southern province crossed by the expedition. The geographer reckons his route through "a narrow portion of the province of Adelaide, with the whole of Victoria" (SC-035-002), the city's name extended over the surrounding district.

Yet the province itself is a monotonous march. It is recorded plainly that "the march across Adelaide presented nothing of any particular interest" (SC-035-007), and that the desert coast has "no communication between it and Melbourne or Adelaide" (SC-030-023), the city remaining a distant goal across empty country.
