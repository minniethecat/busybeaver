## Overview

Utah is the Mormon territory of the American West, crossed by the Pacific Railroad in [[Around the World in Eighty Days]]. As Phileas Fogg's train pressed westward it "penetrated Utah, the region of the Great Salt Lake, the singular colony of the Mormons" (AWED-026-026), a land apart within the young Union.

## In the Narrative

Utah is the stage for one of the voyage's strangest episodes — a Mormon Elder's sermon delivered in the railway car. The Union, Verne recounts, "had made itself master of Utah, and subjected that territory to the laws of the Union, after imprisoning Brigham Young on a charge of rebellion and polygamy" (AWED-027-009).

The Elder demands why "the soldiers of the Union invaded the soil of Utah" (AWED-027-015), and Passepartout learns of its curious customs, for "it is mainly the female citizens of Utah who are anxious to marry" (AWED-027-021). Beyond Fogg's crossing, Utah is counted among the western homelands, its people ranked with "the States of Utah, Oregon, and California" (GM-001-004).

## Geography & Features

Utah is entered from the west through mountain gates — the train "passed through the defiles of the Humboldt Range" before reaching it (AWED-026-026), the region defined by its great inland sea, the Great Salt Lake. It is a broad territory of ridges and desert basins.

From the air it reads the same. In [[Robur the Conqueror]] the [[Albatross]] would "sink again over Oregon or Utah" (RC-010-024), and she was seen "steering southwest so as to cut across Utah diagonally as she neared the ground" (RC-010-026).

## Connections

Utah is the Mormon territory that [[Phileas Fogg]] crosses by rail in [[Around the World in Eighty Days]], the region of the [[Great Salt Lake]] and its capital [[Salt Lake City]]. Its desert diagonals are later overflown by the [[Albatross]] in [[Robur the Conqueror]]. A theocratic colony brought under Union law, it stands among the singular landscapes of the American West.
