# Faroe Islands

## Overview

The Faroe Islands are the real North-Atlantic archipelago off the Norwegian coast, and the site of the wreck that opens *The Waif of the Cynthia*. The lost vessel went down "with her cargo and all on board, just fourteen years ago, in the neighborhood of the Faroe Islands." (WC-006-042)

## In the Narrative

The wreck is the mystery from which the foundling's story unspools: "I have proved to you that the vessel was an American one, and that she was lost off the Faroe Islands, that is to say, near the coast of Norway." (WC-006-052)

The date is fixed precisely: "On the 7th of October, 1858, the 'Cynthia' was shipwrecked off the Faroe Islands." (WC-021-017)

## Geography & Features

The islands lie within reach of an open boat from the disaster: "In forty-nine hours this boat reached one of the Faroe Islands." (WC-021-018)

They sit on the searchers' northern track — "I, however, sought for him among the Faroe and Shetland Islands, and upon the Norwegian coast north of Bergen." (WC-021-019)
