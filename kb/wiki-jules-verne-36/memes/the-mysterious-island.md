## Overview

*The Mysterious Island* is Jules Verne's great robinsonade, in which five prisoners of the American Civil War escape by balloon and are marooned on an uncharted Pacific island they name [[Lincoln Island]]. Led by the engineer [[Cyrus Harding]], the castaways rebuild civilization from raw nature, aided at intervals by an unseen benefactor who is finally revealed as [[Captain Nemo]].

The novel serves as a sequel that ties together the worlds of *[[Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea]]* and *[[in-search-of-the-castaways|In Search of the Castaways]]*.

## Plot Summary

The story opens amid a storm as the balloon plunges toward the sea — "For Heaven's sake heave out the ballast!" (MI-001-001) — casting the five survivors onto the island.

Under Harding's direction they smelt iron, fire pottery, grow crops, and carve out the cliff dwelling of [[Granite House]], whose upper entrance is later served by the [[Granite House Lift]]. Even so, "The Chimneys would have been quite insufficient to protect them against the rigor of winter" (MI-020-001), driving the colony to ever greater engineering.

Mysterious interventions repeatedly save the colonists, and the reclusive [[Captain Nemo]] is at last discovered in a sea cavern beneath the island; his passing is recorded in [[The Death of Captain Nemo]].

The island is destroyed in a volcanic cataclysm that leaves only "An isolated rock, thirty feet in length" (MI-062-001) where [[Mount Franklin]] once stood, "rent asunder by the explosion" (MI-062-002). Full chapter texts run from [[MI-ch01]] to [[MI-ch62]].

## Main Characters

- [[Cyrus Harding]] — the engineer who leads the colony
- [[Captain Nemo]] — the island's hidden benefactor
- [[Top]] — the faithful dog whose instincts repeatedly warn the colonists

## Key Places & Technology

- [[Lincoln Island]] — the castaways' uncharted home
- [[Granite House]] — the cliff dwelling that becomes their fortress
- [[Mount Franklin]] — the island's volcano, whose eruption ends the story
- [[Nitroglycerine]] — explosive Harding manufactures from the island's minerals
- [[The Tabor Island Castaway|Tabor Island]] — the neighboring islet holding a lost castaway

## Themes

The novel is a hymn to human ingenuity: knowledge and cooperation turn a barren island into a thriving colony. Against that optimism runs the counter-current of dependence — the colonists owe their survival to a hidden hand — and of nature's ultimate power, which erases every work of their hands in a single eruption.
