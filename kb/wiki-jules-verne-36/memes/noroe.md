## Overview

Noroe is the little Norwegian village at the opening of The Waif of the Cynthia and the home of its foundling hero. The story begins in a primary school in Noroe, on the western coast of Norway, a few leagues from Bergen. (WC-001-005) It is a quiet fishing hamlet on a fiord, far from the mysteries of Erik's birth.

## In the Narrative

The village is bound up with Dr. Schwaryencrona's enterprise. One of the principal manufactories of the doctor was at Noroe, and its cod-liver-oil bottles stand always before the schoolboys' eyes. (WC-001-008) The tale keeps returning to it — but perhaps it is time for us to return to Noroe, and the doctor whom we have left on the threshold of the school. (WC-001-013)

Its houses ring the water. In a few seconds the boys were scattered around the blue waters of the fiord, where might be seen the turf roofs of the village of Noroe. (WC-001-077) The dwellings are built in the old fashion — the house of Mr. Hersebom was, like all others in Noroe, covered by a turf roof and built of enormous fir timbers. (WC-002-002)

## Geography & Features

A small fishing village on a fiord of Norway's western coast a few leagues from Bergen, Noroe is a place of turf-roofed timber houses and a doctor's oil-works — the humble starting point from which Erik's search for his origins unfolds.
