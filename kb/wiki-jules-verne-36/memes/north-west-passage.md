## Overview

The North-West Passage is the famous Arctic sea route that gives direct communication by sea between the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans. (FC-010-020) Long sought and long doubted, it was spoken of with awe by the seafaring men of the northern latitudes as the famous North-West Passage. (FC-010-012)

## In the Narrative

To the explorers of the polar seas the discovery of the route was the crown of every effort. May it not be to find the North-West Passage, men asked, that such perilous voyages are undertaken. (ACH-046-057) One account of these attempts bore the very title The North-West Passage, or the End of the Voyage. (ACH-042-052)

A commander might declare, with the passage nearly won, that he was only trying to clear the North-West Passage, and that was all. (ACH-049-041)

## Geography & Features

The passage threads the dangerous latitudes of the far north, and for long it was maintained that it remained undiscovered, since not a single ship had ever sailed through from Behring Straits to Baffin Bay. (ACH-047-011) The zone was reckoned so hazardous that its currents were only imperfectly known, and it has been called the North-West Passage since McClure daring discovery. (FC-025-024)

## Connections

The route drew the great search expeditions northward. The Investigator arrived first in [[Behring Strait]] and set out with the declared purpose to find Franklin or the North-West Passage. (ACH-047-025)

It was McClure who at last recorded his intention of returning to England by the North-West Passage he had discovered, by way of [[Lancaster Strait]] and Baffin Bay, opening the sea road that others had died seeking across the [[Arctic Ocean]]. (ACH-047-029)
