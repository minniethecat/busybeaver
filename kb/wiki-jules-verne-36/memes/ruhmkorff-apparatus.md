# Ruhmkorff Apparatus

## Overview

The Ruhmkorff apparatus is the portable electric lamp the explorers carry on their descent in *[[journey-to-the-center-of-the-earth|A Journey to the Centre of the Earth]]*. Among the party's equipment are "two of Ruhmkorff's apparatus, which, by means of an electric current, supplied a safe and handy portable light" (JCE-011-029).

Named for the real induction-coil maker Heinrich Ruhmkorff, the device lets [[Professor Lidenbrock]] and his companions illuminate passages where an open flame would be deadly.

## Design & Operation

Each lamp is worn on the body and powered by a coil-driven lantern: Lidenbrock "took in one hand Ruhmkorff's apparatus, which was hanging from his neck; and with the other he formed an electric communication with the coil in the lantern, and a sufficiently bright light dispersed the darkness" (JCE-018-027).

Its great advantage is safety in foul air. The narrator calls it an "ingenious application of electricity" able to "creat[e] an artificial light even in the midst of the most inflammable gases" (JCE-018-028).

## Role in the Story

Underground the lamps reveal the caverns in brilliant detail: in one lava tunnel "the electric light was here intensified a hundredfold by reflection" (JCE-018-032). The apparatus proves literally life-saving in a gallery filled with firedamp.

There the narrator reflects: "Happily, our light was from Ruhmkorff's ingenious apparatus. If unfortunately we had explored this gallery with torches, a terrible explosion would have put an end to travelling and travellers at one stroke" (JCE-020-030).

## Science & Speculation

The lamp is one of Verne's carefully chosen real technologies rather than an invented one: the Ruhmkorff coil was a genuine nineteenth-century instrument, and Verne turns it into a plausible safety lamp for deep exploration. Its dramatic value lies precisely in the contrast the narrator draws with torches — electricity as the enabling condition of the whole journey (JCE-018-028).
