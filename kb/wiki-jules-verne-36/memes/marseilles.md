## Overview

Marseilles is the Mediterranean port at which Verne's returning travellers make landfall on French soil. (JCE-045-004) It stands so far south that even in a great freeze the sea was frozen only exceptionally at Venice and Marseilles. (ACH-041-041)

## In the Narrative

After the descent and return, the party embarked on the steamer Volturno and in three days reached Marseilles. (JCE-045-004) In another tale, fugitives brought under false names by the steamer Morris landed at Marseilles before taking the train to Paris. (TT-019-057) Stanley, too, landed at Marseilles on the twelfth of July, 1872. (DSCF-032-075)

## Geography & Features

The port lends its name to the fisheries of the coast, where valuable fish leap into the Marseilles tuna net of the Provençal fishermen. (TTLU-031-010) It is the city of naturalists such as Peysonnel of Marseilles, who fixed coral's place in the animal kingdom. (TTLU-024-049)

## Connections

Marseilles opens onto the [[Mediterranean]], the sea whose Provençal shore it commands. (TTLU-031-010) It is the French gateway that closes the return voyage of [[A Journey to the Center of the Earth]]. (JCE-045-004)
