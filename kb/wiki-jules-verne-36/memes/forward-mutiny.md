# The Forward Mutiny

> **Note:** Inline PN citations for this page use a 4-character work code (`ACH`); they render as plain text pending RFC-0003. The underlying PN data is valid.

## Overview

The Forward Mutiny is the crew's revolt against [[captain-hatteras|Captain Hatteras]] in *[[the-adventures-of-captain-hatteras|The Adventures of Captain Hatteras]]*, the betrayal that strands its leader in the Arctic and ends in the destruction of his ship.

As the boatswain Johnson later recounts, almost immediately after the captain's departure Shandon, supported by the others, took the command of the ship (ACH-034-016).

## What Happens

The seeds of the revolt are sown long before. The captain could count on the Doctor, Johnson, Bell, and Simpson, devoted to him body and soul, while a body of undecided men might turn against the captain (ACH-021-027). Driven to the Frozen Pole, Hatteras faces a terrible winter on a ship lost in the midst of the ice with a crew nearly in revolt (ACH-024-002).

When Hatteras leaves the [[the-forward|Forward]] to press north, the mutineers seize their chance. Shandon made no attempt to restrain them; it was his policy to make them believe that their privations and toils were at an end (ACH-034-017), and the abandoned brig was given to the flames.

For two days the [[the-forward|Forward]] struggled with the flames (ACH-034-021), until of the brig that had been so carefully built and had become so dear, not a vestige remained (ACH-033-007).

## Significance

The mutiny is the pivot on which the whole polar quest turns, converting a well-found expedition into a fight for bare survival on the ice. Long afterward the loss still haunts its captain, who dreamt of his ship, the [[the-forward|Forward]], and of the traitors that had burnt it (ACH-054-029).

Even the land conspires to erase the crime: a white sheet of snow three feet thick soon lay over the scene of the explosion, completely obliterating all traces of the [[the-forward|Forward]] (ACH-034-005).

## Participants & Setting

- The revolt and burning of the [[The Forward]] against [[Captain Hatteras]].
- Led by the second-in-command Shandon and recounted by the boatswain Johnson.
