# The Christiania Lottery Drawing

## Overview

The Christiania Lottery Drawing is the climax of *[[ticket-no-9672|Ticket No. 9672]]*—as the great Norwegian lottery is drawn, ticket 9672 wins the grand prize, and Ole Kamp, believed lost with the *Viking*, appears alive to claim it for his betrothed Hulda.

The hall was packed. What a crowd filled the large hall of the University of Christiania in which the drawing of the great lottery was to take place (TN-019-001).

## What Happens

The grand prize came last. Contrary to custom, the drawing of the grand prize was reserved for the last, the hundredth ticket, so that emotion mounted with ever-increasing violence (TN-019-004).

The first figure was nine. "Nine," said the president, stating the number the third little girl had extracted from the urn—the first figure of Ole Kamp's ticket (TN-019-049).

The odds narrowed sharply. The winning number must now lie between 9670 and 9679, leaving one chance in ten for Ole Kamp's ticket to win the prize (TN-019-059).

Then the number was called. "Nine thousand six hundred and seventy-two!" proclaimed one of the directors in a loud voice (TN-019-064).

## Significance

The winner's ticket had been sold. This was the number of Ole Kamp's ticket, now in the usurer Sandgoist's possession, and a profound silence fell instead of applause (TN-019-065).

The prize awaited a claimant. "Number 9672 wins the prize of one hundred thousand marks! Who claims it?" repeated the director (TN-019-067).

The lost sailor returned. A pale young man, bearing the marks of long suffering but truly alive, forced his way through the crowd to answer—it was Ole Kamp (TN-019-073).

## Participants & Setting

- The drawing fills the University of Christiania on 15 July, where one hundred prizes are drawn, the grand prize of one hundred thousand marks reserved for the very last ticket.
- Ole Kamp's ticket 9672, which he had bequeathed to his betrothed Hulda Hansen before the *Viking* was wrecked, had passed into the hands of the usurer Sandgoist of Drammen.
- Professor Sylvius Hogg stands with Hulda as the number is called, and Ole Kamp himself—survivor of the shipwreck—appears to claim the prize; chronicled in *[[ticket-no-9672|Ticket No. 9672]]*.
