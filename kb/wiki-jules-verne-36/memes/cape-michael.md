# Cape Michael

## Overview

Cape Michael is a fictional headland on the drifting peninsula of Cape Bathurst in *The Fur Country*. Named by Hobson's colonists, it marks the eastern limit of their territory: the coast "stretched away without a break for seven miles to the cape they had named Cape Michael." (FC-027-007)

It lies opposite the fort's harbour, for "From Cape Michael to the former Port Barnett the coast ran in an almost straight line from south to north for about eleven miles." (FC-027-051)

## In the Narrative

The cape is the goal of a secret reconnaissance when Hobson suspects the land is adrift. He is "determined to go to Cape Michael, however terrible the storm might be" (FC-029-029), cutting "across in a straight line from Cape Bathurst to Cape Michael" with a pocket compass. (FC-030-015)

From its shore Hobson and Long keep their lonely watch. It is at the cape that they glimpse a distant fire — "the fire which Hobson and Long had seen when crouching on Cape Michael on the night of the 31st August." (FC-032-034)

## Geography & Features

The cape is the point at which the colonists eventually abandon the doomed island. They wend "their way towards Cape Michael, where they would quit the island, properly so called, for the ice-field" (FC-036-004), reaching it "after having rounded the gulf which the cold had imperfectly frozen over." (FC-036-007)

Survey parties patrol the shore between it and the old bay, going "every day from Cape Michael to what was once the corner of the old Walruses' Bay." (FC-035-059)
