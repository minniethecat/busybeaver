## Overview

Callao is the Pacific seaport of Lima, on the coast of Peru. In the Vernean corpus it appears in two works: as the port of the Peruvian capital in the short story *The Pearl of Lima*, and as the last recorded harbour of the lost ship *Britannia* in *In Search of the Castaways*.

## In the Narrative

In *The Pearl of Lima*, Callao is the harbour toward which the heroine rides: "Once in the saddle, Sarah left the city as usual, and began to ride through the country; she directed her way toward Callao." (PL-003-062) Its waters are troubled by a mysterious schooner: "The schooner *Annonciation* has appeared in sight from Callao, tacked for a few moments, then, protected by the point, rapidly disappeared." (PL-002-036) Some hold that this vessel, "laden with Colombian troops, was seeking to seize the principal vessels of Callao." (PL-003-063)

## Geography & Features

Callao is the seaward gate of Lima: "Lima, situated three miles from the sea, was formerly the principal storehouse of America on the Pacific Ocean, thanks to its Port of Callao, built in 1779, in a singular manner." (PL-002-006) In *In Search of the Castaways* it is a provisioning harbour on the route home to Europe: "After reconnoitering Papua, the *Britannia* went to provision herself at Callao, and left that port on the 30th of May, 1862, to return to Europe by the Indian Ocean and the Cape." (SC-033-042)

## Connections

Callao is bound to *In Search of the Castaways* as the last known position of Captain Grant's ship: "The *Britannia* left Callao on the 30th of May, and on the 7th of June, a week afterward, she is lost on the coast of Patagonia." (SC-002-075) It was from Callao that the final letters came, for "no tidings whatever had come since his departure from Callao, in June." (SC-004-005) The mystery of the wreck turns on this port, since "on leaving Callao the *Britannia* went to reconnoiter New Zealand." (SC-063-067)
