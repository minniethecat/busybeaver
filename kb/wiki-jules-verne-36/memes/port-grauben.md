# Port Gräuben

## Overview

Port Gräuben is the little harbour on the shore of the vast subterranean Liedenbrock Sea, named by Professor Liedenbrock at the outset of the raft voyage in *Journey to the Centre of the Earth*. He christens it after his goddaughter: "'Let it be called Port Gräuben; it will look very well upon the map.'" (JCE-032-005) His nephew assents — "'Port Gräuben let it be then.'" (JCE-032-006)

## In the Narrative

The port is the fixed origin from which the underground travellers reckon all their progress across the buried ocean. Axel keeps the log "since our departure from Port Gräuben." (JCE-032-017) At sea he tallies the distance run: "We have crossed two hundred and seventy leagues of sea since leaving Port Gräuben; and we are six hundred and twenty leagues from Iceland." (JCE-034-050)

When storm and current fling the raft back to land, the shore proves unfamiliar — "this shore is south-east of Port Grauben" (JCE-036-059) — showing how far the sea has carried them from the named creek.

## Geography & Features

The port lies on a beach fringed by the giant fungi and petrified forests of the underground world: beyond it stretches "a vast forest, but not one of those forests of fungi which bordered Port Gräuben." (JCE-039-004) It is the one charted name on an otherwise blank map of the Liedenbrock Sea.
