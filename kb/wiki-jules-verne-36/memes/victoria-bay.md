# Victoria Bay

## Overview

Victoria Bay is a fictional inlet of New America, the polar land discovered by Captain Hatteras's expedition in *The Adventures of Captain Hatteras*. It is one of the features the travelers christen after claiming the coast: with the continent itself named New America, the doctor proposes "there will be nothing to prevent us calling this bay Victoria Bay." (ACH-039-058)

The name fixes the bay on the party's map of their new domain.

## In the Narrative

The bay becomes a fixed point of the expedition's overland routes. The doctor lays out the plan of travel: "we shall go by Cape Washington to Victoria Bay, and from thence to Fort Providence, where we shall find food and rest at Doctor's House!" (ACH-039-075)

Later journeys are measured against it: the men "had gone over two hundred miles from Victoria Bay to Altamont Harbour, and were in latitude 87° 5' and longitude 118° 35'." (ACH-052-058)

## Geography & Features

The bay lies on the coast of New America between Cape Washington and the settlement of Fort Providence, a natural anchorage on the party's circuit. When at last they choose to return by sea rather than sledge, they "determined to go round to Victoria Bay by water, instead of crossing New America." (ACH-057-016)

The voyage proves swift — "in a week they accomplished what had taken a fortnight in the sledge, and on the 23rd they cast anchor in Victoria Bay." (ACH-057-017)
