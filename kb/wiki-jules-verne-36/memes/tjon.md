## Overview

The Tjon is a valley in China, so deep and wide as to require a viaduct from three hundred and fifty to four hundred feet long. (ASC-025-003) Its unfinished viaduct, on a branch of the Grand Transasiatic, is the scene of the story's climax, where a band of raiders plot to hurl the treasure-train into the abyss. (ASC-024-062)

## In the Narrative

The Nanking branch is completed for only five or six kilometres, and leads to the viaduct over the Tjon valley. (ASC-024-029) Faruskiar, the bandit chief, sends the train down this unfinished line: the rascal who would have hurled the travellers into the Tjon valley means to walk off with the imperial treasure. (ASC-025-044)

The scoundrels hasten down into the Tjon valley, sure the train must follow. (ASC-025-010) Prudence tells the defenders the bandits cannot be far from the viaduct. (ASC-025-067) In the end the party is saved, for had one man not been aboard, all would have been engulfed in the abyss of the Tjon. (ASC-027-005)

## Geography & Features

Two hundred yards from the brink, the valley opens deep and wide beneath the half-built span. (ASC-025-003) A network of scaffolding appears confusedly in the darkness, above the piers meant to carry the viaduct across the Tjon. (ASC-024-096) At five kilometres' distance the incomplete viaduct waits, toward which the train is precipitated. (ASC-024-062)
