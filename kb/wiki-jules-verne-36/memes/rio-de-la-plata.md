## Overview

The Rio de la Plata is the wide estuary on the east coast of [[South America]], the River Plate that lends its name to a whole region of provinces. The [[Nautilus]] passed the wide estuary formed by the mouth of the Rio de la Plata, and on April 4 lay abreast of Uruguay, fifty miles out. (TTLU-041-040)

## In the Narrative

In [[Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea]] the Plata bounds a spreading blight of the sea. The toxic vegetation of the Torrid Zone spreads unchecked from the mouth of the Rio de la Plata to Florida. (TTLU-041-063) The Plata is named among the great rivers, with the [[Amazon]], the Orinoco, and the Elbe. (TTLU-032-002)

## Geography & Features

The provinces of La Plata name the land behind the estuary. When South America (Peru, Chili, Brazil, the provinces of La Plata, and Columbia) poured in its quota, the sum was a considerable capital. (FEM-012-029) Lincoln Island lay under the same latitude as the provinces of La Plata, where tigers are met with. (MI-022-036)

## Connections

The Rio de la Plata drains the country behind the coast of [[Brazil]] and the plains named for it. (FEM-012-029) It is the estuary the [[Nautilus]] passed off the eastern shore of [[South America]]. (TTLU-041-040)
