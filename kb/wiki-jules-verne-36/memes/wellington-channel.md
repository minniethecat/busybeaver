# Wellington Channel

## Overview

Wellington Channel is the real Arctic passage running north between North Devon and Cornwallis Island, a route the *Forward* weighs on its search for the open polar sea. "Wellington Channel is not very wide; it is bounded by North Devon on the east and Cornwallis Island on the west." (ACH-021-002)

It is bound up with the legend of a sea free of ice at the top of the world.

## In the Narrative

The channel offers one path north: "once at Beechey Island we will go north as far as possible, by Wellington Channel, up to the outlet of the creek which joins Wellington's and Queen's Channels, at the very point where the open sea was" reported. (ACH-013-036)

Hatteras hurries toward it — he "wanted to run along the north coast, in order to reach Beechey Island and the entrance to Wellington Channel sooner." (ACH-014-006) Yet he may bypass it: "we will try to reach Griffith Island, sail round Cornwallis Island, and get into Queen's Channel without going by Wellington Channel." (ACH-014-018)

## Geography & Features

The channel is a hub of Arctic seaways — the brig sits "in the centre of this four-road way... fenced by the outlets of Lancaster Strait, Barrow Strait, Wellington Channel, and Regent's Passage." (ACH-014-010)

It carries a history of exploration: Captain Parry, "on board the *Assistance* and the *Resolute*, explored Wellington Channel." (ACH-014-027) During "Parry's station on the border of Wellington Channel, in 1851, his lieutenant, Stewart, also found himself in the presence of open sea." (ACH-013-019)
