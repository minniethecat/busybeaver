# Aberfoyle

## Overview

Aberfoyle is the Scottish coal-mining district at the heart of *The Underground City*. Its mines border the Alloa mines and occupy part of the county of Stirling, and it is there, in their mysterious depths, that the novel's events unfold. (UC-001-006) The exhaustion of the old Aberfoyle pits sets the entire plot in motion.

## In the Narrative

James Starr had for twenty years been the manager, or "viewer," of the Aberfoyle mines, working alongside the former foreman Simon Ford. (UC-001-006) When the story opens, that long labor has ended: "The Aberfoyle mines, which for so many years have united us in a common work, are now exhausted." (UC-001-014)

All researches had failed to find a fresh vein, and the last block of coal had just been extracted from the Dochart pit. (UC-001-014) Starr is then summoned back by a mysterious letter bearing the Aberfoyle postmark, promising a communication of an interesting nature at the Dochart pit's Yarrow shaft. (UC-001-002)

That summons leads underground to the discovery of a vast new coalfield, [[New Aberfoyle]], reviving the industry that the old pits' exhaustion had seemed to end forever.

## Geography & Features

The mines of Aberfoyle lie in the county of Stirling, adjoining the Alloa coal beds, their galleries sunk deep beneath the Scottish soil. (UC-001-006) Above ground, only the sheds that once sheltered the outside works still marked the spot where each abandoned shaft had been sunk. (UC-001-010)

By the time of the story, the worked-out mine resembled "the body of a huge fantastically-shaped mastodon, from which all the organs of life have been taken, and only the skeleton remains." (UC-001-009) Many mining families had lived for generations in old Aberfoyle and were driven, at its closing, to seek subsistence elsewhere.
