## Overview

Holland is one of the six nations whose rights to the Arctic were held as absolutely established: America, England, Russia, Denmark, Sweden-Norway, and Holland. (TT-001-058) It stands among the powers present to make their bids against the United States before the Commissioner of Baltimore. (TT-002-002)

## In the Narrative

The kingdom is embodied at the auction by a single delegate: Jacques Jansen, formerly Counsellor of the Netherlandish India, a worthy man only a little incredulous. (TT-002-013) So completely does he stand for his country that Holland seemed to speak all alone through his voice. (TT-019-024)

At the bidding table his verdict is curt — "to Holland," said Jacques Jansen, when the delegates named their allegiances. (TT-002-030)

## Geography & Features

Holland's claim to the pole rests on its navigators: her sailors Barentz and Heemskerk visited the Spitsbergen about the end of the sixteenth century. (TT-002-005)

Its purse, however, is modest. Holland could dispose of only fifty riechsthaler for the purpose of the Arctic regions (TT-002-045), yet the lift given by Holland to the sale still started a murmur of satisfaction. (TT-003-025) It is urged to join a syndicate of Sweden, Norway, Russia, Denmark, Holland, and England against America's bids. (TT-002-031)
