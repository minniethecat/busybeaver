# The Assault on Irkutsk

## Overview

The assault on Irkutsk is the fiery climax of *[[Michael Strogoff]]*, in which the traitor Ivan Ogareff, allied with the Tartar Emir, springs his long-plotted betrayal of the besieged Siberian capital. So that his treachery might pass unremarked, Ogareff had planned to produce a catastrophe so frightful that terror would inevitably overwhelm the hearts of the besieged (MS-033-006).

## What Happens

The whole scheme turned on the Bolchaia Gate. Ogareff arranged that this gate should be unguarded or only feebly held, while the attention of the besieged was drawn to another part of the town (MS-033-001).

A false attack masked the real one. A diversion was agreed upon with the Emir—assaults in earnest up and down the river, with a feigned attempt at crossing the Angara from the left bank (MS-033-002).

At two in the morning Ogareff gave the signal. From the terrace of the palace he struck a match, lit a small bunch of tow steeped in priming powder, and threw it into the Angara (MS-033-021).

The river had been made ready to burn. By Ogareff's orders torrents of mineral oil—naphtha drawn from the great springs above Irkutsk—had already been loosed upon the surface of the Angara (MS-033-022).

In an instant the whole river blazed. As if the current had been alcohol, flame ran above and below the town in columns of blue fire, with volumes of vapor curling overhead (MS-033-024).

## Significance

The double blow fell at once. Firing broke out to the north and south, several thousand Tartars rushed the earth-works, and the wooden houses along the bank caught fire in every direction (MS-033-025).

The defenders were trapped between two terrors. Caught between the Tartar attack and the fearful effects of the fire, the population ran, some to the points attacked and others to the burning houses, as the alarm-bells rang (MS-033-027).

Meanwhile the true objective lay open: the Gate of Bolchaia was nearly free, guarded only by a very small force, exactly as the traitor had contrived (MS-033-028).

## Participants & Setting

- Ivan Ogareff, posing as the Czar's courier Michael Strogoff within the palace, engineers the whole betrayal—the feigned diversion, the burning river, and the abandoned gate—waging war against his own countrymen as an ally of the Tartars.
- The Grand Duke and his officers, warned by Ogareff himself to concentrate their defense on the Angara bank, unwittingly strip the Bolchaia Gate of its garrison and are caught wholly unprepared for the fire.
- The setting is the city of [[Irkutsk]] upon the frozen Angara, whose enormous naphtha reservoirs above the town were breached so that the combustible liquid rushed out in torrents and spread over the river (MS-033-023).
