## Overview

Turkestan is the Central Asian region through which the Grand Transasiatic Railway runs in *The Adventures of a Special Correspondent* — by the time of the journey it is merely a province of Asiatic Russia, extending to the frontiers of the Chinese Empire. (ASC-005-004)

## In the Narrative

It is the reporter's gateway to the East. The Transcaspian, now open for passengers and goods, is the chief commercial route between Europe and Turkestan. (ASC-003-042)

The railway hands its travellers across empires: Russians will take them up to the frontier of Turkestan, and Chinese will take them on through China. (ASC-005-019) At the outset the narrator has no fear of remaining dumb in Turkestan and the Celestial Empire. (ASC-001-036)

## Geography & Features

Its steppes set the pace of the crossing — the flat steppes of Kara Koum, which the Grand Transasiatic traverses in Turkestan. (ASC-002-038)

The line binds its towns: the principal towns and villages of Russian Turkestan have been reached by the Transcaspian Railway for some years, and up to the Chinese frontier it runs over 1,360 miles. (ASC-005-013)
