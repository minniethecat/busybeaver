## Overview

The Elbe is the north-German river on whose banks [[Hamburg]] stands. Its swift steamers form the communication between the city and the Hamburg railway, landing their passengers on the banks of the Elbe. (JCE-007-002)

## In the Narrative

In [[A Journey to the Center of the Earth]] the Elbe frames Axel's home walks. He reached the banks of the Elbe, and after bidding good-bye to the swan sailing among the white water lilies, returned to the quay by the steamer. (JCE-003-042) On another day he had followed the banks of the Elbe and passed the town. (JCE-007-008)

## Geography & Features

Axel, accustomed to the swift and sure steamers on the Elbe, found the oars of the rowers a slow means of propulsion. (JCE-012-041) The Elbe is counted among the great rivers of the world, named with the Loire and the Rhine as bringing their waters to the ocean from the most civilized countries. (TTLU-032-002)

## Connections

The Elbe joins [[Hamburg]] to its railway and, through it, to the road that opens [[A Journey to the Center of the Earth]]. (JCE-007-002) It stands in the roll of rivers that feed the sea, beside the Loire and the Rhine. (TTLU-032-002)
