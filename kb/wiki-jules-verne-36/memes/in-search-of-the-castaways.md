## Overview

*In Search of the Castaways* opens aboard the yacht *Duncan*, owned by Lord Glenarvan, "one of the sixteen Scotch peers who sit in the Upper House, and the most distinguished member of the Royal Thames Yacht Club" (SC-001-001). Sailing off the Firth of Clyde, his crew captures a shark and finds a bottle lodged in its stomach (SC-001-003, SC-001-017).

Glenarvan breaks open the bottle, knowing that "bottles found in the sea often contain precious documents" (SC-001-021).

## Plot Summary

The recovered papers form a shipwreck message, half-destroyed, yielding the fragments SINK, ALAND, LOST, and SKIPP — "evidently part of the word SKIPPER" (SC-002-011). One figure survives intact: the castaways lie "in longitude and latitude 37 degrees 11'" (SC-002-079).

The message points to Captain Harry Grant, lost at sea, whose orphaned children Mary and Robert have devoted themselves to finding their father (SC-004-003, SC-004-007). When the Admiralty refuses to act, Glenarvan takes up the quest himself.

The *Duncan* sets out to follow the 37th parallel around the globe. Aboard by accident is the geographer [[Jacques Paganel]], Secretary of the Geographical Society of Paris, who had boarded the wrong ship and now joins the search (SC-006-075, SC-007-007). The pursuit carries them into Patagonia and on around the world.

## Main Characters

- Lord Glenarvan — the Scottish peer who leads the rescue expedition
- [[Jacques Paganel]] — the absent-minded French geographer swept along on the voyage
- Mary Grant — Captain Grant's devoted daughter, "only fourteen" when orphaned
- Major McNabbs — Glenarvan's steady cousin and companion aboard the *Duncan*

## Key Places & Technology

- The *Duncan* — Lord Glenarvan's newly built steam yacht from the Clyde
- Patagonia — the first landfall in the search along the 37th parallel

## Themes

The novel is a global treasure-hunt built on a single fragile clue, dramatizing Verne's faith in geography as the key to the world. It celebrates loyalty — the children's devotion, Glenarvan's chivalry — and turns the earth's whole 37th parallel into a thread to be followed to its end.
