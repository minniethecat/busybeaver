# Walruses' Bay

## Overview

Walruses' Bay is a fictional Arctic inlet named by Lieutenant Hobson's surveying party in *The Fur Country*, one of the features they christen while mapping the coast near Cape Bathurst. Following the curving shore, "to which they gave the name of 'Walruses' Bay,' the party soon reached the foot of the cliff." (FC-015-016)

The bay becomes a fixed waypoint of the little colony's excursions.

## In the Narrative

The bay lies within the range of the settlers' regular marches — a hunting party sets out "Marbre, and Sabine, to Walruses' Bay." (FC-016-025) It marks the horizon when the land begins to burn: "above the western cliffs beyond Walruses' Bay the horizon was on fire." (FC-020-012)

Later travelers reckon their bearings by it, though the ground has grown treacherous: they "lost their way, and turned to the south when they thought they were near to Walruses' Bay." (FC-022-048)

## Geography & Features

The bay is backed by a volcanic country of convulsed cliffs. Behind it, "from 'Walruses' Bay' to the mountains... the country had undergone terrible convulsions, showing clearly that it owed its origin to volcanic eruptions." (FC-015-017)

The upheavals continue to reshape it: an excursion "as far as Walruses' Bay" finds "that the earthquake had considerably altered the form of the cliffs, which were much depressed." (FC-022-032) Weary parties still hope "to pass the few short hours of the night at Walruses' Bay." (FC-026-026)
