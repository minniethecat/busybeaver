## Overview

Lyons is the great French silk-city, its looms a byword for quality across Verne's world. In the bazaars of the East its silks are the measure: the fabrics of Teheran and Shiraz are not worth as much as those of Lyons. (ASC-001-043) Even Samarkand's prized Kanaous is thought not to excel the product of Lyons manufacture. (ASC-012-033)

## In the Narrative

Lyons silk gives the balloon its very skin. The factories of Lyons received a heavy order for the silk required for the body of the balloon. (FWB-002-012) The balloons were made of a strong but light Lyons silk, coated with gutta percha. (FWB-007-013) From Nijni's fair came velvets and silks from Lyons among the wares of two worlds. (MS-006-037)

## Geography & Features

Lyons holds a place in the annals of the air. A like mishap befell the Counts of Laurencin and Dampierre when they ascended at Lyons on the 15th of January, 1784. (DA-001-035) At Lyons the Comtes de Laurencin and de Dampierre left the traces of their glory in the air. (VB-001-085) The pioneers of ballooning are numbered there among the brave. (DA-001-090)

## Connections

Lyons clothes the balloon that crosses Africa in [[Five Weeks in a Balloon]]. (FWB-007-013) Its silks travel the great fair described in [[Michael Strogoff]]. (MS-006-037)
