## Overview

*The Blockade Runners* is Jules Verne's short adventure of the American Civil War, opening on the Clyde, "the first river whose waters were lashed into foam by a steam-boat" (BR-001-002). At Glasgow's Kelvin Dock a crowd gathers to watch the launch of a swift new steamer built for a daring trade.

She is the *Dolphin*, a ship that relies "not... on the wind to escape the Federalists, but rather on her powerful engines" (BR-002-002).

## Plot Summary

The venture is commercial: her captain James Playfair, of the Glasgow firm of Vincent Playfair & Co., means to run the Union blockade of Charleston, selling "a heavy cargo of clothes, ammunition, and provisions" and returning laden with cotton (BR-002-004, BR-010-006).

Among the crew is a young "novice" named John Stiggs, who is in truth Jenny Halliburtt of Boston in disguise, daughter of an imprisoned abolitionist, aided by the devoted sailor Crockston (BR-003-035, BR-008-062). When her secret is out, James pledges "that the *Dolphin* shall not leave Charleston" without her father (BR-008-003).

The rescue grows urgent when a courier from Richmond brings "Jonathan Halliburtt's death-warrant" (BR-008-067). Vowing "in two hours your father will be in safety near you, or I shall have perished," James rows ashore, and by Crockston's ruse the prisoner is spirited from the citadel (BR-008-069, BR-008-058).

The escape runs the gauntlet of the harbour forts. Past Fort Sumter and Fort Moultrie the *Dolphin* races, until, to outstrip a pursuing frigate, James orders "bales of cotton... soaked in spirits" flung into the furnaces for speed (BR-009-036, BR-009-066). Reunited, "Jenny fell into her father's arms" as the ship gained the open Atlantic (BR-009-020).

Home at Glasgow, the tale ends in a wedding: "the marriage of James Playfair, of the firm of Vincent Playfair & Co., of Glasgow, with Miss Jenny Halliburtt, of Boston" — while Uncle Vincent boasts he "sold my cotton at three hundred and seventy-five per cent. profit" (BR-010-006, BR-010-014).

## Main Characters

- James Playfair — the young Glasgow captain of the *Dolphin*, who runs the blockade
- Jenny Halliburtt — the abolitionist's daughter who sails disguised as the novice John Stiggs
- Crockston — Jenny's devoted servant, whose ruse frees her father
- Jonathan Halliburtt — the Boston abolitionist imprisoned and condemned at Charleston
- Vincent Playfair — James's uncle and head of the merchant firm, mindful of profit

## Key Places & Technology

- The *Dolphin* — the swift Clyde-built steamer designed to outrun the Federal blockaders
- Charleston Harbour — the blockaded port, guarded by Forts Sumter and Moultrie

## Themes

Verne braids commerce, war, and romance, letting a profit-seeking blockade run turn into an errand of conscience. The cotton burned in the furnaces to win escape neatly figures the tale's own trade of gain for gallantry, as mercenary daring ripens into love and rescue.
