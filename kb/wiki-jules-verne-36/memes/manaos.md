# Manaos

## Overview

Manaos is the Brazilian city at the junction of the Amazon and the Rio Negro, one of the great landmarks of the raft's descent in *Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon*. It marks a change even in the river's name, for "from the frontier of Brazil to Manaos, where the superb Rio Negro joins it, it takes the name of the Solimaës." (EHLA-005-023)

## In the Narrative

The city is a fixed goal for travellers met along the river. One passenger declares his errand plainly — "no, only to Manaos, where I have business" (EHLA-013-033) — and Fragoso remarks of another, "our compatriot is not going to stop at the frontier... he is going on to Manaos." (EHLA-013-078)

It is a place of trade and errand for the raft's company, who are "intrusted... with a few products of the country for him to dispose of on their account at Manaos and Belem." (EHLA-014-034) It stands too in the family's imagination as a town of some refinement, weighed against "what more could she have learned in a convent at Manaos or Belem?" (EHLA-003-030)

## Geography & Features

Manaos lies where the dark Rio Negro pours into the main stream, the confluence that renames the upper river the Solimaës. (EHLA-005-023) It is an old settlement much diminished from earlier days, for "of the ancient Manaos one can count but a wandering party or two." (EHLA-005-045)

Around it cling the river-legends of the region, such as the tale that Fragoso defends — the belief "in the trunk of Manaos." (EHLA-011-049)
