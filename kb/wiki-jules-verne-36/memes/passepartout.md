# Passepartout

> **Note:** Inline PN citations for this page use a 4-character work code (`AWED`); they render as plain text pending RFC-0003. The underlying PN data is valid.

## Overview

Passepartout is the French manservant who enters [[phileas-fogg|Phileas Fogg]]'s employ on the very day the wager is struck. He introduces himself as "Jean Passepartout, a surname which has clung to me" (AWED-001-018), and when Fogg accepts him with the words that Passepartout "suits me" (AWED-001-019), his fate is bound to the eighty-day voyage.

## Role in the Story

Having sought a tranquil post, Passepartout instead finds himself swept around the globe. From his first brief interview with Mr. Fogg, Passepartout had been carefully observing his new master (AWED-002-004), trying to read the precise, silent gentleman he now serves. Through the journey he supplies the warmth, blunders, and improvisation that his methodical master lacks, repeatedly rescuing the enterprise by accident and by loyalty.

## Character & Traits

Passepartout is the emotional and comic counterweight to Fogg's mechanical exactitude. Where Fogg is silent calculation, Passepartout is impulse and feeling, yet he shares a certain steadiness — physiognomists would call it "repose in action" (AWED-002-004). His good humour and adaptability, signalled by the very name he carries (AWED-001-018), make him the human face of a journey otherwise ruled by clockwork.

## Relationships
