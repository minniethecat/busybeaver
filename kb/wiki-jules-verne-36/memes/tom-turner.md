# Tom Turner

## Overview

He is Robur's second-in-command, for the crew was "Robur, his mate Tom Turner, an engineer and two assistants, two steersman and a cook--eight men all told" (RC-007-032).

He is drawn as a hard English seaman, "an Englishman of about forty-five, broad in the shoulders and short in the legs, a man of iron, with one of those enormous characteristic heads that Hogarth rejoiced in" (RC-010-003).

## Role in the Story

He is the mate who attends the engineer at every command, so that when Robur at last appeared "his mate, Tom Turner, accompanied him" (RC-008-044).

He runs the ship while his master directs it, for Robur "gave the orders, while Tom Turner was at the helm" (RC-012-027).

He is the watchful lookout who reports what the horizon brings, coming up "to the engineer and said, 'Do you see that black spot on the horizon, sir'" (RC-017-018).

## Character & Traits

His is the mouth behind the Albatross's fearful voice, for "the trumpet which blared its startling fanfares through the air was that of the mate, Tom Turner" (RC-008-048).

He is the ship's gunner and hunter, who "seized the arquebus, which was resting against a cleat on the rail" to harpoon a whale (RC-011-033).

Even under the crushing spin of the waterspouts he keeps his post, "crouching behind the swivel amidships where the effect of the centrifugal force was least felt" (RC-016-020).

His seamanship holds the aeronef steady in the worst of it, for near Cape Horn "Tom Turner was at the helm, and it required all his skill to keep her straight" (RC-018-018).

## Relationships

- [[Robur]] — the engineer he serves and counsels, who confides in him mid-voyage that "'That is nearer than I supposed,' said Robur to Tom Turner" (RC-019-014).
- [[Frycollin]] — the terrified captive he is set to manage, for when the crew got ready to fish "the engineer ordered them to shut up Frycollin in his cabin" (RC-013-053).

## Appearances

- [[Robur the Conqueror]] — as the mate of the Albatross to the last, reporting the weather to his chief when "Tom Turner came up to the engineer" about the falling breeze (RC-020-025).
