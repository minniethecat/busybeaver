# Christmas Harbour

## Overview

Christmas Harbour is the real harbour in the Kerguelen Islands where *An Antarctic Mystery* opens. It belongs to the chief islet of the group: "Christmas Harbour belongs to the most important islet of the archipelago, one that is about half as large as Corsica." (AM-001-005)

The narrator has come there for scientific work, having "completed the geological and mineralogical studies which had brought me to the group in general and to Christmas Harbour in particular." (AM-001-004)

## In the Narrative

The harbour is dominated by the Green Cormorant, the inn kept by the American Atkins, where the narrator lodges while awaiting a ship. Atkins recalls "the day of your arrival at Christmas Harbour, when you came to the Green Cormorant" (AM-001-015), and boasts that fifteen years before he "settled down comfortably at Christmas Harbour with my Betsy." (AM-001-023)

The narrator means to "take my passage on board the *Halbrane* so soon as she should come to her moorings in Christmas Harbour." (AM-001-039) When the schooner arrives, "Shortly after the *Halbrane* lay at anchor in the middle of Christmas Harbour." (AM-002-009)

## Geography & Features

The harbour is prized by mariners for its shelter: "It is safe, and easy, and free of access," and "Your ship may ride securely at single anchor in its waters, while the bay remains free from ice." (AM-001-005) It is the sole port of call for the region's whalers, since "no second inn existed at Christmas Harbour." (AM-001-027)

A high rock stands at the head of the bay, where "The departing albatross sat motionless upon a high rock, at the end of the bay of Christmas Harbour." (AM-001-045) In the fishing season, Atkins promises, "there will be more ships in Christmas Harbour than there are houses around the Green Cormorant." (AM-002-052)
