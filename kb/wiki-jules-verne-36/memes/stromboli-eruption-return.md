# The Stromboli Eruption

## Overview

The Stromboli Eruption is the astonishing return that ends *[[journey-to-the-center-of-the-earth|Journey to the Center of the Earth]]*, carrying the explorers back to daylight not by the way they came but through an erupting volcano. Hans had saved the narrator from death as he lay rolling on the edge of the crater (JCE-044-002), the mouth through which they are cast out.

## What Happens

Borne upward on a rising column of lava and steam, the travellers are expelled from the earth into blinding sun. They come out of the crater half naked, meeting the radiant orb to which they had been strangers for two months (JCE-044-010) after their long descent underground.

Above them a volcano at intervals of fifteen minutes issues, with loud explosions, lofty columns of fire, mingled with pumice stones, ashes, and flowing lava (JCE-044-016). Only when a herdboy answers their question does the place have a name: "STROMBOLI" (JCE-044-052) — an island in the Mediterranean, far from the [[iceland|Iceland]] where they began.

## Significance

The eruption is the novel's most audacious stroke, closing an underground descent with a violent ascent by the one route no one planned. That they emerged strangers to the radiant orb after two months below (JCE-044-010) measures the depth and duration of their vanished journey.

When the little herdboy, slipping out of Hans' hands, replies "STROMBOLI" (JCE-044-052), the final surprise lands: they entered by an [[iceland|Iceland]]ic crater and left by a Sicilian one, the planet itself proving stranger than their theories.
