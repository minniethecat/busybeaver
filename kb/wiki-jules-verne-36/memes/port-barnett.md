# Port Barnett

## Overview

Port Barnett is the little harbour at Cape Bathurst, in the Arctic, established by the Hudson's Bay expedition in *The Fur Country*. It is named in honour of the travelling companion Mrs Paulina Barnett: "Out of compliment to the lady of the party... Lieutenant Hobson named the stream Paulina river, and the little harbour Port Barnett." (FC-012-026)

The site is chosen for the founding of a new settlement, its lagoon sheltered and its "wooded heights surrounding the lagoon" ready to supply pine and birch for timber and fuel. (FC-012-026)

## In the Narrative

The harbour serves the colony at the fort as a landmark on the coast. Reindeer fall into a trap set "half way between the fort and Port Barnett" (FC-028-020), and the explorers survey "that part of the coast between Port Barnett and Cape Michael." (FC-040-029)

Its fate turns tragic when the cape proves to be a drifting island. Mrs Barnett remarks ruefully that "you were good enough to give my name to the port and river, and now there will be neither Paulina river nor Port Barnett." (FC-022-012) As the land breaks up, the colonists reckon "We have lost Port Barnett and Paulina River." (FC-026-030)

## Geography & Features

The port lies on a low, even coast: "From Cape Michael to the former Port Barnett the coast ran in an almost straight line from south to north for about eleven miles." (FC-027-051) A small promontory once guarded it — the party reaches "the little promontory which formerly protected Port Barnett from the east winds." (FC-027-053)

As the ice-island melts, the ground about the port is steadily lost. The colonists fear that "the whole district between Cape Michael and Port Barnett... would disappear" (FC-043-006), while Mrs Barnett walks "that part of the island between the former Cape Bathurst and Port Barnett." (FC-042-032)
