## Overview

Madagascar is the great Indian Ocean island that shadows the slave-trade of *Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen* — it was at Madagascar, where slavery was soon to be abolished, that Tom and his companions had been sold. (DSCF-038-015)

## In the Narrative

The island is drawn as a terminus of a cruel commerce. Of the unfortunate captives, some were exported to Cuba, some to Madagascar, others to the Arab or Turkish provinces of Asia, to Mecca or Muscat. (DSCF-019-019)

The men, destined to the hardest labor, went to the factories of the two coasts, exported either to the Spanish colonies or to the markets of Muscat and Madagascar. (DSCF-028-043)

## Geography & Features

Its supply lines run to the African mainland — business was always profitable with upper Egypt and the Mozambique coast, which furnishes all Madagascar. (DSCF-020-073)

In the reordered world it anchors the southern hemisphere: the third section east of Kilimanjaro would include Madagascar, the islands of Marion, Kerguelen, Maurice, and Reunion. (TT-015-019) A traveler departing from Aden, Madagascar, or Zanzibar would have been too late for the polar regions. (TT-015-005)
