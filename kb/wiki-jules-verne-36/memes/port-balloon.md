# Port Balloon

## Overview

Port Balloon is the small, rock-sheltered creek on the southern coast of Lincoln Island that the castaways adopt as their harbour in *The Mysterious Island*. The colonists christen it while surveying the shore: "after having given the creek the very suitable name of 'Port Balloon,' the settlers pursued their way along Claw Cape." (MI-027-093)

Pencroft judges it ideal for their needs — "That little creek, shut in by rocks, seems to me to be just the harbor we want." (MI-037-053)

## In the Narrative

The name commemorates the balloon that first cast the settlers ashore, whose fabric is stored nearby. Fetching the balloon-case requires taking "a cart to Port Balloon, and consequently, necessary to beat a road through the dense forests of the Far West." (MI-029-028)

Neb and Pencroft inspect the store and report "the stock of cloth would suffer no damage in the grotto where it was stored." (MI-029-028)

Pencroft dreams of easy transport: "some day we shall have a railroad from Granite House to Port Balloon, with a branch line to Mount Franklin!" (MI-029-045) In time a straight cutting is driven "through the forests of the Far West, from the angle of the Mercy to Port Balloon" (MI-029-055), and the whole colony rides out along "the road to Port Balloon." (MI-029-057)

## Geography & Features

The port lies on the southern coast, "only a distance of three miles and a half" from Granite House's bridge, a proximity that makes "a good cart-road" between the two practical. (MI-029-015) It becomes one of the fixed points of the island's road network, the daily labours including "improving the roads to the corral and to Port Balloon." (MI-030-037)

As a working harbour it shelters the colonists' craft: the *Bonadventure* stands "off the shore, three or four miles across from Port Balloon" (MI-034-082) and later approaches the coast "in the direction of Port Balloon." (MI-034-106) Hunters, too, set out "in the direction of Port Balloon, in order to reach the marsh." (MI-033-076)
