## Overview

Norway is the Scandinavian land of fjords and mountains that forms an immense horn between Bergen and Christiania, its broad central portion the Telemark. (TN-002-004) Dal lies in the Telemark, the Telemark in Norway, whose thousands of fjords let the sea kiss the feet of its mountains. (TN-002-003) It is the principal setting of Ticket No. 9672.

## In the Narrative

At the date of the story, 1862, Norway was not yet traversed by the railroad that would later join Stockholm to Drontheim by way of Christiania. (TN-002-006) In hospitable Norway such precautions as locked doors are unnecessary. (TN-001-028) Its people move with the slow and measured tread characteristic of the inhabitants. (TN-001-045)

There is no aristocracy in Norway, yet the reigning democracy is aristocratic to the highest degree. (TN-003-001) Lovers are almost invariably publicly betrothed before marriage. (TN-004-056) A traveller reaches the heart of the Telemark by kariol, across the forests, lakes, and valleys of Central Norway. (TN-006-010)

## Geography & Features

The rivers of western Norway lose themselves in the broad ocean that carries letters across the world. (TN-001-073) Offshore the Gulf Stream, after ascending to Newfoundland, inclines north-east along the coast of Norway. (ACH-005-042) A schooner bound for Iceland skirts Norway by Cape Lindness before entering the North Sea. (JCE-009-015)
