## Overview

Moscow is the old Russian city where *Michael Strogoff* begins — the river was the Moskowa, the town Moscow, the fortified inclosure the Kremlin, above which the New Palace threw its light. (MS-002-079)

## In the Narrative

It hosts the fete that opens the story: the Czar had left the ball-room of the New Palace at the height of a fete he was giving to the civil and military authorities and principal people of Moscow. (MS-003-001)

From here the courier is launched. If anyone could accomplish the journey from Moscow to Irkutsk, across a rebellious country, surmounting obstacles and braving perils of all sorts, Michael Strogoff was the man. (MS-004-033)

## Geography & Features

It stands at the western end of a vast traverse — the distance between Moscow and Irkutsk, about to be crossed by Strogoff, was three thousand four hundred miles. (MS-005-001) By the older reckoning it is five thousand two hundred versts from Moscow to Irkutsk. (MS-003-066)

It is a hub of departure: a crowd of travelers had collected at the Moscow station, the terminus of the iron road then uniting Moscow and St. Petersburg. (MS-005-013)
