# Jaspar Hobson

## Overview

Jaspar Hobson is a lieutenant of the Hudson's Bay Company, introduced at Captain Craventy's fête among "some sixty soldiers or employés of the Hudson's Bay Company" (FC-001-011). Verne draws him plainly: "Jaspar Hobson was a man of forty years of age" (FC-001-024), "a child of the Company," born at Fort Assiniboin and "no mere hunter, but a soldier, a brave and intelligent officer."

## Role in the Story

Charged with planting a new fort on the Arctic coast, he sets out in April at the head of an expedition that "the lady traveller was to join ... for the exploration of the north" (FC-001-029). His captain vouches for him without reserve: "Jaspar Hobson has never yet drawn back from a task imposed upon him, however formidable it may have appeared" (FC-002-010).

Guided north, "Hobson determined to get to the coast by the shortest route" (FC-010-007), knowing "he would have to penetrate a good deal further north" before his work was done (FC-010-033). He raises Fort Hope on what seems a peninsula — until earthquake severs it.

He grasps the calamity and its strange corollary: the quake "converted our peninsula into a floating island, and this explains why the furred and other animals imprisoned like ourselves, have become so numerous round the fort!" (FC-024-032), yet "we have absolutely no control over our floating island" (FC-025-014).

## Character & Traits

Command sits heavily but steadily upon him. He measures his position by the chronometer to the second — "'Seventy degrees, forty-four minutes, and thirty-seven seconds,' replied Hobson" (FC-023-080) — and shoulders the burden of choice, for "his office imposed caution, and being called upon to decide which of his companions should go, he chose the Sergeant" (FC-021-041).

He guards his people's peace even at cost to himself: the gravest danger "was kept a profound secret, as Hobson was unwilling to render his companions anxious" (FC-023-030). When at last the fort is lost, Verne absolves him — "no one could possibly blame Hobson or his companions, who had gone through such extraordinary and unexpected trials" (FC-047-003).

## Relationships

- Mrs Paulina Barnett — the intrepid lady traveller who joins his northern expedition and stands beside him through its perils (FC-001-029, FC-010-030).
- Sergeant Long — his steady subordinate, whom he entrusts with the expedition's most dangerous errand (FC-021-041).
- [[The Fur Country]] — the novel whose polar venture he commands from fort-building to shipwreck on the ice.
