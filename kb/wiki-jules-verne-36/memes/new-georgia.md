## Overview

New Georgia is a sub-Antarctic island of the South Atlantic that figures in An Antarctic Mystery as a waypoint toward the polar regions. This land could only be the Isle de Saint Pierre — its British names are South Georgia, New Georgia, and King George's Island — and it belongs to the circumpolar regions. (AM-010-020)

Captain Len Guy's intention was to take the Sandwich Isles for his point of departure towards the south, after making acquaintance with New Georgia, distant eight hundred miles from the Falklands. (AM-010-006)

## In the Narrative

The island lay on the border of the fishing grounds that framed the expedition. For a fishing campaign on the borders of the Polar Circle, between the Sandwich Islands and New Georgia, Captain Len Guy would have had merely to make a selection. (AM-009-043)

Later, adrift on their iceberg, the castaways could hope only that their floating tenement might meet one of the whaling ships whose business in the deep waters lies between the Orkneys and New Georgia. (AM-020-215) In the end all hope was abandoned of finding whaling-ships in the waters of the Sandwich Islands, the South Orkneys, or South Georgia. (AM-025-014)

## Geography & Features

New Georgia is situated within five hundred leagues of the Magellan Straits and belongs to the administrative domain of the Falklands. (AM-010-023) Being exposed to the direct action of the Antarctic polar current, it is freely frequented by marine mammals. (AM-010-024)

The island is barren of timber. It was fortunate that its birds did not want branches to perch on, for there does not exist a tree in New Georgia. (AM-010-025)

## Connections

New Georgia sat within the same belt of southern fishing grounds as the Sandwich Islands, between which the Polar Circle campaigns were framed. (AM-009-043) As a possession administered from the Falklands, it linked the expedition's staging grounds to the circumpolar sea. (AM-010-023)

It shared the deep whaling waters that stretched toward the South Orkneys and South Georgia, the last hope of ships venturing that far south. (AM-025-014)
