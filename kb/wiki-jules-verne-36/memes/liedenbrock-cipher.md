# Deciphering the Saknussemm Cipher

## Overview

The deciphering of the Saknussemm cipher is the discovery that launches *[[journey-to-the-center-of-the-earth|Journey to the Center of the Earth]]*. A piece of parchment, five inches by three, along which were traced certain mysterious characters (JCE-002-029), falls from an old [[iceland|Iceland]]ic book and sets [[professor-lidenbrock|Professor Lidenbrock]] on the hunt for its meaning.

## What Happens

Recognising the writing at once, the Professor declares, "there is a secret in it, and I mean to discover the key" (JCE-003-002), and shuts the household away until the runes yield their sense. He labours over the document without success, fasting and forbidding food to the house until it is solved.

The breakthrough comes by accident. Fanning himself with the transposed copy, [[axel|Axel]] glimpses Latin words running the wrong way, and a sudden light bursts in upon him: "I had discovered the key to the cipher" (JCE-004-021). The message must be read backwards, and it names the crater by which a bold traveller may reach the centre of the Earth.

## Significance

The cipher is the mainspring of the whole novel: without its solution there is no expedition, no descent, no journey. That [[axel|Axel]], fanning himself, found the sudden light by which he had discovered the key to the cipher (JCE-004-021) makes the nephew — not the learned uncle — the unwitting author of the adventure. The mysterious characters traced on the parchment (JCE-002-029) thus turn a scholar's curiosity into a voyage underground.

## Participants & Setting

- Deciphered by [[Axel]] in [[Professor Lidenbrock]]'s house in Hamburg.
- Drives the expedition chronicled in [[Journey to the Center of the Earth]].
