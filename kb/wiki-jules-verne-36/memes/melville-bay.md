# Melville Bay

## Overview

Melville Bay is the real Arctic bay opening off Baffin's Sea that forms the first great objective of the *Forward*'s secret voyage. The captain's sealed orders are to "cross Davis's Straits, and sail up Baffin's Sea to Melville Bay." (ACH-004-043)

It is "one of the most considerable seas in these regions," (ACH-018-032) the threshold beyond which lie the channels leading to the Pole.

## In the Narrative

The crew speculate that the unknown captain "will come as captain on board from the coast of Uppernawik or Melville Bay," (ACH-007-024) though Shandon warns "the difficulty will be to get as far as Melville Bay." (ACH-007-025) The written command is plain: "take a northerly direction towards Melville Bay, and from thence try and penetrate into Smith's Straits." (ACH-009-014)

The bay carries a grim memory — it was here that the lost Franklin ships "were seen for the last time," the whalers having "perceived the two ships in Melville Bay for the last time." (ACH-017-005)

## Geography & Features

The bay lies at the head of a great crossroads of Arctic waters: "at the end of McClintock Channel lies Melville Bay, and from thence go the straits that lead to Baffin's Bay." (ACH-018-030)

The brig enters it after doubling Cape Minto: "the brig entered Melville Bay," (ACH-018-032) finding herself "in the midst of Melville Bay, one of the most considerable seas in these regions." (ACH-018-032) To press on is the true test of the captain: "if when we get there you repeat 'Nothing in Melville Bay,' I shall greet you as the only captain of the *Forward*." (ACH-010-010)
