# Gourbi Island

## Overview

Gourbi Island is the fictional island on the comet Gallia in *Off on a Comet*, the last inhabited remnant of Algeria carried into space. Captain Servadac claims it as his domain: "The new territory of which he had become the monarch he named Gourbi Island." (OC-008-027)

It is all that survives of France on the wandering comet — "that little shred of Algeria, constitutes all that is left of our glorious France?" (OC-017-005)

## In the Narrative

The island is the base of the castaways' expeditions across Gallia. Servadac urges "that the explorations might be deferred until after a visit had been paid to Gourbi Island" (OC-018-004), and the schooner *Dobryna* is ordered to "return to Gourbi Island." (OC-018-040)

It is one of only two inhabited spots on the comet, "Gibraltar... and their own Gourbi Island." (OC-020-003) From its lookout Ben Zoof spies an approaching ship appear "upon the horizon." (OC-019-055)

## Geography & Features

As Gallia recedes from the sun, the island cools: "the heat upon Gourbi Island is not greater now than we might ordinarily expect to find in Algeria." (OC-011-015) A colossal wall of rock borders it, extending "to the south of Gourbi Island" across what had been the Sahara. (OC-018-027)

When the killing winter threatens, the colony abandons it, "conveying man and beast, corn and fodder, across to the volcanic headland." (OC-022-003)

## Connections

- Off on a Comet — Verne's tale of a comet that grazes the Earth
- Gallia — the comet on which Gourbi Island is carried into space
