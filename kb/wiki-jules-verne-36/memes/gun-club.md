## Overview

The Gun Club is a society of [[baltimore|Baltimore]] artillerists founded during the American Civil War, devoted wholly to the science of gunnery and the design of ever more "gigantic cannon, much less useful than sewing-machines, but quite as astonishing" (FEM-001-004). Its membership swelled with astonishing speed:

> One month after its formation it numbered eighteen hundred and thirty-three effective members. (FEM-001-006)

Admission was strictly earned — "One condition was imposed as a _sine quâ non_ upon every one who wished to become a member" (FEM-001-007): each candidate had to have invented, or at least improved, a cannon or firearm.

## Role in the Story

With the war over and no more fortifications to batter, the club's members fall idle, until President [[Impey Barbicane]] revives them with a colossal proposal: "Is it possible to send a projectile to the moon?" (FEM-004-009).

The question transforms the Gun Club from a fraternity of ballistics enthusiasts into the engine of humanity's first attempt to reach another world, drawing "corresponding members by hundreds" (FEM-002-002) to its meeting-halls at 21 Union-square.

## Members

- [[Impey Barbicane]] — president of the club and originator of the Moon-shot, who "succeeded in getting home almost knocked to pieces" (FEM-003-008) after the founding meeting
- [[Michel Ardan]] — the French adventurer who volunteers to ride the projectile

## Activities

The club's defining enterprise is the casting of the [[Columbiad]], a titanic cannon sunk into the Florida earth to hurl a manned projectile at the Moon — the culmination of the members' lifelong obsession with launching "projectiles beyond permitted distances" (FEM-001-010).
