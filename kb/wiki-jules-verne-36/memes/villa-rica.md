## Overview

Villa Rica is the town of the Brazilian diamond district that stood on the road of the diamond convoy. There the officers first make for Villa Rica, where the commandant puts his seal on the sacks, and then the convoy continues its journey to Rio de Janeiro. (EHLA-019-031)

It was also a seat of law. Before he rose to be a judge in the province, Judge Ribeiro was an advocate at Villa Rica, and it was he who defended the prisoner at the trial. (EHLA-023-002)

## In the Narrative

The town is inseparable from the diamond affair. The scoundrels who awaited the convoy just beyond Villa Rica massacred the escort and carried off the stones. (EHLA-039-019) The man convicted of the crime, sentenced to death for the diamond affair, was the one who escaped from the prison at Villa Rica a few hours before he should have been executed. (EHLA-020-007)

The verdict had come from the town itself. The accused had appeared before the jury at Villa Rica, whose verdict was given unanimously, and without even the addition of extenuating circumstances. (EHLA-025-039)

## Geography & Features

The town is defined by its instruments of justice. On the night which preceded his execution, when the gallows was already erected, Joam Dacosta managed to escape from the prison at Villa Rica. (EHLA-023-005) Among the numbers later tried as a cipher was the date of the sentence at the Villa Rica assizes. (EHLA-034-050)

The escape long shadowed his name. Once already, he recalled, he had escaped from the prison at Villa Rica, and people believed he fled from well-merited punishment. (EHLA-037-036)

## Connections

Villa Rica linked the diamond country to the coast and to the men of the story. Its convoy carried the sealed sacks onward to [[Rio de Janeiro]], while the advocate who had defended the accused there was the future chief justice Judge Ribeiro. (EHLA-024-068) In the end the innocence of the convict of Villa Rica was at length admitted. (EHLA-039-043)
