# Navy Island

## Overview

Navy Island is the real island in the Niagara River a short way above the falls, one of the landmarks of the river's final reach in *The Master of the World*. The narrator recalls exploring "Navy Island and also Goat Island, which separates the American falls from those of the Canadian side." (MW-014-007)

## In the Narrative

For the captive narrator the island is a hope of rescue on familiar ground: "At Navy Island I would be able to set foot on territory that I knew well." (MW-014-028)

As the current quickens toward the cataract he wonders, "Should I attempt to gain the shores of Navy Island, if we indeed advanced that far?" (MW-014-053) But the vessel denies him: "The 'Terror' took the left branch of the river in passing Navy Island." (MW-014-057)

## Geography & Features

The island divides the river above the falls: "Between Lake Erie, and the little city of Niagara Falls, two islands divide the current of the river, Navy Island, a league above the cataract, and Goat Island." (MW-014-047)

Towns face it across the stream — "Schlosser on the right bank, and Chippewa on the left, located on either side of Navy Island." (MW-014-048)
