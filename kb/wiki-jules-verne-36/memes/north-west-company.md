## Overview

The North-West Company is the great Montreal fur-trading concern that for decades disputed the northern trade with the [[Hudson's Bay Company]]. Towards 1784 some merchants of Montreal combined to explore the fur country and founded "that powerful North-west Company, which soon became the centre of the fur trade." (FC-002-020)

Its rise made it the most formidable rival the older chartered company had ever faced, and for a generation the two fought for the peltries of the far north.

## Role in the Story

The Company belongs to the historical background against which Lieutenant Hobson's expedition is set, its fortunes traced in the sketch of the fur trade that opens the narrative. It was a hard and grasping rival: the North-west Company "shrank from no act, however iniquitous, if its interests were at stake." (FC-002-021)

The long contest ended in absorption. In 1821, after much discussion, a treaty was made by which the Hudson's Bay Company's old rival the North-west Company was merged into it, ending the rivalry on the continent. (FC-002-022)

That merger frames a later exchange, when Mrs Barnett supposes that after the absorption of the old North-West Company the Hudson's Bay Company had no rivals left, and is corrected that the American [[St Louis Fur Company]] still remained. (FC-012-008)

## Members

The Company was founded and run by the merchants of Montreal who pooled their capital to open the fur country. (FC-002-020) No individual partner is named in the narrative; it is remembered as an institution, the vanished rival whose absorption into the [[Hudson's Bay Company]] is recalled by the travellers, including the laureate [[Paulina Barnett]].
