# The Disintegration of Victoria Island

## Overview

The disintegration of [[Victoria Island]] is the climax of *[[The Fur Country]]*, in which the wandering ice-field that had carried the colonists of Fort Hope out to sea melts, splinters, and finally shrinks to a single jagged floe, from which the survivors are at last delivered onto the [[Aleutian Islands]].

Once the last of the ice-wall had dissolved the island lay stationary, all the force of the current running deep below the waves rather than on the surface (FC-044-002). Of the hundred and forty square miles that had once formed the island, only twenty at length remained (FC-045-016).

## What Happens

The ice was dying from within. On the 1st of June the fresh-water lake at the island's heart turned salt: its bottom had given way, and the sea had flowed in (FC-044-019).

Then, in fog, the raft the colonists had built was gone (FC-045-007). During the night six-sevenths of the district once belonging to Cape Bathurst had silently floated away, without a shock of any kind, so completely had the ice been worn away by the constant action of the waves (FC-045-009).

What remained was a jagged triangular strip of ice, a hundred feet at its base and scarcely five hundred in its greatest extent; and on it twenty-one human beings, some hundred furred animals, a few dogs (FC-046-002).

A strong breeze set in from the offing, bearing to the south-east, the very direction in which lay the nearest of the Aleutian Islands (FC-046-042). Might not a sail be contrived on the islet as on an ordinary raft? (FC-046-044).

A sail made of all the clothes and coverlets that remained was hoisted on a makeshift mast and swelled in the breeze (FC-046-047). At six o'clock Madge pointed to the south-east and cried out: land there was indeed, twelve miles from the island (FC-046-066).

## Significance

It was on the island of Blejinie, the last of the Aleutian group at the extreme south of Behring Sea, that all the colonists of Fort Hope at last landed (FC-047-002).

Of the nineteen persons under the Lieutenant's charge not one was missing, and he had even gained two new members for his little colony (FC-047-003). Six days after their rescue the shipwrecked mariners reached New Archangel, the capital of Russian America (FC-047-004).

The event closes the long involuntary voyage that began when Cape Bathurst broke free of the mainland—the drift itself being a separate episode—and completes the novel's reversal of the Company's scheme, ruin turned to deliverance by the endurance of the little band.

## Participants & Setting

- Lieutenant Hobson, commander of the doomed factory, holds the despairing colonists together on the shrinking floe and conceives the makeshift sail that carries them within reach of land.

- Mrs Barnett is the soul of the party; when the soldier Kellet, crazed by fear, resolves on suicide (FC-046-012), she gentles him with a promise—"we will die together, but not to-day" (FC-046-022).

- The whole company—soldiers, women, the astronomer Thomas Black, and the native girl Kalumah—are the twenty-one souls crowded on the last piece of ice, whom the Lieutenant must pity as he stands surrounded by the miserable despairing creatures (FC-045-011).

- The setting is Victoria Island itself, a melting raft of ice adrift in the Behring Sea, dwindling hour by hour until only a triangular floe bears the castaways toward the Aleutian shore.
