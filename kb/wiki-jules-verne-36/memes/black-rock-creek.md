# Black Rock Creek

## Overview

Black Rock Creek is the secluded inlet on the Lake Erie shore that shelters Robur's machine in *The Master of the World*. A submarine, "presumably the one we sought, had been hidden in Black Rock Creek." (MW-011-049)

The region is remote and forbidding — "Mr. Wells told me that the region around Black Rock Creek was among the wildest in the state." (MW-011-018)

## In the Narrative

The narrator Strock and Arthur Wells lay their ambush there. Wells "knew Black Rock Creek thoroughly, having hunted there more than once." (MW-011-051) Reaching it, "before us stretched the banks of Black Rock Creek." (MW-011-065)

But the machine escapes them: "The 'Terror,' driven by all the power of her engines, made a single bound and darted out across Black Rock Creek." (MW-012-089) Strock is left "thinking of my companions, left behind on the shore of Black Rock Creek." (MW-013-028)

## Geography & Features

The creek is a genuine haven for the vessel, its captain knowing "perfectly the channels and shores of Black Rock Creek, since he ventured here in such darkness." (MW-012-031) Strock wonders whether it is "a regular hiding place of the 'Terror.'" (MW-012-043)

It opens onto the lake by a narrow mouth where the submarine was seen to "stop at the mouth of Black Rock Creek." (MW-011-026) Its narrow waters briefly offered the one chance to seize the machine, an opening in "the narrow waters of Black Rock Creek." (MW-012-061)
