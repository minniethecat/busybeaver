## Overview

*The Master of the World* is Jules Verne's late thriller of technology and megalomania, and a sequel to *[[robur-the-conqueror|Robur the Conqueror]]*. It is told by John Strock, "head inspector in the federal police department at Washington," drawn into "the most extraordinary" events "which this twentieth century will witness" (MW-001-002).

The mystery begins at the Great Eyrie, a Carolina peak where "one stormy night pale flames... cast upon the district below a sinister, warning light" (MW-001-009).

## Plot Summary

Strange sightings multiply across the land. An unknown "automobile which was astonishing the world" travels "at more than double" the speed of the fastest cars, then a vessel proves to be "at once automobile, boat and submarine" (MW-004-005, MW-011-045).

Strock receives a threatening letter signed with initials standing for "Master of the World," warning him against renewing his "attempt on the Great Eyrie" (MW-010-009, MW-010-010). The machine's owner has bound himself to that crater as his hidden fortress (MW-014-011).

Captured and taken "On Board the Terror," Strock at last confronts its maker (MW-009-002). "This was [[robur-the-conqueror|Robur the Conqueror]], who now stood before me, who revealed himself... within his own impenetrable fortress" — the aviator who long ago flew the *[[albatross|Albatross]]* and mocked the balloonists' club (MW-016-004, MW-016-006).

[[robur|Robur]], "burning for revenge," had built anew in secret, and now defies the world in his four-fold machine (MW-016-012). Strock wonders whether the man is not simply "insane, this man who proclaimed himself, who believed himself, Master of the World" (MW-014-043).

The end comes not by human hand but by the heavens. The *Terror* soars into a storm above the Gulf of Mexico, and "the Master of the World had disappeared forever, struck down by those thunder-bolts which he had dared to brave in the regions of their fullest power" (MW-018-004). Strock alone is thrown clear upon the wreckage and saved (MW-018-004).

## Main Characters

- John Strock — the Washington federal inspector who narrates and pursues the mystery
- [[robur|Robur]] — the aviator-genius of *[[robur-the-conqueror|Robur the Conqueror]]*, now the self-styled Master of the World
- Mr. Wells — the officer who aids Strock's pursuit around the Great Lakes

## Key Places & Technology

- [[the-terror|The Terror]] — the machine that runs as automobile, boat, submarine, and aircraft alike
- [[great-eyrie|The Great Eyrie]] — the flame-lit Carolina crater that hides [[robur|Robur]]'s fortress

## Themes

Verne returns to [[robur|Robur]] to warn where unchecked genius may lead: the conqueror of the air has become a tyrant who claims the earth itself. Against such hubris he sets the storm, letting nature, not man, humble the inventor who dared brave the thunderbolts.
