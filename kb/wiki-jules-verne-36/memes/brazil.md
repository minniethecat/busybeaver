## Overview

Brazil is the great South American empire through whose provinces the Amazon runs, the homeland of the jangada voyage in *Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon*. In 1852, the year the story opens, there were still slaves in Brazil, and as a consequence captains of the woods — the *Capitaes do Mato* — engaged in the recapture of fugitive slaves. (EHLA-001-006)

It is a land where, with a little fortune, one could live without doing anything. (EHLA-001-016)

## In the Narrative

Joam Garral's wife Yaquita longs to see the country entire: with her family she would see Brazil, and journey down the splendid river even to the seacoast provinces through which it runs. (EHLA-004-041) So they resolve to see the Amazon in all its glory throughout its course through the provinces of Brazil. (EHLA-004-058)

Joam had wished his son to receive as solid an education as could then be obtained in the large towns of Brazil. (EHLA-003-031)

## Geography & Features

From the frontier of Brazil to Manaos, where the superb Rio Negro joins it, the great river takes the name of the Solimaës, from the Indian tribe Solimao. (EHLA-005-023) The government's greatest honour came on 31 July 1857, when after frontier disputes with France over the Guiana boundary the course of the Amazon was declared free and open. (EHLA-005-040)

The land teems with its own creatures and customs: the "barbado" monkey (EHLA-002-004), the coarse bitter tobacco of Brazil (EHLA-001-026), and the toucans and woodpeckers of Brazil among the golden forest clusters. (EHLA-007-038)
