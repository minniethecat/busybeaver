# Shannon Island

## Overview

Shannon Island is the Arctic land that fixes the search of *A Winter Amid the Ice*. It lies far to the north of the wintering ground: "an island (Shannon Island) lay a hundred miles north of Gaël-Hamkes Bay, where the 'Jeune-Hardie' was wintering." (WAI-008-004)

## In the Narrative

The island becomes the reasoned goal of Louis Cornbutte's rescue party. Since the missing schooner might have driven that way, "supposing that she could not reach Shannon Island, it was here that Louis Cornbutte and his comrades must have sought" the lost men. (WAI-008-005)

Over André Vasling's objection the course is set: "it was decided to direct the explorations on the side towards Shannon Island." (WAI-008-006) When land at last appears, the old sailor names it with certainty — "'It is Shannon Island,' said Jean Cornbutte." (WAI-011-044)

## Geography & Features

The island is a shore of ice and wreckage at the edge of the Greenland Sea. It is there, in the end, that the drifting survivors come to rest: the ship "was broken up by the icebergs floated by the thaw, and the castaways were carried, with a part of the _débris_ of their cabin, on the southern shores of Shannon Island." (WAI-012-020)

Set a hundred miles out from Gaël-Hamkes Bay, it marks the northern limit of the party's desperate winter search.
