## Overview

Russia is the vast empire whose eastern half, Asiatic Russia or Siberia, covers a superficial area of 1,790,208 square miles and contains nearly two millions of inhabitants. (MS-003-002)

It is the stage of *Michael Strogoff*, whose whole action turns on carrying a message across it: the journey through Asiatic Russia usually occupied from four to five weeks, even with every means of transport at the courier's disposal. (MS-005-001)

## In the Narrative

When Michael is twenty, it is decided that he shall enter the personal service of the Emperor of Russia, in the corps of the couriers of the Czar. (MS-004-039) The Czar sends him forth with a charge that names the empire itself: "Go then, Michael Strogoff, go for God, for Russia, for my brother, and for myself!" (MS-004-081)

The rebellion he rides against is not aimed at the emperor but raised against Russia — the country the exiles have never lost hope of seeing again. (MS-003-056)

## Geography & Features

The importance of a uniform in Russia is great, enough by itself to restrain merchants' tongues. (MS-005-018) Passports are no longer required in Russia, so travellers show instead a permit endorsed with a private seal. (MS-005-077) The traitor Ivan Ogareff has stirred the Tartar chiefs of Turkestan to pour their hordes into Siberia and excite a general rising in Asiatic Russia. (MS-003-042)

Russia had already several times been at war with the Khanates, and would need a large force to subdue the formidable, mountain-girt state of Bokhara. (MS-003-064)
