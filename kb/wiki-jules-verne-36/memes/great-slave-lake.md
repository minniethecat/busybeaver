# Great Slave Lake

## Overview

The Great Slave Lake is the real sub-Arctic Canadian lake that anchors the southern reach of *The Fur Country*. It is "one of the largest of the lakes beyond the 61st parallel... two hundred and fifty miles long by fifty across, and is situated exactly at 61° 25' N. lat. and 114° W. long." (FC-004-002)

Its forts and factories form the staging ground from which Lieutenant Hobson's expedition strikes north for the coast.

## In the Narrative

Traders gather at its posts — many "came from Fort Providence or Fort Resolution, of the Great Slave Lake district." (FC-001-011) It is at "Fort Reliance, on the Great Slave Lake" (FC-018-046) that Mrs Barnett recalls the journey's start.

The route north is laid out from here, "between the Slave Lake and that of the Great Bear beyond the Arctic Circle." (FC-005-007) Should the agents fail to appear, a convoy is to "make for the Great Slave Lake, taking with them some of the" party. (FC-022-058)

## Geography & Features

The lake "is dotted with little islands, the granite and gneiss of which they are formed jutting up in several places." (FC-004-003) Its waters "were full of fish; trout in them attained to an immense size, their weight often exceeding forty pounds." (FC-004-005)

It marks a boundary of the animal world: the great bison "seldom go further north than the Great Slave Lake," (FC-004-003) and in ordinary years the reindeer and hares retreat to "the shores of the Great Bear and Slave Lakes, a good many degrees farther south." (FC-033-022)
