## Overview

Suez is the first foreign stage of Phileas Fogg's journey and the place where the detective Fix takes up his watch. The British consul at Suez was in the habit of seeing English ships daily passing on the great canal, by which the old roundabout route from England to India was abridged by at least a half. (AWED-006-004) It is a fast-growing town, thanks to the enterprise of M. Lesseps.

## In the Narrative

Suez sets Fogg's schedule. From Suez to Bombay, by steamer, thirteen days — one line of the timetable that governs the whole wager. (AWED-003-029) The steamer only pauses here — "How long will she stop at Suez?" (AWED-006-020) The answer: four hours, long enough to get in her coal, for it is thirteen hundred and ten miles from Suez to Aden. (AWED-006-021)

It is also where the hunt narrows. "If the robber is on board he will no doubt get off at Suez, so as to reach the Dutch or French colonies in Asia by some other route," reasons Fix. (AWED-006-024)

## Geography & Features

At the head of the Red Sea where Lesseps' canal meets the sea, Suez is the coaling stop between the Mediterranean and India — the choke-point at which Fix hopes to trap his man before the Mongolia sails on to Bombay.
