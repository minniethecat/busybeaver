## Overview

*The Pearl of Lima* is Jules Verne's early tragic romance set in the Peruvian capital of Lima. Its opening chapter surveys a city divided among the proud descendants of Pizarro, the conquered Indians, and the mestizoes of mixed blood (PL-001-005).

Into this riven society Verne sets a doomed love that crosses every barrier of race and creed.

## Plot Summary

The wealthy mestizo André Certa, who "had inherited a large fortune," is betrothed to Sarah, "the beautiful young daughter of old Samuel," a Jewish merchant (PL-001-009, PL-001-021). But Sarah is loved by Martin Paz, a handsome young Indian of the mountains, whose father is the Sambo, secret leader of a brewing Indian revolt (PL-002-029, PL-002-038).

Learning that Sarah is promised to André Certa, Martin Paz fights his rival, then leaps into the current of the Rimac and is presumed dead (PL-002-031, PL-002-051). In truth he survives, and is sheltered by the noble Marquis Don Vegal, who comes to love the young Indian as a son (PL-007-030, PL-007-042).

The revolt breaks out. Martin Paz raises "the black flag of independence," but the government answers with a fusillade, and in the fighting he kills André Certa with his poignard (PL-008-018, PL-008-019, PL-008-047).

A discovered document then overturns everything: a receipt shows that Samuel had been paid to raise a child he "saved from the shipwreck of the San-José" — Sarah is in truth Don Vegal's own lost daughter (PL-008-053).

The Sambo, unappeased, carries Sarah off toward the river Madeira to be sacrificed, and Martin Paz, Don Vegal, and the servant Liberta pursue (PL-009-036). At the cataract Martin Paz casts his lasso and catches her canoe, but "an arrow whistles through the air, and pierces the heart of Martin Paz" (PL-009-060, PL-009-065).

Falling into the boat, he "re-descending the current of the river in her arms, is engulfed with Sarah in the vortex of the cataract" (PL-009-065). Don Vegal "regained Lima, where he died with grief and exhaustion," while the lovers were "in their brief and final re-union, betrothed for eternity" (PL-009-068, PL-009-071).

## Main Characters

- Martin Paz — the young Indian mountaineer who loves Sarah and dies to save her
- Sarah — Samuel's supposed daughter, revealed to be Don Vegal's child lost in a shipwreck
- André Certa — the wealthy mestizo betrothed to Sarah, killed by Martin Paz
- Don Vegal — the Spanish marquis who shelters Martin Paz and proves to be Sarah's father
- Samuel — the Jewish merchant who raised Sarah for payment
- The Sambo — Martin Paz's father and leader of the Indian revolt

## Key Places & Technology

- Lima — the Peruvian capital, divided among Spaniards, Indians, and mestizoes
- The river Madeira and its cataract — the site of the tragic final pursuit

## Themes

Verne weaves race, religion, and revolt into a tragedy of love thwarted by a divided society. The revelation of Sarah's true birth cannot save the lovers, whose union is granted only in death, as private devotion is crushed between the colliding castes of colonial Peru.
