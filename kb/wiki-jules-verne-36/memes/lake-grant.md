# Lake Grant

## Overview

Lake Grant is the freshwater lake set on the high plateau of Lincoln Island, one of the chief landmarks named by the castaways in *The Mysterious Island*. When the colonists christen the island's features, Cyrus Harding proposes "to that lake which is extended under our eyes, that of Lake Grant; nothing could be better, my friends." (MI-011-061)

Harding is drawn to it early, wishing "to reconnoiter Lake Grant, so magnificently framed in trees." (MI-012-012)

## In the Narrative

The lake serves as a reliable point of reference in the settlers' explorations. Harding reassures Herbert that "it will be impossible to lose ourselves, my boy, since, whether we follow the way to Lake Grant, or whether we join the Mercy through the woods of the Far West, we shall be certain to arrive at Prospect Heights." (MI-012-013)

Reaching "the western shore of Lake Grant" (MI-012-064), the explorers follow its banks "by climbing the plateau," though Harding cannot at first "discover the overfall, which, however, must exist somewhere." (MI-012-069)

It later proves central to the colony's engineering: to raise the water level they need only "establish a dam at the two openings made by the lake, and by which were fed Creek Glycerine and Falls River." (MI-048-006)

## Geography & Features

The lake lies on Prospect Heights, close to the sea yet elevated. The colonists site Granite House with it in mind — "we are placed between the Mercy and Lake Grant, an advantage which must not be neglected." (MI-016-012) From their windows "Lake Grant was viewed from thence in all its extent, and no breath disturbed the surface of its waters." (MI-016-032)

Its eastern shore runs close to the coastline: the miners go "to the point which formed the eastern shore of Lake Grant, and was only five hundred feet from the coast." (MI-017-061) A glade "situated two hundred feet from Lake Grant" is reached after a walk of five miles across Prospect Heights. (MI-013-028)
