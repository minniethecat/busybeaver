# Popof

## Overview

He is introduced as the one company man who never abandons his charge — "Popof, our head guard, a true Russian of soldierly bearing" (ASC-005-020).

His station fixes him at the heart of the train's mysteries, for "Popof's little cabin is on the platform of the first car, in the left-hand corner" (ASC-005-028).

## Role in the Story

He is the narrator's fountain of intelligence about the passengers, who "asked if he knew anything of our fellow travelers" bound for China (ASC-006-037).

When a shrouded personage rides in a sealed van, the narrator "asked Popof to discover the name of the defunct" and his escort (ASC-008-078).

His railwayman's knowledge extends to every stop, and "Popof pointed out the stations for the guards on the parapet of the bridge" (ASC-010-068).

He keeps the narrator abreast of who leaves and stays, telling him which travelers "would cross the Russo-Chinese frontier, so that they interested me little" (ASC-013-002).

## Character & Traits

The narrator counts on him above almost all others, for "he is the only one I can really trust besides Popof" (ASC-006-032).

He is a companionable soul over tobacco, and "Popof smiles and soon his perfumed puffs are mingling voluptuously with mine" (ASC-006-034).

He is ever obliging when invited to share a glass, for "Popof did not want asking twice" (ASC-008-016).

## Relationships

- [[Claudius Bombarnac]] — the special correspondent who leans on him for every scrap of news, greeting him "as soon as Popof reappeared I said to him: 'Anything fresh?'" (ASC-008-011).
- [[Faruskiar]] — the imposing traveler whose identity Popof uncovers, for "this leading star, I soon learned from Popof, bore the name of Faruskiar" (ASC-008-082).

## Appearances

- [[The Adventures of a Special Correspondent]] — as the ever-present head guard who alone among the crew "goes through with us" the whole length of the Grand Transasiatic (ASC-008-036).
