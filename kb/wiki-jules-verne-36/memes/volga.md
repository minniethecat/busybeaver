## Overview

The Volga is the great river of European Russia and the first water barrier on Michael Strogoff's road east. Nijni-Novgorod, Lower Novgorod, situate at the junction of the Volga and the Oka, is the chief town in the district of the same name. (MS-006-001) It is here that the courier must quit the railway and trust to river and road.

## In the Narrative

The river frames Strogoff's departure. Once arrived at Nijni-Novgorod, he would either take the land route or the steamer on the Volga, so as to reach the Ural Mountains as soon as possible. (MS-005-014) Walking the wharves, he followed the course of the Volga, whose waters were almost hidden under the countless number of boats floating on its bosom. (MS-006-030)

At dawn he crossed the Volga on a bridge of boats, guarded by mounted Cossacks, and reached the square where he had fallen in with the gipsy camp. (MS-006-034) The river is also a line of defence — the Don Cossacks have already gathered along the course of the Volga, to be opposed to the rebel Kirghiz. (MS-005-039)

## Geography & Features

Draining European Russia southeast toward the Caspian, the Volga carries the fair-traffic of Nijni-Novgorod and marks the threshold beyond which Strogoff's travelling becomes first less speedy and then less safe.
