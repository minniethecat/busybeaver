## Overview

Stapi is a village on the south shore of the Snæfell peninsula, at the very foot of the volcano, to which the guide Hans engaged to conduct the travellers. (JCE-011-011) It consists of about thirty huts, built of lava, at the south side of the base of the volcano. (JCE-014-002)

## In the Narrative

After four hours' walking the horses stopped at the door of the priest's house at Stapi. (JCE-013-035) Hans's engagement was not to terminate with their arrival there, but to continue for the whole period of the researches. (JCE-011-014) The preparations for departure were made the day after arrival. (JCE-014-017) At Hans's signal, they soon left the village behind them. (JCE-014-045)

## Geography & Features

At Stapi the narrator beheld a striking coastal phenomenon in all its beauty. (JCE-014-005) Beyond the village stood the basaltic wall of the fiord of Stapi, past which the party crossed a fibrous peat bog, the remnant of the peninsula's ancient vegetation. (JCE-015-004)

## Connections

Stapi is the threshold of the descent into Snæfell: from the priest's house the travellers set out for the crater, and it was to Stapi that the dismissed Icelanders turned back, descending the outer slopes of the volcano to return. (JCE-016-030)
