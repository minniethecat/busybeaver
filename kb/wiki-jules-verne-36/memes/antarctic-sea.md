# Antarctic Sea

## Overview

The Antarctic Sea is the ice-burdened southern ocean beyond the Polar Circle, the goal of the schooner *Halbrane* in *An Antarctic Mystery*. Its drift bears ice off the polar continent: "the mountains of ice carried out of the Antarctic Sea by the drift were detached from that continent." (AM-010-034)

## In the Narrative

The voyage's aim is announced as a plunge into these waters — the crew learn "that you are to come with us to the far end of the Antarctic seas." (AM-009-005) The prospect stirs the ports of the south: "The news that the schooner was bound for the Antarctic seas had produced some sensation in the Falklands, at Port Egmont." (AM-009-043)

Rumour holds that the vanished are lost somewhere within it: "I believe he has gone far, far into the southern seas, let him be as dumb as a fish about it." (AM-011-026)

## Geography & Features

The sea grows more perilous the further south the *Halbrane* presses: "In that portion of the Antarctic sea the ice-packs were more numerous," and there was reason to fear the tempest. (AM-012-075) Its far reaches raise the question of buried fire — "Are not these countries of the southern seas volcanic?" (AM-016-040)
