# Alcide Jolivet

## Overview

Alcide Jolivet is a French war correspondent covering the Tartar invasion — "The French correspondent was named Alcide Jolivet" (MS-002-039). Light of heart and quick of tongue, he greets even a doubtful assignment with a shrug: "'Even should it be only a wildgoose chase... it may be worth powder and shot'" (MS-002-041).

## Role in the Story

Jolivet's running device is a fictitious relative to whom he wires his news — "'my cousin Madeleine'" (MS-002-047) — and he takes visible pride in his speed, noting that "'my last telegram reached Udinsk,' observed Alcide Jolivet, with some satisfaction" (MS-002-057).

On the battlefield the two reporters become "no longer traveling companions, but rivals, enemies" (MS-018-034). When Blount monopolizes the telegraph wicket, Jolivet is left "anxious to send off his dispatch, addressed to his cousin" (MS-018-045).

Fuming, he "would have liked to strangle the honorable correspondent of the Daily Telegraph" (MS-018-054), and takes his revenge, as Blount "heard Jolivet completing his telegram by singing in a mocking tone" (MS-018-061).

## Character & Traits

Beneath the banter lies both a shrewd newsman and a brave one. His droll creed is that "'to move others, one must be moved one's self!'" (MS-030-010). When wolves assail the raft, "Neither were Jolivet and Blount idle, but fought bravely with the brutes" (MS-030-025), and he "threw himself into the midst of the fierce beasts" beside his old rival (MS-030-027).

## Relationships

- Harry Blount — the English correspondent of the Daily Telegraph, by turns his companion and his fiercest professional rival (MS-018-034).
- [[Michel Strogoff]] — the Czar's courier alongside whom Jolivet creeps forward on the perilous road: "Michael had crept forward; Jolivet followed" (MS-030-037).
