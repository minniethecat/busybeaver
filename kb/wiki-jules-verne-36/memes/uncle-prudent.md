# Uncle Prudent

## Overview

He is "the famous Uncle Prudent, Prudent being his family name" (RC-002-018), president of Philadelphia's ballooning club and "a personage of consideration, and in spite of his name... well known for his audacity" (RC-002-019).

His wealth shapes his standing: "Uncle Prudent was rich, and therefore he had friends, as was natural; but he also had enemies" (RC-002-020), not least the club's secretary, whose rivalry runs beneath every meeting.

## Role in the Story

As president he wields the gavel — or rather "turned on the steam whistle, which did duty for the presidential bell" (RC-003-018) — and it is his money that funds the Institute's great balloon, paid over as "the last installment of the hundred thousand paper dollars he had paid for his invention" (RC-003-008).

Carried off aboard the Albatross, he bends every thought to flight: "Uncle Prudent and Phil Evans had quite made up their minds to escape" (RC-011-002), plotting for the moment the aeronef "may drop to within a few hundred feet of the ground" (RC-013-042).

When the cyclone strikes he watches almost hopefully, wondering "if the cyclone was not playing their game in destroying the aeronef and with her the inventor--and with the inventor the secret of his invention" (RC-018-013).

## Character & Traits

His temper is famously short and hot, the perfect foil to his cool rival — "Uncle Prudent was furiously hot; Phil Evans was abnormally cool" (RC-002-021).

His pride in the cause admits no rival craft: his hatred of the man who steals the sky boils over until, "in a sudden outburst of fury, he began to rave and stamp on the sonorous planks, while his hands sought to strangle an imaginary Robur" (RC-006-052).

Even so his presidency was won by the narrowest chance, leaving "Phil Evans... only secretary of the Weldon Institute, whereas Uncle Prudent was president" (RC-002-032) — and a latent rivalry between them.

## Relationships

- [[Robur]] — the aviator who abducts him, and the embodiment of the heavier-than-air flight he despises.
- Phil Evans — his club rival turned fellow captive, cool where he is hot, and his partner in every escape scheme.

## Appearances

- [[Robur the Conqueror]] — as the balloonist president of the Weldon Institute, seized by Robur and borne around the world aboard the Albatross.
