# The Whale Hunt Disaster

> **Note:** Inline PN citations for this page use a 4-character work code (`DSCF`); they render as plain text pending RFC-0003. The underlying PN data is valid.

## Overview

The Whale Hunt Disaster is the pivotal catastrophe of *[[dick-sand-a-captain-at-fifteen|Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen]]*, the loss that thrusts a boy into command of a ship at sea.

Sighting a jubarte, the master of the Pilgrim resolves to take it. Captain Hull at once made his preparations for capturing the jubarte (DSCF-007-007).

Because his crew can arm only a single whale-boat, Hull was forced to put on Dick Sand the care of guarding the "Pilgrim" (DSCF-007-012), and rows off with every able seaman.

## What Happens

The hunt turns savage when the whale is wounded. The jubarte, after having been struck by the harpoon, was almost entirely overturned on the side, thus discovering a young whale, which she was in process of suckling (DSCF-008-039).

Enraged, the mother lashes the sea. She struck the water so roughly with her formidable tail, that an enormous wave arose, as if the sea were suddenly opened (DSCF-008-083).

Then comes the fatal stroke. A terrible blow from the monster's tail had just struck the whale-boat underneath (DSCF-008-108).

When help arrives it is far too late. A quarter of an hour after, when Dick Sand, followed by the blacks, had reached the scene of the catastrophe, every living creature had disappeared (DSCF-008-112).

## Significance

The sea yields nothing back. Captain Hull and his men had forever disappeared (DSCF-009-002), and the boy left aboard must reckon with the enormity of the loss: all his crew have perished with him (DSCF-009-035).

The disaster is the hinge of the whole novel, for it strips the Pilgrim of every grown sailor and leaves the fifteen-year-old apprentice Dick Sand as her sole captain, bound for a voyage that will carry the survivors into far graver perils.

## Participants & Setting

- The destruction of the Pilgrim's whale-boat and the death of its whole crew, at sea off the schooner [[dick-sand-a-captain-at-fifteen|Pilgrim]].
- Costing the lives of Captain Hull and his sailors, and raising the apprentice Dick Sand to command.
- Chronicled in *[[dick-sand-a-captain-at-fifteen|Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen]]*.
