# The Death of Master Zacharius

## Overview

The Death of Master Zacharius is the damnation of the proud Genevan watchmaker who believed his soul bound to his last clock—at the château of Andernatt the clock bursts, the diabolical Pittonaccio seizes its escaping spring, and Zacharius falls dead—in *[[master-zacharius|Master Zacharius]]*.

His life was in the clock. Zacharius declared that in that clock, the last of all which had gone from his hands, his life was shut up, and that the man who held it could break it and plunge him into chaos (MZ-005-081).

## What Happens

He wound it in a frenzy. Seizing the long key that resembled an uncoiled snake, Master Zacharius ran to the clock and wound it with fantastic rapidity, winding on until he fell from sheer weariness (MZ-005-095).

The clock quickened toward its end. As the hour neared, the hands slipped over the face of the clock with the hiss of a serpent, and the pendulum beat with accelerated strokes (MZ-005-106).

Then he collapsed. Master Zacharius spoke no more; he had fallen to the floor, his throat rattling, and from his oppressed bosom came only the half-broken words, "Life—science!" (MZ-005-107).

The verdict was damnation. At his terrible cry these words appeared: whoever shall attempt to make himself the equal of God shall be for ever damned (MZ-005-114).

## Significance

The clock destroyed him. The old clock burst with a noise like thunder, and the spring, escaping, leaped across the hall in a thousand fantastic contortions while the old man ran after it in vain (MZ-005-115).

The devil claimed his due. At last Pittonaccio seized the spring, and, uttering a horrible blasphemy, engulfed himself in the earth (MZ-005-117).

The watchmaker fell. Master Zacharius fell backwards; he was dead (MZ-005-118).

He was buried in the mountains. The old watchmaker was buried in the midst of the peaks of Andernatt (MZ-005-120).

## Participants & Setting

- Master Zacharius, the great watchmaker of Geneva whose pride had bound his life to his clocks, meets his end at the château of Andernatt among the peaks of the Valais.
- His daughter Gerande and her betrothed Aubert Thün, with the hermit of Notre-Dame-du-Sex and old Scholastique, witness the ruin the diabolical Pittonaccio has engineered.
- Pittonaccio, who bargained for Gerande's hand in exchange for the clock, seizes the escaping spring and vanishes into the earth; chronicled in *[[master-zacharius|Master Zacharius]]*.
