# J. T. Maston

## Overview

J. T. Maston is "a distinguished member and perpetual secretary of the Gun Club" (FEM-001-012), the most fervent of its artillery enthusiasts.

Maimed by his own trade, he is "the famous J.T. Maston, scratching his gutta-percha cranium with his steel hook" (FEM-001-023) — a war wreck who wears his mutilations as badges.

## Role in the Story

When Barbicane forms the executive committee for the great cannon, it is "the inevitable J.T. Maston, to whom were confided the functions of secretary" (FEM-007-003). At the first sitting, "J.T. Maston immediately screwed his pen on to his steel hook and the business began" (FEM-007-004).

His authority in the club is earned: he "interrupted the president, and was heard with the attention which his magnificent past career deserved" (FEM-007-011), and Barbicane defers to him as "our clever calculator, Mr. Maston," to settle a point of figures (FEM-007-069).

He follows the enterprise at every stage, leaving Baltimore "accompanied by J.T. Maston, Major Elphinstone, and the manager of the Goldspring Manufactory" (FEM-013-003). At the foundry he appoints himself guide: "J.T. Maston constituted himself their cicerone... and he forced them to visit the 1,200 furnaces one after the other" (FEM-015-015).

## Character & Traits

His fanaticism for ordnance is total. A relic of his handiwork holds pride of place in the club — "a piece of breech, broken and twisted under the effort of the powder--a precious fragment of J.T. Maston's cannon" (FEM-002-006).

He craves war for its own sake, threatening that if denied a battlefield trial, "I shall send in my resignation as member of the Gun Club, and I shall go and bury myself in the backwoods of Arkansas" (FEM-001-047). The audacious moon-shot delights him utterly, "delighted with the idea of sending an aluminium bullet to the Selenites" (FEM-007-097).

## Relationships

- [[Impey Barbicane]] — the Gun Club president whom Maston serves and adores; Barbicane checks his ardour directly, "Maston," said Barbicane... "I agree with you" (FEM-011-015).
- [[Michel Ardan]] — his companion in the desperate search to prevent the duel; Ardan understands "his wanting to be killed for the man he loves" (FEM-021-065).
- Captain Nicholl — Barbicane's rival, whose threatened bullets Maston rushes to forestall.
