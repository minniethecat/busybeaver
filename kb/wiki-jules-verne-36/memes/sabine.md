# Sabine

## Overview

He is one of the expedition's two chief hunters, for "the chief hunters of the expedition were the soldiers Marbre and Sabine, both very expert at their business" (FC-006-024).

He is counted among the skilful few, since of the ten chosen soldiers "two--Marbre and Sabine--were skilful hunters" (FC-013-004).

## Role in the Story

He masters the sportsman's craft, being "skilled in all the artifices which sportsmen employ in stalking their prey--particularly the reindeer, which are exceedingly wary" (FC-014-014).

He gathers the fort's pelts, and after a kill "Marbre and Sabine taking immediate possession, carried off their skins to be subsequently prepared" (FC-006-057).

He keeps the larder stocked through the seasons, when "Sabine and Marbre killed a good many Polar hares" (FC-020-002).

He is a man the commander leans on, for Hobson "consulted Sergeant Long, Mac-Nab, Rae, Marbre, and Sabine, in whom he had great confidence" (FC-043-030).

## Character & Traits

He prides himself on his speech, replying "in his usual sententious manner" and "much delighted with the elegant way in which he had rounded his sentence" (FC-014-026).

He has a colourful turn of phrase, saying of the martens that it was well "to let them 'ripen,' or, in other words,--to wait for the cold to bleach them" (FC-016-017).

He meets hardship with good humour, remarking "we won't grumble at that, bears' steaks are as good as reindeers', and we get the fur in!" (FC-019-012).

He takes his sport seriously, put "beside himself with rage" that a fox's "subterfuge was unworthy of a respectable fox" (FC-019-015).

He is sure of his eye, insisting "no, no, sir; Marbre and I are not mistaken" when his game was doubted (FC-006-032).

## Relationships

- [[Jaspar Hobson]] — the Lieutenant who commands the hunters and once "wisely forbidden all hunting" on the march to spare valuable game (FC-011-019).
- Marbre — his fellow chief hunter, forever paired with him, the two together "looked forward to make up for lost time" in the winter trapping (FC-016-020).

## Appearances

- [[The Fur Country]] — the Arctic tale in which he hunts for the wandering colony and nearly perishes when "the hunter Sabine left the house in the thick fog, and was nearly drowned" (FC-043-011).
