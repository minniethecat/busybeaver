# Captain Grant

## Overview

Harry Grant is the Scottish captain of the lost ship Britannia, whose bottled message names him: "May 30, 1862, Peru-Callao, with cargo for Glasgow, the BRITANNIA, Captain Grant" (SC-002-072). Lord Glenarvan reads the salvaged document and knows the man at once — "It is just that same Captain Grant" (SC-002-075).

He is drawn as a hardy seaman and widowed father: "Captain Grant was a fearless sailor" (SC-004-003), who "lost his wife when Robert was born, and during his long voyages he left his little ones in charge of his cousin, a good old lady" (SC-004-003).

## Role in the Story

The recovered document tells that "two sailors and Captain Grant are about to land on the continent, where they will be taken prisoners" (SC-002-079). Lady Helena recalls how "my husband determined to lay the document before the Lords of the Admiralty, to induce them to send out a ship immediately in search of Captain Grant" (SC-003-037).

When the Admiralty refuses, Glenarvan resolves the quest himself — "Let us go, Edward; let us start off and search for Captain Grant!" (SC-004-044).

The trail finally leads to New Zealand, where "the three-mast vessel BRITANNIA, of Glasgow, has foundered ... on the coast of New Zealand" (SC-063-101).

The castaway is found alive on Maria Theresa island as his son's cry rings out — "my father is there!" (SC-064-070) — and there he at last confronts his betrayer: "Ayrton was brought up on the poop, and found himself face to face with Harry Grant" (SC-065-068).

## Character & Traits

His children hold him a man of unbreakable will — "A man like my father doesn't die till he has finished his work" (SC-064-043) — and Robert vows his own life "to search, and never cease searching for my father, who would never have given us up" (SC-064-039).

Grant is also a patriot with a founder's dream, remembered for "the project ... which made you so popular in our old country" (SC-065-019), a design Lady Helena calls "a grand project of yours, and worthy of a noble heart" (SC-065-021).

## Relationships

- [[Mary Grant]] — his devoted daughter, who with her brother Robert Grant is left "condemned to orphanage by the cruel Admiralty" until Glenarvan takes up the search.
- [[Lord Glenarvan]] — the Scottish nobleman who reads the bottled document and mounts the expedition to find him (SC-002-075).
- [[Ayrton]] — the treacherous quartermaster of the Britannia, brought at last "face to face with Harry Grant" (SC-065-068).
