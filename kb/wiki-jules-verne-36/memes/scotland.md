## Overview

Scotland is the Highland homeland of Lord Glenarvan, a true Scotchman at heart who keeps his ancestral county open to progress yet acts always for the honour of Scotland. (SC-003-001) It is from here that the search of *In Search of the Castaways* sets out, for it is a devoted son of Scotland — Captain Grant — whom all Scotland would unite to save. (SC-023-068)

## In the Narrative

Grant himself is the adventurous Scotchman who attempted to found a new Scotland on the shores of the Pacific. (SC-002-073) He lived at Dundee, in Perthshire, Scotland (SC-004-003), and it was there he projected the grand scheme that made him popular in Scotland. (SC-004-005)

When Paganel joins the enterprise, Glenarvan tells him that by taking part he will be linking France with Scotland. (SC-008-085)

## Geography & Features

The Tweed, several fathoms wide, digs a deeper trench between Scotland and England than the twenty leagues of the Irish Channel that part Old Caledonia from the Emerald Isle. (SC-032-036)

On the far side of the world a valley reminds Glenarvan of the glens in the lowlands of Scotland (SC-032-020), and a feast recalls the grand dining-hall of Malcolm Castle, in the heart of the Highlands of Scotland. (SC-036-024)

In the reckoning of empire, Scotland stands beside England, Ireland, Malta, and the Hebrides among British possessions. (SC-038-084)
