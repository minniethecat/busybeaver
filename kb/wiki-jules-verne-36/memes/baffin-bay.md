# Baffin Bay

## Overview

Baffin Bay — called "Baffin's Sea" throughout *The Adventures of Captain Hatteras* — is the real ice-choked Arctic sea lying between Greenland and Baffin Island, the gateway through which the brig *Forward* must pass on her secret voyage north. The sailing orders direct the ship to "cross Davis's Straits, and sail up Baffin's Sea to Melville Bay." (ACH-004-043)

It is the first great trial of ice the expedition faces.

## In the Narrative

Once past the straits the brig presses on: "the wind veered round to the south-east, and the brig... sailed up Baffin's Sea." (ACH-009-002) The doctor fears the fate of earlier ships, "to be caught the very first year by the ice in Baffin's Sea, and have to winter in the midst of the icebergs." (ACH-005-015)

A drifting floe seized by the *Forward* had "got clear of the obstacles in that long suite of straits which lead out of Baffin's Bay." (ACH-006-020)

## Geography & Features

The sea's ice keeps to its western side — a stray floe was "drifting from the eastern coast of Greenland more than from Davis's Straits, for ice generally keeps to the west coast of Baffin's Sea." (ACH-006-004) Its waters carry the boreal current the doctor is so keen to study.

Its exploration is bound up with the great names of Arctic discovery: Captain James Ross "in 1818, rounded the coasts of Baffin's Sea, and corrected the hydrographic errors of his predecessors," (ACH-006-030) while Sir John Franklin "penetrated into Baffin's Sea" on his lost voyage. (ACH-006-032)
