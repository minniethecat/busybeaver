# Serpentine Peninsula

## Overview

The Serpentine Peninsula is the long, wooded arm of land projecting from the south-west of Lincoln Island in *The Mysterious Island*. Naming the island's features, the reporter Gideon Spilett proposes the name "Serpentine Peninsula, and that of Reptile-end to the bent tail which terminates it, for it is just like a reptile's tail." (MI-011-063)

Its dense woods are christened in the same survey: "all the masses of impenetrable wood which covered the Serpentine Peninsula were named the forests of the Far West." (MI-011-075)

## In the Narrative

The peninsula's forests are the last unexplored quarter of the colonists' domain. It becomes "of much importance that they should penetrate into all the yet unknown parts of Lincoln Island... to the extremity of the Serpentine Peninsula." (MI-022-060)

Harding insists on a full survey, taking "nothing for granted until he had explored the coast to the very extremity of the Serpentine Peninsula." (MI-026-036)

Its wooded shore offers what the barren coasts cannot: "this coast, that is, all the Serpentine Peninsula, that was to be explored, for this part of the shore offered a refuge to castaways, which the other wild and barren side must have refused." (MI-026-035)

## Geography & Features

The peninsula forms the south-western horn of the island, terminating in the promontory of Reptile End. Even from its tip the coast beyond is hidden: "from the extremity of the Serpentine Peninsula Claw Cape could not be seen." (MI-027-003) When the settlers march out, "there were still two miles between the settlers and the extremity of the Serpentine Peninsula." (MI-026-041)

Cloaked in the forests of the Far West, it long shelters wild beasts and, later, the escaped convicts who traverse "the double shore of the Serpentine Peninsula" on their way to Falls River. (MI-050-002)
