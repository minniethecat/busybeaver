# Paganel

## Overview

Jacques Paganel is the geographer of the rescue expedition in *[[In Search of the Castaways]]*. Secretary of the Geographical Society of Paris and corresponding member of learned societies across Europe and beyond (SC-006-075), he joins the voyage of the *Duncan* entirely by accident.

Absent-minded to a fault, the French traveler had mistaken his vessel and gone aboard while the crew were at church, meaning to sail for India but finding himself bound for South America (SC-007-007). Yet he declares himself only too happy to have made a mistake that turned out so agreeably (SC-007-056).

## Role in the Story

Paganel throws himself into the search for Captain Grant, counting himself among the expedition's natural leaders beside Lord Glenarvan and the Major (SC-010-049). His maps are never far from hand — when he finds a stream unmarked, all the fire of a geographer burns in his veins (SC-011-011).

His learning drives the plot as much as his blunders do. Having studied the wrong language — Spanish in place of Portuguese, so alike that he mistook them (SC-015-055) — he later re-reads the castaways' document and realizes the party has followed a wrong track, reading there what was never written (SC-024-002).

This correction redirects the entire search. Philosophical about his errors, Paganel holds that it is only human to make a mistake, while to persist in one a man must be a fool (SC-027-051).

## Character & Traits

Vain of his expertise and highly incensed whenever his geographical knowledge is questioned or jested about (SC-017-036), Paganel is nonetheless genuinely erudite — Lady Helena grants that he is thoroughly posted up in geographical questions (SC-035-027).

His devotion to his instruments is total: he will spend half a night arranging, wiping, and polishing the lenses of his telescope (SC-034-051).

## Relationships

- Lord Glenarvan — leader of the expedition, whose *Duncan* Paganel boards by mistake and whose search for Captain Grant he joins (SC-007-056).
- Major McNabbs — the dry-humored companion who teases Paganel over his geographical slips (SC-017-036).
- Lady Helena — Glenarvan's wife, who good-naturedly praises the geographer's learning (SC-035-027).

## Appearances
