## Overview

Tibet is the high frontier land skirted by the Grand Transasiatic Railway in *The Adventures of a Special Correspondent* — a country that, the New Review writers said, will only be great when the Muscovite administration have laid hands on Tibet. (ASC-016-002)

## In the Narrative

The railway measures itself against its walls. Had the engineers had to attack the enormous chain of the Kuen Lun, Nan Chan, and Gangar Oola which forms the frontier of Tibet, it would have taken a century to surmount them. (ASC-018-036)

The line crosses the route of Gabriel Bonvalot and Prince Henri of Orleans across Tibet in 1889-90, a much more complete journey than the narrator's. (ASC-022-007)

## Geography & Features

On the right hand the eye never tires of the distant horizon of mountains which bounds the tableland of Tibet to the north. (ASC-018-038) The road from Kothan to Lob-Nor runs at the foot of the Kuen Lun, which divides Chinese Turkestan from Tibet. (ASC-018-041)

Robur's *Albatross* saw it whole from the air — Tibet consists of high table-lands without vegetation, with snowy peaks, barren ravines, and torrents fed by glaciers. (RC-012-012) It passed over Garlock, a town of western Tibet, the capital of the province of Cari Khorsum. (RC-012-015)
