## Overview

*A Journey to the Center of the Earth* is Jules Verne's subterranean adventure, narrated by young [[Axel]], nephew of the impetuous geologist [[professor-lidenbrock|Professor Liedenbrock]]. It opens as "my uncle, [[professor-lidenbrock|Professor Liedenbrock]], rushed into his little house, No. 19 Königstrasse" (JCE-001-002) with a discovery that will send them underground.

That discovery is a runic cipher decoded in [[Deciphering the Saknussemm Cipher]], directing a bold traveller to descend a volcano in [[Iceland]].

## Plot Summary

The cipher's message is a command: "Descend, bold traveller, into the crater of the jokul of [[snaefellsjokull|Sneffels]], which the shadow of Scartaris touches before the kalends of July... and thou wilt attain the centre of the earth" (JCE-005-050).

[[professor-lidenbrock|Liedenbrock]] resolves at once to "Penetrate to the centre of the earth!" (JCE-006-002). With [[axel|Axel]] and the [[iceland|Iceland]]ic guide Hans, he descends the crater into a vast subterranean world of caverns, a great sea, and prehistoric life, before a volcanic eruption hurls the party back to the surface.

## Main Characters

- [[Axel]] — the narrator and reluctant nephew
- [[professor-lidenbrock|Professor Liedenbrock]] — the headstrong geologist who leads the descent
- Hans — the steadfast [[iceland|Iceland]]ic guide

## Key Places & Technology

- [[Iceland]] — where the descent into [[snaefellsjokull|Sneffels]] begins
- [[Deciphering the Saknussemm Cipher]] — the coded message that launches the journey

## Themes

The novel dramatizes the heroic geology of Verne's age, plunging its characters through the strata of the earth into a lost prehistoric world. It sets youthful caution against scientific obsession, with [[axel|Axel]]'s fear balanced by [[professor-lidenbrock|Liedenbrock]]'s unstoppable will to know.
