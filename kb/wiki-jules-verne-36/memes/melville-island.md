# Melville Island

## Overview

Melville Island is a real island of the Canadian Arctic, invoked throughout *The Adventures of Captain Hatteras* as a benchmark of polar discovery. It was there that Parry, "in 1819 and 1820, the celebrated Parry passed through Lancaster Straits, and penetrated, in spite of unnumbered difficulties, as far as Melville Island," winning the Parliamentary prize for crossing the hundred and seventeenth meridian above the seventy-seventh parallel. (ACH-006-030)

## In the Narrative

Doctor Clawbonny recounts the island's place in exploration lore: McClintock "pushed on as far as Melville Island and Cape Dundas, the extreme points attained by Parry in 1819." (ACH-014-027) James Ross "tried to reach Melville Island by a more westerly direction, but he nearly lost his two vessels." (ACH-014-025) Later, Kellett pushed "on to Bridport in Melville Island, explored, without success, that part of the boreal land." (ACH-014-030)

McClure "set out on a sledge, and reached Melville Island, hoping to fall in with some ship or other," finding only a cairn at Winter Harbour. (ACH-047-029)

## Geography & Features

The island is a byword for extreme cold and abundant game. Clawbonny notes the lowest recorded Arctic temperature "has been 61° at Melville Island" (ACH-041-043) and recalls that "when Parry wintered there he always had as much game as he wanted." (ACH-025-011) When Parry wintered there "he started both amusements among his men, and they met with great success." (ACH-042-022) Its musk-oxen set the standard the Forward's crew hoped to match. (ACH-048-013)
