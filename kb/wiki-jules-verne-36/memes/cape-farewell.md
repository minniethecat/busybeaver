# Cape Farewell

## Overview

Cape Farewell is the real southern point of Greenland, the first great landmark set for the *Forward* on her mysterious northern voyage. The sealed sailing order is blunt: "Sail for Cape Farewell." (ACH-004-043)

To round it on time is the crew's first test, the gateway to the ice-choked seas beyond.

## In the Narrative

Reaching it is a matter of schedule and danger: "if we wish to be in sight of Cape Farewell on the 20th of April we must put the steam on, or we shall be thrown on the coasts of Labrador." (ACH-005-064) The brig presses on — "the *Forward*, under her topsails and foresails, glided on towards Cape Farewell." (ACH-006-003)

At last, "under a temperature which at noon was only 12 degrees, under a snowy and foggy sky, Cape Farewell was perceived." (ACH-006-026) It is the first of the empty waypoints the doubters throw at Shandon: "Nothing at Cape Farewell, nothing at Disko Island, nothing at Uppernawik." (ACH-010-009)

## Geography & Features

The cape marks the turning point from the Atlantic approaches into the Arctic proper, standing between the open ocean and "the coasts of Labrador" to the west. (ACH-005-064) It is a threshold of desolation — one of those "wretched solitudes" of "capes, promontories, and bays" that chill the old sailor's heart. (ACH-007-010)
