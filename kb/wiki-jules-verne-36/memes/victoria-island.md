# Victoria Island

## Overview

Victoria Island is the vast, wandering island of ice at the heart of *The Fur Country*. It is not solid land but a frozen floe that had built up in the calm waters along the North American coast, disguised beneath soil and vegetation as the promontory of [[Cape Bathurst]]. (FC-027-017)

When an earthquake severs it from the continent, the island carries Lieutenant Hobson's fort and its inhabitants adrift across the Arctic Ocean, its survival threatened by every mile of its slow, uncontrollable drift.

## In the Narrative

The colonists of Fort Hope kept the promontory's original name of Victoria Island even after learning the terrible truth of its nature. (FC-025-003) Hobson calculated that the island was moving along with the current, parallel with the coast, at an average rate of ten miles a day. (FC-025-063)

The Lieutenant did not conceal the danger. "Nothing now connects it with a continent," he told Mrs Barnett, "and it is doomed to destruction." (FC-026-031) The travellers reached the abrupt angle where the island had broken off, once the connecting-link between Walruses' Bay and Washburn Bay. (FC-026-059)

The gravest fear was that the currents would drive Victoria Island into the warmer western waters of the Pacific, where it would gradually melt and go to pieces beneath the feet of its inhabitants. (FC-025-011) The colonists' survival becomes a race against the slow thaw of the ground they stand on.

## Geography & Features

Victoria Island's foundation is a single great floe, formed in the still coastal waters of North America and topped with enough earth and forest to pass for firm land. (FC-027-017) It broke away in the earthquake of the 8th January, which severed the connecting isthmus and launched the island upon the wide ocean. (FC-026-061)

Because it is ice, the island is impermanent by nature: subject to melting, splitting, and eventual dissolution. Its drift is governed entirely by Arctic currents, giving its human passengers no means of steering their runaway home. Whether it would be soldered fast to the winter ice-field or swept south to destruction remained, for most of the novel, undecided.
