# Kinko

## Overview

He travels the length of Asia as a stowaway who "had only money enough to buy a packing case, a few provisions, and get myself sent off by an obliging friend" (ASC-013-078).

His love drives the ruse, for as the narrator marvels, what would one not do for a sweetheart "who consents to shut himself up in a box for a fortnight, and arrives labelled 'Glass,' 'Fragile'" (ASC-013-085).

## Role in the Story

Once the train is made up, "the baggage van--with Kinko in--is at the head of the train," carrying its hidden passenger onward (ASC-015-003).

At the frontier his fate hangs by a thread: when "the custom-house officers are about to visit it," the narrator confesses, "I tremble for poor Kinko" (ASC-016-060).

Yet the young man endures the confinement with ease, and "a certain snoring proved that Kinko was inside as usual, and sleeping peacefully" (ASC-017-035).

Through the long nights his only lifeline is his confederate, who "was able to communicate with Kinko, to take him some provisions and to have a few minutes' conversation with him" (ASC-023-017).

## Character & Traits

When the crisis comes he is transformed, for "Kinko, energetic and resolute, is as cool as a cucumber" amid the runaway locomotive (ASC-024-078).

He does not flinch from the desperate remedy, ordering the narrator to "shut down the safety valves, and blow up the engine" to stop the train (ASC-024-082).

His courage is total and final: "Kinko has paid with his life for the safety of his fellow passengers" (ASC-025-004).

## Relationships
