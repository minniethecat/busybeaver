# Torres Strait

## Overview

The Torres Strait is the real passage between Australia and New Guinea through which Captain Nemo takes the *Nautilus* in *Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Seas*. Nemo announces his intent: "he intended to reach the Indian Ocean via the Torres Strait." (TTLU-020-020)

Aronnax dreads it — "The Torres Strait is regarded as no less dangerous for its bristling reefs than for the savage inhabitants of its coasts." (TTLU-020-021)

## In the Narrative

The submarine enters the most feared of passages, "a passageway that even the boldest navigators hesitated to clear." (TTLU-020-024) There it strikes bottom, an event Aronnax recalls among his voyage's ordeals: "our running aground in the Torres Strait." (TTLU-039-005)

Nemo predicts the exact moment of release: "tomorrow at 2:40 in the afternoon, the *Nautilus* will float off and exit the Torres Strait undamaged." (TTLU-022-105) The tide obeys, and "safe and sound, it left behind the dangerous narrows of the Torres Strait." (TTLU-022-138)

## Geography & Features

The strait is broad but nearly impassable. "The Torres Strait is about thirty-four leagues wide, but it's obstructed by an incalculable number of islands, islets, breakers, and rocks that make it nearly impossible to navigate." (TTLU-020-025)

It bears the name of its discoverer, "Luis Vaez de Torres," who "faced [it] on returning from the South Seas in Melanesia," where later "sloops of war under Captain Dumont d'Urville ran aground in 1840." (TTLU-020-024) Its tides run stronger than the Pacific's, "a meter-and-a-half difference in level between high and low seas." (TTLU-020-045)
