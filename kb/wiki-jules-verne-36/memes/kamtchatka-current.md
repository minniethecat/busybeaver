## Overview

The Kamtchatka Current is one of the great streams of the northern seas. (FC-025-025) Running from east to west at about a hundred miles at most from the coast, it comes into collision with the current at the opening of the strait, then turns to the south and approaches the shores of [[Russian America]]. (FC-025-026) It is well known to run along the coast of Asia. (FC-040-015)

## In the Narrative

Adrift upon their island, the travellers saw but one current from the offing of [[Cape Bathurst]] bearing directly to the north-west, and that was the dangerous Kamtchatka Current. (FC-025-033)

The situation grew more perilous as the island drew daily nearer to the dangerous Kamtchatka Current, which, once it seized the island in its rapid waters, might carry it far away to the north. (FC-031-024)

At last the island was finally in the grasp of the Kamtchatka Current, drifting towards the unknown latitudes where the great icebergs come into being, on its way to the vast solitudes of the [[Arctic Ocean]]. (FC-033-026) Following its decided course, the island would quickly pass the seventieth degree beyond Cape Bathurst. (FC-033-032)

## Geography & Features

At the strait the drifting mass might follow the Kamtchatka Current to the deserted regions of the north-west, or float down with the Behring Current to be swallowed up in the south. (FC-025-031) The two streams ran contrary ways, for although the Kamtchatka Current runs northward, the ice in the thaw always goes down the Behring Current. (FC-038-007)

Hobson feared the Kamtchatka Current might sweep the island to the north before it could come under the influence of the Behring Current. (FC-039-014) The safest refuge lay between the Kamtchatka Current and the coast, perhaps in some vast eddy unmarked upon the map. (FC-025-038)

## Connections

The Kamtchatka Current was the unseen hand that governed the wanderers fate. It was the Kamtchatka Current which had brought them where they were, and which might seize them again when the thaw set in and carry them yet farther. (FC-038-006)

When the island stirred beneath them, they asked whether it was the Kamtchatka Current which produced the motion. (FC-039-027) Hobson dreaded that the worn island might yet be carried to the Pacific, or seized once more by the Kamtchatka Current. (FC-035-009)
