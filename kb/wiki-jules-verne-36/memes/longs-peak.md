## Overview

Long's Peak is a summit of the Rocky Mountains, described as one of the highest of the range. (TT-004-006) Wishing that its telescope, like the Columbiad, should stand within the States of the Union, the Gun Club settled on the Rockies, and all the necessary material was sent up to the summit of Long's Peak. (FEM-024-022)

## In the Narrative

When the projectile could not be found, watchers resolved to await the telegrams from Long's Peak. (FEM-027-008) J.T. Maston, in despair, set out for the peak himself. (FEM-027-016) At length the projectile was perceived, thanks to the gigantic reflector. (FEM-028-002) Once the notice from Long's Peak was made known, there spread a feeling of surprise and horror. (FEM-028-016)

## Geography & Features

The instrument raised there was a telescope of extraordinary power: set up at Long's Peak, it magnified forty-eight thousand times and brought the moon to within a very short distance. (RM-010-004) The station lay high in the Rocky Mountains, where an observer was posted trying to discover the invisible bullet gravitating in space. (RM-007-048)

## Connections

Long's Peak became the fixed post of the Gun Club's secretary: J.T. Maston's domicile was henceforth the station, his horizon the immense reflector. (FEM-028-018) So established was the place that a message could be addressed simply to the Honourable J.T. Maston, Long's Peak, Rocky Mountains. (RM-021-025) From here Maston and Belfast announced the projectile had been perceived. (RM-021-032)
