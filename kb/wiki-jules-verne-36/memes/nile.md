## Overview

The Nile is the great African river whose sources open the geographic problem of *Five Weeks in a Balloon* — in other days, to seek the sources of the Nile was regarded as a mad endeavor, a chimera that could not be realized. (FWB-002-003)

## In the Narrative

Its source is the prize the balloon voyage means to settle from above, where the ground has failed. The various expeditions that had ascended the Nile could never manage to reach the mysterious source of that river. (FWB-004-016)

The doctor traces its course as a guide across the continent — the instruction is simply to ascend the course of the Nile. (FWB-005-026) A northward stream, he reasons, must necessarily join the Nile, if it be not the Nile itself. (FWB-005-043)

## Geography & Features

Its waters rise among the great lakes: the latest expedition, sent by way of the great lakes to explore the sources of the Nile, had just set out. (FWB-004-003)

The discovery weighs on the whole enterprise — the travellers debate whether this discovery of the sources of the Nile was likely to be of any use. (FWB-005-018)
