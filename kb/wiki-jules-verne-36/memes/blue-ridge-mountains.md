# Blue Ridge Mountains

## Overview

The Blue Ridge Mountains are the Appalachian range of the Carolinas where the strange events of *The Master of the World* begin. It is among their summits that the crowning peak rises: "there, deep amid the Blueridge Mountains rises the crest called the Great Eyrie." (MW-001-004)

## In the Narrative

The range is the first theatre of the alarm. The investigator is asked whether he knows "what has happened down in the Blueridge Mountains near Morganton," (MW-002-009) where fires and rumblings on the heights raise fear of a volcano.

The chief skeptic doubts the cause: "it seems to me highly improbable that an active volcano exists in the Blueridge mountain chain." (MW-002-025) Yet the danger is real enough that "only the inhabitants of the Blueridge region had been in danger from an eruption or possible earthquake at Great Eyrie." (MW-006-009)

## Geography & Features

The chain is high and well timbered. On its upper reaches "a wind from the west sweeping over the long chain of the Blueridge, set the pines and hemlocks wailing on the higher slopes." (MW-001-022) Its peaks draw climbers: local guides are "in constant demand among the tourists who climbed the peaks of the Blueridge and Cumberland Mountains." (MW-003-002)

From the approach the whole range unfolds: "in the afternoon the whole chain of the Blueridge stretched before us at a distance of only six miles." (MW-003-015)
