## Overview

The Thames is the great river of [[London]], famous for its traffic. Not the Strand with its extraordinary traffic, nor a Thames with its prodigious movement of barges and steamboats, was wanting to the city's stir. (ASC-024-007)

## In the Narrative

In [[Five Weeks in a Balloon]] the Thames carries the Resolute to sea. The captain leaped in with his officers and passengers, and the rapid current of the Thames, aiding the strong arms of the rowers, bore them swiftly to Greenwich. (FWB-008-014) The next morning the Resolute, driven by her screw, began to plough the water toward the mouth of the Thames. (FWB-008-015)

## Geography & Features

The Thames can freeze in a hard season. In the memorable winter of 1789 the Thames was frozen as far as Gravesend. (ACH-041-041) Below [[London]] it broadens toward its estuary and the sea.

## Connections

The Thames lends its name to the Royal Thames Yacht Club, so famous throughout the United Kingdom, of which Lord Glenarvan was a distinguished member. (SC-001-001) It runs down past [[London]] to the mouth from which the Resolute put out. (FWB-008-015)
