## Overview

Gallia is the small new world on which the castaways of Off on a Comet are borne through space — a piece of the globe torn away and set in its own orbit.

When a mysterious paper is found, its author is judged to have christened the body: Gallia is the name assigned to it, if a new little planet has been formed by disintegration from the old. (OC-016-038) The travellers adopt the name for their strange home.

## In the Narrative

The document that names it also charts it. A line apparently registers the distance traversed by Gallia, the new little planet, in her own orbit. (OC-016-045) Reasoning it through, Servadac concludes that they must be the population of a young little world called Gallia. (OC-016-051)

The name sticks. Almost unconsciously, the voyagers in the Dobryna fell into the habit of using Gallia as the name of the new world in which they were making an extraordinary excursion through space. (OC-017-001) Observation confirms its course — the inference was irresistible that Gallia was receding from the sun, and travelling far away across the planetary regions. (OC-017-007)

## Geography & Features

A comet-borne fragment carrying a scrap of Mediterranean coast and sea, Gallia is infinitesimally small beside the great planets, wheeling on an elongated orbit that carries its handful of survivors far from the sun and slowly back again.
