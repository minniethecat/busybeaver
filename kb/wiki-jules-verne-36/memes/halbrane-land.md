# Halbrane Land

## Overview

Halbrane Land is the fictional Antarctic coast on which the castaways of *An Antarctic Mystery* come ashore, named in memory of their wrecked schooner. "It was named Halbrane Land, in memory of our schooner, and we called the strait that separated the two parts of the polar continent" for the ship too. (AM-023-029)

It is the party's refuge at the far edge of the world, "that remote corner of the big world which we had dubbed Halbrane Land." (AM-024-045)

## In the Narrative

The survivors establish themselves along its shore. They wonder "whether the amphibia would not forsake Halbrane Land at the approach of winter." (AM-023-030) The great problem of their hunt is "how were we to get it in to the point of Halbrane Land?" (AM-023-044)

It is here that the last threads of the story are gathered — a nearby coast lies "not more than fifty miles from Halbrane Land." (AM-024-041), and on it the two long-separated brothers are "at length reunited." (AM-024-045)

## Geography & Features

The land is part of a divided polar continent, split by the strait the castaways named for the same schooner. (AM-023-029) It sustains them with game, its amphibia yielding flesh "none the less savoury" though the beasts "did not go beyond seven or eight hundred pounds." (AM-023-032)

Its coast is set against that of Poe's island — a place "how like that of Halbrane Land!" and yet "how different a coast from that of Tsalal Island." (AM-024-042) From here the survivors at last set out north, losing sight of the pole. (AM-026-008)
