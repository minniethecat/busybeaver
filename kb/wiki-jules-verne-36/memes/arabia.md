## Overview

Arabia is the great peninsula the *Nautilus* skirts in *Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea*, carved from the sea by the Gulf of Oman between Arabia and the Indian peninsula, which provides access to the Persian Gulf. (TTLU-028-004)

## In the Narrative

Its ancient waterways draw Aronnax's wonder. He supposed that after visiting these unusual waterways of Arabia and Egypt, the *Nautilus* would go back down to the Indian Ocean. (TTLU-028-012)

History runs deep along its shore — in 615 B.C. King Necho II was hard at work on a canal fed by Nile water, running through the Egyptian plains opposite Arabia. (TTLU-028-077)

## Geography & Features

Its stony north is precisely placed: the cape forms the tip of Arabia Petraea, which lies between the Gulf of Suez and the Gulf of Aqaba. (TTLU-029-073) It is an old artery of trade — its peoples long held the commerce between the interior of Africa and Arabia, trading in gums, ivory, fine muslin, and slaves. (FWB-015-004)

It spans a great section of the reordered globe, from the Black Sea to Sweden, taking in Arabia, nearly the whole of India, and Persia. (TT-015-018)
