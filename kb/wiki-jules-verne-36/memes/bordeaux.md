## Overview

Bordeaux is the French port whose collegians muse on the quays of the Gironde, and where "to wander is human." (ASC-001-051) It is the home city claimed by Claudius Bombarnac as he sets out along the great trans-Asian road. (ASC-002-026)

## In the Narrative

Pressed to reach Europe, Phileas Fogg declared he was setting out for Bordeaux and would go to Bordeaux. (AWED-032-029) He asked the captain of the Henrietta point-blank whether he would carry him to Bordeaux. (AWED-032-040) Fogg then took passage for the port, managing the crew with his banknotes over thirty hours aboard. (AWED-033-004)

## Geography & Features

The vessel's engineer warned that they had coal enough only for short steam from New York to Bordeaux, not for full fires. (AWED-033-017) The city was also a cradle of ballooning, where D'Arbelet des Granges left the traces of his ascent. (DA-001-090)

## Connections

Bordeaux stands on France's Atlantic seaboard, a natural link between the ocean crossings of [[Around the World in Eighty Days]] and the Continent. (AWED-032-029) Its ballooning heritage is recalled in the early flights that also touched Lyons and Nantes. (VB-001-085)
