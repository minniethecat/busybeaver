# Ivan Ogareff

## Overview

Ivan Ogareff is a disgraced Russian officer turned enemy of the state — spoken of at court as "the traitor Ivan Ogareff" (MS-002-023). A colonel by rank (MS-015-004), he is "terrible as any of the most savage Tartar chieftains" yet "an educated soldier" (MS-015-005).

## Role in the Story

Ogareff is the hidden hand behind the invasion: the chief of police asks whether "Ivan Ogareff has a hand in this Tartar rebellion" (MS-003-041), and indeed he has crossed the Urals to "foment rebellion amongst" the Kirghiz (MS-003-042).

His treason runs deeper than rebellion — he "is also playing the part of a traitor," a "personal enemy" of the Grand Duke (MS-003-048), and "Irkutsk was the real object of Ivan Ogareff" (MS-015-008).

To reach the Grand Duke, he takes a captured despatch and presents himself under a false identity — "the only name they could give to Ivan Ogareff" was Michael Strogoff himself (MS-033-018).

At the appointed hour he "opened the window and stationed himself at the North angle of the side terrace" to signal the assault (MS-033-020).

## Character & Traits

Vengeance is his engine: "thirsting for vengeance," he "aims at the life of my brother," says the Czar (MS-003-042). Struck in public with the knout, he is "not a man to forgive," and "his vengeance would be merciless" (MS-023-016).

Nicknamed "the Scarred Cheek" and clad in "the uniform of a Tartar officer" (MS-023-007), he condemns his captive with cold finality: "Ogareff approached Michael. 'You shall die!'" (MS-023-038). His cunning matches his cruelty — he deduces that "the pretended Nicholas Korpanoff was Michael Strogoff, courier of the Czar" (MS-015-095).

## Relationships

- [[Michel Strogoff]] — the Czar's courier and his personal quarry, whose name and mission Ogareff usurps (MS-015-095).
- Sangarre — the Tsigane spy who serves him once "Ivan Ogareff entered on the path of treason" (MS-021-009).
