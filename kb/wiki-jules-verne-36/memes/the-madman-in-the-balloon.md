# The Madman in the Balloon

## Overview

The madman in the balloon is the terror at the heart of *[[A Drama in the Air]]*—a stranger stows away on the narrator's ascent, reveals himself as a lunatic bent on death, cuts away the car in mid-flight, and leaves the narrator clinging to the netting until an anchor-cord flings him to safety on Dutch soil.

The stranger's nature became clear. There was no longer any illusion possible; the narrator recognized the horrible truth—he was in the presence of a madman (DA-001-159).

## What Happens

The madman rose to act. In the high, thin air the narrator saw his companion rise up before him, his purpose turned wholly to destruction (DA-001-200).

He proclaimed their doom. "The hour is come!" he said. "We must die. We are rejected of men. They despise us. Let us crush them!" (DA-001-201).

He moved to abandon the car. "Let us cut these cords! Let this car be abandoned in space," he cried, imagining the balloon would then rise toward the sun (DA-001-203).

The two men fought. Despair galvanized the narrator; he threw himself on the madman, but was thrown down and pinned while the lunatic cut through the cords of the car (DA-001-204).

## Significance

The car fell away. As the car dropped into space, the narrator instinctively clung to the cords and hauled himself up into the meshes of the netting (DA-001-210).

An anchor-cord saved him. Two miles from the sea, driven on by the tempest, an abrupt shock loosened his hold; a cord slipped through his fingers and he found himself on solid earth (DA-001-215).

He woke far from the flight. When he came to himself, he was in bed in a peasant's cottage at Harderwick, a village of La Gueldre, fifteen leagues from Amsterdam (DA-001-217).

## Participants & Setting

- The ascent begins as an ordinary balloon voyage over Holland before the narrator discovers the uninvited stranger sharing his car.
- The madman, obsessed with the deaths of famous aeronauts, throws out ballast to climb ever higher and finally cuts the car loose to force a fatal descent.
- The narrator alone survives, saved by the anchor-cord catching in the ground, and recovers in a Dutch cottage near Amsterdam, as told in *A Drama in the Air*.
