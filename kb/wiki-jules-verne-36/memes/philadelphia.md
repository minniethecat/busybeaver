## Overview

Philadelphia is the American city that serves as the base of the ballooning enthusiasts in *[[Robur the Conqueror]]*. Its great aeronautical club meets there, "one of the largest in the Weldon Institute, the well-known club in Walnut Street, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania" (RC-002-016), where the rivalry that drives the novel first ignites.

The city is portrayed as a bustling, self-important place. Its leading citizens set the social tone, for figures like William T. Forbes and his daughters "gave the tone to the best society in Philadelphia" (RC-002-023), and its clubmen pride themselves on their scientific ambitions.

## In the Narrative

Philadelphia is the launching ground for the great aerial contest. In the Turner yard "at Philadelphia there reposed an enormous aerostat, whose strength had been tried by highly compressed air" (RC-003-010), the balloon with which the Institute hopes to conquer the sky.

The theme returns in *Master of the World*, where the [[Weldon Institute]] is "a club devoted to aeronautics under the presidency of one of the chief personages of Philadelphia, commonly called Uncle Prudent" (MW-016-005). It is from "Fairmount Park in Philadelphia" (MW-016-012) that the airship Go-Ahead finally makes its ascent.

## Geography & Features

Philadelphia is a great radiating hub of the eastern United States. In *Master of the World* it is called "the chief city," on whose radiating roads "there circulated an extraordinary vehicle, of which no one could describe the form" (MW-004-004), the mysterious machine crossing the country.

The city is watered by the Schuylkill and linked by rail to its neighbours. Its fiery industry is such that "Philadelphia might well think that a fire was devouring one of its quarters and that all the waters of the Schuyllkill could not put it out" (RC-004-065), and it stands on the railroad network by which "the four railroads of Ohio, Susquehanna, Philadelphia, and Washington... converge at Baltimore" (FEM-003-008).

## Connections

Philadelphia is the home of the [[Weldon Institute]] and its ballooning members in *[[Robur the Conqueror]]*, a great manufacturing city on the eastern seaboard linked by rail to [[Baltimore]] and [[New York]]. Its forges rival Baltimore's in casting work for the [[Gun Club]], for the great gun's parts were "cast night and day at Baltimore, the other forged day and night at Philadelphia" (FEM-010-009).
