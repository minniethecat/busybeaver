# Cape Horn

## Overview

Cape Horn is the storm-beaten rock at the southernmost tip of South America, the great turning point between the Atlantic and Pacific rounded by more than one of the Voyages. In *In Search of the Castaways* it is described plainly: "Cape Horn is nothing but a rock sunk in the sea in latitude 52 degrees." (SC-009-046)

## In the Narrative

In *Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea* the Abraham Lincoln takes the long way south in her hunt for the monster: "Commander Farragut was unwilling to attempt this tortuous passageway and maneuvered instead to double Cape Horn." (TTLU-005-005) On July 6 the frigate "doubled that solitary islet at the tip of the South American continent." (TTLU-005-007)

For the *Duncan* in *In Search of the Castaways* the cape marks the passage home: after her Pacific search "the Duncan had re-provisioned, she sailed along the coast of Patagonia, doubled Cape Horn, and made a swift run up the Atlantic Ocean." (SC-066-003)

## Geography & Features

The cape is one of the two great capes feared by navigators, and Aronnax measures the Nautilus's course against it: "Once it was abreast of Cape Horn, I thought it would strike west of the cape, make for Pacific seas, and complete its tour of the world." (TTLU-036-002) It gives its name to a whole reach of ocean in the submarine's log — "From Cape Horn to the Amazon." (TTLU-041-001)
