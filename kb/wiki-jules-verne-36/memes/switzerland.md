## Overview

Switzerland is the Alpine land of *Master Zacharius*, where the severe climate of Switzerland made one feel sad, while the south wind swept round the house and whistled ominously. (MZ-001-012)

## In the Narrative

Its cities are famed for the old clockmaker's craft — Master Zacharius exhibited his magnificent clocks and repeaters, which commanded such high prices in the cities of France, Switzerland, and Germany. (MZ-003-006)

Beyond the workshop, the nation takes its modest place in the wider world: Switzerland sent the modest sum of 257 francs to the American scheme of the Gun Club. (FEM-012-026)

## Geography & Features

It is the heart of the Alps, where a red substance had been found, its colour proceeding solely from the presence of certain corpuscles. (ACH-051-037) Travelers made excursions in Savoy and Switzerland (AMB-001-008), and the observatory of Sautis stood in the canton of Appenzell. (RC-001-026)

Its mountains and glaciers are a byword: a land might have the mountains and glaciers of Switzerland, but not be Switzerland. (TN-002-005) Prehistoric remains lie in its soil — maxillaries were found in grottoes in France, Switzerland, and Belgium. (JCE-038-007)
