## Overview

Aden is the coaling station at the mouth of the Red Sea, a fixed point on Phileas Fogg's schedule. It is thirteen hundred and ten miles from Suez to Aden, at the other end of the Red Sea, and the steamer has to take in a fresh coal supply. (AWED-006-021) The passage is timed to the hour.

## In the Narrative

The company's regulations govern the run. The distance between Suez and Aden is precisely thirteen hundred and ten miles, and the steamers are allowed one hundred and thirty-eight hours to traverse it. (AWED-009-002) The Mongolia beats her time — instead of reaching Aden on the morning of the 15th, she arrived on the evening of the 14th, a gain of fifteen hours. (AWED-009-027)

The stop is brief but watched. Passing the Strait of Bab-el-Mandeb, the ship put in at Steamer Point, north-west of Aden harbour, to take in coal. (AWED-009-026) Mr. Fogg and his servant went ashore at Aden to have the passport again viséed, and Fix, unobserved, followed them. (AWED-009-028)

## Geography & Features

A fortified British coaling port at the southern end of the Red Sea, Aden is the midpoint of the Suez–Bombay leg — a few hours' halt for coal where Fix trails Fogg ashore, then sixteen hundred more miles of open sea to India.
