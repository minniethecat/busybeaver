# Gallian Sea

## Overview

The Gallian Sea is the body of water that covers a large part of the comet Gallia, torn from the Mediterranean when the comet grazes the Earth in *Off on a Comet*. The castaways name it after their new world: "As the writer adheres to the appellation of Gallia, do you not think that we might call these new waters the Gallian Sea?" (OC-018-055)

## In the Narrative

Surveyed from the heights, the sea proves to be a captured piece of an old one: "Around these there was stretched out the full extent of the Gallian Sea, which apparently comprised about one-half of the Mediterranean." (OC-020-002)

As Gallia recedes from the sun the sea is doomed to freeze, and the count foresees that "the Gallian Sea would ere long be frozen over." (OC-019-017) The change comes suddenly: "A sudden thrill seemed to vibrate across the motionless waters to the distant horizon, and the Gallian Sea had become a solid sheet of ice!" (OC-023-036)

## Geography & Features

Once frozen, the sea becomes a smooth road stretching to every horizon: "far as the eye could reach, the Gallian Sea had become one uniform sheet of ice." (OC-024-010) It fills the low basin of the little world, and from the mountain the explorers look down to where "beneath their feet was extended the basin of the Gallian Sea." (OC-044-002)
