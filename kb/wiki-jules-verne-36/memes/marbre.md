# Marbre

## Overview

He is one of the expedition's two chief hunters, for "the chief hunters of the expedition were the soldiers Marbre and Sabine, both very expert at their business" (FC-006-024).

Among the soldiers chosen for the northward march, "two--Marbre and Sabine--were skilful hunters" (FC-013-004).

## Role in the Story

He and his partner take their quarry and dress it, "Marbre and Sabine taking immediate possession, carried off their skins" (FC-006-057), and through the seasons "Sabine and Marbre killed a good many Polar hares" (FC-020-002).

He is the first to sense the coming peril, for "Marbre, upon whom the truth had first dawned, confided his suspicions to Mac-Nab the carpenter and Rae the blacksmith" (FC-035-005).

He carries grave news to his officer, so that "the hunter Marbre approached Hobson, and said to him in a significant tone" (FC-028-021), and "Marbre's tidings were of grave importance" (FC-028-036).

## Character & Traits

He is a seasoned trapper who cannot be deceived, boasting "old trappers like us are not to be taken in" (FC-006-033).

He is prudent with the expedition's stores, urging his companions to "spare our powder and shot" (FC-006-042).

He is skilled in the arts of the chase, for "Marbre and Sabine were skilled in all the artifices which sportsmen employ in stalking their prey" (FC-014-014), and his counsel shapes the hunt, since "in accordance with the advice of Marbre the hunter, a reindeer trap was constructed" (FC-019-003).

He reads the wild unerringly, declaring "it's no reindeer making that noise... I know well enough what creature has fallen into our pit" (FC-019-009), and takes his prey with address, for "Marbre flung his running noose skilfully over its neck and pulled it tightly" (FC-037-020).

## Relationships

- [[Sabine]] — his fellow chief hunter, the two named together as the soldiers "very expert at their business" (FC-006-024).
- [[Jaspar Hobson]] — the officer he serves and warns, who "told Marbre and Sabine not to go far away" (FC-028-011).
- [[Mac-Nab]] — the carpenter to whom he "confided his suspicions" of the coming danger (FC-035-005).

## Appearances

- [[The Fur Country]] — the Arctic expedition on which he hunts and first divines the drifting island's peril, chosen among the soldiers where "two--Marbre and Sabine--were skilful hunters" (FC-013-004).
