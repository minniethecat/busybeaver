# Claw Cape

## Overview

Claw Cape is the fictional headland forming the southeastern extremity of Lincoln Island in *The Mysterious Island*. It is named by Neb during the settlers' survey of their domain: "'Claw Cape,' cried Neb directly, who also wished to be godfather to some part of his domain." (MI-011-072)

Curved like a blade, it "bent in the form of a yataghan, tapered away nearly four miles to the southeast." (MI-021-017)

## In the Narrative

The cape bounds the settlers' view of their island. From Granite House "the view from the windows extended over a boundless horizon, which was closed by the two Mandible Capes on the north, and Claw Cape on the south." (MI-019-040) From the heights the island runs "from Claw Cape on the southeast, to Reptile End on the southwest." (MI-023-052)

It is a repeated goal of exploration. The settlers resolve "to devote a day to the exploration of that part of the island between the Mercy and Claw Cape" (MI-021-005), and later choose to return by it so that "the exploration would be ended." (MI-027-024)

## Geography & Features

The cape lies far from the colonists' home — "from Claw Cape to Granite House there must be at least another ten miles." (MI-027-009) Seen from the sea, "Twenty-five miles off the coast terminated in the Claw Cape, which loomed dimly through the morning mists," raised by mirage "as if suspended between land and water." (MI-027-027)

It commands the southern shore, the plateau's heights sloping "away to the extremity of Claw Cape, and to the southern side of the island." (MI-013-065)
