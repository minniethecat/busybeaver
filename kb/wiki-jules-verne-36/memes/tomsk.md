# Tomsk

## Overview

Tomsk is one of the great cities of Western Siberia in *Michael Strogoff*, a populous centre on the imperial telegraph line whose menace by the Tartars marks the deepening of the invasion. Verne rates it among the largest of the region's towns: "Tomsk has more inhabitants and is larger" than the seat of its own government. (MS-015-001)

## In the Narrative

The city is a barometer of the war's progress. From the first the Czar orders the general to "telegraph hourly to Tomsk... and keep me informed of all that occurs," (MS-002-006) and its fall from the wire is the first sign of catastrophe, for "telegrams pass Tomsk no longer, sire." (MS-002-075)

As Strogoff pushes east the danger closes on it — travellers report that "the Tartars menace Tomsk!" (MS-006-048) — while the empire strains to save it: "the loyal Cossacks of the government of Tobolsk were advancing by forced marches towards Tomsk, in the hope of cutting off the Tartar columns." (MS-013-031)

## Geography & Features

Tomsk sits on the severed telegraph line running east across Siberia. The wire "extending from Ekaterenburg to Nikolaevsk... had been cut, first beyond Tomsk, and then between Tomsk and Kolyvan," (MS-003-006) fixing the city as the frontier between the connected and the isolated halves of the empire.

Its name recurs in the roll of stations to which the courier's description is dispatched — "Ishim, Omsk, Tomsk, and to all the telegraphic stations with which communication is yet open." (MS-002-025)
