## Overview

Tachkend is a great city of Russian Turkestan, lying on the Grand Transasiatic railway between Samarkand and the Chinese frontier. In the Vernean corpus it belongs to *The Adventures of a Special Correspondent*, where it is one of the halting-places on Claudius Bombarnac's overland run from the Caspian toward Peking.

## In the Narrative

The travellers settle down for the night "with the intention of sleeping till we reach Tachkend," (ASC-013-005) a city toward which the swarming pedlars called Sarthes are "evidently ... going." (ASC-006-011) On arrival "we are at Tachkend, and the time-table shows that we stop here two hours and a half," (ASC-014-003) and afterwards "we left Tachkend at precisely eleven o'clock in the morning." (ASC-014-014) Beyond it "the railway had curved toward the south, so as to reach Khodjend." (ASC-014-029)

## Geography & Features

"The distance from Samarkand to Tachkend is three hundred kilometres." (ASC-013-006) Like the other Russian conquests it is doubled, and "in the streets of old Tachkend a certain number of women are to be met with." (ASC-014-010) Its people differ little "from that met with in other parts of Turkestan," (ASC-014-007) though "it is perhaps at Tachkend that the Jews are gathered in the greatest numbers." (ASC-014-008) The region is unstable: "at Tachkend, as at Samarkand, I saw the traces of these commotions" left by earthquake. (ASC-015-031)

## Connections

Tachkend is bound to *The Adventures of a Special Correspondent* as a commercial city of the Transcaspian line: "In 1870 the Russians endeavored without success to establish a fair at Tachkend which would rival that at Nijni-Novgorod." (ASC-014-001) Its trade reaches outward, for "Khotan silks, cotton, felt, woolen carpets, cloth ... are exported beyond the frontier between Tachkend and Koulja, to the north of Oriental Turkestan." (ASC-016-012)
