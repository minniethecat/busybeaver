## Overview

Calais is the French Channel port whence the Continental journey begins; Fogg's party set out for Dover and Calais within ten minutes of resolving on it. (AWED-004-010) "To Calais; good again!" was Passepartout's relief at the route. (AWED-004-020)

## In the Narrative

A projected tunnel would one day run between Dover and Calais, with no English Parliament to oppose it. (ASC-011-046) In a child's mistaken geography, France was called an English province whose chief city was Calais. (SC-038-092)

## Geography & Features

The strait is storied in the annals of ballooning: Blanchard and Jeffries made the aerial crossing from Dover to Calais. (DA-001-118) After long suspense the aeronauts at last perceived Calais below them. (DA-001-139) Robur's martyrology of flight recalls Pilâtre de Rozier, who died at Calais. (RC-004-047)

## Connections

Calais opens the Continental road from [[London]] across the Channel toward [[Paris]]. (AWED-004-010) Its skies hold a roll of early aeronauts who braved the Dover-to-Calais passage. (VB-001-114)
