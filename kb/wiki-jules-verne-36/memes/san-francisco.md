# San Francisco

## Overview

San Francisco is the Californian port at which Phileas Fogg makes landfall in America during *Around the World in Eighty Days*. It is the gateway to the continental crossing, from which "a railway train from San Francisco to New York, and a transatlantic steamer from New York to Liverpool, would doubtless bring them to the end of this impossible journey round the world." (AWED-024-010)

## In the Narrative

Reaching the city is a race in itself. Fogg must "get to Yokohama by the 14th at the latest, to take the boat for San Francisco," (AWED-020-030) and once the Pacific is crossed "the General Grant entered the bay of the Golden Gate, and reached San Francisco" on the 3rd of December. (AWED-024-036)

Ashore, the travellers plunge into the bustling young city. Fix "begged permission to accompany them in their walk about San Francisco," (AWED-025-011) and in Montgomery Street they meet a boisterous political crowd where "the side-walks, street, horsecar rails, the shop-doors, the windows of the houses, and even the roofs, were full of people." (AWED-025-012)

## Geography & Features

The city opens on the Pacific through the strait of the Golden Gate, the bay by which the steamer enters. (AWED-024-036) Its life centres on thoroughfares like Montgomery Street, where the party takes "a position on the top of a flight of steps situated at the upper end of Montgomery Street." (AWED-025-017)

For all its raw energy it wears a familiar face to the travellers, so that at the International Hotel Passepartout felt as if he "had not... left England at all." (AWED-025-007)
