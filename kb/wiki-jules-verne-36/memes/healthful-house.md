## Overview

Healthful House is a private sanitarium and the setting that opens Facing the Flag, a walled estate on the right bank of the Neuse where wealthy patients are cared for. Healthful House, which contained a select personnel and was assured of the co-operation of the most celebrated doctors in the country, was a private enterprise. (FF-001-009)

Though reserved chiefly for chronic complaints, its management did not decline to admit patients affected by mental troubles. (FF-001-011) The story begins when the director of the establishment received a carte de visite bearing, without escutcheon or coronet, a single name. (FF-001-002)

## In the Narrative

Healthful House owed its notoriety to one inmate. A person of world-wide notoriety had for eighteen months been confined there, a circumstance bound to attract attention to the house. (FF-001-012) He was not confined in a lunatic asylum, but conveyed to Healthful House, which offered every guarantee for the treatment of his malady. (FF-001-037)

That inmate drew the fatal visit. The Count d'Artigas had been authorized to visit Healthful House, and did not wish to go away without seeing Thomas Roch. (FF-002-034) The French inventor was justly regarded as the most interesting inmate of Healthful House, kept in Pavilion No. 17. (FF-002-031)

## Geography & Features

It would have been difficult to find a more agreeable situation than that of Healthful House. (FF-001-010) A warder was detailed to watch the inventor by day and night in Pavilion No. 17, at the end of Healthful House Park, which had been specially set apart for him. (FF-001-013)

The estate was sealed against intrusion. Its visitors had skirted the wall that surrounded the property, high enough to preclude the possibility of climbing it. (FF-003-003) A beech-lined road separated the Healthful House estate from the right bank of the Neuse. (FF-003-002)

## Connections

Healthful House is bound to the abduction that launches the plot. Simon Hart, alias Gaydon, had been an attendant at Healthful House for fifteen months, engaged to look after the new inmate. (FF-001-042) The Count d'Artigas presented himself at the door of Healthful House, accompanied by Captain Spade, commander of the Ebba. (FF-002-012)

The house lay dangerously near the sea. The Ebba's presence in proximity to Healthful House could not fail to excite suspicion, so securely had the Count hidden his prisoners. (FF-003-103)
