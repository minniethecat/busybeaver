# Sea of Clouds

## Overview

The Sea of Clouds, the lunar plain Mare Nubium, is one of the great grey basins the travellers examine as their projectile skirts the Moon in *Round the Moon*. Verne gives it a moralist's gloss: "In the left hemisphere stretches the 'Sea of Clouds,' where human reason is so often drowned." (RM-011-013)

## In the Narrative

To Barbicane and Nicholl the plain is a study in lunar geography: "the 'Sea of Clouds' was an immense depression of ground, with circular mountains scattered about on it; covering a great part of the western side." (RM-011-017) Its edges are ill-charted — "This Sea of Clouds is rather doubtfully traced upon the maps." (RM-012-013)

As the projectile sweeps south the sea marks a stage of the survey: "leaving Pitatus to the right, on the 30th degree, it went along the south of the Sea of Clouds, of which it had already approached the north." (RM-018-074)

## Geography & Features

The Sea of Clouds lies on the Moon's western side, walled off from its northern neighbour: "separated from the Sea of Clouds by high chains of mountains, extends the Sea of Rains, Mare Imbrium." (RM-011-018) Above its floor rises an isolated peak that "rises isolated like a gigantic lighthouse over that of the Sea of Clouds bordering on the Sea of Tempests." (RM-012-018)
