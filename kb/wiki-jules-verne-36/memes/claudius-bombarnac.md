# Claudius Bombarnac

## Overview

Claudius Bombarnac is a French reporter — "Claudius Bombarnac, of Bordeaux" (ASC-002-026) — who narrates the whole voyage in the first person.

His mission arrives by telegram: "Claudius Bombarnac will repair to Uzun Ada, a port on the east coast of the Caspian" (ASC-001-007). As he tells a fellow traveller, "I am a reporter sent by the Twentieth Century to do this journey" (ASC-002-019).

## Role in the Story

The assignment sends him across the Grand Transasiatic Railway, and he embraces its unpredictability — "here was the unexpected, indeed; the uncertainty of a special correspondent's life" (ASC-001-009).

He treats the train as a gallery of subjects to be worked: "to find out who are my traveling companions, whence they come, where they go, is that not the duty of a special correspondent in search of interviews?" (ASC-002-007). Confident of his craft, he trusts that "a special correspondent of the Twentieth Century will know how to make it interesting" (ASC-001-042).

Ever mindful of his editors, once at Uzun Ada he resolves that "I must telegraph to the Twentieth Century... that I am at my post" (ASC-005-007), wanting only "a few incidents of the journey... worthy of the Twentieth Century" (ASC-005-021).

He introduces himself to his chief confidant with full title: "I am a Frenchman, Claudius Bombarnac, special correspondent of the Twentieth Century" (ASC-007-004).

## Character & Traits

He holds precision to be the reporter's first virtue: "a special correspondent who is not precise is a geometer who neglects to run out his calculations to the tenth decimal" (ASC-002-001).

The calling is an absolute for him — "either one is a correspondent or one is not!" (ASC-001-035) — and his restless curiosity has no bounds: "there are moments when a special correspondent is metamorphosed into a daughter of Eve" (ASC-007-075).

His growing renown precedes him, so that a fellow passenger greets him as "Monsieur Claudius Bombarnac, correspondent of the Twentieth Century" (ASC-009-038).

## Relationships

- Major Noltitz — the Russian officer he adopts as travelling confidant, who answers him "Monsieur Bombarnac... I quite agree with you" (ASC-007-007).
