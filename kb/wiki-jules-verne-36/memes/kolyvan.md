## Overview

Kolyvan is a town of Western Siberia lying on the great trunk road and telegraph line, listed between Elamsk and Tomsk on the route from the Ural to the far east. (MS-003-005)

The single telegraph wire from Ekaterenburg to Nikolaevsk runs through it; it is cut first beyond Tomsk, and then between Tomsk and Kolyvan. (MS-003-006)

## In the Narrative

As the invasion spreads, the question is whether the wire between Kolyvan and Tomsk has been cut by Tartar scouts. (MS-003-065) For a time the line is still reported working as far as Kolyvan. (MS-009-025)

It is at Kolyvan that Michael Strogoff is captured, and from there carried in the same convoy on the road to Tomsk. (MS-022-002)

## Geography & Features

Kolyvan lies just beyond the marshes of the Baraba; once free of that swampy district, a traveller expects to reach the town on the morrow. (MS-016-034)

Its fate hangs on the advancing Tartars: scouts ask whether the Emir's men have entered Kolyvan, and the answer for a while is no. (MS-017-016) For the moment, Kolyvan does not yet burn. (MS-017-017)
