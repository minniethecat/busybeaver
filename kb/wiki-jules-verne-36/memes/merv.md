## Overview

Merv is a great oasis city of Transcaspia and a station on the Grand Transasiatic railway. The line stops a few hours at chief towns like Merv, Bokhara, and Samarkand. (ASC-005-018) The train reached Merv Station, over five hundred miles from Uzun Ada. (ASC-010-001)

## In the Narrative

The correspondent had seven hours to stop at Merv. (ASC-010-005) Old Merv lies eighteen miles from the station, and he would hardly see it as he passed. (ASC-010-012) Old Merv, he learned, had been rebuilt four times. (ASC-010-045) From here he wired his despatch, dated "Merv, 16th May, 7 p.m." (ASC-010-040)

## Geography & Features

The soil is formed of alluvial beds extending up to the environs of Merv. (ASC-009-008) The most interesting sight is a Tekke village in the middle of Merv, whose people keep the villainous characteristics of their race. (ASC-010-020) The first locomotive entered Merv station in July, 1886. (ASC-006-036)

## Connections

Merv is one of the oasis towns that break the monotony of the journey with [[Bokhara]] and Samarkand. (ASC-006-008) It stands over five hundred miles east of the Caspian crossing from [[Baku]], on the route of [[The Adventures of a Special Correspondent]]. (ASC-010-001)
