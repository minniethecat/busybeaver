# The Comet's Collision

## Overview

The Comet's Collision is the founding catastrophe of *[[off-on-a-comet|Off on a Comet]]*, the cosmic accident that sets a fragment of Earth adrift among the stars.

The Gallian Academy of Science concludes that on the night of the 31st of December, a comet, crossing the ecliptic, had come into collision with the earth, and that the violence of the shock had severed a piece of the world away (OC-027-037).

## What Happens

At first the survivors can only guess at what has befallen them. Lieutenant Procope submits his opinion that the evidence goes far to confirm his hypothesis that a fragment of the earth has been precipitated into space (OC-016-036).

The conclusion hardens into certainty: the earth and this comet have been in collision, and the result has been that the little world was split off and sent flying far into space (OC-027-030).

The wandering body is given a name — it is to this comet that the astronomer has given the name of Gallia (OC-027-032).

When Professor Rosette is at last confronted, he confirms the very hour: at 47' 35.6" after two o'clock on the morning of the first of January there was a collision, his comet grazed the earth, and the bits of the earth were carried off (OC-028-080). "You are on my comet, on Gallia itself!" he declares (OC-028-083).

## Significance

The collision remakes every law the survivors live by. Gravity itself is transformed, so that a single muscular effort now carries a man to an altitude of thirty feet before he descends again (OC-006-034) — the first sign that the ground beneath them is no longer Earth.

The human cost is written across the shattered coast. On the 6th of January the survivors found four villages had entirely disappeared and headlands swept away (OC-007-038), the wreckage marking the instant a comet turned a corner of the world into a planet of its own.

## Participants & Setting

- The birth of [[Gallia]] from a fragment of Earth struck away by a passing comet.
