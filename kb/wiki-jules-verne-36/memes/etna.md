## Overview

Etna is the great volcano of Sicily, the fiery landmark of Verne's Mediterranean. Far away in the south, that threatening volcano was the fierce Etna. (JCE-044-053) Its peak reared 11,000 feet above the level of the sea. (OC-018-026)

## In the Narrative

Etna stands as a measure of eruptive power. On the eve of an eruption there is everything to be feared, as at Etna, Popocatepetl, and Orizaba. (MI-057-003) Observers kept a meteorological station on Etna in the old Casa Inglesi. (RC-001-027)

## Geography & Features

Etna rises from the centre of its island. There is a Sinai in the middle of Arabia and an Etna in the centre of Sicily. (RM-010-016) Measuring the ancient craters formed by the first eruptions of Vesuvius and Etna, they are found scarcely 6,000 metres wide. (RM-017-027) When the comet tore Sicily away, the very peak of Etna vanished utterly. (OC-018-026)
