# Flag Point

## Overview

Flag Point is the fictional cape on which Godfrey Morgan plants his signal mast — "the name which he had given to the cape on which he had erected his flagstaff." (GM-015-038)

It becomes the castaway's lookout and beacon, the point from which he scans the sea for any sail that might carry him home.

## In the Narrative

When a steamer appears, Godfrey "ran to the end of Flag Point, and began to haul his flag up and down, as if he were saluting." (GM-015-050) But the vessel "remained immovable at the peak, and replied not to that on Flag Point." (GM-015-051) In despair he even thinks "of setting fire to a group of resinous trees which grew at the back of Flag Point." (GM-015-056)

Later a small proa is seen "so as to double Flag Point," (GM-016-018) and Godfrey "swept round the coast to the promontory at Flag Point." (GM-016-071) The savages who land carry off his colours, "for the flag hitherto hoisted at the extremity of the cape had been carried away by them." (GM-017-002)

## Geography & Features

The cape marks one limit of the island's inhabited shore. From it Godfrey scans "all that part of the coast between the Flag Point promontory and the north-east angle of the island," (GM-017-020) finding "a vast desert from Flag Point right away beyond the curve of Dream Bay." (GM-017-024)

Its wooded back is later set ablaze — "a huge glare lighted up... the whole of the coast from Flag Point to the southern cape of Dream Bay." (GM-021-098)
