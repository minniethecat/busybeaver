## Overview

Morganton is a real town in North Carolina, the base from which the narrator Strock investigates the mysterious Great Eyrie. Asked where he is stationed, the answer comes plainly: "In Morganton, in North Carolina." (MW-010-020)

## In the Narrative

The town lives under the shadow of the neighboring heights. The good folk of Morganton and Pleasant Garden fear that the crater had reopened, though Strock is assured that neither the country-folk nor the townfolk of Pleasant Garden and Morganton were in danger of volcanic eruptions or earthquakes. (MW-017-003)

Morganton is also where the case takes an epistolary turn. A threatening letter reaches Strock; its postmark, dating from two days before, was stamped at the post office of Morganton. (MW-006-033) He later notices the resemblance of the handwriting to that of the letter he had received from Morganton. (MW-010-008)

Throughout, he trusts that the mayor of Morganton was as eager for information and as watchful as himself. (MW-006-005)

## Geography & Features

Set in the Blue Ridge country of western North Carolina, Morganton lies below the Great Eyrie, the walled summit whose flames and noises drive the novel's mystery.
