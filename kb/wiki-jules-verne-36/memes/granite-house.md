## Overview

Granite House is the great cliff-cavern that the castaways convert into their permanent home in *[[the-mysterious-island|The Mysterious Island]]*. When the settlers christen their new dwelling, [[cyrus-harding|Cyrus Harding]] gives it the name outright: "'Granite House,' replied Harding" (MI-018-071), fixing the identity of the hollow they have discovered high in the coastal escarpment.

Unlike the settlers' first rough shelter, Granite House is a natural fortress won from the living rock, and it becomes the secure center of all their subsequent industry on [[lincoln-island|Lincoln Island]].

## In the Narrative

Granite House is discovered by reasoning rather than by chance. Studying the cliff, Harding deduces "some vast cavity existed in the interior of the granite" (MI-018-015), a hidden chamber that could be turned into a dwelling once a way in was found.

Gaining entry proves an engineering feat in itself. The descent of the sheer face is made possible because "projections of the granite, forming regular steps" (MI-018-019) offer footholds down the wall. From the outside Harding studies "the position of the front of Granite House" (MI-019-001) to plan its windows, transforming the raw cavity into a habitable stronghold.

## Geography & Features

Granite House is carved into a high granite cliff overlooking the sea, its interior a natural cavity enlarged by the settlers (MI-018-015). Access runs down the rock face by way of the natural granite steps (MI-018-019), a route that can be defended or sealed off entirely.

The dwelling is lit by openings cut in the cliff face. Its front "was then to be lighted by five windows" (MI-019-011), and once the work is done "Granite House was abundantly lighted" (MI-019-012), turning the dark cavern into a bright and defensible home high above the shore.

## Connections

Granite House is the principal dwelling on [[Lincoln Island]], built and named by [[Cyrus Harding]] and the other castaways of *[[The Mysterious Island]]*, and reached by the interior lift they later install (see [[Granite House Lift]]).
