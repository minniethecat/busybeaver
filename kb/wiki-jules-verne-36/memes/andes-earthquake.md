# The Andes Earthquake

## Overview

The Andes Earthquake is the first great peril of *[[in-search-of-the-castaways|In Search of the Castaways]]*, striking the party as it crosses the [[andes|Cordillera]] on the trail of Captain Grant. The travellers are roused in the night when "An earthquake!" exclaimed Paganel, and he was not mistaken (SC-013-073).

A tremendous shock pitched them right off, and sent them rolling to the very foot of the mountain (SC-013-075).

## What Happens

The mountains were already in upheaval before the great shock fell. The guides had warned that the last earthquake has made the route impassable (SC-012-024), and as the party climbed, the entire region had been overturned by recent shocks of earthquake, forcing them ever higher (SC-012-038).

When the convulsion strikes in the night, it flings the whole company down the slope. At last one of the party picked himself up, and stood on his feet, stunned by the shock, but still firm on his legs (SC-013-076), and the survivors gather themselves to count who remains.

The reckoning brings the sharpest blow: young Robert Grant is nowhere to be found, and his friends can only pray, "Heaven grant Robert may be still alive!" (SC-014-007).

## Significance

The earthquake converts a methodical search into a story of loss and endurance, and it marks the land itself as an active adversary.

Even after the ground stilled, the forces below the surface carried on their devastating work, for shocks of earthquake are always occurring in some part or other of the Andes (SC-014-003) — the range is drawn as a country in perpetual convulsion, indifferent to the travellers crossing it.

## Participants & Setting

- The earthquake that struck Lord Glenarvan's party crossing the [[Andes]].
- Endured by Glenarvan, the geographer Paganel, and the Major, and costing the momentary loss of Robert Grant.
- Chronicled in *[[in-search-of-the-castaways|In Search of the Castaways]]*.
