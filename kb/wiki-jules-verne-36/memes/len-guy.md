# Len Guy

## Overview

Len Guy is the master of the schooner _Halbrane_, "Captain Len Guy, of Liverpool," who "was three-fifths owner of the vessel, which he had commanded for nearly six years" (AM-002-002). His reputation among the islanders is bound up with his ship — the innkeeper Atkins swears that if the _Halbrane_ fails to appear "it is because there is no longer a Captain Len Guy" (AM-001-037).

## Role in the Story

He is a hard man to approach — the narrator Jeorling, hoping for passage, resolves "to address myself personally to Len Guy, hard to get at though he might be" (AM-002-053), and finds him wrapped in silence at table (AM-007-007). What loosens his reserve is a name from Edgar Poe's romance: "the _Jane_ ... and Captain Len Guy had now uttered it for the first time" (AM-004-072).

The mystery is a matter of blood: "her captain, Len Guy, is William Guy's own brother" (AM-007-074), and the _Halbrane_ sails south to save the survivors of the wrecked _Jane_.

Over a frozen corpse he breaks — "Captain Len Guy lifted up the hair ... and finally said with a sort of sob" (AM-006-038) — and at the last the brothers are reunited: when he tells William of the clue that brought him, "William Guy hid his face in his hands and wept" (AM-024-035).

Together the two afterward "succeeded with great difficulty in taking an approximate observation" (AM-025-021).

## Character & Traits

He is grave and solitary, marked by grief. Jeorling is "struck by the sadness of his eyes, which were as black as ink" (AM-003-008), and even a first query — "Captain Len Guy?" (AM-001-035) — meets a man who guards his purpose closely.

Yet beneath the reserve runs an unbreakable resolve. Once his brother's plight is spoken, Jeorling is "convinced that the hour would come when Len Guy would again speak to me of his brother, and of the efforts which he intended to make to save him and his companions" (AM-007-007) — and no peril of the southern ice deflects that vow. His whole voyage is fraternal duty made relentless.

## Relationships

- William Guy — his brother, captain of the lost _Jane_ (AM-006-053), whom the expedition is mounted to rescue and with whom he is at last reunited (AM-024-035).
- Jeorling — the American passenger and narrator, to whom he decides "in the end to offer you a passage on the _Halbrane_" (AM-004-019).
- [[An Antarctic Mystery]] — the novel whose southern voyage he commands in search of his brother.
