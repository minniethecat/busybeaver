## Overview

Auckland is the capital of the northern island of New Zealand and, by a striking coincidence, a point on the very line the searchers have followed across the world. Auckland is situated on the self-same parallel — the thirty-seventh — which the explorers had perseveringly followed since they left the coast of Araucania. (SC-046-010) That coincidence draws Glenarvan's party toward its harbour.

## In the Narrative

The port is reached by a chance vessel. Of the three ships anchored in the roadstead one was loading for Auckland, the capital of the northern island of New Zealand. (SC-046-008) Paganel proposes to take her — the brig Macquarie, which only does a coasting trade between Eden and Auckland. (SC-048-009)

The crossing is meant to be brief. Happily, five days, or at most six, would take them to Auckland, no matter how bad a sailor the Macquarie was. (SC-047-005) But her drunkard master cannot be trusted — from Twofold Bay to Auckland the party can expect little of him. (SC-046-054)

## Geography & Features

On the north island of New Zealand at latitude 37° south, Auckland is a British colonial port and the searchers' gateway home — the place from which, Paganel reckons, they could return to Europe by the boats of the Peninsular and Oriental Company.
