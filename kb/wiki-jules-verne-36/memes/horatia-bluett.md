# Miss Horatia Bluett

## Overview

She travels on commercial business, for "Miss Horatia Bluett represented an important firm in London" (ASC-004-035).

She is drawn as all austerity and trade, "as thin, as dry, as plain as ever... and in place of jewelry a noisy bunch of keys" (ASC-019-040).

## Role in the Story

Her affinity with the American drummer is plain, for "there was an understanding between these two perfectly Anglo-Saxon natures" (ASC-004-034).

Their bargain is settled early, so that "as soon as they reach Pekin, Miss Bluett will become Mrs. Ephrinell" (ASC-006-054).

The vows themselves are curt as a receipt, "a dry 'yes' from Horatia Bluett, a short 'yes' from Fulk Ephrinell, and the two are declared to be united in the bonds of matrimony" (ASC-022-030).

Yet the union proves as void as a cancelled contract, and "Miss Horatia Bluett remained Miss Horatia Bluett" (ASC-027-053).

## Character & Traits

Her conversation runs to commerce alone, leaving "Ephrinell and Miss Bluett to talk of brokerages and prices current" (ASC-004-053).

She views the world through her trade, remarking of a distant people that "the women all have hair" (ASC-010-028).

Even at the altar she drives a bargain, meeting the terms with "well, let us say twenty per cent., Miss Bluett" (ASC-022-025).

## Relationships

- [[Ephrinell]] — the commercial suitor she weds and unweds, bound to her by "a close intimacy, founded on a similarity in tastes and aptitudes" (ASC-014-032).
- [[Claudius Bombarnac]] — the narrator who tracks the affair, questioning her when he does "not see him at his post by the side of Miss Horatia Bluett" (ASC-009-011).

## Appearances

- [[The Adventures of a Special Correspondent]] — as one of the Grand Transasiatic's passengers, hurried to safety when "Popof hurried Madame Caterna, Miss Horatia Bluett, and the other women into the cars" during the raid (ASC-020-025).
