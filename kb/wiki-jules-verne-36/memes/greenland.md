## Overview

Greenland is the great Arctic island whose coast recurs across Verne's polar voyages. Its eastern shore is a landmark of northern navigation — in 1619 the navigator Jean Munk explored the east coast of Greenland and discovered several points formerly totally unknown. (TT-002-004)

## In the Narrative

It frames the approaches to the far north. On the coast of Greenland the service of the post must leave much to wish for. (ACH-005-023) Hatteras's ship fixes its position at two hundred miles from the point of Greenland, in 57°37′ latitude by 22°58′ longitude. (ACH-005-042)

Other voyagers skirt the same coast. The Jeune-Hardie directed her way towards Cape Brewster, on the eastern coast of Greenland, near the 70th degree of latitude. (WAI-003-034) Provisions were increased in case the crew would have to winter at some point on the Greenland coast. (WAI-003-030) Mrs Barnett had the patience to translate one of the Greenland sagas, sung to a sad air. (FC-019-055)

## Geography & Features

A vast Arctic island fringed by ice-bound coasts, Greenland is for Verne the threshold of the polar world — its eastern shore near Cape Brewster and the 70th parallel a recurring landmark for ships bound to winter in the far north, its people remembered in the sagas the travellers record.
