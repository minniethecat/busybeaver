## Overview

Italy is the Mediterranean peninsula the *Dobryna* would very probably have visited — Spain, France, or Italy — had the world not been overturned. (OC-010-005) After the catastrophe, the survivors cling to a hope that even here in the north Italy and Sicily and the larger islands of the Mediterranean may still maintain their existence. (OC-018-002)

Their shrunken world holds, among its few peoples, natives of France, Russia, Italy, Spain, and England. (OC-019-065)

## In the Narrative

The peninsula becomes a cry of longing for the lost Earth — "To Sicily, Italy, France!" (OC-013-022) The wonderful event that carried off the comet has cut them from Spain, from France, from Italy, from every country of Europe. (OC-020-042)

Yet Italy keeps a small place at the survivors' table: at the New Year feast, little Nina sits as the representative of Italy among the Frenchmen, Russians, and Spaniards. (OC-037-021)

## Geography & Features

The comet's boundary is measured against the country's shores. The barrier coincided nearly with the fourteenth meridian of east longitude, and if it reached, as probably it did, beyond Sicily to Italy, a vast tract had vanished. (OC-013-017)

Sailing on, the *Dobryna* held a course fifty leagues in advance of the former coast-line of Italy, demonstrating that a new continent must have been formed. (OC-018-026)
