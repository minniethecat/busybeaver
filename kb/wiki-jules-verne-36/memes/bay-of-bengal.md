## Overview

The Bay of Bengal is the broad arm of the [[Indian Ocean]] lying east of the Indian Peninsula, a water Verne's voyagers repeatedly cross and skirt.

In [[Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea]] the [[Nautilus]] moves through it, and [[Captain Nemo]] reckons its waters among the world's great pearl grounds: "there are pearl fisheries in the Bay of Bengal" (TTLU-026-011).

## In the Narrative

The bay is bound up with the Nautilus's eastward course. Verne describes how the Black Current, the Kuroshio, "leaves the Bay of Bengal, crosses the Strait" (TTLU-014-028), tracing the warm river of the sea from these waters.

It is also a place of grim spectacle. At its threshold Aronnax records that "on January 27, at the entrance to the huge Bay of Bengal, we repeatedly encountered a gruesome sight: human corpses floating on the surface of the waves" (TTLU-025-040), the bay carrying the dead of the Ganges out to sea.

## Geography & Features

The bay is studded with islands and framed by distant peninsulas. In [[Around the World in Eighty Days]] the voyagers "came in sight of the great Andaman, the principal of the islands in the Bay of Bengal, with its picturesque Saddle Peak" (AWED-016-005), the archipelago rising within its waters.

Its breadth is such that it registers even from space. In [[Round the Moon]] the travellers make out, in the inverted image of Earth, "the Indian Peninsula, the Bay of Bengal, and Cochin-China" (RM-010-016), the bay a recognisable feature of the turning globe.

## Connections

The Bay of Bengal is a reach of the [[Indian Ocean]] traversed by the [[Nautilus]] under [[Captain Nemo]] in [[Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea]], where it is named among the great pearl fisheries. It holds the Andaman islands sighted in [[Around the World in Eighty Days]] and appears among Earth's features viewed from space in [[Round the Moon]].
