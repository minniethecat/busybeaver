## Overview

Reptile End is the south-western cape of Lincoln Island. From an elevated situation the whole southern portion of the island could be seen, from Claw Cape on the south-east to Reptile End on the south-west. (MI-023-052) It marked one end of the panorama of the shore, from Claw Cape to Reptile End. (MI-034-082)

## In the Narrative

All the shore between Falls River and Reptile End was a mass of wood, magnificent trees whose roots the long sea-swell bathed. (MI-026-035) The distance from Falls River to Reptile End was about twelve miles. (MI-026-038) Towards seven o'clock the weary explorers arrived at Reptile End. (MI-026-042) Later the "Bonadventure" tacked on leaving Port Balloon so as to reach the cape. (MI-041-039)

## Geography & Features

From the heights the gaze swept the horizon in a semi-circle from the cape to Reptile End. (MI-013-065) Sailing offshore, the point was passed in about an hour, at a distance of some ten miles. (MI-035-024) The settlers explored the vast shore of Washington Bay from Claw Cape to Reptile End, together with the marshy west coast and the downs ending at Shark Gulf. (MI-053-023)

## Connections

Reptile End bounds the wooded southern region the settlers explored: on the 18th of February they devoted a day to the shore from Reptile End to Falls River. (MI-054-001) It was on this cape that Herbert once faced a fierce jaguar, like the one killed there before. (MI-038-099)
