## Overview

Lisbon is the Portuguese capital whose government, in "Dick Sand," is charged with not knowing the atrocities of those who boast of being its subjects. (DSCF-025-022) Its speech is a touchstone: the Portuguese of Alvez and Coimbra was a native-mingled idiom a man of Lisbon would scarcely understand. (DSCF-027-037)

## In the Narrative

The city is an Atlantic port of call on the sea routes: a vessel would put into Lisbon as if bound onward for Gibraltar and Suez. (WC-015-023) A castaway reckoned he would be only 180 leagues from Lisbon, and would wait there for some means of transport. (SC-007-054)

## Geography & Features

Lisbon also figures in the annals of flight, remembered as the place where the monk Volador met his death attempting to fly. (RC-004-046) As a colonial capital its writ ran to the African interior, where its nominal subjects traded. (DSCF-025-022)

## Connections

Lisbon guards the Atlantic approach shared with the routes toward [[Suez]] in [[The Waif of the Cynthia]]. (WC-015-023) It is the metropole whose colonial agents drive the plot of [[Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen]]. (DSCF-025-022)
