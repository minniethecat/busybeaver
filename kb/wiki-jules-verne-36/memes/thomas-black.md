# Thomas Black

## Overview

He is introduced by title and name alone, "the astronomer, Thomas Black" (FC-003-022).

He is a man of science of standing, "an astronomer attached to the Greenwich Observatory," and "a sagacious and intelligent observer" (FC-003-053).

## Role in the Story

His whole purpose is the northern sky, for he "seized with avidity the opportunity offered him of studying this luminous halo" (FC-003-057).

The object is precise, "this solar eclipse" fixed for "the 18th July 1860" (FC-012-035).

Once it is past, he is eager to depart, and "made no attempt to conceal his uneasiness, for he was anxious to return... as soon as he had seen his eclipse" (FC-022-053).

## Character & Traits

He is a man of one idea, who "never opened his lips except when his own special mission was discussed" (FC-004-035).

To sport he is cold, and "professed absolute indifference to all athletic exercise" (FC-006-021).

Corpulent and inert, "absorbed in his one idea, [he] never got out of his sledge" (FC-007-030).

Even grandeur leaves him unmoved, for he "was the only one who remained indifferent to the sublime beauty of the scene" (FC-017-012).

At the fort he shuts himself away, forever "screwed and unscrewed his instruments, and looked over his astronomical calculations, remaining almost always shut up in his cabin" (FC-018-009), and once braved the killing cold, "so anxious to take stellar observations... that he braved the rigour of the outside temperature" (FC-018-051).

## Relationships

- [[Jaspar Hobson]] — the expedition's commander, who lodged him well, for "Hobson offered the other with the window in it to Thomas Black, and the astronomer took immediate possession of it" (FC-014-002).

## Appearances

- [[The Fur Country]] — the expedition tale in which he pursues his eclipse to the Arctic.
