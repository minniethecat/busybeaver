## Overview

Ceylon is that pearl dangling from the lower lobe of the Indian peninsula, off whose shores the Nautilus takes its bearings. (TTLU-026-002) The island of Ceylon, Captain Nemo declares, is famous for its pearl fisheries. (TTLU-026-006) Aronnax finds the oyster bank by the ninth parallel off the northwestern shores of Ceylon. (TTLU-026-010)

## In the Narrative

The visit is the heart of the episode: the Nautilus's commander invites his guests for a visit to Ceylon's magnificent pearl fisheries. (TTLU-026-040) Going up Ceylon's west coast during the night, the Nautilus lay west of the gulf formed by the mainland and Mannar Island. (TTLU-027-012)

There Nemo's charity is shown, a magnificent benefaction from the Man of the Waters to the poor Indian from Ceylon. (TTLU-027-073) By the day of January 29 the island of Ceylon disappeared below the horizon. (TTLU-028-002)

## Geography & Features

The island sits on the world's sea-lanes: the Peninsular & Oriental line provides service from the island of Ceylon to Sidney. (TTLU-025-028) Aronnax reads up on it in a volume entitled *Ceylon and the Singhalese* by H. C. Sirr. (TTLU-026-003)

Its wealth is the pearl: the Ceylon fisheries are farmed annually for great profit (TTLU-026-074), one of the banks Nemo counts alongside the Bay of Bengal and the seas of China and Japan. (TTLU-026-011)
