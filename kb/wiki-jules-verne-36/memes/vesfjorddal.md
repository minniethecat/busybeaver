## Overview

Vesfjorddal is the Norwegian valley at the heart of Ticket No. 9672, running below the Rjukan falls in the Telemark. The route lay along the left bank of the Maan, down the charming valley of the Vesfjorddal — a narrow, heavily wooded valley, watered by a thousand dashing cataracts. (TN-016-003)

## In the Narrative

The valley organizes the story's geography. From Dal the travellers could explore the valley of Vesfjorddal between Lakes Mjos and Tinn, and visit the wonderful cataracts of the Rjukan. (TN-002-008) All the houses face the narrow valley of Vesfjorddal, with their backs to the line of hills to the north, at the base of which flows the Maan. (TN-002-001)

Its falls and legends give it colour. One could explore that portion of the valley of Vesfjorddal lying between the two lakes, make the ascent of Gousta, and pay a visit to the Rjukanfos. (TN-010-031) One day Eystein endeavoured to reach his betrothed, the beautiful Marie of Vesfjorddal, by a dangerous path. (TN-008-022)

## Geography & Features

A narrow, wooded Telemark valley along the Maan between Lakes Mjos and Tinn, overlooked by Gousta and threaded by the Rjukan cataracts, the Vesfjorddal is the scenic stage on which Ticket No. 9672 unfolds.
