## Overview

The Province of Amazones is the great Brazilian province at the confluence of the Rio Negro and the Amazon. Since 1826 it has been a full province with its own capital, borrowing its latest name from an Indian tribe. (EHLA-021-006) Through it drifts the jangada of Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon.

## In the Narrative

Twenty-four hours more and the raft would lie off the mouth of the Rio Negro, before the capital of the province of Amazones. (EHLA-018-039) The chelonian eggs of the great river are turned to their most general use in the provinces of Amazones and Para. (EHLA-015-020) A boaster is mocked for having already seen the finest towns in Amazones and Para. (EHLA-016-041)

The province is also the seat of the law that pursues Joam Dacosta: the warrant against him is issued by the magistrate filling the position in the province of Amazones. (EHLA-024-001) Only Ribeiro's nomination to the chief justiceship in the province had once given the accused man hope. (EHLA-023-006) Across its villages the wandering barber Fragoso plies his trade. (EHLA-032-013)

## Geography & Features

At the capital the travellers drink the health of Amazones in glasses of the generous liquor that comes from the coasts of Oporto and Setubal. (EHLA-018-041) The province takes its name and rank from the great river at whose mouth its towns are placed. (EHLA-021-006)
