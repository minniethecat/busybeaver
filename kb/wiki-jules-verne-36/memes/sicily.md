## Overview

Sicily is the great Mediterranean island crowned by Etna, a landmark of Verne's inland sea. Cape Bon is the most northern promontory of Africa and the point nearest the island of Sicily. (OC-012-005) The very peak of Etna, 11,000 feet above the sea, had vanished with the rest of Sicily. (OC-018-026)

## In the Narrative

In the comet's passage the island is torn from the map. A barrier coincided with the fourteenth meridian and, reaching beyond Sicily to Italy, closed in the sea. (OC-013-017) The castaways cried their hope of a course "To Sicily, Italy, France!" (OC-013-022)

## Geography & Features

Sicily marks an ancient land-bridge beneath the waves. At some remote epoch Cape Bon had been connected with Cape Furina, the extremity of Sicily. (OC-012-006) The Nautilus cut across the whole Strait of Sicily, where soundings prove the two continents were once joined. (TTLU-031-019)

## Connections

Sicily lies in the [[Mediterranean]] off the toe of [[Italy]], from which only a strait divides it. (OC-013-017) Its coasts frame the passage the Nautilus follows in [[Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea]]. (TTLU-031-016)
