## Overview

The Mediterranean is the inland sea whose African shore anchors the opening of *Off on a Comet* — that portion of the Algerian coast is bounded on the north by the Mediterranean, and on the west by the right bank of the Shelif. (OC-006-001)

## In the Narrative

The cosmic catastrophe is measured against it. Whence came it, the survivors ask, that the Mediterranean, one instant emptied of its waters, was the next flooded with a foaming surge? (OC-005-005)

They wonder whether the sea itself has drowned their home — whether the Mediterranean had merely invaded the region of the mouth of the Shelif. (OC-007-007)

## Geography & Features

Its azure waters take the colour of the strange new coast: the waters of the Mediterranean, as they softly kissed the strand, were tinged with the reddish hue of the ferriferous rocks that formed its base. (OC-002-016)

The comet redraws its outline — from the base of the Merdeyah to the Mediterranean, a distance of about eighteen miles, a new coast line had come into existence. (OC-007-034)
