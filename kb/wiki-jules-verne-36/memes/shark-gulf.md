# Shark Gulf

## Overview

Shark Gulf is the narrow, jaw-shaped inlet at the south-eastern extremity of Lincoln Island in *The Mysterious Island*. Herbert coins the name during the colonists' survey, "pointing to the other extremity of the island" and proposing "let us call this gulf which is so singularly like a pair of open jaws, Shark Gulf." (MI-011-065)

Its resemblance to a shark's open mouth, formed by the two Mandible Capes, gives the gulf both its name and its forbidding character.

## In the Narrative

The gulf marks the limit of the colonists' explorations. An early expedition is planned "to that side of the island between the north of the lake and Shark Gulf." (MI-014-027) In a storm Harding orders the *Bonadventure* to "run for shelter into Shark Gulf" (MI-041-055), though Herbert warns of "quarters which appear to be filled with rocks." (MI-041-061)

When the colonists test their cannon, the shot grazes "the rocks of Mandible Cape, nearly five miles from Granite House, and disappear in Shark Gulf." (MI-047-040) Their survey traces "the interminable downs, ending at the open mouth of Shark Gulf." (MI-053-023)

## Geography & Features

The gulf is enclosed by high lava cliffs, which the colonists examine "from the base to the crest, although it was extremely difficult to reach even the level of the gulf." (MI-055-091) Harding advances "towards the enormous causeway whose prolongation enclosed the narrow Shark Gulf." (MI-060-075)

He warns that if the volcano erupts, its lava "would be cast on the downs and the neighboring parts of Shark Gulf." (MI-055-079)

The warning proves true. In the island's final cataclysm all vanishes into the abyss, including "the lava jaws of Shark Gulf, the plateau of Prospect Heights... the long Serpentine Peninsula." (MI-062-002)
