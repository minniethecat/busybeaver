## Overview

Valparaiso is the Chilean port at the heart of the *Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen* voyage — the *Pilgrim* would go to Valparaiso, in Chili, to effect her unloading. (DSCF-001-017)

## In the Narrative

It is the promised landfall. Captain Hull is untroubled by two or three weeks' delay in a passage from New Zealand to Valparaiso, worrying only about the extra fatigue for the crew. (DSCF-005-002)

When the ship is orphaned, reaching it becomes the whole hope: it is no longer a question of taking the *Pilgrim* to Valparaiso. (DSCF-009-045) The passengers can have only one hope — that of reaching some part of the American coast, if not Valparaiso itself. (DSCF-010-003)

## Geography & Features

It is a stop on a longer coastal itinerary — after effecting her unloading at Valparaiso, the *Pilgrim* would ascend the American coast as far as California. (DSCF-004-043)

Its trade drives the plot: Dick counts on melting his quantity of lard at Valparaiso to complete the cargo of oil. (DSCF-007-060)
