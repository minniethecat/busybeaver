## Overview

Cassange is a slave-trade market town of the Benguela interior in *Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen*, named among the seats of the western slave-trade alongside Bihe and Kazounde. (DSCF-019-049) It holds one of the trader Alvez's factories. (DSCF-027-006)

## In the Narrative

Cassange belongs to Alvez's network of slave-factories: his principal establishment lay at Kazounde, a second at Bihe, and a third at Cassange, in Benguela. (DSCF-027-006) From Cassange one of his agents once set out with a caravan of slaves, and vanished. (DSCF-020-018) When ruin spread through the region, Cassange was not spared, its factories among those destroyed. (DSCF-032-025)

## Geography & Features

Cassange lies in the province of Benguela, in the interior of the western slave-trade country. (DSCF-027-006) It stands on the route later followed by David Livingstone, who entered Cassange — where the trader Alvez had seen him passing through — before reaching Saint Paul de Loanda. (DSCF-032-045)

## Connections

Cassange belongs to *Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen* as one of the three Angolan slave-markets that anchor the trader Alvez's dominion, with Bihe and Kazounde. (DSCF-033-015) On his return journey David Livingstone passed by Cassange again, leaving it on the 20th of February. (DSCF-032-046)
