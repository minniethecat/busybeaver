## Overview

The Baraba is a swampy district of Western Siberia; Michael Strogoff enters it on the 30th of July after passing the station of Touroumoff. (MS-016-008)

Its vast marshes form the reservoir for all the rain-water that finds no outlet either towards the Obi or towards the Irtych. (MS-016-010)

## In the Narrative

Crossing the Baraba is a trial. Travellers obliged to pass it in summer provide themselves with masks of horse-hair against its clouds of insects. (MS-016-015) Michael leaves Elamsk at dawn and plunges again into the district as the first Tartar scouts are signalled behind him. (MS-016-022)

The marshes prove more detestable than ever, yet beyond them lies the goal: once free of the Baraba, the courier expects to reach Kolyvan. (MS-016-031)

## Geography & Features

Despite its unhealthiness, the Baraba shelters human settlement — one wonders that so pestilent a district could afford an asylum for human beings. (MS-016-018)

Towns stand in the midst of the swamp; one such lies in the very center of the Baraba. (MS-016-025)
