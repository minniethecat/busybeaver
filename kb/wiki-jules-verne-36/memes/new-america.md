## Overview

New America is the unknown polar land the travellers reach in the far north. When a name was sought for the discovery, "New America," replied Altamont. (ACH-039-053) The name was allowed to stand for the continent itself, while its bays, capes and headlands remained to be named, among them Victoria Bay. (ACH-039-058)

## In the Narrative

It was determined that part of every day should be spent hunting and exploring this unknown continent of New America. (ACH-039-077) The extent of the land was uncertain, and Altamont declared that they must know how far New America reached. (ACH-046-069)

## Geography & Features

New America was evidently either a continent or an island of considerable extent. (ACH-049-003) From one point the country became gradually lower, from which the Doctor concluded that it did not stretch to the Pole, but that most probably New America was an island. (ACH-051-014)

## Connections

New America frames the travellers' movements about the polar coast: on the 15th they sighted Altamont Harbour, but as the sea was open along the shore, they resolved to go round to Victoria Bay by water, rather than crossing New America in the sledge. (ACH-057-016)
