## Overview

Calcutta is the Bengal capital and the eastern terminus of Phileas Fogg's crossing of India, seat of British power over the subcontinent. The British Crown has a governor-general stationed at Calcutta. (AWED-010-002)

## In the Narrative

Fogg's schedule allots three days from Bombay to Calcutta by rail after the sea passage from Suez. (AWED-003-029) Many fellow travellers share the route — the greater part of the passengers from Brindisi were bound for India, some for Bombay, others for Calcutta by way of Bombay. (AWED-009-002) On landing, the timing is exact: at eight the train would start for Calcutta. (AWED-010-006)

The city is also the detective's fixed goal; Fix had resolved to follow the supposed robber to Calcutta, and farther if necessary. (AWED-010-022) When Fogg reaches the East, Hong Kong reminds him of the great Indian ports — not unlike Bombay, Calcutta, and Singapore, all betraying English supremacy. (AWED-019-003)

## Geography & Features

Standing on the Hooghly in Bengal, Calcutta closes the Great Indian Peninsula Railway's long line from Bombay and opens onto the sea route toward Hong Kong and the Pacific.
