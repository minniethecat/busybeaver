## Overview

Sydney is the great eastern port of Australia and the old capital of the colony in *[[In Search of the Castaways]]*. Verne recalls that "for fifteen years the colony was part of New South Wales, and recognized Sydney as the capital" (SC-035-016), until in 1851 the southern district broke away and took the name of Victoria.

The city ranks among the chief towns of the continent. The wandering natives, it is noted, "often go to the large towns, such as Adelaide, Sydney and Melbourne" (SC-038-034), the port set beside [[Adelaide]] and [[Melbourne]] as one of the three great Australian cities.

## In the Narrative

Sydney is a goal the shipwrecked party cannot easily reach. From the desolate shore Glenarvan finds that vessels in the bay were bound for none of the great ports—"none of them were bound for Melbourne, nor Sydney, nor Point de Galle" (SC-046-006)—leaving the castaways cut off from rescue.

The city nonetheless shapes their overland plan. Glenarvan "had nearly decided to follow the coast road to Sydney" (SC-046-007), yet the geographer knew "there were no means of transport for Sydney or Melbourne" (SC-046-008), so that the port remained a distant object of the march.

## Geography & Features

Sydney sits at the head of colonial astronomy and observation. When the strange discovery is announced, it is laughed at "in those of Australia at Sydney, Adelaide, and Melbourne" (RC-001-030), the city's observatory joining its sisters in scorning the report of the aerial monster.

It is likewise the seat from which the colony of Victoria was born. Having recognised Sydney as capital, the southern part "in 1851, she was declared independent, and took the name of Victoria" (SC-035-016), the port standing as the parent city of the new province to its south.
