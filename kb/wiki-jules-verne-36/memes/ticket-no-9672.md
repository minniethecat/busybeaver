## Overview

*Ticket No. 9672* is Jules Verne's tender Norwegian romance, unfolding at a country inn in the Telemark. Its heroine is young Hulda Hansen, who keeps house with her mother, Dame Hansen, and her brother Joel, a mountain guide, in the village of Dal (TN-001-002, TN-001-008).

Hulda's betrothed is the sailor Ole Kamp, gone "almost a year" to "the Newfoundland fishing banks" and long unheard from (TN-001-026).

## Plot Summary

Word comes at last in a letter from Ole, snatched joyfully by Hulda: "It is a letter from Ole!" (TN-001-058). But Ole's ship, the *Viking*, is lost. Wrecked upon an iceberg, "Ole hastily penned his farewell message to his betrothed, and after inclosing it in a bottle, cast it into the sea" — and with it lottery ticket No. 9672 (TN-020-011).

The ticket becomes famous across Norway. As the drawing of the Schools Lottery nears, offers pour in — "ten thousand marks have been offered for ticket 9672" — yet in Hulda's hands the token of her lost lover is beyond price (TN-017-015, TN-017-026). The family is befriended and championed by the genial deputy Professor Sylvius Hogg.

Against all hope, Ole Kamp survives, cast upon Greenland and borne home by the Danish brig *Genius* (TN-020-012, TN-020-007). He returns in time for the drawing, and "the young mate of the 'Viking'... had won the grand prize of the Schools Lottery" — one hundred thousand marks — before wedding Hulda (TN-020-003, TN-020-020).

## Main Characters

- Hulda Hansen — the innkeeper's daughter who keeps her lost lover's ticket
- Ole Kamp — her betrothed, mate of the *Viking*, wrecked and given up for dead
- Joel — Hulda's brother, a guide of the Telemark
- Professor Sylvius Hogg — the deputy who befriends and champions the family

## Key Places & Technology

- Dal — the Telemark village whose inn the Hansen family keeps
- Ticket No. 9672 — Ole's farewell lottery ticket, sealed in a bottle and set adrift

## Themes

Verne sets aside his machines for a gentle idyll of Norwegian landscape and constancy, where love outlasts shipwreck and rumor. The lottery ticket, worthless as chance yet priceless as a keepsake, lets him weigh sentiment against speculation and let devotion win.
