## Overview

Pennsylvania is a Mid-Atlantic state of the United States. In the Vernean corpus it is above all the home of the Weldon Institute in Philadelphia, the ballooning club at the heart of *Robur the Conqueror*.

It also figures as the home state of the freedmen in *Dick Sand, A Captain at Fifteen*, as a theatre of the Terror's exploits in *The Master of the World*, and as the site of a petroleum mine in *The Waif of the "Cynthia"*.

## In the Narrative

In *Robur the Conqueror*, the Weldon Institute — "the well-known club in Walnut Street, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania" — is where Robur bursts in upon the balloonists. (RC-002-016) When his airship the Albatross vanishes from the Pennsylvania capital, the newspapers of Philadelphia and of the whole state report the affair in a hundred wrong ways. (RC-005-037)

The Albatross travels "in a straight line from the Pennsylvania capital," (RC-009-011) and later, when it reappears in Fairmount Park, trains pour into the Pennsylvania capital carrying sightseers from the neighbouring states. (RC-022-014)

In *Dick Sand, A Captain at Fifteen*, old Tom draws himself up to declare of his companions, "We are subjects of the State of Pennsylvania, and citizens of free America!" (DSCF-004-023) Their hope throughout is to return to the State of Pennsylvania and the household of James W. Weldon. (DSCF-004-043)

In *The Master of the World*, Pennsylvania is one of the theatres of the mysterious machine's "sportive eccentricities." (MW-004-013) In *The Waif of the "Cynthia"*, Erik's inheritance rests on a petroleum mine discovered in Pennsylvania. (WC-022-004)

## Geography & Features

Pennsylvania is presented as an eastern industrial state whose principal city, Philadelphia, Verne treats as its capital and the site of Fairmount Park. (RC-022-014)

Its resources include the petroleum fields worked in the hills of the state, the basis of a considerable fortune in *The Waif of the "Cynthia"*. (WC-022-004)

## Connections

Pennsylvania is the launching point of *Robur the Conqueror*: the Weldon Institute's balloonists in Philadelphia are the foil against whom Robur pits his heavier-than-air Albatross. (RC-002-016)

Through the freedmen of *Dick Sand, A Captain at Fifteen* it also stands as a symbol of free America — the State of Pennsylvania to which old Tom and his companions long to return, and where the household of James W. Weldon awaits them. (DSCF-004-043)
