## Overview

The Aral Sea is the great inland sea of Central Asia, lying between the Ural steppe and the plateau of the [[Pamir]]. It is drying up, as the Caspian will dry up, an evaporation explained by the powerful concentration of the solar rays on the territories between the Sea of Aral and the plateau of the Pamir. (ASC-006-027)

## In the Narrative

In [[The Adventures of a Special Correspondent]] the Aral Sea bounds the Central Asian steppe. Its wandering Mahometan hordes range between the [[Ural Mountains]] and the Aral Sea. (ASC-006-020) A river that maps once show as a tributary of the Caspian now flows instead into the Sea of Aral. (ASC-010-064)

## Geography & Features

The Aral Sea stands apart from its neighbours. The Caspian is not in communication with the Black Sea or the Sea of Aral, being at a much lower level than they are. (RC-013-056) In an older age the Caspian and Aral Seas joined certain large Asian lakes to form a single uniform expanse of water. (TTLU-014-027)

## Connections

The Aral Sea is bracketed with the [[Caspian Sea]] as a drying remnant of that vanished expanse. (TTLU-014-027) It lies beyond the [[Ural Mountains]] on the road of the Central Asian hordes. (ASC-006-020)
