## Overview

Tai-Youan is a station city of northern China on the Grand Transasiatic railway, reached by Claudius Bombarnac's train late in its passage through the Celestial Empire. In the Vernean corpus it belongs to *The Adventures of a Special Correspondent*, where it is remembered as the depot from which a relief engine is summoned when the train is stranded further down the line. (ASC-025-061)

## In the Narrative

The train runs through gorges and ravines, past "the valley of Lou-Ngan, where we stopped at eleven o'clock," and on amid "wreaths of a sort of yellow steam" until it halts "about ten o'clock at night at Tai-Youan." (ASC-024-009)

Later, when the locomotive has been destroyed down the line, the order goes out: "Go to the nearest station, that of Fuen Choo, and telegraph to Tai-Youan for them to send on a relief engine." (ASC-025-061) Pan-Chao explains matters to the stationmaster, "who telegraphed for an engine to be sent from Tai-Youan to the Nanking line." (ASC-025-071)

## Geography & Features

Tai-Youan lies beyond the valley of Lou-Ngan, some two hundred and thirty kilometres of steam-veiled country from the previous stop. (ASC-024-009) It stands as a railway depot able to dispatch locomotives at need — "before three o'clock the engine from Tai-Youan ought to be here." (ASC-025-064)

## Connections

Tai-Youan is bound to *The Adventures of a Special Correspondent* as a station of the Grand Transasiatic and the source of the relief engine that rescues the stranded train. (ASC-025-071) Because help came forward, "it was not necessary for us to return to Tai-Youan, and we thus saved a delay of an hour and a half." (ASC-025-076)
