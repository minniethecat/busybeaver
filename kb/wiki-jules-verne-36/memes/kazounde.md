# Kazounde

## Overview

Kazounde is the great inland slave-market town of Angola toward which the captives are driven in *Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen*. Dick Sand, "being posted in the facts of modern geography, knew very exactly what is known of Kazounde," (DSCF-025-036) and its very name signals the doom of the captured party: "a very great trade in slaves was carried on there." (DSCF-025-035)

## In the Narrative

The town is the destination of the terrible march. The trafficker's men drive their column onward — "this numerous procession of natives, snatched from their villages by the trader Alvez's agents, were going to the Kazounde market." (DSCF-025-007) The prisoners' worth is reckoned by it: the four strong companions of Mrs. Weldon "will bring a high price in the Kazounde market." (DSCF-020-081)

At its height the market is a spectacle of commerce and cruelty alike. "What made the Kazounde market still more curious was the commerce in stuffs and ivory," (DSCF-028-022) traded beside the human cargo, and on the day of the great sale "the city of Kazounde presented a strange aspect." (DSCF-030-002)

## Geography & Features

The town lies deep in the Angolan interior, a fixed distance from the coast: "Kazounde, situated three hundred miles from the mouth of the Coanza, is one of the principal 'lakonis,' one of the most important markets of the province." (DSCF-027-004) It ranks among the notorious trading seats of the region, named with "its important markets of Bihe, Cassange, and Kazounde." (DSCF-019-049)

Ruled by a native king whose court the trader must court, it is a place where "King Loungga is welcome to the market of Kazounde." (DSCF-029-028)
