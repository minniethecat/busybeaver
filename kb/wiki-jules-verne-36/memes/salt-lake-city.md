## Overview

Salt Lake City is the Mormon capital of Utah that Phileas Fogg passes on his transcontinental crossing in *[[Around the World in Eighty Days]]*. The train "reached Ogden at two o'clock, where it rested for six hours, Mr. Fogg and his party had time to pay a visit to Salt Lake City, connected with Ogden by a branch road" (AWED-027-020), and they spent two hours in the strikingly American town.

It is recognised at once as the seat of the Mormons. Passing above it in *[[Robur the Conqueror]]*, the aeronauts declare "that ought to be the Mormon capital, Salt Lake City" (RC-010-032), the city's fame fixing it unmistakably.

## In the Narrative

Salt Lake City lies squarely on the great railway line across the continent. The road, after leaving [[Omaha]], "passes along the left bank of the [[Platte River]]... turns the [[Great Salt Lake]]" (AWED-026-006) before reaching the Mormon town, the city set on the iron highway that binds the Union.

It is also seen from the air on the flight of the Albatross. The aeronauts note below them "a train on the Pacific Railway on the road to Salt Lake City" (RC-010-026), the city drawing the rails toward it across the western plateau.

## Geography & Features

Salt Lake City sits beside the vast inland sea that gives it its name. The transcontinental route "turns the [[Great Salt Lake]]" (AWED-026-006) as it approaches the town, the city taking its identity from the great salt water at its side.

Its people leave the strongest impression on the traveller. Passepartout, wandering the streets, imagines that "the fair ones of Salt Lake City cast rather alarming glances on his person" (AWED-027-022), the Mormon town's character stamped on every passing face.
