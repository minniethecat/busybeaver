# Creek Glycerine

## Overview

Creek Glycerine is the fictional stream the colonists of Lincoln Island cut to draw off the waters of Lake Grant. A trench "made by the same means in the rocky border of the lake" formed "a small stream, to which they gave the name of Creek Glycerine, and which was thus an affluent of the Mercy." (MI-029-035)

The little watercourse becomes both a working channel and a natural moat before Prospect Heights.

## In the Narrative

The creek serves the settlers' daily labour: the tamed onager is set "drawing loads of wood and carting away the stones which were extracted from the bed of Creek Glycerine." (MI-029-038) A bridge spans it, which Pencroft "lowered" to trap the wild asses. (MI-029-052)

It later marks a line of defence. When Ayrton's rescue is planned, Neb "raised the bridge" behind them at the creek. (MI-048-064) In the war against the convicts, "it was only during the preceding night that the convicts had appeared on the edge of the forest, at the approaches to Creek Glycerine." (MI-052-003)

## Geography & Features

The stream divides settled ground from wild — "situated on the other side of Creek Glycerine, its inhabitants could not reach the plateau nor ravage the newly-made plantation," (MI-030-012) for it "separated the plateau from the forest." (MI-038-098)

It is fed with Falls River from the lake: "to raise this level they had only to establish a dam at the two openings made by the lake, and by which were fed Creek Glycerine and Falls River." (MI-048-006) In the island's death throes the swollen stream "stretched beyond Creek Glycerine, and invaded the plateau of Prospect Heights." (MI-061-078)
