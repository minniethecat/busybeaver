## Overview

Guinea is the West African coastal region, together with the Gulf of Guinea that bears its name, and it serves in the Vernean corpus as a landmark of African exploration. In *Five Weeks in a Balloon* the Gulf of Guinea marks a point of reference for Dr. Ferguson's aerial voyage, while its coasts fringe the wilderness he must cross.

It reappears in *Robur the Conqueror*, over whose northern mountains the *Albatross* passes, and in *Dick Sand, A Captain at Fifteen* as the seat of the colonies of Lower Guinea.

## In the Narrative

In *Five Weeks in a Balloon*, Dr. Ferguson fixes his position by the Gulf, judging from his solar observations that the balloon is "not more than three hundred miles from the Gulf of Guinea." (FWB-024-049)

The doctor reflects that the watershed of the interior determines whether its rivers flow "to the Gulf of Guinea on the one hand, or to the bay of Cape Verde on the other." (FWB-041-002) Yet the coasts themselves are perilous, infested by the "barbarous tribes that infest the coasts of Guinea." (FWB-040-004)

In *Robur the Conqueror*, the *Albatross* "crossed the mountains of northern Guinea, between the Sudan and the gulf which bears their name," (RC-015-050) and the projected Trans-Saharan Railway is imagined running down at last into the Gulf of Guinea. (RC-015-013)

In *Dick Sand, A Captain at Fifteen*, the castaways are reassured that help "would not fail them in the colonies of Lower Guinea." (DSCF-035-040)

## Geography & Features

Verne presents Guinea as the seaward edge of tropical Africa, its gulf receiving the drainage of the interior: the watershed of the Niger and Senegal basins turns the waters either toward the Gulf of Guinea or toward the bay of Cape Verde. (FWB-041-002) Its mountains rise in the north, between the Sudan and the gulf which bears their name. (RC-015-050)

The coast is settled and colonised — the colonies of Lower Guinea offer refuge (DSCF-035-040) — yet beyond them lie the tribes that infest the shore. (FWB-040-004)

## Connections

Guinea is bound above all to *Five Weeks in a Balloon*, in which Dr. Ferguson gauges his progress by his distance from the Gulf of Guinea. (FWB-024-049) It joins *Robur the Conqueror* through the *Albatross*, which crosses the mountains of northern Guinea between the Sudan and the gulf. (RC-015-050)

And it links to *Dick Sand, A Captain at Fifteen* through the colonies of Lower Guinea, where the wanderers hope to find help. (DSCF-035-040)
