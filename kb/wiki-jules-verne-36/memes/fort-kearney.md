# Fort Kearney

## Overview

Fort Kearney is the real garrison station on the Pacific Railroad where Phileas Fogg's westward run is broken by a Sioux attack. The fort's soldiers are the travellers' one hope of safety: "Fort Kearney station, where there was a garrison, was only two miles distant." (AWED-029-061)

The runaway train, its brakes at last biting, halts "less than a hundred feet from Kearney station." (AWED-029-067)

## In the Narrative

At the station "the commanding officer of Fort Kearney was there," (AWED-030-010) and Fogg appeals to him for a party to recover the captured Passepartout. The soldiers "had met and fought the Indians ten miles south of Fort Kearney." (AWED-030-060)

Fogg's dilemma is acute: unwilling "to expose Aouda to the discomforts of travelling in the open air, Mr. Fogg proposed to leave her with Passepartout at Fort Kearney." (AWED-031-017) When the rescue succeeds, "he could now leave Fort Kearney station, and pursue his journey homeward in peace." (AWED-030-035)

## Geography & Features

The fort commands a stretch of the transcontinental line across the plains. The abandoned locomotive, its fire dying, "finally stopped an hour after, some twenty miles beyond Fort Kearney," (AWED-030-037) before the engineer "rebuild the fire in the furnace... and the locomotive returned, running backwards to Fort Kearney." (AWED-030-038)
