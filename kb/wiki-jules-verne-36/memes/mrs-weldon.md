# Mrs. Weldon

## Overview

Mrs. Weldon is "the wife of the 'Pilgrim's' owner," found at Auckland "with her young son Jack, aged about five years, and one of her relatives, her Cousin Benedict" (DSCF-001-013).

She is no timid passenger: "Mrs. Weldon was a courageous woman, whom the sea did not frighten" (DSCF-001-018).

## Role in the Story

Wishing to return home, "Mrs. Weldon and her child were returning to America by the 'Pilgrim'" (DSCF-001-050). Captain Hull warns her plainly that "if you take passage on board the 'Pilgrim,' it is on your own responsibility" (DSCF-001-056); she answers by invoking her absent husband's confidence in the ship (DSCF-001-059).

Aboard, "Mrs. Weldon had been installed on board the 'Pilgrim' as comfortably as possible" (DSCF-002-003).

Her compassion drives events: sighting the derelict, it is she who says "perhaps there are some unhappy shipwrecked ones on that raft" (DSCF-003-008). After Dick Sand brings the survivors through storm to land, she vows, "my husband and I, we shall never forget what you have just done" (DSCF-013-017).

In Africa her wariness sharpens; troubled by the cook's vanishing, she asks "what is Negoro doing?" (DSCF-014-020), and later, "made suspicious first of all by Negoro's disappearance, observed the newly-arrived with extreme attention" (DSCF-015-054).

## Character & Traits

She meets misfortune with equanimity: "Mrs. Weldon did not complain, and philosophically took her misfortune in patience" (DSCF-005-003). She holds firm moral convictions, declaring to the stranger Harris, "We have no longer any slaves in the United States... The North abolished slavery long ago" (DSCF-015-070).

Her maternal warmth extends beyond her own child; toward the young novice she felt that "Mrs. Weldon regarded him as her child--a large elder brother of her little Jack" (DSCF-001-023).

## Relationships

- [[Dick Sand]] — the fifteen-year-old apprentice she cherishes as a son and who becomes the party's captain and protector.
- [[Cousin Benedict]] — her entomologist kinsman and travelling charge.
- Captain Hull — the Pilgrim's master who takes her aboard on her own responsibility, praising Dick Sand as "an excellent person... very superior to his age" in her hearing (DSCF-005-022).
