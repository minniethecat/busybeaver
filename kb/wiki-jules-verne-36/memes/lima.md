## Overview

Lima is the Peruvian capital that gives The Pearl of Lima its name and stage. The city of Lima lies in the valley of the Rimac, nine leagues from its mouth, where the first undulations of the great chain begin. (PL-002-004)

## In the Narrative

The town sits more than four hundred feet above the level of the sea, about a hundred and fifty rods from the bridge thrown over the river Rimac. (PL-002-003) Through its wealthy quarters the conspirators move by night; one returns exhausted to Lima as the cathedral clock is striking four. (PL-008-004)

When the rising comes, its fury centers on the capital — "Imagine the aspect presented by Lima at this terrible moment." (PL-008-018) Yet the revolt fails: though it triumphed in some places, it had received its death-blow at Lima. (PL-009-009) In the aftermath Don Vegal regained Lima, where he died with grief and exhaustion. (PL-009-068)

## Geography & Features

Lima occupies the Rimac valley on Peru's Pacific slope, its cathedral and bridge fixing the heart of the old colonial city between the sea and the rising Andean foothills.
