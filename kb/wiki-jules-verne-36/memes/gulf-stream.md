# Gulf Stream

## Overview

The Gulf Stream is the real Atlantic current the *Nautilus* follows in *Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Seas*. Aronnax presents it as the ocean's most famous flow — "the most remarkable being the one called the Gulf Stream." (TTLU-014-027)

Its fame is universal: "No one is unaware of the existence of that great warm-water current known by name as the Gulf Stream." (TTLU-035-003)

## In the Narrative

Captain Nemo steers his vessel into it. Aronnax names the object of their course plainly — "I mean the Gulf Stream." (TTLU-043-006) He marvels at its warmth against the cold ocean: "the water temperature of the Gulf Stream, which, as it leaves the Gulf of Mexico, is barely different from your blood temperature." (TTLU-043-012)

By night it glowed — "the Gulf Stream's phosphorescent waters rivaled the electric glow of our beacon." (TTLU-043-016) The current also brings storm, "the very homeland of tornadoes and cyclones specifically engendered by the Gulf Stream's current." (TTLU-043-018)

## Geography & Features

Aronnax traces its source and course. "The true source of the Gulf Stream, its starting point, if you prefer, is located in the Bay of Biscay." (TTLU-043-008) Leaving the Old Bahama Channel, "the Gulf Stream moves at the rate of eight kilometers per hour." (TTLU-043-009)

Its bounds are sharp: "There the Gulf Stream is seventy-five miles wide and 210 meters deep." (TTLU-043-017) So distinct is its edge that "the *Nautilus*'s spur cut the waves of the Gulf Stream while its propeller was still churning those belonging to the ocean." (TTLU-043-013) It is what decks "the coasts of Europe... in eternal greenery." (TTLU-043-012)
