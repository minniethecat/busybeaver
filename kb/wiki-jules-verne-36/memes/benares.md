## Overview

Benares is the holy city on the Ganges where Fogg's companion leaves the party. Benares was Sir Francis Cromarty's destination, the troops he was rejoining being encamped some miles northward of the city. (AWED-014-023) The general had boarded at Bombay on his way to join his corps here. (AWED-011-002)

## In the Narrative

The party reaches it by rail after the elephant crossing. Phileas Fogg, Sir Francis Cromarty, and Passepartout, installed in a carriage with Aouda, were whirling at full speed towards Benares. (AWED-014-019) At half-past twelve the train stopped at Benares. (AWED-014-022)

Verne pauses on the city's antiquity — the Brahmin legends assert that it is built on the site of the ancient Casi, which, like Mahomet's tomb, was once suspended between heaven and earth. From here the route follows the great river — the railway, on leaving Benares, passed for a while along the valley of the Ganges. (AWED-014-024)

## Geography & Features

An ancient sacred city on the Ganges in northern India, Benares is a station on the Great Indian Peninsula Railway east of the Allahabad gap — the point where Sir Francis Cromarty parts from Fogg to rejoin his brigade.
