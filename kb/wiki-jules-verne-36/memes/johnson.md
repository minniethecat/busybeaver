# Johnson

## Overview

He is engaged as boatswain of the brig Forward, for "Shandon told him and Johnson (whom he engaged as boatswain) all he knew about the business" (ACH-003-006).

He proves "a valuable acquisition; he understood the navigation of these high latitudes" (ACH-003-014), and even before sailing the boatswain "had thought right to express his opinion to Clifton's friend" upon the ship's soundness (ACH-001-049).

## Role in the Story

He counsels the commander on the eve of departure, urging that "if you will allow me to advise you, you will prepare everything to start; the crew must think that you know what you are about" (ACH-004-026).

An old Arctic hand, "Johnson was used to struggle with the sea; he was an old stager in the Arctic Ocean, and had nothing to learn either in audacity or sang-froid" (ACH-005-041).

When mutiny flares he helps restore order, for "Johnson and Bell disarmed Pen, who no longer made any resistance, and placed him in the hold" (ACH-022-021), being "besides a very strict observer of discipline" (ACH-009-064).

## Character & Traits

He is defined by plain obedience, "a sailor who only cares to obey" (ACH-018-028).

He is inured to the cold, and "Johnson went to fetch some lumps of the new fuel... and soon obtained enough heat" from the stove (ACH-027-002).

He revives frozen companions, and "by dint of shaking and rubbing him with snow, he succeeded" in rousing a stricken man (ACH-033-051), afterward resuming his place "half-frozen with their work" (ACH-033-073).

He tends the sled-dogs, obliged "to take the dogs inside the hut, for they would have been soon frozen outside in such dry weather" (ACH-036-002).

## Relationships

- [[Captain Hatteras]] — the commander he longs to follow, who "took him aside" before the polar march yet bade him turn back to the ship (ACH-028-006).

- [[Dr Clawbonny]] — his companion in long deck-side talks, to whom "Johnson used to say" his weather-lore of Greenland (ACH-007-004).

- [[Richard Shandon]] — the commander who engaged him as boatswain and confided to him the Forward's secret orders (ACH-003-006).

## Appearances

- [[The Adventures of Captain Hatteras]] — the Arctic voyage of the Forward, on which Hatteras tells him "you did all you could to save my ship" (ACH-034-023).
