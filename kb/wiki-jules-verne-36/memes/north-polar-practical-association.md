# North Polar Practical Association

## Overview

The North Polar Practical Association is the mysterious American company at the centre of *The Purchase of the North Pole* (*Topsy-Turvy*). "A company has been founded at Baltimore under the name of the North Polar Practical Association, representing officially the American Union" (TT-001-039).

From the first the Association is deliberately obscure. "This North Polar Association had been so anonymous that it was impossible even to give it a definite name" (TT-001-054). It advertises only a go-between: "Address for information Mr. W.S. Forster, Temporary Agent for the North Polar Practical Association, 93 High Street, Baltimore" (TT-001-041).

## Role in the Story

The Association's scheme is to buy the unclaimed lands around the North Pole. It "wanted then a country which had never been touched before by mankind or discoverers, and which was absolutely uninhabited" (TT-001-056), reasoning that "the proprietorship was clear, and nobody being compelled to live there could object to the auction sale" (TT-001-057).

Under its own proposal "all countries had to be consulted and given a chance at the auction" (TT-002-009), yet "up to this time America had only been represented by Mr. W.S. Forster, of the North Polar Practical Association" (TT-002-011). Rival nations each try "to open communications with the North Polar Practical Association separately, unknown to the other" (TT-002-021).

The company's true identity is the twist of the tale. When Forster deposits the winning bid, "the name he gave was Impey Barbicane and the name of the house was Barbicane & Co." (TT-003-045) — the Association is a screen for the Gun Club. Its purpose is revealed as an engineering scheme to reach and exploit the Pole: "We will pass it---reach even the North Pole," Barbicane declares (TT-005-014).

## Members

The Association's public face and its hidden principals are drawn almost entirely from the Baltimore [[Gun Club]].

- W.S. Forster — the "Temporary Agent" who fronts the company at 93 High Street, Baltimore (TT-001-041)

- [[Impey Barbicane]] — President of the Gun Club and the true head of "Barbicane & Co." (TT-003-045)

- [[J.T. Maston]] — Secretary of the Gun Club, "a remarkable calculator" who computes the scheme (TT-006-003)

- The [[Gun Club]] — whose members stand "lost in the crowd" behind the Association at the auction (TT-003-012)

The firm was to be "presided over by the President of the Gun Club" (TT-004-011). Against it stand foreign rivals such as Major Donellan and Boris Karkof, "semi-military man, semi-diplomat" (TT-002-016), who suspect the American bidders.

## Activities

The Association's business is financial and speculative: to purchase polar territory and float the shares that will pay for exploiting it. Once its plan is public, "shares were issued at $100, to be paid for at once, and the credit of Barbicane & Co. was such that the money ran in as fast as possible" (TT-005-017).

Capital pours in beyond need: "the amount necessary to be subscribed was raised in a very short time, and even thrice the amount" (TT-005-018) — "about three times as much as the amount subscribed to the credit of the Gun Club when it was going to send a projectile from the earth to the moon" (TT-005-019).

The company then summons its investors to act. "On the 22nd of December the subscribers to Barbicane & Co. were summoned to a general meeting" (TT-007-002), a "commercial and peaceful meeting over which Impey Barbicane was going to preside" (TT-007-003), where he asks the stockholders "to rise and go with me to the North Pole immediately" (TT-007-007).
