# Stockholm

## Overview

Stockholm is the Swedish capital and the home of Dr. Schwaryencrona in *The Waif of the Cynthia*. The novel opens on the doctor's renown there, declaring that "there is probably neither in Europe nor anywhere else a scholar whose face is more universally known than that of Dr. Schwaryencrona, of Stockholm." (WC-001-002)

## In the Narrative

The city is the destination that lifts the foundling Erik out of his fishing hamlet. The doctor makes it his single condition — "that you let me take the boy to Stockholm" (WC-003-024) — over the foster-family's grief at the words "Send him to Stockholm!" (WC-003-026)

The doctor soothes their fear of the sea's distance, reminding them "we have the sea around us also at Stockholm," (WC-003-034) and that "the child is not going away forever." (WC-003-046) Years later the city broadcasts the solution, when "Erik's revelations filled the columns of the most largely circulated newspaper in Stockholm, and indeed in all Sweden." (WC-020-035)

## Geography & Features

The capital is a city ringed by water, as the doctor himself insists in likening it to Erik's coastal home. (WC-003-034) It is a place of wealth and standing, where a risen man's "residence in Stockholm is a perfect palace." (WC-002-033)

It is also a port from which great voyages depart: it is recorded that "on the 16th of August, 1879, the 'Alaska' left Stockholm" to attempt the first circumpolar passage. (WC-017-028)
