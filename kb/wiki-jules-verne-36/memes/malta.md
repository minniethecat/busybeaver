## Overview

Malta is the fortified Mediterranean island whose knights and cannon Verne invokes across his tales. At Malta, in the time of its knights, a cannon of Fort Saint Elme hurled projectiles weighing 2,500 lbs. (FEM-007-055) England would never consent to see Malta transformed into a mountain-top lost in the clouds. (TT-016-005)

## In the Narrative

In the comet's passage the island is swallowed whole. When the sounding-line was dropped upon its site, Malta was gone. (OC-013-019) "Malta--town, forts, soldiers, governor, and all--has vanished," cried Servadac. (OC-015-040) The colonel replied coolly that it mattered because Malta belongs to England. (OC-015-044)

## Geography & Features

Malta long guarded the sea-road east. To sail from Malta to Gibraltar by an eastward course once meant passing the Suez Canal, the Red Sea, and the oceans beyond. (OC-016-002) The steamer's course would bring her to Malta on the twenty-second, and thence to Alexandria and Aden. (WC-012-049)

## Connections

Malta commands the [[Mediterranean]] between the fortress of [[Gibraltar]] and the Levant. (OC-016-012) Its disappearance is the central marvel of [[Off on a Comet]]. (OC-015-040)
