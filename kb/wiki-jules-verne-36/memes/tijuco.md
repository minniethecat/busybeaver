## Overview

Tijuco is a real town in Brazil's diamond district, the scene of the old crime that hangs over Joam Dacosta throughout Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon. Once the most forward in accusing him as the principal author of the crime of Tijuco, some now aver that he is not guilty. (EHLA-034-001)

## In the Narrative

The whole plot turns on a cipher that alone can clear Dacosta. Reasoning over it, Judge Jarriquez expects the document to contain "two or three words... 'arrayal,' 'diamond,' 'Tijuco,' 'Dacosta,' and others." (EHLA-034-012) The precious paper is thought to be the document written in the very hand of the true author of the crime of Tijuco. (EHLA-031-051)

To crack the number key, the judge tries dates known to the criminal, among them the date of the sentence at Tijuco. (EHLA-034-050) The search widens to the gang of the ex-captain of the woods, hunting the probable author of this cipher document. (EHLA-035-005)

## Geography & Features

Lying in the Minas Gerais diamond region of interior Brazil, Tijuco is the origin point of the injustice that sends Dacosta down the Amazon toward Belem in search of vindication.
