## Overview

Constantinople is the storied capital whose lordship passed to Count Baldwin, afterwards Emperor of Constantinople. (DOSE-001-002) It stands among the principal cities catalogued when Verne fixes the hours of the world's meridians. (TT-019-010)

## In the Narrative

The city looms large in the history of great guns: at the siege of Constantinople by Mahomet II in 1453, cannon hurled stone bullets weighing 1,900 pounds. (FEM-007-055) It was also a stage of early flight, remembered as the place where the Flying Saracen met his death. (RC-004-046)

## Geography & Features

As a financial centre it hosted the Ottoman Bank, which took subscriptions for the Gun Club's lunar venture. (FEM-012-009) Its position on the world's clock is precise: at Constantinople the reckoning stood at 11.26 p.m. (TT-019-010)

## Connections

Constantinople's artillery legend feeds directly into the great-gun lore of [[From the Earth to the Moon]] and its [[Gun Club]]. (FEM-007-055) It ranks among the European capitals whose observatories and banks Verne enumerates. (FEM-012-009)
