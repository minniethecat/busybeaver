# Pamlico Sound

## Overview

Pamlico Sound is the real North Carolina sound where the mysterious schooner *Ebba* lies at the opening of *Facing the Flag*. The vessel is introduced "on board the schooner Ebba, anchored off New-Berne, Pamlico Sound." (FF-001-005)

It is a wide, sheltered water, "a sort of vast maritime lake" into which the Neuse River empties. (FF-001-006)

## In the Narrative

The sound is the stage for the abduction that opens the story. After the kidnapping "it only remained for the *Ebba* to weigh anchor, descend the estuary" and make for the open sea. (FF-003-102)

The authorities move to seal it — the Governor "instantly wired orders that no vessel was to be allowed to quit Pamlico Sound without having been first" searched. (FF-004-016) Every cutter and launch "was sent out with orders to patrol Pamlico Sound and board yachts." (FF-004-025) Yet the captive is spirited across it unseen, in a vessel able to "clear Pamlico Sound." (FF-005-053)

## Geography & Features

The sound is fed by the Neuse, whose estuary "curves abruptly and shoots off towards the northwest" some twenty miles from New-Berne. (FF-004-028) Its calm puzzles the captive narrator: "How is it that Pamlico Sound is so extraordinarily calm?" (FF-005-043)

It opens to the Atlantic by narrow passes — a vessel leaving "issued by Ocracoke or Hatteras inlet." (FF-005-053) So enclosed is it that its passes could be watched and every ship within it stopped and searched. (FF-004-020)
