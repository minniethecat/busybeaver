# Bergen

## Overview

Bergen is the Norwegian west-coast port that is the seafaring home of Ole Kamp in *Ticket No. 9672*. It is a town of sailors and fishing fleets, where Ole "had served his apprenticeship under his father, who was the master of a Bergen coasting vessel." (TN-004-003) From its harbour the *Viking* sets out on the cruise that sets the whole story in motion.

## In the Narrative

The port is the anchor of Ole's fortunes and the family's hopes. His ship carries a rich cargo home: "the 'Viking' brings back nearly five thousand quintals, deliverable at Bergen, and already sold by the efforts of Help Bros." (TN-001-087) Ole begs his kin not to trouble themselves to "come to Bergen to meet me." (TN-001-086)

The town vouches for its own men, who are "well known and highly respected by all the sailors of Bergen," (TN-004-039) and when the *Viking* is overdue it is to Bergen that Sylvius Hogg proposes to turn: "if you desire it, I will go to Bergen, and make inquiries there." (TN-007-009)

## Geography & Features

Bergen lies at the western end of the great horn of southern Norway, opposite the capital, for "the Telemark composes the broad portion of the immense horn that Norway forms between Bergen and Christiania." (TN-002-004)

It is above all a fishing port, whose crews range far out into the northern seas — the very "fishing cruise which had enticed the inhabitants of Bergen so far from their native land." (TN-001-075)
