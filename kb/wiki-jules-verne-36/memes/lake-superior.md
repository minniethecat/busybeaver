# Lake Superior

## Overview

Lake Superior is the largest of the North American Great Lakes and one of the waters across which the elusive machine of *The Master of the World* appears. Its sudden report there deepens the mystery of the vehicle's reach: the vessel "had not been seen since he was reported from Lake Superior," (MW-011-027) marking the lake as a key point in the chase.

The sighting there is among the strangest in the whole pursuit.

## In the Narrative

The machine's appearance on the lake baffles the investigators, who cannot account for how it crossed the intervening land. If it "had indeed appeared on Lake Superior, how had he covered all the intervening territory unseen?" (MW-010-051) The report becomes a fixed reference: "the last time she had been seen was on Lake Superior." (MW-011-049)

The narrator recalls the moment of the encounter — that the observer found himself "in the very middle of Lake Superior." (MW-008-040)

## Geography & Features

The lake belongs to the great chain of inland seas whose waters flow eastward toward the Atlantic. It empties, with its sister lakes, through the mighty river system: the current "empties the waters of Lake Superior, Michigan, Huron, and Erie into Ontario, the last lake of this mighty chain." (MW-014-046)

Its links to the neighbouring lakes offer the fugitive machine a route of escape: it might have "reascended the Detroit River, and entered Lake Huron, or even Lake Superior beyond." (MW-013-008)
