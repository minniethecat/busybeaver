# Iquitos

## Overview

Iquitos is the Upper Amazon village where the Garral family's fazenda stands and from which the great raft voyage begins in *Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon*. Like its neighbours it is a mission foundation: "Iquitos, like every other collection of huts, hamlet, or village met with in the basin of the Upper Amazon, was founded by the missionaries." (EHLA-003-002)

## In the Narrative

The village is the home the story sets out from. It is there that "Joam Garral was introduced to the farm at Iquitos," (EHLA-003-012) rising until "in 1830 Joam Garral became the new fazender of Iquitos, to the immense satisfaction of all those who composed the staff of the farm." (EHLA-003-027)

Its parish church binds the family to the place, for "Joam and Yaquita had there received the nuptial benediction of the Padre Passanha, who was then the curate of Iquitos parish." (EHLA-004-009) The whole downstream drama is measured against it: the fugitive's peril is that "to return to Iquitos was to follow a road full of difficulties and peril." (EHLA-036-019)

## Geography & Features

The settlement lies among the river's islands near the Peruvian frontier. Setting out, the travellers pass "between the islands of Iquitos and Parianta" to reach "the right bank of the Amazon." (EHLA-007-005) Its surrounding forests teem with game and birds — "the forests of Iquitos... with their wild beasts, agoutis, peccaries, and cabiais." (EHLA-010-051)

Under the Garrals' hand the farm prospered until "the fazenda had become one of the richest establishments on the Upper Amazon." (EHLA-003-018)
