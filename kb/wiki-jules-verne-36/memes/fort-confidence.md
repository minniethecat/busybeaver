# Fort Confidence

## Overview

Fort Confidence is the real fur-trading post on Great Bear Lake that Lieutenant Jaspar Hobson makes for early in *The Fur Country*. His orders send him there — "Jaspar Hobson was to make for Fort Confidence, on the northern extremity of the latter lake." (FC-005-007)

He seeks both supplies and news: "He was anxious to get to Fort Confidence, on the further side of the Great Bear Lake, where he hoped to obtain some useful information." (FC-007-003)

## In the Narrative

The fort is a waypoint on the expedition's northward march. Hobson is "most anxious to reach Fort Confidence, built on the northern extremity of the Great Bear Lake." (FC-008-003) There the factor "showed the greatest readiness to assist the views of the Lieutenant." (FC-008-007)

The party rests and reprovisions — "After a walk of three hours the visitors returned to Fort Confidence, where a good meal of fish and fresh venison awaited them." (FC-008-034) Its little fishing-boat carries Mrs Barnett and Hobson out onto the lake. (FC-008-019)

## Geography & Features

The post stands at a striking spur of the lake, "at the end of the 'right paw,' at least two hundred miles from Coronation Gulf." (FC-008-005) It resembles its southern kin — "Fort Confidence, as a whole, much resembled other factories further south." (FC-008-006)

Its magazines were bare when Hobson came, their stock "just been sent to the central factories in the south, so that the magazines of Fort Confidence were empty." (FC-008-006) Green pines frame its sharp cape against the water. (FC-008-037)
