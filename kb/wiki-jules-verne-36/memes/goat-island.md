## Overview

Goat Island lies in the Niagara River between Lake Erie and the little city of Niagara Falls, dividing the current where it separates the American falls from the Canadian. (MW-014-047)

It is one of the two principal islands of the cataract, with Navy Island a league above it. (MW-014-007)

## In the Narrative

Exploring the river above the falls, the narrator of The Master of the World visits Navy Island and Goat Island in turn. (MW-014-007) Caught in the current, he sees the first trees of Goat Island rise ahead. (MW-014-057)

Then, with an eye of horror, he watches the shores of Goat Island flash by, followed by the Isles of the Three Sisters, drowned in the spray from the abyss. (MW-014-060)

## Geography & Features

Goat Island is the neutral ground between the falls; on its paths Americans and Englishmen walk up the left bank of the Niagara together. (RC-001-012)

It is there, on that shared ground, that a quarrel breaks out over the notes of a distant air, some hearing "Yankee Doodle" and others "Rule Britannia." (RC-001-014)
