## Overview

The Sierra Tandil is a mountain range of the Argentine pampas in *In Search of the Castaways*, rising a thousand feet above the level of the sea. (SC-021-001) It lies on the search-party's route across Patagonia toward the shores of the Atlantic. (SC-010-043)

## In the Narrative

Glenarvan's expedition plans to cross over the Sierra Tandil, pursuing their search as far as Point Medano on the Atlantic shore. (SC-010-043) Learning that a track leads into the Sierra Tandil, some sixty miles off (SC-020-041), Glenarvan decides to follow Thalcave's advice and go to the village of Tandil. (SC-020-044)

As they advance, the first estancias of the Sierra Tandil come in sight. (SC-020-056) Yet the range yields no news of the castaways: the travellers feel there is no chance of coming across Captain Grant between the Sierra Tandil and Carmen. (SC-021-062) Had any such event occurred, Manuel is sure they would have known of it at Tandil. (SC-021-059)

## Geography & Features

The Sierra Tandil rises about a thousand feet above sea level (SC-021-001), some sixty miles from the pampas track the expedition follows. (SC-020-041) Its slopes carry scattered estancias (SC-020-056), and clumps of trees planted by European hands appear here as rarely as in the neighbouring Tandil and Tapalquem Sierras. (SC-026-012)

## Connections

The Sierra Tandil belongs to *In Search of the Castaways* as a landmark on the trans-Patagonian search toward Point Medano. (SC-010-043) Across the plains it overlooks, Indian traders traffic between Tandil and Carmen, at the mouth of the Rio Negro. (SC-021-062)
