# Rae

## Overview

He is the blacksmith of the expedition, for "Rae was most skilful at blacksmith's work, and with the aid of a little portable forge he was able to make all the pins, tenons, bolts, nails, screws, nuts, &c., required in carpentry" (FC-013-004).

He lodges at the fort with his wife, one of the households for which "Mrs Joliffe, Mrs Mac-Nab, and Mrs Rae, with their husbands, occupied the other dormitories" (FC-014-002).

## Role in the Story

He tends the fort's apparatus, for "Rae set up his condensers for collecting the vapour suspended in the air, which were to be emptied twice a week" (FC-017-008).

When bears besiege the factory the Lieutenant "called together Long, Mac-Nab, and Rae the blacksmith, as the bravest men in his party" (FC-021-032), and "all three volunteered for the perilous service" (FC-021-039).

He braves the loft, where "Rae climbed up to the trap-door of the loft, and peeping through it, made sure that the bears were still on the roof" (FC-021-043).

In the crisis "Mac-Nab and Rae rushed to their officer's assistance" (FC-021-055), and later, when the island must be abandoned, "the blacksmith, Rae, had fortunately found a large number of the iron bolts which had been brought from Fort Reliance, and they were invaluable for firmly fastening together the different portions of the framework of the raft" (FC-042-028).

## Character & Traits

He is a tireless craftsman, whose forge supplies "all the pins, tenons, bolts, nails, screws, nuts" the carpentry requires (FC-013-004).

He is reckoned among the bravest, summoned with Long and Mac-Nab "as the bravest men in his party" (FC-021-032), and he keeps watch even as danger mounts, for "Rae had been watching the bears and the loft" (FC-021-047).

He is one of the trusted few, for the Lieutenant "consulted Sergeant Long, Mac-Nab, Rae, Marbre, and Sabine, in whom he had great confidence" (FC-043-030).

## Relationships

- [[Mac-Nab]] — the carpenter beside whom he is warned of the drifting island, when "Marbre... confided his suspicions to Mac-Nab the carpenter and Rae the blacksmith" (FC-035-005).
- [[Marbre]] — the hunter whose suspicion of the floe first reaches him and Mac-Nab (FC-035-005).
- [[Jaspar Hobson]] — the Lieutenant who counts him among those "in whom he had great confidence" (FC-043-030).
- [[Sabine]] — a fellow trusted agent consulted alongside him over the island's fate (FC-043-030).
- His wife (Mrs Rae) — who shares his dormitory and toils in the fort's kitchen, "assisted by Mrs Rae" (FC-018-009).
