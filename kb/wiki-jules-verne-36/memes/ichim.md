## Overview

Ichim is a Western Siberian town on the great road from Ekaterenburg to Irkutsk, the only route Michael Strogoff can take eastward. (MS-013-007)

It lies about four hundred and twenty miles from Ekaterenburg, and it is here that the two war correspondents intend to halt. (MS-013-006)

## In the Narrative

The tarantass drives straight for Ichim, where the correspondents mean to stop unless events force a change of plan. (MS-013-034) A hundred and twenty miles separate Novo-Saimsk from the town, a stage they hope to cover within a day. (MS-013-035)

By the afternoon of the next day the carriages are within thirty versts of Ichim (MS-013-036), and it is eight o'clock in the evening when they finally reach it. (MS-013-044)

## Geography & Features

Ichim gives its name to the surrounding valley: reports confirm that Feofar-Khan's army is rapidly approaching the valley of the Ichim. (MS-013-031)

The town is a relay on the post-road, one of the staging points whose horses and drivers carry travellers across the breadth of Siberia. (MS-013-006)
