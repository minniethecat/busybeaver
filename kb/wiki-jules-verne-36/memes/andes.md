## Overview

The Andes are the immense mountain chain that walls the western edge of South America. Glenarvan's search along the 37th parallel began with a stride across the narrow strip of Chili and over the Cordilleras of the Andes into the heart of the Pampas. (SC-010-033) Crossing the Andes is something more than an ordinary journey, and every arrangement was made for safety and speed. (SC-011-003)

## In the Narrative

The chain frames the geography of Verne's South American voyages. Dick Sand's party, wrecked on a high coast, should have seen the immense chain of the Andes bordering the American land for twenty miles. (DSCF-012-074) The waters of the Amazon, coming from the enormous range of the Andes, run on to lose themselves in the Atlantic. (EHLA-005-002)

## Geography & Features

The Andes run the length of the continent as a single spine. The Rocky Mountains begin at the Straits of Magellan and follow the west coast of South America under the name of the Andes or Cordilleras. (FEM-024-020) From the valley of Lima, nine leagues from the sea, the first undulations rise into the great chain of the Andes. (PL-002-004)

## Connections

The Andes shelter the coast of [[Peru]], where [[Lima]] lies in the valley of the Rimac. (PL-002-004) They divide the Pacific shore from the Amazon basin that drains toward the [[Atlantic Ocean]]. (EHLA-005-002)
