# Hans Bjelke

## Overview

He is the party's Icelandic guide, for "this grave, phlegmatic, and silent individual was called Hans Bjelke; and he came recommended by M. Fridrikssen" (JCE-011-009).

He speaks little, and once the bargain is struck "the treaty concluded, Hans silently withdrew" (JCE-011-018).

## Role in the Story

He engages himself to lead the expedition, agreeing "to conduct us to the village of Stapi, on the south shore of the Snæfell peninsula, at the very foot of the volcano" (JCE-011-011).

He sets a tireless pace, since "Hans moved steadily on, keeping ahead of us at an even, smooth, and rapid pace" (JCE-012-006).

He is entrusted with the burden of the descent, for the Professor decreed that "Hans will take charge of the tools and a portion of the provisions" (JCE-017-009).

He leads the plunge into the gulf, "the descent commenced in the following order; Hans, my uncle, and myself" (JCE-017-018).

## Character & Traits

He is a deft and economical worker, who finished the packing "almost as it were without moving a limb; and yet he did his work cleverly" (JCE-011-041).

He is a prudent, forethoughtful man, who "as a cautious man, had added to our luggage a leathern bottle full of water" against the coming drought (JCE-014-042).

He is fearless before the abyss, accepting the enterprise "with such calmness, such indifference, such perfect disregard of any possible danger" (JCE-017-003).

He resumes the lead in the dark "with perfect coolness," so that the narrator "followed him without a word" (JCE-016-016).

He keeps the eye of his old trade, and when questioned he "examined attentively with the eye of a huntsman" before replying (JCE-018-016).

He bears every hardship without complaint, meeting thirst and danger "with the resignation of his passive nature" (JCE-021-003).

## Relationships

- [[Professor Lidenbrock]] — the geologist who hires and directs him, and who assigns him charge of the tools for the descent (JCE-017-009).
- [[Axel]] — the Professor's nephew, who follows the silent guide down the cone "without a word" and takes comfort that he would not desert them (JCE-016-016).
