## Overview

*Facing the Flag* is Jules Verne's tale of a terrible weapon and the conscience of the man who made it. It opens at "the establishment of Healthful House," a sanitarium near New-Berne, North Carolina, visited one June day by the mysterious "COUNT D'ARTIGAS" aboard his schooner *Ebba* (FF-001-002, FF-001-003, FF-001-005).

The Count is not what he seems: he is "really Ker Karraje, the pirate who some time ago carried on his depredations in the West Pacific" (FF-013-022).

## Plot Summary

Confined at Healthful House is the French inventor Thomas Roch, whose fulgurator is an explosive "whose power is, so to speak, without limit" (FF-013-023). With him is his keeper, "Simon Hart, the French engineer who occupied Pavilion No. 17," posing under the name Gaydon (FF-013-022).

Both men are "kidnapped and carried on board the schooner *Ebba*," then confined in a cavern "in the interior of the islet of Back Cup, which is wrongly regarded as an active volcano" (FF-013-022, FF-013-025). Ker Karraje's aim is plain: once he "has obtained possession of Roch's fulgurator... [he] will be in a position to carry on his crimes with complete impunity" (FF-013-023).

Simon Hart casts a warning document into the sea in a keg, and an entente among the powers sends "five warships to Bermudan waters" to bring the mountain "down like the walls of a bastion, by bombarding it" (FF-018-006). The pirates prepare to defend the island with Roch's own fulgurator (FF-015-048).

The climax turns on a flag. As the French cruiser *Tonnant* "penetrated the danger zone" and "ran up her flag," the sight redeemed the traitor-inventor: "Moved to his very soul at the sight of the tricolor flag, being at last conscious of the crime of lèse-patrie he was about to commit, Thomas Roch rushed through the passage to the magazine" (FF-018-016, FF-018-028).

There he "brought about the terrible explosion which destroyed the island of Back Cup." And so "Ker Karraje and his pirates have disappeared--and with them Thomas Roch and the secret of his invention" (FF-018-028, FF-018-029).

## Main Characters

- Thomas Roch — the French inventor of the fulgurator, deranged yet redeemed by patriotism
- Simon Hart — his keeper and fellow engineer, alias Gaydon, who raises the alarm
- Ker Karraje — the pirate chief posing as Count d'Artigas, master of Back Cup
- Captain Spade — commander of the schooner *Ebba* who carries out the abductions

## Key Places & Technology

- Roch's fulgurator — the boundless explosive weapon coveted by the pirates
- Back Cup — the hollow islet, a false volcano, that serves as Ker Karraje's lair

## Themes

Verne weighs genius against loyalty, letting a weapon of limitless power fall into criminal hands and asking what, if anything, can stay it. His answer is the flag: patriotism reaches even a broken mind, and Roch destroys both his invention and himself rather than fire upon his own country.
