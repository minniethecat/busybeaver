# Ben Zoof

## Overview

Ben Zoof is the orderly and inseparable companion of Captain Hector Servadac. During Servadac's exile on the Algerian coast, "Hector Servadac's sole companion was his orderly, Ben Zoof," a man "devoted, body and soul, to his superior officer" (OC-003-016).

Their first exchange in the tale is the captain's idle challenge across the saddle — "I say, Ben Zoof, did you ever compose any poetry?" (OC-002-029) — to which the plain soldier owns he has only "seen them made" at the fete of Montmartre (OC-002-030).

## Role in the Story

When the cosmic catastrophe strands the captain's party, Ben Zoof follows without hesitation. Set to guard their Algerian remnant while his master explores, he is "'invested with governor's powers,' and took an affecting leave of his master" (OC-010-036) — though not before a debate "whether or not it was desirable for Ben Zoof to accompany his master" (OC-010-035).

Through the two-year voyage on Gallia he is the community's cheerful mainstay: he skates "prodigies in the art" upon the frozen sea (OC-024-005) and greets the new satellite's return "to Ben Zoof's great satisfaction" (OC-024-013).

To the last the marvel remains beyond him — "the career of the comet was ever a mystery which neither Servadac nor his orderly could eliminate from the regions of doubt" (OC-045-024).

## Character & Traits

His two ruling passions are his master and his birthplace. Born on Montmartre, "Ben Zoof had ever possessed the most unreserved admiration for his birthplace" (OC-003-017), swearing its hill no less than a mountain.

Told it wanted only "some thirteen thousand feet to make it as high as Mont Blanc" (OC-003-020), his eyes glistened, "and from that moment Hector Servadac and Montmartre held equal places in his affection" (OC-003-021).

His loyalty is a soldier's debt of blood: "Servadac had saved Ben Zoof's life in Japan; Ben Zoof had rendered his master a like service in the Soudan" (OC-003-018). Once he had meant to quit the army at twenty-eight, but the appointment to Servadac bound him for life.

## Relationships

- [[Hector Servadac]] — his captain and master, saved his life in Japan and was saved in turn in the Soudan; the bond he would never sever (OC-003-016, OC-003-018).
- Count Timascheff — the Russian count and fellow castaway, alongside whom he serves through the Gallia voyage (OC-024-005).
- [[Off on a Comet]] — the novel whose castaway community he sustains with dogged good humor.
