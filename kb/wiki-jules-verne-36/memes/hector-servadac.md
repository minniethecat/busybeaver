# Hector Servadac

## Overview

Hector Servadac is a French staff officer stationed on the Algerian coast — his own card reads "Captain Hector Servadac, Staff Officer, Mostaganem" (OC-002-009).

Verne fixes his measure at once: "Hector Servadac was thirty years of age, an orphan without lineage and almost without means" (OC-003-009), yet "quite the type of an officer ... slim and graceful, with dark curling hair and mustaches, well-formed hands and feet, and a clear blue eye" (OC-003-011).

## Role in the Story

On the eve of a duel he is careless of danger — "'I am quite content,' answered Servadac, with a smile" (OC-002-013) — when a cosmic shock tears a fragment of Earth into space. Marooned with a handful of survivors, he pieces the truth together: "we seem to have settled it to our entire satisfaction that we must be the population of a young little world called Gallia" (OC-016-051).

The name sticks — "the voyagers in the _Dobryna_ fell into the habit of using Gallia as the name of the new world" (OC-017-001).

He turns castaway into commander, mock-hailed "Well, sir, here you are, Governor General of Algeria!" (OC-007-040), and when he sails off to explore he deputes his servant, who "was 'invested with governor's powers,' and took an affecting leave of his master" (OC-010-036).

At the voyage's end he is restored to Earth but changed — "Colonel, no longer Captain, Servadac, his hair slightly streaked with grey" (OC-045-023) — though "the career of the comet was ever a mystery which neither Servadac nor his orderly could eliminate from the regions of doubt" (OC-045-024).

## Character & Traits

He is a soldier of reckless courage: at a breach "Servadac mounted the side-work, laid himself down in the gap, and thus filling up the breach by his own body, shouted, 'March on!'" (OC-003-012).

Idle in peace and a poor hand at the verses he perpetually attempts, he is redeemed by loyalty and dash — his rival in love is "the Russian Count Timascheff" (OC-003-015), yet rivalry never curdles into malice.

His deepest bond is with his orderly, "his sole companion ... his orderly, Ben Zoof" (OC-003-016), a debt of blood twice paid: "Servadac had saved Ben Zoof's life in Japan; Ben Zoof had rendered his master a like service in the Soudan" (OC-003-018).

## Relationships

- Ben Zoof — his devoted Montmartre-born orderly and sole companion, each having once saved the other's life (OC-003-016, OC-003-018).
- Count Timascheff — the Russian count, his rival in love turned fellow castaway aboard Gallia (OC-003-015).
- [[Off on a Comet]] — the novel whose cometary voyage he leads from disaster to homecoming.
