# Tsalal Island

## Overview

Tsalal Island is the fictional Antarctic island at the heart of *An Antarctic Mystery*, the goal of the schooner *Halbrane*'s voyage. Taken from Edgar Allan Poe's *Narrative of Arthur Gordon Pym*, it is the island reached by Pym after "the frightful shipwreck of the *Grampus*," brought "across the Polar Sea to Tsalal Island." (AM-004-059)

The narrator, weighing Pym's account, exclaims: "If Arthur Pym is to be believed, this was Tsalal Island!" (AM-005-059)

## In the Narrative

The whole expedition is a search bent on the island. Captain Len Guy would "take the *Halbrane* to Tsalal Island, as marked upon Patterson's note-book" (AM-007-002), sure "that my brother and five of his sailors were living less than four months ago on some part of Tsalal Island." (AM-008-053)

The crew signs on for the hazard, their articles binding them for a campaign "not to extend beyond Tsalal Island." (AM-009-045) It forms the last link of a chain "whose first link... is Patterson's ice-block, and whose last will be Tsalal Island." (AM-011-019)

## Geography & Features

The island lies far within the Antarctic, reached by following earlier voyagers: the route "Weddell and then William Guy had followed," so that the schooner "must inevitably come to Tsalal Island." (AM-011-010) The unbroken polar daylight is expected to hold "before we should have reached Tsalal Island." (AM-012-003)

Pym's narrative peopled it thickly — "there were thousands of natives on Tsalal Island" (AM-009-035) — a claim the searchers mean to put to the test.
