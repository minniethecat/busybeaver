## Overview

Gondokoro is a station on the Nile in East Africa, lying between the fourth and fifth parallels of north latitude. (FWB-004-016) In *Five Weeks in a Balloon* it marks the northern limit reached by explorers ascending the river, and the point Dr. Ferguson hopes to attain to meet his countrymen. (FWB-018-097)

## In the Narrative

Gondokoro figures first in the history of Nile exploration: the expedition of 1840 under Mehemet Ali stopped at Gondokoro (FWB-004-016), Dr. Penney penetrated one degree beyond it before dying of exhaustion at Khartoum (FWB-004-018), and in 1859 M. Guillaume Lejean could not get past Gondokoro. (FWB-004-019)

As the balloon crosses the region, the doctor measures off the distance on his map: they are not ninety miles from Gondokoro, and less than five miles from the point reached by the explorers from the north. (FWB-018-057) He hopes a northward wind will let them reach it (FWB-018-097), though he fears they will have trouble getting there. (FWB-019-005)

## Geography & Features

Gondokoro sits between the fourth and fifth parallels of north latitude on the course of the Nile. (FWB-004-016) It stands about ninety miles from the balloon's position and lies just beyond the farthest point reached by explorers coming from the north. (FWB-018-057) Beyond it, only Dr. Penney had pushed a single degree of latitude before turning back. (FWB-004-018)

## Connections

Gondokoro belongs to *Five Weeks in a Balloon* as the northern terminus of Nile exploration that the aerial voyage seeks to bridge. (FWB-018-057) The doctor plots his course toward it — "Stop at Gondokoro" — as the meeting-point with countrymen ascending from the north. (FWB-005-028)
