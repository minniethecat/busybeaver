## Overview

The Canadian General Transportation Company is a Canadian shipping firm, owner of the steamer *Cynthia* whose wreck sets the whole mystery of the novel in motion. The vessel, "commanded by Captain Barton, and the property of the Canadian General Transportation Company, was lost, with her cargo and all on board," near the Faroe Islands. (WC-006-042)

The identification of the ship as a Canadian one, not English, is the discovery that reorients the search for the foundling Erik's family. (WC-006-053)

## Role in the Story

The Company survives in the narrative only as a defunct concern whose records might unlock Erik's origins. Because the loss of the *Cynthia* was never explained and its causes never proved, the insurance company refused the claim, and a lawsuit followed which the proprietors lost. (WC-006-044)

Years later Erik's guardians try to trace the firm's papers, hoping the old records of the company might name the passengers of the *Cynthia*. (WC-008-003) But the consignee of the Canadian General Transportation Company knew nothing of the passengers and could speak only of the freight. (WC-008-005)

Every thread frays: the ex-director had died, and the surviving officers, sailors, and employés of the company, questioned one by one, could give no useful information about the *Cynthia*'s last voyage. (WC-008-024)

## Members

The Company acts through its officers and agents, most already scattered or dead by the time of the search. Its ex-director was Mr. Joshua Churchill, the man who might have known its old records; he had died several years before in a hospital, without known heirs. (WC-008-006)

Captain Barton commanded its lost steamer, the *Cynthia*. The firm's story is bound entirely to that wreck at the centre of [[The Waif of the Cynthia]].
