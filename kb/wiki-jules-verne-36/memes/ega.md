## Overview

Ega is the Amazonian town where the jangada breaks its voyage in Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon. The town of Ega is of some importance; it was worthy of a halt to visit it. (EHLA-015-052)

It is a chief town with fifteen hundred inhabitants, and in it reside all the authorities which compose the administration of a considerable city — the military commandant, the chief of police, the judges, and the schoolmaster. (EHLA-016-004)

## In the Narrative

The family breaks its river journey to see it. It was arranged that the jangada should remain on this spot till the 27th of July, and that on the morrow the large pirogue should take the whole family to Ega. (EHLA-015-052) A distance of four leagues separated the mooring-place from the town, and the southern shore of the lake had to be followed to get there. (EHLA-016-031)

The visit sharpens the plot's suspicions. Torres had evinced no desire to visit Ega, to the satisfaction of Manoel, who had taken a great dislike to the man. (EHLA-016-002)

While the family was ashore, Fragoso stayed aboard and saw Torres reading an old faded paper, muttering words he could not understand. (EHLA-027-020) When the two girls entered Ega they were at first much astonished. (EHLA-016-037)

## Geography & Features

A chief town of some fifteen hundred souls on the southern shore of a lake off the Amazon, five hundred leagues from Pará, Ega marks the western reach Peru once sought — attempting to extend as far as the Lake of Ega. (EHLA-012-008)
