# Pacific Railroad

## Overview

The Pacific Railroad is the American transcontinental line that speeds Phileas Fogg across the United States in *Around the World in Eighty Days*. Verne explains that it "is, however, really divided into two distinct lines: the Central Pacific, between San Francisco and Ogden, and the Union Pacific, between Ogden and Omaha." (AWED-026-002)

Its completion collapses a journey once measured in months into a matter of days, making Fogg's wager possible.

## In the Narrative

Fogg boards the railway as the overland leg of his circuit of the globe. Beyond Omaha "the railway crosses a territory which is still infested by Indians and wild beasts," and a tract colonised by the Mormons after their expulsion from Illinois in 1845. (AWED-026-003) Feeder branches join the trunk line "in Iowa, Kansas, Colorado, and Oregon." (AWED-026-006)

Verne dwells on the line's engineering as it climbs the mountains: "The railway track wound in and out among the passes, now approaching the mountain-sides, now suspended over precipices, avoiding abrupt angles by bold curves." (AWED-026-015) It "did not attempt to violate nature by taking the shortest cut from one point to another." (AWED-026-016)

## Geography & Features

The Central Pacific takes "Sacramento for its starting-point" and extends eastward "to meet the road from Omaha." (AWED-026-014) The two halves were joined into a single working line only recently: "the Union Pacific Railroad was inaugurated on the 23rd of October, 1867, by the chief engineer, General Dodge." (AWED-029-005)

Verne calls it "a mighty instrument of progress and civilisation, thrown across the desert, and destined to link together cities and towns which do not yet exist." (AWED-029-005)

The eastern approach runs "from the south-west to the north-west by Great Island, Columbus, an important Nebraska town, Schuyler, and Fremont, to Omaha." (AWED-031-024) It is at Omaha that "the Pacific Railroad proper finds its terminus," where Fogg's party regains the rails after crossing the snow by sledge. (AWED-031-034)

## Connections
