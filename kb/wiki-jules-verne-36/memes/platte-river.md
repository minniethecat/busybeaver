# Platte River

## Overview

The Platte River is the broad Nebraska river whose valley carries the Pacific Railroad — and Phileas Fogg — westward across the American plains in *Around the World in Eighty Days*. The line clings to it from the start of the prairie run: "on leaving Omaha, it passes along the left bank of the Platte River as far as the junction of its northern branch." (AWED-026-006)

## In the Narrative

The river paces the whole American crossing. The train "entered Nebraska at eleven, passed near Sedgwick, and touched at Julesburg, on the southern branch of the Platte River," (AWED-029-004) the rails following the water's every turn — "the road followed the capricious windings of the southern branch of the Platte River, on its left bank." (AWED-029-006)

At the climax of the overland dash it is a landmark of the sledge journey: "about noon Mudge perceived by certain landmarks that he was crossing the Platte River." (AWED-031-030)

## Geography & Features

The river drains the eastern slope of the continental divide. On the high ground "the first streams, branches of the North Platte River, already appeared," (AWED-028-030) marking the descent toward the Atlantic basin.

Its course is the fixed thread the railroad holds to across the plains: the line "followed throughout the right bank of the Platte River." (AWED-031-024)
