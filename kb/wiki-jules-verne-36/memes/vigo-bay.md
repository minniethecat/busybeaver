# Vigo Bay

## Overview

Vigo Bay is the inlet on Spain's northwest coast where the Nautilus pauses over a graveyard of sunken galleons in *Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea*. It is a real anchorage: "the Bay of Vigo, located on Spain's northwest coast and not blockaded." (TTLU-032-055) A whole chapter of Aronnax's narrative bears its name. (TTLU-032-001)

## In the Narrative

Nemo recounts the disaster that made the bay a treasure field: after the Spanish galleons entered it, "English vessels arrived in the Bay of Vigo on October 22, 1702." (TTLU-032-061) The ensuing battle sent a fleet of bullion to the bottom.

Nemo reveals the Nautilus's true errand there: "'we're actually in that Bay of Vigo, and all that's left is for you to probe the mysteries of the place.'" (TTLU-032-064) He describes his quiet harvest of the drowned gold: "'I have only to pick up what other men have lost, and not only in this Bay of Vigo but at a thousand other sites where ships have gone down.'" (TTLU-032-070)

## Geography & Features

The bay is a sheltered northern port the Spanish admiral was directed toward when Cadiz proved unsafe: "They wanted to be taken to a Spanish port, if not to Cadiz, then to the Bay of Vigo." (TTLU-032-055) Its floor holds so much wealth that Nemo counts himself merely "forestalling the efforts of a rival organization." (TTLU-032-071)
