# The Wreck of the Albatross

## Overview

The wreck of the Albatross is the violent climax of *[[Robur the Conqueror]]*—the captive Uncle Prudent smuggles a dynamite cartridge aboard [[Robur]]'s aeronef and, resolved to destroy her even at the cost of his own life, plants an infernal machine that will blow the ship out of the sky above the [[Chatham Islands]].

Phil Evans examined the infernal machine, a metallic canister holding about two pounds of dynamite, enough to shatter the aeronef to atoms; and if the blast did not destroy her at once, it would do so in her fall (RC-019-040).

## What Happens

Prudent had stolen the charge in secret. The night before, while Robur's people were busy keeping the ship safe, he had slipped into the magazine and got hold of a dynamite cartridge (RC-019-034).

He timed the blast with a slow match. Taking gunpowder as well, he made a fuse that would burn slowly into the fulminate, meaning to light it near midnight so the explosion would come at three or four in the morning (RC-019-043).

A little before midnight he lit it. In a sliding locker beneath the berth he laid the dynamite and the slow-match, where it would burn without betraying itself, lit the end, and pushed the box back out of sight (RC-019-055).

Unseen, the fuse crept on. More than a third of the match was now consumed, and the spark was creeping steadily along toward the dynamite while the crew worked twenty yards away in the bow (RC-020-022).

## Significance

The explosion tore the ship apart. A fearful blast shook the Albatross; the cabins flew into splinters, the lamps went out, the electric current failed, and most of the suspensory screws were twisted or broken (RC-020-048).

Robbed of lift, she fell. When the last suspensory screw stopped spinning, the Albatross dropped into the abyss (RC-020-050)—a fall of ten thousand feet for the eight men clinging to the wreck, made faster still by the fore propeller working vertically in the air (RC-020-051).

Robur's coolness saved the survivors. He climbed to the broken deck-house and reversed the rotation so the propeller became a suspender, checking the descent (RC-020-052); yet eighty seconds after the explosion all that remained of the Albatross plunged into the waves (RC-020-053).

## Participants & Setting

- The captives Uncle Prudent and Phil Evans, condemned to indefinite imprisonment aboard the aeronef, resolve to destroy her rather than remain Robur's prisoners, then slip down the anchor-cable to Pitt Island before the charge can go off.
- Robur and his loyal crew, unaware of the hidden cartridge, labor through the night to repair the propellers damaged in the southern storm, intent only on recapturing the fugitives.
- The [[Albatross]] herself, Robur's masterpiece of heavier-than-air flight, is annihilated above the [[Chatham Islands]] and sinks into the Pacific, though her creator survives to build her successor.
