# James Starr

## Overview

James Starr is a retired mining engineer who "had for twenty years, been the manager" of the Aberfoyle coal-mines of Stirling, Scotland (UC-001-006).

The story opens on the letter that draws him back: "IF Mr. James Starr will come to-morrow to the Aberfoyle coal-mines, Dochart pit, Yarrow shaft, a communication of an interesting nature will be made to him" (UC-001-002). It reaches him "by the first post, on the 3rd December" (UC-001-005).

## Role in the Story

He had himself presided over the mine's closing. "The engineer, James Starr, had collected the hundreds of workmen which composed the active and courageous population of the mine" (UC-001-011), and, holding up the last lump of coal, told them it "is like the last drop of blood which has flowed through the veins of the mine!" (UC-001-015).

When Simon Ford's summons is contradicted by an anonymous warning, "Starr also thought it wiser to give more credence to the first letter than to the second" (UC-002-004), and set out for Aberfoyle.

Guided into the depths, he grasps that a fresh seam survives — and his engineer's instinct fixes on the air: "What troubled James Starr was, not lest too much gas mingled with the air, but lest there should be little or none" (UC-006-042). A hidden enemy then seals the passages, and "James Starr and his companions were prisoners in New Aberfoyle" (UC-008-064).

Once the new mine is opened, he makes it his home: "a second habitation was erected in the neighborhood of Simon Ford's cottage: this was for James Starr" (UC-010-007). He governs the settlement so watchfully that "any stranger entering the mine was brought before James Starr, that he might give an account of himself" (UC-018-002).

## Character & Traits

He is a hale, steady man whose learning ranges beyond coal — long a manager, he had "retired, it was with the absolute conviction" that no seam remained (UC-002-017).

Reunited with his old foreman, he is "glad to find the old man just as he used to be" (UC-004-018), the mark of a loyal, unchanging temper. Curiosity keeps him restless: faced with the mine's unexplained menace, "James Starr could not rest till he had penetrated this mystery" (UC-012-043).

## Relationships

- [[The Underground City]] — the novel whose whole action turns on the letter summoning him back to Aberfoyle (UC-001-002).
- Simon Ford — the old overman whose letter recalls him; "James Starr and Simon Ford knew and esteemed each other well" (UC-001-019).
- Nell — the foundling of the mine, to whom he plays teacher of the world above: "every island here has its legend, perhaps its song" (UC-015-009).
