# Peak of Teneriffe

## Overview

The Peak of Teneriffe is the towering volcanic cone of the Canary Islands, the first great landmark the *Duncan* raises on her southward voyage in *In Search of the Castaways*. The geographer Paganel counts it among the sights he has always longed for: "In the Canary Islands, you see, there are three groups to study, besides the Peak of Teneriffe, which I always wished to visit." (SC-008-007)

## In the Narrative

The peak disappoints the scholar when it finally appears, small and distant on the horizon: "though we are forty miles off, yet I tell you the Peak of Teneriffe is quite visible yonder above the horizon." (SC-008-018) Paganel's dream of climbing it is quietly abandoned as the yacht presses on — "you would find it dull work waiting for a vessel in the Peak of Teneriffe." (SC-008-028)

## Geography & Features

The *Duncan* does not call at the island but leaves it to port and holds her course: the captain "gave immediate orders for the yacht to continue her route, steering to the west of the Canary group, and leaving Teneriffe on her larboard." (SC-008-033) Later, far to the south, another height recalls it: a form "seemed almost a reproduction of Teneriffe." (SC-029-005)
