## Overview

Patagonia is the southern region of South America crossed by Glenarvan's party in the first phase of the search for Captain Grant. Its very name becomes a running fixation: Glenarvan insists that the name Patagonia should seem apparent even to the least clear-sighted individual. (SC-010-009)

## In the Narrative

The 37th parallel leads the searchers across it. Paganel makes no question that the Geographical Society would have sent him to Patagonia as soon as to India. (SC-008-077) The horse Thaouka — a word in Patagonia which means bird — carries them over the pampas. (SC-016-002) He would insist that Patagonia without Patagonians was not Patagonia at all. (SC-009-010)

The crossing closes as the Duncan rounds the continent. As soon as the Duncan had re-provisioned, she sailed along the coast of Patagonia, doubled Cape Horn, and made a swift run up the Atlantic Ocean. (SC-066-003) Yet the reading proves wrong here — a plausible theory in Patagonia, but not in the part of Australia intersected by the 37th parallel. (SC-032-011)

## Geography & Features

A wind-swept land of pampas and Indian tribes at the southern tip of South America, Patagonia is the first of the three great search-grounds — traversed on the 37th parallel from the Atlantic coast toward the Andes before the quest turns to other continents.
