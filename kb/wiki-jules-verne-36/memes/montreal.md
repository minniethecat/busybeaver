## Overview

Montreal is the great Canadian city on the St. Lawrence that the airship overflies in *[[Robur the Conqueror]]*. As the [[Albatross]] crosses Canada the aeronauts recognise it from the air, one exclaiming "it seems to me to be Montreal" (RC-008-035), the city spread out plainly beneath them.

It is identified by its famous engineering. "Below the Albatross appeared Montreal, easily recognizable by the Victoria Bridge, a tubular bridge thrown over the St." Lawrence (RC-008-039), a landmark that fixes the city unmistakably as the flying machine passes over.

## In the Narrative

From the air Montreal is a waypoint on the Albatross's northern course. "After Montreal they passed Ottawa, whose falls, seen from above, looked like a vast cauldron in ebullition, throwing off masses of steam with grand effect" (RC-008-040), the city giving way to the Canadian capital beyond.

Montreal also figures as a base of commerce and communication. In *[[The Waif of the Cynthia]]* the travellers arrange for supplies by cable, opening "communications with two large firms, one in Montreal and one in San Francisco" (WC-015-031), and later, "having crossed the Atlantic and reached Montreal, where they took in coal" (WC-015-035), they refit before pressing on.

## Geography & Features

Historically Montreal is the cradle of the northern fur trade. "Towards 1784 some merchants of Montreal combined to explore the fur country, and founded that powerful North-west Company, which soon became the centre of the fur trade" (FC-002-020), making the city the commercial heart of the wilderness.

From it the trappers struck out into the unknown north. In *[[The Fur Country]]* it is recorded that "French hunters, starting from Montreal, their headquarters, pressed on to the north with greater hardihood than any others" (FC-012-013), so that the city stands at the base of every route into the Canadian interior.

## Connections

Montreal is the St. Lawrence city overflown by the [[Albatross]] in *[[Robur the Conqueror]]*, a coaling stop in *[[The Waif of the Cynthia]]* linked by cable to [[San Francisco]], and the historic headquarters of the fur trade whose hunters range the wilderness of *[[The Fur Country]]*.
