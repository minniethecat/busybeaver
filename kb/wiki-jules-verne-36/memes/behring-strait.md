# Behring Strait

## Overview

Behring Strait is the real passage between Asia and America that governs the fate of the drifting island in *The Fur Country*. It is the colony's hoped-for lifeline, the route by which "the vessels from Behring Strait to come right up to the fort, bringing us fresh provisions and taking away our commodities." (FC-011-006)

Having journeyed overland, Mrs Barnett wishes to "go back by Behring Strait." (FC-022-028)

## In the Narrative

As Cape Bathurst breaks free and drifts, the strait becomes the object of dread and hope alike. The current threatens to carry the island "to the south, perhaps through the Behring Strait into the Pacific Ocean" (FC-025-009), and it advances "towards the dreaded Behring Strait." (FC-028-002)

Hobson pins the colony's escape on it, hoping that if "the island were drifted to Behring Strait, the vessel would easily make her way to land, even from the widest part of the passage." (FC-038-016)

## Geography & Features

The strait is the narrowest neck of the Arctic passage, flanked by "Capes East and Prince of Wales, which jut out on either side at the narrowest part of the passage." (FC-031-023) A current "follows the coast of Asia, and passes through Behring Strait, touching Cape East, a promontory of Siberia." (FC-025-025)

The wandering island is measured against it — freed "in Behring Strait two months before," it had "drifted nearly eight hundred miles." (FC-043-017)
