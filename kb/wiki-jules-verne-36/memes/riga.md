## Overview

Riga is the Livonian city on the Baltic, home of Nadia Fedor before her journey into exile. "You are from Riga?" Michael asked. (MS-005-078) In truth the young girl was from Riga; she was Livonian, consequently Russian. (MS-007-010)

## In the Narrative

Riga sets Nadia on her road to Siberia. Her mother died at Riga scarcely a month before, and she was going to Irkutsk to rejoin her exiled father. (MS-009-039) Her father had been a much-esteemed physician at Riga. (MS-009-050) The Tartar invasion was not yet known when she left Riga. (MS-009-046)

## Geography & Features

Riga lies far to the west, a Baltic port under Russian rule. Nadia must have left Riga on the 10th of July to begin her long passage east. (MS-031-063) When she left Riga to come to Irkutsk, she carried only her grief for her mother. (MS-034-023) An American consul, too, was once appointed to Riga. (WC-021-015)
