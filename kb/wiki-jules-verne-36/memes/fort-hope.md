# Fort Hope

## Overview

Fort Hope is the fictional Hudson's Bay Company outpost built at Cape Bathurst in *The Fur Country*, the home base of Lieutenant Hobson's expedition. It stands on the peninsula that, unknown to its builders, is a fragment of ice destined to break away and drift out to sea.

The colonists' whole existence turns on provisioning and defending it. Hobson resolves to "content ourselves for the present with provisioning Fort Hope" (FC-014-008), mindful of "an outrage which had been committed along the coast at no great distance from Fort Hope." (FC-014-011)

## In the Narrative

The fort weathers a long Arctic siege. Wolves and bears menace it — Hobson fears they "might attack Fort Hope before very long" (FC-017-016), and "Some thirty of these snares were set round Fort Hope, and were to be visited at pretty frequent intervals." (FC-017-026)

Despite the hardships, the fort becomes a home. Mrs Paulina Barnett asks "to spend another winter at Fort Hope" (FC-022-028), and the astronomer Thomas Black stays until the solar eclipse, after which "Thomas Black would be free to leave Fort Hope." (FC-022-058)

## Geography & Features

The fort is supplied by the surrounding country. Spruce shoots yielding "spruce-beer" are "gathered in and stored in the cellar of Fort Hope" (FC-014-029), though the colonists know "a long period of bad weather might cut off the communication between Fort Hope and the southern stations." (FC-014-034)

Its isolation becomes absolute when the land drifts out to sea. A returning traveller is forced to conclude "that it is not so easy to get to Cape Bathurst or to Fort Hope as we could wish." (FC-023-013)
