## Overview

The Congo is the great West African river whose mouth anchors the geography of *Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen* — two years before, the dog Dingo had been met on the western coast of Africa, near the mouth of the Congo. (DSCF-004-039)

## In the Narrative

Its mouth marks the point of a lost expedition's departure. The traveller's point of departure was precisely the mouth of the Congo. (DSCF-005-120)

The villain's escape hinges on it: Negoro's intention was to embark on one of the vessels which frequent the mouths of the Congo, and thus avoid the Portuguese stations. (DSCF-033-012)

## Geography & Features

It drains a vast, then little-known territory of three provinces — Benguela, Congo, and Angola — of which little was known except the coast. (DSCF-019-032)

It is the West's great artery: exploration proved the Loualaba to be the upper Zaire, or Congo, and, its course explored as far as the Atlantic, Africa was shown to possess in the West a third great river. (DSCF-019-048)
