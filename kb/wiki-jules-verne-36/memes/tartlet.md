# Tartlet

## Overview

He is a teacher of the genteel arts, "professor of dancing and deportment in the capital of their state" (GM-004-004).

He is a settled bachelor of middle age, "a bachelor, and aged about forty-five at the time we introduce him to our readers" (GM-004-005).

## Role in the Story

He is picked to escort the young traveller, being "this Professor Tartlet, whom William W. Kolderup had chosen as his nephew's companion during the projected voyage" (GM-004-026).

So the engagement is sealed, and "thus was Professor Tartlet selected as the travelling-companion of Godfrey Morgan" (GM-004-052).

His nerves magnify every peril of the crossing, for on the sea it mattered little to Godfrey "and still less to Tartlet, whose troubled spirit exaggerated from day to day the dangers" (GM-005-032).

## Character & Traits

His professional composure is unshakeable on dry land, for placed in unstable equilibrium "the professor preserved an absolute per[fect]" balance (GM-004-027).

At sea that poise deserts him, and "Professor Tartlet, generally so firm on his limbs, had lost all his dancing equilibrium" (GM-005-005).

He is undone by seafaring dread, for "not only did Professor Tartlet suffer from sea-sickness, but also that fear had seized him" (GM-007-014).

Cast ashore, he reclaims his manner at once, so that "Professor Tartlet recovered the aplomb which he had lost since his departure; his feet placed themselves naturally, with their toes turned ou[t]" (GM-008-076).

## Relationships

- Godfrey Morgan — the pupil and master whose fortunes he shares, and who saves his life on the beach, kneeling "down by Tartlet; he unloosed the life-belt and rubbed him vigorously" (GM-008-066).
- William W. Kolderup — the nabob who commissions him, summoning him with "'Mr. Tartlet,' said William W. Kolderup, 'I have sent for you to tell you some news which I imagine will rather surprise you'" (GM-004-028).

## Appearances

- [[Godfrey Morgan]] — as the castaway's companion who clings to his craft even in the wreck, opening his eyes to assure "himself that his precious kit and bow" were safe (GM-008-070).
