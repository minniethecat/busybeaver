## Overview

Havre is the great French port on the Channel, gateway of Atlantic passage and home of many a Verne mariner. When the robbery was discovered, detectives hastened off to Liverpool, Glasgow, Havre, Suez, and other ports. (AWED-003-012) The Hamburg boats did not go to Liverpool or London, but to Havre. (AWED-032-002)

## In the Narrative

From Havre come the passengers of the Chancellor. M. Letourneur and his son André, Frenchmen, are of Havre. (SC2-002-006) It is not unusual, said Curtis, for cotton-laden ships to reach Liverpool or Havre with part of their cargo consumed. (SC2-009-014) Long had the master of the Jeune-Hardie commanded the vessels of a rich shipowner of Havre. (WAI-001-019)

## Geography & Features

Havre looks out on the sea-lanes of two hemispheres. Captain Paul Bos of Le Havre swore he met a monster of colossal size in the seas of the East Indies. (TTLU-042-052) A vanished steamer might well have been one of the New York lines out of Liverpool or Le Havre. (TTLU-043-072) An Indian was once questioned there by Montaigne. (EHLA-012-003)

## Connections

Havre is the Channel port whose passengers embark in [[The Survivors of the Chancellor]]. (SC2-002-006) Its shipping crowds the sea-lanes of [[Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea]]. (TTLU-042-052)
