## Overview

Iowa is the prairie state of the American heartland, memorable in Verne as the final home of the castaways of Lincoln Island. Of Captain Nemo's bequest, "the larger portion was employed in the purchase of a vast territory in the State of Iowa" (MI-062-044), where the colonists built their new world.

## In the Narrative

The Pacific Railroad crosses the state from end to end. In [[Around the World in Eighty Days]] the train "passed rapidly across the State of Iowa, by Council Bluffs, Des Moines, and Iowa City" (AWED-031-036), racing eastward toward the Atlantic seaboard.

Iowa is also where the great voyage of [[The Mysterious Island]] finds its rest. The treasure left by [[Captain Nemo]] to the colonists of [[Lincoln Island]] bought them their prairie estate, a passage repeated word for word in the telling that "the larger portion was employed in the purchase of a vast territory in the State of Iowa" (SI-020-045).

## Geography & Features

Iowa is a broad country of prairie seen best from the air. In [[Robur the Conqueror]] the [[Albatross]] "flew over Iowa after having sighted Iowa City about eleven o'clock in the morning" (RC-009-014), tracing the same land the railway crosses.

Westward the country opens without limit. Beyond the river bluffs "the large plains of western Iowa and Nebraska--immense prairies extending all the way to the foot of the Rocky Mountains" (RC-009-015) roll on toward the mountain wall.

## Connections

Iowa is the prairie state that [[Phileas Fogg]] crosses by rail in [[Around the World in Eighty Days]] and that the [[Albatross]] overflies in [[Robur the Conqueror]], its western plains running to the [[Rocky Mountains]] beside [[Nebraska]]. Above all it is the promised land of [[The Mysterious Island]], where the treasure of [[Captain Nemo]] settles the survivors of [[Lincoln Island]] on a vast estate. Iowa is where Verne's wanderers come home.
