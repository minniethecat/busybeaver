## Overview

Klock-Klock is the native village on Tsalal Island in An Antarctic Mystery, inherited from Poe's narrative of Arthur Pym. The landing party resolves to reach it: they were going ashore, and would try to gain the village of Klock-Klock. (AM-016-008)

## In the Narrative

It is the scene of the earlier catastrophe the searchers investigate. Arthur Pym was said to be mistaken in asserting that Captain William Guy and several of his companions perished in the landslip of the hill at Klock-Klock. (AM-008-048) The question haunts the expedition: did William Guy and his companions all perish in the landslip of the hill of Klock-Klock? (AM-017-110)

The searchers find the site transformed. There was no sign of the wooded hills between which the village of Klock-Klock ought to lie, or of the streams the crew had not ventured to drink. (AM-016-030) The valley of Klock-Klock extended no farther, and they had to retrace their steps to regain the coast. (AM-016-052)

## Geography & Features

A native village set in a valley among wooded hills on Tsalal Island, Klock-Klock is remembered chiefly for the deadly landslip of its hill — the buried catastrophe that An Antarctic Mystery returns to the far south to resolve.
