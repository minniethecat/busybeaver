# The Running of the Charleston Blockade

## Overview

The Running of the Charleston Blockade is the daring passage of the swift steamer Dolphin through the Federal cordon into and out of besieged Charleston, the central exploit of *[[the-blockade-runners|The Blockade Runners]]*.

The venture began as a boast. James Playfair declared he would take command of the steamer, defy all the ships of the Federal marine for speed, and run the blockade of one of the southern ports (BR-001-042).

## What Happens

He chose the hardest target of all. Rather than New Orleans, Wilmington, or Savannah, he meant to go straight to Charleston, where no English boat but the Bermuda had penetrated, trusting his shallow draught to carry him where the Federalists could not follow (BR-001-052).

Arriving off the coast, the run turned on a single choice. With the Dolphin's bearings set, James Playfair had but one thing to do—to decide by which channel he would run into Charleston Bay (BR-006-012).

The forts were the gauntlet. Soon Fort Sumter, on the left, protected her from the batteries of the Federalists as she slipped past (BR-006-030).

Speed and nerve carried her through. Threading the buoys past the Charleston lighthouse above Morris Island, Playfair hoisted the English flag and made his way with wonderful rapidity through the narrow channels, advancing freely into the centre of the bay (BR-006-035).

## Significance

The city greeted the runner as a deliverer. On reaching the Charleston quay the Dolphin was saluted by the cheers of a crowd who, when they learned she had forced the Sullivan passage carrying smuggled ammunition, redoubled their joyful cries (BR-007-002).

The outbound run was the more perilous. As the Charleston beacon sank in the south-west and the firing of the batteries grew fainter, a shell from a cruising gun-boat came whizzing through the air, its course traced by a line of fire (BR-009-070).

Yet speed prevailed once more. Soon the steamer sped through the waters of the Atlantic, the American coast vanishing in the darkness while the distant flashes showed the attack raging on between Morris Island and the forts of Charleston Harbour (BR-009-076).

## Participants & Setting

- Run by Captain James Playfair aboard the fast steamer Dolphin, inbound through the Sullivan channel past Fort Sumter to the [[Charleston]] quay, then outbound under battery fire.
- Undertaken to sell smuggled arms and to reach the besieged, cotton-glutted Confederate port during the American Civil War; Miss Halliburtt aboard to free her imprisoned father.
- The blockade forced in both directions by superior speed and shallow draught; chronicled in *[[the-blockade-runners|The Blockade Runners]]*.
