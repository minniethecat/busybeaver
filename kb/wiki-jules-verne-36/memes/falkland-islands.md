# Falkland Islands

## Overview

The Falkland Islands are the real South-Atlantic archipelago that serves as the *Halbrane*'s last civilized port before her Antarctic voyage in *An Antarctic Mystery*. The schooner puts in to refit and revictual: on "the 15th of October, our schooner cast anchor in Port Egmont, on the north of West Falkland." (AM-009-001)

## In the Narrative

The islands are the point from which Jeorling first means to leave the voyage — "I had finally resolved... to embark for America from the Falkland Isles" — before he is drawn on toward the Pole. (AM-007-031)

Captain Len Guy's trade runs through them: "Independently of his trade with the Cape and the Falklands, he did an important business in seal-skins and the oil of marine animals." (AM-007-009)

News of the schooner's polar destination "had produced some sensation in the Falklands, at Port Egmont, and in the ports of La Soledad." (AM-009-043)

## Geography & Features

The archipelago is two principal isles split by a strait: "On the deep waters of the Falkland Strait, which separates the two principal isles, great masses of extraordinary aquatic vegetation floated." (AM-009-021)

"West Falkland is more extensive than its neighbour, La Soledad, and possesses another fort at the southern point of Byron's Sound." (AM-009-025) The only predator is "the dog-fox, a species peculiar to the fauna of the Falklands." (AM-009-026)

The group is prized as a waystation, for "the Falklands ought to be resorted to for the re-victualling of ships." (AM-009-028)
