# Red Creek

## Overview

Red Creek is the fictional watercourse on Lincoln Island in *The Mysterious Island*, named by the settlers for the colour of its waters: "From this color, the name of Red Creek was immediately given to the watercourse." (MI-012-036)

It runs through wooded country, its "limpid waters of the Red Creek flowed under an arch of... gigantic gum-trees." (MI-012-062)

## In the Narrative

The creek is central to the settlers' industry. Coal "lying open to the sky towards the mouth of the Red Creek" feeds their kiln (MI-013-042), and the shrubs on its banks supply elder for coffee, the plant abounding "towards the mouth of Red Creek." (MI-030-007)

Cyrus Harding plans the corral at its head, intending "to establish it at the sources of the Red Creek, where the ruminants would find fresh and abundant pasture." (MI-029-006)

## Geography & Features

Red Creek feeds the island's great lake. Its mouth "at the place where it entered into the lake, was soon reached" (MI-016-029), and the lake "might be considered as a large center basin, which was filled by the water from the Red Creek." (MI-017-004)

Near Granite House the creek runs close by, so that "At a distance of some hundred feet flowed the Red Creek, and consequently plenty of fresh water was within their reach." (MI-015-039) Its overflow "must also escape by some channel or other." (MI-016-024)
