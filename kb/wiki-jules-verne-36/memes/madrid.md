## Overview

Madrid is the Spanish capital, set so inland that in "Off on a Comet" the sea's new coast ran up to the very spot upon which Madrid had stood. (OC-016-022) It sits among the principal cities whose local time the "Standard Time" scheme sought to fix. (TT-019-008)

## In the Narrative

To arrange a secret coal supply, one of the party must go incognito to Madrid and open telegraphic communication with Montreal and San Francisco. (WC-015-023) Before the newspapers announced their arrival, Mr. Bredejord reached Madrid and, through a banking-house, opened those communications. (WC-015-031)

## Geography & Features

The city has long figured in maritime affairs: a complaint lodged in Madrid won an order from King Philip V to hold the Vigo treasure convoy in custody. (TTLU-032-060) In Verne's future-politics it stands as one of the great prefectures, named beside Rome. (YEAR-001-041)

## Connections

Madrid links to the Atlantic ports through the telegraph lines to [[San Francisco]] used in [[The Waif of the Cynthia]]. (WC-015-023) It lies on the European side of the meridian schemes that also touch London and Paris. (TT-019-008)
