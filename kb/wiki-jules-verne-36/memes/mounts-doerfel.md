# Mounts Doerfel

## Overview

Mounts Doerfel are a lofty lunar range near the Moon's south pole, seen by the travellers of the projectile in *Round the Moon*. Named with their neighbour Leibnitz, "they were Mounts Doerfel and Leibnitz, which stand on the southern circumpolar region of the moon." (RM-016-022)

## In the Narrative

The peaks are among the grandest sights of the circumlunar voyage. As the projectile sweeps over the southern hemisphere the observers reckon their scale against terrestrial giants, noting that "Mounts Doerfel and Leibnitz, on the moon, are as high as Jewahir in the same chain." (RM-016-030)

Their measured height sets them at the very summit of the lunar tables the travellers consult, where Mounts Doerfel are entered at 7,603 metres in the southern latitudes. (RM-012-058)

## Geography & Features

The two ranges sprawl across the Moon's far southern latitudes, for "Mounts Doerfel and Leibnitz formed two separate groups stretching nearly to the South Pole; the former group extends from the Pole to the 84th parallel on the eastern part of the orb." (RM-017-007)

Rather than a simple chain, the mountains rise amid a broken lunar landscape: "these Mounts Doerfel and Leibnitz arose in the midst of plains of moderate extent, bounded by an indefinite succession of amphitheatres and circular ramparts." (RM-017-013)
