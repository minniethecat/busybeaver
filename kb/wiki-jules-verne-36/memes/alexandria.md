## Overview

Alexandria is the Egyptian port on the [[Mediterranean]], a landfall on the sea roads toward the East. On the mail schedule it fell between [[Malta]] and [[Aden]], reached on the twenty-fifth after Malta on the twenty-second and before Aden at the end of the month. (WC-012-049)

## In the Narrative

In [[The Waif of the Cynthia]] Alexandria is a hinge of the mail routes. A traveller might take the mail packet either to Alexandria or [[Suez]], and rejoin his ship there, or defer until Singapore or Yokohama. (WC-013-026) In Five Weeks in a Balloon, letters received in Alexandria in 1860 carried word of a lost traveller. (FWB-005-063)

## Geography & Features

Off the coast of Egypt the schooner of Off on a Comet hoped to reach Alexandria or some other port to gather tidings from Europe. (OC-013-008) The sea basin between Rhodes and Alexandria tallies depths of 3,000 meters, sounded by the [[Nautilus]] as it left the Greek Islands behind. (TTLU-030-132)

## Connections

Alexandria is the Egyptian gate between the [[Mediterranean]] and the road to [[Suez]]. (WC-013-026) It stands on the mail line with [[Malta]] and [[Aden]] toward the East. (WC-012-049)
