# Feofar-Khan

## Overview

Feofar-Khan is the Emir of Bukhara and nominal head of the Tartar invasion — "The present chief, Feofar-Khan, followed in the steps of his predecessors" (MS-003-063). Ambitious and cruel, "the fierce and ambitious Feofar now governed this corner of Tartary" (MS-003-065).

He styles his enemy the Czar with a disdainful epithet, "designat[ing] the Emperor of Russia by this strange title" (MS-021-034).

## Role in the Story

In his camp Feofar receives his lieutenant Ivan Ogareff — "Feofar-Khan was expecting his lieutenant" (MS-021-024) — and hears him with an autocrat's brevity: "Then Feofar spoke. 'I have no need to question you,' said he; 'speak, Ivan'" (MS-021-028). His summons has raised the steppe, for "the Kirghiz hordes rose at the voice of Feofar-Khan" (MS-021-029).

At the sack of Tomsk the Emir parades in barbaric splendor — "Feofar mounted his favorite horse, which carried on its head an aigrette of diamonds" (MS-023-004) — before a court that "admired the magnificence of Feofar-Khan, his wives, his officers, his guards, and all the Eastern pomp" (MS-023-010).

## Character & Traits

Verne draws him as menace incarnate: "Feofar-Khan was a man of forty, tall, rather pale, of a fierce countenance, and evil eyes" (MS-021-025).

His temper flares to threat before the captive courier — "exclaimed Feofar-Kahn in a voice trembling with fury, 'you have come to see what is going on in the Tartar camp'" (MS-023-046) — and to open cruelty as he condemns him: "'Look while you may,' exclaimed Feofar-Kahn, stretching his arm towards Michael in a threatening manner" (MS-024-002).

## Relationships

- [[Ivan Ogareff]] — the Russian renegade who serves as his lieutenant and prompts the sentence; the Emir's headsman "carried out the sentences of Feofar-Khan against offenders" (MS-024-010).
- [[Michel Strogoff]] — the Czar's courier he taunts as a spy, "You came to see our goings out and comings in, Russian spy" (MS-024-040), decreeing "not death, but blindness" (MS-024-041).

## Appearances

- [[Michael Strogoff]] — as the fierce Emir of Bukhara who leads the Tartar invasion and sentences the hero to be blinded.
