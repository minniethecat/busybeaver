# Zanzibar

## Overview

Zanzibar is the East African island from which Dr. Ferguson launches his crossing of the continent in *Five Weeks in a Balloon*. It is a deliberate choice: "the point of departure for this surprising journey is to be the island of Zanzibar, upon the eastern coast." (FWB-002-006) He means to reach it, "where I will inflate my balloon, and from that point we shall launch ourselves." (FWB-003-075)

## In the Narrative

The island is no random choice; "his point of departure had been carefully studied, and it was not without good cause that he had resolved to ascend at the island of Zanzibar." (FWB-004-002) It anchors the expedition's geography, sought on the map "along the coast for the island of Zanzibar, in latitude six degrees south." (FWB-005-033)

Arrival is a set-piece of the voyage: "at length the vessel hove in sight of the town of Zanzibar, upon the island of the same name, and, on the 15th of April, at 11 o'clock in the morning, she anchored in the port." (FWB-011-003) From the rising balloon the whole island "could be seen in its entire extent, marked out by its deeper color upon a vast planisphere." (FWB-012-003)

## Geography & Features

The island is a rich possession of the Sultan of Muscat: it "belongs to the Imaum of Muscat, an ally of France and England, and is, undoubtedly, his finest settlement." (FWB-011-004) Its wealth rests on a grim trade — "it has a large trade in gums, ivory, and, above all, in 'ebony,' for Zanzibar is the great slave-market." (FWB-011-006)

It also fixes the scale of the crossing ahead, for "the distance from Zanzibar to the coast of Senegal is only thirty-five hundred--say four thousand miles." (FWB-008-022)
