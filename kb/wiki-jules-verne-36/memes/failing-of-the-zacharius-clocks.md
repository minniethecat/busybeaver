# The Failing of the Zacharius Clocks

## Overview

The Failing of the Zacharius Clocks is the inciting mystery of *[[Master Zacharius]]*, in which every watch the aged Geneva clockmaker has made stops without apparent cause, the citizens bringing them back one by one, while Zacharius—consumed by the pride of science—refuses to believe his art can fail (MZ-002-003) (MZ-002-008).

## What Happens

Zacharius, the greatest clockmaker in Geneva, had for several days been out of sorts, oppressed by some secret cause of trouble he would not name (MZ-001-005) (MZ-001-014). His apprentice Aubert confided that something absolutely incomprehensible had been going on (MZ-001-029).

On a winter night the old man rose upright in the middle of the room and cried in a hollow voice that it was death (MZ-001-046) (MZ-001-047).

It was certain that the watches had suddenly stopped, and without any apparent reason (MZ-002-003). Yet on the morning after this night of anguish Zacharius seemed to resume work with some confidence, declaring himself better, his strange head-pains chased away by the sun (MZ-002-004) (MZ-002-005).

## Significance

When Aubert ventured that the pride of science had possessed him, Zacharius flared at the word (MZ-002-008) (MZ-002-009). The old man—who had lifted watchmaking from its infancy by inventing the escapement—held that he had put something of his own into each mechanism, and asked whether Aubert had never taken him for a madman (MZ-002-025) (MZ-002-020).

Superb in his vanity, Master Zacharius believed he had discovered the very secrets of existence (MZ-002-022). But his daughter's terror was soon justified: the old servant Scholastique brought back yet another of the accursed watches that had stopped (MZ-002-040). Aubert carefully wound it up, but it would not go (MZ-002-043).

## Participants & Setting

- Master Zacharius, the aged Geneva clockmaker, whose watches have suddenly stopped without any apparent reason and who, in the pride of science, refuses to believe his art can fail (MZ-002-003) (MZ-002-008).

- Aubert Thün, the loyal apprentice, who first perceives that something incomprehensible is happening and warns his master against the pride of science (MZ-001-029) (MZ-002-008).

- Gerande, Zacharius's daughter, who proposes to pray to God to give life to her father's stopping watches (MZ-001-036).

- Scholastique, the old servant, who brings back the citizens' accursed watches one after another as they stop (MZ-002-040).
