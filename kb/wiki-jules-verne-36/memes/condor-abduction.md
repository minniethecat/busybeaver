# The Condor Abduction

## Overview

The Condor Abduction follows immediately upon the [[andes-earthquake|Andes earthquake]] in *[[in-search-of-the-castaways|In Search of the Castaways]]*, as Lord Glenarvan's party scours the [[andes|Cordillera]] for the missing Robert Grant.

High above the searchers a great bird wheels over the crags: the condor was encircling in his flight a sort of inaccessible plateau about a quarter of a mile up the side of the mountain (SC-014-051).

Then the horror grows plain — it was a human body the condor had in his claws, dangling in the air, and apparently lifeless-- it was Robert Grant (SC-014-055).

## What Happens

As the bird labours upward with its burden, hope flares against reason. Oh, suppose Robert were still alive! That bird (SC-014-053), the watchers cry, powerless to reach the plateau where it perches.

A rescuer acts before the condor can carry the boy beyond reach. He did not finish his sentence, but seizing Wilson's carbine, took aim at the condor (SC-014-057).

The shot tells at once. A white smoke rose from between two masses of basalt, and the condor, shot in the head, gradually turned over and began to fall, supported by his great wings spread out like a parachute (SC-014-059).

The party rushes to the fallen bird. Glenarvan flung himself on the corpse, and dragging it from the condor's grasp, placed it flat on the grass, and knelt down and put his ear to the heart (SC-014-061).

## Significance

The saving shot comes from an unseen hand. Glenarvan rushes forward without waiting to see where the shot so providentially came from (SC-014-060), and the marksman proves to be the Patagonian guide Thalcave, whose timely aim binds him to the travellers he serves.

The episode closes not in death but in recovery. When the party went back to Robert, the boy held out his arms to the Patagonian, who silently laid his hand on his head, and proceeded to examine him with the greatest care, gently feeling each of his aching limbs (SC-015-058).

## Participants & Setting

- The abduction and rescue of young Robert Grant, snatched by a giant condor above the [[Andes]].
- Witnessed by Lord Glenarvan, the geographer Paganel, and the Major, with the fatal shot fired by the Patagonian guide Thalcave.
- Chronicled in *[[in-search-of-the-castaways|In Search of the Castaways]]*.
