## Overview

*The Waif of the Cynthia*, written by Jules Verne with André Laurie, is a mystery of lost identity set on the coast of Norway. It centres on the gifted pupil Erik Hersebom of the fishing village of Noroe, "on the western coast of Norway, a few leagues from Bergen" (WC-001-005).

Erik is the star scholar of the gentle schoolmaster Mr. Malarius, able to answer in three languages and to reason through botany and geometry alike (WC-001-034, WC-001-040).

## Plot Summary

Erik is no true son of the Herseboms: as Malarius reveals, "he was not your son, but... had been cast on your shore by a shipwreck, and... you took him in and adopted him" (WC-002-063). He had been found as an infant "tied to a buoy and left to take its chances on the ocean," the buoy bearing the name *Cynthia* (WC-003-011).

A doctor takes up the puzzle but finds "no shipwreck registered at Lloyd's... corresponds with the date of the infant's arrival," and though "two vessels named 'Cynthia' have been lost," none fits (WC-005-061). The mystery deepens into a suspicion of deliberate crime rather than accident.

The truth is at last unravelled: Erik's mother is Mrs. Durrien, his father the engineer George Durrien, who had discovered a petroleum well with a treacherous partner. The villain Tudor Brown, "whose real name was Jones," had contrived "the suspicious wreck of the 'Cynthia,'" casting the infant into the sea to seize the fortune (WC-022-004).

## Main Characters

- Erik Hersebom — the Norwegian foundling who seeks the truth of his birth
- Mr. Malarius — the kindly schoolmaster who nurtures Erik's gifts
- Mrs. Durrien — Erik's true mother, reunited with him at last
- Tudor Brown — the villain, alias Jones, who cast Erik adrift for gain

## Key Places & Technology

- Noroe — the Norwegian fishing village where the foundling Erik is raised
- The buoy of the *Cynthia* — the marker to which the infant Erik was tied and set adrift

## Themes

Verne and Laurie build a detective-like inquiry into birth and inheritance, tracing a foundling's origins across seas and documents. Beneath the adventure runs a study of nature versus fortune, as a fisherman's adopted son proves worthy of a lineage stolen from him by greed.
