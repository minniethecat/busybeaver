# the Sword

## Overview

The Sword is the small British submersible sent secretly into the pirate lagoon of Back Cup to rescue the French inventor [[Thomas Roch]]. Commanded by Lieutenant Davon, it is "a submersible boat of only twelve tons, and consequently much inferior to the tug, both in respect of dimensions and power." (FF-014-075)

Its mission, mounted by the English authorities, was to carry off Thomas Roch before his engines were completed and hand him over to the Bermudan authorities. (FF-018-003)

## Design & Operation

The Sword was divided into three water-tight compartments. (FF-014-077) To dive, the lid was shut and secured, the water compartments filled, and the craft sank out of sight; conversely, opening or emptying her tanks let her rise or settle again. (FF-014-076, FF-014-111)

A search-lamp threw a vague light through the water ahead of the boat, and a periscope could be raised until it "emerged about a foot" above the surface for observation. (FF-014-084, FF-014-103) Driven by an electric current, she stopped the moment the current was turned off, and once clear of the tunnel would rise and make for St. George. (FF-014-084, FF-014-111)

## Role in the Story

Lieutenant Davon's men seized Roch, bound him "before he could offer any resistance," and bore him aboard the moored submersible, which then sank and threaded the underwater tunnel in search of the orifice out of the lagoon. (FF-014-074, FF-014-076)

But the pirates' tug intercepted her: "How could a frail craft like the Sword defend itself against the attacks of Ker Karraje's powerful machine?" (FF-014-121) Rammed on her starboard quarter by repeated shocks, the Sword "lay still" at the bottom of the lagoon. (FF-014-126, FF-014-128)

The pirates passed lines around her hull and raised her, but every attention to Davon and his crew proved useless—all had perished. (FF-015-039, FF-015-041) The English authorities waited in vain for the expedition to return, unable to tell whether the Sword had struck a rock or been destroyed by [[Ker Karraje]]'s pirates. (FF-018-002)

## Science & Speculation

The Sword belongs to Verne's long line of practical submarines, but it is deliberately modest: a twelve-ton naval boat, not a wonder-vessel, and expressly "much inferior" to the pirates' own submersible tug. (FF-014-075)

Its water-tight compartments, ballast tanks, search-lamp, and periscope reflect the real state of 1890s submarine design. Its fate—crushed by a stronger machine in a narrow tunnel—reads as Verne's sober admission that such craft were still fragile instruments, a world away from the invincible [[Nautilus]]. (FF-014-077, FF-014-121)
