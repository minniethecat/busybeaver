# Island of Sein

## Overview

The Island of Sein is the low, rock-girt island off the westernmost point of Brittany, notorious among sailors, that figures in the shipwreck of the *Alaska* in *The Waif of the Cynthia*. Verne notes its old renown: it is "the Island of Sein, the legendary abode of the nine Druidesses." (WC-013-032)

## In the Narrative

As disaster looms the captain turns to the island's people for help: "he contented himself with firing a cannon to ask aid from the inhabitants of the Island of Sein, and with dispatching his small steam launch." (WC-014-003) The wreck itself hangs on the island's reefs: "If the 'Alaska' had not been shipwrecked on the Island of Sein, this fate would surely have awaited her before she could have reached Malta." (WC-014-043)

## Geography & Features

The island is the visible head of a long and lethal reef: "The rocks extend in a westerly direction for nine miles beyond the Island of Sein." (WC-014-013) To warn shipping, "light-houses have been erected on the Island of Sein and at Bec-du-Raze, so that these rocks can be seen and avoided by vessels coming from the west." (WC-014-014)
