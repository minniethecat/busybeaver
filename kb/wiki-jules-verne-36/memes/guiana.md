## Overview

Guiana is the low northern coast of South America, reached where the Nautilus drew near Dutch Guiana by the mouth of the Maroni River. (TTLU-041-062) Twenty miles to the west of that track lay Guiana, French territory where refuge could easily have been taken. (TTLU-041-044)

## In the Narrative

The survivors of the Chancellor found themselves eight hundred miles from land, the nearest shore being the coast of Guiana. (SC2-016-018) They reckoned themselves eight hundred miles from Paramaribo, the nearest portion of Dutch Guiana. (SC2-020-004) Curtis insisted the raft be built with care, for they were still several hundred miles from the coast of Guiana. (SC2-026-004)

## Geography & Features

The Guiana boundary was long disputed between France and Brazil, until on the 31st of July, 1857, the course of the Amazon was declared free. (EHLA-005-040) Its Atlantic shore is gained by the mouth of the Maroni River. (TTLU-041-062)

## Connections

Guiana borders the [[Amazon]] basin whose frontier it long contested. (EHLA-005-040) The coast anchors the desperate raft voyage of [[The Survivors of the Chancellor]]. (SC2-016-018)
