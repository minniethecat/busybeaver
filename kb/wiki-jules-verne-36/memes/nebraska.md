## Overview

Nebraska is the prairie state of the American plains, gateway of the transcontinental railroad in [[Around the World in Eighty Days]]. It was here that "President Lincoln himself fixed the end of the line at Omaha, in Nebraska" (AWED-026-005), making the territory the eastern hinge of the Pacific Railroad.

## In the Narrative

Phileas Fogg's train races across the state through the night. They "entered Nebraska at eleven, passed near Sedgwick, and touched at Julesburg, on the southern branch of the Platte River" (AWED-029-004), climbing on toward the terminus.

The line threads the young towns of the plains, ascending "by Great Island, Columbus, an important Nebraska town, Schuyler, and Fremont, to Omaha" (AWED-031-024) — for "the Pacific Railroad proper finds its terminus at this important Nebraska town" (AWED-031-034), the great junction of the westward route.

## Geography & Features

Nebraska opens westward into vast grassland. In [[Robur the Conqueror]] the bluffs "gave place to the large plains of western Iowa and Nebraska--immense prairies extending all the way to the foot of the Rocky Mountains" (RC-009-015).

Yet the state holds a stranger country too. The [[Albatross]] passed "over the appropriately named Bad Lands of Nebraska--a chaos of ochre-colored hills" (RC-009-026) — the same badlands to which, in [[Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea]], Professor Aronnax had gone on "a scientific undertaking organized to explore the Nebraska badlands in the United States" (TTLU-002-002).

## Connections

Nebraska is the plains state crossed by [[Around the World in Eighty Days]], its railroad climbing beside the [[Platte River]] to the terminus at [[Omaha]]. Its western prairies run to the foot of the [[Rocky Mountains]], and its ochre Bad Lands are overflown by the [[Albatross]] and explored by the naturalist of [[Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea]]. Nebraska is the hinge between the settled East and the open West.
