# Pacific Ocean

## Overview

The Pacific Ocean is the last and greatest of the seas the 37th parallel crosses in *In Search of the Castaways*, and the water on which the search reaches its end. The whole company is drawn toward it: "everyone was eager to reach the goal — that is to say the Pacific Ocean — at that part where the wreck of the BRITANNIA had occurred." (SC-041-034)

## In the Narrative

The ocean measures the scale of the disaster. Glenarvan asks whether "a quick ship make the distance in a month over that part of the Pacific Ocean which lies between America and Australia," (SC-030-008) gauging how far a crippled vessel might have run.

At the crisis it becomes a bargaining ground. The mutineers' captain gives his word "to land you on one of the islands of the Pacific Ocean," (SC-063-049) and the castaways must "decide now on what island of the Pacific Ocean you are to be left." (SC-063-071)

## Geography & Features

The ocean is strewn with the isolated specks on which a shipwrecked man might survive. On the charts "exactly on the 37th parallel" the searchers find "a little isle marked by the name of Maria Theresa, a sunken rock in the middle of the Pacific Ocean." (SC-064-003)

Its islands command the sea-lanes: one archipelago is noted as commanding "the whole Pacific Ocean," (SC-052-004) a crossroads of the routes "generally preferred by steamers on their way to the Pacific Ocean." (SC-009-008)
