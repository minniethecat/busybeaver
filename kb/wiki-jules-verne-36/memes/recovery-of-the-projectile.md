# The Recovery of the Projectile

## Overview

The recovery of the projectile is the closing event of *[[Round the Moon]]*, in which the aluminium car of the [[Gun Club]] falls back into the Pacific and its three travellers—Barbicane, Nicholl, and Michel Ardan—are found alive after a desperate salvage by the corvette Susquehanna.

The corvette Susquehanna, a vessel of the United States Navy, was taking soundings in the Pacific about a hundred leagues from the American coast (RM-020-017). During the night of the 11th to 12th of December she lay exactly in north latitude 27° 7' and longitude 41° 37' west (RM-020-022).

## What Happens

At 1.17 a.m. a distant hissing sound drew Lieutenant Bronsfield's attention high into the air (RM-020-045). An enormous inflamed bolis appeared, and the ignited mass fell with the noise of thunder upon the bowsprit, smashing it off close to the stem before it vanished into the water (RM-020-048). A few feet nearer and the Susquehanna would have gone down with all on board (RM-020-049).

"It is 'they' come back again," cried the midshipman (RM-020-053), and no one doubted that the bolis was the projectile of the Gun Club. Whether living or dead, it was resolved unanimously, the travellers must be brought up from the deep (RM-021-008).

A strong buoy was thrown out to mark the exact point where the projectile had fallen (RM-021-013), and the corvette hastened to San Francisco to telegraph the Gun Club that the projectile of the Columbiad had fallen into the Pacific on December 12th (RM-021-027).

The Susquehanna returned with grappling-irons, diving-dresses, and compressed-air chambers prepared by the engineer Murchison (RM-022-006). At 12.47 p.m. the buoy was sighted (RM-022-017), and the descent of the submarine chamber began at 1.25 p.m. (RM-022-024). For days the search of the sea-bottom was fruitless, and by the 28th all hope was lost (RM-022-042).

## Significance

As the corvette turned homeward, the look-out sighted a buoy on the lee bow (RM-022-046)—and the flag floating above it was an American one (RM-022-052). J.T. Maston grasped the truth at last: the aluminium bullet displaced twenty-eight tons and therefore floated (RM-022-061). The travellers had risen with it to the surface.

The boats rowed out, and a port-light, its glass broken, was opened five feet above the water (RM-022-064). At that moment the clear voice of Michel Ardan rang out in triumph—"Double blank, Barbicane, double blank!" (RM-022-066). Barbicane, Nicholl, and Michel Ardan were calmly playing at dominoes (RM-022-067), unhurt by their fall from the moon.

## Participants & Setting

- The corvette Susquehanna, under Captain Jonathan Blomsberry and Lieutenant Bronsfield, was sounding the Pacific for a submarine cable when the projectile plunged past her bowsprit and nearly sank her.

- J.T. Maston, Secretary of the [[Gun Club]], hurried the salvage on day and night, and it was he who realized that the lightweight projectile floated rather than lay on the ocean bed.

- Barbicane, Nicholl, and Michel Ardan, the three lunar voyagers, were discovered alive within the floating car, having survived both the circumlunar voyage and the fall back to earth.
