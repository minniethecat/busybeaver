## Overview

Egypt is the North African land whose flat coastline, curving southward, Aronnax identifies from the Nautilus. (TTLU-030-010) It marks a stage of the submarine passage: after visiting the unusual waterways of Arabia and Egypt, the Nautilus is expected to go back down to the Indian Ocean. (TTLU-028-012)

## In the Narrative

The country's ancient engineering supplies the tale's lore: it was probably Egypt's King Sesostris who started digging the canal needed to join the Nile with the Red Sea. (TTLU-028-077) Nearby lies Jidda, an important financial center for Egypt, Syria, Turkey, and the East Indies. (TTLU-029-006)

Even in deep history the land looms large — of the ancient sea-peoples it is said their dominion extended even to Egypt. (TTLU-033-063)

## Geography & Features

Its wildlife is distinct: Aronnax notes a species of *Sterna nilotica* unique to Egypt, with black beak and red feet. (TTLU-029-071)

Above all it is the country of the pyramids, whose scale supplies Verne his measure elsewhere: a monument that would overtop even the Great Pyramid of Egypt (FWB-020-019), and termite cones more astonishing than the pyramids of Egypt raised by the hands of men. (DSCF-022-095) There too the sacred *ateuchus* was venerated as a god by the Egyptians of Upper Egypt. (DSCF-031-012)
