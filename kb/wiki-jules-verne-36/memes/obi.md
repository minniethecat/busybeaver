## Overview

The Obi is a real great river of Western Siberia, one of the water barriers on Michael Strogoff's road east. The Tsar's council reckons the invasion by it: the Tartars have not advanced beyond the Irtish and the Obi. (MS-002-022)

## In the Narrative

The river guides Strogoff toward Kolyvan. Following the course of the Obi two versts further, he reached a picturesque little town lying on a small hill. (MS-018-005) His plan is to reach Kolyvan before the Usbeck horsemen, who would ascend the other bank of the Obi to the ferry. (MS-018-006) When battle is joined, the Usbeck horsemen stop on the left of the Obi to await the result. (MS-018-011)

The Obi's tributaries thread the whole region — the Tom, a little affluent of the Obi, passes near Tomsk before losing itself in one of the great northern arteries. (MS-022-006) Later the river is nearly Strogoff's grave: on the Obi, after his horse had been struck by a bullet, he only by a miracle escaped the horsemen pursuing him. (MS-026-079)

## Geography & Features

Draining the West Siberian plain northward to the Arctic, the Obi and its affluents — the Tom among them — cut across the post-road, forcing ferries that Verne turns into scenes of pursuit.
