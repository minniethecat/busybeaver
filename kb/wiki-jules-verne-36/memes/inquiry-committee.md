## Overview

The Inquiry Committee is the official commission of investigation, sitting under the Presidency of John Prestice, charged with extracting from the public the truth behind the alarming scheme of the North Polar Practical Association. When the President of the N.P.P.A. and his associate vanished, the Committee summoned the one man thought to hold the answer: [[J.T. Maston]]. (TT-010-009)

Backed by public alarm, the Committee obtained from the Cabinet of the Government of the United States full permission to do whatever it judged necessary and advisable. (TT-010-011)

## Role in the Story

The Committee's task was to learn where the cannon would be fired and to halt the project before it altered the earth's axis. Its agent was sent to bring Maston from his Ballistic Cottage to the meeting, where the members began at once to question him. (TT-010-009)

Unable to break his silence, the newspapers and the Inquiry Committee alike tried to read the formulae of the celebrated calculator, and from his seized note-book supposed the calculations absolutely correct. (TT-011-005) Yet nothing in the figures revealed the location of the secret point "x." Evidently not, said the Inquiry Committee, there was nothing by which to calculate it. (TT-011-011)

Powerless to deduce the site, the Committee resorted to public warning, advising every inhabitant of Europe, Africa, Asia, America, and Australia to watch all gun foundries and powder factories in their territory. (TT-011-011)

## Members

The Committee acted through its President and its assembled members, a body of officials speaking with one collective voice in its reports. Its President was John Prestice, before whom Maston was ordered to appear and who directed the whole interrogation. (TT-010-009)

The members of the Inquiry Committee urged the prisoner daily to speak and visited him daily in his cell, but could obtain nothing. (TT-012-010) At last President Prestice conceived the idea of enlisting Mrs. Evangelina Scorbitt, and after deliberation the Committee authorized her to visit the prisoner freely and begged her to use her influence with the mathematician. (TT-012-011)

Through this investigation the Committee stood opposed to the [[North Polar Practical Association]] and pressed hardest upon the secretary of the [[Gun Club]]. (TT-010-011)
