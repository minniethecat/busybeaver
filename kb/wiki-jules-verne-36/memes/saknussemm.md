# Arne Saknussemm

## Overview

He is a sixteenth-century Icelandic savant who "was a very well-informed man" and wrote in the Latin "currently adopted by the choice spirits of the sixteenth century" (JCE-003-035).

He was "persecuted for heresy, and in 1573 his books were burned by the hands of the common hangman" (JCE-010-029).

## Role in the Story

He leaves behind a hidden message, prompting the question whether "this Saknussemm concealed under his cryptogram some surprising invention" (JCE-003-022). His runic parchment holds a dangerous secret Axel nearly burns, seizing "not only the paper but Saknussemm's parchment" (JCE-004-030).

His deciphered direction launches the expedition: "Descend, bold traveller, into the crater of the jokul of Sneffels... and you will attain the centre of the earth; which I have done, Arne Saknussemm" (JCE-005-050).

He is the precursor whose trail they follow, for "of the three ways open before us, one had been taken by Saknussemm" (JCE-016-035), and later they walk "the road that Saknussemm has shown us" (JCE-033-015).

## Character & Traits

He guards his discovery with cunning, which "proves the ingenious care with which Saknussemm guarded and defined his discovery" (JCE-006-042).

He is a beacon to later science, such that were his document divulged "a whole army of geologists would be ready to rush into the footsteps of Arne Saknussemm" (JCE-006-010).

His monogram, carved in the deep rock, betrays his passage, for "'A. S.,' shouted my uncle. 'Arne Saknussemm!'" (JCE-039-052), and he is honoured with a headland christened "Cape Saknussemm" after "your own illustrious name" (JCE-040-004).

## Relationships

- [[Professor Lidenbrock]] — the geologist who venerates him, in whose rhapsodical eulogium "Arne Saknussemm was, of course, the hero" (JCE-040-003).
- [[Axel]] — the narrator who nearly destroyed his parchment, seizing "not only the paper but Saknussemm's parchment" (JCE-004-030).

## Appearances

- [[A Journey to the Center of the Earth]] — the descent his cryptogram sets in motion, whose survivor returned long before, for "many years have elapsed since the return of Saknussemm to the surface" (JCE-040-030).
