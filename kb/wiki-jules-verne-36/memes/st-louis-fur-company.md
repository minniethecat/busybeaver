## Overview

The St Louis Fur Company is an American fur-trading concern, described as the one remaining commercial rival of the [[Hudson's Bay Company]] on the northern continent after the older English monopolies had been absorbed. (FC-002-023)

Where the Hudson's Bay Company held a chartered domain of some 3,700,000 square miles, the St Louis firm worked the same trade from the American side, its agents ranging across the disputed hunting grounds of the far north.

## Role in the Story

The company never appears on the page as a body, but its unseen agents shadow Lieutenant Hobson's expedition. When Mrs Barnett supposes the Hudson's Bay Company had no rivals left after swallowing the North-West Company, she is corrected: the American St Louis Fur Company still competes for the same peltries. (FC-012-008)

Through the long Arctic winter the Lieutenant is anxious to learn whether the agents of the St Louis Fur Company have left the country, sending out excursions along the coast to look for their traces. (FC-017-017)

None come. As the expected convoy from Fort Reliance fails to arrive, so too the agents of the St Louis Fur Company "might have come, and they have not done so," their absence deepening the settlement's isolation. (FC-023-011)

## Members

The company is represented only by its anonymous field agents, roving trappers and traders who compete with the servants of the [[Hudson's Bay Company]] for furs. No officer of the firm is named; it is known entirely through the reports and expectations of its English rival.
