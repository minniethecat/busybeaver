## Overview

The Royal Society is the great London learned body of the sciences, distinct from the exploration-funding [[Royal Geographical Society]] with which the narratives sometimes conflate it. It confers standing on the naturalists and travellers who populate the Voyages, and its judgement carries weight across the learned world.

Its authority is invoked from Edinburgh to London: the whaler Scoresby laid his polar observations before the Royal Society of Edinburgh, an affiliate of the same tradition of scientific academies. (ACH-005-045)

## Role in the Story

In *Five Weeks in a Balloon* the Society is the stage on which Dr. Ferguson's African scheme is received. He bears his plan with such calm that he takes the applause of the Royal Society in his stride. (FWB-001-041)

A humorous reply in the *Bulletins de la Societe Geographique* of Geneva pokes fun at the Royal Society of London over the affair, a rivalry of academies played out in print. (FWB-002-010) Ferguson himself feels he cannot recoil before obstacles, having been backed by both the English Government and the Royal Society of London. (FWB-005-020)

## Members

Its laureates and fellows recur across the books. Paulina Barnett travels as "a laureate of the Royal Society," a distinction that marks her as no ordinary woman when she reaches Fort Reliance. (FC-001-027)

A traveller by profession and a laureate of the Society, [[Paulina Barnett]] could not hesitate before the dangers of the far north. (FC-019-047) The explorer [[Dr. Ferguson]], applauded in its hall, is likewise counted among the men the Society sends into the unknown.
