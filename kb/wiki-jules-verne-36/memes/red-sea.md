# Red Sea

## Overview

The Red Sea is the real sea between Africa and Arabia through which the *Nautilus* passes in *Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Seas*. Captain Nemo takes his vessel into it by the southern strait — "at noon, we were plowing the waves of the Red Sea." (TTLU-028-027)

Professor Aronnax notes its dimensions: "This Red Sea is 2,600 kilometers long with an average width of 240." (TTLU-028-029)

## In the Narrative

The sea puzzles Aronnax, who cannot see how the Nautilus will leave it: "So the Red Sea won't be our way back to Europe either." (TTLU-028-009) Nemo's vessel enters through the Gulf of Aden, "a genuine funnel stuck into the neck of Bab el Mandeb and bottling these Indian waters in the Red Sea." (TTLU-028-023)

Nemo delights in it, asking "are you pleased with this Red Sea?" and boasting that his ship "fears neither the Red Sea's dreadful storms nor its currents and reefs." (TTLU-028-046)

## Geography & Features

The Nautilus crosses its full breadth, "the widest part of the Red Sea, measuring 190 miles straight across from Suakin on the west coast to Qunfidha on the east coast." (TTLU-028-042) Its warm waters teem with life Aronnax means to study, including "some trunkfish unique to the Red Sea." (TTLU-025-017)

The sea is walled off from the Mediterranean by land, the two "separated by the insuperable Isthmus of Suez." (TTLU-028-036) Nemo, however, knows a secret passage beneath it.
