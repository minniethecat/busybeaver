# Sergeant Long

## Overview

Sergeant Long is enumerated among the little company bound for the north, "Lieutenant Jaspar Hobson, Sergeant Long, Corporal and Mrs Joliffe" (FC-001-023).

If his commander embodies the good officer, the sergeant is his counterpart in the ranks, for "if the lieutenant was the type of a good officer, Sergeant Long was that of a good soldier" (FC-001-025).

## Role in the Story

He is the lieutenant's habitual counsellor on the march; when the way grew doubtful, Hobson "after consulting with Sergeant Long, gave the order to halt" (FC-007-033).

The same reliance recurs throughout the expedition, so that "after consulting with Sergeant Long, Lieutenant Hobson decided to give his party a day's rest here" (FC-011-003).

## Character & Traits

His obedience is absolute; told he might be given an impossible order, he answers only "I should come back" (FC-005-021).

In quieter hours he shows an unexpected gift, for "Sergeant Long was a first-rate angler, and nothing could surpass the skill and patience with which he whipped the water" (FC-014-019).

When a perilous errand had to be run, "the Sergeant reminded the other two that they were married, and insisted upon being the first" to go (FC-021-039), then "rushed out without a moment's hesitation, dragging the cord behind him" (FC-021-045).

## Relationships

- [[Jaspar Hobson]] — the lieutenant who prizes him above all, telling him, "Ah, Sergeant Long, I know if I gave you an impossible order---" (FC-005-016); when a detachment must be led, "none but Sergeant Long could be appointed to the command," though "the faithful fellow would rather not have been separated from his Lieutenant" (FC-023-033), and to the last "the Sergeant submitted with military obedience" (FC-023-034).
- [[Paulina Barnett]] — a fellow traveller on the northward journey, in which "Jaspar Hobson and Sergeant Long went first; then Mrs Paulina Barnett and Madge" (FC-005-009).

## Appearances

- [[The Fur Country]] — the record of the Company's expedition, in which Captain Craventy first sets him to his duty with the order, "Sergeant Long, go and see what is the matter" (FC-002-054).
