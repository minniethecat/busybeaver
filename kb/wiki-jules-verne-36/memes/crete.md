## Overview

Crete, also called Candia, is the ancient island toward which the [[Nautilus]] once steered. (TTLU-030-072) Among Captain Nemo's crew the narrator thought he recognized a native of either Greece or Crete. (TTLU-018-005)

## In the Narrative

Crete's waters hide one of Nemo's secrets. There the narrator learned where [[Captain Nemo]] had delivered his millions, when the Nautilus navigated the seas where Crete was in rebellion against the Ottoman Empire. (TTLU-032-079) The island then stood in revolt against its tyrannical rulers. (TTLU-030-072)

## Geography & Features

Crete lies at the heart of the eastern seas. Between Rhodes and Crete rises the island the Greeks call Karpathos and the Italians Scarpanto. (TTLU-030-071) The diver Il Pesce roams constantly from one island to another, even to Crete. (TTLU-030-083)

## Connections

Crete is a landmark of the [[Mediterranean]] passage in [[Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea]]. (TTLU-030-072) Its rebellious waters mark where the [[Nautilus]] carried Nemo's treasure. (TTLU-032-079)
