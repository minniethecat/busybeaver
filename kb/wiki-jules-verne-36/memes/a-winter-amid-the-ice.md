## Overview

*A Winter Amid the Ice* is Jules Verne's early polar tale, opening in the Flemish port of Dunkirk. There the old sailor Jean Cornbutte, who "had caused the brig 'Jeune-Hardie' to be constructed at his own expense," awaits the return of his ship and his son (WAI-001-019).

The day is to be doubly joyful, for the curé has promised "the marriage of my son Louis and my niece Marie" (WAI-001-011).

## Plot Summary

But the *Jeune-Hardie* comes in flying "a black flag": André Vasling has "brought back the 'Jeune-Hardie,' but Louis Cornbutte, Marie's betrothed, was not on board" (WAI-001-055, WAI-001-061). Louis had been lost saving another crew in the northern seas.

Jean Cornbutte sails at once in search of his son, steering "towards Cape Brewster" until "the brig confronted immoveable and united ice-masses" (WAI-005-003, WAI-005-004). Marie sails with them, watched over "with paternal care" by the loyal sailor Penellan, while the mate Vasling — secretly coveting Marie — grows "more attentive than ever" (WAI-005-005, WAI-005-006).

Louis is found alive, but the reunited crew must winter amid the ice, where scurvy takes hold. When old Cornbutte lies dying and cries out for the lemons, the treacherous Vasling refuses them, until Louis swears, "if my father dies, you will have murdered him; and I will kill you like a dog!" (WAI-014-018).

Their misery is crowned by a siege of "gigantic bears" mounting "to the assault of the ship" (WAI-014-031, WAI-014-034). In the final battle Louis grapples with Vasling upon the mast, until a bear falls upon them and the traitor perishes: "Dead, Louis; dead, like our enemies!" (WAI-015-046, WAI-016-004).

Old Jean Cornbutte does not survive — "he had left his native land to find his son, and had died in these terrible regions" (WAI-016-006). The lemons at last "restored them to health," and on the 16th of August the brig regains Dunkirk, where "the old curé received Louis Cornbutte and Marie" and wed them (WAI-016-007, WAI-016-022).

## Main Characters

- Jean Cornbutte — the old Dunkirk shipowner who dies seeking his son in the ice
- Louis Cornbutte — his son, lost then found, betrothed to Marie
- Marie — Jean's niece and Louis's betrothed, who joins the rescue voyage
- Penellan — the faithful sailor who guards Marie and stands against the mutineers
- André Vasling — the treacherous mate who covets Marie and withholds the lemons

## Key Places & Technology

- The *Jeune-Hardie* — Jean Cornbutte's brig, built at his own expense at Dunkirk
- Cape Brewster — the Greenland landmark toward which the search voyage is steered

## Themes

Verne binds a rescue quest to a study of endurance, as loyalty, love, and scurvy are all tested by the long Arctic winter. Against Vasling's treachery he sets the constancy of Cornbutte and Penellan, letting devotion survive both the ice and the wickedness of men.
