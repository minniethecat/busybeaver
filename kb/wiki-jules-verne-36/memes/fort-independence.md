# Fort Independence

## Overview

Fort Independence is the frontier post on the Argentine pampas that becomes a waypoint in the overland search of *In Search of the Castaways*. The Patagonian guide Thalcave steers the party toward it in hope of news: "we had better continue our route to the east as far as Fort Independence, and if we don't get news of Captain Grant there we shall hear, at any rate, what has become of the Indians of the Argentine plains." (SC-020-039)

## In the Narrative

The fort is set as a fixed goal across the cattle country. Thalcave "resolved not to stop at any of them, but to go straight on to Fort Independence," (SC-020-056) passing by the great ESTANCIAS without pause.

Its garrison yields an unexpected friend. "The Governor of Fort Independence was a French sergeant, an old comrade of Parachapee," (SC-021-011) a countryman whose welcome eases the travellers' way. When their business is done, "there was nothing further to do at Fort Independence but to shake hands with the Commandant, and thank him and take leave." (SC-021-060)

## Geography & Features

The fort marks a measured stage on the march from ocean to interior. Paganel asks plainly, "Is Fort Independence far away?" (SC-020-040) — and the answer is a distance the narrative later fixes precisely: "a distance of 150 miles separates Fort Independence from the shores of the Atlantic." (SC-022-001)

Standing on the eastern pampas, it commands the plains the Indian bands roam, and so serves the search as both shelter and clearing-house for rumour of the castaways.
