## Overview

Edinburgh is the Scottish capital and a recurring hearth of Verne's northern tales. The engineer James Starr belonged to an old Edinburgh family and was one of its most distinguished members. (UC-001-006) When at last the true bank-robber, James Strand, was taken, it was at Edinburgh on the 17th of December, clearing Phileas Fogg's name. (AWED-036-002)

## In the Narrative

The city and its environs are the country of Verne's Scottish sportsmen. Kennedy lived in the town of Leith, near Edinburgh, in truth a mere suburb of Auld Reekie. (FWB-003-004) The great balloonist reckoned a certain feat no harder than swimming across the Firth of Forth at Edinburgh. (FWB-033-005)

## Geography & Features

Edinburgh is a seat of learning and a hub of the northern railways. The whaler Scoresby laid his curious polar observations before the Royal Society of Edinburgh, of which Dr. Clawbonny was an honorary member. (ACH-005-045) By that same railway a dog of Danish breed was sent from Edinburgh to Liverpool, addressed to Richard Shandon. (ACH-003-054)

## Connections

Edinburgh is joined by rail to [[Liverpool]], down which line the mysterious dog travelled to the Forward. (ACH-003-054) Its old families supplied the engineers of [[The Underground City]]. (UC-001-006)
