# Strait of Magellan

## Overview

The Strait of Magellan is the winding channel dividing continental South America from Tierra del Fuego, the route by which the yacht *Duncan* crosses from Atlantic to Pacific in *In Search of the Castaways*. Glenarvan announces the plan plainly: "We shall pass through the Straits of Magellan, I must tell you." (SC-008-081)

## In the Narrative

The passage is made without delay: "On the 25th of September, the yacht arrived off the Straits of Magellan, and entered them without delay." (SC-009-008) Paganel finds the channel a place of easy navigation and long views — "the Straits of Magellan are very much" travelled, he assures the company. (SC-009-040) Beyond its western mouth the ocean opens fresh: "Before her stem lay a broad, open, sparkling ocean." (SC-009-046)

## Geography & Features

The strait carries the name of its discoverer, whose reports of the natives it recalls: "Though Magellan called the natives Patagonians, the Fuegians called them Tiremenen, the Chilians Caucalhues." (SC-009-019) Later voyages find swifter ways round the continent's foot: "I must apprise your Lordship that I did not go through the Straits of Magellan again." (SC-027-025)
