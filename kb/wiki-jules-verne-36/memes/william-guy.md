# William Guy

## Overview

William Guy is the captain of the schooner _Jane_, of Liverpool — the vessel that, after "the shipwrecked mariners of the _Grampus_ had drifted no less than twenty-five degrees towards the south," "picked up" Arthur Pym and Dirk Peters (AM-005-046).

The whole plot turns on his kinship: "her captain, Len Guy, is William Guy's own brother" (AM-007-074), and it is to save him that the _Halbrane_ sails.

## Role in the Story

In Poe's romance his command carries the _Jane_ to Tsalal, where at Klock-Klock "Captain William Guy and his companions found a population" that Arthur Pym "estimated at ten thousand souls" (AM-005-061). He governs by negotiation, "immediately endeavour[ing] to come to an understanding with Too-Wit" and requesting leave to build sheds ashore (AM-005-062).

When treachery destroys the _Jane_, "Arthur Pym and William Guy escaped the doom of the _Jane_ and the most of her crew" (AM-007-070).

His survival is the mystery that drives Verne's sequel. A message on drifting ice reports "Captain William Guy and five of the men of the _Jane_" still living north of Tsalal Island (AM-006-049), and Len Guy clings to the hope that "my brother William Guy and five of his companions are living" (AM-008-020).

Had he been alive to see it, "William Guy would have recognized" the British flag "and come down to the shore" (AM-022-018).

## Character & Traits

He proves a patient, steady commander in extremity. Reunited at last, the men learn that "as William Guy told us, not an incident occurred to break the monotony of that existence of eleven years" (AM-024-029) — eleven years of marooned endurance.

Even in that plight he keeps exploring, as when "it occurred to William Guy to explore the fissure on the right" though "he found it blocked" (AM-024-026).

At the reckoning his little band, "counting William Guy, the captain, Arthur Pym, and Dirk Peters, formed a body of thirty-two men" (AM-024-004).

## Relationships

- [[An Antarctic Mystery]] — the novel whose southern rescue closes with the _Halbrane_ boat bringing "Captain William Guy and his three companions together" to safety (AM-026-007).
