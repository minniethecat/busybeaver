# Negoro

> **Note:** Inline PN citations for this page use a 4-character work code (`DSCF`); they render as plain text pending RFC-0003. The underlying PN data is valid.

## Overview

Negoro is the antagonist of *[[Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen]]*: a Portuguese by birth who, speaking English fluently, fills the humble position of cook aboard the schooner *Pilgrim* (DSCF-002-008). A man of perhaps forty years (DSCF-002-011), he keeps himself apart from the crew.

Even when the *Pilgrim* falls in with a wreck, Negoro alone does not leave his cabin-kitchen, the only one aboard whom the encounter does not appear to interest (DSCF-003-004).

## Role in the Story

Negoro's treachery is deliberate and secret. It is by his guilty hand that the ship's compass is made inaccurate, so that it henceforth gives only incorrect bearings (DSCF-011-007). When a violent roll throws him against the binnacle, he rises still clutching the piece of iron he has just taken from beneath it, and hides it before [[Dick Sand]] can see (DSCF-012-013).

Once the ship is wrecked, he surveys the strand and the cliff like a man trying to recall recollections and to fix them (DSCF-014-022) — for he knows this coast of Angola, and is in collusion with the American Harris (DSCF-018-112).

## Character & Traits

Negoro is driven by cruelty and old grudges. He declares he has an account to settle with the young novice Dick Sand (DSCF-020-010), and his countenance can express an implacable cruelty (DSCF-020-016). Revealed at last as an agent of the slave-trader Alvez, he coldly plans to make two parties of his captives — those whom he will sell as slaves, and the others (DSCF-020-078).

He is a wretch who is not satisfied with merely torturing his victims; he must also enjoy their sufferings (DSCF-030-012). Holding Mrs. Weldon captive, he schemes to lure her husband within reach by a letter describing himself as a faithful servant escaped from the savages (DSCF-031-066).

## Relationships

- [[Dick Sand]] — the fifteen-year-old novice captain whose voyage Negoro secretly wrecks and against whom he nurses a murderous grudge (DSCF-020-010).
- Harris — the American in whose collusion Negoro leads the castaways into Angola (DSCF-018-112).
- Alvez — the slave-trader in whose service Negoro is finally revealed (DSCF-020-078).

## Appearances

- [[Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen]] — as the *Pilgrim*'s treacherous cook, saboteur, and slave-trading antagonist.
