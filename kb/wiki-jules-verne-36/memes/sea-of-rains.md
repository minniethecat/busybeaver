# Sea of Rains

## Overview

The Sea of Rains, the lunar plain Mare Imbrium, is one of the northern basins the projectile crosses on its circuit of the Moon in *Round the Moon*. Verne pairs it with its neighbour in a wry aside: "Not far off appears the 'Sea of Rains,' fed by all the worries of existence." (RM-011-013)

## In the Narrative

The sea lies north of the Sea of Clouds, walled off by mountains: "separated from the Sea of Clouds by high chains of mountains, extends the Sea of Rains, Mare Imbrium, having its central point in north latitude 35°." (RM-011-018) The projectile's luminous rays "stretch as far as the Sea of Rains." (RM-012-018)

Passing above it is a milestone of the voyage: "About 5 a.m. the northern limit of the Sea of Rains was at last passed." (RM-013-041)

## Geography & Features

The plain is ringed by great lunar ranges: "The Apennines run along the eastern border of the Sea of Rains, and are continued on the north by the Carpathians." (RM-012-059) Its floor bears the scars of upheaval — "These mountainous circles must have been broken up by the vast cataclysm to which the Sea of Rains was due" (RM-012-061) — and it holds lesser heights such as the Helicon on its rim. (RM-013-032)
