# Ham Rock

## Overview

Ham Rock is the lonely reef in the Atlantic on which the foundering *Chancellor* comes to rest, named by her passengers during their enforced stay in *The Survivors of the Chancellor*. The name is chosen half in jest: "'I think we had better ask Captain Curtis to let us call our island Ham Rock.'" (SC2-018-015)

## In the Narrative

The reef becomes a brief refuge with its own landmark grotto: "Since its discovery we have not permitted a day to pass without spending some time in our Ham Rock grotto." (SC2-018-020) But safety is temporary — "There was assuredly no time to be lost before we ought to leave Ham Rock reef." (SC2-021-001)

When the ship at last floats free the castaways watch the reef sink astern: "two hours later the last peak of Ham Rock had vanished." (SC2-021-020) Later, adrift on their raft, they look back on it as solid ground now lost: "we are no longer secured firmly to the solid soil of the Ham Rock reef, but we are floating over an abyss." (SC2-022-012)

## Geography & Features

Ham Rock is a bare reef rising just enough to shelter the grounded ship, marked by the grotto where Andre carves a farewell inscription before departure. Its position lies on the *Chancellor*'s drift toward the sargasso-strewn waters of the mid-Atlantic. (SC2-021-001)
