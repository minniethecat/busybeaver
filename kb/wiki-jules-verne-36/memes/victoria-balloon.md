# Victoria (balloon)

## Overview

The Victoria is the hydrogen balloon in which Dr. Samuel Ferguson crosses Africa in *[[five-weeks-in-a-balloon|Five Weeks in a Balloon]]*. Verne calls it "that marvellous vehicle which was to convey him through the air," the "constant object of his solicitude" (FWB-007-002).

Its crew name her affectionately. When disaster threatens, Joe vows they will save "the Victoria" even if they must "throw overboard our clothes" (FWB-041-025).

## Design & Operation

Ferguson fills the balloon "with hydrogen gas, which is fourteen and a half times lighter than common air" (FWB-007-003), sizing it to lift a total load of "4,000 pounds" (FWB-007-004). In its elongated shape, "fifty feet" across and "seventy-five feet" tall, its capacity reaches "in round numbers... ninety thousand cubic feet" (FWB-007-008).

Its signature feature is a double envelope: Ferguson "constructed two of different sizes, and inclosed the smaller in the larger one," so gas vented from the outer balloon leaves "the smaller one... still remain intact" (FWB-007-010). The envelopes are "a strong but light Lyons silk, coated with gutta percha," waterproof and gas-tight (FWB-007-013).

Below hangs a wicker car "of a circular form and fifteen feet in diameter... protected below by a system of elastic springs" (FWB-007-016). A secret "Buntzen electric battery" and coiled apparatus let Ferguson heat the gas to climb and descend without wasting hydrogen or ballast (FWB-007-018).

## Role in the Story

The Victoria carries Ferguson, Kennedy, and Joe across the continent. Her equilibrium is "extremely sensitive," so that "the loss of an almost insignificant weight suffices to produce a very noticeable displacement" (FWB-007-023). Fleeing over a mountain wall, the crew lighten her again and again as "the Victoria was rushing on with great rapidity" toward the ridge (FWB-041-058).

By the journey's end Joe mourns her like a comrade: "I had got fond of her as the sailor does of his ship... She has done us good service" (FWB-041-032).

## Science & Speculation

The Victoria's real innovation is Ferguson's heat-management scheme, which spares the usual waste of gas and ballast: he takes "only two hundred pounds of ballast for 'unforeseen emergencies'... since otherwise he did not expect to use any, thanks to the peculiarity of his apparatus" (FWB-007-027).

Verne grounds the craft in real aerostatics — accurate lift calculations, hydrogen's known buoyancy, gutta-percha-coated silk — extrapolating only the controllable-dilatation apparatus. Unlike the [[Albatross]]'s heavier-than-air screws, the Victoria embodies the lighter-than-air school that [[robur|Robur]] would scorn.
