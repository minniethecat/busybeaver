# Sullivan Island

## Overview

Sullivan Island is the real island on the northern side of the entrance to Charleston Harbor, the counterpart to Morris Island opposite. "It is enclosed between Morris Island on the south and Sullivan Island on the north." (BR-006-014)

Unlike its Federal-held neighbour, "Sullivan Island... was in the hands of the Confederates, who were also in possession of Moultrie Fort, situated at the extremity of the island." (BR-006-014)

## In the Narrative

The island gives its name to one of the five channels into the estuary: "Five channels led into the estuary, Sullivan Island Channel, the Northern Channel, the Overall Channel, the Principal Channel, and lastly, the Lawford Channel." (BR-006-015)

Its shoal channel is the Dolphin's refuge: "the steamer ranging near Sullivan Island, thanks to her small draft, entered the narrow strait full steam; there she was in safety, for no Federalist cruiser dared follow her." (BR-006-023)

## Geography & Features

Steering the channel that "runs the length of Sullivan Island," James bore toward Fort Moultrie and passed "Moultrieville, situated at the extremity of Sullivan Island, and entered the bay." (BR-006-029)

The Confederate guns on it are the last danger of the run: "Nothing at all, but everything from Fort Moultrie, at the end of Sullivan Island." (BR-009-036) On the escape, James warns, "we must not think of taking the Sullivan Island channel; we should run directly under the Confederate guns." (BR-009-026)
