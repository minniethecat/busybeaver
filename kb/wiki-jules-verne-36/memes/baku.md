## Overview

Baku is the eastern terminus of the Transcaucasian railway, where travellers embark to cross the Caspian. The station at Tiflis is the junction of three lines, the eastern of which ends at Baku. (ASC-001-019) It is the town of the Guebres, the city of the Parsees, the metropolis of the fire-worshippers. (ASC-003-012)

## In the Narrative

It was for Baku that the correspondent booked his passage east; he took a ticket for Baku, first class. (ASC-001-062) Given half a day to see the city, he meant to lose not an hour of it. (ASC-003-011) Though Georgia is a Russian province, Baku keeps its Persian physiognomy. (ASC-003-013)

## Geography & Features

The railway skirts the carriage road from Tiflis, running through Elisabethpol and Aliat to Baku along the valley of the Koura. (ASC-002-003) Baku is the most frequented and the safest port on the Caspian. (ASC-003-042) Its furnaces burn the residue of naphtha, which Baku and Derbent produce in inexhaustible quantities. (ASC-006-006)

## Connections

Baku is bound to [[Tiflis]] by the trunk line that begins at the [[Black Sea]] port of Poti. (ASC-001-019) Its quays open on the [[Caspian Sea]], the great basin the reporter must cross in [[The Adventures of a Special Correspondent]]. (ASC-003-042)
