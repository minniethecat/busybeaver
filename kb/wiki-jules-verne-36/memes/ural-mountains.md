# Ural Mountains

## Overview

The Ural Mountains are the real range that Michael Strogoff must cross on his mission into Siberia in *Michael Strogoff*. They mark the boundary of Asia — Siberia extends "from the Ural Mountains, which separate it from Russia in Europe, to the shores of the Pacific Ocean." (MS-003-002)

The courier's whole errand is framed as passing beyond them: "you shall know why I left the shores of the Baltic to go beyond the Ural Mountains." (MS-008-012)

## In the Narrative

Reaching and clearing the range is Strogoff's first great object. He is filled with the "wish of being beyond the Ural Mountains, so as to judge for" himself of the danger ahead. (MS-009-005) His companion Nadia is told they will soon "be at the foot of the Ural Mountains." (MS-010-027)

The mountains are also the enemy's road: "After leaving Perm, Ivan Ogareff crossed the Ural mountains, entered Siberia, and penetrated the Kirghiz steppes." (MS-003-042) On the 20th of July "they caught the first glimpse of the Ural Mountains in the east." (MS-010-061)

## Geography & Features

The range is immense — "The Ural Mountains extend in a length of over two thousand miles between Europe and Asia." (MS-011-001) It carries the great post road: rivers issuing "from the Ural" pass "through Ekaterenburg, Kasirnov, Tioumen, Ishim, Omsk." (MS-003-005)

The best crossing runs by a known town — travellers go "from Perm to Ekaterenburg, since that is the best and safest route by which to cross the Ural Mountains." (MS-008-038) In wartime the ordinary mail "across the Ural Mountains" was "discontinued." (MS-010-004)
