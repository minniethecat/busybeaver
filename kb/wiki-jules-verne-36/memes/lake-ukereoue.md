# Lake Ukereoue

## Overview

Lake Ukereoue is Verne's name for the great equatorial African lake — the conjectured reservoir of the Nile — sighted from Dr. Ferguson's balloon in *Five Weeks in a Balloon*. It borders the fabled mountains, so that "the ancient legend which made these mountains the cradle of the Nile... really border upon Lake Ukereoue, the conjectured reservoir of the waters of the great river." (FWB-018-003)

## In the Narrative

The lake is tied to Speke's exploration: "When Captain Speke set out to discover Lake Ukereoue, he ascended more to the eastward in a straight line above Kazeh." (FWB-017-008)

From the car the travellers pass "the village of Uyofu, the last northern limit of the Unyamwezi, opposite to the Lake Ukereoue, which could still be seen." (FWB-017-015)

The Nile question is settled as they trace the outflow: "They spoke of a river by which Lake Ukereoue discharged its waters toward the north, and this river exists, and we are descending it." (FWB-018-036)

## Geography & Features

Speke "made a triangulation of this part of the lake, but he could not procure a boat... to visit the great island of Ukereoue which is very populous, is governed by three sultans, and appears to be only a promontory at low tide." (FWB-018-009)

Its position anchors the expedition's reckoning: "take the map and see what is the longitude of the southern point of Lake Ukereoue, reached by Speke." (FWB-029-033)
