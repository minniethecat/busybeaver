# Smith's Straits

## Overview

Smith's Straits is a northern passage of the Arctic archipelago that features in the secret orders driving the *Forward* northward in *The Adventures of Captain Hatteras*. Shandon reads the anonymous captain's instructions aloud: "Have the goodness to take a northerly direction towards Melville Bay, and from thence try and penetrate into Smith's Straits." (ACH-009-014)

## In the Narrative

The order provokes disbelief among the crew, who grasp its danger at once. Shandon repeats that they are "to direct our path not only towards Melville Bay, but again into Smith's Straits" (ACH-009-042), and answers the incredulous echo "Smith's Straits?" (ACH-009-044) with its grim pedigree: "Yes, Smith's Straits, that's the route the American Kane followed in 1853, and at the price of what dangers!" (ACH-009-046)

## Geography & Features

The strait is often sealed by ice, forcing the expedition to seek alternatives. Caught fast, the crew judge "it seems to me impossible, for this year at least, to get into Smith's Strait." (ACH-013-034) When it closes, another route opens: "If Smith's Strait is closed up from us, Lancaster Strait, on the west coast of Baffin's Sea, is not." (ACH-013-036)
