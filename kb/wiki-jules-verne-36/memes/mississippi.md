## Overview

The Mississippi is the magnificent river amid whose basin flows the "king of rivers," as the Americans would style it. (FEM-024-018) It ranks among the world's mightiest waters, one of the two rivers more extensive than the Amazon that cross the North American continent. (EHLA-008-006)

## In the Narrative

Crossing the continent by rail, Fogg's train forded the Mississippi during the night at Davenport, and by Rock Island entered Illinois. (AWED-031-036) An earlier traveller had visited much of America and was about to descend the Mississippi to New Orleans when war recalled him to France. (AMB-001-008)

## Geography & Features

Robur's airship crossed the "Father of Waters," the Mississippi, whose double-decked steam-boats seemed no bigger than canoes from above. (RC-009-014) Its steamboats were famous enough that a cabin aboard the Nautilus was likened to those of the helmsmen on the Mississippi or Hudson. (TTLU-029-090)

## Connections

The river drains the heart of the [[United States]], gathering waters from the continent's most settled regions. (FEM-024-018) It flows past Illinois, where Fogg's line crossed it at Davenport and Rock Island. (AWED-031-036) Verne threads it through [[Around the World in Eighty Days]] as a landmark of the American crossing. (AWED-031-036)
