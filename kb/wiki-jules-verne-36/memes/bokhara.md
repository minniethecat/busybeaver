## Overview

Bokhara is the ancient city and khanate of Central Asia, a great oasis on the line of the Grand Transasiatic. At chief towns like Merv, Bokhara, Samarkand and Tashkend the train would stop a few hours, enough to do them in reporter style. (ASC-005-018) The monotony of the desert journey was broken only on the vast oases of Merv, Bokhara and Samarkand. (ASC-006-008)

## In the Narrative

Bokhara is the revered heart of the region's learning and faith. It is the Rome of Islam, the Noble City, the City of Temples, the centre of the Mahometan religion. (ASC-011-002) From the Tartars of Bokhara and Samarkand, of a higher race than the Kirghizes, came the learned men who made those opulent cities illustrious. (ASC-006-021)

## Geography & Features

The Transcaspian railway reached the Khanate towards the end of 1886, seventeen months after the first sleeper was laid. (ASC-010-053) At five in the morning the train halts at the capital of the Khanate of Bokhara, eleven hundred and seven versts from Uzun Ada. (ASC-010-073)

## Connections

Bokhara is an oasis station of the Grand Transasiatic described in [[The Adventures of a Special Correspondent]], set among the sister cities of Merv and Samarkand. (ASC-005-018) Its khanate was annexed in the Russian campaigns of the 1860s. (ASC-011-002)
