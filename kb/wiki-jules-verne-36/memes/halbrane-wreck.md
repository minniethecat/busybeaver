# The Wreck of the Halbrane

## Overview

The Wreck of the Halbrane is the destruction of the schooner that carried the expedition into the far south, the disaster that strands her crew upon a floating mountain of ice in the [[antarctic-sea|Antarctic Sea]].

The blow comes out of the polar fog. As the boatswain later put it, they came upon an iceberg in the middle of the fog, and were unable to keep clear of it (AM-019-111).

For the narrator the first sign is violence in the dark. Just at that instant a violent shock flung me out of my bunk (AM-018-143).

## What Happens

The iceberg itself was the agent of the wreck. It had chosen just that moment to turn head over heels, and in turning it struck the Halbrane and carried it off just as a battledore catches a shuttlecock, leaving her stranded a hundred feet above the level of the sea (AM-019-113).

The scale of it defies belief. In the middle of these remote regions their only means of transport had just been snatched from its natural element and carried off by the turn of an iceberg to a height of more than one hundred feet (AM-019-115).

There the schooner rests for weeks, marooned aloft. Their iceberg was as motionless as though it had been stranded on a shoal (AM-020-007), while the crew labour to lower her back to the water.

The end, when it comes, is sudden. A minute ago she was one hundred feet in the air; now she was five hundred in the depths of the sea (AM-020-127).

## Significance

Nothing at all was saved. The schooner, carried off as though by an avalanche, had disappeared in the abyss, and not a particle of the Halbrane remained, not even a wreck (AM-020-126).

The loss breaks even the hardest man aboard: when the schooner had disappeared in the abyss, the narrator saw big tears fall from the mate West's eyes (AM-020-129). Deprived of their ship at the bottom of the world, the survivors must go on by boat alone.

## Participants & Setting

- The destruction of the sealing-schooner Halbrane, lifted onto and then plunged from an overturning iceberg in the Antarctic Sea.
- Suffered by the crew under Captain Len Guy and the mate West, with the passenger Jeorling as narrator.
- Chronicled in *[[an-antarctic-mystery|An Antarctic Mystery]]*.
