## Overview

Shanghai is the Chinese port on which Phileas Fogg's American crossing depends. When the Carnatic sails without him from Hong Kong, the pilot Bunsby proposes a run north — by going to Nagasaki, at the extreme south of Japan, or even to Shanghai, which is only eight hundred miles away. (AWED-020-047) The mail packet for San Francisco starts from Shanghai. (AWED-020-049)

## In the Narrative

Fogg cannot afford to miss it. "Pilot," said Mr. Fogg, "I must take the American steamer at Yokohama, and not at Shanghai or Nagasaki." (AWED-020-048) Yet the boat originates here — "And when does the boat leave Shanghai?" he asks, and the answer sets the deadline for the whole race. (AWED-020-052)

The passage up the coast is a gamble against wind and time. The pilot several times assured Mr. Fogg that they would reach Shanghai in time, to which that gentleman responded that he counted upon it. (AWED-021-023)

## Geography & Features

A treaty port on the Chinese coast, Shanghai is the northern terminus of the trans-Pacific mail line — eight hundred miles from Hong Kong, with northward currents that favour a small boat racing to intercept the departing American steamer.
