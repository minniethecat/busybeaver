# Washington Bay

## Overview

Washington Bay is the fictional bay indenting the southern shore of Lincoln Island, one of the features the colonists name when they map their new home. Cyrus Smith proposes giving "to that large hollow on the south, Washington Bay," (MI-011-061) and so "they named the two bays and the mountain, Union Bay, Washington Bay, and Mount Franklin." (MI-011-062)

It becomes a familiar reach of the settlers' surveyed coast.

## In the Narrative

The colonists trace its shore on their long marches — "at one o'clock the colonists arrived at the other side of Washington Bay, they having now gone a distance of twenty miles." (MI-027-038) Its heights serve as a lookout: to watch a vessel slipping past "it would be then necessary to ascend the height of Was[hington Bay]." (MI-043-050)

Their vigilance settles on its waters: "every day their telescope covered the horizon enclosed by Union and Washington Bays." (MI-056-022)

## Geography & Features

The bay sweeps the southern coast between two headlands: the colonists explore "the vast shore of Washington Bay from Claw Cape to Reptile End, the woody and marshy border of the west coast." (MI-053-023)

Together with Union Bay to the east it frames the island's inhabited seaboard, the two great indentations the settlers watch over from Prospect Heights.
