# Gun-Cotton

## Overview

Gun-cotton, or pyroxyle, is the nitro-cellulose explosive that Verne makes the favoured propellant of his engineers, from the lunar cannon of *[[from-the-earth-to-the-moon|From the Earth to the Moon]]* to the castaways of *[[the-mysterious-island|The Mysterious Island]]*. It is defined as "pyroxyle, that is to say gun-cotton, a substance in which cotton is not indispensable, as the elementary tissue of vegetables may be used" (MI-030-007).

Its great appeal is power: Verne notes that "its force is four times that of ordinary powder" (MI-030-008).

## Design & Operation

The *Mysterious Island* engineer [[cyrus-harding|Cyrus Harding]] manufactures it from first principles: "To make pyroxyle, the cotton must be immersed in the fuming azotic acid for a quarter of an hour, then washed in cold water and dried. Nothing could be more simple" (MI-030-009). Lacking fuming acid, he substitutes "ordinary azotic acid, mixed... with... concentrated sulphuric acid" for "the same result" (MI-030-010).

Verne is candid about its dangers: pyroxyle suffers "an excessive inflammability, since it takes fire at one hundred and seventy degrees instead of two hundred and forty, and lastly, an instantaneous deflagration which might damage the firearms" (MI-030-008). Against that, it is "not injured by damp" and "does not make the gun-barrels dirty" (MI-030-008).

## Role in the Story

In *[[from-the-earth-to-the-moon|From the Earth to the Moon]]* gun-cotton is the charge that fires the [[Columbiad]]: the loading means "the introduction into it of 400,000 lbs. of gun-cotton," a mass Captain Nicholl fears "would ignite of itself under the pressure of the projectile" (FEM-025-002).

It is handled with extreme care — "there were never more than 500 lbs. of pyroxyle at once in the inclosure," worked "by workmen walking barefoot" under [[Ruhmkorff Apparatus]] lamps (FEM-025-004).

It is then fired electrically: "it was by means of electricity that fire was to be set to this mass of gun-cotton," a wire running two miles to "a powerful pile of Bunsen" (FEM-025-005). On [[lincoln-island|Lincoln Island]] the same substance renews the colonists' ammunition when lead and powder run short (MI-030-006).

## Science & Speculation

Gun-cotton is one of Verne's real chemistries rather than an invention: nitro-cellulose was a genuine mid-century explosive, and he reproduces its actual synthesis and hazards faithfully.

Its narrative value is that it can be made from common plants — "the textile fiber of hemp and flax, in paper, the pith of the elder" (MI-030-007) — letting shipwrecked heroes rearm themselves, while its four-fold power over gunpowder makes scaling up to a Moon-launching charge feel like magnifying a known force rather than magic.
