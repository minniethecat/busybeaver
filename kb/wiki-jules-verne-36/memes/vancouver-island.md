## Overview

Vancouver Island is the large island off the Pacific coast of North America that serves as a mysterious home port in *[[The Waif of the Cynthia]]*. The elusive Tudor Brown is announced as sailing a vessel "from Vancouver's Island" (WC-015-069), the distant isle standing behind every rumour of his movements.

It lies far out in the western ocean. It is explained that "Mr. Tudor Brown came from Vancouver's Island, in the Pacific, and his yacht had been waiting there for him for three months" (WC-016-019), the island fixing the remote origin of the story's most shadowy figure.

## In the Narrative

Vancouver Island anchors the chase after the vanishing yachtsman. The doctor recounts how Tudor Brown's yacht "was at Valparaiso and he telegraphed for her to wait for him at Victoria, on the coast of Vancouver" (WC-016-022), the island's port of Victoria named as the appointed rendezvous.

The trail leads back to it again and again. Word comes "that his vessel was at Valparaiso when he telegraphed them to wait for him at Vancouver" (WC-017-009), the island being the fixed point to which the wandering yacht is always summoned home across the Pacific.

## Geography & Features

Vancouver Island marks the bend of the northwestern American coast. Verne notes how "the coast curves off from Vancouver Island up to the Aleutians" (RC-011-003), the isle standing at the southern end of the long arc that sweeps toward the [[Aleutian Islands]] and the Russian sphere ceded to the United States in 1867.

It lies squarely within the great Pacific waters. Its port of Victoria and its western shore front the open ocean, so that a yacht kept there commands the whole northern reach of the sea between America and Asia, from Valparaiso in the south to the Aleutian chain in the north (WC-016-019).

## Connections

Vancouver Island is the Pacific isle and home port of Tudor Brown's yacht in *[[The Waif of the Cynthia]]*, the rendezvous to which his vessel is repeatedly summoned, and the southern anchor of the coastal arc that curves toward the [[Aleutian Islands]].
