## Overview

Ekaterenburg is a real Russian town on the eastern, Siberian slope of the Urals, the first great relay Michael Strogoff reaches after crossing the mountains. The trans-Siberian telegraph line, on issuing from the Ural, passes through Ekaterenburg, Kasirnov, Tioumen, Ishim, Omsk, Kolyvan, Tomsk, Krasnoiarsk, Nijni-Udinsk, and Irkutsk. (MS-003-005)

## In the Narrative

The town anchors the wire whose severing sets the plot in motion: it was this wire, extending from Ekaterenburg to Nikolaevsk, which had been cut, first beyond Tomsk, and then between Tomsk and Kolyvan. (MS-003-006) It also marks the only reliable overland approach — except by the direct route from Ekaterenburg to Irkutsk, the often marshy steppes are not easily practicable. (MS-003-060)

Strogoff's own journey converges here. A fellow traveller offers, "I will lend you one of my horses... and to-morrow we will arrive together at Ekaterenburg." (MS-012-045) Rumor even sends the enemy the same way: "he was to take the road from Kasan to Ekaterenburg." (MS-012-081)

## Geography & Features

Lying where the Urals give way to the West Siberian plain, Ekaterenburg is the gateway of the great Siberian post-road and the telegraph that binds Irkutsk to the capital.
