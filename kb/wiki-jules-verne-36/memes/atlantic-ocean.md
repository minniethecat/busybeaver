# Atlantic Ocean

## Overview

The Atlantic Ocean is the first of the three great seas the 37th parallel crosses in *In Search of the Castaways*, and the water on which the *Duncan* begins her round-the-world search. Paganel traces the line from its start: "after leaving America the thirty-seventh parallel crosses the Atlantic Ocean." (SC-023-088)

## In the Narrative

The ocean is the yacht's appointed rendezvous. When the overland party is separated from the ship, it is understood that "the Atlantic Ocean was the appointed meeting place with the DUNCAN." (SC-014-036)

From the American coast the yacht sets her course eastward across it: "shortly after, the DUNCAN had left the American coast, and was running eastward, her sharp keel rapidly cutting her way through the waves of the Atlantic Ocean." (SC-027-091) At the voyage's close she runs it again — she "doubled Cape Horn, and made a swift run up the Atlantic Ocean." (SC-066-003)

## Geography & Features

The ocean holds the lonely mid-sea groups the search must weigh. Of the Tristan cluster the narrator notes: "Inaccessible Island is eighteen miles to the southwest and Nightingale Island is ten miles to the southeast, and this completes the little solitary group of islets in the Atlantic Ocean." (SC-028-023)

Such isolated specks, scattered across its vastness, are exactly the kind of refuge on which a castaway of the *Britannia* might have been cast up.
