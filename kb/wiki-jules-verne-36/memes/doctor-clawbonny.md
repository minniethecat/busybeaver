# Doctor Clawbonny

## Overview

Named in the anonymous letter that assembles the brig's crew, he is recommended as "Dr. Clawbonny, of this town, who will introduce himself to you when necessary" (ACH-002-007).

He is a physician by title but a naturalist by vocation — "a doctor, and a good one, though practising little" (ACH-003-054), who by middle age had become "a _savant_, well known in the town" and a member of Liverpool's learned institutions.

## Role in the Story

He wins his berth by sheer eagerness, presenting the letter that grants him passage: "If Dr. Clawbonny wishes to embark on board the _Forward_ for a long cruise, he may introduce himself to the commander" (ACH-003-042).

His arrival steadies the crew — "the admission of the doctor on board had given the crew more confidence," for "they knew that where the worthy doctor went they could follow" (ACH-004-002).

When the polar march is decided, he stands among the chosen few: after "a short discussion about it between Hatteras and Clawbonny, the journey was persisted in" (ACH-028-003), and the sledge party numbered "four men, Hatteras, Clawbonny, Bell, and Simpson, and seven dogs" (ACH-028-013).

## Character & Traits

His cabin is a floating museum; he was "in his element" the day he took possession of it (ACH-004-004), for its few square feet let him become "instantaneously a doctor, a mathematician, an astronomer, a geographer, a botanist, or a conchologist" (ACH-004-006).

His learning is worn lightly, disclaimed with a modest laugh — "they pretend that I am learned; they are mistaken, commander" (ACH-003-047) — yet the crew marvel that "the doctor has an answer to everything" (ACH-004-015).

His warmth is proverbial: a kindness "would have tamed a tiger," though even so he tried in vain to win the strange dog's graces (ACH-004-007).

## Relationships

- [[Captain Hatteras]] — the expedition's iron-willed leader, with whom he deliberates and whose polar march he shares to the end.
- Johnson — the old boatswain from whom "Clawbonny was getting information," his numerous voyages giving "an interesting and sensible education" (ACH-007-003).

## Appearances

- [[The Adventures of Captain Hatteras]] — as the Forward's learned naturalist, whose science and courage carry the crew through the Arctic, explaining even how "scientific men, and Dr. Clawbonny amongst them" (ACH-024-002) read the frozen world, and turning a snow-wall into shelter (ACH-024-007).
