# Indian Ocean

## Overview

The Indian Ocean is one of the three great seas the search of *In Search of the Castaways* must weigh as it follows the 37th parallel round the globe. Paganel traces the line across it in council: "after leaving the Atlantic, we pass two degrees below the Cape of Good Hope, and into the Indian Ocean." (SC-027-064)

## In the Narrative

The ocean becomes a live theory of Captain Grant's fate. Paganel reasons that "Captain Grant could have crossed the Pacific and found himself in the Indian Ocean," (SC-030-014) and imagines the disaster there: "here, perhaps, Captain Grant, with a disabled ship and diminished crew, had struggled against the tremendous hurricanes of the Indian Ocean." (SC-030-002)

The parallel carries the *Duncan* toward Australia across its waters: "they must be sailing then in that part of the Indian Ocean which washed the Australian continent, and in four days might hope to see Cape Bernouilli appear on the horizon." (SC-031-001)

## Geography & Features

The ocean's lonely island-groups draw the searchers' attention. Amsterdam and St Paul form "this lonely group in the Indian Ocean... two distinct islands, thirty-three miles apart, and situated exactly on the meridian of the Indian peninsula." (SC-029-023)

Its very vastness troubles the reading of the bottle-message: "what likelihood, consequently, would there be that a fragile bottle would ever find its way along such busy thoroughfares right out into the Indian Ocean?" (SC-032-011)
