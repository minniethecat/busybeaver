# Lake Kirdall

## Overview

Lake Kirdall is the landlocked Kansas lake that becomes one of the scenes of Robur's uncanny machine in *The Master of the World*. The narrator introduces it as an obscure body of water: "Lake Kirdall in Kansas, forty miles west of Topeka, is little known." (MW-007-045)

Its isolation is the very point of the mystery, for "Lake Kirdall, however, has no connection with any other waters." (MW-007-054)

## In the Narrative

The lake draws attention when a submarine is seen speeding beneath its surface. The account of the lake is set down deliberately, being "necessary for the understanding of the remarkable facts which we are about to report." (MW-007-050) Observers conclude "that there is actually a submarine boat which darts about beneath the surface of Lake Kirdall with most remarkable rapidity." (MW-007-059)

The puzzle deepens because the lake is sealed: even granting a perfected submarine, "there remains as before the question how could it have reached Lake Kirdall?" (MW-007-056) The machine is Robur's *Terror*, able to travel by land, sea, and air alike.

## Geography & Features

Verne gives the lake precise dimensions. It "covers about seventy-five square miles, and its level is but slightly below that of the heights which surround it" (MW-007-047), and "is in many places fifty feet deep close to shore." (MW-007-048) Ringed by high ground and fed by no outlet, it forms a natural closed basin.

That very enclosure defeats the searchers. "In vain was Lake Kirdall searched from end to end" (MW-008-005); the submarine "was heard from no more, neither in Lake Kirdall nor elsewhere" (MW-008-006), leaving no trace "beneath the waters of Lake Kirdall." (MW-008-020)

## Connections

- Robur — the inventor whose machine the Terror haunts the lake
- The Master of the World — Verne's novel of Robur's return
