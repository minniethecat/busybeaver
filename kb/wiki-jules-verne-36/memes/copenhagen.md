## Overview

Copenhagen is the Danish capital from which the descent into the earth begins. There is only one trip every month from Copenhagen to Rejkiavik, on the 22nd. (JCE-007-053) So the travellers hurried to Copenhagen as fast as they could to secure their passage. (JCE-007-055)

## In the Narrative

At ten in the morning they set foot in Copenhagen and drove to the Phoenix Hotel in Breda Gate. (JCE-008-023) The professor was struck by a church spire in the island of Amak, which forms the south-west quarter of Copenhagen. (JCE-008-035) There the young narrator took his lessons on gulf exploration at the Frelsers Kirk. (JCE-017-004)

## Geography & Features

Copenhagen is a seat of northern learning. Its library holds eight thousand volumes in the old Scandinavian tongue, with all the novelties Copenhagen sends out each year. (JCE-010-006) The city stands near the fifty-fifth parallel, that of Edinburgh and Copenhagen alike. (MS-027-003)

## Connections

Copenhagen opens the road to [[Iceland]] and the crater that leads to [[A Journey to the Center of the Earth]]. (JCE-007-053) It lies across the strait from [[Sweden]], within the realm of [[Denmark]]. (WC-021-029)
