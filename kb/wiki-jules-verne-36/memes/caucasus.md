## Overview

The Caucasus is the great range whose crossing brings the traveller down toward the capital of Transcaucasia. (ASC-001-010) Its western wall rises steeply on the horizon, seen running up as a steamer rounds Cape Apcheron. (ASC-003-066)

## In the Narrative

As a young Siberian, Michael Strogoff first distinguished himself on a journey to the Caucasus, through difficult country ravaged by restless successors of Schamyl. (MS-004-039) The armament of the region's soldiers, with schaska sword and kindjall dagger, marks the fighting men of the Caucasus. (ASC-001-014)

## Geography & Features

The range is famed for its wares: the fair at Nijni-Novgorod displays weapons from the Caucasus alongside furs and Turkey carpets. (MS-006-037) Its highland breeds are equally renowned, such as the long-haired dogs of the Caucasus with their disquieting fangs. (ASC-010-014)

## Connections

The Caucasus divides European [[Russia]] from Transcaucasia, the gateway to Strogoff's eastern road. (ASC-001-010) It borders the country crossed in [[Michael Strogoff]], and its passes feed the traffic of [[The Adventures of a Special Correspondent]]. (MS-004-039)
