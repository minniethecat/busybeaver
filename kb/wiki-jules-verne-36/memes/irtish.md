## Overview

The Irtish is one of the great rivers of Western Siberia in *Michael Strogoff*. The town of Omsk stands upon its banks, and it was there that the courier's aged mother Marfa lived and could never be induced to leave. (MS-004-038) At the outbreak of the Tartar invasion the Czar's ministers were assured that the enemy had not yet advanced beyond the Irtish and the Obi. (MS-002-022)

## In the Narrative

As the war spread, Feofar-Khan's Tartars passed Semipolatinsk and came descending the Irtish. (MS-009-030) For some days, by forced marches, they continued descending the river, overrunning the whole province of Semipolatinsk. (MS-012-075) Aboard the steamer a traveler observed that if the Kirghiz too should descend the Irtish, the road to Irkutsk would no longer be safe. (MS-005-040)

## Geography & Features

The river helps to frame the territory of the Kirghiz hordes: the encampments of the middle horde occupy all the space between the rivers Sara Sou, the Irtish, and the Upper Ishim, along with Lake Saisang and Lake Aksakal. (MS-003-058) Early dispatches placed the Tartar advance no further than the Irtish and the Obi, so the river stood as a frontier of the invasion. (MS-002-022)

## Connections

The Irtish is bound above all to Omsk, the courier's native town, which stands upon its banks and shelters the house that his mother Marfa refused ever to leave. (MS-004-038)
