# London

## Overview

London is the English capital and home of Phileas Fogg in *Around the World in Eighty Days*, the fixed point from which his wager departs and to which it must return. Fogg is a creature of the city's settled routine: "it was at least certain that Phileas Fogg had not absented himself from London for many years." (AWED-001-009)

## In the Narrative

The whole journey is reckoned against a return to London. Fogg is pressed to remember "when you are due in London again," (AWED-004-035) and he "rightly suspected that his departure from London would create a lively sensation at the West End." (AWED-005-002) His fame spreads when "the Illustrated London News came out with his portrait, copied from a photograph in the Reform Club." (AWED-005-003)

The city is also the base of the pursuit against him. "Detectives were also charged with narrowly watching those who arrived at or left London by rail," (AWED-003-012) and Fix reasons that "an English criminal, you know, is always better concealed in London than anywhere else." (AWED-006-025)

## Geography & Features

London is a metropolis of clubs, rail termini, and famous sights — among them the waxworks, for "Madame Tussaud's 'people'... are of wax, and are much visited in London." (AWED-002-003)

Its great rail lines are the threads of Fogg's departure and every traveller's route to the wider world, watched at every station "who arrived at or left London by rail." (AWED-003-012)
