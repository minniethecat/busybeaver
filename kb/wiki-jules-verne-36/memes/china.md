## Overview

China is the vast eastern empire that forms the far terminus of the Grand Transasiatic railway, whose long slip map shows the whole line between the Caspian and the eastern coast of China. (ASC-006-002) Few travellers make the complete journey between the Caspian and the eastern provinces of China — about six thousand kilometres. (ASC-005-013)

For the reporter Claudius Bombarnac the true prize lies at the line's end: a mysterious packing-case addressed to Mademoiselle Zinca Klork, Avenue Cha-Coua, Pekin, China. (ASC-004-073)

## In the Narrative

The Grand Transasiatic traverses the steppes of Kara Koum in Turkestan and crosses the desert of Gobi in China. (ASC-002-038) Along the way Russians carry the travellers up to the frontier of Turkestan, and Chinese take them on through China. (ASC-005-019)

Trade follows the same overland thread: all the green tea consumed in Central Asia is brought by caravan from China through India, and though the transport is longer, it sells more cheaply than the Russian tea. (ASC-008-018)

## Geography & Features

In the bazaars of the line's western end lie the thousand products of Persia, China, Turkey, Siberia, and Mongolia. (ASC-001-043) So great is the reach of commerce that a dental factory sends teeth as far as China, burning a hundred tons of coal a day to supply the dentists of the old and new worlds. (ASC-003-058)

Beneath the surface stirs unrest: the secret society of the Blue Lotus agitates for the abolition of the pigtail, the emblem of the servitude of the Chinese to the Manchu Tartars. (ASC-004-035)
