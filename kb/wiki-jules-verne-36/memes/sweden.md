## Overview

Sweden is the Scandinavian homeland from which the search of *The Waif of the Cynthia* radiates. When questioned, Erik answered that he was from Sweden — or, to be more exact, from Norway — and that his family lived in the province of Bergen. (WC-013-009)

The learned men of Sweden were greatly excited about the proposed arctic expedition of the navigator Nordenskiold, and their enthusiasm was shared by a large proportion of the population. (WC-011-002) He did not conceal his hope of reaching Behring's Strait before autumn, and all Sweden shared that hope. (WC-011-005)

## In the Narrative

The quest keeps returning the searchers homeward: they resolved to pass through Ireland on the way back to Sweden, to see if Patrick O'Donoghan had returned there. (WC-009-029)

For Erik the north-east passage was the most direct way to return to Sweden, joined to a truly scientific desire. (WC-016-043) At any risk he must return to Sweden by way of the Sea of Kara. (WC-017-016) In the end Erik's revelations filled the columns of the most widely circulated newspaper in Stockholm, and indeed in all Sweden. (WC-020-035)

## Geography & Features

Small customs mark the land: the standing coachman is the custom in Norway and Sweden. (WC-003-069) From the fifteenth, the occupants of the *Alaska* beheld the midnight sun, astonishing even for Norwegians and the natives of southern Sweden. (WC-015-048)

Erik's final dash traces its map: he left Stockholm, took the railroad to Malmo on the north-west coast of Sweden, crossed the strait in twenty minutes, and reached Copenhagen. (WC-021-029)
