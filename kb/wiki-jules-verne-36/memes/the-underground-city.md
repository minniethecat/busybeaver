## Overview

*The Underground City* is Jules Verne's subterranean romance of the Scottish coalfields. It opens as the engineer James Starr, former manager of the Aberfoyle mines, is summoned by letter to "the Aberfoyle coal-mines, Dochart pit, Yarrow shaft" (UC-001-002).

At Callander station he is to be met "by Harry Ford, son of the old overman Simon Ford," who once served under him in the pit (UC-001-003).

## Plot Summary

The mines had long been thought spent: "No new seams had been discovered... even the smallest vein had been completely exhausted," so that "the discovery of a new seam of coal would be an important event" (UC-002-017, UC-002-018).

Yet Simon Ford has made just such a find. "It is not a seam that I have found," he tells Starr, but "positive proof that such a seam exists"; he had "discovered the presence of firedamp and consequently that of a new seam of coal in the Dochart pit" (UC-006-012, UC-006-014, UC-006-026).

A hidden menace shadows the work, for "some strange being prowled about in the pit" (UC-005-014). This proves to be old Silfax, whose "threats... were too serious to be disregarded," having "vindictively declared that Nell should never marry Simon's son" (UC-018-001, UC-018-003).

At the wedding of Nell and Harry, "Old Silfax stood there, prepared to fulfill his last dreadful threat--prepared to stop the marriage... by overwhelming the entire population... beneath the ruins of the coal mine" (UC-018-026). His owl seized "the lighted match" and "soared upwards" toward the firedamp, until Nell's cry of "Harfang!" turned the bird aside (UC-018-032, UC-018-034).

The peril past, "Harry and Nell were happy," and "Simon Ford, the ex-overman of New Aberfoyle," saw his family settled in the depths (UC-019-003, UC-019-004). After Silfax's death the owl Harfang haunts Loch Malcolm, where Nell had once been "carried off... from the deep abyss" (UC-019-008).

## Main Characters

- James Starr — the veteran engineer and former manager of the Aberfoyle mines
- Simon Ford — the old overman whose faith in a new seam reopens the pit
- Harry Ford — Simon's son, who rescues and weds Nell
- Nell — the girl raised in the depths, marked out by old Silfax
- Silfax — the vengeful hermit of the mine, master of the owl Harfang

## Key Places & Technology

- New Aberfoyle — the vast underground colliery opened beneath the old Dochart pit
- Loch Malcolm — the subterranean lake of the coal city, haunted by the owl Harfang

## Themes

Verne turns a coal mine into a world entire, a lit cavern-country peopled and inhabited beneath the Scottish hills. Against its wonder he sets the old miner Silfax, whose refusal to leave the exhausted depths curdles into vengeance, so that progress and obsolescence war underground.
