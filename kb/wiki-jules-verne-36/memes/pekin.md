# Pekin

## Overview

Pekin is the Chinese capital that forms the eastern terminus of the Grand Transasiatic railway in *The Adventures of a Special Correspondent*. It is the goal toward which the whole line of travellers is bound; the reporter Bombarnac frames his own assignment by it, resolving that "what I am doing here, I propose to do again in a fortnight at Pekin." (ASC-001-058)

## In the Narrative

The city is the destination that gathers the story's cast. The milliner Zinca Klork travels there for trade, "going to Pekin on account of the said firm, to open an office as a center for the collection of the Chinese hair crop," (ASC-004-035) her address given precisely as "Mademoiselle Zinca Klork, Avenue Cha-Coua, Pekin, Petchili, China." (ASC-004-016)

Every passenger is sorted by whether Pekin is their end. Ephrinell and the Englishwoman are marked as "going to Pekin," (ASC-004-005) as is "that Russian major who is sitting near the captain of the Astara." (ASC-004-029) The reporter counts the whole span of his ride "from Tiflis to Pekin." (ASC-004-058)

## Geography & Features

Pekin lies at the far Chinese end of the transcontinental line, weeks of rail beyond the Caspian. Bombarnac reckons his crossing will not be complete until he reaches it — "before we get to Pekin" (ASC-003-029) — measuring the immense distance the Grand Transasiatic spans.

He even imagines the human encounters the terminus promises, wondering whether he shall "exchange a sympathetic salute with her in the streets of Pekin." (ASC-002-067)
