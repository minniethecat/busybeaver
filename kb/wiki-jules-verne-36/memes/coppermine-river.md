## Overview

The Coppermine River is a large river of Arctic North America that flows from south to north into the Polar Sea, a landmark of the Hudson's Bay Company expedition in The Fur Country. After consulting his map, Lieutenant Hobson judged it best to descend the valley of the Coppermine, a large river which flows into Coronation Gulf. (FC-010-007)

The river's discovery was a feat of early Arctic exploration. The territory through which the Coppermine flows was unknown until the agents of the Hudson's Bay Company opened it. (FC-010-011) Samuel Hearne, after a struggle of nineteen months, discovered the Coppermine river on the 13th of July 1772, and followed its course to the mouth. (FC-010-019)

## In the Narrative

For Hobson's overland party, the Coppermine marked the route to the northern coast. He was advised to go down the Coppermine river, from the north-east of the lake, which would take him to the coast by the shortest route. (FC-008-041)

The country the little troop passed through was intersected by numerous streams, mostly tributaries of the two large rivers, the Mackenzie and Coppermine, which flow from south to north. (FC-007-004) Hobson knew he would have to penetrate further north, and that only part of his journey would be accomplished when he reached the mouth of the Coppermine. (FC-010-033)

## Geography & Features

The Coppermine empties into the Arctic Ocean at Coronation Gulf. Its valley widened sensibly as the travellers neared the Arctic Ocean. (FC-010-031) At the western angle of the gulf opened the mouth of the Coppermine, while to the east a long narrow creek called Bathurst Inlet ran into the mainland. (FC-011-002)

## Connections

The Coppermine belongs to the great net of Arctic rivers charted by the age's explorers. Hearne pushed towards the Polar Sea in 1770 to the discovery of the Coppermine River, one of a series of feats that included Franklin's coastal survey and Mackenzie's own great river. (FC-002-023)

The river remained a route for later expeditions. Dr. Richardson, at the age of seventy, went to Canada and ascended the Coppermine River as far as the Polar Sea. (ACH-014-023)
