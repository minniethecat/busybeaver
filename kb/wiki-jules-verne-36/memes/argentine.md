## Overview

The Argentine is the South American republic the searchers traverse in *In Search of the Castaways* — its soil, between the twenty-second and forty-second degrees, slopes eastward, so the travellers had only to follow the slope right down to the sea. (SC-016-001)

## In the Narrative

It is the land they race across toward the coast. There was quite a rivalry between Glenarvan and the young captain about getting first to the Argentine coast. (SC-010-063)

Its territory frames the long overland march: crossing Chili, the Cordilleras, the Pampas, and the Argentine plains took thirty days, giving the *Duncan* ample time to double Cape Horn and arrive on the opposite side. (SC-026-020)

## Geography & Features

Its interior is the great flat expanse of the Pampas — the Argentine Pampas extend from the thirty-fourth to the fortieth degree of southern latitude. (SC-017-001)

Its plains run east to the ocean: the sun's rays streamed over the Argentine plains and ran across to the Atlantic. (SC-014-004) Past a certain point the Argentine territory resumed its monotonous aspect. (SC-026-012)
