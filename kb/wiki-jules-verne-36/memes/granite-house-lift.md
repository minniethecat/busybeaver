## Overview

The Granite House Lift is a water-powered hoist built by [[Cyrus Harding]] to carry the colonists of [[lincoln-island|Lincoln Island]] up to their dwelling, cut high in a granite cliff face. It replaces the long rope ladder that had been their only access, and is one of the many comforts the engineer contrives from the island's natural resources in *[[the-mysterious-island|The Mysterious Island]]*. (MI-031-019)(MI-031-021)

## Design & Operation

When a companion objects that they have no machine to drive such a lift, Harding replies that he needs no steam engine — only "a water machine." (MI-031-018)(MI-031-019) The power source is the island's own stream:

> it was enough to augment the flow of the little stream which supplied the interior of Granite House with water (MI-031-020)

The mechanism itself is elegantly simple — a paddle wheel turned by falling water, geared to a cable drum:

> Below this fall the engineer fixed a cylinder with paddles, which was joined on the exterior with a strong cable rolled on a wheel, supporting a basket (MI-031-020)

The rising and falling basket serves as the car, drawn up as the water turns the paddles and the cable winds onto its wheel.

## Role in the Story

The lift first operated on the 17th of March, and gave the colony universal satisfaction, sparing them the toilsome climb of the ladder. (MI-031-021) Thereafter it becomes a fixture of daily life at [[granite-house|Granite House]], a mark of how far the castaways have raised themselves above bare survival. (MI-033-079)(MI-034-033)

## Science & Speculation

The lift is entirely plausible engineering: a water wheel driving a winding drum is nineteenth-century mill technology applied to vertical transport, and hydraulic and water-balance lifts were in real use in Verne's day. There is no speculative leap here — the interest lies in Verne's recurring thesis that a knowledgeable engineer, given only water, rock, and ingenuity, can reconstruct the conveniences of civilization from nothing.
