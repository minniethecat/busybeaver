## Overview

Australia is the second great landfall in the hunt for Captain Grant, taken up when the reading of the document is revised. Glenarvan is challenged to contend, with the document in his hand, that the shipwreck of the Britannia happened on the shores of Australia. (SC-024-010) On that reading the whole expedition turns south.

## In the Narrative

Paganel's geography settles the course. The 37th parallel cuts Australia by the province of Victoria — the very line the searchers have followed. (SC-023-096) A quibble over terms is brushed aside: the word continent can hardly be applied to Australia, which is only an island. (SC-024-034)

The decision is made to cross it. Glenarvan announced that the Duncan would sail immediately for Australia. (SC-027-041) Yet doubt shadows the choice — Australia now as evidently appears to be the theatre of the shipwreck of the Britannia as America did before. (SC-027-054)

The land is not empty: there are aborigines in Australia whose presence bears on the searchers' reading of the castaways' fate. (SC-024-018)

## Geography & Features

An island-continent of the southern seas, Australia is crossed by Glenarvan's party along the 37th parallel through the province of Victoria — a traverse from the eastern coast that forms the whole central movement of the search.
