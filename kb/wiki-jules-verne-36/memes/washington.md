## Overview

Washington is the capital of the United States and the seat of its government in Verne's American tales. It stands in the roll of the great towns—"New York, Boston, Albany, Washington, Richmond, New Orleans" (FEM-003-009)—that share in the delirium of the Gun Club's enterprise in *[[From the Earth to the Moon]]*.

It is the city to which every question of state is carried. In *[[The Blockade Runners]]* it is said that "all the ministers in Washington could not devise a better" (BR-008-011) plan, and in *Facing the Flag* an inventor addresses "the Secretary of the Navy at Washington" (FF-001-017), the capital holding the reins of the nation.

## In the Narrative

Washington is bound by rail to the cities of the Union. Verne notes "the four railroads of Ohio, Susquehanna, Philadelphia, and Washington, which converge at [[Baltimore]]" (FEM-003-008), the capital knit by iron to [[New York]] and the wider country.

The city is also the scene of the nation's great tragedy. In *[[The Mysterious Island]]* it is foretold that "a frightful crime would be committed in Washington, and that on Good Friday Abraham Lincoln would fall by the hand of a fanatic" (MI-011-086), the capital marked by the fall of the President.

## Geography & Features

Washington serves as the American prime meridian, the origin from which longitudes are reckoned. A position is fixed at "West long. 5° 7' by the Washington meridian" (FEM-013-047), and it is explained that "Washington is 77deg 3' 11"... counted from the meridian of Greenwich which the Americans take for their starting-point" (MI-014-068).

Its monuments give it a distinct and memorable aspect. Travellers are reminded of "the monuments at Washington" (FEM-015-003), the capital's public buildings standing as a byword for national grandeur across Verne's stories.

## Connections

Washington is the capital and seat of government of the United States in *[[From the Earth to the Moon]]*, the American prime meridian, a rail hub linked through [[Baltimore]] to [[New York]], and the city of Lincoln's fall in *[[The Mysterious Island]]*, ranked beside [[Richmond]] among the great towns of the Union.
