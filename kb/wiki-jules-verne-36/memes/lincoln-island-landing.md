# The Lincoln Island Landing

## Overview

The Lincoln Island Landing opens *[[the-mysterious-island|The Mysterious Island]]*, casting five fugitives from a besieged city onto an unknown shore. Their balloon is caught in a terrible storm from the northeast (MI-001-003), and the tempest hurls them across the ocean toward the land that will become their home.

## What Happens

While the storm rages over the sea, a drama not less exciting was being enacted in the agitated air (MI-001-004): a balloon carrying the castaways is swept helplessly along, losing gas and altitude. The aerial voyage had lasted five days (MI-002-002) before the wind finally drove the failing balloon toward a coast.

Those whom the hurricane had just thrown on this coast (MI-002-001) are flung into the sea near the shore and struggle onto dry land, at first missing their leader, who has vanished in the waves.

## Significance

The landing is the founding event of the whole novel: from this violent arrival springs the colonists' long labour of survival and the island's slowly unfolding mystery. That the aerial voyage had lasted five days (MI-002-002) measures how far chance has carried them from the war they fled.

Those whom the hurricane had just thrown on this coast (MI-002-001) at first miss their vanished leader, and the search for him sets in motion the story that follows.
