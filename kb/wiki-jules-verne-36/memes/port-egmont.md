# Port Egmont

## Overview

Port Egmont is the real Falkland Islands harbour where the schooner *Halbrane* fits out for her Antarctic voyage in *An Antarctic Mystery*. The narrator records the arrival: "on the 15th of October, our schooner cast anchor in Port Egmont, on the north of West Falkland." (AM-009-001)

It is the last civilised port before the polar seas, and the place where the crew is completed.

## In the Narrative

The stop is both provisioning and recruitment. Captain Len Guy declares "Port Egmont has proved capable of supplying us with everything we require." (AM-009-032) The venture stirs the settlement — "the news that the schooner was bound for the Antarctic seas had produced some sensation in the Falklands, at Port Egmont." (AM-009-043)

It is here the mysterious Hunt is enlisted; what is known of him "was confined to Hunt's conduct during his residence at Port Egmont." (AM-009-084) When questioned, he answers only "And you are at Port Egmont?" (AM-009-065)

## Geography & Features

The narrator uses the halt for study: "I should have ample time while we remained in port to explore Port Egmont and its surroundings, and to study the geology and mineralogy of the island." (AM-009-002)

His surveys note the wild life of the place — "one day, when the air was filled with a sound of braying, sufficient to deafen one, I asked an old sailor belonging to Port Egmont" about the din of penguins. (AM-009-022) Yet after several days he wonders "what had I found to observe in the neighbourhood of Port Egmont." (AM-009-020)
