# Loch Katrine

## Overview

Loch Katrine is the real loch in the county of Stirling, Scotland, that lies directly above the subterranean colliery of New Aberfoyle in *The Underground City*. The mining settlement is planted beneath it: "So was founded Coal Town, situated under the eastern point of Loch Katrine, to the north of the county of Stirling." (UC-010-009)

Harry Ford, mapping the underground world against the surface above, "knew that above this seam lay the Firth of Clyde, that there extended Loch Lomond and Loch Katrine." (UC-010-028)

## In the Narrative

The loch is a place of leisure for the miners who dwell below it. Approaching overland, the travellers pass Loch Arklet, where "a steep ascent led to the Inn of Stronachlacar, on the banks of Loch Katrine." (UC-015-018) Jack Ryan looks forward to seeing "that pretty girl on the waters of Loch Katrine, as those ugly ghosts on Loch Malcolm in the coal pit." (UC-015-021)

The loch's fate is bound to the workings beneath it. When it drains, "The cause of this accident was simply that Loch Katrine was all at once emptied, as though an enormous fissure had opened in its bed." (UC-015-029) So little remains that the authorities "erase Loch Katrine from the map of Scotland until (by public subscription) it could be refilled." (UC-016-015)

## Geography & Features

Verne gives the loch precise dimensions: "Loch Katrine is only ten miles in length; its width never exceeds two miles." (UC-015-019) Its shores form a celebrated view, extending "westward like a picture framed between Ben An and Ben Venue." (UC-015-022)

The engineer suspects a connection to the mine even before the disaster, granting "it is not impossible that we should be under Loch Katrine." (UC-008-036) The flooded hollows of the mine are measured against it, forming "vast ponds, or rather lakes larger than Loch Katrine, lying just above them." (UC-007-036)
