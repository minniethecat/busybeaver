# Destruction of Lincoln Island

## Overview

The destruction of Lincoln Island is the cataclysm that ends the colonists' island home—the reawakened volcano of Mount Franklin erupts, floods the island with lava, and finally blows the whole land into the Pacific, leaving only a bare rock, an event chronicled in *[[The Mysterious Island]]*.

The eruption was foretold. Cyrus Harding warned that the volcanic substances were no longer merely seething but had caught fire, and the island was undoubtedly menaced by an approaching eruption (SI-018-023).

## What Happens

The crater burst into flame. A great bouquet of fire shot from the crater, casting luminous sheets in every direction, accompanied by successive explosions like the discharge of a battery of mitrailleuses (SI-018-044).

Lava overran the colony. A torrent of molten lava poured over the granite wall and destroyed the mill, the inner-court buildings, and the stables—a last, terrible blow to all the colonists had built (SI-019-077).

The settlers took to the ship. Driven to their last entrenchment, the colonists resolved to shelter aboard their unfinished vessel, though its upper seams were not yet caulked (SI-019-078).

The island was blown apart. The wall of Dakkar Grotto gave way, the sea rushed in, and an explosion heard a hundred miles off shook the air—fragments of mountains fell into the Pacific and the ocean rolled over where Lincoln Island once stood (SI-019-080).

## Significance

Only a rock remained. An isolated rock thirty feet long and twenty broad was the sole solid point left above the water where the great island had been (SI-020-002).

Everything else was swallowed. All around had vanished into the abyss—Mount Franklin rent asunder—and this narrow rock was all that could now be seen of Lincoln Island (SI-020-003).

The survivors faced death. On the barren rock the six colonists and their dog had existed for nine days, their vessel shattered, without fire or escape, and it seemed they must inevitably perish (SI-020-007).

## Participants & Setting

- The catastrophe destroys Lincoln Island in the South Pacific, home to the balloon castaways for four years, following the reawakening of the Mount Franklin volcano.
- The lava first ruins Granite House and the plateau of Prospect Heights before the final explosion, triggered when the sea breaks into the volcanic shaft through Dakkar Grotto.
- Cyrus Harding, Gideon Spilett, Pencroft, Herbert, Neb, and Ayrton survive only by clinging to the last rock, later rescued by the *Duncan*.
