# The Tjon Viaduct Sacrifice

## Overview

The Tjon viaduct sacrifice is the closing catastrophe of *[[The Adventures of a Special Correspondent]]*, in which the stowaway Kinko blows up the locomotive to halt a train that the manager Faruskiar has switched toward an unfinished viaduct, dying to save a hundred passengers while Faruskiar is unmasked as a bandit chief (ASC-025-004) (ASC-025-044).

## What Happens

The Grand Transasiatic train had been switched onto the Nanking branch, running at tremendous speed toward the valley of the Tjon, which was spanned only by an unfinished viaduct three hundred and fifty to four hundred feet long; had the train been hurled to the bottom of that chasm, a hundred victims would have perished (ASC-025-003).

The scheme was Faruskiar's: utilizing the branch line to the unfinished viaduct, an accomplice at the points switched off the train, whereupon he gained the foot-plate, killed the driver and stoker, and left the steam on full so the cars would work up to full speed (ASC-025-009).

The bandits then hastened down into the Tjon valley, expecting to find amid the wreck the fifteen millions of gold and precious stones and carry off the treasure under cover of night (ASC-025-010).

The train was saved because the boiler exploded at the top and on the side before the verge of the abyss; the wheels being undamaged, the engine ran on and came gradually to a standstill, sparing the passengers a violent shock (ASC-025-015). Of the boiler only shapeless fragments remained—torn plates, twisted tubes, split cylinders (ASC-025-016).

Kinko had paid with his life for the safety of his fellow passengers (ASC-025-004); his coffer in the luggage van was found empty, and he had been the victim of his own sacrifice (ASC-025-006). The true hero had been this humble Roumanian, not Faruskiar—the abominable bandit hidden beneath the skin of a manager (ASC-025-007).

## Significance

When Major Noltitz interrupted the passengers' debate to ask "Where is Faruskiar?", the manager of the Transasiatic could not be found (ASC-025-033) (ASC-025-034), and though they called his name a second time he made no response (ASC-025-040).

Noltitz declared that the rascal who had sent them onto the Nanking line to hurl them into the Tjon valley and walk off with the imperial treasure was Faruskiar (ASC-025-044). The narrator Bombarnac then confirmed it, telling how he had overheard the plot too late to stop it, yet keeping silent about Kinko's intervention (ASC-025-048) (ASC-025-049).

The travellers had to give in to the evidence: Faruskiar, the superb Mongol functionary they had admired, was the most consummate bandit who had ever disgraced Central Asia (ASC-025-052). That the locomotive should explode just on the verge of the abyss seemed miraculous—for Bombarnac alone knew Kinko's secret (ASC-025-056).

## Participants & Setting

- Kinko, the young Roumanian stowaway who blew up the boiler to halt the runaway train and perished in the explosion, the humble hero whose sacrifice saved a hundred lives (ASC-025-004) (ASC-025-007).

- Faruskiar, manager of the Grand Transasiatic and secret bandit chief, who contrived the switch and murder of the crew to plunder the imperial treasure, then vanished before being unmasked (ASC-025-009) (ASC-025-040).

- Major Noltitz, whose suspicions had hardened into certainty and who first demanded to know Faruskiar's whereabouts and named him the author of the crime (ASC-025-033) (ASC-025-044).

- Claudius Bombarnac, the reporter-narrator who had once telegraphed Faruskiar as a hero, and who now confirmed his guilt while guarding Kinko's secret (ASC-025-048) (ASC-025-056).
