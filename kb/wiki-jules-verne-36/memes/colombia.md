## Overview

Colombia is the northern South American republic that disputes the Amazon's paternity in *Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon* — Peru, Ecuador, and Colombia have for years disputed the honor of its glorious paternity. (EHLA-005-019)

## In the Narrative

The great river threads its territory. The Amazon is called the Marañon in its journey through Colombia and Peru up to the Brazilian frontier. (EHLA-005-022)

Its coinage circulates in the story: a purse held ten double-condors in gold of the United States of Colombia, worth about a hundred francs. (EHLA-001-019)

## Geography & Features

Its coasts mark the edge of the known — a captain is asked whether he has explored the coasts of Colombia, those of Chili, or of Patagonia. (DSCF-016-029) Its shore closes the Gulf: continuing in that direction, one should reach the shore of Venezuela or Colombia. (MW-017-049)

Its caverns drew science — the grotto of Guachara, in Colombia, was visited by Humboldt, who investigated it to a depth of 2,500 feet. (JCE-030-013)
