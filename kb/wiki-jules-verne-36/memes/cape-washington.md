# Cape Washington

## Overview

Cape Washington is a promontory on the coast of New America, the far-northern land discovered in *The Adventures of Captain Hatteras*. It is christened by the American Altamont, who claims the naming as his prerogative: "Nothing whatever, provided that yonder cape is called Cape Washington." (ACH-039-059)

## In the Narrative

The cape fixes the travellers' path across the New American coast. Altamont lays out the route: "In our future excursions, then, we shall go by Cape Washington to Victoria Bay, and from thence to Fort Providence, where we shall find food and rest at Doctor's House!" (ACH-039-075) On a hunting march "the hunters set off in the direction of Cape Washington, and the hard snow so favoured their march, that in three hours they had gone fifteen miles." (ACH-040-033)

## Geography & Features

The cape guards the approach to Victoria Bay by sea as well as by land. When the ice loosened, the crew "determined to take her a trial trip to Cape Washington." (ACH-050-005) Sailing north, "after following the windings of the bay and going past Cape Washington, they struck into the direct route for the north, and by seven o'clock had lost sight of the lighthouse and Fort Providence." (ACH-051-003)
