## Overview

Persia is the storied land skirted by the Grand Transasiatic Railway in *The Adventures of a Special Correspondent*. Its border is so near that the traveler wonders — am I in Persia or in Russia? (ASC-003-013)

## In the Narrative

The railway's geography keeps Persia in view. The southern portion of Turkestan and the oasis of Akhal Tekke is contiguous to Persia (ASC-007-019), and a branch line unites the route with Teheran in Persia. (ASC-008-028)

The novel's great secret rides the rails from Persia — the imperial treasure, value fifteen millions, sent from Persia to China (ASC-017-057), composed of gold and precious stones formerly deposited in the hands of the Shah of Persia. (ASC-018-005)

## Geography & Features

It is a crossroads of trade — the bazaars hold the thousand products of Persia, China, Turkey, Siberia, and Mongolia (ASC-001-043), and merchants from Persia, Turkey, India, Turkestan, and China fill the markets. (MS-007-023)

It commands a wide province: Asia to the east of the Caspian Sea, Persia and the provinces to the north of it. (ASC-012-053) Its Shah is a byword for wealth — the explorer Tavernier sold a great pearl to the Shah of Persia for 3,000,000 francs. (TTLU-011-036)
