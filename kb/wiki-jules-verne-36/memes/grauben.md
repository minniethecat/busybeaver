# Gräuben

## Overview

She is the god-daughter of Professor Lidenbrock, introduced as "a young Virlandaise of seventeen" who shares his roof (JCE-001-032).

She is "a lovely blue-eyed blonde, rather given to gravity and seriousness," yet loves Axel very sincerely (JCE-003-040).

## Role in the Story

Far from dissuading her betrothed, she meets his fears with courage, so that he marvels "what, Gräuben, won't you dissuade me from such an undertaking?" (JCE-007-021). On the eve of departure her guardian is "solemnly investing Gräuben with the reins of government" of the house (JCE-007-074).

The lovers walk "hand in hand, but in silence" to the quay (JCE-007-029), and she remains Axel's lodestar through the descent, though "Gräuben was far away; and I never hoped to see her again" (JCE-008-034).

In the depths his memory returns to "my poor Gräuben" (JCE-027-007), and her name is set upon the subterranean map, for he begs "let it be called Port Gräuben; it will look very well upon the map" (JCE-032-005).

## Character & Traits

She is an accomplished woman of science, for "Mademoiselle Gräuben was an accomplished mineralogist; she could have taught a few things to a savant" (JCE-003-041).

She is the object of Axel's devotion, who cries "I love you well, my own dear Gräuben!" (JCE-003-056), and the hope that sustains him, as he rejoices "at the thought of meeting my little Gräuben again" (JCE-019-026).

## Relationships

- [[Axel]] — her betrothed, whom she encourages rather than restrains and to whom she is at last wed, for "I was to be married to Gräuben that day" (JCE-036-009).
- [[Professor Lidenbrock]] — her guardian, whose "god-daughter Gräuben" she is, a young Virlandaise beneath his roof (JCE-001-032).

## Appearances

- [[A Journey to the Center of the Earth]] — the voyage she blesses and awaits, greeting the returned travellers where none could describe "the joy of Gräuben" (JCE-045-005).
