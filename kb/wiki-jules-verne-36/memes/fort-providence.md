# Fort Providence

## Overview

Fort Providence is the fictional Arctic base raised by Captain Hatteras's party in *The Adventures of Captain Hatteras*. The name is chosen by acclaim — "Let our little fort be called Fort Providence." (ACH-039-073) The boatswain approves: "'Fort Providence,' added Johnson, 'sounds well too.'" (ACH-039-075)

It becomes the fixed point of every excursion, "where we shall find food and rest at Doctor's House!" (ACH-039-075)

## In the Narrative

The fort anchors the colonists' survival. After each foray "it was time to think of returning to Fort Providence." (ACH-040-061) When bears lay siege, the doctor deduces their aim: "Evidently the enemy was bent on the siege of Fort Providence." (ACH-043-041)

Departure is heavy with feeling — "The dwellers on Fort Providence could not see the last day dawn without some emotion." (ACH-050-021) On returning from the sea voyage, "the navigators had almost come to the end of their supplies, and had been reckoning on replenishing their stores at Fort Providence." (ACH-057-019)

## Geography & Features

The fort commands a bay teeming with game — "the hunters never went far from Fort Providence, for game was so plentiful that it seemed waiting their guns." (ACH-046-013) Traces of animals ring it closely, "within a circle of two miles round Fort Providence." (ACH-043-008)

It sits along a marked route home, reached "by Cape Washington to Victoria Bay, and from thence to Fort Providence." (ACH-039-075) A lighthouse stands beside it, lost to view only when the party has "gone past Cape Washington." (ACH-051-003)
