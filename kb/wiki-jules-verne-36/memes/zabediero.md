## Overview

Zabediero is the little Siberian village outside Tomsk where the Tartar convoy halts in Michael Strogoff. At nightfall of the 15th of August, the convoy reached the little village of Zabediero, thirty versts from Tomsk. (MS-022-011)

## In the Narrative

It becomes Ivan Ogareff's forward camp. Ivan Ogareff left the Emir at Tomsk, where both had arrived the evening before, and returned to the camp at Zabediero. (MS-022-015) Boats, requisitioned at Zabediero, were brought up to the Tom and formed a line of obstacles impossible to pass. (MS-022-012)

The camp is the scene of Strogoff's capture and recognition. It was beyond doubt that Marfa Strogoff's son, the Czar's courier, was at this moment in Zabediero, among Ivan Ogareff's prisoners. (MS-022-028) There Michael laid open Ivan Ogareff's face with the knout. (MS-029-035)

Then, taking command of the troops at Zabediero, Ogareff marched towards the town where the Emir awaited him. (MS-022-099)

## Geography & Features

A little village thirty versts from Tomsk on the river Tom, Zabediero serves the novel as the Tartar army's staging camp — the last halt from which, after leaving Zabediero, prisoners and troops proceeded rapidly to Tomsk. (MS-023-009)
