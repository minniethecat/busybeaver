## Overview

Frankfort-on-the-Main is the German fair-city from which the narrator makes his fateful balloon ascent. In the month of September he arrived at Frankfort-on-the-Maine. (DA-001-001) The day of the ascent was that of the great September fair, which attracts so many people to Frankfort. (DA-001-003)

## In the Narrative

No sooner had the ascent been announced than the city pressed to join it. Scarcely had the news spread through Frankfort than three of the principal citizens begged to ascend. (DA-001-002) Soon the city fell away below, and Frankfort had already passed from beneath the car. (DA-001-061) They were now six leagues from Frankfort, the aeronaut judged. (DA-001-082)

## Geography & Features

Frankfort is a Rhineland city of German custom and commerce. Forty minutes from Frankfort, heavy clouds came against the wind from the south. (DA-001-091) The magnetic needle pointed back toward Frankfort, flying beneath the balloon. (VB-001-058) There the German baron eats as in some bier-halle at Munich or Frankfort. (ASC-004-047)

## Connections

Frankfort is the launching-place of the aeronaut's ordeal in [[A Drama in the Air]]. (DA-001-001) Its September fair gathers the crowd that witnesses the ascent. (DA-001-003)
