# Davis Strait

## Overview

Davis Strait — written "Davis's Straits" in *The Adventures of Captain Hatteras* — is the real Arctic channel between Greenland and Baffin Island that the brig *Forward* must cross to gain the polar seas beyond. The ship's orders are to "cross Davis's Straits, and sail up Baffin's Sea to Melville Bay." (ACH-004-043)

It is the threshold of the whole northern enterprise.

## In the Narrative

The doctor urges the crew to profit by the thaw "to cross Davis's Straits," (ACH-004-021) reckoning that "like him, we shall find ourselves in Davis's Straits in April, and if we succeed in passing the ice our voyage will be considerably advanced." (ACH-005-014)

The passage is a hard one, its waters working against the ship — Johnson warns "we are making very little way, and the currents of Davis's Straits will soon be against us." (ACH-005-063)

## Geography & Features

The strait forms the southern approach to Baffin Bay, its ice keeping to the Greenland side. A drift-floe seized by the brig "quickly seized by the boreal current came by Davis's Straits" out of the sea above. (ACH-006-020)

It is a recognized measure of the North-West passage: McClure's lieutenant Creswell "was the first who had the honour of rounding the American continent from Behring's Straits to Davis's Straits." (ACH-001-048) Many an adventurer has tried "to cross the sea from Davis's Straits to Behring's Straits." (ACH-003-011)
