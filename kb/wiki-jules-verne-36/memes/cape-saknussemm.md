# Cape Saknussemm

## Overview

Cape Saknussemm is the headland on the shore of the underground sea in *A Journey to the Center of the Earth*, named by Professor Lidenbrock for the old Icelandic alchemist whose trail the explorers follow. Discovering the point, the professor decrees: "for ever henceforth let this cape that advances into the sea discovered by yourself be known by your own illustrious name — Cape Saknussemm." (JCE-040-004)

## In the Narrative

The cape marks a bitter reversal in the subterranean crossing. Axel reflects: "it is providential that whilst supposing we were steering south we should have just got back north at Cape Saknussemm." (JCE-040-013) Believing they had run south across the sea, the travellers find themselves returned to the very shore they left.

From the cape they set out once more: "we took our places, and with our sail set, Hans steered us along the coast to Cape Saknussemm." (JCE-040-019)

## Geography & Features

The cape is the fixed reference from which the explorers reckon their drift across the Central Sea. "From Cape Saknussemm we had been carried due north for hundreds of leagues," (JCE-043-026) Axel records of the storm that seized their raft.

It is also the site of a strange discovery about the deep interior: it was "after our arrival at Cape Saknussemm the north pole of the needle of this confounded compass began to point south instead of north." (JCE-045-029)
