## Overview

The Alps are the great mountain wall of central Europe, crowned by Mont Blanc, the giant of the range. From the Brevent, opposite the Bossons glacier, one may watch the parties that undertake the ascent of the giant of the Alps through nearly their whole climb. (AMB-001-010) On the left rise the Pennine Alps and the Oberland, crowned with snow, raising their magnificent crests. (AMB-001-094)

## In the Narrative

The Alps are Verne's yardstick for every other height. The mountains of the Moon, he wrote, are not very high; the Alps or Himalayas would look down upon them with disdain. (FEM-024-021) Their crimson snows puzzled chemists: the same red substance found in the heart of the Alps in Switzerland proved to come from certain corpuscles. (ACH-051-037)

## Geography & Features

The chain sweeps from the Mediterranean coast up across the continent. Antibes overlooks a panorama embracing the Maritime Alps and reaching beyond Monaco to the Italian heights. (OC-017-029) To lose no time, the Albatross flew over the face of Provence and crossed the whole chain of the Alps or the Pyrenees at a stroke. (RC-014-044)

## Connections

The Alps rise in the heart of [[Switzerland]], their red snows a marvel to its chemists. (ACH-051-037) Their summit, Mont Blanc, is the subject of [[Ascent of Mont Blanc]]. (AMB-001-010)
