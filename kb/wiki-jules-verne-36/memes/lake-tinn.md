# Lake Tinn

## Overview

Lake Tinn is the real Telemark lake that threads through *Ticket No. 9672*, its waters and lakeside hamlets forming the stage on which the Hansen family's fortunes turn. The boatman Joel's daily work runs along it: "Joel went to take a traveler to Lake Tinn, and as he didn't start until very late, I do not think he can get back to Dal before to-morrow." (TN-001-008)

The lake anchors the whole Vesfjorddal country the story inhabits.

## In the Narrative

From the inn at Dal the lake is the gateway to the surrounding region: travellers could "wander over the entire Telemark and Hardanger region, explore the valley of Vesfjorddal between Lakes Mjos and Tinn, and visit the wonderful cataracts of the Rjukan Tun." (TN-002-008)

Its lakeside town of Moel is the pivot of the family's errands. Pastor Andersen is fetched for the betrothal because "Moel was not far off, only about eight miles distant, at the end of Lake Tinn." (TN-004-059) Later "the next day they went to Moel and Lake Tinn," (TN-011-082) and the reaching of Moel is timed to the hour: "it took them only about two hours and a half to reach Moel, which is situated at the end of Lake Tinn." (TN-016-006)

## Geography & Features

The lake sits below the great fall of the Rjukan. The land "makes an abrupt descent of nine hundred feet to the bed of the Maan midway between Lake Mjos and Lake Tinn, nine hundred feet, that is to say six times the height of Niagara." (TN-008-016)

From the heights the whole basin unfolds: "the winding valley of Vesfjorddal between Lakes Tinn and Mjos, Dal, and its miniature houses, and the bright waters of the Maan." (TN-010-077) At the lake's far end the river resumes its rush: "Tinoset is situated near the end of Lake Tinn, and here the Maan plunges majestically into the valley below." (TN-016-035)
