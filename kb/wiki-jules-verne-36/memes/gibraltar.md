## Overview

Gibraltar is the fortress rock at the western mouth of the Mediterranean, and in Off on a Comet one of the few fragments of Earth swept away when the comet Gallia grazes the planet. The travellers' hypothesis is that a fragment comprising the Mediterranean and its shores from Gibraltar to Malta has been developed into a new asteroid. (OC-016-012)

## In the Narrative

The severing of the Rock from the world is measured by silence: the total cessation of all telegraphic communication between England's shores and Gibraltar was a virtual proof that England was beyond the comet's compass. (OC-016-020)

Reaching the surviving crag, the explorers are told plainly by its garrison, "You are at Gibraltar." (OC-015-076) They do not linger: glad to be on board again to resume their voyage of investigation, in two hours they were out of sight of the sole remaining fragment of Gibraltar. (OC-015-083)

Their reckoning traces the distance they had positively sailed from Gourbi Island before their progress was arrested. (OC-016-002)

## Geography & Features

Commanding the strait between the Mediterranean and the Atlantic, Gibraltar survives on Gallia as an isolated rock, its English garrison clinging to a scrap of the vanished continent.
