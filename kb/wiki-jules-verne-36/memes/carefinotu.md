# Carefinotu

## Overview

Carefinotu enters the castaways' lives as an apparent savage whose very right to his name is questioned, "Is he to be allowed to call himself Carefinotu?" (GM-018-007).

He seems to belong to the lowest order of savages, for on his arrival "fire itself seemed to be unknown to him" (GM-018-036).

## Role in the Story

He attaches himself to Godfrey as a devoted companion, and "during the fortnight which followed his arrival on the island, Carefinotu many times accompanied Godfrey on his shooting excursions" (GM-018-051).

By night he makes himself the sentinel of the dwelling, and though it endangered his life, "he had understood that he could thus be of service to his liberators" as the self-appointed guardian of Will Tree (GM-019-012).

## Character & Traits

He is drawn above all to the white men's power, and "what particularly attracted the attention of Carefinotu were the weapons carried by Godfrey and Tartlet" (GM-018-025).

He proves unteachable in one respect only, showing "refractoriness" in learning the English language (GM-018-052).

His courage is past doubt when, hunting-knife in hand, he is seen "struggling with the tiger, holding him by the throat, and at last stabbing him to the heart with a powerful blow" (GM-019-059).

## Relationships

- Godfrey — the young master he serves and shields; when a serpent glides near his feet, "Carefinotu threw himself between Godfrey and the reptile" (GM-020-081), and after Godfrey falls ill "it was to Carefinotu that he mainly owed his return to health" (GM-021-008).
- [[Tartlet]] — the timid dancing-master he hauls out of danger, for at the moment of alarm "Carefinotu rushed towards Tartlet and lifted him up" (GM-020-094).

## Appearances

- [[Godfrey Morgan]] — the tale in which his true identity is disclosed: he keeps up the savage's guise until the climax, when to everyone's shock "Carefinotu had just pronounced these words, and in that English language which up to then he had seemed unable to speak" (GM-021-106), whereupon Uncle Will reveals that "Carefinotu was my faithful Jup Brass, who played his part of Friday marvellously well" (GM-022-030). During the final siege he had taken a post "whence he could deliver a cross fire" (GM-021-085).
