# Sangarre

## Overview

Sangarre is a Tsigane woman in the gypsy band that fronts for the invasion — "Near him was the Tsigane, Sangarre, a woman about thirty years old" (MS-009-009). She and her people serve Ivan Ogareff as hired eyes: "Sangarre and her Zingari, well paid spies, were absolutely devoted to him" (MS-015-050).

## Role in the Story

From the first she conspires in the coded talk Michael overhears — "'You are right, Sangarre!'" (MS-006-024) — and marks him at once, "regarding him with a peculiar gaze, as if to fix his features indelibly in her memory" (MS-009-012). Set to shadow his mother in the Tartar camp, "Sangarre had to wait, and she waited, without losing sight of her whom she was watching" (MS-021-011).

Her patience pays: a private scene "had been observed by Sangarre, Ogareff's spy" (MS-022-026), and pressed by her master she holds firm — "'I am not mistaken, Ivan,' she said" (MS-022-041).

At the crisis "Sangarre advanced towards the group, in the midst of which stood Marfa" (MS-022-051), and with a single cruel word she springs the trap: "Sangarre, close to him, said one word, 'The knout!'" (MS-022-079).

## Character & Traits

She is obedience made flesh — "Whatever order he might give her, Sangarre would execute it" (MS-021-009) — a rootless creature Verne calls "Confidante and accomplice, Sangarre, without country, without family" (MS-021-010).

Her loyalty is a debt repaid, for "once Sangarre, being implicated in a very serious affair, had been saved by the Russian officer" (MS-021-008). Beneath the calm burns malice: "A strange gleam shot from Sangarre's great black eyes" (MS-021-023).

## Relationships

- [[Ivan Ogareff]] — the Russian traitor she serves as confidante and spy, to whom she owes her life (MS-021-008).

- [[Michel Strogoff]] — the courier whose features she fixes in memory and helps to unmask (MS-009-012).
