# Polar Sea

## Overview

The Polar Sea is the name Verne gives to the Arctic Ocean bordering the northern coast of America in *The Fur Country*. It is the goal of the Hudson's Bay Company expedition and, ultimately, the treacherous water on which the fort is carried away. Lieutenant Hobson hopes "to find such specimens of the Arctic Flora even on the verge of the Polar Sea; for these trees would supply him with wood to build his fort." (FC-007-031)

## In the Narrative

The sea is a proving ground for explorers, from "Hearne, towards the Polar Sea, in 1770, to the discovery of the Coppermine River" (FC-002-023) to M'Clure, "reserved to M'Clure in our own day to pass from one ocean to the other across the Polar Sea." (FC-010-021) Captain Craventy tells Black he would himself escort the naturalist "to the shores of the Polar Sea." (FC-003-061)

Hobson's men are chosen to endure "the fatigues of an expedition to the Polar Sea." (FC-004-063) Word reaches him that Europeans "had been seen on the confines of the Polar Sea, and that it was open at this time of year." (FC-008-041)

## Geography & Features

The catastrophe of the novel is that Fort Hope, "founded by Lieutenant Hobson on the borders of the Polar Sea, had drifted!" (FC-024-002) The castaways study "an excellent map of the oceanic and atmospheric currents" of "that part of the Polar Sea between Cape Bathurst and Behring Strait." (FC-025-023) As the thaw comes, "the chain of icebergs, drifted along by the currents of the Polar Sea, gradually drew back and became lost in the fogs." (FC-022-036)
