# The Return of Ole Kamp

## Overview

The return of Ole Kamp is the closing event of *[[Ticket No. 9672]]*, in which the mate of the *Viking*—believed drowned—is revealed alive before all Christiania, the crowd learning that he survived the shipwreck and won the lottery's grand prize, before Sylvius Hogg recounts his rescue and the reunited lovers leave for their wedding at Dal (TN-020-003) (TN-020-006).

## What Happens

Yes; it was Ole Kamp, who by a miracle had survived the wreck of the *Viking*, already on his way to Christiania aboard the vessel that had rescued him (TN-020-001). This was what Sylvius Hogg told and repeated to all who would listen, and the good news flew from group to group through the crowd (TN-020-002).

In a few moments all Christiania knew that the young mate of the *Viking* had returned and had won the grand prize of the Schools Lottery (TN-020-003). Joel nearly smothered Ole in his embrace while Hulda was regaining consciousness (TN-020-004).

"Yes, it is I—your betrothed—soon to be your husband!" said Ole (TN-020-005). "Very soon, my children!" exclaimed Sylvius Hogg; "we will leave this very evening for Dal," where a professor of law and member of the Storthing would be seen dancing at the wedding (TN-020-006).

## Significance

Sylvius Hogg had learned Ole's history only through the last letter the Naval Department had sent him at Dal, which enclosed a second letter from Christiansand: the Danish brig *Genius* had reached that port with several survivors of the *Viking*, among them the young mate (TN-020-007).

Because the shipwrecked men were still very weak, Sylvius had asked for the most profound secrecy about the return, and the facts had been kept from the public (TN-020-008).

On the nights of the third and fourth of May the disabled *Viking* had collided with a drifting iceberg and gone to pieces five minutes afterward (TN-020-010). It was then that Ole penned his farewell to his betrothed, sealed it in a bottle, and cast it into the sea (TN-020-011).

Ole and four comrades leapt upon the iceberg as the vessel went down and were cast, exhausted, upon the barren southern coast of Greenland (TN-020-012). The brig *Genius*, driven from her course, passed by; the survivors' signals were seen, and the men were saved (TN-020-014). Delayed by head-winds, the *Genius* did not reach Christiansand until the 12th of July (TN-020-015).

## Participants & Setting

- Ole Kamp, the mate of the *Viking* who survived the shipwreck, was rescued by the *Genius*, and returned to claim both his betrothed and the lottery prize (TN-020-001) (TN-020-005).

- Hulda Hansen, Ole's betrothed, who faints and revives to find him alive and is at last married to him at Dal (TN-020-004).

- Professor Sylvius Hogg, who alone knew of Ole's rescue, kept it secret, and announced both the return and the coming wedding to the crowd (TN-020-002) (TN-020-007).

- Joel, Hulda's brother, who smothered the returned sailor in his embrace; the reunited party left Christiania that same evening for the wedding at Dal, where a fortune of one hundred thousand marks now dowered the Hansen family and ticket 9672 drew the grand prize (TN-020-020) (TN-020-022) (TN-020-024) (TN-020-025).
