# Omsk

## Overview

Omsk is the Western Siberian city that is Michael Strogoff's own home town in *Michael Strogoff*. It is the seat of imperial power on the frontier: "Omsk is the center of that military organization of Western Siberia which is intended to overawe the Kirghiz population." (MS-003-061) For the courier the name carries a private weight, for it is where his mother still lives.

## In the Narrative

The city is bound to Strogoff's family. "His father--old Peter Strogoff, dead ten years since--inhabited the town of Omsk, situated in the government of the same name; and his mother, Marfa Strogoff, lived there still." (MS-004-035) The peril of his mission is sharpened by the question put to him — "Wilt thou pass through Omsk?" (MS-004-067) — since to be recognised there would betray him.

It stands among the first governments swept by the revolt. When the crisis breaks the Czar asks whether "the governments of Yeniseisk, Omsk, Semipolatinsk, and Tobolsk--are we still in direct communication with them as before the insurrection?" (MS-002-021)

## Geography & Features

Omsk lies on the invasion's path across the western steppe. The Tartar advance reaches toward it, for "the greater horde, occupying the countries situated to the east of the middle one, extends as far as the governments of Omsk and Tobolsk." (MS-003-058)

As a military hub it is a natural node in the empire's chain of telegraphic stations, listed among the towns to which the courier's description is dispatched — "Tioumen, Ishim, Omsk, Tomsk, and to all the telegraphic stations with which communication is yet open." (MS-002-025)
