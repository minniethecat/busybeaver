## Overview

Kamtschatka is the far-eastern peninsula of the Russian empire, reached across the Behring Sea. Robur's aircraft crossed the twelve hundred miles between the first of the Aleutians and the extreme end of Kamtschatka in a single day and night. (RC-011-047) Sweeping over the peninsula, it caught a glimpse of Petropaulovski and the volcano of Kloutschew. (RC-011-049)

## In the Narrative

Kamtschatka stands in Verne's pages as a byword for the utmost remoteness of Asia. Of Oriental Turkestan, it was said, a traveller newly come to Europe by Suez knew no more than he did of Kamtschatka. (ASC-017-008)

## Geography & Features

Administratively the peninsula is one of the districts of Asiatic Russia. Siberia, divided into the governments of Tobolsk, Yeniseisk, Irkutsk, Omsk and Yakutsk, contains the two districts of Okhotsk and Kamtschatka. (MS-003-002) It was in journeys across this difficult country that a hardy young Siberian first distinguished himself, as he had earlier in the Caucasus. (MS-004-039)

## Connections

Kamtschatka is the easternmost march of [[Russia]] and of the wider expanse of [[Siberia]]. (MS-003-002) Its hardy couriers are cut from the same cloth as those who braved the [[Caucasus]]. (MS-004-039)
