# Twofold Bay

## Overview

Twofold Bay is the real Australian inlet on the coast of New South Wales that serves as the eastern goal of the overland search in *In Search of the Castaways*. It lies on the line the searchers follow across the continent: "the 37th parallel touches this continent at Cape Bernouilli, and leaves it at Twofold Bay." (SC-027-067)

Reaching it means completing the traverse of Australia, after which "we cross an arm of the sea which extends to New Zealand." (SC-027-070)

## In the Narrative

The bay becomes the party's rendezvous with their yacht, the *Duncan*. When the ship must go to Melbourne, Paganel resolves, "we will go without her to Twofold Bay." (SC-033-069)

John Mangles reasons "that orders would reach the DUNCAN more easily from Twofold Bay, than if a message was sent two hundred miles over a wild country" (SC-041-037), and "it was decided that they should wait till they came to Twofold Bay." (SC-041-038)

The final stretch is measured against it: "in fifteen days more, should their journey continue to be prosperous, the little party would reach Twofold Bay." (SC-039-001) The travellers ask "could they go over the one hundred and twenty miles which lay between them and Twofold Bay?" (SC-041-051)

## Geography & Features

The bay lies at the terminus of the 37th parallel on Australia's eastern seaboard, its approaches guarded by dangerous reefs — it is off "the dangerous reefs of Twofold Bay" that the *Britannia* is supposed lost. (SC-033-043) The exact longitude of the bay becomes a matter of anxious calculation for Paganel. (SC-042-043)

Between the searchers and the bay runs the Snowy River: "the courageous ladies did not shrink, if necessary, from walking the whole distance between the Snowy River and Twofold Bay." (SC-042-048) In the end a messenger is sent ahead, and the *Duncan*'s officer is ordered "to repair without delay to Twofold Bay." (SC-042-097)
