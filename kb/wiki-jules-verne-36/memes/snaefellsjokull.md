## Overview

Snæfellsjökull — called Sneffels in the novel — is the real extinct glacier-volcano on [[iceland|Iceland]]'s western peninsula that serves as the entrance to the underworld in *[[journey-to-the-center-of-the-earth|Journey to the Center of the Earth]]*. Alone among the world's mountains, its crater is named in the cipher of Arne Saknussemm as the way down. (JCE-005-050)

## In the Narrative

The whole expedition of [[Professor Lidenbrock]] hinges on decoding a runic message that points to this one peak. The Latin cipher directs the traveller to the crater of Sneffels that the shadow of Scartaris caresses in the last days of June:

> to the crater of the jokul of Sneffels, which the shadow of Scartaris... (JCE-005-050)

The condition is precise and time-bound — only when Scartaris' shadow touches the right crater may the descent begin — which is why the party must hurry to reach [[iceland|Iceland]] before the season passes. (JCE-007-055) The claim rests on Saknussemm's own testimony that he reached the bottom of Sneffels centuries before. (JCE-006-044)

## Geography & Features

Sneffels is not a simple cone: the mountain has several craters, which is why the cipher must specify which one by the pointing shadow of the neighbouring peak Scartaris. (JCE-006-042) As a jökull — an [[iceland|Iceland]]ic ice-capped volcano — it is snow-crowned and long extinct, its fires cold enough to walk its throat.
