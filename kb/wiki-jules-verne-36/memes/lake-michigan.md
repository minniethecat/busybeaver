# Lake Michigan

## Overview

Lake Michigan is one of the North American Great Lakes and the setting for an early, headlong episode of *The Master of the World*. A racing track laid across Wisconsin runs "ending a little above Milwaukee on the borders of Lake Michigan," (MW-004-021) bringing the mysterious machine to the very edge of the water.

The lake's shore forms a natural trap that the vehicle appears certain to strike.

## In the Narrative

As the machine tears down the track, the watchers reason it cannot escape the water ahead: the track "ended on the edge of Lake Michigan, and so the vehicle would be forced to stop there anyway, unless its supernatural driver could ride the water as well as the land." (MW-004-046)

The peril mounts as the shore nears — "Lake Michigan was not half a mile beyond, and the machine must certainly be hurled into the water!" (MW-004-050) The onlookers wonder whether, past the city, "it would plunge itself to destruction in the waters of Lake Michigan." (MW-004-051)

## Geography & Features

The lake's edge marks the abrupt end of the land route, its waters seeming to promise the machine's doom. Afterward the investigators debate whether the driver "had perished with his machine in Lake Michigan," (MW-005-050) or had somehow escaped by crossing the lake itself, "engulfed within the waters of Lake Michigan." (MW-005-025)

The doubt over its fate on the lake becomes the opening riddle of the whole pursuit.
