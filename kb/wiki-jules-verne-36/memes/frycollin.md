# Frycollin

## Overview

He enters the story as a servant ill-matched to his employer — Uncle Prudent "for his only servant had his valet Frycollin, who was hardly worthy of being the servant to so audacious a master" (RC-002-019).

His duty binds him to a restless man: "the valet Frycollin waited for Uncle Prudent, his master" outside the club, following where he would rather not go (RC-005-008).

## Role in the Story

He is carried off in the same ambush as his betters, when the attackers set "two onto Frycollin" in the darkness of Fairmount Park (RC-005-030).

Bound and gagged beside them, "Uncle Prudent, Phil Evans, and Frycollin were anything but pleased with their position" (RC-006-002).

Aboard the aeronef he is lodged apart, for "Frycollin, the valet, was quartered forward in a cabin adjoining that of the cook" (RC-009-003).

When his noise grows unbearable during the fishing, "the engineer ordered them to shut up Frycollin in his cabin" (RC-013-053).

## Character & Traits

His governing quality is stated flatly: "Frycollin was a thorough coward" (RC-005-021), and "Frycollin's cowardice had brought him many arduous trials" in the service of so bold a master (RC-005-019).

Airborne, he is undone — he "did not conceal his terror at finding himself borne through space on such a machine, and he took no pains whatever to hide it" (RC-008-003).

Over the open ocean the panic returns, as "finding himself above the boundless sea, [he] was seized with another fit of terror" (RC-013-050).

## Relationships

- [[Uncle Prudent]] — his master, whose audacious ventures forever drag the reluctant valet into danger.
- [[Phil Evans]] — his master's colleague, whom Frycollin overwhelms "with piteous pleadings to be put 'on the ground'" (RC-013-032).
- [[Robur]] — the aviator who holds them all captive, and to whom Frycollin counts "as a quantity of no importance" (RC-011-002).

## Appearances

- [[Robur the Conqueror]] — as the terrified valet borne aloft aboard the Albatross, comic foil to the grim contest of Robur and his prisoners.
