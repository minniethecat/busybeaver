## Overview

Ireland is the western island of the British Isles that the *Nautilus* and the *Great Eastern* pass on their Atlantic courses in *Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea*. Following the transatlantic cable, the Nautilus lay no farther than 150 kilometers from Ireland. (TTLU-044-036)

## In the Narrative

The laying of the cable is measured against its coast. The break happened 638 miles from the coast of Ireland. (TTLU-044-030) By July 23 the *Great Eastern* was lying no farther than 800 kilometers from Newfoundland when it received telegraphed news from Ireland of an armistice signed between Prussia and Austria after the Battle of Sadova. (TTLU-044-033)

In *The Waif of the Cynthia*, Ireland threads a subtler mystery: the coast of Great Britain holds a people of the Celtic race, on her sister island, Ireland. (WC-004-077) The pursuit of Patrick O'Donoghan requires passing through Ireland on the way back to Sweden, to see if he had returned there to pass the remainder of his days planting cabbages. (WC-009-029)

## Geography & Features

The Gulf Stream sets its climate — before entering the Gulf of Mexico this current divides, and its chief arm makes for the shores of Ireland and Norway. (TTLU-035-003) Its rivers hold their own life: the mussels of certain streams in Scotland, Wales, Ireland, Saxony, Bohemia, and France. (TTLU-026-060)

Its ships range wide across the Atlantic — some belong to English ports, and some to Scotland and Ireland. (WC-005-059)
