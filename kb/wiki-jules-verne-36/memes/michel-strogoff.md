# Michel Strogoff

## Overview

Michel Strogoff is a captain in the special corps of the Czar's couriers, ranking as an officer among those picked men (MS-004-032). Summoned to the imperial library, "Michael Strogoff, the courier, entered" to receive a secret charge from the Emperor (MS-004-029).

The son of old Peter Strogoff, a huntsman of Omsk, and of his mother Marfa Strogoff who lives there still (MS-004-035), he is judged the one man who could accomplish the journey from Moscow to Irkutsk across a rebellious country and brave perils of all sorts (MS-004-033).

## Role in the Story

The Czar entrusts him with a letter to be delivered into the hands of the Grand Duke, and to no other but him (MS-004-059). Upon it, the Emperor warns, depends the safety of all Siberia, and perhaps the life of his brother the Grand Duke (MS-004-074).

Dismissed with the charge to go "for God, for Russia, for my brother, and for myself," Strogoff sets out (MS-004-081). The road he must cover between Moscow and Irkutsk stretches some three thousand four hundred miles (MS-005-001).

Through captivity and disaster he presses on, until at the last he stands but half a verst from [[Irkutsk]] and murmurs, "At last!" (MS-030-062).

## Character & Traits

Strogoff has the temperament of the man of action, who does not bite his nails or scratch his head in doubt and indecision (MS-004-030). He is a man who feared neither frost nor snow (MS-005-002), and among a column of prisoners he proves the most docile and patient of them all (MS-020-017).

His hardest trial is one of self-command. Recognized in the street of Omsk by his mother, he answers coldly, "A resemblance deceives you" (MS-015-063); for to acknowledge her would betray his mission. He would have given his life to lock his mother in his arms, yet holds fast to his oath (MS-015-065).

## Relationships

- Nadia — the young woman he escorts across Siberia and at last carries to safety, her whole life "concentrated in her eyes" (MS-029-005).
- Marfa Strogoff — his mother, whom he must deny in the streets of Omsk to protect his mission (MS-015-063).
- Ivan Ogareff — the traitor he is warned to avoid above all, and must contrive not to be seen by (MS-015-052).
- The Czar — who chooses him and sends him forth "for God, for Russia" (MS-004-081).
