## Overview

Rio de Janeiro is the great Brazilian city on the Atlantic whose distant authority hangs over *[[Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon]]*. It is the seat from which judgment must come, the people awaiting "the arrival of the instructions due from Rio de Janeiro" (EHLA-034-002), the far capital holding the fate of the accused in its hands.

The city is the source of every official word. The trembling Manoel asks "have you received anything from Rio de Janeiro" (EHLA-036-041), and the whole drama waits on the answer, so that the remote city governs the action far up the river.

## In the Narrative

Rio de Janeiro is the destination of the fatal document. The prisoner knows "his memoir would have been sent to Rio de Janeiro, and was now in the hands of the chief justice" (EHLA-037-006), the capital being the seat of the highest court to which his appeal must travel.

It is likewise the terminus of the guarded convoy. One official "had been appointed to accompany the convoy to Rio de Janeiro" (EHLA-039-018), and at the close "there was sent to Rio de Janeiro a detailed report of the whole affair" (EHLA-039-041), the city receiving both the prisoners and the truth of the crime.

## Geography & Features

Rio de Janeiro is a fountainhead of Brazilian commerce and fashion. A dealer boasts of wares as "the latest novelties from Belem and Rio de Janeiro" (EHLA-012-055), pairing the capital with [[Belem]] as the twin sources of the finest goods carried up the [[Amazon]].

It is also the destination of the diamond wealth of the interior. The precious lots "are put into sacks and forwarded to Rio de Janeiro" (EHLA-019-031), and being "worth many millions" they travel heavily escorted, the city drawing to itself the treasure of the mines.

## Connections

Rio de Janeiro is the distant seat of justice and commerce in *[[Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon]]*, the destination of both the guarded convoy and the fatal cipher, and the twin of [[Belem]] as a source of the goods and wealth carried along the [[Amazon]].
