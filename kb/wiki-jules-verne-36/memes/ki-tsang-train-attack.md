# Ki-Tsang's Attack on the Grand Transasiatic

## Overview

Ki-Tsang's attack is the great set-piece of *The Adventures of a Special Correspondent*—on the plains of the Gobi, the bandit chief tears up the rails to halt the [[Grand Transasiatic Railway Company|Grand Transasiatic]] train and seize the imperial treasure, only to be repulsed and slain.

The defenders formed up first. The Chinese officer placed his men around the treasure van, bracing to hold the halted train against the raid to come (ASC-020-020).

## What Happens

The ambush sprang from cover. Suddenly there was a burst of shouting as the thicket gave passage to the gang lying in wait—some sixty Mongols, nomads of the Gobi (ASC-020-022).

The bandits opened fire. They loosed a volley and began brandishing their arms and shouting as they closed on the stranded cars (ASC-020-029).

The passengers fought back. Major Noltitz and the narrator threw themselves into the very midst of their assailants to meet the charge (ASC-020-030).

The raiders broke. Instantly the thieves beat a retreat, without even carrying off their dead and wounded (ASC-020-045).

## Significance

The chief was killed. Standing over the wounded bandit, Faruskiar drove a last thrust of the kandijar into his heart, ending the assault (ASC-020-050).

The attacker was named. So it was Ki-Tsang who had just attacked the Grand Transasiatic on the plains of Gobi (ASC-021-001).

The train was left crippled. As the engine had run off the rails, the very first task was evidently to get it back onto them across the broken line (ASC-021-009).

## Participants & Setting

- The raid strikes the halted train halfway between Tcharkalyk and Tchertchen, where about a hundred yards of rail had been lifted to bring the engine to a standstill in the sand.
- Ki-Tsang leads some sixty Mongol nomads out of a deep thicket beside the line, aiming to seize the imperial treasure bound for Peking.
- Faruskiar, general manager of the line, leads the defence and kills Ki-Tsang; the affray leaves three dead and twelve wounded, chronicled in *The Adventures of a Special Correspondent*.
