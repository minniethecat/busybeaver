## Overview

The Liedenbrock Sea is the immense underground ocean that the explorers reach deep beneath the Earth's crust in *[[journey-to-the-center-of-the-earth|Journey to the Center of the Earth]]*. [[professor-lidenbrock|Professor Lidenbrock]] claims the discovery for himself, declaring that it should bear his name: he "insisted on giving it his own name" — the Liedenbrock Sea (JCE-030-004). It is a genuine body of water complete with waves, tides, and a distant coastline, and it forms the central geographic feature of the expedition's descent.

The travelers first behold it as a boundless expanse. The waves of the Liedenbrock Sea break upon its shore (JCE-031-056), and the party roams "on the shores of this subterranean sea" (JCE-030-040) before venturing across it on a raft.

## In the Narrative

The sea marks the turning point of the journey, where the vertical descent gives way to a horizontal voyage. Having "roamed on the shores of this subterranean sea" (JCE-030-040), Lidenbrock and his companions build a raft to cross the water toward the far coast.

The waters themselves are described in maritime terms. The narrator speaks of "the waters of this mediterranean sea" (JCE-031-002), casting the underground expanse as an inland ocean sealed within the Earth. As the voyage proceeds, jagged rocks appear: they pass "rocks that fringe this subterranean sea" (JCE-039-043), the coastal features that ring the whole basin.

## Geography & Features

The Liedenbrock Sea has the physical character of a true ocean. It obeys tides: the professor calculates that "the sea will rise about twelve feet" (JCE-031-009), a genuine tidal range driven by forces reaching even into the Earth's interior.

It is also a bounded body, not an endless flood. The reasoning holds that this "internal sea will be surrounded by a coast" (JCE-031-038), implying an enclosing shoreline on every side. Together with "the waters of this mediterranean sea" (JCE-031-002), these details establish a self-contained subterranean ocean with its own coast, tides, and horizon.

## Connections

The Liedenbrock Sea lies at the deep end of the route that begins at [[Snæfellsjökull]], the [[iceland|Iceland]]ic volcano through whose crater the expedition descends. It is named for and explored by [[Professor Lidenbrock]], whose insistence on the name (JCE-030-004) fixes it on the map of the underworld in *[[Journey to the Center of the Earth]]*.
