# Lake Baikal

## Overview

Lake Baikal is the vast Siberian lake that marks a decisive stage of Michael Strogoff's race toward Irkutsk. It is the limit of the Czar's reach — "the last telegram we were able to send beyond Lake Baikal." (MS-002-020)

Approaching Irkutsk, "no Russian force could be opposed to them between Krasnoiarsk and Lake Baikal," (MS-028-024) so that reaching its shore is itself a triumph: "It was Lake Baikal." (MS-028-094)

## In the Narrative

The lake haunts Nadia's thoughts, which "sometimes left the provinces of Lake Baikal, and returned to her present situation." (MS-013-014) Michael plans "to go through the Lake Baikal provinces in a southeasterly direction." (MS-018-004)

At its shore the fugitives gather — "these Russians were fugitives of different conditions, whom the same interest had united at Lake Baikal." (MS-029-011) They stake their escape on its waters: "a current in the lake runs along by the upper bank to the mouth of the Angara; this current they hoped to utilize." (MS-029-013)

## Geography & Features

The lake is notoriously wild: "Lake Baikal is subject to violent tempests," (MS-029-004) its people sometimes calling it "Sir Lake." Its steppe shore, "usually a desert," is now crowded with fugitives. (MS-029-007)

Winter seals it as they cross: "during the night the temperature fell below zero; ice was already forming on the surface of the Baikal." (MS-029-017) Its outlet feeds the Angara, the river that carries the story on toward Irkutsk.
