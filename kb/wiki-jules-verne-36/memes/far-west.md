## Overview

The Far West is the great western forest of Lincoln Island in The Mysterious Island. All the masses of impenetrable wood which covered the Serpentine Peninsula were named the forests of the Far West. (MI-011-075) These immense woods, to which the name of the Forests of the Far West had been given, long remained unexplored. (MI-019-024)

## In the Narrative

The Far West was the last district the settlers penetrated. For a long time nothing was found there: neither in the forests of the Far West was anything to be seen. (MI-023-055) The first report which awoke the echoes of the forest of the Far West was provoked by the appearance of a beautiful bird, resembling the kingfisher. (MI-025-023)

Cyrus Harding and Herbert, while hunting, entered the forest of the Far West, on the left bank of the Mercy. (MI-031-028) There they examined the part where the cycas grew, took their bearings, and returned to Granite House to make known their discovery. (MI-031-038) In time a straight road, or cutting, was made through the forests of the Far West. (MI-029-055)

## Geography & Features

The forest rose at a distance: behind, in the west, but four miles off, rose the first trees of the forests of the Far West. (MI-021-017) It was part of an unbroken expanse; including Jacamar Wood, as well as the forests of the Far West, it extended as far as the eye could reach. (MI-025-028)

The wood was varied in growth. Among the last trees of the forest of the Far West the settlers found several thick clumps of bamboos. (MI-026-043) Its windings could disorient a traveller, and the engineer frequently consulted his compass to assure himself that a turn of the river was not leading them again into the Far West. (MI-026-028)

## Connections

The Far West joined the island's road network: by following the Mercy through the woods of the Far West, the travellers were certain to reach Prospect Heights and Union Bay. (MI-012-013) The forest was a refuge for game, for the wild animals generally lived in the forests of the Far West, though pressed by hunger they ventured as far as Prospect Heights. (MI-022-035)

To gain easy access, Spilett judged it would be useful to construct a bridge to the Far West. (MI-027-022)
