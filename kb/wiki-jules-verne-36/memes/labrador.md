## Overview

Labrador is the bleak sub-Arctic coast of northeastern Canada that recurs as a waymark in Verne's polar tales. When the destination of the Forward is guessed at in *[[The Adventures of Captain Hatteras]]*, one surmise runs "Labrador or Hudson's Bay?" (ACH-004-016), the coast named among the first thresholds of the northern ice.

It is a shore to be feared as much as sought. The mate warns that unless steam is put on "we shall be thrown on the coasts of Labrador" (ACH-005-064), the rocky seaboard standing as a peril to any ship driven off her course near [[Cape Farewell]].

## In the Narrative

Labrador belongs to the long roll of northern discoveries. Verne lists it among the lands opened by the early navigators—"Labrador, Brazil, the Cape of Good Hope, the Azores, Madeira, Newfoundland, Guinea" (ACH-012-051)—setting the coast in the company of the great feats of exploration.

It is also reckoned among the homes of the far-northern peoples. In *Topsy-Turvy* the account names "the inhabitants of Greenland, of Labrador, of Baffin's Archipelago and of the Aleutian Islands" (TT-001-062), the coast counted with the icebound territories whose folk endure the polar cold.

## Geography & Features

Labrador is divided from its island neighbour by a narrow sea. It is noted that the Straits of Belle Isle "separate Labrador from [[Newfoundland]]" (WC-015-035) in *[[The Waif of the Cynthia]]*, the channel marking the boundary between the mainland coast and the great fishing island.

The region lends its name to the plants and stones of the tundra. In *[[The Fur Country]]* the hunters find that "Labrador tea grew abundantly on the shores of the lagoon" (FC-014-032), and the beach glitters with "Labrador stones, and many other kinds of felspar" (FC-015-015), the coast's name carried into its very flora and minerals.

## Connections

Labrador is a northern threshold and peril of the Forward's cruise in *[[The Adventures of Captain Hatteras]]*, a name in the roll of discoveries beside [[Newfoundland]] and [[Greenland]], and the mainland coast whose tundra plants and stones bear its name in *[[The Fur Country]]*.
