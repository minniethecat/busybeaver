# Cape Esquimaux

## Overview

Cape Esquimaux is a fictional headland on the drifting peninsula of Cape Bathurst in *The Fur Country*, named by Hobson's colonists during their survey. It lies on the southern reach of the wandering coast: "From Cape Esquimaux to Walruses' Bay the coast ran almost due south, following the one hundred and twenty-seventh meridian." (FC-026-053)

A native family camps beside it, "encamped near Cape Esquimaux for eight days." (FC-019-052)

## In the Narrative

The cape is watched anxiously as the island drifts and melts. Sergeant Long warns that "in another two months the waves will no longer break upon the shores of Cape Esquimaux" (FC-026-029), and the colonists fear "who can tell whether Cape Esquimaux and Walruses' Bay may not also disappear in time?" (FC-026-030)

Mrs Barnett makes it a goal of her walks, resolving "to go a few miles along the coast towards Cape Esquimaux" (FC-031-027), though she finds she must keep "at least two miles inland, for they had found it impossible to follow the coast, worn away as it was by the sea." (FC-031-046)

## Geography & Features

Hobson surveys the surrounding land from its summit — "Before starting Hobson once more looked round him from the summit of Cape Esquimaux." (FC-026-037) As the ice wastes, the cape is worn down until it "now rose only a few feet upon the sea-level against the background of the sky, and was quite deserted." (FC-031-064)

It becomes a haunt of Arctic beasts; near its shore "a large white mass was moving about and growling angrily." (FC-031-069)
