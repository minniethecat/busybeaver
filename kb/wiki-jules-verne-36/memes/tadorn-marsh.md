## Overview

Tadorn Marsh is a vast marsh in the south-eastern part of Lincoln Island. On the 3rd of August an excursion long talked of was made towards it, into that quarter of the island. (MI-033-074) It formed part of the eastern country the settlers knew thoroughly, from Claw Cape to the Mandible Capes, near the neighbourhood of Lake Grant. (MI-053-022)

## In the Narrative

The settlers made fine sporting excursions in the vast marsh during the frost. (MI-042-075) It also lay on the convicts' path: they first landed near Flotsam Point and plunged into the Far West forests after crossing Tadorn Marsh. (MI-054-004)

## Geography & Features

The marsh was a refuge for the island's wildlife. When terror seized the animals, jaguars, wild boars, capybaras and game of every kind fled to the banks of the Mercy and to Tadorn Marsh, beyond the road to Port Balloon. (MI-061-060) Those beasts that survived found no refuge but the marsh, save a few sheltered on the plateau of Prospect Heights. (MI-061-078)

## Connections

Tadorn Marsh marks the wild south-eastern reach of Lincoln Island, set against the cultivated heights: after the island's devastation, the side of the ruined forest was even more barren than the marsh itself. (MI-061-062)
