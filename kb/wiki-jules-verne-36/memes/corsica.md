## Overview

Corsica is the mountainous Mediterranean island, a familiar measure of size and place in Verne's seas. Christmas Harbour's chief islet is about half as large as Corsica. (AM-001-005) When the comet struck, the Dobryna reached what had been the southern extremity of Corsica. (OC-018-012)

## In the Narrative

The island's works and waters recur across the voyages. The engineer reduced his iron ore by the Catalan method, simplified as it is used in Corsica. (MI-015-041) Off Corsica are found the mollusks known as Ostrea lamellosa, common in those waters. (TTLU-019-019)

## Geography & Features

Corsica is a land of granite and sea-caves. Among the world's great caverns are the caves of Bonifacio, in Corsica, ranked with Fingal's Cave and the caves of Morgat in Brittany. (MI-057-108) Its southern strait once divided it from the barren rocks to the south before the disaster. (OC-018-012)

## Connections

Corsica lies in the [[Mediterranean]], its southern strait the vanished Strait of Bonifacio. (OC-018-012) Its coasts are swept up in the catastrophe of [[Off on a Comet]]. (OC-018-012)
