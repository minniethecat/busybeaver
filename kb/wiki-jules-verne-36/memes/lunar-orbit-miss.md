# Missing the Moon

## Overview

Missing the Moon is the turning point of *[[round-the-moon|Round the Moon]]*, the sequel to *[[from-the-earth-to-the-moon|From the Earth to the Moon]]*, in which the travellers' aim is spoiled by a chance body in space. [[barbicane|Barbicane]] guesses the cause of their trouble at once — "I know, I guess what this pretended asteroid is!" (RM-006-090) — a wandering mass whose gravity has bent their path.

## What Happens

Observing the projectile's course, [[barbicane|Barbicane]] thought he remarked that the conical end deviated slightly from the normal direction towards the moon (RM-008-050). The deflection grows until the shell is no longer falling toward its target but describing a curve parallel with the lunar disc (RM-009-009).

The verdict is soon plain: the audacious attempt had miscarried by a fortuitous circumstance, and unless anything unexpected happened, the lunar disc could no longer be reached (RM-010-002). Instead of landing, the projectile swings round the Moon as a captive satellite.

## Significance

The miss redefines the whole adventure, converting a voyage *to* the Moon into a voyage *around* it and giving the sequel its title. That a stray body — the pretended asteroid [[barbicane|Barbicane]] guessed at (RM-006-090) — could undo the [[gun-club|Gun Club]]'s vast calculation is Verne's reminder that space obeys chance as well as arithmetic. Once the lunar disc could no longer be reached (RM-010-002), the travellers' survival, not their landing, becomes the story's true concern.

## Participants & Setting

- Befalls [[Barbicane]] and [[Michel Ardan]] aboard the [[Lunar Projectile]].
- Continues the voyage begun in [[From the Earth to the Moon]].
