## Overview

Cape Bon is the most northern promontory of Africa and the point of the continent nearest to the island of Sicily. (OC-012-005) In Off on a Comet the sundering that carries a slice of the earth into space includes this headland in its general devastation, and Hector Servadac's survey of the changed sea turns on it.

## In the Narrative

When the comet tears away its fragment of the globe, Cape Bon is caught up in the upheaval. (OC-012-005) A formation there shows that at some remote epoch the cape had been connected with Cape Furina, the extremity of Sicily, just as Ceuta was once joined to Gibraltar. (OC-012-006)

The convulsion has left no trace of that submerged chain: it is clear the link between Cape Bon and Cape Furina no longer exists, the sea-bottom generally levelled. (OC-012-009)

Later the airship "Albatross," ranging over Tunis, traverses the country from Cape Bon to Cape Carthage, now hovering, now darting at top speed. (RC-015-004)

## Geography & Features

The cape marks the narrows where Africa reaches toward Sicily; in the cramped space between Cape Bon and the Strait of Messina the sea bottom rises almost all at once. (TTLU-031-016) Its rock once bridged, by a drowned chain, to Cape Furina on the Sicilian side — a span the comet's convulsion swept away. (OC-012-006)
