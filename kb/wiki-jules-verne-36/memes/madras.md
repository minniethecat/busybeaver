## Overview

Madras is the great southern Indian city that stands as a seat of British power in *[[Around the World in Eighty Days]]*. The Crown "has a governor-general stationed at Calcutta, governors at Madras, Bombay, and in Bengal" (AWED-010-002), the city ranking with [[Calcutta]] and [[Bombay]] among the chief seats of colonial rule.

It marks the very foothold of English India. Verne recalls that the East India Company was all-powerful "when the English first gained a foothold on the spot where now stands the city of Madras" (AWED-010-003), the town dating the beginning of the Company's long dominion.

## In the Narrative

Madras figures in the peril that dogs the travellers across India. It is feared that the fanatics "would, despite the English police, recover their victim at Madras, Bombay, or Calcutta" (AWED-014-003), the city named among the great ports where the pursuers might strike.

It also belongs to the roll of world cities that hail the Gun Club. When the great enterprise is announced, distant capitals—"Benares, Madras, and Pekin—sent their compliments to the Gun Club" (FEM-012-005), the southern Indian city joining the chorus of applause from across the globe.

## Geography & Features

Madras stands among the chief towns of British India and its neighbouring seas. In the geographical survey it is listed with "Bombay, Madras, Calicut, Aden, Malacca, Singapore, Pegu, Colombo" (SC-038-073), the city set in the company of the great trading ports of the eastern trade.

Its rise is bound to the fortunes of the East India Company. The Company held sway from 1756 "down to the time of the great Sepoy insurrection" (AWED-010-003), the city having grown from that first English foothold into a governor's seat commanding the southern coast.
