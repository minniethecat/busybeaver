## Overview

Chicago is the booming American railway city that marks a crucial stage of the transcontinental crossing in *[[Around the World in Eighty Days]]*. When Phileas Fogg's party reaches it, the city is "already risen from its ruins, and more proudly seated than ever on the borders of its beautiful Lake Michigan" (AWED-031-036), rebuilt after the great fire.

It stands as the hinge between the western plains and the eastern seaboard, for "thence the trains eastward run frequently to Chicago and New York" (AWED-031-016), making it the gateway through which travellers pour toward the Atlantic coast.

## In the Narrative

For Fogg, Chicago is a waypoint in a race against time. The city is linked to the west by rail, since "Omaha is connected with Chicago by the Chicago and Rock Island Railroad, which runs directly east, and passes fifty stations" (AWED-031-034), and from it the final dash begins.

The distance still to cover is spelled out plainly: "nine hundred miles separated Chicago from New York; but trains are not wanting at Chicago" (AWED-031-037), and the abundance of departures lets the travellers press on without delay toward their goal.

## Geography & Features

Chicago sits on the shore of Lake Michigan, deep in the American interior. In *Robur the Conqueror* the aeronauts sight it from the air and reckon their position by it: "if that is Chicago... it is obvious that we are going farther west than is convenient for us" (RC-009-010), the city serving as a landmark on the Great Lakes.

Its position in Illinois makes it a recurring reference point across the voyages. In *Master of the World* the mysterious machine is reported "in Illinois in the neighborhood of Chicago" (MW-004-013), and elsewhere the city hosts such spectacles as a "fat-man show at Chicago" (GM-002-036), a token of its bustling civic life.
