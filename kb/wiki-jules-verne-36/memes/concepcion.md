## Overview

Concepcion is an old city of Chile, lying a little inland from the Pacific coast near the bay of Talcahuano. In the Vernean corpus it belongs to *In Search of the Castaways*, where it is the South American landfall of the yacht *Duncan* and the point from which Lord Glenarvan's party sets out along the thirty-seventh parallel in search of Captain Grant.

## In the Narrative

Concepcion is the yacht's declared destination: told that the wheel need only be turned and "the *Duncan* will sail as easily to Calcutta as to Concepcion." (SC-007-046) As the voyage nears its end, the geographer Paganel hopes "to be able to speak the language fluently when they arrived at Concepcion." (SC-009-006) The place is repeatedly named as their goal in the travellers' exchanges — "To Concepcion," (SC-007-038) and again "At Concepcion." (SC-008-066)

## Geography & Features

The city stands back from its harbour: on landing the party "were informed ... that the British Consul lived at Concepcion, an hour's ride distant." (SC-010-004) Its greatness has passed, for "Concepcion, the ancient city of brave men, had become a village of women and children," (SC-010-005) its men drawn away and its streets diminished by the decline that had overtaken the old Chilean town.

## Connections

Concepcion is bound to *In Search of the Castaways* as the yacht's Chilean landfall, the destination toward which the *Duncan* was said to sail as readily "as to Calcutta." (SC-007-046) It is the place where the party first sought the "British Consul," (SC-010-004) and the "ancient city of brave men" whose decline into "a village of women and children" greets them at the outset of their South American search. (SC-010-005)
