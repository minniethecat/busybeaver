## Overview

Algiers is the chief city of the North African coast, a place Verne repeatedly weighs against catastrophe. Servadac wondered had the towns of Algiers, Oran, and Mostaganem escaped the inundation. (OC-007-007) The governor general would surely send a vessel from Algiers to explore the damage. (OC-008-017)

## In the Narrative

In the comet's passage the city is swallowed with the coast. Algiers, like all the other coast-towns, had apparently been absorbed into the bowels of the earth. (OC-011-017) Poor Hakkabut still whimpered, "But I want to go to Algiers." (OC-022-022) "How often am I to tell you that Algiers is no longer in existence?" came the reply. (OC-022-023)

## Geography & Features

Algiers is built amphitheater-wise above its bay and looks to the desert beyond. The maritime town of Dellis, built like Algiers, amphitheater-wise, had totally disappeared. (OC-012-003) Engineers dreamed of a Trans-Saharan Railway to bind Algiers and Timbuktu. (RC-015-013) From the coast of Algiers to the beaches of Provence, how many ships have wrecked. (TTLU-031-041)

## Connections

Algiers looks north across the [[Mediterranean]] toward Europe. (TTLU-031-041) Its disappearance is among the disasters chronicled in [[Off on a Comet]]. (OC-011-017)
