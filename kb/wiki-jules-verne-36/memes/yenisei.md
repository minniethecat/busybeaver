## Overview

The Yenisei is a real great river of Eastern Siberia, the broad barrier that halts Michael Strogoff's advance below Krasnoiarsk. At this place the Yenisei is not less than a mile in width, and forms two arms, of unequal size, through which the waters flow swiftly. (MS-026-042)

## In the Narrative

The town of Krasnoiarsk stands over it — the outline of the churches and houses built on the high bank of the Yenisei is clearly defined against the evening sky. (MS-026-006) From the heights the travellers survey the obstacle; they were about a hundred feet above the Yenisei, and could therefore survey the whole of its wide course. (MS-026-029)

Nicholas names the task plainly: "We have the Yenisei to cross, and need light to see our way there!" (MS-026-023) The river is also Strogoff's ally — unless the Tartars should bring materials for a bridge of boats, their march toward Irkutsk would certainly be stopped for some time by this barrier. (MS-026-043)

## Geography & Features

Flowing northward past Krasnoiarsk in two swift arms a mile across, the Yenisei is the last great natural defense before Irkutsk, crossable only by ferry or bridge of boats.
