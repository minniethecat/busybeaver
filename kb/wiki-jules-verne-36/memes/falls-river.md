# Falls River

## Overview

Falls River is a stream on the southern coast of Lincoln Island, named by the castaways during their survey of the wooded south in *The Mysterious Island*. As they explore the shore, "it was settled that the name of Falls River should be given to this stream." (MI-026-035)

It waters the forested Serpentine Peninsula, a region richer than the barren coasts elsewhere on the island.

## In the Narrative

The river marks a boundary of the wooded south. "All the part of the shore between Falls River and Reptile End was a mass of wood, magnificent trees, some straight, others bent, so that the long sea-swell bathed their roots." (MI-026-035) The colonists reckon that "the distance which separated Falls River from Reptile End was about twelve miles." (MI-026-038)

It later figures in the pursuit of the escaped convicts, who, "after having traversed the double shore of the Serpentine Peninsula," reach "the mouth of Falls River." (MI-050-002) The colonists' road from the corral runs "along which ran the road from the corral to the mouth of Falls River." (MI-040-052)

## Geography & Features

The river empties on the southern coast; the *Bonadventure* reaches "the mouth of Falls River" about noon on one of its coasting voyages. (MI-041-044) It bounds the imperfectly known western shore — the colonists long had "but an imperfect idea of the shore to the west and north, from the mouth of Falls River to the Mandible Capes." (MI-041-034)

Falls River is fed, with Creek Glycerine, from the plateau lake: to raise the water level the engineer need only "establish a dam at the two openings made by the lake, and by which were fed Creek Glycerine and Falls River." (MI-048-006)
