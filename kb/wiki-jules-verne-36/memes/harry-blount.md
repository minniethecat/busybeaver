# Harry Blount

## Overview

Harry Blount is the English war correspondent covering the Tartar invasion — "Harry Blount was the name of the Englishman" (MS-002-039). He files for the Daily Telegraph, confident that "the readers of the Daily Telegraph would not fail to be as well informed as Alcide Jolivet's 'cousin'" (MS-005-049).

Reserved and watchful, "Harry Blount, speaking little, but listening much, had not inspired his companions with the suspicions which Alcide Jolivet had aroused" (MS-005-047).

## Role in the Story

On the battlefield the two reporters become "no longer traveling companions, but rivals, enemies, now that they were working on the field of battle" (MS-018-034). Blount seizes the telegraph first — "Blount, having distanced his companion, took possession of the wicket" (MS-018-037).

He holds the line by wiring nonsense, for "Harry Blount was telegraphing some verses learned in his childhood, in order to employ the time, and not give up his place to his rival" (MS-018-050).

His dogged coverage costs him: "Harry Blount fell to the ground wounded in the shoulder" (MS-018-071). Carried off with his rival, "Harry Blount and Alcide Jolivet had also been taken to the Tartar camp" (MS-020-021), where he presses on with the newsman's question, "for the first time putting his question direct to his companion" (MS-020-040).

## Character & Traits

Dry and methodical, Blount is the foil to Jolivet's chatter — "Alcide spoke in sentences; Blount replied by monosyllables" (MS-013-018) — and "a methodical eater" whom every "hurried meal" vexes (MS-013-021). His judgments carry "a certain English jealousy with regard to Russian pretensions in Central Asia": "'Too much ambition has lost the greatest empires,' answered Blount" (MS-020-044).

To the last he stays "the reserved Englishman, who had scarcely addressed a word to her during the whole passage over the Ural Mountains" (MS-029-042), yet a man of honor — "'On my word as a gentleman,' added Blount" (MS-029-050) — and no coward, for when wolves assail the raft, "Neither were Jolivet and Blount idle, but fought bravely with the brutes" (MS-030-025).

## Relationships

- [[Alcide Jolivet]] — the French correspondent, by turns his companion and fiercest professional rival, who after Blount's wounding "had not ceased his attentions to him" (MS-020-022).
- [[Michel Strogoff]] — the Czar's courier whose secret the reporters vow to keep: "Alcide and Blount, though they had promised to keep Michael's secret, did not leave him" (MS-029-057).
