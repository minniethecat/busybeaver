## Overview

In *[[the-adventures-of-captain-hatteras|The Adventures of Captain Hatteras]]* the North Pole is not an empty point on the ice but a volcanic island rising from an open polar sea. To reach it is the single obsession of [[captain-hatteras|Captain Hatteras]]: "to attain the Pole was the aim of his life" (ACH-012-056), and the whole expedition is bent toward that goal.

When the travelers finally arrive, they find "a volcano, fixed like a lighthouse on the North Pole of the world" (ACH-055-010). The exact geographic pole turns out to be occupied by an active mountain — christened Queen's Island — burning at the top of the earth.

## In the Narrative

The Pole is the climax toward which the entire novel builds. After crossing the ice and reaching open water, the explorers sight the fiery peak, and at last "the North Pole was indeed before his eyes" (ACH-055-024). Hatteras, half-mad with triumph, plants his flag and cries out that the ground, air, and sea about them all belong to the Pole.

Reaching it fulfills and destroys him at once. The survivor is found "alive at the North Pole, on Queen's Island" (ACH-055-036), the purpose of his life attained — yet the achievement costs Hatteras his reason, leaving the Pole a goal won at ruinous price.

## Geography & Features

The polar island is small and volcanic. It measures "not more than eight or ten miles in circumference" (ACH-055-025), a compact cone rising alone from the sea, and it vents fire from a single vent: "there was only one crater to the volcano" (ACH-055-015), out of which columns of flame and smoke pour continuously.

The novel frames it by comparison with the known volcanoes of the far south, invoking "the existence of two active volcanoes, the Erebus and the Terror" of Antarctica (ACH-054-020) as antarctic counterparts to this northern fire-mountain, binding both ends of the earth by their polar volcanoes.

## Connections

The North Pole is the ultimate destination of [[Captain Hatteras]] and the crew of his expedition in *[[The Adventures of Captain Hatteras]]*, the volcanic island at which the long voyage over the Arctic ice finally ends.
