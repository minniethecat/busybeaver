# The Oxygen Frenzy of Quiquendone

> **Note:** Inline PN citations for this page use a 4-character work code (`DOSE`); they render as plain text pending RFC-0003. The underlying PN data is valid.

## Overview

The Oxygen Frenzy of Quiquendone is Doctor Ox's covert saturation of a sleepy Flemish town with pure oxygen, whipping its placid citizens into fever and war until a chance explosion ends it—the central satirical experiment of *[[doctor-oxs-experiment|Doctor Ox's Experiment]]*.

The method was hidden in the gas-pipes. After laying his mains, Doctor Ox had saturated first the public buildings, then the private dwellings, and finally the streets of Quiquendone with pure oxygen, without letting in the least atom of hydrogen (DOSE-017-002).

## What Happens

The effect on the townsfolk was violent. This gas, tasteless and odorless, spreading in generous quantity through the atmosphere, causes serious agitation when breathed—one who lives in air saturated with oxygen grows excited, frantic, burns (DOSE-017-003).

The change reached an astonishing pitch. The last incident proved to what a degree of excitement the Quiquendonians had been wrought, when the two oldest and gentlest friends in the town flew to violence only minutes after their old sympathy had briefly been restored (DOSE-014-002).

The frenzy hardened into war. The burgomaster and counsellor, once incapable of a quarrel, now swore that forty-eight hours should not pass before the army of Quiquendone had marched upon the enemy of neighbouring Virgamen (DOSE-013-016).

Then came the deliverance. A formidable explosion resounded; all the atmosphere enveloping Quiquendone seemed on fire, and a flame of unwonted vividness shot up into the heavens like a meteor (DOSE-015-002).

## Significance

The cause was Ox's own works. It was soon learned that the gasworks had simply blown up, a communication having formed between the oxygen and hydrogen reservoirs into an explosive mixture to which fire was accidentally applied (DOSE-015-006).

The experimenter vanished with his experiment. This changed everything; but when the army got upon its feet again, Doctor Ox and his assistant Ygène had disappeared (DOSE-015-007).

The town's old nature returned at once. After the explosion, Quiquendone immediately became the peaceable, phlegmatic, and Flemish town it formerly was (DOSE-016-002).

It was, in the end, a lucky accident. It was fortunate for the Quiquendonians that a providential explosion put an end to this dangerous experiment and abolished Doctor Ox's gas-works (DOSE-017-006).

## Participants & Setting

- Contrived by the enigmatic Doctor Ox and his assistant Ygène, who piped pure oxygen through the streets and buildings of the placid Flemish town of Quiquendone.
- The saturated air inflamed the burgomaster Van Tricasse, the counsellor Niklausse, and the whole population into fever, artistic passion, and a war-fever against neighbouring Virgamen.
- Ended by the accidental explosion of Ox's gasworks, which restored the town's phlegm; chronicled in *[[doctor-oxs-experiment|Doctor Ox's Experiment]]*.
