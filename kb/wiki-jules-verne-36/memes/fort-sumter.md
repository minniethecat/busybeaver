# Fort Sumter

## Overview

Fort Sumter is the real island fortress at the mouth of Charleston Harbor, where the American Civil War began. Crockston recalls that his master's father "was at Charleston when the first shot of the Civil War was fired, and the troops of the Union driven from Fort Sumter by the Confederates." (BR-003-067)

## In the Narrative

As the Dolphin works into the harbor, "Fort Sumter on the left protected her from the batteries of the Federalists." (BR-006-030)

Later a gun-flash from its walls serves as a signal: "'That is from Fort Sumter,' cried Crockston, 'and it is the signal of escape.'" (BR-009-017)

Her course forces her "near Fort Sumter; and when scarcely half a mile off all the guns bearing on her were discharged at the same time." (BR-009-029) James asks anxiously, "'Then do you think we have nothing more to fear from Fort Sumter?'" (BR-009-035)

## Geography & Features

The fort stands among a ring of harbor defences. Steering in, James passed Fort Moultrie, "on the other side rose Fort Johnson, a little way to the north of Fort Sumter." (BR-006-028)

On the escape run "the two screws carried the steamer towards the principal channel, away from Fort Sumter." (BR-009-025)

The alarm to the blockading squadron is raised "by the firing from Forts Sumter and Moultrie." (BR-009-047)
