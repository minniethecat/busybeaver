# The Investigation of the Great Eyrie

## Overview

The investigation of the Great Eyrie is the opening expedition of *[[The Master of the World]]*, in which the police inspector Strock and the Mayor of Morganton, Elias Smith, set out to scale the mysterious crater of the [[Great Eyrie]] and learn the source of the flames and sounds that have terrified the countryside.

The ascent was daunting from the start, for the height of the Great Eyrie, though scarcely five thousand feet, was reputed wholly inaccessible, and this unproven report was the very spur to their attempt (MW-003-024).

## What Happens

They set out from [[Morganton]] at dawn. Following the left bank of the Catawba River toward the village of [[Pleasant Garden]], the party was accompanied by two local guides, Harry Horn and James Bruck (MW-003-002).

The climb soon grew brutal. After an hour the slope became so steep that the gorge turned wholly impracticable, its cliff-like sides offering no foothold, so they had to cling by branches and crawl upon their knees (MW-003-036).

A fallen block showed them a path. Working upward, they gained a great gash in the mountain, scattered with uptorn roots and stones reduced to powder, as if an avalanche had rushed down the flank (MW-003-054).

Following that gash, whose firmer earth had resisted the passage of the monster rock, they reached the upper border of the "slide" toward half past eleven (MW-003-057).

Before them, less than a hundred feet away yet towering a hundred feet straight upward, rose the rocky wall that formed the final crest and last defence of the Great Eyrie (MW-003-058).

## Significance

The wall proved unbreachable. After an hour and a half circling its base, they found nowhere a break in the fortification, nowhere a fault in the strata by which one might clamber up (MW-003-064).

Strock was forced to abandon the quest. He could neither tear open the unyielding earth nor overleap the mighty cliff, and so, throwing one last defiant glare at the Great Eyrie, he followed his companions down (MW-003-072).

The mystery of the crater thus survived the first assault intact, driving Strock back to Washington to consult Mr. Ward and setting in motion the larger pursuit of the Great Eyrie's secret master.

## Participants & Setting

- Strock, the federal police inspector, is charged by his chief to snatch the secret from the demon of the Great Eyrie, and pursues the quest with the redoubled curiosity of a man possessed (MW-003-028).
- Elias Smith, the Mayor of Morganton, joins the ascent as a generous provider and eager hunter of the mystery, aided by the guides Harry Horn and James Bruck, natives skilled on the peaks of the [[Blue Ridge Mountains]].
- The Great Eyrie itself, an oval rampart some twelve to fifteen hundred feet in circumference, crowns a deserted height where scarcely a living creature mounts save the birds of prey soaring overhead (MW-003-069).
