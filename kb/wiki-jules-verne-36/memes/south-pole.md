## Overview

The South Pole is the goal of the Nautilus's most audacious southern voyage in *[[Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea]]*. To reach it is framed as almost impossible, for it is "even more unattainable than the North Pole, which still hadn't been reached by the boldest navigators" (TTLU-037-054), yet Captain Nemo resolves to press on.

The venture is built on a hope of open water. The narrator wonders "why wouldn't we find open sea at the South Pole just as at the North Pole?" (TTLU-037-074), though he fears that any polar sea there "may be completely frozen over, so we couldn't come up to the surface!" (TTLU-037-071).

## In the Narrative

The Pole is reached through careful observation. Nemo relies on his instruments alone: "I use only my chronometer... if, after accounting for refraction, the sun's disk is cut exactly in half by the northern horizon, that will mean I'm at the South Pole" (TTLU-038-074), and the next noon confirms the position.

At the moment of triumph he claims the whole region. "In 1868, on this 21st day of March, I myself, Captain Nemo, have reached the South Pole at 90 degrees, and I hereby claim this entire part of the globe, equal to one-sixth of the known continents" (TTLU-038-094). There he plants a black flag, the same emblem "he had left planted at the South Pole" (TTLU-045-044) and would raise again.

## Geography & Features

The waters approaching the Pole are choked with ice. Between "the South Pole and the 60th parallel, the sea is covered with floating ice of dimensions much greater than any found in the north Atlantic" (TTLU-038-009), and the Nautilus must dive beneath the great Ice Bank to make its way.

The Pole is also distinguished from the magnetic pole nearby: as the ship advances "the deranged needles would mark contradictory directions as we approached the southern magnetic pole, which doesn't coincide with the South Pole proper" (TTLU-037-022). Its cold breeds violent weather, for "the vapors which become condensed in the immense glaciers at the South Pole produce a current of air of extreme violence" (SC-031-011).

## Connections

The South Pole is reached by the [[Nautilus]] under [[Captain Nemo]] in *[[Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea]]*, its ice barrier crossed beneath the [[Ice Bank]]. It stands as the southern counterpart to [[The North Pole]], the goal that earlier explorers such as Dumont d'Urville could only skirt, having "discovered... the South Pole with Dumont d'Urville" (GM-003-029).
