# The Drawing of Lots on the Raft

## Overview

The drawing of lots on the raft is the climax of *[[The Survivors of the Chancellor]]*, in which the last castaways of the burnt and foundered ship, adrift on the Atlantic and reduced to eleven starving spectres, resolve to sacrifice one of their number so the rest may live.

By January 23rd only eleven remained, and the end of the tragedy was rapidly approaching (SC2-050-001). Lots were to be drawn, and to each would be assigned his share of the body of the one ordained by fate to be the victim (SC2-053-001).

## What Happens

The eleven names were placed in a hat, and it was unanimously agreed that the last name drawn should be the victim (SC2-053-003).

When the question arose of who would draw the names, M. Letourneur stepped forward and volunteered—a father's devotion in self-sacrifice leading him to undertake the office (SC2-053-004).

One by one the names were called: Burke, Flaypole, the boatswain, Falsten, Curtis, Sandon, then Miss Herbey seventh, the narrator eighth, and the ninth was Letourneur (SC2-053-008).

Asked which Letourneur, the old man answered, "Andre" (SC2-053-010). With one cry his son fell back senseless, and only two names now remained in the hat—those of Dowlas and of M. Letourneur himself (SC2-053-011).

## Significance

The narrator understood it all: the devoted father, having nothing more to give, had given his life for his son, tearing up the true last paper so that his own name would stand as the victim (SC2-054-001).

Just as the boatswain was about to act the butcher and Dowlas stood hatchet in hand, Miss Herbey advanced and pleaded for one more day's reprieve (SC2-054-003). The sailors relented—"Very well, we will wait till daybreak tomorrow," said the boatswain, and threw down his hatchet (SC2-054-007).

But when morning came on January 27th, the sun rose upon a desert ocean, and as the hour of execution drew near all hope seemed to fade (SC2-055-002). In the struggle to save M. Letourneur the narrator was hurled into the sea—and found, to his astonishment, that the water was fresh (SC2-055-020).

The blow had not been struck, and so the victim had not yet fallen (SC2-056-003); the fresh water meant land, for they had drifted near the mouth of the [[Amazon]], whose current freshens the ocean twenty miles from shore (SC2-056-010).

## Participants & Setting

- M. Letourneur, an elderly passenger, is the pivot of the drama—volunteering to draw the lots and secretly substituting his own name for his son's, offering his arms to the famished sailors to buy one more day.

- Andre Letourneur, his crippled son, whose name is truly drawn, faints at the verdict and later throws himself upon his father's executioners.

- Miss Herbey, Robert Curtis the captain, Falsten, and the boatswain make up the eleven; Herbey's merciful plea for delay proves the castaways' salvation.

- A few hours after sighting land the raft was off Cape Magoari on the Island of Marajo, whence fishermen conveyed the survivors to Para (SC2-057-007). Of the thirty-two souls who had left Charleston aboard the *Chancellor*, only eleven survived (SC2-057-009).
