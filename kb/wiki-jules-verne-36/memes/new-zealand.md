## Overview

New Zealand is the two-island British possession of the South Pacific, reached from Australia across an arm of the sea. (SC-027-070) In In Search of the Castaways it is the last stage of the search along the thirty-seventh parallel, a Maori country the travellers enter with dread.

## In the Narrative

Debating where the shipwreck document points, the party at first agrees it cannot apply to New Zealand. (SC-027-072) Later, of three vessels in the roadstead one is loading for Auckland, capital of the northern island of New Zealand. (SC-046-008)

The passage is only five or six days, the distance about a thousand miles. (SC-046-009) It becomes a natural opportunity of visiting the shores of New Zealand. (SC-046-010)

## Geography & Features

For all its remoteness the colony is modern: it has electric telegraphs and universal exhibitions. (SC-037-022)

In the reckoning of empire it is set down plainly — Australia belonging to the English, New Zealand belonging to the English, Tasmania belonging to the English. (SC-038-063) News of the bandit Ben Joyce reaches the party through a copy of the Australian and New Zealand Gazette. (SC-043-013)
