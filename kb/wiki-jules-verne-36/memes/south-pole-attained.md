# The South Pole Attained

> **Note:** Inline PN citations for this page use a 4-character work code (`TTLU`); they render as plain text pending RFC-0003. The underlying PN data is valid.

## Overview

The South Pole Attained is the great polar set-piece of *[[twenty-thousand-leagues|Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea]]s*, in which [[captain-nemo|Captain Nemo]] carries the [[nautilus|Nautilus]] to the geographic South Pole.

Standing on the ice, he declares, "In 1868, on this 21st day of March, I myself, Captain Nemo, have reached the South Pole at 90 degrees, and I hereby claim this entire part of the globe" (TTLU-038-094).

## What Happens

Going along the 55th meridian, the [[nautilus|Nautilus]] cut the Antarctic Circle on March 16 near eight o'clock in the morning (TTLU-037-013). Asked his aim, the captain replies coolly that it is "the Antarctic pole, that unknown spot crossed by every meridian on the globe" (TTLU-037-053).

To fix the moment of arrival, [[captain-nemo|Nemo]] answers, "I use only my chronometer. At noon tomorrow, March 21, if, after accounting for refraction, the sun's disk is cut exactly in half by the northern horizon, that will mean I'm at the South Pole" (TTLU-038-074). When the observation is complete the narrator cries, "The South Pole!" (TTLU-038-091).

The captain then makes his formal claim over one-sixth of the globe (TTLU-038-094), and so saying, [[captain-nemo|Captain Nemo]] unfurled a black flag bearing a gold "N" on its quartered bunting (TTLU-038-097).

## Significance

To overcome all the obstacles around the South Pole — even more unattainable than the North Pole, which still hadn't been reached by the boldest navigators — was framed as an absolutely insane undertaking (TTLU-037-054), so its accomplishment marks the summit of the captain's defiance of every earthly limit.

The ordeal is not costless: long afterward the narrator recalls "that grueling ordeal under the Ice Bank at the South Pole" (TTLU-042-008) as the voyage's hardest trial.

The planted flag becomes the emblem of the whole episode. When [[captain-nemo|Nemo]] later raises his colors again, it is "a black flag, like the one he had left planted at the South Pole" (TTLU-045-044), binding this conquest to his final, vanishing gesture.
