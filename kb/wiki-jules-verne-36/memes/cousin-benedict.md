# Cousin Benedict

## Overview

Cousin Benedict is Mrs. Weldon's kinsman, named among her party at Auckland as "one of her relatives, her Cousin Benedict" (DSCF-001-013).

His whole being bends to a single study: "Cousin Benedict's life was entirely and solely consecrated to entomology" (DSCF-001-046).

## Role in the Story

When Mrs. Weldon takes passage home, "nothing more natural than for Cousin Benedict to accompany them during that passage" (DSCF-001-050), and "Cousin Benedict carried all his curious collection of insects in a special box" (DSCF-001-053).

Aboard the Pilgrim he hunts specimens obsessively, seen "examining closely the interstices of the netting, rummaging under the hen-cages, putting his hand between the seams of the deck" (DSCF-005-033). He even tries "to initiate the young novice into the mysteries of entomology" (DSCF-005-083).

Marooned in Africa, he becomes a charge who must be managed: Hercules "undertook to watch him closely... and it was agreed that he would act with Cousin Benedict as the latter would with an insect; that is, that he would catch him, if necessary, and bring him back" (DSCF-016-018).

His science proves fateful — the fly he prizes, "a fly smaller than a bee, of a dull color, streaked with yellow" (DSCF-017-113), is "the _tsetse_, that dipter picked up by Benedict" (DSCF-018-110), the very insect that betrays their true continent.

## Character & Traits

His expertise is comically narrow. He is "in no sense, a botanist, nor a mineralogist, nor a geologist" (DSCF-001-027); "as Cousin Benedict had confined himself to the study of the articulates of this class, he was only an entomologist" (DSCF-001-042).

Outside his order he is helpless — he "would not know how to distinguish an earth-worm from a medicinal leech, a sand-fly from a glans-marinus, a common spider from a false scorpion" (DSCF-001-039).

His reverence for insect life is a creed: he cites that "Sir John Franklin made a scruple of killing the smallest insect, be it a mosquito" (DSCF-005-069).

## Relationships

- Mrs. Weldon — his cousin and keeper, who pleads, "I beg you very seriously not to go far away" (DSCF-016-011).
- [[Hercules]] — the strong man charged with catching and returning the wandering naturalist.
- [[Dick Sand]] — the young captain whom Benedict tries to school in entomology.
