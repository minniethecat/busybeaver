# Maston's Calculation Error

## Overview

Maston's calculation error is the closing event of *[[Topsy-Turvy]]*, in which the failure of the Gun Club's attempt to shift the Earth's axis with a monstrous cannon-shot is traced to a single slip: the secretary J.T. Maston had dropped three zeros from the Earth's circumference (TT-020-034).

## What Happens

On their return to the United States, President Barbicane and Captain Nicholl pressed J.T. Maston to explain the fiasco. His iron hook pressed to his forehead, the secretary could only conclude that the powder had failed to give the projectile its planned velocity (TT-020-007) (TT-020-017). The thrice-repeated confirmation of his figures had fallen on him like masses of stone (TT-020-014).

A few days later a paragraph signed by the engineer Alcide Pierdeux appeared in the *Times* (TT-020-039). Pierdeux showed that when Maston took the Earth's circumference as the basis of his calculations, he had written 40,000 metres in place of 40,000,000 metres (TT-020-034).

This forgetting of three zeros had produced a change of twelve naughts at the end of the calculation (TT-020-036). The shock had therefore displaced the North Pole by only three-thousandths of a millimetre, and the projectile itself became a small planet of the solar system (TT-020-038).

## Significance

This want of attention had wrecked the gigantic operation and made Barbicane & Co. the memorable laughing-stock of the world (TT-020-031) (TT-020-040). Yet the same mistake had spared the world a terrible catastrophe, and a flood of telegrams arrived congratulating Maston on his error of three naughts (TT-020-041) (TT-020-042).

Maston himself at last remembered how the slip had occurred. Shut up in his Ballistic Cottage, he had written 40,000,000 on his blackboard when a storm sent a stroke of lightning through the telephone and knocked him down (TT-020-045).

Rising to rewrite the half-erased figures, he was interrupted a second time and set down only 40,000, forgetting the last three naughts—a disaster he could not but lay to the interruption (TT-020-046).

## Participants & Setting

- J.T. Maston, secretary of the Gun Club, whose single slip of three zeros in the Earth's circumference doomed the operation; dishonoured yet congratulated, he faced the long years of a hundred-year life ashamed of his mistake (TT-020-047).

- President Barbicane and Captain Nicholl, directors of the North Polar association, who return to demand an explanation and conclude that it was not their science but experience they had lacked (TT-020-027).

- Alcide Pierdeux, the French engineer who alone detected the missing three zeros in time, kept his composure amid the general fright, and published the true explanation of the fiasco (TT-020-044).
