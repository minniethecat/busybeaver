# Ker Karraje

## Overview

His origins are traced by the captive narrator: "I have now reason to believe that Ker Karraje is a Malay" (FF-010-053), a man who, "after spending a few years on the Australian goldfields," made the acquaintance of his accomplices "and managed to seize a ship in the port of Melbourne" (FF-010-054).

His piracies once terrorized the Pacific until "this series of crimes came to an end, and no more was heard of Ker Karraje" (FF-010-057) — for he had vanished behind a new and respectable mask.

## Role in the Story

That mask is a wealthy nobleman: "no one imagined that Count d'Artigas was none other than Ker Karraje, the former pirate of the Pacific" (FF-010-065), and it is under this name that he acquires a submarine, becoming "possessed of the admirable vessel which was to perform the double function of towing the schooner and attacking ships" (FF-010-076).

His whole design turns on a stolen weapon; he holds the inventor Thomas Roch in his lair, where "Roch lives in a private room in Ker Karraje's 'mansion'" and is daily sounded for his secrets (FF-011-016), for the narrator dreads "how much more so will he become if he ever obtains possession of Roch's fulgurator" (FF-010-084).

His reign ends in fire: with his band "scattered on the rocks of the island" (FF-018-017), the explosion that destroys Back Cup sweeps him away, so that "Ker Karraje and his pirates have disappeared" (FF-018-029).

## Character & Traits

His villainy is cold and self-assured; confronted with his true name, he answers only that "the Count d'Artigas is Ker Karraje... just as Warder Gaydon is Engineer Simon Hart; and Ker Karraje will never restore to liberty Engineer Simon Hart, who knows his secrets" (FF-010-093).

He is vigilant to the last, seen at the climax as he and his lieutenants "advance to the extremity of the point, where they sweep the north-western horizon with their telescopes" (FF-017-028).

His single ambition — to arm himself with the fulgurator — is fulfilled in the end, for "the pirate Ker Karraje was in possession of Roch's fulgurator" (FF-018-011).

## Relationships

- [[Thomas Roch]] — the deranged inventor whose fulgurator he covets, holding him captive at Back Cup to extract the secret.
- Engineer Serko — his cleverest accomplice, who proposes the submarine and manages his schemes.
- Simon Hart — the engineer he holds prisoner as "Warder Gaydon," and who unmasks his identity.

## Appearances

- [[Facing the Flag]] — as the pirate-count of Back Cup, master of a stolen submarine, destroyed with his lair when Roch fires the fulgurator at sight of the tricolor.
