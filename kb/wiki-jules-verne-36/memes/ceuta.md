## Overview

Ceuta is a fortified point on the coast of Morocco, standing opposite Gibraltar at the western gate of the Mediterranean. In the Vernean corpus it belongs chiefly to *Off on a Comet*, where it is carried into space by the comet Gallia together with Gibraltar.

It appears also in *Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Seas* as one end of the ancient land-barrier that once closed the strait.

## In the Narrative

On the night of the catastrophe the trader *Hansa* had been "at Ceuta, the point on the coast of Morocco exactly opposite Gibraltar," (OC-019-052) and Isaac Hakkabut had meant to "cruise from Ceuta to Tripoli." (OC-020-024)

Captain Servadac forms a secret design: "the annexation of Ceuta to the French dominion." (OC-041-015) Crossing the frozen strait, however, he learns that the English "had forestalled him in the occupation of Ceuta." (OC-041-045)

## Geography & Features

The little garrison at Ceuta lay "about twelve miles distant from Gibraltar," linked to it across the ice and by telegraph. (OC-041-046) Its allegiance had been quietly transferred: the Spaniards, Servadac is told, "have, by formal contract, made over Ceuta, in its integrity, to the British government." (OC-041-057)

When Gallia at last nears the earth, the surviving fragment "included Ceuta and Gibraltar, with the two English garrisons." (OC-042-078)

## Connections

Ceuta is bound above all to *Off on a Comet*, paired with Gibraltar as the twin rocks of the strait borne away by the comet and disputed between France and England. (OC-042-078)

It links to *Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Seas* through the prehistoric barrier "between Gibraltar and Ceuta" that once "closed off the Mediterranean completely." (TTLU-031-021)
