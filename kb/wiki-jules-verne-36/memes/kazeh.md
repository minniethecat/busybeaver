## Overview

Kazeh is an important establishment in the interior of Central Africa, described in *Five Weeks in a Balloon* as a central rendezvous for traders and caravans. (FWB-004-027) It is reached by Dr. Ferguson's balloon during its passage across the continent, floating over the town in magnificent weather. (FWB-014-092)

## In the Narrative

Kazeh first enters the story through the history of earlier explorers: after four months of incredible suffering, their baggage pillaged and their attendants slain, travellers arrived at Kazeh. (FWB-004-027) One party reentered Kazeh on the 20th of June (FWB-004-028) and reached it again, on the return, on the 25th of August. (FWB-004-029)

When the balloon itself arrives, about two o'clock it floats over the town of Kazeh. (FWB-014-092) The population, comprehending that their prey is slipping through their clutches, sets up prolonged howlings and aims bows and muskets at the ball. (FWB-015-078) A terrified sorcerer, dropping from the balloon, scampers off at a furious pace toward Kazeh. (FWB-015-092)

## Geography & Features

Kazeh lies about three hundred and fifty miles from the coast (FWB-014-092) and stands roughly one hundred miles from the point where the travellers first sight it, an important establishment in the interior of Africa. (FWB-014-091) It is not a city; in truth, there are no cities in the interior of this region. (FWB-015-002)

Around its excavations are numerous native dwellings, wide open spaces for the markets, fields of cannabis and datura, and superb trees with depths of freshest shade. (FWB-015-005) The place is governed by a Sultan of Kazeh (FWB-016-003) and counts Arab merchants among its inhabitants, better informed than the rest. (FWB-015-016)

## Connections

Kazeh belongs to *Five Weeks in a Balloon* as a central rendezvous for traders and caravans on the routes of the African interior. (FWB-004-027) It marks a waypoint on Captain Speke's exploration, who ascends eastward in a straight line above Kazeh toward Lake Ukereoue. (FWB-017-008) From Kazeh the earlier expedition took up the route back to Zanzibar. (FWB-004-029)
