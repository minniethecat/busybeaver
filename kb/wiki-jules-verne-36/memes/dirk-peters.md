# Dirk Peters

## Overview

Dirk Peters is "a half-breed named Dirk Peters," the very man "whom Captain Len Guy had gone to look for in Illinois" (AM-005-026) — for Edgar Poe "stated explicitly that Dirk Peters would be able to furnish information relating to the non-communicated chapters, and that he lived at Illinois" (AM-004-046).

In Poe's earlier romance he was Arthur Pym's fellow-mutineer aboard the _Grampus_, the man who "rushed upon the others, seconded by Augustus Barnard, Arthur Pym, and the dog Tiger" (AM-005-043).

## Role in the Story

Through most of the voyage he hides in plain sight as the silent sailor Hunt, who "distinguished himself by his activity" (AM-013-026) and, by agreement, "went on in front, as it had been agreed that he was to be our guide" (AM-015-015). It is Hunt who finds the pass "through which Arthur Pym and Dirk Peters had crossed the reef" years before (AM-016-017).

The disguise cannot hold. The narrator at last records that "Hunt was the half-breed Dirk Peters, the devoted companion of Arthur Pym," the man Captain Guy had so long sought (AM-017-132); Peters declares himself plainly — "He had said, 'I am Dirk Peters.' He was Dirk Peters" (AM-017-139).

Why he "had hidden himself in the Falklands under the name of Hunt" (AM-017-135) becomes the question that Jeorling presses upon him directly, asking "what it was that induced you to reveal it" (AM-018-127).

## Character & Traits

He is a creature of immense physical force and few words. When his search bears fruit he "suddenly uttered a cry, or rather a sort of savage growl, and held out his enormous hand" (AM-016-054).

His whole being bends southward toward his lost comrade: he casts "such a lightning glance from his brilliant hawk-like eyes" toward the southern horizon (AM-015-031), and aboard ship his "gaze never quitted the horizon of that southern zone for an instant" (AM-015-042).

## Relationships
