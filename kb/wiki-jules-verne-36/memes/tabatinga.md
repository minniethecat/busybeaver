## Overview

Tabatinga is the first Brazilian town on the left bank of the Amazon, standing on the frontier at the entrance of the river's Brazilian reach. (EHLA-012-012)

Its population is estimated at four hundred, nearly all Indians, among them wandering families never long settled at any one spot. (EHLA-012-015)

## In the Narrative

It is on the evening of the 25th of June that the jangada halts before Tabatinga. (EHLA-012-012) The town marks the Brazilian frontier: between Tabatinga and the source of the Amazon the difference of level scarcely exceeds a decimeter per league. (EHLA-010-007)

Fragoso tells Joam that he means to say good-bye and remain at Tabatinga (EHLA-012-031), though he later has less reason to linger there than at Ega. (EHLA-016-003)

## Geography & Features

The frontier post that once stood on the Island of the Ronde, a little above Tabatinga, has been abandoned and transferred to the town itself. (EHLA-012-016)

Tabatinga lies more than six hundred leagues from the Atlantic, so far inland that the influence of ordinary tides is not perceptible there. (EHLA-012-022) Its charming environs open at the mouth of the Javary. (EHLA-012-020)
