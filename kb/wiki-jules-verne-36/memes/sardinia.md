## Overview

Sardinia is the great Mediterranean island south of Corsica, swept away in the comet's passage. Madalena, close to Caprera north of Sardinia, had entirely disappeared in the disaster. (OC-018-023) Where the Strait of Bonifacio had been, a vast expanse of water now spread. (OC-018-012)

## In the Narrative

The island lingers in the memory of the comet's castaways. Little Nina had lost all wish to return with her pet goat to the barren rocks of Sardinia. (OC-031-005) The count related to them all that had happened since the memorable night of the thirty-first of December. (OC-028-075)

## Geography & Features

Sardinia's name reached even to distant Africa. In 1855 Brun-Rollet, consul for Sardinia in Eastern Soudan, set out from Khartoum trading in gums and ivory. (FWB-004-017) To the north the island once looked across the Strait of Bonifacio toward Corsica. (OC-018-012)

## Connections

Sardinia lies south of [[Corsica]] in the [[Mediterranean]], the two once divided by the Strait of Bonifacio. (OC-018-012) It vanishes in the catastrophe of [[Off on a Comet]]. (OC-018-023)
