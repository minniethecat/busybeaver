## Overview

The Guamini is a river and salt-lake district of the Argentine Pampas, lying near the Sierra Ventana, which Lord Glenarvan's party crossed while following the 37th parallel in In Search of the Castaways. Lake Salinas ends the string of lagoons connected with the Sierras Ventana and Guamini. (SC-018-001)

Paganel knew the country intimately. The Major told him he was too well acquainted with the 37th parallel and the river Guamini and the whole Pampas for the others to let him leave the reserve corps. (SC-018-015)

## In the Narrative

The march toward the Guamini was a race against thirst. Eighty miles south lay the Sierra Ventana, toward which the travelers might have to betake themselves should the Guamini disappoint their hopes. (SC-018-025) Thalcave said nothing, thinking that it would be time enough to despair if the Guamini should be dried up. (SC-018-054)

Most fortunately for them, the Guamini ran not far off, and about seven in the morning the little troop reached the inclosure on its banks. (SC-020-001) The water of the Guamini greatly aided digestion apparently. (SC-020-004)

## Geography & Features

The Guamini lies at the end of a chain of lagoons: Lake Salinas ends the string connected with the Sierras Ventana and Guamini. (SC-018-001) Its banks were rich with game, for the banks of the Guamini seemed to be the general rendezvous of all the game in the surrounding plains. (SC-018-075)

The river ran quietly through the plain. The waters of the Guamini ran silently, like a sheet of oil over a surface of marble. (SC-019-001)

## Connections

The Guamini marked a turn in the climate of the journey. From the time of leaving the Guamini, there was a marked change in the temperature, to the great relief of the travelers. (SC-020-007)

It was a landmark in Paganel's geographical learning, the river he knew as well as the whole Pampas along the 37th parallel. (SC-018-015) For Thalcave the Patagonian, it was the hope on which the parched march turned, the water that would decide whether the party pressed on or despaired. (SC-018-054)
