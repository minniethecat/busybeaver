## Overview

Pleasant Garden is a village of western North Carolina lying near the Great Eyrie, whose huge rounded form is distinctly seen from Morganton on the Catawba River, and still more clearly as one approaches the mountains by way of the village. (MW-001-004)

From Morganton a road wound along the left bank of the Catawba River and led to the village of Pleasant Garden, where a traveller might be comfortably lodged for the night with the mayor of the town. (MW-003-002) There the visitors were housed by the mayor, a particular friend of Mr. Smith. (MW-003-007)

## In the Narrative

When the wind blew the smoky cloud eastward toward Pleasant Garden, a few cinders and ashes drifted down upon it. (MW-001-009) Those more immediately threatened were the citizens of Morganton, and even more the good folk of Pleasant Garden and the hamlets and farms yet closer to the mountain. (MW-001-010)

On the night of the fourth of April the good folk of Pleasant Garden were awakened by a sudden uproar. (MW-001-017) Should the [[Great Eyrie]] prove indeed a volcano, many thousands would be in peril, for the convulsions of nature might extend to Pleasant Garden and to [[Morganton]]. (MW-002-039)

## Geography & Features

The village commanded a clear prospect of the heights, for from Pleasant Garden one could see the entire crest of the mountain. (MW-003-010) A carriage might draw up before the house of the Mayor of Pleasant Garden, where travellers passed the night, (MW-003-076) then make an early start if they wished to get back to Pleasant Garden by nightfall. (MW-003-070)

Its air carried the forest odors of the region, recalled far away by the scent of a superb cypress that brought to mind the woods of Morganton and Pleasant Garden. (MW-011-060)

## Connections

Pleasant Garden was bound to [[Morganton]] by road and by fellowship, for the mayor of Pleasant Garden was a reliable man and a friend of the mayor of Morganton. (MW-005-017) The two towns shared every alarm of the [[Great Eyrie]], the flames above the crest and the noises which had so frightened the people of Pleasant Garden and Morganton. (MW-015-041)

Whenever the heights stirred again, the good folk of Morganton and Pleasant Garden would believe that the crater had reopened. (MW-017-029)
