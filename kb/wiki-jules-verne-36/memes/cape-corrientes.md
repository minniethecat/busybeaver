# Cape Corrientes

## Overview

Cape Corrientes is a real headland on the Argentine coast that serves as a rendezvous and waypoint for the yacht *Duncan* in *In Search of the Castaways*. It marks one end of her patrol: John Mangles reports that "the DUNCAN is to cruise between Corrientes and Cape Saint Antonie." (SC-010-046)

The cape frames the yacht's watch along the eastern shore of the continent.

## In the Narrative

The cape is the scene of the *Duncan*'s ordeal by weather. "At Cape Corrientes they encountered the terrible storm which had handled the travelers across the pampas so roughly, but the yacht had borne it bravely." (SC-027-031) Having weathered it, she stood out to sea to rejoin the overland party.

Its landmark is used to reckon the yacht's later course — "five days after losing sight of Cape Corrientes, on the 16th of November, they fell in with fine westerly breezes." (SC-028-011)

## Geography & Features

The cape lies on the pampas coast where woodland gives way to open plain: "the native trees are only found on the edge of long prairies and about Cape Corrientes." (SC-026-012) It stands opposite the far side of the searchers' great circle of the globe, so that the run from "Cape Bernouilli from Cape Corrientes" measures the span between Australia and America along the 37th parallel. (SC-028-001)

The headland reappears near the story's close as a remembered landfall — the yacht recalled "a month ago, when they crossed Cape Corrientes." (SC-045-049)
