# Harris

## Overview

He presents himself to the shipwrecked party as a genial countryman, declaring "my name is Harris and I was born in South Carolina" (DSCF-015-058).

He claims a home far from where they stand, saying "I live in the South, on the Chilian frontier; but at this present moment I am going to Atacama, in the northeast" (DSCF-015-060) — the first thread of a long deceit.

## Role in the Story

He wins their trust by offering hospitality and a mount, promising "a horse which I count on offering to Mrs. Weldon and her son" (DSCF-015-090) to lead them to the Farm of San Felice.

He walks armed at the head of the column, where "Dick Sand and Harris, both armed, one with his long gun, the other with a Remington, kept at the head of the little troop" (DSCF-016-004).

When the deception nears collapse he flees; the pursuing Dick found that "Harris was no longer there, and his horse had disappeared with him" (DSCF-018-105), and the truth breaks out that "the Portuguese Negoro, and the American Harris, must be in collusion" (DSCF-018-112).

## Character & Traits

He masters the party's suspicion with ready lies, turning aside a shot at the last instant so that "the weapon had been rapidly turned aside by Harris, and Dick Sand, skilful as he was, had missed his aim" (DSCF-017-051).

He passes off African giraffes as harmless game, asking "are not those animals more likely to be ostriches?" (DSCF-017-058) to keep the true continent hidden.

His slaver's eye betrays him even in friendship, appraising a companion with "you are a strong companion, Hercules... in the markets of Africa you would be worth a good price" (DSCF-015-117).

## Relationships

- [[Dick Sand]] — the young captain he deceives and who at last kills him, for the natives recalled that "it was here that the white man killed our comrade Harris" (DSCF-029-043).

- [[Negoro]] — his fellow conspirator, with whom he sits scheming, boasting "it is even astonishing that I have succeeded in leading him a hundred miles at least from the coast" (DSCF-020-007).

- [[Hercules]] — the strong black companion whom he coldly prices as merchandise for "the markets of Africa" (DSCF-015-117).
