## Overview

*The Adventures of a Special Correspondent* is Jules Verne's comic railway odyssey across Asia, narrated by the reporter Claudius Bombarnac. His paper wires him orders: "Claudius Bombarnac will repair to Uzun Ada, a port on the east coast of the Caspian" (ASC-001-007).

From there he is to "take the train by the direct Grand Transasiatic between the European frontier and the capital of the Celestial Empire" (ASC-001-007).

## Plot Summary

Bombarnac boards the Grand Transasiatic bound for Peking, hunting stories among his fellow travelers, for "either one is a correspondent or one is not!" (ASC-001-035). His richest discovery is a secret: hidden in a packing case in the baggage van rides the young Roumanian Kinko, a stowaway crossing Asia to rejoin his sweetheart (ASC-015-003).

Kinko travels in his box to reach "Mademoiselle Zinca Klork, evidently the most lovely of Roumanians," who "is expecting you at Pekin" (ASC-010-057). The correspondent, learning the secret, keeps it and befriends the fugitive: "I must do nothing that might get Kinko into trouble" (ASC-014-015).

Danger comes with a treasure train of "fifteen millions of gold and precious stones," which the Mongol grandee Faruskiar and his confederates plot to seize by wrecking the train (ASC-025-010). Kinko saves the passengers at the cost of his own life: "Kinko has paid with his life for the safety of his fellow passengers" (ASC-025-004).

## Main Characters

- Claudius Bombarnac — the reporter of the *Twentieth Century*, narrator of the journey
- Kinko — the Roumanian who crosses Asia hidden in a packing case
- Zinca Klork — Kinko's sweetheart, awaiting him at Peking
- Faruskiar — the Mongol grandee who plots to wreck the train for its treasure

## Key Places & Technology

- The Grand Transasiatic railway — the line from the Caspian to Peking that frames the voyage
- Kinko's packing case — the box in which the stowaway secretly travels

## Themes

Verne turns the newly-linked railways of Asia into a moving stage for a gallery of national types, seen through a journalist's restless eye. Beneath the travelogue and satire runs a tender romance — the poor stowaway's devotion — that redeems the mercenary bustle of the modern express.
