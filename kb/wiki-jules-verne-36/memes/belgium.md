## Overview

Belgium is the small Western European kingdom whose open-handedness shamed larger states. Among the second order of nations she distinguished herself by a gift of 513,000 francs to the Gun Club, about a penny and a fraction for each inhabitant. (FEM-012-019) Her rail lines carry travellers on: one fugitive crossed Holland and Belgium to reach Brussels and the train for Paris. (WC-021-029)

## In the Narrative

Belgium recurs in Verne's catalogues of the learned and the ancient. Its grottoes at Han-sur-Lesse are reckoned among the great caverns of the world, larger than many in England or Piedmont. (FF-009-019) In the grey soil of certain Belgian caves were found human maxillaries and tools of the earliest nations. (JCE-038-007)

## Geography & Features

Belgium's name is bound up with the industrial history of coal and the military history of the air. Scholars traced the French word for coal to a farrier said to have lived in Belgium. (UC-004-005) It was during a campaign with Belgium, in the year that opened the military career of balloons, that their first service was rendered. (VB-001-102)

## Connections

Belgium's gift swelled the international subscription to the [[Gun Club]] venture. (FEM-012-019) Its railways link the Continental road that runs on through Holland and [[Sweden]]. (WC-021-029)
