# The Snæfellsjökull Descent

## Overview

The Snæfellsjökull Descent is the threshold moment of *[[journey-to-the-center-of-the-earth|Journey to the Center of the Earth]]*, where the explorers commit themselves to the volcanic chimney that will lead them underground. For [[axel|Axel]] it is a bottomless pit into which he is about to take a plunge (JCE-017-003), and the supreme hour had come (JCE-017-003) to begin the journey in earnest.

## What Happens

Standing at the crater's edge, [[axel|Axel]] feels the terror of the abyss: there was nothing more treacherous than this attraction down deep abysses (JCE-017-004), the vertigo that seems to pull the traveller into the void. Overcoming it, the party arranges its method of descent.

They fasten a long rope so that each could descend holding both halves of the rope (JCE-017-006), lowering themselves in stages down the chimney's inner wall, then drawing the rope after them for the next drop.

## Significance

The descent marks the true beginning of the underground voyage that gives the novel its name; everything before it is preparation, and everything after unfolds beneath the earth. That [[axel|Axel]] experiences the crater as a bottomless pit into which he is about to take a plunge (JCE-017-003) captures the dread the descent demands, while the rope method by which each could descend holding both halves of the rope (JCE-017-006) shows the disciplined technique that makes the plunge survivable.
