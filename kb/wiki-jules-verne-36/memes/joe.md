# Joe

## Overview

He is the doctor's man, for "Dr. Ferguson had a servant who answered with alacrity to the name of Joe" (FWB-006-002).

He completes the trio of the venture, since "if Ferguson was the head and Kennedy the arm, Joe was to be the right hand of the expedition" (FWB-006-006).

## Role in the Story

He is unshakeably loyal, declaring that "Joe will always stick to the doctor!" whatever the folly of the scheme (FWB-006-020).

He does the deft work aloft, and "thanks to a skilful manoeuvre achieved by Joe, the anchor was disengaged, and Joe reascended to the car" (FWB-013-008).

He shares the labour of the watch, taking "the three o'clock morning watch" while his companions slept (FWB-012-071).

He rises with his masters at the launch, when "Ferguson, Kennedy, and Joe, waved a last good-by to their friends" (FWB-011-041).

## Character & Traits

He is astonishingly nimble, so agile that for a professorship of gymnastics "Joe would certainly have received the appointment" (FWB-006-005).

He is quick and sure of limb, "slipping nimbly down the tree" to make fast the anchor (FWB-013-054).

He is universally liked, for "every body liked him for his frankness and good-humor" (FWB-009-004).

He brims with high spirits, "fairly dancing and breaking out in laughable remarks" at the news of departure (FWB-008-008).

He is happy in the humblest service, "as proud and happy as a prince" among his friends (FWB-011-037).

## Relationships

- [[Samuel Ferguson]] — the doctor whose servant he is and whom he reveres, for such was "what a man the doctor was in the eyes of this worthy Joe!" (FWB-006-003).
- [[Dick Kennedy]] — the sportsman beside whom he hunts, sent off by the doctor who bade him "take two guns--one for yourself and one for Joe" (FWB-013-055).

## Appearances

- [[Five Weeks in a Balloon]] — the tale of the African crossing in which he serves as the expedition's right hand, even turning cook to "get you up a cup of coffee" (FWB-012-033).
