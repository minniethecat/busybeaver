# Erik

## Overview

Erik — "Erik Hersebom!" (WC-001-026) — is the adopted son of a fisher family of Noroe, on the Norwegian coast, and the gifted child at the heart of the novel.

Even his kin marvel at him; Dr. Schwaryencrona declares, "your son, Erik, Master Hersebom, is a most remarkable child" (WC-002-059).

## Role in the Story

He is in truth a foundling: it was known that "Erik believed himself to be your son, and that every one in Noroe had forgotten how he had become yours" (WC-002-071). Struck by the boy's promise — "Erik is twelve years old, nearly thirteen, and he appears to be highly gifted" (WC-003-036) — the doctor carries him off to Stockholm to be educated and to seek his origins.

The mystery proves stubborn. "Nothing had occurred to throw any light on the mystery which surrounded Erik's origin" (WC-006-030), and when he canvasses the ports, "none of them could give him any useful information about the last voyage of the 'Cynthia'" (WC-008-024).

Yet his heart is with the sea rather than the study — "I knew that the child would prefer the sea to all their books" (WC-006-017) — and the quest drives him to a polar expedition aboard the Alaska in search of the truth.

Resolution comes by letter. He learns that "you were picked up at sea about twenty-two years ago by a Norwegian fisherman in the neighborhood of Bergen; that you were tied to a buoy, bearing the name of 'Cynthia'" (WC-021-008).

He reads that "in that case you are my grandson, for whom I have mourned so many years" (WC-021-009). His restoration brings deep joy to "the good, simple-hearted beings who had saved her son's life" (WC-022-002).

## Character & Traits

He wears his gifts modestly: bidden to demonstrate, "I am at the command of the doctor," answered Erik, modestly (WC-001-033).

His learning is real and ready — questioned in botany, "Erik answered without hesitation 'that it was one of the family of umbelliferous plants,' and described them in detail" (WC-001-039). Beneath the talent runs a loyal gratitude to those who raised and helped him: "I am too much obliged to you, dear doctor, for having brought me" (WC-004-027).

## Relationships

- Dr. Schwaryencrona — the physician who recognises his gifts and takes him to Stockholm, judging him "a most remarkable child" (WC-002-059).
- Mr. Durrien — the grieving grandfather whose letter restores him: "you are my grandson, for whom I have mourned so many years" (WC-021-009).
- [[The Waif of the Cynthia]] — the novel whose whole plot is the unravelling of his origin.
