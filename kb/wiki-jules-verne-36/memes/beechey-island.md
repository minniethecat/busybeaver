# Beechey Island

## Overview

Beechey Island is the real Arctic island that serves as a way-station in *The Adventures of Captain Hatteras*. Hatteras fixes it as a goal: "I positively desire to touch at Beechey Island in order to renew my coal provision." (ACH-014-018)

On sighting it he knows it at once — "After taking an observation Hatteras recognised Beechey Island, that general meeting-place of Arctic navigators." (ACH-020-003)

## In the Narrative

The brig strains toward it through the ice. The crew waits "so as not to miss Beechey Island, using a great deal of coal." (ACH-020-002) The mutinous Shandon warns of the stakes: "if he doesn't reach Beechey Island he's a lost man!" (ACH-018-028)

Hatteras counts on the depots left by earlier ships, for "the last ship which anchored at Beechey Island before the *Forward* was the *Fox*; McClintock revictualled there the 11th of August, 1858, and repaired the habitations and magazines." (ACH-020-003)

## Geography & Features

The island is the fixed rendezvous of the whole search for Franklin. McClintock "found traces of Franklin's wintering on Beechey Island in 1845." (ACH-014-027) The Admiralty treated it "as the general rendezvous of expeditions." (ACH-014-032)

Its rocks bear meagre life. The doctor "gathered some plants... some heaths, a few lichens, a sort of yellow ranunculus," growing where the snow had withdrawn. (ACH-020-056) Lady Franklin's memorial tablet was raised there, given "to McClintock to be raised on Beechey Island." (ACH-020-025)
