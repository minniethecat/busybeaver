## Overview

California is the Pacific-coast American state whose capital is San Francisco, the scene of *Godfrey Morgan*. (GM-001-006) Its crowds are drawn from Americans of Utah, Oregon, and California, with a sixth part Frenchmen. (GM-001-004) Subject to the direct action of the polar currents, its first weeks of February resemble the last weeks of March in Central Europe. (GM-001-007)

## In the Narrative

The state made the fortune that drives the story: it was in developing the early placer-mining enterprises in California that William W. Kolderup laid the solid foundations of his incalculable fortune. (GM-002-005)

Never had it occurred to his nephew Godfrey that one day he would leave San Francisco, California, America, to roam the seas. (GM-004-042) The tale's prejudices are its own: in saying there were too many Chinese in California — more than 50,000 in the State alone — Godfrey held the same language as every true Californian. (GM-006-041)

## Geography & Features

Its cities and wilds fill the background: Stockton is one of the most important cities in California, the rival of Sacramento. (GM-002-037) Its fauna resembles that of Lower California, the Bay of Monterey, and New Mexico. (GM-010-027)

Above all it is a land of giant trees: on the edge of a vast prairie rose gigantic trees that could bear comparison with the forests of California (GM-011-031), and it is in those forests that the pursuit of the grizzly is fraught with the greatest danger. (GM-018-075)
