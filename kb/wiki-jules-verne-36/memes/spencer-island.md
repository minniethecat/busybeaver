# Spencer Island

## Overview

Spencer Island is the fictional Pacific island on which Godfrey Morgan is marooned in *Godfrey Morgan*. Purchased at auction by the millionaire William Kolderup, it "lies in the west-south-west of the Bay of San Francisco, about 460 miles from the Californian coast, in 32° 15' north latitude, and 145° 18' west longitude." (GM-001-028)

Cast away there without knowing its name, Godfrey christens it for his betrothed: "I'll call it Phina Island, in memory of her I ought never to have left." (GM-010-062)

## In the Narrative

Under the name Phina Island the castaway makes it his kingdom, surveying it from a height where it "lay unrolled like a relief-map at his feet." (GM-013-054) It lies utterly beyond the shipping lanes — "It seemed that Phina Island was situated out of the way of all the itineraries of commerce." (GM-012-050)

Its true identity is disclosed only at the story's end, when Kolderup corrects him: "You should say Spencer Island!" (GM-022-017) The whole ordeal proves a test staged on the island Kolderup owned.

## Geography & Features

The island sits at the centre of a vast, still eddy of the Pacific — "It is in the centre of this enormous eddy, which has hardly an appreciable movement, that Spencer Island is situated," rising "above the waters like the isolated summit of one of the submarine mountains of the Pacific." (GM-001-029)

It proves a benign refuge: Godfrey concludes "Phina Island has no dangerous animals, neither wild beasts, snakes, nor saurians!" (GM-015-026), while "Game abounded in the woods of Phina Island." (GM-015-013)
