## Overview

Chili is the western South American republic where Glenarvan's overland search begins in *In Search of the Castaways* — Verne's spelling of Chile. Nothing of importance had occurred hitherto in the passage through Chili, but all the obstacles of a mountain journey were about to crowd on the travelers now. (SC-012-001)

## In the Narrative

The expedition's plan is drawn on a map. Glenarvan unrolls a map of Chili and the Argentine provinces and spreads it on the table (SC-010-032), proposing to make a stride across the narrow strip of Chili, and over the Cordilleras of the Andes, into the heart of the Pampas. (SC-010-033)

The blunder that sets the tale in motion is nautical — to mistake a ship and be sailing for Chili when you meant to go to India is a blunder indeed. (SC-007-027) In thirty days the party crosses Chili, the Cordilleras, the Pampas, and the Argentine plains. (SC-026-020)

## Geography & Features

Its coast runs the length of the continent — that long coast line which extends from Peru to Chili. (DSCF-013-047) It is a land of catastrophe: one of those cataclysms frequent in Chili, in the very region where Copiapo had been twice destroyed and Santiago four times laid in ruins in fourteen years. (SC-013-073)

Its chief port receives the ocean traffic: the *Pilgrim* would go to Valparaiso, in Chili, to effect her unloading. (DSCF-001-017)
