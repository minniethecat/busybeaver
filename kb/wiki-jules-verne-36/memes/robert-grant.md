# Robert Grant

## Overview

Robert Grant is the younger child of the lost captain — "Mary and Robert were the captain's only children" (SC-004-003). Still a boy when the search begins, he throws himself into the expedition with a devotion beyond his years, sustained by faith that his father lives.

## Role in the Story

When Glenarvan crosses the pampas on horseback, he is "delighted not to leave Robert behind" (SC-018-013), and the boy rides so well that the Patagonian Thalcave, watching him, spurs on the others while "he sat motionless in the saddle, but often turned his head to look at Robert" (SC-018-026).

Robert answers the guide's praise with a grin — "He wants me to be a sailor" (SC-018-032) — and thinks first of his father: "how he will thank you for saving his life" (SC-018-034).

His greatest peril comes when a condor snatches him aloft: "It was a human body the condor had in his claws, dangling in the air, and apparently lifeless-- it was Robert Grant" (SC-014-055). A marksman's shot fells the bird, and "when they reached the spot the bird was dead, and the body of Robert was quite concealed beneath his mighty wings" (SC-014-061).

## Character & Traits

His courage wins the Patagonian's admiration, who "pointed to Robert, and said, 'A brave!'" (SC-019-110); when Thalcave means to turn back, Robert clings to him — "'friend Thalcave, don't leave us!'" (SC-019-080).

Above all he is his father's son, vowing "to search, and never cease searching for my father, who would never have given us up" (SC-064-039), certain that "a man like my father doesn't die till he has finished his work" (SC-064-043), and swearing of Glenarvan, "He is a brother that will never forsake us, never!" (SC-064-046).

## Relationships

- [[Captain Grant]] — his lost father, at whose recovered voice Robert cries out again and again, "My father!" (SC-064-074).
- [[Mary Grant]] — his elder sister, who raised him and shares the search along the 37th parallel.
- [[Thalcave]] — the Patagonian guide who admires the boy's daring and whom Robert begs not to abandon them (SC-019-080).
