# Lake Erie

## Overview

Lake Erie is the real Great Lake on which Robur's machine is first sighted afloat in *The Master of the World*. When a strange craft appears near shore, the narrator asks: "Was this the mysterious 'Terror' which had thus risen from the depths of Lake Erie?" (MW-011-027)

The pursuers judge it a fitting lair — "it was a very probable place for her." (MW-011-049)

## In the Narrative

Strock and his companions give chase along the water. "We drove rapidly along the shore of Lake Erie, toward the southwest." (MW-011-021) Once aboard as a captive, he watches the vessel run its length: "The craft continued headed to the northeast, following the longer axis of Lake Erie." (MW-013-034)

He fears he is thought drowned — "must not they think that I had perished in the waters of Lake Erie?" (MW-013-005) The machine bears steadily on, certain "before the day closed, the 'Terror' must reach the end of Lake Erie." (MW-013-058)

## Geography & Features

Aronnax-like, Strock recites its measure: "The surface of Lake Erie covers about ten thousand square miles." (MW-011-022) It is shallow enough to freeze: "The greatest known depth of Lake Erie is over one hundred and thirty feet. Hence Lake Erie is sometimes frozen over from shore to shore." (MW-011-023)

Its dimensions leave him no landmark — "Lake Erie is two hundred and twenty miles long, and over fifty wide." (MW-013-015) At its northeast end "Lake Erie has no other outlet than the Niagara River, by which it empties into Lake Ontario." (MW-013-035)
