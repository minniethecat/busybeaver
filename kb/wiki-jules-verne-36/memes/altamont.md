# Altamont

## Overview

He is the American captain of the wrecked Porpoise, and "Hatteras had the men, but Altamont had the ship, and it was hard to say whose was the better right" (ACH-039-006).

He glories in his vessel's reach, sighing "yes, my ship went further than any other has ever ventured" (ACH-037-063), for "Altamont and his crew had left her two months previously, taking the long boat with them on a sledge" (ACH-035-004).

## Role in the Story

He is found near death on the ice, where "Altamont still lived, but he was in a state of complete insensibility" (ACH-032-040), and the Doctor returns "bringing with him Altamont lying on the sledge, wrapped up in the folds of the tent" (ACH-033-063).

As he revives he signals the tally of his lost crew, and "when he got to fifteen, Altamont made a sign to stop" (ACH-034-094); thereafter "Altamont was fast regaining strength" (ACH-039-002).

## Character & Traits

He is fiercely proud of his nation's claim, insisting "this continent belongs to me" (ACH-039-051) and demanding "can you prove to me that an Englishman has set foot here before an American?" (ACH-039-055).

He presses the debt owed to him, retorting "without me and my ship, where would you all be at this moment?" (ACH-039-049).

He is vigorous in the hunt, and "Altamont fell upon him with his hatchet, and cut off his dorsal fins" in felling a walrus (ACH-040-059).

## Relationships

- [[Captain Hatteras]] — his English rival, with whom "there were many points of resemblance between Altamont and Hatteras, but no affinities" (ACH-039-004).

- [[Dr Clawbonny]] — the doctor who saves and tends him, whom he cheers with "Hurrah for Doctor Clawbonny" (ACH-039-023).

- [[Johnson]] — the boatswain who, with the crew, is "quite as much offended as the captain by Altamont's imperious tone" (ACH-039-056).

## Appearances

- [[The Adventures of Captain Hatteras]] — the polar expedition into which his rescue draws him, the Doctor hoping "Altamont will remember he owes his life to us" (ACH-037-081).
