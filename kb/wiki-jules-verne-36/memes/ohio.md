## Overview

Ohio is a state of the United States on the southern shore of Lake Erie. In the Vernean corpus it is above all the theatre of the pursuit in *The Master of the World*, whose narrator tracks the inventor of the Terror toward Toledo and the Ohio cities of the lake.

It appears also in *Journey to the Centre of the Earth*, where the mastodon of the underground sea recalls the fossil bones once found in the marshes of Ohio.

## In the Narrative

In *The Master of the World*, the narrator reasons that if the fugitive is taken now "it would probably be in Ohio, since Toledo is a city of that state." (MW-011-004) His train "sped during the night across West Virginia and Ohio" toward the lake. (MW-011-006)

Later the machine seems ready to "go darting away across the roads of Ohio" as an automobile once more. (MW-012-029)

In *Journey to the Centre of the Earth*, the herd of living mastodons is likened to the extinct beasts "the bones of which were found in the marshes of Ohio in 1801." (JCE-039-008)

## Geography & Features

Ohio is presented as one of the states fronting the great inland sea of Lake Erie, which lies "between Canada on one side and the States of Ohio, Pennsylvania and New York on the other." (MW-011-021) Its lakeside cities are named in turn: "Toledo in Ohio, at the west, with Cleveland and Sandusky, both Ohio cities, at the south." (MW-011-024)

Beyond the lake the state opens into the interior, where the fossil marshes once yielded the bones of mastodons. (JCE-039-008)

## Connections

Ohio is bound above all to *The Master of the World*, for it is toward Toledo, a city of Ohio, that the narrator expects the capture of the Terror's inventor. (MW-011-004) Its lakeside cities — Toledo, Cleveland, and Sandusky — line the southern shore of Lake Erie. (MW-011-024)

And it links to *Journey to the Centre of the Earth* through the mastodon, whose kind left their bones in the marshes of Ohio. (JCE-039-008)
