## Overview

Yakutsk is one of the far eastern governments of Siberia named in Michael Strogoff. Siberia is divided into several governments — those of Tobolsk, Yeniseisk, Irkutsk, Omsk, and Yakutsk. (MS-003-002) It lies at the remote edge of the Tsar's Asiatic dominion.

## In the Narrative

The name marks the extremity of the exile road. The Tsar recalls the political exiles returning from Tobolsk, from Yakutsk, from Irkutsk. (MS-003-021) In the war council, the last telegram would set in motion the troops in the governments of Yenisei, Irkutsk, and Yakutsk, as well as the Amoor and Lake Baikal. (MS-003-044)

Its garrison becomes Irkutsk's hope of relief. At the climax the Russian troops from the government of Yakutsk were advancing towards Irkutsk. (MS-031-023) The defenders hold to a firm hope: to hold out until the arrival of the Yakutsk troops. (MS-031-026)

## Geography & Features

The easternmost of the great governments of Asiatic Russia, Yakutsk stands at the end of the exile road and furnishes the relief column that marches on Irkutsk in the novel's final crisis — a name that measures the immense eastern reach of the courier's world.
