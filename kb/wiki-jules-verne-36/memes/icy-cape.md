# Icy Cape

## Overview

Icy Cape is a promontory on the western coast of Alaska, a fixed landmark against which the drift of the ice-borne Victoria Island is measured in *The Fur Country*. Verne places it precisely: "Icy Cape, a little promontory of western Alaska, and two hundred miles from the American coast." (FC-028-002)

## In the Narrative

The cape is home to an Esquimaux community whose lives run on beside the castaways' peril. "Kalumah on her return home resumed her customary occupations, and worked with the rest of her tribe at the fisheries near Icy Cape." (FC-032-026)

When the great storm strikes, cape and island suffer alike: "It was equally violent at Icy Cape and on Victoria Island, and, as the Lieutenant ascertained in taking his bearings, the latter was then not more than" a short way off. (FC-032-027) On the beach a torch is waved as a signal of rescue. (FC-032-033)

## Geography & Features

The cape stands on the open Alaskan shore, exposed to the full force of Arctic gales; "during the first days of the storm the Esquimaux of Icy Cape were confined to their huts." (FC-032-029) Its position two hundred miles from the drifting island fixes how far the ice-raft has wandered toward the American coast.
