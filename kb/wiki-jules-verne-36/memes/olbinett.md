# Olbinett

## Overview

He is the steward of the yacht Duncan, "M. Olbinett, the steward, who could only acknowledge so polite an attention by announcing that breakfast was ready" (SC-027-007).

He carries the expedition's cookery, for the rear wagon "was intended for the provisions and luggage, and M. Olbinett's portable kitchen" (SC-034-024).

## Role in the Story

His office is the table, and the study of maps proceeds "to the great annoyance of M. Olbinett, who could never get the cloth laid for meals, without disputes on the subject" (SC-009-006).

On the overland march he keeps the party fed: "the tent was pitched, and Olbinett got the supper ready" (SC-038-033), and on one occasion "M. Olbinett had quite excelled himself" (SC-036-024).

He yields his own place for a guest, the cabin "till now occupied by M. Olbinett, who vacated it for the expected guest" (SC-028-003).

## Character & Traits

He has no love of the saddle, "M. Olbinett, who did not much care for equitation, was to make room for himself among the baggage" (SC-034-025).

He keeps his dignity in the wild, announcing "breakfast is ready... with as much dignity as if he was in Malcolm Castle" (SC-058-094).

Yet he does not shirk danger, and when the wagon is attacked "Olbinett rushed to the common defense" (SC-043-005).

He is resourceful over a fire, cooking a bustard's dozen eggs "on hot cinders" when Robert finds a nest (SC-045-034).

## Relationships

- [[Lord Glenarvan]] — his master, who summons him with "Olbinett, we are going to have a turn before breakfast" (SC-006-026).

- [[Lady Helena Glenarvan]] — the mistress he serves, for whom "M. Olbinett was laying out an elaborate breakfast" beside the tent (SC-042-033).

- [[John Mangles]] — the Duncan's captain, who "stood staring at Olbinett and the stranger alternately" (SC-006-060).

## Appearances

- [[In Search of the Castaways]] — the search for Captain Grant, in which he "ranged himself on the deck, ready to execute their captain's orders" (SC-050-004).
