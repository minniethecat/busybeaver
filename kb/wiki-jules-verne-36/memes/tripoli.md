## Overview

Tripoli is a city and stretch of North African coast, the northern gateway to the Great Desert. In the Vernean corpus it is above all the departure and return point of the Saharan explorers whose routes Dr. Ferguson's balloon retraces in *Five Weeks in a Balloon*.

Its coast reappears in *Off on a Comet*, remade by the passage of the comet Gallia, and it marks a point on the proposed new meridian in *The Purchase of the North Pole*.

## In the Narrative

In *Five Weeks in a Balloon* the earlier explorers Richardson, Barth, and Overweg "arrived at Tunis and Tripoli" and pressed on to Mourzouk, capital of Fezzan. (FWB-004-007)

Setting out from the same city, one traveller reached Ngornou only "twelve months after having quitted Tripoli," (FWB-004-009) and regained Tripoli "toward the close of August, 1855," the sole survivor of his party. (FWB-004-012) Another expedition "set out from Tripoli in the month of March" for Mourzouk. (FWB-030-015)

## Geography & Features

Tripoli stands at the head of the desert routes: one line of march, Ferguson observes, "leads to Tripoli, and over the Great Desert." (FWB-037-036)

In *Off on a Comet* the surveyors debate a distant shore and conclude "it could not be the coast of Tripoli," which lay lower and further south. (OC-013-002) Later they find "a new coast had been upheaved right along in front of the coast of Tripoli," of strange geological formation. (OC-015-039)

## Connections

Tripoli is bound above all to *Five Weeks in a Balloon*, whose desert expeditions set out from and return to the city on the edge of the Sahara. (FWB-030-015)

It shares its North African coast with Tunis, the two named together as waypoints of the explorers, (FWB-004-007) and its shore is reshaped by the comet in *Off on a Comet*. (OC-015-039)
