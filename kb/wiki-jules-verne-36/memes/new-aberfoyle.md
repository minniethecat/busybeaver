# New Aberfoyle

## Overview

New Aberfoyle is the immense subterranean coalfield discovered beneath the exhausted pits of old [[Aberfoyle]] in *The Underground City*. When Simon Ford's long-suspected new vein is finally reached, the cry goes up that "the road to New Aberfoyle is open!" (UC-007-028) It is a natural cavern-world of galleries, vaults, and lakes far surpassing the mine it replaces.

## In the Narrative

The discovery transforms the fortunes of the Aberfoyle miners, who had faced destitution when the old seams gave out. James Starr and Simon Ford lead the exploration of the new field, laying rails and setting the wagons running through its depths. (UC-008-045) Within it a whole community takes root.

The miners built a village in the subsoil of the county, served by a double line of railway whose wagons were moved by hydraulic power, and gave it the rather ambitious title of Coal Town. (UC-010-004) Brick houses grew up picturesquely on the banks of the underground lake and beneath the great arches, until it became a regular settlement. (UC-010-009)

Verne stresses that the cavern was no human excavation: "Like these caves, New Aberfoyle was not the work of men, but the work of the Creator." (UC-007-033) The colony beneath the rock becomes the self-contained world in which the novel's later drama plays out.

## Geography & Features

New Aberfoyle stretches for miles beneath the Scottish hills; a natural shaft descends into its vein five miles west of the field, under the solid rock that supports Ben Lomond. (UC-011-032) Beneath its great dome lies a deep lake, comparable to the Dead Sea of the Mammoth Caves, whose transparent waters swarm with eyeless fish. (UC-010-006)

The engineer named that subterranean lake Loch Malcolm, and along its banks Coal Town was raised. (UC-010-009) Lit at full power by electric lamps, its sonorous vaults could echo with the songs of the miners, a habitable underground country sealed off from the surface world. (UC-012-048)
