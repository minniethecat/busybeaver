## Overview

The Sahara is the great African desert whose crossing haunts *Five Weeks in a Balloon* — the doctor thinks, not without secret terror, of the vast solitudes of Sahara, for there whole weeks sometimes pass without the caravans meeting a single spring of water. (FWB-024-009)

## In the Narrative

Its emptiness becomes the voyage's chief dread. As the balloon is driven west, the fear crystallizes in a single question: are we then to be driven clear across Sahara? (FWB-034-023)

Its sheer scale defines the danger — the crossing takes caravans so long because Sahara is a desert more than nine hundred miles in breadth. (FWB-038-022)

## Geography & Features

Its edges touch other worlds of exploration: on the north, the young Duveyrier was exploring Sahara, and bringing the chiefs of the Touaregs to Paris. (FWB-008-017)

It is a byword for barren immensity — an eclipse of 1861 was reckoned total for the Atlantic Ocean, the Mediterranean, and the Sahara Desert. (FC-023-042)
