# Buffalo

## Overview

Buffalo is the New York port at the eastern end of Lake Erie in *The Master of the World*, a landmark on the *Terror*'s wild run across the lake. Verne places it among the great lake-cities: "the principal cities on the borders of this great lake are Buffalo at the east, which belongs to New York State, and Toledo in Ohio, at the west." (MW-011-024)

## In the Narrative

The city is the point the pursuit steers toward. From aboard the captured craft the narrator records that "always we headed toward the northeast end of the lake, and hence toward Buffalo," (MW-014-003) though he cannot fathom "what was our captain thinking of in still heading toward the port of Buffalo!" (MW-014-014)

As they close on it the city rises from the water: "toward four o'clock... I knew we must be approaching Buffalo; and indeed, its outlines began to show some fifteen miles ahead." (MW-014-012) The port is a place of defended waters, for "there were two torpedo-destroyers at the port of Buffalo, at the other extremity of Lake Erie." (MW-011-050)

## Geography & Features

Buffalo commands the outlet where Lake Erie drains toward the Niagara. Beyond it "the hills encircling the end of Lake Erie, came within vision... forming the sort of funnel by which Lake Erie pours its waters into the channel of the Niagara river." (MW-014-013)

The great cataract lies just downstream, for "this river is barred by the famous cataract some fifteen miles beyond the important city of Buffalo." (MW-013-035)
