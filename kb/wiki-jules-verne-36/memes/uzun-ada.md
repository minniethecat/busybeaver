## Overview

Uzun Ada is a port on the east coast of the Caspian, the appointed destination to which Claudius Bombarnac was to repair to begin his assignment. (ASC-001-007) For some years the Transcaspian had been running between Uzun Ada and the Chinese frontier. (ASC-004-004)

The port was a deliberate creation. General Annenkof, the creator of the new railway, was led to found Uzun Ada, and thereby considerably shorten the crossing of the Caspian. (ASC-005-001) Its establishment on the opposite coast doubled the trade which used to pass through Baku. (ASC-003-042)

## In the Narrative

The reporter reached it by boat across the Caspian. Within an hour the vessel came in sight of Uzun Ada, and twenty-seven minutes afterward the travellers set foot in Asia. (ASC-004-089) There Bombarnac expected to meet with his other travelling companions. (ASC-004-005)

The journey began from its station. Bombarnac telegraphed the Twentieth Century by the Caspian cable that he was at his post at the Uzun Ada station, the train starting at four o'clock. (ASC-005-007) From Uzun Ada the run to Pekin would last only eleven days. (ASC-005-018)

## Geography & Features

The name itself was descriptive. It hardly took an hour to see Uzun Ada, the name of which means Long Island. (ASC-005-006) Where the line reaches the arm of the sea that separates Long Island from the continent, it crosses an embankment about 1,200 yards long. (ASC-006-004)

The surrounding country was desolate. On either side of the line ran long sandhills between which the train runs out from Uzun Ada. (ASC-006-004) A bare, arid country, clear of vegetation, extended for one hundred and fifty miles from the port. (ASC-006-006)

## Connections

The port was a young thing on the desert coast. When Skobeleff landed at the port of Mikhailov the port of Uzun Ada was not then in existence, and Annenkof afterward constructed the strategic railway. (ASC-007-020) Mikhailov itself lay a league from Uzun Ada. (ASC-006-005)

From it the great distances of the line were measured. Bokhara stood eleven hundred and seven versts from Uzun Ada, (ASC-010-073) and the Chinese frontier was crossed after a run of nearly two thousand three hundred kilometres, four days from leaving the port. (ASC-015-045)
