# Doctor Ox's Experiment — CHAPTER XII

[DOSE-012-001]IN WHICH YGÈNE, THE ASSISTANT, GIVES A REASONABLE PIECE OF ADVICE, WHICH IS EAGERLY REJECTED BY DOCTOR OX.

[DOSE-012-002]"Well, master," said Ygène next day, as he poured the pails of sulphuric acid into the troughs of the great battery.

[DOSE-012-003]"Well," resumed Doctor Ox, "was I not right? See to what not only the physical developments of a whole nation, but its morality, its dignity, its talents, its political sense, have come! It is only a question of molecules."

[DOSE-012-004]"No doubt; but--"

[DOSE-012-005]"But--"

[DOSE-012-006]"Do you not think that matters have gone far enough, and that these poor devils should not be excited beyond measure?"

[DOSE-012-007]"No, no!" cried the doctor; "no! I will go on to the end!"

[DOSE-012-008]"As you will, master; the experiment, however, seems to me conclusive, and I think it time to--"

[DOSE-012-009]"To--"

[DOSE-012-010]"To close the valve."

[DOSE-012-011]"You'd better!" cried Doctor Ox. "If you attempt it, I'll throttle you!"
