## Overview

Bennet Islet is a small island of the far southern seas in *An Antarctic Mystery*, about a league in circumference, named in honour of the partner of the owner of the Jane. (AM-005-054) Wild and barren, it was the land first announced to the sailors as the Halbrane pressed south. (AM-019-005)

## In the Narrative

Captain Len Guy declared his intention to make for Bennet Islet. (AM-014-006) He meant to put in there for twenty-four hours, the islet lying fifty miles nearer than Tsalal. (AM-014-011) On the morning of the 23rd of December the Halbrane put off from it again, bearing away fresh testimony to the catastrophe that Tsalal Island had witnessed. (AM-015-035)

## Geography & Features

The islet is uninhabited: no huts stood upon its coast, and no smoke rose to betray any dwelling. (AM-015-002) In Captain Guy's plan it was the first landfall to be reached beyond the ice-wall, before the schooner should press on toward Tsalal Island. (AM-011-013)

## Connections

Bennet Islet is bound to the tragedy of the Jane: a plank left where that schooner had once cast anchor still lay untouched, so that it could be concluded no other crew had set foot upon the islet since. (AM-015-018)
