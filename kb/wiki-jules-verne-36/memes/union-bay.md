# Union Bay

## Overview

Union Bay is the broad bay indenting the eastern coast of Lincoln Island, fronting the colonists' dwelling in *The Mysterious Island*. It is among the first features the castaways name: Cyrus Harding proposes "to that vast bay on the east the name of Union Bay, for example." (MI-011-061)

At once "they named the two bays and the mountain, Union Bay, Washington Bay, and Mount Franklin, as the engineer had suggested." (MI-011-062)

## In the Narrative

The bay orients the colonists' entire sense of the island. Harding fixes the compass points "by the height and position of the sun, which placed Union Bay and Prospect Heights to the east." (MI-011-077) It becomes a landmark of return: whichever route the explorers take, they are "certain to arrive at Prospect Heights, and, consequently, at Union Bay!" (MI-012-013)

The bay is framed by two headlands. Its northern limit is "the point which closed Union Bay, and which had received the name of Cape South Mandible" (MI-014-037), and as the canoe voyages south "the two Mandible Capes appeared to move, and surround Union Bay more closely." (MI-024-015)

## Geography & Features

Union Bay opens wide to the sea, offering little shelter. In "this part of Union Bay, which nothing sheltered from the open sea, not even a sandbank, ships beaten by the east winds would have found no shelter." (MI-021-017) Seen from the heights, "all Union Bay was spread before them." (MI-019-040)

It is spanned between the two Mandible Capes, the northern and southern jaws of the eastern coast, and lies directly below Prospect Heights and Granite House, making it the daily prospect of the colony.
