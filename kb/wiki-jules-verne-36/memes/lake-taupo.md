# Lake Taupo

## Overview

Lake Taupo is the real New Zealand lake, high among the mountains of the North Island, where Glenarvan's party is held prisoner by the Maori. Paganel first grasps their destination "for hearing the word 'Taupo' repeatedly uttered between the chief and his warriors." (SC-054-026)

It is "a lake celebrated in geographical annals, and lying in the most mountainous part of the island." (SC-054-026)

## In the Narrative

The captives are led to its shores and imprisoned above them. As "the sun was sinking beyond Lake Taupo, behind the peaks of Tuhahua and Pukepapu, the captives were conducted back to their prison." (SC-057-001) Climbing to escape, "soon they had reached a height of 500 feet above Lake Taupo, and the cold of the morning... was very keen." (SC-057-082)

From the heights the whole basin lies before them — "over Lake Taupo, which stretched toward the west in its setting of picturesque mountains." (SC-058-005) Their flight is measured from it: "the distance that separated Lake Taupo from the Bay of Plenty... about a hundred miles." (SC-059-048)

## Geography & Features

The lake lies high and cold: "the wondrous lake of Taupo, lying 1,250 feet above the level of the sea, and in view of an amphitheater of mountains 2,400 feet high." (SC-055-002)

Its valleys are a Maori stronghold — at nightfall "not a solitary native remained at the foot of the mountain, and when darkness set in over the Taupo valleys, not a fire indicated the presence of the Maories." (SC-059-049)

- Jacques Paganel — the geographer who names the lake from the Maori's speech
