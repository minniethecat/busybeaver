## Overview

Mongolia is the vast desert country of Central Asia through which the Grand Transasiatic railway runs. Its products crowd the bazaars beside those of Persia, China, Turkey and Siberia. (ASC-001-043) Yet the deserts of Mongolia are lawless: there are no juries there as yet, which the correspondent thought a good thing for the Mongols. (ASC-021-001)

## In the Narrative

The name carries a whiff of danger along the great railway. Travellers asked whether scoundrels did not prowl about Mongolia and Northern China. (ASC-008-041) The bandit Ki-Tsang's presence was reported in that part of Mongolia served by the Grand Transasiatic. (ASC-008-054)

## Geography & Features

Mongolia is divided from its southern neighbour by the ruin of the Great Wall. Of that frontier built between Mongolia and China there remain only blocks of granite and red quartzite, a terrace of bricks and a few old cannons. (ASC-024-004) In a fantastical redrawing of the globe, a whole section of Asia from the Black Sea eastward fell under one scheme. (TT-015-018)

## Connections

Mongolia is walled off from [[China]] by the granite ruin of the old frontier. (ASC-024-004) Its bazaars trade with the caravans of [[Siberia]]. (ASC-001-043)
