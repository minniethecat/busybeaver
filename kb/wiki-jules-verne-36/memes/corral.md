## Overview

The corral was the livestock enclosure the castaways built on Lincoln Island in The Mysterious Island, later becoming the home of Ayrton. It had been decided that a corral should be established near the sources of the Red Creek, at the foot of Mount Franklin, to contain the ruminants whose presence would have been troublesome at Granite House. (MI-030-026)

The perimeter of the corral was traced by the engineer, who then had the trees felled for its palisade. (MI-030-029) The construction did not take less than three weeks, for besides the palisade, Cyrus Harding built large sheds in which the animals could take shelter. (MI-030-030)

## In the Narrative

Once the corral was finished, a raid had to be made on the pastures frequented by the ruminants. (MI-030-031) Of a hundred musmons surrounded, thirty animals and ten wild goats were gradually driven back towards the corral, through its open door. (MI-030-034)

The flock prospered under shelter. In the corral the musmons increased, and several lambs already bleated in the sheds. (MI-031-011) The corral was kept largely supplied with stores, so that daily visits were not required, though it was decided that never a week should pass without someone going to it. (MI-033-024)

## Geography & Features

The corral lay four or five miles from Granite House, an enclosure for the colony's domesticated animals. (MI-039-018) Its distance was later measured exactly when wires were stretched over the five miles that separated the corral from Granite House. (MI-040-036)

Sheltered less exposed than the plateau and partly screened by Mount Franklin, the corral received only the remains of the hurricanes that struck the coast. (MI-042-076) Harding intended in time to build a farm there. (MI-038-077)

## Connections

When Ayrton asked to live apart, it was agreed that a wooden house should be built at the corral, made as comfortable as possible. (MI-039-026) Ayrton took possession of the habitation that had been prepared for him. (MI-040-025)

To end his isolation, Harding resolved to put the corral in instantaneous communication with Granite House. (MI-040-028) Once the telegraph was working, Harding sent the current through the wire and asked if all was well at the corral, receiving a reply from Ayrton. (MI-040-045)

The road from the corral ran along the left bank of the Mercy to the mouth of Falls River. (MI-040-052)
