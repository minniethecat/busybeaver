## Overview

Turkey is the Ottoman realm that recurs across Verne's voyages. In *Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea*, when Crete rose against its tyrannical rulers, the Ottoman Empire of Turkey, the *Nautilus* moved unseen through the revolt. (TTLU-030-072)

## In the Narrative

The nation figures in the great inquiry into the sea-monster — after inquiries in England, France, Russia, Prussia, Spain, Italy, America, and even Turkey, the hypothesis of an underwater Monitor was ultimately rejected. (TTLU-002-010)

It also lends to science: Turkey behaved generously, being personally interested in the business, for the moon rules the course of her years and her Ramadan fast. (FEM-012-018)

## Geography & Features

It marks the edge of continents — whether the underground travelers were under Turkey or the Atlantic depended very much on the direction they had moved. (JCE-036-058) It spans a great arc, for the northern hemisphere's first section would take in Europe from Turkey to Greenland. (TT-015-017)

Jidda was an important financial center for Egypt, Syria, Turkey, and the East Indies (TTLU-029-006), and its coasts once nourished the tuna prized by the ancient inhabitants of Turkey and Italy. (TTLU-031-010)
