# Captain Hatteras

## Overview

Captain Hatteras is the monomaniacal explorer of *[[the-adventures-of-captain-hatteras|The Adventures of Captain Hatteras]]*, a man consumed by a single ambition. He conceals his identity until, aboard the mutinous *[[the-forward|Forward]]*, he reveals himself with the declaration, "I am Captain Hatteras!" (ACH-012-041). From that moment the expedition belongs wholly to him and to the goal that governs his life: to attain the Pole was the aim of his life (ACH-012-056).

## Role in the Story

Hatteras commands the voyage toward the northernmost point of the earth, and his authority is absolute even in the face of mutiny. When he reveals himself he remains calm and haughty, and quietly leaves the deck (ACH-012-044), unmoved by the crew's revolt. His purpose never wavers across the ordeal of ice and cold, and the novel closes with the purpose of his life attained (ACH-056-004) at the Pole itself.

## Character & Traits

Hatteras's character is fused with his nationality and his fixed idea. He is proud of being an Englishman (ACH-012-047), and declares of any capable man that he should like to be an Englishman (ACH-012-048), measuring worth by that standard. This pride, welded to his singular aim — to attain the Pole was the aim of his life (ACH-012-056) — makes him at once heroic and dangerously obsessive, driving his men toward a goal that consumes him utterly.
