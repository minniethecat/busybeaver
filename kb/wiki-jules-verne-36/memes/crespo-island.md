# Crespo Island

## Overview

Crespo Island is a small Pacific islet whose surrounding sea-floor forests Captain Nemo uses as a hunting ground in *Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Seas*. It is the destination of the *Nautilus* crew's first underwater excursion, when Nemo "invites Professor Aronnax on a hunting trip that will take place tomorrow morning in his Crespo Island forests." (TTLU-015-029)

Aronnax is puzzled that a man who has "severed all ties with the shore" should own forests at all (TTLU-015-048), until Nemo reveals the paradox: "the forests of Crespo Island are simply underwater forests!" (TTLU-016-004)

## In the Narrative

Consulting his charts, Aronnax locates the islet "in latitude 32 degrees 40' north and longitude 167 degrees 50' west," an isle discovered in 1801 by Captain Crespo and marked on old Spanish charts as Rocca de la Plata, or "Silver Rock." (TTLU-015-039) Nemo teases the professor for thinking him contradictory in proposing a hunt "in my Crespo forests." (TTLU-015-067)

The excursion takes the party across the submerged slopes: "This was the underpinning of Crespo Island. This was land." (TTLU-017-018) By the following night "the tips of Crespo Island had disappeared" as the *Nautilus* moved on. (TTLU-018-003)

The muscular sailor who later cleans the beacon glass is remembered as the man "who had gone with us to Crespo Island during our first underwater excursion." (TTLU-025-010)

## Geography & Features

The island rises as a solitary rock in the North Pacific, but its interest lies wholly beneath the waves, where dense submarine forests clothe its slopes. Aronnax recognises them at once: "It's the forest of Crespo Island," he thinks, "and I was not mistaken." (TTLU-016-038)

There the crew hunt sea-otters, an activity Aronnax later cites as acceptable — "Hunting otters in underwater forests, as we did in the forests of Crespo Island." (TTLU-026-032)

The visit becomes one of the voyage's memorable episodes: in his closing tally Aronnax lists "that hunting trip in the Crespo forests" first among the wonders of the journey, alongside the Torres Strait, the coral cemetery, and Atlantis. (TTLU-039-005)
