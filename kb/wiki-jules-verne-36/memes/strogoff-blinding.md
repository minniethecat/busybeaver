# The Blinding of Michael Strogoff

## Overview

The Blinding of Michael Strogoff is the central ordeal of *[[michael-strogoff|Michael Strogoff]]*, the Emir's cruel sentence upon the captured Tsar's courier. Taken for a spy, the unhappy man was condemned to be blinded (MS-024-041): Michael was going to be blinded in the Tartar fashion, with a hot blade passed before his eyes (MS-024-049).

## What Happens

The sentence is pronounced before the assembled camp at [[Tomsk]]. Feofar-Khan denounces the courier — "You came to see our goings out and comings in, Russian spy" (MS-024-040) — and, stretching his arm toward the prisoner, bids him "Look while you may" (MS-024-002), for sight is about to be torn from him.

In the crowd stands the one face the courier most longs to see and most dreads: Marfa Strogoff, his mother, stood before him (MS-024-044). As his gaze fixes on her, the executioner does his work, and the incandescent blade passed before Michael's eyes (MS-024-051).

## Significance

The episode carries a hidden reversal that shapes the rest of the story. A purely human phenomenon, at the same time moral and physical, had neutralized the action of the incandescent blade which the executioner had passed before his eyes (MS-034-001) — the tears wrung from him by the sight of his mother had shielded his sight.

Yet the deception is complete to all who witness it. His former companions never suspected that their traveling companion had not been put to death, but blinded by order of the Emir (MS-029-037), a belief that lets the courier pursue his mission unseen.

## Participants & Setting

- The sentence and feigned blinding of the courier Michael Strogoff at [[Tomsk]].
- Ordered by the Tartar Emir Feofar-Khan, witnessed by his mother Marfa Strogoff.
- Chronicled in *[[michael-strogoff|Michael Strogoff]]*.
