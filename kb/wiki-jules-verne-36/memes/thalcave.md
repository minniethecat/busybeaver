# Thalcave

## Overview

Thalcave is the Patagonian who guides the overland party across the pampas — "a practised guide, and one of the most intelligent of his class" (SC-015-059). Tall enough that his companions "had to make great strides to keep up with the giant Thalcave" (SC-015-060), he is also "a consummate horseman," a sight worth looking at (SC-016-003).

## Role in the Story

Thalcave leads Glenarvan's small band across a waterless country. He goes first "to beat the bushes and frighten away the cholinas," a deadly species of viper (SC-017-002), and provides for the party, flinging his bolas to capture a swift nandou (SC-018-078).

His knowledge shapes the whole search: "Thalcave has set us on the track," says Glenarvan, "and I have great confidence in him" (SC-018-042). Though his own horse could have galloped ahead to water, "Thalcave would not leave his companions behind, alone in the midst of a desert" (SC-018-056).

## Character & Traits

The Patagonian is marked by his reserve. Realizing that he, the guide, was in truth being guided, "with true Indian reserve, he maintained absolute silence" (SC-016-017).

In hardship he betrays no fear, thinking it time enough "to despair if the Guamini should be dried up-- if, indeed, the heart of an Indian can ever despair" (SC-018-054). Even his thirst he masters, drinking "very quietly, without hurrying himself, taking small gulps" (SC-018-067).

## Relationships

- [[Lord Glenarvan]] — whose search Thalcave guides, and in whom his knowledge inspires confidence (SC-018-042).
- [[Paganel]] — the geographer who interprets for him, so that "each word spoken by Thalcave was instantly translated" (SC-016-061).
- Robert Grant — the boy whom Thalcave befriends and congratulates on his courage (SC-018-027).
- Thaouka — his devoted horse, to whom "Thalcave positively talked... and Thaouka understood perfectly" (SC-018-057).
