# Morris Island

## Overview

Morris Island is the real island on the southern side of the entrance to Charleston Harbor, one of the two guardposts a blockade-runner must pass. "It is enclosed between Morris Island on the south and Sullivan Island on the north." (BR-006-014)

At the time of the Dolphin's run the island "already belonged to the Federal troops, and General Gillmore had caused batteries to be erected overlooking the harbour." (BR-006-014)

## In the Narrative

To slip past the Union guns James Playfair feints south-west, so the enemy "must have thought that the Dolphin intended to make for Morris Island Channel." (BR-006-020)

Drawing near the unknown range of guns, he "turned his rudder quickly, and the ship resumed her northerly course." (BR-006-023) The steamer "was saluted by some shot which did not reach her, from the batteries on Morris Island." (BR-006-029)

The danger is real even so: had the island been armed with "the formidable pieces of artillery which were placed there a few months later, nevertheless its guns and mortars could easily have sunk a ship like the Dolphin." (BR-009-046)

## Geography & Features

The island commands the main entrance channel. James "threaded his way through the buoys which mark the entrance of the channel, leaving behind the Charleston lighthouse, visible above Morris Island." (BR-006-035)

Its batteries and the harbor forts trade fire across the passage — "the attack was general between the batteries of Morris Island and the forts of Charleston Harbour." (BR-009-076)

A few months after the Dolphin's passage the Confederate defence opposite "fell beneath General Gillmore's three hundred-pounders on Morris Island." (BR-006-032)
