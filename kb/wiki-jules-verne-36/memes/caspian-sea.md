# Caspian Sea

## Overview

The Caspian Sea is the great inland sea dividing Transcaucasia from Central Asia, and the starting line of the trans-Asian railway journey in *The Adventures of a Special Correspondent*. The reporter Claudius Bombarnac is ordered to "repair to Uzun Ada, a port on the east coast of the Caspian," (ASC-001-007) there to board the Grand Transasiatic train.

The sea marks the boundary between the European and Asiatic legs of his route.

## In the Narrative

The Caspian frames the whole itinerary — Bombarnac schemes to travel light "on a trip from the Caspian to the Celestial Empire," (ASC-001-044) crossing the width of Asia by rail. He watches the crowd at the water's edge "from the shores of the Caspian, some taking their tickets." (ASC-001-060)

The sea also passes beneath Robur's flying ship in *Robur the Conqueror*: running along the old Turkestan coast, the *Albatross* one morning hung "about three hundred feet above the Caspian." (RC-013-026)

## Geography & Features

The sea is the hub of the region's railways. From Tiflis the eastern line "ends at Baku, where the passengers embark to cross the Caspian," (ASC-001-019) while the track skirts "the carriage road between Tiflis and the Caspian, running through Saganlong, Poily, Elisabethpol... to Baku." (ASC-002-003)

Its opening to commerce transformed the country: since "the opening of the Transcaspian railway... the number of these humped beasts of burden has sensibly diminished." (ASC-001-049)
