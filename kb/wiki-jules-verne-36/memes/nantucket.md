## Overview

Nantucket is an island off the coast of Massachusetts. In the Vernean corpus it is above all the birthplace of Arthur Gordon Pym, the hero of Edgar Poe's romance whose story *An Antarctic Mystery* continues.

Captain Len Guy fixes on the island early, asking the narrator Jeorling whether he knows it, for Nantucket is where Poe's tale begins and where the Pym family lived.

## In the Narrative

Questioning Jeorling aboard the *Halbrane*, Captain Len Guy insists that "Nantucket Island was the birthplace of Arthur Gordon Pym, the hero of your famous romance-writer Edgar Poe." (AM-003-054) The narrator concedes that Poe's romance indeed starts from Nantucket. (AM-003-055)

Because Jeorling belongs to Connecticut and has visited Nantucket Island, the captain hopes he might have known the Pym family. (AM-004-023) He presses the point, asking whether in Connecticut the narrator "personally knew the Pym family who lived in Nantucket Island." (AM-004-030)

## Geography & Features

The island is presented as a seafaring community from which long voyages set out. Arthur Gordon Pym "was born at Nantucket, where he attended the Bedford School until he was sixteen years old." (AM-005-004)

It is also a haven of return: after disaster at sea the survivors, picked up by the *Penguin* of New London, were "taken back to Nantucket." (AM-005-005) From this same port the ill-fated whaling voyage was "just thirty days out from Nantucket" when mutiny broke out. (AM-005-035)

## Connections

Nantucket is bound above all to *An Antarctic Mystery*, for it is the birthplace of Arthur Gordon Pym and the starting point of Poe's romance that the novel prolongs. (AM-003-054)

It links to Connecticut through Jeorling, whom Captain Len Guy questions precisely because, belonging to that state, the narrator had visited Nantucket and might have known the Pym family. (AM-004-023)
