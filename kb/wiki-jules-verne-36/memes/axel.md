# Axel

## Overview

Axel is the narrator of *[[journey-to-the-center-of-the-earth|Journey to the Center of the Earth]]* and the nephew of [[professor-lidenbrock|Professor Lidenbrock]]. The story opens through his eyes on the 24th of May, 1863, when his uncle rushed into the house in great agitation (JCE-001-002), and it is Axel's voice — anxious, reluctant, scientifically cautious — that carries the reader down into the earth.

## Role in the Story

Axel is drawn into the expedition against his own judgement. Once the runic cryptogram is deciphered, his uncle alone filled his thoughts (JCE-002-004), and he is swept along despite his fears. His timidity surfaces repeatedly underground: when separated from the party in the labyrinth he shuddered at the thought of being lost (JCE-019-025), and it is through his terror and wonder that the subterranean world is described.

## Character & Traits

Axel is the cautious counterweight to his uncle's headlong zeal. Where Lidenbrock reasons only from science, Axel is governed by feeling and apprehension, and his attachment to ordinary life anchors the narrative. That attachment centres on his betrothed: the picture of his pretty Virlandaise could throw him in a moment out of the world of theory and danger (JCE-003-040), and her image sustains him through the ordeal.
