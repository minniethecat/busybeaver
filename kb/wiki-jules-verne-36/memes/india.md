## Overview

India is the great reversed triangle of land, its base in the north and apex in the south, embracing fourteen hundred thousand square miles of unequally spread population. (AWED-010-002) British India properly so called embraces only seven hundred thousand square miles and some hundred to a hundred and ten millions of inhabitants. (AWED-010-003)

It is the second great stage of Phileas Fogg's wager to circle the globe.

## In the Narrative

Fogg's whole schedule hangs on the subcontinent: he calculates upon crossing India in three days, a reckoning no traveller could once have relied upon. (AWED-005-005) The detective Fix follows, reasoning that a fugitive would not be safe an hour in India, which is English soil. (AWED-006-024)

Most passengers from Brindisi are bound for India — some for Bombay, others for Calcutta by way of Bombay, now that a railway crosses the Indian peninsula. (AWED-009-002)

## Geography & Features

Formerly one travelled in India by cumbrous methods — on foot, on horseback, in palanquins or unwieldy coaches; now fast steamboats ply the Indus and Ganges, and a great railway joins the coasts. (AWED-010-004) Yet the line is not everywhere complete, and old custom endures: cats were formerly considered in India as sacred animals. (AWED-010-013)

Sir Francis Cromarty had made India his home, almost as familiar as a native with its customs, history, and character. (AWED-011-002) Beyond Callyan the branch line descends toward south-eastern India by Kandallah and Pounah. (AWED-011-004)
