## Overview

*Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen* is Jules Verne's tale of a boy forced into command at sea and then cast into the horrors of the African slave trade. Its hero, "Dick Sand--that was his name--must have been originally from the State of New York," a young novice aboard the schooner *Pilgrim* (DSCF-002-016).

Sailing with him are Mrs. Weldon, wife of the ship's owner James W. Weldon, her small son Jack, and the old servant Nan (DSCF-002-003, DSCF-002-004).

## Plot Summary

The *Pilgrim* carries Mrs. Weldon's party under Captain Hull, "an experienced whaler," along with the absent-minded entomologist Cousin Benedict and a cook, Negoro, "Portuguese by birth... and filled the humble position of cook" (DSCF-008-002, DSCF-002-011). At sea they rescue five castaways: the black men Tom, Bat, Austin, Acteon, and the giant Hercules (DSCF-038-016, DSCF-022-009).

Catastrophe strikes during a whale-hunt. When Dick reaches "the scene of the catastrophe, every living creature had disappeared," and "there was no longer a seaman on board the 'Pilgrim.' Yes, one--Dick Sand--and he was only a beginner, a young man of fifteen" (DSCF-008-112, DSCF-009-010). Negoro sneers, "A captain of fifteen years?" (DSCF-009-021).

Unknown to Dick, treachery diverts the ship: "the compass, made inaccurate by Negoro's guilty hand, henceforth only gave incorrect bearings" (DSCF-011-007). Believing he steers for the American coast, the boy is drawn instead toward Africa, where the survivors fall into the hands of slave-traders.

## Main Characters

- Dick Sand — the fifteen-year-old novice thrust into command of the *Pilgrim*
- Mrs. Weldon — the owner's wife, travelling with her son Jack and old Nan
- Negoro — the treacherous cook whose sabotage wrecks the voyage
- Hercules — the powerful black castaway rescued at sea

## Key Places & Technology

- The *Pilgrim* — the whaling schooner Dick must command after the crew is lost
- The compass — the instrument Negoro tampers with to divert the ship to Africa

## Themes

Verne turns a boy's coming-of-age at sea into an indictment of the slave trade, marching his castaways across Africa toward the mouth of the Congo. The novel pairs the perils of navigation and treachery with a moral outrage rare in his work, as courage and loyalty are tested against human cruelty.
