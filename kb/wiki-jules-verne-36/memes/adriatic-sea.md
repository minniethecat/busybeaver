## Overview

The Adriatic Sea is the arm of the [[Mediterranean]] reaching up between Italy and the Balkan coast. Its waters are marked off by their density, measured at 1.029 against 1.018 for the neighbouring Ionian Sea. (TTLU-023-021)

## In the Narrative

In A Drama in the Air the balloon is driven toward it against the aeronaut's will. It is unfortunate, he says, that we are being driven towards the Adriatic. (DA-001-114) The balloon-history twin of that scene repeats the cry that they are being wafted towards the Adriatic. (VB-001-110)

## Geography & Features

Passing by the mouth of the Adriatic Sea, the [[Nautilus]] naturalist thought he recognized two or three sperm whales of the genus Physeter and some pilot whales of the genus Globicephalus. (TTLU-031-013) In 1509 the Adriatic and the Mediterranean were frozen at Venice and Marseilles, and the Baltic on the 10th of April. (ACH-041-041)

## Connections

The Adriatic Sea opens off the [[Mediterranean]], its mouth crossed by the [[Nautilus]] on the great voyage of [[Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea]]. (TTLU-031-013) Its salt waters were weighed against those of the Ionian Sea. (TTLU-023-021)
