# The Finding of Louis Cornbutte

## Overview

The Finding of Louis Cornbutte is the climax of Jean Cornbutte's Arctic search for his lost son—the rescue party, tracking smoke and cries of distress across the ice off Shannon Island, at last reaches the castaways' snow-hut where Louis, half-dead, drags himself out into his father's arms—in *[[a-winter-amid-the-ice|A Winter Amid the Ice]]*.

The first sign was smoke. A light smoke rising in the north-east indicated the presence of human beings, and Penellan's cries of joy soon convinced the whole party that he had not been mistaken (WAI-011-024).

## What Happens

The smoke gave way to sound. Advancing toward it, the party heard feeble but perceptible sounds—cries of distress, like cries for help, twice repeated, then all was silent again (WAI-011-035).

The first castaway was no survivor. Running ahead, Penellan came upon a man lying on the ice; it was the sailor Courtois, one of the comrades of Louis Cornbutte, frozen to death (WAI-011-039).

Then the snow-hut appeared. A mile farther on they saw smoke escaping from a snow-hut closed by a wooden door; two men rushed out, and Penellan recognized one of them as Pierre Nouquet (WAI-011-045).

At last came Louis himself. A man, almost dead, dragged himself out of the hut and along the ice—it was Louis Cornbutte, so wasted that his rival Vasling had not known him (WAI-012-003).

## Significance

The reunion revived him. Louis Cornbutte fell fainting into the arms of his father and Marie, who drew him toward the hut, where their tender care soon brought him back to life (WAI-012-007).

Deliverance was mutual. "My friends, we are saved!" cried Louis, greeting the father who had exposed himself to so many perils to reach him (WAI-012-014).

A way home remained. The father told Louis that his brig, the *Jeune-Hardie*, lay securely anchored in the ice sixty leagues off, and that they would all rejoin her together (WAI-012-015).

## Participants & Setting

- Led by Jean Cornbutte and the sailor Penellan, with Marie beside them, the search party crossed the ice off Shannon Island to find the missing Louis Cornbutte and the surviving castaways of the *Jeune-Hardie*.
- They first found the sailor Courtois frozen dead, then the snow-hut where Pierre Nouquet and the emaciated Louis had wintered among the wreck of the Norwegian schooner *Froöern*.
- The rival André Vasling, who coveted Marie and had hoped Louis dead, alone refused to extend his hand; chronicled in *[[a-winter-amid-the-ice|A Winter Amid the Ice]]*.
