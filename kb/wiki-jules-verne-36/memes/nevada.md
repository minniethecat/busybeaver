## Overview

Nevada is the silver state of the American Great Basin, a country of desert ranges crossed by the Pacific Railroad in [[Around the World in Eighty Days]]. Phileas Fogg's train "entered the State of Nevada through the Carson Valley about nine o'clock, going always northeasterly; and at midday reached Reno" (AWED-026-017), pressing on toward the Great Salt Lake.

## In the Narrative

The line runs the length of the state along its great river. From Reno "the road, running along Humboldt River, passed northward for several miles by its banks; then it turned eastward, and kept by the river until it reached the Humboldt Range, nearly at the extreme eastern limit of Nevada" (AWED-026-018).

Nevada is also a byword for mineral wealth. In [[Godfrey Morgan]] a man is reckoned "richer than Senator Jones, of Nevada, who has $35,000,000 in the funds" (GM-002-003) — the silver fortunes of the Comstock country made proverbial.

## Geography & Features

Nevada is a land of silver walled off by mountains. In [[Robur the Conqueror]] the [[Albatross]] came "in Nevada over the silver regions, which the Sierra separates from the golden lands of [[California]]" (RC-010-033) — the Sierra Nevada standing as the state's western rampart.

That western wall is pierced by a single gate. The airship crossed it where the railway does, for "it was six o'clock precisely when the Sierra Nevada was crossed by the same pass as that taken by the railway" (RC-010-036), descending from the heights into the basin country.

## Connections

Nevada is the silver state that [[Phileas Fogg]] crosses by rail in [[Around the World in Eighty Days]], entered through the Carson Valley and traversed along the Humboldt River toward the [[Great Salt Lake]]. Its western wall is the Sierra Nevada, which separates it from [[California]] and is overflown by the [[Albatross]] in [[Robur the Conqueror]]. Its proverbial silver wealth echoes in [[Godfrey Morgan]]. Nevada is the desert threshold between the Pacific slope and the mountain interior.
