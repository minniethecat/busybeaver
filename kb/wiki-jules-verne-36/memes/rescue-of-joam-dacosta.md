# The Rescue of Joam Dacosta

## Overview

The rescue of Joam Dacosta is the closing event of *[[Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon]]*, in which the condemned man is saved from the gallows at the last moment when Judge Jarriquez deciphers [[The Amazon Cryptogram]] and reads aloud the confession that proves his innocence.

The order requiring immediate execution had come, and it was fixed for nine o'clock in the morning of the 31st of August (EHLA-038-002). Nothing, it seemed, could now save Joam Dacosta (EHLA-038-005).

## What Happens

On that very morning a man was approaching Manaos with all the speed his exhausted horse could give (EHLA-038-006). It was Fragoso (EHLA-038-009), the faithful barber, returning from a desperate errand. He had recognized the dead Torres as one of the captains of the woods of the Madeira, and had set out to find the man's old company (EHLA-038-013).

Not without difficulty, Fragoso had met the chief of that party (EHLA-038-015). He asked whether Torres had had an intimate friend who had recently died, and what the name of that friend had been (EHLA-038-021)—and the answer he carried back was the single word Ortega.

The mournful procession was already moving toward the gallows when the judge arrived and it halted (EHLA-039-001). Sitting down upon a stone seat, while Joam Dacosta clasped his wife to his heart, Judge Jarriquez read out, amid a profound silence, the document he had at last unravelled (EHLA-039-004).

## Significance

By means of the number derived from the name Ortega, Jarriquez had interpreted the whole of the cryptogram (EHLA-039-016). The paper declared that the real author of the diamond robbery and the murder of the escort was not Joam Dacosta, but Ortega himself (EHLA-039-011). The true criminal had confessed of his own free will to the crime for which an innocent man had been condemned (EHLA-039-015).

The reading was hardly finished when the air was rent with prolonged hurrahs (EHLA-039-012), and Joam Dacosta stood surrounded by his wife, his children, and his friends (EHLA-039-014).

Ortega, wishing to make reparation, had committed all the facts of the crime to writing (EHLA-039-023); and it was the name he had signed, brought back by Fragoso, that had at last afforded the means of reading the cryptogram (EHLA-039-026).

## Participants & Setting

- Joam Dacosta, the convict of Tijuco who had lived for years under the name Joam Garral, stands at the foot of the gallows in Manaos, condemned for a crime he did not commit until the deciphered confession absolves him.

- Judge Jarriquez, who had struggled long over the enciphered document, cracks it at the eleventh hour and reaches the place of execution in time to read Ortega's confession aloud and halt the sentence.

- Fragoso, the wandering barber whose life the Garral family had once saved, rides himself and his horse to exhaustion to bring the dead Ortega's name, the key that unlocks the cryptogram.
