## Overview

Formentera is the smallest of the Balearic Islands, in the western Mediterranean. In the Vernean corpus it belongs wholly to *Off on a Comet*, where it is the retreat of the astronomer Palmyrin Rosette and one of the few fragments of the old world carried off by the comet Gallia.

Servadac and Count Timascheff recognise the name at once "as one of the smallest of the Balearic Islands" when a scrap of the island reappears in Gallia's seas. (OC-025-001)

## In the Narrative

Determined to search the fragment, the party sets a course across the frozen waters, reckoning they would "not be more than three days in reaching Formentera." (OC-025-011)

What they find is a mere splinter: an islet less than a quarter of a mile round, judged "the sole surviving remnant of Formentera, once a member of the Balearic Archipelago." (OC-025-042) There they rescue the recluse astronomer, and by "conveying its contribution from Formentera, the known population of Gallia was raised to a total of thirty-six." (OC-027-001)

## Geography & Features

Before the catastrophe the island had been Rosette's private observatory. Leaving Paris for the Balearic Isles, "he placed his observatory on the highest point of Formentera," and lived there as a recluse with his servant Joseph. (OC-029-019)

So absorbed was he in the heavens that he clung to "the solitude of Formentera" even as the comet struck, (OC-029-039) and afterwards forever bewailed that he had "ever quitted his quarters at Formentera." (OC-038-017)

## Connections

Formentera is bound wholly to *Off on a Comet*, for it was the island observatory of Palmyrin Rosette and the last Balearic fragment reclaimed by the wanderers of Gallia. (OC-027-001)

It stands among the scattered survivors of the old Mediterranean world — Gourbi Island, Gibraltar, and Ceuta — none of which was harmed at the first concussion. (OC-042-003)
