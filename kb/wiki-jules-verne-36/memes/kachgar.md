## Overview

Kachgar is the chief city of Chinese Turkestan, standing where the Grand Transasiatic railway crosses from Russian into Chinese territory. In the Vernean corpus it belongs to *The Adventures of a Special Correspondent*, where the reporter Claudius Bombarnac reaches it as the great frontier halt of his overland journey toward Peking.

## In the Narrative

The city looms over the journey as a threshold: "Over the Pamir plateau, up to Kachgar, the road is carefully guarded; but beyond that, the Grand Transasiatic is under Chinese control." (ASC-008-032) The train "does not arrive at Kachgar without a long delay, and it is half-past four in the morning when we enter the capital of Chinese Turkestan." (ASC-015-075) It is also a place of danger for a stowaway, who fears discovery "at the Chinese frontier--or rather at Kachgar." (ASC-013-108) After the halt, "our train runs at only moderate speed since we left Kachgar." (ASC-017-007)

## Geography & Features

Like the Russian cities before it, "Kachgar is a double town": (ASC-016-007) "the new one is Yangi-Chahr; the old one, three and a half miles off, is Kachgar," (ASC-016-009) the latter "three miles round." (ASC-016-011) Once a capital, it has been reduced to "a station on the Grand Transasiatic, the junction between the Russian and Chinese lines." (ASC-016-008) It remains "an important centre of trade, which is almost entirely in Russian hands," (ASC-016-012) its people "Turkoman, with a considerable mixture of Chinese." (ASC-016-014)

## Connections

Kachgar is bound to *The Adventures of a Special Correspondent* as the pivot of the Grand Transasiatic, where "a detachment of Chinese soldiers has taken over the van at Kachgar, in relief of the Persians." (ASC-018-007) It carries the memory of great-power rivalry, for "an English embassy under Chapman and Gordon in 1873 and 1874 had been sent from Kashmir to Kachgar by way of Kothan and Yarkand," (ASC-016-013) and observers wrote that Central Asia would flourish only "when the Russians lord it at Kachgar." (ASC-016-002)
