## Overview

Krasnoiarsk is a real Eastern Siberian town on the Yenisei, the place on which Michael Strogoff pins his hopes as he nears Irkutsk. He had placed all his hopes on Krasnoiarsk, where he expected to find the means of safely finishing his journey. (MS-026-012)

## In the Narrative

The kibitka comes in sight of Krasnoiarsk at nightfall on the 25th of August. (MS-026-001) Strogoff counts on its resources: at Krasnoiarsk, of which the population is about twelve thousand souls, he depended upon obtaining some means of transport. (MS-026-004)

Fortune seems to favor him: the battle of Tomsk on the 22nd explained why the vanguard of the Emir's army had not appeared at Krasnoiarsk. (MS-026-002) The town is also a journalists' terminus — "And mine only as far as Krasnoiarsk," answers Harry Blount, satisfied with his telegraph credit. (MS-002-058) Yet the traveller finds the town deserted, and must press on toward the Yenisei crossing.

## Geography & Features

Perched on the high left bank of the Yenisei, Krasnoiarsk commands the river road between Tomsk and Irkutsk, the last sizable town before the great river barrier.
