# Aouda

> **Note:** Inline PN citations for this page use a 4-character work code (`AWED`); they render as plain text pending RFC-0003. The underlying PN data is valid.

## Overview

Aouda is the young Parsee woman whom [[phileas-fogg|Phileas Fogg]] rescues from death during his passage through India, and who becomes his companion for the remainder of the voyage. The narrative gives her history plainly — her name was Aouda, and, left an orphan, she was married against her will (AWED-013-011) — establishing her as both victim and, ultimately, Fogg's equal in feeling.

## Role in the Story

Condemned to be sacrificed on her husband's funeral pyre, Aouda is saved by Fogg and [[passepartout|Passepartout]] in a daring rescue, after which the terrors of the Pillaji forest gradually yield to safety in her mind (AWED-014-008). She travels the rest of the journey with the party, and her presence introduces into Fogg's clockwork existence the one variable he had never calculated — human attachment.

## Character & Traits

Aouda is dignified, gentle, and quietly courageous. Even her rescuers remark that she is a charming woman (AWED-014-011), yet her appeal lies less in beauty than in composure under suffering. Married against her will and orphaned young (AWED-013-011), she meets misfortune without bitterness, and it is her steady warmth that slowly draws genuine emotion from the reserved [[phileas-fogg|Phileas Fogg]].
