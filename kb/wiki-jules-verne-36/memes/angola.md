## Overview

Angola is the Portuguese colony on the western coast of Africa, a vast territory composed of three provinces — Benguela, Congo, and Angola — of which little was known then except the coast. (DSCF-019-032) In Dick Sand the shipwrecked party lands here believing itself in South America, and is drawn into the terrible interior crossed by caravans of slaves under the driver's whip. (DSCF-019-053)

## In the Narrative

The "Pilgrim" is thrown precisely on that part of the coast of Angola where the caravans that clear that part of Africa are formed. (DSCF-019-031) Despite English and French cruisers, ships loaded with slaves leave the coasts of Angola and Mozambique every year. (DSCF-004-002)

From the interior markets two directions are given to the caravans: one west toward the Portuguese colony of Angola, the other east toward Mozambique. (DSCF-019-019) It is on this coast that the traitors Harris and Negoro are brought together. (DSCF-020-003)

## Geography & Features

A pernicious climate of warm and damp lands engenders fevers; the natives are barbarous, some still cannibals, amid permanent war between tribes. (DSCF-019-033)

Eighteen years before the tale, two daring discoverers crossed Africa from east to west and arrived near Angola after unheard-of difficulties. (DSCF-019-035) Between the travels of Stanley and Cameron the province had become somewhat better known. (DSCF-019-049)
