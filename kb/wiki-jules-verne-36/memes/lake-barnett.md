# Lake Barnett

## Overview

Lake Barnett is the freshwater lake of the wandering ice-island in *The Fur Country*, christened by Lieutenant Hobson in honour of the traveller Mrs Barnett. When the river fails the colonists, the lake is named as their remaining resource: "although the river is gone, the lake remains, and we will call it Lake Barnett." (FC-022-013)

## In the Narrative

The lake becomes a fixture of the colony's precarious life on the drifting land. As the thaw comes on, "whilst the ice was disappearing from the ocean, Lake Barnett was also laying aside its slippery armour, much to the delight of the thousands of ducks and other water-fowl which began to teem upon its banks." (FC-022-037)

It is prized above all as a store of fresh water: "fortunately we have still Lake Barnett, and I don't suppose our thirsty men will drain it quite dry." (FC-022-040) Its teeming fowl also fill the larder — troops of ducks hasten "back to Lake Barnett, flying close, although they knew it not, to Mrs Joliffe's saucepan." (FC-026-013)

## Geography & Features

The lake lies among the wooded hills of the island's interior; "a good deal of timber was cut in the woods clothing the eastern slopes of Lake Barnett." (FC-028-043) When the caravan sets out, it "at first skirted along the wooded hill on the east of Lake Barnett." (FC-036-005)

Its fate is bound to the island itself. As the ground breaks up at the end, "Lake Barnett had in fact disappeared, as Paulina River had done before," (FC-044-022) swallowed with the dissolving land.
