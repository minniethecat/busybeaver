# Niagara River

## Overview

The Niagara River is the real outlet of Lake Erie, which "empties at its northeastern end into Lake Ontario by means of Niagara River and its celebrated falls." (MW-011-022) At the lake's far end "Lake Erie has no other outlet than the Niagara River." (MW-013-035)

In *The Master of the World* it becomes the trap the pursuers hope to close on Robur's machine, the *Terror*.

## In the Narrative

The narrator reasons that "if he meant to leave the lake by water, there was only the Niagara River to follow; and its Falls would be impassable, even to such a machine as this." (MW-014-004) His pursuit draws on old knowledge: "three years before, a police mission had led me to explore carefully the shores of the Niagara River, both above and below the cataract." (MW-014-007)

The *Terror* makes for the funnel where "Lake Erie pours its waters into the channel of the Niagara river," (MW-014-013) and "a little after seven o'clock the opening of the Niagara River appeared ahead." (MW-014-043)

## Geography & Features

The river "flows between New York and Canada." (MW-014-046) Between Lake Erie and the town of Niagara Falls "two islands divide the current of the river, Navy Island... and Goat Island, which separates the American and the Canadian Falls." (MW-014-047)

Below them "the irresistible rapids of the Niagara River begin, where the river bends sharply to descend toward Lake Ontario." (MW-015-006)
