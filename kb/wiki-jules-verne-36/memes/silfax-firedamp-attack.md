# Silfax's Firedamp Attack

## Overview

Silfax's Firedamp Attack is the vengeful old penman's final attempt to annihilate the colony of New Aberfoyle by igniting the mine's firedamp, the averted catastrophe that forms the climax of *[[the-underground-city|The Underground City]]*.

The threat rests on the gas. James Starr had concluded that the ghostly "fire-maidens" must have been produced by a jet of fire-damp gas which, issuing from that part of the pit, could be lighted by Silfax (UC-018-005).

## What Happens

The blow falls at the wedding. Old Silfax stood there prepared to fulfil his last dreadful threat, prepared to stop the marriage of Nell and Harry by overwhelming the entire population of the mine (UC-018-026).

His weapon is his tamed bird. His enormous owl, whose white plumage was marked with black spots, was seen hovering directly above his head, awaiting his command (UC-018-027).

Seeing Jack Ryan swim out to stop him, Silfax acts: instantly he smashed the glass of his lamp, and, snatching out the burning wick, waved it in the air (UC-018-030).

The firedamp has pooled overhead. Silfax became aware that the gas, lighter than the lower atmosphere, was accumulating far up under the dome, and at a sign from him the owl seized the lighted match (UC-018-032).

## Significance

The stakes are absolute. James Starr, in the calmness of despair, marvelled that the inevitable explosion was even for a moment delayed (UC-018-031). Another second and New Aberfoyle would be no more (UC-018-033).

The rescue turns on Nell. Recognizing Nell's voice, the owl dropped the burning match into the water and, describing a wide circle, flew downwards to alight at the maiden's feet (UC-018-035).

Foiled, the old man ends himself. Baulked in his purpose of revenge, Silfax cast himself headlong into the waters of the lake (UC-018-037).

## Participants & Setting

- The attempted firedamp explosion staged by the vengeful hermit Silfax beneath the dome of New Aberfoyle.
- Turning on his trained owl, which bore the lighted match aloft, and foiled by young Nell's call to the bird.
- Threatening Nell and Harry Ford's wedding party and the engineer James Starr; chronicled in *[[the-underground-city|The Underground City]]*.
