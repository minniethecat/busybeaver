# Peninsular and Oriental Company

## Overview

The Peninsular and Oriental Company is the British steam-navigation firm whose liners carry Phileas Fogg through the eastern stages of his voyage. Its flagship in the story is "the steamer Mongolia, belonging to the Peninsular and Oriental Company, built of iron, of two thousand eight hundred tons burden, and five hundred horse-power" (AWED-006-003).

The company runs a fleet across the eastern seas. A second of its vessels appears later — "the Rangoon--one of the Peninsular and Oriental Company's boats plying in the Chinese and Japanese seas--was a screw steamer, built of iron, weighing about seventeen hundred and seventy tons" (AWED-016-002).

Its staff identify themselves by the firm: "I am one of the agents of the Peninsular Company" (AWED-009-013).

## Role in the Story

The company's ships bind together the Suez-to-Hong-Kong portion of Fogg's race. The Mongolia is "due at eleven o'clock a.m." at Suez (AWED-006-003), and it is aboard her that the detective Fix pursues his suspect: he takes "passage on board the Mongolia, follow[s] my rogue to India" (AWED-008-059).

The line's punctuality is a plot engine. The Mongolia "has been in advance of the time required by the company's regulations, and gained the prize awarded for excess of speed" (AWED-006-006), and Fogg's party benefits when she reaches Bombay early: due "on the 22nd; she arrived on the 20th" (AWED-009-031).

Aboard the company's boats Fogg gathers the companions and pursuers who shape his journey. He plays whist with "Sir Francis Cromarty, one of Mr. Fogg's whist partners on the Mongolia" (AWED-011-002), while Fix — whom Passepartout keeps "recognising" as "his crony of the Mongolia" (AWED-016-016) — dogs the party from ship to ship.

## Members

The company appears through its agents, its ships' officers, and the travellers who cross the seas under its flag.

- The agent of the Peninsular Company — who declares "I am one of the agents of the Peninsular Company" (AWED-009-013)

- The engineer of the Mongolia — offered "a large reward... if he gets us to Bombay well in advance of time" (AWED-008-036)

- [[Phileas Fogg]] — the passenger whose journey depends on the company's liners (AWED-007-030)

- [[Fix]] — the detective who shadows Fogg aboard the Mongolia (AWED-008-060)

Passengers such as [[Sir Francis Cromarty]] (AWED-011-002) join Fogg at the company's tables, so that the liners become floating stages for the novel's intrigue.

## Activities

The company's business is scheduled ocean passenger and mail service across the eastern seas, run to strict timetable and rewarded for speed. Its regulations set the pace, and the Mongolia's early arrival earns "the prize awarded for excess of speed" (AWED-006-006).

Its vessels ply fixed routes with coaling stops: the Mongolia has "still sixteen hundred and fifty miles to traverse before reaching Bombay, and was obliged to remain four hours at Steamer Point to coal up" (AWED-009-027). Its long-hulled iron steamers "roll fearfully" in a cross swell "when the wind came from the African or Asian coast" (AWED-009-003).

The line extends beyond India into the Far East, the Rangoon carrying passengers onward through "the Chinese and Japanese seas" (AWED-016-002). Between them the company's boats provide the continuous chain of sea transport on which Fogg's wager — and the detective's chase — entirely depend.
