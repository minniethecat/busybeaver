## Overview

Hong Kong is the British colonial port that marks a decisive stage of [[phileas-fogg|Phileas Fogg]]'s circuit in *[[around-the-world-in-eighty-days|Around the World in Eighty Days]]*. It is the eastern edge of British territory on his route: "Hong Kong was the last English ground on which he would set foot" (AWED-016-008), and beyond it lie China, Japan, and America, where the detective [[fix|Fix]] loses his power to arrest.

The port is reached by steamer from Calcutta and Singapore, a scheduled waypoint on the tightly timed journey rather than a destination in itself.

## In the Narrative

Hong Kong is the hinge on which [[fix|Fix]]'s pursuit turns. Because it is the last stretch of English jurisdiction, it is here that the detective's warrant must arrive if Fogg is to be arrested at all — and here that [[fix|Fix]] schemes to delay the traveler until the paper comes.

The port is where [[passepartout|Passepartout]] is separated from his master and the group's plans nearly unravel. While ashore, [[passepartout|Passepartout]] "wandered, with his hands in his pockets, towards [[victoria-balloon|the Victoria]] port" (AWED-019-003), gazing at the harbour — the interlude during which [[fix|Fix]] contrives to strand him and hold Fogg back.

## Geography & Features

Hong Kong is drawn as a thoroughly British-made colonial city on a Chinese island. It "came into the possession of the English by the Treaty of Nankin" after the Opium War, and has "beaten Macao in the struggle for the Chinese trade" (AWED-019-002), becoming the dominant entrepôt of the coast. Its docks, hospitals, wharves, "a Gothic cathedral, a government house" and macadamised streets give it the stamp of an English town transplanted east (AWED-019-002).

Its harbour teems with world shipping. At [[victoria-balloon|the Victoria]] port lies "a confused mass of ships of all nations" (AWED-019-003), and the island — "a little Eng[land]" some thirteen hundred miles from Singapore (AWED-017-012) — functions as a crossroads of the maritime routes Fogg depends on.

## Connections

Hong Kong is one of the great waypoints on the world circuit of [[Phileas Fogg]] in *[[Around the World in Eighty Days]]*, the last British port before the Pacific crossing.
