## Overview

Wisconsin is the northern state of the American lakes, remembered in Verne as the setting of the great automobile race in [[The Master of the World]]. The contest "was to be held by the automobile Club of Wisconsin, over the roads of that state of which Madison is the capital" (MW-004-021), and it would draw the whole country's eyes.

## In the Narrative

So great was the event that the state cleared its highways. "The state authorities of Wisconsin had forbidden all other traffic between Prairie-du-chien and Milwaukee during three hours on the morning of the thirtieth of May" (MW-004-022), and along the course "there was an enormous crowd; and it was not composed only of the people of Wisconsin" (MW-004-023).

The precautions were absolute, for "the Wisconsin authorities had taken such precautions that the road would be clear except for contesting automobiles" (MW-004-043). Into this race burst the mysterious machine of the novel's title, of which it was later said, "it was he who smashed the record in the Wisconsin race" (MW-006-025).

## Geography & Features

Wisconsin's roads become a byword for open speed. Its "main highway of Wisconsin on a great race day" (MW-007-010) is invoked as the very image of a place too public to hide in.

The state belongs to the commercial empire of the lakes. In [[Robur the Conqueror]] Chicago is named the vast reservoir "into which flow the products of Indiana, Ohio, Wisconsin, Missouri, and all the States which form the western half of the Union" (RC-009-008), Wisconsin among its tributary lands.

## Connections

Wisconsin is the state whose roads stage the automobile race of [[The Master of the World]], from Prairie-du-Chien to Milwaukee under the capital at Madison, where the novel's mysterious machine first shatters every record. Its produce flows to [[Chicago]], the Queen of the West, as recorded in [[Robur the Conqueror]]. Wisconsin is Verne's arena of American speed and machinery.
