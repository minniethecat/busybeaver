## Overview

Vienna is the Austrian capital, a centre of science whose observatory disputed a sighting with Berlin's until Russia reconciled them. (RC-001-025) It was home to one of the Royal Geographical Societies, named beside London, Paris and Berlin. (FWB-001-035)

## In the Narrative

The Austrian tailor Hermann Zeitung famously travelled shut in a box from Vienna to Paris. (ASC-007-035) Among the vanished aeronauts, one had left for Hamburg and another for Vienna. (DA-001-008)

## Geography & Features

As a financial centre Vienna subscribed to the Gun Club's venture through the house of Rothschild. (FEM-012-009) It figured among the learned institutes of Petersburg, Vienna and New York that honoured Paganel. (SC-006-075)

## Connections

Vienna is a European capital whose observatory joined the international dispute over the sky. (RC-001-025) It ranks among the learned cities enumerated with Paris and New York in the honours paid to a geographer. (SC-006-075)
