## Overview

Spain is the Iberian kingdom counted among the Mediterranean lands the *Dobryna* expects to touch, gathering intelligence from Spain, France, or Italy. (OC-010-005) After the comet's shock, the survivors reckon their fragment of a world contains natives of France, Russia, Italy, Spain, and England. (OC-019-065)

Chief among them is a band of Spaniards, whose one longing is to be sent back to Spain. (OC-020-036)

## In the Narrative

Their homesickness drives the human comedy of the tale. "To Spain, do you say?" echoes Isaac Hakkabut with a hideous yell — do they expect to go back to Spain before they have paid their debts? (OC-020-038)

The catastrophe has undone all such hopes: a most wonderful event has cut them off from Spain, from France, from Italy, from every country of Europe. (OC-020-042) Near the end, gazing earthward, one cries that they are hardly twenty leagues from Spain. (OC-028-037)

## Geography & Features

The kingdom carries a history of disputed frontiers: three times — in 1727, 1779, and 1792 — France and Spain have disputed their title, but always to no purpose. (OC-015-074)

Its coast anchors the story's optics: an assistant is dispatched to a high peak on the coast of Spain to superintend a reverberator that can be seen from Formentera. (OC-029-019) And its vintages close the tale, for the wines of France and Spain are drunk in toasting their respective countries. (OC-037-021)
