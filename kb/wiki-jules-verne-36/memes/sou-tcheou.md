## Overview

Sou-Tcheou is a walled city of western China lying on the route of the Grand Transasiatic railway. In the Vernean corpus it belongs to *The Adventures of a Special Correspondent*, where it is a two-hour halt on Claudius Bombarnac's journey — a double-walled town risen again from the ruins of war and remembered above all as the place where the Great Wall of China reaches its end. (ASC-022-071)

## In the Narrative

The train halts two hours at Sou-Tcheou, its arrival timed so that the telegraph line is repaired at the same moment as the track. (ASC-022-050) The narrator's first act is to run to the telegraph office to wire Europe, (ASC-022-061) sending off his despatch under the heading "Sou-Tcheou, 25th May, 2:25 P.M." (ASC-022-063)

With only "two hours to visit Sou-Tcheou," (ASC-022-066) the correspondent makes the most of the stop, and before the train moves on he takes care to lay in a few provisions at the town. (ASC-023-002)

## Geography & Features

Sou-Tcheou is a double town — Tai-Tchen the outer city and Le-Tchen the inner — both bearing the traces of fire, half-ruined pagodas, and heaps of rubble left by war, for the place had been "taken by the Mussulmans and retaken by the Chinese." (ASC-022-068)

Yet it has recovered its life: "Sou-Tcheou has become populous again within its double wall as in the suburbs around." (ASC-022-069) Its one great curiosity is geographical — it is here that "the Great Wall of China ends." (ASC-022-071)

## Connections

Sou-Tcheou is bound to *The Adventures of a Special Correspondent* as a station of the Grand Transasiatic where the restored telegraph lets the narrator wire news to Europe. (ASC-022-050) It is remembered chiefly as the western terminus of the Great Wall, (ASC-022-071) a twice-walled town grown populous once more after the wars that passed over it. (ASC-022-069)
