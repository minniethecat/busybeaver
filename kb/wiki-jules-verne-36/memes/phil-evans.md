# Phil Evans

## Overview

He is a wealthy manufacturer — "This was Phil Evans, who was also very rich, being the manager of the Wheelton Watch Company, an important manufactory, which makes every day five hundred movements equal in every respect to the best Swiss workmanship" (RC-002-021).

Within the ballooning club he ranks just below his rival: "that is why Phil Evans was only secretary of the Weldon Institute, whereas Uncle Prudent was president" (RC-002-032).

A staunch partisan of the lighter-than-air balloon, he retorts against the heavier-than-air heresy that "the bird has no helix; that we know!" (RC-004-041).

## Role in the Story

Seized in the night, he becomes a helpless captive — "a bandage over the eyes, a gag in the mouth, a cord round the wrists, a cord round the ankles" (RC-006-002).

Freed from the prison of the Albatross, "Uncle Prudent and Phil Evans rushed out of their prison" to discover they are flying above the earth (RC-006-113).

From that hour escape is his fixed purpose: "Uncle Prudent and Phil Evans had quite made up their minds to escape" (RC-011-002).

He reads their captor clearly, warning that Robur "will only give us our liberty when it suits him, and perhaps not at all" (RC-013-035).

## Character & Traits

Where his colleague is hot, Evans is calm; even bound in the dark, "Phil Evans, regaining his coolness, managed to slacken the cord which bound his wrists" (RC-006-005).

His coolness extends to generosity — "Phil Evans did not hesitate to at once set free his rival" once his own hands were loose (RC-006-008).

Again and again he checks the president's temper, as when "Phil Evans drew away his colleague, who was about to commit some act of violence" (RC-013-015).

## Relationships

- [[Uncle Prudent]] — his club rival, whose reckless wrath he perpetually restrains, leading him off to his cabin when fury nearly chokes him (RC-013-086).
- [[Robur]] — the aviator who abducts him aboard the Albatross and holds him captive above the world.
- Frycollin — Prudent's servant, for whom Evans intercedes with Robur "to be taken on board again" (RC-013-092).

## Appearances

- [[Robur the Conqueror]] — as the composed secretary of the Weldon Institute, fellow prisoner of the Albatross and tireless architect of their escape.
