## Overview

The Wimerra is a river and district of the Province of Victoria in Australia, crossed by Lord Glenarvan's expedition in In Search of the Castaways. After a march of sixty miles in two days, the caravan reached the parish of Apsley, the first town in the Province of Victoria, in the Wimerra district. (SC-035-009)

The travellers reached the banks of the Wimerra on the 143d meridian. (SC-036-025) This rara avis of the Australian rivers soon disappeared among the windings of the Wimerra, which water the charming landscape in the most capricious manner. (SC-036-026)

## In the Narrative

The crossing of the river tested the wagon. All went well until they reached the middle of the Wimerra, but then the hollow deepened, and the water rose to the middle of the wheels. (SC-036-034) They resolved to camp on the banks of the Wimerra while Ayrton went ahead. (SC-036-041)

It was from here that the quartermaster's treachery began. The Major saw Ayrton quit the Wimerra camp to go and look for a blacksmith at the Black Point Station. (SC-037-001) At the Wimerra, Ayrton found means of communicating with one of his gang, the blacksmith of Black Point, and left traces of the journey that could be easily recognized. (SC-043-034)

## Geography & Features

The Wimerra lies in the Wimerra district of the Province of Victoria, beyond the parish of Apsley, the first town the caravan reached. (SC-035-009) It was a fordable stream: the Major later remarked that it was a pity Ayrton had not shod all their beasts when they forded the Wimerra. (SC-042-015)

Its central channel ran deep, for the hollow of the ford deepened until the water rose to the middle of the wagon wheels. (SC-036-034)

## Connections

The river became the marker of Ayrton's betrayal in the settlers' later reckoning. A glance exchanged between Ayrton and the blacksmith at the Wimerra River was one of the facts that pointed to the plot. (SC-043-015) It had been so ever since the Wimerra: the horses were dead, the poison never far off. (SC-043-018)

Ever since they left the Wimerra, the Major recalled, Ayrton's gang had been on their track and spying on them, waiting for a favorable opportunity. (SC-043-023)
