# Loch Malcolm

## Overview

Loch Malcolm is the great subterranean lake lying in the depths of New Aberfoyle, beneath the vaulted dome of the reopened Scottish coal mine in *The Underground City*. "Below the dome lay a lake of an extent to be compared to the Dead Sea of the Mammoth caves — a deep lake whose transparent waters swarmed with eyeless fish." (UC-010-006)

## In the Narrative

Around its shores rises the mining community of Coal Town: "The miners' houses, built of brick, soon grew up in a picturesque fashion; some on the banks of Loch Malcolm." (UC-010-009) It becomes the settlement's promenade, where "the two friends were walking together on the shores of Loch Malcolm." (UC-011-002)

The lake is also the stage of the story's disasters. "They perceived at once that the waters of Loch Malcolm were rising" (UC-016-005) when the mine is flooded, and at the climax it swallows the vengeful Silfax: "The waters of Loch Malcolm yielded not their prey: they closed forever over Silfax." (UC-018-039)

## Geography & Features

The loch fills the lowest bed of the vast mine, and a sudden inrush of water served only to "raise the level of Loch Malcolm a few feet." (UC-016-011) Long after the danger has passed, the mine's tame harfang still haunts the water: "at long intervals, Nell would see the creature hovering above Loch Malcolm." (UC-019-008)
