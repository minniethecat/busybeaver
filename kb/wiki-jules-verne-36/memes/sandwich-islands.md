# Sandwich Islands

## Overview

The Sandwich Islands of *An Antarctic Mystery* are the desolate southern group discovered by Cook, not the tropical isles of the same name — Captain Len Guy takes them for the *Halbrane*'s jumping-off point: "Captain Len Guy's intention was to take the Sandwich Isles for his point of departure towards the south." (AM-010-006)

## In the Narrative

The *Halbrane* runs down toward them across open water: "she headed in the direction of the Sandwich Islands, four hundred miles" off. (AM-010-026) The group had once been the seam of a whaling ground — a campaign might have been made "between the Sandwich Islands and New Georgia." (AM-009-043) From them the schooner presses deeper south, sighting "the New South Orkneys group in six days after she had sailed from the Sandwich Islands." (AM-011-001)

## Geography & Features

The islands carry Cook's name and Cook's warning: the crew recall "what Cook's opinion on the subject of the Sandwich group was when he discovered it." (AM-010-034) They mark the last fringe of the whaling world before the ice — beyond them lie only "the South Orkneys, or South Georgia." (AM-025-014)
