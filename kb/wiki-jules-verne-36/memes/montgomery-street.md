## Overview

Montgomery Street is to San Francisco what Regent Street is to London, the Boulevard des Italiens to Paris, and Broadway to New York. (AWED-025-006) It was upon this great avenue that William W. Kolderup kept his opulent mansion, to which he returned in state. (GM-003-002)

In that mansion in Montgomery Street the master of the house slept his ten hours without a break, never troubled in his slumbers. (GM-012-002)

## In the Narrative

When Kolderup came home in triumph, cheers followed him to Montgomery Street, and such was the delirious enthusiasm of the Americans that they even forgot the customary honours. (GM-002-083) On the day of departure a grand farewell breakfast was given at the mansion in Montgomery Street. (GM-005-040)

Cast away far from home, Godfrey and his tutor longed only to be back. They would not be really at home, replied the professor drily, until they were in Montgomery Street with Uncle Kolderup. (GM-020-037) Tartlet lamented that their hardships could never have happened at the uncle's place in Montgomery Street. (GM-016-061)

## Geography & Features

The street was lined with splendid and spacious stores, which exposed their wares behind broad windows. (AWED-025-006) At its upper end rose a flight of steps from which a party might watch the throng without being jostled. (AWED-025-017)

Its houses and shops drew a dense press of people, for on a day of festival the side-walks, the roadway, the horsecar rails, the shop-doors, the windows, and even the roofs were full of onlookers. (AWED-025-012)

Among its establishments were the photographers Stephenson and Co. of Montgomery Street, (GM-005-034) and the bar kept by Oakhurst. (GM-001-043) So genial was the traffic that one need not take three steps down Montgomery Street before meeting a gentleman ready to offer a light for a cigar. (GM-012-029)

## Connections

Montgomery Street stood at the heart of [[San Francisco]], and to leave it was to leave civilisation itself. Here we are not in San Francisco, Godfrey warned his companion, nor in Montgomery Street. (GM-012-030)

When funds ran short in a distant land, the castaways hoped only that Uncle Kolderup would send the cash to get them back to Montgomery Street. (GM-009-013) Even in a rude island cabin, Tartlet sighed that he would rather have a cellar if only it stood in Montgomery Street. (GM-021-015)
