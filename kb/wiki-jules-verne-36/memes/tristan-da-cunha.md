## Overview

Tristan d'Acunha is a lonely volcanic island of the South Atlantic in *An Antarctic Mystery*, lying to the south of the zone of the regular south-west winds. (AM-007-005) The schooner Halbrane neared this curious island — which may be described as the big boiler of the African seas — and put in there to take a fresh supply of water. (AM-007-001)

## In the Narrative

The Halbrane was to proceed direct to Tristan d'Acunha, where Captain Len Guy hoped to seek word of the lost men of the Jane. (AM-003-069) There Jeorling met Glass, an ex-corporal who styled himself Governor-General of the archipelago and recalled the Jane putting in eleven years earlier. (AM-008-001) The schooner had also meant to call there to discharge a cargo of tin and copper. (AM-001-039)

## Geography & Features

The island possesses not a single port. (AM-007-014) Its great volcano rises some eight thousand feet, and along the shore lie Elephant Bay, Hardy Rock, West Point, Cotton Bay, and Daly's Promontory. (AM-008-003) It lies at 37° 8' south latitude and 10° 44' west longitude. (SC-028-023) Its capital is a little village in the heart of the bay, watered by a rapid stream. (SC-028-026)

## Connections

Tristan d'Acunha served the Halbrane as her last inhabited call before she turned south for the Antarctic ice; it was there that Jeorling expected to part with the schooner and her captain for good, before he chose to sail on. (AM-004-073)
