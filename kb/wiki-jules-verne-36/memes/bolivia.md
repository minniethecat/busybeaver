## Overview

Bolivia is the South American land that Harris pretends the shipwrecked survivors have reached in *Dick Sand: A Captain at Fifteen*. He gives the most exact details about this part of Bolivia, witnessing to his great knowledge of the country. (DSCF-017-017) In this land of Bolivia, he claims, they keep no slaves. (DSCF-015-074)

## In the Narrative

The whole deception hinges on the name. Dick Sand believes they have landed on the southern part of Bolivia which borders on Chili (DSCF-015-047), and thought it would not do to hide the American's treason while letting his companions believe they were treading on the soil of Bolivia. (DSCF-021-034)

The truth is a cruel substitution: Bolivia changed to the terrible Angola, with its feverish climate and its natives still more cruel. (DSCF-023-063) It had been easy for Harris to make them take this province for lower Bolivia, to which it has really some resemblance. (DSCF-020-061)

## Geography & Features

The land is drawn as remote and deserted — that coast was that of lower Bolivia, and there was nothing astonishing in its being so deserted. (DSCF-015-051) It adjoins the provinces of Peru and Bolivia where the tempest would have carried the *Pilgrim*. (DSCF-019-052)

Its interior feeds the Amazon, whose affluents form the highways which penetrate Bolivia, Peru, and Ecuador. (EHLA-005-018) The Madeira, descending from the watershed of the Cordilleras, is a regular waterway opening into the very heart of Bolivia. (EHLA-036-023)
