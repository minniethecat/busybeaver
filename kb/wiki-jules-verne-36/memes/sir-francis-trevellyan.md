# Sir Francis Trevellyan

## Overview

He is known at first only by his baggage, which "bore the address in full: Sir Francis Trevellyan, Trevellyan Hall, Trevellyanshire" (ASC-006-071).

He is above all the man who will not talk — "the silent personage, who has not said a word all through the piece--I mean all through the journey" (ASC-026-021).

## Role in the Story

His whole commentary on the great railway is wordless disdain, for he "has nothing but looks of contempt and shrugs of the shoulder for all we have done" (ASC-007-028).

Talk of the Russian achievement galls him, and "this conversation on the Russian enterprise is not very pleasing to Sir Francis Trevellyan" (ASC-011-039).

He will not toast a rival empire, so that when the narrator drank to the Tsar and the Chinese, "Sir Francis Trevellyan abruptly left the" table (ASC-011-056).

Even under attack he keeps his English composure, taking "matters very coolly" while others gave way to fury (ASC-020-033).

## Character & Traits

His favourite eloquence is the shrug: "he merely shrugged his shoulders, as much as to say: 'What management!'" (ASC-015-069).

Amid every alarm he stays aloof, "calm and impassive in his car, utterly regardless of our efforts" (ASC-021-030).

Nothing on the line can stir him, for "Sir Francis Trevellyan was calmly seated in his place, utterly indifferent to all that happened" (ASC-025-043).

## Relationships

- [[Claudius Bombarnac]] — the narrator he snubs to the very end, for "Sir Francis Trevellyan takes a few puffs at his own cigar, and then nonchalantly throws mine on to the platform" (ASC-026-028).
- [[Caterna]] — the actor whose applause leaves him unmoved, for "the bravos lavished on Caterna had no effect on Sir Francis Trevellyan," busy with his onomatopic complaints (ASC-014-059).

## Appearances

- [[The Adventures of a Special Correspondent]] — as the narrator's silent "No. 8," the aloof Englishman "who owes me an apology and a cigar," never heard from again (ASC-027-058).
