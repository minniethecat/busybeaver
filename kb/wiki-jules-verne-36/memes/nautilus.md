# Nautilus

## Overview

The Nautilus is the submarine commanded by [[captain-nemo|Captain Nemo]] in *[[twenty-thousand-leagues|Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea]]s*. Its fittings bear a monogram with the motto *Mobilis in Mobili*. (TTLU-030-087) When the narrator first boards, he is sealed behind a riveted iron wall inside a cabin about twenty feet long by ten wide. (TTLU-008-011)

## Design & Operation

[[captain-nemo|Nemo]] describes the vessel as a very long cylinder with conical ends, shaped like a cigar, seventy metres from end to end with a maximum beam of eight metres. (TTLU-013-004) Completely submerged it displaces 1,500 cubic metres of water and weighs 1,500 metric tons. (TTLU-013-005) It is built from two hulls, one inside the other, joined by iron T-bars that give the ship the utmost rigidity. (TTLU-013-007) Both hulls are made of boilerplate steel whose relative density is 7.8 times that of water, the inner hull no less than five centimetres thick. (TTLU-013-008) Ballast tanks in the lower reaches trim the boat: opening the stopcocks floods the tanks and the vessel sinks flush with the surface. (TTLU-013-010)

Every mechanical function aboard is driven by a single force, which [[captain-nemo|Nemo]] calls powerful, obedient, swift, and effortless, the soul of his mechanical equipment: electricity. (TTLU-012-011) A pressure gauge in contact with the water reports the outside pressure on the hull and thereby the depth of the submersible. (TTLU-012-005) For navigation once the Nautilus has surfaced, the pilot relies on conventional instruments such as thermometer, barometer, compass, sextant, chronometers, and spyglasses. (TTLU-012-003)

## Role in the Story

The narrator Aronnax, taken from the sea with [[ned-land|Ned Land]] and [[conseil|Conseil]], is confined aboard; at first the captives perceive only a sheet-iron monster that rises to the surface to renew its air like a whale. (TTLU-009-009) The ship's silence and sealed iron walls leave them uncertain whether their captor will set them free or keep his secret by force. (TTLU-009-061)

## Science & Speculation

[[captain-nemo|Nemo]] grounds the vessel's buoyancy in explicit calculation. He sizes the hulls so that, afloat, the boat lies nine-tenths submerged and displaces 1,356.48 metric tons. (TTLU-013-006) Supplementary ballast tanks holding about 150.72 metric tons of water submerge it fully when flooded. (TTLU-013-010) He even accounts for the slight compressibility of water at depth, estimating an added weight of only 6.57 metric tons at 1,000 metres down. (TTLU-013-018)
