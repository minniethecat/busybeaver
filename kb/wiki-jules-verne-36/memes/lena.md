## Overview

The Lena is one of the great rivers of eastern [[Siberia]], running north to a delta on the Arctic coast. It was at the mouth of the Lena that the Vega, sailing east, parted from the last of the vessels that had accompanied her thus far. (WC-011-006)

## In the Narrative

In [[Michael Strogoff]] the Lena marks the line of the Tartar advance across Siberia. The invaders had concentrated along the upper course of the Lena. (MS-031-023) Two days before, they were on the banks of the Lena, at Kirensk, and now neither frost nor snow would keep them back. (MS-031-030)

## Geography & Features

In [[The Waif of the Cynthia]] the Lena is an Arctic landmark of Nordenskiold's voyage. After leaving the mouth of the Lena, he steered for the islands of New Siberia, but the ice about them turned him east again. (WC-016-010) The expedition ran from Cape Chelyuskin to the mouths of the [[Yenisei]] and of the Lena. (WC-016-011)

## Connections

The Lena is the easternmost of the Siberian rivers named in Verne, told beside the [[Yenisei]] as a waypoint of the Vega's passage. (WC-016-011) On the 7th of September the ship cast anchor at the mouth of the Lena. (WC-011-006)
