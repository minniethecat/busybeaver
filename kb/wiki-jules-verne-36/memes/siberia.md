## Overview

Siberia is the immense stage of Michael Strogoff — the whole Asiatic dominion the courier must cross under Tartar invasion. Asiatic Russia, or Siberia, covers a superficial area of 1,790,208 square miles, and contains nearly two millions of inhabitants. (MS-003-002) Across that expanse a single message must travel from Moscow to the far east.

## In the Narrative

Its scale defines the mission. The higher governor resides at Irkutsk, the far capital of Eastern Siberia — Strogoff's destination. (MS-003-003) Only one wire spans it: an electric telegraph more than eight thousand versts in length alone affords communication between the western and eastern frontiers of Siberia. (MS-003-005)

The land is both prison and battleground. The Tsar declares that whilst he lives, Siberia is and shall be a country whence men can return. (MS-003-019) The revolt spreads through it — after leaving Perm, Ivan Ogareff crossed the Ural mountains, entered Siberia, and penetrated the Kirghiz steppes to foment rebellion. (MS-003-042)

The danger is separation: a rising would mean the severance of Siberia, to the east of the Yenisei. (MS-003-058)

## Geography & Features

Stretching from the Ural Mountains to the Pacific, Siberia is drained by great rivers — the Obi, Yenisei, and their affluents — and threaded by a single post-road and telegraph line that Verne turns into the artery of the entire novel.
