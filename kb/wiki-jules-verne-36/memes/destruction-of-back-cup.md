# The Destruction of Back Cup

## Overview

The destruction of [[Back Cup]] is the climax of *[[Facing the Flag]]*, in which a squadron of five warships moves against the pirate Ker Karraje's island stronghold and the French inventor Thomas Roch is brought at last to face his own flag. As there was a vast cavern inside the mountain, it was decided to bring Back Cup down like the walls of a bastion by bombardment (FF-018-006).

## What Happens

Roch's fulgurator opened the battle. From behind the trestle he launched three engines that tore through the air in a long curve at a height of three hundred feet toward the leading cruiser (FF-017-052).

The engines swerved back like boomerangs, and the cruiser vanished, blown to pieces by the infinite power of Roch's fulgurator (FF-017-056).

Then the second ship raised her colours. A flag unfurled to the breeze—the tricolor, whose blue, white and red stood out luminously against the sky (FF-017-072).

The sight broke through his hatred. Facing the flag of his own country, Thomas Roch understood, and drew back, refusing to launch his engines against her (FF-017-078).

When the pirates tried to wrest the phial from him, Roch hurled it to the ground and crushed the deflagrator under his foot, disarming his own terrible weapon (FF-017-083).

## Significance

The French cruiser pressed home the attack. The Tonnant, at the risk of being blown to pieces, penetrated the danger zone half a mile and ran up her flag while manoeuvring against the island (FF-018-016).

Then came the end. A shock terrific enough to shake the sky down convulsed the mountain (FF-018-019), and where Back Cup had stood, naught remained but a heap of smoking, crumbling rocks and jagged reefs beaten by the sea (FF-018-020).

The pirate lair was annihilated, and with it Ker Karraje's band, ending the reign of terror that Roch's fulgurator had threatened to unleash upon the fleets of the world.

## Participants & Setting

- Thomas Roch, the deranged French inventor whose fulgurator had been seized by the pirates, is the pivot of the drama—destroying the first cruiser, then recovering his patriotism at the sight of the tricolor and destroying his own engines rather than fire on France.
- Ker Karraje, the pirate chief who had taken possession of Roch's fulgurator, together with Engineer Serko and Captain Spade, perishes in the annihilation of the stronghold (FF-018-011).
- Simon Hart, the engineer held captive in the cavern and chronicler of these events, is the sole survivor—one body found intact upon the reefs, still breathing, a note-book clasped in his hand (FF-018-025).
