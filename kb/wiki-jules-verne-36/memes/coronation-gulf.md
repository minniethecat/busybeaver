# Coronation Gulf

## Overview

Coronation Gulf is the great Arctic estuary of the North American coast that marks a stage in the northward march of *The Fur Country*. The narrator fixes its place: "Fort Confidence was built at the end of the 'right paw,' at least two hundred miles from Coronation Gulf, one of the numerous estuaries which irregularly indent the coast of North America." (FC-008-005)

## In the Narrative

The gulf is the object the party steers for by way of the rivers. Consulting his map, Hobson judges "it seemed best to him to descend the valley of the Coppermine, a large river which flows into" the gulf. (FC-010-007)

Reaching it, the travellers find a broken, island-strewn water. "Coronation Gulf, the large estuary dotted with the islands forming the Duke of York Archipelago, which the party had now reached, was a sheet of water with irregular banks." (FC-011-002)

## Geography & Features

The gulf commands the coastal route the expedition must keep. It was expected "that the convoy would follow the Lieutenant's example, and skirt along the coast from Coronation Gulf," (FC-022-051) tracing the shoreline north and west.

Its strategic worth to the fur trade is part of the book's opening survey of the far north, when the narrator weighs how the Company would "find access to the" open sea should its territories change hands. (FC-002-013)
