## Overview

Kerguelen is the desolate southern island group of *An Antarctic Mystery*, which geographical nomenclature fixes at 49° 45' south latitude and 69° 6' east longitude. (AM-001-003) The group is inhabited, and the small nucleus of Europeans and Americans forming its population had been augmented by the narrator Jeorling as of the 2nd of August, 1839. (AM-001-004)

## In the Narrative

Kerguelen served as a port of call for whalers and other ships, among them the Green Cormorant kept by the innkeeper Atkins. (AM-001-027) Jeorling, weary of the place, desired above all to get away from Kerguelen at the first opportunity that should offer. (AM-003-026) He was promised he would one day see it all again — Atkins, the Green Cormorant, and Kerguelen. (AM-020-080)

## Geography & Features

Kerguelen forms an archipelago whose peaks rise from the southern sea; in the Arthur Pym narrative a bottle holding a sealed letter was said to have been deposited at the foot of one of the Kerguelen peaks. (AM-004-049) The name Kerguelen is the one generally adopted for the group among rival designations. (AM-001-003)

## Connections

Kerguelen belongs to *An Antarctic Mystery* as the starting point of the schooner Halbrane, which set sail from it six and a half months before she lay stuck against an ice-mountain far to the south. (AM-020-055) The first men lost on that voyage fell after the departure from Kerguelen. (AM-019-122)
