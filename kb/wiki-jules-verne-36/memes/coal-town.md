## Overview

Coal Town is the subterranean village buried in the subsoil of the county, reached by a double line of railway whose wagons, moved by hydraulic power, plied from hour to hour, and which bore the rather ambitious title of Coal Town. (UC-010-004) Arriving there, the visitor found a place where electricity played a principal part as an agent of heat and light. (UC-010-005)

It was a young settlement of the deep mine, and its people loved it. It is needless to say that the inhabitants of Coal Town were proud of their place. (UC-010-011)

## In the Narrative

The buried village kept its own hours of rest and festival. When labor was done Coal Town rested from labor, in perfect calm, with no wind and no rain. (UC-011-003) The old overman resolved that, if the wedding took place, the miners of New Aberfoyle should have a merry-making at Coal Town they would never forget. (UC-013-062)

Yet the deep town knew peril. Cries arose from all parts of Coal Town, which was threatened by a sudden inundation. (UC-016-006) When the flood had descended to depths never yet penetrated, Coal Town was uninjured, and it was hoped none had perished. (UC-016-011)

## Geography & Features

The town lay under water it never felt. It was founded under the eastern point of Loch Katrine, to the north of the county of Stirling. (UC-010-009) Above it hung the oppressive vaulted rocky roof, so that only in leaving it did the firmament appear like an abyss. (UC-014-053)

Light in Coal Town was wholly artificial and lavish. It was throughout brilliantly lighted by the electric lamps at full power, (UC-016-002) and on the great day the electric discs which illuminated it blazed like so many suns. (UC-018-014)

## Connections

Coal Town was the heart of the underground colony and its great family of miners. Its population was that of [[New Aberfoyle]], the mining engineers and principal people gathering as friends and comrades of the old overman. (UC-018-009) When danger threatened the subterranean colony, there being no fear of treason among the dwellers of Coal Town, the peril was made known to them all. (UC-018-002)
