# J. R. Taskinar

## Overview

He enters the tale by name and city, "it was J. R. Taskinar, of Stockton" (GM-002-035).

He is a magnate of vast means, "rich, but he was more than proportionately fat" (GM-002-036).

His fortune springs from many veins, gained through "the development of the mines and speculations in wheat" and "petroleum" (GM-002-038).

## Role in the Story

His purpose at the sale is enmity, for he had learnt "that it was the intention of William W. Kolderup to acquire possession of Spencer Island" (GM-002-042).

He came prepared to contest it, entering the auction room "among the curious crowd," having "prepared his batteries" (GM-002-043).

He struck when his rival felt secure, and "woke up with the words shouted in stentorian tones" (GM-002-045).

At last his purse fails him, and "J. R. Taskinar succumbed" (GM-002-080), retiring "throwing a glance of hatred at his conqueror" (GM-002-082).

## Character & Traits

His hatred is settled and particular, for he "particularly detested William W. Kolderup" (GM-002-040).

An old defeat still rankles, "the struggle over the state elections" he "could not forgive his opponent" (GM-002-041).

In bidding he is explosive, one who "burst out like a bomb, and did not seem to require a second to think" (GM-002-060), until "J. R. Taskinar was simply on fire" (GM-002-068).

His spite outlives the auction, for later "his enemy, J. R. Taskinar, his conquered competitor," to be revenged, "had bought a cargo of wild beasts, reptiles, and other objectionable creatures" (GM-022-107).

## Relationships

- [[William W. Kolderup]] — his detested rival and conqueror at the auction, for at the height of the contest "William W. Kolderup had turned towards J. R. Taskinar" (GM-002-054).
