# Martha

## Overview

She is the housekeeper of the Lidenbrock household, named by Axel as "our good housekeeper Martha" (JCE-002-038), and counted among "the living contents" of the house on the Königstrasse (JCE-001-032).

She keeps the kitchen and the table, forever anxious over the Professor's meals, so that when he comes home early "Martha must have concluded that she was very much behindhand, for the dinner had only just been put into the oven" (JCE-001-003).

## Role in the Story

When the Professor bars the doors to labour over the runic cryptogram, she is trapped with the household, for "when our good Martha wanted to go to market, she found the door locked" (JCE-005-018), and "poor Martha had to go away unanswered" (JCE-005-011).

Her larder runs bare, since "Martha had, without thinking any harm, cleared out the larder the night before, so that now there was nothing left in the house" (JCE-005-022), until at last "Martha was set at liberty, ran off to the market, and did her part so well that in an hour afterwards my hunger was appeased" (JCE-006-003).

At the departure "Martha and the young girl, standing at the door, waved their last farewell" (JCE-007-077), and it is through her that the world learns of the venture, for "thanks to Martha's ineradicable tattling, the news that the Professor had gone to discover a way to the centre of the earth had spread over the whole civilised world" (JCE-045-008).

## Character & Traits

She is timid before her master's temper, fleeing his outbursts, for "Martha took to her heels for safety" (JCE-002-040), and again she comes "running out of her kitchen at the noise of the violent slamming of doors" (JCE-004-002).

She is a tender, devoted soul, who over the household's strange confinement "took it very seriously, and, poor woman, was very much distressed" (JCE-005-020).

She is an incorrigible gossip, whose "ineradicable tattling" carries the secret across the civilised world (JCE-045-008).

## Relationships

- [[Professor Lidenbrock]] — the exacting master she serves and dreads, at whose absence from table she marvels, "M. Liedenbrock is not at table!" (JCE-002-043).

- [[Axel]] — the nephew of the house, who shares her captivity and asks "should Martha and I be victims of a position of things in which we had not the smallest interest?" (JCE-005-019).

- [[Gräuben]] — the young girl beside whom she waves the travellers farewell (JCE-007-077), and whose joy she matches in astonishment at their return (JCE-045-005).
