## Overview

Perth is the capital of Western Australia in *[[In Search of the Castaways]]*, notorious as the seat of a great penal settlement. A castaway recalls being "abandoned on a nearly desert coast, but only forty miles from the penal settlement at Perth, the capital of Western Australia" (SC-063-059), the city fixing the colony's western edge.

It is above all a place of convicts. When a lawless band is judged, it is remarked that "this lot have come straight from Perth, and, take my word for it, they will soon be there again" (SC-037-055), the city naming the prison to which such men forever return.

## In the Narrative

Perth is the origin of the danger that dogs Glenarvan's party. The coroner's inquest attributes a crime "to the band of convicts which escaped six months ago from the Penitentiary at Perth, Western Australia" (SC-039-042), the escape from the city setting the whole peril in motion.

The escape becomes a matter of public alarm. A police notice announces "the escape of the convicts from Perth, and offering a reward for the capture of Ben Joyce" (SC-041-018), and later the tale is told again of "the escape of the convicts at Perth, and their appearance in Victoria" (SC-043-013), the city's name following the outlaws across the continent.

## Geography & Features

Perth is defined by its penitentiary above all its other features. A telltale mark is found to bear "the convict's original number at the Perth Penitentiary" (SC-046-063), the prison so central to the city that its very numbering identifies a man.

It stands as the western terminus of the convict system. The escaped band were held there "just as they were about to be transferred to Norfolk Island" (SC-039-042), the city being the mainland gaol from which the worst offenders were shipped onward to the island settlements.

## Connections
