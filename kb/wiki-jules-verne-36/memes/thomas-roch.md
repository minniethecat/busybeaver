# Thomas Roch

## Overview

Thomas Roch is "a Frenchman named Thomas Roch, forty-five years of age" (FF-001-013), confined in the American asylum of Healthful House.

Verne fixes his nature in a phrase: "Thomas Roch was an inventor--an inventor of genius" (FF-001-020), whose disorder has spared the one faculty that matters — "the demon of insanity had respected the man of science, and that in Roch's partially diseased brain the flame of genius still burned brightly" (FF-001-041).

## Role in the Story

His fame rests on a terrible engine: "His latest invention in war-engines bore the name of Roch's Fulgurator" (FF-001-021), "a sort of auto-propulsive engine ... charged with an explosive composed of new substances" (FF-001-024).

Eighteen months before, he had sought "an audience in regard to a communication that Thomas Roch desired to make" to the Secretary of the Navy (FF-001-017), for whoever held the weapon would command "the offensive and defensive superiority of his native country" (FF-001-026).

But greed and pride betrayed him — "Roch would not agree to experiment until the millions at which he valued his fulgurator had first been paid to him" (FF-001-026), his pretensions "excessive" (FF-001-022).

Rejected nation by nation, unhinged and sequestered (FF-001-040), he is abducted by pirates who would turn his genius against the world. At the crisis, ordered to fire on an approaching warship, "Thomas Roch refuses" (FF-017-081) once he learns her flag.

## Character & Traits

He is genius and madness inseparably fused, indifferent to all but his invention — "In the case of Thomas Roch this indifference was practically absolute" (FF-001-015).

His notoriety was such that even a guarded state "could not hesitate to receive the petitioner" (FF-001-019), and observers feared "lest he should die during one of his paroxysms and carry his secret with him to the grave" (FF-001-043).

Yet beneath the wreck a Frenchman endures. The sight of his country's colors breaks the spell of avarice: "Moved to his very soul at the sight of the tricolor flag, being at last conscious of the crime of lèse-patrie he was about to commit, Thomas Roch rushed through the passage to the magazine" (FF-018-028) — choosing self-destruction over betrayal.

## Relationships

- Simon Hart — the French engineer who, as the attendant "Gaydon," guards Roch hoping his secret might benefit France alone (FF-001-040).
- Ker Karraje — the pirate chief who abducts Roch to wield the Fulgurator, and against whose designs his patriotism finally revolts (FF-017-081).
- [[Facing the Flag]] — the novel whose plot turns upon his weapon and his last redemptive act.
