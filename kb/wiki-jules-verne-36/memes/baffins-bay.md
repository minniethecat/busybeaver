## Overview

Baffin's Bay is a great Arctic sea lying to the west of Greenland, reached by the straits that thread the high latitudes. In seasons when the ice broke up it lay almost free and became the haunt of numerous whalers. (ACH-010-030) When the Forward pressed north beyond the seventy-second parallel the straits were not yet sufficiently open to allow her to penetrate into the bay. (ACH-009-073)

## In the Narrative

For Hatteras and his companions the bay marked the road home to Europe. When at last they stood upon its shore, Baffin's Bay lay half frozen over, and three miles off the waves dashed noiselessly on the sharp edges of the ice-field. (ACH-058-004)

Earlier, casting about for escape, the Doctor reckoned that the bay was not more than six hundred miles distant, and they resolved to reach it by the shortest route. (ACH-057-020)

## Geography & Features

The bay forms the eastern outlet of the northern archipelago. At the end of McClintock Channel lies Melville Bay, and from thence run the straits that lead to Baffin's Bay. (ACH-018-030) So the settlers spoke of a long suite of straits which lead out of the bay toward the open sea. (ACH-006-020)

On its shores rose the Crimson Cliffs, whose strange red substance the Doctor gathered into bottles to examine at leisure. (ACH-051-041)

## Connections

The bay stood at one end of the great unsolved sea road of the north. It was maintained that the [[North-West Passage]] was still undiscovered, for no ship had ever sailed through from [[Behring Strait]] to Baffin's Bay. (ACH-047-011)

Captain Inglefield, in the little screw Isabelle, steamed up the bay as far as Victoria Point on the eightieth parallel before turning back to [[Beechey Island]]. (ACH-014-030)
