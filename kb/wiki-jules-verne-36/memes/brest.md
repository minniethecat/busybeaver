## Overview

Brest is the great French naval port on the Atlantic, the Alaska's landfall in her rescue voyage. Thirty miles off, the order was given to steer for the great French port. (WC-012-062) The next day the Alaska entered the harbor of Brest. (WC-013-002)

## In the Narrative

At Brest the ship was received with honour. The admiral and Mayor of Brest, the commander of the port, and the captains of the anchored vessels came to pay an official visit. (WC-013-003) It was only the necessity of stopping at Brest that had brought the Alaska near shore. (WC-013-032) There the traitor entered the captain's room on the pretence of examining the charts. (WC-014-035)

## Geography & Features

Brest guards the western approaches of France. Val-Féray, an old family homestead, lies half a league from Brest. (WC-022-002) From this port sailed the frigates Search and Hope, which left Brest in 1791 under Rear Admiral Bruni d'Entrecasteaux. (TTLU-019-041) In 1794 a squadron gathered at Brest under Rear Admiral Villaret de Joyeuse. (TTLU-044-054)

## Connections

Brest is the Atlantic port where the Alaska makes land in [[The Waif of the Cynthia]]. (WC-013-002) Its roadstead launched the expeditions whose relics surface in [[Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea]]. (TTLU-019-041)
