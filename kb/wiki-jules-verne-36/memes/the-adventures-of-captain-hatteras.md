## Overview

*The Adventures of Captain Hatteras* is Jules Verne's Arctic epic, chronicling a British expedition driven by one man's monomaniacal quest for the [[The North Pole|North Pole]]. It opens with the mysterious sailing of a brig — "the brig *[[the-forward|Forward]]*, Captain K. Z——, Richard Shandon mate, will start from New Prince's Docks for an unknown destination" (ACH-001-002).

The unnamed captain proves to be [[Captain Hatteras]], whose sole obsession is the Pole.

## Plot Summary

The *[[The Forward|Forward]]* sails north under sealed orders, its true captain concealed among the crew until mutiny and ice force his hand. Hatteras reveals himself and his purpose — "To the North Pole, of course; there's not the slightest doubt about that" (ACH-004-012).

Against mutiny, cold, and starvation he presses ever northward, staking everything on the goal: "If ever human foot can step upon the land of the North Pole, it shall be the foot of an Englishman" (ACH-012-041). The expedition reaches the Pole itself, where an active volcano marks the summit of the world.

## Main Characters

- [[Captain Hatteras]] — the captain consumed by his quest for the Pole
- Doctor Clawbonny — the resourceful expedition surgeon and savant

## Key Places & Technology

- [[The North Pole]] — the goal that drives the entire voyage
- [[The Forward]] — the brig built for the polar expedition

## Themes

The novel is a study of obsession carried to the edge of madness, its hero so fused with his goal that the Pole becomes his very identity. Around that fixation Verne builds a meticulous drama of Arctic survival, patriotism, and the will to be first.
