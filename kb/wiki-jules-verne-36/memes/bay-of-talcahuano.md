# Bay of Talcahuano

## Overview

The Bay of Talcahuano is a broad estuary on the coast of Chile, the point from which Lord Glenarvan's yacht launches her search for the shipwrecked Captain Grant in *In Search of the Castaways*. Verne introduces it as the voyage's true beginning: "A week after they had doubled the Cape Pilares, the Duncan steamed into the bay of Talcahuano, a magnificent estuary, twelve miles long and nine broad." (SC-010-001)

## In the Narrative

Finding no trace of the wreck by sea, "Glenarvan, however, would not allow himself to be disheartened; he went back to Talcahuano, and spared neither pains nor expense to make a thorough investigation of the whole seaboard." (SC-010-006) From here the overland party sets out along the parallel: "they marched rapidly along the winding shore of the bay of Talcahuano, in order to gain the extremity of the parallel, thirty miles south." (SC-011-006) The distance travelled is later measured from this origin: "it was twenty-two days since they left the Bay of Talcahuano, and they had gone 450 miles." (SC-020-005)

## Geography & Features

The bay serves as the Duncan's home harbour throughout the circumnavigation. Glenarvan "had left the Bay of Talcahuano on the 14th of October, and arrived on the shores of the Atlantic on the 12th of November." (SC-026-020) When the search resumes across the Pacific, John Mangles "proposed that he should steer for the Bay of Talcahuano, where the Duncan had once before been revictualed." (SC-062-038) The yacht returns at last, when "the Duncan sighted the American coast, and next day dropped anchor in the bay of Talcahuano." (SC-066-001)
