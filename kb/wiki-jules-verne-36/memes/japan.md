## Overview

Japan is the Pacific empire that lies beyond the last English ground of Hong Kong; beyond it, China, Japan, and America offered Fogg an almost certain refuge. (AWED-016-008) The Carnatic, setting sail from Hong Kong, directed her course at full steam towards Japan. (AWED-022-002)

Separated from his master, Passepartout found himself on the way to Japan, and wondered what he should do when he got there. (AWED-022-020)

## In the Narrative

Chance strands the party's fortunes on the crossing: the passengers for Japan would otherwise have had to await a week. (AWED-018-021) One might reach it, Fix notes, by going to Nagasaki, at the extreme south of Japan. (AWED-020-047)

At Yokohama, Passepartout wandered for hours amid the motley crowd, past jewellery establishments glittering with quaint Japanese ornaments. (AWED-022-026) The port is an important call in the Pacific, where mail-steamers between North America, China, Japan, and the Oriental islands put in. (AWED-022-021)

## Geography & Features

From here the great obstacle is the ocean: how to traverse the four thousand seven hundred miles of the Pacific which lay between Japan and the New World. (AWED-023-008) Fogg reassures Aouda that they are now beyond the fantastic countries of Japan and China. (AWED-024-010)

Its seas frame another voyage too: the shores of Japan were less than 200 miles to the Abraham Lincoln's leeward (TTLU-005-027), the starting point from which the Nautilus reckoned its leagues in the seas of Japan. (TTLU-020-018)
