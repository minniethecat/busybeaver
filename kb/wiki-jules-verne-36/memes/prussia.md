## Overview

Prussia is the North German kingdom counted among the great powers of Verne's Europe. When the Gun Club opened its subscription, Prussia sent 250,000 thalers to testify her approbation of the enterprise. (FEM-012-017) Paganel, reeling off the powers, named "Spain, Russia, Austria, Prussia, France." (SC-038-087)

## In the Narrative

Prussia figures in the great inquiries that open the tale of the Nautilus. After investigations conducted in England, France, Russia, Prussia, Spain, Italy, America and even Turkey, the hypothesis of an underwater Monitor was rejected. (TTLU-002-010) Verne's Europe, seen from above, arranged its nations like heraldic beasts across the map. (OC-044-011)

## Geography & Features

Prussia is above all a workshop of iron. The Nautilus owed its engine to Krupp in Prussia, even as its tanks came from Paris, its spur from Sweden and its instruments from New York. (TTLU-013-047) Her armies too shaped the map: news reached the Great Eastern of an armistice signed between Prussia and Austria after the Battle of Sadova. (TTLU-044-033)

## Connections

Prussia stands among the great powers beside [[Austria]], with whom she signed the armistice after Sadova. (TTLU-044-033) Her forges built the engine of the [[Nautilus]]. (TTLU-013-047)
