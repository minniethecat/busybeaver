## Overview

Denmark is one of the six polar powers of *Topsy-Turvy*, whose rights to the Arctic were absolutely established — America, England, Russia, Denmark, Sweden-Norway and Holland. (TT-001-058)

## In the Narrative

Denmark presses its claim to the North Pole, arguing it already possessed islands and fiords on the polar circle where colonies had been founded, such as Disko Island in the Davis Channel. (TT-002-003) Denmark had, she thought, undisputable rights to be proprietor of these regions. (TT-002-004)

The nation also lends its purse to science — Denmark, rather confined for room, gave 9,000 ducats to the Gun Club's lunar scheme, proving her love for scientific experiments. (FEM-012-021)

## Geography & Features

Its Arctic reach is real: Uppernawik was the most northerly settlement that Denmark possesses on those coasts (ACH-009-073), and Korsam lay in Zealand, an island belonging to Denmark. (ACH-058-017)

The *Journey to the Center of the Earth* expedition departs from its capital — three hours' travelling brought the party to the capital of Denmark (JCE-008-017), and the schooner doubled the Skaw at the northern point of Denmark before entering the North Sea. (JCE-009-015)
