# Captain Nicholl

## Overview

Barbicane's great adversary is introduced simply: "His name was Captain Nicholl" (FEM-010-007).

The two men embody opposite trades — "Barbicane was a great caster of projectiles, and Nicholl was an equally great forger of plate-armour" (FEM-010-009).

## Role in the Story

Their feud is a perpetual arms race: "As soon as Barbicane had invented a new projectile, Nicholl invented a new plate armour" (FEM-010-010).

By the latest trials the shield had won — "the cylindro-conical shots of Barbicane had no more effect than pins upon Nicholl's armour-plate" (FEM-010-012) — and the contest froze only because "the war ended the very day that Nicholl terminated a new forged armour-plate" (FEM-010-013).

Barbicane's moon-shot rekindles the quarrel: at the famous communication to the Gun Club "the anger of Captain Nicholl reached its maximum" (FEM-010-021). He assails the project with science, trying "to prove by A + B the falseness of his formulae, and he accused him of being ignorant of the rudimentary principles of ballistics" (FEM-010-023).

Cornered at last, "not being able to fight for his opinion, resolved to pay for it" (FEM-010-027) — staking a series of wagers against every stage of the enterprise.

## Character & Traits

His authority as a doubter carries weight; even "the Greenwich Observatory... boldly denied the possibility of success, and took up Captain Nicholl's theories" (FEM-012-006).

Yet he is a man of honour who settles his losses: when the cannon was finished "Captain Nicholl cleared off his debt to President Barbicane" (FEM-016-014), and to the very end "against the success of which Captain Nicholl had laid his third bet" (FEM-025-002).

Beneath the belligerence runs a hidden tenderness — surprised in the woods, he was found "forgetting the dangers of his situation... occupied in delivering as delicately as possible the victim taken in the meshes of the monstrous spider" (FEM-021-050).

## Relationships

- [[Impey Barbicane]] — his mirror-image rival, of whom he says "there is such rivalry that the death of one of us--" (FEM-021-059), before the two are reconciled.
- [[Michel Ardan]] — the mediator whose tempting proposition halts the duel and turns enemies into fellow travellers.
- [[J. T. Maston]] — the Gun Club secretary who, when the rivals accept Ardan's plan, "resolved to join them, and make a party of four" (FEM-022-018).

## Appearances

- [[From the Earth to the Moon]] — as Barbicane's rival turned companion in the projectile bound for the moon.
