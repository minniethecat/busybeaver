# The Volcano at the Pole

> **Note:** Inline PN citations for this page use a 4-character work code (`ACH`); they render as plain text pending RFC-0003. The underlying PN data is valid.

## Overview

The Volcano at the Pole is the climax of *[[the-adventures-of-captain-hatteras|The Adventures of Captain Hatteras]]*, in which the goal of the whole expedition proves to be no barren point of ice but a mountain of fire. As the party closes the final degrees — by the Doctor's calculations now only nine degrees distant from the Pole (ACH-052-058) — Hatteras is drawn onward like the magnetic needle itself.

## What Happens

Approaching the last open water, the travellers sight a great smoking peak, and the cry goes up: "It is a volcano!" (ACH-054-014). The Doctor, undismayed, reasons aloud, "Why, then, should not volcanoes be found near the North Pole?" (ACH-054-020), fitting the marvel into his science.

For Hatteras the sight fulfils an obsession; in his mind's eye he saw himself at the Pole unfurling the Union Jack! (ACH-054-029). The expedition presses through storm toward the burning summit that marks the northernmost point of the earth.

## Significance

The volcano transforms the Pole from an abstract coordinate into a place of overwhelming physical drama, rewarding Hatteras's monomania with a spectacle no explorer had imagined. That he saw himself at the Pole unfurling the Union Jack (ACH-054-029) exposes the obsession that has driven every earlier hardship, and the Doctor's calm question, "why should not volcanoes be found near the North Pole?" (ACH-054-020), keeps the wonder within the bounds of Verne's science even at the story's most extravagant reach.
