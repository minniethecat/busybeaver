## Overview

Connecticut is a New England state of the United States. In the Vernean corpus it is most closely tied to *An Antarctic Mystery*, where it is the home country of the narrator Jeorling, who names it repeatedly as the place he means to return to after his voyage into the southern ice.

It reappears as a stretch of the New England coast in *The Master of the World* and as the site of a notable tempest in *Robur the Conqueror*.

## In the Narrative

In *An Antarctic Mystery*, Jeorling is bound for home when he speaks of his wish "to return to Connecticut, to Providence, our capital." (AM-001-019) His origin is confirmed in exchange after exchange aboard ship — "You come from Connecticut, sir?" (AM-003-048)

The captain presses the point that, belonging to Connecticut and having visited Nantucket, Jeorling might have known the family of Arthur Gordon Pym. (AM-004-023) He is addressed throughout as "Mr. Jeorling, of Connecticut." (AM-007-038)

In *The Master of the World*, the Connecticut shore is one of the coasts along which the mysterious machine Terror is seen scurrying. (MW-006-025) The state is also recalled as the birthplace of an earlier amphibious craft: "at Bridgeport, Connecticut, there was launched a boat, The Protector, which could go on the water, under the water, and also upon land." (MW-007-055)

## Geography & Features

Connecticut is presented as a coastal New England state, its shoreline named alongside Maine and Massachusetts, and Providence given as its neighbouring capital in Jeorling's account.

Its inland weather furnishes a benchmark for extremity: the tempest in Connecticut on 22 March 1882, whose speed exceeded three hundred miles an hour, is invoked in *Robur the Conqueror* as the only storm comparable to the winds Robur's airship endures. (RC-018-016)

## Connections

Connecticut anchors the identity of Jeorling, whose journey drives *An Antarctic Mystery*, and it links that Antarctic narrative to the wider American geography Verne draws on across his voyages.

Through Bridgeport's submarine boat The Protector it prefigures the amphibious ambitions of the Terror in *The Master of the World*, while the 1882 tempest ties it to the meteorological wonders of *Robur the Conqueror*. It stands among the New England coastal states — beside Massachusetts and Maine — that recur along the seaboard of Verne's American scenes.
