## Overview

*Eight Hundred Leagues on the Amazon* is Jules Verne's mystery of a great river voyage haunted by a coded confession. It opens as the "captain of the woods" Torres pores over a cryptogram — "this strange assemblage of letters" whose meaning is locked without the cipher (EHLA-001-002, EHLA-001-004).

Torres is a former slave-hunter, wandering "the forests of Peru, from which issue the waters of the Upper Amazon" (EHLA-001-005, EHLA-001-008).

## Plot Summary

At Iquitos the wealthy farmer Joam Garral builds an immense jangada — a floating raft that gives "the appearance of a small village got adrift" — to carry his family and produce down the Amazon to Belem (EHLA-009-012, EHLA-009-015). With him travel his wife Yaquita, son Benito, daughter Minha, and Minha's betrothed Manoel (EHLA-008-021).

But Joam Garral is in truth Joam Dacosta, long ago condemned for "the crime of Tijuco" and living in voluntary exile under an assumed name (EHLA-025-014, EHLA-008-031). Torres, who knows the true culprit, holds "the material proof of Joam Dacosta's innocence" and tries to turn it into "an odious bargain" (EHLA-025-028, EHLA-039-025).

When Dacosta surrenders to clear his name, Judge Jarriquez must decipher the cryptogram — the confession of the real criminal, Ortega — to save him from the gallows. The key is found at last, and "the innocence of the fazender of Iquitos" is proclaimed (EHLA-038-075, EHLA-039-013).

## Main Characters

- Joam Garral (Joam Dacosta) — the fazender wrongly condemned for the crime of Tijuco
- Torres — the "captain of the woods" who blackmails Joam with the cryptogram
- Benito — Joam's son, appointed commander of the jangada's cargo
- Judge Jarriquez — the magistrate who decodes the document that saves Dacosta

## Key Places & Technology

- The jangada — the vast timber raft that carries the Garral family down the Amazon
- Iquitos — the Upper Amazon fazenda where the voyage begins

## Themes

Verne weaves a suspense of cipher and false identity into a meticulous descent of the Amazon, pairing riverine natural history with a courtroom race against the hangman. The story turns on the power of the written word — a single deciphered paragraph that can either doom or redeem an innocent man.
