# The Drifting Ice-Island

## Overview

The Drifting Ice-Island is the central catastrophe of *[[the-fur-country|The Fur Country]]*, in which the ground beneath a new Arctic settlement is torn loose from the continent. Connected with the mainland for thousands of centuries, the earthquake of the 8th of January had dragged it away from its moorings, and it was now a floating island, at the mercy of the winds and waves (FC-024-002).

## What Happens

The truth is grasped only slowly. The commander explains that "the earthquake cut off our communication with the mainland, and converted our peninsula into a floating island," accounting even for the animals penned in with the colonists round the fort (FC-024-032).

From the moment the earthquake broke the connecting link, the severed peninsula rose and sank with the ebb and flow of the tide (FC-024-029).

The drifting mass keeps the name it bore as solid ground: its longitude could not be fixed at once, the original name of [[Victoria Island]] being retained (FC-025-003). As the season turns, there could be no doubt that the bitter cold of winter would solder Victoria Island to the vast ice-field (FC-025-015), fixing for a time the course of the wandering colony.

## Significance

The episode transforms a routine trading venture into a slow-motion shipwreck on a raft the size of a country. Should the currents bear it south, Victoria Island, driven to the western waters of the Pacific, would gradually melt and go to pieces beneath the feet of its inhabitants (FC-025-011).

The doom is measured inch by inch: on later inspection the floating island had sunk six inches (FC-028-038).

The peril's reach is underscored when the native girl Kalumah is found on the floating island, two hundred miles from the American coast (FC-032-002), a distance that fixes how far the severed land has wandered from the world it left behind.

## Participants & Setting

- The severing and drift of [[Victoria Island]], once the [[Cape Bathurst]] peninsula.
- Endured by the colonists of [[Fort Hope]] under Lieutenant Jasper Hobson, joined by the Inuit girl Kalumah.
- Chronicled in [[The Fur Country]].
