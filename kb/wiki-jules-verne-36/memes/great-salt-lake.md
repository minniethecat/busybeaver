# Great Salt Lake

## Overview

The Great Salt Lake is the real Utah lake that the transcontinental railroad skirts on Phileas Fogg's dash across America. The train enters "Utah, the region of the Great Salt Lake, the singular colony of the Mormons." (AWED-026-026)

It gives its name to the Mormon capital, Salt Lake City, at which the travellers briefly pause.

## In the Narrative

Through the night "the train ran south-easterly for about fifty miles; then rose an equal distance in a north-easterly direction, towards the Great Salt Lake." (AWED-027-002) By half-past twelve "it reached the northwest border of the Great Salt Lake." (AWED-027-017)

At Ogden the party breaks the journey: they "had time to pay a visit to Salt Lake City, connected with Ogden by a branch road; and they spent two hours" there. (AWED-027-020) Passepartout, lectured on Mormon ways, "felt decidedly repelled from such a vocation." (AWED-027-022)

## Geography & Features

The lake is broad and high: "the Salt Lake, seventy miles long and thirty-five wide, is situated three miles eight hundred feet above the sea." (AWED-027-018)

It marks the summit of the line's climb — "the track up to this time had reached its highest elevation at the Great Salt Lake." (AWED-028-003) Leaving it "at Ogden, passed northward for an hour as far as Weber River, having completed nearly nine hundred miles from San Francisco." (AWED-028-002)
