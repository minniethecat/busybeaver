# Marfa Strogoff

## Overview

Marfa Strogoff is the aged mother of the Czar's courier, living at Omsk since the death of "old Peter Strogoff, dead ten years since" (MS-004-035). She "could never be induced to leave the house of the Strogoffs" (MS-004-038), and is the one being for whom her son feels his only tenderness.

## Role in the Story

When Michael passes through Omsk under a false name, "his mother, the old woman Marfa, was before him!" (MS-015-057). Sensing the danger to his mission, she tests him aloud — "'Thou art not the son of Peter and Marfa Strogoff?'" (MS-015-064) — then publicly disowns him, though she confides, "'Do you think that for anything in the world I would deny a son whom God has given me?'" (MS-015-094).

Seized by the Tartars, "Marfa Strogoff, with firm step, followed the Tartar" into captivity (MS-015-074).

Later, catching sight of Michael among the prisoners, she holds herself rigid: "'It is my son... and you see that I do not make a step towards him!'" (MS-022-022). Michael sees her restraint, and "Marfa, then, had understood all, and kept his secret" (MS-022-024).

## Character & Traits

Marfa's courage is maternal and unbreakable. Brought under the soldiers' whips, "as she did not throw herself quickly in the dust, the guards brutally pushed her. She fell" (MS-023-031); yet her deepest pain is self-reproach — she "longed to ask her son's pardon for the harm she had unintentionally done him" (MS-023-017).

At the climax of his ordeal she stands with "her eyes open wide, her arms extended towards where he stood" (MS-024-051), before "his aged mother fell senseless to the ground" (MS-024-052).

## Relationships

- [[Michel Strogoff]] — her son, the Czar's courier, whose secret she guards at the cost of her own suffering (MS-022-024).
